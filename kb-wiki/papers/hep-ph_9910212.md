# Exclusive Hadronic Reactions at High $Q^2$

> The observed scaling laws for large angle exclusive hadronic reactions are successfully accounted for by the short-distance QCD quark interchange processes. We focus on three hadronic reactions which shows evidence for a slight oscillatory deviation from the expected scaling behaviour. Possible explanations for these oscillations and connections to spin observables will be mentioned. Better data from excisting facilities (or a possible future KEK proton accelerator, JHP) can clarify the theoretical situation.

## Key claim

The observed scaling laws for large-angle exclusive hadronic reactions are successfully accounted for by the short-distance QCD quark interchange model (QIM), but three specific reactions show evidence for a slight oscillatory deviation from the expected smooth power-law fall-off when the scaled cross section is plotted as a function of ln(s). The paper argues that these oscillations may be real and require refinements to the QIM, but cautions that more accurate data at higher energies are needed to draw firm conclusions.

## Method

The author examines the differential cross section dσ/dt at 90° for three exclusive hadronic reactions: pp elastic scattering, π⁻p elastic scattering, and p̅p → π⁰π⁰. For each reaction, the measured cross section is multiplied by s^N (where N is the predicted power-law exponent, e.g., N=10 for pp and N=8 for π⁻p and p̅p→π⁰π⁰) and plotted as a function of ln(s) to reveal deviations from a constant expected value. The paper also discusses spin observables, particularly the analyzing power A₀n, as additional tests of QCD corrections.

## Key numbers

- For pp elastic scattering at 90°, the differential cross section scales as dσ/dt ∼ s⁻¹⁰, and the scaled cross section s¹⁰ dσ/dt shows oscillatory behavior when plotted against ln(s) (Fig. 2).
- For π⁻p elastic scattering at 90°, the differential cross section scales as dσ/dt ∼ s⁻⁸, and the scaled cross section s⁸ dσ/dt is shown as a function of s (Fig. 3), with the plab = 30 GeV/c point having too large error bars to be useful.
- For p̅p → π⁰π⁰ at 90°, the differential cross section scales as dσ/dt ∼ s⁻⁸, and the scaled cross section s⁸ dσ/dt is plotted as a function of ln(s) (Fig. 4), with the highest p̅ energy at the Fermilab antiproton accumulator corresponding to ln(s) ≈ 2.9.
- For p̅p → π⁺π⁻ at plab ≈ 2.2 GeV/c (ln(s) ≈ 1.8), the analyzing power A₀n is approximately +1 (Carter).
- For pp elastic scattering, the beam-target spin correlation Aₙₙ at 90° has been measured up to s = 26 GeV² (Crosbie 1981).

## Context

This work extends the quark interchange model (QIM) scaling predictions of Sivers (review) by focusing on oscillatory deviations observed in pp elastic scattering and searching for similar behavior in π⁻p elastic scattering and p̅p → π⁰π⁰. The paper discusses two theoretical explanations for the oscillations: dibaryon resonances associated with the charm threshold (Brodsky and de Teramond 1988) and interference from the Landshoff process (Landshoff 1974) with its energy-dependent phase from radiative corrections (Ralston 1986, 1988; Botts and Sterman 1989). Better measurements from existing facilities (Fermilab E835 collaboration) or a possible future KEK proton accelerator (JHP) could clarify the theoretical situation.
