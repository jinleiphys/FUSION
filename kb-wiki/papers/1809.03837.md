# Correlated structure of nuclear symmetry energy from covariant nucleon self-energy

> Based on the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, the symmetry energy $J$ and its density slope parameter $L$ are decomposed in terms of the nucleon self-energies within the covariant density functional (CDF) theory. It is found that two structural connections between the different ingredients of $J$ and $L$ construct the fundamental correlation between $L$ and $J$ in the relativistic covariant framework, while the additional contribution from the isovector scalar channel of nucleon-nucleon interaction and those from the second-order symmetry self-energies lead to a deviation, especially the latter limits severely its correlation coefficient and confidence level. In addition, the relationship between the Landau mass $M_L^*$ and the Dirac mass $M_D^*$ is approximated to a reliable linear correlation, which is demonstrate to be sensitive to the momentum dependence of the nucleon self-energies.

## Key claim

The correlation between the symmetry energy \(J\) and its density slope parameter \(L\) in the relativistic covariant framework is built from two underlying linear correlations among their decomposed components, but the correlation is weakened by the isovector scalar channel of the nucleon-nucleon interaction and especially by the model-dependent second-order symmetry self-energy contributions, which severely limit the correlation coefficient and confidence level.

## Method

The authors decompose the symmetry energy \(J\) and its density slope parameter \(L\) at saturation density into components (kinetic, momentum-dependent, first-order symmetry self-energy, cross-term, and second-order symmetry self-energy) using the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem within covariant density functional (CDF) theory. They compute these components for 31 parameter sets spanning relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF), density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD-RMF), nonlinear relativistic mean-field (NL-RMF), and point-coupling relativistic mean-field (PC-RMF) models. Linear fits and Pearson correlation coefficients are extracted for the relationships between the components, and the effects of including the isovector scalar channel and the second-order symmetry self-energies are examined.

## Key numbers

* The symmetry energy \(J\) is estimated as \(33.74^{+9.76}_{-8.14}\) MeV at saturation density using a weighted average over all selected CDFs.
* The density slope parameter \(L\) lies in a wide range \(85.31^{+54.79}_{-38.11}\) MeV.
* The kinetic components obey \(L^{\rm{kin}} = 1.51 J^{\rm{kin}} + 5.72\) MeV with Pearson correlation coefficient \(r = 0.9979\).
* The combined components (excluding the isovector scalar channel) obey \(L^{\rm{mom}}+L^{\rm{1st}}+L^{\rm{cross}} = 2.98 (J^{\rm{mom}}+J^{\rm{1st}}) + 0.45\) MeV with \(r = 0.9999\).
* Without the isovector scalar channel, the overall fit is \(L = 2.91 J - 17.95 + L^{\rm{2nd}}\) MeV with \(r = 0.9905\).
* The second-order symmetry self-energy contribution \(L^{\rm{2nd}} = 2.87^{+29.03}_{-36.17}\) MeV.
* The Landau mass and Dirac mass relation for RMF models is \(M_L^*/M = 0.93 M_D^*/M + 0.11\) with \(r = 0.9959\).

## Context

This work extends the decomposition of symmetry energy in terms of nucleon self-energies previously applied in non-relativistic frameworks by Xu (2011) and Chen (2012), and in relativistic mean-field models by Cai (2012) and Li (2018). It systematically tests 31 CDF parameter sets to reveal the microscopic origin of the \(L\)-\(J\) correlation, showing that two linear correlations among the decomposed components are robust, while the isovector scalar channel and second-order symmetry self-energies introduce deviations. The results enable future studies to use constraints on \(J\) to better determine \(L\) and to test the role of the isovector scalar channel and momentum dependence in nuclear interactions.
