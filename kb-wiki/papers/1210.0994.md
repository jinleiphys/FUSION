# Properties of Z=120 nuclei and the α-decay chains of the (292,304)120 isotopes using relativistic and non-relativistic formalisms

> The ground state and first intrinsic excited state of superheavy nuclei with Z=120 and N=160-204 are investigated using both non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock and the axially deformed Relativistic Mean Field formalisms. We employ a simple BCS pairing approach for calculating the energy contribution from pairing interaction. The results for isotopic chain of binding energy, quadrupole deformation parameter, two neutron separation energies and some other observables are compared with the FRDM and some recent macroscopic-microscopic calculations. We predict superdeformed ground state solutions for almost all the isotopes. Considering the possibility of magic neutron number, two different mode of α-decay chains (292)120 and (304)120 are also studied within these frameworks. The Q_α-values and the half-life T^α_{1/2} for these two different mode of decay chains are compared with FRDM and recent macroscopic-microscopic calculations. The calculation is extended for the α-decay chains of 292120 and 304120 from their exited state configuration to respective configuration, which predicts long half-life T^α_{1/2}(sec.).

## Key claim

The authors predict superdeformed ground state solutions for almost all Z=120 isotopes (N=160-204) using both relativistic and non-relativistic formalisms, but note that shape coexistence makes the ground state assignment ambiguous; for example, in 290120 the two solutions for β2 = 0.555 and β2 = 0.002 are completely degenerate with binding energies of 2047.503 and 2047.202 MeV, meaning the ground state can change to the excited state by a small change in input parameters.

## Method

The authors performed axially deformed Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) calculations with NL3* and NL3 parameter sets and non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations with SkI4 and SLy4 parameter sets for the isotopic chain 280-324120 (Z=120, N=160-204). A simple BCS pairing approach with constant pairing gaps was employed for pairing correlations. The Qα values for α-decay chains of 292120 and 304120 were computed from binding energy differences, and half-lives were estimated using the Viola-Seaborg formula with Sobiczewski-modified parameters.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 304120: 2142.57 MeV (RMF NL3*), 2134.96 MeV (SHF SkI4), 2112.37 MeV (SHF SLy4), 2137.99 MeV (FRDM)
- Quadrupole deformation parameter β2 for 304120: 0.591 (RMF NL3*), 0.002 (SHF SkI4), 0.005 (SHF SLy4), 0.000 (FRDM)
- Two-neutron separation energy S2n for 304120: 12.29 MeV (RMF NL3*), 9.89 MeV (SHF SkI4), 9.38 MeV (SHF SLy4), 11.94 MeV (FRDM)
- Binding energy per particle (BE/A) peaks at A ~ 304 across all models, indicating 304120 (N=184, Z=120) as the most stable isotope
- For 290120, two degenerate solutions exist: β2 = 0.555 with BE = 2047.503 MeV and β2 = 0.002 with BE = 2047.202 MeV (RMF NL3)
- Pairing energy Epair decreases with increasing mass number A across the isotopic chain

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical predictions of shell closures in the superheavy region, where RMF calculations had suggested Z=120 and N=184 as probable magic numbers (Gupta 1997) while macroscopic-microscopic models predicted Z=114, N=184 (Patyk 1991, Nix 1994). The results are compared with Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) calculations (Moller 1997) and earlier microscopic predictions (Rutz 1997, Ren 2001-2004). The study enables future experimental searches for Z=120 isotopes by providing predicted α-decay chain properties and half-lives for 292120 and 304120, which could guide synthesis attempts at laboratories such as GSI, RIKEN, and JINR.
