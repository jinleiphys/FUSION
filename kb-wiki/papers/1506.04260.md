# Inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking phases in isospin-asymmetric matter

> We investigate the effects of isospin asymmetry on inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking phases within the two-flavor NJL model. After introducing a plane-wave ansatz for each quark-flavor condensate, we find that, as long as their periodicities are enforced to be equal, a non-zero isospin chemical potential shrinks the size of the inhomogeneous phase. The asymmetry reached in charge neutral matter is nevertheless not excessively large, so that an inhomogeneous window is still present in the phase diagram. Lifting the constraint of equal periodicities alters the picture significantly, as the inhomogeneous phase survives in a much larger region of the phase diagram.

## Key claim

The paper finds that isospin asymmetry shrinks the inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking phase in the two-flavor NJL model when the periodicities of up- and down-quark condensates are forced to be equal, but that lifting this constraint allows the inhomogeneous phase to survive in a much larger region of the phase diagram, nearly as large as in the isospin-symmetric case.

## Method

The authors employ the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the chiral limit, using a mean-field approximation with a plane-wave (chiral density wave) ansatz for each quark-flavor condensate. They compute the grand potential per unit volume numerically, diagonalizing the effective mean-field Hamiltonian after a Fourier transform to momentum space, and regularize divergent contributions using a Pauli-Villars scheme. The thermodynamic potential is minimized with respect to the amplitudes and wave numbers of the condensates, first enforcing equal periodicities (q_u = -q_d) and then allowing for unequal periodicities with rational ratios R = q_u/q_d.

## Key numbers

- For isospin-symmetric matter (μ_I = 0), the inhomogeneous phase appears as an "island" in the μ̄-T phase diagram between the homogeneously broken and restored chiral symmetry regions.
- For the CDW-type ansatz with equal periodicities, the inhomogeneous phase survives beyond |μ_I| = 120 MeV, though it shrinks as μ_I increases.
- For charge-neutral matter in beta equilibrium at T = 1 MeV, the isospin chemical potential μ_Q remains below 80 MeV in the region where the inhomogeneous phase is expected.
- For μ_I = 60 MeV with unequal periodicities, the favored ratios R = q_u/q_d explored were -1, 4/3, 2, and 5, with different ratios favored in different regions of the phase diagram.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking in the NJL model (reviewed by Buballa (2014)) from isospin-symmetric matter to isospin-asymmetric matter relevant for compact stars. The authors note that earlier studies (Kutschera (1990), Nakano (2004)) introduced the chiral density wave ansatz, while Nickel (2009) developed the Pauli-Villars regularization scheme used here. The results enable more realistic modeling of quark matter in neutron stars, where electric charge neutrality and beta equilibrium impose isospin asymmetry, and suggest that inhomogeneous phases may still be present under those conditions.
