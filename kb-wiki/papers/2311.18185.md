# Recent Developments of Small-$x$ Evolution for Quark and Gluon Helicity

> Helicity of quarks and gluons inside the proton at small Bjorken $x$ is one of the missing pieces of the proton spin puzzle, with limited experimental results due to various challenges. To address the problem, we derive under the framework of color glass condensate (CGC) effective theory a renormalization group equation in rapidity for the parton helicity distributions, which resums double-logarithmic factor, $α_s\ln^2(1/x)$, with $α_s$ being the strong coupling constant. With running coupling, the equation produces results at Bjorken $x\leq 0.1$ that are consistent with the world polarized scattering data. There is an evidence for a significant parton helicity contribution from the small-$x$ region. However, the helicity estimate still contains a large uncertainty that will dramatically reduce with the upcoming measurements from the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). Ongoing attempts to reduce the uncertainty of our helicity prediction in the medium term will also be discussed.

## Key claim
The KPS-CTT evolution equation, which resums double-logarithmic factors α_s ln²(1/x) for parton helicity at small Bjorken x, produces results consistent with world polarized scattering data when running coupling is used, but the resulting estimate of total parton helicity from the small-x region still carries a large uncertainty that will only be dramatically reduced by upcoming Electron-Ion Collider measurements.

## Method
The authors derived a renormalization group equation in rapidity (the KPS-CTT equation) under the color glass condensate effective theory, incorporating sub-eikonal corrections to Wilson lines that carry helicity information. They linearized the equation in the large-N_c & N_f limit by treating the unpolarized dipole amplitude as fixed at its moderate-x initial condition, then performed a global Bayesian analysis using JAM Monte Carlo framework against 226 data points from polarized deep-inelastic scattering and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering experiments (SLAC, EMC, SMC, COMPASS, HERMES) at 0.005 ≤ x ≤ 0.1 and 1.69 GeV² ≤ Q² ≤ 10.4 GeV².

## Key numbers
- The fit to 226 data points from polarized DIS and SIDIS experiments yields χ² = 1.03 per degree of freedom.
- The predicted combined quark and gluon spin contribution from the small-x region (x from 10⁻⁵ to 0.1) at Q² = 10 GeV² is (S_q + S_g)|_{small x} = -0.64 ± 0.60.
- The small-x asymptotic behavior for the flavor singlet combination is ΔΣ(x,Q²) ∼ ΔG(x,Q²) ∼ g₁(x,Q²) ∼ (1/x)^{3.43√(α_s N_c/2π)} for N_f = 3.
- The small-x asymptotic for the flavor non-singlet quark hPDF is Δq⁻(x,Q²) ∼ (1/x)^{√(α_s N_c/π)}.
- The analysis covers the x range 0.005 ≤ x ≤ x₀ = 0.1 and Q² range 1.69 GeV² ≤ Q² ≤ 10.4 GeV².

## Context
This work extends the program of Kovchegov, Pitonyak, Sievert, and collaborators (2015-2023) on small-x helicity evolution, building on the KPS-CTT equation derived by Cougoulic, Tarasov, and Tawabutr (2022). The global analysis by Adamiak, Baldonado, Melnitchouk, Pitonyak, Sato, Sievert, and Tawabutr (2023) demonstrates consistency with world data, in contrast to DGLAP-based approaches which have larger uncertainties when extrapolated to small x. The framework enables predictions for the Electron-Ion Collider and can be extended to study parton orbital angular momentum (Kovchegov 2019, 2023) and other transverse-momentum-dependent distributions at small x.
