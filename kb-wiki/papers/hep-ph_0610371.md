# The kaon optical potential modified by Θ^+ Pentaquark excitation

> We study the kaon nuclear optical potential including the effect of the Θ^+ pentaquark in a self-consistent approach, starting from an extension of the Juelich meson-exchange potential as bare kaon-nucleon interaction. Significant differences between a fully self-consistent calculation and the low-density Tρapproximation are observed. Whereas te influence of the KN -> Θ^+ absorption process is found to be negligible, due to the small Θ^+ KN coupling, the two-nucleon mechanism (KNN -> Θ^+ N), estimated from the two-meson coupling of the pentaquark, provides the necessary additional kaon absorption to reconcile with data the systematically low theoretical K^+ nucleus reaction cross sections.

## Key claim

The two-nucleon kaon absorption mechanism (KNN -> Θ⁺ N), estimated from the two-meson coupling of the pentaquark, provides the necessary additional kaon absorption to reconcile the systematically low theoretical K⁺-nucleus reaction cross sections with experimental data, whereas the one-nucleon KN -> Θ⁺ absorption process has negligible influence due to the small Θ⁺ KN coupling.

## Method

The authors start from an extension of the Jülich meson-exchange potential as the bare kaon-nucleon interaction and compute the in-medium KN interaction using a G-matrix approach that includes Pauli blocking and self-consistent dressing of the kaon and nucleon single-particle energies. They compare this fully self-consistent calculation to the low-density Tρ approximation, and then incorporate the two-nucleon kaon absorption mechanism via Θ⁺ excitation, evaluating the Θ⁺ Kπ N coupling from a model of the pentaquark's two-meson cloud with the pion coupling to particle-hole and Delta-hole excitations. Kaon-nucleus cross sections are calculated in the eikonal formalism using the resulting kaon self-energy.

## Key numbers

- The self-consistent G-matrix calculation gives a kaon optical potential (at zero momentum) of 39 MeV repulsive at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀, which is 10 MeV more repulsive than the Tρ approximation.
- The one-nucleon contribution to the imaginary part of the kaon optical potential indicates kaon widths of about 18 MeV at approximately 400 MeV/c momentum.
- The two-nucleon absorption cross sections per nucleon are about 2-3 mb, below the upper bound of 3.5 mb established in prior work.
- Reaction cross sections per nucleon from the G-matrix calculation (including one-nucleon Θ⁺ excitation) underestimate experimental data by about 15%.
- The two-nucleon contribution to the imaginary part of the kaon optical potential is evaluated as a function of density for a kaon momentum of 500 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends prior self-consistent approaches to the kaon optical potential, such as Korpa (2005) which showed a kaon mass shift of 36 MeV at ρ₀, and addresses a long-standing problem where Tρ approximation calculations (Kaiser, Oset) systematically underestimate K⁺-nucleus reaction cross sections by 10-15%. The study follows the suggestion of Gal (2005) that incorporating K⁺ absorption by nucleon pairs could improve fits to data, and provides a microscopic calculation of this mechanism using the Θ⁺ interaction model of Hosaka (2005). The results enable improved predictions for kaon-nucleus scattering and suggest a resolution to the anomaly in kaon-nucleus reaction cross sections.
