# Partial wave decomposition of finite-range effective tensor interaction

> We perform a detailed analysis of the properties of the finite-range tensor term associated with the Gogny and M3Y effective interactions. In particular, by using a partial wave decomposition of the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter, we show how we can extract their tensor parameters directly from microscopic results based on bare nucleon-nucleon interactions. Furthermore, we show that the zero-range limit of both finite-range interactions has the form of the N3LO Skyrme pseudo-potential, which thus constitutes a reliable approximation in the density range relevant for finite nuclei. Finally, we use Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results to fix the tensor parameters for the three effective interactions.

# Partial wave decomposition of finite-range effective tensor interaction

## Key claim

The tensor parameters of finite-range effective interactions (Gogny D1ST2a and M3Y-P2) can be extracted from microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results via partial wave decomposition of symmetric nuclear matter, but the existing parametrizations of both interactions produce results that disagree with microscopic calculations in sign or magnitude, requiring refitting to achieve consistency.

## Method

The authors perform a partial wave decomposition of the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state, projecting the potential energy per particle onto J, L, S, T subspaces. They construct weighted differences of partial wave contributions (specifically combinations involving P, D, and F waves) to isolate tensor and spin-orbit contributions from central terms. These combinations are then calculated analytically for the Gogny D1ST2a interaction (with a Gaussian tensor form) and the M3Y-P2 interaction (with Yukawa tensor form), and compared to Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results from the Argonne v14 interaction plus Urbana three-body term. The tensor parameters are then refitted to the BHF results for Fermi momenta up to 1.7 fm^{-1}.

## Key numbers

- Gogny D1ST2a original tensor parameters: V_T1+V_T2 = -75 MeV fm^5; V_T1-V_T2 = -195 MeV fm^5
- Gogny D1ST2a refitted tensor parameters: V_T1+V_T2 = 82.3 MeV fm^5; V_T1-V_T2 = -273.9 MeV fm^5
- Gogny D1ST2a spin-orbit parameter: W_0 = 103 MeV fm^5 (smaller than original D1ST value of 130 MeV fm^5)
- Gogny tensor range: mu_G = 1.2 fm
- M3Y-P2 original tensor parameters: t^{TNE}_1 = -131.52 MeV fm^{-2}; t^{TNE}_2 = -3.708 MeV fm^{-2}; t^{TNO}_1 = 29.28 MeV fm^{-2}; t^{TNO}_2 = 1.872 MeV fm^{-2}
- M3Y-P2 refitted tensor parameters: t^{TNE}_1 = 1299.3 MeV fm^{-2}; t^{TNE}_2 = -299.50 MeV fm^{-2}; t^{TNO}_1 = 1734.5 MeV fm^{-2}; t^{TNO}_2 = 29.709 MeV fm^{-2}
- M3Y-P2 ranges: mu_{LS}^{-1} = 0.25 and 0.4 fm; mu_T^{-1} = 0.4 and 0.7 fm
- Fit limited to Fermi momentum k_F <= 1.7 fm^{-1}, corresponding to densities relevant for finite nuclei

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Davesne et al. 2014, 2015) on using partial wave decomposition and linear response theory to constrain effective interaction parameters. It directly addresses inconsistencies in existing tensor parametrizations: the D1ST2a interaction (Graczyk et al. 2013) gives the wrong sign for tensor contributions compared to microscopic BHF results, while M3Y-P2 (Nakada 2003) gives the correct sign but systematically too-small magnitudes. The analysis also shows that the N3LO Skyrme pseudo-potential (Carlsson et al. 2008, Raimondi et al. 2011) provides a reliable low-momentum expansion of any finite-range tensor interaction in the density range relevant for finite nuclei, enabling future fits that combine constraints from both infinite matter and finite nuclei.
