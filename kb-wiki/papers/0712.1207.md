# Light nuclei from chiral EFT interactions

> Recent developments in nuclear theory allow us to make a connection between quantum chromodynamics (QCD) and low-energy nuclear physics. First, chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) provides a natural hierarchy to define two-nucleon (NN), three-nucleon (NNN), and even four-nucleon interactions. Second, ab initio methods have been developed capable to test these interactions for light nuclei. In this contribution, we discuss ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for s-shell and p-shell nuclei with NN and NNN interactions derived within chiEFT.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) two-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (NNN) interactions, when used in no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations, can reproduce many properties of light s-shell and p-shell nuclei, but the low-energy constants \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) of the NNN interaction must be constrained by \(A=3\) binding energies and further tuned (e.g., to \(c_D=-1\)) to avoid overestimating the \(^4\)He radius and to obtain the correct ordering of states in \(^{10}\)B.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for s-shell and p-shell nuclei using the chiral NN interaction at N\(^3\)LO and the chiral NNN interaction at N\(^2\)LO. The NCSM uses a harmonic oscillator basis truncated by a maximal HO excitation energy \(N_{\rm max}\hbar\Omega\) and employs effective interactions derived from the original potentials via a unitary transformation. The two low-energy constants \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) of the NNN interaction were determined by fitting the binding energies of \(^3\)H and \(^3\)He, and then varied to study the sensitivity of nuclear properties in \(^4\)He and p-shell nuclei.

## Key numbers

- \(^3\)H binding energy: 8.482 MeV; \(^3\)He binding energy: 7.718 MeV; used to constrain the \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) LECs.
- \(^4\)He ground-state energy calculations were performed with HO frequencies of \(\hbar\Omega=28\) and 36 MeV, and were fully converged (independent of \(N_{\rm max}\) cutoff and \(\hbar\Omega\)).
- For p-shell nuclei, calculations were performed in basis spaces up to \(N_{\rm max}=6\) (\(6\hbar\Omega\)) with HO frequencies of \(\hbar\Omega=14\) or 15 MeV.
- The \(^{10}\)B B(E2) ratio from the ground state to the first and second \(1^+ 0\) states changes by several orders of magnitude depending on the \(c_D\) value.
- For \(c_D<-2\) the \(^4\)He radius underestimates experiment; for \(c_D>0\) the lowest two \(1^+\) states of \(^{10}\)B are reversed; the authors selected \(c_D=-1\) for further investigation.
- The n+\(^4\)He \(^{2}S_{1/2}\) phase shift was calculated using the chiral NN potential in model spaces up to \(14\hbar\Omega\).

## Context

This work extends earlier NCSM calculations by applying chiral EFT interactions (Weinberg, 1990s; van Kolck, 1994) to both s-shell and p-shell nuclei, demonstrating that the chiral NNN interaction is essential for correct ground-state spins (e.g., in \(^{10}\)B). The results enable future studies of sub-leading NNN interactions and four-nucleon interactions at N\(^3\)LO (Epelbaum, 2006), as well as extensions to nuclear reactions via the Lorentz Integral Transform (Quaglioni, 2007) and a no-core shell model combined with the resonating group method (RGM).
