# Dynamical simulation of heavy-ion collisions in the energy range from a few tens MeV/A to a few hundreds MeV/A

> The overlapping stage of heavy-ion reactions can be simulated by dynamical microscopical models, such as those built on the basis of the Molecular Dynamics (MD) approaches, allowing to study the fragment formation process. The present performances of the Quantum MD (QMD) code developed at the University of Milano are discussed, showing results concerning fragment and particle production at bombarding energies up to $\lsim$ 700 MeV/A, as well as a preliminary analysis on the isoscaling behaviour of isotopic yield ratios for reactions with isospin composition N/Z in the (1 - 1.2) range, at a 45 MeV/A bombarding energy.

## Key claim

The Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) code developed at the University of Milano, when interfaced with the FLUKA de-excitation module, can reproduce experimental data on fragment and particle production from heavy-ion collisions up to about 700 MeV/A, but the agreement is often qualitative rather than quantitative, with systematic discrepancies that point to both model limitations and possible experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The QMD code simulates the overlapping stage of heavy-ion collisions (up to approximately 200-250 fm/c) by treating nucleons as Gaussian wave packets and propagating them under a mean-field potential plus stochastic nucleon-nucleon collisions. Primary fragments are identified at the end of each QMD event using a phase-space correlation algorithm, and these excited prefragments are then passed to the FLUKA de-excitation module, which handles evaporation, fission, and Fermi break-up on a statistical basis. The code was applied to simulate inclusive reactions for various projectile-target combinations at bombarding energies from 45 MeV/A up to 650 MeV/A, and results were compared to experimental data from the literature.

## Key numbers

- Double-differential neutron production yields were simulated for C and Ne beams on C, Al, and Cu targets at 400 MeV/A bombarding energy, with comparisons to both re-evaluated and old experimental data at angles from 0 degrees to 90 degrees.
- Projectile-like fragment production cross sections were calculated for 650 MeV/A Ar and Cl beams fragmenting on C, Al, Cu, and Pb targets; for Fe + H, Fe + C, and Fe + Al at 400 and 500 MeV/A, the agreement between simulation and experiment was within 30-35% for most fragments.
- For isoscaling studies at 45 MeV/A bombarding energy, the average alpha values obtained from the QMD + FLUKA simulation were approximately 0.18 for the Ar + Ni / Ca + Ni ratio and approximately 0.31 for the Ar + Fe / Ca + Ni ratio, for fragments with Z = 3-7.
- In central collisions at 45 MeV/A, the yield of protons emitted in Ca + Ni reactions was approximately 20% larger than the yield of neutrons, while for Ar + Ni and Ar + Fe central collisions, the proton yield was approximately 10% and 15% smaller than the neutron yield, respectively.
- The average alpha value at the end of the overlapping stage (alpha_hot) can be larger than the alpha value after full de-excitation by no more than approximately 20%.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Milano QMD code, previously described in Cosparmv and NDMV papers, to higher bombarding energies and to isoscaling observables, building on the experimental isoscaling studies by Tsang (2001) and Yennello (2004) at the Cyclotron Institute of TAMU and the NSCL of MSU. The results enable further refinement of transport models for heavy-ion collisions, particularly for applications in hadrontherapy, space radiation protection, and microelectronics, while also highlighting the need to include shell effects, spin-orbit coupling, ground-state deformation, and antisymmetrization (as in the AMD model of Ono and Horiuchi) to improve agreement with experiment.
