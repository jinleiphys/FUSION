# Baryon magnetic moments and sigma terms in lattice-regularized chiral perturbation theory

> An SU(3) chiral Lagrangian for the lightest decuplet of baryons is constructed on a discrete lattice of spacetime points, and is added to an existing lattice Lagrangian for the lightest octets of mesons and baryons. A nonzero lattice spacing renders all loop integrations finite, and the continuum limit of any physical observable is identical to the result obtained from dimensional regularization. Chiral symmetry and gauge invariance are preserved even at nonzero lattice spacing. Specific calculations discussed here include the non-renormalization of a conserved vector current, the magnetic moments of octet baryons, and the pi N and KN sigma terms that relate to the nucleon's strangeness content. The quantitative difference between physics at a nonzero lattice spacing and physics in the continuum limit is easily computed, and it represents an expectation for the size of discretization errors in corresponding lattice QCD simulations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that lattice-regularized chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) with explicit decuplet baryon fields produces continuum-limit results identical to dimensional regularization, but the quantitative discretization effects at nonzero lattice spacing are easily computed and represent expectations for lattice QCD simulation errors, though the authors emphasize their minimal Lagrangian does not correspond to any specific lattice QCD action, leaving O(a) parameters undetermined.

## Method

The authors construct an SU(3) chiral Lagrangian on an isotropic hypercubic lattice that includes pseudoscalar mesons, spin-1/2 octet baryons, and spin-3/2 decuplet baryons, using heavy baryon fields and a small-scale expansion. They discretize covariant derivatives and field strength tensors while preserving chiral symmetry and gauge invariance, then compute one-loop corrections to octet baryon electromagnetic vertices and nucleon sigma terms as functions of lattice spacing. The non-renormalization of electric charge is verified analytically by summing all one-loop contributions, and magnetic moments are obtained by evaluating loop integrals numerically at finite lattice spacing.

## Key numbers

- At lattice spacing a = 0.10 fm (π/a = 6.0 GeV), the ratio μ_B(a=0.1 fm)/μ_B(a=0) ranges from 0.97 (Σ⁻) to 2.58 (Ξ⁰), with the continuum-limit magnetic moments (in μ_N) being: Σ⁺ = 1.64, Σ⁰ = 0.12, Σ⁻ = -1.40, Ξ⁰ = -0.14, Ξ⁻ = -0.98, Λ = -0.12, ΛΣ⁰ = 1.11
- The loop integrals use meson masses defined by m_M = (2/a) arcsinh(a x_M/2), with standard input values D = 0.75, F = 0.50, and C = 1.5
- The decuplet-octet mass difference Δ enters the decuplet propagator as (1 + aΔ)(1 - cos(aq_4)) + a²Δ²/2 in the denominator

## Context

This work extends the lattice-regularized chiral perturbation theory of the authors' previous paper (Ref. lchpt) by adding the decuplet baryon fields, and builds upon the small-scale expansion framework of Jenkins and Manohar (Ref. JM) and Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (Ref. HHK). The numerical results for magnetic moments at finite lattice spacing provide a benchmark for discretization errors in lattice QCD simulations, while the sigma term calculations connect to ongoing studies of the nucleon's strangeness content (Ref. BBsigma). The authors note that Rupak and Shoresh (Ref. RandS) have identified five O(a) terms for Wilson-type lattice QCD actions, and emphasize that their minimal Lagrangian can be extended to match specific lattice QCD actions.
