# Qunatum Number Exotic Mesons and Large N QCD

> Exotic mesons with quantum numbers of a hybrid ($\bar{q} q g$) such as $J^{PC} = 1^{-+}$are shown to exist as narrow resonant states in the large $N_c$ limit of QCD. The width of these states is proportional to $1/N_c$. Possible phenomenological implications for the world of $N_c=3$ are discussed.

## Key claim

Exotic mesons with quantum numbers of a hybrid ($\bar{q} q g$) such as $J^{PC} = 1^{-+}$ exist as narrow resonant states in the large $N_c$ limit of QCD, with widths proportional to $1/N_c$. The claim is conditional on the large $N_c$ world being a reasonable caricature of the physical $N_c=3$ world, and the author notes that typical mesonic widths are strongly channel dependent, so it remains possible that all quantum number exotic hybrid widths are "accidentally" large.

## Method

The author uses the $1/N_c$ expansion technique of QCD, analyzing connected n-point correlation functions of currents that carry meson, hybrid, and glueball quantum numbers. By examining the $N_c$ scaling of these correlation functions through 't Hooft diagrams, the author shows that hybrid currents create single-particle states with amplitude $\sim N_c^{1/2}$ and that multi-point functions containing hybrids are of order $N_c$, exactly analogous to ordinary mesons. The derivation proceeds by showing that inclusion of meson, hybrid, and glueball source terms does not alter the $N_c$ structure of QCD graphs, and then using spectral decomposition and consistency arguments to demonstrate that narrow exotic hybrid states must exist.

## Key numbers

- The width of exotic hybrid meson states is proportional to $1/N_c$ in the large $N_c$ limit.
- The vertex coupling $g_{n_h,n_m}$ for $n_h$ quantum number exotic hybrids and $n_m$ ordinary mesons scales as $N_c^{(1 - (n_h + n_m)/2)}$.
- For a hybrid decaying into two mesons ($n_h=1$, $n_m=2$), the amplitude is $N_c^{-1/2}$, implying a partial width of $N_c^{-1}$.
- The amplitude for a hybrid current to create a single-particle hybrid state is $\sim N_c^{1/2}$.
- Connected multi-point functions of $n_h$ hybrid currents, $n_m$ meson currents, and $n_g$ glueball currents (with at least one meson or hybrid field) scale as $\sim N_c$.
- The E852 Collaboration at Brookhaven reported a $J^{PC} = 1^{-+}$ resonance with mass 1370 MeV and width 385 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the standard large $N_c$ analysis of ordinary mesons, as reviewed by Witten (1979) and others, to quantum number exotic hybrid mesons. The author shows that contrary to folklore that exotic mesons do not exist in large $N_c$ QCD, the nonexistence proof applies only to $\bar{q} q \bar{q} q$ states and not to states with hybrid quantum numbers. This is the first argument based on a systematic expansion (as opposed to models) suggesting that narrow exotic states exist in the QCD spectrum, and it provides theoretical support for interpreting the $J^{PC} = 1^{-+}$ resonance reported by the E852 Collaboration as a genuine hybrid state.
