# Large Mass Diphotons From Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We evaluate the production of large mass diphotons from quark annihilation at BNL RHIC and CERN LHC energies from central collisions of gold nuclei. The collision is assumed to lead to either a thermally and chemically equilibrated quark gluon plasma, or a free-streaming quark gluon gas having an identical initial entropy, or a chemically equilibrating quark gluon system, with the same entropy at $T=T_c$. We also obtain an estimate of hard photon pairs from initial state quark annihilation and find that the thermal production dominates the yield up to $M \approx$ 4 GeV at RHIC, and up to 6 GeV at LHC. A simulation study of decay versus thermal diphotons is presented.

## Key claim

Thermal diphoton production from quark-antiquark annihilation in a quark-gluon plasma dominates over hard QCD prompt diphotons up to invariant masses of approximately 4 GeV at RHIC and 6 GeV at LHC, but this conclusion depends on using a temperature-dependent thermal quark mass to regularize the otherwise divergent cross section, rather than the fixed 5 MeV mass used in earlier work.

## Method

The authors calculated the invariant mass spectrum of large-mass diphotons from central Au+Au collisions at RHIC and LHC energies using three different evolution scenarios for the quark-gluon system: a thermally and chemically equilibrated plasma undergoing longitudinal hydrodynamic expansion, a free-streaming quark-gluon gas with identical initial entropy, and a chemically equilibrating system with the same entropy at T=Tc. They also computed the hard QCD diphoton yield from initial-state quark annihilation using the MRSD-prime parton distribution functions with a momentum transfer cutoff of 2 GeV, and performed a mixed-event simulation of decay versus thermal diphotons at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature for central Au+Au at RHIC: 478 MeV (using dN/dy = 1735 and tau0 = 1/(3T0))
- Initial temperature for central Au+Au at LHC: 860 MeV (using dN/dy = 5624 and tau0 = 1/(3T0))
- Thermal diphotons dominate over hard QCD diphotons up to M ≈ 4 GeV at RHIC and up to M ≈ 6 GeV at LHC
- Nuclear thickness function for central Au+Au: T_AB = 293.4 fm^{-2}
- Phase transition temperature T_c = 160 MeV; freeze-out temperature T_f = 100 MeV
- The ratio of diphotons to dileptons shows a minimum at M ≈ 0.8 GeV (from the rho peak in the pion form factor) and a maximum around M ≈ 1.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier diphoton studies (early works cited in the paper) by using a thermal quark mass m_th = sqrt((2 pi alpha_s)/3) T to regularize the annihilation cross section, which previously used a fixed 5 MeV quark mass that overestimated rates by a factor of 3-5. The paper provides the first estimate of hard QCD diphotons from colliding nuclei and presents a simulation study showing that thermal diphotons could be isolated from the decay photon background using mixed-event analysis (pioneered by the WA80 collaboration) with high-statistics data. The comparison of diphoton and dilepton yields is proposed as a way to confirm the temperature dependence of the thermal quark mass and reveal details of the plasma evolution dynamics.
