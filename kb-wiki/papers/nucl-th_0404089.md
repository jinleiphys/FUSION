# Hybrid stars with the color dielectric and the MIT bag models

> We study the hadron-quark phase transition in the interior of neutron stars (NS). For the hadronic sector, we use a microscopic equation of state (EOS) involving nucleons and hyperons derived within the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body theory, with realistic two-body and three-body forces. For the description of quark matter, we employ both the MIT bag model with a density dependent bag constant, and the color dielectric model. We calculate the structure of NS interiors with the EOS comprising both phases, and we find that the NS maximum masses are never larger than 1.7 solar masses, no matter the model chosen for describing the pure quark phase.

# Hybrid stars with the color dielectric and the MIT bag models

## Key claim

The maximum mass of neutron stars containing both hadronic and quark matter is never larger than 1.7 solar masses, regardless of whether the MIT bag model (with constant or density-dependent bag parameter) or the color dielectric model is used to describe the quark phase, provided the quark matter equation of state is constrained by heavy-ion collision phenomenology requiring no phase transition below about 2-3 times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors computed the hadronic equation of state using the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body theory with the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential and Urbana three-body forces, including nucleons and hyperons (Lambda and Sigma-minus) via the Nijmegen soft-core nucleon-hyperon potential. For the quark phase, they employed two models: the MIT bag model with both a constant bag parameter (B = 90 MeV/fm^3) and a Gaussian density-dependent bag parameter, and the color dielectric model with parameters fixed to reproduce nucleon properties. The hadron-quark phase transition was studied in beta-equilibrium, charge-neutral matter using the Glendenning construction for mixed phases.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass never exceeds 1.7 solar masses for any quark model studied
- Hyperon onset densities occur at approximately 2 to 3 times normal nuclear matter density (rho_0)
- Purely nucleonic equation of state including hyperons yields maximum masses below 1.3 solar masses
- MIT bag model with constant B = 90 MeV/fm^3 produces maximum masses around 1.6 solar masses
- Color dielectric model parameters: M = 1.7 GeV, g = 23 MeV, giving G = 198 MeV
- Strange quark mass set to 150 MeV in the MIT bag model calculations
- Renormalization point sigma_ren = 313 MeV used in the one-gluon-exchange term
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV and kaon decay constant f_K = 113 MeV used in the color dielectric model
- Effective bag parameters asymptotically reach values in the range 50-120 MeV fm^-3 at high density for both models

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by the same group (Burgio et al., 2002) that found hyperon inclusion alone produced maximum masses below the observational limit of 1.44 solar masses from the Taylor pulsar. The study directly compares two quark matter models (MIT bag and color dielectric) within the same hadronic framework, addressing the uncertainty in quark matter descriptions noted by Alford (2001) and Fahri and Jaffe (1984). The results enable future comparisons with observations of massive neutron stars above 2 solar masses, which would rule out the soft equations of state studied here, and motivate further work on three-body forces involving hyperons and refinements of the high-density equation of state.
