# Effects Of The Quantity $σ_{TS}$ On The Spin Structure Functions Of Nucleons In The Resonance Region

> In this paper, we investigate the effects of the quantity $σ_{TS}$ on the spin-structure functions of nucleons in the resonance region. The Schwinger sum rule for the spin structure function $g_2(x,Q^2)$ at the real photon limit is derived for the nucleon treated as a composite system, and it provides a crucial constraint on the longitudinal transition operator which has not been treated consistently in the literature. The longitudinal amplitude $S_{\frac 12}$ is evaluated in the quark model with the transition operator that generates the Schwinger sum rule. The numerical results of the quantity $σ_{TS}$ are presented for both spin structure functions $g_1(x,Q^2)$ and $g_2(x,Q^2)$ in the resonance region. Our results show that this quantity plays an important role in the low $Q^2$ region, which can be tested in the future experiments at CEBAF.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the quantity σ_TS, which couples transverse and longitudinal photon polarizations, has significant effects on the spin structure functions g₁(x,Q²) and g₂(x,Q²) in the low Q² resonance region, contrary to the deep inelastic scattering region where it vanishes. The authors derive the Schwinger sum rule for g₂ at the real photon limit within a composite quark model, providing a constraint on the longitudinal transition operator that had not been treated consistently in prior work.

## Method

The authors derived the sum rule for σ_TS in the real photon limit using a constituent quark model with an electromagnetic transition operator that separates center-of-mass from internal motion. They evaluated the longitudinal amplitude S₁/₂ for baryon resonances using a gauge-invariant longitudinal transition operator expanded to order O(v²/c²), and computed resonance contributions to the spin structure functions g₁ and g₂ by summing over all resonances below 2 GeV, including hybrid states for P₁₁(1440) and P₃₃(1600). The Q² dependence of the longitudinal amplitudes was evaluated following the procedure of Foster and Hughes.

## Key numbers

- The resonance contribution to ∫g₁(x,Q²=0)dx at the real photon limit is -0.121, in good agreement with the theoretical prediction -ω_th/(4M_T)κ² with κ_p=1.79 for the proton target.
- The resonance contribution to ∫g₂(x,Q²=0)dx at the real photon limit is 0.182, while the Schwinger sum rule (Eq. 37) gives 0.192 for the proton target.
- The addition of σ_TS shifts the crossing point where the g₁ sum rule is zero from 0.7 GeV² to approximately 0.5 GeV².
- The g₂ sum rule shows a sign change at Q² ≈ 1 GeV².
- The sum rule for σ_TS in the real photon limit is given by lim_{Q²→0} ∫_{ω_th}^{∞} σ_TS dω/√(Q²) = (4π²α)/(4M_T²) e_T κ.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of spin-dependent sum rules from the deep inelastic region into the resonance region, building on the Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule and the work of Soffer and Teryaev (1993) who suggested σ_TS plays a significant role at low Q². The authors provide the first quantitative quark-model calculation of the g₂ sum rule and the σ_TS contribution, which can be tested at CEBAF. The study clarifies inconsistencies in prior treatments of longitudinal transitions by Burkert (1993) and Warns et al. (1990), and establishes that the same transition operator generates both the DHG sum rule and the σ_TS sum rule.
