# Shell-model description of monopole shift in neutron-rich Cu

> Variations in the nuclear mean-field, in neutron-rich nuclei, are investigated within the framework of the nuclear shell model. The change is identified to originate mainly from the monopole part of the effective two-body proton-neutron interaction. Applications for the low-lying states in odd-$A$ Cu nuclei are presented. We compare the results using both schematic and realistic forces. We also compare the monopole shifts with the results obtained from large-scale shell-model calculations, using the same realistic interaction, in order to study two-body correlations beyond the proton mean-field variations.

## Key claim

The variation of the proton mean-field in neutron-rich Cu isotopes is driven primarily by the monopole component of the effective proton-neutron two-body interaction, but the agreement with experimental single-particle centroids remains only moderate due to scarce and unreliable spectroscopic data.

## Method

The authors computed effective single-proton energies for the $2p_{3/2}$, $1f_{5/2}$, $2p_{1/2}$, and $1g_{9/2}$ orbitals in odd-A Cu isotopes using the monopole Hamiltonian formalism, which adds the average proton-neutron interaction (weighted by neutron occupation probabilities from BCS pairing calculations) to bare single-particle energies. They compared results from a schematic zero-range delta interaction (with parameters $V_0 = 400$ MeV.fm$^3$ and $\alpha = 0.1$) against a realistic G-matrix interaction from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock with monopole corrections. Large-scale shell-model diagonalizations using the same realistic interaction in the $(1f_{5/2}2p_{3/2}2p_{1/2}1g_{9/2})$ model space outside a $^{56}$Ni core were performed with the Oxbash code to extract single-particle centroids from up to 400-800 excited states weighted by spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

- The zero-range delta interaction parameters were set to $V_0 = 400$ MeV.fm$^3$ and $\alpha = 0.1$, chosen to match systematics from heavier isotopes and give reasonable two-body matrix elements.
- The pairing strength was taken as $G = 23/A$ MeV, chosen to reproduce pairing gaps in neighboring even-even Ni isotopes.
- For $^{79}$Cu, the realistic interaction predicts an inversion of the $1f_{5/2}$ and $2p_{3/2}$ effective single-proton orbitals, while the delta interaction does not reproduce this crossing.
- The calculated spectroscopic factors for the lowest $3/2^-$ and $1/2^-$ states in $^{59-65}$Cu agree with experiment within 20%, while disagreement increases for $5/2^-$ and $9/2^+$ states.
- The sum rule for proton stripping to $T_f = T_i - 1/2$ states in $^{59}$Cu gives a total $C^2 S(2J+1)$ of 2.39 for the $3/2^-$ state compared to a shell-model value of 2.974, and 2.4 for the $9/2^+$ state compared to a shell-model value of 4.31.

## Context

This work extends the monopole-shift formalism previously applied to neutron-rich nuclei below A approximately 40 by Otsuka et al. (2001, 2002) to the proton mean-field in the Cu isotopic chain, and compares schematic and realistic forces for the first time in this mass region. The study was motivated by recent experimental data on neutron-rich Cu isotopes from the LISOL facility (Franchoo et al., 2001) and addresses the questioned magicity of $^{78}$Ni. The finding that monopole shifts from the simple analytical formula closely match full shell-model centroids enables future studies of other mass regions where large-scale diagonalization is computationally prohibitive, while the remaining discrepancies highlight the need for more precise one-nucleon transfer reaction data.
