# Strange Quark Stars as Probe of Dark Matter

> We demonstrate that the observation of old strange quark stars (SQSs) can set important limits on the scattering cross sections $σ_q$ between the light quarks and the non-interacting scalar dark matter (DM). By analyzing a set of 1403 of solitary pulsarlike compact stars in the Milky Way, we find the old solitary pulsar PSR J1801-0857D can set the most stringent upper limits on $σ_q$ or the DM-proton scattering cross sections $σ_p$. By converting $σ_q$ into $σ_p$ based on effective operator analyses, we show the resulting $σ_p$ limit by assuming PSR J1801-0857D to be a SQS could be comparable with that of the current direct detection experiments but much weaker (by several orders of magnitude) than that obtained by assuming PSR J1801-0857D to be a neutron star (NS), which requires an extremely small $σ_p$ far beyond the limits of direct detection experiments. Our findings imply that the old pulsars are favored to be SQSs rather than NSs if the scalar DM were observed by future terrestrial experiments.

## Key claim

The observation of old strange quark stars (SQSs) can set important limits on the scattering cross sections between light quarks and non-interacting scalar dark matter (DM), but the resulting limits on DM-proton scattering are comparable with current direct detection experiments only if the old pulsar PSR J1801-0857D is assumed to be a SQS; if it is assumed to be a neutron star (NS), the limits are far beyond any terrestrial experiment.

## Method

The authors calculated the capture rate of scalar DM by a compact star using a spherically symmetric accretion model, accounting for Pauli blocking and Fermi motion. They modeled SQSs using the MIT bag model with first-order perturbative QCD corrections and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain the star structure. They then scanned 1403 solitary pulsars from the ATNF Pulsar Catalogue to identify the one that maximizes the product of DM density and stellar age, PSR J1801-0857D, and derived upper limits on the DM-quark and DM-proton scattering cross sections by requiring that the accumulated DM does not collapse into a black hole that would destroy the star.

## Key numbers

- The SQS model with mass 1.4 solar masses yields a radius of 11.47 km.
- PSR J1801-0857D has a spin-down age of 9.71 Gyr and a distance of 3.06 kpc from the galactic center.
- The product w_chi(r) = DM density times age for PSR J1801-0857D is 16.0 GeV Gyr cm^{-3} for the NFW halo profile and 19.1 GeV Gyr cm^{-3} for the Einasto profile.
- The limits on the DM-up-quark scattering cross section sigma_u reach the order of 10^{-49} cm^2 for light DM.
- The conversion factor from sigma_q to sigma_p for SQSs is about 10^6 to 10^7, so the SQS limits on sigma_p are much weaker than the NS limits.
- The NS assumption yields limits on sigma_p that are beyond the sensitivity of the future XENON1T experiment up to DM mass of about 400 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the method of using old compact stars to constrain DM interactions, previously applied to neutron stars by Goldman and Nussinov (1989) and many subsequent authors (e.g., Bertone and Fairbairn 2008, Lavallaz and Fairbairn 2010, Kouvaris 2013, and Zheng and Chen 2015). It introduces SQSs as an alternative host star, showing that the resulting DM-proton cross section limits are much weaker than those from NSs and can be comparable with terrestrial direct detection experiments such as DAMA-Libra, CDMS-II, XENON100, and LUX. This enables a novel way to probe the nature of pulsars: if future experiments like XENON1T detect scalar DM, old pulsars would be favored to be SQSs rather than NSs.
