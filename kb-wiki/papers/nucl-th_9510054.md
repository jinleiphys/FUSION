# Equation of State of an Interacting Pion Gas with Realistic $π$--$π$ Interactions

> Within the finite-temperature Greens-function formalism we study the equation of state of a hot interacting pion gas at zero chemical potential. Employing realistic $ππ$ meson-exchange interactions we selfconsistently calculate the in-medium single-pion selfenergy and the $ππ$ scattering amplitude in the quasiparticle approximation. These quantities are then used to evaluate the thermodynamic potential, $Ω_π$(T), from which the state variables: pressure-, entropy- and energy-density can be derived. In contrast to earlier calculations based on the low-energy Weinberg Lagrangian we find an overall increase as compared to the free-gas results. We also consider the possibility of a dropping $ρ$-meson mass as suggested by the 'Brown-Rho Scaling' law.

## Key claim

Using a realistic meson-exchange model for ππ interactions (the Jülich model) within a selfconsistent finite-temperature Green's-function formalism, the authors find that the pressure, entropy, and energy densities of a hot interacting pion gas are increased relative to the free-gas results, in contrast to earlier calculations based on the low-energy Weinberg Lagrangian which found a decrease for temperatures above about 150 MeV.

## Method

The authors employed the finite-temperature Green's-function formalism to compute the thermodynamic potential Ωπ(T) of an interacting pion gas at zero chemical potential. They used the Jülich meson-exchange model for ππ interactions, which describes vacuum scattering data up to center-of-mass energies of 1.5 GeV, and solved the selfconsistency problem of Brueckner type by numerical iteration to obtain the in-medium single-pion selfenergy and the ππ scattering amplitude in the quasiparticle approximation. The thermodynamic potential was then used to derive pressure, entropy, and energy densities, with contributions from both quasiparticle and interaction (skeleton diagram) terms, including s-channel pole graphs summed to all orders and t-channel ρ-exchange contributions evaluated order by order.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration from lattice gauge calculations is cited as T_c^χ = 140-170 MeV.
- The ρ-meson mass used in the t-channel exchange propagator is m_ρ = 770 MeV.
- The form factor cutoff parameter for the ρ resonance in the Jülich model is Λ_ρ = 3.3 GeV.
- The third-order t-channel ρ exchange contribution to the thermodynamic potential is typically 10% of the second-order contribution.
- The f_0(980) resonance is simulated as a genuine resonance just below the K̅K threshold.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Welke et al. (1990) who used an empirical parametrization of vacuum ππ phase shifts within the relativistic virial expansion and found increased state variables from the ρ resonance, and by Bunatian and Kämpfer (1994) who employed the Weinberg Lagrangian in a Hartree approximation and found decreased state variables for T above about 150 MeV. The present paper uses a more realistic ππ interaction model and goes beyond the Hartree approximation by including selfconsistent in-medium modifications. The results are relevant for understanding the equation of state of hadronic matter in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly at RHIC where a dense pion gas is expected after hadronization, and for studying precursors of chiral symmetry restoration such as the dropping ρ-meson mass proposed by Brown and Rho (1991).
