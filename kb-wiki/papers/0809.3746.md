# Viscous Properties of Strongly Interacting Matter at High Temperature

> Substantial collective flow is observed in collisions between large nuclei at high energy, and the data are well-reproduced by perfect fluid dynamics. In a separate development, calculation of the dimensionless ratio of shear viscosity $η$ to entropy density within AdS/CFT yields $η/s = 1/4π$, which has been conjectured to be a lower bound for any physical system. It is shown that the transition from hadrons to quarks and gluons has behavior similar to helium, nitrogen, and water at and near their phase transitions in the ratio $η/s$. Conversely, there are indications that the ratio of bulk viscosity $ζ$ to entropy density may have a maximum in the vicinity of the phase transition. Therefore it is possible that experimental measurements can pinpoint the location of the transition or rapid crossover in QCD via the ratios $η/s$ and $ζ/s$ in addition to and independently of the equation of state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the dimensionless ratios of shear viscosity to entropy density (η/s) and bulk viscosity to entropy density (ζ/s) can serve as independent signatures to locate the QCD phase transition or rapid crossover, based on the observation that these ratios exhibit characteristic minima (η/s) and maxima (ζ/s) near phase transitions in ordinary atomic and molecular systems such as helium, nitrogen, and water.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical calculations of shear and bulk viscosities in both the hadronic phase (using chiral perturbation theory and explicit calculations with meson resonances) and the quark-gluon plasma phase (using perturbative QCD at leading order). These are compared with experimental data from atomic and molecular systems near their phase transitions, and with results from lattice gauge theory, AdS/CFT correspondence, and molecular dynamics simulations. The Kubo formulas from linear response theory provide the rigorous definition of the viscosities.

## Key numbers

- The AdS/CFT lower bound conjecture gives η/s = 1/4π (in units with ℏ = k_B = c = 1)
- For the hadronic phase in the chiral limit: η/s ≈ (15/16π) (f_π/T)^4, where f_π is the pion decay constant
- For three-flavor massless quark-gluon plasma at leading order: η/s = 5.12 / [g^4 ln(2.42/g)]
- For three-flavor massless quark-gluon plasma bulk viscosity: ζ/s = g^4 / [5000 ln(6.34/g)]
- Lattice results for pure SU(3) gauge theory: η/s ≈ 0.134 at T/T_c = 1.65 and η/s ≈ 0.102 at T/T_c = 1.24
- Lattice results for bulk viscosity in pure gauge theory: ζ/s ≈ 0.008 at T/T_c = 1.65, ζ/s ≈ 0.065 at T/T_c = 1.24, and ζ/s ≈ 0.73 at T/T_c = 1.02 (with N_τ = 8)
- Kharzeev et al. estimate for ζ/s above T_c: ζ/s < 0.01 for T/T_c > 1.4, ζ/s ≈ 0.025 at T/T_c = 1.2, ζ/s ≈ 0.07 at T/T_c = 1.1, and ζ/s ≈ 0.3 at T/T_c = 1.01

## Context

This work extends the observation by Kovtun, Son and Starinets (2005) that η/s has a universal lower bound of 1/4π in certain field theories dual to black branes, and connects it to experimental heavy-ion collision data from RHIC showing strong collective flow. The paper draws parallels between QCD matter and ordinary fluids studied by Enskog and others, showing that helium, nitrogen, and water all exhibit minima in η/s near their critical points. The analysis enables future experimental measurements at RHIC and LHC to potentially locate the QCD phase transition through viscosity measurements independently of equation-of-state studies, and opens connections to cosmology (bulk viscosity in the early universe) and condensed matter physics.
