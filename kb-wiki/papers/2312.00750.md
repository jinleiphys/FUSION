# Shape coexistence and superdeformation in $^{28}$Si

> We study the shape coexistence of differently deformed states within $^{28}$Si using shell-model and beyond-mean-field techniques. Experimentally, $^{28}$Si exhibits shape coexistence between an oblate ground state and an excited prolate structure. The oblate rotational band is described well within the $sd$ shell using the USDB interaction. However, for the prolate band, a modification of this interaction is required, lowering the single-particle energy of the $1d_{3/2}$ orbit. Furthermore, we explore the possibility of a superdeformed configuration in $^{28}$Si. Our calculations, spanning both the $sd$ and $pf$ shells, rule out the existence of a superdeformed $0^+$ bandhead within an excitation energy range of 10-20 MeV.

# Shape coexistence and superdeformation in $^{28}$Si

## Key claim
Shell-model calculations within the $sd$ shell using the USDB interaction reproduce the oblate ground-state band of $^{28}$Si, but a modified interaction (USDB-MOD) that lowers the $1d_{3/2}$ single-particle energy by approximately 1.5 MeV is required to describe the excited prolate band. Beyond-mean-field calculations spanning both the $sd$ and $pf$ shells rule out a superdeformed $0^+$ bandhead in the excitation energy range of 10–20 MeV, contradicting a prior theoretical prediction.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model diagonalizations in the $sd$ valence space (inert $^{16}$O core) using the USDB effective interaction, and compared results with exact diagonalizations using the ANTOINE code. For the prolate band, they introduced a modified interaction (USDB-MOD) that reduces the $1d_{3/2}$ single-particle energy gap by about 1.5 MeV. To search for superdeformation, they employed the generator-coordinate method (GCM) with quadrupole-constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wavefunctions projected onto good particle number and angular momentum, using the SDPF-NR interaction that includes both the $sd$ and $pf$ shells.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state oblate deformation: $\beta \simeq 0.25$, consistent with experimental data.
- Prolate band bandhead excitation energy: approximately 6.5 MeV (experimental).
- Energy gap between $1d_{5/2}$ and $1d_{3/2}$ orbits in USDB: approximately 5 MeV; reduced by approximately 1.5 MeV in USDB-MOD.
- Superdeformed bandhead predicted by prior AMD study: approximately 14 MeV; not found in the present calculations within 10–20 MeV excitation energy.
- Superdeformed deformation parameter: $\beta \approx 0.6$ (from prior AMD study); no states with $\beta \ge 0.5$ found in the 10–20 MeV range.

## Context
This work extends shell-model studies of shape coexistence in $^{28}$Si, which experimentally shows an oblate ground-state band and an excited prolate band with bandhead at approximately 6.5 MeV. The authors build on the USDB interaction of Brown and Richter and use the Taurus suite for beyond-mean-field calculations. The search for superdeformation was motivated by a prior Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) study that predicted a superdeformed structure at approximately 14 MeV, but recent experimental attempts have not confirmed this. The present calculations disfavor such a superdeformed band, clarifying the limits of shape coexistence in this nucleus.
