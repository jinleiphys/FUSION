# Chiral dynamics of $Σ$-hyperons in the nuclear medium

> Using SU(3) chiral perturbation theory we calculate the density-dependent complex mean field $U_Σ(k_f)+ i W_Σ(k_f)$ of a $Σ$-hyperon in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. The leading long-range $ΣN $-interaction arises from one-kaon exchange and from two-pion exchange with a $Σ$- or a $Λ$-hyperon in the intermediate state. We find from the $ΣN\to ΛN$ conversion process at nuclear matter saturation density $ρ_0 = 0.16 $fm$^{-3}$ an imaginary single-particle potential of $W_Σ(k_{f0}) =-21.5$ MeV, in fair agreement with existing empirical determinations. The genuine long-range contributions from iterated (second order) one-pion exchange with an intermediate $Σ$- or $Λ$-hyperon sum up to a moderately repulsive real single-particle potential of $U_Σ(k_{f0})= 59$ MeV. Recently measured $(π^-,K^+$) inclusive spectra related to $Σ^-$-formation in heavy nuclei give evidence for a $Σ$-nucleus repulsion of similar size. Our results suggest that the net effect of the short-range $ΣN$-interaction on the $Σ$-nuclear mean field could be small.

## Key claim

Using SU(3) chiral perturbation theory, the paper calculates that the long-range two-pion exchange dynamics alone can produce a moderately repulsive real single-particle potential of 59 MeV and an imaginary potential of -21.5 MeV for a Σ-hyperon in nuclear matter at saturation density, suggesting that the net effect of the short-range ΣN-interaction on the Σ-nuclear mean field could be small.

## Method

The author computes the density-dependent complex self-energy of a zero-momentum Σ-hyperon placed as a test particle into isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, using the leading-order chiral meson-baryon Lagrangian in flavor SU(3). The calculation evaluates one-kaon exchange Fock diagrams and iterated (second-order) one-pion exchange Hartree diagrams with Σ- or Λ-hyperons in the intermediate state, including Pauli blocking corrections, at the two-loop level of in-medium chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- Imaginary single-particle potential at nuclear matter saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³: W_Σ(k_f0) = -21.5 MeV
- Real single-particle potential at nuclear matter saturation density: U_Σ(k_f0) = 59 MeV
- Kaon-exchange contribution to the real potential is negligible due to small KNΣ-coupling constant g_KNΣ = 4.4
- Threshold density for Pauli blocking in the imaginary part: ρ_th = 0.072 fm⁻³ = 0.45 ρ₀
- ΣΛ-mass splitting: M_Σ - M_Λ = 77.5 MeV
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant: g_πN = 13.2
- Weak pion decay constant: f_π = 92.4 MeV
- SU(3) axial-vector coupling constants: D = 0.84, F = 0.46

## Context

This work extends chiral effective field theory approaches previously applied to nuclear matter binding and saturation (Nucmat) and Λ-hyperon single-particle properties (Lambdapot) to the case of Σ-hyperons. The results are compared with empirical determinations from Σ⁻-atom data reanalyzed by Batty, Friedman and Gal (1997) and from inclusive (π⁻,K⁺) spectra on Si, Ni, In and Bi targets measured at KEK (Noumi, Saha). The calculation also contrasts with G-matrix calculations using the SU(6) quark model by Kohno et al. and with one-boson exchange models (Rijken, Schulze, Yamamoto) which generally give an attractive Σ-nucleus potential, unlike the repulsion found here. The results enable future combined QCD sum rule analyses of nucleons, Λ-hyperons and Σ-hyperons in nuclear matter to better constrain short-distance baryon-nucleon dynamics.
