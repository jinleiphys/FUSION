# An Effective Theory for the Four-Body System

> We consider the non-relativistic four-body system with large scattering length and short-range interactions within an effective theory with contact interactions only. We compute the binding energies of the 4He tetramer and of alpha-particle. The well-known linear correlation between the three-body binding energies and the four-body binding energies of these physical systems can be understood as a consequence of the absence of a four-body force at leading order.

## Key claim

The well-known linear correlation (Tjon line) between three-body and four-body binding energies in both the 4He tetramer and the alpha-particle can be understood as a consequence of the absence of a four-body force at leading order in an effective theory with short-range interactions and large scattering length.

## Method

The author used the Yakubovsky equations to compute binding energies of the four-boson system within a non-relativistic effective field theory with contact interactions only. The two-body coupling constant was matched to the scattering length or two-body binding energy, and the three-body coupling constant was fixed by demanding that the shallowest three-body bound state energy remain constant as the cutoff was varied. For the four-nucleon system, spin and isospin degrees of freedom were included, and the triplet scattering length, deuteron binding energy, and triton binding energy were used as input.

## Key numbers

- 4He tetramer ground state binding energy: 492 mK (leading order effective theory result)
- 4He tetramer first excited state binding energy: 128 mK (leading order effective theory result)
- 4He trimer ground state binding energy: 127 mK (used as input to fix the three-body coupling constant)
- 4He trimer first excited state binding energy: 2.186 mK (used as input to fix the three-body coupling constant, value from Blume and Greene (2000))
- Alpha-particle binding energy: 26.9 MeV (using triplet scattering length, deuteron binding energy, and triton binding energy as input)

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach for few-body systems with large scattering length from the three-body sector to the four-body sector. It explains the Tjon line correlation previously observed in calculations using phenomenological potentials such as LM2M2, TTY, HFD-B, and HFDHE2 for 4He (Lew97, Naka83) and in various nucleon-nucleon potential calculations (Nogga 2000) as a universal consequence of large scattering lengths. The results enable systematic inclusion of effective range corrections and extension to scattering observables in four-body systems.
