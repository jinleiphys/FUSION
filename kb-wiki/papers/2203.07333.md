# Nuclear effects in the deuteron and global QCD analyses

> We report the results of a new global QCD analysis, which includes deep-inelastic $e/μ$ scattering data off proton and deuterium, as well as Drell-Yan lepton pair production in proton-proton and proton-deuterium collisions and $W^\pm/Z$ boson production data from $pp$ and $p \bar p$ collisions at the LHC and Tevatron. Nuclear effects in the deuteron are treated in terms of a nuclear convolution approach with bound off-shell nucleons within a weak binding approximation. The off-shell correction is controlled by a universal function of the Bjorken variable $x$ describing the modification of parton distributions in bound nucleons, which is determined in our analysis along with the parton distribution functions of the proton. A number of systematic studies are performed to estimate the uncertainties arising from the use of various deuterium datasets, from the modeling of higher twist contributions to the structure functions, from the treatment of target mass corrections, as well as from the nuclear corrections in the deuteron. We obtain predictions for the ratios $F_2^n/F_2^p$, and $d/u$, focusing on the region of large $x$. We also compare our results with the ones obtained by other QCD analyses, as well as with the recent data from the MARATHON experiment.

## Key claim

The authors perform a new global QCD analysis that simultaneously determines the proton parton distribution functions (PDFs) and a universal off-shell function describing the modification of parton distributions in bound nucleons within the deuteron, finding that the extracted off-shell function agrees with previous determinations from heavier nuclei but strongly disagrees with the CJ15 analysis, with the discrepancy traced to differences in experimental datasets and underlying model assumptions.

## Method

The analysis uses deep-inelastic scattering data off proton and deuterium targets, Drell-Yan lepton pair production in proton-proton and proton-deuterium collisions, and W/Z boson production from pp and p-pbar collisions at the LHC and Tevatron. Nuclear effects in the deuteron are treated using a nuclear convolution approach with bound off-shell nucleons within a weak binding approximation, where the off-shell correction is controlled by a universal function delta f(x) parametrized as a second-order polynomial. The fit simultaneously determines the proton PDFs, higher-twist contributions, and the off-shell function at NNLO in perturbative QCD, using the deuteron AV18 wave function and imposing a cut of Q2 greater than 2.5 GeV2 and W2 greater than 3 GeV2.

## Key numbers

* Off-shell function parameters: c0 = -0.16 +/- 0.11, c1 = -2.04 +/- 0.73, c2 = 4.86 +/- 1.13
* Total chi-squared per degree of freedom for all data points: 4842/4044
* MARATHON data normalization uncertainty: 0.55 percent
* SLAC-E140 normalization uncertainty: 1.7 percent
* Total chi-squared per number of data points for deuterium datasets: 1166/1083

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Alekhin 2017) by including updated deuterium datasets, particularly the MARATHON experiment data from JLab, and replaces older NMC data with more recent measurements. The results agree with the off-shell function determinations from Kulagin (2004) based on nuclear DIS data for targets with A between 4 and 208, but strongly disagree with the CJ15 analysis (Accardi 2016). The study enables improved constraints on the d/u quark ratio and the F2n/F2p ratio at large x, and provides a framework for comparing with other global QCD analyses including CT18, MSHT20, and NNPDF4.0.
