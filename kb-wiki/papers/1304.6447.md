# Dilatons in Dense Baryonic Matter

> We discuss the role of dilaton, which is supposed to be representing a special feature of scale symmetry of QCD, trace anomaly, in dense baryonic matter. The idea that the scale symmetry breaking of QCD is responsible for the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry is presented along the similar spirit of Freund-Nambu model. The incorporation of dilaton field in the hidden local symmetric parity doublet model is briefly sketched with the possible role of dilaton at high density baryonic matter, the emergence of linear sigma model in dilaton limit.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the explicit breaking of scale symmetry due to the QCD trace anomaly triggers the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry, and that this connection can be modeled by introducing a dilaton field into effective Lagrangians. The authors hedge that this mechanism applies only if the scale symmetry breaking from the "soft" part of the anomaly is treated as small enough that an approximate scale symmetry exists at low energy.

## Method

The authors construct a phenomenological Lagrangian (dHLS) by introducing a dilaton field as a conformal compensator into the hidden local symmetric (HLS) Lagrangian for pions, vector mesons, and parity-doubled baryons. They then analyze the "dilaton limit," defined by the condition that the trace of the chiral field \(\Sigma_s\Sigma_s^\dagger\) goes to zero, and derive constraints on coupling constants from requiring the absence of singular terms in the Lagrangian. The analysis uses large \(N_c\) sum-rule arguments and renormalization group equations to infer the limiting values of couplings.

## Key numbers

- The dilaton limit condition \(\alpha \equiv \frac{F_\pi^2}{F_\chi^2} = 1\) is required for the linear sigma model to emerge from the field reparameterization \(\Sigma_s = U\chi\frac{F_\pi}{F_\chi}\).
- In the dilaton limit, the vector coupling constant satisfies \(g \rightarrow 1\).
- The condition \(g_{V\rho} - g_A \rightarrow 0\) is derived from requiring the singular part of the Lagrangian to vanish.
- Using large \(N_c\) sum-rule arguments, the authors infer \(g_A - 1 \rightarrow 0\).
- The \(\rho\) meson-nucleon coupling \(g_{\rho NN} = g_\rho(g_{V\rho} - 1) \rightarrow 0\) in the dilaton limit, meaning the \(\rho\) meson decouples from the nucleon.
- The \(\omega\)-nucleon coupling remains non-vanishing, with the nucleon part of the Lagrangian containing a term \((1 - g_{V\omega}) g_\omega \bar{\mathcal{N}} \frac{\sbar{\omega}}{2} \mathcal{N}\).

## Context

This work extends the Freund-Nambu model (Freund and Nambu, 1968) which first demonstrated spontaneous scale symmetry breaking with a dilaton field, and builds on the Brown-Rho (BR) scaling idea (Brown and Rho, 1991) where Lagrangian parameters scale with the dilaton vacuum expectation value. The paper also connects to earlier work by Lee and Rho (2009) on the link between scale and chiral symmetry, and to studies of a half-skyrmion phase in dense matter (Lee, Park, and Rho, 2011; Dong et al., 2012). The dilaton limit analysis enables predictions for the behavior of nuclear matter at high density, specifically that the \(\rho\) meson-nucleon coupling vanishes at a critical density before chiral restoration, which can be tested in future dense baryonic matter studies.
