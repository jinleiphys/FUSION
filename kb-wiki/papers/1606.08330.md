# Hidden-charm pentaquarks as a meson-baryon molecule with coupled channels for $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}Λ_{\rm c}$ and $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}Σ^{(\ast)}_{\rm c}$

> The recent observation of two hidden-charm pentaquark states by LHCb collaborations prompted us to investigate the exotic states close to the $\bar{D}Λ_{\rm c}$, $\bar{D}^{\ast}Λ_{\rm c}$, $\bar{D}Σ_{\rm c}$, $\bar{D}Σ^{\ast}_{\rm c}$, $\bar{D}^{\ast}Σ_{\rm c}$ and $\bar{D}^{\ast}Σ^{\ast}_{\rm c}$ thresholds. We therefore studied the hadronic molecules that form the coupled-channel system of $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}Λ_{\rm c}$ and $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}Σ^{(\ast)}_{\rm c}$. As the heavy quark spin symmetry manifests the mass degenerations of $\bar{D}$ and $\bar{D}^\ast$ mesons, and of $Σ_{\rm c}$ and $Σ^\ast_{\rm c}$ baryons, the coupled channels of $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}Σ^{(\ast)}_{\rm c}$ are important in these molecules. In addition, we consider the coupling to the $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}Λ_{\rm c}$ channel whose thresholds are near the $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}Σ^{(\ast)}_{\rm c}$ thresholds, and the coupling to the state with nonzero orbital angular momentum mixed by the tensor force. This full coupled channel analysis of $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}Λ_{\rm c}-\bar{D}^{(\ast)}Σ^{(\ast)}_{\rm c}$ with larger orbital angular momentum has never been performed before. By solving the coupled-channel Schrödinger equations with the one meson exchange potentials that respected to the heavy quark spin and chiral symmetries, we studied the hidden-charm hadronic molecules with $I(J^P)=1/2(3/2^\pm)$ and $1/2(5/2^\pm)$. We conclude that the $J^P$ assignment of the observed pentaquarks is $3/2^+$ for $P^+_{\rm c}(4380)$ and $5/2^-$ for $P^+_{\rm c}(4450)$, which is agreement with the results of the LHCb analysis. In addition, we give predictions for other $J^P=3/2^\pm$ states at 4136.0, 4307.9 and 4348.7 MeV in $J^P=3/2^-$, and 4206.7 MeV in $J^P=3/2^+$, which can be further investigated by means of experiment.

## Key claim

The authors conclude that the LHCb pentaquarks $P^+_{\rm c}(4380)$ and $P^+_{\rm c}(4450)$ can be described as hadronic molecules in a full coupled-channel analysis of $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}\Lambda_{\rm c}-\bar{D}^{(\ast)}\Sigma^{(\ast)}_{\rm c}$, but this assignment depends on fixing the cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ to 1400 MeV to reproduce the mass of $P^+_{\rm c}(4450)$; the $J^P$ assignment is $3/2^+$ for $P^+_{\rm c}(4380)$ and $5/2^-$ for $P^+_{\rm c}(4450)$.

## Method

The authors solved coupled-channel Schrodinger equations for the $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}\Lambda_{\rm c}-\bar{D}^{(\ast)}\Sigma^{(\ast)}_{\rm c}$ system with $I(J^P)=1/2(3/2^\pm)$ and $1/2(5/2^\pm)$, using one meson exchange potentials derived from effective Lagrangians respecting heavy quark spin and chiral symmetries. The calculation included channels with nonzero orbital angular momentum (D-wave, G-wave for negative parity; F-wave, H-wave for positive parity) mixed by the tensor force, and employed the Gaussian expansion method with complex scaling to obtain resonance energies and widths.

## Key numbers

* The cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ was fixed to 1400 MeV to reproduce the mass of $P^+_{\rm c}(4450)$ at 4428.6 MeV (resonance energy) in $J^P=5/2^-$, compared to the experimental mass of $4449.8 \pm 1.7 \pm 2.5$ MeV.
* The state corresponding to $P^+_{\rm c}(4380)$ was found at 4339.7 MeV in $J^P=3/2^+$ with $\Lambda=1400$ MeV, compared to the experimental mass of $4380 \pm 8 \pm 29$ MeV.
* Predicted $J^P=3/2^-$ states at $\Lambda=1400$ MeV: 4136.0 MeV (bound), 4307.9 MeV (resonance with width 37.6 MeV), and 4348.7 MeV (resonance with width 42.2 MeV).
* Predicted $J^P=3/2^+$ state at $\Lambda=1400$ MeV: 4206.7 MeV (resonance with width 82.4 MeV).
* The $\bar{D}\Lambda_{\rm c}$ threshold is at 4153.5 MeV; the $\bar{D}^{\ast}\Lambda_{\rm c}$ threshold is at 4295.5 MeV; the $\bar{D}\Sigma_{\rm c}$ threshold is at 4320.5 MeV; the $\bar{D}\Sigma^{\ast}_{\rm c}$ threshold is at 4385.1 MeV; the $\bar{D}^{\ast}\Sigma_{\rm c}$ threshold is at 4462.5 MeV; the $\bar{D}^{\ast}\Sigma^{\ast}_{\rm c}$ threshold is at 4527.1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hidden-charm molecular pentaquarks by Hofmann (2005), PhysRevLett.105.232001, PhysRevC.84.015202, and Xiao (2013), which considered only S-wave channels and did not include the $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}\Lambda_{\rm c}$ coupling. The full coupled-channel analysis including $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}\Lambda_{\rm c}$ and higher orbital angular momenta had not been performed before. The results enable experimental searches for the predicted $J^P=3/2^\pm$ states at 4136.0, 4206.7, 4307.9, and 4348.7 MeV in future experiments at LHC, J-PARC, and Jefferson Lab.
