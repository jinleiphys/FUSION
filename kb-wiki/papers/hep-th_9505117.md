# Anyons as spinning particles

> A model-independent formulation of anyons as spinning particles is presented. The general properties of the classical theory of (2+1)-dimensional relativistic fractional spin particles and some properties of their quantum theory are investigated. The relationship between all the known approaches to anyons as spinning particles is established. Some widespread misleading notions on the general properties of (2+1)-dimensional anyons are removed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that widespread claims about (2+1)-dimensional anyons, specifically that the spin contribution to the angular momentum must be parallel to the momentum and must satisfy the Lorentz algebra at the level of Poisson brackets, are correct only in special cases and are misleading in general; the authors demonstrate that these properties are not fundamental and that a free relativistic spinning particle can have a nonzero second time derivative of its coordinates.

## Method

The authors analyze the classical canonical theory of (2+1)-dimensional relativistic particles with fixed mass and arbitrary spin, starting from the Poincaré algebra and the Jacobi identities for phase-space variables. They distinguish two formulations: a minimal approach, which uses only coordinate and momentum variables and fixes spin in a strong sense, and an extended approach, which introduces auxiliary internal phase-space variables and fixes spin via a constraint. They then derive the general forms of the coordinate brackets and the angular momentum vector, examine the Lorentz properties of the coordinates, and relate the two formulations through gauge-invariant extensions.

## Key numbers

* Spin \(s\) can take any real value, \(s \in \mathbb{R}\), for (2+1)-dimensional massive particles.
* The mass-shell constraint is \(p^2 + m^2 \approx 0\) with \(m > 0\).
* The covariant coordinates satisfy \(\{x^c_\mu, x^c_\nu\} = -s \epsilon_{\mu\nu\lambda} p^\lambda / (-p^2)^{3/2}\).
* The localizable coordinates satisfy \(\{x^l_\mu, x^l_\nu\} = 0\) and the spin addition in that case is \(J^l_0 = -s p_0 / |p^0|\), \(J^l_i = -s p_i / (\sqrt{-p^2} + |p^0|)\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier model of a relativistic particle with fractional spin by Plyushchay (1991) and the approach using infinite-dimensional representations of the universal covering group of SO(2,1) by Cortés and Plyushchay (1994). It corrects misleading statements from papers by Chou (1991) and Dutra (1995), which claimed that the spin contribution must be parallel to momentum and that the second time derivative of coordinates must vanish for a free anyon. The analysis establishes the relationship between all known classical approaches to anyons as spinning particles and provides a foundation for quantizing such systems without losing manifest covariance.
