# Nuclear symmetry potential in the relativistic impulse approximation

> Using the relativistic impulse approximation with the Love-Franey \textsl{NN} scattering amplitude developed by Murdock and Horowitz, we investigate the low-energy (100 MeV$\leq E_{\mathrm{kin}}\leq 400$ MeV) behavior of the nucleon Dirac optical potential, the Schrödinger-equivalent potential, and the nuclear symmetry potential in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. We find that the nuclear symmetry potential at fixed baryon density decreases with increasing nucleon energy. In particular, the nuclear symmetry potential at saturation density changes from positive to negative values at nucleon kinetic energy of about 200 MeV. Furthermore,the obtained energy and density dependence of the nuclear symmetry potential is consistent with those of the isospin- and momentum-dependent MDI interaction with $x=0$, which has been found to describe reasonably both the isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion collisions and the empirical neutron-skin thickness of $^{208} $Pb.

## Key claim

Using the generalized relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) of Murdock and Horowitz, the nuclear symmetry potential at fixed baryon density decreases with increasing nucleon energy, and at saturation density it changes from positive to negative values at a nucleon kinetic energy of about 200 MeV. The obtained energy and density dependence is consistent with the isospin- and momentum-dependent MDI interaction with \(x=0\), which has been found to describe both isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion collisions and the empirical neutron-skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) with the Love-Franey NN scattering amplitude as developed by Murdock and Horowitz, which includes explicit exchange contributions, pseudovector coupling for the pion, and medium modification from Pauli blocking. The nuclear scalar and vector densities were obtained from the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory using the HA parameter set. The Dirac optical potential was then used to derive the Schrödinger-equivalent potential and the nuclear symmetry potential (Lane potential) for nucleons in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter at kinetic energies between 100 MeV and 400 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear symmetry potential at saturation density (\(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)) changes from positive to negative values at nucleon kinetic energy of about 200 MeV.
- The Schrödinger-equivalent potential in symmetric nuclear matter at normal density varies from \(-27\) MeV at \(E_{\mathrm{kin}} = 100\) MeV to 0 MeV at \(E_{\mathrm{kin}} \approx 200\) MeV.
- The Pauli blocking correction uses a density dependence of \(\rho_B^{2/3}\) with \(\rho_0 = 0.1934\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The study covers baryon densities up to \(\rho_B \lesssim 0.25\) fm\(^{-3}\) and nucleon kinetic energies from 100 MeV to 400 MeV.
- The MDI interaction parameters are set with symmetry energy of 31.6 MeV at \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) and incompressibility \(K_0 = 211\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous high-energy study using the original RIA of McNeil, Ray, and Wallace (MRW) (Chen et al., 2005) to lower energies (100–400 MeV) by employing the generalized RIA of Murdock and Horowitz, which corrects shortcomings of the MRW approach at low energies. The results are compared with the phenomenological MDI interaction (Das et al., 2003; Chen et al., 2005), and the agreement with the \(x=0\) parameterization provides a consistency check for that interaction, which has been used to describe isospin diffusion data and the neutron-skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb. The findings enable future studies using Lorentz-covariant transport models that include isospin degrees of freedom.
