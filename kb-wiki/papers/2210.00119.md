# Mass decomposition of the pion in the 't Hooft model

> We obtain the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) in the 't Hooft model of two-dimensional quantum chromodynamics. The EMT is decomposed into contributions from quark and gluon fields, with all of the (plus component of) the light front momentum being carried by the quark field. The energy is split between quark and gluon fields, with the gluon field carrying the self-energy of the dressed quarks. We consider the pion in the limit of small but non-zero quark masses -- which has previously withstood numerical treatment -- as as a concrete example. We solve for the pion wave function using a variational method and obtain numerical results for its energy breakdown into quark and gluon contributions.

# Mass decomposition of the pion in the 't Hooft model

## Key claim
In the 't Hooft model of two-dimensional QCD, the pion's light-front energy can be decomposed into quark and gluon contributions that are nearly equal (approximately 49% quark, 51% gluon) in the chiral limit of small but non-zero quark masses, a regime that had previously resisted numerical treatment. The gluon field carries the self-energy of the dressed quarks, and the decomposition is gauge-invariant and free of the renormalization scheme ambiguities that complicate analogous decompositions in (3+1)-dimensional QCD.

## Method
The authors work in the 't Hooft model (QCD in (1+1) dimensions with N_c → ∞ and g²N_c fixed) using light-cone gauge (A⁺ = 0). They derive the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) and decompose it into gauge-invariant quark and gluon contributions. For the pion, they solve the 't Hooft equation using a variational method with the trial wave function φ(x) = x^β (1-x)^β √[Γ(4β+2)]/Γ(2β+1), minimizing the expectation value of μ²(β) to determine β for various quark masses. The energy breakdown is obtained by evaluating the separate quark and gluon contributions to the Hamiltonian expectation value.

## Key numbers
- In the chiral limit (m → 0), the energy fractions approach P_g⁻/P⁻ → 0.51 and P_q⁻/P⁻ → 0.49.
- For empirical parameters (m_π = 140 MeV, m = 3.4 MeV), the quark energy fraction is 0.501 and the gluon energy fraction is 0.499.
- For these empirical parameters, the form factors are c̄_q(0) = -0.294 and c̄_g(0) = +0.294.
- The variational parameter β ≈ √(3/π N_c) (m/g) for small quark masses, holding nearly exactly for m up to about 0.1 in units of √(N_c g²).
- The pion mass follows a GMOR-like relation: m_π² ≈ 2g √(π N_c/3) m + 4m².
- For the empirical fit, β = 0.001183, γ = 4.6×10⁻⁶, μ² = 0.007797, and g²N_c = 7.897×10⁶ MeV².

## Context
This work extends the 't Hooft model (t Hooft, 1974) to the small but non-zero quark mass regime, which had previously presented numerical challenges (Phys. Rev. D 19, 3024). It builds on recent interest in (1+1)-dimensional QCD (De Teramond, 2021; Li, 2021; Ahmady, 2021) and the need to include mass effects in light-front holographic QCD (Brodsky, 2014; Chabysheva, 2012). The results enable comparison with (3+1)-dimensional QCD mass decomposition studies (Ji, 1994; Metz, 2020; Lorce, 2021), where the analogous c̄_q(0) = -0.103 (Hatta, 2018) differs in magnitude but shares the same negative sign. The work also provides a UV-finite testing ground for mass decomposition controversies in (3+1)-dimensional QCD (Lorce, 2021).
