# R-mode oscillations and rocket effect in rotating superfluid neutron stars. I. Formalism

> We derive the hydrodynamical equations of r-mode oscillations in neutron stars in presence of a novel damping mechanism related to particle number changing processes. The change in the number densities of the various species leads to new dissipative terms in the equations which are responsible of the {\it rocket effect}. We employ a two-fluid model, with one fluid consisting of the charged components, while the second fluid consists of superfluid neutrons. We consider two different kind of r-mode oscillations, one associated with comoving displacements, and the second one associated with countermoving, out of phase, displacements.

## Key claim

The paper derives the hydrodynamical equations for r-mode oscillations in superfluid neutron stars including a previously neglected dissipative mechanism, the "rocket effect", arising from particle number changing processes (beta decays and crust-core transfusion), but restricts the analysis to a simplified model with uniform density, incompressible matter, and no crust, deferring numerical evaluation of damping timescales to a companion paper.

## Method

The authors employ a two-fluid model separating superfluid neutrons from a combined charged fluid (protons and electrons locked by electromagnetism), and derive linearized Euler and continuity equations for perturbations around a uniformly rotating equilibrium. They perform an expansion in the rotation frequency \(\Omega\) relative to the Kepler frequency \(\Omega_K\), and analyze two decoupled r-mode types: a "standard r-mode" dominated by comoving toroidal displacements and a "superfluid r-mode" dominated by countermoving toroidal displacements, both for the \(l=m=2\) case assuming uniform density and the Cowling approximation (neglecting gravitational potential perturbations).

## Key numbers

- Star modeled with uniform density \(\rho = 2.5 \rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0\) is nuclear saturation density) and radius \(R = 10\) km, giving mass \(M \simeq 1.47 M_\odot\).
- Standard r-mode frequency (corotating frame): \(\omega_r = 2 m \Omega / [l(l+1)]\) for \(l=m=2\).
- Superfluid r-mode frequency (corotating frame): \(\omega_r = [2 m \Omega / (l(l+1))] (1+\bar \epsilon)\), where \(\bar \epsilon\) is the entrainment parameter.
- Mutual friction coefficient for weak drag: \(B = 4 \times 10^{-4} \, \epsilon_c^2 / \sqrt{1-\epsilon_c} \, (x_c/0.05)^{7/6} (\rho/10^{14} \text{ g/cm}^3)^{1/6}\), with \(B' \simeq B^2\).
- Mutual friction damping timescale is of order \(10^4\) s, compared to gravitational-wave emission timescale of order a few seconds for a millisecond pulsar.
- Instability window for standard neutron stars: temperature range approximately \(10^6\) K to \(10^{10}\) K, with shear viscosity damping below \(10^5\) K and bulk viscosity damping above \(10^{10}\)–\(10^{11}\) K.

## Context

This work extends the r-mode analysis of superfluid neutron stars previously developed by Haskell et al. (2009) and Andersson et al. (2008), which included mutual friction but neglected particle number-changing processes. It also builds on the two-fluid hydrodynamical framework of Prix (2002) and Andersson (2006). The rocket effect introduces new dissipative terms that may provide damping timescales comparable to gravitational-wave emission, potentially reducing the "instability window" for r-modes; numerical evaluation of these timescales is performed in the companion paper (Colucci, Mannarelli, Manuel, 2010).
