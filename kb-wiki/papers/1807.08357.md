# The $πγ\to ππ$ transition and the $ρ$ radiative decay width from lattice QCD

> We report a lattice QCD determination of the $πγ\to ππ$ transition amplitude for the $P$-wave, $I=1$ two-pion final state, as a function of the photon virtuality and $ππ$ invariant mass. The calculation was performed with $2+1$ flavors of clover fermions at a pion mass of approximately $320$ MeV, on a $32^3 \times 96$ lattice with $L\approx 3.6$ fm. We construct the necessary correlation functions using a combination of smeared forward, sequential and stochastic propagators, and determine the finite-volume matrix elements for all $ππ$ momenta up to $|\vec{P}|= \sqrt{3} \frac{2π}{L}$ and all associated irreducible representations. In the mapping of the finite-volume to infinite-volume matrix elements using the Lellouch-Lüscher factor, we consider two different parametrizations of the $ππ$ scattering phase shift. We fit the $q^2$ and $s$ dependence of the infinite-volume transition amplitude in a model-independent way using series expansions, and compare multiple different truncations of this series. Through analytic continuation to the $ρ$ resonance pole, we also determine the $πγ\to ρ$ resonant transition form factor and the $ρ$ meson photocoupling, and obtain $|G_{ρπγ}| = 0.0802(32)(20)$.

## Key claim

The paper reports a lattice QCD determination of the πγ → ππ transition amplitude for the P-wave, I=1 two-pion final state, but the calculation is performed at a single unphysical pion mass of approximately 320 MeV rather than the physical pion mass, and the results depend on the specific parametrization chosen for the ππ scattering phase shift.

## Method

The calculation used 2+1 flavors of clover-improved Wilson fermions on a 32³ × 96 lattice with L ≈ 3.6 fm, constructing correlation functions from a combination of smeared forward, sequential, and stochastic propagators. Finite-volume matrix elements were extracted for all ππ momenta up to |P⃗| = √3 (2π/L) and all associated irreducible representations, then mapped to infinite-volume matrix elements using the Lellouch-Lüscher factor with two different Breit-Wigner parametrizations (BWI and BWII) of the ππ scattering phase shift. The q² and s dependence of the infinite-volume transition amplitude was fit using model-independent series expansions with multiple truncations, and analytic continuation to the ρ resonance pole yielded the resonant transition form factor and photocoupling.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: mπ ≈ 320 MeV (amπ = 0.18295(36) in lattice units)
- Lattice size: 32³ × 96, with spatial extent L ≈ 3.649(25) fm
- Number of gauge configurations: 1041
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.11403(77) fm
- mπL = 5.865(32)
- Photocoupling: |Gρπγ| = 0.0802(32)(20)
- Renormalization factor of local vector current: ZV = 0.7903(2)

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Briceno, Hansen, and Walker-Loud (2014) for 1→2 transition matrix elements, previously applied by the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration to compute the πγ → ππ amplitude (Briceno 2015, 2016). The calculation builds upon the authors' own prior work on ππ scattering (Alexandrou 2017) and enables ab-initio determination of the ρ radiative decay width and πγ → ππ cross section, which are relevant for dispersion relations used to calculate hadronic contributions to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon.
