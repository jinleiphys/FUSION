# The $ŽĆ╬│\to ŽĆŽĆ$ transition and the $Žü$ radiative decay width from lattice QCD

> We report a lattice QCD determination of the $ŽĆ╬│\to ŽĆŽĆ$ transition amplitude for the $P$-wave, $I=1$ two-pion final state, as a function of the photon virtuality and $ŽĆŽĆ$ invariant mass. The calculation was performed with $2+1$ flavors of clover fermions at a pion mass of approximately $320$ MeV, on a $32^3 \times 96$ lattice with $L\approx 3.6$ fm. We construct the necessary correlation functions using a combination of smeared forward, sequential and stochastic propagators, and determine the finite-volume matrix elements for all $ŽĆŽĆ$ momenta up to $|\vec{P}|= \sqrt{3} \frac{2ŽĆ}{L}$ and all associated irreducible representations. In the mapping of the finite-volume to infinite-volume matrix elements using the Lellouch-L├╝scher factor, we consider two different parametrizations of the $ŽĆŽĆ$ scattering phase shift. We fit the $q^2$ and $s$ dependence of the infinite-volume transition amplitude in a model-independent way using series expansions, and compare multiple different truncations of this series. Through analytic continuation to the $Žü$ resonance pole, we also determine the $ŽĆ╬│\to Žü$ resonant transition form factor and the $Žü$ meson photocoupling, and obtain $|G_{ŽüŽĆ╬│}| = 0.0802(32)(20)$.

## Key claim

The paper reports a lattice QCD determination of the ŽĆ╬│ ŌåÆ ŽĆŽĆ transition amplitude for the P-wave, I=1 two-pion final state, but the calculation is performed at a single unphysical pion mass of approximately 320 MeV rather than the physical pion mass, and the results depend on the specific parametrization chosen for the ŽĆŽĆ scattering phase shift.

## Method

The calculation used 2+1 flavors of clover-improved Wilson fermions on a 32┬│ ├Ś 96 lattice with L Ōēł 3.6 fm, constructing correlation functions from a combination of smeared forward, sequential, and stochastic propagators. Finite-volume matrix elements were extracted for all ŽĆŽĆ momenta up to |PŌāŚ| = ŌłÜ3 (2ŽĆ/L) and all associated irreducible representations, then mapped to infinite-volume matrix elements using the Lellouch-L├╝scher factor with two different Breit-Wigner parametrizations (BWI and BWII) of the ŽĆŽĆ scattering phase shift. The q┬▓ and s dependence of the infinite-volume transition amplitude was fit using model-independent series expansions with multiple truncations, and analytic continuation to the Žü resonance pole yielded the resonant transition form factor and photocoupling.
