# Effect of the Constant Potential in the Bethe-Salpeter Equation for Proton-Neutron Elastic Scattering

## Key claim

The constant terms in the Bethe-Salpeter-derived nucleon-nucleon potential affect spin observables, particularly the spin correlation parameter \(A_{yy}(\theta)\) at center-of-mass angle \(\theta = 90^\circ\), but the correction factor \(F_1\) derived from including these constant terms yields \(F_1 \approx 1.05\) at \(T = 500\) MeV, which is too small to reproduce experimental data (which requires \(F_1 \sim 1.2\)) unless a non-zero core radius \(r_0\) is introduced.

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter equation for proton-neutron elastic scattering is expanded in a set of \(\Gamma\) matrices, yielding simultaneous equations for pseudoscalar, axial-vector, and tensor waves. These equations are converted to coordinate space via Fourier transform, and the instantaneous approximation (neglecting relative-time derivatives) reduces them to Schrödinger-like form. The tensor potential \(V_T(r)\) for the \(^3S_1\) state is expanded in powers of \(r\) up to the constant order, and the resulting wave function is constructed using Bessel and Neumann functions with a shifted momentum \(k' = \alpha k\) to incorporate the constant potential. The factor \(F_1\) is computed from the ratio of matrix elements with and without the constant-term correction, and a boundary condition \(\psi(r_0)=0\) at a short-range core radius \(r_0\) is imposed to improve agreement with experiment.

## Key numbers

- The cut-off parameter for the pion propagator is \(\Lambda \sim 500\) MeV, adopted from deuteron binding energy calculations.
- The specific mass parameter \(\mu_0\) that maintains the inverse-square potential strength \(g = 1/4\) is \(\mu_0 \sim 450\) MeV.
- At laboratory energy \(T = 500\) MeV, the correction factor \(F_1 \approx 1.05\) when using the simple \(B=0\) approximation, but \(F_1 \approx 1.2\) is achieved at a core radius \(r_0 \approx 1.25\,r_b\) where the divergent point \(r_b \sim 0.4\) fm.
- The spin correlation parameter \(A_{yy}(\theta)\) is computed at \(T = 425\) MeV for three values \(F_1 = 1.1\), \(1.15\), and \(1.2\), showing increasing curves that intersect experimental data at \(\theta = 90^\circ\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Kinpara, 2017) where the Bethe-Salpeter equation was expanded and the \(^3S_1\) state was approximated using only the inverse-square potential part. It addresses discrepancies in the spin correlation parameter \(A_{yy}(\theta)\) observed in experimental data from Bandyopadhyay (year not specified in text). The inclusion of constant and logarithmic terms in the potential, along with a core radius boundary condition, enables improved description of spin observables at intermediate energies, though the energy dependence of the correction factor \(F_1\) remains unresolved and suggests the need for additional relativistic or higher-order corrections.
