# Moments of Inertia of Nuclei in the Rare Earth Region: A Relativistic versus Non-Relativistic Investigation

> A parameter free investigation of the moments of inertia of ground state rotational bands in well deformed rare-earth nuclei is carried out using Cranked Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) and non-relativistic Cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) theories. In CRHB theory, the relativistic fields are determined by the non-linear Lagrangian with the NL1 force and the pairing interaction by the central part of finite range Gogny D1S force. In CHFB theory, the properties in particle-hole and particle-particle channels are defined solely by Gogny D1S forces. Using an approximate particle number projection before variation by means of the Lipkin Nogami method improves the agreement with the experimental data, especially in CRHB theory. The effect of the particle number projection on the moments of inertia and pairing energies is larger in relativistic than in non-relativistic theory.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, in a parameter-free comparison, both Cranked Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) and non-relativistic Cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) theories can describe experimental moments of inertia of well-deformed rare-earth nuclei with similar accuracy after applying approximate particle number projection (APNP) via the Lipkin-Nogami method, but the projection has a larger effect in the relativistic theory, likely due to its lower effective mass.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent cranked mean-field calculations for ground-state rotational bands in rare-earth nuclei (Gd, Dy, Er, Yb isotopes) using two frameworks: CRHB theory with the NL1 relativistic Lagrangian and the Gogny D1S force in the pairing channel, and non-relativistic CHFB theory using the Gogny D1S force in both particle-hole and particle-particle channels. Both sets of calculations were carried out with and without approximate particle number projection before variation using the Lipkin-Nogami method, solving the equations in an anisotropic three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis at a rotational frequency of 0.05 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The average decrease of the moments of inertia due to APNP(LN) over the considered set of nuclei is 1.35 in relativistic calculations and 1.15 in non-relativistic calculations.
- Proton pairing energies increase on average by a factor of 2.16 in relativistic calculations (range: 1.53 for 156Dy to 3.36 for 160Gd) and by a factor of 1.55 in non-relativistic calculations (range: 1.11 for 156Dy to 1.97 for 170Yb).
- Neutron pairing energies increase on average by a factor of 1.81 in relativistic calculations and 1.73 in non-relativistic calculations.
- The effective mass is m*/m ~ 0.6 in RMF theory and ~ 0.7 in the non-relativistic theory.
- Numerical accuracy for the kinematic moment of inertia J(1) and charge quadrupole moments Q0 is estimated at ~ 1.5% or better.
- APNP(LN) provides an additional binding of approximately -2.5 MeV in relativistic calculations.

## Context

This work extends earlier self-consistent cranked calculations of moments of inertia in superdeformed bands (Afanasjev 1999, Egido and Robledo 1993) to the normal-deformed rare-earth region, providing a systematic comparison between relativistic and non-relativistic frameworks. The study builds on the Thouless-Valatin theory of moments of inertia (Thouless and Valatin 1962) and earlier BCS-based calculations by Nilsson and Prior (1961), and it enables future investigations into the sources of remaining discrepancies between theory and experiment, which may relate to the mean-field parametrization, the pairing interaction, or the approximate nature of the particle number projection.
