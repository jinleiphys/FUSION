# Inclusive Studies of Short-Range Correlations: Overview and New Results

> We present an overview of Short-Range Correlations (SRC) studies using the inclusive measurement of the electron scattering off nuclei. A brief introduction of the origin of the SRC is given, followed by the survey of the two-nucleon SRC (2N-SRC) study and its interesting connection to the EMC effect. A discussion of the three-nucleon SRC study (3N-SRC) measured by the Jefferson Lab's Hall B and Hall C experiments which showed contradictory results is given and, most importantly, we report a new result from the Hall A E08-014 experiment which was dedicated on studying 3N-SRC. Our high precision 4He/3He cross section ratios at the x > 2 region do not show a 3N-SRC plateau as predicted by the naive SRC model. To further investigate the 3N-SRC as well as the Isospin effect of the SRC, we have designed several approved experiments in Hall A and in Hall C, including the Tritium experiments using the mirror nuclei (3H and 3He) which are currently running in Hall A.

# Inclusive Studies of Short-Range Correlations: Overview and New Results

## Key claim
The high-precision 4He/3He cross section ratios at x > 2 from the Hall A E08-014 experiment do not show a 3N-SRC plateau as predicted by the naive SRC model, contradicting earlier CLAS results that had suggested such a plateau; however, this does not rule out the presence of significant 3N-SRC contributions, as the prediction for where a plateau should appear for 3N-SRC is much less robust than for 2N-SRC.

## Method
The authors performed inclusive electron scattering measurements off nuclear targets using the high-resolution spectrometers in Jefferson Lab Hall A, extracting cross section ratios of 4He to 3He at x > 2 and Q2 values close to those of the earlier CLAS data (approximately 1.6 GeV2). The inclusive quasi-elastic cross section at x > 1 was decomposed into contributions from mean-field, two-nucleon SRC (2N-SRC), and three-nucleon SRC (3N-SRC) components, with kinematics selected such that Q2 > 1-2 GeV2 and x > 1.4-1.5 to suppress mean-field, final-state interaction, meson-exchange current, and inelastic contributions.

## Key numbers
- The Hall A E08-014 data show no 3N-SRC plateau in the 4He/3He cross section ratio at x > 2, in contrast to the CLAS data which showed clear plateaus for x > 2.25 for three different nuclei.
- The CLAS data were taken at Q2 of approximately 1.6 GeV2, while the E02-019 Hall C data were taken at larger Q2 values of roughly 2.8 GeV2.
- The 2N-SRC plateau is observed for 1.5 < x < 2, with heavier nuclei yielding a2 approximately 5.
- For isolating 2N-SRC, the required kinematics are x > 1.4 and Q2 greater than or equal to 1.5 GeV2.
- Some theoretical calculations argue that Q2 > 10 GeV2 is needed to be sensitive to 3N-SRC for x > 2.

## Context
This work extends the study of short-range correlations from the well-established 2N-SRC region into the 3N-SRC region, where earlier measurements from Jefferson Lab Hall B (Egiyan, 2005) and Hall C (Fomin, 2011) had shown contradictory results. The new Hall A E08-014 results are consistent with the explanation proposed by Higinbotham (2014) that the CLAS result suffered from large bin migration effects due to limited momentum resolution. The paper also discusses the established linear correlation between the SRC contribution and the EMC effect (Arrington, 2012), which was first observed when 9Be showed deviations from simple density scaling in both phenomena (Seely, 2009; Fomin, 2011). The results motivate further theoretical work to understand when the transition from 2N-SRC to 3N-SRC dominance occurs, and enable upcoming experiments including the Hall A Tritium experiments using mirror nuclei (3H and 3He) currently running in Hall A, as well as approved experiments in Hall C (E12-06-105, E12-10-108) designed to investigate SRC and EMC effects over a broader kinematic range.
