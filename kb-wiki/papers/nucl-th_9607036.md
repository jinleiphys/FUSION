# Hadronization in Nuclear Environment

> We present a space-time description of hadronization of highly virtual quarks originating from a deep-inelastic electron scattering (DIS). Important ingredients of our approach are the time- and energy--dependence of the density of energy loss for gluon radiation, the Sudakov's suppression of no radiation, and the effect of color transparency, which suppresses final state interaction of the produced colorless wave packet. The model is in a good agreement with available data on leading hadron production off nucleons and nuclei. The optimal energy range for study of the hadronization dynamics with nuclear target is found to be a few tens of GeV, particularly energies available in the experiment HERMES.

## Key claim

The paper presents a space-time description of hadronization for highly virtual quarks in deep-inelastic scattering, where the model agrees with available data on leading hadron production off nucleons and nuclei; the optimal energy range for studying hadronization dynamics with nuclear targets is a few tens of GeV, particularly the energies available at HERMES.

## Method

The authors treat hadronization perturbatively as gluon bremsstrahlung with radiative energy loss, incorporating the time- and energy-dependence of the energy loss density, Sudakov suppression of no radiation, and color transparency effects that suppress final-state interactions of the produced colorless wave packet. The evolution of the wave packet during propagation through the nucleus is calculated using a path integral technique. The fragmentation function is obtained by projecting the leading quark-antiquark dipole onto the hadron wave function in light-cone representation.

## Key numbers

- The parameter \(k_0 \approx 0.7\ \text{GeV}\) is chosen to reproduce the string tension \(dE/dt \approx 1\ \text{GeV/fm}\) for soft gluon radiation.
- The gluon-gluon correlation radius from QCD lattice results is \(R_c \sim 1/k_0 \sim 0.3\ \text{fm}\).
- The mean production time \(t_{pr}\) exhibits approximate \((1-z_h)\nu\)-scaling and vanishes at \(z_h \approx 1\).
- The optimal energy range for studying hadronization dynamics is a few tens of GeV, specifically the HERMES experiment energy range.

## Context

This work extends earlier treatments of quark fragmentation in nuclear matter for high-\(p_T\) hadron production (references r2, r9), deep-inelastic lepton scattering (references r10, kn1, kn2), and hadroproduction of leading particles on nuclei (references r3, r13). The model provides a parameter-free description that agrees with EMC and SLAC data on nuclear suppression as a function of energy \(\nu\), momentum fraction \(z_h\), and \(Q^2\). The approach enables predictions for the HERMES experiment, where the onset of nuclear effects at moderate \(Q^2\) and in the region \(z_h > 0.8\) can be tested.
