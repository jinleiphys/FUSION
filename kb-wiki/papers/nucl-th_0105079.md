# Coulomb effects in nucleon-deuteron polarization-transfer coefficients

> Coulomb effects in the neutron-deuteron and proton-deuteron polarization-transfer coefficients $K_y^{y'}$, $K_z^{x'}$, $K_y^{x'x'-y'y'}$ and $K_y^{z'z'}$ are studied at energies above the deuteron breakup threshold. Theoretical predictions for these observables are evaluated in the framework of the Kohn Variational Principle using correlated basis functions to expand the three-nucleon scattering wave function. The two-nucleon Argonne $v_{18}$ and the three-nucleon Urbana IX potentials are considered. In the proton-deuteron case, the Coulomb interaction between the two protons is included explicitly and the results are compared to the experimental data available at $E_{lab}=10,19,22.7$ MeV. In the neutron-deuteron case, a comparison to a recent measurement of $K_y^{y'}$ by Hempen {\sl et al.} at $E_{lab}=19$ MeV evidences a contribution of the calculated Coulomb effects opposite to those extracted from the experiment.

# Coulomb effects in nucleon-deuteron polarization-transfer coefficients

## Key claim

Coulomb and three-nucleon interaction (TNI) effects in the polarization-transfer coefficients are of comparable magnitude, but the calculated Coulomb contribution to the minimum of \(K_y^{y'}\) at \(\theta_{c.m.}\approx 110^\circ\) and \(E_{lab}=19\) MeV increases its absolute value, opposite to the trend extracted from the experimental neutron-deuteron data.

## Method

The authors solved the three-nucleon scattering problem using the Kohn Variational Principle with a correlated hyperspherical harmonic basis to expand the scattering wave function. They employed the Argonne \(v_{18}\) two-nucleon potential alone and combined with the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction, and included the Coulomb interaction explicitly for proton-deuteron calculations. Results were compared to experimental data at laboratory energies of 10, 19, and 22.7 MeV.

## Key numbers

- At \(E_{lab}=19\) MeV, the minimum of \(K_y^{y'}\) occurs at \(\theta_{c.m.}\approx 110^\circ\); the n-d AV18 curve describes the n-d datum at that angle, but the p-d AV18+UR curve lies below it.
- At \(E_{lab}=22.7\) MeV, Coulomb and TNI effects in \(K_y^{y'}\) are larger than at \(E_{lab}=19\) MeV.
- At \(E_{lab}=10\) MeV, the minimum of \(K_y^{y'}\) is very shallow and Coulomb and TNI effects are small.
- The triton binding energy calculated with any new local NN interaction is \(B_t\approx 7.6\) MeV; with Coulomb and TNI included, \(B(^3\mathrm{He})\approx 7.7\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev-equation calculations by the Bochum group (Sydow et al., 1999; Hempen et al., 1999) for polarization-transfer coefficients in nucleon-deuteron scattering, which neglected the Coulomb interaction. Those earlier calculations found that all NN models underestimated the minimum of \(K_y^{y'}\) by about 10% and that including the Tucson-Melbourne TNI removed the spreading among models. The present results show that explicit inclusion of Coulomb and the Urbana IX TNI brings the p-d calculations into reasonable agreement with p-d data, but the n-d comparison reveals a discrepancy in the sign of the Coulomb effect. New measurements with more data points are needed to resolve the conflict.
