# On V_ud determination from neutron decay

> The recent results of the PIBETA experiment strongly suggest the presence of a non-(V-A) anomalous interaction in the radiative pion decay. The same interaction should inevitably contribute to the neutron decay and in particular it should affect the V_ud determination. This paper is dedicated to the prediction of the eventual discrepancy in V_ud extracted from the super-allowed Fermi transitions and from the polarized neutron decay.

# On \(V_{ud}\) determination from neutron decay

## Key claim

The paper predicts that a non-(V-A) tensor interaction, previously invoked to explain the PIBETA anomaly in radiative pion decay, should produce a systematic shift in the value of \(V_{ud}\) extracted from polarized neutron decay relative to the value from super-allowed \(0^+\to 0^+\) Fermi transitions. The shift is estimated to be comparable to the current experimental accuracy, partially explaining the existing \(2\sigma\) discrepancy between the two determinations.

## Method

The author introduces a phenomenological tensor interaction Lagrangian with coupling constant \(f_T \simeq 0.013\) from the PIBETA radiative pion decay analysis, then estimates its nucleon matrix element using the tensor charge \(g_T \simeq 0.029(8)\) derived from current quark masses. The resulting effective coupling for neutron decay is \(F_T = 4 f_T g_T = 1.5(4) \times 10^{-3}\). The author computes the modifications to the unpolarized and polarized neutron decay distributions, integrates over the electron spectrum, and extracts the shift in the asymmetry parameter \(A\) and the derived \(\lambda = g_A/g_V\) for the PERKEO II experimental acceptance.

## Key numbers

- Tensor interaction coupling from PIBETA: \(f_T \simeq 0.013\) (dimensionless)
- Tensor charge for neutron: \(g_T \simeq 0.029(8)\), derived from current quark masses \(m_u + m_d = 11.5 \pm 3.4\) MeV at 1 GeV
- Effective coupling for neutron decay: \(F_T = 1.5(4) \times 10^{-3}\), comparable to recoil order \(\Delta/m_n \approx 1.4 \times 10^{-3}\)
- World average \(\lambda = -1.2695(29)\) used for sensitivity estimates
- Sensitivity of asymmetry parameter \(A\) to \(\lambda\): \(\delta A/A \simeq 4.1 \, \delta\lambda/\lambda\)
- Sensitivity of angular correlation coefficient \(a\) to \(\lambda\): \(\delta a/a \simeq 3.6 \, \delta\lambda/\lambda\)
- Systematic contribution from tensor interaction to asymmetry parameter: 0.7% over the PERKEO II fitted region
- Shift in extracted \(\lambda\): from experimental \(\lambda^{\rm exp} = 1.2739(19)\) to corrected \(\lambda = 1.2720(19)\), with \(\Delta\lambda = 0.0019\)
- Corrected \(V_{ud}\) from polarized neutron decay: \(V_{ud} = 0.9729(13)\), using \(\tau_n = 885.7(8)\) s and \(\lambda = 1.2720(19)\)
- \(V_{ud}\) from super-allowed Fermi transitions: \(V_{ud} = 0.9740(5)\)

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of the PIBETA anomaly in radiative pion decay (PIBETA Collaboration, 2004) to neutron beta decay, following the proposal of chiral spin-1 bosons described by antisymmetric tensor fields (Chizhov, 2004). The paper addresses the \(2\sigma\) discrepancy between \(V_{ud}\) from super-allowed \(0^+\to 0^+\) Fermi transitions and from polarized neutron decay measured by the PERKEO II Collaboration. It predicts that future high-precision experiments such as aSPECT, UNCA, and abBA at Spallation Neutron Source facilities could confirm or reject the predicted spectral distortions. The same tensor interaction is also noted to potentially explain discrepancies in two-pion spectral functions from \(e^+e^-\) annihilation versus \(\tau\)-decay.
