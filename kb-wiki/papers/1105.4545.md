# Quarkonium production in high energy proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions

> We present a brief overview of the most relevant current issues related to quarkonium production in high energy proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions along with some perspectives. After reviewing recent experimental and theoretical results on quarkonium production in pp and pA collisions, we discuss the emerging field of polarisation studies. Thereafter, we report on issues related to heavy-quark production, both in pp and pA collisions, complemented by AA collisions. To put the work in a broader perspective, we emphasize the need for new observables to investigate quarkonium production mechanisms and reiterate the qualities that make quarkonia a unique tool for many investigations in particle and nuclear physics.

## Key claim

This paper provides a brief overview of current issues in quarkonium production in high-energy proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions, emphasizing that despite advances, there is still no clear picture of the quarkonium hadroproduction mechanism that can simultaneously explain cross-section and polarization measurements across different collider systems.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental and theoretical results on quarkonium production in pp and pA collisions, discusses polarization studies, and reports on heavy-quark production in pp, pA, and AA collisions. It compiles and compares results from multiple theoretical approaches (Color Singlet Model, Non-Relativistic QCD with Color Octet Mechanism) against experimental data from CDF at the Tevatron, PHENIX and STAR at RHIC, and early LHC results, while also examining cold nuclear matter effects such as shadowing and nuclear absorption.

- The formation time of charmonium wave function is given as tf ~ 1/(m_psi'^2 - m_J/psi^2) (no numerical value provided)
- The accessible x range from charmonium production at LHC kinematics is "one, two or even three orders of magnitude below the safe value of 10^-3"

## Context

This review extends the ongoing discussion of quarkonium production mechanisms that began with the Color Singlet Model (Chang 1980, Baier and Ruckl 1981, Berger and Jones 1981) and Non-Relativistic QCD (Bodwin, Braaten, Lepage 1995). It addresses the persistent puzzle that NRQCD successfully explains the CDF cross-section excess but fails to describe polarization measurements, while NLO Color Singlet Model calculations show improved agreement with polarization data. The paper enables future LHC measurements to resolve these puzzles by advocating for multidimensional polarization measurements and studies of psi' and Upsilon(3S) to avoid feed-down complications, and emphasizes that pA data at LHC are essential to investigate nuclear shadowing in unexplored x ranges and to discriminate among nuclear parton distribution sets such as nDS, nDSg, EKS98, EPS08, and EPS09.
