# Spectra of produced particles at CERN SPS heavy-ion collisions from a parton-cascade model

> We evaluate the spectra of produced particles (pions, kaons, antiprotons) from partonic cascades which may develop in the wake of heavy-ion collisions at CERN SPS energies and which may hadronize by formation of clusters which decay into hadrons. Using the experimental data obtained by NA35 and NA44 collaborations for S+S and Pb+Pb collisions, we conclude that the Monte Carlo implementation of the recently developed parton-cascade/cluster-hadronization model provides a reasonable description of the distributions of the particles produced in such collisions. While the rapidity distribution of the mid-rapidity protons is described reasonably well, their transverse momentum distribution falls too rapidly compared to the experimental values, implying a significant effect of final state scattering among the produced hadrons neglected so far.

## Key claim

The parton-cascade/cluster-hadronization model provides a reasonable description of pion, kaon, and antiproton spectra from S+S and Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS energies, but the transverse momentum distribution of protons falls too rapidly compared to experimental data, implying that final-state scattering among hadrons (neglected in the model) is significant.

## Method

The authors used the Monte Carlo implementation VNI of the parton-cascade/cluster-hadronization model, which treats parton scatterings with transverse momentum above a threshold p0 via perturbative QCD and switches off soft scatterings below p0. They compared the model's predictions for rapidity and transverse momentum distributions of pions, kaons, antiprotons, and protons against experimental data from the NA35 and NA44 collaborations for S+S collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon and Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- For S+S collisions, the slope of the model's proton transverse momentum prediction is lower by about 30% compared to the experimental data.
- For Pb+Pb collisions, the model's slope for protons is too low by about 50% compared to experimental measurements.
- The threshold parameter p0 is parametrized as p0(sqrt(s),A,B) = (a/4) * (E_h/GeV)^b, where a = 2 GeV, b = 0.27, and E_h = 2 sqrt(s)/(A+B) with A and B the mass numbers of beam and target nuclei.
- The model reproduces the rapidity distribution of transverse energy for central S+S collisions in excellent agreement with the NA35 estimate.

## Context

This work extends the parton-cascade/cluster-hadronization approach previously applied to Pb+Pb collisions by Geiger and Srivastava (1996) to S+S collisions, testing it against data from the NA35 and NA44 collaborations. It contrasts with thermal models, hydrodynamic models, and string-phenomenology models (such as Fritiof, VENUS, RQMD, and DPM) by using perturbative QCD with quark and gluon degrees of freedom from the earliest stages. The results enable future inclusion of final-state hadronic scattering, which was shown by Ron (unpublished) to substantially improve proton spectra for Pb+Pb collisions.
