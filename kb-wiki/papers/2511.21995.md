# QCD axions and domain walls in dense matter under compact stellar conditions

> In compact stellar environments, the stability of dense QCD matter requires the simultaneous fulfillment of charge neutrality and beta equilibrium. In this work, we study how temperature and finite chemical potential affect QCD topology and axion properties within this medium, analyzing both cases with and without the charge neutrality condition. Our results show that the topological susceptibility and axion properties are highly sensitive to the critical behavior of the chiral phase transition in both cases. In particular, the axion mass is strongly suppressed near the transition, while the axion self-coupling constant develops a pronounced peak whose magnitude depends on the temperature and density of the medium. Remarkably, around the critical point at $T\simeq70$ MeV and $μ\simeq346$ MeV, the self-coupling constant is enhanced by more than a factor of seven compared to its vacuum value, a feature that to the best of our knowledge has not been reported in previous studies. Such a strong amplification at the phase boundary indicates that axion-mediated interactions could play an important role in shaping the structure and stability of compact stars, with potential implications for their evolution and observable astrophysical signatures.

# QCD axions and domain walls in dense matter under compact stellar conditions

## Key claim

The axion self-coupling constant is enhanced by more than a factor of seven compared to its vacuum value near the critical point at T ≃ 70 MeV and μ ≃ 346 MeV under charge-neutral conditions, a feature the authors state has not been reported in previous studies. This enhancement is conditional on the system being near the chiral phase transition and depends on both temperature and density.

## Method

The authors employ a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a U(1)_A symmetry-breaking term that couples the axion field to quarks, evaluated in the mean-field approximation. They solve the gap equations for scalar and pseudoscalar condensates self-consistently while enforcing electric charge neutrality and beta equilibrium (μ_u + μ_e = μ_d) to model compact stellar matter, computing the thermodynamic potential, topological susceptibility, fourth cumulant, axion mass, self-coupling constant, and domain wall tension as functions of chemical potential at several fixed temperatures.

## Key numbers

- At the critical point (T ≃ 70 MeV, μ ≃ 346 MeV) under charge neutrality, the axion self-coupling constant λ_a/λ_{a0} reaches 7.34, more than seven times its vacuum value.
- The zero-temperature topological susceptibility from the NJL model is χ_t^{1/4} = 79.87 MeV, consistent with the chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) result of 77.8(4) MeV from GrillidiCortona (2015) and the lattice QCD result of 78.1(2) MeV from Borsanyi (2016).
- The zero-temperature product m_a f_a = 6.38 × 10^3 MeV^2 from the NJL model, consistent with the CHPT prediction of 6.06(5) × 10^3 MeV^2 and the standard invisible axion model estimate of approximately 6.0 × 10^3 MeV^2.
- The zero-temperature axion self-coupling λ_a f_a^4 = −(55.64 MeV)^4, in agreement with the CHPT result of −(55.79(92) MeV)^4 from GrillidiCortona (2015).
- At T = 0 MeV and μ = 436.0 MeV under charge neutrality, λ_a/λ_{a0} = 3.64.
- At T = 40 MeV and μ = 382.0 MeV, λ_a/λ_{a0} = 2.56.
- At T = 80 MeV and μ = 335.0 MeV, λ_a/λ_{a0} = 6.55.
- At T = 120 MeV and μ = 282.0 MeV, λ_a/λ_{a0} = 1.20.
- The NJL model parameters used are Λ = 590 MeV, G_0 Λ^2 = 2.435, G_1 = (1−c)G_0, G_2 = c G_0 with c = 0.2, and m_0 = 6 MeV, fixed to reproduce m_π = 140.2 MeV, f_π = 92.6 MeV, and σ_0 = 2(−241.5 MeV)^3.

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model studies of QCD topology and axion properties by Lu (2018) and Zhang (2023) to include the electric charge neutrality condition essential for compact star matter, with a focus on density evolution at low temperatures and high densities. It contrasts with earlier finite-temperature studies that did not quantify the strong enhancement of the axion self-coupling near the critical region, and it goes beyond CHPT (which becomes unreliable near the chiral phase transition) and lattice QCD (which suffers from the sign problem at finite baryon density). The results enable future investigations of axion-mediated interactions in neutron star cores and merger events, potentially linking axion physics to observable astrophysical signatures such as stellar cooling and energy transport.
