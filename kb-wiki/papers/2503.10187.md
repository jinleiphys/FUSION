# Nuclear matter in relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory with local and nonlocal covariant chiral interactions at leading order

> The simultaneous description for nuclear matter and finite nuclei has been a long-standing challenge in nuclear ab initio theory. With the success for nuclear matter, the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory with covariant chiral interactions is a promising ab initio approach to describe both nuclear matter and finite nuclei. In the description of the finite nuclei with the current RBHF theory, the covariant chiral interactions have to be localized to make calculations feasible. In order to examine the reliability and validity, in this letter, the RBHF theory with local and nonlocal covariant chiral interactions at leading order are applied for nuclear matter. The low-energy constants in the covariant chiral interactions determined with the local regularization are close to those with the nonlocal regularization. Moreover, the RBHF theory with local and nonlocal covariant chiral interactions provide equally well description of the saturation properties of nuclear matter. The present work paves the way for the implementation of covariant chiral interactions in RBHF theory for finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The RBHF theory with both local and nonlocal covariant chiral interactions at leading order provides equally good descriptions of the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter, validating the use of local regularization for future finite-nuclei calculations where localization is required for feasibility.

## Method

The authors solved the Thompson equation, a relativistic three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, to obtain the in-medium G-matrix for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter. The LO covariant chiral interactions, consisting of contact terms and one-pion-exchange, were regularized using a Gaussian regulator depending only on transfer momentum, making the interactions local in coordinate space. The low-energy constants were determined by fitting to J≤1 Nijmegen partial wave phase shifts of the np channel up to 100 MeV laboratory kinetic energy at six energy points, with cutoff Λ varied from 500 to 700 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For local regularization at Λ=700 MeV (set 700-I), the saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter is E/A = -14.79 MeV and ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- For nonlocal regularization at Λ=567 MeV, the saturation point is E/A = -15.82 MeV and ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- The low-energy constants for local regularization at Λ=700 MeV (set 700-I): C_S = -656.98 GeV⁻², C_V = 614.94 GeV⁻², C_AV = -122.34 GeV⁻², C_T = -51.84 GeV⁻², with χ̃²/N = 3.59
- The low-energy constants for local regularization at Λ=600 MeV (set 600-I): C_S = -672.28 GeV⁻², C_V = 616.35 GeV⁻², C_AV = -194.20 GeV⁻², C_T = -87.52 GeV⁻², with χ̃²/N = 4.18

## Context

This work extends the previous RBHF study using nonlocal LO covariant chiral interactions by Zou (2023) to the local regularization case, which is necessary for implementing these interactions in the coordinate-space RBHF theory for finite nuclei developed by Shen (2016). The results contrast with nonrelativistic chiral interactions, where next-to-next-to-leading-order calculations with three-nucleon forces are needed to describe saturation, while the RBHF theory with LO covariant chiral interactions alone suffices, supporting the interpretation of nuclear matter saturation as a relativistic effect originally proposed by Anastasio (1980) and Brockmann (1990). The present validation enables future applications to finite nuclei using RBHF or relativistic quantum Monte Carlo methods.
