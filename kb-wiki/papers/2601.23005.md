# Analysis of regulator and cutoff artifacts in the phase diagram of the quark-meson model

> We study regulator and cutoff artifacts in the quark-meson model at finite temperature and quark chemical potential within the functional renormalization-group approach using the local potential approximation. To this end, we discuss the concept of renormalization-group consistency in effective models, which necessitates a nontrivial parameter-fixing procedure to enable a meaningful comparison of results obtained with different regulators and cutoffs. We employ a standard range of cutoff values used in phenomenological studies and regulators that differ significantly in their analytic properties as well as in their classification according to the principle of strongest singularity. We find that regulator and cutoff dependences are small at low temperatures and quark chemical potentials. At high temperatures and low quark chemical potentials, significant cutoff artifacts arise, whereas the properties of the regulator affect the dynamics in the regime governed by a chiral phase transition of first order at low temperatures and high quark chemical potentials.

## Key claim

Regulator and cutoff artifacts in the quark-meson model phase diagram are small at low temperatures and quark chemical potentials, but significant cutoff artifacts arise at high temperatures and low chemical potentials, while regulator properties dominate the first-order chiral phase transition regime at low temperatures and high chemical potentials, provided that a nontrivial parameter-fixing procedure is applied separately for each regulator and cutoff combination to enable meaningful comparisons.

## Method

The authors study the quark-meson model with two quark flavors and three colors within the functional renormalization-group (fRG) approach using the local potential approximation (LPA), which incorporates bosonic fluctuations through the scale-dependent effective potential. They employ three-dimensional regulators acting only on spatial momenta, using three different regulator shape functions: the Litim regulator, an exponential regulator (Exp2), and a smooth Litim-like regulator (SL). The flow equation for the scale-dependent effective potential is solved numerically using the Kurganov-Tadmor scheme, with the UV parameters (m², λ, c, and the Yukawa coupling h) re-tuned separately for each regulator and UV cutoff combination to reproduce the same set of vacuum IR observables at k_IR = 50 MeV.

## Key numbers

- UV cutoffs considered: Λ ∈ {0.6 GeV, 0.8 GeV, 1.0 GeV, 1.2 GeV}, which lie in the range typically employed in quark-meson model studies
- The IR scale for parameter fixing is k_IR = 50 MeV
- Vacuum conditions are approximated at μ = 0 and T = 0.0001 GeV
- The vacuum observables used for parameter fixing include the pion decay constant f_π (identified with the vacuum minimum σ_gs^vac), the sigma meson curvature mass m_c^vac, the constituent quark mass m_q^vac = h σ_gs^vac, and the pion curvature mass m_π^vac

## Context

This work extends previous fRG studies of the quark-meson model by Braun (2009), Tripolt (2017), and Schaefer (2006) by systematically quantifying regulator and UV cutoff artifacts in the phase diagram. It builds on the concept of RG consistency introduced by Braun (2018), which requires re-tuning UV parameters for different regulators and cutoffs to enable meaningful comparisons. The analysis enables future phenomenological studies to assess the reliability of phase diagram predictions from effective models, particularly regarding the location and nature of the chiral phase transition and the potential critical endpoint.
