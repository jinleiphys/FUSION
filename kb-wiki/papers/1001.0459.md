# Above threshold s-wave resonances illustrated by the 1/2$^+$ states in $^9$Be and $^9$B

> We solve the persistent problem of the structure of the lowest $1/2^+$ resonance in $^9$Be which is important to bridge the A=8 gap in nucleosynthesis in stars. We show that the state is a genuine three-body resonance even though it decays entirely into neutron-$^8$Be relative s-waves. The necessary barrier is created by "dynamical" evolution of the wave function as the short-distance $α$-$^5$He structure is changed into the large-distance n-$^8$Be structure. This decay mechanism leads to a width about two times smaller than table values. The previous interpretations as a virtual state or a two-body resonance are incorrect. The isobaric analog 1/2$^+$ state in $^9$B is found to have energy and width in the vicinity of 2.0 MeV and 1.5 MeV, respectively. We also predict another 1/2$^+$ resonance in $^9$B with similar energy and width.

## Key claim

The lowest 1/2⁺ resonance in ⁹Be is a genuine three-body resonance, not a virtual state or a two-body resonance, even though it decays entirely through neutron-⁸Be relative s-waves; the necessary barrier arises from the "dynamical" restructuring of the wave function as it evolves from a short-distance α-⁵He structure to a large-distance n-⁸Be structure, leading to a width about two times smaller than the tabulated value.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body problem for ⁹Be (α+α+n) and ⁹B (α+α+p) using an adiabatic hyperspherical expansion of the Faddeev equations, where angular eigenvalues λₙ(ρ) provide effective adiabatic potentials as functions of the hyperradius ρ. The radial wave functions were obtained from a coupled set of radial equations that include diagonal coupling terms Q(ρ), which arise from second derivatives of the angular eigenfunctions with respect to ρ. Resonance energies and widths were computed via the S-matrix pole found by complex scaling, supplemented by WKB tunneling estimates and extrapolation from the charged (⁹B) to the neutral (⁹Be) case.

## Key numbers

- The α-α two-body s-wave resonance in ⁸Be lies at 0.0918 MeV with a width around 9 eV.
- The tabulated 1/2⁺ state in ⁹Be has an excitation energy of 1.684 MeV (0.110 MeV above the n-⁸Be threshold) and a width of 0.217 MeV (from Tilley (2004)).
- The computed three-body resonance width for ⁹Be at 0.11 MeV energy is estimated to be about 0.1 MeV, roughly half the tabulated value of 0.217 MeV.
- For ⁹B, the first predicted 1/2⁺ resonance has an energy slightly below 2.0 MeV with a width of 1.3 MeV; a second predicted 1/2⁺ resonance lies at 2.05 MeV with a width of 1.6 MeV.
- The first crossing of the lowest adiabatic potential with higher potentials occurs at approximately ρ = 130 fm for ⁹Be.

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing controversy over the structure of the 1/2⁺ state in ⁹Be, which is important for bridging the A=8 gap in stellar nucleosynthesis. Previous interpretations treated the state either as a two-body n-⁸Be resonance (Summers (2002) using R-matrix analysis) or as a virtual state (Efros (1999)), but neither approach reproduced the measured photodissociation cross section consistently. The three-body model reconciles the α-⁵He structure at short distances with the n-⁸Be structure at large distances, and the computed width agrees with the direct measurement of the peak in the photodissociation cross section (Summers (2002)). The results enable more reliable calculations of the nαα recombination rate in astrophysical environments and predict previously unobserved 1/2⁺ resonances in ⁹B.
