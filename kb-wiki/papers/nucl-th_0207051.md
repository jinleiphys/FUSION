# Isospin Fluctuations from a Thermally Equilibrated Hadron Gas

> Partition functions, multiplicity distributions, and isospin fluctuations are calculated for canonical ensembles in which additive quantum numbers as well as total isospin are strictly conserved. When properly accounting for Bose-Einstein symmetrization, the multiplicity distributions of neutral pions in a pion gas are significantly broader as compared to the non-degenerate case. Inclusion of resonances compensates for this broadening effect. Recursion relations are derived which allow calculation of exact results with modest computer time.

# Isospin Fluctuations from a Thermally Equilibrated Hadron Gas

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while Bose-Einstein symmetrization broadens isospin multiplicity distributions in a pion gas, the inclusion of resonances (rho, omega, eta) more than compensates for this broadening, resulting in distributions narrower than the random (mixed-event) benchmark, but only when total isospin is exactly conserved and the system is treated as a canonical ensemble.

## Method

The authors derived recursion relations for canonical partition functions that conserve both additive quantum numbers and total isospin I and its projection M, extending prior work that conserved only additive charges. For degenerate systems, they incorporated Bose-Einstein symmetrization through cycle diagrams and treated resonances as non-degenerate subsystems that are convoluted with the pion partition function. Multiplicity distributions and isospin fluctuations were computed numerically for model systems confined to a cubic volume with half-integer quantum numbers, using a temperature of 125 MeV and densities of 0.1 and 0.3 fm^-3.

## Key numbers

- For a system of 12 pions with only isosinglet states considered (no symmetrization, no resonances), the isospin fluctuation G^2 normalized to the random distribution falls from a factor of 2 to approximately 1 as the system size approaches infinity.
- At a density of 0.3 fm^-3 and temperature 125 MeV, including Bose-Einstein symmetrization for 24 pions in an isosinglet broadens the distribution relative to random, while adding rho, omega, and eta resonances narrows it below the random width.
- At a density of 0.1 fm^-3, both symmetrization and resonance effects on the isospin distribution are small.
- The pairwise emission model (isoscalar pairs only) yields fluctuations exactly twice those of the random distribution for all system sizes.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of isospin fluctuations motivated by disoriented chiral condensates (DCC), specifically the work of Pratt (1993) which neglected isospin conservation, and Pratt (1994) which accounted for isospin only through pairwise emission of neutral pion pairs. The authors also build on recursive methods for canonical partition functions developed by Chase (1995), DasGupta (1998), and Pratt (1999). The formalism enables exact calculation of isospin distributions for systems of a few dozen particles, which is relevant for modeling local charge conservation in heavy ion collisions where domains of limited size are expected to thermalize. The next step would be to include strangeness and baryon number conservation along with additional resonances such as strange mesons and baryonic resonances.
