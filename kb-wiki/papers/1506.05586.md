# New analysis of the low-energy $π^\pm p$ differential cross sections of the CHAOS Collaboration

> In a previous paper, we reported the results of a partial-wave analysis of the pion-nucleon ($πN$) differential cross sections (DCSs) of the CHAOS Collaboration and came to the conclusion that the angular distribution of their $π^+ p$ data sets is incompatible with the rest of the modern (meson-factory) database. The present work, re-addressing this issue, has been instigated by a number of recent improvements in our analysis, namely regarding the inclusion of the theoretical uncertainties when investigating the reproduction of experimental data sets on the basis of a given `theoretical' solution, modifications in the parameterisation of the form factors of the proton and of the pion entering the electromagnetic part of the $πN$ amplitude, and the inclusion of the effects of the variation of the $σ$-meson mass when fitting the ETH model of the $πN$ interaction to the experimental data. The new analysis of the CHAOS DCSs confirms our earlier conclusions and casts doubt on the value for the $πN$ $Σ$ term, which Stahov, Clement, and Wagner have extracted from these data.

# New analysis of the low-energy π±p differential cross sections of the CHAOS Collaboration

## Key claim
The angular distribution of the CHAOS Collaboration's π⁺p differential cross sections is incompatible in shape with the rest of the modern meson-factory low-energy database, casting doubt on the πN Σ term value of 59 ± 12 MeV that Stahov, Clement, and Wagner extracted from these data. This conclusion holds after incorporating theoretical uncertainties, updated proton and pion form factor parameterisations, and variation of the σ-meson mass in the ETH model fits.

## Method
The authors performed a partial-wave analysis of the CHAOS (DENZ04) π±p differential cross sections using two theoretical approaches: standard low-energy parameterisations of the s- and p-wave K-matrix elements, and the ETH model (which includes t-channel σ- and ρ-exchange graphs plus s- and u-channel baryon contributions). They assessed reproduction quality via three statistical tests (overall reproduction, shape, and absolute normalisation) using Monte-Carlo generated baseline solutions from 14 million events per data point, with p-value thresholds at approximately 1.24 × 10⁻² (equivalent to a 2.5σ effect).

## Key numbers
- The πN Σ term extracted by Stahov, Clement, and Wagner from CHAOS data was 59 ± 12 MeV (this value is now cast into doubt).
- For the truncated π⁺p database, minimal χ² = 397.4 for 260 degrees of freedom (compared to 401.2 in the previous analysis).
- For the truncated π⁻p elastic-scattering database, minimal χ² = 352.9 for 261 degrees of freedom (compared to 350.2 previously).
- The common fit to truncated combined π±p databases gave χ² = 750.4 for 521 degrees of freedom.
- The scattering length a^{cc} from the π⁻p fit was approximately 0.042 m_c⁻¹ (where m_c is the charged pion mass), differing from the threshold experimental result by a factor of 2.
- For unsplit data, 72 data points were identified as outliers (65 from π⁺p, 7 from π⁻p).
- The 37.10 MeV π⁺p data set was best reproduced by the baseline solution (p ≈ 3.65 × 10⁻³), while the 25.80 MeV π⁺p data set was worst reproduced.
- All six p-values for overall reproduction of π⁺p data sets were smaller than the significance level of approximately 1.24 × 10⁻².
- The σ-meson mass was varied within the PDG-recommended interval of 400 to 550 MeV (previously fixed at 860 MeV).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous partial-wave analysis (Matsinos and Rasche, 2015) of the CHAOS Collaboration data (Denz et al., 2004), confirming their earlier conclusion that the π⁺p angular distributions are incompatible with the broader low-energy database. The analysis contradicts the Σ term extraction by Stahov, Clement, and Wagner (2014) from the same data. The authors recommend against including these CHAOS differential cross sections in sensitive low-energy analyses until the problems, particularly for the π⁺p reaction, are resolved. The work enables future investigation of these data within Chiral Perturbation Theory using the method of A. C. et al.
