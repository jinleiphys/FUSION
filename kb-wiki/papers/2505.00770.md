# Study of electron-positron annihilation into four pions within chiral effective field theory in the low energy region

> In this paper, we employ chiral effective field theory to study the process of electron-positron annihilation into four pions in the low energy region within $E_{c.m.}\leq 0.6$ GeV. The prediction of the cross section is obtained through $SU(3)$ chiral perturbation theory up to the next-to-leading order, which is smaller than the experimental data in the energy region [0.6-0.65] GeV, though the data has only a few points and poor statistics. Then, the resonance chiral theory is applied to include the resonance contribution, with the lightest scalars and vectors written in the effective Lagrangians. A series of relevant decay widths and the masses of the vectors are studied to fix the unknown couplings. The resonance contribution should be one order larger than that of the chiral perturbation theory but still one to two orders smaller than the data. The significant discrepancy urged the new experimental measurements to give more guidance. We also compute the leading order hadronic vacuum polarization contribution from the four pion channels to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, $(g-2)_μ$. In the energy range from threshold up to 0.6 GeV within resonance chiral theory, the contributions are $a_μ=(0.680\pm0.062)\times10^{-16}$ and $a_μ=(0.597\pm0.058)\times10^{-16}$ for the processes of $e^+e^-\toπ^+π^+π^-π^-$, $π^0π^0π^+π^-$, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) predictions for electron-positron annihilation into four pions are two to three orders of magnitude smaller than experimental data in the 0.6-0.65 GeV region, and that including resonance contributions via resonance chiral theory (RChT) enlarges the cross sections by one to two orders but still leaves a discrepancy of one to two orders with the data, which the authors state "urged the new experimental measurements to give more guidance."

## Method

The authors computed the hadronization vector current for gamma* -> 4pi using SU(3) chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order (Solution I), then applied resonance chiral theory including the lightest vector mesons (rho, omega, phi) and scalar mesons (sigma/f0(500), a0, f0(980)) as explicit degrees of freedom (Solution II). Unknown couplings in the RChT Lagrangian were fixed by fitting to the decay widths of V -> e+e- and V -> S gamma using MINUIT, with the vector meson mixing angle and FV taken from Dai (2013). The cross sections were computed by integrating the four-body phase space using Eq. (6) in the paper.

## Key numbers

* The SU(3) ChPT cross section at Q=0.6125 GeV is roughly three to four orders smaller than the experimental value of 0.02 +/- 0.01 nb from BaBar (2012)
* The leading order hadronic vacuum polarization contribution from the four-pion channel to (g-2)_mu in the energy range from threshold to 0.6 GeV within RChT: a_mu = (0.680 +/- 0.062) x 10^{-16} for e+e- -> pi+pi+pi-pi- and a_mu = (0.597 +/- 0.058) x 10^{-16} for e+e- -> pi0pi0pi+pi-
* Fitted parameters include: lambda_6^V = -0.0636 +/- 0.0009 GeV^{-1}, lambda_{22}^V = -0.0168 +/- 0.0007 GeV^{-1}, lambda_3^{SV} = -3.7707 +/- 0.1307, lambda^{SVV} = 6.3030 +/- 1.0550 GeV, and M_{a0} = 0.9769 +/- 0.0018 GeV
* Fitted decay widths: Gamma_{phi -> a0 gamma} = (3.0886 +/- 0.1302) x 10^{-7} GeV, Gamma_{phi -> f0 gamma} = (1.3283 +/- 0.0374) x 10^{-6} GeV, Gamma_{rho -> e+e-} = (7.023 +/- 0.050) x 10^{-6} GeV, Gamma_{omega -> e+e-} = (6.106 +/- 0.053) x 10^{-7} GeV, Gamma_{phi -> e+e-} = (1.180 +/- 0.027) x 10^{-6} GeV
* The chi^2/N for the fit to decay widths is 2.30

## Context

This work extends previous SU(2) ChPT studies of e+e- -> 4pi by Unterdorfer (2002) and Ecker (2002), who found that SU(2) ChPT could not match data at 0.6-0.65 GeV. The authors apply SU(3) ChPT and find similar results, then incorporate resonance chiral theory following Ecker (1989) and Dai (2013) to include vector and scalar resonances. The results show that even with resonance contributions, the theoretical predictions remain one to two orders below the sparse experimental data from BaBar (2012), motivating new measurements in the sub-0.6 GeV region. The computed hadronic vacuum polarization contributions to (g-2)_mu from the four-pion channel are relevant to the ongoing tension between data-driven and lattice QCD determinations of the muon anomalous magnetic moment.
