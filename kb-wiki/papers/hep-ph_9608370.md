# Charmonium Production in High Energy Collisions

> In recent years, there have been significant developments in heavy quarkonium production, both in theory and in experiment. These developments may have important implications for the use of charmonium as a probe in heavy-ion collisions. A summary of these developments is presented below. Those interested in more details are referred to a recent review article by Braaten, Fleming, and Yuan.

## Key claim

The color-singlet model drastically underestimates the cross section for prompt charmonium production in high-energy hadron collisions; the NRQCD factorization formalism, which includes color-octet contributions, can explain the factor-of-30 discrepancy between the color-singlet model prediction and CDF data at the Tevatron, provided the color-octet matrix elements are treated as phenomenological parameters to be determined from experiment.

## Method

The paper reviews the NRQCD factorization formalism developed by Bodwin, Braaten, and Lepage, which factorizes the inclusive differential cross section for charmonium production into short-distance parton cross sections (calculable in perturbation theory in α_s) and long-distance NRQCD matrix elements that scale with powers of v, the typical relative velocity of the c and ċ pair. For prompt ψ production at large transverse momentum (p_T ≫ m_c), the cross section is expressed via gluon fragmentation functions, and the leading color-octet contribution from the parton process g → cċ (with the cċ pair in a color-octet ^3S_1 state) is compared to CDF data to extract the relevant matrix element.

## Key numbers

- The measured prompt ψ cross section at the Tevatron (p_T range 5 GeV < p_T < 20 GeV) is about a factor of 30 larger than the color-singlet model prediction.
- The extracted color-octet matrix element is ⟨O_8^ψ(^3S_1)⟩ = 0.014 GeV^3.
- The probability density for a pointlike color-octet ^3S_1 cċ pair to bind to form a ψ is 1.8/fm^3, compared to 21/fm^3 for the color-singlet ^3S_1 state, consistent with suppression by v^4 (where v^2 ≈ 1/4).
- The predicted branching fraction for Z^0 → ψ + X is 1.4 × 10^{-4}.

## Context

This work extends the color-singlet model (Schuler, year not given) by incorporating color-octet contributions via the NRQCD factorization formalism (Bodwin, Braaten, and Lepage, year not given), resolving a major discrepancy with CDF data (Sansoni, year not given). It enables predictions for charmonium production in heavy-ion collisions by providing a framework to determine nonperturbative matrix elements from data across multiple collision systems (pN, πN, eN, pċ, e+e-, ep). The paper also predicts that ψ' mesons at large p_T at the Tevatron should be almost completely transversely polarized (Cho and Wise, year not given) and that prompt ψ production should be observable in Z^0 decay at LEP (C-K-Y, year not given).
