# Dynamical response functions in correlated fermionic systems

> Response functions in nuclear matter at finite temperature are considered beyond the usual Hartree-Fock (HF) plus Random Phase Approximation (RPA) scheme. The contributions due to the propagator for the dressed nucleons and the corresponding vertex corrections are treated in a consistent way. For that purpose a semi-realistic Hamiltonian is developed with parameters adjusted to reproduce the nucleon self-energy as derived from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. For a scalar residual interaction the resulting response functions are very close to the RPA response functions. However, the collective modes, if present, get an additional width due to the coupling to multi-pair configurations. For isospin dependent residual interactions we find strong modifications of isospin response functions due to multi-pair contributions in the response function. Such a modification can lead to the disappearance of collective spin or isospin modes in a correlated system and shall have an effect on the absorption rate of neutrinos in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when vertex corrections are consistently included alongside dressed propagators, the response functions of correlated nuclear matter remain very close to the standard Random Phase Approximation (RPA) results, contradicting the naive expectation that dressing propagators alone would produce large high-energy tails. The key qualifier is that this near-equivalence holds for scalar residual interactions, while for isospin-dependent residual interactions the multi-pair contributions can strongly modify the response and potentially eliminate collective spin or isospin modes.

## Method

The authors constructed a semi-realistic Hamiltonian by combining a Gogny-type mean-field interaction with a simple Gaussian residual interaction, adjusting parameters to reproduce the nucleon self-energy from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. They solved the self-consistent Dyson equation for the dressed single-particle propagator in the second-order Born approximation for the residual interaction, then solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the dressed vertex using the Baym-Kadanoff prescription, with the kernel taken as the functional derivative of the self-energy with respect to the Green's function. The response functions were evaluated at finite temperature (15 MeV) and momentum transfer q=220 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter at normal nuclear density.

## Key numbers

- The residual interaction parameters are V0=453 MeV and η=0.57 fm
- The calculations were performed at normal nuclear density and temperature T=15 MeV
- The momentum transfer used in the response function calculations is q=220 MeV
- The ω-sum rule (Eq. sumreq) is fulfilled to within a few percent by the response functions including vertex corrections, while it is severely violated by the naive one-loop response function
- For hole-states in correlated nuclear matter, approximately 15% of the spectral strength is shifted to energies above the Fermi energy, reducing occupation probability from 100% to around 85%

## Context

This work extends the standard Hartree-Fock plus RPA framework (used in neutrino opacity calculations for supernovae and neutron star cooling, e.g., by Reddy et al. and Raffelt) by implementing the Baym-Kadanoff self-consistent scheme for vertex corrections. It directly addresses the violation of the ω-sum rule recently noted by Tamm et al. (2004) in one-loop calculations with dressed propagators for the electron gas. The results enable more reliable calculations of neutrino absorption rates in nuclear matter and provide a benchmark for simpler approximations, showing that the induced interaction terms (the lowest-order contribution of the so-called induced interaction from Babu and Brown) are the crucial ingredient for restoring sum rules.
