# Holographic Photon Production and Anisotropic Flow

> The thermal-photon emission from strongly coupled gauge theories at finite temperature via the bottom-up models in holographic QCD in the deconfined phase is studied. The models are constructed to approximately reproduce the electric conductivity obtained from lattice simulations for the quark gluon plasma (QGP). The emission rates are then embedded in hydrodynamic simulations combined with prompt photons and hadronic contributions to analyze the spectra and anisotropic flow of direct photons in RHIC and LHC. In general, the holographic models enhance the yield and improve the agreement in spectra, while they reduce the flow in low $p_T$ and increase it in high $p_T$.

## Key claim

Holographic QCD models that reproduce lattice QCD electric conductivity improve the agreement with experimental direct-photon spectra in heavy-ion collisions, but they reduce the anisotropic flow at low transverse momentum (p_T) and increase it at high p_T, compared to perturbative QCD rates.

## Method

The authors constructed two bottom-up holographic QCD models (Gubser-Nellore and V-QCD) in the deconfined phase, fitted to reproduce the electric conductivity from lattice simulations. They computed thermal-photon emission rates from these strongly coupled gauge theories, then embedded the rates into hydrodynamic simulations of the quark-gluon plasma evolution, combined with prompt photons and hadronic contributions, to calculate direct-photon spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients v_2 and v_3 at RHIC (200 A GeV Au+Au) and LHC (2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb) energies, as well as in small collision systems (p+Pb at 5.02 TeV and d+Au at 200 GeV).

## Key numbers

* Direct-photon spectra from 0-20% and 20-40% Au+Au collisions at 200 A GeV compared with PHENIX measurements, and from 0-20% and 20-40% Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 A TeV compared with ALICE measurements.
* Anisotropic flow v_2 and v_3 for 0-20% and 20-40% Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV compared with PHENIX measurements.
* Direct-photon v_2 in 0-40% Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 A TeV compared with ALICE measurements.
* In small collision systems, thermal radiation can reach up to a factor of 2 of the prompt contribution.
* Most thermal photon radiation in heavy-ion collisions comes from the phase transition region, 150 < T < 250 MeV, where the space-time volume is largest.

## Context

This work extends prior holographic studies of photon production from strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma (Caron-Huot, 2006) by using bottom-up models (Gubser and Nellore, 2008; Iatrakis et al., 2010) that break conformal invariance and match lattice QCD properties. It addresses the puzzle of large direct-photon anisotropic flow observed at RHIC (Adare, 2015) and LHC (Lohner, 2012), which previous pQCD-based predictions (Arnold, 2001) underestimated. The findings suggest that hadronic contributions dominate the flow at low p_T, while QGP thermal photons from holographic models enhance flow at high p_T, and future measurements in small collision systems could help distinguish between emission mechanisms.
