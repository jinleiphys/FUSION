# Generalized density functional equation of state for astrophysical simulations with 3-body forces and quark gluon plasma

> We present an updated general purpose nuclear equation of state (EoS) for use in simulations of core-collapse supernovae, neutron star mergers and black hole collapse. This EoS is formulated in the context of Density Functional Theory (DFT) and is generalized to include all DFT EoSs consistent with known nuclear and astrophysical constraints. This EoS also allows for the possibility of the formation of material with a net proton excess ($Y_p > 0.5$) and has an improved treatment of the nuclear statistical equilibrium and the transition to heavy nuclei as the density approaches nuclear matter density. We include the effects of pions in the regime above nuclear matter density and incorporate all of the known mesonic and baryonic states at high temperature. We analyze how a 3-body nuclear force term in the DFT at high densities stiffens the EoS to satisfy the maximum neutron star constraint, however the density dependence of the symmetry anergy and the formation of pions at high temperatures allows for a softening of the central core in supernova collapse calculations leading to a robust explosion. We also add the possibility of a transition to a QCD chiral-symmetry-restoration and deconfinement phase at densities above nuclear matter density. This paper details the physics, and constraints on, this new EoS and presents an illustration of its implementation in both neutron stars and core-collapse supernova simulations. We present the first results from core-collapse supernova simulations with this EoS.

## Key claim

The paper presents a generalized nuclear equation of state (EoS) formulated within Density Functional Theory that can incorporate any Skyrme parameterization consistent with nuclear and astrophysical constraints, and demonstrates that the inclusion of a 3-body nuclear force term stiffens the EoS to satisfy the maximum neutron star mass constraint while the density dependence of the symmetry energy and pion formation at high temperatures softens the central core in supernova collapse calculations, potentially leading to a robust explosion.

## Method

The authors constructed the Notre Dame-Livermore (NDL) EoS by dividing baryonic matter into five regimes: baryons below nuclear matter density not in nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE), baryons below nuclear matter density in NSE including pasta phases, hadronic matter above saturation density including pions, a phase transition to quark gluon plasma, and pure quark gluon plasma. The high density hadronic phase was treated with parameterized Skyrme energy density functionals including both 2-body and 3-body interactions, with the free energy per nucleon expressed as a sum of bulk, thermal, and zero-point contributions. The composition was determined by solving chemical potential constraints from the Saha equation combined with charge and baryon number conservation.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear saturation density transition point is parameterized as $\rho_N = 2.66 \times 10^{14} [1 - (1 - 2 Y_e)^{5/2}]$ g cm$^{-3}$
- The energy per nucleon to dissociate $^{56}$Fe into free nucleons is fixed at $\epsilon_{p 0} = 8.37$ MeV for protons and $\epsilon_{n 0} = 9.15$ MeV for neutrons
- The surface tension of cold symmetric nuclear matter is taken as $\sigma(0.5,0) \approx 1.15$ MeV fm$^{-2}$
- The critical temperature for pasta phases is expressed as $T_c(Y_A) = 2.4344 K^{1/2} n_B^{-1/3} Y_A (1 - Y_A)$ MeV
- The average heavy nucleus mass number below saturation is approximated as $\langle A \rangle = 194.0 (1 - Y_e)^2(1 + X + 2X^2 + 3 X^3)$ where $X \equiv (\rho / 7.6 \times 10^{13} {\rm g/cm^{3}})^{1/3}$
- The new EoS leads to an enhancement in supernova kinetic energy at early times ($\sim 250$ ms) compared to the earlier version

## Context

This work extends the original Bowers and Wilson (1982) formulation and the Wilson and Mathews textbook treatment by reformulating the EoS in a generalized DFT framework with Skyrme forces, adding a transition to quark gluon plasma above nuclear saturation density, and including a more realistic treatment of nuclear pasta phases. The NDL EoS complements the two most commonly used astrophysical EoSs, those of Lattimer and Swesty (1991) and H. Shen et al. (1998), by using a DFT approach rather than liquid drop or relativistic mean field formalisms, and it enables comparisons among these three approaches to quantify uncertainties in supernova simulations. The paper presents the first results from core-collapse supernova simulations using this generalized DFT EoS.
