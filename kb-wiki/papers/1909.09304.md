# Light Nuclei Production in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 5-200 GeV from JAM model

> Light nuclei production is sensitive to the baryon density fluctuations and can be used to probe the QCD phase transition in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In this work, we studied the production of proton, deuteron, triton in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 5, 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 54.4, 62.4 and 200 GeV from a transport model (JAM). Based on the coalescence production of light nuclei, we calculated the energy dependence of rapidity density $dN/dy$ and particle ratios ($d/p$, $t/p$, and $t/d$). More importantly, the yield ratio $N_{t} \times N_{p} / N_{d}^{2}$, which is sensitive to the neutron density fluctuations, shows a flat energy dependence and cannot describe the non-monotonic trend observed by the STAR experiment. Based on the nucleon coalescence, this work can provide constraint and reference to search for the QCD critical point and/or first order phase transition with light nuclei production in future heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The JAM transport model, which includes only hadronic degrees of freedom and no QCD phase transition physics, produces a flat energy dependence for the yield ratio \(N_{t} \times N_{p} / N_{d}^{2}\) in central Au+Au collisions, and therefore cannot describe the non-monotonic trend with a peak near 20 GeV observed by the STAR experiment.

## Method

The authors used the JAM (Jet AA Microscopic Transportation Model) hadronic transport model to simulate central Au+Au collisions (impact parameter \(b < 3\) fm) at ten beam energies from \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}\) = 5 to 200 GeV. Light nuclei (deuteron and triton) were not produced directly in the transport simulation but were formed via a coalescence afterburner applied to the final-state nucleon phase space, using fixed coalescence parameters: relative distance \(\Delta R\) = 4 fm for deuteron and 3.4 fm for triton, and relative momentum \(\Delta P\) = 0.3 GeV/c for both.

## Key numbers

* Mid-rapidity yields \(dN/dy\) for protons, deuterons, and tritons were calculated at ten collision energies: \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}\) = 5, 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 54.4, 62.4, and 200 GeV.
* The coalescence parameters were fixed as \(\Delta R\) = 4 fm (deuteron) and \(\Delta R\) = 3.4 fm (triton), with \(\Delta P\) = 0.3 GeV/c for both, chosen to reproduce the STAR measured yield ratio \(N_{t} \times N_{p} / N_{d}^{2}\) at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}\) = 200 GeV.
* The yield ratio \(N_{t} \times N_{p} / N_{d}^{2}\) from JAM shows a flat energy dependence, with values much smaller than the STAR data which exhibit a non-monotonic peak near \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}\) = 20 GeV.
* The theoretical baseline for the yield ratio in the absence of fluctuations is \(g = 1/(2\sqrt{3}) \approx 0.29\).

## Context

This work extends transport-model studies of light nuclei production at RHIC energies, following earlier hadronic transport studies such as Oh (2009) for deuteron production at 200 GeV and the hybrid model study by Oliinychenko (2018) at LHC energies. The JAM results provide a baseline without QCD phase transition physics, against which the non-monotonic energy dependence observed by STAR (2019, 2020) and NA49 (2016) can be compared. The study enables future searches for the QCD critical point and first-order phase transition by showing that a purely hadronic model cannot reproduce the observed peak in the neutron density fluctuation-sensitive yield ratio.
