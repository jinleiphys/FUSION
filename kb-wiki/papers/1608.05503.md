# Deuteron-nucleus total reaction cross sections up to 1 GeV

> Total reaction cross sections of deuteron, $σ_d^{\rm R}$, are calculated by a microscopic three-body reaction model. The reaction model has no free adjustable parameter and applicable to reactions at various deuteron incident energies $E_d$ and with both stable and unstable nuclei. The predicted $σ_d^{\rm R}$ are consistent with those evaluated by a phenomenological optical potential for $E_d\leq 200$ MeV in which the potential has been parametrized. A simple formula of $σ_d^{\rm R}$ up to $E_d=1$ GeV, as a function of $E_d$, the target mass number $A$ and its atomic number $Z$, is given.

## Key claim

The paper claims that deuteron-nucleus total reaction cross sections calculated with a microscopic three-body reaction model (CDCC) with no free adjustable parameters agree well with experimental data and with results from the An-Cai global optical potential for energies up to 200 MeV, but the model is applicable up to 1 GeV. The hedge is that the agreement with experimental data is "almost the same level as that of the global optical potential," and the authors note that data at 160 MeV for carbon deviate from the expected energy dependence.

## Method

The authors used the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC), a non-perturbative three-body reaction model treating the deuteron as a proton-neutron system interacting with a target nucleus. The nucleon-nucleus optical potentials were constructed via a single folding model using the Melbourne nucleon-nucleon g-matrix interaction and nuclear one-body densities from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with SLy4 Skyrme functionals. The model space included s-, p-, and d-waves of the p-n system with continua truncated at a relative wave number of 1.0 fm^-1, and the resulting cross sections converged within 1%.

## Key numbers

- Cross sections were calculated for 15 target nuclei (9Be, 12C, 16O, 28Si, 40Ca, 56Fe, 58Ni, 79Se, 90Zr, 93Zr, 107Pd, 116Sn, 120Sn, 135Cs, 208Pb) at deuteron incident energies from 10 MeV to 1000 MeV.
- The p-n continua were truncated at k = 1.0 fm^-1 with momentum bin width 0.1 fm^-1; maximum r = 100 fm and maximum R = 200 fm.
- Fitting parameters for the simple formula include: a1 = 0.306 fm, a2 = -0.923, a3 = 590 MeV; b1 = 1.33 fm, b2 = -0.112, b3 = 248 MeV; c1 = 0.00204 fm, c2 = -0.788, c3 = 453 MeV; d1 = 0.272 MeV.
- The Coulomb damping factor in the formula is C(E_d, Z) = exp(-0.272 Z / E_d).

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of total reaction cross sections from proton-nucleus reactions (Carlson, 1996) to deuteron-nucleus reactions, where previous global optical potentials (Daehnick et al., 1980; Bojowald et al., 1988; An and Cai, 2006) were limited to energies below about 200 MeV. The microscopic CDCC approach avoids the ambiguities of phenomenological deuteron optical potentials by explicitly treating breakup channel couplings. The resulting simple formula enables practical calculations of deuteron reaction cross sections for both stable and unstable nuclei up to 1 GeV, and the authors note that NASA's formula implemented in PHITS severely undershoots the CDCC results, particularly at low energies.
