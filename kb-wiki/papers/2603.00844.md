# Covariant diffusion tensor for jet momentum broadening out of equilibrium

> Jets are produced in the earliest stages of heavy-ion collisions, where they can interact with a medium that is not yet close to local equilibrium. Motivated by this, we generalize the usual jet transport coefficient $\hat q$ to a Lorentz-covariant diffusion tensor $\hat q^{μν}$ within a leading-order elastic (Boltzmann/Fokker--Planck) description of jet--medium interactions. The tensor formulation organizes medium effects in a frame-covariant way and reveals additional information beyond the standard scalar definition, including energy diffusion and off-diagonal components that encode correlations between energy and momentum exchange which are absent (or redundant) in equilibrium. We illustrate the formalism in (tree-level) massless $λφ^4$ theory for isotropic but out-of-equilibrium states. For sufficiently large jet momentum, quantum statistical effects become subleading, so that the non-equilibrium evolution can be studied reliably in the classical (Boltzmann) limit. This allows us to solve the corresponding Boltzmann equation for the medium and determine the time dependence of $\hat q^{μν}$ as the system approaches equilibrium. We find that out-of-equilibrium corrections can either enhance or reduce jet momentum broadening, depending on the initial distribution function.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the standard scalar jet transport coefficient $\hat q$ to a Lorentz-covariant diffusion tensor $\hat q^{\mu\nu}$ that remains well-defined in out-of-equilibrium and flowing media, but the authors explicitly note that this formulation is derived within a leading-order elastic (Boltzmann/Fokker-Planck) description and is illustrated only in tree-level massless $\lambda\varphi^4$ theory for isotropic but out-of-equilibrium states, with the caveat that quantum statistical effects become subleading only for sufficiently large jet momentum.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation for elastic $2\leftrightarrow 2$ scattering and define the covariant diffusion tensor $\hat q^{\mu\nu}(K) = (1/(2 u_\alpha K^\alpha)) \int dP dK' dP' q^\mu q^\nu \mathcal{W}_a f_{\mathbf{k'}} \tilde{f}_{\mathbf{p}} \tilde{f}_{\mathbf{p'}}$, where $q^\mu$ is the exchanged four-momentum and $u^\mu$ is the local fluid four-velocity. They connect this tensor to a Fokker-Planck description by expanding the collision operator to second order in momentum transfer, identifying $\hat q^{\mu\nu}$ as the rate of growth of the momentum covariance per unit path length. The formalism is then applied to massless $\lambda\varphi^4$ theory, comparing Bose-Einstein and Boltzmann (classical) statistics, and the time dependence of $\hat q^{\mu\nu}(t)$ is evaluated by solving the classical Boltzmann equation for the medium using a method-of-moments framework.

## Key numbers

- The jet transport coefficient $\hat q$ is defined as $\hat q \equiv d\langle p_\perp^2\rangle/dL$, the rate of transverse momentum broadening per unit path length $L$ (from Eq. 1 in the introduction).
- The diffusion tensor $B^{\mu\nu}(K)$ is defined as $B^{\mu\nu}(K) \equiv \int d^4q \, q^\mu q^\nu W(K,q)$, and the broadening tensor is $\hat q^{\mu\nu}(K,u) = B^{\mu\nu}(K)/(u \cdot K)$, where $u \cdot K > 0$ is the jet energy in the local rest frame.
- The paper demonstrates that $\hat q^{\mu\nu}$ is positive semidefinite: $Z_\mu Z_\nu \hat q^{\mu\nu}(K,u) \ge 0$ for all real four-vectors $Z^\mu$, with equality only if $Z \cdot q = 0$ for all scatterings.

## Context

This work extends the standard scalar jet quenching parameter $\hat q$ introduced by Baier et al. (1994, 1996) and Gyulassy et al. (1993) to a fully covariant tensorial form, motivated by the fact that jets are produced in the earliest stages of heavy-ion collisions when the medium is far from equilibrium. It builds upon recent efforts to quantify jet broadening during pre-equilibrium evolution using kinetic theory by Schlichting and Boguslavski (2020), Boguslavski et al. (2023), and Barata et al. (2025), as well as earlier covariant diffusion tensor approaches by Moore and Teaney (2004) and Denicol et al. (2012) that did not focus on jet momentum broadening. The formalism enables future studies of jet quenching in flowing and anisotropic media where the scalar $\hat q$ is insufficient, and it reveals energy diffusion and energy-momentum correlations that are absent or redundant in equilibrium.
