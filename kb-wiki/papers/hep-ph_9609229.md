# Meson Masses in Nuclear Matter

> Mass shifts $Δm$ of particles in nuclear matter relative to their vacuum values are considered. A general formula relating $Δm(E)$ ($E$ is the particle energy) to the real part of the forward particle-nucleon scattering amplitude ${\rm Re} f(E)$ is presented and its applicability domain is formulated. The $ρ$-meson mass shift in nuclear matter is calculated at $2\la E_ρ\la 7$ GeV for transversally and longitudinally polarized $ρ$-mesons with the results: $Δm_ρ^T \sim 50$ MeV and $Δm_ρ^L \sim 10$ MeV at normal nuclear density.

# Meson Masses in Nuclear Matter

## Key claim
The mass shift of a particle in nuclear matter can be related to the real part of the forward particle-nucleon scattering amplitude via a general formula, but this relation is only valid when the particle wavelength is much smaller than the mean internucleon distance and the formation length is less than the nucleus radius. For the rho meson, the mass shift is positive (mass increases in nuclear matter) and energy-dependent, with different values for transverse and longitudinal polarizations, contrary to earlier work that considered only rho mesons at rest.

## Method
The authors derive a general formula Δm(E) = -2π(ρ/m) Re f(E) relating the mass shift in nuclear matter to the forward scattering amplitude, using the optical theorem and treating nuclear matter as an inhomogeneous macroscopic medium in the Fermi gas approximation. For the rho meson, they use vector dominance model (VDM) to relate the rho-nucleon forward scattering amplitude to the photon-nucleon amplitude, then compute Re f from photoproduction data via a dispersion relation with one subtraction. For longitudinal rho mesons, they extend VDM to virtual photons and use deep inelastic scattering data on the nucleon structure function F₂ and the ratio R = σ_L/σ_T.

## Key numbers
- Transverse rho meson mass shift at normal nuclear density: Δm_ρ^T ~ 50 MeV for E_ρ between 2 and 7 GeV
- Longitudinal rho meson mass shift at normal nuclear density: Δm_ρ^L ~ 10 MeV for E_ρ between 2 and 7 GeV
- Upper limit on rho meson momentum from decay inside nucleus: k_ρ < 6 GeV
- Upper limit on rho meson energy from formation length condition: E_ρ < 15 GeV for middle weight nuclei
- Lower limit on particle momentum from independent scattering assumption: k > a few hundred MeV
- Broadening of rho width at E_ρ = 3 GeV and normal nuclear density: ΔΓ_ρ^T ≈ 300 MeV, ΔΓ_ρ^L ≈ 100 MeV
- Normal nuclear density defined as ρ = (4πr_0³/3)⁻¹ with r_0 = 1.25 fm

## Context
This work extends the general method of Akhiezer and Pomeranchuk (1950) for neutron propagation in nuclei to the calculation of meson mass shifts in nuclear matter. It contradicts earlier QCD sum rule calculations by Hatsuda and Lee (1992) and Brown and Rho (1991) that found negative mass shifts for the rho meson at rest, and by Chin (1977) and Bernard and Meissner (1988) that found positive shifts. The authors argue that previous QCD sum rule approaches (Hatsuda, Shiomi, and Kuwabara 1996; Koike 1995) are unreliable because the operator product expansion parameter 1/x ≈ 2.5 for the rho at rest is too small to justify keeping only a few terms. The results enable experimental searches for energy-dependent rho meson mass shifts in heavy ion collisions, where rho mesons are typically produced with energies above 1 GeV.
