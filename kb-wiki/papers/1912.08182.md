# Probing the Sivers function with an unpolarized target: GTMD distributions and the Odderons

> It is commonly believed that the Sivers function has uniquely to do with processes involving a transversely polarized nucleon. In this paper we show that it is not necessarily the case. We demonstrate that exclusive pion production in $un$polarized electron-proton scattering in the forward region is a direct probe of the gluon Sivers function due to its connection to the QCD Odderon.

## Key claim

The authors show that the gluon Sivers function, previously thought to require a transversely polarized nucleon target, can be probed in *unpolarized* electron-proton scattering via exclusive pion production in the forward region, because the spin-flip contribution from the spin-dependent Odderon survives when summing over initial and final proton helicities.

## Method

The authors compute the differential cross section for exclusive $\pi^0$ electroproduction $ep\to e'\pi^{0}p'$ at small $x$ using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework with covariant background field methods. They relate the dipole-type Odderon amplitude to generalized transverse momentum dependent (GTMD) distributions, then take the forward ($t\approx0$) limit where only the spin-dependent Odderon GTMD $g_{1,2}$ (proportional to the gluon Sivers function) survives. The amplitude is squared and summed over initial and final proton spins, yielding a cross section expressed directly in terms of the gluon Sivers function.

## Key numbers

- The decay constant $f_\pi = 131$ MeV is used for the pion distribution amplitude.
- The final cross section formula (Eq.~(\ref{eq:XSfin})) contains a prefactor $\pi^5\alpha_{\mathrm{em}}^{2}\alpha_{s}^{2}f_{\pi}^{2}/(2^3 x_B N_{c}^{2}M^{2}Q^{6})$ times $(1-y+y^{2}/2)$.
- For a model Sivers function $xf_{1T}^{\perp g}(x,\boldsymbol{k}^{2})=\mathcal{C}\frac{\boldsymbol{k}^{2}}{\Lambda^4}(2-\frac{\boldsymbol{k}^{2}}{\Lambda^{2}})e^{-\boldsymbol{k}^{2}/\Lambda^{2}}$, the cross section at large $Q^2$ scales as $\Lambda^4/Q^{10}$ times a logarithmic factor $[5-2\gamma_{E}+2\log(\Lambda^{2}/Q^{2})]^{2}$.
- The Primakoff background cross section scales as $\alpha_{em}^{4}f_{\pi}^{2}/(x_{B}Q^{6}|t|)$ and diverges as $t\to0$.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Zhou (2013) that the gluon Sivers function is related to the Odderon at small $x$, and generalizes the Odderon-GTMD correspondence previously studied by Boer (2018) by including target polarization effects. The authors identify three independent Odderon GTMDs for spin-1/2 hadrons ($g_{1,1}$, $g_{1,2}$, $g_{1,3}$), with $g_{1,2}$ reducing to the gluon Sivers function in the forward limit. The result enables experimental access to the gluon Sivers function at unpolarized colliders such as the EIC and LHeC, and provides a clean signal for the QCD Odderon that avoids the theoretical uncertainties of earlier proposals involving proton excitation to $N^*$ resonances (Berger, 1999).
