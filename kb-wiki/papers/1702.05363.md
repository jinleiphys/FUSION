# Parton correlations in same-sign $W$ pair production via double parton scattering at the LHC

> Same-sign $W$ boson pairs production is a promising channel to look for signatures of double parton interactions at the LHC. The corresponding cross section has been calculated by using double parton distribution functions, encoding two parton correlations, evaluated in a Light-Front quark model. The obtained result is in line with previous estimates which make use of an external parameter, the so called effective cross section, not necessary in our approach. The possibility to observe for the first time two-parton correlations, in the next LHC runs, has been established.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that two-parton correlations encoded in double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) could be observed for the first time in same-sign W boson pair production at the LHC, but only with sufficient integrated luminosity (around 1000 fb^-1 at 68% confidence level), and the observable most sensitive to these correlations is the effective cross section sigma_eff as a function of the product of muon rapidities.

## Method

The authors calculated double parton scattering (DPS) cross sections for same-sign W pair production at sqrt(s)=13 TeV using three different models of dPDFs: a factorized product of MSTW08 PDFs (MSTW), the GS09 model with perturbative correlations, and a Light-Front constituent quark model (QM) providing fully correlated dPDFs. The QM dPDFs were evolved from a hadronic scale Q0^2=0.26 GeV^2 (fixed by matching single W production to DYNNLO predictions) using homogeneous DGLAP evolution at leading logarithmic accuracy. The DPS cross section was evaluated using Eq. (14) without assuming a constant sigma_eff, and the resulting effective cross section was extracted as a function of the product of muon rapidities eta1*eta2.

## Key numbers

- DPS cross section for same-sign W pair production (charge summed) in the fiducial region: 0.69 (+0.18/-0.18 from delta mu_F, +0.12/-0.16 from delta Q0) fb for the QM model; 0.77 (+0.23/-0.21 from delta mu_F, +0.18/-0.18 from delta sigma_eff) fb for MSTW; 0.82 (+0.24/-0.26 from delta mu_F, +0.19/-0.19 from delta sigma_eff) fb for GS09
- Ratio sigma++/sigma--: 3.4 for QM model, 1.9 for GS09 model
- Average effective cross section extracted from the QM model: <sigma_tilde_eff> = 21.04 (+0.07/-0.07 from delta Q0, +0.06/-0.07 from delta mu_F) mb
- Reference experimental sigma_eff used for MSTW and GS09: 17.8 +/- 4.2 mb (average of two extractions in W+ dijet final state)
- Lower limit on sigma_eff in same-sign WW channel: >5.91 mb at 95% CL with 19.7 fb^-1 at 8 TeV
- Initial scale for LF PDFs: Q0^2 = 0.26 GeV^2, varied in range 0.24-0.28 GeV^2
- Integrated luminosity required to discriminate QM and GS09 central values: >300 fb^-1
- Integrated luminosity required to observe departure of sigma_eff from constant: ~1000 fb^-1 at 68% CL

## Context

This work extends previous DPS calculations for same-sign W pair production (Kulesza 1999, Maina 2009, Gaunt 2010) by using fully correlated dPDFs from a Light-Front constituent quark model (Rinaldi 2014, 2015) rather than relying on the external parameter sigma_eff. The results are consistent with earlier estimates using the effective cross section approach, but the model enables predictions without that external parameter. The paper enables future experimental searches at the LHC to extract sigma_eff as a function of kinematic variables (specifically eta1*eta2) to directly probe two-parton correlations, which would be complementary to information from ordinary PDFs and generalized parton distributions.
