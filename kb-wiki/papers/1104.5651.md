# On the Origin of Model Relations among Transverse-Momentum Dependent Parton Distributions

> Transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs) are studied in the framework of quark models. In particular, quark-model relations among TMDs are reviewed, elucidating their physical origin in terms of the quark-spin structure in the nucleon. The formal aspects of the derivation of these relations are complemented with explicit examples, emphasizing how and to which extent the conditions which lead to relations among TMDs are implemented in different classes of quark models.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the three flavor-independent relations among leading-twist TMDs (Eqs. 1-3) have a geometrical origin, arising from rotational invariance of the nucleon state in the canonical-spin basis combined with the assumption that the probed quark does not interact directly with other partons and there are no explicit gluons; the flavor-dependent relation (Eq. 4) additionally requires SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry of the nucleon wave function. The authors emphasize that these relations are not expected to hold identically in QCD because the TMDs follow different evolution patterns, but they may serve as approximate phenomenological guidelines.

## Method

The authors derive TMD relations by first expressing the quark-quark correlator in a four-component (net-polarization) basis and then rotating from the light-cone helicity basis to the canonical-spin basis using a rotation matrix that depends on the quark transverse momentum. They impose spherical symmetry on the canonical-spin tensor correlator, which forces only monopole structures to survive, and then translate these constraints back into relations among TMDs in the light-cone helicity basis. For the flavor-dependent relation, they additionally impose SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry on the nucleon wave function using an overlap representation in terms of three-quark light-cone wave functions.

## Key numbers

* The three flavor-independent relations are: \(g^q_{1L} - [h^q_1 + (\mathbf{k}_\perp^2 / 2M^2) h_{1T}^{\perp q}] = 0\); \(g^q_{1T} + h_{1L}^{\perp q} = 0\); and \((g^q_{1T})^2 + 2 h^q_1 h_{1T}^{\perp q} = 0\).
* The flavor-dependent relation is: \(\mathcal{D}^q f_1^q + g_{1L}^q = 2 h_1^q\), where for a proton target \(\mathcal{D}^u = 2/3\) and \(\mathcal{D}^d = -1/3\).
* The rotation angle \(\theta\) between light-cone and canonical polarizations satisfies \(\theta \to 0\) as \(k_\perp \to 0\).
* The Sivers function \(f_{1T}^{\perp q}\) and Boer-Mulders function \(h_1^{\perp q}\) must vanish identically under spherical symmetry in the canonical-spin basis.

## Context

This work extends earlier observations of TMD relations in specific models such as the bag model (Avakian 2010, Avakian 2008), light-cone constituent quark models (Pasquini 2008), some quark-diquark models (Jakob 1997, She 2009, Zhu 2011), the covariant parton model (Efremov 2009), and the light-cone chiral quark-soliton model (Lorce 2011). It contradicts models where the relations are not satisfied, such as some versions of the spectator model (Bacchetta 2008) and the quark-target model (Meissner 2007). The paper enables a systematic understanding of which model assumptions produce TMD relations, providing guidance for building parametrizations to be tested against experimental data from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan processes.
