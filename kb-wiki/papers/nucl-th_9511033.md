# Can Plane Wave Modes be Physical Modes in Soliton Models?

> I show that plane waves may not be used as asymptotic states in soliton models because they describe unphysical states. When asymptotic states are taken to be physical there is no T-matrix of $\cO(1)$.

# Can Plane Wave Modes be Physical Modes in Soliton Models?

## Key claim
Plane waves cannot be used as asymptotic states in soliton models because they are not annihilated by the Dirac constraint and therefore describe unphysical states; when physical asymptotic states are used instead, there is no T-matrix of order O(1), contradicting earlier claims of a Yukawa coupling between fluctuations and the soliton.

## Method
The author applies the Dirac treatment of collective coordinates to soliton models in 1+1 spacetime dimensions, introducing a constraint from the generator of translations and a gauge condition. The Dirac commutator is derived, yielding a modified commutation relation for the fluctuation field and its conjugate. The author then examines whether plane wave states satisfy the constraint and computes the T-matrix using both plane wave and physical normal modes that diagonalize the quadratic Hamiltonian under the Dirac brackets.

## Key numbers
- The classical soliton solution φ_c is of order O(M^{1/2}), where M is the mass of the soliton, while the fluctuation q is of order O(1).
- The non-canonical transformation used to simplify the constraint adds terms of order O(M^{-1/2}) or smaller to the Hamiltonian.
- The T-matrix for plane waves is given by T(k) = ω_k² |∫ φ'_c ψ_k|² / M, which is nonvanishing for plane waves but vanishes for physical asymptotic states due to orthogonality to the zero mode.

## Context
This work directly contradicts earlier claims by Ohta, Uehara, and Mattis that soliton models contain Born terms of O(1) implying a Yukawa coupling between meson fluctuations and the soliton baryon. The author argues that these authors used plane waves as asymptotic states without verifying that the states satisfy the Dirac constraint. The paper extends the Dirac constraint formalism of Tomboulis (1975) and Gervais et al., and enables a correct perturbative treatment of soliton-meson scattering using physical asymptotic states that are orthogonal to the zero mode.
