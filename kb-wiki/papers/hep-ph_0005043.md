# Dynamical Symmetry Breaking in the Sea of the Nucleon

> We derive the non-analytic chiral behavior of the flavor asymmetry dbar - ubar. Such behavior is a unique characteristic of Goldstone boson loops in chiral theories, including QCD, and establishes the unambiguous role played by the Goldstone boson cloud in the sea of the proton. Generalizing the results to the SU(3) sector, we show that strange chiral loops require that the s - sbar distribution be non-zero.

## Key claim

The paper establishes for the first time the leading non-analytic (LNA) chiral behavior of the flavor asymmetry dbar - ubar in the proton, showing that this behavior is a unique and model-independent signature of Goldstone boson loops in chiral effective theories including QCD. The authors stress that their analysis aims only at establishing this model-independent chiral behavior, not necessarily explaining the entire asymmetry quantitatively.

## Method

The authors compute the leading non-analytic (LNA) contributions to moments of the dbar - ubar distribution from one-pion loop diagrams using chiral perturbation theory, expressing the pi NN coupling constant via the Goldberger-Treiman relation in terms of the axial charge g_A and pion decay constant f_pi. They evaluate the n-th moments of the pion distribution function in the nucleon for both pi N and pi Delta intermediate states, extracting the terms proportional to m_pi^2 log(m_pi^2) that dominate in the chiral limit. The analysis is extended to the SU(3) sector by considering kaon loops and the N to K Lambda splitting function to derive the LNA behavior of the s - sbar difference.

## Key numbers

- The LNA contribution to the n=0 moment of dbar - ubar is (2 g_A^2 / (4 pi f_pi)^2) m_pi^2 log(m_pi^2 / mu^2), where g_A is the axial charge in the chiral SU(2) limit and mu is a mass parameter.
- With a mass scale mu ~ 4 pi f_pi ~ 1 GeV, the magnitude of the LNA contribution at the physical pion mass to the n=0 moment of dbar - ubar is of order 0.2, most of which comes from the pi N component.
- The experimental values for the asymmetry (Dbar - Ubar)^(0) lie between approximately 0.1 and 0.15.
- The LNA contribution to the n-th moment of the pi Delta distribution is (6/25) (g_A^2 / (4 pi f_pi)^2) ((M_Delta + M)^2 / M_Delta^2) (-1)^n (m_pi^(2n+2) / Delta M^(2n)) log(m_pi^2 / mu^2).
- The LNA contribution to the n-th moment of the K Lambda distribution is (27/25) (M^2 g_A^2 / (4 pi f_pi)^2) (M_Lambda - M)^2 (-1)^n (m_K^(2n+2) / Delta M^(2n+4)) log(m_K^2 / mu^2).

## Context

This work extends the earlier prediction by Thomas (1983) of an excess of dbar over ubar quarks in the proton based on the nucleon's pion cloud, and provides the rigorous connection to the chiral properties of QCD that had been missing from earlier meson cloud model analyses by Henley and Miller (1990), Signal, Schreiber and Thomas (1991), and Kumano (1991). The results establish that the measurement of flavor asymmetries in the nucleon sea reveals direct information on dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in QCD, and the extension to strange quarks builds on the proposal by Signal and Thomas (1987) that virtual kaon loops produce non-perturbative strangeness in the nucleon. The findings enable future experimental searches for strange-antistrange asymmetry in deep-inelastic neutrino scattering by the CCFR Collaboration to be interpreted within a model-independent chiral framework.
