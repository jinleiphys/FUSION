# Direct Capture at Low Energies

> The importance of direct capture for (n,$γ$)--reactions on intermediate-- and heavy--mass target nuclei occuring in the s-- and r--process is investigated. It is shown that the direct mechanism is non--negligible for magic and neutron rich target nuclei. For some double magic and neutron rich nuclei in the r--process direct capture is even the dominant reaction mechanism.

## Key claim

Direct capture (DC) is non-negligible for (n,γ) reactions on magic and neutron-rich intermediate- and heavy-mass target nuclei in the s- and r-process, and for some double magic and neutron-rich nuclei in the r-process it is even the dominant reaction mechanism, but only when the level density of the compound nucleus is low (i.e., when both the Q-value and projectile energy are small).

## Method

The authors computed direct capture cross sections using the code TEDCA, with nucleon-nucleus potentials obtained from a folding procedure that folds nuclear densities (from experimental charge distributions or from relativistic mean field theory (RMFT) for unstable nuclei) with an energy- and density-dependent NN interaction. For neutron-rich Sn isotopes, nuclear densities were derived from RMFT using the parameter set NLSH, and the folding potential strengths were adjusted to reproduce a volume integral of 425 MeV fm³ from elastic scattering data on stable Sn isotopes. The direct capture results were compared to compound-nucleus (Hauser-Feshbach) calculations performed with the code SMOKER, using the same masses, Q-values, spin assignments, excitation energies, and optical potentials.

## Key numbers

- For neutron-magic target nuclei at kT = 25 keV, the average DC cross section for ⁴⁸Ca(n,γ)⁴⁹Ca is 0.96 mb, compared to the experimental value of 1.03 ± 0.09 mb.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb(n,γ)²⁰⁹Pb at kT = 25 keV, the average DC cross section is 0.13 mb, compared to the experimental value of 0.31 mb.
- For neutron-rich Sn isotopes at 30 keV, the DC cross section on ¹³²Sn(n,γ)¹³³Sn is 220.7 μb, while the Hauser-Feshbach cross section is 116.4 μb.
- For ¹³⁸Sn(n,γ)¹³⁹Sn at 30 keV, the DC cross section is 60.4 μb, which is about 7 times higher than the Hauser-Feshbach cross section of 8.4 μb.
- The neutron-skin thickness for r-process nuclei (e.g., ¹³²Sn–¹³⁸Sn) is about 0.5 fm, as calculated with RMFT.

## Context

This work extends the direct capture method, previously applied successfully to light nuclei in primordial nucleosynthesis (e.g., Mohr et al. for ²H(α,γ)⁶Li) and the pp-chain, to intermediate- and heavy-mass nuclei in the s- and r-process. It contradicts the common assumption that direct capture is negligible for such nuclei, showing that for magic and neutron-rich targets the DC contribution can be comparable to or exceed the compound-nucleus contribution. The results enable improved reaction rate calculations for r-process freeze-out, particularly for neutron-rich Sn isotopes where no experimental data exist, and demonstrate the utility of RMFT (using parameter sets NLSH and NL1 from Sharma et al. and Reinhard et al.) for providing nuclear densities for unstable nuclei.
