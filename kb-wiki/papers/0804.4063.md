# Dynamical symmetries of two-dimensional systems in relativistic quantum mechanics

> The two-dimensional Dirac Hamiltonian with equal scalar and vector potentials has been proved commuting with the deformed orbital angular momentum $L$. When the potential takes the Coulomb form, the system has an SO(3) symmetry, and similarly the harmonic oscillator potential possesses an SU(2) symmetry. The generators of the symmetric groups are derived for these two systems separately. The corresponding energy spectra are yielded naturally from the Casimir operators. Their non-relativistic limits are also discussed.

## Key claim

The two-dimensional Dirac Hamiltonian with equal scalar and vector potentials exhibits an SO(3) dynamical symmetry for the Coulomb potential and an SU(2) dynamical symmetry for the harmonic oscillator potential, but only because the spin and orbital angular momentum are separately conserved due to the equal scalar and vector potential condition, which removes the spin-orbit coupling that normally breaks these symmetries in relativistic systems.

## Method

The authors constructed a two-dimensional Dirac Hamiltonian with equal scalar and vector potentials and defined a conserved deformed orbital angular momentum operator L. They assumed additional conserved quantities Q in a specific matrix form and solved the commutation relation [Q,H]=0 for the Coulomb and harmonic oscillator potentials separately. The generators of the symmetry groups were derived from these conserved quantities, and the energy spectra were obtained from the Casimir operators of the resulting SO(3) and SU(2) Lie algebras.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb potential energy spectrum: \(E^{\pm}_h = \frac{\pm n^2 - k^2}{n^2 + k^2} M\), where \(n = 2j+1 = 1, 3, 5, ...\) and \(k\) is the Coulomb coupling constant
- Harmonic oscillator energy spectrum: given by the real root of \(\frac{(E^{+}+M)(E^{+}-M)^2}{2M\omega^2} - (n+1)^2 = 0\), where \(n = 0, 1, 2, ...\)
- Non-relativistic limit of Coulomb energy: \(E^{+}_h \rightarrow M - \frac{2k^2}{n^2}M\)
- Non-relativistic limit of harmonic oscillator: \((E^{+}-M)^2 - (n+1)^2\omega^2 = 0\)

## Context

This work extends the dynamical symmetry analysis of Ginocchio (who found U(3) symmetry in the three-dimensional Dirac harmonic oscillator with equal scalar and vector potentials) to the two-dimensional case. It also parallels the known non-relativistic dynamical symmetries: the SO(4) symmetry of the Kepler problem (Rung-Lenz vector) and the SU(3) symmetry of the isotropic harmonic oscillator. The results enable generalization to N-dimensional Dirac systems, predicting SO(N+1) symmetry for the hydrogen atom and SU(N) symmetry for the harmonic oscillator, and suggest that similar dynamical symmetries should exist in spin-0 (Klein-Gordon) systems based on the equivalence shown by Alhaidaria et al.
