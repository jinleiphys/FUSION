# Hydrodynamics and perfect fluids: uniform description of soft observables in Au+Au collisions at RHIC

> It is argued that the use of the initial Gaussian energy density profile for hydrodynamics leads to much better uniform description of the RHIC heavy-ion data than the use of the standard initial condition obtained from the Glauber model. With the modified Gaussian initial conditions we successfully reproduce the transverse-momentum spectra, v2, and the pionic HBT radii (including their azimuthal dependence). The emerging consistent picture of hadron production hints that a solution of the long standing RHIC HBT puzzle has been found.

## Key claim

The paper argues that replacing the standard Glauber-model initial energy density profile with a Gaussian profile in perfect-fluid hydrodynamics enables a uniform description of RHIC Au+Au data, including the previously problematic HBT radii ratio \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\). The claim is hedged by noting that the Gaussian profile is a phenomenological modification whose microscopic origin remains an open question.

## Method

The authors used 2+1 boost-invariant inviscid hydrodynamics with a realistic equation of state interpolating between lattice QCD and a hadron-gas model. Initial conditions were generated from Monte-Carlo Glauber simulations (GLISSANDO) that include eccentricity fluctuations, with the transverse energy distribution parameterized as a two-dimensional Gaussian. The hydrodynamic evolution was coupled to the statistical-hadronization model THERMINATOR for hadron emission from a single freeze-out hypersurface at a universal final temperature of 145 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Final freeze-out temperature: \(T_f = 145\) MeV, used universally across all centrality classes.
- Initial time for the start of hydrodynamics: \(\tau_0 = 0.25\) fm.
- The ratio \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) from the standard Glauber initial condition exceeded experimental values by 20-50%; the Gaussian initial condition reproduced the ratio correctly.
- Transverse-momentum spectra and elliptic flow \(v_2\) were shown for centrality bins 0-5%, 20-30%, and 20-40% and compared to RHIC data, with good agreement.
- HBT radii and their azimuthal oscillations were shown for pion pairs with \(k_T\) bins of 0.15-0.25 GeV, 0.25-0.35 GeV, and 0.35-0.6 GeV, across various centralities.

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic approaches (Teaney 2000, Hirano 2002, Kolb 2003, Huovinen 2003, Eskola 2005, Hama 2005, Nonaka 2006) that successfully reproduced spectra and elliptic flow but failed on HBT radii, a discrepancy known as the RHIC HBT puzzle. The Gaussian initial condition, introduced by Broniowski (2008), resolves this puzzle by producing faster development of transverse flow and a slightly larger freeze-out transverse size, reducing \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\). The results enable further studies of alternative initial conditions (e.g., initial transverse flow, core-corona separation) and viscous effects (Pratt 2008).
