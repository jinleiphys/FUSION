# Fully general relativistic description of rapidly-rotating axially-symmetric neutron stars for constraining nuclear matter equations of state

> Background: Constraining the nuclear matter equation of state (EoS) from neutron star observations is one of the main subjects in nuclear physics today. In general, neutron stars rotate rapidly and structure of neutron stars can be affected, especially in millisecond pulsars. To better constrain the nuclear EoS, it is important to describe neutron star structure taking into account the effects of rotation in a fully relativistic manner. Purpose: In this study, we investigate the internal structure of neutron stars under the influence of rotation. We explore correlations between rotational effects and EoS parameters, based on realistic calculations of rapidly rotating neutron stars based on the KEH method, which provides stable solutions for axially symmetric rotating equilibrium configurations. Results: Using 5 different Skyrme EoS parameter sets, we find that the maximum angular frequency achievable by rotating neutron stars, as calculated via the KEH method, varies depending on the stiffness of the equation of state. We confirm that an increase in the rotating frequency leads to an overall increase in both the mass and radius along the M-R curve. By performing calculations at two frequently referenced neutron stars, we further examine how the changes in mass and radius correlate with the nuclear matter properties at saturation density. Our results suggest that the 716Hz rotational constraint may require a more conservative interpretation when accounting for realistic stellar deformation effects. Conclusions: To place stringent constraints on the nuclear EoS based on observational data, it is sometimes essential to account for the effects of rotation in neutron star models. In particular, the influence of rotation becomes increasingly significant at higher spin frequencies and cannot be neglected in rapidly rotating systems with $>$400Hz.

# Fully general relativistic description of rapidly-rotating axially-symmetric neutron stars for constraining nuclear matter equations of state

## Key claim

Using five Skyrme-type EoS parameter sets and the KEH method for fully general relativistic rotating neutron star models, the authors find that the often-quoted 716 Hz rotational constraint from the fastest known pulsar (PSR J1748-2446ad) may require a more conservative interpretation when accounting for realistic stellar deformation effects, and that rotational effects become significant and cannot be neglected for spin frequencies above approximately 400 Hz.

## Method

The authors developed a new computational code based on the fully general relativistic KEH (Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu) method with the Cook-Shapiro-Teukolsky compactified radial coordinate, solving the Einstein field equations for axially-symmetric rotating equilibrium configurations. They employed five Skyrme-type energy density functionals (BSk24, SkI4, SkM*, SkT5a, and SLy4) for the npeμ matter core, combined with the BPS and BBP EoSs for the outer and inner crusts, and restricted the analysis to rigid-body rotation (the large-A limit of the differential rotation formalism).

## Key numbers

- The five Skyrme parameter sets span incompressibilities K0 from 201.69 MeV (SkT5a) to 247.95 MeV (SkI4), and symmetry energy slopes L from 45.78 MeV (SkM*) to 98.53 MeV (SkT5a).
- The fastest known pulsar, PSR J1748-2446ad, has a spin frequency of 716 Hz, while PSR J0030+0451 rotates at 205 Hz and PSR J0740+6620 at 346 Hz.
- Rotational effects cannot be neglected in rapidly rotating systems with spin frequencies greater than approximately 400 Hz.
- The saturation densities n0 for the five models range from 0.157 fm^{-3} (BSk24) to 0.164 fm^{-3} (SkT5a).

## Context

This work extends the fully general relativistic rotating neutron star framework originally developed by Komatsu, Eriguchi, and Hachisu (1989) and later implemented in the open-source RNS code by Stergioulas and Friedman (1995). The authors note that the Hartle-Thorne perturbative approach, commonly used for slowly rotating configurations, shows significant discrepancies from fully relativistic models at spin frequencies above about 600 Hz, as shown by Kacskovics et al. (2022). The newly developed code enables access to microscopic properties such as internal density distributions and composition profiles, which the RNS code does not directly provide, and is intended to be extended to differential rotation scenarios in future work.
