# Probing vorticity structure in heavy-ion collisions by local $Λ$ polarization

> We study the local structure of the vorticity field and the $Λ$ polarization in Au+Au collisions in the energy range $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=7.7$--$200$ GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=2760$ GeV using A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model. We focus on the vorticity field arising from the non-uniform expansion of the fireball, which gives the circular structure of the transverse vorticity $\boldsymbolω_{\perp}=(ω_{x},ω_{y})$ around the $z$ direction as well as the quadrupole pattern of the longitudinal vorticity $ω_{z}$ in the transverse plane. As a consequence, the three components of the polarization vector $\mathbf{P}=(P_{x},P_{y},P_{z})$ for $Λ$ hyperons show harmonic behaviors as $\mathrm{sgn}(Y)\sinϕ_{p}$, $-\mathrm{sgn}(Y)\cosϕ_{p}$, and $-\sin(2ϕ_{p})$, where $ϕ_{p}$ and $Y$ are the azimuthal angle and rapidity in momentum space. These patterns of the local $Λ$ polarization are expected to be tested in future experiments.

## Key claim

The local vorticity field in heavy-ion collisions has a richer structure than the global net vorticity, and the paper predicts that the three components of the Lambda polarization vector will show specific harmonic patterns as functions of azimuthal angle and rapidity, which are expected to be tested in future experiments rather than claimed as definitively observed.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model as an event generator to simulate Au+Au collisions at energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at 2760 GeV. They calculated the thermal vorticity tensor using a coarse-grain method, produced Lambda hyperons via a coalescence mechanism at the end of the partonic phase, and computed the polarization vector from the spin vector using relativistic formulas. The Lambda hyperons were grouped into bins by azimuthal angle and the sign of rapidity to extract the local polarization patterns.

## Key numbers

- Simulations performed for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV, and Pb+Pb collisions at 2760 GeV
- 5 x 10^5 events generated per collision energy with impact parameter b in the range 0-25 fm
- Vorticity field shown at time t = 5 fm/c for 20-30% central collisions
- Lambda hyperons analyzed in the rapidity region |Y| < 1
- Fourier coefficients F_x, F_y, and F_z extracted from the harmonic behavior of polarization as functions of centrality at 200 GeV for Au+Au and 2760 GeV for Pb+Pb collisions
- The magnitude of the local polarization observable <P_y sgn(Y)> is larger than that of the global polarization P_y^G

## Context

This work extends previous studies of global Lambda polarization measured by the STAR Collaboration (2017) and earlier AMPT model calculations by Li et al. (2017) that focused on the net vorticity from the fireball's tilted shape. The paper systematically analyzes the local vorticity structure arising from non-uniform fireball expansion, which produces a circular transverse vorticity pattern and a quadrupole longitudinal vorticity pattern, building on observations of quadrupole vorticity patterns in earlier numerical simulations by Becattini et al. (2015), Teryaev et al. (2015), Jiang et al. (2016), and others. The predicted harmonic polarization patterns enable future experimental tests of local vorticity structure beyond the global polarization already measured.
