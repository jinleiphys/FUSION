# Beam Energy Dependence of Azimuthal Anisotropy at RHIC-PHENIX

> Recent PHENIX measurements of the elliptic (v2) and hexadecapole (v4) Fourier flow coefficients for charged hadrons as a function of transverse momentum (pT), collision centrality and particle species are presented and compared with results from the PHOBOS and STAR collaborations respectively. The status of extensions to future PHENIX measurements at lower beam energies is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the agreement between PHENIX, PHOBOS, and STAR measurements of elliptic flow (v2) for charged hadrons at RHIC is better than 5% for PHENIX/PHOBOS and 2-5% for STAR/PHENIX below pT ≈ 2.5 GeV/c, but that STAR v2 becomes systematically larger than PHENIX v2 at higher transverse momentum, reaching a 20% difference at pT ≈ 5.5 GeV/c, which is attributed to non-flow effects from di-jets that are suppressed by the rapidity gaps in the PHENIX measurements.

## Method

The PHENIX collaboration measured the elliptic flow (v2) and hexadecapole flow (v4) Fourier coefficients for charged hadrons using event planes reconstructed from four separate reaction-plane detectors spanning the pseudorapidity range 1.0 < |η| < 3.9, with charged hadrons measured in the two PHENIX central arms (|η| ≤ 0.35). The v2 and v4 values were obtained as functions of transverse momentum (pT), collision centrality (Npart), and particle species, with particle identification improved by a time-of-flight detector (TOFw) with 75 ps timing resolution and an Aerogel Cherenkov counter (ACC). The results were compared to PHOBOS v2 data obtained with event planes at 2.05 < η < 3.2 and STAR v2 data obtained using standard and modified event plane methods with the central TPC (|η| < 1.0), as well as multi-particle methods (four-particle cumulant and Lee-Yang Zero methods).

## Key numbers

- PHENIX/PHOBOS v2 ratios show differences ≤ 5% with no significant pT dependence across centralities.
- STAR/PHENIX v2 differences are less than 2-5% below pT ≈ 2.5 GeV/c for midcentral Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.
- At pT ≈ 5.5 GeV/c, STAR v2 is systematically larger than PHENIX v2 by 20% when using the standard STAR event plane method.
- Using the modified STAR event plane method (EP2), the difference at pT ≈ 5.5 GeV/c is reduced to 5-10% for mid-central collisions.
- STAR v2 results from multi-particle methods (four-particle cumulant and Lee-Yang Zero) are smaller than PHENIX v2{EP} by 10-12%, with no pT dependence.
- The event plane resolution factors for v2 and v4 measurements using the PHENIX RXN detector were obtained for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV.
- PHENIX collected approximately 5 × 10^8 minimum-bias Au+Au events at √sNN = 62.4 GeV and approximately 2 × 10^8 minimum-bias Au+Au events at √sNN = 39 GeV in Run 2010.
- The differential elliptic flow for charged hadrons increases by almost 50% from the top SPS energy of 17.3 GeV to √sNN = 62 GeV at RHIC, based on comparison with CERES results (Agakichiev 2003).
- The energy density increases by approximately 30% over the range √sNN = 62.4 to 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous PHENIX flow measurements at √sNN = 200 GeV (Adare 2006, Taranenko 2007, Issah 2008, Shimomura 2009) and provides a comprehensive comparison with results from PHOBOS (Alver 2007) and STAR (Adams 2004, Abelev 2007, Shi 2009) at RHIC energies. The paper also discusses the status of ongoing flow analysis at lower beam energies (39 and 62.4 GeV) collected in Run 2010, which will enable detailed measurements of differential v2 and v4 as functions of pT and centrality for several particle species, and will help constrain the specific shear viscosity (η/s) of the quark-gluon plasma through comparisons with viscous hydrodynamic simulations (Romatschke 2007, Heinz 2009, Lacey 2010).
