# Analytic SU(N) Skyrmions at finite Baryon density

> We construct analytic (3+1)-dimensional Skyrmions living at finite Baryon density in the SU(N) Skyrme model that are not trivial embeddings of SU(2) into SU(N). We used Euler angles decomposition for arbitrary N and the generalized hedgehog Ansatz at finite Baryon density. The Skyrmions of high topological charge that we find represent smooth Baryonic layers whose properties can be computed explicitly. In particular, we determine the energy to Baryon charge ratio for any N showing the smoothness of the large N limit. The closeness to the BPS bound of these configurations can also be analyzed. The energy density profiles of these finite density Skyrmions have \textit{lasagna-like} shape in agreement with recent experimental findings. The shear modulus can be precisely estimated as well and our analytical result is close to recent numerical studies in the literature.

## Key claim

The authors construct analytic (3+1)-dimensional SU(N) Skyrmions at finite baryon density that are not trivial embeddings of SU(2) into SU(N), but the solutions are only local solutions of the differential equations and require additional global conditions, specifically periodicity of the matrix k and correct coordinate ranges, to yield a well-defined topological charge.

## Method

The authors use an Euler angles decomposition for SU(N) combined with a generalized hedgehog Ansatz at finite baryon density, employing a metric with three spatial coordinates r, gamma, and phi confined to a rectangular box. They reduce the Skyrme field equations to a system involving the Cartan subalgebra and simple roots of SU(N), solving for h(r) as a linear function ar v_epsilon where v_epsilon is determined by the inverse Cartan matrix and a set of signs epsilon_j. Global solutions are then classified into SU(2)-type, SO(3)-type, and genuine SU(N) solutions based on periodicity conditions and the behavior of the matrix k.

## Key numbers

- For SU(3) SO(3)-type solutions with |c_1| = |c_2| = 1/sqrt(2), the energy per baryon g_+ takes the form: g_+(m,underline(c)) = L_r L_gamma L_phi (K pi^3 / m) [ 4/L_phi^2 + 1/(8 L_r^2) + lambda/(16 L_phi^2 L_r^2) + (m^2/L_gamma^2)( 2 + lambda/(32 L_r^2) + lambda/(2 L_phi^2) ) ]
- For SU(3) SO(3)-type solutions, the baryon number B = 2m, with m a nonvanishing integer
- The period of exp(h_+(r)) for SU(3) is T_{h_+} = 2pi/a, and the period of exp(h_-(r)) is T_{h_-} = 6pi/a
- For SU(3), the matrix k has eigenvalues lambda_0 = 0 and lambda_± = ± i ||underline(c)||, giving period T_k = 2pi/||underline(c)||
- For SU(4) with c_i = 1, the eigenvalues of k are ± mu_+, ± mu_- with mu_± = (i/2)(sqrt(5) ± 1), and the ratio of periods T_+/T_- = (1/2)(3 + sqrt(5)) is irrational

## Context

This work extends previous analytic constructions of non-spherical Skyrmions at finite density by Canfora (2013) and collaborators, and generalizes earlier numerical studies of non-embedded SU(N) Skyrmions by Kopeliovich (1995), Din (1980), Ioannidou (1999), and Brihaye (2003). The results enable analytic computation of the energy per baryon ratio and shear modulus for lasagna-like nuclear pasta configurations, connecting to experimental findings on nuclear pasta phases and to numerical studies by Dorso (2018), da Silva Schneider (2018), Caplan (2018), and Nandi (2018).
