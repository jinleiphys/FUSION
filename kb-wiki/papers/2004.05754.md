# The QCD critical point from the Nambu-Jona-Lasino model with a scalar-vector interaction

> We study the critical point in the QCD phase diagram in the Nambu-Jona-Lasino (NJL) model by including a scalar-vector coupled interaction. We find that varying the strength of this interaction, which has no effect on the vacuum properties of QCD, can significantly affect the location of the critical point in the QCD phase diagram, particularly the value of the cirtical temperature. This provides a convenient way to use the NJL-based transport or hydrodynamic model to extract information about the QCD phase diagram from relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Varying the strength of the scalar-vector coupled interaction in the Nambu-Jona-Lasino (NJL) model, which has no effect on QCD vacuum properties, can significantly shift the location of the critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram, particularly the critical temperature, providing a flexible way to accommodate different possible critical point locations for comparison with heavy-ion collision data.

## Method

The authors extended the two-flavor and three-flavor NJL model, as well as the Polyakov-loop extended NJL (PNJL) model, by including an eight-fermion scalar-vector coupled interaction term in the Lagrangian density. Using the mean-field approximation, they solved the gap equations for quark condensates and effective masses, and the thermodynamic grand potential, to determine the first-order phase transition coexistence lines and the location of the critical endpoint as a function of the scalar-vector coupling constant \(G_{SV}\).

## Key numbers

- In the two-flavor NJL model with \(G_{SV}=0\), the critical point is located at temperature \(T \approx 36\) MeV and net quark number density \(\rho \approx 0.64\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- With \(G_{SV} = -200 \Lambda^{-8}\) (attractive scalar-vector interaction), the critical point shifts to \(T \approx 105.5\) MeV and \(\rho \approx 0.79\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- With \(G_{SV} = 100 \Lambda^{-8}\) (repulsive scalar-vector interaction), the critical point shifts to \(T \approx 14\) MeV and \(\rho \approx 0.32\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The two-flavor NJL model parameters used are: cutoff \(\Lambda = 651\) MeV, scalar coupling \(G_S \Lambda^2 = 2.135\), current quark mass \(m_{u,d} = 5.5\) MeV, yielding vacuum quark mass \(M_{u,d} = 325.1\) MeV and condensate \(\langle\bar{u}u\rangle^{1/3} = -251.3\) MeV.
- The three-flavor NJL model parameters used are: \(\Lambda = 631.4\) MeV, \(G_S \Lambda^2 = 1.835\), \(K \Lambda^5 = 9.29\), \(m_{u,d} = 5.5\) MeV, \(m_s = 135.7\) MeV, yielding vacuum masses \(M_{u,d} = 335\) MeV and \(M_s = 527\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the QCD phase diagram using NJL-type models (Buballa 2005, Stephanov 2004) by demonstrating that the scalar-vector coupled interaction, previously considered in the context of nuclear matter saturation (Koch 1987) and in earlier NJL studies (Kashiwa 2006, Osipov 2005, Osipov 2006), provides a flexible parameter to shift the critical point temperature from about 14 MeV to over 105 MeV. This enables NJL-based transport or hydrodynamic models to explore a wide range of possible critical point locations for comparison with experimental data from relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and brings the NJL/PNJL predictions closer to those from lattice QCD (Ejiri 2003, Fodor 2004, Datta 2012, Karsch 2015, Bazavov 2018), Dyson-Schwinger equation (Fischer 2014, Gao 2015), and functional renormalization group methods (Fu 2019, Gao 2020).
