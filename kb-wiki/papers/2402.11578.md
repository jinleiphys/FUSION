# QCD Kondo effect for single heavy quark in chiral-symmetry broken phase

> We consider the quantum chromodynamics (QCD) Kondo effect for a single heavy quark in quark matter composed of light quarks with chiral symmetry breaking. Introducing several spinor structures in QCD Kondo condensates, i.e., particle-projected condensate, antiparticle-projected condensate, and normal condensate without projection, we calculate the attractive energy gained by the heavy quark within the mean-field approximation in the path-integral formalism. We show that the normal condensate is favored at low density and the particle-projected condensate is favored at high density, when the light quark has a nonzero mass. We interpret such a density-dependent transition between the two condensates in terms of the Kondo resonances.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for a single heavy quark in quark matter with massive light quarks (chiral-symmetry broken phase), the normal (N+) QCD Kondo condensate is favored at low density while the particle-projected (P±) condensate is favored at high density, with a density-dependent transition between them, a result that depends on the light quark having nonzero mass and on the momentum cutoff parameter.

## Method

The authors introduce a Lagrangian for a single heavy quark interacting with light quarks via scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axialvector channels mimicking one-gluon exchange, with the heavy quark constrained to a single impurity at the origin. They apply the mean-field approximation in the path-integral formalism to compute the impurity energy (energy decrease from Kondo condensate formation) for three types of condensates: normal condensates (N±) containing both particle and antiparticle components, particle-projected condensates (P±) containing only the particle component, and antiparticle-projected condensates (AP±). The free energy is minimized to determine which condensate is favored as a function of chemical potential and light quark mass.

## Key numbers

- For light quark mass m = 0.4 GeV and cutoff Λ = 1 GeV, the N+ condensate is favored for chemical potential μ ≲ 0.65 GeV, while the P± condensate becomes favored for μ ≳ 0.65 GeV.
- For the larger cutoff Λ = 1.2 GeV with m = 0.4 GeV, the N+ condensate is favored for μ ≲ 0.8 GeV, and the P± condensate becomes favored for μ ≳ 0.8 GeV.
- The coupling constant is set to GΛ² = 4.3, with momentum cutoffs of Λ = 1 GeV and Λ = 1.2 GeV used in the calculations.
- For massless quarks (m = 0 GeV), the P± condensates are more favored than the N± condensates for any chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends the study of the QCD Kondo effect for a single heavy quark from the massless light quark case studied by Yasui (2016) to the more realistic case of massive light quarks with chiral symmetry breaking. It also complements previous work by Suzuki et al. (2017) which investigated chiral symmetry breaking effects for heavy quarks distributed uniformly in space (the Kondo lattice), whereas this paper treats an isolated single heavy quark. The results enable future studies of the competition or coexistence of different condensate types and their role in heavy-ion collision phenomenology where heavy quarks are produced in small numbers.
