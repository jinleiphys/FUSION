# Multivariate cumulants in flow analyses: The Next Generation

> We reconcile for the first time the strict mathematical formalism of multivariate cumulants with the usage of cumulants in anisotropic flow analyses in high-energy nuclear collisions. This reconciliation yields to the next generation of estimators to be used in flow analyses. We review all fundamental properties of multivariate cumulants and use them as a foundation to establish two simple necessary conditions to determine whether some multivariate random variable is a multivariate cumulant in the basis they are expressed in. We argue that properties of cumulants are preserved only for the stochastic variables on which the cumulant expansion has been performed directly, and if there are no underlying symmetries due to which some terms in the cumulant expansion are identically zero. We illustrate one possibility of new multivariate cumulants of azimuthal angles by defining them event-by-event and by keeping all non-isotropic terms in the cumulant expansion. Further, we introduce new cumulants of flow amplitudes named Asymmetric Cumulants, which generalize recently introduced Symmetric Cumulants for the case when flow amplitudes are raised to different powers. Finally, we present the new concept of Cumulants of Symmetry Plane Correlations and provide the first realisation for the lowest orders. The new estimators can be used directly to constrain the multivariate probability density function of flow fluctuations, since its functional form can be reconstructed only from its true moments or cumulants. The new definition for cumulants of azimuthal angles enables separation of nonflow and flow contributions, and offers first insights into how the combinatorial background contributes for small multiplicities to flow measurements with correlation techniques. All the presented results are supported by Monte Carlo studies using state-of-the-art models.

## Key claim

The paper reconciles the strict mathematical formalism of multivariate cumulants with their usage in anisotropic flow analyses for the first time, but only under the condition that cumulant expansion is performed directly on the stochastic variables being studied and that all non-isotropic terms in the cumulant expansion are kept. The authors argue that most estimators previously called "cumulants" in the field fail to satisfy fundamental mathematical properties of cumulants.

## Method

The authors review all fundamental properties of multivariate cumulants and establish two simple necessary conditions to test whether a candidate multivariate random variable is a true multivariate cumulant: statistical independence (the expression must reduce to zero when all variables are independent) and a combined reduction/semi-invariance/homogeneity condition. They then systematically test all existing estimators in the field that have been named cumulants against these conditions, including cumulants of azimuthal angles, cumulants of Q-vectors and flow amplitudes, asymmetric cumulants from ATLAS, subevent cumulants in pseudorapidity, and multiparticle mixed-harmonics cumulants. For the estimators that fail, they introduce new alternative definitions that satisfy all fundamental properties, including new event-by-event cumulants of azimuthal angles, Asymmetric Cumulants of flow amplitudes, and Cumulants of Symmetry Plane Correlations.

## Key numbers

- The four-particle cumulant estimator c_n{4} defined as ⟨⟨exp[in(φ1+φ2-φ3-φ4)]⟩⟩ - ⟨⟨exp[in(φ1-φ3)]⟩⟩⟨⟨exp[in(φ2-φ4)]⟩⟩ - ⟨⟨exp[in(φ1-φ4)]⟩⟩⟨⟨exp[in(φ2-φ3)]⟩⟩ fails both necessary conditions for a multivariate cumulant
- The univariate cumulant definitions c_n{2} ≡ ⟨v_n²⟩, c_n{4} ≡ ⟨v_n⁴⟩ - 2⟨v_n²⟩², c_n{6} ≡ ⟨v_n⁶⟩ - 9⟨v_n⁴⟩⟨v_n²⟩ + 12⟨v_n²⟩³, and c_n{8} ≡ ⟨v_n⁸⟩ - 16⟨v_n⁶⟩⟨v_n²⟩ - 18⟨v_n⁴⟩² + 144⟨v_n⁴⟩⟨v_n²⟩² - 144⟨v_n²⟩⁴ all fail the Semi-invariance property for both v_n and v_n² as the stochastic variable
- The ATLAS "asymmetric cumulant" ac_n{3} ≡ ⟨⟨e^(i(nφ1+nφ2-2nφ3))⟩⟩ violates both necessary conditions and violates Theorem 1 on multivariate cumulants from Kubo (1962)

## Context

This work extends the new paradigm established by Mordasini (2019), which demonstrated that cumulant expansion cannot be performed in one set of stochastic variables and then transformed to a new set of variables without losing all fundamental properties of cumulants. The paper contradicts the traditional approach used in flow analyses since the seminal papers by Borghini, Ollitrault, and collaborators (2000-2001), which defined cumulants of azimuthal angles using all-event averages and dropped non-isotropic terms. The new estimators enable direct constraint of the multivariate probability density function of flow fluctuations and offer separation of nonflow and flow contributions, with first insights into how combinatorial background contributes for small multiplicities to flow measurements with correlation techniques.
