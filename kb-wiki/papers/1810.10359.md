# Azimuthal angle dependence of the longitudinal spin polarization in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The azimuthal angle dependence of quark spin polarization in the longitudinal beam direction of non-central relativistic heavy ion collisions is studied in the chiral kinetic approach. Contrary to the prediction from models based on the assumption of thermal equilibrium of spin degrees of freedom that the quark spin polarization always points along the direction of local vorticity field, we find the two can have opposite directions due to the effect from the transversal component of vorticity field, which can lead to a redistribution of axial charges in the produced matter. Our finding is consistent with the azimuthal angle dependence of the longitudinal spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons, which is mainly determined by that of the strange quark, recently measured in the experiments by the STAR Collaboration at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC).

## Key claim

The paper claims that the azimuthal angle dependence of the longitudinal spin polarization of quarks (and thus Λ hyperons) can have an opposite direction to that of the local vorticity field, contrary to predictions from models assuming thermal equilibrium of spin degrees of freedom, due to the effect of the transverse components of the vorticity field causing a redistribution of axial charges.

## Method

The authors used the chiral kinetic approach to describe the dynamics of massless and massive quarks in a vorticity field, with initial phase-space distributions taken from the AMPT event generator for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV and impact parameter b = 8.87 fm (30-40% centrality). They solved the chiral kinetic equations of motion for quarks including scatterings with a constant cross section of 10 mb, while the local vorticity field was calculated from the flow field obtained by averaging quark velocities in cells of size Δx = Δy = 0.5 fm and Δη = 0.2.

## Key numbers

- The average vorticity magnitude is more than 10^21 s^-1 (from prior work cited in the introduction).
- The longitudinal spin polarization of quarks with momenta satisfying p_x p_y > 0 due to ω_x alone is of the order of 10^-2.
- The longitudinal spin polarization due to ω_y alone is also of the order of 10^-2.
- The longitudinal spin polarization due to ω_z alone is of the order of 10^-3.
- The sign of the total longitudinal spin polarization for quarks with p_x p_y > 0 changes from negative to positive after about 5 fm/c.
- The quark scattering cross section used is 10 mb.
- The cell size for flow field averaging is Δx = Δy = 0.5 fm and Δη = 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the chiral kinetic approach previously applied to global spin polarization (Sun and Ko, 2017) to the local azimuthal angle dependence of longitudinal spin polarization. It addresses the discrepancy between experimental results from the STAR Collaboration (Niida, 2018) showing a quadrupole pattern in Λ hyperon longitudinal polarization and predictions from hydrodynamic models (PhysRevLett.120.012302, PhysRevC.98.024905) that assumed thermal equilibrium of spin degrees of freedom. The study enables future work on how the Λ polarization is affected during hadronic evolution after hadronization.
