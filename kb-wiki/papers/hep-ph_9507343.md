# The Return of The Prodigal Goldstone Boson

> We propose that the mass of the $η^\prime$ meson is a particularly sensitive probe of the properties of finite energy density hadronic matter and quark gluon plasma. We argue that the mass of the $η^\prime$ excitation in hot and dense matter should be small, and therefore that the $η^\prime$ production cross section should be much increased relative to that for pp collisions. This may have observable consequences in dilepton and diphoton experiments.

## Key claim

The mass of the η′ meson should decrease rapidly with increasing energy density of hadronic matter, potentially becoming nearly mass-degenerate with the octet of pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons in the quark-gluon plasma, leading to a large enhancement of η′ production in heavy-ion collisions. The authors hedge that the enhancement factor could range from a modest value of 3 up to an absolute upper bound of 50, depending on the strange quark mass effects and the specific quark content of the η′ at high density.

## Method

The authors use the Witten-Veneziano mechanism, relating the η′ mass to the topological susceptibility of the QCD vacuum via instanton-induced ghost fields, and argue that instanton density vanishes at high energy density as α_S → 0. They compute η′ production cross sections using the Hagedorn model, estimate η′ annihilation cross sections with pions using an effective Lagrangian with ρ-meson exchange, and compare predicted dilepton and diphoton yields to data from the CERES and WA80 experiments.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum η′ mass is nearly 1 GeV; the bare η′ mass (without ghost mixing) is estimated as m₀ ≈ 400 MeV from the formula m₀² = (1/3)(2m_K² + m_π²).
- The expected η′/π⁰ production ratio in pp collisions is σ_η′/σ_π⁰ ∼ 2 × 10⁻² from the Hagedorn model.
- The η′–ρ–ρ coupling constant extracted from η′ → ργ decay is g_η′ρρ = 3.96 × 10⁻³/MeV, corresponding to g_η′ρρ² = 6.10 mb.
- At T = 150 MeV, the η′–π annihilation cross section is σ₀ = 2.6 mb, giving a mean free path of 12 fm.
- The η′ lifetime in vacuum is about 1000 fm/c.
- The η → π⁰ ratio in central S+Au collisions measured by WA80 is 0.54 ± 0.14, compared to 0.29 ± 0.13 in minimum bias events.
- The branching ratio of η′ → η is 65%.

## Context

This paper extends the theoretical framework of Witten (1979) and Veneziano (1979) on the role of the U(1) anomaly and topological charge in giving the η′ its large mass, and the earlier work of Dyakonov and Eides on the ghost pole in QCD. It proposes that the vanishing of instanton effects at high energy density, previously discussed by Gross, Pisarski, and Yaffe (1981) and by Dyakonov and Mirlin (1988), should lead to observable signals in dilepton and diphoton experiments at CERN (CERES, HELIOS, WA80). The work enables quantitative predictions for η′ production in heavy-ion collisions as a probe of the quark-gluon plasma and suggests that the η′ mass shift itself may not be directly observable, only the enhanced production cross section.
