# Exact description of non-Markovian effect in open quantum system: the discretized environment method

> An approach, called discretized environment method, is introduced to treat exactly non-Markovian effects in open quantum systems. In this approach, a complex environment described by a spectral function is mapped into a finite set of discretized states with an appropriate coupling to the system of interest. The finite set of system plus environment degrees of freedom are then explicitly followed in time leading to a quasi-exact description. The present approach is anticipated to be particularly accurate in the low temperature and strongly non-Markovian regime. The discretized environment method is validated on a two-level system (qubit) coupled to a bosonic or fermionic heat bath. A perfect agreement with the quantum Langevin approach is found. Further illustrations are made on a three-level system (qutrit) coupled to a bosonic heat-bath. Emerging processes due to strong memory effects are discussed.

# Exact description of non-Markovian effect in open quantum system: the discretized environment method

## Key claim

The discretized environment method (DEM) provides a quasi-exact deterministic description of non-Markovian dynamics in open quantum systems by mapping a continuous spectral function onto a finite set of discretized environment states, and is anticipated to be particularly accurate in the low temperature and strongly non-Markovian regime, the opposite limit from where most standard methods become simple.

## Method

The environment, characterized by a spectral function J(ω), is discretized into N_max uniformly spaced frequency states with spacing Δω and maximal frequency ω_max, with coupling constants g_ν = ℏ √(Δω J(ω_ν)). The full system-plus-environment Hamiltonian is then directly diagonalized in a reduced Hilbert space of dimension (N_S + N_E), where N_S is the number of system states and N_E the number of environment states. Time evolution is obtained analytically from the diagonalized Hamiltonian via the Heisenberg picture, giving access to occupation probabilities and two-time correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- For a two-level system with Lorentz-Drude spectral function at γ/Ω = 12 and k_B T/(ℏΩ) = 1, a cutoff frequency ω_max/Ω = 10 with N_max = 250 equidistant environment states yields converged results.
- The dissipative kernel for the Lorentz-Drude spectral function is K(t) = (g_0/2) γ e^{-γ t} + i (g_0/π) γ² ∫₀^∞ dω sin(ω t)/(γ² + ω²).
- The decay properties are unchanged from (ℏγ/k_B T) = 12 down to (ℏγ/k_B T) ≈ 2, and begin to be modified for lower γ values as the system enters the strongly non-Markovian regime.
- For a three-level system at k_B T/(ℏΩ) = 1.0 with γ/Ω = 12, the upper level occupation shows marked reduction and coherent oscillations compared to the two-level case when coupling strength g_0 = 0.1 and 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the quantum Langevin approach of Vaz (2014) and the Quantum State Diffusion method of Jin (2010) by providing a deterministic alternative that avoids stochastic equations and can handle finite temperature. The DEM is validated against the quantum Langevin approach for two-level bosonic and fermionic systems, and is applied to a three-level system previously studied at zero temperature by Jin (2010) using QSD. The method enables treatment of strongly non-Markovian regimes where γ is small, which is the limit where standard techniques become difficult, and can be extended to more complex systems with multiple levels.
