# Relativistic Hydrodynamics for Heavy--Ion Collisions -- II. Compression of Nuclear Matter and the Phase Transition to the Quark--Gluon Plasma

> We investigate the compression of nuclear matter in relativistic hydrodynamics. Nuclear matter is described by a $σ-ω$--type model for the hadron matter phase and by the MIT bag model for the quark--gluon plasma, with a first order phase transition between both phases. In the presence of phase transitions, hydrodynamical solutions change qualitatively, for instance, one-dimensional stationary compression is no longer accomplished by a single shock but via a sequence of shock and compressional simple waves. We construct the analytical solution to the ``slab-on-slab'' collision problem over a range of incident velocities. The performance of numerical algorithms to solve relativistic hydrodynamics is then investigated for this particular test case. Consequences for the early compressional stage in heavy--ion collisions are pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for nuclear matter undergoing a first-order phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma, one-dimensional stationary compression is not accomplished by a single shock but by a sequence of shocks and compressional simple waves, with the specific structure depending on the incident velocity relative to the Chapman-Jouguet point and the inflection point of the Poisson adiabat.

## Method

The authors constructed a nuclear matter equation of state using a modified sigma-omega model for the hadronic phase (with parameters yielding ground state incompressibility 300 MeV and effective nucleon mass 0.635 times the vacuum mass) and the MIT bag model with bag constant B = (235 MeV)^4 for the quark-gluon plasma phase, connected via Gibbs phase equilibrium conditions. They then analytically solved the relativistic hydrodynamic "slab-on-slab" collision problem for semi-infinite nuclear matter over a range of incident velocities, and tested the SHASTA and relativistic HLLE numerical algorithms against these analytical solutions using a tabulated version of the equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Ground state baryon density: n0 = 0.15891 fm^-3
- Ground state binding energy: B0 = 16 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass in ground state: M*_0 = 0.635 M (where M = 938 MeV)
- Ground state incompressibility: K0 = 300 MeV
- Bag constant: B = (235 MeV)^4
- Phase transition temperature at vanishing baryon density: Tc ≈ 169 MeV
- Chapman-Jouguet point: p_CJ ≈ 1.941 ε0, X_CJ ≈ 0.422 X0, ε_CJ ≈ 6.971 ε0, n_CJ ≈ 4.596 n0, specific entropy σ ≈ 3.545
- CM velocity to reach CJ point: v_CJ ≈ 0.752
- Point B on generalized shock adiabat: p_B ≈ 2.478 ε0, X_B ≈ 0.198 X0, ε_B ≈ 18.271 ε0, n_B ≈ 10.236 n0
- Point C on generalized shock adiabat: p_C = 2.703 ε0, X_C = 0.195 X0, ε_C = 18.944 ε0, n_C = 10.525 n0
- CM velocity to reach point C: v_C ≈ 0.831

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Bugaev (1995) by constructing explicit analytical compression solutions for a more realistic nuclear matter equation of state based on the sigma-omega model, rather than the phenomenological equations of state used previously. The authors had earlier tested the SHASTA and relativistic HLLE algorithms for expansion into vacuum (Rischke, 1995), and here they validate these algorithms for compression scenarios with phase transitions. The results enable reliable numerical simulations of the early compressional stage in heavy-ion collisions and provide a benchmark for testing hydrodynamic codes in the presence of first-order phase transitions to the quark-gluon plasma.
