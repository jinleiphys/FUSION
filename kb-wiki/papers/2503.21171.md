# Are Neutron Stars Rich in H-dibaryons?

> The possible existence of an H-dibaryon near the $Λ-Λ$ threshold has still not been decided experimentally. This raises the question of the potential effects on neutron stars if it does exist. We explore the consequences within the quark-meson coupling model, using the excluded volume formalism. While the H is abundant in heavy stars the maximum mass is only lowered slightly by its presence.

# Are Neutron Stars Rich in H-dibaryons?

## Key claim
Within the quark-meson coupling model using the excluded volume formalism, the H-dibaryon can be successfully incorporated with natural couplings to the sigma and omega fields, and while the H is highly abundant in heavy neutron stars, the maximum mass is only slightly lowered by its presence, but only if the H mass lies near the Λ-Λ threshold.

## Method
The authors model neutron star matter in β-equilibrium at zero temperature using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model with an excluded volume correction. The H-dibaryon is treated as a spinless boson with minimal coupling to the ω field (gω,H = 4/3 gω,N by quark counting) and its effective mass is computed from the scalar polarisability of the 6-quark bag, using a free bag radius RHfree = 1.2 × RNfree. The H is included alongside nucleons and hyperons (Λ, Ξ0, Ξ−), with the excluded volume assigned as twice that of other baryons (hard-core radius r = 0.45 fm). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations then yield mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers
- H-dibaryon masses considered: MH = [2247, 2258, 2269] MeV, as suggested by Shanahan (2013)
- Current experimental lower limit on H mass: MH > 2224 MeV (Takahashi, 2001)
- Λ is the first hyperon to appear at just above 3 n0 in QMC
- Central densities of heavy neutron stars: 0.8–1.0 fm−3
- Tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses: Λ1.4 = 560 (unchanged by H presence)
- Excluded volume hard-core radius: r = 0.45 fm

## Context
This work extends prior QMC studies of hyperonic neutron stars by Leong (2023) and Motta (2020), which established the Λ threshold and the excluded volume treatment. It contrasts with earlier phenomenological approaches by Glendenning (1998) and Faessler (1997) that fitted H-meson couplings to match observations, whereas QMC predicts these couplings without free parameters. The results enable future comparisons with neutron star observations such as PSR J0740+6620 (Riley, 2021; Fonseca, 2021) and suggest that if the H exists near the Λ-Λ threshold, the cores of the heaviest neutron stars would contain a sizable H-matter condensate.
