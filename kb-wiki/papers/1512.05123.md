# Electromagnetic selection rules in the triangular alpha-cluster model of 12C

> After recapitulating the procedure to find the bands and the states occurring in the $\mathcal{D}_{3h}$ alpha-cluster model of $^{12}$C in which the clusters are placed at the vertexes of an equilateral triangle, we obtain the selection rules for electromagnetic transitions. While the alpha cluster structure leads to the cancellation of E1 transitions, the approximations carried out in deriving the roto-vibrational hamiltonian lead to the disappearance of M1 transitions. Furthermore, although in general the lowest active modes are E2, E3, $\cdots$ and M2, M3, $\cdots$, the cancellation of M2, M3 and M5 transitions between certain bands also occurs, as a result of the application of group theoretical techniques drawn from molecular physics. These implications can be very relevant for the spectroscopic analysis of $γ$-ray spectra of $^{12}$C.

## Key claim

While the alpha cluster structure of 12C leads to the cancellation of E1 transitions, the approximations used in deriving the roto-vibrational Hamiltonian cause the disappearance of M1 transitions; additionally, M2, M3, and M5 transitions between certain bands are also cancelled as a result of applying group theoretical techniques from molecular physics, meaning the selection rules differ from those expected from the spherical shell model or the collective model.

## Method

The authors applied the D3h point-group symmetry of three alpha particles at the vertices of an equilateral triangle to classify the roto-vibrational states of 12C, using the characters of irreducible representations for vibrational modes (breathing mode A1' and doubly-degenerate bending mode E') and rotational states (Wigner D matrices). They then determined the transformation properties of electric and magnetic multipole operators under D3h operations, constructing character tables for operators up to λ=6, and applied the vanishing integral rule to derive selection rules for electromagnetic transitions between allowed states.

## Key numbers

- The non-degenerate breathing mode (ω1) transforms as A1', while the doubly-degenerate bending mode (ω2) transforms as E'
- For J=0, K=0, the rotational state transforms as A1'; for J=1, K=0 as A2'; for J=1, K=1 as E''
- For n2=0, the ground state band contains states: 0+, 2+, 3-, 4±, 5-, 6±,+ (up to J=6)
- For n2=1 (E' vibrational character), the band contains: 1-, 2±, 3±, 4±,+, 5±,±, 6±,± (up to J=6)
- The electric dipole operator transforms as A2'' ⊕ E' under D3h
- The magnetic dipole operator transforms as A2' ⊕ E'' under D3h
- For electric multipoles up to λ=6, the μ=0 component transforms as A1' for even λ and A2'' for odd λ
- For magnetic multipoles up to λ=6, the μ=0 component transforms as A1'' for even λ and A2' for odd λ

## Context

This work extends the Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM) developed by Bijker and Iachello (2000) for 12C, which had previously concentrated on the energy spectrum while paying little attention to electromagnetic transitions. The paper provides selection rules that are fundamentally different from those based on the spherical shell model or the collective model, and are instead familiar from molecular physics applications to X3 structures (Bijker et al., 1995). These results enable spectroscopic analysis of gamma-ray spectra from 12C, particularly relevant to recent experimental work by the Birmingham group (Marin-Lambarri et al., 2014) and others (Gai, 2015; Kokalova, 2014).
