# Parton energy loss at LHC tests for a strongly coupled medium

> We construct a measure of transverse momentum loss of jets in nuclear collisions at LHC directly using measurements of jet cross sections in PbPb and pp collisions. The proposal is shown to be equivalent to R_AA and is equally straightforward to construct. Using data from the ATLAS collaboration at two different collision energies, we show that the proposed measure has small statistical uncertainties. We argue that systematic errors can be easily improved over our estimates by the experimental collaboration to such an extent that it directly probes whether the jet-medium interaction is due to a strongly interacting medium or a weakly interacting plasma. We argue that the current data may marginally favour a strongly interacting medium. On the other hand, assuming that the medium is weakly interacting, we are able to provide estimates of the jet quenching parameter qhat which are in rough agreement with previously reported estimates.

# Parton energy loss at LHC tests for a strongly coupled medium

## Key claim
The authors propose that a directly measurable transverse momentum shift (ΔpT) of jets in PbPb collisions relative to pp collisions can distinguish between a strongly coupled and weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma, with current LHC data marginally favouring a strongly coupled medium, but only if systematic uncertainties are reduced by accounting for covariances between error sources.

## Method
The authors construct ΔpT by equating the differential jet cross section in PbPb collisions at a given transverse momentum pT to the pp cross section at pT+ΔpT, using published ATLAS jet cross sections at √S=2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV. They test scaling relations: for a strongly coupled medium, ΔpT ∝ L³ (where L is the jet path length through the fireball), while for a weakly coupled medium, ΔpT ∝ L². They also extract the jet quenching parameter q̂ from a BDMPS-Z formalism assuming weak coupling.

## Key numbers
- Statistical errors of ATLAS jet cross sections in PbPb at √S=2.76 TeV: 1.5–2.5% for pT up to 200 GeV, growing to ~10% for pT>350 GeV; systematic uncertainties ~15–20%.
- Statistical errors for pp jets at √S=2.76 TeV are smaller by a factor of 3, with systematic uncertainties ~3%.
- At √S=5.02 TeV, pp systematic uncertainties ~2%, but statistical errors are 10–15%; PbPb statistical errors range 2–5% for pT≤200 GeV, rising to 10–80% at higher pT.
- Mean path length L in most central 0–10% PbPb events: 4.9 fm (using R₉₉ = 1.53 R_RMS).
- Extracted q̂ assuming weak coupling: 1–4 GeV/fm² (0.2–0.8 GeV²/fm), consistent with prior estimates of ~10±4 GeV/fm² (JET Collaboration, 2013), ~12.4 GeV/fm² (Mehtar-Tani, 2021), and ~3–7 GeV/fm² (JETSCAPE, 2021).
- Using R_RMS instead gives q̂ = 2.3–9.4 GeV/fm².

## Context
This work extends prior proposals for measuring jet energy loss via momentum imbalance between multiple objects (Gupta, 1994; Wang, 1996; Brewer, 2018) by instead using inclusive jet cross sections, which have smaller statistical uncertainties. The scaling tests for strong vs. weak coupling build on earlier attempts (Horowitz, 2007; Jia, 2011; Betz, 2014). The results enable direct experimental discrimination between strongly coupled (AdS/CFT-inspired) and weakly coupled (BDMPS-Z) models of the quark-gluon plasma, and provide a method for experimental collaborations to extract ΔpT and ΔE with reduced systematic uncertainties.
