# Lyapunov instability and finite size effects in a system with long-range forces

> We study the largest Lyapunov exponent $λ$ and the finite size effects of a system of N fully-coupled classical particles, which shows a second order phase transition. Slightly below the critical energy density $U_c$, $λ$ shows a peak which persists for very large N-values (N=20000). We show, both numerically and analytically, that chaoticity is strongly related to kinetic energy fluctuations. In the limit of small energy, $λ$ goes to zero with a N-independent power law: $λ\sim \sqrt{U}$. In the continuum limit the system is integrable in the whole high temperature phase. More precisely, the behavior $λ\sim N^{-1/3}$ is found numerically for $U > U_c$ and justified on the basis of a random matrix approximation.

## Key claim

The largest Lyapunov exponent (LLE) in a system of N fully-coupled classical particles with long-range forces shows a peak just below the critical energy density U_c = 0.75, and this peak persists for very large N (up to N=20000), contradicting earlier suggestions that the peak might grow with N.

## Method

The authors numerically integrated the equations of motion and linearized equations for N classical particles moving on a unit circle with ferromagnetic long-range interactions (the Hamiltonian mean-field model). They used fourth-order symplectic algorithms with time step Δt = 0.2 and energy conservation error below ΔE/E = 10^{-5}, computing the largest Lyapunov exponent via the standard method of Benettin et al. for system sizes from N=100 to N=20000, averaging over approximately 10^6 time steps.

## Key numbers

- Critical energy density: U_c = (E/N)_c = 0.75
- For U → 0, the Lyapunov exponent scales as λ ∼ U^{0.5} (power law with exponent α = 0.5), with no dependence on system size
- For U > U_c, the Lyapunov exponent scales as λ ∼ N^{-1/3}, found numerically and justified by a random matrix approximation
- Peak in Lyapunov exponent occurs at U ∼ 0.67, just below the critical energy
- Largest system size simulated: N = 20000

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Lyapunov exponents in finite-size systems showing phase transitions, particularly the Hamiltonian mean-field model studied by Ruffo (1995) and earlier LLE calculations for moderate sizes (N ≈ 100) by Yama (1995). The results confirm a connection between chaoticity and kinetic energy fluctuations, and show that in the continuum limit (N → ∞) the system becomes integrable in the high-temperature phase (U > U_c). The findings are relevant for nuclear multifragmentation studies where 100-200 particles interact via long-range nuclear and Coulomb forces, and for understanding the relation between microcanonical and canonical ensembles near phase transitions.
