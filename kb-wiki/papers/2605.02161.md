# Relativistic Feshbach-Villars Equation for Two Spin-$0$ Particles

> The Feshbach-Villars version of the relativistic quantum mechanics can be extended for two-body systems in such a way that the center-of-mass motion is separated off. The procedure results in an equation of Feshbach-Villars-type in terms of the relative coordinate.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Feshbach-Villars (FV) formalism, originally a two-component reformulation of the Klein-Gordon equation for a single spin-0 particle, to two-body systems of spin-0 particles, showing that the center-of-mass motion can be separated off exactly, resulting in an effective FV equation in the relative coordinate. The hedge is that this separation works because the FV equation is a genuine eigenvalue equation for the energy, unlike the Klein-Gordon equation, allowing the total energy of non-interacting particles to be expressed as the sum of subsystem energies.

## Method

The author writes the FV Hamiltonian for two non-interacting spin-0 particles as the sum of single-particle FV Hamiltonians, then introduces interactions that depend only on the relative coordinate. The kinetic energy is expressed in center-of-mass and relative coordinates, and the center-of-mass term is eliminated by moving to the center-of-mass frame. The resulting relative-motion Hamiltonian is solved using a Lippmann-Schwinger equation, where the long-range part (including the Coulomb potential) is treated via a matrix continued fraction in a Coulomb-Sturmian basis, and the short-range part is approximated in a finite basis.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of the \(p-\pi^{-}\) system (1s state, \(l=0\)): FV0 result is \(-118.883080\) atomic units (a.u.), compared to KG0 result \(-118.890102\) a.u.
* Binding energy of the \(p-\pi^{-}\) system (2s state, \(l=0\)): FV0 result is \(-29.7206924\) a.u., compared to KG0 result \(-29.7218331\) a.u.
* Binding energy of the \(\pi^{+}-\pi^{-}\) system (1s state, \(l=0\)): FV0 result is \(-68.2842463\) a.u., compared to KG0 result \(-68.2876557\) a.u.
* Binding energy of the \(\pi^{+}-\pi^{-}\) system (2s state, \(l=0\)): FV0 result is \(-17.0709623\) a.u., compared to KG0 result \(-17.0715161\) a.u.
* Masses used: proton mass \(m_p = 1836.15267 m_e\), pion mass \(m_\pi = 273.13244 m_e\), fine-structure constant \(\alpha \simeq 1/137.035999177\).

## Context

This work extends the Feshbach-Villars formalism (Feshbach and Villars, 1958) from single-particle to two-body systems, building on the author's prior solution method for the single-particle FV equation (Motamedi and Papp, 2019; Brown and Papp, 2016) which used a Lippmann-Schwinger equation and matrix continued fractions. The key advance is that the center-of-mass motion separates naturally, unlike in the Klein-Gordon equation where the energy is not additive; this enables a pathway toward a consistent relativistic few-body theory. The numerical results for pionic hydrogen and pionium show small differences between the FV and KG treatments, attributed to the different mass scales (total mass \(M\) vs. reduced mass \(\mu\)) in the rest-energy term.
