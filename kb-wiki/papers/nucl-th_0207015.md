# Theoretical understanding of pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions - a status report

> A status report is given for the current theoretical understanding of pion production close to threshold. In the first part of the talk predictions of a meson exchange model are compared to recent polarized data for the reactions $pp \to ppπ^0$ as well as $pp\to pnπ^+$ revealing, that the former reaction is badly described whereas the predictions for the latter turn out to be consistent with the data. Recent progress in the application of chiral perturbation theory allows to understand this difference.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the long-standing discrepancy between theoretical models and experimental data for near-threshold pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions is channel-dependent: the reaction \(pp \to pp\pi^0\) remains badly described by existing meson-exchange models, whereas \(pp \to pn\pi^+\) and \(pp \to d\pi^+\) are well under control, and chiral perturbation theory provides a natural explanation for this difference.

## Method

The author compares predictions from a meson-exchange model (including direct one-body terms, pion rescattering with a microscopic virtual-pion interaction, and an effective short-range parameter adjusted to the total cross section of \(pp \to pp\pi^0\) near threshold) to recent double-polarized data from IUCF for the reactions \(pp \to pp\pi^0\), \(pp \to pn\pi^+\), and \(pp \to d\pi^+\). The analysis is complemented by a chiral perturbation theory counting argument that examines the convergence of the expansion for pion s-waves and p-waves, focusing on the leading-order Weinberg-Tomazawa term and the role of loop contributions.

## Key numbers

- Existing models for the total cross section of \(pp \to pp\pi^0\) near threshold fell short by a factor of 5–10 when the first close-to-threshold data appeared in 1990.
- Total cross sections for \(pp \to pn\pi^+\) and \(pp \to d\pi^+\) could always be described within a factor of 2.
- In chiral perturbation theory, pion p-waves converge with only tree-level diagrams entering up to N\(^2\)LO; for s-waves, loops enter at NLO.
- At leading order, the rescattering contribution vanishes for \(pp \to pp\pi^0\) but is sizable for charged pions, driven by the Weinberg-Tomazawa term.

## Context

This work extends the earlier meson-exchange model of Hanhart (2002) and compares it to the polarized data from the IUCF program (2001–2002). It contrasts with the original Weinberg counting scheme used in earlier chiral perturbation theory studies of \(pp \to pp\pi^0\) s-waves (Cohen et al., 1996; Hanhart et al., 2001), which ignored the large momentum scale inherent to meson production. The findings enable a systematic effective-field-theory treatment of pion production, with the next step being the estimation of low-energy constants at N\(^2\)LO via resonance saturation.
