# Efimov Physics and Beyond

> Efimov physics relates to 3-body systems with large 2-body scattering lengths a and small effective ranges r. For many systems in nature the assumption of a small effective range is not valid. The present report shows binding energies E of three identical bosons calculated with 2-body potentials that are fitted to scattering data and momentum cut-offs (L) by inverse scattering. Results agree with previous works in the case of r<<a. While energies diverge with momentum cut-off L for r=0, they converge for r>0 when L=~10/r. With 1/a=0 the converged energies are given by E(n) =C(n)/r*r with n labeling the energy-branch and calculated values C(0)=0.77, C(1)=.0028. This gives a ratio ~278 thus differing from the value ~515 in the Efimov case. Efimov's angular dependent function is calculated. Good agreement with previous works is obtained when r<< a. With the increased values of effective range the shallow states still appear Efimov-like. For deeper states the angular dependence differs but is independent of the effective range.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for three identical bosons with non-zero effective range \(r_s > 0\) and infinite scattering length (\(a_s^{-1}=0\)), the three-body binding energies converge to finite values proportional to \(r_s^{-2}\), in contrast to the divergent behavior for \(r_s=0\); however, the ratio between successive energy branches is approximately 278 rather than the Efimov value of 515, and the angular-dependent function \(\Delta'(\xi)\) for the deeper state differs from the Efimov function even though it remains independent of \(r_s\).

## Method

The author used the Faddeev equation in momentum space with rank-1 separable two-body potentials constructed via inverse scattering from phase shifts parameterized by scattering length \(a_s\) and effective range \(r_s\), with a momentum cut-off \(\Lambda\). The potentials were fitted to the effective-range expansion \(k\cot\delta(k) = -1/a_s + \frac{1}{2}r_s k^2\), and the three-body binding energies \(E_3^{(n)}\) were computed for various combinations of \(a_s\), \(r_s\), and \(\Lambda\), with special attention to the convergence behavior as \(\Lambda\) increases.

## Key numbers

- In the Unitary limit (\(r_s = a_s^{-1} = 0\)), the three lowest energy branches (in units of \(\Lambda^2\)) are: \(E_u^{(0)} = -0.1325\), \(E_u^{(1)} = -0.0002520\), \(E_u^{(2)} = -0.0000004568\); the ratio \(E_u^{(0)}/E_u^{(1)} = 515\) agrees with the Efimov result.
- For \(r_s > 0\) and \(a_s^{-1} = 0\), the converged energies (for \(\Lambda > \Lambda_c\)) follow \(E_3^{(n)} = C^{(n)} / r_s^2\) with \(C^{(0)} = 0.770\) and \(C^{(1)} = 0.00298\); the ratio \(C^{(0)}/C^{(1)} \approx 278\) differs from the Efimov value of 515.
- The critical cut-off for convergence is \(\Lambda_c \sim 10/r_s\); for \(r_s = 0.1\) this gives \(\Lambda_c \sim 100\).
- For the shallow state (\(n=1\)) with \(r_s > 0\), the function \(F_1^{(1)}\) maps onto the Efimov function with a scaling factor of approximately 3; for the deeper state (\(n=0\)), the slope of \(\sqrt{F_1^{(0)}}\) versus \(r_s a_s^{-1}\) is approximately 1.5, differing from the Efimov slope of about 1.

## Context

This work extends the classic Efimov physics of three identical bosons with zero-range interactions (Efimov 1970, Thomas 1935) to systems where the effective range \(r_s\) is not negligible, a regime relevant for many physical systems such as cold atoms near Feshbach resonances and nuclear systems. The results agree with prior Effective Field Theory calculations by Braaten et al. (2003) and Mohr (2008) in the limit \(r_s \ll a_s\), and provide a benchmark for recent EFT work including effective range corrections by Ji et al. (2011) and Platter et al. (2006). The finding that the angular function \(\Delta'(\xi)\) for the deeper state differs from the Efimov function but remains independent of \(r_s\) opens the question of whether a universal scaling function exists for finite-range interactions.
