# Chiral Dynamics and Dubna-Mainz-Taipei Dynamical Model for Pion-Photoproduction Reaction

> We demonstrate that the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) meson-exchange dynamical model, which starts from an effective chiral Lagrangian, for pion photoproduction provides an excellent and economic framework to describe both the pi^0 threshold production and the Delta deformation, two features dictated by chiral dynamics.

## Key claim

The Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) meson-exchange dynamical model, built from an effective chiral Lagrangian, can simultaneously describe both threshold π⁰ photoproduction and Δ(1232) deformation, but the model fails to reproduce the photon asymmetry Σ at 159.5 MeV where chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) succeeds, though a new Mainz measurement may favor the DMT prediction.

## Method

The DMT model constructs a γπ transition potential from an effective chiral Lagrangian containing Born terms and ρ/ω exchanges, then solves a coupled-channels scattering equation that includes pion rescattering to all orders via the πN t-matrix. For π⁰ photoproduction near threshold, the E₀₊ multipole is computed by solving coupled equations in the π⁰p and π⁺n channels with physical masses. For the Δ(1232) region, the transition potential is split into a background part and a bare Δ-excitation part, with bare γNΔ coupling constants G_M1 and G_E2 treated as free parameters fitted to Mainz and VPI multipole analyses.

## Key numbers

- The experimental ratio R_EM = E₁₊^(3/2)/M₁₊^(3/2) = -(2.5 ± 0.5)% indicates Δ deformation
- At the resonance position, the background t-matrix t_γπ^B vanishes within K-matrix approximation, leaving only the principal value integral term
- Pion rescattering contributes half of the M₁₊^(3/2) multipole, with the other half from bare γNΔ excitation
- Almost all E2 strength in E₁₊^(3/2) is generated by πN rescattering, not bare Δ excitation

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange dynamical model approach of Yang (1985) and the DMT collaboration (Kamalov and Yang, 1999) to simultaneously address threshold π⁰ production and Δ deformation, two consequences of chiral symmetry. The DMT model provides an alternative to heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (Bernard et al., various years) which describes threshold data well but uses a different theoretical framework; the DMT model's failure on photon asymmetry at 159.5 MeV (Schmidt, 2001) may be resolved by new Mainz measurements (Hornidge, 2010). The finding that bare Δ is almost spherical while observed deformation comes from pion cloud effects clarifies the role of chiral dynamics in baryon structure.
