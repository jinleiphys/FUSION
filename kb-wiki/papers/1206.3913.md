# Uniqueness of Landau-Lifshitz Energy Frame in Relativistic Dissipative Hydrodynamics

> We show that the relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic equation derived from the relativistic Boltzmann equation by the renormalization-group method uniquely leads to the one in the energy frame proposed by Landau and Lifshitz, provided that the macroscopic-frame vector, which defines the local rest frame of the fluid velocity, is independent of the momenta of constituent particles, as it should. We argue that the relativistic hydrodynamic equations for viscous fluids must be defined on the energy frame if it is consistent with the underlying relativistic kinetic equation.

# Uniqueness of Landau-Lifshitz Energy Frame in Relativistic Dissipative Hydrodynamics

## Key claim

The relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic equation derived from the relativistic Boltzmann equation via the renormalization-group method uniquely yields the Landau-Lifshitz (energy) frame, provided that the macroscopic-frame vector defining the local rest frame of the fluid velocity is independent of the momenta of constituent particles. The authors argue that relativistic hydrodynamic equations for viscous fluids must be defined on the energy frame if they are to be consistent with the underlying relativistic kinetic equation.

## Method

The authors apply the renormalization-group (RG) method to the relativistic Boltzmann equation (RBE) to derive the asymptotic hydrodynamic dynamics in the far-infrared, long-wavelength limit. They introduce a macroscopic-frame vector a^mu that defines the covariant coordinate system, require it to be independent of particle momenta, and show it must be proportional to the fluid velocity u^mu. Using the RG method, they solve the RBE perturbatively around a local equilibrium (Juttner) distribution function, resum secular terms via the envelope equation, and obtain the dissipative parts of the energy-momentum tensor and particle current without imposing any matching conditions.

## Key numbers

- The dissipative energy-momentum tensor takes the form delta T^{mu nu} = zeta Delta^{mu nu} (nabla dot u) + 2 eta Delta^{mu nu rho sigma} nabla_rho u_sigma, where zeta is the bulk viscosity and eta is the shear viscosity.
- The dissipative particle current takes the form delta N^mu = lambda (1/hat{h}^2) nabla^mu (mu/T), where lambda is the thermal conductivity and hat{h} is the reduced enthalpy per particle.
- The Landau-Lifshitz constraints are satisfied: delta e = u_mu delta T^{mu nu} u_nu = 0, Q_mu = Delta_{mu nu} delta T^{nu rho} u_rho = 0, and delta n = u_mu delta N^mu = 0.
- The linearized collision operator A has five zero modes given by phi_{0p}^alpha = {p^mu for alpha = 0-3, 1*m for alpha = 4}.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RG-based derivation of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics (Tsumura, Kunihiro, and Ohnishi, 2011) by resolving the ambiguity in the definition of the fluid velocity. It contradicts the Eckart (particle) frame approach, which is known to exhibit unphysical instabilities of the equilibrium state (Hiscock and Lindblom, 1985). The result provides a foundation for the matching conditions that select the energy frame, which were previously imposed heuristically in phenomenological derivations (Landau and Lifshitz, 1959; de Groot, van Leeuwen, and van Weert, 1980). The work enables further extension to multi-component systems and second-order causal relativistic hydrodynamics (extended thermodynamics).
