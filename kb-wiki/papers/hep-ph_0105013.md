# Lattice spacing dependence of phase transition temperature in the classical linear sigma model

> We have investigated the phase transition properties of classical linear sigma model. The fields were kept in contact with a heat bath for sufficiently long time such that fields are equilibrated at the temperature of the heat bath. It was shown that the sigma model fields undergoes phase transition, but the transition temperature depend crucially on the lattice spacing. In the continuum limit, the transition temperature tends to zero or at least to a very low value.

## Key claim

The classical linear sigma model fields do undergo a phase transition, but the critical temperature depends crucially on the lattice spacing; in the continuum limit (lattice spacing approaching zero), the transition temperature tends to zero or at least to a very low value.

## Method

The author solved the Langevin equation for the O(4) fields of the linear sigma model (without the symmetry-breaking term) on a \(32^3\) lattice with periodic boundary conditions, coupling the fields to a heat bath represented by white noise and a friction term consistent with the fluctuation-dissipation theorem. The initial field configuration corresponded to zero temperature, and the fields were evolved until equilibrium at the heat-bath temperature was reached. The volume-averaged sigma condensate was used as the order parameter to identify the phase transition temperature for four different lattice spacings.

## Key numbers

* For lattice spacing a = 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, and 2.5 (units not specified in text, but presumably in fm), the order parameter decreased from approximately \(f_\pi\) at low temperature to exactly zero at a critical temperature \(T_c\).
* The critical temperature decreased as the lattice spacing was reduced, with the trend indicating that in the continuum limit \(T_c \rightarrow 0\).
* The interpolation function used to separate the central region from the rest of the system had parameters \(r_0 = 11a\) and \(\Gamma = 0.5\) fm.

## Context

This work extends studies of disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) formation in relativistic heavy ion collisions, which were pioneered by Anselm and Ryskin (1988) and popularized by the quench scenario of Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) and the Baked-Alaska model of Bjorken et al. (1993). The paper directly addresses the unresolved question of whether the critical temperature of the linear sigma model depends on the momentum cutoff, a known divergence problem in classical field thermodynamics. The strong lattice-spacing dependence found here implies that simulation studies of DCC using classical sigma model fields are highly questionable and their results cannot be believed to represent physical systems.
