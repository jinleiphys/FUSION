# Causality and stability in relativistic viscous non-resistive magneto-fluid dynamics

> We investigate the causality and the stability of the relativistic viscous magneto-hydrodynamics in the framework of the Israel-Stewart (IS) second-order theory, and also within a modified IS theory which incorporates the effect of magnetic fields in the relaxation equations of the viscous stress. We compute the dispersion relation by perturbing the fluid variables around their equilibrium values. In the ideal magnetohydrodynamics limit, the linear dispersion relation yields the well-known propagating modes: the Alfvén and the magneto-sonic modes.In the presence of bulk viscous pressure, the causality bound is found to be independent of the magnitude of the magnetic field. The same bound also remains true, when we take the full non-linear form of the equation using the method of characteristics. In the presence of shear viscous pressure, the causality bound is independent of the magnitude of the magnetic field for the two magneto-sonic modes. The causality bound for the shear-Alfvén modes, however, depends both on the magnitude and the direction of the propagation. For modified IS theory in the presence of shear viscosity, new non-hydrodynamic modes emerge but the asymptotic causality condition is the same as that of IS. In summary, although the magnetic field does influence the wave propagation in the fluid, the study of the stability and asymptotic causality conditions in the fluid rest frame shows that the fluid remains stable and causal given that they obey certain asymptotic causality condition.

## Key claim

The paper investigates causality and stability conditions for relativistic viscous magneto-hydrodynamics within the Israel-Stewart (IS) second-order theory and a modified IS theory that incorporates magnetic field effects in the relaxation equations of viscous stress; the authors find that although the magnetic field influences wave propagation, the fluid remains stable and causal provided certain asymptotic causality conditions are obeyed, with the causality bound for bulk viscosity being independent of magnetic field magnitude, while for shear viscosity the bound depends on both magnitude and direction of propagation for the shear-Alfven modes.

## Method

The authors linearize the energy-momentum conservation equations and Maxwell's equations by applying small sinusoidal perturbations around equilibrium in the fluid rest frame, then compute dispersion relations for the perturbed fluid variables. They analyze the resulting polynomial equations to extract asymptotic group velocities in the large wave-number limit, using the method of characteristics for the full nonlinear equations in the bulk viscosity case. The analysis covers ideal magneto-hydrodynamics (MHD), MHD with bulk viscosity only, MHD with shear viscosity only, and the full viscous case within both the standard IS formalism and a modified IS theory derived from complete moment expansion.

## Key numbers

- Alfven wave speed: \( v_A^2 = B_0^2 / h \), where \( h = \varepsilon_0 + P_0 + B_0^2 \)
- For bulk viscosity with magnetic field, the asymptotic group velocity is \( v_L = \sqrt{\alpha + 1/b_1} \), with causality requiring \( 1/b_1 \leq 1 - \alpha \), where \( \alpha = c_s^2 \) and \( b_1 \equiv h\tau_\Pi/\zeta (1 - v_A^2) \)
- For shear viscosity without magnetic field, the asymptotic group velocity for shear modes is \( v_L = 1/\sqrt{b} \), with causality requiring \( 1/b \leq 1 \); for sound modes, \( v_L = \sqrt{\alpha + 4/(3b)} \), with causality requiring \( 1/b \leq (3/4)(1 - \alpha) \)
- For ideal MHD at \( \theta = \pi/2 \), the fast magneto-sonic wave speed is \( v_f^2 = v_A^2 + \alpha (1 - v_A^2) \)
- The magnetic field strength in heavy-ion collisions is estimated at \( \sim 10^{18} \text{-} 10^{19} \) Gauss

## Context

This work extends prior causality and stability analyses of Israel-Stewart theory without magnetic fields, specifically the studies by Hiscock (1983, 1985), Denicol (2008), and Pu (2009), to the case of relativistic magneto-hydrodynamics. It builds on the ideal MHD causality studies by Grozdanov (2016) and Hernandez (2017), and uses the modified IS theory formulation from Denicol (2018, 2019) derived via complete moment expansion. The results enable systematic numerical simulations of relativistic viscous MHD for heavy-ion collisions, particularly for studying the chiral magnetic effect and other quantum transport phenomena in the quark-gluon plasma.
