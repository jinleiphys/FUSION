# Dynamical coupled-channels study of pi N --> pi pi N reactions

> As a step toward performing a complete coupled-channels analysis of the world data of pi N, gamma^* N --> pi N, eta N, pi pi N reactions, the pi N --> pi pi N reactions are investigated starting with the dynamical coupled-channels model developed in Phys. Rev. C76, 065201 (2007). The channels included are pi N, eta N, and pi pi N which has pi Delta, rho N, and sigma N resonant components. The non-resonant amplitudes are generated from solving a set of coupled-channels equations with the meson-baryon potentials defined by effective Lagrangians. The resonant amplitudes are generated from 16 bare excited nucleon (N^*) states which are dressed by the non-resonant interactions as constrained by the unitarity condition. The data of total cross sections and pi N and pi pi invariant mass distributions of pi^+ p --> pi^+ pi^+ n, pi^+ pi^0p and pi^- p --> pi^+ pi^- n, pi^- pi^0 n, pi^0 pi^0 n reactions from threshold to the invariant mass W = 2 GeV can be described to a very large extent. We show the importance of the coupled-channels effects and the strong interference between the contributions from the pi Delta, sigma N, and rho N channels. The large interference between the resonant and non-resonant amplitudes is also demonstrated. Possible future developements are discussed.

## Key claim

The dynamical coupled-channels model developed for πN elastic scattering (JLMS model, Phys. Rev. C76, 065201) can describe the total cross sections and invariant mass distributions of πN → ππN reactions from threshold to W = 2 GeV to a very large extent, but only when the direct production term v_{πN,ππN} is included; the model uses parameters taken entirely from the JLMS model without any adjustable parameters for the ππN channels.

## Method

The authors solved coupled-channels integral equations including πN, ηN, and ππN channels (the latter with πΔ, ρN, and σN resonant components) using meson-baryon potentials from effective Lagrangians and 16 bare N* states dressed by non-resonant interactions constrained by unitarity. The πN → ππN amplitude was decomposed into a direct production term and contributions from πΔ, ρN, and σN intermediate states, with the direct term approximated using the full T_{πN,πN} amplitude and a N → πN vertex function. The vertex functions for Δ → πN, ρ → ππ, and σ → ππ decays were fixed from fits to πN P33 phase shifts and ππ phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- Energy range covered: from threshold to invariant mass W = 2 GeV (T_π ~ 1.6 GeV)
- 16 bare excited nucleon (N*) states were included in the resonant amplitudes
- Vertex function parameters: f_{πNΔ} = 2.049, g_{σππ} = 0.7750, g_{ρππ} = 0.6684, Λ_{πNΔ} = 649 MeV, Λ_{σππ} = 378 MeV, Λ_{ρππ} = 461 MeV
- Five reaction channels calculated: π⁺p → π⁺π⁺n, π⁺π⁰p and π⁻p → π⁺π⁻n, π⁻π⁰p, π⁰π⁰n

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels model of Matsuyama, Sato, and Lee (2007) from πN elastic scattering to πN → ππN reactions, going beyond earlier tree-diagram calculations (Jensen 1997, Kamano 2004, 2006) that covered only up to W = 1551 MeV and considered only Δ(1232) and N*(1440) resonances. It also goes beyond the chiral perturbation theory calculations (Bernard 1995, 1997; Fettes 2000; Mobed 2005) that were limited to near-threshold energies, and the Julich group tree-diagram calculation (2006) that included coupled-channels effects only up to W = 1.38 GeV. The results demonstrate that coupled-channels effects and interference between πΔ, σN, and ρN channels are essential for describing data up to 2 GeV, and that the available data are insufficient to improve the model parameters, requiring future measurements of angular distributions and polarizations.
