# Clusters and condensates in Fermi systems

> Superconductivity, superfluidity, condensation, cluster formation, etc. are phenomena that might occur in many-particle systems. These are due to residual interactions between the particles. To explain these phenomena consistently in a microscopic approach, at some point, one needs to solve few-body equations that are modified because of the Pauli principle and the interactions of the many particles around.

## Key claim

The paper argues that to consistently explain phenomena like superconductivity, superfluidity, and cluster formation in Fermi systems, one must solve few-body equations that are modified by the Pauli principle and the surrounding many-particle medium, and that three- and four-body correlations are necessary beyond the simpler two-body approximation.

## Method

The author uses a Dyson equation approach to decouple the hierarchy of Green functions for many-particle systems, with all particles of a cluster taken at equal time and ensemble averaging done for an uncorrelated medium. In-medium Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations are derived for two-, three-, and four-body clusters, incorporating Pauli blocking and self-energy corrections. The in-medium few-body equations are applied to heavy-ion collisions via Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) simulations and to infinite nuclear matter to study bound states and phase transitions.

## Key numbers

- In a BUU simulation of 129Xe+119Sn at 50 MeV/A, use of in-medium rates for deuteron production leads to a 20% enhancement compared to isolated rates.
- The critical temperature for alpha-particle condensation is found by solving the homogeneous AGS equation for mu<0, confirming the onset of alpha condensation at higher values than earlier variational calculations using the 2+2 component of the alpha particle.
- The condition for the onset of superfluidity for alpha-particles is B(T_c, mu_c, P=0) = 4 mu_c, where B is the binding energy.

## Context

This work extends the Green function approach of Fetter and Walecka (1971) to include three- and four-body in-medium correlations, building on earlier isolated few-body calculations. It addresses open questions about whether three-particle contributions lead to a lower critical temperature than the simple Thouless criterion, as indicated by recent results in the Hubbard model (Letz), and whether the chiral phase transition for nucleons (three-quark states) occurs at the same density/temperature as for mesons. The results enable more accurate modeling of heavy-ion collisions and the exploration of alpha-particle condensates in nuclear matter.
