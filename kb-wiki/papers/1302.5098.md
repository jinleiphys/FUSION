# The chromo-Weibel instability in an expanding background

> In this proceedings contribution we review recent calculations of the dynamics of the chromo-Weibel instability in the quark gluon plasma. This instability is present in gauge theories with a one-particle distribution function which is momentum-space anisotropic in the local rest frame. The conditions necessary for triggering this instability can be present already in the color-glass-condensate initial state or dynamically generated by the rapid longitudinal expansion of the matter created in a heavy-ion collision. Using the hard-loop framework we study the case that the one-particle distribution function possesses an arbitrary initial momentum anisotropy that increases in time due to longitudinal free streaming. The resulting three-dimensional dynamical equations for the chromofield evolution are solved numerically. We find that there is regeneration of the longitudinal pressure due to unstable plasma modes; nevertheless, the system seems to maintain a high-degree of momentum-space anisotropy. Despite this anisotropy, we find that there is rapid longitudinal thermalization of the plasma due to the non-linear mode couplings inherent in the unstable evolution.

## Key claim
The chromo-Weibel instability regenerates longitudinal pressure in an anisotropically expanding quark-gluon plasma, but the system retains a high degree of momentum-space anisotropy for many fm/c; despite this anisotropy, rapid longitudinal thermalization of the gauge fields occurs through non-linear mode couplings.

## Method
The authors used the hard-expanding-loop (HEL) framework to solve the coupled Vlasov and non-Abelian Yang-Mills equations for a longitudinally free-streaming background of hard particles. The dynamical equations were discretized on a five-dimensional spatial lattice (transverse coordinates, longitudinal rapidity, and a cylindrical velocity surface) and evolved in temporal axial gauge using a leap-frog algorithm. Initial current fluctuations with a UV spectral cutoff were seeded to trigger unstable mode growth.

## Key numbers
- Lattice size: $(N_T^2 \times N_\eta) \times (N_u \times N_\phi) = (40^2 \times 128) \times (128 \times 32)$ with transverse lattice spacing $a = Q_s^{-1}$ and longitudinal spacing $a_\eta = 0.025$.
- For LHC and RHIC initial energy densities, one unit of rescaled proper time $\tilde\tau$ corresponds to approximately 1 fm/c and 1.4 fm/c, respectively.
- The momentum-space anisotropy parameter $\xi = (\tau/\tau_{\rm iso})^2 - 1$ increases quadratically with proper time, where $\tau_{\rm iso}$ is the time at which the distribution is isotropic.
- The maximal unstable growth rate decreases as $\Gamma^* \sim m_D \sqrt{\tau_{\rm iso}/\tau}$, while the wavenumber of the highest unstable mode grows as $k_{z,\rm max} \sim m_D \sqrt{\tau/\tau_{\rm iso}}$.
- The late-time growth of fields changes from pure exponential to $\exp\left( 2 m_D \sqrt{\tau \tau_{\rm iso}} \right)$.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of the chromo-Weibel instability in static anisotropic plasmas (Mrowczynski 1993, Arnold et al. 2003, Rebhan et al. 2004) by incorporating the effect of longitudinal expansion. It builds on the hard-expanding-loop framework developed by Romatschke (2006) and Rebhan (2008), and on pure Yang-Mills simulations with color-glass-condensate initial conditions (Romatschke 2006, Fukushima 2006). The results enable future studies of thermalization and isotropization timescales in heavy-ion collisions, with planned improvements using larger lattice sizes and pure Yang-Mills dynamics in an expanding metric.
