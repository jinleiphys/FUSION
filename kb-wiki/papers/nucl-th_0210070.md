# Some remarks on the statistical model of heavy ion collisions

> This contribution is an attempt to assess what can be learned from the remarkable success of the statistical model in describing ratios of particle abundances in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The success of the statistical model in describing particle abundance ratios in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions may simply reflect "phase-space dominance" rather than the formation of a thermal system in the Boltzmann sense; the extracted temperature and chemical potential are Lagrange multipliers characterizing the phase-space integral and do not necessarily have a physical meaning as thermodynamic variables.

## Method

The paper examines the statistical model by analyzing the conditions under which phase-space averaged matrix elements become constant and independent of multiplicity, allowing particle ratios to be determined solely by phase-space volumes. It compares the statistical fits to particle ratios from elementary collisions (e+e-, proton-proton) with those from nucleus-nucleus collisions, and discusses how conservation laws (particularly strangeness) and multi-particle correlations could distinguish between simple phase-space dominance and true thermal equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- Fits to proton-proton collisions lead to volumes of the order of 20 fm^3, which is somewhat larger than the expected size of 1/Lambda_QCD^3 (about the size of a proton).
- The centrality dependence of the Omega enhancement should flatten out once the volume over which strangeness is conserved exceeds about 20 times the volume of a nucleon.
- Results from the NA57 collaboration show a steep increase of the Omega enhancement up to the highest centralities.
- LHC experiments may lead to the same temperature of T approximately 170 MeV as seen at lower energies.
- Radial flow from RHIC appears slightly larger than that extracted at the SPS.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the statistical model initiated by Fermi (1950) and developed by Hagedorn, Kajantie, and Landau, and contrasts with the critical assessment given by Rafelski in the same conference. The paper builds on the statistical fits to particle ratios in elementary collisions by Becattini and Heinz (various years) and in nucleus-nucleus collisions by Braun-Munzinger and collaborators (various years), and addresses the question of whether thermalized matter has been created in heavy ion collisions. It enables future experimental searches for multi-particle correlations of conserved quantities (such as multiple Omega production) as a definitive test for thermal equilibrium, and cautions that the energy dependence of extracted thermal parameters is not a measurement of the QCD phase separation line.
