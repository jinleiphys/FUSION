# Are there good probes for the di-neutron correlation in light neutron-rich nuclei?

> The di-neutron correlation is a spatial correlation with which two valence neutrons are located at a similar position inside a nucleus. We discuss possible experimental probes for the di-neutron correlation. This includes the Coulomb breakup and the pair transfer reactions of neutron-rich nuclei, and the direct two-neutron decays of nuclei beyond the neutron drip-line.

## Key claim

The di-neutron correlation (two valence neutrons located at a similar position inside a nucleus) can be probed experimentally through Coulomb breakup, pair transfer reactions, and direct two-neutron decays, but each probe has limitations: the Coulomb breakup provides only an average opening angle rather than a detailed distribution, the reaction dynamics of pair transfer is not yet fully clarified, and the direct two-neutron decay measurement remains experimentally challenging.

## Method

The authors analyze three experimental probes using three-body model calculations with a density-dependent contact pairing interaction. For Coulomb breakup of Borromean nuclei (⁶He and ¹¹Li), they apply the cluster sum rule to extract the mean opening angle between valence neutrons from measured matter radii and total E1 strength. For direct two-neutron decay of ²⁶O, they use the two-particle Green's function method with a Woods-Saxon potential for the neutron-²⁴O potential to calculate decay energy spectra and angular correlations of emitted neutrons.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square matter radii from Tanihata et al. (1985) and Ozawa et al. (2001): √⟨r_m²⟩ = 1.57 ± 0.04 fm for ⁴He, 2.48 ± 0.03 fm for ⁶He, 2.32 ± 0.02 fm for ⁹Li, and 3.12 ± 0.16 fm for ¹¹Li
- Root-mean-square neutron-neutron distance extracted from matter radii: √⟨r_nn²⟩ = 3.75 ± 0.93 fm for ⁶He and 5.50 ± 2.24 fm for ¹¹Li
- Mean opening angle from Coulomb breakup analysis: ⟨θ_nn⟩ = 51.56⁺¹¹·²₋₁₂·₄ degrees for ⁶He and 56.2⁺¹⁷·⁸₋₂₁·₃ degrees for ¹¹Li (Hagino and Sagawa 2007)
- Mean opening angle after correcting Pauli forbidden transitions: ⟨θ_nn⟩ = 74.5⁺¹¹·²₋₁₃·₁ degrees for ⁶He and 65.2⁺¹¹·⁴₋₁₃·₀ degrees for ¹¹Li (Hagino and Sagawa 2007)
- Three-body model calculation results: ⟨θ_nn⟩ = 66.33 degrees for ⁶He and 65.29 degrees for ¹¹Li (Hagino and Sagawa 2005)
- Experimental single-particle energies for ²⁵O: ε_{2s1/2} = -4.09(13) MeV and ε_{1d3/2} = 770⁺²⁰₋₁₀ keV (Hoffman et al. 2008)
- Uncorrelated decay energy spectrum peak for ²⁶O: E = 1.54 MeV (twice the single-particle resonance energy 0.77 MeV)
- Uncorrelated mean opening angle: ⟨θ_nn⟩ = 90 degrees

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of di-neutron correlation in Borromean nuclei by Bertsch, Broglia, and Riedel (1967) and Migdal (1973), and three-body model calculations for ¹¹Li and ⁶He by Zhukov et al. (1993) and Hagino and Sagawa (2005). The paper discusses how Coulomb breakup data from Nakamura et al. (2006) and Aumann et al. (1999) can be interpreted using the cluster sum rule approach of Bertsch and Esbensen (1991) and Esbensen, Hagino, and Mueller (2007). It enables future experimental searches for di-neutron correlation through angular correlation measurements in two-neutron decays of unbound nuclei like ²⁶O, and suggests that ultracold atom tunneling experiments (Zurn et al. 2013) may provide complementary insights.
