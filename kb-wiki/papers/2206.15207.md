# Flow correlations from a hydrodynamics model with dynamical freeze-out and initial conditions based on perturbative QCD and saturation

> We extend the applicability of the hydrodynamics, perturbative QCD and saturation -based EKRT (Eskola-Kajantie-Ruuskanen-Tuominen) framework for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions to peripheral collisions by introducing dynamical freeze-out conditions. As a new ingredient compared to the previous EKRT computations we also introduce a non-zero bulk viscosity. We compute various hadronic observables and flow correlations, including normalized symmetric cumulants, mixed harmonic cumulants and flow-transverse momentum correlations, and compare them against measurements from the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC). We demonstrate that the inclusion of the dynamical freeze-out and bulk viscosity allows a better description of the measured flow coefficients in peripheral collisions and enables the use of an extended centrality range when constraining the properties of QCD matter in the future.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that introducing dynamical freeze-out conditions and a non-zero bulk viscosity into the EKRT framework extends the model's agreement with measured flow coefficients to peripheral heavy-ion collisions, whereas previous constant-temperature freeze-out computations were limited to more central collisions.

## Method

The authors use (2+1)-dimensional relativistic second-order Israel-Stewart fluid dynamics with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, initial conditions from the EKRT saturation model based on next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD, and the s95p parametrization of lattice-QCD equation of state. They introduce a new parametrization of specific shear viscosity with a power-law hadronic part and a peaked bulk viscosity near the QCD transition temperature, and implement dynamical freeze-out using two criteria: a local Knudsen number condition and a global condition comparing the relaxation time to the system size. The transverse momentum spectra of hadrons are computed via Cooper-Frye integrals on the decoupling surface, and the results are compared to experimental data from RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

*   The initial state parameter \(K_{\rm sat} = 0.67\) is tuned to reproduce the charged particle multiplicity in 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions.
*   The minimum specific shear viscosity is \((\eta/s)_{min} = 0.11\) at temperature \(T_H = 135\) MeV, with a constant minimum width \(W_{min} = 35\) MeV.
*   The maximum specific bulk viscosity is \((\zeta/s)_{max} = 0.09\) at temperature \(T_{max}^{\zeta/s} = 240\) MeV, with a width of 60 MeV and asymmetry parameter \(a_{\zeta/s} = -0.5\).
*   The chemical freeze-out temperature is reduced from 175 MeV in earlier EKRT computations to 155 MeV in the new parametrization.
*   The dynamical freeze-out uses constants \(C_{Kn} = 0.8\) for the Knudsen number condition and \(C_R = 0.15\) for the global condition.
*   The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections used are 42 mb for 200 GeV Au+Au, 64 mb for 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb, 70 mb for 5.023 TeV Pb+Pb, and 72 mb for 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe collisions.

## Context

This work extends the EKRT framework (Eskola, Kajantie, Ruuskanen, Tuominen, 1999; Paatelainen, 2013; Niemi, 2015) by adding dynamical freeze-out and bulk viscosity, which were absent in previous EKRT computations that used a constant-temperature decoupling at 100 MeV and a chemical freeze-out temperature of 175 MeV. The approach differs from typical hybrid hydro+cascade models (e.g., Ryu, 2015) by keeping transport coefficients continuous throughout the hadronic phase, and it enables future global analyses to constrain QCD matter properties over an extended centrality range, including peripheral collisions where fluid dynamics was previously less successful.
