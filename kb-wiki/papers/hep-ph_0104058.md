# On predictions of the first results from RHIC

> In this talk, I will discuss the predictions of the first results from RHIC: the charged particle multiplicity $dN_{\rm ch}/dη$, its centrality dependence and the elliptic flow $v_2$.

## Key claim

The first RHIC data on charged particle multiplicity and elliptic flow suggest that perturbative QCD and gluon saturation become important at collider energies, but most pre-RHIC models predicted too many particles per unit rapidity, and the theoretical uncertainties in all models are larger than the experimental systematic errorbars.

## Method

The paper reviews and compares predictions from multiple models (HIJING, EKRT saturation model, LEXUS, Dual String Model, AMPT, UrQMD, RQMD, and hydrodynamics) against the first RHIC data from PHOBOS, PHENIX, and STAR at sqrt(s)=130 and 200 AGeV for Au-Au collisions. The author examines three observables: charged particle multiplicity dN_ch/deta, its centrality dependence, and elliptic flow v2, comparing model calculations to measured data points.

## Key numbers

- The PHOBOS data at sqrt(s)=130 AGeV for dN_ch/deta (6% central) were scaled up by a factor 1.1*(200/130)^0.37 to estimate predictions at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV
- The EKRT saturation model gives a scaling law dN_ch/dy = 0.83 A^0.922 (sqrt(s))^0.383 for central collisions
- The gluon liberation constant c from the classical field approach is 1.29 +/- 0.09 from SU(2) lattice calculations, and 1.39 from analytical semiclassical calculations
- The saturation scale Q^2_sat = 2.1 GeV^2 when requiring agreement with PHOBOS data
- Elliptic flow v2 reaches up to 6% for minimum bias events and up to 15% for v2(pT) in Au-Au at sqrt(s)=130 AGeV from STAR
- The EKRT saturation model gives the multiplicity of produced partons as N_AA(p_sat) = 1.383 A^0.922 (sqrt(s))^0.383

## Context

This talk reviews the first confrontations of pre-RHIC model predictions with actual RHIC data from PHOBOS, PHENIX, and STAR, extending earlier work on saturation models (EKRT 1999, McLerran-Venugopalan 1994) and event generators (HIJING 1991, UrQMD, RQMD). The results show that coherence phenomena in particle production become important at RHIC energies, and the strong elliptic flow confirms hydrodynamic predictions (Kajantie et al. 2000), pointing toward early thermalization and pressure formation in the partonic system. The paper enables future systematic studies of the sqrt(s), A, and impact parameter dependence to constrain models further, with transverse energy dE_T/deta identified as a more discriminating observable than multiplicity.
