# A new shock-capturing numerical scheme for ideal hydrodynamics

> We present a new algorithm for solving ideal relativistic hydrodynamics based on Godunov method with an exact solution of Riemann problem for an arbitrary equation of state. Standard numerical tests are executed, such as the sound wave propagation and the shock tube problem. Low numerical viscosity and high precision are attained with proper discretization.

# A new shock-capturing numerical scheme for ideal hydrodynamics

## Key claim
The authors present a Godunov-type numerical scheme for ideal relativistic hydrodynamics that uses an exact solution of the Riemann problem for an arbitrary equation of state, claiming low numerical viscosity and high precision. The hedge is that the scheme is tested only in one spatial dimension, and the authors state that the three-dimensional case remains to be checked.

## Method
The scheme solves the ideal relativistic hydrodynamic equations in conservation form using the Godunov method, with an exact Riemann solver at each cell interface to compute fluxes of conserved variables. The conserved variables are the energy-momentum tensor components expressed in terms of energy density, pressure, and flow velocity. The method is tested on two standard problems: sound wave propagation (with a sinusoidal perturbation) and the shock tube problem (with a discontinuity in energy density), comparing numerical results to analytic solutions.

## Key numbers
- Sound wave test: initial pressure \(p_0 = 10^3\ \mathrm{fm^{-4}}\), pressure perturbation \(\delta p = 10^{-1}\ \mathrm{fm^{-4}}\); precision improves for grids larger than \(N_{cell} \gtrsim 500\).
- Shock tube test: initial energy density \(\epsilon_L = 16\ \mathrm{GeV}\) (left half) and \(\epsilon_R = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\) (right half), with zero initial velocity; results shown after 100 time-steps.
- Numerical viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta_{num}/s\) is compared to the AdS/CFT limiting value \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\) and to the \(\eta/s\) of pion gas; for 1D simulations, suitable grid sizes are \(N_{cell} \gtrsim 400\).

## Context
This work extends the application of Godunov methods with exact Riemann solvers to ideal relativistic hydrodynamics for arbitrary equations of state, building on prior approaches by Romatschke and Gale (2010s) for heavy-ion collision modeling. The scheme is designed to achieve numerical viscosity low enough to simulate quark-gluon plasma, which is expected to have very low physical viscosity. The next step is extension to three dimensions and application to ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions.
