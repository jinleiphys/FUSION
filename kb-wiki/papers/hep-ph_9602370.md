# The connection between the nuclear matter mean-field equation of state and the quark and gluon condensates at high density

> It is known now that chiral symmetry restoration requires the meson-nucleon coupling to be density dependent in nuclear-matter mean-field models. We further show that quite generally, the quark and gluon condensates in medium are related to the trace of energy-momentum tensor of nuclear matter and in these models the incompressibility, K, must be less than thrice the chemical potential, $μ$. In the critical density $ρ_c$, the gluon condensate is only reduced by $20\%$ indicating a larger effective nucleon mass.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in nuclear-matter mean-field models, the quark and gluon condensates in medium are related to the trace of the energy-momentum tensor of nuclear matter, and that for the condensate ratio to decrease monotonically with density (as required by chiral symmetry restoration), the nuclear matter incompressibility K must be less than three times the chemical potential μ, a condition that is not satisfied in the standard Walecka model.

## Method

The authors derive a general expression for the quark condensate ratio R_qq in relativistic mean-field models by relating it to (E - 3P), the trace of the energy-momentum tensor of nuclear matter, using the Hellmann-Feynman theorem and the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation. They then compute the density derivative of this ratio to obtain the condition K < 3μ for monotonic decrease of the condensate, and apply this analysis to the Walecka model, the Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) model, the ZM3 model, and the chiral linear sigma-omega model. They also extend the formalism to gluon condensates using the QCD trace anomaly.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear saturation density ρ₀, with binding energy -15.75 MeV and M_N = 939 MeV, the quark condensate ratio R_qq = 0.69 and the gluon condensate ratio R_gg = 0.95 for σ_N = 45 MeV and S = 0.
- At the critical density ρ_c where the quark condensate vanishes, the trace (E - 3P) = 4 × (171 MeV)^4, yielding a bag constant B^{1/4} = 171 MeV.
- At this critical density, the gluon condensate in medium is (332 MeV)^4, a reduction of only 20% from the vacuum value of (350 MeV)^4.
- At normal nuclear density, the gluon condensate reduction is about 5%.
- For the free nucleon, the gluon sigma term G_N = -795 MeV for S = 0, and ranges from -737 to -693 MeV for S + σ_N = 110 or 160 MeV respectively.
- The pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV is used in the condensate ratio formula.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Malheiro et al., 1995) showing that the Walecka model produces an unphysical increase of the quark condensate at high density, while the Zimanyi-Moszkowski models (Zimanyi and Moszkowski, 1990) yield chiral symmetry restoration. The paper generalizes this by connecting condensate behavior to the equation of state through the trace of the energy-momentum tensor, providing a model-independent criterion (K < 3μ) for a soft EOS that enables chiral symmetry restoration. This result enables future work on neutron star phenomenology and the quark-gluon plasma phase transition, and is consistent with the relativistic Dirac-Brueckner approach of Li and Ko (1995).
