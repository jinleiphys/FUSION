# Searching for the QCD critical point via the rapidity dependence of cumulants

> The search for a possible critical point in the QCD phase diagram is ongoing in heavy ion collision experiments at RHIC which scan the phase diagram by scanning the beam energy; a coming upgrade will increase the luminosity and extend the rapidity acceptance of the STAR detector. In fireballs produced in RHIC collisions, the baryon density depends on rapidity. By employing Ising universality together with a phenomenologically motivated freezeout prescription, we show that the resulting rapidity dependence of cumulant observables sensitive to critical fluctuations is distinctive. The dependence of the kurtosis (of the event-by-event distribution of the number of protons) on rapidity near mid-rapidity will change qualitatively if a critical point is passed in the scan. Hence, measuring the rapidity dependence of cumulant observables can enhance the prospect of discovering a critical point, in particular if it lies between two energies in the beam energy scan.

## Key claim

The rapidity dependence of the baryon chemical potential in heavy ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies gives rise to a strong, non-trivial rapidity dependence of cumulant observables near a possible QCD critical point; binning cumulants in rapidity rather than integrating over the full acceptance reveals qualitative changes in the rapidity dependence of the kurtosis if the beam energy scan passes a critical point, providing a complementary signature to the traditional beam-energy scan.

## Method

The authors model the baryon chemical potential at freezeout as a function of spacetime rapidity using a quadratic form, mu_B(y_s) ~ mu_{B,0} + alpha y_s^2, with alpha taken from SPS measurements (50 MeV) or varied. They compute the critical contribution to the fourth cumulant of the proton multiplicity distribution using Ising universality class scaling, with the correlation length xi and the kurtosis-like factor K_4 determined by the distance and direction from a hypothetical critical point placed at (mu_B^c=260 MeV, T=160 MeV). The momentum-space integrals are performed over finite rapidity bins and transverse momentum cuts (0.4 to 2 GeV), using a blast-wave freezeout model and the freezeout curve from Cleymans (2005) and Andronic (2017). The cumulant ratio omega_{4,sigma} = kappa_4[N]_sigma / <N> is computed for two types of rapidity cuts: varying the total acceptance |y|<y_max, and binning in rapidity bins of width Delta y=0.4 centered at y_c.

## Key numbers

* The baryon chemical potential at freezeout in RHIC BES collisions ranges from approximately 25 MeV to 400 MeV, for beam energies from sqrt(s)=200 AGeV down to 7.7 AGeV.
* The quadratic coefficient alpha for the rapidity dependence of mu_B is taken as 50 MeV from SPS collisions at sqrt(s)=17.3 AGeV, where mu_{B,0}=237 MeV.
* The hypothetical critical point is placed at mu_B^c=260 MeV, T=160 MeV for illustrative calculations.
* The sigma-proton-proton coupling is set to g=7, following Athanasiou (2010).
* The transverse momentum acceptance is fixed to p_min=0.4 GeV and p_max=2 GeV throughout.
* The rapidity bin width used for the binned cumulant analysis is Delta y=0.4.
* The STAR iTPC upgrade will extend rapidity acceptance for protons from |y|<0.5 in BES-I to |y|<0.8 in BES-II.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fluctuation measures as a function of total rapidity acceptance by Ling (2015) and Bzdak (2016), which assumed mu_B independent of rapidity. The authors incorporate the measured rapidity dependence of baryon density at RHIC BES energies from Becattini (2007) and Shen (2017), showing that this dependence produces qualitative signatures in cumulants binned by rapidity that were not captured in prior work. The results enable a new experimental strategy for the RHIC BES-II program (beginning 2019) using the upgraded STAR iTPC detector, where binning cumulants in rapidity can provide a cross-check on critical point signatures seen in the beam-energy dependence of cumulants, and may help determine whether a critical point lies between two beam energies in the scan.
