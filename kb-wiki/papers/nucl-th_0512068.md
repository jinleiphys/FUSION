# The hyperon mean free paths in the relativistic mean field

> The $Λ$- and $Ξ^-$-hyperon mean free paths in nuclei are firstly calculated in the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. The real parts of the optical potential are derived from the RMF approach, while the imaginary parts are obtained from those of nucleons with the relations: $U^{\mathrm{IY}}_{\mathrm{S}} = α_{σ\mathrm{Y}}\cdot U_{\mathrm{S}}^{\mathrm{IN}}$ and $U^{\mathrm{IY}}_{\mathrm{V}} = α_{ω\mathrm{Y}}\cdot U_{\mathrm{V}}^{\mathrm{IN}}$ . With the assumption, the depth of the imaginary potential for $Ξ^-$ is $W_Ξ\simeq-$ 3.5 MeV, and for $Λ$ is $W_Λ\simeq-$ 7 MeV at low incident energy. We find that, the hyperon mean free path decreases with the increase of the hyperon incident energies, from 200 MeV to 800 MeV; and in the interior of the nuclei, the mean free path is about $2\sim 3$ fm for $Λ$, and about $4\sim 8$ fm for $Ξ^-$, depending on the hyperon incident energy.

## Key claim

The hyperon mean free paths for Λ and Ξ⁻ in nuclei are calculated for the first time using relativistic mean field (RMF) theory, under the assumption that the imaginary parts of the hyperon optical potentials scale linearly from the nucleon imaginary potentials via the coupling ratios α_σY and α_ωY, rather than being derived from first principles.

## Method

The real scalar and vector potentials for Λ and Ξ⁻ hyperons are obtained from the RMF Lagrangian using NL-SH parameterization, with hyperon coupling constants fixed by SU(6) symmetry for vector mesons and by fitting to empirical hyperon potential depths (U_Λ = -30 MeV, U_Ξ = -16 MeV) for scalar mesons. The imaginary scalar and vector potentials for hyperons are taken from the nucleon imaginary potentials (extracted from proton elastic scattering data by Hama et al.) multiplied by the same coupling ratios α_σY and α_ωY used for the real parts. The Schrödinger equivalent optical potential is then constructed, and the hyperon mean free path is obtained from the imaginary part of the complex momentum via λ = 1/(2k_I).

## Key numbers

- Depth of imaginary optical potential for Ξ⁻–⁴⁰Ca at T_lab = 100 MeV: W_Ξ ≈ -3.5 MeV, compatible with the prediction of Batty et al. (W_Ξ ≈ 3 MeV)
- Depth of imaginary optical potential for Λ–⁴⁰Ca at low incident energy: W_Λ ≈ -7 MeV
- Depth of imaginary optical potential for nucleon–⁴⁰Ca: W_N ≈ -11 MeV
- In the interior of nuclei, Λ mean free path is about 2–3 fm, depending on incident energy
- In the interior of nuclei, Ξ⁻ mean free path is about 4–8 fm, depending on incident energy
- At T_lab = 200 MeV, Λ mean free path in ⁴⁰Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb is about 7.8 and 7 fm, respectively; at T_lab = 800 MeV, it decreases to about 5 and 4.5 fm
- At T_lab = 200 MeV, Ξ⁻ mean free path in ⁴⁰Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb is about 4 and 3.5 fm, respectively; at T_lab = 800 MeV, it decreases to about 2.5 and 2 fm

## Context

This work extends the RMF-based calculation of nucleon mean free paths (Cheon et al., Rego et al., Clark et al., Li et al.) to hyperons for the first time, addressing a gap due to scarce experimental data on hyperon-nucleon interactions. The assumption of scaling imaginary potentials from nucleons follows a suggestion by Cooper et al. from about ten years prior. The results enable future comparisons with emerging hyperon-nucleus experimental data and provide a baseline for studying temperature-dependent hyperon mean free paths relevant to high-energy heavy-ion collisions.
