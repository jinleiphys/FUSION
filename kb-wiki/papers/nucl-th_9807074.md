# Electromagnetic form factors of the bound nucleon

> We calculate electromagnetic form factors of the proton bound in specified orbits for several closed shell nuclei. The quark structure of the nucleon and the shell structure of the finite nuclei are given by the QMC model. We find that orbital electromagnetic form factors of the bound nucleon deviate significantly from those of the free nucleon.

# Electromagnetic form factors of the bound nucleon

## Key claim

Orbital electromagnetic form factors of a proton bound in specific shell-model orbits of finite nuclei deviate significantly from those of a free proton, with the magnitude of the deviation depending on the local baryon density experienced by the nucleon in its orbit. The authors caution that possible off-shell effects and meson exchange currents are ignored in their approach, and that the bag model results are most reliable at momentum transfers below 1 GeV².

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model in mean-field approximation to self-consistently determine both the quark structure of the nucleon and the shell structure of finite spherical nuclei. They solved the equations of motion for the nucleon, sigma, omega, rho, and Coulomb fields to obtain nucleon shell-model wave functions and local baryon densities, then computed orbital electromagnetic form factors by integrating density-dependent form factors from nuclear matter weighted by the local proton density in each orbit. The bag radius in free space was taken as 0.8 fm with a current quark mass of 5 MeV, and corrections for recoil and center-of-mass motion were applied using the Peierls-Thouless projection method with Lorentz contraction and a perturbative pion cloud.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic moment of the proton in the 1s₁/₂ orbit of ⁴He increases by about 7% relative to the free value.
- For the p-orbit in ¹⁶O, the magnetic moment is reduced by 2–3% compared to the s-orbit value.
- The ratio G_E/G_M for a proton in the 1s orbit of ⁴He or ¹⁶O runs from approximately 0.41 at Q² = 0 to 0.28 at Q² = 1 GeV² and 0.20 at Q² = 2 GeV².
- The ratio of G_E/G_M in-medium relative to free space for p-orbits in ¹⁶O is about 2% higher than for the 1s orbit.
- The bag constant reduction variant (10% decrease in B) produces significantly larger suppression of form factors, especially at higher Q².

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of nucleon electromagnetic properties in uniform nuclear matter using the QMC model (Lu et al., 1998) to the more realistic case of finite nuclei with shell structure. It is motivated by experimental observations including the nuclear EMC effect (Aubert et al., 1983), the quenching of g_A in nuclear beta decay (Buck and Perez, 1983), and missing strength in quasielastic electron scattering response functions. The results are directly relevant to quasielastic scattering polarization measurements underway at TJNAF and Mainz, and the authors suggest that differences between form factors in different shell-model orbits should be detectable with current experimental capabilities.
