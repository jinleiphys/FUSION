# Hadronic contribution to the muon g-2: a Dyson-Schwinger perspective

> We summarize our results for hadronic contributions to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon ($a_μ$), the one from hadronic vacuum-polarisation (HVP) and the light-by-light scattering contribution (LBL), obtained from the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE's) of QCD. In the case of HVP we find good agreement with model independent determinations from dispersion relations for $a_μ^\mathrm{HVP}$ as well as for the Adler function with deviations well below the ten percent level. From this we conclude that the DSE approach should be capable of describing $a_μ^\mathrm{LBL}$ with similar accuracy. We also present results for LBL using a resonance expansion of the quark anti-quark T-matrix. Our preliminary value is $a_μ^\mathrm{LBL}=(217 \pm 91) \times 10^{-11}$.

# Hadronic contribution to the muon g-2: a Dyson-Schwinger perspective

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger approach reproduces the hadronic vacuum polarisation contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment with deviations well below ten percent compared to dispersion-relation results, and the authors argue this capability should extend to the light-by-light scattering contribution with similar accuracy. Their preliminary light-by-light result of (217 ± 91) × 10^{-11} is substantially larger than the combined effective-field-theory estimate of 105(26) × 10^{-11} from Prades (2009), suggesting the uncertainty in the latter may be far too optimistic.

## Method

The authors employ rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD using the Maris-Tandy model for the quark-gluon interaction, solving the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation, the meson Bethe-Salpeter equation, and the inhomogeneous quark-photon vertex Bethe-Salpeter equation. For the hadronic vacuum polarisation they compute the photon self-energy tensor from dressed quark propagators and the self-consistent quark-photon vertex, then obtain a_mu via an integral over the renormalised vacuum polarisation function. For the light-by-light contribution they use a resonance expansion of the quark-antiquark T-matrix that includes a non-perturbatively dressed quark loop and pseudoscalar meson-exchange contributions (pi0, eta, eta'), with the quark-photon vertex treated via the Ball-Chiu construction.

## Key numbers

* Experimental value of the muon anomalous magnetic moment: 116 592 089(63) × 10^{-11} from the E821 experiment at Brookhaven (Bennett 2006, Roberts 2010)
* Standard Model theoretical value: 116 591 828(49) × 10^{-11} from Hagiwara (2011)
* Deviation between experiment and theory: 261(80) × 10^{-11}, corresponding to a 3.2 sigma effect
* Hadronic vacuum polarisation from dispersion relations: a_mu^HVP,DR = 6 949.1(42.7) × 10^{-11} from Hagiwara (2011)
* DSE result for HVP with parameter set I (pion-mass-fitted): a_mu^HVP,I = 7 440 × 10^{-11}
* DSE result for HVP with parameter set II (rho-mass-fitted): a_mu^HVP,II = 6 760 × 10^{-11}
* Pseudoscalar meson-exchange contribution to LBL (pi0, eta, eta'): a_mu^LBL;PS = (80.7 ± 12.0) × 10^{-11}
* Quark-loop contribution with bare vertex: (61 ± 2) × 10^{-11}
* Quark-loop contribution with Ball-Chiu vertex (gamma_mu part only): (107 ± 2) × 10^{-11}
* Quark-loop contribution with full Ball-Chiu vertex: (176 ± 4) × 10^{-11}
* Estimated quark-loop contribution including transverse vertex components: (136 ± 79) × 10^{-11}
* Total preliminary light-by-light result: a_mu^LBL = (217 ± 91) × 10^{-11}
* Light quark masses at scale mu^2 = (19 GeV)^2: set I m_u,d = 3.7 MeV, m_s = 85 MeV; set II m_u,d = 11 MeV, m_s = 72 MeV
* Heavy quark masses: m_c = 827 MeV, m_b = 3 680 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach to hadronic contributions to the muon g-2, building on the Maris-Tandy model (Maris 1999) for the quark-gluon interaction. The hadronic vacuum polarisation results are compared to model-independent dispersion-relation determinations from Eidelman (1998) and to lattice QCD results from the ETMC collaboration (Feng 2011) for N_f=2 and the RBC-UKQCD collaboration (Boyle 2011) for N_f=2+1. The light-by-light result is contrasted with the combined effective-field-theory estimate of 105(26) × 10^{-11} from Prades (2009) and with earlier hadronic-model calculations by de Rafael (1993), Bijnens (1995), Hayakawa (1996, 1997), Knecht (2001), Melnikov (2003), and Dorokhov (2008). The authors identify the next necessary steps as implementing the full quark-photon vertex inside the quark loop and overcoming the resonance approximation for the light-by-light contribution.
