# Splitting of ISGMR strength in the light-mass nucleus $^{24}$Mg due to ground-state deformation

> The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) strength distribution in $^{24}$Mg has been determined from background-free inelastic scattering of 386-MeV $α$ particles at extreme forward angles, including 0$^{\circ}$. The ISGMR strength distribution has been observed for the first time to have a two-peak structure in a light-mass nucleus. This splitting of ISGMR strength is explained well by microscopic theory in terms of the prolate deformation of the ground state of $^{24}$Mg.

## Key claim

The ISGMR strength distribution in the light-mass nucleus ²⁴Mg exhibits a two-peak structure for the first time in a light nucleus, and this splitting is explained by microscopic theory as resulting from the prolate deformation of the ground state, not from a spherical ground-state assumption.

## Method

Inelastic scattering of 386-MeV α particles off an enriched ²⁴Mg target was measured at extreme forward angles (including 0°) using the Grand Raiden magnetic spectrometer at RCNP, Osaka University. Multipole-decomposition analysis (MDA) was performed on the experimental angular distributions to disentangle contributions from L=0 through L=3 multipoles, using DWBA calculations with optical-model parameters derived from elastic scattering data. The extracted ISGMR strength distribution was compared with self-consistent deformed HFB+QRPA calculations using the Skyrme SkM* functional for both prolate-deformed and spherical ground-state configurations.

## Key numbers

- The ISGMR strength distribution shows a narrow peak at Ex ∼ 16 MeV and a broad peak at Ex ∼ 24 MeV.
- A total of 57 ± 7% of the E0 EWSR is exhausted over the excitation-energy region 6–35 MeV.
- The theoretical calculation (SkM* functional) gives an intrinsic quadrupole moment Q0 = 54.0 e fm², compared to 65.9 e fm² estimated from the measured B(E2) of the first 2⁺ state assuming a rigid rotor.
- The theoretical IS monopole strength exhausts 83% of EWSR in the 6–35 MeV region, requiring a scaling factor of 0.69 for comparison with the experimental data.
- The optical-model parameters obtained from fitting elastic scattering data are: V = 33.1 MeV, W = 36.1 MeV, RI = 3.87 fm, aI = 0.778 fm, RC = 3.04 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of ISGMR splitting in deformed nuclei, which had been reported only in heavier systems such as the deformed Sm nuclei (Garg et al., 1980; Itoh et al., 2003) and in fission decay of ²³⁸U, where the splitting is understood as mixing of the ISGMR with the Kπ=0⁺ component of the ISGQR. The results contradict earlier measurements on ²⁴Mg using α and ⁶Li inelastic scattering (Texas A&M group, 1999–2013) that did not reveal discernible splitting. The observation enables future studies of deformation effects on the ISGMR in other light-mass nuclei and provides a new benchmark for testing microscopic nuclear structure models such as the deformed HFB+QRPA approach with Skyrme and Gogny energy-density functionals.
