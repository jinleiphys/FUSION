# New Molecular Dynamics Methods for Simulating Neutron Star Crusts with Superfluid Vortices

> Superfluid vortices in neutron star crusts are thought to be pinned to the lattice of nuclei in the crust. The unpinning of superfluid vortices in spin glitches therefore motivates us to study the vortex-crust interaction explicitly with molecular dynamics. In this work, we present a new molecular dynamics methods to characterize the response of the crust to a rigid vortex. When vortex pinning forces and nearest neighbor Coulomb forces are comparable, we observe a qualitatively new phenomena of lattice entrainment with implications for the elastic evolution of the crust.

## Key claim
When vortex pinning forces and nearest-neighbor Coulomb forces are comparable, the crust enters a qualitatively new "entrainment" regime where nuclei bound to the vortex are transported through the lattice, altering the unpinning threshold and lattice response, a phenomenon the abstract describes as "lattice entrainment" but which is explicitly conditional on the pinning and Coulomb forces being comparable in magnitude.

## Method
The authors developed a molecular dynamics method treating nuclei as classical point particles with screened Coulomb interactions, coupled to an external Gaussian potential representing a rigid superfluid vortex aligned with the simulation z-axis. They simulated a bcc crystal of Z=10, A=40 nuclei at density 2×10¹³ g cm⁻³, with the vortex displaced in the -x direction, and tracked the stress tensor and bond order parameter Q₆ to characterize lattice deformation and breaking events.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear charge Z=10, mass number A=40, number density n_i=7.18×10⁻⁵ fm⁻³, Wigner-Seitz radius a_i=14.9255 fm, density ρ=2×10¹³ g cm⁻³ (assuming 0.75 free neutron fraction)
- Vortex well parameters: energy E_p=4 MeV, width σ=5 fm
- Temperature T=0.01 MeV = 0.2 T_M (melting temperature), plasma coupling Γ=845
- Simulation run for 680,000 timesteps with dt=25 fm/c (ω_p⁻¹/21.5)
- Vortex crosses one unit cell approximately every 10⁴ plasma oscillations

## Context
This work extends the vortex pinning studies of Schwarz (1977, 1978) and Link (2022), which used static idealized crusts when simulating dynamical vortices, by introducing explicit molecular dynamics of the crustal lattice. The authors build on their own recent molecular dynamics formalism (Caplan 2024) and the suggestion from Link (2022) to treat the nucleus-vortex interaction as a Gaussian well. This enables future work to characterize the rich phase diagram of unpinning behavior and to couple dynamic vortex solvers to molecular dynamics in next-generation codes.
