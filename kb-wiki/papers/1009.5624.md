# A new method for the experimental study of topological effects in the quark-gluon plasma

> A new method is presented for the quantitative measurement of charge separation about the reaction plane. A correlation function is obtained whose shape is concave when there is a net separation of positive and negative charges. Correlations not specifically associated with charge, from flow, jets and momentum conservation, do not influence the shape or magnitude of the correlation function. Detailed simulations are used to demonstrate the effectiveness of the method for the quantitative measurement of charge separation. Such measurements are a pre-requisite to the investigation of topological charge effects in the QGP as derived from the "strong $\cal{CP}$ problem".

# A new method for the experimental study of topological effects in the quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

The paper presents a new correlation function C_c(ΔS) whose concave shape indicates charge separation about the reaction plane, and which is claimed to be insensitive to background correlations from elliptic flow, jets, and momentum conservation. The hedge is that the method's sensitivity to the magnitude of the charge-separation parameter a_1 remains even when the reaction plane's ±π ambiguity makes global parity violation sign determination impossible, but the method still requires matching simulation to observed correlation to extract a_1.

## Method

The authors construct a multi-particle charge-sensitive in-event correlator C_c(ΔS) as the ratio of two distributions: N(ΔS_csep), the distribution of the difference between average sine of azimuthal angle relative to the event plane for positive and negative hadrons, and N(ΔS_cmix), the same quantity computed from randomly chosen hadrons irrespective of charge within the same event. Detailed simulations tuned to reproduce experimental features at RHIC were performed, incorporating elliptic flow (v_2), hexadecapole flow (v_4), resonance decays, jet correlations, detector acceptance, and event plane dispersion, to test the correlator's response to the charge-separation parameter a_1 and to various background sources.

## Key numbers

- The charge-separation signal parameter a_1 was tested at values of 0.03 and 0.06, with C_c(ΔS) shown to be responsive even to a relatively small charge asymmetry (Fig. 13).
- Simulations used Fourier coefficients v_2 and v_4 for elliptic and hexadecapole flow, with the azimuthal distribution N(Δφ) ∝ (1 + 2v_2 cos Δφ + 2v_4 cos(4Δφ) + 2a_1 sin(Δφ)).
- The method was tested for scenarios with 50% of events generated with a_1 > 0 and 50% with a_1 < 0 to mimic local parity violation, and also for 51%/49% and 49%/51% splits.
- Simulations were tuned to match experimental data from Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the search for the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral rotation effect (CRE) in heavy-ion collisions, following the STAR collaboration's reported charge asymmetry measurements (Abelev 2009) using a different correlator. The STAR correlator was found to be potentially sensitive to background correlations from elliptic flow, jets, and momentum conservation (Bzdak 2010, Wang 2009, Schlichting 2010). The new method aims to overcome these deficiencies and enable quantitative measurements of charge separation as a function of particle species, transverse momentum, collision centrality, and beam energy, which are prerequisites for investigating topological charge effects in the QGP related to the strong CP problem.
