# KbarN interaction and Kaons in the nuclear medium

> The s-wave meson-nucleon interaction in the S=-1 sector is studied by means of a coupled-channel Lippmann Schwinger equation, using the lowest order chiral Lagrangian and a cut off to regularize the loop integrals. The position and width of the Lambda(1405) resonance and the K^- p scattering cross sections at low energies are well reproduced. The inclusion of the eta Lambda, eta Sigma^0 channels in the coupled system is found very important and allows a solution in terms of only the lowest order Lagrangian. The model is applied to calculate the in-medium K^- self-energy to which we add a small p-wave piece resulting from the coupling to hyperon particle-nucleon hole excitations. The K^- feels an attraction of about -100 MeV at normal nuclear density. The Lambda(1405) resonance shifts to energies above the $K^-p$ threshold and ends up dissolving as density increases. It remains to be seen how these effects persist when the dressing of the Kbar and the pi mesons is incorporated self-consistently in the calculation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the s-wave antikaon-nucleon (KbarN) interaction in the S=-1 sector can be described using only the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian, provided the eta Lambda and eta Sigma^0 channels are included in the coupled-channel calculation; this avoids the need for higher-order terms that previous work (Kaiser et al., 1995) required. The resulting in-medium K^- self-energy gives an attraction of about -100 MeV at normal nuclear density, but the authors caution that this result may change when the dressing of the Kbar and pi mesons is incorporated self-consistently.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the s-wave meson-baryon interaction using the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian, with a cutoff (q_max = 630 MeV) to regularize loop integrals. The coupled channels include Kbar N, pi Lambda, pi Sigma, eta Lambda, eta Sigma, and K Xi states. The model is then applied to nuclear matter by including Pauli blocking on intermediate nucleon states and adding a small p-wave contribution from hyperon particle-nucleon hole excitations to compute the in-medium K^- self-energy.

## Key numbers

- The optimal cutoff is q_max = 630 MeV with f = 1.15 f_pi (where f_pi = 93 MeV).
- Threshold ratio gamma = 2.32 (experimental: 2.36 +/- 0.04), R_c = 0.627 (experimental: 0.664 +/- 0.011), R_n = 0.213 (experimental: 0.189 +/- 0.015).
- K^- p scattering length: (-1.00 + i 0.94) fm (full basis); K^- n scattering length: (0.53 + i 0.62) fm.
- At normal nuclear density rho_0, the K^- effective mass m*_K = 403 MeV, corresponding to an s-wave K^- nucleus potential of about -100 MeV.
- The K^- decay width at rho_0 is Gamma = 34 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel chiral approach of Kaiser et al. (1995) by including eta Lambda and eta Sigma channels, which allows a description using only the lowest-order Lagrangian rather than requiring higher-order terms. The in-medium results are qualitatively consistent with those of Waas, Weise, and Kaiser (1996) but give a K^- optical potential (-100 MeV) that is about half the value extracted from kaonic atom data analyses (Friedman, Gal, and Batty, 1994). The paper identifies the need for future self-consistent calculations that incorporate the dressing of both Kbar and pi mesons in the intermediate states, as attempted by Lutz (1998).
