# Surface-integral formalism of deuteron stripping

> The purpose of this paper is to develop an alternative theory of deuteron stripping to resonance states based on the surface integral formalism of Kadyrov et al. [Ann. Phys. 324, 1516 (2009)] and continuum-discretized coupled channels (CDCC). First we demonstrate how the surface integral formalism works in the three-body model and then we consider a more realistic problem in which a composite structure of target nuclei is taken via optical potentials. We explore different choices of channel wave functions and transition operators and show that a conventional CDCC volume matrix element can be written in terms of a surface-integral matrix element, which is peripheral, and an auxiliary matrix element, which determines the contribution of the nuclear interior over the variable $r_{nA}$. This auxiliary matrix element appears due to the inconsistency in treating of the $n-A$ potential: this potential should be real in the final state to support bound states or resonance scattering and complex in the initial state to describe $n-A$ scattering. Our main result is formulation of the theory of the stripping to resonance states using the prior form of the surface integral formalism and CDCC method. It is demonstrated that the conventional CDCC volume matrix element coincides with the surface matrix element, which converges for the stripping to the resonance state. Also the surface representation (over the variable $r_{nA}$ of the stripping matrix element enhances the peripheral part of the amplitude although the internal contribution doesn't disappear and increases with increase of the deuteron energy. We present calculations corroborating our findings for both stripping to the bound state and the resonance.

## Key claim

The paper develops an alternative theory of deuteron stripping to resonance states by combining the surface-integral formalism of Kadyrov et al. (2009) with continuum-discretized coupled channels (CDCC), showing that the conventional CDCC volume matrix element can be expressed as a surface-integral matrix element plus an auxiliary term arising from the inconsistency of using a real n-A potential in the final state and a complex one in the initial state.

## Method

The authors first demonstrate the surface-integral formalism in a three-body model with real potentials, then extend it to a many-body problem where the target's composite structure is treated via optical potentials. They derive expressions for the stripping matrix element in both post and prior forms, transforming volume integrals into surface integrals over the Jacobian variable ρ at a finite radius, with the second Jacobian variable r integrated over a limited volume due to the bound state wave function. The formalism is applied specifically to deuteron stripping reactions populating resonance states, using the prior form of the surface integral combined with CDCC wave functions calculated in a finite region around the target.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy of 2.224 MeV is required to break the deuteron, with additional energy needed to excite a resonance state, making the Q value negative for stripping to resonances
- The theory is aimed at deuteron incident energies from near the Coulomb barrier up to Ed ∼ 100 MeV
- The surface integral over ρβ is taken at a finite radius R̄β, which can be significantly larger than the nuclear radius, while the volume integral over rβ is confined to Rβ^N ≤ rβ ≤ Rβ, where Rβ is larger than 1/κβ (the bound state wave number)
- The typical accuracy for the volume matrix element convergence is ∼ 1% or better

## Context

This work extends the surface-integral formalism of Kadyrov et al. (2009) and Bray (2002) to deuteron stripping reactions, building on an earlier attempt by Mukhamedzhanov et al. (2011) that was incomplete because the surface integral extended into regions where CDCC fails. The new formulation restricts the surface integral to a finite region where CDCC is valid, addressing limitations of standard CDCC which lacks rearrangement channels in asymptotic regions. The approach enables analysis of deuteron stripping reactions on heavy nuclei (atomic number A ∼ 100) relevant to (n,γ) s- and r-processes in stellar evolution, where the Faddeev formalism is too complicated for routine use and limited to light nuclei due to Coulomb interaction problems.
