# Formation and Evolution of Quark-Gluon Plasma at RHIC and LHC

> Initial conditions for quark-gluon plasma formation at $τ=0.1\fm$ are considered in lowest order perturbative QCD. Chemical composition, thermalization of the formed semihard quark-gluon system and especially implications of the new HERA parton distributions with the enhancement at small $x$ are studied. The plasma at $τ=0.1\fm$ is shown to be strongly gluon dominated both at RHIC and LHC, and a possibility for rapid thermalization of gluons at LHC is pointed out. Uncertainties in the calculations, particularly shadowing corrections to the parton distributions, are discussed. Free streaming and ideal hydro limits for the evolution of the gluonic plasma with the calculated minijet initial conditions are demonstrated, and a lower limit for final multiplicities obtained for the LHC nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

Initial conditions for quark-gluon plasma formation at proper time τ = 0.1 fm are computed using lowest-order perturbative QCD, showing that the plasma is strongly gluon-dominated at both RHIC and LHC energies; however, the results carry significant uncertainties from the K-factor, the momentum cutoff p0, and especially from nuclear and proton shadowing corrections to the parton distributions, which are not yet implemented in the calculation.

## Method

The author computes the integrated minijet cross section and its first transverse-momentum moment using leading-order (O(αs²)) perturbative QCD with a constant K-factor of 2 to simulate higher-order contributions, employing parton distribution sets MRS D0', H, and D-' from Martin, Roberts and Stirling (1995) that incorporate the small-x enhancement observed at HERA. The average number of minijets and transverse energy in the central rapidity unit |y| ≤ 0.5 for central PbPb collisions are obtained by multiplying the pp jet cross section by the nuclear overlap function TAA(0) ≈ 32/mb. The evolution of the gluonic plasma is then studied in the two extreme limits of free streaming (P=0, ε ~ τ⁻¹) and ideal hydrodynamics (P=ε/3, ε ~ τ⁻⁴/³).

## Key numbers

- For LHC (√s = 5500 GeV) with p0 = 2 GeV and MRS H parton distributions: total minijet number N̅AA(0) = 3250 in |y| ≤ 0.5, of which 2710 are gluons, 276 quarks, and 266 antiquarks (Table 1).
- For RHIC (√s = 200 GeV) with p0 = 2 GeV and MRS H distributions: total minijet number N̅AA(0) = 200 in |y| ≤ 0.5, of which 157 are gluons, 26 quarks, and 17 antiquarks (Table 1).
- Initial transverse energy in central PbPb collisions at LHC with MRS H: E̅T = 10300 GeV in |y| ≤ 0.5; at RHIC with MRS H: E̅T = 547 GeV (Eq. 5).
- Initial energy density at τ = 0.1 fm for MRS D0' distributions: ε(τi) ≈ 410 GeV/fm³ at LHC and 43 GeV/fm³ at RHIC.
- Ratio of gluon number to quark+antiquark number from pQCD: ~3.7 at RHIC and ~5 at LHC, compared to the ideal gas equilibrium ratio ≤ 0.9 for Nf ≥ 2 (Eqs. 6-7).
- Energy per gluon at LHC for MRS H: 3.2 GeV, compared to 2.7Teq = 2.6 GeV for a thermalized gluon gas (Eq. 8).
- Lower limit for charged final multiplicity dNch/dy at LHC: 2180 for MRS H, ranging from 914 (DO1) to 3360 (D-') (Eq. 10).

## Context

This work extends earlier minijet calculations for ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions by Wang and Gyulassy (1991) and the Parton Cascade Model of Geiger and Müller (1992), incorporating the new HERA parton distributions from Martin, Roberts and Stirling (1995) that show a steep rise at small x. The results demonstrate that at LHC energies the initial gluon density may be high enough for rapid thermalization, unlike at RHIC where secondary interactions are still needed to degrade gluon energies. The paper identifies the need for future work to implement nuclear shadowing corrections using the Gribov-Levin-Ryskin-Mueller-Qiu formalism (1983) and to perform a full DGLAP-GLRMQ evolution for nuclear parton distributions, which would reduce the computed initial energy densities and multiplicities.
