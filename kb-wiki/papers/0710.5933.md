# Three-body description of direct nuclear reactions: Comparison with the continuum discretized coupled channels method

> The continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) method is compared to the exact solution of the three-body Faddeev equations in momentum space. We present results for: i) elastic and breakup observables of d-12C at E_d=56 MeV, ii) elastic scattering of d-58Ni at E_d=80 MeV, and iii) elastic, breakup and transfer observables for 11Be+p at E_{11Be}/A=38.4 MeV. Our comparative studies show that, in the first two cases, the CDCC method is a good approximation to the full three-body Faddeev solution, but for the 11Be exotic nucleus, depending on the observable or the kinematic regime, it may miss out some of the dynamic three-body effects that appear through the explicit coupling to the transfer channel.

## Key claim

The continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) method is a good approximation to the exact three-body Faddeev solution for deuteron-induced reactions on stable targets, but for reactions involving the exotic halo nucleus 11Be, CDCC may miss dynamic three-body effects that appear through explicit coupling to the transfer channel, depending on the observable or kinematic regime.

## Method

The authors solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations in momentum space for three-body systems, treating the Coulomb interaction via screening and renormalization techniques with a screening radius R and smoothness parameter n=4. They compared these exact solutions to CDCC calculations using both the standard breakup (CDCC-BU) approach, where the projectile continuum is discretized and coupled, and the transfer-to-continuum (CDCC-TR*) approach, where the CDCC wavefunction is used in the prior form of the transition amplitude for transfer reactions.

## Key numbers

- d+12C elastic and breakup at Ed=56 MeV: CDCC reproduces exact three-body results up to very large scattering angles; agreement with data at forward angles (theta_CM < 60 degrees) but deteriorates for backward angles
- d+58Ni elastic at Ed=80 MeV: CDCC is a good approximation to the full three-body Faddeev solution
- 11Be+p at E_11Be/A=38.4 MeV: CDCC-BU calculations required partial waves up to l_max=8 and multipoles up to Q_max=8 for the n-10Be relative motion, with continuum bins up to Emax=34 MeV for l<=6 and Emax=32 MeV for l=7,8; CDCC-TR* calculations used partial waves up to l<=8 for pn relative motion with bins up to Emax=35 MeV

## Context

This work provides the first direct numerical comparison between CDCC and exact Faddeev/AGS solutions for nuclear reactions, extending earlier formal discussions by Austern (1989, 1996) that lacked numerical verification. The study builds on recent progress by Deltuva (2005) for proton-deuteron scattering and earlier CDCC applications to deuteron reactions by Yahiro (1982-1986) and to radioactive nuclear beams by Tostevin (2001, 2002) and Moro (2003, 2006). The results enable benchmarking of CDCC accuracy for reactions involving halo nuclei like 11Be, where previous work by Moro (2006) showed that different CDCC basis choices gave substantially different breakup cross sections.
