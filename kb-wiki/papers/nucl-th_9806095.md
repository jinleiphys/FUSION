# Relativistic Equation of State of Nuclear Matter for Supernova Explosion

> We construct the equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter at finite temperature and density with various proton fractions within the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory for the use in the supernova simulations. The Thomas-Fermi approximation is adopted to describe the non-uniform matter where we consider nucleus, alpha-particle, proton and neutron in equilibrium. We treat the uniform matter and non-uniform matter consistently using the RMF theory. We tabulate the outcome as the pressure, free energy, entropy etc, with enough mesh points in wide ranges of the temperature, proton fraction, and baryon mass density.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a tabulated equation of state (EOS) for nuclear matter at finite temperature and density with various proton fractions, using the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the TM1 parameter set, specifically for use in supernova simulations; the authors note that this EOS should be considered a "basis EOS" that may later require inclusion of hyperons, mesons, or quarks at high density and temperature.

## Method

The authors adopt the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with non-linear sigma and omega meson self-coupling terms (parameter set TM1) to describe homogeneous nuclear matter. For densities below about 10^14.2 g/cm^3 and temperatures below 15 MeV, they use the Thomas-Fermi approximation to model non-uniform matter as a mixture of free neutrons, free protons, alpha-particles, and a single species of heavy nuclei arranged in a body-centered-cubic lattice, minimizing the free energy with respect to eight independent parameters. Uniform matter and non-uniform matter are treated consistently within the same RMF framework, and the resulting thermodynamical quantities (pressure, free energy, entropy) are tabulated over wide ranges of density, temperature, and proton fraction.

## Key numbers

- Density range of the EOS table: ρ_B = 10^5.1 to 10^15.4 g/cm^3
- Temperature range: T = 0 to 100 MeV
- Proton fraction range: Y_p = 0 to 0.56
- Symmetry energy with TM1 parameter set: 36.9 MeV
- Incompressibility with TM1 parameter set: 281 MeV
- Binding energy of alpha-particle relative to free neutrons: B_α = 28.3 MeV
- Effective volume of an alpha-particle: v_α = 24 fm^-3
- Surface energy parameter: F_0 = 70 MeV·fm^5
- BCC lattice Coulomb correction coefficient: C_BCC = 0.006562
- Non-uniform matter phase disappears above approximately ρ_B ~ 10^14.2 g/cm^3

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field approach to nuclear matter EOS by including alpha-particles in the Thomas-Fermi treatment of non-uniform matter, building on the earlier zero-temperature results of Shen et al. (1998) which did not include alpha-particles. The EOS table is designed for use in supernova simulations, where the authors note that previous non-relativistic EOS studies have been used, and the relativistic framework provides a more consistent description of nuclear saturation properties. The work enables future studies of supernova explosion mechanisms and neutron star birth using a relativistic EOS that has been checked against finite nuclei properties, with the authors stating that at high density and temperature the inclusion of hyperons, mesons, or quarks may eventually be needed.
