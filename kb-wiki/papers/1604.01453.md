# Monopole transitions to cluster states in $^{10}$Be and $^9$Li

> Isoscalar monopole transitions from the ground states to cluster states in $\Be$ and $\Li$ are investigated with $\He+α$ and $\He+t$ cluster models, respectively. In $\Be$, significant monopole strengths to $\He+α$ cluster resonances of $\Be(0^+_{3,4})$ above the $α$-decay threshold are obtained, whereas those to $\He+t$ cluster resonances in $^9$Li are not enhanced because of the large fragmentation of the strengths in the corresponding energy region. The monopole transition to $\Be(0^+_2)$ having the molecular orbital structure is relatively weak compared with those to $\He+α$ cluster resonances. Monopole strength distributions do not directly correspond to distributions of $\He(0^+)+α$ and $\He(0+)+t$ components but they reflect component of the deformed $\He$ cluster with a specific orientation, which is originally embedded in the ground state.

## Key claim

Isoscalar monopole (ISM) transitions can serve as a good probe for observing \(^6\text{He}+\alpha\) cluster resonances in \(^{10}\text{Be}\), but the same does not hold for \(^6\text{He}+t\) cluster resonances in \(^9\text{Li}\) because the monopole strengths are too fragmented across many states in the relevant energy region.

## Method

The author used the generator coordinate method (GCM) with \(^6\text{He}+\alpha\) and \(^6\text{He}+t\) cluster wave functions, employing the Bloch-Brink formalism with harmonic oscillator single-particle wave functions. The \(^6\text{He}\) cluster was described by a full \(p\)-shell configuration space combining \(ls\)-coupling and \(jj\)-coupling schemes, and the basis included six configurations at inter-cluster distances \(D = 1\) to \(8\) fm (and up to \(15\) fm for continuum coupling). Resonance states were obtained in a bound-state approximation, and their decay widths were estimated from reduced width amplitudes at channel radii of 5, 6, and 7 fm.

## Key numbers

- The calculated total decay width of the \(^{10}\text{Be}(0^+_3)\) resonance is \(\Gamma_{\text{sum}} = 1.6\) MeV, with dominant \(^6\text{He}(0^+)+\alpha\) decay at a decay energy of 3.2 MeV.
- The calculated total decay width of the \(^{10}\text{Be}(0^+_4)\) resonance is \(\Gamma_{\text{sum}} = 1.0\) MeV, with comparable \(^6\text{He}(0^+)+\alpha\) and \(^6\text{He}(2^+)+\alpha\) contributions at a decay energy of 4.7 MeV.
- The calculated total decay widths of the \(^9\text{Li}(3/2^-_3)\), \(3/2^-_4\), and \(3/2^-_5\) resonances are \(\Gamma_{\text{sum}} = 0.65\), 0.75, and 2.0 MeV, respectively.
- The energy-weighted sum (EWS) of ISM strengths in \(^{10}\text{Be}\) is \(2.7 \times 10^2\) MeV fm\(^4\), which is 61% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) of \(4.5 \times 10^2\) MeV fm\(^4\).
- The EWS in \(^9\text{Li}\) is \(1.68 \times 10^2\) MeV fm\(^4\), which is 46% of the EWSR of \(3.7 \times 10^2\) MeV fm\(^4\).
- The calculated matter radius of \(^{10}\text{Be}\) is 2.34 fm, compared to the experimental value of 2.30(2) fm; for \(^9\text{Li}\) the calculated matter radius is 2.22 fm, compared to 2.32(2) fm experimentally.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of cluster states in neutron-rich Be and Li isotopes, particularly the generalized two-center cluster model (GTCM) calculations by Ito et al. (2003-2014) that predicted \(^8\text{He}+^4\text{He}\) and \(^6\text{He}+^6\text{He}\) cluster resonances in \(^{12}\text{Be}\). The author had previously predicted \(^6\text{He}(0^+)+t\) cluster resonances in \(^9\text{Li}\) using the same cluster model (Kanada-En'yo, 2011). The present results show that ISM transitions can experimentally identify \(^6\text{He}+\alpha\) cluster resonances in \(^{10}\text{Be}\), but the strong fragmentation of ISM strengths in \(^9\text{Li}\) makes such identification difficult for the \(^6\text{He}+t\) cluster resonances.
