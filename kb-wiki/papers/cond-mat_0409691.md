# A symmetry-projected variational approach to the 1-dimensional Hubbard-model

> We apply a variational method devised for the nuclear many--body problem to the 1-dimensional Hubbard--model with nearest neighbor hopping and periodic boundary conditions. The test wave function consist for each state out of a single Hartree--Fock determinant mixing all the sites (or momenta) as well as the spin--projections of the electrons. Total spin and linear momentum are restored by projection methods before the variation. It is demonstrated that this approach reproduces the results of exact diagonalisations for half--filled N=12 and N=14 lattices not only for the energies and occupation numbers of the ground but also of the lowest excited states rather well. Furthermore, a system of 10 electrons in a N=12 lattice is investigated and, finally, a N=30 lattice is studied. In addition to energies and occupation numbers we present the spectral functions computed with the help of the symmetry--projected wave functions, too. Also here nice agreement with the exact results (where available) is reached.

## Key claim

The symmetry-projected variational method (LMSPHF), using a single Hartree-Fock determinant with total spin and linear momentum restored before variation, reproduces exact diagonalization results for the 1D Hubbard model at half-filling for N=12 and N=14 lattices, but only for the ground and lowest excited states, and only "rather well" rather than exactly.

## Method

The authors apply a variational approach using a single general Hartree-Fock determinant that mixes all lattice sites (or momenta) and spin projections as the test wave function. Total spin is restored via Villars' projection operator and linear momentum via a finite 1D projection operator, both applied before the variation. The energy functional is minimized with respect to the Hartree-Fock transformation (parameterized via Thouless' theorem) and the mixing coefficients, using a quasi-Newton procedure, with excited states treated by projecting out lower-lying solutions before variation.

## Key numbers

- For half-filled N=12 lattice with U/t=4.0, the ground state energy from LMSPHF is -17.34 t, compared to exact diagonalization result of -17.38 t (difference of 0.04 t).
- For half-filled N=14 lattice with U/t=4.0, the ground state energy from LMSPHF is -20.28 t, compared to exact diagonalization result of -20.33 t (difference of 0.05 t).
- For the half-filled N=30 lattice with U/t=4.0, the LMSPHF ground state energy is -43.62 t (no exact result available for comparison).
- For the 10-electron system on N=12 lattice with U/t=4.0, the LMSPHF ground state energy is -15.87 t, compared to exact diagonalization result of -15.91 t (difference of 0.04 t).
- For half-filling with N=12 and U/t=4.0, the first excited state (S=0, ξ=0) energy from LMSPHF is -16.82 t, compared to exact result of -16.87 t.

## Context

This work extends the VAMPIR approach, previously applied successfully to nuclear many-body problems (Schmid, 2004), to the 1-dimensional Hubbard model as a test of the method's applicability to condensed matter systems. The approach is contrasted with Green's function Monte Carlo methods (which suffer from the sign problem for fermions) and the Quantum Monte Carlo Diagonalization method (Otsuka, 2001). The results demonstrate that symmetry-projected variational methods can provide accurate approximations for lattice fermion systems where exact diagonalization becomes intractable (e.g., N=30 lattices with ~10^14 configurations), enabling future studies of larger systems and spectral functions.
