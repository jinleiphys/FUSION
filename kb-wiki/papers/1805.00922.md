# On the Properties of Neutrinos

> Our present understanding of neutrino properties is reviewed with a particular emphasis on observable differences between Majorana and Dirac neutrinos. Current and future experimental efforts towards measuring neutrino properties are summarized. Consequences of the Majorana vs.Dirac nature of neutrinos on neutrino masses, neutrino decays, and neutrino electromagnetic properties are described.

# On the Properties of Neutrinos

## Key claim

The paper reviews neutrino properties with a particular emphasis on observable differences between Majorana and Dirac neutrinos, but notes that for ultra-relativistic neutrinos (the only kind directly studied so far), helicity acts as a substitute for lepton number, making Majorana neutrinos behave as if they are Dirac neutrinos under almost all circumstances.

## Method

The authors present a comprehensive review of neutrino physics, covering the formalism of neutrino oscillations, the parameterization of the Pontecorvo-Maki-Nakagawa-Sakata (PMNS) mixing matrix, and the theoretical construction of Dirac and Majorana mass terms in the Lagrangian. They describe the See-Saw mechanism as a leading candidate for explaining small neutrino masses, and discuss experimental approaches including oscillation experiments, direct beta-decay mass measurements (Tritium experiments), cosmological constraints from cosmic microwave background observations, and neutrinoless double beta decay searches.

- Smaller mass-squared difference: (7.53 ± 0.18) × 10⁻⁵ eV² (Patrignani 2016)
- Larger mass-squared difference (normal hierarchy): (2.45 ± 0.05) × 10⁻³ eV²; (inverted hierarchy): (2.52 ± 0.05) × 10⁻³ eV² (Patrignani 2016)
- Troitsk experiment limit: m_β < 2.2 eV from m_β² = -0.67 ± 2.53 eV² (Aseev 2011)
- Mainz experiment limit: m_β < 2.3 eV from m_β² = -0.6 ± 2.2 (stat) ± 2.1 (syst) eV² (Kraus 2004)
- KATRIN expected sensitivity: 0.2 eV (Osipowicz 2001)
- Project 8 potential sensitivity: m_β ~ 40 meV (Esfahani 2017)
- Planck Collaboration N_eff = 3.15 ± 0.23 (Ade 2015)
- Planck Collaboration upper limit on sum of neutrino masses: 0.23 eV (Ade 2015)
- Higgs vacuum expectation value: v = 174 GeV
- See-Saw prediction example: for m_ν ~ 0.1 eV and m_D ~ m_μ, heavy neutrino mass m_N ~ 10⁸ GeV

## Context

This review extends the understanding of neutrino properties established by oscillation experiments (Mikheev, Smirnov, Wolfenstein 1986, 1979) and direct mass measurements from Troitsk and Mainz. It contrasts the Dirac mass mechanism (requiring right-handed neutrinos with Yukawa coupling y ~ 10⁻¹²) with Majorana mass mechanisms that involve physics beyond the Standard Model, particularly the See-Saw mechanism (refK.3). The paper enables future experimental programs including KATRIN, Project 8, and neutrinoless double beta decay searches to determine whether neutrinos are Dirac or Majorana particles.
