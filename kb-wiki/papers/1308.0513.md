# Rotation and alignment of high-$j$ orbitals in transfermium nuclei

> The structure of nuclei with $Z\sim100$ is investigated systematically by the Cranked Shell Model (CSM) with pairing correlations treated by a Particle-Number Conserving (PNC) method. In the PNC method, the particle number is conserved and the Pauli blocking effects are taken into account exactly. By fitting the experimental single-particle spectra in these nuclei, a new set of Nilsson parameters ($κ$ and $μ$) is proposed. The experimental kinematic moments of inertia and the band-head energies are reproduced quite well by the PNC-CSM calculations. The band crossing, the effects of high-$j$ intruder orbitals and deformation are discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The Cranked Shell Model with a Particle-Number Conserving (PNC) method, using a newly fitted set of Nilsson parameters, reproduces experimental kinematic moments of inertia and band-head energies for transfermium nuclei (Z~100), but the agreement depends on including the proton N=7 shell and specific deformation values to capture the observed signature splitting in 251Md.

## Method

The authors diagonalized the Cranked Shell Model Hamiltonian (Nilsson Hamiltonian plus Coriolis term and monopole/quadrupole pairing) in a cranked many-particle configuration (CMPC) space with Fock-space truncation, conserving particle number exactly and treating Pauli blocking fully. They fitted a new set of Nilsson parameters (κ and μ) to experimental single-particle levels in odd-A nuclei with Z≈100, then calculated kinematic moments of inertia J^(1) for rotational bands in Bk, Es, and Md isotopes. The proton N=7 shell was included in the CMPC space to study the effect of the 1/2^-[770] intruder orbital on band crossing in 251Md.

## Key numbers

- New Nilsson parameters for protons: for N=4 (all l) κ_p=0.0670, μ_p=0.654; for N=5, l=1: κ_p=0.0250, μ_p=0.710; for N=5, l=3: κ_p=0.0570, μ_p=0.800; for N=5, l=5: κ_p=0.0570, μ_p=0.710; for N=6 (all l): κ_p=0.0570, μ_p=0.654.
- New Nilsson parameters for neutrons: for N=6, l=0: κ_n=0.1600, μ_n=0.320; for N=6, l=2: κ_n=0.0640, μ_n=0.200; for N=6, l=4,6: κ_n=0.0680, μ_n=0.260; for N=7, l=1,3,5,7: κ_n=0.0634, μ_n=0.318.
- Effective pairing strengths used for all odd-N nuclei: G_n=0.30 MeV, G_2n=0.02 MeV, G_p=0.25 MeV, G_2p=0.01 MeV.
- In 251Md, a sharp backbending of the α=-1/2 signature band of the 1/2^-[521] band occurs at ℏω≈0.15 MeV, while the α=+1/2 band varies smoothly; the band crossing between 1/2^-[521] and 1/2^-[770] configurations occurs at ℏω≈0.15 MeV for α=-1/2 and ℏω≈0.30 MeV for α=+1/2.
- For 251Md with the proton N=7 shell included, signature splitting occurs at ℏω≈0.225 MeV for deformation ε_2=0.28 and at ℏω≈0.275 MeV for ε_2=0.255; without the N=7 shell, no signature splitting appears at either deformation.

## Context

This work extends the PNC-CSM method, previously applied to rare-earth and actinide nuclei by Zeng and collaborators (1983, 1994), to the transfermium region (Z~100) where experimental in-beam spectroscopy is limited. The new Nilsson parameters improve upon the standard set from Nilsson (1969) and Bengtsson (1985), which were optimized for lighter deformed nuclei. The results enable detailed study of how high-j intruder orbitals (specifically the proton 1j_15/2 orbital, labeled 1/2^-[770]) affect rotational band crossing and signature splitting in 251Md, and provide a framework for predicting the structure of heavier superheavy nuclei where the 2f_5/2-2f_7/2 spin-orbit splitting influences the Z=114 shell gap.
