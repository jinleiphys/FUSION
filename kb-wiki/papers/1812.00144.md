# Impact of final state interactions on neutrino-nucleon pion production cross sections extracted from neutrino-deuteron reaction data

> The current and near-future neutrino oscillation experiments require significantly improved neutrino-nucleus reaction models. Neutrino-nucleon pion production data play a crucial role to validate corresponding elementary amplitudes that go into such neutrino-nucleus models. Thus the currently available data extracted from charged-current neutrino-deuteron reaction data ($ν_μd\to μ^-πNN$) must be corrected for nuclear effects such as the Fermi motion and final state interactions (FSI). We study $ν_μd\to μ^-πNN$ with a theoretical model including the impulse mechanism supplemented by FSI from $NN$ and $πN$ rescatterings. An analysis of the spectator momentum distributions reveals that the FSI effects significantly reduce the spectra over the quasi-free peak region, and leads to a useful recipe to extract information of elementary $ν_μN\to μ^-πN$ processes using $ν_μd\to μ^-πNN$ data, with the important FSI corrections taken into account. We provide $ν_μN\to μ^-πN$ total cross sections by correcting the deuterium bubble chamber data for the FSI and Fermi motion. The results will bring a significant improvement on neutrino-nucleus reaction models for the near-future neutrino-oscillation experiments.

## Key claim

Final state interactions (FSI) from nucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon rescatterings significantly reduce the spectator momentum distributions in neutrino-deuteron pion production, and correcting the existing bubble chamber data for these effects yields improved neutrino-nucleon pion production cross sections that are essential for neutrino oscillation experiments.

## Method

The authors developed a theoretical model for the reaction νμd → μ-πNN that includes the impulse mechanism plus FSI from NN and πN rescatterings, using elementary weak pion production amplitudes and πN scattering amplitudes from a dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model. The deuteron wave function and NN scattering amplitudes were generated from the CD-Bonn potential. They analyzed spectator momentum distributions in νμd → μ-πNN and derived a phenomenological formula for the correction factor Nα(Eν, ps) that relates the deuteron-target cross section to the elementary νμN → μ-πN cross section, then applied this correction to reanalyzed ANL and BNL bubble chamber data.

## Key numbers

- FSI effects reduce the neutron momentum distribution in νμd → μ-π+pn by 10-20% for Eν = 0.5 GeV and 5-10% for Eν = 1.5 GeV
- The reduction of the proton momentum distribution near the quasifree peak is approximately 2 times larger than the reduction of the neutron spectra
- The correction factors for the total cross sections are 1.05-1.12 for νμp → μ-π+p, 1.10-1.27 for νμn → μ-π+n, and 1.01-1.02 for νμn → μ-π0p
- Corrected ANL data for νμp → μ-π+p gives σ = 0.065 × 10-38 cm2 in the energy bin 0.3-0.6 GeV and σ = 0.821 × 10-38 cm2 in the bin 1.5-2.2 GeV
- Corrected BNL data for νμn → μ-π+n gives σ = 0.160 × 10-38 cm2 in the bin 0.4-0.6 GeV and σ = 0.361 × 10-38 cm2 in the bin 1.8-2.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of FSI effects in νμd → μ-πNN by Wu et al. (2015), who found sizable FSI effects in the quasifree Δ(1232)-production region but did not cover the full phase space needed for correcting the existing data. The authors had previously validated their DCC-based approach on γd → πNN and γd → ηpn reactions, showing that significant FSI effects bring model predictions into agreement with data. The corrected cross sections enable improved neutrino-nucleus reaction models for oscillation experiments such as T2K and DUNE, and the recipe for extracting elementary amplitudes from future deuteron-target experiments provides a path to further reduce systematic uncertainties.
