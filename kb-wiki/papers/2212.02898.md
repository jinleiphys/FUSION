# Half-lives for proton emission and α decay within the deformed Gamow-like model

> In the present work, we study α decay and proton emission half-lives within the modified Gamow-like model, which introduces the effects of the nucleus's deformation. The calculations show that it is necessary to consider the deformation in the calculation for nuclei far from the shell. Moreover, we use the improved model to predict the proton emission half-lives of the nuclei far from the shell. The calculation results indicate that our model is in good agreement with most models. Furthermore, the deformed Gamow-like model is used to find the following neutron magic number. This work is meaningful for future research on superheavy nuclei.

# Half-lives for proton emission and α decay within the deformed Gamow-like model

## Key claim

The modified Gamow-like model that includes nuclear deformation improves agreement with experimental half-life data for nuclei far from shell closures, but the improvement is modest: the standard deviation for proton emission half-lives improves from 0.618 to 0.556 (about 10% overall), and for nuclei far from shell (Z ≤ 67 or Z = 83) the improvement reaches 22%.

## Method

The authors modify the Gamow-like model by introducing quadrupole (β₂), hexadecapole (β₄), and hexacontatetrapole (β₆) deformations taken from the FRDM2012 nuclear mass model into the square-well radius and Coulomb potential. The deformed Coulomb potential is computed using the double-folding model with Fourier transform approximation, and the total penetration probability is obtained by averaging over all orientation angles of the emitted particle relative to the daughter nucleus symmetry axis. The adjustable parameter r₀ is redetermined by least-squares fitting to experimental data: r₀ = 1.21 fm for proton emission and r₀ = 1.19 fm for α decay, with hindrance factors hₙ = 0.400, hₚ = 0.346, and hₙₚ = 0.528 for odd-neutron, odd-proton, and doubly-odd nuclei respectively.

## Key numbers

- Standard deviation for proton emission half-lives: σ_cal1 = 0.618 (Gamow-like model) vs σ_cal2 = 0.556 (deformed Gamow-like model), an improvement of approximately 10%
- For the 12 nuclei far from shell (Z ≤ 67 or Z = 83), the standard deviation improvement is 22%
- Deformed Gamow-like model reproduces proton emission half-lives within a factor of 3.59
- For α decay of 190 even-even nuclei: σ₁ = 0.453 (Gamow-like) vs σ₂ = 0.516 (deformed), with 12.21% improvement in agreement
- For α decay of 137 even-Z, odd-N nuclei: σ₁ = 0.785 vs σ₂ = 0.846, improvement 7.21%
- For α decay of 124 odd-Z, even-N nuclei: σ₁ = 0.712 vs σ₂ = 0.761, improvement 5.3%
- For α decay of 123 doubly-odd nuclei: σ₁ = 0.841 vs σ₂ = 0.909, improvement 7.5%
- The adjustable parameter r₀ = 1.21 fm for proton emission and r₀ = 1.19 fm for α decay
- Hindrance factors: hₙ = 0.400, hₚ = 0.346, hₙₚ = 0.528
- The lowest value of σⁿ_avg occurs at neutron number N = 152, indicating a candidate for the next neutron magic number

## Context

This work extends the Gamow-like model originally proposed by Zdeb et al. (2013) for α decay and later extended to proton emission by Zdeb et al. (2016), by incorporating nuclear deformation effects that were previously neglected. The calculations show that for nuclei far from shell closures, deformation significantly affects the penetration probability and half-life calculations. The model is used to predict proton emission half-lives for five unquantified nuclei (¹¹¹Cs, ¹²⁷Pm, ¹³⁷Tb, ¹⁸⁵Bi, and an isomer of ¹⁸⁵Bi) and compared with predictions from the universal decay law for proton emission (UDLP), the Coulomb and proximity potential model with Guo-2013 formalism (CPPM-Guo2013), the two-potential approach with DDM3Y folding potentials (TPA-DDM3Y), the new Geiger-Nuttall law (NGNL), and the two-potential approach based on Skyrme-Hartree-Fock with MQSP (TPA-SHF-MQSP). The analysis of σⁿ_avg for even-even nuclei with neutron numbers 140-160 suggests N = 152 as a candidate for the next neutron magic number, which is relevant for superheavy nuclei research.
