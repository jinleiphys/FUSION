# Charmonium mass in hot and dense hadronic matter

> We investigate mass shifts of charmonia driven by change of the gluon condensate below but near transition temperatures at finite baryonic chemical potential. Extending previous prescription on the relation between gluon condensates and thermodynamic quantities, we model the gluon condensates of hadronic matter at finite temperature and baryonic chemical potential such that the scalar gluon condensate fits with the latest lattice QCD data. By making use of the QCD sum rule and the second order Stark effect, we find that the smoother transition in the full QCD can lead to moderate mass shifts of charmonia even below the transition temperature. We also find larger mass shift at fixed temperature as chemical potential increases. Existing data on charmonium-charmonium ratio is found to be consistent with the statistical hadronization scenario including the obtained mass shift.

## Key claim

The authors find that charmonia experience moderate downward mass shifts (10–20 MeV at high collision energies) in hot and dense hadronic matter below the pseudocritical temperature, with larger shifts at fixed temperature as baryonic chemical potential increases; however, the magnitude of the shift at low collision energies depends strongly on which freeze-out parameterization is used, ranging from 10 to 60 MeV.

## Method

The authors model the scalar and twist-2 gluon condensates of hadronic matter at finite temperature and baryonic chemical potential using a hadron resonance gas, fitting the scalar gluon condensate to lattice QCD data from the HotQCD and Budapest-Wuppertal collaborations. They then compute charmonium mass shifts using two complementary approaches: the second order Stark effect formula (derived from potential non-relativistic QCD) and QCD sum rules in the Borel framework, both of which relate the mass shift to the temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent gluon condensates.

## Key numbers

- Downward mass shift of J/ψ at high collision energies (√sNN > 30 GeV): 10–20 MeV, including uncertainty.
- Downward mass shift at the lowest SPS energy (√sNN ≈ 8.76 GeV): ranges from 10–60 MeV depending on the choice of freeze-out parameterization (FOI vs FOII).
- Pseudocritical temperature range from lattice QCD: 147 MeV (chiral susceptibility) to 165 MeV (strange quark number susceptibility).
- Vacuum scalar gluon condensate fixed to (0.35 GeV)^4.
- Charm quark mass used in Stark effect calculation: mc = 1704 MeV; Bohr radius a = 0.271 fm; αs = 0.57.
- Freeze-out temperature parameterization FOI: T(μB) = 0.166 − 0.139 μB^2 − 0.053 μB^4 (in GeV units).
- Freeze-out temperature parameterization FOII: Tlim = 161 ± 4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach using QCD sum rules and the second order Stark effect (Morita et al., 2009; Lee and Morita, 2009) from pure SU(3) gauge theory to full QCD with dynamical light quarks, incorporating the crossover nature of the transition found by lattice QCD (Aoki et al., 2006; Borsanyi et al., 2010). The results enable comparison with experimental charmonium-to-charmonium ratios from heavy-ion collisions at SPS and RHIC energies, and show consistency with the statistical hadronization scenario (Andronic et al., 2009) when the calculated mass shifts are included.
