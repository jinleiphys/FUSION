# Transverse flow of nuclear matter in collisions of heavy nuclei at intermediate energies

> The Quantum Molecular Dynamics Model (IQMD) is used to investigate the origin of the collective transverse velocity observed in heavy ion experiments. We find that there are three contributions to this effect: initial-final state correlations, potential interactions and collisions. For a given nuclear equation of state (eos) the increase of the transverse velocity with increasing beam energy is caused by the potential part. For a given beam energy the collective transverse velocity is independent of the nuclear eos but the relative contributions of potential and collisions differ. In view of the importance of the potential interactions between the nucleons it is not evident that the similarity of the radial velocities measured for fragments at beam energies below 1 AGeV and that for mesons at beam energies above 2 AGeV is more than accidental.

## Key claim

The collective transverse velocity observed in heavy ion collisions arises from three distinct contributions (initial-final state correlations, potential interactions, and collisions), and while the increase of this velocity with beam energy is driven by the potential part (compression), the total transverse velocity at a given beam energy is nearly independent of the nuclear equation of state because the potential and collisional contributions counterbalance each other.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model with a Gaussian wave function variance of L=4.33 fm² and static potentials to simulate central (b=1 fm) Au+Au collisions at intermediate energies. They decomposed the transverse velocity of fragments observed at mid-rapidity (y^CM/y_Proj.^CM ≤ 0.3) into three components: the initial transverse velocity of nucleons that later form fragments, the velocity change from the nuclear potential, and the velocity change from baryon-baryon collisions. The collective transverse velocity β_t and temperature T were extracted by fitting the mass-dependent transverse velocities v_t(A) to the formula v_t(A) = sqrt(β_t² + 3T/(Am)).

## Key numbers

- For beam energies above 500A MeV, the collisional contribution to β_t is approximately constant at β ≈ 0.1, and the initial velocity contribution is about β ≈ 0.04.
- The hard and soft equations of state correspond to compressibilities κ = 376 MeV and κ = 200 MeV, respectively.
- At 1A GeV, the potential contributes between 30% and 60% of the total radial velocity, depending on the equation of state.
- At 250A MeV for Au+Au at b=1 fm, the potential plays only a minor role, with the transverse velocity dominated by initial-final state correlations and collisions.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of collective flow in heavy ion collisions beyond the directed in-plane flow (bounce-off) and out-of-plane flow (squeeze-out) studied previously, as reviewed by the authors' references (cathechism, joerg, rr, rrr). It directly addresses the question raised by experimental observations of radial flow at energies from 150A MeV to 1A GeV (radflow1-radflow4) and at AGS/CERN energies (sta1, sta2), questioning whether the smooth joining of fragment-based β_t below 1A GeV with meson-based β_t above 2A GeV has a common origin. The finding that the potential contributes substantially to fragment flow but is absent for mesons from string decay casts doubt on the assumption of a smooth transition, and the result that total β_t is nearly independent of compressibility means that radial flow alone cannot be used to extract the nuclear equation of state, contrary to earlier hopes.
