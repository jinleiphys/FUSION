# Beyond the MSW effect: Neutrinos in a dense medium

> We present a theory of neutrino oscillations in a dense medium which goes beyond the effective matter potential used in the description of the MSW effect. We show how the purity of the neutrino state is degraded by neutrino interactions with the environment and how neutrino--matter interactions can be a source of decoherence. We present new oscillation formulae for neutrinos interacting with leptons and carry out a numerical analysis which exhibits deviations from the MSW formulae for propagation through the Earth of ultra-high energy neutrinos. In particular, we show that at high density and/or high neutrino energy, the vanishing transition probabilities derived for MSW effect, are non zero when the scattering is taken into account.

## Key claim

The paper claims that at high neutrino energy and/or high medium density, the vanishing transition probabilities predicted by the MSW effect become non-zero when explicit neutrino-matter scattering events are taken into account, because the scattering introduces decoherence that the effective MSW potential cannot capture.

## Method

The authors model neutrino propagation in a dense medium as a sequence of unitary coherent evolution intervals (mixing Hamiltonian plus MSW matter potential) punctuated by nearly instantaneous scattering events described by a quantum mechanical interaction Hamiltonian derived from field-theoretical amplitudes for neutrino-lepton elastic and quasi-elastic scattering. They compute the neutrino density matrix by averaging over the Poisson-distributed number of collisions and the random times at which they occur, then trace out the charged lepton degrees of freedom to obtain the reduced neutrino density matrix, which exhibits dissipative (non-unitary) evolution.

## Key numbers

* For electronic densities of order \(N_e = N_A~\text{cm}^{-3}\) (with \(N_A\) Avogadro's number) and neutrino energies \(E \sim 1\) GeV, the interaction rate \(\Gamma = N_e \sigma_T\) is as low as \(\Gamma \sim 10^{-7}~\text{s}^{-1}\).
* The total cross-section for neutrino-lepton scattering grows linearly with neutrino energy: \(\sigma_{\nu l} \propto E_{\nu}\).
* The MSW matter potential is \(V_{\alpha}(\pmb{x},t) = \pm \delta_{\alpha e} \sqrt{2} G_F N_e (\pmb{x},t)\), where the minus sign is for antineutrinos.
* The mixing Hamiltonian parameter is \(\omega_0 = \Delta m^2 / (4 E_{\nu})\), where \(\Delta m^2 = m_2^2 - m_1^2\).
* The scattering Hamiltonian parameters include \(\beta^{(+)} = (4 G_F / V) \, m_e / \sqrt{m_e^2 + m_{\mu}^2}\) and \(\gamma^{(+)} = (4 G_F / V) \, m_e / \sqrt{m_e^2 + m_{\mu}^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the MSW effect (Wolfenstein 1978, Mikheyev and Smirnov 1985) by explicitly incorporating quantum decoherence from neutrino-matter collisions, which the effective matter potential approach neglects because it preserves unitary evolution. It provides a microscopic foundation for phenomenological decoherent neutrino oscillation models (Benatti and Floreanini 2000, 2001) and complements kinetic equation approaches (Raffelt 1993, 2018; Vlasenko et al. 2013; Blaschke and Cirigliano 2016) by deriving the dissipative dynamics from first-principles scattering amplitudes. The results enable quantitative predictions for ultra-high energy neutrino propagation through Earth that could be tested in future experiments.
