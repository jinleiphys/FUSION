# High-pT paradigms revisited

> We present an attempt at a critical overview of the current status of modeling for high-pT processes in nuclei. The paper covers several topics including coherence phenomena, in particular gluon shadowing and CGC; nuclear effects related to the restrictions imposed by energy conservation at large xL and xT ; space-time development of hadronization of highly virtual light and heavy partons and the related time scales; and the role of early production and subsequent attenuation of pre-hadrons in a dense medium. We identify several intriguing problems in the current paradigms for high-pT processes and propose solutions for some of them.

## Key claim

The paper argues that many current paradigms for high-pT processes in nuclei are based on unjustified assumptions or misinterpretations of data, and proposes that the suppression observed at forward rapidities in d-Au collisions at RHIC is more likely due to energy conservation constraints near kinematic bounds rather than coherence effects like gluon shadowing or color glass condensate.

## Method

The authors critically review existing theoretical approaches to nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs), gluon shadowing, saturation scales, and jet quenching models. They derive reciprocity equations for mutual boosting of saturation scales in nucleus-nucleus collisions, and develop a model for pre-hadron production length and absorption in dense medium based on dipole cross sections and path-integral techniques.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale for quarks in nuclei: Q_{qA}^2(b,E) = 2 C_q(E, r_T=1/Q_{qA}) T_A(b), where C_q relates to the gluon distribution via C_q(E,r_T) = (pi^2/3) alpha_s(1/r_T^2) xG(x,1/r_T^2)
- Mean size of gluonic dipole from data: r_0 approximately 0.3 fm, dictating the semi-hard scale Q_0^2 approximately 4/r_0^2 = 1.7 GeV^2
- Saturation scale for quarks on nucleon: Q_{qN}(E) = 0.19 GeV x (E/1 GeV)^{0.14}, fitted to DIS data
- Boosted saturation scale for heavy nuclei at LHC may reach about 10 GeV^2
- Onset of quark shadowing occurs at x approximately 0.07 from theory and data; gluon shadowing onset must occur at smaller x than for quarks

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous path-integral approach to gluon shadowing (Kopeliovich et al., 2004) and challenges the interpretations in global nPDF analyses such as EKS98, nDSg (de Florian and Sassot, 2004), EPS08, EPS09, and HKN07. The paper identifies specific problems: EPS09 misinterprets the Cronin effect by attributing it to nPDFs rather than transverse momentum broadening; nDSg uses an ad hoc gluon ratio that contradicts DIS data; and HKN07 produces unphysical x-dependence for gluon shadowing. The proposed mutual boosting of saturation scales in AA collisions enables stronger gluon shadowing than in pA collisions, and the pre-hadron absorption model provides parameter-free predictions for R_AA at high pT that can be tested against RHIC data for light and heavy flavored hadrons.
