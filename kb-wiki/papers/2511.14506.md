# Simulating quantum electrodynamics in 2+1 dimensions with qubits and qumodes

> We develop a hybrid qubit-qumode framework for simulating quantum electrodynamics in 2+1 dimensions. In this approach, fermionic matter fields are represented by qubits, while U(1) gauge fields are encoded in continuous-variable bosonic modes whose canonical quadratures capture the electric and vector-potential components of the theory. To reconcile the non-compact phase space of the qumodes with the compact U(1) gauge symmetry, we introduce and compare two complementary constraint-enforcement strategies: (i) a squeezing-based projection that confines qumode states to the unit circle through an effective modification of the inner product, and (ii) a method that dynamically enforces compactness via a penalty Hamiltonian term. We construct the corresponding hybrid Hamiltonian, derive its decomposition into experimentally accessible qubit-qumode gates, and analyze its spectrum in the analytically tractable single-plaquette limit. The hybrid formulation reproduces the correct gauge-invariant dynamics and provides a scalable route toward simulating Abelian lattice gauge theories coupled to fermionic matter on near-term hybrid quantum architectures. Ground-state preparation and convergence are demonstrated using a continuous-variable extension of the Quantum Imaginary Time Evolution (QITE) algorithm, establishing a general framework for hybrid discrete-continuous quantum simulations of lattice gauge theories.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a hybrid qubit–qumode framework can simulate quantum electrodynamics in 2+1 dimensions without truncating the gauge field Hilbert space, but this is demonstrated only for the analytically tractable single-plaquette limit and the method relies on two distinct constraint-enforcement strategies (squeezing-based projection or penalty Hamiltonian) whose scalability to larger lattices is not fully tested.

## Method
The authors map fermionic matter fields to qubits via a Jordan–Wigner transformation and U(1) gauge fields to continuous-variable bosonic modes (qumodes) whose canonical quadratures represent electric and vector-potential components. They introduce two strategies to enforce compact U(1) gauge symmetry on the non-compact qumode phase space: a squeezing-based projection that modifies the inner product to confine states to the unit circle, and a penalty Hamiltonian term that dynamically enforces compactness. The hybrid Hamiltonian is decomposed into experimentally accessible qubit–qumode gates, and ground-state preparation is demonstrated using a continuous-variable extension of the Quantum Imaginary Time Evolution (QITE) algorithm.

## Key numbers
- The single-plaquette gauge spectrum is given analytically by Mathieu functions: eigenenergies \(E_{n,g} = \frac{g^2}{2}a_n(-1/g^4) + 1/g^2\) for even \(n\) and \(E_{n,g} = \frac{g^2}{2}b_{n+1}(-1/g^4) + 1/g^2\) for odd \(n\), where \(g\) is the gauge coupling.
- The ground-state energy of the single-plaquette system with fermions is \(E_0 = -2m_0 + \frac{g^2}{2} a_0(1/g^4) + 1/g^2 - 1/(2m_0) + \mathcal{O}(m_0^{-2})\) in the large-mass perturbative expansion.
- For a square lattice with \((2N+1)\times(2N+1)\) plaquettes, the normal-mode frequencies of the gauge field are \(\omega^g_{\bm{k}} = 2\sin^2(\pi k_x / [4(N+1)]) + 2\sin^2((k_x+1)\pi/2 + \pi k_y / [4(N+1)])\), with the gap given by \(4\sin^2(\pi / [4(N+1)])\) for \(k_x=k_y=1\).
- The fermionic modes have frequencies \(\omega^f = \pm m_0\), where \(m_0\) is the bare fermion mass.

## Context
This work extends the Hamiltonian formulation of Kogut and Susskind (1974) for lattice gauge theories by introducing a hybrid discrete-continuous encoding that avoids the Hilbert-space truncation required in qubit-only approaches such as those by Chandrasekharan (1996), Zohar (2015), and Martinez (2016). It builds on continuous-variable quantum simulation methods for field theories, including studies of the O(3) model by Jha (2023), scalar field theory by Briceno (2023) and Abel (2024), and SU(2) lattice gauge theory by Ale (2025). The framework enables near-term hybrid quantum architectures to simulate Abelian lattice gauge theories coupled to fermionic matter, with potential extension to non-Abelian theories and applications in quantum chemistry and condensed-matter physics.
