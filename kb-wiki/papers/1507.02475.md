# The BCS pairing gap in the on-shell limit of the Similarity Renormalization Group

> The pairing gap plays a fundamental role in the nuclear many-body problem and many large scale and accurate mass formula fits suggest the smooth nuclear mass dependence $Δ\sim 6(1)~ A^{-1/3}~{\rm MeV}$ in the liquid drop model which lacks a theoretical motivation. Within the BCS theory we analyze the impact of phase equivalent interactions on the pairing gap for a translational invariant many-fermion system such as nuclear and neutron matter. To that end we use explicitly the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) transformations. We show that in the on-shell and continuum limits the pairing gap vanishes. For finite size systems the pairing gap can be computed directly from the scattering phase-shifts by the formula $$ Δ_{nn} (p_F) = Δε_F ~ δ^{^1S_0}_{nn}(p_F) /π~ , $$ where $p_F$ is the Fermi momentum and $Δε_F$ the level spacing at the Fermi energy which for the harmonic oscillator shell model becomes $Δε_F= \hbar ω\sim 41 ~ A^{-1/3}~{\rm MeV}$, so that $$ Δ_{nn} (p_F) \sim 4 ~ A^{-1/3}~{\rm MeV} ~ . $$ The comparison with double differences from binding energies of stable nuclei is satisfactory and the discrepancy with the large scale analysis may be attributed to the lack of three-body forces. Nevertheless, the on-shell two-body interaction provides a basis for the $c~A^{-1/3}$ dependency and accounts for 75\% of the coefficient $c$.

## Key claim

The BCS pairing gap for finite nuclei can be expressed directly in terms of the neutron-neutron scattering phase-shift and the level spacing at the Fermi energy, yielding a natural \(A^{-1/3}\) dependence; however, in the strict continuum limit (infinite system) the pairing gap vanishes, so the finite-size discretization of momenta is essential for obtaining a non-zero result.

## Method

The authors solve the BCS gap equation on a finite momentum grid for the \(^1S_0\) channel, using the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) to generate a family of phase-equivalent interactions parameterized by the SRG cutoff \(\lambda\). They evolve the Hamiltonian toward the on-shell (diagonal) limit \(\lambda \to 0\), where the potential becomes diagonal in momentum space and the BCS equation can be solved analytically in terms of the energy-shift phase-shift. The momentum grid is then matched to a harmonic oscillator basis to encode the finite size of the nucleus, relating the grid spacing at the Fermi momentum to the oscillator frequency \(\hbar\omega\).

## Key numbers

* The empirical pairing gap from large-scale mass formula fits is \(\Delta \sim 6(1)~A^{-1/3}~{\rm MeV}\) (Eq. (1) in the text, summarizing multiple studies).
* For the harmonic oscillator shell model, the level spacing at the Fermi energy is \(\Delta\epsilon_F = \hbar\omega \sim 41~A^{-1/3}~{\rm MeV}\).
* The on-shell formula gives \(\Delta_{nn}(p_F) \sim 4~A^{-1/3}~{\rm MeV}\), which accounts for 75% of the coefficient \(c\) in the empirical \(\Delta \sim c~A^{-1/3}\) dependence.
* The pairing gap in the on-shell limit is given by \(\Delta_F \sim \Delta p_F \frac{p_F \delta(p_F)}{\pi M}\), where \(\Delta p_F\) is the integration weight at the Fermi momentum, scaling as \(1/R\) for a finite system of radius \(R\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of phase-equivalent interactions and the on-shell limit previously applied to few-body systems (Arriola et al., 2013, 2014, 2016) and to unitary neutron matter (Arriola et al., 2014). It contradicts the common assumption that the BCS pairing gap in nuclear matter is determined solely by the phase-shifts and remains finite in the continuum limit; instead, the gap vanishes for an infinite system and only survives due to finite-size quantization. The result provides a microscopic justification for the \(A^{-1/3}\) dependence found in large-scale mass formula fits (Vogel et al., 1984; Madland and Moller, 1988; Myers and Swiatecki, 1995; Satula and Wyss, 1998; and many others) and suggests that three-body forces are needed to account for the remaining 25% of the empirical coefficient.
