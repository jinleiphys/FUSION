# Time-dependent wave-packet approach for fusion reactions of halo nuclei

> The fusion reaction of a halo nucleus 11Be on 208Pb is described by a three-body direct reaction model. A time-dependent wave packet approach is applied to a three-body reaction problem. The wave packet approach enables us to obtain scattering solutions without considering the three-body scattering boundary conditions. The time evolution of the wave packet also helps us to obtain intuitive understanding of the reaction dynamics. The calculations indicate a decrease of the fusion probability by the presence of the halo neutron.

# Time-dependent wave-packet approach for fusion reactions of halo nuclei

## Key claim

The fusion probability of a halo nucleus projectile is suppressed by the presence of the halo neutron, contrary to many other theoretical works that claimed enhancement; however, this conclusion depends critically on including sufficiently high partial waves (up to \(l_{max}=70\)) in the three-body calculation, and calculations truncated at low angular momentum (e.g., \(l_{max}=2\)) produce the opposite, false enhancement.

## Method

A three-body direct reaction model was solved using a time-dependent wave-packet approach for the reaction \(^{11}\text{Be} + ^{208}\text{Pb}\) at zero total angular momentum (head-on collision). The time-dependent Schrödinger equation was discretized on a radial grid (\(0<R<50\) fm, \(0<r<60\) fm) with partial-wave expansion up to \(l_{max}=70\), and the fusion probability was extracted by energy projection from the initial and final wave-packet states via Fourier transform of the time-evolved wave function.

## Key numbers

- The average total energy of the initial wave packet was set to \(E_0 \approx 38\) MeV, close to the barrier top energy.
- The n-C potential was adjusted to give the \(2s\) orbital energy at \(-0.6\) MeV, close to the neutron separation energy of \(^{11}\text{Be}\).
- The radial grid used \(\Delta R = 0.5\) fm and \(\Delta r = 1\) fm, with \(R_0 = 25\) fm and \(\gamma = 0.05\) fm\(^{-2}\) for the initial Gaussian packet.
- The partial-wave expansion required \(l_{max}=70\) (about 420,000 grid points for the wave function \(u_l(R,r,t)\)).
- Three n-T potential strengths were tested: \(V_{nT}=0\) MeV, \(V_{nT}=-48\) MeV, and \(V_{nT}=-42\) MeV; in all cases the three-body fusion probability was smaller than the two-body \(^{10}\text{Be}+^{208}\text{Pb}\) calculation.
- Calculations with \(l_{max}=2\) gave strong enhancement of fusion at low incident energy, while \(l_{max}=70\) showed suppression across the entire energy region.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of \(^{11}\text{Be}\) on a medium-mass target \(^{40}\text{Ca}\) (Yabana, 1997), which also found fusion suppression, and contradicts the conclusions of Hagino (2000) and Diaz-Torres (2002), who reported strong enhancement for the same system using three-body calculations with low partial-wave truncation. The paper argues that those earlier results are artifacts of insufficient partial-wave convergence, and that the suppression arises primarily from the Coulomb interaction between core and target, with the n-T nuclear potential playing a minor role. The approach enables future calculations at finite total angular momenta using a body-fixed frame, which is under development.
