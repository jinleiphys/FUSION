# FeynOnium: Using FeynCalc for automatic calculations in Nonrelativistic Effective Field Theories

> We present new results on FeynOnium, an ongoing project to develop a general purpose software toolkit for semi-automatic symbolic calculations in nonrelativistic Effective Field Theories (EFTs). Building upon FeynCalc, an existing Mathematica package for symbolic evaluation of Feynman diagrams, we have created a powerful framework for automatizing calculations in nonrelativistic EFTs (NREFTs) at tree- and 1-loop level. This is achieved by exploiting the novel features of FeynCalc that support manipulations of Cartesian tensors, Pauli matrices and nonstandard loop integrals. Additional operations that are common in nonrelativistic EFT calculations are implemented in a dedicated add-on called FeynOnium. While our current focus is on EFTs for strong interactions of heavy quarks, extensions to other systems that admit a nonrelativistic EFT description are planned for the future. All our codes are open-source and publicly available. Furthermore, we provide several example calculations that demonstrate how FeynOnium can be employed to reproduce known results from the literature.

## Key claim
FeynOnium extends FeynCalc to enable semi-automatic symbolic calculations in nonrelativistic Effective Field Theories (NREFTs) at tree- and 1-loop level, but the authors hedge that the current focus is on EFTs for strong interactions of heavy quarks (NRQCD and pNRQCD), with extensions to other nonrelativistic systems only planned for the future.

## Method
The authors modified FeynCalc 9.3 to support Cartesian tensors, Pauli matrices, temporal components of 4-vectors, and nonstandard loop integrals, adding new internal symbols such as CartesianPair, CartesianMomentum, CartesianIndex, TemporalPair, TemporalMomentum, and PauliSigma. They then built a dedicated add-on called FeynOnium that implements additional operations common in nonrelativistic EFT calculations, such as breaking manifest Lorentz covariance via the LorentzToCartesian function. The toolkit is demonstrated by reproducing known results from the NRQCD and pNRQCD literature, including matching calculations between QCD and NRQCD and between NRQCD and pNRQCD.

## Context
This work extends the FeynCalc package (Mertig 1991, Shtabovenko 2016) to handle nonrelativistic calculations, addressing a gap where most public EFT tools (such as SMEFTsim, MatchingTools, CoDEx, and others) assume manifest Lorentz covariance and cannot handle NREFTs. The authors contrast their approach with private in-house codes commonly used in the NRQCD community (e.g., FDC by Wang 2004) and with tools like MadOnia (Artoisenet 2007) and HELAC-ONIA (Shao 2012) that generate tree-level amplitudes but do not provide the full symbolic framework. The work enables systematic higher-order calculations in NRQCD and pNRQCD that can be shared and reproduced by the community.
