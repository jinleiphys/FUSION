# Equation of state in hybrid stars and the stability window of quark matter

> Properties of hybrid stars with a mixed phase composed of asymmetric nuclear matter and strange quark matter are studied. The quark phase is investigated by the quark quasiparticle model with a self-consistent thermodynamic and statistical treatment. We present the stability windows of the strange quark matter with respect to the interaction coupling constant versus the bag constant. We find that the appearance of the quark-hadron mixed phases is associated with the meta-stable or unstable regions of the pure quark matter parameters. The mass-radius relation of the hybrid star is dominated by the equation of state of quark matter rather than nuclear matter. Due to the appearance of mixed phase, the mass of hybrid star is reduced to 1.64 M$_{\odot}$ with radius 10.6 km by comparison with neutron star.

## Key claim

The mass-radius relation of hybrid stars is dominated by the equation of state of quark matter rather than nuclear matter, but this conclusion is conditional on the quark matter parameters being chosen in the meta-stable or unstable regions of the pure quark matter parameter space; if absolutely stable parameter sets are chosen, no mixed phase exists and the hybrid star collapses to a strange star.

## Method

The nuclear matter equation of state was computed using relativistic mean-field theory with four parameter sets (TM1, NL3, BKA20, TW-99) and the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland model for the crust. The quark phase was described by the quark quasiparticle model, where effective quark masses follow from the hard-dense-loop approximation and the bag function is obtained self-consistently from thermodynamic consistency. The hadron-quark mixed phase was constructed using Gibbs conditions for phase equilibrium with global charge neutrality, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain hybrid star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of hybrid stars is reduced to 1.64 solar masses with radius 10.6 km, compared to pure neutron stars.
- For pure neutron stars with the TM1 parameter set, the maximum mass is 2.06 solar masses; with the TW-99 set, the maximum mass reaches 2.86 solar masses.
- The mixed phase exists in the density range of about 1 to 3 times nuclear saturation density.
- The electron chemical potential decreases from a maximum of 85 MeV to 20 MeV across the mixed phase.
- For different quark matter parameter choices, the maximum mass of hybrid stars ranges from 1.54 solar masses (radius about 9.64 km) up to 2.04 solar masses (radius in the range 9.64 to 12.4 km).
- The pulsar PSR J1614-2230 with mass 1.97 plus or minus 0.04 solar masses falls within the computed hybrid star parameter range.

## Context

This work extends the quark quasiparticle model previously developed by the author for strangelets (Wen 2009) and magnetized strange quark matter (Wen 2012) to the study of hybrid stars with mixed phases. It contrasts with the conventional bag model approach used by Burgio (2002) and addresses the "masquerade effect" noted by Alford (2005), where hybrid stars can have mass-radius relations similar to pure neutron stars. The results enable future investigations of Color-Flavor-Locked matter, especially magnetized CFL matter as studied by Ferrer (2011), at even higher densities.
