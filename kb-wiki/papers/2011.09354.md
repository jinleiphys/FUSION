# Heat release in accreting neutron stars

> Observed thermal emission from accreting neutron stars (NSs) in a quiescent state is believed to be powered by nonequilibrium nuclear reactions that heat the stellar crust (deep crustal heating paradigm). We derive a simple universal formula for the heating efficiency, assuming that an NS has a fully accreted crust. We further show that, within the recently proposed thermodynamically consistent approach to the accreted crust, the heat release can be parametrized by the only one parameter -- the pressure $P_{\rm oi}$ at the outer-inner crust interface (as we argue, this pressure should not necessarily coincide with the neutron-drip pressure). We discuss possible values of $P_{\rm oi}$ for a selection of nuclear models that account for shell effects, and determine the net heat release and its distribution in the crust as a function of $P_{\rm oi}$. We conclude that the heat release should be reduced by a factor of few in comparison to previous works.

## Key claim

The heat release in a fully accreted neutron star crust is reduced by a factor of a few compared to previous works, and can be parametrized by a single parameter, the pressure at the outer-inner crust interface, which should not necessarily coincide with the neutron-drip pressure.

## Method

The authors derive a universal formula for the deep crustal heating efficiency in a fully accreted crust, assuming the inner crust respects the neutron hydrostatic/diffusion equilibrium condition (constant redshifted neutron chemical potential). They calculate the heat release and its distribution (outer crust, inner crust, and at the outer-inner interface) as a function of the pressure at the outer-inner crust interface, using several nuclear mass models (BSk24, BSk25, BSk26 from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations and FRDM12 from the finite-range droplet model) and a compressible liquid drop model with shell corrections.

## Key numbers

* The heat release Q (defined as Q = Q^∞ e^{-ν_oi/2}) ranges from approximately 0.2 to 1 MeV/nucleon for the pressure region depicted in Figure 1.
* For the CLD+sh model with BSk24 functional and A=56 ashes, the minimal pressure at the outer-inner interface is P_oi^(min) ≈ 0.91 P_nd^(cat), where P_nd^(cat) is the neutron drip pressure in the catalyzed crust.
* For the same model, the instability pressure at P_oi^(min) is P_inst(P_oi^(min)) ≈ 0.267 MeV fm^{-3}, and the inner crust heat release Q_i(P_oi^(min)) ≈ 0.06 MeV/nucleon.
* The lower bound P_oi^(0) (where Q_i = 0) is approximately 0.89 P_nd^(cat) for the CLD+sh model.
* For the FRDM12 model, the total heat release Q ranges from 0.21 MeV/nucleon (at P_oi = P_oi^(0) = 0.85 P_nd^(cat)) to 0.53 MeV/nucleon (at P_oi = P_nd^(cat)).
* For the BSk24 model, Q ranges from 0.29 MeV/nucleon (at P_oi^(0) = 0.92 P_nd^(cat)) to 0.46 MeV/nucleon (at P_oi = P_nd^(cat)).
* The deep crustal heating energy release Q^∞ is estimated as (0.21–0.53) e^{ν_oi/2} MeV/nucleon, several times smaller than the traditional approach value of (1.5–2) e^{ν_oi/2} MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamically consistent approach to the accreted crust proposed by Gusakov and Chugunov (2020), which accounts for neutron hydrostatic/diffusion equilibrium in the inner crust, an effect ignored in traditional models dating back to Sato (1979) and Haensel and Zdunik (1990). The results contradict earlier calculations by Haensel and Zdunik (2003, 2008), Fantina et al. (2018), and Lau et al. (2018), which found heat releases of 1.5–2 MeV/nucleon. The new lower heating efficiency calls for reinterpretation of observational data on thermal emission from transiently accreting neutron stars and enables future modeling of thermal relaxation and quiescent temperatures within the nHD approach, which may help constrain the pressure at the outer-inner crust interface.
