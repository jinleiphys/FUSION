# Multi-cluster dynamics in $^{13}_Λ{\rm C}$ and analogy to clustering in $^{12}{\rm C}$

> We investigate structure of $^{13}_Λ{\rm C}$ and discuss the difference and similarity between the structures of $^{12}{\rm C}$ and $^{13}_Λ{\rm C}$ by answering the questions if the linear-chain and gaslike cluster states, which are proposed to appear in $^{12}{\rm C}$, survives, or new structure states appear or not. We introduce a microscopic cluster model called, Hyper-Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (H-THSR) wave function, which is an extended version of the THSR wave function so as to describe $Λ$ hypernuclei. We obtained two bound states and two resonance (quasi-bound) states for $J^π=0^+$ in $^{13}_Λ{\rm C}$, corresponding to the four $0^+$ states in $^{12}{\rm C}$. However, the inversion of level ordering between the spectra of $^{12}{\rm C}$ and $^{13}_Λ{\rm C}$, i.e. that the $0_3^+$ and $0_4^+$ states in $^{13}_Λ{\rm C}$ correspond to the $0_4^+$ and $0_3^+$ states in $^{12}{\rm C}$, respectively, is shown to occur. The additional $Λ$ particle reduces sizes of the $0_2^+$ and $0_3^+$ states in $^{13}_Λ{\rm C}$ very much, but the shrinkage of the $0_4^+$ state is only a half of the other states. In conclusion, the Hoyle state becomes quite a compact object with ${^{9}_Λ{\rm Be}}+α$ configuration in $^{13}_Λ{\rm C}$ and is no more gaslike state composed of the $3α$ clusters. Instead, the $0_4^+$ state in $^{13}_Λ{\rm C}$, coming from the $^{12}{\rm C}(0_3^+)$ state, appears as a gaslike state composed of $α+α+^{5}_Λ{\rm He}$ configuration, i.e. the Hoyle analog state. A linear-chain state in a $Λ$ hypernucleus is for the first time predicted to exist as the $0_3^+$ state in $^{13}_Λ{\rm C}$ with more shrunk arrangement of the $3α$ clusters along $z$-axis than the $3α$ linear-chain configuration realized in the $^{12}{\rm C}(0_4^+)$ state.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in $^{13}_{\Lambda}{\rm C}$, the Hoyle state analog (the $0_2^+$ state) becomes a compact object with a $^{9}_{\Lambda}{\rm Be}+\alpha$ configuration and is no longer a gaslike $3\alpha$ cluster state, while a linear-chain state in a $\Lambda$ hypernucleus is predicted for the first time as the $0_3^+$ state, but this assignment depends on an inversion of level ordering between the $0_3^+$ and $0_4^+$ states of $^{12}{\rm C}$ and $^{13}_{\Lambda}{\rm C}$.

## Method

The authors used a microscopic cluster model called the Hyper-Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (H-THSR) wave function, which extends the THSR wave function for $^{12}{\rm C}$ by adding a $\Lambda$ particle wave function with axially symmetric deformation parameters. They solved the Hill-Wheeler equation for $J^\pi=0^+$ states using a linear combination of H-THSR basis states with different size and deformation parameters, employing the Volkov No. 2 nucleon-nucleon force and the YNG (ESC04a) $\Lambda N$ interaction adjusted to reproduce the $\Lambda$ binding energies of $^{5}_{\Lambda}{\rm He}$ and $^{9}_{\Lambda}{\rm Be}$.

## Key numbers

- The $0_2^+$ state in $^{13}_{\Lambda}{\rm C}$ is a bound state located 0.8 MeV below the $\Lambda+^{12}{\rm C}(0_1^+)$ threshold.
- The root-mean-square radius of the $0_2^+$ state in $^{13}_{\Lambda}{\rm C}$ is 2.9 fm, compared to 3.7 fm for the Hoyle state ($0_2^+$) in $^{12}{\rm C}$.
- The $0_4^+$ state in $^{13}_{\Lambda}{\rm C}$ has a root-mean-square radius of 4.3 fm, larger than the Hoyle state radius of 3.7 fm in $^{12}{\rm C}$.
- The $\Lambda$ binding energy ${\cal E}_\Lambda$ for the $0_1^+$ state is 15.0 MeV (experimental value 11.69 MeV), for the $0_2^+$ state is 8.5 MeV, for the $0_3^+$ state is 8.3 MeV, and for the $0_4^+$ state is 4.1 MeV.
- The partial decay widths of the $0_3^+$ state are at most 0.6 MeV ($\Lambda+^{12}{\rm C}(0_1^+)$ channel) and 0.4 MeV ($\alpha+^{9}_{\Lambda}{\rm Be}(0_1^+)$ channel).
- The partial decay widths of the $0_4^+$ state are at most 1.1 MeV ($\alpha+^{9}_{\Lambda}{\rm Be}(0_1^+)$), 1.0 MeV ($\alpha+^{9}_{\Lambda}{\rm Be}(2_1^+)$), and 0.4 MeV ($^{8}{\rm Be}(0^+)+^{5}_{\Lambda}{\rm He}$).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of cluster structures in $^{12}{\rm C}$ using the THSR wave function (Funaki et al., 2015, 2016), which successfully described the Hoyle state as a $3\alpha$ gaslike state and the $0_4^+$ state as having a linear-chain configuration. The paper also builds on earlier cluster model studies of $^{13}_{\Lambda}{\rm C}$ using the Resonating Group Method (Yamada et al., 1985) and Orthogonality Condition Model (Hiyama et al., 1997), which focused on the Hoyle analog state but did not predict a linear-chain state. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted linear-chain state in $^{13}_{\Lambda}{\rm C}$ and further theoretical studies of multi-cluster dynamics in $\Lambda$ hypernuclei.
