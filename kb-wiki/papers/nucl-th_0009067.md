# Chiral dynamics in few-nucleon systems

> We employ the chiral nucleon-nucleon potential derived using the method of unitary transformation up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) to study bound and scattering states in the two-nucleon system. The predicted partial wave phase shifts and mixing parameters for higher energies and higher angular momenta beyond the ones which are fitted are mostly well described for energies below 300 MeV. Various deuteron properties are discussed. We also apply the next-to-leading order (NLO) potential to 3N and 4N systems. The resulting 3N and 4N binding energies are in the same range what is found using standard NN potentials. Experimental low-energy 3N scattering observables are also very well reproduced like for standard NN forces. Surprisingly the long standing Ay-puzzle is resolved at NLO. The cut-off dependence of the scattering observables is rather mild.

## Key claim

The chiral nucleon-nucleon potential derived via unitary transformation up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) provides a quantitative description of two-nucleon bound and scattering states, and the next-to-leading order (NLO) potential applied to three- and four-nucleon systems yields binding energies and low-energy scattering observables comparable to standard realistic NN potentials, while surprisingly resolving the long-standing \(A_y\)-puzzle at NLO.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral NN potential using the method of unitary transformation, which yields an energy-independent potential. At leading order (LO) the potential consists of one-pion exchange (OPE) and two S-wave contact interactions; at NLO it includes leading chiral two-pion exchange (TPE) and seven contact terms with two derivatives; at NNLO it adds subleading TPE with low-energy constants (LECs) taken from chiral \(\pi N\) scattering studies. The nine contact-term parameters were fitted separately to the Nijmegen phase shift analysis for S- and P-waves and the mixing parameter \(\epsilon_1\) for laboratory energies below 100 MeV (NLO) or 50 MeV (NNLO). The potential was then applied to the two-nucleon bound state and scattering problems, and the NLO potential was used to solve the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for the three- and four-nucleon systems.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy at NNLO: \(E_d = -2.2238\) MeV (experimental value \(-2.224575(9)\) MeV), reproduced within about one third of a permille.
- Deuteron binding energy at NLO: \(E_d = -2.1650\) MeV (2.5 percent accuracy).
- Asymptotic \(D/S\) ratio at NNLO: \(\eta = 0.0245\) (experimental \(\eta = 0.0256(4)\)).
- Asymptotic wave function strength at NNLO: \(A_S = 0.884\) fm\(^{-1/2}\) (experimental \(A_S = 0.8846(16)\) fm\(^{-1/2}\)).
- Root-mean-square matter radius at NNLO: \(r_d = 1.967\) fm (experimental \(r_d = 1.9671(6)\) fm).
- Quadrupole moment at NNLO: \(Q_d = 0.262\) fm\(^2\) (experimental \(Q_d = 0.2859(3)\) fm\(^2\)).
- \(^3\)H binding energy at NLO with cut-off \(\Lambda = 540\) MeV: \(-8.284\) MeV (experimental \(-8.48\) MeV).
- \(^4\)He binding energy at NLO with cut-off \(\Lambda = 540\) MeV: \(-28.03\) MeV (Coulomb-corrected experimental \(-29.00\) MeV).
- \(^3\)H binding energy at NLO with cut-off \(\Lambda = 600\) MeV: \(-7.564\) MeV.
- \(^4\)He binding energy at NLO with cut-off \(\Lambda = 600\) MeV: \(-23.96\) MeV.
- \(^3S_1\) scattering length at NNLO: \(a = 5.424\) fm (Nijmegen PSA: \(5.420\) fm).
- \(^3S_1\) effective range at NNLO: \(r = 1.741\) fm (Nijmegen PSA: \(1.753\) fm).

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to nuclear forces, originally proposed by Weinberg (1990), from the two-nucleon system to three- and four-nucleon systems within the Hamiltonian framework for the first time. It contrasts with the earlier energy-dependent potential of the Texas-Seattle group (which required 26 free parameters) by using an energy-independent potential with only nine contact-term parameters. The NLO predictions for 3N and 4N binding energies are in the same range as those from standard realistic potentials such as CD-Bonn, and the resolution of the \(A_y\)-puzzle (where standard forces underpredict the analyzing power maximum by about 30%) opens the door to including the leading three-nucleon force at NNLO for further improvement.
