# Configuration interaction in symmetry-conserving covariant density functional theory

> A new method to calculate spectroscopic properties of deformed nuclei is proposed: configuration interaction on top of projected density functional theory (CI-PDFT). The general concept of this approach is discussed in the framework of covariant density functional theory and its validity is illustrated in an application to the yrast band of the nucleus Cr-54. It is found that the experimentally observed excitation energies for the yrast band in Cr-54 can be well reproduced. In contrast to conventional shell-model calculations, there is no core and only a relatively small number of configurations is sufficient for a satisfying description. No new parameters are necessary, because the effective interaction is derived from an universal density functional given in the literature.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new method, configuration interaction on top of projected density functional theory (CI-PDFT), that combines covariant density functional theory with shell-model configuration mixing to describe spectroscopic properties of deformed nuclei. The method is illustrated for the yrast band of Cr-54, where the experimentally observed excitation energies can be well reproduced, but the calculated B(E2;2+→0) transition probability is roughly a factor two smaller than the experimental value and the calculated band is too extended for high-spin states (I=8, 10 ℏ).

## Method

Starting from a self-consistent covariant density functional theory (CDFT) solution using the relativistic point-coupling Lagrangian PC-PK1, the authors construct a configuration space consisting of the quasiparticle vacuum and two-quasiparticle excitations (both neutron and proton) with an energy cutoff of 6.5 MeV. Each configuration is projected onto good angular momentum using the projection operator, and the Hamiltonian is diagonalized in the nonorthogonal projected basis using the residual interaction derived as the second derivative of the energy density functional with respect to the density matrix.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of Cr-54 has a prolate quadrupole deformation β₂ = 0.24, in agreement with the experimental value β₂ = 0.247 ± 0.067 from Pritychenko (2016).
- The configuration space consists of 37 states: the quasiparticle vacuum, 18 two-quasineutron states, and 18 two-quasiproton states, truncated at a two-quasiparticle excitation energy cutoff E_cut = 6.5 MeV.
- The calculated B(E2;2+→0) transition probability is roughly a factor of two smaller than the experimental value from Devlin (1999).
- The calculated yrast band energies for I=2, 4, 6 ℏ and nonyrast I=2, 4 states are in good agreement with data, but the calculated band for I=8, 10 ℏ is too extended (moments of inertia too small).

## Context

This work extends the generator coordinate method (GCM) and cranking model approaches in density functional theory by providing a method that can mix collective and multiparticle excitations with proper angular momentum projection. It is technically reminiscent of the phenomenological projected shell model (PSM) of Hara and Sun (1995), but uses a self-consistent density functional (PC-PK1) rather than a Nilsson potential with adjustable parameters, and the effective interaction is derived from the functional rather than being phenomenological. The method enables future global studies of nuclear spectroscopic properties across the nuclide chart without additional parameters beyond those of the underlying density functional, though the present calculations neglect time-odd components and four-quasiparticle configurations, which may be needed for high-spin states.
