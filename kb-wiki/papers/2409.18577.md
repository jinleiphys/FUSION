# Light $Λ$ hypernuclei studied with chiral hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon-nucleon forces

> A study of light $Λ$ hypernuclei in chiral effective field theory is presented. For the first time chiral $Λ$NN and $Σ$NN three-body forces are included consistently. The calculations are performed within the no-core shell model. Results for the separation energies of the hypernuclei $^3_Λ\mathrm{H}$, $^4_Λ\mathrm{H}$/$^4_Λ\mathrm{He}$, $^5_Λ\mathrm{He}$, and $^7_Λ\mathrm{Li}$ are given. It is found that the experimental values can be fairly well reproduced once YNN three-body forces are taken into account.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that light Lambda hypernuclei (A=3-7) can be described fairly well by chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) only when chiral YNN three-body forces are included consistently, but the description requires relaxing the strict decuplet-saturation constraints on the three-body force low-energy constants to achieve agreement with experiment for the A=4 and A=5 systems simultaneously.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for the hypernuclei 3_Lambda_H, 4_Lambda_H/4_Lambda_He, 5_Lambda_He, and 7_Lambda_Li using the SMS NN potential at N4LO+ with a 550 MeV cutoff and a corresponding N2LO 3NF, combined with the chiral N2LO YN potential (SMS N2LO(550b)). For the first time, the full leading chiral Lambda NN and Sigma NN three-body forces at N2LO were included consistently, with their low-energy constants determined by fitting to the separation energies of 4_Lambda_H/4_Lambda_He and 5_Lambda_He, guided by resonance saturation via decuplet baryons.

## Key numbers

- 3_Lambda_H separation energy: 0.155(3) MeV with full YNN force (including C'_2), compared to experimental 0.164(43) MeV
- 4_Lambda_He (0+,1/2) separation energy: 2.220(2) MeV with full YNN force, compared to experimental average 2.258(55) MeV
- 4_Lambda_He (1+,1/2) separation energy: 0.984(12) MeV with full YNN force, compared to experimental average 1.011(72) MeV
- 5_Lambda_He separation energy: 3.196(20) MeV with full YNN force, compared to experimental 3.102(30) MeV
- 7_Lambda_Li (1/2+,0) separation energy: 5.623(52) MeV with full YNN force, compared to experimental 5.619(60) MeV
- 7_Lambda_Li (3/2+,0) separation energy: 5.040(57) MeV with full YNN force, compared to experimental 4.927(60) MeV
- 7_Lambda_Li (5/2+,0) separation energy: 3.356(60) MeV with full YNN force, compared to experimental 3.568(60) MeV
- The LEC C'_2 was determined to be 1310 GeV^{-5} when relaxing decuplet-saturation constraints
- All potentials were SRG-evolved to a flow parameter of lambda = 1.88 fm^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT approach to hypernuclei beyond previous studies that used only two-body YN forces, such as the earlier NCSM calculations by Le (2022) and the pionless EFT study by Contessi (2018) which was restricted to leading order. It also goes beyond exploratory calculations by Kamada (2023) and Kohno (2023) that were limited to the hypertriton and used dimensional arguments rather than actual separation energies to fix three-body force strengths. The results enable future studies of heavier hypernuclei and provide constraints for the hyperon puzzle in neutron stars, as the observed trend of repulsive three-body force effects with increasing A may support one possible solution to that puzzle.
