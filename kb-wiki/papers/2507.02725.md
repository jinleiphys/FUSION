# Constraining color-charge effects of partonic energy loss with jet axis-based inclusive jet substructure measurement

> This study investigates the color-charge dependence of parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium and the associated relative modifications of quark and gluon jet fractions compared to vacuum, using jet axis decorrelation observables. Recent CMS jet axis decorrelation measurements in PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV are interpreted using Pythia simulations with varied quark/gluon jet compositions and emulated color-charge dependent energy loss. A template-fit procedure is employed to estimate the limits on gluon jet fractions in the published CMS data and average shift in jet momentum due to quenching for quark- and gluon-initiated jets traversing the QGP. The extracted gluon jet fractions and the estimated quark and gluon energy losses based on this study of jet axis decorrelations are found to be consistent with other model calculations based on inclusive observables. This work illustrates the use of jet substructure measurements for providing constraints on the color-charge dependence of parton energy loss and offers valuable insights for jet quenching models.

# Constraining color-charge effects of partonic energy loss with jet axis-based inclusive jet substructure measurement

## Key claim
The narrowing of jet axis decorrelation distributions observed in CMS PbPb data at 5.02 TeV can be partially explained by a reduced fraction of gluon-initiated jets in the surviving jet population due to their larger color-charge-dependent energy loss, but the high-\DRaxis tail in central collisions requires additional medium-induced substructure modifications beyond simple changes in quark/gluon composition or momentum shifts.

## Method
The authors performed template fits to CMS jet axis decorrelation (\DRaxis) distributions using Pythia8 simulations with CP5 tune, separating quark- and gluon-initiated jet templates. They applied a chi-square minimization procedure to estimate both the lower limits on gluon jet fractions and the average transverse momentum shifts for inclusive, quark, and gluon jets by systematically increasing jet \pt in Pythia to match the CMS PbPb data across four \pt intervals (120–300 GeV) and four centrality bins (0–10%, 10–30%, 30–50%, 50–80%).

## Key numbers
- Gluon jet fraction in Pythia8 vacuum simulations is approximately 53% at \pt = 120 GeV, decreasing to about 45% at \pt = 300 GeV
- For 0–10% central collisions, the gluon-like jet fraction is reduced by approximately 50% relative to the 50–80% peripheral centrality interval
- Inclusive jet \pt shift in 0–10% central collisions: from nominal mean \pt of 131.98 GeV to shifted mean of 154.26 GeV for the 120–150 GeV bin; from 255.50 GeV to 301.41 GeV for the 230–300 GeV bin
- Quark jet shifted mean \pt in 0–10% central collisions: 135.13 GeV (from nominal 132.11 GeV) for the 120–150 GeV bin; 255.81 GeV (unchanged from nominal) for the 230–300 GeV bin
- Gluon jet shifted mean \pt in 0–10% central collisions: 150.12 GeV (from nominal 131.87 GeV) for the 120–150 GeV bin; 303.05 GeV (from nominal 255.05 GeV) for the 230–300 GeV bin
- Jet \Raa estimates constructed from the \pt shifts are consistent with previously measured CMS and ATLAS jet \Raa values

## Context
This work extends the CMS jet axis decorrelation measurement (CMS 2024) by providing a phenomenological interpretation using Pythia8 simulations with varied quark/gluon fractions and emulated energy loss. It builds on prior CMS template-fit studies using jet-charge observables (CMS 2020) which found no significant evidence of gluon fraction modification, and on ATLAS photon-tagged jet measurements (ATLAS 2023) that provided evidence for color-charge dependent energy loss. The extracted gluon fractions and energy loss estimates are consistent with the medium q/g model (Ringer, Sato) and with Bayesian analyses of multiple measurements (ref. eg:partonloss). The results enable improved constraints for jet quenching models by demonstrating that jet axis decorrelation measurements can probe color-charge dependence beyond what inclusive observables provide, while also revealing that the high-\DRaxis tail requires modeling of medium-induced substructure modifications.
