# The neutron skin thickness from the measured electric dipole polarizability in $^{68}$Ni, $^{120}$Sn, and $^{208}$Pb

> The information on the symmetry energy and its density dependence is deduced by comparing the available data on the electric dipole polarizability $α_D$ of ${}^{68}$Ni, ${}^{120}$Sn, and ${}^{208}$Pb with the predictions of the Random Phase Approximation, using a representative set of nuclear energy density functionals. The calculated values of $α_D$ are used to validate different correlations involving $α_D$, the symmetry energy at the saturation density $J$, the corresponding slope parameter $L$ and the neutron skin thickness $Δr_{\!np}$, as suggested by the Droplet Model. A subset of models that reproduce simultaneously the measured polarizabilities in ${}^{68}$Ni, ${}^{120}$Sn, and ${}^{208}$Pb are employed to predict the values of the symmetry energy parameters at saturation density and $Δr_{\!np}$. The resulting intervals are: $J\!=\!30 \text{-}35$ MeV, $L\!=\!20 \text{-} 66$ MeV; and the values for $Δr_{\!np}$ in ${}^{68}$Ni, ${}^{120}$Sn, and ${}^{208}$Pb are in the ranges: 0.15\text{-}0.19 fm, 0.12\text{-}0.16 fm, and 0.13\text{-}0.19 fm, respectively. The strong correlation between the electric dipole polarizabilities of two nuclei is instrumental to predict the values of electric dipole polarizabilities in other nuclei.

## Key claim

The neutron skin thickness and symmetry energy parameters can be constrained by simultaneously comparing theoretical Random Phase Approximation predictions with measured electric dipole polarizabilities in three nuclei (68Ni, 120Sn, and 208Pb), but only after subtracting quasi-deuteron contributions from the experimental polarizability values for 120Sn and 208Pb.

## Method

Self-consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA) and Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations of the electric dipole polarizability were performed for 68Ni, 120Sn, and 208Pb using a large representative set of nuclear energy density functionals, including non-relativistic Skyrme interactions and relativistic models. The calculated polarizabilities were used to validate correlations between the product of polarizability and symmetry energy at saturation (alpha_D * J), the slope parameter L, and the neutron skin thickness as suggested by the Droplet Model. A subset of models that simultaneously reproduce the measured polarizabilities in all three nuclei was then selected to predict the symmetry energy parameters and neutron skin thicknesses.

## Key numbers

- Electric dipole polarizability of 68Ni (including low-energy and high-energy extrapolations): 3.88 +/- 0.31 fm^3
- Electric dipole polarizability of 120Sn (after subtracting 0.34 +/- 0.08 fm^3 quasi-deuteron contribution): 8.59 +/- 0.37 fm^3
- Electric dipole polarizability of 208Pb (after subtracting 0.51 +/- 0.15 fm^3 quasi-deuteron contribution): 19.6 +/- 0.6 fm^3
- Symmetry energy at saturation density J: 30-35 MeV
- Slope parameter L: 20-66 MeV
- Neutron skin thickness in 68Ni: 0.15-0.19 fm
- Neutron skin thickness in 120Sn: 0.12-0.16 fm
- Neutron skin thickness in 208Pb: 0.13-0.19 fm
- Correlation coefficient between alpha_D * J and neutron skin thickness in 68Ni: 0.94
- Correlation coefficient between alpha_D * J and neutron skin thickness in 120Sn: 0.95
- Correlation coefficient between alpha_D(208Pb) * J and alpha_D(68Ni) * J: 0.99
- Correlation coefficient between alpha_D(208Pb) * J and alpha_D(120Sn) * J: 0.99
- Correlation coefficient between alpha_D(120Sn) * J and alpha_D(68Ni) * J: 0.98

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Tamii et al. (2011) and Piekarewicz et al. (2012) that used only 208Pb data to constrain the neutron skin thickness, and by Roca-Maza et al. (2013) who established the strong correlation between alpha_D * J and neutron skin thickness in 208Pb. By incorporating new measurements of the electric dipole polarizability in 68Ni (Rossi et al., 2013) and 120Sn (Hashimoto et al., 2015), the authors demonstrate that simultaneous analysis of multiple nuclei provides tighter constraints on the symmetry energy parameters and enables predictions for the electric dipole polarizability of 48Ca and 90Zr, which are planned for future experimental investigation at RCNP.
