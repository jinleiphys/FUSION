# Effect of thermalized charm on heavy quark energy loss

> The recent experimental results on the flow of $J/ψ$ at LHC show that ample amount of charm quarks is present in the quark gluon plasma and probably they are thermalized. In the current study we investigate the effect of thermalized charm quarks on the heavy quark energy loss to leading order in the QCD coupling constant. It is seen that the energy loss of charm quark increases due to the inclusion of thermal charm quarks. Running coupling has also been implemented to study heavy quark energy loss and we find a modest increase in the heavy quark energy loss due to heavy-heavy scattering at higher temperature to be realized at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The inclusion of thermalized charm quarks as scattering centers in the quark-gluon plasma increases the collisional energy loss of an energetic charm quark, with the effect growing at higher temperatures relevant to LHC energies; the increase is modest, amounting to approximately 5% at 400 MeV and 8% at 600 MeV for a charm quark momentum of 25 GeV.

## Method

The authors computed the leading-order collisional energy loss of a heavy charm quark propagating through a quark-gluon plasma that contains thermalized charm quarks in addition to light quarks and gluons. They used the Braaten-Yuan prescription to separate soft and hard momentum-transfer regimes, employing hard-thermal-loop (HTL) resummed propagators for the soft sector and tree-level propagators for the hard sector, and summed contributions from all 2-to-2 elastic scattering processes (heavy-heavy, heavy-light, and heavy-gluon) in the t-channel at leading logarithmic accuracy. The calculation was performed both with a fixed strong coupling constant and with a running coupling parameterized in the space-like sector using an effective coupling from Dokshitzer (1996) that remains finite in the infrared.

## Key numbers

- At a temperature of 0.4 GeV and charm quark momentum of 25 GeV, the inclusion of heavy-heavy (QQ) scatterings increases the total heavy quark energy loss by approximately 5%.
- At a temperature of 0.6 GeV and charm quark momentum of 25 GeV, the inclusion of heavy-heavy (QQ) scatterings increases the total heavy quark energy loss by approximately 8%.
- Number densities at 0.4 GeV: light quarks plus anti-quarks 18.26 fm^-3, gluons 16.23 fm^-3, heavy quarks plus anti-quarks 2.55 fm^-3.
- Number densities at 0.6 GeV: light quarks plus anti-quarks 61.63 fm^-3, gluons 54.78 fm^-3, heavy quarks plus anti-quarks 16.06 fm^-3.
- The charm quark mass was taken as 1.25 GeV and the fixed strong coupling constant as 0.3.
- The maximum momentum transfer per scattering was scaled to q_max = sqrt(4 E_p T).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of heavy quark collisional energy loss in a QCD plasma (Thoma, 1991; Braaten and Thoma, 1991; Peigne and Peshier, 2008) that considered only scatterings with light quarks and gluons, by adding scatterings with thermalized charm quarks. The motivation comes from ALICE measurements at LHC of J/psi nuclear modification factor R_AA = 0.58 +/- 0.01 (stat) +/- 0.09 (syst) and elliptic flow v_2 up to 0.116 +/- 0.046 (stat) +/- 0.029 (syst) (Abbas, 2013), which indicate a significant population of thermalized charm quarks in the plasma. The results enable more realistic modeling of heavy quark energy loss for comparison with experimental data on nuclear modification factors and elliptic flow at LHC energies.
