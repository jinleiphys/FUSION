# Peripheral NN-Scattering: Role of Delta-Excitation, Correlated Two-Pion and Vector Meson Exchange

> We evaluate, within one-loop chiral perturbation theory, the two-pion exchange diagrams with single and double delta-isobar excitation contributing to elastic NN-scattering. We find that virtual $Δ$-excitation processes (in the static limit) produce the correct amount of isoscalar central attraction as needed in the peripheral partial waves with $L\geq 3$. Furthermore we compute the two-loop diagrams involving the $ππ$-interaction (so-called correlated $2π$-exchange). Contrary to common believe these processes lead to negligibly small and repulsive corrections to the NN-potential. The exchange of vector mesons ($ρ, ω$) turns out to be important for the F-wave phase shifts above $T_{lab}=150$ MeV. Without adjustable parameters we are able to reproduce the empirical NN phase shifts up to 350 MeV for $L\geq 3$ and up to about (50--80) MeV for the D-waves. This is therefore the characteristic window in which the NN-interaction is basically governed by chiral symmetry. Not surprisingly, the lower partial waves require non-perturbative methods and additional short-distance parametrizations of the NN-dynamics.

# Peripheral NN-Scattering: Role of Delta-Excitation, Correlated Two-Pion and Vector Meson Exchange

## Key claim

Within one-loop chiral perturbation theory, virtual delta-isobar excitation processes (in the static limit) produce the correct amount of isoscalar central attraction needed in peripheral partial waves with L ≥ 3, but only up to 350 MeV for L ≥ 3 and up to about 50–80 MeV for D-waves; the lower partial waves require non-perturbative methods and additional short-distance parametrizations.

## Method

The authors evaluated one-loop two-pion exchange diagrams with single and double delta-isobar excitation using covariant perturbation theory and dimensional regularization in the heavy baryon limit (M → ∞), plus first relativistic 1/M corrections. They also computed two-loop diagrams involving the chiral ππ-interaction (correlated 2π-exchange) and included vector meson (ρ, ω) exchange with standard coupling constants but no ad hoc form factors. The resulting NN-amplitudes were transformed into local coordinate space potentials and used to compute phase shifts and mixing angles perturbatively for partial waves where these quantities are smaller than 10°.

## Key numbers

- Delta-nucleon mass splitting Δ = 293 MeV (M_Δ − M, with M = 939 MeV)
- πNΔ coupling constant set via large-N_c value g_πNΔ = 3g_πN/√2 with g_πN = 13.4 (from Goldberger-Treiman relation with f_π = 92.4 MeV, giving g_A = 1.32)
- Calculated Δ → πN decay width: 110.6 MeV, compared to empirical value (115 ± 5) MeV
- ω-meson coupling constant: g_ωN²/4π = 12.9; ρ-meson tensor-to-vector coupling ratio κ_ρ = 6
- Empirical NN phase shifts reproduced up to 350 MeV for L ≥ 3 and up to about 50–80 MeV for D-waves
- Isoscalar central potential from single Δ-excitation at r = 1 fm: approximately −36/Δ times squared 1π-exchange potentials
- Correlated 2π-exchange isoscalar central potential at r = 1.0 fm: 0.731 MeV (repulsive); at r = 1.5 fm: 0.090 MeV; at r = 2.0 fm: 0.016 MeV
- Correlated 2π-exchange isovector central potential at r = 1.0 fm: 3.811 MeV; at r = 1.5 fm: 0.571 MeV; at r = 2.0 fm: 0.092 MeV
- Nucleon scalar form factor difference σ_N(2m_π²) − σ_N(0) = 14.9 MeV from one-loop calculation (8.0 MeV from nucleon loop + 6.9 MeV from Δ-isobar loop), compared to dispersion-theoretical value 15.2 ± 0.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework for NN-scattering developed by Kaiser, Brockmann, and Weise (1997), which evaluated 2π-exchange contributions up to third order in small momenta using low-energy constants c_i from πN data. The authors show that explicit Δ(1232) degrees of freedom cure the excessive attraction found in that earlier work for F-waves above 180 MeV and D-waves around 50 MeV. The results contradict common belief that correlated 2π-exchange produces the "σ-meson" enhancement around 550 MeV seen in dispersion-theoretical analyses by Hoehler (1983); instead, these diagrams give negligibly small repulsive corrections. The work enables parameter-free chiral NN phase shifts for L ≥ 3 to be used as input in future phase shift analyses, particularly with upcoming precision data from the Indiana Cooler Synchrotron Facility (IUCF).
