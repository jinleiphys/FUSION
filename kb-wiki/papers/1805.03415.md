# Electromagnetic couplings of radially excited light vector mesons from QCD sum rules

> The couplings of unflavored vector mesons to the $e^+e^-$ annihilation are an important source of information on the nature and structure of these resonances which play a prominent role in the hadron phenomenology. The couplings of many radially excited heavy vector mesons are measured, while the corresponding couplings for light vector mesons are not known. We propose a phenomenological method allowing to estimate these unknown couplings. The method is suggested by our observation that the electromagnetic coupling of the $n$-th radial excitation of $S$-wave heavy vector meson decouples from the $e^+e^-$ annihilation with nearly exponential rate with $n$. It becomes natural to assume that the same effect takes place in the light vector mesons and this would allow to estimate the unknown couplings. We tested this assumption with the help of a generalized version of borelized QCD spectral sum rules saturated by a linear radial trajectory of meson states taken from the phenomenology. This leads to a consistent setup which is able to predict the decoupling rate. The calculated rate turned out to be almost the same as in the heavy vector mesons. Our result may be interpreted as an effective account for non-trivial electromagnetic formfactor in meson decays to $e^+e^-$, thus looking beyond the large-$N_c$ limit. On the one hand, we argue that the given decoupling does not necessary contradict to the previous large-$N_c$ results for the Regge like meson spectra.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the electromagnetic couplings of radially excited light vector mesons to \(e^+e^-\) annihilation decrease nearly exponentially with excitation number \(n\), contradicting the standard large-\(N_c\) prediction of constant couplings, and they test this assumption using a generalized version of Borelized QCD spectral sum rules saturated by a linear radial trajectory of meson states.

## Method

The authors propose an ansatz \(F_n^2/F_0^2 = \beta^n\) (geometric progression) for the squared electromagnetic couplings of radial excitations, motivated by the observed exponential decoupling in heavy vector mesons (charmonium and bottomonium). They insert this ansatz into the Borel-transformed QCD spectral sum rules for the two-point vector correlator, using a linear radial mass spectrum \(m_n^2 = a n + m_0^2\) and summing over an infinite number of narrow states. The resulting sum rules are solved for the ground-state mass \(m_0\) and coupling \(F_0\) as functions of the Borel parameter \(M^2\) and the decoupling parameter \(\beta\), and the stability region (Borel window) is identified to extract the allowed range of \(\beta\).

## Key numbers

*   For the \(\rho\) meson, the ratio \(\beta \equiv F_1^2/F_0^2\) extracted from modern PDG data for \(\rho(1450)\) is \(\beta \approx 0.31(3)\).
*   For heavy vector mesons, the extracted \(\beta\) values are concentrated near \(\beta \approx 0.5\)–\(0.6\): for \(\psi(2S)\) \(\beta = 0.50 \pm 0.02\), for \(\Upsilon(2S)\) \(\beta = 0.48 \pm 0.01\).
*   The QCD sum rule analysis for the \(\rho\) meson, using a slope \(a = 1.14\ \text{GeV}^2\) and standard condensate values (\(\langle\frac{\alpha_s}{\pi}G^2\rangle = (330\ \text{MeV})^4\), \(\langle\bar{q}q\rangle = -(250\ \text{MeV})^3\)), yields a ground-state mass \(m_0 \approx 800\)–\(830\ \text{MeV}\) (expected in the zero-width and linear-trajectory approximations) for \(\beta \approx 0.5\)–\(0.6\).
*   The conservative estimate for the decoupling rate from the sum rule analysis is \(\beta \approx 0.3\)–\(0.8\).

## Context

This work extends the observation of nearly exponential decoupling of electromagnetic couplings in heavy vector mesons (charmonium and bottomonium, from PDG data) to the light vector meson sector (\(\rho\), \(\omega\), \(\phi\)), where experimental couplings for excited states are unknown. It contradicts the standard large-\(N_c\) prediction (Witten 1979) that couplings should be \(n\)-independent for highly excited states, as derived from the extended Vector Meson Dominance model (Bramon 1972, Sakurai 1973). The result enables a phenomenological estimate of the unknown electromagnetic decay widths of light excited vector mesons and suggests that the decoupling may be interpreted as an effective electromagnetic formfactor effect beyond the large-\(N_c\) limit.
