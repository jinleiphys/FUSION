# Isoscalar amplitude dominance in $e^+e-$ annihilation to $N\bar{N}$ pair close to the threshold

> We use the Paris nucleon-antinucleon optical potential for explanation of experimental data in the process $e^+e^- \rightarrow p\bar p$ near threshold. It turns out that final-state interaction due to Paris optical potential allows us to reproduce available experimental data. It follows from our consideration that the isoscalar form factor is much larger than the isovector one.

# Isoscalar amplitude dominance in $e^+e-$ annihilation to $N\bar{N}$ pair close to the threshold

## Key claim
Using the Paris nucleon-antinucleon optical potential, the isoscalar (I=0) amplitude dominates the cross section for $e^+e^- \rightarrow N\bar{N}$ near threshold, with the isoscalar form factor much larger than the isovector one. This conclusion depends on the assumption that the difference between $p\bar{p}$ and $n\bar{n}$ cross sections is small, and the result is specific to the Paris potential model.

## Method
The authors used the Paris nucleon-antinucleon optical potential, which includes a real part from G-parity transform of the Paris NN potential and an imaginary absorptive part for annihilation into mesons, to compute the final-state interaction effects on the $N\bar{N}$ production amplitude. They solved the non-relativistic Schrodinger equation for the $N\bar{N}$ wave function in the isospin channels I=0 and I=1, then extracted the dressed Dirac form factors and Sachs form factors from the wave functions at the origin. The cross sections were calculated and compared to experimental data for $e^+e^- \rightarrow p\bar{p}$ from threshold up to 2.2 GeV center-of-mass energy, with a single free parameter $|G_s^I|^2$ normalized to the third experimental point above threshold.

## Key numbers
- The isoscalar cross section $\sigma^0$ fits the data with $|G_s^0|^2 = 101.8$, giving $|G_s^0| = |F_1^0 + F_2^0| \approx 10$, described as "reasonable."
- The isovector cross section $\sigma^1$ requires $|G_s^1|^2 = 10184$ to fit the same data point, described as "not look[ing] reasonable."
- The Coulomb interaction between proton and antiproton is negligible for kinetic energies $T \gg 1$ MeV, where the threshold for Coulomb effects is $T \lesssim (\pi\alpha)^2 M \sim 1$ MeV.
- The analysis covers energies from threshold up to $2E = 2.2$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends the use of phenomenological $N\bar{N}$ optical potentials (Paris potential from Paris 1982 and Paris 1994) to explain the strong energy dependence of the ratio $|G_E/G_M|$ observed in $e^+e^- \rightarrow p\bar{p}$ by Aubert (2006) and earlier experiments by Bardin (1994) and Armstrong (1993). The approach is similar to final-state interaction explanations for enhancements in $J/\Psi \rightarrow \gamma p\bar{p}$ (BES 2003) and $B$ meson decays (Belle 2002, BaBar 2005). The results enable future tests using other optical potential models (e.g., Bonn potentials from 1991 and 1995) to confirm the isoscalar dominance claim.
