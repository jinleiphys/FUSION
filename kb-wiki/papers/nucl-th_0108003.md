# Energy and centrality dependences of charged multiplicity density in relativistic nuclear collisions

> Using a hadron and string cascade model, JPCIAE, the energy and centrality dependences of charged particle pseudorapidity density in relativistic nuclear collisions were studied. Within the framework of this model, both the relativistic $p+\bar p$ experimental data and the PHOBOS and PHENIX $Au+Au$ data at $\sqrt s_{nn}$=130 GeV could be reproduced fairly well without retuning the model parameters. The predictions for full RHIC energy $Au+Au$ collisions and for $Pb+Pb$ collisions at the ALICE energy were given. Participant nucleon distributions were calculated based on different methods. It was found that the number of participant nucleons, $<N_{part}>$, is not a well defined variable both experimentally and theoretically. Therefore, it is inappropriate to use charged particle pseudorapidity density per participant pair as a function of $<N_{part}>$ for distinguishing various theoretical models.

## Key claim

The JPCIAE hadron and string cascade model reproduces the PHOBOS and PHENIX charged particle pseudorapidity density data at √s_nn = 130 GeV without retuning parameters, but the authors argue that the number of participant nucleons ⟨N_part⟩ is not a well-defined variable either experimentally or theoretically, making the commonly used ratio of charged particle density per participant pair as a function of ⟨N_part⟩ unsuitable for distinguishing between theoretical models.

## Method

The authors used the JPCIAE (hadron and string cascade) model, which is based on PYTHIA and includes nucleon-nucleon collisions with string formation for center-of-mass energies above a 4 GeV cutoff, plus two-body collisions below that cutoff. Nucleons in the colliding nuclei were distributed randomly in a sphere of radius 1.12A^(1/3) fm with Woods-Saxon sampling, and the model includes rescatterings among participant nucleons, spectator nucleons, and produced particles. The authors calculated ⟨N_part⟩ using six different methods: a geometric method, a Glauber model method, three JPCIAE counting methods (c, e, f), and the FRITIOF 7.02 method, then compared the resulting centrality dependences.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle pseudorapidity density at mid-pseudorapidity in Au+Au collisions at √s_nn = 130 GeV was reproduced by JPCIAE for central to mid-central collisions, with agreement becoming less satisfactory for peripheral collisions.
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section at RHIC used in the Glauber model calculation was σ_in ≈ 40 mb.
- The nuclear radius parameter used in the geometric method was 1.12A^(1/3) fm.
- The string formation cutoff energy in JPCIAE was 4 GeV.
- ⟨N_part⟩ values from different methods showed 10%–15% differences for most central collisions, with discrepancies increasing for less central collisions.
- Rescattering in JPCIAE led to only a few percent increase in charged multiplicity, though it could enhance strangeness yields (e.g., Ξ⁻ + Ξ̄⁻) by a couple of times.

## Context

This work extends the JPCIAE model studies to RHIC energies and directly compares with the PHOBOS (2001) and PHENIX (2001) Au+Au data at √s_nn = 130 GeV. The paper contrasts its findings with the EKRT model (Eskola et al., 2000), which predicted a decreasing trend of charged particle density per participant pair with centrality, and with the HIJING model (Wang, 2000) and the KN model (Kharzeev and Nardi, 2001), which both predicted an increase. The authors' main conclusion, that ⟨N_part⟩ is not a well-defined variable, challenges the standard practice of using participant-normalized multiplicity to discriminate between models and suggests that future comparisons should use centrality measures based on the percentage of geometrical cross section instead.
