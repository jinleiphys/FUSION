# On the elliptical flow and asymmetry of the colliding nuclei

> A study of elliptical flow is presented with respect to the asymmetry of colliding nuclei using the reactions of 24Cr50 +44 Ru102, 16S32 +50 Sn120 and 8O16 +54 Xe136 at incident energies between 50 and 250 MeV/nucleon within the framework of isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model. For the present analysis, total mass of the colliding pairs is kept fixed and asymmetry is varied as ? = 0.2, 0.3, 0.7. The elliptical flow shows a transition from in-plane to out-of-plane in the mid rapidity region with incident energy. The transition energy is found to increase with the asymmetry for lighter fragments.

## Key claim

The transition energy at which elliptical flow changes from in-plane to out-of-plane in the mid-rapidity region increases with the asymmetry parameter for light charged particles, but this behavior is only observed when restricting the analysis to the mid-rapidity region and not when integrating over the entire rapidity range.

## Method

The authors simulated three reactions (24Cr50 + 44Ru102, 16S32 + 50Sn120, and 8O16 + 54Xe136) with a fixed total mass of 152 nucleons but varying asymmetry parameters η = 0.3, 0.5, and 0.7, using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model at incident energies between 50 and 250 MeV/nucleon for semi-central impact parameter with a soft equation of state. The phase space was analyzed using the minimum spanning tree method with a binding distance of 4 fm to identify fragments, and elliptical flow was quantified by the second-order Fourier coefficient ⟨v2⟩ from the azimuthal distribution of particles.

## Key numbers

- Total reacting mass fixed at 152 nucleon units for all three reactions.
- Asymmetry parameters used: η = 0.3 (24Cr50 + 44Ru102), η = 0.5 (16S32 + 50Sn120), and η = 0.7 (8O16 + 54Xe136).
- Incident energy range: 50 to 250 MeV/nucleon.
- Gaussian width L = 2.16 fm² used in the IQMD model.
- Nucleon radius initialization: R = 1.12A^(1/3) fm.
- Yukawa potential parameters: μ = 0.4 fm, t₃ = -6.66 MeV, t₄ = 100 MeV.
- Neutron-rich system (8O16 + 54Xe136) has N/Z = 1.4.
- Mid-rapidity region defined as -0.1 ≤ Y^(red) ≤ 0.1.

## Context

This work extends studies of collective flow in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the role of reaction asymmetry which had been explored in multifragmentation but not systematically for elliptical flow, as noted by the authors referencing the FOPI group (Diogene, Plastic, Nautilus, EOS, LAND collaborations) and earlier work by Sanjeev (2004). The results are compared with findings by Colonna and Toro (various years) on Gaussian-shaped behavior of elliptical flow, Zhang (various years) on N/Z effects, and Chen (various years) on symmetry energy effects in light charged particle production. The study enables future investigations into how asymmetry influences the nuclear equation of state and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-sections through the transition energy dependence.
