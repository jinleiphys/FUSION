# Holographic hybrid stars with slow phase transitions

> The $D_3$-$D_7$ holographic model is used to describe the core of the hybrid star, composed by quark matter, while its crust is modeled from a hadronic relativistic mean field (RMF) model capable of reproducing low-energy nuclear physics data as well as some astrophysical observations. The $D_3$-$D_7$ brane configuration and the RMF model lead to an equation of state that is used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations. For different model parameters, the mass-radius diagram is presented. The conditions for the dynamic stability of stellar configurations are discussed, considering the radial oscillation criterion for hybrid stars with slow phase transitions. Strikingly, it is shown that the models generate stable star configurations with a core of quarks. We compare our results with NICER observational data for the pulsars PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 and show that the compact stars generated from this method fall within the corresponding observational regions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that stable hybrid stars with a quark-matter core can be obtained from the D3-D7 holographic model combined with the NL3* hadronic model, but only when slow phase transitions are assumed; previous work using the same holographic model with fast phase transitions had not produced stable configurations with a quark core.

## Method

The quark phase is described by the D3-D7 top-down holographic brane model, which yields an equation of state with the constituent quark mass as the only free parameter. The hadronic crust is modeled using the NL3* relativistic mean-field (RMF) parameterization, with the outer crust described by the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS) equation and the inner crust by a polytropic form. The hadron-quark interface is treated as a sharp discontinuity via the Maxwell construction, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved together with the radial oscillation equations to assess dynamical stability under the slow phase transition criterion.

## Key numbers

* For constituent quark mass m = 390 MeV, the maximum hybrid star mass reaches 2.7 solar masses.
* For m = 300 MeV, the transition pressure p_t = 97.18 MeV/fm^3, the energy density at transition epsilon_t = 403.24 MeV/fm^3, and the energy density gap Delta epsilon_t = 241.46 MeV/fm^3.
* For m = 390 MeV, the transition pressure p_t = 286.00 MeV/fm^3, epsilon_t = 668.30 MeV/fm^3, and Delta epsilon_t = 1213.33 MeV/fm^3.
* For a 2.2 solar mass hybrid star with m = 300 MeV, the quark core mass is 0.231 solar masses and the core radius is 4.788 km.
* For a 2.2 solar mass hybrid star with m = 390 MeV, the quark core mass is 0.385 solar masses and the core radius is 3.263 km.
* The outer crust density range used is 6.3 x 10^{-12} fm^{-3} to 2.5 x 10^{-4} fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Hoyos et al. (2016) and Annala et al. (2018) that applied the D3-D7 holographic model to hybrid stars assuming fast phase transitions, which produced non-standard configurations (HS2 and HS3 types) without a stable quark core. By instead assuming slow phase transitions and applying the radial oscillation stability criterion from Chandrasekhar (1964), the authors obtain stable hybrid stars with a quark core that fall within the 95% confidence intervals from NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620. The results enable future work incorporating SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking and beta equilibrium in the quark sector.
