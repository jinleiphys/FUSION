# Symmetries of temporal correlators and the nature of hot QCD

> The temperature of the chiral restoration phase transition at 130 MeV as well as the temperature of the center symmetry ("deconfinement") phase transition in a pure glue theory at 300 MeV are two independent temperatures and their interplay determines a structure of different regimes of hot QCD. Given a chiral spin symmetry of the color charge and of the chromoelectric interaction we can conclude from observed symmetries of spatial and temporal correlators of N_F=2 QCD with domain wall Dirac operator at physical quark masses that above the chiral symmetry restoration crossover around T_pc but below rougly 3T_pc there should exist an intermediate regime (the stringy fluid) of hot QCD that is characterized by approximate chiral spin symmetry and where degrees of freedom are chirally symmetric quarks bound into color singlet objects by the chromoelectric field. Above this intermediate regime the color charge and the chromoelectric field are Debye screened and one observes a transition to QGP with magnetic confinement.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hot QCD above the chiral restoration crossover (around \(T_{pc} \sim 155\) MeV) but below roughly \(3T_{pc}\) contains an intermediate "stringy fluid" regime characterized by approximate chiral spin symmetry, contradicting the earlier lore of a single common deconfinement-chiral restoration crossover near 155 MeV. The claim is hedged by the observation that the renormalized Polyakov loop shows its inflection point around 300 MeV, suggesting that the center symmetry ("deconfinement") transition in full QCD is smeared but still occurs near that higher temperature.

## Method

The authors analyze spatial and temporal correlators of meson operators (\(J=0,1\)) computed on \(48^3 \times 12\) lattices using \(N_F=2\) domain wall Dirac operator configurations at physical quark masses (JLQCD configurations). They compare the symmetry patterns observed in these correlators, specifically degeneracies under \(U(1)_A\), \(SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R\), \(SU(2)_{CS}\), and \(SU(4)\) transformations, to those from free noninteracting quarks on the same lattice, and interpret the emergence or absence of these symmetries as signatures of different QCD regimes.

## Key numbers

- Chiral restoration pseudocritical temperature: \(T_{pc} \sim 155\) MeV (from the quark condensate dropping to zero between \(\sim 100\) MeV and \(\sim 200\) MeV).
- Center symmetry ("deconfinement") phase transition temperature in pure glue theory: \(\sim 300\) MeV; the renormalized Polyakov loop in full QCD shows an inflection point around this temperature.
- Chiral phase transition temperature: \(T_c \sim 130\) MeV (from reference [K]).
- Temperature of temporal correlator analysis: \(T = 220\) MeV, on \(48^3 \times 12\) lattices.
- Upper boundary of the intermediate stringy fluid regime: roughly \(T \sim 500\) MeV (approximately \(3T_{pc}\)), above which chiral spin and \(SU(4)\) symmetries disappear.
- Effective potential between static sources shown at \(T = 141\) MeV and \(T = 166\) MeV, both exhibiting string breaking but no Debye screening.

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of chiral symmetry restoration (Wuppertal group, Bielefeld group) and the renormalized Polyakov loop (reference [P]) to show that deconfinement and chiral restoration are not simultaneous. It builds on prior predictions by Glozman (references [G1,G2,G3,G4]) that chiral spin symmetry should emerge above \(T_{pc}\) based on zero-temperature truncation of near-zero Dirac modes (references [D1,D2]). The results enable a refined picture of hot QCD with three regimes: hadron gas, stringy fluid, and quark-gluon plasma, and motivate further temporal correlator studies at higher temperatures and with larger \(N_t\) to confirm the transition near 500 MeV.
