# Nucleon-number scalings of anisotropic flows and nuclear modification factor for light nuclei in the squeeze-out region

> The number of nucleon (NN) scaling of the directed flow $v_1$ and elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) as well as the nuclear modification factor ($R_{cp}$) are tested for light nuclei which are produced in 0.4$A$ GeV $^{197}Au + ^{197}Au$ collisions at different impact parameters with two different in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections in the framework of an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model. In that energy domain, the emission of light nuclei can be well described by the squeeze-out phenonomenon. The results show a nice NN scaling behavior for flow parameters $v_{1}$, $v_{2}$ and ${R_{cp}}$. These results demonstrate that the nucleon coalescence mechanism is responsible for nucleon-number scaling of above physical observables in squeeze-out region in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energy.

# Nucleon-number scalings of anisotropic flows and nuclear modification factor for light nuclei in the squeeze-out region

## Key claim

The nucleon-number scaling of directed flow ($v_1$), elliptic flow ($v_2$), and nuclear modification factor ($R_{cp}$) holds for light nuclei produced in 0.4$A$ GeV Au+Au collisions where the emission exhibits squeeze-out behavior (negative elliptic flow), but only after applying a constant factor difference between light nuclei and protons for the $R_{cp}$ scaling. This scaling supports the nucleonic coalescence mechanism for light nucleus formation at kinetic freeze-out in the intermediate-energy regime, which had not been previously tested for the squeeze-out region.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate 0.4$A$ GeV $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au collisions at different impact parameters with two in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section reduction factors ($\eta = 0.0$ and $\eta = 0.5$). Light nuclei (protons, deuterons, tritons) were identified using a coalescence prescription with a distance cutoff of 3.5 fm in coordinate space and a relative momentum cutoff of 0.3 GeV/c. Anisotropic flows $v_1$ and $v_2$ were calculated via Fourier expansion of the azimuthal distribution, and $R_{cp}$ was computed as the ratio of particle yields in central collisions (0-10%, 10-20%, 20-40%) to peripheral collisions (60-80%), scaled by binary collision number.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 0.4$A$ GeV for $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au collisions
- Coalescence parameters: distance cutoff = 3.5 fm, relative momentum cutoff = 0.3 GeV/c
- In-medium NNCS reduction factors: $\eta = 0.0$ and $\eta = 0.5$ (with $\eta \approx 0.2$ noted as better reproducing flow data from prior work)
- For $R_{cp}$ scaling, a constant factor of 1/0.76 was applied to proton $R_{cp}$ to match deuteron and triton scaled values (for panels (a) and (d))
- Soft equation of state with momentum-dependent interaction (SM+MDI) was used, with incompressibility $K = 200$ MeV
- Results extracted at 200 fm/c (freeze-out stage)

## Context

This work extends the nucleon-number scaling concept for anisotropic flows, first proposed by Yan and Ma for lower-energy heavy-ion collisions, into the intermediate-energy squeeze-out regime where elliptic flow is negative. It also extends the nucleon-number scaling of $R_{cp}$, previously proposed by one of the authors for relativistic energies (NA49, RHIC, LHC), to intermediate energies. The study uses the IQMD model, which has been successful for reaction dynamics at these energies. The results enable experimental tests of coalescence mechanism predictions in the intermediate-energy domain, complementing prior experimental confirmations at RHIC energies (200, 62.4, 39, 27, 19.6, 11.5, and 7.7 GeV) and lower-energy studies.
