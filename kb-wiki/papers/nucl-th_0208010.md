# Chiral dynamics of p-wave in K^- p and coupled states

> We perform an evaluation of the p-wave amplitudes of meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness S=-1 sector starting from the lowest order chiral Lagrangians and introducing explicitly the Sigma^* field with couplings to the meson-baryon states obtained using SU(6) symmetry. The N/D method of unitarization is used, equivalent, in practice, to the use of the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a cut-off. The procedure leaves no freedom for the p-waves once the s-waves are fixed and thus one obtains genuine predictions for the p-wave scattering amplitudes, which are in good agreement with experimental results for differential cross sections, as well as for the width and partial decay widths of the Sigma^*(1385).

## Key claim

The paper claims that p-wave meson-baryon scattering amplitudes in the strangeness S=-1 sector can be predicted with no free parameters once the s-wave amplitudes are fixed using the same cut-off (or subtraction constants) from earlier s-wave work, and that these predictions agree well with experimental differential cross sections and the properties of the Sigma*(1385) resonance.

## Method

The authors start from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian coupling the octet of pseudoscalar mesons to the octet of 1/2+ baryons, with D=0.85 and F=0.52. They include explicit Lambda, Sigma, and Sigma* pole terms, with the Sigma* couplings obtained using SU(6) symmetry. The tree-level p-wave amplitudes are used as the kernel of the Bethe-Salpeter equation (equivalent to the N/D method), regularized either by a cut-off of 630 MeV or by dimensional regularization with subtraction constants fixed from earlier s-wave work by Angels (2000) and Cornelius (2001). The unitarized amplitudes are separated into spin-non-flip (f) and spin-flip (g) components, which are then combined into f+ (J=3/2) and f- (J=1/2) amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Cut-off value: q_max = 630 MeV, used to regularize loop functions, taken from earlier s-wave work by Angels (2000)
- Subtraction constants (set from Cornelius (2001)): a_KbarN = -1.84, a_piSigma = -2.00, a_piLambda = -1.83, a_etaLambda = -2.25, a_etaSigma = -2.38, a_KXi = -2.67
- Bare masses used: M_tilde_Lambda = 1078 MeV, M_tilde_Sigma = 1104 MeV, M_tilde_Sigma* = 1359 MeV
- Fine-tuned subtraction constants: a_KbarN = -1.75, a_piSigma = -2.10, a_piLambda = -1.62, a_etaLambda = -2.56, a_etaSigma = -1.54, a_KXi = -2.67
- Fine-tuned bare masses: M_tilde_Lambda = 1069 MeV, M_tilde_Sigma = 1195 MeV, M_tilde_Sigma* = 1413 MeV
- Threshold ratios with original parameters: gamma = 2.30, R_c = 0.618, R_n = 0.257
- Threshold ratios with fine-tuned parameters: gamma = 2.36, R_c = 0.634, R_n = 0.178
- Sigma*(1385) width: Gamma_Sigma* approximately 31 MeV (experimental value: 35 +/- 4 MeV)
- Ratio of partial decay widths: Gamma_piLambda / Gamma_piSigma = 7.7 (experimental value: 7.5 +/- 0.5)

## Context

This work extends the s-wave coupled-channel approach of Angels (2000) and Cornelius (2001) for K- p scattering to include p-waves, using the same lowest-order chiral Lagrangian and regularization scheme. It contrasts with the approach of Lutz (2002), which introduced approximately 25 free parameters fitted to data, while the present work achieves parameter-free p-wave predictions. The results enable a complete description of low-energy meson-baryon dynamics in the S=-1 sector, including both s-wave and p-wave contributions, and provide a framework for studying the Sigma*(1385) resonance properties without adjustable parameters.
