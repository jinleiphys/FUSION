# Baryon diffusion near the QCD critical point

> Fireballs created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at different beam energies have been argued to follow different trajectories in the QCD phase diagram in which the QCD critical point serves as a landmark. Using a (1+1)-dimensional model setting with transverse homogeneity, we study the complexities introduced by the fact that the evolution history of each fireball cannot be characterized by a single trajectory but rather covers an entire swath of the phase diagram, with the finally emitted hadron spectra integrating over contributions from many different trajectories. Studying the phase diagram trajectories of fluid cells at different space-time rapidities, we explore how baryon diffusion shuffles them around, and how they are affected by critical dynamics near the QCD critical point. We find a striking insensitivity of baryon diffusion to critical effects. Its origins are analyzed and possible implications discussed.

## Key claim

The authors find that baryon diffusion in heavy-ion collisions is strikingly insensitive to critical effects near the QCD critical point, even for fluid cells passing close to it, because the baryon diffusion current decays to a tiny value before the system enters the critical region where correlation length enhancement occurs.

## Method

The authors performed (1+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations of central Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 19.6 GeV using the BEShydro code, assuming transverse homogeneity and a static initial longitudinal flow profile in Milne coordinates with vanishing initial baryon diffusion current. They incorporated critical scaling of the isothermal susceptibility χ, baryon diffusion coefficient κn, and relaxation time τn with the correlation length ξ according to χ ∼ ξ², κn ∼ ξ, and τn ∼ ξ², using an analytical parametrization where ξmax/ξ0 = 10, and compared trajectories with and without critical effects at different space-time rapidities.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: √sNN = 19.6 GeV for Au-Au collisions
- Initial proper time: τi = 1.5 fm/c
- Critical point location assumed at Tc = 149 MeV, μc = 250 MeV
- Maximum correlation length ratio: ξmax/ξ0 = 10
- Baryon diffusion current decays to negligible values by τ ≈ 3.5 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the Hydro+/++ framework developed by Stephanov (2017) and An (2019) for incorporating off-equilibrium critical fluctuations, and uses the neos equation of state from Monnai (2019) for non-zero net baryon density. The results suggest that calibrating the bulk medium evolution at Beam Energy Scan energies may be possible without including critical effects on baryon diffusion, though the authors caution that full simulations including shear and bulk viscous effects simultaneously are needed for quantitative evaluation.
