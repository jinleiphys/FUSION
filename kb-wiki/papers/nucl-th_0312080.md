# New analytic solutions of the collective Bohr hamiltonian for a beta-soft, gamma-soft axial rotor

> New analytic solutions of the quadrupole collective Bohr hamiltonian are proposed, exploiting an approximate separation of the beta and gamma variables to describe gamma-soft prolate axial rotors. The model potential is a sum of two terms: a beta-dependent term taken either with a Coulomb-like or a Kratzer-like form, and a gamma-dependent term taken as an harmonic oscillator. In particular it is possible to give a one parameter paradigm for a beta-soft, gamma-soft axial rotor that can be applied, with a considerable agreement, to the spectrum of 234U.

# New analytic solutions of the collective Bohr hamiltonian for a beta-soft, gamma-soft axial rotor

## Key claim
The paper presents new analytic solutions of the Bohr hamiltonian that describe a beta-soft, gamma-soft prolate axial rotor, using an approximate separation of beta and gamma variables. The key qualifier is that the gamma-dependent harmonic oscillator potential is meaningful only around gamma = 0 degrees, where it approximates the correct periodic even function, and the separation of variables is only approximate.

## Method
The authors solve the Bohr hamiltonian by separating it into beta and gamma parts, using a potential that is a sum of a beta-dependent term (either Coulomb-like or Kratzer-like) and a gamma-dependent harmonic oscillator term. For the beta part, they substitute xi_L(beta) = chi_L(beta) * beta^(-2) to obtain a standard second-order differential equation, which reduces to Whittaker's equation whose regular solution is the Whittaker function M_{k,mu}(x). The gamma part is solved by expanding sine functions in powers of gamma and using Laguerre polynomials. Electromagnetic transition rates are evaluated by separating the integral into angular, gamma, and beta parts, with the beta integral calculated numerically.

## Key numbers
- For the Coulomb-like potential, the energy of the first 4+ state relative to the first 2+ state is 1.718 (in units of the (v=0, L=2) energy minus the (v=0, L=0) energy).
- For the Coulomb-like potential, the energy of the (v=1, L=0) state (beginning of the second band) is 1.423 in the same units.
- For the Coulomb-like potential, the threshold corresponding to infinite L quantum number is located at 2.5616 in the same units.
- For the Kratzer-like potential, the ratio (epsilon_{0,4} - epsilon_{0,0})/(epsilon_{0,2} - epsilon_{0,0}) varies between 1.718 and 10/3 as the parameter B varies from small to large values.
- For the Kratzer-like potential applied to 234U, the parameters used are B = 393.7415 and c = -41.5629.
- For the Kratzer-like potential, the B(E2) value for the 4+_0 to 2+_0 transition tends to the rigid rotor value of 10/7 when B is large.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analytic solution for gamma-unstable potentials (Fortunato, 2003) and builds on Iachello's approximate solution for the axially symmetric deformed rotor with an infinite square well (Iachello, 2001). The approach provides a one-parameter paradigm for beta-soft, gamma-soft axial rotors that can be applied to experimental spectra, demonstrated here for 234U. The solution enables systematic surveys of experimental data across a broad range of nuclear deformations, from gamma-soft to rigid rotor limits.
