# Comparing many-body approaches against the helium atom exact solution

> Over time, many different theories and approaches have been developed to tackle the many-body problem in quantum chemistry, condensed-matter physics, and nuclear physics. Here we use the helium atom, a real system rather than a model, and we use the exact solution of its Schrödinger equation as a benchmark for comparison between methods. We present new results beyond the random-phase approximation (RPA) from a renormalized RPA (r-RPA) in the framework of the self-consistent RPA (SCRPA) originally developed in nuclear physics, and compare them with various other approaches like configuration interaction (CI), quantum Monte Carlo (QMC), time-dependent density-functional theory (TDDFT), and the Bethe-Salpeter equation on top of the GW approximation. Most of the calculations are consistently done on the same footing, e.g. using the same basis set, in an effort for a most faithful comparison between methods.

## Key claim

The helium atom, with its exact Hylleraas solution, serves as a superior benchmark for many-body theories compared to simplified models like spherium, and the paper demonstrates that condensed-matter approaches incorporating screening (such as RPA and its variants) pass this severe test surprisingly well, though the renormalized RPA (r-RPA) results are presented as new calculations beyond the standard random-phase approximation.

## Method

The authors performed a consistent comparison of many-body approaches against the exact Hylleraas solution for the helium atom, using the same Gaussian basis set (d-aug-cc-pV5Z) for most methods to ensure faithful comparison. They implemented the renormalized RPA (r-RPA) within the self-consistent RPA (SCRPA) framework originally developed in nuclear physics, which iteratively updates occupation numbers and single-particle energies from correlated RPA amplitudes until self-consistency (requiring at most four cycles for 10⁻⁴ Ha accuracy). Additionally, they calculated ground and excited states using Hartree-Fock (HF), configuration interaction (CI), quantum Monte Carlo (QMC), time-dependent density-functional theory (TDDFT), the Bethe-Salpeter equation on top of GW (GW+BSE), and direct RPA (dRPA) on top of HF, GW, and DFT-LDA electronic structures.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy from exact Hylleraas solution: -2.903724377034119598311159245194404446696925 Ha (35 decimal digit accuracy from Schwartz (2006))
* Hylleraas original solution had a relative error of only 10⁻⁴
* The LDA approximation error with respect to exact Hylleraas ground state is 7·10⁻² Ha
* Basis-set convergence error for helium can be reduced from 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻⁶ Ha using basis-set families optimized for DFT LDA
* The depletion/repletion of occupation numbers in r-RPA for helium constitutes a correction of less than 1%
* Full-CI ground-state energy with cc-pV5Z basis: -2.903151884 Ha
* Full-CI ground-state energy with d-aug-cc-pVQZ basis: -2.903151884 Ha

## Context

This work extends the tradition of using the helium atom as a testbed for many-body theories, following the pioneering Hylleraas (1929) solution and subsequent improvements by Kinoshita (1957), Frankowski and Pekeris (1966), and Schwartz (2006). It directly compares condensed-matter approaches (GW, BSE, TDDFT, dRPA) with nuclear-physics methods (RPA, r-RPA, SCRPA) on the same footing, addressing the issue that previous comparisons were hampered by inconsistent numerical techniques. The r-RPA calculations had previously only been applied to nuclear physics models and the jellium-sphere model (Catara and Van Giai, 1996; Catara and Sambataro, 1998), not to real systems, and the work enables future full SCRPA calculations while providing a standardized benchmark methodology for evaluating new many-body developments.
