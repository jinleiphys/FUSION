# Robust ab initio predictions for dimensionless ratios of E2 and radius observables. II. Estimation of E2 transition strengths by calibration to the charge radius

> Converged results for E2 observables are notoriously challenging to obtain in ab initio no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) approaches. Matrix elements of the E2 operator are sensitive to the large-distance tails of the nuclear wave function, which converge slowly in an oscillator basis expansion. Similar convergence challenges beset ab initio prediction of the nuclear charge radius. However, we exploit systematic correlations between the calculated E2 and radius observables to yield meaningful predictions for relations among these observables. In particular, we examine ab initio predictions for dimensionless ratios of the form B(E2)/(e^2r^4), for nuclei throughout the p shell. Meaningful predictions for E2 transition strengths may then be made by calibrating to the ground-state charge radius, if experimentally known.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that dimensionless ratios of the form \(B(E2)/(e^2 r^4)\), relating E2 transition strengths to the ground-state charge radius, are sufficiently convergent in no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations to provide meaningful predictions for E2 strengths by calibration to experimentally known charge radii, even when the ground-state quadrupole moment is unavailable for calibration.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio NCCI (no-core shell model) calculations for p-shell nuclei using three internucleon interactions: Daejeon16, JISP16, and a chiral LENPIC interaction. They computed the dimensionless ratio \(B(E2)/(e^2 r_p^4)\) for low-lying E2 transitions in nuclei where the ground-state angular momentum does not support a quadrupole moment (J=0 or J=1/2), including \({}^{10}\)Be, \({}^{10}\)C, \({}^{11}\)Be, \({}^{12}\)C, \({}^{13}\)C, and \({}^{13}\)N, and compared convergence properties with the ratio \(B(E2)/(eQ)^2\) using \({}^{9}\)Be as an illustrative case.

## Key numbers

- For \({}^{9}\)Be, the experimental \(B(E2;5/2^-\rightarrow 3/2^-)\) is \(28(2)\,e^2\text{fm}^4\), with the \(5/2^-\) state at \(2.42\,\text{MeV}\) having a width of \(\approx 0.8\,\text{keV}\)
- For the Daejeon16 interaction in \({}^{9}\)Be, values of \(B(E2)/(eQ)^2\) for successive \(\Nmax\) differ by \(\lesssim 1.5\%\) even at the lowest \(\Nmax\) shown
- The rotational value for \(B(E2)/(eQ)^2\) in a \(K=3/2\) band is \(75/(28\pi)\approx 0.8526\), with Daejeon16 results lying \(\approx 5\%\) above this value
- For \({}^{10}\)Be, the experimental \(B(E2;2^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_1)\) is \(9.34({+46\atop-36})\,e^2\text{fm}^4\)
- The convergence rate parameter \(\eta\) for \(B(E2)\) in \({}^{9}\)Be is \(\eta\approx 0.9\), while for \(B(E2)/(e^2 r_p^4)\) it is reduced to \(0.6\lesssim\eta\lesssim 0.8\)
- For the \(A=10\) mirror nuclei, relative differences between successive \(\Nmax\) values are about twice as large for \({}^{10}\)C as for \({}^{10}\)Be, with similar difference ratios (\(\eta\approx 0.8\)) for both

## Context

This work extends the approach of Caprio et al. (2021, 2022) on dimensionless ratios of E2 and radius observables, following Part I which considered the ratio \(Q/r^2\) for ground-state quadrupole moments and radii. The paper addresses cases where calibration to the ground-state quadrupole moment is impossible (even-even nuclei and odd-mass nuclei with J=1/2 ground states), enabling E2 strength predictions for nuclei such as \({}^{10}\)Be, \({}^{10}\)C, \({}^{11}\)Be, \({}^{12}\)C, \({}^{13}\)C, and \({}^{13}\)N. The results enable extraction of proton and neutron deformation parameters under the assumption of axial symmetry, and provide a pathway for predicting E2 strengths in nuclei where only the charge radius is experimentally known.
