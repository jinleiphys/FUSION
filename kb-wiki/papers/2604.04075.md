# Anisotropic Flow of light (anti-)(hyper-)nuclei in Pb+Pb Collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.36$ TeV

> Using the coalescence model with nucleon phase-space distributions generated by the hybrid MUSIC framework, we study the elliptic flow ($v_2$) and triangular flow ($v_3$) of (anti-)protons, (anti-)deuterons, (anti-)$^3\mathrm{He}$, and ${^3_Λ\mathrm{H}}$ in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.36$ TeV. We find that the simple $v_2$ scaling with the number of constituent nucleons $A$ breaks down at high transverse momentum $p_T/A > 1.5$ GeV/$c$, while an improved scaling relation holds well up to $p_T/A \approx 3$ GeV/$c$. In contrast, $v_3$ exhibits similar behavior under both scaling prescriptions, with no significant difference. We also make predictions for $v_2$ and $v_3$ of the hypertriton and find these flows are insensitive to the Lambda-deuteron ($Λ-d$) distance inside the hypertriton. Our results are compared with preliminary experimental measurements by the ALICE Collaboration and offer insight into the production mechanisms of light (anti-)(hyper-)nuclei in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The simple scaling of elliptic flow ($v_2$) with the number of constituent nucleons $A$ breaks down at high transverse momentum ($p_T/A > 1.5$ GeV/$c$), but an improved scaling relation derived from the $A$-th power of the proton azimuthal distribution remains valid up to $p_T/A \approx 3$ GeV/$c$; for triangular flow ($v_3$), both scaling prescriptions give similar results with no significant difference.

## Method

The authors used the hybrid MUSIC+UrQMD+COAL framework, where the quark-gluon plasma evolution is modeled with (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC) initialized by the IP-Glasma model, followed by hadronic rescatterings via UrQMD. The resulting freeze-out phase-space distributions of nucleons and $\Lambda$ hyperons were then input into a coalescence model using harmonic-oscillator Wigner functions to compute the production and anisotropic flow of light nuclei and hypernuclei. Flow coefficients $v_2$ and $v_3$ were extracted using the event-plane method with three-subevent resolution correction.

## Key numbers

- The simple $v_2$ scaling with $A$ breaks down at $p_T/A > 1.5$ GeV/$c$; the improved scaling holds up to $p_T/A \approx 3$ GeV/$c$.
- For the deuteron, the size parameter $\sigma_d = 2.26$ fm, derived from the root-mean-square radius $r_d \approx 1.96$ fm.
- For $^3$He, the width parameters are $\sigma_r = \sigma_\lambda = 1.76$ fm, equal to its root-mean-square radius.
- For the hypertriton, three values of $\sigma_\lambda$ are used: 5.45 fm, 6.52 fm, and 7.96 fm, corresponding to different $\Lambda$ separation energies $B_\Lambda = 0.17 \pm 0.06$ MeV.
- The collision energy is $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.36$ TeV for Pb+Pb collisions.

## Context

This work extends previous coalescence model studies of light nuclei anisotropic flow at RHIC and LHC energies, following the NCN scaling framework first proposed by Yan and Ma (2006) and confirmed by STAR and BM@N experimental data. It builds on earlier ALICE measurements of deuteron and $^3$He $v_2$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV, and on more realistic coalescence calculations using hydrodynamics with UrQMD afterburner by Yin (2017), Zhu (2017), and Zhao (2018). The results provide predictions for upcoming LHC Run 3 data and demonstrate that hypertriton flow is insensitive to its halo structure, unlike its yield and $p_T$ spectrum studied by Liu (2024).
