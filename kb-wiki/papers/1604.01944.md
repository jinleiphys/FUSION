# Neutron star radii and crusts: uncertainties and unified equations of state

> The uncertainties in neutron star (NS) radii and crust properties due to our limited knowledge of the equation of state (EOS) are quantitatively analysed. We first demonstrate the importance of a unified microscopic description for the different baryonic densities of the star. If the pressure functional is obtained matching a crust and a core EOS based on models with different properties at nuclear matter saturation, the uncertainties can be as large as $\sim 30\%$ for the crust thickness and $4\%$ for the radius. Necessary conditions for causal and thermodynamically consistent matchings between the core and the crust are formulated and their consequences examined. A large set of unified EOS for purely nucleonic matter is obtained based on 24 Skyrme interactions and 9 relativistic mean-field nuclear parametrizations. In addition, for relativistic models 17 EOS including a transition to hyperonic matter at high density are presented. All these EOS have in common the property of describing a $2\;M_\odot$ star and of being causal within stable NS. A span of $\sim 3$ km and $\sim 4$ km is obtained for the radius of, respectively, $1.0\;M_\odot$ and $2.0\;M_\odot$ star. Applying a set of nine further constraints from experiment and ab-initio calculations the uncertainty is reduced to $\sim 1$ km and $2$ km, respectively. These residual uncertainties reflect lack of constraints at large densities and insufficient information on the density dependence of the EOS near the nuclear matter saturation point. The most important parameter to be constrained is shown to be the symmetry energy slope $L$ which exhibits a linear correlation with the stellar radius, particularly for masses $\sim 1.0\;M_\odot$. Potential constraints on $L$, the NS radius and the EOS from observations of thermal states of NS are also discussed. [Abriged]

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using non-unified equations of state (EOS) for neutron stars, where the crust and core are described by different nuclear models, introduces uncertainties of up to ~30% for crust thickness and ~4% for radius, but these uncertainties are largely eliminated when a unified EOS (same nuclear interaction for all densities) is employed.

## Method

The authors constructed a large set of unified EOS for purely nucleonic matter using 24 Skyrme interactions and 9 relativistic mean-field (RMF) nuclear parametrizations, plus 17 RMF EOS including hyperonic matter, all capable of describing a 2 solar mass star while remaining causal. They systematically compared these unified EOS against non-unified EOS built by matching crust models (such as Baym-Pethick-Sutherland or Douchin-Haensel) to core models (such as GM1, NL3, NL3ωρ) at various matching densities, and formulated necessary conditions for thermodynamically consistent and causal matchings using the pressure-chemical potential plane.

## Key numbers

- For non-unified EOS, relative differences compared to unified EOS can reach ~30% for crust thickness and ~4% for radius (e.g., for a 1.0 solar mass star, radius differences up to ~1 km and crust thickness differences up to ~0.5 km)
- For the unified EOS set, a span of ~3 km is obtained for the radius of a 1.0 solar mass star and ~4 km for a 2.0 solar mass star
- Applying nine further constraints from experiment and ab-initio calculations reduces the radius uncertainty to ~1 km for 1.0 solar mass and ~2 km for 2.0 solar mass stars
- The symmetry energy slope L exhibits a linear correlation with stellar radius, particularly clear for masses around 1.0 solar mass
- The normal nuclear density is defined as n0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} (corresponding to mass-energy density ρ0 = 2.7 × 10^{14} g cm^{-3})

## Context

This work extends the analysis of neutron star structure uncertainties by systematically comparing unified versus non-unified EOS approaches, building on prior studies such as Glendenning (1999) which used piecewise matching of BPS crust to RMF cores, and Read et al. which used piecewise polytropes with a single low-density EOS (Douchin-Haensel). The paper provides a large set of unified EOS made available on the CompOSE database, enabling future work to quantify residual uncertainties from the density dependence of the EOS near saturation, particularly the symmetry energy slope L, which could be further constrained by observations from upcoming X-ray missions like NICER, Athena, and LOFT.
