# Symplectic Symmetry of the Neutrino Mass and the See-Saw Mechanism

> We investigate the algebraic structure of the most general neutrino mass Hamiltonian and place the see-saw mechanism in an algebraic framework. We show that this Hamiltonian can be written in terms of the generators of an Sp(4) algebra. The Pauli-Gursey transformation is an SU(2) rotation which is embedded in this Sp(4) group. This SU(2) also generates the see-saw mechanism.

# Symplectic Symmetry of the Neutrino Mass and the See-Saw Mechanism

## Key claim

The most general neutrino mass Hamiltonian for a single flavor can be written in terms of the generators of an Sp(4) algebra, and the see-saw mechanism is generated by a particular SU(2) rotation (the Pauli-Gursey transformation) embedded within this Sp(4) group; however, this algebraic framework does not specify the values of the left-handed and right-handed Majorana masses, and in particular the value m_L = 0 is not excluded.

## Method

The authors constructed the ten independent generators of the Sp(4) Lie algebra from bilinear charges of the neutrino field operators (left-handed and right-handed Dirac and Majorana pairing operators), using canonical equal-time anti-commutation relations. They then expressed the most general neutrino mass Hamiltonian (containing Dirac mass m_D, left-handed Majorana mass m_L, and right-handed Majorana mass m_R) as a linear combination of these generators, and showed that an SU(2) subgroup (denoted SU(2)_PG) generates the Pauli-Gursey transformation. By applying this SU(2)_PG rotation to the mass Hamiltonian and choosing the rotation parameter to eliminate the Dirac mass term, they derived the see-saw mechanism as a specific transformation of the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- The Sp(4) algebra has ten independent generators: D_+, D_-, L_+, L_-, L_0, R_+, R_-, R_0, A_+, and A_-.
- The see-saw result is obtained by choosing m_L = 0 and m_R >> m_D, yielding H_m' approximately equal to m_R (R_+ + R_-) - (m_D^2 / m_R) (L_+ + L_-).
- The rotation parameter tau is related to an angle delta by tau = tan(delta), and the condition to eliminate the Dirac mass term is tan(2 delta) = -2 m_D / (m_L - m_R).
- The transformed Hamiltonian after eliminating the Dirac term contains a factor (1/2)[4 m_D^2 + (m_L - m_R)^2]^{1/2} multiplying the difference term [(L_+ + L_-) - (R_+ + R_-)].

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach to neutrino masses by placing the see-saw mechanism (Gell-Mann, Ramond, and Slansky 1979; Yanagida 1979; Mohapatra and Senjanovic 1980) and the Pauli-Gursey transformation (Pauli 1957; Gursey 1957) into a common Sp(4) algebraic framework. The paper shows that the neutrino mass Hamiltonian sits in the Sp(4)/SU(2)_PG x U(1)_chi coset space, where the U(1) corresponds to a chirality transformation. This algebraic formulation could potentially be extended to make statements about the neutrino mixing matrix, which the authors note is currently under investigation, particularly in light of atmospheric neutrino observations (Superkamiokande collaboration 1998, 1999) suggesting maximal mixing of muon neutrinos.
