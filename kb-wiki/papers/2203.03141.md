# General relativistic treatment of $f$-mode oscillations of hyperonic stars

> We present a systematic study of $f$-mode oscillations in neutron stars containing hyperons, extending recent results obtained within the Cowling approximation to linearized General Relativity. Employing a relativistic mean field model, we find that the Cowling approximation can overestimate the quadrupolar $f$-mode frequency of neutron stars by up to 30\% compared to the frequency obtained in the linearized general relativistic formalism. Imposing current astrophysical constraints, we derive updated empirical relations for gravitational wave asteroseismology. The frequency and damping time of quadrupole $f$-mode oscillations of hyperonic stars are found to be in the range of 1.47 - 2.45kHz and 0.13 - 0.51 sec respectively. Our correlation studies demonstrate that among the various parameters of the nucleonic and hyperonic sectors of the model, the nucleon effective mass shows the strongest correlation with mode characteristics and neutron star observables. Estimates for the detectability of $f$-modes in a transient burst of gravitational waves from isolated hyperonic stars is also provided.

## Key claim

The Cowling approximation (neglecting metric perturbations) can overestimate the quadrupolar f-mode frequency of hyperonic neutron stars by up to 30% compared to the full linearized general relativistic calculation, and the authors derive updated empirical relations for gravitational wave asteroseismology that incorporate current astrophysical constraints from maximum mass and tidal deformability measurements.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with nucleonic and hyperonic degrees of freedom, randomly sampling saturation nuclear parameters within physically motivated ranges and applying astrophysical constraints (maximum mass ≥ 2 solar masses and tidal deformability from GW170817) to select 1483 pure nucleonic and 1123 hyperonic equation of state models. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for stellar structure and then compute complex f-mode frequencies by integrating the Lindblom-Detweiler perturbation equations inside the star and matching to outgoing wave solutions of the Zerilli equation outside the star, searching for complex eigenfrequencies where the incoming wave amplitude vanishes.

## Key numbers

- The Cowling approximation can overestimate the quadrupolar f-mode frequency by up to 30% compared to full general relativistic results
- Quadrupole f-mode frequencies of hyperonic stars range from 1.47 kHz to 2.45 kHz
- Damping times of quadrupole f-modes range from 0.13 sec to 0.51 sec
- For nucleonic matter, the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 imposes a lower limit on the nucleon effective mass: m* > 0.60 m_N
- For hyperonic matter, the 2 solar mass maximum mass constraint imposes an upper limit m* ≤ 0.70 m_N, combined with the lower limit m* > 0.60 m_N from tidal deformability
- The asteroseismology relation for f-mode frequency in this work gives coefficients a_r = 0.535 kHz and b_r = 36.20 kHz×km (for the relation f = a_r + b_r × sqrt(M/R^3))
- The asteroseismology relation for damping time gives coefficients a_i = 0.080 and b_i = -0.245 (for the relation R^4/(M^3 τ_f) = a_i + b_i × (M/R))

## Context

This work extends previous studies of f-mode oscillations in neutron stars, moving beyond the Cowling approximation used in Pradhan and Chatterjee (2021) to a full general relativistic treatment, and extends results from purely nucleonic stars to stars containing hyperons. The empirical relations update those of Andersson and Kokkotas (1998), Benhar and Ferrari (2001), and Doneva et al. (2013) by incorporating only equations of state that satisfy current observational constraints (2 solar mass maximum mass and GW170817 tidal deformability limits). The results enable more reliable extraction of neutron star interior composition from future gravitational wave detections of f-modes in isolated neutron stars or post-merger remnants.
