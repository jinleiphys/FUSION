# Proton structure functions in the dipole picture at next-to-leading order

> We predict heavy quark production cross sections in Deep Inelastic Scattering at high energy by applying the CGC effective theory. We demonstrate that when the calculation is performed consistently at next-to-leading order accuracy with massive quarks it becomes possible, for the first time in the dipole picture with perturbatively calculated center-of-mass energy evolution, to simultaneously describe both light and heavy quark production data at small $x$. We furthermore show how the heavy quark cross section data provides additional strong constraints on the extracted non-perturbative initial condition for the small-$x$ evolution equations.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that when both the impact factor and the small-x evolution equation are computed consistently at next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy with massive quarks, it becomes possible for the first time in the dipole picture with perturbatively calculated center-of-mass energy evolution to simultaneously describe both light and heavy quark production data at small x, but only 3 out of 12 previously published fits to light quark data survive the additional constraint from charm data.

## Method

The authors compute total virtual photon-proton cross sections at NLO by combining the qqbar contribution with loop corrections and the tree-level qqbar-g contribution, using perturbative impact factors from Beuf (2021) and dipole amplitudes that satisfy approximate NLO Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution equations (KCBK, ResumBK, TBK) which include resummation of transverse logarithms. They take 12 existing fits to HERA light quark data from Beuf (2020) that differ by resummation scheme, running coupling scheme, and initial rapidity, then calculate charm quark production at small x < 0.01 and test compatibility with HERA charm data by varying the charm quark mass between 1.1 GeV and 1.6 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Only 3 out of 12 fits to light quark data are allowed by the charm data, using a threshold of chi_c^2/N < 2.5
- The charm quark mass is varied within the range 1.1 GeV < m_c < 1.6 GeV to determine optimal values for each fit
- The calculation is performed for small x < 0.01
- The reduced cross section is defined using the inelasticity y = Q^2/(sx) where sqrt(s) is the electron-proton center-of-mass energy

## Context

This work extends the dipole picture calculations of Hänninen, Mäntysaari, Paatelainen, and Penttala (2022) by demonstrating that NLO accuracy is essential for simultaneous description of inclusive and heavy quark data, resolving a long-standing discrepancy where leading-order calculations in the CGC framework (Albacete 2010, Lappi 2013) could not simultaneously describe total and charm production data from HERA (H1 2015, H1 2018). The results enable future global analyses with more flexible parametrizations for the initial condition of small-x evolution and provide a foundation for precise predictions of nuclear structure functions to be measured at the Electron-Ion Collider.
