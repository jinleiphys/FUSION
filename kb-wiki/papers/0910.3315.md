# Delta electromagnetic form factors and quark transverse charge densities from lattice QCD

> We discuss the techniques to extract the electromagnetic Delta form factors in Lattice QCD. We evaluate these form factors using dynamical fermions with smallest pion mass of about 350 MeV. We pay particular attention to the extraction of the electric quadrupole form factor that signals a deformation of the Delta. The magnetic moment of the $Δ$ is extrapolated using a chiral effective field theory. Using the form factors we evaluate the transverse density distributions in the infinite momentum frame showing deformation in the Delta.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that lattice QCD can extract the subdominant electric quadrupole form factor of the Delta baryon with sufficient precision to connect it to a well-defined transverse charge density, showing that the Delta+ quark charge density is elongated along the axis of the spin (prolate deformation), but only for pion masses down to about 350 MeV, where the Delta is still stable on the lattice.

## Method

The authors compute two-point and three-point correlation functions for the Delta+ using sequential inversions through the sink, with Gaussian smearing applied to both source and sink to suppress excited-state contamination. They extract the four independent multipole form factors (G_E0, G_E2, G_M1, G_M3) by solving an over-constrained linear system from carefully chosen combinations of Dirac and vector indices, using jackknife binning for statistical errors. The calculations are performed on three different lattice setups: quenched Wilson fermions, N_F=2 dynamical Wilson fermions, and a mixed-action approach with domain-wall valence quarks on a staggered N_F=2+1 sea.

## Key numbers

- Smallest pion mass used: about 350 MeV (0.353(2) GeV for the mixed-action calculation)
- Delta+ magnetic moment at the lightest quenched point (m_pi=0.411 GeV): 1.811(69) nuclear magnetons
- Delta+ magnetic moment at the lightest N_F=2 point (m_pi=0.384 GeV): 1.58(11) nuclear magnetons
- Delta+ magnetic moment from mixed action (m_pi=0.353 GeV): 1.91(16) nuclear magnetons
- Delta+ charge radius from quenched calculation at m_pi=0.411 GeV: 0.6516(87) fm
- Delta+ quadrupole moment Q^Delta_{3/2} from quenched calculation at m_pi=0.411 GeV: 0.83(21) (units of e/M_Delta^2)
- Lattice spacing for quenched Wilson: 0.092 fm; for N_F=2 Wilson: 0.077 fm; for mixed action: 0.124 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier quenched lattice QCD calculations of Delta form factors by Alexandrou et al. (2007) to include dynamical fermions with two and three flavors, and develops the light-front formalism connecting form factors to transverse charge densities for spin-3/2 particles. The magnetic moment results are compared to chiral effective field theory calculations by Pascalutsa (2004) and agree with background field calculations using dynamical improved Wilson fermions by Aubin et al. (2008). The techniques enable future calculations at lighter pion masses where the Delta becomes unstable, and provide a rigorous framework for discussing hadron shape through transverse charge densities rather than classical three-dimensional shapes.
