# Delta(1232) and the Polarizabilities of the Nucleon

> Previous calculations of the polarizabilities of the nucleon within the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory have included the contribution of the $Δ$(1232) only its effect on various contact terms or have been performed in chiral SU(3) where systematic errors are difficult to control. Herein we perfrom a corresponding calculation in chiral SU(2) wherein $Δ$(1232) is treated as an explicit degree of freedom and the expansion is taken to third order in soft momenta, the pion mass and the quantity $M_Δ-M_N$, collectively denoted by $ε$. We present the results of a systematic $O(ε^3)$ calculation of forward Compton scattering off the nucleon, extract the electric polarizability $\barα_E$, the magnetic polarizability $\barβ_M$ and the spin polarizability $γ$ and compare with available information from experiments and from previous calculations. Concluding with a critical discussion of our results, we point out the necessity of a future $O(ε^4)$ calculation.

## Key claim

The authors perform a systematic calculation of nucleon polarizabilities at order $\epsilon^3$ in chiral SU(2) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, treating the $\Delta(1232)$ as an explicit degree of freedom rather than integrating it out into contact terms; they find that the delta resonance contributes significantly to the magnetic and spin polarizabilities but caution that a complete understanding requires a future calculation at order $\epsilon^4$.

## Method

The authors use heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with a small-scale expansion parameter $\epsilon$ that includes soft momenta, the pion mass, and the mass splitting $\Delta = M_\Delta - M_N$. They compute forward Compton scattering off the nucleon to order $\epsilon^3$, including 18 one-loop diagrams and Born graphs with s- and u-channel delta resonance exchange, using the M1 $\gamma N\Delta$ transition vertex at order $\epsilon^2$ with coupling $b_1$ determined phenomenologically from Davidson, Mukhopadhyay and Wittman (1991).

## Key numbers

- Electric polarizability from leading-order chiral perturbation theory: $\bar{\alpha}_E^p = \bar{\alpha}_E^n = 12.2 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$ (from the ${\cal O}(p^3)$ result of Bernard, Kaiser, Kambor and Mei{\ss}ner (1992))
- Experimental proton polarizabilities: $\bar{\alpha}_E^p = (11.6 \pm 0.6 \pm 0.6) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$, $\bar{\beta}_M^p = (2.6 \mp 0.6 \mp 0.6) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$ (from Nathan (1996))
- Experimental neutron polarizabilities: $\bar{\alpha}_E^n = (12.6 \pm 1.5 \pm 2.0) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$, $\bar{\beta}_M^n = (3.2 \mp 1.5 \mp 2.0) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$ (from Schmiedmayer (1991))
- Baldin sum rule for proton: $\bar{\alpha}_E^p + \bar{\beta}_M^p = (14.2 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$
- Baldin sum rule for neutron: $\bar{\alpha}_E^n + \bar{\beta}_M^n = (15.8 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$
- Leading-order chiral perturbation theory spin polarizability: $\gamma_p = \gamma_n = +4.4 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^{-4}$ (from Bernard, Kaiser, Kambor and Mei{\ss}ner (1992))
- Multipole analysis spin polarizabilities: $\gamma_p = -1.3 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^{-4}$, $\gamma_n = -0.4 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^{-4}$ (from Arndt, Morrison and Strakovsky (1994))
- Relativistic one-loop chiral perturbation theory spin polarizabilities: $\gamma_p^{\text{1-loop}} = +2.2 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^4$, $\gamma_n^{\text{1-loop}} = +3.2 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^4$ (from Bernard, Kaiser, Kambor and Mei{\ss}ner (1992))
- Quark model diamagnetic recoil contribution: $\bar{\beta}_M^{\text{dia}} = -10.2 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$ (proton), $-8.5 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$ (neutron)
- Quark model paramagnetic delta contribution: $\bar{\beta}_M^\Delta = +12 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$ (both proton and neutron)
- Coupling constant: $g_{\pi N\Delta}^{HHK} \approx 1.5 \pm 0.2$ (from Davidson, Mukhopadhyay and Wittman (1991))
- Counterterm coupling: $b_1 \approx -(2.5 \pm 0.35)$ (from Davidson, Mukhopadhyay and Wittman (1991))

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculations of nucleon polarizabilities by Bernard, Kaiser, Kambor and Mei{\ss}ner (1992) at order $p^3$ and Bernard, Kaiser, Schmidt and Mei{\ss}ner (1993) at order $p^4$, which treated the delta resonance only through contact terms or resonance saturation. By including the $\Delta(1232)$ as an explicit degree of freedom, the authors aim to reduce the large uncertainties in the magnetic polarizability from the earlier ${\cal O}(p^4)$ calculation and to resolve the sign discrepancy between chiral perturbation theory and multipole analysis for the spin polarizability $\gamma$. The work enables future complete ${\cal O}(\epsilon^4)$ calculations that will include all pion, nucleon and delta contributions systematically.
