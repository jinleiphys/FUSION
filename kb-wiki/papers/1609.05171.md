# Cumulants and nonlinear response of high $p_T$ harmonic flow at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV

> Event-by-event fluctuations caused by quantum mechanical fluctuations in the wave function of colliding nuclei in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions were recently shown to be necessary for the simultaneous description of $R_{AA}$ as well as the elliptic and triangular flow harmonics at high $p_T$ in PbPb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. In fact, the presence of a finite triangular flow as well as cumulants of the flow harmonic distribution that differ from the mean are only possible when these event-by-event fluctuations are considered. In this paper we combine event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics and jet quenching to make predictions for high $p_T$ $R_{AA}$, $v_2\{2\}$, $v_3\{2\}$, and $v_2\{4\}$ in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV. With an order of magnitude larger statistics we find that high $p_T$ elliptic flow does not scale linearly with the soft elliptical flow, as originally thought, but has deviations from perfectly linear scaling. A new experimental observable, which involves the difference between the ratio of harmonic flow cumulants at high and low $p_T$, is proposed to investigate the fluctuations of high $p_T$ flow harmonics and measure this nonlinear response. By varying the path length dependence of the energy loss and the viscosity of the evolving medium we find that $R_{AA}(p_T)$ and $v_2\{2\}(p_T)$ strongly depend on the choice for the path length dependence of the energy loss, which can be constrained using the new LHC run 2 data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that high-\(p_T\) elliptic flow does not scale perfectly linearly with soft-sector elliptic flow on an event-by-event basis, as previously assumed, but instead exhibits deviations from linear response that produce distinct values for the ratio \(v_2\{4\}(p_T)/v_2\{2\}(p_T)\) in the hard sector compared to the soft sector. This nonlinear effect is a consequence of the tomographic nature of jet energy loss and can be tested with LHC PbPb run 2 data.

## Method

The authors combine event-by-event 2+1 viscous relativistic hydrodynamics (v-USPhydro code) with the BBMG jet energy loss model to compute the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and the high-\(p_T\) flow harmonic cumulants \(v_2\{2\}\), \(v_3\{2\}\), and \(v_2\{4\}\) for PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV. They use MCKLN initial conditions, a lattice-based equation of state (EOS S95n-v1), and an isothermal Cooper-Frye freeze-out at \(T_F = 120\) MeV, with shear viscosity to entropy density ratios \(\eta/s = 0.05\) and \(0.12\). The jet energy loss rate is modeled as \(dE/dL \sim L\) or \(dE/dL \sim L^2\), and the soft-hard cumulants are computed using multiplicity weighting and centrality rebinning procedures that mimic experimental analyses.

## Key numbers

* \(R_{AA}(p_T = 10\ \text{GeV}) \approx 0.17\) is used to fix the jet-medium coupling \(\kappa\) in the BBMG model.
* The soft-sector \(v_2\{2\}\) and \(v_3\{2\}\) are computed for \(0.2 \leq p_T \leq 3\) GeV and compared to ALICE data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV; \(\eta/s = 0.05\) gives reasonable agreement, while \(\eta/s = 0.12\) is incompatible with the data.
* The initial time for hydrodynamic simulations is \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm, and the freeze-out temperature is \(T_F = 120\) MeV.
* The change in eccentricity \(\varepsilon_2\) between \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV and \(5.02\) TeV is only \(\Delta \varepsilon_2 \sim \pm 1\%\), while a longer hydrodynamic expansion can generate up to a \(6\%\) increase in \(v_2\) for the most peripheral collisions.
* The analysis uses an order of magnitude more events than the previous study Noronha-Hostler (2016) to reveal the nonlinear scaling of high-\(p_T\) elliptic flow.

## Context

This work extends the first simultaneous description of high-\(p_T\) \(R_{AA}\), \(v_2\{2\}\), and \(v_3\{2\}\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV reported by Noronha-Hostler (2016) to the higher collision energy of \(5.02\) TeV, using larger statistics and a systematic variation of the path-length dependence of energy loss and viscosity. It contradicts the earlier assumption of perfect linear scaling between hard and soft elliptic flow, which was based on approximate linear response seen in scatter plots at lower energy. The predictions enable experimental tests with LHC run 2 data to discriminate between energy loss models (e.g., \(dE/dL \sim L\) vs. \(dE/dL \sim L^2\)) and to constrain the shear viscosity of the QGP at high temperatures.
