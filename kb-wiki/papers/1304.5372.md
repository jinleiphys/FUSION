# Formation of droplets with high baryon density at the QCD phase transition in expanding matter

> We consider the (3+1) dimensional expansion and cooling of the chirally-restored and deconfined matter at finite net-baryon densities as expected in heavy-ion collisions at moderate energies. In our approach, we consider chiral fields and the Polyakov loop as dynamical variables coupled to a medium represented by a quark-antiquark fluid. The interaction between the fields and the fluid leads to dissipation and noise, which in turn affect the field fluctuations. We demonstrate how inhomogeneities in the net-baryon density may form during an evolution through the spinodal region of the first-order phase transition. For comparison, the dynamics of transition through the crossover and critical end point is also considered.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that inhomogeneities in net-baryon density (droplets of high baryon density) can form during an expanding fireball's evolution through the spinodal region of a first-order QCD phase transition, even though the baryon density is not directly coupled to a stochastic source term; this effect is not observed for evolution through the crossover or critical end point.

## Method

The authors use a (3+1)-dimensional nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics model coupling the sigma field and Polyakov loop (order parameters) via Langevin equations to a quark-antiquark fluid described by ideal fluid dynamics. A spherical droplet of quark matter is initialized on a 128³ grid with Woods-Saxon profiles for temperature and chemical potential, and the system is evolved using the SHASTA algorithm for the fluid and a staggered leap-frog algorithm for the fields, with noise fields correlated over volumes of 1/m³ to avoid artificial correlations.

## Key numbers

- The critical end point (CEP) is located at (T_cp, μ_cp) = (152, 160) MeV.
- The sigma field damping coefficient η_σ is given by an expression that vanishes when m_σ(T) < 2m_q(T) = 2gσ_eq(T); for the range 2m_q > m_σ(T) > 2m_π, a constant value η = 2.2/fm is used.
- The Polyakov loop damping coefficient is set to η_ℓ = 5/fm.
- The numerical grid uses 128³ cells with cell spacing Δx = 0.2 fm; the fluid time step is Δt = 0.4·Δx = 0.08 fm, and the field time step is Δt = 0.1·Δx = 0.02 fm.
- The correlation length reaches values of 1.5–2.0 fm around the CEP and remains small at about 0.2–0.4 fm for a first-order transition.
- The initial droplet radius is R = 4.0 fm with surface thickness a = 0.5 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics (Nahrgang 2011, Herold 2013) by including finite baryochemical potential and explicit propagation of the Polyakov loop, enabling study of baryon density inhomogeneities. It builds on earlier work by Mishustin (1998) and Paech (2003) on chiral fluid dynamics with classical Euler-Lagrange propagation, and on studies by Scavenius (2000) and others showing that the PQM model predicts a first-order phase transition at large μ_B. The results suggest that azimuthal fluctuations of net-baryon number and enhanced higher harmonics of baryonic flow could serve as signatures of a first-order phase transition in heavy-ion collisions at FAIR and NICA energies.
