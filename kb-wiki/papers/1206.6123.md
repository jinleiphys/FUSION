# Color-neutral heavy particle production in nucleus-nucleus collisions in the quasi-classical approximation

> We use a diagrammatic approach to study color-neutral heavy particle production in nucleus-nucleus collisions in a quasi-classical approximation without small-$x$ evolution. In order to treat the two nuclei symmetrically, we use the Coulomb gauge which gives the appropriate light cone gauge for each nucleus. The resulting cross section is factorized into a product of two Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distributions of the two nuclei when the transverse momentum of the produced scalar particle is around the saturation momentum. We confirm our results in covariant gauge where the transverse momentum broadening of hard gluons can be described as a diffusion process. The transverse momentum factorization manifests itself in light cone gauge but not so clearly in covariant gauge.

## Key claim

The cross section for color-neutral heavy scalar particle production in nucleus-nucleus collisions factorizes into a product of two Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distributions of the two nuclei, but only when the transverse momentum of the produced scalar particle is around the saturation momentum; at higher transverse momenta the factorization takes a different form involving both the standard and linearly polarized gluon distributions.

## Method

The author uses a diagrammatic approach in Coulomb gauge, which is equivalent to light cone gauge \(A_{+}=0\) for a right-moving nucleus and \(A_{-}=0\) for a left-moving nucleus, treating the two nuclei symmetrically. Slavnov-Taylor-Ward identities are applied to resum soft gluon exchanges as gauge rotations, reducing the calculation to an effective diagram where hard gluons from each nucleus are identified with the non-Abelian Weizsäcker-Williams fields. The calculation is confirmed in covariant gauge, where transverse momentum broadening of hard gluons is described as a diffusion process from multiple scattering.

## Key numbers

- The cross section in the regime \(\ul{l}^2 \sim Q_s^2\) is given by \(\frac{d^3 \sigma}{d^2 \ul{l} dy} = \frac{g_\phi^2 \pi^3}{16} \int d^2 \ul{x} e^{i \ul{l} \cdot \ul{x}} \tilde{N}_1(\ul{x}) \tilde{N}_2(\ul{x})\), where \(\tilde{N}_i(\ul{x}) = \frac{N_c^2 - 1}{4 \pi^4 \alpha N_c \ul{x}^2} \int d^2 \ul{b}_i [1 - e^{-Q_{iS}^2 \ul{x}^2 / 4}]\).
- For \(\ul{l}^2 \gg Q_s^2\), the unintegrated WW gluon distribution becomes additive: \(N_i(\ul{k}) \approx A_i \frac{\alpha C_F}{\pi^2} \frac{N_c}{\ul{k}^2}\), where \(A_i\) are the atomic numbers of the nuclei.
- The saturation momentum is defined as \(Q_s^2 = \frac{8 \pi^2 \alpha N_c \sqrt{R^2 - \ul{b}^2} \rho x G(x, 1/\ul{x}^2)}{N_c^2 - 1}\) for a spherical nucleus of radius \(R\).

## Context

This work extends the study of color-neutral particle production (previously studied for pp and pA collisions by Lipatov (1997), Hautmann (2002), and others) to nucleus-nucleus collisions in the quasi-classical approximation without small-\(x\) evolution. It provides the first exact analytic formula for a physical process involving momenta on the order of \(Q_s\) in nucleus-nucleus collisions, and identifies a process that can directly probe the Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution in addition to the dijet production discussed by Dominguez et al. (2011). The method may be further applied to study more complicated processes such as gluon production.
