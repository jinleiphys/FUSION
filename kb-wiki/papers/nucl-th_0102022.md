# Radiative alpha-capture cross sections from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions and variational Monte Carlo wave functions

> We report the first calculations of cross sections for the radiative capture reactions t(alpha,gamma)^7Li and ^3He(alpha,gamma)^7Be below 2 MeV which use wave functions derived from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions by the variational Monte Carlo technique. After examining several small corrections to the dominant E1 operator, we find energy dependences for the low-energy S-factors which agree reasonably with experimental measurements. There is no contradiction with the previous theoretical understanding of these processes, but the zero-energy derivative of the t(alpha,gamma)^7Li S-factor is smaller than in most models. While this method can in principle predict cross section normalizations, the normalizations of our results are mostly too low.

## Key claim

This paper reports the first calculations of radiative alpha-capture cross sections for t(alpha,gamma)^7Li and ^3He(alpha,gamma)^7Be below 2 MeV using wave functions derived from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions via the variational Monte Carlo technique; the energy dependences of the low-energy S-factors agree reasonably with experiment, but the normalizations are mostly too low, and the zero-energy derivative of the t(alpha,gamma)^7Li S-factor is smaller than in most models.

## Method

The authors used variational Monte Carlo (VMC) wave functions for the bound states of ^3H, ^3He, ^4He, ^7Li, and ^7Be, based on the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential, with asymptotic cluster behavior enforced by matching Whittaker functions. Scattering states were generated from phenomenological cluster-cluster potentials taken from the literature (Dubovichenko 1995, Kim 1981, Buck 1985, Buck 1988) that reproduce alpha-trinucleon phase shifts, and the electromagnetic capture cross sections were computed via a multipole expansion of the current operator, including small corrections to the dominant E1 operator.

## Key numbers

- For the ^7Li ground state, the VMC asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) is 3.4 +/- 0.1 fm^{-1/2}, compared to a literature "world average" of 3.55 +/- 0.27 fm^{-1/2} from Brune et al. (1999).
- For the ^7Li first excited state, the VMC ANC is 2.65 +/- 0.10 fm^{-1/2}; for ^7Be ground state, 3.55 +/- 0.15 fm^{-1/2}; for ^7Be first excited state, 2.9 +/- 0.1 fm^{-1/2}.
- The VMC binding energy of ^7Li is -31.26(8) MeV (experiment: -39.24 MeV); for ^7Be, -29.55(8) MeV (experiment: -37.60 MeV).
- At 20 keV, more than 10% of the cross section for ^3He(alpha,gamma)^7Be comes from cluster separations beyond 20 fm.

## Context

This work extends the application of realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials and VMC wave functions (previously used for d(alpha,gamma)^6Li by Nollett et al.) to the astrophysically important reactions t(alpha,gamma)^7Li and ^3He(alpha,gamma)^7Be, which are major sources of uncertainty in primordial ^7Li abundance from big bang nucleosynthesis and in solar neutrino predictions. The results are compared with potential models (Dubovichenko 1995, Kim 1981, Buck 1985, Buck 1988, Mohr 1993) and resonating-group method calculations (Kajino 1986, Altmeyer 1988, Csoto 2000); the VMC approach provides a first-principles alternative that can in principle predict normalizations, though the present normalizations are mostly too low, enabling future tests with improved three-nucleon potentials and more sophisticated wave functions.
