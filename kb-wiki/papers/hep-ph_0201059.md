# Anisotropy Parameters for the Effective Description of Crystalline Color Superconductors

> In the high density low temperature limit, Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) exhibits a transition to a phase characterized by color superconductivity and by energy gaps in the fermion spectra. Under specific circumstances the gap parameter has a crystalline pattern, breaking translational and rotational invariance. The corresponding phase is the the crystalline color superconductive phase (or {\it LOFF} phase). In the effective theory the fermions couple to the phonon arising from the breaking of rotation and translation invariance. We compute the parameters of the low energy effective lagrangian describing the motion of the free phonon in the high density medium and derive the phonon dispersion law.

## Key claim

The authors compute the low-energy effective Lagrangian parameters for the phonon (the Nambu-Goldstone boson arising from spontaneous breaking of translational and rotational invariance) in the crystalline color superconducting (LOFF) phase of QCD, under the simplifying approximation that both the chemical potential and the Cooper pair momentum are taken to infinity, which yields a phonon dispersion law that is anisotropic.

## Method

The authors employ a velocity-dependent effective Lagrangian approach for fermions near the Fermi surface, previously developed for the 2SC and CFL phases. They introduce a fictitious gluon propagator corresponding to a local four-fermion coupling to derive gap equations via a truncated Schwinger-Dyson equation. The phonon field is introduced as a fluctuation of the lattice planes defined by the crystalline condensate, and its coupling to fermion quasiparticles is derived. The parameters of the effective phonon Lagrangian are then computed at first order in the derivative expansion.

## Key numbers

- The scalar gap in the LOFF phase is given by Δ₀ = δ / sinh(3Λ²π²/(μ²k_R)), where δ is the cutoff, Λ is the coupling scale, μ is the average chemical potential, and k_R is a factor from the smearing procedure.
- For the 2SC model with μ = 400 MeV and cutoff K = 800 MeV (Λ = 181 MeV), the gap parameter Δ = 39 MeV; for K = 700 MeV (Λ = 154 MeV), Δ = 68 MeV.
- The lower boundary of the LOFF window is δμ₁ ≈ 0.71 Δ_{2SC}, and the upper boundary is δμ₂ ≈ 0.90 Δ_{2SC} in the large-R approximation, compared to δμ₂ ≈ 0.754 Δ_{2SC} from earlier work by LOFF.
- For R ≈ 1 and δμ = δμ₁, the gap Δ₀ ≈ 5.5 MeV; for R ≈ 1.5, Δ₀ ≈ 0.25 MeV and δμ₂ ≈ 0.83 Δ_{2SC}.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the crystalline color superconducting (LOFF) phase previously studied by LOFF (Alford, Bowers, Rajagopal) and by the same authors in Casalbuoni et al. (2001, LOFF5), where the Goldstone structure was first examined. The present paper computes the phonon dispersion law parameters that were not derived in that earlier work. The results enable further study of transport properties and astrophysical implications of the LOFF phase in dense quark matter, such as in neutron star interiors.
