# Cooperation of anti-aligning and aligning shell-model forces for N=Z

> For two neutrons and two protons or two neutron holes and two proton holes in a single j-shell, the state |phi> with isospin and seniority zero and the lowest angular momentum zero state |chi> produced by an attractive interaction of quasinucleon pairs with angular momentum 2j have a large overlap for all relevant j and large contents of quasinucleon pairs with angular momenta 2j and 0, respectively. In the 1f7/2 and 1g9/2 shells, the large negative matrix elements of the effective interaction in these two channels relative to most of the rest therefore_cooperate_ to produce a ground state which is essentially a linear combination of |phi> and |chi> with comparable coefficients. Interaction matrix elements in other channels influence significantly the_ratio_ of these coefficients. The state |phi> makes up about 80 % of the calculated ground states. The overlaps of the latter with |chi> are_less_ in the 1g9/2 shell than in the 1f7/2 shell.

## Key claim

For two neutrons and two protons (or two neutron holes and two proton holes) in a single j-shell, the large negative matrix elements of the effective interaction in the J=0 and J=2j channels cooperate to produce a ground state that is essentially a linear combination of the seniority-zero state |φ⟩ and the lowest angular-momentum-zero state |χ⟩ from an attractive V₂ⱼ interaction, with comparable coefficients; however, the overlap of the ground state with |χ⟩ is consistently less in the 1g₉/₂ shell than in the 1f₇/₂ shell, meaning large |χ⟩ character does not arise specifically from approaching ¹⁰⁰Sn.

## Method

The author constructed the isospin T=0 and seniority s=0 state |φ⟩ and the antisymmetrized state |χ⟩ = N P |Jd=2j⟩ (the lowest I=0 state of an attractive V₂ⱼ interaction) for single-j-shell configurations of two neutrons and two protons or two neutron holes and two proton holes. For the 1f₇/₂ and 1g₉/₂ shells, the ground state |ψ⟩ was calculated using multiple effective interactions from the literature (Schiffer-True empirical and fitted, Zamick-Robinson Models I and II, Qi-Liotta-Wyss, Zamick-Escuderos INTc and INTd, Coraggio-Covello-Gargano-Itaco Vlow-k, Zerguine-Van Isacker SLGT0, GF, and Nb90ZI, and others), and the squared overlaps |⟨φ|ψ⟩|², |⟨χ|ψ⟩|², and |⟨ξ|ψ⟩|² (where |ξ⟩ is orthogonal to both) were tabulated.

## Key numbers

- For j=9/2, the J=0 component makes up only 73% of the s=T=0 state |φ⟩.
- For j=9/2, the overlap |⟨φ|χ⟩|² is 52%.
- In the 1f₇/₂ shell, |⟨χ|ψ⟩|² ranges from 95% to 98% across the interactions studied (SchTr empirical: 0.97; SchTr fit: 0.95; ZR I: 0.95; ZR II: 0.98).
- In the 1g₉/₂ shell, |⟨χ|ψ⟩|² ranges from 83% to 93% (SchTr empirical: 0.93; SchTr fit: 0.93; QLW: 0.92; ZE I: 0.84; ZE II: 0.92; ZE III: 0.83; ZE IV: 0.89; CCGI: 0.89; SLGT0: 0.91; GF: 0.93; Nb90ZI: 0.93).
- |⟨φ|ψ⟩|² is about 80% in both shells (0.76–0.89 in 1g₉/₂; 0.76–0.83 in 1f₇/₂).
- For j=9/2, n(0,|φ⟩) makes up only about one third of the total weight distribution, and n(2j,|φ⟩) makes up about one sixth.
- For j=9/2, n(2j,|χ⟩) contributes almost exactly one third of the total weight distribution.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Qi et al. (2011), who found that in N=Z nuclei approaching ¹⁰⁰Sn, the Jd=2j configuration contributes 92–95% of the ground state and inferred a spin-aligned isoscalar pair mode replacing isovector pairing. Neergård shows that the state |φ⟩ (seniority zero, isospin zero) makes up about 80% of the calculated ground states, so conventional isovector pairing is far from absent. The analysis uses the symplectic group Sp(2j+1) and Flowers's method to classify states by seniority, and builds on prior work by Edmonds and Flowers (1952), Moya de Guerra et al. (1987), and Zamick and Escuderos (2012). The results enable a more nuanced understanding of the competition between J=0 and J=2j pairing modes in N=Z nuclei.
