# Electrical conductivity of QGP with quasiparticle quarks and Gribov gluon

> We investigate the electrical conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) using a non-perturbative resummation scheme incorporating the Gribov-modified gluon propagator. The electrical conductivity is evaluated by solving the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation within the relaxation-time approximation, where the relaxation times are obtained from microscopic two-body scattering amplitudes. A quasiparticle description is employed for quarks, providing a unified framework for studying transport properties across both weakly and strongly coupled regimes. Above the deconfinement transition temperature, we estimate the electrical conductivity of the QGP and compare our results with available lattice QCD data and various phenomenological models, finding good agreement with the lattice results.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the electrical conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), computed using a Gribov-modified gluon propagator combined with a quasiparticle description for quarks, shows good agreement with lattice QCD results, but this agreement is conditional on the specific choice of the Gribov parameter fixed from pure gauge lattice thermodynamics and the running coupling fixed from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD entropy density.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation within the relaxation-time approximation, where relaxation times are obtained from microscopic two-body scattering amplitudes computed at tree level for quark-quark, quark-antiquark, and quark-gluon scattering processes. The gluon propagator is modified using the Gribov-Zwanziger approach with a temperature-dependent Gribov parameter fixed by matching the entropy density of pure gauge lattice results, while quarks are treated as quasiparticles with temperature-dependent effective masses obtained from hard-thermal loop approximation. The running coupling is fixed by equating the total entropy density of the QGP medium (Gribov gluons plus quasiparticle quarks) with the entropy density from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature \(T_c\) is taken as 0.155 GeV.
- The bare masses used for light quarks (\(u,d\)) are 5 MeV and for strange quarks (\(s\)) are 95 MeV.
- The Gribov parameter \(\gamma_G/T\) and running coupling \(g(T)\) are plotted as functions of \(T/T_c\) in the interval \(1.05 \le T/T_c \le 2.5\), both decreasing monotonically with increasing temperature above \(T_c\).
- Scattering cross-sections are evaluated at two representative temperatures: \(T/T_c = 1.2\) and \(T/T_c = 2.2\), with the overall magnitude of cross-sections decreasing as temperature increases.
- For quark-quark scattering, the cross-section increases sharply at smaller \(\sqrt{s}\) and tends to saturate for \(\sqrt{s} \gtrsim 2.5\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Zwanziger approach, previously applied to shear and bulk viscosity by Madni et al. (2024) and to thermodynamic quantities by Jaiswal et al. (2020), to the calculation of electrical conductivity of the QGP. The results are compared with lattice QCD data and various phenomenological models, with the Gribov framework providing a non-perturbative resummation scheme that bridges weakly and strongly coupled regimes. The study enables further investigation of electromagnetic field evolution in heavy-ion collisions and the chiral magnetic effect, building on prior work by Fukushima et al. (2008) and Kharzeev et al. (2007).
