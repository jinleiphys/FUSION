# The Effects of Superhigh Magnetic Fields on Equations of States of Neutron Stars

> By introducing Dirac ?-function in superhigh magnetic field, we deduce a general formula for pressure of degenerate and relativistic electrons, Pe, which is suitable for superhigh magnetic fields, discuss the quantization of Landau levels of electrons, and consider the quantum electrodynamic(QED) effects on the equations of states (EOSs) for different matter systems. The main conclusions are as follows: the stronger the magnetic field strength, the higher the electron pressure becomes; compared with a common radio pulsar, a magnetar could be a more compact oblate spheroid-like deformed neutron star due to the anisotropic total pressure; and an increase in the maximum mass of a magnetar is expected because of the positive contribution of the magnetic field energy to the EOS of the star. Since this is an original work in which some uncertainties could exist, to further modify and perfect our theory model should be considered in our future studies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in superhigh magnetic fields (B >> B_cr), the electron pressure increases with field strength, contrary to the popular view that stronger fields lower the electron pressure; this result depends on introducing a Dirac delta function to handle the quantization of Landau levels and on treating electrons as an ideal Fermi gas with equivalent pressures in all directions despite the presence of Landau levels.

## Method

The authors derive a general formula for the pressure of degenerate, relativistic electrons in a superhigh magnetic field by starting from the relativistic Dirac equation in a uniform external magnetic field along the z-axis, using the Landau level quantization and the electron Fermi energy expression. They then apply this electron pressure formula to two matter models: the BPS model for the outer crust (Baym, Pethick & Sutherland 1971) and an ideal npe gas under beta-equilibrium in the density range 0.5–2.5 times nuclear saturation density, incorporating quantum electrodynamic (QED) effects and the anisotropy of total pressure from the magnetic field energy-momentum tensor.

## Key numbers

- Electron Fermi energy in superhigh MFs: \(E_{\rm F}(e) \simeq 43.44 \left(\frac{B}{B_{\rm cr}}\right)^{1/4} \left(\frac{\rho}{\rho_0} \frac{Y_e}{0.0535}\right)^{1/4}\) MeV, where \(\rho_0 = 2.8 \times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) is the standard nuclear density.
- Electron pressure in the super-relativistic limit: \(P_e \simeq 6.266 \times 10^{30} \left(\frac{\rho}{\rho_0} \frac{B}{B_{\rm cr}} \frac{Y_e}{0.0535}\right)\) dyne cm\(^{-2}\).
- For an ideal npe gas in a superhigh MF, the proton pressure: \(P_p = 1.169 \times 10^{30} (\rho/\rho_0)^{10/3}\) dyne cm\(^{-2}\); electron pressure: \(P_e = 1.825 \times 10^{31} (\rho/\rho_0)^{8/3}\) dyne cm\(^{-2}\); neutron pressure: \(P_n = 6.807 \times 10^{33} (\rho/\rho_0)^{5/3}\) dyne cm\(^{-2}\); electron fraction: \(Y_e = 0.005647 (\rho/\rho_0)\).
- For \(B^* = 100\), the matter pressure \(P_m \sim 10^{33}\)–\(10^{34}\) dyne cm\(^{-2}\) and the magnetic pressure \(B^2/(8\pi) \sim 10^{29}\)–\(10^{30}\) dyne cm\(^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of magnetic field effects on neutron star equations of state, particularly the prediction by Thompson and Duncan (1996) of superhigh magnetic fields in magnetars (surface dipolar fields \(\sim 10^{14}\)–\(10^{15}\) G) and the work of Franzon et al. (2015) on strong magnetic fields in hybrid stars. The paper also builds on the BPS model of Baym, Pethick and Sutherland (1971) for the outer crust and the ST83 approximation of Shapiro and Teukolsky (1983) for the npe gas. The results enable future calculations of magnetar mass-radius relations and deformations, though the authors note that the stability coefficients of Landau levels (introduced as \(g_n = g_0 n^\alpha\) with \(\alpha < 0\)) remain to be determined.
