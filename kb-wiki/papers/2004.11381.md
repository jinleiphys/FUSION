# Rethinking Jets with Energy Correlators: Tracks, Resummation and Analytic Continuation

> We introduce an infinite set of jet substructure observables, derived as projections of $N$-point energy correlators, that are both convenient for experimental studies and maintain remarkable analytic properties derived from their representations in terms of a finite number of light ray operators. We show that these observables can be computed using tracking or charge information with a simple reweighting by integer moments of non-perturbative track or fragmentation functions. Our results for the projected $N$-point correlators are analytic functions of $N$, allowing us to derive resummed results to next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy for all $N$. We analytically continue our results to non-integer values of $N$ and define a corresponding analytic continuation of the observable, which we term a $ν$-correlator, that can be measured on jets of hadrons at the LHC. This enables observables that probe the leading twist collinear dynamics of jets to be placed into a single analytic family, which we hope will lead to new insights into jet substructure.

## Key claim

The paper introduces projected N-point energy correlators as jet substructure observables that are both experimentally convenient and maintain simple analytic properties, but the key hedge is that these observables are weighted cross sections rather than standard delta-function observables, meaning they do not take a single value on a given jet and are therefore not directly useful for tagging applications.

## Method

The authors define projected energy correlators as angular projections of N-point energy flow operator correlation functions, producing observables that depend on a single scaling variable. They compute these observables using tracking information by reweighting with integer moments of non-perturbative track functions, and derive resummed results to next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy for all N. The results are analytically continued to non-integer values of N, defining a new class of observables called nu-correlators that can be measured on jets of hadrons at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- The projected N-point correlators are computed to next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy for all integer N
- The track function formalism requires only integer moments T_i^(n) = integral_0^1 dx x^n T_i(x,mu) with n less than or equal to N
- For the two-point energy correlator (EEC) at LO in e+e- collisions, the result is sigma_0 * (alpha_s/(2pi)) * C_F * ((T_q^(1))^2 * I + ...) where T_q^(1) is the first moment of the quark track function
- The analytic continuation defines nu-correlators for non-integer values of nu, with scale evolution determined by anomalous dimensions of non-integer twist-2 spin-N operators

## Context

This work bridges the divide between formal studies of energy correlators in conformal field theory and experimental jet substructure at the LHC. It extends the track function formalism of Chang (2013) and the energy flow operator framework of Sveshnikov (1995) and Hofman (2008), while contrasting with standard delta-function observables like thrust (Farhi 1977) and angularities (Berger 2003) that require infinite sums over energy correlator moments. The results enable precision calculations of track-based observables at higher perturbative orders and provide a mathematically precise family of observables probing twist-2 collinear dynamics of jets.
