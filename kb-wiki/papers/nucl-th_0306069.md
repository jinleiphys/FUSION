# Renormalization of Singlet NN-Scattering with One Pion Exchange and Boundary Conditions

> We present a simple and physically compelling boundary condition regularization scheme in the framework of effective field theory as applied to nucleon-nucleon interaction. It is free of off-shell ambiguities and ultraviolet divergences and provides finite results at any step of the calculation. Low energy constants and their non-perturbative evolution can directly be obtained from experimental threshold parameters in a completely unique and model independent way when the long range explicit pion effects are removed. This allows to compute scattering phase shifts which are, by construction consistent with effective range expansion to a given order in the CM momentum and are free from finite cut-off artifacts. We illustrate how the method works in the $^1S_0$ channel for the One Pion Exchange potential.

## Key claim

The paper presents a boundary condition regularization scheme for nucleon-nucleon effective field theory that is free of off-shell ambiguities and ultraviolet divergences, but the method's applicability is explicitly limited to the \(^1S_0\) channel with One Pion Exchange only, and the authors caution that results should not be considered realistic below about 1.5 fm or above center-of-mass momenta of about 100-150 MeV where two-pion exchange contributions become important.

## Method

The authors formulate the reduced Schrodinger equation for NN scattering in the \(^1S_0\) channel with the One Pion Exchange potential, parameterizing unknown short-distance physics via an energy-dependent boundary condition at a matching radius \(R\). They derive a variable phase equation describing the evolution of the phase shift as the boundary radius is moved, and corresponding renormalization group equations for the running low-energy parameters (scattering length \(\alpha_0\), effective range \(r_0\), and shape parameter \(v_2\)) as functions of \(R\). Starting from experimental threshold parameters at infinity, they evolve these equations downward to extract short-distance parameters at the origin, then integrate upward to predict phase shifts without additional fitting.

## Key numbers

- Experimental \(^1S_0\) scattering length: \(\alpha_0 = -23.73\) fm (also given as \(-23.7\) fm and \(-23.739\) fm in different places)
- Experimental effective range: \(r_0 = 2.68\) fm
- Experimental shape parameter: \(v_2 = -0.48\) fm\(^3\)
- Extrapolated short-distance parameters at the origin: \(\alpha_{S,0} = 0\), \(r_{S,0} = 4.08\) fm, \(v_{S,2} = 0.43\) fm\(^3\)
- At short-distance radius \(R_S = 0.1\) fm: \(\alpha_{S,0} = -0.9867\) fm, \(r_{0,S} = 3.819\) fm, \(v_{2,S} = 0.397\) fm\(^3\)
- OPE potential parameters: nucleon mass \(M_N = 938.92\) MeV, pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, pion mass \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, axial coupling \(g_A = 1.25\)
- The OPE plus contact and derivative interactions to NNLO describes the \(^1S_0\) phase shift up to CM momentum of about 140 MeV

## Context

This work extends the boundary condition approach to NN scattering (Lomon and Feshbach, 1967) within the modern effective field theory framework, and provides an alternative to the subtraction method of Frederico, Timoteo, and Tomio (1999) which could not easily include derivative interactions. The authors compare their extracted short-distance parameters with those from the two-Yukawa model of Steele, Beane, and van Kolck (1998) (\(\alpha_{S,0} = -1.72\) fm, \(r_{S,0} = 1.60\) fm, \(v_{S,2} = -0.024\) fm\(^3\)) and the three-Yukawa model of Beane, Bedaque, and van Kolck (2001) (\(\alpha_{S,0} = -3.38\) fm, \(r_{S,0} = 2.60\) fm, \(v_{S,2} = 0.313\) fm\(^3\)), finding qualitative agreement at the relevant short-distance scales. The method enables unique, model-independent extraction of short-distance low-energy parameters and prediction of phase shifts without fitting, and the authors indicate that extensions to include two-pion exchange and all partial waves are planned.
