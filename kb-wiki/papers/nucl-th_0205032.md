# Baryon stopping and strange baryon/antibaryon production at SPS energies

> The amount of proton stopping in central Pb+Pb collisions from 20-160 AGeV as well as hyperon and antihyperon rapidity distributions are calculated within the UrQMD model in comparison to experimental data at 40, 80 and 160 AGeV taken recently from the NA49 collaboration. Furthermore, the amount of baryon stopping at 160 AGeV for Pb+Pb collisions is studied as a function of centrality in comparison to the NA49 data. We find that the strange baryon yield is reasonably described for central collisions, however, the rapidity distributions are somewhat more narrow than the data. Moreover, the experimental antihyperon rapidity distributions at 40, 80 and 160 AGeV are underestimated by up to factors of 3 - depending on the annihilation cross section employed - which might be addressed to missing multi-meson fusion channels in the UrQMD model.

# Baryon stopping and strange baryon/antibaryon production at SPS energies

## Key claim

The UrQMD model reasonably describes strange baryon yields for central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, but the calculated rapidity distributions are somewhat more narrow than the NA49 data, and the experimental antihyperon rapidity distributions at 40, 80 and 160 AGeV are underestimated by up to factors of 3 depending on the annihilation cross section employed, which might be addressed to missing multi-meson fusion channels in the UrQMD model.

## Method

The UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) transport model (version 1.3) was used to calculate net proton rapidity distributions, hyperon (Λ + Σ⁰) rapidity distributions, and antihyperon (anti-Λ + anti-Σ⁰) rapidity distributions for central and peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at beam energies of 20, 40, 80, and 160 AGeV. The calculations were compared to experimental data from the NA49 collaboration, with two different parametrizations of the anti-Λ p annihilation cross section tested to study their effect on antihyperon yields.

## Key numbers

- Net proton rapidity distributions at 160 AGeV show the UrQMD model overestimates stopping for the most central rapidity bin; the data show a slight dip at midrapidity and a two-peak structure, indicating full stopping is not achieved at 160 AGeV
- Hyperon (Λ + Σ⁰) yield at midrapidity at 40 and 80 AGeV is practically the same in UrQMD calculations, while NA49 data show a decreasing hyperon yield at midrapidity with higher bombarding energy
- Antihyperon rapidity distributions at 40 and 80 AGeV are underestimated by about a factor of 2 (parametrization-1) or 3 (parametrization-2) compared to NA49 data
- Antihyperon rapidity distributions at 160 AGeV are underestimated by about factors of 1.5-2
- For parametrization-1, about half of the antihyperons are annihilated; for parametrization-2, about 2/3 of the antihyperons disappear
- The (rapidity integrated) anti-Λ/anti-p ratio from UrQMD is approximately 0.9 for parametrization-1 and 0.6 for parametrization-2 across all bombarding energies from 20 to 160 AGeV
- The anti-Λ p annihilation cross section at low momentum is approximately 55 mb for parametrization-1 (default in UrQMD)

## Context

This work extends previous UrQMD studies of pion, kaon, and antikaon production at SPS energies (Weber et al., prior work) and addresses the question of strange baryon and antibaryon production to obtain complete information on s and anti-s quark abundances. The results are compared to NA49 experimental data at 40, 80, and 160 AGeV (NA49 collaboration, recent measurements) and to earlier transport model studies using RQMD, UrQMD, and HSD approaches. The missing antihyperon yield is attributed to multi-meson fusion channels involving K, anti-K, K⁺, and anti-K* mesons (Rapp et al., Carsten et al., Wolfgang et al.) that are not accounted for in the reported calculations, or alternatively to disoriented chiral condensates (Kapusta and Wong, 2002). The work provides predictions for 20 AGeV where experimental measurements are expected (SPS20).
