# Radiative and chiral corrections to elastic lepton-proton scattering in chiral perturbation theory

> A unified treatment of both chiral and radiative corrections to the low-energy elastic lepton-proton scattering processes is presented in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbations Theory. The proton hadronic chiral corrections include the next-to-next-to leading order corrections whereas the radiative corrections include the next-to-leading order terms in our novel power counting scheme. We find that the net fractional well-defined chiral corrections with respect to the leading order Born cross section can be as large as $10\%$ ($20\%$) for electron (muon) scattering process for MUon proton Scattering Experiment (MUSE) kinematics. We show {\it via} our model-independent treatment of the low-energy lepton-proton kinematics, that the largest theoretical uncertainty is due to the recent different published values of the proton's rms radius while, e.g., the next higher order hadronic chiral terms are expected to give rather nominal errors. For the radiative corrections we demonstrate a systematic order by order cancellation of all infrared singularities and present our finite ultraviolet regularization results. We find that the radiative corrections for muon-proton scattering is of the order of $2\%$, whereas for electron scattering the radiative corrections could be as large as $25\%$. We attribute such a contrasting result partially to the fact that in muon scattering the leading radiative order correction goes through zero in some intermediate low-momentum transfer region, leaving the sub-leading radiative chiral order effects to play a dominant role in this particular kinematic region. For the low-energy MUSE experiment, the often neglected lepton mass as well as the Pauli form factor contributions of the relativistic leptons are incorporated in all our computations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a unified treatment of chiral and radiative corrections to elastic lepton-proton scattering in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory yields fractional chiral corrections as large as 10% for electron and 20% for muon scattering relative to the leading-order Born cross section, but these values are conditional on the specific MUSE kinematics and depend on the chosen proton rms radius, which constitutes the largest theoretical uncertainty.

## Method

The authors employ Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBχPT) with an SU(2) isospin scheme to compute chiral corrections up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLOχ) and radiative corrections up to next-to-leading order (NLOα) in a unified power counting scheme. The radiative corrections include one-loop virtual diagrams (self-energies, vertex corrections, vacuum polarization, two-photon exchange) and soft photon bremsstrahlung, with infrared divergences cancelled order by order using dimensional regularization. The chiral corrections incorporate the proton's Dirac and Pauli form factors expressed in terms of mean square radii at NNLOχ, while the radiative corrections are evaluated for MUSE kinematics in the laboratory frame with a detector acceptance threshold set to approximately 1% of the incoming lepton energy.

## Key numbers

- Net fractional chiral corrections with respect to the leading-order Born cross section can be as large as 10% for electron-proton scattering and 20% for muon-proton scattering for MUSE kinematics.
- Radiative corrections for muon-proton scattering are of the order of 2%, whereas for electron scattering the radiative corrections could be as large as 25%.
- The chiral expansion parameter is Q/Λχ ~ Q/M ~ 0.2, where Q ≈ 0.2 GeV/c for MUSE kinematics and Λχ ≈ 1 GeV.
- The NNLOχ corrections are of order Q²/M² ≈ 0.04, and N³LOχ contributions of order Q³/M³ ≈ 0.008 are not included and constitute an important theoretical uncertainty.
- The NLOα radiative corrections are of order αQ/M ≈ 0.0015, where α ≈ 1/137 ≈ 0.007.
- The detector acceptance parameter Δγ* is chosen as approximately 1% of the incoming lepton energy for numerical evaluations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of two-photon-exchange contributions at NLOα accuracy in HBχPT (Talukdar 2019) and provides a systematic alternative to conventional approaches that model hadron structure effects using phenomenological proton form factors. The unified EFT framework enables simultaneous evaluation of chiral and radiative corrections for the MUSE experiment at PSI, which aims to measure the proton's rms radius at better than 1% precision, and the results demonstrate that the largest theoretical uncertainty comes from different published values of the proton's rms radius rather than from higher-order chiral terms.
