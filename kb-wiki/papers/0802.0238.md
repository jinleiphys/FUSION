# $K^-pp$ system with chiral SU(3) effective interaction

> The $K^-pp$ system is investigated using a variational approach with realistic two-body interactions: the Argonne v18 $NN$ potential and an energy dependent $\bar{K}N$ effective interaction derived from chiral SU(3) coupled-channel dynamics. Uncertainties in subthreshold extrapolations of the $\bar{K}N$ interaction are considered. A weakly bound $K^-pp$ state is found, with a binding energy $B = (19\pm 3)$ MeV substantially smaller than suggested in previous calculations. The decay width $Γ(K^-pp\to πΣN)$ is estimated to range between about 40 and 70 MeV.

# K⁻pp system with chiral SU(3) effective interaction

## Key claim
The K⁻pp system is found to be weakly bound with a binding energy of (19 ± 3) MeV, substantially smaller than the 50–80 MeV suggested by previous variational and Faddeev calculations, because the chiral SU(3) dynamics places the Λ(1405) quasibound state at a binding energy of about 12 MeV rather than the 27 MeV used in simple potential models.

## Method
A variational calculation was performed using a non-relativistic Hamiltonian with the Argonne v18 NN potential and an energy-dependent, complex s-wave K̅N effective interaction derived from chiral SU(3) coupled-channel dynamics. The K⁻pp wave function was constructed with Gaussian trial functions, NN correlation functions to handle short-distance repulsion, and K̅N correlation functions, with self-consistent iteration to handle the energy dependence of the K̅N potential. The decay width was estimated perturbatively from the expectation value of the imaginary part of the K̅N interaction.

## Key numbers
- K⁻pp binding energy: B = (19 ± 3) MeV, combining Type I (15.6–17.7 MeV) and Type II (19.6–21.6 MeV) energy-splitting scenarios across four chiral SU(3) model variants (ORB, HNJH, BNW, BMN)
- K⁻pp → πΣN decay width: Γ = 40–70 MeV, with Type I giving 39–61 MeV and Type II giving 54–72 MeV
- Λ(1405) quasibound state binding energy in the I=0 K̅N channel: B = 9.97–13.31 MeV across the four chiral models, with mean K̅N distance √⟨r²⟩ = 1.72–1.99 fm
- Average NN distance in K⁻pp: R_NN ≈ 2.2 fm; average K̅N distance: R_K̅N ≈ 1.9 fm
- Range parameters a_s of the Gaussian K̅N potentials: 0.41–0.52 fm across the four models

## Context
This work extends the chiral SU(3) coupled-channel approach of Hyodo and Weise (2007) to the three-body K⁻pp system, contradicting earlier variational calculations by Yamazaki and Akaishi (2002, 2007) and Faddeev calculations by Shevchenko et al. (2007) and Ikeda and Sato (2007) that predicted binding energies of 50–80 MeV. The key difference is that the chiral dynamics places the Λ(1405) quasibound state at a binding energy of about 12 MeV rather than 27 MeV, leading to weaker K̅N attraction. The results enable future comparisons with precision measurements from kaonic hydrogen and deuterium experiments, and motivate further studies of non-mesonic decay channels and p-wave K̅N interactions.
