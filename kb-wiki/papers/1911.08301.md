# Microscopic optical potential from chiral effective field theory

> We formulate a microscopic optical potential from chiral two- and three-body forces. The real and imaginary central terms of the optical potential are obtained from the nucleon self-energy in infinite matter, while the real spin-orbit term is extracted from a nuclear energy density functional constructed from the density matrix expansion using the same chiral potential. The density-dependent optical potential is then folded with the nuclear density distributions for selected Calcium isotopes resulting in energy-dependent nucleon-nucleus optical potentials from which we study proton-nucleus elastic scattering cross sections calculated using the TALYS reaction code. We compare the results of the microscopic calculations to phenomenological models and experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors construct a fully microscopic optical potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering using chiral two- and three-body forces, but the resulting imaginary central term is too strongly absorptive, requiring replacement with the phenomenological Koning-Delaroche imaginary potential to achieve good agreement with experimental elastic scattering data.

## Method

The real and imaginary central terms of the optical potential are obtained from the nucleon self-energy in infinite homogeneous nuclear matter computed to second order in many-body perturbation theory using a chiral nuclear interaction with momentum-space cutoff Λ = 450 MeV. The real spin-orbit term is extracted from a nuclear energy density functional constructed via the Negele-Vautherin density matrix expansion using the same chiral potential. The density-dependent optical potential is then folded with nuclear density distributions for calcium isotopes using an improved local density approximation (ILDA) that accounts for the finite range of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, with the range parameter t varied between 1.15 fm and 1.25 fm. The resulting energy-dependent nucleon-nucleus optical potentials are implemented in the TALYS reaction code to compute proton-nucleus elastic scattering cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Proton-nucleus differential elastic scattering cross sections calculated at incident energies E = 25 MeV and E = 45 MeV for isotopes ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁴Ca, and ⁴⁸Ca.
- The ILDA range parameter t is varied within the range 1.15 fm < t < 1.25 fm.
- The chiral nuclear interaction used has a momentum-space cutoff Λ = 450 MeV.

## Context

This work extends microscopic optical potentials previously constructed in homogeneous nuclear matter (ChiralOP 2015, ChiralOP2 2017) to finite nuclei, specifically calcium isotopes. The authors compare their results to the phenomenological Koning-Delaroche optical potential (Koning and Delaroche 2003) and experimental data, finding that the microscopic imaginary potential is too absorptive, a known issue that semi-microscopic optical potentials address through energy-dependent strength factors (Goriely 2007). This approach enables future studies of nucleon scattering off exotic isotopes where experimental data are scarce, and the authors plan to investigate higher-order perturbative contributions to improve the imaginary part.
