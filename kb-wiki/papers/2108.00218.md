# Study of tetraquarks in dipole-dipole interaction potential

> In recent years tetraquark and pentaquark states have received much attention due to the significant experimental findings. In this work masses of some heavy tetraquarks are estimated by considering the spin-spin interaction, the dipole-dipole interaction, and the meson-meson interaction. It is found that such interactions give significant mass contributions. The Regge trajectory of X(3872) state is also studied and is found to be non-linear. Masses of some tetraquark states are also proposed.

## Key claim

The authors estimate masses of heavy tetraquarks by including spin-spin, diquark-antidiquark, meson-meson, and dipole-dipole interactions, finding that these interactions give "significant mass contributions" but that the Cornell potential yields results "much closer to the experimental values" than the Yukawa potential, with the caveat that for higher-mass states the difference from experiment "gradually increases."

## Method

The total tetraquark mass is computed as the sum of constituent quark masses, a spin-spin interaction term, and an average correction from diquark-antidiquark, meson-meson, and dipole-dipole interactions. The diquark-antidiquark and meson-meson interactions are evaluated using both a Cornell potential (with color factors) and a Yukawa potential; the dipole-dipole interaction is treated via a thermally averaged \(1/r^6\) potential solved with the Schrodinger equation. The Regge trajectory of the X(3872) state is then studied by computing masses in higher angular momentum states.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses used: \(m_u = 336\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_d = 340\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_s = 486\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_c = 1550\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_b = 4730\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Spin-spin coupling constant: \(A' = 2.36 \times 10^7\ \mathrm{MeV}^3\) for charm tetraquarks and \(A' = -1.6 \times 10^8\ \mathrm{MeV}^3\) for bottom tetraquarks.
- For the X(3872) state, the calculated mass using the Cornell potential is \(3867\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (experimental: \(3872\ \mathrm{MeV}\), error \(0.13\%\)); using the Yukawa potential it is \(3769\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (error \(2.6\%\)).
- For the fully charm tetraquark \(cc\bar{c}\bar{c}\) (experimental \(\approx 6900\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), the calculated mass is \(6455\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (Cornell, error \(6.4\%\)) and \(6186\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (Yukawa, error \(10\%\)).
- For the fully bottom tetraquark \(bb\bar{b}\bar{b}\), the predicted mass is \(18882\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The Regge trajectory for X(3872) gives masses: \(l=s\): \(3868\ \mathrm{MeV}\); \(l=p\): \(3941\ \mathrm{MeV}\); \(l=d\): \(4351\ \mathrm{MeV}\); \(l=f\): \(6016\ \mathrm{MeV}\); \(l=g\): \(6728\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of tetraquark masses using potential models, building on the diquark-antidiquark picture of Ebert, Faustov, and Galkin (2008) and the hadronic molecule approach of Gutsche, Kesenheimer, and Lyubovitskij (2013). It also follows the suggestion by Rathaud and Rai (2017) that dipole-like interactions exist between color-neutral states. The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD calculations such as those by Prelovsek and Leskovec (2013) on X(3872) and by Junnarkar, Mathur, and Padmanath (2019) on \(qq\bar{Q}\bar{Q}\) systems, and provide predicted masses for yet-unobserved tetraquark states like \(bb\bar{b}\bar{b}\).
