# Strange atoms, strange nuclei and kaon condensation

> Analyses of comprehensive sets of strong-interaction data consisting of level shifts, widths and yields in strange atoms of K mesons and Sigma hyperons are reviewed. The introduction of density dependent optical potentials, in both cases, improves significantly the fit to the data. A strongly attractive antikaon-nuclear potential of order 150-200 MeV in nuclear matter is suggested by fits to the kaonic-atom data, with interesting possible repercussions on kaon condensation and on the evolution of strangeness in high-density stars. The case for relatively narrow, deeply bound antikaon atomic states is made, essentially independent of the nuclear potential depth. Dynamical models for calculating binding energies and widths of antikaon-nuclear states are discussed and a lower bound of approximately 50 MeV on the width is established. For Sigma atoms, the fitted potential becomes repulsive inside the nucleus, in agreement with recently reported (pi-,K+) spectra from KEK, implying that Sigma hyperons generally do not bind in nuclei. This repulsion significantly affects calculated compositions and masses of neutron stars.

## Key claim

Analyses of strong-interaction data from kaonic and sigma atoms, using density-dependent optical potentials, suggest a strongly attractive antikaon-nuclear potential of order 150–200 MeV in nuclear matter, but the paper notes that this depth relates to potentials at threshold, whereas information for quasibound states is needed at energies roughly 100 MeV below threshold, adding ambiguity to predictions.

## Method

The authors reviewed global fits of density-dependent (DD) optical potentials to comprehensive sets of kaonic-atom data (65 data points across the periodic table) and sigma-atom data, using Klein-Gordon equations with potentials of the form \(t\rho(r)\) extended by a density-dependent term \(b_0 + B_0 [\rho(r)/\rho_0]^\alpha\) or a geometric switching function \(F(r)\). They applied a functional derivative (FD) method to identify radial sensitivity regions and compared results from phenomenological potentials with chirally-based coupled-channel calculations constrained by low-energy \(K^-p\) scattering and reaction data.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit density-dependent potential for kaonic atoms yields a real potential depth of order 150–200 MeV in nuclear matter (from fits to kaonic-atom data).
- A lower bound of approximately 50 MeV is established for the width of antikaon-nuclear states (\(\Gamma_{\bar K} \gtrsim 50\) MeV).
- For the \(2p\) state of kaonic atoms in \(^{208}\)Pb, the width saturates as the absorptive strength parameter Im \(b_0\) reaches about 20% of its best-fit value of 0.9 fm.
- The chiral leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa term gives \(V_{\bar K} \sim -55\) MeV at nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Self-consistent chiral calculations yield Re \(V_{\bar K}(\rho_0) \sim -(40-60)\) MeV.
- QCD sum-rule estimates give \(V_{\bar K}(\rho_0) \sim -130\) MeV; a mean-field argument gives \(\sim -170\) MeV.
- The KaoS collaboration estimate from \(K^-/K^+\) production ratios yields \(V_{\bar K}(\rho_0) \sim -80\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier review by Batty, Friedman, and Gal (1997) and the more recent analysis by Friedman and Gal (2007) on strange-atom optical potentials, using improved density-dependent parameterizations that respect the low-density limit. The results contradict shallow chirally-based potentials (e.g., Baca et al., 2000) of depth about 50 MeV, which give substantially worse fits to kaonic-atom data, and support deeper potentials that have implications for kaon condensation in neutron stars. The finding that sigma hyperons do not bind in nuclei, due to a repulsive interior potential, affects calculations of neutron-star compositions and masses, and agrees with \((\pi^-,K^+)\) spectra from KEK.
