# Rotational Bands at the Limit of Angular Momentum

> The properties of rotational bands at the limit of angular momentum are discussed on the example of smooth terminating bands observed in the A~110 mass region. The effective alignment approach is used for the study of their relative properties which provides additional insight into the properties of such bands.

# Rotational Bands at the Limit of Angular Momentum

## Key claim
Smooth terminating bands in the A~110 mass region provide a unique laboratory for studying the gradual interplay between collective and single-particle degrees of freedom, but the effective alignment approach shows that changes in deformation between bands play a much more important role than in superdeformed bands, meaning effective alignment does not necessarily reflect the alignment of single-particle orbitals.

## Method
The authors used the configuration-dependent shell-correction approach with the cranked Nilsson potential, where virtual crossings between single-particle orbitals are removed to obtain smooth diabatic orbitals, and total energy is minimized in deformation space (ε₂, ε₄, γ) for each fixed configuration and spin separately. Pairing correlations were neglected, making calculations fully realistic for spins above I ~ 20ħ in the A~110 mass region. The effective alignment approach was applied, defined as the difference in spin between two bands at constant rotational frequency, to study relative properties of linked and unlinked bands in ¹⁰⁹Sb, ¹¹⁰Sb, and ¹⁰⁸Sn.

## Key numbers
- Pairing correlations are negligible for spins above I ~ 20ħ in the A~110 mass region.
- Band 1 in ¹¹⁰Sb was predicted as yrast over the spin range 25ħ–45ħ, one year before experimental data became available.
- At rotational frequencies ħω > 0.7 MeV, calculations reproduce the drop of dynamic moment of inertia J⁽²⁾ rather well.
- The effective alignment in the ¹⁰⁸Sn(2)_bot*/¹⁰⁹Sb(1) pair is ≈ 1ħ lower than the pure single-particle alignment of the lowest π h₁₁/₂ orbital.
- The difference in γ-deformation between two neighbouring states within a band can reach ~ 30° in this mass region.

## Context
This work extends the study of terminating bands from the A~158 mass region, where terminating states are seen but rotational bands are difficult to observe over a large spin range, to the A~110 mass region where smooth terminating bands were recently found by Ragnarsson (1995) and others. The effective alignment approach was previously applied to superdeformed bands in the A~150 mass region by Ragnarsson (1991) and in cranked relativistic mean field theory by Afanasjev et al. (1997). The paper enables future searches for similar bands in the A~70–80 mass region, where calculations indicate configurations may continue as collective rotation beyond the maximum spin I_max due to strong mixing of N-shells at large deformations.
