# Constraining the strangeness content of the nucleon by measuring the $ϕ$ meson mass shift in nuclear matter

> The behavior of the $ϕ$ meson at finite density is studied, making use of a QCD sum rule approach in combination with the maximum entropy method. It is demonstrated that a possible mass shift of the $ϕ$ in nuclear matter is strongly correlated to the strangeness content of the nucleon, which is proportional to the strange sigma term, $σ_{sN} = m_s \langle N | \overline{s}s | N \rangle$. Our results furthermore show that, depending on the value of $σ_{sN}$, the $ϕ$ meson could receive both a positive or negative mass shift at nuclear matter density. We find that these results depend only weakly on potential modifications of the width of the $ϕ$ meson peak and on assumptions made on the behavior of four-quark condensates at finite density. To check the stability of our findings, we take into account several higher order corrections to the operator product expansion, including $α_s$-corrections, terms of higher order in the strange quark mass and terms of higher twist that have not been considered in earlier works.

## Key claim

The mass shift of the ϕ meson in nuclear matter is strongly correlated to the strangeness content of the nucleon (the strange sigma term σ_sN), and depending on the value of σ_sN, the ϕ meson could receive either a positive or negative mass shift at nuclear matter density, contrary to earlier sum rule studies that all obtained a negative mass shift.

## Method

The authors used a QCD sum rule approach combined with the maximum entropy method (MEM) to extract the most probable spectral function of the ϕ meson directly from the sum rules without strong assumptions on its functional form. They computed the operator product expansion (OPE) for the ϕ meson channel up to dimension six, including several higher-order corrections such as α_s-corrections, terms of higher order in the strange quark mass, and twist-2 gluonic operator terms not considered in earlier works. The density dependence of the OPE coefficients was treated at linear order in density, and the strange sigma term was treated as a free parameter to study its correlation with the ϕ meson mass shift.

## Key numbers

- The ϕ meson mass shift at nuclear matter density lies roughly in the range of +10 MeV to -10 MeV, based on lattice QCD values of σ_sN from the MILC collaboration (σ_sN = 61 ± 9 MeV) and the JLQCD collaboration (σ_sN = 8 ± 21 MeV).
- The MEM analysis of mock data showed that the precision of the mass shift extraction by MEM is of the order of 5 MeV.
- The vacuum peak position obtained from the OPE analysis was 1.075 GeV, which lies 56 MeV above the experimental value of 1.019 GeV.
- The strange sigma term values considered from lattice QCD were 61 ± 9 MeV (MILC) and 8 ± 21 MeV (JLQCD).
- The twist-2 gluonic operator term at dimension four was found to be almost as large as the twist-2 strange quark operator term, with A^g_2 = 0.359 ± 0.146.

## Context

This work extends earlier QCD sum rule studies of light vector mesons at finite density (Hatsuda, 1992; Klingl, 1997; Zschocke, 2011) that had all obtained a negative mass shift for the ϕ meson, by showing that recent smaller lattice QCD values of the strange sigma term (Freeman, 2012; Ohki, 2013) allow for both positive and negative mass shifts. The results are consistent with hadronic model calculations using effective Lagrangians (Klingl, 1997; Cabrera, 2004) that predict a small negative mass shift below 10 MeV, but would exclude too-small values of σ_sN for which the mass shift becomes positive. The approach enables experimental determination of the strangeness content of the nucleon through precise measurement of the ϕ meson mass in nuclear matter.
