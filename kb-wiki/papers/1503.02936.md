# Predictions for the $Λ_b \to J/ψ~ Λ(1405)$ decay

> We calculate the shape of the $πΣ$ and $\bar K N$ invariant mass distributions in the $Λ_b \to J/ψ\, πΣ$ and $Λ_b \to J/ψ\,\bar K N$ decays that are dominated by the $Λ(1405)$ resonance. The weak interaction part is the same for both processes and the hadronization into the different meson-baryon channels in the final state is related by SU(3) symmetry. The most important feature is the implementation of the meson-baryon final-state interaction using two chiral unitary models from different theoretical groups. Both approaches give a good description of antikaon-nucleon scattering data, the complex energy shift in kaonic hydrogen and the line shapes of $πΣK$ in photoproduction, based on the two-pole scenario for the $Λ(1405)$. We find that this reaction reflects more the higher mass pole and we make predictions of the line shapes and relative strength of the meson-baryon distributions in the final state.

## Key claim

The reaction $\Lambda_b \to J/\psi \, \Lambda(1405)$ reflects more the higher-mass pole of the two-pole structure of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance, and the predictions are made for the line shapes and relative strength of the meson-baryon distributions without providing absolute rates.

## Method

The weak decay of the $\Lambda_b$ produces a $c\bar c$ pair forming the $J/\psi$ and a three-quark $uds$ state, which hadronizes into a meson-baryon pair ($K^-p$, $\bar K^0 n$, $\eta\Lambda$, $\eta'\Lambda$) with relative weights determined by SU(3) symmetry. The final-state interaction of the meson-baryon system is then described using two different chiral unitary models (the Bonn model and the Murcia-Valencia model), both of which generate the $\Lambda(1405)$ dynamically via coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations. The invariant mass distributions for $\pi\Sigma$ and $\bar K N$ final states are computed from the resulting amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda_b$ mass is 5619 MeV and the $J/\psi$ mass is 3097 MeV, giving a phase space up to 2522 MeV for the meson-baryon system.
- In the Bonn model, the two $\Lambda(1405)$ poles are located at $(1429^{+8}_{-7} - i\,12^{+2}_{-3})$ MeV and $(1325^{+15}_{-15} - i\,90^{+12}_{-18})$ MeV.
- In the MV-model, the two $\Lambda(1405)$ poles are located at $1352 - 48i$ MeV and $1419 - 29i$ MeV.
- The Bonn model gives a narrower highest pole (24 MeV width) compared to the MV-model (58 MeV width).
- The averaged meson decay constant used in the MV-model is $f = 1.123 f_\pi$ with $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the method of using weak decays of heavy hadrons to study dynamically generated resonances, previously applied to mesonic systems like $f_0(980)$ in $B^0_s$ decays (Aaij 2011, Li 2011) and scalar mesons in $D^0$ decays (Daid), now to baryonic resonances. It relates the $\Lambda_b \to J/\psi \, \Lambda(1405)$ decay to the already measured $\Lambda_b \to J/\psi \, K^- p$ decay (LHCb 2014, CDF 2014), enabling experimental extraction of the $\Lambda(1405)$ properties and further testing of the two-pole scenario established in chiral unitary approaches (Oller 2000, cola, Mai 2012).
