# Evidence for coexisting shapes in $^{98}$Zr through lifetime measurements

> The lifetimes of first excited 2$^+$, 4$^+$ and 6$^+$ states in $^{98}$Zr were measured with the Recoil-Distance Doppler Shift method in an experiment performed at GANIL. Excited states in $^{98}$Zr were populated using the fission reaction between a 6.2 MeV/u $^{238}$U beam and a $^{9}$Be target. The $γ$ rays were detected with the EXOGAM array in correlation with the fission fragments identified in mass and atomic number in the VAMOS++ spectrometer. Our result shows very small B(E2;2$_1^+$ $\rightarrow$ 0$_1^+$) value in $^{98}$Zr thereby confirming the very sudden onset of collectivity at $N=60$. The experimental results are compared to large-scale Monte Carlo Shell model and beyond mean field calculations. The present results indicate coexistence of two additional deformed shapes in this nucleus along with the spherical ground state.

## Key claim

The paper claims that lifetime measurements of the first excited 2⁺, 4⁺, and 6⁺ states in ⁹⁸Zr provide evidence for the coexistence of three distinct shapes (spherical, moderately deformed, and well-deformed) at low spin, with the very small B(E2;2₁⁺→0₁⁺) value confirming the sudden onset of collectivity at N=60 rather than a gradual increase already in ⁹⁸Zr.

## Method

Excited states in ⁹⁸Zr were populated via fission reactions between a 6.2 MeV/u ²³⁸U beam and a ⁹Be target at GANIL. Lifetimes were measured using the Recoil-Distance Doppler Shift (RDDS) method with a compact plunger device, where recoiling nuclei with average velocity 37.3 μm/ps were slowed in a Mg degrader foil; γ rays were detected with the EXOGAM array in coincidence with fission fragments identified in mass and atomic number by the VAMOS++ spectrometer. The differential decay curve method (DDCM) was applied to the intensities of shifted and unshifted γ-ray components as a function of seven target-degrader distances from 37 to 1554 μm.

## Key numbers

- Lifetime of the 2₁⁺ state: τ = 3.8 ± 0.8 ps (first direct measurement; previous upper limit was ≤6 ps from Ansari et al.)
- Lifetime of the 4₁⁺ state: τ = 7.5 ± 1.5 ps (previous upper limit ≤15 ps from Ansari et al.)
- Lifetime of the 6₁⁺ state: τ = 2.6 ± 0.9 ps
- B(E2;2₁⁺→0₁⁺) = 2.9 ± 0.6 W.u. (statistical) ± 0.2 W.u. (systematic), confirming very small collectivity in ⁹⁸Zr
- B(E2;2₁⁺→0₂⁺) = 28.3 ± 6.0 W.u. (statistical) ± 2.4 W.u. (systematic), indicating a moderately deformed structure with quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ ≈ 0.21
- B(E2;4₁⁺→2₁⁺) = 43.3 ± 8.7 W.u. (statistical) ± 10.8 W.u. (systematic)
- B(E2;6₁⁺→4₁⁺) = 103.0 ± 35.7 W.u.

## Context

This work extends previous lifetime studies in the A~100 region, including measurements in ⁹⁶,⁹⁸Sr by Clement et al. (2016) and in ⁹⁴,⁹⁶Zr by Chakraborty et al. (2013) and Kremer et al. (2016), which established shape coexistence in neighboring isotopes. The results are compared to large-scale Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) calculations by Togashi et al. (2016) and beyond-mean-field calculations using the Gogny-D1S force by Delaroche et al. (2010); the MCSM calculations reproduce the sudden onset of collectivity at N=60, while mean-field approaches predict a more gradual increase. The measured B(E2) values enable testing of the type-II shell evolution mechanism and provide evidence for triple shape coexistence (spherical, prolate, triaxial) in ⁹⁸Zr, a key nucleus for understanding the quantum phase transition at N=60.
