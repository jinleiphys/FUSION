# Potential energy surfaces of superheavy nuclei

> We investigate the structure of the potential energy surfaces of the superheavy nuclei 258Fm, 264Hs, (Z=112,N=166), (Z=114,N=184), and (Z=120,N=172) within the framework of self-consistent nuclear models, i.e. the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach and the relativistic mean-field model. We compare results obtained with one representative parametrisation of each model which is successful in describing superheavy nuclei. We find systematic changes as compared to the potential energy surfaces of heavy nuclei in the uranium region: there is no sufficiently stable fission isomer any more, the importance of triaxial configurations to lower the first barrier fades away, and asymmetric fission paths compete down to rather small deformation. Comparing the two models, it turns out that the relativistic mean-field model gives generally smaller fission barriers.

# Potential Energy Surfaces of Superheavy Nuclei

## Key claim

The potential energy surfaces of superheavy nuclei show systematic differences from those of actinides: there is no stable fission isomer, triaxial configurations become less important for lowering the first barrier in heavier systems, and asymmetric fission paths extend down to much smaller deformations than in lighter nuclei. However, these conclusions depend on the specific self-consistent model used, as the relativistic mean-field model (with parametrisation PL-40) generally gives smaller fission barriers than the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach (with parametrisation SkI4).

## Method

The authors performed constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations for five superheavy nuclei (258Fm, 264Hs, 278112, 298114, and 292120) using both the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach with the SkI4 parametrisation and the relativistic mean-field model with the PL-40 parametrisation. The calculations allowed for axial and reflection-asymmetric shapes, with additional triaxial calculations near the first barrier, and treated pairing correlations in the BCS scheme using a delta pairing force.

## Key numbers

- The shell effect in the prolate ground state of 258Fm lowers the binding energy by 19.3 MeV compared to a spherical shape
- The first barrier of 258Fm is lowered from 11.8 MeV to 7.7 MeV when allowing for triaxial configurations (SkI4)
- The first barrier of 278112 (SkI4) is 7.3 MeV, compared with 10.6 MeV for 264Hs (SkI4)
- The spherical configuration of 298114 is lowered by 7 MeV for SkI4 but raised by approximately 1.3 MeV for PL-40 relative to the reference point
- The triaxial lowering of the first barrier for 298114 is approximately 3 MeV for SkI4, while for PL-40 the gain is only a few hundred keV
- The uncertainty from the center-of-mass correction is smaller than 0.2 MeV; the uncertainty from pairing is approximately 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier self-consistent studies of superheavy nuclei by the same group (Bender et al., 1997; 1998) that demonstrated uncertainties in extrapolating shell structure to superheavy nuclei, and by Cwiok et al. (1996) who discussed triaxial shapes in Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations. It contrasts with macroscopic-microscopic calculations (Moller et al., 1994; Cwiok et al., 1992; Smolanczuk et al., 1995) by including reflection-asymmetric shapes in a fully self-consistent framework. The results enable better predictions for fission stability and optimal fusion paths for synthesizing new superheavy nuclei, while highlighting that the existence and stability of superheavy nuclei is a sensitive probe for present mean-field models.
