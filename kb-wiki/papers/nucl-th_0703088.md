# Hybrid stars within a covariant, nonlocal chiral quark model

> We present a hybrid equation of state (EoS) for dense matter in which a nuclear matter phase is described within the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach and a two-flavor quark matter phase is modelled according to a recently developed covariant, nonlocal chiral quark model. We show that modern observational constraints for compact star masses (M ~ 2 M_sun) can be satisfied when a small vector-like four quark interaction is taken into account. The corresponding isospin symmetric EoS is consistent with flow data analyses of heavy ion collisions and points to a deconfinement transition at about 0.55 fm^-3.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a hybrid equation of state combining a Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock nuclear matter phase with a two-flavor quark matter phase from a covariant nonlocal chiral quark model can satisfy the modern observational constraint of compact star masses around 2 solar masses, but only when a small vector-like four-quark interaction is included; the deconfinement transition occurs at about 0.55 fm^-3, which is consistent with flow data from heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid equation of state using a two-phase Maxwell construction: the nuclear matter phase is described by the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with the Bonn-A potential, while the quark matter phase is modeled by a covariant nonlocal chiral quark model that includes scalar, vector, and diquark interactions. The model parameters are fixed by reproducing the empirical pion mass (139 MeV), pion decay constant (92.4 MeV), and chiral condensate, with the vector coupling strength and diquark coupling ratio treated as free parameters tuned to satisfy both heavy-ion flow constraints and compact star mass-radius constraints.

## Key numbers

- Deconfinement transition density: about 0.55 fm^-3 for isospin symmetric matter
- Maximum neutron star mass from DBHF nuclear matter alone: 2.33 solar masses
- Typical neutron star radii from DBHF: 12-13 km
- Nuclear saturation density: n_sat = 0.16 fm^-3
- Pion mass used for model calibration: m_pi = 139 MeV
- Pion decay constant used for model calibration: f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- Chiral condensate used for model calibration: <0|bar q q|0>^(1/3) = -230 MeV
- Current quark mass: m_c = 6.49 MeV
- Scalar coupling: G_S = 0.515 x 10^-4 MeV^-2
- Cutoff parameter: Lambda = 678 MeV
- Diquark coupling ratios considered: h = H/G_S = 0.70 and 0.74
- Vector coupling ratios that satisfy constraints: g = G_V/G_S = 0.05 (for h=0.70) and g = 0.07 (for h=0.74)
- Observed pulsar mass constraint: M = 2.1 +/- 0.2 solar masses for J0751+1807
- Observed compact star mass: M = 2.0 +/- 0.1 solar masses for 4U 1636-536
- Observed radius constraint: R > 12 km for RX J1856.5-3754

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Klahn et al. (2006) to a more elaborate nonlocal chiral quark model formalism developed by Gomez Dumm et al. (2001, 2005). The paper addresses the tension between the stiff DBHF nuclear equation of state, which produces high maximum masses but violates flow constraints from heavy-ion collisions above three times saturation density as shown by Danielewicz (2002), and the need to simultaneously satisfy both the high-mass pulsar observations and the flow data. The results enable future development of unified approaches that describe both quark and nuclear matter from a single chiral quark model, as called for by Lawley (2006) and Rezaeian (2006).
