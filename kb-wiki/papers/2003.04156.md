# Nuclear shadowing in DIS at electron-ion colliders

> We present a revision of predictions for nuclear shadowing in deep-inelastic scattering at small Bjorken $x_{Bj}$ corresponding to kinematic regions accessible by the future experiments at electron-ion colliders. The nuclear shadowing is treated within the color dipole formalism based on the rigorous Green function technique. This allows incorporating naturally color transparency and coherence length effects, which are not consistently and properly included in present calculations. For the lowest $|q\bar q\rangle$ Fock component of the photon, our calculations are based on an exact numerical solution of the evolution equation for the Green function. Here the magnitude of shadowing is tested using a realistic form for the nuclear density function, as well as various phenomenological models for the dipole cross section. The corresponding variation of the transverse size of the $q\bar q$ photon fluctuations is important for $x_{Bj}\gtrsim 10^{-4}$, on the contrary with the most of other models, which use frequently only the eikonal approximation with the "frozen" transverse size. At $x_{Bj}\lesssim 0.01$ we calculate within the same formalism also a shadowing correction for the higher Fock component of the photon containing gluons. The corresponding magnitudes of gluon shadowing correction are compared adopting different phenomenological dipole models. Our results are tested by available data from the E665 and NMC collaborations. Finally, the magnitude of nuclear shadowing is predicted for various kinematic regions that should be scanned by the future experiments at electron-ion colliders.

## Key claim

The authors revise predictions for nuclear shadowing in deep-inelastic scattering at small Bjorken x using the color dipole formalism with a Green function technique, claiming that this approach naturally incorporates color transparency and coherence length effects that are not consistently included in most other calculations, which rely on the eikonal approximation with a "frozen" transverse size of the q-qbar pair.

## Method

The authors treat nuclear shadowing within the light-cone color dipole formalism using a rigorous Green function technique, solving the two-dimensional Schrodinger equation for the Green function describing q-qbar pair propagation through nuclear matter. For the lowest |q-qbar> Fock component of the photon, they use an exact numerical solution of the evolution equation for the Green function, testing the magnitude of shadowing with a realistic nuclear density function and various phenomenological models for the dipole cross section (GBW, KST, BGBK, IP-Sat). At x_Bj less than or equal to 0.01, they also calculate shadowing corrections for the higher Fock component containing gluons (|q-qbar-G>), comparing magnitudes across different dipole models.

## Key numbers

* For x_Bj greater than or approximately equal to 10^{-4}, the variation of the transverse size of the q-qbar photon fluctuations is important, contrary to most other models that use the eikonal approximation with a "frozen" transverse size.
* At x_Bj less than or approximately equal to 0.01, the authors calculate shadowing corrections for the higher Fock component of the photon containing gluons.
* The coherence length for the |q-qbar> state is given by t_c = 2*nu / (Q^2 + M_q-qbar^2), where nu is the photon energy, Q^2 is the photon virtuality, and M_q-qbar is the effective mass of the q-qbar pair.
* The mean transverse separation of the q-qbar fluctuation is approximately 1/epsilon = 1 / sqrt(Q^2 * alpha * (1-alpha) + m_q^2), where alpha is the momentum fraction carried by the quark and m_q is the quark mass.

## Context

This work extends and revises earlier calculations of nuclear shadowing by Nemchik (2003) and Kopeliovich (2008) within the color dipole formalism, improving upon them by using the Green function technique that properly includes quantum coherence and color transparency effects. The predictions are tested against available data from the E665 and NMC collaborations, and the results are intended to guide future measurements at electron-ion colliders (EICs) at RHIC and LHC facilities. The authors provide public numerical grids for the gluon shadowing factor R_G on Zenodo (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.3470138) for various nuclear targets, Q^2 values, x_Bj values, and nuclear impact parameters.
