# Neutrinos and Heavy Element Nucleosynthesis

> This chapter discusses three nucleosynthesis processes involved in producing heavy nuclei beyond the iron group that are influenced or shaped by neutrino interactions: the v process, the vp process and the r process. These processes are all related to explosive events involving compact objects, such as core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers, where an abundant amount of neutrinos are emitted. The interactions of the neutrinos with nucleons and nuclei through both charged-current and neutral-current reactions play a crucial role in the nucleosynthesis processes. During the propagation of neutrinos inside the nucleosynthesis sites, neutrinos may undergo flavor oscillations that can also potentially affect the nucleosynthesis yields. Here we provide a general overview of the possible effects of neutrinos and neutrino flavor conversions on these three heavy-element nucleosynthesis processes.

## Key claim

This chapter reviews three heavy-element nucleosynthesis processes (the ν process, νp process, and r process) that are shaped by neutrino interactions in explosive astrophysical environments, and summarizes how neutrino flavor transformations, including collective oscillations, matter-neutrino resonance, and fast flavor conversions, can potentially affect the nucleosynthesis yields, though the exact impact depends on the specific site and oscillation scenario.

## Method

The authors provide a narrative review of the theoretical and observational status of neutrino-influenced nucleosynthesis processes in core-collapse supernovae (CCSN) and binary neutron star mergers (NSM). They survey the roles of charged-current and neutral-current neutrino reactions on nucleons and nuclei, and discuss how active-active and active-sterile neutrino flavor transformations (collective oscillations, MSW resonance, matter-neutrino resonance, fast pairwise conversions) modify neutrino spectra and thereby affect the electron fraction, neutron-to-proton ratio, and final nucleosynthetic yields in each process.

## Key numbers

- The ν process contributes entirely or significantly to the production of rare isotopes including ⁷Li, ¹¹B, ¹⁵N, ¹⁹F, ⁹²Nb, ⁹⁸Tc, ¹³⁸La, ¹⁸⁰Ta, ²²Na, and ²⁶Al.
- The νp process can produce light p-nuclei such as ⁹²,⁹⁴Mo and ⁹⁶,⁹⁸Ru in proton-rich neutrino-driven winds.
- For the r process in NSM dynamical ejecta, the electron fraction is Y_e ∼ 0.03–0.2, while in the neutrino-driven wind from the merger accretion disk it is Y_e ∼ 0.25–0.5.
- The r process produces more than 90% of heavy elements such as Eu, Au, and Pt in the solar system.
- The r process is responsible for all nuclei heavier than Bi.
- In CCSN, collective neutrino oscillations may occur within a radius of ∼100 km above the proto-neutron star.
- MSW flavor transformations in CCSN occur at baryon density ρ ∼ 10²–10³ g/cm³.
- The light isotope ratio ⁷Li/¹¹B can probe the θ₁₃ mixing angle; the abundance ratio ¹³⁸La/¹¹B is sensitive to the neutrino mass hierarchy, with solar meteoritic abundances preferring normal mass hierarchy.

## Context

This chapter extends the foundational work of Burbidge (1957) on the s, r, and p processes, and reviews subsequent developments including the ν process (Woosley 1990), the νp process (Frohlich 2006, Pruet 2006, Wanajo 2006), and modern r-process studies in CCSN and NSM. It synthesizes results from numerous studies on neutrino flavor transformations (Duan 2010, Chakraborty 2016, Tamborra 2020, Capozzi 2022) and their effects on nucleosynthesis yields. The review enables future work on the interplay between neutrino oscillations and heavy-element production, particularly in light of the multi-messenger observation of GW170817 and ongoing debates about the relative contributions of NSM, CCSN, collapsars, and magneto-rotational supernovae to galactic r-process abundances.
