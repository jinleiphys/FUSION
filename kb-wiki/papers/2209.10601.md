# Coupled-channel dynamics with chiral long-range forces in the open-charm sector of QCD

> We perform an analysis of Lattice QCD data in the open-charm sector based on the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian. The low-energy constants are adjusted to recover the open-charm meson masses on Lattice QCD ensembles from HPQCD, ETMC and HSC with pion and kaon masses smaller than 550 MeV. A significant set of low-energy parameters is obtainable only if the most recent information from HSC on scattering observables is included in our global fit. For the first time our analysis considers the effect of left-hand cuts as developed in terms of a generalized potential approach (GPA) previously by one of the authors. Here we use coupled-channel interaction terms at the one-loop level. The elastic s-wave and p-wave $D\,π$, $D K $ and $D \bar K $ scattering phase shifts on ensembles with nominal pion masses of about 239 MeV and 391 MeV are reproduced faithfully. Based on such low-energy parameters we predict s- and p-wave phase shifts and inelasticities at physical quark masses, where the statistical uncertainties in the phase shifts are smaller than 1 degree always. Most striking would be the exotic s-wave $D_s π$ channel, for which we predict a resonance state at about 2.287 GeV where the phase shift passes through 90 degrees.

## Key claim

The authors claim that, for the first time, a global fit to Lattice QCD data in the open-charm sector that includes both meson masses and scattering observables from HSC, and that treats left-hand cuts via the generalized potential approach (GPA), can reproduce elastic s-wave and p-wave phase shifts on ensembles with pion masses of about 239 MeV and 391 MeV, and predict phase shifts at physical quark masses with statistical uncertainties smaller than 1 degree. The most striking prediction is an exotic s-wave resonance in the \(D_s \pi\) channel at about 2.287 GeV.

## Method

The authors perform a global fit of low-energy constants (LECs) from the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian to Lattice QCD data on open-charm meson masses from HPQCD, ETMC, and HSC, and to s-wave and p-wave scattering phase shifts from HSC on ensembles with nominal pion masses of about 239 MeV and 391 MeV. The analysis uses coupled-channel interaction terms at the one-loop level and, for the first time in this context, incorporates the effect of left-hand cuts via the generalized potential approach (GPA) developed previously by one of the authors. The LECs are adjusted to recover the open-charm meson masses on ensembles with pion and kaon masses smaller than 550 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The elastic s-wave and p-wave \(D\pi\), \(DK\), and \(D\bar K\) scattering phase shifts are reproduced faithfully on ensembles with nominal pion masses of about 239 MeV and 391 MeV.
- The predicted s- and p-wave phase shifts and inelasticities at physical quark masses have statistical uncertainties smaller than 1 degree always.
- The exotic s-wave \(D_s \pi\) channel is predicted to have a resonance state at about 2.287 GeV, where the phase shift passes through 90 degrees.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the GSI group (Guo 2018, Guo 2021) that used chiral Lagrangians to analyze Lattice QCD data on open-charm meson masses and s-wave scattering lengths, but which suffered from limited empirical constraints and residual dependence on the coupled-channel computation setup. By including the newer HSC data on both s- and p-wave scattering phase shifts and applying the GPA method, the authors achieve a more accurate description and can predict phase shifts at physical quark masses. This enables future studies of exotic open-charm resonances and tests of heavy-quark spin symmetry and chiral symmetry in the charm sector.
