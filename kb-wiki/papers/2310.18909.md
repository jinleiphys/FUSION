# Impact of Medium Anisotropy on Quarkonium Dissociation and Regeneration

> Quarkonium production in ultra-relativistic collisions plays a crucial role in probing the existence of hot QCD matter. This study explores quarkonia states dissociation and regeneration in the hot QCD medium while considering momentum anisotropy. The net quarkonia decay width ($Γ_{D}$) arises from two essential processes: collisional damping and gluonic dissociation. The quarkonia regeneration includes the transition from octet to singlet states within the anisotropic medium. Our study utilizes a medium-modified potential that incorporates anisotropy via particle distribution functions. This modified potential gives rise to collisional damping for quarkonia due to the surrounding medium, as well as the transition of quarkonia from singlet to octet states due to interactions with gluons. Furthermore, we employ the detailed balance approach to investigate the regeneration of quarkonia within this medium. Our comprehensive analysis spans various temperature settings, transverse momentum values, and anisotropic strengths. Notably, we find that, in addition to medium temperatures and heavy quark transverse momentum, anisotropy significantly influences the dissociation and regeneration of various quarkonia states.

## Key claim

The study demonstrates that momentum anisotropy in the quark-gluon plasma significantly influences both the dissociation and regeneration of quarkonium states, with the effect being most distinguishable at low transverse momentum and high effective temperature, while diminishing at high transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors extended the Unified Model for Quarkonia Suppression (UMQS) framework to incorporate momentum anisotropy by modifying the medium's particle distribution functions through stretching and squeezing along one direction, quantified by the anisotropic parameter ζ. They derived a medium-modified Cornell potential with complex dielectric permittivity, then calculated the net decay width (Γ_D) from collisional damping and gluonic dissociation, and the recombination reactivity (Γ_F) from octet-to-singlet transitions using detailed balance, incorporating relativistic Doppler shift effects via an effective temperature (T_eff).

## Key numbers

- Net decay width Γ_D for J/ψ increases with anisotropic parameter ζ from 0 to 0.4 at temperatures T = 200, 300, and 400 MeV, with the effect most pronounced at p_T < 3 GeV
- For J/ψ at T = 400 MeV, the dissociation temperature T_D is approximately 350 MeV, above which regeneration probability suppresses to a narrow p_T range
- For Υ(1S) at T = 400 MeV, Γ_D remains almost constant up to p_T ~ 20 GeV, then decreases; at T = 200 and 300 MeV, the difference in Γ_D across ζ values remains consistent up to p_T = 30 GeV
- J/ψ regeneration Γ_F shows negligible dependence on anisotropy at T = 200 MeV, small dependence at intermediate p_T (5 < p_T < 17 GeV) at T = 300 MeV, and non-monotonic behavior at T = 400 MeV with maximum recombination at p_T ~ 8-10 GeV for the isotropic case

## Context

This work extends the UMQS model developed by Singh et al. (crs1-crs5) for isotropic media to include momentum anisotropy, building on prior studies of quarkonium dissociation in anisotropic QGP by Jamal (2018) and others (Romatschke 2003, Carrington 2014). The study addresses the previously unexplored aspect of quarkonium regeneration in anisotropic media, motivated by experimental observations at LHC of increased J/ψ production at low momentum suggesting recombination during the QGP phase (Andronic 2017). The formalism can be extended to calculate survival probabilities of quarkonia at RHIC and LHC energies, though this is left for future work.
