# Phase Transitions at Finite Temperature and Dimensional Reduction for Fermions and Bosons

> In a recent Letter we discussed the fact that large-$N$ expansions and computer simulations indicate that the universality class of the finite temperature chiral symmetry restoration transition in the 3D Gross-Neveu model is mean field theory. This was seen to be a counterexample to the standard 'sigma model' scenario which predicts the 2D Ising model universality class. In this article we present more evidence, both theoretical and numerical, that this result is correct. We develop a physical picture for our results and discuss the width of the scaling region (Ginzburg criterion), $1/N$ corrections, and differences between the dynamics of BCS superconductors and Gross-Neveu models. Lattices as large as $12 \times 72^2$ are simulated for both the $N=12$ and $N=4$ cases and the numerical evidence for mean field scaling is quite compelling. We point out that the amplitude ratio for the model's susceptibility is a particulartly good observable for distinguishing between the dimensional reduction and the mean field scenerios, because this universal quantity differs by almost a factor of $20$ in the two cases. The simulations are done close to the critical point in both the symmetric and broken phases, and correlation lengths of order $10$ are measured. The critical indices $β_{mag}$ and $δ$ also pick out mean field behavior. We trace the breakdown of the standard scenario (dimensional reduction and universality) to the composite character of the mesons in the model. We point out that our results should be generic for theories with dynamical symmetry breaking, such as Quantum Chromodynamics. We also simulated the $O(2)$ model on $8 \times 16^3$ lattices to establish that our methods give the results of dimensional reduction in purely bosonic

## Key claim

The finite temperature chiral symmetry restoration transition in the 3D Gross-Neveu model belongs to the mean field universality class rather than the 2D Ising class predicted by dimensional reduction, and this failure of the standard scenario is traced to the composite character of the mesons in the model.

## Method

The authors performed large-N expansions and Monte Carlo simulations of the 3D Gross-Neveu model on lattices up to 12 × 72² for N=12 and N=4 fermion species, measuring the magnetization (order parameter), susceptibility, and correlation length near the critical point in both symmetric and broken phases. They also simulated the O(2) scalar model on 8 × 16³ lattices as a control case where dimensional reduction is expected to hold, and Z₂ Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models on 8 × 16³ lattices for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Critical exponents for the Gross-Neveu model: β_mag = 1/2, δ = 3, γ = 1 (mean field values), compared to 2D Ising values β_mag = 1/8, δ = 15, γ = 7/4
- The susceptibility amplitude ratio C_+/C_- = 2 in mean field theory versus 37.69 for the 2D Ising model, a difference of nearly a factor of 20
- For the O(2) scalar model on 8 × 16³ lattices: β_mag = 0.34(2) (fit over couplings 0.17 to 0.155), consistent with the 3D O(2) value 0.346(2) and rejecting mean field 0.50
- O(2) model susceptibility exponent γ = 1.22(1) (fit over couplings 0.17 to 0.155 with β_c = 0.1519), compared to 3D O(2) value 1.32(2) and mean field 1.00
- O(2) model critical exponent δ = 4.7(2) (fit over m range 0.002-0.010 at β_c = 0.1519), compared to 3D O(2) value 4.808(7) and mean field 3.00
- O(2) model critical coupling β_c = 0.1519(1) from magnetization fit
- Correlation lengths of order 10 measured in Gross-Neveu simulations

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier Letter (Kocic and Kogut, 1995) providing more evidence that the Gross-Neveu model violates the dimensional reduction scenario proposed by Pisarski and Wilczek (1983), which predicted that finite temperature chiral transitions should fall into the universality class of lower-dimensional scalar models. The results contradict the expectation that the 3D Gross-Neveu model with Z₂ symmetry should exhibit 2D Ising critical behavior. The authors argue that their findings should be generic for theories with dynamical symmetry breaking such as Quantum Chromodynamics, and they contrast the Gross-Neveu dynamics with that of BCS superconductors, where long-range interactions produce a narrow scaling region. The O(2) scalar model simulations serve to validate that their numerical methods correctly reproduce dimensional reduction in purely bosonic theories where it is theoretically well-founded.
