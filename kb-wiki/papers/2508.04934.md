# Anisotropic modifications to the transport phenomena and observables in a hot QCD medium at finite baryon asymmetry

> We have studied how the transport of charge and heat as well as associated observables become influenced by a weak-momentum anisotropy arising due to the asymptotic expansion of baryon asymmetric matter in the initial stages of heavy ion collisions. This study facilitates the understanding of the local equilibrium property of the medium through the Knudsen number, and explores the correlation between the heat flow and the charge flow through the Lorenz number in the Wiedemann-Franz law for an anisotropic hot QCD medium at finite baryon asymmetry. We have determined the electrical and the thermal conductivities by solving the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation within the kinetic theory approach. The interactions among partons are appended through their distribution functions within the quasiparticle model of the hot QCD medium at finite temperature, anisotropy and baryon asymmetry. We have observed a decrease in both electrical and thermal conductivities in the presence of expansion-induced anisotropy for baryonless scenario as well as for baryon asymmetric scenario. Conversely, these conductivities are found to be larger in the baryon asymmetric matter as compared to their counterparts in the baryonless matter. The impact of anisotropy on the baryon asymmetric matter is as conspicuous as on the baryonless matter. The above results are attributed to the squeezing of the distribution function due to the momentum anisotropy generated by the asymptotic expansion of baryon asymmetric matter and the dispersion relations of partons in the presence of anisotropy. Additionally, the aforesaid observables are also modulated by the expansion-induced anisotropy in the baryon asymmetric medium, indicating new predictions for the equilibrium characteristic and the relative behavior between the heat and charge flow for the said medium.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both electrical and thermal conductivities of a hot QCD medium decrease in the presence of weak momentum anisotropy arising from asymptotic expansion, but these conductivities are larger in baryon asymmetric matter compared to baryonless matter, with the caveat that these results are obtained within the relaxation time approximation using a momentum-independent relaxation time and a quasiparticle model where parton masses depend on temperature, chemical potential, and the anisotropic parameter.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation within kinetic theory to compute the electrical and thermal conductivities of a hot QCD medium. They incorporated a weak momentum anisotropy (parameterized by ξ < 1) through a Romatschke-Strickland form of the parton distribution functions expanded to first order in ξ, and included finite baryon asymmetry via a nonzero quark chemical potential. The interactions among partons were modeled using quasiparticle distribution functions with medium-dependent masses that depend on temperature, chemical potential, and the anisotropic parameter, with the one-loop running coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- The baryon chemical potential at temperature around 160 MeV is approximately 300 MeV, citing earlier studies
- In the strong magnetic field regime, the baryon chemical potential was observed to go up from 0.1 GeV to 0.6 GeV, citing Fukushima (2016)
- The relaxation time for quark (antiquark) of fth flavor is given by τ_f = 1/[5.1 T α_s² log(1/α_s) (1 + 0.12(2N_f + 1))]
- The characteristic length used for the Knudsen number calculation is l = 4 fm
- The bare masses of u, d, and s quark flavors used are 3 MeV, 5 MeV, and 100 MeV, respectively
- The one-loop running coupling uses Λ_MS = 0.176 GeV with Λ = 2π√(T² + μ_f²/π²) for quarks and Λ = 2πT for gluons

## Context

This work extends the framework developed in Rath (2019) and Rath (2020) for transport coefficients in magnetized QCD media to the physically distinct setting of expansion-induced momentum anisotropy at finite baryon asymmetry, in the absence of magnetic fields. It builds on earlier studies of anisotropic dilepton and photon yields by Martinez (2008) and Ryblewski (2015), and of electrical conductivity in anisotropic media by Srivastava (2015), while incorporating the effect of anisotropy on parton masses that was neglected in those prior works. The results enable predictions for the Knudsen number and Lorenz number in a more realistic QGP medium that simultaneously accounts for both momentum anisotropy and baryon asymmetry, relevant for understanding local equilibrium and the relative behavior of heat and charge flow in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
