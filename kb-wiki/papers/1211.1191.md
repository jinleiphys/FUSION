# Next-to-Leading Order correction to inclusive particle spectra in the Color Glass Condensate framework

> In [arXiv:0804.2630], we have analyzed the leading logarithms of energy that appear in the inclusive spectrum of gluons produced in heavy ion collisions, calculated in the Color Glass Condensate framework. The main result of this paper was that these logarithms are intrinsic properties of the colliding projectiles, and that they can be resummed by letting the distributions of color sources in the nuclei evolve according to the JIMWLK equation. An essential step in the proof of this factorization result is the calculation of the gluon spectrum at Next-to-Leading order, and in particular a functional relationship that expresses the NLO correction as the action of a certain operator on the LO spectrum. In this paper, we show that this type of relation between spectra at LO and NLO is not specific to the production of gluons, but that it is in fact generic for inclusive spectra in heavy ion collisions. To illustrate this, we compute up to NLO the inclusive spectrum of some hypothetical scalar fields, either color neutral or colored, that couple to gluons.

## Key claim

The authors show that the functional relationship between leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) inclusive spectra, previously established for gluon production in the Color Glass Condensate framework, is generic and applies also to hypothetical scalar fields (both color neutral and colored) that couple to gluons, not just to gluon production itself.

## Method

The authors compute the inclusive spectrum of a color neutral scalar field coupled to the square of the gluon field strength, and of a colored scalar field in the adjoint representation of the gauge group, up to NLO in the CGC framework. They use a diagrammatic method based on the operator T_u (generator of shifts of the classical color field on a Cauchy surface) to express the NLO correction as the action of a specific operator on the LO spectrum, generalizing the result previously obtained for gluons in Gelis and Laidet (2008).

## Key numbers

- The LO gluon spectrum is of order O(g^{-2}) when both projectiles are saturated.
- The LO phi spectrum (color neutral scalar) is of order g_phi^2/g^4, where g_phi is the effective coupling of the scalar to gluons.
- The first connected contribution to the phi two-point function starts at order g_phi^2/g^2.
- The center-of-mass energy considered for LHC heavy ion collisions is 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair, probing partons with momentum fraction x ~ 10^{-3} or lower.

## Context

This work extends the factorization proof for inclusive gluon spectra in the CGC framework, previously established by Gelis, Laidet, and Venugopalan (2008-2009), to inclusive spectra of scalar particles coupled to gluons. The result enables future extension to quark production, for which factorization had been assumed but not demonstrated, and confirms that the operator relationship between LO and NLO spectra is a general feature of inclusive observables in heavy ion collisions within the CGC effective theory.
