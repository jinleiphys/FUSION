# Jet Tomography of Hot and Cold Nuclear Matter

> Medium modification of parton fragmentation functions induced by multiple scattering and gluon bremsstrahlung is shown to describe the recent HERMES data in deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) very well, providing the first evidence of $A^{2/3}$-dependence of the modification. The energy loss is found to be $<dE/dL> \approx 0.5$ GeV/fm for a 10-GeV quark in a $Au$ nucleus. Including the effect of expansion, analysis of the $π^0$ spectra in central $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=130$ GeV yields an averaged energy loss equivalent to $<dE/dL> \approx 7.3$ GeV/fm in a static medium. Predictions for central $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV are also given.

# Jet Tomography of Hot and Cold Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

Medium modification of parton fragmentation functions from multiple scattering and gluon bremsstrahlung describes the HERMES deeply inelastic scattering data, providing the first evidence of an A^{2/3}-dependence of the modification; the extracted energy loss in cold nuclear matter is about 0.5 GeV/fm for a 10-GeV quark, while analysis of PHENIX pi0 spectra in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130 GeV yields an averaged energy loss equivalent to about 7.3 GeV/fm in a static medium when expansion effects are included.

## Method

The authors compute modified fragmentation functions using a pQCD approach with a systematic expansion of higher-twist corrections, including twist-4 contributions from double parton scattering and a dipole-like form factor that encodes Landau-Pomeranchuck-Migdal (LPM) interference. They fit a single free parameter, the quark-gluon correlation strength C-tilde(Q^2), to the HERMES data on nuclear modification ratios for N and Kr targets, then extend the formalism to hot QCD matter by assuming a one-dimensionally expanding gluon density profile and using an effective model where the modified fragmentation function is approximated by shifting the vacuum fragmentation function by a fractional energy loss Delta-z.

## Key numbers

- Energy loss in cold Au nucleus: dE/dL ≈ 0.5 GeV/fm for a 10-GeV quark, extracted from HERMES data with average x_B ≈ 0.124 and average path length L_A = R_A sqrt(2/pi) for a Gaussian nuclear distribution.
- Quark-gluon correlation strength from HERMES fit: C-tilde(Q^2) = 0.0060 GeV^2 at Q^2 ≈ 3 GeV^2 with alpha_s = 0.33.
- Equivalent static-medium energy loss from PHENIX pi0 data at sqrt(s)=130 GeV: (dE/dL)_0 ≈ 7.3 GeV/fm for a 10-GeV parton, obtained by scaling the extracted 1-d expanding loss (dE/dL)_1d ≈ 0.34 ln E / ln 5 GeV/fm by the factor R_A/(2 tau_0) with R_A ≈ 6 fm and tau_0 ≈ 0.2 fm.
- Nuclear broadening of Drell-Yan transverse momentum: <Delta q_perp^2> = 0.016 A^{1/3} GeV^2, giving C-tilde(M^2_{l l-bar}) = 0.013 GeV^2 at <M^2_{l l-bar}> ≈ 40 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist formalism for modified fragmentation functions developed by Guo and Wang (2001) and applies it to both cold nuclear matter (HERMES data) and hot dense matter (PHENIX data at RHIC). The extracted energy loss in cold matter is compared to the opacity expansion approach of Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, and Schiff (1997) and to the nuclear broadening of Drell-Yan dileptons measured by the E772 collaboration. The analysis of heavy-ion collisions builds on the effective model of Wang and Huang (1998) and provides predictions for pi0 spectra at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, enabling future tests of the extracted gluon density in the initial stage of Au+Au collisions.
