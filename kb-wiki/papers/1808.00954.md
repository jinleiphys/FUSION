# Semi-inclusive back-to-back production of a hadron pair and a single hadron in $e^+e^-$ annihilation

> Inclusive hadron production in $e^+e^-$ annihilation has long been used to study both single hadron fragmentation functions (FF) and dihadron fragmentation functions (DiFF). In particular, the polarized DiFFs can be accessed in electron-positron annihilation by measuring azimuthal correlations between two back-to-back pairs of hadrons in the center of mass system, where the relevant structure functions can be expressed as convolutions of two (polarized) DiFFs. Here we explore the advantages of measuring the inclusive back-to-back production of a single hadron on one side against a hadron pair on the opposite side of the detector in two jet events. The leading twist cross section for this process contains convolutions of the corresponding single hadron FFs on one side and the DiFFs for the hadron pair on the other side, which furnishes several interesting new opportunities. A measurement of the unpolarized cross section with a number of different types of observed hadrons will help in untangling the quark flavor dependence of the unpolarized DiFFs, when the results are analyzed together with the inclusive measurements of dihadron pairs, such as those recently performed by the $\texttt{BELLE}$ collaboration. Even more interesting, with a polarized hyperon on one side we can study the quark spin-dependent DiFFs of an unpolarized hadron pair on the other side. This, in turn, will allow us to test the universality of the spin-dependent DiFFs entering the cross sections of electron-positron annihilation and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering processes.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring semi-inclusive back-to-back production of a single hadron against a hadron pair in electron-positron annihilation can disentangle the quark flavor dependence of unpolarized dihadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs) and provide access to the sign of polarized DiFFs, which is not possible in the previously studied back-to-back hadron-pair measurements.

## Method

The authors derive the leading-twist cross section for the process \(e^+e^- \to (h_1 h_2) + \Lambda + X\) in the center-of-mass frame, using the quark-quark correlator formalism for both the dihadron fragmentation of one quark and the single-hadron fragmentation of the antiquark. They express the cross section as convolutions of single-hadron fragmentation functions (FFs) and DiFFs, then integrate over transverse momenta and azimuthal angles to obtain the collinear unpolarized cross section and weighted azimuthal asymmetries that isolate specific polarized DiFFs.

## Key numbers

- The unpolarized collinear cross section after integration over transverse momentum, relative azimuthal angle, and momentum fraction \(\xi\) is given by \(\frac{d\sigma}{dz\,dM_h^2\,d\bar{z}\,dy} = \frac{3\alpha_{em}^2}{(2\pi)^2 Q^2} A(y) \sum_a e_a^2 D_1^{a\to h_1h_2}(z, M_h^2) \bar{D}_1^{\bar{a}\to\Lambda}(\bar{z})\), where \(A(y) = 1/2 - y + y^2\).
- For \(\pi^+\pi^-\) pairs, the one-pair inclusive cross section scales as \(\frac{5}{9} D_1^{u\to\pi^+\pi^-}(z) + \frac{1}{9} D_1^{s\to\pi^+\pi^-}(z)\), while the associated production with a \(\pi^+\) on the opposite side scales as \(\frac{5}{9} D_1^{u\to\pi^+\pi^-}(z) D_1^{u^+\to\pi^+}(\bar{z}) + \frac{1}{9} D_1^{s\to\pi^+\pi^-}(z) D_1^{s^+\to\pi^+}(\bar{z})\).
- The Collins-effect weighted asymmetry for an unpolarized \(\Lambda\) is \(A^{Coll} = \frac{B(y)}{A(y)} \frac{\sum_a e_a^2 H_1^{\SA,a\to h_1h_2}(z, M_h^2) H_1^{\perp\bar{a},[1]}(\bar{z})}{\sum_a e_a^2 D_1^{a\to h_1h_2}(z, M_h^2) \bar{D}_1^{\bar{a}\to\Lambda}(\bar{z})}\), where \(B(y) = y(1-y)\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism for back-to-back hadron-pair production in electron-positron annihilation developed by Boer (2003) and Matevosyan (2018) to the mixed case of a single hadron opposite a hadron pair. It directly builds on the BELLE collaboration measurements of dihadron pair production (Seidl, 2017) and the proposed measurements of the helicity-dependent DiFF \(G_1^\perp\) in both SIDIS and electron-positron annihilation by Matevosyan (2017). The proposed measurements enable flavor separation of unpolarized DiFFs when combined with existing single-hadron FF extractions from de Florian (2014), Hirai (2016), Sato (2016), Ethier (2017), and Bertone (2017), and allow testing the universality of spin-dependent DiFFs between electron-positron annihilation and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering.
