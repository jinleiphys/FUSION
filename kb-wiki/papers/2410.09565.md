# Fission fragment distributions within time-dependent density functional theory

> A notable issue, the proper description of mass and charge distributions of fission fragments within nonadiabatic descriptions of fission dynamics, is investigated by performing double particle number projection on the outcomes of time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) simulation. The induced fission process of the benchmark nucleus 240Pu is studied. In the three-dimensional Cartesian coordinate without any symmetry restrictions, we get the static fission pathway from the two-dimensional potential energy surface, and then the fission dynamics from saddle to scission point are obtained using TDHF. We show that the charge numbers of primary heavy fragments from TDHF simulation strongly depend on the deformations of initial configurations via the two asymmetric fission channels, which can be distinguished according to the dynamical fission trajectories. The charge distribution of fission fragments is well reproduced using the double particle number projection technique. After applying the Gaussian kernel estimation based on the distribution from the double particle number projection technique, the mass distribution is also consistent with the experimental results. Besides, the results of the total kinetic energy of fission fragments are reasonably consistent with the experiments.

## Key claim

The authors show that the charge and mass distributions of fission fragments from TDHF simulations can be brought into agreement with experimental data only after applying double particle number projection and Gaussian kernel estimation, but the mass distribution from TDHF+FOA+PNP alone is not well reproduced and requires the additional Gaussian smoothing with a fitted width of sigma=5.1.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock with BCS pairing (CHF+BCS) to construct a two-dimensional potential energy surface in the (Q20, Q30) plane for 240Pu, then selected 15 initial configurations from this surface as starting points for time-dependent Hartree-Fock with frozen occupation approximation (TDHF+FOA) simulations. The fission dynamics from saddle to scission were evolved in a three-dimensional Cartesian grid without symmetry restrictions, and the resulting fragment properties were analyzed using double particle number projection (PNP) to obtain charge and mass distributions, with Gaussian kernel estimation (GKE) applied to improve the mass distribution.

## Key numbers

- The inner and outer fission barriers are located at Q20 approximately 21 b and Q20 approximately 43 b, respectively, with the primary barrier height about 10 MeV.
- The mass numbers of heavy fragments from the 15 trajectories are around A_H = 134 to 143, and charge numbers Z_H = 52 to 56.
- For the standard I (SI) channel, the mean mass number of heavy fragments is 135.2 with standard deviation 0.90; for the standard II (SII) channel, the mean is 142.4 with standard deviation 0.94.
- The Gaussian kernel estimation used sigma = 5.1 for mass distributions and sigma = 1.6 for charge distributions, obtained by fitting to experimental data.
- The calculated total kinetic energy (TKE) values differ from experimental data by about 10 MeV, with higher TKE (approximately 190 MeV) for the SI channel and lower TKE (approximately 175 MeV) for the SII channel.

## Context

This work extends previous TDHF studies of 240Pu fission by Goddard (2015), Bulgac (2016, 2019), and Scamps (2018) by implementing double particle number projection to address the long-standing problem of reproducing fragment distributions within non-adiabatic descriptions. The results are compared with experimental data from Wagemans (1984), Schillebeeckx (1992), and Nishio (1995), as well as the ENDF/B-VIII.0 and JEFF-3.3 libraries. The approach enables future systematic studies of fission dynamics by combining TDHF with proper treatment of pairing correlations and trajectory weighting, potentially using WKB approximation or Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling based on the potential energy surface.
