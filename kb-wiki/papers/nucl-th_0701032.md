# Causal Viscous Hydrodynamics for Central Heavy-Ion Collisions II: Meson Spectra and HBT Radii

> Causal viscous hydrodynamic fits to experimental data for pion and kaon transverse momentum spectra from central Au+Au collisions at \sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 GeV are presented. Starting the hydrodynamic evolution at 1 fm/c and using small values for the relaxation time, reasonable fits up to moderate ratios η/s\simeq 0.4 can be obtained. It is found that a percentage of roughly 50 η/s to 75 η/s of the final meson multiplicity is due to viscous entropy production. Finally, it is shown that with increasing viscosity, the ratio of HBT radii R_{out}/R_{side} approaches and eventually matches the experimental data.

## Key claim

Causal viscous hydrodynamics can fit pion and kaon transverse momentum spectra from central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV for shear viscosity to entropy density ratios up to eta/s ~ 0.4, but only when using a smaller relaxation time than the weak-coupling QCD value; the bound on eta/s depends on the a priori unconstrained relaxation time parameter.

## Method

The causal viscous hydrodynamic equations of Israel-Stewart type were solved numerically on a 512-site lattice with spacing 0.25 GeV^-1, starting at tau_0 = 1 fm/c with initial energy density from a Glauber wounded-nucleon profile and the Laine-Schroeder crossover equation of state. Particle spectra were computed via the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription at temperature T_f, including resonance decays up to 2 GeV mass, and HBT radii were extracted from the two-particle correlation function using the Bertsch-Pratt parameterization.

## Key numbers

- Reasonable fits to pion and kaon spectra are obtained up to eta/s ~ 0.25 for relaxation time tau_Pi = (eta/s)*(6/T) (weak-coupling QCD value)
- Reasonable fits are obtained up to eta/s ~ 0.4 for the smaller relaxation time tau_Pi = (eta/s)*(1.5/T)
- Initial central temperatures used: T_0 = 0.34, 0.33, 0.33 GeV for eta/s = 0.08, 0.16, 0.24 (tau_Pi = (eta/s)*(6/T)); T_0 = 0.34, 0.32, 0.31 GeV for eta/s = 0.16, 0.32, 0.40 (tau_Pi = (eta/s)*(1.5/T))
- Viscous entropy production increases final multiplicities by a fraction of ~0.75*(eta/s) relative to ideal hydrodynamics; specifically, for eta/s = 0.40 the ratio of viscous to ideal pion multiplicity is 1.28
- The freeze-out temperature upper limit was imposed at T_f <= 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends causal viscous hydrodynamic calculations (Baier 2006, Muronga 2004) to include meson spectra and HBT radii from central Au+Au collisions, addressing the "HBT puzzle" where ideal hydrodynamics produces R_out/R_side ratios far above experimental data (Dumitru 2002, Teaney). The results show that increasing viscosity brings R_out/R_side closer to STAR data, but absolute values of R_out and R_side remain below experiment, suggesting that continuous emission freeze-out (Grassi 2000, Sinyukov 2002) or other effects may be needed. The bound eta/s <= 0.4 lies between weak-coupling QCD results and the strong-coupling N=4 SYM result (Kovtun 2004), indicating the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC may be a border-case between weak and strong coupling.
