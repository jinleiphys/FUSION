# Theory Support for the Excited Baryon Program at the Jlab 12 GeV Upgrade

> This document outlines major directions in theoretical support for the measurement of nucleon resonance transition form factors at the JLab 12 GeV upgrade with the CLAS12 detector. Using single and double meson production, prominent resonances in the mass range up to 2 GeV will be studied in the range of photon virtuality $Q^2$ up to 12 GeV$^2$ where quark degrees of freedom are expected to dominate. High level theoretical analysis of these data will open up opportunities to understand how the interactions of dressed quarks create the ground and excited nucleon states and how these interactions emerge from QCD. The paper reviews the current status and the prospects of QCD based model approaches that relate phenomenological information on transition form factors to the non-perturbative strong interaction mechanisms, that are responsible for resonance formation.

## Key claim

Precision measurements of nucleon-resonance transition form factors at the JLab 12 GeV upgrade can be used to chart the momentum evolution of the dressed-quark mass function and thereby map the infrared behavior of QCD's universal beta-function, provided that the transition boundary between constituent-quark and current-quark domains lies at momentum scales accessible with Q² up to 12 GeV².

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches including Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) solved in a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev framework that treats baryons as bound states of dressed quarks correlated as diquarks, generalized parton distributions (GPDs) applied to resonance transitions, and light-cone sum rules (LCSRs) that express form factors in terms of distribution amplitudes. These methods are used to compute nucleon elastic form factors and transition form factors for the N→Δ(1232), N→P₁₁(1440), and N→S₁₁(1535) channels, with the goal of extracting the dressed-quark mass function from experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The dressed-quark mass function shows a sharp drop at p² ∼ 0.6 GeV², marking the transition between constituent-quark and current-quark domains.
- To cover the momentum domain p² ∈ [0.5, 1.0] GeV² in the dressed-quark mass function, one requires Q² ∈ [5, 10] GeV² for the probe, based on each dressed quark absorbing a momentum fraction Q/3.
- The nucleon mass obtained from the Faddeev equation at current-quark mass m̂ = 6.1 MeV is M_N = 1.26(2) GeV, compared with lattice-QCD results.
- The pion mass at the same current-quark mass is m_π = 0.138 GeV.
- The DSE-based Faddeev equation calculation of nucleon form factors shows that at Q² ≈ 2–3 M_N² the difference between calculation and data is an order of magnitude smaller than in confining NJL model calculations.
- The N→Δ M1 transition form factor G_M at Q²=0 is empirically 3, but confining-NJL model calculations miss significant strength near Q²=0.

## Context

This white paper outlines the theoretical program supporting the CLAS12 excited baryon program at the JLab 12 GeV upgrade, extending DSE-based studies of nucleon structure by Cloet, Roberts, and collaborators (2008-2009) and GPD-based approaches by Frankfurt, Goeke, and Stoler (2000s). It builds on prior DSE work by Maris, Roberts, and Tandy (1997-2000) on meson properties and by Eichmann and collaborators (2008) on unified meson-baryon frameworks. The program enables future extraction of the dressed-quark mass function from transition form factor data, identification of the transition boundary between constituent and current-quark domains, and determination of whether the Roper resonance N(1440) is a radial excitation of the nucleon.
