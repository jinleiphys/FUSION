# Gamow-Teller sum rule in relativistic nuclear models

> Relativistic corrections are investigated to the Gamow-Teller(GT) sum rule with respect to the difference between the $β_-$ and $β_+$ transition strengths in nuclei. Since the sum rule requires the complete set of the nuclear states, the relativistic corrections come from the anti-nucleon degrees of freedom. In the relativistic mean field approximation, the total GT strengths carried by the nucleon sector is quenched by about 12% in nuclear matter, while by about 8% in finite nuclei, compared to the sum rule value. The coupling between the particle-hole states with the nucleon-antinucleon states is also discussed with the relativistic random phase approximation, where the divergence of the response function is renormalized with use of the counter terms in the Lagrangian. It is shown that the approximation to neglect the divergence, like the no-sea approximation extensively used so far, is unphysical, from the sum-rule point of view.

# Gamow-Teller sum rule in relativistic nuclear models

## Key claim
Relativistic corrections from anti-nucleon degrees of freedom quench the Gamow-Teller sum rule value carried by nucleon particle-hole states by about 12% in nuclear matter and about 8% in finite nuclei, but when the coupling between particle-hole and nucleon-antinucleon states is properly renormalized in the relativistic random phase approximation, the quenching is almost entirely canceled, making the total strength nearly equal to the sum rule value.

## Method
The authors calculate the Gamow-Teller sum rule in the relativistic mean field approximation for both nuclear matter and finite nuclei, using the effective nucleon mass from relativistic models (NL-SH and NL3 parameter sets). They then extend the analysis to the relativistic random phase approximation (RPA) for nuclear matter, introducing relativistic Landau-Migdal parameters for the particle-hole interaction and employing n-dimensional regularization with counter terms in the Lagrangian to renormalize the divergent response function from nucleon-antinucleon excitations.

## Key numbers
- The total GT strength carried by the nucleon sector is quenched by about 12% in nuclear matter compared to the sum rule value, using effective mass M* = 0.6M and Fermi momentum k_F = 1.36 fm^{-1}
- The quenching is about 6.3% in ^40Ca, 7.7% in ^90Zr, and 8.4% in ^208Pb using the NL3 parameter set
- The renormalized coupling constant κ'(nbar)_ren is approximately -4π M^2 * (2/3) * (1 - M*/M)^3 - (16π/15) * k_F^2 * v_F^3, which is negative for M* < M
- Using g' = 0.6, M* = 0.7306M, and k_F = 1.3 fm^{-1}, the renormalized RPA gives S_- ≈ 2(N-Z), nearly canceling the Hartree-level quenching

## Context
This work extends earlier non-relativistic treatments of the Gamow-Teller sum rule (Osterfeld, 1992) and addresses the role of Δ-isobar degrees of freedom studied by Bentz (2001) and Suzuki (1995). The authors show that the no-sea approximation (Furnstahl, 1997), used extensively in previous relativistic calculations (Kurasawa, 1995; Ma, 2001; Maruyama, 2002), is unphysical because it neglects divergent terms and artificially converts repulsive coupling into attractive coupling. The renormalization procedure enables consistent treatment of nucleon-antinucleon contributions and suggests that 8% is the upper limit of relativistic corrections in finite nuclei, which is relevant for interpreting experimental quenching observed in ^90Zr (Yako, 2005).
