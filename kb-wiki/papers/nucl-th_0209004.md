# η-Scaling of dN_{ch}/dηat sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200 GeV by the PHOBOS Collaboration and the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck stochastic process

> Using the latest data concerning $dN_{\rm ch}/dη$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, we have analyzed them by means of stochastic theory named the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process with two sources. Moreover, we display that $z_r = η/\sqrt{< η^2>} = η/η_{rms}$ scaling with centrality cuts are described by two Gaussian distributions.

## Key claim
The PHOBOS data at 200 GeV show that the charged-particle pseudorapidity density per participant pair, when scaled by the rms width, is nearly indistinguishable from the same scaled distribution at 130 GeV, except for the region \(|z_r| \gtrsim 2.2\). The dip structure in the central region cannot be explained by the Jacobian from a single Gaussian rapidity distribution alone when the full pseudorapidity range is considered.

## Method
The authors fit the PHOBOS \(dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta\) data for six centrality bins at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV using a two-source Ornstein-Uhlenbeck (OU) stochastic process. The probability density evolves from two initial sources at \(\pm y_{\rm max} = \pm \ln(\sqrt{s_{NN}}/m_N)\) with a frictional coefficient \(\gamma\) and variance \(\sigma^2\). They also test a power-law scaling of the central density with participant number and compare the scaled variable \(z_r = \eta/\eta_{\rm rms}\) between 200 GeV and 130 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Intercepts \(dn/d\eta|_{\eta=0}\) for centrality bins 35–45% to 0–6% lie in the range \(0.123 \pm 0.012\) to \(0.130 \pm 0.012\).
- Power-law fit to \((0.5\langle N_{\rm part}\rangle)^{-1} dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta|_{\eta=0}\) yields \(A = 2.05\) and \(\alpha = 0.101\) at 200 GeV (correlation coefficient 0.991); at 130 GeV, \(A = 1.79\), \(\alpha = 0.103\) (correlation coefficient 0.993).
- For the OU fit, the parameter \(p = 1 - e^{-2\gamma t}\) ranges from \(0.855 \pm 0.006\) (45–55% centrality) to \(0.876 \pm 0.006\) (0–6% centrality); \(V^2(t)\) ranges from \(3.62 \pm 0.26\) to \(2.79 \pm 0.13\).
- \(\eta_{\rm rms}\) values decrease from \(2.57\) (45–55%) to \(2.42\) (0–6%).
- For the scaled distribution \(dn/dz_r\) at 200 GeV: \(p = 0.867 \pm 0.002\), \(V_r^2(t) = 0.506 \pm 0.011\), \(\chi^2/{\rm n.d.f.} = 14.6/321\); at 130 GeV: \(p = 0.854 \pm 0.002\), \(V_r^2(t) = 0.494 \pm 0.010\), \(\chi^2/{\rm n.d.f.} = 25.5/321\).
- Assuming an interaction region size of 10 fm, the frictional coefficient \(\gamma\) averages \(0.101\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(\sigma^2\) averages \(0.744\) fm\(^{-1}\) across centralities.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis of PHOBOS data at 130 GeV (Biyajima 2002) to the new 200 GeV data from Nouicer (2002). It contrasts the OU stochastic approach with the Jacobian-based single-Gaussian method used by Kharzeev (2001) and Eskola (2002), showing that the Jacobian alone cannot reproduce the dip in the full pseudorapidity range. The \(z_r\) scaling result enables a direct comparison of the shape of the pseudorapidity distribution across collision energies, suggesting that the underlying particle-production mechanism is largely energy-independent in this range.
