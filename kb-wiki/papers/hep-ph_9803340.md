# Isospin Mass Splittings of Baryons in Potential Models

> We discuss the isospin-breaking mass differences among baryons, with particular attention in the charm sector to the $Σ_c^{+}-Σ_c^0$, $Σ_c^{++}-Σ_c^0$, and $Ξ_c^+-Ξ_c^0$ splittings. Simple potential models cannot accommodate the trend of the available data on charmed baryons. More precise measurements would offer the possibility of testing how well potential models describe the non-perturbative limit of QCD.

# Isospin Mass Splittings of Baryons in Potential Models

## Key claim

Non-relativistic potential models, despite successfully reproducing isospin splittings for light baryons, cannot accommodate the trend of available data on charmed baryon splittings, particularly the $\Sigma_c^{+}-\Sigma_c^{0}$ and $\Xi_c^{+}-\Xi_c^{0}$ mass differences, even when electrostatic interactions, quark mass differences, dipole-dipole terms, three-body forces, instanton effects, and running coupling are included.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Schrödinger equation for baryons using two reliable numerical methods (variational and perturbative) with three different potential models (BCN, AL1, AP1), each containing a linear confining term, a Coulomb term, and a smeared spin-spin interaction. They introduced isospin breaking by setting $m_u \neq m_d$, adjusting the mass difference $\Delta m = m_d - m_u$ to reproduce the neutron-proton and $\Sigma^{-} - \Sigma^{+}$ splittings, and added electrostatic Coulomb interactions between quarks as well as magnetic dipole-dipole terms.

## Key numbers

- $\Delta m = m_d - m_u$ values used: 11 MeV for AL1 ($m_u=327$ MeV, $m_d=338$ MeV), 16.85 MeV for BCN ($m_u=337$ MeV, $m_d=353.85$ MeV), and 23.5 MeV for AP1 ($m_u=277$ MeV, $m_d=300.5$ MeV)
- Experimental $\Sigma_c^{++} - \Sigma_c^{0} = 0.8 \pm 0.4$ MeV is well reproduced by models, but experimental $\Sigma_c^{+} - \Sigma_c^{0} = 1.4 \pm 0.6$ MeV disagrees with model predictions of approximately $-0.5$ to $-0.7$ MeV
- Experimental $\Xi_c^{+} - \Xi_c^{0} = 6.3 \pm 2.3$ MeV (PDG average) or $4.7 \pm 2.1$ MeV (PDG fit) compared to model prediction of 2.2 MeV (AL1) and 1.5 MeV with three-body term included
- Preliminary CLEO result for $\Xi_c^{0} - \Xi_c^{+} = 2.5 \pm 1.7 \pm 1.1$ MeV and for $\Xi'_c^{0} - \Xi'_c^{+} \simeq 1.7$ MeV with large error bar
- Constituent quark masses used in AL1 model: $m_q=315$ MeV, $m_s=577$ MeV, $m_c=1836$ MeV, $m_b=5227$ GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of isospin-violating mass differences by Miyazawa and Yonezawa (MiY) and calculations by Wright (Wright), Deshpande et al. (Desh), Itoh (Itoh), Ono (Ono), Chan (Chan), Lichtenberg and Wills (LW), Donoghue (Don), Kalman (Kalman), Capstick (capstik), Isgur (Isgur), and others, which had already shown a wide spread of predictions for charmed baryon splittings. The authors demonstrate that even with accurate three-body solutions and inclusion of all known contributions, potential models fail to reproduce the ordering of $\Sigma_c$ states, confirming Franklin's (Franklin) observation that experimental data violate mass relations expected to hold in quark models. The results motivate searching for new dynamical contributions such as electromagnetic penguins (Penguins) or alternative approaches like meson-exchange models (Gloz, Gloz2), and call for more precise experimental measurements to clarify whether the discrepancy persists.
