# Pseudo-spin symmetry in density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock theory

> The pseudo-spin symmetry (PSS) is investigated in the density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock theory by taking {the} doubly magic nucleus $^{132}$Sn as a representative. It is found that the Fock terms bring significant contributions to the pseudo-spin orbital potentials (PSOP) and make it comparable to the pseudo-centrifugal barrier (PCB). However, these Fock terms in the PSOP are counteracted by other exchange terms due to the non-locality of the exchange potentials. The pseudo-spin orbital splitting indicates that the PSS is preserved well for the partner states $\lrb{ν3s_{1/2}, \nu2d_{3/2}}$ of $^{132}$Sn in the relativistic Hartree-Fock theory.

## Key claim

The pseudo-spin symmetry (PSS) is preserved as a good symmetry for the partner states (ν3s₁/₂, ν2d₃/₂) in ¹³²Sn within density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory, despite the Fock terms bringing substantial contributions to the pseudo-spin orbital potential (PSOP); these contributions are cancelled by other exchange terms arising from the non-locality of the exchange potentials.

## Method

The authors derived the pseudo-spin orbital potential (PSOP) by transforming the coupled radial Dirac equations of DDRHF theory into a Schrödinger-type equation for the lower component, explicitly including the non-local Fock terms. They then calculated neutron single-particle energies and pseudo-spin orbital splittings for the doubly magic nucleus ¹³²Sn using the DDRHF effective Lagrangian PKO1, and compared the results with those from the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with PKDD. The contributions of the Fock terms to the PSOP and the pseudo-centrifugal barrier (PCB) were analyzed by decomposing the exchange potentials and examining their radial dependence relative to the Dirac wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-spin orbital splitting ΔE_PSO for the partner (ν3s₁/₂, ν2d₃/₂) in ¹³²Sn is more than ten times smaller than that for (ν2s₁/₂, ν1d₃/₂), as shown in both DDRHF and RMF calculations.
- Single-particle energies for ν3s₁/₂ and ν2d₃/₂ in DDRHF with PKO1 are -8.33 MeV and -8.66 MeV, respectively, giving a splitting of 0.33 MeV.
- Single-particle energies for ν3s₁/₂ and ν2d₃/₂ in RMF with PKDD are -8.15 MeV and -8.44 MeV, respectively, giving a splitting of 0.29 MeV.
- The PCB contribution to the single-particle energy for ν3s₁/₂ is 11.11 MeV and for ν2d₃/₂ is 11.32 MeV in DDRHF; the exchange term contribution (ĉV^E) is 18.22 MeV for ν3s₁/₂ and 18.17 MeV for ν2d₃/₂.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of pseudo-spin symmetry from the relativistic mean field (RMF) framework, where it was interpreted by Ginocchio (1997) and Meng (1998) as arising from cancellation between scalar and vector potentials, to the more complete density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory. The study uses the DDRHF Lagrangian PKO1 developed by Long (2006) and compares with the RMF model PKDD from Meng (2006). The results show that the inclusion of Fock terms does not spoil the PSS, enabling future applications of DDRHF to study pseudo-spin symmetry in exotic nuclei where exchange effects may be more important.
