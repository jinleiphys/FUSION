# Gravitons and Pions

> Both gravitons and pions are described by non-linear and non-renormalizable actions at low energies. These are most usefully treated by effective field theory, which is a full quantum field theoretic approach that relies only on the low energy degrees of freedom and their interactions. The gravitational case is particularly clean because of the masslessness of the graviton and the wide separation of scales. This essay provides an overview of this approach.

# Gravitons and Pions

## Key claim
Effective field theory (EFT) provides a controlled and rigorous quantum field theoretic treatment of both gravity and pion physics, but the gravitational case is "exceptionally clean" because the graviton is massless, whereas the pion mass introduces additional scales that complicate nuclear physics. The quantum corrections to the Newtonian potential and light bending angle are finite, universal low-energy theorems that cannot be modified by any unknown high-energy physics.

## Method
The paper reviews the effective field theory approach to quantum general relativity, showing that the Einstein-Hilbert action (with its two-derivative structure and dimensional coupling constant κ² = 32πG) yields a non-renormalizable but perfectly quantizable theory. The key insight is that non-analytic terms in momentum space (powers of √q² and log q²) arise from low-energy graviton propagation and are reliably calculable, while local analytic terms from unknown high-energy physics cannot mimic these non-local effects. The same EFT logic is applied to an imaginary world of massless pions, where the chiral Lagrangian yields a calculable internucleon potential.

## Key numbers
- The quantum correction to the Newtonian potential is given by the final term in V(r) = -(G m₁ m₂)/r [1 + 3G(m₁+m₂)/r + (41/(10π)) (Gℏ)/r²], where the quantum term is finite and independent of any parameters.
- The bending angle of light by a large mass M is θ = 4GM/b + (15/4) G²M²π/b² + (8 bu^η - 47 + 64 log(2r₀/b))/π · G²ℏM/b³, where bu^η takes values (371/120, 113/120, -29/8) for (scalar, photon, graviton) respectively.
- In the massless-pion world, the scalar-isoscalar central potential is V_S(r) = -300 MeV [(r₀/r)⁶ + 0.24 (r₀/r)⁷] with r₀ = 1 fm = (200 MeV)⁻¹.
- The isospin tensor potential is V_T(r) = -29 MeV [(r₀/r)³ - 0.66 (r₀/r)⁶].
- Quantum gravity corrections are estimated to be of order 10⁻⁵⁰ to 10⁻¹⁰⁰ compared to the leading classical term.

## Context
This essay extends the effective field theory philosophy pioneered in chiral perturbation theory (Donoghue 1992) and gravitational EFT (Donoghue 1994) to argue that quantum general relativity is "the best perturbative theory ever seen" rather than incompatible with quantum mechanics. The work contrasts with pre-EFT hand-wringing about non-renormalizability and builds on the quantization of GR by Feynman (1963) and DeWitt (1967), as well as one-loop divergence calculations by 't Hooft (1974) and two-loop results by Goroff (1985). The massless-pion thought experiment extends the author's own earlier work (Donoghue 2006) on the nuclear force in the chiral limit and suggests a path to interpolate between the chiral limit and physical pion mass using lattice QCD calculations of mass-dependent parameters like g_A.
