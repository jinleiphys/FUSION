# Core-crust transition properties of neutron stars within systematically varied extended relativistic mean-field model

> The model dependence and the symmetry energy dependence of the core-crust transition properties for the neutron stars are studied using three different families of systematically varied extended relativistic mean field model. Several forces within each of the families are so considered that they yield wide variations in the values of the nuclear symmetry energy $a_{\rm sym}$ and its slope parameter $L$ at the saturation density. The core-crust transition density is calculated using a method based on random-phase-approximation. The core-crust transition density is strongly correlated, in a model independent manner, with the symmetry energy slope parameter evaluated at the saturation density. The pressure at the transition point dose not show any meaningful correlations with the symmetry energy parameters at the saturation density. At best, pressure at the transition point is correlated with the symmetry energy parameters and their linear combination evaluated at the some sub-saturation density. Yet, such correlations might not be model independent. The correlations of core-crust transition properties with the symmetry energy parameter are also studied by varying the symmetry energy within a single model. The pressure at the transition point is correlated once again with the symmetry energy parameter at the sub-saturation density.

## Key claim
The core-crust transition density is strongly correlated with the symmetry energy slope parameter L at saturation density in a model-independent manner, but the pressure at the transition point shows no meaningful correlation with symmetry energy parameters at saturation density; any apparent correlation of pressure with symmetry energy parameters at sub-saturation densities may not be model independent.

## Method
The authors used three families of extended relativistic mean-field (ERMF) models, each with seven parameter sets (BSR1-BSR21) systematically varied to yield wide ranges of the symmetry energy coefficient a_sym and its slope L at saturation density, while keeping symmetric nuclear matter properties nearly fixed. The core-crust transition density was calculated using the relativistic random-phase-approximation (RPA) method based on the longitudinal dielectric function condition. For comparison, results were also obtained for standard RMF parameter sets NL3, FSU, TM1, and GM1, and compared with dynamical and thermo-dynamical methods from prior work.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density for all BSR forces: ρ₀ = 0.148 ± 0.003 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per nucleon for symmetric nuclear matter: B/A = 16.11 ± 0.04 MeV
- Nuclear matter incompressibility: K = 230.24 ± 9.80 MeV
- Nucleon effective mass: M*/M = 0.605 ± 0.004
- Neutron-skin thickness in ²⁰⁸Pb varied over range 0.16 – 0.28 fm across the parameter sets
- Maximum neutron star mass for the three families: 1.7 – 2.4 M⊙
- Variance of a_sym(ρ) has minimum at ρ ≈ 0.08 fm⁻³
- Correlation coefficient C(P_t, L − α K_sym) peaks at ρ = 0.09 fm⁻³ for α = 1.31
- Correlation coefficient C(P_t, −K_sym) peaks at ρ = 0.1 fm⁻³

## Context
This work extends studies of core-crust transition properties by Ducoin et al. (2011), Xu et al. (2009), Moustakidis et al. (2010), and Fattoyev and Piekarewicz (2012), which found conflicting results on whether pressure at transition correlates with symmetry energy parameters. The authors use systematically varied ERMF models with σ-ρ and ω-ρ mixed interaction terms not previously studied in detail for these correlations. The results enable better understanding of how model dependence affects predictions for neutron star crust properties, pulsar glitches, and crust relaxation phenomena.
