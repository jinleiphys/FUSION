# In-medium similarity renormalization group for a pairing-plus-particle-hole model

> We benchmark two implementations of the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) method, IMSRG(2) and IMSRG(2*), for the low-lying states of a pairing-plus-particle-hole model with varying numbers of fermions. In IMSRG(2), all operators are truncated up to the normal-ordered two-body terms, whereas IMSRG(2*) includes an additional term to partially account for higher-body contributions. The results are compared against exact solutions. We find that IMSRG(2*) consistently outperforms IMSRG(2) for both ground and excited states, although achieving convergence for excited states remains more challenging in strongly correlated systems than for the ground state.

## Key claim

IMSRG(2*) consistently outperforms IMSRG(2) for both ground and excited states of the pairing-plus-particle-hole model, but achieving convergence for excited states remains more challenging than for the ground state in strongly correlated systems.

## Method

The authors benchmark IMSRG(2) and IMSRG(2*) against exact configuration-interaction diagonalization for a pairing-plus-particle-hole (P3H) Hamiltonian with single-particle model space L=4 and particle numbers N=2, 4, and 6. IMSRG(2) truncates all operators at the normal-ordered two-body level, while IMSRG(2*) incorporates an auxiliary normal-ordered one-body operator chi(s) into the flow equation to partially account for higher-body contributions while maintaining the same O(N^6) computational scaling. The White generator is used primarily, with automatic switching to its arc-tangent variant or the imaginary-time generator when singularities arise.

## Key numbers

- For N=4 with g=1.4 and b=0, the discrepancy between IMSRG(2) and exact results is noticeably larger than for N=2 and N=6, with IMSRG(2*) reducing discrepancies for the k=1 state by 1-2 orders of magnitude
- For the k=2 state with N=4, IMSRG(2*) improvement appears generally in regions with g>1
- The energy difference between results obtained with the White generator and the imaginary-time generator is typically within 1%
- IMSRG(2*) closely reproduces exact results across model spaces L=4, 6, and 8 for N=4, while IMSRG(2) significantly deviates at stronger interactions and larger model spaces

## Context

This work extends previous IMSRG benchmarking studies that focused primarily on ground states (Hergert 2016, Stroberg 2024) by systematically investigating low-lying excited states in a solvable model. The P3H model allows direct comparison with exact solutions, complementing earlier benchmarks against the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model and carbon isotopes (Stroberg 2024). The results enable future extension to multi-reference IMSRG(2*) frameworks based on particle-number-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov reference states for realistic applications to open-shell nuclei.
