# The reaction $Δ+N\to N+N+ϕ$ in ion-ion collisions

> We study the threshold $ϕ$-meson production in the process $Δ+N\to N+N+ϕ$, which appears as a possible important mechanism in high energy nuclei-nuclei collisions. The isotopic invariance of the strong interaction and the selection rules due to P-parity and total angular momentum result in a general and model independent parametrization of the spin structure of the matrix element in terms of three partial amplitudes. In the framework of one-pion exchange model these amplitudes can be derived in terms of the two threshold partial amplitudes for the process $π+N\to N+ϕ$. We predict the ratio of cross sections for $ϕ-$meson production in $pp$- and $ΔN$-collisions and the polarization properties of the $ϕ$-meson, in $Δ+N\to N+N+ϕ$, as a function of a single parameter, which characterizes the relative role of transversal and longitudinal $ϕ$-meson polarizations in the process $π+N\to N+ϕ$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the reaction Δ+N→N+N+φ may be a non-negligible mechanism for φ-meson production in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions, particularly at bombarding energies around 1 GeV/A where about 1/3 of baryons are excited to Δ-resonance states; however, this claim is conditional on the specific one-pion exchange model and the unknown value of a single parameter x that characterizes the relative role of transversal and longitudinal φ-meson polarizations in the subprocess π+N→N+φ.

## Method

The authors first establish a model-independent parametrization of the threshold matrix element for Δ+N→N+N+φ in terms of three partial amplitudes (f₁, f₂, f₃), using isotopic invariance, P-parity conservation, and total angular momentum selection rules. They then apply a one-pion exchange model where the amplitudes are derived from the two threshold partial amplitudes (h₁, h₂) of the subprocess π+N→N+φ, using the ΔNπ coupling constant and Fierz transformations to relate the two sets of amplitudes. The ratio of cross sections between ΔN and pp collisions and the polarization properties of the φ-meson are expressed as functions of a single complex parameter x = h₂/h₁.

## Key numbers

- The threshold total invariant energy for Δ+N→N+N+φ is W_th = 2m + m_φ, where m is the nucleon mass and m_φ is the φ-meson mass.
- The Δ decay width Γ(Δ→Nπ) ≈ 120 MeV is used, with g²_πNN/4π = 15, leading to the estimate that the cross section ratio R = σ(ΔN→NNφ)/σ(pp→ppφ) ≥ 1.
- The threshold value of the momentum transfer squared for the ΔN process is given by t = -mm_φ + (M²-m²)(m_φ+m)/(m_φ+2m), where M is the Δ mass.
- The ratio (t-m_π²)/(mm_φ+m_π²)² ≈ 4, indicating the pion pole is closer to the physical region for ΔN collisions than for pp collisions.
- For the ρ-exchange model, the parameter x = -1, giving the minimal value for the cross section ratio R and a sin²θ angular dependence for φ→K+K⁻ decay products.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework previously developed for φ-meson production in NN collisions (Rekalo, Arvieux, and Tomasi-Gustafsson, 1997) to the ΔN channel, motivated by the suggestion of Shor (1985) that enhanced φ/ω ratios in heavy ion collisions could signal quark-gluon plasma formation, as studied experimentally by the NA38 Collaboration (Baglin et al., 1991). The paper builds on earlier work by Peters, Mosel, and Engel (1996) who considered ΔN reactions for η-meson production, and enables future experimental tests of the OZI rule and hidden strangeness in nucleons through polarization measurements of φ-mesons produced in nuclear collisions.
