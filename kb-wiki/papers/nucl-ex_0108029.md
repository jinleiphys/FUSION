# Event-by-Event Fluctuations in Particle Multiplicities and Transverse Energy Produced in 158.A GeV Pb+Pb collisions

> Event-by-event fluctuations in the multiplicities of charged particles and photons, and the total transverse energy in 158$\cdot A$ GeV Pb+Pb collisions are studied for a wide range of centralities. For narrow centrality bins the multiplicity and transverse energy distributions are found to be near perfect Gaussians. The effect of detector acceptance on the multiplicity fluctuations has been studied and demonstrated to follow statistical considerations. The centrality dependence of the charged particle multiplicity fluctuations in the measured data has been found to agree reasonably well with those obtained from a participant model. However for photons the multiplicity fluctuations has been found to be lower compared to those obtained from a participant model. The multiplicity and transverse energy fluctuations have also been compared to those obtained from the VENUS event generator.

## Key claim

Event-by-event fluctuations in charged particle and photon multiplicities, and total transverse energy, in 158 A GeV Pb+Pb collisions are studied over a wide centrality range; the centrality dependence of charged particle multiplicity fluctuations agrees reasonably well with a participant model, but photon multiplicity fluctuations are found to be lower than those obtained from the same participant model.

## Method

The WA98 experiment at the CERN SPS used the silicon pad multiplicity detector (SPMD) for charged particles in the pseudorapidity range 2.35 ≤ η ≤ 3.75, the preshower photon multiplicity detector (PMD) for photons in 2.9 ≤ η ≤ 4.2, and the MIRAC calorimeter for transverse energy in 3.5 ≤ η ≤ 5.5. Centrality was selected using narrow bins of 2% width in the minimum-bias cross section based on total transverse energy or forward energy, and multiplicity distributions were fitted to Gaussians to extract mean and variance for calculating relative fluctuations ω = σ²/⟨X⟩.

## Key numbers

- For the common coverage region (2.9 ≤ η ≤ 3.75), the relative fluctuation for γ-like clusters is constant over the full centrality range with an average value of 2.2 ± 0.21.
- For charged particles in the common coverage, the relative fluctuation is 1.56 ± 0.13 at the most central bin (0-2%) and increases to 2.8 ± 0.16 for the least central bin (62-64%).
- The photon counting efficiency for central to peripheral events varied from 68% to 73%, and the purity of the photon sample varied from 65% to 54%.
- The MIRAC calorimeter had an energy resolution of 17.9%/√E for electromagnetic energy and 46.1%/√E for hadronic energy (E in GeV).
- The average charged particle multiplicity in nucleon-nucleon collisions at this energy is 7.2, and the average photon multiplicity is 6.3, as parametrized from data in the literature.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of event-by-event fluctuations at the SPS, such as the near-perfect Gaussian distributions of mean transverse momentum and particle ratios reported by NA49, and provides a systematic comparison to a participant model following the framework of Stephanov et al. and Heiselberg et al. The results enable future searches for fluctuations from QCD phase transitions or disoriented chiral condensates, and the demonstration that narrow centrality bins (2% width) minimize impact parameter fluctuations provides a methodological basis for subsequent fluctuation analyses at SPS and RHIC energies.
