# MagnetoHydrodynamics with chiral anomaly: phases of collective excitations and instabilities

> We study the relativistic hydrodynamics with chiral anomaly and dynamical electromagnetic fields, namely Chiral MagnetoHydroDynamics (CMHD). We formulate CMHD as a low-energy effective theory based on a generalized derivative expansion. We demonstrate that the modification of ordinary MagnetoHydroDynamics (MHD) due to chiral anomaly can be obtained from the second law of thermodynamics and is tied to chiral magnetic effect. We further study the real-time properties of chiral fluid by solving linearized CMHD equations. We discover a remarkable "transition" at an intermediate axial chemical potential $μ_{A}$ between a stable Chiral fluid at low $μ_{A}$ and an unstable Chiral fluid at large $μ_{A}$. We summarize this transition in a "phase diagram" in terms of $μ_{A}$ and the angle of the wavevector relative to the magnetic field. In the unstable regime, there are four collective modes carrying both magnetic and fluid helicity, in contrary to MHD waves which are unpolarized. The half of the helical modes grow exponentially in time, indicating the instability, while the other half become dissipative.

## Key claim

The paper claims a "transition" at an intermediate axial chemical potential μA between a stable chiral fluid at low μA and an unstable chiral fluid at large μA, but this transition is conditional on the presence of a small anomaly coefficient CA that serves as an additional expansion parameter alongside the gradient expansion, and the stability at low μA holds for generic angles between the wavevector and magnetic field except degenerate cases.

## Method

The authors formulate Chiral MagnetoHydroDynamics (CMHD) as a low-energy effective theory using a double expansion in gradients (k times mean free path) and the anomaly coefficient CA, which is small because it tracks quantum effects suppressed by powers of ℏ. They derive constitutive relations for the stress-energy tensor, axial current, and electric field up to first order in this expansion by imposing the second law of thermodynamics, obtaining the chiral magnetic effect (CME) term in the electric field. They then solve the linearized CMHD equations for fluctuations around a uniform static background to obtain dispersion relations and polarization properties of collective modes, using a simplified equation of state with sound velocity cs and Alfvén velocity uA.

## Key numbers

- Anomaly coefficient: CA = e²/(2π²) (from QED)
- Relaxation rate of axial charge density: ΓA ∝ CA² (proportional to square of anomaly coefficient)
- Alfvén velocity: uA ≡ B/√(εf + pf), assumed uA ≪ 1
- Dimensionless parameter: εA = CA μA / σ (ratio of anomaly-induced to Ohmic effects)
- Group velocity V satisfies a sixth-order polynomial equation: (V² - uA² cos²θ)[V⁴ - (uA² + cs²)V² + cs² uA² cos²θ] + εA² V²(V² - cs²) = 0
- For εA = 0, MHD wave velocities: VA² = uA² cos²θ; VF,S² = [(uA² + cs²) ± √(uA⁴ + cs⁴ - 2uA² cs² cos(2θ))]/2
- For εA/uA ≫ 1, Chern-Simon modes have purely imaginary V = ± i εA
- Perturbative shift of Alfvén wave at small εA: ΔV = -εA²(VA² - cs²)VA / [2(VA² - VF²)(VA² - VS²)]

## Context

This work extends ordinary magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) by incorporating chiral anomaly effects, building on prior studies of chiral plasma instabilities by Laine (2005) and Akamatsu (2013), and on earlier formulations of chiral MHD by Yamamoto (2016), Rogachevskii (2017), Boyarsky (2015), and Giovannini (2016). The authors identify a stable regime at small but finite μA for generic wavevector angles, contrasting with chiral plasma which becomes unstable at infinitesimal μA. The work enables future study of anomaly-induced phenomena in specific chiral matter systems such as Weyl semimetals and the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions, and suggests extension to include baryon density dynamics relevant to low-beam-energy scans at RHIC.
