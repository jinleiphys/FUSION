# Rescattering and finite formation time effects in electro-disintegration of the deuteron in the cumulative region

> The role of rescattering due to the final state interaction (FSI) and the influence of the finite formation time (FFT) on the inclusive $D(e,e')X$ and exclusive $D(e,e'p)n$ electro-disintegration of the deuteron are studied in the cumulative kinematical region $x>1$ and moderate values of the 4-momentum transfer $Q^2=2÷10$ (GeV/c)$^2$. The spins are averaged out. It is found that in the inclusive process the relative magnitude of rescattering steadily grows with $x$ and that at $x=1.7$ it has the same order as the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) contribution, with the finite formation time effects decreasing the rescattering contribution by $\sim 30$%. In the exclusive process, with increasing momentum transfer, FFT substantially reduces the effects from FSI, although the latter are still appreciable in the region of momentum transfer investigated.

## Key claim

Rescattering from final-state interactions (FSI) grows with the Bjorken scaling variable x and at x=1.7 is comparable in magnitude to the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) contribution; finite formation time (FFT) effects reduce the rescattering contribution by about 30%, but not enough to neglect FSI entirely in the cumulative region at Q² = 2–10 (GeV/c)².

## Method

The authors calculated inclusive D(e,e')X and exclusive D(e,e'p)n cross sections using Feynman diagrams for PWIA and rescattering amplitudes, treating all particles as scalar and averaging over spins. They introduced colour transparency (CT) effects via the FFT of the struck nucleon, modelled by a monopole form factor with a second state of mass 1.8 GeV, and used the AV14 interaction for the deuteron wave function with an effective electromagnetic form factor chosen to reproduce full-spin PWIA results.

## Key numbers

- At x=1.71, Q²=2.65 (GeV/c)², the rescattering contribution raises the inclusive cross section by a factor of about 2.5 relative to PWIA; FFT effects decrease it back by a factor of 1.6.
- FFT effects reduce the rescattering contribution by approximately 30% at the maximum x studied (x=1.71).
- At x=1.8 and Q²=10 (GeV/c)², with missing momentum |p₂| ~ 1–1.5 GeV/c, FFT decreases the pure FSI results by a factor of 4.
- The inclusive cross section was calculated for incident electron energy E=9.761 GeV and scattering angle θ=10°, covering x from 1.0 to 1.71 and Q² from 2.50 to 2.65 (GeV/c)².

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of colour transparency in nuclear reactions (Kopeliovich, cited as "Ref. ~\cite{kop}") and builds on the approach of Ciofi degli Atti et al. (2000) for deuteron electro-disintegration with full spins. The results show that at moderate Q² (2–10 (GeV/c)²) and high x, FSI remain significant even after including FFT/CT effects, so that direct access to the high-momentum deuteron wave function via PWIA is not yet possible; higher Q² is needed for CT to suppress FSI sufficiently. The method also enables comparison with exclusive data from the experiment of Ref. ~\cite{exp} (kinematics listed in Table 1).
