# Excited states of odd-mass nuclei with different deformation-dependent mass coefficients

> Experimental data indicate that the mass tensor of collective Bohr Hamiltonian cannot be considered as a constant but should be considered as a function of the collective coordinates. In this work our purpose is to investigate the properties of low-lying collective states of the odd nuclei $^{173}$Yb and $^{163}$Dy by using a new generalized version of the collective quadrupole Bohr Hamiltonian with deformation-dependent mass coefficients. The proposed new version of the Bohr Hamiltonian is solved for Davidson potential in $β$ shape variable, while the $γ$ potential is taken to be equal to the harmonic oscillator. The obtained results of the excitation energies and B(E2) reduced transition probabilities show an overall agreement with the experimental data. Moreover, we investigate the effect of the deformation dependent mass parameter on energy spectra and transition rates in both cases, namely: when the mass coefficients are different and when they are equal. Besides, we will show the positive effect of the present formalism on the moment of inertia.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a generalized Bohr Hamiltonian with deformation-dependent mass (DDM) coefficients, using Davidson potential in β and harmonic oscillator in γ, yields improved agreement with experimental excitation energies and B(E2) transition probabilities for the odd-mass nuclei ¹⁷³Yb and ¹⁶³Dy, but only when the mass coefficients for rotational, β-, and γ-motion are treated as different from each other; the improvement from DDM is most pronounced when the mass coefficients are taken as equal, and the γ-band in ¹⁷³Yb is not influenced by the DDM parameter when mass coefficients are already different.

## Method

The authors solved a generalized collective quadrupole Bohr Hamiltonian with three different deformation-dependent mass coefficients (B_rot, B_β, B_γ) using the asymptotic iteration method (AIM). The β-vibrational potential was taken as the Davidson potential V(β) = V₀(β/β₀ − β₀/β)², the γ-potential as a harmonic oscillator around γ=0, and the deformation function was f(β)=1+aβ² with a≪1. Energy spectra and B(E2) transition probabilities were calculated for the ground-state, β-, and γ-bands of ¹⁷³Yb and ¹⁶³Dy, with parameters fitted to experimental data via least-squares minimization of the root-mean-square deviation σ.

## Key numbers

* For ¹⁷³Yb, the optimal fitted parameters in the case of different mass coefficients (a=0) were: g=0.0094, g_β=1033.26, B_β/B_γ=1.3008, B_β/B_rot=7.5; for equal mass coefficients: g=0.0464, g_β=0.3643.
* For ¹⁶³Dy, the optimal fitted parameters in the case of different mass coefficients (a=0) were: g=0.003, g_β=235.44, B_β/B_γ=2.3731, B_β/B_rot=7.9063; for equal mass coefficients: g=0.0628, g_β=0.5853.
* For the ground-state band of ¹⁷³Yb, the r.m.s. deviation σ was 0.0399 (different masses, a=0), 0.0253 (different masses with DDM, a=0.001, β₀=6.94), 1.2419 (equal masses, a=0), and 0.0235 (equal masses with DDM, a=0.0881, β₀=1.029).
* For the β-band of ¹⁷³Yb, σ was 0.6839 (different masses, a=0), 0.6837 (different masses with DDM, a=0.1724, β₀=0.0913), 1.1682 (equal masses, a=0), and 1.1682 (equal masses with DDM, a=1.94×10⁻¹¹, β₀=3.8091).
* For the γ-band of ¹⁷³Yb, σ was 0.347 (different masses, a=0) and 0.34 (different masses with DDM).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Jolos and von Brentano (2009) requiring three different mass coefficients for collective modes, and the deformation-dependent mass formalism of Bonatsos et al. (2011), by combining both into a single generalized Bohr Hamiltonian for odd-mass nuclei. It corrects the energy formula previously used by Ermamatov et al. (2015) for ¹⁷³Yb and ¹⁶³Dy, and shows that the DDM parameter a significantly improves agreement with experiment when mass coefficients are taken as equal, while having little effect when they are already different. The results enable more accurate predictions for interband B(E2) transitions where experimental data are not yet available, and demonstrate that the DDM formalism slows the unphysical increase of the moment of inertia with deformation.
