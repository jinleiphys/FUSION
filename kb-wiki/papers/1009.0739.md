# Meta-stable States in Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We study the meta-stable states in high temperature phase of QCD characterised by nonzero expectation values for the imaginary part of the Polyakov loop. We consider $N_f= 2, 3$ dynamical staggered quarks, and carry out simulations at various values of the coupling $β$ to observe these states. In particular, we find the value of the coupling ($β_m$) above which the meta-stable states appear. The resulting value of $β_m$ corresponds to temperature $T_m \gtrsim 750$MeV for $N_f=2$.

## Key claim
The paper claims that Z(3) meta-stable states in QCD with dynamical quarks do not exist near the deconfinement transition temperature, but only appear at temperatures above approximately 750 MeV for N_f=2, contrary to previous expectations that such states would be present just above T_c.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with N_f=2 and N_f=3 dynamical staggered quarks on lattices of spatial volumes 16^3, 24^3, and 32^3 with temporal extents N_tau=4 and 6. They used the MILC code with the Hybrid R algorithm, starting from specially prepared initial configurations corresponding to Z(3)-rotated Polyakov loop phases (theta = 0, +/- 2pi/3, pi) to enhance the probability of observing meta-stable states. For each coupling beta in the range 5.2 to 6.0, they collected 2500 gauge configurations and analyzed the histogram of the Polyakov loop to identify distinct peaks corresponding to meta-stable states.

## Key numbers
- The coupling threshold for meta-stable states on a 32^3 x 4 lattice with N_f=2 is beta_m ~ 5.80
- For N_tau=6 on a 32^3 volume, beta_m ~ 5.89
- The temperature corresponding to beta_m = 5.80 with am_q = 0.01 is estimated as T_m ~ 750 MeV for N_f=2
- The quark mass used is am_{u,d} = 0.01, giving m_{u,d}/T = 0.04
- The deconfinement transition temperature is approximately 200 MeV for comparison
- For N_f=3, the meta-stable states appear at higher beta_m than for N_f=2

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD studies of Z(3) meta-stable states, which had previously been observed in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Meisinger (1995) and in lattice QCD with sextet fermions by Machtey (2009). The authors find that contrary to earlier expectations from effective potential calculations by Dixit (1991) and Belyaev (1991), the explicit symmetry breaking from dynamical quarks in the fundamental representation is strong enough to eliminate meta-stable states near T_c. The result implies that such states may only be accessible at LHC energies rather than at RHIC, and could have implications for early Universe cosmology and heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
