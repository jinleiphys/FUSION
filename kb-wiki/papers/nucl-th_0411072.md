# Particle-number conservation within self-consistent random-phase approximation

> The self-consistent random-phase approximation (SCRPA) is reexamined within a multilevel-pairing model with double degeneracy. It is shown that the expressions for occupation numbers used in the original version of SCRPA violate the particle number for non-symmetric particle-hole ($ph$) spectra. A renormalization is introduced to restore the particle number, which leads to the expressions of occupation numbers similar to those derived by Hara et al. for the ph case. The results of calculations within the $ph$-symmetric case show that this number-conserving SCRPA yields the energies of the ground state and first excited state of the system with $Ω+2$ particles relative to the ground state of the system with $Ω$ particles in close agreement with those obtained within the original SCRPA. However it gives a slightly larger correlation energy.

## Key claim

The original self-consistent random-phase approximation (SCRPA) violates particle number for non-symmetric particle-hole spectra, and a renormalization is introduced to restore particle-number conservation, yielding occupation-number expressions similar to those derived by Hara et al. for the particle-hole case.

## Method

The paper reexamines the SCRPA within a multilevel-pairing model with double degeneracy (the picket-fence model), consisting of Ω two-fold equidistant levels interacting via a pairing force with constant parameter G. The author derives the SCRPA equation for additional and removal modes, computes the expectation values of pair-operator products using the inverse transformation and ground-state vacuum condition, and then introduces a bosonized representation for the particle-number operator to replace the original fermion-based expression, leading to number-conserving occupation-number formulas.

## Key numbers

- The particle-number violation measure |δΩ| is of order O(Y⁴), where Y are the RPA amplitudes, indicating the violation is small within the validity region of RPA where |Yᵢᵛ| are small.
- In the particle-hole symmetric case (Ωₚ = Ωₕ = Ω/2), the number-conserving SCRPA yields ground-state and first-excited-state energies of the system with Ω+2 particles relative to the ground state of the system with Ω particles in close agreement with the original SCRPA, but gives a slightly larger correlation energy.
- The model uses Ω two-fold equidistant levels with single-particle energies εᵢ = iε, where i runs over all Ω levels, and a pairing force with constant parameter G.

## Context

This work extends the SCRPA approach developed by Dukelsky and Hirsch (2000s) for the picket-fence model, which previously was applied only to particle-hole symmetric spectra. The paper identifies that the original SCRPA occupation-number expressions violate particle number for non-symmetric spectra, and introduces a renormalization similar to that derived by Hara (1970s) and Rowe (1960s) for the particle-hole case. This enables application of SCRPA to realistic nuclear spectra, such as those in light neutron-rich nuclei studied by Vinh Mau (1990s), where particle-hole asymmetry is strong and Pauli-principle effects cannot be neglected.
