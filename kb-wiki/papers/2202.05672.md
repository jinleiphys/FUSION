# Expected yields of $^{181}$Ta (e,e') $^{181}$Ta$^*$ in the multi-keV range with a plasma-cathode electron beam

> Calculations of the cross sections of inelastic electron scattering $(e,e')$ on a nucleus in the multi-keV energy range strongly depend on the description of the screening of the nuclear Coulomb potential as well as on the deformation of the wave functions of the incoming and outgoing electron in the vicinity of the nucleus. These cross sections are evaluated at values lower than $10^{-30}$ cm$^2$ which vary by several orders of magnitude according to the models. Experimental measurements would be required to constrain the models but it is a real challenge to measure such low cross sections. In this study, we demonstrate that inelastic electron scattering is the main nuclear excitation mechanism in a $^{181}$Ta target irradiated with a new intense 10 - 30 keV electron beam produced with a biased laser-plasma. Calculations show that through the detection of conversion electrons, it should be possible to measure the nuclear excitation yields. The effect of electron beam heating and of plasma deposition on the tantalum target are quantified, thus allowing the dimensioning of a possible experimental configuration to study $(e,e')$ processes in this range of energy for the first time.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that inelastic electron scattering (e,e') is the dominant nuclear excitation mechanism in a 181Ta target irradiated by a 10-30 keV plasma-cathode electron beam, and that detection of conversion electrons should enable experimental measurement of the excitation yields, though the cross sections vary by several orders of magnitude depending on the theoretical model used for Coulomb potential screening.

## Method

The authors calculated (e,e') cross sections for 181Ta using four theoretical approaches: semi-classical Coulomb excitation, Plane Wave Born Approximation (PWBA), Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) with unscreened and screened Coulomb potentials, and Relativistic Hartree-Fock-Slater (RHFS) method. They then used the Geant4 Monte Carlo code with Livermore Physics List to simulate electron transport and nuclear excitation yields in a tantalum anode irradiated by measured electron energy distributions from a laser-plasma source at target voltages of 10-30 kV. The depth distributions of excited nuclei were calculated, and the yields of gamma-ray photons and conversion electrons escaping the anode were estimated for each model.

## Key numbers

- (e,e') cross sections for 181Ta in the 7-100 keV range are evaluated at values lower than 10^-30 cm^2, varying by several orders of magnitude between models
- The first isomeric state of 181Ta lies at 6.2 keV above the ground state with a half-life of 6.05 microseconds
- The electron source delivers up to 9x10^13 electrons per bunch at 30 kV target voltage, with average electron energy of approximately 8 keV
- The internal conversion coefficient for the E1 transition is measured as alpha(E1) = 70 in a neutral atom
- At 30 kV target voltage, the number of conversion electrons exiting the anode front side per 10,000 laser shots ranges from approximately 100 (RHFS screened model) to approximately 2,000 (DWBA screened model)
- The number of detectable gamma-ray photons per 10,000 laser shots at 30 kV ranges from approximately 3 (RHFS model) to approximately 800 (semi-classical model)
- The photoexcitation cross section is 1.7x10^-18 cm^2, while the photoelectric cross section at 6.2 keV is 9x10^-20 cm^2

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of low-energy (e,e') processes by Letokhov (1994), Gosselin (2009), Dzyublik (2013), and Tkalya (2012), who developed models for keV-range electron-nucleus scattering with different treatments of Coulomb potential screening. The authors use their recently developed 10 Hz plasma-cathode electron source (Gobet 2020) to evaluate whether experimental measurements can constrain these models for the first time in the multi-keV energy range. The study enables the design of a specific experimental configuration to measure (e,e') cross sections on 181Ta, which would help resolve theoretical uncertainties about screening effects that are relevant for nuclear excitation processes in astrophysical plasmas.
