# Exotic shapes in Ca-40 and Ar-36 studied with Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics

> The structures of the ground and excited states of sd-shell nuclei: Ca-40 and Ar-36 were studied with a method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. Recently observed rotational bands were described with deformed intrinsic states which are dominated by excited configurations such as 4p-4h, 8p-8h and 4p-8h states. The results showed the coexistence of various kinds of exotic shapes with oblate, prolate and large prolate deformatios in the low-energy region of Ca-40. Possible cluster aspects in heavy sd-shell nuclei were implied in these exotic shapes.

# Exotic shapes in Ca-40 and Ar-36 studied with Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics

## Key claim
The paper claims that the recently observed rotational bands in Ca-40 and Ar-36 can be described by deformed intrinsic states dominated by excited configurations (4p-4h, 8p-8h, and 4p-8h), but the theoretical intrinsic deformation of the super-deformed band in Ca-40 (beta=0.4) is still smaller than the value beta=0.6 expected for a typical super deformation, suggesting the calculations underestimate the deformation.

## Method
The authors applied antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with a generator coordinate method, varying the total oscillator quanta under a constraint and then superposing spin-parity projected states by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian and norm matrices. They used a central interaction containing a finite-range three-body term, combined with the G3RS spin-orbit force and Coulomb force, with parameters chosen to reproduce the sizes and binding energies of He-4, O-16, and Ca-40.

## Key numbers
- The theoretical E2 transition strength for the 4+ to 2+ transition in the super-deformed band (band 5) of Ca-40 is 53 W.u., compared to the experimental value B(E2;6.54 MeV to 5.63 MeV) = 100 W.u.
- The oblate and prolate deformed states in Ca-40 from 4p-4h configurations both have deformation parameter beta = 0.2.
- The large prolate deformation in Ca-40 from 8p-8h configurations has deformation parameter beta = 0.4.
- The theoretical band-head of the K-pi=0- band in Ca-40 is a 1- state at 11.7 MeV, about 3 MeV higher than the theoretical band-head of the positive-parity super deformation.
- In Ar-36, excited bands with large prolate deformation beta = 0.3 are dominated by 4p-8h configurations and start from 4 MeV excitation energy.

## Context
This work extends the AMD method, previously applied to light nuclei such as Ne-20 and unstable nuclei like Be-10 and Be-12 (Kanada-En'yo, 1995; Kanada-En'yo, 1998), to the heavier sd-shell nuclei Ca-40 and Ar-36. The study addresses the shape coexistence problem in the doubly magic nucleus Ca-40, following experimental observations of rotational bands by Ideguchi (2001) and Svensson (2001). The results suggest possible cluster aspects in heavy sd-shell nuclei, such as a Si-28 + C-12-like clustering in the super-deformed state, and enable future comparisons with intruder states in neutron-rich nuclei like Mg-32.
