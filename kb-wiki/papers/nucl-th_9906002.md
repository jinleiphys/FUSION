# Relativistic heavy ion collisions --- Where are we now? Where do we go?

> 8 pages, 4 postscript figures, LaTex, Invited talk presented at the KEK-Tanashi International Symposium on ``Physics of Hadrons and Nuclei'', Dec.14-17, 1998 Tokyo, Japan, Submitted to Nuclear Physics A

# Relativistic heavy ion collisions, Where are we now? Where do we go?

## Key claim

The paper reviews the status of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at CERN SPS energies (160–200 GeV/A) and argues that while evidence exists for the formation of hot, thermalized matter and possible new forms of matter, the interpretation of signals such as J/psi suppression and dilepton spectra remains ambiguous and does not yet decisively confirm quark-gluon plasma formation. The author emphasizes that the sudden drop in J/psi yield observed in PbPb collisions cannot be naively attributed to a first-order phase transition, because the energy density jump at such a transition would produce a gradual rather than sudden change.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results from CERN SPS fixed-target experiments (PbPb at 160 GeV/A, SU at 200 GeV/A, and pBe/pAu collisions) focusing on three observables: transverse mass distributions of final-state hadrons, J/psi suppression as a function of transverse energy, and dilepton spectra. Theoretical analysis uses the linear sigma model for disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) formation, QCD sum rules for hadron modification in medium, and the exact relation between the spectral function and dilepton production rate.

## Key numbers

- CERN SPS heavy ion collisions at E_lab = 160–200 GeV/A (PbPb and SU systems)
- RHIC will accelerate gold nuclei at 100 GeV/A (per nucleon) with sqrt(s) = 200 GeV/A
- The e+e- invariant mass resolution at RHIC is 20–30 times better at 1 GeV than the current CERES setup at CERN SPS
- J/psi yield (measured as B_mumu sigma(J/psi)/sigma(Drell-Yan)) drops suddenly at a specific transverse energy E_T in PbPb collisions at 160 GeV/A
- The freeze-out time scale is a few tens of fm
- The slope parameter T_i in transverse mass distributions follows T_i = a + b m_i, where a = T_f (freeze-out temperature) and b = <v^2> (mean squared flow velocity)
- The DCC probability distribution P(R) = 1/(2 sqrt(R)) for R = N_pi0/(N_pi0+N_pi++N_pi-), with average R = 1/3
- The JHF project is capable of accelerating heavy ions at E_lab = 50 GeV/A and lower

## Context

This invited talk at the KEK-Tanashi International Symposium (December 1998) reviews the status of heavy ion physics just before RHIC operations began. It extends the analysis framework of Matsui (1997) on challenges for heavy ion theory and discusses J/psi suppression originally proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986) as a quark-gluon plasma signature. The paper references specific experimental results from the NA50, NA44, NA49, CERES, WA98, and E877 collaborations at CERN SPS. It argues that central ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions are not the best place to look for DCC formation (contrary to the quench mechanism proposed by Rajagopal and Wilczek, 1993), and recommends searching in not-so-central collisions with low-pT pions. The paper calls for running RHIC at lower energies and for the JHF project to fill the energy gap between AGS (~12 GeV/A) and SPS (~160 GeV/A).
