# On the $β$-decay of $^{186}$Hg

> The Gamow-Teller strength distribution of the decay of $^{186}$Hg into $^{186}$Au has been determined for the first time using the total absorption gamma spectroscopy technique and has been compared with theoretical QRPA calculations using the SLy4 Skyrme force. The measured Gamow-Teller strength distribution and the half-life are described by mixing oblate and prolate configurations independently in the parent and daughter nuclei. The best description of the experimental beta strength is obtained with dominantly prolate components for both parent $^{186}$Hg and daughter $^{186}$Au. The approach also allowed us to determine an upper limit of the oblate component in the parent state. The complexity of the analysis required the development of a new approach in the analysis of the X-ray gated total absorption spectrum.

## Key claim

The Gamow-Teller strength distribution of the decay of 186Hg into 186Au has been determined for the first time using total absorption gamma spectroscopy, and the best description of the experimental beta strength is obtained with dominantly prolate components for both parent 186Hg and daughter 186Au, which contradicts the interpretation of charge-radii measurements that suggested an oblate ground state for 186Hg.

## Method

The experiment was performed at CERN-ISOLDE using the Lucrecia total absorption spectrometer (TAS), a NaI(Tl) mono-crystal with 38 cm diameter and 38 cm length. The 186Hg isotopes were produced by bombarding a UCx target with 1.4 GeV protons, ionized selectively with the RILIS laser source, and mass-separated with the General Purpose Separator. The TAGS spectrum was generated in coincidence with X-rays detected in a planar germanium detector to select the electron capture component. A new approach was developed for the analysis because the conventional response function could not reproduce the X-ray gated spectrum, due to the sizable internal conversion coefficients of the gamma transitions de-exciting the strongly populated 1+ state at 363.6 keV in 186Au and the resulting X-ray summing effects; the new method used GEANT4 simulations to generate the response function including X-ray production from both electron capture and internal conversion processes.

## Key numbers

- Half-life of 186Hg: 1.38(6) minutes
- Q-value for the decay: 3176(24) keV
- Excitation energy of the most strongly populated state in 186Au: 363.6 keV (1+ state)
- Internal conversion coefficients for the de-exciting transitions: alpha_total = 0.315 for the 112.1 keV transition (E1) and alpha_total = 0.558 for the 251.5 keV transition (M1)
- Total efficiency of the Lucrecia TAS: 90% for mono-energetic gamma rays in the range 300-3000 keV, approximately 99% for gamma cascades of more than one gamma ray
- Best-fit mixing parameters from the double mixing scenario: lambda = 0.46 and alpha = 0.46 (dominantly prolate components for both parent and daughter)
- Second minimum in the mixing parameter surface: (lambda, alpha) = (0.8, 0.8)

## Context

This work extends the method of using beta-decay strength distributions to probe nuclear shapes, originally introduced by Hamamoto and later developed by Sarriguren et al., which had previously been applied to cases like 74Kr and 76Sr where mixing in the parent ground state was assumed to be small. The study contradicts the interpretation of charge-radii measurements by Frauendorf and others that suggested 186Hg has an oblate ground state, and instead finds that a double mixing scenario with dominantly prolate components in both parent and daughter provides the best description. The new analysis approach developed for handling X-ray gated spectra in the presence of large internal conversion coefficients can be applied to other cases where the beta+ component of the decay contaminates the X-ray gated spectra.
