# Radiative energy loss and v2 spectra for viscous hydrodynamics

> This work investigates the first correction to the equilibrium phase space distribution and its effects on spectra and elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions. We show that the departure from equilibrium on the freezeout surface is the largest part of the viscous corrections to $v_2(p_T)$. However, the momentum dependence of the departure from equilibrium is not known {\it a priori}, and it is probably not proportional to $p_T^2$ as has been assumed in hydrodynamic simulations. At high momentum in weakly coupled plasmas it is determined by the rate of radiative energy loss and is proportional to $p_T^{3/2}$. The weaker $p_T$ dependence leads to straighter $v_2(p_T)$ curves at the same value of viscosity. Further, the departure from equilibrium is generally species dependent. A species dependent equilibration rate, with baryons equilibrating faster than mesons, can explain ``constituent quark scaling'' without invoking coalescence models.

# Radiative energy loss and v2 spectra for viscous hydrodynamics

## Key claim

The first viscous correction to the equilibrium phase space distribution in heavy ion collisions is not necessarily proportional to p_T^2 as has been assumed in hydrodynamic simulations; at high momentum in weakly coupled plasmas it is determined by the rate of radiative energy loss and is proportional to p_T^{3/2}, leading to straighter v_2(p_T) curves at the same value of viscosity.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Boltzmann equation for pure-glue QCD in three approximation schemes: a leading-log Fokker-Planck treatment of momentum diffusion, a treatment including only 2→2 collisions, and a leading-order treatment including collinear 1→2 splitting processes (bremsstrahlung) with LPM suppression. They then compute the viscous correction χ(p) to the distribution function and use a second-order viscous hydrodynamic model with Cooper-Frye freezeout to compute the resulting elliptic flow v_2(p_T) for each functional form of χ(p).

## Key numbers

- For pure-glue QCD at leading order (including bremsstrahlung), the viscous correction scales as χ(p) ∝ p^{1.38} at intermediate momentum (5 ≲ p/T ≲ 10), with the explicit form χ(p) = (2.84 η/sT) p^{1.38}
- At asymptotically large momentum where ln^{-1}(p/T) ≪ 1, the viscous correction scales as χ_g(p) ≈ 0.7/(α_s T √q̂) p^{3/2}, where q̂ is the transport parameter characterizing transverse momentum broadening
- For scalar λφ^4 theory in a Boltzmann approximation, the exact solution gives χ(p) ∝ p^2 with viscosity η = 1536π T^3/λ^2
- In the leading-log Fokker-Planck treatment, the asymptotic solution is χ(p) = p^2/(2Tμ) where μ = g^4 C_A^2 T^2/(24π) log(T/m_D)
- For 2→2 collisions only, the asymptotic behavior is χ(p) ∝ p^2/log(p/T)

## Context

This work extends the viscous hydrodynamic calculations of Baier (2006), Romatschke (2007), Song (2007), and Dusling (2007), which all assumed a quadratic ansatz χ(p) ∝ p^2 for the viscous correction. The authors show that this assumption is not secure for QCD-like theories, where radiative energy loss (bremsstrahlung) dominates equilibration at high momentum, as established by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2003) for shear viscosity calculations. The results enable a consistent treatment of viscous corrections that can explain "constituent quark scaling" in baryon and meson elliptic flow without invoking coalescence models, and demonstrate the need for consistency between the shear viscosity η/s and the transport parameter q̂ extracted from jet quenching measurements.
