# "Square Root" of the Proca Equation: Spin-3/2 Field Equation

> New equations describing particles with spin 3/2 are derived. The non-local equation with the unique mass can be considered as "square root" of the Proca equation in the same sense as the Dirac equation is related to the Klein-Gordon-Fock equation. The local equation describes spin 3/2 particles with three mass states. The equations considered involve fields with spin-3/2 and spin-1/2, i.e. multi-spin 1/2, 3/2. The projection operators extracting states with definite energy, spin, and spin projections are obtained. All independent solutions of the local equation are expressed through projection matrices. The first order relativistic wave equation in the 20-dimensional matrix form, the relativistically invariant bilinear form and the corresponding Lagrangian are given. Two parameters characterizing non-minimal electromagnetic interactions of fermions are introduced, and the quantum-mechanical Hamiltonian is found. It is proved that there is only causal propagation of waves in the approach considered.

## Key claim

The paper derives new spin-3/2 field equations by taking a "square root" of the Proca equation analogous to how the Dirac equation relates to the Klein-Gordon-Fock equation; the local equation describes particles with spin 3/2 and 1/2 (two copies) and three different mass states, while the non-local equation describes a unique mass state, and the approach avoids the noncausal propagation problems found in the Rarita-Schwinger theory.

## Method

The author starts from the Proca equation for a massive vector field and factorizes its second-order differential operator into a product of two first-order operators containing Dirac matrices and a nonlocal term involving the inverse d'Alembertian and its square root. By replacing the nonlocal operator with its on-shell value (where the d'Alembertian equals the mass squared), a local equation is obtained with an arbitrary parameter \(b\). The local equation is then cast into a first-order relativistic wave equation in a 20-dimensional matrix representation, and projection operators for energy, spin, and spin projections are constructed using the Pauli-Lubanski vector and the method of Fedorov. Non-minimal electromagnetic interactions are introduced via two free parameters \(\sigma_0\) and \(\sigma_1\), and causality is checked by examining the characteristic determinant.

## Key numbers

* The local equation (13) contains the parameter \(b = 1 \pm \sqrt{2}\) from the nonlocal construction, but is considered for arbitrary \(b\).
* The mass spectrum from the local equation gives three masses: \(m\) (the original Proca mass), \(m_1 = m(1 - \sqrt{4b+1})/(2b)\), and \(m_2 = m(1 + \sqrt{4b+1})/(2b)\), with the relation \(m_1 + m_2 = m/b\).
* The squared masses satisfy \(m_1^2 = m^2 (2b+1 - \sqrt{4b+1})/(2b^2)\) and \(m_2^2 = m^2 (2b+1 + \sqrt{4b+1})/(2b^2)\).
* The condition for real masses is \(b \geq -1/4\); the value \(b = 1 - \sqrt{2}\) does not satisfy this requirement.
* The eigenvalues of the matrix \(\beta_4\) in the 20-dimensional formulation are \(\pm 1\), \(\pm \theta_1\), \(\pm \theta_2\) with \(\theta_1 = (-1 - \sqrt{4b+1})/2\) and \(\theta_2 = (-1 + \sqrt{4b+1})/2\), giving field masses \(m/\theta_1\) and \(m/\theta_2\) that match \(m_1\) and \(m_2\).
* The squared Pauli-Lubanski vector for the 16-dimensional representation satisfies the minimal equation \((\sigma^2 - 3/4)(\sigma^2 - 15/4) = 0\), corresponding to spins \(s=1/2\) and \(s=3/2\).

## Context

This work extends the program of constructing higher-spin field equations initiated by Dirac (1936), Fierz (1939), and Rarita and Schwinger (1941), and addresses the well-known causality problem for massive spin-3/2 particles in external electromagnetic fields identified by Velo and Zwanziger (1969). The author's multi-spin approach, which includes both spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 components in a single 4-vector-spinor, was previously applied to non-Abelian gauge theories (Kruglov 1986, 2001). The paper enables further study of effective hadron descriptions and massive gravitinos in supergravity, while leaving the determination of the gyromagnetic ratio (expected to be \(g=2\) from unitarity arguments by Weinberg) for future work.
