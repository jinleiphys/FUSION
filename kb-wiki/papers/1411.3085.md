# Systematic study of 16O-induced fusions with the improved quantum molecular dynamics model

> The heavy-ion fusion reactions with 16O bombarding on 62Ni, 65Cu, 74Ge, 148Nd, 180Hf, 186W, 208Pb, 238U are systematically investigated with the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model. The fusion cross sections at energies near and above the Coulomb barriers can be reasonably well reproduced by using this semi-classical microscopic transport model with the parameter sets SkP* and IQ3a. The dynamical nucleus-nucleus potentials and the influence of Fermi constraint on the fusion process are also studied simultaneously. In addition to the mean field, the Fermi constraint also plays a key role for the reliable description of fusion process and for improving the stability of fragments in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model can reasonably reproduce fusion cross sections for 16O-induced reactions at energies near and above the Coulomb barrier, but systematically over-predicts sub-barrier fusion for heavy targets, particularly for doubly-magic 208Pb, due to the model's neglect of shell effects and slightly over-predicted surface diffuseness.

## Method

The authors used the ImQMD model (version 2.1) with two parameter sets, SkP* and IQ3a, to simulate eight fusion reactions of 16O with targets from 62Ni to 238U. For each incident energy and impact parameter, 100-200 bombarding events were created, and fusion events were counted when the center-to-center distance fell below the compound nucleus radius. The dynamical nucleus-nucleus potential was extracted from the simulations by separating the relative motion kinetic energy from the total energy, and the influence of the Fermi constraint (an effective Pauli blocking procedure) on fragment stability and fusion cross sections was studied by switching it off after the nuclei begin to touch.

## Key numbers

- Fusion cross sections for eight reactions (16O + 62Ni, 65Cu, 74Ge, 148Nd, 180Hf, 186W, 208Pb, 238U) were calculated at energies near and above the Coulomb barrier.
- The incompressibility coefficient of nuclear matter is K∞ = 195 MeV for SkP* and 225 MeV for IQ3a.
- For 16O+186W at Ec.m. = 66 MeV (slightly below the most probable barrier), the dynamical barrier height from ImQMD with SkP* was about 63 MeV.
- When the Fermi constraint was switched off for 16O+92Zr at Ec.m. = 45 MeV, the fusion cross section increased by about 60 mb.
- For 16O+92Zr at Ec.m. = 45 MeV and b = 5 fm, the fusion probability was smaller than 0.03 with the Fermi constraint active.
- The initial distance between reaction partners was set to d0 = 40 fm for the heavy systems.

## Context

This work extends previous ImQMD studies of 16O-induced fusion on lighter targets (46Ti, 56Fe, 92Zr, 154Sm) reported in ImQMD (2014) to heavier systems including 208Pb and 238U. The results are compared with the empirical barrier distribution approach using the Skyrme energy-density functional plus extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF2) approximation from Liu (2006) and Wang (2009), and with coupled-channel calculations using CCFULL from Hagino (1999). The study demonstrates that the Fermi constraint is essential for reliable fragment description in semi-classical QMD simulations, and identifies the need to incorporate shell effects for accurate sub-barrier fusion predictions, particularly for doubly-magic nuclei.
