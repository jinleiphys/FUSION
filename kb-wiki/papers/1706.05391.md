# Towards an effective field theory approach to the neutrinoless double-beta decay

> Weak interaction in nuclei represents a well-known venue for testing many of the fundamental symmetries of the Standard Model. In particular, neutrinoless double-beta decay offers the possibility to test Beyond Standard Model theories predicting that neutrinos are Majorana fermions and the lepton number conservation is violated. This paper focuses on an effective field theory approach to neutrinoless double-beta decay for extracting information regarding the properties of the Beyond Standard Model Lagrangian responsible for this process. We use shell model nuclear matrix elements and the latest experimental lower limits for the half-lives to extract the lepton number violating parameters of five nuclei of experimental interest, and lower limits for the energy scales of the new physics.

## Key claim

Under the assumption that a single coupling in the Beyond Standard Model Lagrangian dominates the neutrinoless double-beta decay amplitude, the authors extract new limits for the effective Majorana mass and for 11 additional low-energy effective field theory couplings using shell model nuclear matrix elements and the latest experimental half-life lower limits for five nuclei, while also adding three new pion-exchange diagrams to the effective field theory analysis for the first time.

## Method

The authors start from the effective field theory formalism of Hirsch (1996), Pas (1999, 2001), and Deppisch (2012) and calculate 23 nuclear matrix elements using the interacting shell model approach, including short-range correlation effects based on the CD-Bonn parametrization, finite-size effects, and optimal closure energies. They compute nine phase-space factors using an effective method fine-tuned to account for Coulomb field distorting finite-size proton distribution in the final nucleus, then extract upper limits for lepton number violating parameters under the "on-axis" assumption that only one term in the decay amplitude dominates, using experimental half-life lower limits for five isotopes.

## Key numbers

- For $^{136}$Xe, the extracted limit on the effective Majorana neutrino mass is $\left< m_{\beta\beta}\right>\sim 140$ meV, corresponding to $\left|\eta_{0\nu}\right| = 0.28 \times 10^{-6}$.
- The experimental half-life lower limits used are: $^{48}$Ca $> 2.0 \times 10^{22}$ years (Nemo3, 2016), $^{76}$Ge $> 5.3 \times 10^{25}$ years (Gerda, 2017), $^{82}$Se $> 2.5 \times 10^{23}$ years (Waters, 2016, preliminary), $^{130}$Te $> 4.0 \times 10^{24}$ years (Cuore, 2015), and $^{136}$Xe $> 1.07 \times 10^{26}$ years (KamLAND-Zen, 2016).
- The light neutrino exchange nuclear matrix elements $M_{0\nu}$ range from 1.03 ($^{48}$Ca) to 3.64 ($^{76}$Ge), while the heavy neutrino exchange NME $M_{0N}$ range from 75.5 ($^{48}$Ca) to 202 ($^{76}$Ge).
- For the long-range coupling $\epsilon^{V+A}_{V-A}$, the extracted limit for $^{136}$Xe is $\left| \eta_\eta \right| = 1.05 \times 10^{-9}$.
- For the short-range coupling $\varepsilon_1$, the extracted limit for $^{136}$Xe is $\left| \eta_{\tilde{q}} \right| = 2.91 \times 10^{-9}$.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Hirsch (1996), Pas (1999, 2001), and Deppisch (2012) by adding three new pion-exchange diagrams to the analysis that were previously only considered in the context of specific mechanisms such as the left-right symmetric model and R-parity violating supersymmetric models. The use of shell model nuclear matrix elements, which the authors argue are more reliable than older QRPA calculations because they take into account all correlations around the Fermi surface and respect all symmetries, enables more robust constraints on the lepton number violating parameters and energy scales of new physics that can be compared with results from the Large Hadron Collider and other experiments.
