# A Realistic Calculation of the Effects of Nucleon-Nucleon Correlations in High-Energy Scattering Processes Off Nuclei

> A new linked cluster expansion for the calculation of ground state observables of complex nuclei with realistic interactions has been developed [1-3]; using the V8' potential [4] the ground state energy, density and momentum distribution of complex nuclei have been calculated and found to be in good agreement with the results of [5], obtained within the Fermi Hyper Netted Chain, and Variational Monte Carlo [6] approaches. Using the same cluster expansion, with wave function and correlations Realistic Calculation of the Effects of Nucleon-Nucleon Correlations in High-Energy Scattering Processes Off Nuclei parameters fixed from the calculation of the ground-state observables, the semi-inclusive reaction of type A(e,e'p)X has been calculated taking final state interaction effects into account within a Glauber type calculation as in Ref. [7]; the comparison between the resulting distorted and undistorted momentum distributions provides an estimate of the transparency of the nuclear medium to the propagation of the hit proton. The effect of color transparency has also been considered within the approach of [8,9]; it is shown that at high values of Q^2 finite formation time effects strongly reduce the final state interaction, consistently with the idea of a reduced interaction of the hadron produced inside the nucleus [10]. The total neutron-nucleus cross section at high energies has also been calculated [11] by considering the effects of nucleon-nucleon correlations, which are found to increase the cross section by about 10% in disagreement with the experimental data. The inclusion of inelastic shadowing effects of Refs. [12,13] decreases back the cross section, leading to a good agreement between experimental data and theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new linked cluster expansion, using realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (the V8' potential), can reliably calculate ground-state observables and scattering processes in complex nuclei without free parameters, but the agreement with experimental data for the total neutron-nucleus cross section is only achieved after including inelastic shadowing effects, which counteract a 10% enhancement from correlations that initially worsened the disagreement.

## Method

The authors developed a linked cluster expansion to evaluate expectation values of operators using a correlated wave function built from a shell-model mean field and a state-dependent correlation operator. They applied this expansion to calculate ground-state properties (energy, density, momentum distributions) of 16O and 40Ca using the V8' realistic interaction. They then generalized the same cluster expansion to compute the distorted momentum distributions for the semi-inclusive A(e,e'p)X reaction, treating final state interactions within a Glauber approach and including finite formation time effects for color transparency, and separately calculated the total neutron-nucleus cross section including two-body correlations and inelastic shadowing.

## Key numbers

- The inclusion of nucleon-nucleon correlations in the target wave function increases the total neutron-nucleus cross section by about 10% relative to the mean field result, worsening agreement with experimental data.
- The charge densities and momentum distributions of 16O and 40Ca calculated with the cluster expansion show good agreement with results from the Fermi Hyper Netted Chain approach of Fabrocini (2001) and Variational Monte Carlo of Pieper (2001).
- The forward-backward asymmetry A_FB for the A(e,e'p)X process was calculated for 16O and 40Ca at various values of the four-momentum transfer Q^2, showing that at high Q^2 the finite formation time mechanism strongly reduces final state interactions.

## Context

This work extends the linked cluster expansion method previously developed by Alvioli (2001, 2002, 2003) for ground-state calculations to scattering processes, providing a parameter-free framework that contrasts with simplified nuclear structure models often used in medium and high energy scattering. The results for ground-state properties are benchmarked against the Fermi Hyper Netted Chain calculations of Fabrocini (2001) and Variational Monte Carlo of Pieper (2001). The treatment of final state interactions in A(e,e'p)X follows the Glauber approach of Ciofi degli Atti (2001), while the color transparency effects use the finite formation time mechanism of Braun (2001) and Morita (2001). The neutron-nucleus cross section calculations incorporate inelastic shadowing effects from Gribov (2001) and Jensen (2001) to achieve agreement with experimental data, resolving the discrepancy introduced by correlations.
