# Neutrinoless $ββ$ decay nuclear matrix elements complete up to N$^2$LO in heavy nuclei

> We evaluate all nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) up to next-to-next-to leading order (N$^2$LO) in chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) for the neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay of the nuclei most relevant for experiments, including $^{76}$Ge, $^{100}$Mo, and $^{136}$Xe. We use the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) and the nuclear shell model to calculate the N$^2$LO NMEs from very low-momentum (ultrasoft) neutrinos and from loop diagrams usually neglected in $0νββ$ studies. Our results indicate that the overall N$^2$LO contribution is centered around $-(5$-$10)\%$ for the shell model and $+(10$-$15)\%$ for the pnQRPA, with sizable uncertainties due to the scale dependence of the ultrasoft NMEs and the short-range nature of the loop NMEs. The sign discrepancy between many-body methods is common to all studied nuclei and points to the different behaviour of the intermediate states of the $0νββ$ decay. Within uncertainties, our results for the ultrasoft NME are of similar size as contributions usually referred to as ``beyond the closure approximation''.

# Neutrinoless $\beta\beta$ decay nuclear matrix elements complete up to N$^2$LO in heavy nuclei

## Key claim

The overall N$^2$LO contribution to neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements is centered around -(5-10)% for the nuclear shell model and +(10-15)% for the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation, with a sign discrepancy between the two many-body methods that is common to all studied nuclei and points to different behavior of the intermediate states of the decay. The results carry sizable uncertainties due to the scale dependence of the ultrasoft NMEs and the short-range nature of the loop NMEs.

## Method

The authors evaluated all nuclear matrix elements up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) in chiral effective field theory for the neutrinoless double-beta decay of nine medium-mass and heavy nuclei, using both the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) and the nuclear shell model (NSM). The N$^2$LO NMEs were calculated from very low-momentum (ultrasoft) neutrinos and from loop diagrams usually neglected in $0\nu\beta\beta$ studies, with the NSM calculations performed using the codes ANTOINE and NATHAN across three different valence spaces, and the pnQRPA using no-core single-particle bases with interactions derived from the Bonn-A potential.

## Key numbers

- For $^{76}$Ge: LO long-range NME = 5.23(58) (pnQRPA) and 3.57(25) (NSM); LO short-range NME = 2.65(116) (pnQRPA) and 0.97(48) (NSM); N$^2$LO total ultrasoft NME = 0.39(31) (pnQRPA) and -0.29(9) (NSM); N$^2$LO loop soft NME = 0.28(41) (pnQRPA) and 0.05(16) (NSM)
- For $^{100}$Mo: LO long-range NME = 3.44(73) (pnQRPA); LO short-range NME = 2.96(130) (pnQRPA); N$^2$LO total ultrasoft NME = 1.15(52) (pnQRPA); N$^2$LO loop soft NME = 0.38(43) (pnQRPA)
- For $^{136}$Xe: LO long-range NME = 2.99(28) (pnQRPA) and 2.26(53) (NSM); LO short-range NME = 1.36(60) (pnQRPA) and 0.86(41) (NSM); N$^2$LO total ultrasoft NME = 0.06(14) (pnQRPA) and -0.19(11) (NSM); N$^2$LO loop soft NME = 0.16(32) (pnQRPA) and 0.05(13) (NSM)
- For $^{48}$Ca (NSM only): LO long-range NME = 0.92(14); LO short-range NME = 0.43(20); N$^2$LO total ultrasoft NME = 0.01(6); N$^2$LO loop soft NME = 0.05(7)
- The N$^2$LO total ultrasoft NME for $^{100}$Mo reaches 40% of the LO NME, and the combined N$^2$LO contribution can reach up to 60% for this nucleus
- The ultrasoft scale was varied as $\mu_{\rm us} = m_\pi/2$ to $2m_\pi$, and the renormalization scale $\mu$ was varied from 500 to 1500 MeV
- Gaussian regulators were varied between $\Lambda = 349$ and 550 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory treatment of $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay NMEs beyond the leading-order short-range NME promoted by Cirigliano (2018, 2019), which was found to be a 50% correction for various many-body methods including the NSM (Jokiniemi 2021), pnQRPA (Jokiniemi 2021), and VS-IMSRG (Belley 2024). The N$^2$LO ultrasoft NME had only been studied for sterile neutrino scenarios in $^{76}$Ge and $^{136}$Xe with the NSM (Dekens 2023, 2024), while loop NMEs had only been calculated in light systems with quantum Monte Carlo (Pastore 2018) and in $^{76}$Ge using VS-IMSRG (Belley 2024). The results enable future work using chiral EFT Hamiltonians to check that the scale dependence of the ultrasoft NME vanishes, and inclusion of two-body currents that enter at N$^3$LO, which may be critical for understanding the "quenching" of matrix elements in many-body calculations.
