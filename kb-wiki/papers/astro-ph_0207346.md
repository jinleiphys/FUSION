# Quark color superconductivity and the cooling of compact stars

> The thermal conductivity of the color-flavor locked phase of dense quark matter is calculated. The dominant contribution to the conductivity comes from photons and Nambu-Goldstone bosons associated with the breaking of baryon number, both of which are trapped in the quark core. Because of their very large mean free path the conductivity is also very large. The cooling of the quark core arises mostly from the heat flux across the surface of direct contact with the nuclear matter. As the thermal conductivity of the neighboring layer is also high, the whole interior of the star should be nearly isothermal. Our results imply that the cooling time of compact stars with color-flavor locked quark cores is similar to that of ordinary neutron stars.

## Key claim
The cooling time of compact stars with color-flavor locked (CFL) quark cores is similar to that of ordinary neutron stars, provided the outer nuclear layer is not extremely thin. This conclusion depends on the assumption that the thermal energy of the quark core is negligible compared to that of the nuclear matter, and that heat transfer across the core–nuclear interface is efficient.

## Method
The authors calculated the thermal conductivity of the CFL phase of dense quark matter using a Kubo-type formula in linear response theory, with contributions from photons and Nambu-Goldstone (NG) bosons. The mean free paths of the massless NG boson \(\phi\) and photons were estimated from decay widths and scattering cross sections, and the total conductivity was obtained by summing the two contributions. The cooling time was then derived by comparing the thermal energy stored in the quark core and the nuclear layer with the neutrino and photon luminosities.

## Key numbers
- Thermal conductivity of CFL quark matter: \(\kappa_{CFL} \simeq 1.2 \times 10^{32} \, T_{\rm MeV}^{3} R_{0,{\rm km}} \; \text{erg cm}^{-1} \text{sec}^{-1} \text{K}^{-1}\), where \(T_{\rm MeV}\) is temperature in MeV and \(R_{0,{\rm km}}\) is core radius in km.
- Mean free path of NG bosons from self-scattering: \(\ell_{\phi\phi} \sim 8 \times 10^5 \, \mu_{500}^8 / T_{\rm MeV}^9 \; \text{km}\), where \(\mu_{500} \equiv \mu/(500\ \text{MeV})\).
- Mean free path of NG bosons from decay into quarks: \(\ell_{\phi\to q q} \sim (v/T) \exp\left(\sqrt{3/2} \, \Delta/T\right)\); for \(\Delta \simeq 50\) MeV and \(T \lesssim 1.5\) MeV, this exceeds 30 km.
- Plasma frequency of nuclear matter: \(\Omega_p \simeq 4.7 \times 10^2 \sqrt{\rho Y_e / \rho_0} \; \text{MeV}\), with \(\rho_0 \approx 2.8 \times 10^{14} \ \text{g cm}^{-3}\) and \(Y_e \simeq 0.1\).
- Thermal energy of CFL quark core: \(E_{CFL}(T) \simeq 2.1 \times 10^{42} R_{0,{\rm km}}^3 T_{\rm MeV}^4 \; \text{erg}\).
- Thermal energy of nuclear layer: \(E_{NM}(T) \simeq 8.1 \times 10^{49} \frac{M-M_0}{M_\odot} (\rho_0/\rho)^{2/3} T_{\rm MeV}^2 \; \text{erg}\).
- Time to wash out a temperature gradient across a 1 km core: \(\sim 6 \times 10^{-4}\) sec.

## Context
This work extends studies of color superconductivity in dense quark matter, particularly the CFL phase proposed by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999), and builds on earlier calculations of neutrino and photon emission rates in the CFL phase by Jaikumar, Prakash, and Schäfer (2002). The authors provide the first quantitative calculation of thermal conductivity in the CFL phase, showing that it is many orders of magnitude larger than that of ordinary neutron-star matter (van Riper, 1991). The results imply that the cooling of stars with CFL cores is nearly indistinguishable from ordinary neutron stars, contradicting earlier expectations that exotic cores would significantly alter cooling. This enables future work on the detectability of such stars via thermal emission and on the possibility of bare CFL quark stars as dark matter candidates.
