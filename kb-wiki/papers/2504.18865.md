# Relativistic four-nucleon calculations with rank-one separable potential

> A solution to the relativistic generalization of the four-particle integral Faddeev-Yakubovsky equation is carried out. Only states with zero orbital angular momentum, $S$ states, are considered in the calculations. A rank-one separable potential for nucleon-nucleon interaction is used to solve the two-nucleon Bethe-Salpeter equation. Calculations are carried out taking into account both "3+1" and "2+2" subchannels in the equation. The system of integral equations is solved by the iteration method and the binding energy of the helium-4 nucleus is found. The calculated results are compared with experimental data and nonrelativistic.

## Key claim

The relativistic generalization of the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equation for four nucleons, solved with a rank-one separable potential and including both "3+1" and "2+2" subchannels, yields helium-4 binding energies that differ from experimental data by several MeV, with the inclusion of the "2+2" subchannel reducing the binding energy by 2-7 MeV in most cases.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic generalization of the four-particle integral Faddeev-Yakubovsky equation using an iteration method with Gaussian quadrature for integral calculations. Only S-states with zero orbital angular momentum were considered, and a rank-one separable Yamaguchi potential in monopole (Y) and dipole (Y2) forms was used for the nucleon-nucleon interaction via the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The system of equations was solved on a mesh of (N_q4, N_q) = (11,9) points, with interpolation used for off-mesh values, and the binding energy was found as the parameter where the ratio of successive iterations approaches 1.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of helium-4 with monopole potential (Y), including both ^1S_0 and ^3S_1 states and the "2+2" subchannel: 24 MeV (relativistic calculation), compared to experimental value of 28.3 MeV.
* Binding energy of helium-4 with dipole potential (Y2), including both ^1S_0 and ^3S_1 states and the "2+2" subchannel: 22 MeV (relativistic calculation).
* Binding energy of helium-4 with monopole potential (Y), ^3S_1 state only, without "2+2" subchannel: 58 MeV (relativistic calculation); with "2+2" subchannel: 51 MeV.
* Binding energy of helium-4 with dipole potential (Y2), ^3S_1 state only, without "2+2" subchannel: 51 MeV; with "2+2" subchannel: 45 MeV.
* Deuteron binding energy from dipole potential (Y2): 2.2264 MeV (experimental value: 2.2246 MeV).
* Scattering length from dipole potential (Y2): 5.423 Fm for ^3S_1, -23.78 Fm for ^1S_0 (experimental: 5.424 Fm and -23.748 Fm, respectively).
* Effective radius from dipole potential (Y2): 1.77 Fm for ^3S_1, 2.71 Fm for ^1S_0 (experimental: 1.756 Fm and 2.75 Fm, respectively).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic generalization of the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equation (Bondarenko 2024) by including the "2+2" subchannel, which was omitted in the earlier calculation. The approach builds on the Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev formalism developed for three-nucleon systems (Bondarenko 2020, Bondarenko 2021) and contrasts with non-relativistic methods (Faddeev, Yakubovsky 1966) and other relativistic approaches such as Gross's single-particle-off-mass-shell method and the Glockle group's Poincare-invariant formalism (W). The results enable future calculations with multirank potentials and the computation of the charge form factor of helium-4.
