# Hadron electric polarizability -- finite volume corrections

> We use the background field method to extract the polarizability for the neutral "pion". In our previous study we found that the polarizability for this system is negative which is believed to be a finite volume artifact. To address this issue, we carry out simulations for different lattice sizes and we also look at the influence of the boundary conditions on these results. We find that for pion masses lower than 400 MeV the polarizability remains negative even on larger lattices. An infinite volume extrapolation is attempted, but the results are not conclusive due mainly to a lack of an analytical form for the finite volume corrections for this system.

## Key claim
The "connected" part of the neutral pion electric polarizability remains negative for pion masses below 400 MeV even on larger lattices, contradicting the expectation that the negative sign is purely a finite volume artifact; however, an infinite volume extrapolation is inconclusive because no analytical form for the finite volume corrections is available.

## Method
The authors used the background field method on quenched lattice QCD with Wilson fermions at β=6.0 (a=0.093 fm), introducing a static electric field via a complex phase factor with Dirichlet boundary conditions in both time and the electric field direction. They computed the mass shift of the neutral "pion" for three lattice sizes (Nx × 24² × 48 with Nx = 24, 36, 48) at four quark masses corresponding to pion masses from 270 to 700 MeV, and compared results with periodic boundary conditions using two different time origins.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.093 fm
- Pion mass range studied: mπ = 270 – 700 MeV
- Smallest spatial size: L = 24a, giving mπL = 3.2 – 8
- Electric field in dimensionless units: η = a² |qd| E = a² e E/3 = 5.76 × 10⁻³
- Quark masses used: κ = 0.1567, 0.1565, 0.1562, 0.1546
- Statistics: 1000 configurations for Dirichlet boundary conditions, 600 for periodic boundary conditions

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Alexandru 2009) which found negative connected polarizability for the neutral pion at low masses, suspected to be a finite volume artifact. It also relates to calculations by Detmold (2009) that observed similar negative values using dynamical configurations, and to chiral perturbation theory predictions by Tiburzi (2008) that expected small positive values. The results highlight the need for an analytical form of finite volume corrections for polarizabilities, and the authors plan to add more ensembles and increase statistics for periodic boundary conditions to improve the reliability of infinite volume extrapolation.
