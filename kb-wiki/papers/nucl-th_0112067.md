# Total Photoabsorption Cross Sections of A=6 Nuclei with Complete Final State Interaction

> The total photoabsorption cross sections of 6He and 6Li are calculated microscopically with full inclusion of the six-nucleon final state interaction using semirealistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method and the effective interaction approach for the hyperspherical formalism are employed. While 6Li has a single broad giant resonance peak, there are two well separated peaks for 6He corresponding to the breakup of the neutron halo and the alpha core, respectively. The comparison with the few available experimental data is discussed.

## Key claim
The first microscopic calculation of a six-body inclusive reaction with complete final state interaction is presented, but the authors note that the use of semirealistic central S-wave nucleon-nucleon potentials means the results are not expected to be realistic in all details, particularly at low energies where missing P-wave interactions likely affect the cross section.

## Method
The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method was applied to calculate the total photoabsorption cross sections of 6He and 6Li, using the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) approach to expand the ground and Lorentz states. The LIT was evaluated via the Lanczos algorithm, avoiding direct solution of the Schrodinger-like equation, with semirealistic MTI-III and MN nucleon-nucleon potentials.

## Key numbers
- 6He low-energy peak (T=1 channel) at ω = 7.5 MeV (MN potential) and 9.5 MeV (MTI-III potential), attributed to neutron halo breakup
- 6He second peak at approximately ω = 35 MeV, corresponding to alpha core breakup, with about 40% of strength from the T=2 channel
- Enhancements κ_TRK of the classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn value (59.74 NZ/A MeVmb): 0.42 (6Li, MN), 0.47 (6Li, MTI-III), 0.45 (6He, MN), and 0.50 (6He, MTI-III)
- Estimated errors of less than 10% in the calculated cross sections from convergence behavior

## Context
This work extends the LIT method, previously applied to few-body nuclei up to A=4 (Efros et al. 1997, Barnea et al. 2001), to the six-body system for the first time with complete final state interaction. The results are compared to experimental data from Aumann (1998) for 6He Coulomb excitation and to 6Li data from Berman (1965), Junghans (1979), and Sherman (1975), though the authors note the experimental situation is not settled. The calculation enables future work with realistic NN and three-nucleon forces and motivates new experimental measurements of six-nucleon photoabsorption.
