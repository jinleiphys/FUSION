# Argon spectral function and neutrino interactions

> The argon spectral function is constructed and applied to neutrino-argon cross section computations in the plane wave impulse approximation with the Pauli blocking final state interaction effect taken into account. The approximations of the construction method are critically analyzed using the example of oxygen for which more detailed computations are available. An effective description of nucleus based on the information contained in a spectral function is proposed. It is demonstrated that its predictions are close to those obtained from the complete spectral function. The effective description can be easily applied in Monte Carlo event generators.

## Key claim

The authors construct an argon spectral function using an approximate method based on the Kulagin-Petti approach, and they claim that this approximate spectral function yields predictions close to those from a complete spectral function, but only after verifying the method against oxygen for which detailed computations exist.

## Method

The spectral function is built from mean-field and short-range correlation contributions using the Kulagin-Petti formalism, with the mean-field part approximated by a single average one-nucleon separation energy and the correlation part modeled from two-nucleon clusters. For argon, the authors approximate the missing nuclear structure data (energy levels and occupation probabilities) using calcium data, and they include an isovector contribution for the neutron excess. The cross sections are computed in the plane wave impulse approximation with Pauli blocking treated as a final state interaction effect, using the de Forest prescription for off-shell nucleons.

## Key numbers

- One-nucleon separation energy for oxygen: 19.18 MeV for protons, 22.32 MeV for neutrons
- Two-nucleon separation energy for oxygen: 26.33 MeV
- Mean square momentum for oxygen: (162.2 MeV/c)^2
- One-nucleon separation energy for argon (neutrons): 29.26 MeV
- Two-nucleon separation energy for argon: 16.46 MeV
- Mean square momentum for argon: (177.2 MeV)^2
- Average Fermi momentum used for Pauli blocking: 209 MeV for oxygen, 217 MeV for argon
- Axial mass used in all calculations: M_A = 1.03 GeV

## Context

This work extends the spectral function approach, previously applied to light nuclei up to oxygen by Benhar et al. and to infinite nuclear matter, to the case of argon, which is relevant for liquid-argon neutrino detectors such as ICARUS and the proposed T2K experiment. The authors follow the method of Kulagin and Petti (2005) and compare their oxygen results against the detailed spectral function computations of Benhar, Farina, and Nakamura to validate the approximations. The effective description proposed in the paper is intended for implementation in Monte Carlo event generators, which currently rely on simpler Fermi gas models such as the Smith-Moniz or Bodek-Ritchie versions.
