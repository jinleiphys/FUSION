# Valence Quark Polarization In the Nucleon And the Deuteron Data

> Within the framework of the so-called "valon" model, we argue that a substantial part of the nucleon spin, about 40%, is carried by the polarized valence quarks. The remaining is the result of cancellations between gluon polarization and the orbital angular momentum, where the gluon polarization is the dominant one. It is shown that the sea quark contribution to the spin of any hadron is simply marginal and consistent with zero. Our findings point to a substantially smaller value for a_8 thaninferred from hyperon- $β$ decay, suggesting that fullSU(3) symmetric assumption needs to be reconsidered. New and emerging experimental data tend to support this finding. Finally, we show that within the model described here the experimental data on the polarized structure functions $g_{1}^{p,n,d}$ are reproduced.

## Key claim

Within the valon model, about 40% of the nucleon spin is carried by polarized valence quarks, while the remaining spin arises from cancellations between gluon polarization (dominant) and orbital angular momentum; the sea quark contribution to the spin of any hadron is marginal and consistent with zero, and the value of a_8 is substantially smaller than inferred from hyperon beta decay, suggesting the full SU(3) symmetric assumption needs reconsideration.

## Method

The authors use the valon model, in which a nucleon is composed of three dressed valence quarks (valons), each with internal structure calculated in next-to-leading order QCD in the MSbar scheme with Lambda_QCD = 0.22 GeV and Q_0^2 = 0.283 GeV^2. The polarized structure functions of the proton, neutron, and deuteron are obtained via convolution integrals of the valon helicity distributions in the hadron with the polarized structure functions of the valons themselves, without any fit to experimental data. The deuteron structure function g_1^d is computed as the average of proton and neutron structure functions with a D-wave correction factor.

## Key numbers

- Valence quark contribution to the proton spin (Delta Sigma) lies in the range 0.410–0.420 for Q^2 = [1, 10] GeV^2.
- The integral of (Delta u_v + Delta d_v) from COMPASS is 0.41 ± 0.07 ± 0.05, and from HERMES is 0.359 ± 0.026 ± 0.018.
- The model value for a_8 is 0.39, compared to the hyperon beta decay value of 0.579 ± 0.025.
- COMPASS data gives a_8 = 0.40 ± 0.07 ± 0.05; HERMES gives a_8 = 0.285 ± 0.046 ± 0.057.
- The total quark spin contribution in a valon (Delta Sigma_valon) is approximately 0.88 for Q^2 >= 1 GeV^2, nearly independent of Q^2.
- The sea quark polarization for individual flavor in a valon is around -0.002.
- The Bjorken sum rule value a_3 = g_A = 1.2573 ± 0.0028 is reproduced accurately.

## Context

This work extends the valon model approach of Arash (2005) to analyze spin structure, and directly addresses the findings of the COMPASS collaboration (2007) and HERMES collaboration (2004, 2005) that sea quark polarization is consistent with zero. The results contradict the standard assumption of full SU(3) flavor symmetry used to extract a_8 from hyperon beta decay, as previously questioned by Ratcliffe (1983) and others. The model enables a consistent description of polarized structure functions g_1^p, g_1^n, and g_1^d without fitting to data, and points toward the need for reassessment of the SU(3) symmetric sea assumption in future analyses.
