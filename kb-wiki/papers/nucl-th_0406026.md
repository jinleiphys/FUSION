# The Nucleon-Mass Difference in Chiral Perturbation Theory and Nuclear Forces

> A new method is developed for treating the effect of the neutron-proton mass difference in isospin-violating nuclear forces. Previous treatments utilized an awkward subtraction scheme to generate these forces. A field redefinition is used to remove that mass difference from the Lagrangian (and hence from asymptotic nucleon states) and replace its effect by effective interactions. Previous calculations of static Class II charge-independence-breaking and Class III charge-symmetry-breaking potentials are verified using the new scheme, which is also used to calculate Class IV nuclear forces. Two-body forces of the latter type are found to be identical to previously obtained results. A novel three-body force is also found. Problems involving Galilean invariance with Class IV one-pion-exchange forces are identified and resolved.

## Key claim

The paper develops a field-redefinition method that removes the neutron-proton mass difference from asymptotic nucleon states and replaces it with effective interactions, verifying previous static Class II and III charge-symmetry-breaking potentials while also calculating Class IV nuclear forces and identifying a novel three-body force, with the caveat that the method works to leading order in the mass difference and requires perturbative treatment.

## Method

The authors apply a time-dependent isospin rotation to the nucleon and pion fields, transforming away the δM_N τ_3 term from the Lagrangian and generating new effective interactions that compensate for the removal. They then use these transformed interactions to derive one-pion-exchange and two-pion-exchange potentials for Class II, III, and IV charge-symmetry-breaking forces in chiral perturbation theory, verifying consistency with previous subtraction-scheme calculations by Friar, van Kolck, Rentmeester, and Timmermans (2003) for Class III forces and by Friar and van Kolck (1999) for Class II forces.

## Key numbers

- δM_N = m_p - m_n ≈ 1.29 MeV (the neutron-proton mass difference, dominated by the up-down quark-mass difference with ε = (m_d - m_u)/(m_d + m_u) ≈ 0.3)
- δm_π² ≈ (38 MeV)² (the pion mass-squared splitting, dominated by electromagnetic contributions of order αΛ²/π)
- g_A ≈ 1.26 (the axial coupling constant, an O(1) parameter)
- The Class IV one-pion-exchange potential has radial function w_a(r) = (δM_N g_A²)/(4 f_π² M_N) × h_0'(r)/r, where h_0(z) = (1/(4πz))e^{-m_π z}
- The Breit-interaction Class IV force has w_b^γ(r) = (α κ_n)/(4 M_N² r³) with κ_n ≈ -1.91 (neutron anomalous magnetic moment)
- The leading short-range Class IV interaction is of chiral order n=4, with coupling δ₁ = O(ε m_π²/Λ⁴)

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of isospin violation in nuclear forces, previously applied to Class II forces by Friar and van Kolck (1999) and to Class III forces by Friar, van Kolck, Rentmeester, and Timmermans (2003), by completing the classification with Class IV forces. The new field-redefinition method simplifies calculations and enables future treatment of three-body isospin-violating forces, which would be difficult with the earlier subtraction scheme. The results are directly relevant to experiments measuring neutron-proton analyzing-power differences at 183 MeV (Iowa group), 347 MeV (TRIUMF), and 477 MeV (TRIUMF), where the dominant Class IV mechanisms are the electromagnetic Breit interaction, the one-pion-exchange force from the nucleon mass difference, and short-range ρ-ω mixing.
