# A domain wall and chiral edge currents in holographic chiral phase transitions

> We investigate spatially inhomogeneous solutions in a top-down holographic model: the D3/D7 model which provides a holographic description of the chiral phase transition for a finite external magnetic field, chemical potential, and temperature. We numerically find a domain wall (or kink) solution in the three dimensional space, which incorporates between the chiral symmetry broken phase at the spatial infinity, under the homogeneous sources. Along with the inhomogeneity of the chiral condensate, the charge density is also spatially modulated. The modulated charge density and finite magnetic field lead to the chiral edge current close to the domain wall. We explore the dependences of those profiles on the chemical potential and temperature near the first and second order phase transition points. Our results indicate that the inhomogeneous solutions we found are in good agreement with those obtained by the Ginzburg--Landau theory in the vicinity of the transition points.

## Key claim

The authors numerically find a domain wall (kink) solution connecting chiral-symmetry-broken phases in the top-down D3/D7 holographic model, but only in the vicinity of the chiral phase transition points and only when the external magnetic field is present to enable spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in the homogeneous setup.

## Method

The authors solve the nonlinear Dirac-Born-Infeld equations of motion for the probe D7-brane in an AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background, with a finite external magnetic field along the z-direction, chemical potential, and temperature. They impose Neumann boundary conditions at spatial infinity and regularity at the black hole horizon, then extract the dual field theory operators (chiral condensate, charge density, and current density) from the asymptotic expansion near the AdS boundary. The numerical solutions are obtained for a one-dimensional spatial modulation along the x-direction with a system size fixed to l=8.

## Key numbers

* The second order phase transition point at fixed temperature $\pi T = 0.070711\sqrt{2B}$ occurs at $\mu_c = 0.60146 \sqrt{B}$.
* The wavenumber of the static perturbation near the second order transition follows $k_{\text{sp}}/\mu_c \approx 4.62 \sqrt{1 - \mu/\mu_c}$.
* For the kink solution at $\mu/\sqrt{B}=0.55$ and $\pi T/\sqrt{2B}=0.075$, the coefficient relating chiral edge current to the charge density gradient is $\sqrt{B} \gamma \approx 0.3426$.
* The healing length from the Ginzburg-Landau theory is given by $\xi = \sqrt{2/[a(\mu_c-\mu)]}$.

## Context

This work extends the study of spatially inhomogeneous solutions from bottom-up holographic superfluid models (Keranen 2009, Albash 2009, Montull 2009) to the top-down D3/D7 model, where the dual field theory is the N=4 SYM theory with N=2 fundamental hypermultiplets. The results agree with the Ginzburg-Landau theory near the transition points, and the chiral edge current arises from the combination of the modulated charge density and the finite magnetic field, analogous to edge currents in condensed matter systems. The findings enable future studies of inhomogeneous phases in holographic QCD-like models and provide a top-down realization of phenomena previously studied only in bottom-up approaches.
