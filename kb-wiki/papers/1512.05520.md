# Antiproton--proton annihilation into pion pairs within effective meson theory

> Antiproton--proton annihilation into light mesons is revisited in the few GeV energy domain, in view of a global description of the existing data. An effective meson model is developed, with mesonic and baryonic degrees od freedom in $s$, $t$, and $u$ channels. Regge factors are added to reproduce the proper energy behavior and the forward and backward peaked behavior. A comparison with existing data and predictions for angular distributions and energy dependence are done for charged and neutral pion pair production.

## Key claim

The authors develop an effective meson model for antiproton-proton annihilation into pion pairs that, with a minimal set of parameters fixed primarily on neutral pion data, can simultaneously describe charged pion production and crossed-channel pion-proton elastic scattering without readjustment, though the agreement is less satisfactory at lower energies where oscillations near cosθ=0 are not reproduced.

## Method

The model computes amplitudes for baryon exchange (t-channel neutron and Δ⁰, u-channel Δ⁺⁺) and s-channel ρ-meson exchange, with coupling constants fixed from known particle decays. Regge factors are added to t- and u-channel diagrams to reproduce the proper energy dependence and forward/backward peaking, with form factors introduced at vertices to account for the composite nature of hadrons. The parameters are first adjusted to reproduce neutral pion angular distribution data from the E760 collaboration, then applied without change to charged pion production and to π±p elastic scattering via crossing symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Energy range of applicability: 2 ≤ √s ≤ 5.5 GeV, corresponding to the accessible domain for the PANDA experiment at FAIR
- Neutral pion angular distributions reproduced for energies spanning 2.911 ≤ √s ≤ 4.274 GeV from E760 data (Armstrong 1997)
- Regge trajectory slope parameter for nucleon: rαs/π = 0.7; for Δ resonance: rαs/π = 1.4
- Regge scale parameter s₀ = 1.4 GeV²
- Form factor cutoff for Np\bar{p} vertex: p_N = 0.8 GeV; for Np\bar{Δ} vertex: p_Δ = 5 GeV
- ρNN coupling: g²_ρNN/(4π) = 0.55, with anomalous magnetic moment κ_ρ = 3.7 and cutoff Λ = 0.911 GeV
- ρ-meson diagram renormalization factor N_ρ = 0.9, with s-dependent relative phase φ_ρ = φ₀ + φ₁ s where φ₀ = 1 and φ₁ = 0.004
- Δ resonance mass: M_Δ = 1232 ± 2 MeV, width Γ_Δ = 117 ± 3 MeV, coupling g_ΔπN = 0.0157 ± 0.0004 MeV⁻¹
- Quark counting rule prediction of s⁻⁸ behavior at large angles compared for cosθ = 0.0125

## Context

This work extends earlier models by Moussallam (1984) applicable below 1 GeV beam momentum and by VandeWiele (2010) at larger energies, which the authors note warned against application to neighboring energies due to Regge trajectory extrapolation issues. The model is developed in anticipation of data from the PANDA experiment at FAIR, where pions constitute an important background for many other channels. The approach enables future extension to other binary channels with appropriate changes of constants and implementation into Monte Carlo simulations for experimental predictions.
