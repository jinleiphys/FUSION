# Precision spectroscopy of pionic 1s states of Sn nuclei and evidence for partial restoration of chiral symmetry in the nuclear medium

> Deeply bound 1s states of $π^-$ in $^{115,119,123}$Sn were preferentially observed using the Sn($d$,$^3$He) pion-transfer reaction under the recoil-free condition. The 1s binding energies and widths were precisely determined, and were used to deduce the isovector parameter of the s-wave pion-nucleus potential to be $b_1 =-0.115\pm 0.007 ~m_π^{-1}$. The observed enhancement of $|b_1|$ over the free $πN$ value ($b_1^{\rm free}/b_1 = 0.78 \pm 0.05$) indicates a reduction of the chiral order parameter, $f^{*}_π (ρ)^2/f_π^2 \approx 0.64$, at the normal nuclear density, $ρ= ρ_0$.

## Key claim

The measured isovector s-wave pion-nucleus potential parameter \(b_1 = -0.115 \pm 0.007~m_\pi^{-1}\) is enhanced over the free \(\pi N\) value, with the ratio \(b_1^{\rm free}/b_1 = 0.78 \pm 0.05\), which indicates a reduction of the chiral order parameter \(f^{*}_\pi(\rho)^2/f_\pi^2 \approx 0.64\) at normal nuclear density \(\rho = \rho_0\), but this interpretation assumes that the effective density sampled by the pionic 1s states is \(\rho_e \approx 0.6\rho_0\).

## Method

Deeply bound pionic 1s states in \(^{115,119,123}\)Sn were produced via the Sn(\(d\),\(^3\)He) pion-transfer reaction under recoil-free conditions using a 503.388 MeV deuteron beam from the SIS synchrotron at GSI, with the Fragment Separator as a high-resolution forward spectrometer. The binding energies and widths were extracted by decomposing the measured \(^3\)He kinetic-energy spectra according to theoretical predictions for the dominant \((1s)_\pi(3s)_n^{-1}\) configuration, using the non-pionic Sn(\(d\),\(^3\)He)In(g.s.) reactions for momentum calibration and the \(p(d,^3\)He)\(\pi^0\) reaction from a mylar layer for absolute energy calibration. A simultaneous fit of the 1s binding energies and widths from the three Sn isotopes together with those from light symmetric nuclei (\(^{16}\)O, \(^{20}\)Ne, \(^{28}\)Si) was performed to extract the s-wave optical potential parameters \(b_0\), \(b_1\), Re\(B_0\), and Im\(B_0\).

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of the 1s state in \(^{115}\)Sn: \(B_{1s} = 3.906 \pm 0.024\) MeV (total error)
* Binding energy of the 1s state in \(^{119}\)Sn: \(B_{1s} = 3.820 \pm 0.018\) MeV (total error)
* Binding energy of the 1s state in \(^{123}\)Sn: \(B_{1s} = 3.744 \pm 0.018\) MeV (total error)
* Width of the 1s state in \(^{115}\)Sn: \(\Gamma_{1s} = 0.441 \pm 0.087\) MeV (total error)
* Width of the 1s state in \(^{119}\)Sn: \(\Gamma_{1s} = 0.326 \pm 0.080\) MeV (total error)
* Width of the 1s state in \(^{123}\)Sn: \(\Gamma_{1s} = 0.341 \pm 0.072\) MeV (total error)
* Isovector s-wave parameter: \(b_1 = -0.1149 \pm 0.0074~m_\pi^{-1}\)
* Ratio of free to in-medium isovector parameter: \(b_1^{\rm free}/b_1 = 0.78 \pm 0.05\)
* Chiral order parameter reduction: \(f^{*}_\pi(\rho_0)^2/f_\pi^2 \approx 0.64\)
* Isoscalar parameter: \(b_0 = -0.0233 \pm 0.0038~m_\pi^{-1}\)
* Imaginary part of s-wave parameter: Im\(B_0 = 0.0472 \pm 0.0013~m_\pi^{-4}\)
* Real part of s-wave parameter: Re\(B_0 = -0.019 \pm 0.017~m_\pi^{-4}\)
* Incident deuteron energy: 503.388 \(\pm\) 0.100 MeV
* Overall energy resolution: 394 \(\pm\) 33 keV (FWHM)

## Context

This work extends the earlier measurement of the 1s state in \(^{205}\)Pb (Yamazaki et al., 1997) and the global fits of pionic atom data by Batty et al. (1997) and Friedman (2002), providing a more precise determination of the isovector parameter \(b_1\) through systematic isotope studies. The observed reduction of the chiral order parameter at nuclear density supports predictions from chiral perturbation theory (Meissner, 2002) and the leading-order density dependence of the quark condensate via the pion-nucleon sigma term (Drukarev, 1988), enabling quantitative tests of chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear matter.
