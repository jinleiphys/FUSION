# Two complex problems on the lattice: transport coefficients and finite chemical potential

> After a few remarks about the problem of extracting transport coefficients from lattice QCD calculations, I report on recent developments in applying stochastic quantization and complex Langevin dynamics to field theories with a complex action due to a nonzero chemical potential. First results demonstrate that the sign problem poses no obstacle for this approach, even in the thermodynamic limit. I conclude with a comparison of two simple one-link models, describing a euclidean system at finite chemical potential and a Minkowski system in real time.

## Key claim

Stochastic quantization with complex Langevin dynamics can handle the sign problem in field theories with a nonzero chemical potential without difficulty, even in the thermodynamic limit, but the approach is not guaranteed to converge for Minkowski (real-time) dynamics where no real-action reference point exists.

## Method

The author applies complex Langevin dynamics to two systems: a relativistic Bose gas (complex scalar field with O(2) symmetry and quartic interaction) at nonzero chemical potential, and SU(3) gauge theory with three heavy quark flavors in the hopping expansion. In both cases, the action is complexified and the field variables are extended to complex values (scalar fields become complex, SU(3) matrices become elements of SL(3, C)). The method is compared with phase-quenched simulations and with a one-link U(1) model in both euclidean and Minkowski settings, where the smallest nonzero eigenvalue of the complex Fokker-Planck operator is computed numerically.

## Key numbers

* Relativistic Bose gas: transition from zero-density groundstate to Bose condensed phase occurs at critical chemical potential mu_c ~ 1.15 (in lattice units with m = lambda = 1) on lattices of size N^4 with N = 4, 6, 8, 10.
* Heavy quark QCD: results on a 4^4 lattice with beta = 5.6, kappa = 0.12, and N_f = 3 show a transition from confined low-density to deconfined high-density phase as chemical potential increases.
* One-link U(1) model: in the euclidean case at kappa = 1/2, the smallest nonzero eigenvalue of the complex Fokker-Planck operator remains strictly positive for all values of beta and mu; in the Minkowski case, eigenvalues become negative for larger beta, with the threshold depending on the reweighting parameter p.

## Context

This work extends the revival of complex Langevin dynamics for nonequilibrium quantum field dynamics in Minkowski spacetime by Berges (2005-2007) to euclidean theories at nonzero chemical potential. It addresses the sign problem that prevents standard importance sampling in lattice QCD at nonzero baryon density, and demonstrates the Silver Blaze problem (Cohen, 2003) in the relativistic Bose gas. The comparison of euclidean and Minkowski one-link models quantifies why real-time dynamics is expected to be more difficult than finite chemical potential, building on earlier work by Berges (2007). The results enable further application of complex Langevin dynamics to QCD at finite density and suggest that transport coefficient extraction from euclidean correlators remains challenging, particularly when the transport peak is narrow.
