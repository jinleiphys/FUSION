# Roles of fast neutrino-flavor conversion on the neutrino-heating mechanism of core-collapse supernova

> One of the greatest uncertainties in any modeling of inner engine of core-collapse supernova (CCSN) is neutrino flavor conversions driven by neutrino self-interactions. We carry out large-scale numerical simulations of multi-energy, multi-angle, three-flavor framework, and general relativistic quantum kinetic neutrino transport in spherical symmetry with an essential set of neutrino-matter interactions under a realistic fluid profile of CCSN. Our result suggests that the neutrino heating in the gain region is reduced by $\sim 40\%$ due to fast neutrino-flavor conversion (FFC). We also find that the total luminosity of neutrinos is enhanced by $\sim 30 \%$, for which the substantial increase of heavy-leptonic neutrinos by FFCs are mainly responsible. This study provides evidence that FFC has a significant impact on the delayed neutrino-heating mechanism.

## Key claim
The paper suggests that fast neutrino-flavor conversion (FFC) reduces neutrino heating in the gain region by approximately 40%, but this result carries the caveat that the simulations use an attenuated Hamiltonian potential (parameter xi) and a modified electron-fraction profile with a 10% reduction from spherical symmetry, both of which are pragmatic approximations rather than fully self-consistent treatments.

## Method
The authors solve the general relativistic quantum kinetic equation (QKE) for multi-energy, multi-angle, three-flavor neutrino transport in spherical symmetry using the GRQKNT code, assuming Schwarzschild spacetime. They adopt a fluid background from a 15 solar mass progenitor at 300 ms after bounce, with electron-fraction reduced by 10% to mimic multi-dimensional effects that produce ELN crossings, and include an essential set of neutrino-matter interactions (electron capture, positron capture, inverse reactions, and isoenergetic scattering). The simulations cover 20 km to 100 km radius with 12 energy bins from 0 to 80 MeV and 96 angular bins, running for 1 ms until quasisteady state with time-averaging over the final 0.05 ms.

## Key numbers
- Neutrino heating in the gain region is reduced by approximately 40% due to FFC (M3FH model, highest resolution).
- Total luminosity of neutrinos at the outer boundary is enhanced by approximately 33% (M3F model, three-flavor flat spacetime).
- Gain radius increases by approximately 7%, causing a reduction of baryon mass in the gain region of approximately 23% (M3F model).
- Net gain energy reduction is approximately 48% in M3F, compared to approximately 16% in the two-flavor framework (M2F model).
- Simulations use an attenuation parameter xi = 10^-4 (M3F, M2F, M3FGR) or 2 x 10^-4 (M3FH), with radial grid sizes of 12,288 or 24,576 points and minimum grid width of 30 cm or 15 cm.

## Context
This work extends previous findings that fast neutrino-flavor conversion can ubiquitously occur in CCSN cores (Nagakura 2021, Abbar 2021) by quantifying its impact on neutrino heating and cooling using full quantum kinetic transport rather than parametric treatments. The results contradict the assumption that neutrino flavor conversion is suppressed by matter refractive effects, showing FFC reduces heating efficiency, but the authors note that radiation-hydrodynamic simulations (e.g., Ehring 2023) are needed to determine whether the competing effects of reduced heating and enhanced cooling ultimately help or hinder explosions. The study enables future work on time-dependent FFC dynamics and multi-dimensional effects, while leaving open questions about the role of many-body neutrino correlations and the dependence on progenitor models and time snapshots.
