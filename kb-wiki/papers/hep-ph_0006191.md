# Radial Excitations of low-lying Baryons and the Structure of the Z^+ Penta-QuarK

> Within the collective quantization scheme for chiral solitons we discuss states in higher dimensional representations of flavor SU(3) and their relation to radially excited states in the octet. We also consider states which do not have counterparts of the same quantum numbers in the octet or decuplet and cannot be built from three quarks. We focus on the Z^+ penta-quark, presumably the lightest such state, by estimating its mass and decay width.

## Key claim

Within the collective quantization scheme for chiral solitons, the Z+ penta-quark is predicted to be the lightest exotic baryon state that cannot be built from three quarks, with its mass and decay width estimated under the condition that radial degrees of freedom are quantized alongside flavor rotations.

## Method

The author uses a chiral Lagrangian in flavor SU(3) with a classical soliton solution, quantizing both flavor rotations via collective coordinates A(t) and radial scaling via a collective coordinate ξ(t). The flavor-symmetric Hamiltonian is diagonalized for given SU(3) representations and radial excitation numbers, then symmetry-breaking terms are included to mix states from different representations, yielding the baryon spectrum. Decay widths are estimated using a flavor operator approach with a scaling function parameterized by a power n, with the constant CΔ fixed to reproduce the experimental Δ → πN width of 120 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The Roper resonance is predicted about 90 MeV too low compared to experimental data.
- The mass difference M(Z+) - M(N) is approximately 630 MeV.
- The predicted mass of the Z+ is about 1.57 GeV.
- The estimated width for the decay Z+ → KN is about 120 MeV.
- The Roper → Nπ decay width ranges from 145 to 429 MeV depending on the scaling parameter n and the model parameter e, compared to the experimental range of 200 to 320 MeV.
- The Σ* → Σπ width is predicted as 1-2 MeV (experimental: 4±1 MeV).
- The Σ* → Λπ width is predicted as 33-43 MeV (experimental: 32±4 MeV).
- The Ξ* → Ξπ width is predicted as 5-11 MeV (experimental: 10±2 MeV).
- The ratio gπNN/gπNΔ is predicted as 0.77-0.83 (experimental: 0.68).

## Context

This work extends the collective quantization scheme for chiral solitons developed by Schechter and Weigel (1991) and Weigel (1996, 1998) to include radial excitations, resolving a puzzle where a naive analysis predicted the Roper as a state from the anti-decuplet representation rather than a radial excitation. The study enables further experimental searches for the Z+ penta-quark, such as the one proposed by Polyakov et al. (1999), and provides a framework for interpreting exotic baryon states within chiral soliton models.
