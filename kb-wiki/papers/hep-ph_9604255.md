# Possible Phases in Strong Interaction Vacuum

> A study of the possible vacuum phases in a strongly interacting two flavor light quark system is presented. Four possible phases are found with some of their properties presented. The possible existence of localized U(1) EM symmetry breaking phases inside a nucleon and excited hadrons is investigated by showing the potential of solving three selected current ``puzzles'' in experimental obervations if such a possibility is taken into account. Base on these results, the potential of producing these phases in heavy ion collisions is speculated.

## Key claim

The strong interaction vacuum of a two-flavor light quark system can exist in four distinct phases, including two phases (beta and omega) that spontaneously break the U(1) baryon number symmetry via diquark condensation; the author suggests that localized regions of these diquark-condensed phases inside nucleons and excited hadrons could resolve three selected experimental puzzles, though this remains a speculative interpretation rather than a proven result.

## Method

The author introduces two massless fermionic models for the strong interaction that inherit the basic symmetries of QCD at long distances, using 4-fermion contact interactions with auxiliary field methods (half-bosonization) and an 8-component Dirac spinor representation. The vacuum phase structure is determined by computing the effective potential in the one-loop Hartree-Fock approximation, minimizing it as a function of the auxiliary fields sigma (quark-antiquark condensate) and chi (diquark condensate). The second part of the study examines three experimental issues (PCAC relation for the nucleon, the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule, and baryon-antibaryon rapidity correlations in e+e- annihilation) using model-independent methods such as symmetry constraints and sum rules.

## Key numbers

* The effective potential minima are located on either the sigma axis (chi=0) or the chi axis (sigma=0), with dimensionless effective potentials given by v_eff(sigma,0) = 3 f(sigma^2/Lambda^2) + (1/(16 pi alpha_0)) sigma^2/Lambda^2 and v_eff(0,chi) = 2 f(chi^2/Lambda^2) + (1/(16 pi alpha_3')) chi^2/Lambda^2, where Lambda is the Euclidean momentum cutoff.
* The dimensionless coupling constants are defined as alpha_0 = G_0 Lambda^2/(4 pi) and alpha_3' = G_3' Lambda^2/(8 pi), with the function f(x) = (1/(8 pi^2))[-x + ln(1+1/x) x^2 - ln(1+x)].
* The phase diagram in the alpha_0-alpha_3' plane shows boundaries at alpha_3' = pi/12 and alpha_0 = pi/8 separating the gamma-phase (chiral symmetric), alpha-phase (chiral symmetry broken to SU(2)_V), and 0-phase (chiral symmetric).

## Context

This work extends the Nambu Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model approach to strong interactions by including color-triplet diquark interaction channels, building on prior work by the author (Ying, 1995) and collaborators. The four phases found (bare vacuum, alpha-phase, beta-phase, omega-phase) go beyond the standard quark-gluon plasma and chiral symmetry restoration phases known from lattice QCD simulations. The study enables future experimental searches for localized diquark-condensed phases inside nucleons and in heavy ion collisions, and suggests modifications to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule that could be tested against experimental data from the OPAL collaboration and others.
