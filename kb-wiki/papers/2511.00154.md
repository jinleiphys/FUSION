# Hadronic scattering in (1+1)D SU(2) lattice gauge theory from tensor networks

> We present a first real-time study of hadronic scattering in a (1+1)-dimensional SU(2) lattice gauge theory with fundamental fermions using tensor-network techniques. Working in the gaugeless Hamiltonian formulation -- where the gauge field is exactly integrated out and no truncation of the electric flux is required -- we investigate scattering processes across sectors of fixed global baryon number $B = 0, 1, 2$. These correspond respectively to meson-meson, meson-baryon, and baryon-baryon collisions. At strong coupling, the $B = 0$ and $B = 2$ channels exhibit predominantly elastic dynamics closely resembling those of the U(1) Schwinger model. In contrast, the mixed $B = 1$ sector shows qualitatively new behavior: meson and baryon wave packets become entangled during the collision, and depending on their initial kinematics, the slower state becomes spatially delocalized while the faster one propagates ballistically. We characterize these processes through local observables, entanglement entropy, and the information-lattice, which together reveal how correlations build up and relax during the interaction. Our results establish a first benchmark for non-Abelian real-time scattering from first principles and open the path toward quantum-simulation studies of baryon-number dynamics and inelastic processes in gauge theories.

## Key claim

The paper presents a first real-time study of hadronic scattering in a non-Abelian (1+1)D SU(2) lattice gauge theory using tensor-network methods, but the results are obtained in the strong-coupling limit (ga = 5, ma = 0.2) and in the gaugeless Hamiltonian formulation where the gauge field is exactly integrated out, meaning the findings are specific to this regime and may not generalize to weaker couplings.

## Method

The authors work in the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian formulation of SU(2) lattice gauge theory with fundamental fermions, using a Jordan-Wigner transformation to map the theory to a spin Hamiltonian on N = 60 qubits. They prepare Gaussian wavepacket states for mesons (B = 0), baryons (B = 1), and anti-baryons (B = -1) using DMRG, then simulate scattering processes via the time-dependent variational principle (TDVP) algorithm with bond dimension chi_max = 80 and time step a*delta_t = 0.1. The dynamics are characterized through local observables including chiral condensate, baryon number, electric charge, chromoelectric energy, meson and baryon projection operators, entanglement entropy, and the information lattice.

## Key numbers

- Lattice parameters: ga = 5, ma = 0.2, corresponding to dimensionless variables mu = 2m/(ag^2) = 0.016 and x = 1/(ag)^2 = 0.04, with volume V = N/sqrt(x) = 300
- DMRG used N_sweep = 2000 sweeps with maximum bond dimension chi_max = 200 and numerical cutoff epsilon_cutoff = 10^{-12}; final results use chi_max = 80
- TDVP time step a*delta_t = 0.1
- Wavepacket width sigma = 4 for all scattering states
- Vacuum state MPS bond dimension chi_{|Omega>} = 42
- Meson mass at leading power: M_m approx 2m + 3ag^2/8
- Baryon mass at leading power: M_b approx 2m
- Meson to baryon mass ratio M_m/M_b approx 1 + 3(ga)^2/(16 ma)

## Context

This work extends recent tensor-network and quantum-simulation studies of real-time scattering from Abelian gauge theories (e.g., the U(1) Schwinger model studied by Papaefstathiou (2024), Chai (2023), and others) to the non-Abelian SU(2) case, which features an additional conserved baryon number B absent in U(1). The results establish a first benchmark for non-Abelian real-time scattering from first principles and open the path toward quantum-simulation studies of baryon-number dynamics and inelastic processes in gauge theories, with potential extensions to (2+1)D models as explored by Pavesic (2025), Cobos (2025), and others.
