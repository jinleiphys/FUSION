# Correlation between balance energy and transition energy for symmetric colliding nuclei

> We study the correlation between balance energy and transition energy of fragment in heavy-ion collisions for different systems at incident energies between 40 and 1200 MeV/nucleon using an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model. With increasing incident energy, the elliptic flow shows a transition from positive (in-plane) to negative (out-of-plane) flow. This transition energy is found to depend on the size of fragments, composite mass of reacting system, and the impact parameter of reaction. It has been observed that reduced cross-section can explain the experimental data. There is a correlation between transition energy and balance energy as their difference decreases with increase in the total mass of colliding nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims a correlation between balance energy and transition energy in heavy-ion collisions, specifically that their difference decreases as the total mass of the colliding nuclei increases, but this correlation is conditional on using a stiff equation of state with a reduced isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross-section (σ = 0.9 σ_NN) within the IQMD model.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate symmetric heavy-ion reactions (⁴⁰Ar+⁴⁵Sc, ⁹³Nb+⁹³Nb, ¹³⁹La+¹³⁹La, and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au) at incident energies between 40 and 1200 MeV/nucleon. They employed a stiff equation of state with a reduced isospin-dependent cross-section (σ = 0.9 σ_NN) and analyzed the phase space using the minimum spanning tree (MST) method to identify fragments, following the time evolution up to 200 fm/c. The elliptic flow coefficient v₂ was calculated as a function of beam energy to extract the transition energy (where v₂ changes sign) and compared with previously calculated balance energies.

## Key numbers

- The exponent τ for the power-law fit of transition energy versus system mass (E_Trans = C(A_tot^(-τ))) is -0.30 ± 0.03 for A=1 fragments, -0.29 ± 0.02 for fragments with 1 ≤ A ≤ 4, and -0.17 ± 0.07 for fragments with 2 ≤ A ≤ 4.
- The exponent τ for the power-law fit of the difference between transition and balance energy versus system mass is -0.14 ± 0.017 for A=1 fragments, -0.061 ± 0.036 for fragments with 1 ≤ A ≤ 4, and -0.0043 ± 0.031 for fragments with 2 ≤ A ≤ 4.
- The maximal negative value of v₂ for Z=2 particles is obtained around E = 500 MeV/nucleon with the reduced isospin-dependent cross-section.
- The Gaussian width L used in IQMD is 8.66 fm² for Au nuclei and 4.33 fm² for Ca nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Kumar, 2010) which showed that experimental balance energies can be explained with reduced isospin-dependent NN cross-section and a hard equation of state. The present study aims to pin down the relation between balance energy and transition energy, finding that they are closer in heavy mass systems compared to lighter ones. The results are compared with experimental data from the INDRA, FOPI, and PLASTIC BALL collaborations (Lukasik, 2005; Andronic, 2001) and show agreement with trends reported by Zhang (2006). This enables future work to use the correlation between these two energies as a probe of the nuclear equation of state and isospin effects in heavy-ion collisions.
