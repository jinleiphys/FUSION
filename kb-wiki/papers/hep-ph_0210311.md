# $S=-1$ Meson-Baryon Unitarized Coupled Channel Chiral Perturbation Theory and the $S_{01}-$ $Λ$(1405) and $- Λ$(1670) Resonances

> The $s-$wave meson-baryon scattering is analyzed for the strangeness $S=-1$ and isospin I=0 sector in a Bethe-Salpeter coupled channel formalism incorporating Chiral Symmetry. Four channels have been considered: $πΣ$, $\bar K N$, $ηΛ$ and $K Ξ$. The required input to solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation is taken from lowest order Chiral Perturbation Theory in a relativistic formalism. There appear undetermined low energy constants, as a consequence of the renormalization of the amplitudes, which are obtained from fits to the $πΣ\toπΣ$ mass-spectrum, to the elastic $\bar K N \to \bar K N$ and $ \bar K N\to πΣ$ $t$--matrices and to the $ K^- p \to ηΛ$ cross section data. The position and residues of the complex poles in the second Riemann Sheet of the scattering amplitude determine masses, widths and branching ratios of the $S_{01}-$ $Λ$(1405) and $-Λ$(1670) resonances, in reasonable agreement with experiment. A good overall description of data, from $πΣ$ threshold up to 1.75 GeV, is achieved despite the fact that three-body channels have not been explicitly included.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a Bethe-Salpeter coupled-channel formalism using only lowest-order Chiral Perturbation Theory as input can describe meson-baryon scattering data from the pi Sigma threshold up to 1.75 GeV, provided the renormalization procedure introduces enough low-energy constants to capture the off-shell structure. The authors note that three-body channels have not been explicitly included, yet a good overall description is still achieved.

## Method
The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for s-wave meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness S=-1, isospin I=0 sector, coupling four channels: pi Sigma, anti-K N, eta Lambda, and K Xi. The potential is taken from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian, and the resulting divergent integrals are renormalized by introducing 12 undetermined low-energy constants. These constants are fitted to experimental data including the pi Sigma mass spectrum, the real and imaginary parts of the anti-K N elastic and anti-K N to pi Sigma t-matrices from Gopal et al. (1977), and the K- p to eta Lambda total cross section from the Crystal Ball Collaboration (Starostin et al., 2001).

## Key numbers
* Anti-K N scattering length: a = (-1.20 +/- 0.09) + i (1.29 +/- 0.09) fm, compared to the experimental value (-1.71 + i 0.68) fm from Martin (1981).
* First pole (broad, associated with Lambda(1405) region): mass M_R = 1368 +/- 12 MeV, width Gamma_R = 250 +/- 23 MeV.
* Second pole (narrower, associated with Lambda(1405) region): mass M_R = 1443 +/- 3 MeV, width Gamma_R = 50 +/- 7 MeV.
* Third pole (identified as Lambda(1670)): mass M_R = 1677.5 +/- 0.8 MeV, width Gamma_R = 29.2 +/- 1.4 MeV, compared to the PDG (2002) values M_R = 1670 +/- 10 MeV, Gamma_R = 35 (+15, -10) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the unitarized chiral approach previously applied by the authors to the S=0, I=1/2 sector (Nieves and Ruiz Arriola, 2001) to the S=-1, I=0 channel. It improves upon earlier coupled-channel studies by Oset and Ramos (1998) and Oset, Ramos, and Bennhold (2002), which used a three-momentum cutoff and described data only in a narrow energy window. The inclusion of more low-energy constants, consistent with the flexibility allowed by effective field theory renormalization, enables a description from threshold up to 1.75 GeV, including both the Lambda(1405) and Lambda(1670) resonances. The results enable future studies of the pole structure of these resonances and provide a framework for incorporating three-body channels or extending to other strangeness sectors.
