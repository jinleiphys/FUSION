# Hyperonic mixing in five-baryon double-strangeness hypernuclei in a two-channel treatment

> Properties of hypernuclei $_{ΛΛ}^5$H and $_{ΛΛ}^5$He are studied in a two-channel approach with explicit treatment of coupling of channels ^3\text{Z}+Λ+Λand α+Ξ. Diagonal ΛΛand coupling ΛΛ-ΞN interactions are derived within G-matrix procedure from Nijmegen meson-exchange models. Bond energy ΔB_{ΛΛ} in $_{ΛΛ}^5$He exceeds significantly that in $_{ΛΛ}^5$H due to the channel coupling. Diagonal Ξαattraction amplifies the effect, which is sensitive also to Λ-core interaction. The difference of the ΔB_{ΛΛ} values can be an unambiguous signature of the ΛΛ-ΞN coupling in ΛΛhypernuclei. However, improved knowledge of the hyperon-nucleus potentials is needed for quantitative extraction of the coupling strength from future data on the ΛΛhypernuclear binding energies.

## Key claim
The bond energy $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ in $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^5$He is significantly larger than in $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^5$H due to the explicit treatment of the $\Lambda\Lambda-\Xi N$ channel coupling, but the quantitative extraction of the coupling strength from future data requires improved knowledge of hyperon-nucleus potentials.

## Method
The authors solved a two-channel Hartree-Fock model for the five-baryon systems $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^5$H and $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^5$He, treating the nuclear cores ($^3$Z and $\alpha$) as inert and coupling the $^3$Z+$\Lambda$+$\Lambda$ and $\alpha$+$\Xi$ channels. Diagonal $\Lambda\Lambda$ and coupling $\Lambda\Lambda-\Xi N$ interactions were derived from Nijmegen meson-exchange models (NHC-D, NHC-F, NSC89, NSC97e/f) using a $G$-matrix procedure in nuclear matter, with the diagonal $\Lambda\Lambda$ potentials adjusted to reproduce the Nagara event value of $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^6\text{He}) \approx 1.0$ MeV.

## Key numbers
- $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^5\text{H})$ in the single-channel approximation (no coupling) is 0.58–0.63 MeV across the potential models used.
- $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^5\text{He})$ in the single-channel approximation (no coupling) is 0.65–0.69 MeV across the potential models used.
- The threshold-energy difference $\Delta e$ for the two channels is 8.4 MeV for $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^5$H and 2.4 MeV for $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^5$He.
- The $\Xi^-$ hyperon is bound by the $\alpha$ particle in $_{\Xi^-}^5$H with $B_{\Xi^-} = 0.4$–0.5 MeV (depending on folding details), while $_{\Xi^0}^5$He is unbound for the potential fitted to a 14 MeV well depth in $^{11}$B.
- The volume integral of the coupling potential $V_{\Lambda\Lambda,\Xi N}$ ranges from 250.9 MeV fm$^3$ (NHC-D) to 582.3 MeV fm$^3$ (NHC-F).

## Context
This work extends the earlier study by Myint et al. (2003), who used a single-channel approach with effective coupling, by solving the two-channel problem explicitly and incorporating the diagonal $\Xi\alpha$ potential. It builds on the Nagara event (2001) which established a weaker $\Lambda\Lambda$ attraction ($\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda} \approx 1$ MeV) than previously assumed, making the stronger $\Lambda\Lambda-\Xi N$ coupling of models like NHC-F more plausible. The results suggest that the difference in $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ between $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^5$He and $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^5$H could serve as an unambiguous signature of the coupling, but further experimental and theoretical work on $\Xi$-nucleus potentials is needed for quantitative analysis.
