# Target mass number dependence of subthreshold antiproton production in proton-, deuteron- and alpha-particle-induced reactions

> Data from KEK on subthreshold $\bar{\mrm{p}}$ as well as on $π^\pm$ and $\mrm{K}^\pm$ production in proton-, deuteron- and $α$-induced reactions at energies between 2.0 and 12.0 A GeV for C, Cu and Pb targets are described within a unified approach. We use a model which considers a nuclear reaction as an incoherent sum over collisions of varying numbers of projectile and target nucleons. It samples complete events and thus allows for the simultaneous consideration of all final particles including the decay products of the nuclear residues. The enormous enhancement of the $\bar{\mrm{p}}$ cross section, as well as the moderate increase of meson production in deuteron and $α$ induced compared to proton-induced reactions, is well reproduced for all target nuclei. In our approach, the observed enhancement near the production threshold is mainly due to the contributions from the interactions of few-nucleon clusters by simultaneously considering fragmentation processes of the nuclear residues. The ability of the model to reproduce the target mass dependence may be considered as a further proof of the validity of the cluster concept.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of subthreshold antiproton production in deuteron- and alpha-induced reactions compared to proton-induced reactions is well reproduced for all target nuclei (C, Cu, Pb) by a model that attributes the effect primarily to interactions of few-nucleon clusters, provided that fragmentation processes of the nuclear residues are simultaneously considered.

## Method

The authors used the Rossendorf collision (ROC) model, which treats a nuclear reaction as an incoherent sum over collisions of varying numbers of projectile and target nucleons within a spectator-participant picture. Partial cross sections for these interactions are derived from a probabilistic interpretation of Glauber theory, and particle production proceeds via intermediate fireball states characterized by temperature and radius parameters. The nuclear residue is assumed to become excited during the reaction, and its fragmentation is described in analogy to fireball decay using a separate temperature parameter that depends on target mass number.

## Key numbers

- For Cu target at 3.5 A GeV, the calculated antiproton production cross section with the standard temperature parameter (Theta_R^max = 7.5 MeV) is 2.7 +/- 0.1 nb for proton-induced reactions, 0.39 +/- 0.05 microbarn for deuteron-induced reactions, and 1.5 +/- 0.2 microbarn for alpha-induced reactions.
- The temperature parameter for the nuclear residue is taken as Theta_R^max = 12 MeV for light nuclei up to carbon, 10 MeV for Al, 7.5 MeV for Cu (by interpolation), and 6.8 MeV for Ag and Pb (mass numbers above 100).
- The radius parameter for the nuclear residue is fixed at R_R = 1.7 fm, and the parameter bar-a = 0.5 defines the dependence of the temperature on the number of participants.
- Deviations between ROC model calculations and experimental data are typically less than a factor of 2 across all targets and ejectile species.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of subthreshold antiproton production in pC, dC, and alpha-C reactions (Mueller and Komarov, 2004; Komarov and Mueller, 2004) to heavier target nuclei Cu and Pb, using KEK data from Sugaya et al. (1998). The results contradict the interpretation of Sugaya et al. that internal nucleon motion in the deuteron cannot explain the enhancement, and instead support the cluster concept where few-nucleon groups interact analogously to single nucleons. The model enables predictions for pPb and alpha-Pb reactions not yet measured, and suggests that experiments on subthreshold production with light target nuclei could probe the transition from hadronic to quark-gluon degrees of freedom.
