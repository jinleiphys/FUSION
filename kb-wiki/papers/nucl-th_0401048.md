# Deducing the asymptotic normalization constant of the 2+ subthreshold state in 16O from 12C + alpha elastic scattering

> R-matrix analyses of the 12C + alpha elastic-scattering phase shifts deduced from a recent high-precision measurement of the differential cross sections are performed. The l=0 phase shifts constrain the R-matrix radius a around 5.85 fm, while the l=2 phase shifts lead to a strong constrain neither on a nor on the asymptotic normalization constant C of the 2+ subthreshold state (except for a loose upper limit). This contradicts previous R-matrix analyses of the 12C + alpha elastic scattering and explains the incompatibility between values of C obtained in these analyses.

# Deducing the asymptotic normalization constant of the 2+ subthreshold state in 16O from 12C + alpha elastic scattering

## Key claim

Contrary to previous R-matrix analyses that claimed to extract a specific value for the asymptotic normalization constant (ANC) of the 2+ subthreshold state in 16O, the author finds that the l=2 phase shifts from high-precision elastic scattering data provide no strong constraint on the ANC, only a loose upper limit that depends on the R-matrix radius. This explains the incompatibility between previously published ANC values and suggests those values are unreliable.

## Method

The author performed R-matrix analyses of l=0 and l=2 phase shifts deduced from 402 differential cross sections measured at center-of-mass energies between 2 and 6.5 MeV. For each partial wave, fits were performed with combinations of hard-sphere background terms, high-energy phenomenological states, and bound-state terms, and the chi-squared per degree of freedom was mapped as a function of the R-matrix radius a and the bound-state ANC C.

## Key numbers

- The l=0 phase shifts constrain the R-matrix radius a around 5.85 fm when a high-energy background state is included in the fit.
- Without a high-energy state, the best l=0 fit gives a = 3.66 fm, close to the sum of the matter radii of 12C and alpha, but the fit is unsatisfactory.
- For l=2, the chi-squared surface shows a valley of satisfactory fits ranging from C = 0 to at least C = 200 x 10^3 fm^{-1/2}, with no clear minimum.
- The best l=2 fits correspond to sqrt(chi-squared/N) approximately 3.7, indicating systematic errors are underestimated.
- A potential model analysis considering multiple partial waves simultaneously yields C = 144.5 +/- 8.5 x 10^3 fm^{-1/2}.

## Context

This work contradicts previous R-matrix analyses by Angulo (2000), who obtained C = 402 x 10^3 fm^{-1/2} with no error bar, and Tischhauser (2002), who obtained C = 154 +/- 18 x 10^3 fm^{-1/2} from the same high-precision data. The author argues that the Angulo (2000) minimum resulted from too restrictive hypotheses on the R-matrix radius and high-energy state, while the Tischhauser (2002) error bar neglected systematic errors. The result enables a more cautious approach to extracting bound-state ANCs from elastic scattering and points toward potential model analyses that simultaneously fit multiple partial waves, as demonstrated by Sparenberg (2004) using the 0_1+ rotational band to which the 2+ state belongs.
