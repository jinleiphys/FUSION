# The Search for the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We provide an overview of the present understanding of the transition from hadrons to a quark-gluon plasma, its signatures, and the experimental results so far. We discuss results of numerical simulations of the lattice gauge theory and critically evaluate the various observables that have been proposed as signatures of the QCD phase transition. We place the existing data from relativistic heavy ion experiments at the Brookhaven AGS and CERN SPS into perspective and provide an overview of the techniques and strategies that will be employed in the search for the quark-gluon plasma at heavy ion colliders, such as RHIC and the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a transition from hadronic matter to a quark-gluon plasma is expected based on QCD theory and lattice simulations, but emphasizes that experimental confirmation remains incomplete because signals must be disentangled from backgrounds emitted by the hot hadronic gas phase that follows hadronization, and because the plasma lifetime is predicted to be only 5–10 fm/c.

## Method

The authors review lattice gauge theory simulations of finite-temperature QCD, thermal perturbation theory calculations of the equation of state, and dynamical models (parton cascades and hydrodynamics) for relativistic heavy ion collisions. They then critically evaluate proposed experimental signatures, kinematic probes, electromagnetic probes (lepton pairs and direct photons), deconfinement probes (quarkonium suppression and strangeness enhancement), chiral symmetry restoration probes (disoriented chiral condensates and medium effects on hadron properties), and hard QCD probes (jet energy loss and acoplanarity), placing existing data from the Brookhaven AGS and CERN SPS into context.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature from lattice QCD: \(T_c \approx 150 \pm 10\) MeV at vanishing net quark density.
- Pure SU(3) gauge theory (no dynamical quarks) exhibits a first-order phase transition at \(T_c \approx 260\) MeV.
- Critical baryon density for chiral symmetry restoration at zero temperature: \(4\rho_0 < \rho_c < 10\rho_0\), where \(\rho_0\) is ground-state nuclear matter density.
- Quark condensate in the QCD vacuum: \(\langle \bar\psi\psi\rangle \approx (235\ \text{MeV})^3\).
- Gluon condensate: \(\langle \alpha_s G_{\mu\nu}G^{\mu\nu}\rangle \approx (500\ \text{MeV})^4\).
- Parton cascade models predict thermalization on a proper time scale of 0.3–0.5 fm/c at RHIC energies.
- Plasma lifetime estimate: approximately 4 fm/c before mixed phase forms; mixed phase decays on a time scale of 10 fm/c.
- Hydrodynamic approach becomes invalid when temperature falls below 120–130 MeV.
- Center-of-mass energies per nucleon pair: 4.84 GeV for Au+Au at the AGS and 17.2 GeV for Pb+Pb at the SPS.
- Charged particle multiplicity density at midrapidity: approximately 150 per unit rapidity at the AGS and 270 per unit rapidity at the SPS.

## Context

This review extends earlier theoretical work on the QCD phase transition, including lattice simulations by Blum (1995) and Boyd (1995), and dynamical models by Eskola (1994) and Geiger (1992, 1995). It critically assesses proposed signatures such as quarkonium suppression (Matsui and Satz, 1986) and strangeness enhancement (Rafelski, 1982) in light of data from the AGS and SPS. The paper enables future searches at RHIC and the LHC by identifying the most promising observables and the experimental strategies needed to distinguish plasma signals from hadronic backgrounds.
