# Space-time regions of high baryon density and baryon stopping in heavy-ion collisions

> Four-volumes ($V_4=$ spatial-3-volume$\times$lifetime) are calculated within the model of three-fluid dynamics (3FD) and compared with those of the the JET AA Microscopic Transport Model (JAM). The calculations are performed for central Au+Au collisions at energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3 -- 19.6 GeV. These $V_4$ indicate optimal collision-energy ranges for realizing macroscopic high baryon-density matter. It is found that the 3FD four-volumes noticeably exceed those in the JAM, which indicates a stronger baryon stopping in the 3FD model as compared to that JAM. It is argued that this difference in the baryon stopping correlates with stiffness of the EoS implemented in these models. Contrary to JAM, the four-volume, where a baryon density ($n_B$) exceeds three times the normal nuclear density ($n_0$), does not exhibit a maximum as a function of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$. It decreases monotonically with increasing $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$, remaining at a fairly macroscopic level (i.e. $V_4\geq 5.5^4$ fm$^4$/c). For higher baryon densities, $V_4$ exhibits maxima in its dependence on $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$. The optimal energy range for densities $n_B/n_0>$ 4 is located at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3.2--8 GeV. Even for $n_B/n_0>$ 6, the four-volume remains quite macroscopic ($V_4\geq 4^4$ fm$^4$/c) at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 4.5--9 GeV contrary to the JAM.

## Key claim

The four-volume (spatial volume times lifetime) of high-baryon-density matter predicted by three-fluid dynamics (3FD) noticeably exceeds that of the JAM transport model, indicating stronger baryon stopping in 3FD; this difference is argued to correlate with the stiffness of the equation of state (EoS) implemented in each model, and contrary to JAM, the four-volume for baryon densities above three times normal nuclear density does not exhibit a maximum as a function of collision energy but decreases monotonically.

## Method

Four-volumes \(V_4(\widetilde{n}_B) = \int d^4 x \;\Theta (n_B(x)-\widetilde{n}_B)\) were calculated within the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model for central Au+Au collisions at impact parameter \(b = 2\) fm, using a crossover EoS (primarily) and a first-order phase transition (1PT) EoS for comparison. The 3FD model treats the collision as three interacting fluids (projectile, target, and fireball) governed by hydrodynamic equations with friction terms, and the four-volume integration runs over space-time regions where the local proper baryon density exceeds a threshold value. Results were compared with those from the JET AA Microscopic Transport Model (JAM) reported by Taya et al. (2025).

## Key numbers

- For baryon density threshold \(n_B/n_0 > 3\), the four-volume decreases monotonically with increasing \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) and remains at a fairly macroscopic level, i.e. \(V_4 \geq 5.5^4\) fm\(^4\)/c.
- The optimal energy range for densities \(n_B/n_0 > 4\) is located at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.2\)–8 GeV.
- For \(n_B/n_0 > 6\), the four-volume remains quite macroscopic (\(V_4 \geq 4^4\) fm\(^4\)/c) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.5\)–9 GeV, contrary to the JAM model.
- Calculations were performed for central Au+Au collisions at energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\)–19.6 GeV.
- The normal nuclear density used is \(n_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of four-volumes in heavy-ion collisions by Ivanov and Soldatov (2020) and by Taya et al. (2025), who introduced the comparison of four-volumes across different dynamical models. The 3FD results are compared with the JAM model (Nara et al., 2000, 2023) to highlight differences in baryon stopping and EoS stiffness. The analysis of equilibrated baryonic matter four-volumes enables identification of optimal collision energies for producing macroscopic high-baryon-density matter, relevant for the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and future facilities such as NICA, FAIR, HIAF, and J-PARC-HI.
