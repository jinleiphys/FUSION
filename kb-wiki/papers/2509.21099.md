# Effects of mean-field momentum dependence on pion production in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Pion production in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies provides an important probe of the collision dynamics and nuclear matter equation of state, especially the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy. Using the lattice Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model with a recently developed nuclear effective interaction based on the so-called N$5$LO Skyrme pseudopotential, we investigate the effects of the momentum dependence of nucleon mean-field potentials on the pion production in Au+Au collisions at a beam energy of $1.23$~GeV/nucleon. We find that a stronger momentum dependence, for which the nucleon mean-field potentials increase faster with momentum, generally suppresses pion production. This feature can be understood in terms of the mean-field-induced modification of nucleon high-momentum phase space during the compression stage: a stronger momentum dependence can reduce the relative fraction of high-momentum nucleons in heavy-ion collisions, thereby suppressing the production of $Δ$ resonances and pions.

## Key claim

The paper finds that a stronger momentum dependence of nucleon mean-field potentials (where potentials increase faster with momentum) systematically suppresses pion production in Au+Au collisions at 1.23 GeV/nucleon, but the authors emphasize this is a qualitative investigation and not a comprehensive quantitative study, noting that their SP10 interaction still over-predicts the measured pi- yield.

## Method

The authors used the lattice Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (LBUU) transport model with three extended Skyrme interactions (SP10, SP10-FL, and SP10-MID) based on the N5LO Skyrme pseudopotential, which span from strong to vanishing momentum dependence. They simulated Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.4 GeV (beam energy 1.23 GeV/nucleon) and compared the resulting charged-pion rapidity distributions and multiplicities with HADES experimental data. A new parameterization of the pp -> n Delta++ cross section was introduced by fitting experimental data from threshold up to about 7 GeV.

## Key numbers

* Maximum central baryon densities reached in 0-10% centrality Au+Au collisions: about 2.9 rho_0 for SP10 (strongest momentum dependence), 2.95 rho_0 for SP10-FL, and 3.2 rho_0 for SP10-MID (momentum-independent).
* For 0-10% centrality, SP10-MID (momentum-independent) gives pion yields about 29.6% larger than SP10 and about 23.1% larger than SP10-FL.
* For 0-10% centrality, the predicted pi- multiplicities are: SP10 = 20.3, SP10-FL = 21.3, SP10-MID = 25.6, compared to HADES data of 17.2(11).
* For 0-10% centrality, the predicted pi+ multiplicities are: SP10 = 10.5, SP10-FL = 11.1, SP10-MID = 14.3, compared to HADES data of 9.3(7).
* The simulation used 50,000 test particles, a lattice spacing of 1 fm, and was stopped at 60 fm/c with a time step of 0.2 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pion production in heavy-ion collisions at HADES energies (HADES Collaboration, 2020) and addresses the model dependence found in transport model analyses of FOPI data (FOPI Collaboration, 2006). It builds on the Transport Model Evaluation Project (TMEP, 2016-2023) efforts to quantify uncertainties, and uses the N5LO Skyrme pseudopotential framework developed by Wang et al. (2024) that had previously reproduced proton flow data. The mechanism described is conceptually analogous to isospin fractionation driven by the symmetry energy (Li, 2000; Li, 2002), but here the effect originates from momentum dependence rather than density dependence. The work enables future studies incorporating in-medium corrections to NN inelastic cross sections and threshold effects.
