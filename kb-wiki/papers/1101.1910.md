# Low-momentum interactions with Brown-Rho-Ericson scalings and the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy

> We have calculated the nuclear symmetry energy $E_{sym}(ρ)$ up to densities of $4 \sim 5 ρ_0$ with the effects from the Brown-Rho (BR) and Ericson scalings for the in-medium mesons included. Using the $V_{low-k}$ low-momentum interaction with and without such scalings, the equations of state (EOS) of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter have been calculated using a ring-diagarm formalism where the particle-particle-hole-hole ring diagrams are included to all orders. The EOS for symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter obtained with linear BR scaling are both overly stiff compared with the empirical constraints of Danielewicz {\it et al.} \cite{daniel02}. In contrast, satisfactory results are obtained by either using the nonlinear Ericson scaling or by adding a Skyrme-type three-nucleon force (TNF) to the unscaled $V_{low-k}$ interaction. Our results for $E_{sym}(ρ)$ obtained with the nonlinear Ericson scaling are in good agreement with the empirical values of Tsang {\it et al.} \cite{tsang09} and Li {\it et al.} \cite{li05}, while those with TNF are slightly below these values. For densities below the nuclear saturation density $ρ_0$, the results of the above calculations are nearly equivalent to each other and all in satisfactory agreement with the empirical values.

## Key claim

The nuclear symmetry energy $E_{sym}(\rho)$ calculated with the nonlinear Brown-Rho-Ericson (BRE) scaling agrees well with empirical constraints up to $4\sim5\rho_0$, while the linear Brown-Rho scaling produces equations of state that are overly stiff compared to the empirical constraints of Danielewicz et al. (2002). The results depend critically on which in-medium scaling is used, with the nonlinear scaling or a Skyrme-type three-nucleon force both giving satisfactory results, but with different predictions for neutron matter stiffness.

## Method

The authors used the $V_{low-k}$ low-momentum interaction derived from the BonnA one-boson exchange potential via a T-matrix equivalence renormalization group approach, with a decimation scale $\Lambda = 3.0\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$. They calculated the equations of state for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter using a ring-diagram formalism where particle-particle hole-hole ($pphh$) ring diagrams are summed to all orders, and obtained the symmetry energy from the energy difference between neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter. The in-medium meson masses were scaled using either linear Brown-Rho scaling ($m^*/m = 1 - C\rho/\rho_0$) or nonlinear Ericson scaling ($m^*/m = (1 + D\rho/\rho_0)^{-1/3}$), applied to the $\rho$, $\omega$, and $\sigma$ mesons of the BonnA potential.

## Key numbers

- The empirical saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is $\rho_0 \simeq 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$ with binding energy $E \simeq -16\ \mathrm{MeV}$ per nucleon.
- The linear BR scaling parameters used were $C_{\omega}=0.128$, $C_{\rho}=0.113$, and $C_{\sigma}=0.102$, giving $E_0/A \simeq -15.5\ \mathrm{MeV}$ and $\rho_0 \simeq 0.17\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$.
- The nonlinear BRE scaling parameters used were $D_{\omega}=D_{\rho}=0.40$ and $D_{\sigma}=0.30$, with the theoretical value $D = 0.35 \pm 0.06$ from the quark condensate relation.
- The Skyrme-type three-nucleon force parameter was $t_3 = 2000\ \mathrm{MeV}\text{-}\mathrm{fm}^{6}$.
- The symmetry energy at $\rho_0$ is close to $\sim 30\ \mathrm{MeV}$ for all calculations.
- The fitted density exponent $\gamma$ (from $E_{sym}(\rho) \approx 31.6(\rho/\rho_0)^\gamma$) was 0.82 for nonlinear BRE and 0.53 for TNF, compared to the empirical range 0.69-1.1 from Li et al. (2005).
- The fitted exponent $\gamma_i$ (from the Tsang et al. (2009) form) was 1.04 for nonlinear BRE and 0.43 for TNF, compared to the empirical value 0.7 from Tsang et al. (2009).

## Context

This work extends the use of the $V_{low-k}$ low-momentum interaction, previously applied to nuclear structure, to the study of the nuclear symmetry energy at high densities up to $4\sim5\rho_0$. It contrasts the linear Brown-Rho scaling, which is suitable only at low densities, with the nonlinear Ericson scaling that provides a natural extension to higher densities, and compares both to the approach of adding a Skyrme-type three-nucleon force. The results are benchmarked against empirical constraints from heavy-ion collision data by Danielewicz et al. (2002), Li et al. (2005), and Tsang et al. (2009), and the work enables further studies of neutron star properties and the possibility of a supersoft symmetry energy as suggested by Xiao et al. (2009).
