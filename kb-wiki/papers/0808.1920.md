# Ground state normalization in the nonmesonic weak decay of $^{12}_ΛC$ hypernucleus within a nuclear matter formalism

> The nonmesonic weak decay width of $^{12}_ΛC$ hypernucleus has been evaluated within a nuclear matter formalism, using the local density approximation. In addition to the one-body induced decay $(ΛN \to n N)$, it has been also considered the two-body induced decay $(ΛNN \to n N N)$. This second decay is originated from ground state correlations, where a renormalization procedure to ensure a ground state normalized to one has been implemented. Our results show that the plain addition of the two-body induced decay implies a lost in the ground state-norm, which adds $\sim 38%$ of spurious intensity to the nonmesonic weak decay width. By an adequate selection of the $ΛN$-transition potential, our result for the nonmesonic weak decay width of $^{12}_ΛC$ is 0.956, in good agreement with the most recent data.

## Key claim

The plain addition of the two-body induced decay (ΛNN → nNN) to the one-body induced decay (ΛN → nN) adds approximately 38% spurious intensity to the nonmesonic weak decay width because the ground state is not normalized to one; implementing a renormalization procedure that ensures a properly normalized ground state brings the calculated width into agreement with the most recent experimental data.

## Method

The nonmesonic weak decay width of ¹²_ΛC was evaluated using a nuclear matter formalism with the local density approximation, including both one-body (ΛN → nN) and two-body (ΛNN → nNN) induced decays. The two-body decay originates from ground state correlations, and a renormalization factor 𝒩(k_F) was applied to ensure the ground state is normalized to one. Short range correlations were implemented using a correlation function in momentum space with a cutoff q_c = 780 MeV/c, rather than the commonly used approximate prescription which is inaccurate for the momentum transfers relevant to Λ-decay (q ≈ 400 MeV/c). The transition potential included π, η, K, ρ, ω, and K* meson exchanges, with coupling constants from the Nijmegen and NSC97f parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- The spurious intensity added to Γ_NM by the plain addition of Γ₂ without ground state renormalization is ~38%.
- The final nonmesonic weak decay width for ¹²_ΛC using the Nijmegen transition potential with renormalization is Γ_NM = 0.956 (in units of the free Λ-decay rate Γ⁰ = 2.52 × 10⁻⁶ eV).
- The experimental value from Outa et al. (2005) is Γ_NM_exp = 0.940 ± 0.035.
- The ratio Γ₂/Γ₁ with renormalization is 0.28.
- The Γ_n/Γ_p ratio is roughly unaffected by renormalization, with values 0.260 (Nijmegen) and 0.234 (NSC97f).
- The short range correlation model reduces Γ_NM by ~35% compared to the approximate prescription (Eq. 13) for the same q_c-value.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear matter formalism for nonmesonic weak decay developed by Bauer (2003, 2004) by correcting the ground state normalization, a point previously noted by Van Neck et al. (1992). It addresses the problem that previous theoretical predictions for Γ_NM were always too large compared to the most precise experimental data from Outa et al. (2005). The renormalization procedure is applicable to any calculation that includes the two-body induced decay, including the semi-phenomenological models of Alberico et al. (1991) and Ramos et al. (1994), and enables a self-consistent selection of the ΛN-transition potential for future calculations of nucleon emission spectra.
