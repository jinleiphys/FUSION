# Maps of the Little Bangs Through Energy Density and Temperature Fluctuations

> In this letter we propose for the first time to map the heavy-ion collisions at ultra-relativistic energies, similar to the maps of the cosmic microwave background radiation, using fluctuations of energy density and temperature in small phase space bins. We study the evolution of fluctuations at each stage of the collision using an event-by-event hydrodynamic framework. We demonstrate the feasibility of making fluctuation maps from experimental data and its usefulness in extracting considerable information regarding the early stages of the collision and its evolution.

## Key claim

The authors propose that temperature fluctuation maps in heavy-ion collisions, analogous to CMBR maps, can be constructed from experimental data and used to quantitatively probe the early stages of the collision, provided that a correspondence is made between measured fluctuations at freeze-out and the time evolution of fluctuations from hydrodynamic calculations.

## Method

The authors used a (2+1)-dimensional event-by-event ideal hydrodynamic framework with a lattice-based equation of state to model the space-time evolution of central (0-5%) Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV. They generated initial conditions using a Monte Carlo Glauber model with a wounded nucleon profile and a Gaussian smearing parameter sigma = 0.4 fm. To demonstrate experimental feasibility, they used the AMPT event generator (string melting mode) as a proxy for experimental data, dividing the y-phi phase space into 16x36 bins, constructing pT spectra of charged pions in each bin, and extracting effective temperatures by fitting Maxwell-Boltzmann functions in the range 0.5 to 1.0 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Initial average energy density at tau = 0.14 fm: approximately 168 GeV/fm^3, falling to approximately 20 GeV/fm^3 at tau = 1 fm
- Initial average temperature at tau = 0.14 fm: approximately 530 MeV, falling to approximately 300 MeV at tau = 1 fm
- Freeze-out temperature: 160 MeV
- Initial energy density fluctuations: approximately 90% at early times
- Initial temperature fluctuations: approximately 35% at early times
- Bin-to-bin effective temperature fluctuation from AMPT: 2% (mean 195 MeV, sigma 3.8 MeV) over all y-phi bins
- Event-by-event effective temperature distribution from AMPT: mean 182 MeV, sigma 15 MeV, yielding heat capacity C_v = 147 at freeze-out
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section: 64 mb
- Initial formation time tau_0: 0.14 fm
- Transition temperature from QGP to hadronic phase: 170 MeV
- Gaussian smearing parameter sigma: 0.4 fm

## Context

This work extends the analogy between heavy-ion collisions ("little bangs") and the Big Bang/CMBR studies, as previously discussed by Heinz, Paul, and others, by proposing a direct mapping method using temperature fluctuations. It builds on event-by-event hydrodynamic frameworks developed by Holopainen and collaborators, and uses the AMPT model of Lin et al. The approach enables future power spectrum analysis of heavy-ion collisions similar to CMBR methods, and opens the possibility of extracting thermodynamic parameters such as heat capacity and speed of sound from experimental data, as previously discussed by Stodolsky, Korus, and others.
