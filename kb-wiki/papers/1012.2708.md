# Limits to the Fixed Center Approximation to Faddeev equations: the case of the $ϕ(2170)$

> The Fixed Center Approximation to the Faddeev equations (FCA) has been used lately with success in the study of bound systems of three hadrons. It is also important to set the limits of the approach in those problems to prevent proliferation of inaccurate predictions. In this paper we study the case of the $ϕ(2170)$, which has been described by means of Faddeev equations as a resonant state of $ϕ$ and $K \bar{K}$, and show the problems derived from the use of the FCA in its study. At the same time we also expose the limitations of an alternative approach recently proposed.

## Key claim

The Fixed Center Approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations fails to produce a resonant structure near the $\phi(2170)$ mass when applied to the $\phi f_0(980)$ system, contrary to the full Faddeev calculation of Torres et al. (2009); this failure arises because the FCA neglects intermediate-state excitation of the $f_0(980)$ cluster, which becomes significant at the 170 MeV excitation energy available in this system.

## Method

The authors applied the FCA to the $\phi f_0(980)$ system, treating the $f_0(980)$ as a bound $K\bar{K}$ cluster and computing the $\phi f_0$ scattering amplitude via the coupled equations $T_1 = t_1 + t_1 G T_2$, $T_2 = t_2 + t_2 G T_1$, where $t_1$, $t_2$ are the $\phi K$ and $\phi \bar{K}$ two-body $t$-matrices from chiral unitary theory (Roca et al., 2005) and $G$ is the $\phi$ propagator between the $K$ and $\bar{K}$ components. They then evaluated a specific three-body Faddeev diagram (Fig. 3a) that allows the $K\bar{K}$ system to be excited in the intermediate state, using the $K\bar{K}$ interaction $t_{K\bar{K}}$ from the same chiral framework, and compared its magnitude to the FCA double-scattering term.

## Key numbers

- The $\phi(2170)$ resonance has experimental mass $M_X = 2175 \pm 10 \pm 15$ MeV and width $\Gamma_X = 58 \pm 16 \pm 20$ MeV from BABAR; $M_X = 2186 \pm 10 \pm 6$ MeV and $\Gamma_X = 65 \pm 23 \pm 17$ MeV from BES; and $M_X = 2079 \pm 13^{+79}_{-28}$ MeV and $\Gamma_X = 192 \pm 23^{+25}_{-61}$ MeV from Belle.
- The $\phi$ momentum in the $\phi f_0$ center-of-mass frame at $\sqrt{s} = 2170$ MeV is approximately 420 MeV/c.
- The three-body diagram of Fig. 3a (allowing $f_0$ excitation) has an amplitude of the same order of magnitude as the FCA double-scattering term around $\sqrt{s} = 2170$ MeV, with opposite signs for the real parts, leading to strong destructive interference.
- The $|T_{\phi f_0}|^2$ from the FCA shows no peak near 2170 MeV when the $f_0$ form factor is included, in contrast to the clear peak seen in the full Faddeev calculation of Torres et al. (2009).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the $\phi(2170)$ resonance, previously described by Torres et al. (2009) using full Faddeev equations as a $\phi K\bar{K}$ resonant state with the $K\bar{K}$ pair forming an $f_0(980)$ cluster. It directly tests the limits of the FCA, which had been successfully applied to other three-hadron systems such as $N K\bar{K}$ (Xie et al., 2010) and $\bar{K}NN$ (Bayar et al., 2011), and finds that the FCA is inadequate when the excitation energy (170 MeV in this case) is large enough to break the cluster. The paper also critiques an alternative approach by Alvarez-Ruso et al. (2009, 2010) that introduces free parameters (a subtraction constant for the $\phi f_0$ loop and a fitted $\phi K$ interaction) and produces results two orders of magnitude larger than the chiral unitary prediction, concluding that this approach is not predictive for the $\phi(2170)$.
