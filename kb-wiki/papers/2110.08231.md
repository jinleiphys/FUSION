# Unified picture of nucleon pairs playing leading roles in nuclear collectivity

> Proxy-SU(3) symmetry is an approximation scheme extending the Elliott SU(3) algebra of the sd shell to heavier shells. When introduced in 2017, the approximation had been justified by calculations carried out within the Nilsson model, with nucleon pairs differing by $ΔK [ΔN Δn_z ΔΛ]=0[110]$ in the Nilsson quantum numbers playing a major role in the approximation. Recently our group managed to map the cartesian basis of the Elliott SU(3) model onto the spherical shell model basis, fully clarifying the approximations used within the proxy-SU(3) scheme and paving the way for using the proxy-SU(3) approximation in shell model calculations for heavy nuclei. As a by-product, the relation of the 0[110] Nilsson pairs used in proxy-SU(3) to the earlier used de Shalit-Goldhaber pairs $|Δn Δl Δj Δm_j\rangle = | 0110\rangle$ in spherical shell model notation is clarified, while the Federman-Pittel (FP) pairs known to play a major role at the onset of deformation are identified as $| 0010\rangle$ pairs, and the FP pairs further increasing the deformation after its onset are found to be $| 0110\rangle$ pairs, i.e. identical to the de-Shalit-Goldhaber pairs. The connection between the proxy-SU(3) scheme and the spherical shell model has also been worked out in the original framework of the Nilsson model, with identical results.

## Key claim
The proxy-SU(3) approximation, which extends Elliott SU(3) symmetry to heavy nuclei by replacing intruder orbitals, is shown to be equivalent to the de Shalit-Goldhaber and Federman-Pittel pair schemes in the spherical shell model basis, with the qualification that the correspondence holds primarily for Nilsson orbitals possessing the highest total angular momentum within their shell.

## Method
The authors mapped the cartesian basis of the Elliott SU(3) model onto the spherical shell model basis using a unitary transformation \(R\) followed by Clebsch-Gordan coefficients \(C\), producing the combined transformation \(R \cdot C\) that connects Elliott orbitals \([n_z n_x n_y m_s]\) to shell model orbitals \(|n l j m_j\rangle\). They then compared the Nilsson quantum number differences \(\Delta K[\Delta N \Delta n_z \Delta \Lambda] = 0[110]\) used in proxy-SU(3) to the spherical shell model differences \(|\Delta n \Delta l \Delta j \Delta m_j\rangle\), and verified the mapping by examining Nilsson model expansions of deformed orbitals into the shell model basis at various deformation values.

## Key numbers
- The transformation matrix \(R \cdot C\) is explicitly given for the \(N=2\) harmonic oscillator shell (8-20 nucleons), showing the 12 Elliott basis states mapped onto 12 shell model states.
- For the Nilsson orbital \(3/2[541]\) at deformation \(\epsilon = 0.05\), the leading shell model component is \(|5\ 5\ 11/2\ 3/2\rangle\) with amplitude 0.9979; at \(\epsilon = 0.30\) the same component still dominates with amplitude 0.9366.
- For the Nilsson orbital \(3/2[651]\) at \(\epsilon = 0.05\), the leading component is \(|6\ 6\ 13/2\ 3/2\rangle\) with amplitude 0.9966; at \(\epsilon = 0.30\) this drops to 0.9010.
- For the Nilsson orbital \(1/2[431]\) at \(\epsilon = 0.05\), the leading component is \(|4\ 4\ 7/2\ 1/2\rangle\) with amplitude 0.9893; at \(\epsilon = 0.30\) this drops to 0.7447 with several other components exceeding 0.25.
- For the Nilsson orbital \(1/2[541]\) at \(\epsilon = 0.05\), the leading component is \(|5\ 3\ 7/2\ 1/2\rangle\) with amplitude 0.9780; at \(\epsilon = 0.30\) the largest amplitude is 0.4829 with five components above 0.3.

## Context
This work unifies three previously separate pair classifications: the 0[110] Nilsson pairs used in proxy-SU(3) (Bonatsos et al., 2017), the de Shalit-Goldhaber pairs (de Shalit and Goldhaber, 1953) expressed as \(|0110\rangle\) in shell model notation, and the Federman-Pittel pairs (Federman and Pittel, 1977) which are identified as \(|0010\rangle\) pairs at the onset of deformation and \(|0110\rangle\) pairs for further deformation. The mapping enables the use of proxy-SU(3) symmetry in shell model calculations for heavy nuclei, analogous to the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model approach (Launey et al.) used in light nuclei, and is expected to improve convergence for deformed heavy nuclei where traditional shell model calculations become intractable.
