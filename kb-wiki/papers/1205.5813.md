# Collins Fragmentation Function within NJL-jet Model

> The NJL-jet model is extended to accommodate hadronization of a transversely polarized quark in order to explore the Collins effect within a multihadron emission framework. This is accomplished by calculating the polarized quark spin flip probabilities after a pseudoscalar hadron emission and the elementary Collins functions. The model is used to calculate the number densities of the hadrons produced in the polarized quark's decay chain. The full Collins fragmentation function is extracted from the sine modulation of the polarized number densities with respect to the polar angle between the initial quark's spin and hadron's transverse momentum. Two cases are studied here. First, a toy model for elementary Collins function is used to study the features of the transversely polarized quark-jet model. Second, a full model calculation of transverse momentum dependent pion and kaon Collins functions is presented. The remarkable feature of our model is that the 1/2 moments of the favored Collins fragmentation functions are positive and peak at large values of z but decrease and oscillate at small values of z. The 1/2 moments of the unfavored Collins functions have comparable magnitude and opposite sign to the favored functions, vanish at large z and peak at small values of z. This feature is observed for both the toy and full models and can be attributed to the quark-jet picture of hadronization. Moreover, the transverse momentum dependencies of the model Collins functions differ significantly from the Gaussian form widely used in the empirical parametrizations. Finally, a naive interpretation of the Schafer-Teryaev sum rule is proven not to hold in our model, where the transverse momentum conservation is explicitly enforced. This is attributed to the sizable average transverse momentum of the remnant quark that needs to be accounted for to satisfy the transverse momentum sum-rule.

## Key claim

The NJL-jet model, extended to include transverse quark spin, produces Collins fragmentation functions whose 1/2 moments for favored channels are positive and peak at large z but oscillate at small z, while unfavored functions have comparable magnitude and opposite sign, peaking at small z; however, the transverse momentum dependence of these model Collins functions differs significantly from the Gaussian form commonly used in empirical parametrizations, and a naive interpretation of the Schafer-Teryaev sum rule does not hold when transverse momentum conservation is explicitly enforced.

## Method

The authors extended the NJL-jet model to describe hadronization of a transversely polarized quark by calculating polarized quark spin-flip probabilities after pseudoscalar hadron emission and elementary Collins functions. They used Monte Carlo simulations within the quark-jet framework to compute number densities of hadrons produced in the polarized quark's decay chain, extracting the full Collins fragmentation function from the sine modulation of these densities with respect to the polar angle between the initial quark's spin and the hadron's transverse momentum. Two cases were studied: a toy model for the elementary Collins function to explore general features, and a full model calculation of transverse momentum dependent pion and kaon Collins functions.

## Key numbers

* The light constituent quark mass was set to M = 0.4 GeV, with a corresponding 3-momentum cutoff Λ₃ = 0.77 GeV fixed by reproducing the experimental pion decay constant.
* The strange constituent quark mass was M_s = 0.59 GeV, obtained by reproducing the experimental kaon mass.
* The quark-meson coupling constants were g_{πqQ} = 4.24 and g_{KqQ} = 4.52.
* The strong coupling constant α_s was fixed to 0.444, the largest value that still allowed the positivity bound d_{h/q↑} ≥ 0 to be satisfied.
* The typical hadronic scale corresponding to this α_s value at next-to-leading order was Q² = 1 GeV², while the typical NJL-jet model scale was Q₀² = 0.2 GeV².
* In the toy model, the elementary Collins function was set to -H̃₁^{⊥h/q}(z,p⊥²) p⊥ S_q / (m_h z) = 0.1 d₁^{h/q}(z,p⊥²), giving d_{h/q↑}^{(toy)} = d₁^{h/q}(1 + 0.1 sin φ).

## Context

This work extends the NJL-jet model previously developed by Ito (2009), Matevosyan (2010, 2011) for unpolarized fragmentation functions to include transverse spin and the Collins effect, building on the quark-jet hadronization picture of Field and Feynman (1976, 1977). The results differ from earlier spectator model calculations by Bacchetta (2001, 2002) and Amrath (2005) which produced Collins functions too small to describe data, and from the gauge-link approach of Amrath (2005), Bacchetta (2007), and Gamberg (2003) which lacked unfavored fragmentation functions. The model enables calculation of both favored and unfavored Collins functions for pions and kaons, with features that can be tested against experimental results from HERMES, COMPASS, and JLab, and it provides a framework for future inclusion of hadronic resonances and QCD evolution.
