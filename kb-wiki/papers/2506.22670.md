# Quark Phase Space Distributions in Nuclei

> In [PRC 110, 025201], the authors construct a model for nuclear matter which features a quarkyonic phase. A main feature in this model is that the nucleon occupation is strongly reduced at small momenta. Somewhat surprisingly, this result is supported by data for electron scattering from nuclear matter, where a reduction of the cross section consistent with suppression of nucleons with small momenta is seen. Since nuclear matter data are obtained by extrapolation of electron scattering data on increasingly heavier systems, this feature should manifest at least to some degree in heavy nuclei. To check if this is plausible we extend the approach of [PRC 110, 025201] to finite nuclei by considering the nuclear Wigner distribution. We use non-relativistic and relativistic independent particle models to determine the nuclear Wigner distribution, in addition to the local-density approximation (LDA). Phase-space distributions of quarks are obtained as a convolution of the Wigner distribution with a quark momentum distribution. We highlight some properties of the Wigner distribution in spherical systems, which can spoil the interpretation of the quark phase-space distribution as occupation numbers in the Fermi sea. On the other hand, we show that large systems behave essentially like infinite nuclear matter in their interior, and that LDA and full results are quantitatively similar for large A. We then compute the fraction of baryons that would be in a quarkyonic phase in the same sense as in [PRC 110, 025201] for a set of nuclei with mass $12 \leq A \leq 238$. We find that this fraction systematically tends to a constant at large $A$. It is hence plausible that the suppression seen in the nuclear matter data is a genuine feature, present in large finite nuclei. This result is counter-intuitive and we discuss possible electron scattering measurements that could rule out this model.

# Quark Phase Space Distributions in Nuclei

## Key claim

The authors extend the IdylliQ quarkyonic matter model from infinite nuclear matter to finite nuclei and find that the fraction of baryons in a quarkyonic phase systematically tends to a constant at large mass number A, making it plausible that the low-momentum nucleon suppression seen in extrapolated nuclear matter data is a genuine feature present in heavy finite nuclei. However, this result is counter-intuitive, and the authors discuss possible electron scattering measurements that could rule out the model.

## Method

The authors replace the Fermi sea occupation numbers used in the infinite nuclear matter model of Koch et al. (2024) with the nuclear Wigner distribution for finite spherical nuclei. They compute the Wigner distribution using three approaches: the local-density approximation (LDA), relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations with the NL-SH parameter set, and non-relativistic Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with the SLY4 functional. The quark phase-space distribution is then obtained as a convolution of the nuclear Wigner distribution with a simple exponential quark momentum distribution, and the condition f_q > 1 (violation of the quark Pauli principle) is used to identify the quarkyonic regime.

## Key numbers

- The critical Fermi momentum for which the quark occupation number at k=0 equals 1 is k_F = 0.90 Lambda for N_c = 3, where Lambda is the parameter in the quark momentum distribution.
- The quark momentum distribution parameter Lambda is taken as 260 MeV for the main results, with values of 240, 260, 280, and 300 MeV explored.
- The fraction of baryons in the quarkyonic phase P_{f_q>1} = N_q/A plateaus for A > 50 and tends to a constant in the nuclear matter limit.
- The size of the "hole" in the nucleon momentum distribution (k_hole/k_F) is of order 20-30 percent, comparable to the nuclear matter results of Koch et al. (2024).
- The study covers nuclei with mass numbers 12 ≤ A ≤ 238, including 12C, 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, 56Fe, 58Ni, 118Sn, 120Sn, 186W, 208Pb, and 238U.

## Context

This work extends the IdylliQ model of quarkyonic matter developed by Fujimoto et al. (2023) and applied to normal nuclear densities by Koch et al. (2024) (PRC 110, 025201), which showed that low-momentum nucleon suppression is consistent with extrapolated (e,e') quasi-elastic scattering data from Day et al. (1989). The present paper tests whether this suppression can plausibly exist in finite nuclei by computing Wigner distributions, finding that large systems behave like infinite nuclear matter in their interior. The results enable experimental tests via inclusive electron scattering near the quasielastic peak, single nucleon knockout (e,e'p) measurements probing deep s-states, or exclusive pion production (e,e' p, pi-) experiments, which could rule out the model if no low-momentum suppression is observed.
