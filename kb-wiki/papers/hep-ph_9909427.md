# The Effect of Shadowing on Initial Conditions, Transverse Energy and Hard Probes in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The effect of shadowing on the early state of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is investigated along with transverse energy and hard process production, specifically Drell-Yan, $J/ψ$, and $Υ$ production. We choose several parton distributions and parameterizations of nuclear shadowing, as well as the spatial dependence of shadowing, to study the influence of shadowing on relevant observables. Results are presented for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5$ TeV.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how nuclear shadowing, the modification of parton distributions in nuclei, affects initial conditions, transverse energy, and hard probe production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, but the authors emphasize that the exact spatial dependence of shadowing remains poorly constrained by data, and their calculations show that heavy ion collision studies will likely not distinguish between different spatial-dependence models.

## Method

The authors calculate minijet production at leading order in perturbative QCD for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.5 TeV, using two different free-proton parton distribution sets (GRV 94 LO and MRST LO) combined with three nuclear shadowing parameterizations (S1, S2, S3) and three models for the spatial dependence of shadowing (local density S_WS, trajectory-integrated S_ρ, and thickness-based S_R). They compute the rapidity distributions, first and second transverse energy moments, initial energy densities, and production rates for Drell-Yan, J/ψ, and Υ as functions of impact parameter.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV (RHIC) and Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.5 TeV (LHC) are studied
- Nuclear radii used: R_Au = 6.38 fm, R_Pb = 6.62 fm
- Minijet cutoff p0 = 2 GeV is used as the safe perturbative scale
- At LHC, x values as low as x1,2 ~ 2p0/√s_NN ~ 7 × 10^-4 at midrapidity
- Shadowing can reduce the number of produced partons by up to a factor of two at LHC, depending on parameterization
- At LHC, gluons alone are sufficient to saturate the transverse area even with shadowing; at RHIC, saturation requires p0 ~ 1 GeV
- Incoming gluons with x=0.1 can suffer up to an average of 5 hard scatterings in central collisions with GRV 94 LO and no shadowing; shadowing reduces this by ~30%
- With MRST LO distributions, gluons experience 1.4-2 scatterings per central collision at LHC
- At RHIC, incoming partons experience less than one collision on average
- Inhomogeneous shadowing reduces the energy density by ~3-8% at b=0 at LHC
- For Pb+Pb at LHC, T_AA(0) = 30.4/mb; saturation occurs if hard cross section > 74 (p0/2 GeV)^2 mb

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of shadowing effects in heavy ion collisions by the same authors (Klein, Vogt, and collaborators, late 1990s) and builds on nuclear parton distribution parameterizations from EKS (Eskola, Kolhinen, Salgado) and other groups. The study is motivated by upcoming measurements at RHIC and LHC, and it critically examines the concept of fast thermalization via minijet production, finding that transverse saturation and unitarity violation at LHC energies suggest early equilibration by minijets alone is unlikely. The paper enables future experimental searches for spatial dependence of shadowing through impact-parameter-dependent observables like transverse energy and hard probe production.
