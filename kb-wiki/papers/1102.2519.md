# Time Modulation of K-Shell Electron Capture Decay Rates of H-Like Heavy Ions and Neutrino Masses

> We calculate the neutrino masses, using the experimental data on the periods of the time modulation of the K-shell electron capture (EC) decay rates of the H-like heavy ions, measured at GSI. The corrections to neutrino masses, caused by interaction of massive neutrinos with the strong Coulomb field of the daughter ions, are taken into account.

## Key claim
The paper calculates neutrino masses from the time modulation of K-shell electron capture decay rates of H-like heavy ions measured at GSI, but the result depends on corrections to neutrino masses caused by interaction with the strong Coulomb field of daughter ions, which the authors argue must be included to resolve a discrepancy with reactor antineutrino oscillation data.

## Method
The authors solve systems of two algebraic equations relating the squared mass differences extracted from GSI modulation periods for pairs of H-like ions (¹²²I⁵²⁺, ¹⁴⁰Pr⁵⁸⁺, ¹⁴²Pm⁶⁰⁺) to the proper neutrino masses plus Coulomb-field corrections. The Coulomb corrections to neutrino masses are calculated for each daughter ion using a nuclear radius parameterization R = 1.1 × A¹/³, with mixing angles θ₁₂ = 34° and θ₂₃ = 45° taken from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers
- Periods of time modulation: T_EC = 7.10(22) s for ¹⁴²Pm⁶⁰⁺, 7.06(8) s for ¹⁴⁰Pr⁵⁸⁺, and 6.11(3) s for ¹²²I⁵²⁺
- Modulation amplitudes: a_EC = 0.23(4) for ¹⁴²Pm⁶⁰⁺, 0.18(3) for ¹⁴⁰Pr⁵⁸⁺, and 0.22(2) for ¹²²I⁵²⁺
- Squared mass differences from GSI data: (Δm²₂₁)_GSI = 2.20(7)×10⁻⁴ eV² (¹⁴²Pm), 2.18(3)×10⁻⁴ eV² (¹⁴⁰Pr), 2.19(1)×10⁻⁴ eV² (¹²²I); combined value 2.19×10⁻⁴ eV²
- KamLAND value for comparison: (Δm²₂₁)_KL = 7.59(21)×10⁻⁵ eV²
- Recommended neutrino masses from the (I, Pm) pair: m₁ = 0.01345 eV, m₂ = 0.01991 eV, m₃ = 0.05735 eV; sum Σm_j = 0.08624 eV
- Proper squared mass difference from recommended masses: Δm²₂₁ = 2.155×10⁻⁴ eV²
- Coulomb corrections to neutrino masses: δm₁ = −8.967×10⁻⁴ eV (¹²²I daughter), −10.680×10⁻⁴ eV (¹⁴⁰Pr daughter), −11.610×10⁻⁴ eV (¹⁴²Pm daughter); δm₂ = −5.068×10⁻⁴ eV, −5.990×10⁻⁴ eV, −6.507×10⁻⁴ eV respectively

## Context
This work extends the proposal by Ivanov et al. (2010) that massive neutrinos acquire mass corrections in strong Coulomb fields via dissociation into ℓ⁻W⁺ pairs, applied to the GSI time modulation measurements of K-shell electron capture decays. The authors argue that the KamLAND reactor antineutrino oscillation result (Δm²₂₁ = 7.59×10⁻⁵ eV²) cannot be used directly for proper neutrino masses without applying Coulomb corrections to antineutrino masses. The calculated neutrino masses satisfy cosmological constraints (0.05 eV/c² < Σm_j < 0.17 eV/c²) and enable a proposed reanalysis of solar neutrino data using Δm²₂₁ = 2.155×10⁻⁴ eV² instead of the KamLAND value.
