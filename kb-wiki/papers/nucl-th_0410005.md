# n-p Interaction Effects on the Double Beta Decay Nuclear Matrix Elements for Medium Mass Nuclei

> The quality of HFB wave functions are tested by comparing the theoretically calculated results with the available experimental data for a number of spectroscopic properties like yrast spectra, reduced B(E2) transition probabilities, quadrupole moments and g-factors for the nuclei involved in 2$ν$ $ββ$ decay. It is observed that the np interactions vis-à-vis the deformations of the intrinsic ground states of medium mass nuclei play a crucial role in the fine tuning of the nuclear matrix elements, M$_{2ν}.$

# n-p Interaction Effects on the Double Beta Decay Nuclear Matrix Elements for Medium Mass Nuclei

## Key claim
The neutron-proton (n-p) interaction strength, specifically the proton-neutron component of the quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) interaction, plays a crucial role in fine-tuning the 2ν ββ decay nuclear matrix elements M₂ν for medium-mass nuclei, but the validity of nuclear models cannot be uniquely established due to experimental error bars and uncertainty in the axial-vector coupling constant gₐ.

## Method
The authors employed the Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) model with a valence space spanning orbits 1p₁/₂, 2s₁/₂, 1d₃/₂, 1d₅/₂, 0g₇/₂, 0g₉/₂, and 0h₁₁/₂ for both protons and neutrons, treating ⁷⁶Sr as an inert core. The effective two-body interaction was of the PPQQ type; the proton-neutron strength χₚₙ of the QQ interaction was fixed by fitting the calculated yrast spectra of the nuclei involved in ββ decay to experimental data, while pairing strengths were set to Gₚ = -30/A MeV and Gₙ = -20/A MeV. The resulting wave functions were then used to compute the double Gamow-Teller matrix elements M₂ν for 2ν ββ decay.

## Key numbers
- For ¹⁰⁰Mo, the optimum χₚₙ value was 0.01906 MeV b⁻⁴; for ¹⁰⁰Ru, the optimum χₚₙ was -0.01838 MeV b⁻⁴.
- The calculated M₂ν for ¹⁰⁰Mo → ¹⁰⁰Ru was 0.152, yielding half-lives of 4.57 × 10¹⁸ yr (gₐ = 1.25) and 11.15 × 10¹⁸ yr (gₐ = 1.0); the experimental half-life from the NEMO collaboration was 9.5 ± 0.4 ± 0.9 × 10¹⁸ yr.
- For ¹⁰⁶Cd → ¹⁰⁶Pd, the PHFB model gave |M₂ν| = 0.238, with predicted half-lives for β⁺β⁺ decay of 35.42 × 10²⁵ yr (gₐ = 1.25) and 89.56 × 10²⁵ yr (gₐ = 1.0); the experimental lower limit was > 2.4 × 10²⁰ yr.
- The phase space factor used for ¹⁰⁰Mo was G₂ν = 9.434 × 10⁻¹⁸ yr⁻¹, with an energy denominator E_d = 11.2 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the PHFB approach of Dixit et al. (2004) to systematically test wave function quality against spectroscopic observables before computing ββ decay matrix elements. It builds on the suggestion by Federman and Pittel that n-p interaction in spin-orbit partner orbits drives deformation in Mo isotopes with A > 100, and on the method of fixing p-n interaction strength through yrast spectra demonstrated for ¹¹⁰Cd and ¹¹⁴Cd. The results enable future application of the same PHFB wave functions to 0ν ββ decay calculations for medium-mass nuclei.
