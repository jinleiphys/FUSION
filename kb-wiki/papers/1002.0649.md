# Constraints on models for the initial collision geometry in ultra relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Monte Carlo (MC) simulations are used to compute the centrality dependence of the collision zone eccentricities ($ε_{2,4}$), for both spherical and deformed ground state nuclei, for different model scenarios. Sizable model dependent differences are observed. They indicate that measurements of the $2^{\text{nd}}$ and $4^{\text{th}}$ order Fourier flow coefficients $v_{2,4}$, expressed as the ratio $\frac{v_4}{(v_2)^2}$, can provide robust constraints for distinguishing between different theoretical models for the initial-state eccentricity. Such constraints could remove one of the largest impediments to a more precise determination of the specific viscosity from precision $v_{2,4}$ measurements at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC).

## Key claim
The ratio of the fourth-order to the square of the second-order eccentricity, \(\frac{\varepsilon_4}{(\varepsilon_2)^2}\), shows sizable model-dependent differences between the MC-Glauber and MC-KLN approaches, which can be exploited to experimentally distinguish between these initial-state models via measurements of the corresponding flow coefficient ratio \(\frac{v_4}{(v_2)^2}\) for collisions of near-spherical and deformed nuclei.

## Method
Monte Carlo simulations were performed using the MC-Glauber and MC-KLN models to compute the centrality dependence of the eccentricities \(\varepsilon_2\) and \(\varepsilon_4\) for collisions of both spherical and deformed nuclei. The spatial distribution of nucleons was generated using a deformed Woods-Saxon function with deformation parameters \(\beta_2\) and \(\beta_4\), and the eccentricities were calculated from the transverse density profile of sources after random orientation of the nuclear polarization axes. The ratio \(\frac{\varepsilon_4}{(\varepsilon_2)^2}\) was then evaluated as a function of the number of participants \(N_{\text{part}}\) for each model and for different nuclear species.

## Key numbers
- For Au+Au collisions, the MC-Glauber and MC-KLN models give \(\varepsilon_{2,4}\) values that differ by up to \(\sim 25\%\) for a given \(N_{\text{part}}\), leading to an approximate factor of two uncertainty in extracted \(\eta/s\) values.
- The ratio \(\frac{\varepsilon_4}{(\varepsilon_2)^2}\) for near-spherical \(^{148}\)Dy and deformed \(^{158}\)Dy shows a significant difference over the full range of \(N_{\text{part}}\) for MC-Glauber, while the MC-KLN results show a smaller difference and a different \(N_{\text{part}}\) dependence.
- For Au+Au collisions, the MC-KLN results give a relatively flat \(\frac{\varepsilon_4}{(\varepsilon_2)^2}\) for \(40 \lesssim N_{\text{part}} \lesssim 200\), in contrast to a characteristic decrease seen in the MC-Glauber results over the same range.
- A mixing parameter \(\gamma = 0.2\) between the principal axes of the quadrupole and hexadecapole density profiles was used to increase \(\frac{\varepsilon_4}{(\varepsilon_2)^2}\) for \(N_{\text{part}} \gtrsim 200\) without significant change for \(N_{\text{part}} \lesssim 200\).

## Context
This work extends prior efforts to extract the specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) of the quark-gluon plasma from anisotropic flow measurements at RHIC, where uncertainties in the initial eccentricity are a major impediment (Luzum 2008, Song 2008, Chaudhuri 2009). The study directly compares the two primary models used for eccentricity estimates, the Glauber model (Alver 2006, Miller 2007) and the factorized Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (fKLN) model (Lappi 2006, Drescher 2007), and proposes that systematic measurements of \(\frac{v_4}{(v_2)^2}\) for isotopic pairs like \(^{148}\)Dy and \(^{158}\)Dy can provide a robust experimental constraint to select between them. This would enable a more precise determination of \(\eta/s\) from precision flow measurements.
