# Neutrino-nucleon scattering in supernova matter from the virial expansion

> We generalize our virial approach to study the neutral current neutrino response of nuclear matter at low densities. In the long-wavelength limit, the virial expansion makes model-independent predictions for neutrino-nucleon scattering rates and the density S_V and spin S_A responses. We find S_A is significantly reduced from one even at low densities. We provide a simple fit S_A^f(n,T,Y_p) of the axial response as a function of density n, temperature T and proton fraction Y_p. This fit reproduces our model independent virial results at low densities and reproduces the Burrows and Sawyer random phase approximation (RPA) results at high densities. Our fit can be incorporated into supernova simulations in a straight forward manner. Preliminary one dimensional supernova simulations suggest that the reduction in the axial response may enhance neutrino heating rates in the gain region during the accretion phase of a core-collapse supernovae.

## Key claim

The virial expansion predicts that the axial response \(S_A\) of nuclear matter is significantly reduced from unity even at low densities, which may enhance neutrino heating rates in the gain region during the accretion phase of a core-collapse supernova; the paper provides a fit that reproduces the model-independent virial results at low densities and the Burrows and Sawyer random phase approximation (RPA) results at high densities.

## Method

The authors generalized their virial expansion approach to describe neutral-current neutrino-nucleon scattering in low-density nuclear matter composed of protons and neutrons, in the long-wavelength limit (\(q \rightarrow 0\)). They calculated the vector response \(S_V\) and the axial response \(S_A\) using the virial equation of state expanded to second order in fugacities, with second virial coefficients computed from nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering phase shifts (Nijmegen partial-wave analysis). They then constructed an interpolating fit function \(S_A^f(n,T,Y_p)\) that matches the virial results at low densities and the Burrows and Sawyer RPA results at high densities, and implemented this fit in one-dimensional supernova simulations using the GR1D code.

## Key numbers

* The axial response \(S_A\) is significantly reduced from 1; for example, at \(T=4\) MeV and low densities, the virial coefficient \(b_a \approx -0.648\) and \(b_{pn}^a \approx 0.785\) (from Table 1).
* The fit function \(S_A^f\) uses parameters \(A_0 = 920\), \(B_0 = 3.05\), \(C_0 = 6140\), and \(D_0 = 1.5 \times 10^{13}\) (with \(n\) in fm\(^{-3}\) and \(T\) in MeV), and is most accurate for \(5 < T < 10\) MeV, \(Y_p \le 0.3\), and \(n < 0.05\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* In one-dimensional supernova simulations, the virial-corrected rates increase the heating rate by about 5% around 100 ms after bounce, compared to simulations with free particle rates; the assumed strange-quark contribution (\(g_a^s = -0.2\)) raises the heating rate by about 20% around 100 ms after bounce.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous virial approach for pure neutron matter (Horowitz et al., 2012) to nuclear matter with both neutrons and protons, and provides a model-independent treatment of neutrino-nucleon scattering at low densities relevant to the neutrinosphere in core-collapse supernovae. The results contradict the assumption that the axial response is near unity at low densities, and the fit bridges the virial results with the Burrows and Sawyer (1998) RPA calculations used in supernova simulations. The preliminary simulations suggest that the reduced axial response may enhance neutrino heating rates, enabling future studies of the impact on explosion dynamics in two- and three-dimensional simulations.
