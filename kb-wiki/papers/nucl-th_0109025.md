# Shell-Model Calculations with Realistic Effective Interactions

> In this paper, we present some results of shell-model calculations employing effective interactions derived from the CD-Bonn free nucleon-nucleon potential. These concern 18O, 134Te, and 210Po, and are part of a comprehensive study of nuclei around doubly closed shells. Comparison of the calculated results with experimental data illustrates the degree of accuracy of modern realistic shell-model calculations.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations using effective interactions derived from the CD-Bonn free nucleon-nucleon potential can reproduce experimental low-energy excitation energies in nuclei with two valence particles around doubly closed shells, with discrepancies less than 150 keV for most states. The authors note that most of their calculations have concerned nuclei with identical valence nucleons, and that a careful test of the T=0 matrix elements is equally important.

## Method

The authors first calculate the Brueckner G-matrix from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, including a Pauli correction term, using a harmonic oscillator parameter derived from the expression hbar-omega = (45A^{-1/3} - 25A^{-2/3}) MeV. They then compute the irreducible vertex function (Q-box) composed of first- and second-order G-matrix diagrams, and sum the folded-diagram series to all orders using the Lee-Suzuki iteration method to obtain an energy-independent effective interaction. Single-particle energies are taken from the experimental spectra of the corresponding single-particle valence nuclei, and calculations are performed using the OXBASH shell-model code.

## Key numbers

- In 18O: calculated 2+ state at 1.824 MeV (experimental 1.982 MeV); calculated 4+ state at 3.269 MeV (experimental 3.555 MeV); calculated second 0+ state at 3.720 MeV (experimental 3.634 MeV)
- In 134Te: calculated 2+ state at 1.188 MeV (experimental 1.279 MeV); calculated 4+ state at 1.472 MeV (experimental 1.576 MeV); calculated 6+ state at 1.605 MeV (experimental 1.691 MeV)
- In 210Po: calculated 2+ state at 1.076 MeV (experimental 1.181 MeV); calculated 4+ state at 1.342 MeV (experimental 1.427 MeV); calculated 6+ state at 1.435 MeV (experimental 1.473 MeV); calculated 8+ state at 1.498 MeV (experimental 1.557 MeV)
- Discrepancy between calculated and experimental excitation energies is less than 150 keV for most states across all three nuclei

## Context

This work extends earlier realistic shell-model calculations by the same group using the Bonn-A potential (Andreozzi et al., 1997; Coraggio et al., 1999) for 134Te and 210Po, now employing the more recent CD-Bonn potential which gives the most accurate reproduction of nucleon-nucleon scattering data. The results demonstrate that modern realistic effective interactions can provide quantitative descriptions of nuclear structure properties near doubly closed shells. This enables further studies of nuclei with both neutrons and protons outside closed shells, such as the doubly odd nucleus 132Sb studied by Andreozzi et al. (1999), to test the T=0 matrix elements of the effective interaction.
