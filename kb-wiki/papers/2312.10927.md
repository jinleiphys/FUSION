# Non-Abelian chiral soliton lattice in rotating QCD matter: Nambu-Goldstone and excited modes

> The ground state of QCD with two flavors at a finite baryon chemical potential under rapid rotation is a chiral soliton lattice (CSL) of the $η$ meson, consisting of a stack of sine-Gordon solitons carrying a baryon number, due to the anomalous coupling of the $η$ meson to the rotation. In a large parameter region, the ground state becomes a non-Abelian CSL, in which due to the neutral pion condensation each $η$ soliton decays into a pair of non-Abelian sine-Gordon solitons carrying $S^2$ moduli originated from Nambu-Goldstone (NG) modes localized around it, corresponding to the spontaneously broken vector symmetry SU$(2)_{\rm V}$. There, the $S^2$ modes of neighboring solitons are anti-aligned, and these modes should propagate in the transverse direction of the lattice due to the interaction between the $S^2$ modes of neighboring solitons. In this paper, we calculate excitations including gapless NG modes and excited modes around non-Abelian and Abelian ($η$) CSLs, and find three gapless NG modes with linear dispersion relations (type-A NG modes): two isospinons ($S^2$ modes) and a phonon corresponding to the spontaneously broken vector SU$(2)_{\rm V}$ and translational symmetries around the non-Abelian CSL, respectively, and only a phonon for the Abelian CSL because of the recovering SU$(2)_{\rm V}$. We also find in the deconfined phase that the dispersion relation of the isospinons becomes of the Dirac type, {\it i.~e.~} linear even at large momentum.

## Key claim

The ground state of two-flavor QCD under rapid rotation can host a non-Abelian chiral soliton lattice (CSL) where each η meson soliton decays into a pair of non-Abelian sine-Gordon solitons carrying S² moduli; the paper calculates that this non-Abelian CSL supports three gapless Nambu-Goldstone modes with linear dispersion relations (two isospinons and one phonon), while the Abelian (η) CSL supports only one phonon, with the isospinon dispersion becoming Dirac-type (linear even at large momentum) in the deconfined phase.

## Method

The authors start from the chiral Lagrangian for two-flavor QCD with an anomalous coupling of the η meson to rotation, parametrize the U(2) field in terms of η and pion fields, and construct the ground state configurations for the confined (Abelian η CSL), deconfined, and dimer phases by solving the equations of motion with appropriate boundary conditions. They then expand the Lagrangian to quadratic order in fluctuations around these background configurations, separate the fluctuations into neutral (phonon) and charged (isospinon) components, and apply Bloch's theorem with Born–von Karman boundary conditions to numerically obtain the dispersion relations of the gapless and excited modes.

## Key numbers

- Critical angular velocity for CSL formation: S_c = 4/π (dimensionless), corresponding to the condition S ≥ 4/π for the CSL to be energetically more stable than the vacuum.
- The non-Abelian CSL yields three gapless NG modes with linear dispersion relations (type-A): two isospinons from spontaneously broken SU(2)_V symmetry and one phonon from broken translational symmetry.
- The Abelian (η) CSL yields only one gapless NG mode (a phonon) because SU(2)_V is not broken.
- In the deconfined phase, the isospinon dispersion relation becomes of Dirac type, linear even at large momentum.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of non-Abelian CSLs in rotating QCD matter (Eto 2021) by calculating the excitation spectrum, including the Nambu-Goldstone and massive modes, which was not done before. It also builds on the earlier prediction of a CSL of the η' meson under rotation by Huang (2017) and Nishimura (2020), and on the classification of NG modes by Watanabe (2012, 2014) and Hidaka (2012). The results enable future studies of transport properties and experimental signatures of these phases in heavy-ion collisions, and connect to the broader program of understanding QCD phase diagrams under external fields such as rotation and magnetic fields.
