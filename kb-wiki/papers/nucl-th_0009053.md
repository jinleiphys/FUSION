# Uncertainties in the eta-Nucleon Scattering Length and Effective Range

> The coupled eta-N, pi-N, gamma-N system is described by a K-matrix method. The parameters in this model are adjusted to get an optimal fit to pi-N --->pi- N$, pi-N --->eta-N$, gamma-N--->pi-N and gamma-N --->eta-N data in an energy range of about 100MeV or so each side of the eta threshold. The outcome is the appearance of two solutions one which has an eta-nucleon scattering length (a) of about 1.0 fm and a second with a~0.2fm. However, the second solution has an unconventional non-Lorentian form for the T-matrix in the region of the 1535(20)MeV and 1650(30)MeV S-wave pi-N resonances.

## Key claim

The eta-nucleon scattering length remains ambiguous, with two distinct solutions emerging from a K-matrix fit to coupled pi-N, eta-N, and gamma-N data: one with a scattering length of about 1.0 fm and another with about 0.2 fm, the latter being unconventional because it produces a non-Lorentzian T-matrix shape near the 1535 and 1650 MeV S-wave pi-N resonances.

## Method

The authors used a K-matrix method to simultaneously fit pi-N to pi-N, pi-N to eta-N, gamma-N to pi-N, and gamma-N to eta-N data within about 100 MeV of the eta threshold. The model included explicit channels for pi-N, eta-N, and gamma-N, with parameters describing pole positions, channel couplings, and energy-independent background terms; the pi-pi-N channel was included implicitly. The fit was performed using the Minuit minimization program, and the resulting effective range parameters were extracted from the T-matrix expansion.

## Key numbers

*   Eta-nucleon scattering length (conventional solution): a = 0.75(4) + i0.27(3) fm, from the earlier two-channel K-matrix fit (Green and Wycech, 1997).
*   Eta-nucleon scattering length (unconventional solution GW21): a = 0.21 + i0.30 fm, from the two-channel K-matrix fit with an energy-dependent photoproduction amplitude.
*   Eta-nucleon scattering length (extended three-channel model, solution A): a = 0.87 + i0.27 fm, using 32 gamma-N to pi-N data points with center-of-mass energy up to 1550 MeV.
*   Eta-nucleon scattering length (extended three-channel model, solution B): a = 1.05 + i0.27 fm, using 48 gamma-N to pi-N data points with center-of-mass energy up to 1650 MeV.
*   Resonance widths for the 1535 MeV resonance: conventional solution gave 152 MeV, unconventional solution gave 354 MeV; Particle Data Group value is 150(5) MeV.
*   Resonance widths for the 1650 MeV resonance: conventional solution gave 150 MeV, unconventional solution gave 133 MeV; Particle Data Group range is 145–190 MeV.
*   Chi-squared per degree of freedom for the two solutions with 155 data points and 12 parameters: 1.036 (conventional) and 1.029 (unconventional).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier K-matrix analyses (Green and Wycech, 1997, 1998, 1999) by incorporating new experimental data from the GRAAL collaboration for gamma-p to eta-p and new pi-N to eta-N data from Tom and from Strakovsky. The existence of two nearly degenerate minima in the parameter space, one with a large scattering length (~1 fm) and one with a small scattering length (~0.2 fm), complicates the determination of the eta-nucleon scattering length. The ambiguity directly impacts predictions for eta-nuclear quasi-bound states, first suggested by Haider and Liu (1986), and the interpretation of reactions such as pn to d-eta, eta-d to eta-d, and pd to He-3 eta. The new gamma-p to eta-p data favor the conventional solution with a larger scattering length, but the new pi-N to eta-N data introduce a structure near threshold that neither solution can reproduce, suggesting either an omitted effect in the K-matrix model or a need for experimental confirmation of the structure.
