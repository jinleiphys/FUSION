# Analysis of Dalitz decays with intrinsic parity violating interactions in resonance chiral perturbation theory

> Observables of light hadron decays are analyzed in a model of chiral Lagrangian which includes resonance fields of vector mesons. In particular, transition form factors are investigated for Dalitz decays of $V\to Pl^+l^-$ and $P\to γl^+l^-$ $(V=1^-, P=0^-)$. Moreover, the differential decay width of $P\to π^+π^-γ$ and the partial widths of $P\to2γ, V\to Pγ, η^\prime\to Vγ, ϕ(1020)\toω(782)π^0$ and $V\to 3P$ are also calculated. In this study, we consider a model which contains octet and singlet fields as representation of $SU(3)$. As an extension of chiral perturbation theory, we include 1-loop ordered interaction terms. For both pseudoscalar and vector meson, we evaluate mixing matrices in which isospin/$SU(3)$ breaking is taken into account. Furthermore, intrinsic parity violating interactions are considered with singlet fields. For parameter estimation, we carry out $χ^2$ fittings in which a spectral function of $τ$ decays, vector meson masses, decay widths of $V\to Pγ$ and transition form factor of $V\to Pl^+l^-$ are utilized as input data. Using the estimated parameter region in the model, we give predictions for decay widths and transition form factors of intrinsic parity violating decays. As further model predictions, we calculate the transition form factors of $ϕ(1020)\to π^0l^+l^-$ and $η^\prime\toγl^+l^-$ in the vicinity of resonance regions, taking account of the contribution for intermediate $ρ(770)$ and $ω(782)$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model of resonance chiral perturbation theory with SU(3) octet and singlet fields, including 1-loop ordered counter terms and intrinsic parity violating interactions, and claims that after fitting parameters to experimental data (spectral functions, masses, and decay widths), the model can predict transition form factors and decay widths for intrinsic parity violating decays such as $\phi(1020)\to\pi^0 l^+l^-$ and $\eta^\prime(958)\to\gamma l^+l^-$, but the predictions depend on the estimated parameter region from $\chi^2$ fitting and include SU(3) breaking only at tree level for the intrinsic parity violating sector in the first part of the analysis.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral Lagrangian with vector meson resonance fields (octet and singlet under SU(3)), including 1-loop ordered counter terms based on power counting of superficial degree of divergence. They compute 1-loop corrections to self-energies for both vector mesons and pseudoscalars, diagonalize the mass matrices to obtain physical states ($\rho(770)$, $\omega(782)$, $\phi(1020)$ and $\pi^0$, $\eta$, $\eta^\prime$), and include kinetic mixing between neutral vector mesons and $V-\gamma$ mixing. Parameters are estimated via $\chi^2$ fitting using the Belle spectral function of $\tau^-\to K_S\pi^-\nu$ decay, vector meson masses, decay widths of $V\to P\gamma$, and transition form factors of $V\to Pl^+l^-$.

## Key numbers

- The pole mass squared of neutral vector mesons is defined as $\cM_I^2 - m_I^2 \mathrm{Re}\delta B_{VI}(m_I^2) = 0$, with the width given by $\Gamma_I = m_I Z_I \mathrm{Im}\delta B_{VI}(m_I^2)$
- The tree-level $\rho\pi\pi$ coupling is $(g_{\rho\pi\pi})_{\mathrm{tree}} = M_V^2/(2gf^2)$, and the 1-loop ordered coupling is $g_{\rho\pi\pi} = M_V^2/(2g f_\pi^2)$
- The partial decay width for $V\to PP$ is $\Gamma[V^I\to PP] = (m_I |g_{VPP}^{\mathrm{eff}}|^2)/(48\pi) \left(1 - 4M_P^2/m_I^2\right)^{3/2}$
- The renormalization scale is fixed as $\mu = m_{K^{*+}}$
- The mass matrix elements for neutral vector mesons include terms such as $M_\rho^2 = M_V^2 + C_1^r M_\pi^2 + C_2^r (2\bar{M}_K^2 + M_\pi^2) - 4g_{\rho\pi\pi}^2 (\mu_\pi + \bar{\mu}_K/2) f^2$

## Context

This work extends the resonance chiral perturbation theory framework of Kimura (2014) by including the $\phi$ meson, electromagnetic mass of pseudoscalars, and intrinsic parity violating interactions with singlet fields. It builds on the hidden local symmetry approach of Bando (1984) and the vector meson formulation of Ecker (1988, 1989), and provides numerical predictions for transition form factors that can be tested against high-precision data from NA60 (Arnaldi, 2016) and BES III (Ablikim, 2015) collaborations, as well as earlier analyses by Bramon (1994), Hashimoto (1996), Terschluesen (2010, 2012), and Escribano (2015).
