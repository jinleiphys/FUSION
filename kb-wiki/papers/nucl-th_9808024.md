# Non-ideal Particle Distributions from Kinetic Freeze Out Models

> In fluid dynamical models the freeze out of particles across a three dimensional space-time hypersurface is discussed. The calculation of final momentum distribution of emitted particles is described for freeze out surfaces, with both space-like and time-like normals, taking into account conservation laws across the freeze out discontinuity.

# Non-ideal Particle Distributions from Kinetic Freeze Out Models

## Key claim
The paper argues that the post-freeze-out momentum distribution of particles emitted from a fluid dynamical freeze-out hypersurface is not a simple thermal (Juttner) distribution, but rather a "comet shaped" distribution that is asymmetric and elongated in the freeze-out direction, especially when the freeze-out surface has a space-like normal vector. The authors qualify that this conclusion depends on the assumption of a stationary, planar freeze-out front with negligible curvature, and that the idealized sharp freeze-out hypersurface is an over-simplification for heavy ion reactions except possibly when freeze-out coincides with a rapid phase transition.

## Method
The authors develop kinetic freeze-out models in the rest frame of the freeze-out front (RFF), starting from a fully equilibrated Juttner distribution and evolving coupled differential equations for an interacting component and a free-streaming component. They first present a simple model without rescattering, then an improved model with rescattering treated via the relaxation time approximation and an "immediate re-thermalization" limit where the interacting component is assumed to instantly re-equilibrate to a spherical Juttner shape at each infinitesimal step. Conservation laws for baryon current and energy-momentum tensor across the freeze-out discontinuity are used to determine the changing parameters (temperature, density, flow velocity) of the interacting component as particles are transferred to the free component.

## Key numbers
- After about three mean free paths, the amount of interacting matter reduces to 5 per cent (i.e., ~95% of matter is frozen out by x = 3λ).
- The freeze-out distribution at large distances (x → ∞) in the simple model without rescattering becomes a "cut Juttner distribution" where only particles with p^μ dσ_μ > 0 are present.
- For a massless, baryon-free Bose gas, the post-freeze-out temperature T and flow velocity u^μ are determined from the conservation laws via equations involving the Stefan-Boltzmann constant σ_SB, with the solution given by x = (a^μ dσ_μ + sqrt((a^μ dσ_μ)^2 + 3 a^μ a_μ)) / (a^μ a_μ) and u^μ = (x a^μ + dσ^μ) / (2 sqrt(x a^μ dσ_μ + 1)).

## Context
This work extends the general theory of discontinuities in relativistic flow, originally developed by Taub (1948) for propagating hypersurfaces with space-like normals and generalized by Csernai (1987) to both space-like and time-like surfaces. It builds on the Cooper-Frye formula (1974) for calculating momentum distributions from freeze-out surfaces and on earlier four-volume emission models (Anderlik and Csernai, 1998). The paper shows that the commonly used assumption of a thermal (Juttner) post-freeze-out distribution is inadequate, and that the actual distribution is a superposition of cut Juttner components from a series of gradually slowing-down thermal distributions. This enables future dynamical calculations of non-thermal post-freeze-out hadron momentum distributions, particularly for rapid hadronization of quark-gluon plasma with simultaneous freeze-out.
