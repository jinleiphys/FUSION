# Study of the A(e,e'$π^+$) Reaction on $^1$H, $^2$H, $^{12}$C, $^{27}$Al, $^{63}$Cu and $^{197}$Au

> Cross sections for the p($e,e'π^{+}$)n process on $^1$H, $^2$H, $^{12}$C, $^{27}$Al, $^{63}$Cu and $^{197}$Au targets were measured at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility (Jefferson Lab) in order to extract the nuclear transparencies. Data were taken for four-momentum transfers ranging from $Q^2$=1.1 to 4.8 GeV$^2$ for a fixed center of mass energy of $W$=2.14 GeV. The ratio of $σ_L$ and $σ_T$ was extracted from the measured cross sections for $^1$H, $^2$H, $^{12}$C and $^{63}$Cu targets at $Q^2$ = 2.15 and 4.0 GeV$^2$ allowing for additional studies of the reaction mechanism. The experimental setup and the analysis of the data are described in detail including systematic studies needed to obtain the results. The results for the nuclear transparency and the differential cross sections as a function of the pion momentum at the different values of $Q^2$ are presented. Global features of the data are discussed and the data are compared with the results of model calculations for the p($e,e'π^{+}$)n reaction from nuclear targets.

## Key claim

The paper reports cross sections and nuclear transparencies for the A(e,e'π⁺) reaction across multiple nuclei, but the extracted transparencies and cross section ratios are presented as global features compared to model calculations rather than as definitive evidence for color transparency, with the authors noting that the quasi-free approximation underlying the transparency extraction requires validation through comparison of longitudinal-to-transverse cross section ratios between nuclear and free-proton targets.

## Method

The experiment used the continuous-wave electron beam at Jefferson Lab Hall C, with energies up to 5.8 GeV incident on liquid hydrogen, liquid deuterium, and solid foil targets of ¹²C, ²⁷Al, ⁶³Cu, and ¹⁹⁷Au. Scattered electrons were detected in the Short Orbit Spectrometer (SOS) and electroproduced π⁺ mesons in the High Momentum Spectrometer (HMS), with particle identification using Čerenkov detectors and calorimeters. Cross sections were measured at five Q² settings from 1.1 to 4.8 GeV² at fixed center-of-mass energy W = 2.14 GeV, and nuclear transparencies were extracted as the ratio of acceptance-corrected cross sections from nuclear targets to those from the free proton.

## Key numbers

- Q² range: 1.1 to 4.8 GeV², with central values at 1.10, 2.15, 3.00, 3.91, and 4.69 GeV²
- Fixed center-of-mass energy: W = 2.14 GeV (with two settings at 2.21 and 2.26 GeV)
- Longitudinal-to-transverse cross section ratio extracted for ¹H, ²H, ¹²C, and ⁶³Cu targets at Q² = 2.15 and 4.0 GeV²
- SOS tracking efficiency: >96% with 0.5% uncertainty; HMS tracking efficiency: >92% with 1.0% uncertainty
- Pion transmission through HMS: approximately 95%, weakly dependent on pion momentum between 2.1 and 4.4 GeV/c
- Čerenkov detector efficiencies: >99.2% for SOS electron identification, >98.2% for HMS pion identification at high momentum settings
- Beam current: 35-80 μA, measured to 0.4% accuracy
- Computer dead time: approximately 25% during the experiment

## Context

This work extends earlier nuclear transparency measurements from the A(e,e'p) reaction at SLAC (Makins, Neill) and Jefferson Lab (Garrow, Abbott), which found no significant color transparency effect in proton knockout, and from coherent and incoherent ρ⁰ production at Fermilab (Adams) and DESY (Airape). The simultaneous measurement of Q² and A dependence of pion transparency across six targets enables tests of color transparency predictions from the quantum diffusion model (Farrar) and provides data for validating QCD factorization theorems for meson electroproduction (Collins), which require color transparency as a precondition.
