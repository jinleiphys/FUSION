# Zero-mode contribution and quantized first order phase transition in a droplet quark matter

> The finite size effect on hadron physics and quark matter has attracted much interest for more than three decades, normally both the periodic (with zero-momentum mode) and the anti-periodic (without zero-momentum mode) spatial boundary condition are applied for fermions. By comparing the thermodynamical potential, it is found that if there is no other physical constraint, the droplet quark matter is always more stable when the periodic spatial boundary condition is applied, and the catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking is observed with the decrease of the system size, while the pions excited from the droplet vacuum keep as pseudo Nambu-Goldstone bosons. Furthermore, it is found that the zero-momentum mode contribution brings significant change of the chiral apparent phase transition in a droplet of cold dense quark matter: the 1st-order chiral apparent phase transition becomes quantized, i.e., the 1st-order apparent phase transition is completed in two steps, which is a brand-new quantum phenomena. It is expected that the catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking and the quantized 1st-order apparent phase transition are common features for fermionic systems with quantized momentum spectrum with zero-mode contribution, which also show up in quark matter under magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a droplet of cold dense quark matter, the zero-momentum mode contribution from periodic spatial boundary conditions causes the first-order chiral apparent phase transition to become "quantized," meaning it is completed in two distinct steps rather than one. This result is conditional on the system size being in the range of approximately 2 fm to 5 fm, where the zero-mode contribution is non-negligible but higher modes also contribute.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a four-fermion interaction in the scalar channel, placing quark matter in a cubic box of finite side length L. They replace momentum integrals with discrete momentum summations under either periodic boundary conditions (including the zero-momentum mode) or anti-periodic boundary conditions (excluding it). The thermodynamical potential is computed in the Pauli-Villars regularization scheme, and the gap equation is solved to obtain the constituent quark mass as a function of temperature, chemical potential, and system size.

## Key numbers

- The pion Compton wavelength is given as \(\lambda_{\pi} = 1/m_{\pi} \sim 1.41\) fm.
- Model parameters are fixed as \(\Lambda = 782.37\) MeV and \(G = 6.197/\Lambda^2\) by fitting \(f_{\pi} = 93\) MeV and constituent quark mass \(M = 330\) MeV.
- For infinite volume, the critical end point in the \((T,\mu_B)\) plane is located at \((T^E = 48\) MeV, \(\mu_B^E = 994\) MeV).
- The quantized two-step first-order apparent phase transition appears in the size range \(2\) fm \(< L < 5\) fm.
- The pressure difference between \(L = 2\) fm and \(L = \infty\) is in the order of \([200\) MeV\(]^4\), comparable to the bag constant in the MIT bag model.

## Context

This work extends studies of finite-size effects on QCD phase structure, which have been investigated for over three decades by authors such as Fisher (1972), Luscher (1985), and Gasser (1987). It directly compares with prior work by Kiriyama (2006), Shao (2006), Palhares (2009), and Almasi (2016) that applied either periodic or anti-periodic boundary conditions for fermions in finite volume. The paper also draws a parallel to magnetic catalysis in quark matter under strong magnetic fields, as studied by Gusynin (1994) and Miransky (2015), noting that the zero-mode contribution in small systems is analogous to the lowest Landau level dominance in strong magnetic fields. The results enable future investigations of how zero-mode contributions affect the equation of state of quark droplets in neutron stars, particularly in Wigner-Seitz cell structures as studied by Wu (2017).
