# Kaon Eletromagnetic Form Factor in the Light-Front Formalism

> Numerical calculations are performed and compared to the experimental data for the electromagnetic form factor of the kaon, which is extracted from both components of the electromagnetic current, $J^{+}$ and $J^{-}$, with a pseudo-scalar coupling of the quarks to the kaon. In the case of $J^{+}$ there is no pair term contribution in the Drell-Yan frame ($q^{+}=0$). However, for $J^{-}$, the pair term contribution is different from zero and necessary in order to preserve the rotational symmetry of the current. The free parameters are the quark masses and the regulator mass.

# Kaon Electromagnetic Form Factor in the Light-Front Formalism

## Key claim
The kaon electromagnetic form factor can be extracted equivalently from either the $J^{+}$ or $J^{-}$ component of the electromagnetic current in light-front formalism, provided that for $J^{-}$ the pair-term (zero-mode) contribution is included; without the pair term, the $J^{-}$ result disagrees with the covariant calculation, while $J^{+}$ (in the Drell-Yan frame $q^{+}=0$) has no pair term and automatically matches the covariant result.

## Method
The authors compute the kaon electromagnetic form factor using a constituent quark model with pseudo-scalar quark-kaon coupling, evaluating both the $J^{+}$ and $J^{-}$ components of the electromagnetic current in the Breit frame. For $J^{+}$, only the valence contribution (interval $0 < k^{+} < P^{+}$) is needed; for $J^{-}$, an additional pair-term contribution from the interval $P^{+} < k^{+} < P^{\prime +}$ is calculated and added. The model uses a regulator function $\Lambda(k,p)=N/[(p-k)^2-m^2_R+i\epsilon]$ to control divergences, with free parameters being the quark masses and the regulator mass.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark masses: $m_u = 0.220$ GeV, $m_{\bar{s}} = 0.419$ GeV
- Regulator mass: $m_R = 0.946$ GeV
- Calculated kaon electromagnetic radius: $\langle r^2_{K^+}\rangle = 0.354$ fm$^2$
- Experimental kaon electromagnetic radius: $\langle r^2_{K^+}\rangle = 0.340$ fm$^2$ (Amendolia 1986)
- Number of colors: $N_c = 3$

## Context
This work extends the light-front formalism for pseudoscalar mesons, building on earlier studies of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and light-front wave functions by Bogolyubov (1983), Terent'ev (original light-front wave-function models), and subsequent work by Chung (1988), Ji (1990), Frederico (1992), Cardarelli (1996), and others. The specific treatment of pair terms follows the approach of Pacheco (1998) and Naus (1998) for composite boson bound states and the Ward-Takahashi identity. The results enable consistent light-front calculations of kaon electromagnetic properties and can be extended to vector mesons, where even $J^{+}$ requires pair terms for full covariance (Pacheco 1998, Jaus 1999).
