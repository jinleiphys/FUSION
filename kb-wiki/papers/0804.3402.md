# Systematics of collective correlation energies from self-consistent mean-field calculations

> The collective ground-state correlations stemming from low-lying quadrupole excitations are computed microscopically. To that end, the self-consistent mean-field model is employed on the basis of the Skyrme-Hartre-Fock (SHF) functional augmented by BCS pairing. The microscopic-macroscopic mapping is achieved by quadrupole-constrained mean-field calculations which are processed further in the generator-coordinate method (GCM) at the level of the Gaussian overlap approximation (GOA). We study the correlation effects on energy, charge radii, and surface thickness for a great variety of semi-magic nuclei. A key issue is to work out the influence of variations of the SHF functional. We find that collective ground-state correlations (GSC) are robust under change of nuclear bulk properties (e.g., effective mass, symmetry energy) or of spin-orbit coupling. Some dependence on the pairing strength is observed. This, however, does not change the general conclusion that collective GSC obey a general pattern and that their magnitudes are rather independent of the actual SHF parameters.

## Key claim

Collective ground-state correlations from low-lying quadrupole excitations in semi-magic nuclei are robust under changes of nuclear bulk properties (effective mass, symmetry energy) and spin-orbit coupling, but show some dependence on pairing strength; the authors conclude that these correlations obey a general pattern and their magnitudes are rather independent of the actual Skyrme-Hartree-Fock parameters, though the study is limited to nearly spherical nuclei where the Gaussian overlap approximation is reliable.

## Method

The authors performed quadrupole-constrained Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) mean-field calculations with BCS pairing (augmented by Lipkin-Nogami correction) to generate a collective path of axially deformed states. They then mapped these states into a five-dimensional collective Bohr-Hamiltonian using the generator-coordinate method at the level of the Gaussian overlap approximation (GCM-GOA), including dynamical response for the collective mass (dynamic GCM). The collective Schrodinger equation was solved to obtain ground-state correlation energies, charge radii, diffraction radii, and surface thicknesses for a wide range of semi-magic isotopic and isotonic chains, comparing results across different Skyrme parameterizations (SkM*, SLy6, SkI3) and systematically varied bulk properties.

## Key numbers

- Correlation energies reach up to approximately 2-3 MeV for mid-shell isotopes in lighter systems (Z=20, N=20), with some cases like 40Ca showing slightly positive correlation energies around +0.5 MeV (within expected precision of the method)
- Correlation effects on charge radii are about 0.02 fm or less for heavier systems, comparable to typical uncertainties in well-fitted Skyrme forces
- For Sn isotopes (Z=50), correlation energies are large within isotopic chains (up to ~2 MeV) while remaining moderately small (below ~1 MeV) in N=82 isotonic chains
- The dynamical response (dynamic GCM) reduces correlation energies by about 20-50% compared to the static GCM approximation
- For 208Pb with SkI3, the small-amplitude limit (RPA) yields E(2_1+) = 5.4 MeV, while linearized constrained Hartree-Fock yields 6.8 MeV, indicating problems with the constrained path for doubly-magic nuclei

## Context

This work extends systematic surveys of collective correlations using Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (Ben05a, Ben06a, Sab07a) and a similar study using the Gogny force (Ber07e) by exploring in detail the dependence on the SHF functional parameters. The results show that collective ground-state correlations are robust under variations of effective mass, symmetry energy, and spin-orbit coupling, but depend on pairing strength. The findings prepare ground for revising phenomenological adjustments of Skyrme functionals by identifying the least correlated nuclei, and indicate that full adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (ATDHF) would improve performance at and around doubly-magic nuclei where the constrained path overestimates collectivity.
