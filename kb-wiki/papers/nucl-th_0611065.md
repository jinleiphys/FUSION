# Properties of few-body systems in relativistic quantum mechanics and constraints from transformations under Poincaré space-time translations

> Different approaches have been applied to the calculation of form factors of various hadronic systems within relativistic quantum mechanics. In a one-body current approximation, they can lead to results evidencing large discrepancies. Looking for an explanation of this spreading, it is shown that, for the largest part, these discrepancies can be related to a violation of Poincaré space-time translation invariance. Beyond energy-momentum conservation, which is generally assumed, fulfilling this symmetry implies specific relations that are generally ignored. Their relevance within the present context is discussed in detail both to explain the differences between predictions and to remove them.

# Properties of few-body systems in relativistic quantum mechanics and constraints from transformations under Poincaré space-time translations

## Key claim

Large discrepancies between form factor predictions from different relativistic quantum mechanics (RQM) approaches can be largely attributed to a violation of Poincaré space-time translation invariance, not merely to violations of Lorentz invariance or energy-momentum conservation. The standard front-form (with q+=0) and instant-form (Breit frame) approaches naturally satisfy the required constraints, while other forms require many-body currents to restore the symmetry.

## Method

The author calculates charge form factors of the ground state in the Wick-Cutkosky model (scalar particles exchanging a massless scalar meson) using six different RQM approaches: front-form with perpendicular kinematics, instant-form in the Breit frame, point-form (Bakamjian and Sokolov), Dirac-inspired point-form, front-form with parallel kinematics, and instant-form with parallel kinematics. The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude from the solvable model serves as the "experiment" for comparison. The violation of Poincaré space-time translation invariance is tested via the double commutator relation [Pμ,[Pμ,Jν(x)]] = -∂μ∂μJν(x), which requires that the squared 4-momentum transferred to the system (q²) equals the squared 4-momentum transferred to the constituents ((pi-pf)²). Corrections are applied by modifying the boost coefficient to enforce this equality.

## Key numbers

- For the Dirac-inspired point-form (D.P.F.), the violation factor of the double commutator relation is 3 at the highest Q² considered
- For the front-form and instant-form with parallel kinematics (F.F.+I.F.(parallel)), the violation factor is 30 at the highest Q²
- For the point-form of Bakamjian and Sokolov ("P.F."), the violation factor is 35000 at the highest Q²
- The charge radius in the uncorrected approaches scales like M⁻¹, where M is the total mass of the system
- After correction for Poincaré space-time translation invariance, the undesirable dependence of the charge radius on M vanishes and discrepancies of many orders of magnitude at high Q are largely removed

## Context

This work extends earlier form factor calculations by Desplanques (2004) in the Wick-Cutkosky model and by Amghar (2002) on mass operator solutions. It directly addresses the question of why different RQM forms (Dirac 1949) produce widely varying predictions for hadronic form factors, particularly for light systems like the pion. The author notes that similar results have been found for the charge pion form factor by Dong, indicating that constituent spin is not essential to the issue. Remaining discrepancies between approaches (front-form perpendicular, instant-form Breit frame, point-form, and a dispersion-relation approach by Krutov) can be completely removed by using a modified current choice, after accounting for translation invariance effects. This work enables future studies to focus on the underlying dynamics rather than on uncertainties from the relativistic implementation.
