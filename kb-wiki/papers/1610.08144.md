# Departure from Equilibrium of the Quasi-Particle Distribution Functions in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> In simulations of high energy heavy ion collisions that employ viscous hydrodynamics, single particle distributions are distorted from their thermal equilibrium form due to gradients in the flow velocity. These are closely related to the formulas for the shear and bulk viscosities in the quasi-particle approximation. Distorted single particle distributions are now commonly used to calculate the emission of photons and dilepton pairs, and in the late stage to calculate the conversion of a continuous fluid to individual particles. We show how distortions of the single particle distribution functions due to both shear and bulk viscous effects can be done rigorously in the quasi-particle approximation and illustrate it with the linear $σ$ model at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the commonly used approximation of momentum- and species-independent shear and bulk viscous distortion functions for single-particle distributions in viscous hydrodynamics is only a rough first approximation; for precision studies, the distortion functions must be calculated using the same particle species and interactions as in the equation of state, and they show significant momentum dependence and species variation (factors of 2-3 for shear functions, even wider for bulk functions) in the linear σ model.

## Method

The authors derive rigorous expressions for the shear and bulk viscous distortion functions \(C_a\) and \(A_a\) in the quasi-particle approximation, using the relaxation time approximation with temperature-dependent effective masses. They illustrate the formalism with the linear σ model at finite temperature (T = 120 and 150 MeV), computing relaxation times and distortion functions for pions and σ mesons with vacuum σ masses of 600 and 900 MeV, and enforcing the Landau-Lifshitz condition of fit for the bulk viscosity functions.

## Key numbers

- Shear functions \(C_\sigma\) are significantly larger than \(C_\pi\) at all momenta shown, with the difference varying by factors of 2-3 depending on momentum and temperature
- Relaxation times for pions and σ mesons are computed at T = 120 MeV and T = 150 MeV for vacuum σ masses of 600 MeV and 900 MeV, shown as functions of momentum in Figure 1
- The bulk functions \(A_\pi\) and \(A_\sigma\) show strong momentum dependence, with the σ meson contribution differing even more widely from the pion than in the shear case, as shown in Figure 3 for the same temperature and mass combinations
- The shear viscosity is almost entirely determined by the pion contribution because the σ contribution is exponentially suppressed by the Boltzmann factor, despite \(C_\sigma > C_\pi\)

## Context

This work extends the standard approach introduced by Teaney (2003) which assumed momentum-independent and species-independent shear distortion functions \(C_a = \eta/(2sT^3)\) and \(A_a = 0\), and follows the quasi-particle transport formalism of earlier work (Chakraborty and Kapusta, 2011). The results enable more precise calculations of photon and dilepton emission from viscous hydrodynamics, and more accurate conversion of fluid to particles via the Cooper-Frye formula, particularly for precision studies at RHIC and LHC. The authors plan future comparisons with the Chapman-Enskog method and extensions to resonance gas models with all hadrons and resonances, nonzero chemical potentials, and mean fields.
