# Similarity Renormalization Group Evolution of Chiral Effective Nucleon-Nucleon Potentials in the Subtracted Kernel Method Approach

> Methods based on Wilson's renormalization group have been successfully applied in the context of nuclear physics to analyze the scale dependence of effective nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) potentials, as well as to consistently integrate out the high-momentum components of phenomenological high-precision $NN$ potentials in order to derive phase-shift equivalent softer forms, the so called $V_{low-k}$ potentials. An alternative renormalization group approach that has been applied in this context is the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG), which is based on a series of continuous unitary transformations that evolve hamiltonians with a cutoff on energy differences. In this work we study the SRG evolution of a leading order (LO) chiral effective $NN$ potential in the $^1 S_0$ channel derived within the framework of the Subtracted Kernel Method (SKM), a renormalization scheme based on a subtracted scattering equation.

## Key claim

The paper studies the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) evolution of a leading-order (LO) chiral effective nucleon-nucleon potential in the \(^1S_0\) channel, derived using the Subtracted Kernel Method (SKM) as an alternative renormalization scheme that avoids cutoff regularizing functions; the key condition is that the SKM approach is applied only at LO in the chiral expansion and only for the \(^1S_0\) channel, not for higher partial waves or higher orders.

## Method

The authors apply Wegner's formulation of the SRG, using the generator \(\eta_s = [T_{\text{rel}}, H_s]\) with flow parameter \(s = \lambda^{-4}\), to evolve the LO chiral effective NN potential in the \(^1S_0\) channel. The potential is first constructed within the SKM framework, which regularizes the Lippmann-Schwinger equation by performing subtractions in the kernel at a given energy scale rather than introducing a cutoff function. The SRG flow equation is then solved numerically to evolve the potential to lower similarity cutoffs, and the resulting phase shifts are computed from the evolved potential.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is \(\Lambda_\chi \sim 1\ \text{GeV}\), used as the natural cutoff for the chiral expansion.
- In the two-dimensional Dirac-delta contact interaction example, the exact on-shell T-matrix is \(T^{(l=0)}(k,k;k^2) = -2\left[\ln(k^2/E_0) - i\pi\right]^{-1}\), where \(E_0\) is the binding energy.
- For the two-dimensional Dirac-delta model, the cutoff-dependent inverse T-matrix error is \(\Delta T^{-1} = -\frac{(E_0 + k^2)}{2\Lambda^2} + \frac{(E_0^2 - k^4)}{4\Lambda^4} + \mathcal{O}\left[\frac{E_0^3}{\Lambda^6}; \frac{k^6}{\Lambda^6}\right]\).
- In the two-dimensional Dirac-delta model, the renormalized running coupling is \(\alpha_\Lambda = \frac{4\pi}{\ln\left(1 + \frac{\Lambda^2}{E_0}\right)}\).

## Context

This work extends the application of the SRG, previously applied to phenomenological high-precision NN potentials (e.g., Argonne V18, Nijmegen) by Bogner, Furnstahl, and Perry (2007) and others, to chiral effective potentials derived via the SKM approach. The SKM was developed by Timoteo, Szpigel, and Duraes (2008-2010) as an alternative renormalization scheme for interactions with contact terms. The results enable future studies of the SRG evolution of higher-order chiral potentials and the consistent evolution of many-body forces within the SKM framework.
