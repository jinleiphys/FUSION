# Comment on "No-core configuration-interaction model for the isospin- and angular-momentum-projected states" by Satuła, Baczyk, Dobaczewski, and Konieczka

> The authors state that results recently published by Carlsson and Toivanen "seem to contradict" the conclusions of some of our work. We show that this statement is based on a misinterpretation of the results of Carlsson and Toivanen.

# Comment on "No-core configuration-interaction model for the isospin- and angular-momentum-projected states"

## Key claim

The authors show that the statement by Satula, Baczyk, Dobaczewski, and Konieczka (2016) that results by Carlsson and Toivanen (2014) "seem to contradict" the conclusions of Bentley, Neergard, and Frauendorf (2013, 2014) is based on a misinterpretation; in fact, the Carlsson and Toivanen findings concur with some of the authors' conclusions, specifically that a strong isoscalar pairing interaction eliminates the N=Z doubly odd doubly even mass staggering.

## Method

The authors model masses of nuclei in the range A=24-100 with no or little neutron excess, treating the lowest T=0 and T=1 states separately in doubly odd N=Z nuclei. In Bentley (2013), an independent nucleon plus isovector pairing Hamiltonian is diagonalized exactly in a small valence space of Nilsson levels, with a T(T+1) term added. In Bentley (2014), the exact diagonalization is replaced by Hartree-Bogolyubov plus random phase approximation (RPA) with Strutinskij renormalization, allowing larger valence spaces. The authors then compare their approach to the RPA calculations of Carlsson and Toivanen (2014), which address excitation energies of neutron-proton pair and neutron hole-proton hole pair excitations of a doubly magic core.

## Key numbers

- The 112 masses of doubly even nuclei in the sample are reproduced with a root mean square deviation of 0.95 MeV (Bentley 2014).
- Carlsson and Toivanen (2014) find a ratio of about 1.4 of their isoscalar and isovector coupling constants gives the best fit in neighbors of doubly magic nuclei with N ≠ Z.
- When the same interaction is applied to neighbors of doubly magic nuclei with N=Z, the RPA calculations give imaginary excitation energies, implying the N=Z doubly odd doubly even mass staggering vanishes in the neighborhoods of doubly magic nuclei.

## Context

This comment responds to Satula et al. (2016), who claimed that Carlsson and Toivanen (2014) contradicted the authors' earlier work (Bentley 2013, 2014) on the role of isoscalar pairing and the Wigner energy. The authors show that Carlsson and Toivanen (2014) actually support their conclusion that strong isoscalar pairing eliminates N=Z mass staggering, and that Carlsson and Toivanen did not address the Wigner energy at all. The work also references a shell model calculation for A=48 by Satula, Dean, Gary, Mizutory, and Nazarewicz (1997), which the authors previously commented on in Bentley (2014), showing that the Wigner energy decreases when isoscalar pair interactions are switched off but the Wigner X simultaneously increases.
