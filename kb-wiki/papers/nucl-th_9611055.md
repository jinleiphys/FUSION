# Baryon Magnetic Moments in a Relativistic Quark Model

> Magnetic moments of baryons in the ground-state octet and decuplet are calculated in a light-front framework. We investigate the effects of quark mass variation both in the current operator and in the wavefunctions. A simple fit uses single oscillator wavefunctions for the baryons and allows the three flavors of quark to have nonzero anomalous magnetic moments. We find a good fit to the data without allowing for strange quark contributions to the nucleon moments. A slightly better fit is obtained by allowing for explicit SU(3)_f breaking in the wavefunctions through a simple mechanism. The predictions for magnetic moments in our relativistic model are also much less sensitive to the values chosen for the constituent quark masses than those of nonrelativistic models. Relativistic effects can be of order 20% in general, and can alter familiar relationships between the moments based on SU(3)_f and a nonrelativistic treatment of spin.

# Baryon Magnetic Moments in a Relativistic Quark Model

## Key claim

A good fit to the measured ground-state baryon magnetic moments can be achieved in a light-front relativistic quark model without requiring strange quark contributions to the nucleon moments, and the fit is slightly improved by allowing explicit SU(3)_f breaking in the wavefunctions. The relativistic model predictions are much less sensitive to the values of constituent quark masses than those of nonrelativistic models, with relativistic effects of order 20% that can alter familiar SU(3)_f-based relationships between moments.

## Method

Magnetic moments of ground-state octet and decuplet baryons were calculated using a light-front framework with single-oscillator Gaussian wavefunctions. The calculation allowed the three flavors of quark (u, d, s) to have nonzero anomalous magnetic moments, and investigated the effects of quark mass variation both in the current operator and in the wavefunctions, including a simple mechanism for SU(3)_f breaking by allowing the harmonic oscillator size parameters to depend on quark masses.

## Key numbers

- Proton magnetic moment changes from 2.42 μ_N to 2.76 μ_N (a 14% enhancement) when light quark masses are reduced from 330 MeV to 220 MeV and strange quark mass from 550 MeV to 419 MeV
- Three-parameter fit (allowing anomalous moments κ_u, κ_d, κ_s) reduced root-mean-square deviation from nine precise data points from 0.135 μ_N to 0.076 μ_N
- Resulting quark anomalous moments: κ_u = -0.011, κ_d = -0.048, κ_s = -0.020 (in units of quark magnetons e/2m_q)
- With SU(3)_f-breaking wavefunctions, r.m.s. deviation further reduced to 0.068 μ_N, with quark anomalous moments κ_u = -0.006, κ_d = -0.047, κ_s = -0.022
- Σ^0-Λ^0 mixing angle θ_ΣΛ = +0.0135 radians; mixing lowers μ_Λ by about -0.04 μ_N and raises μ_Σ→Λ by 0.01 to 0.02 μ_N
- Relativistic effects are of order 20% in general

## Context

This work extends earlier light-front calculations by Aznauryan (1984), Tupper and McKellar (1988), Chung and Coester (1991), Schlumpf (1993), and Cardarelli (1995), which showed that proton and neutron magnetic moments cannot be fit simultaneously without modifying quark model parameters. The authors provide greater generality by studying unequal quark masses in both wavefunctions and current matrix elements, and by calculating moments for the entire ground-state octet and decuplet. The results enable comparison with direct calculations of quark anomalous magnetic moments via meson loops (Cohen and Weber, Ito) and with the chiral-symmetry-based approach of Cheng and Li (1995, 1996) for parameterizing quark sea effects.
