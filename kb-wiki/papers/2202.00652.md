# Sexaquark dilemma in neutron stars and its solution by quark deconfinement

> Following the idea that a stable sexaquark state with quark content (uuddss) would have gone unnoticed by experiment so far and that such a particle would be a good dark matter candidate, we investigate the possible role of a stable sexaquark in the physics of compact stars given the stringent constraints on the equation of state that stem from observations of high mass pulsars and GW170817 bounds on the compactness of intermediate mass stars. We find that there is a "sexaquark dilemma" (analogous to the hyperon dilemma) for which the dissociation of the sexaquark in quark matter is a viable solution fulfilling all present constraints from multi-messenger astronomy. The parameters needed to model the hybrid star including sexaquarks are in line with parameters of pre-existing quark- and hadronic-matter models. We find that current constraints -- tidal deformability in accordance with GW170817 and maximum mass above the lower limit from PSR J0740+6620 -- can be satisfied two ways: with early quark deconfinement such that neither sexaquarks nor hyperons are present in any NS interiors, or with later deconfinement such that a neutron-sexaquark shell surrounds the inner quark matter core.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a stable sexaquark (a six-quark bound state with quark content uuddss) can be compatible with current neutron star observations only if quark deconfinement occurs, either early enough that sexaquarks never appear in neutron star interiors or later such that a neutron-sexaquark shell surrounds a quark matter core; a sexaquark with mass above approximately 1885 MeV is compatible with observations, but without deconfinement the sexaquark creates a "sexaquark dilemma" analogous to the hyperon puzzle.

## Method

The authors construct a generalized relativistic density functional (GRDF) model for hadronic matter using the DD2 parameterization, extended to include all baryon octet members and a sexaquark with a density-dependent mass shift parameterized by a single adjustable coefficient x_S. They combine this with a two-flavor constant-speed-of-sound equation of state for deconfined quark matter and use two interpolation methods to construct hybrid star configurations, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- Sexaquark mass range considered: 1885 MeV to 2054 MeV (the lower bound avoids condensation below saturation density; the upper bound is the threshold for weak decay)
- For a density-independent sexaquark mass of 1941 MeV, Bose condensation occurs exactly at nuclear saturation density (0.149065 fm^-3), producing a maximum neutron star mass of only 0.21 solar masses
- For a density-independent sexaquark mass of 2054 MeV, Bose condensation occurs at 0.7 solar masses
- The DD2 model predicts a maximum neutron star mass of 2.4 solar masses in a pure nucleonic scenario
- Nuclear saturation density in the DD2 model: 0.149065 fm^-3; binding energy per nucleon: 16.02 MeV; incompressibility: 242.7 MeV; symmetry energy: 31.67 MeV with slope parameter 55.04 MeV
- Hyperon potentials in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation: U_Lambda = -28 MeV, U_Sigma = 30 MeV, U_Xi = -14 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the hyperon puzzle and its solution by quark deconfinement (Shahrbaf et al., 2019, 2020) to the analogous sexaquark dilemma. It addresses the argument by McDermott et al. (2018) that a deeply bound sexaquark would be incompatible with supernova 1987A observations, noting that the suppressed interconversion between sexaquark and two baryons resolves that concern. The paper also builds on the sexaquark dark matter proposal by Farrar (2018) and the thermal production estimate by Blaschke et al. (2021). The results enable future constraints on sexaquark mass from neutron star observations once the hadron-quark transition is better understood.
