# $Φ$-meson--nucleus bound states

> $ϕ$-meson--nucleus bound state energies and absorption widths are calculated for seven selected nuclei by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with complex optical potentials. Essential input for the calculations, namely the medium-modified $K$ and $\overline{K}$ meson masses, as well as the density distributions in nuclei, are obtained from the quark-meson coupling model. The attractive potential for the $ϕ$-meson in the nuclear medium originates from the in-medium enhanced $K\overline{K}$ loop in the $ϕ$-meson self-energy. The results suggest that the $ϕ$-meson should form bound states with all the nuclei considered. However, the identification of the signal for these predicted bound states will need careful investigation because of their sizable absorption widths.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the φ-meson should form bound states with all seven nuclei considered (⁴He, ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, ²⁰⁸Pb), but the identification of these bound states will need careful investigation because of their sizable absorption widths, which may make experimental observation difficult.

## Method

The authors calculated φ-meson–nucleus bound state energies and absorption widths by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with complex optical potentials derived from the in-medium enhanced K‾K loop in the φ-meson self-energy. The medium-modified K and K‾ meson masses and the nuclear density distributions were obtained from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The calculations used a local density approximation and were performed for three values of the cutoff parameter Λ_K (2000, 3000, and 4000 MeV) in the form factor at the φ-K‾K vertex.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, the in-medium kaon Lorentz scalar mass decreases by about 13% relative to its vacuum value.
- At ρ₀, the φ-meson downward mass shift is approximately 2% (from the vacuum mass of 1019.461 MeV), while the decay width broadens by an order of magnitude relative to its vacuum value of 4.266 MeV.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb with Λ_K = 4000 MeV, the 1s bound state energy is -25.8 MeV with a half-width Γ/2 of 16.0 MeV (including the imaginary part of the potential).
- For ⁴He with Λ_K = 4000 MeV, the 1s bound state energy is -1.0 MeV with a half-width of 8.3 MeV; no bound state is found for Λ_K = 2000 or 3000 MeV when the imaginary part is included.
- The real part of the potential depth varies from -20 MeV to -35 MeV for ⁴He and from -20 MeV to -30 MeV for ²⁰⁸Pb, depending on Λ_K.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of φ-meson mass shift and decay width in nuclear matter (Cobos-Martinez 2017) to finite nuclei, and improves upon an earlier calculation by Yamagata-Sekihara (2010) that found only two bound states using a weaker theoretical potential. The results are relevant to planned experiments at J-PARC (E29 collaboration) and Jefferson Lab (12 GeV upgrade) that aim to study φ-meson in-medium mass modification via φ-nucleus bound state formation, and complement invariant mass measurements such as those by the KEK-E325 collaboration (Muto 2005).
