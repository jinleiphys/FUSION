# Sub-threshold resonances in few-neutron systems

> Three- and four-neutron systems are studied within the framework of the hyperspherical approach with a local S-wave nn-potential. Possible bound and resonant states of these systems are sought as zeros of three- and four-body Jost functions in the complex momentum plane. It is found that zeros closest to the origin correspond to sub-threshold (nnn) (1/2-) and (nnnn) (0+) resonant states. The positions of these zeros turned out to be sensitive to the choice of the $nn$--potential. For the Malfliet- Tjon potential they are E(nnn)=-4.9-i6.9 (MeV) and E(nnnn)=-2.6-i9.0 (MeV). Movement of the zeros with an artificial increase of the potential strength also shows an extreme sensitivity to the choice of potential. Thus, to generate ^3n and ^4n bound states, the Yukawa potential needs to be multiplied by 2.67 and 2.32 respectively, while for the Malfliet-Tjon potential the required multiplicative factors are 4.04 and 3.59.

## Key claim

The three-neutron (nnn) and four-neutron (nnnn) systems do not form bound states with physical neutron-neutron potentials, but they do support sub-threshold resonant states (with negative real parts of the energy) whose positions in the complex momentum plane are highly sensitive to the choice of the nn-potential.

## Method

The authors used the hyperspherical harmonic approach in the minimal approximation (L = L_min) to reduce the three- and four-body Schrödinger equations to effective single hyperradial equations. They defined a many-body Jost function for these "democratic" systems and located its zeros in the complex momentum plane by combining the variable-constant method with complex coordinate rotation to handle the long-range tails of the effective potentials.

## Key numbers

- For the Malfliet-Tjon potential, the three-neutron (1/2-) sub-threshold resonance is at E = -4.9 - i6.9 MeV.
- For the Malfliet-Tjon potential, the four-neutron (0+) sub-threshold resonance is at E = -2.6 - i9.0 MeV.
- To generate a bound three-neutron state, the Yukawa potential must be multiplied by a factor of 2.67; for the Malfliet-Tjon potential the factor is 4.04.
- To generate a bound four-neutron state, the Yukawa potential must be multiplied by a factor of 2.32; for the Malfliet-Tjon potential the factor is 3.59.

## Context

This work extends earlier hyperspherical and Faddeev calculations by Gorbatov (1989), Birger (1977), Glöckle (1978), and others, who searched for multineutron resonances above threshold and found none for the states considered here. The authors show that the poles corresponding to the 1/2- three-neutron and 0+ four-neutron states do not vanish when the potential is reduced to its physical strength but instead move below threshold, becoming sub-threshold resonances. This result enables future studies of such resonances in environments where an external attractive field (e.g., inside a nucleus) could make them observable, and it provides a lower bound for the resonance energies and an upper bound for their widths.
