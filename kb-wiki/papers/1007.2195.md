# On extraction of value of axial mass from MiniBooNE neutrino quasi-elastic double differential cross section data

> MiniBooNE charge current quasi-elastic double differential cross section data are analyzed and confronted with predictions of two theoretical nucleus models: Fermi gas and spectral function. The fitting procedure includes the overall flux uncertainty multiplicative factor. In order to obtain a reliable value of the axial mass, bins with large contribution from small momentum transfer are eliminated from the analysis. It is shown that the best fit axial mass value becomes smaller as the momentum transfer cut is more restrictive. For $q_{cut}=500$~MeV/c the obtained values of axial mass are $M_A=1350\pm 66$~MeV for the Fermi gas and $M_A=1343\pm 60$~MeV for the spectral function. The value $M_A=1030$~MeV is excluded on the level which goes far beyond $5σ$.

## Key claim

The best-fit axial mass \(M_A\) extracted from MiniBooNE CCQE double-differential cross section data becomes smaller as a momentum-transfer cut is applied to exclude bins dominated by low-\(q\) events, but even with the most restrictive meaningful cut (\(q_{cut}=500\) MeV/c) the resulting values (\(M_A\approx 1.35\) GeV) remain far above the old world average of 1.03 GeV, which is excluded at well beyond \(5\sigma\).

## Method

The authors fit the MiniBooNE flux-averaged CCQE double-differential cross section in muon kinetic energy and scattering angle (137 non-zero bins) using predictions from two nuclear models, Fermi gas (FG) and spectral function (SF), both implemented in the NuWro Monte Carlo event generator. They construct a \(\chi^2\) function that includes a multiplicative flux-uncertainty parameter \(\lambda\) with a 10.7% penalty term, following the D'Agostini method. To avoid the region where the impulse approximation is unreliable, bins for which more than 50% of the predicted events have momentum transfer \(q < q_{cut}\) are eliminated; the cut is varied in steps of 50 MeV/c from 300 to 500 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- For \(q_{cut}=500\) MeV/c, best-fit axial mass: \(M_A = 1350 \pm 66\) MeV for the Fermi gas model.
- For \(q_{cut}=500\) MeV/c, best-fit axial mass: \(M_A = 1343 \pm 60\) MeV for the spectral function model.
- At \(q_{cut}=500\) MeV/c, the best-fit normalization factor is \(\lambda = 1.063\) for FG and \(\lambda = 1.23\) for SF.
- The value \(M_A = 1030\) MeV is excluded at a level far beyond \(5\sigma\).
- Minimal \(\chi^2\) at \(q_{cut}=500\) MeV/c: 14.45 (FG) and 23.2 (SF), with 108 bins retained.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of MiniBooNE CCQE data beyond the one-dimensional \(d\sigma/dQ^2\) fits reported by the MiniBooNE collaboration (2010) and by Butkevich (2010), using for the first time the full two-dimensional double-differential distribution. The results contradict the older deuterium-based world average of \(M_A \approx 1.03\) GeV from bubble-chamber experiments and are consistent with the larger values found by K2K (2005), MINOS (2008), and MiniBooNE itself. The authors suggest that the discrepancy may be resolved by including 2p-2h meson-exchange current contributions, as proposed in the non-relativistic model of Marteau and Martini, which could account for the missing cross section without requiring a large axial mass.
