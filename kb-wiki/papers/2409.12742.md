# Medium modifications of heavy-flavor jet angularities in high-energy nuclear collisions

> We present the first theoretical study of heavy-flavor jet angularities ($λ^κ_α$) in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=$ 5.02 TeV. The initial production of heavy-flavor jets is carried out using the POWHEG+PYTHIA8 prescription, while the jet evolution in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is described by the SHELL transport model. In p+p collisions, we observe narrower angularity distributions for D$^0$-tagged jets compared to inclusive jets, consistent with the ALICE preliminary results. We then demonstrate that jet quenching in the QGP slightly widens the angularity distribution of D$^0$-tagged jets in Pb+Pb collisions relative to that in p+p collisions for jet transverse momentum of $10 < p_{\rm T,jet} < 20$ GeV/c, while the angularity distributions of inclusive and D$^0$-tagged jets become narrower in Pb+Pb collisions relative to p+p at $p_{\rm T,jet} > 20$ GeV/c due to the strong influence of the selection bias. Additionally, by comparing the average angularities $\langle λ^κ_α \rangle$ of inclusive, D$^0$-tagged and B$^0$-tagged jets with varying $α$ and $κ$, we show that the larger the quark mass is, the lower the jet's $\langle λ^κ_α \rangle$ values are. As a result of the slenderer initial distribution, we predict that as compared to inclusive jets, the heavy-flavor jets, especially the B$^0$-tagged ones, will suffer stronger modifications of $\langle λ^κ_α \rangle$ in Pb+Pb relative to p+p at $10 < p_{\rm T,jet} < 20$ GeV/c. For a larger jet radius, a more significant broadening of jet angularities is predicted because of the enhanced contributions of the wide-angle particles.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first theoretical study of heavy-flavor jet angularities in Pb+Pb collisions, predicting that the modification pattern depends on jet transverse momentum: at low pT (10-20 GeV/c) the angularity distribution of D0-tagged jets is slightly broadened by the QGP, while at higher pT (>20 GeV/c) both inclusive and D0-tagged jets show narrower distributions due to selection bias effects that dominate over the genuine medium modification.

## Method
The initial production of heavy-flavor and inclusive jets in p+p collisions is simulated using the POWHEG+PYTHIA8 prescription, which matches next-to-leading order matrix elements with parton shower resummation. The in-medium evolution of jets through the quark-gluon plasma is modeled with the SHELL transport model, which combines modified Langevin equations for elastic scatterings with the higher-twist formalism for medium-induced gluon radiation, including a dead-cone suppression factor for heavy quarks. The medium background is provided by the CLVisc hydrodynamic model, and medium response (wake) particles are included via a Cooper-Frye expansion at the freeze-out hypersurface.

## Key numbers
- Jet angularities are calculated for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, with jet radius R = 0.2 and R = 0.4
- For D0-tagged jets at 10 < pT,jet < 20 GeV/c, the angularity distribution is slightly broadened in Pb+Pb relative to p+p; at pT,jet > 20 GeV/c, both inclusive and D0-tagged jets show narrower distributions in Pb+Pb
- The spatial diffusion coefficient is set to 2pi T D_s = 4.0, extracted from chi-squared fitting to D meson yield suppression from CMS and ALICE
- The medium transport coefficient q_0 = 1.2 GeV^2/fm, extracted from identified hadron production in A+A collisions
- The critical temperature for hadronization is T_c = 0.165 GeV
- For inclusive jets with R = 0.2 in the range 40 < pT,jet < 60 GeV/c, the angularity distributions show enhancement at small lambda and suppression at large lambda in Pb+Pb relative to p+p
- The average angularities <lambda^kappa_alpha> decrease with quark mass: inclusive jets have the largest values, D0-tagged jets intermediate, and B0-tagged jets the smallest
- The PbPb/pp ratio of average angularities shows reversed ordering: B0-tagged jets have the largest ratio, inclusive jets the smallest
- For larger jet radius R = 0.4, the angularity broadening of heavy-flavor jets is more significant than for R = 0.2

## Context
This work extends previous studies of heavy-flavor jet substructure observables such as radial profiles (Wang 2019, Wang 2020), fragmentation functions (Li 2022), and splitting functions (Li 2017) to the generalized jet angularities, which simultaneously probe the transverse momentum and angular distributions of jet constituents. The results are compared to ALICE preliminary data on D0-tagged jet angularities in p+p collisions (Dhankher 2024) and inclusive jet angularities in Pb+Pb collisions (Vertesi 2024). The calculations enable future experimental tests at the LHC of the predicted mass-dependent modifications of heavy-flavor jet angularities, particularly the broadening at low pT and for larger jet radii.
