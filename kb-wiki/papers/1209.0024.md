# Evaluation of the gn-->pi-p differential cross sections in the Delta-isobar region

> Differential cross sections for the process gn-->pi-p have been extracted from MAMI-B measurements of gd-->pi-pp, accounting for final-state interaction effects, using a diagrammatic technique taking into account the NN and piN final-state interaction amplitudes. Results are compared to previous measurements of the inverse process, pi-p--> ng, and recent multipole analyses.

## Key claim

The paper extracts differential cross sections for the reaction γ n → π⁻ p from MAMI-B measurements of γ d → π⁻ pp, but the extraction requires model-dependent final-state interaction (FSI) corrections that vary from 10% to 30% depending on kinematics, and the results assume the neutron is at rest to relate laboratory angles to center-of-mass angles.

## Method

The authors used a diagrammatic technique that includes NN and πN final-state interaction amplitudes to correct the MAMI-B deuteron data for FSI effects. They employed SAID phenomenological amplitudes for γN → πN, NN-elastic, and πN-elastic scattering as inputs, with the Bonn potential for the deuteron description. The extracted cross sections were compared to previous measurements from the inverse reaction π⁻ p → γ n and to multipole analyses from SAID and MAID.

## Key numbers

- 126 experimental points covering Eγ = 301 to 455 MeV and pion CM angle θ = 58° to 141°
- FSI correction factor R < 1, with (1-R) values varying from 10% to 30% depending on kinematics
- FSI contribution to overall systematics estimated at 2%
- χ²/data = 249.4/104 = 2.4 for MAMI-B data after FSI corrections and fitting (PE12 solution)
- Prior to fitting, χ²/data = 605.8/104 = 5.8 for SN11 prediction and 623.2/104 = 6.0 for MAID07 prediction
- At 6 overlapped energies (313, 337, 361, 385, 409, 432 MeV), χ²/data = 97.7/102 = 1.0 for CB@BNL data and 103.9/45 = 2.3 for MAMI-B data with PE12 solution
- Data renormalization changed from average 7% (SAID prediction) to about 4.5% after inclusion in fit
- Momentum threshold for protons ~270 MeV/c and for charged pions ~80 MeV/c
- Angular acceptance between 21° and 159° in the laboratory

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous FSI correction method applied to CLAS γ d → π⁻ pp data at higher energies (Eγ = 1050-2700 MeV) down to the Delta-isobar region (301-455 MeV). The results are compared to inverse reaction data from the Crystal Ball Collaboration at BNL (Aziz 2007) and to predictions from the SAID SN11 solution and MAID07. The paper notes that the dominant Δ(1232) multipole contribution is not changed significantly, and further progress requires polarized measurements expected from the CLAS Collaboration.
