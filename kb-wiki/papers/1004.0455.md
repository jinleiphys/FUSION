# Toward a unified description of hadro- and photoproduction: S-wave pi- and eta-photoproduction amplitudes

> The Chew-Mandelstam parameterization, which has been used extensively in the two-body hadronic sector, is generalized in this exploratory study to the electromagnetic sector by simultaneous fits to the pion- and eta-photoproduction S-wave multipole amplitudes for center-of-mass energies from the pion threshold through 1.61 GeV. We review the Chew-Mandelstam parameterization in detail to clarify the theoretical content of the SAID hadronic amplitude analysis and to place the proposed, generalized SAID electromagnetic amplitudes in the context of earlier employed parameterized forms. The parameterization is unitary at the two-body level, employing four hadronic channels and the gamma-N electromagnetic channel. We compare the resulting fit to the MAID parameterization and find qualitative agreement though, numerically, the solution is somewhat different. Applications of the extended parameterization to global fits of the photoproduction data and to global fits of the combined hadronic and photoproduction data are discussed.

## Key claim

The Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix parameterization, previously used only for hadronic two-body reactions, is generalized to the electromagnetic sector by simultaneously fitting S-wave pion- and eta-photoproduction multipole amplitudes, producing an eta photoproduction multipole with a resonant shape that is qualitatively similar to a Breit-Wigner form but shows significant deviation from it.

## Method

The authors extended the Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix approach, which enforces two-body unitarity, to include the gamma-N electromagnetic channel coupled to four hadronic channels (pi-N, eta-N, and others). They performed simultaneous fits to the isospin T=1/2 pi-photoproduction E0+ amplitude and the modulus of the eta-photoproduction E0+ amplitude, taking as input hadronic T-matrix elements from realistic fits to pion-nucleon scattering data. The resulting amplitudes were compared to the MAID parameterization.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy range covered: from pion threshold through 1.61 GeV
- The S-wave eta-photoproduction multipole was studied near the center-of-mass energy W = 1535 MeV, where the S11(1535) N* resonance is significantly coupled to the eta-N channel
- The hadronic input T-matrix elements came from fits with chi-squared per datum approximately 1

## Context

This work extends the Chew-Mandelstam parameterization used in the SAID hadronic amplitude analysis (Arndt et al., 2006) to the electromagnetic sector, building on earlier K-matrix analyses of coupled pi-N, eta-N, and gamma-N channels by Green et al. (1997, 1999) and Arndt et al. (1998). The results are compared to the MAID parameterization and to calculations by Kaiser et al. (1996), Tiator et al. (1998), and Aznauryan (2003). The framework is intended to enable future global fits combining hadroproduction and photoproduction data, which could allow electromagnetic data to back-constrain hadronic amplitudes.
