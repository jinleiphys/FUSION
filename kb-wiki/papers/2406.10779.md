# Quarkonium fragmentation in a variable-flavor number scheme: Towards NRFF1.0

> We report progress on the determination and study of quarkonium production within the fragmentation approximation. Our analyses address the moderate and large transverse-momentum regime, where the collinear fragmentation of a single parton is expected to dominate over the short-distance production, directly from the hard scattering, of the constituent $(Q \bar{Q})$ system. Parton fragmentation channels to pseudoscalar and vector quarkonia are built on the basis of non-Relativistic QCD next-to-leading computations, which we use to model initial-scale fragmentation inputs. Thus, a preliminary family of Variable-Flavor Number-Scheme (VFNS) fragmentation functions, named NRFF1.0, are constructed through standard DGLAP evolution. Statistical uncertainties are obtained from a Monte Carlo, replica-like approach embodying missing higher-order uncertainties.

## Key claim
The paper reports progress toward constructing a preliminary family of Variable-Flavor Number Scheme (VFNS) fragmentation functions for quarkonium, named NRFF1.0, using next-to-leading order (NLO) non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) inputs; the claim is hedged by stating these are preliminary results that will replace existing determinations, implying the scheme is not yet finalized.

## Method
The author built parton fragmentation channels to pseudoscalar and vector quarkonia using NLO NRQCD computations to model initial-scale fragmentation inputs. These inputs were then evolved via standard DGLAP evolution in a two-step process: first an expanded and decoupled evolution (EDevo) using the JETHAD code, then an all-order evolution (AOevo) using APFEL++. Statistical uncertainties were obtained from a Monte Carlo replica-like approach that embodies missing higher-order uncertainties (MHOUs).

## Key numbers
- Charm to eta_c (color-singlet) and charm to J/psi (color-singlet) fragmentation functions were shown for factorization scale mu_F ranging from 30 to 120 GeV.
- The analysis addresses the moderate and large transverse-momentum regime where collinear fragmentation of a single parton is expected to dominate over short-distance production.

## Context
This work extends prior NRQCD-based fragmentation studies by introducing a consistent VFNS DGLAP evolution scheme (HFNRevo) for quarkonia, building on NLO NRQCD initial-scale inputs. It is intended to replace the ZCW19+ and ZCFW22 determinations currently used for vector quarkonia (Celiberto, 2022; Celiberto, 2023) and B_c mesons (Celiberto, 2022; Celiberto, 2024). The NRFF1.0 sets will provide guidance for quarkonium physics at the HL-LHC, the EIC, and future lepton machines, and serve as a benchmark for AI-based extractions.
