# Mach cone induced by $γ$-triggered jets in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> MMedium excitation by jet shower propagation inside a quark-gluon plasma is studied within a linear Boltzmann transport and a multiphase transport model. Contrary to the naive expectation, it is the deflection of both the jet shower and the Mach-cone-like excitation in an expanding medium that is found to gives rise to a double-peak azimuthal particle distribution with respect to the initial jet direction. Such deflection is the strongest for hadron-triggered jets which are often produced close to the surface of dense medium due to trigger-bias and travel against or tangential to the radial flow. Without such trigger bias, the effect of deflection on $γ$-jet showers and their medium excitation is weaker. Comparative study of hadron and $γ$-triggered particle correlations can therefore reveal the dynamics of jet-induced medium excitation in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The double-peak azimuthal particle distribution observed in dihadron correlations arises not from a pure Mach cone in a uniform medium, but from the deflection of both the jet shower and the Mach-cone-like excitation by the radial flow and density gradient of the expanding medium; this deflection is weaker for gamma-triggered jets because they lack the trigger bias that forces hadron-triggered jets to be produced near the surface and travel against or tangential to the flow.

## Method

The authors used a linearized Monte Carlo simulation of the Boltzmann transport equation with only elastic parton scatterings (using a screened gluon propagator with constant screening mass) to track both jet shower partons and recoiled medium partons, with local temperature and flow velocities from a (3+1)D ideal hydrodynamic calculation for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC. They also employed the AMPT Monte Carlo model (a full Boltzmann parton and hadron transport model with only elastic parton collisions and HIJING initial conditions) to simulate hadron-triggered and gamma-triggered jet events and compute the resulting azimuthal correlations.

## Key numbers

- Initial quark energy of 20 GeV used in the linearized Boltzmann transport simulation in a uniform medium with temperature T = 300 MeV, with alpha_s = 0.5 and screening mass mu = 1.0 GeV.
- For the AMPT simulations in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV/nucleon, associated hadrons were selected with p_T^{asso} = 1-2 GeV/c.
- The opening angle between the two peaks in the double-peak structure approaches Delta-phi approximately 1 radian at very large parton cross sections (hydrodynamic limit).
- Gamma-triggered jet showers used initial jet energy E_T^gamma = 20 GeV with initial position (x,y,z) = (-4,0,0) fm.

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic studies of Mach cones (Casalderrey-Solana 2004, Ruppert 2005, Chaudhuri 2005, Betz 2009) and string theory calculations (Chesler 2007, Gubser 2009) that found Mach-cone-like excitations but failed to reproduce the observed conic azimuthal correlations. It also builds on AMPT simulations (Ma 2006) that suggested jet-induced wakes deflected by radial flow could produce the observed correlations. The authors propose that comparative study of dihadron and gamma-hadron azimuthal correlations can disentangle the jet-induced Mach-cone dynamics from other mechanisms such as triangular flow (Alver 2010) and hot spots (Takahashi 2009) that contribute to dihadron but not gamma-hadron correlations.
