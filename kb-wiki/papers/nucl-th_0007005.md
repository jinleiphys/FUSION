# Shadowing and Fragmentation in High-Energy Photo-Mesonproduction

> A model for the description of photonuclear reactions in the multi-GeV range is described that combines the coherent initial state interactions (shadowing) of the incoming photon with an incoherent coupled channel description of final state interactons of outgoing particles. Particle production itself is treated either, at low energies, through nucleon resonance decays or, at higher energies, through string fragmentation. The initial state nucleons can be far-off-shell due to ground state correlations. Results are shown for semi-inclusive meson production.

# Shadowing and Fragmentation in High-Energy Photo-Mesonproduction

## Key claim
The model combines Glauber shadowing for initial state interactions with a coupled-channel transport treatment of final state interactions, but the observable effects of in-medium vector meson mass shifts (such as a 0.18 fraction reduction per nuclear matter density) are largely masked by strong pion reabsorption and rescattering, making such shifts hardly visible in the final pion invariant mass spectrum.

## Method
The initial state interactions of the photon with the nucleus are treated via a coherent multiple-scattering Glauber model that includes vector meson photoproduction amplitudes and correlated two-body densities. Particle production is handled either through explicit nucleon resonance cross sections for invariant energies below 2.1 GeV or through the LUND string fragmentation model (FRITIOF) for higher energies. Final state interactions are described by a semiclassical coupled-channel transport calculation that includes all nucleon resonances up to 2.1 GeV invariant mass, all relevant mesons, and the FRITIOF string fragmentation mechanism, with nuclear ground state correlations implemented via spectral functions derived from collision integrals.

## Key numbers
- Shadowing reduces the total cross section for K+ photoproduction on Pb to about 60% of its unshadowed value at 7 GeV photon energy.
- The transition between nucleon-resonance-dominated production and string fragmentation occurs at an invariant energy threshold of roughly 2 GeV in the photon-nucleon system.
- The standard formation time used in the string fragmentation model is 0.8 fm/c.
- The predicted in-medium rho meson mass shift follows the relation m*_rho = (1 - 0.18 * rho/rho_0) * m_rho, where rho_0 is nuclear matter density.
- The free threshold for charmed D0-bar and D- meson photoproduction is about 3.7 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the Glauber model approach reviewed by Bauer to include full coupled-channel final state interactions, building on earlier transport calculations developed for heavy-ion collisions and applied to photonuclear reactions at MAMI energies (up to 800 MeV) by Lehr and Effenberger. The model enables quantitative studies of semi-inclusive meson production on nuclei, investigations of color transparency and the EMC effect, and analysis of experiments aiming to determine nuclear structure functions and their transition to partonic degrees of freedom. The approach connects to ongoing research on vector meson properties in nuclei reviewed by Cassing and Bratkovskaya, and to many-body calculations of nuclear spectral functions by Benhar.
