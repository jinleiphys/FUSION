# Electromagnetic spectral properties and Debye screening of a strongly magnetized hot medium

> We have evaluated the electromagnetic spectral function and its spectral properties by computing the one-loop photon polarization tensor in presence of magnetic field, particularly in a strong field approximation compared to the thermal scale. When the magnetic scale is higher than the thermal scale the lowest Landau level (LLL) becomes effectively (1+1) dimensional strongly correlated system that provides a kinematical threshold based on the mass scale. Beyond this threshold the photon strikes the LLL and the spectral strength starts with a high value due to the dimensional reduction and then falls off with increase of the photon energy due to LLL dynamics in a strong field approximation. This strongly enhances the dilepton rate over the thermal perturbative leading order (Born) rate at very low invariant mass. We have also investigated the electromagnetic screening by computing the Debye screening mass and it depends distinctively on three different scales (mass of the quasiquark, temperature and the magnetic field strength) of a hot magnetized system. The mass dependence of the Debye screening supports the occurrence of a magnetic catalysis effect in the strong field approximation.

## Key claim

The authors compute the one-loop photon polarization tensor in a strong magnetic field (where the magnetic scale exceeds the thermal scale, \(q_fB \gg T^2\)), finding that the electromagnetic spectral function diverges at the threshold \(p_\shortparallel^2 = 4m_f^2\) due to dimensional reduction to (1+1) dimensions in the lowest Landau level (LLL), and that this strongly enhances the dilepton rate over the perturbative leading-order (Born) rate at very low invariant mass.

## Method

The authors evaluate the one-loop photon polarization tensor using the Schwinger proper-time formalism for the fermion propagator in a constant magnetic field, then take the strong-field limit where only the lowest Landau level (LLL) contributes. They perform the Matsubara sum at finite temperature using the mixed-representation method of Pisarski, extract the imaginary part via the discontinuity of the propagator, and obtain the electromagnetic spectral function from the imaginary part of the traced polarization tensor. The Debye screening mass is computed from the static limit of the longitudinal component of the polarization tensor.

## Key numbers

* The spectral function contains a factor \(\left(1 - 4m_f^2 / p_\shortparallel^2\right)^{-1/2}\) that diverges at the threshold \(p_\shortparallel^2 = 4m_f^2\), arising from dimensional reduction to (1+1) dimensions in the LLL.
* The spectral function is proportional to the magnetic field strength \(B\) and vanishes in the massless quark limit (\(m_f \to 0\)).
* The dilepton production rate from LLL is of order \(\mathcal{O}[|eB|^2]\) when the lepton pair is produced inside the magnetized medium, with an additional kinematical threshold due to the lepton mass.
* The initial magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions is estimated as \(eB \approx m_\pi^2\) at RHIC and \(eB \approx 10 m_\pi^2\) at LHC, where \(m_\pi\) is the pion mass.
* The quark mass used in numerical calculations is \(m_f = m_u = m_d = 5\) MeV for a two-flavor system.

## Context

This work extends the formal field-theoretic treatment of dilepton production in magnetized media by Sadooghi and Taghinavaz (2015) using the Ritus eigenfunction method, and contrasts with the semi-classical Weizsacker-Williams approach of Tuchin (2013) that approximated photon virtuality as negligible. The results enable more precise calculations of dilepton and photon observables in non-central heavy-ion collisions where strong transient magnetic fields are generated, and the Debye screening analysis supports the magnetic catalysis effect previously studied by Gusynin, Miransky, and Shovkovy (1995) and Ferrer and de la Incera (2002).
