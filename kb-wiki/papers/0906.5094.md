# Using the $Δ_3$ statistic to test for missed levels in mixed sequence neutron resonance data

> The $Δ_3(L)$ statistic is studied as a tool to detect missing levels in the neutron resonance data where 2 sequences are present. These systems are problematic because there is no level repulsion, and the resonances can be too close to resolve. $Δ_3(L)$ is a measure of the fluctuations in the number of levels in an interval of length $L$ on the energy axis. The method used is tested on ensembles of mixed Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) spectra, with a known fraction of levels ($x%$) randomly depleted, and can accurately return $x$. The accuracy of the method as a function of spectrum size is established. The method is used on neutron resonance data for 11 isotopes with either s-wave neutrons on odd-A, or p-wave neutrons on even-A. The method compares favorably with a maximum likelihood method applied to the level spacing distribution. Nuclear Data Ensembles were made from 20 isotopes in total, and their $Δ_3(L)$ statistic are discussed in the context of Random Matrix Theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the $\Delta_3(L)$ statistic can be used to estimate the fraction of missed levels in neutron resonance data containing two mixed sequences, but the method's accuracy is limited by spectrum size and the uncertainties in the estimated depletion fraction can be as large as the depletion itself for small data sets (e.g., $N=90$).

## Method

The author generated ensembles of 500 mixed Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) spectra with known mixing fraction $\alpha$ and a known fraction $x\%$ of levels randomly removed, then computed the spectral average of $\Delta_3(L)$ for each ensemble. For each experimental isotope, the observed $\Delta_3(L)$ was compared to these ensemble averages via a $\chi^2$ minimization to estimate $x$, and the results were compared with a maximum likelihood method (MLM) applied to the level spacing distribution $P(s)$. The method was applied to s-wave neutron resonance data from 7 odd-A isotopes and p-wave neutron data from 4 even-even isotopes.

## Key numbers

* For $N=90$ mixed GOE spectra with $\alpha=0.25$, the $\Delta_3(L)$ method returned $\overline{x}=4.7\%$ for a true depletion of $5.3\%$, with a standard deviation $\sigma_x=3.4\%$; for $N=400$, it returned $\overline{x}=5.0\%$ for a true depletion of $5.0\%$, with $\sigma_x=1.9\%$.
* For $N=90$ mixed GOE spectra with $\alpha=0.25$, the MLM returned $\overline{x}=6.1\%$ for a true depletion of $5.0\%$, with $\sigma_x=3.4\%$; for $N=400$, it returned $\overline{x}=5.6\%$ for a true depletion of $5.0\%$, with $\sigma_x=1.6\%$.
* The $\Delta_3(L)$ analysis of the first 950 levels of $^{235}\text{U}$ gave $x=3\%$ missed levels, with errors estimated at about $1\%$ given the large $N$.
* For the even-even NDE of 9 isotopes from prior work (Mulhall 2007), the average $\Delta_3(L)$ gave a depletion estimate of $x=4\%$.
* The ensemble variance of $\Delta_3(L)$ for a single GOE spectrum is $\sigma_e^2 = (0.110)^2$, and for $m$ independent GOE spectra the ensemble variance is $\sigma_e^2 = (0.110\,m)^2$.

## Context

This work extends the use of the $\Delta_3(L)$ statistic, previously applied to single-sequence neutron resonance data in Mulhall (2007), to the more challenging case of two mixed sequences where level repulsion is absent. The method is compared favorably with the maximum likelihood approach of Agvaanluvsan et al. (2003) for detecting missed levels. The results enable more reliable completeness estimates for mixed-sequence neutron resonance data, which is important for testing Random Matrix Theory predictions and for nuclear data evaluation, though the author notes that the p-wave even-even data showed large discrepancies between the $\Delta_3(L)$ and MLM methods that warrant further investigation.
