# Double-$β$ Decay Matrix Elements from Lattice Quantum Chromodynamics

> A lattice quantum chromodynamics (LQCD) calculation of the nuclear matrix element relevant to the $nn\to ppee\overlineν_e\overlineν_e$ transition is described in detail, expanding on the results presented in Ref. [1]. This matrix element, which involves two insertions of the weak axial current, is an important input for phenomenological determinations of double-$β$ decay rates of nuclei. From this exploratory study, performed using unphysical values of the quark masses, the long-distance deuteron-pole contribution to the matrix element is separated from shorter-distance hadronic contributions. This polarizability, which is only accessible in double-weak processes, cannot be constrained from single-$β$ decay of nuclei, and is found to be smaller than the long-distance contributions in this calculation, but non-negligible. In this work, technical aspects of the LQCD calculations, and of the relevant formalism in the pionless effective field theory, are described. Further calculations of the isotensor axial polarizability, in particular near and at the physical values of the light-quark masses, are required for precise determinations of both two-neutrino and neutrinoless double-$β$ decay rates in heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

This exploratory LQCD calculation, performed at unphysical quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of approximately 806 MeV, demonstrates that the isotensor axial polarizability of the two-nucleon system, a quantity accessible only through double-weak processes and not from single-beta decay, can be extracted and is found to be smaller than the long-distance deuteron-pole contribution but non-negligible.

## Method

The authors employed a fixed-order background-field technique on a single ensemble of gauge-field configurations with N_f = 3 degenerate light-quark flavors at the SU(3) flavor-symmetric point. Compound propagators were constructed with flavor-diagonal background axial-vector fields at six nonzero values of the field-strength parameters, and the first- and second-order responses of two-point correlation functions for single and two-nucleon states were analyzed to extract the isotensor axial polarizability of the ^1S_0 two-nucleon system. The leading Delta I=2 low-energy constant of pionless effective field theory was then constrained from the calculated two-nucleon matrix element.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: m_pi ~ 806 MeV (unphysical, corresponding to the SU(3) flavor-symmetric point with degenerate up, down, and strange quark masses tuned to the physical strange quark mass)
* Lattice volume: L^3 x T = 32^3 x 48, with lattice spacing a ~ 0.12 fm
* Number of gauge configurations: 437, spaced by 10 Hybrid Monte Carlo trajectories
* Background field-strength parameters: lambda_{u,d} = {+/- 0.05, +/- 0.1, +/- 0.2} and zero
* Source-sink separations restricted to t < T/3 to suppress thermal effects by at least e^{-2 m_pi T/3} ~ 10^{-7}

## Context

This work extends the LQCD background-field methodology introduced by Savage et al. (2016) for single-weak processes to double-weak matrix elements relevant for double-beta decay. The calculation provides a first-principles determination of the isotensor axial polarizability, which cannot be constrained from single-beta decay measurements, and demonstrates a pathway to constrain few-nucleon inputs to ab initio many-body calculations of both two-neutrino and neutrinoless double-beta decay rates in heavy nuclei. Future calculations at physical quark masses, combined with effective field theory matching as demonstrated by Menendez et al. (2011), could enable rigorous extrapolations to phenomenologically relevant regimes.
