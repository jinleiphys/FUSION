# Non-equilibrium dilepton production in hadronic transport approaches

> In this work the non-equilibrium dilepton production from a hadronic transport approach (SMASH) is presented. The dilepton emission from the hadronic stage is of interest for current HADES results measured at GSI in the beam energy range from 1.25 - 3.5 GeV. Also at high collision energies (RHIC/LHC) the later dilute stages of the reaction are dominated by hadronic dynamics. The newly developed hadronic transport approach called SMASH (=Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) is introduced first. After explaining the basic interaction mechanisms, a comparison of elementary cross sections for pion production to experimental data is shown. The dilepton production within SMASH is explained in detail. The main contribution to the dilepton spectra in the low energy regime of GSI/FAIR/RHIC-BES originates from resonance decays. Results of the dilepton production with SMASH such as invariant mass spectra are shown.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first dilepton production results from the SMASH hadronic transport approach, showing that the main contribution to dilepton spectra in the low energy regime of GSI/FAIR/RHIC-BES originates from resonance decays. However, the results are only qualitative at this stage, as the authors state the spectra "match the expectations qualitatively" and that validation against experimental data from HADES is left as the next step.

## Method

The authors developed SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-Interacting Hadrons), a new hadronic transport approach that provides an effective numerical solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equation. All well-established hadrons from the Particle Data Group up to a mass of 2 GeV are included, with interactions limited to 1-to-2 or 2-to-2 processes to conserve detailed balance. Dilepton production is implemented via the Time-Integration-Method (Shining-Method), where decay probabilities for dilepton decays are integrated over the entire lifetime of the decaying resonance, allowing improved statistics for these rare electromagnetic decays with branching ratios of order 10^{-5}.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range of interest for HADES measurements at GSI: 1.25 - 3.5 GeV
- All hadrons from the PDG up to mass of 2 GeV are included in the model
- Typical branching ratios for dilepton decays are of order 10^{-5}
- Invariant mass spectrum of di-electrons shown for pp collisions at kinetic energy of 3.5 GeV
- Rapidity spectrum of di-electrons shown for pp collisions at kinetic energy of 3.5 GeV in a fixed-target setup
- Pion m_T spectra shown for Carbon-Carbon collisions at kinetic energy of 1 A GeV

## Context

This work extends the development of hadronic transport approaches for heavy-ion collisions, following the tradition of models like those described by Bass (1998). The SMASH approach is intended to provide a standard reference for describing hadronic systems with vacuum properties, and can be used as a standalone model for low-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions or as an afterburner for hydrodynamic simulations. The next step is to validate the dilepton yields by comparing with HADES data for low-energy proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions, and the approach can later be combined with hydrodynamic models in hybrid approaches to explore the behavior of electromagnetic radiation relative to transport approaches.
