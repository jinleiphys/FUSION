# Investigation of the Coupling Potential by means of S-matrix Inversion

> We investigate the inelastic coupling interaction by studying its effect on the elastic scattering potential as determined by inverting the elastic scattering $S$-matrix. We first address the effect upon the real and imaginary elastic potentials of including excited states of the target nucleus. We then investigate the effect of a recently introduced novel coupling potential which has been remarkably successful in reproducing the experimental data for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C, $^{12}$C+$^{24}$Mg and $^{16}$O+$^{28}$Si reactions over a wide range of energies. This coupling potential has the effect of deepening the real elastic potential in the surface region, thereby explaining a common feature of many phenomenological potentials. It is suggested that one can relate this deepening to the super-deformed state of the compound nucleus, $^{24}$Mg.

# Investigation of the Coupling Potential by means of S-matrix Inversion

## Key claim
The new coupling potential introduced by Boztosun and Rae produces a deepening of the real elastic potential in the surface region that is substantially greater than the added absorption, unlike conventional coupling where added attraction and absorption are comparable; this deepening may be related to the super-deformed state of the compound nucleus ²⁴Mg.

## Method
The authors performed coupled-channels (CC) calculations for the ¹²C+¹²C system at laboratory energies of 93.8 MeV and 126.7 MeV, using both conventional and new coupling potentials. They then applied the iterative perturbative (IP) S-matrix inversion method (code IMAGO) to extract the effective local elastic scattering potential from the elastic S-matrix, allowing identification of the dynamical polarization potential (DPP) induced by inelastic coupling.

## Key numbers
- At 126.7 MeV with conventional coupling including mutual excitation: ΔJ_R ≈ 35 MeV fm³ and ΔJ_I ≈ 35 MeV fm³ (added attraction and absorption nearly equal).
- At 126.7 MeV with the new coupling potential: ΔJ_R ≈ 35 MeV fm³ and ΔJ_I ≈ 15 MeV fm³ (added attraction much greater than added absorption).
- At 93.8 MeV with the new coupling potential (smooth solutions): ΔJ_R ≈ 38 MeV fm³ and ΔJ_I ≈ 28 MeV fm³.
- With conventional single-2⁺ coupling at 126.7 MeV: ΔJ_R ≈ 15 MeV fm³ and ΔJ_I ≈ 20 MeV fm³.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Boztosun and Rae (2001) who showed that standard CC calculations under-predicted mutual-2⁺ cross-sections in ¹²C+¹²C by a large factor, and that a new coupling potential resolved this problem for ¹²C+¹²C, ¹²C+²⁴Mg, and ¹⁶O+²⁸Si over a wide energy range. The inversion results confirm that inelastic coupling contributes substantially to the real potential (not just absorption, as often assumed), and the surface deepening explains the ad hoc potentials required in earlier folding-model analyses by Kobos and Satchler (1985) and Kobos, Satchler and Mackintosh (1982). The findings enable future studies of how inelastic processes modify the root-mean-square radius of the elastic potential, which is relevant for extracting nuclear size information from elastic scattering of unstable nuclei.
