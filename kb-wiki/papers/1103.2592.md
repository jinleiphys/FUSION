# Lambda(1405) and kaonic few-body states in chiral dynamics

> The present status of the Lambda(1405) structure study in chiral dynamics is briefly reviewed. It turns out that the Lambda(1405) resonance can be described by hadronic dynamics. The idea of the hadronic molecular state is extended to kaonic few-body states. It is concluded that, due to the fact that K and N have similar coupling nature in s-wave Kbar couplings, there are few-body quasibound states with kaons systematically just below the break-up thresholds, like KbarNN, KbarKN and KbarKK, as well as Lambda(1405) as a KbarN quasibound state and f_0(980) and a_0(980) as KbarK.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Lambda(1405) resonance is not a single state but is composed of two distinct pole states with different coupling natures, and that this hadronic molecular picture can be systematically extended to predict quasibound kaonic few-body states (KbarNN, KbarKN, KbarKK) just below their respective breakup thresholds.

## Method

The author reviews the chiral unitary model (coupled-channels approach based on chiral effective theory with s-channel unitarity) applied to s-wave meson-baryon scattering with strangeness S=-1. For the few-body systems, single-channel non-relativistic potential models and three-body Faddeev calculations (including coupled-channels) are used to predict binding energies and spatial properties of kaonic few-body states.

## Key numbers

- Two Lambda(1405) poles found in chiral dynamics: one at 1390 - 66i MeV (strong coupling to piSigma channel) and another at 1426 - 16i MeV (dominant coupling to KbarN channel)
- Electric radius of Lambda(1405) at the pole position: -0.157 + 0.238i fm^2, whose modulus is much larger than that of the neutron
- In the SU(3) flavor limit, two bound states appear with 70 MeV and 5 MeV binding energies in the flavor singlet and octet channels, respectively
- KbarNN system predicted with binding energy around 20 MeV in chiral dynamics
- KbarKN system found bound with 20 MeV binding energy
- KbarKK system: three-body Faddeev calculation found a resonance state at 1420 MeV; potential model suggested a quasibound state with binding energy 20 MeV
- Root mean-squared radii of KbarKN and KbarKK systems: approximately 1.7 fm, similar to the radius of He-4

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecular picture from the Lambda(1405) as a KbarN quasibound state (originally suggested by Nogami (1963) and Akaishi (2002)) to a systematic family of kaonic few-body states. The author contrasts the chiral dynamics approach with constituent quark models and with the approach of Akaishi (2002), where the Lambda(1405) position at 1405 MeV was an input rather than a consequence of scattering data. The predictions enable experimental searches at J-PARC and DAFNE, particularly for the KbarNN system and for confirming the double-pole structure via the K- d -> Lambda(1405) n reaction.
