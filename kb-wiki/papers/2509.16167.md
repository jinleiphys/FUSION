# Probing QGP using local charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

> We revisit the D-measure of event-by-event net-electric charge fluctuations, an idea first introduced over 20 years ago as a potential signature for the presence of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in heavy-ion collisions. We developed a quantitative framework that incorporates resonance-decay effects, global and local charge conservation, and experimental kinematic cuts. Folding these effects into a formalism of density correlations yields an improved expression for the $D$-measure inside acceptance. We make comparisons with ALICE data for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$~TeV. We find that a hadron-gas scenario can describe the data only for a very short charge conservation range, while a QGP scenario is in good agreement with the measurement and is relatively insensitive to the charge conservation range. A Bayesian comparison of both scenarios shows moderate evidence for freeze-out of charge fluctuations in the QGP phase.

# Probing QGP using local charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The D-measure of net-electric charge fluctuations in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV shows moderate Bayesian evidence (Bayes factor ~4.7–9.7) for freeze-out of charge fluctuations in the quark-gluon plasma phase rather than in a hadron gas, but only when local charge conservation effects are properly accounted for; a hadron gas scenario can describe the data only for a very short charge conservation range.

## Method
The authors developed a formalism that combines density correlations with a two-point correlator for local charge conservation, resonance decay effects, and a binomial acceptance model based on blast-wave fits to single-particle spectra. They computed the D-measure as a function of pseudorapidity cut for both a hadron gas scenario (ω ≈ 1.1 from HRG) and a QGP scenario (ω ≈ 0.36 from Stefan-Boltzmann susceptibilities and isentropic evolution), then performed a two-parameter Bayesian analysis varying ω and the local charge conservation volume ratio Vc/Vtot.

## Key numbers
- For the QGP scenario, ω = 0.36 ± 0.04, derived from χ₂^Q/T³ = 2/3, s/T³ = 19π²/9, S/⟨Nch⟩ = 6.7 ± 0.8, and γ_Q = 1.67.
- For the hadron gas scenario, ω ≈ 1.1 from HRG including Bose-Einstein statistics for pions and multi-charged hadrons.
- Bayes factor for QGP over HG under uniform priors: B ≈ 9.7; under a prior favoring local conservation (Vc/Vtot ~ N(0.20, 0.05²)): B ≈ 4.7.
- Local charge conservation parameter Vc/Vtot = 0.2 (σ_y = 0.78) used for representative local conservation curves.
- Data compared from ALICE Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV for 0-5% and 40-50% centrality.

## Context
This work revisits the D-measure originally proposed by Jeon and Koch (2000) and Asakawa and Heinz (2000) as a QGP signature, extending it beyond the grand-canonical ensemble estimates that gave D_HG ≈ 2.8–4 and D_QGP ≈ 1–1.5. The formalism incorporates local charge conservation effects from Vovchenko (2024) and uses blast-wave parameters from ALICE (2013). The results enable quantitative comparison with upcoming LHC Run 2 data and provide a framework for distinguishing QGP from hadron gas freeze-out scenarios using fluctuation observables.
