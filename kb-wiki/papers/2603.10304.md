# Effective theory of surface oscillations in self-bound superfluid droplets

> We investigate the low-energy dynamics of small-amplitude surface oscillations of spherical superfluid droplets in vacuum. Starting from the effective field theory of superfluid phonons, we derive an effective action governing the surface oscillations under a fixed particle-number constraint. The normal-mode eigenfrequencies $ω_{\ell}$ for each angular momentum quantum number $\ell$ are determined and shown to depend on a dimensionless parameter measuring the ratio of surface tension to bulk compressibility energy. We identify a critical value of this parameters at which the breathing mode ($\ell = 0$) becomes mechanically unstable, and show that all multipole surface modes with $\ell \geq 2$ enter the low-energy regime when the surface tension is sufficiently small. Within this regime, we further quantize the surface oscillations, whose quanta correspond to ripplons, allowing the construction of general multi-ripplon states obeying angular-momentum selection rules. We also apply our formalism to a concrete example: a weakly interacting two-component Bose mixture realizing a self-bound superfluid droplet. The resulting description is universal in the sense that it applies to surface dynamics of generic nonrelativistic superfluids with a free interface, independent of microscopic details.

## Key claim

The paper derives a universal effective field theory for small-amplitude surface oscillations of spherical superfluid droplets, showing that the normal-mode eigenfrequencies depend on a dimensionless parameter measuring the ratio of surface tension to bulk compressibility energy, with the caveat that the description applies only when the droplet is sufficiently large compared to the healing length near the surface.

## Method

Starting from the effective field theory of superfluid phonons with a free boundary, the authors derive an effective action governing surface oscillations under a fixed particle-number constraint, treating the chemical potential as a time-dependent Lagrange multiplier. They linearize the equations of motion around a static spherical background, integrate out the bulk superfluid phonons to obtain an effective theory for the surface displacement field, and then quantize the surface oscillations in the low-energy regime where surface tension is sufficiently small.

## Key numbers

- The breathing mode (ℓ = 0) becomes mechanically unstable at a critical value of the dimensionless parameter measuring the ratio of surface tension to bulk compressibility energy.
- All multipole surface modes with ℓ ≥ 2 enter the low-energy regime when the surface tension is sufficiently small.
- The sound velocity is given by c_s = √(n̄/(Mχ)), where n̄ is the background particle-number density, M is the mass of the microscopic constituent, and χ is the compressibility.
- The equilibrium radius R₀ satisfies the Young-Laplace equation p̄ = 2σ/R₀, where p̄ is the background pressure and σ is the surface tension.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory framework for superfluid interfaces previously developed for flat geometries by Watanabe (2014) to finite spherical droplets, and builds on the liquid-drop model descriptions of nuclear physics dating back to Bohr (1939) and the subsequent work of Bohr and Mottelson (1953, 1974). The formalism enables the construction of general multi-ripplon states obeying angular-momentum selection rules and applies to generic nonrelativistic superfluids with a free interface, including the recently realized self-bound quantum droplets in two-component Bose mixtures observed in experiments with ³⁹K by Cabrera (2018) and Semeghini (2018), and with ⁴¹K-⁸⁷Rb by D'Errico (2019) and Burchianti (2020).
