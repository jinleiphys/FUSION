# Precision Measurements of the 7Be(p,gamma)8B Reaction with Radioactive Beams and the 8B Solar Neutrino Flux

> The 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction is one of the major source of uncertainties in estimating the 8B solar neutrino flux and is critical for undertsanding the Solar Neutrino Problem and neutrinos. The main source of uncertainty is the existence of conflicting data with different absolute normalization. Attempts to measure this reaction rate with 7Be beams are under way by the UConn-LLN collaboration, and we discuss a newly emerging method to extract this cross section from the Coulomb dissociation of the radioactive beam of 8B. We discuss some of the issues relevant for this study including the question of the E2 contribution to the Coulomb dissociation process which was measured to be small. The Coulomb dissociation appears to provide a viable alternative method for measuring the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction rate, with a weighted average of the RIKEN1, RIKEN2 and GSI1 published results of S17(0) = 19.4 +/- 1.3 eV-b.

# Precision Measurements of the 7Be(p,gamma)8B Reaction with Radioactive Beams and the 8B Solar Neutrino Flux

## Key claim
The Coulomb dissociation method provides a viable alternative for measuring the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction rate, yielding a weighted average of S17(0) = 19.4 ± 1.3 eV-b from RIKEN1, RIKEN2, and GSI1 results, but the accuracy is limited by understanding of the Coulomb dissociation process itself, believed to be approximately ±10%.

## Method
The authors analyzed Coulomb dissociation of 8B beams on 208Pb targets at two energies: 51.9 A MeV (RIKEN2) and 254 A MeV (GSI). At GSI, a 8B beam produced by fragmentation of a 350 A MeV 12C beam on beryllium was incident on a 199.7 mg/cm2 208Pb target, with breakup products (7Be and proton) detected in the KaoS magnetic spectrometer using silicon micro-strip detectors for track reconstruction. The UConn-LLN collaboration proposed a complementary direct measurement using a 7Be radioactive beam on a hydrogen target at Louvain-La-Neuve, measuring the 7Be(p,gamma)8B cross section relative to elastic scattering to remove systematic uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- Weighted average S17(0) from Coulomb dissociation experiments: 19.4 ± 1.3 eV-b
- RIKEN1 result: S17(0) = 16.9 ± 3.2 eV-b
- RIKEN2 result: S17(0) = 18.9 ± 1.8 eV-b
- GSI1 result: S17(0) = 20.6 ± 1.2 ± 1.0 eV-b
- Upper limit on SE2/SE1 at 0.6 MeV from GSI: less than 3 × 10^-5 (3-sigma)
- Upper limits on SE2/SE1 from GSI at specific relative energy bins: 0.06 × 10^-4 for 0.3-0.5 MeV, 0.3 × 10^-4 for 0.5-0.7 MeV, and 0.6 × 10^-4 for 1.0-1.2 MeV
- MSU result for SE2/SE1 at 0.63 MeV: 6.7 +2.8 -1.9 × 10^-4
- Expected count rates for UConn-LLN direct measurement: approximately 5, 4, and 2 counts per hour at Ecm = 1.0, 0.8, and 0.5 MeV respectively, with cross sections of 0.5, 0.4, and 0.2 microbarn

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy in the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction rate, where six precise direct measurements (Parker 1968, Kavanagh 1969, Vaughn 1970, Filippone 1983, Hammache 1998, Ha 1999) disagree in absolute normalization by about 30%. The Coulomb dissociation results are consistent with the lower measured values and provide an independent cross-check. The small E2 component measured at both RIKEN and GSI (less than 3 × 10^-5) contradicts the larger E2 amplitude claimed by Davids et al. from MSU, and supports the earlier upper limits from Gai and Bertulani (1994). The proposed UConn-LLN direct measurement with a 7Be beam aims to achieve 5% precision at each data point, testing extrapolation procedures that currently rely on theoretical models such as those by Johnson et al. (1992) and Jennings et al. (1998).
