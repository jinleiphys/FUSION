# QCD: Restoration of Chiral Symmetry and Deconfinement for Large N_f

> Exploiting the recent lattice results for the infrared gluon propagator with light dynamical quarks, we solve the gap equation for the quark propagator. We thus model the chiral symmetry breaking mechanism with increasing number of flavours and study confinement (intimately tied with the analytic properties of QCD Schwinger functions) order parameters. We obtain, with this approach, clear signals of chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement when the number of light quark flavors exceeds a critical value of $N_f^c \approx 8 \pm 1$, in agreement with the state-of-the-art direct lattice analysis of chiral symmetry restoration in QCD.

## Key claim
Using a Schwinger-Dyson equation analysis combined with lattice gluon propagator data for light dynamical quarks, the authors find clear signals of chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement when the number of light quark flavors exceeds a critical value of \(N_f^c \approx 8 \pm 1\). The abstract's claim of agreement with direct lattice analysis is hedged by the fact that the two extrapolation schemes (linear and exponential) yield different critical values (\(N_f^c \approx 7.1\) and \(N_f^c \approx 9.4\) respectively), and the final quoted value \(8.2 \pm 1.2\) is an average with uncertainty.

## Method
The authors solve the quark gap equation (Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quark propagator) using a model for the gluon propagator whose flavor dependence is extracted by fitting lattice data for \(N_f = 0, 2, 2+1+1\) (effectively 3 light flavors) from the ETM collaboration. The gluon propagator is parametrized by the Refined Gribov-Zwanziger (RGZ) form, with flavor-dependent parameters \(m_0\) and the dimension-two gluon condensate \(\langle A^2 \rangle\) extrapolated to higher \(N_f\) via either a linear or exponential scaling law. The quark-gluon vertex is replaced by an effective coupling \(g_{\rm eff}(q^2)\) that is taken to be flavor-independent in the infrared, following Maris and Tandy (1998). Order parameters for chiral symmetry (Euclidean pole mass, quark condensate, pion decay constant) and confinement (oscillation onset in the spatially averaged Schwinger function \(\Delta(t)\)) are computed as functions of \(N_f\).

## Key numbers
- Critical number of light flavors for chiral symmetry restoration: \(N_f^c \approx 7.1\) (linear extrapolation) and \(N_f^c \approx 9.4\) (exponential extrapolation); combined result \(N_f^c \approx 8.2 \pm 1.2\).
- Gluon mass parameter at \(N_f=0\): \(m_0(0) = 0.333\) GeV; dimension-two gluon condensate at \(N_f=0\): \(g^2 \langle A^2 \rangle(0) = 7.856\).
- Linear scaling parameters: \(A = 0.083\), \(B = 0.080\); exponential scaling parameters: \(A = 0.095\), \(B = 0.091\).
- RGZ mass parameter \(M^2 = 4.85\) GeV\(^2\) (flavor-independent).
- Lattice data for \(N_f=2+1+1\) correspond effectively to 3 light flavors, as confirmed by the partially unquenched approach of Aguilar et al. (2012).
- The phase transition is second order, with mean-field scaling \(m_{\rm dyn} \sim \sqrt{N_f^{c_2} - N_f}\).

## Context
This work extends the Schwinger-Dyson approach to chiral symmetry breaking by incorporating modern lattice gluon propagator data with dynamical quarks from the ETM collaboration (Baron et al., 2010, 2011; Blossier et al., 2010, 2011; Ayala et al., 2012). It directly addresses the question of a conformal window in QCD, previously studied via lattice simulations by Iwasaki et al. (2012) and Aoki et al. (2013), who found evidence that \(N_f=8\) QCD still exhibits broken chiral symmetry but with a large anomalous dimension indicating proximity to the conformal window. The results enable future studies incorporating a flavor-dependent quark-gluon vertex and solving the coupled system of Green functions simultaneously.
