# Elastic Electron Scattering off Exotic Nuclei

> Electron scattering will provide the most direct information about charge distribution in exotic nuclei. A technical proposal for an electron-heavy-ion collider has been incorporated in the GSI/Germany physics program (spokesperson: Haik Simon - GSI). The main purpose is to study the structure of nuclei far from the stability line. In this work I explore the possibilities to study charge distributions of nuclei far form the stability line with the proposed facility.

## Key claim

Elastic electron scattering at a proposed electron-heavy-ion collider can directly probe charge distributions of exotic nuclei far from stability, but the practical extraction of detailed charge-density profiles will be limited by the finite range of momentum transfer accessible in experiments (likely up to about 1 fm\(^{-1}\)), and the inverse scattering problem requires careful basis-function expansions to avoid introducing spurious oscillations.

## Method

The author uses plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA) and distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) formalisms to relate elastic electron scattering cross sections to nuclear charge form factors and charge densities. For specific cases, Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with Skyrme interactions (BKN and SLy4) are performed to generate synthetic charge densities for halo nuclei such as \(^{11}\)Li and \(^{8}\)B, and the resulting form factors are analyzed. The inverse scattering problem is addressed using Fourier-Bessel and Laguerre-Gauss basis expansions to reconstruct charge densities from limited momentum-transfer data.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{132}\)Sn, a luminosity larger than \(10^{26}\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\) is required to measure the charge form factor up to the first maximum within one week.
- For \(^{100}\)Sn, the first minimum of the form factor is expected at \(q_1 = 0.806\) fm\(^{-1}\) (159 MeV/c); for \(^{132}\)Sn, at \(q_1 = 0.754\) fm\(^{-1}\) (149 MeV/c).
- For \(^{53}\)Ca, the first minimum occurs at \(q_1 = 1.11\) fm\(^{-1}\); to achieve \(\delta\langle r_p^2\rangle^{1/2} < 0.05\) fm, the experimental resolution on \(q_1\) must be within 1\%.
- The neutron skin of calcium isotopes varies from \(-0.15\) fm for \(^{35}\)Ca (proton-rich) to \(0.25\) fm for \(^{53}\)Ca, based on the fitted formula \(\Delta r_{np} = (-0.04 \pm 0.03) + (1.01 \pm 0.15)\delta\) fm from antiprotonic atom data (Trz01).
- For \(^{8}\)B, the width of the charge form factor squared is \(\Delta_{ch} = 0.505\) fm\(^{-1} = 99.6\) MeV/c, compared to \(\Delta_{tot} = 0.545\) fm\(^{-1} = 108\) MeV/c for the total matter distribution (about 10\% difference).
- The calculated rms charge radius of \(^{8}\)B is \(\langle r_{ch}^2\rangle^{1/2} = 2.82\) fm, close to the experimental value \(2.76 \pm 0.08\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for using electron scattering to study exotic nuclei, building on the pioneering electron scattering experiments of Hofstadter (1961) and the discovery of halo nuclei such as \(^{11}\)Li by Tanihata (1985). The paper directly supports the technical proposal for an electron-heavy-ion collider at GSI (Haik Simon, 2005) and a similar program at RIKEN (Sud01). It enables future experiments to determine charge radii and neutron skins of unstable nuclei, complementing existing techniques such as interaction cross-section measurements (Tanihata, 1985) and isotope-shift measurements (OST01), and provides guidance on the experimental resolution needed to distinguish theoretical models of nuclear structure.
