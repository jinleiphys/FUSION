# Exploring Baryon Chiral Multiplets

> The full QCD chiral symmetry algebra has predictive consequences at low energies. I discuss the ground-state chiral multiplet involving the light baryons and emphasize the special role of the Roper resonance.

# Exploring Baryon Chiral Multiplets

## Key claim

The nucleon, Delta resonance, and Roper resonance appear to form a reducible chiral multiplet, specifically the sum of (1,2) and (2,3) representations of SU(2)×SU(2), with maximal mixing, but this interpretation relies on pole saturation of the Adler-Weisberger sum rule and neglect of continuum contributions, which the author acknowledges makes the remarkable agreement with experiment "to some degree fortuitous."

## Method

The author analyzes the Adler-Weisberger sum rule for pion-nucleon scattering by saturating it with established N and Delta resonances from the Particle Data Group, computing each resonance's contribution from experimental widths. The sum rule is then reinterpreted algebraically using the chiral symmetry algebra in a helicity-conserving frame, where the axial-vector coupling matrix elements satisfy commutation relations equivalent to generalized Adler-Weisberger sum rules. A reducible chiral representation (2,3)⊕(1,2) is constructed using tensor analysis, and the mass-squared matrix is constrained by assuming superconvergence relations and the absence of inelastic diffractive scattering.

## Key numbers

- The Adler-Weisberger sum rule yields g_A = 1.26 from resonance saturation, compared to the experimental value 1.2670 ± 0.0035.
- The Delta(1232) contributes I_R = 1.02 and the Roper N(1440) contributes I_R = 0.23 to the sum rule; saturating with only these two states gives g_A = 1.34.
- With maximal mixing (ψ = π/4), the predicted axial couplings are: |g_A| = 4/3, |g'_A| = 1/3, |g''_A| = 4/3, |C_{ΔN}| = √2, |C_{ΔN'}| = √2, |H_{ΔΔ}| = 3.
- Using the nucleon and Delta masses as input, the maximal-mixing condition M_Δ² − M_N² = M_{N'}² − M_Δ² predicts M_{N'} = 1467 MeV.
- Experimental values from PDG: |g_A| = 1.26, |C_{ΔN}| = 1.2 ± 0.1, |H_{ΔΔ}| = 2.2 ± 0.6, |g'_A| = 0.71 ± 0.20, |C_{ΔN'}| = 1.38 ± 0.50.

## Context

This work extends the chiral symmetry analysis of Weinberg (Weinberg, 1966) and the earlier Adler-Weisberger sum rule (Adler, 1965) by identifying the specific reducible chiral multiplet containing the nucleon, Delta, and Roper. It contradicts the naive constituent quark model prediction that g_A = 5/3 and that the Roper has zero axial coupling to the nucleon (g'_A = 0). The results enable future lattice QCD and experimental studies at JLab to map out chiral multiplets for higher baryon excitations, though the author notes that pole saturation becomes unreliable for higher states due to resonance broadening.
