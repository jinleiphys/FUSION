# Dynamical calculation of $d^*$ mass and NN decay width in the quark delocalization, color screening model

> The mass estimate of the $d^* (IJ^P=03^+)$ dibaryon is improved by a dynamical calculation in the quark delocalization, color screening model. The partial decay width of $d^*$ into an $NN$ D-wave state is also obtained. The mass obtained is slightly larger than that obtained in adiabatic calculations, due to the anharmonicity of the effective potential between two $Δ$'s. The value of the width obtained due to tensor one-gluon-exchange is about 5 MeV, comparable in magnitude to earlier results found using pion exchange.

## Key claim

The mass of the $d^* (IJ^P=03^+)$ dibaryon obtained from a dynamical calculation in the quark delocalization, color screening model is slightly larger than that from earlier adiabatic calculations, rising above the $NN\pi\pi$ threshold, but the state should remain a narrow resonance due to extremely small phase space.

## Method

The authors used the resonating group method (RGM) with generator coordinates to solve the six-quark Hamiltonian in the quark delocalization, color screening model (QDCSM). They performed single-channel ($\Delta\Delta$ S-wave), two-channel ($\Delta\Delta$ S-wave + $NN$ D-wave), and full four-channel coupling ($\Delta\Delta$ S=3 L=0, $\Delta\Delta$ S=3 L=2, $NN$ S=1 L=2, $\Delta\Delta$ S=1 L=2) calculations. The partial decay width for $d^* \rightarrow NN$ D-wave was computed using Fermi's Golden Rule with the tensor one-gluon-exchange interaction as the transition operator, employing a box normalization method to handle the scattering state.

## Key numbers

- $d^*$ mass in full four-channel coupling: 2176 MeV, compared to 2134 MeV from the earlier adiabatic calculation
- $d^*$ root-mean-square radius in full four-channel coupling: 1.3 fm
- Partial decay width $\Gamma$ for $d^* \rightarrow NN$ D-wave: 4.32 MeV total, with stretched ($m_J = \pm 3$) states contributing 1.95 MeV each
- Model parameters: $m_u=m_d=313$ MeV, $b=0.603$ fm, $a=25.13$ MeV/fm$^2$, $\alpha_s=1.54$, $\mu=1.0$ fm$^{-2}$

## Context

This work extends earlier adiabatic calculations of the $d^*$ dibaryon in the QDCSM by Ping, Wang, and Goldman (1999) and provides a dynamical treatment that yields a higher mass. The calculated width of about 4.32 MeV from tensor one-gluon-exchange is comparable in magnitude to earlier results found using pion exchange by the same group. The authors note that the deuteron cannot be reproduced without including one-pion exchange, which they plan to add in future work, but argue that the compactness of the $d^*$ limits the effect of pion exchange on this state. The results are compared to experimental limits from Lisowski (1982) on $np$ total cross sections.
