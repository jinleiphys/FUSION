# Hybrid stars in the light of the massive pulsar PSR J1614-2230

> We perform a systematic study of hybrid star configurations using several parametrizations of a relativistic mean-field hadronic EoS and the NJL model for three-flavor quark matter. For the hadronic phase we use the stiff GM1 and TM1 parametrizations, as well as the very stiff NL3 model. In the NJL Lagrangian we include scalar, vector and 't Hooft interactions. The vector coupling constant $g_v$ is treated as a free parameter. We also consider that there is a split between the deconfinement and the chiral phase transitions which is controlled by changing the conventional value of the vacuum pressure $- Ω_0$ in the NJL thermodynamic potential by $- (Ω_0 + δΩ_0)$, being $δΩ_0$ a free parameter. We find that, as we increase the value of $δΩ_0$, hybrid stars have a larger maximum mass but are less stable, i.e. hybrid configurations are stable within a smaller range of central densities. For large enough $δΩ_0$, stable hybrid configurations are not possible at all. The effect of increasing the coupling constant $g_v$ is very similar. We show that stable hybrid configurations with a maximum mass larger than the observed mass of the pulsar PSR J1614-2230 are possible for a large region of the parameter space of $g_v$ and $δΩ_0$ provided the hadronic equation of state contains nucleons only. When the baryon octet is included in the hadronic phase, only a very small region of the parameter space allows to explain the mass of PSR J1614-2230. We compare our results with previous calculations of hybrid stars within the NJL model. We show that it is possible to obtain stable hybrid configurations also in the case $δΩ_0=0$ that corresponds to the conventional NJL model for which the pressure and density vanish at zero temperature and chemical potential.

# Hybrid stars in the light of the massive pulsar PSR J1614-2230

## Key claim

Stable hybrid star configurations with maximum masses equal to or larger than the observed mass of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses) are possible for a significant region of the parameter space of the vector coupling constant g_v and the vacuum pressure shift δΩ₀, provided a stiff enough hadronic equation of state without hyperons is used; however, when the baryon octet is included in the hadronic phase, only a very small region of the parameter space allows to explain the pulsar mass.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic study of hybrid star configurations using a Maxwell construction to match a relativistic mean-field hadronic equation of state (with GM1, TM1, and NL3 parametrizations) to a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) quark matter model including scalar, vector, and 't Hooft interactions. The vector coupling constant g_v and a parameter δΩ₀ controlling the split between deconfinement and chiral phase transitions were treated as free parameters, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved for spherically symmetric static stars to determine maximum masses and stability.

## Key numbers

- The observed mass of pulsar PSR J1614-2230 is 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses (Demorest 2010).
- Pure hadronic star maximum masses for the three parametrizations (nucleons only): GM1 gives 2.32 solar masses, TM1 gives 2.18 solar masses, NL3 gives 2.73 solar masses.
- The conventional NJL model vacuum pressure Ω₀ is 5076.2 MeV fm⁻³; typical departures δΩ₀/Ω₀ ∼ 0.1% are sufficient to obtain massive enough stable hybrid stars.
- The NJL model parameters used: Λ = 631.4 MeV, g_sΛ² = 1.829, g_tΛ⁵ = -9.4, m_u = m_d = 5.6 MeV, m_s = 135.6 MeV, fitting vacuum values m_π = 139.0 MeV, f_π = 93.0 MeV, m_K = 495.7 MeV, f_K = 98.9 MeV, ⟨uū⟩¹/³ = ⟨dđ⟩¹/³ = -246.7 MeV, ⟨sš⟩¹/³ = -266.9 MeV.
- The nuclear saturation density ρ₀ is taken as 0.17 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of hybrid stars using the NJL model, contrasting with earlier studies by Schertler (1999), Baldo (2007), and Benhar (2011) who used softer parametrizations and found no stable hybrid configurations. It is closely related to Bonanno (2012), who treated the deconfinement density as a free parameter and obtained high-mass hybrid stars with color-superconducting cores, and to Pagliara (2008), who fixed the bag constant by requiring deconfinement at the chiral transition chemical potential. The present study uses the stiffer NJL parametrization of Kunihiro (1989) and Ruivo (1999) rather than the softer Rehberg (1996) parametrization, and does not include color superconductivity, focusing instead on the weak diquark coupling regime. The results enable future comparisons with observations of neutron star masses a few percent larger than PSR J1614-2230, which would require very stiff hadronic models such as NL3 with nucleons only.
