# Electromagnetic corrections for the analysis of low energy pi-p scattering data

> We calculate the electromagnetic corrections to the isospin invariant mixing angle and to the two eigenphases for the s and p-waves for low energy pi-p elastic and charge exchange scattering. These corrections have to be applied to the nuclear quantities obtained from phase shift analyses of the experimental data in order to obtain the hadronic phases. We compare our results with earlier calculations and estimate the uncertainties in the corrections.

## Key claim

The paper calculates electromagnetic corrections to hadronic phase shifts for low-energy pi-p elastic and charge exchange scattering, but these corrections rely on the assumption of isospin invariance at the effective hadronic level and energy-independent hadronic potentials, an assumption that yields a statistically poor but just acceptable fit to existing data, and which the authors note does not constitute evidence for the assumption itself.

## Method

The authors use relativised Schrodinger equations (RSEs) containing the sum of an electromagnetic potential and an effective hadronic potential for the two-channel (pi-p, pi0 n) system, with the T=3/2 hadronic phase shifts fixed from a prior pi+ p analysis. The electromagnetic correction matrices t^em_{l±} are obtained by adding electromagnetic potential matrices to isospin-invariant hadronic potential matrices, solving coupled RSEs, and extracting the corrections C1, C3, and Δφ to the hadronic eigenphases and mixing angle for s-, p1/2-, and p3/2-waves at pion lab kinetic energies from 10 to 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior calculation of electromagnetic corrections for pi+ p scattering (Gashi et al., 2000) to the two-channel pi-p/pi0 n system, and compares results with earlier calculations by the NORDITA group (1970s) and Zimmermann (1980). The corrections are necessary to extract hadronic phase shifts from experimental data, and the results enable future phase-shift analyses of low-energy pi± p scattering data and comparison with pionic hydrogen 1s level measurements to test isospin invariance beyond electromagnetic effects.
