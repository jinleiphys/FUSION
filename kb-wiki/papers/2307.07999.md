# Heavy quark $κ$ and jet $\hat{q}$ transport coefficients in the Glasma early stage of heavy-ion collisions

> We study the impact of the Glasma fields, used to describe the very early stage of heavy-ion collisions, on the transport of hard probes, namely heavy quarks and jets. We perform numerical simulations of the strong classical fields using techniques from real-time lattice gauge theory. The resulting fields are used as background for the classical transport of ensembles of particles, described by Wong's equations. For this purpose, we develop a numerical solver for the transport of the probes, based on colored particle-in-cell methods. We focus on the dynamics of heavy quarks and jets in the classical colored fields. To quantify the effect of the Glasma, we extract the momentum broadening of hard probes and evaluate the anisotropy transfer from the Glasma to the probes. Lastly, we evaluate the heavy quark $κ$ and jet $\hat{q}$ transport coefficients in the Glasma, which turn out to be large and exhibit a peak, irrespective of the particle initialization.

## Key claim

The heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient κ and jet transport coefficient q̂ in the Glasma are large and exhibit a sharp peak at very early times (around 0.03-0.05 fm/c), but this behavior depends on the details of particle initialization such as mass, formation time, and initial momentum, and differs from the limiting cases of static or lightlike probes.

## Method

The authors performed real-time lattice gauge theory simulations of classical Yang-Mills fields in SU(3) to generate Glasma background fields, then solved Wong's equations for ensembles of colored particles using a colored particle-in-cell method. Heavy quarks were initialized with formation time proportional to inverse mass and given transverse momenta, while jets were initialized at zero formation time with longitudinal momenta. The momentum broadening was extracted from particle trajectories and differentiated with respect to proper time to obtain the instantaneous transport coefficients κ and q̂.

## Key numbers

- Saturation momentum of the Glasma: Q_s = 2 GeV
- Beauty quark mass: m_beauty ≈ 4.2 GeV
- Heavy quark κ_peak ≈ 19-22 GeV²/fm, located at δτ_peak ≈ 0.03-0.05 fm/c
- Jet q̂_peak ≈ 24-30 GeV²/fm, located at τ_peak ≈ 0.04 fm/c
- Heavy quark initial p_T(τ_form) values explored: 0, 2, 5, 10 GeV
- Jet initial p_x(τ_form) values explored: 1, 2, 5, 10 GeV
- Typical Glasma stage duration: δτ_Glasma ≈ 0.3 fm/c

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy quark diffusion in the Glasma by Ruggieri (2018) and Sun (2019), and lattice extractions of q̂ from Wilson loops by Ipp (2020). It surpasses approximations in those works by using full SU(3) lattice gauge theory for the Glasma and implementing complete particle dynamics via Wong's equations rather than relying solely on field correlators. The results complement findings by Boguslavski (2020) on static heavy quark transport in over-occupied gluon plasmas and by Carrington (2021, 2022) on analytical studies of transport coefficients. The large early-time transport coefficients in the Glasma bridge to smaller values from subsequent hydrodynamic stages as studied by Boguslavski (2023) using effective kinetic theory.
