# Strangeness in Quark Matter 2013: Opening Talk

> We discuss several new developments in the field of strange and heavy flavor physics in high energy heavy ion collisions. As shown by many recent theoretical works, heavy flavored particles give us a unique opportunity to study the properties of systems created in these collisions. Two in particular important aspects, the production of (multi) strange hypernuclei and the properties of heavy flavor mesons, are at the core of several future facilities and will be discussed in detail.

# Strangeness in Quark Matter 2013: Opening Talk

## Key claim
The production of (multi)strange hypernuclei and heavy flavor mesons are two key topics at the core of future facilities such as FAIR with CBM and NICA, and the authors present model comparisons suggesting that large and multi-strange nuclear clusters exhibit a production maximum at a lower beam energy of 10 A GeV, making these facilities well suited for their search.

## Method
The authors use two complementary approaches to estimate hypernuclei and di-baryon yields: a hadronic transport model (DCM) with a coalescence prescription, and a thermal production model from a fluid dynamical description (UrQMD hybrid model). For heavy quark transport, they employ a hybrid model combining UrQMD with (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics and a relativistic Langevin approach for heavy-quark propagation, using different drag and diffusion coefficients to compare with experimental data from RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers
- Production maximum for large and multi-strange nuclear clusters occurs at a lower beam energy of E_lab = 10 A GeV
- For hyperon absorption calculations, the effective potential uses parameters alpha = 57.5 MeV and beta = 0.522
- Peripheral collisions simulated at impact parameter b = 8.5 fm
- Central collisions defined as b < 3.4 fm
- Elliptic flow v_2 and nuclear modification factor R_AA calculated for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, with rapidity cut |y| < 0.35
- Both DCM and UrQMD transport models predict spectator residuals with up to 3 absorbed Lambda hyperons

## Context
This work extends prior studies on strange particle production as a signal for deconfinement (Rafelski 1982, Koch 1986) and hypernuclei formation in heavy ion collisions (Ko 1985, Botvina 2011). The authors build on recent lattice QCD calculations suggesting the H-dibaryon may be weakly bound or a resonant state (Beane 2011, Inoue 2010), and address the open puzzle of enhanced Xi- yields at HADES (Agakishiev 2010). The results enable future searches for exotic hypernuclei at FAIR and NICA, and provide a framework for extracting transport properties of the QCD medium from heavy quark observables.
