# Two particle-two hole final states in quasi elastic neutrino-nucleus interactions

> The excitation of two particle-two hole final states in neutrino-nucleus scattering, not taken into account in Monte Carlo simulations for data analysis, has been advocated by many authors as the source of the excess cross section observed by the MiniBooNE collaboration in the quasi elastic channel. We analyse the mechanisms leading to the appearance of two particle-two hole states, and show that interference between the amplitudes involving one- and two-nucleon currents, not consistently included in existing calculations, plays a major role. A novel approach allowing to treat one- and two-nucleon current contributions on the same footing is outlined.

## Key claim

Interference between amplitudes involving one- and two-nucleon currents, not consistently included in existing calculations based on the Independent Particle Model, plays a major role in the excitation of two particle-two hole final states in neutrino-nucleus scattering, and may explain the excess cross section observed by MiniBooNE in the quasi elastic channel.

## Method

The authors analyze the mechanisms leading to two particle-two hole (2p2h) final states: Initial State Correlations among nucleons in the target ground state, Final State Correlations between the struck nucleon and spectator particles, and interactions involving two-nucleon meson-exchange currents. They employ the Green's Function Monte Carlo computational scheme to calculate sum rules of the electromagnetic response of carbon, isolating the contribution of interference terms between correlations and meson-exchange currents. They also outline a novel approach extending the factorization ansatz of the impulse approximation to treat one- and two-nucleon current contributions on the same footing, using two-nucleon spectral functions.

## Key numbers

- At momentum transfer |q| ≥ 300 MeV, the contribution of interference terms to the transverse sum rule of carbon is comparable to, in fact even larger than, that obtained from squaring the matrix element of the two-nucleon current.
- The mean momentum transfer of charged current quasi elastic events obtained by averaging over the MiniBooNE neutrino flux is ~640 MeV; for the Minerνa flux it is ~880 MeV.
- The electron-carbon cross section at beam energy E_e = 730 MeV and scattering angle θ_e = 37 deg is shown, with the 2p2h contribution from Initial State Correlations isolated.
- The relative momentum distribution of a nucleon pair in isospin symmetric nuclear matter at equilibrium density shows a quenching of the peak and a high momentum tail compared to the Fermi Gas model prediction.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of 2p2h final states beyond the Independent Particle Model calculations of Martini (2011), Coletti (2012), and Nieves (2012), which do not consistently include interference between one- and two-nucleon currents. The results demonstrate that a consistent treatment of nucleon-nucleon correlations and meson-exchange currents is needed, building on the Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations of Lovato (2012) for carbon sum rules. The proposed factorization scheme using two-nucleon spectral functions, previously calculated for nuclear matter by the authors of Ref. spec2, provides a path toward a unified treatment applicable at the large momentum transfers relevant to MiniBooNE and Minerνa analyses.
