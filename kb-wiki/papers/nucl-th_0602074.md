# Sigma exchange in the nuclear force and effective field theory

> In the phenomenological description of the nuclear interaction a crucial role is traditionally played by the exchange of a scalar I=0 meson, the sigma, of mass 500-600 MeV, which however is not seen clearly in the particle spectrum and which has a very ambiguous status in QCD. I show that a remarkably simple and reasonably controlled combination of ingredients can reproduce the features of this part of the nuclear force. The use of chiral perturbation theory calculations for two pion exchange supplemented by the Omnes function for pion rescattering suffices to reproduce the magnitude and shape of the exchange of a supposed $σ$ particle, even though no such particle is present in this calculation. I also show how these ingredients can describe the contact interaction that enters more modern descriptions of the internucleon interaction.

## Key claim
Chiral perturbation theory calculations for two-pion exchange, when supplemented by the Omnes function for pion rescattering, reproduce the magnitude and shape of the scalar isoscalar central nuclear force traditionally attributed to sigma meson exchange, even though no sigma resonance is present in the calculation. The author shows that this description can also account for the contact interaction used in modern effective field theory treatments of the internucleon interaction.

## Method
The author computed the spectral function for the scalar isoscalar channel using chiral perturbation theory diagrams for two-pion exchange (diagrams a, b, c), then multiplied the contributions by the Omnes function constructed from the I=0, J=0 pi-pi scattering phase shifts from Colangelo et al. (CGL). The resulting spectral function was used to construct the coordinate-space potential via a dispersion integral, and the strength of the contact interaction G_S was obtained by evaluating the momentum-space potential at q^2=0.

## Key numbers
- The sigma meson traditionally invoked in nuclear force phenomenology has a mass of 500-600 MeV.
- The phenomenological contact interaction strength G_S required by nuclear studies is 300-450 MeV^-2.
- The parameter c_3 extracted from pion-nucleon data is c_3 = -4.7 (+1.2/-1.0) GeV^-2; when using the Omnes representation the constraint becomes c_3 = -3.7 (+1.0/-0.8) GeV^-2.
- Other parameters used: c_1 = -0.64 GeV^-2, c_2 = 3.3 GeV^-2.
- With a form factor modification (n=1, m=800 MeV), the value of G_S is 40% smaller than without it for a given c_3.
- The elastic region for the I=0, J=0 pi-pi channel extends effectively up to 1000 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the program of understanding nuclear forces from QCD, addressing the long-standing puzzle of the sigma meson's ambiguous status in the particle spectrum. It builds on chiral perturbation theory calculations of two-pion exchange (NNLO, NNNLO) and the dispersion relation approach of Brown and Ericson, while using the Omnes function method previously applied to gamma-gamma to pi-pi scattering by Donoghue, Holstein, and Lin. The results enable controlled studies of the quark mass dependence of nuclear binding and strengthen the connection between nuclear interactions and QCD.
