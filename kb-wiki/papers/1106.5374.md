# The nucleon axial mass and the MiniBooNE Quasielastic Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering problem

> The charged-current double differential neutrino cross section, measured by the MiniBooNE Collaboration, has been analyzed using a microscopical model that accounts for, among other nuclear effects, long range nuclear (RPA) correlations and multinucleon scattering. We find that MiniBooNE data are fully compatible with the world average of the nucleon axial mass in contrast with several previous analyses which have suggested an anomalously large value. We also discuss the reliability of the algorithm used to estimate the neutrino energy.

## Key claim

MiniBooNE charged-current quasielastic data are fully compatible with the world-average nucleon axial mass (M_A ≈ 1.03 GeV) once long-range nuclear correlations (RPA) and multinucleon mechanisms are included in the theoretical model, contradicting earlier analyses that extracted M_A ≈ 1.35 GeV from a simple relativistic Fermi gas fit.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic local Fermi gas model of the carbon nucleus that incorporates RPA correlations (including Δ-hole degrees of freedom and π/ρ exchange) and multinucleon knockout mechanisms (two- and three-nucleon excitations, Δ-isobar contributions) with no free parameters for nuclear effects, all fixed by prior electron, photon, and pion scattering studies. They fitted the MiniBooNE double differential cross section d²σ/dT_μ dcosθ_μ (137 angle-energy bins) using a χ² minimization that includes a global normalization scale λ with its experimental uncertainty (Δλ = 0.107), treating the axial mass M_A as a free parameter in some fits and fixing it to 1.049 GeV in others.

## Key numbers

- World-average axial mass M_A = 1.03 ± 0.02 GeV (from deuterium bubble chamber and pion electroproduction data)
- MiniBooNE simple Fermi gas fit (no RPA, no multinucleon): M_A = 1.32 ± 0.03 GeV, λ = 0.96 ± 0.03, χ² = 35/137 bins
- Full model with M_A fixed to 1.049 GeV: χ² = 52/137 bins, λ = 0.89 ± 0.01
- Full model fit with M_A free: M_A = 1.077 ± 0.027 GeV, λ = 0.917 ± 0.029, χ² = 50/137 bins
- Full model with momentum transfer cut q_cut = 400 MeV (removing 14 bins): M_A = 1.007 ± 0.034 GeV, λ = 0.83 ± 0.04, χ² = 30/123 bins
- MiniBooNE flux mean neutrino energy ⟨E_ν⟩ ≈ 800 MeV

## Context

This work extends the microscopic nuclear model of Nieves (2004, 2005, 2011), previously successful for electron, photon, and pion scattering, to the MiniBooNE double differential cross section, resolving the tension between the MiniBooNE data and the world-average axial mass. It supports the earlier findings of Martini (2009, 2010) and Amaro (2010) that multinucleon mechanisms contribute significantly to the quasielastic signal, and contradicts the large-M_A conclusions of Benhar (2009, 2010), Juszczak (2010), and Butkevich (2010). The results enable more reliable neutrino energy reconstruction for oscillation experiments and demonstrate that sophisticated nuclear models are required for precision neutrino physics.
