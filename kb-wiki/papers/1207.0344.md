# Chromoelectric oscillations in a dynamically evolving anisotropic background

> We study the oscillations of a uniform longitudinal chromoelectric field in a dynamically-evolving momentum-space anisotropic background in the weak field limit. Evolution equations for the background are derived by taking moments of the Boltzmann equation in two cases: (i) a fixed relaxation time and (ii) a relaxation time that is proportional to the local inverse transverse momentum scale of the plasma. The second case allows us to reproduce 2nd-order viscous hydrodynamical dynamics in the limit of small shear viscosity to entropy ratio. We then linearize the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation in a dynamically-evolving background and obtain an integro-differential evolution equation for the chromoelectric field. We present numerical solutions to this integro-differential equation for a variety of different initial conditions and shear viscosity to entropy density ratios. The dynamical equations obtained are novel in that they include a non-trivial time-dependent momentum-space anisotropic background and the effect of collisional damping for the first time.

## Key claim

The paper derives and solves an integro-differential equation for the evolution of a uniform longitudinal chromoelectric field in a dynamically evolving momentum-space anisotropic background, including collisional damping for the first time; the authors emphasize that the dynamical equations are novel because they incorporate both a non-trivial time-dependent anisotropic background and the effect of collisional damping, which previous work by Bialas and Czyz (1987) had omitted.

## Method

The authors take moments of the Boltzmann equation with a Romatschke-Strickland anisotropic distribution function to obtain (0+1)-dimensional boost-invariant background evolution equations, using either a fixed relaxation time of 1 fm/c or a relaxation time proportional to the local inverse transverse momentum scale that reproduces 2nd-order viscous hydrodynamics in the small-anisotropy limit. They then linearize the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation around this time-evolving anisotropic background, couple the fluctuations to the Maxwell equations via color currents, and derive an integro-differential equation for the chromoelectric field, which they solve numerically for various initial anisotropies and shear viscosity to entropy density ratios.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time for hydrodynamic evolution: \(\tau_0 = 0.1\) fm/c; final time: \(\tau_f = 10\) fm/c.
- Three initial anisotropy values considered: \(\xi_0 = 99\), \(\xi_0 = 0\), and \(\xi_0 = -0.99\).
- Initial transverse-momentum scale set as \(\Lambda_0 = (1\ \text{GeV}) \cdot (1+\xi_0)^{1/6}\) to ensure equal initial number density across all \(\xi_0\).
- For the fixed relaxation time case: \(\tau_{\rm eq} = 1\) fm/c.
- For the dynamical relaxation time: \(\tau_{\rm eq}(\tau) = 5 \bar\eta / (2 {\cal R}^{1/4}(\xi) \Lambda)\), with \(\bar\eta = \eta_s/{\cal S}\) taken as time-independent; results shown for \(4\pi\bar\eta = 1\) and \(4\pi\bar\eta = 10\).
- In the longitudinal free streaming limit, the asymptotic solution involves \(\mu \equiv 3 \pi \tau_{\rm iso} \omega_{\rm pl}^2/4\), where \(\omega_{\rm pl}^2 = \kappa g^2 \Lambda_0^2\) and \(\kappa = 4/9\) for \(N_c=3\) and \(N_f=2\) when using quantum statistical distributions.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of Bialas and Czyz (1987), who derived a similar integro-differential equation for an ideal hydrodynamical (isotropic) background without collisional damping, by including a dynamically evolving anisotropic background and collisional damping via a relaxation time approximation. The results enable future study of non-Abelian plasma instabilities in dynamically evolving anisotropic backgrounds, building on the analysis of stable and unstable collective modes by Rebhan (2009) in the longitudinally free streaming limit, with which the authors show agreement as a cross-check.
