# Multi-particle correlations and KNO scaling in the medium-induced jet evolution

> We study the gluon distribution produced via successive medium-induced branchings by an energetic jet propagating through a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma. We show that under suitable approximations the evolution of the jet can be described as a classical stochastic process, which is exactly solvable. For this process, we construct exact analytic solutions for all the n-point correlation functions (the n-body densities in the space of energy). The corresponding results for the one-point and the two-point functions were already known, but those for the higher-point functions are new. These results demonstrate strong correlations associated with the existence of common ancestors in the branching process. By integrating these n-point functions over the gluon energies, we deduce the mean gluon multiplicity $\langle N\rangle$ as well as the higher moments $\langle N^p\rangle$ with $p\ge 2$. We find that the multiplicities of the soft gluons are parametrically large and show a remarkable regularity, known as Koba-Nielsen-Olesen (KNO) scaling: the reduced moments $\langle N^p\rangle/\langle N\rangle^p$ are pure numbers, independent of any of the physical parameters of the problem. We recognize a special negative binomial distribution which is characterized by large statistical fluctuations. These predictions can be tested in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, via event-by-event measurements of the di-jet asymmetry.

## Key claim

The authors show that for medium-induced jet evolution in a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma, under the approximations that successive branchings are quasi-independent and the BDMPSZ rate applies, the gluon distribution exhibits KNO scaling (reduced moments are pure numbers independent of physical parameters) and follows a specific negative binomial distribution with parameter r=2, which is broader than the corresponding distribution for vacuum jet evolution.

## Method

The authors model the medium-induced gluon branching as a Markovian stochastic process using a generating functional approach, with the branching rate given by a simplified version of the BDMPSZ spectrum (kernel K_0(z) = 1/[z(1-z)]^(3/2)). They derive and solve evolution equations for all n-point factorial moment densities N^(p)(x_1,...,x_p|tau) in energy-fraction space, constructing exact analytic solutions recursively from the known one-point and two-point functions to obtain higher-point correlations for p >= 3.

## Key numbers

- The branching energy scale is omega_br(L) = abar^2 * q_hat * L^2, where abar = alpha_s * N_c/pi, q_hat is the jet quenching parameter, and L is the medium size
- For the high-energy regime E >> omega_br(L), the average gluon multiplicity scales as <N> ~ (omega_br/omega_0)^(1/2) >> 1, where omega_0 is an infrared cutoff
- The dispersion in the energy lost by the jet satisfies sigma_E ~ <E> ~ omega_br(L), where sigma_E^2 = <E^2> - <E>^2
- The reduced moments kappa^(p) = <N^p>/<N>^p are pure numbers independent of alpha_s, q_hat, L, and E
- The di-jet asymmetry variance follows <(E_2 - E_1)^2> proportional to (L_1^2 - L_2^2)^2 + (L_1^4 + L_2^4)

## Context

This work extends the analytic framework for medium-induced jet evolution previously developed by Blaizot et al. (2013) and the authors' own earlier study of two-point correlations (Escobedo and Iancu, 2016). It provides the first exact analytic solutions for three-point and higher n-point gluon correlation functions, complementing numerical Monte-Carlo studies using JEWEL by Milhano and Zapp (2015) that demonstrated the importance of fluctuations for di-jet asymmetry. The results enable event-by-event predictions for multi-gluon correlations that can be tested in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, and establish a connection between medium-induced jet evolution and wave turbulence physics.
