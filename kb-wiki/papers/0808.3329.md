# Variational calculations for K-few-nucleon systems

> Deeply bound KNN, KNNN and KNNNN states are discussed. The effective force exerted by the K meson on the nucleons is calculated with static nucleons. Next the binding energies are obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation or by variational calculations. The dominant attraction comes from the S-wave Lambda(1405) and an additional contribution is due to Sigma(1385). The latter state is formed at the nuclear peripheries and absorbs a sizable piece of the binding energy. It also generates new branches of quasi-bound states. The lowest binding energies based on a phenomenological KN input fall into the 40-80 MeV range for KNN, 90-150 MeV for KNNN and 120-220 MeV for K-alpha systems. The uncertainties are due to unknown KN interactions in the distant subthreshold energy region.

## Key claim

The paper argues that deeply bound K-few-nucleon states exist with binding energies in the 40-220 MeV range, but the uncertainties are large due to unknown KN interactions in the subthreshold energy region, and the results depend strongly on the position of the Lambda(1405) resonance and the treatment of short-range NN repulsion.

## Method

The authors first calculate the effective force exerted by the K meson on static nucleons using multiple scattering equations with separable KN potentials. They then solve the Schrodinger equation or perform variational calculations for the KNN, KNNN, and KNNNN systems, incorporating both S-wave Lambda(1405) and P-wave Sigma(1385) interactions, with the Argonne NN potential used for nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

- KNN binding energies: 40-80 MeV range
- KNNN binding energies: 90-150 MeV range
- K-alpha (KNNNN) binding energies: 120-220 MeV range
- For the KNN system specifically, the lowest state is bound by about 40-80 MeV
- The Sigma(1385) contribution adds some 5-10 MeV to KNN binding and 10-20 MeV to KNNN binding
- The correction term Delta E_kin contributes approximately 0.2 MeV to the binding energy
- The variational precision for E is about 0.3 MeV, compared with the overall binding of about 50 MeV
- The KN interaction range parameter kappa is varied within 3-6 fm
- The P-wave Sigma(1385) solution exists for NN separations r greater than or equal to 1.6 fm

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) who predicted (E_B, Gamma) = (48,60) MeV for KNN, and by Dote and Weise (2007) who obtained E_B less than 50 MeV. It contrasts with Faddeev/AGS calculations by Schevchenko et al. (2007) yielding (55-70, 95-110) MeV and Ikeda and Sato (2007) with (about 80, about 73) MeV. The paper introduces two new elements: explicit treatment of P-wave Sigma(1385) interactions and emphasis on strong KN spatial correlations induced by S and P wave resonances. The results enable future experimental searches for these states and highlight the need for better determination of the Lambda(1405) position and KN scattering amplitudes in the subthreshold region.
