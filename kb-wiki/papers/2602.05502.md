# Electromagnetic polarizabilities of the triplet hadrons in heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory

> We investigate the electromagnetic polarizabilities of singly heavy mesons and doubly heavy baryons within the framework of heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory up to $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$. We estimate the low-energy constants using the non-relativistic constituent quark model. A striking prediction of our study is the giant electric polarizabilities of the $D^*$ mesons: $α_E(\bar{D}^{*0}) \approx 291.4 \times 10^{-4} \text{fm}^3$ and $α_E(D^{*-}) \approx -0.4-64.4 i \times 10^{-4} \text{fm}^3$. These anomalously large values arise from the near-degenerate mass between $D^*$ and $D π$, which are orders of magnitude larger than those of their bottom counterparts. This kinematic coincidence induces a pronounced cusp structure in the chiral loops, reflecting the long-range dynamics of a pion cloud. For doubly heavy baryons, polarizabilities depend strongly on heavy-flavor composition: the $bcq$ system differs markedly from $ccq$ and $bbq$ due to mixing with scalar heavy-diquark states. Using heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry (HDAS), we unify the chiral dynamics of singly heavy mesons and doubly heavy baryons in the heavy-quark limit. The pion-loop contributions dominate the electromagnetic structure of heavy hadrons and provide essential benchmarks for future lattice QCD simulations.

# Electromagnetic polarizabilities of the triplet hadrons in heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The electric polarizabilities of the $D^*$ mesons are predicted to be anomalously large, $\alpha_E(\bar{D}^{*0}) \approx 294 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$, but this giant enhancement depends critically on the near-degeneracy between the $D^*$ and $D\pi$ masses, which is a kinematic coincidence specific to the charmed sector and not present for bottom mesons.

## Method

The authors compute electromagnetic polarizabilities of singly heavy mesons and doubly heavy baryons using heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory (HH$\chi$PT) up to $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$. Low-energy constants (LECs) are estimated via the non-relativistic constituent quark model, with axial coupling constants extracted from $D^{*+}$ decay width data and lattice QCD. The polarizabilities are extracted from the spin-averaged forward Compton scattering tensor, with tree-level magnetic dipole transitions and chiral loop diagrams (dominated by pion-cloud contributions) evaluated analytically.

## Key numbers

- $\alpha_E(\bar{D}^{*0}) \approx 294(106) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$ (total electric polarizability, with uncertainty from error propagation)
- $\alpha_E(D^{*-}) \approx 1.42(34) - 64.5(155)i \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$ (complex value due to open $\bar{D}^{0}\pi^{-}$ channel)
- $\alpha_E(\bar{D}^{0}) \approx 1.50(36) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$ (pseudoscalar charmed meson, no giant enhancement)
- $\beta_M(\bar{D}^{0}) \approx 11.45(191) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$ (magnetic polarizability of neutral $D$ meson)
- $\beta_M(B^{+}) \approx 22.85(470) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$ (magnetic polarizability of charged $B$ meson)
- $\alpha_E(B^{*+}) \approx 2.89(67) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3$ (bottom vector meson, orders of magnitude smaller than charmed counterpart)
- Mass splitting $\Delta = m_{P^*} - m_P \approx 142$ MeV for charmed mesons, compared to $m_\pi \approx 140$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous HH$\chi$PT calculations of electromagnetic polarizabilities for singly heavy baryons by Chen (2024) and Wen (2025) to the flavor triplet of heavy hadrons, including both singly heavy mesons and doubly heavy baryons. The study exploits heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry (HDAS), proposed by Savage (1990), to unify the chiral dynamics of these systems in the heavy-quark limit. The results provide theoretical benchmarks for future lattice QCD simulations, which currently lack direct calculations of these polarizabilities for heavy hadrons. For the $bcq$ baryon system, the authors adopt the $cq$ spin-clustering scheme from quark model calculations by Ma (2022), which differs from the $bc$ spin-diquark structure.
