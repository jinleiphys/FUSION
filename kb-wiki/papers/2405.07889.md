# Tritium $β$-decay and Proton-Proton Fusion in Pionless Effective Field Theory

> The Gamow-Teller and Fermi matrix elements, ${\left<\mathbf{GT}\right>}$ and ${\left<\mathbf{F}\right>}$, respectively, for tritium $β$-decay are calculated to next-to-leading order (NLO) in pionless effective field theory in the absence of Coulomb and isospin violation giving the leading order predictions ${\left<\mathbf{GT}\right>}_{0}=0.9807$ and ${\left<\mathbf{F}\right>}_{0}=1$. Using an experimentally determined value for the tritium-$β$ decay GT matrix element, the two-body axial current low energy constant is fixed at NLO yielding $L_{1,A}=6.01\pm2.08$ fm$^{3}$ at the renormalization scale of the physical pion mass, which agrees with predictions based on naive dimensional analysis. Finally, the consequences of Wigner-SU(4) spin-isospin symmetry are considered for the Gamow-Teller matrix element.

# Tritium β-decay and Proton-Proton Fusion in Pionless Effective Field Theory

## Key claim

The Gamow-Teller and Fermi matrix elements for tritium β-decay are calculated to next-to-leading order (NLO) in pionless effective field theory under the assumptions of no Coulomb interactions and exact isospin symmetry, yielding a fitted two-body axial current low-energy constant \(L_{1,A}=6.01\pm2.08\) fm³ at the renormalization scale of the physical pion mass; the authors note that omitted Coulomb effects are perturbative corrections of approximately 6% and that a full next-to-next-to-leading order calculation would require a new three-body axial current counterterm.

## Method

The authors use the dibaryon formalism of pionless effective field theory with the Z-parametrization for nucleon-nucleon interactions, fitting strong-interaction parameters to the \(^3S_1\) and \(^1S_0\) scattering poles and residues. The Gamow-Teller and Fermi matrix elements are computed from the axial and vector form factors at zero momentum transfer by evaluating diagrams for the three-nucleon system at leading order and next-to-leading order, including one-body currents, two-body axial current contributions from \(L_{1,A}\), and NLO corrections to the three-nucleon vertex function. The two-body axial current low-energy constant is then fixed by fitting to the experimentally determined Gamow-Teller matrix element, and the resulting value is used to predict the threshold \(pp\)-fusion reduced matrix element.

## Key numbers

- Leading-order Gamow-Teller matrix element: \(\langle\mathbf{GT}\rangle_0 = \sqrt{3} \times 0.9807\)
- Leading-order Fermi matrix element: \(\langle\mathbf{F}\rangle_0 = 1\)
- NLO Gamow-Teller matrix element with \(L_{1,A}=0\): \(\langle\mathbf{GT}\rangle_{0+1} = \sqrt{3} \times 0.9935\)
- Fitted two-body axial current LEC: \(L_{1,A}(\mu=m_\pi) = 6.01 \pm 2.08\) fm³
- Naturalness estimate for \(L_{1,A}\): \(|L_{1,A}(\mu=m_\pi)| \approx 6.5\) fm³
- Experimental Gamow-Teller matrix element used for fit: \(\langle\mathbf{GT}\rangle = \sqrt{3} \times 0.9511(13)\) (from Baroni 2016)
- Predicted \(pp\)-fusion reduced matrix element at threshold: \(\Lambda(0) = 2.77(33)\), with a 12% NLO EFT error estimate
- Phenomenological value of \(\Lambda(0)\): 2.65(1) (from Adelberger 2010)
- Triton binding energy: \(B = 8.48\) MeV
- \(^3S_1\) bound state momentum: \(\gamma_t = 45.7025\) MeV
- \(^1S_0\) virtual bound state momentum: \(\gamma_s = -7.890\) MeV
- \(Z\)-parametrization residues: \(Z_t = 1.6908\), \(Z_s = 0.9015\)
- Estimated Coulomb correction size: \(\alpha M_n / p_* \sim 6\%\), with \(p_* \sim 112\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the pionless effective field theory treatment of weak processes in few-nucleon systems, building on the formalism of Vanasse (2017) for three-nucleon form factors and the earlier NLO calculation of \(pp \to de^+\nu_e\) by Kong and Ravndal (2000) and Butler (2001), which were limited by the unknown \(L_{1,A}\). The fitted value of \(L_{1,A}\) from tritium β-decay enables improved predictions for solar neutrino processes relevant to the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory and for the upcoming MuSUN muon capture experiment. The authors note that a previous calculation by De-Leon (2016) found \(\langle\mathbf{GT}\rangle_0 = \sqrt{3}\) when dropping Coulomb and isospin breaking, but did not specify that this result holds only in the Wigner-SU(4) limit; the present work clarifies this and demonstrates that the full leading-order result is \(\sqrt{3} \times 0.9807\). The outlook identifies that a next-to-next-to-leading order calculation would require a new three-body axial current counterterm, analogous to the three-body current needed for magnetic moments as shown by Lin (2022).
