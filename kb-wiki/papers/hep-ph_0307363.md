# Quasi-coherent state of pions in the nucleon

> Making use of the quasi-coherent state developed by Eriksson et al., we can find a nucleon solution accompanied by the pion field with trivial topology. We compare our approach with other related works, and examine a coherent state description of pions in the baryon structure. Our solution suggests a kind of nucleon resonance due to the topological change of pion field without the usual quark excitation.

# Quasi-coherent state of pions in the nucleon

## Key claim

The authors find a nucleon solution with a pion field that has trivial topology (winding number 0), which suggests a kind of nucleon resonance due to topological change of the pion field without quark excitation, but only if the pion distribution is taken as a simple real function rather than the hedgehog form; the resulting nucleon mass is about 300 MeV higher than the hedgehog result, implying this solution may correspond to an excited state rather than the ground state.

## Method

The authors construct a quasi-coherent state (QCS) for the pion field by applying the Peierls-Yoccoz projection operator to the standard coherent state, extracting eigenstates of isospin and angular momentum. They then build a nucleon state as a linear combination of three-quark states (with spin-isospin 1/2 and 3/2) coupled to QCS pion states with T=L=0 or T=L=1, plus a coherent sigma meson state, and perform a variational calculation of the nucleon mass in the linear sigma model. The pion distribution is assumed to have the same momentum dependence for all three isospin components, with a real function f_mu(k) = f_mu * xi(k).

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass with QCS: 1113 MeV (using G=5, m_sigma=700 MeV)
- Delta mass with QCS: 1248 MeV (using G=5, m_sigma=700 MeV)
- Nucleon mass with CPA (coherent pair approximation): 1093 MeV (using G=5, m_sigma=700 MeV)
- Delta mass with CPA: 1233 MeV (using G=5, m_sigma=700 MeV)
- Nucleon mass with QCS: 1215 MeV (using G=5, m_sigma=1200 MeV)
- Hedgehog ansatz nucleon mass from Fiolhais et al. (1988): 938 MeV (using G=5, m_sigma=1200 MeV)
- Pion mass fixed to observed value: 140 MeV
- Pion decay constant fixed to observed value: 93 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quasi-coherent state approach developed by Eriksson et al. (1981) for the pion field, using the Peierls-Yoccoz projection method from Peierls and Yoccoz (1957). It compares with the hedgehog ansatz from Skyrme (1961) and Fiolhais et al. (1988), and with the coherent pair approximation (CPA) from Bolsterli (1981) and Goeke et al. (1988). The authors find that the QCS clarifies the theoretical foundation of the CPA, which was previously defined intuitively, and that the topological difference between the QCS (trivial winding number) and the hedgehog state (winding number 1) leads to a 300 MeV mass difference, suggesting the QCS solution may represent a nucleon resonance rather than the ground state. This opens the possibility of describing baryon excitations through topological changes in the pion field rather than quark excitations, relevant to resonances like the Roper resonance that are not fully explained by constituent quark models.
