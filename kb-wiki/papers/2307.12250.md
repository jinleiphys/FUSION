# Spin polarization of heavy quarks in matter: predictions from effective field theories

> The spin polarization of heavy quarks in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC is estimated from effective field theories (EFTs). One EFT is similar to the HQET used at zero temperature. This gives a coupling of the heavy quark spin to colour and electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions. The second EFT describes the interaction of heavy quarks and hydrodynamic modes, and gives the coupling between the heavy quark spin and the local vorticity of the fireball. Using these, we find that the measurement of polarization of the heavy quark from small to moderate p_T at the LHC is predicted with a single free parameter proportional to the vorticity. As a result, the heavy quark polarization is the same whether it is derived from the spin alignment of heavy vector mesons or the polarization of heavy baryons. We also predict that the parameter does not differ much between charm and bottom quarks.

## Key claim

The spin polarization of heavy quarks in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC can be predicted from effective field theories with a single free parameter proportional to the vorticity, but this prediction relies on the assumption that the heavy quark spin freezes out at a relic temperature of about 0.4–0.5 GeV after approximately 3 fm, and the quantitative accuracy depends on whether the heavy quark mass is sufficiently larger than the fireball temperature to allow weak-coupling matching of low-energy constants.

## Method

Two bottom-up effective field theories were constructed: a thermal Heavy Quark Effective Theory (HQET) with a UV cutoff Λ satisfying m > Λ ≫ T, which couples the heavy quark spin to colour and electromagnetic fields via a dimension-5 operator; and Heavy Quark Sono-Dynamics (HQSD) with a UV cutoff Λ̃ satisfying T > Λ̃ ≫ ℳ (where ℳ is the inverse fireball lifetime), which couples the heavy quark spin to the local fluid vorticity via a dimension-4 operator. The spin polarization was then computed using a 2×2 density matrix for the heavy quark spin at the freeze-out temperature, with the energy splitting ΔE derived from the relevant spin Hamiltonian in the centre-of-mass frame of the fireball.

## Key numbers

- Bottom quark mass: m = 4.18 ± 0.03 GeV; charm quark mass: m = 1.280 ± 0.025 GeV (PDG values)
- Initial fireball temperature at LHC: about 0.5 GeV; at top RHIC energy: about 0.3 GeV
- HQET UV cutoff for bottom: Λ ≈ 2 GeV, where αS ≈ 0.3
- HQSD UV cutoff: Λ̃ = 50–100 MeV, with fireball expansion rate ℳ ≈ 20 MeV (corresponding to fireball lifetime of about 10 fm)
- Bottom quark spin relaxation time: a little more than 3 fm at T = 0.5 GeV, approaching 6 fm at T = 0.4 GeV
- Electromagnetic field parameter at spin freeze-out: ζ = 0.01, giving ΔE of order a few tens of keV for pT ranging from 5–50 GeV, leading to polarization far below one percent
- Vorticity parameter central value: 2c₄|w_y| = 10 MeV, with uncertainty range 3–30 MeV, predicting polarization of a few tens of percent

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of heavy quark thermalization studies using effective field theories (Braaten 1991, Moore 2004) and lattice QCD (Banerjee 2011, Ding 2012, Altenkort 2020) to the spin degree of freedom, which had previously been studied experimentally for light and strange baryons (STAR 2017, ALICE 2019, STAR 2020) and vector mesons (ALICE 2019, STAR 2022). The approach enables a controlled prediction that heavy quark polarization from spin alignment of vector mesons (D* and B*) should agree with that from heavy baryon polarization, and that the vorticity coupling parameter should not differ much between charm and bottom quarks, providing a testable signature for future LHC measurements.
