# Baryon self energies in the chiral loop expansion

> We compute the self energies of the baryon octet and decuplet states at the one-loop level applying the manifestly covariant chiral Lagrangian. It is demonstrated that expressions consistent with the expectation of power counting rules arise if the self energies are decomposed according to the Passarino-Veltman scheme supplemented by a minimal subtraction. This defines a partial summation of the chiral expansion. A finite renormalization required to install chiral power counting rules leads to the presence of an infrared renormalization scale. Good convergence properties for the chiral loop expansion of the baryon octet and decuplet masses are obtained for natural values of the infrared scale. A prediction for the strange-quark matrix element of the nucleon is made.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a manifestly covariant chiral Lagrangian, when combined with a Passarino-Veltman reduction and a minimal subtraction scheme (called the \(\chi\)-MS scheme), can produce baryon self energies that respect chiral power counting rules, provided an infrared renormalization scale \(\mu_{IR}\) is introduced. The claim is hedged by the condition that good convergence properties are obtained only for "natural values" of the infrared scale, and that the scheme defines a partial summation of the chiral expansion rather than a full perturbative treatment.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop self energies of the baryon octet and decuplet states using the manifestly covariant chiral Lagrangian, with meson-baryon vertices defined by coupling constants \(F, D, C, H\) and a parameter \(Z\). They apply the Passarino-Veltman reduction to decompose the self energies into scalar master loop functions (tadpoles \(I_Q, I_R\) and the two-point function \(I_{QR}(p^2)\)), then renormalize these master loops via a minimal subtraction scheme that introduces an infrared renormalization scale \(\mu_{IR}\) in the subtraction term \(\delta I_{QR}\). The renormalized quantities are constructed so that the baryonic tadpole \(\bar I_R\) vanishes and the mesonic tadpole \(\bar I_Q\) follows the \(\widetilde{MS}\)-scheme, while the finite part of \(\bar I_{QR}(p^2)\) is adjusted to enforce chiral power counting.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is taken as \(f = 92.4\ \text{MeV}\).
- Tree-level parameters from mass differences: \(b_D \simeq +0.06\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\), \(b_F \simeq -0.21\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\), \(d_D \simeq -0.48\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\).
- Coupling constants used: \(F = 0.45\), \(D = 0.80\), \(H = 9F - 3D\), \(C = 2D\), and \(Z = 0.72\).
- The renormalized mesonic tadpole is \(\bar I_Q = \frac{m_Q^2}{(4\pi)^2} \ln\left( \frac{m_Q^2}{\mu_{UV}^2} \right)\).
- The renormalized two-point function at the baryon mass is \(\bar I_{QR}(M_R^2) = \frac{1}{8\pi^2} \frac{\mu_{IR}}{M_R} - \frac{m_Q}{16\pi M_R} \left(1 - \frac{m_Q^2}{8 M_R^2}\right) + \frac{1}{(4\pi)^2} \left(1 - \frac{3}{2} \ln\left( \frac{m_Q^2}{M_R^2}\right) \right) \frac{m_Q^2}{M_R^2} + \mathcal{O}\left( \frac{m_Q^4}{M_R^4}, d-4 \right)\).

## Context

This work extends the scheme proposed by Lutz (2000) and Lutz and Kolomeitsev (2002) for baryon self energies, offering an alternative to the heavy-baryon formulation of Jenkins and Manohar (1991) and the infrared scheme of Becher and Leutwyler (1999). It addresses poor convergence observed in earlier SU(3) chiral perturbation theory computations for baryon octet masses by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1993) and Borasoy and Meissner (1997), as well as the failure of the extended on-mass shell scheme (EOMS) by Gegelia and Japaridze (1999) to produce small subleading terms (Lehnhart, Gegelia, and Scherer, 2005). The \(\chi\)-MS scheme enables a prediction for the strange-quark matrix element of the nucleon and provides a framework for future studies of baryon properties with improved convergence.
