# Microscopic Clustering in Light Nuclei

> We review recent experimental and theoretical progress in understanding the microscopic details of clustering in light nuclei. We discuss recent experimental results on $α$-conjugate systems, molecular structures in neutron-rich nuclei, and constraints for ab initio theory. We then examine nuclear clustering in a wide range of theoretical methods, including the resonating group and generator coordinate methods, antisymmetrized molecular dynamics, Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke wave function and container model, no-core shell model methods, continuum quantum Monte Carlo, and lattice effective field theory.

## Key claim

This review argues that nuclear clustering in light nuclei is an emergent phenomenon arising from a delicate balance of short-range repulsive forces, Pauli blocking, medium-range nuclear attraction, and Coulomb repulsion, but the authors explicitly note that several key issues remain unresolved and there are disagreements among different theoretical methods, with some of the most interesting results expected in the near future.

## Method

The paper surveys recent experimental results on alpha-conjugate systems and molecular structures in neutron-rich nuclei, then examines a wide range of theoretical approaches for microscopic clustering. These include the resonating group method and generator coordinate method, antisymmetrized molecular dynamics, the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Ropke wave function and container model, no-core shell model methods, continuum quantum Monte Carlo, and lattice effective field theory. The review focuses on microscopic clustering where clusters emerge from nucleonic degrees of freedom, and discusses both the strengths and challenges of each theoretical approach.

## Context

This review extends the long line of clustering research dating back to Rutherford (1899), Gamow (1928), and Wheeler (1937), and builds upon the Ikeda diagrams (1968) linking clustering to decay thresholds. The paper discusses recent experimental results that test ab initio calculations such as Green's function Monte Carlo by Wiringa (2000) and lattice effective field theory by Epelbaum (2011, 2012), and it addresses open questions about the nature of the Hoyle state, the existence of linear chain structures, and the meaning of rotational bands in short-lived continuum states as discussed by Garrido (2013).
