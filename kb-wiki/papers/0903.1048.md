# 6He beta-decay rate and the suppression of the axial constant in nuclear matter

> We present a microscopic calculation of the 6He beta-decay into the ground state of 6Li. To this end, we use chiral perturbation theory at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order to describe the nuclear weak-currents. The nuclear wave functions are derived from the J-matrix inverse scattering nucleon-nucleon potential (JISP), and the Schroedinger equation is solved using the hyperspherical-harmonics expansion. Our calculation brings the theoretical decay-rate within 3% of the measured one. This success is attributed to the use of chiral-perturbation-theory based mesonic currents, whose contribution is qualitatively different compared to standard nuclear physics approach, where the use of meson exchange currents worsens the comparison to experiment. The inherent inconsistency in the use of the JISP potential together with chiral-perturbation-theory based is argued not to affect this conclusion, though a more detailed investigation is called for. We conclude that any suppression of the axial constant in nuclear matter is included in this description of the weak interaction in the nucleus.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the discrepancy between previous theoretical calculations and the experimental beta-decay rate of 6He originates in the model of the weak interaction inside the nucleus, not in the nuclear wave functions. They claim that using chiral perturbation theory based mesonic currents brings the theoretical decay rate within 3% of the measured value, and that any suppression of the axial constant in nuclear matter is already included in this description, though the inherent inconsistency of using the JISP potential together with chiral perturbation theory is argued not to affect this conclusion.

## Method

The authors performed a microscopic calculation of the 6He beta-decay into the ground state of 6Li using chiral perturbation theory at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) to describe the nuclear weak currents. Nuclear wave functions were derived from the J-matrix inverse scattering nucleon-nucleon potential (JISP16), and the Schroedinger equation was solved using the hyperspherical-harmonics expansion for the six-body system. The triton beta-decay was used to calibrate the contact interaction part of the meson-exchange currents, making the calculation free of any free parameters.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical 6He beta-decay rate is within 3% of the measured value
- The experimental 6He comparative half-life is (fT1/2)_t = 812.8 ± 3.7 sec, yielding |GT(6He)|_expt = 2.161 ± 0.005
- The predicted Gamow-Teller matrix element is |GT(6He)|_theo = 2.198 ± 0.007, overpredicting the experimental value by about 1.7%
- The JISP16 potential leads to an underbinding of about 80 keV for 3He and 120 keV for the triton
- For the 6-body nuclei, extrapolated binding energies are 28.70 MeV for 6He and 31.46 MeV for 6Li, roughly 550 keV below experimental values
- The leading order GT matrix element for 6He-6Li using JISP16 is GT = 2.225(2)
- The triton leading order GT matrix element using JISP16 is 1.6524(2), compared to the experimental value of 1.656(3)

## Context

This work extends the study of Schiavilla and Wiringa (2002), who used the realistic Argonne v18 potential with Urbana-IX three-nucleon-force and found that two-body currents increased the Gamow-Teller matrix element, worsening comparison with experiment. The present calculation using chiral perturbation theory based mesonic exchange currents qualitatively changes this behavior, finding that the contact interaction contributes negatively to the matrix element. This enables a first-principles test of weak interaction models in nuclear matter and suggests that the suppression of the axial constant g_A from its vacuum value of 1.2695 to unity, as argued by Buck (1983) and Wilkinson (1973), is already accounted for in the chiral perturbation theory description.
