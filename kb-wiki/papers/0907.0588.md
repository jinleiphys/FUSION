# Electron-muon correlation as a new probe to strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma

> As a new and clean probe to the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP), we propose an azimuthal correlation of an electron and a muon which originate from the semileptonic decay of charm and bottom quarks. By solving the Langevin equation for the heavy quarks under the hydrodynamic evolution of the hot plasma, we show that substantial quenching of the away-side peak in the electron-muon correlation can be seen if the sQGP drag force acting on heavy quarks is large enough as suggested from the gauge/gravity correspondence. The effect could be detected in high-energy heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and the Large Hadron Collider.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the azimuthal correlation of an electron and a muon from semileptonic decays of back-to-back heavy quarks is a clean probe of the drag force in strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP), but the observable's sensitivity depends on the drag parameter being large enough (gamma = 1-3) as suggested by gauge/gravity correspondence rather than perturbative QCD.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Langevin equation for charm and bottom quarks propagating through a perfect-fluid hydrodynamic background that reproduces experimental hadronic observables at low transverse momentum. Heavy quarks are generated via PYTHIA 6.4, diffuse in the QGP fluid until the critical temperature T_c = 170 MeV, then hadronize to D or B mesons which undergo semileptonic decays simulated by PYTHIA. The drag coefficient is parametrized as Gamma(p) = gamma T^2/M, with the diffusion constant determined by the fluctuation-dissipation theorem.

## Key numbers

- Drag parameter gamma = 1-3 extracted from analysis of the nuclear modification factor R_AA for single electrons at RHIC, consistent with gamma ~ 2 from gauge/gravity correspondence but much larger than gamma ~ 0.2 from leading-order perturbative QCD (Moore 2004)
- For trigger electrons with p_T >= 3.0 GeV/c and associate muons with p_T >= 3.0 GeV/c, the away-side quenching factor I_AA^(e-mu) is as small as 0.55 for gamma = 3
- Averaged temperature <T> ~ 0.21 GeV for charm and bottom quarks in hot matter at RHIC
- Averaged staying time of charm and bottom quarks inside QGP fluid is 3-4 fm/c
- Critical temperature T_c = 170 MeV
- Impact parameter b = 3.1 fm for nearly central collisions
- For electron-hadron correlation with trigger electrons 2.0 <= p_T^(e) <= 3.0 GeV/c and associate hadrons 0.5 <= p_T^(h) <= 1.0 GeV/c, gamma = 3 leads to near-side and away-side quenching factors as small as 0.35

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Langevin + hydro approach (Akamatsu 2009) for studying heavy quark energy loss in sQGP, which was motivated by the small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio from gauge/gravity correspondence (Son 2007) and lattice QCD (Nakamura 2004). The electron-muon correlation is proposed as an improvement over electron-hadron correlations, which suffer from final-state hadronic interactions and contamination from anisotropic collective flow, and over same-flavor dilepton correlations (e+e- or mu+mu-), which receive contamination from virtual photons and neutral vector mesons. The observable enables experimental determination of the heavy quark drag coefficient at RHIC and LHC, and studies of the peak width in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions could probe gluon distribution inside nuclei.
