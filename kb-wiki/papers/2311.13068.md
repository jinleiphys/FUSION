# Magnetic field dependence of the neutral pion longitudinal screening mass in the linear sigma model with quarks

> We use the linear sigma model with quarks to study the magnetic-field-induced modifications on the longitudinal screening mass for the neutral pion at one-loop level. The effects of the magnetic field are introduced into the self-energy, which contains the contributions from all the model particles. We find that, to obtain a reasonable description for the behavior with the field strength, we need to account for the magnetic field dependence of the particle masses. We also find that the couplings need to decrease fast enough with the field strength to then reach constant and smaller values as compared to their vacuum ones. The results illustrate the need to treat the magnetic corrections to the particle masses and couplings in a self-consistent manner, accounting for the backreaction of the field effects for the magnetic field dependence of the rest of the particle species and couplings in the model.

## Key claim

The neutral pion longitudinal screening mass at zero temperature can be made to follow the lattice QCD profile as a function of magnetic field strength only if the model couplings decrease rapidly with the field and the magnetic field dependence of the particle masses is accounted for self-consistently; the authors find that a rapid decrease of the couplings is needed for solutions to exist beyond very small field strengths.

## Method

The authors used the linear sigma model with quarks (LSMq) at one-loop level, computing the neutral pion self-energy from quark-antiquark loops, quark tadpoles, and charged pion tadpoles in a constant external magnetic field. The magnetic field effects were introduced via Schwinger proper-time propagators for charged fermions and bosons, and the longitudinal screening mass was obtained by solving the dispersion relation with the external three-momentum taken along the field direction and the energy set to zero. The magnetic field dependence of the quark, pion, and sigma pole masses was taken as input from a previous self-consistent calculation (Ayala et al., 2021), and the coupling constant was treated as a field-dependent parameter adjusted to reproduce the lattice QCD behavior.

## Key numbers

* The longitudinal screening mass normalized to the zero-field pion pole mass was computed for magnetic field strengths up to about 0.8 GeV\(^2\) (for the smallest coupling tested, \(g=0.33\)); for the vacuum coupling \(g=2.75\), solutions to the dispersion relation ceased to exist beyond \(q_fB \sim 0.2\) GeV\(^2\).
* With the vacuum coupling \(g=2.75\), the screening mass decreased monotonically with field strength until solutions became impossible; with reduced couplings of \(g=1.5\), \(g=0.75\), and \(g=0.33\), the range of field strengths where solutions existed increased to about 0.35, 0.55, and 0.8 GeV\(^2\), respectively.
* The quark mass at zero field was \(m_f = g v_0\) with \(v_0 = \sqrt{(a^2 + m_0^2)/\lambda}\); the pion mass at zero field was \(m_0\), and the sigma mass was \(m_\sigma^2 = 2a^2 + 3m_0^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the pion pole mass in a magnetic field within the LSMq (Ayala et al., 2020; Ayala et al., 2021) to the longitudinal screening mass at zero temperature, where the pole and screening masses should coincide in a Lorentz-invariant system. The results are motivated by lattice QCD findings (Ding, 2021) that the pion screening mass decreases monotonically with magnetic field, and by NJL model calculations (Sheng, 2022) that found opposite behaviors for transverse and longitudinal screening masses. The authors argue that a self-consistent treatment of magnetic corrections to all particle masses and couplings is necessary, and that the framework can be extended to study directional sound velocities or the connection between screening masses and inverse magnetic catalysis.
