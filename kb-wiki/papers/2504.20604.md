# Investigation of the properties of $1^-_1$ excited states of even-even nuclei

> The commissioning of new sources of monochromatic $γ$-quanta will allow obtaining a large amount of new data on low-lying collective states of negative parity of even-even nuclei. The purpose of this work is to derive relations between observables characterizing the low-energy negative parity states in even-even nuclei and to investigate the dependence of E2 transitions between the low-energy collective states in these nuclei on the parity of states. The Hamiltonian of the Collective Model and the fermionic Q-phonon representation of the vectors of collective states are used. Relations between different E1 transition matrix elements are derived and the parity dependence of E2 transitions is investigated. Relations between the observables characterizing the low-lying collective states of both parities in even-even nuclei are obtained, which can be verified using experimental data produced by facilities generating intense monochromatic beams of $γ$-quanta.

## Key claim

The paper derives theoretical relations between observables of low-lying negative parity states in even-even nuclei, but these relations are approximate and rely on specific model assumptions (harmonic octupole vibrations, neglect of two-phonon terms in the quadrupole operator, and the Q-phonon representation of wave vectors) that may break down in transitional or deformed nuclei.

## Method

The authors use the Hamiltonian of the Collective Model, coupling quadrupole and octupole modes, and the fermionic Q-phonon representation of collective state vectors to derive analytic expressions for excitation energies and E1 and E2 transition matrix elements. They apply these relations to specific nuclei (e.g., 148Nd) by inserting experimental values for known observables (B(E3), quadrupole moments, excitation energies) and compare the predicted values to measured data.

## Key numbers

- In 138Ba: E(1⁻₁) = 4026 keV, E(3⁻₁) + E(2⁺₁) = 4317 keV.
- In 142Nd: E(1⁻₁) = 3425 keV, E(3⁻₁) + E(2⁺₁) = 3661 keV.
- In 142Ce: E(1⁻₁) = 2188 keV, E(3⁻₁) + E(2⁺₁) = 2294 keV.
- For 148Nd: predicted E(1⁻₁) = 1.062 MeV from relation (E9) using B(E3;3⁻₁→0⁺₁) = 45442 e²fm⁶, ⟨2⁺₁||Q₂||2⁺₁⟩ = -185 e·fm², E(2⁺₁) = 0.302 MeV, E(3⁻₁) = 0.999 MeV; experimental E(1⁻₁) = 1.024 MeV.
- The ratio B(E1;1⁻₁→0⁺₁)/B(E1;3⁻₁→2⁺₁) is predicted to be 7/3 from the Q-phonon approach.
- For 148Nd: calculated |⟨1⁻₁||Q₂||1⁻₁⟩| = 118 e·fm² from branching ratio relation; experimental value is -39⁺⁴⁰₋₈ e·fm²; direct calculation from Eq. (35) gives -29 e·fm².

## Context

This work extends the Q-phonon approach of Pietralla (2000) and Jolos, Shneidman, and Voronov (JShV) to derive relations for negative parity states, building on earlier studies of quadrupole-octupole two-phonon structures in near-magic nuclei by Cottle, Zilges, Herzberg, Kneissl, Ibbotson, Fransen, Pietralla, and Shneidman. The derived relations are intended to be tested with data from new monochromatic gamma-ray facilities (e.g., laser Compton scattering sources such as those described by Litvinenko, Hajima, and INOK). The paper also discusses the parity dependence of E2 transitions in deformed nuclei, contrasting the behavior in nuclei with octupole deformation (e.g., 144Ba, studied by Shneidman) versus those without (e.g., 148Nd, studied by Ibbotson).
