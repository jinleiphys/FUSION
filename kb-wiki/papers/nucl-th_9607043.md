# The $η^\prime$ Photoproduction off the Nucleon in the quark model

> The reaction $γ+ p\to η^\prime + p$ is investigated in the quark model approach. The quark model predicts that the off-shell contributions from the resonance $S_{11}(1535)$ dominate the $\etap$ production in the threshold region. We find that the coulping constant $α_{\etap NN}$ from the fit to a few total cross section data is small. The results of the total and differential cross sections are presented at different energies, which can be tested in the future experiments. If the future total cross data indeed show a resonance structure around 2 GeV suggested in a recent study, it would be an evidence of a non $qqq$ structure in its wavefunction.

# The η′ Photoproduction off the Nucleon in the Quark Model

## Key claim

The quark model predicts that off-shell contributions from the S₁₁(1535) resonance dominate η′ photoproduction in the threshold region, contrary to an effective Lagrangian study suggesting dominance by resonances around 2 GeV. If future total cross section data show a resonance structure around 2 GeV, it would be evidence of a non-qqq (exotic) structure in that resonance's wavefunction.

## Method

The reaction γ + p → η′ + p is investigated using a quark model approach with SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry, where transition amplitudes are expressed in standard CGLN (Chew-Goldberger-Low-Nambu) formalism. The calculation includes s-channel baryon resonances below 2 GeV with known masses and widths from Particle Data Group (1994), while resonances above 2 GeV are treated as degenerate continuum contributions up to 2.6 GeV. Only one free parameter, the coupling constant α_{η′NN}, is fitted to total cross section data; the constituent quark mass m_q = 0.34 GeV and harmonic oscillator parameter α² = 0.16 GeV² are fixed from the quark model.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant α_{η′NN} = 0.35 from fit to total cross section data, consistent with small values suggested in prior studies
- Constituent quark masses: m_u = m_d = 0.34 GeV
- Harmonic oscillator parameter: α² = 0.16 GeV²
- Without the S₁₁(1535) contribution, the total cross section is only about 20% of that with the S₁₁(1535) included
- A small bump appears around E_lab = 2.1 GeV from resonances G₁₇(2190) and H₁₉(2220)
- Only four total cross section data points exist for E_lab ≤ 2.4 GeV, from ABBHHM (1960s) and AHHM (1970s) collaborations

## Context

This work extends the quark model framework previously developed by Li (1995) for kaon and η photoproductions to the η′ channel, which had received no theoretical attention since the early experiments by ABBHHM and AHHM collaborations. It contradicts the effective Lagrangian approach of the RPI group (1995), which suggested dominance of the N*(2080) resonance but excluded off-shell contributions from resonances in the 1.5–1.7 GeV region. The results enable future experiments at CEBAF to test the off-shell behavior of quark model CGLN amplitudes and to search for non-qqq states by measuring differential cross sections and polarization observables.
