# Extrapolation of scattering data to the negative-energy region

> Explicit analytic expressions are derived for the effective-range function for the case when the interaction is represented by a sum of the short-range square-well and long-range Coulomb potentials. These expressions are then transformed into forms convenient for extrapolating to the negative-energy region and obtaining the information about bound-state properties. Alternative ways of extrapolation are discussed. Analytic properties of separate terms entering these expressions for the effective-range function and the partial-wave scattering amplitude are investigated.

## Key claim

Explicit analytic expressions for the effective-range function (ERF) are derived for a short-range square-well plus Coulomb potential, and transformed into forms suitable for extrapolating scattering data from positive to negative energies to extract bound-state properties such as asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs). The authors show that the function Δ_l(E), despite possessing an essential singularity at E=0, can be analytically continued along the real axis to negative energies, justifying its use in place of the full ERF which contains large pure-Coulomb terms.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation for a potential consisting of a square well (radius R, depth V_0) plus repulsive Coulomb interaction, obtaining analytic expressions for the Coulomb-modified phase shifts δ_l. These are used to construct the effective-range function K_l(E) and related quantities (k^{2l+1}C_l^2(η)cotδ_l, Δ_l(E), and the renormalized amplitude f̃_l) in three different representations using regular and irregular Coulomb functions. Numerical calculations are performed for the d+α system (l=0) with parameters fitted to reproduce the ⁶Li ground state binding energy and ANC from Faddeev calculations, and the accuracy of each representation is compared at E=+5 MeV and E=-5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For the d+α system (S-state, l=0): square-well depth V_0 = 7.64386 MeV, radius R = 3.73473 fm.
- Binding energy ε obtained from analytic continuation of scattering data: ε = 1.268 MeV; dimensionless ANC C̃_0 = 2.683.
- These values agree with direct solution of the Schrödinger equation for the bound state.
- For comparison, with Coulomb turned off: ε = 2.409 MeV, C̃_0 = 2.29; experimental ε = 1.47 MeV; experimental extrapolation gives C̃_0 = 2.93 (Blokhintsev et al. 2015).
- ERF K_0(5 MeV) = 0.3120231949 (real part, version Eq. 3); K_0(-5 MeV) = -0.2802915316 (real part, version Eq. 3), with imaginary parts below 10⁻⁹ in the best version.

## Context

This work extends the effective-range formalism for charged-particle scattering, building on the foundational treatment by Hamilton (1954) and the renormalized amplitude approach of Biedenharn, Blatt, and others. It directly addresses the practical problem of extracting ANCs from elastic scattering data, as previously done for ⁶Li by Blokhintsev et al. (2015) and for ¹⁶O by Sparen (1999) using α-¹²C scattering. The results justify the use of Δ_l(E) for analytic continuation (as suggested by Sparen 1999) and show it is more effective for heavier nuclei where the Coulomb parameter is larger. The work enables more reliable extraction of ANCs for nuclear astrophysics reactions, such as the α+d → ⁶Li+γ radiative capture, and is relevant to effective field theory practitioners fitting elastic scattering data.
