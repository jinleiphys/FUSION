# Extrapolating Lattice QCD Results using Effective Field Theory

> Lattice simulations are the only viable way to obtain ab-initio Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) predictions for low energy nuclear physics. These calculations are done, however, in a finite box and therefore extrapolation is needed to get the free space results. Here we use nuclear Effective Field Theory (EFT), designed to provide a low energy description of QCD using baryonic degrees of freedom, to extrapolate the lattice results from finite to infinite volumes. To this end, we fit the EFT to the results calculated with nonphysical high quark masses and solve it with the stochastic variational method in both finite and infinite volumes. Moreover, we perform similar EFT calculations of the physical point and predict the finite-volume effects to be found in future Lattice QCD calculations for atomic nuclei with mass number $A\le4$.

## Key claim

The authors use a leading-order pionless Effective Field Theory (EFT), fitted to finite-volume Lattice QCD results at a heavy pion mass, to extrapolate binding energies to infinite volume; they then invert the procedure at the physical pion mass to predict the finite-volume corrections that future Lattice QCD calculations will need to apply for nuclei with mass number A ≤ 4.

## Method

The authors fit the low-energy constants (C0, C1, D) of a leading-order pionless EFT to the finite-volume binding energies of the deuteron, di-neutron, and triton from NPLQCD at mπ = 806 MeV, using a Gaussian regulator and cutoff values from 2 to 10 fm⁻¹. They solve the Schrödinger equation with periodic boundary conditions using the Stochastic Variational Method with a correlated Gaussian basis, then extrapolate to infinite volume by solving the same EFT in the L → ∞ limit. For the physical pion mass, they fit the EFT to experimental binding energies in the continuum and compute the finite-volume dependence for lattice sizes from 4 to 30 fm.

## Key numbers

- Di-neutron infinite-volume binding energy: 13.8 ± 1.8 MeV (EFT extrapolation), compared to NPLQCD's 15.9 ± 3.8 MeV from the largest lattice (L = 6.7 fm)
- Deuteron infinite-volume binding energy: 20.2 ± 2.3 MeV (EFT), compared to NPLQCD's 19.5 ± 4.8 MeV
- Triton infinite-volume binding energy: 58.2 ± 4.7 MeV (EFT), compared to NPLQCD's 53.9 ± 10.7 MeV
- ⁴He infinite-volume binding energy: 113 ± 10 MeV (EFT), compared to NPLQCD's 107 ± 24 MeV
- For physical pion mass, deuteron binding energy converges to infinite volume only at L ≳ 20 fm
- For physical pion mass, triton binding energy converges at L ≳ 12 fm
- For physical pion mass, ⁴He binding energy converges at L ≳ 8 fm

## Context

This work extends the approach of Barnea, Contessi, and Gazit (2015), who first applied pionless EFT to extrapolate LQCD results for larger nuclei, by focusing specifically on finite-volume extrapolation rather than extrapolation in baryon number. The method provides an alternative to the Luscher formalism (Luscher, 1986, 1991) for two-body systems and to the developing three-body finite-volume formalisms of Briceno, Davoudi, and collaborators (2013-2017), offering a technique applicable to arbitrary particle number. The results enable future LQCD calculations at physical pion mass to be performed on smaller lattices (saving computational cost) by using the EFT to correct for finite-volume effects, and the authors note that differences between their finite-volume predictions and those of Korber and Luu (2016) for small boxes could be used to probe the short-range part of the nuclear interaction.
