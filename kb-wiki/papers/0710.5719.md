# Shear Viscosity in a Gluon Gas

> The relation of the shear viscosity coefficient to the recently introduced transport rate is derived within relativistic kinetic theory. We calculate the shear viscosity over entropy ratio ╬Ě/s for a gluon gas, which involves elastic gg-> gg perturbative QCD (PQCD) scatterings as well as inelastic gg<->ggg PQCD bremsstrahlung. For ╬▒_s=0.3 we find ╬Ě/s=0.13 and for ╬▒_s=0.6, ╬Ě/s=0.076. The small ╬Ě/s values, which suggest strongly coupled systems, are due to the gluon bremsstrahlung incorporated.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy ratio ╬Ě/s for a gluon gas can be as low as 0.13 for ╬▒_s=0.3 and 0.076 for ╬▒_s=0.6 when including both elastic ggÔćĺgg and inelastic ggÔćöggg perturbative QCD bremsstrahlung, reaching values comparable to the AdS/CFT lower bound of 1/4¤Ç only at the larger coupling ╬▒_s=0.6, which is at the edge of perturbative QCD applicability.

## Method

The authors derived a formula for the shear viscosity coefficient within relativistic kinetic theory that relates ╬Ě to the total transport collision rate R^tr. They applied this formula to a thermal gluon gas treated as Boltzmann particles, calculating the transport collision rates for elastic ggÔćĺgg scatterings and inelastic ggÔćöggg bremsstrahlung using perturbative QCD matrix elements with Debye screening and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression, then obtained ╬Ě/s from the equilibrium limit.

## Key numbers

- ╬Ě/s = 0.13 for ╬▒_s = 0.3 when including both elastic and inelastic processes
- ╬Ě/s = 0.076 for ╬▒_s = 0.6 when including both elastic and inelastic processes
- ╬Ě/s = 1.03 for ggÔćĺgg only at ╬▒_s = 0.3, roughly a factor of 7 larger than the combined result
- Mean transport path l^tr = 0.32 fm at T = 400 MeV for ╬▒_s = 0.3
- Mean transport path l^tr = 0.2 fm at T = 400 MeV for ╬▒_s = 0.6

## Context

This work extends earlier perturbative QCD calculations that included only elastic ggÔćĺgg scatterings, which gave ╬Ě/s values much larger than the AdS/CFT lower bound (Arnold et al., 2000; Huovinen and Molnar, 2006) and could not explain the nearly perfect fluid behavior observed at RHIC. The authors build on their Boltzmann Approach of Multi-Parton Scatterings (BAMPS) framework (Xu and Greiner, 2005, 2007), which showed that ggÔćöggg bremsstrahlung is a factor of 5 more efficient for thermal equilibration than elastic scatterings. The results enable a perturbative QCD explanation for the small ╬Ě/s of the quark-gluon plasma created at RHIC, though the required coupling ╬▒_s=0.6 pushes the applicability of perturbative methods.
