# High precision studies of soft dipole mode in two-neutron halo nuclei: $^{6}$He case

> The "soft dipole" E1 strength function is calculated for the transition from the $^{6}$He $0^+$ ground state to the $1^-$ continuum $^{4}$He+$n$+$n$. The calculations were performed within the hyperspherical harmonics formalism. The sensitivity of the results to the $^{6}$He ground state structure and to final state interactions, are analyzed. The large-basis calculations show the reliably converged results for soft dipole strength function and for momentum correlations of the $^{6}\mbox{He} \rightarrow \, ^{4}$He+$n$+$n$ dissociation products. Transition mechanisms are analyzed based on the momentum correlations. The comparison with experimental data is provided.

## Key claim

The soft dipole mode in the three-body system $^{6}$He cannot be attributed entirely to initial-state geometry as in two-body haloes; continuum dynamics, which is an entangled mixture of resonant and nonresonant behavior, must be included for a decent approximation, and reliable convergence of the E1 strength function requires hyperspherical basis sizes up to $K_{\max}=101$ with the Feshbach reduction procedure.

## Method

The authors solved the inhomogeneous three-body Schrodinger equation within the hyperspherical harmonics formalism for the transition from the $^{6}$He $0^+$ ground state to the $1^-$ continuum of $^{4}$He+$n$+$n$. The ground state wave function was obtained from a homogeneous three-body model with phenomenological pairwise potentials (SBB for alpha-neutron, BJ or GPT for neutron-neutron) plus a short-range three-body potential to reproduce the experimental binding energy. The continuum wave function was computed by solving coupled differential equations with a source term from the E1 dipole operator, using the Feshbach reduction method to effectively increase the basis size up to $K_{\max}=101$ while keeping dynamical calculations at $K_{FR}=25$.

## Key numbers

- The ground state binding energy correction uses a three-body potential $V_3(\rho)$ with parameters $\rho_0 = 2.5$ fm and $d_3 = 0.4$ fm.
- The Schrodinger equations were solved up to $\rho_{\max}=400$ fm, with matching of momentum distributions performed at $\rho_{\max}=70-90$ fm.
- At $K_{\max}=25$ the number of channels reaches 260; complete convergence of the strength function is achieved at $K_{\max}=60-70$, with maximum basis size $K_{\max}=101$ used for safe results at $E_T > 0.4$ MeV.
- The low-energy part of the strength function below $E_T=1$ MeV is strongly dominated by the lowest hyperspherical component $\{K,\gamma\} = \{1\,1\,0\,0\,1\}$.
- The effective core charge for the dipole operator in $^{6}$He is $Z_{\text{eff}}^2 = e^2/3$.
- The alpha-neutron potential SBB uses $r_0=2.30$ fm, $V_c^{(0)}=50$ MeV, $V_c^{(1)}=-47.32$ MeV, $V_c^{(2)}=-23$ MeV, and $V_{ls}=-11.71$ MeV.
- The neutron-neutron potential BJ uses $V_0=-31$ MeV and $r_0=1.8$ fm.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of soft dipole excitations in $^{6}$He by methods including Danilin (1993), Cobis (1997), Myo (2001), de Diego (2010), Lay (2010), Descouvemont (2012), and Singh (2016), addressing shortcomings in convergence and providing fully converged results for the E1 strength function and well-converged results for three-body correlations. The only experimental data available for $^{6}$He dissociation are from Aumann (1999), which are over 20 years old and of limited quality; the authors call for new, detailed experimental data to make $^{6}$He a reference case for soft dipole mode studies in three-cluster systems. The work enables accurate treatment of the E1 strength function needed for extrapolating Coulomb dissociation cross sections to astrophysically relevant low energies, as discussed for the $^{4}$He+$n$+$n \rightarrow ^{6}$He+$\gamma$ process in a forthcoming paper by Grigorenko (2020b).
