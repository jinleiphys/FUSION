# Unified Extraction of In-Medium Heavy Quark Potentials from RHIC to LHC Energies via Deep Learning

> We use deep learning under Bayesian perspective to quantitatively extract the in-medium heavy quark (HQ) potential from bottomonium nuclear modification factors ($R_{AA}$) measured across multiple heavy ion collision systems at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC). The in-medium HQ potential, comprising both a real and imaginary part, is parameterized and incorporated into a time-dependent Schrödinger equation to model the wave function evolution of $b\bar{b}$ dipoles within a hydrodynamically evolving hot QCD medium. We construct Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to capture the non-linear correspondence between the heavy quark potential $V(T,r)$ and the bottomonium $R_{AA}$ for Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and 2.76 TeV, and Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV. Training datasets are generated by sampling the potential parameters and are further augmented using Principal Component Analysis (PCA) and Gaussian Process Regression (GPR). After validating the stability and correctness of the CNNs, we employ Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) to perform a simultaneous Bayesian inverse extraction of the optimal potential parameters and their posterior distributions using experimental data of bottomonium $R_{AA}$ in both LHC and RHIC energies. Our joint multi-energy extraction suggests that, within the present parametrization and hydrodynamic background, the real part of the in-medium potential remains close to the vacuum Cornell form, corresponding to a relatively weak screened Debye mass across RHIC to LHC energies. By contrast, the imaginary part is more strongly constrained by the data and provides the dominant contribution to bottomonium suppression from RHIC to LHC energies.

## Key claim

Within the specific parametrization and hydrodynamic background used, the real part of the in-medium heavy quark potential remains close to the vacuum Cornell form with a relatively weak screened Debye mass across RHIC to LHC energies, while the imaginary part provides the dominant contribution to bottomonium suppression and is more strongly constrained by experimental data.

## Method

The authors parameterize the in-medium heavy quark potential (real and imaginary parts) with six parameters and incorporate it into a time-dependent Schrödinger equation to model the wave function evolution of b-bbar dipoles within a hydrodynamically evolving QGP medium using the MUSIC package. They generate training datasets by sampling potential parameters and computing corresponding bottomonium R_AA values for Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and 2.76 TeV, and Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV, then augment the data using Principal Component Analysis and Gaussian Process Regression. Convolutional Neural Networks are trained to map potential parameters to R_AA observables, after which Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics performs Bayesian inverse extraction of optimal potential parameters and their posterior distributions using experimental bottomonium R_AA data.

## Key numbers

- Bottom quark mass set to m_b = 4.62 GeV for the Schrödinger equation
- Vacuum Cornell potential parameters: alpha = pi/12, sigma = 0.2 (GeV/c)^2
- Pseudo-critical temperature set to T_c = 0.17 GeV
- Switching temperature T_sw sampling range: [0.17, 0.25] GeV
- Debye mass parameter a_4 sampling range: [0.1, 1.0]
- Imaginary potential parameters sampling ranges: a_0 [1.0, 2.0], a_1 [0.0, 0.5], a_2 [0.2, 0.7], a_3 [2.0, 3.0]
- Maximum initial temperatures for most central collisions: 510 MeV (5.02 TeV Pb-Pb), 484 MeV (2.76 TeV Pb-Pb), 390 MeV (200 GeV Au-Au)
- Initialization time t_0 = 0.6 fm/c for all collision systems
- Training dataset generated from N=1000 samples of potential parameter vectors

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian inference studies that extracted diffusion coefficients (Xu 2017) and in-medium heavy quark potentials (Zheng 2025) from experimental heavy-flavor data, while incorporating deep neural networks to capture nonlinear mappings between potential parameters and observables. The joint multi-energy extraction using data from both RHIC and LHC simultaneously constrains the temperature and distance dependence of the complex heavy quark potential, enabling quantitative comparison with lattice QCD calculations (Burnier 2017) that yield distinct potentials depending on extraction methodology (Bala 2021).
