# Magneto-vortical Effect in Strong Magnetic Field

> We develop covariant chiral kinetic theory with Landau level basis. We use it to investigate a magnetized plasma with a transverse electric field and a steady vorticity as perturbations. After taking into account vacuum shift in the latter case, we find the resulting current and stress tensor in both cases can be matched consistently with constitutive equations of magnetohydrodynamics. We find the solution in the vorticity case contains both shifts in temperature and chemical potential as well as excitations of the lowest Landau level states. The solution gives rise to an vector charge density and axial current density. The vacuum parts coming from both shifts and excitations agree with previous studies and the medium parts coming entirely from excitations leads to a new contribution to vector charge and axial current density consistent with standard chiral vortical effect.

## Key claim

The paper develops a covariant chiral kinetic theory in the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation to show that, in a magnetized plasma with steady vorticity, the resulting vector charge density and axial current contain both a "vacuum" part (from shifts in temperature and chemical potential) and a "medium" part (from excitations of LLL states), with the medium part giving a new contribution consistent with the standard chiral vortical effect.

## Method

The authors construct a covariant chiral kinetic theory using a Landau level basis, starting from the two-point correlator of chiral fermions coupled to an external gauge field and performing a gradient expansion. They solve the kinetic equations to first order in gradients for two perturbed settings: a magnetized plasma with a transverse electric field (drift state) and a magnetized plasma with steady vorticity parallel to the magnetic field. The resulting current and stress tensor are matched to the constitutive equations of magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) to determine thermodynamic functions.

## Key numbers

- Vector charge density from vacuum shift: \(\Delta J_V^0 = q_f \frac{1}{4\pi^2} \mathbf{B} \cdot \boldsymbol{\omega}\) (Equation labeled mve)
- Axial current from vacuum shift: \(\Delta \mathbf{J}_A = |q_f| \frac{1}{4\pi^2} (\mathbf{B} \cdot \boldsymbol{\omega}) \hat{\mathbf{B}}\) (Equation labeled mve)
- Hall conductivity from drift solution: \(\sigma_H = \frac{\mu}{2\pi^2}\) (from \(\mathcal{J}_{(1)\mathcal{D}}^\mu\))
- Charge susceptibility from LLL density of states: \(\chi_\mu = \frac{B}{2\pi^2}\) (from \(\mathcal{N}_{(0)}\))
- Standard chiral vortical effect axial current: \(\Delta \mathbf{J}_A = \left( \frac{\mu^2 + \mu_5^2}{2\pi^2} + \frac{T^2}{6} \right) \boldsymbol{\omega}\) (Equation labeled CVE_standard)

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral magnetic and vortical effects in strong magnetic fields, particularly building on the proposal by Hattori and Yin (2016) that vorticity shifts LLL energies via spin-orbit coupling. It develops a covariant chiral kinetic theory framework (Lin, 2019) to treat both vacuum and medium contributions, and matches results with the magnetohydrodynamic constitutive relations of Hernandez (2017) and others. The approach enables systematic inclusion of medium effects beyond the vacuum shift, and the drift-state solution provides a cross-check for determining the magneto-vortical susceptibility \(M_\omega\).
