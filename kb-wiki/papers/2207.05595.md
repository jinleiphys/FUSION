# Energy Dependence of the Breit-Wheeler process in Heavy-Ion Collisions and its Application to Nuclear Charge Radius Measurements

> The collision energy dependence of the cross section and the transverse momentum distribution of dielectrons from the Breit-Wheeler process in heavy-ion collisions are computed in the lowest-order QED and found to be sensitive to the nuclear charge distribution and the infrared-divergence of the ultra-Lorentz boosted Coulomb field. Within a given experimental kinematic acceptance, the cross section is found to increase while the pair transverse momentum ($\sqrt{\langle p_{T}^{2} \rangle}$) decreases with increasing beam energy. We demonstrate that the transverse-momentum component of Weizsacker-Williams photons is due to the finite extent of the charge source and electric field component in the longitudinal direction. We further clarify the connection between the nuclear charge distribution and the kinematics of produced $e^+e^-$ from the Breit-Wheeler process, and propose a criterion for the validity of the Breit-Wheeler process in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Following this approach we demonstrate that the experimental measurements of the Breit-Wheeler process in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be used to quantitatively constrain the nuclear charge radius. The extracted parameters show sensitivity to the impact parameter dependence, and can be used to study the initial-state and final-state effects in hadronic interactions.

## Key claim

The cross section and transverse momentum distribution of dielectrons from the Breit-Wheeler process in heavy-ion collisions, computed in lowest-order QED, are sensitive to the nuclear charge distribution and the infrared-divergence of the ultra-Lorentz boosted Coulomb field; the authors demonstrate that experimental measurements of this process can quantitatively constrain the nuclear charge radius, but the extracted parameters show sensitivity to the impact parameter dependence and to the choice of neutron emission probability model.

## Method

The authors compute the cross section for electron-positron pair production via the Breit-Wheeler process in heavy-ion collisions using lowest-order QED with the Feynman diagram formalism of Hencken (1994) and Alscher (1996), assuming nuclei travel along straight-line trajectories with undeflected external fields. The nuclear charge distribution is modeled with a Woods-Saxon profile (radius 6.38 fm, skin depth 0.535 fm for gold), and the multi-dimensional integration over impact parameter and particle momenta is performed with the VEGAS Monte Carlo routine. The calculations incorporate experimental acceptance conditions from the STAR experiment (rapidity [-1,1], electron transverse momentum [0.2 GeV, 1.4 GeV], pair transverse momentum below 200 MeV) and include nuclear dissociation probabilities via both the Equivalent Photon Approximation method and a parameterized probability function.

## Key numbers

- Woods-Saxon radius for gold: 6.38 fm; skin depth: 0.535 fm (from fits to low-energy electron scattering data)
- The photon flux low-virtuality cutoff is approximately (2 MeV)^2 for STAR acceptance and collision energy; the high-virtuality cutoff is approximately (30 MeV)^2 from the form factor (1/R)^2
- The cross section and sqrt(<pT^2>) plateau sets in at beam energy around 100 GeV
- For a nuclear radius of 6.9 fm compared to 6.38 fm, the cross section and average pair transverse momentum both decrease, with ratios deviating from unity
- The extracted RMS charge radius for gold from the combined UPC+MB analysis with XnXn neutron selection and default sigma_NN=41.6 mb is 5.67 fm, with uncertainties +0.03 -0.09 fm (compared to the default value of 5.33 fm from R=6.38 fm, d=0.535 fm)

## Context

This work extends the generalized Equivalent Photon Approximation and Wigner function formalism developed by Brandenburg (2021), Zha (2018), and Klusek-Gawenda (2020) that successfully described the impact-parameter dependence of the Breit-Wheeler process in heavy-ion collisions. The authors clarify that the transverse momentum of Weizsacker-Williams photons arises from the finite projection of the electric field along the beam direction (proportional to omega/gamma) rather than directly from the nuclear transverse size via the uncertainty principle, contradicting the common assumption in Baltz (2007), Klein (2017), and Bertulani (1987). The results enable quantitative extraction of nuclear charge radii from Breit-Wheeler measurements at RHIC and LHC, and can be used to study initial-state and final-state effects in hadronic interactions by comparing peripheral and ultra-peripheral collisions.
