# Microscopic Nuclear Structure Based upon a Chiral NN Potential

> We report on shell-model calculations employing effective interactions derived from a new realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential based on chiral effective field theory. We present results for 18O, 134Te, and 210Po. Our results are in excellent agreement with experiment indicating a remarkable predictive power of the chiral NN potential for low-energy microscopic nuclear structure.

# Microscopic Nuclear Structure Based upon a Chiral NN Potential

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations using effective interactions derived from the chiral Idaho-B NN potential reproduce experimental spectra and binding energies for 18O, 134Te, and 210Po with an accuracy comparable to that obtained from modern meson-theoretic potentials such as CD-Bonn, demonstrating that the chiral potential is a valid input for microscopic nuclear structure calculations.

## Method

The authors derived a low-momentum NN potential (Vlow-k) from the chiral Idaho-B NN potential by integrating out high-momentum components using an iterative method analogous to a renormalization group transformation. They then calculated shell-model effective interactions from Vlow-k using the Q-box plus folded diagram method, and performed shell-model calculations for three two-valence-particle nuclei spanning light, medium, and heavy mass regions: 18O, 134Te, and 210Po.

## Key numbers

- The chiral Idaho-B NN potential reproduces NN data below 210 MeV with a chi-squared per datum of 0.98.
- The rms deviation between calculated and experimental excitation energies is 140 keV for 18O, 111 keV for 134Te, and 86 keV for 210Po.
- The calculated ground-state binding energy for 18O is 12.19 MeV, matching the experimental value of 12.19 +/- 0.00 MeV.
- The calculated ground-state binding energy for 134Te is 20.64 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 20.56 +/- 0.03 MeV.
- The calculated ground-state binding energy for 210Po is 8.78 MeV, matching the experimental value of 8.78 +/- 0.00 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the program of deriving nuclear forces from chiral effective field theory, initiated by Weinberg (1990) and developed by Ordonez and van Kolck (1992, 1994) and others, to shell-model calculations across a wide mass range. The authors use the Vlow-k renormalization approach of Bogner, Kuo, and Coraggio (2001) to handle the chiral potential. The results enable future microscopic nuclear structure studies using chiral potentials as a first-principles alternative to meson-theoretic potentials like CD-Bonn, with the same level of predictive power for low-energy nuclear properties.
