# Next-to-the-Leading-Order Hadronic Corrections for Standard Model and Beyond

> We explore various extensions of computational packages such as Feynarts and FormCalc in application to calculation of the electron-proton scattering asymmetries relevant to the G0 and QWeak experiments. Our calculations where completed up to the Next-to-the-Leading-Order (one-loop) using the dipole form factor in hadronic vertices. We saw no significant difference between monopole and dipole type of asymmetry over the range of Q^2 = 0.1 - 1.0 GeV^2. Our results are in the good agreement with theoretical predictions by the G0 collaboration, but show discrepancy from the experiment. This discrepancy from the experimental results is due to the strange content of the proton and possibly new physics as well.

## Key claim

The authors show that their NLO (one-loop) calculations of parity-violating electron-proton scattering asymmetries, using a dipole form factor in hadronic vertices, agree with the G0 collaboration's theoretical predictions but disagree with experimental results; they attribute this discrepancy to the strange content of the proton and possibly new physics, noting that the strange quark contribution was explicitly excluded from their calculations.

## Method

The authors extended the FeynArts and FormCalc packages to handle electron-nucleon scattering at NLO by implementing Dirac and Pauli form factors in monopole and dipole approximations for the hadronic vertices. They performed calculations in the on-shell renormalization scheme using Feynman gauge, treated infrared divergences with both soft and hard photon bremsstrahlung, and numerically differentiated monopole-type asymmetries to obtain dipole-type results.

## Key numbers

- Asymmetry prediction for the QWeak experiment at Q^2 = 0.03 GeV^2: A = -0.265 ± 0.007 ppm, with uncertainty from different cuts on emitted hard-photon-bremsstrahlung photon energy.
- Calculations were performed over the momentum transfer range Q^2 = 0.1 - 1.0 GeV^2 for comparison with the G0 experiment.
- The monopole form factor parameter was set to Λ^2 = 0.83 m_N^2, obtained from a fit of electromagnetic form factors in the low momentum transfer region.

## Context

This work extends the automated NLO calculation methods of FeynArts and FormCalc into the hadronic electroweak sector, building on prior theoretical frameworks for parity-violating electron-proton scattering asymmetries used by the G0 collaboration. The results enable comparison with upcoming QWeak experimental data to test for new physics beyond the Standard Model, and the authors plan to develop advanced symbolic computational methods for studying production and decay channels in hadronic physics with higher precision.
