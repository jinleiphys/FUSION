# Relativistic Dissipative Fluid Dynamics from Non-Equilibrium Statistical Operator

> We present a new derivation of second-order relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics for quantum systems using Zubarev's formalism for the non-equilibrium statistical operator. In particular, we discuss the shear-stress tensor to second order in gradients and argue that the relaxation terms for the dissipative quantities arise from memory effects contained in the statistical operator. We also identify new transport coefficients which describe the relaxation of dissipative processes to second order and express them in terms of equilibrium correlation functions, thus establishing Kubo-type formulae for the second-order transport coefficients.

## Key claim

The paper derives second-order relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics for quantum systems using Zubarev's non-equilibrium statistical operator formalism, showing that relaxation terms for dissipative quantities arise from memory effects contained in the statistical operator rather than from nonlinear couplings alone.

## Method

The authors expand the non-equilibrium statistical operator (NESO) around the local-equilibrium state to second order in gradients of thermodynamic parameters (temperature, chemical potential, and fluid 4-velocity). They compute statistical averages of the shear-stress tensor, bulk viscous pressure, and diffusion current operators using two-point and three-point correlation functions, expressing the resulting transport coefficients in terms of equilibrium correlation functions via Kubo-type formulae.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity relaxation time tau_pi is related to the frequency derivative of the shear viscosity via eta * tau_pi = -i * d(eta(omega))/d(omega) evaluated at omega = 0, which equals (1/10) * d^2/d(omega)^2 Re[G^R_{pi_{mu nu} pi^{mu nu}}(omega)] at omega = 0
- The second-order transport coefficient lambda_pi is given by (12/35) * beta^2 * integral of the three-point correlation function (pi_mu^nu(x), pi_nu^lambda(x1), pi_lambda^mu(x2))
- The coefficient lambda_{pi Pi} describing shear-bulk coupling equals -(beta^2/5) * integral of (pi_{mu nu}(x), pi^{mu nu}(x1), p^*(x2))
- The coefficient lambda_{pi J} describing shear-diffusion coupling equals (1/5) * integral of (pi_{mu nu}(x), J^mu(x1), J^nu(x2))

## Context

This work extends the NESO method previously applied to quantum fields by Huang et al. (2011) and to systems in strong magnetic fields, providing a systematic derivation of second-order fluid dynamics that avoids the acausality of first-order Navier-Stokes theory (Eckart 1940, Landau and Lifshitz). The results enable calculation of second-order transport coefficients for strongly correlated matter such as the quark-gluon plasma using equilibrium finite-temperature field theory methods, and provide a framework for comparing with alternative formulations by Baier et al. (2008) and Romatschke (2010).
