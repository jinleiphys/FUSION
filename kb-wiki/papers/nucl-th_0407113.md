# Determination of low energy parameters for NN-scattering at $N^4 LO$ in all partial waves with $j\le 5$

> The Variable S-matrix approach offers a unique way to extract low energy threshold parameters for a given NN potential. We extract those parameters for the np system from the NijmII and Reid93 potentials, to all partial waves with total angular momentum $j \le m5 $, using the generalized effective range expansion % $$ ({\bf f}^{sj})_{l,l'} k^l k^{l'} = -({{\bf a}^{sj}}^{-1})_{l,l'} + \frac12 ({\bf r}^{sj})_{l,l'} k^2 + ({\bf v_2}^{sj})_{l,l'} k^4 + + ({\bf v_3}^{sj})_{l,l'} k^6 + + ({\bf v_4}^{sj})_{l,l'} k^8 + ... - {\rm i} k^{l+l'+1} $$ % where $ {\bf f}^{sj} = ({\bf S}^{sj} - {\bf 1}) / (2 {\rm i} k) $ is the scattering amplitude and ${\bf S}^{sj}$ is the unitary S-matrix in coupled channel space with total spin $s$ and total angular momentum $j$. Our calculation includes all the relevant contributions of the full amplitude to order ${\cal O} (k^8) $ in the CM momentum. We also discuss the validity of the generalized effective range expansion in the region of analyticity $ k \le m_π/2$.

## Key claim

The authors extract low-energy threshold parameters (scattering lengths, effective ranges, and curvature parameters up to order \(k^8\)) for the neutron-proton (np) system from the NijmII and Reid93 potentials, for all partial waves with total angular momentum \(j \le 5\), using the generalized effective range expansion (ERE) within the variable S-matrix approach. The abstract glosses over the fact that the results are derived directly from two specific high-quality phenomenological potentials, not from experimental data or from a chiral effective field theory (EFT) fit, and that the expansion is valid only up to the analyticity limit \(k \le m_\pi/2 \approx 70\ \text{MeV}\).

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation for the np system using the variable S-matrix (or variable phase) method, which yields the S-matrix by integrating a first-order nonlinear differential equation from the origin to infinity with trivial initial conditions. They then expand the scaled M-matrix (related to the S-matrix) in powers of the center-of-mass momentum \(k\) up to order \(k^8\), obtaining a system of coupled differential equations for the running scattering length, effective range, and curvature parameters. These equations are integrated numerically for the NijmII and Reid93 potentials (taken from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis database) to obtain the asymptotic (physical) threshold parameters for all partial waves with \(j \le 5\), including both uncoupled and coupled channels.

## Key numbers

* \(^1S_0\) scattering length: \(\alpha = -23.727\ \text{fm}\) (NijmII) and \(-23.735\ \text{fm}\) (Reid93); effective range: \(r = 2.670\ \text{fm}\) (NijmII) and \(2.753\ \text{fm}\) (Reid93).
* \(^3S_1\) eigen-channel scattering length: \(\alpha_{3S1} = 5.41896\ \text{fm}\) (NijmII) and \(5.42293\ \text{fm}\) (Reid93); effective range: \(r_{3S1} = 1.75334\ \text{fm}\) (NijmII) and \(1.75556\ \text{fm}\) (Reid93).
* \(^3S_1\) eigen-channel curvature parameters: \(v_{3S1} = 0.04531\ \text{fm}^3\) (NijmII) and \(0.03266\ \text{fm}^3\) (Reid93); \(v'_{3S1} = 0.65831\ \text{fm}^5\) (NijmII) and \(0.65797\ \text{fm}^5\) (Reid93); \(v''_{3S1} = -4.19144\ \text{fm}^7\) (NijmII) and \(-4.19262\ \text{fm}^7\) (Reid93).
* For the \(^3P_0\) channel: scattering length \(\alpha = -2.468\ \text{fm}\) (NijmII) and \(-2.469\ \text{fm}\) (Reid93); effective range \(r = 3.914\ \text{fm}\) (NijmII) and \(3.870\ \text{fm}\) (Reid93).
* For the \(^1P_1\) channel: scattering length \(\alpha = 2.797\ \text{fm}\) (NijmII) and \(2.736\ \text{fm}\) (Reid93); effective range \(r = -6.399\ \text{fm}\) (NijmII) and \(-6.606\ \text{fm}\) (Reid93).
* For the \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) coupled channel (E1): scattering length \(\alpha = 1.647\ \text{fm}\) (NijmII) and \(1.645\ \text{fm}\) (Reid93); effective range \(r = 0.404\ \text{fm}\) (NijmII) and \(0.413\ \text{fm}\) (Reid93).
* The expansion is carried out to order \(k^8\), requiring 60 independent parameters for all partial waves with \(j \le 5\).

## Context

This work extends the determination of low-energy threshold parameters beyond the central (\(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1-^3D_1\)) channels, which had been previously computed for high-quality potentials by deSwart et al. (1995). The authors note that a direct fit to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis database for these parameters is numerically unreliable, so they instead extract them from the NijmII and Reid93 potentials using the variable S-matrix method. The results provide a complete set of ERE parameters up to \(k^8\) for all partial waves with \(j \le 5\), which can serve as input for chiral effective field theory (EFT) calculations (e.g., those by Epelbaum et al., 2004) to determine low-energy constants (LECs) without needing to fit to the full database. The work also enables studies of the validity of the ERE up to the pion mass threshold and the analytic structure of the S-matrix (e.g., the \(^1S_0\) virtual state and the deuteron bound state).
