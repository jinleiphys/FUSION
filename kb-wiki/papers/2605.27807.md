# Light nuclear scattering from neural quantum states

> We present a method of studying few-body nuclear scattering by means of neural quantum states, without requiring time-evolution. A recently developed family of stable minimum principles for Schrodinger's equation provides conservative uncertainties on cross sections and partial wave amplitudes computed in this way. We use this method to study both elastic and inelastic neutron-deuteron scattering with realistic nuclear two-body forces.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for computing few-body nuclear scattering observables using neural quantum states without time evolution, but the method's practical utility is limited by the need for an accurate bound-state wavefunction (here the deuteron) and by the fact that the error bounds from the minimum principle require knowledge of the true scattering state's maximum amplitude, which is generally unavailable a priori.

## Method

The authors train neural networks to represent approximate scattering states that satisfy fixed ingoing boundary conditions (plane waves for nucleon-nucleon scattering; a neutron and deuteron wavepacket for neutron-deuteron scattering) and minimize the L1 norm of the Schrödinger equation violation over sampled spatial points. For neutron-deuteron scattering, the loss functional is modified to subtract terms involving the approximate deuteron wavefunction, ensuring the integral remains finite despite the inexact bound state. The asymptotic outgoing wave is parameterized either by a neural network on the sphere or by a truncated spherical harmonic expansion (up to l=2 for neutron-proton scattering), and cross sections or phase shifts are extracted from the learned asymptotic function.

## Key numbers

- For single-particle scattering from a hard sphere (V0=1, r0=1, mass M=1), the cross section is computed across a range of incident energies; the L1 loss provides a linear bound on the cross-section error with coefficient estimated from the slope of loss versus error at energy E=1.
- For neutron-proton scattering with the Argonne v6' potential, the 1S0 phase shift is computed as a function of lab energy; error bars are estimated as twice the deviation of the partial-wave amplitude magnitude from unity (unitarity violation).
- For neutron-deuteron elastic scattering in the quartet channel at Elab = 2 MeV, the cross section is computed for various window sizes r0 (approximately 5 fm gives optimal tradeoff); the training history shows convergence of the loss over iterations.

## Context

This work extends the neural quantum state approach from bound-state calculations (Adams 2020, Gnech 2021, Parnes 2025) to scattering problems, building on a recently developed minimum principle for Schrödinger's equation (Lawrence 2026). The method is demonstrated on neutron-deuteron scattering, which is otherwise accessible via Faddeev equations (Phillips 1966, Suslov 2013) and other ab initio approaches including Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations, quantum Monte Carlo, shell model, and hyperspherical harmonics; the authors note their approach does not outperform these existing methods but may generalize to larger A.
