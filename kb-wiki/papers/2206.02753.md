# Relation to a property of the angular momentum zero space of states of four fermions in an angular momentum $j = 9/2$ shell unexpectedly found to be stationary for any rotationally invariant two-body interaction

> The existence of states with angular momenta $I = 4$ and~6 of four fermions in an angular momentum $j = 9/2$ shell that are stationary for any rotationally invariant two-body interaction despite the presence of other states with the same angular momentum, the Escuderos-Zamick states, is shown to be equivalent to the invariance to any such interaction of the span of states generated from $I = 0$ states by one-body operators. This invariance is verified by exact calculation independently of previous verifications of the equivalent statement. It explains the occurrence of the Escuderos-Zamick states for just $I = 4$ and 6. The action of an arbitrary interaction on the invariant space and its orthogonal complement is analyzed, leading to a relation of the Escuderos-Zamick energy levels to levels with $I = 10$ and 12. Aspects of the observed spectra of $^{94}$Ru, $^{96}$Pd, and $^{74}$Ni are discussed in the light of this relation.

## Key claim

The existence of Escuderos-Zamick states (solvable multiplets with angular momenta I = 4 and 6 for four fermions in a j = 9/2 shell) is equivalent to the invariance of the space Φ₄ (states generated from I = 0 states by one-body operators) to any rotationally invariant two-body interaction, which explains why solvable multiplets occur only for I = 4 and 6.

## Method

The author constructs explicit basis states for the I = 0 space Φ₀ (two-dimensional) and the M_I = 0 subspace Φ₄₀ of Φ₄, then verifies by exact calculation that Φ₄₀ and its orthogonal complement Φ₄₀^⊥ are invariant under each of the five basic two-body interactions V_J (J = 0, 2, 4, 6, 8) by applying the interaction operators to basis states and checking that the results remain within the respective spaces. The action of each V_J on the 4 basis states of Φ₄₀^⊥ and 14 basis states of Φ₄₀ is displayed in matrix form.

## Key numbers

- The I = 0 space Φ₀ is 2-dimensional; two linearly independent solutions φ₀ and φ₁ are given explicitly, with φ₀ having seniority v = 0 and φ₀ + 2φ₁ having v = 4.
- The space Φ₄₀ is 14-dimensional, spanned by 5 states n_m φ₀ (m = 9/2, 7/2, 5/2, 3/2, 1/2) and 9 states n_m φ₁ (m = 9/2, 7/2, ..., -7/2).
- The orthogonal complement Φ₄₀^⊥ is 4-dimensional, spanned by states χ₁, χ₂, χ₃, χ₄ given explicitly.
- For the pairing force V₀, the matrix on Φ₄₀^⊥ is the zero matrix.
- The ratios r^J_I = μ^J_I/ν_J (eigenvalues of V_J in the four-fermion system relative to two-fermion states) are confirmed: for I = 4, r^J_4 = 68/33, 1, 13/15, 114/55 for J = 2, 4, 6, 8; for I = 6, r^J_6 = 19/11, 12/13, 1, 336/143 for J = 2, 4, 6, 8; additionally r^J_10 = 23/33, 98/143, 233/165, 2292/715 and r^J_12 = 0, 75/143, 93/55, 246/65 for J = 2, 4, 6, 8.
- For ⁹⁴Ru, yrast I = 4, 6, 10, 12 levels have excitation energies 2186.6, 2498.0, 3991.2, and 4716.6 keV; extrapolation by the spacing rule from I = 10 and 12 gives Escuderos-Zamick levels at 2571.9 and 2918.9 keV.
- For ⁹⁶Pd, extrapolation predicts Escuderos-Zamick levels at 2237.9 and 2616.8 keV, compared to yrast levels at 2099.01 and 2424.19 keV.
- For ⁷⁴Ni, interpreting the second observed I = 4 and 6 levels as Escuderos-Zamick levels leads to predicted I = 10 and 12 levels at 4287 and 5577 keV.

## Context

This work extends the numerical discovery by Escuderos and Zamick (2006) of solvable multiplets for four fermions in a j = 9/2 shell, which was subsequently confirmed by Van Isacker and Heinze (2008, 2014) and Qi, Xu, and Liotta (2012) through case-by-case calculations. The author provides the first explanation for why solvable multiplets occur only for I = 4 and 6, by showing equivalence to a property of the I = 0 state space. The analysis reveals a regularity in the action of interactions on Φ₄₀^⊥ that leads to a spacing rule relating Escuderos-Zamick levels to I = 10 and 12 levels, which is applied to experimental spectra of ⁹⁴Ru, ⁹⁶Pd, and ⁷⁴Ni. The origin of this regularity remains unexplained and may point toward a more intuitive understanding of the invariance.
