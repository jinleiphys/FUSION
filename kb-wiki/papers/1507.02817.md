# The Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model and its symmetry

> The hypercentral CQM, which is inspired by Lattice QCD calculations for quark-antiquark potentials, is presented, stressing its underlying symmetry. Its results for the spectrum, the helicity amplitudes and the elastic form factors are briefly reported. # The Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model and its symmetry

## Key claim

The hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM), which uses a hyperradius-dependent three-quark potential inspired by lattice QCD, can reproduce the baryon spectrum and, when relativistic corrections are included, produces a deviation from the standard dipole behaviour of the proton electric-to-magnetic form factor ratio for the first time in a quark model framework, in agreement with recent Jefferson Lab data.

## Method

The authors solve the three-body Schrödinger equation using hyperspherical coordinates, where the hyperradius x replaces the Jacobi coordinates ρ and λ. The quark interaction is taken as a hypercentral potential V(x) = −τ/x + αx (Coulomb-like plus linear confinement), with the hyperradial equation solved numerically. For electromagnetic transitions, the transverse helicity amplitudes are computed using a non-relativistic quark current operator, while for elastic form factors relativistic corrections are introduced by boosting to a common Breit frame and expanding the quark current to lowest order in quark momentum.

## Key numbers

- The hypercentral CQM contains only three free parameters, fitted to reproduce the experimental baryon spectrum.
- The hyperCoulomb potential energy levels are given by E = −mτ² / [2(C₂(O(n+1)) + ((n−1)/2)²)], where n is the spatial dimension.
- The ratio R = μₚ Gₑ/Gₘ calculated with the hCQM including relativistic kinematical corrections reaches a value of about 0.6, in agreement with Jefferson Lab data up to 4 GeV².
- In the Isgur-Karl model, the harmonic oscillator constant 1/α ≃ 0.5 fm is adjusted to reproduce the A₃/₂ amplitude for the D₁₃ resonance at the photon point.

## Context

This work extends the hypercentral approach to constituent quark models, building on the algebraic u(7) model of Iachello, Bijker and Leviatan and on earlier hypercentral approximations. The hCQM improves upon the Isgur-Karl model (which uses a harmonic oscillator basis with a shifting two-body potential) and the analytical hypercentral model (which treats linear confinement as a perturbation). The results enable parameter-free predictions for helicity amplitudes and show that relativistic effects, rather than other mechanisms, explain the deviation from dipole behaviour seen in Jefferson Lab experiments. The model also reproduces the S₁₁(1650) amplitude, providing a test of SU(6) violation since without configuration mixing that amplitude would be exactly zero.
