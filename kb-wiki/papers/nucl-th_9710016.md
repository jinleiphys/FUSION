# Electromagnetic and Strong Decays in a Collective Model of the Nucleon

> We present an analysis of electromagnetic elastic form factors, helicity amplitudes and strong decay widths of non-strange baryon resonances, within a collective model of the nucleon. Flavor-breaking and stretching effects are considered. Deviations from the naive three-constituents description are pointed out.

## Key claim

Within a collective model where baryons are treated as rotations and vibrations of a Y-shaped string (an oblate top), the authors show that while the model can reproduce the non-zero neutron electric form factor by breaking spin-flavor symmetry, this breaking worsens the fit to the proton electric and neutron magnetic form factors, suggesting that additional contributions such as meson cloud coupling are needed in the low-\(Q^2\) region.

## Method

The authors model nucleon and delta resonances as collective rotations and vibrations of a Y-shaped string configuration with an oblate-top shape, using a normalized charge and magnetization distribution \(g(\beta) = \beta^2 e^{-\beta/a} / 2a^3\). Electromagnetic and strong transition operators are assumed to involve absorption or emission from a single constituent, and collective form factors are obtained by folding matrix elements of these operators with the probability distribution. Flavor-breaking is introduced via separate scale parameters \(a_u\) and \(a_d\) for up and down quarks, and stretching effects are included by making the scale parameter energy-dependent as \(a = a_0 (1 + \xi (W-M)/M)\).

## Key numbers

- Scale parameter from \(SU_{sf}(6)\)-symmetric fit: \(a = 0.232\) fm, with \(\mu_u = \mu_d = \mu_p = 2.793\ \mu_N\) (0.127 GeV\(^{-1}\)).
- With spin-flavor symmetry broken: \(a_u = 0.230\) fm, \(a_d = 0.257\) fm, \(\mu_u = 2.777\ \mu_N\) (0.126 GeV\(^{-1}\)), \(\mu_d = 2.915\ \mu_N\) (0.133 GeV\(^{-1}\)).
- Calculated \(N\pi\) decay width for \(N(1520)D_{13}\): 115 MeV (experimental: \(67 \pm 9\) MeV).
- Calculated \(N\pi\) decay width for \(N(1535)S_{11}\): 85 MeV (experimental: \(79 \pm 38\) MeV).
- Calculated \(N\eta\) decay width for \(N(1535)S_{11}\): 0.1 MeV (experimental: \(74 \pm 39\) MeV).
- Calculated \(N\eta\) decay width for \(N(1650)S_{11}\): 8 MeV (experimental: \(11 \pm 6\) MeV).
- Discrepancies noted in the low-\(Q^2\) region \(0 \leq Q^2 \leq 1\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the collective model of Bijker, Iachello, and Leviatan (1994) by simultaneously analyzing electromagnetic elastic form factors, helicity amplitudes, and strong decay widths of non-strange baryon resonances. It contrasts with non-relativistic quark models (Isgur and Karl, 1978-1979) and relativized quark models (Capstick and Isgur, 1986), which treat baryons as single-particle excitations in a confining potential, whereas the collective model spreads wave functions over many oscillator shells. The results enable future studies of meson cloud contributions (Iachello, Jackson, and Lande, 1973) and suggest that the large \(\eta\) width of \(N(1535)S_{11}\) may require configurations beyond the conventional \(q^3\) picture, such as quasi-bound meson-baryon resonances (Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise, 1995) or exotic \(q^4\bar{q}\) states.
