# Dilepton production from parton interactions in the early stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We address the dilepton production from the parton interactions in the early stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions within the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) off-shell transport approach. The description of partons in PHSD is based on the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) matched to reproduce lattice QCD results in thermodynamic equilibrium. According to the DQPM the constituents of the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) are massive and off-shell quasi-particles (quarks and gluons) with broad spectral functions. In order to address the electromagnetic radiation of the sQGP, we derive off-shell cross sections of q+qbar->gamma*, q+qbar->gamma*+g and q+g->gamma*+q (qbar+g->gamma*+qbar) reactions taking into account the effective propagators for quarks and gluons from the DQPM. Dilepton production in In+In collisions at 158 AGeV and in Au+Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV is calculated by implementing these off-shell processes into the PHSD transport approach. By comparing to the data of the NA60 and PHENIX Collaborations, we study the relative importance of different dilepton production mechanisms and point out the regions in phase space where partonic channels are dominant.

## Key claim

The partonic channels (quark-antiquark annihilation, gluon Compton scattering, and annihilation with gluon bremsstrahlung) dominate the dilepton yield in the intermediate mass range (M > 1 GeV) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but only when off-shell cross sections derived from the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) are used; the hadronic channels alone underestimate the PHENIX data by approximately a factor of two in the mass range from 1 to 4 GeV, and the partonic contribution fills this gap.

## Method

The authors derived off-shell cross sections for the elementary partonic processes q + qbar → γ*, q + qbar → γ* + g, and q + g → γ* + q (and the charge-conjugate reactions) using effective quark and gluon propagators from the DQPM, which reproduces lattice QCD thermodynamics. These off-shell cross sections were implemented into the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) off-shell transport approach, which solves generalized Kadanoff-Baym equations for Green's functions. The resulting dilepton spectra were calculated for In+In collisions at 158 AGeV and Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV and compared to data from the NA60 and PHENIX Collaborations.

## Key numbers

- In Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, the hadronic channels (HSD) underestimate the measured dilepton yield in the invariant mass range from 0.2 to 0.6 GeV by approximately a factor of 5.
- In the mass range from 1 to 4 GeV, the hadronic channels (HSD) underestimate the PHENIX data by approximately a factor of two.
- The dileptons generated by quark-antiquark annihilation in the sQGP constitute about half of the observed yield in the mass range between the φ meson mass and the J/ψ meson mass.
- For M > 2.5 GeV, the partonic yield dominates over the D-meson contribution.
- The off-shell Born cross section was computed for various quark mass combinations, e.g., m1 = 0.3 GeV, m2 = 0.3 GeV (dash-dotted red line) and m1 = 0.6 GeV, m2 = 0.6 GeV (dash-dot-dot green line).

## Context

This work extends the earlier HSD transport calculations (Bratkovskaya 2008, Cassing and Bratkovskaya 2009) by incorporating explicit partonic degrees of freedom and off-shell partonic cross sections into the PHSD framework. It addresses the discrepancy between hadronic-only models and the NA60 data for In+In at 158 AGeV (which favored a "melting ρ" scenario) and the PHENIX data for Au+Au at √s = 200 GeV. The results enable quantitative separation of hadronic and partonic contributions to dilepton spectra, showing that partonic sources dominate above 1 GeV and that the low-mass discrepancy (0.2–0.6 GeV) in Au+Au remains unexplained even when partonic radiation is included.
