# Charmonium in Medium: From Correlators to Experiment

> We set up a framework in which in-medium charmonium properties are constrained by thermal lattice QCD and subsequently implemented into a thermal rate equation enabling the comparison with experimental data in heavy-ion collisions. Specifically, we evaluate phenomenological consequences for charmonium production originating from two different scenarios in which either the free or the internal energy are identified with the in-medium 2-body potential between charm and anti-charm quarks. These two scenarios represent $J/ψ$ "melting temperatures" of approximately 1.25\,$T_c$ ("weak binding") and 2\,$T_c$ ("strong binding"), respectively. Within current uncertainties in dissociation rates and charm-quark momentum spectra, both scenarios can reproduce the centrality dependence of inclusive $J/ψ$ yields in nuclear collisions at SPS and RHIC reasonably well. However, the "strong-binding" scenario associated the the internal energy as the potential tends to better reproduce current data on transverse momentum spectra at both SPS and RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a framework connecting in-medium charmonium properties from thermal lattice QCD to experimental observables in heavy-ion collisions, testing two scenarios where either the free energy or the internal energy is identified as the in-medium heavy-quark potential. Both scenarios can reproduce the centrality dependence of inclusive J/ψ yields at SPS and RHIC within current uncertainties, but the "strong-binding" scenario (internal energy as potential) tends to better reproduce transverse momentum spectra at both facilities.

## Method

The authors extract temperature-dependent charm-quark masses, charmonium binding energies, and dissociation temperatures from a thermodynamic T-matrix potential model using both free and internal energies as the in-medium potential. They then construct in-medium charmonium spectral functions using a relativistic Breit-Wigner plus continuum ansatz, constrained by lattice QCD correlator ratios, and implement the resulting properties into a thermal rate equation that accounts for dissociation and regeneration in an expanding fireball background. The dissociation rates are calculated using a quasifree approximation with perturbative QCD, and cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing, Cronin effect, nuclear absorption) are included via a Glauber model.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ "melting temperatures": approximately 1.25 Tc (weak-binding scenario using free energy) and 2.0 Tc (strong-binding scenario using internal energy)
- χc dissociation temperatures: 1.3 Tc (strong-binding) and 1.0 Tc (weak-binding)
- Effective strong coupling constant αs ≈ 0.3 used in quasifree dissociation rates
- J/ψ dissociation rate in strong-binding scenario at T ≈ 200 MeV is approximately 5 times smaller than χc rate
- Cronin effect coefficient a_gN = 0.076 GeV²/fm for SPS Pb-Pb at √s = 17.3 AGeV
- Cronin effect coefficient a_gN = 0.1 GeV²/fm (mid rapidity) and 0.2 GeV²/fm (forward rapidity) for RHIC Au-Au at √s = 200 AGeV
- J/ψ-nucleon absorption cross section σ_abs = 7.3 ± 1 mb at SPS (√s = 17.3 GeV)
- J/ψ-nucleon absorption cross section σ_abs ≈ 3.5 mb (mid rapidity) and 5.5 mb (forward rapidity) at RHIC (√s = 200 AGeV)
- pp J/ψ production cross section dσ_pp^Ψ/dy = 37 nb at SPS (√s = 17.3 AGeV)
- pp J/ψ production cross section dσ_pp^Ψ/dy = 750 nb (mid rapidity) and 500 nb (forward rapidity) at RHIC (√s = 200 AGeV)
- pp charm-quark cross section dσ_cc̄/dy (y=0) = 2.2 μb at SPS
- Vacuum open-charm threshold s0 = 2m_D = 3.74 GeV
- Continuum rescaling factors: B_J/ψ ≈ 2 (vector channel), B_χc ≈ 4 (scalar channel)

## Context

This work extends prior potential-model studies of charmonium in medium (Mocsy 2007, Wong 2006) and kinetic rate equation approaches (Grandchamp 2003, Zhao 2007) by establishing a quantitative connection between lattice QCD constraints and experimental observables. The T-matrix approach of Riek (2010) provides the spectral functions used as input. The framework enables systematic comparison of the free-energy versus internal-energy potential scenarios against SPS and RHIC data, and can be extended to LHC energies and to other heavy-quark systems such as bottomonium.
