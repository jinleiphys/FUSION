# Evaluation of $^{86}$Kr Cross Sections For Use in Fusion Diagnostics

> The National Ignition Facility at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory uses $^{86}$Kr as a diagnostic tool to measure the neutron flux produced by fusion reactions. As krypton is chemically inert, it can be implanted directly into the fuel capsule, and the reaction products can be measured to determine the flux of fusion neutrons. $^{86}$Kr cross sections also provide model constraints for the $^{85}$Kr branching point in the s-process and the neutron flux in stars. In this work, experimental data on the neutron production, radiative capture, inelastic scattering, and total cross sections of $^{86}$Kr were used in conjunction with the fast region nuclear reaction code EMPIRE and a new resonance-region evaluation to produce a new evaluation of neutron-induced reactions on $^{86}$Kr. For the EMPIRE calculations, we fitted the optical model potential up to 12 MeV to simultaneously reproduce the experimental data for the total cross section and the main inelastic gamma transition from the $2^+$ state to the $0^+$ ground state. For energies above 12 MeV, due to large fluctuations and uncertainties in the total cross section data, we preferred to adopt the Koning-Delaroche global spherical optical model potential. With these models and corrections to the structure of $^{86}$Kr, the evaluated cross sections matched the experimental data. The new evaluation has been submitted for incorporation in the next release of the ENDF/B nuclear reaction library.

## Key claim
The new evaluation of neutron-induced reactions on $^{86}$Kr provides recommended values for gamma-ray production and isomeric cross section production that were absent in the previous ENDF/B-VIII.0 evaluation, but the thermal capture cross section of 3 mb is based solely on measurements of 4 primary gammas and the thermal values do not have uncertainties because a full R-matrix analysis was outside the scope of this work.

## Method
The authors performed a new resolved resonance region evaluation using the Reich-Moore formalism, converting from the previous Multi-level Breit-Wigner approximation, and fitted the optical model potential up to 12 MeV to simultaneously reproduce experimental total cross section data and the dominant inelastic gamma transition from the 2+ state to the 0+ ground state. For energies above 12 MeV, they adopted the Koning-Delaroche global spherical optical model potential and tuned the exciton pre-equilibrium model to reproduce (n,2n) data and high-energy inelastic data. Coupled-channel calculations within a rigid rotational model with quadrupole deformation beta2 = 0.145 were employed to describe level couplings.

## Key numbers
- Thermal capture cross section: 3.028e-3 b (this work), compared to (3 +/- 2)e-3 b from Atlas (2018)
- Thermal total cross section: 6.695 b (this work), compared to 6.72 +/- 0.37 b from Atlas (2018)
- Resonance integral for (n,gamma): (20.4 +/- 1.3)e-3 b (this work), compared to (18 +/- 2)e-3 b from Atlas (2018)
- Maxwellian averaged cross section (MACS) at 30 keV for (n,gamma): 5.08 +/- 0.28 mb (this work), compared to 4.76 +/- 0.28 mb from Mutti (2005)
- Scattering radius R' set to 7.8 +/- 0.1 fm as recommended by the Atlas
- Quadrupole deformation beta2 = 0.145 used for coupled-channel calculations

## Context
This work extends the ENDF/B-VIII.0 evaluation (Brown, 2018) which did not contain any isomeric nor gamma-production information and had significant issues in the thermal and resonance regions. The new evaluation improves upon the previous resonance parameters by converting from Multi-level Breit-Wigner to Reich-Moore format, adding a missing resonance at 188.89 keV from Carlton (1988), and setting the scattering radius to the Atlas-recommended value. The evaluation has been submitted for incorporation in the next release of the ENDF/B nuclear reaction library and will enable more accurate neutron flux diagnostics at the National Ignition Facility as well as improved constraints for the 85Kr branching point in the s-process in AGB stars.
