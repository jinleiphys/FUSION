# Fragile Signs of Criticality in the Nuclear Multifragmentation

> Deviations from an idealized equilibrium phase transition picture in nuclear multifragmentation is studied in terms of the entropic index. We investigate different heat-capacity features in the canonical quantum statistical model of nuclear multifragmentation generalized in the framework of Tsallis nonextensive thermostatistics. We find that the negative branch of heat capacity observed in quasi-peripheral Au+Au collisions is caused primarily by the non-generic nonextensivity effects.

## Key claim

The negative branch of heat capacity observed in quasi-peripheral Au+Au collisions is caused primarily by non-generic nonextensivity effects, not by an idealized equilibrium phase transition. The authors find that a tiny deviation from Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (entropic index q ≈ 1.0005) can produce the observed signatures, whereas the standard equilibrium picture would predict no negative heat capacity in heavy systems like Au+Au.

## Method

The authors extended the canonical quantum statistical model of nuclear multifragmentation into the framework of Tsallis nonextensive thermostatistics, replacing the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs entropy with the Tsallis entropy characterized by an entropic index q. They calculated the partition function, pressure, inverse isothermal compressibility, and heat capacities at constant volume and constant pressure exactly (without Monte Carlo) using recurrence relation techniques for systems of different sizes (A0 = 100, 200, 300 nucleons) and various entropic indices (q = 1.0, 1.0005, 1.001). The model includes fragment binding energies, temperature-dependent surface energy, and Coulomb interactions in the Wigner-Seitz approximation.

## Key numbers

- For A0 = 100, the global critical density ρc/ρ0 equals 0.547 for q = 1, 0.783 for q = 1.0005, and 0.925 for q = 1.001.
- For A0 = 200, the global critical point exists only for q = 1, with ρc/ρ0 = 0.904.
- The negative branch of CP in quasi-peripheral Au+Au collisions at 35A.MeV is reproduced by the model for q ≈ 1.0005 and A0 ≈ 200.
- The same value q ≈ 1.0005 is consistent with both caloric curve data and negative heat capacity data from different experiments (quasi-peripheral Au+Au at 35A.MeV and central Xe+Sn at 32A.MeV).
- The inverse compressibility 1/κT has zeros for A0 = 100 and 200 in the BGSM limit (q = 1), but for heavier systems zeros appear only for q > 1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous nonextensive fragmentation model (Gudima et al., 2000) and applies it to interpret experimental caloric curve data (Pochodzalla et al., 1997) and negative heat capacity data (D'Agostino et al., 2000). It contrasts with the Van der Waals fluid description of nuclear matter (Elliott and Hirsch, 2000; Jaqaman, Mekjian, and Zamick, 1983), which yields much lower critical densities (ρc ≈ 0.3ρ0) and does not produce a negative branch of heat capacity. The work enables a quantitative framework for interpreting multifragmentation data in the critical region, showing that the mass dependence of criticality signatures is determined by a competition between Coulomb repulsion and nonextensive entropy features.
