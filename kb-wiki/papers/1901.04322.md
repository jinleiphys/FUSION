# Scattering cross-section under external magnetic field using the optical theorem

> The cross-section for the lowest order $2\rightarrow2$ elastic scattering between two charged scalars under external magnetic field mediated via a neutral scalar, has been computed in strong as well as weak magnetic field limits. This has been done by applying the optical theorem where the cross-section is expressed in terms of the imaginary parts of different one-loop graphs contributing to the forward scattering amplitudes. The modification in the amplitudes due to the external magnetic field has been done by means of replacing the charged scalar propagators with the Schwinger proper-time ones. Significant modifications of the cross-sections with respect to the vacuum cross-section are observed due to the external magnetic field.

# Scattering cross-section under external magnetic field using the optical theorem

## Key claim
The cross-section for elastic $b^+b^-\rightarrow b^+b^-$ scattering mediated by a neutral scalar $B^0$ is computed under external magnetic field using the optical theorem rather than direct tree-level Feynman rules, which are unavailable for non-zero magnetic fields. The results are obtained only under the separate approximations of weak field ($eB \ll m^2$) and strong field ($eB \gg m^2$), and the cross-section depends on four independent Lorentz scalars ($s_\parallel$, $s_\perp$, $k_\perp^2$, $p_\perp^2$) rather than the single Mandelstam $s$ of the vacuum case.

## Method
The optical theorem was applied to express the total cross-section in terms of the imaginary parts of four one-loop forward scattering amplitudes (pure $s$-channel, pure $t$-channel, and two interference diagrams). The external magnetic field was incorporated by replacing charged scalar propagators with Schwinger proper-time propagators, then evaluated in two limits: the weak-field expansion up to $\mathcal{O}(B^2)$ and the strong-field lowest-Landau-level (LLL) approximation. The imaginary parts of the loop amplitudes were computed analytically using Cutkosky rules and the resulting cross-sections were obtained from the optical theorem relation $\sigma = -\lambda^{-1/2} \text{Im}(\Pi_A+\Pi_B+\Pi_C+\Pi_D)$.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum cross-section from tree graphs and from the optical theorem agree exactly, with ratio $\sigma_\text{Tree}/\sigma_\text{Optical} = 1$ for all $\sqrt{s}$ (shown in inset of Fig. 2).
- For weak field ($eB = 0.005, 0.010, 0.015, 0.020$ GeV$^2$), the ratio $\sigma_\text{Weak}/\sigma_0$ shows a large enhancement near threshold $\sqrt{s}=2m$ due to a threshold singularity scaling as $(s-4m^2)^{-\eta}$ with $\eta>0$.
- For strong field ($eB = 0.15, 0.20, 0.25, 0.30$ GeV$^2$), the ratio $\sigma_\text{Strong}/\sigma_0$ is shown as a function of $\sqrt{s}/2m$ at fixed $k_\perp=200$ MeV, $p_\perp=100$ MeV, $\theta=\pi/3$.
- The masses used for numerical estimation are $M=400$ MeV for $B^0$ and $m=300$ MeV for $b^\pm$.
- At high $\sqrt{s} \gtrsim 4m$, the weak-field cross-section becomes less than the vacuum cross-section ($\sigma_\text{Weak} < \sigma_0$), indicating a negative net correction from the $(eB)^2$ terms.

## Context
This work extends the use of the optical theorem, previously applied to decay widths under magnetic fields (Aguirre 2018, Bandyopadhyay 2016, Hattori 2012, Ghosh 2016), to the calculation of $2\rightarrow2$ scattering cross-sections in a magnetized background. It differs from prior direct $\hat{S}$-matrix approaches for processes such as inverse beta decay (Bhattacharya 2002) and electron-muon scattering (Binoy Tiwari 2018), which required explicit solutions of the Klein-Gordon or Dirac equation. The toy model of charged scalars avoids spin complications and provides a foundation for future generalizations to QED and QCD processes relevant to heavy-ion collision phenomenology, particularly the calculation of transport coefficients and heavy-quark collisional energy loss in magnetized quark-gluon plasma.
