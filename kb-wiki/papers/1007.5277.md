# Chiral corrections to $ŽĆ^-╬│\to 3ŽĆ$ processes at low energies

> We calculate in chiral perturbation theory the double-pion photoproduction processes $ŽĆ^-╬│\to ŽĆ^- ŽĆ^0ŽĆ^0$ and $ŽĆ^-╬│\to ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-ŽĆ^-$ at low energies. At leading order these reactions are governed by the chiral pion-pion interaction. The next-to-leading order corrections arise from pion-loop diagrams and chiral-invariant counterterms involving the low-energy constants $\bar\ell_1,\, \bar\ell_2,\, \bar\ell_3$ and $\bar\ell_4$. The pertinent production amplitudes $A_1$ and $A_2$ depending on five kinematical variables are given in closed analytical form. We find that the total cross section for neutral pion-pair production $ŽĆ^-╬│\toŽĆ^-ŽĆ^0ŽĆ^0$ gets enhanced in the region $\sqrt{s}< 7m_ŽĆ$ by a factor $1.5 - 1.8$ by the next-to-leading order corrections. In contrast to this behavior the total cross section for charged pion-pair production $ŽĆ^-╬│\to ŽĆ^+ ŽĆ^-ŽĆ^-$ remains almost unchanged in the region $\sqrt{s}< 6m_ŽĆ$ in comparison to its tree-level result. Although the dynamics of the pion-pair production reactions is much richer, this observed pattern can be understood from the different influence of the chiral corrections on the pion-pion final state interaction ($ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^- \to ŽĆ^0ŽĆ^0$ versus $ŽĆ^-ŽĆ^- \to ŽĆ^-ŽĆ^-$). We present also results for the complete set of two-pion invariant mass spectra. The predictions of chiral perturbation theory for the $ŽĆ^-╬│\to 3ŽĆ$ processes can be tested by the COMPASS experiment which uses Primakoff scattering of high-energy pions in the Coulomb field of a heavy nucleus to extract cross sections for $ŽĆ^-╬│$ reactions with various final states.

## Key claim

The next-to-leading order chiral corrections enhance the total cross section for neutral pion-pair production ($\pi^-\gamma \to \pi^-\pi^0\pi^0$) by a factor of 1.5ŌĆō1.8 in the region $\sqrt{s} < 7m_\pi$, while the total cross section for charged pion-pair production ($\pi^-\gamma \to \pi^+\pi^-\pi^-$) remains almost unchanged for $\sqrt{s} < 6m_\pi$; this pattern is attributed to the different influence of chiral corrections on the pion-pion final state interaction ($\pi^+\pi^- \to \pi^0\pi^0$ versus $\pi^-\pi^- \to \pi^-\pi^-$).

## Method

The author calculates the double-pion photoproduction processes $\pi^-\gamma \to \pi^-\pi^0\pi^0$ and $\pi^-\gamma \to \pi^+\pi^-\pi^-$ using chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order. The calculation includes pion-loop diagrams and chiral-invariant counterterms involving the low-energy constants $\bar\ell_1$, $\bar\ell_2$, $\bar\ell_3$, and $\bar\ell_4$, with the production amplitudes $A_1$ and $A_2$ expressed in closed analytical form depending on five independent Lorentz-invariant kinematical variables. The total cross sections and two-pion invariant mass spectra are obtained by integrating the squared T-matrix over the three-pion phase space.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for $\pi^-\gamma \to \pi^-\pi^0\pi^0$ is enhanced by a factor of 1.5ŌĆō1.8 after including next-to-leading order chiral corrections in the region $\sqrt{s} < 7m_\pi$
- Total cross section for $\pi^-\gamma \to \pi^+\pi^-\pi^-$ remains almost unchanged for $\sqrt{s} < 6m_\pi$ compared to its tree-level result
- Relative uncertainty of the total cross section at $\sqrt{s} = 6m_\pi$ from the low-energy constants: $\pm 5.1\%$ from $\delta\bar\ell_1$, $\pm 1.4\%$ from $\delta\bar\ell_2$, $\pm 0.2\%$ from $\delta\bar\ell_3$, $\pm 0.9\%$ from $\delta\bar\ell_4$, giving a total relative uncertainty of $\pm 5.4\%$
- Low-energy constants used: $\bar\ell_1 = -0.4 \pm 0.6$, $\bar\ell_2 = 4.3 \pm 0.1$, $\bar\ell_3 = 2.9 \pm 2.4$, $\bar\ell_4 = 4.4 \pm 0.2$, as determined in Colangelo et al.
- Pion decay constant $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV, charged pion mass $m_\pi = 139.570$ MeV, neutral pion mass $m_{\pi^0} = 134.977$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the tree-level calculations of the $\pi^-\gamma \to 3\pi$ processes by Kaiser and Friedrich (2008) to next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory. The predictions can be tested by the COMPASS experiment at CERN, which uses Primakoff scattering of high-energy pions in the Coulomb field of a heavy nucleus to extract cross sections for $\pi^-\gamma$ reactions. The results complement earlier chiral perturbation theory studies of related processes such as $\pi^-\gamma \to \pi^-\pi^0$ (which tests the QCD chiral anomaly via the $\gamma 3\pi$ coupling constant $F_{\gamma 3\pi} = 9.72$ GeV$^{-3}$) and pion Compton scattering $\pi^-\gamma \to \pi^-\gamma$ (which probes pion polarizabilities).
