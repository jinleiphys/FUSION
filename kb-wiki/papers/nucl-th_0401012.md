# Reaction dynamics for fusion of weakly-bound nuclei

> We discuss several open problems of fusion reactions induced by weakly bound nuclei. For this purpose, we solve a one dimensional three-body Hamiltonian with the coupled-channels formalism. We show that the continuum-continuum couplings substantially reduce the total fusion probability at energies above the barrier compared with the no-breakup case, although the fusion probability remains enhanced at subbarrier energies. We then discuss a role of transfer process in fusion of weakly bound nuclei, and point out that removing spurious Pauli forbidden transfer components from the calculation may be crucial at energies below the barrier. Calculations based on the three-body classical trajectory Monte Carlo (CTMC) method are also presented in order to discuss how to model complete fusion process.

## Key claim

The paper argues that continuum-continuum couplings substantially reduce total fusion probability at energies above the barrier compared with the no-breakup case, while fusion probability remains enhanced at subbarrier energies; however, the authors qualify that their main conclusions for complete fusion remain valid only at a qualitative level, and that removing spurious Pauli forbidden transfer components from calculations may be crucial at energies below the barrier.

## Method

The authors solve a one-dimensional three-body Hamiltonian using the coupled-channels formalism, discretizing the continuum states of the projectile nucleus on an orthogonal polynomial basis up to n=28. They also perform three-body classical trajectory Monte Carlo (CTMC) calculations for the ⁶Li + ²⁰⁹Bi system, solving two-dimensional classical Newtonian equations to model complete and incomplete fusion processes. The coupled-channels calculations include up to seven discretized continuum states together with the ground state, and the modified log-derivative method is used to obtain numerically stable solutions at low energies.

## Key numbers

- The first five eigenstate energies for the projectile Hamiltonian are: ε₀ = -26.09 MeV, ε₁ = -11.42 MeV, ε₂ = -0.515 MeV, ε₃ = 0.135 MeV, and ε₄ = 0.193 MeV.
- The absorption radius for projectile fragments is r_abs = 1.1 × (A_T^{1/3} + A_{P_i}^{1/3}) in the CTMC calculations.
- Five bound states exist in the target nucleus at -40.5, -31.9, -20.6, -8.85, and -0.357 MeV.
- The polynomial basis used for discretizing continuum states extends up to n = 28.
- A factor of 2(m_C + 1)/(2m_C + 1) is multiplied to transfer matrix elements to mimic the recoil effect.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled-channels calculations (Hagino et al., 2000) by including continuum-continuum couplings, which were omitted in that earlier study. The paper addresses the discrepancy between the coupled-channels approach, which predicts enhanced fusion at subbarrier energies, and the time-dependent wave packet approach of Yabana et al. (1997, 2003), which predicts hindrance even below the barrier. The findings enable future work that must include only Pauli-allowed transfer channels together with breakup channels, and suggest that the time-dependent wave packet approach needs to introduce projection operators to exclude Pauli-forbidden channels explicitly.
