# Thermal radiation and inclusive production in the KLN model for ion-ion collisions

> We show that in order to obtain a successful description of the transverse momenta distribution for charged particles in ion-ion collisions, one must include a thermal emission term. The temperature of this emission $T_{\rm th}$ turns out to be proportional to the saturation scale, $T_{\rm th} = 1.8/2π\,Q_s$. The formalism for the calculation of the transverse momenta spectra in CGC/saturation approach is developed, in which two stages of the process are seen: creation of the colour glass condensate, and hadronization of the gluon jets. Our calculations are based on the observation that even for small values of $p_T$, the main contribution in the integration over the dipole sizes stems from the kinematic region in vicinity of the saturation momentum, where theoretically, we know the scattering amplitude. Non-perturbativ corrections need to be included in the model of hadronization. This model incorporates the decay of a gluon jet with effective mass $m^2_{\rm eff} = 2 Q_s μ_{\rm soft}$ where $μ_{\rm soft}$ denotes the soft scale, with the fragmentation functions at all values of the transverse momenta. We use the KLN model which, provides a simple way to estimate the cross sections for the different centrality classes. Comparing the results of this paper with the transverse distribution in the proton-proton scattering, we see two major differences. First, a larger contribution of the thermal radiation term is needed, in accord with higher parton densities of the produced colour glass condensate. Second, even changing the model for the hadronization, without a thermal radiation term, we fail to describe the $p_T$ spectrum. Consequently, we conjecture that theexistence of the thermal radiation term is independent of the model of confinement.

## Key claim

To successfully describe the transverse momentum distribution of charged particles in ion-ion collisions, a thermal emission term must be included in addition to the hard emission term from the CGC/saturation approach; the authors conjecture that the existence of this thermal radiation term is independent of the model of confinement, and that without it, even changing the hadronization model fails to describe the \(p_T\) spectrum.

## Method

The authors develop a formalism within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC)/saturation approach for calculating transverse momentum spectra in ion-ion collisions, using the KLN model to estimate cross sections for different centrality classes. The calculation proceeds in two stages: creation of the color glass condensate, followed by hadronization of gluon jets using phenomenological fragmentation functions, where the gluon jet has an effective mass \(m^2_{\rm eff} = 2 Q_s \mu_{\rm soft}\) with \(\mu_{\rm soft}\) denoting the soft scale. The dipole-nucleus scattering amplitude is evaluated in the vicinity of the saturation scale using geometric scaling behavior, with the impact-parameter-dependent CGC dipole model fitted to HERA data from Ref. [RESH].

## Key numbers

* The thermal temperature is proportional to the saturation scale: \(T_{\rm th} = 1.8 / (2\pi) \, Q_s\).
* The saturation momentum energy dependence parameter is \(\lambda = 0.2063 \pm 0.0004\) from the fit to HERA data.
* The critical anomalous dimension in leading-order BFKL is \(\gamma_{cr} = 0.37\), giving \(\bar{\gamma} = 1 - \gamma_{cr} = 0.63\).
* The fitted parameters from the CGC dipole model (Ref. [RESH]) include \(\bar{\gamma} = 0.6599 \pm 0.0003\), \(N_0 = 0.3358 \pm 0.0004\), and \(x_0 = 0.00105 \pm 1.13 \times 10^{-5}\).
* The effective exponent \(\gamma_{\rm eff}\) replaces \(\bar{\gamma}\) via \(\gamma_{\rm eff} = \bar{\gamma} + \ln(1/\langle \tau \rangle) / (\kappa \lambda \ln(1/x))\) with \(\kappa \approx 9.9\) in LO BFKL.
* At \(\tilde{p}_T = p_T / Q_s = 0\), the average dipole size is \(\langle \tau \rangle = 0.478 \approx 1/2\); at large \(\tilde{p}_T\), \(\langle \tau \rangle \propto 1/(4 \tilde{p}_T)\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CGC/saturation approach for hadron-hadron scattering (Gotsman and Levin, Ref. [GOLETE]) to heavy ion collisions, where higher parton densities make the thermal radiation term more prominent. The thermal emission arises from the rapid quench of the entangled partonic state in the CGC framework, as discussed in Refs. [KHTU, KLT, BAKH, DUHA, CKS], and the temperature relation \(T_{\rm th} = c Q_s / (2\pi)\) with semi-classical estimate \(c = 1.2\) is used. The results enable a unified description of \(p_T\) spectra in both proton-proton and ion-ion collisions within the CGC approach, showing that the thermal term is necessary regardless of the hadronization model.
