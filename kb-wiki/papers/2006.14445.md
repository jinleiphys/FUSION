# Masses of fully heavy tetraquarks $QQ \bar Q \bar Q$ in an extended relativized quark model

> Inspired by recent measurement of possible fully charmed tetraquarks in LHCb Collaboration, we investigate the mass spectra of fully heavy tetraquarks $QQ \bar Q \bar Q$ in an extended relativized quark model. Our estimations indicate that the broad structure around 6.4 GeV should contain one or more ground $cc \bar c \bar c$ tetraquark states, while the narrow structure near 6.9 GeV can be categorized as the first radial excitation of $cc \bar c \bar c$ system. Moreover, with the wave functions of the tetraquarks and mesons, the strong decays of tetraquarks into heavy quarkonium pair are qualitatively discussed, which can be further checked by the LHCb and CMS Collaborations.

## Key claim

The broad structure around 6.4 GeV observed by LHCb should contain one or more ground-state \(cc\bar c \bar c\) tetraquark states, while the narrow structure near 6.9 GeV can be categorized as the first radial excitation of the \(cc\bar c \bar c\) system, based on calculations in an extended relativized quark model.

## Method

The authors solve a four-body Hamiltonian for \(QQ\bar Q \bar Q\) systems using a variational method with Gaussian basis functions, extending the relativized quark model of Godfrey and Isgur to tetraquarks. The Hamiltonian includes relativistic kinetic energy, one-gluon-exchange potential with spin-spin interactions, and a confining potential. The wave functions incorporate color (both \(\bar 3 3\) and \(6 \bar 6\) representations), flavor, spin, and spatial degrees of freedom, and the generalized eigenvalue problem is solved for \(S\)-wave states with up to \(10^3\) Gaussian bases.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state \(cc\bar c \bar c\) masses lie in the range \(6435\) to \(6543\) MeV, above the \(J/\psi J/\psi\) threshold.
- The first radial excitations of \(cc\bar c \bar c\) are predicted around \(6849\) to \(6948\) MeV, corresponding to the LHCb structure near \(6.9\) GeV/\(c^2\).
- For the \(0^{++}\) ground state at \(6435\) MeV, the hidden-color component \(|88\rangle\) is \(46.0\%\) and the root-mean-square radius \(\langle \boldsymbol r_{13}^2 \rangle^{1/2}\) is \(0.405\) fm.
- The decay ratio \(R = \Gamma[cc\bar c \bar c \to J/\psi J/\psi] / \Gamma[cc\bar c \bar c \to \psi(2S) J/\psi]\) is \(0.113\) for the \(6849\) MeV \(0^{++}\) state and \(0.075\) for the \(6948\) MeV \(2^{++}\) state.
- The ratio \(R_{4\mu}\) (accounting for muon branching fractions) for the \(6849\) MeV \(0^{++}\) state is \(0.843\).
- For \(bb\bar b \bar b\) ground states, masses range from \(19201\) to \(19262\) MeV, all above the \(\Upsilon \Upsilon\) threshold.
- The first radial excitations of \(bb\bar b \bar b\) are predicted around \(19567\) to \(19633\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativized quark model previously applied to doubly heavy tetraquarks by Lu (2020) to the fully heavy \(QQ\bar Q \bar Q\) systems. It directly addresses the LHCb observation (2020) of a structure near 6.9 GeV in the \(J/\psi\) pair invariant mass spectrum, as well as the broader structure around 6.4 GeV. The results are consistent with nonrelativistic quark model calculations by Liu (2019), Wang (2019), Chen (2019), and Deng (2020) that place ground states above threshold, and contrast with models predicting ground states below threshold (e.g., Karliner (2016), Anwar (2017)). The predicted decay ratios into \(\psi(2S) J/\psi\) and \(\Upsilon(2S) \Upsilon\) provide testable signatures for future LHCb and CMS searches.
