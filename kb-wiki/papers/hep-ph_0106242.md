# Constraints on the size of the quark gluon plasma

> We use simple entropy arguments to estimate the possible size of the QGP at the AGS and the SPS. We find that the possibility to form a large volume of QGP at the AGS or the SPS is very small. The size of the QGP at RHIC and the LHC is also predicted.

## Key claim

Using simple entropy arguments based on the second law of thermodynamics, the paper argues that even if a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is formed at AGS or SPS energies, its radius should be only around 1–2 fm, making finite-size effects important; the abstract glosses over the strong dependence of this result on the assumed freezeout temperature and the assumption that the initial state is QGP.

## Method

The author applies the second law of thermodynamics (S_QGP ≤ S_Had) to relate the entropy of the QGP phase to that of the hadron phase, using entropy densities calculated from massless pion, quark, and gluon gases. The hadron phase volume is taken from Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) interferometry measurements of the pion source radius at AGS and SPS (5–7 fm), and the ratio of entropy densities (s_QGP/s_had = 12–16) is used to infer the QGP volume. The calculation is refined by accounting for the difference between freezeout temperature (T_f ≈ 120–138 MeV) and critical temperature (T_c ≈ 200 MeV), and by using the Bjorken hydrodynamical model for an independent estimate.

## Key numbers

- HBT source radius at AGS and SPS: 5–7 fm (pion source radius).
- QGP source radius at AGS and SPS (using T_f = T_c): 2–3 fm.
- QGP source radius at AGS and SPS (using T_f = 120–138 MeV, T_c = 200 MeV): 1–2 fm.
- For a droplet scenario with two droplets, droplet size: 0.5–1.2 fm.
- Hadron phase radius at RHIC: 14–26 fm; corresponding QGP radius: 4–8 fm.
- Hadron phase radius at LHC: 18–36 fm; corresponding QGP radius: 6–11 fm.
- Critical energy density from Bjorken model: 2.1 GeV/fm³.
- Critical temperature from energy density: 191 MeV (consistent with input 200 MeV).
- Entropy density of pions at T_f = 120 MeV with pion mass 138 MeV: 0.246 fm⁻³.
- Revised volume ratio using massive pions: V_had ≥ 69 V_QGP.
- Average pion momentum at T_f = 120 MeV: 372 MeV.
- de Broglie wavelength for pions: 3.3 fm; for gluons: 2.3 fm; for quarks: 2.6 fm.

## Context

This work extends the entropy-based picture of Lee (1990s) to constrain QGP size using HBT data from AGS and SPS, and predicts sizes for RHIC and LHC. It contradicts the assumption of a large QGP volume at lower energies, and highlights that finite-size effects, previously studied by Elze, Greiner, and Rafelski (1990s), must be considered for QGP signatures. The results enable future work on finite-size corrections to parton distributions and QGP observables, and suggest that at RHIC and LHC the QGP may be large enough to neglect such effects.
