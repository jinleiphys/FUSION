# Study of the pd(dp) -> 3He pi pi reactions close to threshold

> New experimental data on the pd -> 3He pi+ pi- reaction obtained with the COSY-MOMO detector below the three-pion threshold are presented. The reaction was also studied in inverse kinematics with a deuteron beam and the higher counting rates achieved were especially important at low excess energies. The comparison of these data with inclusive pd -> 3He+X rates allowed estimates also to be made of pi0 pi0 production. The results confirm our earlier findings that close to threshold there is no enhancement at low excitation energies in the pi+ pi- system, where the data seem largely suppressed compared to phase space. Possible explanations for this behavior, such as strong p- waves in the pi+pi- -system or the influence of two-step processes, are explored.

# Study of the pd(dp) -> 3He pi pi reactions close to threshold

## Key claim
Close to threshold (below Q ≈ 100 MeV), there is no enhancement at low excitation energies in the pi+pi- system, the data are largely suppressed compared to phase space, which contradicts the ABC effect observed at higher excess energies. The suppression may be explained by strong p-waves in the pi+pi- system or by two-step processes, but the data at low Q are dominated by isovector (I=1) pion pair production rather than the isoscalar (I=0) production responsible for the ABC effect.

## Method
The COSY-MOMO detector measured the pd -> 3He pi+pi- reaction using both proton and deuteron beams incident on liquid deuterium or hydrogen targets. The 3He ions were detected in the Big Karl high-resolution spectrograph, while the two charged pions were measured in the MOMO scintillating fiber detector. By comparing inclusive 3He yields (from Big Karl alone) with exclusive 3He pi+pi- events (from combined Big Karl and MOMO), estimates of the pd -> 3He pi0pi0 cross section were extracted via subtraction.

## Key numbers
- At Q = 70 MeV and Q = 92 MeV for pd -> 3He pi+pi-, the differential cross sections show no ABC enhancement and are better described by phase space weighted by a T_pipi factor (indicating p-wave) than by pure phase space.
- At Q = 8 MeV for dp -> 3He pi+pi-, the pi+pi- distribution is well described by phase space modified by a T_pipi factor.
- The total cross section for pd(dp) -> 3He pi+pi- follows approximately a Q^3 dependence near threshold, while pd(dp) -> 3He pi0pi0 follows a Q^2 dependence.
- At Q = 79 MeV and Q = 101 MeV, the pi0pi0 cross sections are typically an order of magnitude lower than for charged pion production.
- The acceptance for dp kinematics is about an order of magnitude higher than for pd kinematics across the energy range studied.
- Beam intensities were typically 5x10^8 protons per spill (4 s length, 11 s repetition rate) and 7x10^9 deuterons per spill (30 s length).
- Systematic uncertainty in cross section normalization is estimated to be below 10%.

## Context
This work extends the earlier MOMO measurement at Q = 70 MeV (Bellemann et al., 1999) and confirms the absence of the ABC effect at low excess energies, in contrast to the strong ABC enhancement observed at higher Q by CELSIUS-WASA (Bashkanov et al., 2006) and COSY-ANKE (Mielke et al., 2016). The results are compared with a two-step model (Faldt and Wilkin, 2000) that links the reaction to pi- p -> pi0 pi0 n amplitudes from the Valencia model (Vicente Vacas and Oset, 1999). The data enable future studies of the transition between the low-Q regime (dominated by isovector pion pairs) and the high-Q regime (dominated by the isoscalar ABC effect), and suggest that the d*(2380) dibaryon resonance may play a role in the energy dependence.
