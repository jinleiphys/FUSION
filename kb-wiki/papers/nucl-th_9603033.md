# Some New Aspects of Bimodal Fission in 258Fm Isotope

> Using the multidimensional dynamic--programming method (MDPM) in the four--dimensional deformation space $\{β_λ\}$ with $λ$=2, 4, 35 and 6 we were able to study evolution of the action integral for the fissioning nucleus $^{258}$Fm. We found the second minimum on the cross--section of the action integral for $β_2 \approx 1$ , what we interpret as the dynamical evidence of the bimodal fission in this heavy Fm isotope.

## Key claim
Using the multidimensional dynamic-programming method (MDPM) in a four-dimensional deformation space, the authors find a second local minimum on the cross-section of the action integral for quadrupole deformation β₂ ≈ 1.05, which they interpret as dynamical evidence of bimodal fission in ²⁵⁸Fm, but this second minimum appears only in the last step before the exit point from the barrier, not throughout the entire fission path.

## Method
The authors performed dynamical calculations of the action integral for ²⁵⁸Fm using the WKB semiclassical approximation in a four-dimensional deformation space spanned by β₂, β₄, β₃₅ (combining β₃ and β₅), and β₆. They computed the potential energy via the macroscopic-microscopic model (Yukawa-plus-exponential finite-range model for the macroscopic part, Strutinsky shell correction with Woods-Saxon single-particle potential and BCS pairing for the microscopic part) and the inertia tensor in the cranking approximation. The least-action trajectory was found using the multidimensional dynamic-programming method (MDPM) extended to four degrees of freedom, with β₂ serving as the coordinate linearly related to the fission process.

## Key numbers
- The deformation space grid contained 24 × 12 × 6 × 7 = 12,096 points, with β₂ ranging from 0.15 to 1.30 in steps of 0.05, β₄ from -0.08 to 0.36 in steps of 0.04, β₃₅ from 0.00 to 0.25 in steps of 0.05, and β₆ from -0.12 to 0.12 in steps of 0.04.
- The second local minimum on the action integral cross-section appears at β₂ = 1.05, with the exit point from the barrier at coordinates (β₂ = 1.10, β₄ = 0.05, β₃₅ = 0.01, β₆ = -0.06).
- The penetration energy E is defined as the ground-state energy V(X⁰_λ) plus a zero-point energy of 0.5 MeV in the fission direction.

## Context
This work extends the static descriptions of bimodal fission in ²⁵⁸Fm by the Los Alamos group (Möller and collaborators, early 1990s) and the Warsaw group (Cwiok and collaborators, early 1990s) by providing a dynamical analysis based on the action integral rather than potential-energy surfaces alone. The results agree with the Los Alamos group's finding that the fission trajectories split before the exit point from the barrier, in contrast to the Warsaw group's description where the split occurs behind the exit point. The dynamical fission trajectory leads to compact shapes (CS), with a branch to elongated shapes (ES) appearing near β₂ ≈ 0.95, and both dynamical barriers show two humps unlike the single-hump static barrier reported by the Warsaw group.
