# Constraints on the IR behavior of the gluon propagator in Yang-Mills theories

> We present rigorous upper and lower bounds for the zero-momentum gluon propagator D(0) of Yang-Mills theories in terms of the average value of the gluon field. This allows us to perform a controlled extrapolation of lattice data to infinite volume, showing that the infrared limit of the Landau-gauge gluon propagator in SU(2) gauge theory is finite and nonzero in three and in four space-time dimensions. In the two-dimensional case we find D(0) = 0, in agreement with Ref. [1]. We suggest an explanation for these results. We note that our discussion is general, although we only apply our analysis to pure gauge theory in Landau gauge. Simulations have been performed on the IBM supercomputer at the University of Sao Paulo.

## Key claim
The Landau-gauge gluon propagator at zero momentum in SU(2) Yang-Mills theory is finite and nonzero in three and four dimensions, but zero in two dimensions. The authors derive rigorous bounds that allow controlled extrapolation to infinite volume, rather than relying on direct extrapolation of the propagator itself.

## Method
The authors derive rigorous lower and upper bounds for the zero-momentum gluon propagator D(0) in terms of the average absolute value of the gluon field components at zero momentum, denoted M(0). They perform lattice Monte Carlo simulations of pure SU(2) gauge theory in Landau gauge in two, three, and four dimensions, using large lattice volumes up to 320^2 in 2d, 320^3 in 3d, and 128^4 in 4d, and extrapolate the bounds to infinite volume.

## Key numbers
- In 3d at beta = 3.0: lower bound D(0) >= 0.4(1) GeV^{-2} and upper bound D(0) <= 4(1) GeV^{-2}; after including a factor beta = 3.0, the bounds become 1.2(3) <= D(0) <= 12(3) GeV^{-2}
- In 4d at beta = 2.2: lower bound D(0) >= 2.2(3) GeV^{-2} and upper bound D(0) <= 29(5) GeV^{-2}
- In 2d at beta = 10: both lower and upper bounds extrapolate to zero, implying D(0) = 0
- Lattice volumes correspond to physical sizes of approximately (70 fm)^2 in 2d, (85 fm)^3 in 3d, and (27 fm)^4 in 4d
- The exponent l for the scaling of M(0) ~ 1/L^l is 1.367(8) in 2d, 1.48(3) in 3d, and 1.99(2) in 4d

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing controversy over the infrared behavior of the gluon propagator in Yang-Mills theories. The Gribov-Zwanziger scenario predicts D(0) = 0 (maximal violation of reflection positivity), while other approaches such as Aguilar and collaborators (2004) and Frasca (2007) find a finite nonzero D(0) with a dynamically generated mass. The results support the finite nonzero scenario in 3d and 4d, in agreement with recent lattice studies by Cucchieri and Mendes (2007) and Bogolubsky and collaborators (2007), while confirming the 2d result of Maas (2007). The method of using bounds on M(0) rather than direct extrapolation of D(0) provides a controlled way to reach the infinite-volume limit, and the authors note that similar analysis can be applied to nonzero momenta and to SU(3).
