# Pionic correlations and meson-exchange currents in two-particle emission induced by electron scattering

> Two-particle two-hole contributions to electromagnetic response functions are computed in a fully relativistic Fermi gas model. All one-pion exchange diagrams that contribute to the scattering amplitude in perturbation theory are considered, including terms for pionic correlations and meson-exchange currents (MEC). The pionic correlation terms diverge in an infinite system and thus are regularized by modification of the nucleon propagator in the medium to take into account the finite size of the nucleus. The pionic correlation contributions are found to be of the same order of magnitude as the MEC.

## Key claim

The pionic correlation contributions to two-particle two-hole (2p-2h) electromagnetic response functions are found to be of the same order of magnitude as the meson-exchange current (MEC) contributions, but only after regularizing an otherwise divergent double-pole term by introducing a finite imaginary part in the nucleon propagator that accounts for the finite size of the nucleus.

## Method

The authors computed the 2p-2h contributions to the longitudinal and transverse electromagnetic response functions for inclusive electron scattering using a fully relativistic Fermi gas model. All one-pion exchange diagrams in perturbation theory were included, comprising both meson-exchange currents (seagull, pion-in-flight, and Delta current) and pionic correlation diagrams (forward and backward), with the correlation divergence cured by introducing a finite parameter epsilon in the nucleon propagator representing the finite propagation time of a nucleon in a nucleus. The resulting seven-dimensional integrals were evaluated using a mixed Monte Carlo-Simpson integration procedure.

## Key numbers

- The regularization parameter epsilon was estimated as approximately 200 MeV for a nucleus such as C-12, based on a nuclear radius R ~ 2 fm and the relation epsilon ~ 2 hbar c / R.
- For Fe-56 at momentum transfer q = 550 MeV/c, the correlation 2p-2h contribution showed a maximum near the quasielastic peak with a shift of about 40 MeV to higher energies for epsilon = 100 MeV.
- The height of the correlation responses in the quasielastic region approximately halves when epsilon doubles, consistent with the leading 1/epsilon dependence from the pole in the propagator.
- For q = 1140 MeV/c, the longitudinal MEC contribution became larger than at lower momentum transfer because q/m_N exceeds one, enhancing the time component of the MEC.
- The pion-nucleon form factor cutoff Lambda was set to 1.3 GeV, and the pion-nucleon coupling constant was f^2/4pi = 0.08.

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic calculations of 2p-2h contributions by DePace (2003, 2004) that included only MEC, and earlier non-relativistic work by Van Orden (1980) and relativistic work by Dekker (1991, 1992, 1994, 1995). The paper addresses the long-standing divergence problem in pionic correlation diagrams previously encountered by Alberico (1984, 1991) and Gil (1997), providing a physical regularization based on finite nuclear size rather than the usual nucleon self-energy width. The results enable more complete calculations of inclusive electron scattering cross sections in the dip region between the quasielastic and Delta peaks, though the authors note that final-state interaction corrections to the one-body response and pion emission channels must also be included for a full description.
