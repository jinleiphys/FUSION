# Electromagnetic Radiation from Hot and Dense Hadronic Matter

> The modifications of hadronic masses and decay widths at finite temperature and baryon density are investigated using a phenomenological model of hadronic interactions in the Relativistic Hartree Approximation. We consider an exhaustive set of hadronic reactions and vector meson decays to estimate the photon emission from hot and dense hadronic matter. The reduction in the vector meson masses and decay widths is seen to cause an enhancement in the photon production. It is observed that the effect of $ρ$-decay width on photon spectra is negligible. The effects on dilepton production from pion annihilation are also indicated.

# Electromagnetic Radiation from Hot and Dense Hadronic Matter

## Key claim

The reduction of vector meson masses and decay widths at finite temperature and baryon density causes an enhancement in photon production from hot and dense hadronic matter, but the effect of the rho decay width on photon spectra is negligible; the authors qualify that these results are obtained within a phenomenological model using the Relativistic Hartree Approximation and Mean Field Theory, and that the delta-hole polarization effects on pions have been neglected.

## Method

The authors used Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) in the Relativistic Hartree Approximation (RHA) to calculate the temperature and baryon density dependence of nucleon, rho, and omega meson masses and decay widths, including vacuum fluctuation corrections to Mean Field Theory. They then computed photon emission rates from a hadronic gas composed of pions, rho, omega, and eta mesons by evaluating the reactions pi pi -> rho gamma, pi rho -> pi gamma, pi pi -> eta gamma, pi eta -> pi gamma, and the decays rho -> pi pi gamma and omega -> pi gamma, using effective Lagrangians with Vector Meson Dominance. The spectral function formalism was applied for unstable particles appearing in external lines.

## Key numbers

- At T = 160 MeV and two times normal nuclear matter density (rho/rho_0 = 2), the effective nucleon mass shows a 35% reduction compared to its free mass of 939 MeV.
- At T = 160 MeV and rho/rho_0 = 2, the rho meson mass decreases by 45% compared to its free space value of 770 MeV.
- For the channel pi pi -> rho gamma at T = 160 MeV and rho/rho_0 = 2, the photon emission rate in the energy range 1-3 GeV increases by a factor of approximately 6 compared to the case with no medium effects.
- At twice normal nuclear matter density and T = 160 MeV, the total photon yield is higher by a factor of approximately 4 compared to the case where thermal bath effects on hadronic properties are neglected, across almost the entire energy range considered.
- For dilepton production at T = 180 MeV and twice nuclear matter density, inclusion of Bose enhancement effects suppresses the yield by a factor of 3 at the invariant mass M = m_rho* (the effective rho mass).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by the same group (Roy et al., 1997) that studied the effects of temperature-modified masses and widths on photon spectra relevant to RHIC and LHC energies, now including finite baryon density effects relevant to SPS energies where partial stopping of baryonic matter occurs. The approach contrasts with the QCD sum rule calculations of Hatsuda and collaborators and the Brown-Rho scaling conjecture, which predict decreasing hadronic masses with temperature, while the nonlinear sigma model had shown the opposite trend of increasing rho mass with temperature. The results enable more realistic estimates of photon and dilepton backgrounds in heavy ion collision experiments, and the authors note that the Bose enhancement effect on the rho decay width plays a crucial role in low-mass lepton pair production, contrary to the work of Li, Ko and Brown (1995) who attributed the observed low-mass dilepton enhancement solely to mass modification while neglecting Bose enhancement.
