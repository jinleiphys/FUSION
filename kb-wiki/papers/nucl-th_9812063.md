# Survey of heavy-meson observables

> We employ a Dyson-Schwinger equation model to effect a unified and uniformly accurate description of light- and heavy-meson observables, which we characterise by heavy-meson leptonic decays, semileptonic heavy-to-heavy and heavy-to-light transitions - B -> D*, D, rho, pi; D -> K*, K, pi, radiative and strong decays - B(s)* -> B(s) gamma; D(s)* -> D(s) gamma, D pi, and the rare B-> K* gamma flavour-changing neutral-current process. We elucidate the heavy-quark limit of these processes and, using a model-independent mass formula valid for all nonsinglet pseudoscalar mesons, demonstrate that their mass rises linearly with the mass of their heaviest constituent. In our numerical calculations we eschew a heavy-quark expansion and rely instead on the observation that the dressed c,b-quark mass functions are well approximated by a constant, interpreted as their constituent-mass: we find M_c=1.32 GeV and M_b=4.65 GeV. The calculated heavy-meson leptonic decay constants and transition form factors are a necessary element in the experimental determination of CKM matrix elements. The results also show that this framework, as employed hitherto, is well able to describe vector meson polarisation observables.

## Key claim

Using a Dyson-Schwinger equation model with a constant-mass approximation for heavy quarks (constituent masses M_c=1.32 GeV and M_b=4.65 GeV), the authors achieve a unified and uniformly accurate description of heavy-meson observables without employing a heavy-quark expansion, though the approximation works better for b-quarks than c-quarks because the c-quark mass is not large enough to fully suppress momentum-dependent dressing.

## Method

The authors employ a Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) model where heavy c- and b-quark propagators are approximated by a constant constituent-mass form S_Q(p) = 1/(iγ·p + M̂_Q), while light-quark propagators (u, s) use algebraic parametrizations that capture dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. Meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are modeled with Gaussian forms for heavy mesons and algebraic forms for light mesons, and the impulse approximation is used to calculate leptonic decays, semileptonic transitions (B→D*, D, ρ, π; D→K*, K, π), radiative decays (B(s)*→B(s)γ; D(s)*→D(s)γ), strong decays (D*→Dπ), and the rare B→K*γ flavor-changing neutral-current process, with parameters determined by χ²-fitting to a subset of observables.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses: M̂_c = 1.32 GeV, M̂_b = 4.65 GeV
- Light-meson decay constants: f_π = 0.131 GeV, f_K = 0.160 GeV
- Vector meson decay constant: f_ρ = 216 ± 5 MeV (from Γ_{ρ⁰→e⁺e⁻} = 6.77 ± 0.32 keV, giving g_ρ = 5.03 ± 0.12)
- Lattice results quoted for comparison: f_D = 200 ± 30 MeV, f_B = 170 ± 35 MeV
- Chiral limit vacuum quark condensate: ⟨q̄q⟩⁰ ≈ -(0.24 GeV)³
- Model parameters: λ = 0.566 GeV, Λ_QCD = 0.2 GeV, yielding ⟨ūu⟩_{1 GeV²} = (-0.22 GeV)³ and ⟨s̄s⟩_{1 GeV²} = 0.8 ⟨ūu⟩_{1 GeV²}
- Ratio of DCSB importance measure L_f = M_f^E/m_f^ζ: L_{u,d} = 150, L_s = 10, L_c = 2.2, L_b = 1.2
- Heavy-meson binding energy and width: E_{B_f} ≈ 0.70 GeV, ω_{B_f} ≈ 1.3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation phenomenology previously applied to light mesons (π-π scattering, electromagnetic form factors, anomalous processes) to heavy-meson observables, building on earlier studies by the same group (Ivanov, Kalinovsky, Roberts, 1998) that used the heavy-quark propagator approximation of Eq. (hqf). The approach contrasts with heavy-quark effective theory (HQET) and light cone sum rules (LCSRs) as formulated by Neubert (1994) and Braun, respectively, which fail for D-meson processes where the c-quark mass is insufficient for the heavy-quark expansion. The results provide the necessary form factors and decay constants for experimental determination of CKM matrix elements and demonstrate that the framework can describe vector meson polarization observables, enabling future studies of heavy-meson structure without requiring a heavy-quark expansion.
