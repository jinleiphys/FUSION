# Considerations on measuring spin-spin correlation of hyeprons in heavy-ion experiments

> The significant global spin alignment observed for $ϕ$ mesons in heavy-ion collisions has sparked intense discussions about its origin and implications. One explanation suggests that fluctuations in the strong force field may introduce strong spin correlations between strange ($s$) and anti-strange ($\bar{s}$) quarks, leading to the global spin alignment of $ϕ$ mesons. Extending this line of research, the theoretical community has proposed studying the spin correlation between $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hyperons. In this paper, we construct experimental observables and make connections between them and the theoretical proposed quantities.

# Considerations on measuring spin-spin correlation of hyperons in heavy-ion experiments

## Key claim
The paper constructs experimental observables for measuring global spin-spin correlations between Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons in heavy-ion collisions, connecting these observables to theoretically proposed quantities. The authors propose that the spin balance function can isolate the additional spin correlation between strange and anti-strange quarks arising from strong force field fluctuations, while canceling detector effects and flavor-independent correlations.

## Method
The authors derive the relationship between the experimental observable (the average product of cosines of decay proton angles in the Lambda rest frame) and the theoretical spin correlation quantity c'_ij, which is defined as the spin correlation minus the product of individual polarizations. They then project the angular distributions onto the transverse plane relative to the reaction plane to correct for finite event plane resolution, using the sub-event method to determine the resolution correction factor. Finally, they construct a spin balance function comparing same-sign pairs (Lambda-Lambda or anti-Lambda-anti-Lambda) with opposite-sign pairs (Lambda-anti-Lambda) to isolate the strong-force-induced correlation.

## Key numbers
- The spin correlation observable c'_LambdaLambda is given by (9/alpha_Lambda^2) * <cos(theta_i*) cos(theta_j*)> - P_Lambda^2, where alpha_Lambda is the decay constant and P_Lambda is the polarization.
- After projecting onto the transverse plane, c'_LambdaLambda = (64/(pi^2 * alpha_Lambda^2)) * <sin(Delta_phi_i*) sin(Delta_phi_j*)> - P_Lambda^2.
- The event plane resolution correction uses <cos^2(Psi_EP - Psi_RP)> = (1 + <cos(2(Psi_EP - Psi_RP))>)/2, where <cos(2(Psi_EP - Psi_RP))> is determined from the sub-event method.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical proposal by Lv et al. (2024) to study spin correlations between Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons, motivated by the discrepancy between the measured global spin alignment of phi mesons (STAR Collaboration, 2022) and predictions from the quark coalescence model based on Lambda polarization data (Liang and Wang, 2004; Yang et al., 2017). The spin correlation between strange and anti-strange quarks due to strong-force fields was proposed by Sheng et al. (2019, 2022) to explain the phi meson spin alignment. This paper enables experimental tests of that explanation by providing measurable observables for hyperon spin-spin correlations in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
