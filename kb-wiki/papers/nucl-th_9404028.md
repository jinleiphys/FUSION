# Effects of N$^{*}$(1440) resonance on subthreshold kaon, antikaon and antiproton production

> We study the effects of $N^{*}(1440)$ resonance on the production of kaons, antikaons and antiprotons in heavy-ion collisions at subthreshold energies. Using free-space widths for the $Δ(1232)$ and the $N^{*}(1440)$ resonance and free-space thresholds for particle production, it is found that the $N^{*}$-baryon interactions contribute about 10\%, 25\% and 80\% to the yield of kaon, antikaon and antiproton, respectively. Doubling the widths of resonances due to possible medium effects, both the total and the $N^{*}$ induced antiproton yield decrease by about a factor of 3. Using a reduced antiproton production threshold in a nuclear medium as a result of dropping in-medium nucleon and antiproton masses, the total and the $N^{*}$ induced antiproton yield increase by a factor of 35 and 27, respectively, and the contribution of the $N^{*}$ induced collisions is reduced to about 50\% of the total.

# Effects of N*(1440) resonance on subthreshold kaon, antikaon and antiproton production

## Key claim

The N*(1440) resonance contributes about 80% to antiproton yield in subthreshold heavy-ion collisions when using free-space widths and thresholds, but this contribution drops to about 50% when a reduced in-medium antiproton production threshold is used; the effect on kaon and antikaon production is smaller (about 10% and 25%, respectively) and does not substantially alter previous conclusions from studies that omitted the N*(1440) resonance.

## Method

The authors used a hadronic transport model for heavy-ion collisions that includes both the Delta(1232) and N*(1440) resonances, with particle production probabilities calculated perturbatively and without final-state interactions. Elementary cross sections for kaon, antikaon, and antiproton production were taken from the literature, with kaon production cross sections in N*-involved baryon-baryon collisions assumed equal to those in Delta-induced collisions, and antikaon and antiproton cross sections in resonance-induced collisions assumed equal to those in proton-proton collisions.

## Key numbers

- In Au+Au collisions at beam energies between 1 and 2 GeV/nucleon and impact parameters between 1 and 9 fm, N*-involved collisions contribute about 11% to total kaon production probability across the whole energy and impact parameter range.
- In Ni+Ni collisions at 1.85 and 2.1 GeV/nucleon, N*-involved collisions contribute about 25% to total antikaon yields.
- For antiproton production in Ni+Ni collisions, N*-involved collisions contribute about 90% at 1.85 GeV/nucleon and about 70% at 2.1 GeV/nucleon.
- Doubling the widths of Delta and N* resonances reduces both total and N*-induced antiproton yield by about a factor of 3, and reduces the N* contribution from 70% to 65% at 2.1 GeV/nucleon.
- Using a reduced density-dependent antiproton production threshold, the total antiproton yield increases by a factor of 35 and the N*-induced yield increases by a factor of 27, with the N* contribution dropping to about 50% of the total.
- In central Au+Au collisions, the maximum number of N*(1440) resonances increases from 7 to 17 as beam energy increases from 1 to 2 GeV/nucleon, while the maximum number of Delta(1232) resonances increases from 52 to 89; the N*/Delta abundance ratio increases from 14% to 19%.

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the relativistic transport model that included only the Delta(1232) resonance and omitted higher resonances, such as those by Ko et al. (1992) on kaons, Ko et al. (1993) on antikaons, and Ko et al. (1993) on antiprotons. The authors find that the N*(1440) resonance does not significantly affect previous conclusions about kaon and antikaon production, but it does contribute appreciably to antiproton production, meaning future quantitative studies of in-medium antiproton properties should include the N*(1440) resonance. The paper also enables further investigation of whether antiproton production can serve as a probe of N*(1440) resonance formation in the resonance matter created in heavy-ion collisions.
