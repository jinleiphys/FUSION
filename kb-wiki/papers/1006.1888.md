# A study of nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering in configuration space

> A new computational method for solving the nucleon-deuteron breakup scattering problem has been applied to study the elastic neutron- and proton-deuteron scattering on the basis of the configuration-space Faddeev-Noyes-Noble-Merkuriev equations. This method is based on the spline-decomposition in the angular variable and on a generalization of the Numerov method for the hyperradius. The Merkuriev-Gignoux-Laverne approach has been generalized for arbitrary nucleon-nucleon potentials and with an arbitrary number of partial waves. The nucleon-deuteron observables at the incident nucleon energy 3 MeV have been calculated using the charge-independent AV14 nucleon-nucleon potential including the Coulomb force for the proton-deuteron scattering. Results have been compared with those of other authors and with experimental proton-deuteron scattering data.

# A study of nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering in configuration space

## Key claim
The authors present a new computational method for solving the nucleon-deuteron breakup scattering problem in configuration space, generalizing the Merkuriev-Gignoux-Laverne approach to arbitrary nucleon-nucleon potentials with an arbitrary number of partial waves. The method is demonstrated for elastic neutron-deuteron and proton-deuteron scattering at 3 MeV using the AV14 potential, but the results show minor disagreements with benchmark calculations (2-3% for some polarization observables) attributed to truncation of the two-body angular momentum to j23 = 3 or 4 rather than the larger values used in reference calculations.

## Method
The authors solve the Faddeev-Noyes-Noble-Merkuriev equations in configuration space using a polar coordinate system (hyperradius rho and angle theta). The numerical method combines spline-decomposition of the angular variable using fifth-degree Hermitian splines with a generalization of the Numerov method for the hyperradial coordinate, and the resulting linear system is solved via a least-squares method for the elastic scattering amplitudes. For proton-deuteron scattering, the Coulomb interaction is included exactly through partial-wave expansion of the Coulomb potential in the MGL basis, with asymptotic conditions implemented using regular and irregular Coulomb functions.

## Key numbers
- Incident nucleon energy: 3 MeV for both neutron-deuteron and proton-deuteron elastic scattering calculations
- Total three-body angular momentum up to M = 15/2 used in all calculations
- For nd scattering: two-body angular momentum j23 taken up to 3 (62 partial waves); reference calculation used j23 up to 4 (98 partial waves)
- For pd scattering: j23 taken up to 4 (152 partial waves); reference calculation used j23 up to 5 (207 partial waves)
- Differences between the authors' nd results and Kievsky et al. benchmark: about 2-3% for Ay and iT11 maximum values; about 1% for T20, T21, and T22
- For pd scattering: the authors' Ay prediction is about 5% lower than Deltuva et al.; iT11 prediction is about 10% higher; tensor analyzing powers agree to better than 5%
- For pd breakup at Elab = 14.1 MeV with Malfliet-Tjon I-III potential: disagreement in inelasticity parameters about 1% and about 0.1 degree for phase shifts compared to Deltuva et al.
- Coulomb potential parameters: e^2 = 1.44 MeV*fm, hbar^2/m = 41.47 MeV*fm^2

## Context
This work extends the configuration-space Faddeev approach initiated by Merkuriev, Gignoux, and Laverne (MGL), which was previously limited to S-wave interactions and simple nucleon-nucleon potentials. The results are compared with benchmark calculations by Kievsky et al. (hyperspherical harmonic method with Kohn Variational Principle) and Deltuva et al. (momentum-space screening and renormalization procedure). The method enables future inclusion of three-nucleon forces and studies of the Ay puzzle in nucleon-deuteron scattering, as well as investigations using Kukulin's potential and LS-modified three-nucleon forces.
