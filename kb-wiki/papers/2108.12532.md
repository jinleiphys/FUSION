# Mass Spectra of One or Two Heavy Quark Mesons and Diquarks within a Nonrelativistic Potential Model

> In this article, the mass spectra of mesons with one or two heavy quarks and their diquarks partners are estimated within a non-relativistic framework by solving Schrödinger equation with an effective potential inspired by a symmetry preserving Poincaré covariant vector-vector contact interaction model of quantum chromodynamics. Matrix Numerov method is implemented for this purpose. In our survey of mesons with heavy quarks, we fix the model parameter to the masses of ground-states and then extend our calculations for radial excitations and diquarks. The potential model used in this work gives results which are in good agreement with experimental data and other theoretical calculations.

## Key claim
The paper estimates mass spectra of heavy-light and heavy-heavy mesons and their diquark partners using a non-relativistic potential model, but the authors acknowledge that the non-relativistic description "might not be fully justified" for light quarks, and the approach is restricted to pseudoscalar and vector mesons (plus scalar and axial-vector diquarks) where spin-orbit terms vanish.

## Method
The authors solve the radial Schrodinger equation using the Matrix Numerov Method with an effective potential derived from the Fourier transform of a symmetry-preserving vector-vector contact-interaction (CI) gluon propagator. Dressed-quark masses are fixed to ground-state meson masses, then spin-spin corrections are added using a parametrized hyperfine interaction, and the same potential (with a factor 1/2 reduction in interaction strength) is applied to compute diquark masses as color-antitriplet partners of the corresponding mesons.

## Key numbers
- Dressed-quark masses used: M_u = 0.40 GeV, M_s = 0.53 GeV, M_c = 1.48 GeV, M_b = 4.73 GeV
- Model parameters: Lambda = 0.905 GeV, m_g = 0.5 GeV, lambda = 0.24 GeV, alpha = 0.93 pi
- Ground-state D0 meson mass: 1.86 GeV (experimental), 1.98 GeV (model with spin-spin correction)
- Ground-state B+ meson mass: 5.28 GeV (experimental), 5.25 GeV (model with spin-spin correction)
- Ground-state scalar diquark [c u] mass: 1.88 GeV (model with best-fit spin-spin term)
- Ground-state scalar diquark [u b] mass: 5.31 GeV (model with best-fit spin-spin term)
- Mass splitting between pseudoscalar and vector D mesons: 150 MeV (experimental); for B mesons: 50 MeV (experimental)
- Excited 2S pseudoscalar D0 mass: 2.67 GeV (model), compared to 2.58 GeV from Godfrey (2015)

## Context
This work extends the contact-interaction (CI) model approach previously applied to light hadrons by Gutierrez-Guerrero (2010) and to charmed and bottom mesons by Bedolla (2015) and Serna (2017). The results are compared with the Godfrey-Isgur model (Godfrey 2015) for charm mesons and with Lu (2016) for bottom mesons, and with diquark predictions from Gutierrez-Guerrero (2019) and Yin (2019). The computed diquark masses are intended for future use in calculating baryon masses within a quark-diquark picture.
