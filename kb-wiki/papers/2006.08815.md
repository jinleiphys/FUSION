# Analytical attractor for Bjorken expansion

> We present an analytic solution of a simple set of equations that govern the expansion of boost-invariant plasmas of massless particles. These equations describe, approximately, the early time, collisionless regime, and the transition to hydrodynamics at late time. Their mathematical structure encompasses all versions of second order hydrodynamics when applied to Bjorken flows. The analytic solution provides an explicit expression for the attractor solution that connects the collisionless regime to hydrodynamics. It also constitutes a neat example of an application of the theory of resurgence in asymptotic series.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact analytical solution for the attractor that connects the early-time collisionless (free-streaming) regime to the late-time hydrodynamic regime in Bjorken-expanding plasmas, but the solution is derived from a two-moment truncation of the full kinetic equation, which only approximately captures the full dynamics.

## Method

The authors start from a kinetic equation with a relaxation-time approximation collision term for massless particles undergoing boost-invariant (Bjorken) expansion. They truncate the infinite hierarchy of Legendre-moment equations to the first two moments (energy density and viscous pressure), obtaining a pair of coupled ordinary differential equations in proper time. By introducing a dimensionless variable w (the ratio of proper time to relaxation time, acting as an inverse Knudsen number) and a function g(w) related to the pressure anisotropy, they reduce the system to a single first-order nonlinear ODE. This ODE is then transformed into Kummer's confluent hypergeometric equation, yielding an exact solution in terms of confluent hypergeometric functions M and U.

## Key numbers

* The coefficients in the moment equations are fixed numbers: a0 = 4/3, a1 = 38/21, b1 = 8/15, c0 = 2/3.
* The free-streaming fixed points of the nonlinear ODE are g+ = -0.929 (stable) and g- = -2.213 (unstable).
* The hydrodynamic fixed point is g* = -4/3.
* The radius of convergence of the small-w expansion of the attractor is approximately w ≈ 0.3314.
* The exponential correction factor in the trans-series is ζ(w) = e^{-w} w^{a0 - a1} = e^{-w} w^{4/3 - 38/21}.

## Context

This work extends the identification of attractor solutions in relativistic hydrodynamics, previously studied by Heller and Spalinski (2015), Romatschke (2017), Denicol and Noronha (2017), and Kurkela et al. (2019), by providing the first fully analytic expression for the attractor. The solution unifies all versions of second-order hydrodynamics (e.g., Israel-Stewart, Baier et al. 2007, Denicol et al. 2012) for Bjorken flows, and it enables precise tests of resurgence theory in asymptotic series, as well as a clean demonstration of how exponential "non-hydrodynamic" modes cancel imaginary parts in Borel summation.
