# Constraints on proton structure from precision atomic physics measurements

> Ground-state hyperfine splittings in hydrogen and muonium are very well measured. Their difference, after correcting for magnetic moment and reduced mass effects, is due solely to proton structure--the large QED contributions for a pointlike nucleus essentially cancel. The rescaled hyperfine difference depends on the Zemach radius, a fundamental measure of the proton, computed as an integral over a product of electric and magnetic proton form factors. The determination of the Zemach radius, (1.043 +/- 0.016) fm, from atomic physics tightly constrains fits to accelerator measurements of proton form factors. Conversely, we can use muonium data to extract an ``experimental'' value for QED corrections to hydrogenic hyperfine data; we find that measurement and theory are consistent.

## Key claim

The difference between the ground-state hyperfine splittings of hydrogen and muonium, after correcting for magnetic moment and reduced mass effects, isolates the proton structure contribution because the large QED contributions for a pointlike nucleus essentially cancel. This atomic-physics determination of the Zemach radius, (1.043 ± 0.016) fm, tightly constrains fits to accelerator measurements of proton form factors, particularly in light of discrepancies between Rosenbluth and polarization transfer measurements of the electric form factor.

## Method

The authors define a fractional difference Δ_hfs between the measured hydrogen and muonium hyperfine splittings, rescaled by the ratio of magnetic moments and reduced mass factors. They then subtract known QED, recoil, vacuum polarization, and weak interaction corrections to isolate the proton structure contribution Δ_S. From Δ_S they subtract the polarization contribution (computed from spin-dependent structure functions) to extract the Zemach term Δ_Z, which is proportional to the Zemach radius ⟨r⟩_Z, defined as an integral over the product of the proton's electric and magnetic form factors G_E and G_M.

## Key numbers

- Zemach radius extracted from atomic physics: ⟨r⟩_Z = (1.043 ± 0.016) fm
- Total proton structure contribution to hydrogen hyperfine splitting: Δ_S = -38.62(16) ppm
- Zemach term: Δ_Z = -(40.0 ± 0.6) ppm
- Polarization contribution: Δ_pol = 1.4 ± 0.6 ppm
- Fractional difference Δ_hfs = 145.51(4) ppm
- Measured hydrogen hyperfine splitting: 1,420.4057517667(9) MHz
- Measured muonium hyperfine splitting: 4,463.302765(53) MHz
- Ratio of muon to proton magnetic moments: μ_μ/μ_p = 3.18334520(20)
- Proton anomalous magnetic moment: κ_p = 1.792847351(28)
- Radiative correction to Zemach term: δ_Z^rad = 0.0153 (for dipole form factors with Λ² = 0.71 GeV²)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Zemach (1956) and Friar (2003) by using the cancellation of QED corrections between hydrogen and muonium hyperfine splittings to obtain a precise proton structure constraint. The result provides an independent check on form factor parameterizations such as those of Arrington (2003) and Brodsky et al. (2003), and is particularly relevant given the discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization transfer measurements of G_E reported by Jones (1999) and Gayou (2001). The method can be extended to extract the proton charge radius from Lamb shift differences, and to obtain an experimental value for QED bound-state radiative corrections.
