# Calibrating the medium effects of light clusters in heavy-ion collisions

> We propose a Bayesian inference estimation of in-medium modification of the cluster self-energies from light nuclei multiplicities measured in selected samples of central $^{136,124}$Xe$+^{124,112}$Sn collisions with the INDRA apparatus. The data are interpreted with a relativistic quasi-particle cluster approach in the mean-field approximation without any prior assumption on the thermal parameters of the model. An excellent reproduction is obtained for H and He isotope multiplicities, and compatible posterior distributions are found for the unknown thermal parameters. We conclude that the cluster-$σ$-meson coupling is temperature dependent, becoming weaker when the temperature increases, in agreement with microscopic quantum statistical calculations. This implies a faster decrease of the light cluster abundances with temperature than previously estimated.

## Key claim

The cluster-σ-meson coupling ratio \(x_s\) is temperature dependent, becoming weaker as temperature increases, which implies a faster decrease of light cluster abundances with temperature than previously estimated; this conclusion is drawn from a Bayesian analysis that avoids the ideal gas assumption used in prior work.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference on light-nuclei multiplicities (H and He isotopes) measured in central \(^{136,124}\text{Xe}+^{124,112}\text{Sn}\) collisions at 32 MeV/nucleon with the INDRA apparatus, sorted into bins of average Coulomb-corrected radial velocity \(v_{\text{surf}}\). The data were interpreted using a relativistic mean-field (RMF) quasi-particle model (FSU and DD2 parameterizations) that includes nucleons and clusters as independent particles coupled to \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), and \(\rho\) mesons, with the cluster-\(\sigma\) coupling ratio \(x_s\) treated as a free parameter; no prior assumption was made on the thermal parameters (temperature \(T\) and baryon density \(\rho\)), and independent posterior distributions were obtained for each velocity bin and colliding system.

## Key numbers

- The average baryonic density extracted from the data is \(\sim 0.015\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), interpreted as the chemical freeze-out density.
- Temperatures extracted from the data range from \(\sim 6\ \text{MeV}\) to \(\sim 10\ \text{MeV}\).
- For the FSU model, the quadratic fit \(x_s = aT^2 + bT + c\) yields median parameters: \(a = -0.00203\ \text{MeV}^{-2}\) (1\(\sigma\): \(\pm 0.00003\)), \(b = 0.01477\ \text{MeV}^{-1}\) (1\(\sigma\): \(\pm 0.00047\)), \(c = 0.90560\) (1\(\sigma\): \(\pm 0.0018\)).
- For the DD2 model, the quadratic fit yields median parameters: \(a = -0.00171\ \text{MeV}^{-2}\) (1\(\sigma\): \(\pm 0.00003\)), \(b = 0.01289\ \text{MeV}^{-1}\) (1\(\sigma\): \(\pm 0.00039\)), \(c = 0.91893\) (1\(\sigma\): \(\pm 0.00150\)).
- Above \(T \sim 8\ \text{MeV}\), the total cluster abundances predicted with the temperature-dependent \(x_s\) are systematically lower than those from a constant \(x_s = 0.92 \pm 0.02\) (from Pais et al. 2020), with a difference exceeding 20% at \(T \sim 10\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of INDRA data (Qin et al. 2011, Hempel et al. 2015, Pais et al. 2020) that calibrated cluster in-medium couplings using equilibrium constants or ideal-gas assumptions; here, the authors directly fit the measured particle abundances without those hypotheses, enabling an a-posteriori verification of chemical equilibrium. The results are consistent with microscopic quantum statistical calculations (Ropke et al. 2015) and provide a temperature-dependent cluster-\(\sigma\) coupling that can be used in equations of state for supernova core-collapse and binary neutron star merger simulations, where cluster abundances affect transport properties and nucleosynthesis.
