# What can we learn from electromagnetic plasmas about the quark-gluon plasma?

> Ultra-relativistic electromagnetic plasmas can be used for improving our understanding of the quark-gluon plasma. In the weakly coupled regime both plasmas can be described by transport theoretical and quantum field theoretical methods leading to similar results for the plasma properties (dielectric tensor, dispersion relations, plasma frequency, Debye screening, transport coefficients, damping and particle production rates). In particular, future experiments with ultra-relativistic electron-positron plasmas in ultra-strong laser fields might open the possibility to test these predictions, e.g. the existence of a new fermionic plasma wave (plasmino). In the strongly coupled regime electromagnetic plasmas such as complex plasmas can be used as models or at least analogies for the quark-gluon plasma possibly produced in relativistic heavy-ion experiments. For example, pair correlation functions can be used to investigate the equation of state and cross section enhancement for parton scattering can be explained.

## Key claim
Ultra-relativistic electromagnetic plasmas can serve as testbeds for quark-gluon plasma (QGP) physics, but the analogy is strongest in the weakly coupled regime where both systems yield similar results from transport theory and quantum field theory; in the strongly coupled regime, electromagnetic plasmas (especially complex plasmas) provide only qualitative analogies rather than direct quantitative predictions.

## Method
The paper reviews the parallel theoretical treatment of ultra-relativistic QED plasmas (electron-positron) and QGP using the Vlasov equation and finite-temperature quantum field theory to derive dielectric tensors, dispersion relations, and collective modes. For the strongly coupled regime, it compares results from molecular dynamics simulations and experimental observations of complex (dusty) plasmas, such as pair correlation functions and viscosity measurements, with QGP properties inferred from relativistic heavy-ion collision data at RHIC.

## Key numbers
- The effective strong coupling constant at RHIC energies (T < 4 T_c) is α_s = 0.3 – 0.5, making perturbation theory questionable.
- The Coulomb coupling parameter Γ for the QGP at RHIC (T = 200 MeV, α_s = 0.3 – 0.5, d = 0.5 fm) is estimated as Γ = 1.5 – 6.
- The Debye screening length from the static limit of the longitudinal dielectric function is λ_D = 1/(√3 m_γ), where m_γ = eT/3 for QED and m_g = √[(1 + n_F/6)/3] gT for QGP.
- The minimum of the viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio in the one-component plasma at Γ = 12 is about 5 times above the string theory (AdS/CFT) lower limit of η/s ≤ ħ/(4π k_B).
- The estimated parton cross section enhancement in the QGP due to non-linear effects is a factor of 2 – 9 compared to perturbative results.

## Context
This review extends the analogy between electromagnetic and QCD plasmas previously discussed by Mrowczynski (review article) and others, showing that transport theory methods from classical plasma physics (Vlasov equation, Boltzmann equation with relaxation time approximation) and finite-temperature field theory (Matsubara formalism, hard thermal loop resummation by Braaten and Pisarski) yield parallel results for both systems. The paper enables future experimental tests of QGP predictions, such as the existence of the plasmino mode, using ultra-relativistic electron-positron plasmas produced by ultra-strong laser fields (Shen, Thoma), and suggests that complex plasma experiments (Thomas, Fortov) can provide qualitative insight into the strongly coupled QGP liquid phase observed at RHIC.
