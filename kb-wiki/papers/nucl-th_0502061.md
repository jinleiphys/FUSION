# Spin-dependent neutrino-induced nucleon knockout

> We study neutrino-induced nucleon knockout off atomic nuclei and examine the polarization properties of the ejectile. A detailed study of the spin dependence of the outgoing nucleon is presented. The numerical results are derived within a non-relativistic plane-wave impulse-approximation approach. Our calculations reveal large polarization asymmetries, and clear dissimilarities between neutrino- and antineutrino-induced reactions. They reflect the fact that neutrino-induced nucleon knockout is dominated by the transverse axial current and gains its major contributions from forward nucleon emission and backward lepton scattering.

## Key claim

Neutrino-induced nucleon knockout exhibits large polarization asymmetries, with the ejectile's spin predominantly anti-parallel to its momentum for neutrinos and parallel for antineutrinos, arising from the dominance of the transverse axial current and the specific spin dependence of the hadronic matrix elements.

## Method

The authors derived expressions for the spin-dependent differential cross section of neutral-current neutrino- and antineutrino-induced nucleon knockout within a non-relativistic plane-wave impulse-approximation approach. They computed cross sections and longitudinal polarization asymmetries for nucleon knockout from 16O and 208Pb using a mean-field Woods-Saxon single-particle potential, focusing on the longitudinal spin component of the outgoing nucleon along its momentum direction.

## Key numbers

- For neutrino-induced nucleon knockout from 16O, the cross section peaks strongly for forward nucleon knockout (θ_N ≈ 0) and is dominated by spin-down (s_N^l = -1) ejectile polarization, with the difference growing steadily with incoming neutrino energy.
- The longitudinal asymmetry A^l for neutrino-induced reactions is large and negative at forward scattering angles (e.g., at θ_N ≈ 0 for ε_i = 50-250 MeV), switching to positive values at backward angles (θ_N = π).
- For antineutrino-induced reactions, the asymmetry A^l is positive at forward scattering angles, indicating dominance of s_N^l = +1 nucleon knockout, with less pronounced angular dependence than for neutrinos.
- Neutrino-induced cross sections are larger than antineutrino-induced cross sections, with the difference rising steeply with incoming lepton energy (shown for ε_i up to 250 MeV in 16O).
- The kinematic factors v_T and v_T' are of the same order of magnitude and same sign, leading to suppression of spin-up contributions (v_T - v_T' very small) for neutrinos and enhancement for antineutrinos.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Jachowicz et al., 2004) on the spin dependence of neutrino-induced nucleon knockout to a systematic investigation of ejectile polarization asymmetries. It contrasts with electron scattering polarization studies (Ryckebusch et al., 1999; Lava et al., 2004; Strauch et al., 2003) by focusing on weak interaction processes where axial contributions dominate. The results enable potential applications in distinguishing neutrinos from antineutrinos in neutral-current reactions at energies below charged-current thresholds, relevant for astrophysical processes and CP-violation studies in neutrino oscillations.
