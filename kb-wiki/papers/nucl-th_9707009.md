# Relativistic Calculations for the Exclusive Photoproduction of Eta Mesons from Complex Nuclei

> A relativistic model for the quasifree photoproduction of eta meson from complex nuclei is developed. The interactions between fields are introduced through effective Lagrangians. Contributions from several nucleon resonances as well as nucleon Born terms and vector meson exchange diagrams are included. Nucleon and eta wavefunctions are solutions of Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations, respectively. Final state interactions of the outgoing particles are included via optical potentials. The effects of these interactions on the cross sections and photon asymmetries are studied and are found to be large. Calculations indicate that at energies near threshold the exclusive reaction takes place mainly through formation of the S_{11}(1535) resonance. Comparisons with the non-relativistic calculations show differences between the two approaches both for the cross sections and photon asymmetries. We give some detailed predictions for the reaction observables for exclusive photoproduction on ^{12}C, ^{16}O and ^{40}Ca.

## Key claim

The paper develops a relativistic model for exclusive quasifree photoproduction of eta mesons from complex nuclei, finding that the S11(1535) resonance dominates the reaction from threshold up to photon energies of about 1.1 GeV, but the authors note that no experimental data exist for this exclusive reaction, so the predictions are intended to identify kinematical regions where cross sections may be large enough to measure.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic model using effective Lagrangians for the interactions between fields, including contributions from nucleon Born terms, t-channel vector meson exchange, and five nucleon resonances (S11(1535), P11(1440), S11(1650), P11(1670), and D13(1520)). Bound nucleon wavefunctions are solutions of the Dirac equation with scalar and vector potentials from relativistic mean field theory, while the eta meson wavefunction solves the Klein-Gordon equation. Calculations are performed in both the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) and the Distorted Wave Approximation (DWA), where final state interactions of the outgoing proton and eta meson are included via optical potentials. The triple differential cross section and photon asymmetry are computed for targets of 12C, 16O, and 40Ca.

## Key numbers

- Incident photon energy of 750 MeV is used for the main cross section calculations on 12C leading to the ground state of 11B
- The S11(1535) resonance dominates the cross section from threshold up to a photon energy of about 1.1 GeV
- Largest cross sections occur in the photon energy range 750-950 MeV
- For 12C, relatively large cross sections are predicted for eta polar angles 10-30 degrees and proton polar angles 15-30 degrees
- The pair of angles theta_eta = theta_p = 30 degrees is identified as a preferable experimental setting
- The S11(1535) resonance decays nearly 50% of the time to an eta meson and a nucleon, while the S11(1650) has a decay rate below 2%

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach of Benmerrouche et al. (1995) for the elementary gamma + p -> eta + p reaction to complex nuclei, and builds on the nonrelativistic calculations of Lee et al. (1995) for quasifree photoproduction on heavier nuclei. The relativistic treatment is motivated by the successes of the relativistic mean field approach of Walecka (1974) and follows a similar methodology to the kaon photoproduction model of Bennhold and Wright (1991). The authors compare their results to the nonrelativistic approach of Lee et al. (1995) and find differences between the two approaches for both cross sections and photon asymmetries. The work enables future experimental measurements at facilities such as Jefferson Lab, Mainz, and Bates to test these predictions and investigate medium modifications of hadron properties in nuclei.
