# Applied Chiral Perturbation Theory

> I consider some selected topics in chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) as probed at colliders such as DA$Φ$NE. Emphasis is put on processes involving pions in the isospin zero S-wave which require multi-loop calculations. These include the scalar form factor of the pion, two--photon fusion into pion pairs and $K_{\ell 4}$--decays. The physics of the chiral anomaly is briefly touched upon

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) provides a rigorous, systematic framework for calculating low-energy hadronic processes, but multi-loop calculations are essential for processes involving pions in the isospin zero S-wave because strong final-state interactions produce corrections that can reach 30% or more even near threshold, as demonstrated for the scalar form factor of the pion and two-photon fusion into neutral pion pairs.

## Method

The paper reviews selected applications of CHPT at next-to-leading order (one loop, O(E⁴)) and next-to-next-to-leading order (two loop, O(E⁶)) for processes involving pions in the isospin zero S-wave. For the scalar form factor of the pion, a two-loop representation is constructed using unitarity to relate the imaginary part to known one-loop ππ scattering amplitudes, with subtraction constants fixed from empirical knowledge of the form factor at the origin. For γγ → π⁰π⁰, a full two-loop calculation is performed, with three new low-energy constants estimated from resonance exchange (primarily the ω meson). For Kℓ4 decays, a dispersive representation using a modified Omnès function is employed to incorporate higher-order effects beyond the one-loop calculation.

## Key numbers

- Weinberg's leading-order prediction for the S-wave, isospin zero ππ scattering length: a₀⁰ = 7 Mπ² / (32π Fπ²)
- One-loop CHPT prediction for a₀⁰: 0.20 ± 0.01, compared to experimental value 0.23 ± 0.08
- Phase of the CP-violation parameter ε': Φ(ε') = (45 ± 6)° at s = M_K⁰², compared to experimental value (43 ± 8)°
- Ratio of experimental to one-loop cross section for γγ → π⁰π⁰ at 400 MeV: (σ_exp / σ_1-loop)^(1/2) = 1.3
- Tree-level prediction for Kℓ4 form factors F and G: F = G = M_K / (√2 Fπ) = 3.74; empirical threshold value: 5.59 ± 0.14
- Leading-order WZW prediction for the vector form factor H in Kℓ4 decays: H = -√2 M_K³ / (8π² Fπ³) = -2.66; experimental value: -2.68 ± 0.68
- π⁰ → 2γ decay width from chiral anomaly: Γ = (7.6 eV) × (N_c²/9); experimental value: (7.7 ± 0.6) eV
- Leading-order WZW prediction for γ → 3π amplitude: 9.7 GeV⁻³; empirical determination: 12.9 ± 0.9 ± 0.5 GeV⁻³

## Context

This invited lecture reviews and extends the CHPT framework developed by Weinberg (1966, 1979), Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985), and others, focusing on processes relevant for the DAΦNE φ-factory and intense kaon facilities. The two-loop calculations for the scalar form factor (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1984; Bijnens, Colangelo, and Gasser, 1994) and for γγ → π⁰π⁰ (Bellucci, Gasser, and Sainio, 1994) resolve long-standing discrepancies between one-loop CHPT predictions and experimental data. The work enables precise determination of low-energy constants L₁, L₂, L₃ from Kℓ4 decays and tests of the mechanism of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, particularly the size of the quark condensate parameter B, which distinguishes the standard CHPT framework from the alternative proposed by Stern et al. (1993).
