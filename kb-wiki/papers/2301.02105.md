# Topology and emergent symmetries in dense compact star matter

> It has been found that the topology effect and the possible emergent scale and hidden local flavor symmetries at high density reveal a novel structure of the compact star matter. The $N_f\geq2$ baryons can be described by the skyrmion in the large $N_c$ limit and there is a robust topology change in the skyrmion matter approach to dense nuclear matter. The hidden scale and local flavor symmetries which are sources introducing the lightest scalar meson -- dilaton -- and lowest lying vector mesons into to nonlinear chiral effective theory are seen to play important roles in understanding the nuclear force. We review in this paper the generalized nuclear effective theory (G$n$EFT), which applicable to nuclear matter from low density to the compact star density, constructed with the robust conclusion from the topology approach to dense matter and emergent scale and hidden local flavor symmetries. The topology change at density larger than two times saturation density $n_0$ encoded in the parameters of the effective field theory is interpreted as the hadron-quark continuity in the sense of Cheshire Cat Principle. A novel feature predicted in this theory that has not been found before is the precocious appearance of the conformal sound velocity in the cores of massive stars, although the trace of the energy-momentum tensor of the system is not zero. That is, in contrast to the usual picture, the cores of massive stars are composed of quasiparticles of fractional baryon charges, neither baryons nor deconfined quarks. Hidden scale and local flavor symmetries emerge and give rise a resolution of the longstanding $g_A$ quench problem in nuclei transition. To illustrate the rationality of the GnEFT, we finally confront the generalized effective field theory to the global properties of neutron star and the data from gravitational wave detections.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at densities above approximately twice nuclear saturation density, combined with emergent scale and hidden local flavor symmetries, leads to a "pseudoconformal" structure in dense compact star matter where the sound velocity reaches the conformal limit \(v_s^2/c^2 \simeq 1/3\) even though scale symmetry is not restored and the system remains in the confined phase.

## Method

The authors construct a generalized nuclear effective field theory (G\(n\)EFT) that includes nucleons, pions, the lightest scalar meson (dilaton \(\sigma\)), and the lowest-lying vector mesons (\(\rho\) and \(\omega\)), incorporating hidden scale symmetry via the Crewther-Tunstall (CT) scheme and hidden local symmetry (HLS) for vector mesons. Model-independent conclusions from the skyrmion crystal approach to dense matter, specifically the topology change at density \(n_{1/2}\) and the density-independence of the pion decay constant and nucleon mass above that density, are implemented through (extended) Brown-Rho scaling of medium-modified parameters. The equation of state is constructed using the \(V_{lowk}\) renormalization group approach, constrained by nuclear matter properties around saturation density and by astrophysical observations.

## Key numbers

* The topology change occurs at a density \(n_{1/2} \gsim 2n_0\), where \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is nuclear saturation density; the constraint from terrestrial experiments and astrophysical observations gives \(2.0 n_0 \lsim n_{1/2} \lsim 4.0 n_0\).
* In the half-skyrmion phase above \(n_{1/2}\), the sound velocity saturates the conformal limit \(v_s^2/c^2 \simeq 1/3\).
* The medium-modified pion decay constant \(f_\pi^\ast\) and nucleon mass \(m_N^\ast\) decrease with density until \(n_{1/2}\), then become density-independent above \(n_{1/2}\).
* The space-averaged quark condensate \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle^\ast\) goes to zero in the half-skyrmion matter, while chiral symmetry is not restored.

## Context

This work extends the generalized nuclear effective field theory (G\(n\)EFT) developed by Paeng, Ma, and collaborators (2015-2018), which incorporates hidden scale and local flavor symmetries into chiral effective theory. The topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions, first observed in the crystal approach by Kugler and collaborators (1988-1989), provides a robust model-independent input that distinguishes this approach from standard chiral effective field theory (S\(\chi\)EFT) and from hadron-quark continuity models such as those by Baym (2017) and McLerran (2018). The predicted pseudoconformal structure with precocious conformal sound velocity enables a resolution of the longstanding \(g_A\) quench problem in nuclear transitions and provides a framework for interpreting neutron star properties and gravitational wave data.
