# Strong Coupling Universality at Large N for Pure CFT Thermodynamics in 2+1 dimensions

> Pure CFTs have vanishing $β$-function at any value of the coupling. One example of a pure CFT is the O(N) Wess-Zumino model in 2+1 dimensions in the large N limit. This model can be analytically solved at finite temperature for any value of the coupling, and we find that its entropy density at strong coupling is exactly equal to 31/35 of the non-interacting Stefan-Boltzmann result. We show that a large class of theories with equal numbers of N-component fermions and bosons, supersymmetric or not, for a large class of interactions, exhibit the same universal ratio. For unequal numbers of fermions and bosons we find that the strong-weak thermodynamic ratio is bounded to lie in between 4/5 and 1.

## Key claim

The entropy density of the O(N) Wess-Zumino model in 2+1 dimensions at strong coupling is exactly 31/35 of the non-interacting Stefan-Boltzmann result, and this ratio is universal for a large class of theories with equal numbers of N-component fermions and bosons in the large N limit, regardless of supersymmetry or the specific form of the interaction potential.

## Method

The authors analytically solved the supersymmetric O(N) Wess-Zumino model at finite temperature in the large N limit by introducing auxiliary fields and evaluating the partition function via saddle point methods. They derived non-perturbative gap equations for the in-medium bosonic and fermionic thermal masses, solved these equations numerically for all coupling values, and computed the entropy density from the pressure. They then generalized the calculation by modifying the interaction potential and varying the relative numbers of bosonic and fermionic degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- Entropy density at strong coupling for equal numbers of bosons and fermions: \( s = \frac{31}{35} s_{\text{free}} \approx 0.885714 \, s_{\text{free}} \)
- Strong coupling limit of the bosonic in-medium mass: \( m_B = 2 \ln \frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2} \simeq 0.96 \)
- Strong coupling limit of the fermionic in-medium mass: \( m_F = 0 \)
- Free theory entropy density: \( s_{\text{free}} = \frac{21 N T^2}{8 \pi} \zeta(3) \)
- Bosonic entropy density at strong coupling: \( s_B = \frac{4}{5} s_B(0) = \frac{6 N T^2}{5 \pi} \zeta(3) \)
- Ratio of fermionic to bosonic free entropy density in 2+1 dimensions: \( s_F(0) = \frac{3}{4} s_B(0) \)
- Bound on the strong-weak thermodynamic ratio for unequal numbers of fermions and bosons: \( \frac{4}{5} \leq \lim_{\lambda\rightarrow \infty} \frac{s}{s_{\text{free}}} \leq 1 \)

## Context

This work extends the finding of a strong-weak thermodynamic ratio of 4/5 for purely bosonic pure CFTs in 2+1 dimensions from Romatschke (2019) to theories containing fermions. It generalizes the earlier result for scalar \(\phi^4\) theory in 2+1 dimensions from Drummond (1997) and Sachdev (1993). The universal ratio 31/35 for theories with equal numbers of bosons and fermions provides a new analytic benchmark for strongly coupled CFT thermodynamics, and the bounded range 4/5 to 1 for unequal numbers suggests possible constraints on gravitational duals or other non-perturbative approaches.
