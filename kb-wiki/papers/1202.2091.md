# Multi-minijet Contribution to Hadronic Spectra and Correlations in Pb-Pb Collisions at 2.76 TeV and beyond

> In heavy-ion collisions at very high energy the density of produced jets can be so high that the possibility of hadrons produced by recombination of shower partons in overlapping minijets may become important. We study such multi-minijet contribution to the hadron spectra and to dihadron correlation in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV at LHC. We adjust the parameter controlling the momentum degradation of semihard partons by fitting the charged-particle distribution up to $p_T\sim 16$ GeV/c. The relative magnitudes of different identified hadrons and of various partonic components are fixed by the recombination formalism. We find that the coalescence of shower patons from adjacent miniijets can be as much as from single jets for meson production, and even more so for proton, but never dominant over other components. In 3-shower-parton recombination the ratio of 2-jet to 1-jet contributions increases with collision energy; its maximum can exceed 2 at 5.5 TeV. Two-hadron correlation exhibits a broad peak on transverse rapidities, confirming that minijets play a central role at low $p_T$.

## Key claim

The coalescence of shower partons from adjacent minijets can be as much as from single jets for meson production, and even more so for proton production, but this multi-minijet contribution is never dominant over other components such as thermal-shower recombination.

## Method

The authors used the recombination formalism to calculate hadron spectra and dihadron correlations in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, including contributions from thermal partons, single-jet shower partons, and multi-minijet (two-jet and three-jet) shower parton recombination. They adjusted the momentum degradation parameter kappa for semihard partons by fitting the charged-particle distribution up to pT ~ 16 GeV/c, using a q-dependent form kappa(q) = kappa0/(1 + kappa1 q^2) to account for the energy dependence of parton energy loss. The relative magnitudes of different identified hadrons and partonic components were fixed by the recombination formalism without additional free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Thermal parton inverse slope T = 0.38 GeV and momentum degradation parameter kappa = 2.6, determined from fitting ALICE data on pi, K, p, Lambda spectra for pT < 5 GeV/c
- Momentum degradation parameters: kappa0 = 3, kappa1 = 0.0018 (GeV/c)^-1, determined by fitting the charged hadron spectrum up to pT ~ 16 GeV/c
- For proton production at 2.76 TeV, the ratio (SSS)^2j/(SSS)^1j reaches approximately 1 at pT ~ 2 GeV/c
- At 5.5 TeV, the ratio (SSS)^2j/(SSS)^1j for proton production exceeds 2 at its peak
- The (SSS)^3j component for proton production at 5.5 TeV increases by almost two orders of magnitude at pT ~ 16 GeV/c compared to 2.76 TeV
- The peaks in transverse rapidity yt distributions occur at pT ~ 0.6 GeV/c for pions and pT ~ 1.1 GeV/c for protons

## Context

This work extends the recombination model previously applied to RHIC energies and to LHC data for pT < 5 GeV/c by Hwa and Zhu (2011), adding multi-minijet contributions that become important at LHC energies due to the high density of produced jets. The approach differs from the two-component model of Trainor and collaborators (2005-2008), which separates hadronic spectra into soft and hard components at the hadronic level, by working at the partonic level with explicit shower parton distributions. The results confirm that minijets play a central role at low pT and provide a partonic basis for the observed broad peak in transverse rapidity distributions, enabling predictions for hadron spectra and correlations at 5.5 TeV for future LHC runs.
