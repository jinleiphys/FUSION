# Rearrangement of the Fermi Surface of Dense Neutron Matter and Direct Urca Cooling of Neutron Stars

> It is proposed that a rearrangement of single-particle degrees of freedom may occur in a portion of the quantum fluid interior of a neutron star. Such a rearrangement is associated with the pronounced softening of the spin-isospin collective mode which, under increasing density, leads to pion condensation. Arguments and estimates based on fundamental relations of many-body theory show that one realization of this phenomenon could produce very rapid cooling of the star via a direct nucelon Urca process displaying a $T^5$ dependence on temperature.

# Rearrangement of the Fermi Surface of Dense Neutron Matter and Direct Urca Cooling of Neutron Stars

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a rearrangement of single-particle degrees of freedom in dense neutron matter, occurring as a precursor to pion condensation, could enable the direct Urca process to operate at densities much lower than the previously estimated threshold of at least five times the saturation density of nuclear matter, potentially producing very rapid cooling of neutron stars. The authors hedge that this is a possibility contingent on the softening of the spin-isospin collective mode, and that the effect depends on model parameters that cannot yet be derived from definitive microscopic treatments.

## Method

The authors apply Landau Fermi-liquid theory to dense neutron matter near the critical point for neutral pion condensation, using a parametrized propagator for the soft collective mode to compute the singular part of the scattering amplitude. They integrate the Landau transport equation to obtain the single-particle spectrum and solve the bifurcation condition for the quasiparticle energy to find the critical density for rearrangement and the new Fermi momenta. Numerical calculations are performed for several choices of the input parameters (β, γ, k₀) to map the phase diagram and determine the structure of the bubble rearrangement.

## Key numbers

- The critical density for neutral pion condensation ρ_{cπ} is estimated to lie in the range (0.2–0.5) fm⁻³, roughly 1–3 times the saturation density ρ₀ of symmetrical nuclear matter.
- The bifurcation point for bubble formation occurs at a momentum p₀ < 0.2 p_F, where p_F is the neutron Fermi momentum.
- The spectrum ξ(p) shows a deep depression for p ∼ (0.5–0.6) p_F.
- The conventional threshold for direct Urca cooling requires a proton fraction exceeding 11–14%, corresponding to a baryon density of at least five times ρ₀.
- The standard direct Urca emissivity formula is given as ε(n → p e⁻ ν̄) ≈ 1.2 × 10²⁷ (M*_n/M_n)(M*_p/M_p)(μ_e/100 MeV) T₉⁶ erg cm⁻³ s⁻¹, where T₉ is temperature in units of 10⁹ K.

## Context

This work extends the standard neutron-star cooling scenario, where the modified Urca process (Bahcall and Wolf, 1965; Friman and Maxwell, 1979) produces slow cooling, by proposing a mechanism that could explain the rapid cooling observed in stars like Vela, PSR2334+61, PSR0656+14, and Geminga. It builds on predictions by Migdal (1978) regarding softening of the spin-isospin mode and prior work on pion condensation (Migdal, 1978; MSTV; Ph) and kaon condensation (Brown, 1994). The paper enables future studies of neutrino cooling in the density regime just below pion condensation, including effects of superfluidity and neutrino opacity.
