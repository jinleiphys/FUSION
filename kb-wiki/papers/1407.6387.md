# The equation of state in (2+1)-flavor QCD

> We present results for the equation of state in (2+1)-flavor QCD using the highly improved staggered quark action and lattices with temporal extent $N_τ=6,~8,~10$, and $12$. We show that these data can be reliably extrapolated to the continuum limit and obtain a number of thermodynamic quantities and the speed of sound in the temperature range $(130-400)$ MeV. We compare our results with previous calculations, and provide an analytic parameterization of the pressure, from which other thermodynamic quantities can be calculated, for use in phenomenology. We show that the energy density in the crossover region, $145~ {\rm MeV} \leq T \leq 163$ MeV, defined by the chiral transition, is $ε_c=(0.18-0.5)~{\rm GeV}/{\rm fm}^3$, $i.e.$, $(1.2-3.1)\ ε_{\rm nuclear}$. At high temperatures, we compare our results with resummed and dimensionally reduced perturbation theory calculations. As a byproduct of our analyses, we obtain the values of the scale parameters $r_0$ from the static quark potential and $w_0$ from the gradient flow.

## Key claim
The paper presents a continuum-extrapolated equation of state for (2+1)-flavor QCD using the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action, claiming reliable results in the temperature range 130-400 MeV. The hedge is that the energy density in the crossover region (145-163 MeV) is given as a range (0.18-0.5 GeV/fm^3) rather than a single value, reflecting the crossover nature of the transition rather than a sharp phase boundary.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with the HISQ action on lattices with temporal extent Nτ = 6, 8, 10, and 12, using spatial volumes Nσ = 4 Nτ. They calculated the trace anomaly (interaction measure) from the difference between the gluon action density and quark condensates at zero and nonzero temperature, then obtained the pressure, energy density, entropy density, and speed of sound through thermodynamic integration. The lattice spacing was set using the static quark potential parameter r1 = 0.3106 fm, with cross-checks from the gradient flow scale w0 and hadronic observables.

## Key numbers
- Energy density in the crossover region (145 MeV ≤ T ≤ 163 MeV): εc = (0.18-0.5) GeV/fm^3, corresponding to (1.2-3.1) ε_nuclear
- Temperature range of the study: 130-400 MeV
- Continuum extrapolated ratio w0/r1 = 0.5619(21), giving w0 = 0.1749(14) fm
- Continuum ratio r0/r1 = 1.5092(39) from a constant fit to data for β ≥ 6.423
- Continuum extrapolated kaon decay constant: (r1 fK)^cont = 0.17186(24)
- Continuum extrapolated φ meson mass: (r1 mφ) = 1.5961(30)
- Lattice spacings used: as fine as a = 0.041 fm (β = 7.825)

## Context
This work extends the HotQCD collaboration's previous equation of state calculations (Bazavov 2011) to finer lattices (Nτ = 12) and provides a controlled continuum extrapolation, improving upon earlier staggered fermion results. The results are compared with resummed and dimensionally reduced perturbation theory at high temperatures and with hadron resonance gas models at low temperatures. The analytic parametrization of the pressure enables use in hydrodynamic modeling of heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and the scale parameters r0 and w0 provide inputs for other lattice QCD studies.
