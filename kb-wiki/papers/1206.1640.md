# Pairing correlations with single Cooper pair transfer to individual quantal states

> With the help of the static and dynamic mean field spectroscopic amplitudes, taking into account successive and simultaneous transfer channels properly corrected because of non-orthogonality effects, as well as describing the associated elastic channels in terms of experimentally determined optical potentials, one obtains absolute, two-particle transfer differential cross sections which provide an overall account of the data within experimental errors. One of the first results connected with such quantitative studies of pairing correlations in nuclei is the observation of phonon mediated pairing in the exotic halo nucleus $^{11}$Li, and the associated discovery of a new mechanism to break nuclear gauge symmetry: bootstrap, pigmy-resonance-mediated Cooper pair binding.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that absolute two-particle transfer differential cross sections, calculated using static and dynamic mean-field spectroscopic amplitudes with proper treatment of successive and simultaneous transfer channels and non-orthogonality corrections, can reproduce experimental data within errors. The abstract's claim of "overall account of the data within experimental errors" glosses over the condition that this agreement depends on using experimentally determined optical potentials for the elastic channels and on the cancellation between simultaneous and non-orthogonality terms being nearly complete in the independent-particle limit.

## Method
The authors computed two-nucleon transfer cross sections using a semiclassical approximation with three amplitude components: simultaneous transfer (first-order), successive transfer via one-nucleon intermediate channels (second-order), and a non-orthogonality correction term (second-order). The nuclear structure input consisted of BCS and QRPA spectroscopic amplitudes for pairing rotations and pairing vibrations, while the elastic channels were described by experimentally determined optical potentials. The calculations were applied to (p,t) reactions on Sn isotopes and to the halo nucleus ¹¹Li.

## Key numbers
- For ¹²⁰Sn(p,t)¹¹⁸Sn(gs), the BCS gap was fixed to Δ = 1.41 MeV (experimental value), leading to G = 0.51 MeV with E_cutoff = 0 MeV and G = 0.22 MeV with E_cutoff = 60 MeV.
- The ratio of ground-state to pairing-vibrational cross sections for Sn isotopes (A ≈ 120) was predicted as R ≈ 24 from the single-j shell model, and R ≈ 25 from the relation (Δ/G)² with Δ ≈ 1 MeV and G ≈ 0.2 MeV.
- For the two cutoff scenarios (E_cutoff = 0 MeV and 60 MeV), the valence-orbital contribution to α'_0 was 2.12 and 2.08 respectively, giving a squared relative difference of about 2 × 10⁻³ between predicted cross sections.
- The pairing coupling constant scaling is G ≈ 25 MeV / A, and the ratio G/|U(r=R₀)| ≈ 0.2 MeV / 25 MeV ≈ 10⁻².

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of two-nucleon transfer reactions as a specific probe of pairing correlations, building on the BCS theory of Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer (1957) and the pairing vibration concept of Bohr (1964) and Bes (1966). It resolves the Bardeen-Josephson controversy (1962) by showing that successive one-nucleon transfer, not simultaneous transfer, is the dominant mechanism for Cooper pair tunneling, analogous to the Josephson effect in superconductors. The results enable quantitative testing of pairing correlations in exotic nuclei, particularly the discovery of phonon-mediated pairing and bootstrap Cooper pair binding in the halo nucleus ¹¹Li.
