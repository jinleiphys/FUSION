# Pion Nucleon Scattering: Some Results from Lattice QCD

> Including the meson-baryon (5 quark) intermediate states in a lattice simulation is challenging. However, it is important in order to obtain the correct energy eigenstates and to relate them to scattering phase shifts. Recent results for the negative parity nucleon channel and the problem of baryonic resonances in lattice calculations are discussed.

# Pion Nucleon Scattering: Some Results from Lattice QCD

## Key claim
Including meson-baryon (5-quark) intermediate states in lattice QCD simulations of the negative parity nucleon channel significantly changes the energy spectrum compared to calculations using only three-quark operators, demonstrating that earlier lattice studies of baryonic resonances may have missed important coupled-channel effects. The results are obtained at a pion mass of 266 MeV, which shifts resonance masses approximately 150 MeV above their physical values.

## Method
The authors performed a lattice QCD calculation on a 16^3 × 32 lattice with two mass-degenerate dynamical quarks at a lattice spacing of 0.1239 fm and pion mass 266 MeV, using gauge configurations from Hasenfratz. They constructed a 7 × 7 cross-correlation matrix including three-quark nucleon interpolators and a five-quark Nπ interpolator (with both particles at rest, projected to isospin 1/2), applied the variational method to extract energy eigenvalues, and used the distillation method with N_v = 32 and 64 eigenvectors to compute the 29 Wick contraction graphs required for the full coupled system.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: 0.1239 fm on a 16^3 × 32 lattice
- Pion mass: 266 MeV
- Nucleon mass lies roughly 130 MeV above the physical nucleon mass
- Resonance masses lie approximately 150 MeV above their physical values due to the unphysical pion mass
- The lowest N(0)π(0) state lies closely below the Nπ threshold (which is at m_N + m_π)
- The N(1535) and N(1650) resonances (shifted upward) lie above 1800 MeV in this simulation

## Context
This work extends the approach used in meson spectroscopy, where including meson-meson interpolators revealed missing energy levels and shifted resonance positions (as seen in ρ → ππ studies), to the baryon sector. It directly addresses the absence of meson-baryon decay states in earlier baryon excitation studies by Edwards (2011), Mahbub (2012), Engel (2013), and Alexandrou (2013), which used only three-quark operators. The results enable future coupled-channel analyses of baryon resonances using Lüscher's finite-volume method, though the authors note that more operators and treatment of coupled channels (including Nη, Nππ, and ΛK thresholds) will be needed for a complete description.
