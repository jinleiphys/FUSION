# Joint Inference of Population, Cosmology, and Neutron Star Equation of State from Gravitational Waves of Dark Binary Neutron Stars

> Gravitational waves (GWs) from binary neutron stars (BNSs) are expected to be accompanied by electromagnetic (EM) emissions, which help identify the host galaxy. Since GWs directly measure their luminosity distances, joint GW-EM observations from BNSs help with the study of cosmology, particularly the Hubble constant, unaffected by cosmic distance ladder systematics. However, detecting the EM emissions is not always possible. Additionally, the tidal deformability of neutron stars (NSs), combined with the knowledge of the NS EoS, can break the degeneracy between mass parameters and redshift, allowing for the inference of the Hubble constant. While several studies have aimed to infer the Hubble constant using dark BNSs (without EM counterparts), none have consistently combined the uncertainties of population, cosmology, and NS EoS within a Bayesian framework. In this study, we propose a novel Bayesian analysis to jointly constrain the NS EoS, population, and cosmological parameters using a population of dark BNSs detected through GW observations. We demonstrate the statistical robustness of our method using $50$ simulated BNS events following Gaussian and double Gaussian mass distributions, detected by Advanced LIGO and Advanced Virgo detectors operating at O5 sensitivity. We show that such measurements can constrain the Hubble constant with a precision of $\lesssim 35\%$ ($90\%$ credible interval). This level of precision is unattainable without incorporating NS EoS, especially when observing BNS mergers without EM counterpart information. We also report the Hubble constant measurements obtained from a more realistic set of $5$ simulated BNS events.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that jointly inferring the neutron star equation of state (EoS), binary neutron star (BNS) mass and redshift distributions, and the Hubble constant from a population of "dark" BNSs (those without electromagnetic counterparts) within a unified Bayesian framework can constrain the Hubble constant to a precision of ≲35% (90% credible interval) using 50 simulated events at Advanced LIGO and Advanced Virgo O5 sensitivity, a precision unattainable without incorporating EoS information.

## Method

The authors perform a hierarchical Bayesian analysis that jointly infers hyperparameters for the NS EoS (using a hybrid nuclear+piecewise-polytrope parameterization), the BNS mass distribution (Gaussian or double Gaussian), the redshift evolution of the merger rate (power-law index γ), and cosmology (H0, Ωm) from a population of simulated dark BNS events. Individual event posteriors on source-frame masses, redshift, and tidal deformabilities are obtained from simulated GW signals using a cosmology-independent prior, then combined via a population likelihood that includes a selection function to correct for detector bias. The method is tested on catalogs of 5 and 50 simulated BNS events detected with signal-to-noise ratio ≥20, using both Gaussian and double Gaussian mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- Injected Hubble constant value: H0 = 70 km s⁻¹ Mpc⁻¹

- Injected double Gaussian mass distribution parameters: μ₁ = 1.34 M⊙, σ₁ = 0.07 M⊙, μ₂ = 1.8 M⊙, σ₂ = 0.21 M⊙, w = 0.65
- Injected merger-rate index: γ = 0

## Context

This work extends previous efforts by Chatterjee et al. (2021) and Dhani et al. (2022), which assumed a known EoS, and by Ghosh et al. (2022) and Shiralilou et al. (2022), which allowed EoS uncertainties but assumed a uniform mass distribution. Unlike those studies, this paper simultaneously infers the mass distribution, redshift distribution, NS EoS, and Hubble constant, thereby mitigating potential bias in mass distribution parameters as noted by Wysocki et al. (2020) and Golomb et al. (2021). The method enables cosmological inference from dark BNSs without relying on electromagnetic counterparts, which are unlikely to be detectable beyond redshift z ∼ 1 for kilonovae or z ∼ 2 for gamma-ray bursts.
