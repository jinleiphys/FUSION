# A six-quark dressed-bag description of np -> d γradiative capture

> The radiative capture process $n p \to d γ$ is considered within the framework of a recently developed six-quark dressed-bag model for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The calculations presented here include both the nucleon current and the meson-exchange current contributions. The latter uses short-range hadronic form factors for the pion exchange currents consistent with the soft cut-off parameter $Λ_{πNN}$ from the $NN$-potential. Contributions of the pion exchange current and $Δ$-isobar current to the total cross section still cannot explain the discrepancy between the theoretical and experimental cross sections. Possibilities for new types of meson exchange currents associated with chiral fields inside multi-quark dressed-bag states in nuclei are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that even after including nucleon current, pion exchange current, and Delta-isobar current contributions within a six-quark dressed-bag model for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, the theoretical cross section for thermal neutron radiative capture still cannot explain the discrepancy with the experimental value, suggesting that new types of meson exchange currents associated with chiral fields inside multi-quark dressed-bag states may be needed.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio calculations of the np to d gamma cross section using the Moscow-Tubingen dressed-bag-state (DBS) model for the NN interaction, which includes both a conventional NN cluster component and a six-quark plus sigma-meson component in the wave function. They solved the Schrodinger equation with an effective Hamiltonian containing one-pion exchange, two-pion exchange, and a short-range interaction from intermediate dressed-bag production, using soft cut-off parameters (Lambda_piNN approximately 0.5 GeV) consistent with chiral filter ideas. The electromagnetic current included the impulse approximation, meson-exchange currents from pion exchange with soft form factors, and Delta-isobar currents, all computed with wave functions from the same potential model to maintain gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- Experimental cross section for thermal neutron capture at neutron velocity 2200 m/s: sigma_np = 334.2 +/- 0.5 mb
- Soft cut-off parameter for piNN form factor used in the model: Lambda_piNN approximately 0.5 GeV (compared to 1.5-1.7 GeV in conventional OBE models)
- Weight of the DBS component in the deuteron wave function: 3.66 percent
- Deuteron binding energy from the DBS model: 2.22454 MeV (experimental: 2.2245475(9) MeV)
- Deuteron D-state probability in the DBS model (NN component only): 5.42 percent; (NN + 6q): 5.22 percent
- Deuteron quadrupole moment from the DBS model (NN + 6q): 0.275 fm^2 (experimental: 0.2859(3) fm^2)
- Deuteron magnetic moment from the DBS model (NN + 6q): 0.8548 mu_N (experimental: 0.857406(1) mu_N)
- Deuteron asymptotic S-wave normalization from the DBS model (NN + 6q): A_S = 0.8864 fm^{-1/2} (experimental: 0.8846(44) fm^{-1/2})
- Deuteron D/S ratio from the DBS model (NN + 6q): 0.0264 (experimental: 0.0264)
- Bag radius: b = 0.5 fm
- Sigma meson mass used: mu_sigma approximately 400 MeV

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to explain the 10 percent discrepancy in the np to d gamma cross section first pointed out by Austern (1953). It directly challenges the conventional one-boson exchange models (such as the Bonn potential) that use hard cut-off parameters around 1.5-1.7 GeV, and instead employs a dressed-bag model with soft cut-offs consistent with chiral perturbation theory predictions from Rho (1991) and Kubodera (1978). The paper enables future investigation of new meson exchange currents arising from the six-quark dressed-bag component, which the authors suggest may resolve the remaining discrepancy that persists even after including standard pion exchange and Delta-isobar currents.
