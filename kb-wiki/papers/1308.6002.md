# From nuclear structure to neutron stars

> Recent progress in quantum Monte Carlo with modern nucleon-nucleon interactions have enabled the successful description of properties of light nuclei and neutron-rich matter. As a demonstration, we show that the agreement between theoretical calculations of the charge form factor of 12C and the experimental data is excellent. Applying similar methods to isospin-asymmetric systems allows one to describe neutrons confined in an external potential and homogeneous neutron-rich matter. Of particular interest is the nuclear symmetry energy, the energy cost of creating an isospin asymmetry. Combining these advances with recent observations of neutron star masses and radii gives insight into the equation of state of neutron-rich matter near and above the saturation density. In particular, neutron star radius measurements constrain the derivative of the symmetry energy.

# From nuclear structure to neutron stars

## Key claim

Quantum Monte Carlo calculations using realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (Argonne AV8' plus three-body forces) can simultaneously describe the charge form factor of 12C and the equation of state of neutron-rich matter, but the uncertainty in the three-neutron force model produces a spread of about 20 MeV in the neutron matter energy at densities near 3 times saturation density, and the resulting neutron star radius constraints depend strongly on the assumed symmetry energy and the interpretation of observational data.

## Method

The authors solve the many-body nuclear ground state using Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) for light nuclei and Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) for neutron-rich systems, employing the Argonne AV8' two-body potential combined with various three-body forces (Urbana IX, Illinois, and custom three-neutron models). They compute the charge form factor of 12C, the energies of neutron drops confined in harmonic oscillator potentials, and the equation of state of pure neutron matter, then use the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to generate neutron star mass-radius relations and perform a Bayesian analysis incorporating observations of photospheric radius expansion X-ray bursts and quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries.

## Key numbers

- The charge form factor of 12C calculated with AV18+IL7 shows excellent agreement with experimental data up to momentum transfer q ≈ 3 fm⁻¹, where two-body operators become appreciable.
- Two-body operators contribute up to 50% in the transverse electromagnetic sum-rule of 12C.
- GFMC and AFDMC energies for neutron drops in a harmonic oscillator well with ħω = 10 MeV agree within 1%; the difference increases to 4% for ħω = 5 MeV.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density is constrained to 32 < E_sym < 34 MeV at 68% confidence from neutron star observations.
- The slope parameter L of the symmetry energy is constrained to 43 < L < 52 MeV at 68% confidence from neutron star data; model C (line-segment EoS) allows L as large as 83 MeV.
- The radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star lies between 10.4 and 12.9 km across all EoS models and data interpretations considered.
- The Argonne AV8' Hamiltonian alone cannot support a 1.97(4) solar mass neutron star; adding a three-body force provides sufficient repulsion.
- The maximum neutron star radius implied by modern neutron matter EoS is not larger than about 13 km.

## Context

This work extends the program of quantum Monte Carlo nuclear structure calculations by Wiringa (1995), Pieper (2001), and Pudliner (1997) from light nuclei to neutron-rich matter and neutron stars. The symmetry energy constraints from neutron star observations are compared with experimental determinations from neutron skins by Warda (2009), nuclear masses by Liu (2010), heavy-ion collisions by Tsang (2009), and electric dipole polarizability by Tamii (2011). The results enable improved calibration of Skyrme energy density functionals (such as SLy4 by Chabanat, 1995) for applications to neutron-rich nuclei and neutron star crust structure, and provide a bridge between ab-initio nuclear theory and astrophysical observations of neutron star masses by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013).
