# Electromagnetic structure of Bc and heavy quarkonia in the light-front quark model

> We investigate the electromagnetic structure of heavy quarkonia and the $B_c$ meson within the light-front quark model (LFQM) to better understand the internal spatial charge distributions and QCD dynamics of heavy mesons. The light-front wave functions (LFWFs) are obtained using a variational approach with a few set of harmonic oscillator basis functions, providing a flexible yet tractable description of the bound-state dynamics. Using these LFWFs, we compute the electromagnetic form factors and compare our results with available lattice QCD data and other model calculations. Our results are roughly consistent with previous model predictions, showing that the electromagnetic radii of the $2S$ and $3S$ states are approximately 1.5 times and 1.9 times larger than those of their corresponding $1S$ states, reflecting the expected growth of spatial size in radial excitations.

## Key claim

The light-front quark model (LFQM) using a variational harmonic oscillator basis expansion and the Bakamjian-Thomas construction yields electromagnetic radii for heavy quarkonia and the Bc meson that are "roughly consistent" with previous model predictions, with the essential difference being the use of a variational basis expansion and the Bakamjian-Thomas construction to maintain rotational symmetry.

## Method

The authors constructed light-front wave functions (LFWFs) for heavy quarkonia (c-cbar, b-bbar) and Bc mesons up to the 3S radial excitation using a variational approach with harmonic oscillator basis functions. They computed electromagnetic form factors in the Drell-Yan-West (q+=0) frame using the plus component of the electromagnetic current, applying the Bakamjian-Thomas construction to replace physical meson masses with non-interacting invariant masses in the Lorentz factor to maintain rotational symmetry and avoid zero-mode issues. The charge radii were extracted from the slope of the form factor at Q2=0.

## Key numbers

* rms charge radius of eta_c(1S): 0.249(40) fm
* rms charge radius of eta_c(2S): 0.369(59) fm
* rms charge radius of eta_c(3S): 0.466(75) fm
* The electromagnetic radius of 2S states is approximately 1.5 times larger than their corresponding 1S states
* The electromagnetic radius of 3S states is approximately 1.9 times larger than their corresponding 1S states
* The eta_c(2S) radius grows roughly 1.48 times more than eta_c(1S), while the increase from eta_c(2S) to eta_c(3S) is around 1.26 times
* The total radius increase from eta_c(1S) to eta_c(3S) accumulates to about 1.87 times
* Relative errors: approximately 16% for charmonia, 20% for Bc mesons, and 13% for bottomonia
* Model parameters: m_b = 4.97(4) GeV, m_c = 1.61(4) GeV, beta_{c-cbar} = 0.542(8) GeV, beta_{b-cbar} = 0.702(9) GeV, beta_{b-bbar} = 1.060(11) GeV, theta_12 = 12.12(30) degrees, theta_13 = theta_23 = 8.44(14) degrees

## Context

This work extends the LFQM approach of Arifi (2022) and Ridwan (2024) by computing electromagnetic form factors and charge radii for heavy quarkonia and Bc mesons up to the 3S radial excitation. The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations by Li (2020), Dudek (2006), and Delaney (2023), as well as other model calculations including Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) by Li (2015), the Bethe-Salpeter equation approach by Hernandez-Pinto (2023), Adhikari (2018), and Serafin (2020). The study enables further investigation of the spatial charge distributions of heavy mesons and provides a benchmark for future experimental measurements, which are currently unavailable due to the short lifetimes and production challenges of heavy quarkonia and Bc mesons.
