# n+p -> d+gamma for Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis

> The cross section for n+p -> d+gamma is calculated at energies relevant to big bang nucleosynthesis using the recently developed effective field theory that describes the two-nucleon sector. The E1 amplitude is computed up to NNNLO and depends only upon nucleon-nucleon phase shift data. In contrast, the M1 contribution is computed up to NLO, and the four-nucleon-one-magnetic-photon counterterm that enters is determined by the cross section for cold neutron capture. The uncertainty in the calculation for nucleon energies up to E ~ 1 MeV is estimated to be < 4%.

## Key claim

The cross section for n+p -> d+gamma at big-bang nucleosynthesis energies is calculated using pionless effective field theory (EFT) with an estimated uncertainty of less than 4% for nucleon energies up to about 1 MeV, but this uncertainty estimate depends on adding errors linearly and is largest (about 4%) near 200 keV, dropping below 1% for energies below 8 keV.

## Method

The E1 amplitude was computed up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N^3LO) using only nucleon-nucleon phase shift data, while the M1 amplitude was computed up to next-to-leading order (NLO) with a four-nucleon-one-magnetic-photon counterterm (L_one) fixed by the measured cold neutron capture cross section of 334.2 mb. The strong interaction parameters were determined from the effective range expansion of the triplet S-wave channel (binding momentum gamma_t, effective range rho_d, shape parameter w_2) and from P-wave phase shifts from the Nijmegen analysis. The total cross section was obtained by summing the E1 and M1 contributions, with the E1 amplitude dominating above approximately 200 keV.

## Key numbers

- Cold neutron capture cross section: sigma_expt = 334.2 ± 0.5 mb at incident neutron speed |v| = 2200 m/s, used to fix the counterterm Lnp = -4.513 fm^2
- Triplet S-wave effective range parameters: gamma_t^{-1} = 4.318946 fm (deuteron binding momentum), rho_d = 1.764 ± 0.002 fm (effective range), w_2 = 0.389 fm^3 (shape parameter)
- Singlet S-wave scattering length: a_1 = -23.714 ± 0.013 fm; effective range: r_0 = 2.73 ± 0.03 fm
- At E = 1.264 × 10^{-8} MeV (cold neutrons): total cross section = 334.2 mb (input)
- At E = 1.0 × 10^{-3} MeV (1 keV): total cross section = 1.173(4) mb (M1: 1.171 mb, E1: 1.42 × 10^{-3} mb)
- At E = 0.100 MeV: total cross section = 0.064(2) mb (M1: 0.0501 mb, E1: 0.0136 mb)
- At E = 1.00 MeV: total cross section = 0.035(1) mb (M1: 0.00375 mb, E1: 0.0310 mb)
- Deuteron binding energy: B = 2.224575 MeV
- P-wave coefficients from Nijmegen analysis: C_2^{(P0)} = +6.53 fm^4, C_2^{(P1)} = -5.91 fm^4, C_2^{(P2)} = +0.57 fm^4

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of n+p -> d+gamma from cold neutrons (Savage, Scaldeferri, Wise 1999; Chen, Rupak, Savage 1999) to the finite-energy regime relevant for big-bang nucleosynthesis, where both E1 and M1 contributions are important. The calculation improves upon effective range theory (Bethe 1949; Noyes 1965) by including a four-nucleon-one-magnetic-photon counterterm that captures short-distance physics beyond nucleon-nucleon scattering, analogous to meson-exchange current contributions (Riska and Brown 1972). The results enable more precise predictions for primordial element abundances, as the 5% uncertainty assigned by Smith, Kawano and Malaney (1993) and used by Burles, Nollet, Truran and Turner (1999) contributes significantly to BBN abundance uncertainties.
