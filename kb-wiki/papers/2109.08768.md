# Pauli radius of the proton

> Using a procedure based on interpolation via continued fractions supplemented by statistical sampling, we analyse proton magnetic form factor data obtained via electron+proton scattering on $Q^2 \in [0.027,0.55]\,$GeV$^2$ with the goal of determining the proton magnetic radius. The approach avoids assumptions about the function form used for data interpolation and ensuing extrapolation onto $Q^2\simeq 0$ for extraction of the form factor slope. In this way, we find $r_M = 0.817(27)\,$fm. Regarding the difference between proton electric and magnetic radii calculated in this way, extant data are seen to be compatible with the possibility that the slopes of the proton Dirac and Pauli form factors, $F_{1,2}(Q^2)$, are not truly independent observables; to wit, the difference $F_1^\prime(0)-F_2^\prime(0)/κ_p = [1+κ_p]/[4 m_p^2]$, viz. the proton Foldy term.

## Key claim

The authors use a form-unbiased interpolation method (the statistical Schlessinger Point Method, SPM) to extract the proton magnetic radius from A1 Collaboration data, finding that the difference between the proton's Dirac and Pauli form factor slopes may not be an independent observable but instead is fixed by the proton Foldy term, though this is established only at a 26% confidence level.

## Method

The authors apply the statistical Schlessinger Point Method (SPM) to 68 proton magnetic form factor data points from the A1 Collaboration on the domain \(Q^2 \in [0.027, 0.55]\,\text{GeV}^2\). They construct continued fraction interpolations from randomly selected subsets of the data, generate 5000 smooth interpolating functions per subset, and extrapolate each to \(Q^2=0\) to extract the magnetic radius via the slope at the origin. Uncertainties are estimated through a statistical bootstrap procedure that generates 1000 replicas of the data, combined with variation of the number of input points.

## Key numbers

- Proton magnetic radius from A1 data using SPM: \(r_M = 0.817 \pm 0.027_{\text{stat}}\,\text{fm}\)
- Proton electric radius from A1 data using SPM (from prior work): \(r_E^{\text{A1-SPM}} = 0.856 \pm 0.014_{\text{stat}}\,\text{fm}\)
- PDG average of magnetic radius: \(r_M = 0.851 \pm 0.026\,\text{fm}\)
- Proton electric radius (settled value): \(r_E = 0.8409(4)\,\text{fm}\)
- Deviation parameter \(\delta_P\) from Eq. (Foldy) using A1 SPM results: \(\delta_P^{\text{A1}_{\text{SPM}}} = 0.546 \pm 0.366\)
- Uncertainty-weighted average of \(\delta_P\) from three combinations: \(\delta_P = 0.218 \pm 0.193\)

## Context

This work extends the SPM-based analysis previously applied to the proton electric radius by Cui et al. (2021) to the magnetic radius, using the same A1 Collaboration data (Bernauer et al., 2010; A1 Collaboration, 2013). The results are compared with dispersion theory extractions by Alarcon et al. (2020) and Lin et al. (2021), lattice QCD results by Djukanovic et al. (2021), and the PDG average. The analysis enables tests of whether the proton's Dirac and Pauli radii are independent observables, with implications for understanding emergent hadron mass (EHM) in QCD.
