# Spin polarization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Polarization has opened a new physics chapter in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Since the first prediction and experimental observation of global spin polarization, a lot of progress has been made in understanding its features, both at experimental and theoretical level. In this paper, we give an overview on the recent advances in this field. The covered topics include a review of measurements of global and local spin polarization of hyperons and the global spin alignment of vector mesons. We account for the basic theoretical framework to describe spin polarization in a relativistic fluid such as the Quark Gluon Plasma, including statistical quantum field theory and local thermodynamic equilibrium, spin hydrodynamics, relativistic kinetic theory with spin and coalescence models.

## Key claim

This review paper surveys the state of spin polarization research in relativistic heavy-ion collisions up to 2023, noting that while global spin polarization measurements of Lambda hyperons agree with hydrodynamic predictions based on local thermodynamic equilibrium, local spin polarization as a function of azimuthal angle shows stark discrepancies with those same models, and the spin alignment of vector mesons remains unexplained by equilibrium theory.

## Method

The authors review experimental measurements of global and local spin polarization of Lambda and Xi hyperons and global spin alignment of phi and K*0 vector mesons across multiple collision energies at RHIC and LHC. They present the theoretical framework including quantum statistical field theory with the mean spin vector derived from the Pauli-Lubanski operator, Wigner function methods for connecting quantum field theory to measurable spin observables, spin hydrodynamics, relativistic kinetic theory with spin, and coalescence models for vector meson spin alignment.

## Key numbers

- Global polarization of Lambda hyperons at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV in Au+Au collisions is about 1%, in agreement with hydrodynamic predictions
- The spin alignment rho_00 - 1/3 for phi mesons at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV is approximately 10^{-2}, growing at lower collision energies
- The hydrodynamic-local equilibrium model predicts spin alignment of vector mesons roughly of order 10^{-4}, which is consistently smaller than the measured value
- From observed global polarization of Lambda hyperons, one can estimate rho_00^phi - 1/3 ~ P_s P_anti-s ~ 10^{-4}, incompatible with the measured phi meson alignment

## Context

This review extends the work following the first prediction of global spin polarization by Liang and Wang (2004) and the first experimental observation by STAR (2017), which confirmed the link between spin and rotation predicted by the Barnett and Einstein-De Haas effects. The discrepancy between measured local spin polarization and hydrodynamic predictions (Becattini and collaborators, 2020) motivated development of kinetic theory with spin and spin hydrodynamics by multiple groups. The unexplained large spin alignment of vector mesons has led to proposals involving vector field fluctuations in strong interactions (Sheng and collaborators, 2019-2023). The paper enables future work using spin polarization as a probe of local parity violation, energy loss of partons in QGP, and signatures of the critical point.
