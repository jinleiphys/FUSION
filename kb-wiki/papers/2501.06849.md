# Systematic uncertainties from higher-twist corrections in DIS at large x

> We investigate the systematic uncertainties and potential biases arising from the inclusion of large-$x$ corrections to proton and deuteron deep inelastic scattering (DIS) data in global quantum chromodynamics (QCD) analyses. Using the CTEQ-JLab framework, we examine various approaches to implementing higher-twist corrections in nucleon structure functions and off-shell PDF modifications in deuteron targets. We analyze how these components interact and influence the determination of the $d$-quark PDF and the neutron structure function at large $x$. We find that it is very important to consider isospin-dependent higher-twist corrections in order to minimize implementation biases in the extracted quantities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that isospin-independent implementations of higher-twist (HT) corrections in global QCD analyses introduce a systematic bias in the extracted large-x d-quark PDF and neutron structure function, which can be partially compensated by artificially deforming the fitted off-shell nucleon corrections in the deuteron. This bias is removed by using isospin-dependent HT functions.

## Method

The authors performed global QCD fits within the CTEQ-JLab (CJ22) framework at next-to-leading order, using the same dataset and kinematic cuts as the CJ22 analysis. They compared multiplicative and additive implementations of higher-twist corrections to the target-mass-corrected leading-twist structure functions, fitting polynomial parametrizations of the off-shell nucleon deformation function δf(x) with three free parameters. The fits were performed both with isospin-independent HT functions (C_p = C_n or H_p = H_n) and with separate isospin-dependent HT functions for protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

- The n/p ratio of F2 structure functions at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2 and x = 0.8 differs by approximately 25% between the additive and multiplicative isospin-independent HT implementations
- The off-shell function δf(x) is fitted using a second-degree polynomial (k=2, i.e., 3 free parameters), with no significant χ^2 improvement from higher-degree polynomials
- The initial PDF parametrization scale is Q_0^2 = 1.69 GeV^2, using a five-parameter functional form for most parton species
- The tolerance for fit uncertainty is T^2 = 2.7

## Context

This work extends the CJ22 global QCD analysis (Accardi 2023) by systematically studying the interplay between higher-twist corrections and off-shell nucleon deformations in deuteron DIS data. It builds on earlier studies of power corrections by Alekhin (2003, 2008), Blumlein (2006, 2008, 2012), and the JAM collaboration (Cocuzza 2021), and on off-shell nucleon studies by Kulagin (1994, 2004, 2007) and the AKP group (Alekhin 2022). The results enable future analyses to properly account for HT implementation biases when extracting the d/u ratio and neutron structure function from combined proton and deuteron DIS data, particularly with upcoming higher-Q^2 data from the JLab 22 GeV upgrade.
