# Chiral Thermodynamics of Dense QCD

> We show a phase diagram of a two-flavored parity doublet model on top of the nuclear matter ground state within the mean field approximation. An exotic phase with unbroken center symmetry of chiral group is also discussed. When crossing this phase boundary the baryon number susceptibility exhibits a characteristic feature.

## Key claim
The parity doublet model, when placed on top of the nuclear matter ground state within mean field approximation, produces a phase diagram where the existence and order of chiral symmetry restoration depend critically on the mass of the negative-parity nucleon; with a lower mass (1.2 GeV) there is no true chiral phase transition but only a crossover, while with the standard mass (1.5 GeV) a weak first-order chiral transition appears at very high density.

## Method
A two-flavor parity doublet model was solved in the mean field approximation, incorporating the nuclear matter saturation point at baryon density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³. The phase diagram was mapped for two different masses of the negative-parity nucleon (1.5 GeV and 1.2 GeV) by solving the gap equations for the chiral order parameter and baryon density as functions of temperature and baryon chemical potential. Additionally, a Ginzburg-Landau free energy approach was used to study a possible exotic phase with unbroken center symmetry of the chiral group, driven by four-quark condensates.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Liquid-gas transition at zero temperature occurs at μ_B = 923 MeV
- Liquid-gas transition survives up to T = 27 MeV
- With m_{N-} = 1500 MeV: weak first-order chiral transition at ρ ~ 10 ρ₀
- With m_{N-} = 1200 MeV: chiral crossover at ρ ~ 3 ρ₀
- Intersection of chiral crossover and meson-baryon transition lines at (T, μ_B) ~ (150, 450) MeV

## Context
This work extends parity doublet model studies of hot and dense hadronic matter (Sasaki, previous work) by explicitly placing the model on the nuclear matter ground state, following the approach of Walecka-type models. The predicted three-domain structure (chirally broken meson-dominated, chirally broken baryon-dominated, and chirally restored) is identified with quarkyonic matter as proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (2007). The possibility of an intermediate phase with unbroken center symmetry, based on a breaking pattern suggested by Shifman (2001), introduces a new critical point at low temperatures and connects to studies of the O(2) model (Watanabe, 2009) and Skyrme crystals.
