# Dilatons in Hidden Local Symmetry for Hadrons in Dense Matter

> With the explicit breaking of scale invariance by the trace anomaly of QCD rephrased in terms of spontaneous breaking, low-energy strong interaction dynamics of dense (and also hot) matter can be effectively captured by -- in addition to the Nambu-Goldstone bosons and the vector mesons -- two dilaton fields, the "soft" ($χ_s$) field that is locked to chiral symmetry and the "hard" ($χ_h$) field which remains unaffected by chiral symmetry. The interplay of the soft and hard dilatons plays a subtle role in how chiral symmetry is manifested in hot and/or dense matter. The scale anomaly in which the soft component intervenes vanishes at the chiral transition in a way analogous to the restoration of scale symmetry in the Freund-Nambu model, while that of the hard component remains broken throughout the QCD sector. Most remarkable of all is its role in the chiral anomaly sector through a "homogeneous Wess-Zumino (hWZ) term" of the form $ω_μB^μ$ on the structure of a single baryon as well as dense baryonic matter. It figures crucially in predicting a "Little Bag" for the nucleon and a "quarkyonic phase" in the form of a half-skyrmion matter at high density. We show how the vanishing of the vector-meson mass at the vector manifestation fixed point in hidden local symmetry theory can be related to the property of the "matter field" in the Freund-Nambu model that leaves scale symmetry invariant. The emerging structure of dense hadronic matter in the model so constructed suggests what could be amiss in describing dense matter in holographic dual QCD at its large $N_c$ and 't Hooft limit.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the trace anomaly of QCD can be rephrased in terms of spontaneous breaking of scale symmetry using two dilaton fields, a "soft" component locked to chiral symmetry and a "hard" component unaffected by it, and that the soft dilaton's condensate vanishes at the chiral transition in a manner analogous to the Freund-Nambu model, while the hard dilaton remains massive across the transition.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian incorporating hidden local symmetry for vector mesons, two dilaton fields (soft χ_s and hard χ_h), pions, and a homogeneous Wess-Zumino term of the form ω_μ B^μ. They use lattice QCD results on the gluon condensate as a function of temperature to motivate splitting the condensate into soft and hard components, then integrate out the hard dilaton fluctuations to obtain a Lagrangian depending only on the soft dilaton. The resulting model is applied to describe the scaling of hadron properties in dense matter, including the vector meson mass and the pion decay constant.

## Key numbers

- The lattice result suggests that at zero temperature, the soft and hard components each contribute approximately half of the total gluon condensate: ⟨G²_s⟩_0 ≈ ⟨G²_h⟩_0 ≈ ½ ⟨G²⟩_0.
- At the chiral transition temperature T_c ≈ 200 MeV, the gluon condensate retains about half its zero-temperature value: ⟨G²⟩_{T_c} ≈ ⟨G²_h⟩_{T_c} ≈ ½ ⟨G²⟩_0.
- The "flash temperature" where the gluon condensate starts melting steeply is about T_f ∼ 120 MeV.
- In the Freund-Nambu model, the dilaton mass m_χ = τ φ_0 goes to zero linearly as τ → 0, while the matter field mass m_ψ = f φ_0 remains nonzero for fixed f.

## Context

This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling idea (Brown and Rho, 1991) by providing a dilaton-based mechanism that connects the scaling of hadron masses in medium to the behavior of the gluon condensate. It also relates the vector manifestation fixed point in hidden local symmetry theory (Harada and Yamawaki, 2003) to the Freund-Nambu model's property that the matter field becomes massless when the dilaton condensate vanishes. The paper identifies a potential shortcoming in holographic dual QCD approaches (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005) applied to dense matter, where certain meson properties behave contrary to QCD expectations as density increases. The results enable further study of the "Little Bag" model for the nucleon and the half-skyrmion phase of dense matter.
