# Spin squeezing and entanglement in spinor-1 condensates

> We analyze quantum correlation properties of a spinor-1 (f=1) Bose Einstein condensate using the Gell-Mann realization of SU(3) symmetry. We show that previously discussed phenomena of condensate fragmentation and spin-mixing can be explained in terms of the hypercharge symmetry. The ground state of a spinor-1 condensate is found to be fragmented for ferromagnetic interactions. The notion of two bosonic mode squeezing is generalized to the two spin (U-V) squeezing within the SU(3) formalism. Spin squeezing in the isospin subspace (T) is found and numerically investigated. We also provide new results for the stationary states of spinor-1 condensates.

## Key claim

The paper claims that spin squeezing and entanglement in a spinor-1 Bose-Einstein condensate can be understood and generalized beyond previous restrictive limits (where one mode is highly populated) by decomposing the Hamiltonian into commuting Kitagawa-Ueda and Barnett-Dupertuis type nonlinearities, which simultaneously squeeze all three SU(2) subspaces of the SU(3) structure.

## Method

The authors reformulate the spinor-1 condensate Hamiltonian under the single-mode approximation using the Gell-Mann realization of SU(3) symmetry, identifying three pseudo-spin subspaces (T, U, V) and the hypercharge operator Y. They decompose the Hamiltonian into commuting parts involving T₃² and a U-V two-spin interaction term, then derive a generalized two-spin squeezing criterion based on quadrature operators constructed from the U and V spins. Numerical diagonalization for N=1000 atoms in the T₃=0 block is performed to verify analytical ground state results.

## Key numbers

- Ground state fragmentation for ferromagnetic interactions gives ⟨n₀⟩ = N/2 and ⟨n±⟩ = N/4, verified analytically and numerically for N = 1000 atoms.
- The hypercharge symmetry point is Y₀ ≈ -N/6 for N ≫ 1, corresponding to n₀ = N/2.
- The two-spin (U-V) squeezing condition is ξ_uv^α = [Δ(Q_+^α)² + Δ(Q_-^{α+π/2})²] / |⟨Y⟩| < 3/4.
- Stationary states require the phase condition 2δ₀ = δ₋ + δ₊ and, for the special case α₋ = α₊, require P₀ = 1/2.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spinor-1 condensates by Ho (1998), Ohmi and Machida (1998), and Zhang et al. (2000) by providing a full SU(3) treatment that goes beyond the low-excitation limit considered by Duan et al. (2000) and the pseudo-spin approach of Pu et al. (2000). The decomposition of the Hamiltonian into commuting Kitagawa-Ueda and Barnett-Dupertuis type nonlinearities enables simultaneous squeezing in all three SU(2) subspaces, which was not possible in previous SU(2)-only treatments. The results enable new approaches to generating many-particle entanglement and spin squeezing for quantum information and atom interferometry applications with macroscopic populations in all three spin components.
