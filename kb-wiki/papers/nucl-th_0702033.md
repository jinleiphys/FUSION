# Di-electrons from $η$ meson Dalitz decay in proton-proton collisions

> The reaction $ pp \to pp η\to pp γe^+ e^-$ is discussed within a covariant effective meson-nucleon theory. The model is adjusted to data of the subreaction $pp \to pp η$. Our focus is on di-electrons from Dalitz decays of $η$ mesons, $η\to γγ^* \toγe^+e^-$, and the role of the corresponding transition form factor $F_{ηγγ^*}$. Numerical results are presented for the intermediate energy kinematics of HADES experiments.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete description of the reaction $pp \to pp \eta \to pp \gamma e^+ e^-$ that treats the intermediate $\eta$ meson as off-shell, allowing di-electron invariant masses larger than the $\eta$ pole mass, rather than using a factorized on-shell approximation. The model is adjusted to data of the subreaction $pp \to pp \eta$ and focuses on the role of the transition form factor $F_{\eta \gamma \gamma^*}$ in the Dalitz decay $\eta \to \gamma e^+ e^-$.

## Method

The authors employ a covariant effective meson-nucleon theory with one-boson exchange (OBE) to describe $\eta$ production in proton-proton collisions, including nucleon, meson conversion, and nucleon resonance currents (specifically $S_{11}(1535)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, and $P_{11}(1440)$ resonances). The Dalitz decay $\eta \to \gamma e^+ e^-$ is implemented via the transition form factor $F_{\eta \gamma \gamma^*}$, with the full reaction cross section factorized into production and decay parts using duplication kinematics. Initial state interactions are treated with a reduction factor from inelasticities and phase shifts, while final state interactions are accounted for via the Jost function formalism.

## Key numbers

- The branching ratio $Br(\eta \to \gamma \gamma) = (39.38 \pm 0.26)\%$ is used to determine the coupling constant $|f_{\eta \gamma \gamma}| \simeq 0.025 \, \text{GeV}^{-1}$ from the total width $\Gamma_{\eta, \text{tot}} = (1.3 \pm 0.07) \, \text{keV}$.
- The coupling constants for the conversion current are $g_{\omega \omega \eta} \simeq 4.85$ and $g_{\rho \rho \eta} \simeq 4.95$, calculated from radiative decay within VMD and SU(3).
- The nucleon cut-off parameter for the bremsstrahlung $NN\eta$ vertex is $\Lambda^{\text{br.}}_{\eta NN} = 1.2 \, \text{GeV}$, with resonance cut-offs $\Lambda^{\text{br.}}_{\eta NN_{1535}} = 1.3 \, \text{GeV}$, $\Lambda^{\text{br.}}_{\eta NN_{1520}} = 1.1 \, \text{GeV}$, and $\Lambda^{\text{br.}}_{\eta NN_{1440}} = 1.2 \, \text{GeV}$.
- The reduction factors for initial state interaction are $\zeta = 0.277$ for the $^1P_1$ wave and $\zeta = 0.243$ for the $^3P_0$ wave.

## Context

This work extends previous OBE-based models for $\eta$ production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, such as those by Wilkin (1993), Santra (1994), Gedalin (1994), and Nakayama (2001), by including a complete treatment of the Dalitz decay with off-shell $\eta$ mesons. The results are directly relevant to the HADES experiment at intermediate energies, where $\eta$ Dalitz decays constitute a prominent source of di-electrons in the invariant-mass region 150-500 MeV, and the approach enables better control of background processes for studies of chiral symmetry restoration in compressed nuclear matter.
