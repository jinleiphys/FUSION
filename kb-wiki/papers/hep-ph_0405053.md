# Non-Fermi Liquid Effects in QCD at High Density

> We study non-Fermi liquid effects due to the exchange of unscreened magnetic gluons in high density QCD by using an effective field theory. A one-loop calculation gives the well known result that magnetic gluons lead to a logarithmic enhancement in the fermion self energy near the Fermi surface. The self energy is of the form $Σ(ω)\sim ωγ\log(ω)$, where $ω$ is the energy of the fermion, $γ=O(g^2)$, and $g$ is the coupling constant. Using an analysis of the Dyson-Schwinger equations we show that, in the weak coupling limit, this result is not modified by higher order corrections even in the regime where the logarithm is large, $γ\log(ω)\sim 1$. We also show that this result is consistent with the renormalization group equation in the high density effective field theory.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the one-loop result for the fermion self energy in high-density QCD, which has the form \(\Sigma(\omega)\sim \omega\gamma\log(\omega)\) with \(\gamma=O(g^2)\), is not modified by higher-order corrections even in the regime where the logarithm becomes large (\(\gamma\log(\omega)\sim 1\)), provided the coupling is weak. This conclusion holds despite the breakdown of naive perturbation theory at very low energies.

## Method

The authors analyze the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark self energy in the high density effective theory (HDET), using hard dense loop (HDL) resummed transverse gluon propagators that account for Landau damping. They solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation both analytically and numerically for the fermion self energy as a function of energy, and also study the renormalization group equation for the quark propagator in the effective theory, including the running of the Fermi velocity and the coupling constant.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop fermion self energy is \(\Sigma(\omega) = \frac{g^2}{9\pi^2} \omega \log\left(\frac{\Lambda}{\omega}\right)\), where \(\omega\) is the fermion energy and \(\Lambda\) is a cutoff.
* The scale of non-Fermi liquid effects is \(\omega_{\it nfl} \sim \mu \exp(-c_{\it nfl}/g^2)\) with \(c_{\it nfl}=9\pi^2\); for \(\mu=0.5\) GeV this scale is \(7 \cdot 10^{-4}\) GeV, and for \(\mu=100\) GeV it is \(2 \cdot 10^{-25}\) GeV.
* The scale of superconductivity is \(\omega_{bcs} \sim b_0 \mu g^{-5} \exp(-c_{bcs}/g)\) with \(c_{bcs}=3\pi^2/\sqrt{2}\) and \(b_0=512\pi^4 \exp(-(\pi^2+4)/8)\); for \(\mu=0.5\) GeV this scale is \(2.2 \cdot 10^{-2}\) GeV.
* The anomalous term in the specific heat is \(C_v^{anom} = N_f(N_c^2-1)\frac{g^2\mu^2 T}{72\pi^2} \log\left(\frac{\Lambda}{T}\right)\), where \(C_v^{free}=N_c N_f \mu^2 T/3\).
* The correction to the superconducting gap from non-Fermi liquid effects reduces the gap by a factor \(\exp[-(\pi^2+4)(N_c-1)/16] \sim 0.18\) for \(N_c=3\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of non-Fermi liquid effects in dense QCD by Baym, Manuel, Brown, Boyanovsky, and others, who identified the logarithmic singularity in the quark self energy from unscreened magnetic gluon exchange. The paper resolves a contradiction between the Dyson-Schwinger approach and a renormalization group analysis by Boyanovsky and de Vega (2000), who had proposed a power-law self energy \(\Sigma(\omega) \sim \omega (\Lambda/\omega)^\gamma\). The authors show that the correct solution is the one-loop logarithmic form, consistent with the renormalization group when the non-vanishing beta function is included. This result enables reliable calculations of transport properties (thermal conductivity, neutrino emissivity) in neutron star matter and clarifies that non-Fermi liquid effects do not qualitatively alter the superconducting phase, though they give a calculable correction to the gap.
