# Photoproduction of the Lambda(1405) on the proton and nuclei

> We study the gamma p ---> K^+ Lambda(1405) reaction at energies close to threshold using a chiral unitary model where the resonance is generated dynamically from K^-p interaction with other channels constructed from the octets of baryons and mesons. Predictions are made for cross sections into several channels and it is shown that the detection of the K^+ is sufficient to determine the shape and strength of the Lambda(1405) resonance. The determination of the resonance properties in nuclei requires instead the detection of the resonance decay channels. Pauli blocking effects on the resonance, which have been shown to be very important for the resonance at rest in the nucleus, are irrelevant here where the resonance is produced with a large momentum. The nuclear modifications here would thus offer information on the resonance and K^- nucleus dynamics complementary to the one offered so far by K^- atoms.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the shape and strength of the Lambda(1405) resonance can be determined from the gamma p -> K+ Lambda(1405) reaction by detecting only the K+ meson, but only for the elementary reaction on a free proton; for nuclear targets, the K+ detection alone loses all resonance information due to Fermi motion spreading, and one must instead detect the resonance decay products (pi Sigma) to extract nuclear medium effects.

## Method

The authors use a chiral unitary model where the Lambda(1405) resonance is generated dynamically from a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation involving 10 meson-baryon channels (K- p, anti-K0 n, pi0 Lambda, pi0 Sigma0, eta Lambda, eta Sigma0, pi+ Sigma-, pi- Sigma+, K+ Xi-, K0 Xi0). The photon coupling is introduced via a contact term derived from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian, and calculations are performed at a photon energy of E_gamma Lab = 1.7 GeV where meson-pole terms are negligible. For nuclear targets, the cross section is obtained by convoluting the elementary cross section with the Fermi momentum distribution of the nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy chosen: E_gamma Lab = 1.7 GeV, where meson-pole contributions are less than 5%
- The pion decay constant used: f_pi = 93 MeV, with an average value f = 1.15 f_pi adopted in the model
- Cutoff parameter for loop regularization: q_max = 630 MeV, the only fitted parameter
- Predicted cross sections: of the order of 5 microbarn/GeV, stated to be in the easily attainable range at TJNAF and LEPS of SPring8/RCNP
- The Lambda(1405) resonance decays only into the pi Sigma channel, with a 12% branching ratio for the competing p-wave Sigma(1385) resonance into the same channel

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary coupled-channel approach of Oset and Ramos (1998) and earlier studies by Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) that describe the Lambda(1405) as a dynamically generated quasibound state of mesons and baryons, rather than a genuine three-quark baryon. The paper addresses the unresolved theoretical debate about nuclear medium modifications of the resonance: earlier work by Waas, Kaiser, and Weise (1996) found strong Pauli blocking effects shifting the resonance upward, while more recent self-consistent calculations by Ramos and Oset (1998) and by Cieply, Friedman, Gal, and Mares (1998) found the resonance position barely moves but becomes wider. The proposed photoproduction reaction offers a new experimental probe complementary to kaonic atom data, and the authors note that Pauli blocking is irrelevant here because the resonance is produced with large momentum, so any observed nuclear modifications would reflect other dynamical effects.
