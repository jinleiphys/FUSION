# $α$ particles and the pasta phase

> The effects of the $α$ particles in nuclear matter at low densities are investigated within three different parametrizations of relativistic models at finite temperature. Both homogeneous and inhomogeneous matter (pasta phase) are described for neutral nuclear matter with fixed proton fractions and stellar matter subject to $β$-equilibrium and trapped neutrinos. In homogeneous matter, $α$ particles are only present at densities below 0.02 fm$^{-3}$ and their presence decreases with the increase of the temperature and, for a fixed temperature, the $α$ particle fraction decreases for smaller proton fractions. A repulsive interaction is important to mimic the dissolution of the clusters in homogeneous matter. The effects of the $α$ particles on the pasta structure is very small except close to the critical temperatures and / or proton fractions when it may still predict a pasta phase while no pasta phase would occur in the absence of light clusters. It is shown that for densities above 0.01 fm$^{-3}$ the $α$ particle fraction in the pasta phase is much larger than the $α$ particle fraction in homogeneous matter.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the influence of alpha particles on both homogeneous nuclear matter and the pasta phase at low densities, finding that alpha particles are only present in homogeneous matter below 0.02 fm$^{-3}$ and that their effect on pasta structure is very small except near critical temperatures or proton fractions, where they may still predict a pasta phase when no pasta phase would occur without light clusters.

## Method

The authors use three different parametrizations of relativistic mean-field models (NL3, GM1, and TW) at finite temperature, describing both homogeneous and inhomogeneous matter via the coexisting phases method with a newly parametrized surface energy coefficient derived from Thomas-Fermi calculations. Alpha particles are included as bosons with a chemical potential given by $\mu_{\alpha}=2(\mu_p+\mu_n)$, and a repulsive vector interaction ($\Gamma_{\alpha}=4\Gamma_v$) is introduced to mimic the excluded volume effect and cluster dissolution at high densities. Calculations are performed for neutral nuclear matter with fixed proton fractions and for stellar matter subject to beta-equilibrium with trapped neutrinos and a lepton fraction of 0.4.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the pasta phase by Avancini et al. (2009) and Avancini et al. (2010) that used the coexisting phases method and Thomas-Fermi approximation with relativistic models, where it was found that beta-equilibrium matter shows small or non-existent pasta structure at finite temperatures above approximately 4 MeV. The inclusion of alpha particles follows the approach of Blaschke et al. (2009) for light clusters in a density-dependent relativistic model, and the results are compared with the virial equation of state of Horowitz and Schwenk (2006). The paper enables more complete equations of state for supernova simulations and protoneutron star evolution by incorporating light cluster effects into the pasta phase description, particularly for matter with trapped neutrinos where the pasta phase may persist to higher temperatures.
