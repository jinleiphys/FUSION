# Real Time Nonequilibrium Dynamics of Quantum Plasmas. Quantum Kinetics and the Dynamical Renormalization Group

> We implement the dynamical renormalization group (DRG) using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation for the real-time nonequilibrium dynamics in hot plasmas. The focus is on the study of the relaxation of gauge and fermionic mean fields and on the quantum kinetics of the photon and fermion distribution functions. As a concrete physical prediction, we find that for a QGP of temperature T sim 200 MeV and lifetime 10 < t < 50 fm/c there is a new contribution to the hard (k \sim T) photon production from off-shell bremsstrahlung (q rightarrow q gamma and bar{q} rightarrow bar{q} gamma) at just O (alpha) that grows logarithmically in time and is comparable to the known on-shell Compton scattering and pair annihilation at O(alpha alpha_s).

## Key claim
For a quark-gluon plasma at temperature T ~ 200 MeV and lifetime 10 to 50 fm/c, there is a new contribution to hard (k ~ T) photon production from off-shell bremsstrahlung (q -> q gamma and anti-q -> anti-q gamma) at just O(alpha) that grows logarithmically in time and is comparable to the known on-shell Compton scattering and pair annihilation at O(alpha alpha_s). This result is extrapolated from QED to a thermalized QGP with two flavors of light quarks.

## Method
The author implements the dynamical renormalization group (DRG) using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation for real-time nonequilibrium dynamics in hot plasmas. The Schwinger-Dyson equation for the expectation value of the gauge field is solved via Laplace transform, and the DRG resummation is applied to study relaxation of gauge and fermionic mean fields as well as quantum kinetics of photon and fermion distribution functions. The analysis covers soft momentum (k ~ eT), ultrasoft momentum (k << eT), and semihard momentum (eT << k << T) regimes under weak electromagnetic coupling.

## Key numbers
- For a QGP at temperature T ~ 200 MeV and lifetime 10 to 50 fm/c, off-shell bremsstrahlung photon production at O(alpha) is comparable to on-shell Compton scattering and pair annihilation at O(alpha alpha_s).
- The total number of hard photons per invariant phase space volume at time t is N(t) = (5 alpha T^3)/(18 pi^2 k^2) {ln[2k(t-t0)] + gamma_E - 1} for k(t-t0) > 1, with t0 ~ 1 fm/c.
- For semihard momentum (eT << k << T), the gauge mean field relaxes as (t/tau_0)^(-e^2 T^2 / (12 k^2)), where tau_0 ~ 1/k.
- For ultrasoft momentum (k << eT), the Landau damping width is Gamma_k = 12 k^3 / (pi e^2 T^2).
- The fermion mean field for hard momentum relaxes as exp(-alpha T t [ln(omega_P t) + 0.12652...]) times oscillating phases, with omega_P the plasma frequency.
- The departure from equilibrium of the photon distribution function relaxes as (t-t0)^(-e^2 T^2 / (6 k^2)) for k(t-t0) >> 1.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous programme on real-time evolution of field theory, which began in 1995 studying scalar theories and was later generalized to hot scalar QED (de Vega 1995, 1996). It provides a comprehensive study of nonequilibrium dynamics of abelian QED and nonabelian QCD plasmas, using the DRG as an alternative to the Bloch-Nordsieck treatment of infrared divergences associated with soft photon bremsstrahlung in the medium (Blaizot and Iancu 1997). The results enable direct real-time calculation of photon production from off-shell effects in heavy ion collisions, bypassing the assumption of completed collisions used in standard kinetic descriptions (Kapusta 1989).
