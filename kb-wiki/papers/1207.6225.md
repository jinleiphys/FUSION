# Toward understanding the microscopic origin of nuclear clustering

> Open Quantum System (OQS) description of a many-body system involves interaction of Shell Model (SM) states through the particle continuum. In realistic nuclear applications, this interaction may lead to collective phenomena in the ensemble of SM states. We claim that the nuclear clustering is an emergent, near-threshold phenomenon, which cannot be elucidated within the Closed Quantum System (CQS) framework. We approach this problem by investigating the near-threshold behavior of Exceptional Points (EPs) in the realistic Continuum Shell Model (CSM). The consequences for the alpha-clustering phenomenon are discussed.

## Key claim
The authors claim that nuclear clustering is an emergent, near-threshold phenomenon that arises from the coupling of Shell Model states to the particle continuum, and cannot be explained within a Closed Quantum System framework. The hedge is that this mechanism is asserted to be universal for all types of clustering (including alpha-clustering), but the paper explicitly demonstrates it only for one-proton decay channels, arguing by analogy that the same mechanism applies to heavier charged clusters.

## Method
The authors employed the real-energy Continuum Shell Model (CSM) using the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) framework, which couples Shell Model states to the particle continuum via an effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian. They analyzed the behavior of Exceptional Points (EPs) and their trajectories (exceptional threads, ETs) in the complex-extended Hamiltonian as a function of system energy E and continuum-coupling strength V0, focusing on the region around the one-proton decay threshold in 16Ne and 24S. The collective mixing of SM eigenstates was studied through the distribution of weights in the correlated OQS eigenstates and the continuum-coupling correlation energy.

## Key numbers
- For 16Ne, the turning point energy ETP for all ETs involving the aligned 0+ state is approximately 0.55 MeV above the proton decay threshold.
- The continuum-coupling strength V0 for reproducing experimental decay widths in 15F is V0 = -3500 ± 450 MeV·fm3 for the ground state 1/2+ and V0 = -1100 ± 50 MeV·fm3 for the first excited state 5/2+.
- The full width at half maximum σ of the correlation energy peak for charged particle decay channels is less than approximately 0.5 MeV, compared to 1 MeV ≤ σ ≤ 3 MeV for neutron decay channels.
- For the 8Be + α case (Zt = 8, R0 = 4 fm), relevant to the Hoyle resonance in 12C, the turning point energy ETP agrees well with the curve obtained from 24S wave functions.

## Context
This work extends the Ikeda conjecture (Ikeda et al., 1968) that alpha-cluster states appear near alpha-particle decay thresholds, reformulating it as a general consequence of continuum coupling in Open Quantum Systems rather than a property of nuclear forces or dynamical symmetries. The authors argue that the failure of Closed Quantum System approaches (such as the standard Shell Model) to predict cluster states at observed low excitation energies is resolved by including continuum coupling. The results enable predictions for the optimal energy window for cluster correlations in any charged-particle decay channel, including unstable clusters like dineutron or 8Be, and suggest that the disruptive influence of the spin-orbit interaction on alpha-clustering in heavier nuclei (as discussed in Ref. 43) may instead be understood as a Coulomb-barrier effect.
