# Properties of strange vector mesons in dense and hot matter

> We investigate the in-medium properties of strange vector mesons ($K^*$ and $\bar K^*$) in dense and hot nuclear matter based on chirally motivated models of the meson selfenergies. We parameterise medium effects as density or temperature dependent effective masses and widths, obtain the vector meson spectral functions within a Breit-Wigner prescription (as often used in transport simulations) and study whether such an approach can retain the essential features of full microscopic calculations. For $μ_B\ne 0$ the medium corrections arise from $\bar K^* (K^*) N$ scattering and the $\bar K^* (K^*) \to \bar K (K) π$ decay mode (accounting for in-medium $\bar K (K)$ dynamics). We calculate the scattering contribution to the $K^*$ selfenergy based on the hidden local symmetry formalism for vector meson nucleon interactions, whereas for the $\bar K^*$ selfenergy we implement recent results from a selfconsistent coupled-channel determination within the same approach. For $μ_B\simeq 0$ and finite temperature we rely on a phenomenological approach for the kaon selfenergy in a hot pionic medium consistent with chiral symmetry, and evaluate the $\bar K^* (K^*) \to \bar K (K) π$ decay width. The emergence of a mass shift at finite temperature is studied with a dispersion relation over the imaginary part of the vector meson selfenergy.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether a simple Breit-Wigner parameterisation with density- or temperature-dependent effective masses and widths can capture the essential features of full microscopic calculations for strange vector mesons ($K^*$ and $\bar K^*$) in dense and hot nuclear matter, finding that while it works reasonably for $K^*$, it fails to reproduce the complex multi-mode structure of the $\bar K^*$ spectral function due to interference with collective excitations.

## Method

The authors calculate the $K^*$ selfenergy using a $t\rho$ approximation and a unitarised Bethe-Salpeter approach within the hidden local symmetry formalism for $K^* N$ scattering, and adopt selfconsistent coupled-channel results from Tolos (2010) for the $\bar K^*$ selfenergy in cold nuclear matter. For hot matter at zero baryon chemical potential, they evaluate the $K^*$ decay width using in-medium kaon spectral functions from chiral perturbation theory (Fuchs et al., 2002) and compute the real part of the selfenergy via a regularised dispersion relation to obtain the mass shift. The in-medium spectral functions are constructed using a relativistic Breit-Wigner prescription with density- or temperature-dependent effective masses and widths.

## Key numbers

- $K^*$ vacuum decay width: $\Gamma_{K^{*},\bar{K}^{*}}^{0} = 42$ MeV at nominal mass $\mu_0=892$ MeV
- $K^*$ mass shift at normal nuclear matter density ($\rho_0=0.17$ fm$^{-3}$): $\delta M_{K^*}\simeq 50$ MeV (repulsive) in $t\rho$ approximation, reduced to $\delta M_{K^*}\simeq 30$ MeV with unitarised amplitudes
- $K^*$ mass shift at $\rho_N=\rho_0$ estimated within errors: approximately 5% (positive, repulsive)
- $\bar K^*$ width at $\rho_N=\rho_0$: enlarged beyond 200 MeV, with in-medium $\bar K \pi$ decay mode contributing about 100 MeV
- $\bar K^*$ nuclear optical potential: moderate attraction of about 50 MeV
- Kaon in hot matter at $T=150$ MeV: $\Gamma^*_K \simeq 50$ MeV, $\delta M_K \simeq -35$ MeV (attractive)
- $K^*$ mass shift in hot matter: repulsive, below 10 MeV up to $T=150$ MeV, practically negligible up to $T=100$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strange meson properties in nuclear matter, particularly the selfconsistent coupled-channel determination of $\bar K^*$ selfenergy by Tolos (2010) and the hidden local symmetry approach for vector meson-nucleon interactions by Oset (2009). The authors also build on chiral perturbation theory calculations of kaon selfenergies in hot pionic matter by Fuchs et al. (2002). The results are relevant for interpreting transport simulations of heavy-ion collisions, such as the HADES Collaboration's recent observation of reduced $K^{*0}$ yield in Ar+KCL collisions at 1.76 $A$GeV, and for future experiments at FAIR and NICA facilities.
