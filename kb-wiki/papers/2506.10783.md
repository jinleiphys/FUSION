# Electric conductivity and flavor diffusion in a viscous, resistive quark-gluon plasma for weak and strong magnetic fields

> We present a microscopic calculation of the electric conductivity and net-particle diffusion coefficients for a viscous and resistive ultra-relativistic plasma. Our results might be of interest for several astrophysical and cosmological problems, but the main physical application we have in mind is the hot deconfined matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Accordingly, as charged particles of the medium we take three species (flavors) of light (massless for the sake of simplicity) quarks -- $u$, $d$ and $s$ -- and antiquarks, entailing the existence of three macroscopic conserved charges: baryon number ${\cal B}$, electric charge $Q$ and strangeness $S$. Our results are valid both in a weakly and in a strongly-magnetized plasma, where the energy stored in the magnetic field is comparable to the one carried by the medium particles. Actually, for a conformal fluid, the behavior of the system only depends on the ratio between the thermal and the magnetic pressure, the so-called plasma beta-parameter, acting as a scaling variable. Our calculation, starting from a relativistic Boltzmann-Vlasov equation, is based on a generalized Chapman-Enskog approach in which space-time gradients and the local electric field are treated as first-order quantities in a perturbative expansion, while terms containing magnetic corrections are considered of zeroth order and hence self-consistently resummed. We find that, also in the strong-field limit, for each conserved charge a generalized Wiedemann-Franz law, connecting charge conductivity and diffusion coefficient, exists. However these transport coefficients acquire a non-trivial tensor structure, reflecting the development of a longitudinal, a transverse and a Hall current as a response to electric fields or density gradients.

## Key claim

The paper presents a microscopic calculation of electric conductivity and net-particle diffusion coefficients for a viscous, resistive quark-gluon plasma that is valid for both weak and strong magnetic fields, where the energy stored in the magnetic field is comparable to the thermal energy. The authors find that a generalized Wiedemann-Franz law connecting charge conductivity and diffusion coefficient exists for each conserved charge even in the strong-field limit, but the transport coefficients acquire a non-trivial tensor structure with longitudinal, transverse, and Hall components.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic Boltzmann-Vlasov equation for quark and antiquark phase-space distributions in relaxation-time approximation, considering three massless quark flavors (u, d, s) and their antiparticles. They perform a generalized Chapman-Enskog expansion in which space-time gradients and the local electric field are treated as first-order quantities in a perturbative expansion, while magnetic corrections are considered zeroth order and self-consistently resummed to all orders. For a conformal plasma, all dimensionful quantities are rescaled by powers of temperature, making the transport coefficients functions of a single scaling variable: the plasma beta-parameter (ratio of thermal to magnetic pressure).

- The magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC reach values of order 10^15-10^16 T (from the introduction, citing Skokov (2009), Deng (2012), and Tuchin (2013))

- The electric conductivity is proportional to the relaxation time as sigma_Q ~ tau_R e^2 T^2

## Context

This work extends previous kinetic descriptions of resistive MHD for heavy-ion collisions by Denicol (2019), Panda (2021), and Kushwah (2024) to the regime of strong magnetic fields where magnetic and thermal pressures are comparable. The results are intended for implementation in relativistic magneto-hydrodynamics codes used to model the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions, as well as for astrophysical applications such as magnetized neutron stars, pulsar wind nebulae, and accretion onto black holes where the plasma beta-parameter can be small. The approach generalizes the Muller-Israel-Stewart theory to include multiple conserved charges (baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness) in the presence of strong electromagnetic fields.
