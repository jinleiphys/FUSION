# Pressure and non-linear quark number susceptibilities in QCD

> We report the first measurements of sixth order non-linear quark number susceptibilities in continuum QCD. This extends our earlier computation of the continuum limit of non-linear quark number susceptibilities and the pressure in QCD for m/Tc=0.1. For T/Tc > 1.5, no phase transitions are observable for mu<30T. Systematics of off-diagonal susceptibilities are reported. The equation of state in the high temperature phase of the QCD plasma is constructed.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurements of sixth-order non-linear quark number susceptibilities in continuum QCD, but the continuum limit of the sixth-order diagonal susceptibility \(\chi_{uuuuuu}\) is consistent with zero at the 99% confidence level, and the sixth-order term in the Taylor expansion of the pressure is totally negligible for \(T/T_c \ge 1.5\) at chemical potentials relevant to SPS and RHIC experiments.

## Method

The authors compute quark number susceptibilities up to sixth order using lattice QCD in the quenched approximation for \(T \ge 1.5 T_c\) and in dynamical two-flavor computations near \(T_c\), with quark mass \(m/T_c = 0.1\). Off-diagonal susceptibilities are evaluated using noisy estimators with random complex Gaussian vectors, and the sixth-order diagonal susceptibility \(\chi_{uuuuuu}\) is reconstructed from the sixth derivative of the partition function using a combination of operator traces. Continuum extrapolations are performed using data at multiple lattice spacings (varying temporal extent \(N_t\) from 4 to 14).

## Key numbers

- Sixth-order diagonal susceptibility \(T^2 \chi_{uuuuuu}\) at \(T/T_c = 2.0\): \(0.931(2)\) for \(N_t=4\), decreasing to \(0.02(3)\) for \(N_t=14\) (quenched QCD)
- Sixth-order off-diagonal susceptibility \(T^2 \chi_{uuuudd}\) at \(T/T_c = 2.0\): \(0.0385(2)\) for \(N_t=4\), decreasing to \(0.00482(7)\) for \(N_t=14\) (quenched QCD)
- Radius of convergence from fourth order: \(\mu_*^{(4)}/T \approx 4.6\) in the continuum limit for \(1.5 \le T/T_c \le 3\)
- Radius of convergence from sixth order: \(\mu_*^{(6)}/T \approx 28\) for \(N_t=14\), possibly significantly larger in the continuum
- At SPS energies (\(\mu/T_c = 0.45\)), the fourth-order term gives a 5% correction to \(\Delta P/T^4\), and the sixth-order term is negligible for \(T/T_c \ge 1.5\)
- At RHIC (\(\mu/T_c = 0.15\)), the leading term contributes more than 99% of the total pressure

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier computation of the continuum limit of non-linear quark number susceptibilities and the pressure in QCD (Gavai and Gupta, 2002). The results confirm that for \(T/T_c > 1.5\), no phase transitions are observable for \(\mu < 30T\), and the equation of state is dominated by the second-order term, justifying the scaling approach used by Karsch et al. (2001). The measurements enable construction of the equation of state at finite chemical potential via Taylor expansion, which is the simplest and most accurate route for the temperature and chemical potential range relevant to heavy-ion collision experiments at SPS and RHIC.
