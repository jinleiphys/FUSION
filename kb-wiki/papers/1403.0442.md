# Zeta Function Regularization of Photon Polarization Tensor for a Magnetized Vacuum

> In this paper, we have developed a systematic technique to regularize double summations of Landau levels and analytically evaluated the photon vacuum polarization at an external magnetic field. The final results are described by Lerch transcendent $Φ(z,s,v)$ or its $z$-derivation. We have found that the tensor of vacuum polarization is split into not only longitudinal and transverse parts but also another mixture component. We have obtained a complete expression of the magnetized photon vacuum polarization at any kinematic regime and any strength of magnetic field for the first time. In the weak $B$-fields, after canceling out a logarithmic counter term, all three scalar functions are limited to the usual photon polarization tensor without turning on magnetic field. In the strong $B$-fields, the calculations under Lowest Landau Level approximation are only valid at the region $M^2\gg q_{\shortparallel}^2$, but not correct while $q_{\shortparallel}^2\gg M^2$, where, an imaginary part has been missed. It reminds us, a recalculation of the gap equation under a full consideration of all Landau Levels is necessary in the next future.

## Key claim

The paper claims to have obtained, for the first time, a complete expression of the magnetized photon vacuum polarization tensor valid at any kinematic regime and any strength of magnetic field, using zeta function regularization of double Landau-level summations. However, the final analytic expressions rely on an approximation for the function \(h(x,\tau)\) (Equation 18) and the authors note that slight differences between scalar functions remain due to this necessary simplification, meaning the result is not fully exact for all regimes.

## Method

The authors start from the fermion propagator in a constant magnetic field decomposed over Landau levels and compute the one-loop photon vacuum polarization tensor \(\Pi^{\mu\nu}\). They introduce Feynman parameters and a proper-time representation to combine denominator factors, then perform the double summation over Landau levels using the generating function of Laguerre polynomials with a regulator \(z = e^{-\epsilon}\). The resulting integrals over longitudinal and transverse momenta are evaluated using dimensional regularization in the longitudinal subspace, and the remaining proper-time integral is expressed in terms of the Lerch transcendent \(\Phi(z,s,v)\) and its \(z\)-derivative. The final scalar functions \(\pi_{\parallel}, \pi_{\perp}, \pi_{m}\) are given in terms of di-gamma functions and zeta functions after taking the limit \(z \to 1\).

## Key numbers

* For strong magnetic fields (\(eB \gg q^2, M^2\)), the imaginary part of the longitudinal scalar function is \(\Im \pi_{\parallel} = -4\pi eB M^2 / (q^2 \sqrt{\Delta_{\parallel}})\) where \(\Delta_{\parallel} = q_{\parallel}^4 - 4 q_{\parallel}^2 M^2\).
* For strong fields, the real part of \(\pi_{\parallel}\) in the region \(q_{\parallel}^2 \gg M^2\) reduces to \(\Re \pi_{\parallel} = -2eB/q_{\parallel}^2 - \gamma/3\) (with \(\gamma \approx 0.577216\)).
* For strong fields, the mixture scalar function is \(\pi_m = -4 \ln A\) where \(A \approx 1.28243\) (Glaisher-Kinkelin constant).
* For weak fields (\(eB \ll q^2, M^2\)), the imaginary parts of \(\pi_{\parallel}\) and \(\pi_{\perp}\) are given by \(\Im \pi_{\parallel} = \pi_{\mathbb{I}} + 4\pi eB M^2/(q^2\sqrt{\Delta})\) and \(\Im \pi_{\perp} = \pi_{\mathbb{I}} + 8\pi eB M^2/(q^2\sqrt{\Delta})\) with \(\pi_{\mathbb{I}} = -\frac{\pi}{3}\sqrt{1-4M^2/q^2}(1+2M^2/q^2)\).
* For weak fields and \(q^2 \gg M^2\), the regularized real part of \(\pi_{\parallel}\) is \(\Re \hat{\pi}_{\parallel} = \frac{1}{3}(\ln(q^2/M^2) - 5/3) + 2M^2/q^2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of the magnetized photon vacuum polarization that were limited to the Lowest Landau Level (LLL) approximation, such as those by Calucci (1994) and Gusynin (1996). The authors confirm that the LLL approximation is valid only when \(M^2 \gg q_{\parallel}^2\) and misses an imaginary part when \(q_{\parallel}^2 \gg M^2\). The complete expression enables recalculation of the gap equation for dynamical fermion mass without relying on the LLL approximation, and the authors note that Hattori and Itakura (2013) observed the same kind of pair creation process using an alternative double proper-time method. The results are expected to be relevant for studies of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC where magnetic fields of order \(10^{18-20}\) G are produced.
