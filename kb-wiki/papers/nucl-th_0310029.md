# "Super-radiance" and the width of exotic baryons

> It is suggested that the narrow width of the recently observed resonance $Θ^{+}(1540)$ with strangeness $S=+1$ could be a result of the super-radiance mechanism of the redistribution of the widths of overlapping resonances due to their coupling through common decay channels.

## Key claim

The narrow width of the exotic baryon resonance Θ⁺(1540) with strangeness S=+1 could be a result of the super-radiance mechanism, where the redistribution of widths among overlapping resonances due to their coupling through common decay channels creates a very narrow state at the expense of a broad super-radiant state, provided the intrinsic states have spacings smaller than their decay widths.

## Method

The authors apply the Feshbach projection method to construct an effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian for a set of internal states coupled to open decay channels, where the anti-Hermitian part takes a separable form dictated by unitarity. They illustrate the mechanism with a schematic model of degenerate states coupled to a single channel, then extend to a two-resonance model with a single KN decay channel, solving the secular equations for the complex eigenvalues and computing the scattering cross section. The decay amplitudes are parameterized with a p-wave threshold energy dependence and a high-energy cutoff.

## Key numbers

- The observed Θ⁺(1540) resonance has a width smaller than 25 MeV, situated on top of a broad background peak over 100 MeV wide.
- For two overlapping resonances with energy spacing Δε ≈ 20 MeV and bare widths Γ₁,₂ ≈ 200 MeV, the estimate from Eq. (6) gives a narrow resonance width Γ_nar ≈ 4 MeV.
- A direct calculation for a p-wave resonance in a square well of radius a=1 fm gives width 190 MeV at depth V=333 MeV; for a=2 fm and V=14 MeV the width is 327 MeV.
- For an f-wave resonance with a=1 fm, the width is 4 MeV at V=1829 MeV; for a=2 fm the width is 49 MeV at V=316 MeV.
- In the two-resonance model with parameters ε₁=1535 MeV, ε₂=1560 MeV, v=1 MeV, and bare widths γ₁=120 MeV and γ₂=60 MeV at 1550 MeV, the solution gives a narrow resonance at 109 MeV above threshold with width 2.5 MeV and a broad bump with centroid at 120 MeV and width 178 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the super-radiance mechanism, originally introduced by Dicke (1954) for atomic physics and later applied to nuclear physics by several authors including Sokolov and Zelevinsky, to the newly observed exotic baryon Θ⁺(1540). The authors draw a direct analogy with their earlier work on Δ-isobar resonances in nuclei (Auerbach and Zelevinsky), where narrow resonances were observed superimposed on a broad peak in the ¹²C(e,e'pπ⁻)¹¹C experiment at Mainz. The mechanism is presented as universal and compatible with any intrinsic dynamics, whether quasimolecular KN states as discussed by Iachello or many-quark bag states, and it enables the interpretation of narrow pentaquark resonances without requiring special selection rules or exotic dynamics.
