# Effects of Strong Magnetic Fields on the Hadron-Quark Deconfinement Transition

> The aim of the present work is to investigate the effects of strong magnetic fields on the hadron-quark phase transition point at zero temperature. To describe the hadronic phase, a relativistic mean field (RMF) model is used and to describe the quark phase a density dependent quark mass model (DDQM) is employed. As compared with the results obtained with non-magnetised matter, we observe a shift of the transition point towards higher pressures and, generally also towards higher chemical potentials. An investigation of the phase transitions that could sustain hybrid stars is also performed.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a strong magnetic field shifts the hadron-quark deconfinement transition point towards higher pressures and generally also towards higher chemical potentials compared to non-magnetised matter, but this result is obtained by neglecting the pure magnetic field contribution to the pressure in both models to enable comparison with unmagnetised results.

## Method

The hadronic phase is described using the GM1 parameterization of a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with the full baryon octet, while the quark phase is described using a density dependent quark mass (DDQM) model. The phase transition is treated as a first-order transition with flavor conservation imposed during the transition, and the Gibbs criteria of equal pressure and chemical potential are used to find coexistence points for various DDQM parameter sets at zero temperature with and without a magnetic field of B = 3 × 10^18 G.

## Key numbers

- For C = 0, √D = 158.5 MeV: coexistence chemical potential μ₀ shifts from 960 MeV (B=0) to 958 MeV (B=3×10^18 G); coexistence pressure p₀ shifts from 1.55 MeV/fm³ to 1.80 MeV/fm³
- For C = 0, √D = 165 MeV: μ₀ shifts from 1062 MeV to 1066 MeV; p₀ shifts from 21.98 MeV/fm³ to 24.70 MeV/fm³
- For C = 0.23, √D = 155 MeV: μ₀ shifts from 1130 MeV to 1145 MeV; p₀ shifts from 43.62 MeV/fm³ to 51.32 MeV/fm³
- For C = 0.365, √D = 142 MeV: μ₀ shifts from 1105 MeV to 1109 MeV; p₀ shifts from 34.98 MeV/fm³ to 38.30 MeV/fm³
- For C = 0.5, √D = 135.75 MeV: μ₀ shifts from 1202 MeV to 1242 MeV; p₀ shifts from 72.66 MeV/fm³ to 94.93 MeV/fm³
- For C = 0.68, √D = 130 MeV: μ₀ shifts from 1440 MeV to 1475 MeV; p₀ shifts from 215.50 MeV/fm³ to 247.53 MeV/fm³
- Hybrid star maximum masses (without magnetic field effects): M = 1.63 M_Sun for C=0.0, D^1/2=158.5 MeV; M = 1.78 M_Sun for C=0.23, D^1/2=145.75 MeV; M = 1.92 M_Sun for C=0.5, D^1/2=135.75 MeV
- A deconfined quark core appears at M = 0.90 M_Sun for parameters C=0.0, D^1/2=158.5 MeV
- For C=0.68, D^1/2=130 MeV, the maximum stable star is a pure hadronic star of M=1.96 M_Sun and R=12.42 km

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the hadron-quark phase transition at zero temperature (Marquez 2017, 2019) by incorporating strong magnetic fields, which had not been investigated with two different models for the hadronic and quark phases. The use of a density dependent quark mass model (DDQM) is novel for this transition problem, as prior work on magnetised phase transitions used the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (PRD85, 2012; Marcio 2015). The results enable future studies of the full magnetised QCD phase diagram at finite temperature and investigations of whether magnetars could contain deconfined quark cores, though the authors note that a proper general relativistic treatment of magnetised stars using the LORENE code remains to be done.
