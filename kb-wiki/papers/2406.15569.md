# Stochastic fluctuations and the relaxation time in transient relativistic fluids

> We argue that the ratio between the shear viscosity and the shear relaxation time, $η/τ_π$, should be defined as a thermodynamic quantity obtained from the equal-time symmetric correlator of the shear-stress tensor. In kinetic theory, we show that this ratio does not depend on the type of interaction. Similarly, an exact expression for this ratio is obtained for holographic gauge theories. We also determine how stochastic fluctuations change $η/τ_π$ in transient relativistic hydrodynamics and show that thermal fluctuations do not spoil causality and stability.

# Stochastic fluctuations and the relaxation time in transient relativistic fluids

## Key claim
The ratio η/τ_π (shear viscosity divided by shear relaxation time) can be defined as a thermodynamic quantity obtained from the equal-time symmetric correlator of the shear-stress tensor, remaining meaningful even when the non-hydrodynamic sector is gapless or when thermal fluctuations are present. This definition avoids the divergence problems that arise when τ_π is defined via Kubo formulas from gradient expansions.

## Method
The authors propose a new definition for the relaxation time τ_π based on an integral identity: 1/τ_π ≡ (1/η) ∫ (dΩ/2π) [𝒢(Ω,0)/Ω], where 𝒢 is the spectral density of the shear-stress tensor. They compute this integral exactly in kinetic theory using linear response to metric perturbations, and also evaluate it for holographic gauge theories using the shear spectral sum rule from Romatschke (2009). They then incorporate stochastic noise into the linearized Israel-Stewart equations and compute the leading-order corrections to η and η/τ_π from thermal fluctuations using the symmetrized correlator of the shear-stress tensor.

## Key numbers
- For massless particles in kinetic theory: τ_π = 5 η/(ε+P), independent of interaction type
- For strongly coupled holographic gauge theories with η/s = 1/4π: τ_π = (10/3) η/(ε+P) = (10/6)(1/(2πT))
- The causality and stability bound requires τ_π ≥ 2 η/(ε+P)
- Leading-order correction to shear viscosity from thermal fluctuations: η_LO = η + (17 T Λ (ε+P))/(120π² η), where Λ is a hard UV cutoff
- Leading-order correction to η/τ_π from thermal fluctuations: (η/τ_π)|_LO = η/τ_π + (T Λ³)/(6π²)
- Leading-order correction to energy density from fluctuations: ε_LO = ε + (T Λ³)/(2π²)

## Context
This work extends the discussion of relaxation time definitions in relativistic hydrodynamics, addressing shortcomings in previous approaches. The method of determining τ_π from the slowest non-hydrodynamic pole (Denicol, 2011) fails when the non-hydrodynamic sector is gapless, as proven for kinetic theories with vanishing cross-sections at high energies (Gavassino, 2024). The Kubo formula approach via gradient expansion (Baier, 2007) can yield negative τ_π and diverges when thermal fluctuations generate nonanalytic ω^(3/2) terms in the retarded Green's function (Kovtun, 2011). The new definition enables calculation of η/τ_π in kinetic theory, holography, and potentially on the lattice, and shows that stochastic transient relativistic hydrodynamics preserves causality and stability under thermal fluctuations.
