# Charmonium properties in hot quenched lattice QCD

> We study the properties of charmonium states at finite temperature in quenched QCD on large and fine isotropic lattices. We perform a detailed analysis of charmonium correlation and spectral functions both below and above $T_c$. Our analysis suggests that both S wave states ($J/ψ$ and $η_c$) and P wave states ($χ_{c0}$ and $χ_{c1}$) disappear already at about $1.5 T_c$. The charm diffusion coefficient is estimated through the Kubo formula and found to be compatible with zero below $T_c$ and approximately $1/πT$ at $1.5 T_c\lesssim T\lesssim 3 T_c$.

## Key claim

The analysis suggests that both S-wave charmonium states (J/ψ and ηc) and P-wave states (χc0 and χc1) disappear already at about 1.5 Tc, with the caveat that this conclusion depends on the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) analysis of spectral functions reconstructed from Euclidean correlation data on large isotropic lattices.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations using O(a)-improved Wilson (clover) fermions on isotropic lattices with lattice spacings from 0.01 fm to 0.03 fm, corresponding to temperatures from about 0.73 Tc to 2.93 Tc. They calculated Euclidean temporal correlation functions for scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector channels on lattices up to 128^3 × 96, then reconstructed spectral functions using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) and estimated the charm diffusion coefficient through the Kubo formula applied to the vector spectral function at vanishing frequency.

## Key numbers

- The finest lattice used was 128^3 × 96 at 0.73 Tc, with lattice spacing a = 0.010 fm and inverse lattice spacing a^{-1} = 18.97 GeV
- The J/ψ mass on the finest lattice was 3.472(2)(114) GeV, about 10% larger than the physical J/ψ mass of 3.097 GeV
- The ηc mass on the finest lattice was 3.341(2)(104) GeV
- The χc1 mass on the finest lattice was 4.02(2)(23) GeV
- The χc0 mass on the finest lattice was 4.52(2)(37) GeV
- The charm diffusion coefficient was found to be compatible with zero below Tc and approximately 1/(πT) at temperatures 1.5 Tc ≲ T ≲ 3 Tc
- The renormalized quark mass in the MSbar scheme at its own scale on the finest lattice was 1.093(2) GeV at 0.73 Tc and 1.095(2) GeV at 2.93 Tc

## Context

This work extends previous quenched lattice QCD studies of charmonium at finite temperature by Datta et al. (2004) using significantly larger isotropic lattices (doubling the number of temporal data points compared to that earlier study) and provides a more detailed MEM analysis of spectral functions. The results contradict earlier claims from anisotropic lattice studies by Asakawa et al. (2004), Umeda et al. (2005), and Aarts et al. (2007) that J/ψ survives to temperatures well above Tc, suggesting instead that both S-wave and P-wave states dissociate around 1.5 Tc. The charm diffusion coefficient estimate enables comparison with phenomenological models of heavy quark energy loss and elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions.
