# Neutrino propagation in Neutron Matter and the Nuclear Equation of State

> We study the propagation of neutrinos inside dense matter under the conditions prevailing in a proto-neutron star. Equations of state obtained with different nuclear effective interactions (Skyrme type and Gogny type) are first discussed. It is found that for many interactions, spin and/or isospin instabilities occur at densities larger than the saturation density of nuclear matter. From this study we select two representative interactions, SLy230b and D1P. We calculate the response functions in pure neutron matter where nuclear correlations are described at the Hartree-Fock plus RPA level. These response functions allow us to evaluate neutrino mean free paths corresponding to neutral current processes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the choice of nuclear effective interaction (Skyrme-type vs. Gogny-type) critically determines whether spin and/or isospin instabilities appear at densities relevant to proto-neutron stars, and that these instabilities in turn strongly affect neutrino mean free paths; the authors hedge that only two specific interactions (SLy230b and D1P) are selected because they avoid the most severe instabilities, and the results are therefore not generalizable to all effective interactions.

## Method

The authors first compare equations of state from several Skyrme and Gogny effective interactions against variational calculations, selecting SLy230b (Skyrme) and D1P (Gogny) as representative forces that avoid spin/isospin instabilities up to high densities. They then compute the dynamical structure factors for pure neutron matter at the Hartree-Fock plus RPA level, using these interactions, and from the structure factors evaluate neutrino mean free paths for neutral-current scattering via the standard electroweak formula.

## Key numbers

* The asymmetry energy coefficient at saturation density is approximately 32 MeV, as deduced from the semi-empirical mass formula.
* For the Skyrme interaction SLy230b, the ferromagnetic instability occurs at a density of 0.56 fm\(^{-3}\) in pure neutron matter (proton fraction \(X_p=0\)).
* The neutrino mean free path is calculated for an incident neutrino energy of \(E_1=60\) MeV, and at very low temperatures it follows a \(T^{-2}\) law.
* For the Gogny interaction D1P, RPA correlations increase mean free paths up to about \(5\rho_0\) (five times the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter).

## Context

This work extends prior variational calculations of neutron matter by Wiringa, Fiks, and Fabrocini (1988) based on realistic two- and three-body interactions (AV14, UV14, UVII), and it uses the effective interactions SLy230b (Chabanat et al., 1997) and D1P (Fornes et al., 1999) which were fitted to those variational results. The paper enables future studies of neutrino transport in proto-neutron star evolution by showing that RPA correlations can either increase or decrease mean free paths depending on the interaction, and that Gogny-type interactions (which avoid ferromagnetic instabilities) make neutron matter more transparent to neutrinos than the free Fermi gas model.
