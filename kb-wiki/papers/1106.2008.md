# Experimental equation of state in proton-proton and proton-antiproton collisions and phase transition to quark gluon plasma

> We deduce approximate equations of state from experimental measurements in proton-proton and proton-antiproton collisions. Thermodynamic quantities are estimated combining the measure of average transverse momentum <pt> vs pseudorapidity density dN/deta with the estimation of the interaction region size from measures of Bose Einstein correlation, or from a theoretical model which relates dN/deta to the impact parameter. The results are very similar to theory predictions in case of crossover from hadron gas to quark gluon plasma. According to our analysis, the possible crossover should start at dN/deta about 6 and end at dN/deta about 24.

## Key claim

The authors claim that combining experimental measurements of average transverse momentum versus pseudorapidity density with estimates of the interaction region size (from Bose-Einstein correlations or a model relating multiplicity to impact parameter) yields curves that reproduce the shape of theoretical equations of state for hadronic matter undergoing a crossover to quark-gluon plasma, with the crossover starting at dN/deta approximately 6 and ending at dN/deta approximately 24.

## Method

The authors combined experimental data on average transverse momentum <p_T> versus pseudorapidity density dN/deta from pp and p-antip collisions at center-of-mass energies from 31 GeV to 7000 GeV with estimates of the interaction area S. The area was obtained either from measurements of the invariant radius R_inv from Bose-Einstein correlations (using a combined polynomial and linear fit to R_inv versus dN/deta data from ALICE, CMS, UA1, STAR, and ISR experiments) or from a model relating impact parameter to multiplicity. From these, they computed the entropy density per unit area sigma_S = (dN/deta * 3/2)/S and constructed plots of sigma_S/<p_T>^3 versus <p_T> as an experimental approximation of sigma/T^3 versus T, and estimated the squared sound velocity c_s^2 = (sigma_S/<p_T>) * (d<p_T>/d sigma_S).

## Key numbers

* The slope change in <p_T> versus dN/deta occurs at dN/deta = 5.5 +/- 1.2, based on 21 curves from 22 to 7000 GeV (from prior work Campanini (2010)).
* The possible crossover from hadron gas to quark gluon plasma starts at dN/deta approximately 6 and ends at dN/deta approximately 24.
* The minimum value of the estimated squared sound velocity c_s^2 in different experimental curves varies from 0.08 to 0.18.
* The minimum of c_s^2 versus energy density epsilon_S occurs for epsilon_S values in the range 1.5-2.0 GeV/fm^2.
* At dN/deta = 7.5, the invariant radius R_inv value is 1.08 fm.
* The ratio between sigma/<p_T>^3 values corresponding to dN/deta >= 24 and those corresponding to dN/deta <= 6 varies from 2 to 3, depending on the area calculation method.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Van Hove (1982) that anomalous behavior of average transverse momentum as a function of multiplicity could signal a phase transition, and follows the observation by Campanini (2010) of a slope change at dN/deta = 5.5 +/- 1.2. The authors note that Alexopoulos (2002) assumed the system produced in p-antip at sqrt(s) = 1800 GeV for dN/deta > 6.75 was above the deconfinement transition. The results are compared to lattice QCD predictions for crossover from hadron gas to quark gluon plasma, and the authors claim this is the first attempt to obtain a complete equation of state for hadronic matter using experimental data only. The work enables future comparisons of measurements in the regions 2 < dN/deta < 6, dN/deta > 6, 6 < dN/deta < 24, and dN/deta > 24 to test whether the observed behaviors have physical meaning.
