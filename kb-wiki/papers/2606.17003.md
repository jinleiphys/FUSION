# Hadronic tensor in lattice gauge theories by quantum computing

> The hadronic tensor encodes crucial information regarding the internal structure of hadrons, reflecting the non-perturbative features of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). In this work, we directly compute the hadronic tensor within (1+1)-dimensional $\rm U(1)$ and $\rm SU(2)$ gauge theories by evaluating real-time current-current correlation functions. Utilizing quantum algorithms executed on classical hardware, we demonstrate that the hadron form factors for both meson and baryon states can be reliably extracted from the hadronic tensor. Our methodology is validated by strong agreement with both direct calculation and exact diagonalization of the form factors.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that hadron form factors for both meson and baryon states can be reliably extracted from the hadronic tensor computed via real-time current-current correlation functions in (1+1)-dimensional U(1) and SU(2) lattice gauge theories, but only in the low-Q² regime where the momentum transfer is of order the hadron mass squared; extension to the deep inelastic scattering regime is deferred to future fault-tolerant quantum computers.

## Method
The authors use a Hamiltonian lattice formulation with Wilson fermions, which preserves charge conjugation symmetry at finite lattice spacing, to simulate (1+1)-dimensional U(1) and SU(2) gauge theories. They prepare hadron states (vacuum, meson, and baryon) using a quantum-number-resolving variational quantum eigensolver with a quantum alternating operator ansatz, then evaluate the real-time current-current correlation functions via an ancilla-qubit-based quantum algorithm. The hadronic tensor is obtained by Fourier transforming the correlation functions, and form factors are extracted by integrating over the elastic peaks in the energy spectrum.

## Key numbers
- U(1) lattice gauge theory: bare parameters m = 0.25, g = 1, e_f = 1, resulting in meson mass m_M a_l = 1.04, simulated with N_q = 22 qubits.
- SU(2) lattice gauge theory: bare parameters m = 0.30, g = 1, e_f = 1, resulting in meson mass m_M a_l = 0.83 and baryon mass m_B a_l = 0.83 (degenerate due to charge conjugation symmetry), simulated with N_q = 20 qubits.
- Spatial momentum takes discrete values q¹ a_l = 2π k / N, where k = 0, 1, ..., N-1.

## Context
This work extends the quantum-computing approach to hadronic structure pioneered by Lamm et al. (2019), who computed PDFs and the hadronic tensor in the (1+1)-dimensional Thirring model, and complements the recent tensor-network study of the DIS hadronic tensor in the Schwinger model by Ikeda et al. (2025). Unlike those works, the authors focus on the low-Q² region to extract elastic form factors, using Wilson fermions instead of staggered fermions to preserve charge conjugation symmetry. The framework is benchmarked in both Abelian U(1) (Schwinger model) and non-Abelian SU(2) gauge theories, and the results agree with exact diagonalization, validating the approach for future application to (3+1)-dimensional QCD on fault-tolerant quantum computers.
