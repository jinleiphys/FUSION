# Single electrons from heavy-flavor mesons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the single electron spectra from $D-$ and $B-$meson semileptonic decays in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=$200, 62.4, and 19.2 GeV by employing the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) transport approach that has been shown to reasonably describe the charm dynamics at RHIC and LHC energies on a microscopic level. In this approach the initial heavy quarks are produced by using the PYTHIA which is tuned to reproduce the FONLL calculations. The produced heavy quarks interact with off-shell massive partons in QGP with scattering cross sections which are calculated in the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM). At energy densities close to the critical energy density the heavy quarks are hadronized into heavy mesons through either coalescence or fragmentation. After hadronization the heavy mesons interact with the light hadrons by employing the scattering cross sections from an effective Lagrangian. The final heavy mesons then produce single electrons through semileptonic decay. We find that the PHSD approach well describes the nuclear modification factor $R_{\rm AA}$ and elliptic flow $v_2$ of single electrons in d+Au and Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=$ 200 GeV and the elliptic flow in Au+Au reactions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=$ 62.4 GeV from the PHENIX collaboration, however, the large $R_{\rm AA}$ at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=$ 62.4 GeV is not described at all. Furthermore, we make predictions for the $R_{\rm AA}$ of $D-$mesons and of single electrons at the lower energy of $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=$ 19.2 GeV. Additionally, the medium modification of the azimuthal angle $ϕ$ between a heavy quark and a heavy antiquark is studied. We find that the transverse flow enhances the azimuthal angular distributions close to $ϕ=$ 0 because the heavy flavors strongly interact with nuclear medium in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and almost flow with the bulk matter.

## Key claim

The PHSD transport approach, extended to include bottom production, describes the nuclear modification factor $R_{\rm AA}$ and elliptic flow $v_2$ of single electrons in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV and the elliptic flow at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=62.4$ GeV, but fails to describe the large $R_{\rm AA}$ at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=62.4$ GeV.

## Method

The authors employed the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, where initial charm and bottom quarks were produced using the PYTHIA event generator tuned to reproduce FONLL calculations. Heavy quarks interacted with off-shell massive partons in the quark-gluon plasma via scattering cross sections calculated in the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM), and were hadronized into $D-$ and $B-$mesons near the critical energy density through either coalescence or fragmentation. The heavy mesons then interacted with light hadrons using scattering cross sections from an effective Lagrangian before producing single electrons through semileptonic decay.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections for charm production in p+p collisions: 0.8 mb at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV and 0.12 mb at $\sqrt{s}=62.4$ GeV, from STAR measurement and PHSD interpolation
- Ratio of bottom cross section to charm cross section: 0.75% at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV and 0.145% at $\sqrt{s}=62.4$ GeV from FONLL calculations
- Total cross sections for charm and bottom production decrease by 8% and increase by 21% respectively in 0-10% central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV due to shadowing effects
- Total cross sections for charm and bottom production increase by 18% and 21% respectively in 0-20% central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=62.4$ GeV due to shadowing effects
- Contribution from $B-$meson decay becomes compatible to $D-$meson decay around $p_T \approx 4.5$ GeV at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV and around $p_T \approx 5.5$ GeV at $\sqrt{s}=62.4$ GeV
- Charm quark mass taken as 1.5 GeV and bottom quark mass as 4.8 GeV in the calculations
- Fragmentation parameter $\epsilon_Q$ = 0.01 for charm and 0.004 for bottom

## Context

This work extends the PHSD approach previously applied to charm production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV (Song 2015) and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV (Song 2015) by incorporating bottom production and comparing with single electron data from the PHENIX collaboration. The study enables predictions for $D-$meson and single electron production at the lower energy of $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=19.2$ GeV relevant for the RHIC beam energy scan program, and investigates medium modification of the azimuthal angle between heavy quark pairs.
