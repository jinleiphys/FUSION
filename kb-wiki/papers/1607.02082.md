# Testing the running coupling $k_{T}$-factorization formula for the inclusive gluon production

> The inclusive gluon production at midrapidities is described in the Color Glass Condensate formalism using the $k_T$ - factorization formula, which was derived at fixed coupling constant considering the scattering of a dilute system of partons with a dense one. Recent analysis demonstrated that this approach provides a satisfactory description of the experimental data for the inclusive hadron production in $pp/pA/AA$ collisions. However, these studies are based on the fixed coupling $k_T$ - factorization formula, which does not take into account the running coupling corrections, which are important to set the scales present in the cross section. In this paper we consider the running coupling corrected $k_T$ - factorization formula conjectured some years ago and investigate the impact of the running coupling corrections on the observables. In particular, the pseudorapidity distributions and charged hadrons multiplicity are calculated considering $pp$, $dAu/pPb$ and $AuAu/PbPb$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. We compare the corrected running coupling predictions with those obtained using the original $k_T$ - factorization assuming a fixed coupling or a prescription for the inclusion of the running of the coupling. Considering the Kharzeev - Levin - Nardi unintegrated gluon distribution and a simplified model for the nuclear geometry, we demonstrate that the distinct predictions are similar for the pseudorapidity distributions in $pp/pA/AA$ collisions and for the charged hadrons multiplicity in $pp/pA$ collisions. On the other hand, the running coupling corrected $k_T$ - factorization formula predicts a smoother energy dependence for $dN/dη$ in $AA$ collisions.

## Key claim

The running coupling corrected \(k_T\)-factorization formula, conjectured by Kovchegov and Horowitz, yields predictions for pseudorapidity distributions and charged hadron multiplicities in \(pp/pA/AA\) collisions that are similar to those from the original fixed-coupling formula and from two ad-hoc running-coupling prescriptions, except that the corrected formula predicts a smoother energy dependence for \(dN/d\eta\) in \(AA\) collisions.

## Method

The authors computed inclusive gluon production at midrapidity using the Color Glass Condensate formalism with the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (KLN) model for the unintegrated gluon distribution, a simplified nuclear geometry (minimum bias with \(A_{eff}=20\) for Pb and \(18.5\) for Au), and the principle of Local Parton-Hadron Duality with an effective mass \(m_h=0.350\) GeV. They compared four treatments of the coupling constant: fixed coupling (\(\alpha_s=0.25\)), two ad-hoc running-coupling prescriptions (RC1 from Albacete et al. and RC2 from Amir et al.), and the running-coupling corrected \(k_T\)-factorization formula (CF) from Kovchegov and Horowitz, normalizing each to data at \(\eta=0\) for each collision system.

## Key numbers

* Pseudorapidity distributions in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=0.9\), 2.36, and 7 TeV: the CF formula required a change in normalization between 0.9 and 7 TeV smaller than 1.0%, while the other predictions required changes larger than 19%.
* Pseudorapidity distributions in \(dAu\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=0.2\) TeV and \(pPb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV: the CF formula required a normalization change of approximately 17% between the two energies, while the other predictions required changes of order 40%.
* Pseudorapidity distributions in \(AuAu\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=0.2\) TeV and \(PbPb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV: the CF formula required a normalization change of approximately 25% between the two energies.
* Energy dependence of charged hadron multiplicities at \(\eta=0\): the CF formula predicted a slower growth with energy compared to the original \(k_T\)-factorization formula, and described \(pp\) data at \(\sqrt{s}<0.9\) TeV better than the other approaches using the same inputs.

## Context

This work extends the KLN approach (Kharzeev, Levin, Nardi, 2001-2005) and subsequent phenomenological studies by Amir et al. (2011), Albacete et al. (2010), and others, which used ad-hoc running-coupling prescriptions in the fixed-coupling \(k_T\)-factorization formula. The paper tests the running-coupling corrected formula conjectured by Kovchegov and Horowitz (2010) based on BLM scale-setting, finding that the corrections have a small impact on pseudorapidity distributions but a noticeable effect on the energy dependence in heavy-ion collisions. This motivates future calculations with more realistic unintegrated gluon distributions and nuclear geometry treatments.
