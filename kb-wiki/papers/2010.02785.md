# Many-body forces and nucleon clustering near the QCD critical point

> It has been proposed that one can look for the QCD critical point (CP) by the Beam Energy Scan (BES) accurately monitoring event-by-event fluctuations. This experimental program is under way at the BNL RHIC collider. Separately, it has been studied how clustering of nucleons at freezeout affects proton multiplicity distribution and light nuclei production. It was found that even a minor increase of the range of nuclear forces dramatically increases clustering, while large correlation length $ξ$ near CP makes attraction due to binary forces unrealistically large. In this paper we show that repulsive many-body forces near CP should overcome the binary ones and effectively suppress clustering. We also discuss current experimental data and point out locations at which a certain drop in clustering may already be observed.

## Key claim

The authors show that repulsive many-body forces induced by critical fluctuations near the hypothetical QCD critical point should overcome attractive binary forces and suppress nucleon preclustering, predicting a non-monotonous signal in clustering as a function of beam energy, rather than the monotonic enhancement that binary-only forces would produce.

## Method

The authors use path-integral Monte Carlo (PIMC) simulations of four-nucleon clusters at freezeout conditions from their previous work (DeMartini 2020), then perturbatively average multibody interaction diagrams (three-body and four-body) over the 9-dimensional cluster configurations. They evaluate the dependence of these diagrams on the correlation length ξ relative to cluster size, using coupling constants estimated from the Walecka model and the universal effective potential for Ising-type critical fluctuations parameterized following Tsypin (1994).

## Key numbers

* The minimal sound wavelength at BES energies is λ_min ≈ 6 fm, corresponding to maximal harmonic number n_max = 6 (STAR).
* The maximal correlation length expected to be observable via preclustering is ξ_max ∼ 1.5–2 fm.
* The 9-dimensional correlation volume for four-nucleon clusters at √s = 7.7 GeV freezeout is V_cor^(9)(7.7) ≈ 4.3 × 10^4 fm^9.
* The ambient nucleon density at √s = 7.7 GeV freezeout is n_B(7.7) ≈ 0.037 /fm^3, giving ambient inter-nucleon separation R_amb ≈ 3.0 fm.
* The cluster density is n_cl ≈ 0.114 /fm^3, giving cluster size R_cl ≈ 2.0 fm.
* The ratio of average many-body interactions between cluster and ambient matter for ξ = 2 fm: ⟨V_b⟩_cl/⟨V_b⟩_amb = 4.00, ⟨V_c⟩_cl/⟨V_c⟩_amb = 10.73, ⟨V_d⟩_cl/⟨V_d⟩_amb = 6.52.
* For ambient matter at ξ_max ∼ 2 fm, the binary term scales as (ξ_max/R_amb)^2 ≈ 0.444 while the many-body repulsion scales as (ξ_max/R_amb)^7 ≈ 0.058.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of nucleon preclustering (Shuryak 2018, Shuryak 2019, DeMartini 2020) which used only binary nuclear forces and found that adding critical fluctuations via binary potentials alone produced unrealistically large clustering. The paper resolves this by including repulsive many-body forces from the nonlinear effective Lagrangian of the critical mode, which grow faster with correlation length than binary forces. This enables predictions of a non-monotonous signal in proton multiplicity kurtosis and light nuclei yields across the Beam Energy Scan, with suppression of clustering near the critical point.
