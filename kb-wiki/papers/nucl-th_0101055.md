# Theory of the Nucleon Spin-Polarizabilities II

> Following up on the plenary talk given by Ulf Meißner, I discuss a generalization of the concept of nucleon spin-polarizabilities in terms of multipole excitation/de-excitation and present an overview of the available theoretical predictions for these elusive but interesting structure parameters of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the concept of nucleon spin-polarizabilities by expressing the four Ragusa spin-polarizabilities in a multipole basis that clarifies their physical meaning as dipole excitation/de-excitation responses, and extends this framework to include quadrupole and octupole contributions; however, none of these dipole spin-polarizabilities has been measured directly yet, with results from future polarized Compton scattering experiments on the proton still awaited.

## Method

The author reinterprets the four Ragusa spin-polarizabilities \(\gamma_i\) as linear combinations \(\gamma_{E1}, \gamma_{M1}, \gamma_{E2}, \gamma_{M2}\) corresponding to electric or magnetic dipole excitation followed by dipole or quadrupole de-excitation in Compton scattering. This multipole analysis is then extended to \(l=2\) in the multipole expansion of the six Compton structure amplitudes \(R_i(\omega,\theta)\), yielding four dispersion spin-polarizabilities (slopes of the dipole polarizabilities) and four proper higher-order spin-polarizabilities associated with quadrupole excitations and quadrupole or octupole de-excitations. Theoretical predictions are compared from heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to \({\cal O}(p^4)\), the small scale expansion (SSE) to \({\cal O}(\epsilon^3)\), and dispersion analyses.

## Key numbers

- Isoscalar dipole spin-polarizabilities from HBChPT (GHM): \(\gamma_{E1}^{(s)} = -3.0 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^4\), \(\gamma_{M1}^{(s)} = +0.4 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^4\) (with \(+\,2.5\times10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^4\) from \(\Delta(1232)\) pole still missing), \(\gamma_{E2}^{(s)} = +2.0 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^4\), \(\gamma_{M2}^{(s)} = +0.6 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^4\)
- Isoscalar dipole spin-polarizabilities from dispersion analysis (BGLMN): \(\gamma_{E1}^{(s)} = -4.5 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^4\), \(\gamma_{M1}^{(s)} = +3.3 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^4\), \(\gamma_{E2}^{(s)} = +2.4 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^4\), \(\gamma_{M2}^{(s)} = -0.2 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^4\)
- Isoscalar dispersion spin-polarizability \(\gamma_{E1,\nu}^{(s)}\) from \({\cal O}(p^4)\) ChPT: \(-3.40 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^6\); from dispersion theory: \(-3.42 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^6\)
- Isoscalar higher-order spin-polarizability \(\gamma_{ET}^{(s)}\) from \({\cal O}(p^4)\) ChPT: \(+0.08 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^6\); from SSE: \(-0.28 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^6\)
- Isoscalar higher-order spin-polarizability \(\gamma_{MT}^{(s)}\) from \({\cal O}(p^4)\) ChPT: \(+0.06 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^6\); from SSE: \(-0.03 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^6\)

## Context

This work extends the multipole interpretation of nucleon spin-polarizabilities introduced by Ragusa (Ragusa) and builds on the plenary talk by Meissner (Meissner) discussing the six Compton structure functions. The generalization to quadrupole and octupole excitations follows the analysis of Holstein et al. (HDPV) and provides a framework for future polarized Compton scattering experiments on the proton. The results enable comparison between chiral effective field theory predictions and dispersion analyses, with the finding that isoscalar components dominate over isovector components for both dipole and higher-order spin-polarizabilities, reinforcing the picture of the nucleon as a rigid object under deformation forces.
