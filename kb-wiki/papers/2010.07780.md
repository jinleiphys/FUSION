# Hydrogen molecule spectrum by many-body GW and Bethe-Salpeter equation

> We check the ab initio GW approximation and Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) many-body methodology against the exact solution benchmark of the hydrogen molecule H$_2$ ground state and excitation spectrum, and in comparison with the configuration interaction (CI) and time-dependent Hartree-Fock methods. The comparison is made on all the states we could unambiguously identify from the excitonic wave functions' symmetry. At the equilibrium distance $R = 1.4 \, a_0$, the GW+BSE energy levels are in good agreement with the exact results, with an accuracy of 0.1~0.2 eV. GW+BSE potential-energy curves are also in good agreement with the CI and the exact result up to $2.3 \, a_0$. The solution no longer exists beyond $3.0 \, a_0$ for triplets ($4.3 \, a_0$ for singlets) due to instability of the ground state. We tried to improve the GW reference ground state by a renormalized random-phase approximation (r-RPA), but this did not solve the problem.

## Key claim

At the equilibrium distance of 1.4 Bohr, the GW+BSE approach achieves an accuracy of 0.1–0.2 eV on the hydrogen molecule excitation spectrum, which is four times better than time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF), but the method fails beyond 3.0 Bohr for triplets and 4.3 Bohr for singlets due to ground-state instability, and this instability is not resolved by switching to a renormalized random-phase approximation (r-RPA) reference ground state.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations using a d-aug-cc-pV5Z Gaussian basis set (210 basis elements total), then applied the GW self-energy with contour deformation and eigenvalue self-consistency (evGW), followed by a Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) calculation beyond the Tamm-Dancoff approximation to obtain excitation energies and excitonic wave functions. Ground-state energies were computed using the trace formula. The results were compared against full configuration interaction (CI), TDHF (also called HF+RPAx), and exact numerical solutions from the literature for the clamped-nuclei nonrelativistic H₂ Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- At equilibrium distance R = 1.4 a₀, GW+BSE energy levels agree with exact results to within 0.1–0.2 eV
- GW+BSE potential-energy curves agree with CI and exact results up to R = 2.3 a₀
- GW+BSE solution no longer exists beyond R = 3.0 a₀ for triplets and beyond R = 4.3 a₀ for singlets
- The GW ionization potential (IP) is 16.62 eV (evGW) versus the exact value of 16.45 eV, an error of 0.17 eV; HF gives 16.19 eV (error 0.26 eV)
- Full CI achieves an accuracy of 2 × 10⁻⁴ Ha for states up to h, degrading to 5 × 10⁻³ Ha for higher states
- The exact ground-state energy of H₂ at R = 1.4 a₀ is -1.17447571 Ha (Pachucki 2010)
- The exact ground-state energy of H₂⁺ at R = 1.4 a₀ is -0.5699 Ha (Olivares-Pilon and Turbiner 2018)

## Context

This work benchmarks the GW+BSE many-body methodology against the exact numerical solution of the hydrogen molecule, which is known to an accuracy of 10⁻¹⁵ Ha (Pachucki 2010; Pachucki and Komasa 2016). The results extend earlier qualitative comparisons by Rebolini and Savin (2013) using minimal basis sets, and contrast with the approach of Olsen and Thygesen (2014) who used the adiabatic-connection fluctuation-dissipation theorem (ACFDT) to avoid the triplet instability problem at dissociation. The study enables systematic assessment of GW+BSE for molecular systems and identifies the triplet instability as a key limitation that future work (e.g., fully self-consistent RPA or improved reference states) must address.
