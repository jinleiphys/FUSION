# Electromagnetic meson form factor from a relativistic coupled-channel approach

> Point-form relativistic quantum mechanics is used to derive an expression for the electromagnetic form factor of a pseudoscalar meson for space-like momentum transfers. The elastic scattering of an electron by a confined quark-antiquark pair is treated as a relativistic two-channel problem for the $q\bar{q}e$ and $q\bar{q}eγ$ states. With the approximation that the total velocity of the $q\bar{q}e$ system is conserved at (electromagnetic) interaction vertices this simplifies to an eigenvalue problem for a Bakamjian-Thomas type mass operator. After elimination of the $q\bar{q}eγ$ channel the electromagnetic meson current and form factor can be directly read off from the one-photon-exchange optical potential. By choosing the invariant mass of the electron-meson system large enough, cluster separability violations become negligible. An equivalence with the usual front-form expression, resulting from a spectator current in the $q^+=0$ reference frame, is established. The generalization of this multichannel approach to electroweak form factors for an arbitrary bound few-body system is quite obvious. By an appropriate extension of the Hilbert space this approach is also able to accommodate exchange-current effects.

## Key claim

The paper derives an expression for the electromagnetic form factor of a pseudoscalar meson using point-form relativistic quantum mechanics, but the derivation relies on the approximation that the total velocity of the electron-meson system is conserved at electromagnetic interaction vertices, which introduces cluster separability violations that become negligible only when the invariant mass of the electron-meson system is chosen large enough.

## Method

The elastic scattering of an electron by a confined quark-antiquark pair is treated as a relativistic two-channel problem for the qq̅e and qq̅eγ states within a Bakamjian-Thomas type mass operator in a velocity-state representation. After eliminating the qq̅eγ channel, the electromagnetic meson current and form factor are read off from the one-photon-exchange optical potential. The calculation is performed both on the hadronic level (with a phenomenological vertex form factor) and on the constituent level (with an instantaneous confining potential between quark and antiquark), and the form factor is identified by comparing appropriate matrix elements of the optical potentials.

## Key numbers

- The on-shell matrix elements of the optical potential yield the relation Δm = Δm′ = ω_{k_γ′′} = |k_M′ − k_M| = √(−q_μ q^μ) = Q, where Q is the magnitude of the 4-momentum transfer between incoming and outgoing meson.
- The electromagnetic meson current is expressed as J_ν^{phen}(k_M′; k_M) = Q_M (k_M′ + k_M)_ν f(Q), where f(Q) is the phenomenological form factor and Q_M is the meson charge.
- The photon propagator appears as −g^{μν}/Q^2 in the one-photon-exchange amplitude, with Q^2 being the squared 4-momentum transfer.

## Context

This work extends the formal construction of Poincaré-covariant current operators for bound few-body systems proposed by Lev (1995) by providing an explicit application to meson form factors within constituent quark models, rather than staying at the formal level. It differs from the point-form spectator model of Wagenbrunn (2000) and Boffi (2001) by starting from a Poincaré-invariant treatment of electron-hadron scattering that explicitly includes the dynamics of the exchanged photon. The approach enables future generalization to electroweak form factors for arbitrary bound few-body systems and can accommodate exchange-current effects by appropriate extension of the Hilbert space.
