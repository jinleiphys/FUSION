# Centrality dependence of Electrical and Hall conductivity at RHIC and LHC energies for a Conformal System

> In this work, we study electrical conductivity and Hall conductivity in the presence of electromagnetic field using Relativistic Boltzmann Transport Equation with Relaxation Time Approximation. We evaluate these transport coefficients for a strongly interacting system consisting of nearly massless particles which is similar to Quark-Gluon Plasma and is likely to be formed in heavy-ion collision experiments. We explicitly include the effects of magnetic field in the calculation of relaxation time. The values of magnetic field are obtained for all the centrality classes of Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt {s_{\rm NN}} =$ 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt {s_{\rm NN}} =$ 2.76 TeV. We consider the three lightest quark flavors and their corresponding antiparticles in this study. We estimate the temperature dependence of the electrical conductivity and Hall conductivity for different strengths of magnetic field. We observe a significant dependence of temperature on electrical and Hall conductivity in the presence of magnetic field.

## Key claim

The authors calculate electrical and Hall conductivities for a conformal, nearly massless quark-gluon plasma by solving the Relativistic Boltzmann Transport Equation with a relaxation time that explicitly depends on magnetic field, but they note that this work ignores QCD dynamics and treats the system as a free gas, making it an "introductory level study" rather than a full QCD calculation.

## Method

The authors solve the Relativistic Boltzmann Transport Equation in the relaxation time approximation, incorporating a magnetic-field-dependent relaxation time and a magnetic-field-dependent coupling constant. They consider three light quark flavors (u, d, s) and their antiparticles, using centrality-dependent magnetic field values obtained from the optical Glauber model for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV. The electrical and Hall conductivities are computed from the resulting distribution function in the static limit with perpendicular electric and magnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field values for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV range from eB=0.045 GeV^2 (0-10% centrality) to eB=0.103 GeV^2 (60-70% centrality)
- Magnetic field values for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV range from eB=0.59 GeV^2 (0-10% centrality) to eB=1.33 GeV^2 (60-70% centrality)
- Baryon chemical potential used: mu_B=25 MeV for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV; mu_B=2 MeV for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV
- Quark masses used: m_u=3 MeV, m_d=5 MeV, m_s=100 MeV
- Temperature range studied: 0.15 to 0.6 GeV
- Hall conductivity values for Pb+Pb collisions are approximately an order of magnitude smaller than those for Au+Au collisions
- The ratio sigma_el/sigma_H in Au+Au collisions reaches several hundred at higher temperatures

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Hall conductivity in heavy-ion collisions by Feng et al. (2017) and Das et al. (2019) by explicitly incorporating magnetic field effects into the relaxation time calculation and using a magnetic-field-dependent coupling constant rather than a temperature-dependent one. The authors provide centrality-dependent results for both RHIC and LHC energies, making the study more phenomenologically relevant than prior work. The results differ significantly from lattice QCD estimates by Gupta (2003) but are close to values obtained in the PHSD framework by Cassing (2013). This work enables future studies that could incorporate QCD dynamics and explore scenarios at proposed facilities like FAIR or NICA where large baryon chemical potential may coexist with strong magnetic fields.
