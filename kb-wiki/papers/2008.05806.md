# Sum rule for the Compton amplitude and implications for the proton-neutron mass difference

> The Cottingham formula expresses the leading contribution of the electromagnetic interaction to the proton-neutron mass difference as an integral over the forward Compton amplitude. Since quarks and gluons reggeize, the dispersive representation of this amplitude requires a subtraction. We assume that the asymptotic behaviour is dominated by Reggeon exchange. This leads to a sum rule that expresses the subtraction function in terms of measurable quantities. The evaluation of this sum rule leads to $m_{QED}^{p-n}=0.58\pm 0.16\,\mbox{MeV}$.

## Key claim

The authors assume that the asymptotic behavior of the forward Compton amplitude is dominated by Reggeon exchange (Reggeon dominance), which leads to a sum rule that fully determines the subtraction function in the Cottingham formula from measurable structure functions. The resulting electromagnetic contribution to the proton-neutron mass difference is \(m_{QED}^{p-n}=0.58\pm 0.16\,\text{MeV}\), with the uncertainty reflecting both experimental errors and theoretical ambiguities from the renormalization scale.

## Method

The authors decompose the spin-averaged forward Compton amplitude into two invariant amplitudes \(T_1\) and \(T_2\) (or equivalently \(\bar{T}=T_1+\frac12 T_2\) and \(T_2\)), which are free of kinematic zeros. They write dispersion relations for these amplitudes, where \(\bar{T}\) requires a subtraction while \(T_2\) obeys an unsubtracted relation. Under the assumption of Reggeon dominance, they derive a sum rule that expresses the subtraction function \(\bar{S}(-Q^2)\) as an integral over the structure function \(\bar{F}(x,Q^2)\). The Cottingham formula is then evaluated by Wick rotation, separating the mass shift into elastic, structure-function, and subtraction-function contributions, with the latter regularized by a cutoff and renormalized by absorbing divergences into the electromagnetic renormalization of quark masses and the QCD coupling constant.

## Key numbers

* The electromagnetic contribution to the proton-neutron mass difference is \(m_{QED}^{p-n}=0.58\pm 0.16\,\text{MeV}\).
* An earlier evaluation by Gasser and Leutwyler (1975) using Reggeon dominance and models for missing phase space gave \(m_{QED}=0.7\pm 0.3\,\text{MeV}\).
* The normalization constant for the structure-function integrals is \(N=3\alpha_{\text{em}}/(8\pi m)\), where \(\alpha_{\text{em}}\) is the fine-structure constant and \(m\) is the nucleon mass.
* The function \(f(y)\) that suppresses large-\(Q^2\) contributions in the structure-function integrals behaves as \(f(y)=1/(16y^2)+O(y^{-3})\) for large \(y=Q^2/(4m^2x^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the original Cottingham formula approach (Cottingham, 1963) and the Reggeon-dominance sum rule proposed by Elitzur and Harari (1970). It resolves inconsistencies claimed by Walker-Loud, Carlson and Miller (2012), who replaced the sum rule with a model parametrization of the subtraction function. The result enables a data-driven determination of the electromagnetic part of the proton-neutron mass difference that can be compared with lattice QCD calculations, which have recently yielded higher values for \(m_{QED}\).
