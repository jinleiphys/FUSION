# Correlations far from equilibrium in charged strongly coupled fluids subjected to a strong magnetic field

> Within a holographic model, we calculate the time evolution of 2-point and 1-point correlation functions (of selected operators) within a charged strongly coupled system of many particles. That system is thermalizing from an anisotropic initial charged state far from equilibrium towards equilibrium while subjected to a constant external magnetic field. One main result is that thermalization times for 2-point functions are significantly (approximately three times) larger than those of 1-point functions. Magnetic field and charge amplify this difference, generally increasing thermalization times. However, there is also a competition of scales between charge density, magnetic field, and initial anisotropy, which leads to an array of qualitative changes on the 2- and 1-point functions. There appears to be a strong effect of the medium on 2-point functions at early times, but approximately none at later times. At strong magnetic fields, an apparently universal thermalization time emerges, at which all 2-point functions appear to thermalize regardless of any other scale in the system. Hence, this time scale is referred to as saturation time scale. As extremality is approached in the purely charged case, 2- and 1-point functions appear to equilibrate at infinitely late time. We also compute 2-point functions of charged operators. Our results can be taken to model thermalization in heavy ion collisions, or thermalization in selected condensed matter systems.

## Key claim

Thermalization times for 2-point correlation functions are approximately three times larger than those for 1-point functions in a charged strongly coupled fluid thermalizing from an anisotropic initial state under a constant external magnetic field, with magnetic field and charge amplifying this difference; however, at strong magnetic fields a universal saturation time scale emerges at which all 2-point functions thermalize regardless of length separation, and as extremality is approached in the purely charged case both 2- and 1-point functions appear to equilibrate at infinitely late time.

## Method

The authors use holographic duality (AdS/CFT correspondence) to model a strongly coupled N=4 Super-Yang-Mills fluid thermalizing from an anisotropic initial state. They solve the Einstein-Maxwell equations in a Vaidya-type spacetime using the characteristic formulation of general relativity with spectral methods, evolving the system with a 4th order Adams-Bashforth routine. The 2-point correlation functions of scalar operators with large operator dimension are computed via the geodesic approximation, while 1-point functions are obtained from the asymptotic coefficients of the metric near the AdS boundary.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time for 2-point functions is approximately 3 times that of 1-point functions (t_2pt approximately 3 t_1pt)
- At strong magnetic fields (B=3), the saturation time scale for all 2-point functions is t_saturation approximately 1.45 (in units of fixed energy density)
- For the neutral case with zero magnetic field, the 1-point function thermalization time is t_th = 0.687
- At magnetic field B=1 with charge density rho=0.85 and energy density epsilon_B=3/4, the dimensionless ratios are B^2/epsilon_B = 1.33 and |rho|^(4/3)/epsilon_B = 1.07
- At magnetic field B=3 with charge density rho=0.85 and energy density epsilon_B=3.22, the dimensionless ratios are B^2/epsilon_B = 2.79 and |rho|^(4/3)/epsilon_B = 0.25
- The charge density upper bound for the Reissner-Nordstrom black brane is rho_e = sqrt(2)/3^(1/4) with the chosen energy density
- The charge density used in the charged cases is rho = 0.7896 rho_e (approximately 0.78 rho_e)
- The initial anisotropy function uses parameters w=0.5, r_0=4, and amplitude beta=1.5

## Context

This work extends the study of thermalization in anisotropic strongly coupled fluids by Fuini and Yaffe (2015), who introduced external magnetic fields and charge density but found surprisingly little change in 1-point functions. The authors fill a gap by computing 2-point correlation functions (non-local probes) in addition to 1-point functions, building on earlier holographic thermalization studies using the geodesic approximation by Aparicio, Abajo-Arrastia and Lopez (2011) and Balasubramanian et al. (2011). The results enable modeling of thermalization in heavy ion collisions with magnetic field effects, as well as thermalization in selected condensed matter systems, and reveal a competition of scales between magnetic field, charge density, and initial anisotropy that produces qualitative changes in correlation functions.
