# Chiral soliton lattice effect on baryonic matter from skyrmion crystal model

> The chiral soliton lattice (CSL) has been studied in condensed-matter system such as chiral magnets, which arises as a parity-violating topological soliton. In hadron physics, various attempts have also been made to apply the idea of CSL to baryonic matter. In this work, we explore the CSL effects on the baryonic matter based on the skrymion crystal approach. It is found that the CSL causes an inverse catalysis for the topology change in the dense baryonic matter. Furthermore, we observe that the CSL makes the single-baryon shape deformed to be highly oscillating as the frequency of the CSL gets larger, which leads to the enhancement of a baryon energy. Of interest is that in a high density region, the CSL goes away due to the topology change in the baryonic matter. What we find here might deepen our understanding of dense matter systems as well as compact stars.

## Key claim

The chiral soliton lattice (CSL) causes an inverse catalysis for the topology change in dense baryonic matter, meaning it shifts the critical density for the skyrmion-to-half-skyrmion transition to lower values; however, this effect is only valid when the parameter \(a\) (encoding the CSL remnant) is constrained to the range \(-1 \leq a \leq 0\) to avoid tachyonic instabilities in the dynamical pion field.

## Method

The authors embed a one-dimensional chiral soliton lattice (CSL) configuration into a face-centered cubic (FCC) skyrmion crystal by decomposing the chiral field \(U = \breve u \bar U \breve u\) with \(\breve u = \exp[i\pi_3(z)\tau_3/2]\). They then take the space average of the medium-modified factors from the skyrmion matter to obtain a mean-field Lagrangian for the fluctuating pion field, whose equation of motion yields the CSL solution expressed in Jacobi elliptic functions. The per-baryon energy and the order parameter \(\langle \phi_0 \rangle\) (which signals the topology change) are computed by minimizing the total energy as a function of crystal size \(L\) and the CSL parameter \(a\).

## Key numbers

- The critical crystal size for the topology change at \(a=0\) is \(L \simeq 1.3\ \text{fm}\), corresponding to a critical density \(n_{1/2} \simeq 1.3 n_0\) with normal nuclear matter density \(n_0 \simeq 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- For \(a=0.9\), the topology change point shifts to a lower density \(n_{1/2} \simeq 0.55 n_0\).
- The critical value of the CSL parameter for vanishing \(\langle \phi_0 \rangle\) is found to be \(a_c = 1\).
- Input model parameters are \(f_\pi = 92.4\ \text{MeV}\) and \(g = 5.93\).
- The per-baryon energy increases with \(a\) for \(a < 1\) at fixed crystal size, but becomes nearly independent of \(a\) for \(a \geq 1\).

## Context

This work extends the skyrmion crystal approach to dense baryonic matter, previously developed by Lee (2003) and others, by incorporating the chiral soliton lattice structure studied in condensed-matter systems such as chiral magnets (Dzyaloshinsky, 1964; Togawa, 2012). The CSL effect on baryonic matter had not been fully examined in hadron physics prior to this study. The findings may deepen understanding of dense matter systems and compact stars, and the observed inverse catalysis of the topology change provides a new connection between QCD effective models and condensed-matter physics.
