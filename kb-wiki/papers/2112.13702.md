# Light pseudoscalar meson and heavy meson scattering lengths to $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$ in heavy meson chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate the threshold $T$ matrices of the light pseudoscalar meson and heavy meson scattering to fourth order in heavy meson chiral perturbation theory. We determine the low-energy constants by fitting to the lattice QCD data points through both the perturbative and iterated methods and obtain the physical scattering lengths in both formalisms. The values of the scattering lengths tend to be convergent at fourth order for most of the channels in the perturbative method. The value of the scattering length for the channel $DK (I=0)$, which involves the bound state $D_{s0}^{*}(2317)$, is obtained correctly in the iterated method. Based on the heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry, we also estimate the meson and doubly charmed (bottom) baryon scattering lengths, and find that the bound states can be generated with high probability in the channels $\bar{K}Ξ_{cc}(I=0)$ and $\bar{K}Ξ_{bb}(I=0)$. We strongly urge the LHCb Collaboration to look for the very narrow $\bar{K}Ξ_{cc}$ state with $IJ^P=0{1\over 2}^-$ through either the electromagnetic decay or the iso-spin violating strong decay $Ω_{cc} π$.

## Key claim

The authors calculate light pseudoscalar meson and heavy meson scattering lengths to fourth order in heavy meson chiral perturbation theory (HMChPT), finding that perturbative results tend to converge for most channels but that the iterated method is necessary to correctly obtain the negative scattering length for the DK(I=0) channel, which corresponds to the bound state D_{s0}*(2317).

## Method

The authors computed threshold T matrices for light pseudoscalar meson (π, K, η) and heavy meson (D, D_s) scattering up to O(p^4) in heavy meson chiral perturbation theory, including tree diagrams, one-loop diagrams, and counterterms from the chiral Lagrangian up to fourth order. They determined the low-energy constants (LECs) c_2, c_3, c_4, c_5, κ̄, ē_1, and ē_2 by fitting to lattice QCD data from Liu et al. (2013) using both a perturbative scattering length formula and an iterated (Lippmann-Schwinger) formula with a cutoff scale μ. Using heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry, they then estimated scattering lengths for meson-doubly charmed (bottom) baryon systems.

## Key numbers

- For the DK(I=0) channel, the perturbative total scattering length from Fit p1 is 1.98(93) fm, from Fit p2 is 2.20(52) fm, and from Fit p3 is 1.44(30) fm; none of these perturbative values become negative, unlike the lattice estimate of -1.05 fm from Liu et al. (2013)
- For the Dπ(I=3/2) channel, the perturbative total scattering length is -0.09(3) fm (Fit p1), -0.09(2) fm (Fit p2), and -0.07(2) fm (Fit p3)
- For the Dπ(I=1/2) channel, the perturbative total scattering length is 0.27(2) fm (Fit p1), 0.27(2) fm (Fit p2), and 0.28(1) fm (Fit p3)
- The LECs from Fit p1 include c_2 = 0.53 ± 3.12 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -2.82 ± 3.59 GeV^{-1}, κ̄ = 0.87 ± 0.51 GeV^{-2}, with χ²/d.o.f. = 1.14
- The LECs from Fit p2 include c_2 = 0.96 ± 2.02 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -3.46 ± 1.84 GeV^{-1}, κ̄ = 0.98 ± 0.29 GeV^{-2}, with χ²/d.o.f. = 1.30
- The LECs from Fit p3 include c_2 = -1.05 ± 1.15 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -0.34 ± 0.83 GeV^{-1}, κ̄ = 0.54 ± 0.17 GeV^{-2}, with χ²/d.o.f. = 1.67
- Physical parameters used for predictions: m_π = 139.57 MeV, m_K = 493.68 MeV, f_π = 92.07 MeV, f_K = 110.03 MeV, M_D = 1869.66 MeV, M_Ds = 1968.35 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous O(p^3) HMChPT calculation (Liu et al., 2009) to O(p^4), addressing the need for higher-order convergence in scattering length predictions. The study uses lattice QCD data from Liu et al. (2013) and Mohler et al. (2013) to determine LECs, and the iterated method follows approaches used by Guo et al. (2009), Geng et al. (2010), and others who obtained negative scattering lengths for DK(I=0) at lower orders. The predictions for meson-doubly charmed (bottom) baryon scattering lengths enable experimental searches by the LHCb Collaboration for narrow states such as K̄Ξ_cc with IJ^P = 0(1/2)^-.
