# Strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ super Yang-Mills plasma on the Coulomb branch II: Transport coefficients and hard probe parameters

> We study $\mathcal{N} = 4$ super Yang-Mills theory on the Coulomb branch (cSYM) by using its Type IIB supergravity dual. We compute the transport coefficients, and hard probe parameters of $\mathcal{N} = 4$ cSYM at finite temperature $T$. We use the rotating black 3-brane solution of Type IIB supergravity with a single non-zero rotation parameter $r_{0}$ after analytically continuing $r_{0}\rightarrow -ir_{0}$, and in an ensemble where the Hawking temperature $T$ and a scalar condensate $<\mathcal{O}>\sim r_{0}^4$ are held fixed. We find that the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio of the large black hole branch decreases with temperature and has a maxima around the critical temperature $T_{c}$ while, for the small black hole branch, it increases with temperature. The other transport coefficients and parameters of hard probes, such as the conductivity, jet quenching parameter, drag force, and momentum diffusion coefficients of the large black hole branch increase with temperature and asymptote to their conformal value while, for the small black hole branch, they decrease with temperature.

## Key claim

The paper computes transport coefficients and hard probe parameters for strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills theory on the Coulomb branch (cSYM) using its Type IIB supergravity dual, finding that for the large black hole branch these quantities increase with temperature and asymptote to their conformal values, while for the small black hole branch they decrease with temperature; the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio of the large black hole branch has a maximum around the critical temperature Tc.

## Method

The authors use the rotating black 3-brane solution of Type IIB supergravity with a single non-zero rotation parameter r0, after analytically continuing r0 to -ir0, in an ensemble where the Hawking temperature T and a scalar condensate <O> ~ r0^4 are held fixed. They compute shear viscosity from transverse metric fluctuations, bulk viscosity using the Einstein-Maxwell-scalar action in the gauge of the scalar field, and conductivity from both flavor U(1) and R-charge two-point correlation functions via Kubo formulas. For hard probes, they use the Nambu-Goto action for a trailing string configuration to compute drag force, momentum diffusion coefficients, and the jet quenching parameter from the 10-dimensional uplifted metric.

## Key numbers

*   Bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio zeta/s has a maximum around the critical temperature Tc for the large black hole branch.
*   Conductivity of a U(1) flavor charge: sigma_f = (Nc Nf T0)/(4 pi) (1 - kappa)^(1/6), where kappa = r0^2/rh^2.
*   Conductivity of a single R-charge: sigma_R = (Nc^2 T0)/(32 pi) (2 - kappa)^2 / sqrt(1 - kappa).
*   Drag force: F_drag = -(1/2) pi sqrt(lambda) T0^2 gamma v Q(kappa, gamma), where Q(kappa, gamma) = kappa/(2 gamma) (1 + sqrt(1 + 4 gamma^2 (1 - kappa)/kappa^2)).
*   Jet quenching parameter normalized to conformal value: qhat/qhat_0 = (K(1/sqrt(2))/K(n)) (2 n^2)^2 (2 n'^2)^(1/2), where n^2 = (1 - kappa)/(2 - kappa) and qhat_0 = (pi^(3/4) Gamma(3/4))/(sqrt(2) Gamma(5/4)) sqrt(lambda) T^3.
*   Entropy density: s = (pi^2 Nc^2 T0^3)/2 (1 - kappa)^(1/2).
*   Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: eta/s = 1/(4 pi).

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamics study of N=4 cSYM by Mamo (2016) to transport coefficients and hard probes, using the same dual geometry. It generalizes prior computations of transport coefficients for rotating black 3-branes by Avramis (2006), Caceres (2006), Son (2006), and DeWolfe (2011), which were limited to grand canonical and canonical ensembles, by working in an ensemble with fixed temperature and scalar condensate. The results enable comparison of non-conformal plasma properties near the critical temperature with conformal N=4 SYM results, and the finding that drag force decreases with temperature near Tc may help explain the heavy quark puzzle in hydrodynamic simulations.
