# Analytical formulas, general properties and calculation of transport coefficients in the hadron gas: shear and bulk viscosities

> Elaborated calculations of the shear and the bulk viscosities in the hadron gas, using the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model cross sections, are made. These cross sections are analyzed and improved. A special treatment of the resonances is implemented additionally. All this allows for better hydrodynamical description of the experimental data. The previously considered approximation of one constant cross section for all hadrons is justified. It's found that the bulk viscosity of the hadron gas is much larger than the bulk viscosity of the pion gas while the shear viscosity is found to be less sensitive to the hadronic mass spectrum. The maximum of the bulk viscosity of the hadron gas is expected to be approximately in the temperature range ${T=150 190 MeV}$ with zero chemical potentials. This range covers the critical temperature values found from lattice calculations. We comment on some important aspects of calculations of the bulk viscosity, which were not taken into account or were not analyzed well previously. Doing this, a generalized Chapman-Enskog procedure, taking into account deviations from the chemical equilibrium, is outlined. Some general properties, features, the physical meaning of the bulk viscosity and some other comments on the deviations from the chemical equilibrium supplement this discussion. Analytical closed-form expressions for the transport coefficients and some related quantities within a quite large class of cross sections can be obtained. Some examples are explicitly considered. Comparisons with some previous calculations of the viscosities in the hadron gas and the pion gas are done.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the bulk viscosity of the hadron gas is much larger than that of the pion gas, with a maximum expected in the temperature range T=150–190 MeV at zero chemical potentials, while the shear viscosity is less sensitive to the hadronic mass spectrum; however, these results rely on the approximation of classical Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics, an ideal gas equation of state, and neglect of medium effects, and are considered precise only at low temperatures where elastic collisions dominate.

## Method

The author calculates shear and bulk viscosities for the hadron gas using corrected UrQMD model cross sections within the framework of the Boltzmann equation, employing classical Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics and an ideal gas equation of state without medium effects. A generalized Chapman-Enskog procedure is outlined to account for deviations from chemical equilibrium, and analytical closed-form expressions for transport coefficients are derived for a class of cross sections, including explicit formulas for the elastic pion-pion isospin averaged cross section. The calculations are performed for both the hadron gas (with many components) and the pion gas, and comparisons are made with prior results from Prakash (1993) and Davesne (1996).

## Key numbers

- The maximum of the bulk viscosity of the hadron gas is expected approximately in the temperature range T=150–190 MeV with zero chemical potentials.
- For the pion gas, discrepancies with Prakash (1993) are up to a factor of 3.5 for the bulk viscosity and up to a factor of 2.5 for the shear viscosity.
- At T=20 MeV (60 MeV), the bulk viscosity from Fernandez-Fraile (2010) is larger approximately 39 (8) times than the bulk viscosity in this paper; maximal values differ by approximately 24 times.
- At T=140 MeV, the bulk viscosity from Lu and Moore (2011) is nearly 57 times larger than the bulk viscosity calculated in this paper.
- The ratio ξ/s of the hadron gas in Toneev (2012) is 3.65–11.3 times larger (in the temperature range 0.1 GeV < T < 0.18 GeV) than the ξ/s calculated in this paper; at T=100 MeV it is 11.3 times larger.
- The shear viscosity η in Muronga (2003) is 1.14–1.77 times smaller than the η for the hadron gas in this paper; at T=0.15 GeV it is 1.77 times smaller.
- The ratio ξ/s in Noronha-Hostler (2012) deviates from the ξ/s of this paper up to a factor of 1.8 in the temperature range 0.14 GeV < T < 0.18 GeV and differs by 4% at T=0.14 GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of shear and bulk viscosities in the hadron gas by Moroz (2011) using one constant cross section for all hadrons, now improved with corrected UrQMD cross sections and a special treatment of resonances. It contradicts the results of Prakash (1993) and Davesne (1996) for the pion gas by factors up to 3.5, and disagrees significantly with Fernandez-Fraile (2010) and Lu and Moore (2011) for the bulk viscosity of the pion gas. The calculations enable better hydrodynamical description of experimental data from heavy ion collisions and provide a basis for comparing with lattice QCD results near the critical temperature.
