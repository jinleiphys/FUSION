# Dynamics of relativistic spin-polarized fluids

> We briefly review the foundations of a new relativistic fluid dynamics framework for polarized systems of particles with spin one half. Using this approach we numerically study the dynamics of the spin polarization of a rotating medium resembling the ones created in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper presents a relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamics framework for spin-1/2 particles that extends previous equilibrium treatments (Becattini et al., 2009, 2013) to systems in local equilibrium, and shows through numerical simulation that the spin polarization tensor undergoes nontrivial evolution during the hydrodynamic stage, which may significantly affect final results used for interpreting experimental data.

## Method
The authors derive conservation laws for baryon charge, energy-momentum, and total angular momentum, obtaining a closed set of six coupled partial differential equations for the space-time dependent temperature, baryon chemical potential, spin chemical potential, and fluid four-velocity, plus evolution equations for the normalized spin polarization tensor components. They numerically solve these equations for a Gaussian source with initial rotation profile resembling systems created in low-energy heavy-ion collisions, starting at t0 = 0.1 fm and evolving the spin polarization tensor on top of the hydrodynamic background.

## Key numbers
- Initial baryon chemical potential: μ0 = 200 MeV (peak value in Gaussian source)
- Initial spin chemical potential: Ωi = 0.03 × Ti/2 (where Ti is the initial temperature)
- Initial time: t0 = 0.1 fm
- Rotation parameter: r0 = 1 fm (sets rotation speed via tanh(r/r0)/r)
- Gaussian source widths: σx, σy, σz (values not specified in text)
- Equilibrium vortex solution: ωij = Ω̃/T0 = 2Ω0/T0 for i=x, j=y (with Ω̃ the rotation frequency)

## Context
This work extends the equilibrium spin polarization framework of Becattini et al. (2009, 2013) to a full hydrodynamic description of spin-polarized fluids in local equilibrium, building on the authors' prior theoretical developments (Florkowski et al., 2017a, 2017b, 2018a, 2018b). The numerical results demonstrate that spin polarization dynamics during the hydrodynamic stage are nontrivial and can significantly affect final polarization observables, which is missing in models used so far for interpreting STAR collaboration data on Λ hyperon polarization. This enables more realistic modeling of spin polarization evolution in heavy-ion collisions.
