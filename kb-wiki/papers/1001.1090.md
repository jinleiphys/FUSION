# Quantal molecular description and universal aspects of the spectra of bosons and fermions in the lowest Landau level

> Through the introduction of a class of trial wave functions portraying combined rotations and vibrations of molecules formed through particle localization in concentric polygonal rings, a correlated basis is constructed that spans the translationally invariant part of the lower-Landau-level (LLL) spectra. These trial functions, referred to as rovibrational molecular (RVM) functions, generalize our previous work which focused exclusively on electronic cusp states, describing them as pure vibrationless rotations. From a computational viewpoint, the RVM correlated basis enables controlled and systematic improvements of the original strongly-correlated variational wave function. Conceptually, it provides the basis for the development of a quantal molecular description for the full LLL spectra. This quantal molecular description is universal, being valid for both bosons and fermions, for both the yrast and excited states of the LLL spectra, and for both low and high angular momenta. Furthermore, it follows that all other translationally invariant trial functions (e.g., the Jastrow-Laughlin, compact composite-fermion, or Moore-Read functions) are reducible to a description in terms of an excited rotating/vibrating quantal molecule.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the full lowest-Landau-level (LLL) spectra for both bosons and fermions can be universally described by quantal molecular trial functions representing combined rotations and vibrations of particles localized in concentric polygonal rings, and that all other translationally invariant trial functions (such as Jastrow-Laughlin, composite-fermion, or Moore-Read functions) are reducible to this molecular description. The hedge is that this description applies specifically to the translationally invariant subspace of the LLL spectra, not to the full Hilbert space including spurious center-of-mass excitations.

## Method

The authors constructed a correlated basis of trial wave functions called rovibrational molecular (RVM) functions, which generalize their previous rotating electron molecule (REM) and rotating boson molecule (RBM) functions by incorporating both rotational and vibrational excitations. These RVM functions were derived through a two-step process: first constructing permanents (for bosons) or Slater determinants (for fermions) from displaced single-particle Gaussian orbitals representing particles localized at polygonal-ring equilibrium configurations, then projecting onto eigenstates of total angular momentum. The completeness of the RVM basis for spanning the translationally invariant subspace was verified by comparing RVM diagonalization results with exact diagonalization (EXD) calculations for systems of N=3 bosons, N=3 and N=4 electrons, and N=5 bosons.

## Key numbers

- For N=3 scalar bosons with contact interaction, the energy eigenvalue for the TI state at L=0 is 1.5000000 in units of g/(πΛ²), and at L=2 is 0.7500000 in the same units.
- The dimension of the translationally invariant subspace D^TI(L) equals D^EXD(L) - D^EXD(L-1), where D^EXD(L) is the dimension of the full exact-diagonalization space at angular momentum L.
- For N particles, the smallest total angular momentum for spin-polarized fermions in the LLL is N(N-1)/2.
- The magic angular momenta for purely rotational molecular states are given by L = L_0 + Σ n_q k_q, where L_0 = N(N-1)/2 for electrons and L_0 = 0 for bosons, with k_q being nonnegative integers.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of rotating electron molecules (REMs) for electronic cusp states (Yannouleas and Landman, 2002, 2007) to include vibrational excitations and to cover the full LLL spectra for both bosons and fermions. It provides a unified framework that contrasts with prior quantum-liquid descriptions such as the Jastrow-Laughlin functions (Laughlin, 1983), composite-fermion theory (Jain, 1989), and Moore-Read Pfaffian states (Moore and Read, 1991), showing that these can be reinterpreted as molecular states. The RVM correlated basis enables controlled systematic improvements of variational wave functions and is relevant to rapidly rotating ultracold bosonic gases and fractional quantum Hall systems in semiconductor heterostructures and graphene quantum dots.
