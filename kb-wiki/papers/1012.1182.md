# Estimation of the breakup cross sections in $^6$He+$^{12}$C reaction within high-energy approximation and microscopic optical potential

> The breakup cross sections in the reaction $^6$He+$^{12}$C are calculated at about 40 MeV/nucleon using the high-energy approximation (HEA) and with the help of microscopic optical potentials (OP) of interaction with the target nucleus $^{12}$C of the projectile nucleus fragments $^4$He and 2n. Considering the di-neutron $h$=2n as a single particle the relative motion $hα$ wave function is estimated so that to explain both the separation energy of $h$ in $^6$He and the rms radius of the latter. The stripping and absorbtion total cross sections are calculated and their sum is compared with the total reaction cross section obtained within a double-folding microscopic OP for the $^6$He+$^{12}$C scattering. It is concluded that the breakup cross sections contribute in about 50% of the total reaction cross section.

# Estimation of the breakup cross sections in $^6$He+$^{12}$C reaction within high-energy approximation and microscopic optical potential

## Key claim

The breakup cross sections contribute about 50% of the total reaction cross section in $^6$He+$^{12}$C scattering at around 40 MeV/nucleon, but this estimate depends on using a simplified di-neutron ($h$=2n) cluster model of $^6$He and the high-energy approximation (HEA) method, which yields total reaction cross sections about 20% lower than those obtained from exact DWUCK4 calculations with the same folded optical potentials.

## Method

The authors model $^6$He as a two-cluster system consisting of an $\alpha$ particle and a di-neutron ($h$=2n) bound by a Woods-Saxon potential adjusted to reproduce the $h$ separation energy of 0.975 MeV and the $^6$He rms radius. Microscopic optical potentials for $\alpha$+$^{12}$C and $n$+$^{12}$C are constructed using double-folding real parts (with CDM3Y6 Paris NN forces) and imaginary parts from the high-energy approximation (Glauber theory optical limit). These fragment potentials are folded with the $h\alpha$ relative-motion density to obtain the $^6$He+$^{12}$C optical potential. Total absorption, stripping, and breakup cross sections are then calculated analytically within the HEA eikonal formalism using the S-matrix probabilities for each fragment.

## Key numbers

- Separation energy of the di-neutron $h$ in $^6$He: $\varepsilon = 0.975$ MeV, reproduced by a Woods-Saxon potential with $V_0 = 28.3$ MeV, $R = 1.45$ fm, $a = 0.3$ fm.
- rms radius of $^6$He from the $h\alpha$-model: $r_{rms} = 2.62$ fm (compared to 2.586 fm from the large-scale shell-model).
- Total reaction cross sections from DWUCK4 with $h\alpha$-model folded potentials: 1018 mb (using $W^{H(b)}$ imaginary part) and 1042 mb (using $V^{DF(b)}$ imaginary part).
- Total reaction cross sections from HEA eikonal formula (eq. 16) with the same folded potentials: 952 mb and 965 mb.
- HEA $h\alpha$-model breakup cross sections: $\sigma_{bu}^{tot} = 412$ mb (with $W^{H(b)}$) and 389 mb (with $V^{DF(b)}$).
- HEA $h\alpha$-model absorption cross sections: $\sigma_{abs}^{tot} = 392$ mb (with $W^{H(b)}$) and 447 mb (with $V^{DF(b)}$).
- HEA $h\alpha$-model total reaction cross sections: $\sigma_R^{tot} = 804$ mb (with $W^{H(b)}$) and 830 mb (with $V^{DF(b)}$).

## Context

This work extends previous elastic scattering analyses of $^6$He+$^{12}$C at 38.3 and 41.6 MeV/nucleon by Lukyanov et al. (2010), where microscopic optical potentials were fitted to experimental data from Lapoux (2002) and Khalili (1996). The present study decomposes the total reaction cross section into breakup and absorption components using a cluster model, complementing the earlier work that focused on elastic channel phenomenology. The $h\alpha$-cluster approach follows similar treatments by Khalili (1996), while the HEA formalism for breakup reactions builds on the Glauber (1955) and Akhiezer (1955) theories originally developed for deuteron stripping. The results enable future comparisons with more complete dynamical calculations and suggest that breakup channels account for roughly half of the total reaction cross section at these energies.
