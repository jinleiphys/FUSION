# An Effective Field Theory study of medium heavy quark evolution

> The evolution of hard probes in a medium is a complex multiscale problem that significantly benefits from the use of Effective Field Theories (EFTs). Within the EFT framework, we aim to define a series of EFTs in a way that addresses each energy scale individually in separate steps. However, studying hard probes in a medium presents challenges. This is because an EFT is typically constructed by formulating the most general Lagrangian compatible with the problem's symmetries. Nevertheless, medium effects may not always be encoded adequately in an effective action. In this paper, we construct an EFT that is valid for studying the evolution of a heavy quark in a QCD plasma containing few other heavy quarks, where degrees of freedom with an energy of the order of the temperature scale are integrated out. Through this example, we explicitly demonstrate how to handle the doubling of degrees that arise in non-equilibrium field theory. As a result, we derive a Fokker-Planck equation using only symmetry and power counting arguments. The methods introduced in this paper will pave the way for future developments in the study of quarkonium suppression.

# An Effective Field Theory study of medium heavy quark evolution

## Key claim

The paper constructs an Effective Field Theory (EFT) for a heavy quark evolving in a QCD plasma containing few other heavy quarks, where degrees of freedom at the temperature scale are integrated out, and derives a Fokker-Planck equation using only symmetry and power counting arguments. The construction explicitly handles the doubling of degrees of freedom that arises in non-equilibrium field theory, which the abstract notes is a challenge because medium effects may not always be encoded adequately in an effective action.

## Method

The author starts from NRQCD and defines a sequence of EFTs that address each energy scale separately: first NRQCD for semi-hard fields (NRQCDsh) valid for energies below sqrt(MT), then a Langevin Effective Theory (LET) valid for energies below T. The matching between NRQCDsh and LET is performed in the one-gluon exchange approximation, and the influence functional formalism is used to handle terms that mix type-1 and type-2 fields on the Schwinger-Keldysh contour. The dilute limit approximation is applied throughout, and the resulting LET is used to compute the evolution of the heavy quark distribution function.

## Key numbers

- The heavy quark spatial momentum p is of order sqrt(TM), where T is the temperature and M is the heavy quark mass.
- The residual momentum k is of order T.
- The expansion for the energy shift delta E is given as: delta E = alpha_0 T + alpha_1 (T^2/M) + alpha_2 (p^2 T / M^2) + O(T^3/M^2).
- The expansion for the decay width Gamma is given as: Gamma = beta_0 T + beta_1 (T^2/M) + beta_2 (p^2 T / M^2) + O(T^3/M^2).
- The heavy quark propagator at tree level in NRQCDsh has the spectral function 2 pi delta(k_0 - p^2/(2M)) (1 - 2f^0(p+k,R)).

## Context

This work extends the application of EFT methods to hard probes in heavy-ion collisions, building on prior developments of NRQCD (Caswell 1985, Bodwin 1994) and pNRQCD (Pineda 1997, Brambilla 1999, 2004) for quarkonium at zero temperature, and on medium-modified versions used to compute thermal corrections to mass and decay width (Escobedo 2008, Brambilla 2008, 2010). The approach differs from Bu (2021) which used AdS/CFT in Supersymmetric Yang-Mills as a starting point, while this work aims to be agnostic about the properties of the medium. The methods introduced are intended to enable future studies of quarkonium suppression in the regime where T ~ 1/r, where the matching between NRQCD and pNRQCD at finite temperature can be done in the static limit.
