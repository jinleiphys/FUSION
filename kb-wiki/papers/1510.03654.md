# Constraints on the chiral unitary $\bar KN$ amplitude from $πΣK^+$ photoproduction data

> A chiral unitary approach for antikaon-nucleon scattering in on-shell factorization is studied. We find multiple sets of parameters for which the model describes all existing hadronic data similarly well. We confirm the two-pole structure of the $Λ(1405)$. The narrow $Λ(1405)$ pole appears at comparable positions in the complex energy plane, whereas the location of the broad pole suffers from a large uncertainty. In the second step, we use a simple model for photoproduction of $K^+πΣ$ off the proton and confront it with the experimental data from the CLAS collaboration. It is found that only a few of the hadronic solutions allow for a consistent description of the CLAS data within the assumed reaction mechanism.

## Key claim

The chiral unitary approach for antikaon-nucleon scattering yields multiple parameter sets that describe all existing hadronic data equally well, but only a few of these solutions also allow a consistent description of the CLAS photoproduction data within the assumed simple reaction mechanism, thereby reducing the ambiguity in the location of the broad pole of the Λ(1405).

## Method

The authors constructed a next-to-leading order chiral potential for meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness S = -1 sector, using an on-shell factorization of the Bethe-Salpeter equation to reduce computational time by roughly a factor of 30. They fitted the low-energy constants and regularization scales to reproduce cross sections for K⁻p → MB at laboratory momenta below 300 MeV, decay ratios, and the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen shift and width, obtaining eight best solutions. They then coupled these hadronic amplitudes to a simple semi-phenomenological model for the reaction γp → K⁺πΣ, where the photoproduction mechanism is described by energy-dependent complex constants Cⁱ(Ẇ) and the final-state interaction by the standard Höhler partial waves, and fitted the resulting invariant mass distributions to the CLAS data for all three πΣ charge channels at nine total energy values from 2.0 to 2.8 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Eight best hadronic solutions were obtained, with χ² per degree of freedom ranging from 0.90 (solution #8) to 1.35 (solution #1) for the hadronic data.
- For the photoproduction fit quality, the total χ² per data point (χ²_pp) ranged from 1.77 (solution #4) to 6.11 (solution #6); solutions #2, #4, and #5 gave the lowest values, while the next best solution had a 40% larger χ²_pp than the best one.
- The narrow Λ(1405) pole for the surviving solutions is at 1434⁺²₋₂ − i·10⁺²₋₁ MeV (solution #2) and 1429⁺⁸₋₇ − i·12⁺²₋₃ MeV (solution #4).
- The broad Λ(1405) pole for the surviving solutions is at 1330⁺⁴₋₅ − i·56⁺¹⁷₋₁₁ MeV (solution #2) and 1325⁺¹⁵₋₁₅ − i·90⁺¹²₋₁₈ MeV (solution #4).

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Mai (2012) and Bruns (2010) for antikaon-nucleon scattering by incorporating the CLAS photoproduction data from Moriya (2013) as a new constraint. It confirms the two-pole structure of the Λ(1405) found by Oller (2000) and analyzed by Hyodo (2011), and is similar in approach to the theoretical analyses of Roca (2013) and Nakamura (2013). The results enable future studies using a theoretically more robust model for two-meson photoproduction, such as a generalization of the one-meson photoproduction model presented by Borasoy (2007) and Mai (2012).
