# The QCD axion beyond the classical level: A lattice study

> The axion is a hypothetical elementary particle postulated by the Peccei-Quinn theory to resolve the strong CP problem in QCD. If axions exist and have low mass, they are a candidate for dark matter as well. So far our knowledge of the properties of the QCD axion rests on semi-classical arguments and effective theory. In this work we perform, for the first time, a fully dynamical investigation of the Peccei-Quinn theory, focussing on the axion mass, by simulating the theory on the lattice. The results of the simulation are found to be in conflict with present axion phenomenology.

## Key claim

The first fully dynamical lattice simulation of the Peccei-Quinn theory finds no light axion for axion decay constants between approximately 1.5 and 150 GeV, contradicting the classical prediction that the axion mass is given by \(m_a^2 = \chi_t / f_a^2\); instead, the axion mass approaches the mass of the \(\eta\) meson in the infinite volume limit and is largely independent of \(f_a\).

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with three flavors of dynamical quarks at the SU(3) flavor symmetric point, using the SLiNC action with Symanzik improved glue. The anomalous axion-gluon coupling was rotated into the fermion mass matrix via a space-time dependent axial transformation, and the resulting action was expanded to leading order in \(\phi_a/f_a\). Simulations were carried out at imaginary values of the axion decay constant \(f_a^* = i f_a\) to avoid the sign problem, with analytic continuation to physical couplings, on lattices of sizes \(12^3 \times 24\), \(24^3 \times 48\), and \(32^3 \times 64\) at a single lattice spacing \(a = 0.074(2)\) fm.

## Key numbers

* Axion mass on the \(24^3 \times 48\) lattice: \(m_a = 230 \pm 13\) MeV for \(1/f_a^* = 0.0068\) GeV\(^{-1}\); \(m_a = 221 \pm 13\) MeV for \(1/f_a^* = 0.068\) GeV\(^{-1}\); \(m_a = 238 \pm 11\) MeV for \(1/f_a^* = 0.68\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
* \(\eta^\prime\) mass on the \(24^3 \times 48\) lattice: \(m_{\eta^\prime} = 700 \pm 110\) MeV for \(1/f_a^* = 0.0068\) GeV\(^{-1}\); \(m_{\eta^\prime} = 660^{+50}_{-350}\) MeV for \(1/f_a^* = 0.068\) GeV\(^{-1}\); \(m_{\eta^\prime} = 670 \pm 120\) MeV for \(1/f_a^* = 0.68\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
* Axion mass on the \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice: \(m_a = 293 \pm 55\) MeV for \(1/f_a^* = 0.068\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
* Topological susceptibility on the \(24^3 \times 48\) lattice: \(\chi_t^{1/4} = 153 \pm 11\) MeV for \(1/f_a^* = 0.0068\) GeV\(^{-1}\); \(\chi_t^{1/4} = 148 \pm 9\) MeV for \(1/f_a^* = 0.068\) GeV\(^{-1}\); \(\chi_t^{1/4} = 151 \pm 8\) MeV for \(1/f_a^* = 0.68\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
* Pearson correlation coefficient between \(\bar{\phi}_a\) and topological charge \(Q\) on the \(24^3 \times 48\) lattice: \(r_{\bar{\phi}_a Q} = 0.016(113)\), compatible with zero.
* In the infinite volume limit, the axion mass extrapolates to approximately 400 MeV for \(1/f_a^* = 0.068\) GeV\(^{-1}\), consistent with the \(\eta\) meson mass of 415 MeV at the SU(3) flavor symmetric point.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD methods used in calculations of the neutron electric dipole moment (Guo 2015) to a fully dynamical study of the Peccei-Quinn theory, going beyond the semi-classical instanton gas picture and effective theory approaches that have dominated axion phenomenology since Weinberg (1977) and Wilczek (1977). The results contradict the classical axion mass formula \(m_a^2 = \chi_t / f_a^2\) and the dilute instanton gas approximation, instead showing that the axion mixes with the pseudoscalar meson sector in a manner analogous to the \(\eta^\prime\) meson. The findings challenge the standard interpretation of the QCD axion as a pseudo-Goldstone boson and call into question its viability as a dark matter candidate, while opening the question of whether the theory has a well-defined continuum limit.
