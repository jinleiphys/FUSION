# Bulk viscosity-driven suppression of shear viscosity effects on the flow harmonics at RHIC

> The interplay between shear and bulk viscosities on the flow harmonics, $v_n$'s, at RHIC is investigated using the newly developed relativistic 2+1 hydrodynamical code v-USPhydro that includes bulk and shear viscosity effects both in the hydrodynamic evolution and also at freeze-out. While shear viscosity is known to attenuate the flow harmonics, we find that the inclusion of bulk viscosity decreases the shear viscosity-induced suppression of the flow harmonics bringing them closer to their values in ideal hydrodynamical calculations. Depending on the value of the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio, $ζ/s$, in the quark-gluon plasma, the bulk viscosity-driven suppression of shear viscosity effects on the flow harmonics may require a re-evaluation of the previous estimates of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, $η/s$, of the quark-gluon plasma previously extracted by comparing hydrodynamic calculations to heavy ion data.

## Key claim

The inclusion of bulk viscosity in relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of RHIC collisions reduces the suppression of flow harmonics caused by shear viscosity, bringing the computed values closer to ideal hydrodynamic results; this interplay may require a re-evaluation of previously extracted estimates of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of the quark-gluon plasma, depending on the actual magnitude of the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio.

## Method

The authors used the newly developed relativistic 2+1 hydrodynamical code v-USPhydro, which employs Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) to solve the equations of motion including both bulk and shear viscosity effects in the hydrodynamic evolution and at freeze-out. They performed event-by-event simulations with Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, using a temperature-dependent shear viscosity parametrization from Niemi et al. (2012) and a bulk viscosity coefficient inspired by Buchel's formula for strongly coupled plasma. The freeze-out was performed on an isothermal hypersurface at T=150 MeV using the Cooper-Frye formalism with viscous corrections to the distribution function.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio parametrization uses T_tr=180 MeV as the transition temperature
- The hydrodynamic evolution begins at tau_0=1 fm
- Freeze-out temperature T_0=150 MeV
- 150 hydrodynamical events were calculated per centrality class
- 15,000 events were used for binning centrality classes via N_part
- The bulk relaxation time tau_Pi=9*zeta/(epsilon-3p) and shear relaxation time tau_pi=5*eta/(epsilon+p)
- For pions at T_0=150 MeV, the Moments Method bulk correction coefficients are: B_0=-65.85 fm^4, D_0=171.27 fm^4/GeV, E_0=-63.05 fm^4/GeV^2
- For pions at T_0=150 MeV, the Monnai-Hirano bulk correction coefficients are: B_0=-0.69 fm^4, D_0=-38.96 fm^4/GeV, E_0=49.69 fm^4/GeV^2

## Context

This work extends previous studies of bulk viscosity effects on elliptic flow by Song and Heinz (2010), Bozek (2010), Monnai and Hirano (2009), Denicol et al. (2009, 2010), and Dusling and Schafer (2012), and builds on the authors' own earlier event-by-event study of bulk viscosity effects on flow harmonics at RHIC (Noronha-Hostler et al., 2013). The paper enables a more complete extraction of transport coefficients from heavy-ion collision data by demonstrating that previous estimates of eta/s, which neglected bulk viscosity, may need revision. The code v-USPhydro provides a framework for future studies that include both shear and bulk viscosity simultaneously in hydrodynamic modeling of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
