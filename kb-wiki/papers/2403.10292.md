# Low-energy theorems for neutron-proton scattering in $χ$EFT using a perturbative power counting

> Low-energy theorems (LETs) for effective-range parameters in nucleon-nucleon scattering encode properties of the long-range part of the nuclear force. We compute LETs for S-wave neutron-proton scattering using chiral effective field theory with a modified version of Weinberg power counting. Corrections to the leading order amplitude are included in distorted-wave perturbation theory and we incorporate contributions up to the third order in the power counting. We find that LETs in the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ partial waves agree well with empirical effective-range parameters. At the same time, phase shifts up to laboratory scattering energies of about 100 MeV can be reproduced. We show that it is important to consider the pion mass splitting in the one-pion exchange potential in the $^1S_0$ partial wave while the effect is negligible in the $^3S_1$ partial wave. We conclude that pion exchanges, as treated in this power counting, accurately describe the long-range part of the $S$-wave nuclear interaction.

## Key claim
Low-energy theorems (LETs) for S-wave neutron-proton scattering computed in chiral effective field theory with the Long and Yang perturbative power counting agree well with empirical effective-range parameters, but only when the leading isospin-breaking effect from pion mass splitting is included in the one-pion exchange potential for the \(^1S_0\) partial wave; without this correction, convergence to empirical values is not observed as chiral order increases.

## Method
The LO amplitude was solved non-perturbatively via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, while sub-leading corrections up to N\(^3\)LO (\(\nu=3\)) were included using distorted-wave perturbation theory. The unknown low-energy constants (LECs) were calibrated to reproduce the scattering length, effective range, and Nijmegen phase shifts at selected laboratory energies, with calculations performed at two momentum cutoffs (500 MeV and 2500 MeV) to estimate residual cutoff dependence. The effective range function was computed perturbatively order-by-order, and LETs for the shape parameters \(v_2, v_3, v_4\) were extracted by fitting the effective range expansion polynomial to the computed function.

## Key numbers
- Empirical scattering length in \(^1S_0\): \(a = -23.735(16)\) fm (NavarroPerez 2014)
- Empirical effective range in \(^1S_0\): \(r = 2.68(3)\) fm
- Empirical shape parameters in \(^1S_0\): \(v_2 = -0.48(2)\) fm\(^3\), \(v_3 = 3.9(1)\) fm\(^5\), \(v_4 = -19.6(5)\) fm\(^7\)
- At N\(^3\)LO with CIB and \(\Lambda=500\) MeV, predicted LETs in \(^1S_0\): \(r = 2.68(0)\) fm, \(v_2 = -0.50(0)\) fm\(^3\), \(v_3 = 4.02(0)\) fm\(^5\), \(v_4 = -19.8(2)\) fm\(^7\)
- At N\(^3\)LO with CIB and \(\Lambda=2500\) MeV, predicted LETs in \(^1S_0\): \(r = 2.67(0)\) fm, \(v_2 = -0.52(0)\) fm\(^3\), \(v_3 = 4.26(2)\) fm\(^5\), \(v_4 = -20.0(7)\) fm\(^7\)
- Phase shifts reproduced up to laboratory scattering energies of about 100 MeV
- Pion masses used: \(m_{\pi^0} = 134.977\) MeV, \(m_{\pi^\pm} = 139.570\) MeV; average pion mass \(m_\pi = 138.039\) MeV

## Context
This work extends the perturbative and renormalization-group-invariant power counting proposed by Long and Yang (2012) to compute low-energy theorems, following the approach of Cohen and Hansen (1998, 1999) who showed that LETs serve as a non-trivial test of the long-range part of the nuclear force. Earlier studies found that LETs in Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) counting (Kaplan 1998) agreed poorly with empirical ERE parameters, while Weinberg power counting (Epelbaum 2003) showed good agreement. The present results demonstrate that the Long and Yang counting can simultaneously describe both phase shifts and LETs when isospin-breaking effects are included, enabling future studies that incorporate a full EFT truncation error analysis.
