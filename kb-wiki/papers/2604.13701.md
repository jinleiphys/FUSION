# Global polarization of $Λ$ hyperons in hot QCD matter at TeV energies

> The study of spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions provides insights into the angular momentum and vortical structure of the possible existence of QGP. The present study examines the global spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons using a second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamic framework that incorporates medium vorticity, shear viscosity, and evolving magnetic fields. It explores thermal vorticity evolution in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and evaluates its value at the decoupling isothermal freeze-out surface. We quantify the contributions of thermal vorticity and magnetic field to the global spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons. Comparing results with recent ALICE measurements in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV shows qualitative agreement, offering new insights into the vortical structure of QCD matter. It also explores the relationship between magnetic and rotational dynamics, with implications for spin polarization at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamic framework incorporating medium vorticity, shear viscosity, and evolving magnetic fields can qualitatively reproduce ALICE measurements of global \(\Lambda\) hyperon spin polarization in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, but the agreement is only qualitative and the spread between different magnetic field scenarios provides an estimate of theoretical uncertainty.

## Method

The authors developed a second-order Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) relativistic viscous hydrodynamic framework that couples evolution equations for temperature, shear viscosity, and vorticity, incorporating both time-dependent and constant magnetic fields. They solved three non-linear coupled differential equations for energy density (temperature), shear stress tensor, and vorticity using initial conditions tuned for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV at (15-50)% centrality, with initial temperature \(T_0 = 0.350\) GeV, initial proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.3\) fm, and initial vorticity \(\omega_0 = 5.0\) fm\(^{-1}\). The thermal vorticity evaluated at the isothermal decoupling freeze-out hypersurface was then used to compute the mean spin vector of \(\Lambda\) hyperons via Eq. (13), integrating over the freeze-out surface.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions: \(T_0 = 0.350\) GeV, \(\tau_0 = 0.3\) fm, \(\omega_0 = 5.0\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(\Phi_0 = 0.053\) GeV\(^4\) for Pb+Pb collisions at (15-50)% centrality
- The initial orbital angular momentum in peripheral heavy-ion collisions is estimated to be of order \(10^5 \hbar\)
- The magnetic moment of \(\Lambda\) hyperons used: \(\mu_\Lambda \approx -0.61 \mu_N \approx -0.61 e/(2m_p)\)
- Comparison is made with ALICE data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV for (15-50)% centrality
- The magnetic field decay profile: \(B(\tau) = B_0 \exp(-\tau/\tau_B)\) with decay parameter \(\tau_B\)

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic and transport model calculations of global spin polarization by Karpenko (2016), Alzhrani (2022), Vitiuk (2019), Sun (2017), and Ivanov (2019, 2022) by incorporating a self-consistent vorticity dissipation-rate equation coupled with magnetic field evolution within second-order MIS theory. The framework builds on the landmark measurement of \(\Lambda\) hyperon spin polarization by the STAR Collaboration (2017) and enables future extensions to 3+1D event-by-event hydrodynamics, lattice QCD-based equations of state, and spin hydrodynamics where spin is treated as an explicit dynamical degree of freedom.
