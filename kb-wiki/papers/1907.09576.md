# Studying transverse momentum distributions with jets at N$^3$LL

> Semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) is a promising channel for the extraction of transverse momentum dependent distributions at future colliders. In this context, we recently developed a framework that uses jets (instead of single hadrons) to achieve reduced sensitivity to final-state non-perturbative effects. A suitable non-standard jet definition allows us to apply the factorization formulas valid for hadrons to jets of arbitrary size, by just replacing fragmentation functions with the jet functions we computed. Besides presenting the framework, we will show numerical predictions at N$^3$LL accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using recoil-free (Winner-Take-All) jets instead of single hadrons in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) allows the same TMD factorization formulas to apply, with the key condition that the jet radius parameter R must be comparable to or larger than the power-counting parameter theta (approximately 2q_T/Q) for the approximation to hold.

## Method

The authors apply Soft-Collinear Effective Theory to derive factorization formulas for SIDIS with jets, replacing fragmentation functions with jet functions computed at next-to-leading order (NLO). They extract the two-loop constant for the large-R limit of the Winner-Take-All jet function numerically using the Event2 program, and implement the results in the arTeMiDe code to obtain predictions at N^3LL accuracy.

## Key numbers

- For LEP dijet production at sqrt(s) = 91.2 GeV, the large-R approximation with R = 0.7 almost coincides with the exact result, while R = 0.5 shows small deviations only in the tail of the q_T distribution
- For SIDIS at HERA (sqrt(s) = 318 GeV) with 25 < Q < 50 GeV, the sensitivity to non-perturbative initial-state physics is approximately 5% at low q_T
- For SIDIS at a hypothetical 100-GeV EIC with 10 < Q < 25 GeV, the non-perturbative sensitivity is similarly mild at approximately 5% at low q_T
- The factorization scales are varied by a factor of 2 (0.5) around central values mu = sqrt(s) and mu_0 = 2e^{-gamma_E}/b_T + 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous framework (Gutierrez-Reyes et al., 2018) proving TMD factorization with jets, and supplements their analysis (Gutierrez-Reyes et al., 2019) with numerical predictions. It builds on the Winner-Take-All jet axis definition from Bertolini et al. (2013) and uses the non-perturbative TMD PDF model from Bertone et al. (2019). The framework enables cleaner extraction of TMD parton distribution functions at future colliders like the Electron-Ion Collider, with reduced sensitivity to final-state non-perturbative effects compared to using single hadrons.
