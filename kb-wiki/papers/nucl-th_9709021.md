# Selectivity of the 16O(e,e'pp) reaction to discrete final states

> Resolution of discrete final states in the $^{16}$O(e,e$'$pp)$^{14}$C reaction may provide an interesting tool to discriminate between contributions from one- and two-body currents in this reaction. This is based on the observation that the $0^+$ ground state and first $2^+$ state of $^{14}$C are reached predominantly by the removal of a $^1S_0$ pair from $^{16}$O in this reaction, whereas other states mostly arise by the removal of a $^3P$ pair. This theoretical prediction has been supported recently by an analysis of the pair momentum distribution of the experimental data. In this paper we present results of reaction calculations performed in a direct knock-out framework where final-state interaction and one- and two-body currents are included. The two-nucleon overlap integrals are obtained from a calculation of the two-proton spectral function of $^{16}$O and include both long-range and short-range correlations. The kinematics chosen in the calculations is relevant for recent experiments at NIKHEF and Mainz.

## Key claim

The selectivity of the $^{16}$O(e,e$'$pp) reaction to discrete final states in $^{14}$C can discriminate between one-body and two-body current contributions, but this discrimination relies on the specific angular momentum and parity of the final state: the $0^+$ ground state and first $2^+$ state are reached predominantly by removal of a $^1S_0$ pair (sensitive to short-range correlations via the one-body current), whereas other states arise mostly from $^3P$ pair removal (dominated by the two-body $\Delta$-current).

## Method

The authors performed reaction calculations in a direct knock-out framework for the $^{16}$O(e,e$'$pp)$^{14}$C reaction, including final-state interactions (via optical potentials for each outgoing proton, neglecting mutual interaction) and both one-body and two-body currents (the latter from $\Delta$-isobar excitation with one-pion exchange). The two-nucleon overlap integrals were obtained from a calculation of the two-proton spectral function of $^{16}$O, incorporating both long-range correlations (via a shell-model space up to the $2p1f$ shells) and short-range correlations (via defect functions from the Bethe-Goldstone equation using Bonn-A, Bonn-C, and Reid Soft Core potentials). The kinematics matched experiments at NIKHEF and Mainz, with $E_0 = 584$ MeV, $\omega = 212$ MeV, $q = 300$ MeV/$c$, and $T'_1 = 137$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- The missing energy for the $0^+_1$ ground state of $^{14}$C is $E_{2\rm{m}} = 22.33$ MeV; for the $0^+_2$ state it is $E_{2\rm{m}} = 32.08$ MeV; for the $2^+_1$ state it is $E_{2\rm{m}} = 30$ MeV; for the $2^+_2$ state it is $E_{2\rm{m}} = 35.47$ MeV; for the $1^+$ state it is $E_{2\rm{m}} = 33.64$ MeV.
- The squares of the two-nucleon removal amplitudes for the low-lying states add up to only about 0.6, consistent with the product of two one-nucleon removal spectroscopic factors $(0.75)^2$.
- The one-nucleon removal spectroscopic factor for the $p$ shell of $^{16}$O is reduced by a factor 0.75 compared to the independent-particle shell model, which is about 10% larger than the experimental factor of 0.65.
- The dipole form factor for the $\Delta$-isobar uses a cutoff mass of 855 MeV: $F(q_{\mu}^2) = [1 - q_{\mu}^2/(855\ {\rm MeV})^2]^{-2}$.
- The $\Delta$ resonance mass is $M_{\Delta} = 1232$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the reaction description of Giusti and Pacati (1997) by combining it with the many-body calculation of the two-particle spectral function in $^{16}$O from Allaart et al. (1996), which includes both long-range and short-range correlations consistently. The theoretical prediction that the $0^+$ ground state and first $2^+$ state of $^{14}$C are reached predominantly by $^1S_0$ pair removal, while other states arise from $^3P$ pair removal, was supported experimentally by an analysis of pair momentum distributions at NIKHEF (Onderwater et al., 1996). This selectivity enables future studies to isolate short-range correlations in the $^1S_0$ channel and to calibrate two-body current contributions via the $^3P$ channels, with the longitudinal structure function $f_{00}$ identified as particularly sensitive to the choice of NN potential.
