# Two interacting spins in external fields. Four-level systems

> In the present article, we consider the so-called two-spin equation that describes four-level quantum systems. Recently, these systems attract attention due to their relation to the problem of quantum computation. We study general properties of the two-spin equation and show that the problem for certain external backgrounds can be identified with the problem of one spin in an appropriate background. This allows one to generate a number of exact solutions for two-spin equations on the basis of already known exact solutions of the one-spin equation. Besides, we present some exact solutions for the two-spin equation with an external background different for each spin but having the same direction. We study the eigenvalue problem for a time-independent spin interaction and a time-independent external background. A possible analogue of the Rabi problem for the two-spin equation is defined. We present its exact solution and demonstrate the existence of magnetic resonances in two specific frequencies, one of them coinciding with the Rabi frequency, and the other depending on the rotating field magnitude. The resonance that corresponds to the second frequency is suppressed with respect to the first one.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for certain external backgrounds (equal fields for both spins), the two-spin (four-level) problem reduces exactly to a one-spin (two-level) problem, and that a generalization of the Rabi problem for two interacting spins yields magnetic resonances at two distinct frequencies, one being the standard Rabi frequency and the other depending on the rotating field magnitude, with the second resonance suppressed relative to the first.

## Method

The authors define the two-spin Schrodinger equation for two interacting spin-1/2 particles subject to external vector fields G and F and a spin-spin interaction J(t). They use algebraic properties of Pauli matrices and a specific orthogonal matrix A to relate solutions with swapped fields, and apply a rotating coordinate system transformation to reduce the equal-field case to a one-spin problem. For the Rabi analogue, they introduce a rotating frame that makes the Hamiltonian time-independent, reducing the problem to an eigenvalue equation for constant fields and constant interaction.

## Key numbers

* Rabi frequency for one spin: omega_R^2 = A^2 + (A_0 - omega/2)^2, where A is the rotating field magnitude, A_0 is the constant field component, and omega is the rotation frequency.
* Transition probability between orthogonal one-spin states: |<2|u_F(t)|1>|^2 = [(omega_R/omega)^2 - a_0^2] / (omega_R/omega)^2 * sin^2(omega_R t), with a_0 = A_0/omega - 1/2.
* For the two-spin Rabi analogue with equal fields, the stationary state energies (for A=0) are: lambda_1 = J/2 + 2A_0, lambda_2 = J/2, lambda_3 = -3J/2, lambda_4 = J/2 - 2A_0.
* For the two-spin Rabi analogue with different fields, the problem reduces to a constant matrix D(lambda) with parameters gamma = J/(2omega), a' = A/omega, a_0 = A_0/omega - 1/2, b' = B/omega, b_0 = B_0/omega - 1/2.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior comprehensive study of the one-spin (two-level) equation (Bagrov, Baldiotti, Gitman, Levin 2005) to the two-spin (four-level) case, which is relevant for quantum computation where two-qubit gates correspond to four-level systems. The paper generalizes the classic Rabi problem (Rabi 1937) for magnetic resonance to interacting two-spin systems, and provides exact solutions for 26 types of external fields based on previously known one-spin solutions. The results enable exact treatment of four-level quantum systems in time-dependent fields, with applications to quantum dots and other two-qubit systems.
