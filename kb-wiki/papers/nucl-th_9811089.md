# Duration and non-locality of a nucleon-nucleon collision

> For a set of realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials we evaluate microscopic parameters of binary collisions: a time duration of the scattering state, a mean distance and a rotation of nucleons during a collision. These parameters enter the kinetic equation as non-instantaneous and non-local corrections of the scattering integral, i.e., they can be experimentally tested. Being proportional to off-shell derivatives of the scattering T-matrix, non-instantaneous and non-local corrections make it possible to compare the off-shell behavior of different potentials in a vicinity of the energy shell. The Bonn one-Boson-exchange (A-C) and Paris potentials are found to yield very close results, while the separable Paris potential differs.

# Duration and non-locality of a nucleon-nucleon collision

## Key claim
For realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials, the collision duration, hard-sphere displacement, and rotational displacement can be extracted from off-shell derivatives of the scattering T-matrix; these parameters show that the hard-sphere model is inadequate for any energy region, while the Wigner collision delay approximation covers the dominant non-local corrections at low energies (below 30 MeV).

## Method
The authors evaluate the collision duration Δ_t, hard-sphere displacement Δ_HS, and rotational displacement Δ_φ from the retarded T-matrix using derivatives with respect to energy and momentum, evaluated on the energy shell after differentiation. The T-matrix is decomposed into partial waves (S, P, D waves with coupled channels including ³S₁-³D₁, ³P₂-³F₂, and ³D₃-³G₃) for five nucleon-nucleon potentials: three Bonn one-boson-exchange potentials (A-C), the Paris potential, and the separable Paris potential. The spin-averaged trace element of the T-matrix is used to avoid complications from tensor forces.

## Key numbers
- Collision duration at low energies scales as Δ_t ∝ 1/|κ| (inversely proportional to relative momentum), reaching large negative values; at 80 MeV lab energy and θ=90°, a 1/x-singularity appears due to destructive interference between S and D waves causing T→0.
- Hard-sphere displacement Δ_HS vanishes as |κ|→0 (S-wave dominance); typical values are on the order of 0.5 fm for the parallel component (Δ_∥) at low energies, with the perpendicular component (Δ_⊥) about three times smaller.
- Rotational displacement Δ_φ also vanishes at low energies; for perpendicular scattering at 80 MeV, a 1/x-discontinuity appears, similar to the collision duration.
- The Wigner collision delay yields a parallel displacement Δ_∥ with typical value 0.5 fm; the perpendicular component averages out and is about three times smaller than the parallel component.

## Context
This work extends the Green's function approach to kinetic equations developed by Spička, Lipavský, and Morawetz (1996), which provided a unified framework for non-local and non-instantaneous collisions. It contrasts with the hard-sphere model used by Halbert (1981), Malfliet (1984), and Kortemeyer, Daffin, and Bauer (1996), where collisions are instantaneous and non-local with displacement equal to the hard-sphere diameter. The results enable comparison with the Wigner collision delay approach of Danielewicz and Pratt (1996), who evaluated virial corrections to nuclear matter pressure; the present work shows that the Wigner delay is a good approximation below 30 MeV but fails at higher energies where angular and energy dependencies become too complex for a simple model. The numerical values provide input for non-instantaneous and non-local corrections to the scattering integral in kinetic equations for heavy-ion reaction simulations.
