# Roper excitation in $\vec{p}+α\to \vec{p}+α+X$ reactions

> We calculate differential cross sections and the spin transfer coefficient $D_{nn}$ in the $\vec{p}+α\to \vec{p}+α+π^0$ reaction for proton bombarding energies from 1 to 10 GeV and $π^0 - p$ invariant masses spanning the region of the N$^*$(1440) Roper resonance. Two processes -- $Δ$ excitation in the $α$-particle and Roper excitation in the proton -- are included in an effective reaction model which was shown previously to reproduce existing inclusive spectra. The present calculations demonstrate that these two contributions can be clearly distinguished via $D_{nn}$, even under kinematic conditions where cross sections alone exhibit no clear peak structure due to the excitation of the Roper.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the normal spin transfer coefficient \(D_{nn}\) in exclusive \(\vec{p}+\alpha \to \vec{p}+\alpha+\pi^0\) reactions can clearly distinguish Roper resonance excitation from \(\Delta\) excitation in the \(\alpha\)-particle, even under kinematic conditions where the cross section alone shows no clear peak structure for the Roper.

## Method

The authors use an effective reaction model including two dominant processes: \(\Delta\) excitation in the \(\alpha\)-particle and Roper excitation in the proton, with amplitudes derived from meson-exchange and \(\sigma\)-meson exchange mechanisms. They calculate differential cross sections and the spin transfer coefficient \(D_{nn}\) for proton bombarding energies from 1 to 10 GeV, incorporating proton-\(\alpha\) distortions via a spin-independent eikonal approximation. The calculations are performed for exclusive \(\vec{p}+\alpha \to \vec{p}+\alpha+\pi^0\) reactions under a restricted kinematic condition where the final proton is emitted along the Roper polarization axis in the Roper rest frame, ensuring \(D_{nn}=1\) for the Roper process.

## Key numbers

- Proton bombarding energies from 1 to 10 GeV are considered, spanning the N\(^*\)(1440) Roper resonance region.
- At \(T_p = 1\) GeV and \(\theta_1 = 20^\circ\), the calculated cross section shows only a shoulder rather than a clear Roper peak, but \(D_{nn}\) changes from negative to positive (\(\sim 1\)) in the Roper-dominated energy region.
- For \(T_p = 2\) GeV and \(\theta_1 = 20^\circ\), Roper excitation is manifested clearly in both cross sections and \(D_{nn}\).
- At \(T_p = 10\) GeV, \(D_{nn}\) reaches a maximum value around the Roper peak before decreasing toward higher \(T_{\alpha'}\).
- For the \(T_p=2\) GeV, \(\theta_1=20^\circ\) case, one maintains \(D_{nn} > 0.95\) for the Roper process at its peak if decay protons are detected over a \(\pm 0.5^\circ\) angular and \(\pm 25\) MeV energy range in the laboratory.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of Hirenzaki (1996), which reproduced existing inclusive spectra from Morsch (1992) for \(\alpha\)-p inelastic scattering at \(T_\alpha = 4.2\) GeV, by adding polarization transfer observables to distinguish the two dominant mechanisms. The calculations enable experimental design for coincidence measurements with polarized beams, potentially improving determinations of the Roper resonance mass, width, and decay branching ratios, and can be extended to search for other \(\frac{1}{2}^+\) and \(\frac{1}{2}^-\) nucleon resonances.
