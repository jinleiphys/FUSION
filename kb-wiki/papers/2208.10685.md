# Isospin blocking and its effects in heavy-ion collisions

> A concept of \emph{isospin blocking} in the process of isospin diffusion in heavy-ion collisions is raised. Generally, in the process of isospin diffusion, isospin asymmetry would diffuse from the place with large asymmetry to the place with small asymmetry. However, our study shows that the isospin diffusion could be blocked in case the local value of the symmetry energy is larger. We dub this phenomenon ``isospin blocking''. To check this behavior, in the framework of an Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, isospin diffusions in the isotope Sn+Sn reactions at 270 MeV/nucleon are studied. It is shown that the value of the after-diffusion asymmetry is distinctly blocked if the local symmetry energy is large. The effects of the isospin blocking on the isospin asymmetry of dilute and dense matter and the final $π^-/π^+$ ratio in heavy ion collisions are demonstrated.

# Isospin blocking and its effects in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The authors propose that isospin diffusion in heavy-ion collisions can be blocked when the local symmetry energy is large, a phenomenon they term "isospin blocking." This effect means that the double π⁻/π⁺ ratio, often used as a probe of the high-density symmetry energy, is also influenced by the low-density symmetry energy through recoil effects, particularly when the high-density symmetry energy is rather soft.

## Method
The authors used the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with a Skyrme-type parametrization for the isoscalar mean field and a density-dependent symmetry energy of the form E_sym(ρ) = 32(ρ/ρ₀)^γ. They fixed a mildly soft high-density symmetry energy (γ = 0.5 for ρ ≥ ρ₀) and varied the low-density symmetry energy between a strong form (γ = 0.5, large symmetry energy) and a weak form (γ = 1, small symmetry energy) for ρ < ρ₀. Central collisions of ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn and ¹⁰⁸Sn+¹¹²Sn at 270 MeV/nucleon were simulated to study isospin asymmetry distributions and the double π⁻/π⁺ ratio.

## Key numbers
- Incident beam energy: 270 MeV/nucleon for Sn+Sn reactions.
- Symmetry energy parametrization: E_sym(ρ) = 32(ρ/ρ₀)^γ, with γ = 0.5 (strong low-density symmetry energy) and γ = 1 (weak low-density symmetry energy) for ρ < ρ₀.
- Isoscalar mean field potential: U(ρ) = -232(ρ/ρ₀) + 179(ρ/ρ₀)^1.3.
- The double π⁻/π⁺ ratio increases monotonously as the high-density symmetry energy becomes soft, except when a flat high-density symmetry energy and a weak low-density symmetry energy are used simultaneously, where the trend reverses.

## Context
This work extends the study of isospin diffusion and fractionation in heavy-ion collisions, building on prior theoretical work by Shi (2000, 2003) and Lin and Pan (2002) on isospin equilibrium conditions between liquid and gas phases. The concept of isospin blocking is introduced to explain how the low-density symmetry energy can affect observables intended to probe the high-density symmetry energy, such as the π⁻/π⁺ ratio measured by the SπRIT collaboration (Estee 2021, Jhang 2021). The study is directly relevant to interpreting recent Sn+Sn reaction data at 270 MeV/nucleon and may help constrain the high-density symmetry energy by accounting for the interplay between low- and high-density symmetry energies.
