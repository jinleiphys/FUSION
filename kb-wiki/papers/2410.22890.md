# Possibility of quantum Hall effect in dense quark matter environments: A chiral model approach

> A high baryon density and strong magnetic fields are expected in peripheral collisions in heavy ion collision experiments, such as the upcoming CBM experiment at FAIR in Germany and NICA in Russia. Such densities are also likely in the core of massive neutron stars, possibly with mixed quark-hadron phases. We employed the chiral effective model to obtain the constituent quark mass in this non-perturbative QCD regime. A quantized version of conductivity and resistivity is found reliable in the quantum domain of low density and high magnetic fields. Landau quantization gives rise to phenomena similar to SdH oscillations and quantum Hall effect in this regime. We have used a density-dependent magnetic field to observe SdH-type oscillations and the possibility of the quantum Hall effect in the interior of neutron stars where the magnetic field varies as a function of the baryon density. Our results indicate the possibility of observing the quantum Hall effect in a neutron star environment.

## Key claim
The paper suggests that quantum Hall effect (QHE) and Shubnikov-de Haas (SdH) oscillations could be observable in neutron star interiors, but only within the limited density range where quarks remain in the normal (non-superconducting) phase with gapless dispersion relations, and the quantized patterns are expected to diminish as one approaches the CBM/NICA experimental regimes.

## Method
The authors employed the chiral effective model to compute density-dependent constituent quark masses from quark condensates, then used the relaxation time approximation of kinetic theory to calculate electrical conductivity and resistivity tensors. They derived both classical and quantized (Landau-quantized) expressions for the conductivity components at zero temperature (T=1 MeV, representing neutron star cores) and at finite temperature (T=100 MeV, representing CBM/NICA conditions), using a density-dependent magnetic field that varies as a function of baryon density in the neutron star interior.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark mass in vacuum: M_q(ρ_B=0) = 313 MeV, taken as one-third of the nucleon mass
- Vacuum quark condensate: [⟨q̄q⟩(ρ_B=0)]^{1/3} = 263.5 MeV
- Temperature for neutron star conditions: T = 1 MeV
- Temperature for CBM/NICA conditions: T = 100 MeV
- Baryon density range considered: ρ = 0 to ρ = 4ρ_0, where ρ_0 is nuclear saturation density
- Magnetic field strength on neutron star surface: 10^12 G to 10^15 G
- Net baryon density expected at CBM: 2-6 times nuclear saturation density
- Coupling constants used: g_σp = g_σn = 10.567, g_ζp = g_ζn = -0.467
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93.3 MeV; kaon decay constant: f_K = 122.143 MeV
- Pion mass: m_π = 139 MeV; kaon mass: m_K = 498 MeV

## Context
This work extends preliminary estimates by Dey et al. (2021) that considered massless quark matter, by using the chiral effective model to provide a more realistic, non-perturbative QCD estimation of electrical conductivity with density-dependent constituent quark masses. The study builds on prior work on transport coefficients in dense QCD matter at finite magnetic fields by Ghosh et al. (2018-2024), Dey et al. (2019-2021), and others, and on magnetohydrodynamics descriptions of neutron star plasmas by Sedrakian (1987), Potekhin (1999), and Chamel (2008). The results enable future comparison with observations of neutron star magnetospheres and predictions for the upcoming CBM experiment at FAIR and NICA at JINR.
