# Scattering matrix pole expansions for complex wavenumbers in $R$-matrix theory

> In this follow-up article to [Shadow poles in the alternative parametrization of R-matrix theory, Ducru (2020)], we establish new results on scattering matrix pole expansions for complex wavenumbers in R-matrix theory. In the past, two branches of theoretical formalisms emerged to describe the scattering matrix in nuclear physics: R-matrix theory, and pole expansions. The two have been quite isolated from one another. Recently, our study of Brune's alternative parametrization of R-matrix theory has shown the need to extend the scattering matrix (and the underlying R-matrix operators) to complex wavenumbers. Two competing ways of doing so have emerged from a historical ambiguity in the definitions of the shift $\boldsymbol{S}$ and penetration $\boldsymbol{P}$ functions: the legacy Lane \& Thomas "force closure" approach, versus analytic continuation (which is the standard in mathematical physics). The R-matrix community has not yet come to a consensus as to which to adopt for evaluations in standard nuclear data libraries, such as ENDF. In this article, we argue in favor of analytic continuation of R-matrix operators. We bridge R-matrix theory with the Humblet-Rosenfeld pole expansions, and unveil new properties of the Siegert-Humblet radioactive poles and widths, including their invariance properties to changes in channel radii $a_c$. We then show that analytic continuation of R-matrix operators preserves important physical and mathematical properties of the scattering matrix -- cancelling spurious poles and guaranteeing generalized unitarity -- while still being able to close channels below thresholds.

## Key claim

The paper argues that analytic continuation of R-matrix operators to complex wavenumbers (rather than the legacy Lane and Thomas "force closure" approach) should become the standard for nuclear cross section evaluations, because it bridges R-matrix theory with Humblet-Rosenfeld pole expansions, cancels spurious poles, and guarantees generalized unitarity while still allowing channels to close below thresholds.

## Method

The authors establish the Siegert-Humblet expansion into radioactive states as the link between R-matrix theory and scattering matrix pole expansions, deriving the Kapur-Peierls operator's Mittag-Leffler expansion in terms of complex poles and residues. They then prove theorems on the invariance of Siegert-Humblet radioactive parameters under changes in channel radius, and compare the analytic continuation approach against the Lane and Thomas force-closure method for extending the scattering matrix to complex wavenumbers.

## Key numbers

- The Siegert-Humblet radioactive poles are decomposed as $\mathcal{E}_j \triangleq E_j - \mathrm{i}\frac{\Gamma_j}{2}$, where poles on the positive semi-axis of purely-imaginary $k_c \in \mathrm{i}\mathbb{R}_+$ correspond to bound states with $\Gamma_j = 0$, while all other poles on the lower-half $k_c$ plane have $\Gamma_j > 0$ (resonance or radioactively decaying states).
- The Wronskian condition for all channels is $w_c \triangleq O_c^{(1)}I_c - I_c^{(1)}O_c = 2\mathrm{i}$, expressed as $\boldsymbol{w} = 2\mathrm{i} \Id{}$.
- The Mittag-Leffler expansion of the reduced logarithmic derivative $L_c(\rho)/\rho$ is given by $\frac{-\ell}{\rho} + \mathrm{i} + \sum_{n \geq 1} \frac{1}{\rho-\omega_n}$, where $\{\omega_n\}$ are the roots of the outgoing wavefunctions $O_c(\omega_n) = 0$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of Brune's alternative parametrization of R-matrix theory (Ducru, 2020), which showed the need to extend scattering matrix operators to complex wavenumbers. The paper bridges the historically isolated branches of R-matrix theory (Wigner-Eisenbud, Kapur-Peierls) and scattering matrix pole expansions (Humblet and Rosenfeld, 1961-1965), and has practical implications for evaluation codes such as EDA, SAMMY, and AZURE used to build nuclear data libraries (ENDF, JEFF, BROND, JENDL, CENDL, TENDL). The results enable a resolution to the ambiguity between the Lane and Thomas force-closure approach and analytic continuation for sub-threshold channel closure.
