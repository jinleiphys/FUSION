# Shape transitions in odd-mass $γ$-soft nuclei within the interacting boson-fermion model based on the Gogny energy density functional

> The interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM), with parameters determined from the microscopic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation, based on the parametrization D1M of the Gogny energy density functional (EDF), is employed to study the structural evolution in odd-mass $γ$-soft nuclei. The deformation energy surfaces of even-even nuclei, single-particle energies and occupation probabilities of the corresponding odd-mass systems have been obtained within the constrained HFB approach. Those building blocks are then used as a microscopic input to build the IBFM Hamiltonian. The coupling constants of the boson-fermion interaction terms are taken as free parameters, fitted to reproduce experimental low-lying spectra. The diagonalization of the IBFM Hamiltonian provides the spectroscopic properties for the studied odd-mass nuclei. The procedure has been applied to compute low-energy excitation spectra and electromagnetic transition rates, in the case of the $γ$-soft odd-mass systems $^{129-137}$Ba, $^{127-135}$Xe, $^{129-137}$La and $^{127-135}$Cs. The calculations provide a reasonable agreement with the available experimental data and agree well with previous results based on the relativistic mean-field approximation.

## Key claim

The interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) with parameters determined from the Gogny-D1M energy density functional provides a reasonable description of shape transitions in odd-mass γ-soft nuclei in the A≈130 mass region, but the coupling constants of the boson-fermion interaction terms must be treated as free parameters fitted to experimental low-lying spectra, and the monopole strength parameter for positive-parity states must be allowed to be j-dependent to better reproduce the ordering of energy levels.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1M energy density functional to obtain deformation energy surfaces for even-even core nuclei (128-136Ba and 126-134Xe), along with single-particle energies and occupation probabilities for the corresponding odd-mass systems. These microscopic inputs were used to determine the parameters of the interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian via a fermion-to-boson mapping procedure, while the boson-fermion coupling constants (Γ0, Λ0, and A0) were fitted separately for positive- and negative-parity states to reproduce experimental excitation spectra. The full IBFM Hamiltonian was then diagonalized in a spherical basis to compute spectroscopic properties for the odd-mass nuclei 129-137Ba, 127-135Xe, 129-137La, and 127-135Cs.

## Key numbers

- The IBM Hamiltonian parameters for the boson core nuclei include εd values ranging from 0.120 MeV (128Ba) to 1.000 MeV (136Ba and 134Xe), κ values from -0.079 (126Xe) to -0.090 (136Ba and 134Xe), and χ values from -0.10 (132Ba) to -0.87 (134Xe)
- The effective fermionic charge was taken as eFπ = 0.25 e b for protons and eFν = 0.125 e b for neutrons
- The gs gyro-magnetic factor was quenched by 30% for both protons and neutrons from the Schmidt values (gs = 5.58 μN2 for protons, gs = -3.82 μN2 for neutrons)
- The 7/2+1 and 9/2+1 states in 129Ba are almost purely (96% and 97%, respectively) composed of the 1g7/2 configuration
- The positive-parity ground state spin changes from Jπ = 1/2+ to 3/2+ at neutron number N=79 for Ba isotopes

## Context

This work extends the method previously developed by Nomura et al. (2016) for axially-deformed odd-mass Sm and Eu nuclei to γ-soft odd-mass nuclei in the A≈130 mass region, and complements a parallel study by the same authors using the relativistic DD-PC1 energy density functional (Nomura et al., 2017). The results agree well with those obtained from the relativistic mean-field approximation and with available experimental data, while also showing consistency with earlier phenomenological IBFM calculations by Cunningham (1982), Alonso (1984, 1987), Arias (1985), and Dellagiacoma (1988). The approach enables systematic spectroscopic studies of odd-mass nuclei without the computational demands of fully microscopic generator coordinate method calculations, which have been mainly limited to light nuclei as demonstrated by Bally (2014) and Borrajo (2016).
