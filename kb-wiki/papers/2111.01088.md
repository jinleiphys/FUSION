# Asymptotics of transverse momentum broadening in dense QCD media

> We study the asymptotic behaviour of the transverse momentum broadening distribution of an energetic quark or gluon propagating through dense QCD matter, in the large system size $L$ limit, taking into account radiative corrections in the double logarithmic approximation. Thanks to a connection between the evolution of the jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ and the formation of traveling wave fronts in nonlinear physics, we obtain a formula for the $L$ dependence of the characteristic transverse momentum scale $Q_s$ of the distribution valid up to terms of order $1/\ln(L)$. We briefly discuss the physical implications of this formula for jet quenching and small-$x$ phenomenology.

## Key claim

The paper derives an asymptotic expansion for the saturation scale \(Q_s\) that controls the transverse momentum broadening distribution, valid up to corrections of order \(1/\ln(L)\), by exploiting an analogy between the evolution of the jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) and traveling wave fronts in nonlinear physics. The result is universal, meaning it does not depend on the tree-level initial condition for the broadening distribution.

## Method

The author studies the nonlinear system formed by the double-logarithmic-approximation evolution equation for \(\hat{q}(Y,\rho)\) and the saturation condition \(\hat{q}(\tau, Q_s^2(\tau))\tau = Q_s^2(\tau)\). Using the traveling wave formalism (Ebert and Van Saarloos method), the author performs a "front interior expansion" and a "leading edge expansion" around the scaling limit, matches them, and solves the resulting triangular system of differential equations to extract the asymptotic expansion of the front velocity \(d\rho_s/dY\).

## Key numbers

* The front velocity in the double logarithmic approximation is \(c \simeq 1 + 2\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_s}\), where \(\bar{\alpha}_s = \alpha_s N_c/\pi\).
* The first sub-asymptotic correction to the saturation scale is \(b_1 = -3c/(1+c)\), giving a term \(-[3c/(1+c)]\ln(Y)\) in \(\rho_s(Y)\).
* The second sub-asymptotic correction is \(b_2 = 3c\sqrt{2\pi(c-1)}/(1+c)^2\), giving a term \(-[6c\sqrt{2\pi(c-1)}/(1+c)^2](1/\sqrt{Y})\) in \(\rho_s(Y)\).
* The typical width of the transverse momentum distribution scales as \(\langle k_\perp^2 \rangle \propto L^{c}\) with \(c > 1\), indicating super-diffusive behavior faster than Brownian diffusion.
* The transverse momentum distribution at large \(k_\perp\) has a power-law tail \(\mathcal{P}(k_\perp) \propto 1/k_\perp^{4-2\beta}\) with \(\beta \simeq \sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_s}\), which is weaker than the Rutherford tail \(1/k_\perp^4\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of radiative corrections to the jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) initiated by Liou, Mueller, and Wu (2013), Blaizot, Iancu, and Mehtar-Tani (2014), and Iancu (2014). It differs from the approach of Iancu (2014) and Mueller (2016) by using a lower boundary \(\rho_s(Y)\) in the evolution integral instead of \(Y\), leading to a parametrically different result of order \(\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_s}\ln\ln L\). The result provides a pQCD-motivated functional form for initial conditions to the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation in small-\(x\) physics, analogous to the geometric scaling property of the gluon distribution found by Stasto, Golec-Biernat, and Kwiecinski (2000).
