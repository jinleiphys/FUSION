# Fermionic Symmetries: Extension of the two to one Relationship Between the Spectra of Even-Even and Neighbouring Odd mass Nuclei

> In the single j shell there is a two to one relationship between the spectra of certain even-even and neighbouring odd mass nuclei e.g. the calculated energy levels of J=0^+ states in ^{44}Ti are at twice the energies of corresponding levels in ^{43}Ti(^{43}Sc) with J=j=7/2. Here an approximate extension of the relationship is made by adopting a truncated seniority scheme i.e. for ^{46}Ti and ^{45}Sc we get the relationship if we do not allow the seniority v=4 states to mix with the v=0 and v=2 states. Better than that, we get very close to the two to one relationship if seniority v=4 states are admixed perturbatively. In addition, it is shown that the higher isospin states do not contain seniority 4 admixtures.

## Key claim

The two-to-one energy relationship between certain even-even and odd-mass nuclei, previously established exactly for the pair (⁴⁴Ti, ⁴³Sc) in the single j = 7/2 shell, can be extended approximately to the pair (⁴⁶Ti, ⁴⁵Sc) by truncating to seniority v = 0 and v = 2 states; the relationship holds even more closely when seniority v = 4 states are admixed perturbatively, because the mixing matrix elements and energy denominators both scale by a factor of two.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations in the f₇/₂ single-j configuration space for the pairs (⁴⁴Ti, ⁴³Sc) and (⁴⁶Ti, ⁴⁵Sc), using the MBZ (McCullen, Bayman, Zamick) interaction. They compared the Hamiltonian matrix elements for J=0 states in the even-even nucleus with those for J=j=7/2 states in the odd-mass nucleus, showing analytically that each matrix element in the even-even case is twice the corresponding element in the odd-mass case when the basis is restricted to seniority v=0 and v=2 states. They then treated seniority v=4 admixtures perturbatively and demonstrated that the two-to-one ratio is preserved to first order.

## Key numbers

- For the pair (⁴⁸Ti, ⁴⁹Ti), the experimental T=4 J=0 excitation energy in ⁴⁸Ti is 17.379 MeV and the T=7/2 J=j=7/2⁻ excitation energy in ⁴⁹Ti is 8.724 MeV; the deviation from a two-to-one relation is -0.40%.
- For the pair (⁵²Fe, ⁵³Fe), the experimental T=2 and T=3/2 excitation energies are 8.559 MeV and 4.25 MeV respectively; the percent deviation from two-to-one is 0.69%.
- For the pair (⁴⁶Ti, ⁴⁷Ti), the percent deviation from two-to-one is -1.54%.
- For the pair (⁴⁴Ti, ⁴⁶Ti), the percent deviation from two-to-one is -0.98%.
- For the pair (⁵⁰Cr, ⁵¹Cr), the T=3 – T=1 splitting in ⁵⁰Cr is 13.222 MeV and the T=5/2 – T=3/2 splitting in ⁵¹Cr is 6.611 MeV; the two-to-one relationship holds to four significant digits.
- The calculated seniority v=4 admixture in the ground state of ⁴⁶Ti is 2.232% and in ⁴⁵Sc is 2.335%.

## Context

This work extends the exact two-to-one relationship first noted by McCullen, Bayman, and Zamick (MBZ, 1964) for the (⁴⁴Ti, ⁴³Sc) pair to nuclei with more valence particles, specifically (⁴⁶Ti, ⁴⁵Sc). It builds on the observation by de-Shalit and Talmi (1963) and Talmi (1993) that for a two-body effective interaction in the f₇/₂ shell, the spectra of seniority v=0 and v=2 states in (f₇/₂)ⁿ configurations are nearly identical. The work also connects to experimental comparisons by Zamick and Zheng (1992) and to a simple isospin-dependent interaction noted by Sherr (private communication). The results enable further tests of seniority-based symmetries in medium-mass nuclei and suggest that the two-to-one relationship may hold more broadly than the exact single-j case.
