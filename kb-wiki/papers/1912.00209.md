# A study of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ polarization splitting by meson field in PICR hydrodynamic model

> With the PICR hydrodynamic model, we study the polarization splitting between $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ at RHIC BES energy range, based on the meson field mechanism. Our results fit to the experimental data fairly well. Besides, two unexpected effect emerges: (1) the baryon density gradient has non-trivial and negative contribution to the polarization splitting; (2) for 7.7 GeV Au+Au collisions within the centrality range of 20\%-50\%, the polarization splitting surprisingly increases with the centrality decreases. The second effect might help to explain the significant signal of polarization splitting measured in STAR's Au+Au 7.7 Gev collisions.

## Key claim

The meson-field mechanism, with the free parameter removed and vorticity explicitly included, can reproduce the experimentally observed polarization splitting between Λ and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) at RHIC BES energies, but only if the baryon density gradient term (which gives a negative contribution) and the centrality-dependent freeze-out time are both accounted for; the model yields a splitting as large as 3.5% at 7.7 GeV, exceeding previous upper-bound estimates.

## Method

The authors used the (3+1)D Particle-In-Cell Relativistic (PICR) hydrodynamic model with an initial state of slab-slab collisions and Yang-Mills flux-tubes to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\)–200 GeV. They modified the polarization splitting formula from Csernai et al. (2019) by removing the free parameter \(C\) and explicitly including the vorticity and the baryon density gradient term \(\vec{\nabla}\rho_{\rm B} \times \vec{v}\). The freeze-out time was varied from 5.9 fm/c to 7.9 fm/c across the energy range to match the average freeze-out temperature to theoretical and experimental values, and the polarization splitting was computed from the meson-field-induced Zeeman term in the Foldy-Wouthuysen Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

* For Au+Au 7.7 GeV collisions at centrality \(c = 49\%\) (\(b_0 = 0.7\)), the polarization splitting \(\Delta P_J\) is 3.49%, with vorticity-only contribution \(\Delta P_{\omega} = 4.39\%\) and baryon density gradient contribution \(\Delta P_{\rho}\) negative.
* For Au+Au 7.7 GeV at centrality \(c = 20\%\) (\(b_0 = 0.45\)), \(\Delta P_J = 4.28\%\) and \(\Delta P_{\omega} = 4.49\%\), showing that splitting increases with decreasing centrality (more central collisions give larger splitting).
* For Au+Au 11.5 GeV at \(c = 49\%\), \(\Delta P_J = 1.33\%\) and \(\Delta P_{\omega} = 2.23\%\); at \(c = 20\%\), \(\Delta P_J = 0.83\%\) and \(\Delta P_{\omega} = 1.43\%\), showing the opposite centrality trend.
* The average freeze-out temperature at 7.7 GeV is 133.8–134.8 MeV across centralities 20%–49%; at 11.5 GeV it is 141.5–142.2 MeV.
* The average \(y\)-directed vorticity \(\langle -\omega_y \rangle\) at freeze-out for 7.7 GeV ranges from 0.140 fm\(^{-1}\) (20% centrality) to 0.156 fm\(^{-1}\) (49% centrality).
* The correction to global \(\Lambda\) polarization from the splitting effect is about 5%–20% for most energies, but about 30% at 7.7 GeV (e.g., at 11.5 GeV, splitting 1.33% vs. polarization 3.65% gives an 18% correction).

## Context

This work extends the meson-field mechanism for polarization splitting proposed by Csernai et al. (2019) by removing the free parameter and explicitly incorporating vorticity and baryon density gradients. It contrasts with the magnetic-field mechanism of Guo et al. (2019), which gave an upper limit below 1% at 7.7 GeV, whereas the present model achieves 3.5% at that energy. The results enable future investigations into why the centrality dependence of freeze-out time reverses below 11.5 GeV, and whether the PICR model remains valid at 7.7 GeV or whether different hydrodynamics are needed.
