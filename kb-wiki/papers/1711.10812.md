# Quantum and Classical Dynamics of Heavy Quarks in a Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We derive equations for the time evolution of the reduced density matrix of a collection of heavy quarks and antiquarks immersed in a quark gluon plasma. These equations, in their original form, rely on two approximations: the weak coupling between the heavy quarks and the plasma, the fast response of the plasma to the perturbation caused by the heavy quarks. An additional semi-classical approximation is performed. This allows us to recover results previously obtained for the abelian plasma using the influence functional formalism. In the case of QCD, specific features of the color dynamics make the implementation of the semi-classical approximation more involved. We explore two approximate strategies to solve numerically the resulting equations in the case of a quark-antiquark pair. One involves Langevin equations with additional random color forces, the other treats the transition between the singlet and octet color configurations as collisions in a Boltzmann equation which can be solved with Monte Carlo techniques.

## Key claim

The paper derives a quantum master equation for the reduced density matrix of heavy quarks in a quark-gluon plasma, but the semi-classical approximation that yields Fokker-Planck and Langevin equations works straightforwardly only for abelian plasmas; for QCD, the discrete nature of color changes prevents a simple semi-classical limit, requiring two approximate numerical strategies that are valid only in specific regimes.

## Method

The authors start from the full Hamiltonian of heavy quarks interacting with a quark-gluon plasma via Coulomb-gauge gluon exchange, then trace out the plasma degrees of freedom using perturbation theory to second order in the coupling, assuming weak coupling and fast plasma response. They obtain a Markovian master equation for the reduced density matrix, then perform a semi-classical expansion in the heavy quark mass to derive Fokker-Planck and Langevin equations for the abelian case. For the non-abelian QCD case, they implement two approximate numerical approaches: one using Langevin equations with additional random color forces (valid near a maximum-entropy color state), and another treating singlet-octet color transitions as collisions in a Boltzmann equation solved with Monte Carlo techniques.

## Key numbers

- The heavy quark mass satisfies \(M \gg T\), making the thermal wavelength \(\lambda_{\rm th} \sim 1/\sqrt{MT}\) small compared to the microscopic length scale \(\sim 1/T\).
- The typical heavy quark thermal velocity is \(\sim \sqrt{T/M} \ll 1\).
- The plasma frequency and Debye screening mass \(m_D\) set the characteristic plasma time scale \(\sim m_D^{-1}\); during this time the heavy quark moves a distance small compared to \(m_D^{-1}\).
- The typical momentum transfer in collisions is \(q \lesssim m_D \ll M\), with energy transfer \(\sim q^2/M \sim m_D^2/M \ll m_D\).
- The time integrals over plasma correlators yield the zero-frequency retarded propagator \(\Delta^R(\omega=0,\mathbf{r})\) and the less-than propagator \(\Delta^<(\omega=0,\mathbf{r})\), identified as the real part \(V(\mathbf{r})\) and imaginary part \(W(\mathbf{r})\) of a complex potential.

## Context

This work extends the influence functional formalism previously applied to abelian plasmas by Blaizot (2015) to the non-abelian QCD case, building on open quantum systems approaches to heavy quark dynamics in quark-gluon plasmas. It connects to prior work by Akamatsu (2012) using a similar formalism, and to the broader literature on quarkonium dissociation via screening (Matsui, 1986), recombination (Braun-Munzinger, 2000; Thews, 2000), and imaginary potential approaches (Laine, 2006; Beraudo, 2007; Brambilla, 2008; Escobedo, 2008; Rothkopf, 2011). The results enable time-dependent, out-of-equilibrium treatments of heavy quark-antiquark pairs, including both dissociation and recombination, which previous static potential models and rate-equation approaches could not fully address.
