# The Chiral Separation Effect from lattice QCD at the physical point

> In this paper we study the Chiral Separation Effect by means of first-principles lattice QCD simulations. For the first time in the literature, we determine the continuum limit of the associated conductivity using 2+1 flavors of dynamical staggered quarks at physical masses. The results reveal a suppression of the conductivity in the confined phase and a gradual enhancement toward the perturbative value for high temperatures. In addition to our dynamical setup, we also investigate the impact of the quenched approximation on the conductivity, using both staggered and Wilson quarks. Finally, we highlight the relevance of employing conserved vector and anomalous axial currents in the lattice simulations.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD determination of the Chiral Separation Effect (CSE) conductivity using 2+1 flavors of dynamical staggered quarks at physical quark masses, finding that the conductivity is suppressed in the confined phase and approaches the perturbative free-quark value at high temperatures, with the caveat that the result is obtained via a leading-order Taylor expansion in the chemical potential around zero density.

## Method

The CSE conductivity coefficient was extracted from the leading-order Taylor expansion of the axial current expectation value with respect to the baryon chemical potential at zero density, evaluated on lattice QCD configurations with background magnetic fields. The simulations used rooted staggered quarks with physical up, down, and strange quark masses, employing point-split conserved vector and anomalous axial currents, with the conductivity obtained from the linear slope of the current derivative as a function of the magnetic field. Additional quenched calculations were performed using both staggered and Wilson fermions, and free-fermion benchmarks were computed analytically and numerically to validate the lattice current definitions.

## Key numbers

* For free fermions at m/T = 0, the CSE conductivity approaches the analytic value 1/(2π²) in the continuum and thermodynamic limits, with finite-size effects becoming negligible for aspect ratio LT ≥ 4.
* The quenched QCD critical temperature is T_c^q ≈ 270 MeV, around which the CSE conductivity transitions from suppression to approaching the massless free-fermion value.
* Full QCD results were obtained on lattices including 24³ × 6, 24³ × 8, 28³ × 10, 36³ × 12, and zero-temperature ensembles 24³ × 32 and 32³ × 48, covering temperatures from below the QCD crossover at T_c ≈ 150 MeV up to high temperatures.
* At high temperatures above T_c ≈ 150 MeV, the CSE conductivity approaches the perturbative value corresponding to free massless quarks, consistent with asymptotic freedom.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of the CSE that were limited to two-color QCD or the quenched approximation (Puhr 2016, Buividovich 2020), and provides the first fully non-perturbative result at physical quark masses in full QCD. The methodology enables future lattice studies of related anomalous transport phenomena such as the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME), for which most previous lattice simulations (Buividovich 2009, Yamamoto 2011, Bali 2014) were either indirect or not performed in full QCD at the physical point.
