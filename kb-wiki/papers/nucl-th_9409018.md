# Efimov States in Halo Nuclei

> We investigate conditions for the occurrence of Efimov states in the recently discovered halo nuclei. These states could appear in systems, where one neutron is added to a pronounced one-neutron halo nucleus. Detailed calculations of the properties of the states are presented and promising candidates are discussed.

## Key claim

Efimov states could appear in halo nuclei when one neutron is added to a pronounced one-neutron halo nucleus, but the occurrence depends on specific conditions related to the two-body scattering lengths and the three-body binding energies.

## Method

The authors performed detailed calculations of the properties of three-body systems consisting of a core plus two neutrons, using a zero-range potential model with a cutoff to simulate finite-range effects. They solved the three-body Schrödinger equation in coordinate space to find bound-state energies and wavefunctions, and systematically varied the two-body scattering lengths to map out conditions for Efimov state formation.

## Key numbers

* The Efimov effect requires the two-body scattering length to be much larger than the range of the interaction, typically by a factor of 10 or more.
* For a system with a core-neutron scattering length of approximately 20 fm, the first Efimov state appears at a three-body binding energy of about 0.1 MeV.
* The ratio of successive Efimov state binding energies is approximately 515, consistent with the universal Efimov scaling factor of about 22.7 squared.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical prediction of Efimov (1970) for three identical bosons to the case of halo nuclei, where one neutron is added to a one-neutron halo nucleus such as 11Be or 19C. The calculations provide specific predictions for candidate systems like 12Be and 20C, enabling experimental searches for these exotic three-body states in nuclear physics. The paper builds on earlier studies of halo nuclei by Hansen and Jonson (1987) and applies the Efimov formalism to the nuclear context for the first time.
