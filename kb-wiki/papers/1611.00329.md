# Collectivity in small collision systems : an initial state perspective

> Measurements of multi-particle correlations in the collisions of small systems such as $p+p$, $p/d/^3He+A$ show striking similarity to the observations in heavy ion collisions. A number of observables measured in the high multiplicity events of these systems resemble features that are attributed to collectivity driven by hydrodynamics. However alternative explanations based on initial state dynamics are able to describe many characteristic features of these measurements. In this brief review we highlight some of the recent developments and outstanding issues in this direction.

## Key claim

This review argues that initial-state dynamics within the Color-Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory can describe many characteristic features of long-range azimuthal correlations observed in high-multiplicity p+p, p/d/³He+A collisions, but the relative importance of initial-state versus final-state (hydrodynamic) effects depends on event multiplicity, with a crossover expected around dN/dy ∼ 100 based on weak-coupling estimates.

## Method

The authors review theoretical calculations of multi-particle production in the CGC framework, focusing on perturbative computations using k⊥-factorization with unintegrated gluon distributions (UGDs) derived from the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution equation. They describe how two-particle correlations arise from two distinct contributions: "Jet graphs" (back-to-back di-jets from single hard scatterings) and "Glasma graphs" (multi-parton processes producing symmetric correlations around Δφ=π/2), with the relative strength of Glasma graphs enhanced by a factor of αS⁻⁴ in high-multiplicity events.

## Key numbers

- Crossover between initial-state and final-state dominated regimes estimated at dN/dy ∼ 100, derived from equilibration time τeq/R ≃ √(100/(dN/dy))
- Typical min-bias multiplicities at LHC: dN/dy ∼ 6 for p+p and ∼ 17 for p+Pb collisions
- Equilibration time estimate: Qs τeq ≃ 10 for (η/s)Teq ≃ 5/4π at coupling g²Nc ≃ 10
- Multiplicity relation: dN/dy ≃ ξ Qs² π R² with ξ ≃ 1/4

## Context

This review extends the ongoing debate on the origin of collectivity in small collision systems, triggered by CMS ridge observations in p+p at 7 TeV (Khachatryan 2010) and subsequent measurements in p+Pb, d+Au, and ³He+Au at LHC and RHIC. It contrasts initial-state explanations (CGC-based approaches by Kovner, Levin, Dusling, Dumitru, Lappi, and others) with final-state hydrodynamic descriptions (Bozek, Bzdak, Werner, Schenke, and others), noting that similar debates occurred in the 1990s regarding Tevatron p+p̄ data. The work enables future systematic comparisons between theory and experiment to map the boundaries between initial-state and final-state dominated multiplicity regimes.
