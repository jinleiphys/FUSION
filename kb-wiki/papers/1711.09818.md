# Phase transitions for rotational states within an algebraic cluster model

> The catastrophe theory, an effective method for the description of phase transitions, is applied to the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) as an example of a non-trivial theory. The ground state and excited, rotational phase transitions are investigated. A short introduction to the SACM and the catastrophe theory is given. We apply the formalism to the test case of 16O+alpha-->20Ne.

## Key claim

The paper applies catastrophe theory to the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) to classify phase transitions in both the ground state and excited rotational states, using the test case of 16O+alpha→20Ne. The authors hedge that the method is demonstrated for a specific system and parameter choices (N=12, n0=8) and that the general structure of the separatrix is maintained independent of the particular application.

## Method

The semi-classical potential of the SACM Hamiltonian is obtained via a coherent state expectation value, expressed in terms of control parameters A, B, C, D, and Omega that are functions of the Hamiltonian parameters. Catastrophe theory is applied by expanding the potential around the fundamental root alpha=0, identifying the germ (the point of maximum degeneracy) at (rho2, rho1, D) = (12/19, -4/3, 0), and then constructing the separatrix from bifurcation sets and Maxwell sets within the essential parameter space. The bifurcation set for non-zero alpha critical points and the Maxwell set for shape coexistence are derived using mapping singularities, and the resulting separatrix divides the parameter space into regions of different shape stability.

## Key numbers

* For the test case 16O+alpha→20Ne, the Wildermuth condition gives n0=8 and N=12 is chosen.
* The germ point in the essential parameter space is located at (rho2, rho1, D) = (12/19, -4/3, 0).
* The asymptotic value of the potential at the germ is V(alpha→∞, v=0) = 11/57.
* The bifurcation set C0 is given by the line rho1c(rho2) = -19/16 rho2 - 7/12.
* The Maxwell set within the (D, rho1) sub-space at constant rho2=1.12324 shows a change in curvature at D=0.21, separating spherical-deformed coexistence from deformed-deformed coexistence.

## Context

This work extends previous pedestrian studies of quantum phase transitions in the SACM by Yepez-Martinez (2012) and Fraser (2012) and of excited rotational states by Morales (2012), applying the more systematic catastrophe theory framework developed by Gilmore (1981) and previously used in nuclear systems by Lopez-Moreno (2011, 2012). The method enables a detailed classification of phase transitions in cluster models and is intended as a template for applying catastrophe theory to non-trivial models in other fields of physics.
