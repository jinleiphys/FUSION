# Boson Realization of the su(3)-Algebra. I

> Following a general form for the Schwinger boson representation of the su(M+1) Lipkin model presented in the previous paper, three types of the orthogonal sets characterizing the su(3)-algebra are proposed. In these three, third is presented in Appendix. The intrinsic state is specified by two quantum numbers and two excited state generating operators play a central role.

## Key claim

The paper constructs three complete orthogonal sets for the su(3)-algebra in the Schwinger boson representation of the Lipkin model, where the intrinsic state is specified by two quantum numbers and the excited states are generated by two operators; the third form, presented in the Appendix, requires a projection operator to enforce the condition that the state is a lowest-weight state of the su(2) subalgebra.

## Method

The authors express the eight generators of the su(3)-algebra and the three generators of the associated su(1,1)-algebra in terms of six kinds of boson operators (a, a*, b, b*, a±, a±*, b±, b±*). They construct the intrinsic state |T,R⟩ that satisfies the lowest-weight conditions for the D±, I−, and T− operators, and then build three distinct orthogonal bases by applying excited-state generating operators (D±*, I+, T+) in different ways: (1) treating D±* as a spherical tensor of rank 1/2 and using Clebsch-Gordan coupling, (2) using modified operators D±* that yield a monomial form, and (3) using the original D±* together with a projection operator that picks out the lowest I0 component.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic state |T,R⟩ has T values starting from T = 3/2, 2, 5/2, 3, ... (T ≥ 3/2)
- The quantum number R ranges from -(T-3/2) to (T-3/2) in integer steps
- The eigenvalue of the su(3) Casimir operator for the intrinsic state is 2[(T-3/2)(T+1/2) + (1/3)R²]
- The eigenvalue of the su(1,1) Casimir operator for the intrinsic state is 2T(T-1)
- In terms of M⁰ and I⁰, the su(3) Casimir eigenvalue is 2[(1/3)M⁰(M⁰+3) + I⁰(I⁰+1)]
- The su(1,1) Casimir eigenvalue in terms of M⁰ and I⁰ is 2·(1/2)(M⁰+I⁰+3)·(1/2)(M⁰+I⁰+1)
- The quantum numbers M⁰ and I⁰ correspond to λ and μ in the Elliott model with M⁰ = λ and I⁰ = μ

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger boson representation of the su(M+1) Lipkin model, previously sketched in the authors' earlier paper (referred to as (A)), to the complete su(3) case. The su(2)-Lipkin model has been a standard tool for studying collective motion in nuclei, and this generalization to su(3) enables the coupling of collective and non-collective degrees of freedom, as well as the schematic study of finite temperature effects. The three orthogonal sets constructed here provide the foundation for a Holstein-Primakoff boson realization, which the authors state will be presented in a subsequent paper.
