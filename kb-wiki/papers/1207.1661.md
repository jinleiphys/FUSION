# $\bar{K}NN$ Absorption within the Framework of the Fixed Center Approximation to Faddeev equations

> We present a method to evaluate the $\bar{K}$ absorption width in the bound $\bar{K}NN$ system. Most calculations of this system ignore this channel and only consider the $\bar{K}N \rightarrow πΣ$ conversion. Other works make a qualitative calculation using perturbative methods. Since the $ Λ(1405) $ resonance is playing a role in the process, the same resonance is changed by the presence of the absorption channels and we find that a full nonperturbative calculation is called for, which we present here. We employ the Fixed Center Approximation to Faddeev equations to account for $\bar{K}$ rescattering on the $ (NN) $ cluster and we find that the width of the states found previously for $ S=0 $ and $ S=1 $ increases by about 30 MeV due to the $\bar{K}NN$ absorption, to a total width of about 80 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors present a nonperturbative calculation of the \(\bar{K}NN\) absorption width, finding that the total width of the bound \(\bar{K}NN\) states (for both \(S=0\) and \(S=1\)) increases by about 30 MeV due to this absorption channel, reaching a total width of about 80 MeV. This result is conditional on using the Fixed Center Approximation to Faddeev equations and on the specific input for the \(\bar{K}NN\) absorption taken from earlier work on the \(\bar{K}\)-nucleus optical potential.

## Method

The authors employ the Fixed Center Approximation (FCA) to Faddeev equations to describe \(\bar{K}\) multiple scattering on a bound \((NN)\) cluster. They then evaluate the \(\bar{K}NN \rightarrow \Lambda N, \Sigma N, \Sigma^{*}N\) absorption diagrams and convert these into a modification of the \(\bar{K}N\) loop function (\(\delta G\)) within the chiral unitary coupled-channel approach. This modified \(\bar{K}N\) amplitude is then used nonperturbatively in the FCA to recalculate the \(\bar{K}(NN)\) scattering amplitude, from which the width of the \(\bar{K}NN\) states is extracted.

## Key numbers

* The width of the \(\bar{K}NN\) states for \(S=0\) and \(S=1\) increases by about 30 MeV due to \(\bar{K}NN\) absorption, leading to a total width of about 80 MeV.
* For the \(S=0\) case, the width without absorption is about 50 MeV; with absorption it increases to about 80 MeV.
* The binding energies used for the \(\bar{K}NN\) system are in the range of 15-30 MeV, as converging results from other groups.
* The parameter \(\alpha\) for the \(NN\) cluster wave function is set to 1.7 fm\(^{-1}\), corresponding to a relative distance of around 2 fm.
* The momentum of the produced hyperons (\(p_{Z(H)}\)) is around 550 MeV/c.
* The cutoff \(q_{max}\) used in the \(\delta G\) integration is 630 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the \(\bar{K}NN\) system, which mostly ignored the \(\bar{K}NN\) absorption channel or treated it perturbatively (e.g., Dote et al. (2008)). Prior nonperturbative Faddeev calculations (Shevchenko et al. (2006, 2007), Ikeda et al. (2007)) and variational approaches (Yamazaki et al. (2002), Dote et al. (2008), Barnea et al. (2012)) had obtained binding energies and widths from the \(\bar{K}N \rightarrow \pi\Sigma\) conversion alone. The present work provides a full nonperturbative evaluation of the absorption width, using input for the \(\bar{K}NN\) absorption from the \(\bar{K}\)-nucleus optical potential of Ramos et al. (1999) and Oset et al. (2000), which was tested against kaonic atom data. The results suggest that the large width (about 80 MeV) relative to the small binding (15-30 MeV) makes experimental observation of the \(\bar{K}NN\) state problematic.
