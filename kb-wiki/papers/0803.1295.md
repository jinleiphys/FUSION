# $Z^+(4430)$ as a $D_1'{D}^* $ ($D_1{D}^* $) molecular state

> We reexamine whether $Z^+(4430)$ could be a $D_1'-{D}^*$ or $D_1-{D}^*$ molecular state after considering both the pion and $σ$ meson exchange potentials and introducing the form factor to take into account the structure effect of the interaction vertex. Our numerical analysis with Matlab package MATSLISE indicates the contribution from the sigma meson exchange is small for the $D_1'-{D}^*$ system and significant for the $D_1-{D}^*$ system. The S-wave $D_1-\bar{D}^*$ molecular state with only $J^{P}=0^-$ and $D_1'-{D}^*$ molecular states with $J^P=0^-,1^-,2^-$ may exist with reasonable parameters. One should investigate whether the broad width of $D_1'$ disfavors the possible formation of molecular states in the future. The bottom analog $Z_B$ of $Z^+(4430)$ has a larger binding energy, which may be searched at Tevatron and LHC. Experimental measurement of the quantum number of $Z^+(4430)$ may help uncover its underlying structure.

## Key claim

The paper reexamines whether the exotic resonance Z+(4430) could be a D1'-D* or D1-D* molecular state, finding that such bound states may exist with reasonable parameters only when both pion and sigma meson exchange potentials are included, along with a form factor to account for vertex structure effects. The authors hedge that the broad width of the D1' meson (around 384 MeV) may disfavor the possible formation of molecular states, requiring further investigation.

## Method

The authors derived the one-pion and sigma meson exchange potentials for the D1'-D* and D1-D* systems using effective Lagrangians constructed in chiral symmetry and the heavy quark limit, with coupling constants estimated from the quark model and experimental data. They introduced a dipole-type form factor at each interaction vertex to account for the off-shell effects of exchanged mesons and the structure of the interaction vertex. The Schrodinger equation was solved numerically using the MATSLISE package (a graphical Matlab software for Sturm-Liouville problems) to obtain binding energies and wave functions for S-wave molecular states with J^P = 0^-, 1^-, and 2^-.

## Key numbers

- The mass of Z+(4430) is M = 4433 +/- 4 (stat) +/- 1 (syst) MeV and its width is Gamma = 44 +17/-13 (stat) +30/-11 (syst) MeV, as reported by Belle.
- For the D1'-D* system with J=0, using parameters [g*g' = 0.1, h = 0.84] and cutoff Lambda = 0.7 GeV, the binding energy is -23.9 MeV with r_rms = 1.5 fm and r_max = 1.3 fm; with Lambda = 1.5 GeV the binding energy is -5.5 MeV with r_rms = 2.1 fm and r_max = 1.5 fm.
- For the D1-D* system with J=0, using parameters [g*g'' = 0.2, h' = 0.55 GeV^-1, g_sigma*g_sigma'' = 0.58, h_sigma' = 0.2] and cutoff Lambda = 2.9 GeV, the binding energy is -1.3 MeV with r_rms = 2.6 fm and r_max = 0.2 fm.
- For the bottom analog B1'-B* system with J=0, using [g*g' = 0.1, h = 0.84] and Lambda = 1.5 GeV, the binding energy is -14.6 MeV with r_rms = 1.6 fm and r_max = 1.5 fm.
- The mass difference q0 = M_D1 - M_D* is approximately 410 MeV, about three times the pion mass (m_pi = 135.0 MeV).
- The sigma meson mass is taken as m_sigma = 600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Liu et al., 2008) which considered only one-pion exchange and used variational methods with simple trial wave functions, finding that pion exchange alone was insufficient to bind the D1'-D* or D1-D* systems. The present paper adds sigma meson exchange (analogous to the intermediate-range attraction in nuclear physics that binds the deuteron) and introduces a form factor, following a similar approach used by the same authors for the X(3872) as a D-D* molecule. The results enable future experimental searches for the bottom analog Z_B at Tevatron and LHC, and emphasize that experimental measurement of the quantum numbers of Z+(4430) would help uncover its underlying structure.
