# Self-consistent collective coordinate for reaction path and inertial mass

> We propose a numerical method to determine the optimal collective reaction path for the nucleus-nucleus collision, based on the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method. We use an iterative method combining the imaginary-time evolution and the finite amplitude method, for the solution of the ASCC coupled equations. It is applied to the simplest case, the $α-α$ scattering. We determine the collective path, the potential, and the inertial mass. The results are compared with other methods, such as the constrained Hartree-Fock method, the Inglis's cranking formula, and the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (ATDHF) method.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a numerical method to determine the optimal collective reaction path for nucleus-nucleus collisions using the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method, but the method is demonstrated only for the simplest case of α-α scattering, and the curvature term in the ASCC equations is neglected, which the authors state has only a minor effect on the final result.

## Method

The authors solve the ASCC coupled equations using an iterative procedure that combines the imaginary-time method for the moving Hartree-Fock (HF) equation with the finite amplitude method (FAM) for the moving random-phase approximation (RPA) equations, implemented in a three-dimensional coordinate-space representation with the BKN energy density functional. The collective path is determined by moving stepwise along the coordinate q using the local generator \(\hat{Q}(q)\), with the constraint \(\langle \psi(q+\delta q) | \hat{Q}(q) | \psi(q+\delta q) \rangle = \delta q\) to control the step size. The collective inertial mass is set to unity by scaling the coordinate q, and then mapped to the physical variable R (the distance between the two α particles) using the derivative \(dR/dq\) calculated from the local generator \(\hat{P}(q)\).

## Key numbers

- The three-dimensional coordinate-space box has volume \(10 \times 10 \times 16\) fm\(^3\) with mesh size \(h = 0.8\) fm.
- A small real parameter \(\eta = 10^{-4}\) is used in the finite amplitude method for calculating the RPA matrix elements.
- The BKN energy density functional parameter set is taken from reference BKN76, with the nucleon mass set to the bare mass (no center-of-mass correction applied).

## Context

This work extends the ASCC method, previously applied to nuclear structure problems with separable interactions (Hinohara et al., 2008, 2009; Sato et al., 2012), to nuclear reaction studies with non-separable Hamiltonians. The method addresses the non-uniqueness problem of the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (ATDHF) method (Goeke et al., 1983; Reinhard and Goeke, 1987) and provides a self-consistent alternative to the constrained Hartree-Fock method with Inglis's cranking formula for calculating the inertial mass. The approach enables the construction of a quantized collective Hamiltonian for nuclear reactions, which could be applied to sub-barrier fusion and spontaneous fission processes that are inaccessible to standard TDHF calculations.
