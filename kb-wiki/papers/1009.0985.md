# Description of Pairing correlation in Many-Body finite systems with density functional theory

> Different steps leading to the new functional for pairing based on natural orbitals and occupancies proposed in ref. [D. Lacroix and G. Hupin, arXiv:1003.2860] are carefully analyzed. Properties of quasi-particle states projected onto good particle number are first reviewed. These properties are used (i) to prove the existence of such a functional (ii) to provide an explicit functional through a 1/N expansion starting from the BCS approach (iii) to give a compact form of the functional summing up all orders in the expansion. The functional is benchmarked in the case of the picked fence pairing Hamiltonian where even and odd systems, using blocking technique are studied, at various particle number and coupling strength, with uniform and random single-particle level spacing. In all cases, a very good agreement is found with a deviation inferior to 1% compared to the exact energy.

## Key claim

The authors prove that the energy of a projected-BCS (PBCS) state can be written as an explicit functional of natural orbital occupancies alone, and they provide a compact resummed form of this functional that avoids the slow convergence of a 1/N expansion in the weak-coupling limit.

## Method

The authors start from a BCS quasi-particle state projected onto good particle number (PBCS) and derive recurrence relations for the overlaps of states with one or more pairs removed. Using these relations, they express the correlation matrix elements of the projected state first as functionals of the BCS parameters {x_i}, then as implicit functionals of the occupation numbers {n_i} via a set of secular equations. They then construct an explicit functional by performing a 1/N expansion around the BCS limit and subsequently resumming all orders to obtain a simplified linear form for the key coefficient α_i = a_0 - a_1 n_i, where a_0 and a_1 depend on moments of the occupation numbers. The resulting functional is benchmarked on the picket-fence pairing Hamiltonian for both even and odd systems with uniform and random single-particle level spacings.

## Key numbers

* The new functional reproduces exact energies with a deviation inferior to 1% for all tested cases (even and odd systems, various particle numbers and coupling strengths, uniform and random level spacings).
* For the picket-fence Hamiltonian, the system size is A = 2N particles with a single-particle Hilbert space of size Ω = 2A, and the level spacing is denoted Δε.
* In the Hartree-Fock limit (weak coupling), the functional reduces to C_{ij} → √(n_i n_j), recovering the form proposed by Csányi and Arias (2000) for electronic systems.

## Context

This work extends the density matrix functional theory (DMFT) framework, originally proposed by Gilbert (1975) as a generalization of the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem, to treat pairing correlations with exact particle-number conservation. It addresses the shortcomings of standard BCS or Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) theory, which conserves particle number only in average and exhibits a threshold at low coupling where correlations are missed. The new functional avoids the ambiguities and pathologies encountered in multi-reference energy density functional (MR-EDF) approaches that combine projection with density functionals, as analyzed by Lacroix et al. (2009), Bender et al. (2009), and Duguet et al. (2009). The approach enables systematic calculations of pairing correlations in finite systems (nuclei, ultrasmall metallic grains) without the numerical complexity of variation-after-projection methods, and is expected to be adaptable to non-equilibrium evolution and finite-temperature studies.
