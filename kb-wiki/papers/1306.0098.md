# Hyperfine-induced effects on the linear polarization of the K$α_1$ emission from helium-like ions

> The linear polarization of the characteristic photon emission from few-electron ions is studied for its sensitivity with regard to the nuclear spin and magnetic moment of the ions. Special attention is paid, in particular, to the K$α_1$ ($1s 2p_{3/2} ^{1,3}P_{1,2} \to 1s^2 ^1S_0$) decay of selected helium-like ions following the radiative electron capture into initially hydrogen-like species. Based on the density matrix theory, a unified description is developed that includes both, the many-electron and hyperfine interactions as well as the multipole-mixing effects arising from the expansion of the radiation field. It is shown that the polarization of the K$α_1$ line can be significantly affected by the mutipole mixing between the leading $M2$ and hyperfine-induced $E1$ components of $1s2p ^3P_2, F_i \to 1s^2 ^1S_0, F_f$ transitions. This $E1$-$M2$ mixing strongly depends on the nuclear properties of the considered isotopes and can be addressed experimentally at existing heavy-ion storage rings.

## Key claim

The linear polarization of the Kα₁ emission from helium-like ions is sensitive to the nuclear spin and magnetic moment because of the interference between the leading M2 and hyperfine-induced E1 decay channels of the 1s2p ³P₂ → 1s² ¹S₀ transition, but this effect is only significant for certain hyperfine components (e.g., Fᵢ = 3/2 → F_f = 1/2) and depends strongly on the magnitude and sign of the nuclear magnetic dipole moment.

## Method

The authors developed a unified density-matrix formalism that includes many-electron correlations, hyperfine interactions, and multipole-mixing effects from the radiation field expansion. They computed the alignment parameters for excited ionic states produced by radiative electron capture into initially hydrogen-like ions, including cascade feeding from higher-lying states up to n ≤ 6, and evaluated the reduced transition matrix elements using multiconfiguration Dirac-Fock wave functions from the Grasp92 and Ratip programs.

## Key numbers

- For the Fᵢ = 3/2 → F_f = 1/2 hyperfine transition in ²⁰⁵₈₁Tl⁷⁹⁺ (I = 1/2, μ_I = 1.638 μ_N) at projectile energy T_p = 50 MeV/u, the absolute degree of polarization reduces from -0.30 to -0.24 (about 20% reduction) when the hyperfine-induced E1 amplitude is included.
- For ²⁰⁹₈₂Pb⁸⁰⁺ (I = 1/2, μ_I = 0.593 μ_N) under the same conditions, the shift in polarization of the Fᵢ = 3/2 → F_f = 1/2 transition does not exceed 5% at any angle.
- For the averaged 1s2p ³P₂ → 1s² ¹S₀ fine-structure transition in ²⁰⁵₈₁Tl⁷⁹⁺, the E1-M2 mixing reduces the linear polarization by about 11%.
- For the overall Kα₁ emission from ²⁰⁵₈₁Tl⁷⁹⁺ at θ = 90° and projectile energies T_p ≲ 10 MeV/u, the E1-M2 mixing reduces the absolute degree of polarization from about -15.8% to -14.6%.
- For ¹⁶¹₆₆Dy⁶⁴⁺ (I = 5/2, μ_I = -0.4804 μ_N), the E1-M2 interference depolarizes the Kα₁ emission by about 4%, while for ¹⁶³₆₆Dy⁶⁴⁺ (μ_I = +0.6726 μ_N), the degree of polarization is enhanced by about 3%.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Henderson et al. (1990) and DuBois and Gwinner (1994) on hyperfine effects in electron-impact excited helium-like Sc¹⁹⁺ ions, and by Beiersdorfer and collaborators (2006) on polarization of Kα lines. It provides a theoretical framework for using x-ray polarimetry at heavy-ion storage rings, such as those at GSI and FAIR, as a tool to determine nuclear spins and magnetic dipole moments of stable and radioactive isotopes, complementing conventional optical laser spectroscopy techniques used by Kluge and Nörtershäuser (2003) and Blaum et al. (2013).
