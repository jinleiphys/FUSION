# Spectra and Decay Constants of $B_c$-like and $B^*_0$ Mesons in QCD

> Using the existing state of art of the QCD expressions of the two-point correlators into the Inverse Laplace sum rules (LSR) within stability criteria, we present a first analysis of the spectra and decay constants of B_c-like scalar (0^{++}) and axial-vector (1^{++}) mesons and revisit the ones of the B^*_c(1^{--}) vector meson. Improved predictions are obtained by combining these LSR results with the some mass-splittings from Heavy Quark Symmetry (HQS). We complete the analysis by revisiting the B^*_{0}(0^{++}) mass which might be likely identified with the B^*_J(5732) experimental candidate. The results for the spectra collected in Table 2 are compared with some recent lattice and potential models ones. New estimates of the decay constants are given in Table 3.

## Key claim

Using Inverse Laplace sum rules (LSR) with stability criteria, combined with mass-splitting relations from Heavy Quark Symmetry (HQS), the paper presents a first analysis of the spectra and decay constants of B_c-like scalar (0^{++}) and axial-vector (1^{++}) mesons, and revisits the B*_c(1^{--}) vector meson and the B*_0(0^{++}) meson; the results are conditional on the choice of stability regions and the requirement that the QCD continuum contribution to the spectral function does not exceed 20-25% of the lowest resonance contribution.

## Method

The analysis uses the QCD Inverse Laplace sum rules (LSR) of two-point correlators, employing the SVZ Operator Product Expansion (OPE) with perturbative contributions to N2LO and non-perturbative contributions from the gluon condensate and the charm quark condensate. The spectral function is parametrized by a single narrow resonance plus a QCD continuum above a threshold t_c, and optimal results are extracted by requiring stability of the observables against variations of the LSR variable tau, the continuum threshold t_c, and the subtraction scale mu. The LSR results for masses are then combined with mass-splitting relations from Heavy Quark Symmetry (HQS) to obtain improved predictions.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous LSR analyses of the B_c pseudoscalar meson (Narison, 2020; Narison, 2019) to the scalar and axial-vector B_c-like channels for the first time, and revisits the vector B*_c channel previously studied using q^2=0 moments (Narison, 1990s) and more recent LSR estimates (Narison, 2015). The results are compared with recent lattice QCD calculations and potential model predictions (Quigg, Bagan). The analysis enables improved determinations of heavy-light meson decay constants and masses, and provides a QCD-based test for the identification of the B*_J(5732) experimental candidate as the B*_0(0^{++}) meson.
