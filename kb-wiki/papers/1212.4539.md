# Constraints on the symmetry energy from neutron star observations

> The modeling of many neutron star observables incorporates the microphysics of both the stellar crust and core, which is tied intimately to the properties of the nuclear matter equation of state (EoS). We explore the predictions of such models over the range of experimentally constrained nuclear matter parameters, focusing on the slope of the symmetry energy at nuclear saturation density $L$. We use a consistent model of the composition and EoS of neutron star crust and core matter to model the binding energy of pulsar B of the double pulsar system J0737-3039, the frequencies of torsional oscillations of the neutron star crust and the instability region for r-modes in the neutron star core damped by electron-electron viscosity at the crust-core interface. By confronting these models with observations, we illustrate the potential of astrophysical observables to offer constraints on poorly known nuclear matter parameters complementary to terrestrial experiments, and demonstrate that our models consistently predict $L<70$ MeV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three independent neutron star observables, the binding energy of pulsar B in J0737-3039, torsional crust oscillation frequencies, and the r-mode instability window, can each constrain the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation density \(L\) to values below about 70 MeV, but the authors explicitly caution that the theoretical interpretations of the observations remain uncertain and the astrophysical modeling is almost certainly over-simplified.

## Method

The authors constructed a set of consistent neutron star crust and core equations of state (EoSs) using a Skyrme-like nuclear matter energy functional that reproduces a pure neutron matter EoS from microscopic calculations at low densities, while varying the symmetry energy slope parameter \(L\) smoothly over the range \(25 \lesssim L \lesssim 115\) MeV and keeping symmetric nuclear matter fixed. They applied these EoSs to model three observables: the gravitational binding energy of pulsar J0737-3039B under the assumption of an electron-capture supernova formation scenario; the fundamental and first overtone frequencies of torsional crust oscillations, including effects of nuclear pasta phases and neutron superfluid entrainment; and the onset frequency of the Chandrasekhar-Friedman-Schutz r-mode instability damped by electron-electron viscosity at the crust-core interface.

## Key numbers

- The gravitational mass of pulsar J0737-3039B is measured as \(M_{\rm G} = 1.2489 \pm 0.0007 M_{\odot}\); combining this with a predicted baryon mass range of \(1.358 < M_{\rm B} < 1.375 M_{\odot}\) from electron-capture supernova modeling yields the constraint \(L < 70\) MeV.
- For torsional crust oscillations in a \(1.4 M_{\odot}\) neutron star, matching any observed fundamental mode frequency from soft gamma-ray repeaters requires \(L \lesssim 60\) MeV; if the 625 Hz mode is matched to the first overtone, \(L \lesssim 60\) MeV and the pasta phases must behave as an elastic solid.
- For the r-mode instability in a \(2.0 M_{\odot}\) neutron star, consistency with the observed spin frequency of the millisecond pulsar 4U 1608 forces \(L < 65\) MeV under the assumption that the r-mode instability limits neutron star spin.
- The conservative range of \(L\) explored in the EoS set is \(25 \lesssim L \lesssim 115\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to constrain the symmetry energy slope \(L\) from terrestrial experiments (heavy ion collisions, nuclear masses, neutron skins, nuclear resonances) and from simpler neutron star observables such as mass and radius measurements (Steiner 2010, Steiner 2011). The paper specifically contrasts its results with a similar torsional oscillation analysis by Sotani (2012) that concluded \(100 \lesssim L \lesssim 130\) MeV, and with an r-mode analysis by Vidana (2012) that concluded \(L > 50\) MeV but did not consistently model crust and core together. The consistent EoS set used here is made available online for general use, enabling future work to combine multiple neutron star observables for tighter constraints on the nuclear equation of state.
