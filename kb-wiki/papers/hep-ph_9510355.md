# New $I=J$ Rules for the Baryon Vertices in $1/N_c$ Expansion

> We apply the $1/N_c$ expansion in QCD to the baryon vertices. We find new model independent properties for the isoscalar and isovector baryon vertices from the view point of $1/N_c$ expansion in QCD. One of these results, $I=J$ rule, have been already found. The other properties, new $I=J$ rules, are the rules about the isospin and strangeness dependence for the baryon vertices.

## Key claim

The paper derives new model-independent properties for baryon vertices in the $1/N_c$ expansion of QCD, specifically that the isospin and strangeness dependence of baryon vertices are suppressed by $1/N_c$ when the operator has $I=J$, but survive at leading order when $I\neq J$; these results rely on the assumptions of quark confinement, $SU(3)$ flavor symmetry, and that spin and isospin of baryon states are $O(N_c^0)$.

## Method

The author constructs general isoscalar and isovector baryon vertex formulas for arbitrary $N_c$ using the Wigner-Eckart theorem, expressed in terms of $F/D$ ratios. The $F/D$ ratios for spin-flip and spin-nonflip vertices are then evaluated in the large $N_c$ limit using two specific models: the non-relativistic quark model (NRQM) and the chiral soliton model (CSM), with expansions in $1/N_c$ given explicitly. The resulting formulas are analyzed to extract the $N_c$ scaling of isospin and strangeness-dependent terms.

## Key numbers

- For the spin-nonflip vertex in NRQM: $(F_+/D_+)_{SU(6)~NRQM} = -(N_c+1)/(N_c-3) = -1 - 4/N_c + 12/N_c^2 + \cdots$
- For the spin-nonflip vertex in CSM: $(F_+/D_+)_{SU(3)~CSM} = -(N_c^2+4N_c-1)/(N_c^2+4N_c-9) = -1 + 0/N_c - 8/N_c^2 + \cdots$
- For the spin-flip vertex in NRQM: $(F_-/D_-)_{SU(6)~NRQM} = (N_c+5)/[3(N_c+1)] = 1/3 + 4/(3N_c) - 4/(3N_c^2) + \cdots$
- For the spin-flip vertex in CSM: $(F_-/D_-)_{SU(3)~CSM} = (N_c^2+8N_c+27)/[3(N_c^2+8N_c+3)] = 1/3 + 0/N_c + 8/N_c^2 + \cdots$
- The isoscalar spin-nonflip vertex ($I=J=0$) has the form $H(I=J=0) = N_c a + b K + (c/N_c)[I(I+1)-(K+1/2)(K+3/2)]$ where $a,b,c$ are $O(N_c^0)$
- The isovector spin-flip vertex ($I=J=1$) has the form $H^{ia}(I=J=1) = [N_c d + e K + (f/N_c)(I(I+1)-(K+1/2)(K+3/2))] X_0^{ia}$ where $d,e,f$ are $O(N_c^0)$

## Context

This work extends the $I=J$ rule previously found by Mattis and collaborators (Mattis, early 1980s) for baryon vertices in the $1/N_c$ expansion, which concerned only the leading order in $1/N_c$. The new rules describe the $N_c$-dependent part of isospin and strangeness contributions, showing that when spin and isospin of the octet operator are equal, baryon vertices are $O(N_c)$ and isospin/strangeness dependence is suppressed by $1/N_c$, but when $I\neq J$ the vertices are $O(N_c^0)$ and isospin/strangeness dependence survives in the large $N_c$ limit. The results are consistent with the consistency condition approach of Dashen, Jenkins and Manohar (Dashen, 1994) and with work by Luty (Luty, 1994) and Carone (Carone, 1994). The paper enables model-independent predictions for baryon magnetic moments and mass relations in the $1/N_c$ expansion framework.
