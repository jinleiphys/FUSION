# Quark Number Susceptibilities from Two-Loop Hard Thermal Loop Perturbation Theory

> We use the recently obtained two-loop hard thermal loop perturbation theory thermodynamics functions of a plasma of quarks and gluons to compute the diagonal second- and fourth-order quark number susceptibilities. The two-loop hard thermal loop perturbation theory thermodynamic functions used are reliable in the limit that the ratio of the quark chemical potential to temperature is small. Using this result, we are able to obtain (semi-)analytic expressions for the quark number susceptibilities at leading- and next-to-leading-order in hard thermal loop perturbation theory. We compare the hard thermal loop perturbation theory results with perturbative quantum chromodynamics calculations, a Polyakov-loop Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model calculation, and lattice quantum chromodynamics results.

## Key claim

The two-loop hard thermal loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) calculation of second- and fourth-order quark number susceptibilities is reliable only in the limit that the ratio of quark chemical potential to temperature is small, and the next-to-leading-order (NLO) results suffer from over-counting at higher orders in the coupling, requiring a next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) calculation to fix the problem through order g^5.

## Method

The authors used the previously obtained two-loop HTLpt thermodynamic functions for a quark-gluon plasma, expanded through fourth order in μ/T, to compute the diagonal second- and fourth-order quark number susceptibilities. They derived (semi-)analytic expressions at leading order (LO) and NLO in HTLpt, then numerically solved the variational gap equations for the Debye screening mass m_D and the quark thermal mass m_q to obtain the NLO susceptibilities. The results were compared with perturbative QCD (pQCD), Polyakov-loop Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model calculations, and lattice QCD (LQCD) results.

## Context

This work extends the HTLpt framework previously applied to the equation of state by Haque et al. (2013) to compute quark number susceptibilities, building on earlier LO HTLpt calculations by Andersen et al. (2013) and pQCD calculations by Vourinen (2011). The results are compared with LQCD data from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (2010), RBC-Bielefeld collaboration (2008), and MILC collaboration (2008), as well as PNJL model calculations by Paramita et al. (2013). The authors note that a NNLO HTLpt calculation, which has begun, is needed to fix over-counting problems at order g^4 and g^5 and potentially improve agreement with LQCD results, particularly for the fourth-order susceptibility.
