# Complex scaling in finite volume

> Quantum resonances, i.e., metastable states with a finite lifetime, play an important role in nuclear physics and other domains. Describing this phenomenon theoretically is generally a challenging task. In this work, we combine two established techniques to address this challenge. Complex scaling makes it possible to calculate resonances with bound-state-like methods. Finite-volume simulations exploit the fact that the infinite-volume properties of quantum systems are encoded in how discrete energy levels change as one varies the size of the volume. We apply complex scaling to systems in finite periodic boxes and derive the volume dependence of states in this scenario, demonstrating with explicit examples how one can use these relations to infer infinite-volume resonance energies and lifetimes.

## Key claim

The paper combines complex scaling with finite-volume periodic boundary conditions to derive analytic formulas for how resonance energies and widths depend on box size, enabling extraction of infinite-volume resonance properties from finite-volume calculations without relying on avoided crossings in the spectrum.

## Method

The authors apply uniform complex scaling (rotating coordinates by an angle phi) to two-body systems in periodic cubic boxes, then derive the leading and subleading volume dependence of complex-scaled resonance energies by expanding the Lüscher quantization condition around the infinite-volume pole. They implement a numerical framework for calculating complex-scaled few-body systems in finite volume and demonstrate the method with explicit examples using short-range interactions.

## Key numbers

* Leading-order volume dependence for a three-dimensional S-wave resonance: Delta E(L) = (3 gamma_inf^2 / mu) * exp(i zeta p_inf L) / (zeta L), where zeta = exp(i phi) is the complex scaling factor and p_inf is the infinite-volume resonance momentum
* Subleading corrections include terms proportional to exp(i zeta p_inf L), exp(i sqrt(2) zeta p_inf L), and exp(i zeta sqrt(3) p_inf L), with prefactor 6 p_inf / (zeta mu [K_0'(zeta p_inf) - i] L)
* The bound-state result is recovered by substituting p_inf = i kappa with real kappa > 0, giving the known form Delta E(L) = (3 gamma_inf^2 / mu) * exp(-kappa L) / L

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume methodology of Lüscher (1990) and the complex scaling method of Aguilar (1971) and Balslev (1971) to enable direct extraction of resonance energies and lifetimes from finite-volume simulations. It provides an alternative to the standard approach of identifying resonances through avoided crossings in the finite-volume spectrum (Wiese 1988, Luscher 1991), and is closely related to the work of Guo (2020) which used analytic continuation to imaginary box sizes. The derived formulas enable future lattice QCD and lattice EFT calculations to determine resonance properties more efficiently than previous methods.
