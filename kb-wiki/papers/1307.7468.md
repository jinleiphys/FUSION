# Magnetic Field Effects in Fermion Pairings

> This paper considers various fermion pairings of interest for the QCD phases. The effects of an external magnetic field on the pairing mechanisms, on the realization of new condensates, and on the properties of the magnetized phases are all explored and discussed.

## Key claim

External magnetic fields universally induce an additional spin-one (anomalous magnetic moment) condensate alongside the conventional chiral condensate in the magnetic catalysis phenomenon, and similarly generate extra condensates in color-superconducting and quarkyonic phases, because the rotational symmetry broken by the field opens new interaction channels that make such condensates unavoidable.

## Method

The paper reviews the effects of a uniform external magnetic field on three types of fermion pairing in QCD: (i) chiral symmetry breaking via particle-antiparticle pairing in vacuum (magnetic catalysis), (ii) Cooper pairing in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase at high density, and (iii) density-wave pairing in quarkyonic matter at intermediate density in the large-\(N_c\) limit. For each case, the author analyzes how Landau quantization modifies the fermion spectrum and how the explicit breaking of rotational symmetry by the field allows new condensates (e.g., spin-one diquark condensates in the magnetized CFL phase, and a magnetic-moment/pion spiral in quarkyonic matter) via Fierz-transformed interaction channels.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic fields at magnetar surfaces: \(10^{14}\)–\(10^{15}\) G; inner fields estimated at \(10^{18}\) G for hybrid stars with nuclear cores, \(10^{19}\) G for hybrid stars with quark matter cores, and up to \(10^{20}\) G for strange stars.
- Magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions: up to \(10^{18}\) G at RHIC (Au-Au) and \(\sim 10^{19}\) G at LHC (Pb-Pb); these fields decay to a tenth of their original value on a time scale of order the inverse saturation scale at RHIC.
- In the magnetized CFL (MCFL) phase, the field strength at which the MCFL phase becomes physically distinct from the CFL phase is of order \(\Delta^2_{\mathrm{CFL}}\) (the square of the CFL gap).
- In quarkyonic matter, the condition for the existence of the phase is \(m_D \ll \Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}} \ll \mu\), with \(m_D\) the gluon screening mass, \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}\) the QCD scale, and \(\mu\) the quark chemical potential; the magnetic field considered satisfies \(B \le \Lambda^2_{\mathrm{QCD}}\).

## Context

This review extends the author's prior work on magnetic catalysis (MC) in QED and NJL models (de la Incera, various years) and on the magnetized CFL phase (MCFL) (de la Incera, 2010–2012), which itself built on the original CFL phase of color superconductivity (Alford, Rajagopal, Wilczek, 1999). The discussion of quarkyonic chiral spirals in a magnetic field follows the large-\(N_c\) quarkyonic matter proposal (McLerran, Pisarski, 2007) and the chiral spiral condensate (Kojo, Hidaka, McLerran, Pisarski, 2010). The paper notes that recent lattice QCD results (Bali et al., 2012) show a decrease of the chiral transition temperature with magnetic field, contradicting the magnetic catalysis scenario at finite temperature, an issue still under scrutiny. The work enables further study of transport properties and cooling of magnetized neutron stars, and of observable signatures in heavy-ion collisions at FAIR, NICA, and JPARC.
