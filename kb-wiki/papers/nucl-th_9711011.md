# Effects of collective expansion on light cluster spectra in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We discuss the interplay between collective flow and density profiles, describing light cluster production in heavy ion collisions at very high energies. Calculations are performed within the coalescence model. We show how collective flow can explain some qualitative features of the measured deuteron spectra, provided a proper parametrization of the spatial dependence of the single particle phase space distribution is chosen.

## Key claim

Collective flow can explain qualitative features of measured deuteron spectra in relativistic heavy ion collisions, but only if a proper parametrization of the spatial dependence of the single-particle phase space distribution is chosen; the most common parametrizations (gaussian density with linear flow profile) fail to reproduce the observed A-dependence of slope parameters and the pT-dependence of coalescence coefficients.

## Method

The authors use the coalescence model, where the phase space distribution of mass-A clusters is approximated as the A-th power of the single-nucleon distribution at position r and momentum p/A. The nucleon distribution is assumed to be in local thermal equilibrium at temperature T0 with a collective velocity field split into independent longitudinal and transverse components; the transverse velocity is parametrized as v_T(r_T) = v_f (r_T/R0)^α e_T, and the transverse momentum spectrum of clusters is obtained by integrating the phase space distribution over the fireball volume.

## Key numbers

- The effective temperature (slope parameter) for a gaussian density with linear flow (α=1) is T* = T0 + m v_f^2, which is independent of cluster mass number A.
- For a gaussian density with α=1, the coefficient Λ_α^Gauss(A) = 1/A, exactly canceling the A factor from the cluster mass M = A m in the effective temperature expression.
- For a box density profile, Λ_α^Box(A) = 1/(2α+2), independent of A, giving the strongest A-dependence of T*.
- For a gaussian density with α=1/2, Λ_α^Gauss(A) = 2^(α-1) Γ(α+1) / A^α, maintaining a weak A-dependence.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model framework described in earlier reviews (Coester and Kalos, 1986) and builds on source parametrizations used in interferometry studies (Chapman, Nix, and Heiselberg, 1996; Csorgo and Lorstad, 1996). The authors show that the common gaussian density with linear flow profile contradicts experimental observations from the NA44 collaboration (1997) and the E802 collaboration (1994), which show an A-dependence of slope parameters and a pT-dependence of the coalescence constant B_A. The results suggest that surface formation of clusters at an earlier time, with a depleted central density as suggested by Sinyukov, Nazarenko, and Karpenko (1995), may improve the scenario, and the work enables future quantitative conclusions when cluster spectra for large symmetric collision systems become available.
