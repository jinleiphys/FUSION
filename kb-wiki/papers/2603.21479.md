# Critical dynamics of the superfluid phase transition in Model F

> We describe numerical simulations of the critical dynamics near the superfluid phase transition. The calculations are based on an implementation of a stochastic hydrodynamic theory known as model F in the classification of Hohenberg and Halperin. This theory is expected to describe dynamic scaling near the lambda transition in liquid $^4$He, Bose-Einstein condensation in ultracold atomic gases, and the superfluid transition in the unitary Fermi gas. Our simulation is based on a Metropolis algorithm previously applied to the critical endpoint of the liquid-gas phase transition in ordinary fluids. In the model E truncation of model F we obtain the expected dynamical exponent $z\simeq 3/2$. We observe the emergence of a propagating second sound mode at the phase transition. The second sound diffusivity $D_s$ is consistent with the scaling relation $D_s\sim ξ^{x_κ}$, where $ξ$ is the correlation length and $x_κ=1/2$.

## Key claim

The authors numerically simulate the critical dynamics of the superfluid phase transition using a stochastic hydrodynamic theory (model F) and obtain the expected dynamical exponent \(z\simeq 3/2\) in the model E truncation, but only for the specific truncation where the coupling \(\gamma_0\) and the imaginary part of \(\Gamma\) are set to zero.

## Method

The authors implement a lattice discretization of model F, which describes the coupled evolution of a complex order parameter \(\phi\) and a conserved density \(\psi\) (proportional to entropy per particle). The dissipative part of the evolution is simulated using a Metropolis algorithm previously applied to model H for the liquid-gas critical endpoint, while the non-dissipative (ideal) part is integrated using a third-order Runge-Kutta scheme that exactly conserves the Hamiltonian and the conserved density on the lattice. The static critical behavior is first determined by locating the critical value of \(m^2\) using Binder cumulants and reweighting techniques.

## Key numbers

* The critical value of the inverse bare correlation length squared is \(m_c^2 = -3.1046(3)\) for \(\lambda=4\) in the \(O(2)\) model, determined from infinite volume extrapolation of Binder cumulant crossings.
* The universal critical value of the Binder cumulant for the \(O(2)\) model is \(U_4^* = 0.585\).
* The dynamical exponent obtained in the model E truncation is \(z \simeq 3/2\).
* The second sound diffusivity \(D_s\) scales as \(D_s \sim \xi^{x_\kappa}\) with \(x_\kappa = 1/2\), where \(\xi\) is the correlation length.

## Context

This work extends numerical simulations of stochastic hydrodynamic theories from model A (relaxational dynamics, Schaefer 2022), model B (diffusion of a conserved charge, Chattopadhyay 2023), model G (order parameter dynamics in an anti-ferromagnet, Florio 2021), and model H (fluid dynamics near a liquid-gas endpoint, Chattopadhyay 2024) to model F, which describes the superfluid phase transition. The results verify non-perturbatively the predictions of the epsilon expansion by Halperin (1976) and Dohm (1979, 1987, 1991, 2006) that the dynamical exponent is exactly \(z=3/2\) and that the second sound diffusivity diverges as \(\xi^{1/2}\). The framework enables future computation of non-universal quantities that can be compared with cold gas experiments and may have implications for understanding critical transport in neutron star cooling and QCD phase transitions.
