# Pion-Nucleon Scattering in the Infinite Momentum Frame

> 7 pages, two figures, text of invited talk presented at the 7th Int'l Symposium On Meson-Nucleon Physics and the Structure of the Nucelon, July 28 - August 1, 1997 in Vancouver, B.C., Canada

## Key claim

Light-front quantization of a chiral Lagrangian can reproduce the early soft-pion theorems for low-energy pion-nucleon scattering, but only when the instantaneous fermion terms and the quadratic pion-nucleon coupling are included, because the direct and crossed graphs involving matrix elements of γ₅ between u spinors vanish near threshold and the pair terms vanish due to plus-momentum conservation.

## Method

The author applies light-front (infinite momentum frame) quantization to a non-linear chiral Lagrangian with nucleons, pions, scalar mesons, and vector mesons, using a linear representation of chiral symmetry with pseudoscalar γ₅ pion-nucleon coupling. The light-front Hamiltonian P⁻ is constructed from the energy-momentum tensor, and the dependent fermion field ψ₋ is eliminated using the field equations. Pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes are computed to second order in 1/f using time-ordered (x⁺-ordered) perturbation theory, and the resulting invariant amplitude is evaluated at threshold.

## Key numbers

- The threshold invariant amplitude for πN scattering is ℳ = δ_if (2m_π²/f²) + 2i ε_fin τ_n (m_π M/f²), to leading order in m_π/M.
- The nucleon mass M and pion mass m_π appear in the threshold kinematics with k'⁺ = k⁺ = M and q'⁺ = q⁺ = m_π.
- The calculation is performed to second order in 1/f, where f is the pion decay constant.

## Context

This work extends the light-front treatment of nuclear matter in the mean-field approximation (Miller 1997) and provides the necessary chiral πN scattering amplitude for constructing a light-front chiral nucleon-nucleon force. The resulting two-pion exchange potential is compared to the Partovi-Lomon potential (which used pseudoscalar coupling) and the Bonn potential (which ignores Z-graphs), with the expectation that the quadratic π²-N coupling cancels large pair terms and reduces the attraction relative to Partovi-Lomon. The approach enables a fully relativistic chiral treatment of both πN and NN scattering for use in light-front Brueckner theory of nuclear matter.
