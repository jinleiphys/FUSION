# QCD phase transition in the chiral limit

> We present a lattice QCD based determination of the chiral phase transition temperature in QCD with two massless (up and down) and one strange quark having its physical mass. We propose and calculate two novel estimators for the chiral transition temperature for several values of the light quark masses, corresponding to Goldstone pion masses in the range of $58~{\rm MeV}\lesssim m_π\lesssim 163~{\rm MeV}$. The chiral phase transition temperature is determined by extrapolating to vanishing pion mass using universal scaling analysis. After thermodynamic, continuum and chiral extrapolations we find the chiral phase transition temperature $T_c^0=132^{+3}_{-6}$ MeV. We also present some preliminary calculations on interplay of effective $U_A(1)$ restoration and chiral phase transition towards chiral limit.

## Key claim

The chiral phase transition temperature in QCD with two massless light quarks and a physical strange quark is determined to be \(T_c^0 = 132^{+3}_{-6}\) MeV, which is about 25 MeV lower than the pseudo-critical crossover temperature at physical quark masses. The result depends on the assumption of \(O(4)\) universality class scaling and is obtained after thermodynamic, continuum, and chiral extrapolations, with systematic uncertainties from the choice of lattice spacings included in the error budget.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action for (2+1)-flavor QCD with degenerate up and down quark masses and a physical strange quark mass. They computed the renormalized chiral order parameter \(M\) and chiral susceptibility \(\chi_M\) on lattices with temporal extents \(N_\tau = 6, 8, 12\) and spatial extents \(N_\sigma\) such that \(4 \le N_\sigma/N_\tau \le 8\), for light quark mass ratios \(H = m_l/m_s\) corresponding to pion masses \(58\ \text{MeV} \lesssim m_\pi \lesssim 163\ \text{MeV}\). Two novel estimators for the chiral transition temperature, \(T_\delta\) and \(T_{60}\), were defined from the ratio \(H\chi_M/M = 1/\delta\) and from the temperature where \(\chi_M\) reaches 60% of its peak value, respectively; these estimators are designed to lie close to the true critical point \(z=0\) in the scaling variable. The chiral phase transition temperature was obtained by extrapolating these estimators to the thermodynamic limit, then to the continuum limit, and finally to the chiral limit using universal \(O(4)\) scaling functions.

## Key numbers

- Chiral phase transition temperature: \(T_c^0 = 132^{+3}_{-6}\) MeV, after thermodynamic, continuum, and chiral extrapolations.
- Pion mass range studied: \(58\ \text{MeV} \lesssim m_\pi \lesssim 163\ \text{MeV}\), corresponding to light quark mass ratios \(H = m_l/m_s\) from \(1/160\) to \(1/20\).
- Pseudo-critical crossover temperature at physical quark masses: \(T_{pc} = (156.5 \pm 1.5)\) MeV, cited from Bazavov (2018) for comparison.
- Kaon decay constant used for normalization: \(f_K = 156.1(9)/\sqrt{2}\) MeV.
- Systematic shift in \(T_c^0\) from including or discarding the coarsest \(N_\tau = 6\) lattices: about \(2\)–\(3\) MeV.
- Agreement between \(T_c^0\) values from the two estimators \(T_\delta\) and \(T_{60}\): better than 1% accuracy for each extrapolation ansatz.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD determinations of the chiral phase transition temperature in the chiral limit, building on earlier studies of the pseudo-critical crossover temperature at physical quark masses by Aoki (2009), Bazavov (2011), Bonati (2015), and Bazavov (2018). The use of novel estimators \(T_\delta\) and \(T_{60}\), which are designed to lie near the critical point \(z=0\) in the scaling variable, improves control over the chiral extrapolation compared to earlier approaches that relied on the peak of the chiral susceptibility (Bazavov 2011). The results support a second-order phase transition in the \(O(4)\) universality class at vanishing light quark masses, consistent with the finite-size scaling behavior observed, and provide a precise target for future studies of the interplay between effective \(U_A(1)\) restoration and the chiral transition. Preliminary calculations of the disconnected chiral susceptibility suggest that \(U_A(1)\) symmetry remains broken at the chiral phase transition temperature, motivating further work to clarify the nature of the axial anomaly near \(T_c^0\).
