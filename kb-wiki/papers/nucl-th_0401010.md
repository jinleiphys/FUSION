# Rescattering Contributions to Final State Interactions in (e,e'p) Reactions at High (p_m,E_m)

> The contribution of rescattering to final state interactions in the (e,e'p) cross section is studied using a semiclassical model. This approach considers a two-step process with the propagation of an intermediate nucleon and uses Glauber theory to account for the reduction of the experimental yield due to N--N scattering. This calculation has relevance for the analysis of data at high missing energies and in particular at the kinematics of the E97-006 experiment done at JLab. It is found that rescattering is strongly reduced in parallel kinematics and that the excitation of nucleon resonances is likely to give important contributions to the final state interactions in the correlated region. For heavy nuclei, further enhancement to the rescattering is expected to be generated from the strength in the mean field region.

## Key claim

Rescattering contributions to final state interactions in (e,e'p) reactions are strongly reduced in parallel kinematics compared to perpendicular kinematics, but the excitation of nucleon resonances (particularly the Delta resonance) is likely to give important contributions that are not yet included in the semiclassical model and may explain discrepancies between calculations and experimental data at high missing energies.

## Method

The authors used a semiclassical model that treats rescattering as a two-step process: first, an intermediate nucleon (proton or neutron) is knocked out by the electron in the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA), then it propagates through the nucleus and scatters from another bound nucleon, producing the detected proton. The model employs Glauber theory with in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections extracted from the SAID phase shift analysis, includes Pauli blocking and Fermi motion effects, and uses a local density approximation with pair distribution functions to account for nucleon correlations.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear transparency T = 0.63 for Carbon-12 and T = 0.29 for Gold-197, for outgoing protons with energy E_f approximately 1.8 GeV
- For parallel kinematics: angle between momentum transfer and final proton momentum theta_qf approximately 5 degrees, final proton energy E_f approximately 1.6 GeV, four-momentum transfer Q^2 approximately 0.40 GeV^2
- For perpendicular kinematics: theta_qf approximately 30 degrees, E_f approximately 1 GeV, Q^2 approximately 0.40 GeV^2
- At missing energies E_m greater than 250 MeV in parallel kinematics, experimental results exceed calculated direct plus rescattering contributions by about an order of magnitude
- The Delta resonance excitation requires about 300 MeV more energy than the nucleon rescattering channels

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical model of VjP (1994) and Marcel (2003) to the kinematics of short-range correlation studies, specifically for the E97-006 experiment at Jefferson Lab. The calculations confirm the predictions of E97proposal that parallel kinematics minimize final state interactions, and are consistent with the trend predicted by MuetherFSI for (e,e'NN) reactions in superparallel kinematics. The results enable extraction of the correlated part of the nuclear spectral function from experimental data, but require future inclusion of Delta resonance and pion production channels to fully describe the data, particularly for heavy nuclei like Gold-197 where mean field contributions also become relevant at high missing energies.
