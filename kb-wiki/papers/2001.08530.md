# Skewness, kurtosis and the 5th and 6th order cumulants of net baryon-number distributions from lattice QCD confront high-statistics STAR data

> We present new results on up to $6^{th}$ order cumulants of net baryon-number fluctuations at small values of the baryon chemical potential, $μ_B$, obtained in lattice QCD calculations with physical values of light and strange quark masses. Representation of the Taylor expansions of higher order cumulants in terms of the ratio of the two lowest order cumulants, $M_B/σ_B^2=χ_1^B(T,μ_B)/χ_2^B(T,μ_B)$, allows for a parameter free comparison with data on net proton-number cumulants obtained by the STAR Collaboration in the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC. We show that recent high statistics data on skewness and kurtosis ratios of net proton-number distributions, obtained at beam energy $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=54.4$ GeV, agree well with lattice QCD results on cumulants of net baryon-number fluctuations close to the pseudo-critical temperature, $T_{pc}(μ_B)$, for the chiral transition in QCD. We also present first results from a next-to-leading order expansion of $5^{th}$ and $6^{th}$ order cumulants on the line of pseudo-critical temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper shows that recent high-statistics STAR data on skewness and kurtosis ratios of net proton-number distributions at beam energy 54.4 GeV agree well with lattice QCD results for net baryon-number cumulants evaluated on the pseudo-critical line, but only for small values of the baryon chemical potential (μ_B/T ≲ 1). The authors also present first next-to-leading order results for 5th and 6th order cumulant ratios, which are predicted to be negative at this beam energy, in contrast to preliminary STAR findings for 6th order cumulants.

## Method

The HotQCD collaboration performed lattice QCD calculations with (2+1)-flavor HISQ fermions at physical quark masses on 32³×8 and 48³×12 lattices, using 294,000 to 1.6 million gauge configurations per temperature. They computed up to 8th order cumulants of net baryon-number, strangeness, and electric-charge fluctuations at zero chemical potentials, then constructed Taylor expansions for cumulant ratios under strangeness-neutral conditions with n_Q/n_B = 0.4. Continuum extrapolations were performed via joint fits of Nτ=8 and Nτ=12 data with O(a²) cutoff corrections.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical temperature at μ_B=0: T_pc⁰ = (156.5 ± 1.5) MeV, with curvature κ₂^B = 0.012(4)
- Difference R₄₂^B − R₃₁^B at μ_B=0 and T_pc: 0.008(3) (slightly positive)
- Difference R₆₂^B − R₅₁^B at μ_B=0 on Nτ=8 lattices: 0.029(9)
- NNLO corrections to R₃₁^B at μ_B/T = 0.8: about 5%; at μ_B/T = 1: about 10%
- NLO corrections to R₅₁^B at μ_B/T = 0.8 are about an order of magnitude larger than those in R₃₁^B at the same μ_B/T
- HRG model and QCD results for R₁₂^B agree up to μ_B ≈ 125 MeV (μ_B/T ≈ 0.8)

## Context

This work extends earlier next-to-leading order lattice QCD calculations of the first four cumulants by Bazavov et al. (2017) to include NNLO results for skewness and kurtosis ratios and first NLO results for 5th and 6th order cumulants. It confronts these calculations with high-statistics STAR data from the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC at 54.4 GeV (STAR Collaboration, 2020), which improves on the larger statistical errors that made earlier BES-I data at 62.4 GeV inconclusive. The results enable parameter-free comparison between lattice QCD and experimental cumulant ratios, and provide predictions for hyper-skewness and hyper-kurtosis that can be tested by ongoing and future STAR analyses.
