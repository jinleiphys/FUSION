# Analysis of low energy pion spectra

> The transverse mass spectra and the rapidity distributions of $π^+$ and $π^-$ in Au-Au collisions at 2, 4, 6, and 8 GeV$\cdot$A by E895 collaboration are fitted using an elliptically expanding fireball model with the contribution from the resonance decays and the final state Coulomb interaction. The ratio of the total number of produced $π^-$ and $π^+$ is used to fit the data. The resulting freeze-out temperature is rather low($T_f < 60$ MeV) with large transverse flow and thus resonance contribution is very small. The difference in the shape of $m_t$ spectra of the oppositely charged pions are found to be due to the Coulomb interaction of the pions.

## Key claim

The freeze-out temperature of pions in Au-Au collisions at 2–8 A·GeV is rather low (T_f < 60 MeV) with large transverse flow, making the resonance contribution very small; the difference in shape of the transverse mass spectra for oppositely charged pions is attributed to the Coulomb interaction of pions with the charged fireball, not to resonance decay.

## Method

The authors fit the transverse mass spectra and rapidity distributions of π⁺ and π⁻ measured by the E895 collaboration using an elliptically expanding fireball model with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and a linear transverse flow profile. They include contributions from resonance decays and a static final-state Coulomb interaction, treating the π⁻/π⁺ ratio and the Coulomb momentum shift as free fit parameters. The Cooper-Frye formalism is used to compute the invariant cross section from a Lorentz-boosted Boltzmann distribution at freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature T_f = 46 MeV at 2 A·GeV, 57 MeV at 4 A·GeV, 54 MeV at 6 A·GeV, and 55 MeV at 8 A·GeV (all below 60 MeV).
- π⁻/π⁺ ratio = 1.96 at 2 A·GeV, 1.95 at 4 A·GeV, 1.40 at 6 A·GeV, and 1.38 at 8 A·GeV (expected value near threshold is ~1.94).
- Coulomb momentum shift p_c = 25 MeV/c at 2 A·GeV, 24 MeV/c at 4 A·GeV, 18 MeV/c at 6 A·GeV, and 15 MeV/c at 8 A·GeV.
- Longitudinal expansion velocity > 0.8c required to fit the rapidity distribution width.
- χ²/n values: 1.3 at 2 A·GeV, 2.9 at 4 A·GeV, 2.4 at 6 A·GeV, and 1.8 at 8 A·GeV.

## Context

This work extends the elliptically expanding fireball model of Dobbler, Sollfrank, and Heinz (1999) to low beam energies (2–8 A·GeV) where the π⁻/π⁺ ratio deviates from unity due to isospin asymmetry, unlike at RHIC energies. The analysis contradicts earlier claims (Stock, 1986; Hong, 2005) that the low-momentum component of the pion spectrum arises from Δ resonance decay, showing instead that the low freeze-out temperature makes resonance contributions negligible. The results enable systematic study of the beam-energy dependence of the Coulomb interaction and the approach to chemical equilibrium in isospin as energy increases.
