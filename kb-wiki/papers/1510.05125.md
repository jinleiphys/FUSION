# Chiral phase transition and Schwinger mechanism in a pure electric field

> We systematically study the chiral symmetry breaking and restoration in the presence of a pure electric field in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential. In addition, we also study the effect of the chiral phase transition on the charged pair production due to the Schwinger mechanism. For these purposes, a general formalism for parallel electric and magnetic fields is developed at finite temperature and chemical potential for the first time. In the pure electric field limit $B\rightarrow0$, we compute the order parameter, the transverse-to-longitudinal ratio of the Goldstone mode velocities, and the Schwinger pair production rate as functions of the electric field. The inverse catalysis effect of the electric field to chiral symmetry breaking is recovered. And the Goldstone mode is find to disperse anisotropically such that the transverse velocity is always smaller than the longitudinal one, especially at nonzero temperature and baryon chemical potential. As expected, the quark-pair production rate is greatly enhanced by the chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a pure electric field always suppresses the chiral condensate (inverse catalysis) in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, and that the resulting chiral symmetry restoration significantly enhances the Schwinger pair production rate; however, these conclusions are derived within the NJL model framework and depend on the regularization scheme used to handle the non-renormalizability of the model.

## Method

The authors develop a general formalism for parallel electric and magnetic fields at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential using the Schwinger proper-time method in Euclidean space, then take the pure electric field limit \(B \to 0\). They solve the gap equation for the dynamical quark mass \(m\) and compute the thermodynamic potential, the transverse-to-longitudinal ratio of Goldstone mode velocities, and the Schwinger pair production rate, regularizing divergences with a three-momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\).

## Key numbers

- The dynamical quark mass decreases with increasing electric field strength \(E\), with chiral symmetry restored via a second-order phase transition at a critical field \(E_c\) (no explicit numerical value for \(E_c\) is given in the truncated text).
- The transverse sound velocity \(v_\perp\) relative to the longitudinal velocity \(v_3\) decreases with \(E\) and vanishes as \(E\) approaches \(E_c\) at zero temperature.
- The Schwinger pair production rate is given by \(\Gamma = \sum_{f=u,d} \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{N_c (q_f E)^2}{4\pi} \frac{e^{-n\pi m^2/|q_f E|}}{(n\pi)^2}\), which depends implicitly on temperature and chemical potential through the dynamical mass \(m\).
- The model parameters used are \(G = 5.01\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) and \(\Lambda = 0.65\ \text{GeV}\) in the chiral limit.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Klevansky (1989) on electric-field-induced chiral symmetry restoration at zero temperature, and by Gusynin, Miransky, and Shovkovy (1994–1995) on magnetic catalysis, by developing a finite-temperature and finite-chemical-potential formalism for parallel electric and magnetic fields. It also connects the chiral phase transition to the Schwinger mechanism, which was not previously combined in a single quark model. The results enable further studies of anomalous transport phenomena in heavy-ion collisions, such as the chiral electric separation effect proposed by Huang (2013).
