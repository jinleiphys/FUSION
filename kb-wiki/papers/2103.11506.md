# QCD Analysis of Near-Threshold Photon-Proton Production of Heavy Quarkonium

> The near threshold photo or electroproduction of heavy vector quarkonium off the proton is studied in quantum chromodynamics. Similar to the high-energy limit, the production amplitude can be factorized in terms of gluonic Generalized Parton Distributions and the quarkonium distribution amplitude. At the threshold, the threshold kinematics has a large skewness parameter $ξ$, leading to the dominance of the spin-2 contribution over higher-spin twist-2 operators. Thus threshold production data are useful to extract the gluonic gravitational form factors, allowing studying the gluonic contributions to the quantum anomalous energy, mass radius, spin and mechanical pressure in the proton. We use the recent GlueX data on the $J/ψ$ photoproduction to illustrate the potential physics impact from the high-precision data from future JLab 12 GeV and EIC physics program.

## Key claim

The paper claims that near-threshold photoproduction of heavy quarkonium can be factorized in QCD in terms of gluonic Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs), and that at threshold the large skewness parameter ξ → 1 causes the spin-2 (tensor) part of the gluon energy-momentum tensor to dominate over higher-spin twist-2 operators and over the scalar F² operator proposed in earlier work. This factorization is derived in the heavy-quark mass limit M_V → ∞, and the authors acknowledge that for realistic J/ψ production (M_V = 3.097 GeV) finite-mass corrections are important and that the leading-moment approximation receives about 25% contribution from higher moments in the asymptotic limit.

## Method

The authors performed a leading-order QCD factorization analysis of the two-gluon exchange diagrams for γ p → V p in the heavy-quark limit, using light-cone gauge and a non-relativistic approximation for the heavy meson wave function. They expressed the production amplitude in terms of gluon GPDs and then Taylor-expanded the hard kernel in moments of the GPD, showing that near threshold (ξ ≈ 1) the amplitude reduces to the matrix element of the gluon energy-momentum tensor. Using the resulting cross-section formula expressed in terms of gravitational form factors A_g(t), B_g(t), and C_g(t), they fitted the GlueX data on J/ψ photoproduction to extract the dipole mass parameter m_A and the form factor C_g(0), and used these to predict the cross section for Υ photoproduction.

## Key numbers

- For J/ψ photoproduction, the fit to GlueX data (with A_g(0)=0.58 and m_C=0.48 GeV fixed from lattice) yields m_A = 1.64 ± 0.11 GeV and C_g(0) = -0.84 ± 0.82.
- The non-relativistic wave function at origin for J/ψ is |ψ_NR(0)|² = 1.0952/(4π) (GeV)³; for Υ it is |ψ_NR(0)|² = 5.8588/(4π) (GeV)³.
- The strong coupling used is α_S(μ=2 GeV)=0.3 for J/ψ and α_S=0.2 for Υ.
- The ratio of the second moment to the leading moment of the gluon GPD at threshold for J/ψ (ξ=0.6) is approximately 0.1, based on CTEQ PDFs at μ=1.3 GeV.
- The scalar radius extracted from the fit combined with lattice quark data is ⟨r²⟩_s ≈ (1.03 fm)² and the mass radius is ⟨r²⟩_m ≈ (0.68 fm)².
- The threshold momentum transfer for J/ψ is -t_th = M_N M_V²/(M_N+M_V), which is of order M_V M_N.

## Context

This work extends the QCD factorization formalism for heavy vector meson photoproduction, previously established at high energy and small t by Ivanov (2004) and others, into the near-threshold region where the skewness ξ approaches 1. It contradicts the earlier proposal by Kharzeev (1995, 1998) that the process is dominated by the scalar gluon operator F² (related to the trace anomaly), showing instead that the spin-2 gluon energy-momentum tensor gives the leading contribution. The result agrees with holographic QCD predictions by Mamo (2019, 2021) that the leading contribution comes from 2⁺⁺ exchange. The analysis enables extraction of gluonic gravitational form factors from existing GlueX data and future JLab 12 GeV and EIC measurements, providing access to the gluon contributions to the proton's mass radius, spin, and mechanical pressure.
