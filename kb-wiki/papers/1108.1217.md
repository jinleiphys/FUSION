# Charge Symmetry Breaking and Nuclear Pion Production Reactions

> Large momentum transfer reactions such as pion production represent the frontier of Chiral Perturbation Theory and must be understood before more complex reactions can be considered. Pion production is also interesting in its own right, one application being the hadronic extraction of a charge symmetry breaking parameter: the contribution of the down-up quark mass difference to the neutron-proton mass difference. This dissertation reports on two primary projects: (1) a calculation of the charge symmetry breaking forward-backward asymmetry of the differential cross section of the n p -> d pi^0 reaction, and (2) the development of a new theoretical framework addressing the issue of reducibility in the impulse approximation's contribution to pion production. It is shown that the traditional one-body impulse approximation must be replaced by a two-body operator which makes a larger contribution to s-wave pion production.

## Key claim

This dissertation calculates the charge symmetry breaking (CSB) forward-backward asymmetry in the differential cross section of the \(np \to d\pi^0\) reaction using chiral perturbation theory, and demonstrates that the traditional one-body impulse approximation for pion production must be replaced by a two-body operator that makes a larger contribution to s-wave pion production. The claim is hedged by the use of specific phenomenological potentials and cutoff parameters, and the results depend on the inclusion of new CSB contributions discovered by Filin (2009).

## Method

The author performed a chiral perturbation theory calculation at next-to-leading order, including explicit Delta degrees of freedom, to compute reduced matrix elements for strong and CSB pion production in the \(np \to d\pi^0\) reaction. Nucleon-nucleon wave functions were obtained by numerically solving the Schrodinger equation with phenomenological potentials for both uncoupled and coupled channels (including the \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) deuteron channel), and the rescattering diagrams were evaluated using a hybrid momentum-position space technique with Fourier transforms. The CSB forward-backward asymmetry was then constructed from the interference of strong and CSB reduced matrix elements, incorporating the appropriate phase shifts for each partial wave.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of pion production reactions to include charge symmetry breaking effects, building on the strong-sector calculations of Baru (2009) and incorporating the new CSB contributions identified by Filin (2009). The development of a two-body operator to replace the traditional impulse approximation addresses a fundamental issue in the reducibility of the one-body contribution, which is necessary before more complex reactions such as pion production on heavier nuclei can be reliably calculated within the chiral effective field theory framework.
