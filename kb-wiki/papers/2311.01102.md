# Polarization effects in elastic deuteron-electron scattering

> The differential cross section and polarization observables for the elastic reaction induced by deuteron scattering off electrons at rest are calculated in the one-photon-exchange (Born) approximation. Specific attention is given to the kinematical conditions, that is, to the specific range of incident energy and transferred momentum. The specific interest of this reaction is to access very small transferred momenta. Numerical estimates are given for polarization observables that describe the of single- and double-spin effects, provided that the polarization components (both, vector and tensor) of each particle in the reaction are determined in the rest frame of the electron target.

## Key claim

The authors calculate polarization observables for elastic deuteron-electron scattering in the one-photon-exchange (Born) approximation, specifically for the inverse kinematics regime where the deuteron beam is much heavier than the electron target at rest. The key condition is that the accessible momentum transfer squared is extremely small (proportional to the electron mass squared), making this reaction a potential probe of deuteron electromagnetic form factors at very low \(Q^2\) values that are inaccessible in direct kinematics experiments.

## Method

The differential cross section and polarization observables are derived analytically in the laboratory frame (electron at rest) using the one-photon-exchange approximation, with the hadronic current expressed in terms of the three deuteron electromagnetic form factors (charge monopole \(G_C\), magnetic dipole \(G_M\), and charge quadrupole \(G_Q\)). Numerical estimates are performed using a parameterization of the deuteron form factors based on a two-component model (intrinsic structure plus vector meson cloud) with parameters fitted to world data, restricting the deuteron beam energy to a maximum of 200 GeV. The polarization observables are calculated for single-spin effects (tensor analyzing powers of the deuteron beam, tensor polarization of the scattered deuteron) and double-spin effects (polarization transfer coefficients and spin correlation coefficients involving vector-polarized deuterons and polarized electrons).

## Key numbers

- Maximum momentum transfer squared at \(E = 10\) GeV: \(Q^2_{max} \leq 25\) MeV\(^2\)
- Maximum recoil electron energy at \(E = 10\) GeV: \(\epsilon_{2max} \leq 25\) MeV
- Maximum deuteron beam energy considered: \(E = 200\) GeV, giving \(Q^2_{max} \leq 0.012\) GeV\(^2\)
- Deuteron magnetic moment normalization: \(G_M(0) = (M/m_N)\mu_d = 1.714\) with \(\mu_d = 0.857\)
- Deuteron quadrupole moment normalization: \(G_Q(0) = M^2 \hat{Q}_d = 25.83\) with \(\hat{Q}_d = 0.2857\) fm\(^2\)
- Maximum electron scattering angle considered: \(\theta_e \leq 50\) mrad

## Context

This work extends the formalism previously developed for elastic proton-electron scattering by Gakh (2011) to the deuteron case, motivated by the proton charge radius puzzle where muonic hydrogen measurements (Pohl, 2010) give \(r_c = 0.84184(67)\) fm, discrepant with earlier electron scattering results from the A1 Collaboration at Mainz (Bernauer, 2010) and CODATA values. The inverse kinematics approach allows access to very small momentum transfers, which could improve the extraction of the deuteron charge radius (currently \(<r^2> = 2.12799(74)\) fm from CODATA) by reducing uncertainties from extrapolation to \(Q^2 = 0\). The results also suggest that polarization observables in this reaction could be used to build beam polarimeters for high-energy polarized deuteron beams, similar to proposals for proton beams at RHIC energies (Glavanakov, 1996).
