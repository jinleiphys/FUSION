# Parity- and Time-Reversal-Violating Form Factors of the Deuteron

> We calculate the electric-dipole and magnetic-quadrupole form factors of the deuteron that arise as a low-energy manifestation of parity and time-reversal violation in quark-gluon interactions of effective dimension four and six: the QCD vacuum angle, the quark electric and chromo-electric dipole moments, and the gluon chromo-electric dipole moment. Within the framework of two-flavor chiral perturbation theory, we show that the relative sizes of the corresponding moments allow an identification of the symmetry-breaking source.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the parity- and time-reversal-violating electric-dipole and magnetic-quadrupole form factors of the deuteron within chiral perturbation theory, showing that the relative sizes of the corresponding moments can identify the dominant source of symmetry violation at the quark-gluon level, but only when combined with nucleon electric dipole moment measurements.

## Method

The authors use two-flavor chiral perturbation theory with pions, nucleons, and photons to compute the leading-order contributions to the deuteron electric-dipole form factor (EDFF) and magnetic-quadrupole form factor (MQFF) arising from four sources: the QCD vacuum angle (theta-bar), the quark electric dipole moment (qEDM), the quark chromo-electric dipole moment (qCEDM), and the gluon chromo-electric dipole moment (gCEDM). The calculation employs power-divergence subtraction with dimensional regularization and incorporates the fine-tuned nature of the deuteron bound state by summing leading nucleon-nucleon contact interactions to all orders.

## Key numbers

* The deuteron binding momentum is gamma approximately 45 MeV, which sets the scale of momentum variation for the form factors.
* For the theta-bar source, the deuteron electric dipole moment is bounded below by |d_d| greater than or approximately equal to 2.8 times 10 to the minus 4 times theta-bar e fm, using the most important long-range contributions as a lower bound.
* For the theta-bar source, the magnetic quadrupole moment is estimated as M_d approximately 2.0 times 10 to the minus 3 times theta-bar e fm squared.
* A null measurement of the deuteron EDM at the 10 to the minus 16 e fm level would constrain theta-bar less than or approximately 3 times 10 to the minus 13 and the dimensionless parameters for qCEDM, gCEDM, and qEDM to values corresponding to a new physics scale M_PT greater than or approximately 3 times 10 to the 7 GeV.
* The relative error of the results is estimated as gamma/M_NN approximately 30 percent, based on the expansion parameter of the power-counting scheme.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the deuteron electric dipole moment and magnetic quadrupole moment by Khriplovich and Korkin (1999), which did not exploit the chiral properties of the fundamental parity- and time-reversal-violating sources. The chiral perturbation theory framework provides a direct link to QCD by using the distinct chiral transformation properties of the theta-bar term, quark electric and chromo-electric dipole moments, and gluon chromo-electric dipole moment, as established in earlier work by de Vries, Mereghetti, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2011) and by Hockings and van Kolck (2005). The results enable the interpretation of planned storage-ring experiments measuring the deuteron electric dipole moment at the 10 to the minus 16 e fm level, and motivate investigation of the feasibility of measuring the deuteron magnetic quadrupole moment.
