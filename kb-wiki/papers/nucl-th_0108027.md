# $ω$-Meson Photoproduction on Nucleons In the Near Threshold Region

> The $ω$-meson photoproduction, $γ+p\to p+ω$, is studied in the framework of a model, containing $π$-meson exchange in t-channel and nucleon-exchange in s- and u-channels. Considering both $ωNN$-coupling constants in the region of time-like meson four momenta as the free parameters, we find different sets of solutions for these constants from the existing data on the t-dependence of the differential cross sections, $dσ(γ+p\to p+ω)/dt$, in the near threshold region $E_γ\leq 2$ GeV. These sets of $ωNN$-coupling constants, corresponding to destructive and constructive $π\bigotimes N$-interference contributions to $dσ/dt$ can be well distinguished by measurements of beam asymmetry, induced by linear photon polarization.

# ω-Meson Photoproduction on Nucleons In the Near Threshold Region

## Key claim

The existing differential cross section data for γ+p→p+ω in the near threshold region (Eγ ≤ 2 GeV) can be described by a model containing only π-meson exchange in the t-channel and nucleon exchange in s- and u-channels, without Pomeron exchange, provided the ωNN coupling constants are treated as free parameters in the time-like region. However, the fit is not unique: two distinct sets of ωNN coupling constants, corresponding to constructive versus destructive π⊗N interference, reproduce the data equally well and can only be distinguished by measurements of beam asymmetry Σ.

## Method

The authors constructed a model for γ+N→N+ω containing π-meson exchange in the t-channel and nucleon exchange in the s- and u-channels, using the standard transversal amplitude formalism in the center-of-mass system. The ωNN vector (gVωNN) and tensor (gTωNN) coupling constants in the time-like region (q² = m²ω) were treated as free fitting parameters, while the well-known coupling constants gωπγ and gπNN were fixed. A χ² minimization was performed against existing differential cross section data dσ(γp→pω)/dt at four photon energy intervals (1.23, 1.45, 1.68, and 1.92 GeV) with monopole form factors for the π-exchange vertices.

## Key numbers

- Four energy intervals used in the fit: 1.1 < Eγ < 1.35 GeV, 1.35 < Eγ < 1.55 GeV, 1.55 < Eγ < 1.8 GeV, and 1.8 < Eγ < 2.03 GeV, with central values Eγ = 1.23, 1.45, 1.68, and 1.92 GeV
- Solution (a) with "standard" cut-off parameters ΛπNN = 0.7 GeV and Λωπγ = 0.77 GeV: gVωNN = -1.4, gTωNN = 0.4, with χ²/ndf = 2.2
- Solution (b) with ΛπNN = 0.5 GeV, Λωπγ = 1.0 GeV: gVωNN = 0.5, gTωNN = 0.1, with χ²/ndf = 1.6
- Solution (c) with same cut-offs as (b): gVωNN = -0.4, gTωNN = 1.0, with χ²/ndf = 2.5
- For solution (b), the beam asymmetry Σ for γ+p→p+ω is predicted to be ≤ 0.1
- For solutions (a) and (c), the beam asymmetry Σ for γ+n→n+ω can reach the maximal value |Σ| = 1 at t ≈ 1.0 GeV²

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of vector meson photoproduction in the near threshold region (Eγ < 2 GeV) by addressing the nonresonant background mechanisms needed for missing resonance physics. Unlike previous approaches that relied on pure π-exchange or included Pomeron exchange (which the authors argue is invalid in the near threshold region where s ≫ M² and s ≫ t do not hold), this model demonstrates that nucleon exchange can reproduce the data. The authors note that the σ-exchange mechanism, important for ρ⁰ photoproduction, is problematic for ω photoproduction because the ωσγ coupling constant from Novosibirsk group measurements of ω→π⁰π⁰γ would require large σNN coupling that contradicts NN-potential constraints. The results enable future experiments at JLAB to distinguish between the coupling constant sets via beam asymmetry measurements, and the extracted time-like ωNN coupling constants can be applied to other ω-production processes such as π+N→N+ω and e⁻+N→e⁻+N+ω.
