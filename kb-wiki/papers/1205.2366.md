# P-Odd Pion Azimuthal Charge Correlations in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We argue that the large instanton induced Pauli form factor in polarized proton-proton scattering may cause, through topological fluctuations, substantial charge-dependent azimuthal correlations for pi^+/pi^- production in peripheral heavy ion collisions both at RHIC and LHC, thanks to the large induced magnetic field. Our results compare favorably to the measured pion azimuthal correlations by the STAR and ALICE collaborations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that large chirality-flip effects from QCD instanton and anti-instanton fluctuations, previously invoked to explain spin asymmetries in polarized proton-proton scattering, can also produce substantial charge-dependent azimuthal correlations for charged pions in peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, provided the large induced magnetic field polarizes the colliding protons; the mechanism is presented as complementary to the chiral magnetic effect and the results are described as comparing favorably to STAR and ALICE data, though the authors note their estimates for unlike-charge correlations overshoot the data for heavier ions.

## Method

The authors compute the ratio of P-odd to P-even contributions to the parton-parton scattering cross section using an instanton-induced vertex with a Pauli form factor, then convert this ratio into single-particle azimuthal asymmetry coefficients a_+ and a_- for pi^+ and pi^- production by folding with spin-polarized parton distribution functions. The two-particle azimuthal correlations are obtained from the variance of the topological charge Q in the prompt collision 4-volume, estimated using the instanton density, the collision geometry (transverse overlap area, proper time, pseudorapidity interval), and the deviation from Poissonian statistics in the instanton vacuum.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength: eB/m_pi^2 ≈ 1 at RHIC and eB/m_pi^2 ≈ 15 at LHC, lasting for about 1-3 fm/c.
- Instanton density: n_I ≈ 1/fm^4; typical instanton size: rho_c ≈ 1/3 fm; constituent quark mass: m_q* ≈ 300 MeV.
- For the numerical estimates, the authors assume <x> = 1/3, <x_F> = <z> = 0.5, tau = 3 fm, pseudorapidity interval (-4,4) for STAR and (-5,5) for ALICE, and nuclear radius R = 1 fm × A^(1/3).
- The results are compared to STAR data at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV for AuAu and CuCu collisions, and to ALICE data at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV for PbPb collisions.

## Context

This work extends the instanton-based mechanism for large single spin asymmetries in polarized proton-proton scattering (previously studied by Kochelev (1996), Qian and Zahed (2011), and others) to the case of unpolarized heavy ion collisions, where the magnetic field provides the proton polarization. The results are compared to the measured pion azimuthal charge correlations reported by the STAR collaboration (Abelev et al., 2009) and the ALICE collaboration (Selyuzhenkov et al., 2012), and the authors position their mechanism as complementary to the chiral magnetic effect proposed by Kharzeev et al. (1998, 2004, 2007) and Fukushima (2008), which operates in the deconfined vacuum rather than the confined instanton vacuum considered here.
