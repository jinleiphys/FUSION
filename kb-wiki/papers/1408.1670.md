# Universality far from equilibrium: From superfluid Bose gases to heavy-ion collisions

> Isolated quantum systems in extreme conditions can exhibit unusually large occupancies per mode. This over-population gives rise to new universality classes of many-body systems far from equilibrium. We present theoretical evidence that important aspects of non-Abelian plasmas in the ultra-relativistic limit admit a dual description in terms of a Bose condensed scalar field theory.

## Key claim

The paper presents theoretical evidence that longitudinally expanding non-Abelian gauge theories (relevant for heavy-ion collisions) and scalar field theories (relevant for ultracold Bose gases) belong to the same dynamical universality class far from equilibrium, despite having different symmetries and scattering processes. This universality is conditional on the regime of high mode occupancies and is demonstrated over a characteristic inertial range of transverse momenta, with the caveat that the deep infrared behavior and hard momentum regimes may differ between the two theories.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale real-time lattice simulations of both a massless scalar field theory with quartic interaction and a non-Abelian gauge theory (SU(2) color) in three spatial dimensions with longitudinal expansion. They initialized both systems with over-populated distribution functions and evolved them in proper time, computing the time-dependent distribution functions from equal-time correlation functions. The scaling exponents and scaling functions were extracted by analyzing the time evolution of the distribution as a function of transverse and longitudinal momentum, and comparing the results between the scalar and gauge theories.

## Key numbers

- The scaling exponents for both theories are α = -2/3 and γ = 1/3 to few percent accuracy, with β = 0, satisfying the scaling relation α - γ + 1 = 0.
- The reduced distribution function for the scalar theory follows a power law ~1/p_T for transverse momenta p_T ≲ Q, where Q is the characteristic momentum scale.
- The scalar theory simulations used up to 192² × 768 lattices with transverse spacings Qa_T = 0.5 - 1.1 and longitudinal spacings Qt₀ a_η = 0.04 - 0.075.
- The gauge theory simulations used 256² × 2048 lattices.
- The scalar theory was evolved up to t/t₀ = 85, while the gauge theory was evolved up to t/t₀ = 40.
- The scalar zero-mode condensate evolves in time as ~(t/t₀)^{-1/3}.
- The initial amplitude parameter n₀ = 35 and initial anisotropy ξ₀ = 1 were used for the scalar theory, with Qt₀ = 10³.

## Context

This work extends the concept of nonthermal fixed points previously studied in isotropic systems (Berges 2008, Scheppach 2009, Nowak 2010, Berges 2012) to longitudinally expanding systems, and directly compares scalar and gauge theories for the first time. It builds on earlier observations of scaling behavior in non-Abelian plasmas (Berges 2013, Schlichting 2012, Kurkela 2012) and in scalar field theories (Berges 2008, Berges 2008). The finding that both conservation laws (energy and particle number) constrain the same momentum regime in expanding systems, unlike the dual cascade seen in isotropic systems (Micha 2004), suggests a larger universality class. This work enables future experimental access to universal properties of extreme quantum systems using ultracold atomic gases, and challenges the understanding of thermalization in the Quark-Gluon Plasma at high energies.
