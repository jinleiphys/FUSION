# Dynamical evolution of the spectator systems produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> In peripheral heavy-ion collisions at ultra-relativistic energies, usually only parts of the colliding nuclei effectively interact with each other. In the overlapping zone, a fireball or quark-gluon plasma is produced. The excitation energy of the heavy remnant can range from a few tens to several hundreds of MeV, depending on the impact parameter. The decay of these excited spectators is investigated in this work for the first time within a dynamical approach based on the multi-dimensional stochastic Langevin equation. The potential of this exploratory work to understand the connection between electromagnetic fields generated by the heavy spectators and measured pion distributions is discussed.

## Key claim

This work investigates for the first time the decay of excited spectator remnants produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions using a dynamical multi-dimensional stochastic Langevin approach, rather than the usual statistical models. The exploratory study suggests that the time evolution of these spectators may be important for understanding electromagnetic field effects on charged pion distributions, but the authors note that the abrasion picture's upper limit of applicability at these beam energies was never tested.

## Method

The authors first compare three abrasion models (geometrical macroscopic, Gaimard-Schmidt ABRA, and microscopic Glauber) to determine the mass, charge, and excitation energy of spectator prefragments as a function of impact parameter for 208Pb+208Pb collisions at 158 GeV/nucleon. They then feed these prefragment properties into a dynamical decay model based on the multi-dimensional stochastic Langevin equation with four collective coordinates (elongation, neck thickness, left-right asymmetry, and orientation), restricted to evaporation and fission channels only. The dynamical results are compared to predictions from the statistical abrasion-ablation code ABRABLA.

## Key numbers

* Excitation energy of the spectator remnant can range from a few tens to several hundreds of MeV depending on impact parameter (abstract).
* For the geometrical macroscopic 'sphere-sphere' scenario, excitation energy can reach up to 500 MeV for close-to-central collisions.
* The 'sphere-plane' and 'sphere-cylinder' scenarios predict excitation energies below about 100 MeV and 150 MeV, respectively, for semi-central collisions.
* The spread in excitation energy depending on the liquid drop model parameterization used can reach 40 MeV.
* The Glauber model uses sigma_NN = 40 mb for practical calculations, with mean excitation energy of 10 to 20 MeV per abraded nucleon.
* The reaction studied is 208Pb+208Pb at 158 GeV/nucleon (sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV).
* The impact parameter considered in the prior work on electromagnetic effects on pions (Rybicki, 2007) is b approximately 10.5 fm.

## Context

This work extends the abrasion-ablation approach, previously successful at beam energies from about 100 to several thousands of MeV/nucleon (Gaimard, 1991; Kelic, 2008), to the ultra-relativistic energy domain of CERN SPS. The study is motivated by the suggestion of Rybicki (2007, 2013) that the electromagnetic field generated by fast-moving charged spectators can distort charged pion trajectories, which requires knowledge of the spectator lifetime. The dynamical Langevin approach enables time-dependent calculations that statistical models cannot provide, potentially enabling future connections between spectator decay timescales and measured pion distributions.
