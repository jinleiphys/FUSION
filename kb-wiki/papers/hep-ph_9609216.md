# Rho primes in analyzing e+e- annihilation, MARK III, LASS and ARGUS data

> The results of an analysis are presented of some recent data on the reactions $e^+e^-\toπ^+π^-π^+π^-$, $e^+e^-\toπ^+π^-π^0π^0$ with the subtracted $ωπ^0$ events, $e^+e^-\toωπ^0$, $e^+e^-\toηπ^+π^-$, $e^+e^-\toπ^+π^-$, $K^-p\toπ^+π^-Λ$, the decays $J/ψ\toπ^+π^-π^0$, $tau^-\toν_tauπ^+π^-π^-π^0$ $tau^-\toν_τωπ^-$, upon taking into account both the strong energy dependence of the partial widths on energy and the previously neglected mixing of the $ρ$ type resonances. The above effects are shown to exert an essential influence on the specific values of masses and coupling constants of heavy resonances and hence are necessary to be accounted for in establishing their true nature.

## Key claim

The inclusion of both the strong energy dependence of partial widths and the previously neglected mixing of rho-type resonances is shown to exert an essential influence on the specific values of masses and coupling constants of heavy resonances, and these effects are necessary to account for in establishing their true nature; the fits to data on e+e- annihilation into four-pion, omega-pi0, eta-pi+pi-, and pi+pi- final states, as well as J/psi, tau, and LASS data, do not at all demand the presence of the rho1' resonance if the pi+pi- production channels are discarded.

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous analysis of e+e- annihilation data (pi+pi-pi+pi-, pi+pi-pi0pi0 with subtracted omega-pi0 events, omega-pi0, eta-pi+pi-, pi+pi-), J/psi -> pi+pi-pi0 decays, tau- -> nu_tau pi+pi-pi-pi0 and tau- -> nu_tau omega pi- decays, and K-p -> pi+pi-Lambda data, using a field-theory-inspired approach that sums loop corrections to propagators of unmixed states. The analysis included three isovector resonances (rho(770), rho1', rho2') with energy-dependent partial widths and mixing via common decay channels, with the imaginary parts of the mixing polarization operators fixed by unitarity and the real part of the rho1'-rho2' mixing treated as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- rho(770) mass from fitting e+e- -> pi+pi-: m_rho = 774 +/- 10 MeV, with g_{rho-pi-pi} = 5.9 +/- 0.2, f_rho = 5.1 +/- 0.2, and delta_{rho-omega} = 2.4 +/- 1.4 MeV
- rho1' mass from e+e- -> pi+pi-pi+pi-: m_{rho1'} = 1.35 +/- 0.05 GeV
- rho1' mass from e+e- -> pi+pi-: m_{rho1'} = 1.37^{+0.09}_{-0.07} GeV
- rho1' mass from K-p -> pi+pi-Lambda: m_{rho1'} = 1.36^{+0.18}_{-0.16} GeV
- rho1' coupling to omega-pi from e+e- -> pi+pi-pi+pi-: g_{rho1'-omega-pi} = 14.9^{+3.6}_{-2.6} GeV^{-1}
- rho1' coupling to omega-pi from e+e- -> pi+pi-: g_{rho1'-omega-pi} = 16.6^{+2.2}_{-1.5} GeV^{-1}
- rho1' coupling to rho+rho- from e+e- -> pi+pi-pi0pi0: g_{rho1'-rho+rho-} = 7 +/- 3
- Leptonic width of rho1' from e+e- -> pi+pi-pi+pi-: Gamma_{rho1'-ee} = 5.2^{+2.2}_{-1.9} keV
- Leptonic width of rho1' from e+e- -> pi+pi-pi0pi0: Gamma_{rho1'-ee} = 12.9^{+4.5}_{-4.3} keV
- Suppression factor for rho1' coupling to rho-eta relative to quark model prediction: y_eta = 0.7 +/- 0.2
- Mass shift of rho(770) due to mixing with higher states: delta m_rho ~ 4 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Ercal and Olsson (1986) and Donnachie and collaborators (1987-1995) that ignored mixing and energy-dependent widths, and by Bityukov et al. (1987) who used naive Breit-Wigner models with fixed partial widths. The analysis also addresses the interpretation of resonance structures as possible exotic non-q-qbar states, following the approach of Achasov and Kozhevnikov (1988, 1990) who showed that such signals can be imitated by decays of higher excitations of the ground state vector nonet. The results enable future extraction of bare masses and coupling constants of heavy isovector resonances for comparison with predictions of their nature, and identify the threshold region in e+e- -> pi+pi-pi0pi0 as especially promising for improving the measurement of the rho1'-rho+rho- coupling constant.
