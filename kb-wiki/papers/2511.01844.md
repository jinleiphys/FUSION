# Accurate and Efficient Emulation of Proton-Deuteron Scattering via the Reduced Basis Method and Active Learning

> We introduce highly accurate and efficient emulators for proton-deuteron scattering below the deuteron breakup threshold. We explore two different reduced-basis method strategies: one based on the Kohn variational principle and another on Galerkin projections of the underlying system of linear equations. We use the adaptive greedy algorithm previously developed for two-body scattering for optimal selection of high-fidelity training points in the input parameter space. We demonstrate that these emulators reproduce ab initio hyperspherical harmonics calculations of $R$-matrix elements with remarkable precision, achieving relative errors as low as $10^{-7}$ with a small number of training points, even in regions of strong nonlinear parameter dependence. They also dramatically accelerate the exploration of the scattering predictions in the parameter space, a capability highly desired for calibrating (chiral) three-nucleon forces against scattering measurements. Our formalism can be further generalized to handle nucleon-deuteron scattering above the breakup threshold. These emulator developments will provide valuable tools to accelerate uncertainty quantification and rigorous parameter inference in the study of nuclear forces.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that reduced-basis method (RBM) emulators, combined with an adaptive greedy algorithm for training point selection, can reproduce ab initio hyperspherical harmonics calculations of proton-deuteron scattering R-matrix elements with relative errors as low as 10^{-7} using a small number of training points, but only for scattering energies below the deuteron breakup threshold where inelastic channels are absent.

## Method

The authors constructed two types of RBM emulators for proton-deuteron scattering: one based on the Kohn variational principle and another using Galerkin or Least-Squares Petrov-Galerkin projections of the underlying linear system. Both approaches exploit the affine parameter dependence of the Hamiltonian in chiral effective field theory, where the Hamiltonian decomposes as H(theta) = sum_i theta_i H_i. The adaptive greedy algorithm iteratively selects high-fidelity training points in the (c_D, c_E) parameter space by using the residual norm as a computationally inexpensive error proxy, then constructs a low-dimensional subspace from the snapshot solutions at those points.

## Key numbers

- Relative emulation errors as low as 10^{-7} achieved with 12 training points in the two-dimensional (c_D, c_E) parameter space for the half-plus channel
- With only 7 training points, relative errors already at about 10^{-4} or smaller for all R-matrix elements
- High-fidelity calculation solves a linear system of order ~10^4, taking about 150 seconds per evaluation
- Emulator solves a linear system of order ~10, taking about 1 microsecond per evaluation
- Residual norm ||r^{a=1}|| reaches 10^{-4} level in worst cases when approximately 10 training points are used at scattering energy E = 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior RBM emulation techniques developed for two-body scattering (Maldonado 2025) and three-body scattering (Zhang 2021) to realistic proton-deuteron scattering with chiral effective field theory forces, including coupled-channel effects and Coulomb interaction. The emulators overcome the computational bottleneck that previously prevented systematic exploration of three-nucleon force parameter space for Bayesian inference and uncertainty quantification. The approach enables large-scale calibration of three-nucleon force low-energy constants against experimental scattering data, and can be generalized to handle nucleon-deuteron scattering above the breakup threshold by including inelastic channels.
