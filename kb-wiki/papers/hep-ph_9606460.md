# Charmonium Suppression by Comover Scattering in Pb+Pb Collisions

> The first reports of $ψ$ and $ψ'$ production from experiment NA50 at the CERN SPS are compared to predictions based on a hadronic model of charmonium suppression. Data on centrality dependence and total cross sections are in good accord with these predictions.

# Charmonium Suppression by Comover Scattering in Pb+Pb Collisions

## Key claim
The apparent threshold effect in the NA50 data on ψ production in Pb+Pb collisions is not a signal of quark-gluon plasma formation, but instead reflects the approach to the geometrical limit of the mean path length L as collisions become increasingly central; the data can be explained by a hadronic model of charmonium suppression via comover scattering using parameters fixed earlier to describe S+U data.

## Method
The authors computed ψ and ψ′ production cross sections in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon using a hadronic comover model that includes nucleon absorption and scattering with produced mesons (comovers). The survival probability for nucleon absorption was calculated using a standard exponential formula with a ψ-nucleon absorption cross section σψN ≈ 4.8 mb fixed from pA data, while the comover survival probability was computed from the initial density of ρ, ω, and η mesons, the transverse size of the system, and a comover cross section σco ≈ 2σψN/3. The transverse energy dependence was obtained by folding impact-parameter-dependent cross sections with a Gaussian probability distribution P(ET,b) parameterized using phenomenological calorimeter-dependent constants ε and ω, adjusted for the NA50 calorimeter acceptance (1.1 < η < 2.3) and the dimuon mass range 2.9 < M < 4.5 GeV.

## Key numbers
- ψ-nucleon absorption cross section: σψN ≈ 4.8 mb, fixed from pA data in Ref. Gavin et al. (1994)
- Comover cross section: σco ≈ 2σψN/3 ≈ 3.2 mb
- Initial comover density in central collisions: n̄0 = 0.8 fm⁻³, chosen to fit the central S+U datum
- Transverse size of Pb system: RT ≈ RA = 6.6 fm, compared to 3.6 fm for S+U
- Comover contribution logarithm in survival probability increases by ≈ 60% in the larger Pb system relative to S+U
- Total ψ production cross section per nucleon pair: (σ/AB)ψ = 0.54 nb for Pb+Pb at 158 GeV (in NA50 acceptance), compared to experimental value 0.44 ± 0.005 ± 0.032 nb
- Total ψ production cross section per nucleon pair: (σ/AB)ψ = 0.95 nb for S+U at 200 GeV, compared to experimental value 1.03 ± 0.04 ± 0.10 nb
- Drell-Yan cross section per nucleon pair: (σ/AB)DY = 37.2 pb for Pb+Pb, compared to NA50 value 32.8 ± 0.9 ± 2.3 pb
- Elementary Drell-Yan cross section: σNNμ+μ− ≈ 37.2 pb for mass range 2.9 < M < 4.5 GeV
- Charm K-factor: Kc = 2.7; color evaporation coefficient: Fψ = 2.54%; Drell-Yan K-factor: KDY = 2.4
- Calorimeter parameters for NA50: ε = 0.35 GeV, ω = 3.2; for NA38: ε = 0.64 GeV, ω = 3.2
- Comover formation time: τ0 ∼ 2 fm; average ψ-ρ relative velocity: vrel ∼ 0.6
- ψ′ to ψ production ratio from pp data: Bσψ′NN/BσψNN = 0.02
- ψ′ absorption cross section in octet model: σψ′co ≈ 12 mb

## Context
This work extends the hadronic comover model developed by Gavin and Vogt (1990) and Gavin, Satz, Thews, and Vogt (1994) to explain the NA50 Pb+Pb data, which had been interpreted by Blaizot and Ollitrault (1996) and Kharzeev (1996) as possible evidence for quark-gluon plasma formation. The authors had previously shown that comover scattering was necessary to describe S+U data from NA38 (1991 run), and the 1992 NA38 data support that conclusion. The paper enables further experimental tests through SPS inverse-kinematics experiments (B < A) and AGS pA studies near the ψ threshold to distinguish between hadronic and plasma-based suppression mechanisms. The authors note that cascade calculations by Cassing and Ko (1996), Kahana, Kahana, and Pang (in progress), and Gerland et al. (in progress) confirm their conclusions.
