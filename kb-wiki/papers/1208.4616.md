# Symmetries of Doubly Heavy Baryons

> For very heavy quarks, the two heavy quarks in a doubly heavy baryon are expected to form a pointlike, heavy diquark in an antitriplet color configuration. In this limit the dynamics of the light degrees of freedom `factorize' from the dynamics of the heavy diquark system, and a superflavor symmetry emerges which relates the properties of doubly heavy baryons to heavy mesons. The charm quark may not be heavy enough for the $cc$ diquark in a $Ξ_{cc}$ to be regarded as pointlike. However, there are indications from the results of a nonrelativistic constituent quark model that many of the consequences of factorization emerge even though the model does not assume a quark-diquark structure and the mean separation of the two heavy quarks in the model is not small. We discuss the consequences of factorization for the spectroscopy of doubly heavy baryons and compare these consequences to results from a quark model. We also discuss the possibility of treating the strange quark as a heavy quark and applying these ideas to the $Ξ$.

## Key claim

For doubly heavy baryons with very heavy quarks, the two heavy quarks are expected to form a pointlike diquark, leading to a superflavor symmetry that relates their properties to heavy mesons; however, the charm quark may not be heavy enough for the cc diquark in a Xi_cc to be regarded as pointlike, yet results from a nonrelativistic constituent quark model indicate that many consequences of factorization emerge even though the model does not assume a quark-diquark structure and the mean separation of the two heavy quarks is not small.

## Method

The authors extracted pion-emission Heavy Diquark Symmetry (HDS) amplitudes from the nonrelativistic constituent quark model of Roberts and Pervin (2008) using the 3P0 model for transitions. They computed these amplitudes for transitions between multiplets of doubly heavy baryons (Xi_cc, Xi_bc, Xi_bb) and for analogous transitions in Xi, Xi_c, and Xi_b baryons by treating the strange quark as a heavy quark. Phase space and spin-counting factors were divided off so that all amplitudes within each section should be equal according to lowest-order HDS predictions.

## Key numbers

- For Xi_cc baryons, the HDS amplitude for the transition from the 1^3S_1 x 1P_1/2 multiplet (J^P = 1/2-, 3/2-) to the 1^3S_1 x 1S_1/2 multiplet (J^P = 1/2+, 3/2+) is 0.708 GeV^{-1/2} for the 1/2- to 1/2+ transition and 0.786 GeV^{-1/2} for the 3/2- to 3/2+ transition.
- For Xi_bb baryons in the same transition, the HDS amplitude is 0.748 GeV^{-1/2} (1/2- to 1/2+) and 0.774 GeV^{-1/2} (3/2- to 3/2+).
- For Xi_bc baryons in the same transition, the HDS amplitude is 0.724 GeV^{-1/2} (1/2- to 1/2+) and 0.738 GeV^{-1/2} (3/2- to 3/2+).
- For Xi baryons (treating the strange quark as heavy) in the same transition, the HDS amplitude is 0.486 GeV^{-1/2} (1/2- to 1/2+) and 0.667 GeV^{-1/2} (3/2- to 3/2+).

## Context

This work extends the Heavy Diquark Symmetry framework developed by Eakins and Roberts (2012) and builds on the earlier Heavy Quark Symmetry of Isgur and Wise (1991). The results test whether the pointlike diquark assumption is necessary for the symmetry to hold, using the Roberts-Pervin quark model (Roberts and Pervin, 2008) which does not assume a pointlike diquark. The findings enable future tests of HDS predictions for strong decays of doubly heavy baryons, with more comprehensive results presented in a companion paper by Eakins and Roberts (2012, arXiv:1207.7163).
