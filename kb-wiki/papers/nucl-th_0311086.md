# Further explorations of Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass formulas. II: Role of the effective mass

> We have constructed four new complete mass tables, referred to as HFB-4 to HFB-7, each one including all the 9200 nuclei lying between the two drip lines over the range of Z and N>8 and Z<120. HFB-4 and HFB-5 have the isoscalar effective mass M*_s$ constrained to the value 0.92 M, with the former having a density-independent pairing, and the latter a density-dependent pairing. HFB-6 and HFB-7 are similar, except that M*_s is constrained to 0.8 M. The rms errors of the mass-data fits are 0.680, 0.675, 0.686, and 0.676 MeV, respectively, almost as good as for the HFB-2 mass formula, for which M*_s was unconstrained. However, as usual, the single-particle spectra depend significantly on M*_s. This decoupling of the mass fits from the fits to the single-particle spectra has been achieved only by making the cutoff parameter of the delta-function pairing force a free parameter. An improved treatment of the center-of-mass correction was adopted, but although this makes a difference to individual nuclei it does not reduce the overall rms error of the fit. The extrapolations of all four new mass formulas out to the drip lines are essentially the same as for the original HFB-2 mass formula.

## Key claim

The authors show that constraining the isoscalar effective mass \(M^*_s/M\) to values as low as 0.8 (consistent with infinite nuclear matter calculations) yields mass-table fits with rms errors almost as good as those from unconstrained fits (where \(M^*_s/M\) naturally settles near 1.0), but only if the cutoff parameter of the delta-function pairing force is treated as a free parameter to compensate for the change in effective mass.

## Method

Four new Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mass tables (HFB-4 through HFB-7) were constructed for all 9200 nuclei between the drip lines with \(Z, N \ge 8\) and \(Z \le 120\). The isoscalar effective mass at saturation density was constrained to either \(0.92M\) (HFB-4 and HFB-5) or \(0.8M\) (HFB-6 and HFB-7), with each value tested both with density-independent pairing and with density-dependent pairing of the form given by Garrido et al. (1999). An improved center-of-mass correction was applied perturbatively, and the pairing cutoff parameter \(\varepsilon_{\Lambda}\) was treated as a free parameter in the fits to the 2135 measured masses from the 2001 Atomic Mass Evaluation.

## Key numbers

- RMS errors of the mass-data fits: 0.680 MeV (HFB-4), 0.675 MeV (HFB-5), 0.686 MeV (HFB-6), and 0.676 MeV (HFB-7), compared to 0.674 MeV for the unconstrained HFB-2.
- The isoscalar effective mass \(M^*_s/M\) was constrained to 0.92 for HFB-4 and HFB-5, and to 0.80 for HFB-6 and HFB-7.
- The pairing cutoff parameter \(\varepsilon_{\Lambda}\) was set to 16 MeV for HFB-4 and HFB-5, and to 17 MeV for HFB-6 and HFB-7.
- The improved center-of-mass correction changed the rms error negligibly, from 0.680 to 0.681 MeV when refitting the BSk4 force with the old approximation.
- For charge radii of 523 nuclei, the rms deviations were 0.0282 fm (HFB-4), 0.0270 fm (HFB-5), 0.0262 fm (HFB-6), and 0.0260 fm (HFB-7).
- The symmetry coefficient \(J\) was set to 28.0 MeV for all forces except BSk5, where it was constrained to 28.7 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier HFB-2 and HFB-3 mass tables (Goriely et al., 2002; Samyn et al., 2003) by systematically testing the role of the isoscalar effective mass, a parameter previously left unconstrained. It contradicts the earlier finding by Farine et al. (2001) that constraining \(M^*_s/M\) to 0.8 forced the rms error above 1.1 MeV; the present work achieves near-optimal fits by exploiting the pairing cutoff as a free parameter. The new mass tables enable more reliable extrapolations to neutron-rich nuclei for astrophysical applications such as stellar collapse and neutron-star mergers, and provide forces with neutron-matter equations of state that avoid the collapse seen for BSk2 at high densities.
