# Three-Body Halo Fragmentation: Polarization effects

> Momentum distributions of particles from nuclear break-up of fast three-body halos are calculated for polarized beam and outgoing fragments. These momentum distributions are combined in observable quantities which emphasize the two-body correlations in the initial three-body structure as well as in the final two-body system. Applying the formalism to 11Li we find a sensitive dependence on the p-wave content and on the position of the p-resonances in 10Li. Polarization experiments are then a new tool to study correlations in nuclear halos.

## Key claim

The paper claims that polarization observables (asymmetries) in three-body halo fragmentation reactions are significantly more sensitive to the \(p\)-wave content and the positions of \(p\)-resonances in the neutron-core subsystem than unpolarized momentum distributions, but this sensitivity depends critically on the inclusion of final-state interactions (FSI) between the two surviving particles.

## Method

The authors use the sudden (Serber) approximation, where the transition matrix is proportional to the overlap between the initial three-body wave function (obtained by solving Faddeev equations in coordinate space and expanded in hyperspherical harmonics) and the final-state wave function (including a distorted wave for the two-body subsystem). They compute one- and two-dimensional neutron momentum distributions for polarized beam and polarized fragments, then define asymmetries \(A_i^{MM'}\) and \(A_i^{MM';\sigma\sigma'}\) that vanish for unpolarized beams and isolate contributions from \(\ell_x \neq 0\) waves.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{11}\)Li fragmentation, the lowest virtual \(s\)-state in \(^{10}\)Li is at 50 keV, and the lowest \(p\)-resonance is at 0.5 MeV, corresponding to a momentum of 30 MeV/c.
* Three different \(p\)-wave contents in the neutron-\(^9\)Li channel are considered: 4%, 18%, and 35%.
* The second \(p\)-resonance in \(^{10}\)Li is placed at 1.5 MeV (momentum \(\sim\)50 MeV/c) for the 35% \(p\)-wave case, 2 MeV (\(\sim\)60 MeV/c) for the 18% case, and 6 MeV (\(\sim\)100 MeV/c) for the 4% case.
* The \(^{11}\)Li wave function contains more than 95% of total orbital angular momentum \(L=0\) configuration.

## Context

This work extends the sudden-approximation treatment of halo fragmentation (Serber 1947; Zhukov 1993; Thompson 1993) by incorporating polarization of the beam and outgoing fragments, building on earlier studies that showed FSI improves agreement with data (Zinser 1995; Korsheninnikov 1995; Garrido 1995). The method enables future experiments to use polarization asymmetries as a new tool to extract the \(p\)-wave content and resonance structure of unbound two-body subsystems like \(^{10}\)Li, which are difficult to probe with unpolarized momentum distributions alone.
