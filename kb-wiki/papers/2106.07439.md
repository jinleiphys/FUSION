# Comparative study of ${}^6$He $β$-decay based on different similarity-renormalization-group evolved chiral interactions

> We report on a study of the Gamow-Teller matrix element contributing to ${}^6$He $β$-decay with similarity renormalization group (SRG) versions of momentum- and configuration-space two-nucleon interactions. These interactions are derived from two different formulations of chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) -- without and with the explicit inclusion of $Δ$-isobars. We consider evolution parameters $Λ_{\rm SRG}$ in the range between 1.2 and 2.0 fm$^{-1}$ and, for the $Δ$-less case, also the unevolved (bare) interaction. The axial current contains one- and two-body terms, consistently derived at tree level (no loops) in the two distinct $χ$EFT formulations we have adopted here. The ${}^6$He and ${}^6$Li ground-state wave functions are obtained from hyperspherical-harmonics (HH) solutions of the nuclear many-body problem. In $A\,$=$\,6$ systems, the HH method is limited at present to treat only two-body interactions and non-SRG evolved currents. Our results exhibit a significant dependence on $Λ_{\text{SRG}}$ of the contributions associated with two-body currents, suggesting that a consistent SRG-evolution of these is needed in order to obtain reliable estimates. We also show that the contributions from one-pion-exchange currents depend strongly on the model (chiral) interactions and on the momentum- or configuration-space cutoffs used to regularize them. These results might prove helpful in clarifying the origin of the sign difference recently found in No-Core-Shell-Model and Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of the ${}^6$He Gamow-Teller matrix element.

## Key claim

The Gamow-Teller matrix element for ${}^6$He $\beta$-decay shows a significant dependence on the similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution parameter $\Lambda_{\text{SRG}}$ for two-body current contributions, and the one-pion-exchange current contributions depend strongly on the chiral interaction model and regularization scheme, suggesting that consistent SRG evolution of currents is needed for reliable estimates.

## Method

The authors used hyperspherical-harmonics (HH) solutions of the nuclear many-body problem to obtain ${}^6$He and ${}^6$Li ground-state wave functions from two-nucleon interactions only, neglecting three-nucleon interactions. They employed two different chiral effective field theory interactions, one without $\Delta$-isobars (the E model, N2LO with 450 MeV cutoff) and one with $\Delta$-isobars (the P model, NV2-Ia with regulators $R_S=0.8$ fm and $R_L=1.2$ fm), and evolved them via SRG at $\Lambda_{\text{SRG}}$ values of 1.2, 1.5, 1.8, and 2.0 fm$^{-1}$, plus the bare (unevolved) E interaction. The axial current included one- and two-body terms derived at tree level in the corresponding chiral formulations, but the currents were not SRG-evolved.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for ${}^6$Li with the E model range from 30.33(20) MeV (bare) to 33.47(2) MeV (SRG1.5); experimental value is 31.99 MeV.
- Binding energies for ${}^6$He with the E model range from 27.51(23) MeV (bare) to 30.31(1) MeV (SRG1.5); experimental value is 29.27 MeV.
- The LO (one-body) reduced matrix element (RME) for the E model ranges from 2.321(9) (bare) to 2.345(2) (SRG1.2).
- The N3LO(OPE) two-body contribution to the RME for the E model varies from +0.002(1) (bare) to -0.038(1) (SRG1.2), changing sign with SRG evolution.
- The N3LO(OPE) two-body contribution to the RME for the P model ranges from +0.011 (SRG1.2) to +0.022 (SRG2.0), remaining positive.
- The full RME for the E model ranges from 2.271(3) (SRG1.2) to 2.303(11) (bare); for the P model from 2.251(3) (SRG1.5) to 2.265(2) (SRG1.2); experimental value is 2.1609(40).
- The low-energy constant $z_0$ fitted to tritium $\beta$-decay ranges from 0.0193 fm$^3$ (E-bare) to 0.1104 fm$^3$ (E-SRG1.2) for the E model, and from 0.3764 fm$^3$ (P-SRG2.0) to 0.4665 fm$^3$ (P-SRG1.2) for the P model.

## Context

This work extends the HH method to $A=6$ nuclei (Gnech 2020) and aims to clarify the sign difference in two-body current contributions to the ${}^6$He Gamow-Teller matrix element found between No-Core-Shell-Model calculations (Gysbers 2019) and Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations (Pastore 2018, King 2020). The results show that the sign of the one-pion-exchange current contribution depends on the interaction model and SRG evolution, which may help resolve the discrepancy. The study enables future work that includes consistent SRG evolution of axial currents and three-nucleon interactions to obtain fully reliable estimates.
