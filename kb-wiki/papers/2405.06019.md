# BBN catalysis by doubly charged particles

> We consider primordial nucleosynthesis in the presence of hypothetical quasi-stable doubly charged particles. Existence of $X^{--}$ with macroscopic lifetimes will lead to the formation of its bound states with $^4$He and other light elements, significantly facilitating the subsequent formation of lithium nuclei. From observational constraints on maximum allowable amount of lithium, that we update in this work, we derive strong constraints on the abundance and lifetime of $X^{--}$. In a likely cosmological freeze-out scenario with temperatures initially exceeding the mass of $X^{--}$, the BBN constrains the lifetime of these particles to be less than about 100 seconds. For parametrically long lifetimes, lithium abundance data constrain $X^{--}$ abundance to be less than $10^{-9}$ relative to protons, regardless of whether these particles decay or remain stable. Stable particles could saturate the dark matter density only if their mass is comparable to or in excess of $10^{10}$ GeV, and most of $X^{--}$ will be found in bound states with beryllium nuclei, so that chemically they would appear as abnormally heavy helium isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hypothetical quasi-stable doubly charged particles ($X^{--}$) with macroscopic lifetimes would catalyze lithium production during Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) by forming bound states with $^4$He and other light elements, but the abstract's claim that BBN constrains the lifetime to less than about 100 seconds applies only under a specific freeze-out scenario where temperatures initially exceed the mass of $X^{--}$; for parametrically long lifetimes, the constraint comes instead from lithium abundance data limiting $X^{--}$ abundance to less than $10^{-9}$ relative to protons.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation for two-body Coulomb bound states of $X^{--}$ with light nuclei, treating nuclei as uniformly charged spheres with known r.m.s. charge radii, to compute binding energies and reaction rates. They then construct a catalyzed BBN reaction network treating $X^{--}$ as a perturbation to the standard BBN reaction network, working to first order in $X^{--}$ concentration, and calculate yields of lithium, beryllium, and boron isotopes produced via reactions such as $(^4\text{He}X^{--}) + ^4\text{He} \to (^8\text{Be}X^{--}) + \gamma$. Observational constraints on primordial lithium, beryllium, and boron abundances are updated and compared with the catalyzed yields to derive limits on $X^{--}$ abundance and lifetime.

## Context

This work extends the CBBN (Catalyzed Big Bang Nucleosynthesis) framework previously developed for singly charged particles $X^-$ by Pospelov (2006) to the case of doubly charged particles $X^{--}$, which had not been studied before. The authors note that muon-catalyzed fusion was investigated by Breunlich (1989) and that a heavy doubly charged particle was recently argued by Akhmedov (2021) to potentially achieve positive energy balance in catalyzed reactions. The analysis provides constraints that complement collider searches at ATLAS and CMS (ATLAS 2019, CMS 2016) and cosmic ray searches, filling the gap for lifetimes between nanoseconds and years where only indirect cosmological studies are possible. The results also provide a lower limit on the mass of dark matter candidates consisting of stable $X^{--}$ bound to helium and beryllium nuclei, as suggested by Fargion (2005) and Cudell (2014).
