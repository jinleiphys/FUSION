# Covariant description of inelastic electron--deuteron scattering:predictions of the relativistic impulse approximation

> Using the covariant spectator theory and the transversity formalism, the unpolarized, coincidence cross section for deuteron electrodisintegration, $d(e,e'p)n$, is studied. The relativistic kinematics are reviewed, and simple theoretical formulae for the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) are derived and discussed. Numerical predictions for the scattering in the high $Q^2$ region obtained from the RIA and five other approximations are presented and compared. We conclude that measurements of the unpolarized coincidence cross section and the asymmetry $A_ϕ$, to an accuracy that will distinguish between different theoretical models, is feasible over most of the wide kinematic range accessible at Jefferson Lab.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that measurements of the unpolarized coincidence cross section and the asymmetry \(A_\phi\) for deuteron electrodisintegration, to an accuracy that will distinguish between different theoretical models, are feasible over most of the wide kinematic range accessible at Jefferson Lab, but only within the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) which omits final state interactions and interaction currents.

## Method

The authors used the covariant spectator theory and the transversity formalism to derive and compute the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) for the \(d(e,e'p)n\) reaction. They calculated the unpolarized coincidence cross section and the asymmetry \(A_\phi\) from the RIA and five other relativistic or semi-relativistic approximations, covering a broad range of \(Q^2\) and \(W\) values accessible at Jefferson Lab. The RIA matrix element was constructed from pole diagrams with one nucleon on-shell and the deuteron described by a covariant \(dnp\) vertex, and the calculation was performed in the center-of-momentum frame to avoid singularities in the lab frame Jacobian.

## Key numbers

* At high \(Q^2\), relativistic and nonrelativistic predictions for \(d(e,e'p)n\) often differ by as much as an order of magnitude, extending the observations of Arenhövel, Beck, and Wilbois.
* The symmetry point momenta \(p_s\) reach near one GeV at large \(Q^2\) (about 2 to 3 GeV\(^2\)) for large \(x\) values where pion production is not large.
* The analysis covers the kinematic region up to \(Q^2 = 3\) GeV\(^2\) and includes values of the Bjorken scaling variable \(x\) from 0.5 to 2.0.

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator theory previously applied to \(NN\) scattering by Gross, Van Orden, and Holzwarth (1992) and to deuteron electromagnetic form factors by the same group. It provides a fully relativistic framework for analyzing the high-\(Q^2\) data expected from Jefferson Lab, where earlier nonrelativistic calculations by Arenhövel, Beck, and Wilbois were shown to be inadequate. The paper enables future inclusion of final state interactions and interaction currents, which are necessary for a complete gauge-invariant calculation, and provides a baseline RIA prediction against which more complete models can be compared.
