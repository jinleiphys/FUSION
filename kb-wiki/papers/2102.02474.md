# Entrainment effects in neutron-proton mixtures within the nuclear-energy density functional theory. I. Low-temperature limit

> Mutual entrainment effects in cold neutron-proton mixtures are studied in the framework of the self-consistent nuclear energy-density functional theory. Exact expressions for the mass currents, valid for both homogeneous and inhomogeneous systems, are directly derived from the time-dependent Hartree-Fock equations with no further approximation. The equivalence with the Fermi-liquid expression is also demonstrated. Focusing on neutron-star cores, a convenient and simple analytical formulation of the entrainment matrix in terms of the isovector effective mass is found, thus allowing to relate entrainment phenomena in neutron stars to isovector giant dipole resonances in finite nuclei. Results obtained with different functionals are presented. These include the Brussels-Montreal functionals, for which unified equations of state of neutron stars have been recently calculated.

## Key claim

Mutual entrainment effects in cold neutron-proton mixtures can be exactly described within the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) framework without further approximation, and the entrainment matrix in neutron-star cores reduces to a simple analytical form in terms of the isovector effective mass, provided the system is homogeneous and currents are small compared to critical currents for superfluidity breakdown.

## Method

The authors derived exact expressions for local nucleon mass currents directly from the TDHF equations, assuming temperatures much lower than superfluid critical temperatures and currents small enough to ignore pairing effects. They then specialized to homogeneous neutron-star core matter with stationary currents, obtaining the entrainment matrix elements in terms of functional derivatives of the nuclear energy with respect to isoscalar and isovector combinations of kinetic and momentum densities. The equivalence with the Fermi-liquid theory expression was demonstrated by evaluating the same functional derivatives in the static (zero-current) configuration and relating them to Landau effective masses and ℓ=1 Landau parameters.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless determinant of the entrainment matrix, Υ = (ρₙₙρₚₚ − ρₙₚ²)/(ρₙρₚ), coincides with the inverse of the isovector effective mass m/mᵥ⊕.
- Current estimates of the isovector effective mass at saturation density lie in the range mᵥ⊕/m ∼ 0.6–0.9.
- The Brussels-Montreal functionals BSk19 and BSk26 yield isovector effective masses significantly smaller than microscopic results from LNS (Cao 2006) and Skχm* (Zhang 2018) functionals.
- The functionals BSk21, BSk24, and BSk25 are consistent with microscopic predictions.
- The standard Skyrme functionals SLy4 (Chabanat 1998) and UNEDF (Kortelainen 2012) give substantially higher isovector effective masses than the microscopically calculated ones.
- For Skyrme-like functionals, Υ = 1 + (2/ℏ²)(C₀^τ − C₁^τ)ρ, where C₀^τ and C₁^τ are constant for standard Skyrme functionals and density-dependent for extended ones.

## Context

This work extends previous Fermi-liquid calculations of the entrainment matrix in neutron-proton mixtures (Borumand 1996, Gusakov 2005, Chamel 2006, Leinson 2017) by providing exact TDHF derivations valid for both homogeneous and inhomogeneous systems. It also generalizes earlier relativistic mean-field approaches (Comer 2003, Kheto 2014, Sourie 2016). The key result, linking entrainment to the isovector effective mass, enables direct connections between neutron-star dynamics and laboratory measurements of isovector giant dipole resonances in finite nuclei. The paper identifies BSk24 and BSk25 as particularly suitable functionals for dynamical simulations of superfluid neutron stars, building on unified equations of state previously calculated for the Brussels-Montreal functional series (Potekhin 2013, Pearson 2018).
