# Effect of nuclear deformation on Gamow-Teller strength distributions of Hg isotopes

> Recent studies \cite{1,2} predicted the sensitivity of the Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions to nuclear deformation in neutron-deficient Hg isotopes. Motivated by this work, we investigate nuclear ground-state properties and GT strength distributions for neutron-deficient Hg isotopes ($^{177\hbox{-}193}$Hg). The nuclear deformation ($β(E2)$) values were calculated using the **Relativistic Mean Field (RMF)** model. The RMF approach, with different density-dependent interactions (**DD-ME2** and **DD-PC1**), was employed to compute nuclear shape parameters. These computed deformation values were then used within the framework of the **deformed proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA)** model, with a separable interaction, to calculate the allowed GT strength distributions for these Hg isotopes. Our calculations validate the findings of \cite{1} and confirm the effect of deformation on GT strength distributions. This study may further provide a complementary signature for nuclear shape isomers. Noticeable differences are highlighted between our results and previous calculations. The study of \cite{1} suggests that $^{177\hbox{-}182}$Hg possess prolate shapes, while $^{184\hbox{-}196}$Hg exhibit oblate shapes. In contrast, our calculations predict **prolate** shapes for $^{177\hbox{-}188}$Hg and **oblate** shapes for $^{189\hbox{-}193}$Hg isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions in neutron-deficient Hg isotopes are sensitive to nuclear deformation, but that the summed GT strengths are not good observables for studying deformation effects. The authors qualify this by showing that their half-life calculations do change with deformation, contradicting the earlier finding of Moreno (2006) that half-lives are not sensitive enough to study deformation effects.

## Method

The authors first computed nuclear quadrupole deformation parameters (β₂) for ¹⁷⁷–¹⁹³Hg isotopes using the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model with two density-dependent interactions (DD-ME2 and DD-PC1). These deformation values were then used as input to the deformed proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with a separable schematic interaction to calculate allowed GT strength distributions and β-decay half-lives. The Nilsson model was employed for the deformed single-particle basis, and BCS approximation was used for pairing correlations.

## Key numbers

- The RMF calculation predicts prolate shapes for ¹⁷⁷–¹⁸⁸Hg and oblate shapes for ¹⁸⁹–¹⁹³Hg, in contrast to the earlier calculation by Boi15 which found prolate shapes for ¹⁷⁷–¹⁸²Hg and oblate for ¹⁸⁴–¹⁹⁶Hg
- The oblate DD-ME2 deformation values are larger by an average factor of 0.63 for the selected region compared to FRDM calculations of Moller (2012)
- As mass increases from ¹⁷⁷Hg to ¹⁹³Hg, the computed deformation decreases by a factor of 1.07, consistent with FRDM deformations which show a decrement factor of 0.99
- The ph and pp force parameters were set using χ_GT = 5.2/A⁰·⁷ and κ_GT = 0.58/A⁰·⁷ following Homma (1996)
- The pairing gaps were calculated using Δ_n = Δ_p = 12/√A MeV
- The ratio g_A/g_V was taken as -1.254 and D = 6295 s for half-life calculations

## Context

This work extends the studies of Moreno (2006) and Boi15, who predicted sensitivity of GT strength distributions to nuclear deformation in neutron-deficient Hg isotopes. The authors validate the finding of Boi15 that β-decay properties of neutron-deficient Hg isotopes are altered by changing nuclear deformation values, but contradict Moreno (2006) regarding the sensitivity of half-lives to deformation. The study enables future investigations of shape coexistence in this mass region, with the authors suggesting that transfer reaction data, B(E2), and ρ²(E0) measurements may help theoretical models better understand shape coexistence physics.
