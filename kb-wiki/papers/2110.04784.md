# System evolution of forward-backward multiplicity correlations in a multi-phase transport model

> The initial geometry effect on forward-backward multiplicity correlations $C(N_{f},N_{b})$ is studied in relativistic collisions between light nuclei by using a multiphase transport model (AMPT). It is found that tetrahedron $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O gives a more uniform and symmetrical fireball which produces a more isotropic distribution of final particles after the expansion and evolution, and leads to a small $C(N_{f},N_{b})$. Forward-backward multiplicity correlation could be taken as a useful probe to distinguish the pattern of $α$-clustered $^{16}$O in experiments by comparing the neighboring colliding nuclear systems like $^{14}$N + $^{14}$N and $^{19}$F + $^{19}$F.

## Key claim

The forward-backward multiplicity correlation \(C(N_f,N_b)\) can serve as a probe to distinguish the \(\alpha\)-clustered tetrahedral structure of \(^{16}\mathrm{O}\) from a Woods-Saxon distribution in relativistic collisions, but only when the neighboring systems \(^{14}\mathrm{N}+^{14}\mathrm{N}\) and \(^{19}\mathrm{F}+^{19}\mathrm{F}\) are also measured for comparison; the effect is visible in the AMPT model but the paper does not claim experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (a multi-phase transport) model to simulate collisions of light nuclei at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6370\) GeV and Au+Au at 200 GeV. They configured the initial nucleon distribution of \(^{16}\mathrm{O}\) either with a Woods-Saxon profile or with a tetrahedral four-\(\alpha\) cluster structure (parameters from the EQMD model, side length 3.42 fm). They computed the forward-backward multiplicity correlation coefficient \(C(N_f,N_b)\) for charged particles in symmetric pseudorapidity windows of width \(\delta\eta = 0.2\) as a function of centrality, impact parameter, pseudorapidity gap, transverse sphericity, and initial eccentricity coefficients \(\varepsilon_2\) and \(\varepsilon_3\).

## Key numbers

* Tetrahedral \(^{16}\mathrm{O}\) has a side length of 3.42 fm, giving an rms radius of 2.699 fm, compared to 2.726 fm for the Woods-Saxon configuration and the experimental value 2.6991 fm.
* In \(^{197}\mathrm{Au}+^{197}\mathrm{Au}\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the mean charged-particle multiplicity in \(0<\eta<0.2\) ranges from 93.23 (0%-10% centrality) down to 1.523 (90%-100% centrality); the ratio std/average rises from 0.147 to 0.951.
* In \(^{16}\mathrm{O}+^{16}\mathrm{O}\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6370\) GeV, the mean charged-particle multiplicity in the same window ranges from 27.59 (0%-10%) to 0.940 (90%-100%); the ratio std/average rises from 0.244 to 1.167.
* For \(p+p\) collisions, \(C(N_f,N_b)\) increases with collision energy: the mean multiplicity in \(0<\eta<0.8\) is about 1 at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 10\) GeV and about 3.5 at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5020\) GeV.
* The participant nucleon number for Woods-Saxon \(^{14}\mathrm{N}+^{14}\mathrm{N}\) has mean 12.11 and relative fluctuation 0.130; for Woods-Saxon \(^{16}\mathrm{O}+^{16}\mathrm{O}\) mean 14.10 and relative fluctuation 0.113; for tetrahedral \(^{16}\mathrm{O}+^{16}\mathrm{O}\) mean 14.31 and relative fluctuation 0.097; for Woods-Saxon \(^{19}\mathrm{F}+^{19}\mathrm{F}\) mean 16.99 and relative fluctuation 0.098.

## Context

This work extends the study of \(\alpha\)-clustering effects in relativistic collisions, previously explored through harmonic flows (PhysRevLett.112.112501, PhysRevC.90.064902), to the forward-backward multiplicity correlation observable. It uses the AMPT model, which has been applied to RHIC and LHC energies for flow and correlation studies (STARFlowAMPT, AMPTFlowLHC). The paper proposes that systematic measurements of \(^{16}\mathrm{O}+^{16}\mathrm{O}\) together with its neighbors \(^{14}\mathrm{N}+^{14}\mathrm{N}\) and \(^{19}\mathrm{F}+^{19}\mathrm{F}\) at LHC energies could experimentally distinguish the tetrahedral \(\alpha\)-cluster configuration from the Woods-Saxon one, and it provides predictions for the correlation strength as a function of impact parameter and pseudorapidity gap.
