# Characterizing the hydrodynamic response to the initial conditions

> In hydrodynamics, the momentum distribution of particles at the end of the evolution is completely determined by initial conditions. We study quantitatively to what extent anisotropic flow is determined by predictors such as the initial eccentricity in a set of realistic simulations, and we also show the importance of nonlinear terms in order to correctly predict the quadrangular flow. This knowledge will be important for making a more direct link between experimental observables and hydrodynamic initial conditions, the latter being poorly constrained at present.

# Characterizing the hydrodynamic response to the initial conditions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while elliptic flow ($v_2$) and triangular flow ($v_3$) are well predicted by the corresponding initial eccentricities $\varepsilon_2$ and $\varepsilon_3$, quadrangular flow ($v_4$) and pentagonal flow ($v_5$) require nonlinear combinations of initial-geometry terms, specifically $\varepsilon_2^2$ for $v_4$ and $\varepsilon_2\varepsilon_3$ for $v_5$, to achieve a good quantitative prediction across all centralities.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event ideal hydrodynamics using the NeXSPheRIO code with NeXus initial conditions for Au-Au collisions at top RHIC energy, generating 150 events per 10% centrality class plus 115 zero-impact-parameter events. They defined a quantitative "Quality" measure (the correlation coefficient between the true flow vector and a linear estimator) and minimized the mean-square error to find the best linear estimator of $v_n e^{in\Psi_n}$ from candidate initial-state quantities such as $\varepsilon_n e^{in\Phi_n}$ and nonlinear combinations like $(\varepsilon_2 e^{i2\Phi_2})^2$.

## Key numbers

- 150 events were generated in each 10% centrality class, plus 115 events with zero impact parameter.
- Particles from the pseudorapidity interval $|\eta| < 1$ were used in the analysis.
- For elliptic flow, the Quality of the $\varepsilon_2$ estimator is close to 1 for all centralities, including central collisions where $v_2$ arises from fluctuations.
- For triangular flow, the $r^3$-weighted $\varepsilon_3$ is a better predictor than the $r^2$-weighted definition for most centralities.
- For quadrangular flow, the combined estimator (linear $\varepsilon_4$ plus nonlinear $\varepsilon_2^2$) achieves good Quality for all centralities, whereas $\varepsilon_4$ alone gives Quality that decreases and becomes negative in peripheral collisions.

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event hydrodynamic studies of Gardim (2011), Petersen (2010), Qin (2010), and Qiu (2011), which had shown that $v_2$ and $v_3$ correlate with $\varepsilon_2$ and $\varepsilon_3$ but that $v_4$ and $v_5$ do not. It builds on the cumulant expansion framework of Teaney and Yan (2010), who suggested that higher-order or nonlinear terms beyond the simple eccentricity $\varepsilon_n$ could improve the hydrodynamic response description. The quantitative Quality measure enables direct comparison of different initial-state predictors, which will help constrain poorly known initial conditions from experimental flow data.
