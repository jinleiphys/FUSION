# Two-pion exchange three-nucleon potential: O(q^4) chiral expansion

> We present the expansion of the two-pion exchange three-nucleon potential (TPE-3NP) to chiral order q^4, which corresponds to a subset of all possibilities at this order and is based on the \piN amplitude at O(q^3). Results encompass both numerical corrections to strength coefficients of previous O(q^3) terms and new structures in the profile functions. The former are typically smaller than 10% whereas the latter arise from either loop functions or non-local gradients acting on the wave function. The influence of the new TPE-3NP over static and scattering three-body observables has been assessed and found to be small, as expected from perturbative corrections.

## Key claim

The paper presents the chiral expansion of the two-pion exchange three-nucleon potential (TPE-3NP) to order q^4, but this represents only a subset of all possible contributions at that order; the new terms are expected to be small perturbative corrections, with numerical changes to strength coefficients typically smaller than 10%.

## Method

The authors derived the TPE-3NP by relating it to the off-shell πN amplitude at O(q^3), using covariant chiral perturbation theory with the πN amplitude decomposed into pseudovector Born terms, Weinberg-Tomozawa contact terms, polynomial contributions from low-energy constants (LECs), and medium-range loop contributions from the nucleon pion cloud. The potential was constructed in momentum space and transformed to configuration space, yielding both local and non-local terms, with strength coefficients expressed in terms of the LECs c3 and c4, the scalar form factor σ(2m²), and the Goldberger-Treiman relation.

- Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy yields an 8% decrease in all parameters when using gA/fπ instead of g/m

## Context

This work extends the O(q³) TPE-3NP calculation of the Brazil group (CDR, 1990s) to O(q⁴) within the chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Weinberg (1990) and Becher and Leutwyler (2001). The new terms include loop functions (C₃⁺, C₃⁻) and non-local gradient interactions (C₂⁻) not present in earlier Tucson-Melbourne potentials (TM'93, TM'99). The results enable consistent inclusion of three-nucleon forces at the same chiral order as modern two-nucleon potentials, with the small corrections suggesting perturbative convergence of the chiral expansion for three-body forces.
