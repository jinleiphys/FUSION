# Shell structures in oxygen isotopes described with modern nucleon-nucleon interactions

> Shell structures in the N\simeq Z nucleus ^{17}O and the neutron-rich oxygen isotopes ^{23}O and ^{25}O are microscopically described by calculating single-particle energies with modern nucleon-nucleon interactions within the framework of the unitary-model-operator approach. It is found that the effect of three-body cluster terms on the single-particle energy is more important in ^{23}O and ^{25}O than ^{17}O.

# Shell structures in oxygen isotopes described with modern nucleon-nucleon interactions

## Key claim
The three-body cluster (3BC) effect on single-particle energies is repulsive and significantly larger in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes (²³O and ²⁵O) than in the N≈Z nucleus ¹⁷O, but this improvement for neutron-rich nuclei comes at the cost of worsening agreement with experiment for ¹⁷O. The authors note that a genuine three-body force (not included) would likely contribute attractively and could alter these conclusions.

## Method
The authors applied the unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA) in a particle-basis formalism to calculate single-particle energies for the ground states of ¹⁷O, ²³O, and ²⁵O using the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. They constructed a two-body effective interaction in a large harmonic-oscillator model space (boundary parameter ρ₁=12) and evaluated three-body cluster correction terms for both the closed-shell nuclei (¹⁶O, ²²O, ²⁴O) and the single-particle states. Single-particle energies were obtained as the difference between ground-state energies of the single-particle nucleus and the corresponding closed-shell nucleus, with optimal harmonic-oscillator energy ħω chosen separately for each nucleus.

## Key numbers
- The model space boundary parameter was taken as ρ₁=12, which the authors state is a sufficiently large value for convergence.
- For ²³O, the three-body cluster effect on the single-particle state is "considerably large" and brings the calculated single-particle energy into good agreement with the experimental value.
- For ²²O and ²⁴O, the three-body cluster effects on the closed-shell nuclei do not show significant contributions to the relative energy between these two nuclei, and the calculated results agree with experimental values both with and without the 3BC effect.
- The same tendency (repulsive 3BC effect, larger for heavier isotopes) was observed with the N³LO potential based on chiral perturbation theory as with the CD-Bonn potential.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous application of the unitary-model-operator approach (Fujii, 2003) from the N=Z nucleus ¹⁶O to neutron-rich oxygen isotopes, where earlier work had not evaluated three-body cluster terms. The study addresses the question raised by Ozawa (2000) and Otsuka (2001) about how shell structure and magic numbers change in neutron-rich nuclei near the drip lines. The results demonstrate that three-body cluster corrections are indispensable for describing single-particle energies in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes within the particle-hole formalism, but also highlight the need to include genuine three-body forces for fully reliable results.
