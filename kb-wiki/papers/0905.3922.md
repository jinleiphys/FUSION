# Altarelli-Parisi Equation in Non-Equilibrium QCD

> The $Q^2$ evolution of fragmentation function in non-equilibrium QCD by using DGLAP evolution equation may be necessary to study hadron formation from quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC. In this paper we study splitting functions in non-equilibrium QCD by using Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time path integral formalism. For quarks and gluons with arbitrary non-equilibrium distribution functions $f_q({\vec p})$ and $f_g({\vec p})$, we derive expressions for quark and gluon splitting functions in non-equilibrium QCD at leading order in $α_s$. We make a comparison of these splitting functions with that obtained by Altarelli and Parisi in vacuum.

## Key claim

The paper derives leading-order quark and gluon splitting functions in non-equilibrium QCD that depend on arbitrary non-equilibrium parton distribution functions \(f_q(\vec{p})\) and \(f_g(\vec{p})\), generalizing the vacuum Altarelli-Parisi splitting functions; these non-equilibrium splitting functions may be necessary for studying the \(Q^2\) evolution of fragmentation functions in quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC, but the work assumes space-translational invariance at the initial time and does not prove that the relaxation time of the non-equilibrium state is long enough for the DGLAP evolution to be applicable.

## Method

The author uses the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time path integral formalism to compute splitting functions at leading order in \(\alpha_s\) for the processes \(q \rightarrow gq\), \(q \rightarrow qg\), and \(g \rightarrow gg\) in a non-equilibrium QCD medium. The initial state \(|in\rangle\) is defined with arbitrary non-equilibrium distribution functions for quarks and gluons, and the S-matrix elements are evaluated using free-field commutation relations in the interaction picture, with the medium effects entering through factors \([1+f(\vec{k})]^2\) multiplying the vacuum splitting functions.

## Key numbers

- The maximum relaxation time of the non-equilibrium state at RHIC and LHC is estimated to be \(\sim 1.5\) fm, based on transport cross sections for \(gg \rightarrow gg\) scattering and parton number densities.
- The quark to gluon splitting function in non-equilibrium QCD is \(P_{gq}(z) = C_2(R) [1+f_q(p)]^2 [1+f_g(p_T,zp)]^2 [1+f_q(-p_T, (1-z)p)]^2 \frac{1+(1-z)^2}{z}\).
- The quark to quark splitting function in non-equilibrium QCD is \(P_{qq}(z) = C_2(R) [1+f_q(p)]^2 [1+f_g(-p_T,(1-z)p)]^2 [1+f_q(p_T, zp)]^2 \frac{1+z^2}{1-z}\).
- The gluon to gluon splitting function in non-equilibrium QCD is \(P_{gg}(z) = 2C_A [1+f_g(p)]^2 [1+f_g(p_T,zp)]^2 [1+f_g(-p_T, (1-z)p)]^2 [\frac{1-z}{z} + \frac{z}{1-z} + z(1-z)]\).

## Context

This work extends the vacuum Altarelli-Parisi splitting functions (Altarelli and Parisi, 1977) to non-equilibrium QCD, building on the author's prior derivation of fragmentation functions in non-equilibrium QCD (Nayak, 2008) and factorization theorems in non-equilibrium QED and QCD (Nayak, 2008). The results are intended to enable DGLAP evolution of fragmentation functions for studying high \(p_T\) hadron production from quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC, but the paper notes that the typical QCD evolution time scale is set by \(\ln Q\) and that the relaxation time of the non-equilibrium state (\(\sim 1.5\) fm) must be compared to this evolution time to justify the approach.
