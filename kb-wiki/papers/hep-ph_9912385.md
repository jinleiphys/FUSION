# Symmetries of Excited Heavy Baryons In The Heavy Quark And Large N_c Limit

> We demonstrate in a model independent way that, in the combined heavy quark and large N_c limit, there exists a new contracted U(4) symmetry which connects orbitally excited heavy baryons to the ground states.

# Symmetries of Excited Heavy Baryons In The Heavy Quark And Large N_c Limit

## Key claim
In the combined heavy quark and large N_c limit, a contracted U(4) symmetry emerges in QCD that connects orbitally excited heavy baryons to the ground states, but this symmetry only applies to a particular subspace (the "ground state band") and the analysis depends on the heavy quark limit being taken before the large N_c limit.

## Method
The authors demonstrate that in the heavy baryon Hilbert space, the QCD Hamiltonian can be decomposed into a simple harmonic oscillator form plus a commuting term, by studying double and triple commutators of the Hamiltonian with position and momentum operators of the brown muck (light degrees of freedom). They show that as N_c → ∞, the triple commutators vanish, forcing the Hamiltonian to be at most bilinear in the creation and annihilation operators a_j and a_j^†, and that the resulting Hamiltonian exhibits a contracted U(4) spectrum generating algebra that becomes an emergent symmetry as the oscillator frequency ω → 0.

## Key numbers
- The excitation energy ω scales as N_c^{-1/2} and vanishes in the large N_c limit.
- The spring constant κ is of order N_c^0 (order one in the large N_c counting).
- The reduced mass μ equals m_N (the light baryon mass), which scales like N_c.
- The triple commutator T^{(0)} ∼ O(N_c^{-3/4}), T^{(1)} ∼ N_c^{-9/4}, T^{(2)} ∼ N_c^{-7/4}, and T^{(3)} ∼ N_c^{-5/4}, all smaller than ω ∼ N_c^{-1/2}.
- The excitation energy of the first excited state is around 330 MeV.
- The pion-heavy baryon coupling g_{πΛ_QΛ_Q} ∼ N_c^{1/2}, while g_{πΛ_QΛ_Q^*} is suppressed in the large N_c limit.
- The splitting parameter σ ∼ N_c^{-1} for states with I + s_ℓ = 0.

## Context
This work extends the bound state picture of heavy baryons developed by Guralnik et al. (1993) and Jenkins et al. (1993), where a heavy baryon is treated as a bound state of a heavy meson and a light baryon. It also builds on the heavy quark symmetry of Isgur and Wise (1991) and the large N_c spin-flavor symmetry of Dashen et al. (1994). The authors recast previous results on Isgur-Wise form factors from Chow and Wise (1994) in a model-independent way, showing that all derivatives of these form factors at zero recoil can be expressed as calculable functions of m_N and ω. The symmetry provides an organizational principle for low-energy properties of heavy baryons, though quantitative predictions may be limited by corrections of order N_c^{-1/2} from anharmonic terms.
