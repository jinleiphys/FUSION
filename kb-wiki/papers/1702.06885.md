# Multiplicity Dependence of Non-extensive Parameters for Strange and Multi-Strange Particles in Proton-Proton Collisions at $\sqrt{s}= 7$ TeV at the LHC

> The transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) spectra in proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV, measured by the ALICE experiment at the LHC are analyzed with a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution. The information about the freeze-out surface in terms of freeze-out volume, temperature and the non-extenisivity parameter, $q$, for $K^{0}_{S}$, $Λ+\barΛ$, $Ξ^{-}+\barΞ^{+}$ and $Ω^{-}+\barΩ^{+}$ are extracted by fitting the $p_{\rm T}$ spectra with Tsallis distribution function. The freeze-out parameters of these particles are studied as a function of charged particle multiplicity density ($dN_{ch}/dη$). In addition, we also study these parameters as a function of particle mass to see any possible mass ordering. The strange and multi-strange particles show mass ordering in volume, temperature, non-extensive parameter and also a strong dependence on multiplicity classes. It is observed that with increase in particle multiplicity, the non-extensivity parameter, $q$ decreases, which indicates the tendency of the produced system towards thermodynamic equilibration. The increase in strange particle multiplicity is observed to be due to the increase of temperature and not to the size of the freeze-out volume.

## Key claim

The Tsallis non-extensive distribution provides an excellent description of strange and multi-strange particle transverse momentum spectra in proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV without incorporating radial flow, and the extracted non-extensive parameter q decreases with increasing charged particle multiplicity, indicating a tendency toward thermodynamic equilibration in high-multiplicity events.

## Method

The transverse momentum spectra of K0S, Lambda+anti-Lambda, Xi-+anti-Xi+, and Omega-+anti-Omega+ measured by the ALICE experiment in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s)=7 TeV were fitted with a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution function. The freeze-out parameters (temperature T, non-extensive parameter q, and volume V) were extracted for ten charged-particle multiplicity classes using the TMinuit class in ROOT, with all parameters left free. For Omega-+anti-Omega+, adjacent multiplicity classes were combined to improve statistics.

## Key numbers

- For K0S in the highest multiplicity class (Mul1, mean dNch/deta = 21.3 +/- 0.6): T = 0.152 +/- 0.001 GeV, q = 1.141 +/- 0.001
- For K0S in the lowest multiplicity class (Mul10, mean dNch/deta = 2.26 +/- 0.12): T = 0.068 +/- 0.003 GeV, q = 1.147 +/- 0.002
- For Lambda+anti-Lambda in Mul1: T = 0.245 +/- 0.0 GeV, q = 1.086 +/- 0.006
- For Lambda+anti-Lambda in Mul10: T = 0.010 +/- 0.0 GeV, q = 1.139 +/- 0.001
- For Xi-+anti-Xi+ in Mul1: T = 0.308 +/- 0.0 GeV, q = 1.069 +/- 0.015
- For Xi-+anti-Xi+ in Mul10: T = 0.045 +/- 0.001 GeV, q = 1.122 +/- 0.002
- For Omega-+anti-Omega+ in combined Mul[1+2]: T = 0.422 +/- 0.0 GeV, q = 1.035 +/- 0.028
- For Omega-+anti-Omega+ in combined Mul[9+10]: T = 0.052 +/- 0.0 GeV, q = 1.124 +/- 0.006
- All fits yielded chi-squared per degree of freedom values below 1, indicating excellent description of the data

## Context

This work extends the application of Tsallis non-extensive statistics to strange and multi-strange particles in proton-proton collisions, building on prior studies of charged hadrons and pions at RHIC and LHC (STAR, PHENIX, ALICE, CMS collaborations). The multiplicity-dependent analysis complements earlier mass-dependent differential freeze-out studies by Thakur (2016) and provides thermodynamic insight into the heavy-ion-like behavior observed in high-multiplicity proton-proton events, such as strangeness enhancement reported by Adam (2016). The results enable further investigation of the approach to equilibrium in small systems and can be compared with similar analyses of CMS data by Khachatryan (2016).
