# Prolate-to-oblate transition and backbending along the yrast line induced by quasiparticle alignment

> The yrast lines in Kr isotopes with $N=42$, 44, and 46 are investigated in a beyond mean field framework with both prolate-oblate coexistence and quasiparticle alignment taken into account. Quasiparticle orbitals with high-$j$ and low-$Ω$ on the oblate side are shown to be responsible for the sharp backbending observed in $^{82}$Kr, by driving the yrast shape from prolate to oblate. This suggests that quasiparticle alignment may not be neglected in the investigation of the shape evolution along the yrast line.

## Key claim

The sharp backbending observed in the yrast band of $^{82}$Kr is caused by a crossing between a prolate-dominated vacuum and an oblate two-quasiparticle state, driven by quasiparticle alignment of high-$j$, low-$\Omega$ orbitals near the top of the $\nu g_{9/2}$ subshell; this mechanism is distinct from traditional backbending in well-deformed nuclei, which involves alignment within a fixed prolate deformation.

## Method

The authors use a beyond-mean-field framework that generalizes the projected shell model (PSM) by including multiple axially symmetric HFB vacua with different deformations (both prolate and oblate) and two-quasiparticle configurations built on each vacuum. The Hamiltonian is of pairing-plus-quadrupole type, and the wave functions are projected onto good angular momentum and particle numbers before solving the Hill-Wheeler equation. The model space includes neutron and proton major shells $N=2,3,4$, with Nilsson parameters adjusted to reproduce energy curves, and the calculations are performed for $^{78,80,82}$Kr (neutron numbers $N=42,44,46$).

## Key numbers

- The prolate minimum in $^{78}$Kr is located at $\beta \sim 0.33$, coinciding with the deformation used in earlier PSM calculations (Liu 2015).
- The oblate minimum in $^{78}$Kr lies about 1.5 MeV above the prolate minimum at $I=0$.
- In $^{82}$Kr, the $B(E2)$ value at $I=8$ is only of the order of 1 W.u., indicating a dramatic structural change.
- The effective charges used in $B(E2)$ calculations are $e^\pi = 1.35e$ for protons and $e^\nu = 0.35e$ for neutrons.
- The quadrupole pairing strength is set to $G_Q = 0.16 G_M$, where $G_M$ is the monopole pairing strength taken from earlier PSM calculations (Palit 2001, Liu 2015).

## Context

This work extends the PSM approach (Sun 2016, Palit 2001) by allowing deformation variation, which is essential for transitional nuclei where shape coexistence is important. It contrasts with the fixed-deformation assumption in traditional PSM calculations for Kr isotopes (Liu 2015, Wu 2017) and with the total Routhian surface (TRS) method (Satula 1994), which lacks configuration mixing and rotational symmetry restoration. The results show that oblate quasiparticle alignment can induce a prolate-to-oblate shape transition along the yrast line, a mechanism that may be relevant for other nuclei where the Fermi level lies near the top of a high-$j$ subshell.
