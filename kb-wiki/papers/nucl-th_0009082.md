# On the uncertainty of hyperon couplings and the electrochemical potential in neutron star matter

> Uncertainty of the hyperon couplings, in particular, that of the $Σ^-$, in dense matter raises the question of the behavior of the electrochemical potential in neutron star matter, which is crucial to the possible presence of the kaon condensed phase. We show that regardless of this uncertainty, the $Λ$ hyperon, whose coupling can be constrained by its binding in nuclear matter and other observations, also aided by the $Ξ^-$, introduce a saturation of the electrochemical potential just as the $Σ^-$ would otherwise do, which tends to mitigate against kaon condensation. The maximum possible mass of neutron stars appears to be $\sim 1.5 \msun$ independent of the uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper shows that even if the $\Sigma^-$ hyperon is entirely absent from dense neutron star matter due to repulsive interactions, the $\Lambda$ hyperon (whose coupling can be constrained by its binding in nuclear matter) together with the $\Xi^-$ hyperon still cause the electrochemical potential to saturate and then decrease with increasing density, which tends to mitigate against kaon condensation regardless of the uncertainty in the $\Sigma^-$ coupling.

## Method

The author uses a relativistic mean-field model (a generalization of the Johnson-Teller and Walecka models) solved in the mean-field approximation for charge-neutral matter in general beta equilibrium. The five nucleon coupling constants are determined by nuclear matter saturation properties (binding energy -16.3 MeV, density 0.153 fm$^{-3}$, symmetry energy 32.5 MeV, compression modulus 240 MeV, effective mass 0.78), while hyperon couplings are constrained by $\Lambda$ binding in nuclear matter and hypernuclear level data, with $x_\sigma = 0.6$ and $x_\omega = 0.658$. Two cases are compared: all hyperons present, and the extreme case where the $\Sigma^-$ is excluded entirely due to strong repulsion.

## Key numbers

- The electrochemical potential reaches a maximum value of about 200 MeV at densities of 2.5 to 3 times nuclear density, then decreases monotonically at higher density, far below the vacuum K$^-$ mass of 494 MeV.
- The maximum possible neutron star mass appears to be approximately 1.5 $M_\odot$ independent of the hyperon coupling uncertainties, with an upper limit of about 1.7 $M_\odot$ compatible with uncertainties but less favored.
- Hyperons reduce the maximum neutron star mass by as much as 3/4 $M_\odot$ compared to models with only nucleons and leptons.
- The $\Lambda$ hyperon coupling ratio $x_\sigma$ is limited to the range 0.5 to 0.7 based on binding data and neutron star constraints.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier analysis (Glendenning 1985) showing that hyperons can saturate the electron chemical potential and preempt kaon condensation, and confirms subsequent work by Schaffner (1996) and Balberg (1997). It directly addresses a suggestion by Brown that the absence of the $\Sigma^-$ would mitigate the negative effect of hyperons on kaon condensation. The paper uses hyperon coupling constraints from Dover (1988) on $\Lambda$ binding in nuclear matter, from Rufa (1990) on hypernuclear levels, and from Gal (1994) on the inconclusive evidence for $\Sigma^-$ repulsion. The results enable further progress by showing that accurate evaluation of the K$^-$ mass as a function of density and continued experimental work on hyperon interactions are needed to resolve the question of kaon condensation.
