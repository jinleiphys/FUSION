# Many-body effects in (p,pN) reactions within a unified approach

> We study knockout reactions with proton probes within a theoretical framework where {\it ab initio} Quantum Monte Carlo wave functions are combined with the Faddeev/Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas few-body reaction formalism. New Quantum Monte Carlo wave functions are used to describe $^{12}$C, yielding, for the first time, results consistent with the experimental point rms radii, electron scattering data and (p,2p) total cross sections data. Our results for $\mathrm{A}\leq 12$ and $(N-Z) \leq 3$ nuclei show that the theoretical ratios between the (i) {\it ab initio} and Mean Field Approximation theoretical cross sections, $\mathcal{R}_σ$, (ii) corresponding ratios between the spectroscopic factors, $\mathcal{R}_Σ$, summed over states below particle emission, depend moderately on the nucleon separation energy S$_{\rm N}$. These ratios are determined by a delicate interplay between the radii of the parent and the residual nuclei and the nucleon separation energy, and were found to be always smaller for the knockout of the more correlated deficient species nucleon. In the case of the symmetric $^{12}$C nucleus, the theoretical ratios still appear to indicate that protons are more correlated than neutrons.

# Many-body effects in (p,pN) reactions within a unified approach

## Key claim

The theoretical ratios between *ab initio* Quantum Monte Carlo and Mean Field Approximation cross sections and spectroscopic factors for (p,pN) reactions on light nuclei (A ≤ 12, N-Z ≤ 3) depend only moderately on nucleon separation energy, but are always smaller for knockout of the more correlated deficient species nucleon; for the symmetric 12C nucleus, the ratios still appear to indicate that protons are more correlated than neutrons.

## Method

The authors combined *ab initio* Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) wave functions, using the Argonne V18 two-nucleon and Urbana X three-nucleon potentials, with the Faddeev/Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (F/AGS) few-body reaction formalism to solve the three-body scattering problem for (p,pN) knockout reactions. Spectroscopic overlaps between parent and residual nuclei were calculated from variational Monte Carlo (VMC) wave functions for 7Li, 9Li, 10Be, and 12C, with new improved VMC wave functions for 12C and 11B, and then incorporated into the F/AGS framework using the Koning-Delaroche and Cooper optical parametrizations for nucleon-core and proton-core interactions.

## Key numbers

- For 12C (p,2p) at 400 MeV/u: total theoretical cross section σ_th(QMC) = 21.66 mb, compared to experimental σ_exp = 19.2(18)(12) mb, giving a ratio of 0.886(10)
- Experimental sum of spectroscopic factors for 12C (p,2p): Σ_exp = 2.96; for (e,e'p): Σ_exp = 2.18(15)(1.00); for (d,3He): Σ_exp = 2.1(1.00)
- Ratio of VMC/MFA spectroscopic factors for ground state transitions: 0.81 for 7Li→6Li (p knockout), 0.56 for 7Li→6He (n knockout); 0.96 for 9Li→8Li, 0.67 for 9Li→8He; 0.83 for 10Be→9Be, 0.60 for 10Be→9Li; 0.77 for 12C→11C, 0.77 for 12C→11B
- Ratio of VMC/MFA spectroscopic sums below particle threshold (BPT): range from 0.563(1) for 7Li→6He to 0.967(3) for 9Li→8Li
- About 15% of protons in 12C have momenta above 1.4 fm^-1, unaccountable in any mean field approximation
- Point proton rms radii from VMC: 2.37 fm for 12C, 2.26 fm for 7Li, 2.07 fm for 9Li, 2.31 fm for 10Be, all consistent with experimental values

## Context

This work extends the *ab initio* QMC approach previously applied to transfer reactions (Nollett 2011) and (e,e'p) data (Lapikas 1999) to (p,pN) reactions for the first time, using new VMC wave functions for 12C. It addresses conflicting results between nucleon knockout experiments with composite projectiles (Tostevin 2014, Grinyer 2012) that showed strong dependence on asymmetry parameter ΔS, and (p,pN) and transfer studies (Leyla, Paloma, Flavigny 2013) that found small and nearly constant reduction factors. The unified framework enables consistent comparison of spectroscopic factors from different reaction probes and demonstrates that the quenching of cross sections relative to mean field predictions arises necessarily from many-body partitions beyond the (A-1)+N configuration in the parent nucleus wave function, independent of interaction models.
