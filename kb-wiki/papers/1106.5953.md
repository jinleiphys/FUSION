# Spectra, flow and HBT in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC

> The transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow and interferometry radii for Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC are calculated in relativistic viscous hydrodynamics. For Glauber model initial conditions, we find that the data can be described using a small value of shear viscosity eta/s=0.08. The viscosities and the equation of state are the same as used for RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC can be described using the same small shear viscosity to entropy ratio (η/s = 0.08) and the same equation of state as used for RHIC energies, but only when Glauber model initial conditions are assumed.

## Method

The author applied 2+1 dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with shear and bulk viscosities to model Pb-Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV. The shear viscosity was fixed to η/s = 0.08, the bulk viscosity to ζ/s = 0.04 in the hadronic phase, and the initial entropy density profile was taken proportional to a mixture of participant and binary collision densities with α = 0.15. Second order viscous hydrodynamic equations were solved, and hadrons were emitted at freeze-out with deviations from equilibrium momentum distributions implemented using quadratic and asymptotically linear ansätze for shear and bulk viscosity corrections respectively.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio η/s = 0.08, fixed to the AdS/CFT value
- Bulk viscosity to entropy ratio ζ/s = 0.04, applied in the hadronic phase
- Multiplicity of charged particles in central collisions increased by a factor of approximately 2.4 compared to the highest RHIC energies
- HBT radii described to within 10-15% accuracy
- Initial entropy density profile mixing parameter α = 0.15, fixed to reproduce the measured centrality dependence of charged particle multiplicity

## Context

This work extends the relativistic viscous hydrodynamic description that was successfully applied to Au-Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV at RHIC (Luzum 2008, Song 2010) to the higher energy Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. The study shows that the same viscosity coefficients and equation of state work at both energies, with no sign of a change in η/s for the higher temperatures reached at the LHC. The results enable further studies of the temperature dependence of transport coefficients and suggest that a realistic non-zero bulk viscosity in the hadronic stage is needed to correctly reproduce the mass splitting of elliptic flow without a hadronic cascade after-burner, as previously shown by Bozek (2009).
