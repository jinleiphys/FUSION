# Consistent Thermodynamics for Quasiparticle Boson System with Zero Chemical Potential

> The thermodynamic consistency of quasiparticle boson system with effective mass $m^*$ and zero chemical potential is studied. We take the quasiparticle gluon plasma model as a toy model. The failure of previous treatments based on traditional partial derivative is addressed. We show that a consistent thermodynamic treatment can be applied to such boson system provided that a new degree of freedom $m^*$ is introduced in the partial derivative calculation. A pressure modification term different from the vacuum contribution is derived based on the new independent variable $m^*$. A complete and self-consistent thermodynamic treatment for quasiparticle system, which can be widely applied to effective mass models, has been constructed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a thermodynamically consistent treatment for a quasiparticle boson system with zero chemical potential can be achieved by introducing the effective mass \(m^*\) as a new independent degree of freedom in the partial derivative calculation, rather than by including extra terms from derivatives of \(m^*\) with respect to temperature and density as in previous treatments.

## Method

The authors take the quasiparticle gluon plasma (qGP) model as a toy system with zero chemical potential and temperature-dependent effective mass \(m^*(T)\). They compare two thermodynamic treatments: treatment (I), which uses traditional partial derivatives that generate extra terms involving \(\partial m^*/\partial T\) and \(\partial m^*/\partial \rho\), and treatment (III), which treats \(m^*\) as an independent variable in the differential relation \(d\Omega = -S dT - p dV + X dm^*\). For both treatments, they compute pressure, energy density, and entropy density for SU(2) and SU(3) qGP models and compare the results to lattice QCD simulation data from Engels (1989) and Boyd (1995). They then derive a pressure modification term involving an arbitrary function \(f(m^*)\) from the consistent treatment and fit it to lattice data.

## Key numbers

- For the SU(3) model I, the effective mass is given by \(m^{*2} = a_0 \alpha_s(T) \rho / T\) with constant \(a_0 = 2.15\) and the parameter \(\lambda = \Lambda_T / T_c = 0.83\) fitted to lattice data.
- For the SU(3) model II, the effective mass is given by \(m^{*2} = 3 a_0 \alpha_s(T) \rho / (2T)\) with the same \(a_0 = 2.15\) and \(\lambda = 0.7\) fitted to lattice data.
- The two-loop running coupling constant is \(\alpha_s(T) = \frac{2\pi}{11 \ln(T/\Lambda_T)} \left(1 - \frac{51 \ln(2 \ln(T/\Lambda_T))}{121 \ln(T/\Lambda_T)}\right)\).
- The pressure modification term derived from the consistent treatment is \(p = T \left[ \int^T \frac{\varepsilon}{t^2} dt + f(m^*) \right]\), where \(f(m^*)\) is an arbitrary function of the effective mass.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous consistent thermodynamic treatment for fermion systems (Yin, 2008) to boson systems with zero chemical potential, using the quasiparticle gluon plasma model as a test case. It directly contradicts the traditional partial derivative approach (treatment I) used by Benrenuto (1995), Peng (2000), and Wen (2005), which the authors show produces results that deviate catastrophically from lattice QCD data. The treatment also differs from approaches that introduce a temperature-dependent vacuum energy (YangShinNan, 1995; Biro, 2003; Wang, 2000) to enforce thermodynamic consistency. The new formalism enables a self-consistent description of negative pressure at temperatures below the critical temperature \(T_c\) without invoking vacuum contributions, and can be applied to any effective mass quasiparticle model.
