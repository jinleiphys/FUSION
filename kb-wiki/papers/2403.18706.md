# $p\barО›$ final-state interaction in the reactions $e^+e^- \to K^- p \bar О›$ and $J/П€\to K^- p \bar О›$

> Near-threshold $p\barО›$ mass spectra for the reactions $e^+e^- \to K^- p\barО›$ and $J/П€\to K^- p\barО›$ are investigated with an emphasis on the role played by the interaction in the $p\barО›$ system. As guideline for the $p\barО›$ interaction a variety of $О›\barО›$ potential models is considered that have been established in the analysis of data on $p\bar p\to О›\barО›$ in the past. Arguments why the properties of the $p\barО›$ and $О›\barО›$ interactions can be expected to be very similar are provided. It is shown that the near-threshold enhancement in the invariant mass observed for the $e^+e^-$ reaction can be reproduced quantitatively by the assumed $p\barО›$ final-state interaction in the partial wave suggested by an amplitude analysis of the experiment. The effect of the $p\barО›$ final-state interaction in other decays is explored, including the recently measured reactions $B^- \to J/П€\, \bar p О›$ and $B^+ \to J/П€\, p \barО›$. It is found that the final-state interaction improves the description of the measured invariant mass near threshold in most cases.

## Key claim

The near-threshold enhancement in the $p\bar\Lambda$ invariant mass observed in $e^+e^- \to K^- p \bar\Lambda$ and $J/\psi \to K^- p \bar\Lambda$ can be reproduced quantitatively by the final-state interaction (FSI) in the $p\bar\Lambda$ system, using $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ potential models as a guide, without invoking new resonances, though the authors note that the FSI interpretation is limited to excess energies of about 50вЂ“100 MeV and that the momentum dependence of the production mechanism becomes relevant at higher invariant masses.

## Method

The authors treat the $p\bar\Lambda$ final-state interaction using the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA), where the reaction amplitude includes the primary production amplitude and a term involving the $p\bar\Lambda$ $T$-matrix obtained from solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. As a substitute for a direct $p\bar\Lambda$ potential, they employ four $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ potential models (IвЂ“IV) previously fitted to $p\bar p \to \Lambda\bar\Lambda$ data from the PS185 Collaboration, arguing that the $p\bar\Lambda$ and $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ interactions are expected to be very similar due to common annihilation dominance, absence of one-pion exchange, and single-isospin nature. The invariant-mass spectra are computed for specific partial waves (primarily $^3P_1$ for $J^P=1^+$ and $^3S_1$ for $J^P=1^-$) and compared to digitized experimental data from BES, BESIII, CMS, and LHCb.

## Key numbers

- The $p\bar\Lambda$ threshold is at 2053.95 MeV.
- For $e^+e^- \to K^- p\bar\Lambda$, the BESIII amplitude analysis attributed the enhancement to a $1^+$ ($P$-wave) state with reported parameters $m = 2084^{+4}_{-2} \pm 9$ MeV, $\Gamma = 58^{+4}_{-3} \pm 25$ MeV.
- For $J/\psi \to K^- p\bar\Lambda$, the BES Collaboration reported resonance parameters $m = 2075 \pm 12 \pm 5$ MeV, $\Gamma = 90 \pm 35$ MeV ($S$-wave assumption) or $m = 2044 \pm 17$ MeV, $\Gamma = 20 \pm 45$ MeV ($P$-wave assumption).
- For $\chi_{c0} \to K^+ \bar p \Lambda$, a fit yielded $m = 2053 \pm 13$ MeV, $\Gamma = 292 \pm 14$ MeV with preference for $0^-$ ($^1S_0$ state).
- The ratio of reduced masses $\mu_{p\bar\Lambda} / \mu_{\Lambda\bar\Lambda} = 1.09$, implying the $p\bar\Lambda$ potential would be about 10% stronger than that for $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ if scaled by reduced mass.
- The available phase space for $B^+ \to J/\psi\, p\bar\Lambda$ is less than 130 MeV.
- The bin width for the $e^+e^- \to K^- p\bar\Lambda$ data is 26 MeV; for $J/\psi \to K^- p\bar\Lambda$ it is 10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous FSI analyses of baryon-antibaryon systems, including $p\bar p$ and $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ in reactions such as $e^+e^- \to \Lambda\bar\Lambda$, $e^+e^- \to \eta\Lambda\bar\Lambda$, and $e^+e^- \to \phi\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ (Haidenbauer 2023). It directly addresses the interpretation of near-threshold enhancements previously attributed to resonances by BES (2004) and BESIII (2023), arguing that FSI effects must be accounted for before claiming new states. The work also connects to measurements of $p\bar\Lambda$ and $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ correlation functions by STAR (2006) and ALICE (2020, 2022), which show similar momentum dependence, and to a recent BESIII (2024) measurement of $p\bar\Lambda$ scattering at 1.074 GeV that agrees with predictions from the $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ potentials. The results enable more rigorous analyses of future high-statistics data from BESIII, LHCb, and GlueX, and caution against misinterpreting threshold enhancements as resonances.
