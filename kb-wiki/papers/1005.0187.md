# $ρN$ resonance dynamics in the proton nucleus reaction

> The strong coupling of rho meson to the nucleon produces $s$ and $p$ wave rho meson nucleon $(ρN)$ resonances. In a nucleus, the $ρN$ resonance-hole polarization generates the optical potential or self-energy for the rho meson. The scattering of rho meson due to this potential provides valuable informations about the $ρN$ resonance dynamics in a nucleus. To investigate it, we use this potential to calculate the mass distribution spectrum for the $ρ$ meson produced coherently in the proton nucleus reaction. The cross sections arising due to $s$ and $p$ wave $ρN$ resonances have been presented. The coherent and incoherent contributions to the cross sections due to these resonances are compared. In addition, the calculated results due to non-relativistic and relativistic $ρ$ meson self-energy are illustrated.

# ρN resonance dynamics in the proton nucleus reaction

## Key claim

The coherent ρ⁰ meson production cross section in the (p,p') reaction on ¹²C at 3.5 GeV beam energy is sensitive to the individual contributions of s-wave and p-wave ρN resonances, with the p-wave resonances dominating the cross section; however, the relativistic treatment reverses the ordering of the largest p-wave contributions compared to the non-relativistic calculation, and the Δ(1232) contribution can only be interpreted qualitatively due to uncertainty in its ρN coupling constant.

## Method

The coherent ρ⁰ meson mass distribution spectrum was calculated for the (p,p') reaction on ¹²C at 3.5 GeV beam energy with the ejectile angle fixed at 1°. The ρ meson self-energy (optical potential) due to each ρN resonance-hole polarization was taken from Peters et al., using both the non-relativistic (nucleon rest frame) and relativistic (resonance rest frame) formulations of the Lindhard function. The distorted wave functions for the outgoing ρ meson were generated by solving the relativistic Schrödinger equation with the ρ-nucleus optical potential, while proton distortions were treated via an attenuation factor using the "tρ" approximation.

## Key numbers

- Peak cross section due to N(1520) (s-wave): 5.64 μb/(GeV sr) at ρ mass 720 MeV (non-relativistic); 20.04 μb/(GeV sr) at 730 MeV (relativistic)
- Peak cross section due to Δ(1905) (p-wave): 36.63 μb/(GeV sr) at ρ mass 640 MeV (non-relativistic); 20.92 μb/(GeV sr) at 650 MeV (relativistic)
- Peak cross section due to Δ(1232) (p-wave): 35.17 μb/(GeV sr) at ρ mass 720 MeV (non-relativistic); 26.84 μb/(GeV sr) at 720 MeV (relativistic)
- Peak cross section due to N(1720) (p-wave): 31 μb/(GeV sr) at ρ mass 720 MeV (non-relativistic); 19.68 μb/(GeV sr) at 720 MeV (relativistic)
- Coherent sum of all resonances (non-relativistic): 1.35 mb/(GeV sr) at 730 MeV; incoherent sum: 0.126 mb/(GeV sr) at 710 MeV; coherent enhancement factor ≈ 10.7
- Coherent sum of all resonances (relativistic): 0.86 mb/(GeV sr) at 730 MeV; incoherent sum: 0.11 mb/(GeV sr) at 720 MeV; coherent enhancement factor ≈ 7.82
- ρN coupling constants f_RNρ range: 0.9–7.0 for s-wave resonances; 6.3–15.3 for p-wave resonances
- Free ρ meson mass m_ρ ≈ 770 MeV; free decay width Γ(m²_ρ)_ρ→ππ ≈ 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Das (2009) on coherent ρ⁰ meson production by providing the distinct contribution from each ρN resonance, which was not shown previously. It builds upon the non-relativistic ρ meson self-energy calculation by Peters et al. (1998) for all four-star s-wave and p-wave ρN resonances, and incorporates the relativistic formulation advocated by Post et al. (2001) to compare the two approaches. The results enable future experimental tests of ρN resonance dynamics in nuclear reactions and can guide the interpretation of ρ meson production data in terms of in-medium modifications of the ρ meson, complementing earlier work by Kondratyuk et al. (1993), Friman et al. (1995), and Hatsuda and Lee (1992).
