# Elliptic flow from an on-shell parton cascade

> Differential elliptic flow out to p_t ~ 5 GeV/c is calculated using the MPC elastic parton cascade model for Au+Au at Ecm ~ 130A GeV. The results are compared to recent STAR/RHIC elliptic flow data. An elliptic flow pattern comparable to the data seems to require initial parton densities at least twice higher than predicted by the HIJING model. Elliptic flow is also shown to be sensitive to the hadronization procedure.

## Key claim

The observed saturation of elliptic flow at transverse momentum around 2 GeV/c can be quantitatively reproduced from parton kinetic theory, but only if the initial parton density is at least twice as high as predicted by the HIJING model; the result is also sensitive to the hadronization procedure.

## Method

The authors used the MPC elastic parton cascade model, a covariant Boltzmann transport theory for an on-shell massless gluon gas with 16 internal degrees of freedom, evolving with elastic 2-to-2 scattering via a Debye-screened matrix element. They modeled Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130A GeV as longitudinally boost-invariant Bjorken cylinders with radius 6 fm, initial temperature 500 MeV, and initial proper time 0.1 fm/c, using parton subdivision (l=100) to recover Lorentz covariance. Simulations were performed with isotropic and gluonic cross sections of 3 mb and 10 mb at impact parameter b=8 fm.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow saturates at transverse momentum around 2 GeV/c for all cross sections studied.
- For the 3 mb gluonic cross section, v2 decreases after 2 GeV/c due to elastic energy loss.
- Initial conditions: temperature T0 = 500 MeV, proper time tau0 = 0.1 fm/c, dN/deta = 400 for central collisions, based on HIJING predictions.
- Impact parameter chosen to maximize elliptic flow: b = 8 fm.
- Elliptic flow reaches its final asymptotic value by tau = 4 fm/c for all cross sections studied.
- Lund fragmentation produces an order of magnitude reduction in elliptic flow at low pT compared to partonic flow.

## Context

This work extends prior parton cascade studies by Zhang (1999) and the authors' own work at Quark Matter 1999, which found that HIJING-based initial parton densities were too low to produce observed elliptic flow without artificially enhanced cross sections. The results support the possibility that gluon saturation models by Eskola (2000), which predict up to five times higher initial densities, may generate the observed collective flow even with pQCD elastic cross sections. The sensitivity of elliptic flow to hadronization schemes provides a new constraint for discriminating between different hadronization scenarios, with a more detailed study in progress.
