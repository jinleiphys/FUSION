# The Renormalized Yukawa Hamiltonian: Spectrum, Parton Distribution Functions, and Resource Estimates for Quantum Simulation

> We apply the Renormalization Group Procedure for Effective Particles (RGPEP) to the front form Yukawa Hamiltonian, yielding a renormalized (effective) Hamiltonian, accurate up to second order in the coupling strength. Subsequently, we examine the spectrum and parton distribution functions produced by the renormalized Hamiltonian, and show that the addition of counterterms leads to finite results. Resource estimates for quantum simulation are calculated for a single `Ladder Operator Block Encoding' (LOBE), and show that the cost to block encode the renormalized Hamiltonian is comparable to block encoding the bare Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The Renormalization Group Procedure for Effective Particles (RGPEP) applied to the front form Yukawa Hamiltonian yields a renormalized effective Hamiltonian accurate to second order in the coupling strength, but the finite results for the spectrum and parton distribution functions depend on the addition of counterterms and the removal of the regulator cutoff Lambda, with the cost to block encode the renormalized Hamiltonian being comparable to block encoding the bare Hamiltonian rather than significantly different.

## Method

The authors applied the RGPEP to the front form Yukawa Hamiltonian, solving the RGPEP equation perturbatively up to second order in the coupling constant g. They introduced a regulator cutoff Lambda on the total energy difference at interaction vertices and added counterterms for fermion and boson mass renormalization, then computed the spectrum and parton distribution functions from the resulting effective Hamiltonian. Resource estimates for quantum simulation were calculated using a single Ladder Operator Block Encoding (LOBE) approach.

## Key numbers

- The fermion mass counterterm X_delta_m2 is proportional to I_F(Lambda^{-2}), where I_F(s) = g^2 integral over x from 0 to 1 of (dx/(4 pi x(x-1))) times (m^2/x + mu^2/(1-x) - m^2)^{-1} times (m^2(1+x)^2/x) times exp(-2s/(q^+)^2 (m^2/x + mu^2/(1-x) - m^2)^2)
- The boson mass counterterm X_delta_mu2 = I_B(Lambda^{-2}), where I_B(s) = 2g^2 integral over x from 0 to 1 of (dx/(4 pi x(x-1))) times (m^2/x + m^2/(1-x) - mu^2)^{-1} times (m^2(2x-1)^2/(x(1-x))) times exp(-2s/(q^+)^2 (m^2/x + m^2/(1-x) - mu^2)^2)
- The non-relativistic 1D Yukawa potential is V(z) = -g^2/(2 mu) e^{-mu |z|}
- For the non-relativistic case with mu = 0.5, one expects 1 bound state for g = 0.3 and 2 bound states for g = 1.0

## Context

This work extends the application of the RGPEP approach previously developed by Serafin (2019) and others for bound state calculations in light-front Hamiltonian field theory. The authors acknowledge discussions with James Vary and Yang Li on their previous work on the Yukawa model. The paper enables future quantum simulations of renormalized Hamiltonians in nuclear physics by demonstrating that the block encoding cost for the renormalized Hamiltonian is comparable to that of the bare Hamiltonian, making quantum simulation of renormalized theories feasible.
