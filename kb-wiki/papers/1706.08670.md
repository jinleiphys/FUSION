# Color Screening and Regeneration of Bottomonia in High-Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The production of ground-state and excited bottomonia in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions is investigated within a kinetic-rate equation approach including regeneration. We augment our previous calculations by an improved treatment of medium effects, with temperature-dependent binding energies and pertinent reaction rates, $B$-meson resonance states in the equilibrium limit near the hadronization temperature, and a lattice-QCD based equation of state for the bulk medium. In addition to the centrality dependence of the bottomonium yields we compute their transverse-momentum ($p_T$) spectra and elliptic flow with momentum-dependent reaction rates and a regeneration component based on $b$-quark spectra from a nonperturbative transport model of heavy-quark diffusion. The latter has noticeable consequences for the shape of the bottomonium $p_T$ spectra. We quantify how uncertainties in the various modeling components affect the predictions for observables. Based on this we argue that the $Υ(1S)$ suppression is a promising observable for mapping out the in-medium properties of the QCD force, while $Υ(2S)$ production can help to quantify the role of regeneration from partially thermalized $b$ quarks.

# Color Screening and Regeneration of Bottomonia in High-Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The authors argue that the $\Upsilon(1S)$ suppression pattern is a promising observable for extracting the in-medium properties of the QCD force, while $\Upsilon(2S)$ production can help quantify the role of regeneration from partially thermalized $b$ quarks, but these conclusions depend on the specific choice of in-medium binding energies (the T-matrix binding scenario) and the inclusion of momentum-dependent reaction rates.

## Method

The authors solve a kinetic-rate equation for bottomonium abundances in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, incorporating temperature-dependent binding energies from thermodynamic T-matrix calculations using lattice-QCD internal-energy potentials, along with both gluo-dissociation and quasifree dissociation rates with interference corrections. They compute the centrality dependence of yields, transverse-momentum spectra, and elliptic flow for $\Upsilon(1S)$, $\Upsilon(2S)$, $\Upsilon(3S)$, $\chi_b(1P)$, and $\chi_b(2P)$ states, using $b$-quark spectra from a nonperturbative transport model of heavy-quark diffusion and a lattice-QCD based equation of state for the bulk medium evolution.

## Key numbers

- The $\Upsilon(1S)$ binding energy at $T\simeq350$ MeV drops to about 200 MeV in the baseline T-matrix binding scenario (TBS with $\eta=1.0$), compared to its vacuum value
- At $T=350$ MeV, varying the $\Upsilon(1S)$ binding energy from ~300 MeV ($\eta=0.9$) to ~100 MeV ($\eta=1.1$) changes the quasifree dissociation width from 60 MeV to 120 MeV (baseline 80 MeV)
- Regeneration contributions in 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions are at a ~20% level of the total $\Upsilon(1S)$ yield in central collisions (including feeddown from higher states)
- Primordial $\Upsilon(2S)$ states are suppressed down to $\lesssim$5% in central Pb-Pb(2.76 TeV) collisions, making regeneration the dominant source for semi-central and central collisions
- The $b$-quark relaxation time is $\tau_b\simeq11$ fm/$c$ at $\sim2T_c$
- The total entropy for Pb-Pb(2.76 TeV) covering $\Delta y=1.8$ units in rapidity is $S_{\rm total}=22000$
- The QGP formation time is 0.2 fm at LHC energies and 0.6 fm at RHIC energies
- The pseudo-critical temperature is $T_{\rm pc}=170$ MeV, with a switching temperature of 220 MeV for phasing out $B$-meson resonance states

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Emerick, Zhao, and Rapp (2011) by implementing in-medium binding energies from T-matrix calculations rather than the earlier strong-binding scenario (SBS) or weak-binding scenario (WBS), and by using a lattice-QCD based equation of state instead of a massless quasiparticle model. The results are compared to CMS data on $\Upsilon(1S)$ and $\Upsilon(2S)$ $p_T$ spectra from Khachatryan et al. (2016) and to ALICE data at forward rapidity from Abelev et al. (2014). The approach enables systematic extraction of in-medium QCD force properties from bottomonium observables and quantification of regeneration from partially thermalized $b$ quarks, with predictions provided for 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions.
