# Beyond the relativistic mean-field approximation (III): collective Hamiltonian in five dimensions

> The framework of relativistic energy density functionals is extended to include correlations related to restoration of broken symmetries and fluctuations of collective variables. A new implementation is developed for the solution of the eigenvalue problem of a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian for quadrupole vibrational and rotational degrees of freedom, with parameters determined by constrained self-consistent relativistic mean-field calculations for triaxial shapes. The model is tested in a series of illustrative calculations of potential energy surfaces and the resulting collective excitation spectra and transition probabilities of the chain of even-even gadolinium isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper extends the relativistic energy density functional framework to include correlations from symmetry restoration and collective fluctuations by implementing a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian for quadrupole vibrational and rotational degrees of freedom, with parameters determined from constrained self-consistent relativistic mean-field calculations for triaxial shapes, but the method relies on the cranking approximation for mass parameters and zero-point energy corrections rather than the more complete Thouless-Valatin masses.

## Method

The authors construct a collective Bohr Hamiltonian in five dimensions (β, γ, and three Euler angles) with deformation-dependent mass parameters, moments of inertia, and collective potential determined from constrained self-consistent relativistic mean-field plus BCS calculations using the PC-F1 point-coupling functional. The eigenvalue problem is solved by expanding eigenfunctions in a complete basis of square-integrable functions of β and γ combined with Wigner D-functions, followed by Cholesky-Banachiewicz orthogonalization and matrix diagonalization. The mass parameters and moments of inertia are calculated using the cranking approximation (Inglis-Belyaev formula), and zero-point energy corrections are subtracted from the mean-field energy to obtain the collective potential.

## Context

This work extends the generator coordinate method (GCM) approach developed in the first two parts of this series (Niksic et al., 2008), which was restricted to axially symmetric shapes, to include the full triaxial degree of freedom and β-γ coupling. The importance of β-γ coupling was emphasized by Caprio (2005) for the X(5) critical point and by recent studies of shape transitions in Nd isotopes using the Gogny interaction (Rodriguez and Egido, 2008; Robledo et al., 2008). The method provides a computationally feasible alternative to full seven-dimensional GCM calculations with triaxial shapes and angular momentum projection, which remain too demanding for medium-heavy and heavy nuclei.
