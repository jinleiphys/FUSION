# Hydrodynamic flow in small systems, or: "How the heck is it possible that a system emitting only a dozen particles can be described by fluid dynamics?"

> The "unreasonable effectiveness" of relativistic fluid dynamics in describing high energy heavy-ion and even proton-proton collisions are demonstrated and discussed. Several recent ideas of optimizing relativistic fluid dynamics for the specific challenges posed by such collisions will be presented, and some thoughts will be offered why the framework works better than originally expected. I will also address the unresolved question where exactly hydrodynamics breaks down, and why.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the absolute number of final-state hadrons per unit rapidity is a poor criterion for judging the applicability of relativistic fluid dynamics; instead, the initial entropy per unit rapidity is a better starting point, and dissipative hydrodynamics can work even far from local thermal equilibrium and momentum isotropy, with quantitative precision.

## Method

The authors compare seven different hydrodynamic approximation schemes (ideal, Navier-Stokes, Israel-Stewart, DNMR, third-order Chapman-Enskog, anisotropic hydrodynamics, and viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics) against exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation in Relaxation Time Approximation for Bjorken and Gubser flows. They also present phenomenological comparisons of hydrodynamic model simulations (iEBE-VISHNU, superSONIC, EPOS3.076) with experimental data from RHIC and LHC for p-p, p-Pb, d-Au, and Pb-Pb collisions, using the T_RENTo model for initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- For a pp collision at LHC with dN_ch/deta = 12, the corresponding imaginary glueball world would yield only dN_G/deta = 2 if lightest glueballs have mass 1.5 GeV or more.
- Isotherms are compared for Pb-Pb, p-Pb, and p-p collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV with the same final charged hadron pseudorapidity density dN_ch/deta = 100, and for p-p with dN_ch/deta = 20.
- For Bjorken flow, the normalized shear stress and pressure anisotropy are shown for specific shear viscosities 4pi eta/s = 1, 3, and 10.
- For Gubser flow, the third-order Chapman-Enskog approach approaches an asymptotic value pi/P_eq = 1.6 instead of the exact value 2.

## Context

This work extends the successful application of relativistic viscous fluid dynamics from large systems (Au-Au at RHIC, Pb-Pb at LHC) to "small" systems such as p-p, p-Pb, d-Au, and He-3-Au, as reviewed by Dusling (2015), Li (2017), and Floris (2019). It builds on prior hydrodynamic modeling by Heinz (2009, 2013), Gale (2013), and Romatschke (2017), and uses the T_RENTo initial condition model by Moreland (2014) and Bayesian calibration by Bernhard (2016). The paper enables future (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamic studies to further test the limits of the hydrodynamic paradigm in small systems.
