# $\bar{D}ÎŁ^*_c$ and $\bar{D}^*ÎŁ_c$ interactions and the LHCb hidden-charmed pentaquarks

> Very recently, two hidden-charmed resonances $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ consistent with pentaquark states were observed at the LHCb detector. The two $P_c$ states locate just below the $\bar{D}ÎŁ^*_c$ and $\bar{D}^*ÎŁ_c$ thresholds with mass of gaps about 5 and 15 MeV, respectively. Inspired by this fact we perform a dynamical investigation about the $\bar{D}ÎŁ_c^*(2520)$ and $\bar{D}^*ÎŁ_c(2455)$ interactions which are described by the meson exchanges. A bound state which carries spin-parity $J^P=3/2^-$ is produced from the $\bar{D}ÎŁ^*_c(2520)$ interaction, which is consistent with the $P_c(4380)$ observed at the LHCb detector. From the $D^*ÎŁ_c(2455)$ interaction, a bound state with $5/2^+$ is produced, which can be related to the $P_c(4450)$. The results suggest that the $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ are good candidates of $\bar{D}ÎŁ_c^*(2520)$ and $\bar{D}^*ÎŁ_c(2455)$ molecular states, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the LHCb pentaquarks \(P_c(4380)\) and \(P_c(4450)\) are hadronic molecular states, specifically bound states from the \(\bar{D}\Sigma_c^*(2520)\) and \(\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c(2455)\) interactions respectively, with the hedge that this identification depends on the choice of a specific sign combination for the coupling constants (the combination \(+++\)) and on a cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) tuned to reproduce the experimental masses.

## Method

The interactions between anti-charmed mesons (\(\bar{D}\), \(\bar{D}^*\)) and charmed baryons (\(\Sigma_c\), \(\Sigma_c^*\)) are described by one-boson-exchange potentials including pseudoscalar (\(\pi,\eta\)), vector (\(\rho,\omega\)), and scalar (\(\sigma\)) meson exchanges, with coupling constants taken from the literature. A Bethe-Salpeter equation with a spectator quasipotential approximation is solved to search for poles in the scattering amplitude, using an exponential regulator with a cutoff \(\Lambda\) varied from 0.5 to 5 GeV to find bound states with specific spin-parity \(J^P\).

## Key numbers

* The \(P_c(4380)\) mass is reproduced as a \(\bar{D}\Sigma_c^*\) bound state with \(J^P=3/2^-\) at a cutoff \(\Lambda\) of about 1 GeV; the experimental mass is \(4380 \pm 8 \pm 29\) MeV.
* The \(P_c(4450)\) mass is reproduced as a \(\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c\) bound state with \(J^P=5/2^+\) at a cutoff \(\Lambda\) of about 2.8 GeV; the experimental mass is \(4449.8 \pm 1.7 \pm 2.5\) MeV.
* The mass gaps between the observed states and their respective thresholds are about 5 MeV for \(P_c(4380)\) relative to the \(\bar{D}\Sigma_c^*\) threshold, and about 15 MeV for \(P_c(4450)\) relative to the \(\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c\) threshold.
* The width of \(P_c(4380)\) is \(205 \pm 18 \pm 86\) MeV; the width of \(P_c(4450)\) is \(39 \pm 5 \pm 19\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of the LHCb pentaquarks as hadronic molecules, following earlier QCD sum rule studies by Chen (2015) that identified them as anti-charmed meson and charmed baryon composites, and a coupled-channel calculation by Roca (2015) that supported a \(3/2^-\) assignment for \(P_c(4450)\). The paper specifically argues that the proximity of the observed masses to the \(\bar{D}\Sigma_c^*\) and \(\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c\) thresholds (rather than the \(\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c\) and \(\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c^*\) thresholds considered in other works) makes the present assignment more natural, and it enables future experimental and theoretical work to distinguish between competing molecular configurations by their spin-parity and binding energies.
