# Relativistic Kinetic Equations for Finite Domains and Freeze-out Problem

> The relativistic kinetic equations for the two domains separated by the hypersurface with both space- and time-like parts are derived. The particle exchange between the domains separated by the time-like boundaries generates source terms and modifies the collision term of the kinetic equation. The correct hydrodynamic equations for the ``hydro+cascade'' models are obtained and their differences from existing freeze-out models of the hadronic matter are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives relativistic kinetic equations for two domains separated by a hypersurface with both space-like and time-like parts, showing that the particle exchange across time-like boundaries generates source terms and modifies the collision term of the kinetic equation; the abstract glosses over the condition that the derived equations are necessary to resolve causal paradoxes and conservation law violations in existing "hydro+cascade" models, particularly for the time-like parts of the boundary where the standard Cooper-Frye formula fails.

## Method

The author considers two semi-infinite domains ("in" and "out") separated by a hypersurface Σ* parameterized as t = t*(x̄), derives collisionless kinetic equations from particle number conservation using the Gauss theorem, and then extends the derivation to include collision terms by modifying the standard Boltzmann collision integral to account for interactions between particles from different domains on the boundary. The resulting system of equations is then used to analyze the hydrodynamic equations for the "hydro+cascade" models by multiplying the kinetic equations by p^ν and integrating over momentum.

## Key numbers

- The loss term for particles leaving the "in" domain through Σ* is given by N_loss* = ∫_{Σ*(t1,t2)} dΣ_μ (d³p/p⁰) p^μ φ_in(x,p) Θ(p^ν dΣ_ν), where the Θ-function cuts off particles returning to the "in" domain for time-like parts of the hypersurface

## Context

This work extends the author's previous treatment of the freeze-out problem in relativistic hydrodynamics (Bugaev 1996, Bugaev 1999b), which derived the correct cut-off formula for time-like hypersurfaces and provided global conservation laws, and addresses difficulties in the "hydro+cascade" models of Bass and Dumitru (2000) and Teaney, Lauret, and Shuryak (2001). The derived kinetic equations enable a consistent treatment of the boundary between hydrodynamic and cascade domains in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, resolving the causal paradoxes and conservation law violations that arise when using the standard Cooper-Frye formula (Cooper and Frye 1974) for time-like hypersurfaces.
