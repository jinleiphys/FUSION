# Constraints on new physics with (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering data

> New physics contributions to the (anti)neutrino-nucleon elastic scattering process can be constrained by precision measurements, with controlled Standard Model uncertainties. In a large class of new physics models, interactions involving charged leptons of different flavor can be related, and the large muon flavor component of accelerator neutrino beams can mitigate the lepton mass suppression that occurs in other low-energy measurements. We employ the recent high-statistics measurement of the cross section for $\barν_μp \to μ^+ n$ scattering on the hydrogen atom by MINERvA to place new confidence intervals on tensor and scalar neutrino-nucleon interactions: $\mathfrak{Re} C_T = -1^{+14}_{-13} \times 10^{-4}$, $|\mathfrak{Im} C_T| \le 1.3 \times 10^{-3}$, and $|\mathfrak{Im} C_S| = 45^{+13}_{-19} \times 10^{-3}$. These results represent a reduction in uncertainty by a factor of $2.1$, $3.1$, and $1.2$, respectively, compared to existing constraints from precision beta decay.

## Key claim
The paper uses MINERvA’s high-statistics measurement of the antineutrino-proton elastic cross section to set new constraints on tensor and scalar neutrino-nucleon interactions, under the assumption that new physics contributions are proportional to the charged lepton mass (i.e., Minimal Flavor Violation in the lepton sector). The quoted improvements in uncertainty (factors of 2.1, 3.1, and 1.2) are relative to existing bounds from precision beta decay, but only apply when the lepton-flavor dependence linking muon and electron observables holds.

## Method
The authors parameterize the most general Lorentz-invariant charged-current elastic antineutrino-proton amplitude, including eight invariant amplitudes. They fit the MINERvA antineutrino-hydrogen cross-section data (15 data points with full covariance matrix) by floating the real or imaginary part of one new-physics amplitude at a time, using a dipole form factor ansatz for the Q² dependence, while allowing Standard Model vector and axial-vector form factors to vary within their uncertainties. A log-likelihood minimization accounting for bin-to-bin correlations yields 68% confidence intervals for each amplitude at Q²=0.

## Key numbers
- Real part of tensor coefficient: Re C_T = -1^{+14}_{-13} × 10⁻⁴ (from MINERvA data)
- Imaginary part of tensor coefficient: |Im C_T| ≤ 1.3 × 10⁻³ (from MINERvA data)
- Imaginary part of scalar coefficient: |Im C_S| = 45^{+13}_{-19} × 10⁻³ (from MINERvA data)
- Improvement factor over beta-decay constraints: 2.1 for Re C_T, 3.1 for |Im C_T|, and 1.2 for |Im C_S|
- For comparison, beta-decay fit gives Re C_T = -0.0025(28), Im C_T = -0.0005(42), Im C_S = -0.007(30) from Gonzalez-Alonso et al. (2018)
- Constraint on real part of second-class axial amplitude: |Re f_A3| = 0.2–1.4 at 90% C.L., compared to |Re f_A3| ≤ 2.0 at 90% C.L. from Baker et al. (1981) at BNL

## Context
This work extends the use of neutrino-nucleon scattering as a probe of new physics, building on the MINERvA Collaboration’s (2023) first high-statistics measurement of the antineutrino-proton cross section. It improves upon constraints from precision beta decay (Gonzalez-Alonso et al., 2018; Hardy et al., 2020) for tensor and scalar interactions, and provides the first constraints on the imaginary parts of the second-class axial and induced pseudoscalar amplitudes. The results enable future studies with DUNE/LBNF data and motivate simultaneous fits of multiple amplitudes in specific new physics models, as well as comparisons with LHC constraints on muon-flavor interactions.
