# Systematic study of elliptic flow parameter in the relativistic nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC energies

> We employed the new issue of a parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE 2.1 to systematically investigate the charged particle elliptic flow parameter $v_2$ in the relativistic nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. With randomly sampling the transverse momentum $x$ and $y$ components of the particles generated in string fragmentation on the circumference of an ellipse instead of circle originally, the calculated charged particle $v_2(η)$ and $v_2(p_T)$ fairly reproduce the corresponding experimental data in the Au+Au/Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=0.2/2.76 TeV. In addition, the charged particle $v_2(η)$ and $v_2(p_T)$ in the p+p collisions at $\sqrt s$=7 TeV as well as in the p+Au/p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=0.2/5.02 TeV are predicted.

## Key claim

The authors claim that modifying the random sampling of transverse momentum components from a circle to an ellipse in the string fragmentation step of the PACIAE 2.1 model allows them to reproduce charged particle elliptic flow data in Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions, but the agreement depends on an extra parameter C that varies with centrality (C=2 for 20-40% centrality and C=1 for 40-50% centrality in Au+Au at 0.2 TeV).

## Method

The authors used the PACIAE 2.1 parton and hadron cascade model, which is based on PYTHIA for nucleon-nucleon collisions but extended to nucleus-nucleus collisions via the Glauber model for initial nucleon spatial distribution. The key modification from PACIAE 2.0 is that the transverse momentum x and y components of particles from string fragmentation are randomly sampled on the circumference of an ellipse (with half-axes p_T'(1+δ_p) and p_T'(1-δ_p)) instead of a circle, where the deformation parameter δ_p is related to the initial spatial eccentricity via δ_p = C δ_r with C as a free parameter. The model parameters K (higher-order correction), β (Lund string fragmentation parameter), and Δt (minimum collision time interval) were first fitted to charged particle pseudorapidity density data for each collision system.

## Key numbers

* Charged particle pseudorapidity density at mid-rapidity for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=0.2 TeV: experiment 640±50, PACIAE 626
* Charged particle pseudorapidity density at mid-rapidity for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV: experiment 1612±55, PACIAE 1659
* Charged particle pseudorapidity density at mid-rapidity for p+p (NSD) at sqrt(s)=7 TeV: experiment 5.78±0.01±0.23, PACIAE 5.74
* Charged particle pseudorapidity density at mid-rapidity for p+Pb (NSD) at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV: experiment 16.81±0.71, PACIAE 16.5
* Predicted elliptic flow parameter v2 estimated from v2(η) reaches approximately 0.04 in p+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=0.2 TeV, 0.07 in p+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, and 0.016 in p+p at sqrt(s)=7 TeV
* Model parameter C=2 for 20-40% centrality Au+Au v2(η) and C=1 for 40-50% centrality Au+Au v2(p_T) at sqrt(s_NN)=0.2 TeV

## Context

This work extends the PACIAE model series (PACIAE 2.0, Sa et al. 2012) by introducing an elliptical transverse momentum sampling to address the long-standing underestimation of elliptic flow by hadronic transport models, which was previously noted for UrQMD (Petersen et al. 2006) and the default AMPT model (Lin et al. 2005). The approach differs from the AMPT string melting model (Lin et al. 2005), which achieves agreement with v2 data by melting all hadrons to partons and using enlarged parton-parton cross sections, whereas PACIAE 2.1 retains string fragmentation but modifies the momentum sampling. The work enables predictions for v2 in small systems (p+p, p+Au, p+Pb) that may be testable experimentally, and identifies the need for further study of the centrality and energy dependence of the C parameter.
