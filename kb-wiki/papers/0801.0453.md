# Probing Nuclear Matter with Jet Conversions

> We discuss the flavor of leading jet partons as a valuable probe of nuclear matter. We point out that the coupling of jets to nuclear matter naturally leads to an alteration of jet chemistry even at high transverse momentum $p_T$. In particular, QCD jets coupling to a chemically equilibrated quark gluon plasma in nuclear collisions, will lead to hadron ratios at high transverse momentum $p_T$ that can differ significantly from their counterparts in $p+p$ collisions. Flavor measurements could complement energy loss as a way to study interactions of hard QCD jets with nuclear matter. Roughly speaking they probe the inverse mean free path $1/λ$, while energy loss probes the average squared momentum transfer $μ^2/λ$. We present some estimates for the rate of jet conversions in a consistent Fokker-Planck framework and their impact on future high-$p_T$ identified hadron measurements at RHIC and LHC. We also suggest some novel observables to test flavor effects.

## Key claim

The authors argue that flavor-changing interactions between high-energy jets and a chemically equilibrated quark-gluon plasma can significantly alter the flavor composition of leading jet partons, leading to observable differences in hadron ratios at high transverse momentum compared to proton-proton collisions. However, the magnitude of these effects depends on an enhancement factor K (taken as 4 for quark-gluon conversions) that is much larger than leading-order perturbative calculations predict, indicating that either higher-order corrections or non-perturbative mechanisms are required.

## Method

The authors model jet propagation through an expanding QGP fireball using coupled Fokker-Planck equations for momentum evolution and rate equations for flavor conversion, with conversion widths computed from leading-order perturbative QCD cross sections for annihilation, Compton, and pair creation processes. A test-particle Monte Carlo simulation distributes quark and gluon jets according to binary collision geometry in the transverse plane, with the fireball evolution treated as boost-invariant with accelerated transverse expansion and temperature evolution from entropy conservation. The authors explore two extreme scenarios: K=0 (no flavor conversions) and K=4 (enhanced conversion rates to match previous work on p/π ratios).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear modification factor R_AA ≈ 0.2 for hadrons in most central Au+Au collisions at RHIC for p_T > 5 GeV/c
- Initial temperature T_0 = 350 MeV at proper time τ_0 = 0.6 fm/c for central Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Critical temperature T_c = 175 MeV reached at proper time τ_c = 5.0 fm/c
- Strange quark ratio w_jet(p_T = 10 GeV/c) ≈ 6.4% for initial jet partons at RHIC
- Chemically equilibrated strange quark ratio w_ce(T_c) = 0.47 at T_c ≈ 180 MeV with m_s = 100 MeV
- Jet flavor conversions reduce high momentum quark jets by 30%
- For LHC Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 5.5 TeV: initial temperature T_0 = 700 MeV, initial proper time τ_0 = 0.2 fm/c, ⟨N_coll⟩ ≈ 1700
- Direct photon elliptic flow v_2 from jet-photon conversions is negative with magnitude about 2%
- Double ratio r_γ/π+ differs by factor 2 between scenarios with and without conversions at RHIC

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of jet flavor conversions (Liu and Fries, 2007) and jet-photon conversions (Fries, 2005) into a unified framework, addressing inconsistencies in radiative energy loss models noted by Samu (2007) and the failure of perturbative energy loss to explain heavy quark quenching at RHIC (STAR, 2006). The paper proposes that measurements of identified hadrons at high p_T, particularly strange hadrons like K^0_S, can provide complementary constraints on the mean free path λ of jets in the medium, while energy loss measurements probe the transport coefficient q̂ = μ²/λ. The approach enables future experimental tests at both RHIC and LHC using double ratios such as R_AA^γ/R_AA^π and R_AA^p/R_AA^π to cancel systematic uncertainties from fragmentation functions and binary collision normalization.
