# Effects of finite sizes of atomic nuclei on shear modulus and torsional oscillations in neutron stars

> The shear modulus of neutron star matter is one of the important properties for determining torsional oscillations in neutron stars. We take into account the effects of finite sizes of spherical nuclei on the shear modulus and examine the frequencies of crustal torsional oscillations. The shear modulus decreases owing to the finite-size effect, which in turn decreases the frequencies of torsional oscillations. In particular, the finite-size effect becomes more crucial for oscillations with a larger azimuthal quantum number and for neutron star models with a weaker density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy. In practice, when one identifies the quasi-periodic oscillations from a neutron star, where the magnetic effect is negligible, with crustal torsional oscillations, the finite-size effect can be more significant at frequencies higher than $\sim 100$ Hz.

## Key claim

The finite-size effect of spherical nuclei reduces the shear modulus of neutron star crust matter, which in turn lowers the frequencies of crustal torsional oscillations; this effect becomes significant for oscillations with a larger azimuthal quantum number and for neutron star models with a weaker density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy, particularly at frequencies above approximately 100 Hz when magnetic effects are negligible.

## Method

The authors modified the standard zero-temperature shear modulus formula for a body-centered cubic (bcc) lattice, originally derived for point-like nuclei, by multiplying it by a correction factor \(f_{\rm L} = 1 - \frac{1}{3}(r_{\rm p}/a)^2\), where \(r_{\rm p}\) is the reference proton radius and \(a\) is the Wigner-Seitz cell radius. They then solved the eigenvalue problem for torsional oscillations in nonmagnetized neutron star crusts constructed from three different equations of state (Shen, SLy4, and Togashi), applying zero-traction and zero-torque boundary conditions at the crust bottom and stellar surface, respectively.

## Key numbers

* The shear modulus correction factor \(f_{\rm L}\) reduces the shear modulus by up to approximately 15% at the bottom of the crust, independent of the equation of state.
* For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, the azimuthal quantum number \(\ell\) above which the finite-size frequency shift exceeds half the spacing between adjacent \(\ell\) modes is 12 for the Togashi EOS, 13 for the SLy4 EOS, and 19 for the Shen EOS.
* The transition density from spherical nuclei to uniform matter (bottom of the inner crust) is 0.0602 fm\(^{-3}\) for the Shen EOS, 0.0760 fm\(^{-3}\) for the SLy4 EOS, and 0.0758 fm\(^{-3}\) for the Togashi EOS.

## Context

This work extends the standard shear modulus formulation of Strohmayer et al. (1991) by incorporating the finite nuclear size, which was previously neglected. It builds on prior studies of crustal torsional oscillations in neutron stars (e.g., Sotani et al. 2012, 2013a) and their identification with quasi-periodic oscillations observed in magnetar giant flares from SGR 1900+14 and SGR 1806-20 (Watts and Strohmayer 2005, 2006). The results enable more precise asteroseismology of weakly magnetized neutron stars, where the finite-size effect may become distinguishable from the stronger magnetic field effects studied by Sotani (2007) and Gabler et al. (2013).
