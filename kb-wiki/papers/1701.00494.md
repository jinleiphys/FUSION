# DPS in CGC: HBT correlations in double inclusive photon production

> We introduce a technique to study double parton scattering (DPS) in the Color-Glass-Condensate (CGC) approach. We show that the cross-section of the DPS in the CGC approach is calculable in terms of new nonperturbative objects, generalized double transverse momentum-dependent parton distribution (2GTMD) functions. We investigate the production of pairs of prompt photons from two partons in the projectile hadron in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions. We show that even for independent partons in the projectile, the prompt photon correlation function exhibits Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) correlations. The width of the HBT peak is controlled by the transverse distance between the parton of the pair, which is of the order of the proton size. Thus, the HBT measurements in two-particle production such as prompt photon pairs provide useful information about the nonperturbative 2GTMDs.

## Key claim

The paper shows that even for independent partons in the projectile, the prompt photon correlation function exhibits Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) correlations, and the width of the HBT peak is controlled by the transverse distance between the partons of the pair, which is of the order of the proton size, this means the HBT signal is a probe of the nonperturbative generalized double transverse momentum-dependent parton distribution (2GTMD) functions, but the authors limit themselves to qualitative analysis based on a simple model of the initial wave function rather than making detailed quantitative predictions.

## Method

The authors derive the cross-section for double prompt photon production from two valence quarks in the projectile hadron scattering off a Color-Glass-Condensate (CGC) target, using the hybrid formalism appropriate for forward rapidity in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions. They compute the amplitude at leading order in the strong and electromagnetic couplings, assuming the target is described by a classical color field and the projectile is dilute, and they perform the color and spin summations at large \(N_c\) to express the cross-section in terms of dipole scattering amplitudes and the two-quark wave function of the projectile. The final expression involves products of the two-quark amplitude \(\mathcal{P}\) that are interpreted as generalized double transverse momentum-dependent parton distribution (2GTMD) functions.

## Key numbers

- The HBT peak width is controlled by the inverse of the transverse distance between the partons, which is "of the order of the proton size" (no explicit numerical value given for the proton size).
- At large \(N_c\), the quadrupole contribution is suppressed relative to the dipole contribution by a factor \(1/N_c\).
- The longitudinal momentum fractions of the two quarks satisfy \(x_1 + x_2 \le 1\), with \(x_1 = (q^- + k_1^-)/\sqrt{s/2}\) and \(x_2 = (q^{\prime -} + k_2^-)/\sqrt{s/2}\) where \(\sqrt{s}\) is the nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy.
- The cross-section is proportional to \(e_q^4\) (the fourth power of the fractional electric charge of the projectile quark).

## Context

This work extends the hybrid CGC formalism, previously developed for single parton scattering (SPS) by Dumitru and Jalilian-Marian (2002), to include double parton scattering (DPS) processes. It builds on the authors' prior work (Kovner and Rezaeian, 2016) on two-photon production from a single quark, and on earlier studies of HBT correlations for hadrons in p-A scattering within the CGC approach (Kovner and Lublinsky, 2011). The results enable future measurements of HBT correlations in prompt photon pairs at RHIC and LHC to extract information about the nonperturbative 2GTMDs, and the authors note that di-hadron correlations at forward rapidity should be sensitive to di-gluon 2GTMDs, which should be included as corrections to the di-hadron calculations of Albacete and Marquet (2010).
