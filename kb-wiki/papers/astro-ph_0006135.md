# Inner edge of neutron-star crust with SLy effective nucleon-nucleon interactions

> The boundary between the solid crust, assumed to be in complete thermodynamic equilibrium (cold catalyzed matter), and the liquid core of a neutron star is studied using Skyrme SLy effective N-N interactions. An approximate value of the density at the inner edge of the crust is obtained from the threshold for the instability of homogeneous $npe$ matter with respect to small periodic density perturbations. Calculations of the bottom layer of the crust are performed within the Compressible Liquid Drop Model. Spherical nuclei are energetically preferred over exotic ones (cylinders, plates, tubes, bubbles), down to the inner edge of the crust, found at the density 0.08 fm^{-3}.

# Inner edge of neutron-star crust with SLy effective nucleon-nucleon interactions

## Key claim

The inner edge of a neutron-star crust (the boundary between solid crust and liquid core) is found at a density of 0.08 fm⁻³ using SLy effective nucleon-nucleon interactions, with spherical nuclei remaining energetically preferred over exotic shapes (cylinders, plates, tubes, bubbles) all the way down to this edge, but this result depends on the specific effective force used, as older forces like Sk1′ do predict exotic nuclei.

## Method

The density at which homogeneous neutron-proton-electron (npe) matter becomes unstable against small periodic density perturbations was computed using the Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) treatment with Skyrme SLy4 and SLy7 interactions, keeping only quadratic gradient terms. The bottom layers of the crust were then modeled within the Compressible Liquid Drop Model (CLDM), minimizing the energy density for five possible nuclear shapes (spheres, cylinders, slabs, tubes, bubbles) under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality, including surface and curvature corrections. A Maxwell construction was used to determine the first-order phase transition between the crust and the uniform liquid core.

## Key numbers

- Instability density ρ_Q = 0.079 fm⁻³ for both SLy4 and SLy7 forces (compared to 0.075 fm⁻³ for SkM* and 0.101 fm⁻³ for Sk1′).
- Crust-liquid core transition density ρ_edge = 0.076 fm⁻³ (from Maxwell construction), with a density jump Δρ/ρ_edge = 1.4%.
- Transition pressure P_edge = 0.34 MeV/fm³.
- For a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star: crust mass fraction ΔM_crust/M = 1.2%; crust moment of inertia fraction ΔI_crust/I = 2.6%.
- At the crust edge: nucleon number A ≈ 600, proton number Z ≈ 50; filling fraction u ≈ 30%.
- Spherical nuclei are energetically preferred down to ρ_crit = 0.077 fm⁻³ (SLy4 force).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the crust-core transition using Skyrme forces such as SkM* and Sk1′ (Lattimer et al. 1993, Oyamatsu 1993, Pethick et al. 1995, Cheng 1997), which gave ρ_edge ≈ 0.08–0.10 fm⁻³, and the classical estimate near normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ from Baym et al. (1971). The SLy forces were specifically fitted to reproduce the UV14+UVII equation of state of neutron matter from Wiringa et al. (1988) and properties of neutron-rich nuclei, making them particularly suitable for the highly asymmetric matter (neutron excess > 80%) at the crust bottom. The finding that spherical nuclei persist to the crust edge (unlike for the FPS force, which predicts exotic shapes in the bottom half of the crust) has implications for crust transport and elastic properties, and enables a unified SLy4 equation of state for the entire neutron-star interior beyond neutron drip.
