# Quantum Many-Body Calculations using Body-Centered Cubic Lattices

> It is often computationally advantageous to model space as a discrete set of points forming a lattice grid. This technique is particularly useful for computationally difficult problems such as quantum many-body systems. For reasons of simplicity and familiarity, nearly all quantum many-body calculations have been performed on simple cubic lattices. Since the removal of lattice artifacts is often an important concern, it would be useful to perform calculations using more than one lattice geometry. In this work we show how to perform quantum many-body calculations using auxiliary-field Monte Carlo simulations on a three-dimensional body-centered cubic (BCC) lattice. As a benchmark test we compute the ground state energy of 33 spin-up and 33 spin-down fermions in the unitary limit, which is an idealized limit where the interaction range is zero and scattering length is infinite. As a fraction of the free Fermi gas energy $E_{\rm FG}$, we find that the ground state energy is $E_0/E_{\rm FG}= 0.369(2), 0.371(2),$ using two different definitions of the finite-system energy ratio. This is in excellent agreement with recent results obtained on a cubic lattice \cite{He:2019ipt}. We find that the computational effort and performance on a BCC lattice is approximately the same as that for a cubic lattice with the same number of lattice points. We discuss how the lattice simulations with different geometries can be used to constrain the size lattice artifacts in simulations of continuum quantum many-body systems.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that auxiliary-field Monte Carlo simulations of fermionic many-body systems can be performed on a body-centered cubic (BCC) lattice with computational effort and accuracy comparable to simple cubic lattice calculations, and that the ground state energy results for the unitary Fermi gas agree between the two lattice geometries within statistical uncertainties.

## Method
The authors perform auxiliary-field Monte Carlo simulations on a three-dimensional BCC lattice with cubic periodic boundary conditions, using a free Hamiltonian accurate up to O(p^6) and a nonlocal smearing interaction tuned to infinite scattering length with effective range about 0.06 lattice units. They compute the ground state energy of 33 spin-up and 33 spin-down fermions in the unitary limit for lattice lengths L=4 through L=10, using the shuttle algorithm for auxiliary field updates and asymptotic fits in the number of time steps to extract ground state energies.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy ratio for 33+33 fermions in the unitary limit: E0/E_FG = 0.369(2) using the thermodynamic definition (xi^thermo) and 0.371(2) using the finite-system definition (xi^few)
- Lattice time step a_t = 0.0107 m a_latt^2 hbar^{-1}
- Interaction parameters: C_0 = -0.744917 in lattice units, s_NL = -9.5337 x 10^{-4}
- Modified transfer matrix uses interaction coefficient tilde{C}_0 = 1.6 C_0 and time step tilde{a}_t = 5 a_t
- BCC lattice sizes range from 2L^3 = 128 points (L=4) to 2L^3 = 2000 points (L=10)
- Extracted xi^few values for individual lattice sizes: L=4: 0.3208(2), L=5: 0.3468(1), L=6: 0.3567(2), L=7: 0.3626(2), L=8: 0.3669(3), L=9: 0.3684(11), L=10: 0.3692(12)
- Ratio xi^thermo/xi^few = 0.995 for N_up = N_down = 33

## Context
This work extends lattice quantum Monte Carlo methods for fermionic systems from simple cubic lattices to BCC lattices, providing a second lattice geometry for constraining lattice artifacts in continuum extrapolations. The results agree with recent cubic lattice calculations by He (2019) for the same 33+33 fermion system, and are consistent with other numerical calculations by Forbes (2010), Carlson (2011), Endres (2012), and Jensen (2019), as well as the experimental measurement of 0.376(4) by Ku (2012). The ability to perform calculations on multiple lattice geometries enables future studies of nuclear matter and neutron star physics without requiring computationally expensive calculations at very small lattice spacings.
