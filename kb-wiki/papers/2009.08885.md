# New equations of state constrained by nuclear physics, observations, and QCD calculations of high-density nuclear matter

> We present new equations of state for applications in core-collapse supernova and neutron star merger simulations. We start by introducing an effective mass parametrization that is fit to recent microscopic calculations up to twice saturation density. This is important to capture the predicted thermal effects, which have been shown to determine the proto-neutron star contraction in supernova simulations. The parameter range of the energy-density functional underlying the equation of state is constrained by chiral effective field theory results at nuclear densities as well as by functional renormalization group computations at high densities based on QCD. We further implement observational constraints from measurements of heavy neutron stars, the gravitational wave signal of GW170817, and from the recent NICER results. Finally, we study the resulting allowed ranges for the equation of state and for properties of neutron stars, including the predicted ranges for the neutron star radius and maximum mass.

## Key claim

The authors construct new equations of state for core-collapse supernova and neutron star merger simulations that are consistent with chiral effective field theory results up to twice saturation density, functional renormalization group QCD calculations at high densities, and observational constraints from heavy neutron stars, GW170817, and NICER; however, the resulting EOS bands depend on the choice of four representative symmetry energy and slope parameter pairs and three high-density effective mass scenarios, and not all combinations simultaneously satisfy all constraints.

## Method

The authors introduce an effective mass parametrization fit to microscopic self-consistent Green's function calculations of pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter up to twice saturation density, then construct an energy-density functional with a temperature-dependent kinetic term (using density-dependent effective masses) and a temperature-independent interaction part. The functional parameters are constrained by chiral EFT results for neutron matter, functional renormalization group calculations of symmetric nuclear matter from 3 to 10 times saturation density, and observational constraints including the 1.965 solar mass lower bound on maximum neutron star mass, the GW170817 tidal deformability, and NICER radius measurements. The authors study four representative symmetry energy and slope parameter pairs and three high-density effective mass limits to produce uncertainty bands.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: n0 = 0.164(7) fm^-3, binding energy B = 15.86(57) MeV, extracted from fits to nuclear masses
- Nuclear incompressibility: K = 215(40) MeV
- Four representative (symmetry energy, slope parameter) pairs in MeV: (30,35), (31,55), (33,65), (34,55)
- Lower bound on maximum neutron star mass: M = 1.965 solar masses, from averaging PSR J0740+6620 (M = 2.14^{+0.20}_{-0.18} solar masses at 2 sigma) and PSR J0348+0432 (M = 2.01 +/- 0.04 solar masses)
- Three high-density effective mass limits: m*/m -> 0.7, 1.0, and 1.3
- Typical conservative neutron star radius range: 10 km less than or equal to R less than or equal to 14 km for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star

## Context

This work extends the approach of piecewise polytrope parametrizations (Hebeler et al., 2013) and speed-of-sound parametrizations (Tews et al., 2018; Greif et al., 2019) by incorporating microscopic effective mass calculations from Carbone and Schwenk (2019) and high-density functional renormalization group results from Leonhardt et al. (2019). The new EOS functionals are designed for use in core-collapse supernova and neutron star merger simulations, where thermal effects governed by the nucleon effective mass determine proto-neutron star contraction (Yasin et al., 2020; Schneider et al., 2019). The work enables systematic uncertainty quantification for hot dense matter EOSs and neutron star properties including radius and maximum mass predictions.
