# Chiral asymmetry of the Fermi surface in dense relativistic matter in a magnetic field

> It is revealed that in dense relativistic matter in a magnetic field, there exists a contribution to the axial current associated with a relative shift of the longitudinal momenta in the dispersion relations of opposite chirality fermions. Unlike the topological contribution in the axial current at the lowest Landau level, recently discussed in the literature, the dynamical one appears only in interacting matter and affects the fermions in all Landau levels, including those around the Fermi surface. The induced axial current and the shift of the Fermi surfaces of the left-handed and right-handed fermions are expected to play an important role in transport and emission properties of matter in various types of compact stars as well as in heavy ion collisions.

# Chiral asymmetry of the Fermi surface in dense relativistic matter in a magnetic field

## Key claim

In the normal phase of dense relativistic matter in a magnetic field, there exists a dynamical contribution to the axial current, distinct from the previously known topological contribution from the lowest Landau level, that arises from a relative shift of longitudinal momenta in the dispersion relations of opposite-chirality fermions, appears only in interacting matter, and affects fermions in all Landau levels including those around the Fermi surface.

## Method

The authors studied a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with chiral U(1)_L × U(1)_R symmetry in an external magnetic field, using a mean-field approximation to derive self-consistent gap equations for the fermion propagator. They introduced a chiral shift parameter Δ in the propagator ansatz, solved the gap equation in the weak-coupling regime, and computed the dispersion relations, free energies, and induced axial current density for the normal-phase ground state with vanishing Dirac mass.

## Key numbers

- The Dirac mass in the vacuum state is given by m_0^2 = (1/(π l^2)) exp(-Λ^2 l^2 / g), where l = 1/√(|eB|) is the magnetic length, Λ is the ultraviolet cutoff, and g = G_int Λ^2/(4π^2) ≪ 1 is the weak-coupling parameter.
- The chiral shift parameter in the normal phase is Δ = g μ (eB) / [Λ^2 (1 + 2 a g) + g |eB|], where a is a dimensionless constant of order 1 determined by the regularization scheme.
- The normal phase with nonzero Δ becomes energetically favorable when the chemical potential exceeds μ_c ≃ m_0/√2, analogous to the Clogston relation in superconductivity.
- The induced axial current density is ⟨j_5^3⟩ = (eB/(2π^2)) μ - (|eB|/(2π^2)) Δ - (|eB|/π^2) Δ Σ_{n=1}^∞ κ(√(2n|eB|), Λ), where the sum over higher Landau levels gives a Λ^2/|eB| with a = O(1).
- The free energy of the Δ state is Ω_Δ ≃ -μ_0^2/((2π l)^2) (1 - g |eB|/Λ^2), while the massive state has Ω_m ≃ -m_0^2/(2(2π l)^2) (1 + (m_0 l)^2 ln|Λ l|).

## Context

This work extends the magnetic catalysis phenomenon (Gusynin, Miransky, Shovkovy 1995, 1996) to dense matter, revealing a new chiral asymmetry in the normal phase that was not captured in earlier studies of the topological axial current from the lowest Landau level (Son 2004, Metlitski 2005, Charbonneau 2009, Rebhan 2009). The results are motivated by analogous dynamics in graphene in 2+1 dimensions (Gorbar, Gusynin, Miransky 2007) and are expected to affect transport and emission properties in compact stars and heavy ion collisions, including a possible new mechanism for pulsar kicks and supernova explosions.
