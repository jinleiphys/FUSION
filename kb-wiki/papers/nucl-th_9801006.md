# On the role of the $Δ(1232)$ on the transverse nuclear response in the $(e,e')$ reaction

> The transverse nuclear response to an electromagnetic probe which is limited to create (or destroyed) a particle-hole ($ph$) or delta-hole ($Δh$) pair is analyzed. Correlations of the random phase approximation (RPA) type and self energy insertions are considered. For RPA correlations we have developed a scheme which includes explicitly the $Δ$ and the exchange terms. Self energy insertions over $ph$ and $Δh$ bubbles are studied. Several residual interactions based on a contact plus a ($π+ ρ$)-meson exchange potential are used. All calculations are performed in non-relativistic nuclear matter. The main effect of the $Δ$ is to reduce the intensity over the nuclear quasi-elastic peak. Exchange RPA terms are very important, while the self energy depends strongly on the residual interaction employed. We compare our final result with data for $^{40}Ca$ at momentum transfer $q=410$ and $q=550$ MeV/c.

## Key claim
The inclusion of the Δ(1232) resonance in a non-relativistic nuclear matter calculation of the transverse response reduces the intensity of the quasi-elastic peak, but the magnitude of this effect and the importance of exchange RPA terms and self-energy insertions depend strongly on the specific residual interaction employed, particularly the Landau-Migdal parameters.

## Method
The transverse nuclear response function for electron scattering was calculated in non-relativistic nuclear matter using a formalism that includes particle-hole (ph) and delta-hole (Δh) excitations. Random Phase Approximation (RPA) correlations were treated with a scheme that sums direct terms to infinite order (ring approximation) while including exchange terms up to second order, using a residual interaction split into a contact term and a finite-range (π+ρ)-meson exchange potential. Separately, self-energy insertions on ph and Δh bubbles were evaluated by averaging over the hole momentum and summing to infinite order via a folding procedure.

## Key numbers
- The effective Fermi momentum used for nuclear matter was \(k_F = 235\) MeV/c.
- Calculations were compared with data for \(^{40}\text{Ca}\) at momentum transfers \(q = 410\) and \(q = 550\) MeV/c.
- The Landau-Migdal parameters used for the interaction \(V_a\) were \(g_{NN}=0.3\), \(g'_{NN}=0.7\), with \(g'_{2\,NN}=0.5\), \(g'_{2\,\Delta N}=0.0\), and \(g'_{2\,\Delta\Delta}=0.3\).
- The pion coupling constant was \(f_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi = 0.081\), with \(f_{\pi \Delta N} = 2 f_{\pi NN}\) and \(f_{\pi \Delta\Delta} = \frac{4}{5} f_{\pi NN}\).
- The cut-off for the \(\pi NN\) vertex was \(\Lambda_{\pi NN} = 1300\) MeV/c, and for the \(\rho NN\) vertex it was \(\Lambda_{\rho NN} = 1750\) MeV/c.

## Context
This work extends the many-body theory approach to nuclear response functions, specifically building on the RPA formalism of Alberico et al. (1989) and Shoda (1986) by explicitly including exchange terms and the Δ(1232) degree of freedom. It addresses the long-standing difficulty in simultaneously reproducing the longitudinal and transverse response functions, as noted by Jourdan (1995), by showing that the Δ reduces the quasi-elastic peak intensity. The results enable future calculations that combine these RPA and self-energy effects with other important mechanisms like meson exchange currents (MEC), as studied by Alberico et al. (1984) and Amaro et al. (1994), to work toward a complete theoretical description.
