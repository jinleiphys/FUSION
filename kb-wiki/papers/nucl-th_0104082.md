# Ground state particle-particle correlations and double beta decay

> A self-consistent formalism for the double beta decay of Fermi type is provided. The particle-particle channel of the two-body interaction is considered first in the mean field equations and then in the QRPA. The resulting approach is called the QRPA with a self-consistent mean field (QRPASMF). The mode provided by QRPASMF, does not collapse for any strength of the particle-particle interaction. The transition amplitude for double beta decay is almost insensitive to the variation of the particle-particle interaction. Comparing it with the result of the standard pnQRPA, it is smaller by a factor 6. The prediction for transition amplitude agrees quite well with the exact result. The present approach is the only one which produces a strong decrease of the amplitude and at the same time does not alter the stability of the ground state.

# Ground state particle-particle correlations and double beta decay

## Key claim
The QRPASMF formalism, which includes the particle-particle proton-neutron interaction in the mean field equations before applying QRPA, produces a double beta decay transition amplitude that is smaller by a factor of 6 compared to the standard pnQRPA result and is almost insensitive to variations of the particle-particle interaction strength, while the standard pnQRPA approach collapses at a critical strength near the realistic value.

## Method
The authors constructed a self-consistent formalism (QRPASMF) for Fermi-type double beta decay by first including the particle-particle proton-neutron interaction in the generalized BCS equations that define the static ground state, then applying QRPA with respect to quasiparticle operators that have this self-consistent ground state as vacuum. They restricted the model to a single j=19/2 shell for a system of 12 neutrons and 4 protons, solving the generalized BCS equations for both mother and daughter nuclei for each value of the particle-particle strength χ₁, and compared the QRPASMF results with both an exact diagonalization of the model Hamiltonian and the standard pnQRPA approach.

## Key numbers
- The QRPASMF transition amplitude is smaller by a factor of 6 compared to the standard pnQRPA result at the stable plateau at small χ₁.
- The single-particle energies were set to ε_p = ε_n = 3 MeV, with pairing strengths G_p = 0.25 MeV and G_n = 0.12 MeV, and particle-hole strength χ = 0.20 MeV.
- The particle-particle strength χ₁ was varied freely in the interval [0.0, 1.5] MeV.
- The Ikeda sum rule predicted by QRPASMF differs from the N-Z value by 25% for χ₁ = 1 MeV.
- The phonon backward amplitude Y does not exceed 10% of the forward amplitude X in a large interval of χ₁ within QRPASMF, whereas in standard pnQRPA the Y amplitude becomes equal to the X amplitude (with opposite sign) at χ₁ ≈ 1 MeV for the daughter nucleus.
- The energy shift ΔE = 2.5 MeV was used in the denominator of the double beta transition amplitude expression.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous investigation (Raduta et al., 2000) of including particle-particle interactions in the mean field to prevent QRPA collapse, now applied to double beta decay. It contrasts with the standard pnQRPA approach (Vogel, Civitarese, Klapdor, Suhonen, 1980s-1990s) where the particle-particle interaction was included only in the QRPA but not in the mean field, causing the QRPA to collapse at g_pp ≈ 1. Earlier attempts to stabilize the pnQRPA ground state include the boson expansion technique (Raduta, 1990s), the multiple commutators method (Griffin, Su, 1990s), and the renormalized pnQRPA (Toivanen, 1990s), but these violated the Ikeda sum rule by 15-30%. The present approach enables a stable description of Fermi double beta decay without ground state collapse, and the authors state that extension to Gamow-Teller double beta decay is under work.
