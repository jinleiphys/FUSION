# The Scale Invariant Vacuum Paradigm: Main Results plus the Current BBNS Progress

> We summarize the main results within the Scale Invariant Vacuum (SIV) paradigm as related to the Weyl Integrable Geometry (WIG) as an extension to the standard Einstein General Relativity (EGR). After a short sketch of the mathematical framework, the main results until 2023 [1] are highlighted in relation to: the inflation within the SIV [2], the growth of the density fluctuations [3], the application of the SIV to scale-invariant dynamics of galaxies, MOND, dark matter, and the dwarf spheroidals [4],and the most recent results on the BBNS light-elements' abundances within the SIV [5]. Keywords: cosmology: theory, dark matter, dark energy, inflation, BBNS; galaxies: formation, rotation; Weyl integrable geometry; Dirac co-calculus.

# The Scale Invariant Vacuum Paradigm: Main Results plus the Current BBNS Progress

## Key claim

The Scale Invariant Vacuum (SIV) paradigm, based on Weyl Integrable Geometry, can reproduce the same scale-factor evolution as LambdaCDM with less total matter, and its application to Big Bang Nucleosynthesis yields compatible light-element abundances with baryon and dark matter content reduced by a factor of three to five, provided the SIV parameter lambda is close to 1 when fitted to observational data.

## Method

The authors derive the SIV gauge condition (lambda = t_0/t) from the requirement of a Ricci-flat Einstein GR vacuum within Weyl Integrable Geometry, which eliminates the cosmological constant from the FLRW equations. They apply the weak-field approximation of the SIV geodesic equation to derive a universal Radial Acceleration Relation for galaxies, and modify the continuity, Poisson, and Euler equations to study density fluctuation growth. For BBNS, they use analytic SIV expressions for the expansion factor a(T) and time tau(T) as inputs to the PRIMAT code, incorporating SIV-guided modifications to reaction rates and temperature dependences.

## Key numbers

- The SIV gauge solution gives lambda = t_0/t with t_0 = sqrt(3/Lambda_E) (c=1)
- For galaxy dynamics, the SIV Radial Acceleration Relation yields g = g_N + k^2/2 + (1/2)*sqrt(4*g_N*k^2 + k^4), where g approaches a constant k^2 as g_N goes to 0
- In BBNS within SIV, the baryon density Omega_b is approximately 1.6% (compared to standard 4.9%), and total matter Omega_m is approximately 6% or 23% depending on whether lambda-scaling connections are used
- The photon-to-baryon ratio eta_10 in SIV is approximately 2, compared to the standard value of 6.09
- For density fluctuations, initial delta = 10^(-5) at z = 1376 with Omega_m = 0.10 shows faster growth in SIV than in the Einstein-de Sitter model

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SIV results on inflation (SIV-Inflation 2021), density fluctuation growth (Maeder and Gueorguiev 2019), and galaxy dynamics including MOND and dwarf spheroidals (Maeder and Gueorguiev 2020). The SIV paradigm provides an alternative to dark matter and dark energy explanations by modifying gravity through Weyl Integrable Geometry, with MOND shown to be a peculiar case of the SIV theory (Maeder 2023). The BBNS results (Gueorguiev and Maeder 2023) enable further exploration of SIV-guided modifications to reaction rates and temperature dependences, particularly the non-in-scalar factor T^beta in reverse reaction rates.
