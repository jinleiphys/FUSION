# Interplay of static and dynamic effects in 6He+ 238U Fusion

> We investigate the influence of the neutron halo and the breakup channel in 6He + 238U fusion at near-barrier energies. To include static effects of the 2n-halo in 6He nuclei, we use a single-folding potential obtained from an appropriate nucleon-238U interaction and a realistic 6He density. Dynamical effects arising from the breakup process are then included through coupled-channel calculations. These calculations suggest that static effects dominate the cross section at energies above the Coulomb barrier, while the sub-barrier fusion cross section appears to be determined by coupling to the breakup channel. This last conclusion is uncertain due to the procedure employed to measure the fusion cross-section.

# Interplay of static and dynamic effects in 6He+238U Fusion

## Key claim
Static effects from the neutron halo dominate the fusion cross section at energies above the Coulomb barrier, while coupling to the breakup channel appears to determine the sub-barrier fusion cross section; however, this conclusion is uncertain because the experimental procedure used to measure the fusion cross section may have misidentified transfer-fission events as fusion-fission events.

## Method
The authors used a single-folding model with a realistic 6He density and a nucleon-238U interaction to construct the optical potential, incorporating static halo effects. They then performed coupled-channel calculations with an effective breakup channel represented by a single state at the breakup threshold energy, including both Coulomb dipole and nuclear dipole coupling form factors, to evaluate dynamical effects on the fusion cross section.

## Key numbers
- Coulomb barrier height: V_B ≈ 22.3 MeV for 6He+238U
- Breakup threshold energy: ε₁ = 0.975 MeV (the energy binding the dineutron to 4He in 6He)
- Reduced transition probability: B(E1,0→1) = 1.37 e² fm² (from cluster model)
- 6He r.m.s. radius: r_rms = 2.30 fm (from realistic parametrization)
- 4He r.m.s. radius: r_rms = 1.49 fm (used for test calculation)
- Nuclear potential depth parameter: W₀ = 50 MeV for the imaginary part

## Context
This work extends the study of fusion reactions induced by halo nuclei, following experimental data from Trotta et al. (2000) showing large enhancement of 6He+238U fusion relative to 4He+238U. The authors compare with earlier coupled-channel treatments by Hagino et al. (2000) and Diaz-Torres et al. (2002) that used continuum discretization. The paper concludes that neutron-transfer channels, not breakup coupling alone, are likely responsible for the sub-barrier enhancement, consistent with later reanalysis by Trotta et al. (2003) indicating transfer-fission contamination. This enables future work incorporating transfer channels into fusion models for halo nuclei.
