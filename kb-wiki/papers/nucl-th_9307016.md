# Inertial parameters of the Skyrmion-Skyrmion system with the product ansatz

> The kinetic energy of the Skyrmion-Skyrmion system is calculated in the product ansatz. The energy is expanded in a set of rotationally invariant quadratic functions of translational and iso-rotational velocities in the product ansatz and all inertial parameters are evaluated. Most of the iso-spin dependent parameters are small at separations larger than 1 fm. However, some parameters of the coupling between the iso-rotational velocities of the Skyrmions decay slowly, as $1/R$, when zero pion mass is used. Otherwise we find that the results are not sensitive to the addition of the pion mass term.

## Key claim

The kinetic energy of the Skyrmion-Skyrmion system is expanded in rotationally invariant quadratic functions of translational and iso-rotational velocities within the product ansatz, and all inertial parameters are evaluated. Most iso-spin dependent parameters are small at separations larger than 1 fm, but some coupling parameters between the iso-rotational velocities of the two Skyrmions decay slowly as 1/R when zero pion mass is used, although the results are otherwise not sensitive to the addition of the pion mass term.

## Method

The authors substitute the product ansatz for the B=2 Skyrmion system, with time-dependent collective coordinates (relative separation R and iso-rotation matrices A1 and A2 for each Skyrmion), into the Skyrme Lagrangian. They expand the resulting kinetic energy density in a set of rotationally invariant quadratic functions of the translational velocity V and the iso-rotational velocities Rt(A1) and Rt(A2), then integrate over space to obtain the kinetic energy as a sum of terms with coefficients (inertial parameters) that depend on the separation R and the relative iso-orientation matrix C = A1^dagger A2. The three-dimensional integrals are reduced to two-dimensional integrals using axial symmetry around the axis connecting the two Skyrmions.

## Key numbers

- Skyrme model parameters used: f_pi = 64.5 MeV, e = 5.45 for zero pion mass; f_pi = 54.1 MeV, e = 4.84 for physical pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV.
- The coefficient d1^(0) (translational kinetic energy term independent of iso-orientation) goes over to 1/4 the mass of a single Skyrmion at large R.
- The coefficient d1^(1) (pure iso-kinetic energy for a single Skyrmion) goes over to twice the moment of inertia 2 Lambda at large R.
- The coupling term u3^(1), principally responsible for iso-spin independent spin-orbit coupling, is reported to be about twice as large as previously calculated by Riska and Otofuji.
- Some coupling parameters between iso-rotational velocities decay slowly as 1/R when zero pion mass is used.

## Context

This work provides a complete systematic analysis of the kinetic energy in the B=2 Skyrmion system within the product ansatz, extending prior partial studies by Oka and Odawara (who studied translational inertial parameters only), Riska and co-workers (who investigated velocity-iso-rotation coupling terms for spin-orbit interactions), and Otofuji et al. (similar spin-orbit studies). The authors note that their recent work (Shao, Walet, Amado, SpinOrbit) demonstrated that proper treatment of the spin-orbit interaction requires inversion of the mass matrix before projection onto nucleon states, which cannot be done when coupling terms are studied alone. The results enable construction of a quantum mechanical Hamiltonian for the Skyrmion-Skyrmion system, though the authors do not carry out the matrix inversion or projection onto nucleon states in this paper.
