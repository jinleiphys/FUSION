# Covariant density functional theory: Reexamining the structure of superheavy nuclei

> A systematic investigation of even-even superheavy elements in the region of proton numbers $100 \leq Z \leq 130$ and in the region of neutron numbers from the proton-drip line up to neutron number $N=196$ is presented. For this study we use five most up-to-date covariant energy density functionals of different types, with a non-linear meson coupling, with density dependent meson couplings, and with density-dependent zero-range interactions. Pairing correlations are treated within relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory based on an effective separable particle-particle interaction of finite range and deformation effects are taken into account. This allows us to assess the spread of theoretical predictions within the present covariant models for the binding energies, deformation parameters, shell structures and $α$-decay half-lives. Contrary to the previous studies in covariant density functional theory, it was found that the impact of $N=172$ spherical shell gap on the structure of superheavy elements is very limited. Similar to non-relativistic functionals some covariant functionals predict the important role played by the spherical $N=184$ gap. For these functionals (NL3*, DD-ME2 and PC-PK1), there is a band of spherical nuclei along and near the $Z=120$ and $N=184$ lines. However, for other functionals (DD-PC1 and DD-ME$δ$) oblate shapes dominate at and in the vicinity of these lines. Available experimental data are in general described with comparable accuracy and do not allow to discriminate these predictions.

## Key claim

Contrary to earlier covariant density functional theory studies, the spherical N=172 shell gap has only a very limited impact on the structure of superheavy elements, while the N=184 gap plays an important role for some functionals (NL3*, DD-ME2, PC-PK1) but not for others (DD-PC1, DD-MEδ), and available experimental data cannot discriminate between these predictions.

## Method

The authors performed systematic relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations for even-even superheavy nuclei with proton numbers Z=100-130 and neutron numbers from the proton-drip line up to N=196, using five covariant energy density functionals (NL3*, DD-ME2, DD-MEδ, DD-PC1, and PC-PK1). Pairing correlations were treated with a separable finite-range Gogny interaction, and axial deformation effects were included by calculating potential energy curves over a quadrupole deformation range from β2=-1.0 to β2=1.05 in steps of 0.02.

## Key numbers

- The study covers proton numbers 100 ≤ Z ≤ 130 and neutron numbers up to N=196
- For NL3*, the band of spherical nuclei at Z≈120 extends on average three even-even nuclei in the Z direction for N=172-188
- For NL3*, the band of spherical nuclei at N≈184 extends on average four even-even nuclei in the N direction for Z=96-120
- The separable pairing interaction uses parameters G=738 fm³ and a=0.636 fm, derived from the Gogny D1S force
- The oscillator basis truncation uses N_F=20 fermionic shells and N_B=20 bosonic shells, with tests up to N_F=26 and N_B=26 for heavy neutron-rich nuclei
- Outer fission barriers in many functionals are around 2 MeV or less, with triaxial or octupole deformation reducing them by 2-4 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier CDFT studies of superheavy nuclei from the 1990s and early 2000s (Rutz 1997, Bender 1999, Afanasjev 2005) that were mostly restricted to spherical shapes and older functionals like NL1 and NL3. It uses the new generation of functionals whose global performance was established in Agbemava (2014) and Zhao (2014). The results show that deformation effects can drastically change the predicted ground-state shapes, with some functionals predicting oblate ground states where spherical minima were previously assumed, and the study enables future work on symmetry-unrestricted RHB calculations for fission barriers and improved single-particle descriptions through particle-vibration coupling.
