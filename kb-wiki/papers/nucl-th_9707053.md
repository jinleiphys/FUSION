# SMMC studies of N=Z pf-shell nuclei with pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian

> We perform Shell Model Monte Carlo calculations of selected N=Z pf-shell nuclei with a schematic hamiltonian containing isovector pairing and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions. Compared to realistic interactions, this hamiltonian does not give rise to the SMMC ``sign problem'', while at the same time resembles essential features of the realistic interactions. We study pairing correlations in the ground states of N=Z nuclei and investigate the thermal dependence of selected observables for the odd-odd nucleus Co54 and the even-even nuclei Zn60 and Ni60. Comparison of the present results to those with the realistic KB3 interaction indicates a transition with increasing temperature from a phase of isovector pairing dominance to one where isoscalar pairing correlations dominate. In addition, our results confirm the qualitative reliability of the procedure used to cure the sign problem in the SMMC calculations with realistic forces.

# SMMC studies of N=Z pf-shell nuclei with pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian

## Key claim

Using a schematic Hamiltonian containing only isovector pairing and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions (which avoids the SMMC sign problem entirely), the authors find a transition with increasing temperature from isovector pairing dominance to isoscalar pairing dominance in odd-odd N=Z pf-shell nuclei, and confirm that the g-extrapolation procedure used to cure the sign problem in SMMC calculations with realistic forces is qualitatively reliable.

## Method

Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations were performed for selected N=Z pf-shell nuclei using a Hamiltonian consisting of single-particle energies from the KB3 interaction, an isospin-symmetric isovector pairing term with strength G=20/A MeV, and a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction rescaled by a factor 0.6 from the original Bes and Sorensen (1969) parametrization to match realistic KB3 results for quadrupole observables. Ground-state properties were computed at a temperature of T=0.25 MeV, and thermal dependence was studied over the range T=0.25-5 MeV for ⁵⁴Co, ⁶⁰Zn, and ⁶⁰Ni. Pair correlations were measured using the BCS pair operator for isovector s-wave (J=0) pairing in the pp, nn, and pn channels.

## Key numbers

- Pairing constant: G = 20/A MeV, taken from Bes and Sorensen (1969)
- Quadrupole coupling rescaled by factor 0.6 from the original Bes and Sorensen (1969) parametrization χ = 240/(b⁴ A⁵/³) MeV/fm⁴
- Ground-state energy displacement between odd-odd and even-even N=Z nuclei: approximately 1.1 MeV (about half the experimental value)
- For ⁵⁸Cu at λ=0 (no pairing): ⟨T²⟩ = 0.58, ⟨J²⟩ = 8; for λ ≥ 1: ⟨T²⟩ ≈ 2, ⟨J²⟩ ≈ 2
- Mean-field values for ⁵⁸Cu (no interaction): ⟨J²⟩ = 8.5, ⟨T²⟩ = 0.8, 𝒩₀ = 4.1, 𝒩₁ = 4.0
- Level density parameter a exceeds mean-field by about 1.5/MeV for ⁶⁰Zn and about 1/MeV for ⁵⁴Co at T=1 MeV
- Pair breaking transition in even-even nuclei occurs near T ≈ 1 MeV
- At T=2.5 MeV, the g-extrapolation with KB3 underestimates isovector pair correlations by about 20%
- SMMC calculations performed for temperature range 0.25 MeV to 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier SMMC studies of N=Z nuclei using the realistic KB3 interaction by Langanke et al. (1995, 1997) and Engel et al. (1996), which required a g-extrapolation procedure to overcome the sign problem. The schematic pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian, originally parametrized by Bes and Sorensen (1969), allows exact SMMC calculations without the sign problem and reproduces the essential features of isovector pairing correlations seen with realistic forces. The results confirm the earlier finding of a transition from isovector to isoscalar pairing dominance with increasing temperature in odd-odd N=Z nuclei, and validate the g-extrapolation procedure used in prior work. The study also contrasts with earlier Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Goodman (1979) and Sandhu and Rustgi (1975, 1976) which had concluded that T=1 pn pairing is unimportant, a conclusion contradicted by the ground-state T=1 assignments of odd-odd N=Z nuclei with A≥40. The work enables further study of isoscalar correlations, identified as the focus of ongoing work.
