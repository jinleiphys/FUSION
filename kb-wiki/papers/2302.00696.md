# Deciding on the anomalous magnetic moment of quarks in a framework of nonlocal NJL model

> Anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of quarks in presence of an external magnetic field has been explored using a nonlocal Nambu\textemdash Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. Various strengths of AMM differing in orders of magnitude are used in the literature. We explore them in our nonlocal framework to decide on their strength. We checked the validity of using constant AMM of quarks and investigate two different temperature and magnetic field dependent forms. The forms are taken as the AMM being proportional to the i) meanfield and the ii) square of the meanfield. On comparison with the lattice data for both the condensate averages and differences, it turns out that the second choice is the most suitable one, in such an effective model scenario. In the process, we also keep track of the phase diagram in the $T-eB$ plane arising from our model calculation. The outcome is reasonable as far as the lattice QCD result is concerned.

# Deciding on the anomalous magnetic moment of quarks in a framework of nonlocal NJL model

## Key claim
The authors determine that a temperature- and magnetic-field-dependent anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of quarks proportional to the square of the scalar meanfield ($\kappa_f \propto \sigma_{s,f}^2$) with proportionality constant $v=0.01$ is the most suitable choice within a nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, based on comparison with lattice QCD data for both condensate averages and condensate differences. This conclusion is conditional on the specific nonlocal framework and the Gaussian form factor used.

## Method
The authors employ a 2-flavor nonlocal NJL model with a Gaussian form factor, incorporating an external magnetic field and an anomalous magnetic moment term for quarks. They compute the free energy, gap equations, and regularized quark condensates using the Ritus eigenfunction method to handle the magnetic field, then introduce temperature via the Matsubara formalism. They test constant AMM values (two sets differing by an order of magnitude) and two temperature-dependent forms where the AMM is proportional to either the meanfield ($\sigma_s$) or its square ($\sigma_s^2$), each with proportionality constants $v=0.1$ and $v=0.01$, comparing the resulting condensate averages and differences against lattice QCD data from Bali et al. (2012).

## Key numbers
- Constant AMM set-I: $\kappa_u = 0.29016\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$, $\kappa_d = 0.35986\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$, rejected as it fails to reproduce lattice condensate averages.
- Constant AMM set-II: $\kappa_u = 0.00995\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$, $\kappa_d = 0.07975\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$, rejected as condensate averages do not match lattice data for magnetic fields above $0.6\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.
- The successful form $\kappa_f \propto \sigma_{s,f}^2$ uses $v = 0.01$ (dimensionless proportionality constant) and reproduces lattice data for magnetic field values up to at least $0.6\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.
- The form $\kappa_f \propto \sigma_{s,f}$ with $v=0.01$ reproduces condensate averages but fails for condensate differences at $eB > 0.6\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of quark AMM in local NJL models (Fayazbakhsh et al., 2014; Xu et al., 2020; Ghosh et al., 2021; Kawaguchi et al., 2022) to a nonlocal NJL framework, which naturally produces inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) without explicit magnetic-field-dependent couplings (Pagura et al., 2016). The study is motivated by the lack of consensus on AMM strength in the literature, where values can differ by orders of magnitude. The results enable future effective model studies to use a physically motivated, temperature-dependent AMM form that is consistent with lattice QCD data from Bali et al. (2012), and the approach can be extended to investigate other observables such as meson spectra and magnetic susceptibility.
