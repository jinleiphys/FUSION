# Modified Quark-Meson Coupling Model for Nuclear Matter

> The quark-meson coupling model for nuclear matter, which describes nuclear matter as non-overlapping MIT bags bound by the self-consistent exchange of scalar and vector mesons, is modified by introducing medium modification of the bag constant. We model the density dependence of the bag constant in two different ways: one invokes a direct coupling of the bag constant to the scalar meson field, and the other relates the bag constant to the in-medium nucleon mass. Both models feature a decreasing bag constant with increasing density. We find that when the bag constant is significantly reduced in nuclear medium with respect to its free-space value, large canceling isoscalar Lorentz scalar and vector potentials for the nucleon in nuclear matter emerge naturally. Such potentials are comparable to those suggested by relativistic nuclear phenomenology and finite-density QCD sum rules. This suggests that the reduction of bag constant in nuclear medium may play an important role in low- and medium-energy nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper modifies the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model by introducing a density-dependent bag constant that decreases in nuclear matter, and shows that when this reduction is significant, the model naturally produces large canceling isoscalar Lorentz scalar and vector potentials for the nucleon comparable to those from relativistic nuclear phenomenology and finite-density QCD sum rules, thereby recovering the essential features of relativistic nuclear phenomenology that the original QMC model failed to reproduce.

## Method

The authors modify the standard QMC model, which describes nuclear matter as non-overlapping MIT bags interacting via self-consistent scalar and vector meson exchange in mean-field approximation, by introducing two distinct parametrizations of a density-dependent bag constant: a direct coupling model where the bag constant couples to the scalar meson field via a power-law or exponential form, and a scaling model where the bag constant scales as a power of the in-medium nucleon effective mass. For each model, the coupling constants are adjusted to reproduce the nuclear matter binding energy of -16 MeV at saturation density 0.17 fm^{-3}, and the self-consistent equations for the bag radius, quark wave function eigenvalue, and scalar mean field are solved simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: 0.17 fm^{-3}; binding energy: -16 MeV
- For the direct coupling model with R_0 = 0.6 fm and delta = 13 (g_sigma^q = 0), B/B_0 = 0.36, M_N^* = 660-720 MeV, U_v = 150-215 MeV at saturation density
- For the scaling model with R_0 = 0.6 fm and kappa = 2.95, B/B_0 = 0.36, M_N^* = 660-680 MeV, U_v = 190-225 MeV at saturation density
- Free-space bag constant values for R_0 = 0.6, 0.8, 1.0 fm: B_0^{1/4} = 188.1, 157.5, 136.3 MeV respectively
- In the direct coupling model with g_sigma^q = 0 and delta = 4, the model reproduces QHD-I results with M_N^*/M_N = 0.55, U_v/M_N = 0.37, compressibility K_V^{-1} = 540 MeV
- Bag radius and quark RMS radius increase by 25-30% in nuclear matter relative to free space when B/B_0 = 0.36

## Context

This work extends the original QMC model of Guichon (1988) and its refinements by Fleck et al. (1990) and Saito and Thomas (1994), which predicted much smaller scalar and vector potentials than required by relativistic nuclear phenomenology and finite-density QCD sum rules. The authors had previously pointed out in Jin and Jennings (1996) that fixing the bag constant at its free-space value was questionable. The modified model recovers potentials comparable to those from Dirac phenomenology (Hama et al., 1990; Wallace, 1987), Brueckner calculations, and QCD sum rules (Cohen et al., 1995), and enables further study of how the reduction of the bag constant in nuclear medium may affect low- and medium-energy nuclear physics, including spin-orbit splittings in finite nuclei and nucleon-nucleus scattering.
