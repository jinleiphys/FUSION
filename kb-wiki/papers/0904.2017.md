# Suppression of isoscalar pairing

> The short-range nuclear attraction is stronger in the isoscalar channel than in the isovector channel, as evidenced by the existence of the deuteron and not the dineutron. Nevertheless, apart from light N=Z nuclei, pairing is only seen in the isovector channel. This is explained by the effect of the strong spin-orbit splitting on the single-particle energies. A semiquantitative argument is presented treating the high-j orbitals at the Fermi surface as plane waves on a two-dimensional sheet.

# Suppression of isoscalar pairing

## Key claim

The short-range nuclear attraction is stronger in the isoscalar (spin-triplet, T=0) channel than in the isovector (spin-singlet, T=1) channel, yet pairing is only observed in the isovector channel except in light N=Z nuclei. This is explained by the strong spin-orbit splitting of single-particle energies, which suppresses isoscalar pairing by a factor of four compared to a factor of two suppression for isovector pairing when the space is restricted to the preferred spin orientation of high-j orbitals.

## Method

The authors model high-j orbitals at the nuclear surface as plane waves on a two-dimensional tangent plane, where the spin-orbit field forces the spin to be perpendicular to the momentum in the plane. They construct two-particle states with opposite momenta and preferred spin orientations, then compute interaction matrix elements using a contact interaction with spin projectors P(0) and P(1) for singlet and triplet channels. By analyzing the angular momentum M of the mixed states, they determine the expectation values of the interaction energy for different M values and compare them to the full spin-space results.

## Key numbers

- For M=0 states (T=1, isovector), the interaction energy expectation value is v₀/2, representing a factor of 2 reduction from the full spin-space strength v₀.
- For M=±1 states (T=0, isoscalar), the interaction energy expectation value is v₁/4, representing a factor of 4 reduction from the full spin-space strength v₁.
- The recoupling coefficient for (jj)^{J=0} from (ℓℓ)^{L=0}, S=0 is √(2j+1)/(4j), which approaches √(1/2) in the limit of large j, confirming the factor of 2 suppression for singlet pairing.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Baldo et al. (1995) showing that realistic interactions predict strong isoscalar pairing in nuclear matter, and shell-model calculations by Poves and Martinez-Pinedo (1998) that addressed the nonappearance of T=0 pairing. The semiquantitative analytic treatment provides a clear physical mechanism for why isoscalar pairing is suppressed in heavy nuclei despite being intrinsically stronger, and it enables future studies of pairing in exotic neutron-rich nuclei where the competition between channels may be modified.
