# Exclusive coherent production of heavy vector mesons in nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC

> Heavy nuclei at collider energies are a source of high energy Weizsaecker-Williams photons. This photon flux may be utilized to study high energy photon-nucleus interactions. Here we concentrate on the coherent diffractive production of heavy vector mesons on nuclear targets and show how it probes the unintegrated glue of the nucleus in the saturation domain. We present predictions for rapidity distributions of exclusive coherent J/Psi and Upsilon mesons which can be measured by the ALICE experiment at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that exclusive coherent photoproduction of heavy vector mesons (J/Psi and Upsilon) in nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC energies can probe the unintegrated gluon distribution of the nucleus in the saturation domain, but the nuclear effects are stronger for J/Psi than for Upsilon because the smaller color dipoles relevant for Upsilon production experience weaker rescattering.

## Method

The authors use a color-dipole formulation combined with k_perp-factorization to compute the forward amplitude for gamma A -> V A. Multiple scattering of color dipoles on the nuclear target is summed via a Glauber-Gribov series, and the nuclear unintegrated gluon distribution is constructed from multiple convolutions of the free nucleon unintegrated glue. Small-x evolution is included by adding the Q Qbar g-Fock state contribution via the first iteration of the nonlinear Balitsky-Kovchegov equation. The nucleus-nucleus cross section is then obtained from the equivalent photon approximation with absorptive corrections (gap survival probability).

## Key numbers

- For lead-lead collisions at W_NN = 2760 GeV, the total cross section for J/Psi production increases by two orders of magnitude compared to RHIC energy (W_NN = 200 GeV)
- The ratio R_coh (full nuclear cross section divided by impulse approximation) deviates from unity more strongly for J/Psi than for Upsilon on a 208Pb target
- The impact parameter distributions are shown for two energies: W = 200 GeV and W = 2760 GeV for lead nuclei
- The rapidity distributions for AA collisions are much narrower than similar distributions in proton-proton collisions due to the large nuclear size and the sharper charge form factor

## Context

This work extends the color-dipole approach previously applied to free proton targets by the same authors (RSS08) and to nuclear structure functions (NSZZ). It differs from earlier estimates by Klein and Nystrand (2004), who used vector-meson dominance with an effective J/Psi-nucleon cross section, and from Goncalves and Machado (2007), who absorbed all saturation effects into the dipole-nucleon cross section before eikonalization. The predictions for rapidity distributions of exclusive coherent J/Psi and Upsilon mesons can be tested by the ALICE experiment at the LHC.
