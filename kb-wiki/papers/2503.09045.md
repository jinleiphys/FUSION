# Advancing multimessenger approaches in heavy-ion collisions: Insights from electromagnetic probes

> Electromagnetic (EM) probes, including photons and dileptons, do not interact strongly after their production in heavy-ion collisions, allowing them to carry undistorted information from their points of origin. This makes them powerful tools for studying early-stage equilibration and the thermodynamic properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). In these proceedings, we highlight recent theoretical advancements in EM probes, focusing on their role in probing early-stage dynamics and extracting medium properties. We also discuss the emerging multimessenger approach, which combines hadronic and electromagnetic probes to achieve a more comprehensive understanding of the QGP.

## Key claim

Electromagnetic probes (photons and dileptons) can serve as reliable thermometers of the QGP, but the temperatures extracted from experimental spectra represent effective or averaged quantities over the entire space-time evolution rather than well-defined thermodynamic properties; only through advanced theoretical modeling can a strong correlation be established between the extracted temperature and the average initial hydrodynamic temperature of the medium.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical calculations of thermal photon and dilepton emission rates using both perturbative QCD (extended to next-to-leading order with Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal resummation) and lattice QCD methods, then applies these rates within hydrodynamic and kinetic transport models (including KøMPøST for pre-equilibrium dynamics) to compute spectra and anisotropic flows for Au+Au and Pb-Pb collisions. The extracted effective temperatures from model-calculated dilepton invariant mass spectra in the range 1 ≤ M ≤ 3 GeV are correlated with the average initial hydrodynamic temperature of the medium. A multimessenger approach is demonstrated by combining temperatures extracted from photon transverse momentum spectra (with and without Doppler shift corrections) with those from dilepton invariant mass spectra to infer radial flow development.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 27 GeV, the effective temperature T_eff is extracted from the dilepton spectrum by linear fits in the mass range 1 ≤ M ≤ 3 GeV
- The correlation between extracted T_eff and initial average hydrodynamic temperature ⟨T_in⟩ is studied for collision energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV
- Dilepton elliptic flow v_n(p_T) is computed for Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, showing that faster equilibration leads to larger yields and smaller v_n(p_T)
- In the non-relativistic limit (M ≫ T), the dilepton invariant mass spectrum follows (MT)^(3/2)e^(-M/T); for photons with p_T ≫ T, the transverse momentum spectrum behaves as e^(-p_T/T)

## Context

This proceedings paper from the Hard Probes 2024 conference extends the theoretical framework for electromagnetic probes developed by Rapp (2014), Shen (2013), and the JETSCAPE collaboration (2020, 2024), building on recent NLO pQCD calculations by Churchill (2023) and lattice QCD comparisons by Ali (2024). The work enables future Bayesian analyses that systematically combine multimessenger data (photons, dileptons, and hadrons) to reduce model dependencies and provide tighter constraints on QGP properties such as temperature, viscosity, and initial conditions, with next-generation facilities at GSI/FAIR, HIAF, and NICA expected to extend these studies to different temperature and density conditions.
