# Phenomenological analysis of the double pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions up to 2.2 GeV

> With an effective Lagrangian approach, we analyze several NN \to NNππchannels by including various resonances with mass up to 1.72 GeV. For the channels with the pion pair of isospin zero, we confirm the dominance of N*(1440)\to Nσin the near threshold region. At higher energies and for channels with the final pion pair of isospin one, we find large contributions from N*(1440)\to Δπ, double-Delta, Δ(1600) \to N*(1440)π, Δ(1600) \to Δπand Δ(1620) \to Δπ. There are also sizeable contributions from Δ\to Δπ, Δ\to Nπ, N \to Δπand nucleon pole at energies close to the threshold. We well reproduce the total cross sections up to beam energies of 2.2 GeV except for the pp\to ppπ^0π^0 channel at energies around 1.1 GeV and our results agree with the existing data of differential cross sections of pp \to ppπ^+π^-, pp \to nnπ^+π^+ and pp \to ppπ^0π^0 which are measured at CELSIUS and COSY.

## Key claim

Using an effective Lagrangian approach that includes resonances up to 1.72 GeV, the authors reproduce total cross sections for six isospin channels of NN → NNππ up to 2.2 GeV, except for the pp → ppπ⁰π⁰ channel around 1.1 GeV where the model fails to describe a level-off behavior in the total cross section.

## Method

The authors constructed Feynman diagrams for NN → NNππ including resonance production via N*(1440), Δ, Δ(1600), Δ(1620), and other resonances up to 1.72 GeV, using effective Lagrangians for πNN, πΔΔ, σNN, and ρNN couplings with off-shell form factors from the Bonn potential model. Coupling constants were determined from empirical partial decay widths from PDG, with the N*(1440) → Δπ branching ratio set to nearly half the PDG value to match recent data. The model calculated total and differential cross sections for six isospin channels and compared them to data from CELSIUS and COSY up to beam energies of 2.2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections reproduced up to beam energies of 2.2 GeV, except for pp → ppπ⁰π⁰ around 1.1 GeV
- N*(1440) → Nσ dominates near threshold (below 1000 MeV) in isospin-zero channels, with branching ratio 0.075 for Nσ and 0.135 for Δπ (reduced from PDG value)
- Double-Δ mechanism dominates pp → nnπ⁺π⁺ between 1000 and 1700 MeV, with Δ(1600) → N*(1440)π taking over above 1700 MeV
- For pp → ppπ⁰π⁰, the model overestimates high-energy data and fails to reproduce the level-off in total cross section between 1000 and 1200 MeV
- Coupling constants used: f²_πNN/4π = 0.078, g²_σNN/4π = 5.69, g²_ρNN/4π = 0.9, with cutoff parameters Λ_π = Λ_η = 1.0 GeV, Λ_σ = 1.3 GeV, Λ_ρ = 1.6 GeV
- σ-meson mass = 550 MeV, width = 500 MeV; g²_σππ = 6.06, g²_ρππ = 2.91

## Context

This work extends the Valencia model (Alvarez, ~2000) which included only N*(1440) and Δ resonances, by incorporating all N* and Δ* resonances up to 1.72 GeV to address the isospin problem where the Valencia model predicted the pp → ppπ⁰π⁰ total cross section about four times larger than pp → nnπ⁺π⁺, while data showed approximately equal values. The analysis uses comprehensive differential cross section data from CELSIUS and COSY (2000-2008) that were not available for earlier OPE model calculations (Ferrari, ~1965). The results enable better determination of N*(1440) decay properties and provide a framework for studying Δ* resonances at higher energies, though the failure to describe the pp → ppπ⁰π⁰ channel around 1.1 GeV indicates the need for further investigation of ππ system dynamics.
