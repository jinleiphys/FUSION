# Fast Heating Dissociation of Upsilon(1S) in Heavy Ion Collisions at RHIC

> With the adiabatic assumption in the cooling process, we discussed a new mechanism on Upsilon(1S) suppression that is due to the fast heating process at the early stage of the fireball instead of its finite decay width in finite temperature medium produced in the heavy ion collisions. We calculated the transition probability after the fast heating dissociation as a function of the temperature of the medium and the nuclear modification factor in central collisions, and found that the suppression is not negligible at RHIC, even if the width of Upsilon(1S) vanishes.

# Fast Heating Dissociation of Upsilon(1S) in Heavy Ion Collisions at RHIC

## Key claim

Even if the in-medium dissociation rate (width) of Upsilon(1S) vanishes at finite temperature, suppression can still occur in heavy-ion collisions due to the fast heating process at the very early stage of the fireball, because the sudden change of the potential causes the initial wave function to project onto excited states. This suppression is not negligible at RHIC, and the mechanism can be used as a thermometer for the early-stage fireball temperature, though the measured temperature should be interpreted as the temperature the Upsilon(1S) "feels" rather than the highest temperature of the fireball.

## Method

The authors solve the Schroedinger equation for the Upsilon(1S) wave function using a screened Cornell potential with temperature-dependent screening mass parameterized from lattice QCD free energy data, setting the in-medium width to zero. They compute the transition probability as the squared overlap between the zero-temperature eigenstate and the finite-temperature eigenstate, then integrate this probability over the transverse spatial profile of the fireball using a Glauber model to obtain the nuclear modification factor R_AA in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- The dissociation temperature of Upsilon(1S) is T_d approximately 3 T_c, where T_c is the critical temperature of the phase transition.
- The survival probability during adiabatic cooling from 2 T_c to T_c over 10 fm/c is about 0.98; over 5 fm/c it is about 0.93, compared to the transition probability P(2.0) = 0.76.
- The nuclear modification factor R_AA is above 0.9 if the central temperature T(0) is lower than 1.6 T_c, and below 0.8 when T(0) is higher than 2.1 T_c.
- The screening mass parameterization uses s = 0.587, a = 2.150, b = 1.054, sigma_t = 0.07379, with sigma = 0.2 GeV^2 and alpha = pi/12.
- The nuclear radius R = 6.38 fm and mass number A = 197 for gold, with inelastic nucleon cross section sigma_NN = 41 mb at RHIC energy.

## Context

This work extends the sequential dissociation model of Karsch (2005) and dynamic studies of quarkonium dissociation rates by Thews (2000), Grandchamp (2001), and others, by introducing a new mechanism that operates even when the dissociation width vanishes. The authors note that J/psi at RHIC and higher energies is complicated by regeneration and scattering, so they focus on Upsilon(1S) where both dissociation and regeneration rates are small at RHIC energy, following Liu (2009) and Du (2017). The result enables using Upsilon(1S) suppression as a thermometer for the early-stage fireball temperature, complementary to temperature extraction from light hadron spectra via the statistical model of Andronic (2009).
