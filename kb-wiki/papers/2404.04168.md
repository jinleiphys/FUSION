# Jet angularities in dijet production in proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions at RHIC

> We study jet angularities for dijet production at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) in proton-proton (pp) and nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions at 200 GeV nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass collision energy. In particular, we provide $\mathrm{NLL}$ resummed predictions for angularity observables of groomed and ungroomed jets produced in $\rm pp$ collisions matched to next-to-leading order QCD calculations resulting in $\mathrm{NLO+NLL^\prime}$ accuracy. Our parton-level predictions are corrected for non-perturbative effects, such as hadronization and underlying event, using parton-to-hadron level transfer matrices obtained with the Sherpa event generator. Furthermore, we use the Q-Pythia and JEWEL generators to estimate the impact of the interaction between quarks and gluons produced by the parton shower with the dense medium formed in heavy-ion collisions on the considered jet angularities.

## Key claim

The paper provides next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) resummed predictions for jet angularities in dijet production at RHIC, matched to next-to-leading order QCD for NLO+NLL' accuracy, but these predictions are strictly valid only in the limit of small soft-drop grooming parameter zcut (zcut much less than 1) and the authors assume that finite-zcut corrections for their chosen zcut=0.2 are negligible without including related corrections.

## Method

The authors used the Caesar resummation formalism implemented in the Sherpa event generator framework to perform NLL resummation for jet angularities (lambda_{1/2}, lambda_1, lambda_2) in dijet production at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, matched to NLO fixed-order calculations using multiplicative matching separately for quark and gluon jets. They corrected parton-level resummed predictions for non-perturbative effects using parton-to-hadron level transfer matrices obtained from Sherpa simulations, and estimated medium effects in heavy-ion collisions using the Q-Pythia and JEWEL event generators.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass collision energy: 200 GeV nucleon-nucleon at RHIC
- Jet radius parameter: R0 = 0.4
- Leading jet transverse momentum cut: pT,j1 > 30 GeV
- Subleading jet transverse momentum cut: pT,j2 > 20 GeV
- Jet rapidity acceptance: |y_j1,j2| < 0.7
- Soft-drop grooming parameters: zcut = 0.2, beta = 0
- Strong coupling constant: alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.118
- PDF set: NNLO PDF4LHC21_40_pdfas
- Central scale choice: mu_F = mu_R = H_T/2
- At LHC (13 TeV), MPI multiplicity peaks at n_MPI approximately 10; at RHIC with Monash tune, peak at n_MPI = 2; with Detroit tune, peak at n_MPI = 0

## Context

This work extends the NLO+NLL' accurate jet angularity predictions from Caletti (2021) and Reichelt (2021) from LHC energies to RHIC energies, where non-perturbative effects are expected to be much larger due to lower collision energy. The predictions are intended for future measurements with the sPHENIX detector at RHIC, and the authors find that non-perturbative corrections at RHIC are dominated by hadronization effects rather than underlying event activity, in contrast to LHC conditions. The study enables tests of hadronization models in general-purpose Monte Carlo generators such as Pythia, Sherpa, and Herwig at lower collision energies.
