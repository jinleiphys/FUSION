# Strangeness in QGP: Hadronization Pressure

> We review strangeness as signature of quark gluon plasma (QGP) and the hadronization process of a QGP fireball formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions in the entire range of today accessible reaction energies. We discuss energy dependence of the statistical hadronization parameters within the context of fast QGP hadronization. We find that QGP breakup occurs for all energies at the universal hadronization pressure $P = 80\pm 3\,\mathrm{MeV/fm}^3 $.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the hadronization of a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) fireball occurs at a universal pressure of \(P = 80 \pm 3\,\mathrm{MeV/fm}^3\) across nearly all accessible heavy-ion collision energies, with the notable exception of the lowest SPS energy (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.26\,\mathrm{GeV}\)), which appears to belong to a different class of events.

## Method

The authors apply a chemical non-equilibrium statistical hadronization model (SHM) to analyze particle production yields from central Pb-Pb and Au-Au collisions at CERN-SPS, BNL-RHIC, and LHC, covering a center-of-mass energy range from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.26\,\mathrm{GeV}\) to \(2760\,\mathrm{GeV}\). They fit the data using the SHARE with CHARM program to extract freeze-out temperature \(T\), baryochemical potential \(\mu_B\), and phase space occupancies for light quarks (\(\gamma_q\)) and strangeness (\(\gamma_s\)). The total hadronization pressure is then computed by summing the partial pressure contributions of all directly observed and inferred hadron species.

## Key numbers

*   Universal hadronization pressure: \(P = 80 \pm 3\,\mathrm{MeV/fm}^3\) for all collision systems except the lowest SPS energy.
*   Freeze-out temperature at high energy: \(T \simeq 140\,\mathrm{MeV}\), changing overall by about 10% across energies.
*   Light quark phase space occupancy: \(\gamma_q \simeq 1.6\) across all energies except the lowest SPS point.
*   Strangeness phase space occupancy: \(1.5 < \gamma_s < 2.4\) depending on reaction energy, excluding the lowest SPS energy.
*   Ratio of strangeness to entropy \(s/S\) at LHC: near 0.030; at RHIC: clustering at 0.0325.
*   Increase in \(s/S\) by 50% between the two lowest SPS energies: \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.26\,\mathrm{GeV}\) and \(7.61\,\mathrm{GeV}\).
*   Baryochemical potential \(\mu_B\) varies by a factor of 500 across the energy range, from a fraction of the nucleon mass to MeV range at LHC.
*   At the lowest SPS energy (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.26\,\mathrm{GeV}\)), hadronization temperature is \(T \sim 152\,\mathrm{MeV}\) and phase space occupancies \(\gamma_{s,q} < 1\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of strangeness as a signature of QGP, originally proposed by Rafelski (1982) and developed with input from Redlich (1985), to the full range of heavy-ion collision energies from SPS to LHC. The finding of a universal hadronization pressure, first suggested based on SPS data by Rafelski (2009), is now confirmed across RHIC and LHC energies, providing strong evidence for a sudden hadronization mechanism of the QGP fireball. The results enable the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and future facilities like NICA and FAIR-GSI-CBM to identify the onset of deconfinement by studying strange particle production in the energy range \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.26-7.61\,\mathrm{GeV}\).
