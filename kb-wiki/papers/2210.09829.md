# Exciting the long-lived Higgs mode in superfluid Fermi gases with particle removal

> Experimental evidence of the Higgs mode in strongly interacting superfluid Fermi gases had not been observed until recently [Behrle et al., Nat. Phys. 14, 781 (2018)]. Due to the coupling with other collective modes and quasiparticle excitations, generating stable Higgs-mode oscillations is challenging. We study how to excite long-lived Higgs-mode oscillations in a homogeneous superfluid Fermi gas in the BCS-BEC crossover. We find that the Higgs mode can be excited by time-periodically modulating the scattering length at an appropriate amplitude and frequency. However, even for a modulation frequency below twice the pairing-gap energy, quasiparticles are still excited through the generation of higher harmonics due to nonlinearity in the superfluid. More importantly, we find that persistent Higgs-mode oscillations with almost constant amplitude can be produced by removing particles at an appropriate momentum, and the oscillation amplitude can be controlled by the number of removed particles. Finally, we propose two ways to experimentally realize particle removal.

## Key claim

Persistent Higgs-mode oscillations with almost constant amplitude can be produced in a homogeneous superfluid Fermi gas by removing particles at an appropriate momentum corresponding to the peak of the pair wave function, and the oscillation amplitude can be controlled by the number of removed particles; this scheme works in the BCS-BEC crossover from the BCS limit to the unitary regime, but not in the deep BEC regime.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the time-dependent Bogoliubov-de Gennes (TD-BdG) equations for a homogeneous superfluid Fermi gas at zero temperature in the BCS-BEC crossover. They first prepared an equilibrium state for a given interaction strength \(1/k_F a\), then either applied a time-periodic modulation of the coupling constant \(g(t) = g_0 (1 + A \sin \omega t)\) or removed particles in a finite momentum range around a chosen wave number \(k_{\text{hole}}\) to simulate a quench. The time evolution of the pairing gap \(\Delta(t)\) and momentum distribution \(n_k\) was monitored to characterize the Higgs-mode oscillations.

## Key numbers

* For the particle-removal scheme with \(5\%\) of particles removed at \(k_{\text{hole}}/k_F = 0.975\) at unitarity (\(1/k_F a = 0\)), the oscillation frequency of \(|\Delta(t)|\) is around \(2\Delta_0/\hbar\), consistent with the Higgs mode frequency.
* The relative amplitude of the Higgs-mode oscillation at the first maximum time \(t_1\) is of the order of \(1\%\) for \(\delta n/n = 5\%\), which is much larger than the order of \(0.1\%\) observed in the experiment of Behrle et al. (2018).
* For \(\delta n/n = 10\%\), the oscillation amplitude is roughly double that for \(\delta n/n = 5\%\), while the effective lifetime \(t_1\) remains almost the same.
* The threshold frequency \(\omega_{\text{th}}\) for the time-periodic modulation scheme approaches \(2|\Delta_0|/\hbar\) in the limit of modulation amplitude \(A \rightarrow 0\) for \(1/k_F a = -1\) and \(0\).
* For the BEC side at \(1/k_F a = 1\), the threshold frequency in the limit \(A \rightarrow 0\) is \(2\sqrt{\mu^2+|\Delta_0|^2}/\hbar \approx 3.11 E_F/\hbar\).
* The particle-removal scheme is applicable for exciting long-lived Higgs-mode oscillations up to the shallow BEC side of \(1/k_F a \lesssim 0.5\).

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies on the Higgs mode in superfluid Fermi gases, which began with Volkov and Kogan (1973) and includes predictions of power-law decay in the BCS regime (\(t^{-1/2}\)) and BEC regime (\(t^{-3/2}\)) by Gurarie (2009). It addresses the challenge of generating stable Higgs-mode oscillations against decay, which was identified as difficult due to coupling with the Goldstone mode and quasiparticle excitations. The proposed particle-removal scheme enables long-lived oscillations with larger amplitude than those observed experimentally by Behrle et al. (2018), and could allow probing material phase information such as interband couplings in multiband superconductors or superconductivity in photoinduced states.
