# $Λ$ - anomaly in the hadron chemical freeze-out

> A new way to overcome the $Λ$ hyperon selective suppression, which is known as the $Λ$-anomaly, has been suggested. In particular, the additional radius of a $Λ$ hyperon is introduced into the model of hadron resonance gas with the multicomponent hard-core repulsion. The proposed approach allows one to describe the hadron multiplicity ratios measured at the AGS, SPS and RHIC energies with the accuracy $χ^{2}/dof=$52/55$\simeq $0.95.

## Key claim

Introducing an additional hard-core radius specifically for the Λ hyperon into the multicomponent hadron resonance gas model (HRGM) can overcome the selective suppression of Λ yields (the "Λ-anomaly") and improve the description of hadron multiplicity ratios across a wide energy range, achieving a global fit quality of χ²/dof = 52/55 ≈ 0.95.

## Method

The author extends the multicomponent HRGM with hard-core repulsion by fitting separate hard-core radii for baryons, mesons, pions, kaons, and Λ hyperons. The model uses a Boltzmann gas with excluded-volume interactions, includes the strangeness suppression factor γ_s as a free parameter, and accounts for resonance decays via Breit–Wigner averaging. Global fits are performed to experimental hadron multiplicity ratios measured at midrapidity for 14 collision energies from AGS to RHIC (√s_NN = 2.7 to 200 GeV).

## Key numbers

- Global fit quality: χ²/dof = 52/55 ≈ 0.95 for all hadron multiplicity ratios across 14 collision energies.
- Fitted hard-core radii: R_b = 0.355 fm (baryons), R_m = 0.4 fm (mesons), R_π = 0.1 fm (pions), R_K = 0.38 fm (kaons), R_Λ = 0.11 fm (Λ hyperons).
- Λ/π⁻ ratio description: χ²/dof = 10.22/12 (with free γ_s), improved from χ²/dof = 14.85/12 in Bugaev et al. (2013).
- Ᾱ/π⁻ ratio description: χ²/dof = 6.49/8 (with free γ_s), improved from χ²/dof = 10/8 in Bugaev et al. (2013).
- K⁺/π⁺ ratio (Strangeness Horn) description: χ²/dof = 3.92/14 (with free γ_s).
- For γ_s fixed to 1: χ²/dof = 75.49/69 ≈ 1.09, compared to χ²/dof = 80.5/69 ≈ 1.16 in Bugaev et al. (2013) with four hard-core radii.

## Context

This work extends the multicomponent HRGM developed by Bugaev et al. (2013) and Bugaev et al. (2013) by adding a dedicated hard-core radius for Λ hyperons, directly addressing the long-standing Λ-anomaly noted in earlier studies (Andronic et al., 2005; Bugaev et al., 2013). The improvement in describing Λ/π⁻ and Ᾱ/π⁻ ratios, combined with the finding that γ_s > 1 at low energies (confirming strangeness enhancement rather than suppression), supports the hypothesis of separate chemical freeze-outs for strange and non-strange hadrons proposed by Bugaev et al. (2013). The local minimum of γ_s near √s_NN = 4.3–4.9 GeV suggests a possible signal of the deconfinement phase transition, motivating a detailed experimental scan in that energy range.
