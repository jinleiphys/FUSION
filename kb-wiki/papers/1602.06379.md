# First-forbidden transitions and stellar $β$-decay rates of Zn and Ge isotopes

> First-forbidden (FF) charge-changing transitions become relatively important for nuclei as their proton number increases. This is because the strength of allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions decreases with increasing Z. The FF transitions play an important role in reducing the half-lives as against those calculated from taking the GT transitions alone into account. In this paper we calculate allowed GT as well as $0^{+} \rightarrow 0^{-}$ and $0^{+} \rightarrow 2^{-}$ transitions for neutron-rich Zn and Ge isotopes. Two different pn-QRPA models were used with a schematic separable interaction to calculate GT and FF transitions. Half-lives calculated after inclusion of FF transitions were in excellent agreement with the experimental data. Our calculations were also compared to previous QRPA calculations and were found to be in better agreement with measured data. Stellar $β$-decay rates were calculated for these nuclei including allowed GT and unique FF transitions for astrophysical applications. $^{86,88}$Ge has a sizeable contribution to the total stellar rate from unique FF transitions.

# First-forbidden transitions and stellar β-decay rates of Zn and Ge isotopes

## Key claim
First-forbidden (FF) charge-changing transitions significantly reduce the calculated β-decay half-lives of neutron-rich Zn and Ge isotopes compared to calculations considering only allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions, with the effect being particularly pronounced for heavier isotopes. The authors show that for 86,88Ge roughly half the contribution to the total decay rate comes from unique first-forbidden (U1F) transitions, but this result depends on using a quenching factor of 0.6 for both allowed and forbidden transitions in the pn-QRPA models.

## Method
Two different proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) models were employed: a spherical model using a Woods-Saxon potential basis (pn-QRPA(WS)) and a deformed model using a Nilsson basis (pn-QRPA(N)). The pn-QRPA(WS) model calculated allowed GT transitions via the Pyatov method and both rank-0 (0+→0-) and rank-2 (0+→2-) first-forbidden transitions within the ξ approximation, while the pn-QRPA(N) model calculated allowed GT and U1F transitions using a schematic separable interaction in both particle-hole and particle-particle channels. Stellar β-decay rates were computed over a temperature range of T9 = 0.01 to 30 and densities from 10 to 1011 g/cm3, with experimental Unevaluated Nuclear Data List (XUNDL) levels incorporated where available.

## Key numbers
- Quenching factor of 0.6 was applied to both pn-QRPA(WS) and pn-QRPA(N) models for all charge-changing transitions, consistent with shell model calculations.
- For 76Zn at low and intermediate densities, allowed β-decay rates are one order of magnitude bigger at low temperatures and around two orders of magnitude bigger at T9 = 30 compared to U1F rates.
- For 84Ge, U1F β-decay rates are around a factor six smaller at low temperatures and densities; at high temperatures allowed rates are a factor 50 bigger.
- The DF3+CQRPA calculation of Borunov (2005) gave half-lives roughly a factor two bigger than experimental data for Zn and Ge isotopes.
- The allowed GT calculation by Moller (1997) was up to a factor 18 bigger than experimental data but agreed for 82Zn and 88Ge.
- For 86,88Ge, roughly half the contribution to the total decay rate comes from U1F transitions.
- Phase space factors for U1F transitions get amplified with increasing neutron number for a given element.
- The interaction constant χ was taken as 4.2/A MeV for allowed transitions and 56.16/A MeV fm-2 for U1F transitions.

## Context
This work extends previous pn-QRPA calculations of stellar weak rates by Nabi and Klapdor (1999, 2004) and specifically follows the study of Nabi (2013) on unique first-forbidden β-decay rates in Ni isotopes, where the authors expressed interest in including rank-0 contributions. The calculations are compared to the self-consistent density functional plus continuum QRPA (DF3+CQRPA) approach of Borunov (2005) and the folded-Yukawa QRPA calculations of Moller (1997), which did not include particle-particle interaction or FF decays. The results enable more accurate stellar β-decay rates for r-process nucleosynthesis simulations, potentially shifting the third peak of the r-process abundance distribution toward higher mass region as suggested by Suzuki (2011, 2012).
