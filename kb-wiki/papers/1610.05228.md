# Single-Transverse-Spin-Asymmetry studies with a fixed-target experiment using the LHC beams (AFTER@LHC)

> We discuss the potential of AFTER@LHC to measure single-transverse-spin asymmetries in open-charm and bottomonium production. With a HERMES-like hydrogen polarised target, such measurements over a year can reach precisions close to the per cent level. This is particularly remarkable since these analyses can probably not be carried out anywhere else

## Key claim

AFTER@LHC can measure single-transverse-spin asymmetries (STSAs) in open-charm and bottomonium production with a HERMES-like hydrogen polarised target, reaching precisions close to the per cent level over a year, but only if the assumed effective polarisation of 0.6 and the projected luminosities are achieved; the authors note that these analyses can probably not be carried out anywhere else.

## Method

The authors evaluate the feasibility of STSA measurements at AFTER@LHC by combining the LHC proton beam with an internal polarised gas target (inspired by the HERMES experiment) and a detector like LHCb or ALICE. They compute expected luminosities from the product of the LHC proton current and the areal density of the polarised storage cell target, then project statistical precisions for D0 mesons and Upsilon states using LHCb-like acceptance and background assumptions. For bottomonium, they set the central value of the asymmetry to zero to estimate the statistical uncertainty, as quantitative predictions for these STSAs are lacking.

## Key numbers

- For a hydrogen target at 300 K with a 1 m long cell, the expected areal density is 2.5 x 10^14 cm^-2, yielding a luminosity of 900 μb^-1 s^-1 and an integrated luminosity of 9 fb^-1 per year.
- For a deuterium target, the expected areal density is 3.2 x 10^14 cm^-2, yielding a luminosity of 1200 μb^-1 s^-1 and an integrated luminosity of 12 fb^-1 per year.
- The effective polarisation of H or D can be as high as 0.8, but the authors adopt a conservative working hypothesis of P = 0.6.
- For D0 meson STSAs at AFTER@LHC, the projected statistical precision lies at the per cent level.
- For the least produced Upsilon(3S), the statistical precision on A_N is better than 5% after including a full background simulation.

## Context

This work extends the case for STSA measurements at AFTER@LHC (Brodsky 2012) beyond Drell-Yan and J/psi production, which were previously discussed by the same collaboration (Lansberg 2016). The paper argues that open-charm and bottomonium STSAs provide a direct access to tri-gluon correlation functions in the Collinear Twist-3 formalism (Kang 2008) and to the gluon Sivers effect (Boer 2015), which remain nearly unexplored experimentally. These measurements would complement lepton-induced studies to test the generalised universality of TMD-related observables, and would be uniquely accessible at AFTER@LHC due to the large x^uparrow region where Sivers effects are expected to be largest.
