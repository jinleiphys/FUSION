# Non-dipolar Wilson links for transverse-momentum-dependent wave functions

> We propose a new definition of a transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) wave function with simpler soft subtraction for $k_T$ factorization of hard exclusive processes. The un-subtracted wave function involves two pieces of non-light-like Wilson links oriented in different directions, so that the rapidity singularity appearing in usual $k_T$ factorization is regularized, and the pinched singularity from Wilson-link self-energy corrections is alleviated to a logarithmic one. In particular no soft function is needed, when the two pieces of Wilson links are orthogonal to each other. We show explicitly at one-loop level that the simpler definition with the non-dipolar Wilson links exhibits the same infrared behavior as the one with the dipolar Wilson links and complicated soft subtraction. It is pointed out that both definitions reduce to the naive TMD wave function as the non-light-like Wilson links approach to the light cone. Their equivalence is then extended to all orders by considering the evolution in the Wilson-link rapidity.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new definition of a transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) wave function that requires no soft subtraction factor when the two pieces of non-light-like Wilson links are orthogonal to each other, while still reproducing the same infrared behavior as the complicated definition with dipolar Wilson links and a square-root soft subtraction factor.

## Method

The authors construct a TMD pion wave function where the two Wilson links are rotated away from the light cone into different directions, specifically choosing orthogonal gauge vectors \(n_2\) and \(v\) with \(n_2\cdot v=0\). They compute all one-loop diagrams for this new definition and compare the resulting infrared logarithms with those from the standard Collins definition that includes a square-root soft subtraction factor. The equivalence between the two definitions is extended to all orders by analyzing the evolution equations with respect to the Wilson-link rapidity parameter \(y_2\).

## Key numbers

- The one-loop result for the new TMD wave function reproduces the same \(\ln k_T^2\) term as the Collins definition, specifically the term \(-(\alpha_s C_F / 4\pi) [\ln(k_+/p_+) + 2] \ln k_T^2\) in the convolution with the leading-order hard kernel.
- The pinched singularity from Wilson-link self-energy corrections, which arises from the integrand \([(n\cdot l+i0)(n\cdot l-i0)]^{-1}\) in the dipolar case, is alleviated to a logarithmic singularity from \([(n\cdot l+i0)(n'\cdot l-i0)]^{-1}\) in the non-dipolar case.
- The soft kernel \(K(z; y_1, y_2)\) collects only single logarithms \(\ln(n_2\cdot n_1)\) and \(\ln(n_2\cdot u)\), with the relation \(\ln(n_2\cdot u)=-\ln(n_2\cdot n_1)\) holding for arbitrary finite \(y_2\) in the limit \(y_1=-y_u\to\infty\).

## Context

This work extends the TMD factorization framework for exclusive processes developed by Collins (2011) and Collins (2014), which required a complicated soft subtraction factor involving a square root of a ratio of soft functions. The new definition simplifies the soft subtraction structure while maintaining the same infrared behavior, building on earlier work by Collins and Soper (1982) on rapidity regularization and by Li (2013) on evolution equations. The results enable simpler practical calculations of TMD wave functions for exclusive processes such as the pion transition form factor, and the authors note potential extensions to B-meson light-cone wave functions and polarized TMD parton densities.
