# Chaotic Behavior in Warm Deformed Nuclei Induced by Residual Two-Body Interactions

> Band mixing calculations in rapidly rotating well-deformed nuclei are presented, investigating the properties of energy levels and rotational transitions as a function of excitation energy. Substantial fragmentation of E2 transitions is found for $E_x \gsim$ 800 keV above yrast, which represents the onset of rotational damping. Above $E_x \approx $ 2 MeV, energy levels and E2 strengths display fluctuations typical of quantum chaotic systems, which are determined by the high multipole components of the two-body residual interaction.

## Key claim

Band mixing calculations show that the onset of rotational damping (fragmentation of E2 transitions) occurs at excitation energies above approximately 800 keV above yrast, while fully chaotic fluctuations in both energy levels and E2 strengths, consistent with the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE), appear only above approximately 2 MeV. The abstract's claim of chaotic behavior above 2 MeV is conditioned on the presence of high multipole components in the residual two-body interaction; a pairing plus quadrupole force containing only low multipoles does not produce GOE-level chaos in the same energy region.

## Method

The authors performed band mixing calculations for the rapidly rotating deformed nucleus 168Yb at a rotational frequency of 0.5 MeV (average angular momentum I ≈ 40) using the cranking model. They diagonalized the two-body surface delta interaction (SDI) among the lowest 1000 many-particle many-hole (np-nh) basis states, yielding stable solutions for the lowest 300 levels (up to about 2.3 MeV above yrast). Stretched E2 transition strengths were calculated between levels at the initial rotational frequency and those at a lower frequency (0.47 MeV) corresponding to the rotational transition energy.

## Key numbers

- Onset of rotational damping (branching number > 2) occurs at excitation energy U0 ≈ 800 keV above yrast.
- Number of non-fragmented rotational bands (branching number < 2) at the onset is 36, in reasonable agreement with the 25 undamped bands inferred from fluctuation analysis of experimental Eγ-Eγ spectra (Herskind et al., 1992).
- Chaotic fluctuations (Porter-Thomas distribution for E2 strengths and GOE limit for Δ3 statistics) set in at excitation energy U1 ≈ 2 MeV above yrast.
- Calculations were performed for 168Yb at rotational frequency ħω = 0.5 MeV, corresponding to average angular momentum I ≈ 40, with a moment of inertia J = 67 ħ²/MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier band mixing studies by Aberg (1990), who assumed constant two-body matrix elements with random signs, by using a realistic surface delta interaction with explicit multipole structure. The results provide a microscopic explanation for the rotational damping phenomenon first described by Lauritzen, Dossing, and Broglia (1986), and they support the fluctuation analysis method developed by Herskind et al. (1992) for interpreting Eγ-Eγ spectra from warm rotating nuclei. The finding that high multipole components of the residual interaction are essential for generating chaotic fluctuations opens the way to studying how specific multipolarities of the nuclear force govern the transition from regular to chaotic behavior in deformed nuclei.
