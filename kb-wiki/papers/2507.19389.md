# Pushing the hybrid approach to low beam energies with dynamic initial conditions from hadronic transport

> While hybrid approaches of relativistic hydrodynamics+transport have been well established for the dynamical description of heavy-ion collisions at high beam energies, moving to lower beam energies is challenging. In this work, we propose dynamic initial conditions for the viscous hydrodynamic evolution in heavy-ion collisions at low to intermediate beam energies. They are comprised of core hadrons based on the local energy density during the pre-equilibrium hadronic evolution. The SMASH-vHLLE hybrid approach is then applied to lower beam energies, achieving good agreement with measured bulk observables between $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 3$ and $9.1\ \mathrm{GeV}$, thus providing guidance for measurements in STAR-BES and CBM at FAIR.

## Key claim

The authors propose dynamic initial conditions for viscous hydrodynamic evolution in heavy-ion collisions at low to intermediate beam energies, using a local energy density threshold to determine when hadrons should be converted into fluid, rather than the traditional fixed-time initialization. The approach achieves good agreement with measured bulk observables between $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 3$ and $9.1\ \mathrm{GeV}$, but the authors note that the bulk observables for which data is available do not have enough sensitivity to constrain the threshold energy density parameter further.

## Method

The SMASH hadronic transport code simulates the initial collision dynamics, and a coarse lattice is constructed where local energy densities are computed from hadrons using a covariant smearing kernel. Hadrons are converted into fluid (core) when the local energy density in the Landau rest frame exceeds a threshold $\varepsilon_\mathrm{th}$, with an additional constraint that string fragmentation products must wait a minimum time $t_f$ before fluidization; the core particles are then fed as source terms into the viscous 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamic code vHLLE, which evolves the fluid until a switching energy density $\varepsilon_\\mathrm{sw}=0.334\ \mathrm{GeV/fm}^3$ is reached, after which particlization via the Cooper-Frye procedure feeds hadrons back into SMASH for the late-stage hadronic rescattering.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range studied: $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 3$ to $9.1\ \mathrm{GeV}$ (with additional exploration down to $2.42\ \mathrm{GeV}$)
- Threshold energy density $\varepsilon_\mathrm{th} = 0.5\ \mathrm{GeV/fm}^3$ used for default parameter set
- Switching energy density for particlization: $\varepsilon_\mathrm{sw} = 0.334\ \mathrm{GeV/fm}^3$
- String fluidization time $t_f = 0.25\ \mathrm{fm}$ used for default parameter set
- Smearing kernel width $\sigma$ and $t_f$ tuned by comparing to NA49 data at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 7.73$ and $17.3\ \mathrm{GeV}$
- Lattice update interval $\Delta t = 0.05\ \mathrm{fm}$ with cubic cells of side $\Delta x_i = 0.5\ \mathrm{fm}$
- Net energy loss from core-corona elastic interactions: at most 2\% at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 3\ \mathrm{GeV}$, and 1\% at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 19.6\ \mathrm{GeV}$
- Minimum 15 particles required to sustain a stable hydrodynamic evolution before hypersurface construction

## Context

This work extends the SMASH-vHLLE hybrid approach (Schafer 2021) from fixed-time initial conditions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 4.3\ \mathrm{GeV}$ down to lower energies, using a method similar to the JAM hybrid (Akamatsu 2018) but with a distinct implementation that allows all hadrons as sources, includes finite viscosity, and treats string formation time as a model parameter. The approach provides a dynamical framework for interpreting measurements from the STAR Beam Energy Scan and the future CBM experiment at FAIR, and enables systematic studies of phase transition effects by changing the equation of state in a state-of-the-art evolution framework.
