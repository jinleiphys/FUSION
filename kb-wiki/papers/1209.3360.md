# Neutron Star Properties in the Chiral Quark-Meson Coupling Model

> We study the properties of neutron star using the chiral quark-meson coupling model, in which the quark-quark hyperfine interaction due to the exchanges of gluon and pion based on chiral symmetry is considered. We also examine the effects of hyperons and $Δ$-isobars in a neutron star. Extending the SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry to more general SU(3) flavor symmetry in the vector-meson couplings to baryons, the maximum mass of neutron star can reach the recently observed, massive pulsar mass, $1.97 \pm 0.04 M_{\odot}$. In this calculation, $Λ$ and $Ξ$ are generated in a neutron star, while $Σ$ and $Δ$-isobars do not appear.

## Key claim

By extending the vector-meson couplings from SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry to the more general SU(3) flavor symmetry, the maximum mass of a neutron star can reach the observed massive pulsar mass of 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses, even when using the simpler relativistic Hartree approximation instead of the relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation used in the authors' prior work.

## Method

The authors applied the chiral quark-meson coupling (CQMC) model, which includes quark-quark hyperfine interactions from gluon and pion exchanges based on chiral symmetry, to compute the equation of state for neutron star matter. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation using the CQMC equation of state in relativistic Hartree approximation, with baryon-baryon interactions mediated by sigma, omega, rho, sigma-star, and phi mesons. The vector-meson couplings to hyperons were determined using the SU(3) flavor symmetry parameters from the Nijmegen extended soft-core (ESC) model, specifically taking the ratio z = g8/g1 = 0.1949 for vector mesons and assuming ideal mixing.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass reaches 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses (the mass of PSR J1614-2230) when using SU(3) flavor symmetry for vector-meson couplings in the CQMC model
- The ratio z = g8/g1 for vector mesons is taken as 0.1949 from the Nijmegen ESC model, compared to z = 1/sqrt(6) = 0.4082 in SU(6) symmetry
- The mixing angle for vector mesons is 37.50 degrees, close to the ideal mixing angle of 35.26 degrees
- Lambda and Xi hyperons are generated in the neutron star, while Sigma hyperons and Delta-isobars do not appear

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the CQMC model within relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation (Miyatsu 2011, Katayama 2012) by demonstrating that the same maximum mass can be achieved in the simpler relativistic Hartree approximation when SU(3) flavor symmetry is used for vector-meson couplings. The results address the long-standing "hyperon puzzle" where inclusion of hyperons typically softens the equation of state and reduces the maximum neutron star mass below the observed 1.97 solar mass pulsar. The approach enables future studies of neutron star properties using the CQMC model with SU(3) symmetry, and the authors note that the Fock term from their earlier Hartree-Fock calculations contributes significantly to the nuclear symmetry energy.
