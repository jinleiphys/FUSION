# Deuteron electromagnetic form factors and tensor polarization observables in the framework of the hard-wall AdS/QCD model

> We study the electromagnetic form factors and tensor polarization observables of the deuteron in the framework of the hard-wall AdS/QCD model. We find a profile function for the bulk twist $τ=6$ vector field, which describes the deuteron on the boundary and fix the infrared boundary cut-off of AdS space in accordance with the ground state mass of the deuteron. We obtain the deuteron charge monopole, quadrupole, and magnetic dipole form factors and tensor polarization observables from the bulk Lagrangians for the deuteron and photon field interactions. We plot the momentum transfer dependence of the form factors and tensor polarization observables and compare our numerical results with those in the soft-wall model and experimental data.

# Deuteron electromagnetic form factors and tensor polarization observables in the framework of the hard-wall AdS/QCD model

## Key claim

The hard-wall AdS/QCD model can reproduce the general shape and Q-dependence of deuteron electromagnetic form factors and tensor polarization observables, but the numerical values show shifts relative to experimental data and the soft-wall model results, and no holographic model can completely describe all phenomenological quantities.

## Method

The authors construct a hard-wall AdS/QCD model with a bulk twist tau=6 vector field to describe the deuteron on the boundary, fixing the infrared cutoff at z_m = 0.9225 fm from the first zero of the Bessel function J_5 using the deuteron ground state mass M_D = 1.876 GeV. They derive the deuteron profile function by solving the Schrodinger-type equation of motion, then compute the charge monopole, quadrupole, and magnetic dipole form factors from three bulk Lagrangian terms describing minimal gauge interaction, magnetic dipole coupling, and non-minimal coupling. The constants c_2 and c_3 are fixed by normalizing the quadrupole and magnetic dipole form factors at Q^2=0 to experimental values.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron ground state mass: M_D = 1.876 GeV = 9.508 fm^{-1}
- Infrared boundary cutoff: z_m = 4.6756 GeV^{-1} = 0.9225 fm, fixed from the first zero of J_5 (alpha_0 = 8.77)
- Normalization constants: c_2 = 1.67066 and c_3 = 22.507, fixed from G_Q(0) = 25.83 and G_M(0) = 1.714
- Nucleon mass used: m_N = 4.764 fm^{-1}
- Experimental data range for comparison: 0.86 fm^{-1} <= Q <= 6.64 fm^{-1}
- Tensor polarization observables evaluated at scattering angle theta = 70 degrees

## Context

This work extends previous holographic QCD studies of deuteron electromagnetic properties from the soft-wall AdS/QCD model (Refs. 19, 20) to the hard-wall model, which provides confinement and chiral symmetry breaking through an explicit IR cutoff rather than a dilaton field. The results are compared with experimental data (Refs. 33, 34) and with soft-wall model calculations. The hard-wall results for T_20 and T_20R are similar to soft-wall results, while T_21 and T_22 differ, highlighting that different holographic models can yield significantly different predictions for the same quantities. This enables further refinement of AdS/QCD approaches to nuclear structure.
