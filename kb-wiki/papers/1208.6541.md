# Nuclear Shadowing in Electro-Weak Interactions

> Shadowing is a quantum phenomenon leading to a non-additivity of electroweak cross sections on nucleons bound in a nucleus. It occurs due to destructive interference of amplitudes on different nucleons. Although the current experimental evidence for shadowing is dominated by charged-lepton nucleus scattering, studies of neutrino nucleus scattering have recently begun and revealed unexpected results.

## Key claim

Nuclear shadowing in neutrino-nucleus interactions is predicted to occur via a mechanism analogous to vector meson dominance for photons, but mediated by diffractive pion production rather than direct pion fluctuations of the axial current, because the pion pole does not contribute to the neutrino interaction amplitude at high energies due to the transversality of the lepton current.

## Method

The authors derive expressions for the nuclear ratio R_A/N in neutrino-nucleus total cross sections using a two-step model: a direct inelastic interaction of the axial current with a bound nucleon interferes with a process where a diffractively produced pion subsequently undergoes an inelastic collision. The formalism incorporates the longitudinal momentum transfer q_L^pi = (m_pi^2 + Q^2)/(2 nu) and the pion-nucleon total cross section sigma^{pi N}_{tot} in an exponential attenuation factor, following the same structure as the vector dominance model for photon shadowing.

## Key numbers

- Effective mass of heavy axial states dominating the neutrino cross section at small Q^2: M_eff ≈ 1.1 GeV, extracted from fitting the measured Q^2-dependence with parametrization (Q^2 + M_eff^2)^{-2}
- Fermi coupling constant: G = 1.166 × 10^{-5} GeV^{-2}
- Pion mass and decay coupling: m_pi and f_pi ≈ 0.93 m_pi
- Longitudinal momentum transfer for elastic neutrino-production of pions: q_L^pi = (m_pi^2 + Q^2)/(2 nu), giving coherence time t_c^pi = 2 nu/(m_pi^2 + Q^2)
- Fluctuation lifetime for neutrino to fluctuate into l + a: t_fluct = 2E/M_eff^2, requiring E ≳ 10 GeV to become comparable with heavy nuclear radii

## Context

This work extends the theory of nuclear shadowing from charged-lepton and photon scattering (reviewed by Bauer (1978)) to neutrino interactions, building on the Adler relation (Adler, 1964) which connects neutrino and pion cross sections at Q^2=0. The authors clarify that PCAC does not imply pion pole dominance (Bell, 1995; Kopeliovich and Marage, 1992) but rather that heavy axial states conspire to imitate the pion pole, leading to a coherence time for shadowing onset that is much longer than the fluctuation lifetime. This enables predictions for neutrino-nucleus experiments at relatively low energies (few hundred MeV) where shadowing may already appear, contrary to expectations based solely on fluctuation lifetimes.
