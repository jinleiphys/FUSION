# Higher Charmonia

> This paper gives results for the spectrum, all allowed E1 radiative partial widths (and some important M1 widths) and all open-charm strong decay amplitudes of all 40 c-cbar states expected up to the mass of the 4S multiplet, just above 4.4 GeV. The spectrum and radiative widths are evaluated using two models, the relativized Godfrey-Isgur model and a nonrelativistic potential model. The electromagnetic transitions are evaluated using Coulomb plus linear plus smeared hyperfine wavefunctions, both in a nonrelativistic potential model and in the Godfrey-Isgur model. The open-flavor strong decay amplitudes are determined assuming harmonic oscillator wavefunctions and the 3P0 decay model. This work is intended to motivate future experimental studies of higher-mass charmonia, and may be useful for the analysis of high-statistics data sets to be accumulated by the BES, CLEO and GSI facilities.

# Higher Charmonia

## Key claim

The paper provides comprehensive predictions for the spectrum, electromagnetic transition rates, and strong decay amplitudes of all 40 expected charmonium states up to 4.4 GeV, but these predictions are model-dependent and show significant variation between the two potential models used, particularly for higher orbital angular momentum states where experimental constraints are absent.

## Method

The authors computed the charmonium spectrum using both a nonrelativistic Schrodinger equation with Coulomb plus linear potential and smeared hyperfine interaction, and the relativized Godfrey-Isgur potential model. Electromagnetic E1 and M1 transition rates were evaluated using wavefunctions from both models, while open-flavor strong decay amplitudes were calculated using the 3P0 decay model with simple harmonic oscillator wavefunctions of width parameter beta = 0.5 GeV and pair production amplitude gamma = 0.4.

## Key numbers

- 40 c-cbar states predicted up to the mass of the 4S multiplet, just above 4.4 GeV
- Nonrelativistic model parameters: alpha_s = 0.5461, b = 0.1425 GeV^2, m_c = 1.4794 GeV, sigma = 1.0946 GeV
- Godfrey-Isgur model parameters: b = 0.18 GeV^2, m_c = 1.628 GeV
- Mean multiplet masses differ by approximately 50 MeV for D-wave and up to approximately 100 MeV for 1G multiplet between the two models
- DD open-charm threshold at 3.73 GeV
- Predicted DD width of psi(3770) as pure 3D1 state: 43 MeV (experimental: 23.6 +/- 2.7 MeV)
- Fitting psi(3770) width requires 3D1-2S mixing angle theta = -17.4 degrees +/- 2.5 degrees
- Predicted total width of psi(4040) as 3S state: 74 MeV (PDG average: 52 +/- 10 MeV)
- Pair production strength gamma = 0.35 gives average error of 29% for known charmonium states above threshold
- SHO wavefunction width parameter beta = 0.5 GeV
- Constituent quark masses: m_u,d = 0.33 GeV, m_s = 0.55 GeV, m_c = 1.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier charmonium decay calculations of LeYaouanc et al. (1970s) by using correct reduced mass coordinates for asymmetric open-charm meson wavefunctions and considering all energetically allowed decay modes including S+P decays and singlet-triplet mixing in the 1+ D mesons. The predictions are intended to guide experimental searches at BES, CLEO, and the planned GSI facility, and complement lattice gauge theory calculations of the charmonium spectrum by Okamoto (2001), Liao (2002), and Hashimoto (2004). The results enable identification of conventional c-cbar states as background for searches for exotic states such as hybrids, which lattice gauge theory predicts near 4.4 GeV.
