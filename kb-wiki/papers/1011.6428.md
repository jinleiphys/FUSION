# Effects of Near-side Two-jet Recombination on Away-side Correlation at LHC

> The creation of partons with transverse momenta around 10 GeV/c in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC is expected to be so copious that the parton density may be high enough for neighboring jet cones to overlap. If the shower partons in adjacent jets can recombine to form trigger particles, the distribution of hadrons on the away side should be distinguishable from that due to the recoil of a single harder parton that can produce a similar trigger particle by fragmentation. The two-particle azimuthal correlation on the away side is generated by simulation of the two trigger types. Moments of the correlation distributions are calculated to exhibit their differences. A measure that can be determined by suitable analysis of the LHC data is suggested. It may reveal the footprint of two-jet recombination.

# Effects of Near-side Two-jet Recombination on Away-side Correlation at LHC

## Key claim

If parton densities at LHC are high enough for shower partons from adjacent jets to recombine into trigger particles, the two-particle azimuthal correlation on the away side should be distinguishable from the case where the trigger arises from fragmentation of a single harder parton; the ratio of off-diagonal to diagonal moments of the pair distribution in azimuth may reveal the presence of two-jet recombination, provided the lower bound of away-side hadron momenta is set high enough (above 5 GeV/c) to suppress medium-response effects.

## Method

The authors simulate two event types: 1-jet fragmentation triggers (where a single hard parton with momentum k > 10 GeV/c fragments into a pion) and 2-jet recombination triggers (where two hard partons with momenta k1 and k2, each generated independently, produce shower partons that recombine across jets to form a pion with p_t > 10 GeV/c). For each accepted trigger event, the recoil parton(s) propagate through the medium via a Markovian multiple-scattering model with Gaussian angular deflections and exponential momentum degradation; upon exiting, each parton produces a jet of hadrons via recombination (TS and SS components), and only pions with p_b > 5 GeV/c on the away side are retained. The two-particle azimuthal correlation distribution D(phi_1, phi_2) is constructed from randomly selected pairs of away-side hadrons, transformed to variables phi_+ and phi_-, and the moments M_n(phi_+) and N_n(phi_-) are computed from Gaussian fits to the distributions.

## Key numbers

- Trigger threshold: p_t > 10 GeV/c for the near-side trigger particle.
- Away-side hadron lower bound: p_b > 5 GeV/c to suppress thermal medium response.
- The momentum degradation factor is exp(-zeta) with zeta in (0, 0.2 xi), where xi = 3 is the maximum dynamical path length used for Pb-Pb collisions at LHC.
- The Gaussian width for angular deflection at each step is sigma(ell) = k_0/k(ell) with k_0 = 9 GeV/c.
- The jet cone width for hadrons around the jet axis is 0.5 rad.
- The thermal temperature parameter for the TS component is T = 0.5 GeV.
- The hard-parton distribution exponent is beta = 5.6; the shower-parton distribution parameter b = 2.5.
- For n=4, the ratio R_4 (off-diagonal to diagonal moment at phi_+ = 0) for 2-jet triggers is more than 6 times larger than for 1-jet triggers.
- The mixing parameter alpha (ratio of 2-jet to 1-jet events) produces a factor larger than 4 increase in R_4 when 2-jet triggers become important.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of two-jet recombination at LHC (Hwa and Yang, 2010), where they found that the p/pi ratio could be as large as 20 and that the nuclear modification factor R_AA could exceed 1. It builds on the Markovian-Parton-Scattering Model (Cao and Hwa, 2009) for parton energy loss and angular deflection. The away-side correlation structure studied here differs from the Mach-cone shock wave mechanism (Heinz and Stoecker, 2005; Casalderrey-Solana et al., 2005) and other models proposed for the double-hump structure observed at RHIC (Abelev et al., 2009; Adare et al., 2008; Afanasiev et al., 2009). The suggested method of measuring the ratio R_n as a function of trigger momentum p_t could be applied to LHC Pb-Pb data to test for the presence of two-jet recombination, and could also be used to quantify the double-hump structure already observed at RHIC.
