# Coupled-cluster renormalization group

> The coupled cluster method (CCM) is one of the most successful and universally applicable techniques in quantum many-body theory. The intrinsic nonlinear and non-perturbative nature of the method is considered to be one of its advantages. We present here a combination of CCM with the Wilsonian renormalization group which leads to a powerful framework for construction of effective Hamiltonian field theories. As a toy example we obtain the two-loop renormalized $ϕ^{4}$ theory.

# Coupled-cluster renormalization group

## Key claim
The authors combine the coupled cluster method (CCM) with the Wilsonian renormalization group to construct effective Hamiltonian field theories, but the demonstration is limited to a perturbative expansion in a toy scalar field theory, not a fully non-perturbative calculation.

## Method
The authors introduce a double non-unitary transformation using extended CCM to decouple high-energy (Q-space) degrees of freedom from low-energy (P-space) degrees of freedom, imposing decoupling conditions that partially diagonalize the Hamiltonian. They expand the cluster operators \(\hat{S}\) and \(\hat{S}'\) in powers of \(\mu/\Lambda\) and apply a SUB(n,m) truncation scheme, then solve the decoupling equations order by order in the coupling constant. The method is applied to \(\phi^4\) theory in equal-time quantization, splitting fields into low-momentum (\(k \leq \mu\)) and high-momentum (\(\mu < k \leq \Lambda\)) modes.

## Key numbers
- The divergent contribution from the \(m=0\) truncation yields a term \(\frac{3g}{4\pi^{2}}(\Lambda^{2}-\mu^{2})\int d^{3}x \phi^{2}(x)\) for the mass renormalization.
- The two-loop wave-function renormalization gives \(Z_{\phi}=1-\frac{3g^{2}}{8\pi^{4}}\ln\left(\frac{\Lambda}{\mu}\right)\).
- The coupling renormalization factor is \(Z_{g}=1+\frac{9g^{2}}{2\pi^{2}}\ln\left(\frac{\Lambda}{\mu}\right)+\frac{g^{3}}{4\pi^{4}}\left(81\left(\ln\left(\frac{\Lambda}{\mu}\right)\right)^{2}-51\ln\left(\frac{\Lambda}{\mu}\right)\right)\).
- The mass renormalization factor is \(Z_{m}=1-\frac{3g}{2\pi^{2}}(\Lambda^{2}-\mu^{2})\).
- The \(m=1\) truncation contributes a divergent term \(-\frac{3g^{2}}{16\pi^{4}}\ln\left(\frac{\Lambda}{\mu}\right)\int d^{3}x \pi^{2}(x)\) to the wave-function renormalization.

## Context
This work extends the coupled cluster method, originally developed in nuclear physics by Coester and Kümmel (1960s), into a renormalization group framework for Hamiltonian field theories. The approach resembles the similarity renormalization group method of Glazek, but uses a double non-unitary transformation to achieve decoupling. The authors claim the method can eliminate irrelevant degrees of freedom beyond just high-momentum modes, and that it avoids the small-energy denominators that plague Bloch-Feshbach perturbation theory. The next step would be to apply the method non-perturbatively to more realistic field theories beyond the \(\phi^4\) toy model.
