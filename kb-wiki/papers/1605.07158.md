# 3-D Glasma initial state for relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We extend the impact parameter dependent Glasma model (IP-Glasma) to three dimensions using explicit small x evolution of the two incoming nuclear gluon distributions. We compute rapidity distributions of produced gluons and the early time energy momentum tensor as a function of space-time rapidity and transverse coordinates. We study rapidity correlations and fluctuations of the initial geometry and multiplicity distributions and compare to existing models for the three dimensional initial state.

## Key claim

The paper extends the impact-parameter-dependent Glasma model (IP-Glasma) to three spatial dimensions by including explicit small-x JIMWLK evolution of both incoming nuclear gluon distributions, thereby providing a first-principles framework for the rapidity-dependent initial state; however, the factorization scheme used is rigorously justified only for single-inclusive observables and for multi-rapidity correlations the rapidity separation must be at most of order alpha_s^{-1}, so the results for unequal-rapidity observables represent a first approximation that may require future corrections.

## Method

Starting from the IP-Glasma parametrization of color charge densities at an initial rapidity scale, the authors numerically solve the JIMWLK Langevin equation for the Wilson lines of both nuclei to evolve them to the rapidities corresponding to a given observation rapidity. They then construct the classical Yang-Mills fields in the forward light-cone at each rapidity slice using the evolved Wilson lines and solve the source-free Yang-Mills equations forward in time to compute the gluon multiplicity and energy-momentum tensor at tau = 0.2 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Gluon multiplicity relative to its value at Y=0 is computed for alpha_s = 0.15, 0.225, and 0.3 with m = 0.4 GeV; the rapidity dependence scales approximately with alpha_s Y.
- The saturation scale growth rate lambda = d ln Q_s^2 / dY is found to be lambda approx 0.28-0.3 for alpha_s = 0.15 and lambda approx 0.6-0.8 for alpha_s = 0.3.
- For m = 0.2 GeV the dN/dY at Y = +/- 2.4 is approximately 15% smaller than for m = 0.4 GeV.
- The Legendre coefficient sqrt(a_{1,1}) of the gluon multiplicity correlator is approximately 0.015 in ATLAS data for central events, lying between the results for alpha_s = 0.15 and alpha_s = 0.225.
- The decorrelation ratio r_n(eta_a, eta_b) for n=2 and n=3 with eta_b = 2.4 is computed for alpha_s = 0.15, 0.225, and 0.3; the experimental data from CMS is closest to the result with alpha_s = 0.225 and m = 0.4 GeV.
- For m = 0.2 GeV and alpha_s = 0.3, the drop of r_3 with rapidity is approximately two times faster than for m = 0.4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the boost-invariant IP-Glasma model (Schenke 2012) to three dimensions by incorporating JIMWLK evolution, which previously provided only two-dimensional initial conditions. It enables first-principles studies of rapidity-dependent observables such as the factorization ratio r_n(eta_1, eta_2) measured by CMS (2015) and Legendre coefficients of two-particle multiplicity correlations, and provides initial conditions for three-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations that can constrain the temperature dependence of eta/s as explored by Denicol (2015). The results are compared to the torque model (Bozek 2015), the 3D Monte Carlo Glauber model (Monnai 2015), and AMPT initial conditions (Pang 2015).
