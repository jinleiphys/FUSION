# Total Hadronic Cross Sections via the Holographic Pomeron Exchange

> The analysis on the hadron-hadron scattering at high energies in the framework of holographic QCD is presented. Combining the Pomeron exchange kernel and the hadron-Pomeron couplings which are obtained from the bottom-up AdS/QCD models, we calculate the total cross sections. Our calculation for the nucleon-nucleon scattering is consistent with the experimental data including the recent ones measured by the TOTEM collaboration at the LHC. Within the framework, one can consider other processes involving other hadrons. As examples to show this feature of the model, the resulting total cross sections of the pion-nucleon and pion-pion scattering are also presented.

# Total Hadronic Cross Sections via the Holographic Pomeron Exchange

## Key claim
The holographic QCD framework, combining the BPST Pomeron exchange kernel with gravitational form factors from bottom-up AdS/QCD models, reproduces nucleon-nucleon total cross sections across a wide energy range including recent TOTEM data, but shows substantial deviation from the COMPETE empirical fit at center-of-mass energies above 10 TeV.

## Method
The author uses the optical theorem to relate total cross sections to the forward scattering amplitude, expressed via the eikonal representation in five-dimensional AdS space with the BPST Pomeron exchange kernel. The density distributions of hadrons are extracted from gravitational form factors calculated in hard-wall AdS/QCD models for the nucleon (Abidin and collaborators, 2009) and the pion (Abidin and collaborators, 2008). Two adjustable parameters, g0 and ρ, control the magnitude and energy dependence of the cross section and are fitted to experimental data in the range 10^2 < √s < 10^5 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The nucleon-nucleon total cross section calculation agrees with experimental data including TOTEM measurements in the range 10^2 < √s < 10^5 GeV.
- The calculation is consistent with the COMPETE empirical fit at √s < 10 TeV, but substantial difference appears at higher energies.
- The predicted ratio σtot^{πN} / σtot^{NN} = 0.63, consistent with the value extracted from the soft Pomeron exchange analysis of Donnachie and Landshoff (1992).
- The predicted ratio σtot^{ππ} / σtot^{NN} = 0.45.
- The relevant hard scale for nucleon-nucleon scattering is the nucleon mass, mN ~ 1 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the holographic Pomeron approach previously applied to deep inelastic scattering at small x (Brower and collaborators, 2010; Watanabe, 2012, 2013, 2015) to hadron-hadron scattering, using the BPST kernel (Brower, Polchinski, Strassler, and Tan, 2006) and gravitational form factors from bottom-up AdS/QCD models (Abidin and collaborators, 2008, 2009). The framework enables predictions for other hadronic processes, such as pion-nucleon and pion-pion scattering, without additional parameters. Further experimental data above 10 TeV are needed to distinguish the holographic prediction from the COMPETE empirical fit.
