# Gamow-Teller strength of $^{12,14,16}$C within deformed quasiparticle random-phase approximation

> We investigate the Gamow-Teller (GT) transition strength distributions in the light carbon isotopes $^{12,14,16}$C within the framework of the deformed quasiparticle random-phase approximation (DQRPA). Nuclear deformation is explicitly incorporated through Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean-field calculations combined with the QRPA formalism. The residual particle-hole $(p-h)$ and particle-particle $(p-p)$ interactions are derived from Brückner $G$-matrix calculations based on the CD-Bonn potential, and their impact on the low-lying GT strengths is systematically examined by varying the corresponding interaction strengths. We find that nuclear deformation, associated with a reduced spin-orbit strength, plays a significant role in interpreting the GT strength distribution of $^{12}$C. In contrast, the calculated GT$^{(-)}$ strength distribution of $^{14}$C in the spherical limit reproduces the essential features of the experimental $(p,n)$ charge-exchange data. The case of $^{16}$C reveals additional high-lying GT strength associated with deformation-induced configuration mixing.

## Key claim

Nuclear deformation, associated with a reduced spin-orbit strength (set to 60% of the original value), plays a significant role in interpreting the Gamow-Teller strength distribution of $^{12}$C, while the GT$^{(-)}$ strength distribution of $^{14}$C in the spherical limit reproduces the essential features of experimental $(p,n)$ charge-exchange data, and $^{16}$C reveals additional high-lying GT strength from deformation-induced configuration mixing.

## Method

The authors performed deformed quasiparticle random-phase approximation (DQRPA) calculations for $^{12,14,16}$C using Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean fields (with SLy4, SkP, and SGII parameter sets) combined with BCS pairing and QRPA. The residual particle-hole and particle-particle interactions were derived from Brückner $G$-matrix calculations based on the CD-Bonn potential, with their strengths varied systematically via renormalization factors $g_{\text{ph}}$ and $g_{\text{pp}}$.

## Key numbers

* $^{12}$C: The low-energy GT$^{(-)}$ peak calculated with SGII and standard spin-orbit strength appears at $E_{ex} \approx 2.15$ MeV (K=0) and $E_{ex} \approx 2.58$ MeV (K=1), with B(GT) values of 1.10 and 1.70 respectively, compared to experimental data from Anderson (1996).
* $^{12}$C: With reduced spin-orbit strength (0.6 times the original), the SGII mean field yields an oblate deformation of $\beta_2 = -0.34$, and the BCS-level GT peak appears near $E_{ex} \approx 0$ MeV.
* $^{14}$C: Two GT peaks are calculated at approximately 0 MeV and 2-3 MeV across all three Skyrme parameter sets, with the second peak lying slightly below the experimental position from Anderson (1991).
* $^{16}$C: The SGII interaction gives a deformation parameter $\beta_2 = 0.14$, while SLy4 and SkP yield $\beta_2 = 0.04$ and 0.00 respectively.
* Pairing strength parameters: $V_0 = -295.3$ (SLy4), $-220.2$ (SkP), and $-266.5$ (SGII), with $V_1 = 0.5$ and $\gamma = 1.0$ for all sets, using a pairing energy cut-off of 60 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous DQRPA calculations by Ha et al. (2015a, 2015b, 2016) by incorporating Skyrme-type mean fields instead of Woods-Saxon potentials, and applies the formalism to light carbon isotopes relevant to supernova neutrino studies and the JSNS$^2$ and LSND experiments. The reduction of spin-orbit strength to 60% follows Sagawa (2004), who showed such reduction improves binding energies and shell evolution in light p-shell nuclei. The results enable more reliable modeling of neutrino-induced reactions on carbon targets and highlight the need for future inclusion of $T=0$ pairing in $N \approx Z$ nuclei.
