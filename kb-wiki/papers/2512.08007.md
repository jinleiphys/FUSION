# (3+1)D event-by-event pre-equilibrium dynamics in heavy-ion collisions

> So far a major source of uncertainty in the study of heavy-ion collisions arises from the early time dynamics which includes initial state and pre-equilibrium dynamics. The state-of-the-art framework, KoMPoST, employs non-equilibrium Green's functions to propagate the initial energy-momentum tensor to the hydrodynamic phase, yet currently only treats transverse plane dynamics under boost-invariant conditions. In this work, we extend KoMPoST to include non-boost-invariant responses to initial conditions, essential for accurately capturing the longitudinal structures observed in heavy-ion collisions. Non-boost-invariant fluctuations on top of a homogeneous background are evolved using (3+1)D response functions calculated in kinetic theory. To assess kinetic theory's transition towards hydrodynamic evolution, we systematically compare the out-of-equilibrium shear-stress tensor from KoMPoST-3D with estimates based on Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics. Subsequently, a comprehensive (3+1)D framework, McDIPPER+KoMPoST-3D+CLVisc+SMASH, is utilized to simulate the complete spacetime evolution of heavy-ion collisions. The sensitivity of key observables, including longitudinal structure of anisotropic flow, to variations in the hydrodynamic initialization time is thoroughly investigated.

## Key claim
The authors extend the existing KoMPoST pre-equilibrium framework from (2+1)D boost-invariant dynamics to full (3+1)D, enabling the propagation of non-boost-invariant fluctuations of the energy-momentum tensor from initial conditions to the hydrodynamic phase, but the linearization is performed on a longitudinally boost-invariant and transversely homogeneous background, so the approach is strictly valid only for small perturbations around such a background.

## Method
The Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) is solved for a conformal system of massless bosons, decomposing the distribution function into a boost-invariant homogeneous background and small perturbations. The linearized Boltzmann equation is transformed to Fourier space and solved via the method of moments, yielding (3+1)D non-equilibrium Green's functions that relate initial perturbations of the energy-momentum tensor at an early time to the evolved tensor at a later time. These response functions are then used in a multi-stage framework (McDIPPER+KoMPoST-3D+CLVisc+SMASH) to simulate the complete spacetime evolution of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers
- The response functions are universal functions of the scaled time variable \(\tilde{\omega}=T(\tau) \tau / (4\pi \eta_v/s)\), where \(\eta_v/s\) is the shear-viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio.
- By the time \(\tilde{\omega}\approx 1\), initial perturbations have spread around one unit in rapidity, indicating the system is sufficiently close to equilibrium for hydrodynamics to apply.
- Gaussian smearing kernels with \(\sigma=0.1\) and \(\sigma_\eta=0.1\) are applied to regulate large wavenumber tails in the Fourier transform of the response functions.

## Context
This work extends the KoMPoST framework (Kurkela et al., 2018) from boost-invariant (2+1)D to full (3+1)D pre-equilibrium dynamics, enabling the description of longitudinal structures in heavy-ion collisions that are inaccessible to the original formulation. It builds on earlier developments in kinetic theory response functions (Kamata et al., 2020) and provides a smooth transition from 3D initial conditions (e.g., from Trento-3D or 3D-Glasma) to (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamics, reducing the dependence on the hydrodynamic initialization time. The framework enables systematic studies of longitudinal flow observables and their sensitivity to the pre-equilibrium stage.
