# Isospin and symmetry energy effects on nuclear fragment production in liquid-gas type phase transition region

> We have demonstrated that the isospin of nuclei influences the fragment production during the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. Calculations for Au197, Sn124, La124 and Kr78 at various excitation energies were carried out on the basis of the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM). We analyzed the behavior of the critical exponent tau with the excitation energy and its dependence on the critical temperature. Relative yields of fragments were classified with respect to the mass number of the fragments in the transition region. In this way, we have demonstrated that nuclear multifragmentation exhibits a 'bimodality' behavior. We have also shown that the symmetry energy has a small influence on fragment mass distribution, however, its effect is more pronounced in the isotope distributions of produced fragments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the isospin (neutron-to-proton ratio) of the source nucleus influences fragment production during the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, and that the symmetry energy has a small influence on fragment mass distributions but a more pronounced effect on isotope distributions, with the caveat that these conclusions are drawn from statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) calculations rather than from experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed SMM calculations for four nuclei (Au197, Sn124, La124, Kr78) at excitation energies from 2 to 20 MeV/nucleon, using a freeze-out volume of V=3V0 (density approximately rho0/3). They analyzed the critical exponent tau from power-law fits to fragment mass and charge yields (Y(A) proportional to A^{-tau} and Y(Z) proportional to Z^{-tau_Z}) for fragments in the range 6 <= A <= 40 and 3 <= Z <= 18, and examined the behavior of the largest fragment mass, temperature, and their variances as functions of excitation energy. The symmetry energy coefficient gamma was varied between 8 MeV and 25 MeV to study its effects, and a new evaporation code was developed to account for symmetry energy evolution during secondary deexcitation.

## Key numbers

- The four nuclei studied have neutron-to-proton (N/Z) ratios: Au197 (1.49), Sn124 (1.48), La124 (1.18), Kr78 (1.17).
- The multifragmentation onset is about 5 MeV/nucleon for Au197 and Sn124, and about 4 MeV/nucleon for La124 and Kr78 at standard SMM parameters.
- Minimum values of tau occur at excitation energies of approximately 5.3, 5.2, 4, and 4.2 MeV/nucleon for Au197, Sn124, La124, and Kr78, respectively.
- The critical temperature of infinite nuclear matter used in the surface energy parameterization is Tc = 18 MeV.
- The symmetry energy coefficient gamma was varied between 25 MeV (standard) and 8 MeV for Sn124 and La124.
- The maximum variance of Amax/A0 for all sources occurs at an excitation energy of 4-5 MeV/nucleon.
- The temperature variance exhibits a maximum at about 5-6 MeV for standard SMM parameters.

## Context

This work extends previous SMM studies (Botvina, Bondorf) and experimental analyses (Agostino, Hauger) of nuclear multifragmentation by focusing specifically on isospin and symmetry energy effects. The paper builds on earlier investigations of the phase diagram and critical behavior in finite nuclei (Ogul, Srivastava) and addresses the need for understanding isospin effects relevant to astrophysical applications such as supernova explosions and neutron star formation (Bethe, Botvina). The results enable future experimental extraction of the symmetry energy from isotope distributions and isoscaling analyses (Lozhkin, LeFevre, Shetty), which is important for nuclear astrophysics.
