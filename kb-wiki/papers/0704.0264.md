# Gluon Radiation of an Expanding Color Skyrmion in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The density of states and energy spectrum of the gluon radiation are calculated for the color current of an expanding hydrodynamic skyrmion in the quark gluon plasma with a semiclassical method. Results are compared with those in literatures.

## Key claim

The density of states and energy spectrum of gluon radiation are calculated for the color current of an expanding hydrodynamic skyrmion in the quark-gluon plasma using a semiclassical method, with the caveat that only the lowest (spherically symmetric) mode of the nonabelian current is considered and higher polarization effects are deferred.

## Method

The nonabelian color current of an expanding skyrmion solution in a nonlinear sigma model fluid is derived using the hedgehog ansatz and an equation of state approximating radiation (ε = 3p). The semiclassical gluon radiation amplitude is computed by coupling this classical current to a free quantum gluon field, omitting gluon self-interactions due to a hierarchy between the Yang-Mills coupling and the effective coupling g_eff. The spatial Fourier transform is performed analytically, and the time integral is evaluated numerically for the expanding soliton radius R(t) ≈ R₀(t/τ + 1)^(4/3)θ(t), with the small parameter λ = τ/R₀.

## Key numbers

- The small parameter λ = τ/R₀ is defined, with the condition τ ≪ R₀ for the approximation to be valid.
- Numerical results for the density of states and energy spectrum are presented for λ = 1/15, 2/15, and 1/5.
- The asymptotic behavior of the number density in the infrared (small k) is ∼ 1/√k, in contrast to the 1/k form found in Kharzeev and Rischke (2006).
- The characteristic frequency for medium-induced gluon radiation in jet quenching is ω_C = (1/2) q̂ L², with the quenching parameter q̂ estimated to be 0.04 to 0.16 GeV²/fm, which is significantly larger than the characteristic momentum 1/R₀ in this work.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous discovery of expanding skyrmion solutions in color fluid (Dai 2007) by calculating their gluon radiation pattern. The results are compared with the medium-induced gluon radiation approach of Gyulassy and Wang (1994) and its extension to expanding media by Salgado and Wiedemann (2002), where the medium is modeled as static scattering centers and the radiation by the expanding medium itself is not included. The hydrodynamical approach opens the possibility to address the eccentricity of elliptic flow through nonabelian color currents or gluon radiation patterns.
