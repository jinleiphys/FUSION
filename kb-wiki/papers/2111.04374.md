# The effect of the energy functional on the pasta-phase properties of catalysed neutron stars

> Nuclear pasta, that is an inhomogeneous distribution of nuclear matter characterised by non-spherical clustered structures, is expected to occur in a narrow spatial region at the bottom of the inner crust of neutron stars, but the width of the pasta layer is strongly model dependent. In the framework of a compressible liquid-drop model, we use Bayesian inference to analyse the constraints on the sub-saturation energy functional and surface tension imposed by both ab-initio chiral perturbation theory calculations and experimental measurements of nuclear masses. The posterior models are used to obtain general predictions for the crust-pasta and pasta-core transition with controlled uncertainties. A correlation study allows extracting the most influential parameters for the calculation of the pasta phases. The important role of high-order empirical parameters and the surface tension is underlined.

## Key claim

The paper uses Bayesian inference to show that the width and properties of the nuclear pasta layer in catalysed neutron stars are strongly influenced by high-order empirical parameters of the nuclear energy functional and the surface tension, with the specific condition that applying chiral effective field theory constraints down to 0.02 fm⁻³ (rather than only from 0.1 fm⁻³) significantly reduces the uncertainty on the crust-pasta and pasta-core transition densities.

## Method

The authors employ a compressible liquid-drop model with a meta-modelling approach for the nuclear energy functional, expanded to fourth order around saturation density with 13 bulk parameters, plus five surface and curvature parameters. They generate 10⁸ prior models from flat priors, then apply Bayesian inference using three constraints: (i) a low-density filter from chiral N³LO effective field theory calculations of symmetric and pure neutron matter by Drischler et al. (2016), (ii) a high-density filter requiring stability, causality, positive symmetry energy, and a maximum neutron star mass above 1.97 M⊙, and (iii) a likelihood from fitting nuclear masses from the Atomic Mass Evaluation 2016. The posterior models are used to compute the equilibrium pasta configurations by minimising the thermodynamic potential for each geometry (spheres, rods, slabs, tubes, bubbles) at each baryon density.

## Key numbers

* The saturation density parameter is varied in the prior from 0.15 fm⁻³ to 0.17 fm⁻³.
* The symmetry energy at saturation (E_sym) is varied from 26 MeV to 38 MeV in the prior.
* The slope parameter L_sym is varied from 10 MeV to 80 MeV in the prior.
* The incompressibility K_sat is varied from 190 MeV to 270 MeV in the prior.
* The maximum neutron star mass constraint is M_max > 1.97 M⊙.
* 10⁸ models were generated for the prior; when the low-density EFT filter is applied from 0.02 fm⁻³, 7008 models are retained; when applied from 0.1 fm⁻³, 7714 models are retained from 2×10⁶ generated models.
* The proton fraction of the clustered pasta structure (y_p = Z/A) does not exceed 0.25, and in the high-density pasta regime varies between 0.05 and 0.1.

## Context

This work extends the compressible liquid-drop model of Carreau et al. (2019, 2020) to include non-spherical pasta structures, building on the earlier study by Dinh Thi et al. (2021). It contrasts with previous Bayesian analyses such as Balliet et al. (2020), which found pasta contributes more than 50% of the crust by mass and 15% by thickness, by systematically including the uncertainty on surface and curvature parameters and applying low-density constraints down to 0.02 fm⁻³. The results enable more robust predictions for neutron star crust properties relevant to cooling, transport, and gravitational-wave observations, and highlight the need for improved modelling of the zero-density limit in phenomenological functionals.
