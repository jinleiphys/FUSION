# Point-Form Analysis of Elastic Deuteron Form Factors

> Point-form relativistic quantum mechanics is applied to elastic electron-deuteron scattering. The deuteron is modeled using relativistic interactions that are scattering-equivalent to the nonrelativistic Argonne $v_{18}$ and Reid '93 interactions. A point-form spectator approximation (PFSA) is introduced to define a conserved covariant current in terms of single-nucleon form factors. The PFSA is shown to provide an accurate description of data up to momentum transfers of 0.5 ${\rm GeV}^2$, but falls below the data at higher momentum transfers. Results are sensitive to the nucleon form factor parameterization chosen, particularly to the neutron electric form factor.

# Point-Form Analysis of Elastic Deuteron Form Factors

## Key claim

The point-form spectator approximation (PFSA) provides an accurate description of elastic electron-deuteron scattering data up to momentum transfers of 0.5 GeV², but falls below the data at higher momentum transfers; results are sensitive to the nucleon form factor parameterization chosen, particularly to the neutron electric form factor.

## Method

The authors applied point-form relativistic quantum mechanics to elastic electron-deuteron scattering, constructing a mass operator from relativistic interactions that are scattering-equivalent to the nonrelativistic Argonne v₁₈ and Reid '93 potentials. They introduced a point-form spectator approximation (PFSA) to define a conserved covariant current in terms of single-nucleon form factors, using the Gari-Krümpelmann and Mergell-Meissner-Drechsel parameterizations of nucleon form factors. The deuteron current matrix elements were computed in the Breit frame, with the momentum transfer seen by the struck nucleon being greater than the total deuteron momentum transfer Q².

## Key numbers

- The PFSA provides accurate description of data up to momentum transfers of 0.5 GeV², but falls below data at higher momentum transfers
- For the charge monopole form factor G_E, both Gari-Krümpelmann and Mergell-Meissner-Drechsel parameterizations predict zeroes near 0.8 GeV²; G-K predicts a second zero near 5.5 GeV² while MMD predicts a second zero between 6 and 7 GeV²
- For the magnetic dipole form factor G_M, both parameterizations predict the first zero at 1.6 GeV²
- For the electric quadrupole form factor G_Q, G-K form factors show a zero between 4.5 and 5.0 GeV², while MMD form factors produce a zero approximately one GeV² higher
- The calculated deuteron electric quadrupole moment approaches about 90% of the experimental value, compared to 99% for the magnetic moment calculations
- For A(Q²) between 0.5 and 3 GeV², the PFSA with Gari-Krümpelmann form factors fits data fairly closely, while Mergell-Meissner-Drechsel form factors produce results that fall short by an order of magnitude

## Context

This work extends point-form relativistic quantum mechanics to elastic electron-deuteron scattering, complementing previous direct interaction approaches that used Dirac's instant-form (Schiavilla) or front-form (Chung et al., 1998; Kondratyuk; Terent'ev) dynamics. The PFSA results are compared with covariant approaches (Gross; Van Orden et al.) and nonrelativistic calculations (Carlson and Schiavilla), showing that the impulse approximation in various approaches falls short of data at intermediate momentum transfers. The paper enables future application of the PFSA to more complex hadronic targets, as the method can be generalized to any hadronic system.
