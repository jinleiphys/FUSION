# Traces of Thermalization from Transverse Momentum Fluctuations in Nuclear Collisions

> Scattering of particles produced in Au+Au collisions at RHIC can wrestle the system into a state near local thermal equilibrium. I illustrate how measurements of the centrality dependence of the mean transverse momentum and its fluctuations can exhibit this thermalization.

## Key claim

The similar centrality dependence of mean transverse momentum and its fluctuations in Au+Au collisions at RHIC can be explained by the onset of local thermal equilibrium, provided that the temperature at thermalization is quite high (T ∼ 400 MeV), suggesting partonic contributions.

## Method

The author develops a transport framework using the Boltzmann-Langevin equation in the relaxation-time approximation to describe the evolution of the phase space density toward local equilibrium. The mean transverse momentum and dynamic fluctuations are expressed in terms of a survival probability S that decreases with centrality, with initial values determined from the wounded nucleon model and pp data, and equilibrium values estimated from spatial inhomogeneity correlations parameterized by Gaussian transverse radius and correlation length.

## Key numbers

- ⟨δp_t1δp_t2⟩_pp/⟨p_t⟩_pp² ≈ 0.015 from ISR measurements
- R_pp ∼ 0.45 and R_AA ∼ 0.0037 for central Au+Au from HIJING for rapidity interval Δη = 1.5
- N ≈ 825 charged particles in Δη = 1.5 for central collisions (dN/dη ≈ 550)
- ⟨p_t⟩_o ≈ 350 MeV near that measured in pp collisions, implying T ∼ 400 MeV at thermalization
- F = 0.046 for ξ_t/R_t = 1/6 in the near-equilibrium fluctuation calculation
- α = 4 and γ = 0.15 assumed in central collisions for the survival probability parameterization

## Context

This work extends the study of event-by-event transverse momentum fluctuations measured by STAR (Voloshin) and PHENIX at RHIC, as well as earlier measurements by NA49 and CERES. The author attributes the centrality dependence to thermalization rather than QCD phase transition effects proposed by Mrowczynski, Rajagopal, Shuryak, and Stephanov. The approach enables future tests of whether thermalization is species independent through identified particle measurements, and suggests that rapidity dependence measurements can distinguish this thermalization explanation from alternative models.
