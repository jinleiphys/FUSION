# Generalized Jordan-Wigner Transformations

> We introduce a new spin-fermion mapping, for arbitrary spin $S$ generating the SU(2) group algebra, that constitutes a natural generalization of the Jordan-Wigner transformation for $S=1/2$. The mapping, valid for regular lattices in any spatial dimension $d$, serves to unravel hidden symmetries in one representation that are manifest in the other. We illustrate the power of the transformation by finding exact solutions to lattice models previously unsolved by standard techniques. We also present a proof of the existence of the Haldane gap in $S=$1 bilinear nearest-neighbors Heisenberg spin chains and discuss the relevance of the mapping to models of strongly correlated electrons. Moreover, we present a general spin-anyon mapping for the case $d \leq 2$.

# Generalized Jordan-Wigner Transformations

## Key claim

The paper introduces a spin-fermion mapping valid for arbitrary spin S and any spatial dimension d, generalizing the original Jordan-Wigner transformation which was limited to S=1/2. The mapping is claimed to be exact for regular lattices, but the proof of the Haldane gap in S=1 chains relies on a perturbative argument treating the particle non-conserving term as a linear-order perturbation to the t-Jz model, rather than a rigorous non-perturbative demonstration.

## Method

The authors construct composite fermionic operators from Hubbard operators that form a subalgebra of the double graded algebra Spl(1,2), then define string operators K_j to enforce the correct spin commutation relations. For arbitrary spin S, they present separate transformation formulas for half-odd integer and integer spins, mapping the 2S+1 spin states onto 2S constrained fermion flavors with a hard-core condition (single occupancy per site). The mapping is extended to two-dimensional lattices by replacing the one-dimensional kink operators with angle-dependent string operators, and further generalized to a spin-anyon mapping by introducing a statistical parameter θ.

## Key numbers

- The total number of fermion flavors is N_f = 2S for any spin S
- For S=1, the mapping uses 2 fermion flavors (σ = 1, -1) with η_σ = √((S-σ)(S+σ+1)) giving η_0 = √2
- The S=1 bilinear-biquadratic Hamiltonian H₂(Δ) is studied for -1 ≤ Δ ≤ 1, with quantum critical points at Δ = ±1
- For the S=1 XXZ chain with anisotropy Δ, the perturbative analysis maps onto the S=1/2 XYZ model with couplings J_x = 2+η, J_y = 2-η, J_z = -1, where η > 0 is the perturbation strength

## Context

This work extends the original Jordan-Wigner transformation (Jordan and Wigner, 1928) from S=1/2 to arbitrary spin, building on prior bosonic representations for arbitrary spin such as Holstein-Primakoff, Schwinger, and Dyson-Maleev. The mapping enables exact solutions for S=1 models previously unsolved by standard techniques, including a family of bilinear-biquadratic Hamiltonians that map onto exactly solvable S=1/2 t-Jz models (Batista and Ortiz, 2000). The transformation also establishes a duality between S=1 Heisenberg models and lattice-gauge Chern-Simons theories, with potential applications to high-Tc superconductivity and quantum Hall physics.
