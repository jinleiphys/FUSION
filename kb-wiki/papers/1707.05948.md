# Astrophysical $^{3}{\rm He}(α, γ)^{7}{\rm Be}$ and $^{3}{\rm H}(α,γ)^{7}{\rm Li}$ direct capture reactions in a potential model approach

> The astrophysical $^{3}{\rm He}(α, γ)^{7}{\rm Be}$ and $^{3}{\rm H}(α, γ)^{7}{\rm Li}$ direct capture processes are studied in the framework of the two-body model with the potentials of a simple Gaussian form, which describe correctly the phase-shifts in the s-, p-, d-, and f-waves, as well as the binding energy and the asymptotic normalization constant of the ground $p_{3/2}$ and the first excited $p_{1/2}$ bound states. It is shown that the E1-transition from the initial s-wave to the final p-waves is strongly dominant in both capture reactions. On this basis the s-wave potential parameters are adjusted to reproduce the new data of the LUNA collaboration around 100 keV and the newest data at the Gamov peak estimated with the help of the observed neutrino fluxes from the Sun, $S_{34}$(23$^{+6}_{-5}$ keV)=0.548$\pm$0.054 keV b for the astrophysical S-factor of the capture process $^{3}{\rm He}(α, γ)^{7}{\rm Be}$. The resulting model describes well the astrophysical S-factor in low-energy Big Bang nucleosynthesis region of 180-400 keV, however has a tendency to underestimate the data above 0.5 MeV. Two-body potentials, adjusted on the properties of the $^7$Be nucleus, $^3{\rm He}+α$ elastic scattering data and the astrophysical S-factor of the $^{3}{\rm He}(α, γ)^{7}{\rm Be}$ direct capture reaction, are able to reproduce the properties of the $^7$Li nucleus, the binding energies of the ground 3/2$^-$ and first excited 1/2$^-$ states, and phase shifts of the $^3 {\rm H}+α$ elastic scattering in partial waves. Most importantly, these potential models can successfully describe both absolute value and energy dependence of the existing experimental data for the mirror astrophysical $^{3}{\rm H}(α, γ)^{7}{\rm Li}$ capture reaction without any additional adjustment of the parameters.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a simple two-body potential model with Gaussian form, adjusted to reproduce the astrophysical S-factor of the $^{3}{\rm He}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}$ reaction at low energies (around 100 keV and at the Gamow peak), can successfully describe both the absolute value and energy dependence of the mirror $^{3}{\rm H}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Li}$ capture reaction without any additional parameter adjustment, though the model tends to underestimate the $^{3}{\rm He}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}$ data above 0.5 MeV due to the absence of coupling to inelastic channels like $^{6}{\rm Li}+p$.

## Method

The authors solve the two-body Schrodinger equation with simple Gaussian-form potentials for the $\alpha+^{3}{\rm He}$ and $\alpha+^{3}{\rm H}$ systems, adjusting potential parameters to reproduce experimental phase shifts in the s-, p-, d-, and f-waves, binding energies, and asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) of the $p_{3/2}$ ground and $p_{1/2}$ excited bound states of $^{7}{\rm Be}$ and $^{7}{\rm Li}$. The s-wave potential parameters are then specifically adjusted to reproduce the LUNA collaboration data around 100 keV and the solar-neutrino-derived S-factor at the Gamow peak, while keeping the p-wave parameters fixed to empirical ANC values extracted from scattering data via the DWBA method. The same nuclear potentials (with only the Coulomb part modified for the different charge of $^{3}{\rm H}$) are then applied to the mirror $^{3}{\rm H}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Li}$ reaction without further adjustment.

## Key numbers

- Astrophysical S-factor at the Gamow peak for $^{3}{\rm He}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}$: $S_{34}(23^{+6}_{-5}\ {\rm keV}) = 0.548 \pm 0.054$ keV b, estimated from observed solar neutrino fluxes.
- Binding energies of $^{7}{\rm Be}$ bound states: $E_b(3/2^-) = 1.5866$ MeV and $E_b(1/2^-) = 1.16082$ MeV.
- Binding energies of $^{7}{\rm Li}$ bound states: $E_b(3/2^-) = 2.467$ MeV and $E_b(1/2^-) = 1.990$ MeV.
- ANC values for $^{7}{\rm Be}$ from DWBA analysis (Olimov 2016): $C(3/2^-) = 4.785$ fm$^{-1/2}$ and $C(1/2^-) = 4.243$ fm$^{-1/2}$.
- ANC values for $^{7}{\rm Be}$ from S-factor analysis (Qahraman 2012): $C(3/2^-) = 4.80$ fm$^{-1/2}$ and $C(1/2^-) = 3.94$ fm$^{-1/2}$.
- ANC values from the initial Dubovichenko potential $V_D$: $C(3/2^-) = 4.34$ fm$^{-1/2}$ and $C(1/2^-) = 3.71$ fm$^{-1/2}$.
- The model describes the astrophysical S-factor well in the Big Bang nucleosynthesis region of 180-400 keV, but underestimates data above 0.5 MeV for the $^{3}{\rm He}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}$ reaction.

## Context

This work extends the potential model approach of Dubovichenko (2010) for $\alpha+^{3}{\rm He}$ and $\alpha+^{3}{\rm H}$ systems by adjusting s-wave parameters to reproduce the precise LUNA data (Bemmerer 2006, Confortola 2007) and the solar-neutrino-derived S-factor (Takacs 2015), while also incorporating empirical ANC values from Olimov (2016) and Qahraman (2012). The results are compared with ab-initio calculations from the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) by Dohet-Eraly (2016) and the fermionic molecular dynamics (FMD) model by Neff (2011), showing that the potential model yields an energy dependence closer to NCSMC than to FMD. The work enables improved reaction rate estimates for Big Bang nucleosynthesis and solar neutrino production, and highlights the role of Pauli forbidden states in reducing the overlap integrals that determine the astrophysical S-factor.
