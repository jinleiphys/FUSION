# 7Be breakup on heavy and light targets

> In this paper we present all-order quantum mechanical calculations of 7Be breakup on heavy (208Pb) and light (12C) targets. We examine the issues concerning the extraction of the astrophysical S-factor from the breakup data. We discuss the interplay between Coulomb and nuclear breakup, and the importance of higher-order couplings on the cross section. We show that nuclear and Coulomb contributions are not separable using the standard angular selection criterion as nuclear breakup remains large for small scattering angles, even for the heavy target. However, by selecting an upper limit on the relative energy between the final fragments, the contribution from the nuclear breakup can be significantly reduced such that Coulomb breakup is the main reaction mechanism. We show that the extraction of the asymptotic normalization coefficient may require more careful consideration of the nuclear interior than previously used.

## Key claim

All-order quantum mechanical calculations show that for 7Be breakup on both heavy (208Pb) and light (12C) targets, nuclear and Coulomb contributions are not separable using the standard angular selection criterion, as nuclear breakup remains large even at small scattering angles; however, selecting an upper limit on the relative energy between the final fragments can significantly reduce nuclear breakup such that Coulomb breakup becomes the main reaction mechanism.

## Method

The authors performed fully-quantum mechanical continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) calculations using the coupled-channels code FRESCO for 7Be breakup on 208Pb at 100 MeV/nucleon and on 12C at 25 MeV/nucleon. The α+3He continuum included partial waves for ℓ≤3, each discretized into 10 bins up to 20 MeV (lead target) or 30 MeV (carbon target), with the α+3He potential from Buck et al. (1985) and cluster-target optical potentials fixed by elastic scattering data at the relevant energies.

## Key numbers

- The sum of projectile and target radii is 9.8 fm for the lead target and 5.3 fm for the carbon target; breakup cross section for impact parameters below these radii is 28% of total for lead and 16% for carbon.
- The impact parameter beyond which nuclear effects become negligible is approximately 16 fm for the lead target and 13 fm for the carbon target, corresponding to center-of-mass scattering angles of 2.5° and 1.4° respectively.
- The Buck model produces a root-mean-square charge radius for 7Be of 2.62 fm, compared to the experimentally determined value of 2.52±0.03 fm (Tanihata, 1985).
- The calculated B(E2:3/2⁻→1/2⁻) is 19.0 e²fm⁴, consistent with Mertelmeier (1986).
- The α+3He binding energies are 1.587 MeV for the 2p₃/₂ ground state and 1.158 MeV for the 2p₁/₂ first excited state.
- Sensitivity of breakup cross section to different optical potentials is less than 2% at 0° for both targets.
- Non-relativistic kinematics at 100 MeV/nucleon on the lead target introduces a 3% error in momentum.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear and Coulomb interplay in breakup reactions, particularly for 8B→7Be+p (Nunes, 1998, 1999; Thompson, 2001; Esbensen, 1999) and for 7Li and 6Li resonant breakup (Shyam, 1991; Shotter, 1988a, 1988b, 1989; Hirabayashi, 1992). The calculations were motivated by two planned experiments: one at NSCL measuring 7Be breakup on 208Pb at 100 MeV/nucleon, and another at Texas A&M using a 7Be 25 MeV/nucleon beam on a 12C target. The results enable assessment of whether the Coulomb dissociation method or the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) method can improve the uncertainty on the astrophysical S-factor S₃₄(0) for the ³He(α,γ)⁷Be reaction, which is currently recommended as 0.53±0.05 keV b (Adelberger, 1998).
