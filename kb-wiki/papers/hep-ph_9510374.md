# The Anomalous Decay eta -> pi pi gamma gamma

> We investigate the rare radiative eta decay modes eta -> pi+ pi- gamma gamma and eta -> pi0 pi0 gamma gamma within the framework of chiral lagrangians at o(p^4) and present photon spectra for both processes.

# The Anomalous Decay eta -> pi pi gamma gamma

## Key claim

The authors calculate the rare radiative eta decay modes eta -> pi+ pi- gamma gamma and eta -> pi0 pi0 gamma gamma within chiral perturbation theory at O(p^4), finding that the charged mode has a branching fraction of approximately 10^-4 for a bremsstrahlung cut of about 30 MeV, which should be detectable at new facilities, while the neutral mode is more than two orders of magnitude smaller and likely extremely difficult to detect.

## Method

The invariant amplitude is constructed from the standard O(p^2) chiral Lagrangian and the anomalous Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) Lagrangian at O(p^4), using tree-level diagrams only. For the charged decay mode, three classes of Feynman diagrams contribute: a four-meson interaction with a neutral meson propagator and a one-meson-two-photon vertex (class 1), a three-meson-one-photon interaction with a second photon emitted from a charged pion line (class 2), and a contact three-meson-two-photon interaction (class 3). The neutral decay mode receives contributions only from class 1 diagrams, with eta-eta' mixing included phenomenologically assuming nonet symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The partial width for eta -> pi+ pi- gamma gamma with a bremsstrahlung energy cut of delta m_brems = 195 MeV is smaller than the experimental upper limits of Price and Crawford (1967) by about three orders of magnitude.
- For a bremsstrahlung cut of delta m_brems ~ 30 MeV, the branching fraction is approximately 10^-4.
- The partial width for the neutral mode eta -> pi0 pi0 gamma gamma is more than two orders of magnitude smaller than for the charged mode.
- The diphoton spectrum is presented as a function of z = s_gamma / m_eta^2, where s_gamma is the invariant center-of-mass energy squared of the diphoton system.

## Context

This work extends chiral perturbation theory calculations of anomalous WZW interactions to rare eta decays, building on the framework of Weinberg (1979) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985). The matrix element for eta -> pi+ pi- gamma gamma is closely related to that of gamma gamma -> pi+ pi- pi0 treated by Bos (1994) in ChPT, allowing for a consistency check between the two processes. The predictions are intended to be tested at new facilities such as DAΦNE, ITEP, CELSIUS, and GRAAL, which are expected to produce 10^8 to 10^9 etas per year.
