# Reconstructing the Source in Heavy Ion Collisions from Particle Interferometry

> The preliminary CERN SPS NA49 Pb+Pb 158 GeV/A negative one- and two-particle spectra at mid-rapidity are consistent with a source of temperature 130 MeV, lifetime 9 fm/c, transverse flow 0.35, and a transverse geometric size which is twice as large as the cold Pb nucleus.

## Key claim

The preliminary NA49 Pb+Pb 158 GeV/A data are consistent with a source model where the temperature, transverse flow, lifetime, and transverse size are extracted sequentially from different observables, but these parameters are not obtained from a simultaneous fit and therefore no errors are quoted.

## Method

The authors model the phase-space distribution of pions at freeze-out using a Gaussian parametrization that includes local thermalization at temperature T, a transverse Gaussian width R, average emission time τ₀, emission duration Δτ, longitudinal extension Δη, and a linear transverse flow profile with strength η_f. They include resonance decays from ρ, Δ, K*, Σ*, ω, η, η', K_S^0, Σ, and Λ with branching ratios above 5%. The model parameters are determined stepwise: the transverse one-particle spectrum gives the blue-shifted temperature T_eff, the M_⊥-slope of the HBT radius R_⊥ disentangles T and η_f, R_⊥ fixes R, R_∥ determines τ₀, and the rapidity distribution width fixes Δη.

## Key numbers

- Temperature at freeze-out: T ≈ 130 MeV
- Lifetime (average emission time): τ₀ ≈ 9 fm/c
- Transverse flow strength: η_f ≈ 0.35
- Transverse Gaussian width: R ≈ 7 fm
- Cold Pb nucleus Gaussian radius: R_Pb(cold) ≈ 3.5 fm, so the source is R ≈ 2 × R_Pb(cold)
- Longitudinal extension parameter: Δη = 1.3
- Emission duration: Δτ = 1.5 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the standard Hanbury-Brown Twiss (HBT) interferometry framework by applying it to preliminary NA49 Pb+Pb data at 158 GeV/A, using a source model that includes resonance decays and transverse flow. The reconstruction strategy enables a systematic extraction of freeze-out parameters and provides a basis for distinguishing between different dynamical scenarios of heavy ion collisions, including tests of opaque source models which are excluded by the data. The analysis also develops an azimuthally sensitive parametrization of HBT radii for collisions with finite impact parameter, building on earlier work by Dronte, Voloshin, and others, and allowing future experiments to probe anisotropic dynamics and elliptic geometry through the parameters α₁ and α₂.
