# Real Time Quarkonium Transport Coefficients in Open Quantum Systems from Euclidean QCD

> Recent open quantum system studies showed that quarkonium time evolution inside the quark-gluon plasma is determined by transport coefficients that are defined in terms of a gauge invariant correlator of two chromoelectric field operators connected by an adjoint Wilson line. We study the Euclidean version of the correlator for quarkonium evolution and discuss the extraction of the transport coefficients from this Euclidean correlator, highlighting its difference from other problems that also require reconstructing a spectral function, such as the calculation of the heavy quark diffusion coefficient. Along the way, we explain why the transport coefficient $γ_{\rm adj}$ differs from $γ_{\rm fund}$ at finite temperature at $\mathcal{O}(g^4)$, in spite of the fact that their corresponding spectral functions differ only by a temperature-independent term at the same order. We then discuss how to evaluate the Euclidean correlator via lattice QCD methods, with a focus on reducing the uncertainty caused by infrared renormalons in determining the renormalization factor nonperturbatively.

## Key claim
The paper establishes that quarkonium transport coefficients (κ_adj and γ_adj) can be extracted from a Euclidean chromoelectric correlator G_adj(τ) calculable via lattice QCD, but crucially the spectral function ρ_adj^{++}(ω) is not odd in ω, unlike the heavy quark diffusion case, which fundamentally changes the extraction formulas and explains why γ_adj differs from γ_fund at O(g⁴) despite the spectral functions differing only by a temperature-independent term at the same order.

## Method
The authors analytically continue the real-time adjoint chromoelectric field correlator [g_adj^{++}]^>(t) to Euclidean time, obtaining G_adj(τ) = [g_adj^{++}]^>(-iτ). They derive the KMS relation for this correlator, showing the spectral function ρ_adj^{++}(ω) contains both ω-odd and ω-even parts. Using perturbative NLO calculations, they compute the difference Δρ(ω) = ρ_adj^{++}(ω) − ρ_fund(ω) and integrate it to obtain Δγ = γ_adj − γ_fund. They then provide a lattice discretization of G_adj(τ) using link variables and discuss renormalization including linear divergences from the Wilson line.

## Key numbers
- Δγ ≡ γ_adj − γ_fund = −(16 ζ(3)/3) T_F C_F N_c α_s² T³ at O(α_s²), where C_F = (N_c² − 1)/(2N_c), T_F = 1/2, N_c = 3
- Δρ(ω) = (g⁴ T_F (N_c² − 1) π²) / (3 (2π)³) |ω|³ at NLO
- ΔG(τ) ≡ G_adj(τ) − G_fund(τ) = (g⁴ T_F (N_c² − 1) / (2π)³) π T⁴ [ζ(4, τT) − ζ(4, 1 − τT)] + O(g⁶), where ζ(s,a) is the Hurwitz zeta function
- The QGP lifetime in heavy ion collisions is roughly 10 fm/c in the laboratory frame

## Context
This work extends the open quantum system framework for quarkonium evolution in the quark-gluon plasma (Brambilla 2017, Yao 2020) by providing a concrete path to nonperturbative lattice QCD determination of the transport coefficients κ_adj and γ_adj. It resolves a puzzle about the difference between adjoint and fundamental representation correlators (Scheihing-Hitschfeld 2022) and contrasts with the heavy quark diffusion coefficient case (Caron-Huot 2009, Burnier 2010). The results enable future lattice QCD calculations of quarkonium transport, which is crucial because recent AdS/CFT calculations in N=4 SYM (Nijs 2023) found the analog quarkonium transport coefficients to be zero, in stark contrast to the heavy quark diffusion coefficient value of √λ π T³ at large coupling.
