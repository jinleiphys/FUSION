# Precritical pair fluctuations and formation of a pseudogap in low-density nuclear matter

> Low-density nuclear matter at finite temperature is considered representing the strong coupling situation of a highly correlated fermion system. One-particle se lf-energies and the density of states in the vicinity of the pairing transition point are presented. Within the Green function approach model calculations are p erformed using the thermodynamic $T$ matrix in ladder approximation. As a main r esult the formation of a pseudogap in the level density near the critical temper ature of the pairing phase transition has been found. It is shown that compared to mean field, the picture of the onset of the pairing transition is essentially changed if correlations are taken into account.

# Precritical pair fluctuations and formation of a pseudogap in low-density nuclear matter

## Key claim

Using a self-consistent T-matrix approach in ladder approximation that includes hole-hole contributions, the authors find that a pseudogap forms in the single-particle level density of low-density symmetric nuclear matter at temperatures above the critical temperature for superfluidity, fundamentally altering the mean-field BCS picture of the pairing transition onset.

## Method

The authors solve a self-consistent set of equations coupling the single-particle self-energy (real part) to the two-particle T-matrix via the Galitskii-Feynman equation, using a separable Yamaguchi nucleon-nucleon potential in the 3S1 channel. The single-particle propagator is treated in a quasi-particle approximation, and the level density is computed from the momentum derivative of the quasi-particle energy. The calculation is performed for symmetric nuclear matter at density rho = rho_0/3 and temperature T = 5 MeV, just above the critical temperature T_c = 3.6 MeV obtained from the Thouless criterion.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for superfluidity in the 3S1 channel: T_c = 3.6 MeV at density rho = rho_0/3
- Nuclear matter saturation density: rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3
- Fermi momentum at rho = rho_0/3: p_F = 0.92 fm^-1
- Temperature used in the calculation: T = 5 MeV (above T_c)
- BCS critical temperature at same density: T_c^BCS = 5.08 MeV
- BCS gap at Fermi momentum: Delta(p_F) = 2.1 MeV
- Yamaguchi potential parameters: beta = 1.4488 fm^-1, lambda = 151.7 MeV fm^3 (1S0 channel) and 216.1 MeV fm^3 (3S1 channel)

## Context

This work extends the study of precritical pair fluctuations from the high-T_c superconductivity literature (Ding et al., 1996; Loeser et al., 1996; Trivedi and Randeria, 1995) to low-density nuclear matter, where the ratio Delta/epsilon_F is comparable to that in high-T_c materials. The self-consistent T-matrix approach goes beyond mean-field BCS theory and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations (Lejeune et al., 1986; Schulze et al., 1995), which either show a gap below T_c or an enhanced level density at the Fermi surface. The finding of a pseudogap above T_c is novel in the nuclear context and opens questions about the competition between particle-particle and particle-hole correlations in finite nuclei and neutron star matter, where previous work by Vonderfecht et al. (1993) had calculated the single-particle potential at zero temperature including the BCS gap.
