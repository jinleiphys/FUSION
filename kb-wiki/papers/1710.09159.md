# Phonon-particle coupling effects in odd-even mass differences of semi-magic nuclei

> A method to evaluate the particle-phonon coupling (PC) corrections to the single-particle energies in semi-magic nuclei, based on a direct solving the Dyson equation with PC corrected mass operator, is used for finding the odd-even mass difference between 18 even Pb isotopes and their odd-proton neighbors. The Fayans energy density functional (EDF) DF3-a is used which gives rather high accuracy of the predictions for these mass differences already on the mean-field level, with the average deviation from the existing experimental data equal to 0.389 MeV. It is only a bit worse than the corresponding value of 0.333 MeV for the Skyrme EDF HFB-17 which belongs to a family of Skyrme EDFs with the highest overall accuracy in describing the nuclear masses. Account for the PC corrections induced by the low-laying phonons $2^+_1$ and $3^-_1$ significantly diminishes the deviation of the theory from the data till 0.218 MeV.

## Key claim

The Fayans energy density functional DF3-a, when augmented with particle-phonon coupling corrections from the low-lying \(2^+_1\) and \(3^-_1\) phonons, reduces the average rms deviation of predicted odd-even mass differences for 18 even Pb isotopes from 0.389 MeV (mean-field level) to 0.218 MeV, surpassing the 0.333 MeV accuracy of the Skyrme EDF HFB-17; however, the method neglects the blocking effect of the odd particle in the QRPA equation for the phonon vertex, which is justified only for strongly collective states with \(n_L \simeq 20-30\) particle-hole contributions.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson equation directly with a mass operator that includes particle-phonon coupling corrections, using the Fayans EDF DF3-a to generate the mean-field basis for even Pb isotopes (A=180-214). The PC corrections are computed in the \(g_L^2\)-approximation for the \(2^+_1\) and \(3^-_1\) phonons, where the non-pole (tadpole) term is approximated by neglecting the in-volume part of the phonon creation amplitude. The odd-even mass differences \(\mu_+\) and \(\mu_-\) are extracted from the solutions of the Dyson equation as the lowest-energy poles of the Green function, corresponding to the ground states of the odd-proton neighbors.

## Key numbers

- Average rms deviation of DF3-a mean-field predictions from experimental odd-even mass differences: \(\langle \delta \mu \rangle_{\rm rms} = 0.389\) MeV (34 data points).
- Average rms deviation for the Skyrme EDF HFB-17: \(\langle \delta \mu \rangle_{\rm rms} = 0.333\) MeV.
- Average rms deviation after including PC corrections from \(2^+_1\) and \(3^-_1\) phonons: \(\langle \delta \mu \rangle_{\rm rms} = 0.218\) MeV.
- For \(^{198}\)Pb, the predicted \(\mu_+ = -2.212\) MeV and \(\mu_- = -4.716\) MeV.
- Phonon excitation energies for \(^{198}\)Pb: \(\omega_2 = 0.762\) MeV (experiment 1.064 MeV), \(\omega_3 = 2.506\) MeV (no experimental value listed).
- Phonon amplitude coefficients for \(^{198}\)Pb: \(\alpha_2 = 0.35\) fm, \(\alpha_3 = 0.31\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the method developed by Saperstein et al. (2015) for solving the Dyson equation with PC corrections in semi-magic nuclei, applying it to the chain of even Pb isotopes where experimental odd-even mass differences are available. It contrasts with the Skyrme EDF HFB-17 of Goriely et al. (2009), which achieves high mass accuracy without explicit PC corrections, and with the earlier Fayans EDF DF3 of Fayans (1998). The results enable systematic inclusion of PC effects in nuclear mass predictions for semi-magic nuclei and suggest that the Fayans EDF with PC corrections can achieve accuracy competitive with the best Skyrme functionals.
