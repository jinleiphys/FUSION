# Temperature dependence of nuclear matter generalized isovector symmetry energy with Skyrme-type interactions

> The temperature dependence of the nuclear matter isovector symmetry energy coefficient ($\cA_{0,1}$) is investigated in the framework of the generalized nuclear polarizability with Skyrme interactions, as worked out in Refs. \cite{npa,prc}. The variation of $\cA_{0,1}(T)$ is very small (of the order of 1 MeV) for temperatures (T) in the range of 0 and 18 MeV. Different behaviors with temperature are found strongly depending on the Skyrme parameterization, in particular at densities lower than the saturation density $ρ_0$.

# Temperature dependence of nuclear matter generalized isovector symmetry energy with Skyrme-type interactions

## Key claim

The bulk isovector symmetry energy coefficient shows a very small variation (of order 1 MeV) over a temperature range of 0 to 18 MeV, but the sign and monotonicity of this variation depend strongly on the Skyrme parameterization and on the nuclear matter density, with non-monotonic behavior appearing at densities below saturation density for some forces.

## Method

The authors compute the generalized isovector polarizability at zero momentum and energy exchange using Skyrme-type effective interactions, with the temperature dependence entering through the finite-temperature fermion occupation numbers in the integrals for the density of states, kinetic energy density, and related functions. They evaluate the isovector symmetry energy coefficient for four Skyrme parameterizations (SGII, SLyb, SkSC4, SkSC6) at several total densities (0.5, 0.75, 1.0, and 1.33 times saturation density) and for different neutron-proton density asymmetries.

## Key numbers

- The total variation of the isovector symmetry energy coefficient over the temperature range 0 to 18 MeV is approximately 0.5 to 1.0 MeV at saturation density, depending on the Skyrme interaction.
- For the SGII force at saturation density with zero asymmetry, the variation is nearly zero.
- At a density of 0.75 times saturation density with asymmetry parameter b=0.25, the SGII force shows a non-monotonic behavior: the coefficient decreases for low temperatures and then increases above approximately 3 to 5 MeV.
- At a density of 0.5 times saturation density, the SLyb force shows non-monotonic behavior for both b=0.25 and b=0.5, while other forces show monotonic increase.
- The temperature range investigated extends from 0 to approximately 18 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the generalized nuclear polarizability framework developed by the same author in earlier papers (Braghin, 2004; Braghin, 2008) to include temperature dependence. It addresses the question of whether the strong decrease of the symmetry energy observed in multifragmentation experiments should be attributed to temperature dependence or to density changes and surface effects, as argued by Souza and collaborators and by Samaddar and collaborators. The results contradict earlier claims of a strong temperature dependence by Botvina and collaborators, and are consistent with the weak temperature dependence found by Moustakidis (2007), Dean (1995), and Xu (2006), though the sign of the variation differs from some of those works. The work enables better interpretation of multifragmentation data by clarifying that the symmetry free energy (including entropic contributions) rather than the symmetry energy itself is the relevant quantity for those experiments.
