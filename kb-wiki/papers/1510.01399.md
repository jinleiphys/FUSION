# The Wigner-Eckart Theorem for Reducible Symmetric Cartesian Tensor Operators

> We explicitly establish a unitary correspondence between spherical irreducible tensor operators and cartesian tensor operators of any rank. That unitary relation is implemented by means of a basis of integer-spin wave functions that constitute simultaneously a basis of the spaces of cartesian and spherical irreducible tensors. As a consequence, we extend the Wigner--Eckart theorem to cartesian irreducible tensor operators of any rank, and to totally symmetric reducible ones. We also discuss the tensorial structure of several standard spherical irreducible tensors such as ordinary, bipolar and tensor spherical harmonics, spin-polarization operators and multipole operators. As an application, we obtain an explicit expression for the derivatives of any order of spherical harmonics in terms of tensor spherical harmonics.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a unitary correspondence between spherical irreducible tensor operators and Cartesian irreducible tensor operators of any rank, using a basis of integer-spin wave functions that simultaneously serve as a basis for both Cartesian and spherical irreducible tensors. This allows the extension of the Wigner-Eckart theorem to Cartesian irreducible tensor operators of any rank and to totally symmetric reducible tensor operators, a result the author notes has not been considered before in the literature.

## Method

The author constructs an orthonormal basis of spin wave functions for any integer spin s, defined recursively via Clebsch-Gordan coefficients coupling spin-(s-1) and spin-1 states, which are simultaneously eigenfunctions of the squared spin operator and the z-component of spin in the space of rank-s Cartesian tensors. Using this basis, the author proves two lemmas establishing a bijective correspondence between spherical irreducible tensor operators and Cartesian irreducible tensor operators, and then derives the Wigner-Eckart theorem for Cartesian irreducible tensor operators by applying the standard spherical-tensor theorem through this unitary mapping. The extension to totally symmetric reducible tensor operators is obtained by decomposing such tensors into their irreducible components and applying the theorem to each component.

## Key numbers

* The space of rank-n irreducible Cartesian tensors has dimension 2n+1, matching the number of magnetic substates for spin n.
* The squared spin operator eigenvalue for rank-n irreducible tensors is n(n+1), verified explicitly for n=2 where the eigenvalue is 6 (corresponding to s=2).
* The basis tensors satisfy orthonormality: ε_(n)(m')* · ε_(n)(m) = δ_(m'm), and complex conjugation: ε_(n)(m)* = (-1)^m ε_(n)(-m).
* The explicit formula for the basis tensors is given by equation (14): ε_(n)(m) = sqrt((n+m)!(n-m)!/(2n)!) times a sum over products of spin-1 basis vectors with combinatorial factors.

## Context

This work extends the classic treatment of Zemach (1965) on the relationship between spherical and Cartesian tensors, and complements more recent results cited as Nor82. The paper provides a systematic framework for applying angular-momentum techniques to Cartesian tensor operators, which is relevant for perturbative calculations in molecular, atomic, and nuclear systems, as well as rotationally invariant many-body problems. The results enable the computation of matrix elements of tensor powers of position and momentum operators, and generalize the well-known gradient formula of Bethe (1933) for first derivatives of spherical harmonics to derivatives of any order.
