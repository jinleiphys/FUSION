# Correlation energy of the pairing Hamiltonian

> We study the correlation energy associated with the pair fluctuations in BCS theory. We use a schematic two-level pairing model and discuss the behavior of the correlation energy across shell closures, including the even-odd differences. It is shown that the random phase approximation (RPA) to the ground state energy reproduces the exact solutions quite well both in the normal fluid and in the superfluid phases. In marked contrast, other methods to improve the BCS energies such as Lipkin-Nogami method, only work when the strength of a pairing interaction is large. We thus conclude that the RPA approach is better for a systematic theory of nuclear binding energies.

## Key claim

The random phase approximation (RPA) formula for the correlation energy reproduces exact solutions of the pairing Hamiltonian quite well in both normal-fluid and superfluid phases, while the Lipkin-Nogami method only works when the pairing interaction strength is large and fails near shell closures.

## Method

The authors used a schematic two-level pairing model with state-independent pairing interaction, solving the Hamiltonian exactly using the quasi-spin formalism. They compared the exact ground state energies to those obtained from BCS theory, BCS plus RPA correlation energy (using QRPA in the superfluid phase and pp-RPA in the normal-fluid phase), and the Lipkin-Nogami method, for both symmetric two-level systems and a model of oxygen isotopes.

## Key numbers

- For the symmetric two-level model with pair degeneracy Omega = 8, the critical pairing strength for the phase transition is G_crit = epsilon/(2*Omega - 1)
- For oxygen isotopes modeled with Omega_1 = 3 and Omega_2 = 6, the deviation of BCS from exact solutions is around 2 MeV for even-A systems and around 1.2 MeV for odd-A systems, varying within about 0.5 MeV along the isotopes
- The correlation energy for neutron modes in O isotopes was shown to be of order 2 MeV with relatively strong mass dependence
- For the two-pair system in a single j-shell, the Richardson solution gives ground state energy E_gs = -2*G*(Omega - 1)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of RPA correlation energy for deformations and center-of-mass localization by Hagino and Bertsch (2000) to pairing correlations. It builds on an early study by Bang and Krumlinde (1970) showing RPA reproduces exact correlation energy in schematic models, and a comparison by Kyotoku et al. (1996) finding only RPA gave the exact 1/N coefficient in the condensed phase. The paper demonstrates that RPA is superior to the Lipkin-Nogami method for systematic nuclear binding energy calculations, particularly across shell closures where the Lipkin-Nogami method fails to reproduce the correct mass number dependence.
