# Probing effective nucleon-nucleon interaction at band termination

> Low-energy nuclear structure is not sensitive enough to resolve fine details of nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction. Insensitivity of infrared physics to the details of short-range strong interaction allows for consistent, free of ultraviolet divergences, formulation of local theory at the level of local energy density functional (LEDF) including, on the same footing, both particle-hole as well as particle-particle channels. Major difficulty is related to parameterization of the nuclear LEDF and its density dependence. It is argued that structural simplicity of terminating or isomeric states offers invaluable source of informations that can be used for fine-tuning of the NN interaction in general and the nuclear LEDF parameters in particular. Practical applications of terminating states at the level of LEDF and nuclear shell-model are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the structural simplicity of terminating and high-spin isomeric states provides a uniquely clean probe for fine-tuning effective nucleon-nucleon interaction parameters in local energy density functionals (LEDF) and shell-model interactions, but this sensitivity is conditional on the states being sufficiently simple that pairing, single-particle, and collective degrees of freedom decouple to the largest possible extent.

## Method

The author uses self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations with various Skyrme parameterizations (SLy4, SkO) and density-dependent delta interactions (DDDI) for the pairing channel, comparing three variants: volume-active, surface-active, and mixed pairing. For high-spin isomeric states in N=83 isotones, the deformed independent particle model (DIPM) and diabatic potential energy surface model (DPES) are employed. For terminating states in the A~45 mass region, the energy difference between two maximally-aligned configurations is computed within both SHF and the full fp-shell shell model (SM), with isospin corrections applied via the Bansal-French-Zamick (BFZ) formalism.

## Key numbers

* Neutron pair gaps calculated with D1S Gogny force follow empirical three-point odd-even staggering (OES) data; no low-mass enhancement of the pair gap following the conventional Δ ~ 1/√A estimate is seen in either data or calculations.
* In shape-gap correlation plots for ⁴⁶Ti, surface-active and mixed pairing scenarios are excluded by the consistency requirement between calculated β₂ and Δₙ, while volume-active pairing remains viable.
* For N=83 high-spin isomeric states, the DPES model predicts the C_oo configuration in ¹⁵⁰Ho to lie approximately 150 keV above the configuration involving four aligned h₁₁/₂ protons.
* For terminating states in the A~45 region, a reduction of the monopole interaction strength b by δb = 700 keV (approximately 20% in this mass region) would correct SM values of ΔE by: 525 keV in N=Z nuclei; 0 keV in T=1/2 nuclei ⁴³Sc, ⁴⁵Ti, and ⁴⁷V; -175 keV in T=1 nuclei ⁴²Ca, ⁴⁴Sc, and ⁴⁶Ti; -350 keV in T=3/2 nucleus ⁴⁵Sc; -525 keV in T=2 nucleus ⁴⁴Ca.

## Context

This work extends the program of using terminating states to constrain nuclear EDF parameters, building on prior studies by Afanasjev (1999) and Satula (2005) on superdeformed and terminating states. The analysis of isovector pairing via shape-gap correlations follows the systematic Skyrme-HFB calculations of Dobaczewski (2002) and contrasts with finite-range Gogny pairing results by Hilaire (2002). The investigation of isospin dependence of shell-model matrix elements using the BFZ formula connects to earlier work by Bansal and French (1964) and Zamick (1965), and the suggestion that SM matrix elements may have incorrect isospin dependence follows concerns raised by Richter (1991) and Warburton (1990). The results enable future systematic studies of the hierarchy of deformed-to-spherical phase transitions versus pairing type throughout the periodic table, and further tests of the regularized superfluid local density approximation (SLDA) being developed by Bulgac and Yu (2002).
