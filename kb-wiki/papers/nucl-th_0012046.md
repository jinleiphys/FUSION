# The E2/M1 and C2/M1 ratios and form factors in N -> Delta transitions

> A partial wave analysis of pion photoproduction has been obtained in the framework of fixed-t dispersion relations valid from threshold up to 500 MeV. In the resonance region we have precisely determined the electromagnetic properties of the Δ(1232) resonance, in particular the E2/M1 ratio R_{EM}=(-2.5 +- 0.1) %. For pion electroproduction recent experimental data from Mainz, Bates and JLab for Q^2 up to 4.0 (GeV/c)^2 have been analyzed with two different models, an isobar model (MAID) and a dynamical model. The E2/M1 ratios extracted with these two models show, starting from a small and negative value at the real photon point, a clear tendency to cross zero, and become positive with increasing Q^2. This is a possible indication of a very slow approach toward the pQCD region. The C2/M1 ratio near the photon point is found as R_{SM}(0)=(-6.5 +- 0.5) %. At high Q^2 the absolute value of the ratio is strongly increasing, a further indication that pQCD is not yet reached.

## Key claim

The E2/M1 ratio for the N→Δ transition, extracted from pion electroproduction data using two different models (MAID and a dynamical model), shows a clear tendency to cross zero and become positive with increasing Q², in contrast to previous findings that it stayed negative; this is a possible indication of a very slow approach toward the perturbative QCD region, which is not yet reached even at Q² = 4.0 (GeV/c)².

## Method

A partial wave analysis of pion photoproduction was performed using fixed-t dispersion relations from threshold up to 500 MeV, solving coupled integral equations with unitarity constraints and fitting 9 free parameters to the data. For pion electroproduction, experimental data from Mainz, Bates, and JLab for Q² up to 4.0 (GeV/c)² were analyzed with two models: the isobar model MAID2000 and a dynamical model, both incorporating Breit-Wigner resonance contributions and πN phase shifts from the GWU group. The magnetic transition form factor G_M* and the ratios R_EM and R_SM were extracted by global fits to differential cross section data using the MINUIT code.

## Key numbers

- R_EM at the real photon point (Q²=0): (-2.5 ± 0.1)% from dispersion theory, in agreement with the Mainz group value (-2.5 ± 0.1_stat ± 0.2_syst)% (Beck et al., 2000)
- R_EM at Q²=2.8 (GeV/c)²: (-0.56 ± 0.33)% from MAID, (-1.28 ± 0.32)% from dynamical model
- R_EM at Q²=4.0 (GeV/c)²: (0.09 ± 0.50)% from MAID, (-0.84 ± 0.46)% from dynamical model
- R_SM near the photon point: (-6.5 ± 0.5)%
- R_SM at Q²=2.8 (GeV/c)²: (-9.14 ± 0.54)% from MAID, (-11.65 ± 0.52)% from dynamical model
- R_SM at Q²=4.0 (GeV/c)²: (-13.37 ± 0.95)% from MAID, (-17.70 ± 1.0)% from dynamical model
- G_M* at Q²=2.8 (GeV/c)²: (6.78 ± 0.05)×10⁻² from MAID, (7.00 ± 0.04)×10⁻² from dynamical model
- G_M* at Q²=4.0 (GeV/c)²: (2.86 ± 0.02)×10⁻² from MAID, (3.04 ± 0.02)×10⁻² from dynamical model
- Magnetic N→Δ transition moment: μ_NΔ = 3.46 ± 0.03 nuclear magnetons
- Complex ratio of residues at the pole: R_Δ = -0.035 - 0.046 i
- Pole position: W_R = (1211 - 50 i) MeV

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-theoretical analysis of Hanstein et al. (1998) and provides a new partial wave analysis for pion photoproduction with improved uncertainties. The results contradict the earlier analysis of Frolov et al. (1999), which concluded that R_EM stays negative and becomes more negative with increasing Q²; instead, the present analysis shows R_EM crossing zero, suggesting a slower approach to the pQCD regime than previously thought. The extracted form factors and ratios enable more precise tests of constituent quark models (Isgur, 1982; Buchmann, 1997), relativized quark models (Capstick, 1990, 1992), and chiral bag models (Bernard, 1988), and provide benchmarks for lattice QCD calculations.
