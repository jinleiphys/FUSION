# On Goldstone bosons decoupling from high-lying hadrons

> In this paper, we discuss a decoupling of the Goldstone bosons from highly excited hadrons in relation to the restoration of chiral symmetry in such hadrons. We use a generalised Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with the interaction between quarks in the form of an instantaneous Lorentz-vector confining potential. This model is known to provide spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry in the vacuum via the standard selfenergy loops for valence quarks. For highly excited hadrons, where the typical momentum of valence quarks is large, the loop contributions represent only a small correction to the chiral-invariant classical contributions and asymptotically vanish. Consequently the chiral symmetry violating Lorentz-scalar dynamical mass of quarks vanishes. Then the conservation of the axial vector current in the chiral limit requires, via the Goldberger-Treiman relation, that the valence quarks decouple from the Goldstone boson. As a consequence, the whole hadron decouples from the Goldstone boson as well, which implies that its axial constant also vanishes.

## Key claim

Highly excited hadrons asymptotically decouple from Goldstone bosons because the dynamical Lorentz-scalar quark mass \(A_p\) (which controls the pion-quark coupling via the Goldberger-Treiman relation) vanishes at large quark momenta, and the momentum distribution in highly excited hadrons is shifted to large momenta where the pion wave function \(\sin\phi_p\) is suppressed.

## Method

The authors use a generalized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (GNJL) model with an instantaneous Lorentz-vector confining potential. They solve the mass-gap equation for the chiral angle \(\phi_p\) and the dressed quark propagator in the rainbow approximation (large-\(N_C\) limit). The pion emission amplitude for the process \(h \to h' + \pi\) is computed from triangle diagrams using the Bethe-Salpeter equation for mesonic Salpeter amplitudes, and the pion-quark coupling is related to the scalar part \(A_p\) of the quark self-energy via a microscopic Goldberger-Treiman relation.

## Key numbers

- The chiral condensate is calculated as \(\langle\bar{q}q\rangle = -(0.51 K_0)^3\); the mass parameter is fixed to \(K_0 = 490\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to reproduce the standard value of the chiral condensate.
- For a power-like confining potential \(V_0(|\vec{x}|) = K_0^{\alpha+1} |\vec{x}|^\alpha\) with \(0 \leq \alpha \leq 2\), the chiral angle asymptotically behaves as \(\phi_p \propto 1/p^{4+\alpha}\) as \(p \to \infty\).
- The ratio \(g_\pi(p)/g_\pi(0)\) (pion coupling to a dressed quark of momentum \(p\) relative to its value at zero momentum) is plotted and shows a fast decrease with increasing quark momentum, with the momentum scale set by \(K_0 = 490\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the argument for effective chiral symmetry restoration in highly excited hadrons (Glozman 2000, parity doubling papers) to the specific mechanism of Goldstone boson decoupling. It provides a microscopic demonstration within the GNJL model (Orsay group, 1980s-1990s; Lisbon group) of the qualitative picture previously outlined in Glozman (2003) and Jaffe-Pirjol-Scoccola (2005). The result enables quantitative predictions for the suppression of pion emission widths and axial coupling constants in high-lying hadron spectra, and clarifies that the decoupling is not an input parameter but follows from the momentum dependence of the dynamical quark mass.
