# Model-independent determination of the parity of $Ξ$ hyperons

> Based on reflection symmetry in the reaction plane, it is shown that measuring the transverse spin-transfer coefficient $K_{yy}$ in the $\bar{K}N \to KΞ$ reaction directly determines the parity of the produced cascade hyperon in a model-independent way as $π_Ξ=K_{yy}$, where $π_Ξ=\pm 1$ is the parity. This result based on Bohr's theorem provides a completely general, universal relationship that applies to the entire hyperon spectrum. A similar expression is obtained for the photoreaction $γN \to K K Ξ$ by measuring both the double-polarization observable $K_{yy}$ and the photon-beam asymmetry $Σ$. Regarding the feasibility of such experiments, it is pointed out that the self-analyzing property of the $Ξ$'s can be invoked, thus requiring only a polarized nucleon target.

# Model-independent determination of the parity of $\Xi$ hyperons

## Key claim
Measuring the transverse spin-transfer coefficient $K_{yy}$ in the $\bar{K}N \to K\Xi$ reaction directly yields the parity of the produced $\Xi$ hyperon as $\pi_\Xi = K_{yy}$, a model-independent result that holds for any spin of the $\Xi$ and applies to the entire hyperon spectrum. The claim is conditional on the availability of polarized nucleon targets and sufficient cross-section yields, with the ground state being the most practical candidate due to its self-analyzing weak decays.

## Method
The authors apply Bohr's theorem, which follows from rotation and parity invariance and reflection symmetry in the reaction plane, to derive the spin structure of the reaction amplitude for $\bar{K}N \to K\Xi$. For a spin-1/2 $\Xi$, they explicitly show that the amplitude has distinct forms for positive and negative parity, leading to $K_{yy} = \pi_\Xi$ from the trace relations of the spin-transfer coefficient. For arbitrary spin $j$, they generalize using the relation $\pi_{fi} = (-1)^{M_f - M_i}$, where $M_f$ and $M_i$ are sums of spin projections perpendicular to the reaction plane, yielding the same result. For the photoproduction reaction $\gamma N \to KK\Xi$, they derive $\pi_\Xi = K_{yy}/\Sigma$, involving both the double-polarization observable and the photon-beam asymmetry.

## Key numbers
- The unpolarized cross section for $K^- p \to K^+ \Xi^-$ is of the order of $10\,\mu$b/sr around $\sqrt{s} \sim 2$ GeV.
- With a polarized nucleon target having a typical degree of polarization of $\sim 20\%$, the cross-section yield is expected to be of the order of $1\,\mu$b/sr.
- Only two $\Xi$ hyperons, $\Xi(1318)$ and $\Xi(1530)$, have four-star status in the PDG compilation, compared to about 40 non-strange baryon resonances.

## Context
This work extends the application of Bohr's theorem (Bohr, 1959) to parity determination of cascade hyperons, providing a universal alternative to earlier methods that were limited to resonances above threshold with odd relative orbital angular momentum between decay products (Minn 1977; TDDG 1978; BBBB 1987). The result is directly relevant to upcoming experiments at J-PARC, where $\bar{K}N \to K\Xi$ reactions will be studied, and at JLab, where photoproduction reactions like $\gamma p \to K^+ K^+ \Xi^-$ have already produced the first exclusive data (CLAS, 2007). The method enables model-independent parity assignments for the entire $\Xi$ spectrum and, in principle, for $\Omega$ hyperons as well, addressing a critical gap in baryon spectroscopy where the parity of the $\Xi(1318)$ ground state has never been measured but is only assigned based on quark-model predictions.
