# Kaon dispersion relation and flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Within the framework of a relativistic transport model (ART) for heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies, we examine the effects of kaon dispersion relation on the transverse flow of kaons and their transverse momentum and azimuthal angle distributions. We find that the transverse flow is the most sensitive observable for studying the kaon dispersion relation in dense medium.

# Kaon dispersion relation and flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The transverse flow of kaons in heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies (beam momenta of 4 and 12 GeV/c) is a sensitive probe of the kaon dispersion relation in dense nuclear matter, while the inclusive transverse momentum spectrum and azimuthal angle distribution are not. The effect is strongest around midrapidity, where low-energy kaons experience the largest force from the density gradient.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic transport model (ART) for heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies, which evolves phase-space distribution functions of baryons (N, Δ(1232), N*(1440), N*(1535), Λ, Σ) and mesons (π, ρ, ω, η, K) under hadron-hadron scatterings and optional mean fields. The kaon dispersion relation was implemented using the impulse approximation based on the kaon-nucleon scattering length a_KN ≈ -0.255 fm, giving a kaon potential U(p,ρ_b) = ω(p,ρ_b) - (m_K² + p²)^(1/2). The authors compared results for Au+Au collisions at beam momenta of 4 and 12 GeV/c with impact parameters smaller than 4 fm, both with and without the kaon mean-field potential.

## Key numbers

- Kaon-nucleon scattering length used: a_KN ≈ -0.255 fm (isospin-averaged)
- For Au+Au at P/A = 12 GeV/c, average transverse momentum at projectile rapidity: nucleons ≈ 0.12 GeV/c, kaons ≈ 0.04 GeV/c, pions ≈ 0.01 GeV/c
- Beam momenta studied: 4 GeV/c and 12 GeV/c (AGS energies)
- Impact parameter selection: smaller than 4 fm (typical central collisions)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Li et al. (1995) and Li et al. (1995) that first proposed kaon collective flow as a probe of the kaon dispersion relation at SIS/GSI energies (E/A ≤ 2 GeV) using the RVUU model of Ko (1990). The present paper shows the same sensitivity persists at the higher AGS energies relevant to experiments by the E859, E866, and E877 collaborations at Brookhaven. The finding that flow is sensitive but inclusive spectra are not mirrors the known behavior for nucleons at Bevalac, SIS, and AGS energies. The results motivate experimental kaon flow analysis at AGS and support the long-range plan at GSI for studying kaon in-medium properties.
