# Lambda(1405) N to Y N transition in nuclear medium for non-mesonic absorption of Kbar in nucleus

> Non-mesonic transition of Lambda(1405) N to YN is investigated as one of the essential processes for the non-mesonic absorption of Kbar in nuclei. Using one-meson exchange model in the calculation of the transition, we find that the non-mesonic transition ratio Gamma_{Lambda N} / Gamma_{Sigma N} depends strongly on the ratio of the Lambda(1405) (Lambda^*) couplings to Kbar N and pi Sigma. Especially a larger Lambda^*-Kbar N coupling leads to enhancement of the transition to Lambda N. Using the chiral unitary model for the description of the Lambda^*, we obtain Gamma_{Lambda N} / Gamma_{Sigma^{0} N} is approximately 1.2 which is almost independent of the nucleon density, and find the total non-mesonic decay width of the Lambda^* in uniform nuclear matter to be 22 MeV at the normal density.

## Key claim

The ratio of the non-mesonic decay widths of the Lambda(1405) resonance to Lambda N versus Sigma^0 N final states is approximately 1.2 and almost independent of nucleon density, but this result depends on using the chiral unitary model to fix the Lambda(1405) couplings; the ratio would change strongly if the relative strength of the Lambda(1405) coupling to Kbar N versus pi Sigma were different.

## Method

The authors calculated the Lambda(1405) N to Y N transition rates using a one-meson exchange model, where the transition amplitudes include an s-wave Lambda(1405)-meson-baryon coupling, a meson propagator with short-range correlation, and a p-wave meson-baryon-baryon coupling determined by flavor SU(3) symmetry. They fixed the Lambda(1405) coupling constants from the chiral unitary approach (ChUA) for Kbar N scattering amplitudes at the resonance energy, and summed the transition rates over nucleon states in a free Fermi gas approximation for uniform nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of non-mesonic decay widths Gamma_{Lambda N} / Gamma_{Sigma^0 N} is approximately 1.2, almost independent of nucleon density.
- Total non-mesonic decay width of the Lambda(1405) in uniform nuclear matter is 22 MeV at normal nuclear density (rho_B = 0.17 fm^{-3}).
- The in-vacuum mesonic decay width of the Lambda(1405) to pi Sigma is 40 MeV, with the Lambda(1405) mass taken as 1420 MeV.
- The Lambda(1405) mass used in the coupling-ratio study is 1420 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Lambda(1405) doorway picture for anti-kaon absorption in nuclei, where the Lambda(1405) resonance acts as an intermediate state for the non-mesonic absorption process. The study uses the chiral unitary approach (Kaiser 1995, Oset 1997, Jido 2003) to describe the Lambda(1405) properties, and the one-meson exchange model follows earlier work by Oset (1979) and Jido (2001) for short-range correlations. The results enable calculation of the absorption width of anti-kaons in nuclear medium and provide decay ratios that are insensitive to production details, which is valuable for experimental searches for kaonic nuclei.
