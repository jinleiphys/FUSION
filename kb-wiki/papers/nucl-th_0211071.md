# Three-Body Dynamics in One Dimension

> We discuss the general three-particle quantum scattering problem, for motion restricted to the full line. Specifically, we formulate the three-body problem in one dimension in terms of the (Faddeev-type) integral equation approach. As a first application, we develop a spinless, one-dimensional (1-D) model that mimics three-nucleon dynamics in one dimension. In addition, we investigate the effects of pionic 3NF diagrams.

## Key claim

The paper formulates the three-body quantum scattering problem on the full one-dimensional line using a Faddeev-type (AGS) integral equation approach, producing a 6x6 reduced S-matrix for two-fragment processes. The abstract glosses over the fact that the numerical application is limited to a spinless, one-dimensional model that only mimics three-nucleon dynamics, and the investigation of pionic three-nucleon force (3NF) effects is restricted to bound-state calculations rather than scattering.

## Method

The authors developed a coupled integral equation formalism for three-body transition operators (AGS equations) adapted to one-dimensional systems on the full line, incorporating the two-channel structure of transmission and reflection processes. They solved the homogeneous version of the AGS equation numerically for a spinless one-dimensional model that mimics three-nucleon dynamics, using three different two-body potentials with varying "stiffness" of repulsion that all produce the same two-nucleon binding energy at -2.225 MeV. They then added one-pion exchange (OPE3) and two-pion exchange (TPE3) three-nucleon force correction terms to study their effects on the one-dimensional "triton" binding energy.

## Key numbers

- Three two-body potentials (V5, V10, V20) all produce the same 2N binding at -2.225 MeV
- With only two-nucleon force (2NF), the "triton" binding energies are: V5 = -7.722 MeV, V10 = -7.684 MeV, V20 = -7.668 MeV
- With 2NF + OPE3: V5 = -7.708 MeV, V10 = -7.662 MeV, V20 = -7.642 MeV
- With 2NF + TPE3: V5 = -8.733 MeV, V10 = -8.670 MeV, V20 = -8.631 MeV
- With 2NF + OPE3 + TPE3: V5 = -8.718 MeV, V10 = -8.650 MeV, V20 = -8.608 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev-type integral equation approach (AGS equations from Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas, 1967) to one-dimensional systems, building on prior one-dimensional quantum scattering studies by Blatt and Weisskopf (1985) and Blatt (2001). The numerical application tests pionic 3NF corrections previously developed by Canton (1998) and Canton, Melde, and Svenne (2001) in a simplified one-dimensional model. The formalism enables future investigations of 2+1 fragment scattering regimes and tunneling effects for two-body clusters impinging on external barrier potentials.
