# Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Calculations of the Rotational Band of the Very Heavy $^{254}$No Nucleus

> We report on Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations of the ground-state rotationnal band of the heavy nucleus $^{254}$No recently observed experimentally. The calculated quadrupole deformation is consistent with the experimental value of $β=0.27$ and is almost constant over the whole band. We also reproduce fairly well the excitation spectra and moments of inertia of this isotope up to the maximal experimentally observed state of spin 20. The rather high stability of this nucleus against fission is illustrated by the deformation energy curve providing very high fission barriers at zero spin within the HFB and HFB plus Lipkin-Nogami formalisms. The variation of these barriers with increased angular velocities is also studied.

## Key claim
The paper reports that Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the SkM* Skyrme interaction and a seniority pairing force reproduce the experimental ground-state rotational band of 254No up to spin 20, but the authors note that the results are limited by the treatment of pairing correlations in weak-pairing regions at higher angular velocities, and that the convergence of results from different effective forces (SkM* vs. SLy4 vs. Gogny D1S) means the data do not yet provide a completely stringent benchmark for effective interactions.

## Method
The authors performed fully self-consistent triaxial HFB calculations using a Skyrme-type effective interaction (SkM* parameterization) for the particle-hole channel and a modified seniority pairing force for the particle-particle channel, with particle-number projection approximated via the Lipkin-Nogami (LN) scheme. The calculations used a deformation-dependent truncation scheme equivalent to a 13 major-shell spherical harmonic oscillator basis, and the HFB equations were solved iteratively by first obtaining Hartree-Fock eigenstates and then solving in a truncated configuration space. At finite spins, the solutions broke axial symmetry but remained nearly axially symmetric (gamma angle never exceeding 1 degree), allowing a Fourier-series decomposition of densities.

## Key numbers
- Quadrupole deformation parameter beta = 0.27, identical to the experimental value, with the mass quadrupole moment Q0 varying between 32.9 and 32.7 barns along the rotational band.
- Neutron pairing energy vanishes above angular velocity hbar*Omega = 0.22 MeV; within the same angular velocity range, proton pairing energy is reduced by more than 40 percent.
- First fission barrier height at zero spin is slightly lower in pure HFB than in HFB+LN; at I=12 hbar, the HFB+LN first fission barrier height is increased by 500 keV compared to zero-spin.
- Pairing force matrix elements: g_n = 14.3 MeV (neutrons) and g_p = 15.5 MeV (protons) in pure HFB; g_n = 10.65 MeV and g_p = 14.1 MeV in HFB+LN, with an energy cutoff of 6.2 MeV above the Fermi level.

## Context
This work extends earlier semiclassical calculations using the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approach (Laftchiev et al., 2000) that showed rotating liquid drop models needed spin-dependent refinements to predict fission stability. It also directly compares with HFB+LN calculations by Duguet et al. (using SLy4 interaction) and by Egido and Robledo (using Gogny D1S force), finding better agreement for both kinematic and dynamic moments of inertia than those prior calculations. The results enable further tests of effective interactions for predicting stability of superheavy nuclei, but the authors highlight that improved treatment of pairing correlations (e.g., particle-number-conserving approaches like those of Pillet et al.) is needed for weak-pairing regions at high spins.
