# Effects of core degrees of freedom in the low energy $^7$Be$(p,γ)^8$B reaction

> Quadrupole and octupole couplings to all bound states of $^7$Be are included in describing the capture reaction $^7$Be$(p,γ)^8$B. We verify, contrary to what we had initially stated, that the energy behaviour of the Astrophysical S-factor in the energy range of interest is not significantly sensitive to the core couplings in the g.s. (g.s.) of $^8$B although its overall magnitude is shifted. We find there is some sensitivity to the various deformation models when introducing the nuclear interaction for calculating the scattering states. The various deformation models predict quite different contributions to the quadrupole and magnetic moments but in order to compare with the data the quadrupole and magnetic moment of $^7$Be need to be measured.

# Effects of core degrees of freedom in the low energy $^7$Be$(p,\gamma)^8$B reaction

## Key claim
Contrary to the authors' earlier claim, the energy dependence of the Astrophysical S-factor for $^7$Be$(p,\gamma)^8$B in the low-energy range is not significantly altered by quadrupole or octupole couplings to core excited states of $^7$Be, although the overall magnitude of the S-factor is shifted. The shape of the S-factor energy behavior remains unchanged across all deformation models tested.

## Method
Coupled-channel calculations were performed for the p+$^7$Be system, including quadrupole ($\beta_2$) and octupole ($\beta_3$) deformations of the $^7$Be core, with couplings to all bound states of $^7$Be ($\frac{3}{2}^-$, $\frac{1}{2}^-$, and $\frac{7}{2}^-$). The Woods-Saxon central interaction depth was adjusted to reproduce the correct binding energy, using the same radius and diffuseness parameters as Tombrello, Robertson, and Kim, with the spin-orbit depth taken from Kim. The deformation parameter was taken as $|\beta_2| = 0.5$ for the core, with both prolate and oblate possibilities considered, and octupole deformation $\beta_3 = 0.5$ was also tested.

## Key numbers
- The deformation parameter of $^7$Be is taken as $|\beta_2| = 0.5$, corresponding to a quadrupole moment slightly larger than that of $^7$Li ($Q = -4.06 \pm 0.08$ e fm$^2$).
- The measured quadrupole moment of $^8$B is $|Q| = 6.83 \pm 0.21$ e fm$^2$.
- The measured magnetic moment of $^8$B is $\mu = 1.0355 \pm 0.0003 \mu_B$.
- The interior contribution to the S-factor at $E \simeq 20$ keV is of the order of $4\%$ for $r < 7$ fm.
- The nuclear interaction in the continuum can produce up to $15\%$ effect on the low-energy behavior of $S_{E1}$.
- The S-factor at $E=20$ keV shows a nearly quadratic dependence on $\beta_2$.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by Nunes et al. (1996) that had incorrectly suggested core quadrupole couplings could modify the low-energy behavior of the S-factor; the present paper corrects that result due to a numerical error. The study uses the same radius and diffuseness parameters as Tombrello, Robertson, and Kim, and compares with microscopic calculations by Baye and Csoto (1992) that predicted larger deformation parameters ($\beta_2 = 0.6-0.7$). The results enable improved constraints on the $^7$Be$(p,\gamma)^8$B reaction rate for astrophysical applications, but the authors emphasize that measurements of the quadrupole and magnetic moments of $^7$Be are needed to distinguish between deformation models.
