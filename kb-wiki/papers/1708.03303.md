# Two-photon exchange contribution to elastic $e^-$-proton scattering: Full dispersive treatment of $πN$ states and comparison with data

> We evaluate the two-photon exchange correction to the elastic electron-proton scattering cross section within a dispersive framework. Besides the elastic contribution, we account for all $πN$ intermediate state contributions using the phenomenological MAID fit as an input. We develop a novel method for the analytical continuation of the two-photon exchange amplitudes into the unphysical region and generalize our previous work to the momentum transfer region $ 0.064~\mathrm{GeV}^2 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 1~\mathrm{GeV}^2$. We compare our results with recent OLYMPUS, CLAS and VEPP-3 data as well as with empirical fits and estimates in the forward angular region.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to elastic electron-proton scattering is evaluated using a fully data-driven dispersive framework that accounts for all πN intermediate states via the MAID2007 fit, extending the momentum transfer range to 0.064 GeV² ≲ Q² ≲ 1 GeV², with the caveat that the method requires a novel analytical continuation of the TPE amplitudes into the unphysical region.

## Method

The authors compute the TPE correction to the elastic e⁻-p cross section using fixed-Q² dispersion relations, where the imaginary parts of the TPE amplitudes are obtained from unitarity relations using phenomenological πN electroproduction multipoles from the MAID2007 fit as input. A novel contour deformation method is developed for the analytical continuation of the TPE amplitudes into the unphysical region, tested on the Δ(1232) resonance contribution. The real parts of the TPE amplitudes are then reconstructed via unsubtracted dispersion relations, and the resulting TPE correction δ₂ᵧ is compared with experimental data from OLYMPUS, CLAS, and VEPP-3.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range covered: 0.064 GeV² ≲ Q² ≲ 1 GeV²
- Proton charge radius from e-p scattering: R_E = 0.879(8) fm (Bernauer 2010, 2013)
- Δ(1232) mass: M_Δ = 1.232 GeV; Δ width: Γ_Δ = 0.117 GeV
- Pion mass: m_π ≈ 0.135 GeV
- Magnetic transition FF at Q²=0: G*_M(0) = 3.02
- Proton magnetic moment: μ_p = 2.793; neutron magnetic moment: μ_n = -1.913
- Dipole form factor parameter: Λ = 0.843 GeV
- Boundary between physical and unphysical region at Q² = 0.624 GeV²: ν_ph ≈ 0.402 GeV²
- TPE correction to muonic hydrogen 1S hyperfine splitting: estimated as 213 ppm (Carlson 2011) and 102 ppm (Tomalak 2017); elastic proton structure uncertainty: 48 ppm (Tomalak 2017)

## Context

This work extends the dispersive TPE framework of Tomalak (2016) from low momentum transfer (Q² ≲ 0.064 GeV²) to the range 0.064 GeV² ≲ Q² ≲ 1 GeV², enabling comparison with dedicated TPE experiments (OLYMPUS, CLAS, VEPP-3). It improves upon hadronic model calculations (Blunden 2003, Kondratyuk 2005, 2007) that use off-shell vertex substitutions, and upon earlier dispersive approaches (Borisyuk 2008, 2012, 2013, 2015) that reparametrized experimental input as sums of monopole form factors. The results enable more precise extraction of proton form factors and charge/magnetic radii, and reduce model dependence in TPE corrections relevant to the proton radius puzzle and hyperfine splitting measurements.
