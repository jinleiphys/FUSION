# Stability of complex Langevin dynamics in effective models

> The sign problem at nonzero chemical potential prohibits the use of importance sampling in lattice simulations. Since complex Langevin dynamics does not rely on importance sampling, it provides a potential solution. Recently it was shown that complex Langevin dynamics fails in the disordered phase in the case of the three-dimensional XY model, while it appears to work in the entire phase diagram in the case of the three-dimensional SU(3) spin model. Here we analyse this difference and argue that it is due to the presence of the nontrivial Haar measure in the SU(3) case, which has a stabilizing effect on the complexified dynamics. The freedom to modify and stabilize the complex Langevin process is discussed in some detail.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the difference in performance of complex Langevin dynamics between the three-dimensional XY model (where it fails in the disordered phase) and the three-dimensional SU(3) spin model (where it works across the entire phase diagram) is due to the nontrivial Haar measure in the SU(3) case, which has a stabilizing effect on the complexified dynamics; this stabilization is conditional on the effective couplings not being too large.

## Method

The authors construct effective one-link models for both the U(1) XY model and the SU(3) spin model, then analyze the classical flow diagrams and linear stability of the real manifold under complex fluctuations. They compare the drift contributions from the action alone versus the combined action plus reduced Haar measure, and perform numerical complex Langevin simulations using two-angle, three-angle, and matrix formulations for the SU(3) one-link model, as well as coordinate transformations for stabilization.

## Key numbers

- Critical beta in the SU(3) spin model at vanishing mu: beta_c ~ 0.133
- In the SU(3) one-link model, the real manifold is linearly stable for beta less than approximately 1.75, and unstable for larger beta
- In the three-dimensional SU(3) spin model, the distribution in the imaginary direction drops exponentially as P(phi^I) ~ exp(-b|phi^I|) with b ~ 35-45
- In the SU(2) case with real beta, the real manifold is stable as long as beta/d is less than approximately 5.196
- For the Gaussian model with sigma = -1 + i, the transformation x = u^3 yields correct results for the correlator <x^2> = 1/sigma even for a <= 0

## Context

This work extends the analysis of complex Langevin dynamics previously applied to the three-dimensional XY model (arXiv:1005.3468) and the three-dimensional SU(3) spin model (Aarts 2011). The paper builds on the mathematical foundation for correctness criteria established by the same authors (arXiv:0912.3360, arXiv:1101.3270). The findings enable better understanding of when complex Langevin simulations can be trusted for QCD at nonzero chemical potential, and the stabilization techniques (coordinate transformations, kernels) provide a route to cure incorrect convergence in problematic cases. The effective one-link models also connect to the heavy dense approximation for lattice QCD (Aarts 2008, Fromm 2011, Fromm 2012).
