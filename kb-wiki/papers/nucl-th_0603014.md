# Target mass corrections revisited

> We propose a new implementation of target mass corrections to nucleon structure functions which, unlike existing treatments, has the correct kinematic threshold behavior at finite Q^2 in the x -> 1 limit. We illustrate the differences between the new approach and existing prescriptions by considering specific examples for the F_2 and F_L structure functions, and discuss the broader implications of our results, which call into question the notion of universal parton distribution at finite Q^2.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new implementation of target mass corrections (TMCs) for nucleon structure functions that, unlike existing treatments, produces the correct kinematic threshold behavior (structure functions vanishing at x=1) at finite Q^2 using only twist-two contributions, without appealing to higher twist effects. This calls into question the notion of universal, Q^2-independent parton distributions at finite Q^2.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the F_2 and F_L structure functions and their Nachtmann moments within the operator product expansion framework, using a "threshold dependent" (TD) parton distribution q^TD(ξ) that vanishes at the kinematic limit ξ_0 = ξ(x=1). They compare three prescriptions: standard TMC (sTMC) integrating from 0 to 1, modified TMC (mTMC) integrating from 0 to ξ_0, and their proposed TD prescription using q^TD(ξ) ∝ (ξ_0 - ξ)^3, and compute the ratios of Nachtmann moments to parton distribution moments as functions of Q^2.

## Key numbers

- For the TD prescription (prescription C), the ratio μ_2^n / A_n for n=2 differs from unity by only ~0.7% at Q^2 ~ 0.3 GeV^2
- For the sTMC approach, the deviation of μ_2^n / A_n from unity is between 10% and 20% for Q^2 ≲ 1 GeV^2 for n=4 and n=6 moments
- For the mTMC approach (prescription B), the deviation is of order 5% over the same Q^2 region for n=4 and n=6
- For the TD prescription C, the deviation remains around 1% even at low Q^2 values for n=4 and n=6
- The TD prescription produces Nachtmann moments that agree with the moments A_n of q^TD to within 1% for n=2, 4, and 6 for Q^2 as low as 1 GeV^2 and even lower

## Context

This work extends the original TMC formulation by Georgi and Politzer (1976) and the Nachtmann moment approach (1973), which suffered from the "threshold problem" where the corrected structure function does not vanish at x=1. The authors address the earlier argument by De Rujula et al. (1977) that higher twist effects must resolve the threshold problem, showing instead that a self-consistent twist-two formulation is possible. The results have practical implications for analyzing low-Q^2 structure function data from Jefferson Lab and for neutrino oscillation experiments using neutrino scattering data at relatively low energies.
