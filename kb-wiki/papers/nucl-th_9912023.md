# Ground state correlations and mean-field in $^{16}$O: Part II

> We continue the investigations of the $^{16}$O ground state using the coupled-cluster expansion [$\exp({\bf S})$] method with realistic nuclear interaction. In this stage of the project, we take into account the three nucleon interaction, and examine in some detail the definition of the internal Hamiltonian, thus trying to correct for the center-of-mass motion. We show that this may result in a better separation of the internal and center-of-mass degrees of freedom in the many-body nuclear wave function. The resulting ground state wave function is used to calculate the "theoretical" charge form factor and charge density. Using the "theoretical" charge density, we generate the charge form factor in the DWBA picture, which is then compared with the available experimental data. The longitudinal response function in inclusive electron scattering for $^{16}$O is also computed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including the three-nucleon interaction (Urbana IX) and a center-of-mass Hamiltonian correction in the coupled-cluster expansion for $^{16}$O yields a ground state wave function that produces charge form factors and longitudinal response functions in reasonable agreement with experimental data, though the authors note that the persistent discrepancy at large momentum transfer may indicate a breakdown of the nonrelativistic approach.

## Method

The authors applied the coupled-cluster expansion [exp(S)] method to $^{16}$O using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction, working in a harmonic oscillator basis of 50 hbar-omega (the truncated Hilbert space) and including up to 4-particle-4-hole clusters. They introduced a mean-field Hamiltonian with a maximum overlap condition (S1=0), solved the coupled-cluster equations self-consistently for 2-particle-2-hole correlations while correcting for higher-order correlations via a 1/epsilon truncation scheme, and added a supplemental center-of-mass Hamiltonian to constrain center-of-mass motion. The three-nucleon interaction was treated exactly for first- and second-order contributions to the mean-field and binding energy, while higher-order corrections used a density-dependent reduction to an effective two-body interaction.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled-cluster calculation for $^{16}$O using only the Argonne v18 potential (Heisenberg and Mihaila, 1999) by adding the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction and a center-of-mass Hamiltonian correction. The results are directly comparable to Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations using the same interactions, particularly the GFMC results for A less than or equal to 8 from Pudliner et al. (1997). The calculation enables future work on discrete excited states, neighboring odd-even nuclei, and modeling of (e,e'N) reactions, as well as potential inclusion of relativistic effects in the charge form factor calculation.
