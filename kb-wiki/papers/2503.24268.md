# Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory Description of $^{238}$U(n,f), $^{240,242}$Pu(n,f) and $^{237}$Np(n,f) Reactions

> In nuclei with an odd nucleon number the non-vanishing spin number density is the source of a pseudo-magnetic field, which favors the splitting of the nucleon Cooper pairs. Such an pseudo-magnetic field is generated always in the dynamics of any nucleus, but its effects on Cooper pairs is significantly enhanced in the dynamic evolution of nuclei with an odd number of nucleons. We present for the first time a microscopic study of the induced fission of the odd neutron compound nuclei $^{239}$U, $^{241, 243}$Pu, and the odd proton, odd neutron compound nucleus $^{238}$Np, performed within the time-dependent density functional theory extended to superfluid fermion systems, without any simplifying assumptions and with controlled numerical approximations, and for a very large number of initial conditions. Due to the presence of the unpaired odd nucleon(s), the time-reversal symmetry of the fission compound nucleus is spontaneously broken, an aspect routinely neglected in the most advanced microscopic approaches of the past. The emerging fission fragment properties are quite similar to the properties of fission fragments of neighboring even-even nuclei. The time from saddle-to-scission is often significantly longer in odd-N-odd-Z or odd-A nuclei than for even-even nuclei since systems with unpaired nucleons are easier to excite and the potential energy surfaces of these nuclei have more structure, often resembling a very complicated obstacle course, rather than a more direct evolution of the nuclear shape from the top of the outer fission barrier to the scission configuration. The Pauli blocking approximation, often invoked in the literature, expected to inhibit the fission of nuclei with unpaired nucleons, is surprisingly strongly violated during the fission dynamics.

## Key claim

For the first time, a microscopic study of induced fission of odd-neutron compound nuclei (239U, 241,243Pu) and the odd-proton, odd-neutron compound nucleus 238Np is performed using time-dependent density functional theory extended to superfluid fermion systems, without simplifying assumptions and with controlled numerical approximations. The Pauli blocking approximation, often invoked to inhibit fission of nuclei with unpaired nucleons, is surprisingly strongly violated during the fission dynamics due to the emergence of pseudo-magnetic fields that break time-reversal symmetry.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA) simulations of induced fission for 239U (23 trajectories), 241Pu (43 trajectories), 243Pu (35 trajectories), and 238Np (48 trajectories), using the SeaLL1 energy density functional. Initial states were generated by starting from a "seed" even-even nucleus with identical average N and Z, then flipping various low-energy quasiparticle states with occupation probability n_k ≈ 0.5 ± 0.2 to create odd-nucleon systems, considering various initial quadrupole and octupole deformations (Q20, Q30) outside the outer fission barrier.

## Key numbers

- 149 total trajectories simulated for odd-nucleon nuclei: 23 for 239U, 43 for 241Pu, 35 for 243Pu, and 48 for 238Np
- 40 total trajectories simulated for corresponding even-even "seed" nuclei: 6 for 239U, 13 for 241Pu, 10 for 243Pu, and 11 for 238Np
- Saddle-to-scission times for odd-nucleon systems with low quasiparticle energy (E_QP < 1 MeV) often exceed 10000 fm/c (e.g., 10348 fm/c for one 241Pu symmetric trajectory), compared to ~4440-5151 fm/c for corresponding even-even trajectories
- Total kinetic energy (TKE) distributions for odd-even and odd-odd systems are about twice as broad as those for even-even nuclei
- For 241Pu Run 00 Symmetric, TKE values range from 149.37 MeV (odd, flip 0) to 157.97 MeV (odd, flip 4), compared to 149.07 MeV for the even-even seed

## Context

This work extends the TDSLDA approach previously applied to even-even nuclei (Bulgac 2016, 2019c, 2020) to odd-A and odd-odd compound nuclei, addressing a long-standing theoretical difficulty. It contrasts with the equal filling approximation (EFA) used in recent studies by Schunck (2023) and Pore (2024), which treats odd-nucleon systems as even-even with unbroken time-reversal symmetry, and demonstrates that both the Pauli blocking approximation and EFA are inadequate for describing fission dynamics. The results enable future studies of dissipative effects in fission, beta-decay of fission fragments, and P-even/T-odd asymmetries in angular correlations with polarized neutrons.
