# Neutron-Mirror-Neutron Oscillation and Neutron Star Cooling

> It was pointed out in a recent paper that the observed cooling rate of old, cold neutron stars (NS) can provide an upper limit on the transition rate of neutron to mirror neutron ($n-n'$). This limit is so stringent that it would preclude any discovery of $n \to n'$ oscillation in the current round of terrestrial searches for the process. Motivated by this crucially important conclusion, we critically analyze this suggestion and note an interesting new effect present in nearly exact mirror models for $n \to n'$ oscillation, which significantly affect this bound. The new element is the $β$ decay $n' \to p'+ e' +\barν'_{e}$, which creates a cloud of mirror particles $n'$, $p'$, $e'$ and $D'$ inside the NS core. The $e'$ can "rob" the energy generated by the $n \to n'$ transition via $e-e'$ scattering enabled by the presence of a (minute) milli-charge in mirror particles. This energy is emitted as unobserved mirror photons via fast mirror bremsstrahlung leading to a relaxation of this upper limit.

# Neutron-Mirror-Neutron Oscillation and Neutron Star Cooling

## Key claim

The stringent upper limit on the neutron-to-mirror-neutron ($n \to n'$) transition rate derived from the observed cooling of old, cold neutron stars (which would preclude discovery in current terrestrial searches) is significantly relaxed when one accounts for the $\beta$ decay of mirror neutrons into mirror protons, electrons, and antineutrinos, followed by mirror deuteron formation and energy transfer via electron-mirror-electron scattering enabled by a minute millicharge on mirror particles.

## Method

The authors analyze the evolution of mirror neutrons produced via $n \to n'$ oscillation inside a neutron star, following their $\beta$ decay ($n' \to p' + e' + \bar{\nu}'_e$) and subsequent formation of mirror deuterons ($D'$) via $p' + n' \to D' + \gamma'$. They solve the hydrostatic equation for the resulting $e'$-$D'$ fluid confined by gravity and degenerate pressure, obtaining the radial density profile and core radius. They then compute the rate of energy transfer from ordinary electrons to mirror electrons via millicharge-mediated scattering, and compare the resulting mirror photon luminosity to the ordinary blackbody luminosity of the neutron star surface.

## Key numbers

- For the coldest pulsar PSR J2144--3933, the surface temperature upper bound is $T_s \sim 42000$ K, corresponding to an internal temperature $T_{\rm int} \simeq 0.35$ keV.
- The rate of mirror neutron generation from $n \to n'$ transitions is $d{\cal N}_{n'}/dt \sim 0.45 \times 10^{32} \; {\rm sec}^{-1}$ for PSR J2144--3933.
- Over its 330 million year lifetime, about ${\cal N}_{n'} \sim 10^{48}$ neutrons convert to mirror neutrons, comprising a fraction ${\cal N}_{n'}/{\cal N}_{n} \sim 10^{-9}$ of the total ${\cal N}_n \sim 2 \times 10^{57}$ neutrons in the star.
- The mirror electron fluid core has radius $R_c \simeq 1.18$ km, with Fermi energy $E_F(0) \simeq 4$ MeV at the center and dimensionless Fermi momentum $X_F(0) \simeq 8.9$.
- The thermal time scale of the star is $t_{\rm thermal} \sim 3 \times 10^{15}$ sec, comparable to the star's age.
- The condition for mirror luminosity to dominate requires $e$-$e'$ scattering cross section $\sigma_{ee'} \gg 10^{-50} \; {\rm cm}^2$, which translates to millicharge requirement $\epsilon^2 \gg 10^{-27}$.
- The estimated $e$-$e'$ scattering cross section is $\sigma_{ee'} \simeq 4\pi \alpha^2 \epsilon^2 / E_F T \simeq 10^{-23} \epsilon^2 \; {\rm cm}^2$.
- Existing upper bounds on millicharge $\epsilon$ are $\epsilon \leq 10^{-9}$ from BBN constraints and $\epsilon \leq 10^{-7}$ from asymmetric inflation consistency, leaving nine orders of magnitude margin above the required $\epsilon^2 \gg 10^{-27}$.

## Context

This work critically reexamines the luminosity bound on $n \to n'$ oscillation derived in Berezhiani (2005) and Pospelov (2021) from the cooling of the cold pulsar PSR J2144--3933 (Guillot 2019), which gave $\epsilon_{nn'} \leq 10^{-17}$ eV. The authors extend the analysis by considering the subsequent $\beta$ decay of mirror neutrons and the formation of a mirror particle cloud, an effect not included in prior treatments. The mechanism is similar in philosophy to Zurab (2021) regarding energy transfer between sectors. The result relaxes the astrophysical bound sufficiently that terrestrial searches for $n \to n'$ oscillation (currently sensitive to $\epsilon_{nn'} \sim 10^{-17}$ eV) remain viable. The bound from binary pulsar orbital period changes (Goldman 2019) giving $\epsilon_{nn'} \leq 10^{-13}$ eV is noted as a separate, less stringent constraint.
