# Probing surface distributions of $α$ clusters in $^{20}$Ne via $α$-transfer reaction

> Direct evidence of the $α$-cluster manifestation in bound states has not been obtained yet, although a number of experimental studies were carried out to extract the information of the clustering. In particular in conventional analyses of $α$-transfer reactions, there exist a few significant problems on reaction models, which are insufficient to qualitatively discuss the cluster structure. We aim to verify the development of the $α$-cluster structure from observables. As the first application, we plan to extract the spatial information of the cluster structure of the $^{20}$Ne nucleus in its ground state through the cross section of the $α$-transfer reaction $^{16}$O($^6$Li,~$d$)$^{20}$Ne. For the analysis of the transfer reaction, we work with the coupled-channel Born approximation (CCBA) approach, in which the breakup effect of $^6$Li is explicitly taken into account by means of the continuum-discretized coupled-channel method based on the three-body $α+ d + {}^{16}$O model. The two methods are adopted to calculate the overlap function between $^{20}$Ne and $α+ {}^{16}$O; one is the microscopic cluster model (MCM) with the generator coordinate method, and the other is the phenomenological two-body potential model (PM). We show that the CCBA calculation with the MCM wave function gives a significant improvement of the theoretical result on the angular distribution of the transfer cross section, which is consistent with the experimental data. Employing the PM, it is discussed which region of the cluster wave function is probed on the transfer cross section. It is found that the surface region of the cluster wave function is sensitive to the cross section. The present work is situated as the first step in obtaining important information to systematically investigate the cluster structure.

## Key claim

The authors show that the surface region (r ≳ 4-5 fm) of the α-¹⁶O cluster wave function in ²⁰Ne is what the α-transfer reaction ¹⁶O(⁶Li, d)²⁰Ne actually probes, and that the coupled-channel Born approximation (CCBA) with a microscopic cluster model (MCM) wave function yields a physical spectroscopic factor (below unity), unlike previous distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) analyses which gave unphysical normalization factors exceeding unity.

## Method

The authors analyzed the α-transfer reaction ¹⁶O(⁶Li, d)²⁰Ne using the coupled-channel Born approximation (CCBA) with the continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) method to explicitly include the breakup effect of ⁶Li into α + d within a three-body α + d + ¹⁶O model. For the α-¹⁶O overlap function, they employed two approaches: a microscopic cluster model (MCM) using the generator coordinate method (GCM), and a phenomenological two-body potential model (PM) with Woods-Saxon potentials of varying geometry parameters.

## Key numbers

- At E_in = 20.0 MeV, the MCM spectroscopic factor S_MCM = 0.261 was extracted from χ² fit to experimental data, compared to the previous DWBA normalization factor of 2.70 from Anantaraman (1979)
- At E_in = 42.1 MeV, the MCM spectroscopic factor S_MCM = 0.769 was extracted, compared to the previous DWBA normalization factor of 2.59 from Becchetti (1978)
- The PM spectroscopic factors at 20.0 MeV were: S_PM(PM1) = 0.258, S_PM(PM2) = 0.407, S_PM(PM3) = 0.156
- The PM spectroscopic factors at 42.1 MeV were: S_PM(PM1) = 0.667, S_PM(PM2) = 1.276, S_PM(PM3) = 0.297
- The α-¹⁶O binding energy used in the PM was 4.73 MeV
- The PM potential parameters: PM1 used r₀ = 1.25 × (16)^(1/3) fm and a₀ = 0.76 fm; PM2 used r₀ = 1.25 × (16)^(1/3) fm and a₀ = 0.52 fm; PM3 used r₀ = 1.40 × (16)^(1/3) fm and a₀ = 0.85 fm

## Context

This work extends previous experimental studies of α-cluster structure in ²⁰Ne via the ¹⁶O(⁶Li, d)²⁰Ne reaction by Becchetti (1978), Anantaraman (1979), and Tanabe (1981), and the inverse reaction ²⁰Ne(d, ⁶Li)¹⁶O by Oelert (1979), which used conventional DWBA analyses that produced unphysical normalization factors exceeding unity. The present CCBA approach avoids the ambiguity of the ⁶Li optical potential that plagued those earlier analyses, and the MCM wave function gives properties of ²⁰Ne consistent with experimental ones as shown by En'yo (2014). This work is the first step toward systematically extracting spatial information about α-cluster distributions from transfer reaction observables, enabling future studies of cluster structure in other sd-shell and unstable nuclei.
