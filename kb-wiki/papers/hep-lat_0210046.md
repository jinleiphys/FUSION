# Partially-Quenched Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering

> Nucleon-nucleon scattering is studied to next-to-leading order in a partially-quenched extension of an effective field theory used to describe multi-nucleon systems in QCD. The partially-quenched nucleon-nucleon amplitudes will play an important role in relating lattice simulations of the two-nucleon sector to nature.

## Key claim

Nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes in partially-quenched QCD (PQQCD) are computed to next-to-leading order (NLO) using BBSvK power-counting, with the caveat that the convergence of the chiral expansion in the $^1S_0$ channel is expected to be slow, so higher-order calculations (NNLO and beyond) are needed for reliable chiral extrapolations to physical quark masses.

## Method

The authors construct a partially-quenched effective field theory (EFT) for the two-nucleon sector using BBSvK power-counting, which resums the chiral limit of the tensor force at leading order in the $^3S_1-^3D_1$ coupled channels while treating pions perturbatively in the $^1S_0$ channel and higher partial waves. For higher partial waves (L > 0), analytic expressions for scattering amplitudes are derived from one-pseudo-Goldstone-boson-exchange (OPGBE) at NLO. For the $^1S_0$ channel, the scattering amplitude is computed analytically at NLO by resumming bubble chains of four-nucleon operators and including dressed OPGBE contributions. For the $^3S_1-^3D_1$ coupled channels, the NN potential at NLO is provided, requiring numerical solution of the Schrödinger equation with a spatial square-well regulator.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter is $Q \sim 1/3 \sim m_\pi/\Lambda_{NN}$, where $\Lambda_{NN} = 8\pi f^2/(g_A^2 M_N)$
- The pion decay constant $f \sim 132$ MeV
- The physical pion mass $m_\pi^{(\text{PHYS})} = 139$ MeV
- The low-energy constant $\overline{l}_4 = 4.4 \pm 0.2$ from chiral perturbation theory
- The low-energy constant $c_1 \sim -1$ GeV$^{-1}$ from $\pi N$ sector
- Three determinations of $\overline{d}_{16}$: $-0.91 \pm 0.74$, $-1.01 \pm 0.72$, and $-1.76 \pm 0.85$ GeV$^{-2}$
- Four determinations of $\overline{d}_{18}$: $-0.78 \pm 0.27$, $-0.83 \pm 0.06$, $-1.4 \pm 0.24$, and $-10.14 \pm 0.45$ GeV$^{-2}$
- P-wave scattering volumes at NLO: $a(^1P_1) = g_A^2 M_N/(4\pi f^2 m_\pi^2) + g_0^2 M_N/(12\pi f^2 m_\pi^2) \cdot (m_{SS}^2 - m_\pi^2)/m_\pi^2$; $a(^3P_0) = -g_A^2 M_N/(4\pi f^2 m_\pi^2) + g_0^2 M_N/(4\pi f^2 m_\pi^2) \cdot (m_{SS}^2 - m_\pi^2)/m_\pi^2$; $a(^3P_1) = g_A^2 M_N/(6\pi f^2 m_\pi^2) - g_0^2 M_N/(6\pi f^2 m_\pi^2) \cdot (m_{SS}^2 - m_\pi^2)/m_\pi^2$; $a(^3P_2) = 0$

## Context

This work extends the BBSvK power-counting approach (Beane, Bedaque, Savage, van Kolck, 2002) from QCD to partially-quenched QCD, building on earlier developments of partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory (Bernard and Golterman, 1994; Sharpe, 1997; Golterman and Leinweber, 1998; Sharpe and Shoresh, 2000, 2001) and partially-quenched heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (Labrenz and Sharpe, 1996; Chen and Savage, 2001; Beane and Savage, 2002). The authors previously showed (Beane and Savage, 2002) that hairpin diagrams in PQQCD produce a component of the NN potential that falls exponentially at long distance rather than with Yukawa behavior, motivating the focus on S-matrix elements rather than the potential itself. The results enable lattice QCD practitioners to relate partially-quenched lattice simulations of two-nucleon systems to physical QCD observables through chiral extrapolation, though the authors caution that NNLO calculations are needed to assess convergence in the $^1S_0$ channel.
