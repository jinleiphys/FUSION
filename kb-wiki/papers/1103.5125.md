# Kaon and pion femtoscopy at top RHIC energy in hydrokinetic model

> The hydrokinetic model is applied to restore the initial conditions and space-time picture of the matter evolution in central Au+Au collisions at the top RHIC energy. The analysis is based on the detailed reproduction of the pion and kaon momentum spectra and femtoscopic data in whole interval of the transverse momenta studied by both STAR and PHENIX collaborations. A good description of the pion and kaon transverse momentum spectra and interferometry radii is reached with both initial energy density profiles motivated by the Glauber and Color Glass Condensate (CGC) models, however, at different energy densities.

# Kaon and pion femtoscopy at top RHIC energy in hydrokinetic model

## Key claim

The hydrokinetic model can simultaneously reproduce pion and kaon transverse momentum spectra and femtoscopic radii across the full measured transverse momentum range for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV, but only if the initial energy density profile is tuned differently for Glauber versus Color Glass Condensate (CGC) initial conditions, with maximal energy densities differing by about 20%.

## Method

The hydrokinetic model (HKM) combines hydrodynamic expansion with a microscopic description of particle decoupling via escape probabilities, using a realistic equation of state matched to lattice QCD at high temperatures and a chemically non-equilibrated hadron resonance gas below T_ch=165 MeV. Two sets of initial conditions were tested: a Glauber model where energy density is proportional to participant nucleon density, and a CGC-like model where energy density scales as the 3/2 power of participant density. The two free parameters were the maximal initial energy density epsilon_0 and the initial transverse rapidity slope alpha, fitted to reproduce STAR and PHENIX pion and kaon spectra and interferometry radii.

## Key numbers

- For Glauber initial conditions at tau=1 fm/c: epsilon_0 = 16.5 GeV/fm^3 (average energy density 11.69 GeV/fm^3) and alpha = 0.248 (average transverse velocity 0.224)
- For CGC-like initial conditions at tau=1 fm/c: epsilon_0 = 19.5 GeV/fm^3 (average energy density 13.22 GeV/fm^3) and alpha = 0.23 (average transverse velocity 0.208)
- The CGC initial conditions require about 20% higher maximal energy density than Glauber initial conditions
- Chemical freeze-out parameters: T_ch=165 MeV, mu_B=29 MeV, mu_S=7 MeV, mu_E=-1 MeV, strangeness suppression factor gamma_S=0.935
- The CGC-like initial condition has R_T=3.88 fm

## Context

This work extends the hydrokinetic model previously developed by the same authors (Karpenko and Sinyukov, 2009-2010) to include resonance decays and chemically non-equilibrated hadronic matter, enabling simultaneous description of both pion and kaon femtoscopic data. The results contrast with other studies (Broniowski, 2008) by comparing interferometry radii across the full measured p_T interval at top RHIC energy. The approach enables discrimination between Glauber and CGC initial conditions through their different required energy densities, and demonstrates that pre-thermal transverse flow and shear viscosity effects must be accounted for to reproduce both spectra and femtoscopic scales.
