# $J/ψ$ suppression in the threshold model at RHIC and LHC energy

> In the QGP based threshold model \cite{Blaizot:2000ev,Blaizot:1996nq}, in addition to the normal nuclear absorption, $J/ψ$'s are subjected to an 'anomalous' suppression such that above a threshold density $n_{J/ψ}$, all the $J/ψ$'s are melted. In the threshold model we have analysed the recent PHENIX data on the centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ suppression in Au+Au collisions at RHIC. Feedback from decay of the state $χ$ is accounted for. $J/ψ$'s are anomalously suppressed above a threshold density, $n_{J/ψ}=3.57\pm 0.17$ $fm^{-2}$. Threshold density for anomalous suppression of the state $χ$ is uncertain to a large extent, $n_χ=0.32 \pm 0.32$ $fm^{-2}$. The fraction $F$ of the state $χ$ can not be determined unambiguously, depending on the nuclear absorption, it can vary from 20% to 40%. We have also predicted for the suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy. In central Pb+Pb collisions, $J/ψ$'s are suppressed by a factor of 3-4. Suppression pattern is rather similar to that in Au+Au collisions, if not slighty less in central collisions. Using the PHENIX data on the participant number dependence of the Bjorken energy density, we have also estimated the QGP formation time. For critical temperature $T_c$=192 MeV, estimated QGP formation time ranges between 0.07-0.09 fm/c.

## Key claim

The threshold model, which assumes complete melting of J/ψ mesons above a critical local transverse density, can explain the PHENIX mid-rapidity data on J/ψ suppression in Au+Au collisions at RHIC only when feedback from decay of the higher charmonium state χ is included; the fraction F of J/ψ from χ decay and the nuclear absorption cross-section cannot be determined independently from the data, with F ranging from 20% to 40% depending on the assumed absorption.

## Method

The threshold model of Blaizot et al. (2000, 1996) was extended to include feedback from the χ state, which decays to produce approximately 30% of observed J/ψ mesons. The model assumes that J/ψ and χ are anomalously suppressed (completely melted) when the local transverse density n(b,s) exceeds threshold densities n_J/ψ and n_χ respectively, in addition to conventional nuclear absorption parameterized by absorption cross-sections σ_abs and σ_χ_abs. The model was fit to PHENIX data on the centrality dependence of J/ψ suppression in Au+Au collisions at √s=200 GeV, with the χ-nucleon absorption cross-section fixed to 2.07 times the J/ψ-nucleon cross-section based on the ratio of meson radii.

## Key numbers

- Threshold density for anomalous suppression of direct J/ψ: n_J/ψ = 3.57 ± 0.17 fm⁻²
- Threshold density for anomalous suppression of the χ state: n_χ = 0.32 ± 0.32 fm⁻²
- Fraction F of J/ψ from χ decay: 0.3 ± 0.1, varying from 20% to 40% depending on nuclear absorption
- In central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (√s=5500 GeV), J/ψ suppressed by a factor of 3-4
- For critical temperature T_c = 192 MeV, estimated QGP formation time ranges between 0.07-0.09 fm/c for 2-flavored QGP and 0.06-0.07 fm/c for 3-flavored QGP

## Context

This work extends the threshold model of Blaizot et al. (2000, 1996), originally developed to explain NA50 data on anomalous J/ψ suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy, by incorporating feedback from χ decay that was neglected in the author's earlier analysis (Chaudhuri, 2006, 2007). The analysis shows that the threshold model can explain PHENIX mid-rapidity data but not forward-rapidity data (as previously concluded), and provides predictions for LHC Pb+Pb collisions. The estimated QGP formation time of ~0.07-0.09 fm/c is consistent with Color Glass Condensate models but significantly smaller than the initial time τ_i=0.6 fm obtained from hydrodynamic analyses by Kolb and Heinz (2003). The results enable future comparison with LHC data and suggest that sufficiently hot deconfined medium (T ≥ 2.1 T_c) is created in central and mid-central Au+Au collisions with N_part ≥ 150.
