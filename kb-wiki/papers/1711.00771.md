# Neutrino-Oxygen CC0$π$ scattering in the SuSAv2-MEC model

> We present the predictions of the SuSAv2-MEC model for the double differential charged-current muonic neutrino (antineutrino) cross section on water for the T2K neutrino (antineutrino) beam. We validate our model by comparing with the available inclusive electron scattering data on oxygen and compare our predictions with the recent T2K $ν_μ$-$^{16}$O data, finding good agreement at all kinematics. We show that the results are very similar to those obtained for $ν_μ-^{12}$C scattering, except at low energies, and we comment on the origin of this difference. A factorized spectral function model of $^{16}$O is also included for purposes of comparison.

## Key claim

The SuSAv2-MEC model predicts charged-current neutrino-oxygen cross sections that are very similar to those on carbon, except at low energies, and shows good agreement with T2K data across all kinematics, though the 2p-2h MEC contributions do not significantly improve the comparison with data due to the narrow T2K flux distribution and large experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors apply the SuSAv2 (SuperScaling Approach version 2) model, which combines Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) and Relativistic Plane-Wave Impulse Approximation (RPWIA) scaling functions via a q-dependent blending function, to compute quasielastic responses for oxygen. They add fully relativistic 2p-2h meson exchange current (MEC) responses calculated within the Relativistic Fermi Gas model, using a parametrization originally developed for carbon and rescaled for oxygen by a factor of 1.35. The model is validated against inclusive electron scattering data on oxygen at six kinematic settings, then applied to the T2K muon neutrino beam flux to produce double differential cross sections for CC0π scattering on water.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum for oxygen: k_F = 230 MeV/c; energy shift: E_shift = 16 MeV
- Fermi momentum for carbon: k_F = 228 MeV/c; energy shift: E_shift = 20 MeV
- The 2p-2h MEC responses for oxygen are obtained by rescaling the carbon parametrization with a factor of 1.35, close to the ratio 8/6 × [k_F(O)/k_F(C)]^2
- The MEC contribution is about 15% of the total cross section at the peak maximum, and about 20% at forward angles
- The MEC parametrization covers the momentum transfer range q = 50 to 2000 MeV/c
- Electron scattering data are shown for incident energies from 700 MeV to 1500 MeV at scattering angles of 32 degrees and 37.1 degrees

## Context

This work extends the SuSAv2-MEC model, previously validated for carbon (Megias 2016), to oxygen targets relevant for the T2K experiment where near detectors use mineral oil (carbon) and far detectors use water (oxygen). The model provides a consistent framework for extrapolating cross sections between nuclear targets, which is essential for reducing systematic uncertainties in neutrino oscillation measurements. The results enable implementation of oxygen cross sections into Monte Carlo generators for current (T2K, NOvA) and future (HyperK, DUNE) experiments, while also highlighting that the factorized spectral function model (Benhar 1994, Benhar 2005, Van Orden 2017) overpredicts forward-angle cross sections due to its plane-wave limit.
