# BCS theory of hadronic matter at high densities

> The equilibrium between the so-called 2SC and CFL phases of strange quark matter at high densities is investigated in the framework of a simple schematic model of the NJL type. Equal densities are assumed for quarks $u,d$ and $s$. The 2SC phase is here described by a color-flavor symmetric state, in which the quark numbers are independent of the color-flavor combination. In the CFL phase the quark numbers depend on the color-flavor combination, that is, the number of quarks associated with the color-flavor combinations $ur,dg,sb$ is different from the number of quarks associated with the color flavor combinations $ug,ub,dr,db,sr,sg$. We find that the 2SC phase is stable for a chemical potential $μ$ below $μ_c=0.505$ GeV, while the CFL phase is stable above, the equilibrium pressure being $P_c=0.003$ GeV$^4$. We have used a 3-momentum regularizing cutoff $Λ=0.8$ GeV, which is somewhat larger than is usual in NJL type models. This should be adequate if the relevant chemical potential does not exceed 0.6 GeV.

# BCS theory of hadronic matter at high densities

## Key claim
The 2SC phase (described as a color-flavor symmetric state) is stable for chemical potential μ below 0.505 GeV, while the CFL phase is stable above that value, but this result depends on using a 3-momentum cutoff Λ = 0.8 GeV (larger than usual in NJL models) and neglecting all current quark masses including the strange quark, which is only acceptable if the chemical potential does not exceed 0.6 GeV.

## Method
The authors investigate the equilibrium between color-superconducting phases of strange quark matter using a schematic Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type model with a four-fermion interaction, assuming equal densities for u, d, and s quarks and neglecting all current quark masses. They construct BCS-type mean-field states for the color-flavor symmetric phase (where quark numbers are independent of color-flavor combination) and the CFL phase (where quark numbers depend on the color-flavor combination), solving the gap equations and computing thermodynamical potentials at zero temperature with a 3-momentum cutoff Λ = 0.8 GeV and coupling constant G_c = 5 GeV⁻².

## Key numbers
- Critical chemical potential μ_c = 0.505 GeV for the transition between the color-flavor symmetric (2SC) phase and the CFL phase
- Equilibrium pressure at the transition: P_c = 0.003 GeV⁴
- Critical quark density for the color-flavor symmetric phase: ρ₁ = 0.022 GeV³ (5.39 times nuclear saturation density)
- Critical quark density for the CFL phase: ρ₂ = 0.025 GeV³ (6.13 times nuclear saturation density)
- 3-momentum regularizing cutoff: Λ = 0.8 GeV
- Coupling constant: G_c = 5 GeV⁻²
- For μ = 0.4 GeV in the color-flavor symmetric phase, the gap Δ = 0.0532 GeV
- For μ = 0.6 GeV in the CFL phase, the gap Δ = 0.102 GeV
- Maximum density presented corresponds to 9.808 times nuclear saturation density

## Context
This work extends the BCS treatment of color superconductivity introduced by Bohr et al. (2011) using a formalism that avoids the Lagrange multiplier μ₈ typically employed in conventional 2SC treatments (as in Buballa (2005) and Ruster et al. (2005)) to enforce color neutrality, arguing that this constraint unnecessarily reduces stability. The results are consistent with the hypothesis of Alford et al. (2002) that neutron star interiors may consist of strange matter. The paper enables future investigations of the equilibrium between color symmetric flavor asymmetric and CFL phases including finite strange quark mass and finite temperature effects.
