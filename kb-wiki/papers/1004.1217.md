# QMC and the nature of dense matter: written in the stars?

> We discuss the recent progress in calculating the properties of 'hybrid stars' (stellar objects similar to neutron stars, classified by the incorporation of non-nucleonic degrees of freedom, including but not limited to hyperons and/or a quark-matter core) using the octet-baryon Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model. The version of QMC used is a recent improvement which includes the in-medium modification of the quark-quark hyperfine interaction.

## Key claim

The Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model, including hyperons and a recent improvement to the in-medium quark-quark hyperfine interaction, can predict hybrid star properties, but a phase transition from hyperonic QMC to Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) quark matter is not possible for any reasonable bag constant because the hyperon-softened baryon equation of state is too soft to allow the Gibbs conditions to be met.

## Method

The author uses the octet-baryon QMC model, where baryons are treated as MIT bags of three confined quarks in a mean field of scalar-isoscalar sigma, vector-isoscalar omega, and vector-isovector rho mesons, with couplings fitted to reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties. The baryon effective mass has a quadratic form incorporating a scalar polarizability, which prevents negative effective masses for hyperons. The baryonic equation of state is calculated at zero temperature for charge-neutral beta-equilibrium matter, and then a first-order phase transition to quark matter is modeled using the Gibbs conditions, with quark matter described by either the MIT bag model or the NJL model. Stellar masses and radii are obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter saturation properties reproduced: energy per baryon of -15.86 MeV, baryon density of 0.16 fm^-3, and symmetry energy of 32.5 MeV.
* The MIT bag constant used for the quark phase is B^(1/4) = 180 MeV.
* For hyperonic QMC with an MIT bag model quark phase, the transition to a mixed phase occurs below saturation density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3).
* No phase transition from hyperonic QMC to NJL quark matter is possible for any reasonable value of the bag constant B.

## Context

This work extends the QMC model (Guichon 2008) by incorporating hyperons and a phase transition to quark matter, addressing the long-standing problem of modeling dense matter in neutron stars. It contrasts with Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) (Serot 1984), which cannot model hyperonic matter because its linear effective mass becomes negative at high scalar fields. The finding that hyperonic QMC cannot transition to NJL quark matter highlights the importance of both hyperon degrees of freedom and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, and motivates future work on Hartree-Fock calculations including pion exchange to stiffen the equation of state enough to match the most massive observed pulsars.
