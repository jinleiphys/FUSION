# Study of $p\barΛ$ and $p\barΣ$ systems in constituent quark models

> The $p\barΛ$ systems with J=0 and J=1 are dynamically investigated within the framework of two constituent quark models: the chiral quark model and the quark delocalization color screening model. The model parameters are taken from our previous work, which gave a good description of the proton-antiproton S-wave elastic scattering cross section experimental data. The $p\barΛ$ elastic scattering processes with coupling to $p\barΣ$ state are studied. The results show that, there is no s-wave bound state as indicated by an enhancement near the threshold of $p\barΛ$ in $J/ψ$ decay. However, a $IJ = {1/2}0$ $p\barΣ$ resonance state is given in the quark delocalization color screening model.

# Study of \(p\bar{\Lambda}\) and \(p\bar{\Sigma}\) systems in constituent quark models

## Key claim

The paper finds no \(s\)-wave bound state of \(p\bar{\Lambda}\) that would explain the near-threshold enhancement observed in \(J/\psi\) decay, but the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) predicts a \(p\bar{\Sigma}\) resonance state with \(IJ = \frac{1}{2}0\) that could appear in \(p\bar{\Lambda}\) scattering, whereas the chiral quark model (ChQM) yields no such resonance when annihilation interactions are included.

## Method

The authors performed dynamical calculations of the \(p\bar{\Lambda}\) system with spins \(J=0\) and \(J=1\) using two constituent quark models: the chiral quark model (ChQM) and the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM). They solved the bound-state resonating group method (RGM) equation for the \(p\bar{\Lambda}\) system, including coupling to the \(p\bar{\Sigma}\) channel, with model parameters previously fitted to proton-antiproton \(S\)-wave elastic scattering cross section data. Annihilation interactions (gluon-induced and Goldstone-boson-induced) were included in both models.

## Key numbers

- No bound state found for \(p\bar{\Lambda}\) with \(J=0\) or \(J=1\) in either model when annihilation interactions are included; without annihilation, binding energies are \(B_{p\bar{\Lambda}} = -14.1\) MeV (\(J=0\)) and \(-12.5\) MeV (\(J=1\)) in QDCSM, and \(-14.7\) MeV (\(J=0\)) and \(-13.2\) MeV (\(J=1\)) in ChQM.
- In QDCSM with annihilation interactions included, a \(p\bar{\Sigma}\) resonance state with \(J=0\) is found at mass \(M = 2172.9\) MeV and decay width \(\Gamma = 0.15\) MeV, from channel-coupling calculation.
- In QDCSM without annihilation interactions, the \(p\bar{\Sigma}\) (\(J=0\)) resonance mass is \(M = 2134.1\) MeV with width \(\Gamma = 6.5\) MeV; for \(J=1\) without annihilation, \(M = 2151.9\) MeV and \(\Gamma = 3.1\) MeV.
- In ChQM with annihilation interactions, no \(p\bar{\Sigma}\) resonance is found (unbound); without annihilation, the \(p\bar{\Sigma}\) (\(J=0\)) resonance mass is \(M = 2165.6\) MeV with \(\Gamma = 7.6\) MeV, and for \(J=1\) without annihilation, \(M = 2189.3\) MeV with \(\Gamma = 7.0\) MeV.
- The theoretical \(p\bar{\Sigma}\) threshold is 2176.5 MeV in QDCSM and 2191.8 MeV in ChQM.

## Context

This work extends previous constituent quark model studies of nucleon-antinucleon interactions (Huang et al., 2005; Ping et al., 2006) to the \(p\bar{\Lambda}\) system, motivated by near-threshold enhancements observed by the BES Collaboration in \(J/\psi \rightarrow pK^{-}\bar{\Lambda}+c.c.\) decays (BES, 2008) and by Belle in \(B \rightarrow p\bar{\Lambda}\pi\) decays (Belle, 2008). The results contradict the interpretation of the observed enhancement as an \(s\)-wave \(p\bar{\Lambda}\) bound state, but the predicted \(p\bar{\Sigma}\) resonance in QDCSM provides an alternative candidate for future experimental searches. The authors note that the \(N\bar{\Lambda}-N\bar{\Sigma}\) conversion process is a clean test of short-range baryon-antibaryon interaction mechanisms because one-gluon-exchange gives null contribution while \(\rho\)-meson exchange is nonzero, unlike in the nucleon-nucleon case.
