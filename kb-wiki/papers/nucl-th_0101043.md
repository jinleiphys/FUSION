# Asymmetry in $\vec{n} + p -> d + γ$

> Heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) is applied to the asymmetry $A_γ$ in $\vec{n} + p \to d + γ$ at threshold, which arises due to the weak parity non-conserving interactions. Instead of appealing to Siegert's theorem, transition operators up to next-to-leading chiral order are derived and the corresponding amplitudes are evaluated with the Argonne $v_{18}$ wavefunctions. In addition to the impulse contribution, both parity-conserving and parity-non-conserving two-body one-pion-exchange diagrams appear up to this order. Our prediction for the asymmetry is $A_γ= - 0.10 \hpiNN$, which is close to the Siegert's theorem based result, $A_γ\simeq -0.11 \hpiNN$. This illustrates that HBChPT is effective in the parity-non-conserving physics.

## Key claim

Heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) up to next-to-leading order (NLO) predicts the parity-non-conserving asymmetry in polarized neutron-proton capture at threshold as \(A_\gamma = -0.10\, h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\), which is within 10% of the Siegert's theorem based result \(A_\gamma \simeq -0.11\, h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\); however, the chiral convergence is rather slow, with the LO and NLO contributions individually large and cancelling (ratio \(\bar{E}_{\rm 2B}/\bar{E}_{\rm 1B} \simeq -2/3\)).

## Method

The authors applied heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) following Weinberg's power counting to derive transition operators up to next-to-leading chiral order for the asymmetry \(A_\gamma\) in \(\vec{n} + p \to d + \gamma\) at threshold. They evaluated the parity-conserving and parity-non-conserving one-pion-exchange two-body currents and the impulse (one-body) contribution using Argonne \(v_{18}\) wavefunctions, without invoking Siegert's theorem. The asymmetry was computed from the ratio of the parity-non-conserving E1 amplitude \(\mathrm{E}({}^3S_1)\) to the dominant isovector M1 amplitude \(\mathrm{M}({}^1S_0)\).

## Key numbers

- Asymmetry prediction: \(A_\gamma^{\rm ChPT} = -0.10\, h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\) (dimensionless, proportional to the weak pion-nucleon coupling constant).
- Siegert's theorem result with same wavefunctions: \(A_\gamma \simeq -0.11\, h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\).
- Reduced E1 amplitude from HBChPT: \(\bar{E} = 0.0136\ \mathrm{fm}^2\) (sum of one-body \(\bar{E}_{\rm 1B} = 0.0428\ \mathrm{fm}^2\) and two-body \(\bar{E}_{\rm 2B} = -0.0302\ \mathrm{fm}^2\)).
- Siegert's theorem reduced E1 amplitude: \(\bar{E}^{\rm Siegert} = 0.0148\ \mathrm{fm}^2\).
- Ratio of HBChPT to Siegert result: \((\bar{E}_{\rm 1B}+\bar{E}_{\rm 2B})/\bar{E}^{\rm Siegert} = 0.92\).
- Two-body current contribution to the M1 matrix element: \(\delta_{\rm 2B} = (4.6 \pm 0.3)\%\).
- Spin-singlet \(np\) scattering length: \(a_s \simeq -23.7\ \mathrm{fm}\); spin-triplet scattering length: \(a_t \simeq 5.42\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- Experimental ILL result: \(A_\gamma^{\rm ILL} = -(1.5 \pm 4.8) \times 10^{-8}\), implying \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)} = (1.4 \pm 4.4) \times 10^{-7}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of the asymmetry by Danilov (1972) and by Desplanques (1975) using realistic potentials (Hamada-Johnston, Reid-soft-core, Tourreil-Sprung), which all gave \(A_\gamma \approx -0.11\, h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\) via Siegert's theorem. It contrasts with the effective field theory calculation by Kaplan, Savage, Springer and Wise (1999) which found \(A_\gamma = 0.17\, h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\) (sign convention difference aside), attributed by Desplanques (2000) to the zero-range approximation. The HBChPT result confirms the conventional Siegert-based value and demonstrates that a systematic chiral EFT can describe parity-non-conserving observables in two-nucleon systems, enabling more precise extraction of the weak pion-nucleon coupling constant \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\) from upcoming experiments at LANSCE aiming for \(10^{-9}\) accuracy.
