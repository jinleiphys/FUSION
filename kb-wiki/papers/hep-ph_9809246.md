# Anomalous Hypercharge Axial Current And The Couplings Of The eta And f_1 (1420) Mesons To The Nucleon

> It is argued that the precise three flavor symmetry of hadrons is not SU(3)_F but rather U(4)_F restricted to SU(2)_{ud}*SU(2)_{cs}*U(1) and considered in the limit of frozen charm degree of freedom. Within this scheme the only hypercharge generator which is consistent with the Gell-Mann--Nishijima relation appears to be an element of the su(2)_{cs}*u(1) subalgebra as it contains the unit matrix generating the baryon number current. In considering now hypercharge axial transformations, the u(1) generator will give rise to the anomalous U(1)_A axial current and the resulting hypercharge axial current will be anomalous, too. The only anomaly free neutral strong axial current having a well defined chiral limit turns out to be identical (up to a constant factor) with the weak axial current, and the eta meson acquires features of a `masked' strange Goldstone boson. The eta N and f_1(1420)N couplings will now proceed via a purely strange isosinglet axial current and appear strongly suppressed relative to the predictions of the octet quark model. For this reason, loop corrections like the a_0πN and KK^*Y triangles to the respective eta NN and f_1(1420)NN vertices acquire importance. Here we calculate them in using effective lagrangians and show that the model developed is convenient for data description beyond the limits of applicability of chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the precise three-flavor symmetry of hadrons is not SU(3)_F but rather U(4)_F restricted to SU(2)_{ud} × SU(2)_{cs} × U(1) in the limit of frozen charm, which makes the hypercharge axial current anomalous; consequently, the η meson is not an octet Goldstone boson but a "masked" strange Goldstone boson, and its coupling to the nucleon proceeds via a purely strange isosinglet axial current rather than the hypercharge current.

## Method

The authors first demonstrate that the hypercharge generator consistent with the Gell-Mann–Nishijima relation belongs to the su(2)_{cs} × u(1) subalgebra of U(4)_F, not to SU(3)_F, making the hypercharge axial current anomalous due to the U(1)_A anomaly. They then calculate loop corrections to the ηNN and f₁(1420)NN vertices using effective Lagrangians, specifically evaluating triangular diagrams of the type a₀(980)πN for the ηNN vertex and K*(892)KY for the f₁(1420)NN vertex, to account for the strong suppression of the tree-level couplings.

## Key numbers

- Δs = −0.08 ± 0.05: the fraction of proton helicity carried by the strange quark sea, which determines the tree-level ηN and f₁(1420)N couplings
- Mixing angle ε = −45.4° for the η meson wave function: |η⟩ = cos ε (−|s̄s⟩) − sin ε (1/√2)(|ūu + d̄d⟩)
- Mixing angle ε ≈ 15° for the axial vector mesons f₁(1285) and f₁(1420)
- f_{a₀ηπ} = 0.44: coupling extracted from the experimental decay width of a₀(980) → η + π
- f_{πNN}²/4π = 0.075: standard pseudovector πN coupling constant
- |G₁(k² = m_η²)| = 0.06 g_{a₀NN}: effective pseudovector ηNN coupling from the a₀πN triangle
- |G₂(k² = m_η²)| = 0.22 g_{a₀NN}: effective pseudoscalar ηNN coupling from the a₀πN triangle
- g_{a₀NN} varies between approximately 3.11 and 10 depending on the NN potential model
- g_{ηNN}²/4π ≈ 0.4: value suggested by data analyses of η photoproduction near threshold
- g²_{a₀NN}/4π ≈ 6.79: maximal magnitude reported in Ma (1989) that fits the ηN coupling data

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Kirchbach (1997) that the η meson is a "masked" strange Goldstone boson rather than an octet Goldstone boson, contradicting the standard SU(3)_F chiral symmetry framework underlying chiral perturbation theory. The paper provides a phenomenological field-theoretical approach for describing meson-nucleon vertices beyond the limits of applicability of chiral perturbation theory, complementing the meson cloud model of Szczurek (1995) which studied meson effects on electromagnetic and weak vector form factors. The results enable a description of η photoproduction data and suggest that loop corrections from triangular diagrams can account for the experimentally observed suppression of ηN couplings, which has been found in analyses of η photoproduction (Tiator and Kamalov), p̄p collisions (Kroll), and NN/NY phase shifts (Reuber).
