# Is there elliptic flow without transverse flow?

> Azimuthal anisotropy of final particle distributions was originally introduced as a signature of transverse collective flow. We show that finite anisotropy in momentum space can result solely from the shape of the particle emitting source. However, by comparing the differential anisotropy to recent data from STAR collaboration we can exclude such a scenario, but instead show that the data favour strong flow as resulting from a hydrodynamical evolution.

# Is there elliptic flow without transverse flow?

## Key claim
Finite elliptic flow can arise solely from the shape of a particle-emitting source without any transverse collective flow, but comparison with STAR data rules out this scenario and instead requires strong transverse flow as produced by hydrodynamic evolution.

## Method
The authors constructed a simple "Bjorken brick" model: a boost-invariant, rectangular source with no transverse expansion, constant temperature, and particle emission from both surfaces and volume via a modified Cooper-Frye procedure with a theta-function to remove negative contributions. They derived an analytic expression for the differential elliptic flow coefficient v2(p_t) and compared it to STAR data, then generalized the model to include anisotropic flow fields and source shapes, and finally performed full hydrodynamic calculations with two different equations of state.

## Key numbers
- Thermalization time fixed to τ0 = 0.6 fm/c, motivated by hydrodynamic calculations.
- For the no-flow brick model, v2 saturates already around p_t = 200–300 MeV/c.
- Hydrodynamic initial conditions for most central collisions yield maximum energy densities of ε = 22.3–23 GeV/fm³ and temperatures of T = 270–330 MeV, depending on the equation of state.
- Hydrodynamic results agree with charged particle data up to p_t ≈ 1.5 GeV/c, above which the data saturate while the hydrodynamic result continues increasing.
- In the generalized brick with anisotropic flow, a fit using v_x = 0.6, v_y = 0.57, and l_x/l_y = 0.93 shows v2 saturating around p_t = 1.5–2 GeV/c, matching the data region.

## Context
This work extends the earlier proposal by Ollitrault (1992) that azimuthal anisotropy signals transverse collective flow, and contrasts with a Gaussian source parametrization by Urs (1999) that did not include emission from surfaces with spacelike normal vectors. The authors show that surface-only emission can produce finite v2 without flow, but the differential p_t dependence cannot match STAR data (2001) without strong transverse flow. The hydrodynamic calculations, using initial conditions scaled from SPS fits by Kolb (2000), reproduce the data up to 1.5 GeV/c and provide upper limits on elliptic flow, supporting early thermalization and hydrodynamic evolution in central and semi-central Au+Au collisions at RHIC.
