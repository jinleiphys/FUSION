# Accurate calculation of low energy scattering phase shifts of charged particles in a harmonic oscillator trap

> Considering the elastic scattering of two charged particles, we present two methods for numerically solving the generalized Coulomb-corrected BERW formula with high accuracy across the entire energy spectrum. We illustrate these methods using p-alpha scattering, employing a phenomenological p-alpha short-range interaction. Our results reproduce the phase shifts computed with the Numerov method for all l=0 and l=1 channels. We also provide full access to the Python script used to obtain these results, which can be readily applied to a wide range of core-fragment scattering problems in nuclear and atomic physics.

# Accurate calculation of low energy scattering phase shifts of charged particles in a harmonic oscillator trap

## Key claim

The authors present two numerical methods (successive approximation with feedback mixing and direct matrix inversion) for solving the Dyson equation to obtain the Coulomb and Coulomb-plus-harmonic-oscillator Green functions needed for the generalized BERW formula, but the successive approximation method fails to converge for positive phase shifts after the energy passes the pole positions of the non-interacting harmonic oscillator Green function, and the direct method fails at those same pole positions.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson equation for the Coulomb Green function \(G^C_l(r,r')\) and the Coulomb-plus-harmonic-oscillator Green function \(G^{C,\omega}_l(r,r')\) on a numerical grid with variable step size, using either (i) an iterative successive approximation method with a feedback mixing parameter \(\epsilon\) and a convergence threshold \(\Delta = 10^{-8}\) on the trace of the diagonal, or (ii) direct matrix inversion of the discretized Dyson equations. The resulting Green functions are inserted into the generalized Coulomb-corrected BERW formula to extract free-space Coulomb phase shifts \(\delta^C(E)\) from the bound-state energies of the two-charged-particle system confined in a harmonic oscillator trap. The methods are tested on \(p-\alpha\) scattering using a phenomenological square-well potential with spin-orbit coupling from Ali (1985).

## Key numbers

* For the \(p-\alpha\) system, the reduced mass is set to \(\mu = 4/5 m_N\) with \((\hbar c)^2/m_N = 41.47\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm^2}\) and \(e^2 = 1.44\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}\).
* The numerical grid uses \(r_{\text{min}} = 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{fm}\); for \(r_{\text{min}} \leq 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{fm}\) the ratio in the limit becomes numerically unstable, while for \(10^{-3}\ \mathrm{fm} \leq r_{\text{min}} \leq 10^{-1}\ \mathrm{fm}\) the extracted phase shifts vary by less than \(1\%\).
* With \(N=400\) grid points and endpoint factor \(f=10\) (giving \(r_{\text{max}} = 10 \cdot b\)), the phase shifts change by less than \(1\%\) compared to \(N=800\) or \(f=20\).
* For harmonic oscillator frequencies \(\omega \lesssim 0.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (trap length \(b \gtrsim 6\ \mathrm{fm}\)), the extracted \(p-\alpha\) phase shifts differ by less than \(1\%\) from free-space Numerov calculations.
* The potential parameters are \(V_0 = -33.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(a = 2.55\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(\beta = 0.103\), fitted to experimental \(p-\alpha\) phase shifts in the \(^2S_{1/2}\), \(^2P_{1/2}\), and \(^2P_{3/2}\) partial waves.
* The harmonic oscillator frequencies used span \(0.015\ \mathrm{MeV} \leq \omega \leq 2.35\ \mathrm{MeV}\), corresponding to trap lengths \(4.7\ \mathrm{fm} \leq b \leq 60\ \mathrm{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the generalized BERW formula for charged particles developed by Guo (2021, 2021) and the perturbative approach applied by Zhang et al. (2024) to proton-proton scattering with the Minnesota potential. The authors address the convergence issues reported by Zhang et al. (2024) for the perturbative series when bound-state energies pass the non-interacting harmonic oscillator eigenenergies, by providing a direct matrix inversion method that works at all energies except at the pole positions themselves. The Python script released with the paper can be applied to any core-fragment scattering problem in nuclear and atomic physics, and the authors note that future work should extend the method to coupled-channel and inelastic scattering as formulated by Guo (2022) and Zhang et al. (2024).
