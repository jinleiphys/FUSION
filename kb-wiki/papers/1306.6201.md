# Random Phase Approximation without Bogoliubov Quasi-particles

> A new version of random phase approximation is proposed for low-energy harmonic vibrations in nuclei. The theory is not based on the quasi-particle vacuum of the BCS/HFB ground state, but on the pair condensate determined in Ref. [4]. The current treatment conserves the exact particle number all the time. As a first test the theory is considered in two special cases: the degenerate model (large pairing limit) and the vanished-pairing limit.

# Random Phase Approximation without Bogoliogov Quasi-particles

## Key claim

A number-conserving random phase approximation (N-RPA) can be formulated for low-energy harmonic vibrations in nuclei by replacing the BCS/HFB quasi-particle vacuum with a pair condensate that has definite particle number, but the theory is tested only in two limiting cases (the degenerate model and the zero-pairing limit) and requires further applications to realistic nuclei.

## Method

The unperturbed ground state is taken as an N-pair condensate, whose pair structure, occupation numbers, and pair-transition amplitudes are determined by the number-conserving pairing theory of Jia (2013). The N-RPA equation is derived by linearizing the Heisenberg equation of motion for the density matrix operator, approximating the correlated ground state by the unperturbed pair condensate, and applying Wick-like contractions to two- and three-body density matrices. The resulting linear homogeneous equation for the phonon creation operator coefficients yields the vibrational frequency ω when its determinant vanishes.

## Key numbers

- In the degenerate model with quadrupole-plus-pairing force, at infinitesimal quadrupole coupling κ, the N-RPA frequency ω ≈ GΩ, where G is the pairing strength and 2Ω is the degeneracy; this equals the correct pairing gap.
- In the degenerate model, the occupation number n₁ = N/Ω, where N is the number of pairs and Ω is the half-degeneracy.
- In the zero-pairing limit, the N-RPA equation reduces to the conventional RPA equation ω r₁₂ = e₁₂ r₁₂ − n₁₂ Σ₃₄ V₁₄₃₂ r₃₄, with r₁₂ = c₁₂ n₂(1 − n₁).

## Context

This work extends the number-conserving pairing theory of Jia (2013) to include small-amplitude collective vibrations, addressing the particle-number violation inherent in conventional QRPA based on BCS/HFB quasi-particle vacua. The approach avoids the need for projection techniques to restore particle number and remains valid at pairing strengths below the BCS critical point. The theory enables future calculations of anharmonicities by constructing Hamiltonian matrices in the collective subspace and could be extended to odd-mass nuclei and particle-particle RPA.
