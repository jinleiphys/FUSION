# Effect of anisotropy on HBT radii using leptonpair interferometry

> The effect of initial state momentum-space anisotropy on invariant mass dependence of HBT radii extracted from the leptonpair interferometry is presented here. We have studied the Bose-Einstein Correlation Function (BECF) for two identical virtual photons decaying to leptonpairs at most central collision of LHC energy having fixed transverse momentum of one of the virtual photons ($k_{1T}$= 2 GeV). The {\em free streaming interpolating} model with fixed initial condition has been used for the evolution in anisotropic Quark Gluon Plasma (aQGP) and the relativistic (1+2)d hydrodynamics model with cylindrical symmetry and longitudinal boost invariance has been used for both isotropic Quark Gluon Plasma (iQGP) and hadronic phases. We found a significant change in the spatial and temporal dimension of the evolving system in presence of initial state momentum-space anisotropy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that initial-state momentum-space anisotropy in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produces a significant, quantitative change in the invariant-mass dependence of HBT radii extracted from lepton-pair interferometry, but this effect is conditional on the isotropization time (\(\tau_{iso}\)) being longer than the initial formation time (\(\tau_i\)), with the magnitude of the change controlled by the specific value of \(\tau_{iso}\) (2 or 3 fm versus the isotropic case of 0.08 fm).

## Method

The authors computed the Bose-Einstein correlation function (BECF) for two identical virtual photons decaying to lepton pairs in central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, fixing the transverse momentum of one virtual photon at \(k_{1T} = 2\) GeV. The space-time evolution of the system was modeled using the *free streaming interpolating* model for the anisotropic QGP phase (from \(\tau_i\) to \(\tau_{iso}\)) and a relativistic (1+2)d ideal hydrodynamics model with cylindrical symmetry and longitudinal boost invariance for the isotropic QGP and hadronic phases. HBT radii (\(R_{out}\) and \(R_{side}\)) were extracted by fitting the calculated correlation function to a Gaussian parametrization for different invariant mass windows (\(\langle M \rangle\)) and for three values of \(\tau_{iso}\) (0.08, 2, and 3 fm).

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature \(T_i = 646\) MeV and initial formation time \(\tau_i = 0.08\) fm for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV.
- Critical temperature \(T_c = 175\) MeV, chemical freeze-out temperature \(T_{ch} = 170\) MeV, and kinetic freeze-out temperature \(T_{fo} = 120\) MeV.
- HBT radii were extracted for invariant mass windows \(\langle M \rangle = 0.3, 0.5, 0.77, 1.02, 1.6, 2.5\) GeV.
- The isotropic scenario corresponds to \(\tau_{iso} = \tau_i = 0.08\) fm; anisotropic scenarios used \(\tau_{iso} = 2\) fm and \(\tau_{iso} = 3\) fm.
- The transverse momentum of one virtual photon was fixed at \(k_{1T} = 2\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of lepton-pair interferometry (e.g., Mohanty et al., 2011; Alam et al., 2007) by incorporating the effect of initial-state momentum-space anisotropy in the QGP, which was previously modeled for dilepton production by Martinez and Strickland (2009) and Schenke et al. (2012). The results suggest that the invariant-mass dependence of HBT radii, particularly the ratio \(R_{out}/R_{side}\) and the difference \(\sqrt{R_{out}^2 - R_{side}^2}\), can serve as a tool to probe the spatial and temporal evolution of the system in the presence of anisotropy, potentially enabling experimental discrimination between isotropic and anisotropic initial conditions at LHC energies.
