# Artificial atoms from cold bosons in one dimension

> We investigate the ground-state properties of weakly repulsive one-dimensional bosons in the presence of an attractive zero-range impurity potential. First, we derive mean-field solutions to the problem on a finite ring for the two asymptotic cases: (i) all bosons are bound to the impurity and (ii) all bosons are in a scattering state. Moreover, we derive the critical line that separates these regimes in the parameter space. In the thermodynamic limit, this critical line determines the maximum number of bosons that can be bound by the impurity potential, forming an artificial atom. Second, we validate the mean-field results using the flow equation approach and the multi-layer multi-configuration time-dependent Hartree method for atomic mixtures. While beyond-mean-field effects destroy long-range order in the Bose gas, the critical boson number is unaffected. Our findings are important for understanding such artificial atoms in low-density Bose gases with static and mobile impurities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a one-dimensional attractive impurity potential can bind a finite number of weakly repulsive bosons into a many-body bound state ("artificial atom"), with the critical boson number given by \(N_{\mathrm{cr}} = 2|c|/g + 1\) in the mean-field approximation; beyond-mean-field effects destroy long-range order in the Bose gas but leave this critical number unchanged.

## Method

The authors solve the Gross-Pitaevskii equation (GPE) for \(N\) bosons on a finite ring with periodic boundary conditions, obtaining analytic mean-field solutions for the many-body bound state and the scattering state using Jacobi elliptic functions. They validate these mean-field predictions using two beyond-mean-field numerical methods: the in-medium similarity renormalization group for bosons (IM-SRG) and the multi-layer multi-configuration time-dependent Hartree method for atomic mixtures (ML-MCTDHX), focusing on the zero-density limit where the ring length \(L\) is taken to infinity while \(N\) is fixed.

## Key numbers

- Critical boson number: \(N_{\mathrm{cr}} = 2|c|/g + 1\), where \(c\) is the impurity-boson coupling and \(g\) is the boson-boson coupling (Eq. 13).
- At the point of transition, the energy is \(E = -N_{\mathrm{cr}} c^2 M / 6\), independent of system size \(L\) (Eq. 14).
- For the many-body bound state in the zero-density limit, the characteristic width is \(x_{\mathrm{mbb}} = (1/M|c|)(N_{\mathrm{cr}}-1)/(N_{\mathrm{cr}}-N)\).
- At the point of transition, the mean-field wave function tail decays as \(1/(M|c|x|+1)\) (Eq. 17).
- The half-probability width at the transition is \(x_{\mathrm{PoT}} = 1/(|c|M)\) (Eq. 18).

## Context

This work extends the mean-field studies of Gunn (1980) and Kolomeisky (2004) on bosons bound to an impurity in one dimension, and addresses a puzzle raised by a phenomenological argument from TrappingCollapse (2007) that suggested only a single boson could be bound due to fermionization. The results are relevant for understanding the Bose polaron problem, particularly the polaron-to-molecule transition region studied by Catani (2012), Jorgensen (2016), and Hu (2016). The validated mean-field solution enables future studies of mobile impurities and finite-range effects in low-density Bose gases.
