# A Computational Phase Function Method for $α-α$ Scattering: Wavefunction Construction from Single and Two-Term Morse Potentials

> In this work, the phase function method (PFM) is employed for the first time to explicitly construct scattering wavefunctions for the $αα$ system using a single-term Morse potential. Unlike earlier PFM-based studies that primarily focused on reproducing scattering phase shifts, the present approach directly reconstructs radial wavefunctions for the $\ell = 0$, 2, and 4 partial waves without solving the Schr$\ddot{\text{o}}$dinger equation. For comparison, we adopt the interaction potential parameters reported by Sastri et al., who determined them using a two-term reference potential approach with genetic algorithm optimization to accurately reproduce the $αα$ scattering phase shifts. Without re-optimization, we construct the corresponding wavefunctions and find very good agreement with those obtained using our single-term Morse potential. The results also show excellent consistency with the resonating-group method calculations of Hiura \textit{et al.}.These findings demonstrate that PFM provides a numerically stable, efficient, and unified framework for scattering wavefunction construction in cluster-cluster systems.

## Key claim

The phase function method (PFM) is used for the first time to explicitly construct scattering wavefunctions for the α-α system using a single-term Morse potential, rather than only reproducing scattering phase shifts as in earlier PFM-based studies; the wavefunctions show very good agreement with those obtained using a two-term reference potential approach and with resonating-group method calculations.

## Method

The authors model the short-range nuclear interaction between two α particles using a single-term Morse potential and incorporate the long-range Coulomb repulsion through an error-function representation with the α-particle RMS radius taken as 1.44 fm. They numerically integrate the first-order nonlinear phase equation and the coupled amplitude equation simultaneously using a Runge-Kutta scheme, starting from the origin with initial conditions δℓ(0)=0 and Aℓ(0)=1, up to asymptotic distances where the potential becomes negligible. For comparison, they adopt the two-component Morse reference potential parameters reported by Sastri et al., which were determined using a genetic algorithm optimization, and construct the corresponding wavefunctions without re-optimization.

## Key numbers

- The constant ħ²/2μ for the α-α system is 10.44217 MeV·fm².
- The RMS radius of the α particle is taken as Rα = 1.44 fm.
- The center-of-mass energy is related to the laboratory energy through ECM = ½ Elab.
- The s-wave scattering wavefunction is shown at 19.45 MeV in comparison with results of Yoshiharu et al.

## Context

This work extends earlier PFM-based studies that focused only on reproducing scattering phase shifts, such as the authors' own previous investigation of α-α scattering where mean absolute percentage errors of 1.17, 0.69, and 1.77 were achieved for the ℓ = 0⁺, 2⁺, and 4⁺ partial waves up to 25.5 MeV. The results are compared with the two-component reference potential approach of Sastri et al., who used genetic algorithm optimization, and with the resonating-group method calculations of Hiura et al. The methodology can be extended to other cluster and nucleus-nucleus scattering systems and to different interaction models.
