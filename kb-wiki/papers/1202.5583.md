# Testing new strongly intensive measures of transverse momentum fluctuations

> Recently, the new strongly intensive measures of fluctuations $Δ$ and $Σ$ have been proposed. In this publication their properties are tested using an example of event-by-event transverse momentum fluctuations. The obtained values are compared to the long used $Φ$ measure of pT fluctuations. Several tests are preformed within data produced by fast generators, as well as by the UrQMD model. The UrQMD calculations are presented for the systems and energies which are planned to be studied in the NA61/SHINE experiment at the CERN Super Proton Synchrotron.

## Key claim

The paper tests the newly proposed strongly intensive measures Δ and Σ for transverse momentum fluctuations, showing that they are indeed strongly intensive (independent of system size and its fluctuations) in superposition models, but that their values differ from the established Φ measure because they do not vanish for independent particle production.

## Method

The author generated Monte Carlo events using fast generators where each event was composed of a given number of identical sources, with particle multiplicity drawn from Poisson distributions and transverse momentum from exponential transverse mass spectra with inverse slope parameter T = 150 MeV. The number of sources per event was varied (constant, Poisson, or Negative Binomial) and source-by-source or event-by-event "temperature" fluctuations were introduced with σ_T = 25 MeV. Additionally, the UrQMD3.3 transport model was used to generate minimum-bias events for Be+Be, Ar+Ca, and Xe+La collisions at beam energies from 13A to 158A GeV, selecting the 20% most central collisions (and in some cases 7.2% most central) to compare Δ^(XN), Σ^(XN), and Φ_(p_T) measures.

## Key numbers

- For independent particle production with constant number of sources, Φ_(p_T) is consistent with zero, while Δ^(XN) and Σ^(XN) take non-zero values that remain constant as the number of sources varies from 5 to 800.
- For event-by-event T fluctuations with σ_T = 25 MeV and mean number of sources = 100, all three measures increase with σ_T; at σ_T/⟨T⟩ ≈ 3%, the simplified formula for Φ_(p_T) begins to deviate from Monte Carlo results.
- In UrQMD for Xe+La collisions at SPS energies, applying a 0.005 < p_T < 1.5 GeV/c cut reduces all three fluctuation measures by 15-19% compared to no kinematic restrictions.
- For Xe+La at 13A GeV beam energy, restricting centrality from 0-20% down to approximately 7% most central reduces Φ_(p_T) for all charged particles to values similar to those for negatively charged particles alone.
- In UrQMD for 7.2% most central collisions, Φ_(p_T) for negatively charged particles is close to zero across all energies, while for positively charged particles in Be+Be at lower SPS energies a significant increase remains.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of strongly intensive fluctuation measures proposed by G. Wilk and Z. Wlodarczyk (2009) by testing the newly defined Δ and Σ families on transverse momentum fluctuations, comparing them to the established Φ measure previously used by the NA49 experiment (2004, 2009). The UrQMD calculations are performed for the systems and energies planned for the NA61/SHINE experiment at CERN SPS, enabling future experimental searches for the critical point of strongly interacting matter through a 2D scan of collision energy and system size.
