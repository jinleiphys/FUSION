# A holographic calculation of the electric conductivity of the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma near the deconfinement transition

> The frequency dependent conductivity $σ(ω)$ of the strongly coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) is estimated using a bottom up holographic model that can adequately describe recent lattice data for QCD thermodynamics at zero chemical potential. Different choices for the coupling between the bulk gauge field and the other bulk fields that define the background (the metric and a scalar field) are used in order to fit the lattice data for the electric charge susceptibility $χ_2^Q/T^2$. The ratio $σ_{DC}/T$ is found to vary near the deconfinement transition in a way that is similar to recent lattice results. This model is used to compute the charge diffusion coefficient $D$ of the strongly coupled plasma. We find that the dimensionless combination $DT$ has the same type of temperature dependence displayed by $σ_{DC}/T$ and, thus, charge diffusion is suppressed at low temperatures. The frequency dependent conductivity $σ(ω)$ reveals some nontrivial structure for values of the temperature near the phase transition. None of these structures appear in the associated Euclidean correlator, which we also compute. Our results suggest that the conformal invariance violation near the QCD deconfinement phase transition may be seen in the Euclidean correlator through a downward shift of its value at the minimum, which gives a rough estimate of the temperature dependence of the DC conductivity in the plasma.

## Key claim

The holographic model predicts that the DC conductivity normalized by temperature, σ_DC/T, varies rapidly near the deconfinement transition in a way similar to recent lattice results, but this prediction is contingent on the specific choice of the gauge coupling function f(φ) that is fixed to match lattice data for the electric charge susceptibility χ₂^Q/T².

## Method

The authors use a bottom-up Einstein-Scalar-Maxwell holographic model in 5 dimensions, where the metric and scalar field define a non-conformal background fitted to lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero chemical potential from Borsanyi et al. (2010). The bulk U(1) gauge field is treated as a probe (no backreaction) and its coupling to the scalar field, f(φ), is fixed by requiring that the holographic electric charge susceptibility matches lattice data from Borsanyi et al. (2011). The DC conductivity is computed via the membrane paradigm formula σ_DC = f(φ(z_h)) e^{Ã(z_h)}, and the AC conductivity is obtained by solving a Riccati flow equation from the horizon to the boundary.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound squared c_s² from the holographic model is fitted to lattice QCD data in the temperature interval T ~ 150–300 MeV, with T_c = 150 MeV set by matching the minimum of c_s².
- The scalar potential parameters are γ = 0.606, b₂ = 0.703, b₄ = -0.12, b₆ = 0.0044.
- For the gauge coupling parametrizations, the fitted constants are a₁ = 0.4, a₂ = 4.0, a₃ = 0.23.
- The Stefan-Boltzmann limit for the charge susceptibility in 3-flavor QCD is χ₂^SB/T² = 2/3.
- The holographic results for χ₂^Q/χ₂^CFT agree with lattice data for T < 300 MeV, with the conformal regime reached at T ~ 3–4 T_c.
- The DC conductivity σ_DC/σ_DC,CFT shows a rapid variation in the crossover region near T_c = 150 MeV, with values below the conformal limit at low temperatures.
- The charge diffusion constant D/D_CFT becomes very sensitive to the choice of f(φ) for T < 150 MeV, but D/D_CFT < 1 at low temperatures is robust.
- For T ~ 2 T_c, the difference between non-conformal and conformal AC conductivity is negligible.

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of electric transport in strongly coupled plasmas beyond conformal theories such as N=4 SYM, which was previously investigated by Teaney (2006) and others. The bottom-up approach follows the framework of Gubser et al. (2008) and Noronha et al. (2009, 2010) for non-conformal holographic QCD models, and incorporates the gauge field coupling method of DeWolfe et al. (2010). The results enable comparison with lattice QCD calculations of the electric conductivity by Amato et al. (2013) and provide predictions for the charge diffusion coefficient that may be tested by future lattice calculations. The finding that the Euclidean correlator's minimum shifts downward near the transition suggests a practical way to estimate the DC conductivity from lattice Euclidean correlators.
