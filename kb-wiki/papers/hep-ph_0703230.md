# QCD Viscosity to Entropy Density Ratio in the Hadronic Phase

> Shear viscosity (eta) of QCD in the hadronic phase is computed by the coupled Boltzmann equations of pions and nucleons in low temperatures and low baryon number densities. The eta to entropy density ratio eta/s maps out the nuclear gas-liquid phase transition by forming a valley tracing the phase transition line in the temperature-chemical potential plane. When the phase transition turns into a crossover, the eta/s valley gradually disappears. We suspect the general feature for a first-order phase transition is that eta/s has a discontinuity in the bottom of the eta/s valley. The discontinuity coincides with the phase transition line and ends at the critical point. Beyond the critical point, a smooth eta/s valley is seen. However, the valley could disappear further away from the critical point. The eta/s measurements might provide an alternative to identify the critical points.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) in the hadronic phase of QCD forms a valley that traces the nuclear gas-liquid phase transition line in the temperature-chemical potential plane; the authors suspect that for a first-order phase transition, η/s has a discontinuity at the bottom of this valley that coincides with the phase transition line and ends at the critical point, but their calculation using free-particle entropy and Boltzmann equations does not capture this discontinuity.

## Method

The authors solved coupled linearized Boltzmann equations for pions and nucleons using leading-order chiral perturbation theory for ππ and πN scattering amplitudes, and effective range expansion for NN scattering amplitudes. The shear viscosity was computed by expanding the deviation functions B^l(p) in orthogonal polynomials and solving the resulting matrix equation, while the entropy density was computed as a free gas of pions and nucleons using the partition function.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass m_π = 139 MeV, nucleon mass m_N = 938 MeV, pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV
- Spin/isospin degeneracy factors: g_π = 3 for pions, g_N = 4 for nucleons
- NN scattering parameters: a_1 = -17.9 fm (singlet scattering length), a_3 = 5.42 fm (triplet scattering length), r_1 = 2.77 fm, r_3 = 1.76 fm
- η/s is μ-independent for μ < 500 MeV, with nucleon contributions dominating when μ > 800 MeV
- The η/s valley gradually disappears at around T ~ 30 MeV when the phase transition turns into a crossover
- The critical point is estimated at T ~ 10-15 MeV, consistent with experimental extractions of 7-16 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-chemical-potential calculation (Chen 2006) to finite baryon chemical potential μ, and builds on observations by Csernai (2006) that η/s has a cusp at the critical temperature for water, helium, and nitrogen below the critical pressure. The results suggest that η/s measurements in heavy-ion collisions might provide an alternative method to identify QCD critical points, complementing lattice QCD approaches which suffer from the fermion sign problem at finite μ. The authors note that their free-particle treatment of entropy is poor near the phase transition, and that the regime near μ ≈ m_N is dominated by nucleon degrees of freedom, exhibiting universal properties shared with cold fermionic atoms near Feshbach resonances.
