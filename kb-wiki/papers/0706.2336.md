# Testing AdS/CFT Deviations from pQCD Heavy Quark Energy Loss with Pb+Pb at LHC

> Heavy quark jet quenching in nuclear collisions at LHC is predicted and compared using the classical gravity AdS/CFT correspondence and Standard Model perturbative QCD. The momentum independence and inverse quark mass dependence of the drag coefficient in AdS/CFT differs substantially from the characteristic log(pT/M)/pT variation of the drag in QCD. We propose that the measurement of the momentum dependence of the double ratio of the nuclear modification factors of charm and bottom jets is a robust observable that can be used to search for strong coupling deviations from perturbative QCD predictions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the momentum dependence of the double ratio of charm to bottom jet nuclear modification factors (R_AA^c / R_AA^b) can robustly distinguish between AdS/CFT-based and perturbative QCD predictions for heavy quark energy loss at LHC, provided that the AdS/CFT drag coefficient formula (which assumes infinite coupling, large N_c, and M_Q >> T^*) is a valid approximation for QCD.

## Method

The authors computed nuclear modification factors R_AA^Q for charm and bottom quarks in central Pb+Pb collisions at 5.5 ATeV using two approaches: (1) the AdS/CFT drag coefficient mu_Q = pi sqrt(lambda) (T^*)^2 / (2 M_Q) from Herzog (2006) and Gubser (2006), with energy loss modeled as dpt/dt = -mu_Q pt; and (2) the WHDG pQCD model (Wicks, 2005) convolving elastic and inelastic parton energy loss with fluctuating path geometry. Both calculations included Bjorken 1D Hubble flow expansion, binary collision geometry averaging, and isentropic temperature evolution from initial gluon rapidity densities.

## Key numbers

* AdS/CFT drag coefficient: dpt/dt = -mu_Q pt with mu_Q = pi sqrt(lambda) (T^*)^2 / (2 M_Q), giving inverse quark mass dependence and no explicit pt dependence.
* pQCD average fractional energy loss in static uniform plasma: bar-epsilon approximately kappa L^2 q-hat log(pt/M_Q)/pt, giving asymptotic suppression that vanishes at high pt.
* AdS/CFT diffusion coefficient: D/2pi T^* = 4/sqrt(lambda); using the "obvious" prescription with alpha_SYM = 0.3 gives D/2pi T ~ 1.2, while D/2pi T = 3 (claimed to fit PHENIX data) requires alpha_SYM ~ 0.05.
* AdS/CFT double ratio approximation: R^cb_AdS approximately M_c/M_b ~ 0.26, with all lambda, T^*, L, and spectral index dependences canceling.
* pQCD double ratio approximation: R^cb_pQCD approximately 1 - p_cb/pt, where p_cb = kappa n(pt) L^2 log(M_b/M_c) q-hat.
* AdS/CFT speed limit for applicability: gamma_c = (1 + 2M/(sqrt(lambda) T^*))^2 approximately 4M^2/(lambda (T^*)^2), giving a pt cutoff that depends on quark mass and temperature.

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT heavy quark drag calculations of Herzog (2006), Gubser (2006), and Gubser (2007) to LHC phenomenology, and contrasts them with the pQCD energy loss framework of WHDG (Wicks, 2005) and earlier work by Dokshitzer (2001) and Djordjevic (2005). The paper was motivated by the failure of pQCD models to quantitatively account for RHIC nonphotonic electron data from STAR (Abelev, 2006) and PHENIX (Adare, 2006), and proposes the double ratio as a robust observable that cancels normalization uncertainties and amplifies the qualitative difference between the two theoretical approaches. The measurement requires identified charm and bottom jets at LHC and would be complemented by p+A control experiments to deconvolute initial state effects.
