# Dispersion Relations and the Nucleon Polarizability

> Recent experimental results on the proton and neutron polarizabilities are examined from the point of view of backward dispersion relations. Results are found to be in reasonable agreement with the measured values. A rigorous relationship between the nucleon and pion polarizabilities is derived and shown to be in excellent agreement with several models.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a simple valence quark model of the nucleon fails to explain the experimental finding that the neutron's electric polarizability is larger than the proton's, and that including mesonic (pion cloud) degrees of freedom is required; dispersion relations provide a rigorous framework that yields results consistent with experiment, but the evaluation depends on the correctness of the s- and t-channel integrands.

## Method

The authors evaluate the backward dispersion sum rule for the difference of the electric and magnetic polarizabilities (ᾱ_E − β̄_M) of the proton and neutron. The s-channel contribution is computed using a multipole decomposition of single-pion photoproduction from the VPI&SU group and a model for multi-pion final states from L'vov, while the t-channel contribution is evaluated using amplitudes for ππ → N̄N from Bohannon and Signell and for γγ → ππ from a dispersive/chiral analysis. The Baldin-Lapidus forward sum rule for ᾱ_E + β̄_M is also used to extract individual polarizabilities and their neutron-proton differences.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric polarizability: ᾱ_E^p = (11.3 ± 0.7 ± 0.8) × 10^{-4} fm^3; magnetic: β̄_M^p = (2.9 ∓ 0.7 ∓ 0.8) × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Neutron electric polarizability: ᾱ_E^n = (12.6 ± 1.5 ± 2.0) × 10^{-4} fm^3; magnetic: β̄_M^n = (3.2 ∓ 1.5 ∓ 2.0) × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Baldin-Lapidus sum rule: ᾱ_E + β̄_M = (14.2 ± 0.5) × 10^{-4} fm^3 for proton, (15.8 ± 0.5) × 10^{-4} fm^3 for neutron
- Dispersion relation prediction for neutron-proton electric polarizability difference: ᾱ_E^n − ᾱ_E^p = 1.2 × 10^{-4} fm^3, compared to experimental value 1.3 ± 1.9 × 10^{-4} fm^3 and valence quark model prediction −3.6 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Chiral prediction for charged pion electric polarizability: ᾱ_E^π = 2.8 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Dispersion relation result for the pion contribution to the nucleon polarizability: δᾱ_E^N = −δβ̄_M^N = 0.5 ᾱ_E^π

## Context

This work extends earlier dispersion relation analyses of nucleon polarizabilities by Bernabeu and Tarrach (1974), Guiasu and Radescu (1978), and Budnev and Karnakov (1979), using improved data on γγ → ππ amplitudes and a new multipole analysis of single-pion photoproduction. The paper contradicts the simple valence quark model prediction of Frishman (1975) that the proton electric polarizability should exceed the neutron's, and instead supports chiral perturbation theory calculations by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1991) that include mesonic degrees of freedom. The derived relation between nucleon and pion polarizabilities enables future experimental tests of the pion polarizability, with planned measurements at Brookhaven, Fermilab, and DaΦne.
