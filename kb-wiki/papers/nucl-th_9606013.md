# Influence of the pion-nucleon interaction on the collective pion flow in heavy ion reactions

> We investigate the influence of the real part of the in-medium pion optical potential on the pion dynamics in intermediate energy heavy ion reactions at 1 GeV/A. For different models, i.e. a phenomenological model and the $Δ$--hole model, a pionic potential is extracted from the dispersion relation and used in Quantum Molecular Dynamics calculations. In addition with the inelastic scattering processes we thus take care of both, real and imaginary part of the pion optical potential. A strong influence of the real pionic potential on the pion in-plane flow is observed. In general such a potential has the tendency to reduce the anticorrelation of pion and nucleon flow in non-central collisions.

## Key claim

The real part of the in-medium pion optical potential has a strong influence on the collective pion in-plane flow in non-central heavy ion collisions at 1 GeV/A, generally reducing the anticorrelation of pion and nucleon flow; however, the magnitude of this effect depends on which model (a soft Δ–hole model or a stronger phenomenological potential) is used, and the authors state that no final conclusions can be drawn from the present pion flow data.

## Method

The authors performed Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) simulations of Ca+Ca collisions at 1 GeV/A, including Δ(1232) and N*(1440) resonances explicitly. They extracted the real part of the pion optical potential from two different in-medium dispersion relations: a Δ–hole model and a phenomenological ansatz by Gale and Kapusta (1987). The pions were propagated between collisions under the influence of this potential, while the imaginary part was treated via inelastic scattering processes, and the nuclear mean field was described by a soft momentum-dependent Skyrme force.

## Key numbers

- The system studied was Ca+Ca at 1 GeV/A, with impact parameters varied to study non-central collisions.
- For central La+La collisions at 1.35 GeV/A, the π⁻ energy spectra were compared to BEVELAC data; the inclusion of a pionic potential improved agreement at low energies.
- The directed transverse momentum p^{dir}_x = (1/N) Σ_i sign(Y_{CM}^i) p_x^i was shown as a function of impact parameter for Ca+Ca at 1 GeV/A, demonstrating negative directed flow (anticorrelation) in non-central collisions that is reduced by the attractive potential.
- The phenomenological potential (Pot.2) was strong enough to convert the anticorrelation into a correlation, while the Δ–hole potential (Pot.1) produced a weaker effect.
- The total pion multiplicities were overestimated by about 20% in the calculations, a common feature of transport calculations according to the authors.

## Context

This work extends earlier transport calculations (Li, 1991; Bauer, 1995) that included only the imaginary part of the pion optical potential via collision processes, which qualitatively reproduced DIOGENE data but underpredicted the total amount of flow (Li, 1991; Hartnack, 1988). The study also builds on measurements of pion in-plane flow by the DIOGENE group (Ne+Pb at 800 MeV/A) and squeeze-out by the TAPS collaboration (Brill et al.). The results open the possibility to extract information about the pion dispersion relation from pion flow data, but the authors note that more refined measurements from the FOPI collaboration are needed before quantitative statements can be made.
