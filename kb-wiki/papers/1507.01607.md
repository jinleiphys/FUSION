# The role of spin-flipping terms in hadronic transitions of $Υ(4S)$

> Recent experimental data on the $Υ(4S)\toΥ(1S)η$ and $Υ(4S)\to h_{b}(1P)η$ processes seem to contradict the naive expectation that hadronic transitions with spin-flipping terms should be suppressed with respect those without spin-flip. We analyze these transitions using the QCD Multipole Expansion (QCDME) approach and within a constituent quark model framework that has been applied successfully to the heavy-quark sectors during the last years. The QCDME formalism requires the computation of hybrid intermediate states which has been performed in a natural, parameter-free extension of our constituent quark model based on the Quark Confining String (QCS) scheme. We show that i) the M1-M1 contribution in the decay rate of the $Υ(4S)\toΥ(1S)η$ is important and its supression until now is not justified; ii) the role played by the $L=0$ hybrid states, which enter in the calculation of the M1-M1 contribution, explains the enhancement in the $Υ(4S)\toΥ(1S)η$ decay rate; and iii) the anomalously large decay rate of the $Υ(4S)\to h_{b}(1P)η$ process has the same physical origin.

## Key claim

The anomalously large decay rates of $\Upsilon(4S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\eta$ and $\Upsilon(4S)\to h_{b}(1P)\eta$ are explained by the previously neglected M1-M1 chromomagnetic multipole contribution, which is enhanced because a predicted $L=0$ hybrid meson at 10571 MeV lies very close in mass to the $\Upsilon(4S)$ at 10607 MeV.

## Method

The authors use the QCD Multipole Expansion (QCDME) formalism within a constituent quark model that describes both conventional bottomonium and hybrid mesons. Hybrid intermediate states are computed via a parameter-free extension of the quark model based on the Quark Confining String (QCS) scheme, where gluon excitations are modeled as vibrational modes of the string connecting the quark and antiquark. The hadronization of gluons into the $\eta$ meson is treated using the 2-gluon emission approximation (H2-approach), which allows consistent inclusion of both M1-M1 and E1-M2 multipole contributions.

## Key numbers

- Predicted mass of the $|KL\rangle=|10\rangle$ hybrid meson: 10571 MeV, very close to the $\Upsilon(4S)$ mass of 10607 MeV (CQM prediction)
- $\Gamma(\Upsilon(4S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\eta) = 6.05$ keV (H2-approach), compared to experimental $(4.02\pm0.76)$ keV
- $R_{\eta}[\Upsilon(4S)] = \Gamma(\Upsilon(4S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\eta) / \Gamma(\Upsilon(4S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\pi^{+}\pi^{-}) = 2.38$ (H2-approach), compared to experimental $2.42\pm0.39$
- $\Gamma(\Upsilon(4S)\to h_{b}(1P)\eta) = 37.89$ keV, compared to experimental $(44.69\pm6.95)$ keV
- $\Gamma(\Upsilon(2S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\pi^{+}\pi^{-}) = 5.71$ keV (used to fix constants, matches experimental $5.71\pm0.48$ keV)
- $\Gamma(\Upsilon(2S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\eta) = 9.27\times10^{-3}$ keV (used to fix constants, matches experimental $(9.27\pm1.49)\times10^{-3}$ keV)

## Context

This work extends the QCDME analysis of hadronic transitions previously applied to the charmonium sector by Segovia et al. (2014), where two-pion spin-nonflip transitions were successfully described. It contradicts the naive expectation that spin-flipping M1-M1 contributions should be negligible compared to E1-M2 terms, a suppression that was assumed in earlier QCDME treatments such as the Kuang-Yan model. The results enable a consistent explanation of both the $\Upsilon(4S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\eta$ and $\Upsilon(4S)\to h_{b}(1P)\eta$ anomalies through the same mechanism of near-threshold $L=0$ hybrid states, and provide a testable prediction for the $\Upsilon(3S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\eta$ decay rate that awaits more precise experimental determination.
