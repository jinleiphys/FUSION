# Medium effects on $ﾎ杰-$ production in the nuclear ($K^-$, $K^+$) reaction

> We study theoretically medium effects on $ﾎ杰-$ production in the ($K^-$, $K^+$) reaction, using the optimal Fermi-averaging procedure which describes the Fermi motion of a nucleon on the on-energy-shell $K^-p\to K^+ﾎ杰-$ reaction condition in nuclei. The result shows the strong energy and angular dependence of the in-medium $K^-p\to K^+ﾎ杰-$ cross section,which affects significantly the shape and magnitude of the production spectrum for $ﾎ杰-$ hypernuclear states in the ($K^-$, $K^+$) reaction on a nuclear target.The application to the $ﾎ杰-$ quasi-free production via the ($K^-$, $K^+$) reaction on a $^{12}$C target is also discussed in a Fermi gas model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that medium effects on the $K^-p \to K^+\Xi^-$ reaction in nuclei, modeled via the optimal Fermi-averaging procedure, produce a strong energy and angular dependence of the in-medium cross section that significantly modifies the shape and magnitude of the $\Xi^-$ production spectrum; however, this claim is conditional on the use of the on-energy-shell condition for the struck nucleon and on the specific parametrization of the elementary $K^-p \to K^+\Xi^-$ angular distributions, which themselves have limited experimental data.

## Method

The authors employed the optimal Fermi-averaging procedure, which imposes the on-energy-shell condition for the struck proton in the nucleus via Eq. (7), to compute the in-medium $K^-p \to K^+\Xi^-$ $t$ matrix and cross section. They parametrized the elementary $K^-p \to K^+\Xi^-$ differential cross sections in the center-of-mass frame using Legendre polynomial expansions up to $\ell_{\rm max}=6$, fitting 242 angular-distribution data points and 9 total-cross-section data points. The resulting optimal Fermi-averaged cross sections were then applied to calculate the $\Xi^-$ quasi-free production spectrum on a $^{12}$C target at $p_{K^-}=1.8$ GeV/$c$ using a non-relativistic Fermi gas model with eikonal distortion.

## Key numbers

- The elementary $K^-p \to K^+\Xi^-$ differential cross section data were fitted at nine center-of-mass energies: 1.948, 1.966, 2.069, 2.110, 2.134, 2.235, 2.276, 2.331, and 2.420 GeV.
- The fit achieved $\chi^2/N = 1.48$ for $N=242$ angular-distribution data points and $\chi^2/N = 2.35$ for $N=56$ total-cross-section data points.
- For the $^{12}$C target at $p_{K^-}=1.8$ GeV/$c$ and $\theta_{\rm lab}=4^\circ$, the quasi-free peak is located at $\omega \simeq 480$ MeV (about 80 MeV above the $\Xi^-$ emitted threshold) with a width $2\Delta \simeq 150$ MeV.
- For $\theta_{\rm lab}=8^\circ$, the quasi-free peak shifts to $\omega \simeq 500$ MeV with a width $2\Delta \simeq 200$ MeV.
- The effective number of protons for $^{12}$C was $Z_{\rm eff}=2.09$, using $\bar{\sigma}=24.2$ mb ($\sigma_{K^-N}=28.9$ mb, $\sigma_{K^+N}=19.4$ mb) and nuclear MHO density parameters $a=2.234$, $d=1.516$ fm.
- The Fermi momentum was taken as $k_F=270$ MeV/$c$, with well depths $V_N=50$ MeV and $V_\Xi=14$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the optimal Fermi-averaging procedure to $\Sigma^-$ production in ($\pi^-$, $K^+$) reactions (Harada 2005, 2006, 2018) to the $\Xi^-$ production channel in ($K^-$, $K^+$) reactions. It contrasts with the ordinary Fermi averaging used in standard DWIA calculations (Auerbach 1983, Dover 1980, Bando 1990) and with the optimal momentum approximation (Gurvitz 1986, Zofka 1984, Sotona 1989), which the authors argue is insufficient for capturing the energy and angular dependence of the elementary amplitude. The results are intended to inform the ongoing analysis of the J-PARC E05 experiment (Nagae 2018) and to enable more reliable extraction of the $\Xi$-nucleus potential from measured spectra, complementing earlier work by Maekawa (2007), Hashimoto (2008), and Kohno (2019) using semiclassical distorted wave methods.
