# Exact treatment of Pauli exclusion effects in (p,p') reactions

> This paper presents the first calculations of proton inelastic scattering in which the medium effect of Pauli blocking is included through an exact, rather than angle averaged, operator. This improvement is important in the isoscalar channel at proton energies near 100 MeV and fades as the energy rises. However, processes that emphasize finite-range exchange (such as $0^+\to 0^-$ reactions) still see significant effects at 200 MeV. The results depend on the directions of the incident and struck nucleon momenta that produce the most important contributions to the DWBA integral at each scattering angle.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first calculations of proton inelastic scattering where Pauli blocking is treated with an exact, rather than angle-averaged, operator; the improvement is important in the isoscalar channel near 100 MeV and fades as energy rises, but processes emphasizing finite-range exchange (such as 0+ to 0- reactions) still show significant effects at 200 MeV.

## Method

The authors computed G-matrix elements from a modified Bonn-B potential using the Bethe-Goldstone equation with an exact Pauli projection operator that retains the full angular dependence of the step functions on the Fermi sphere, rather than averaging over the intermediate-state scattering angle. They transformed these partial-wave matrix elements into NN scattering amplitudes via an expansion in L (where L=0 recovers the spherical Pauli result and L=2 terms were included), then fitted the amplitudes to a sum of Yukawa functions for use in distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations. Calculations were performed for two choices of the system momentum direction: along the incident projectile direction (theta_P=0) and at half the scattering angle (theta_P=theta/2), using the distorted-wave programs LEA and DWBA86.

## Key numbers

- For the 2+ state at 6.917 MeV in 16O at 200 MeV, Pauli blocking effects from the spherical operator are noticeable only at the largest angles in the analyzing power; replacing with the exact operator produces only small additional changes.
- At 100 MeV for the same 2+ state, the change from spherical to exact Pauli operator makes a much larger difference, especially for the cross section.
- For the 0- state at 10.957 MeV in 16O at 200 MeV, the cross section with theta_P=theta/2 is substantially different from the theta_P=0 calculation, demonstrating sensitivity to the system momentum choice for exchange-sensitive transitions.
- The G-matrix calculations included partial waves up to J,J'=6 for medium effects, with spherical Pauli operator values used for 7 <= J <= 15; no values for J>15 were considered.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous G-matrix calculations (Sammarruca et al., 2000) where new couplings in J and l from the exact Pauli operator were observed, and addresses the question of whether these differences can be observed in selected nuclear reactions. The paper builds on systematic studies (von Geramb, 1983; Rikus et al., 1984; Nakayama and Love, 1988; Amos et al., 2000) showing Pauli blocking is important at lower energies, and on earlier work (Cheon and Redish, 1989; Schiller et al., 1999; Suzuki et al., 2000) that considered the spherical approximation. The results enable more precise treatment of medium effects in (p,p') reactions and show that the exact treatment is necessary near 100 MeV to avoid systematic errors that could affect interpretation of other medium effects such as relativistic mean fields and Delta-resonance coupling.
