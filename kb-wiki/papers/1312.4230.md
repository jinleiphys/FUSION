# Onset of radial flow in p+p collisions

> It has been debated for decades whether hadrons emerging from p+p collisions exhibit collective expansion. The signal of the collective motion in p+p collisions is not as clear/clean as in heavy-ion collisions because of the low multiplicity and large fluctuation in p+p collisions. Tsallis Blast-Wave (TBW) model is a thermodynamic approach, introduced to handle the overwhelming correlation and fluctuation in the hadronic processes. We have systematically studied the identified particle spectra in p+p collisions from RHIC to LHC using TBW and found no appreciable radial flow in p+p collisions below $\sqrt{s}=900$ GeV. At LHC higher energy of 7 TeV in p+p collisions, the radial flow velocity achieves an average value of $<β>= 0.320\pm0.005$. This flow velocity is comparable to that in peripheral (40-60%) Au+Au collisions at RHIC. Breaking of the identified particle spectra $m_T$ scaling was also observed at LHC from a model independent test.

## Key claim

The paper claims that radial flow in minimum-bias p+p collisions is negligible at center-of-mass energies below 900 GeV, but becomes appreciable at 7 TeV with an average radial flow velocity comparable to that in peripheral Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The hedge is that the extraction is model-dependent, relying on the Tsallis Blast-Wave (TBW) framework, and that the signal is not as clean as in heavy-ion collisions due to low multiplicity and large fluctuations.

## Method

The authors performed simultaneous Tsallis Blast-Wave (TBW) model fits to identified particle transverse momentum spectra (pions, kaons, protons, lambdas, xi baryons, and inclusive charged hadrons) from p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, 540 GeV, 900 GeV, and 7 TeV. The TBW model incorporates non-extensive Tsallis statistics with a blast-wave expansion, using four common parameters: average radial flow velocity <beta>, temperature T, and separate non-extensivity parameters q_M for mesons and q_B for baryons. The fits were performed over a broad transverse momentum range (0-10 GeV/c) using published data from STAR, PHENIX, UA1, UA2, UA5, E735, CMS, and ALICE.

## Key numbers

*   Average radial flow velocity in p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV: <beta> = 0.320 +/- 0.005.
*   Average radial flow velocity in p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 900 GeV: <beta> = 0.264 +/- 0.005.
*   Average radial flow velocity in p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 540 GeV: <beta> = 0.000 (+0.105, -0.000).
*   Average radial flow velocity in p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV: <beta> = 0.000 (+0.124, -0.000).
*   Temperature T extracted from fits: 70.3 +/- 0.8 MeV at 7 TeV; 74.6 +/- 0.5 MeV at 900 GeV; 81.8 +/- 0.6 MeV at 540 GeV; 92.3 +/- 2.7 MeV at 200 GeV.
*   The radial flow velocity at 7 TeV (0.320) is comparable to that in peripheral (40-60%) Au+Au collisions at RHIC (0.282 +/- 0.017, from Tang (2008)).
*   Chi-squared per degree of freedom for the simultaneous fits: 490/431 at 7 TeV; 545/501 at 900 GeV; 205/168 at 540 GeV; 268/268 at 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the debate on whether p+p collisions exhibit collective expansion, a topic previously addressed by hydrodynamic simulations and models such as the color glass condensate (CGC) approach of Dumitru (2010) and the holographic pomeron model of Shuryak (2013). The finding of an onset of radial flow above 900 GeV provides a benchmark for testing the applicability of hydrodynamics to small systems, and the TBW framework offers a model-dependent method to extract flow velocity from data that can be compared with more fundamental QCD theory or hydrodynamic simulations. The observed breaking of m_T scaling at high energies, attributed to radial flow, enables future studies of the relationship between radial flow and azimuthal correlations in p+p collisions.
