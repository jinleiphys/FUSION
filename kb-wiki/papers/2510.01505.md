# Singly heavy tetraquark resonant states with multiple strange quarks

> We systematically investigate the S-wave singly heavy tetraquark systems containing two or three strange quarks, $Qs\bar{s}\bar{s}$, $Qn\bar{s}\bar{s}$ and $Qs\bar{s}\bar{n}\left( Q=c,b,n=u,d \right) $, within the constituent quark potential model. We solve the four-body Schrödinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) and identify resonances via the complex scaling method (CSM). There are no bound states below the lowest two-meson thresholds. We obtain several compact resonances with $J^P=0^+,2^+$ in $Qs\bar{s}\bar{s}$, and $J^P=2^+$ in $Qn\bar{s}\bar{s}$ and $Qs\bar{s}\bar{n}$. The pole positions are mainly distributed around $7.0-7.2$ GeV (bottom) and $3.7-3.9$ GeV (charm), with widths from a few to several tens of MeV. These resonances decay into $D_sη^\prime ,{D_{(s)}^*}ϕ,{D_s}^*K^*$ and $D_s^*\bar{K}^*$ (and their bottom counterparts), providing targets for future experimental searches.

## Key claim

The paper systematically investigates S-wave singly heavy tetraquark systems with two or three strange quarks and finds no bound states below the lowest two-meson thresholds, only compact resonances; the abstract's claim of "several compact resonances" is qualified by the fact that resonances are only found for specific quantum numbers ($J^P=0^+,2^+$ in $Qs\bar{s}\bar{s}$, and $J^P=2^+$ in $Qn\bar{s}\bar{s}$ and $Qs\bar{s}\bar{n}$) and that no resonances are identified for $J^P=1^+$ in any system.

## Method

The authors employ the AL1 constituent quark potential model, solving the four-body Schrodinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) with S-wave bases and multiple Jacobi coordinate structures (diquark-antidiquark and dimeson). Resonances are identified via the complex scaling method (CSM), which rotates coordinates and momenta by an angle theta to make resonant wave functions square-integrable, allowing their extraction as complex eigenvalues $E_R = M_R - i\Gamma_R/2$ that remain invariant under changes in the rotation angle.

## Key numbers

- $bs\bar{s}\bar{s}$ $J^P=0^+$ resonances: $7060-25i$ MeV (width 50 MeV) and $7108-2i$ MeV (width 4 MeV)
- $bs\bar{s}\bar{s}$ $J^P=2^+$ resonances: $7210-24i$ MeV (width 48 MeV) and $7223-2i$ MeV (width 4 MeV)
- $cs\bar{s}\bar{s}$ $J^P=0^+$ resonances: $3726-12i$ MeV (width 24 MeV) and $3763-5i$ MeV (width 10 MeV)
- $cs\bar{s}\bar{s}$ $J^P=2^+$ resonances: $3888-11i$ MeV (width 22 MeV) and $3923-1i$ MeV (width 2 MeV)
- $bn\bar{s}\bar{s}$ $J^P=2^+$ resonances: $7042-29i$ MeV (width 58 MeV) and $7146-1i$ MeV (width 2 MeV)
- $cn\bar{s}\bar{s}$ $J^P=2^+$ resonances: $3736-27i$ MeV (width 54 MeV) and $3858-1i$ MeV (width 2 MeV)
- Pole positions are mainly distributed around $7.0-7.2$ GeV for bottom systems and $3.7-3.9$ GeV for charm systems

## Context

This work extends earlier quark model studies of singly heavy tetraquarks by Ebert (2010), Lu (2016), Lu (2020), Guo (2021), and Jalili (2023), which predicted $bs\bar{s}\bar{s}$ masses in the 6.0-6.9 GeV range and $cs\bar{s}\bar{s}$ masses in the 2.6-3.6 GeV range, but those studies could only treat bound states and may have misidentified continuum excitations as low-lying levels. The use of complex scaling method enables clear isolation of resonance poles above thresholds, pushing predicted masses higher (above 7.0 GeV for bottom and above 3.7 GeV for charm), and provides specific decay channels ($D_s\eta^\prime$, $D_{(s)}^*\phi$, $D_s^*K^*$, $D_s^*\bar{K}^*$ and their bottom counterparts) as targets for future experimental searches at LHCb and other facilities.
