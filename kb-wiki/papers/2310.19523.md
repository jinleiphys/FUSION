# First-forbidden transition of nuclear $β$ decay by projected shell model

> The first-forbidden transition of nuclear $β$ decay is expected to play crucial roles in many aspects in nuclear physics, nuclear astrophysics and particle physics such as the stellar $β$-decay rates and the reactor anti-neutrino spectra. In this work we develop the projected shell model (PSM) for description of first-forbidden transition of nuclear $β$ decay for the first time. Detailed theoretical framework and logics are provided, and 35 dominant first-forbidden transitions that are expected to be important for the reactor anti-neutrino spectra problems are calculated and compared systematically with the data to test the new development of the PSM. The corresponding experimental Log$f_0 t$ values are described reasonably, and the quenching factors of nuclear matrix elements are found to affect the Log$f_0 t$ values as well as the related shape factors, which may be helpful for better understanding of the reactor anti-neutrino spectra problems.

## Key claim

The projected shell model (PSM) is extended to describe first-forbidden transitions of nuclear beta decay for the first time, and the calculated Log$f_0 t$ values for 35 dominant transitions relevant to reactor anti-neutrino spectra agree reasonably with experimental data, though the quenching factors of nuclear matrix elements significantly affect both the Log$f_0 t$ values and the shape factors.

## Method

The authors develop the theoretical framework for first-forbidden transitions within the PSM by implementing angular-momentum projection techniques and calculating the reduced nuclear matrix elements for the transition operators using a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with large configuration spaces of multi-quasiparticle states. The shape factor coefficients $k$, $a$, $b$, and $c$ are computed from the reduced matrix elements, and the comparative half-lives Log$f_0 t$ are obtained by integrating the phase-space integrals with and without the shape factor. Calculations are performed both without quenching factors ($f_q=1$) and with the shell-model quenching factors from Zhi et al. (2013) for the various matrix elements.

## Key numbers

* The phase-space integral constant $K_0 = 6146$ s is used for the $ft$ relation.
* The weak axial coupling constant is taken as $g_A = -1.2701(25)$.
* For $^{89}$Br ($5/2^- \rightarrow 5/2^+$, $Q_\beta = 8.26$ MeV, $E_f = 0.03$ MeV), the experimental Log$f_0 t$ is 6.5; PSM without quenching gives 6.96 and with SM quenching gives 6.57.
* For $^{90}$Rb ($0^- \rightarrow 0^+$, $Q_\beta = 6.58$ MeV, $E_f = 0$ MeV), the experimental Log$f_0 t$ is 7.35; PSM without quenching gives 9.04 and with SM quenching gives 8.26.
* For $^{91}$Kr ($5/2^+ \rightarrow 5/2^-$, $Q_\beta = 6.77$ MeV, $E_f = 0.11$ MeV), the experimental Log$f_0 t$ is 6.36; PSM without quenching gives 6.98 and with SM quenching gives 6.40.
* For $^{92}$Rb ($0^- \rightarrow 0^+$, $Q_\beta = 8.10$ MeV, $E_f = 0$ MeV), the experimental Log$f_0 t$ is 5.75; PSM without quenching gives 7.63 and with SM quenching gives 6.71.
* For $^{93}$Rb ($5/2^- \rightarrow 5/2^+$, $Q_\beta = 7.47$ MeV, $E_f = 0$ MeV), the experimental Log$f_0 t$ is 6.14; PSM without quenching gives 7.14 and with SM quenching gives 6.87.
* For $^{94}$Y ($2^- \rightarrow 2^+$, $Q_\beta = 4.92$ MeV, $E_f = 0.92$ MeV), the experimental Log$f_0 t$ is 6.5; PSM without quenching gives 7.03 and with SM quenching gives 6.68.

## Context

This work extends the PSM framework, previously developed for allowed Gamow-Teller transitions and stellar weak-interaction rates by Gao et al. (2006), Wang et al. (2018), and Chen et al. (2023), to first-forbidden transitions for the first time. It directly addresses the reactor anti-neutrino anomaly and spectral shoulder problems identified by Fallot et al. (2012) and Hayen et al. (2019), where dominant first-forbidden transitions are expected to mitigate the discrepancies between experimental anti-neutrino count rates and theoretical predictions. The development enables systematic calculations of first-forbidden transitions for heavier nuclei where such transitions become the major decay mode, complementing existing shell-model and quasiparticle random phase approximation approaches used by Zhi et al. (2013), Marketin et al. (2016), and Sharma et al. (2022).
