# The Equation of State for Two Flavor QCD at Non-zero Chemical Potential

> We present results of a simulation of QCD on a 4x16^3 lattice with 2 continuum flavors of p4-improved staggered fermion with mass m/T=0.4. Derivatives of the thermodynamic grand potential with respect to quark chemical potential mu_q up to fourth order are calculated, enabling estimates of the pressure, quark number density and associated susceptibilities as functions of mu_q via Taylor series expansion. Discretisation effects associated with various staggered fermion formulations are discussed in some detail. In addition it is possible to estimate the radius of convergence of the expansion as a function of temperature. We also discuss the calculation of energy and entropy densities which are defined via mixed derivatives of the thermodynamic grand potential with respect to the bare couplings and quark masses.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Taylor expansion of the QCD grand potential up to fourth order in the quark chemical potential, enabling estimates of the pressure, quark number density, and susceptibilities at non-zero chemical potential, but the expansion is strictly valid only along lines of fixed bare mass and fixed m/T, and the results for energy and entropy densities are considerably harder to calculate in the critical region due to the presence of a critical singularity.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations on a 16^3×4 lattice using Symanzik improved gauge and p4-improved staggered fermion actions with two continuum flavors and bare quark mass ma=0.1 (corresponding to m/T=0.4). They calculated derivatives of the thermodynamic grand potential with respect to quark chemical potential up to fourth order at μ_q=0, then used these derivatives to construct Taylor series expansions for the pressure, quark number density, and susceptibilities as functions of μ_q/T. The cutoff dependence of the expansion coefficients was analyzed analytically for standard, Naik, and p4-improved staggered fermion actions in the free gas limit.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: 16^3×4, with temporal extent N_τ=4
- Bare quark mass: ma=0.1, corresponding to m/T=0.4
- Pseudocritical point at zero chemical potential: β_c≈3.649(2)
- Continuum Stefan-Boltzmann coefficients for massless quarks: C_0=7π^2/60, C_2=1/2, C_4=1/(4π^2)
- Continuum coefficients for m/T=0.4: C_0=1.113632, C_2=4.880455×10^{-1}, C_4=2.531101×10^{-2}, C_6=1.877659×10^{-6}
- For the standard action, C_6≈-0.015 N_τ^{-2} in the massless case
- The curvature of the phase transition line from previous work: T_c(d^2T_c/dμ_q^2)|_{μ_q=0}≈-0.14(6)
- The μ_q/T expansion up to O((μ_q/T)^4) is sufficient for μ_q/T<1 and m/T<1, with continuum deviation smaller than 0.01%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hybrid reweighting-Taylor approach (Allton et al., 2002) by calculating derivatives to fourth order entirely within the analytic framework, rather than using reweighting. It builds on earlier lattice QCD studies of non-zero chemical potential using reweighting methods (Fodor and Katz, 2002) and analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potential (de Forcrand and Philipsen, 2002). The results enable estimates of the radius of convergence of the Taylor expansion as a function of temperature and confirm that the critical line curvature from the previous study can be trusted out to O(100 MeV) near T_c, with a considerably larger radius of convergence expected at higher temperatures.
