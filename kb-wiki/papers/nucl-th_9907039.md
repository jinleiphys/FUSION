# Color plasma oscillation in strangelets

> The dispersion relation and damping rate of longitudinal color plasmons in finite strange quark matter (strangelets) are evaluated in the limits of weak coupling, low temperature, and long wavelength. The property of the QCD vacuum surrounding a strangelet makes the frequency of the plasmons nearly the same as the color plasma frequency of bulk matter. The plasmons are damped by their coupling with individual excitations of particle-hole pairs of quarks, of which the energy levels are discretized by the boundary. For strangelets of macroscopic size, the lifetime of the plasmons is found to be proportional to the size, as in the case of the usual plasma oscillations in metal nanoparticles.

## Key claim

The frequency of longitudinal color plasmons in finite strange quark matter (strangelets) is nearly identical to the color plasma frequency of bulk quark matter, because the QCD vacuum surrounding the strangelet has a vanishing color dielectric constant, which eliminates surface color charge effects that would otherwise modify the frequency.

## Method

The author calculated the one-loop (random-phase approximation) proper polarization function for longitudinal color oscillations in a spherical strangelet, incorporating discrete quark energy levels from the MIT bag model boundary conditions. The dispersion relation and damping rate were then evaluated in the limits of weak coupling, low temperature, and long wavelength, with the damping arising from resonant energy transfer into discrete particle-hole pair excitations of quarks.

## Key numbers

- Chemical potential set to μ = 300 MeV for all quark flavors, with u and d quarks taken as massless (m_u = m_d = 0) and strange quark mass m_s ranging from 0 to 300 MeV
- The color plasma frequency ω_p = √(∑_i g² n_i / 6 μ_i) for bulk quark matter, calculated as a function of η_s = m_s/μ for α_s = 0.4 (shown in Fig. 1)
- The RPA damping rate Γ_RPA behaves as ~ c/R, proportional to the inverse of the strangelet radius R
- For strangelets with radius R ≲ 3 fm (baryon number less than ~30), higher-order terms in (k_F,i R)⁻¹ may affect the frequency and damping rate

## Context

This work extends the theory of color plasma oscillations in bulk quark matter, previously investigated by Baym and Chin (1976) and Kalashnikov (1984), to finite strangelets. It draws an analogy to the size-dependent damping of surface plasmons in metal nanoparticles studied by Kawabata and Kubo (1966) and Kreibig and Vollmer (1995). The results enable estimates of how color plasmons in strangelets might decay into particle-hole pairs and subsequently produce observable electromagnetic signals (photons and lepton pairs) in heavy-ion collision experiments, though the author notes that nonperturbative effects and collisional damping remain uncertain.
