# Self-similarity of strangeness production in pp collisions at RHIC

> New experimental data on transverse momentum spectra of strange particles (KS0, K-, K*, phi,...) produced in pp collisions at sqrt s = 200 GeV obtained by the STAR and PHENIX collaborations at RHIC are analysed in the framework of z-scaling approach. Scaling properties of the data z-presentation are illustrated. Self-similarity of strange particle production is discussed. A microscopic scenario of constituent interactions developed within the z-scaling approach is used to study constituent energy loss, proton momentum fraction and recoil mass in dependence on the transverse momentum, strangeness, and mass of the inclusive particle. The obtained results can be useful for understanding strangeness origin, for searching for new physics with strange probes and can serve as a benchmark for complex analyses of self-similar features of strange production in heavy ion collisions.

# Self-similarity of strangeness production in pp collisions at RHIC

## Key claim

The z-scaling approach, previously established for pions, can describe transverse momentum spectra of strange hadrons (mesons and baryons) produced in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV via a universal scaling function, provided that the fragmentation dimension epsilon_F is adjusted for each particle species according to its strangeness content.

## Method

The authors applied the z-scaling formalism to published STAR and PHENIX data on inclusive cross sections of strange particles (KS0, K-, K*, phi, Lambda, Xi-, Omega, Sigma*(1385), Lambda*(1520)) in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV. They computed the scaling variable z and the scaling function Psi(z) from the measured transverse momentum spectra, multiplicity density, and inelastic cross section, then extracted the momentum fractions x1, x2, recoil mass M_X, and relative energy loss Delta E/E = (1-y_a) as functions of pT for each particle species.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s)=200 GeV for pp collisions at RHIC
- Fragmentation dimension for pions: epsilon_pi=0.2 (used as reference scaling curve)
- Relative energy loss Delta E/E = (1-y_a) decreases with increasing transverse momentum for all strange particles
- For pT>1 GeV/c, the energy loss is larger for strange baryons than for strange mesons, with increasing tendency for baryons containing more strange valence quarks
- The momentum fraction x1 increases with the mass of the strange meson for given strangeness and pT
- The recoil mass M_X shows the same increasing tendency with strange hadron mass

## Context

This work extends the z-scaling approach, previously applied to inclusive spectra from U70, SppS, SPS, ISR, Tevatron, and RHIC (Tokarev and Zborovsky, earlier works), to strange particle production at RHIC. The analysis confirms flavor independence of the scaling function Psi(z) and establishes a hierarchy of constituent energy loss depending on hadron strangeness content. The results provide a benchmark for future studies of self-similar features in heavy ion collisions and can serve as a reference for searches for new physics with strange probes.
