# Disentangling stopped proton and inclusive net-proton fluctuations at RHIC

> The recent results on net-proton and net-charge multiplicity fluctuations from the beam energy scan program at RHIC have drawn much attention to explore the critical point in the QCD phase diagram. Experimentally measured protons contain contribution from various processes such as secondaries from higher mass resonance decay, production process, and protons from the baryon stopping. Further, these contributions also fluctuate from event to event and can contaminate the dynamical fluctuations due to the critical point. We present the contribution of stopped proton and produced proton fluctuations in the net-proton multiplicity fluctuation in \auau collisions measured by STAR experiment at RHIC. The produced net-proton multiplicity fluctuations using cumulants and their ratios are studied as a function collision energies. After removing the stopped proton contribution from the inclusive proton multiplicity distribution, a non-monotonic behavior is even more pronounced in the net-proton fluctuations around \sqsn = 19.6 GeV, both in $Sσ$ and $κσ^2$. The present study will be useful to understand the fluctuations originating due to critical point.

## Key claim

After removing the stopped proton contribution from the inclusive proton multiplicity distribution, the non-monotonic behavior in net-proton fluctuations around √sNN = 19.6 GeV becomes even more pronounced in both Sσ and κσ², which may indicate the presence of a critical point or phase transition in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors used a Monte-Carlo approach assuming Binomial distributions for inclusive proton and anti-proton multiplicities, with mean values taken from STAR experimental data for 0%–5% central Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies. They constructed stopped proton and produced proton distributions by weighting the inclusive proton distribution by the fraction of stopped and produced protons, estimated from mean values of inclusive and stopped protons reported in prior work. Net-proton cumulants were then calculated both from inclusive protons minus anti-protons and from produced protons minus anti-protons, and compared as a function of collision energy.

## Key numbers

- Mean inclusive proton multiplicity decreases from 18.918 ± 0.009 at √sNN = 7.7 GeV to 5.664 ± 0.001 at √sNN = 200 GeV in 0%–5% central Au+Au collisions
- Mean stopped proton multiplicity decreases from 17.21 ± 0.86 at √sNN = 7.7 GeV to 1.54 ± 0.33 at √sNN = 200 GeV
- Mean anti-proton multiplicity increases from 0.165 ± 0.001 at √sNN = 7.7 GeV to 4.116 ± 0.001 at √sNN = 200 GeV
- The C₄/C₂ (κσ²) ratio from inclusive net-protons shows maximum deviation from Poisson expectation at √sNN = 19.6 GeV
- After removing stopping contribution, the C₃/C₂ and C₄/C₂ ratios of net-proton multiplicity show non-monotonic behavior around √sNN = 19.6 GeV
- At √sNN = 62.4 GeV, the cumulants and ratios for stopped and produced protons become similar, indicating equal contributions from stopping and production

## Context

This work extends the net-proton fluctuation studies from the STAR Beam Energy Scan program (Adamczyk 2013) by disentangling the contributions of stopped and produced protons, which had been identified as a potential contaminant to critical point signals by Bzdak (2016). The method builds on estimates of stopped proton mean values from net-proton rapidity distributions (Thakur 2016) and complements prior studies of resonance decay contributions to net-proton fluctuations (Mishra 2016, Nahrgang 2014). The results enable more targeted searches for the QCD critical point by isolating the produced-particle fluctuations that are expected to carry the critical signal.
