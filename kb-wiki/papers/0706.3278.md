# Simulations on the Heavy Hadron Transport at RHIC

> Based on the hadron transport frames, detailed simulations are carried out to discuss $ϕ$, $Ω$ productions and the significant enhancements in the very low $p_T$ region for some of the soft spectra in RHIC. Elastic interactions are introduced in the simulations. The elastic cross sections vary from different hadrons and energy scales, which can qualitatively explain the different collective motions of various hadrons.

## Key claim

Elastic collisions, with cross sections that vary widely between hadron species, can qualitatively explain both the significant enhancement of heavy hadron yields at very low transverse momentum and the different collective motions of various hadrons at RHIC, though the method is "neither strict nor complete" and the mean elastic cross section values are "just qualitative."

## Method

The authors simulate the transport of heavy hadrons (φ, Ω, Σ*(1385), Ξ*(1530)) through a medium described by a 1+1D hydrodynamic evolution with an invariant boost for central Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV. The medium is separated into an ideal hydrodynamic part and a free-expanding part by an energy density cut ε_c, with hadron production from QGP droplets parameterized by production and absorption rates. Elastic collisions are introduced via separate "push" (acceleration by pressure gradient) and "drag" (deceleration by relative motion) effects, each parameterized by a mean elastic cross section.

## Key numbers

- φ meson: the drag effect with mean elastic cross section ⟨σ⟩_id = 20 mb reproduces the very low p_T enhancement observed by STAR and PHENIX; the push effect requires ⟨σ⟩_ip = 20–25 mb
- Ω baryon: the elastic cross section is no more than 20 mb, as the spectrum is well described by inelastic transport alone
- Pions: the push effect reproduces the inclusive pion distribution with ⟨σ⟩_ip ≈ 75 mb; for direct pions (no decay contributions), ⟨σ⟩_ip ≈ 250 mb
- Medium particles near T_c ≈ 166 MeV have average momentum ⟨p_j⟩ ≈ 500 MeV
- The inelastic free path in the later stage is as long as 4 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier transport model (Miao and Gao, 2007) which used a decoupling hypersurface of splitting QGP droplets and successfully described light hadron spectra but failed for φ, Ω, and other heavy resonances. The simulations also address the observed enhancement at very low p_T for Λ (STAR, 2005), Σ*(1385), and φ, which the hypersurface model could not reproduce. The results enable future work to include full microscopic simulations of the medium evolution and feedback, and to handle the reconstruction of hadrons from decay products whose momenta are shifted by elastic collisions.
