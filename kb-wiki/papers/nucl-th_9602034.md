# Description of deformed nuclei in the sdg boson model

> We present a study of deformed nuclei in the framework of the sdg interacting boson model utilizing both numerical diagonalization and analytical $1/N$ expansion techniques. The focus is on description of high-spin states which have recently become computationally accessible through the use of computer algebra in the $1/N$ expansion formalism. A systematic study is made of high-spin states in rare-earth and actinide nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the sdg interacting boson model (sdg-IBM), using both numerical diagonalization truncated to a maximum number of g bosons and analytical $1/N$ expansion techniques extended to high orders via computer algebra, can provide a satisfactory simultaneous description of both low-lying band structures and high-spin states in deformed rare-earth and actinide nuclei, provided that a Hamiltonian including d-boson and g-boson energies plus quadrupole and hexadecapole interactions is employed. The hedge is that the $1/N$ expansion formalism currently covers only ground- and single-phonon bands, and calculations for multi-phonon bands have not yet been performed.

## Method

The authors employed the sdg-IBM with a Hamiltonian containing one-body energies for d and g bosons, quadrupole-quadrupole interactions, and hexadecapole-hexadecapole interactions, reducing the 10 free parameters to 5 by scaling quadrupole parameters from SU(3) values and determining hexadecapole parameters from a commutation condition. They performed numerical diagonalization in a truncated basis with a maximum number of g bosons $n_{g\max}=N/3$ for boson numbers up to $N=14$, and independently developed analytical $1/N$ expansion formulas to third layer (including terms up to $\bar L^3/N^6$) using computer algebra (Mathematica) for ground band and $\gamma$ band energies and electromagnetic transitions, with mean fields determined by variation after projection.

## Key numbers

- For a typical deformed Hamiltonian with $\kappa_2=-20$ keV, $\kappa_4=0$, $q=0.5$, $\eta_d=1.5$, $\eta_g=4.5$, and $N=10$, truncation to $n_{g\max}=1$ gives band excitation energies and E2 transitions off by about 10-20%, while $n_{g\max}=3$ yields convergence to accuracy of a few percent for almost all cases.
- At spins $L\sim 2N$, the $n_{g\max}=1$ calculations for ground band excitation energies and E2 transitions are off by about 20-30%, with errors increasing at higher spin.
- Diagonalization truncated to $n_{g\max}=N/3$ gives reliable description for states with spins $L<2N$, extending applicability of the SDGBOSON code to $N=14$, which covers roughly half of the deformed nuclei.
- The $1/N$ expansion normalization integral $F(N,L)$ is expressed as a double expansion in $1/N$ and $\bar L=L(L+1)$ with coefficients $\alpha_{nm}$ given up to sixth order, enabling description of moment of inertia at high spins through inclusion of terms of order $\bar L^3/N^6$.

## Context

This work extends the sdg-IBM framework beyond earlier studies that were limited to low-lying states or used truncated SU(3) basis calculations (Yoshinaga 1988) or single g-boson coupling to an sd core (Isacker 1982). It addresses criticisms by Barrett and McCarthy (1982) regarding the inability of the IBM to describe high-spin states in deformed nuclei. The $1/N$ expansion method builds on the formalism of Kuyucak and Morrison (1988) and extends it to higher orders using computer algebra (Kuyucak 1995), enabling systematic study of model parameters and efficient data analysis. The results enable future application to high-spin data in rare-earth and actinide nuclei, though multi-phonon band calculations remain an open challenge.
