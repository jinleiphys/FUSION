# Muon capture on 3H

> The muon capture on 3H leading to muonic neutrino and three neutrons in the final state is studied under full inclusion of final state interactions. Predictions for the three-body break-up of 3H are calculated with the AV18 potential, augmented by the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. Our results are based on the single nucleon weak current operator comprising the dominant relativistic corrections. This work is a natural extension of our investigations of the muon capture on 3He leading to 3H or n+d or n+n+p and muonic neutrino in the final state, presented in Phys. Rev. C90, 024001 (2014).

## Key claim
The muon capture on tritium (³H) leading to three neutrons and a muonic neutrino is calculated with full inclusion of final-state interactions using modern two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces, but the results are restricted to the single nucleon weak current operator (without meson-exchange currents), which limits the completeness of the calculation.

## Method
The authors solve Faddeev-like equations in momentum space for the three-neutron final state, using the AV18 two-nucleon potential alone or combined with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. The nuclear matrix elements are computed via partial wave decomposition with total three-nucleon angular momentum up to J=9/2 and two-nucleon subsystem angular momentum j ≤ 3. The single nucleon weak current operator is used in both nonrelativistic and relativistic-corrected forms, with weak nucleon form factors taken from Shen et al. (2012).

## Key numbers
- Maximal neutrino energy (relativistic): 94.3078 MeV
- Maximal neutrino energy (nonrelativistic): 94.3073 MeV
- Total capture rate with AV18 only (nonrelativistic current): 37.5 s⁻¹ (27.2 s⁻¹ in plane wave approximation)
- Total capture rate with AV18 only (relativistic corrections): 36.5 s⁻¹ (26.6 s⁻¹ in plane wave approximation)
- Total capture rate with AV18 + Urbana IX (relativistic corrections): 32.6 s⁻¹
- F=0 hyperfine state capture rate with AV18 + Urbana IX (relativistic corrections): 118.7 s⁻¹
- F=1 hyperfine state capture rate with AV18 + Urbana IX (relativistic corrections): 3.92 s⁻¹
- Relativistic corrections reduce the F=0 rate by approximately 4% and increase the F=1 rate by nearly 9%

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of muon capture on ³He (Golak et al., 2014, Phys. Rev. C90, 024001) to the analogous reaction on ³H, which produces three neutrons in the final state. It improves upon earlier calculations from the 1970s and 1980s (Phillips 1975, Torre 1978, Torre 1979, Dzhibuti 1984) by using modern two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces with full inclusion of final-state interactions. The results enable future studies of neutron-neutron interactions and three-neutron forces in the total isospin T=3/2 channel, though experimental measurement remains extremely challenging due to tritium radioactivity and meso-molecular complications.
