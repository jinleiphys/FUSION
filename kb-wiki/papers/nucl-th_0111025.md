# From Light Nuclei to Nuclear Matter. The Role of Relativity?

> The success of non-relativistic quantum dynamics in accounting for the binding energies and spectra of light nuclei with masses up to A=10 raises the question whether the same dynamics applied to infinite nuclear matter agrees with the empirical saturation properties of large nuclei.The simple unambiguous relation between few-nucleon and many-nucleon Hamiltonians is directly related to the Galilean covariance of nonrelativistic dynamics. Relations between the irreducible unitary representations of the Galilei and Poincare groups indicate thatthe ``nonrelativistic'' nuclear Hamiltonians may provide sufficiently accurate approximations to Poincare invariant mass operators. In relativistic nuclear dynamics based on suitable Lagrangeans the intrinsic nucleon parity is an explicit, dynamically relevant, degree of freedom and the emphasis is on properties of nuclear matter. The success of this approach suggests the question how it might account for the spectral properties of light nuclei.

## Key claim

Non-relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics that successfully reproduces binding energies and spectra of light nuclei up to A=10 may provide sufficiently accurate approximations to Poincaré-invariant mass operators, but there is no firm theoretical basis to predict that such "realistic" Hamiltonians will also fit empirical nuclear matter saturation properties without additional many-body forces.

## Method

The paper examines the mathematical relationship between Galilei-covariant and Poincaré-covariant nuclear dynamics, analyzing the coupled-cluster representation of homogeneous nuclear matter for both standard Hamiltonian dynamics (with two- and three-nucleon potentials) and Dirac dynamics (with four-component spinor wave functions). For the non-relativistic case, the ground state of nuclear matter is defined by a linear functional over Fock space vectors using an exponential operator S that is a polynomial in particle and hole creation operators, with the energy per nucleon determined by S₂ and S₃. For the relativistic Dirac case, a modified Fermi gas state with a momentum-dependent projection operator incorporates dynamical effects that would appear as correlations in ordinary many-body wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies of light nuclei computed with accuracy of ~1% for A ≤ 10 using the Galilei-invariant Casimir Hamiltonian
- The "relativistic correction" implied by the relation between Galilean and Poincaré Casimir Hamiltonians is less than 0.5% for nuclear binding energies
- The Fermi-gas ground state density is given by ρ₀ = (4/(2π)³)(4π/3)k_F³

## Context

This paper examines the tension between the successful non-relativistic few-nucleon calculations by Carlson and collaborators and the relativistic effective theories of nuclear matter developed by Serot and others. It extends earlier work by Coester, Day, and others on the inadequacy of scattering-equivalent two-nucleon potentials alone to reproduce empirical nuclear matter saturation properties. The paper suggests that Dirac dynamics with explicit intrinsic parity degrees of freedom might account for both light nuclei and nuclear matter without many-body forces, while standard Hamiltonian dynamics with two- and three-body forces may require additional many-body forces to fit nuclear matter.
