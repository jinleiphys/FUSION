# Revisiting Theoretical Analysis of Electric Dipole Moment of $^{129}$Xe

> Linear response approach to the relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) theory has been extended to estimate contributions from the parity and time-reversal violating pseudoscalar-scalar (Ps-S) and scalar-pseudoscalar (S-Ps) electron-nucleus interactions along with electric dipole moments (EDMs) of electrons ($d_e$) interacting with internal electric and magnetic fields. Random phase approximation (RPA) is also employed to produce results to compare with the earlier reported values and demonstrate importance of the non-RPA contributions arising through the RCC method. It shows that contributions from the S-Ps interactions and $d_e$ arising through the hyperfine-induced effects are very sensitive to the contributions from the high-lying virtual orbitals. Combining atomic results with the nuclear shell-model calculations, we impose constraints on the pion-nucleon coupling coefficients, and EDMs of proton and neutron. These results are further used to constrain EDMs and chromo-EDMs of up- and down-quarks by analyzing particle physics models.

## Key claim

The paper extends the linear-response relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) method to compute contributions to the electric dipole moment (EDM) of \(^{129}\)Xe from the pseudoscalar-scalar (Ps-S) and scalar-pseudoscalar (S-Ps) electron-nucleus interactions, as well as from the electron EDM (\(d_e\)) interacting with internal electric and magnetic fields, finding that the hyperfine-induced contributions from S-Ps interactions and \(d_e\) are very sensitive to high-lying virtual orbitals and differ substantially from earlier estimations.

## Method

The authors employed the Dirac-Coulomb Hamiltonian to obtain mean-field wave functions, then used the relativistic coupled-cluster singles and doubles (RCCSD) method and the random phase approximation (RPA) to compute EDM enhancement factors for \(^{129}\)Xe from various P,T-odd interactions. For the scalar-type interactions (S-Ps and \(d_e\) interacting with the internal electric field), which do not contribute at second order in a closed-shell atom, the hyperfine interaction (\(M1_{hf}\)) was included as an additional perturbation to obtain third-order contributions. Nuclear structure coefficients for the nuclear Schiff moment (NSM) were computed using large-scale shell-model (LSSM) calculations with the KSHELL code, using effective Hamiltonians SN100PN and SNV.

## Key numbers

- Atomic polarizability \(\alpha_d\): final RCCSD value 27.55(30) a.u., compared to experimental value 27.815(27) a.u. (Hohm, 2000).
- NSM enhancement factor \(d_\mathrm{a}^{Sm}\): final RCCSD value \(0.337(10) \times 10^{-17} \frac{S}{e\ \text{fm}^3}\) e-cm.
- T-Pt e-N interaction enhancement factor \(d_\mathrm{a}^T\): final RCCSD value \(0.510(10) \times 10^{-20} \langle \sigma \rangle C_\mathrm{T}\) e-cm.
- Ps-S e-N interaction enhancement factor \(d_\mathrm{a}^{Ps}\): final RCCSD value \(1.442(25) \times 10^{-23} \langle \sigma \rangle C_\mathrm{P}\) e-cm.
- \(d_e\) interacting with nuclear magnetic field enhancement factor \(d_\mathrm{a}^B\): final RCCSD value \(0.716(15) \times 10^{-4}\) e-cm.
- \(d_e\) interacting with internal electric field (hyperfine-induced) enhancement factor \(d_\mathrm{a}^e\): final RCCSD value \(10.75(25) \times 10^{-4}\) e-cm.
- S-Ps e-N interaction (hyperfine-induced) enhancement factor \(d_\mathrm{a}^{Sc}\): final RCCSD value \(3.91(10) \times 10^{-23} (C_\mathrm{S}/A)\) e-cm.
- Nuclear spin matrix element for neutron: \(\langle \sigma_n \rangle = 0.66\) (mean of SN100PN and SNV results).
- Nuclear spin matrix element for proton: \(\langle \sigma_p \rangle = 0.002\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier RCC-based calculations for \(^{129}\)Xe by Singh et al. (2016) and Sakurai et al. (2019), which focused on T-Pt interactions and NSM, to include Ps-S, S-Ps, and electron EDM contributions. The results enable combined constraints on pion-nucleon coupling coefficients (\(\bar{g}_{\pi NN}^{(0)}\), \(\bar{g}_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\), \(\bar{g}_{\pi NN}^{(2)}\)), nucleon EDMs (\(d_p\), \(d_n\)), and ultimately quark (chromo-)EDMs (\(d_u\), \(d_d\), \(\tilde{d}_u\), \(\tilde{d}_d\)) by combining atomic calculations with nuclear shell-model results and particle physics models. The finding that hyperfine-induced contributions are highly sensitive to high-lying virtual orbitals and differ substantially from earlier estimates by Flambaum (1976) and Martensson-Pendrill (1985) motivates further experimental EDM searches for \(^{129}\)Xe, with three experiments currently underway.
