# Spectral functions for $\bar{D}$ meson and $\bar{D}_0^*$ meson in nuclear matter with partial restoration of chiral symmetry

> We investigate the in-medium masses of a $\bar{D} (0^-)$ meson and a $\bar{D}_0^* (0^+)$ meson and spectral functions for $\bar{D}$ and $\bar{D}_0^*$ meson channels in nuclear matter. These mesons are introduced as chiral partner in the chiral symmetry broken vacuum, hence they are useful to explore the partial restoration of the broken chiral symmetry in nuclear matter. We consider the linear sigma model to describe the chiral symmetry breaking. Our study shows that the loop corrections to $\bar{D}$ and $\bar{D}_0^*$ meson masses provide a smaller mass splitting at finite density than that in vacuum, whose result indicates a tendency of the restoration of the chiral symmetry. We investigate also the spectral function for $\bar{D}_0^*$ meson channel, and find three peaks. The first peak which corresponds to the resonance of $\bar{D}_0^*$ meson is broadened by collisions with nucleons in medium, and the peak position shifts to lower mass due to the partial restoration of chiral symmetry as the density increases. The second peak is identified as a threshold enhancement which shows a remarkable enhancement as the density increase. The third peak is Landau damping. The obtained properties of $\bar{D}$ and $\bar{D}_0^*$ mesons in nuclear matter will provide useful information for experiments.

## Key claim

The loop corrections to the mean-field sigma meson contribution cause the mass splitting between the chiral partner $\bar{D}$ and $\bar{D}_0^*$ mesons to become smaller at finite nuclear density than in vacuum, indicating a tendency toward partial restoration of chiral symmetry, though the effect is computed perturbatively at one-loop order and does not include all possible higher-order contributions.

## Method

The authors employ a linear sigma model to describe nuclear matter, including nucleons, pions, and sigma mesons, with the mean field of the sigma meson determined by a gap equation at finite baryon density. They then introduce $\bar{D}$ and $\bar{D}_0^*$ mesons as chiral partners using a Lagrangian that respects both chiral symmetry and heavy quark spin symmetry, and compute the one-loop self-energies for these mesons arising from virtual pion and sigma meson fluctuations in the nuclear medium. The in-medium spectral functions for the pion and sigma meson are first obtained from nucleon-loop diagrams, and these are then used to calculate the modifications to the $\bar{D}$ and $\bar{D}_0^*$ meson masses and spectral functions.

## Key numbers

- The mean field $\sigma_0^*$ decreases with increasing density, indicating partial restoration of chiral symmetry; at $\rho_B = 0.066$ fm$^{-3}$, the spectral functions for pion and sigma meson are shown.
- In vacuum, the spin-averaged mass of ($\bar{D},\bar{D}^*$) is $M_{(\bar{D},\bar{D}^*)} = 2190$ MeV and that of ($\bar{D}_0^*,\bar{D}_1$) is $M_{(\bar{D}_0^*,\bar{D}_1)} = 2190 + 430 = 2620$ MeV, giving $\Delta_m = 430$ MeV.
- The coupling constant $g_A$ is estimated from the $D^* \to D\pi$ decay width as $g_A = 0.50$.
- The vacuum masses used are $m_{\bar{D}} = 1869$ MeV, $m_{\bar{D}^*} = 2010$ MeV, $m_{\bar{D}_0^*} = 2318$ MeV, and $m_{\bar{D}_1} = 2427$ MeV.
- The parameter $G_\pi = \Delta_m / f_\pi = 4.65$ with $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Suenaga, Yasui, and Harada (2014) and Harada, Suenaga, and Yasui (2016) that considered only mean-field contributions from sigma and omega mesons to $\bar{D}$ meson masses in dense matter, by now including one-loop corrections from pion and sigma meson fluctuations. It also builds on the linear sigma model framework for nuclear matter developed by Birse (1994). The results provide predictions for the in-medium spectral functions of $\bar{D}$ and $\bar{D}_0^*$ mesons that can be tested at facilities such as J-PARC and FAIR, and enable future studies of chiral symmetry restoration using heavy-light meson probes.
