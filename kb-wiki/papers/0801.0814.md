# Information on the structure of the a1 from tau decay

> The decay $τ\to πππν$ is analysed using different methods to account for the resonance structure, which is usually ascribed to the a1. One scenario is based on the recently developed techniques to generate axial-vector resonances dynamically, whereas in a second calculation the a1 is introduced as an explicit resonance. We investigate the influence of different assumptions on the result. In the molecule scenario the spectral function is described surprisingly well by adjusting only one free parameter. This result can be systematically improved by adding higher order corrections to the iterated Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction. Treating the a1 as an explicit resonance on the other hand leads to peculiar properties.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the $\tau \to \pi\pi\pi\nu$ decay spectral function can be described surprisingly well by a "molecule" scenario in which the $a_1$ resonance is generated dynamically from coupled-channel $\pi\rho$ and $KK^\ast$ interactions, using only one free parameter, whereas treating the $a_1$ as an explicit resonance leads to peculiar properties.

## Method

The authors calculate the hadronic tensor for $\tau^- \to 2\pi^0\pi^-\nu_\tau$ using two approaches: (1) a dynamical generation of the $a_1$ via the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a Weinberg-Tomozawa kernel for coupled $\pi\rho$ and $KK^\ast$ channels, and (2) an explicit $a_1$ resonance introduced as a bare state with its width generated from self-energy contributions. The spectral function $a_1(s)$ is extracted from the transverse part $W_1$ of the hadronic tensor. Higher-order corrections to the kernel are also investigated, and a simplified $W$ form-factor approach is presented for comparison.

## Key numbers

- The main bump in the data appears at about $1.5\,\text{GeV}^2$ in the spectral function.
- The onset of the rise in the spectral function occurs in the region $0.5-0.7\,\text{GeV}^2$.
- The relation $f_V-2g_V \approx 0.1 f_V$ is numerically almost fulfilled, i.e. $f_V >> (f_V-2g_V)$.
- The $a_1$ mass $M_{a_1}$ is treated as a free parameter; the on-shell relation $M_{a_1}^2 = 2M_\rho^2$ is used for comparison with prior work.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical generation of axial-vector resonances developed by Lutz and collaborators (Lutz, 2002) and applied to the $a_1$ by Oset and collaborators (Oset, 2003). It contrasts with pure chiral perturbation theory calculations (Colangelo, 1996) which cannot describe the data above $1\,\text{GeV}$. The molecule scenario enables a parameter-free description of the $a_1$ structure from $\tau$ decay data, while the explicit resonance scenario reveals inconsistencies that motivate further study of the $a_1$ nature.
