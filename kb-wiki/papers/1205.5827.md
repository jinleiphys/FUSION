# Transition between nuclear and quark-gluon descriptions of hadrons and light nuclei

> We provide a perspective on studies aimed at observing the transition between hadronic and quark-gluonic descriptions of reactions involving light nuclei. We begin by summarizing the results for relatively simple reactions such as the pion form factor and the neutral pion transition form factor as well as that for the nucleon and end with exclusive photoreactions in our simplest nuclei. A particular focus will be on reactions involving the deuteron. It is noted that a firm understanding of these issues is essential for unraveling important structure information from processes such as deeply virtual Compton scattering as well as deeply virtual meson production. The connection to exotic phenomena such as color transparency will be discussed. A number of outstanding challenges will require new experiments at modern facilities on the horizon as well as further theoretical developments.

## Key claim

The transition between hadronic and quark-gluon descriptions of reactions involving light nuclei is not yet fully observed; while evidence for color transparency (CT) exists in meson reactions, searches in nucleon (proton) reactions have not been convincing, and the approach to perturbative QCD (pQCD) scaling in electron-deuteron elastic scattering is expected only at much higher momentum transfers (near 144 GeV\(^2\)) than currently accessible.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental and theoretical studies of color transparency in reactions such as A(p,2p), A(e,e'p), A(e,e'\(\pi\)), A(e,e'\(\rho\)), pion and J/\(\psi\) photoproduction, and coherent pion diffractive jet production on nuclei. It also examines hadronic and quark-gluon descriptions of the deuteron through electron-deuteron elastic scattering (form factors A, B, T\(_{20}\)) and two-nucleon photodisintegration (\(\gamma d \to pn\)), as well as photoreactions in \(^3\)He (\(\gamma ^3{\rm He} \to pp + n_{spectator}\)), comparing data to calculations from Hamiltonian dynamics, propagator dynamics, the Hard Rescattering Model (HRM), and the Quark Gluon String Model (QGS).

## Key numbers

- For coherent pion diffractive jet production on C and Pt targets with a 500 GeV pion beam, the per-nucleus cross section follows \(\sigma = \sigma_\circ A^{\alpha}\) with \(\alpha = 1.6\), consistent with color transparency predictions; the predicted yield ratio between Pt and C is about an order of magnitude more than expected from ordinary diffraction.
- In electron-deuteron elastic scattering, the approach to pQCD is expected near 144 GeV\(^2\); meson exchange currents (MEC) have a profound effect on A and B above 1 GeV\(^2\) and on T\(_{20}\) above 0.5 GeV\(^2\).
- For \(\gamma d \to pn\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 90^\circ\), cross sections follow \(s^{-11}\) scaling for \(E_{\gamma} > 1\) GeV; the threshold for scaling behavior is given approximately by \(p_T = 1.1\) GeV/\(c\).
- For \(\gamma ^3{\rm He} \to pp + n_{spectator}\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 90^\circ\) with \(E_{\gamma} > 2\) GeV, cross sections exhibit approximate \(s^{-11}\) scaling at a level a factor of 20 smaller than the \(\gamma d \to pn\) data; for 1 GeV \(< E_{\gamma} < 2\) GeV, cross sections are slightly less than 1/2 of the \(\gamma pn\) cross sections.
- The linearly polarized photon asymmetry \(\Sigma(\theta_{cm} = 90^\circ)\) for \(\gamma d \to pn\) is positive above about 600 MeV and hints at an increase with energy above 1 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the discussion of color transparency and the transition from hadronic to quark-gluon descriptions, building on earlier reviews such as Gilman (2001) for the deuteron and prior experimental searches for CT in A(p,2p) and A(e,e'p) reactions (Mardor 1998, Aclander 2004, Makins 1994, Garrow 2001, Rohe 2005). It enables future experiments at upgraded JLab and COMPASS-II to search for more compelling evidence of CT in meson electroproduction and to test models of quark dynamics in photoreactions of light nuclei, particularly through \(\gamma ^3{\rm He}\) disintegration and the study of \(\Delta\Delta\) pair production in the deuteron.
