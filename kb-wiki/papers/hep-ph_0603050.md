# Susceptibilities and speed of sound from PNJL model

> We present the Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure in quark number chemical potential $μ_0=μ_B / 3=μ_u=μ_d$, for the strongly interacting matter as described by the PNJL model for two light degenerate flavours of quarks u and d. The expansion has been done upto eighth order in $μ_0$, and the results are consistent with recent estimates from Lattice. We have further obtained the specific heat $C_V$, squared speed of sound $v_s^2$ and the conformal measure $\cC$.

## Key claim

The PNJL model, using parameters fixed only from pure gauge lattice data, reproduces the qualitative features of the Taylor expansion coefficients of pressure up to eighth order in quark chemical potential, with the results consistent with lattice QCD estimates, though the fourth-order coefficient \(c_4\) remains about double the Stefan-Boltzmann limit even at \(2.5 T_c\) and shows only weak convergence toward that limit.

## Method

The authors computed the thermodynamic potential of the PNJL model for two light degenerate quark flavours in mean-field approximation, minimizing it with respect to the chiral condensate \(\sigma\) and the traced Polyakov loop fields \(\Phi\) and \(\bar{\Phi}\). The pressure was obtained as a function of temperature and quark chemical potential \(\mu_0\), then fitted to an eighth-order even polynomial in \(\mu_0\) to extract the Taylor expansion coefficients \(c_2, c_4, c_6, c_8\). The specific heat \(C_V\), squared speed of sound \(v_s^2\), and conformal measure \(\cC\) were obtained from temperature derivatives of the thermodynamic potential at \(\mu_0 = 0\).

## Key numbers

- The crossover temperature used for scaling is \(T_c = 227\) MeV, with chiral transition at \(T_{chiral} \simeq 229\) MeV and deconfinement transition at \(T_{deconfinement} \simeq 225\) MeV.
- At \(T = 227\) MeV (\(T_c\)), the fitted Taylor coefficients are \(c_0 = 0.3890060(1)\), \(c_2 = 0.317329(9)\), \(c_4 = 0.2775(2)\) for an eighth-order fit over \(\mu_0\) range 0–100 MeV.
- At \(T = 350\) MeV, the coefficients are \(c_0 = 2.380420(0)\), \(c_2 = 0.7585680(6)\), \(c_4 = 0.103069(9)\) for an eighth-order fit over \(\mu_0\) range 0–200 MeV.
- The squared speed of sound \(v_s^2\) reaches a minimum near 0.08 at \(T_c\), close to the softest point \(P/\epsilon = 0.075\) from isentropic expansion.
- The conformal measure \(\cC\) near \(T_c\) ranges from 0.7 to 0.75.
- The pressure reaches about 90% of the Stefan-Boltzmann ideal gas value at \(2.5 T_c\), compared to about 80% from continuum lattice estimates.

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model studies of Ghosh et al. (2006) and Ratti et al. (2006) by computing higher-order susceptibilities up to eighth order and thermodynamic quantities such as specific heat, speed of sound, and conformal measure. The results are compared with lattice QCD data from Allton et al. (2005) and Gavai and Gupta (2005), showing reasonable agreement for the second-order coefficient \(c_2\) but discrepancies for \(c_4\) above \(T_c\). The findings enable further exploration of the QCD phase diagram near the critical endpoint and provide a baseline for comparing effective model predictions with lattice results at non-zero chemical potential.
