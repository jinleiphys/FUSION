# Entanglement suppression for $ΩΩ$ scattering

> We study entanglement suppression in $s$-wave $ΩΩ$ scattering, where each baryon has spin $3/2$. By treating the $S$-matrix as a quantum operator acting on the spin states, we quantify its ability to generate entanglement and identify the conditions on the phase shifts of the spin channels that minimize entanglement generation in the system. In $ΩΩ$ scattering, only antisymmetric spin channels are allowed due to Fermi-Dirac statistics. Applying the entanglement-suppression framework to $ΩΩ$ scattering, we find two solutions for the phase shifts: one leading to a spin SU(4) symmetry and the other to a nonrelativistic conformal symmetry. We show that the solution associated with the nonrelativistic conformal symmetry originates from the specific structure of the Clebsch-Gordan coefficients in the $3/2 \otimes 3/2$ system.

## Key claim

The entanglement suppression framework applied to s-wave Omega-Omega scattering yields two minimal-entanglement solutions for the phase-shift difference: one corresponding to spin SU(4) symmetry and the other to nonrelativistic conformal symmetry, but unlike in nucleon-nucleon scattering the minimal entanglement power is not zero (it is 1/2) because the symmetric spin channels J=1 and J=3 are forbidden by Fermi-Dirac statistics.

## Method

The S-matrix for low-energy s-wave Omega-Omega scattering was constructed as an operator acting on spin states, with only the antisymmetric J=0 and J=2 channels allowed due to Fermi-Dirac statistics. The entanglement power was computed using the linear entropy averaged over initial product states of two spin-3/2 particles, employing a weighted entanglement power (k=2) to handle the non-unit normalization of final states caused by the absence of symmetric spin channels. The conditions for minimal entanglement were derived by analyzing the dependence of the entanglement power on the phase-shift difference delta_0 minus delta_2.

## Key numbers

* The entanglement power for Omega-Omega scattering is E_2 = (1/48)(25 - cos[4 delta_02]), where delta_02 = delta_0 - delta_2.
* The minimal value of the entanglement power is 1/2, achieved when |delta_02| = 0 or |delta_02| = pi/2.
* The linear entropy of the normalized antisymmetric state is exactly 1/2, independent of the initial state.
* Lattice QCD reports the scattering length of the J=2 channel as a_J=2 approximately 4.6 fm, much larger than the typical strong interaction scale of about 1 fm.

## Context

This work extends the entanglement suppression framework previously applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering by Beane (2018) and Low (2021), where the minimal entanglement solutions gave the identity gate (spin-flavor SU(4) symmetry) and the SWAP gate (nonrelativistic conformal symmetry). The Omega-Omega system provides the first application to spin-3/2 baryon scattering, motivated by lattice QCD results from Gongyo (2017) showing a large scattering length in the J=2 channel. The analysis reveals that the special structure of Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for the 3/2 tensor 3/2 system eliminates the cos[2 delta_02] term present in other two-channel systems such as deuteron-deuteron scattering studied by Kirchner (2023), enabling the |delta_02| = pi/2 solution to achieve the same minimal entanglement as the |delta_02| = 0 solution.
