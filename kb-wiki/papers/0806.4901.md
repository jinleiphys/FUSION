# Selected vector-meson decay-distributions in reactions of polarized photons with protons

> We develop a formalism for studying vector meson ($V$) photo-production at the proton ($p$) with polarized photons, $\vecγp \to V p $, through an analysis of the decay distribution in the channel $V\toπ^0γ$. We show that this decay distribution differs noticeably from the distributions of purely hadronic decays, like $ϕ\to K^+K^-$, $ω\to π^0π^+π^-$. Formulas for the decay distributions are presented which are suitable for data analysis and interpretation.

## Key claim

The formalism for analyzing vector-meson photo-production with polarized photons is extended to the radiative decay channel \(V\to\pi^0\gamma\) (and equivalently \(V\to\eta\gamma\)), showing that the resulting angular decay distributions differ qualitatively from those of purely hadronic decays such as \(\phi\to K^+K^-\) or \(\omega\to\pi^0\pi^+\pi^-\), with the asymmetries having opposite signs and the absolute values for the hadronic decay being two times larger.

## Method

The authors express the cross section for \(\vec\gamma p \to V p \to \pi^0\gamma p\) as a product of the branching ratio, the unpolarized differential cross section, and a decay distribution \(W^{\pi^0\gamma}(\Omega_\pi,\Psi)\). They compute the decay distribution by summing over the polarization states of the outgoing photon in the vector-meson rest frame, using the photon density matrix for linear and circular polarizations, and then express the result in terms of spin-density matrix elements. The resulting formulas are compared to the corresponding distributions for hadronic decays derived from the standard formalism of Schilling (1970).

## Key numbers

- For \(\phi\) meson photo-production, the polar angular distribution is evaluated at photon energies \(E_\gamma = 1.97-2.17\) GeV and momentum transfer \(t = t_{\rm max} - 0.2\) GeV\(^2\).
- For \(\omega\) meson photo-production, the calculation is performed at \(E_\gamma = 1.6\) GeV and \(t = t_{\rm max} - 0.2\) GeV\(^2\).
- The beam asymmetry \(\Sigma^{\pi^0\gamma}_b\) equals \(\Sigma^{h}_b = 2\rho^1_{11} + \rho^1_{00}\), independent of the decay mode.
- The azimuthal decay asymmetry for unpolarized photons satisfies \(\Sigma^{\pi^0\gamma}_{\Phi} = -\frac12 \Sigma^{h}_{\Phi} = -\rho^0_{1-1}\).
- The azimuthal decay asymmetry relative to the photon polarization plane satisfies \(\Sigma^{\pi^0\gamma}_d = -\frac12 \Sigma^{h}_d = -\rho^1_{1-1}\).
- The azimuthal asymmetry for circularly polarized photons satisfies \(\Sigma^{\pi^0\gamma}_C = -\frac12 \Sigma^{h}_C = -{\rm Im}\,\rho^3_{1-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Schilling (1970) for analyzing vector-meson photo-production with polarized photons to the radiative decay channel \(V\to\pi^0\gamma\), which is accessible with detectors such as the Crystal Barrel at ELSA. The results enable experimental analysis of \(\phi\) and \(\omega\) photo-production data from the LEPS collaboration, which showed deviations from conventional Pomeron-exchange predictions (Titov and Lee, 2003) and a bump structure at \(E_\gamma \sim 2\) GeV (Mibe, 2005), as well as studies of nucleon resonance excitation in \(\omega\) photo-production near threshold (Ajaka, 2006; Titov and Lee, 2002; Zhao, 2001; Shklyar, 2004). The derived formulas provide independent observables complementary to hadronic decay channels for extracting spin-density matrix elements.
