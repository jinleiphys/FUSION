# Response relation in Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV

> We carry out simulations using a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model to describe the response relation between $v_2$ and $\varepsilon_2$ in Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, respectively. To simulate such relation, two methods have been introduced in the calculation: one is the directed response (DR) method, which correlates the outgoing particles with the initial anisotropy directly, and the other one is the cumulants response (CR) method, which is constructed from a cumulants correlation between outgoing particles. Based on calculations of the DR and CR methods, the response relations as a function of the transverse momentum are both shown in Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions. By comparing the DR and CR methods, we found that the linear response relations are almost identical in all the present collisions. Similar results of linear+cubic response relations are also shown in the higher multiplicity systems, and it has become a significant difference in the lower multiplicity systems, i.e., the peripheral Pb-Pb collisions and p-Pb collisions. Throughout the whole $p_{T}$-dependent simulations, the $κ_2$ in the linear response and in the linear+cubic response are almost identical, except for in the lower multiplicity systems by the DR method. If one implements a pseudorapidity gap by the CR calculation, the $p_{T}$-dependent and $η$-independent response relations are similarly shown in peripheral Pb-Pb systems and p-Pb systems, which may imply that a collective response exists in the most central p-Pb collisions. These collective behaviors are dominantly produced on the stage of the medium expansions.

## Key claim

The linear response relation between elliptic flow \(v_2\) and initial eccentricity \(\varepsilon_2\) is almost identical in Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV when using either the directed response (DR) or cumulants response (CR) method, but the linear+cubic response differs significantly between higher-multiplicity and lower-multiplicity systems, with the cubic term becoming non-negligible only in peripheral Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions when calculated by the CR method.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event simulations using the multi-phase transport (AMPT) model for Pb-Pb (30-40% and 70-80% centrality) and p-Pb (impact parameter b = 0-3 fm) collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV. They extracted the linear response coefficient \(\kappa_2\) and cubic response coefficient \(\kappa_2'\) from the relation \(V_2 = \kappa_2 \mathcal{E}_2 + \kappa_2' \varepsilon_2^2 \mathcal{E}_2 + \delta_2\) using two methods: the directed response (DR) method, which correlates outgoing particles directly with the initial anisotropy, and the cumulants response (CR) method, which uses two- and four-particle correlations with a pseudorapidity gap to subtract residual noise.

## Key numbers

- For 30-40% Pb-Pb collisions, the integral value of \(\kappa_2'\) is about 0.08 at 2.76 TeV (from hydrodynamic and AMPT models).
- In the \(\eta\)-independent analysis for midcentral 30-40% Pb-Pb collisions, \(\kappa_2 \approx 0.2\) and \(\kappa_2' \approx 0.08\).
- The cubic response \(\kappa_2'\) in 30-40% Pb-Pb collisions shows a trend of increasing up to \(p_T \sim 1.2\) GeV, then decreasing toward higher \(p_T\), becoming less than zero when \(p_T > 1.5\) GeV.
- All charged particles were selected with \(0.3 < p_T < 2.0\) GeV and \(|\eta| < 2.5\).
- The pseudorapidity gap used in the CR method was \(|\Delta\eta| > 1\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMPT-based study of response relations in Pb-Pb systems (De 2018) to p-Pb collisions, comparing with ALICE data (Acharya 2018) and hydrodynamic simulations (Qiu 2012) at 2.76 TeV. The results show that the linear response agrees with ALICE data for \(v_2\{2,|\Delta\eta|>2\}/\varepsilon_2\{2\}\) based on MC Glauber initial states, while the cubic response behavior in low-multiplicity systems raises questions about the validity of the linear+cubic response model for small systems, as noted by hydrodynamic studies (Rao 2019). The \(\eta\)-independent response found in peripheral Pb-Pb and p-Pb systems suggests collective behavior exists in central p-Pb collisions, enabling further investigation of whether a fluidlike QGP is created in small systems.
