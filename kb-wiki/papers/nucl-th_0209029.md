# Quark model predictions for the SU(6)-breaking ratio of the proton momentum distributions

> The ratio between the anomalous magnetic moments of proton and neutron has been recently parametrized by the ratio of proton momentum fractions $M_{2}^{q_{val}}$. This ratio is evaluated using different constituent quark models, starting from the CQM density distributions and calculating the next-to leading order distributions. We show that this momentum fractions $M_{2}^{q_{val}}$ ratio is a sensitive test for SU(6)-breaking effects and therefore it is useful to distinguish among different CQMs. We investigate also the possibility of getting constraints on the formulation of quark structure models.

## Key claim

The paper tests a proposed relation between the ratio of proton and neutron anomalous magnetic moments and the ratio of proton momentum fractions carried by valence quarks, finding that while the relation holds to within a few percent for all constituent quark models studied, the experimental value of the magnetic moment ratio is not reproduced by these models, suggesting that the SU(6)-breaking mechanism in the models differs from that in phenomenological parton distributions.

## Method

The authors start from three different constituent quark models (Isgur-Karl, hypercentral Coulomb-plus-linear confinement, and algebraic U(7) model) and compute the quark momentum density distributions in the nucleon rest frame. They then use the formalism of Traini et al. to obtain unpolarized valence parton distributions at a low hadronic scale, and perform next-to-leading order QCD evolution up to \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\) to obtain the momentum fractions \(M_2^{q_{val}}\) for u and d valence quarks. The ratio \(R\) defined by the right-hand side of the proposed relation is compared with the experimental ratio of anomalous magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic scale parameters used for NLO evolution: \(\mu_0^2 = 0.094\) GeV\(^2\), \(\Lambda_{\text{NLO}} = 248\) MeV, \(\alpha_s(\mu_0^2)/(4\pi) = 0.142\).
- For the hypercentral model (model 2_bis), the two sides of the relation agree within 0.2%, but the \(\kappa\)-ratio differs from the experimental value (\(\sim -0.937\)) by about 7%.
- In a simplified Isgur-Karl model with only D-wave SU(6) breaking (D-model), fitting the \(\kappa\)-ratio to \(-0.937\) gives \(a_S = 0.955\) and \(a_D = 0.295\), yielding \(R = 0.9988\) with \(\alpha = 2.1\) fm\(^{-1}\), differing by about 7% from the \(\kappa\)-ratio.
- The U(7) model reproduces the \(\kappa\)-ratio correctly by construction, but the relation is violated up to a few percent.

## Context

This work extends the relation proposed by Goeke, Polyakov, and Vanderhaeghen (2001) connecting anomalous magnetic moments to valence quark momentum fractions, which had been verified phenomenologically using NLO and NNLO parton distributions (MRST01, CTEQ6M). The paper tests this relation using three different constituent quark models (Isgur-Karl, hypercentral model of Ferraris et al., and algebraic model of Iachello et al.) and finds that while the relation holds approximately, the models fail to reproduce the experimental magnetic moment ratio, indicating that additional degrees of freedom beyond three constituent quarks (such as quark-antiquark pairs or pions) may be needed. This enables future work to use the relation as a test for models incorporating such additional degrees of freedom.
