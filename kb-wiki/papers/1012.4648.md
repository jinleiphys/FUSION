# Note on triangle anomaly with improved momentum cutoff

> A Lorentz and gauge symmetry preserving regularization method has been proposed recently in 4 dimension based on Euclidean momentum cutoff. It is shown that the triangle anomaly can be calculated unambiguously with this new improved cutoff. The anticommutator of γ^{5} and γ^μ multiplied by five gamma is proportional to terms that do not vanish under a divergent loop-momentum integral, but cancel otherwise.

## Key claim

The improved momentum cutoff regularization, which preserves Lorentz and gauge symmetries by construction and makes loop integrals invariant under momentum shifts, can still reproduce the correct triangle anomaly. The anomaly arises not from shifting a linearly divergent integral but from the fact that the anticommutator of γ⁵ and γ^μ does not vanish under a divergent loop-momentum integral when multiplied by five additional gamma matrices, even though it cancels in finite cases.

## Method

The authors recalculate the one-loop triangle diagram for the axial-vector current using the improved Euclidean momentum cutoff method of Uj (2009). They evaluate the trace of γ⁵ with six gamma matrices using only the Clifford algebra anticommutator {γ_μ, γ_ν} = 2g_μν, without assuming {γ^μ, γ⁵} = 0. The loop integrals are performed with the regularization rules that replace k_μ k_ν under divergent integrals by (1/2n) g_μν times a scalar integral, rather than the naive (1/4) g_μν k² substitution. The Schouten identity is applied only after the loop-momentum identifications have been made.

## Key numbers

- The axial-vector Ward identity acquires the anomaly term: -(q₁+q₂)_ρ T^{μνρ} = 2m T^{5μν} - i (e² / 2π²) ε^{μναβ} q_{1α} q_{2β}.
- The finite part of the divergent integral ∫ d⁴k / (k² - Δ²)² with cutoff Λ gives (1/(4π)²) [ ln((Λ²+Δ²)/Δ²) + Δ²/(Λ²+Δ²) - 1 ].
- The integral ∫ d⁴k k_μ k_ν / (k² - Δ²)³ with the improved regularization gives (1/(4π)²) (g_μν/4) [ ln((Λ²+Δ²)/Δ²) + Δ²/(Λ²+Δ²) - 1 ], where the naive substitution would give a constant -3/2 instead of -1.

## Context

This work extends the improved momentum cutoff regularization proposed by Uj (2009), which was successfully applied to a non-renormalizable theory. It contradicts the expectation that momentum-cutoff methods cannot handle chiral anomalies, and it resolves the ambiguity in momentum routing that plagues simple cutoff calculations. The result is consistent with dimensional regularization and the 't Hooft-Veltman scheme (Collins, 1984), but works entirely in four dimensions. The method is promising for theories with quadratic divergences, such as gauge theories with gravitational interactions (Toms, 2009), and could be extended to higher loops or non-abelian gauge theories.
