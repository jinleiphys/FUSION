# Probing the nuclear EOS with fragment production

> We discuss fragmentation mechanisms and isospin transport occurring in central collisions between neutron rich systems at Fermi energies. In particular, isospin effects are analyzed looking at the correlations between fragment isotopic content and kinematical properties. Simulations are based on an approximate solution of the Boltzmann-Langevin (BL) equation. An attempt to solve the complete BL equation, by introducing full fluctuations in phase space is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that correlations between the isotopic content (N/Z) and kinetic energy of intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) produced in central collisions of neutron-rich nuclei at Fermi energies are sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy, but this sensitivity is conditional on the initial asymmetry of the colliding system and the interplay with Coulomb effects.

## Method

The authors performed simulations of central collisions (b = 2 fm) of symmetric Sn+Sn systems with three different initial asymmetries (112Sn+112Sn, 124Sn+124Sn, 132Sn+132Sn) at a beam energy of 50 MeV/A using the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) approach, an approximate solution of the Boltzmann-Langevin equation implemented in the TWINGO code. A soft equation of state with compressibility modulus K = 200 MeV was used, and two parameterizations of the symmetry energy were compared: a stiff form roughly proportional to density squared (asystiff) and a soft form with saturation above normal density (asysoft, SKM*). 1200 events were run for each reaction and each symmetry energy parameterization. A new method to solve the full Boltzmann-Langevin equation by implementing complete fluctuations in phase space was also developed and tested on a box of nuclear matter at equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 50 MeV/A for all reactions
- Impact parameter: b = 2 fm (central collisions)
- Compressibility modulus: K = 200 MeV (soft EOS)
- Initial N/Z ratios: 1.24 (112Sn+112Sn), 1.48 (124Sn+124Sn), 1.64 (132Sn+132Sn)
- Average fragment multiplicity: approximately 6 for the reactions considered
- Fragment charge range for IMF analysis: Z between 3 and 10
- Density cutoff for fragment identification: rho_cut = 1/5 rho_0
- For the phase-space fluctuation method test: box size l = 26 fm, 2820 nucleons, density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3, 500 test particles per nucleon, temperature 5 MeV, cross section sigma = 160 mb, momentum cell size l_s = 30 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of isospin transport and multifragmentation in Fermi-energy heavy-ion collisions, particularly the investigation of isoscaling behavior in fragment yields by Tsang et al. (2001) and the study of pre-equilibrium emission sensitivity to the symmetry energy by Famiano et al. (2006). The correlation analysis between fragment isotopic content and kinetic energy provides a complementary observable to pre-equilibrium studies for constraining the low-density behavior of the symmetry energy. The new method for solving the full Boltzmann-Langevin equation improves upon the approximate SMF approach of Colonna et al. (1998) and the earlier fluctuation method of Bauer et al. (1987), enabling more accurate treatment of phase-space fluctuations in transport simulations.
