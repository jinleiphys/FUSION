# Constraining hypernuclear density functional with $Λ$-hypernuclei and compact stars

> We present a simultaneous calculation of heavy single-$Λ$ hypernuclei and compact stars containing hypernuclear core within a relativistic density functional theory based on a Lagrangian which includes the hyperon octet and lightest isoscalar-isovector mesons which couple to baryons with density-dependent couplings. The corresponding density functional allows for SU(6) symmetry breaking and mixing in the isoscalar sector, whereby the departures in the $σ$-$Λ$ and $σ$-$Σ$ couplings away from their values implied by the SU(3) symmetric model are used to adjust the theory to the laboratory and astronomical data. We fix $σ$-$Λ$ coupling using the data on the single-$Λ$ hypernuclei and derive an upper bound on the $σ$-$Σ$ from the requirement that the lower bound on the maximum mass of a compact star is $2 M_{\odot}$.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the combined constraints from laboratory data on single-Λ hypernuclei and the astronomical requirement that the maximum mass of a compact star be at least 2 M⊙ can simultaneously narrow the parameter space of a relativistic hypernuclear density functional, specifically fixing the σ-Λ coupling and providing an upper bound on the σ-Σ coupling.

## Method
The authors used a relativistic density functional theory with density-dependent couplings, based on a Lagrangian including the full baryon octet and the lightest isoscalar and isovector mesons. They performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations for the single-Λ hypernuclei ¹⁷_ΛO, ⁴¹_ΛCa, and ⁴⁹_ΛCa in a spherical basis, varying the σ-Λ coupling to match the Λ 1s₁/₂ separation energies from a mass formula. They then computed the equation of state and mass-radius relations for compact stars at zero temperature, varying the σ-Σ coupling while keeping the σ-Λ coupling fixed at the value optimized from hypernuclei, and imposing the observational lower bound of 2 M⊙ on the maximum mass.

## Key numbers
- Optimal σ-Λ coupling: x_{σΛ} = 0.6164, obtained by fitting the Λ 1s₁/₂ single-particle energies from hypernuclei to the mass formula of Levai (1998).
- Allowed range for σ-Σ coupling: 0.15 ≤ x_{σΣ} ≤ 0.45, where the upper limit is set by the requirement that the maximum mass of a compact star be at least 2 M⊙, and the lower limit by the inequality 1 ≤ (1/2)(3 x_{σΛ} + x_{σΣ}) ≤ 2.
- For the optimal model with x_{σΛ} = 0.6164, the maximum mass drops below 2 M⊙ for x_{σΣ} ≥ 0.45.
- Saturation density of the nuclear functional: ρ₀ = 0.152 fm⁻³, binding energy per nucleon E/A = -16.14 MeV, incompressibility K₀ = 250.90 MeV, symmetry energy J = 32.30 MeV, symmetry energy slope L = 51.24 MeV.
- For the optimal model, the Λ 1s₁/₂ single-particle energies are -11.716 MeV in ¹⁷_ΛO, -17.821 MeV in ⁴¹_ΛCa, and -19.618 MeV in ⁴⁹_ΛCa.
- The predicted radii of massive hypernuclear stars are in the range of 13 km.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic density functional approach of Colucci (2013), which used SU(3) symmetric couplings and focused on the sensitivity of the hypernuclear equation of state to unknown hyperon-scalar-meson couplings. It provides a combined constraint from both laboratory hypernuclear data and the astronomical 2 M⊙ pulsar observations of Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013), addressing the "hyperonization puzzle" where hyperon softening of the equation of state can reduce maximum masses below observational limits. The method enables future studies to constrain any theoretical framework describing hypernuclear systems using current laboratory and astrophysical data.
