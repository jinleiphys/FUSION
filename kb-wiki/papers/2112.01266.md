# Two-neutron halo structure and anti-halo effect in 31F

> We perform a detailed analysis for the structure of 31F, which is a candidate of the halo nucleus. We calculate the radius and reaction cross-section using a three-body model of 29F+n+n and discuss how the competition between the neutron-pairing and the single-particle energy induces structural changes of 31F. The present analysis further clarifies a new aspect of the anti-halo efect that suppresses the halo structure.

## Key claim

The halo structure of 31F is not primarily determined by the two-neutron separation energy, but rather by the energy gap between the p3/2 and f7/2 orbits in the unbound subsystem 30F; when this gap is small, an anti-halo effect suppresses the nuclear radius even though the separation energy remains small.

## Method

The authors performed three-body calculations of 31F using a 29F+n+n cluster-orbital shell model (COSM) with the Gaussian expansion method (GEM). They employed a Woods-Saxon potential for the 29F+n interaction, adjusting the strength parameter under 12 different parameter sets while maintaining the condition that 30F is unbound and 31Ne is bound, and used the central part of the Minnesota potential for the neutron-neutron interaction. Correlation analysis using Pearson's correlation coefficient was applied to examine relationships between the energy gap in 30F, the matter radius, neutron occupation numbers, and the neutron-neutron interaction expectation value.

## Key numbers

* Two-neutron separation energies S2n for 31F range from 0.44 to 1.37 MeV across the 12 parameter sets.
* Matter radius Rrms of 31F varies from 3.48 to 3.70 fm across the parameter sets.
* Reaction cross-sections for the largest radius case (Rrms = 3.70 fm) are 1530 mb at 240 MeV/nucleon and 1640 mb at 900 MeV/nucleon.
* Pearson correlation coefficient between S2n and Rrms is rxy = -0.801, while between the energy gap Δε in 30F and Rrms it is rxy = 0.935.
* In the largest radius case (Rrms = 3.70 fm), neutron occupation numbers are N(p) = 1.63 and N(f) = 0.19 (p-wave dominant); in the smallest radius case (Rrms = 3.48 fm), N(p) = 0.38 and N(f) = 1.53 (f-wave dominant).
* Pearson correlation coefficient between Δε and the absolute value of the neutron-neutron interaction expectation value is rxy = -0.945.
* Pearson correlation coefficient between neutron-neutron distance r12rms and core-to-center-of-mass distance Rcrms is rxy = 0.997.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-body calculation of 31F (Masui 2020) by providing a detailed mechanism for the anti-halo effect, which was originally discussed by Bennaceur (2000) and Hagino (2011) in terms of pairing correlations. The study shows that for nuclei in the "island of inversion" where valence orbits are inverted, the energy gap between the p3/2 and f7/2 orbits in the unbound subsystem 30F determines whether a halo structure or an anti-halo effect occurs, enabling predictions for other drip-line nuclei with inverted valence orbits.
