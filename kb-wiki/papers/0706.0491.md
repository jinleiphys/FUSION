# Reflection asymmetric relativistic mean field approach and its application to the octupole deformed nucleus $^{226}$Ra

> A Reflection ASymmetric Relativistic Mean Field (RAS-RMF) approach is developed by expanding the equations of motion for both the nucleons and the mesons on the eigenfunctions of the two-center harmonic-oscillator potential. The efficiency and reliability of the RAS-RMF approach are demonstrated in its application to the well-known octupole deformed nucleus $^{226}$Ra and the available data, including the binding energy and the deformation parameters, are well reproduced.

## Key claim

The Reflection ASymmetric Relativistic Mean Field (RAS-RMF) approach reproduces the binding energy and deformation parameters of the octupole deformed nucleus ²²⁶Ra, but the method is demonstrated only for this single nucleus and the octupole degree of freedom of the two-center harmonic-oscillator basis is explored while the Δz degree of freedom is left for future studies.

## Method

The authors expand the Dirac equation for nucleons and the Klein-Gordon equations for mesons on the eigenfunctions of the two-center harmonic-oscillator (TCHO) potential, which allows reflection-asymmetric shapes while maintaining axial symmetry. The TCHO basis is specified by three parameters (δ₂, δ₃, Δz), but only δ₃ is varied while Δz is set to approximately zero. The method is applied to ²²⁶Ra using the NL1 effective force and a constant pairing gap of Δ = 11.2 MeV/√A.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of ²²⁶Ra: RAS-RMF prediction 1731.8 MeV, experimental value 1731.6 MeV
- Quadrupole deformation β₂ for ²²⁶Ra: RAS-RMF gives 0.21 (matter), experimental charge β₂ = 0.20
- Octupole deformation β₃ for ²²⁶Ra: RAS-RMF gives −0.16 (matter), experimental charge β₃ = −0.13
- The asymmetric ground state of ²²⁶Ra is lower than the symmetric ground state by roughly 2.7 MeV
- Convergence of RAS-RMF calculations is achieved at N = 16 shells for ²⁰⁸Pb and ¹⁶O

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field (RMF) model, which previously assumed reflection symmetry in basis expansion methods (Ring 1997, Hirata 1996), to allow octupole-deformed nuclei. The approach builds on the two-center shell model developed by the Frankfurt group (Holzer 1969, Maruhn 1972) and earlier non-relativistic Hartree-Fock applications (Passler 1973, Dreizler 1974). The method enables future studies of atomic electric-dipole moments in octupole deformed nuclei such as ²²⁵Ra, which are sensitive to time-reversal violation beyond the Standard Model, and may be extended to study fission and fusion processes by including the Δz degree of freedom.
