# Multi-step processes in heavy-ion induced single-nucleon transfer reactions

> It was first noted during the 1970s that finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (FR-DWBA) calculations were unable satisfactorily to describe the shape of the angular distributions of many single-proton (and some single-neutron) transfer reactions induced by heavy ions, with calculations shifted to larger angles by up to ~ 4 degrees compared with the data. These reactions exhibited a significant mismatch, either of the reaction Q value or the grazing angular momentum of the entrance and exit channels, and it was speculated that the inclusion of multi-step transfer paths via excited state(s) of the projectile and/or ejectile could compensate for the effect of this mismatch and yield good descriptions of the data by shifting the calculated peaks to smaller angles. However, to date this has not been explicitly demonstrated for many reactions. In this work we show that inclusion of the two-step transfer path via the 4.44-MeV 2+ excited state of the 12C projectile in coupled channel Born approximation calculations enables a good description of the 208Pb(12C,11B)209Bi single-proton stripping data at four incident energies which could not be described by the FR-DWBA. We also show that inclusion of a similar reaction path for the 208Pb(12C,13C)207Pb single-neutron pickup reaction has a relatively minor influence, slightly improving the already good description obtained with the FR-DWBA.

## Key claim

Inclusion of the two-step transfer path via the 4.44-MeV 2+ excited state of 12C in coupled channel Born approximation (CCBA) calculations enables a good description of the 208Pb(12C,11B)209Bi single-proton stripping data at four incident energies that could not be described by finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (FR-DWBA) calculations; however, for the 208Pb(12C,13C)207Pb single-neutron pickup reaction the same two-step path has only a relatively minor influence, slightly improving the already good FR-DWBA description.

## Method

The authors performed CCBA calculations using the code fresco for the 208Pb(12C,11B)209Bi proton stripping reaction and the 208Pb(12C,13C)207Pb neutron pickup reaction at bombarding energies of 77.4, 97.9, 101, and 116.4 MeV. For the proton stripping, the post form of the DWBA formalism was employed with the full complex remnant term, including coupling to the 4.44-MeV 2+ state of 12C via rotational model form factors; for the neutron pickup, the prior form was used. Two sets of spectroscopic amplitudes for the projectile-like overlaps were tested in each case: those of Cohen and Kurath (1967) and those of Rudchik et al. (2001) for the proton stripping, and those of Cohen and Kurath (1967) and Ziman et al. (1997) for the neutron pickup.

## Key numbers

- The 4.44-MeV 2+ excited state of 12C was used as the intermediate state in the two-step transfer path for both reactions.
- Data were analyzed at four 12C bombarding energies: 77.4, 97.9, 101, and 116.4 MeV.
- For the proton stripping reaction, FR-DWBA calculations were shifted to larger angles by up to approximately 4 degrees compared with the data.
- The nuclear deformation length for the 12C 2+ state was taken as delta_2 = -1.40 fm.
- For the neutron pickup reaction, the Cohen and Kurath (1967) spectroscopic factor for the <13C | 12C(0+) + n> overlap is approximately a factor of 1.7 greater than that of Ziman et al. (1997).
- The Coulomb barrier height used for matching calculations was 57.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the observations of Toth et al. (1976) and Oertzen et al. (1984), who noted that FR-DWBA calculations for heavy-ion induced single-proton transfer reactions showed angular shifts compared to data, particularly when Q-value or grazing angular momentum mismatch was present. The authors explicitly demonstrate that the previously speculated mechanism of multi-step transfer via excited states of the projectile can compensate for this mismatch, using the 12C + 208Pb system as a test case. This enables improved analysis of single-nucleon transfer reactions induced by light heavy ions, which is relevant for studies with radioactive beams.
