# Isospin dependence of the critical quark-deconfinement densities

> We explore the dependence of the critical density, separating hadronic matter from a mixed phase of quarks and hadrons, on the ratio $Z/A$. We use both the MIT bag model and the Color Dielectric Model to describe the quark dynamics, while for the hadronic phase we employ various relativistic equations of state. We find that, if the parameters of quark models are fixed so that the existence of quark stars is allowed, then the critical density drops dramatically in the range $Z/A \sim $ 0.3--0.4. Moreover, for $Z/A \sim $ 0.3 the critical density is only slightly larger than the saturation density of symmetric nuclear-matter. This opens the possibility to verify the Witten-Bodmer hypothesis on absolute stability of quark matter using ground-based experiments in which neutron-rich nuclei are tested.

## Key claim

If the parameters of quark models are fixed so that the existence of quark stars is allowed (the Witten-Bodmer hypothesis), then the critical density for the transition from hadronic matter to a mixed quark-hadron phase drops dramatically in the range Z/A ~ 0.3–0.4, and for Z/A ~ 0.3 the critical density is only slightly larger than the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors used the MIT bag model (with and without first-order gluon exchange) and the Color Dielectric Model (CDM) for the quark phase, and several relativistic non-linear Walecka-type equations of state (GM1, GM2, GM3, and NLHρ with and without δ-meson exchange) for the hadronic phase. The deconfinement phase transition was described by imposing Gibbs equilibrium conditions to determine the critical density separating pure hadronic matter from the mixed phase as a function of the proton fraction Z/A. Transport simulations of semi-central 132Sn+132Sn collisions at 300 A.MeV and 1 A.GeV were performed using a Relativistic Transport Code to check whether the critical densities could be reached in experiments.

## Key numbers

- For the MIT bag model with parameters satisfying the Witten-Bodmer hypothesis, the bag constant B^(1/4) must be ~ 140–150 MeV (citing Farhi 1984).
- In the upper panel of Fig. 1 (GM2 hadronic EOS, MIT bag without gluons), the critical density drops sharply in the range Z/A ~ 0.3–0.4, reaching values of order ρ₀ (nuclear matter saturation density) for Z/A ~ 0.3 when B^(1/4) = 148 MeV.
- In the lower panel of Fig. 1 (GM1 hadronic EOS, MIT bag with α_s = 0.3), a similar sharp drop is observed for Z/A ~ 0.3–0.4.
- In the transport simulation of 132Sn+132Sn at 300 A.MeV, the system reaches the border of the mixed phase; at 1 A.GeV, the system is well inside the mixed phase.
- At the maximum density reached in the simulation (ρ ~ 2.6 ρ₀), the quadrupole momentum of the nucleon momentum distribution dropped to ~ 10% of its initial value, indicating thermalization.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Mueller (1997), who found only a ~10% reduction in critical density for Z/A ~ 0.3 using a single set of model parameters, by systematically exploring parameter sets that satisfy the Witten-Bodmer hypothesis on absolute stability of quark matter (Witten 1984, Bodmer 1971). The results open the possibility to test the deconfinement transition in low-energy experiments with radioactive nuclear beams, and suggest that the EMC effect (Aubert 1983) may have an isospin dependence due to enhanced multi-quark cluster formation in neutron-rich nuclei. The analysis also enables future experimental searches for precursor signals of deconfinement, such as an earlier softening of the hadronic equation of state for large isospin asymmetries observable in collective flows.
