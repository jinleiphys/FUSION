# Nonextensive effects on the relativistic nuclear equation of state

> The Walecka many-body field theory is investigated in the context of quantum nonextensive statistical mechanics, characterized by a dimensionless parameter $q$. We consider nuclear matter described statistically by a power-law distribution which generalizes the standard Fermi-Dirac distribution ($q = 1$). We show that the scalar and vector meson fields become more intense due to the nonextensive effects ($q \neq 1$). From a numerical treatment, we also show that as the nonextensive parameter $q$ increases, the nucleon effective mass diminishes and the equation of state becomes stiffer. Finally, the usual Maxwell construction seems not to be necessary for isotherms with temperatures in the range 14 Mev$<k_BT<$20 MeV.

## Key claim

The Walecka quantum hadrodynamics (QHD-I) mean-field theory, when combined with a power-law generalization of the Fermi-Dirac distribution from Tsallis nonextensive statistics, yields a stiffer nuclear equation of state and eliminates the first-order liquid-gas phase transition for isotherms in the temperature range 14 MeV < k_B T < 20 MeV, provided the nonextensive parameter q is chosen appropriately.

## Method

The authors replace the standard Fermi-Dirac distribution in the QHD-I mean-field equations with the Teweldeberhan-Plastino-Miller (TPM) q-generalized fermion distribution, which uses a deformed exponential with a cut-off prescription. They then solve the self-consistent equations for the scalar density, baryon density, energy density, and pressure numerically for symmetric nuclear matter (γ_N=4) and pure neutron matter (γ_N=2), using the coupling constants (g_σ/m_σ)^2 = 11.798 fm^2 and (g_ω/m_ω)^2 = 8.653 fm^2 from Walecka (1974). The convergence of the momentum integrals restricts the nonextensive parameter to the range 1 < q < 5/4.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constants used are (g_σ/m_σ)^2 = 11.798 fm^2 and (g_ω/m_ω)^2 = 8.653 fm^2, fixed to give binding energy E_bind = -15.75 MeV and Fermi momentum k_F = 1.42 fm^{-1}.
- The convergence of all integrals in the mean-field equations requires the nonextensive parameter to satisfy 1 < q < 5/4.
- The first-order phase transition is eliminated for temperatures in the range 14 MeV < k_B T < 20 MeV, where a critical parameter q_c can be defined.
- The critical parameter q_c as a function of temperature is parametrized as q_c = 1.7724(±0.003) - 0.0384(±0.0001) × (k_B T / 1 MeV) for the range 14 MeV < k_B T < 20 MeV.
- In the high-temperature limit (T → ∞), the energy density scales as ε → (γ_N/π^2) ξ_3(q) (k_B T)^4, with p = ε/3, recovering the Stefan-Boltzmann limit when q → 1 (ξ_3 → 7π^4/120).

## Context

This work extends the Walecka mean-field theory (Walecka, 1974) by incorporating Tsallis nonextensive statistics (Tsallis, 1988) via the TPM quantum distribution (Teweldeberhan, Plastino and Miller, 2005), which differs from earlier generalizations of quantum statistics for fermions applied to the Boguta-Bodmer equation of state by Drago et al. (2004). The results enable future astrophysical studies of protoneutron star masses and heavy-ion collision dynamics at temperatures of several tens of MeV, where nonextensive effects may become observable.
