# Monte-Carlo generator of heavy ion collisions DCM-SMM

> The new monte-carlo generator of heavy ion collisions, DCM-SMM, based on Dubna Cascade Model (DCM-QGSM) and Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) is described. The model aimed to generate particle--nucleus and nucleus--nucleus collisions at a wide range of energy was created to provide the computer simulation support to new experimental facilities BMN and MPD at the accelerator complex NICA. It can simulate the production of both light particles and nuclear fragments and hyperfragments on the event by event basis.

## Key claim

The DCM-SMM Monte-Carlo generator combines the Dubna Cascade Model (DCM-QGSM) with the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) to simulate particle-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions from approximately 100 MeV to 100 GeV, but the paper acknowledges that the model's rapidity distributions for light fragments deviate from experimental data, suggesting that collective effects such as hydrodynamical-like expansion are not yet included.

## Method

The generator operates in three stages: first, the DCM-QGSM simulates the dynamical stage of the collision using a Monte-Carlo solution of relativistic kinetic equations with binary interactions, including cascade-cascade interactions and hadron formation times. Second, the excited residual nucleus is treated as a thermalized system and its break-up into primary fragments is described by the SMM, which considers all possible partitions of the system into nucleons and excited fragments under statistical equilibrium at a low-density freeze-out volume. Third, hot primary fragments undergo secondary de-excitation via Fermi break-up for light fragments (A ≤ 16), Weisskopf evaporation for heavier fragments, and fission for heavy nuclei (A > 100), with all stages simulated event-by-event.

## Key numbers

- Energy range of the model: from approximately 100 MeV to 100 GeV
- Coalescence momentum parameters for light fragment formation: p_c(d) = 90 MeV/c, p_c(t) = 108 MeV/c, p_c(³He) = 108 MeV/c, p_c(α) = 115 MeV/c
- Nuclear density parameters: r_0 = 1.19A^{1/3} + 1.61A^{-1/3} fm, a = 0.54 fm
- String tension in QGSM: κ ≈ 1 GeV/fm
- Binding energy of infinite nuclear matter: W_0 = 16 MeV
- Surface coefficient: B_0 = 18 MeV
- Critical temperature of infinite nuclear matter: T_c = 18 MeV
- Symmetry energy parameter: γ = 25 MeV
- Coulomb parameter r_0 = 1.17 fm
- Cutoff energy for cascade particles: 1 MeV (plus Coulomb barrier for protons) above the Fermi level
- Time scale for equilibration of nuclear spectator matter: estimated around or less than 100 fm/c
- Excitation energy threshold for transition from compound nucleus to multifragmentation: E* ≥ 3 MeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends the earlier DCM-QGSM model (Toneev 1983, Amelin 1990) by incorporating the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) developed by Botvina and collaborators, which has been successfully applied to describe fragment production in heavy ion collisions around Fermi energy by groups such as ALADIN, INDRA, and EOS. The DCM-SMM generator is created to provide simulation support for the new BMN and MPD experiments at the NICA accelerator complex, and it enables event-by-event simulation of light particles, nuclear fragments, and hyperfragments including Lambda polarization and nuclear vorticity effects.
