# Bayesian uncertainty quantification for nuclear matter incompressibility

> Within a Bayesian statistical framework using the standard Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model, the maximum {\it a posteriori} (MAP) values and uncertainties of nuclear matter incompressibility and isovector interaction parameters are inferred from the experimental data of giant resonances and neutron-skin thicknesses of typical heavy nuclei. With the uncertainties of the isovector interaction parameters constrained by the data of the isovector giant dipole resonance and the neutron-skin thickness, we have obtained $K_0 = 223_{-8}^{+7}$ MeV at 68% confidence level using the data of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance in $^{208}$Pb measured at the Research Center for Nuclear Physics (RCNP), Japan, and at the Texas A&M University (TAMU), USA. Although the corresponding $^{120}$Sn data gives a MAP value for $K_0$ about 5 MeV smaller than the $^{208}$Pb data, there are significant overlaps in their posterior probability distribution functions.

# Bayesian uncertainty quantification for nuclear matter incompressibility

## Key claim

Using a Bayesian framework with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations, the authors obtain a nuclear matter incompressibility of \(K_0 = 223_{-8}^{+7}\) MeV at 68% confidence level by averaging results from the RCNP and TAMU isoscalar giant monopole resonance data for \(^{208}\)Pb, but note that the \(^{120}\)Sn data give a MAP value about 5 MeV smaller, with significant overlap in the posterior probability distributions.

## Method

The authors perform a Bayesian analysis using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model with random-phase approximation to compute observables from four model parameters: symmetry energy \(E_{sym}^0\), its slope \(L\), isovector nucleon effective mass \(m_v^\star\), and incompressibility \(K_0\). They use Markov-Chain Monte Carlo sampling with the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm to infer posterior probability distributions from combined experimental data of isoscalar giant monopole resonances, isovector giant dipole resonances (centroid energy and electric polarizability), and neutron-skin thicknesses of \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{120}\)Sn.

## Key numbers

- \(K_0 = 223_{-8}^{+7}\) MeV at 68% confidence level, from averaging the RCNP and TAMU \(^{208}\)Pb data sets
- \(K_0 = 221 \pm 4\) MeV at 68% confidence level from the \(^{208}\)Pb-RCNP data alone
- \(K_0 = 230 \pm 10\) MeV at 68% confidence level from the \(^{208}\)Pb-TAMU data alone
- \(K_\tau = -355 \pm 30\) MeV at 68% confidence level, averaged from the \(^{208}\)Pb-RCNP and \(^{208}\)Pb-TAMU data
- \(K_\tau = -350^{+35}_{-25}\) MeV from the \(^{208}\)Pb-RCNP data
- \(K_\tau = -360 \pm 35\) MeV from the \(^{208}\)Pb-TAMU data
- MAP value of \(L\) changes from about 44 MeV to about 54 MeV when including PREXII neutron-skin data

## Context

This work extends decades of efforts to constrain nuclear matter incompressibility, starting from Blaizot (1980) who gave \(K_0 = (210 \pm 30)\) MeV, through later analyses giving ranges of 220-260 MeV (Garg and Colo, 2018; Colo et al., 2014) or around \(235 \pm 30\) MeV (Khan et al., 2012; Margueron et al., 2018). The Bayesian approach quantifies uncertainties more rigorously than traditional \(\chi^2\) minimization and addresses the "soft Tin puzzle" by showing that the posterior PDFs from \(^{120}\)Sn and \(^{208}\)Pb data have significant overlap despite the 5 MeV difference in MAP values. The results enable more precise constraints on the nuclear equation of state for applications in nuclear structure and neutron star physics.
