# QCD-based charge symmetry breaking interaction and the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly

> An approach is proposed to link the charge symmetry breaking (CSB) nuclear interaction and the low-energy constants in quantum chromodynamics (QCD) by matching the CSB effect in nuclear matter. The resulting CSB interaction is applied to study the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, still lacking a satisfactory microscopic understanding, on the energy differences of mirror nuclei by taking $ {}^{17} \mathrm{F} $-$ {}^{17} \mathrm{O} $, $ {}^{15} \mathrm{O} $-$ {}^{15} \mathrm{N} $, $ {}^{41} \mathrm{Sc} $-$ {}^{41} \mathrm{Ca} $, and $ {}^{39} \mathrm{Ca} $-$ {}^{39} \mathrm{K} $ as typical examples. The magnitude and sign of the QCD-based CSB interactions are found to resolve the anomaly successfully within theoretical uncertainties.

## Key claim

The authors propose a method to link the parameters of a Skyrme-type charge symmetry breaking (CSB) nuclear interaction to low-energy constants of quantum chromodynamics (QCD), specifically the up-down quark mass difference and the pion-nucleon sigma term, by matching the CSB effect in infinite nuclear matter. They find that this QCD-based CSB interaction resolves the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer (ONS) anomaly for the energy differences of mirror nuclei within theoretical uncertainties, but the major uncertainty originates from the values of the QCD constants gamma and sigma_piN.

## Method

The authors match the density-dependent neutron-proton mass difference in symmetric nuclear matter derived from QCD sum rules (via the in-medium chiral condensate) to the same quantity derived from a Skyrme-type CSB energy density functional expanded up to order rho^(5/3). This matching constrains the contact (s0_tilde) and momentum-dependent (s1_tilde, s2_tilde) CSB interaction parameters using the QCD constants gamma (related to the up-down quark mass difference) and sigma_piN (the pion-nucleon sigma term). The resulting CSB interaction is then applied in Hartree-Fock calculations with two Skyrme energy density functionals (SGII and SAMi) to compute the mass differences of four mirror-nuclei pairs: F-17/O-17, O-15/N-15, Sc-41/Ca-41, and Ca-39/K-39.

## Key numbers

* The QCD-based contact CSB parameter is s0_tilde = -15.5 (+8.8 / -12.5) MeV fm^3.
* The QCD-based momentum-dependent combination is s1_tilde + 3 s2_tilde = 0.52 (+0.42 / -0.29) MeV fm^5.
* The CSB contribution to the ONS anomaly from the contact term (s0_tilde) dominates, ranging from 211 to 324 keV across the four mirror nuclei and two EDFs.
* For the F-17/O-17 pair with SGII, the total calculated mass difference including CSB is 3.656 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 3.543 MeV (Huang 2021).
* For the Sc-41/Ca-41 pair with SGII, the total calculated mass difference including CSB is 7.252 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 7.278 MeV (Huang 2021).
* The up-down quark mass difference is delta m = 3.6 MeV (renormalization group invariant).
* The experimental neutron-proton mass difference in vacuum is Delta_np(0) = 1.29 MeV.
* The pion-nucleon sigma term is taken as sigma_piN = 45 +/- 15 MeV.
* The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological studies of CSB interactions using Skyrme-type contact forces (Roca-Maza 2018, Baczyk 2018, Sagawa 2019, Naito 2022) by providing a first-principles QCD-based determination of the interaction parameters, which previously had poorly constrained signs and magnitudes. It also builds on QCD sum rule calculations of the neutron-proton mass difference in nuclear matter (Hatsuda 1991) and in-medium chiral perturbation theory (Goda 2013). The results enable systematic studies of isospin symmetry breaking phenomena such as isobaric analog states, super-allowed beta decay for the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix unitarity test, and mass predictions for mirror and isotriplet nuclei near the proton drip line.
