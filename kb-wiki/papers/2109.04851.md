# Exploring the halo character and dipole response in the dripline nucleus $^{31}$F

> Lying at the lower edge of the `island of inversion', neutron-rich Fluorine isotopes ($^{29-31}$F) provide a curious case to study the configuration mixing in this part of the nuclear landscape. Recent studies have suggested that a prospective two-neutron halo in the dripline nucleus $^{31}$F could be linked to the occupancy of the $pf$ intruder configurations. Focusing on configuration mixing, matter radii and neutron-neutron ($nn$) correlations in the ground-state of $^{31}$F, we explore various scenarios to analyze its possible halo nature as well as the low-lying electric dipole ($E$1) response within a three-body approach. We use an analytical, transformed harmonic oscillator basis under the aegis of a hyperspherical formalism to construct the ground state three-body wave function of $^{31}$F. The $^{31}$F ground-state configuration mixing and its matter radius are computed for different choices of the $^{30}$F structure coupled to the valence neutron. The admixture of {$p_{3/2}$, $d_{3/2}$, and $f_{7/2}$} components is found to play an important role, favouring the dominance of inverted configurations with dineutron spreads for two-neutron halo formation. The increase in matter radius with respect to the core radius, $Δr \geqslant$ 0.30 fm and the dipole distributions along with the integrated $B(E1)$ strengths of $\geqslant$ 2.6 $e^2$fm$^2$ are large enough to be compatible with other two-neutron halo nuclei. Three-body results for $^{31}$F indicate a large spatial extension in its ground state due to the inversion of the energy levels of the normal shell model scheme. The increase is augmented by and is proportional to the extent of the $p_{3/2}$ component in the wave function. Additionally, the enhanced dipole distributions and large $B(E1)$ strengths all point to the two-neutron halo character of $^{31}$F.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the dripline nucleus $^{31}$F exhibits a two-neutron halo character, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific admixture of $p_{3/2}$, $d_{3/2}$, and $f_{7/2}$ components in the ground-state wave function, with the halo character being proportional to the extent of the $p_{3/2}$ component.

## Method

The authors model $^{31}$F as a three-body system consisting of an inert $^{29}$F core plus two valence neutrons, using a hyperspherical formalism with a transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis. The neutron-neutron interaction is described by the Gogny-Pires-Tourreil (GPT) potential, while the core-neutron interactions are modeled with Woods-Saxon potentials whose central depths are adjusted to reproduce different configuration scenarios for the unbound $^{30}$F subsystem. The ground-state wave function is constructed by diagonalizing the three-body Hamiltonian, and the dipole response is computed using the pseudostates method.

## Key numbers

- Increase in matter radius relative to the core radius: $\Delta r \geqslant 0.30$ fm
- Integrated $B(E1)$ strength: $\geqslant 2.6$ $e^2$fm$^2$
- Woods-Saxon radius parameter: $r_0 = 1.25$ fm
- Woods-Saxon diffuseness: $a = 0.75$ fm
- Spin-orbit potential depth: $V_{ls} = 16.690$ MeV
- Compton wavelength: $\lambda_\pi = 1.414$ fm
- Central potential depths ($V_0$) for different $lj$ configurations range from 32.750 MeV to 46.490 MeV, with resonance energies ($E_R$) ranging from 0.11 MeV to 2.00 MeV (see Table I in the paper for full listing)

## Context

This work extends previous studies on neutron-rich fluorine isotopes at the lower edge of the "island of inversion," particularly following the experimental establishment of the dripline at $^{31}$F by Ahn et al. (2019) and the confirmation of $^{29}$F as the heaviest Borromean halo by several groups (2020). The paper addresses competing predictions from earlier work: the "pairing anti-halo effect" proposed by Hagino and Sagawa (2011) and Bennaceur et al. (2000), and the Gamow Shell Model calculations by Ma et al. (2020) suggesting a $p_{3/2}$-dominated halo with $S_{2n}=0.17$ MeV. The three-body approach enables calculation of both structural properties and dipole response, providing observables that can be compared with future experimental measurements to distinguish between normal shell-model, intruder, and inverted configurations.
