# A covariant gauge-invariant three-dimensional description of relativistic bound-states

> A formalism is presented which allows covariant three-dimensional bound-state equations to be derived systematically from four-dimensional ones without the use of delta-functions. The amplitude for the interaction of a bound state described by these equations with an electromagnetic probe is constructed. This amplitude is shown to be gauge invariant if the formalism is truncated at the same coupling-constant order in both the interaction kernel of the integral equation and the electromagnetic current operator.

## Key claim

The paper presents a covariant three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation that avoids delta-function constraints and maintains gauge invariance order-by-order, provided the interaction kernel and the electromagnetic current operator are truncated at the same coupling-constant order.

## Method

The authors split the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter kernel \(K\) into a part \(K_1\) that does not depend on the component of relative momentum parallel to a chosen four-vector \(D\), and a remainder \(K_2\). By choosing \(D = P\) (the total four-momentum of the two-body system), they obtain a covariant three-dimensional integral equation for the vertex function \(\Gamma_1\). The electromagnetic current for the bound state is constructed by inserting the photon into the three-dimensional Green's function, and gauge invariance is verified by showing that the Ward-Takahashi identity holds when the kernel and current are truncated at the same order in the coupling constant.

## Key numbers

* The three-dimensional propagator \(\langle G_0 \rangle\) is given explicitly for spin-zero and spin-half particles in Eq. (3.13), with the form \(\langle G_0 \rangle (p_{\perp D};P^2) = \frac{\Lambda_1^+ \Lambda_2^+}{P_{\parallel D} + i\eta - \epsilon_1 - \epsilon_2} - \frac{\Lambda_1^- \Lambda_2^-}{P_{\parallel D} - i\eta + \epsilon_1 + \epsilon_2}\), where \(\epsilon_i = \sqrt{m_i^2 - {p_i}_{\perp D}^2}\).
* The second-order kernel \(K_1^{(2)}\) is given by Eq. (3.14): \(K_1^{(2)} = \langle G_0 \rangle^{-1} \langle G_0 K^{(2)} G_0 \rangle \langle G_0 \rangle^{-1}\).
* The fourth-order kernel \(K_1^{(4)}\) is given by Eq. (3.15): \(K_1^{(4)} = \langle G_0 \rangle^{-1} \bigl( \langle G_0 K^{(4)} G_0 \rangle + \langle G_0 K^{(2)} G_0 K^{(2)} G_0 \rangle \bigr) \langle G_0 \rangle^{-1} - K_1^{(2)} \langle G_0 \rangle K_1^{(2)}\).
* The bound-state mass is denoted \(M\) (no numerical value given), and the photon momentum is \(Q\).

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional reduction formalism of Phillips and Wallace (1996) to a covariant setting, and addresses the gauge-invariance problem that arises when reducing four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equations to three dimensions. It builds on the Bethe-Salpeter analyses of nucleon-nucleon scattering by Fleischer and Tjon (1975, 1977, 1980) and electron-deuteron scattering by Zuilhof and Tjon (1980, 1981), as well as the three-dimensional approaches of Blankenbecler-Sugar (1966), Logunov-Tavkhelidze (1963), and Klein (1953, 1954, 1958, 1974). The formalism enables gauge-invariant calculations of electromagnetic observables for relativistic bound states (e.g., the deuteron) using three-dimensional equations, which are simpler than full four-dimensional treatments and can be extended to three-body systems.
