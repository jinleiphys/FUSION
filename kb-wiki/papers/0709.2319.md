# Electroproduction of Soft Pions at Large Momentum Transfers

> 8 pages, 3 figures; Invited talk at the workshop on Exclusive Reactions at High Momentum Transfer, 21-24 May 2007, Newport News, Virginia, U.S.A. and International Conference on hadron Physics TROIA'07, 30 Aug. - 3 Sept. 2007, Canakkale, Turkey

## Key claim

The authors present a QCD-motivated model for the S-wave multipoles \(E_{0+}\) and \(L_{0+}\) in threshold pion electroproduction that interpolates between the low-energy theorem predictions at \(Q^2 \sim 1\) GeV\(^2\) and the large-\(Q^2\) limit, but they caution that the model accuracy is only about 50% and that the predictions for the structure function \(F_2^p\) lie somewhat below the existing SLAC E136 data.

## Method

The authors use light-cone sum rules (LCSR) to calculate the generalized form factors \(G_1^{\pi N}\) and \(G_2^{\pi N}\) for the reactions \(\gamma^* p \to \pi^0 p\) and \(\gamma^* p \to \pi^+ n\) at threshold, for momentum transfers \(Q^2\) in the range 1–10 GeV\(^2\). They reanalyze sum rules from Braun (2006) by including semi-disconnected pion-nucleon contributions, which extends the applicability to lower \(Q^2\). The LCSR predictions are used only for form factor ratios, and the absolute normalization is taken from experimental fits to the nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

## Key numbers

- The dipole formula used for normalization is \(G_D(Q^2) = 1/(1+Q^2/\mu_0^2)^2\) with \(\mu_0^2 = 0.71\) GeV\(^2\).
- The proton electric form factor fit from combined JLab data in the range \(0.5 < Q^2 < 5.6\) GeV\(^2\) is \(\mu_p G_E^p / G_M^p = 1 - 0.13 (Q^2 - 0.04)\).
- The integrated cross sections scale as \(\sigma_{\gamma^*p \to \pi N} \sim 1/Q^6\).
- The ratio of \(\pi^0 p\) to \(\pi^+ n\) final states is approximately \(1:2\) and almost \(Q^2\)-independent.
- The nucleon mass is taken as \(m = 939\) MeV, the axial coupling \(g_A = 1.267\), and the pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the LCSR approach previously applied to baryon form factors by Braun (2001) and to pion electroproduction by Braun (2006), and it bridges the gap between the low-energy theorem predictions from current algebra and chiral perturbation theory (Bernard, 1995) and the perturbative QCD factorization approach suggested by Pire, Polyakov, and Szymanowski (2001). The results provide a model for threshold cross sections that can be tested at Jefferson Laboratory, HERMES, and MAMI, and the authors note that improvements would require radiative corrections to the LCSRs and more accurate lattice calculations of nucleon distribution amplitudes.
