# Baryonic coherent state formation from small domain disoriented chiral condensates

> Rare hyperon yields such as the Omega and AntiOmega in heavy ion collision experiments are hard to be reproduced by numerical models. This, in combination with the thermal fit to SPS data, seems to call for a new production mechanism beyond the usual ones. Small domain disoriented chiral condensates (DCC) were proposed to be such a source of rare hyperons through skyrmion formation at the chiral phase transition. Here skyrmions are treated as coherent states of baryons on a compact manifold so that the distribution of baryons produced from a skyrmion can be known. From this more refined treatment, the number of topological defects produced are more than doubled to 30 or more and the domain size at the SPS is found to be even smaller than before at 1.1--1.5 fm. IT IS IMPERATIVE THEREFORE NOT TO USE ONLY PION DISTRIBUTION BUT OTHER MEANS FOR OBSERVING DCC.

# Baryonic coherent state formation from small domain disoriented chiral condensates

## Key claim

Small domain disoriented chiral condensates (DCC) can produce rare hyperons like Omega and AntiOmega through skyrmion formation at the chiral phase transition, but the earlier assumption of equal production probability for all octet and decuplet baryons from a skyrmion is incorrect; the refined treatment using coherent states on a compact manifold shows the octet-to-decuplet ratio is between 5:1 and 14:1, leading to a domain size of 1.1–1.5 fm at SPS energies, which is even smaller than the previous estimate of 2.0 fm.

## Method

The authors constructed baryonic coherent states on the compact manifold S³ using the collective coordinate quantization of the Skyrme model, mapping from SU(2) to SO(3) coordinate operators to avoid mixing of physical half-integer spin states with unphysical integer spin states. They then applied the resulting probability distribution for baryon states emerging from a skyrmion at low energy appropriate to DCC, using the octet and decuplet baryon mass difference and setting the fundamental energy scale ω ~ T_c (150–200 MeV), to compute the relative abundances of baryon species.

## Key numbers

- Octet-to-decuplet baryon ratio from a skyrmion: between 5:1 and 14:1, depending on the exact value of T_c used (150–200 MeV)
- Domain size ξ at SPS: 1.1–1.5 fm, smaller than the previous estimate of 2.0 fm from Kapusta and Wong (2001)
- Number of topological defects (skyrmions and antiskyrmions) produced: 30–65, more than double the previous estimate of 14
- Skyrmion formation probability: 0.8 skyrmion and antiskyrmion per correlation volume ξ³, from Kapusta and Srivastava (1992)

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Kapusta and Wong (2001) that DCC could produce hyperons through skyrmion formation, removing their earlier assumption of equal production probability for any octet and decuplet baryon. The refined treatment uses the coherent state formalism of Hall (1994) and Twareque Ali et al. (1995) on compact manifolds, applied to the Skyrme model wavefunctions of Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983). The results show that DCC domains at SPS are too small for observation via pion distributions as argued by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993), but the mechanism remains compatible with SPS data and motivates alternative observational strategies such as pion/kaon fluctuations proposed by Gavin and Kapusta (2002).
