# Relativistic Viscous Hydrodynamics in the Density Frame: Numerical Tests and Comparisons

> We conduct a numerical study of relativistic viscous fluid dynamics in the Density Frame for one-dimensional fluid flows. The Density Frame is a formulation of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics that is first-order in time, requires no auxiliary fields, and has no non-hydrodynamic modes. We compare our results to QCD kinetic theory simulations and find excellent agreement within the regime of applicability of hydrodynamics. Additionally, the Density Frame results remain well-behaved and robust near the boundary of applicability. We also compare our findings to the second-order-in-time hydrodynamic theory developed by Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun (BDNK) and a well-known Müller-Israel-Stewart-type hydrodynamics code, MUSIC, which is commonly used to simulate heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The Density Frame formulation of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, which is first-order in time and has no non-hydrodynamic modes, shows excellent agreement with QCD kinetic theory simulations within the regime of applicability of hydrodynamics, and remains well-behaved and robust near the boundary of applicability where second-order formulations like BDNK develop oscillatory structures.

## Method
The authors solved the relativistic Navier-Stokes equations in the Density Frame for one-dimensional fluid flows using an operator-splitting approach with Implicit-Explicit (IMEX) time integrators, where the ideal hydrodynamic evolution was treated explicitly via a Kurganov-Tadmor scheme and the viscous evolution was treated implicitly. They compared these results to QCD kinetic theory simulations for a 1+1 dimensional expanding system, to the BDNK second-order hydrodynamic theory using the finite difference code of Pandya and Pretorius (2021), and to the MUSIC code implementing DNMR-type Müller-Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics.

- Gaussian initial condition parameters: amplitude A = 0.48 GeV^4, width L = 5 GeV^(-1), baseline δ = 0.12 GeV^4
- Evolution time: t = 50.0 GeV^(-1) ≈ 5.8 L/c_s

- Shear viscosity kernel: κ^(xxxx)(v) = (4/3) η / [γ^4 (1 - v^2 c_s^2)^2], where c_s^2 = dp/de

## Context
This work extends the Density Frame approach previously studied for the advection-diffusion equation by Basar et al. (2024) to the full relativistic Navier-Stokes equations, and compares it to the BDNK formulation developed by Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun (2017-2019) and to the DNMR variant of Müller-Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics implemented in the MUSIC code by Schenke et al. (2010-2015). The numerical tests are guided by the BDNK study of Pandya and Pretorius (2021). The QCD kinetic theory simulations are the first of their kind for 1+1 dimensional expanding systems, extending earlier work by Kurkela et al. (2014-2018) and Du et al. (2020). The Density Frame's first-order-in-time structure and absence of non-hydrodynamic modes enables cleaner separation of viscous response from non-universal dynamics, and provides a framework for stochastic relativistic fluid dynamics via Metropolis-type algorithms.
