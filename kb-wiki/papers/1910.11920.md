# Derivation of Relativistic Yakubovsky Equations under Poincaré Invariance

> Relativistic Faddeev-Yakubovsky four-nucleon scattering equations are derived including a 3-body force. We present these equations in the momentum space representation. The quadratic integral equations using the iteration method, in order to obtain boosted potentials and 3-body force, are demonstrated.

## Key claim

Relativistic Faddeev-Yakubovsky four-nucleon scattering equations are derived under Poincaré invariance, including a 3-body force, but the equations keep a similar form to the nonrelativistic ones because the relativistic effects are absorbed into boosted potentials and boosted 3-body forces defined through quadratic integral equations solved by iteration.

## Method

The author starts from the Bakamjian-Thomas theory and defines boosted two-body potentials by Lorentz-transforming the invariant mass of a two-body subsystem to a frame with nonzero total momentum. For the three-body system, the relativistic Faddeev equation is written using these boosted potentials and a three-body Green's function. For the four-body system, the Yakubovsky equations are rewritten with boosted potentials, a four-body Green's function, and boosted 3-body forces, using permutation operators to handle identical particles and separating the equations into two Yakubovsky components (ψ₁ and ψ₂) for the [3+1] and [2+2] partition channels.

## Key numbers

- The Jacobian for transforming two-body momenta to relative and total momentum in the equal-mass case is given as 𝒥₁₂|_(m₁=m₂) = (E₁ E₂)/(E₁+E₂) × 4/√((E₁+E₂)² - (p₁+p₂)²) (Eq. G3.16).
- The boosted potential V₁₂(P₁₂) is defined as √((2√(m²+k₁₂²)+v₁₂)² + P₁₂²) - √(4(m²+k₁₂²)+P₁₂²) (Eq. V).
- The three-body Green's function is G₀ = 1/[M₁₂₃ - (√(4(m²+k̂ᵢⱼ²)+p̂ₖ²) + √(m²+p̂ₖ²))].
- The four-body Green's function is G₀^(4) = 1/[M₁₂₃₄ - (√((√(4(m²+k̂₁₂²)+q̂₃²)+√(m²+q̂₃²))²+p̂₄²)+√(m²+p̂₄²))] or equivalently 1/[M₁₂₃₄ - (√((2√(m²+k̂₁₂²))²+ŝ₄²)+√((2√(m²+k̂₃₄²))²+ŝ₄²))] (Eq. Green4).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous relativistic Faddeev three-nucleon calculations (Kamada 2003, Kamada 2008, Kamada 2017) to the four-nucleon system, following the Bakamjian-Thomas approach (Bakamjian 1953). It generalizes the nonrelativistic Yakubovsky equations (Yakubovsky 1966) to a Poincaré-invariant framework, and also incorporates 3-body forces following earlier work (Huber 1996, Kamada 2019). The equations enable future four-nucleon scattering calculations where relativistic effects, which are small in three-body scattering (Sekiguchi 2005, Maeda 2007) but may accumulate with more particles, can be systematically studied.
