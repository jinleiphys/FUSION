# Probing nuclear skins and halos with elastic electron scattering

> I investigate the elastic electron scattering off nuclei far from the stability line. The effects of the neutron and proton skins and halos on the differential cross sections are explored. Examples are given for the charge distribution in Sn isotopes and its relation to the neutron skin. The neutron halo in $^{11}$Li and the proton halo in $^{8}$B are also investigated. Particular interest is paid to the inverse scattering problem and its dependence on the experimental precision. These studies are of particular interest for the upcoming electron ion colliders at the GSI and RIKEN facilities.

## Key claim

Elastic electron scattering can reveal signatures of neutron skins, proton skins, and halos in nuclei far from stability, but the extraction of detailed charge-density profiles from measured cross sections is limited by experimental precision and the inverse scattering problem, particularly for heavy nuclei where the shift of the first diffraction minimum with neutron skin is small (Δq₁ ≈ 2/A^(8/3) fm⁻¹).

## Method

The author uses the plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA) with an effective momentum transfer correction (q_eff) to account for Coulomb distortion, and also employs full Dirac-equation distorted-wave calculations for realistic cross sections. Charge densities are constructed from point-proton and point-neutron distributions folded with nucleon charge form factors (using the Galster parametrization for the neutron), with nuclear densities generated from Fermi distributions for heavy nuclei and from Skyrme Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations (BKN interaction with spin-orbit term) for light halo nuclei such as ¹¹Li and ⁸B. The inverse scattering problem is studied by examining how the position of the first diffraction minimum (q₁) and the height of the second maximum depend on the neutron skin thickness and asymmetry parameter δ = (N−Z)/A.

## Key numbers

- For ¹³²Sn, the first diffraction minimum occurs at q₁ = 0.754 fm⁻¹ = 149 MeV/c; for ¹⁰⁰Sn, q₁ = 0.806 fm⁻¹ = 159 MeV/c.
- For ⁵³Ca, q₁ = 1.11 fm⁻¹; the experimental resolution on q₁ must be within 1% to achieve δ⟨r_p²⟩^(1/2) < 0.05 fm.
- The neutron skin of stable nuclei follows Δr_np = (−0.04 ± 0.03) + (1.01 ± 0.15)δ fm (from antiprotonic atom data, Trz01).
- For ¹¹Li, the HF calculation yields a two-neutron separation energy S_2n(theo) = 0.274 MeV, compared to the experimental S_2n(exp) = 0.247 ± 0.080 MeV; the calculated matter rms radius is 3.26 fm (core ⁹Li: 2.45 fm) versus the experimental 3.62 ± 0.19 fm.
- For ⁸B, the charge form factor width is Δ_ch = 0.505 fm⁻¹ = 99.6 MeV/c; the charge rms radius is ⟨r_ch²⟩^(1/2) = 2.82 fm, matching the experimental value 2.82 ± 0.06 fm (Blan97).
- The width difference between matter and charge form factors in ⁸B is approximately 10% (Δ_tot = 0.545 fm⁻¹ = 108 MeV/c vs. Δ_ch = 0.505 fm⁻¹).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of elastic electron scattering (Hofstadter, 1956; 1961) to unstable nuclei far from stability, building on the discovery of halo nuclei via interaction cross sections (Tanihata, 1985) and on neutron-skin studies from antiprotonic atoms (Trzcinska, 2001) and liquid-drop model improvements (Danielewicz, 2003). It provides predictions for upcoming electron-ion collider experiments at GSI (Haik, 2005) and RIKEN (Suda, 2001), showing that while neutron halos produce a narrow peak in the matter form factor at low q (as seen in knockout reactions), the charge form factor of proton-halo nuclei like ⁸B does not show a similarly pronounced two-component structure, motivating future electron scattering measurements to resolve this discrepancy.
