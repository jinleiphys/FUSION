# Validity of Certain Soft Photon Amplitudes

> Certain soft photon amplitudes which have been recently suggested as alternatives to the usual Low form of the soft photon approximation are studied and it is demonstrated that problems exist in their relation to the corresponding non-radiative amplitude. The non-radiative amplitude, which is an input to soft photon calculations, is in certain cases required to be evaluated outside of its physical phase space region. Also, for the case of two-body identical particle bremsstrahlung processes, the symmetrized or antisymmetrized form of these soft photon amplitudes cannot be written in terms of the symmetrized or antisymmetrized amplitude for the non-radiative process. It is found that the usual Low form of the soft photon theorem is essentially unaffected by these problems.

## Key claim

The alternative soft photon amplitudes (TuTts and TsTts) proposed by Liou, Timmermans and Gibson (1995) suffer from two fundamental problems that the standard Low form avoids: the non-radiative amplitude must be evaluated outside its physically measurable phase space region in certain cases, and for identical-particle bremsstrahlung the symmetrized or antisymmetrized form of these amplitudes cannot be expressed in terms of the symmetrized or antisymmetrized non-radiative amplitude.

## Method

The authors derived the spin-0 versions of the Low, TsTts, and TuTts soft photon amplitudes from the external radiation contribution by expanding the off-shell non-radiative amplitude in powers of photon momentum and imposing gauge invariance. They then analyzed the kinematic constraints on the Mandelstam variables (s, t, u) for the radiative process compared to the non-radiative elastic process, mapping the regions of phase space accessible to each amplitude for pi- p and pp scattering. For the symmetrization problem, they examined whether the symmetrized radiative amplitude could be written as a function of the symmetrized non-radiative amplitude by checking the algebraic relations among the radiative variables.

## Key numbers

* For pi- p at a laboratory pion energy of 298 MeV (corresponding to the experiment of Nefkens (1978)), the TsTts amplitude requires evaluation of the non-radiative amplitude at points (s_34, t_24) that extend far outside the physically measurable region of the (s,t) plane, where the elastic amplitude is known up to sqrt(s) approximately 1.35 GeV.
* For the TRIUMF pp experiment of Michaelian (1990) at 280 MeV, the TsTts amplitude points (s_34, t_13) and (s_34, t_24) fall outside the physical elastic pp region, while (s_12, t_13) and (s_12, t_24) lie inside.
* The pi- p gamma experiment of Nefkens (1978) measured photon energies in the range 15 to 150 MeV, covering the majority of radiative phase space where the TsTts phase space problem occurs.
* The 200 MeV pp bremsstrahlung experiment of Rogers (1980) with outgoing proton angles fixed at 16.4 degrees on each side of the beam axis also produces TsTts evaluation points outside the elastic region.
* For identical particle scattering, the Low amplitude variables (overline(s), overline(t)) satisfy overline(s) + overline(t) + overline(u) = 4m^2 and the constraints overline(s) >= 4m^2 and 0 >= overline(t), overline(u) >= -(overline(s)-4m^2), ensuring all radiative points lie within the non-radiative region.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of soft photon approximations for bremsstrahlung processes, particularly pp bremsstrahlung, where the Low form (Low, 1958) had given a good description of older data (Rogers, 1980) but showed disagreement with newer polarization data from the TRIUMF 280 MeV experiment (Michaelian, 1990), indicating non-trivial off-shell effects. The paper directly examines the alternative TsTts and TuTts amplitudes proposed by Liou, Timmermans and Gibson (1995), which claimed better agreement with pp gamma data. The findings show that the Low form is immune to both the phase space and symmetrization problems, while the alternatives are not, which enables future work to confidently use the Low form for model-independent comparisons between bremsstrahlung data and non-radiative scattering, and clarifies that discrepancies between the TuTts amplitude and data cannot be interpreted as evidence for off-shell effects without resolving these issues.
