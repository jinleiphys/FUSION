# Nuclear shell structure and response toward the limits of mass, temperature and isospin

> We present a short overview of our recent theoretical developments aiming at the description of exotic nuclear phenomena to be reached and studied at the next-generation radioactive beam facilities. Applications to nuclear shell structure and response of nuclei at the limits of their existence, with a special focus on the physics cases of astrophysical importance, are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a short overview of recent theoretical developments, not a single new result; the abstract's claim of "applications to nuclear shell structure and response" is hedged by the fact that the work is a conference proceedings summary of multiple ongoing projects, each with its own conditions (e.g., the shell-gap delocalization in superheavy Z=120 isotopes depends on the interplay of pairing and quasiparticle-vibration coupling, and the finite-temperature upbend appears only in medium-mass nuclei, not heavy ones).

## Method

The authors apply three complementary theoretical frameworks: (1) a relativistic quasiparticle-vibration coupling (QVC) model extending covariant density functional theory to superfluid systems, solving the Dyson equation in doubled quasiparticle space of Dirac spinors; (2) a comparison of Gamow-Teller response in neutron-rich 132Sn using non-relativistic QRPA with realistic G-matrix interaction, covariant DFT-based RRPA and its extension RTBA (relativistic time blocking approximation), and shell-model configuration interaction; (3) a finite-temperature continuum QRPA using Matsubara temperature Green functions in coordinate space to compute radiative dipole strength functions.

## Key numbers

- Single-quasiparticle spectrum of 120Sn: QVC results show excellent agreement with experimental data (Fig. 1a), though no explicit numerical values are given in the text.
- Superheavy Z=120 isotopes: neutron number increases from N=172 to N=184; the proton shell closure at Z=120 remains "rather stable" while the neutron shell gap is "almost steady" with no sharp neutron numbers as spherical shell closures.
- Gamow-Teller strength distribution in 132Sn: compared across QRPA, RRPA, RTBA, and shell-model (Fig. 2b), but no explicit numerical strength values are provided in the text.
- 94Mo gamma-strength at excitation energy around its neutron separation energy: the upbend appears as a typical feature in medium-mass nuclei, while in heavy nuclei the strength is flat at E_gamma -> 0 (no explicit numerical values given for the strength function).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic quasiparticle-vibration coupling framework of Litvinova and Ring (2006) and Litvinova (2012) to superfluid systems and superheavy nuclei, and contrasts with pure mean-field studies by Zhang, Meng, and Zhou (2005) that predicted sharp neutron shell closures. The finite-temperature upbend phenomenon, first reported by Voinov (2004) and observed systematically by Wiedeking (2012), is shown to have significant impact on r-process nucleosynthesis as demonstrated by Litvinova and Goriely (2010). The comparative study of Gamow-Teller response in 132Sn across ab-initio, shell-model, and DFT approaches (citing Bertsch, 2013; Fang, 2013; and Holt and Brown, 2013) enables future constraints from rare isotope facility measurements.
