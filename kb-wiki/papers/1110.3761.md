# Conference Discussion of the Nuclear Force

> Discussion of the nuclear force, lead by a round table consisting of T. Cohen, E. Epelbaum, R. Machleidt, and F. Gross (chair). After an invited talk by Machleidt, published elsewhere in these proceedings, brief remarks are made by Epelbaum, Cohen, and Gross, followed by discussion from the floor moderated by the chair. The chair asked the round table and the participants to focus on the following issues: (i) What does each approach (chiral effective field theory, large Nc, and relativistic phenomenology) contribute to our knowledge of the nuclear force? Do we need them all? Is any one transcendent? (ii) How important for applications (few body, nuclear structure, EMC effect, for example) are precise fits to the NN data below 350 MeV? How precise do these fits have to be? (iii) Can we learn anything about nonperturbative QCD from these studies of the nuclear force? The discussion presented here is based on a video recording made at the conference and transcribed afterward.

## Key claim
The paper is a transcribed round-table discussion, not a presentation of new results; its central claim is that multiple theoretical approaches (chiral effective field theory, large Nc, and relativistic phenomenology) each contribute valuable but incomplete insights into the nuclear force, and no single approach is transcendent, though the relativistic Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) may offer a more efficient description of data with fewer parameters than chiral EFT.

## Method
The session began with short opening statements from Epelbaum (on chiral EFT), Cohen (on large Nc), and Gross (on relativistic CST), followed by moderated discussion from the floor. The chair posed three guiding questions: (i) what each approach contributes and whether any is transcendent; (ii) how important precise fits to NN data below 350 MeV are for applications; (iii) whether nonperturbative QCD can be learned from these studies. The discussion was video recorded and later transcribed.

## Key numbers
- The WJC-1 CST model, with 27 parameters, achieved a χ²/N_data of 1.06 on the 2007 np data set (3788 data points) below 350 MeV.
- The WJC-2 CST model, with only 15 parameters, achieved a χ²/N_data of approximately 1.12 on the same data set.
- Chiral EFT at N³LO requires 24 parameters for precision fits to NN data.
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ_χ is estimated as 4πF_π ~ 1200 MeV.
- The deuteron binding energy is of order N_c^1 (using large N_c analysis) but is only 2 MeV, while the nucleon-delta mass difference (order 1/N_c) is nearly 150 times larger.
- The CST model with off-shell parameter ν_σ that gives the best fit to two-body data also yields the correct triton binding energy without any three-body forces.

## Context
This discussion extends the long-standing debate on the nature of the nuclear force, contrasting chiral EFT (Weinberg 1990), large Nc QCD (t Hooft 1973, Witten 1979), and relativistic phenomenology (Gross 1969, 1974, 1982). It directly addresses the tension between scale-separation arguments in EFT and the empirical success of meson-exchange models. The paper enables future work on reconciling these approaches, particularly by testing whether the CST’s off-shell couplings can systematically replace infinite classes of loop diagrams and three-body forces, and by exploring whether the large Nc limit can justify the one-boson-exchange approximation from QCD.
