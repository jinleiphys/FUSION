# The eLSM at nonzero density

> The extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) is an effective model of QCD which includes in the mesonic sector (pseudo)scalar and (axial-)vector quarkonia mesons as well as one dilaton/glueball field and in the baryonic sector the nucleon doublet and its chiral partner in the mirror assignment. The chiral partner of the pion turns out to be the resonance $f_{0}(1370),$ which is then predominantly a quarkonium state. As a consequence, $f_{0}(500)$ is predominately not a quarkonium state but a four-quark object and is at first not part of the model. Yet, $f_{0}(500)$ is important in the baryonic sector and affects nuclear matter saturation, the high-density behavior, and nucleon-nucleon scattering. In these proceedings, we show how to enlarge the two-flavour version of the eLSM in order to include the four-quark field $f_{0}(500)$ in a chiral invariant manner. We then discuss homogeneous and inhomogeneous chiral restoration in a dense medium.

# The eLSM at nonzero density

## Key claim

The extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) can be enlarged to include the four-quark field \(f_0(500)\) in a chirally invariant manner, and this inclusion is necessary to describe nuclear matter saturation, compressibility, and the high-density behavior; however, the homogeneous chiral phase transition predicted at a critical chemical potential \(\mu_c^{\text{hom}} \sim 1\) GeV is preempted by an inhomogeneous chiral-spiral condensation that occurs at a lower critical chemical potential \(\mu_c^{\text{inhom}} \lesssim 1\) GeV, meaning chiral symmetry is only partially restored in dense matter.

## Method

The two-flavor eLSM Lagrangian, which contains (pseudo)scalar and (axial-)vector quarkonia mesons, a dilaton/glueball field, and nucleon doublets in the mirror assignment, was extended by adding a chiral-singlet scalar field \(\chi\) representing \(f_0(500)\) with a coupling to baryons that replaces the chirally invariant mass parameter \(m_0\) by \(a\chi\). The thermodynamic potential was minimized with respect to the condensates \(\phi = \langle \sigma_N \rangle\), \(\bar{\chi} = \langle \chi \rangle\), and \(\langle \omega^0 \rangle\) at finite chemical potential \(\mu\), first assuming homogeneous condensation and then allowing for an inhomogeneous chiral-spiral ansatz \(\phi(z) = \bar{\phi} \cos(2fz)\) with a neutral pion condensate \(\langle \pi^3 \rangle = \bar{\phi} \sin(2fz)\).

## Key numbers

- The critical chemical potential for homogeneous chiral restoration is \(\mu_c^{\text{hom}} \sim 1\) GeV, corresponding to a density \(\rho / \rho_0 \sim 2.7\) (where \(\rho_0\) is nuclear matter saturation density), with a mixed phase extending from \(2.7 \rho_0\) to \(10 \rho_0\).
- The compressibility \(K\) lies in the range \(200\)–\(250\) MeV, consistent with the experimental range of \(200\)–\(300\) MeV.
- The mass of \(f_0(500)\) is \(m_\chi = (0.475 \pm 0.25)\) GeV.
- In the inhomogeneous phase, the wave number \(f\) jumps to a finite value of about \(400\) MeV at \(\mu_c^{\text{inhom}}\) and then slowly increases with \(\mu\).

## Context

This work extends the eLSM framework developed for \(N_f=2\) in Refs. (elsmnf2) and for baryons in Refs. (susannaold) by incorporating the four-quark field \(f_0(500)\), which was previously omitted because the chiral partner of the pion is identified with \(f_0(1370)\) as a quarkonium state. The results build on the homogeneous density study of Ref. (susagiu) and the inhomogeneous condensation analysis of Ref. (achim2), showing that the chiral-spiral ansatz is energetically favored over homogeneous condensation. Future work should test arbitrary types of inhomogeneous condensation using the numerical procedure of Ref. (achimlast).
