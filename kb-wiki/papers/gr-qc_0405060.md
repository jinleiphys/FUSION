# Simplification of the Spectral Analysis of the Volume Operator in Loop Quantum Gravity

> The Volume Operator plays a crucial role in the definition of the quantum dynamics of Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG). Efficient calculations for dynamical problems of LQG can therefore be performed only if one has sufficient control over the volume spectrum. While closed formulas for the matrix elements are currently available in the literature, these are complicated polynomials in 6j symbols which in turn are given in terms of Racah's formula which is too complicated in order to perform even numerical calculations for the semiclassically important regime of large spins. Hence, so far not even numerically the spectrum could be accessed. In this article we demonstrate that by means of the Elliot -- Biedenharn identity one can get rid of all the 6j symbols for any valence of the gauge invariant vertex, thus immensely reducing the computational effort. We use the resulting compact formula to study numerically the spectrum of the gauge invariant 4 -- vertex. The techniques derived in this paper could be of use also for the analysis of spin -- spin interaction Hamiltonians of many -- particle problems in atomic and nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that by applying the Elliot-Biedenharn identity, all 6j symbols and sums over intertwiners can be eliminated from the matrix element formula for the volume operator in Loop Quantum Gravity for any valence of the gauge invariant vertex, thereby enabling numerical calculations for the semiclassically important regime of large spins that were previously impossible.

## Method

The authors start from the closed formula for volume operator matrix elements expressed as complicated polynomials in 6j symbols and sums over intertwiners, as given in prior work by Thiemann (2001). They apply the Elliot-Biedenharn identity to systematically eliminate all 6j symbols and summation over intermediate recoupling steps, reducing the expression to a polynomial of simple roots of fractional expressions in the spins and intertwiners without factorials. The resulting compact formula is then used to numerically diagonalize the volume operator for the gauge invariant four-valent vertex, with spin occupations up to approximately 2j_max ≈ 10^2.

## Key numbers

- Previous numerical calculations could not go beyond j=3 for the four-valent vertex using the matrix element formulas available in the literature
- The new method enables diagonalization of the volume operator for the four-valent vertex "in a couple of hours" for spin occupations up to 2j_max ≈ 10^2
- The paper reports numerical evidence for a universal density distribution in terms of properly rescaled quantities valid at large spin
- The paper reports numerical evidence for the existence of a volume gap (smallest non-zero eigenvalue) for the four-valent vertex

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the volume operator spectrum previously studied by De Pietri (1997) and Thiemann (2001), who provided closed formulas for matrix elements but could not access the spectrum numerically even for moderate spins. The simplification enables numerical investigations of dynamical questions in canonical LQG, potentially extending results from cosmological minisuperspace truncations to the full theory, and could also be applied to convergence issues of spin foam models and to spin-spin interaction Hamiltonians in atomic and nuclear physics.
