# Cumulant Expansion in Gluon Saturation, and Five and Six-Gluon Azimuthal Correlations

> Correlations between the momenta of the final state hadrons measured in proton or nucleus collisions contain information that sheds light on the initial conditions and evolutionary dynamics of the collision system. These correlation measurements have revealed the long-range rapidity correlations in p-p and p-Pb systems, and they have also made it possible to extract the elliptic flow coefficient from hadron correlation measurements. In this work, we calculate five- and six-gluon correlation functions in the framework of saturation physics by using superdiagrams. We also derive the cumulant expansion of the gluon correlators that is valid in the gluon saturation limit. We show that the cumulant expansion of the gluon correlators that is used for counting the number of diagrams to be calculated does not follow the standard cumulant expansion. We also explain how these findings can be used in obtaining experimentally relevant observables such as flow coefficients calculated from correlations as well as ratios of the correlation functions of different orders.

## Key claim

The paper derives five- and six-gluon correlation functions in the gluon saturation framework, but the cumulant expansion used to count glasma diagrams must be modified from the standard form because the leading rainbow glasma approximation never includes diagrams that mix upper and lower rainbow components.

## Method

The author calculates connected n-gluon azimuthal correlation functions using glasma superdiagrams, which encode the unintegrated gluon distribution functions of the target and projectile. A modified "rainbow" cumulant expansion is derived using partial Bell polynomials, where terms mixing upper and lower rainbow diagrams are suppressed by factors of 1/2. The five- and six-gluon correlation functions are then constructed from the general n-gluon formula and verified by comparing the number of terms against the rainbow cumulant prediction.

## Key numbers

- The fourth-order moment μ₄ = 209, with κ₁ = 1, κ₂ = 4, κ₃ = 16, and the corrected κ₄ = 96 connected rainbow glasma diagrams (not 72 from the standard cumulant expansion)
- The fifth-order moment μ₅ = 1889, yielding κ₅ = 768 connected diagrams for the five-gluon correlation function
- The sixth-order moment μ₆ = 20789, yielding κ₆ = 7680 connected diagrams for the six-gluon correlation function
- The five-gluon correlation function requires 3 superdiagrams (plus 3 mirror images) to generate all 768 terms
- The six-gluon correlation function requires 4 superdiagrams (plus 4 mirror images) to generate all 7680 terms

## Context

This work extends the glasma diagram approach previously applied to three- and four-gluon correlations by Ozonder (2014) to five- and six-gluon correlations. The modified cumulant expansion corrects a counting error first identified in that earlier four-gluon calculation. The six-gluon correlation function can be used to compute the elliptic flow coefficient v₂{6} for comparison with experimental measurements from the LHC, and the results can be contrasted with hydrodynamic predictions for small collision systems such as p-p and p-Pb.
