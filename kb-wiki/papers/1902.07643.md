# Constraining energy loss from high-$p_{\rm T}$ azimuthal asymmetries

> The nuclear modification factor $R_{\rm AA}$ has been satisfactorily described by various jet quenching models. Nonetheless, all these formalisms, until very recently, underpredicted the high-$p_{\rm T}$ (> 10 GeV) elliptic flow $v_2$. We find that the simultaneous description of these observables requires to strongly suppress the quenching for the first $\sim 0.6$ fm after the collision. This shows the potential of jet quenching observables to constrain the dynamics of the initial stages of the evolution.

# Constraining energy loss from high-$p_{\rm T}$ azimuthal asymmetries

## Key claim
The simultaneous description of the nuclear modification factor $R_{\rm AA}$ and the high-$p_{\rm T}$ elliptic flow $v_2$ in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC requires that energy loss be strongly suppressed for the first approximately 0.6 fm after the collision, which is a condition that prior jet quenching models implicitly adopted but did not explicitly test.

## Method
The authors computed $R_{\rm AA}$ and the high-$p_{\rm T}$ elliptic flow $v_2$ for charged particle production in 20–30% centrality Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV using the Quenching Weights (QWs) formalism at next-to-leading order (NLO) in perturbative QCD, with CTEQ6.6M proton PDFs, EPS09 nuclear corrections, and DSS07 fragmentation functions. The medium evolution was provided by EKRT event-by-event hydrodynamic profiles, and the jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}(\xi)$ was taken proportional to $\varepsilon^{3/4}(\xi)$, where $\varepsilon$ is the local energy density, with a single free $K$-factor fitted to $R_{\rm AA}$ data. The authors varied both the starting time of energy loss (0 fm, 0.197 fm, and 0.572 fm) and the ending temperature (chemical freeze-out at 175 MeV versus kinetic freeze-out at 100 MeV) to study the sensitivity of the observables.

## Key numbers
- The analysis is performed for 20–30% centrality Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV.
- The hydrodynamic initialization proper time is $\tau_{\rm f}$ = 0.197 fm.
- Three early-time cases are considered: $\tau_{\rm q}=0$ fm (quenching from the start), $\tau_{\rm q}=0.197$ fm (quenching begins at hydrodynamic initialization), and $\tau_{\rm q}=0.572$ fm (quenching begins after approximately 0.6 fm).
- The chemical freeze-out temperature is $T_{\mathrm{chem}}$ = 175 MeV; the kinetic freeze-out temperature is $T_{\mathrm{dec}}$ = 100 MeV.
- The high-$p_{\rm T}$ elliptic flow $v_2^{\rm SP}$ is only correctly described when energy loss is absent for the first approximately 0.6 fm after the collision (Case iii, $\tau_{\rm q}=0.572$ fm).

## Context
This work extends prior jet quenching studies that successfully described $R_{\rm AA}$ but systematically underpredicted the high-$p_{\rm T}$ elliptic flow $v_2$ at the LHC. It builds on the Quenching Weights formalism of Salgado and Wiedemann (2003) and the EKRT event-by-event hydrodynamic model of Niemi et al. (2015). The finding that energy loss must be suppressed for the first approximately 0.6 fm aligns with the implicit setup used by Noronha-Hostler et al. (2016), Betz et al. (2016), Zigic et al. (2018), and Shi et al. (2018) to describe jet harmonic coefficients. The result demonstrates that high-$p_{\rm T}$ azimuthal asymmetries can directly constrain the dynamics of the initial stages of heavy-ion collisions.
