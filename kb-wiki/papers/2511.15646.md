# Relativistic mean-field model with density- and isospin-density-dependent couplings

> We present a new hadronic EoS with hyperons built within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism with baryon-density- and isospin-density-dependent couplings. Motivated by microscopic calculations showing density- and isospin-asymmetry-dependence of self-energies, we implement a new form for the baryon-meson couplings. The parameters for the couplings are constrained by a Bayesian analysis, which anchors the model to nuclear saturation properties, chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) predictions for pure neutron matter, heavy-ion collision data, and HALQCD-based hyperon potential calculations at 3-momentum $|\mathbf{k}|=0$ in both isospin-symmetric and pure neutron matter. The resulting EoS satisfies neutron star mass-radius constraints from NICER and GW170817, providing another way to address the hyperon puzzle. The low-density part of the EoS is described via nuclear statistical equilibrium with modern mass tables (AME20/FRDM12, 8244 nuclei), providing a novel and complete general-purpose EoS for astrophysical simulations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new hadronic equation of state (EoS) with hyperons that introduces both baryon-density-dependent and isospin-density-dependent couplings in the relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism, motivated by microscopic Dirac–Brückner–Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations showing that self-energies depend on both density and isospin asymmetry. The model claims to satisfy neutron star mass-radius constraints from NICER and GW170817 while addressing the hyperon puzzle, but the key hedge is that the isospin-density dependence is necessary specifically to reproduce the HALQCD-based hyperon potentials in pure neutron matter, which traditional density-dependent RMF models cannot capture.

## Method

The authors construct an RMF model with baryon-meson couplings that depend on both total baryon density \(n_B\) and isospin asymmetry \(\beta = (n_n - n_p)/n_B\), using a functional form that interpolates between isospin-symmetric matter and pure neutron matter. The coupling parameters are constrained via Bayesian analysis using nuclear saturation properties, chiral effective field theory (\(\chi\)EFT) predictions for pure neutron matter, heavy-ion collision data, and HALQCD-based hyperon potential calculations at zero momentum in both isospin-symmetric and pure neutron matter. The low-density part of the EoS (\(n_B < n_0\)) is described using nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) with the HS model (Hempel 2009) incorporating modern mass tables (AME20/FRDM12, 8244 nuclei) and an excluded-volume mechanism for cluster dissolution.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_0 \simeq 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)
- \(\Lambda\) hyperon potential in isospin-symmetric matter at saturation: \(U_\Lambda^{\text{ISM}} = -28.15 \pm 2.02\ \text{MeV}\) (from HALQCD-based BHF calculations)
- \(\Lambda\) hyperon potential in pure neutron matter at saturation: \(U_\Lambda^{\text{NM}} = -25.42 \pm 1.78\ \text{MeV}\) (from HALQCD-based BHF calculations)
- The model covers a temperature range \(T = (0-100)\ \text{MeV}\), baryon density range \(n_B = (10^{-11}-10)\,n_0\), and electron fraction \(Y_e = (0-0.6)\)
- The DD2Y model with an added \(f_0(980)\) meson lowered the maximum neutron star mass from \(2.04\ M_\odot\) to \(1.87\ M_\odot\)

## Context

This work extends the DD2 density-dependent RMF model (Typel 2009) and its hyperon extensions BHB14 (Banik 2014) and DD2Y (Marques 2017) by introducing a novel isospin-density dependence in the couplings, motivated by DBHF calculations (van Dalen 2004, 2005) and HALQCD-based BHF results (Inoue 2018). The model provides a complete general-purpose EoS for astrophysical simulations (to be made available on CompOSE) that simultaneously satisfies constraints from nuclear saturation properties, \(\chi\)EFT neutron matter predictions, heavy-ion collision data, hyperon potentials, and neutron star observations from NICER and GW170817, offering another approach to resolving the hyperon puzzle beyond previous attempts using additional repulsion in hyperon-nucleon interactions (Schaffner-Bielich 2000, Oertel 2012, Banik 2014).
