# Efimov effect from functional renormalization

> We apply a field-theoretic functional renormalization group technique to the few-body (vacuum) physics of non-relativistic atoms near a Feshbach resonance. Three systems are considered: one-component bosons with U(1) symmetry, two-component fermions with U(1)\times SU(2) symmetry and three-component fermions with U(1) \times SU(3) symmetry. We focus on the scale invariant unitarity limit for infinite scattering length. The exact solution for the two-body sector is consistent with the unitary fixed point behavior for all considered systems. Nevertheless, the numerical three-body solution in the s-wave sector develops a limit cycle scaling in case of U(1) bosons and SU(3) fermions. The Efimov parameter for the one-component bosons and the three-component fermions is found to be approximately s=1.006, consistent with the result of Efimov.

## Key claim

The functional renormalization group (RG) method, applied to the three-body sector of non-relativistic atoms at unitarity, reproduces the Efimov effect and yields a limit-cycle scaling for one-component bosons and three-component fermions, but not for two-component fermions. The claim is conditional on the specific truncation (vertex expansion) and the vacuum limit, where the flow equations for the n-body sector decouple from higher-body sectors.

## Method

The authors apply a field-theoretic functional renormalization group (Wetterich equation) to a two-channel model of non-relativistic atoms near a Feshbach resonance, using a vertex expansion truncated at third order in the fields. They study three systems: one-component bosons (U(1) symmetry), two-component fermions (U(1)×SU(2) symmetry), and three-component fermions (U(1)×SU(3) symmetry), all in the vacuum limit (zero temperature and density) at the unitarity point (infinite scattering length). The RG flow for the atom-diatom vertex is solved analytically in a pointlike approximation and numerically with full momentum dependence, yielding the Skorniakov–Ter-Martirosian integral equation.

## Key numbers

* Efimov parameter for one-component bosons and three-component fermions: \( s \approx 1.006 \), consistent with the result of Efimov.
* For two-component fermions, no limit-cycle scaling is observed in the three-body sector at unitarity.
* The Yukawa coupling \( h \) is not renormalized in vacuum for all three models: \( \partial_t h = 0 \).
* The inverse diatom propagator at scale \( k \) is given by \( P_{\varphi, k}(Q) = \frac{h^2}{4\pi(3+p)}\Big(-a^{-1}+\sqrt{\frac{i\omega_{\mathbf{q}}}{2}+\frac{\mathbf{q}^2}{4}-\mu_{\psi}+k^{2}} \Big) \), where \( p = +1 \) for bosons and \( p = -1 \) for fermions.

## Context

This work extends the functional renormalization group approach to few-body physics, previously applied to two-component fermions by Diehl et al. (2007) and Birse (2007), to one-component bosons and three-component fermions. It confirms the presence of Efimov states for SU(3) fermions, complementing the earlier study by Floerchinger et al. (2008) that used a trion field approximation. The result enables future investigations of three-body losses in three-component mixtures of lithium-6 atoms, as observed experimentally by Jochim (2008) and O'Hara (2008).
