# Rooting the EDF method into the ab initio framework. PGCM-PT formalism based on MR-IMSRG pre-processed Hamiltonians

> Recently, ab initio techniques have been successfully connected to the traditional valence-space shell model. In doing so, they can either explicitly provide ab initio shell-model effective Hamiltonians or constrain the construction of empirical ones. In the present work, the possibility to follow a similar path for the nuclear energy density functional (EDF) method is analyzed. For this connection to be actualized, two theoretical techniques are instrumental: the recently proposed ab initio PGCM-PT many-body formalism and the MR-IMSRG pre-processing of the nuclear Hamiltonian. Based on both formal arguments and numerical results, possible new lines of research are briefly discussed, namely to compute ab initio EDF effective Hamiltonians at low computational cost, to constrain empirical ones or to produce them directly via an effective field theory that remains to be invented.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the ab initio PGCM-PT many-body formalism, combined with MR-IMSRG pre-processing of the nuclear Hamiltonian, can formally and numerically connect the ab initio framework to the nuclear energy density functional (EDF) method, but this connection is conditional on the pre-processing step being the "missing link" that reconciles results from the two schemes; the authors do not claim to have constructed a working ab initio EDF, only that the prerequisites for doing so are now fulfilled.

## Method

The authors compare the ab initio PGCM-PT(2) method, which uses a projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) state as the unperturbed reference and adds second-order perturbative corrections for dynamical correlations, against the multi-reference EDF (MR-EDF) method, which restricts the A-body dynamics to the PGCM subspace and uses an empirical effective Hamiltonian. Both methods are applied to the doubly open-shell nucleus ²⁰Ne, using a VSRG-pre-processed chiral Hamiltonian for the ab initio calculation and a standard empirical EDF parametrization for the EDF calculation. The comparison is performed at the level of the ground-state binding energy and the rotational band.

- The runtime complexity of the PGCM step (the full cost of the EDF method) scales as O(n_proj n_gcm² n_dim⁴), while the PGCM-PT(2) second-order correction scales as O(n_proj n_gcm² n_dim⁸), where n_dim is the dimension of the truncated one-body Hilbert space.
- The runtime complexity of full configuration interaction (FCI) scales as O(n_dim^A), where A is the mass number.

## Context

This work extends the recent successful connection of ab initio techniques to the valence-space shell model (e.g., providing ab initio shell-model effective Hamiltonians) by attempting a similar path for the EDF method. It builds on the PGCM-PT formalism introduced by Frosini et al. (2021) and the MR-IMSRG pre-processing technique, and it contrasts with prior empirical EDF approaches (e.g., Bender et al., 2003; Sheikh et al., 2019) that lack systematic improvability. The results enable future research to compute ab initio EDF effective Hamiltonians at low computational cost, constrain empirical ones, or produce them via an effective field theory that remains to be invented.
