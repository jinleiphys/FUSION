# Modern theories of low-energy astrophysical reactions

> We summarize recent ab initio studies of low-energy electroweak reactions of astrophysical interest, relevant for both big bang nucleosynthesis and solar neutrino production. The calculational methods include direct integration for np radiative and pp weak capture, correlated hyperspherical harmonics for reactions of A=3,4 nuclei, and variational Monte Carlo for A=6,7 nuclei. Realistic nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions and consistent current operators are used as input.

## Key claim

This paper reviews recent *ab initio* calculations of low-energy electroweak reaction rates for big bang nucleosynthesis and solar neutrino production, using realistic nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions with consistent current operators, but notes that the theoretical results depend on model choices such as the treatment of two-body currents and the specific Hamiltonian used, and that for systems with A>4 the continuum wave functions are treated with a semi-phenomenological prescription rather than fully *ab initio* methods.

## Method

The authors employ three complementary *ab initio* methods: direct integration for the np radiative capture and pp weak capture reactions, correlated hyperspherical harmonics (CHH) for reactions involving A=3 and A=4 nuclei, and variational Monte Carlo (VMC) for reactions involving A=6 and A=7 nuclei. The calculations use realistic two-nucleon potentials (Argonne v18, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen I/II, Reid93) combined with three-nucleon potentials (Urbana IX, Tucson-Melbourne, or Illinois models) and include one- and two-body electromagnetic and weak current operators, with two-body axial currents adjusted to reproduce the tritium Gamow-Teller matrix element.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy with AV18/UIX Hamiltonian: CHH gives 8.479 MeV, VMC gives 8.227(6) MeV, GFMC gives 8.461(6) MeV, experiment is 8.482 MeV
- Alpha particle binding energy with AV18/UIX: CHH gives 27.89 MeV, VMC gives 27.40(3) MeV, GFMC gives 28.31(2) MeV, experiment is 28.30 MeV
- nd doublet scattering length with AV18/UIX: CHH gives 0.63 fm, experiment is 0.65(4) fm
- nd quartet scattering length with AV18/UIX: CHH gives 6.33 fm, experiment is 6.35(2) fm
- p-3He singlet scattering length with AV18/UIX: CHH gives 11.5 fm, experiment is 10.8(2.6) fm
- p-3He triplet scattering length with AV18/UIX: CHH gives 9.13 fm, experiment is 8.1(5) fm
- Realistic NN potentials give triton binding ranging from 7.6 MeV (local potentials) to 8.0 MeV (most non-local), compared to experimental 8.48 MeV
- Alpha particle binding from realistic NN potentials alone ranges from 24.0 to 26.3 MeV, compared to experimental 28.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier *ab initio* studies of few-nucleon systems by Marcucci, Schiavilla, and collaborators (2001) and Pieper, Varga, and Wiringa (2001) to astrophysical reaction rates, providing theoretical cross sections for reactions that are difficult or impossible to measure in the laboratory, such as the pp weak capture. The review contrasts these *ab initio* approaches with simpler potential models (Dubovichenko 1995, Kim 1981, Buck 1985, 1988, Mohr 1993) that require experimental spectroscopic factors, and with resonating group method calculations (Kajino 1984, 1986, Csoto 2000) that use system-specific interactions and limited model spaces. The results enable improved predictions for big bang nucleosynthesis yields of deuterium and lithium-7, and for solar neutrino fluxes from the pp chain, particularly for the hep reaction which is inaccessible to experiment.
