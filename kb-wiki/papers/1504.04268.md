# Magnetized plasminos in cold and hot QED plasmas

> The complete quasi-particle spectrum of a magnetized electromagnetic plasma is systematically explored at zero and nonzero temperatures. To this purpose, the general structure of the one-loop corrected propagator of magnetized fermions is determined, and the dispersion relations arising from the pole of this propagator are numerically solved. It turns out that in the lowest Landau level, where only one spin direction is allowed, the spectrum consists of one positively (negatively) charged fermionic mode with positive (negative) spin. In contrast, in higher Landau levels, as an indirect consequence of the double spin degeneracy of fermions, the spectrum consists of two massless collective modes with left- and right-chiralities. The mechanism through which these new collective excitations are created in a uniform magnetic field is similar to the production mechanism of dynamical holes (plasminos) at finite temperature and zero magnetic fields. Whereas cold magnetized plasminos appear for moderate magnetic fields and for all positive momenta of propagating fermions, hot magnetized plasminos appear only in the limit of weak magnetic fields and soft momenta.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a magnetized QED plasma, new collective fermionic excitations (magnetized plasminos) appear as poles of the one-loop corrected fermion propagator, but only under specific conditions: cold magnetized plasminos exist for moderate magnetic fields and all positive momenta, whereas hot magnetized plasminos appear only in the limit of weak magnetic fields and soft momenta.

## Method

The authors determine the general structure of the one-loop corrected propagator of magnetized fermions using the Ritus eigenfunction method to solve the Dirac equation in a constant magnetic field. They compute the one-loop fermion self-energy at zero and nonzero temperatures, then numerically solve the dispersion relations arising from the poles of the dressed propagator separately for the lowest Landau level (LLL) and higher Landau levels (HLL). A Hard-Thermal Loop (HTL) approximation is introduced for the finite-temperature case in the limit of weak magnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass: \( m_D^2 = e^2 T^2 / 8 \), used in the HTL approximation for the hot QED plasma case
- For the LLL, the free fermion propagator reduces to \( S_0^{(\pm)}(\tilde{k}) = \mathcal{P}_{\pm} / (\mathbf{\tilde{k}}_\parallel - m_q) \), reflecting dimensional reduction from D=4 to D=2
- For HLL (\( n \neq 0 \)), the free fermion propagator is \( S_n^{(\pm)}(\tilde{k}) = 1 / (\tilde{k}_n - m_q) \), with double spin degeneracy
- The integrals \( \int_0^\infty dp\, p f_f(p) = \pi^2 T^2 / 6 \) and \( \int_0^\infty dp\, p f_b(p) = \pi^2 T^2 / 12 \) are used in the HTL calculation

## Context

This work extends the concept of plasminos, originally discovered in hot QED and QCD plasmas at finite temperature and zero magnetic field by Klimov (1982) and Weldon (1982, 1989), to the case of magnetized plasmas. The mechanism for creating magnetized plasminos is analogous to the production of dynamical holes at finite temperature. The results are relevant for understanding the quasi-particle spectrum of deconfined quarks in heavy-ion collisions, where strong magnetic fields are believed to be created in the early stage of non-central collisions (McLerran 2007), and for applications to magnetar physics and condensed matter systems such as graphene and Dirac semi-metals (Shovkovy 2015).
