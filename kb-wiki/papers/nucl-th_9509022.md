# Polarization observables in vector meson photoproduction

> An analysis of all possible polarization observables for the case of vector meson photoproduction from a nucleon target is presented. The question of which observables are needed to determine completely the basic photoproduction amplitudes and the relationships between spin observables are addressed.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete analysis of all possible polarization observables for vector meson photoproduction from a nucleon target, but the abstract's claim of addressing "which observables are needed to determine completely the basic photoproduction amplitudes" is qualified by the finding that many spin observables yield redundant information, and that one needs to go beyond single plus double spin observables to obtain a complete set of experiments.

## Method

The authors represent all spin observables as bilinear products of helicity amplitudes using a trace formalism, then map these into a bilinear helicity product (BHP) form using 16 Hermitian 4x4 matrices (Gamma) for the nucleon/photon helicity space and 9 Hermitian 3x3 matrices (omega) for the vector meson helicity space. They enumerate all 144 possible spin observables (16 x 9 combinations) for the reaction gamma + N -> V + N', classify them by parity conservation into single, double, triple, and quadruple spin observables, and examine their general angular dependence near thresholds and resonances. The analysis uses Jacob and Wick phase conventions and transversity amplitudes to identify which observables vanish at 0 and 180 degrees and which have nodes.

## Key numbers

* There are 12 independent complex helicity amplitudes (24 real numbers) at each energy and angle for vector meson photoproduction, compared to 4 independent helicity amplitudes for pseudoscalar meson production.
* The total number of possible spin observables is 144 (16 Gamma matrices times 9 omega matrices) for the vector meson case, compared to 16 for the pion case.
* Of the 99 possible double spin observables, 51 are nonzero after parity conservation; of these 51, 37 are nonzero near threshold.
* Of the 18 single spin observables (including cross-section), 8 survive parity conservation; of these 8, 5 are nonzero near or at threshold (cross-section plus 4 vector meson spin observables: T_xx, T_yy, T_zz, and T_xz).
* To determine the 12 complex amplitudes completely, 23 independent measurements are needed (24 real numbers minus one overall arbitrary phase).

## Context

This work extends prior analyses of polarization observables for pseudoscalar meson photoproduction (Walker; Barker, Donnachie, and Storrow) to the vector meson case, and is similar in approach to studies of the p-bar p -> Lambda-bar Lambda reaction near threshold (Tabakin; Tabakin and collaborators). The paper enables experimental programs at CEBAF (now Jefferson Lab) to design complete sets of measurements for rho, omega, and phi photoproduction, particularly relevant for studying the phi meson's strange quark content and testing quark models of baryon structure. The authors note that generalized discussions already exist in the literature (Simonius; Conzett) but their treatment using bilinear helicity products is novel.
