# Faddeev description of two-hole one-particle motion and the single-particle spectral function

> The Faddeev technique is employed to address the problem of describing the influence of both particle-particle and particle-hole phonons on the single-particle self-energy. The scope of the few-body Faddeev equations is extended to describe the motion of two-hole one-particle (two-particle one-hole) excitations. This formalism allows to sum both particle-particle and particle-hole phonons, obtained separately in the Random Phase Approximation. The appearance of spurious solutions for the present application of the Faddeev method is related to the inclusion of a consistent set of diagrams. The formalism presented here appears practical for finite nuclei and achieves a simultaneous inclusion of particle-particle and particle-hole phonons to all orders while the spurious solutions are properly eliminated.

## Key claim

The Faddeev technique can be extended to describe two-hole one-particle (and two-particle one-hole) motion in finite nuclei, enabling the simultaneous inclusion of both particle-particle and particle-hole phonons (obtained from the Random Phase Approximation) to all orders in the nucleon self-energy, provided that spurious solutions arising from the Faddeev equations are properly identified and eliminated.

## Method

The authors formulate the problem using self-consistent Green's functions (SCGF) and derive a set of Faddeev equations for the four-time two-particle one-hole (2p1h) propagator, which is then approximated to a tractable two-time form. The particle-particle and particle-hole irreducible vertices are replaced by dressed RPA (DRPA) phonon vertices, and the Faddeev equations are solved for the 2p1h propagator components, with the spurious eigenstates separated by enforcing closure relations for the pp and ph amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The removal probability for nucleons from closed-shell nuclei is reduced by about 35% compared to the simple shell model, as observed in (e,e'p) reactions.
* For nuclear matter, short-range correlations yield a strength removal of about 15%.
* For 16O, short-range correlations lead to removal of single-particle strength of the order of 10%.
* The experimental spectroscopic strength for knockout of a proton from both the p1/2 and p3/2 shells in 16O corresponds to about 60%.
* Calculations for 16O including both long- and short-range correlations yield about a 25% reduction, still about 20% away from the experimental data.
* The center-of-mass motion enhances the probability for p removal in 16O by about 7%.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Schuck (1980s) for including both pp and ph phonons in the self-energy, which suffered from unphysical solutions and double-counting issues as noted by Rijsdijk (1996). It also builds on the Green's function calculations of Geurts (1996) for 16O, which included pp and ph excitations only at the Tamm-Dancoff approximation level. The formalism enables a self-consistent treatment of the coupling between single-particle states and collective excitations, which is necessary to resolve the remaining discrepancy between theoretical and experimental spectroscopic factors in 16O.
