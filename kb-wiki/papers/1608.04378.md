# Hadronic matter at the edge: A survey of some theoretical approaches to the physics of the QCD phase diagram

> In the past few years a wealth of high quality data has made possible to test current theoretical ideas about the properties of hadrons subject to extreme conditions of density and temperature. The relativistic heavy-ion program carried out at the CERN-SPS and under development at the BNL-RHIC and CERN-LHC has provided results that probe the evolution of collisions of hadronic matter at high energies from the initially large density to the late dilute stages. In addition, QCD on the lattice has produced results complementing these findings with first principles calculations for observables in a regime where perturbative techniques cannot describe the nature of strongly coupled systems. This work aims to review some recent developments that make use of field theoretical methods to describe the physics of hadrons at finite temperature and density. I concentrate on two of the main topics that have been explored in the last few years: (1) The search for the structure of the phase diagram and (2) analytical signals linked to the chiral symmetry restoration/deconfinement.

# Hadronic matter at the edge: A survey of some theoretical approaches to the physics of the QCD phase diagram

## Key claim
This review argues that field-theoretical methods beyond mean-field approximation, particularly those incorporating plasma screening effects, can self-consistently describe the QCD phase diagram including inverse magnetic catalysis and the location of the critical end point, but notes that most of the phase diagram remains unknown and that lattice QCD results at finite chemical potential are hindered by the sign problem.

## Method
The paper surveys field-theoretical approaches to finite-temperature and finite-density QCD, focusing on two topics: (1) the structure of the phase diagram, including the crossover transition at zero chemical potential, the curvature of the transition line at finite baryon chemical potential, and the effects of magnetic fields on chiral and deconfinement transitions; and (2) analytical signals of chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement using QCD sum rules (QCDSRs) and finite energy sum rules (FESR), which relate hadronic spectral densities to condensates and the threshold parameter s0.

## Key numbers
- Lattice QCD pseudocritical temperatures for 2+1 flavors: MILC collaboration obtained Tc = 169(12)(4) MeV; BNL-RBC-Bielefeld collaboration reported Tc = 193(7)(4) MeV; Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration obtained Tc = 147(2)(3) MeV; HotQCD collaboration reported Tc = 154(9) MeV
- Curvature of the transition line at mu_B=0: values between kappa = 0.006 and 0.02 reported from lattice calculations
- At magnetic field strength 3.25 GeV^2, different definitions of Tc cluster between 109 and 112 MeV with no significant difference between chiral and deconfinement transition temperatures
- The mixing parameter in the chiral limit at low temperatures is epsilon(T) = T^2/(6 f_pi^2), where f_pi is the pion decay constant
- Parametrization of the crossover transition line for small mu: Tc(mu) = Tc(mu=0) - 0.218 mu - 0.139 mu^2 (with mu in appropriate units)

## Context
This review extends the line of work on the QCD phase diagram using effective models and sum rules, complementing lattice QCD results from the MILC collaboration (2009), BNL-RBC-Bielefeld collaboration (2010), Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (2010), and HotQCD collaboration (2012). It discusses the sign problem identified by Forcrand (2009) that prevents direct lattice simulations at finite chemical potential. The paper enables future experimental searches for the critical end point through the RHIC beam energy scan (BES) program and upcoming facilities like FAIR at GSI and NICA at JINR-Dubna, as well as continued theoretical work on inverse magnetic catalysis and spectral functions.
