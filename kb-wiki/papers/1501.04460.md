# Nuclear Matter Incompressibility Effect on the Cross Section of Fusion Reactions with a weakly bound projectile

> Fusion reactions with a weakly bound projectile are studied using the double-folding model along with a repulsive interaction modifying term. Using this modified potential, including nuclear matter incompressibility effects, the fusion reaction cross sections and suppression parameters are calculated for 9Be +209Bi,208Pb,29Si and 27Al reactions. The results show that applying these effects at energies near the Coulomb barrier improves the agreement between the calculated and experimental cross sections, and modifies the mean values of the suppression parameter.

## Key claim

Applying a repulsive core term to the double-folding nuclear potential to account for nuclear matter incompressibility improves the agreement between calculated and experimental fusion cross sections for reactions with a weakly bound projectile near the Coulomb barrier, and modifies the mean values of the suppression parameter.

## Method

The authors calculated the nuclear interaction potential using the double-folding model with M3Y-Paris nucleon-nucleon interactions, then added a repulsive term to simulate the effect of nuclear matter incompressibility (the "MIP" potential). Using the CCFULL coupled-channels code, they computed fusion cross sections for the systems 9Be + 209Bi, 208Pb, 29Si, and 27Al, and compared these to experimental data. The suppression parameter, defined as SP = (1 - σ_exp / σ_theor) × 100, was extracted for each system using both the standard M3Y potential and the modified MIP potential.

## Key numbers

- For 9Be + 209Bi: SP(M3Y) = 18.27 percent, SP(MIP) = 6.14 percent
- For 9Be + 208Pb: SP(M3Y) = 46.00 percent, SP(MIP) = 44.98 percent
- For 9Be + 29Si: SP(M3Y) = 22.46 percent, SP(MIP) = 20.20 percent
- For 9Be + 27Al: SP(M3Y) = 14.31 percent, SP(MIP) = 11.28 percent
- The difference in SP(M3Y) between the 9Be + 209Bi and 9Be + 208Pb systems is approximately 28 percent
- The saturation density of nuclear matter used in the model is ρ₀ ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Seyyedi and Golnarkar, 2015) on complete fusion suppression in reactions with light and heavy targets, and addresses discrepancies in measured complete fusion cross sections for 9Be-induced reactions on 209Bi and 208Pb reported by multiple experimental groups (e.g., Dasgupta et al., 2007; Signorini et al., 1998). The modified potential approach enables more accurate predictions of fusion cross sections for weakly bound projectiles, which is relevant for forming super-heavy nuclei and understanding nuclear structure.
