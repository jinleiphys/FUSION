# Search for Three-Nucleon Force Effects in Two-Body Photodisintegration of 3He(3H) and in the Time Reversed Proton-Deuteron Radiative Capture Process

> Faddeev calculations have been performed for nucleon-deuteron photodisintegration of 3He(3H) and proton-deuteron radiative capture. The bulk of the results is based on the AV18 nucleon-nucleon force and the Urbana IX three-nucleon force together with explicit exchange currents or applying the Siegert approach. Three-nucleon force effects are predicted for both processes and supported by most of the data.

## Key claim

Three-nucleon force (3NF) effects are predicted for both nucleon-deuteron photodisintegration of 3He/3H and the time-reversed proton-deuteron radiative capture process, and these predictions are qualitatively supported by available data, though the authors note that the theoretical treatment of many-body currents is approximate and that improved data and theory are needed for a clearer view.

## Method

Faddeev calculations were performed for the two-body photodisintegration of 3He and 3H and for proton-deuteron radiative capture, using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon (NN) force combined with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. Exchange currents were included either via the Siegert approximation (which corrects only electric multipoles for many-body currents) or by explicitly including pi- and rho-like meson-exchange currents (MECs) consistent with AV18. The formalism was carried out nonrelativistically in momentum space, with total two-nucleon angular momenta up to j_max=3 and total 3N angular momenta up to J_max=15/2.

## Key numbers

- The AV18+Urbana IX combination gives a 3H binding energy of -8.48 MeV (by construction) and a 3He binding energy of -7.739 MeV, compared to the experimental value of -7.718 MeV; AV18 alone gives -7.628 MeV for 3H and -6.917 MeV for 3He (with Coulomb).
- The 3NF effect on the 3He photodisintegration cross section maximum vanishes at approximately E_gamma = 28 MeV; below this energy the cross section decreases with 3NF inclusion, above it increases.
- At E_gamma = 12 MeV, the total two-body photodisintegration cross section of 3He/3H shows a 5% spread with 3NFs and a 10% spread without 3NFs across different force/current choices.
- At E_gamma = 100 MeV, switching off the 3NF in the continuum (while keeping it in the bound state) changes the cross section by about 16% relative to the difference from dropping the 3NF entirely.
- The pd capture cross section was calculated at deuteron laboratory energies E_d = 19.8, 95, 200, 300, and 400 MeV (corresponding to proton energies E_p = 5-200 MeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier Faddeev calculations for nucleon-deuteron photodisintegration and pd capture (Golak et al., 2000; Schadow et al., 2001) by systematically including three-nucleon forces via the Urbana IX model alongside the AV18 NN force. It builds on a benchmark comparison (Golak et al., 2002) that validated the Faddeev and hyperspherical harmonic approaches for the total 3N photodisintegration cross section. The results enable future studies of 3N force effects in electromagnetic reactions, though the authors note that improved treatment of many-body currents and more precise experimental data are needed to draw definitive conclusions.
