# Soft nuclear equation-of-state from heavy-ion data and implications for compact stars

> Measurements of kaon production at subthreshold energies in heavy-ion collisions point to a soft nuclear equation-of-state for densities up to 2-3 times nuclear matter saturation density. We apply these results to study the implications on compact star properties, especially in the context of the recent measurement of the two solar mass pulsar PSR J1614-2230. The implications are two-fold: Firstly, the heavy-ion results constrain nuclear matter at densities relevant to light neutron stars. Hence, a radius measurement could provide information about the density dependence of the symmetry energy which is a crucial quantity in nuclear physics. Secondly, the information on the nucleon potential obtained from the analysis of the heavy-ion data can be combined with restrictions from causality on the nuclear equation-of-state. From this we can derive a limit for the highest allowed compact star mass of three solar masses.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a soft nuclear equation-of-state (EoS) with a stiffness parameter K ≤ 200 MeV, inferred from subthreshold kaon production in heavy-ion collisions at GSI, is compatible with existing neutron star observations, but the claim is hedged by noting that the isospin asymmetry of the colliding Au+Au system could increase K by about 10%.

## Method
The authors use a Skyrme-type EoS fitted to reproduce the soft nucleon potential from the KaoS experiment, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute radii and moments of inertia for non-rotating neutron stars with masses between 1.1 and 1.6 solar masses. For maximum mass limits, they apply the causal EoS method of Rhoades and Ruffini (1974), connecting the KaoS-constrained EoS at densities up to 2-3 times nuclear saturation density to the stiffest causal EoS at higher densities.

## Key numbers
- The compression modulus of isospin symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density is K₀ = (235 ± 14) MeV from giant monopole resonance studies.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density is S₀ = 28-32 MeV.
- For a 1.25 M☉ neutron star with K ≈ 200 MeV, stiff versus soft symmetry energy configurations yield differences of ΔR ≈ 1.5 km in radius and ΔI ≈ 2.5 × 10⁴³ g cm² in moment of inertia.
- Central densities of 1.25 M☉ neutron stars are ≤ 3.4 n₀, where n₀ ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³ is nuclear saturation density.
- The highest possible compact star mass derived from the KaoS constraint is about 3 M☉ for a critical density n_crit ≈ 2 n₀, and about 2.4 M☉ for n_crit ≈ 3 n₀.
- A pulsar mass of 2.7 M☉ (as discussed for PSR J1748-2021) is marginally compatible, requiring the stiffest EoS to set in at n_crit ≈ 2.2-2.5 n₀.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of subthreshold kaon production from the KaoS collaboration (Sturm 2001; Fuchs 2001; Hartnack 2006) to astrophysical implications, specifically connecting to the measured 1.97 ± 0.04 M☉ pulsar PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010). It contrasts with earlier maximum-mass estimates (Kalogera 1996; Wiringa 1988) that relied on extrapolations from saturation density, and it enables future radius measurements from missions like LOFT to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The paper also discusses the compatibility of a soft EoS with the potentially higher-mass pulsar PSR J1748-2021 (Freire 2008).
