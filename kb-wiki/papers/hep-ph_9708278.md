# Quark-antiquark correlation in the pion

> The electromagnetic tensor for inclusive electron scattering off the pion, ($W^{μν}$), for momentum transfers such that $q^+=0$, ($q^+=q^0+q^3$) is shown to obey a sum-rule for the component $W^{++}$. From this sum-rule, one can define the quark-antiquark correlation function in the pion, which characterizes the transverse distance distribution between the quark and antiquark in the light-front pion wave-function. Within the realistic models of the relativistic pion wave function (including instanton vacuum inspired wave function) it is shown that the value of the two-quark correlation radius ($r_{q\bar q}$) is near twice the pion electromagnetic radius ($r_π$), where $r_π \approx 2/3$ fm. We also define the correlation length $l_{corr}$ where the two - particle correlation have an extremum. The estimation of $l_{corr}\approx 0.3 - 0.5$ fm is very close to estimations from instanton models of QCD vacuum. It is also shown that the above correlation is very sensitive to the pion light-front wave-function models.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a sum-rule for the light-cone component \(W^{++}\) of the inclusive hadronic tensor for electron-pion scattering, evaluated at \(q^+=0\), defines a quark-antiquark correlation function in the pion; the correlation radius \(r_{q\bar q}\) is found to be near twice the pion electromagnetic radius \(r_\pi \approx 2/3\) fm, but this result is model-dependent and the correlation is very sensitive to the choice of the pion light-front wave-function model.

## Method

The authors derive a sum rule for the "good-good" component \(W^{++}\) of the inclusive hadronic tensor for the pion, integrated over \(q^-\) at fixed transverse momentum transfer \(q_\perp^2\) and \(q^+=0\). They separate the sum rule into direct and exchange contributions, subtract the elastic part, and define the quark-antiquark correlation function \(C_{corr}(q_\perp^2)\) from the difference between the charged and neutral pion sum rules. Numerical calculations are performed using four light-front wave-function models: instanton, Gaussian, hydrogen-atom, and the model of Godfrey and Isgur (1985).

## Key numbers

* Pion electromagnetic radius: \(r_\pi \approx 2/3\) fm.
* Two-quark correlation radius \(r_{q\bar q}\) is near twice the pion electromagnetic radius (i.e., approximately \(4/3\) fm).
* Correlation length \(l_{corr} \approx 0.3 - 0.5\) fm, defined as \(l_{corr} = 1/\sqrt{\bar q_\perp^2}\) where the absolute value of the correlation function has an extremum.
* Constituent quark masses used: 250 - 350 MeV.
* Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.
* Isospin trace factors: \(I_D^\alpha = 5/9\) for \(\pi^+, \pi^-, \pi^0\); \(I_E^\alpha = -5/9\) for \(\pi^0\) and \(+4/9\) for \(\pi^\pm\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic generalization of the Coulomb sum rule for inelastic electron scattering, originally formulated by Gottfried (1967) and West (1975), to the light-front formalism for the pion. It provides a method to extract the quark-antiquark transverse correlation from inclusive electron-pion scattering data, which could be measured at moderate momentum transfers where resonances dominate. The results are compared with instanton models of the QCD vacuum, and the correlation length estimate of \(0.3 - 0.5\) fm is very close to instanton-model predictions. The approach enables future experimental extraction of the correlation function from the difference between charged and neutral pion structure functions, similar to the procedure applied to the nucleon by Schmidt (1994).
