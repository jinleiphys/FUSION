# Updated study of the $η_c$ and $η_c^\prime$ decays into light vector mesons

> We re-investigate the exclusive decays of $η_c$ and $η_c^\prime$ to a pair of light vector mesons, i.e. $η_c (η_c')\to VV$. The long-distance intermediate meson loop (IML) effects are evaluated as a non-perturbative mechanism in addition to the short-distance $c\bar{c}$ annihilation contributions. We show that both processes can be reasonably well constrained with the help of the available experimental data. Since $η_c$ and $η_c'$ are the spin-0 partners of $J/ψ$ and $ψ'$, respectively, our study is useful for gaining insights into the pQCD helicity selection rule violations in charmonium decays and the long-standing "$ρπ$ puzzle".

## Key claim

The paper shows that the long-distance intermediate meson loop (IML) contributions are negligible in ηc decays and relatively small in ηc′ decays, in contrast to the significant role they play in ψ′ decays, because the relevant open charm threshold for ηc′ (D*D̅ + c.c.) is much higher than the DD̅ threshold relevant for ψ′.

## Method

The authors parametrize the short-distance strong transition amplitudes for ηc(ηc′) → VV using a gluon-counting scheme with SOZI and DOZI contributions, including an SU(3) flavor breaking parameter. They compute the long-distance IML contributions via triangle diagrams with intermediate charmed mesons (D, D*, Ds, Ds*) using effective Lagrangians based on heavy quark symmetry, with a tri-monopole form factor to regulate loop divergences. The model parameters are fitted to five ηc → VV decay channels (ωω, φφ, K*K̅*, ρρ, ωφ) using experimental data, and then the short-distance amplitudes for ηc′ are scaled by the ratio of wavefunctions at the origin, while the IML contributions are evolved to the ηc′ mass.

## Key numbers

- SOZI coupling strength gηc = (3.913 ± 1.03) × 10−2 for ηc → VV
- SU(3) flavor breaking parameter R = 0.854 ± 0.158, consistent with fπ/fK ≈ 0.838
- DOZI parameter r = −0.154 ± 0.117, indicating suppressed doubly-disconnected contributions
- Relative phase between short and long-distance amplitudes θ = 174.29 ± 91.798 degrees for ηc decays
- Form factor cutoff parameter α = 0.30 ± 0.151
- Reduced χ2 = 3.67 for the ηc → VV fit
- Predicted branching ratio for ηc′ → ωφ = (1.82 ± 0.47) × 10−4
- Ratio of wavefunctions at origin |ηc′(0)|/|ηc(0)| = 0.62 ± 0.16, taken from ψ2S/ψ1S comparison
- Short-distance contributions dominate ηc → VV: e.g., BR(ηc → ρρ)short = 1.19 × 10−2 vs BR(ηc → ρρ)long = 9.53 × 10−6

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the "ρπ puzzle" in J/ψ(ψ′) → VP decays (Wang 2012, Zhao 2006, Li 2007) by examining the spin-0 partners ηc and ηc′. The IML mechanism that was found to suppress ψ′ → VP amplitudes near the DD̅ threshold is shown to have much weaker effects in ηc′ → VV because the relevant open charm threshold (D*D̅ + c.c.) is higher. The study enables future tests of the helicity selection rule violation in charmonium decays and provides predictions testable at BES-III, particularly for ηc′ → ωφ.
