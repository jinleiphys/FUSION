# Resonant Nuclear Fusion at Second Order

> We study the possibility of low energy nuclear fusion assisted by a low energy resonance. We use a simple potential model and replace repulsive Coulomb barrier with a step potential barrier. This is convenient since it allows us to perform analytic calculations and allows better control on approximations. The fusion process involves a proton and another nucleus. We first consider a process in which this transition takes place by emission of a single photon at first order in perturbation theory. At this order we find a very large cross section over a very narrow range of initial momenta, corresponding to the resonance width. The cross section rapidly drops to very small values as we move away from resonance. In any experimental situation, the rate for this process would be negligible since it is practically impossible to have a significant number of initial state particles at precisely the resonant energy. We next consider another process, which involves emission of two photons and gets dominant contribution at second order in perturbation theory. In this case the initial state energy is taken to be larger than the resonance energy. We find a significant cross section, provided the initial energy is larger than the resonant energy. The exponential suppression factors cancel out in this calculation. The experimental signature for this process is two photon emission within very narrow range of energies. One of the photon would be emitted at low energy of order of the initial state energy and the second would correspond to the nuclear energy.

# Resonant Nuclear Fusion at Second Order

## Key claim
Low-energy nuclear fusion can achieve experimentally observable cross sections through a second-order two-photon emission process when a low-energy resonance exists, provided the initial proton energy exceeds the resonant energy. The abstract's claim of "significant cross section" depends on the specific condition that the initial energy be larger than the resonant energy, and the result is demonstrated using a simplified step-potential model rather than a realistic Coulomb barrier.

## Method
The authors model the proton-nucleus system using a spherically symmetric step potential with a deep nuclear well (depth \(V_0\)) and a repulsive barrier step (height \(V_1\)), replacing the Coulomb barrier for analytic tractability. They compute first-order (single photon emission) and second-order (two photon emission) transition amplitudes using perturbation theory, with the intermediate state energy set to the resonant energy via a delta-function approximation. The cross section is evaluated by integrating over photon energies, where the dominant contribution comes from a narrow energy interval corresponding to the resonance width.

## Key numbers
- Resonance energy: \(E_R = 0.53869\) atomic units (approximately 14.7 eV)
- Second-order cross section at initial energy 1 atomic unit (27.2 eV): \(7 \times 10^{-12}\) barns
- First-order cross section at the same initial energy (27.2 eV): \(9.5 \times 10^{-41}\) barns
- Nuclear potential depth: \(V_0 = 5.0 \times 10^7\) MeV
- Barrier height: \(V_1 = 100\) atomic units
- Barrier width: \(L_b = 0.1\) atomic units
- Nuclear well width: \(L_n = 0.574585 \times 10^{-4}\) atomic units
- Final nuclear state energy: \(E_f = -13.56\) MeV (first radial excitation, \(l=1\))

## Context
This work extends the second-order perturbation theory approach to low-energy nuclear reactions previously pursued by Jain (2020, 2021), Ramkumar (2022), and Kumar (2023), which found that contributions from high-energy intermediate states tend to cancel, leading to very small rates. The present paper addresses this cancellation by invoking a low-energy resonance, as suggested in Ramkumar (2024) and Jain (2024). The result enables experimental searches for two-photon emission signatures with one photon at low energy (order of the initial state energy) and the second at nuclear energy, potentially explaining some low-energy nuclear reaction claims.
