# Antiproton Induced Reactions

> The various aspects of antiproton physics are shortly reviewed, and its relevance for the possible discovery of new particles and effects is pointed out. Then a survey of the nucleon-antinucleon interactions is given. In the nucleon-antinucleon annihilations, there is a big amount of experimental data that call for theoretical explanation. Importance of specific spin and isospin channels for our understanding of antiproton physics is stressed.

## Key claim

This lecture review argues that antiproton physics offers a broad and interconnected field of investigation, ranging from fundamental symmetry tests (CP, CPT, gravity) to hadron spectroscopy and nucleon-antinucleon dynamics, but it stresses that the theoretical understanding of the annihilation mechanism remains incomplete and that specific spin and isospin channels are crucial for progress.

## Method

The paper surveys the experimental and theoretical landscape of antiproton-induced reactions as of 1993-1994, covering CP violation studies via tagged K⁰ and Λ\(\overline{\Lambda}\) production at LEAR, CPT tests using Penning traps and antiprotonic atoms, charmonium spectroscopy via p\(\overline{\rm p}\) → c\(\overline{\rm c}\) reactions, and the nucleon-antinucleon (N\(\overline{\rm N}\)) interaction. For the N\(\overline{\rm N}\) interaction, the author reviews the G-parity rule linking NN and N\(\overline{\rm N}\) potentials, empirical optical models with a meson-exchange tail and a complex Wood-Saxon core, and the analysis of integrated and differential cross-sections along with spin observables.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of inelastic to elastic cross-sections for p\(\overline{\rm p}\) is approximately σ_inel/σ_el ≈ 2, which is larger than the black-sphere value of about 1.
- The absorption range in local optical models is about 0.8–1.0 fm.
- The spin-spin potential expectation value for S-waves in charmonium is ⟨V_SS⟩_{l=0} = ¼[M(J/ψ) – M(η_c)] ≈ 30 MeV.
- The measured quantity δM = (1/9)[M(³P₀) + 3M(³P₁) + 5M(³P₂)] – M(¹P₁) is very small, approximately –1 MeV, indicating negligible spin-spin force on P-waves.
- The CPT test goal for the proton-antiproton mass difference is Δm/m ≤ 10⁻⁸.
- The proton lifetime bound is τ(p) ≳ 10³¹–³³ years.
- The antiproton lifetime from storage is τ(\(\overline{\rm p}\)) ≳ several days.
- The integrated cross-sections for p\(\overline{\rm p}\) are on the order of 100 mb.

## Context

This review extends the earlier comprehensive surveys by Amsler and Myhrer, and by Dover et al., and summarizes the results from the LEAR program at CERN and the E760 experiment at Fermilab. It connects the G-parity rule to the Paris, Bonn, and Nijmegen NN potentials, and discusses the unresolved ambiguities in optical model parameters (e.g., the work of Dalkarov and Richard, and of Reif and Sander). The paper enables future work on polarized antiproton beams, very cold antiprotons for antihydrogen formation and gravity experiments, and the SuperLEAR project for charmonium and exotic hadron spectroscopy.
