# Predictions of the FBD model for the synthesis cross sections of Z = 114-120 elements based on macroscopic-microscopic fission barriers

> A complete set of existing data on hot fusion reactions leading to synthesis of superheavy nuclei of Z =114-118, obtained in a series of experiments in Dubna and later in GSI Darmstadt and LBNL Berkeley, was analyzed in terms of a new angular-momentum dependent version of the Fusion by Diffusion (FBD) model with fission barriers and ground-state masses taken from the Warsaw macroscopic-microscopic model (involving non-axial shapes) of Kowal et al. The only empirically adjustable parameter of the model, the injection-point distance (sinj), has been determined individually for all the reactions and very regular systematics of this parameter have been established. The regularity of the obtained sinj systematics indirectly points at the internal consistency of the whole set of fission barriers used in the calculations. Having fitted all the experimental excitation functions for elements Z = 114-118, the FBD model (with the new sinj systematics) was used to predict cross sections for synthesis of elements Z = 119 and 120. Regarding prospects to produce the new element Z = 119, our calculations prefer the 252Es(48Ca,xn)300-x119 reaction, for which the synthesis cross section of about 0.2 pb in 4n channel at Ec.m.= 220 MeV is expected. The most favorable reaction to synthesize the element Z = 120 turns out to be 249Cf(50Ti,xn)299-x120, but the predicted cross section for this reaction is only 6 fb (for 3n and 4n channels).

## Key claim

The FBD model, calibrated against all existing hot-fusion data for Z=114-118 using the Warsaw macroscopic-microscopic fission barriers of Kowal et al., predicts that the synthesis of element Z=119 is marginally possible (about 0.2 pb) via the 252Es(48Ca,4n) reaction, while the synthesis of element Z=120 is essentially out of reach with a predicted cross section of only 6 fb for the most favorable reaction 249Cf(50Ti,xn), but these predictions depend entirely on the internal consistency of the chosen theoretical fission barriers, and using alternative barriers (Moller et al.) yields inconsistent systematics.

## Method

The authors applied an angular-momentum dependent version of the Fusion by Diffusion (FBD) model, using fission barriers and ground-state masses from the Warsaw macroscopic-microscopic model (Kowal et al., including non-axial shapes). The single adjustable parameter, the injection-point distance sinj, was determined individually for each reaction by fitting to experimental excitation functions for Z=114-118 elements produced in 48Ca-induced hot fusion reactions; a linear systematics of sinj versus energy above the Coulomb barrier was then established. Using this calibrated sinj systematics, the model was used to predict cross sections for unexplored reactions aimed at synthesizing Z=119 and 120, with no further adjustable parameters.

## Key numbers

- For the 252Es(48Ca,xn)300-x119 reaction, the predicted cross section is about 0.2 pb in the 4n channel at Ec.m. ≈ 220 MeV.
- For the 254Es(48Ca,xn)302-x119 reaction, the predicted cross section is only about 15 fb (one order of magnitude lower than for 252Es).
- For the 249Bk(50Ti,xn)299-x119 reaction, the predicted cross section is about 30 fb for both 3n and 4n channels at Ec.m. ≈ 225 and 232 MeV, respectively.
- For the 249Cf(50Ti,xn)299-x120 reaction, the predicted cross section is only 6 fb (for 3n and 4n channels).
- For the 251Cf(50Ti,xn)301-x120 reaction, the maximum cross section is about 3 fb for the 4n channel.
- For the 248Cm(54Cr,xn)302-x120 reaction, the predicted cross sections are of the order of 1 fb for 3n and 4n channels.
- For the 238U(64Ni,xn)302-x120 and 244Pu(58Fe,xn)302-x120 reactions, the calculated 3n and 4n cross sections are about 0.3 fb and 0.1 fb, respectively.
- The linear systematics of the injection-point distance is given by sinj ≈ 4.09 fm − 0.192 (Ec.m. − B0) fm/MeV.

## Context

This work extends the FBD model analysis of cold fusion reactions (Siwek-Wilczynska et al., 2011) to hot fusion reactions for Z=114-118, and provides the first systematic test of the Warsaw macroscopic-microscopic fission barriers (Kowal et al., 2010) against the complete set of experimental data from Dubna, GSI, and LBNL. The authors show that using the alternative fission barriers of Moller et al. (2009) leads to inconsistent sinj systematics and incorrect excitation function positions, whereas the Kowal et al. barriers yield consistent results. The predictions for Z=119 and 120 are considerably less optimistic than those of Zagrebaev et al. (2012) for 254Es(48Ca) (0.3 pb) and Liu et al. (2011) for 249Bk(50Ti) (0.6 pb), and are comparable to or lower than other model calculations for 249Cf(50Ti) (typically about 50 fb). The results suggest that experimental synthesis of Z=120 may be impossible with current technology if the Kowal et al. barriers are correct.
