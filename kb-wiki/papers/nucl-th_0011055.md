# Statistical Multifragmentation in Thermodynamic Limit

> An exact analytical solution of the statistical multifragmentation model is found in thermodynamic limit. The model exhibits a 1-st order phase transition of the liquid-gas type. The mixed phase region of the phase diagram, where the gas of nuclear fragments coexists with the infinite liquid condensate, is unambiguously identified. The peculiar thermodynamic properties of the model near the boundary between the mixed phase and the pure gaseous phase are studied. The results for the caloric curve and specific heat are presented and a physical picture of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition is clarified.

# Statistical Multifragmentation in Thermodynamic Limit

## Key claim

The statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) exhibits a first-order liquid-gas phase transition in the thermodynamic limit, with an exact analytical solution obtained via the grand canonical ensemble and isobaric partition function. The sharp peak and finite discontinuity in the specific heat at constant density occur at the boundary between the mixed phase and pure gaseous phase, contradicting earlier numerical expectations of an infinite peak of zero width in this limit.

## Method

The authors solved the SMM analytically in the grand canonical ensemble by introducing an isobaric partition function, which reduces the problem to studying singularities of that function. The pressure of the system in the thermodynamic limit is defined by the farthest-right singularity of the isobaric partition function, with a simple pole singularity corresponding to the gaseous phase and a singularity of the function itself corresponding to the liquid phase (infinite fragment condensate). The model incorporates Van der Waals excluded volume effects with normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and uses a liquid-drop-motivated fragment free energy with temperature-dependent surface tension.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, used as the fixed density of the pure liquid phase and the excluded volume parameter b = 1/ρ₀
- Bulk binding energy per nucleon: W₀ = 16 MeV
- Fermi-gas excitation parameter: ε₀ = 16 MeV
- Surface tension parameters: σ₀ = 18 MeV, critical temperature T_c = 18 MeV (surface tension vanishes at T ≥ T_c)
- Nucleon internal states: z₁ = 4 (spin-isospin degeneracy)
- Caloric curves show leveling of temperature at energies per nucleon between 10 and 20 MeV for densities ρ/ρ₀ = 1/6, 1/3, 1/2, 2/3
- Specific heat c_ρ(T) at fixed density ρ/ρ₀ = 1/3 shows a finite discontinuity at the mixed-phase/gaseous-phase boundary

## Context

This work provides the first exact analytical solution of the statistical multifragmentation model in the thermodynamic limit, extending previous numerical studies by Chomaz (1995) and Gulminelli (1998, 1999, 2000) that were limited to finite nuclear systems in canonical and microcanonical ensembles. The authors explicitly contradict the expectation of Gulminelli (1998, 1999) that an infinite peak of zero width would appear in the specific heat in the thermodynamic limit, and clarify that the rapid energy increase at fixed density near the mixed-phase boundary is not a "boiling point" phenomenon (which occurs at constant pressure, not constant density). The solution also represents an exact treatment of Fisher's droplet model (Fisher, 1967) with excluded volume effects incorporated. This enables rigorous study of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition and clarifies the phase diagram structure that was previously unclear from finite-system calculations.
