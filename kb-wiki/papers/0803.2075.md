# Parity-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction from different approaches

> Two-pion exchange parity-violating nucleon-nucleon interactions from recent effective field theories and earlier fully covariant approaches are investigated. The potentials are compared with the idea to obtain better insight on the role of low-energy constants appearing in the effective field theory approach and the convergence of this one in terms of a perturbative series. The results are illustrated by considering the longitudinal asymmetry of polarized protons scattering off protons, $\vec{p}+p -> p+p$, and the asymmetry of the photon emission in radiative capture of polarized neutrons by protons, $\vec{n}+p -> d+γ$.

## Key claim

The two-pion exchange parity-violating nucleon-nucleon potentials from effective field theory and from earlier covariant approaches can be made to agree only after accounting for contact-term contributions (low-energy constants), and the convergence of the perturbative expansion in the effective field theory approach remains uncertain beyond next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Method

The authors compared the isovector two-pion exchange parity-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction derived from a fully covariant dispersion-relation formalism (retaining only the four leading terms in a 1/M expansion) with the corresponding interaction from chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order, using both the maximal-subtraction and minimal-subtraction schemes. They also derived expressions from time-ordered diagrams in the non-relativistic limit as an intermediate check, and examined the large-nucleon-mass limit of the covariant results to facilitate comparison. The potentials were then applied to calculate the longitudinal asymmetry in polarized proton-proton scattering and the photon asymmetry in radiative neutron-proton capture.

## Key numbers

* The covariant approach yields spectral functions g_ij(t') that asymptotically behave as t'^{-1/2} (up to log terms), ensuring convergence of the dispersion integrals; for example, g_44(t') ~ (2/M sqrt(t')) (ln(t'/M^2) - 1) for large t'.
* In the large-M limit, the covariant expressions for v_44(q) and v_56(q) reduce to forms proportional to g_A^3 L(q) and combinations of L(q) and H(q), with the scale Lambda_chi = 4 pi f_pi approximately 1 GeV.
* The time-ordered diagram approach gives v_44(q) proportional to (1 - L(q)) after subtraction, with the divergent part contributing approximately 4 pi ln(Lambda_tilde / 2 m_pi) in the small-q limit.

## Context

This work extends earlier parity-violating nucleon-nucleon studies from the 1970s by Desplanques (1972), Pirner (1973), and Chemtob (1974), who used covariant Feynman diagrams or dispersion relations, and compares them with the effective field theory approach of Zhu et al. (2005). The comparison clarifies the role of low-energy constants in reconciling the two formalisms and highlights that the two-pion exchange contribution, while well-determined, is small compared to other uncertainties in parity-violating observables. The results enable future work to assess whether higher-order corrections beyond next-to-next-to-leading order are needed in the effective field theory expansion.
