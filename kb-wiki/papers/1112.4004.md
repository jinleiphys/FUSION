# Condensate Fraction in Neutron Matter

> We study the Bose-Einstein condensation of fermionic pairs in the uniform neutron matter by using the concept of the off-diagonal long-range order of the two-body density matrix of the system. We derive explicit formulas for the condensate density $ρ_c$ and the condensate fraction $ρ_c/ρ$ in terms of the scaled pairing energy gap $Δ/ε_F$, where $ε_F$ is the Fermi energy. We calculate the condensate fraction $ρ_c/ρ$ as a function of the density $ρ$ by using previously obtained results for the pairing gap $Δ$. We find the maximum condensate fraction $(ρ_c/ρ)_{max}= 0.42$ at the density $ρ=5.3\cdot 10^{-4}$ fm$^{-3}$, which corresponds to the Fermi wave number $k_F= 0.25$ fm$^{-1}$.

# Condensate Fraction in Neutron Matter

## Key claim

The condensate fraction of neutron matter reaches a maximum of 0.42 at a density of 5.3 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³, but this result depends on using previously computed pairing gap data from the Argonne V18 nuclear potential; the system never reaches the deep BEC regime of 100% condensate fraction because neutron-neutron pairs do not form a true bound state.

## Method

The author derives analytical formulas for the condensate density and condensate fraction as functions of the scaled pairing gap Δ/εF within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach, using the off-diagonal long-range order (ODLRO) concept of Yang. The condensate fraction is then evaluated numerically by inserting previously published pairing gap data from Matsuo (using the G3RS potential) and from Gezerlis and Carlson (using the Argonne V18 potential), rather than solving the full gap equation anew.

## Key numbers

- Maximum condensate fraction (ρcon/ρ)max = 0.42 at density ρ = 5.3 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³, corresponding to Fermi wave number kF = 0.25 fm⁻¹ (using Argonne V18 potential data)
- In the very-low-density BCS regime (kF|as| ≪ 1, with scattering length as = −18.5 fm), the condensate fraction follows ρcon/ρ = (3π/e²) exp[−π/(2kF|as|)]
- The finite-temperature Path Integral Monte Carlo data of Wlazlowski and Magierski at ρ = 0.003 fm⁻³ give a condensate fraction of about 0.35 at zero temperature
- Nuclear saturation density is 0.16 fm⁻³, much larger than the density at maximum condensate fraction

## Context

This work extends the ODLRO-based approach previously applied to ultracold fermionic atoms in the BCS-BEC crossover by Salasnich and by Ortiz to the case of uniform neutron matter, which is relevant for neutron star crusts. It complements the finite-temperature Path Integral Monte Carlo simulations of Wlazlowski and Magierski (performed only at ρ = 0.003 fm⁻³) by providing a systematic zero-temperature study across a range of densities. The results show that neutron matter exhibits a BCS-quasiunitary-BCS crossover as density increases, with a maximum condensate fraction of about 40%, compared to about 70% calculated for the unitarity Fermi gas.
