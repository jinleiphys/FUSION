# Self-consistent relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation and its applications to charge-exchange excitations and $β$-decay half-lives

> The self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) approach is formulated in the canonical single-nucleon basis of the relativistic Hatree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) theory. This approach is applied to study the isobaric analog states (IAS) and Gamov-Teller resonances (GTR) by taking Sn isotopes as examples. It is found that self-consistent treatment of the particle-particle residual interaction is essential to concentrate the IAS in a single peak for open-shell nuclei and the Coulomb exchange term is very important to predict the IAS energies. For the GTR, the isovector pairing can increase the calculated GTR energy, while the isoscalar pairing has an important influence on the low-lying tail of the GT transition. Furthermore, the QRPA approach is employed to predict nuclear $β$-decay half-lives. With an isospin-dependent pairing interaction in the isoscalar channel, the RHFB+QRPA approach almost completely reproduces the experimental $β$-decay half-lives for nuclei up to the Sn isotopes with half-lives smaller than one second. Large discrepancies are found for the Ni, Zn, and Ge isotopes with neutron number smaller than $50$, as well as the Sn isotopes with neutron number smaller than $82$. The potential reasons for these discrepancies are discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The self-consistent RHFB+QRPA approach reproduces experimental β-decay half-lives for nuclei up to Sn isotopes with half-lives smaller than one second, but only when an isospin-dependent pairing interaction is used in the isoscalar channel; large discrepancies remain for Ni, Zn, and Ge isotopes with neutron number smaller than 50, and Sn isotopes with neutron number smaller than 82.

## Method

The authors formulated the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) in the canonical single-nucleon basis of the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) theory, using the PKO1 effective interaction. They applied this self-consistent RHFB+QRPA approach to compute isobaric analog states (IAS) and Gamow-Teller resonances (GTR) for Sn isotopes, and then calculated β-decay half-lives for neutron-rich even-even nuclei with 20 ≤ Z ≤ 50, employing a finite-range isoscalar pairing interaction with an isospin-dependent strength parameter.

## Key numbers

- The IAS in ¹¹⁴Sn, with Coulomb interaction switched off, exhausts 99.94% of the N−Z sum rule at an energy of 0.05 MeV when both particle-hole and particle-particle residual interactions are included.
- The symmetry energy for PKO1 is 34.4 MeV, compared to 32.3 MeV for DD-ME2, with the constraint Esym = 32.5 ± 2.5 MeV from combined terrestrial and astrophysical data.
- The total GT strength in the nucleon sector is reduced by about 12% in nuclear matter and by 6–7% in finite nuclei relative to the Ikeda sum rule when Dirac sea effects are neglected.
- The strength parameter V0 for the isoscalar pairing interaction uses VL = 134.0 MeV, VD = 121.1 MeV, a = 8.5, and b = −0.4, providing the best description of available half-life data in the region 20 ≤ Z ≤ 50.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent relativistic RPA approach developed by Liang et al. (2008, 2012) for doubly magic nuclei to open-shell nuclei by incorporating pairing correlations via the RHFB theory, following the earlier RHB+QRPA framework of Paar et al. (2003, 2004) and Niksic et al. (2005). The RHFB+QRPA approach enables systematic predictions of β-decay half-lives for neutron-rich nuclei relevant to r-process simulations, and the identified discrepancies for Ni, Zn, Ge, and Sn isotopes point to needed improvements in the treatment of pairing and shell effects near N=50 and N=82.
