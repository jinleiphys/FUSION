# Non-perturbative Approach to effective chiral Lagrangians and Meson Interactions

> We develop a coupled channel unitary approach describing the behavior at higher energies of systems whose low-energy dynamics is given by effective O(p^2) and O(p^4) chiral Lagrangians. Our free parameters are those of the O(p^4) Lagrangian. When applied to the meson-meson interaction, it yields a remarkable agreement with data up to sqrt{s}=1.2 GeV, dynamically generating the sigma, f_0, a_0,rho and K^* resonances. Further applications are also proposed.

# Non-perturbative Approach to effective chiral Lagrangians and Meson Interactions

## Key claim
The authors develop a coupled-channel unitary method that extends the range of effective chiral Lagrangians to energies up to about 1.2 GeV, dynamically generating the sigma, f0, a0, rho and K* resonances. The method works at O(p^4) using the chiral Lagrangian coefficients as free parameters, but the approximation neglects crossed-channel loop contributions and tadpole effects, which are reabsorbed into the fitted parameters.

## Method
The authors combine elements of the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) and coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger (LS) equations to construct a unitary T-matrix. They approximate the real part of the O(p^4) amplitude as the sum of the O(p^4) tree-level polynomial plus the product T2 * Re(G) * T2, where G is a diagonal loop integral regularized with a cutoff. This yields the final formula T = T2 * [T2 - T4^P - T2 * G * T2]^{-1} * T2, which is then fitted to meson-meson scattering data.

## Key numbers
- The method reproduces data up to sqrt(s) = 1.2 GeV, where multi-meson channels become relevant.
- The cutoff in the loop integral is set to q_max = 0.9 GeV, adjusted to data.
- The sigma meson is found around 500 MeV with a large width, consistent with Truong (1988) and Oller and Oset (1997).
- The rho(770) and K*(892) resonances are reproduced, as well as the f0(980) and a0(980) in the scalar sector.

## Context
This work extends the Inverse Amplitude Method of Truong (1988) by incorporating coupled channels, which were necessary to reproduce the f0 and a0 resonances that elastic IAM could not generate. It also improves upon the earlier coupled-channel LS approach of Oller and Oset (1997), which used only O(p^2) amplitudes and failed to reproduce the rho and K* resonances. The method enables future applications to gamma-gamma to meson-meson reactions, form factors, meson-nucleon interactions, and studies of the strongly interacting symmetry breaking sector at LHC energies.
