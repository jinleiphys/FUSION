# Superallowed Fermi transitions in RPA with a relativistic point-coupling energy functional

> The self-consistent random phase approximation (RPA) approach with the residual interaction derived from a relativistic point-coupling energy functional is applied to evaluate the isospin symmetry-breaking corrections δc for the 0+\to0+ superallowed Fermi transitions. With these δc values, together with the available experimental ft values and the improved radiative corrections, the unitarity of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix is examined. Even with the consideration of uncertainty, the sum of squared top-row elements has been shown to deviate from the unitarity condition by 0.1% for all the employed relativistic energy functionals.

## Key claim

Even when including theoretical uncertainties, the sum of squared top-row elements of the CKM matrix deviates from unitarity by about 0.1% for all four relativistic energy functionals tested (PC-F1, PC-PK1, PKO1, and DD-ME2), indicating that additional effects such as deformation and pairing correlations may be needed to resolve the discrepancy.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent charge-exchange random phase approximation (RPA) calculations using residual interactions derived from relativistic point-coupling energy functionals (PC-F1 and PC-PK1). The isospin symmetry-breaking corrections δc were computed for 0+ → 0+ superallowed Fermi transitions in nuclei from 10C to 74Rb, and these corrections were combined with experimental ft values from Hardy (2009) and improved radiative corrections from Towner (2008) to extract nucleus-independent Ft values and the CKM matrix element |Vud|.

## Key numbers

- The isospin symmetry-breaking correction δc for 10C → 10B is 0.109% with PC-F1, compared to 0.082% with PKO1 and 0.150% with DD-ME2
- The δc value for 74Rb → 74Kr is 0.668% with PC-F1, 0.597% with PC-PK1, 1.088% with PKO1, and 1.258% with DD-ME2
- The average nucleus-independent Ft value is 3080.3(7) s for PC-F1 and 3081.1(7) s for PC-PK1
- The χ²/ν for constancy of Ft values is 1.1 for PC-F1 and 1.4 for PC-PK1
- |Vud| = 0.97290(21) from PC-F1 and 0.97278(22) from PC-PK1
- The sum of squared top-row CKM elements is 0.9974(10) for PC-F1 and 0.9972(10) for PC-PK1, deviating from unitarity by about 0.1%

## Context

This work extends the relativistic RPA approach for superallowed Fermi transitions from meson-exchange functionals (PKO1 and DD-ME2, studied by Liang in 2009) to the simpler point-coupling functionals PC-F1 and PC-PK1. The results are consistent with previous findings that the Ft values show good constancy, but the 0.1% deviation from CKM unitarity persists across all functionals. The authors suggest that future work should incorporate deformation and pairing correlations within a deformed QRPA framework with particle number and angular momentum projection.
