# Neutrino propagation in nuclear medium and neutrinoless double-beta decay

> We discuss a novel effect in neutrinoless double beta (0νββ) decay related with the fact that its underlying mechanisms take place in the nuclear matter environment. We study the neutrino exchange mechanism and demonstrate the possible impact of nuclear medium via Lepton Number Violating (LNV) 4-fermion interactions of neutrino with quarks from decaying nucleus. The net effect of these interactions is generation of an effective in-medium Majorana neutrino mass matrix. The enhanced rate of the 0νββ-decay can lead to the apparent incompatibility of observations of the 0νββ-decay with the value of the neutrino mass determined or restricted by the β-decay and cosmological data. The effective neutrino masses and mixing are calculated for the complete set of the relevant 4-fermion neutrino-quark operators. Using experimental data on the 0νββ-decay in combination with the β-decay and cosmological data we evaluate the characteristic scales of the LNV operators: Λ > 2.4 TeV.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that neutrinoless double-beta decay rates can be enhanced by Lepton Number Violating (LNV) 4-fermion interactions between neutrinos and quarks in the nuclear medium, generating an effective in-medium Majorana neutrino mass matrix. This enhancement could cause apparent incompatibility between 0νββ-decay observations and neutrino mass limits from beta-decay and cosmological data, but only if the LNV operators exist with characteristic scales below about 2.4 TeV.

## Method
The authors calculated the effect of nuclear matter on 0νββ-decay by introducing effective LNV and lepton-number-conserving 4-fermion neutrino-quark operators of the lowest dimension, then applying a mean-field approximation where the scalar quark condensate ⟨q̄q⟩ replaces the quark bilinear operator. They diagonalized the resulting in-medium neutrino Lagrangian to obtain effective neutrino masses and mixing matrices, and used experimental limits from 0νββ-decay (EXO-200, KamLAND-Zen, GERDA), tritium beta-decay, and cosmological data (Planck) to constrain the scale of the LNV operators.

## Key numbers
- Lower limit on the LNV operator scale: Λ_LNV ≥ 2.4 TeV (from Planck cosmological data) and Λ_LNV ≥ 1.1 TeV (from tritium beta-decay limits)
- Dimensionless coupling parameter ε_ij ≤ 0.02 (Planck) and ε_ij ≤ 0.1 (Tritium), characterizing the LNV operator strength relative to the Fermi constant G_F
- Current experimental limit on the effective Majorana mass parameter: |m_ββ| ≤ 0.2 - 0.4 eV from EXO-200, KamLAND-Zen (¹³⁶Xe) and GERDA (⁷⁶Ge) experiments
- Tritium beta-decay limit: m_β² ≤ (2.2 eV)² at 95% C.L.
- Planck limit on sum of neutrino masses: Σ m_i ≤ 0.23 - 1.08 eV, implying m_0 ≤ 0.07 eV
- Nuclear scalar quark condensate at saturation: ⟨q̄q⟩ ≈ 0.25 fm⁻³
- Fermi momentum of nucleons in nuclei: p_F ∼ 280 MeV

## Context
This work extends the standard treatment of 0νββ-decay mechanisms, which conventionally assume LNV processes occur in vacuum with nuclear effects included only as smearing via nuclear wavefunctions. The authors introduce a novel nuclear medium effect via LNV neutrino-quark interactions, contrasting with the standard Majorana neutrino exchange mechanism. The results enable future tests where improved cosmological and beta-decay neutrino mass limits might conflict with 0νββ-decay evidence, potentially requiring new TeV-scale physics. The derived limits are comparable to the most stringent constraints on non-standard vector-type neutrino interactions reviewed by Ohlsson (2012).
