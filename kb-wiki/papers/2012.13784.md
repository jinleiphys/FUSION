# Sphaleron Transition Rates and the Chiral Magnetic Effect

> The chiral magnetic effect is a novel quantum phenomenon proposed for high-energy nuclear collisions but which has yet to be observed. We quantify the axial charge relaxation time, due to sphalerons, which enters in simulations of this effect. An extrapolation of weak coupling calculations of the sphaleron rate yields rather different relaxation times than strong coupling AdS/CFT calculations. The AdS/CFT relaxation time is the larger one of the two by an order of magnitude, but the weak coupling relaxation time may not be reliable because it is only marginally bigger than the microscopic thermalization time. The role of quark masses has yet to be accurately assessed.

## Key claim

The axial charge relaxation time due to sphalerons, which enters simulations of the chiral magnetic effect, differs by an order of magnitude between weak coupling extrapolations and strong coupling AdS/CFT calculations; however, the weak coupling result may not be reliable because it is only marginally larger than the microscopic thermalization time, and the role of quark masses has yet to be accurately assessed.

## Method

The authors rederive the axial charge equilibration time from the sphaleron transition rate at finite temperature for massless quarks using the fluctuation-dissipation theorem. They compare two approaches for the sphaleron rate: a semi-analytical weak coupling expression fit to real-time lattice Hamiltonian simulations from Moore (2000), and a strong coupling result from AdS/CFT in N=4 Super Yang-Mills theory. The strong coupling alpha_s as a function of temperature is taken from fits to lattice QCD with physical quark masses from Albright (2015). The microscopic thermalization time is taken from effective kinetic theory calculations by Kurkela (2012).

## Key numbers

- The AdS/CFT sphaleron rate is Gamma_sphal = (alpha_s N_c)^2 / (16 pi) T^4, giving an axial charge relaxation time that is larger than the weak coupling result by an order of magnitude.
- The weak coupling sphaleron rate formula for SU(3) is Gamma_sphal = [792/(6+N_f)] [ln(m_D/gamma) + 3.041] alpha_s^5 T^4, with color Debye mass m_D^2 = (1 + N_f/6) 4 pi alpha_s T^2 and color randomization rate gamma = 3 alpha_s T [ln(m_D/gamma) + 3.041].
- The ratio of numerical to semi-analytical sphaleron rates reaches 0.624 +/- 0.011 at the largest coupling alpha_s = 0.093 used in lattice simulations.
- The microscopic thermalization time is 1/tau_eq = [1 - 0.12 ln(12 pi alpha_s)] 2 pi^2 alpha_s^2 T, valid for couplings in the range 5/(600 pi) <= alpha_s <= 5/(6 pi) approximately 0.265.
- The axial charge relaxation time is computed for temperatures between 200 and 1000 MeV, with heavy ion collision temperatures likely not exceeding 600 MeV.
- The strange quark mass m_s approximately 110 MeV is small compared to temperature but the relevant scale for sphaleron transitions is t_sphal, not T; when m t_sphal becomes of order 1 the charge separation effect essentially disappears.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of spin equilibration mechanisms in heavy ion collisions, including the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft interaction (Kapusta, Rrapaj, and Rudaz, 2018, 2019, 2020). It addresses the axial charge relaxation time needed for simulations of the chiral magnetic effect proposed by Fukushima (2008) and reviewed by Kharzeev (2013, 2016). The paper compares weak coupling calculations from Moore (2000) and Arnold (1997, 2000) with strong coupling AdS/CFT results from Son and Starinets (2002), finding that neither approach yields a definitive conclusion about whether axial charge can equilibrate within the expansion time scales of heavy ion collisions (1 to 10 fm/c). The role of quark masses on the sphaleron rate remains an open question, with preliminary calculations by Mace (2016) suggesting that strange quarks may suppress the charge separation effect.
