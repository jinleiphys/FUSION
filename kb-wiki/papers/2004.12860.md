# Size properties of the largest fragments produced in the framework of the statistical multifragmentation model

> We study the size properties of the largest intermediate mass fragments in each partition mode, produced in the prompt statistical breakup of a thermally equilibrated nuclear source, at different temperatures. We find that an appreciable amount of events have primary intermediate mass fragments of similar sizes. Our results suggest that, depending on the temperature of the fragmenting source, their production may be much larger than what would be expected from considerations based on purely combinatorial arrangements of the nucleons in the fragmenting system. We also find that the isospin composition of the largest fragments is sensitive to their rank size within the event. We suggest that experimental analyses, conceived to reconstruct the breakup configuration, should be employed to investigate the validity of our findings.

## Key claim
The production of multiple intermediate mass fragments of similar sizes in nuclear multifragmentation can arise from statistical phase-space considerations within the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM), not exclusively from spinodal instabilities predicted by dynamical mean-field calculations. The balance between purely combinatorial arrangements of nucleons and statistical weights is sensitive to the breakup temperature, with higher temperatures favoring statistical emission of similarly-sized fragments.

## Method
The authors employed a version of the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) based on exact recurrence relations developed by Das Gupta and Mekjian (1995), which allows calculation of individual partition properties without Monte Carlo sampling. They derived expressions to compute the probability of observing specific subsets of fragments (the M largest fragments with atomic number z >= 5) within all possible partitions of a source with A0=189 and Z0=83, corresponding to 80% of the 124Xe+112Sn system studied experimentally by Borderie et al. (2018). The analysis compared results using full statistical weights against results assuming only combinatorial arrangements (omega_i=1) to isolate the role of phase-space versus combinatorial constraints.

## Key numbers
- Source size: A0=189, Z0=83 (80% of the 124Xe+112Sn system)
- Breakup temperatures studied: T = 5.0, 5.5, and 6.0 MeV
- Breakup density: rho = rho0/6, where rho0 is normal nuclear matter density
- For M=3 fragments with z >= 5, the number of partitions is 2.43961 x 10^8; for M=4 fragments, 1.62527 x 10^10 partitions
- The ratio R_Y between full statistical weight and combinatorial-only distributions was analyzed for Y >= 10^-4 and limited to R_Y <= 5
- At T=5.5 MeV, R_Y < 1 for average charge <z> less than approximately 15 (except for sigma_z >= 10), while R_Y > 1 for <z> >= 15
- At T=6.0 MeV, R_Y >> 1 in most of the overlapping region between the two distributions, particularly at small sigma_z
- Source asymmetry parameter x = 1 - 2Z0/A0 values studied: x=0.121 (symmetric case), x=0.185, and x=0.058

## Context
This work extends the experimental findings of Borderie et al. (2018), who reported indications of similarly-sized fragments in heavy-ion collisions, and addresses the question raised in that work and by fragSizeCorrelations2002 of whether such events arise from combinatorial arrangements or spinodal instabilities. The authors use the exact recurrence formulation of Das Gupta and Mekjian (1995) and ChaseMekjian1995 to overcome sampling limitations of standard Monte Carlo SMM approaches. The results suggest that experimental analyses aimed at reconstructing the breakup configuration (as in freezeOut, primFragsIndra2003, primaryFrags2014, primaryFrags2014_2, expRecEex2013, fragReconstructionNPA) should be employed to test these predictions, and that the isospin composition of the largest fragments may help investigate the asymmetry energy term in the nuclear equation of state at breakup.
