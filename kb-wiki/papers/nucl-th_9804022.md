# Strangeness Production and Ultrarelativistic Cascades

> A two phase cascade, LUCIFER II, developed for the treatment of ultra high energy ion-ion collisions is applied to the production of strangeness at SPS energies $\sqrt{s}=17-20$. This simulation is able to simultaneously describe both hard processes such as Drell-Yan and slower, soft processes such as the production of light mesons, including strange mesons, by separating the dynamics into two steps, a fast cascade involving only nucleons in the original colliding relativistic ions followed, after an appropriate delay, by multiscattering of the resulting excited baryons and mesons produced virtually in the first step. No energy loss can take place in the short time interval over which the first cascade takes place. The chief result is a reconciliation of the important Drell-Yan measurements with the apparent success of standard cascades to describe the nucleon stopping and meson production in heavy ion experiments at the CERN SPS. A byproduct, obtained here in preliminary calculations, is a description of strangeness production in the collision of massive ions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the apparent contradiction between Drell-Yan measurements (which show no energy loss for the incoming proton) and the successful description of nucleon stopping and meson production by standard cascades can be reconciled by separating the dynamics into a fast, high-energy cascade followed by a slow, low-energy cascade, with the caveat that the strangeness production results are preliminary.

## Method

The authors developed a two-phase cascade code, LUCIFER II, which first runs a fast, high-energy cascade involving only nucleons from the original colliding ions, recording collision histories without allowing energy loss. After an intermediate reinitialization that uses the space-time and energy-momentum history to construct groups of nucleons and deposit lost energy into generic meson resonances, a second, low-energy hadronic cascade is performed using generic baryon and meson resonances (masses between 0.3 and 1.6 GeV) that rescatter and decay into pions, kaons, and hyperons.

## Key numbers

- SPS collision energies: sqrt(s) = 17-20 GeV
- Generic meson resonance mass centered near 600-700 MeV
- Generic baryon masses between m_N and 1.6 GeV; normal meson masses between 0.3 and 1.0 GeV
- Average number of collisions in central S+S is near 3.5; in Pb+Pb near 7.5
- Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV/c per nucleon incoming laboratory momentum
- Energy densities achieved in Pb+Pb simulations: approximately 4-5 GeV/(fm)^3
- Low-energy cascade collisions occur at center-of-mass energies less than sqrt(s) = 5-8 GeV
- Drell-Yan cross-section in p+A collisions scales as sigma ~ A

## Context

This work extends the LUCIFER I cascade (Kahana and Kahana, earlier work) by introducing a two-stage time-separation approach that reconciles hard processes like Drell-Yan with soft hadronic processes. The approach builds on time-scale arguments from Gottfried (year not given), Koplik (year not given), and Dokshitzer (year not given), and uses the wounded nucleon model concept. The results enable future investigation of whether a purely hadronic explanation of J/psi suppression observed by NA50 is possible, as questioned by Kharzeev and Satz (year not given).
