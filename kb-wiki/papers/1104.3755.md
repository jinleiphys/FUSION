# Finite-size scaling as a tool in the search for the QCD critical point in heavy ion data

> Given the short lifetime and the reduced volume of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed in high-energy heavy ion collisions, a possible critical endpoint (CEP) will be blurred in a region and the effects from criticality severely smoothened. Nevertheless, the non-monotonic behavior of correlation functions near criticality for systems of different sizes, given by different centralities in heavy ion collisions, must obey finite-size scaling. We apply the predicting power of scaling plots to the search for the CEP of strong interactions in heavy ion collisions using data from RHIC and SPS. The results of our data analysis exclude a critical point below chemical potentials $μ\sim 450 $MeV. Extrapolating the analysis, we speculate that criticality could appear slightly above $μ\sim 500 $MeV. Using available data we extrapolate our scaling curves to predict the behavior of new data at lower center-of-mass energy, currently being investigated in the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC. If it turns out that the QGP phase is no longer achievable in heavy ion experiments before the CEP is reached, FSS might be the only way to experimentally estimate its position in the phase diagram.

## Key claim

Finite-size scaling (FSS) analysis of existing heavy-ion data from RHIC and SPS can exclude a QCD critical endpoint below a baryochemical potential of about 450 MeV, and extrapolation suggests criticality could appear slightly above 500 MeV, but the exact location is highly dependent on the assumed functional form of the scaling function.

## Method

The authors applied finite-size scaling to experimental data on transverse momentum fluctuations (σ_{p_T}/⟨p_T⟩) measured by STAR at RHIC for collision energies √s_{NN} = 19.6, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV, binned by centrality (0-5% through 60-70% most central). They estimated system sizes L from a Glauber Monte-Carlo model (ranging from 3.4 fm to 12.4 fm) and used the Ising universality class exponent ν = 2/3 with γ_x ≈ 1. The scaled observable σ_{p_T}/⟨p_T⟩ × L^{-γ_x/ν} was plotted against baryochemical potential μ (converted from √s_{NN} via a thermal model parameterization), and polynomial fits were performed to find crossing points of curves for different system sizes.

## Key numbers

- A critical point below μ ∼ 450 MeV is excluded by the data analysis.
- Extrapolation using a second-order polynomial fit yields a critical point at μ ∼ 510 MeV, corresponding to √s_{NN} = 5.75 GeV.
- System sizes L for centrality bins range from 3.4 fm (60-70% peripheral) to 12.4 fm (0-5% central).
- Data sets analyzed include √s_{NN} = 17.3, 19.6, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV.
- For ν = 2/3, a central collision (L₂ = 4L₁) at μ₂ can be compared to a peripheral collision at μ₁ when μ₁ - μ_c ≈ 8(μ₂ - μ_c).

## Context

This work extends the finite-size scaling approach proposed by Palhares (2009) to actual experimental data, applying a technique from statistical mechanics (Fisher 1971; Brezin 1981, 1985) to the search for the QCD critical point. It complements lattice QCD results from de Forcrand (2007) that exclude a chiral critical point at small μ, and provides predictions for the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC at lower energies (11.5, 7.7, and 5 GeV). The method offers an alternative to direct searches for non-monotonic fluctuation signatures (Stephanov 1998, 2008) which are blurred by finite-size and finite-lifetime effects.
