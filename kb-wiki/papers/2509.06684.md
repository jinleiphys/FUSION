# Core or Halo? Two-Fluid Analysis of Dark Matter-Admixed Quarkyonic Stars in the Multi-Messenger Era

> For the first time, we explore dark matter (DM) admixed quarkyonic stars (DAQSs) within a two-fluid formalism, where the normal/visible sector is modeled by a quarkyonic equation of state (EOS) in the Effective Relativistic Mean Field (E-RMF) framework and the DM component is treated as a degenerate fermionic gas with scalar and vector self-interactions. Our analysis begins with the mass-radius (M-R) relation, showing that the inclusion of DM enables stellar configurations to reach the mass range compatible with the GW190814 event. We identify both DM core and DM halo morphologies among the viable EOSs, with core dominated and halo dominated cases exhibiting distinct signatures. By fixing the stellar mass within the GW190814 range, we constrain the possible dark matter fractions and explore the role of different interaction channels. Using the EOSs consistent with these constraints, we further investigate the tidal deformability ($Λ$), moment of inertia (MOI), and stellar radius, finding broad agreement with constraints from GW170817, GW190814, and NICER. Finally, we compile the characteristic properties of DAQSs, including EOS type, DM fractions, morphology (core vs halo), and macroscopic observables in a comparative summary. This study provides a unified two-fluid framework to explore dense QCD matter and dark matter in the multi-messenger era, suggesting that the GW190814 secondary object could plausibly be interpreted as either a DM core or a DM halo quarkyonic star.

# Core or Halo? Two-Fluid Analysis of Dark Matter-Admixed Quarkyonic Stars in the Multi-Messenger Era

## Key claim
The GW190814 secondary object (2.50–2.67 solar masses) could plausibly be interpreted as either a dark matter core quarkyonic star or a dark matter halo quarkyonic star, depending on the dark matter particle mass, interaction type, and fraction. The claim is conditional on the specific quarkyonic equation of state parameters (transition density 0.3 fm⁻³, QCD confinement scale 800 MeV) and the two-fluid formalism where dark matter and normal matter interact only gravitationally.

## Method
The authors model the visible sector using a quarkyonic equation of state within the Effective Relativistic Mean Field (E-RMF) framework with G3 and IOPB-I parametrizations, where nucleons occupy a thin shell near the Fermi surface and up/down quarks fill low-momentum states below a transition density. The dark matter component is treated as a degenerate fermionic gas with scalar and vector self-interactions, characterized by attractive coupling C_DS and repulsive coupling C_DV. The two components are coupled only through gravity in a two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) system, from which mass-radius relations, tidal deformability, and moment of inertia are computed.

## Key numbers
- Pure quarkyonic star maximum masses: 2.75 M⊙ (G3 force) and 2.50 M⊙ (IOPB-I force) at n_t = 0.3 fm⁻³ and Λ_cs = 800 MeV
- Six DAQS configurations (DAQS1–DAQS6) consistent with GW190814 secondary mass range (2.50–2.67 M⊙) are identified
- DAQS2 and DAQS6 (M_DM = 0.7 GeV, mixed interaction) show anomalous moments of inertia reaching I ≃ 8–15 × 10⁴⁵ g·cm², well above the typical 0.4–6 × 10⁴⁵ g·cm² range
- DAQS4 (G3, M_DM = 1.0 GeV, attractive interaction, ratio parameter 2.0) shows central DM density peaking at ~1.4 fm⁻³, far exceeding NM density ~0.4 fm⁻³
- DAQS5 (IOPB-I, M_DM = 2.0 GeV, attractive interaction) is consistent with GW190814 tidal deformability constraint
- DAQS1, DAQS3, and DAQS4 satisfy tidal deformability bounds from GW170817

## Context
This work extends the quarkyonic matter framework of Dey (2024) and the two-fluid dark matter approach of Sandin (2009) and Xiang (2014) to simultaneously address the GW190814 secondary mass challenge and multi-messenger constraints from GW170817 and NICER. The study builds on prior work showing that dark matter admixture can help explain the heaviest observed compact objects (Dey 2024, Rather 2021). The results enable future multi-messenger discrimination between DM core and DM halo morphologies in compact stars, using tidal deformability and moment of inertia as distinguishing observables.
