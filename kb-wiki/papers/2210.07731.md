# Thermal Transitions in Dense Two-Colour QCD

> The infamous sign problem makes it impossible to probe dense (baryon density $μ_B>0$) QCD at temperatures near or below the deconfinement threshold. As a workaround, one can explore QCD-like theories such as two-colour QCD (QC2D) which don't suffer from this sign problem but are qualitively similar to real QCD. Previous studies on smaller lattice volumes have investigated deconfinement and colour superfluid to normal matter transitions. In this study we look at a larger lattice volume $N_s=24$ in an attempt to disentangle finite volume and finite temperature effects. We also fit to a larger number of diquark sources to better allow for extrapolation to zero diquark source.

## Key claim

The superfluid phase transition in two-colour QCD occurs around T ~ 84 MeV, which is significantly lower than the deconfinement crossover, confirming that the two transitions are distinct; however, an improved action and more data are required to determine the transition temperature more precisely with proper error bounds.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of two-colour QCD (QC2D) using an unimproved Wilson action with two quark flavours on a spatial volume N_s = 24, at fixed chemical potential μ = 443 MeV. They conducted a temperature sweep by varying the temporal extent N_τ from 3 to 20, corresponding to temperatures from 55 MeV to 365 MeV, and used four diquark source values (including aj = 0.010) to enable improved extrapolation to zero diquark source. The diquark condensate was fitted to the form ⟨qq⟩ = A + B j^α to extract the zero-source value, and thermodynamic observables were evaluated using the derivative method with Karsch coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a = 0.178(6) fm (or 0.9 per GeV)
- Pion mass m_π = 717(25) MeV; ratio m_π/m_ρ = 0.805(9)
- Chemical potential μ = 443 MeV (aμ = 0.400)
- Temperature range: 55 MeV to 365 MeV (N_τ = 3–20)
- Superfluid phase transition estimated at T ~ 84 MeV
- Polyakov loop renormalisation constants: Scheme A gives Z_L = 1.37495 (L_R = 0.5 at N_τ = 4, μ = 0); Scheme B gives Z_L = 1.15619 (L_R = 1.0 at N_τ = 4, μ = 0)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-colour QCD on smaller spatial volumes (N_s = 12 in Hands et al. (2010) and N_s = 16 in Boz et al. (2013)) by using a larger N_s = 24 lattice to disentangle finite-volume and finite-temperature effects. The results are qualitatively consistent with recent thermodynamic studies by Iida and Itou (2022) and Fujimoto et al. (2022). The larger volume and four diquark sources provide improved control over the j → 0 extrapolation compared to earlier work by Cotter et al. (2013) and Boz et al. (2013, 2020). The findings enable future studies using Symanzik-improved fermion actions and finer lattices with lighter quarks to achieve more physically realistic measurements.
