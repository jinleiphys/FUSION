# Impact-Parameter Dependence of Multiple Lepton-pair Production from Electromagnetic Fields

> In relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the strong Lorentz-contracted electromagnetic fields are capable of producing copious numbers of lepton pairs through the two-photon mechanism. Monte Carlo techniques have been developed that allow the exact calculation of production by this mechanism when a semi-classical approximation is made to the motion of the two ions. Here, we develop a hybrid Monte Carlo technique that enables us to calculate the impact parameter dependence of the two-photon mechanism for lepton-pair production, and by using this result, we obtain the probability distribution for multiple-pair production as a function of impact parameter. Computations are performed for S$+$Au and Pb$+$Pb systems at 200 A GeV and 160 A GeV, respectively. We also compare our results with the equivalent photon approximation and elucidate the differences.

## Key claim

The authors develop a hybrid Monte Carlo technique that enables calculation of the impact-parameter dependence of the two-photon mechanism for lepton-pair production, which previous Monte Carlo methods could not achieve because the analytic integration over impact parameter had to be abandoned. The method works within the semiclassical approximation that the heavy ions follow straight-line trajectories, and the authors note that this assumption may not be correct for central impact parameters but is reliable for peripheral collisions where most pair production occurs.

## Method

The authors generalize a previous Monte Carlo technique (Ref. CBMS) that integrated over all impact parameters analytically, instead computing the nine-dimensional integral for the function F(q) at fixed values of the momentum transfer q using Monte Carlo sampling. They fit a smooth exponential function to the Monte Carlo-generated F(q) points, then perform the final Bessel-function integral over q analytically to obtain the impact-parameter-dependent differential cross section dσ/db. The resulting single-pair probability P(b) is used as the mean of a Poisson distribution to compute multiple-pair production probabilities as a function of impact parameter.

## Key numbers

- For S+Au at 200 A GeV: total one-pair cross section = 160 barns
- For Pb+Pb at 160 A GeV: total one-pair cross section = 3569 barns
- For Pb+Pb at 160 A GeV: two-pair cross section = 95 barns
- For Pb+Pb at 160 A GeV: total multi-pair cross section (excluding single-pair) ≈ 100 barns, about 3% of the total cross section
- The smooth fit to F(q) for Pb+Pb: F(q) = 3835.7 exp(-1.34029 q)
- The smooth fit to F(q) for S+Au: F(q) = 163.5 exp(-1.3519 q)
- The equivalent-photon approximation overestimates the two-photon contribution by approximately 5% in both systems, with largest disagreement at small impact parameters
- For Pb+Pb, the equivalent-photon approximation overestimates the 2-pair cross section by 40% and the 3-pair cross section by a factor of 2

## Context

This work extends the Monte Carlo method of Ref. CBMS (which integrated over all impact parameters) to compute impact-parameter-dependent cross sections, enabling study of the electromagnetic background for hadronic interactions at RHIC. The results are compared with the equivalent-photon approximation as formulated in Refs. BB88 and RBW91, showing that the equivalent-photon approximation overestimates pair production at small impact parameters and becomes unreliable below one Compton wavelength. The calculated impact-parameter-dependent probabilities enable computation of multiple-pair production via the Poisson distribution derived by Baur (1990), RBW91, and Best (1992), and the authors note that for S+Au and Pb+Pb at these energies, a large portion of the cross section resides at impact parameters b > 2R (R ≈ 6.8 fm for gold, while the electron Compton wavelength is 386 fm), reducing concern about hadronic debris for low invariant masses.
