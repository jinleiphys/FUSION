# Neutron matter at the interface(s): static response and effective mass

> Neutron matter is interesting both as an extension of terrestrial nuclear physics and due to its significance for the study of neutron stars. In this work, after some introductory comments on nuclear forces, nuclear ab initio theory, and nuclear phenomenology, we employ two techniques, Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) and Energy Density Functionals, to practically handle an extended system composed of strongly interacting neutrons. We start by summarizing work on the static response of neutron matter, which considers the impact of external influences on the time-independent system. We then proceed to discuss new results of the energy of quasiparticle excitations in neutron matter, including QMC calculations with chiral or phenomenological nucleon-nucleon interactions. As part of this study, we carefully study the approach of our finite-number computations toward the infinite-system limit.

## Key claim

The paper reports new Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of the quasiparticle effective mass in neutron matter, but the extraction is systematically dependent on the finite-number extrapolation to the infinite-system limit, and the authors find that the effective mass approaches unity at low densities while decreasing steadily at higher densities, a trend they describe qualitatively rather than quantitatively.

## Method

The authors employed Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) to compute ground-state and excited-state energies for neutron systems of 66, 67, and 68 particles in a periodic box, using both the phenomenological Argonne v8' (AV8') plus Urbana-IX (UIX) three-body potential and chiral effective field theory interactions truncated at next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO). They extracted the quasiparticle effective mass by probing the energy dispersion relation near the Fermi surface, placing an additional particle at various excited momenta and subtracting the ground-state energy, then applied a finite-size extrapolation prescription to correct for periodic boundary conditions and approach the infinite-system limit.

## Key numbers

- The static response calculations used neutron number densities from 0.02 fm$^{-3}$ to 0.12 fm$^{-3}$, motivated by densities in a neutron star's crust and outer core.
- AFDMC static response calculations were performed for 66 neutrons at a density of 0.10 fm$^{-3}$ with external potential strengths of 0, 0.25, 0.3, 0.35, 0.5, and 0.75 times the Fermi energy $E_F = \hbar^2 k_F^2/(2m)$.
- The unmodified isovector gradient coefficient $C^{\Delta n}_1$ values were -16 MeV fm$^5$ for SLy4, -6 MeV fm$^5$ for SLy7, and -17 MeV fm$^5$ for SkM*; these were modified by fitting to the QMC response at each density.
- The free Fermi gas ground-state energy per particle in the thermodynamic limit is $\frac{3}{5}E_F$, where $k_F^3 = 3\pi^2 n$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous static response studies (Buraczynski 2016, Buraczynski 2017) that matched Skyrme energy density functionals to Quantum Monte Carlo results, and provides the first QMC extraction of the effective mass in neutron matter, building on earlier many-body extractions by Friedman (1981), Schwenk (2003), Drischler (2014), and others that gave inconsistent results. The effective mass results impact calculations of neutron star maximum mass, thermal properties, and giant quadrupole resonances, and the methodology enables future matching of Landau's Fermi liquid theory to microscopic QMC calculations.
