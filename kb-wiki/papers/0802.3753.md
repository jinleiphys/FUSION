# Interacting Quantum Dot Coupled to a Kondo Spin: A Universal Hamiltonian Study

> We study a mesoscopic interacting quantum dot described by the "universal Hamiltonian" that is coupled to a Kondo spin. The ferromagnetic exchange interaction within the dot leads to a stepwise increase of the ground state spin; this Stoner staircase is modified non-trivially by the Kondo interaction. We find that the spin-transition steps move to lower values of the exchange coupling for weak Kondo interaction, but shift back up for sufficiently strong Kondo coupling. The problem is solved numerically by diagonalizing the system Hamiltonian in a customized good-spin basis and analytically in the weak and strong Kondo coupling limits. The interplay of Kondo and ferromagnetic exchange can be probed with experimentally tunable parameters.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Stoner staircase (stepwise increase of ground-state spin with ferromagnetic exchange coupling) in a quantum dot is modified non-monotonically by Kondo coupling: spin-transition steps move to lower exchange coupling for weak Kondo interaction but shift back up for sufficiently strong Kondo coupling, with a smooth crossover between these limits.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the universal Hamiltonian (UH) plus Kondo coupling term numerically in a customized good-spin basis using a Lanczos-Arnoldi algorithm, for dots with up to N ~ 12 single-particle levels. They also perform analytical perturbation theory in the weak and strong Kondo coupling limits, deriving effective Hamiltonians for each regime.

## Key numbers

- For N = 11 (odd) and N = 12 (even) electrons, spin transition curves are computed in the two-dimensional parameter space of exchange coupling J_s and Kondo coupling J_k.
- At J_k → 0, spin transitions occur at J_s^m = δ(m+1)/(m+2) for m = 1,3,5,... (odd N) or m = 2,4,6,... (even N).
- The weak-coupling perturbative result is J_s^m = [(m+1)δ - α_m J_k]/(m+2), where α_m are positive constants of order one, valid for J_k ρ ≲ 0.1.
- The strong-coupling limit requires J_k ρ ≳ 1 (or J_k ≳ 2D) for N ≲ 12, where the Kondo spin and site-0 spin form a singlet and the reduced dot has one less level and one less electron.
- The renormalized exchange constant in strong coupling is J_s → J_s(1 + J_s/J_k) to lowest order in 1/J_k.

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field studies by Murthy (2005) that investigated Kondo correlations near the Stoner instability, and contrasts with bulk results by Larkin (1972) where a Kondo impurity enhances polarization. The universal Hamiltonian approach builds on the framework established by Alhassid (2000) and Rupp (2004) for describing chaotic quantum dots. The results enable experimental probing of the interplay between Kondo and ferromagnetic exchange by measuring spin transitions via Coulomb blockade peak shifts as a function of in-plane magnetic field and tunable Kondo coupling.
