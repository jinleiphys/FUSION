# The structure of nuclear systems derived from low momentum nucleon-nucleon potentials

> Various nuclear structure observables are evaluated employing low-momentum nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials $V_{\rm low-k}$ derived from the CD-Bonn and Nijmegen NN interactions $V_{NN}$. By construction, the high momentum modes of the original $V_{NN}$ are integrated out in $V_{\rm low-k}$, with the requirement that the deuteron binding energy and low energy phase shifts of $V_{NN}$ are exactly reproduced. Using this interaction, we evaluate the bulk properties (binding energy and saturation density) of nuclear matter and finite nuclei, in particular their dependence on the cut-off parameter. We also study the pairing gap and the residual interaction in nuclear matter in terms of the Landau parametrization. At low and medium densities, the HF and BHF binding energies for nuclear matter calculated with the $V_{\rm low-k}$'s derived from the CD-Bonn and Nijmegen potentials are nearly identical. The pairing gaps and Landau parameters derived from $V_{\rm low-k}$ are remarkably close to those given by the full-space $V_{NN}$. The $V_{\rm low-k}$ interactions, however, fail to reproduce the saturation property of nuclear matter at higher densities if the cut-off for the high momentum modes is assumed density independent.

## Key claim

The low-momentum nucleon-nucleon potential \(V_{\rm low-k}\) reproduces many nuclear structure observables from the full-space CD-Bonn and Nijmegen interactions at low and medium densities, but it fails to reproduce the saturation property of nuclear matter at higher densities if the cut-off for high-momentum modes is assumed density independent.

## Method

The authors constructed \(V_{\rm low-k}\) by integrating out high-momentum modes of the CD-Bonn and Nijmegen NN potentials, requiring that the deuteron binding energy and low-energy phase shifts of the original potentials are exactly reproduced. They then performed Hartree-Fock (HF) and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter and for the finite nucleus \(^{16}\)O, and solved the gap equation for neutron-neutron pairing in the \(^1S_0\) channel at a fixed Fermi momentum. The residual interaction was characterized via Landau parameters derived from the bare interaction and the \(G\)-matrix.

## Key numbers

- The HF calculation for the bare CD-Bonn interaction yields positive energies between 3 MeV per nucleon at small densities and 20 MeV per nucleon at \(k_F \approx 1.8\) fm\(^{-1}\) (density about 2.3 times empirical saturation density).
- The conventional BHF calculation for the bare CD-Bonn interaction yields an energy of -17 MeV per nucleon and a saturation density around twice the empirical saturation density.
- The pairing gap was evaluated for neutron matter at \(k_F = 1.36\) fm\(^{-1}\) using the CD-Bonn interaction and \(V_{\rm low-k}\) with effective masses \(m^* = m\) and \(m^* = 700\) MeV.
- The Landau parameters and the effective mass \(m^*/m\) (determined from \(f_1\) via \(m^*/m = 1 + f_1/3\)) and the symmetry energy coefficient \(\beta\) (given by \(\beta = (1/3)(k_F^2/(2m^*))(1 + f'_0)\)) were computed for the CD-Bonn potential and its \(V_{\rm low-k}\) derivatives.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization-group and effective-field-theory approach of Bogner et al. (2001, 2002) and Bogner and Schwenk (2002), who first constructed \(V_{\rm low-k}\) from realistic NN potentials. It tests \(V_{\rm low-k}\) against the full CD-Bonn and Nijmegen interactions in many-body calculations, finding that while \(V_{\rm low-k}\) works well at low densities and for finite nuclei like \(^{16}\)O, it overbinds nuclear matter at high densities because it does not account for the dispersive quenching of high-momentum modes that is present in the full \(G\)-matrix. The results enable the use of \(V_{\rm low-k}\) in low-energy nuclear structure calculations but caution against its application at high densities or when the single-particle spectrum above the cut-off is important.
