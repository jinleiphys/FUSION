# Astrophysical S_{17}(0) factor from a measurement of d(7Be,8B)n reaction at E_{c.m.} = 4.5 MeV

> Angular distribution measurements of $^2$H($^7$Be,$^7$Be)$^2$H and $^2$H($^7$Be,$^8$B)$n$ reactions at $E_{c.m.}\sim$~4.5 MeV were performed to extract the astrophysical $S_{17}(0)$ factor using the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) method. For this purpose a pure, low emittance $^7$Be beam was separated from the primary $^7$Li beam by a recoil mass spectrometer operated in a novel mode. A beam stopper at 0$^{\circ}$ allowed the use of a higher $^7$Be beam intensity. Measurement of the elastic scattering in the entrance channel using kinematic coincidence, facilitated the determination of the optical model parameters needed for the analysis of the transfer data. The present measurement significantly reduces errors in the extracted $^7$Be(p,$γ$) cross section using the ANC method. We get $S_{17}$~(0)~=~20.7~$\pm$~2.4 eV~b.

## Key claim

The astrophysical S\(_{17}\)(0) factor is determined to be 20.7 ± 2.4 eV b from the d(\(^7\)Be,\(^8\)B)n transfer reaction at E\(_{c.m.}\) = 4.5 MeV, with the total error including a conservative estimate from different choices of data points added in quadrature to the statistical and systematic errors.

## Method

Angular distributions of the \(^2\)H(\(^7\)Be,\(^7\)Be)\(^2\)H elastic scattering and \(^2\)H(\(^7\)Be,\(^8\)B)n transfer reactions were measured at E\(_{c.m.}\) ~ 4.5 MeV using a pure, low-emittance \(^7\)Be beam produced by a recoil mass spectrometer operated in a novel mode. The elastic scattering data were used to determine deuteron optical model parameters, and the transfer data were analyzed within the finite-range distorted wave Born approximation to extract the asymptotic normalization coefficient for the proton in \(^8\)B, from which the S\(_{17}\)(0) factor was calculated.

## Key numbers

- S\(_{17}\)(0) = 20.7 ± 2.4 eV b, extracted from the d(\(^7\)Be,\(^8\)B)n reaction at E\(_{c.m.}\) = 4.5 MeV.
- The S\(_{17}\)(0) values from 80 combinations of optical model and bound state potentials ranged from 18.8 eV b to 22.1 eV b, with a mean of 20.7 eV b and RMS deviation of 0.9 eV b.
- The statistical error was ± 1.4 eV b, and the systematic error (including target thickness uncertainty of ± 2%) was ± 1.0 eV b.
- Using the first 3, 5, and all 11 data points gave S\(_{17}\)(0) values of 22.3 ± 2.7 eV b, 22.7 ± 1.9 eV b, and 18.5 ± 1.6 eV b, respectively.
- The compound nuclear contribution to the transfer cross section was ~ 7.5% at 44 degrees and ~ 1% at 8 degrees.

## Context

This work improves upon the earlier d(\(^7\)Be,\(^8\)B)n measurement by Liu et al. (1996), which yielded S\(_{17}\)(0) = 27.4 ± 4.4 eV b but lacked elastic scattering data to constrain the optical model parameters. The present result of 20.7 ± 2.4 eV b is in good agreement with the direct capture measurement by Junghans et al. (2003) (~ 22.0 eV b) and with the CDCC analysis of \(^8\)B breakup by Ogata et al. (2005), reducing the previous discrepancy between direct and indirect methods. This demonstrates that the ANC method can be applied to measure (p,γ) S-factors for short-lived nuclei where direct capture measurements are difficult.
