# The Quark-Gluon-Plasma Liquid

> The quark-gluon plasma close to the critical temperature is a strongly interacting system. Using strongly coupled, classical, non-relativistic plasmas as an analogy, we argue that the quark-gluon plasma is in the liquid phase. This allows to understand experimental observations in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions and to interpret lattice QCD results. It also supports the indications of the presence of a strongly coupled QGP in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The quark-gluon plasma (QGP) close to the critical temperature is a strongly interacting system that, by analogy with strongly coupled classical non-relativistic plasmas, should be considered to be in the liquid phase rather than a gas, which helps explain experimental observations from ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions and lattice QCD results.

## Method

The author defines a Coulomb coupling parameter for the QGP as Γ = Cg²/(dT), where C is the Casimir invariant (4/3 for quarks, 3 for gluons), g is the strong coupling constant, d is the interparticle distance, and T is the temperature. Using typical temperatures reachable in heavy-ion collisions (T ≈ 200 MeV) and an ideal gas density estimate for two active quark flavors, the author computes Γ values and compares them to known phase behavior of classical non-relativistic plasmas. The author then examines two consequences of a liquid QGP: the pair correlation function as an alternative interpretation of lattice QCD meson correlators, and a modified Coulomb logarithm for transport cross sections derived from complex plasma physics.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the hadron-to-QGP transition: T_c ≈ 170 MeV
- For T = 200 MeV, the strong fine structure constant α_s is between 0.2 and 0.5, corresponding to g = 1.5 to 2.5
- Particle density (ideal gas, 2 quark flavors): n = (34/π²) ζ(3) T³, giving interparticle distance d ≈ 0.5 fm
- Coulomb coupling parameter Γ for the QGP at T = 200 MeV: Γ = 10 to 30
- Thermal de Broglie wavelength λ_th ≈ 1 fm, comparable to interparticle distance d = 0.5 fm
- Debye screening length at T = 200 MeV: less than 0.2 fm (using non-perturbative estimate μ = 6T)
- Coulomb radius for a thermal parton with energy 3T: ρ = Cg²/3T = 1 to 6 fm
- Ratio β = ρ/λ_D = 5 to 30
- For β = 10: standard Coulomb logarithm Λ = 0.005, modified Λ* = 0.1, giving transport cross section enhancement by a factor of 20
- For gluon scattering with β = 10 and maximum impact parameter 4.6 λ_D: cross section enhancement factor of 13

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Gyulassy and Heinz that the QGP is strongly coupled, based on the success of hydrodynamical models for RHIC data. The paper contradicts the perturbative QCD picture of a weakly interacting QGP and provides an alternative to the interpretation of lattice QCD meson correlators as indicating bound states above T_c (Shuryak). The analogy enables future quantitative work using molecular dynamics simulations for a classical relativistic Yukawa system of partons with effective temperature-dependent masses, opening a new field of "high-energy plasma physics."
