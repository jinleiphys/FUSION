# A new combined analysis of elastic and charge exchange $K N(\bar{K}N)$ scatterings in the Regge realm

> The Regge features of elastic $K^\pm N\to K^\pm N$, and charge exchange $K^-p\to\bar{K}^0n$ and $K^+n\to K^0p$ reactions are described by using a combined analysis of all these channels at high momenta. Based on the meson exchanges in the $t$-channel with their decays to $K\bar{K}$ pair allowed, the exchanges of $ρ(775)+ω(782)$ are excluded in contrast to existing model calculations and the present work follows a new scheme for the meson exchanges $a_0(980)+ϕ(1020)+f_2(1275)+a_2(1320)$ reggeized for the forward elastic scattering amplitude together with Pomeron. The charge exchange reactions are described by $a_0(980)+a_2(1320)$ exchanges in the $t$-channel. Dominance of the isoscalar $f_2$ and Pomeron exchanges beyond $P_{\rm Lab}\approx3$ GeV/$c$ is shown in the elastic process. Both the isovector $a_0$ and $a_2$ exchanges in charge exchange reactions play the respective roles in the low and high momentum region. Differential and total cross sections are presented to compare with existing data. Discussion is given to the polarization of $K^-p\to K^-p$ at $P_{Lab}=10$ GeV/$c$ and $K^-p\to \bar{K}^0n$ at $P_{Lab}=$ 8 GeV/$c$.

# A new combined analysis of elastic and charge exchange \(K N(\bar{K}N)\) scatterings in the Regge realm

## Key claim

The authors claim that the \(\rho(775)\) and \(\omega(782)\) meson exchanges, conventionally included in Regge models of kaon-nucleon scattering, can be excluded from the combined analysis of elastic and charge exchange channels because their decays to \(K\bar{K}\) are kinematically forbidden, making them highly off-shell and their coupling constants model-dependent. The paper presents a new scheme using only mesons with kinematically allowed \(K\bar{K}\) decays: \(a_0(980)\), \(\phi(1020)\), \(f_2(1275)\), and \(a_2(1320)\), together with the Pomeron, to describe all six channels simultaneously.

## Method

The authors reggeize relativistic Born amplitudes for \(t\)-channel meson exchanges, using coupling constants determined from the mesons' partial decay widths to \(K\bar{K}\) as listed by the Particle Data Group. For elastic \(K^\pm N \to K^\pm N\) scattering, the amplitude includes \(a_0(980)\), \(\phi(1020)\), \(f_2(1275)\), \(a_2(1320)\), and a Pomeron constructed from a quark-Pomeron coupling picture; the charge exchange reactions \(K^-p \to \bar{K}^0 n\) and \(K^+n \to K^0 p\) are described by only \(a_0(980)\) and \(a_2(1320)\) exchanges via isospin relations. The Pomeron trajectory is \(\alpha_\mathbb{P}(t) = 0.12 t + 1.06\), and the kaon form factor uses a cutoff parameter \(\mu = 2.5\) GeV with \(n=1\) for proton targets and \(n=3\) for neutron targets.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of elastic cross sections at \(P_{\rm Lab} = 250\) GeV/\(c\) gives \(\sigma_{el}(K^+p)/\sigma_{el}(\pi^+p) \approx 0.836\), yielding the quark-kaon coupling constant \(f_{Kqq} = 0.988\) (close to the estimated 0.82 from the ratio).
- The \(a_0(980)\) coupling constants are \(g_{a_0KK} = \pm 5.33\) (from \(\Gamma(a_0 \to K^+K^-) = 5.06\) MeV with threshold mass 988 MeV) and \(g_{a_0NN} = 15.5\).
- The \(\phi(1020)\) coupling constants are \(g_{\phi KK} = \pm 4.46\) (from \(\Gamma(\phi \to K^+K^-) = 2.1\) MeV) and \(g^v_{\phi NN} = -3.0\) with \(g^t_{\phi NN} = 0\).
- The tensor meson coupling constants are \(g_{f_2KK} = \pm 3.53\) (from \(\Gamma(f_2 \to K\bar{K}) = 4.29\) MeV), \(g_{a_2KK} = \pm 2.45\) (from \(\Gamma(a_2 \to K\bar{K}) = 2.69\) MeV), with \(g^{(1)}_{f_2NN} = 6.45\), \(g^{(1)}_{a_2NN} = 1.4\), and \(g^{(2)}_{f_2NN} = g^{(2)}_{a_2NN} = 0\).
- The Pomeron trajectory is \(\alpha_\mathbb{P}(t) = 0.12 t + 1.06\), with quark-Pomeron coupling strengths \(\beta_u = 2.07\) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \(\beta_s = 1.6\) GeV\(^{-1}\), and constituent quark mass \(m_q = 500\) MeV.
- The dominance of isoscalar \(f_2\) and Pomeron exchanges sets in beyond \(P_{\rm Lab} \approx 3\) GeV/\(c\) for elastic processes.
- Polarization data are shown for \(K^-p \to K^-p\) at \(P_{\rm Lab} = 10\) GeV/\(c\) and \(K^-p \to \bar{K}^0 n\) at \(P_{\rm Lab} = 8\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Regge model for \(\pi N\) scattering (Yu, Kong, and Pin, 2017) to \(KN\) and \(\bar{K}N\) channels, contradicting the conventional approach that includes \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) exchanges based on \(SU(3)\) symmetry (Phillips and Rarita, 1965; Butt et al., 1971; Nys et al., 2017; Bs Zou, 2006). The exclusion of \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) is justified by their kinematically forbidden decays to \(K\bar{K}\), making them highly off-shell compared to the on-shell Born amplitudes used for reggeization. The analysis enables future work incorporating partial wave contributions for the threshold region, including exotic channels in \(K^+N\) reactions and meson-baryon couplings in \(\bar{K}N\) channels, to complete the understanding of \(KN(\bar{K}N)\) scattering with the \(t\)-channel exchanges as a background contribution.
