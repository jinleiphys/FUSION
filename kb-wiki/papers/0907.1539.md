# The Triton:Low-momentum Interactions and Off-shell effects

> A microscopic theory of nuclei based on a 'free' scattering NN-potential is meaningful only if this potential fits on-shell scattering data.This is a necessary but not sufficient condition for the theory to be successful.It has been demonstrated repeatedly in the past that 2-body off-shell adjustments or many-body forces are necessary.It has been shown however, using Eff. Field Theory and formal scattering theory, that off-shell and many-body effects can not be separated.This 'equivalence theorem' allows us to concentrate on the off-shell effects.Examples of on-shell equivalent potentials Paris, Bonn etc but here separable potentials are calculated by inverse scattering from NN-scattering and Deuteron data, Earlier calculations showed these S-state potentials to agree with Bonn-B results in Brueckner nuclear matter calculations. They are here also used to compute the Triton binding energy and the n-D scattering length.The results are found to lie on the Phillips line defined in early calculations but like these miss the experimental point on this line and overbind the Triton but is reached by modifying the off-shell properties adding a short-range repulsion without affecting fits to the experimental low-energy phase-shifts.The off-shell induced correlations result in a repulsive component in the Triton effective interactions.In nuclear matter the same effect is referred to as the dispersion correction, which is a main contributor to nuclear saturation.In finite nucleus Brueckner-HF calculations these same correlations give an important contribution to the selfconsistent (reaarangement term), without which the finite nucleus would collapse.The main purpose of the present work is to illustrate that NN-correlations are as important in the Triton as they are in nuclear matter or other finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two-body off-shell effects and three-body forces are indistinguishable in many-body systems (the "equivalence theorem"), and that modifying the off-shell properties of the NN interaction by adding short-range repulsion can shift the calculated Triton binding energy and n-D scattering length along the Phillips line toward the experimental point, without affecting fits to low-energy phase-shifts.

## Method

Separable potentials were constructed by inverse scattering from NN-scattering phase-shifts (Arndt phases for channels with J ≤ 5) and Deuteron data (Bonn-A, B, or C parameters). The Triton binding energy and n-D scattering length were then computed by solving Faddeev equations with these potentials. Short-range repulsion was incorporated by using rank-2 separable potentials with repulsive phase-shifts parameterized by a range parameter r_c, allowing modification of off-shell properties while preserving fits to experimental low-energy phase-shifts.

## Key numbers

- The experimental Triton binding energy is 8.48 MeV.
- For nuclear matter at k_f = 1.35 fm⁻¹, the separable potential contributions to energy per particle from the ¹S₀ state were -16.57 MeV (Bonn-B gave -16.66 MeV) and from the ³S₁ state were -21.33 MeV (Bonn-B gave -21.34 MeV).
- For the ³D₁ state, there was a 0.55 MeV difference in binding contribution between separable and Bonn potentials.
- The ³P₁ state with separable potentials gave 1.48 MeV more repulsive binding than Bonn.
- With short-range repulsion parameter r_c = 0.8 fm, the calculated point on the Phillips line came very close to the experimental point.
- The contribution to nuclear matter binding in ¹S₀ and ³S₁-³D₁ states at k_f = 1.35 fm⁻¹ varied from 36.22 MeV/A (r_c = 0) to 31.79 MeV/A (r_c = 0.8 fm).

## Context

This work extends earlier Brueckner nuclear matter calculations with separable potentials (Kohler 1995) and connects to the Effective Field Theory approach initiated by Weinberg (1990) and implemented by van Kolck (2001), which established that off-shell and many-body effects cannot be separated. The results lie on the Phillips line defined by Phillips (1968) and fitted by Harms (1972), consistent with earlier separable potential calculations. The paper shows that NN correlations are as important in the Triton as in nuclear matter, where they contribute the dispersion correction essential for saturation (Moszkowski 1960), and in finite nuclei where they provide the rearrangement term without which the nucleus would collapse. The work enables future calculations starting from QCD or EFT methods rather than phenomenological approaches.
