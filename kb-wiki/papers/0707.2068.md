# Effective potentials for heavy quarks above deconfinement

> Using methods previously developed by Kelbg and others for creating effective potentials for electron-ion plasmas, we investigate quarkonium potentials above deconfinement. Using results for the internal energy of a static quark-antiquark pair, a set of effective potentials are constructed that take into account quantum effects and symmetry conditions. Bound state effects are explicitly included in order to account for the strongly coupled nature of plasmas. It is hoped that these effective potentials will be useful in simulations of heavy quarks or plasma quasiparticles when the dynamics is treated classically.

# Effective potentials for heavy quarks above deconfinement

## Key claim

The authors construct classical effective potentials for heavy quark-antiquark pairs above the deconfinement temperature that incorporate quantum-mechanical effects and symmetry conditions, but the applicability is limited to regimes where the coupling parameter Gamma is sufficiently small and the linearized approximation for the Bloch equation is valid.

## Method

The authors apply the Kelbg method, originally developed for electron-ion plasmas, to lattice QCD results for the internal energy of static quark-antiquark pairs. They solve the Bloch equation for the two-particle density matrix in the linearized approximation to obtain an effective potential u1(r), then explicitly include bound state contributions using the Brillouin-Planck-Larkin (BPL) convention. The internal energy from lattice data is parameterized with a screened Cornell-type form with temperature-dependent coupling, Debye mass, and string tension, and the effective potentials are computed for charmonium and bottomonium systems.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass parameterization: mu(T) = 0.0675 (T/T_c) - (0.196 T/T_c)^5, where T_c = 0.27 GeV
- String tension parameterization: sigma(T)/(GeV^2) = 0.32 (T_c/T) + 0.8 (T_c/T)^16 + 0.005
- Coupling constant: alpha(T) = [2.4 ln(2T/T_c)]^(-1)
- Fit parameters: a = 5.5, b = 0.37 GeV
- Temperature range of the lattice parameterization: 1.1 - 2 T_c
- At T = 1.2 T_c, including the lowest bound state lowers the effective potential by about 5% for charmonium and about 15% for bottomonium
- The effective potential reaches a finite value at r=0, with the modification occurring for distances r < 1 fm

## Context

This work extends the Kelbg method (Kelbg, 1963) for constructing effective quantum potentials from electron-ion plasmas to the quark-gluon plasma regime, building on lattice calculations of the Q\bar{Q} free energy by Kaczmarek (2004) and internal energy by Kaczmarek (2005). The effective potentials are intended for use in classical molecular dynamics simulations of the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma, as previously pursued by the authors in MD1, MD2, MD3, and for Langevin simulations of charmonium in the sQGP. The paper notes an unresolved debate between Shuryak and Zahed (2004), who argue for using internal energy as the interaction potential, and Mocsy and Petreczky (2005), who find that free energy better describes lattice spectral functions.
