# Mean-field theory of baryonic matter for QCD in the large $N_{c}$ and heavy quark mass limits

> We discuss theoretical issues pertaining to baryonic matter in the combined heavy-quark and large $N_c$ limits of QCD. Witten's classic argument that baryons and interacting systems of baryons can be described in a mean-field approximation with each of the quarks moving in an average potential due to the remaining quarks is heuristic. It is important to justify this heuristic description for the case of baryonic matter since systems of interacting baryons are intrinsically more complicated than single baryons due to the possibility of hidden color states---states in which the subsystems making up the entire baryon crystal are not color-singlet nucleons but rather colorful states coupled together to make a color-singlet state. In this work, we provide a formal justification of this heuristic prescription. In order to do this, we start by taking the heavy quark limit, thus effectively reducing the problem to a many-body quantum mechanical system. This problem can be formulated in terms of integrals over coherent states, which for this problem are simple Slater determinants. We show that for the many-body problem, the support region for these integrals becomes narrow at large $N_c$, yielding an energy which is well-approximated by a single coherent state---that is a mean-field description. Corrections to the energy are of relative order $1/N_c$. While hidden color states are present in the exact state of the heavy quark system, they only influence the interaction energy at sub-leading order in $1/N_{c}$.

## Key claim

The paper provides a formal justification that the mean-field (single Slater determinant) approximation for baryonic matter in the combined heavy-quark and large \(N_c\) limits of QCD yields the ground-state energy to leading order in \(1/N_c\), despite the presence of hidden color states in the exact wave function; the corrections to the energy are of relative order \(1/N_c\), and hidden color states only influence the interaction energy at sub-leading order.

## Method

The authors take the heavy quark limit at the outset, reducing QCD to a nonrelativistic many-body quantum mechanical system with a color-Coulomb interaction. They formulate the problem using generalized coherent states (single Slater determinants) and exploit the overcompleteness of this basis to write any color-singlet state as an integral over coherent states. By analyzing the overlap between coherent states, which falls off exponentially with \(N_c\) as the difference in their parameters increases, they show that the support region for the integral representing the exact ground state becomes narrow at large \(N_c\), so the energy is well-approximated by a single coherent state.

## Key numbers

- Corrections to the mean-field energy are of relative order \(1/N_c\).
- The overlap between two distinct color-singlet coherent states is a number with norm less than unity raised to the power of \(N_c\), causing exponential narrowing in parameter space as \(N_c\) becomes large.
- The one-body operators in the Hamiltonian have strength of order \(N_c^0\) and the two-body operators have strength of order \(1/N_c\).

## Context

This work extends Witten's (1979) heuristic mean-field argument for single baryons to the case of baryonic matter, which is complicated by the possibility of hidden color states. It directly addresses a concern raised in a recent quantitative study of large \(N_c\) baryonic matter in 3+1 dimensions (Adhikari and Cohen, 2013), which noted that the mean-field ansatz is a poor approximation to the exact wave function but argued the energy might still be accurate. The paper also builds on a 1+1-dimensional analysis (Adhikari and Cohen, 2013) that showed agreement between mean-field and exact numerical calculations. The formalism enables a rigorous justification that hidden color states do not affect the leading-order interaction energy.
