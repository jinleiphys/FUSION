# Improvement in a phenomenological formula for ground state binding energies

> The phenomenological formula for ground state binding energy derived earlier (International Journal of Modern Physics E {\bf 20} (2011) 179) has been modified. The parameters have been obtained by fitting the latest available tabulation of experimental values. The major modifications include a new term for pairing and introduction of a new neutron magic number at $N=160$. The new formula reduced the root mean square deviation to 363 keV, a substantial improvement over the previous version of the formula.

## Key claim

The modified phenomenological formula reduces the root mean square deviation in ground state binding energies to 363 keV for 2353 nuclei, a substantial improvement over the previous version's 376 keV for 2140 nuclei, but this result depends on introducing a new neutron magic number at N=160 and a new pairing term, with the N=160 parameter fitted from alpha decay energies rather than binding energy data.

## Method

The author modified a previous phenomenological formula (Gangopadhyay, 2011) based on the charged liquid drop model by incorporating a new pairing term from Mendoza-Temis et al. and adding a neutron magic number at N=160. The 52 parameters were obtained through a least squares fit to the experimental binding energies of 2353 nuclei with Z ≥ 8 and N ≥ 8 from the AME2012 compilation, with the epsilon parameter for N=160 determined separately from alpha decay energies of superheavy nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Root mean square deviation of 363 keV for 2353 nuclei with Z ≥ 8 and N ≥ 8, compared to 376 keV for 2140 nuclei in the previous version (Gangopadhyay, 2011)
- Only 36 nuclei show error greater than 1 MeV, and only one nucleus shows error greater than 1.5 MeV (compared to 39 nuclei with >1 MeV error and 7 with >1.5 MeV error in the previous version)
- Introducing the N=160 magic number decreased the rms deviation from 369 keV to 363 keV for AME2012 data
- Root mean square error of 299 keV for 1175 alpha decay energies after introducing the N=160 parameter
- The particle-hole pairing term decreased the rms deviation by 2 keV

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological binding energy formula of Gangopadhyay (2011) by incorporating the pairing prescription of Mendoza-Temis et al. and fitting to the updated AME2012 mass compilation. The formula achieves competitive accuracy (363 keV rms) compared to other approaches such as the Duflo-Zuker mass formulas (374 keV with 33 parameters, as reported by Qi), the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method of Goriely et al. (580 keV), and the microscopic-macroscopic approach of Wang et al. (516 keV), while requiring no input beyond nucleon numbers. The introduction of N=160 as a new neutron magic number enables improved predictions for superheavy nuclei and alpha decay energies above mass 270.
