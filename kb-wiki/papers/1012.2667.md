# Quark Number Susceptibility with Finite Quark Mass in Holographic QCD

> We study the effect of a finite quark mass on the quark number susceptibility in the framework of holographic QCD. We work in a bottom-up model with a deformed AdS black hole and D3/D7 model to calculate the quark number susceptibility at finite temperature with/without a finite quark chemical potential. As expected the finite quark mass suppresses the quark number susceptibility. We find that at high temperatures $T\ge 600$ MeV the quark number susceptibility of light quarks and heavy quarks are almost equal in the bottom-up model. This indicates that the heavy quark like charm contribution to thermodynamics of a QCD-like system may start to become significant at temperatures $T\sim 600$ MeV. In D3/D7 model, we focus on the competition between the quark chemical potential, which enhances the quark number susceptibility, and the quark mass that suppresses the susceptibility. We observe that depending on the relative values of the quark mass and the quark chemical potential, the quark number susceptibility shows a diverging or converging behavior. We also calculate the chiral susceptibility in D3/D7 model to support the observation made with the quark number susceptibility.

## Key claim

The finite quark mass suppresses the quark number susceptibility in holographic QCD models, and at high temperatures (T ≥ 600 MeV) the susceptibilities of light and heavy quarks become nearly equal in the bottom-up model, suggesting charm quarks may contribute significantly to QCD thermodynamics at those temperatures. The authors note that the quark mass in their model can differ from that in QCD by a constant factor (e.g., a factor of √3 from the hard wall model), and that results for temperatures below the deconfinement transition are illustrative only.

## Method

The authors calculate the quark number susceptibility using two holographic approaches: a bottom-up model with a deformed AdS black hole background that includes back-reaction from finite quark mass, and a D3/D7 probe brane model in a D3-brane black hole geometry. In the bottom-up model, they compute the susceptibility via both hydrodynamic analysis of the retarded Green function and thermodynamic relations from the grand potential. In the D3/D7 model, they solve the DBI action numerically for the brane embedding and gauge field, then extract the susceptibility from the quark number density obtained via the grand potential.

## Key numbers

- At temperatures T ≥ 600 MeV, the quark number susceptibility of light quarks and heavy quarks are almost equal in the bottom-up model
- The quark mass parameter M_q is varied from 100 to 2000 MeV in the bottom-up model for illustration
- The deconfinement temperature in the hard wall model (used as reference) is shown as a vertical dashed line in the bottom-up model results
- In the D3/D7 model, the ratio a = μ̃/m = μ/(√2 M_q) controls the behavior, with a ≪ 1 (M_q ≫ μ/√2) suppressing susceptibility and a > 1 showing diverging behavior
- The quark number density in D3/D7 model is given by n_q = (1/4)√(λ/2) N_c N_f T³ d̃
- The quark condensate in D3/D7 model is ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩ = -(1/8)√λ N_c N_f T³ c

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of quark number susceptibility at finite temperature and chemical potential (Kim et al. 2009, Kim et al. 2010, Son and Stephanov 2008) from massless to massive quarks, including light, strange, and charm quarks. The authors compare their finding that charm contributions become significant at T ∼ 600 MeV with lattice QCD results (Petreczky et al. 2010) showing charm fluctuations comparable to light quarks at T ∼ 800 MeV, and with perturbative QCD claims (Laine and Schroeder 2006) that charm plays a role at T ∼ 350 MeV. The D3/D7 model analysis reveals multiple points where susceptibility diverges, which the authors note as a limitation for addressing the single critical end point in the QCD phase diagram.
