# Robust signatures of solar neutrino oscillation solutions

> With the goal of identifying signatures that select specific neutrino oscillation parameters, we test the robustness of global oscillation solutions that fit all the available solar and reactor experimental data. We use three global analysis strategies previously applied by different authors and also determine the sensitivity of the oscillation solutions to the critical nuclear fusion cross section, S_{17}(0), for the production of 8B. The favored solutions are LMA, LOW, and VAC in order of g.o.f. The neutral current to charged current ratio for SNO is predicted to be 3.5 +- 0.6 (1 sigma), which is separated from the no-oscillation value of 1.0 by much more than the expected experimental error. The predicted range of the day-night difference in charged current rates is (8.2 +- 5.2)% and is strongly correlated with the day-night effect for neutrino-electron scattering. A measurement by SNO of either a NC to CC ratio > 3.3 or a day-night difference > 10%, would favor a small region of the currently allowed LMA neutrino parameter space. The global oscillation solutions predict a 7Be neutrino-electron scattering rate in BOREXINO and KamLAND in the range 0.66 +- 0.04 of the BP00 standard solar model rate, a prediction which can be used to test both the solar model and the neutrino oscillation theory. Only the LOW solution predicts a large day-night effect(< 42%) in BOREXINO and KamLAND. For the KamLAND reactor experiment, the LMA solution predicts 0.44 of the standard model rate; we evaluate 1 sigma and 3 sigma uncertainties and the first and second moments of the energy spectrum.

## Key claim

The global oscillation solutions that fit all available solar and reactor data are robust to different analysis strategies, but the specific allowed parameter regions and the ranking of favored solutions (LMA, LOW, VAC in order of goodness-of-fit) depend on the adopted value of the critical nuclear fusion cross section S17(0) for 8B production and on the analysis prescription used.

## Method

The authors computed allowed regions in neutrino oscillation parameter space (Δm² and tan²θ) using three different global analysis strategies previously applied in the literature, incorporating all available solar neutrino and reactor oscillation data including the SNO charged current measurement, Super-Kamiokande day and night energy spectra, and the Chooz reactor limit. They used the BP00 standard solar model fluxes and uncertainties, comparing results obtained with the new precise measurement of S17(0) by Junghans et al. (22.3 ± 0.9 eVb) against the previous standard value (19 +4 -2 eVb), and predicted measurable quantities for SNO, BOREXINO, and KamLAND experiments.

## Key numbers

- S17(0) = (22.3 ± 0.7 (expt) ± 0.5 (theor)) eVb from Junghans et al. measurement
- Predicted NC to CC ratio for SNO: 3.45 +0.70 -0.54 (1σ), compared to no-oscillation value of 1.0
- Predicted day-night difference in CC rates: 8.3 +5.0 -5.6 (1σ)%
- Predicted 7Be neutrino-electron scattering rate in BOREXINO and KamLAND: 0.65 +0.04 -0.03 (1σ) of the BP00 standard solar model rate
- For KamLAND reactor experiment, LMA solution predicts charged current rate relative to standard model: 0.44 +0.22 -0.07 (1σ) with Ethreshold = 1.22 MeV
- 3σ upper limits on Δm² (in units of 10⁻⁴ eV²): case a: 4.7, case b: 3.0, case c: 6.5
- Best-fit LMA parameters: Δm² = 3.7 × 10⁻⁵ eV², tan²θ = 3.7 × 10⁻¹, χ²_min = 34.5, g.o.f. = 67%
- Best-fit LOW parameters: Δm² = 1.0 × 10⁻⁷ eV², tan²θ = 6.7 × 10⁻¹, χ²_min = 40.8, g.o.f. = 39%
- Best-fit VAC parameters: Δm² = 4.6 × 10⁻¹⁰ eV², tan²θ = 2.5 × 10⁰, χ²_min = 42.3, g.o.f. = 33%
- Inferred 8B neutrino flux f_B = 0.88 ± 0.48 (free 8B analysis) or f_B = 0.86 ± 0.38 (standard analysis) in units of BP00 + New 8B flux
- Only LOW solution predicts large day-night effect (≤ 42%, 3σ) in BOREXINO and KamLAND

## Context

This work extends the global oscillation analyses previously performed by the same authors (Bahcall, Gonzalez-Garcia, Pena-Garay 2001) and by other groups including Krastev and Smirnov (2001), the Bari group (Fogli et al. 2001), and Barger et al. (2001), by systematically testing the robustness of predictions to three different analysis strategies and to the updated S17(0) measurement. The paper provides quantitative predictions for upcoming SNO neutral current measurements, BOREXINO and KamLAND solar neutrino observations, and the KamLAND reactor experiment, enabling experimental discrimination between the LMA, LOW, and VAC oscillation solutions. The reduction in allowed parameter space from the new S17(0) value eliminates the previously marginal SMA and Just-So² solutions at 3σ.
