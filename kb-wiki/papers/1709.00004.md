# $Δv=2$ seniority changing transitions in yrast $3^-$ states and B(E3) systematics of Sn isotopes

> We show for the first time that the generalized seniority scheme explains reasonably well the $B(E3)$ systematics for the $(0^+ \rightarrow 3_1^-)$ transitions in the Sn-isotopes, which are odd-tensor $E3$ transitions connecting different seniority states ($Δv = 2$). Additionally, we also present Large Scale Shell Model (LSSM) calculations to support our interpretation. The generalized seniority scheme points to the octupole character of these $3^-$ states in Sn isotopes.

## Key claim
The generalized seniority scheme, previously applied to same-seniority (Δv=0) transitions, can also explain the B(E3) systematics for the (0⁺ → 3₁⁻) transitions in Sn isotopes as seniority-changing (Δv=2) odd-tensor transitions, with the caveat that the agreement depends on choosing a valence space of d₅/₂ ⊗ h₁₁/₂ (Ω=9) rather than including additional orbitals.

## Method
The authors used the generalized seniority formalism for multi-j degenerate orbits to calculate B(E3↑) values for 0⁺ → 3₁⁻ transitions in Sn isotopes, applying a seniority reduction formula that expresses the n-particle matrix elements in terms of v-particle matrix elements. They fitted the experimental B(E3) values for ¹¹⁶Sn and ¹¹⁸Sn as midpoints for two valence spaces: Ω=9 (d₅/₂ ⊗ h₁₁/₂) and Ω=11 (d₅/₂ ⊗ d₃/₂ ⊗ h₁₁/₂), with ¹⁰⁸Sn as the core. Large Scale Shell Model (LSSM) calculations were also performed using the Nushell code with SN100PN interaction and a truncated valence space, with some calculations testing a shifted single-particle energy for the s₁/₂ orbit.

## Key numbers
- The B(E3) systematics show a peak at ¹¹⁶Sn in the experimental data, with the generalized seniority calculations using Ω=9 (d₅/₂ ⊗ h₁₁/₂) in better agreement than Ω=11.
- The LSSM calculations with original single-particle energies produce a sharp peak at ¹¹⁴Sn, which deviates from the experimental trend; shifting the s₁/₂ orbit single-particle energy by 1.3 MeV to -9.9944 MeV resolves this kink.
- An effective charge value of 1.6 was used in the LSSM calculations.
- Full space LSSM calculations for ¹²⁴Sn reproduce the observed B(E3) value very well, with the triangle and circle symbols almost superposed on the measured value.

## Context
This work extends the generalized seniority scheme previously applied by Maheshwari et al. to same-seniority (Δv=0) odd-tensor E1 transitions in Sn isotopes, now showing it works for seniority-changing (Δv=2) odd-tensor E3 transitions. It contrasts with earlier theoretical work by Ansari and Ring (2000) using relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (RQRPA), which obtained the qualitative trend but with values differing from experiment, and with earlier measurements by Jonsson et al. (1981). The results support the octupole vibrational character of the 3⁻ states and suggest the generalized seniority scheme can be used to estimate transition probabilities where measurements are not yet available.
