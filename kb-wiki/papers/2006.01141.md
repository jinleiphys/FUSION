# Unified weak/strong coupling framework for nuclear matter and neutron stars

> Ab initio methods using weakly interacting nucleons give a good description of condensed nuclear matter up to densities comparable to the nuclear saturation density. At higher densities palpable strong interactions between overlapping nucleons become important; we propose that the interactions will continuously switch over to follow a holographic model in this region. In order to implement this, we construct hybrid equations of state (EoSs) where various models are used for low density nuclear matter, and the holographic V-QCD model is used for non-perturbative high density nuclear matter as well as for quark matter. We carefully examine all existing constraints from astrophysics of compact stars and discuss their implications for the hybrid EoSs. Thanks to the stiffness of the V-QCD EoS for nuclear matter, we obtain a large family of viable hybrid EoSs passing the constraints. We find that quark matter cores in neutron stars are unstable due to the strongly first order deconfinement transition, and predict bounds on the tidal deformability as well as on the radius of neutron stars. By relying on universal relations, we also constrain characteristic peak frequencies of gravitational waves produced in neutron star mergers.

## Key claim
The authors propose that at densities above nuclear saturation, the interactions between nucleons continuously switch over from a weakly coupled description to a holographic model (V-QCD), but the matching between the two regimes is not a physical crossover; instead it is enforced as a second-order phase transition by tuning two free parameters of the holographic model, and the resulting hybrid equations of state are only viable if the matching density lies in a specific range determined by astrophysical constraints.

## Method
The authors construct hybrid equations of state by combining low-density nuclear matter models (HLPS, APR, SLy, IUF, DD2) with the holographic V-QCD model for high-density nuclear matter and quark matter. The matching between the two sectors is performed at a chosen transition density \(n_\mathrm{tr}\) by requiring continuity of pressure and its first derivative with respect to chemical potential, which fixes two free parameters of the holographic model (the normalization \(c_b\) and the Chern-Simons parameter \(b\)). The resulting EoSs are then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for nonrotating neutron stars, and the predictions are compared to astrophysical constraints from neutron star mass measurements and the tidal deformability bound from GW170817.

## Key numbers
- The matching density \(n_\mathrm{tr}\) is varied in the range \(1.2 n_s \le n_\mathrm{tr} \le 2.6 n_s\), where \(n_s = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) is the nuclear saturation density.
- The LIGO/Virgo bound on tidal deformability is \(580 \geq \Lambda(1.4 M_\odot) \geq 70\) (GW170817).
- The maximum mass constraint is \(M \geq 2 M_\odot\).
- All hybrid EoSs with \(n_\mathrm{tr}/n_s < 1.4\) are excluded by the tidal deformability bound.
- For the HLPSs nuclear matter model combined with potential 5b, the allowed window is \(1.4 < n_\mathrm{tr}/n_s < 1.6\); with potential 8b, the allowed window is \(1.5 < n_\mathrm{tr}/n_s < 2.1\).

## Context
This work extends the hybrid EoS construction of Ecker (2019) by systematically exploring the parameter space of nuclear matter models and V-QCD potentials, and by applying the full set of astrophysical constraints from mass measurements (Antoniadis 2013, Cromartie 2019) and the tidal deformability bound from GW170817 (Abbott 2018). The approach is similar in spirit to the regulated analytic interpolations of Annala (2019), but uses physically motivated models rather than polytropic fits. The results enable predictions for neutron star radii, tidal deformabilities, and gravitational wave peak frequencies that can be tested by future NICER and LIGO/Virgo observations.
