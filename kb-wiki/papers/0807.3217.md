# New Narrow Nucleon N*(1685)

> We argue that the existence of a new narrow (Gamma < 25 MeV) nucleon resonance N*(1685) is strongly supported by recent data on eta photoproduction off the nucleon. The resonance has much stronger photo-coupling to the neutron than to the proton. This nucleon resonance is a good candidate for the non-strange member of the exotic anti-decouplet of baryons -- the partner of the pentaquark Theta+. All up to date known properties of new N*(1685) are summarized.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a new narrow nucleon resonance N*(1685) with width ≤ 25 MeV is strongly supported by recent data on eta photoproduction off the nucleon, but the claim is hedged by noting that alternative explanations involving known resonances (S11(1650) and P11(1710) or S11(1535) and S11(1650)) can also reproduce the neutron anomaly, and that the definitive test requires observation of narrow structures in proton-channel observables.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed GRAAL data on the Σ beam asymmetry for eta photoproduction off the free proton using narrow energy bins (Eγ = 0.85–1.15 GeV, corresponding to W = 1.55–1.75 GeV). They compared the data to the SAID partial-wave analysis (E429 solution) as a smooth background model, then added Breit-Wigner resonances with various quantum numbers (S11, P11, P13, D13) and varied mass, width, photocouplings, and phase to minimize χ². The neutron photocoupling was estimated from quasi-free cross-section data from GRAAL and CBELSA/TAPS.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the candidate resonance: M = 1.685 ± 0.005 ± 0.007 GeV
- Upper limit on width: Γ ≤ 25 MeV
- Best-fit widths for specific quantum numbers: Γ ∼ 8 MeV for P13 and D13, Γ ∼ 19 MeV for P11
- χ²/dof values for resonance fits: 56/22 for P11, 25/20 for P13, 39/20 for D13
- Proton photocoupling for P11: √(Br_ηN) A^p_{1/2} ∼ 1 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²
- Neutron photocoupling: √(Br_ηN) A^n_{1/2} ∼ 15 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/² (with possible theoretical errors up to a factor of two)
- Ratio of neutron to proton photocoupling strength: Γ(n* → nγ) / Γ(p* → pγ) ∼ 50–250
- Upper limit on πN branching: Br_πN ≤ 5%
- Mixing angles between N*(1685) and ground state nucleon, P11(1440), and P11(1710): |θ₁,₂,₃| ≤ 12°

## Context

This work extends the 2004 observation by Kuznetsov et al. of a narrow peak in quasi-free neutron cross sections and ηn invariant mass spectra at GRAAL, which was subsequently confirmed by CBELSA/TAPS and LNS-Sendai. The resonance is proposed as the non-strange member of the exotic anti-decouplet of baryons predicted by the Chiral Quark-Soliton Model (Diakonov et al., 1997), making it a partner of the pentaquark Θ⁺. Alternative explanations by Shklyar et al. (2005) and Anisovich et al. (2005) attribute the neutron anomaly to known resonances with tuned neutron photocouplings, but the present analysis uses proton beam asymmetry data to discriminate between these interpretations. The results provide circumstantial evidence for the existence of the Θ⁺ baryon, which has been reported by LEPS and DIANA but remains controversial due to negative results from CLAS.
