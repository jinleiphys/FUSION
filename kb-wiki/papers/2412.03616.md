# Giving wake to energy-energy correlators: Hydrodynamic response on the celestial sphere

> The observation of the medium response generated by the propagation of high energy partons in the quark gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions would provide a clear and unmistakable evidence for the hydrodynamic behavior of the bulk. Recently, it has been argued that the features of the medium's back-reaction to the jet could be cleanly imprinted in the correlations of asymptotic energy flows, in principle allowing to isolate this signal from other uncorrelated physical processes. Nonetheless, the current limited theoretical understanding of these jet observables in heavy-ion collisions constrains their applicability as probes of the medium (hydro)dynamics. In this work, we provide an analytic picture for the medium back-reaction's effect on the energy flux and two point energy correlator. We show that the medium response leads to the emergence of an universal classical scaling law, competing with the perturbative QCD contribution at large angles. Comparing the associated correlator to recent experimental measurements, we find that the observed large angle features can be qualitatively described by a purely hydrodynamically driven response and its interplay with the hard jet component.

## Key claim
The medium back-reaction to a jet in quark-gluon plasma produces a universal classical scaling law in the two-point energy correlator (EEC), \(d\Sigma_{\rm response}^{(2)} \propto d\cos\theta\), which competes with the perturbative QCD contribution at large angles. The authors show that the observed large-angle features in experimental EEC data can be qualitatively described by a purely hydrodynamically driven response and its interplay with the hard jet component, but they caution that further theoretical developments are needed to separate medium response effects from those of the perturbative jet cascade.

## Method
The authors linearize relativistic hydrodynamics around a static, homogeneous, spherical medium, treating the jet as an external source of energy and momentum. They solve for the time-integrated energy-momentum tensor perturbation using a classical current that models the leading parton's energy loss (via a simplified gluon distribution kernel) and transverse momentum exchange (as a stochastic Gaussian process). The resulting energy flux on the celestial sphere is then used to compute the soft-soft contribution to the EEC, and the full EEC is constructed by adding a leading-order vacuum-like perturbative term and a hard-soft correlation term.

## Key numbers
* The characteristic distance scale of the model is \(r \sim 1/a_\tau \simeq 47\ \text{fm}\) for parameters \(p_t = 200\ \text{GeV}\), \(x_0 = 0.001 = T/p_t\), \(g=2\), \(\hat{q} = 0.2\ \text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}\), and \(\Gamma_\eta = 1/(4\pi T)\).
* The steady (energy-inflow) contribution to the small-angle EEC coefficient scales as \(c_1 \sim (r^2/\Gamma_\eta^2) \cdot (\hat{q} r^2 / p_t)\).
* The stochastic (transverse-momentum) contribution scales as \(c_{1\perp} \sim (r^2/\Gamma_\eta^2) \cdot (\hat{q} r / p_t^2)\), which is suppressed relative to the steady part by a factor of \(p_t r\).
* The full EEC distribution is compared to CMS data for jets with \(120\ \text{GeV} < p_t < 140\ \text{GeV}\), jet radius \(R=0.4\), and most central collisions.

## Context
This work extends analytic studies of medium response in heavy-ion collisions, building on the linearized hydrodynamic framework of Casalderrey-Solana (2004) and Neufeld (2008, 2009). It provides an analytic complement to numerical studies such as the Hybrid model of Casalderrey-Solana (2014, 2015, 2016) and to recent EEC-focused work by Bossi (2024) and Yang (2023). The results enable a direct, qualitative comparison to CMS EEC data (CMS 2024) and suggest that the large-angle enhancement in the PbPb-to-pp ratio can be interpreted as a hydrodynamic signature, though separating it from perturbative jet-medium modifications requires further study.
