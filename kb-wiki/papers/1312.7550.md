# LIFE ON EARTH -- AN ACCIDENT? Chiral Symmetry and the Anthropic Principle

> to appear in the proceedings of The Seventh International Symposium on Chiral Symmetry in Hadrons and Nuclei, Beihang Univ., China, Oct. 2013, 8 pp, 4 figs, uses ws-procs9x6.cls

# LIFE ON EARTH -- AN ACCIDENT? Chiral Symmetry and the Anthropic Principle

## Key claim
The fine-tuning of nuclear forces for carbon and oxygen production is real but not extreme: variations in the average light quark mass of up to 2–3% and in the fine-structure constant of about 2.5% still allow carbon-oxygen based life, though beyond these ranges the anthropic principle appears necessary to explain observed elemental abundances.

## Method
The author uses chiral effective field theory (EFT) to express the quark-mass dependence of nuclear forces, then computes the response of Big Bang nucleosynthesis abundances and the triple-alpha process energy differences to variations in the light quark mass and the fine-structure constant. Nuclear lattice simulations with Auxiliary Field Quantum Monte Carlo techniques provide the derivatives of binding energies with respect to the pion mass, while the quark-mass dependence of hadron properties is taken from lattice QCD combined with chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers
- Quark mass K-factors from lattice QCD+ChPT: $K_{M_\pi}^q = 0.494^{+0.009}_{-0.013}$, $K_{F_\pi}^q = 0.048\pm 0.012$, $K_{m_N}^q = 0.048^{+0.002}_{-0.006}$
- NN scattering length K-factors: $K^q_{1S0} = 2.3^{+1.9}_{-1.8}$, $K^q_{3S1} = 0.32^{+0.17}_{-0.18}$, $K^q_{\rm BE(deut)} = -0.86^{+0.45}_{-0.50}$
- BBN constraint on light quark mass variation: $\delta m_q/m_q = (2\pm 4)\%$
- Higgs vacuum expectation value constraint from neutron lifetime: $|\delta v/v| = |\delta m_q/m_q| \leq 0.9\%$
- Triple-alpha process energy $\varepsilon = 379.47(18)$ keV; stellar modeling allows $\pm 100$ keV envelope
- Radiative width of Hoyle state: $\Gamma_\gamma = 3.7(5)$ meV
- Allowed quark mass variation for carbon-oxygen life: up to 2–3%
- Allowed variation in fine-structure constant $\alpha_{\rm EM}$: $\simeq 2.5\%$

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of fine-tuning in nuclear physics by Hoyle (1954), who identified the need for the 7.65 MeV excited state in $^{12}$C, and by Oberhummer and collaborators (2000) who estimated the $\pm 100$ keV tolerance in the triple-alpha energy. The author uses modern chiral EFT and lattice methods to replace earlier model-dependent estimates (Muther 1987, Beane 2002, Epelbaum 2002, Flambaum 2007, Soto 2011) with controlled uncertainties. The results enable sharper tests of the anthropic principle as discussed by Carter (1974), Carr (1979), and Weinberg (1989), and point to needed improvements in lattice QCD at physical quark masses and in nuclear lattice simulations of reactions.
