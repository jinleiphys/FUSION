# Time-dependent Dirac equation applied to one-proton radioactive emission

> Relativistic energy-density functional (REDF) theory has been developed and utilized for self-consistent meanfield calculations of atomic nuclei. The proton-emitting radioactivity can provide a suitable reference to improve the predicting ability of REDF especially on the proton-drip line. One needs to consider the quantum tunneling effect, which plays an essential role in nucleon-emitting radioactive processes. However, the relativistic quantum tunneling has been less investigated compared with the non-relativistic case. This work is devoted to a theoretical evaluation of one-proton ($1p$) radioactivity based on the relativistic Dirac formalism. For this purpose, I develop the time-dependent (TD) Dirac-spinor calculation to simulate the $1p$ emission. By utilizing the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculation with the DD-PCX parameters, single-proton potentials for the time-dependent Dirac spinor are determined. The TD-Dirac calculation is applied to the $1p$ emissions from the $^{37}$Sc and $^{39}$Sc nuclei, which can be well approximated as the valence proton and the proton-close-shell cores. The sensitivity of $1p$-emission energy and decaying width to the mass number is demonstrated. Remarkable sensitivity exists due to the size of system, which affects the nuclear part of potentials and energy levels, whereas the Coulomb barrier is common with the same atomic number. The calculated $1p$ energy and decaying lifetime are roughly consistent to the experimental limitation. The present TD-Dirac calculation is expected as applicable widely to proton-rich nuclides in order to improve the REDF by utilizing the $1p$-emission data.

## Key claim

The time-dependent Dirac equation can successfully simulate one-proton radioactive emission from proton-rich nuclei, with the calculated Q-values and lifetimes roughly consistent with experimental limitations, though the method's accuracy depends on the mass-dependent sensitivity of the nuclear potential while the Coulomb barrier remains common for nuclei with the same atomic number.

## Method

The author develops a time-dependent Dirac-spinor calculation to simulate one-proton emission, where single-proton scalar and vector potentials are first determined from relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using the DD-PCX parameter set with the no-sea approximation. The initial state is prepared using a confining Hamiltonian that localizes the valence proton inside the potential barrier, then the time evolution is computed by expanding the initial state on eigenstates of the original Dirac Hamiltonian within a finite box of 300 fm. The survival probability is extracted from the time-dependent wavefunction to obtain the decaying width, with results cross-checked against stabilization and complex-scaling methods.

## Key numbers

- For $^{37}$Sc ($^{36}$Ca$+p$): the TD-Dirac calculation yields $Q_p = 2.864$ MeV and $\Gamma_p = 3.74 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV (lifetime $\tau = 1.76 \times 10^{-19}$ s), consistent with the experimental Q-value of $2.9(3)$ MeV from NNDC_Chart and Wang (2021)
- For $^{39}$Sc ($^{38}$Ca$+p$): the TD-Dirac calculation yields $Q_p = 0.662$ MeV and $\Gamma_p = 3.87 \times 10^{-9}$ MeV (lifetime $\tau = 1.70 \times 10^{-13}$ s), consistent with the experimental upper limit $\tau < 400$ ns from NNDC_Chart
- The stabilization method for $^{37}$Sc gives $Q_p = 2.863$ MeV and $\Gamma_p = 3.62 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV, while the complex-scaling method gives $Q_p = 2.863$ MeV and $\Gamma_p = 3.82 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV
- The $f_{7/2}$ channel is identified as the resonant channel for both nuclei, with the $^{37}$Sc resonance energy at approximately 2.9 MeV from the stabilization graph

## Context

This work extends the relativistic energy-density functional (REDF) framework from static meanfield calculations to dynamic proton-emission processes, addressing the gap where relativistic quantum tunneling has been less investigated compared to non-relativistic cases. It builds on prior non-relativistic time-dependent calculations by Gurvitz (1987, 1988, 2004) and others, as well as relativistic complex-momentum representation studies by Niu (2016), Fang (2017), and Wang (2019). The method enables future evaluation of one-proton radioactivity across various REDF parameter sets for proton-rich nuclides, potentially improving predictive power for nuclei where experimental access remains challenging, and could be extended to two-proton radioactivity.
