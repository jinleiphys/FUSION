# Can dissipation prevent explosive decomposition in high-energy heavy ion collisions?

> We discuss the role of dissipation in the explosive spinodal decomposition scenario of hadron production during the chiral transition after a high-energy heavy ion collision. We use a Langevin description inspired by microscopic nonequilibrium field theory results to perform real-time lattice simulations of the behavior of the chiral fields. We show that the effect of dissipation can be dramatic. Analytic results for the short-time dynamics are also presented.

## Key claim

Even if a quark-gluon plasma expands and cools rapidly enough to reach the spinodal region of the QCD phase transition, dissipation may prevent the explosive spinodal decomposition of the chiral field; for a conservative dissipation coefficient of Γ = 2T, the time evolution of the chiral field toward its vacuum value is retarded by approximately 100% compared to the dissipation-free case, potentially leaving insufficient time for the spinodal explosion within the expansion timescale of RHIC collisions (≈ 5 fm⁻¹).

## Method

The authors performed real-time lattice simulations of the Langevin equation for a real scalar chiral field φ (the sigma direction of the linear σ-model coupled to quarks) on a 64³ cubic lattice with spacing a = 0.91 fm, using a semi-implicit finite-difference scheme and Fast Fourier Transform for spatial dependence. The effective potential U_eff(φ,T) was fitted by a sixth-degree polynomial, and the system was quenched to the spinodal temperature T_sp ≈ 108 MeV, with the dissipation coefficient Γ taken as a temperature-dependent constant (Γ/T = 0, 2, 4). Short-time analytic solutions of the linearized Langevin equation were also derived and compared to the full nonlinear results.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c ≈ 123 MeV; spinodal temperature T_sp ≈ 108 MeV.
- Lattice simulations used 64³ sites with lattice spacing a = 0.91 fm and time step Δt = 0.001/T.
- For Γ/T = 2, dissipation retards the time evolution of φ toward its vacuum value by approximately 100% compared to the Γ = 0 case.
- Expansion timescales of order 5 fm⁻¹ (≈ 5 fm/c) are comparable to RHIC collision timescales.
- The inflexion point of U_eff occurs at φ₀ ≈ 0.162 T.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of explosive spinodal decomposition in the QCD phase transition (e.g., the Polyakov loop model and low-energy chiral effective models by Csernai, Zabrodin, Scavenius, Shukla, and Paech) by incorporating dissipation effects beyond mean-field analysis. It also connects to earlier work on disoriented chiral condensate formation (Biro, Greiner, Krishna, and others) where dissipation proved important. The results suggest that dissipation must be included alongside expansion and finite-size effects (Fraga, 2003) to determine whether explosive hadronization actually occurs at RHIC; the authors plan to address these combined effects in a future publication.
