# Three-alpha structures in C-12

> We search for three-alpha resonances in C-12 by using the complex scaling method in a microscopic cluster model. All experimentally known low-lying natural-parity states are localized. For the first time we unambiguously show that the 0+_2 state in C-12, which plays an important role in stellar nucleosynthesis, is a genuine three-alpha resonance.

# Three-alpha structures in C-12

## Key claim

For the first time, the authors unambiguously show that the 0+_2 state in C-12 is a genuine three-alpha resonance, not merely an enhancement from sequential decay through Be-8 + alpha. This conclusion is conditional on the use of the complex scaling method which correctly handles three-body boundary conditions, though the calculated width of this state is likely overestimated due to limitations of the method for very narrow resonances.

## Method

The authors employ a microscopic three-cluster resonating group method (RGM) with alpha+alpha+alpha trial wave functions, using the complex scaling method to transform the three-body resonance problem into a bound-state-like problem. Three different effective nucleon-nucleon interactions (Minnesota, Volkov 1, and Volkov 2) are used, with their exchange mixture parameters fixed to reproduce the Be-8 ground state energy. The intercluster relative motion functions are expanded in tempered Gaussian basis functions, and the complex-scaled many-body Hamiltonian matrix elements are calculated analytically using computer algebra.

## Key numbers

- The 0+_2 state in C-12 is found at resonance energies of 0.64 MeV (MN), 0.71 MeV (V1), and 0.83 MeV (V2) relative to the three-alpha threshold, compared to the experimental value of 0.3796 +/- 0.0002 MeV
- The calculated widths for the 0+_2 state are 0.014 MeV (MN), 0.031 MeV (V1), and 0.077 MeV (V2), compared to the experimental width of (8.5 +/- 1.0) x 10^-6 MeV
- The C-12 ground state (0+_1) is found at -10.43 MeV (MN), -7.56 MeV (V1), and -5.27 MeV (V2) relative to the three-alpha threshold, compared to the experimental value of -7.2746 MeV
- The Be-8 ground state energy is reproduced at 0.092 MeV relative to the two-alpha threshold by all three interactions, with widths of 6.15 x 10^-6 MeV (MN), 2.36 x 10^-6 MeV (V1), and 5.17 x 10^-6 MeV (V2), compared to the experimental width of (6.8 +/- 1.7) x 10^-6 MeV
- The 1- state in C-12 is found at 3.71 MeV (MN), 3.72 MeV (V1), and 3.82 MeV (V2), compared to the experimental value of 3.569 +/- 0.016 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic three-alpha models of C-12 (Fukushima and Kamimura 1978; Kamimura 1981; Descouvemont and Baye 1987) which used two-body Be-8+alpha scattering asymptotics and could not definitively establish whether the 0+_2 state is a genuine three-body resonance. It also addresses a controversy raised by Csoto (1995) who speculated the state might be an enhancement from sequential decay, and the subsequent criticism by Hayes and Sterbenz (1995). The results enable future studies of the triple-alpha process in stellar nucleosynthesis with proper three-body dynamics, though the authors note that major improvements including rearrangement channels with nonzero spin would be needed to achieve quantitative agreement with experimental resonance parameters.
