# Chiral expansion of baryon masses and $σ$-terms

> We analyze the octet baryon masses and the pion/kaon--nucleon $σ$--terms in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. We include {\it all} terms up-to-and-including quadratic order in the light quark masses, $m_q$. We develop a consistent scheme to estimate low--energy constants related to scalar--isoscalar operators in the framework of resonance exchange involving one--loop graphs. The pertinent low--energy constants can only be estimated up to some finite coefficients. Including contributions from loop graphs with intermediate spin--3/2 decuplet and spin--1/2 octet states and from tree graphs including scalar mesons, we use the octet baryon masses and the pion--nucleon $σ$--term to fix all but one of these coefficients. Physical results are insensitive to this remaining parameter. It is also demonstrated that two--loop corrections only modify some of the subleading low--energy constants. We find for the baryon mass in the chiral limit, $m_0 = 770 \pm 110$ MeV. While the corrections of order $m_q^2$ are small for the nucleon, they are still sizeable for the $Λ$, the $Σ$ and the $Ξ$. Therefore a definitive statement about the convergence of three--flavor baryon chiral perturbation can not yet be made. The strangeness content of the nucleon is $y = 0.21 \pm 0.20$. We also estimate the kaon--nucleon $σ$--terms and some two--loops contributions to the nucleon mass.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a consistent chiral perturbation theory calculation of octet baryon masses and sigma-terms to fourth order (including all terms quadratic in the light quark masses) is possible, but that the convergence of three-flavor baryon chiral perturbation theory remains uncertain because corrections of order \(m_q^2\) are small for the nucleon but still sizeable for the \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\), and \(\Xi\).

## Method

The authors performed a heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation including all terms up to and including quadratic order in the light quark masses (\(m_q\)). They estimated the low-energy constants (LECs) related to scalar-isoscalar operators using resonance exchange, incorporating one-loop graphs with intermediate spin-3/2 decuplet and spin-1/2 octet states, as well as tree graphs with scalar mesons. The octet baryon masses and the pion-nucleon sigma-term were used to fix all but one of the LEC coefficients, with physical results shown to be insensitive to the remaining free parameter.

## Key numbers

- Baryon mass in the chiral limit: \(m_0 = 770 \pm 110\) MeV
- Strangeness content of the nucleon: \(y = 0.21 \pm 0.20\)
- The pion-nucleon sigma-term shift \(\sigma_{\pi N}(2M_\pi^2) - \sigma_{\pi N}(0) = 15\) MeV (from Gasser, Leutwyler, and Sainio (1991), used as empirical input)

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Borasoy and Meißner (1996) by providing a correct and more detailed treatment of s-channel resonance exchanges to the low-energy constants, fixing an inconsistency in their previous treatment of decuplet intermediate states. It builds upon the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory framework of Jenkins and Manohar (1991) and addresses criticisms by Lebed and Luty (1994) regarding the computability of quark mass ratios and deviations from Gell-Mann-Okubo relations. The results enable future studies of two-loop contributions to the nucleon mass and provide a framework for estimating kaon-nucleon sigma-terms, though the authors note that a definitive statement about the convergence of three-flavor baryon chiral perturbation theory cannot yet be made.
