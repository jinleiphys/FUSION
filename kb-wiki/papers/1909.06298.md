# Fierz-complete NJL model study III: Emergence from quark-gluon dynamics

> Our understanding of the dynamics and the phase structure of dense strong-interaction matter is to a large extent still built on the analysis of low-energy models, such as those of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type. In this work, we analyze the emergence of the latter class of models at intermediate and low energy scales from fundamental quark-gluon interactions. To this end, we study the renormalization group flow of a Fierz-complete set of four-quark interactions and monitor their strength at finite temperature and quark chemical potential. At small quark chemical potential, we find that the scalar-pseudoscalar interaction channel is dynamically rendered most dominant by the gauge degrees of freedom, indicating the formation of a chiral condensate. Moreover, the inclusion of quark-gluon interactions leaves a significant imprint on the dynamics as measured by the curvature of the finite-temperature phase boundary which we find to be in accordance with lattice QCD results. At large quark chemical potential, we then observe that the dominance pattern of the four-quark couplings is changed by the underlying quark-gluon dynamics, without any fine-tuning of the four-quark couplings. In this regime, the scalar-pseudoscalar interaction channel becomes subleading and the dominance pattern suggests the formation of a chirally symmetric diquark condensate. In particular, our study confirms the importance of explicit $U_{\mathrm{A}}(1)$ breaking for the formation of this type of condensate at high densities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL)-type four-quark interactions emerge dynamically from fundamental quark-gluon interactions via renormalization group (RG) flow, without requiring fine-tuned initial conditions for the four-quark couplings; the dominance pattern of interaction channels changes from scalar-pseudoscalar (chiral condensate) at low chemical potential to diquark (chirally symmetric condensate) at high chemical potential, contingent on explicit U_A(1) symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors employed the functional RG (Wetterich) equation to study the RG flow of a Fierz-complete set of ten pointlike four-quark interaction channels in two-flavor QCD, starting from the perturbative high-momentum regime with only the strong coupling g_s as input. They integrated out fluctuations from the initial UV scale Λ down to the infrared, monitoring the dimensionless renormalized four-quark couplings λ_i at finite temperature T and quark chemical potential μ, using the divergence of a coupling as an indicator of spontaneous symmetry breaking. The analysis was performed in Feynman gauge at leading order of the derivative expansion, with contributions from quark-gluon box diagrams, triangle diagrams, and purely fermionic diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The curvature of the finite-temperature phase boundary at small quark chemical potential was found to be in accordance with lattice QCD results (no explicit numerical value given for the curvature in the truncated text).
- The Fierz-complete basis consists of ten four-quark interaction channels, with N_c = 3 colors and N_f = 2 flavors.
- The U_A(1) symmetry breaking is quantified by two sum rules: S_{U_A(1)}^{(1)} = λ_CSC + λ_{(S+P)_-^adj} = 0 and S_{U_A(1)}^{(2)} = λ_{(S+P)_-} - ((N_c - 1)/(2N_c)) λ_CSC + (1/2) λ_{(σ-π)} = 0, where N_c = 3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Fierz-complete NJL model studies (Braun 2017, Braun 2018) by incorporating dynamical gauge degrees of freedom, following earlier functional RG studies of QCD (Braun 2005, Braun 2006, Braun 2008, Braun 2014). It enables a top-down first-principle approach to the QCD phase diagram at high densities, resolving ambiguities in parameter determination that plague conventional NJL-type models (Buballa 2003, Schaefer 2008, Zhang 2009) and lifting the finite UV extent limitation of such models. The results confirm the importance of explicit U_A(1) breaking for diquark condensate formation at high densities, as previously suggested in the literature.
