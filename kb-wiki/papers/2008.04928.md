# Medium-induced fragmentation and equilibration of highly energetic partons

> We investigate the energy loss and equilibration of highly energetic particles/jets inside a QCD medium. Based on an effective kinetic description of QCD, including $2\leftrightarrow 2$ elastic processes, radiative $1\leftrightarrow 2$ processes, as well as the back-reaction of jet constituents onto the thermal medium, we describe the in-medium evolution of jets from the energy scale of the jet $\sim E$ all the way to the medium scale $\sim T $. While elastic processes and back-reaction are important to describe the equilibration of soft fragments of the jet, we find that the energy loss is dominated by an inverse turbulent cascade due to successive radiative branchings, which has interesting implications for the energy spectra and chemistry of jet fragments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the energy loss of highly energetic partons in a QCD medium is dominated by an inverse turbulent cascade due to successive radiative branchings, rather than by elastic processes or medium recoil, but this conclusion is qualified by the condition that it applies to partons with energy significantly above the medium temperature (≳ 30 T), while less energetic partons are more sensitive to medium-scale physics.

## Method

The authors employ an effective kinetic description of QCD that includes leading-order small-angle elastic 2↔2 processes, radiative 1↔2 processes (with LPM-suppressed splitting rates), and the back-reaction of jet constituents onto the thermal medium. They linearize the collision integrals around a static homogeneous equilibrium background and evolve the energy distributions D_a(x,t) for gluon and quark jets with initial energy E=1000T, decomposing quark distributions into flavor singlet and valence components. The evolution is followed from the jet energy scale x∼1 down to the medium scale x∼T/E, using a dimensionless time variable τ = t / t_split(E) normalized to the typical splitting timescale.

## Key numbers

- Jet energy E = 1000 T is used as the default for numerical results, with additional comparisons for E/T = 10, 30, 100, 1000.
- The typical splitting timescale is t_split(E) = 1/(g⁴ T) √(E/T), which is enhanced by a factor √(E/T) relative to the mean free path ~ 1/(g⁴ T).
- Early-time energy loss rate for a gluon jet: γ^(soft-radiation) = 0.0072 (from g→gg) + 1.16×10⁻⁶ N_f (from g→q\bar{q}); for a quark jet: γ^(soft-radiation) = 0.0038 (from q↔gq).
- The equilibrium momentum diffusion constant is \hat{\bar q}_eq = (g⁴/π) (T³/2) (N_c/3 + N_f/6).
- The Kolmogorov-Zhakarov spectrum for the singlet-to-gluon ratio is D_S(x)/D_g(x) = S/G ≈ 0.07 × 2N_f.
- The cut-off scale for defining jet constituents is μ = 2π T, below which particles are considered part of the equilibrated medium.

## Context

This work extends earlier findings by Mehtar-Tani (2018) and Blaizot (2013) that energy loss is dominated by a turbulent cascade from successive radiative branchings, and it parallels studies of QGP thermalization at early times by Kurkela (2018) and Schlichting (2019). The authors demonstrate that highly energetic partons (≳ 30 T) act as genuine non-equilibrium probes, while less energetic jets may allow exploration of QGP thermalization physics. The framework enables future studies of path-length dependence, angular structure of medium-induced showers, and out-of-cone energy loss for unified descriptions of soft and hard probes in heavy-ion collisions.
