# Step wise destruction of the pair correlations in micro clusters by a magnetic field

> The response of $nm$-size spherical superconducting clusters to a magnetic field is studied for the canonical ensemble of electrons in a single degenerate shell. For temperatures close to zero, the discreteness of the electronic states causes a step like destruction of the pair correlations with increasing field strength, which shows up as peaks in the susceptibility and heat capacity. At higher temperatures the transition becomes smoothed out and extends to field strengths where the pair correlations are destroyed at zero temperature.

# Step wise destruction of the pair correlations in micro clusters by a magnetic field

## Key claim

For nm-size spherical superconducting clusters at temperatures close to zero, the pair correlations are destroyed in discrete steps by an increasing magnetic field due to the successive excitation of quasiparticle levels with higher magnetic moment, which manifests as peaks in susceptibility and heat capacity; however, this stepwise behavior is only observable for clusters with fewer than about 2×10^4 electrons (shell degeneracy M<50) and temperatures below 0.1 T_c, and at higher temperatures the transition becomes smoothed out and pair correlations persist to field strengths that would destroy them at zero temperature.

## Method

The authors model a spherical superconducting cluster as a single degenerate electron shell (with degeneracy 2M, where M=2l+1) and treat the pair correlations using an exact SU(2) solution for the pairing Hamiltonian in the canonical ensemble, including the Zeeman interaction with the magnetic field. They calculate the canonical partition function by summing over seniority states (number of unpaired electrons) and their magnetic moments, from which they derive the canonical gap parameter Δ_c, the magnetic susceptibility χ, and the heat capacity C as functions of temperature and magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- For Al clusters with radius R=(1-5) nm, the number of free electrons is N≈2×10^3 to 3×10^5.
- The BCS gap parameter for Al clusters with R=5-10 nm is Δ(0)=0.3-0.4 meV.
- The diamagnetic susceptibility for nm-scale clusters is χ_D∼10^{-3}, which is three orders of magnitude larger than the Landau diamagnetic susceptibility of normal bulk metals.
- For a half-filled shell, the critical Larmor frequency at which pairing is completely destroyed at T=0 is ω_c=3Δ(0)/M for even electron number and ω_c=4Δ(0)/M for odd electron number.
- The mean field critical temperature for the half-filled shell is T_c=Δ(0)/2.
- The first seniority jump (gapless superconductivity onset) occurs at ω_g=G for even systems and ω_g=G(1+1/(M-2)) for odd systems.
- The ratio B_g/B_crit predicted by the single-shell model is approximately 2.57.

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Ralph, Black, and Tinkham (1997) on electron transport through nm-scale Al clusters, who observed that pair correlations sustain magnetic fields of several Tesla (compared to the bulk Al critical field of 99 Gauss) and suggested a stepwise destruction of pairing. The authors use exact solutions from nuclear physics (Esebag and Edigo, 1993) for pairing in a degenerate shell, and relate the stepwise seniority changes to the "High-K isomer" phenomenon in deformed rotating nuclei (Szymanski, 1983). The results enable experimental detection of discrete pairing destruction steps in small spherical clusters via susceptibility and heat capacity measurements, and predict a temperature-induced pairing reentrance at fields above the zero-temperature critical field that is unique to the canonical ensemble.
