# Nuclear cluster structure effect in $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions at the top RHIC energy

> Using the improved string-melting version of a Multi-Phase Transport model, we investigated the impact of nuclear geometry of $^{16}$O on anisotropic flows in O+O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200$ GeV. To evaluate the influence of nuclear structure and potential alpha clustering, we implemented four candidate configurations: Woods-Saxon, tetrahedron, square, and Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory. Initial-state geometry is quantified via the eccentricity cumulant ratio $\varepsilon_{2}\{4\}/\varepsilon_{2}\{2\}$, which provides a robust and evolution-independent measure sensitive to configuration differences. The model reproduces $v_{2}(p_{\rm T})$ at low $p_{\rm T}$ and $v_{3}(p_{\rm T})$ across the full $p_{\rm T}$ range, with integrated $v_{2}\{2\}$ and $v_{3}\{2\}$ matching the STAR data, demonstrating that transport dynamics captures the essential collectivity in this intermediate-size system. These findings establish a baseline for extending nuclear-structure studies in O+O collisions to other energies and differential observables within a unified transport model framework.

## Key claim

The paper claims that anisotropic flow observables in O+O collisions at 200 GeV are sensitive to the nuclear cluster structure of 16O, but this sensitivity is conditional: the improved AMPT transport model with a phenomenologically adjusted hadron formation time can reproduce STAR data, and the differences among configurations are most pronounced in central collisions, while v3 shows little sensitivity to the different configurations.

## Method

The authors used the improved string-melting version of the AMPT (a Multi-Phase Transport) model with four candidate configurations for 16O: Woods-Saxon (3-parameter Fermi distribution), tetrahedron cluster, square cluster, and Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) configurations. They introduced an impact-parameter-dependent hadron formation time to stabilize the parton-to-hadron transition, set the parton scattering cross section to 0.7 mb, and compared the resulting anisotropic flow coefficients v2 and v3 with STAR measurements using two-particle correlation method with non-flow subtraction from 60-80% peripheral collisions.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Woods-Saxon parameters: R0 = 2.608 fm, a = 0.513 fm, w = -0.051
- Tetrahedron cluster: distance between cluster centers l = 3.5 fm, cluster rms radius rc = 1.23 fm
- Square cluster: l = 3.0 fm, rc = 1.23 fm
- Nuclear charge rms radius of 16O: 2.6991 fm
- Hadron formation proper time: tau_hadron = 0.7 fm/c
- Peak energy density of hadronic matter in central cell: approximately 0.3 GeV/fm^3
- Parton scattering cross section used: 0.7 mb
- v2(pT) calculations describe STAR measurements for pT < 0.6 GeV/c but underestimate for pT > 1 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear structure effects in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly following the STAR Collaboration's first measurements of anisotropic flow in O+O collisions at 200 GeV (Huang 2023). It builds on earlier theoretical studies suggesting that anisotropic flow in O+O collisions may probe alpha clustering in 16O (Rybczynski 2019, Li 2020, Li 2021, Behera 2021, Ding 2023). The improved AMPT model addresses limitations of the public AMPT-SM model, which produced incorrect centrality dependence of mean pT in O+O collisions. The results establish a baseline for extending nuclear-structure studies to other collision energies and differential observables, and for future measurements in the fixed-target LHC program at LHCb using 16O isotopes.
