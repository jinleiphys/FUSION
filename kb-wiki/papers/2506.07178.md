# Squeezing effect on three-dimensional Hanbury Brown-Twiss radii

> This paper examines the impacts of the squeezing effect caused by the particle's in-medium mass modification on the three-dimensional Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) radii. An analysis is conducted on how the squeezing effect impacts the three-dimensional HBT radii of $ϕϕ$, $D^0$$D^0$, and $K^+$$K^+$. The squeezing effect suppresses the impacts of transverse flow on the transverse source distribution and broadens the space-time rapidity distribution of the particle-emitting source, leading to an increase in the HBT radii, notably in out and longitudinal direction. This phenomenon becomes more significant for higher transverse pair momentum, resulting in a non-monotonic decrease in the HBT radii with increasing transverse pair momentum. The impact of the squeezing effect on the HBT radii is more pronounced for $D^0$$D^0$ than for $ϕϕ$. Furthermore, this effect is also more significant for $ϕ$$ϕ$ than for $K^+$$K^+$. The findings presented in this paper could offer fresh perspectives on investigating the squeezing effect.

# Squeezing effect on three-dimensional Hanbury Brown-Twiss radii

## Key claim
The squeezing effect, caused by in-medium mass reduction of bosons, suppresses transverse flow effects on the source distribution and broadens the space-time rapidity distribution, producing a non-monotonic (non-flow) decrease of three-dimensional HBT radii with increasing transverse pair momentum. This effect is strongest for heavy bosons (D0 > phi > K+) and is more pronounced at lower freeze-out temperatures (0.14 GeV vs 0.15 GeV) and for the out and longitudinal HBT radii compared to the side radius.

## Method
The authors model a locally thermalized cylinder expansion source with a constant proper-time freeze-out (tau0 = 10 fm/c), transverse Gaussian width R_G = 6 fm, and space-time rapidity width delta_eta = 3.0. The emission function incorporates the squeezing effect via Bogoliubov transformations parameterized by the in-medium mass modification delta_m = m - m_*, where m_* is the in-medium boson mass. Three-dimensional HBT radii (R_o, R_s, R_l) are computed from Gaussian fits to the correlation function for phi-phi, D0-D0, and K+-K+ pairs at transverse flow parameters beta = 0, 0.3, 0.5 and freeze-out temperatures T = 0.14 and 0.15 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum masses used: phi = 1.01946 GeV, D0 = 1.86484 GeV, K+ = 0.49368 GeV (from PDG 2024)
- In-medium mass reduction delta_m taken as 0.01 GeV for phi and K+, 0.005 GeV for D0, based on pionic medium expectations
- Source parameters: R_G = 6 fm, delta_eta = 3.0, tau_0 = 10 fm/c
- Freeze-out temperatures: 0.14 GeV and 0.15 GeV for all three meson species
- Transverse flow parameters beta = 0, 0.3, 0.5
- For phi-phi at K_T = 1.5 GeV and beta = 0.5, the squeezing effect increases R_o and R_l significantly, producing non-monotonic behavior; R_s shows smaller increases
- For D0-D0, the squeezing effect on HBT radii is greater than for phi-phi due to the larger D0 mass
- For K+-K+, the squeezing effect on HBT radii is weaker than for phi-phi and D0-D0
- At delta_m < 0.02 GeV for phi-phi, the slopes of R_o, R_s, R_l versus delta_m are significantly larger at K_T = 1.5 GeV than at K_T = 0.5 GeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the squeezing effect on one-dimensional HBT radii using spherically symmetric Gaussian sources (DudPad10; Zhang2024) to a more realistic three-dimensional cylinder expansion source model. The cylinder source (s1,s2) allows independent treatment of transverse and longitudinal dimensions, revealing that the squeezing effect impacts R_o and R_l more strongly than R_s. The non-flow behavior of HBT radii offers a new observable for studying in-medium mass modifications beyond squeezed back-to-back correlations (SBBC), which are suppressed for sources with broad temporal distributions (DudPad10; Zhang-EPJC16). The results suggest that D0-D0 and phi-phi pairs are promising experimental probes for detecting this effect, while current K+-K+ HBT measurements (khbt1-khbt6) have not yet reached the transverse momentum region where non-flow behavior would appear.
