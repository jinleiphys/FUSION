# Holographic calculation of hadronic contributions to muon g-2

> Using the gauge/gravity duality, we compute the leading order hadronic (HLO) contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of muon, amu(HLO). Holographic renormalization is used to obtain a finite vacuum polarization. We find amu(HLO) =470.5 x 10^{-10} in AdS/QCD with two light flavors, which is compared with the currently revised BABAR data estimated from e^+ e^- -> pi^+ pi^- events, amu(HLO)[pipi]=(514.1 +- 3.8) x 10^{-10}.

## Key claim

Using the gauge/gravity duality (AdS/QCD) with two light flavors, the authors compute the leading-order hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment and find a value that agrees with the revised BABAR data only within 30% errors, suggesting that 1/N_c corrections and isospin-breaking corrections could account for the discrepancy.

## Method

The authors use the holographic QCD model of Erlich et al. (2005) in a slice of five-dimensional anti-de Sitter space, with U(2)_L x U(2)_R gauged flavor symmetry and a bulk scalar field. They solve the equations of motion for the vector gauge fields, obtain the vector current correlator via holographic renormalization, and express it as a sum over an infinite tower of vector meson exchanges. The calculation is truncated at the fourth excited state (n=4), which saturates the full contribution within about 1% accuracy, and the result is inserted into the standard dispersion integral formula for a_mu^HLO.

## Key numbers

* a_mu^HLO (AdS/QCD, N_f=2) = 470.5 x 10^{-10}
* a_mu^HLO[pi pi] (BABAR, from e+e- -> pi+ pi- events) = (514.1 +/- 3.8) x 10^{-10}
* The lowest vector meson mass is fitted to the rho meson mass: M_V1 = M_rho = 0.775 GeV, fixing z_m = 3.101 GeV^{-1}
* Truncation at n=4 excited states saturates the full infinite-tower contribution within about 1% accuracy

## Context

This work extends the application of holographic QCD to hadronic contributions to muon g-2, following a similar successful estimate of the hadronic light-by-light contribution by Hong et al. (2009). The result is compared to the revised BABAR data from Aubert et al. (2009) and the dispersion integral analysis by Davier et al. (2009). The calculation is performed at leading order in the large-N_c limit, and the authors note that incorporation of 1/N_c corrections as done by Harada et al. (2006) would be needed for comparison with lattice QCD results from Blum et al. (2002) that include meson-loop contributions.
