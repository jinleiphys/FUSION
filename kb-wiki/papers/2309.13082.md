# Why is ${\rm Pb}^{208}$ the heaviest stable nuclide?

> In an effort to understand nuclei in terms of quarks we develop an effective theory to low-energy quantum chromodynamics in which a single quark contained in a nucleus is driven by a mean field due to other constituents of the nucleus. We analyze the reason why the number of $d$ quarks in light stable nuclei is much the same as that of $u$ quarks, while for heavier nuclei beginning with ${\rm Ca}^{40}$, the number of $d$ quarks is greater than the number of $u$ quarks. To account for the finiteness of the periodic table, we invoke a version of gauge/gravity duality between the dynamical affair in stable nuclei and that in extremal black holes. With the assumption that the end of stability for heavy nuclei is dual to the occurrence of a naked singularity, we find that the maximal number of protons in stable nuclei is $Z_{\max}^{\rm H}\approx 82$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the end of nuclear stability at lead-208 (Z=82) is dual, via gauge/gravity correspondence, to the appearance of a naked singularity in anti-de Sitter space, which violates the classical-quantum interface that stable nuclei and extremal black holes share.

## Method

The authors develop an effective low-energy QCD theory in which a single quark in a nucleus is driven by a mean field from other constituents, described by a Dirac Hamiltonian with pseudospin symmetry condition and a Cornell potential. They then invoke gauge/gravity duality between this effective theory and a Dirac particle moving in the gravitational and electromagnetic background of a charged, rotating extremal black hole in AdS5, and analyze the condition under which the black hole's event horizon disappears into a naked singularity.

## Key numbers

* The maximal number of protons in stable nuclei is found to be Z_max^H ≈ 82.
* The excess of neutrons along the drip line from Z=20 to Z=82 is approximately ΔN = 0.71(Z − 20).
* The constituent quark mass used in the effective theory is m = 0.33 GeV.
* The Cornell potential parameters are α_s = 0.7 and σ = 0.1 GeV^2, borrowed from quarkonia descriptions.
* The cavity size r_* scales as ρ_0 A^{1/3} with ρ_0 ≈ 1 fm.
* The radial quantum number n_r ranges from 12 to 35 for stable nuclei from Ca-40 to Pb-208.
* The maximum allowable separation of quarks in stable heavy nuclei is estimated at about 7 fm, with standard deviation of the quark wave function ≈ 0.6 fm for constituent quark mass 330 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous effective theory of nuclei in terms of quarks (Kosyakov, Popov, Vronsky, 2020-2022) and applies gauge/gravity duality, originally developed by Maldacena (1998) and others, to the nuclear stability problem. The approach contrasts with the conventional Yukawa meson-exchange picture, which the authors argue cannot satisfactorily explain why stability ends at Pb-208 rather than at lighter nuclei where Coulomb repulsion is less efficient. The holographic mapping of extremal black holes to stable nuclei enables a new method for determining the upper limit of the periodic table.
