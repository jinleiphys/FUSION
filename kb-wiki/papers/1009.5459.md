# The early thermalization and HBT puzzles at RHIC

> The early thermalization and HBT puzzles in relativistic heavy-ion collisions studied at RHIC are shortly reviewed. The results of recent hydrodynamic calculations that shed light on these two intriguing issues are presented. In particular, the role of the elliptic flow as a signature of early thermalization is critically examined.

## Key claim

The early thermalization and HBT puzzles at RHIC may be circumvented in the pionic sector by models that postpone complete thermalization to a comfortable time of 1 fm, rather than requiring thermalization times below 0.25 fm; the large pion elliptic flow is not a good signature of very early thermalization.

## Method

The paper reviews hydrodynamic calculations that incorporate a pre-equilibrium stage (free-streaming or transverse hydrodynamics) between τ = 0.25 fm and τ = 1 fm, followed by a sudden equilibration to perfect-fluid hydrodynamics via Landau matching conditions. The model results for transverse-momentum spectra, elliptic flow coefficient v₂, and pion HBT radii are compared to RHIC data from the STAR and PHENIX collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization times assumed in hydrodynamic codes remain well below 1 fm, with starting times as short as τ_eq ∼ 0.10–0.25 fm (Pratt 2008, Bozek 2009).
- In the free-streaming scenario, the hydrodynamic stage starts at τ₀ = 0.25 fm with initial central temperature T_i = 460 MeV for centrality c = 20–30% and T_i = 500 MeV for c = 0–5%; after free-streaming to τ = 1 fm, the central temperature is T_i = 305 MeV for c = 20–30% and T_i = 330 MeV for c = 0–5%.
- The bottom-up thermalization scenario (Baier 2000) gives an equilibration time of at least 2.6 fm.
- The ratio R_out/R_side from data is approximately 1; the discrepancy between data and theory after improvements remains at the level of about 10%.
- The number of pion trajectory crossings after freeze-out is estimated at about 1.5–1.7 per pion.
- Resonance decays make the studied systems larger by about 1 fm.
- The model with transverse hydrodynamics describes the data at the level of 20% (relative difference Δ between data and model results).
- Optimal freeze-out temperatures are around T_f > 140–150 MeV for early freeze-out and T_f < 140–150 MeV for late freeze-out.

## Context

This review extends the work of Heinz and Kolb (2002) who first addressed both puzzles simultaneously, and builds on hydrodynamic calculations by Broniowski (2008), Pratt (2008), and Bozek (2009). The paper shows that models with delayed thermalization (free-streaming or transverse hydrodynamics) can reproduce soft hadronic observables, challenging the earlier paradigm that large elliptic flow requires very early thermalization. Recent work by Bozek (2010) suggests that directed flow, rather than elliptic flow, may be a signature of early thermalization, potentially shifting the ten-year-old paradigm.
