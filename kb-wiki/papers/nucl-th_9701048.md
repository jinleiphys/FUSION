# Equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter and collisions of neutron-rich nuclei

> The ratio of pre-equilibrium neutrons to protons from collisions of neutron-rich nuclei is studied as a function of their kinetic energies. This ratio is found to be sensitive to the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, but is independent of the compressibility of symmetric nuclear matter and the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections. The experimental measurement of this ratio thus provides a novel means for determining the nuclear equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The ratio of pre-equilibrium neutrons to protons from collisions of neutron-rich nuclei is sensitive to the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, but is independent of the compressibility of symmetric nuclear matter and the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, provided the Coulomb potential is accounted for separately.

## Method

The authors used an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model initialized with proton and neutron densities from nonlinear relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory. They simulated collisions of 112Sn+112Sn, 124Sn+124Sn, and 132Sn+132Sn at 40 MeV/nucleon beam energy, including isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross sections, Pauli blocking, a symmetry potential parameterized with three different density dependences (F1(u)=2u^2/(1+u), F2(u)=u, F3(u)=u^1/2), and the Coulomb potential for protons. Free nucleons were identified using a phase-space coalescence method at 200 fm/c after initial contact.

## Key numbers

- The bulk symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density S0 is known to be in the range of 27-36 MeV from mass measurements; non-relativistic Hartree-Fock theory predicts 27-38 MeV, and RMF theory predicts 35-42 MeV.
- For 132Sn+132Sn collisions at 40 MeV/nucleon, in the absence of Coulomb and symmetry potentials, the ratio Rn/p(Ekin) is almost independent of nucleon kinetic energy and has a constant value of about 2.1 ± 0.3 for both central and peripheral collisions.
- Varying the compressibility K of symmetric nuclear matter from 210 MeV to 380 MeV left the ratio Rn/p(Ekin) almost unchanged for all impact parameters in 132Sn+132Sn collisions.
- Setting the neutron-proton and neutron-neutron (proton-proton) cross sections equal changed the yields and their ratios by less than 10% even in peripheral collisions of 132Sn+132Sn.
- The experimental neutron-proton collision cross section is about three times that for neutron-neutron (proton-proton) collisions in the energy range studied.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of the asymmetric nuclear matter equation of state by Siemens (1970), Wiringa et al. (1988), and Prakash et al. (1988), who predicted varying density dependences of the symmetry energy. It also builds on the isospin-dependent BUU model previously used to explain isospin-dependent phenomena in heavy-ion collisions by Li et al. (1995) and to predict isospin dependence of collective flow and balance energy later confirmed experimentally by Pak et al. at NSCL/MSU. The proposed method complements Tanihata's (1996) approach of extracting the asymmetric EOS from properties of neutron-rich nuclei such as density distributions, radii, and neutron skin thickness. The results enable experimental determination of the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy using existing radioactive ion beam facilities, which is important for understanding neutron star properties, supernova explosion scenarios, and the structure of neutron-rich nuclei.
