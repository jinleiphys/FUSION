# Detectability of Strange Matter in Heavy Ion Experiments

> We discuss the properties of two distinct forms of hypothetical strange matter, small lumps of strange quark matter (strangelets) and of hyperon matter (metastable exotic multihypernuclear objects: MEMOs), with special emphasis on their relevance for present and future heavy ion experiments. The masses of small strangelets up to A = 40 are calculated using the MIT bag model with shell mode filling for various bag parameters. The strangelets are checked for possible strong and weak hadronic decays, also taking into account multiple hadron decays. It is found that strangelets which are stable against strong decay are most likely highly negative charged, contrary to previous findings. Strangelets can be stable against weak hadronic decay but their masses and charges are still rather high. This has serious impact on the present high sensitivity searches in heavy ion experiments at the AGS and CERN facilities. On the other hand, highly charged MEMOs are predicted on the basis of an extended relativistic mean-field model. Those objects could be detected in future experiments searching for short-lived, rare composites. It is demonstrated that future experiments can be sensitive to a much wider variety of strangelets.

# Detectability of Strange Matter in Heavy Ion Experiments

## Key claim

Strangelets that are stable against strong decay are most likely highly negatively charged, contrary to previous findings; while strangelets can be stable against weak hadronic decay, their masses and charges remain rather high, which has serious implications for current high-sensitivity searches at AGS and CERN facilities.

## Method

The authors calculated masses of small strangelets up to baryon number A_B = 40 using the MIT bag model with exact single-particle Dirac states and shell mode filling for various bag parameters, checking stability against strong and weak hadronic decays including multiple hadron emission and fission. For metastable exotic multihypernuclear objects (MEMOs), they employed an extended relativistic mean-field model with strange scalar and vector mesons (sigma* and phi) coupling to strangeness, calculating bound configurations of {n,p,Λ,Ξ^0,Ξ^-} baryons up to filled s- and p-shells (8 baryons of each type).

## Key numbers

- Strangelets with bag constant B^{1/4} = 170 MeV: only a few very light strangelets for A_B ≤ 6 remain metastable, with Z/A_B < -0.5 and strangeness fraction f_s > 2.5
- For B^{1/4} = 160 MeV: nearly no metastable strangelets appear with f_s < 1 or Z/A > 0.6 in the mass range considered; maximum charge is Z = +2 for light systems
- For B^{1/4} = 150 MeV: metastable strangelets exist for a wide range of charge (|Z|/A ≤ 1) and strangeness fraction; only for f_s < 0.4 are strangelets unstable against strong decay
- MEMO binding energies per baryon: E_B/A_B ≈ -22 MeV for larger systems, but not more than -16 MeV for baryon numbers less than A_B ≈ 16
- MEMO baryon densities: 2.5 to 3 times that of ordinary nuclei
- Weak nonleptonic decay lifetime estimates for strangelets: τ_nl ≈ 10^{-6} to 10^{-5} s (Koch), τ_nl ≈ 3 × 10^{-7} s (Henning), or τ_nl = 10^{-5} to 10^{-10} s (Madsen)
- Weak leptonic decay lifetime: τ_l = 10^{-4} to 10^{-5} s in infinite matter
- MEMO weak decay lifetime: τ_MEMO ≈ τ_Λ ≈ 10^{-10} s
- Most promising long-lived candidates: A_B = 10, Z = -4; A_B = 12, Z = -6; A_B = 16, Z = -10 (appear for all bag parameters studied)
- Experiment E814 limit: 1.2 × 10^{-4} per event for multiply charged strangelets
- Experiment E886 sensitivity: down to 10^{-7} per event for 0 < A/Z < 14
- Experiment E864 limits for Z = +1, +2 and A_B > 10: about 10^{-5} to 10^{-6} per event
- Experiment E858/E878 sensitivity: 10^{-9} to 10^{-10} per event for -1 < A/Z < -7, no strangelet found
- Experiment NA52 limits for negatively charged strangelets: sensitivity down to 10^{-8} to 10^{-10} per event for M/|Z| = 10-40, but for M/|Z| ≈ 2 the limit is about 10^{-6} to 10^{-7} per event

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Farhi and Jaffe (1984) and Gilson and Jaffe (1993) on strangelet stability, but finds that the cascade of strong decays does not stop at the absolute minimum in energy per baryon, leading to highly negatively charged candidates rather than low positive charges. The authors compare strangelets with MEMOs predicted by Schaffner et al. (1992, 1993) using relativistic mean-field theory, noting that both forms of strange matter appear in complementary regions of the charge-strangeness plane. The results suggest that future experiments should search for highly charged strange matter, as current searches at AGS (E814, E858, E864, E878, E886) and CERN (NA52) are mostly sensitive to particles with small charge-to-mass ratios, while the most promising candidates like A_B=10, Z=-4 remain unexplored.
