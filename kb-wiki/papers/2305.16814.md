# The Fate of excited state of $^4\text{He}$

> We investigate the properties of the excited state of $^4\mathrm{He}$, $^4\mathrm{He}^*$, within the framework of Efimov physics and its connection to the unitary point of the nuclear interaction. We explore two different approaches to track the trajectory of $^4\mathrm{He}^*$ as it crosses the $^3\mathrm{H}$+p threshold and potentially becomes a resonant state. The first approach involves an analytical continuation of the energy with respect to the Coulomb coupling, while the second approach introduces an artificial four-body force that it is gradually released. By utilizing Padé approximants and extrapolation techniques, we estimate the energy and width of the resonance. Our results suggest a central energy value of $E_R=0.060(3)$ MeV and a width of $Γ/2=0.036(6)$ MeV using the Coulomb analysis, and $E_R=0.068(1)$ MeV and $Γ/2=0.007(5)$ MeV with the four-body force analysis. Interestingly, these results are consistent with calculations based on {\it ab-initio} nuclear interactions but differ from the accepted values of the $0^+$ resonance energy and width. This highlights the challenges in accurately determining the properties of resonant states in light nuclei and calls for further investigations and refinements in theoretical approaches.

## Key claim

The $0^+$ resonance of $^4$He is likely the excited state of the Efimov characterization of light nuclei, pushed above the $^3$H+p threshold by the Coulomb interaction; however, the authors' extrapolated resonance energies (0.060-0.068 MeV) are consistent with ab-initio calculations but disagree with the experimentally accepted value of 0.39 MeV, suggesting limitations in the R-matrix analysis of the experimental data.

## Method

The authors used a spin-isospin-dependent Gaussian two-body potential plus a three-body force, tuned to reproduce the binding energies of $^3$H, $^3$He, and $^4$He, to model the four-nucleon system within the universal Efimov window. They tracked the excited $0^+$ state of $^4$He as the Coulomb interaction was gradually switched on (via a parameter $\epsilon$), observing it cross the $^3$H+p threshold at $\epsilon_{th}=0.7897$, and then applied analytic continuation in the coupling constant (ACCC) using Pade approximants to extrapolate the resonance energy and width. Two independent ACCC procedures were employed: one continuing in the Coulomb coupling $\epsilon$ to its physical value $\epsilon=1$, and another introducing an artificial four-body force that binds the resonance and then extrapolating to zero four-body force strength.

## Key numbers

- Threshold Coulomb coupling at which $^4$He* crosses the $^3$H+p threshold: $\epsilon_{th}=0.7897$
- Coulomb ACCC result: resonance energy $E_R=0.060(3)$ MeV, half-width $\Gamma/2=0.036(6)$ MeV
- Four-body force ACCC result: resonance energy $E_R=0.068(1)$ MeV, half-width $\Gamma/2=0.007(5)$ MeV
- Experimental accepted value from R-matrix analysis (Tilley 1992): $E_R + i\Gamma/2 = 0.39$ MeV $+ i\,0.50/2$ MeV
- Gaussian potential parameters: $V_{01}=-37.9$ MeV, $V_{10}=-60.575$ MeV, range $r_0=1.65$ fm; three-body force $W_0=7.6044$ MeV, $R_3=3.035$ fm
- Model binding energies: $^3$H = $-8.482$ MeV, $^3$He = $-7.716$ MeV, $^4$He = $-28.305$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the application of Efimov physics to nuclear systems, following the framework established by Gattobigio (2019) and Kievsky (2021) showing that light nuclei lie within the universal Efimov window. The study directly addresses the discrepancy between ab-initio calculations (Viviani 2018) predicting $E_R\simeq 0.08(1)$ MeV and the accepted experimental value of 0.39 MeV from R-matrix analysis (Tilley 1992). The results enable future investigations into whether the R-matrix analysis of the $^4$He $0^+$ resonance requires refinement, and suggest that the resonance is the Coulomb-displaced remnant of the Efimov tetramer state predicted by Platter (2004) and Gattobigio (2012).
