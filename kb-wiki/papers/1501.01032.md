# A non-abelian quasi-particle model for gluon plasma

> We propose a quasi-particle model for the thermodynamic description of the gluon plasma which takes into account non-abelian characteristics of the gluonic field. This is accomplished utilizing massive non-linear plane wave solutions of the classical equations of motion with a variable mass parameter, reflecting the scale invariance of the Yang-Mills Lagrangian. For the statistical description of the gluon plasma we interpret these non-linear waves as quasi-particles with a temperature dependent mass distribution. Quasi-Gaussian distributions with a common variance but different temperature dependent mean masses for the longitudinal and transverse modes are employed. We use recent Lattice results to fix the mean transverse and longitudinal masses while the variance is fitted to the equation of state of pure $SU(3)$ on the Lattice. Thus, our model succeeds to obtain both a consistent description of the gluon plasma energy density as well as a correct behaviour of the mass parameters near the critical point.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a non-abelian quasi-particle model (NAQPM) for the gluon plasma that uses non-linear plane wave solutions of the classical SU(2) Yang-Mills equations as microstates, with a temperature-dependent mass distribution, and claims to reproduce two different sets of lattice results for the SU(3) equation of state using a single free parameter (the variance of the mass distribution), while avoiding the singular behavior of the mean mass near the critical point that contradicts lattice data.

## Method

The authors model gluon plasma microstates as non-linear plane wave solutions of the classical SU(2) Yang-Mills equations with period 5.244, interpreting these as quasi-particles with a continuously varying mass. They employ quasi-Gaussian mass distributions (truncated to positive values) with different temperature-dependent mean masses for transverse and longitudinal gluonic degrees of freedom, fixed by lattice data from Silva (2014), and a common variance fitted to the SU(3) equation of state from Boyd (1996) and Katz (2012). The energy density is calculated by integrating over the mass distributions and momenta, and the pressure is obtained via the thermodynamically consistent relation from Bannur (2007) to avoid inconsistencies from temperature-dependent masses.

## Key numbers

* The period of the non-linear plane wave solutions is 5.244, compared to 2π for linear waves, increasing the number of stationary states fitting in a fixed volume by a factor of (2π/5.244)^3.
* For temperatures T/T_c > 1.5, the common mean mass follows the linear relation μ(T)/T_c = 1.18 (T/T_c) + 0.48, with T_c approximately 270 MeV.
* The variance σ is determined by a Monte-Carlo search to fit lattice energy density and pressure within 10^{-4} accuracy; changing the convergence criterion to 10^{-3} changes σ only in the second decimal digit.
* The model reproduces lattice results for energy density and pressure scaled by T^4 from both Boyd (1996) and Katz (2012) across the temperature range T ≥ T_c, approaching the Stefan-Boltzmann limit from below at high temperatures.

## Context

This work extends quasi-particle models (QPMs) for the gluon plasma, which previously used temperature-dependent gluon masses fitted to the equation of state (e.g., Peshier (1994), Bannur (2007)), by incorporating non-abelian characteristics through classical non-linear plane wave solutions from Savvidy. It directly addresses the contradiction between earlier QPMs and lattice results from Silva (2014) showing that transverse and longitudinal gluon masses differ at low temperatures and behave smoothly near the critical point, unlike the stiff increase predicted by earlier models. The model enables future inclusion of matter degrees of freedom and suggests that the gluon deconfining transition may be interpreted as a change in the gluon mass spectrum, reflected in the rapid increase of the mass variance near the critical point.
