# Breakup and finite-range effects on the 8B(d,n)9C reaction

> The astrophysical factor of $^8$B($p$,$γ$)$^9$C at zero energy, $S_{18}(0)$, is determined by a three-body coupled-channels analysis of the transfer reaction $^{8}$B($d$,$n$)$^{9}$C at 14.4 MeV/nucleon. Effects of the breakup channels of $d$ and $^9$C are investigated with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method. It is found that, in the initial and final channels, respectively, the transfer process through the breakup states of $d$ and $^9$C, its interference with that through their ground states in particular, gives a large increase in the transfer cross section. The finite-range effects with respect to the proton-neutron relative coordinate are found to be about 20%. As a result of the present analysis, $S_{18}(0)=22 \pm 6~{\rm eV~b}$ is obtained, which is smaller than the result of the previous distorted-wave Born approximation analysis by about 51%.

## Key claim

The astrophysical factor $S_{18}(0)$ for $^8$B($p$,$\gamma$)$^9$C is determined to be $22 \pm 6$ eV b from a three-body coupled-channels analysis of the $^8$B($d$,$n$)$^9$C transfer reaction at 14.4 MeV/nucleon, which is about 51% smaller than the previous distorted-wave Born approximation result, but this value depends on including breakup channels of both deuteron and $^9$C and their interference with ground-state transfer.

## Method

The authors performed a continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) analysis of the $^8$B($d$,$n$)$^9$C reaction at 14.4 MeV/nucleon using a three-body ($p+n+^8$B) model, with the transition matrix calculated in the post-form coupled-channels Born approximation (CCBA). Breakup channels of both the deuteron (in the initial channel) and $^9$C (in the final channel) were included via pseudostate discretization, and finite-range effects with respect to the proton-neutron relative coordinate were treated through a local energy approximation correction.

## Key numbers

- $S_{18}(0) = 22 \pm 6$ eV b, obtained from the CCBA analysis including breakup channels of both $d$ and $^9$C
- $(C_{p {}^{8}\mbox{\scriptsize B}}^{^{9}\mbox{\scriptsize C}})^2 = 0.59 \pm 0.02$ (theor.) $\pm 0.13$ (exp.) fm$^{-1}$, the asymptotic normalization coefficient for the $p$-$^8$B(g.s.) overlap in $^9$C
- Inclusion of breakup channels increases the transfer cross section by about 58% at $0^\circ$ compared to the calculation without breakup states
- The finite-range effects are about 20% on the transfer cross section
- The previous DWBA analysis gave $S_{18}(0) = 45 \pm 13$ eV b, about 51% larger than the present result

## Context

This work extends the CDCC methodology previously applied to the $^7$Be($d$,$n$)$^8$B reaction (Fukui et al., 2014) by including breakup channels of both the projectile and residual nucleus, and resolves a discrepancy between the transfer reaction result and the reanalysis of $^9$C breakup reactions (breakupS18, 2014) which gave $S_{18}(0)=66 \pm 10$ eV b. The result enables more reliable determination of the $^8$B($p$,$\gamma$)$^9$C astrophysical factor for hot $pp$ chain nucleosynthesis in low-metallicity supermassive stars, and demonstrates that breakup effects must be included in transfer reactions involving weakly-bound nuclei.
