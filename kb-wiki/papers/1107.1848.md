# Unprejudiced Look at Effective Continuum Thresholds in Borel Dispersive Sum Rules

> Dispersive sum rules constitute long-standing tools for extracting hadron features from QCD. We estimate the systematic uncertainties induced by assuming quark-hadron duality and improve the accuracy of the resulting predictions by elevating the effective thresholds involved in this approximation from constants to functions of momenta and a parameter entering upon Borel transformation. A rigorous scrutiny of the applicability of our proposed sum-rule modifications to QCD gives us great confidence that our sum-rule alterations will prove to be fruitful for hadron phenomenology.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the systematic uncertainties in Borel dispersive sum rules, which arise from the assumption of quark-hadron duality, can be reduced by allowing the effective continuum threshold to become a function of the Borel parameter (T or τ) and momentum, rather than treating it as a constant.

## Method
The authors construct "dual correlators" in which the QCD contribution, truncated at an effective threshold s_eff, exactly cancels the hadronic ground-state contribution. They determine the functional form of s_eff by fitting known hadron features, using polynomial Ansätze of degree n (specifically n=1 and n=2). The method is tested by comparing extractions of ground-state decay constants in quantum mechanics (using a funnel potential for heavy-quark bound states) with analogous extractions of heavy pseudoscalar-meson decay constants in QCD.

## Key numbers
- For the funnel potential describing heavy-quark bound states, the dual energy E_dual is compared to the true ground-state energy E_g, and the decay constant √R_g (where R_g ≡ |Ψ(0)|²) is extracted.
- For QCD, the B-meson mass M_dual is compared to its experimental value M_B, and the dual decay constant f_dual is predicted as a function of the Borel parameter.
- The band delimited by the n=1 and n=2 polynomial Ansätze for the effective continuum threshold provides an estimate of the intrinsic errors in bound-state features such as √R_g.

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior studies on sum-rule uncertainties (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2009-2011) by systematically estimating the error induced by the quark-hadron duality approximation. It builds on the concept of effective thresholds introduced in earlier sum-rule literature and aims to improve the accuracy of hadron phenomenology predictions, particularly for decay constants of heavy mesons. The similarity between the quantum-mechanical test case and QCD results gives confidence that the modified sum-rule algorithm will be applicable to hadron phenomenology.
