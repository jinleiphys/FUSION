# Electron capture rates in stars studied with heavy ion charge exchange reactions

> Indirect methods using nucleus-nucleus reactions at high energies (here, high energies mean $\sim$ 50 MeV/nucleon and higher) are now routinely used to extract information of interest for nuclear astrophysics. This is of extreme relevance as many of the nuclei involved in stellar evolution are short-lived. Therefore, indirect methods became the focus of recent studies carried out in major nuclear physics facilities. Among such methods, heavy ion charge exchange is thought to be a useful tool to infer Gamow-Teller matrix elements needed to describe electron capture rates in stars and also double beta-decay experiments. In this short review, I provide a theoretical guidance based on a simple reaction model for charge exchange reactions.

## Key claim

Heavy ion charge exchange reactions at high energies (above 50 MeV/nucleon) can be used to extract Gamow-Teller matrix elements for electron capture rates in stars, but the widely used empirical formula relating forward-angle cross sections to transition strengths lacks a solid theoretical basis and can yield misleading results if the full reaction mechanism is not treated properly.

## Method

The author derives a DWBA scattering amplitude for heavy ion charge exchange using eikonal wavefunctions and an effective nucleon-nucleon interaction expressed in terms of central, tensor, and spin-orbit components with isospin and spin-isospin operators. The formalism is based on meson exchange (pion and rho) rather than physical nucleon exchange, which is suppressed at energies above 50 MeV/nucleon due to phase-space limitations. The author then compares this exact treatment to the empirical forward-angle formula of Taddeucci (1987) and shows that the empirical formula requires neglecting the momentum dependence of the nucleon-nucleon interaction and assuming no refraction or absorption.

## Key numbers

- High energies for charge exchange are defined as approximately 50 MeV/nucleon and higher
- At around 200 MeV/nucleon, the spin-isospin part of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction clearly dominates over the isospin part
- Double charge exchange total cross sections in heavy ion collisions are estimated to be smaller by a factor of 10^-4 to 10^-5 compared to single charge exchange
- The peak forward-angle differential cross section for single charge exchange is of the order of millibarns, while for double charge exchange it is estimated to be of the order of microbarns
- The effective interaction parameter V_11^0 was determined from (p,p') and (p,n) data to be 12 +/- 2.5 MeV (Auslander 1972)

## Context

This short review extends the theoretical understanding of charge exchange reactions beyond the empirical approach of Taddeucci (1987) and earlier work by Goodman (1980), Petrovich (1980), and Osterfeld (1992). The author shows that the simple forward-angle formula used routinely in experimental analysis fails in some cases, as demonstrated by detailed calculations in Bertulani (1993) and Bertulani and Lotti (1997). The work enables more reliable extraction of Gamow-Teller matrix elements for electron capture rates in core-collapse supernovae and for neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements, where the two-step charge exchange process (pion and rho exchange) could provide indirect measurements with future radioactive beam facilities.
