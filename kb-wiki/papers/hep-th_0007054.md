# Theories of violation of statistics

> I discuss theories of violations of statistics, including intermediate statistics, parastatistics, parons, and quons. I emphasize quons, which allow small violations of statistics. I analyze the quon algebra and its representations, implications of the algebra including the observables allowed by the superselection rule separating inequivalent representations of the symmetric group, the conservation of statistics rules, and the rule for composite systems of quons. I conclude by raising the question of possible origins of violations of statistics and of the level at which violations should be expected if they exist.

# Theories of Violation of Statistics

## Key claim

Quons provide the best formalism so far to describe small violations of statistics, with a continuous parameter q that interpolates between Fermi (q = -1) and Bose (q = +1) statistics; however, the quon theory necessarily fails to satisfy spacelike commutativity of observables, and interacting relativistic field theory remains in doubt.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches to violating quantum statistics, including Gentile's intermediate statistics, Green's parastatistics, the Ignatiev-Kuzmin model, parons, infinite statistics, and quons. The quon algebra is defined by the deformed commutation relation a(k)a†(l) − q a†(l)a(k) = δ(k,l), with the Fock-like representation selected by the vacuum condition a(k)|0⟩ = 0. The author analyzes the positivity of norms using Zagier's determinant formula and Speicher's probabilistic ansatz, examines observables under the superselection rule separating inequivalent representations of the symmetric group, and derives conservation of statistics rules and bound-state composition rules for quons.

## Key numbers

- Ramberg-Snow experiment bound on violation parameter for electrons: v_F ≤ 1.7 × 10⁻²⁶
- Goldhaber experiment bound on violation parameter for electrons: v_F ≤ 5 × 10⁻²
- Zagier's determinant formula for the n! × n! matrix of scalar products: det Mⁿ_P,Q(q) = ∏_{k=1}^{n-1} (1 − q^{k(k+1)})^{((n−k)n!)/(k(k+1))}
- For two identical quons in orthogonal states, the normalized symmetric and antisymmetric states have normalization factors N_{s,a} = 1/√(2(1 ± q))
- The two-particle density matrix coefficients for quons: α = √((1+q)/2) for the symmetric component, β = √((1−q)/2) for the antisymmetric component
- Relation between violation parameters and q: q_F = 2v_F − 1 (or v_F = (1/2)(1+q_F)) for fermions; q_B = 1 − 2v_B (or v_B = (1/2)(1−q_B)) for bosons
- Bound-state composition rule: a bound state of n identical quons with parameter q_constituent has parameter q_bound = q_constituent^{n²}
- Relation between electron and photon q parameters from conservation of statistics: q_e² = q_γ

## Context

This review extends the author's prior work with Messiah (1964) on quantum mechanics without the symmetrization postulate and with Mohapatra on parons and experimental bounds. It contrasts quons with earlier approaches: Gentile's intermediate statistics (1926) fails basis invariance; Green's parastatistics (1953) gives only gross violations with integer order p; the Ignatiev-Kuzmin model (1987) and parons suffer from negative-norm states as shown by Govorkov. The paper builds on the Doplicher-Haag-Roberts analysis (1974) which found parabose, parafermi, and infinite statistics from algebraic field theory. The quon formalism enables quantitative interpretation of high-precision experiments such as the Ramberg-Snow search for anomalous x-ray emission, and raises open questions about possible origins of violations from CPT violation, locality violation, Lorentz invariance violation, extra dimensions, discrete spacetime, or noncommutative spacetime.
