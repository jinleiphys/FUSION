# Dispersion Theoretical Analysis of Pion Photoproduction at Threshold and in the Delta Region

> A new partial wave analysis of pion photoproduction has been obtained in the framework of fixed-t dispersion relations valid form threshold up to 500 MeV. It is based on new Mainz data for π^{0} and π^{+} production off the proton and both older and more recent data from Bonn, Frascati and TRIUMF for π^{+} and π^{-}. At threshold we obtain a good agreement with the existing data for both charged and neutral pion production. In the resonance region we have precisely determined the electromagnetic properties of the Δ(1232) resonance, in particular the E2/M1 ratio R_{EM}=(-2.5 \pm 0.1) %. We show that a model independent resonance background subtraction is possible with the speed-plot technique and obtain the Δpole at W =(1211 - 50i) MeV and the E2/M1 ratio of the residues as R_Δ = - 0.035 - 0.046 i.

## Key claim

A new partial wave analysis of pion photoproduction, constrained by fixed-t dispersion relations and unitarity, yields precise electromagnetic properties of the Δ(1232) resonance; the abstract's claim of "good agreement" at threshold is qualified by the fact that the threshold amplitudes are predictions from data above 160 MeV, not direct fits to threshold data.

## Method

The analysis solves coupled integral equations from fixed-t dispersion relations for the invariant amplitudes, imposing Watson's final-state theorem below two-pion threshold and a unitarity-based ansatz above it up to 2 GeV, with t-channel ρ and ω exchange replacing the dispersive integrals from 2 GeV to infinity. Nine free parameters are fitted to a data base including recent Mainz (MAMI) data for π⁰ and π⁺ production off the proton from 160 to 420 MeV, plus older and newer data from Bonn, Frascati, and TRIUMF for π⁺ and π⁻. The speed-plot technique is applied to the P33 partial wave to extract the resonance pole and separate background from resonant contributions.

## Key numbers

- E2/M1 ratio at the Δ(1232) resonance position (W = 1232 MeV): R_EM = (-2.5 ± 0.1)%
- Δ pole position in the complex plane: W_R = (1211 - 50i) MeV
- Complex E2/M1 ratio of the residues at the pole: R_Δ = -0.035 - 0.046 i
- Threshold s-wave amplitude for γp → π⁺n: E_0+ = (28.0 ± 0.2) × 10⁻³/m_π⁺
- Threshold s-wave amplitude for γp → π⁰p: E_0+ = (-1.22 ± 0.16) × 10⁻³/m_π⁺
- Threshold s-wave amplitude for γn → π⁻p: E_0+ = (-31.7 ± 0.2) × 10⁻³/m_π⁺
- Charge-exchange pion-nucleon scattering length from Panofsky ratio: a_CEX = (-0.120 ± 0.002) × m_π⁻¹
- Photon couplings of the Δ resonance: A_1/2 = (-132 ± 2) × 10⁻³/√GeV and A_3/2 = (-253 ± 3) × 10⁻³/√GeV

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-theoretical approach of Schwela (1969) and Pfeil (1972) to new precise Mainz data, improving uncertainties over previous partial wave analyses such as the VPI (SAID) solutions and the RPI Lagrangian approach of Davidson (1990). The threshold predictions enable comparison with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (Bernard, 1996) and independent determination of the charge-exchange scattering length via the Panofsky ratio, while the pole-extracted ratio R_Δ provides a model-independent benchmark for microscopic quark models of the Δ resonance.
