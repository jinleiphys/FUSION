# Ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions: exploring the phase diagram of QCD

> I present the motivation for studying nuclear collisions at ultrarelativistic energies which is to map the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter under very extreme conditions. The relevant experimental efforts are overviewed and problems in connecting observables with physics of the hot and dense matter are pointed out. I review the current highlights of the experimental programme: excitation functions of various quantities from the SPS which suggest a possibility of the onset of deconfinement, and the evidence of deconfined perfect fluid at RHIC.

## Key claim

The phase diagram of strongly interacting matter can be explored through ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the main technical problem is that hadrons observed in detectors escape from the less excited hadronic gas, not directly from the hot and dense early phase, so observables must be related to the properties of the early hot state through indirect methods.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results from the AGS, SPS, and RHIC accelerators, focusing on excitation functions of hadron yields and ratios, elliptic flow measurements, and jet quenching observations. The chemical composition of produced hadrons is fitted to a chemical equilibrium model to extract freeze-out temperatures and baryochemical potentials, while single-particle momentum spectra and momentum correlations are used to infer transverse collective expansion velocities and source sizes.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD calculations indicate the energy density changes dramatically but smoothly at a temperature of **173 ± 15 MeV** (about 2 × 10¹² K, roughly 100,000 times hotter than the centre of the Sun).
- The Lorentz contraction factor γ spans from about **4** (at lowest SPS energy) up to **100** at RHIC and more than **2000** at the LHC.
- In central collisions at RHIC, the average transverse collective expansion velocity ⟨v_t⟩ reaches up to **0.6c**.
- The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density η/s for QGP just above the critical temperature is as small as **0.2**; for comparison, water at normal conditions has η/s of order **10³**.
- The ratio of positively charged kaons to pions peaks around √s ≈ **7 A GeV** and then drops, a non-monotonic structure dubbed "the horn".
- The fireball evolution from initial impact until freeze-out lasts about **10 fm/c** or more (about 3 × 10⁻²³ s).

## Context

This review extends the line of work on mapping the QCD phase diagram through heavy-ion collisions, building on lattice QCD calculations (Karsch, 2002) and the Statistical Model of Early Stage (Gorenstein and Gazdzicki, 2004). The results from RHIC showing perfect fluid behaviour and jet quenching confirm the strongly interacting QGP regime predicted by Shuryak (2004), while the SPS excitation functions (NA49 collaboration, Blume 2004) suggest a possible onset of deconfinement around √s ≈ 7 A GeV. The upcoming LHC and FAIR facilities will extend these studies to higher temperatures and baryochemical potentials respectively, potentially revealing a transition to a less strongly interacting regime at higher momentum scales.
