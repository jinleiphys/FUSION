# Magnon-mediated Binding between Holes in an Antiferromagnet

> The long-range forces between holes in an antiferromagnet are due to magnon exchange. The one-magnon exchange potential between two holes is proportional to cos(2ϕ)/r^2 where r is the distance vector of the holes and ϕis the angle between r and an axis of the square crystal lattice. One-magnon exchange leads to bound states of holes with antiparallel spins resembling d-wave symmetry. The role of these bound states as potential candidates for the preformed Cooper pairs of high-temperature superconductivity is discussed qualitatively.

## Key claim

One-magnon exchange between two holes in an antiferromagnet produces a long-range potential proportional to cos(2φ)/r², which can bind holes with antiparallel spins into an infinite number of bound states, provided short-range repulsion is modeled by a hard-core cutoff; the paper does not claim these bound states are the Cooper pairs of high-temperature superconductivity, only that they are "potential candidates" discussed qualitatively.

## Method

The authors construct a systematic low-energy effective field theory for magnons and holes in a square-lattice antiferromagnet, analogous to chiral perturbation theory for pions and nucleons. They expand the magnon field around the ordered staggered magnetization, compute the one-magnon exchange Feynman diagram between two holes, and derive the resulting coordinate-space potential. They then solve the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation for the relative motion of two holes with different flavors (α and β), using Mathieu functions for the angular part and Bessel functions for the radial part, with a hard-core boundary condition at radius r₀ to model short-range contact interactions.

## Key numbers

- The one-magnon exchange potential between holes of different flavors is V^{αβ}(r) = γ cos(2φ)/r², with γ = Λ²/(2πρ_s), where Λ is the hole-one-magnon coupling and ρ_s is the spin stiffness.

- The eigenvalue λ for the angular equation, to leading order in γ², is λ = (1/8)(M'γ)².
- The bound-state energies for large quantum number n scale as E_n ~ -(M'r₀²)⁻¹ exp(-2πn/√λ), where M' is the kinetic mass of a hole and r₀ is the hard-core radius.

## Context

This work extends the magnon effective field theory approach of Hasenfratz and Niedermayer (1990, 1993) and the hole-magnon effective theory of Kämpfer et al. (2005) to the two-hole bound-state problem, analogous to how one-pion exchange dominates long-range forces in the effective theory for light nuclei (Weinberg 1990, Kaplan et al. 1998, van Kolck 1998). A similar magnon exchange potential was extracted from the t-J model by Kuchiev and Sushkov (1993) and Sushkov et al. (2004), but the authors note that calculation was affected by uncontrolled approximations. The results enable future nonperturbative investigations of whether such magnon-mediated bound states can condense into a superconducting state, and provide an analytic form of the hole-pair wave function that could be compared with ARPES measurements of hole pockets at (π/2a, ±π/2a) (Wells et al. 1995, LaRochelle et al. 1997, Kim et al. 1998, Ronning et al. 1998).
