# Proton dominance in the 2^+_2 -> 0^+_1 transition of N = Z\pm 2 nuclei around Si-28

> E2 transitions in Si-30 are investigated in relation with intrinsic deformations based on a method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. By comparing E2 transition strengths in the mirror nuclei Si-30 and S-30, transition matrix amplitudes M_p and M_n for protons and neutrons are discussed in mirror analysis. Particular attention is paid to the M_n/M_p ratio in the transition from the 2^+_2 state to the 0^+_1 state. The M_n/M_p ratio in Mg-26 and Si-26 is also investigated. It is found that the proton dominance in the transition 2^+_2 -> 0^+_1 in Si-30 and Si-26 originates in the oblate trend of the Z=14 proton structure.

## Key claim
The proton dominance (small M_n/M_p ratio) observed in the 2^+_2 → 0^+_1 transition of 30Si and 26Si originates specifically in the oblate trend of the Z=14 proton structure, which leads to a triaxial proton shape that decouples from the neutron structure.

## Method
The author applied antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations with parity projection and angular-momentum projection to 30Si, 30S, 26Mg, and 26Si. Two local minimum intrinsic states were obtained for each nucleus, and the transition matrix amplitudes M_p and M_n were computed from the superposed wave functions and compared with experimental data via mirror analysis, where M_n in one nucleus is assumed equal to M_p in its mirror partner.

## Key numbers
- Experimental M_n/M_p ratio for 2^+_2 → 0^+_1 in 30Si: 0.52 ± 0.03
- Experimental M_n/M_p ratio for 2^+_2 → 0^+_1 in 26Si: 0.50 ± 0.07
- Experimental B(E2) for 2^+_2 → 0^+_1 in 30Si: 1.7 ± 0.5 e^2 fm^4
- Experimental B(E2) for 2^+_2 → 0^+_1 in 26Si: 1.6 ± 0.5 e^2 fm^4
- Experimental B(E2) for 2^+_1 → 0^+_1 in 30Si: 8.5 ± 1.1 e^2 fm^4
- Experimental B(E2) for 2^+_1 → 0^+_1 in 26Si: 15.4 ± 1.5 e^2 fm^4
- Experimental M_n/M_p ratio for 2^+_1 → 0^+_1 in 30Si: 1.1 (close to unity)
- Experimental M_n/M_p ratio for 2^+_1 → 0^+_1 in 26Si: 0.94 (close to unity)
- Deformation parameters for 30Si state (A): (β_p,γ_p) = (0.18, 0.01π), (β_n,γ_n) = (0.20, 0.00π)
- Deformation parameters for 30Si state (C): (β_p,γ_p) = (0.27, 0.28π), (β_n,γ_n) = (0.21, 0.15π)

## Context
This work extends the author's previous AMD studies of deformation in 28Si, 24Ne, 22O, and 20C (Kanada-En'yo, 2004) and of C isotopes (Kanada-En'yo, 2004), where isovector triaxiality and decoupled proton-neutron shapes were first suggested. The proton dominance in the 2^+_2 → 0^+_1 transition had been experimentally determined by Alexander (1982) and Sciuccati (1985) and qualitatively reproduced by shell model calculations (Brown, 1982). The present AMD analysis provides a deformation-based interpretation: the oblate Z=14 proton structure creates a triaxial side band whose rotation yields dominant proton contributions. This mechanism is analogous to the predicted proton dominance in 10C and 16C from the oblate Z=6 proton structure. The work enables future experimental searches for the 2^+_2 state in 10C and 16C to test the predicted M_n/M_p quenching.
