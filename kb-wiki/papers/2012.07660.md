# Dynamical instabilities for model with point-coupling interactions: Vlasov formalism method

> We explore the effects of the density dependence of symmetry energy on the dynamical instabilities and crust-core phase transition in the cold and warm neutron stars in the RMF theory with point-coupling interactions using the Vlasov approach. The role of the temperature and neutrino trapping has also been considered. The distillation effect, crust-core transition density and pressure, the cluster size and growth rates have been discussed. The present work shows that the slope of symmetry energy, temperature, and neutrino trapping have obvious effects.

## Key claim

The slope of symmetry energy, temperature, and neutrino trapping have obvious effects on dynamical instabilities and crust-core phase transitions in neutron stars modeled with point-coupling interactions, but the specific behavior of the transition pressure depends nontrivially on the slope parameter L and can be model-dependent, with different trends observed for neutrino-free versus neutrino-trapped matter.

## Method

The authors used the Vlasov formalism method within the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with point-coupling interactions (DD-PC1 parametrization) to study small-amplitude density oscillations in neutron star matter. They varied the density dependence of symmetry energy by adjusting isovector channel parameters while keeping the symmetry energy at saturation fixed at 33 MeV, and solved the linearized Vlasov equations to obtain dispersion relations for collective modes. The unstable modes (imaginary frequencies) were analyzed to determine transition densities, pressures, growth rates, and cluster sizes for both cold and warm neutron star matter, including the effects of neutrino trapping.

## Key numbers

- For the original DD-PC1 parametrization at T=0 MeV, the thermodynamical method gives transition densities of 0.079 fm^{-3} (neutrino-free) and 0.093 fm^{-3} (neutrino-trapped), while the Vlasov formalism gives about 10% smaller values: 0.072 fm^{-3} and 0.084 fm^{-3}, respectively.
- The smallest cluster size is about 4 MeV for neutrino-free matter and about 3 MeV when including neutrinos.
- For neutrino-free matter, no nonhomogeneous phase exists at temperatures above 4 MeV for L > 70 MeV, and even for very low L, no nonhomogeneous phase exists at T=12 MeV.
- For neutrino-trapped matter with lepton fraction Y_l=0.4, the nonhomogeneous phase still exists until T=12 MeV.
- At T=0 MeV for neutrino-free matter, the transition pressure P_t increases with increasing L for L less than about 55 MeV and the opposite behavior occurs for L greater than about 55 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of core-crust transition densities using the DD-PC1 parametrization at zero temperature within the thermodynamical approach (Moustakidis 2010) by applying the Vlasov formalism method, which incorporates surface and Coulomb effects and is more realistic than the thermodynamical approach. The anticorrelation of transition density n_t and slope L has been found in various earlier studies (Oyamatsu 2007, Xu 2009, Ducoin 2011, Grill 2012, Suleiman 2014, Bao 2014, Bao 2015, Wei 2018, Goncalves 2019). The results enable better understanding of neutron star crust properties and protoneutron star evolution, particularly regarding how temperature and neutrino trapping affect the existence and characteristics of nonhomogeneous phases.
