# Theoretical study of incoherent phi photoproduction on a deuteron target

> We study the photoproduction of phi mesons in deuteron, paying attention to the modification of the cross section from bound protons to the free ones with the aim of comparing with recent results at LEPS. For this purpose we take into account Fermi motion in single scattering and rescattering of the phi to account for phi absorption on a second nucleon as well as the rescattering of the proton. We find that the contribution of the double scattering is much smaller than the typical cross section of gamma p to phi p in free space, which implies a very small screening of the phi production in deuteron. The contribution from the proton rescattering, on the other hand, is found to be not negligible compared to the cross section of gamma p to phi p in free space, and leads to a moderate reduction of the phi photoproduction cross section on a deuteron at forward angles if LEPS set up is taken into account. The Fermi motion allows contribution of the single scattering in regions forbidden by phase space in the free case. In particular, we find that for momentum transferred squared close to the maximum value, the Fermi motion changes drastically the shape of d sigma / dt, to the point that the ratio of this cross section to the free one becomes very sensitive to the precise value of t chosen, or the size of the bin used in an experimental analysis. Hence, this particular region of t does not seem the most indicated to find effects of a possible phi absorption in the deuteron. This reaction is studied theoretically as a function of t and the effect of the experimental angular cuts at LEPS is also discussed, providing guidelines for future experimental analyses of the reaction.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the double scattering contribution to incoherent phi photoproduction on the deuteron is much smaller than the free proton cross section, implying very small screening of phi production in the deuteron; however, the proton rescattering contribution is not negligible and leads to a moderate reduction of the cross section at forward angles when the LEPS experimental setup is considered.

## Method

The authors calculated the gamma d to phi p n reaction using a phenomenological amplitude for the elementary gamma p to phi p process, parameterized with an exponential t-dependence and an s-dependent strength factor fitted to experimental data. They included single scattering with Fermi motion using a realistic deuteron wave function parameterization, double scattering where the phi rescatters on the second nucleon, and proton rescattering on the neutron, with the double scattering amplitude evaluated by taking the phi on-shell in the propagator and using the imaginary part of the phi n to phi n amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The slope parameter for the phi photoproduction amplitude is b = 3.38 GeV^-2, taken from Mibe (2005)
- The binding energy per nucleon in the deuteron is taken as B_{1/2} = 1.112 MeV
- At E_gamma^lab = 1.6 GeV, the free proton t range is t_min = -0.69 GeV^2 and t_max = -0.36 GeV^2, while the deuteron t_phi range is t_phi,min = -2.17 GeV^2 and t_phi,max = -0.19 GeV^2
- The double scattering interference around t_max is less than 7% of the single scattering contribution
- The double scattering alone contributes less than one per thousand of the single scattering cross section

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of phi photoproduction on nucleons and nuclei, following experimental measurements at LEPS (Chang, 2009) and Jefferson Lab (Haiyan). It contrasts with the work of Titov (2007) on near-threshold phi photoproduction on the deuteron, and supports the findings of Haiyan that the extracted differential cross section is consistent with quasifree mechanisms. The results provide guidelines for future experimental analyses by identifying that the region near t_max is not well suited for studying phi absorption effects due to strong Fermi motion distortions.
