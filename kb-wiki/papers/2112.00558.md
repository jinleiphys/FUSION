# Maximum latent heat of neutron star matter without GR

> We show how the specific latent heat is relevant to characterize the first-order phase transitions in neutron stars. Our current knowledge of this dynamical quantity strongly depends on the uncertainty bands of Chiral Perturbation Theory and of pQCD calculations and can be used to diagnose progress on the equation of state. We state what is known to be hadron-model independent and without feedback from neutron star observations and, therefore, they can be used to test General Relativity as well as theories beyond GR, such as modified gravity.

## Key claim

The specific latent heat of a first-order phase transition in neutron star matter can be bounded from above using only hadron physics (Chiral Perturbation Theory and perturbative QCD) without input from General Relativity or astrophysical observations, yielding a maximum value around 0.9 in natural units; this bound is less restrictive than the Seidov limit from General Relativity but applies to any theory of gravity.

## Method

The author constructs equations of state (EoS) for neutron star matter by interpolating between low-density Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) bands and high-density perturbative QCD (pQCD) bands, enforcing only thermodynamic stability (monotony) and causality (speed of sound between 0 and 1). A 500² grid samples the allowed region in the (ε, P) plane, and at each grid point the maximum possible first-order phase transition (a stretch with P' = 0) is computed without violating the constraints. The analysis is then refined by additionally imposing causality constraints in the (μ, n) plane from Komoltsev (2021), which further restricts the allowed EoS band.

## Key numbers

- Maximum latent heat found: L|_ε ≈ 0.79 (dimensionless, in natural units) for an EoS matched to nuclear matter with maximum slope c²_s ≈ 1 at 1.2 n_sat, at energy density ε ≈ 11 ε_sat.
- After applying new (μ, n) plane constraints, the maximum latent heat increases to L = 0.88.
- The low-density ChPT approximation is used up to n_m = 1.2 n_sat, where n_sat ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³.
- The high-density pQCD regime starts at baryon chemical potential μ ≥ 2.6 GeV, corresponding to energy density about 15 GeV/fm³.
- For the softest EoS (c²_s = 0.09), the two definitions of latent heat differ by 4% at ε = 4.2 ε_s; for the stiffest EoS (c²_s = 1), they differ by 12% at ε = 2.6 ε_s.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous EoS construction (Oter, 2019) by incorporating new causality constraints from Komoltsev (2021) in the (μ, n) plane, which further restrict the allowed EoS band. The maximum latent heat from hadron physics alone is compared to the Seidov limit (Seidov, 1971), which is a small-core approximation valid only in General Relativity; the hadronic bound is less tight but applies to any theory of gravity, enabling tests of General Relativity and modified gravity theories using neutron star observations.
