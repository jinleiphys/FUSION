# Transport coefficients in a finite volume Polyakov--Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> Finite size consideration of matter significantly affects transport coefficients like shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, electrical conductivity, which we have investigated here in the framework of the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. Owing to the basic quantum mechanics, a non-zero lower momentum cut-off is implemented in momentum integrations, used in the expressions of constituent quark masses and transport coefficients. When the system size decreases, the values of these trans- port coefficients are enhanced in low temperature range. At high temperature domain, shear viscos- ity and electrical conductivity become independent of system sizes. Whereas, bulk viscosity, which is associated with scale violating quantities of the system, faces some non-trivial size dependence in this regime. In the phenomenological direction, our microscopic estimations can also be linked with the macroscopic outcome, based on dissipative hydrodynamical simulation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that finite system size significantly enhances shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and electrical conductivity in the low temperature range, while at high temperature shear viscosity and electrical conductivity become independent of system size; however, this conclusion is conditional on using a simplified lower momentum cut-off approximation rather than a full discrete momentum summation, and the authors note this is a qualitative first study.

## Method

The authors implement finite size effects in the 2+1 flavor Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model by introducing a lower momentum cut-off of λ = π/R in momentum integrations, where R is the linear dimension of a cubical volume, replacing the infinite sum over discretized momentum values with continuous integration from this infra-red cut-off. Transport coefficients (shear viscosity η, bulk viscosity ζ, electrical conductivity σ) are calculated using relaxation time approximation expressions derived from kinetic theory, with the constituent quark masses and Polyakov loop fields determined self-consistently from the finite-volume thermodynamic potential. The relaxation time is taken as a constant τ = 1 fm for all calculations.

## Key numbers

- For R = 2 fm at T = 170 MeV, shear viscosity η shows approximately 70% enhancement relative to the infinite volume case (Δη/η = -70%, where negative Δ indicates enhancement)
- For R = 2 fm at T = 170 MeV, the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s shows approximately 40% enhancement (Δ(η/s)/(η/s) = -40%)
- For R = 2 fm at T = 170 MeV, electrical conductivity σ shows approximately 80% enhancement (Δσ/σ = -80%)
- For R = 2 fm at T = 200 MeV, electrical conductivity σ shows approximately 8% reduction (Δσ/σ = +8%) while shear viscosity η shows approximately 4% enhancement (Δη/η = -4%)
- System sizes considered: R = ∞, 4 fm, 3 fm, and 2 fm
- Temperature range explored: approximately 0.100 to 0.400 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous finite-size studies of thermodynamic properties in the PNJL model by Bhattacharyya et al. (2015) to transport coefficients, and complements recent investigations of finite volume effects on transport in the hadron resonance gas model by Samanta et al. and Sarkar et al., which are only valid in the hadronic temperature domain. The PNJL model approach allows exploration across both hadronic and quark phases, including finite volume effects on the quark condensate that produce major changes in transport coefficients. This enables future connections to dissipative hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions where the produced fireball has finite system volume depending on colliding nuclei size, center-of-mass energy, and collision centrality.
