# Neutron skin of 48Ca deduced from interaction cross section

> The neutron skin thickness of 48Ca was deduced from the interaction cross section by adopting a microscopic optical potential. The optical potential used was constructed by folding a chiral g matrix and the Skyrme mean-field densities renormalized by considering the information of the interaction cross section. The result was R_skin = 0.139 \pm 0.058 fm.

## Key claim

The neutron skin thickness of ⁴⁸Ca is deduced as R_skin = 0.139 ± 0.058 fm using a microscopic optical potential constructed from a chiral g matrix and Skyrme mean-field densities renormalized to reproduce the interaction cross section, which is consistent with the reference value of 0.146 ± 0.060 fm from Tanaka et al. (2019) but with the caveat that the renormalization procedure is necessary to match experimental proton radii and interaction cross sections.

## Method

The authors constructed a microscopic optical potential by folding a chiral g matrix (based on N³LO two-body and NNLO three-body forces) with Skyrme mean-field densities from a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation using the SLy7 parameter set. The proton density was first scaled to reproduce the experimental proton RMS radius from electron scattering (R_p = 3.385 fm), then the neutron density was scaled so that the calculated interaction cross section σ_I for ⁴⁸Ca + ¹²C scattering at 280 MeV/nucleon matched the experimental data within its error bar.

## Key numbers

- R_skin = 0.139 ± 0.058 fm for ⁴⁸Ca, deduced from the double-folding calculation with renormalized densities
- Direct Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation with SLy7 yields (R_n, R_p, R_skin) = (3.600, 3.447, 0.153) fm
- Renormalized neutron radius R_n = 3.524 ± 0.058 fm
- Reference data from Tanaka et al. (2019): R_skin = 0.146 ± 0.060 fm using the Glauber model with Woods-Saxon density
- Gogny D1S force yields R_skin = 0.105 ± 0.06 fm
- High-resolution E1 polarizability experiment: 0.17 ± 0.03 fm (Birkhan 2016)
- Ab initio coupled-cluster calculation: 0.135 ± 0.015 fm (Hagen 2015)

## Context

This work extends the method previously applied to ²⁰⁸Pb by Tagami et al. (2020) and PhysRevC (2021), which extracted neutron skin thickness from reaction cross sections using microscopic optical potentials and obtained results consistent with the PREX II parity-violating electron scattering measurement (Adhikari 2021). The result for ⁴⁸Ca suggests a rather soft symmetry energy slope parameter L around 27 MeV, in contrast to the stiffer values implied by the ²⁰⁸Pb results, and the authors note that a precision datum from the ongoing CREX project will provide further constraints.
