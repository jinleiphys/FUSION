# Model of Centauro and strangelet production in heavy ion collisions

> We discuss the phenomenological model of Centauro event production in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions. This model makes quantitative predictions for kinematic observables, baryon number and mass of the Centauro fireball and its decay products. Centauros decay mainly to nucleons, strange hyperons and possibly strangelets. Simulations of Centauro events for the CASTOR detector in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies are performed. The signatures of these events are discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper presents a quantitative phenomenological model of Centauro event production in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, assuming that a primary fireball with high baryochemical potential and moderate temperature forms in the projectile fragmentation region and evolves through strangeness enrichment into a slightly strange quark matter fireball that decays into baryons and possibly strangelets. The model's predictions depend critically on the assumed values of the baryochemical potential (mu_b = 1.5-3.0 GeV), temperature (T = 130-250 MeV), and the rapidity shift due to nuclear stopping (Delta y_fb = 2.0-3.5), which are not uniquely determined by the model.

## Method

The authors constructed a Monte Carlo event generator (CNGEN) based on a phenomenological model where the Centauro fireball's baryon number is determined from the nuclear overlap geometry in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.5 x A TeV. The fireball's thermodynamic properties (energy density, quark number density, volume, mass) are calculated using first-order perturbative QCD with a strong coupling constant alpha_s = 0.3 at critical temperature T_c = 170 MeV and strangeness equilibration factor gamma_s = 0.4. The fireball evolution proceeds through kaon emission from s-bar s pairs, followed by isotropic decay of the remaining strange quark matter fireball into baryons and strangelets using the Jetset event generator, with simulations performed for the CASTOR detector geometry covering pseudorapidities 5.6 < eta < 7.0.

## Key numbers

- Baryon number of the Centauro fireball in central Pb+Pb collisions (b = 0 fm): N_b = 186, with a factor 0.9 applied to exclude boundary contributions
- For central collisions at mu_b = 1.8 GeV and T = 190 MeV: fireball mass M_fb = 466 GeV/c^2, energy density epsilon = 7.7 GeV/fm^3, quark number density n_q = 9.2 fm^{-3}, volume V_fb = 61 fm^3
- For central collisions at mu_b = 1.8 GeV and T = 250 MeV: fireball mass M_fb = 607 GeV/c^2, energy density epsilon = 13.6 GeV/fm^3, number of emitted kaons N(K^{+,0}) = 50
- Maximum rapidity for central collisions with N_b = 186, T = 190 MeV, mu_b = 1.8 GeV: y_max = 7.69
- Average transverse momentum of hadrons in Centauro events: <p_T> = 1.34 GeV/c (mu_b=1.8 GeV, T=190 MeV), 1.47 GeV/c (mu_b=1.8 GeV, T=250 MeV), and 1.75 GeV/c (mu_b=3.0 GeV, T=250 MeV); compared to <p_T> = 0.44 GeV/c in conventional HIJING events
- Mean ratio of hadron to total multiplicity: <N_h / N_tot> = 0.93 for mu_b=1.8 GeV, T=190 MeV
- Mean ratio of summed hadronic to total energy: <sum E_h / sum E_tot> = 0.99 for mu_b=1.8 GeV, T=190 MeV
- Geometrical detection efficiency for charged hadrons in CASTOR ranges from 0.35 to 0.46 depending on model parameters; for strangelets from 0.36 to 0.67
- Average detected charged hadron multiplicity in Centauro events: 30-60; in conventional HIJING events: approximately 1000 (about 20 times higher)
- Recoil system mass fraction M_rec / sqrt(s_aa) ranges from 0.93 to 0.98 for Delta y_fb = 2.0 to 3.5

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological model of Centauro events originally developed from cosmic ray observations (Lattes 1980, Cosmic) and formalized by Panagiotou and collaborators (Pana89, Pana92, Pana94) to make quantitative predictions for LHC energies. The model enables experimental searches for Centauro events and strangelets using the CASTOR detector in CMS at the LHC, providing specific signatures such as low charged-particle multiplicity (30-60 compared to ~1000 in conventional events), high mean transverse momentum (1.34-1.75 GeV/c versus 0.44 GeV/c), and near-absence of electromagnetic components. The simulations are compared with conventional events from the HIJING generator, and the results inform the design of experimental searches for exotic quark matter states in heavy ion collisions.
