# Core-collapse supernova equations of state based on neutron star observations

> Many of the currently available equations of state for core-collapse supernova simulations give large neutron star radii and do not provide large enough neutron star masses, both of which are inconsistent with some recent neutron star observations. In addition, one of the critical uncertainties in the nucleon-nucleon interaction, the nuclear symmetry energy, is not fully explored by the currently available equations of state. In this article, we construct two new equations of state which match recent neutron star observations and provide more flexibility in studying the dependence on nuclear matter properties. The equations of state are also provided in tabular form, covering a wide range in density, temperature and asymmetry, suitable for astrophysical simulations. These new equations of state are implemented into our spherically symmetric core-collapse supernova model, which is based on general relativistic radiation hydrodynamics with three-flavor Boltzmann neutrino transport. The results are compared with commonly used equations of state in supernova simulations of 15 and 40 solar mass progenitors. We do not find any simple correlations between individual nuclear matter properties at saturation and the outcome of these simulations. However, the new equations of state lead to the most compact neutron stars among the relativistic mean-field models which we considered. The new models also obey the previously observed correlation between the time to black hole formation and the maximum mass of an s=4 neutron star.

## Key claim

The authors construct two new equations of state (SFHo and SFHx) that are consistent with recent neutron star observations, but they do not find any simple correlations between individual nuclear matter properties at saturation and the outcome of core-collapse supernova simulations.

## Method

The authors parameterize nucleonic matter using a new relativistic mean field (RMF) model with 17 parameters, and describe nuclei and non-uniform nuclear matter using the statistical model from Hempel (2009). The equations of state are implemented into the spherically symmetric AGILE-BOLTZTRAN supernova model, which uses general relativistic radiation hydrodynamics with three-flavor Boltzmann neutrino transport, and simulations are performed for 15 and 40 solar mass progenitors.

## Key numbers

- SFHo has symmetry energy J = 31.57 MeV and symmetry energy slope L = 47.10 MeV; SFHx has J = 28.67 MeV and L = 23.18 MeV
- SFHo gives a 1.4 solar mass neutron star radius of 11.88 km; SFHx gives 11.97 km
- SFHo has maximum neutron star mass 2.059 solar masses at T=0; SFHx has 2.130 solar masses
- SFHo has nuclear incompressibility K = 245.4 MeV; SFHx has K = 238.8 MeV
- The new models give binding energies for 208Pb of -7.76 MeV (SFHo) and -7.87 MeV (SFHx), both within 2% of experimental values
- The maximum mass for stars with constant entropy s=4 is 2.27 solar masses for SFHo and 2.36 solar masses for SFHx

## Context

This work extends the equation of state comparison study of Hempel (2012) by adding the new SFHo and SFHx parameterizations, and addresses the problem that commonly used supernova equations of state like LS (Lattimer, 1991) and STOS (Shen, 1998) give neutron star radii too large and maximum masses too small to match observations from Demorest (2010) and Steiner (2010). The new equations of state enable more flexible study of the dependence on nuclear matter properties, particularly the symmetry energy, and provide the most compact neutron stars among the relativistic mean-field models considered.
