# Particle Correlations in Jets

> We study particle correlations in high energy jets by comparing the measured energy-energy correlator (EEC) with that constructed from two individual energy flows with respect to the jet axis. This comparison demonstrates that genuine correlations exists for small angle and moderate/large angle, indicating that they are coming from correlated splitting. This method will provide a unique tool to disentangle different physics, by comparing the genuine correlations in jet EEC between heavy ion collisions and proton-proton collisions. It will help to expose the medium modification of parton splitting in hot QCD medium. On the other hand, the medium responses are expected to be canceled out in the genuine correlations.

## Key claim

Genuine two-particle correlations exist in both the small-angle (non-perturbative TMD fragmentation) and moderate-to-large-angle (collinear splitting) regions of the jet energy-energy correlator (EEC), and these correlations can be isolated by subtracting a "trivial" EEC constructed from the product of single-particle energy flows, with the caveat that the trivial correlator requires a normalization factor (approximately 0.85 for typical LHC jet energies) to match the full EEC normalization.

## Method

The authors define the full EEC inside a jet as an energy-weighted pair distribution over all particle pairs at relative angle θ, and separately construct a "trivial" EEC from the convolution of two single-particle energy flow distributions (Eq. 8) or equivalently from mixed jets (Eq. 9). The genuine correlation is extracted as the difference between the full and trivial EECs (Eq. 11), and a ratio of the two distributions (Eq. 12) is used to expose correlation strength across all angular regions. The analysis is performed using PYTHIA8.306 event generator simulations for jets at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- The normalization factor for the trivial correlation is N_trivial ≈ 0.85 for typical LHC jet energies, equal to the integral of the full EEC over θ for a particular jet energy range.
- The ratio R_EEC(θ) shows stronger correlations at both small and moderate angles for all jet energies studied, with higher jet energies producing larger ratios in these regions.
- Between the small-angle and moderate-angle regions, lower jet energies yield ratios closer to 1, indicating the measured EEC approaches the trivial EEC in that intermediate angular range.

## Context

This work extends the decades-old study of energy-energy correlators in QCD, originally introduced by Basham et al. (1978) for e+e- annihilation, and connects to recent applications of EECs to jet substructure in pp and heavy-ion collisions at LHC and RHIC. The method of subtracting uncorrelated pairs via mixed events is analogous to techniques used in particle interferometry for source size measurements (Wiedemann, 1999). The three characteristic angular regions identified (free hadrons, transition, collinear) correspond to those classified by Komiske et al. (2022). The proposed double ratio between AA and pp collisions (Eq. 14) is intended to isolate medium modification of parton splitting while canceling medium response contributions, and can be extended to multi-point energy correlators.
