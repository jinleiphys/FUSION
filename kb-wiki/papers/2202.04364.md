# Probing the Impact of WIMP Dark Matter on Universal Relations, GW170817 Posterior and Radial Oscillations

> In this study, we investigate the impact of Weakly Interacting Massive Particles (WIMPs) dark matter (DM) on $C-Λ$ universal relations, GW170817 posterior and radial oscillations of neutron stars (NSs) by considering the interactions of uniformly trapped neutralinos as a DM candidate with the hadronic matter through the exchange of the Higgs boson within the framework of the Next-to-Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model (NMSSM). The hadronic equation of state (EOS) is modeled using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism with IOPB-I, G3, and QMC-RMF series parameter sets. Presence of DM softens the EOS at both the background and the perturbation levels that implies a small shift to the left in the posterior accompanied by a much larger jump in the left of the mass-radius curves with increasing DM mass. It is observed that EOSs with DM also satisfy the $C-Λ$ universality relations among their-selves but get slightly shifted to the right in comparison to that without considering DM. Additionally, we find that the inclusion of DM allows the mass-radius ($M-R$) curves to remain consistent with observational constraints for HESS J1731-347, indicating the possibility of classifying it as a dark matter-admixed neutron star (DMANS). Moreover, we explore the impact of DM on the radial oscillations of pulsating stars and investigate the stability of NSs. The results demonstrate a positive correlation between the mass of DM and the frequencies of radial oscillation modes.

# Probing the Impact of WIMP Dark Matter on Universal Relations, GW170817 Posterior and Radial Oscillations

## Key claim

The presence of Weakly Interacting Massive Particle (WIMP) dark matter inside neutron stars softens the equation of state and shifts the C-Lambda universal relations slightly to the right compared to the no-dark-matter case, but the qualitative universal behavior is preserved; the mass-radius curves with dark matter remain consistent with the observational constraints for HESS J1731-347, suggesting it could be a dark matter-admixed neutron star.

## Method

The authors model the hadronic equation of state using the relativistic mean-field formalism with IOPB-I, G3, and QMC-RMF series parameter sets. They consider neutralinos as WIMP dark matter candidates interacting with hadronic matter through Higgs boson exchange within the Next-to-Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model, with a Higgs mass of 40 GeV and Higgs-nucleon coupling parameter f=0.3. The dark matter-admixed neutron star equations of state are obtained by combining hadronic, dark matter, and leptonic contributions under mean-field approximations, then solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for mass-radius profiles and the Sturm-Liouville equation for radial oscillation frequencies.

## Key numbers

- Dark matter Fermi momentum fixed at k_f^DM = 0.06 GeV, assuming baryonic density of neutron star about 10^3 times greater than dark matter density inside the star
- Higgs boson mass M_h = 40 GeV chosen to keep Yukawa coupling in perturbative regime (y < 1)
- Higgs vacuum expectation value v = 246 GeV
- Higgs-nucleon coupling parameter f = 0.3 based on lattice computations
- For M_DM = 24 GeV, all mass-radius curves satisfy the HESS J1731-347 observational bound (mass 0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17} M_sun, radius 10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78} km)
- Fitting coefficients for C-Lambda relation at M_DM = 0 GeV: c_0 = 0.34791, c_1 = -0.06568, c_2 = -0.00358, c_3 = 0.00136, c_4 = -6.90e-05, chi^2_r = 6.0516e-6
- Fitting coefficients for C-Lambda relation at M_DM = 24 GeV: c_0 = 0.35821, c_1 = -0.07416, c_2 = 9.26e-04, c_3 = 6.27e-04, c_4 = -3.31e-05, chi^2_r = 7.9838e-6

## Context

This work extends previous studies of WIMP dark matter effects on neutron stars by Panotopoulos (2017), Das (2019), and Quddus (2020) within the relativistic mean-field formalism, and builds on the two-fluid approach of Ivanytskyi (2020) which constrained fermionic dark matter mass to about 60 GeV for a 2 M_sun neutron star. The study also connects to the work of Chakravarti (2020) on extra dimensions and neutron star properties, where extra dimensions shifted mass-radius curves to the right (opposite to the dark matter effect found here). The results enable future gravitational wave observations to potentially distinguish dark matter-admixed neutron stars from ordinary neutron stars through shifts in the C-Lambda universal relations and radial oscillation frequencies.
