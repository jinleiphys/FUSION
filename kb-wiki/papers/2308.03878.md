# Liouvillian Dynamics of the Open Schwinger Model: String Breaking and Kinetic Dissipation in a Thermal Medium

> Understanding the dynamics of bound state formation is one of the fundamental questions in confining quantum field theories such as Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). One hadronization mechanism that has garnered significant attention is the breaking of a string initially connecting a fermion and an anti-fermion. Deepening our understanding of real-time string-breaking dynamics with simpler, lower dimensional models like the Schwinger model can improve our understanding of the hadronization process in QCD and other confining systems found in condensed matter and statistical systems. In this paper, we consider the string-breaking dynamics within the Schwinger model and investigate its modification inside a thermal medium, treating the Schwinger model as an open quantum system coupled to a thermal environment. Within the regime of weak coupling between the system and environment, the real-time evolution of the system can be described by a Lindblad evolution equation. We analyze the Liouvillian gaps of this Lindblad equation and the time dependence of the system's von Neumann entropy. We observe that the late-time relaxation rate decreases as the environment correlation length increases. Moreover, when the environment correlation length is infinite, the system exhibits two steady states, one in each of the sectors with definite charge-conjugation-parity (CP) quantum numbers. For parameter regimes where an initial string breaks in vacuum, we observe a delay of the string breaking in the medium, due to kinetic dissipation effects. Conversely, in regimes where an initial string remains intact in vacuum time evolution, we observe string breaking (melting) in the thermal medium. We further discuss how the Liouvillian dynamics of the open Schwinger model can be simulated on quantum computers and provide an estimate of the associated Trotter errors.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the real-time string-breaking dynamics in the Schwinger model are modified inside a thermal medium, with the specific outcome depending on the parameter regime: in regimes where an initial string breaks in vacuum, the medium delays the breaking due to kinetic dissipation effects, while in regimes where the string remains intact in vacuum, the thermal medium induces string breaking (melting). These results are obtained within the weak-coupling, high-temperature (quantum Brownian motion) limit where the evolution is Markovian and described by a Lindblad equation.

## Method

The authors treat the Schwinger model (1+1D QED) as an open quantum system coupled to a thermal scalar field environment via a Yukawa-type interaction. They work in the quantum Brownian motion limit (high environment temperature relative to system energy levels) where memory effects are negligible, allowing a Lindblad master equation description. The Schwinger model is discretized on a spatial lattice using staggered fermions with open boundary conditions, and the electric field flux is truncated at |ℓ_n|_max = 1. The environment correlator D(x) is modeled with three functional forms (delta function, Gaussian, and constant) to test dependence on correlation length. The Liouvillian spectrum is computed by diagonalizing the Lindblad superoperator, and the real-time evolution of the von Neumann entropy and string-breaking dynamics are studied numerically.

## Key numbers

* For the lattice parameters used (N=4 sites, N_f=8 fermion sites, e=0.8, m=0.5, β=0.1, D_0=1), the Liouvillian eigenvalues are plotted for different environment correlator types, with the real parts ranging from 0 to approximately -8.5 (the constant correlator case has additional eigenvalues at more negative real values).
* The Liouvillian gap Δ₁ (absolute value of the real part of the first non-zero eigenvalue) decreases as the environment correlation length increases, vanishing in the limit of infinite correlation length (constant correlator) where two steady states emerge, one in each CP sector.
* The second Liouvillian gap Δ₂ reaches a non-zero value in the infinite correlation length limit, characterizing the relaxation dynamics when the first gap vanishes.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the static string tension in the Schwinger model at finite temperature and chemical potential (PhysRevD.19.1188, Pisarski (1982), Buyens (2016), Xie (2022)) to the dynamical case of real-time string breaking in a thermal medium. The open quantum system framework builds on prior work for quarkonium dynamics inside the quark-gluon plasma (Akamatsu (2011), Brambilla (2016), Yao (2018), among others). The results enable future studies of dissipative phase transitions in field theories and provide a starting point for quantum simulations of open systems, with the authors estimating that Trotter errors from the Lindblad evolution may not increase the total error compared to vacuum calculations due to error cancellations.
