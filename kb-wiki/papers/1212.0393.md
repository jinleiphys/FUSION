# Neutron resonances in few-body systems and the EOS of neutron star crust

> The effective interactions formed by neutron rescattering between the nuclei fixed in nodes of the crystalline lattice of neutron star crusts have been considered. In the case of two-body resonances in neutron-nucleus subsystems new neutron resonances of few-body nature come into existence in the overdense crystal under certain conditions. The energies and widths of new resonances get additional dependence on the lattice parameters. The effective interactions result in nonlinear correction to the equation of state determined by the balance of gravitational, Coulomb and nuclear resonance forces. This leads to resonant oscillations of density in the accordant layers of crusts that are accompanied by oscillations of gamma radiation. The phenomena may clarify some processes connected with few-body neutron resonances in neutron star crusts, that have influence on the microstructure of pulsar impulses.

# Neutron resonances in few-body systems and the EOS of neutron star crust

## Key claim

New few-body neutron resonances arise in overdense crystalline structures of neutron star crusts when the lattice parameter d approaches specific resonance values d_r, but only under the condition that two-body neutron-nucleus resonances already exist in the subsystems. These resonances produce effective interactions that introduce a nonlinear correction to the equation of state, potentially leading to local density oscillations and associated gamma radiation that may explain microstructure in pulsar impulses.

## Method

The authors model neutron scattering on subsystems of two or more heavy nuclei fixed at lattice nodes in the crystalline crust, using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation with neutron-nucleus t-matrices taken in separable or Breit-Wigner form. The effective interaction between lattice nuclei is derived from the three-body scattering problem, where the determinant D = 0 in the complex k_0 plane defines the resonant states of the system. The pressure balance equation is then modified to include the resonance contribution P^{ef}_{res} alongside gravitational and Coulomb pressures, and the Hellmann-Feynman relationship is used to determine the effective energy density from the neutron wave functions.

## Key numbers

- For neutrons with energies E ~ 100 keV, resonances arise when d_r ~ 50 fm; for E ~ 100 eV, d_r ~ 400 fm
- The two-body (n + 57Fe) resonance used in the calculation has energy E_R = 52.7 keV and width Gamma = 0.24 eV
- Mass densities in the outer crust range from 10^6 g·cm^{-3} < rho < 4 x 10^11 g·cm^{-3}
- Mass densities in the inner crust range from 4 x 10^11 g·cm^{-3} > rho > 1.4 x 10^14 g·cm^{-3}
- Neutron densities used in the pressure calculations are xi_n = 6 x 10^{-5} fm^{-3} and xi_n = 1.44 x 10^{-4} fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the few-body neutron scattering formalism developed by Takibayev (no year given) to the astrophysical context of neutron star crusts, where the crystalline lattice provides the necessary inter-nuclear distances (d_r ~ 50-400 fm) that cannot be achieved under normal terrestrial conditions. The paper builds on the standard model of neutron star crust structure from Shapiro and Teukolsky (no year given), where electron capture reactions produce neutron-rich nuclei and free neutrons in the inner crust. The results suggest that resonant oscillations in local crustal layers could generate comb-like radiation patterns, potentially explaining the complex microstructure observed in pulsar impulses, though the authors note that the resonance forces are significant only when d is close to d_res and vanish elsewhere.
