# Centrality and pT dependence of J/psi suppression in proton-nucleus collisions from parton energy loss

> The effects of parton energy loss and pT-broadening in cold nuclear matter on the pT and centrality dependence, at various rapidities, of J/psi suppression in p-A collisions are investigated. Calculations are systematically compared to E866 and PHENIX measurements. The very good agreement between the data and the theoretical expectations further supports pT-broadening and the associated medium-induced parton energy loss as dominant effects in J/psi suppression in high-energy p-A collisions. Predictions for J/psi (and Upsilon) suppression in p-Pb collisions at the LHC are given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that parton energy loss induced by transverse momentum broadening in cold nuclear matter is the dominant mechanism for J/ψ suppression in proton-nucleus collisions, with the caveat that the model's agreement with data relies on a single parameter (the transport coefficient \(\hat{q}_0 = 0.075\) GeV²/fm) previously extracted from E866 data, and that the factorization of energy loss and broadening effects is an approximation valid for the kinematic ranges considered.

## Method

The authors generalize their earlier model by implementing a double shift in the quarkonium energy \(E\) (in the nucleus rest frame) and transverse momentum \(\vec{p}_\perp\) to account for parton energy loss \(\varepsilon\) and transverse momentum broadening \(\Delta \vec{p}_\perp\) of the color octet \(c\bar{c}\) pair propagating through the nucleus. The nuclear modification factor \(R_{\mathrm{pA}}^\psi(y, \pt)\) is computed using a quenching weight \(\mathcal{P}(\varepsilon, E)\) derived from the medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum, with the transport coefficient \(\hat{q}_{\mathrm{A}}\) depending on the parton momentum fraction \(x_{\mathrm{A}}\) and the effective path length \(L_{\mathrm{A}}\) through the target nucleus. The pp cross section is parametrized from existing data, and centrality dependence is incorporated via Glauber model calculations of the average path length for each centrality class.

## Key numbers

- Transport coefficient \(\hat{q}_0 = 0.075\) GeV²/fm, extracted from a fit to E866 data for \(R_{\mathrm{W/Be}}^{\jpsi}\) and used as the only free parameter
- Effective path lengths for minimum bias collisions: \(L_{\mathrm{Be}} = 3.24\) fm, \(L_{\mathrm{Fe}} = 6.62\) fm, \(L_{\mathrm{W}} = 9.35\) fm, \(L_{\mathrm{Au}} = 10.23\) fm, \(L_{\mathrm{Pb}} = 10.11\) fm
- For d–Au at RHIC, centrality class A (0–20%): \(L_{\mathrm{Au}} = 12.87\) fm; class D (60–88%): \(L_{\mathrm{Au}} = 3.84\) fm
- For p–Pb at LHC, centrality class 1 (most central): \(L_{\mathrm{Pb}} = 13.46\) fm; class 4 (most peripheral): \(L_{\mathrm{Pb}} = 3.39\) fm
- J/ψ mass used in calculations: \(M = 3\) GeV; Υ mass: \(M = 9\) GeV
- Strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s = 0.5\) and \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}} = 0.25\) GeV used in the radiation spectrum

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model (Arleo et al., 2012) that described J/ψ suppression as a function of rapidity or \(x_F\) in minimum bias p–A and d–A collisions, now generalizing to the \(\pt\) and centrality dependence at fixed-target (E866), RHIC, and LHC energies. The approach contrasts with models based on nuclear absorption cross sections (Kopeliovich, 2011; Ferreiro, 2012) or modifications of nuclear parton distribution functions, and differs from other parton energy loss treatments (Sharma, 2012) by including interference between initial and final state gluon radiation. The successful description of E866 and PHENIX data across multiple observables enables predictions for J/ψ and Υ suppression in p–Pb collisions at the LHC, providing a baseline for disentangling cold nuclear matter effects from hot QGP effects in heavy-ion collisions.
