# Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions from Effective Field Theory

> We have established a new convergent scheme to treat analytically nucleon-nucleon interactions from a chiral effective field theory. The Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) amplitudes are resummed to fulfill the unitarity or right hand cut to all orders below pion production threshold. This is achieved by matching order by order in the KSW power counting the general expression of a partial wave with resummed unitarity cut, with the inverses of the KSW amplitudes. As a result, a new convergent and systematic KSW expansion is derived for an on-shell interacting kernel \cR in terms of which the partial waves are computed. The agreement with data for the S-waves is fairly good up to laboratory energies around 350 MeV and clearly improves and reestablishes the phenomenological success of the KSW amplitudes when treated within this scheme.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a new convergent scheme for treating nucleon-nucleon interactions analytically from chiral effective field theory by resumming the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) amplitudes to fulfill unitarity to all orders below pion production threshold, but the convergence and phenomenological success is demonstrated primarily for S-waves up to laboratory energies around 350 MeV, while higher partial waves require further corrections.

## Method

The unitarity (right-hand) cut is resummed using a dispersion relation for the inverse of the partial wave amplitude, yielding an on-shell interacting kernel \(\cR\) that is determined by matching order-by-order with the inverses of the KSW amplitudes following the KSW power counting. The partial waves are then computed from the expression \(T = -[\cR^{-1} + g]^{-1}\), where \(g\) contains the unitarity bubble contributions with subtraction constants \(\nu_i\). The matching procedure is performed separately for the \(^1S_0\) elastic channel, the coupled \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) system, and the higher partial waves (\(P\), \(D\), \(^3F_2\), \(^3G_3\)), with the interacting kernel \(\cR\) expanded to the appropriate chiral order.

## Key numbers

- The agreement with data for S-waves is fairly good up to laboratory energies around 350 MeV (center-of-mass three-momentum \(p \lesssim 400\) MeV).
- The pure KSW NNLO calculations badly diverge from experiment for center-of-mass three-momentum above \(\sim 100\) MeV.
- The subtraction constants \(\nu_i\) from a finite cut-off regularization range from 64 MeV (for cut-off \(Q = 100\) MeV) to 670 MeV (for \(Q = 1300\) MeV).
- The unitarity condition applies below the \(NN\pi\) threshold around \(p \simeq 280\) MeV.
- The S-wave KSW amplitudes start at order \(p^{-1}\), while higher partial waves start at order \(p^0\).

## Context

This work extends the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) effective field theory approach (KSW, 1996; NNLO, 1998) by resumming the unitarity cut to address the poor convergence of the original KSW scheme, which failed for momenta above \(\sim 100\) MeV due to large contributions from twice-iterated pion exchange in triplet channels. The method contrasts with Weinberg's approach (Weinberg, 1990; 1991), which solves the Lippmann-Schwinger equation numerically and suffers from cut-off dependence and power-counting inconsistencies, while the present scheme provides analytic amplitudes. The formalism enables systematic improvement order-by-order and fixes many counterterms in terms of effective range expansion parameters \(a_s\) and \(r_0\) for S-waves, with the Nijmegen partial wave analysis (Nijmegen group) used as the data reference.
