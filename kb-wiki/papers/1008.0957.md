# Neutron stars with small radii -- the role of delta resonances

> Recent neutron star observations suggest that the masses and radii of neutron stars may be smaller than previously considered, which would disfavor a purely nucleonic equation of state. In our model, we use a the flavor SU(3) sigma model that includes delta resonances and hyperons in the equation of state. We find that if the coupling of the delta resonances to the vector mesons is slightly smaller than that of the nucleons, we can reproduce both the measured mass-radius relationship and the extrapolated equation of state.

# Neutron stars with small radii -- the role of delta resonances

## Key claim
If the vector-meson coupling of delta resonances is slightly smaller (by about 10%) than that of nucleons, the resulting equation of state can reproduce the small masses and radii reported by Özel et al. (2010), but this agreement depends on which observational analysis is correct; a competing analysis by Steiner et al. (2010) allows larger radii consistent with a purely nucleonic equation of state.

## Method
The authors use a flavor SU(3) sigma-omega model in mean-field approximation, including baryons from the octet (nucleons and hyperons) and the spin-3/2 decuplet (delta resonances and excited hyperons), plus leptons. They introduce a single scaling parameter r_v = g_{Δω} / g_{Nω} to vary the vector coupling of the delta resonations relative to nucleons, solve the equations of motion at T=0, compute the equation of state, and integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for spherical static stars with a standard BPS crust.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.15 particles per fm³, with binding energy B/A = -16 MeV, compressibility K = 297.32 MeV, and asymmetry energy E_sym = 32.5 MeV.
- For r_v around 0.9 (vector coupling of delta resonances reduced by about 10% relative to nucleons), the mass-radius relation agrees with the observational constraints from Özel et al. (2010) of radii between 8 and 10 km and masses between 1.6 and 1.9 solar masses.
- Delta resonances begin to appear at densities between 0.2 and 0.5 particles per fm³ (approximately 1.3 to 3.1 times ρ₀); hyperons appear only at very high densities beyond six times ρ₀.
- For r_v values of 0.7 or lower, the equation of state may yield unphysical results such as delta resonances appearing in the nuclear ground state.

## Context
This work extends earlier SU(3) sigma-omega model studies of nuclear matter (Papazouglou) and neutron stars (Dexheimer, 2008; Dexheimer, Parity Doublet) by explicitly including delta resonances from the spin-3/2 decuplet. It directly addresses the observational tension between the small radii (8–10 km) reported by Özel et al. (2010) and the stiffer purely nucleonic equations of state that produce larger radii. The paper contrasts with the analysis of Steiner et al. (2010), who relaxed assumptions about photospheric radius and found consistency with nucleonic equations of state. The results enable future work to test whether delta resonances, rather than quark cores or dark matter cores as proposed by Drago et al. and Ciarcelluti et al., can explain compact neutron star observations.
