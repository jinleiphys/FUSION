# Scattering of Nucleons in the Classical Skyrme Model

> Classically spinning B=1 Skyrmions can be regarded as approximations to nucleons with quantised spin. Here, we investigate nucleon-nucleon scattering through numerical collisions of spinning Skyrmions. We identify the dineutron/diproton and dibaryon short-lived resonance states, and also the stable deuteron state. Our simulations lead to predictions for the polarisation states occurring in right angle scattering. Videos of our numerical results are shown at http://www.bristoltheory.org/people/david.foster/skyrmevideos/.

# Scattering of Nucleons in the Classical Skyrme Model

## Key claim

Classically spinning B=1 Skyrmions with carefully fixed angular velocities can model nucleon-nucleon scattering, including the deuteron bound state and short-lived resonances, but only when the initial collision speed is sufficient to overcome a repulsive barrier that arises from the spinning motion itself, at zero initial speed, spinning Skyrmions repel even when they represent a proton and neutron that should attract.

## Method

The authors numerically evolved the Skyrme field equations using a finite-difference leap-frog algorithm on a lattice, colliding two classically spinning B=1 Hedgehog Skyrmions with angular velocity |ω| = 0.28 (set to match quantised spin 1/2). Initial configurations were constructed using the product ansatz with Lorentz-boosted Skyrmions, and the Skyrme model was calibrated with pion mass m = 0.7 and Planck's constant ħ = 30.8 in Skyrme units, following the Manton-Lau calibration for Carbon-12.

## Key numbers

- Angular velocity for nucleon spin: |ω| = 0.28 in Skyrme units, derived from ħ = 30.8 and moment of inertia λ = 55.89
- Nucleon mass from calibration: M_N = 1243 MeV (approximately 30% larger than the physical 939 MeV)
- Deuteron mass from rigid-body quantisation: 2324 MeV (compared to experimental 1876 MeV)
- Deuteron rms matter radius from rigid-body quantisation: 1.08 fm (less than half the experimental ~2.14 fm)
- Initial collision speed for deuteron formation: v = 0.2 (in natural units)
- Initial collision speed for dineutron/diproton simulation: v = 0.02
- Relative energy of dineutron resonance: approximately 0.25 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical studies of non-spinning Skyrmion scattering by Foster (2014) and the analytical treatment of spinning Skyrmion scattering at large impact parameters by Gisiger and Paranjape. It also builds on the rigid-body quantisation framework for B=1 and B=2 Skyrmions developed by Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983), Braaten (1988), and Manko (2007). The classical approach provides predictions for polarisation-dependent scattering that could be compared with experimental proton-proton scattering where all particle polarisations are measured, and enables further study of nucleon-nucleon interactions within the Skyrme model without the complexity of full quantum mechanical treatment.
