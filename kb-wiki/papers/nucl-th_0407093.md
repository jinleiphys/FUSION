# Chiral solitons in nuclei: Electromagnetic form factors

> We calculate the electromagnetic form factors of a bound proton. The Chiral Quark-Soliton model provides the quark and antiquark substructure of the proton, which is embedded in nuclear matter. This procedure yields significant modifications of the form factors in the nuclear environment. The sea quarks are almost completely unaffected, and serve to mitigate the valence quark effect. In particular, the ratio of the isoscalar electric to the isovector magnetic form factor decreases by 20% at Q^2=1 GeV^2 at nuclear density, and we do not see a strong enhancement of the magnetic moment.

## Key claim

The Chiral Quark-Soliton model predicts that the ratio of the isoscalar electric to the isovector magnetic form factor of a bound proton decreases by 20% at Q² = 1 GeV² at nuclear density, but the magnetic moment shows only a 1.3% enhancement at nuclear density and a 2.3% enhancement at 1.5 times nuclear density, contrary to stronger enhancements seen in other models.

## Method

The authors embed the Chiral Quark-Soliton (CQS) model, which provides quark and antiquark substructure of the proton, into symmetric nuclear matter using a self-consistent quark-meson coupling calculation. The medium dependence is incorporated by adding a constant scalar density contribution from other nucleons to the quark scalar density in the profile function field equation, with a free parameter fitted to nuclear binding. The isoscalar electric and isovector magnetic form factors are then extracted from the quark wave functions at leading order in the number of colors (N_C = 3), using a single Pauli-Villars subtraction for regularization.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the isoscalar electric to the isovector magnetic form factor decreases by 20% at Q² = 1 GeV² at nuclear density.
- The magnetic moment shows a 1.3% enhancement at nuclear density (k_F = 1.38 fm⁻¹) and a 2.3% enhancement at 1.5 times nuclear density.
- The free nucleon mass is computed to be M_N(k_F = 0) = 1209 MeV.
- The binding energy per nucleon is fitted to B = -15.75 MeV at the minimum, with Fermi momentum k_F = 1.38 fm⁻¹ (consistent with the known value 1.35 ± 0.05 fm⁻¹).
- The nuclear compressibility is K = 348.5 MeV.
- The Pauli-Villars mass is M_PV ≃ 580 MeV, determined by reproducing f_π = 93 MeV.
- The constituent quark mass is M = 420 MeV.
- The effective condensate falls approximately 30% at nuclear density.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the nuclear EMC effect using the CQS model (Smith and Miller, 2003) to electromagnetic form factors, and provides a counterpoint to the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model of Lu et al. (1999), which uses the MIT bag model and does not include sea quarks. The results help validate the QMC model's success in describing polarization transfer experiments at TJNAF (Strauch et al., 2003), but the key difference is the lack of magnetic moment enhancement due to the sea quarks being almost completely unaffected in the nuclear medium. This enables future experiments on the bound nucleon magnetic form factor at low Q² to distinguish between models and determine the role of sea quarks in nuclei.
