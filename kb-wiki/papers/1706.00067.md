# The pseudo-Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model model: Heavy nuclei

> The Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) is extended to heavy nuclei, making use of the pseudo-SU(3) model. As a first step, the concept of forbiddenness will be resumed. One consequence of the forbiddenness is that the ground state of a nucleus can in general be described by two internally excited clusters. After that, the pseudo- SACM is formulated. The basis of pseudo-SACM is constructed, defin- ing each cluster within the united nucleus with the same oscillator fre- quency and deformation of the harmonic oscillator as a mean field and dividing the nucleons in those within the unique and normal orbitals, consistently for both clusters and the united nucleus. As test cases, this model is applied to 236U $\rightarrow$ 210Pb+26Ne and 224Ra $\rightarrow} 210Pb+14C. Some spectroscopic factors will be calculated as predictions.

## Key claim

The pseudo-Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (pseudo-SACM) extends the SACM to heavy nuclei by defining clusters within the united nucleus using the same harmonic oscillator frequency and deformation, ensuring a consistent separation of nucleons into normal and unique orbitals across both clusters and the parent nucleus, which resolves previous inconsistencies in treating light versus heavy clusters.

## Method

The authors construct the pseudo-SACM basis by first determining the number of nucleons in normal orbitals for each cluster using the Nilsson scheme at the fixed deformation of the united nucleus, filling the heavy cluster first and then the light cluster on top. The pseudo-SU(3) irreducible representations for each cluster are then coupled with the relative motion oscillator representation, and the resulting list is compared to the shell model irreps of the pseudo-oscillator to enforce the Pauli principle via the Wildermuth condition. The model is applied to two test systems: 236U → 210Pb + 26Ne and 224Ra → 210Pb + 14C, using a Hamiltonian restricted to the SU(3) symmetry limit with a simplified expression for spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

- For 236U → 210Pb + 26Ne and 224Ra → 210Pb + 14C, spectroscopic factors are calculated as predictions (no numerical values given in the text).
- The harmonic oscillator energy for the united nucleus is given by ℏω = 45 A^(-1/3) - 25 A^(-2/3) for light nuclei, with the alternative ℏω = 41 A^(-1/3) noted as more common for heavy nuclei.
- For the sd-shell example with n0 = 8, the estimated parameter B ≈ -0.13 from the e^(-aR^2) approximation, compared to the fitted value of -0.36 from prior work (Specfac-Draayer).
- The forbiddenness n_C is determined by equation (forbid-min) involving the Wildermuth condition n0, the cluster irrep (λ_C, μ_C), and the final united nucleus irrep (λ, μ).

## Context

This work extends the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM), originally developed for light nuclei by Cseh (1992) and Cseh and Levai (1994), to heavy nuclei using the pseudo-SU(3) model of Hecht and Arima. It corrects numerical errors in the forbiddenness definition of Smirnov (1990) and addresses inconsistencies in prior extensions by Cseh and Scheid (2000) and Cseh and Algora (2000), where light clusters were treated with standard SU(3) while heavy clusters used pseudo-SU(3). The pseudo-SACM enables future calculations of excited states and spectroscopic factors in heavy cluster systems, with applications to radioactive decay and fission preferences as studied by the authors in earlier works (Sacm-fission1, Sacm-fission2, Sacm-fission3).
