# The Role of Produced Hadrons in J/psi Suppression

> Hadrons produced in high-energy heavy-ion collisions can suppress the production of J/psi and other charmonium states by charmonium spontaneous dissociation as the hot hadronic environment alters the interaction between the charm quark and charm antiquark. Furthermore, hadrons can thermalize a charmonium to excite it to higher charmonium states which subsequently dissociate spontaneously. They can also collide with a charmonium to lead to its prompt dissociation into an open charm pair.

## Key claim

The paper argues that produced hadrons in high-energy heavy-ion collisions can suppress J/ψ production through three distinct mechanisms (spontaneous dissociation, thermalization, and collisional dissociation), and that this hadronic suppression must be understood and subtracted before any anomalous suppression can be attributed to quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation for charmonium states using a temperature-dependent potential (Yukawa plus exponential) inferred from lattice gauge calculations by Karsch et al. (2000), including spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor interactions. They calculate dissociation temperatures from the temperature-dependent eigenenergies, evaluate survival probabilities from thermal Bose-Einstein distributions, and compute π+J/ψ dissociation cross sections using the Barnes and Swanson (1992) model with the same temperature-dependent potential.

## Key numbers

- ψ' dissociation temperature: T_d/T_c = 0.50
- χ_c2 dissociation temperature: T_d/T_c = 0.91
- χ_c1 dissociation temperature: T_d/T_c = 0.90
- J/ψ dissociation temperature: T_d/T_c = 0.99
- For central Pb-Pb collision at 158A GeV (SPS), with initial temperature T_0 ~ T_c, freeze-out pion density ρ_freeze = 0.5/fm^3, and initial absorption time τ_0 = 3 fm/c + R/γ, the J/ψ survival probability S ~ 0.5
- For central Au-Au collision at RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV), with same parameters, the J/ψ survival probability S ~ 0.1

## Context

This work extends the lattice-based calculations of Digal, Petreczky, and Satz (2001) on charmonium dissociation temperatures, incorporating additional selection rules and spin-dependent interactions that yield different numerical values. The paper quantifies three hadronic suppression mechanisms that must be accounted for before anomalous J/ψ suppression can be used as a quark-gluon plasma signature, as originally proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986). The results enable future microscopic investigations of heavy quarkonium dissociation in hadronic collisions at SPS and RHIC energies.
