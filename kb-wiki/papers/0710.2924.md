# Neutron Polarisabilities from Deuteron Compton Scattering in \chiEFT

> Chiral Effective Field Theory is for photon energies up to 200 MeV the tool to accurately determine the polarisabilities of the neutron from deuteron Compton scattering. A multipole analysis reveals that dispersive effects from an explicit Delta(1232) prove in particular indispensable to understand the data at 95 MeV measured at SAL. Simple power-counting arguments derived from nuclear phenomenology lead to the correct Thomson limit and gauge invariance. At next-to-leading order, the static scalar dipole polarisabilities are extracted as identical for proton and neutron within the error-bar of available data: α^n=11.6\pm1.5_stat\pm0.6_Baldin, β^n=3.6\mp1.5_stat\pm0.6_Baldin for the neutron, in units of 10^-4 fm^3, compared to α^p=11.0\pm1.4_stat\pm0.4_Baldin, β}^p=2.8\mp1.4_stat\pm0.4_Baldin for the proton in the same framework. New experiments e.g. at MAXlab (Lund) will improve the statistical error-bar.

## Key claim
Chiral Effective Field Theory (χEFT) can determine neutron polarisabilities from deuteron Compton scattering data up to 200 MeV, but only if dispersive effects from the explicit Δ(1232) resonance are included; the static scalar dipole polarisabilities of proton and neutron are found to be identical within the error bars of available data.

## Method
The author performed a multipole analysis of deuteron Compton scattering within χEFT, including an explicit Δ(1232) resonance and two energy-independent short-distance coefficients (δα, δβ) fitted to proton data. Two-body contributions from meson-exchange currents and NN-rescattering were subtracted using a Green's function method, with the power-counting scheme ensuring the correct Thomson limit and gauge invariance. The iso-scalar Baldin sum rule was used as input to extract the neutron polarisabilities from all deuteron Compton data below 100 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Neutron static scalar dipole polarisabilities: α^n = 11.6 ± 1.5_stat ± 0.6_Baldin, β^n = 3.6 ∓ 1.5_stat ± 0.6_Baldin (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)
- Proton static scalar dipole polarisabilities from same framework: α^p = 11.0 ± 1.4_stat ± 0.4_Baldin, β^p = 2.8 ∓ 1.4_stat ± 0.4_Baldin (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)
- Iso-scalar polarisabilities from deuteron data: α^s = 11.3 ± 0.7_stat ± 0.6_Baldin ± 1_th, β^s = 3.2 ∓ 0.7_stat ± 0.6_Baldin ± 1_th (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)
- Iso-scalar Baldin sum rule: α^s + β^s = 14.5 ± 0.6 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)
- Short-distance coefficients from proton data: δα = -5.9 ± 1.4, δβ = -10.7 ± 1.2 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)
- Δ(1232) contribution to static magnetic dipole-polarisability: β_Δ = +[7...13] (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)
- Energy-dependence of β_M1 at 95 MeV: multipole analysis gives ≈ 4 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3), compared to ≈ 1.7 from Taylor expansion truncation

## Context
This work resolves conflicting analyses of the SAL deuteron Compton data at 94 MeV, where previous extractions by Levchuk and L'vov gave α^s=11±2, β^s=7±2, and Beane et al. found α^s=13±4, β^s=-2±3. The key advance is including the energy-dependent Δ(1232) resonance in the dynamical polarisabilities, which was missing in earlier χEFT calculations by Beane et al. (2005) and deuteron Compton analyses by Hornidge (2000). This enables future high-precision experiments at MAXlab (Lund), TUNL/HIγS, S-DALINAC, and MAMI to extract neutron polarisabilities with improved statistical errors and to investigate spin-polarisabilities via polarisation asymmetries.
