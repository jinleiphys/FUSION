# New approach to $^4$He charge distribution

> We present a study of the $^4$He charge distribution based on realistic nucleonic wave functions and incorporation of the nucleon's quark substructure. The central depression of the proton point density seen in modern four-body calculations is too small by itself to lead to a correct description of the charge distribution. We utilize six-quark structures calculated in the Chromodielectric Model for N-N interactions, and we find a swelling of the proton charge distribution as the internucleon distance decreases. These charge distributions are combined with the $^4$He wave function using the Independent Pair Approximation and two-body distributions generated from Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations. We obtain a reasonably good fit to the experimental charge distribution without including meson exchange currents.

# New Approach to $^4$He Charge Distribution

## Key claim

The central depression of the proton point density in $^4$He from modern four-body calculations is too small by itself to reproduce the experimental charge distribution, but incorporating a variable proton size that swells as internucleon distance decreases, derived from six-quark Chromodielectric Model calculations, yields a reasonably good fit without including meson exchange currents.

## Method

The authors used a Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) solution of the non-relativistic Schrodinger equation with the Argonne $v_{18}$ nucleon-nucleon interaction and Urbana 9 three-nucleon interaction to obtain the $^4$He wave function. They combined this with a variable proton charge radius parameterized as a Gaussian function of internucleon separation, fitted to six-quark Chromodielectric Model calculations, and constructed the charge distribution using the Independent Pair Approximation with two-body correlation functions from the GFMC wave function.

## Key numbers

- Free proton rms charge radius: 0.83 fm (used as baseline $b_0 = \sqrt{2/3} \times 0.83$ fm)
- Gaussian fit parameters for variable proton size: $A = 0.45$ and $s = 1.92$ fm in $b(r') = b_0 [1 + A e^{-r'^2/s^2}]$
- Central depression of the point-proton density: approximately 30% extending to about 0.8 fm (from Sick's unfolding of experimental data)
- Half normal nuclear density: 0.17/2 fm$^{-3}$ (shown as reference in figure)
- Electron scattering experiments performed up to momentum transfers of 4.5 fm$^{-1}$, yielding spatial resolution of approximately 0.3 fm

## Context

This work extends previous few-body calculations of $^4$He by Friar et al. (1981) and GFMC calculations by Wiringa (1991) and Carlson (1991), which found only small central depressions in the point-proton density that could not explain the experimental charge distribution extracted by McCarthy, Sick and Whitney (1977). The approach contrasts with nucleon-plus-meson-exchange models by Schiavilla and Riska (1990) that require hard meson-nucleon form factors, and instead builds on six-quark Chromodielectric Model studies by Koepf, Pepin, Stancu and Wilets (1994-1996). The results enable future work on meson exchange contributions using quark structure functions and predictions for quasi-elastic scattering as a critical test of nuclear structure models.
