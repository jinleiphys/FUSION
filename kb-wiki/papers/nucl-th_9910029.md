# K -> pi pi and a light scalar meson

> We explore the Delta-I= 1/2 rule and epsilon'/epsilon in K -> pi pi transitions using a Dyson-Schwinger equation model. Exploiting the feature that QCD penguin operators direct K^0_S transitions through 0^{++} intermediate states, we find an explanation of the enhancement of I=0 K -> pi pi transitions in the contribution of a light sigma-meson. This mechanism also affects epsilon'/epsilon.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the enhancement of isospin-zero (I=0) K → ππ transitions (the ΔI = 1/2 rule) can be explained by the contribution of a light scalar sigma-meson (0⁺⁺) intermediate state, mediated by QCD penguin operators Q₅ and Q₆. However, this same mechanism yields a value for ε′/ε that is approximately 15 times larger than the contemporary experimental average unless a means is found to suppress its contribution to the imaginary part of the I=0 amplitude.

## Method

The authors calculate K → ππ transition amplitudes using a Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) model with algebraic parametrizations of dressed-quark propagators and meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, previously fitted to light- and heavy-meson observables. For neutral kaon decays, they separate the QCD penguin operator contributions into two classes: those that proceed through a scalar (sigma) intermediate state, modeled with a simple-pole propagator and impulse approximation coupling to ππ, and those that do not. The two free parameters (sigma mass m_σ and width parameter ω_σ in the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude) are determined by fitting to the experimental widths of K_S⁰ → π⁺π⁻, K_S⁰ → π⁰π⁰, and Γ(σ → ππ).

## Key numbers

* The experimental ratio Γ(K_S⁰ → ππ) / Γ(K⁺ → π⁺π⁰) = 660, corresponding to Re(A₀)/Re(A₂) ≈ 22.
* The fitted sigma-meson mass is m_σ = 1.14 m_K (with m_K = 0.449 GeV from the model calculation), and the sigma width is Γ(σ → ππ) = 0.54 GeV.
* The calculated pure isospin amplitudes are Re(A₀) = 31.7 × 10⁻⁸ GeV and Re(A₂) = 1.47 × 10⁻⁸ GeV, yielding 1/w = 21.6.
* Without the sigma-meson contribution, the calculated ratio becomes 1/w = 2.9, demonstrating the sigma's role in the ΔI = 1/2 enhancement.
* The calculated ε′/ε including both sigma and electroweak penguin contributions is 31.7 × 10⁻³, compared to the experimental average of (2.1 ± 0.46) × 10⁻³.
* The model quark masses are m_u^(1 GeV) = 5.4 MeV and m_s^(1 GeV) = 119 MeV.

## Context

This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of meson phenomenology (Roberts and collaborators, 1990s) to the nonleptonic kaon decay problem, specifically the ΔI = 1/2 rule and direct CP violation. The authors note that the impulse approximation for the K⁺ → π⁺π⁰ amplitude is algebraically equivalent to the vacuum saturation Ansatz, yielding a result approximately twice too large, which they identify as a challenge for models. The sigma-meson mechanism builds on experimental evidence for a broad scalar resonance in τ decays (reported around 1998) and provides a plausible long-range explanation for the I=0 enhancement, though it simultaneously overpredicts ε′/ε unless the sigma contribution to the imaginary part of A₀ is suppressed, a procedure the authors cannot currently justify.
