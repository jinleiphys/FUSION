# Transient anomalous charge production in strong-field QCD

> We investigate axial charge production in two-color QCD out of equilibrium. We compute the real-time evolution starting with spatially homogeneous strong gauge fields, while the fermions are in vacuum. The idealized class of initial conditions is motivated by glasma flux tubes in the context of heavy-ion collisions. We focus on axial charge production at early times, where important aspects of the anomalous dynamics can be derived analytically. This is compared to real-time lattice simulations. Quark production at early times leading to anomalous charge generation is investigated using Wilson fermions. Our results indicate that coherent gauge fields can transiently produce significant amounts of axial charge density, while part of the induced charges persist to be present even well beyond characteristic decoherence times. The comparisons to analytic results provide stringent tests of real-time representations of the axial anomaly on the lattice.

# Transient anomalous charge production in strong-field QCD

## Key claim

Coherent gauge fields can transiently produce significant amounts of axial charge density at early times, and part of the induced charge persists well beyond the characteristic decoherence time of the macroscopic gauge fields, even though the ensemble-averaged anomaly term approaches zero after decoherence.

## Method

The authors study two-color QCD out of equilibrium by solving the real-time evolution starting from spatially homogeneous strong gauge fields with fermions initially in vacuum. They derive analytic solutions for the early-time dynamics using a linearized expansion in fluctuations around the macroscopic field, and compare these to classical-statistical lattice simulations for the pure gauge sector and to real-time lattice simulations with Wilson fermions for the full fermion sector.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density: \(Q^4/(2g^2)\) for characteristic scale \(Q\) and weak gauge coupling \(g \ll 1\)
- Characteristic decoherence time: \(t_\Theta \sim Q^{-1} \ln(g^{-2})\)
- Largest negative eigenvalue for fluctuations: \(-\mathcal{A}^2\) at momentum \(p_\star = |\mathcal{A}|\), with \(\mathcal{A} \sim Q\)
- Lattice parameters for gauge simulations: \(g = 10^{-3}\), \(N_\text{latt} = 64^3\), \(Qa = 0.312\), \(N_\text{conf} = 128\)
- Lattice parameters for fermion simulations: \(Q^3 V = 20^3\), Wilson parameter \(r = 1\), \(m/Q = 0.1\) (and \(m/Q = 0.5\) for comparison)

## Context

This work extends the study of anomalous processes in non-Abelian gauge theories out of equilibrium, building on prior work on sphaleron transitions in thermal equilibrium (Arnold 1996, Moore 1997) and on early-universe electroweak baryogenesis from fast quench dynamics (Saffin 2011). The initial conditions are motivated by the glasma flux-tube scenario in heavy-ion collisions (Lappi 2006). The analytic solutions provide a stringent precision test for real-time lattice implementations of the axial anomaly, enabling future non-perturbative studies of more realistic field configurations and later times.
