# Constraints on the parameter space in dark matter admixed neutron stars

> We investigate the impact of dark matter on neutron star properties using the relativistic mean-field theory. By incorporating the dark matter model, we explore how dark matter parameters, specifically dark matter mass and Fermi momentum, influence nuclear saturation properties, the equation of state, and the mass-radius relationship of neutron stars. We also examine the universal relation between dimensionless tidal deformability and compactness in the presence of dark matter. Our results show that the inclusion of dark matter significantly alters nuclear saturation properties, leading to higher incompressibility and symmetry energy values. Notably, higher dark matter Fermi momenta and masses result in more compact neutron star configurations with reduced radii and lower maximum masses, highlighting a complex interplay between dark matter and nuclear matter. Deviations from the universal relation are observed with dark matter inclusion, particularly for neutron stars with lower compactness. By leveraging observational data from PSR J0740+6620, GW170817, and Neutron star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER) measurements of PSR J0030+0451, we derive stringent constraints on dark matter parameter space within neutron stars, emphasizing the necessity of integrating multimodal observations to delineate the properties of dark matter along with neutron stars. Our findings underscore the importance of considering dark matter effects in neutron star modeling and suggest potential refinements for current theoretical frameworks to accurately predict neutron star properties under various astrophysical conditions.

## Key claim
The paper shows that including dark matter in neutron star models alters nuclear saturation properties and the mass-radius relationship, but the effect is conditional: higher dark matter Fermi momenta and masses produce more compact stars with lower maximum masses, contrary to the naive expectation that a stiffer equation of state would increase maximum mass. The authors derive constraints on the dark matter parameter space (mass and Fermi momentum) by requiring consistency with observations from PSR J0740+6620, GW170817, and NICER measurements of PSR J0030+0451.

## Method
The authors use relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with six parameter sets (NL3, BigApple, IOPB-I, G3, IU-FSU, FSUGold) to model nuclear matter, and add a fermionic dark matter component that interacts with nucleons via the Standard Model Higgs boson. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for the combined system, varying the dark matter particle mass (1 to 400 GeV) and Fermi momentum (0.01 to 0.15 GeV for neutron star calculations), and compare the resulting mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities against multimessenger observational constraints.

## Key numbers
- The critical Fermi momentum for binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter follows the empirical relation: k_f,BE^DM = 0.08791 - 0.06505x + 0.02048x^2 - 0.002597x^3, where x = log10[M_chi/(1 GeV)], with maximum deviation of about 2.5% across all RMF parameter sets.
- For the IOPB-I parameter set with M_chi = 100 GeV and k_f^DM = 0.04 GeV, varying the Higgs-DM coupling y from 0.004 to 0.06 produces only a 0.14% change in maximum mass and a 3.42% change in the corresponding radius.
- The saturation density n0 for NL3 without DM is 0.148 fm^-3; with M_chi = 100 GeV and k_f^DM = 0.04 GeV it increases to 0.295 fm^-3.
- The experimental constraint on incompressibility is K0 = 230 ± 40 MeV (PhysRevC.88.034319).
- The mass of PSR J0740+6620 is M = 2.08^{+0.07}_{-0.07} M_sun (68.3% credibility).
- The radius of PSR J0740+6620 from NICER is R = 12.39^{+2.63}_{-1.68} km (95% credibility) with a lower bound R >= 10.71 km.
- The 1.4 M_sun neutron star radius must be less than 13.6 km from GW170817 constraints.

## Context
This work extends the dark matter admixed neutron star model of Panotopoulos and Lopes (2017), who first applied RMF theory with fermionic dark matter interacting via the Higgs boson, and builds on studies by Das, Malik, and Nayak (2019) who showed that dark matter softens the equation of state and lowers tidal deformability to match GW170817 data. Unlike previous studies that fixed the dark matter particle mass, this work systematically varies both dark matter mass and Fermi momentum to derive constraints from multimessenger observations, and includes the crust region using the SLy4 equation of state, which most prior studies neglected. The results enable future work to test specific dark matter models against neutron star observations and refine theoretical frameworks for predicting neutron star properties under dark matter admixture.
