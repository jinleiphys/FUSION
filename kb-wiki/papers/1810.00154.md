# A new and finite family of solutions of hydrodynamics: Part III: Advanced estimate of the life-time parameter

> We derive a new formula for the longitudinal HBT-radius of the two particle Bose-Einstein correlation function from a new family of finite and exact, accelerating solution of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics for a temperature independent speed of sound. The new result generalizes the Makhlin-Sinyukov and Herrmann-Bertsch formulae and leads to an advanced life-time estimate of high energy heavy ion and proton-proton collisions.

# A new and finite family of solutions of hydrodynamics: Part III: Advanced estimate of the life-time parameter

## Key claim

The paper derives a new formula for the longitudinal HBT-radius that generalizes the Makhlin-Sinyukov and Herrmann-Bertsch formulae, but the result is conditional on the assumption of a temperature-independent speed of sound and applies only to the specific CKCJ family of accelerating, finite solutions of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors evaluate Bose-Einstein correlation functions in a Gaussian approximation from the CKCJ (Csörgő-Kasza-Csanád-Jiang) family of exact, accelerating solutions of 1+1 dimensional relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics. The Cooper-Frye formula is integrated using a saddle-point approximation near mid-rapidity, where the fluid rapidity is approximated as a linear function of space-time rapidity (Ω ≈ λ η_x). The longitudinal HBT radius R_L is then expressed in terms of the freeze-out proper time τ_f, the acceleration parameter λ, the freeze-out temperature T_f, and the transverse mass m_T.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal HBT-radius formula for the CKCJ solution: R_L = (τ_f / √[λ(2λ-1)]) √(T_f / m_T)
- The saddle-point space-time rapidity is proportional to rapidity y as: η_x^s ≈ y / (2λ-1)
- The width of the Gaussian emission function at mid-rapidity: Δη_x ≈ √(T_f / m_t) × 1/√[λ(2λ-1)]
- The Makhlin-Sinyukov formula for comparison: R_L = τ_Bj √(T_f / m_T)
- The Herrmann-Bertsch formula: R_L = τ_f √(T_f / m_T) √[2K_2(m_T/T_f) / K_1(m_T/T_f)]
- The CNC solution formula: R_L = (τ_f / λ) √(T_f / m_T)

## Context

This work extends the Makhlin-Sinyukov (1987) and Herrmann-Bertsch (1994) formulae for longitudinal HBT radii, which were derived from the Hwa-Bjorken accelerationless flow solution, and the CNC solution formula of Nagy and Csanád (2007) for accelerating flows. The new result enables an advanced life-time estimate of high energy heavy ion and proton-proton collisions once the acceleration parameter λ is determined from fits to (pseudo)rapidity distributions as demonstrated in the first part of this series (Csörgő et al., 2018). The formula is independent of the equation of state, which is a surprising feature that distinguishes it from earlier estimates.
