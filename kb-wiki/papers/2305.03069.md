# Probing the onset of maximal entanglement inside the proton in diffractive DIS

> It has been proposed that at small Bjorken $x$, or equivalently at high energy, hadrons represent maximally entangled states of quarks and gluons. This conjecture is in accord with experimental data from the electron-proton collider HERA at the smallest accessible $x$. In this Letter, we propose to study the onset of the maximal entanglement inside the proton using Diffractive Deep Inelastic Scattering. It is shown that the data collected by the H1 Collaboration at HERA allows to probe the transition to the maximal entanglement regime. By relating the entanglement entropy to the entropy of final state hadrons, we find a good agreement with the H1 data using both the exact entropy formula as well as its asymptotic expansion which indicates the presence of a nearly maximally-entangled state. Finally, future opportunities at the Electron Ion Collider are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that diffractive deep inelastic scattering (DDIS) can probe the transition to a maximally entangled state inside the proton, and that this transition is delayed relative to inclusive DIS due to the rapidity gap; the H1 data at moderate values of beta (0.06 to 0.41) show a gradual approach to maximal entanglement rather than its full realization.

## Method

The authors model the diffractive system as a set of color dipoles using a 1+1 dimensional cascade equation, with the probability distribution for n dipoles given by a modified geometric series that allows for multiple initial dipoles at the start of evolution. They fix the model parameters Delta and C by matching the average number of dipoles at low beta to predictions from leading-order diffractive parton distribution functions (GKG18-DPDFs Set A), then compute the hadron entropy from the dipole probabilities assuming local parton-hadron duality. The resulting entropy is compared to charged particle multiplicity distributions measured by the H1 Collaboration at HERA, combining forward and backward hemisphere data into total multiplicity distributions.

## Key numbers

- Delta = 0.287 +/- 0.006 +/- 0.050, the growth rate of dipole number with rapidity, extracted from diffractive PDFs
- C = 4.20 +/- 0.29 +/- 0.52, the effective number of initial dipoles at y_X = 0, extracted from diffractive PDFs
- C' = 2.80 +/- 0.19 +/- 0.34, the charged-hadron adjusted initial dipole number (2/3 of C)
- Goodness-of-fit chi^2/ndf = 2.97 for the exact entropy formula versus H1 data, compared to 17.13 for the asymptotic expansion
- The H1 data cover the beta range [0.06, 0.41] with a minimum pseudorapidity gap of approximately 4.3 units
- Q^2 range for the analysis: Q^2_min = 7.5 GeV^2 to Q^2_max = 100 GeV^2
- x_P range: x_P_min = 0.0003 to x_P_max = 0.05

## Context

This work extends the entanglement entropy framework proposed by Kharzeev (2017) from inclusive DIS to diffractive DIS, following experimental confirmation of maximal entanglement in inclusive DIS at HERA by Hentschinski (2021, 2022) and in pp collisions at the LHC by Tu (2019). The study uses the dipole cascade model of Mueller (1994) and diffractive PDFs from Goharipour (2018), and finds that the rapidity gap in DDIS delays the onset of maximal entanglement by reducing the available Hilbert space of partonic states. The results enable future quantitative studies of entanglement entropy at the Electron-Ion Collider, particularly in the ePIC experiment with its larger forward pseudorapidity coverage (up to approximately 3.5-4) compared to HERA.
