# Probing parton saturation and the gluon dipole via diffractive jet production at the Electron-Ion Collider

> We demonstrate that hard dijet production via coherent inelastic diffraction is a promising channel for probing gluon saturation at the Electron-Ion Collider. By $inelastic$ diffraction we mean a process in which the two hard jets - a quark-antiquark pair generated by the decay of the virtual photon - are accompanied by a softer gluon jet, emitted by the quark or the antiquark. This process can be described as the elastic scattering of an effective gluon-gluon dipole. The cross section takes a factorised form, between a hard factor and a unintegrated ("Pomeron") gluon distribution describing the transverse momentum imbalance between the hard dijets. The dominant contribution comes from the black disk limit and leads to a dijet imbalance of the order of the target saturation momentum $Q_s$ evaluated at the rapidity gap. Integrating out the dijet imbalance, we obtain a collinear factorization where the initial condition for the DGLAP evolution is set by gluon saturation.

## Key claim

Hard dijet production via coherent inelastic diffraction at the Electron-Ion Collider is a promising channel for probing gluon saturation, provided the gluon jet is sufficiently soft (transverse momentum of order the target saturation momentum \(Q_s\)) and the hard dijets are nearly back-to-back; the sensitivity to saturation persists even after integrating over the soft gluon to obtain collinear factorization, unlike the case of inclusive dijets.

## Method

The authors compute the cross-section for diffractive production of a quark-antiquark pair plus a softer gluon jet in deep inelastic scattering at small Bjorken \(x\), using the color glass condensate (CGC) formalism and the dipole picture. They work in the correlation limit where the hard dijet transverse momenta are much larger than their imbalance, and the gluon is soft enough that its emission can be treated in the eikonal approximation; the process is described as elastic scattering of an effective gluon-gluon dipole. The cross-section is factorized into a hard impact factor (photon splitting to \(q\bar q\) plus gluon emission vertex) and an unintegrated gluon distribution of the Pomeron, which is the square of a Bessel transform of the adjoint dipole scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The typical dijet momentum imbalance is of order the target saturation momentum \(Q_s(Y_{\mathbb{P}})\) evaluated at the rapidity gap, not the confinement scale \(\Lambda\).
- For the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model, the integrated Pomeron gluon distribution yields \(\kappa = 2\ln 2\) in the relation \(\int d^2\mathbf{K}\,[\mathcal{G}(K_\perp)]^2 \simeq \pi\kappa\, Q_s^2(Y_{\mathbb{P}})\).
- The initial condition for DGLAP evolution of the Pomeron gluon distribution at scale \(Q^2 \gtrsim Q_s^2(Y_{\mathbb{P}})\) is \(xG_{\mathbb{P}}(x, x_{\mathbb{P}}, Q^2) = S_\perp (1-x)^2 \frac{N_c^2-1}{(2\pi)^3}\kappa\,Q_s^2(Y_{\mathbb{P}})\).
- For the EIC, with \(\mathrm{x_{Bj}} = 10^{-3}\) and \(Q^2 \le 10\) GeV\(^2\), the nuclear (Pb) saturation momentum is estimated as \(Q_s^2 \simeq 1.5\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of diffractive trijet production beyond the earlier \(k_T\)-factorization treatment of Bartels (1999), which overlooked saturation, and the dipole-picture analysis of Kovchegov (2001), which focused on diffractive gluon production only. It also contrasts with inclusive dijet production in the correlation limit (Dominguez 2011, Metz 2011), where the cross-section decays only as \(1/K_\perp^2\) at large imbalance and saturation effects are diluted. The proposed diffractive channel enables a clean extraction of the Pomeron gluon distribution from first principles, providing an initial condition for DGLAP evolution that is set by gluon saturation, and opens the door to similar studies in ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC.
