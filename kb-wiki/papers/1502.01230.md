# The Light-Front Vacuum

> Background: The vacuum in the light-front representation of quantum field theory is trivial while vacuum in the equivalent canonical representation of the same theory is non-trivial. Purpose: Understand the relation between the vacuum in light-front and canonical representations of quantum field theory and the role of zero-modes in this relation. Method: Vacuua are defined as linear functionals on an algebra of field operators. The role of the algebra in the definition of the vacuum is exploited to understand this relation. Results: The vacuum functional can be extended from the light-front Fock algebra to an algebra of local observables. The extension to the algebra of local observables is responsible for the inequivalence. The extension defines a unitary mapping between the physical representation of the local algebra and a sub-algebra of the light-front Fock algebra. Conclusion: There is a unitary mapping from the physical representation of the algebra of local observables to a sub-algebra of the light-front Fock algebra with the free light-front Fock vacuum. The dynamics appears in the mapping and the structure of the sub-algebra. This correspondence provides a formulation of locality and Poincaré invariance on the light-front Fock space.

## Key claim

The light-front vacuum is not inherently trivial; rather, the apparent triviality arises because the vacuum functional defined on the light-front Fock algebra must be extended to the algebra of local observables, and this extension, not the annihilation-operator condition alone, determines whether the vacuum is equivalent to the canonical vacuum.

## Method

The authors define vacuua as positive linear functionals on different algebras of field operators (local algebra, canonical equal-time algebra, light-front algebra, and Schlieder-Seiler algebra) and use the GNS construction to relate these functionals to Hilbert-space representations. For free scalar fields, they show that the light-front Fock vacuum is well-defined only on the Schlieder-Seiler algebra (where test functions vanish at p⁺=0) and that extending this functional to the local algebra yields a unitary mapping from the physical representation of the local algebra to a sub-algebra of the light-front Fock algebra. For interacting fields, they invoke the Haag expansion to express Heisenberg fields in terms of asymptotic free fields, each of which can be extended from the light-front restriction.

## Key numbers

* The light-front inner product has a logarithmic singularity for functions non-zero at p⁺=0 (Section 2, around Eq. n.19).
* For a free scalar field, the canonical transformation changing mass m→m' yields a generator G with ‖G|0⟩‖² = (1/4)∫ η(p)² dp δ(0) = ∞ (Section 3, Eq. irep:13), showing the transformation is not unitarily implementable.
* The light-front annihilation operator a_lf(p⁺,p_⊥) is related to the canonical annihilation operator a(p) by a_lf(p⁺,p_⊥) = √[ω_m(p)/p⁺] a(p) (Section 4, Eq. lfv:5).
* The light-front creation/annihilation commutator is [a_lf(p⁺,p_⊥), a_lf†(q⁺,q_⊥)] = δ(p⁺−q⁺)δ(p_⊥−q_⊥) (Section 4, Eq. lfv:6), independent of mass.
* The vacuum expectation value on the Schlieder-Seiler algebra is ⟨0|U(f̃)|0⟩ = e^{(1/2)(f̃,f̃)} (Section 6, Eq. f.15), where (f̃,f̃) is ill-defined for functions non-zero at p⁺=0.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic analysis of Leutwyler, Klauder, and Streit (1970) and Schlieder and Seiler (1972) on the light-front vacuum, resolving the apparent contradiction that light-front vacuua for different-mass free fields are unitarily equivalent while canonical vacuua are not. The key insight, that the vacuum functional depends on the choice of algebra, explains why zero-mode contributions (at p⁺=0) are needed to maintain equivalence with covariant perturbation theory, as noted by Bylev (1996) and Bakker (2011). The results enable a formulation of locality and Poincaré invariance on light-front Fock space, with implications for non-perturbative treatments of strong interactions and electroweak probes.
