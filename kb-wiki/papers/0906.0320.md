# Comparing neutron star predictions by various microscopic models

> We calculate several neutron star properties, for static and/or rotating stars, using equations of state based on different microscopic models. These include our Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock model and others derived from the non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach implemented with microscopic three-body forces. The model dependence is discussed.

# Comparing neutron star predictions by various microscopic models

## Key claim

The model dependence of neutron star properties is larger than previously appreciated, but currently available observational constraints are still insufficient to discriminate among the different microscopic equations of state. The authors stress that the tight connection with the underlying two-body potential in microscopic approaches facilitates physical understanding in terms of the characteristics of the nuclear force and its behavior in the medium.

## Method

The authors calculated neutron star properties for static and rotating stars using equations of state from their Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) model and from non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approaches with microscopic three-body forces (TBF) from Li and Schulze (2008). The BHF+TBF models employed the Argonne V18, Bonn B, Nijmegen 93, and Urbana UIX potentials. All EoSs were joined with crustal equations of state from Harrison and Wheeler (1965) and Negele and Vautherin (1973), and neutron star properties were calculated using public software from http://www.gravity.phys.uwm.edu/rns.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses range from 1.8 solar masses (UIX) to 2.5 solar masses (BOB) across all models
- Radii for all models under consideration (DBHF or BHF+TBF) range between 10 and 12 km
- At about 6 times normal density, the DBHF energy of symmetric matter is 67% of the BOB energy; at ten times normal density it is 51%
- In pure neutron matter at about 6 times normal density, the DBHF energy is 49% of the BOB energy; at ten times normal density it is 30%
- The 716 Hz rotational frequency corresponds to the most rapidly rotating pulsar PSR J1748-2446
- A lower bound on the moment of inertia from Crab nebula luminosity was inferred to be I greater than or equal to 4-8 times 10^44 g cm^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous comparison of neutron skin predictions in Pb-208 (Sammarruca and Liu, 2009) to neutron star observables, correlating differences with the slope of the symmetry energy. The paper contrasts the DBHF approach, which effectively incorporates a class of three-body forces through Dirac effects, with the BHF+TBF approach of Li and Schulze (2008) that includes explicit microscopic three-body forces. The results show that the inclusion of microscopic TBF in the BHF model introduces considerably more repulsion than the Dirac effects, especially in neutron matter, and that the two-body potential dependence is very large. The work enables future discrimination among EoSs as observational constraints from neutron star mass measurements and gravitational wave observations become more stringent.
