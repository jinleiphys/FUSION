# Asymptotic normalization coefficients from ab initio calculations

> We present calculations of asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for one-nucleon removals from nuclear states of mass numbers 3 to 9. Our ANCs were computed from variational Monte Carlo solutions to the many-body Schroedinger equation with the combined Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon potentials. Instead of computing explicit overlap integrals, we applied a Green's function method that is insensitive to the difficulties of constructing and Monte Carlo sampling the long-range tails of the variational wave functions. This method also allows computation of the ANC at the physical separation energy, even when it differs from the separation energy for the Hamiltonian. We compare our results, which for most nuclei are the first ab initio calculations of ANCs, with existing experimental and theoretical results and discuss further possible applications of the technique.

## Key claim

The authors present the first systematic *ab initio* calculations of asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for one-nucleon removals from light nuclei, using a Green's function method that avoids the difficulties of directly computing long-range overlap integrals. The computed ANCs agree well with experimental values only when the experimental separation energy is used in place of the Hamiltonian's separation energy, which the authors justify by arguing that the short-range wave function is insensitive to small changes in the potential that would shift the separation energy.

## Method

The authors used variational Monte Carlo (VMC) wave functions for the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon potentials, constructing wave functions for nuclei with mass numbers 3 to 9 and their residual nuclei. Instead of computing the overlap integral directly, they applied a Green's function method that expresses the ANC as an integral over the short-range interior of the wave function (limited to separations below about 7 fm), using the factor (U_rel - V_C) which is short-ranged. The ANC integral was evaluated by Monte Carlo integration using the same sampling scheme as the energy calculations, and was computed both with the Hamiltonian's separation energy B_H and with the experimental separation energy B_expt.

The results enable tests of nuclear structure calculations against experimentally-inferred ANCs from transfer, knockout, and breakup reactions (Gulamov 1995, Trache 2003, Azhari 2001), and the authors note that ongoing Green's function Monte Carlo calculations with improved three-body interactions (Brida 2011) appear to support their values.
