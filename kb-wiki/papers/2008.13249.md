# Advances in modeling nuclear matrix elements of neutrinoless double beta decay

> Accurate nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double beta decays of candidate nuclei are important for the design and interpretation of future experiments. Significant progress has been made in the modeling of these NMEs from first principles. The NME for 48Ca shows a good agreement among three different ab initio calculations starting from the same nuclear interaction constructed within the chiral EFT and the same decay operator. These studies open the door to ab initio calculations of the matrix elements for the decay of heavier nuclei such as 76Ge, 130Te, and 136Xe. The ultimate goal is the computation of NMEs in many-body calculations with controllable approximations, using nuclear interactions and weak transition operators derived consistently from chiral EFT. We are expecting more progress towards this goal in the near future.

## Key claim

The paper reports that three different *ab initio* calculations of the neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix element for ⁴⁸Ca, all starting from the same chiral EFT interaction (EM1.8/2.0) and the same decay operator, show good agreement; however, the author cautions that these values are subject to change when truncation errors in the chiral EFT expansion and many-body methods are fully accounted for, and that contributions from a leading-order short-range operator and two-body weak currents are missing.

## Method

The paper reviews and compares three *ab initio* approaches applied to the ⁴⁸Ca → ⁴⁸Ti decay: the in-medium generator-coordinate method (IM-GCM), the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) combined with the interacting shell model, and equation-of-motion coupled-cluster theory with singles-doubles-and-triples excitations (CCSDT1). All three calculations start from the same SRG-softened chiral two-plus-three-nucleon interaction EM1.8/2.0 and the same standard light-Majorana neutrino-exchange decay operator. The IM-GCM framework combines multi-reference IMSRG with the generator-coordinate method to describe deformed medium-mass nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The IM-GCM calculation yields a nuclear matrix element for ⁴⁸Ca of 0.61⁺⁰·⁰⁴₋₀·₀₅.
- The VS-IMSRG calculation predicts a nuclear matrix element for ⁴⁸Ca of 0.58(1).
- The CCSDT1 calculation predicts a nuclear matrix element for ⁴⁸Ca between 0.25 and 0.75, depending on the choice of reference state.
- The current best experimental half-life lower limits exceed 10²⁵ years for ¹³⁶Xe (Gando 2016), ⁷⁶Ge (Agostini 2018), and ¹³⁰Te (Adams 2020).
- Next-generation tonne-scale experiments aim for a discovery potential of half-life 10²⁸ years.

## Context

This work extends the line of *ab initio* nuclear structure calculations to the neutrinoless double beta decay matrix element, a problem previously dominated by phenomenological models such as energy density functional (EDF) methods (Rodriguez 2010, Yao 2015) and effective interaction approaches (Menendez 2009, Horoi 2016), which predict NMEs differing by a factor of about three. The agreement among the three *ab initio* methods for ⁴⁸Ca opens the door to calculations for heavier candidate nuclei such as ⁷⁶Ge, ¹³⁰Te, and ¹³⁶Xe. The VS-IMSRG calculation has already produced NMEs for ⁷⁶Ge and ⁸²Se (Belley 2020), which await confirmation by other *ab initio* methods; the ultimate goal is to compute NMEs with fully quantified theoretical uncertainties using interactions and operators derived consistently from chiral EFT.
