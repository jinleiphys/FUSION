# Pion and Eta Photoproduction in Nuclei

> We calculate the quasifree photoproduction of pions and eta mesons on nuclei from 12C to 208Pb. Assuming that for the quasifree process the cross sections for pi/eta - photoproduction on a nucleon in medium are -- besides a lowering due to Pauli blocking -- identical to those on a free nucleon, we use these as an input. The produced mesons or resonances then propagate through the nucleus where they can undergo several scattering and absorption processes. This final state interaction, which leads to a lowering of the initial meson yield up to 70%, we simulate in a coupled channel Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model. It allows us to study in detail the influence of Fermi motion, Pauli blocking and of the additional decay channels for nucleon resonances in medium on the photoproduction cross section. The calculated total and differential cross sections are compared to experimental data and the influence of the different medium effects on the cross sections is discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper calculates quasifree photoproduction of pions and eta mesons on nuclei from 12C to 208Pb, assuming that in-medium cross sections on a nucleon are identical to free ones except for Pauli blocking, and then simulating final-state interactions with a coupled-channel Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model. The key hedge is that the strong broadening of the Delta resonance peak seen in experimental data for pion photoproduction is not reproduced by the calculations, suggesting either an overestimate of pion absorption at high energies or a need for stronger medium-induced resonance broadening.

## Method

The authors used a coupled-channel BUU transport model that includes nucleons, Delta, N(1440), N(1535) resonances, pions, and eta mesons, with cross sections taken from experiment or parametrized from data. For pion photoproduction, the elementary cross section was split into a Delta-resonance part and a Born-term part, with the nucleus initialized either with a Delta resonance or a pion-nucleon pair; for eta photoproduction, the primary step was assumed to proceed via N(1535) excitation, with the gamma n to eta n cross section taken as half the gamma p to eta p value. The produced mesons and resonances then propagated through the nucleus, undergoing scattering and absorption according to the transport model, with Pauli blocking checked either numerically or analytically.

## Key numbers

* Total pion photoproduction cross sections on 12C and 208Pb overestimate data at the Delta resonance peak energy and decrease faster at higher and lower energies.
* Pauli blocking shifts the effective production threshold for pions up by more than 50 MeV.
* Absorption lowers the pion cross section by another factor of 3.
* For eta photoproduction, the gamma p to eta p cross section was fitted with a Lorentz curve; the gamma n to eta n cross section was taken as 10 microbarns maximum (half the proton value).
* The eta photoproduction cross section on nuclei shows an A^(2/3) mass dependence, calculated at a photon energy of 750 MeV for C, Ca, Sn, and Pb.
* Only about 15% of eta mesons rescatter for Pb, 9% for C.
* Primary pion photoproduction contributes only about 12% to the eta photoproduction cross section for Pb, nearly independent of photon energy.
* The statistical uncertainty from using 1000 testparticles per nucleon leads to an uncertainty of about 6% on the total cross section.

## Context

This work extends earlier models by Carrasco (1993) and Carrasco, Oset and Salcedo (1993) for eta and pion photoproduction, which treated the final-state interaction with Monte Carlo codes but neglected processes like eta N to pi N to eta N and resonance propagation. The present coupled-channel BUU approach includes these channels explicitly, but the authors find that photoproduction experiments are hardly usable for determining resonance properties in the nuclear medium because mesons created in the dense center are most likely absorbed, while those from the surface only probe low-density zones. The paper enables future comparisons with data from the TAPS collaboration at MAMI and highlights the need for better constraints on the gamma n to eta n cross section.
