# Study of Participant-Spectator Matter at the Energy of Vanishing Flow

> We aim to study the participant-spectator matter over a wide range of energies of vanishing flow and masses. For this, we employed different model parameters at central and semi-central colliding geometries. Remarkably, a nearly mass independent nature of the participant matter was obtained at the energy of vanishing flow. This makes it a very strong alternative candidate to study the energy of vanishing flow. We also show that the participant matter can also act as an indicator to study the degree of thermalization. The degree of thermalization reached in central collisions is nearly the same for different colliding nuclei at the energy of vanishing flow.

## Key claim

The participant matter at the energy of vanishing flow (EVF) is nearly independent of the mass of the colliding system across a wide range of model parameters (equations of state, momentum-dependent interactions, and nucleon-nucleon cross sections), but this holds only for central and semi-central colliding geometries (b/b_max = 0.2 and 0.4) and for symmetric colliding nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) simulations of symmetric heavy-ion collisions (from 12C+12C to 238U+238U) at small energy steps between 30 and 250 MeV/nucleon, using both soft and hard equations of state with and without momentum-dependent interactions (MDI) and isotropic nucleon-nucleon cross sections of 40 and 55 mb. They extracted the participant matter at each system's EVF using two definitions: one based on nucleon-nucleon collisions (PM-C, counting nucleons that experienced at least one collision) and another based on cuts in reduced rapidity distributions (PM-R1, PM-R2, PM-R3). The EVF for each system was determined by straight-line interpolation of the transverse flow as a function of incident energy.

## Key numbers

- The participant matter defined by nucleon-nucleon collisions (PM-C) is nearly mass-independent at EVF for all model parameter sets tested, with power-law fits of the form cA^tau showing very weak mass dependence.
- The variation in EVF for a particular colliding mass using different model ingredients can be as large as 75 MeV/nucleon.
- Simulations were carried out for eight symmetric systems: 12C+12C, 20Ne+20Ne, 40Ca+40Ca, 58Ni+58Ni, 93Nb+93Nb, 131Xe+131Xe, 197Au+197Au, and 238U+238U.
- The energy range studied was 30 MeV/nucleon to 250 MeV/nucleon.
- The momentum-dependent potential parameters used were t4 = 1.57 MeV and t5 = 5 x 10^-4 MeV^-2.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Sood and Puri, 2004) which first reported that participant-spectator matter at EVF is insensitive to the mass of the colliding system for central collisions. The present paper generalizes that finding to different equations of state (soft and hard), momentum-dependent interactions, and nucleon-nucleon cross sections (40 and 55 mb), as well as to semi-central geometries (b/b_max = 0.4). The mass-independent nature of participant matter at EVF is proposed as an alternative experimental observable for determining the energy of vanishing flow, which has traditionally been studied through collective flow measurements in systems such as those reported by Ogilvie (1990), Krofcheck (1992), and others. The work also shows that the degree of thermalization reached at EVF is nearly the same for different colliding nuclei, enabling future studies of thermalization using participant matter as an indicator.
