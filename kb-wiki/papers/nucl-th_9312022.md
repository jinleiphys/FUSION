# Dynamical Color Correlations in a $SU(2)_c$ Quark Exchange Model of Nuclear Matter

> The quark exchange model is a simple realization of an adiabatic approximation to the strong-coupling limit of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD): the quarks always coalesce into the lowest energy set of flux tubes. Nuclear matter is thus modeled in terms of its quarks. We wish to study the correlations imposed by total wavefunction antisymmetry when color degrees of freedom are included. To begin with, we have considered one-dimensional matter with a $SU(2)$ color internal degree of freedom only. We proceed by constructing a totally antisymmetric, color singlet {\it Ansatz} characterized by a variational parameter $λ$ (which describes the length scale over which two quarks in the system are clustered into hadrons) and by performing a variational Monte Carlo calculation of the energy to optimize $λ$ for a fixed density. We calculate the $q-q$ correlation function as well, and discuss the qualitative differences between the system at low and high density.

## Key claim

The paper presents a "magic" model for nuclear matter in one spatial dimension with $SU(2)$ color, where the variational wavefunction is constructed to be fully antisymmetric and a color singlet by imposing constraints directly on the Ansatz rather than through a potential with a color projector. The authors claim that this treatment of color is "complete" within the context of the quark exchange model, though they acknowledge the study is limited to $SU(2)$ color and one spatial dimension for tractability.

## Method

The authors constructed a totally antisymmetric, color-singlet variational wavefunction for $N$ quarks in one spatial dimension, using Gaussian clustering factors $\exp[-\lambda(x_i-x_j)^2]$ multiplied by Fermi gas spatial wavefunctions built from cosines of the hadronic center-of-mass coordinates. They performed variational Monte Carlo calculations to optimize the variational parameter $\lambda$ (which describes the length scale over which two quarks cluster into hadrons) for fixed density, and computed the quark-quark correlation function. The matrix elements were evaluated by enumerating topologically distinct exchange terms, exploiting permutation symmetry to reduce the number of non-trivial terms from factorial growth to a manageable set of topological classes.

## Key numbers

- The number of distinct topological terms for 8 quarks is 4: $[1]$, $[2]$, $[3]$, $[1+1]$
- The number of distinct topological terms for 12 quarks is 10: $[1]$, $[2]$, $[3]$, $[4]$, $[5]$, $[1+1]$, $[1+2]$, $[1+3]$, $[2+2]$, $[1+1+1]$
- The total number of terms in the 8-quark wavefunction is 105, while for 12 quarks it is 10395
- The color matrix element for the reference pairing yields $\langle (12)_0 (34)_0 (56)_0 \ldots | (12)_0 (34)_0 (56)_0 \ldots \rangle = 2^{N/2}$
- The sum of all coefficients including the color matrix element equals $(N/2)!$, which is 24 for the 8-quark case

## Context

This work extends the quark exchange model of Lenz et al. (1986) from $U(1)$ color to $SU(2)$ color, and from the $q^2\overline{q}^2$ system to many-quark matter. The authors compare their full $SU(2)_c$ treatment with the approximate "painted" model of Horowitz and Piekarewicz (1992), where quarks are given fixed colors, and show that the full calculation contains an additional term in the wavefunction that suggests the Fermi "wound" should heal more quickly. The study enables future extension to $SU(3)$ color and three spatial dimensions, and provides a framework for understanding the structure of nuclear matter as a function of density in terms of quark degrees of freedom.
