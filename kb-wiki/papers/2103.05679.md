# Quarkyonic Effective Field Theory, Quark-Nucleon Duality and Ghosts

> We present a field theoretical description of quarkyonic matter consisting of quark, nucleon and ghost fields coupling to mesonic degrees of freedom. The ghosts are present to cancel over-counting of nucleon states that are Pauli blocked by the quark Fermi sea. Such a theory becomes an effective field theory of nucleons at low baryon density, and as such will reproduce nucleonic matter phenomenology. This theory can accommodate chiral symmetry restoration and the dynamical generation of a shell of nucleons at the Fermi surface. It is valid for finite temperature and density. In such a theory, quark-nucleon duality is accomplished by inclusion of ghost fields so that the nucleons extra degrees of freedom, that are beyond those of quarks, are compensated by the ghost fields.

## Key claim

The paper presents a field-theoretical formulation of quarkyonic matter that uses ghost fields with negative metric to cancel the overcounting of nucleon states that are Pauli-blocked by the quark Fermi sea, thereby enforcing quark-nucleon duality. The construction is explicitly conditional on the large-\(N_c\) limit of QCD, where fermion loops are suppressed by \(1/N_c\) and deconfinement at finite density cannot occur until \(\mu_Q \sim \sqrt{N_c} \Lambda_{QCD}\).

## Method

The authors construct a Euclidean action containing unconstrained nucleon fields, quark fields, and ghost nucleon fields (commuting c-number fields rather than Grassmann variables) with identical Lorentz structure and masses but different chemical potentials. The ghost fields are integrated as c-number fields, yielding a determinant with opposite sign to the nucleon determinant, so that the grand potential becomes the sum of quark and nucleon contributions minus the ghost contribution. The formalism is then extended to include excluded-volume effects (van der Waals-type repulsion) and to couple the fields to mesonic degrees of freedom via a sigma-model interaction, where the nucleon and ghost masses are replaced by couplings to scalar sigma and pseudoscalar pion fields.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of nucleon pressure to quark pressure for a free gas is \(P^N / P^Q = N_c^3\), where \(N_c\) is the number of colors.
- The nucleon Fermi momentum is defined as \(k_F^N = \sqrt{\mu_N^2 - M_N^2}\) and the quark Fermi momentum as \(k_F^Q = \sqrt{\mu_Q^2 - M_Q^2}\), with the additive quark-nucleon relation \(k_F^N = N_c k_F^Q\).
- The ghost chemical potential is set to \(\mu_G \sim N_c \mu_Q\), and the ghost density is \(\rho^G = 1/(1 + e^{\beta(E_N - \mu_G)})\).
- The hard-core nucleon density is of order \(n_0 \sim \Lambda_{QCD}^3\), and nucleonic matter density saturates at this scale.
- The degeneracy factor for isodoublet quarks and nucleons is \(g = 2\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of quarkyonic matter introduced by McLerran (2007) and the large-\(N_c\) limit of QCD developed by 't Hooft (1974) and Witten (1979). It provides a field-theoretic foundation for earlier phenomenological models of quarkyonic matter with excluded-volume effects (Jeong 2019) and for studies of the quarkyonic phase diagram at finite temperature (Sen 2020). The formalism enables future dynamical investigations of chiral symmetry restoration in quarkyonic matter, the formation of a nucleon shell at the Fermi surface, and the construction of realistic models of nuclear matter that match known phenomenology at nuclear matter density.
