# Low-lying dipole strengths for probable $p$-wave one-neutron halos in the medium mass region

> The one-neutron halos lying in the island of inversion around $N=20$ has provided the podium, to study the variation of total low-lying dipole strength with the neutron separation energy. We study three probable p-wave one-neutron halo candidates 31Ne and 34Na and 37Mg lying in the island of inversion. A simple analytic model has been used for the calculation of the total low-lying dipole strength for the medium mass p-wave one-neutron halos. A correction factor to this analytical model has been estimated with a realistic Woods-Saxon potential. A comparison of these analytic calculations has been made with the those performed by a finite-range distorted-wave Born approximation theory of the Coulomb dissociation. We also make an estimate of the one-neutron separation energies of 31Ne, 34Na and 37Mg.

## Key claim

The total low-lying dipole strength for three probable p-wave one-neutron halos (³¹Ne, ³⁴Na, ³⁷Mg) can be estimated using a simple analytic model, but only after applying a correction factor derived from a realistic Woods-Saxon potential to account for finite-size effects; the method of comparing analytic and FRDWBA total dipole strengths can yield estimates of one-neutron separation energies consistent with experimental data.

## Method

The authors used an analytic model based on asymptotic forms of bound and continuum wave functions to calculate the dipole strength distribution for transitions from a bound p-state to continuum s- and d-states. They derived a multiplicative correction factor (CF) using a Woods-Saxon potential with radius parameter 1.236 fm and diffuseness 0.62 fm to account for finite core size. The analytic results were compared with finite-range distorted-wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) calculations of Coulomb dissociation on a ²⁰⁸Pb target at beam energies of 234, 100, and 244 MeV/u for ³¹Ne, ³⁴Na, and ³⁷Mg respectively.

## Key numbers

- For ³¹Ne (Sn=0.29 MeV): total integrated dipole strength is 1.3 e²fm² (analytic), 1.1 e²fm² (FRDWBA), and 1.3 e²fm² (model independent); CF=0.3; maximum of strength at Ec=0.05 MeV (p→s) and Ec=0.48 MeV (p→d)
- For ³⁴Na (Sn=0.17 MeV): total integrated dipole strength is 1.7 e²fm² (analytic), 2.0 e²fm² (FRDWBA), and 2.2 e²fm² (model independent); CF=0.23; maximum at Ec=0.03 MeV (p→s) and Ec=0.28 MeV (p→d)
- For ³⁷Mg (Sn=0.35 MeV): total integrated dipole strength is 1.4 e²fm² (analytic), 1.0 e²fm² (FRDWBA), and 1.1 e²fm² (model independent); CF=0.5; maximum at Ec=0.06 MeV (p→s) and Ec=0.58 MeV (p→d)
- Estimated one-neutron separation energies from the scaling method: 0.18 MeV for ³¹Ne, 0.24 MeV for ³⁴Na, and 0.15 MeV for ³⁷Mg
- Percentage contributions: ³¹Ne: 48% p→s, 52% p→d; ³⁴Na: 44% p→s, 56% p→d; ³⁷Mg: 51% p→s, 49% p→d

## Context

This work extends the analytic model of Nagarajan (2005) for dipole strength in one-neutron halos from lighter systems like ¹⁹C to medium-mass nuclei in the island of inversion around N=20. It builds on experimental evidence from Nakamura (2009) for p-wave dominance in ³¹Ne and from Kobayashi (2014) for ³⁷Mg, as well as previous FRDWBA studies by Shubhchintak (2014, 2015) and Singh (2016). The method enables initial estimates of one-neutron separation energies for weakly bound exotic nuclei where experimental data are scarce, and provides a framework for connecting analytic and reaction-theory approaches to dipole strength calculations.
