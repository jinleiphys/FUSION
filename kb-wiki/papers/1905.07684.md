# Thermal Diffusion and Quantum Chaos in Neutral Magnetized Plasma

> We calculate the thermal diffusion constant $D_T$ and butterfly velocity $v_B$ in neutral magnetized plasma using holographic magnetic brane background. We find the thermal diffusion constant satisfies Blake's bound. The constant in the bound $D_T2πT/v_B^2$ is a decreasing function of magnetic field. It approaches one half in the large magnetic field limit. We also find the existence of a special point defined by Lyapunov exponent and butterfly velocity on which pole-skipping phenomenon occurs.

## Key claim

The thermal diffusion constant in neutral magnetized plasma satisfies Blake's bound, but the ratio \(D_T 2\pi T / v_B^2\) is a decreasing function of magnetic field and approaches 1/2 in the large magnetic field limit, not 1 as in the incoherent metal case.

## Method

The authors calculate the thermal diffusion constant \(D_T\) and butterfly velocity \(v_B\) in a neutral magnetized plasma using a holographic magnetic brane background (a 5D Einstein-Maxwell solution that interpolates between an IR fixed point \(BTZ \times R^2\) and asymptotic \(AdS_5\)). The thermal diffusion constant is obtained from magnetohydrodynamics and confirmed by solving constraint equations near the black hole horizon following the method of Donos, Gauntlett, and Ziogas. The butterfly velocity is computed from shock wave solutions in the same background, and the ratio \(\lambda_L D_T / v_B^2\) is evaluated numerically as a function of \(B/T^2\).

## Key numbers

* The ratio \(\lambda_L D_T / v_B^2\) is a monotonically decreasing function of \(B/T^2\) with an asymptotic value of \(1/2\) in the large magnetic field limit.
* In the large \(B\) limit, the analytic metric is \(BTZ \times R^2\) with \(r_h = 2\pi T/3\), yielding \(\lambda_L D_T / v_B^2 = 1/2\).
* The special point (pole-skipping) occurs at \(\omega = i \lambda_L = 2i\pi T\) and \(k = i \lambda_L / v_x\).
* The ratio \(\lambda_L D_T / v_B^2\) equals 1 at \(B/T^2 \approx 33.67\).
* The butterfly velocity in the \(x_1\) direction is \(v_{x1}^2 = 4\pi^2 T^2 / (V_x(r_h) m^2)\), with effective mass \(m^2 = \pi T (2V_x'(r_h) V_y(r_h) + V_y'(r_h) V_x(r_h)) / (V_x(r_h) V_y(r_h))\).

## Context

This work extends the study of Blake's bound on thermal diffusion to neutral magnetized plasma, complementing prior work on charge diffusion in magnetic backgrounds by Li et al. (2018) and on thermal diffusion in incoherent metals by Blake et al. (2016). The holographic model uses the magnetic brane solution of D'Hoker and Kraus (2009), which has an anisotropic IR fixed point \(BTZ \times R^2\) for odd spacetime dimensions, in contrast to the isotropic \(AdS_2 \times R^2\) fixed point in even dimensions. The results enable further tests of the connection between low-energy transport and quantum chaos, and can be generalized to charged magnetized plasma where charge and thermal diffusion mix.
