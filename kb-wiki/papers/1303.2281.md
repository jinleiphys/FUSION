# Incoherent $J/ψ$ electroproduction from the deuteron at JLab energies and the elastic $J/ψ$-nucleon scattering amplitude

> Calculations are presented for incoherent $J/ψ$ electroproduction from the deuteron at JLab energies, including the effects of $J/ψ$-nucleon rescattering in the final state, in order to determine the feasibility of measuring the $J/ψ$-nucleon scattering length, or the $J/ψ$-nucleon scattering amplitude at lower relative energies than in previous measurements. It is shown that for a scattering length of the size predicted by existing theoretical calculations, it would not be possible to determine the scattering length. However, it may be possible to determine the scattering amplitude at significantly lower relative energies than the only previous measurements.

## Key claim

For a J/psi-nucleon scattering length of the size predicted by existing theoretical calculations, it would not be possible to determine the scattering length from incoherent J/psi electroproduction on the deuteron at JLab energies; however, it may be possible to determine the J/psi-nucleon scattering amplitude at significantly lower relative energies than the only previous measurements from SLAC in 1977.

## Method

The authors calculated the invariant amplitudes for the reaction gamma* + d -> J/psi + p + n using Feynman diagrams that include impulse diagrams (quasi-elastic production on one nucleon with the spectator recoiling freely), proton-neutron rescattering diagrams, and J/psi-nucleon rescattering diagrams. They evaluated the one-loop diagrams by integrating over the internal nucleon momentum, separating the on-shell and off-shell parts, and used the Argonne v18 deuteron wavefunction along with experimental parameters for the elementary J/psi production amplitude from Cornell data at photon energies of 9.3 to 10.4 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The threshold virtual photon energy for J/psi electroproduction on a single nucleon (at small Q^2) is nu_thresh approximately 8.2 GeV in the LAB frame
- For an incident virtual photon energy nu = 9 GeV and zero relative momentum of the J/psi-neutron pair, the minimum possible LAB momentum of the neutron is approximately 0.85 GeV; for nu = 6.5 GeV it is approximately 1 GeV
- The condition for only S-wave J/psi-n scattering requires the total kinetic energy in the 2-body center-of-mass frame T* to be much less than 30 MeV, taking the interaction range as approximately 1 fm
- The elementary J/psi production amplitude parameters from Cornell data (nu = 9.3 to 10.4 GeV) are A1 = 1.1 +/- 0.17 nb/GeV^2 and B1 = 1.31 +/- 0.19 GeV^{-2}
- For the J/psi-n rescattering diagram (F3a) at both nu = 9 GeV and nu = 6.5 GeV with T*_Vn = 30 MeV, the lower integration limit n- is greater than approximately 0.6 GeV for all theta_cm

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for extracting the J/psi-nucleon scattering amplitude from electroproduction on the deuteron, motivated by a JLab proposal (JLab 2010) to measure the J/psi-nucleon scattering length. The only previous experimental measurement of elastic J/psi-nucleon scattering was at SLAC in 1977, where the total cross-section was extracted using an optical model for J/psi rescattering on nuclei. Theoretical calculations by Savage (1992), Brodsky (1997), Kawanai (2010), and Hayashigaki (1998) predict a J/psi-nucleon scattering length too small to produce a J/psi-nucleon bound state but potentially large enough for a nucleus-J/psi bound state. The results enable future experimental analysis at JLab's 12 GeV upgrade to extract the J/psi-nucleon scattering amplitude at lower relative energies than the 1977 SLAC measurement, provided the amplitude is somewhat larger than the SLAC value.
