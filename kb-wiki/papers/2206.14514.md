# Quasi-bound state in the $\bar{K}NNN$ system

> The paper is devoted to the $\bar{K}NNN$ system, which is an exotic system consisting of an antikaon and three nucleons. Dynamically exact four-body Faddeev-type equations were solved and characteristics of the quasi-bound state in the system were evaluated. Three antikaon-nucleon and three nucleon-nucleon potentials were used, so the dependence of the four-body pole positions on the two-body interaction models was studied. The resulting binding energies $B^{\rm Chiral}_{K^-ppn} \sim 30.5 - 34.5$ MeV obtained with chirally motivated and $B^{\rm SIDD}_{K^-ppn} \sim 46.4 - 52.0$ MeV obtained with phenomenological antikaon-nucleon potentials are close to those obtained for the $K^- pp$ system with the same $\bar{K}N$ and $NN$ potentials, while the four-body widths $Γ_{K^- ppn} \sim 38.2 - 50.9$ MeV are smaller.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a quasi-bound state exists in the $\bar{K}NNN$ system (specifically the $K^-ppn$ or $\bar{K}^0nnp$ configuration), with binding energies and widths that depend strongly on the choice of antikaon-nucleon interaction model; the four-body widths are smaller than those of the three-body $K^-pp$ system, while the binding energies are similar.

## Method

Dynamically exact four-body Faddeev-type equations in the Grassberger-Sandhas form were solved in momentum representation, using separable two-body $T$-matrices as input and constructing separable three-body amplitudes via the Energy Dependent Pole Approximation (EDPA). Three different separable antikaon-nucleon potentials (two phenomenological SIDD models and one chirally motivated model, each in exact optical one-channel form) and three different separable nucleon-nucleon potentials (TSA-A, TSA-B, and TSN) were used as two-body inputs, and the four-body pole positions were evaluated for the $K^-ppn$ system with total isospin zero and spin one-half.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies with chirally motivated $\bar{K}N$ potential: $B^{\rm Chiral}_{K^-ppn} \sim 30.5 - 34.5$ MeV across the three $NN$ models.
- Binding energies with phenomenological SIDD $\bar{K}N$ potentials: $B^{\rm SIDD}_{K^-ppn} \sim 46.4 - 52.0$ MeV across the three $NN$ models.
- Four-body widths: $\Gamma_{K^- ppn} \sim 38.2 - 50.9$ MeV across all combinations of $\bar{K}N$ and $NN$ potentials.
- For the $K^-pp$ three-body subsystem with the TSN $NN$ potential: binding energies range from $29.9$ MeV (chiral) to $53.3$ MeV (1,SIDD) and widths from $48.2$ MeV (chiral) to $64.7$ MeV (1,SIDD).
- For the $K^-np$ three-body subsystem with the TSN $NN$ potential: binding energies range from $1.9$ MeV (1,SIDD) to $5.6$ MeV (2,SIDD) and widths from $45.5$ MeV (chiral) to $68.7$ MeV (1,SIDD).
- For the $NNN$ three-body subsystem: binding energies are $9.03$ MeV (TSA-A), $9.04$ MeV (TSA-B), and $9.52$ MeV (TSN).
- For the $2+2$ partition $\bar{K}N+NN$ with the TSN $NN$ potential: binding energies range from $17.9$ MeV (chiral) to $25.1$ MeV (2,SIDD) and widths from $53.7$ MeV (chiral) to $82.2$ MeV (1,SIDD).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous series of calculations on the three-body $\bar{K}NN$ and $\bar{K}\bar{K}N$ systems (Shevchenko, 2015-2022) using the same Faddeev-type AGS formalism and the same set of $\bar{K}N$ and $NN$ potentials. Prior four-body calculations of the $\bar{K}NNN$ system existed (e.g., by Akaishi and Yamazaki, and by other groups), but this is the first dynamically exact four-body Faddeev-type calculation that can treat energy-dependent $\bar{K}N$ potentials exactly. The results enable comparison with future experimental searches for antikaon-nucleon quasi-bound states beyond the $K^-pp$ system, and they show that the four-body widths are systematically smaller than the corresponding three-body widths, which may aid experimental identification.
