# The phase diagram of QCD in a BAG+HRG based equation of state: appearance of a pseudo-critical point

> Mapping the QCD phase boundary and locating critical end point still remains as an open problem in strong interaction physics. Predictions about the co-ordinates of the critical point in the $(T, μ_B)$ plane, from different QCD motivated models show a wide variation. Lattice QCD calculations are also available, that give an estimation of the critical point for chiral phase transition, where the transition changes its nature from rapid cross over to first order transition. Recently co-ordinates of the critical point for deconfinement phase transition are claimed to be found as an endpoint of the first order phase transition line, in Bag model scenario. In the present paper we have shown that Bag model gives a complete first order phase transition line in the $(T, μ_B)$ plane, and one can not have any point where the transition changes its nature.

## Key claim
The Bag model combined with a hadron resonance gas (HRG) equation of state yields a complete first-order phase transition line in the (T, μ_B) plane, with no point where the transition changes its nature; the apparent critical endpoint reported in prior work by Singh et al. (2009) is a "pseudo-critical point" that arises from insufficient numerical resolution in the μ_B-scan method.

## Method
The authors constructed the QCD phase boundary by applying Gibbs' equilibrium criterion (pressure equality) between a weakly interacting quark-gluon plasma (QGP) described by a Bag model equation of state with perturbative α_s corrections, and a hadronic phase modeled as an excluded-volume hadron resonance gas (HRG) including all hadrons and resonances up to 2.5 GeV mass. They performed two numerical zero-search procedures: a μ_B-scan (fixing T and varying μ_B in steps of 5 MeV) and a T-scan (fixing μ_B and varying T in steps of 5 MeV), then systematically reduced the step length in the μ_B-scan to test the stability of the apparent endpoint.

## Key numbers
- For the μ_B-scan with step length δT = 5 MeV, the first-order transition line appeared to end at a point near (T_c ≈ 160 MeV, μ_c ≈ 156 MeV) for hard-core radius r_c = 0.6 fm and Bag constant B^{1/4} = 216 MeV, and at (T_c = 146 MeV, μ_c = 156 MeV) for r_c = 0.8 fm and B^{1/4} = 200 MeV.
- The T-scan with the same step length (δT = 5 MeV) produced a phase boundary reaching μ_B = 0, contradicting the existence of an endpoint.
- For B^{1/4} = 200 MeV and excluded volume v_ex = 1 fm³, reducing the μ_B-scan step length from δT = 5 MeV to smaller values caused the apparent endpoint to move toward the T-axis, eventually reaching μ_B = 0.
- At μ_B = 0, a first-order transition was found at T = 129.6 MeV for B^{1/4} = 200 MeV, as shown by a kink in the Helmholtz free energy density.

## Context
This work extends the line of Bag model plus HRG phase boundary calculations previously performed by Satz, Dixit, Cleymans, Magas, and Castorina (none of whom reported a critical endpoint), and directly contradicts the claim by Singh et al. (2009) that a critical endpoint for deconfinement exists in this framework. The authors argue that while lattice QCD calculations (e.g., Cheng 2007, Aoki 2009) support the existence of a true QCD critical point, the Bag model + HRG approach cannot locate it; the apparent endpoint is a numerical artifact. This result clarifies that future searches for the QCD critical point must rely on methods beyond simple Bag model + HRG comparisons.
