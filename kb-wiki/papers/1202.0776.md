# Stimulated Neutrino Transformation with Sinusoidal Density Profiles

> Large amplitude oscillations between the states of a quantum system can be stimulated by sinusoidal external potentials with frequencies that are similar to the energy level splitting of the states or a fraction thereof. Situations when the applied frequency is equal to an integer fraction of the energy level splittings are known as parametric resonances. We investigate this effect for neutrinos both analytically and numerically for the case of arbitrary numbers of neutrino flavors. We look for environments where the effect may be observed and find that supernova are the one realistic possibility due to the necessity of both large densities and large amplitude fluctuations. The comparison of numerical and analytic results of neutrino propagation through a model supernova reveals it is possible to predict the locations and strengths of the stimulated transitions that occur.

# Stimulated Neutrino Transformation with Sinusoidal Density Profiles

## Key claim

Large-amplitude neutrino flavor transformations can be stimulated by sinusoidal density fluctuations when the fluctuation frequency matches an integer fraction of the energy-level splitting between neutrino matter eigenstates (parametric resonance), but the effect is only realistically observable in supernovae because it requires both high matter densities and large-amplitude fluctuations simultaneously.

## Method

The authors derived analytic solutions for the matter-basis transition probabilities of neutrinos propagating through a constant matter density with a superimposed sinusoidal perturbation, using the Rotating Wave Approximation (RWA) to isolate the resonant Fourier component for each pair of matter eigenstates. They then applied these analytic results to a supernova test problem by superposing a single sinusoidal fluctuation (reduced wavelength 10 km, amplitude 10% of the base density) onto a parametrized supernova density profile from Fogli et al. (2003), and compared the predicted transition locations and strengths against exact numerical three-flavor neutrino evolution calculations using a Runge-Kutta integrator.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino energy: 20 MeV; vacuum mixing angle: 0.573 degrees; mass splitting: 3 x 10^{-3} eV^2; base density V_star set to one half the MSW resonance density; fluctuation amplitude C_star = 0.1; sinusoidal wavelength lambda_star = 5.27 km (test problem for n=1 resonance).
- For the n=1 parametric resonance at maximal conditions, the transition wavelength lambda_1 = 2 pi / |kappa_1| is at least 10,000 km for terrestrial densities (1 g/cm^3) with C_star = 1.
- For solar core densities (rho ~ 100 g/cm^3) with C_star ~ 10^{-5}, the transition wavelength lambda_1 ~ 10^6 km, larger than the solar radius.
- For supernova H-resonance densities (~10^4 g/cm^3) with C_star = 1%-10%, the transition wavelength lambda_1 ~ 100-1000 km.
- In the supernova test problem: mass splittings delta m_{12}^2 = 3 x 10^{-3} eV^2, delta m_{23}^2 = 8 x 10^{-5} eV^2; mixing angles theta_{12}=33 degrees, theta_{13}=9 degrees, theta_{23}=45 degrees; CP phase delta=0; neutrino energy 20 MeV; perturbation reduced wavelength = 10 km; amplitude C_star = 0.1.
- The width of the n-th parametric resonance in k_star space is Delta k_star = 4|kappa_n|/n, and for small z_{12} the width scales as C_star^n.

## Context

This work extends the study of parametric resonances in neutrino oscillations, previously investigated by Akhmedov (1999) and Ermilova et al. (1986), by deriving new analytic expressions valid for arbitrary numbers of neutrino flavors, at all ambient densities, and both on and off parametric resonance. The authors find that stimulated transitions are unlikely in terrestrial experiments (consistent with Akhmedov (2001) and Jacobsson et al. (2002)) or in the Sun (studied by Loreti et al. (1994) and Haxton and Zhang (1992)), but are viable in supernovae where large densities and large-amplitude fluctuations coexist. The work enables future studies of neutrino propagation through turbulent supernova density profiles, where multiple Fourier components may simultaneously drive transitions between multiple matter eigenstates.
