# Double Giant Dipole Resonance in ^{208}Pb

> Double-dipole excitations in ^{208}Pb are analyzed within a microscopic model explicitly treating 2p2h-excitations. Collective states built from such 2p2h-excitations are shown to appear at about twice the energy of the isovector giant dipole resonance, in agreement with the experimental findings. The calculated cross section for Coulomb excitation at relativistic energies cannot explain simultaneously the measured single-dipole and double-dipole cross sections, however.

## Key claim
The microscopic model reproduces the DGDR energy at about twice the GDR energy and the fragmentation width, but the calculated Coulomb excitation cross sections cannot simultaneously match the measured single-dipole and double-dipole cross sections for any single choice of the minimum impact parameter.

## Method
The authors diagonalize a nuclear Hamiltonian in a model space of 1p1h and 2p2h excitations, using Woods-Saxon single-particle states over three major shells with continuum states discretized in a harmonic oscillator basis. They select 2p2h states with energy below 40 MeV and a double-dipole matrix element exceeding 5×10⁻⁵ of the total strength, reducing the space to 996 states for Jπ=0⁺ and 2011 for Jπ=2⁺. The single-particle energies are scaled by an effective mass m*/m = 0.75 to reproduce the experimental GDR energy and strength. Coulomb excitation cross sections are computed using second-order perturbation theory in a semiclassical straight-line trajectory approach.

## Key numbers
- GDR mean energy after scaling: 13.5 MeV (with 131% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule)
- DGDR mean energy: 26.96 MeV for 0⁺, 26.53 MeV for 2⁺ (about twice the GDR energy)
- DGDR fragmentation width: 3.48 MeV for 0⁺, 3.36 MeV for 2⁺ (factor 1.24–1.29 larger than GDR width of 2.70 MeV)
- For ²⁰⁸Pb projectile on Pb target at 640 MeV/A with Rmin = r0(At¹/³+Ap¹/³), r0 = 1.31 fm: GDR cross section 4.42 b (experiment 3.28 b), DGDR cross section 0.361 b (experiment 0.38 b)
- For r0 = 1.50 fm: GDR cross section 3.47 b (experiment 3.28 b), DGDR cross section 0.162 b (experiment 0.38 b)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous microscopic model for double-dipole excitations in ⁴⁰Ca (Nishizaki, 1995) to the heavy nucleus ²⁰⁸Pb. It addresses the discrepancy between measured DGDR cross sections and theoretical estimates previously noted by Ponomarev et al. (1996) for ¹³⁶Xe and by Boretzky et al. (1996) for ²⁰⁸Pb. The results show that the simple folding model of Boretzky et al. predicts only a 33% enhancement of the DGDR cross section while reproducing the GDR cross section, whereas the microscopic model cannot simultaneously fit both, leaving open whether the discrepancy arises from nuclear structure or reaction mechanism treatment.
