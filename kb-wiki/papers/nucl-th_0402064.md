# Quark structure and nuclear effective forces

> We formulate the quark meson coupling model as a many-body effective Hamiltonian. This leads naturally to the appearance of many-body forces. We investigate the zero range limit of the model and compare its Hartree-Fock Hamiltonian to that corresponding to the Skyrme effective force. By fixing the three parameters of the model to reproduce the binding and symmetry energy of nuclear matter, we find that it allows a very satisfactory interpretation of the Skyrme force.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nuclear effective force itself is a direct manifestation of the quark structure of the nucleon, and that the quark meson coupling (QMC) model, when formulated as a many-body effective Hamiltonian, yields an effective interaction that agrees remarkably well with the phenomenological Skyrme force SkIII, provided the three free parameters are fixed to reproduce the binding energy, symmetry energy, and saturation density of nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors formulate the quark meson coupling (QMC) model as a many-body effective Hamiltonian by solving for the quark structure of nucleons (treated as non-overlapping quark bags) in the presence of classical scalar (σ) and vector (ω, ρ) meson fields, using a Born-Oppenheimer approximation and expanding the fields around the bag center. They then take the zero-range limit of the model, eliminate the meson fields using the field equations, and derive a quantum effective Hamiltonian containing two-, three-, and four-body contact interactions. The three free parameters (G_σ, G_ω, G_ρ) are fixed by fitting the volume coefficient a₁ = -15.85 MeV and symmetry coefficient a₄ = 30 MeV of nuclear matter binding energy, plus the saturation condition at ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, and the resulting Hamiltonian is compared term-by-term with the Skyrme force SkIII via their respective Hartree-Fock Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Volume coefficient of binding energy: a₁ = -15.85 MeV
- Symmetry coefficient: a₄ = 30 MeV
- For bag radius R_B = 0.8 fm and m_σ = 500 MeV: G_σ = 12.63 fm², G_ω = 9.62 fm², G_ρ = 9.68 fm²
- For bag radius R_B = 0.8 fm and m_σ = 600 MeV: G_σ = 11.97 fm², G_ω = 8.1 fm², G_ρ = 6.46 fm²
- QMC-derived Skyrme parameters for m_σ = 600 MeV: t₀ = -1082 MeV·fm³, x₀ = 0.59, t₃ = 14926 MeV·fm⁶, 3t₁+5t₂ = 475 MeV·fm⁵, 5t₂-9t₁ = -4330 MeV·fm⁵, W₀ = 97 MeV·fm⁵, compressibility K = 327 MeV
- For comparison, SkIII parameters: t₀ = -1129 MeV·fm³, x₀ = 0.45, t₃ = 14000 MeV·fm⁶, 3t₁+5t₂ = 710 MeV·fm⁵, 5t₂-9t₁ = -4030 MeV·fm⁵, W₀ = 120 MeV·fm⁵, K = 355 MeV
- The scalar polarizability parameter d = 0.22 R_B (with R_B in fm)
- The convergence parameter d·g_σ σ ≈ 0.33 at nuclear matter density
- The compressibility decreases by 37 MeV when the 4-body force is restored

## Context

This work extends the quark meson coupling model of Guichon (1988, 1996) by formulating it as a many-body effective Hamiltonian and taking the zero-range limit, enabling direct comparison with the Skyrme effective force pioneered by Skyrme (1955). The paper demonstrates that the QMC model can provide a microscopic interpretation of the phenomenologically successful Skyrme force SkIII (Friedrich and Reinhard, 1986), suggesting that the response of nucleon internal structure to the nuclear medium plays a vital role in nuclear structure. This enables future work to include long-range pion forces and to improve the treatment of momentum-dependent terms, with the goal of achieving a more fundamental understanding of nuclear effective interactions from quark degrees of freedom.
