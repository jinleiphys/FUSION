# Multiple reflection-asymmetric type band structures in $^{220}Th$ and dinuclear model

> The negative parity bands in $^{220}$Th are analyzed within the dinuclear system model which was previously used for describing the alternating-parity bands in deformed actinides. The model is based on the assumption that cluster type shapes are produced by the motion of nuclear system in the mass-asymmetry coordinate. To describe the reflection-asymmetric collective modes characterized by nonzero values of $K$, the intrinsic excitations of clusters are taken into account. The angular momentum dependence of the parity splitting and the staggering behavior of the $B(E1)/B(E2)$ ratios as functions of angular momentum are explained.

## Key claim
The negative parity bands in $^{220}$Th, including those with nonzero $K$ values, can be described by extending the dinuclear system model to include harmonic quadrupole oscillations of the heavy cluster, under the condition that the heavy fragment is nearly spherical and the light fragment (alpha-particle) can roll along its surface.

## Method
The authors model $^{220}$Th as a dinuclear system consisting of a nearly spherical heavy fragment and an alpha-particle light fragment. The Hamiltonian includes harmonic quadrupole oscillations of the heavy fragment and relative rotations of the two fragments, with an interaction term coupling these degrees of freedom. The wave functions are constructed as superpositions of basis states combining the quadrupole phonon states of the heavy fragment with the rotational wave functions of the relative motion, and the Hamiltonian is diagonalized numerically. Electric multipole transition probabilities are calculated using the resulting wave functions and the dinuclear system's charge distribution.

## Key numbers
- The energy differences between neighboring states in the ground-state band and first negative parity band vary from 185 keV up to 240 keV across the observed angular momentum range.
- The parity splitting $S(I^-)$ becomes negative for higher angular momenta when $\frac{\hbar^2}{2\mu R^2} < \frac{1}{2}\omega$, where $\hbar\omega$ is the quadrupole vibration frequency and $\frac{\hbar^2}{2\mu R^2}$ is the rotational energy parameter of the light fragment.
- The $B(E2)$ values for intraband transitions increase linearly with angular momentum $I$, as expected for harmonic quadrupole oscillations.
- The $B(E1)/B(E2)$ ratios show staggering: ratios for odd initial angular momentum (transitions from negative parity states) lie systematically lower than ratios for even initial angular momentum (transitions from ground-state band states), except for two data points at $13^-$ and $14^+$.

## Context
This work extends the dinuclear system model previously applied to alternating-parity bands in deformed actinides (Shneidman et al., earlier publications) to describe multiple negative parity bands with nonzero $K$ in $^{220}$Th. The structure of $^{220}$Th had been analyzed in several theoretical publications (Reviol, Nazarewicz, Otsuka). The model enables description of the angular momentum dependence of parity splitting and the staggering behavior of $B(E1)/B(E2)$ ratios, which were challenges for previous theoretical approaches.
