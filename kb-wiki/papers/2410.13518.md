# Proton internal pressure from deeply virtual Compton scattering on collider kinematics

> The unique experimental connection to the QCD energy-momentum tensor offered by generalised parton distributions has been strongly highlighted in the past few years with attempts to extract the pressure and shear forces distributions within the nucleon. If, in principle, this can be performed in a model independent way from experimental data, in practice, the current limited precision and kinematic coverage make such an extraction very challenging. Moreover, the limitation to a leading-order description in the strong coupling of the data has provided only an indirect and weakly sensitive access to gluon degrees of freedoms, solely through their mixing to quarks via evolution. In this paper we address this issue by pro viding a next-to-leading order formalism allowing a reanalysis of global fits with genuine gluonic degrees of freedom. In addition, we provide an estimate of the reduction in uncertainty that could stem from the extended kinematic range relevant for the future Electron Ion Collider. Finally, we stress the connection between the analysis of the dispersion relation in terms of generalised parton distributions and the deconvolution problem.

## Key claim

The paper provides a next-to-leading order (NLO) formalism for the dispersion relation of deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) amplitudes, enabling a reanalysis of global fits with genuine gluonic degrees of freedom, but the extraction of the D-term and gravitational form factors remains plagued by shadow D-term contributions that limit model-independent access to the proton's mechanical properties.

## Method

The authors derive a generalised dispersion relation for Compton Form Factors (CFFs) at any order in perturbation theory using analytic properties of the scattering amplitude in the complex plane, connecting the real and imaginary parts of CFFs to the D-term and double distributions. They then apply this formalism to an existing global fit of DVCS data to perform the first NLO extraction of the subtraction constant, and estimate the reduction in uncertainty from the extended kinematic range of the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC).

## Key numbers

- The subtraction constant for quarks is given by \( \mathcal{S}^q = \int_{-1}^1 \Re T^{q}(\omega) D^q(\omega) \,\mathrm{d}\omega \), where \( T^q \) is the DVCS coefficient function and \( D^q \) is the D-term.
- The gravitational form factor \( C_a(t) \) is related to the D-term by \( C_a(t) = \frac{1}{4}\int_{-1}^{1} \mathrm{d}\alpha\,\alpha^{1-p_a} D^a(\alpha,t) \), with \( p_q = 0 \) for quarks and \( p_g = 1 \) for gluons.
- The \( \ell^{\text{th}} \) Mellin moment of the imaginary part of the CFF is connected to the \( \beta \)-moment of the double distribution via \( \frac{2}{\pi}\int_{0}^1\Im \mathcal{H}(\xi')\left(\xi'\right)^{\ell}\,\mathrm{d}\xi' = \frac{1}{\ell!}\int_{-1}^1\mathrm{d}\alpha \frac{\partial^{\ell} T}{(\partial \alpha)^{\ell}}(\alpha) \int_{-1+|\alpha|}^{1-|\alpha|}\mathrm{d}\beta \beta^{\ell} f^q(\beta,\alpha) \).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order dispersive analysis of DVCS data performed by Dutrieux et al. (2021) by providing a full NLO treatment that includes direct gluon contributions to the subtraction constant, which were previously only accessible indirectly through mixing with quarks via evolution. The formalism builds on earlier dispersion relation derivations by Teryaev (2005), Anikin (2007), and Diehl (2007), and addresses the deconvolution problem identified by Bertone (2021) that prevents unambiguous GPD extraction. The results enable future experimental programs at the EIC, EicC, and LHeC to extract independent quark and gluon contributions to the proton's pressure and shear force distributions.
