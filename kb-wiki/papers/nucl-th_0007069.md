# Microscopic substructure effects in potential-model descriptions of the $^7$Be$(p,γ)^8$B reaction

> The spectroscopic factor arises from short-range effects in the nuclear wave function. On the other hand, cross sections for external capture reactions, such as $^7$Be$(p,γ)^8$B at low energies, depend primarily on the asymptotic normalization of the bound-state wave function -- a long-range property. We investigate the relationship between potential models and the full many-body problem to illustrate how microscopic substructure effects arise naturally in the relevant transition matrix element and can be (in part) accounted for by a spectroscopic factor.

## Key claim

The spectroscopic factor should be explicitly included in potential-model calculations of the $^7$Be(p,$\gamma$)$^8$B cross section at low energies, contrary to a recent argument by Csoto (2000) that it should be omitted because the reaction depends only on the asymptotic normalization of the bound-state wave function. The short-range and long-range parts of the wave function are not independent, and the spectroscopic factor arises naturally at second order in a perturbative treatment of the many-body problem.

## Method

The authors expand the eight-body wave functions for $^8$B and the $^7$Be-p scattering state in a complete set of seven-body $^7$Be eigenstates, obtaining exact coupled Schr\"odinger-like equations for the expansion coefficients. They treat the off-diagonal coupling matrix elements $V_{nm}$ between different $^7$Be configurations as a perturbation, identifying the zeroth-order approximation as the potential-model wave function (the spectroscopic amplitude) and showing that the spectroscopic factor appears as the norm of the bound-state coefficient at second order.

## Key numbers

- The first excited state of $^7$Be lies at $E^2_7 = 0.429$ MeV, below which the $n=1$ term dominates the long-range behavior of the scattering wave function.
- The $^7$Be(p,$\gamma$)$^8$B reaction at solar energies occurs at center-of-mass energies $E_{cm} \leq 20$ keV.
- The asymptotic normalization of the $^8$B bound state is given by $A_n = \lim_{\rho_7 \rightarrow \infty} \phi^1_B(\rhov_7) \rho_7 \exp[+\sqrt{2 \mu_7 (E^n_7-E^B_8)} \, \rho_7 ]$, where $\mu_7$ is the reduced mass of the eight-body system.

## Context

This work directly addresses the argument of Csoto (2000) that spectroscopic factors should not be included in potential-model calculations of the $^7$Be(p,$\gamma$)$^8$B reaction, and builds on the connection between short-range and long-range parts of the wave function outlined by Jennings (2000). The paper provides a formal many-body justification for including spectroscopic factors in external capture reactions at low energies, while noting that at higher energies additional microscopic effects beyond the spectroscopic factor become important.
