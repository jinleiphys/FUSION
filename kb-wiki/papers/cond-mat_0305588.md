# Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory versus local-density approximation for superfluid trapped fermionic atoms

> We investigate a gas of superfluid fermionic atoms trapped in two hyperfine states by a spherical harmonic potential. We propose a new regularization method to remove the ultraviolet divergence in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations caused by the use of a zero-range atom-atom interaction. Compared with a method used in the literature, our method is simpler and has improved convergence properties. Then we compare Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the semiclassical local-density approximation. We observe that for systems containing a small number of atoms shell effects, which cannot be reproduced by the semiclassical calculation, are very important. For systems with a large number of atoms at zero temperature the two calculations are in quite good agreement, which, however, is deteriorated at non-zero temperature, especially near the critical temperature. In this case the different behavior can be explained within the Ginzburg-Landau theory.

## Key claim

The authors propose a simpler regularization method for the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations that removes the ultraviolet divergence from zero-range interactions, and they show that the agreement between HFB and local-density approximation (LDA) calculations is good at zero temperature for large atom numbers but deteriorates at non-zero temperature, especially near the critical temperature, where the LDA systematically overestimates the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors solved the HFB (Bogoliubov-de Gennes) equations for a spherically symmetric harmonic trap with a zero-range contact interaction, using a new regularization scheme based on the Thomas-Fermi approximation for the Green's function that replaces the local Fermi momentum with the full momentum including the Hartree-Fock mean field. They compared these results with LDA calculations, where the system is treated locally as infinite homogeneous matter, and also derived analytic expressions for the gap within LDA for both regularization schemes.

## Key numbers

- For a chemical potential μ = 32 ℏω (N ≈ 1.7 × 10⁴ atoms) and coupling g = -1 (in trap units), the pairing field at the center Δ(0) converges to about 6.86 ℏω using regularization method (b) at high cutoff, while method (a) still gives only 6.37 ℏω at N_C = 10³ and converges only at N_C = 10⁶.
- With regularization method (b), HFB gives Δ(0) = 6.81 ℏω for cutoff N_C = 100 and 6.86 ℏω for N_C = 125, showing fast convergence.
- The pairing field at the center becomes zero at harmonic oscillator magic numbers N = 240, 330, 440, ... for small systems.
- The LDA and HFB results agree reasonably for N > 5000 atoms at zero temperature.
- The criterion for LDA validity is Δ_LDA > ℏω; the local critical temperature within LDA is T_C ≈ 0.57 Δ₀, where Δ₀ is the zero-temperature gap.

## Context

This work extends the HFB approach for trapped fermionic atoms introduced by Bruun and Heiselberg (2002) and Bruun (2002), who first applied the method from nuclear physics to ultracold gases. The authors improve upon the regularization prescription used in those earlier works by adopting a method originally developed for nuclear HFB calculations by Bulgac (2002), which has better convergence properties. The paper enables more reliable comparisons between fully quantum HFB calculations and semiclassical LDA calculations, showing that LDA fails at non-zero temperature even for large systems, and that the Ginzburg-Landau theory of Baranov and Petrov (1998) correctly predicts the Gaussian shape of the pairing field near the critical temperature.
