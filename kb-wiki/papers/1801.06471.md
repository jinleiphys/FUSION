# Nuclear structure calculations for neutron-star crusts

> The goal of this paper is to investigate properties of clusterized nuclear matter which is believed to be present in crusts of neutron stars at subnuclear densities. It is assumed that the whole system can be represented by the set of Wigner-Seitz cells, each containing a nucleus and an electron background under the condition of electroneutrality. The nuclear structure calculations are performed within the relativistic mean-field model with the NL3 parametrization. The first set of calculations is performed assuming the constant electron background. The evolution of neutron and proton density distributions was systematically studied along isotopic chains until very neutron-rich system beyond the neutron dripline. Then we have replaced the uniform electron background with the realistic electron distributions, obtained within the Thomas-Fermi approximation in a self-consistent way with the proton distributions. Finally, we have investigated the evolution of the $β$-stability valley as well as neutron and proton driplines with the electron density.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how the properties of clusterized nuclear matter in neutron-star crusts change when the electron background is treated realistically via the Thomas-Fermi approximation rather than as a uniform density, finding that the beta-stability valley and proton dripline shift to more proton-rich systems due to the stabilizing effect of electrons.

## Method

Nuclear structure calculations were performed within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model using the NL3 parametrization, treating the system as a set of Wigner-Seitz cells each containing a nucleus and an electron background under electroneutrality. The authors first assumed a constant electron background and systematically studied neutron and proton density distributions along isotopic chains beyond the neutron dripline, then replaced the uniform electron background with realistic electron distributions obtained self-consistently from the Thomas-Fermi approximation. A constraint requiring zero slope of the neutron density at the cell boundary was implemented to conserve particle number and avoid artifacts from density buildup at the cell edge.

## Key numbers

- The neutron number was reduced by up to 15% in test runs using the averaging procedure from Negele (1973), motivating the new constraint method.
- For electron Fermi momenta of approximately 0.05 per femtometer, the beta-stability line reaches the neutron dripline, and free neutrons occur.
- The electron-to-baryon fraction was fixed at 0.3 in the earlier work by Ebel (2015) that developed the realistic electrostatic potentials used here.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RMF-NL3 calculations from Burvenich (2007), which studied the influence of a uniform electron background on nuclear ground states and decay modes, by now including dripping-out neutrons that populate unbound states and using realistic electron distributions. The approach builds on the seminal Wigner-Seitz cell framework from Baym (1971) and Negele (1973), and the realistic electrostatic treatment follows from Ebel (2015). The results enable more accurate modeling of the nuclear composition in neutron-star crusts and supernova matter, particularly for determining the beta-stability valley and dripline positions at subnuclear densities.
