# Transverse strange quark spin structure of the nucleon

> We investigate the transverse quark spin densities of the nucleon with the lowest moment within the framework of the SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model, emphasizing the strange quark spin density. Based on previous results of the vector and tensor form factors, we are able to determine the impact-parameter dependent probability densities of transversely polarized quarks in an unpolarized nucleon as well as those of unpolarized quarks in a transversely polarized nucleon. We find that the present numerical results for the transverse spin densities of the up and down quarks are in good agreement with those of the lattice calculation. We predict the transvere spin densities of the strange quark. It turns out that the polarized strange quark is noticeably distorted in an unpolarized proton.

# Transverse strange quark spin structure of the nucleon

## Key claim
Within the SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model, the transverse spin densities of up and down quarks agree well with lattice QCD results, and the model predicts that polarized strange quarks in an unpolarized proton are noticeably distorted in impact-parameter space, even though the strange quark anomalous tensor magnetic moment is almost compatible with zero.

## Method
The authors used previously computed vector and tensor form factors from the SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model (chiQSM) with a dynamical quark mass of 420 MeV, including explicit SU(3) symmetry breaking and the full Dirac sea. They performed Fourier transforms of the flavor-decomposed form factors (F1, F2, HT, ET, and tilde-HT) to obtain b-dependent densities, then evaluated the transverse quark spin densities for the lowest moment using the formalism of Diehl (2005) and the lattice QCD approach of Gockeler (2006).

## Key numbers
- Up quark anomalous magnetic moment: kappa^u = 1.35; down quark: kappa^d = -1.80; ratio kappa^u/kappa^d = 0.75 (phenomenological values: kappa^u = 1.67, kappa^d = -2.03, ratio 0.82)
- Proton anomalous magnetic moment from chiQSM: kappa_p = 1.49 (experimental value: kappa_p^Exp = 1.79)
- Up quark anomalous tensor magnetic moment: kappa_T^u = 3.56; down quark: kappa_T^d = 1.83 (lattice QCD at mu^2 = 0.36 GeV^2: kappa_T^u = 3.70, kappa_T^d = 2.35)
- Strange quark magnetic moment: mu_s = 0.12 mu_N
- Strange quark anomalous tensor magnetic moment: kappa_T^s = 0.01 (essentially zero)
- Strange Pauli form factor F2^s becomes negative from Q^2 approximately 0.2 GeV^2

## Context
This work extends the analysis of transverse spin densities from lattice QCD (Gockeler 2006) and the formalism of impact-parameter dependent parton distributions (Burkardt 2000, 2002; Diehl 2005) to the strange quark sector using the SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model. The authors had previously computed the nucleon vector form factors (Silva 2001, 2002) and tensor form factors (Ledwig 2010) within the same framework. The results enable predictions for the spatial distribution of strange quark transverse spin in the nucleon, which can be tested by future lattice QCD calculations or experiments sensitive to transversity.
