# Final state interactions in two-particle interferometry

> We reconsider the influence of two-particle final state interactions (FSI) on two-particle Bose-Einstein interferometry. We concentrate in particular on the problem of particle emission at different times. Assuming chaoticity of the source, we derive a new general expression for the symmetrized two-particle cross section. We discuss the approximations needed to derive from the general result the Koonin-Pratt formula. Introducing a less stringent version of the so-called smoothness approximation we also derive a more accurate formula. It can be implemented into classical event generators and allows to calculate FSI corrected two-particle correlation functions via modified Bose-Einstein "weights".

## Key claim

The paper derives a new general expression for the symmetrized two-particle cross section including final state interactions (FSI) for a chaotic source, explicitly accounting for the free propagation of the first emitted particle between different emission times, an aspect the authors claim was not fully discussed in previous FSI studies.

## Method

The authors start from a non-relativistic quantum mechanical treatment of two-particle emission, using the two-particle Schrödinger equation with a potential for the FSI. They express the measured two-particle momentum amplitude as an overlap integral at the earliest time when both particles are present, then factorize the two-particle wave function into single-particle states assuming independent emission. The final two-particle cross section is obtained by averaging over the source density matrix, introducing the single-particle Wigner density S(x,K), and performing the time integrations using residue calculus.

## Key numbers

- The total two-particle probability is given by \( P_2({\bf p}_a,{\bf p}_b) = 2 P^{(aa)}({\bf p}_a,{\bf p}_b) \), where \( P^{(aa)} \) is defined in Eq. (43a) of the paper.
- The relative momentum is defined as \( {\bf q} = {\bf p}_a - {\bf p}_b \) and the average momentum as \( {\bf K} = \frac{1}{2}({\bf p}_a + {\bf p}_b) \), with \( K^0 = \frac{1}{2}(E_a+E_b) \).
- The reduced mass for identical particles is \( \mu = m/2 \) and the total mass is \( M = 2m \).

## Context

This work extends the Koonin-Pratt formula for two-particle correlations with FSI by providing a more rigorous derivation that includes the effect of different emission times. It builds on prior treatments by Pratt (1986, 1990, 1995), Bowler (1988), Baym (1996), and Lednicky, which the authors note differ in their formal results due to different approximations used at intermediate stages. The derived expression can be implemented into classical event generators to calculate FSI-corrected two-particle correlation functions via modified Bose-Einstein weights, enabling more accurate extraction of source space-time structure from experimental data.
