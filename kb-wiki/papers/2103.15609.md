# Nuclear Fermi Momenta of $^{2}$H, $^{27}$Al and $^{56}$Fe from an Analysis of CLAS data

> Nuclear Fermi momentum is a basic property of a nucleus where many nucleons dwell. However, in experiments only the nuclear Fermi momenta of just a few nuclei are measured using quasielastic electron scattering on the nuclear targets so far. Particularly, we still do not know experimentally the Fermi momentum of the lightest nucleon composite -- the deuteron. In this paper, we apply both gaussian distribution and Cauchy distribution to describe the quasielastic peak in the cross section of electron-nucleus scattering. The dip of the cross-section ratio at about $x_{\rm B}=1$ is explained with the nuclear Fermi momentum. By performing the least-square fits to the published CLAS data in the narrow kinematic region of quasielastic scattering, we obtain the nuclear Fermi momenta of $^{2}$H, $^{27}$Al and $^{56}$Fe, which are $116\pm 7$ MeV/c, $232\pm 27$ MeV/c, and $244\pm 28$ MeV/c respectively. The extracted nuclear Fermi momenta are compared to the simple calculations based on Fermi gas model, and the consistencies are found.

# Nuclear Fermi Momenta of $^{2}$H, $^{27}$Al and $^{56}$Fe from an Analysis of CLAS data

## Key claim
The dip in the cross-section ratio of heavy nucleus to deuteron at $x_{\rm B}\sim 1$ can be explained by the different widths of the quasielastic peaks, modeled with a Cauchy distribution. The extracted Fermi momenta are consistent with Fermi gas model calculations for heavy nuclei, but the deuteron Fermi momentum of $116\pm 7$ MeV/c is slightly smaller than the model prediction, suggesting the Fermi gas model may not work well for very light nuclei.

## Method
The authors fit the per-nucleon quasielastic cross-section ratios of $^{12}$C, $^{208}$Pb, $^{27}$Al, and $^{56}$Fe to deuteron, using published CLAS data from Schmookler et al. (2019). Both Gaussian and Cauchy distributions were applied to describe the quasielastic peak shape, with the width of the peak taken as proportional to the nuclear Fermi momentum. The Cauchy-distribution model provided superior fits (reduced chi-squared near 1) and was used to extract the width ratios, which were then converted to Fermi momenta using previously measured values for $^{12}$C and $^{208}$Pb from Moniz (1971).

## Key numbers
- Deuteron ($^{2}$H) Fermi momentum: $116 \pm 7$ MeV/c (first experimental extraction)
- $^{27}$Al Fermi momentum: $232 \pm 27$ MeV/c
- $^{56}$Fe Fermi momentum: $244 \pm 28$ MeV/c
- Width ratio $\Gamma_{\rm D}/\Gamma_{\rm C}$ (Cauchy model): $0.51 \pm 0.03$ from C/D fit
- Width ratio $\Gamma_{\rm D}/\Gamma_{\rm Pb}$ (Cauchy model): $0.46 \pm 0.04$ from Pb/D fit
- Width ratio $\Gamma_{\rm Al}/\Gamma_{\rm D}$ (Cauchy model): $2.0 \pm 0.2$
- Width ratio $\Gamma_{\rm Fe}/\Gamma_{\rm D}$ (Cauchy model): $2.1 \pm 0.2$
- Fermi gas model prediction for $^{2}$H: 140 MeV/c; for $^{27}$Al: 226 MeV/c; for $^{56}$Fe: 231 MeV/c

## Context
This work extends the experimental determination of nuclear Fermi momenta to the deuteron, which had not been measured previously via quasielastic scattering. It uses the CLAS data from Schmookler et al. (2019) and builds on the earlier measurements of Fermi momenta for $^{12}$C ($221\pm 5$ MeV/c) and $^{208}$Pb ($265\pm 5$ MeV/c) by Moniz (1971). The results enable improved modeling of nuclear effects in high-energy scattering experiments, particularly for neutrino-nucleus interactions and deep inelastic scattering on nuclear targets. The extracted Fermi momenta for $^{27}$Al and $^{56}$Fe are compared to Fermi gas model calculations and to a superscaling analysis by Murphy (2019), with the aluminum value found to be smaller than the superscaling estimate, though consistent within uncertainties.
