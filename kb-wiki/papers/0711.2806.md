# Perturbative Treatment of Symmetry Breaking Within Random Matrix Theory

> We discuss the applicability, within the Random Matrix Theory, of perturbative treatment of symmetry breaking to the experimental data on the flip symmetry breaking in quartz crystal. We found that the values of the parameter that measures this breaking are different for the spacing distribution as compared to those for the spectral rigidity. We consider both twofold and threefold symmetries. The latter was found to account better for the spectral rigidity than the former. Both cases, however, underestimate the experimental spectral rigidity at large L. This discrepancy can be resolved if an appropriate number of eigenfrequecies is considered to be missing in the sample. Our findings are relevant to isospin violation study in nuclei.

## Key claim
The perturbative Random Matrix Theory treatment of symmetry breaking, when applied to quartz crystal acoustic eigenfrequency data, yields different values of the mixing parameter Lambda for the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution than for the spectral rigidity; a threefold symmetry (3GOE) description accounts for the spectral rigidity better than a twofold (2GOE) description, but both underestimate the experimental spectral rigidity at large L unless a fraction of missing eigenfrequencies is assumed.

## Method
The authors applied Leitner's perturbative RMT formulas for the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution P(s) and the spectral rigidity Delta_3(L) to experimental acoustic eigenfrequency data from Ellegaard et al. (1996) on a quartz crystal with gradually broken flip symmetry. They fitted the data using both 2GOE (twofold symmetry) and 3GOE (threefold symmetry) models, extracting the symmetry-breaking parameter Lambda from P(s) and from Delta_3(L) separately. To resolve discrepancies at large L, they incorporated a missing-level correction from Bohigas and Pato, introducing a parameter g representing the fraction of missing eigenfrequencies.

## Key numbers
- For the uncut crystal (data set a), the mixing parameter Lambda from fitting P(s) with m=3 was 0.0067, while from fitting Delta_3(L) with m=3 it was 0.0056.
- For data set b (r=0.5 mm, 1414 eigenfrequencies), a missing-level fraction of g=6% (94% of eigenfrequencies accounted for) gave a perfect fit to Delta_3(L) with the 3GOE model.
- For the same data set b, the 2GOE model required g=18% missing levels to approach the data, three times larger than the 3GOE value.
- For the fully broken symmetry case (data set x), the Lambda value from P(s) with m=3 was 0.34, while from Delta_3(L) with m=3 it was 2.4.

## Context
This work extends the perturbative RMT symmetry-breaking treatment of Leitner (1997), who addressed only the spacing distribution, and of Abul-Magd (2004), who extended it to spectral rigidity. The authors show that a 3GOE description, rather than the previously assumed 2GOE, better accounts for the quartz crystal data of Ellegaard et al. (1996), and that missing-level effects can explain the large-L spectral rigidity without invoking pseudointegrable trajectories as proposed by Abul-Magd. The findings are relevant to isospin violation studies in nuclei, where 2GOE descriptions have been used for Al-26 (Mitchell et al.) and 3GOE descriptions may be needed for heavier odd-odd nuclei with T=2 states.
