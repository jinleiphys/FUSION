# Regularization-independent studies of nonperturbative field theory

> We propose a regularization-independent method for studying a renormalizable field theory nonperturbatively through its Dyson-Schwinger equations. Using QED_4 as an example, we show how the coupled equations determining the nonperturbative fermion and photon propagators can be written entirely in terms of renormalized quantities, which renders the equations manifestly finite in a regularization-independent manner. As an illustration of the technique, we apply it to a study of the fermion propagator in quenched QED_4 with the Curtis-Pennington electron-photon vertex. At large momenta the mass function, and hence the anomalous mass dimension gamma_m(alpha), is calculated analytically and we find excellent agreement with previous work. Finally, we show that for the CP vertex the perturbation expansion of gamma_m(alpha) has a finite radius of convergence.

# Regularization-independent studies of nonperturbative field theory

## Key claim

The authors propose that Dyson-Schwinger equations can be rewritten entirely in terms of renormalized quantities, eliminating the regulator analytically before any numerical work, thereby avoiding gauge-covariance violations from momentum-cutoff regulators and the numerical difficulties of handling widely separated momentum scales. The method is illustrated for quenched QED₄ with the Curtis-Pennington vertex, but the authors hedge that the approach cannot study dynamical chiral symmetry breaking by simply setting the bare mass to zero, because the renormalized mass M_μ does not distinguish between explicit and dynamical symmetry breaking.

## Method

The Dyson-Schwinger equations for the fermion and photon propagators in QED₄ are re-expressed by using the momentum-independence of the renormalization constants Z₁, Z₂, Z₃ and the bare mass m₀ to eliminate these constants through subtraction at the renormalization point μ. This yields finite integral equations for the renormalized wavefunction renormalization Z(μ²;p²), mass function M(p²), and photon renormalization G(μ²;q²) that contain no regulator. For quenched QED₄ with the Curtis-Pennington vertex, the resulting one-dimensional integral equations are solved analytically at large momenta by a power-law ansatz, and the anomalous mass dimension γ_m(α) is derived from the asymptotic exponent s.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling for the CP vertex in Landau gauge is α = 0.933667; in Feynman gauge it is α = 0.890712.
- The anomalous mass dimension expansion coefficients: γ_m(α) = (3/2)(α/π) + (9/8)(α/π)² + (9/64)(9+π²)(α/π)³ + (81/256)(6+π²)(α/π)⁴ + O(α⁶).
- The large-order behavior of the expansion coefficients follows c_n = (α_cr/π)^{-n} e^{-β₀} / n^{3/2} with β₀ ≈ 0.52, indicating a finite radius of convergence.
- The exponent ν for the wavefunction renormalization is ν = αξ/(4π).
- The logarithmic slope s of the mass function at large momenta agrees with numerical results from Hawes, Williams and Roberts (1996) to better than 1 part in 10⁵ for a variety of gauge parameters and couplings.

## Context

This work extends the subtractively renormalized quenched QED studies of Hawes, Williams and Roberts (1995-1997) and addresses the gauge-covariance violations identified by Dong, Munczek and Roberts (1994) that arise from using a hard momentum cutoff. It also contrasts with the dimensional regularization approach of Schreiber, Sizer and Williams (1998), which introduced a spurious fermion mass in D ≠ 4 dimensions. The method enables analytical calculation of the asymptotic mass function and anomalous mass dimension, and the authors anticipate it will make feasible the study of full (unquenched) QED₄ and extensions beyond two-point functions.
