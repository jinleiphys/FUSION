# What Thermodynamics tells about QCD Plasma near Phase Transition

> Due to a rapid change of the entropy density $s(T)$ across the critical temperature $T_c$ of the QCD phase transition, the pressure $P(T)$ and the energy density $e(T)$ above $T_c$ generally deviate from their Stefan-Boltzmann values. We shall demonstrate this both analytically and numerically for a general class of $s(T)$ consistent with thermodynamical constraints and make a qualitative comparison of the result with the lattice QCD data. Quantities related to $ds(T)/dT$ such as the specific heat and sound velocity are also discussed.

# What Thermodynamics tells about QCD Plasma near Phase Transition

## Key claim
Most of the "unexpected" behaviors of bulk thermodynamic quantities observed in lattice QCD simulations near the critical temperature, such as the slow approach of pressure to the Stefan-Boltzmann limit, the peak in energy density just above T_c, and the nonzero interaction measure, can be explained at least qualitatively as simple consequences of the rapid increase of entropy density s(T) across T_c, without invoking non-perturbative interactions of quarks and gluons above T_c.

## Method
The authors parametrize the entropy density s(T) as a smooth interpolation between a hadronic gas (pion gas) at low T and a quark-gluon plasma at high T, using a tanh-based weighting function with width parameter Γ. From this s(T), they compute pressure P(T), energy density e(T), specific heat C_V, and sound velocity c_s^2 via standard thermodynamic relations at zero chemical potential, and compare the resulting curves qualitatively with lattice QCD data.

## Key numbers
- The entropy density of the hadronic phase is taken as s_h(T) = 12(π²/90)T³ and of the quark-gluon phase as s_q(T) = 148(π²/90)T³, corresponding to massless free gas with two flavors.
- For Γ = 0 (sharp transition), P(T)/P_SB(T) = 50% at T/T_c = 1.2 and 90% at T/T_c = 1.8, independent of dynamics.
- The transition width Γ/T_c = 0.05 is noted as similar to lattice measurements showing a rapid variation of s(T) in a narrow region of T with width ~10 MeV.
- The sound velocity c_s^2 shows a sudden drop in the narrow region T_c − Γ < T < T_c + Γ.

## Context
This work extends the bag model approach of Satz (1990s) by providing a thermodynamically consistent parametrization that does not require a bag constant and can treat both first-order transitions and crossovers. The results are compared qualitatively with lattice QCD data from Blum (1990s) and Lae (1990s). The paper enables a cleaner separation of genuine non-perturbative effects from trivial thermodynamic consequences of a rapid entropy change, and suggests that identifying true non-perturbative effects from lattice data is difficult because the basic curves already resemble the data.
