# Re-analysis of the $D^* Dπ$ coupling in the light-cone QCD sum rules

> The recent measurement from the CLEO experiment presents the $DD^*π$ coupling, $17.9\pm 0.3 \pm 1.9$. This value is much larger than any of QCD sum rule predictions available in literature. We report that, with a relevant treatment of the continuum subtraction as well as with the asymptotic form of the twist-2 pion wave function, the light-cone QCD sum rule can provide the coupling comparable to the experimental value. The stability of the resulting sum rule becomes much better with these corrections.

# Re-analysis of the $D^* D\pi$ coupling in the light-cone QCD sum rules

## Key claim
With a corrected treatment of the continuum subtraction (removing a mathematically spurious term) and using the asymptotic form of the twist-2 pion wave function, the light-cone QCD sum rule can yield a $D^*D\pi$ coupling comparable to the experimental value of $17.9 \pm 0.3 \pm 1.9$, whereas previous sum rule predictions gave values around 12 or lower.

## Method
The author re-examines the light-cone QCD sum rule (LCQSR) for the $D^*D\pi$ coupling, focusing on the continuum subtraction procedure. The leading-order OPE integral $\int_0^1 du / [m_c^2 - u p_1^2 - (1-u) p_2^2]$ is analyzed via a double dispersion relation; the author argues that the standard continuum subtraction factor $M^2 e^{-S_0/M^2}$ arises from a mathematically spurious term in the integration-by-parts decomposition and should be dropped. The sum rule is then recalculated using the asymptotic twist-2 pion wave function with $\varphi_\pi(0.5)=1.5$ and without the spurious continuum contribution.

## Key numbers
- CLEO experimental value: $g_{D^*D\pi} = 17.9 \pm 0.3 \pm 1.9$
- Previous LCQSR result (Belyaev et al., 1995): $g_{D^*D\pi} = 12.5$, obtained from $f_D f_{D^*} g_{D^*D\pi} = 0.51$ GeV$^2$ using $f_D = 170$ MeV and $f_{D^*} = 240$ MeV
- Using asymptotic twist-2 wave function alone increases the coupling by only 9%
- With the continuum subtraction removed, the minimum of the sum rule gives $f_D f_{D^*} g_{D^*D\pi} = 0.66$ GeV$^2$, yielding $g_{D^*D\pi} = 16.2$
- Including uncertainties in decay constants ($f_D = 170 \pm 10$ MeV, $f_{D^*} = 240 \pm 20$ MeV) gives the range $14.1 \le g_{D^*D\pi} \le 18.75$

## Context
This work addresses the longstanding discrepancy between QCD sum rule predictions for the $D^*D\pi$ coupling (which gave values $\le 12$) and the CLEO measurement of $17.9 \pm 0.3 \pm 1.9$ (Anastassov et al., 2001). Previous LCQSR calculations by Belyaev et al. (1995) and Colangelo et al. (1998) used $\varphi_\pi(0.5) \sim 1.2$ and the standard continuum subtraction, yielding couplings around 12. The author's correction brings the LCQSR result into agreement with quark model predictions (Becirevic et al., 1999) and lattice calculations (Abada et al., 2002), and enables a consistent theoretical description of the $D^*D\pi$ vertex within QCD sum rules.
