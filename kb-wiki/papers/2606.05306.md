# Gauge field flow for chiral gauge theories on a slab

> The proposal to formulate chiral gauge theories using domain wall fermions on $2n+1$ dimensional Euclidean lattice with a slab geometry involves $2n$ dimensional dynamical gauge fields residing on one of the domain walls. The gauge fields are extended into the extra dimension using gradient flow decoupling the mirror fermions on the anti-wall. We implement this construction on the lattice for $n=1$ in the presence of $2n$ dimensional background gauge fields. We also formulate and implement an additional gauge field flow proposal, where the gauge fields satisfy $2n+1$ dimensional equation of motion away from the domain wall, known as the EOM (equation of motion) flow. In both cases, we couple the gauge fields to fermions and demonstrate how current conservation and anomaly inflow work on the lattice.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that both gradient flow and equation-of-motion (EOM) flow can be implemented on the lattice to decouple mirror fermions in the slab (Grabowska-Kaplan) proposal for chiral gauge theories, and that these constructions realize current conservation and anomaly inflow, but the results depend on the specific gauge field configuration and the finite width of the boundary fermion modes.

## Method

The authors construct a 2+1 dimensional Euclidean lattice with slab geometry (periodic in the extra dimension s) and couple four species of massive Dirac fermions (charges 3, 4, 5, 0) to U(1) gauge fields via a Wilson fermion action with a mass defect that changes sign at s=0 and s=L_s/2. They implement three gauge field prescriptions in the extra dimension: s-independent gauge fields (conventional domain wall), gradient flow following ∂_s Ā_μ = (ξ ε(s)/|Λ|) ∂_λ F̄_λμ, and EOM flow following ∂_s² Ā_μ + ∂_ν(∂_ν Ā_μ − ∂_μ Ā_ν)=0, all with Ā_s=0. For each case, they compute the 2+1 dimensional fermion current densities and the 1+1 dimensional chiral currents on the wall and anti-wall, using a specific background gauge field configuration A_x = (L_t/2π) E_0 cos(2π t/L_t), A_t=0.

## Key numbers

* Lattice size L_t = L_x = 16, L_s = 26, with Wilson parameter w = 0.566, μ_0 = 0.972, and m_0 = 1.132.
* Background electric field amplitude E_0 = 0.001 (in lattice units).
* Gradient flow parameter ξ = 3 used for the gauge action density distribution plots.
* The two lowest eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian (with gauge fields off) are ±0.06, with wavefunctions spanning approximately 7 lattice sites in the s direction around each defect.
* Anomaly ratio R_Anomaly ≈ 1.045 for Q=3,4 and ≈ 1.029 for Q=5 (deviations from the continuum value of 1 due to finite lattice spacing and finite width effects).

## Context

This work extends the continuum proposals of Grabowska and Kaplan (2015) for gradient flow in the slab geometry, and of Kaplan (2023, 2024) for EOM flow in the disk geometry, by providing explicit lattice implementations for n=1 (target 1+1 dimensional chiral gauge theory). It builds on the lattice demonstration of anomaly inflow for conventional domain wall fermions by Jansen (1992) and shows how the same physics works with flowed gauge fields. The results enable future non-perturbative lattice simulations of chiral gauge theories such as the 3-4-5-0 model, and the companion paper (PaperB) addresses the disk proposal.
