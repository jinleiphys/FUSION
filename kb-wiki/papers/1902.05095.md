# Equation of state at finite densities for QCD matter in nuclear collisions

> We construct the QCD equation of state at finite chemical potentials including net baryon, electric charge, and strangeness, based on the conserved charge susceptibilities determined from lattice QCD simulations and the equation of state of the hadron resonance gas model. For the application to relativistic heavy ion collisions we consider the situation of strangeness neutrality and matter with a fixed electric charge-to-baryon ratio, resembling that of heavy nuclei. The importance of finite electric charge and strangeness chemical potentials for particle production in heavy ion collisions is demonstrated using hydrodynamic simulations.

# Equation of state at finite densities for QCD matter in nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The authors construct a hybrid QCD equation of state (NEOS) with multiple conserved charges (baryon, electric charge, and strangeness) that is applicable to relativistic heavy ion collisions, but the construction relies on a Taylor expansion method that is only valid when the fugacity $\mu_{B,Q,S}/T$ is sufficiently small, and the results are limited to the range where $\mu_B/T$ is small enough for lattice QCD to be reliable.

## Method

The equation of state is constructed by connecting a lattice QCD equation of state (using Taylor expansion up to fourth-order and selected sixth-order susceptibilities) at high temperatures with a hadron resonance gas model at low temperatures, using a hyperbolic tangent switching function centered at a baryon-chemical-potential-dependent connecting temperature. The authors impose conditions relevant to heavy ion collisions: strangeness neutrality ($n_S = 0$) and a fixed electric charge-to-baryon ratio ($n_Q = 0.4 n_B$), and solve for the corresponding chemical potentials $\mu_S$ and $\mu_Q$ as functions of temperature and $\mu_B$. The resulting equation of state is then used in 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic simulations with a hadronic afterburner to compute particle yields and ratios.

## Key numbers

- The connecting temperature is $T_c(\mu_B) = 0.16\ \mathrm{GeV} - 0.4\times(0.139\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1} \mu_B^2 + 0.053\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-3} \mu_B^4)$, with a connecting width $\Delta T_c = 0.1 T_c(0)$.
- Constant entropy density over net baryon density lines $s/n_B = 420, 144, 51, 30$ correspond to Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200, 62.4, 19.6, 14.5$ AGeV, respectively.
- The squared sound velocity reaches 94.8% of the Stefan-Boltzmann limit ($c_s^2 = 1/3$) at $T=0.6$ GeV and 97.2% at $T=0.8$ GeV for $s/n_B = 420$.
- The hydrodynamic simulations use a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of $\eta/s = 0.08$ and a switching energy density $e_{\rm sw} = 0.26\ \mathrm{GeV/fm}^3$.
- The typical $Z/A$ ratio for heavy nuclei such as Au is 0.401 and for Pb is 0.394.

## Context

This work extends previous constructions of finite-density QCD equations of state (such as those by Nonaka (2004), Bluhm (2004, 2007), Steinheimer (2010), Huovinen (2011), Hempel (2013), Albright (2014, 2015), Rougemont (2017), Critelli (2017), Vovchenko (2017, 2018), Parotto (2018), Fu (2018), Motornenko (2018), and Plumberg (2018)) by incorporating multiple conserved charges simultaneously. The authors build on lattice QCD results from Borsanyi (2011, 2018), Bellwied (2015), Bazavov (2012, 2014, 2017), and Ding (2015). The work enables more realistic hydrodynamic simulations of the Beam Energy Scan programs at RHIC and SPS, and future experiments at FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC, by providing an equation of state that properly accounts for strangeness neutrality and the fixed charge-to-baryon ratio of heavy nuclei.
