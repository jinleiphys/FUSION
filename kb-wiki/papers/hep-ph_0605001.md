# The Zero-Bin and Mode Factorization in Quantum Field Theory

> We study a Lagrangian formalism that avoids double counting in effective field theories where distinct fields are used to describe different infrared momentum regions for the same particle. The formalism leads to extra subtractions in certain diagrams and to a new way of thinking about factorization of modes in quantum field theory. In non-relativistic field theories, the subtractions remove unphysical pinch singularities in box type diagrams, and give a derivation of the known pull-up mechanism between soft and ultrasoft fields which is required by the renormalization group evolution. In a field theory for energetic particles, the soft-collinear effective theory (SCET), the subtractions allow the theory to be defined with different infrared and ultraviolet regulators, remove double counting between soft, ultrasoft, and collinear modes, and give results which reproduce the infrared divergences of the full theory. Our analysis shows that convolution divergences in factorization formulæoccur due to an overlap of momentum regions. We propose a method that avoids this double counting, which helps to resolve a long standing puzzle with singularities in collinear factorization in QCD. The analysis gives evidence for a factorization in rapidity space in exclusive decays.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that double counting between distinct infrared momentum regions for the same particle in effective field theories (such as NRQCD and SCET) can be systematically removed by implementing "zero-bin subtractions," which resolve unphysical pinch singularities and endpoint singularities that arise from overlapping momentum regions, independent of the choice of UV and IR regulators.

## Method

The authors formulate a Lagrangian-based zero-bin subtraction procedure that removes contributions from overlapping momentum regions when multiple fields describe the same particle. In NRQCD, this modifies soft box graphs by excluding the zero-momentum bin (p=0) from sums over soft intermediate states, which eliminates unphysical pinch singularities. In SCET, the procedure removes double counting between soft, ultrasoft, and collinear modes by subtracting contributions where collinear momenta become soft, and similarly converts infrared divergences in collinear graphs into ultraviolet divergences that contribute to anomalous dimensions.

## Key numbers

- In NRQCD, soft photons have energies of order mv and ultrasoft photons have energies of order mv^2, where v << 1 is the typical fermion velocity in the non-relativistic bound state
- For SCET, energetic particles near the n direction have momenta with p^- ~ Q, p^+ ~ Q λ^2, and p_perp ~ Q λ, where λ << 1 and Q >> Λ_QCD
- For exclusive processes in SCETb, the expansion parameter η ~ Λ_QCD/Q
- The zero-bin subtraction converts convolution integrals like ∫₀¹ dx φ_π(x)/x² (divergent when φ_π(x) vanishes linearly as x→0) into finite expressions of the form ∫₀¹ dx [φ_π(x) - φ_π(0) - x φ_π'(0)]/x² + φ̃_π

## Context

This work extends the method of regions approach of Beneke (1997) and the pullup mechanism previously studied by Manohar (2000) and Hoang (2001) in NRQCD, providing a Lagrangian formalism that automatically implements these subtractions. The zero-bin procedure resolves a long-standing puzzle with divergent convolutions in QCD factorization formulas, including endpoint singularities encountered in the pion form factor at large Q² (Geshkenbein, 1982), B→πℓν̄ form factors (Akhoury, 1993), and other exclusive processes. The analysis provides evidence for factorization in rapidity space in exclusive decays, connecting to the rapidity-dependent factorization formulas of Collins and Soper (1981) for fragmentation processes.
