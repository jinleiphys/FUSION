# A comparative analysis of in-medium spectral functions for $N(940)$ and $N^*(1535)$ in real-time thermal field theory

> In the real-time thermal field theory, the nucleon self-energy at finite temperature and density is evaluated where an extensive set of pion-baryon ($πB$) loops are considered. On the other side the in-medium self-energy of $N^*(1535)$ for $πN$ and $ηN$ loops is also determined in the same framework. The detail branch cut structures for these different $πB$ loops for nucleon $N(940)$ and $πN$, $ηN$ loops for $N^*(1535)$ are addressed. Using the total self-energy of $N(940)$ and $N^*(1535)$, which contain the contributions of their corresponding loop diagrams, the complete structures of their in-medium spectral functions have been obtained. The Landau and unitary cut contributions provide two separate peak structures in the nucleon spectral function while $N^*(1535)$ has single peak structure in its unitary cuts. At high temperature, the peak structures of both at their individual poles are attenuated while at high density Landau peak structure of nucleon is completely suppressed and its unitary peak structure is tending to be shifted towards the melted peak of $N^*(1535)$. The non-trivial modifications of these chiral partners may indicate some association of chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key claim
The in-medium spectral functions of the nucleon N(940) and its chiral partner N*(1535) exhibit non-trivial modifications at finite temperature and density that may indicate some association with chiral symmetry restoration, though the effective hadronic model used does not directly incorporate temperature-dependent quark condensate as in chiral models like NJL or LSM.

## Method
The nucleon self-energy at finite temperature and density was evaluated in real-time thermal field theory (RTF) using an extensive set of pion-baryon (πB) loops for 12 baryon resonances (including N(940), Δ(1232), N*(1440), N*(1520), N*(1535), Δ*(1600), Δ*(1620), N*(1650), Δ*(1700), N*(1700), N*(1710), N*(1720)). The N*(1535) self-energy was calculated separately for πN and ηN loops. Coupling constants for the effective BNπ interaction Lagrangians were fixed from experimental vacuum decay widths of the corresponding B → Nπ channels. The imaginary and real parts of the diagonalized self-energy were computed, and the spectral functions were obtained from the Dyson equation. For N*(1535), an additional folding procedure was applied using the in-medium nucleon spectral function.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of in-medium baryon spectral functions using effective hadronic models, building on approaches by Lee, DeTar (1989), Gottlieb, Jido (2001), Shuryak, and Gallas. It contrasts with chiral model approaches (NJL, linear sigma model) by fixing interaction strengths from experimental decay widths rather than from chiral symmetry considerations. The results enable comparison with predictions from lattice QCD (DeTar, Gottlieb) and QCD sum-rule approaches (Jido) regarding chiral symmetry restoration in hot and dense nuclear matter, and may inform calculations of transport coefficients such as shear viscosity (Ghosh, 2014).
