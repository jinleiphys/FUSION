# A Comparative Study of Mass Extraction Schemes and $π^\pm-ρ^\pm$ Mixing

> We study the origin of the non-monotonic magnetic-field dependence of the lowest charged pion excitation observed in lattice QCD. In a magnetic field, the charged pion mixes with the longitudinally polarized charged rho meson, which shares the same quantum numbers. Within the SU(2)$_f$ Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model supplemented by a gauge invariant tree-level $π-ρ$ mixing operator constrained by the experimental $ρ^\pm\rightarrowπ^\pmγ$ decay width, we compare four mass-extraction schemes: rest-mass reconstruction, local bosonization, direct determinant solving with Landau projection, and near-pole expansion. The rest-mass scheme cannot reproduce the lattice-type turnover, while in the local derivative-expansion scheme the turnover presence but is weak which occurs at large magnetic field. By contrast, the direct determinant and near-pole schemes both retain a robust non-monotonic lowest mode. The former is most faithful to the Landau-level kinematics of the charged excitation, while the latter most clearly shows that residue suppression enhances the effective mixing after canonical normalization. Our results indicate that the lattice behavior is a genuine quasiparticle mixing effect, but one whose robustness depends crucially on how the charged-meson pole structure is extracted in a magnetic field.

## Key claim

The non-monotonic magnetic-field dependence of the lowest charged pion excitation observed in lattice QCD is a genuine quasiparticle mixing effect from π±–ρ± mixing, but its robustness depends crucially on how the charged-meson pole structure is extracted in a magnetic field; the turnover appears robustly only when the charged meson is treated as a Landau-level quasiparticle with consistent pole normalization.

## Method

The authors work within an SU(2)f Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a gauge-invariant tree-level π–ρ mixing operator constrained by the experimental ρ±→π±γ decay width (κexp ≃ 0.222 GeV−1). They compare four mass-extraction schemes applied to the same microscopic quadratic kernel in the (π±, ρ±s_z=0) sector: rest-mass reconstruction from the RPA pole at zero spatial momentum, local bosonized derivative expansion, direct determinant solving with Landau projection onto the lowest Landau level, and near-pole quasiparticle expansion with canonical normalization. The quark propagator is taken in Landau-level representation, the gap equation is solved at T = 0.01 GeV, and Pauli-Villars regularization is used with Λ = 1.25 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Experimental ρ±→π±γ partial width: Γexp ≃ 0.068 MeV, yielding κexp ≃ 0.222 GeV−1
- Vacuum loop-induced mixing coefficient from weak-field expansion: gρπloop,vac = 0.064556 (evaluated at M = 0.4 GeV, q0 = mπ = 0.133 GeV)
- Tree-level remainder fixed by matching: gρπtree = 0.09104 GeV−1
- Vacuum wave-function normalization factors: Zπvac = 0.109, Zρvac = 0.413
- NJL model parameters: Λ = 1.25 GeV, GS = 3.1 GeV−2, GV = 5.019 GeV−2, yielding mπ = 0.133 GeV, mρ = 0.77 GeV
- Landau level summation performed to over 200 levels

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Wang 2026) on π–ρ mixing as a mechanism for the non-monotonic charged meson energy observed in lattice QCD (Bali 2012, Ding 2020, Ding 2026). It systematically compares four mass-extraction schemes that have been used in effective approaches including NJL-type models (Coppola 2018, Coppola 2019, Carlomagno 2022), functional methods, and holographic models (Mamo 2015). The results enable a refined interpretation of lattice observables: the turnover probes not only mass shifts but also the magnetic-field dependence of charged pole structure and quasiparticle residues, and the comparison clarifies why some earlier effective-model studies (Avancini 2021, Wen 2023) found it difficult to reproduce the lattice trend robustly.
