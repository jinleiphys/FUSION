# Effects of Finite Temperature and Pairing Correlations in Multi-$Λ$ Hypernuclei

> The influence of finite temperatures and pairing correlations on the ground state properties of multi $Λ$- Ca, Sn and Pb hypernuclei is explored using finite temperature Hartree Fock Bogoliubov approach and contact pairing interaction. A critical temperature is predicted and is in agreement with the Bardeen Cooper Schrieffer relationship $k_B T_C^Λ\approx 0.5 Δ^{T=0}_Λ$, beyond which pairing correlations drop to zero. Particle densities, $Λ$ single particle energies, and nuclear radii are weakly impacted by pairing as well as by finite temperatures. However, other nuclear properties which are more sensitive to pairing correlations, such as $Λ$ pairing gaps, condensation energies, and abnormal densities are also more impacted by finite temperature, especially around the critical temperature. Furthermore, calculations show the occurrence of the pairing re-entrance effect in the $^{280}_{70Λ}$Pb hyperon drip line hypernucleus. Our study provides insight into the thermal evolution of $Λ$ pairing, i.e. the emergence and vanishing of pairing correlations in multi $Λ$ hypernuclei as a function of temperature.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) relation for uniform matter, \(k_B T_C^\Lambda \approx 0.5 \Delta^{T=0}_\Lambda\), is well reproduced in finite multi-\(\Lambda\) hypernuclei, but this agreement holds only for hypernuclei with closed proton and neutron shells; for hypernuclei near the hyperon drip line with open neutron shells, the pairing re-entrance effect can occur, where pairing correlations vanish at low temperature and reappear at higher temperature.

## Method

The authors employed the finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (FT-HFB) approach in spherical symmetry, using the SLy5 Skyrme interaction for the nucleon sector and density functionals DF-NSC89, DF-NSC97a, and DF-NSC97f (adjusted to Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results) for the \(\Lambda\)N interaction, combined with the EmpC prescription for the \(\Lambda\Lambda\) interaction. The \(\Lambda\Lambda\) pairing interaction was modeled as a volume-type contact interaction with strength \(V_{\Lambda_0}\) adjusted to reproduce the maximum theoretical BCS pairing gap in uniform matter. The FT-HFB equations were solved numerically in a spherical box of radius 30 fm with 0.1 fm resolution, iterating until total energy converged to about \(10^{-8}\) MeV.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{46}_{6\Lambda}\)Ca using DF-NSC97a+EmpC: \(\Delta_\Lambda^{T=0} = 1.04\) MeV and \(k_B T_C^\Lambda = 0.54\) MeV.
* For \(^{160}_{28\Lambda}\)Sn using DF-NSC97a+EmpC: \(\Delta_\Lambda^{T=0} = 0.82\) MeV and \(k_B T_C^\Lambda = 0.44\) MeV.
* For \(^{272}_{64\Lambda}\)Pb using DF-NSC97a+EmpC: \(\Delta_\Lambda^{T=0} = 0.76\) MeV and \(k_B T_C^\Lambda = 0.44\) MeV.
* The condensation energy peak reaches nearly 3 MeV at \(T = 0\) for \(^{132-S}_{-S\Lambda}\)Sn hypernuclei, decreasing to approximately 2 MeV at \(k_B T = 0.3\) MeV.
* The \(\Lambda\) hyperon radius changes by about 0.3% as temperature increases from 0 to \(T_C^\Lambda\) for \(^{46}_{6\Lambda}\)Ca, \(^{160}_{28\Lambda}\)Sn, and \(^{272}_{64\Lambda}\)Pb; for \(^{208-S}_{-S\Lambda}\)Pb hyper-isotopes, the increase reaches approximately 4%.
* Pairing re-entrance in \(^{280}_{70\Lambda}\)Pb using DF-NSC97f+EmpC occurs with two critical temperatures: \(k_B T_{c1} = 0.19\) MeV and \(k_B T_{c2} = 0.41\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous zero-temperature studies of \(\Lambda\) pairing in multi-strange hypernuclei by the same group (PhysRevC.98.014318) by incorporating finite temperature effects for the first time. It also builds on earlier finite-temperature studies of hypernuclei using the relativistic Thomas-Fermi approach (PhysRevC.94.054325), which showed that \(\Lambda\) radii increase faster with temperature than nucleon radii. The results are relevant for understanding the thermal evolution of hyperon pairing in proto-neutron stars, where the presence of \(\Lambda\) superfluidity can suppress neutrino emission processes such as the direct URCA cycle involving \(\Sigma^{-} \rightarrow \Lambda+e^{-}+\bar{\nu}\) and \(\Lambda \rightarrow p+e^{-}+\bar{\nu}\) decays.
