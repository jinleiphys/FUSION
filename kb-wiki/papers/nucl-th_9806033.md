# Overbarrier Resonances as Solutions of Set Inhomogeneous Schrödinger Equations

> In the paper the Schrödinger equation for quasibound resonance state with complex energy is considered. The system of inhomogeneous differential equations is obtained for the real and imaginary parts of wave function. On the base of known solution of corresponding homogeneous equation, the inhomogeneus system is solved with help of iteration procedure. The single-particle neutron $2p$-state in the Woods - Saxon potential is analyzed for $^{13}C$ nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to solve for overbarrier resonance states (which do not exist in the complete theory as discrete states) by converting the complex-energy Schrodinger equation into a system of inhomogeneous differential equations for the real and imaginary parts of the wave function, solved iteratively using the solution of the corresponding homogeneous equation.

## Method

The authors separate the complex-energy Schrodinger equation for a quasibound resonance state into real and imaginary parts, yielding a set of two coupled inhomogeneous differential equations for the radial wave function components u(r) and v(r). They solve these equations through an iteration procedure that uses the solution of the homogeneous equation (obtained via expansion in Sturm-Liouville functions) as the starting point, with successive iterations incorporating the coupling terms proportional to the resonance width. The method is applied to the neutron 2p_{1/2} state in a Woods-Saxon potential for the 13C nucleus, with the potential depth adjusted to reproduce the experimental 1p_{1/2} binding energy.

## Key numbers

- Neutron binding energy in 1p_{1/2} shell of 13C: E_bind(1p) = -4.947 MeV (used to determine Woods-Saxon potential depth V_0 = 32.3 MeV)
- Woods-Saxon parameters: r_0 = 1.46 fm, a = 0.67 fm
- Resonance energy for 2p_{1/2} state: E_{2p_{1/2}} = 8.1 MeV (obtained with M=30 expansion terms, convergence criterion |E^{(M)} - E^{(M-1)}| <= 0.1 MeV)
- Resonance width for 2p_{1/2} state: Gamma_{2p_{1/2}} = 22.244 MeV
- Root-mean-square radius for neutron in 2p_{1/2} state: <r^2_{2p_{1/2}}>^{1/2} = 11.92 fm
- Root-mean-square radius for neutron in 1p_{1/2} state: <r^2_{1p_{1/2}}>^{1/2} = 3.40 fm

## Context

This work addresses the problem of overbarrier resonances, which are not accessible through standard R-matrix theory (Lane and Thomas, 1958) or potential models with continuum coupling (Garcia-Calderon, 1976). Previous approaches for such resonances include the quasiclassical method (Mur and Popov, 1993) and the complex scaling method (Gyarmati and Vertse, 1971; Homma, Myo and Kato, 1997). The authors note that the complex scaling method yields complex root-mean-square radii (e.g., <r^2_{3S1/2}> = 9.41 + i4.52 fm for a 3S_{1/2} overbarrier resonance), whereas their time-dependent treatment produces a real r.m.s. radius. The method enables calculation of wave functions, energies, and widths for overbarrier resonances in realistic nuclear potentials, with planned applications to 2p states in 13C and 13N for analyzing electron scattering and beta transitions.
