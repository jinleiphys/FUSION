# Cumulants and Correlation Functions vs the QCD phase diagram

> In this paper we discuss the relation of particle number cumulants and correlation functions. It is argued that measuring couplings of the genuine multi-particle correlation functions could provide cleaner information on possible non-trivial dynamics in heavy-ion collisions. We extract integrated multi-proton correlation functions from the presently available experimental data on proton cumulants. We find that the STAR data contain significant four-proton correlations, at least at the lower energies, with indication of changing dynamics in central collisions. We also find that these correlations are rather long-ranged in rapidity. Finally, using the Ising model, we demonstrate how the signs of the multi-proton correlation functions may be used to exclude certain regions of the phase diagram close to the critical point.

## Key claim

The paper argues that measuring genuine multi-particle correlation functions (rather than cumulants) provides cleaner information about possible non-trivial dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, because cumulants mix correlations of different orders and a large fourth-order cumulant does not necessarily imply strong four-particle correlations.

## Method

The authors derive relations between particle number cumulants and integrated genuine multi-particle correlation functions for a single particle species (protons). They apply these relations to extract two-, three-, and four-proton integrated correlation functions and reduced correlation functions (couplings) from preliminary STAR data on proton cumulants at collision energies of 7.7, 11.5, and 19.6 GeV. They analyze the centrality, rapidity, and energy dependence of these correlation functions and compare the scaling behavior to expectations from independent source models and long-range correlation scenarios.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s) = 7.7 GeV, the cumulant ratio for protons is K4/K2 ~ 3.5, which the analysis shows is dominated by genuine four-proton correlations (C4 > 0) rather than two- or three-proton correlations.
- For the most central collisions at 7.7 GeV, the average number of measured antiprotons in |y| < 0.5 is approximately 0.3 compared to roughly 40 protons.
- For central collisions at 7.7 GeV, the average number of protons in |y| = 1 is <N_{Delta y=1}> = 39.3; at 19.6 GeV it is 24.9.
- The reduced two-proton correlation c2 scales as 1/<N>^0.85 with the number of protons, close to but deviating from the independent source expectation of 1/<N>.
- For N_part < 200, three- and four-proton couplings are consistent with 1/<N>^2 and 1/<N>^3 scaling respectively, but flatten out for N_part > 200 at the lowest energies.
- At 19.6 GeV, the cumulant ratio K4/K2 drops below the Poisson baseline (K4/K2 < 1), which the analysis attributes to negative two-proton correlations rather than negative four-proton correlations.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework connecting cumulants to correlation functions previously discussed by Ling and Stephanov (2015) and Bzdak (2012). It directly addresses the interpretation of STAR collaboration data on proton cumulants (Adamczyk 2014, Luo 2015) by extracting genuine multi-particle correlations. The results enable future experimental searches for the QCD critical point by showing that the signs of multi-proton correlation functions can be used to exclude certain regions of the phase diagram near the critical point, as demonstrated using the Ising model. The analysis also motivates measurements at even lower beam energies to further investigate the strong energy dependence observed in three- and four-proton correlations.
