# First-order CP phase transition in two-flavor QCD at $θ= π$ under electromagnetic scale anomaly via a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio description

> We discuss the thermal CP phase transition in QCD at $θ=π$ under a weak magnetic field background, where the electromagnetic scale anomaly gets significant. To explicitize, we work on a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at $θ=π$ in the mean field approximation, including the electromagnetic-scale anomaly term. We find that the thermal CP phase transition becomes first order and the strength of the first order gets more prominent as the magnetic field increases. The associated potential barrier is thermally created by the electromagnetic scale anomaly and gives rise to criticality due to the induced potential of a non-perturbative form $\sim \frac{|eB|^3}{f_π} \frac{|P|}{P^2 + m_0^2}$, where $eB$ denotes the magnetic field strength; $P$ the CP order parameter, and $m_0$ the isospin-symmetric current-quark mass.

## Key claim

The thermal CP phase transition in two-flavor QCD at θ = π under a weak magnetic field becomes first order due to the electromagnetic scale anomaly, with the first-order strength increasing as the magnetic field increases; this result depends on the inclusion of the electromagnetic-scale anomaly term in a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at mean field approximation, and the associated potential barrier is thermally created by the anomaly.

## Method

The authors employed a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at θ = π in the mean field approximation, incorporating the electromagnetic-scale anomaly term via a tadpole potential. They used the imaginary time formalism and Landau level decomposition to evaluate thermomagnetic contributions to the quark condensate, with a smooth cutoff regulator function, and determined the order parameters for chiral and CP phase transitions by solving stationary conditions of the total thermomagnetic potential.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass m_π = 0.1402 GeV, pion decay constant f_π = 0.0926 GeV, and quark condensate ⟨ūu⟩ = ⟨dd⟩ = (−0.2415 GeV)^3 used as empirical inputs to fix model parameters at eB = T = 0
- Model parameters: current-quark mass m_0 = 0.006 GeV, cutoff Λ = 0.59 GeV, coupling G_0Λ^2 = 2.435 with c = 0.2
- The CP phase transition criticality occurs around T ∼ 300 MeV for the case without the tadpole contribution
- For the case with the tadpole term, the first-order criticality shifts to higher temperatures as eB increases, with eB = 0.02 GeV^2 shown as an example
- The induced potential barrier scales as ∼ |eB|^3/f_π · |P|/(P^2 + m_0^2), where eB is the magnetic field strength, P the CP order parameter, and m_0 the current-quark mass

## Context

This work extends previous studies of CP symmetry at θ = π in QCD, which have been explored using chiral effective models (Dashen 1970, Witten 1980, Pisarski 1996, Creutz 2003, Sakai 2011) and more recently via higher-form symmetry and 't Hooft anomaly matching (Gaiotto 2017, Cordova 2019). The electromagnetic scale anomaly effect, previously shown to be significant for chiral phase transitions at θ = 0 in weak magnetic fields (Kawaguchi 2021, Yuanyuan Wang 2022, 2024), is here applied to the θ = π case for the first time. The results enable further investigation of first-order CP phase transitions in the early universe, potentially relevant for gravitational wave production and domain wall collapse scenarios.
