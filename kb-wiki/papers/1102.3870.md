# The Inglis-Belyaev formula and the hypothesis of the two-quasiparticle excitations (version 2012)

> The goal of the present work is to revisit the cranking formula of the vibrational parameters, especially its well known drawbacks. The latter can be summarized as spurious resonances or singularities in the behavior of the mass parameters in the limit of unpaired systems. It is found that these problems are simply induced by the presence of two derivatives in the formula. In effect, this formula is based on the hypothesis of contributions of excited states due only to two quasiparticles. But it turns out that this is not the case for the derivatives. We deduce therefore that the derivatives are not well founded in the formula. We propose then simply to suppress these terms from the formula. Although this solution seems to be simplistic, it solves definitively all its inherent problems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the spurious singularities and unphysical resonances in the Inglis-Belyaev cranking formula for vibrational mass parameters arise because the derivatives of the pairing gap Δ and the Fermi level λ with respect to deformation are not consistent with the fundamental hypothesis that only two-quasiparticle excitations contribute. The author proposes simply removing these derivative terms from the formula, claiming this solves all inherent problems definitively.

## Method

The author revisits the standard Inglis-Belyaev cranking formula for mass parameters in the BCS pairing framework, analyzing the derivation of the "diagonal" term that contains derivatives of Δ and λ with respect to deformation parameters. By examining the formal structure of the formula and its behavior in the limit Δ→0 (unpaired system), the author identifies that the derivative terms originate from quantities that depend on the entire single-particle spectrum rather than on specific two-quasiparticle states, violating the formula's foundational hypothesis. The proposed correction removes these derivative terms, reducing the diagonal contribution to a simple diagonal matrix element of the single-particle Hamiltonian derivative.

## Key numbers

- For the magic nucleus ⁵⁴¹³⁶Xe₈₂, the old formula with derivatives produces a resonance (singularity) in the vibrational parameter B_ββ near deformation β=0.09, with a value of approximately 7,000,000 ħ² MeV⁻¹.
- The proposed formula without derivatives gives small finite values B_ββ ∼ 25 ħ² MeV⁻¹ in the deformation range β=0 to β=0.15.
- During the pairing phase transition (0.1 ≲ Δ ≲ 0.2 MeV), the vibrational parameters increase to B_ββ ∼ 500 ħ² MeV⁻¹ due to pseudo level crossings near the Fermi level.

## Context

This work addresses a long-standing problem in nuclear collective models where the Inglis-Belyaev cranking formula for mass parameters produces unphysical singularities near the pairing phase transition, a difficulty previously reported by multiple authors (e.g., the works cited as Ref. 1, Ref. 10-14 in the paper). The problem has led to contradictory conclusions in the literature, with some researchers claiming the derivative terms are negligible for large pairing gaps while others argue they are important for fission dynamics. The proposed correction makes the mass parameter formula consistent with the Inglis-Belyaev formula for moments of inertia, which does not contain such derivative terms, and enables more reliable calculations of collective spectra using the Generalized Bohr Hamiltonian or Generator Coordinate Method approaches.
