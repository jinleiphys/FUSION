# Spin Polarized Asymmetric Nuclear Matter and Neutron Star Matter Within the Lowest Order Constrained Variational Method

> In this paper, we calculate properties of the spin polarized asymmetrical nuclear matter and neutron star matter, using the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method with the $AV_{18}$, $Reid93$, $UV_{14}$ and $AV_{14}$ potentials. According to our results, the spontaneous phase transition to a ferromagnetic state in the asymmetrical nuclear matter as well as neutron star matter do not occur.

## Key claim
Using the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method with four realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (AV18, Reid93, UV14, AV14), the authors find that spontaneous phase transition to a ferromagnetic state does not occur in asymmetrical nuclear matter or neutron star matter at any density considered, contrary to some earlier calculations that predicted such a transition.

## Method
The authors applied the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method, a fully self-consistent formalism with no free parameters, to calculate the energy per particle of spin-polarized asymmetrical nuclear matter and neutron star matter. They used a Jastrow-type trial wave function with a cluster expansion truncated at the two-body term, minimizing the two-body energy subject to a normalization constraint. For neutron star matter, they imposed charge neutrality and beta equilibrium conditions and included leptonic contributions from electrons and muons.

## Key numbers
- Energy per particle of polarized asymmetrical nuclear matter was calculated for densities up to approximately 1.5 fm⁻³ using four potentials (AV18, Reid93, UV14, AV14); no crossing of energy curves for different polarizations was observed at any density.
- The ratio χ/χF (magnetic susceptibility relative to free Fermi gas) decreases monotonically with density for all isospin asymmetry parameters β, with no sudden change indicating magnetic instability.
- The Landau parameter G0 is always positive and increases monotonically with density for all potentials, never reaching the instability condition G0 < -1.
- For the AV18 potential, the equation of state (pressure vs. density) becomes stiffer with increasing polarization at all isospin asymmetry values.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous LOCV calculations on polarized neutron matter (Bordbar and Bigdeli, 2007) and polarized symmetric nuclear matter (Bordbar and Bigdeli, 2007) to the case of asymmetrical nuclear matter and neutron star matter. It contradicts earlier predictions of ferromagnetic transitions by Brownell and Callaway (1969) using a hard sphere gas model (transition at kF ≈ 2.3 fm⁻¹) and by Østgaard (1970) using a simple central potential (transition at kF ≈ 4.1 fm⁻¹). The results agree with Vidana et al. (2002) and Zuo et al. (2003) using Brueckner-Hartree-Fock methods, and with Pandharipande et al. (1972) using variational methods, all finding no ferromagnetic transition. The calculations enable more realistic modeling of neutron star interiors and their magnetic properties, particularly for understanding pulsar magnetic fields.
