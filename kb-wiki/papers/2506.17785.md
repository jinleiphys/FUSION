# Imaging nuclear shape through anisotropic and radial flow in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Most atomic nuclei exhibit ellipsoidal shapes characterized by quadrupole deformation $β_2$ and triaxiality $γ$, and sometimes even a pear-like octupole deformation $β_3$. The STAR experiment introduced a new "imaging-by-smashing" technique [arXiv:2401.06625, arXiv:2501.16071] to image the nuclear global shape by colliding nuclei at ultra-relativistic speeds and analyzing outgoing debris. Features of nuclear shape manifest in collective observables like anisotropic flow $v_n$ and radial flow via mean transverse momentum $[p_{\mathrm{T}}]$. We present new measurements of the variances of $v_n$ ($n=2$, 3, and 4) and $[p_{\mathrm{T}}]$, and the covariance of $v_n^2$ with $[p_{\mathrm{T}}]$, in collisions of highly deformed $^{238}$U and nearly spherical $^{197}$Au. Ratios of these observables between the two systems effectively suppress common final-state effects, isolating the strong impact of uranium's deformation. By comparing results with state-of-the-art hydrodynamic model calculations, we extract $β_{2\mathrm{U}}$ and $γ_{\mathrm{U}}$ values consistent with those deduced from low-energy nuclear structure measurements. Measurements of $v_3$ and its correlation with $[p_{\mathrm{T}}]$ also provide the first experimental suggestion of a possible octupole deformation for $^{238}$U. These findings provide significant support for using high-energy collisions to explore nuclear shapes on femtosecond timescales, with implications for both nuclear structure and quark-gluon plasma studies.

# Imaging nuclear shape through anisotropic and radial flow in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The STAR experiment demonstrates that ratios of anisotropic flow variances and covariances between U+U and Au+Au collisions can isolate nuclear deformation effects from final-state hydrodynamic responses, enabling extraction of quadrupole deformation parameters β₂ and γ for ²³⁸U that are consistent with low-energy nuclear structure measurements, while also providing the first experimental suggestion of a possible octupole deformation β₃ in ²³⁸U.

## Method
The STAR Collaboration measured event-by-event variances of anisotropic flow coefficients v₂, v₃, and v₄, the variance of mean transverse momentum [pT], and the covariance of vₙ² with [pT] in ²³⁸U+²³⁸U collisions at √sNN = 193 GeV and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV using charged particles reconstructed within |η| < 1 and 0.2 < pT < 3.0 GeV/c. Ratios of these observables between the two systems were constructed to suppress common final-state effects, and the results were compared to state-of-the-art hydrodynamic model calculations to extract nuclear shape parameters.

## Key numbers
- Quadrupole deformation of ²³⁸U extracted: β₂U = 0.287 ± 0.007 (consistent with low-energy electric quadrupole transition data and rigid rotor assumption)
- Triaxiality parameter for ²³⁸U: γU ≲ 8° (consistent with low-energy measurements from PhysRevC.54.2356)
- Estimated octupole deformation for ²³⁸U: β₃U ∼ 0.08 (model-dependent estimate from Moss (1971) and Moller (2015))
- Estimated hexadecapole deformation for ²³⁸U: β₄U ∼ 0.1 (model-dependent estimate)
- ¹⁹⁷Au quadrupole deformation range from low-energy models: β₂Au = 0.1–0.14 with γAu ∼ 45° (Ryssens (2023), Bally (2023))
- Non-flow systematic uncertainties in 0–5% centrality ratios: 2.8% for R(v₂²), 3.5% for R((δpT)²), and 11% for R(v₂²δpT)
- Estimated non-flow fraction in 0–5% central collisions: about 6% for ⟨v₂²⟩ and about 1.4% for ⟨v₂²δpT⟩
- Dataset: approximately 528 million minimum-bias and 120 million triggered central Au+Au events; approximately 300 million minimum-bias and 5 million triggered central U+U events

## Context
This work extends the STAR Collaboration's previous "imaging-by-smashing" technique (STAR, 2024; Jia, 2025) by providing comprehensive measurements of anisotropic flow correlations with radial flow in U+U and Au+Au collisions, building on earlier studies that recognized the influence of β₂ on v₂ (Li, 1999; Shuryak, 1999; Filip, 2009; Shou, 2014; Goldschmidt, 2015) and confirmed by experiments at RHIC (STAR, 2015) and the LHC (Acharya, 2018; Sirunyan, 2019; Aad, 2019). The approach enables extraction of nuclear shape parameters on femtosecond timescales, complementing traditional low-energy spectroscopic and scattering experiments (Verney, 2025; Cline, 1986; Yang, 2022), and provides a new tool for constraining initial conditions in quark-gluon plasma studies.
