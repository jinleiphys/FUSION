# Auxiliary Field Quantum Monte Carlo for Dilute Neutrons on the Lattice

> We employ constrained path Auxiliary Field Quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) in the pursuit of studying physical nuclear systems using a lattice formalism. Since AFQMC has been widely used in the study of condensed-matter systems such as the Hubbard model, we benchmark our method against published results for both one- and two-dimensional Hubbard model calculations. We then turn our attention to cold-atomic and nuclear systems. We use an onsite contact interaction that can be tuned in order to reproduce the known scattering length and effective range of a given interaction. Developing this machinery allows us to extend our calculations to study nuclear systems within a lattice formalism. We perform initial calculations for a range of nuclear systems from two- to few-body neutron systems.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that constrained path Auxiliary Field Quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC), previously successful in condensed-matter systems, can be extended to study dilute neutron systems on a lattice, but only after tuning the lattice dispersion relations and on-site interaction to reproduce the known neutron-neutron scattering length and effective range.

## Method

The authors implement constrained path AFQMC on a cubic lattice, using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to convert the two-body on-site interaction into one-body operators coupled to auxiliary fields. They propagate Slater-determinant walkers through imaginary time using a second-order Trotter-Suzuki decomposition, with a free-particle trial wave function and a constraint to control the fermion sign problem. The method is first benchmarked against analytic results for the 1D and 2D Hubbard model, then extended to cold atomic systems (unitary Fermi gas) and finally to few-body neutron systems by tuning the lattice dispersion relations and interaction strength to reproduce the singlet s-wave neutron-neutron scattering length and effective range.

## Key numbers

- For the 1D Hubbard model at half filling on a 16-site chain with \(N_\uparrow=N_\downarrow=8\), AFQMC ground-state energies agree with analytic results from Woynarovich (1983); the percent difference error increases for weakly interacting systems but improves with twist-averaged boundary conditions.
- For the unitary Fermi gas, the Bertsch parameter extracted from four-particle AFQMC calculations is \(\xi=0.30(1)\), consistent with previously published values of \(\xi=0.288(3)\) (Carlson et al., 2011) and \(\xi=0.280(4)\) (Bour et al., 2011).
- For the neutron-neutron interaction, the dispersion relations are tuned to reproduce scattering length \(a = -18.5\ \text{fm}\) and effective range \(r_e = 2.7\ \text{fm}\) on a lattice with spacing \(\alpha = 1.5\ \text{fm}\); the tuned parameters include \(U\frac{m\alpha^2}{\hbar^2} = -9.6082(9)\) for \(\epsilon_k^{(n_1)}\) and \(-7.389(3)\) for \(\epsilon_k^{(n_2)}\).

## Context

This work extends AFQMC, previously applied to the Hubbard model (Chang and Zhang, 2008; Shi and Zhang, 2013) and the unitary Fermi gas (Carlson et al., 2011), to nuclear systems on a lattice. The authors benchmark against analytic results for the Hubbard model (Lieb and Wu, 1968; Woynarovich, 1983) and exact diagonalization (Husslein et al., 1997; Shi and Zhang, 2013), and against effective range expansion predictions for two unitary fermions (Beane et al., 2004). The tuned dispersion relations enable future AFQMC studies of low-density neutron matter relevant to neutron star crusts and neutron-rich nuclei, building on prior work by Wlazlowski et al. (2014) and Carlson et al. (2003).
