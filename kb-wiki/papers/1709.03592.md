# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of weak transitions in $A\,$=$\,$6--10 nuclei

> Ab initio calculations of the Gamow-Teller (GT) matrix elements in the $β$ decays of $^6$He and $^{10}$C and electron captures in $^7$Be are carried out using both variational and Green's function Monte Carlo wave functions obtained from the Argonne $v_{18}$ two-nucleon and Illinois-7 three-nucleon interactions, and axial many-body currents derived from either meson-exchange phenomenology or chiral effective field theory. The agreement with experimental data is excellent for the electron captures in $^7$Be, while theory overestimates the $^6$He and $^{10}$C data by $\sim 2\%$ and $\sim 10\%$, respectively. We show that for these systems correlations in the nuclear wave functions are crucial to explain the data, while many-body currents increase by $\sim 2$--$3\%$ the one-body GT contributions. These findings suggest that the longstanding $g_A$-problem, i.e., the systematic overprediction ($\sim 20 \%$ in $A\le 18$ nuclei) of GT matrix elements in shell-model calculations, may be resolved, at least partially, by correlation effects.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that *ab initio* Quantum Monte Carlo calculations, using realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions and axial many-body currents, can reproduce measured Gamow-Teller matrix elements in light nuclei without quenching the axial coupling constant \(g_A\), suggesting that the long-standing "\(g_A\)-problem" in shell-model calculations may be at least partially resolved by including correlation effects in nuclear wave functions. However, the theory still overestimates the \(^{10}\)C data by \(\sim 10\%\), which the authors attribute to possible deficiencies in the AV18+IL7 wave functions for \(A=10\) nuclei or contamination from a nearby excited state.

## Method

Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) wave functions were generated using the Argonne \(v_{18}\) two-nucleon and Illinois-7 three-nucleon interactions. Gamow-Teller matrix elements were computed for the \(\beta\) decays of \(^6\)He and \(^{10}\)C and electron captures in \(^7\)Be, using axial many-body currents derived from either meson-exchange phenomenology or chiral effective field theory (\(\chi\)EFT) up to N4LO, including one-body, two-body, and three-body operators.

## Key numbers

- The LO (one-body) Gamow-Teller reduced matrix element for \(^6\)He \(\beta\)-decay is 2.168 (GFMC) / 2.174 (VMC); the experimental value is 2.1609(40).
- The LO Gamow-Teller reduced matrix element for \(^7\)Be electron capture to the \(^7\)Li ground state is 2.294 (GFMC) / 2.334 (VMC); the experimental value is 2.3556(47).
- The LO Gamow-Teller reduced matrix element for \(^7\)Be electron capture to the \(^7\)Li first excited state is 2.083 (GFMC) / 2.150 (VMC); the experimental value is 2.1116(57).
- The LO Gamow-Teller reduced matrix element for \(^{10}\)C \(\beta\)-decay is 2.032 (GFMC) / 2.062 (VMC); the experimental value is 1.8331(34).
- Many-body currents (beyond LO) increase the one-body GT contributions by \(\sim 2\)–\(3\%\) for all processes studied.
- The theory overestimates the \(^6\)He data by \(\sim 2\%\) and the \(^{10}\)C data by \(\sim 10\%\).
- The N4LO contributions obtained with GFMC wave functions are about \(20\%\) larger than those with VMC wave functions for the \(^6\)He and \(^7\)Be-to-\(^7\)Li ground-state transitions.

## Context

This work extends earlier Quantum Monte Carlo studies of weak transitions in \(A=6\)–7 nuclei by Schiavilla (2002) and Pervin (2007), which used either approximate VMC wave functions or only one-body GT operators. The present calculations use more accurate GFMC wave functions and include consistent chiral many-body currents up to N4LO. The results contradict the common shell-model practice of quenching \(g_A\) to fit data, as seen in Chou (1993), and suggest that correlation effects, rather than an effective quenching, can explain the observed matrix elements. The work enables future calculations using consistent chiral potentials and currents, which are noted as being in progress.
