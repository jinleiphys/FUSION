# Projections of structure functions in a spin-one hadrons

> There exist new polarized structure functions in a spin-one hadron. In deep inelastic electron scattering from a spin-one hadron, there are eight structure functions F_1, F_2, g_1, g_2, b_1, b_2, b_3, and b_4. We derive projections to these eight functions from the hadron tensor W^{mu nu} by combinations of the hadron momentum and its polarization vectors.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit projection operators to extract all eight structure functions (F1, F2, g1, g2, b1, b2, b3, b4) from the hadron tensor Wμν for a spin-one hadron, including off-diagonal spin transitions needed for higher-twist contributions, and provides simplified expressions in the Bjorken scaling limit.

## Method

The authors construct eight independent combinations of the hadron momentum pμ, polarization vectors Eμ, and spin vectors sμ, using specific spin states (λ=1,0) and their transitions. They then contract these combinations with the hadron tensor Wμνλfλi to obtain projection formulas for each structure function, working in the target rest frame with the photon moving along the z-axis. The projections are given both in full form and in the Bjorken scaling limit (ν, Q2 → ∞ at finite x).

## Key numbers

- Eight structure functions exist for spin-one hadrons: F1, F2, g1, g2, b1, b2, b3, b4
- In the Bjorken scaling limit, the Callan-Gross type relation holds: 2x F1 → F2 and 2x b1 → b2
- The spin vector for λ=1 state is sμ11 = (0,0,0,1) and for λ=1→0 transition is sμ10 = (1/√2)(0,1,-i,0)
- The polarization vectors are Eμ(λ=±1) = (1/√2)(0,∓1,-i,0) and Eμ(λ=0) = (0,0,0,1)
- The first measurement of b1 was made by HERMES in 2005 at x<0.1

## Context

This work extends prior projection operator methods developed for spin-1/2 nucleons (Projection, 1990; CPS, 1990) to the spin-one case. The paper builds on the theoretical formalism for spin-one hadron structure functions established by Frankfurt and Strikman (1983), Hoodbhoy, Jaffe, and Manohar (1989), and others. The derived projections enable theoretical calculations of tensor structure functions for comparison with experimental measurements at JLab and J-PARC, and are particularly relevant for understanding the b1 structure function, which vanishes if spin-1/2 constituents are in an orbital S state and is sensitive to non-nucleonic degrees of freedom in the deuteron.
