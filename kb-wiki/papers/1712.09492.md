# Kaonic deuterium and low-energy antikaon-nucleon interaction

> A new evaluation of the $1s$ level shift and width of kaonic deuterium is presented based on an accurate $\bar{K}NN$ three-body calculation, using as input a realistic antikaon-nucleon interaction constrained by the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data. The three-body Schrödinger equation is solved with a superposition of a large number of correlated Gaussian basis functions extending over distance scales up to several hundred fm. The resulting energy shift and width of the kaonic deuterium $1s$ level are $E\simeq 0.67$ keV and $Γ\simeq 1.02$ keV, with estimated uncertainties at the 10% level.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new calculation of the kaonic deuterium 1s level shift and width using a full three-body coupled-channels approach, finding values of ΔE ≈ 0.67 keV and Γ ≈ 1.02 keV with estimated uncertainties at the 10% level; the abstract's claim of "realistic antikaon-nucleon interaction constrained by the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data" glosses over the fact that the energy dependence of the antikaon-nucleon potential within the three-body system is handled by setting the two-body energy to zero at threshold as a default choice, with subthreshold corrections estimated to be small but not fully solved self-consistently.

## Method

The coupled-channels Schrödinger equation for the K⁻pn ↔ K̄⁰nn system was solved variationally using a superposition of up to approximately 4000 correlated Gaussian basis functions, covering distance scales from 0.1 fm to several hundred fm. The input antikaon-nucleon interaction was the complex, energy-dependent Kyoto potential based on chiral SU(3) effective field theory, which reproduces low-energy K̄N data including the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen results with χ²/d.o.f. ≈ 1. Physical masses for nucleons and antikaons were used to account for isospin-breaking effects, and the nucleon-nucleon interaction was included via a realistic potential.

## Key numbers

- Kaonic deuterium 1s level shift: ΔE ≈ 0.67 keV (670 eV), with estimated uncertainty less than 10%.
- Kaonic deuterium 1s level width: Γ ≈ 1.02 keV (1016 eV), with estimated uncertainty about 10%.
- The 2p → 1s transition energy is 7.134 keV.
- The K⁻d scattering length derived from the fixed center approximation using the Kyoto potential is a_{K⁻d} = (-1.42 + i 1.60) fm.
- For kaonic hydrogen, the full Schrödinger equation gives ΔE = 283 eV and Γ = 607 eV, compared to the SIDDHARTA experimental values of ΔE = 283 ± 36 ± 6 eV and Γ = 541 ± 89 ± 22 eV.
- The K⁻p scattering length with physical masses is a_{K⁻p} = (-0.66 + i 0.89) fm.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of kaonic deuterium, which fell into two categories: approximate two-body treatments relating kaonic deuterium observables to K̄N scattering lengths (Meissner 2006, Gal 2007, Doring 2011), and three-body Faddeev calculations using separable potentials constrained by SIDDHARTA data (recent Faddeev computations). The present calculation improves on these by solving the full three-body coupled-channels Schrödinger equation with a realistic, chiral-based K̄N potential and a flexible correlated Gaussian basis. The results provide predictions for upcoming experiments SIDDHARTA-2 at LNF and E57 at J-PARC, and the paper demonstrates that kaonic deuterium can provide a significantly improved constraint on the isospin I=1 component of the low-energy K̄N interaction, particularly the poorly known K⁻-neutron sector, if the level shift can be determined within approximately 25% accuracy.
