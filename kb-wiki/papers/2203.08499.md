# Significance of chiral three-nucleon force contact terms for understanding of elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering

> We investigate the importance of the three-nucleon (3N) force contact terms in elastic nucleon-deuteron (Nd) scattering by applying the N$^4$LO$^+$ chiral semi-local momentum space (SMS) regularized nucleon-nucleon (NN) chiral potential supplemented by N$^2$LO and all subleading N$^4$LO three-nucleon force (3NF) contact terms. Strength parameters of the contact terms were obtained by least squares fitting of theoretical predictions to cross section and analyzing powers data at three energies of the impinging nucleon. Although the N$^3$LO contributions to the 3N force were completely neglected, the results calculated with the contact terms multiplied by the fitted strength parameters yield an improved description of the elastic Nd scattering observables in a wide range of incoming nucleon energies below the pion production threshold.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that including all subleading N⁴LO three-nucleon force contact terms, while completely neglecting the N³LO contributions, can improve the description of elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering observables across a wide energy range below pion production threshold, but caution that this study must be considered preliminary until the missing N³LO 3NF components become available.

## Method

The authors performed momentum-space Faddeev calculations for elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering using the N⁴LO⁺ chiral semi-local momentum space (SMS) regularized nucleon-nucleon potential with cutoff Λ = 450 MeV, combined with N²LO and all subleading N⁴LO three-nucleon force contact terms. They employed a perturbative emulator that reduces the computational problem to solving N+1 Faddeev equations once, after which observables for any combination of the 15 contact term strengths (two from N²LO and thirteen from N⁴LO, with two pairs identical leaving 11 independent N⁴LO terms) can be obtained by simple linear combinations. Strength parameters were determined by least-squares fitting to cross section and analyzing power data at three energies (10, 70, and 135 MeV) using the Levenberg-Marquardt method.

## Key numbers

- The N⁴LO⁺ SMS NN potential with regularization parameter Λ = 450 MeV was used as the two-nucleon input
- Five laboratory energies studied: E = 10, 70, 135, 190, and 250 MeV
- The quantity Δ was computed over N_θ = 73 angular points with step 2.5°
- At 190 MeV, the cross section Δ reaches approximately 100% for some contact terms
- Fitted strengths include: c_E1 = 6.40, c_E2 = 7.80, c_E3 = 6.97, c_E7 = -7.40
- Correlation coefficients: ρ(c_E1,c_E2) = 0.95, ρ(c_E1,c_E3) = 0.98, ρ(c_E2,c_E3) = 0.93, ρ(c_E3,c_E4) = -0.92, ρ(c_E7,c_E8) = 0.99
- Final χ² per data point χ²/N ≈ 35 for 786 data points at θ_c.m. > 40°
- Pion decay constant F_π = 92.4 MeV and chiral scale Λ_χ = 700 MeV used in strength definitions
- In the pseudo-data test with incomplete dynamics (missing 2π-exchange term), fitted strengths shifted to c_D = 3.98 ± 0.08 and c_E = 2.99 ± 0.03 with χ²/N ≈ 80

## Context

This work extends the approach of the Pisa group (Girlanda et al., 2019), who showed that augmenting the Urbana IX 3NF with N⁴LO contact terms could describe elastic pd scattering below deuteron breakup threshold together with the ³H binding energy. The present study uses a consistent chiral framework with the N⁴LO⁺ SMS NN potential from the Bochum group (Reinert et al., 2018) rather than phenomenological interactions. The authors' perturbative emulator, developed in their previous work (Witała et al., 2021), enables efficient fitting of all 15 contact term parameters to continuum data, which was previously computationally prohibitive. The results enable future investigations once the missing N³LO 3NF components become available, potentially resolving long-standing discrepancies such as the low-energy analyzing power puzzle and the underprediction of elastic scattering angular distributions above 60 MeV.
