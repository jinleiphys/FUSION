# Constraining MeV to 10 GeV majoron by Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> We estimate the Big Bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) constraint on the majoron in the mass range between $1\,{\rm MeV}$ to $10\,{\rm GeV}$ which dominantly decays into the standard model neutrinos. When the majoron lifetime is shorter than $1\,{\rm sec}$, the injected neutrinos mainly heat up background plasma, which alters the relation between photon temperature and background neutrino temperature. For a lifetime longer than $1\,{\rm sec}$, most of the injected neutrinos directly contribute to the protons-to-neutrons conversion. In both cases, deuterium and helium abundances are enhanced, while the constraint from the deuterium is stronger than that from the helium. $^7{\rm Li}$ abundance gets decreased as a consequence of additional neutrons, but the parameter range that fits the observed $^7{\rm Li}$ abundance is excluded by the deuterium constraint. We also estimate other cosmological constraints and compare them with the BBN bound.

## Key claim
The paper estimates Big Bang Nucleosynthesis constraints on majorons in the 1 MeV to 10 GeV mass range that decay dominantly to standard model neutrinos, finding that deuterium abundance provides a stronger constraint than helium-4, and that the parameter space which could resolve the lithium-7 problem is excluded by the deuterium bound.

## Method
The authors implement majoron decay effects into the public BBN code PArthENOPE by modifying the Boltzmann equations for light nuclei to include nonthermal neutrino injection, altered heating of background neutrino and electromagnetic plasma, and modified expansion rates. They treat the initial majoron yield as a free parameter and compute the evolution of the nonthermal neutrino distribution function using a simplified single-scattering thermalization assumption that provides conservative constraints.

## Key numbers
- Majoron mass range considered: 1 MeV to 10 GeV
- Majoron lifetime formula: tau_J = 0.11 sec * (10^{-11}/g)^2 * (1 GeV/m_J)
- Observed deuterium abundance used for constraints: D/H = (25.47 +/- 0.29) x 10^{-6} (2 sigma exclusion)
- Observed helium-4 abundance used: Y_p = 0.245 +/- 0.003 (2 sigma exclusion)
- Observed helium-3 upper bound: He-3/H < (1.09 +/- 0.18) x 10^{-5}
- Observed lithium-7 abundance: Li-7/H = (1.6 +/- 0.3) x 10^{-10}
- Baryon asymmetry fixed to eta_b = 6.1 x 10^{-10} from Planck 2018
- Neutron lifetime used: tau_n = 879.4 sec
- Delta N_eff upper bound used: 0.29 at 95% confidence from Planck 2018

## Context
This work extends previous BBN constraints on majorons by Chang (1993), Blinov (2019), and Sabti (2019) that focused on the regime where majorons contribute to relativistic degrees of freedom (m_J less than about 10 MeV, g greater than about 10^{-10}). The present analysis covers heavier majorons (1 MeV to 10 GeV) that are thermally decoupled before BBN and decay to neutrinos, providing constraints complementary to supernova bounds from Fiorillo (2022). The results enable future CMB Stage-4 experiments to probe additional parameter space through Delta N_eff measurements.
