# Angular momentum and parity projected multidimensionally constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model

> The nuclear deformations are of fundamental importance in nuclear physics. Recently we developed a multi-dimensionally constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (MDCRHB) model, in which all multipole deformations respecting the $V_4$ symmetry can be considered self-consistently. In this work we extend this model by incorporating the angular momentum projection (AMP) and parity projection (PP) to restore the rotational and parity symmetries broken in the mean-field level. This projected-MDCRHB (p-MDCRHB) model enables us to connect certain nuclear spectra to exotic intrinsic shapes such as triangle or tetrahedron. We present the details of the method and an exemplary calculation for $^{12}$C. We develop a triangular moment constraint to generate the triangular configurations consisting of three $α$ clusters arranged as an equilateral triangle. The resulting $^{12}$C spectra are consistent with that from a triangular rigid rotor for large separations between the $α$ clusters. We also calculate the $B(E2)$ and $B(E3)$ values for low-lying states and find good agreement with the experiments.

## Key claim
The paper extends the multidimensionally constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (MDCRHB) model by adding angular momentum projection (AMP) and parity projection (PP) to restore rotational and parity symmetries broken at the mean-field level, enabling the connection of nuclear spectra to exotic intrinsic shapes such as triangles or tetrahedra. The authors note that the resulting spectra for \(^{12}\)C are consistent with a triangular rigid rotor model only for large separations between the alpha clusters, indicating a conditional agreement rather than a universal description.

## Method
The authors solve the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equation in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator (ADHO) basis with \(V_4\) symmetry imposed, using the density-dependent point-coupling interaction DD-PC1 and a separable pairing force of finite range. They apply angular momentum projection and parity projection to the mean-field states to restore broken symmetries, and use a triangular moment constraint to generate triangular configurations of three alpha clusters arranged as an equilateral triangle for \(^{12}\)C. The method allows simultaneous treatment of triaxial and octupole deformations within a unified framework.

## Key numbers
- Pairing strength fixed to \(G = 1.1 G_0\) with \(G_0 = 728.0\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) and range \(a = 0.644\) fm, determined by fitting to the pairing gap in nuclear matter.
- The \(B(E2)\) and \(B(E3)\) values for low-lying states of \(^{12}\)C are calculated and found to be in good agreement with experiments (no specific numerical values for these transition strengths are provided in the truncated text).
- The nuclear radius used in deformation calculations is \(R = 1.2 A^{1/3}\) fm, where \(A\) is the number of nucleons.

## Context
This work extends the MDCRHB model previously developed by Lu and Zhao (2014, 2017) for studying potential energy surfaces and fission barriers in actinides and superheavy nuclei, and builds on angular momentum projection methods applied to triaxially deformed ground states by Yao (2009) and to axially deformed reflection-asymmetric states by Yao (2015) and Xia (2018). The projected model enables future studies of alpha-clustering structures in light nuclei, such as the Hoyle state in \(^{12}\)C, and can be further extended with particle number projection and the generator coordinate method for soft potential energy surfaces.
