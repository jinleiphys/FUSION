# Relativistic RPA for Isobaric Analogue and Gamow-Teller Resonances in Closed Shell Nuclei

> We perform a self-consistent relativistic RPA calculation for the isobaric analogue and Gamow-Teller resonances based on relativistic mean field theory results for the ground states of $^{48}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb. We use the parameter set NL1 for the $σ$, $ω$ and $ρ$ mesons, and experimental values for the pion and nucleon. An extra parameter, related to the intensity of the contact term in the pion-exchange interaction, is crucial to reproduce the latter resonances.

## Key claim
A self-consistent relativistic RPA calculation, using the NL1 parameter set for the sigma, omega and rho mesons and experimental values for the pion and nucleon, can reproduce the isobaric analogue state (IAS) and Gamow-Teller resonance (GTR) excitation energies in closed-shell nuclei, but only if an extra contact term of strength g' = 0.7 is added to the pion-exchange interaction; without this term the GTR is underestimated by more than 10 MeV.

## Method
The authors performed a relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) calculation based on relativistic mean field theory (RMFT) ground states for 48Ca, 90Zr and 208Pb. They used the NL1 parameter set for the sigma, omega and rho mesons, experimental values for the pion and nucleon, and solved the RRPA equations for Jπ = 0+ and 1+ states in a model-space including only 0ℏΩ and 2ℏΩ excitations, with the residual interaction derived from the same Lagrangian used for the mean field. A Landau-Migdal-type contact term of adjustable strength g' was added to the pion-exchange interaction to reproduce the GTR.

## Key numbers
- IAS in 208Pb: calculated excitation energy 18.6 MeV (experimental 18.83 ± 0.02 MeV), strength 99% of sum rule (experimental ~100%)
- GTR in 208Pb: calculated excitation energy 18.9 MeV (experimental 19.2 ± 0.2 MeV), strength 80% of sum rule (experimental 60-70%)
- IAS in 90Zr: calculated excitation energy 11.9 MeV (experimental 12.0 ± 0.2 MeV), strength 101.3% of sum rule (experimental ~100%)
- GTR in 90Zr: calculated excitation energy 16.0 MeV (experimental 15.6 ± 0.3 MeV), strength 80% of sum rule (experimental 28%)
- IAS in 48Ca: calculated excitation energy 7.48 MeV (experimental 7.17 MeV), strength 100.4% of sum rule (experimental ~100%)
- GTR in 48Ca: calculated excitation energy 11.0 MeV (experimental ~10.5 MeV), strength 82% of sum rule (experimental 35%)
- With the bare interaction (g' = 0), the GTR in 208Pb was calculated at ~6 MeV, more than 10 MeV below the experimental value of 19.2 MeV
- The sum rule was obeyed at the level of 99.5% or better for the IAS and 92.8% or better for the GTR

## Context
This work extends relativistic RPA calculations, previously applied to other giant resonances, to charge-exchange excitations (IAS and GTR) for the first time. It contrasts with nonrelativistic calculations such as those by Colo et al. (1994) using Skyrme forces, which overestimate the GTR excitation energy by 2-3 MeV, and with the work of Krewald et al. (1981) who used a Landau-Migdal interaction of adjustable strength in a nonrelativistic framework. The results enable future studies incorporating 2p-2h excitations or delta-hole excitations to address the remaining quenching of GTR strength.
