# Quarkyonic or baryquark matter? On the dynamical generation of momentum space shell structure

> We study the equation of state of a mixture of (quasi-)free constituent quarks and nucleons with hard-core repulsion at zero temperature. Two opposite scenarios for the realization of the Pauli exclusion principle are considered: (i) a Fermi sea of quarks surrounded by a shell of baryons -- the quarkyonic matter, and (ii) a Fermi sea of nucleons surrounded by a shell of quarks which we call \emph{baryquark matter}. In both scenarios, the sizes of the Fermi sea and shell are fixed through energy minimization at fixed baryon number density. While both cases yield a qualitatively similar transition from hadronic to quark matter, we find that baryquark matter is energetically favored in this setup and yields a physically acceptable behavior of the speed of sound without the need to introduce an infrared regulator. In order to retain the theoretically more appealing quarkyonic matter as the preferred form of dense QCD matter will thus require modifications to the existing dynamical generation mechanisms, such as, for example, the introduction of momentum-dependent nuclear interactions.

# Quarkyonic or baryquark matter? On the dynamical generation of momentum space shell structure

## Key claim

Baryquark matter (a Fermi sea of nucleons surrounded by a shell of quarks) is energetically favored over quarkyonic matter (a Fermi sea of quarks surrounded by a shell of baryons) in a quasiparticle model of constituent quarks and nucleons with hard-core repulsion at zero temperature, but retaining quarkyonic matter as the preferred form of dense QCD matter would require modifications such as momentum-dependent nuclear interactions.

## Method

The authors model isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature as a mixture of (quasi-)free constituent quarks and nucleons, with hard-core repulsion among nucleons incorporated via an excluded volume prescription a la van der Waals. For both quarkyonic and baryquark momentum-space configurations, the sizes of the Fermi sea and shell (parameterized by Fermi momentum k_F and shell width Delta) are determined by minimizing the energy density at fixed baryon number density, following the procedure of Jeong (2019). The resulting equations of state are compared in terms of energy per baryon, quark fraction, speed of sound, and trace anomaly.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass m_N = 0.938 GeV/c^2; number of colors N_c = 3; nuclear saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Limiting nucleon density due to excluded volume: n_0 = 5 rho_0
- In quarkyonic matter, quarks appear only above a threshold density of n_B approximately 4.4 rho_0
- At baryon density n_B = 4.8 rho_0, the energy density exhibits a minimum at nonzero quark fraction for both configurations (with n_0 = 5 rho_0)
- In baryquark matter with attractive mean field (a n_0 = 0.1 m_N), the onset of quarks occurs at n_B approximately 3.4 rho_0
- The speed of sound in baryquark matter exceeds the conformal limit of v_s^2 = 1/3 without requiring an infrared regulator

## Context

This work extends the dynamical generation mechanism for quarkyonic matter introduced by McLerran (2018) and Jeong (2019), which assumed a baryon Fermi shell on top of a quark Fermi sea as the only possible realization of the Pauli exclusion principle. The authors introduce the opposite configuration (baryquark matter) and show it is energetically favored, contradicting the assumption that quarkyonic matter is the natural ground state. This enables future work on isospin-asymmetric matter for neutron star phenomenology, as well as studies of transport properties and heat capacity that would differ significantly between the two scenarios.
