# The Influence of High Multiplicities at RHIC on the Gamov Factor

> The corrections for two-pion correlations due to electromagnetic final-state interactions at high secondary multiplicities are investigated. The analysis is performed by solving the Schrödinger equation with a potential which is dictated by the multi-particle environment. Two different post-freeze-out scenarios are examined. First, for a uniformly spread environment of secondary particles, a screened Coulomb potential is exploited. It is shown that the presence of a static and uniform post-freeze-out medium results in a noticeable deviation from the standard Gamov factor. However, after going to a more realistic model of an expanding pion system, this conclusion changes drastically. We argue that the density of the secondary pions n_π(t,R), where R is a distance from the fireball, is bounded from above by n_π(t,R)\le const/R^2 for all times t. Then, a two-particle scalar potential which is found as a solution of the Maxwell equation for non-uniform medium replaces the screened one. Even this upper limit does not result in an essential deviation from the Gamov correction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while a static, uniform post-freeze-out medium of secondary pions would cause a noticeable deviation from the standard Gamov factor for two-pion Coulomb corrections, a more realistic expanding-pion model shows that even an upper-limit estimate of the secondary pion density (bounded by const/R^2) does not produce an essential deviation from the standard Gamov correction.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrodinger equation for two like-sign pions using a two-particle Coulomb potential modified by the multipion environment. They first examined a static scenario with a uniformly spread secondary particle density, using a screened Coulomb potential. They then developed a more realistic expansion scenario by parametrizing the pion density as n(R) = n_f * R_f^2 / R^2, solving the Maxwell equation for a non-uniform medium to obtain a two-particle scalar potential, and using this potential in the stationary Schrodinger equation to compute the correction factor G_cor = |psi_q(r=0)|^2.

## Key numbers

- For the static scenario with freeze-out density n_pi = 0.25 fm^-3 (LHC conditions) and T_f = 190 MeV, the screening radius R_scr is approximately 7.9 fm.
- For the static scenario with freeze-out density n_pi = 0.03 fm^-3 (SPS conditions) and T_f = 190 MeV, the screening radius R_scr is approximately 22.4 fm.
- The expansion scenario uses freeze-out conditions of n_f = 0.03 fm^-3, T_f = 190 MeV, and R_f = 7.1 fm for SPS conditions.
- For LHC freeze-out conditions (n_f = 0.25 fm^-3), the correction factor shows deviations from the standard Gamov factor that increase with decreasing ratio v_rel/v_cm.
- For SPS freeze-out conditions (n_f = 0.03 fm^-3), the correction factor shows only small deviations from the standard Gamov factor across the momentum range studied.
- The correction factor for the expansion scenario practically coincides with the Gamov factor for relative pion momentum q <= 50 MeV/c, and shows only small deviations for q >= 50-70 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of two-particle final-state interactions (FSI) in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the treatment of Coulomb corrections via the Gamov factor as formulated by Gyulassy, Kauffmann, and Wilson (1979) and by Boal, Gelbke, and Jennings (1990). The paper addresses the question of whether the high multiplicities expected at RHIC and LHC (where Pb+Pb at 160 GeV x A produces 800-900 charged pions) would significantly alter the standard Coulomb FSI treatment. The authors' earlier work (Anchishkin, Zajc, and Zinovjev, 1996) using a quasi-classical approximation is cited as being in good qualitative agreement with the present results. The findings enable continued use of the standard Gamov factor for two-pion correlation analyses at RHIC and LHC energies, despite the high particle multiplicities.
