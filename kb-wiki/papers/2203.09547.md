# Next-to-leading order scalar contributions to $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion

> Within a class of models in which lepton flavor violation is induced dominantly by scalar particle exchanges, we estimate the $μ\to e$ conversion rate in several nuclei. We include next-to-leading order (NLO) terms in the one- and two-nucleon interactions in chiral effective theory, rectifying some incorrect results in the previous literature. We provide an uncertainty budget for the conversion rates and we find that NLO contributions affect the amplitudes at the level of $10\%$, which could be larger than the uncertainty on the leading order couplings, dominated by the strange and non-strange nucleon sigma terms. We study the implications of our results for testing Higgs-mediated CLFV in the future by combining results from various experimental searches, such as $μ\to e$ conversion in multiple target nuclei and $μ\to e γ$.

## Key claim

Within models where lepton flavor violation is dominated by scalar particle exchanges, next-to-leading order (NLO) contributions from one- and two-nucleon interactions in chiral effective theory affect the μ→e conversion amplitudes at the level of 10%, which could be larger than the uncertainty on the leading order couplings that is dominated by the strange and non-strange nucleon sigma terms.

## Method

The authors compute μ→e conversion rates in several nuclei starting from a Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SM-EFT) framework restricted to photonic dipole and scalar four-fermion operators. They match quark-level operators to nucleon-level interactions using SU(2) chiral perturbation theory, including NLO single-nucleon scalar form factors and two-nucleon interactions, and reduce the two-nucleon contributions to effective one-nucleon operators via a Fermi gas average and nuclear shell model calculations. The lepton wave functions are obtained by solving the Dirac equation numerically, and the conversion branching ratios are evaluated as conversion-to-capture ratios for 27Al, 48Ti, 197Au, and 208Pb.

## Key numbers

- The NLO contributions affect the amplitudes at the level of 10%.
- The baseline nucleon sigma term used is σπN = 59.1(3.5) MeV from the Roy-Steiner equations analysis of Hoferichter (2015).
- The strange sigma term used is σs = 41(9) MeV from the lattice QCD average of Aoki (2021).
- The strong-isospin contribution to the nucleon mass splitting is δmN = 2.32(17) MeV from the lattice QCD determination of Brantley (2016).
- The effective single-nucleon coupling from the Fermi gas average is fSIeff = 0.43+0.03-0.22 for 27Al, 0.49+0.03-0.25 for 48Ti, and 0.55+0.03-0.28 for both 197Au and 208Pb.
- The muon capture rates are 0.463 neV for 27Al, 1.705 neV for 48Ti, 8.603 neV for 197Au, and 8.853 neV for 208Pb.
- The Fermi gas average estimate of the two-nucleon contribution is approximately 2-3 times larger than the shell model estimate for 27Al and 48Ti.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of scalar-mediated μ→e conversion by correcting two errors in the NLO calculation of Bartolotta (2017): eliminating unphysical contributions to overlap integrals and providing a corrected expression for the effective one-nucleon interaction from the Fermi gas average. It builds on the chiral perturbation theory framework introduced by Crivellin (2014) and the nuclear-level effective theory of Rule (2021). The results enable improved model discrimination between Higgs-mediated charged lepton flavor violation scenarios by combining μ→e conversion measurements in multiple target nuclei with μ→eγ searches.
