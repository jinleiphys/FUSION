# Holographic vector mesons from spectral functions at finite baryon or isospin density

> We consider gauge/gravity duality with flavor for the finite-temperature field theory dual of the AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background with embedded D7-brane probes. In particular, we investigate spectral functions at finite baryon density in the black hole phase. We determine the resonance frequencies corresponding to meson-mass peaks as function of the quark mass over temperature ratio. We find that these frequencies have a minimum for a finite value of the quark mass. If the quotient of quark mass and temperature is increased further, the peaks move to larger frequencies. At the same time the peaks narrow, in agreement with the formation of nearly stable vector meson states which exactly reproduce the meson mass spectrum found at zero temperature. We also calculate the diffusion coefficient, which has finite value for all quark mass to temperature ratios, and exhibits a first-order phase transition. Finally we consider an isospin chemical potential and find that the spectral functions display a resonance peak splitting, similar to the isospin meson mass splitting observed in effective QCD models.

## Key claim

The resonance frequencies of vector mesons at finite baryon density have a minimum at a finite value of the quark mass over temperature ratio, and the spectral functions display a first-order phase transition in the diffusion coefficient that disappears above a critical baryon density, while for an isospin chemical potential the spectral functions show a resonance peak splitting similar to effective QCD models.

## Method

The authors use gauge/gravity duality with D7-brane probes embedded in an AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background, which is dual to thermal N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory with N_f fundamental hypermultiplets. They solve the equations of motion for the D7-brane embedding and background gauge field numerically, then compute the retarded Green functions and spectral functions for flavor current fluctuations at finite baryon or isospin chemical potential by solving the linearized equations of motion for gauge field fluctuations in momentum space.

## Key numbers

- Critical baryon density parameter: \(\tilde d^* = 0.00315\), where \(\tilde d = 2^{5/2}n_B/(N_f\sqrt{\lambda}T^3)\), above which the first-order phase transition between black hole embeddings disappears
- In the large density limit, the diffusion coefficient asymptotes to \(D \cdot T = 1/(2\pi)\)
- The zero-temperature meson mass formula: \(M = (L_\infty/R^2)\sqrt{2(n+1)(n+2)}\) for s-wave modes with angular quantum number \(l=0\)
- The scaled quark mass is defined as \(m = 2m_q/(\sqrt{\lambda}T) = \lim_{\rho\to\infty} \rho\,\chi(\rho)\)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of spectral functions at finite temperature from Myers et al. (2007), who studied the case of vanishing chemical potential and found discrete spectra at large quark mass with quasiparticle structure at low quark mass. The authors connect their results to the phase diagram found by Mateos et al. (2007) and Karch et al. (2007), which shows regions of Minkowski and black hole embeddings at finite chemical potential. The first-order phase transition in the diffusion coefficient relates to the fundamental confinement/deconfinement transition previously studied by Kobayashi et al. (2006). The isospin chemical potential analysis extends earlier work by Erdmenger et al. (2007) from the massless quark case to non-vanishing quark mass, and the observed peak splitting is compared to effective QCD models.
