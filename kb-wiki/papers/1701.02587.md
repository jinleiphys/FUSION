# Applications of nuclear physics to a wider context: from molecules to stars passing through hypernuclei

> In this contribution I will review some of the researches that are currently being pursued in Padova (mainly within the In:Theory and Strength projects), focusing on the interdisciplinary applications of nuclear theory to several other branches of physics, with the aim of contributing to show the centrality of nuclear theory in the Italian scientific scenario and the prominence of this fertile field in fostering new physics. In particular, I will talk about: i) the recent solution of the long-standing "electron screening puzzle" that settles a fundamental controversy in nuclear astrophysics between the outcome of lab experiments on earth and nuclear reactions happening in stars; the application of algebraic methods to very diverse systems such as: ii) the supramolecular complex H2@C60, i.e. a diatomic hydrogen molecule caged in a fullerene and iii) to the spectrum of hypernuclei, i.e. systems made of a Lambda particles trapped in (heavy) nuclei.

# Applications of nuclear physics to a wider context: from molecules to stars passing through hypernuclei

**Authors:** Fortunato, Lorenzo  
**Date:** 2017-01-10

## Key claim

The long-standing "electron screening puzzle" in nuclear astrophysics can be resolved by recognizing that cluster structure in certain nuclei (such as lithium, beryllium, and boron) significantly alters fusion dynamics at stellar energies, rather than requiring exotic atomic physics explanations. The paper also claims that algebraic methods inspired by nuclear physics can provide unified theoretical frameworks for both endohedrally confined diatomic molecules and hypernuclei, with fewer parameters than competing approaches.

## Method

The author reviews three research lines from the Padova group. For the electron screening puzzle, experimental screening potentials from 21 reactions are compared to theoretical adiabatic limits, and the discrepancy is attributed to cluster structure effects in the reacting nuclei, modeled by calculating angle-averaged tunneling probabilities for dicluster configurations using the WKB approximation. For the H2@C60 supramolecular complex, an algebraic model is constructed by coupling a u(4) vibron model (describing the free diatomic molecule) with a u(3) algebra (describing spatial confinement inside the fullerene cage), with parameters fitted to approximately 70 experimental transition lines. For hypernuclei, an algebraic model based on a u(3/2) superalgebra is developed, combining a u(3) bosonic anharmonic oscillator for the Lambda particle's orbital motion with a u(2) fermionic symmetry for the hyperon degree of freedom, and fitted to experimental mass spectra of 89_Lambda Y and 51_Lambda V hypernuclei from KEK-PS experiments.

## Key numbers

- For the 2H(d,t)1H reaction: theoretical adiabatic screening limit U_e^adlim = 14 eV, experimental screening potential U_e^exp = 13.2 +/- 1.8 eV
- For the 3He(d,p)4He reaction: theoretical adiabatic screening limit U_e^adlim = 120 eV, experimental screening potential U_e^exp = 219 +/- 7 eV
- For the 9Be(p,alpha_0)6Li reaction: theoretical adiabatic screening limit U_e^adlim = 240 eV, experimental screening potential U_e^exp = 900 +/- 50 eV
- For the 11B(p,alpha_0)8Be reaction: theoretical adiabatic screening limit U_e^adlim = 340 eV, experimental screening potential U_e^exp = 472 +/- 120 eV
- The algebraic model for H2@C60 uses 10 parameters to describe all vibrational bands, compared to competing theories that fit bands separately with a larger number of parameters
- For 89_Lambda Y hypernucleus: best-fit parameters alpha = 5.39547 MeV, beta = 0.506972 MeV, gamma = -0.321663 MeV, E_Lambda = -22.6373 MeV
- For 51_Lambda V hypernucleus: best-fit parameters alpha = 7.2807 MeV, beta = 0.495241 MeV, gamma = -0.476428 MeV, E_Lambda = -19.003 MeV

## Context

This paper extends the work on the electron screening puzzle previously reported by the author and collaborators (Spit, news references), where the discrepancy between laboratory and stellar nuclear reaction rates had been a fundamental controversy in nuclear astrophysics. The algebraic approach to H2@C60 builds on the Vibron model of Iachello and Levine (Iach) and prior experimental work on endohedrally confined molecules by Komatsu (Koma), Mamone (Mamo), and Horse (Horse). The hypernuclear model extends shell-model calculations by Motoba (Motoba08) and the experimental work of Hotchi (Hotchi) and Hashimoto (Hashi) on Lambda particle spectroscopy at KEK-PS. The paper enables future applications of algebraic methods to other confined quantum systems and suggests a new method for solving the non-relativistic Schroedinger equation applicable to atomic, molecular, and nuclear physics.
