# Chiral and deconfinement transition from correlation functions: SU(2) vs. SU(3)

> We study a gauge invariant order parameter for deconfinement and the chiral condensate in SU(2) and SU(3) Yang-Mills theory in the vicinity of the deconfinement phase transition using the Landau gauge quark and gluon propagators. We determine the gluon propagator from lattice calculations and the quark propagator from its Dyson-Schwinger equation, using the gluon propagator as input. The critical temperature and a deconfinement order parameter are extracted from the gluon propagator and from the dependency of the quark propagator on the temporal boundary conditions. The chiral transition is determined using the quark condensate as order parameter. We investigate whether and how a difference in the chiral and deconfinement transition between SU(2) and SU(3) is manifest.

## Key claim

The study demonstrates that the chromoelectric (longitudinal) part of the Landau gauge gluon propagator reacts strongly to the deconfinement phase transition, and this behavior can be used to extract gauge-invariant critical temperatures for both SU(2) and SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, with the chiral and deconfinement transitions occurring at essentially the same temperature within the resolution of the temperature grid employed.

## Method

The gluon propagator was computed from lattice gauge theory using the Wilson action for SU(2) and SU(3) at temperatures between zero and roughly twice the critical temperature, with the Landau gauge fixed via the minimal or average-B prescription. The resulting gluon propagator was used as input into the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator, from which the conventional quark condensate (order parameter for chiral symmetry breaking) and the dressed Polyakov loop (order parameter for deconfinement) were extracted as functions of temperature.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for SU(2): T_c = 277 MeV
- Critical temperature for SU(3): T_c = 303 MeV
- The electric screening mass m_L = D_L(p → 0)^{-1/2} drops to a minimum at T_c and then quickly increases; the associated susceptibility χ_T = ∂m_L/∂T is much larger for SU(3) than for SU(2)
- Quark-gluon vertex fit parameter d_1 = 7.6 GeV^2 for SU(2) and d_1 = 4.6 GeV^2 for SU(3), with d_2 = 0.5 GeV^2 for both
- Quark mass used in massive calculations: m ≈ 80 MeV (strange quark mass)
- Renormalization scale: 2 GeV, with α(μ) = 0.3

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Fischer, Maas, and Mueller (2009) and Fischer et al. (2009) by using much finer temperature resolution and larger physical volumes for the lattice gluon propagator, and by extending the analysis from SU(2) to SU(3). The results confirm earlier findings by Cucchieri, Maas, and Mendes (2007) that the longitudinal gluon propagator shows a dramatic response to the phase transition, and the extracted critical temperatures agree with those determined from the Polyakov loop by Fingberg, Karsch, and Peikert (1992) and Lucini, Teper, and Wenger (2003). The approach enables future studies of the QCD phase diagram at non-zero chemical potential using functional methods.
