# The Nuclear Structure and Associated Electron Capture Rates on Odd-Z Nucleus $^{51}$V in Stellar Matter

> The Gamow-Teller strength distribution function, B(GT), for the odd Z parent $^{51}$V, $N-Z$ =5, up to 30 MeV of excitation energy in the daughter $^{51}$Ti is calculated in the domain of proton-neutron Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (pn-QRPA) theory. The pn-QRPA results are compared against other theoretical calculations, (n, p) and high resolution (d, $^{2}$He) reaction experiments. For the case of (d, $^{2}$He) reaction the calibration was performed for $0\leq E_{j} \leq 5.0$ MeV, where the authors stressed that within this excitation energy range the $ΔL = 0$ transition strength can be extracted with high accuracy for $^{51}$V. Within this energy range the current pn-QRPA total B(GT) strength 0.75 is in good agreement with the (d, $^{2}$He) experiment's total strength of 0.9 $\pm$ 0.1. The pn-QRPA calculated Gamow-Teller centroid at 4.20 MeV in daughter $^{51}$Ti is also in good agreement with high resolution (d, $^{2}$He) experiment which placed the Gamow-Teller centroid at 4.1 $\pm$ 0.4 MeV in daughter $^{51}$Ti. The low energy detailed Gamow-Teller structure and Gamow-Teller centroid play a sumptuous role in associated weak decay rates and consequently affect the stellar dynamics. The stellar weak rates are sensitive to the location and structure of these low-lying states in daughter $^{51}$Ti. The calculated electron capture rates on $^{51}$V in stellar matter are also in good agreement with the large scale shell model rates.

## Key claim

The pn-QRPA calculation reproduces the low-energy Gamow-Teller strength distribution for $^{51}$V in good agreement with high-resolution (d, $^{2}$He) experimental data, but only within the calibrated excitation energy range of 0 to 5.0 MeV where $\Delta L = 0$ transitions can be extracted with high accuracy; the agreement is less satisfactory when compared to (n,p) reaction data at higher excitation energies.

## Method

The authors used the proton-neutron Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (pn-QRPA) theory with a model space of $7\hbar\omega$ to calculate the Gamow-Teller strength distribution B(GT$_{+}$) for the odd-Z parent nucleus $^{51}$V up to 30 MeV excitation energy in the daughter $^{51}$Ti. The Hamiltonian included single-particle, pairing, particle-hole GT, and particle-particle GT forces, with pairing treated in the BCS approximation. The calculated GT strength was scaled with a quenching factor of 0.36, and the resulting electron capture rates were computed for a grid of stellar temperatures and densities using the phase space integrals and Boltzmann occupation probabilities for parent excited states.

## Key numbers

- Total B(GT) strength from pn-QRPA for $^{51}$V within 0 to 5.0 MeV excitation energy: 0.75 (quenched), compared to (d, $^{2}$He) experimental total strength of 0.9 $\pm$ 0.1.
- pn-QRPA Gamow-Teller centroid at 4.20 MeV in daughter $^{51}$Ti, in agreement with (d, $^{2}$He) experiment centroid at 4.1 $\pm$ 0.4 MeV.
- (n,p) reaction reports total strength of 1.2 $\pm$ 0.1 up to 8 MeV for $^{51}$V; Aufderheide et al. extracted total GT strength of 1.5 $\pm$ 0.2 up to 12 MeV.
- Shell model (KB3G interaction) placed GT centroid at 4.34 MeV in daughter $^{51}$Ti; large-scale shell model (Caurier et al.) gave total GT strength of 1.4 with centroid at 5.18 MeV.
- FFN calculation placed GT resonance centroid at 3.83 MeV excitation energy in daughter.
- At high density ($\rho Y_{e} = 10^{11} g cm^{-3}$), pn-QRPA electron capture rates are suppressed by a factor of two compared to large-scale shell model rates, and by roughly an order of magnitude compared to FFN rates.

## Context

This work extends the application of pn-QRPA theory to odd-Z nuclei in the iron region, specifically $^{51}$V, which plays a key role in presupernova evolution and neutrino-process nucleosynthesis. The results are compared against the pioneering FFN calculations (Fuller, Fowler, and Newman, 1982), large-scale shell model calculations by Caurier et al. (1999) and Langanke et al. (2001), and experimental data from (d, $^{2}$He) reactions (Baumer et al., 2003) and (n,p) reactions (Alford et al., 1993). The calculated electron capture rates, being smaller than FFN rates at high densities, may affect simulations of Type-Ia supernova nucleosynthesis where earlier FFN rates led to overproduction of iron-group nuclei (Brachwitz et al., 2000). This work enables improved input for core-collapse supernova simulation codes that require weak interaction rates for hundreds of nuclei.
