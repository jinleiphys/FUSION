# Triangle mechanisms in the build up and decay of the $N^*(1875)$

> We have studied the $N^*(1875) (3/2^-)$ resonance with a multichannel unitary scheme, considering the $Δπ$ and $Σ^* K$, with their interaction extracted from chiral Lagrangians, and then have added two more channels, the $N^*(1535) π$ and $N σ$, which proceed via triangle diagrams involving the $Σ^* K$ and $Δπ$ respectively in the intermediate states. The triangle diagram in the $N^*(1535) π$ case develops a singularity at the same energy as the resonance mass. We determine the couplings of the resonance to the different channels and the partial decay widths. We find a very large decay width to $Σ^* K$, and also see that, due to interference with other terms, the $N σ$ channel has an important role in the $ππ$ mass distributions at low invariant masses, leading to an apparently large $N σ$ decay width. A discussion is done, justifying the convenience of an experimental reanalysis of this resonance to the light of the findings of the paper, using multichannel unitary schemes.

## Key claim

The $N^*(1875)$ resonance is not merely a threshold effect from the $\Sigma^*K$ channel as previously thought, but is reinforced by a triangle singularity in the $N^*(1535)\pi$ channel that develops at the same energy as the resonance mass, and the $N\sigma$ channel plays an important role in the $\pi\pi$ mass distributions at low invariant masses due to interference, leading to an apparently large $N\sigma$ decay width.

## Method

The authors constructed a multichannel unitary coupled-channel scheme starting from the $\Delta\pi$ and $\Sigma^*K$ channels with interactions from chiral Lagrangians, then added the $N^*(1535)\pi$ and $N\sigma$ channels via triangle diagrams involving $\Sigma^*K$ and $\Delta\pi$ intermediate states respectively. Effective $s$-wave transition potentials were constructed from the $p$-wave triangle amplitudes, and a $4\times4$ Bethe-Salpeter equation was solved to extract resonance couplings and partial decay widths.

## Key numbers

- The $N^*(1875)$ resonance mass from PDG is 1820-1920 MeV (1875 MeV estimate) with width 250 $\pm$ 70 MeV; from Anisovich et al. the mass is 1880 $\pm$ 20 MeV with width 200 $\pm$ 70 MeV; from Sokhoyan et al. the mass is 1875 $\pm$ 20 MeV with width 200 $\pm$ 25 MeV.
- The most important decay modes from PDG are $N\omega$ (15-25%), $\Delta(1232)\pi$ (10-35%), and $N\sigma$ ($Nf_0(500)$) (30-60%).
- The coupling $g_{\Sigma^*,\pi\Lambda} = 0.0090$ MeV$^{-1}$ was obtained from the experimental $\Sigma^*\rightarrow\pi\Lambda$ width.
- The coupling $g_{N^*,K\Lambda} = -1.28$ was taken from the chiral unitary approach of Inoue et al.
- The coupling $f_{\pi N\Delta} = 2.2$ was used for the $\Delta\rightarrow N\pi$ decay.
- The $\sigma$ meson parameters used were $m_\sigma = 445$ MeV and $\Gamma_\sigma/2 = 275$ MeV.
- The effective transition potential $\tilde{V}_{i,\pi N^*}/V^{(I=1/2)}_{i,\Sigma^*K}$ reaches about 0.22 at the peak around 1900 MeV.
- The effective transition potential $\tilde{V}_{i,\sigma N}/V^{(I=1/2)}_{i,\Delta\pi}$ ranges from 0.73 to 0.69 between 1800 and 2100 MeV.
- The triangle singularity condition from the Coleman-Norton theorem gives a solution at 1878 MeV for the $N^*(1535)\pi$ channel.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Sarkar et al. (2005) which identified a peak in the $\Sigma^*K$ channel at 1875 MeV as a threshold effect rather than a genuine resonance. The present paper adds triangle mechanisms leading to $N^*(1535)\pi$ and $N\sigma$ channels, showing that the $N^*(1875)$ is reinforced by a triangle singularity. The methodology follows the treatment of triangle singularities by Landau (1959) and the Coleman-Norton theorem, and uses the formulation of Guo et al. for evaluating triangle integrals. The work enables experimental reanalysis of the $N^*(1875)$ resonance using multichannel unitary schemes, particularly to clarify the role of the $N\sigma$ decay channel and the $\pi\pi$ mass distributions at low invariant masses.
