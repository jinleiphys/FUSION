# Medium-induced jet evolution: wave turbulence and energy loss

> We study the gluon cascade generated via successive medium-induced branchings by an energetic parton propagating through a dense QCD medium. We focus on the high-energy regime where the energy $E$ of the leading particle is much larger than the characteristic medium scale $ω_c=\hat q L^2/2$, with $\hat q$ the jet quenching parameter and $L$ the distance travelled through the medium. In this regime the leading particle loses only a small fraction $\simα_s(ω_c/E)$ of its energy and can be treated as a steady source of radiation for gluons with energies $ω\leω_c$. For this effective problem with a source, we obtain exact analytic solutions for the gluon spectrum and the energy flux. The solutions exhibit wave turbulence: the basic physical process is a continuing fragmentation which is `quasi-democratic' (i.e. quasi-local in energy) and which provides an energy transfer from the source to the medium at a rate (the energy flux $\mathcal{F}$) which is quasi-independent of $ω$. The locality of the branching process implies a spectrum of the Kolmogorov-Obukhov type, i.e. a power-law spectrum which is a fixed point of the branching process and whose strength is proportional to the energy flux: $D(ω)\sim\mathcal{F}/\sqrtω$ for $ω\llω_c$. Via this turbulent flow, the gluon cascade loses towards the medium an energy $ΔE\simα_s^2ω_c$, which is independent of the initial energy $E$ of the leading particle and of the details of the thermalization mechanism at the low-energy end of the cascade. This energy is carried away by very soft gluons, which propagate at very large angles with respect to the jet axis. Our predictions for the value of $ΔE$ and for its angular distribution appear to agree quite well, qualitatively and even semi-quantitatively, with the phenomenology of di-jet asymmetry in nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

In the high-energy regime where the leading particle energy \(E\) is much larger than the medium scale \(\omega_c = \hat q L^2/2\), the leading particle loses only a small fraction \(\sim \alpha_s (\omega_c/E)\) of its energy and can be treated as a steady source of radiation for gluons with energies \(\omega \le \omega_c\); the resulting gluon cascade exhibits wave turbulence with a Kolmogorov-Obukhov type spectrum \(D(\omega) \sim \mathcal{F}/\sqrt{\omega}\) for \(\omega \ll \omega_c\), and the energy lost to the medium \(\Delta E \sim \alpha_s^2 \omega_c\) is independent of the initial energy \(E\).

## Method

The authors derived and solved the rate equation for the gluon spectrum \(D(\omega,t)\) describing successive medium-induced branchings, using the BDMPSZ splitting rate for gluon emissions in a dense QCD medium characterized by the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\). They focused on the high-energy regime \(E \gg \omega_c\) where the leading particle acts as a steady source, obtaining exact analytic solutions for the gluon spectrum and energy flux via a classical stochastic branching process with quasi-democratic (quasi-local in energy) branchings.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic medium scale \(\omega_c = \hat q L^2/2\), with \(\hat q\) the jet quenching parameter and \(L\) the distance travelled through the medium.
- The soft scale \(\omega_s = \bar{\alpha}^2 \omega_c\), where \(\bar{\alpha} \equiv \alpha_s N_c/\pi\).
- The energy lost by the gluon cascade to the medium is \(\Delta E_{\rm flow} \simeq \upsilon \, \omega_s\) with \(\upsilon \simeq 4.96\), independent of the initial energy \(E\).
- For phenomenologically motivated values \(\hat q = 1\) GeV\(^2\)/fm and \(\bar{\alpha}=0.3\), with \(L\) from 2 to 6 fm: \(\omega_c \simeq (10, 40, 90)\) GeV and \(\omega_s \simeq (1, 4, 9)\) GeV, yielding \(\Delta E_{\rm flow} \simeq 10\) to 20 GeV.
- The gluon spectrum for \(\omega \ll \omega_c\) follows \(D(\omega) \sim \mathcal{F}/\sqrt{\omega}\), where \(\mathcal{F}\) is the energy flux.
- The average energy loss \(\langle \Delta E \rangle \sim \bar{\alpha} \sqrt{\omega_c \omega_{\rm max}}\) with \(\omega_{\rm max} \equiv {\rm min}(\omega_c, E)\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of medium-induced gluon cascades from the low-energy regime \(E \lesssim \omega_c\) studied by Blaizot et al. (2013) to the high-energy regime \(E \gg \omega_c\) relevant for di-jet asymmetry in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC. The turbulent flow mechanism provides a natural explanation for the energy transport to large angles observed in LHC experiments (Chatrchyan et al., 2011; Aad et al., 2012), which earlier studies based solely on the BDMPSZ spectrum (Salgado and Wiedemann, 2003) failed to predict. The results enable quantitative predictions for the energy loss and angular distribution of soft gluons in heavy-ion collisions.
