# New graphical criterion for the selection of complete sets of polarization observables and its application to single-meson photoproduction as well as electroproduction

> This paper combines the graph-theoretical ideas behind Moravcsik's theorem with a completely analytic derivation of discrete phase-ambiguities, recently published by Nakayama. The result is a new graphical procedure for the derivation of certain types of complete sets of observables for an amplitude-extraction problem with $N$ helicity-amplitudes. The procedure is applied to pseudoscalar meson photoproduction ($N = 4$ amplitudes) and electroproduction ($N = 6$ amplitudes), yielding complete sets with minimal length of $2N$ observables. For the case of electroproduction, this is the first time an extensive list of minimal complete sets is published. Furthermore, the generalization of the proposed procedure to processes with a larger number of amplitudes, i.e. $N > 6$ amplitudes, is sketched. The generalized procedure is outlined for the next more complicated example of two-meson photoproduction ($N = 8$ amplitudes).

## Key claim

The paper presents a new graphical procedure for deriving complete sets of polarization observables for amplitude-extraction problems, claiming that for pseudoscalar meson photoproduction (N = 4 amplitudes) and electroproduction (N = 6 amplitudes), minimal complete sets of exactly 2N observables can be obtained. The hedge is that the procedure is demonstrated for these specific cases, while generalization to processes with larger numbers of amplitudes (N > 6) is only sketched.

## Method

The author combined graph-theoretical ideas underlying Moravcsik's theorem with an analytic derivation of discrete phase-ambiguities recently published by Nakayama. This hybrid approach was then applied to pseudoscalar meson photoproduction (with 4 helicity amplitudes) and electroproduction (with 6 helicity amplitudes) to derive complete sets of polarization observables. The generalization of the procedure was outlined for two-meson photoproduction, which involves 8 amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- For pseudoscalar meson photoproduction: minimal complete sets contain 2N = 8 observables (N = 4 amplitudes)
- For pseudoscalar meson electroproduction: minimal complete sets contain 2N = 12 observables (N = 6 amplitudes)
- For two-meson photoproduction: the generalized procedure is outlined for N = 8 amplitudes

## Context

This work extends the graph-theoretical framework of Moravcsik's theorem by incorporating the analytic phase-ambiguity analysis of Nakayama (recently published). The paper provides the first extensive published list of minimal complete sets for electroproduction, which had not been previously available. The procedure enables future work on amplitude extraction for processes with larger numbers of helicity amplitudes, with the next step being application to two-meson photoproduction.
