# Proton 0.01 MeV resonance width and low-energy $S$-factor of p+10B fusion

> {\bf{Purpose}:} In this paper, the proton resonance width of the near-threshold resonance is calculated using two different approaches. The values of the proton width are used to calculate the low-energy $S$-factor. \\ {\bf{Method:}} First, the proton resonance width is estimated using the mirror symmetry of the resonance $ {}^{11}{\rm C}(E_{x}=8.70\, {\rm MeV};\,\frac{5}{2}^{+})$ and the mirror bound state ${}^{11}{\rm B}(E_{x}=9.272\, {\rm MeV};\,\frac{5}{2}^{+})$. In the second approach, this width is estimated using the $R$-matrix definition of the observable resonance width, which is expressed in terms of the $p-{}^{10}{\rm B}$ resonant wave function calculated in the potential approach and the spectroscopic factor. \\ {\bf{Results}:} Depending on the method chosen, the calculated proton resonance width varies from $\,1.03 \times 10^{-19}\,$MeV to $\,2.96 \times 10^{-19}\,$MeV. The role of the near-threshold resonance is determined using fitting of two low-energy $S$-factors from direct measurements [C. Angulo {\it et al.}, Z. Phys. A {\bf 345}, 333 (1993)] and from the indirect Trojan horse method (THM) [ A. Cvetinović {\it et al.}, Phys. Rev. C {\bf 97}, 065801 (2018)]. Within the framework of the $R$-matrix method using the determined proton resonance widths and the modified THM parameters for six low-lying resonances, the low-energy $S$-factors were calculated and compared with the corresponding experimental $S$-factors. The closest agreement with the data is achieved with the proton resonance widths $1.0 \times 10^{-19}$ MeV when fitting the $S$-factor from the THM indirect measurements, and $\,1.68 \times 10^{-19}\,$ MeV and $\,2.5 \times 10^{-19}\,$ MeV when fitting the $S$-factor from [Angulo {\it et al}, Z. Phys. A {\bf 345}, 333 (1993)].\

## Key claim
The proton resonance width of the near-threshold resonance in 11C (Ex=8.70 MeV; 5/2+) is calculated to be on the order of 10^-19 MeV, which is about an order of magnitude larger than the phenomenological value of 2.3 x 10^-20 MeV deduced from the R-matrix fit to higher-energy direct measurements by Van de Kolk (2022), but the calculated widths successfully fit the low-energy S-factor data from direct and indirect measurements.

## Method
The proton resonance width was calculated using two approaches: (1) mirror symmetry, relating the resonance in 11C to the mirror bound state in 11B (Ex=9.272 MeV; 5/2+) via the ratio of the resonance width to the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the mirror state, using single-particle wave functions from a Woods-Saxon potential; (2) the R-matrix definition, expressing the observable width in terms of the penetrability factor and the observable reduced width, which depends on the internal resonance wave function and the spectroscopic factor. The calculated widths were then used in R-matrix fits to low-energy S-factor data from direct measurements by Angulo (1993) and from the indirect Trojan horse method (THM) by Cvetinović (2018), including contributions from six low-lying resonances and one subthreshold state.

## Key numbers
- The calculated proton resonance width ranges from 1.03 x 10^-19 MeV to 2.96 x 10^-19 MeV, depending on the method and input ANC.
- From mirror symmetry using the neutron ANC from stripping reactions (0.83 fm^-1/2), the width is 1.03 x 10^-19 MeV (for channel radius 4.0 and 5.0 fm).
- From mirror symmetry using the shell-model ANC (1.114 fm^-1/2), the width is 1.87 x 10^-19 MeV (for channel radius 4.0 and 5.0 fm).
- From the R-matrix approach with spectroscopic factor 0.31, the width is 1.68 x 10^-19 MeV (channel radius 4.0 fm) and 1.86 x 10^-19 MeV (channel radius 5.0 fm).
- The best fits to the S-factor from Angulo (1993) are achieved with proton resonance widths of 1.68 x 10^-19 MeV and 2.5 x 10^-19 MeV.
- The best fit to the THM S-factor from Cvetinović (2018) uses a proton resonance width of 1.0 x 10^-19 MeV.
- The alpha-particle resonance width of the near-threshold resonance is well established at 0.015 keV (1.5 x 10^-5 MeV).

## Context
This work addresses the large uncertainty in the proton resonance width of the 0.01 MeV resonance in the 10B(p,alpha)7Be reaction, which is critical for nuclear astrophysics (boron destruction in stars) and aneutronic fusion applications. The calculated widths are about an order of magnitude larger than the phenomenological value from the R-matrix fit by Van de Kolk (2022), but significantly smaller than the 2.2 x 10^-17 MeV value from the shell model embedded in the continuum (SMEC) approach by Okolowicz. The results enable improved low-energy S-factor evaluations and resolve discrepancies between direct measurements by Angulo (1993) and indirect THM data by Cvetinović (2018), while also providing input for future experiments and fusion reactor modeling.
