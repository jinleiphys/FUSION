# Effects of a chiral three-nucleon force on nucleus-nucleus scattering

> We investigate the effects of chiral NNLO three-nucleon force (3NF) on nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering, using a standard prescription based on the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method and the g-matrix folding model. The g-matrix calculated in nuclear matter from the chiral N3LO two-nucleon forces (2NF) is close to that from the Bonn-B 2NF. Because the Melbourne group have already developed a practical g-matrix interaction by localizing the nonlocal g-matrix calculated from the Bonn-B 2NF, we consider the effects of chiral 3NF, in this first attempt to study the 3NF effects, by modifying the local Melbourne g-matrix according to the difference between the g-matrices of the chiral 2NF and 2NF+3NF. For nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering, the 3NF corrections make the folding potential less attractive and more absorptive. The latter novel effect is due to the enhanced tensor correlations in triplet channels. These changes reduce the differential cross section at the middle and large angles, improving the agreement with the experimental data for 16O-16O scattering at 70 MeV/nucleon and 12C-12C scattering at 85 MeV/nucleon.

## Key claim

Chiral NNLO three-nucleon force (3NF) corrections make the folding potential for nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering less attractive and more absorptive, with the novel absorptive effect arising from enhanced tensor correlations in triplet channels; this is the first estimate of chiral-3NF effects on nucleus-nucleus scattering, using a simple prescription that modifies the local Melbourne g-matrix by the ratio of single-particle potentials from chiral 2NF+3NF to those from chiral 2NF alone.

## Method

The authors used the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) method for symmetric nuclear matter to calculate single-particle potentials from chiral N3LO two-nucleon forces (2NF) plus NNLO 3NF, with the 3NF reduced to an effective 2NF via the mean-field approximation by averaging over the third nucleon in the Fermi sea. They then introduced 3NF corrections to the local Melbourne g-matrix interaction (originally derived from Bonn-B 2NF) by multiplying it channel-by-channel by the ratio of the single-particle potential from chiral 2NF+3NF to that from chiral 2NF alone. Finally, they used the g-matrix folding model with the frozen density approximation to compute folding potentials and differential cross sections for 16O-16O scattering at 70 MeV/nucleon and 12C-12C scattering at 85 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- For 16O-16O scattering at 70 MeV/nucleon, the total reaction cross section with 3NF corrections is 1423 mb, compared to 1401 mb without 3NF (no experimental data available).
- For 12C-12C scattering at 85 MeV/nucleon, the total reaction cross section with 3NF corrections is 1025 mb, compared to 989 mb without 3NF; the experimental value is 998 ± 13 mb (from Tak09).
- Chiral 3NF effects on the single-particle potential are negligible for Fermi momenta kF below about 1.1 fm^-1, corresponding to densities below about half normal nuclear density (ρ0/2).
- The 3NF corrections reduce differential cross sections at middle and large angles (θ > 5°) for both systems, improving agreement with experimental data from Nuo98 and Bue81.

## Context

This work extends the Melbourne group's g-matrix folding model (Amo00), which used the Bonn-B 2NF and was highly reliable for nucleon-nucleus scattering, to include chiral 3NF effects for the first time in nucleus-nucleus scattering. It contrasts with previous phenomenological 3NF treatments (Fur08, Fur09a, Fur09b, Raf13) that made the folding potential less attractive and less absorptive; the chiral 3NF instead enhances absorption due to tensor correlations. The results enable future studies of higher-order corrections such as off-shell effects from chiral 3NF and corrections to the mean-field approximation, as well as investigations of projectile/target excitation effects (Fur13).
