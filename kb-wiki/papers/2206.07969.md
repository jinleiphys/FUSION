# Properties of pasta phases in catalyzed neutron stars

> Exotic non-spherical configurations of nuclei, known as ``pasta" phases, are expected to be present at the bottom of the inner crust of a neutron star. We study the properties of these configurations in catalyzed neutron stars within a compressible liquid-drop model approach, with surface parameters optimized to reproduce experimental nuclear masses. Our results show that the properties of the pasta phases exhibit strong model dependence. To estimate the model uncertainties, a Bayesian analysis is performed, combining information from nuclear physics experiments and chiral perturbation theoretical calculations with astrophysical observations. The inferred posterior distributions are discussed, with particular focus on the effect of the low-density energy functional on the predictions.

## Key claim

The properties of pasta phases in catalyzed neutron stars exhibit strong model dependence, and constraining the nuclear energy functional at very low densities (down to 0.02 fm⁻³) is crucial for reducing uncertainties in pasta-phase observables, whereas applying constraints only above 0.1 fm⁻³ allows for posterior distributions compatible with a small or even null pasta layer.

## Method

The authors model pasta phases in cold catalyzed neutron star matter using a compressible liquid-drop model (CLDM) with a meta-modeling approach for the bulk nuclear energy density, expanded to fourth order around saturation density, and surface parameters optimized by fitting to experimental nuclear masses from the Atomic Mass Evaluation 2016. For each baryon density, the equilibrium geometry (spheres, rods, slabs, tubes, or bubbles) and composition are determined by minimizing the thermodynamic potential of a Wigner-Seitz cell with respect to five variational parameters. A Bayesian analysis is performed starting from flat priors on 18 model parameters, applying low-density filters from chiral effective field theory calculations and high-density filters from causality, stability, and the requirement that the maximum neutron star mass exceeds 1.97 solar masses.

## Key numbers

- The relative thickness and mass of the pasta layer compared to the whole crust were previously estimated at 12.8% and 48.5%, respectively (from the authors' prior work, table 5 in HoaAA21).
- When applying the low-density filter in the range [0.1, 0.2] fm⁻³, 2×10⁶ prior models yielded 7714 models in the final posterior.
- When applying the low-density filter in the range [0.02, 0.2] fm⁻³, 10⁸ prior models yielded 7008 models in the final posterior.
- The variation of fractional pasta thickness with neutron star mass is approximately 20% when changing the mass from 0.4 M⊙ to 2 M⊙.
- The variation of fractional pasta mass with neutron star mass is approximately 10% when changing the mass from 0.4 M⊙ to 2 M⊙.
- The high-density filter requires Mmax ≥ 1.97 M⊙, referencing the observed massive neutron star from Antoniadis (2013).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CLDM studies (HoaAA21, HoaEPJA21) by providing additional analysis of model dependence and the effect of low-density constraints on pasta-phase predictions. The results confirm the robustness of pasta-phase existence predicted by earlier work including Ravenhall (1983), Hashimoto (1984), and Oya (1984), while showing that quantitative properties depend strongly on the chosen energy functional (BSk24, DD-MEδ, and TM1 are compared). The Bayesian analysis demonstrates that chiral EFT constraints from Drischler (2016) at densities down to 0.02 fm⁻³ are essential for reducing uncertainties, enabling more reliable predictions of pasta-layer thickness and mass for applications to neutron star cooling, transport properties, crust oscillations, and pulsar evolution.
