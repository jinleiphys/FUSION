# New Results in the Analysis of the $^{16}$O+$^{28}$Si Elastic Scattering by Modifying the Optical Potential

> The elastic scattering of the $^{16}$O+$^{28}$Si system has been analyzed with a modified potential within the framework of the optical model over a wide energy range in the laboratory system from 29.0 to 142.5 MeV. This system has been extensively studied over the years and a number of serious problems has remained unsolved: The explanation of the anomalous large angle scattering data; the out-of-phase problem between theoretical predictions and experimental data; the reproduction of the oscillatory structure near the Coulomb barrier; the consistent description of angular distributions together with the excitation functions data are just some of these problems. We propose the use of a modified potential method to explain these problems over this wide energy range. This new method consistently improves the agreement with the experimental data and achieves a major improvement on all the previous Optical model calculations for this system.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a modified optical potential, using two small derivative Woods-Saxon terms added to the real nuclear potential, can consistently describe the elastic scattering of the 16O+28Si system from 29.0 to 142.5 MeV, resolving long-standing problems such as anomalous large-angle scattering and the out-of-phase issue. However, the method only achieves this agreement for angular distributions across the full energy range; the excitation function at 180 degrees is only well-reproduced up to about 50 MeV, and the two small potentials are not needed above 50 MeV.

## Method

The author used the optical model with a real nuclear potential of the square Woods-Saxon form, fixed at V0=761.5 MeV, r0=0.75 fm, and a=1.425 fm. Two small additional real potentials, each a derivative of a Woods-Saxon shape, were added to modify the surface region between approximately 5.5 and 9.0 fm. The imaginary potential consisted of a Woods-Saxon volume term and a surface derivative term, with radii increased linearly with center-of-mass energy up to 54.0 MeV and then held constant; calculations were performed using a modified version of the code CHUCK.

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers a laboratory energy range from 29.0 to 142.5 MeV for the 16O+28Si elastic scattering system.
- The fixed real nuclear potential parameters are V0=761.5 MeV, r0=0.75 fm, and a=1.425 fm.
- The Coulomb radius is fixed at Rc=7.78 fm.
- The imaginary volume potential has WV=59.9 MeV and aV=0.127 fm; the imaginary surface potential has WS=50.0 MeV and aS=0.250 fm.
- The imaginary volume radius increases as RV = 0.06084*ECM - 0.544 (fm) up to 54.0 MeV.
- The imaginary surface radius increases as RS = 0.2406*ECM - 2.191 (fm) up to 54.0 MeV.
- The two small additional potentials modify the nuclear potential between approximately 5.5 fm and 9.0 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous coupled-channels analyses by the same author (Boztosun, 1999) for systems such as 12C+12C, 16O+28Si, 16O+24Mg, and 12C+24Mg, which showed extreme sensitivity to the surface shape of the nuclear potential. It directly addresses the failure of standard optical model calculations (Mackintosh, Kobos, and Satchler, 1980; Kobos, 1983, 1984) to explain anomalous large-angle scattering, the out-of-phase problem, and oscillatory structure near the Coulomb barrier for this system. The method enables a global optical model description of the 16O+28Si elastic scattering data over a wide energy range, though further work to derive the surface modification from a microscopic viewpoint is noted as ongoing.
