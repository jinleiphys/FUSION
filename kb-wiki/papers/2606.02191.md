# Hypernucleus production in p+Au reactions at the FAIR facility

> We explore the production of hypernuclei in p+Au reactions using the UrQMD model accompanied by a standard phase space coalescence model. We focus on the proton beam energy range of $E_{\rm lab}= 5 - 30$ GeV as this energy range will be investigated by the CBM-experiment at the upcoming FAIR facility. Starting from proton, $Λ$, $Σ$, $Ξ$ and $Ω$ production, we predict the yields, rapidity and transverse momentum distributions of $^{3}_ΛH$, $^{4}_ΛH$, $Ξ$N and $Ξ$NN hypernuclei. We conclude that the production rates of novel multi-strange hypernuclei are well within the reach of the CBM-experiment.

## Key claim
The production rates of multi-strange hypernuclei (such as ΞN and ΞNN) in p+Au collisions at FAIR energies are predicted to be within the reach of the CBM experiment, provided that the target rapidity region provides sufficient phase-space overlap between hyperons and target nucleons. The abstract's claim of "well within the reach" is conditioned on the specific beam energy range of 5–30 GeV and the use of a coalescence model that assumes hypernuclei form after kinetic freeze-out.

## Method
The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model, version 4.0, to simulate minimum-bias p+Au collisions at beam energies from 5 to 30 GeV. A standard phase-space coalescence mechanism was applied after the kinetic freeze-out of baryons to form hypernuclei, with coalescence parameters fixed from previous studies and independent of energy or collision system. The model was validated by comparing proton and deuteron rapidity distributions to E802 data at 14.6 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Hyperon multiplicity: events with up to 6 Λ or Σ hyperons per event are possible in the energy range 5–30 GeV; up to 3 Ξ hyperons per event are expected.
- Ω hyperon production is only observed at the highest beam energy (30 GeV), and double-Ω production is "significantly suppressed" and deemed unfeasible.
- The predicted multiplicities for hypernuclei in p+Au reactions range from above 10⁻⁵ for the most exotic states (ΞNN) to 10⁻³ for known states like the hypertriton (³ΛH).
- The transverse momentum distributions of hypernuclei peak around p_T ≈ 500 MeV within |y_lab| < 0.5.

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of hypernucleus production in nucleus-nucleus collisions (Botvina 2019, Reichert 2022) and pion-induced reactions at GSI (Kittiratpattana 2023) to proton-induced reactions at FAIR energies. The study is motivated by the upcoming CBM experiment at FAIR, which will begin operation in 2028, and complements programs such as QCD@FAIR, R3B, and CBM. The results enable predictions for the discovery potential of multi-strange hypernuclei, including ΞN and ΞNN states, which could provide constraints on hyperon-hyperon interactions relevant for neutron star equations of state.
