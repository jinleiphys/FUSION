# Nucleon observables and axial charges of other baryons using twisted mass fermions

> We present results on the nucleon scalar, axial and tensor charges, as well as, on the first moments of the unpolarized, polarized and transversity parton distributions using $N_f=2$ and $N_f=2+1+1$ twisted mass fermions. These include an ensemble that yields the physical value of the ratio of the nucleon to the pion mass. Results on the axial charges of hyperons and charmed baryons are also presented for a range of pion masses including the physical one.

## Key claim

The authors compute nucleon scalar, axial, and tensor charges, as well as first moments of parton distributions, using twisted mass fermion lattice QCD with ensembles that include one at the physical pion mass; however, results at the physical point are described as preliminary, requiring larger statistics for final values, and the scalar charge in particular needs sink-source separations larger than 1.5 fm to ensure ground state dominance.

## Method

The nucleon matrix elements at zero momentum transfer are extracted from ratios of three-point to two-point correlation functions computed on \(N_f=2\) and \(N_f=2+1+1\) twisted mass fermion ensembles with lattice spacings below 0.1 fm. Ground state dominance is checked using plateau fits, the summation method, and two-state fits including the first excited state; disconnected contributions are evaluated for the isoscalar charges using approximately 150,000 measurements on GPUs. The same techniques are applied to compute axial charges of hyperons and charmed baryons using the fixed current method with a single sequential inversion per quark flavor.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon isovector axial charge \(g_A\) at the physical pion mass agrees with the experimental value, indicating small lattice artifacts.
- Tensor charge \(g_T\) shows similar behavior to \(g_A\); the value at the physical point is taken as a prediction for the nucleon tensor charge.
- Scalar charge \(g_s\) requires sink-source separation \(\sim 1.5\) fm for ground state dominance; results at \(t_s-t_0 \sim 1.1\) fm are preliminary.
- Disconnected contributions to the isoscalar axial charge \(g_A^{u+d}\) are of order 10%; disconnected contributions to \(g_T^{u+d}\) are consistent with zero.
- Disconnected contributions to \(g_s^{u+d}\) from light quark loops exceed 10%; strange quark loop contributions are smaller by a factor of about three.
- Mass of \(\Xi_{cc}^*\): 3.678(8) GeV; \(\Omega^+_{cc}\): 3.708(10) GeV; \(\Omega^{*+}_{cc}\): 3.767(11) GeV; \(\Omega^{++}_{ccc}\): 4.746(3) GeV, assuming negligible cutoff effects.
- SU(3) breaking parameter \(\delta_{\rm SU(3)} = g_A^N - g_A^\Sigma + g_A^\Xi\) reaches about 0.15 at the physical point.
- B55 ensemble: \(N_f=2+1+1\) TMF on \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice, \(a = 0.082\) fm, \(m_\pi = 373\) MeV.
- Physical ensemble: \(N_f=2\) TMF with clover term on \(48^3 \times 96\) lattice, \(a = 0.094(1)\) fm, \(m_\pi = 130\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of baryon masses by Alexandrou et al. (2014) and Kallidonis using twisted mass fermions to include nucleon charges and parton distribution moments at the physical pion mass. The results provide lattice input for beyond-Standard-Model searches in ultra-cold neutron decay (scalar and tensor couplings) and for phenomenological models of hyperon and charmed baryon axial charges, which are poorly measured or unknown. The computation of the tensor charge is timely for new Jefferson Lab experiments using polarized \(^3\)He/proton that aim to increase experimental accuracy by an order of magnitude.
