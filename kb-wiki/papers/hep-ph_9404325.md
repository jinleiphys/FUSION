# Where the excess photons and dileptons in SPS nuclear collisions come from?

> Recently the first single photon spectra from CERN energy heavy-ion collisions were reported by WA80, while NA34/3 and NA38 have obtained the spectra for dileptons with the mass up to 4-5 GeV. The production rates for photons and dileptons significantly increase when reactions involving the $A_1$ meson are included. However, with the conventional expansion scenario, the absolute yields are still significantly smaller than the observed ones. It may indicate that expansion in the ``mixed state" takes much more time.

## Key claim

The observed excess of photons and dileptons in CERN SPS heavy-ion collisions cannot be explained by conventional expansion models even when including reactions involving the A1 meson, which significantly increase production rates; the discrepancy may indicate that the expansion in the "mixed state" takes much more time (about 30-40 fm/c) than the conventional frozen motion model predicts (about 10 fm/c).

## Method

The authors calculated photon and dilepton production rates from the mixed phase of nuclear collisions using a "frozen motion model" (FMM) that assumes no transverse expansion and uses experimental pion rapidity distributions to determine the space-time volume of the mixed phase. They included hadronic reactions such as pi rho -> A1 -> pi gamma for photons and pi+pi-, K+K-, and pi A1 annihilation for dileptons, then compared these calculations to data from the WA80, NA34/3, and NA38 experiments at CERN SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- The mixed phase proper time in the conventional FMM is about 10 fm/c for matter at central rapidity (y_c ~ 2.6 for S-W collisions)
- The slow expansion model (SEM) proposes a mixed phase expansion time of about 30-40 fm/c instead of 10 fm/c
- For T_c = 180 MeV, the volume per pion at the end of the mixed phase is V_pi = 3.7 fm^3
- The "apparent temperature" (p_t slope) of the observed single photon spectrum is about 3/4 of that for pions
- The dilepton excess is observed in the invariant mass region M ~ 1-2 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of photon production in hot hadronic matter by Kapusta, Lichard, and Seibert (1991) and by Xiong, Shuryak, and Brown (1992), who found that the pi rho -> A1 -> pi gamma reaction increases the photon rate by a factor of 3. The authors also build on dilepton production calculations by Gale and Lichard and by Song, Ko, and Gale (1993) involving the A1 meson. The paper proposes a slow expansion scenario that could explain not only the photon and dilepton excesses but also other puzzles such as the low-p_t component in kaon spectra from E814 and the excess of Xi hyperons from E802. This work enables experimental tests comparing dilepton excess at central versus forward rapidities and searching for spectral features near the A1 mass threshold.
