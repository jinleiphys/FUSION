# Constraining three-nucleon forces with multimessenger data

> We report the results of a study aimed at inferring direct information on the repulsive three-nucleon potential $V^R_{ijk}$\textemdash driving the stiffness of the nuclear matter equation of state at supranuclear densities\textemdash from astrophysical observations. Using a Bayesian approach, we exploit the measurements of masses, radii and tidal deformabalities performed by the NICER satellite and the LIGO/Virgo collaboration, as well as the mass of the heaviest observed pulsar, to constrain the strength of $V^R_{ijk}$. The baseline of our analysis is the widely employed nuclear Hamiltonian comprising the Argonne $v_{18}$ nucleon-nucleon potential andthe Urbana IX model of three-nucleon potential. The numerical results, largely determined by the bound on the maximum mass, suggest that existing and future facilities have the potential to provide valuable new insight into microscopic nuclear dynamics at supranuclear densities.

## Key claim

The strength of the repulsive three-nucleon potential can be constrained by multimessenger astrophysical data, but the numerical results are largely determined by the bound on the maximum neutron star mass rather than by the tidal deformability or radius measurements alone.

## Method

The authors generated a family of nuclear matter equations of state by scaling the repulsive term of the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential by a factor α, while keeping the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential fixed. They then used a Bayesian Monte Carlo Markov Chain approach to sample the posterior probability distribution of α, comparing the predicted neutron star masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities against data from the NICER observation of PSR J0030+0451, the gravitational-wave event GW170817 from LIGO/Virgo, and the mass measurement of PSR J0740+6620.

## Key numbers

- The 90% confidence interval for α from GW170817 alone, including the maximum mass bound, gives α_GW = 1.25^{+0.48}_{-0.53}.
- The 90% confidence interval for α from NICER data alone, including the maximum mass bound, gives α_EM = 1.52^{+0.43}_{-0.47}.
- The 90% confidence interval for α from the combined multimessenger analysis, including the maximum mass bound, gives α_GW+EM = 1.32^{+0.48}_{-0.51}.
- The maximum mass prior for PSR J0740+6620 is modeled as a normal distribution with mean μ = 2.14 M_sun and standard deviation σ = 0.09 M_sun.
- The chirp mass of GW170817 is fixed to the median value M = 1.186 M_sun.
- The central energy density of the heavier neutron star in GW170817 ranges between 6.2 x 10^14 g/cm^3 and 1.2 x 10^15 g/cm^3 for 0.7 ≤ α ≤ 2.0.

## Context

This work extends the approach of constraining the nuclear equation of state from astrophysical data (e.g., Annala 2017, Margalit 2017, Radice 2017) to directly constrain the microscopic three-nucleon potential itself, rather than just the equation of state. The baseline Hamiltonian is the widely used combination of the Argonne v18 potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential, which yields the APR equation of state from Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998). The results enable future studies where improved gravitational-wave detectors like the Einstein Telescope and longer NICER observations can narrow the bounds on α and test more sophisticated models with density-dependent three-body forces.
