# Charge Pickup Reaction Cross Section for Neutron-Rich p-Shell Isotopes at 900A MeV

> We report charge pickup reaction cross sections for 24 \textit{p}-shell isotopes, including $^{8,9}$Li, $^{10-12}$Be, $^{10,13-15}$B, $^{12,14-19}$C and $^{14,15,17-22}$N, measured at relativistic energies (approximately 900$A$ MeV) on both hydrogen and carbon targets. For the first time, we reveal a universal rapid increase in the charge pickup cross sections of unstable projectiles with isospin asymmetry along several isotopic chains. The cross sections can be decoupled into distinct contributions from the mass number and isospin asymmetry of the projectile, highlighting the critical role of the latter, and can be formulated empirically.

# Charge Pickup Reaction Cross Section for Neutron-Rich p-Shell Isotopes at 900A MeV

## Key claim

The authors report that charge pickup cross sections for neutron-rich p-shell isotopes increase rapidly with isospin asymmetry along isotopic chains, and that this dependence can be decoupled from mass number dependence in a new empirical formula. However, the claimed "universal" trend is qualified by the fact that the empirical formula was derived and validated only within the relativistic energy region of 0.7-2.1 GeV/nucleon, and the exact functional form may change when more measurements of neutron-deficient and sd- or pf-shell nuclides are added.

## Method

Charge pickup reaction cross sections were measured for 24 p-shell isotopes using the fragment separator FRS at GSI, with projectiles produced by fragmentation of 1A GeV 22Ne and 40Ar ions on a beryllium target. The isotopes were identified event-by-event using magnetic rigidity, time-of-flight, and energy loss measurements, and cross sections were determined on both carbon (4.01 g/cm2) and polyethylene (3.375 g/cm2) targets at approximately 900A MeV, with hydrogen-target cross sections derived by subtraction.

## Key numbers

- Charge pickup cross section for 19C on carbon target: 8.2 ± 0.6 mb at 880A MeV
- Charge pickup cross section for 19C on hydrogen target: 3.8 ± 0.5 mb at 880A MeV
- Charge pickup cross section for 22N on carbon target: 8.5 ± 1.0 mb at 882A MeV
- Charge pickup cross section for 22N on hydrogen target: 6.7 ± 1.0 mb at 882A MeV
- Charge pickup cross section for 12C on carbon target: 0.0 ± 0.1 mb at 928A MeV (consistent with zero for stable N=Z nucleus)
- Overlap parameter c in gamma_PT = A_P^(1/3) + A_T^(1/3) - c: 1.42 ± 0.17
- Mass number exponent m in new empirical formula: 1.12 ± 0.03 (reduced from 2.0 in prior work)
- Isospin asymmetry exponent n in new empirical formula: 6 ± 2
- Scaling factor a: 0.0014 ± 0.0003 mb
- Isospin asymmetry tuning parameter b: 2.5 ± 1.4

## Context

This work extends prior measurements of charge pickup cross sections for stable nuclei by Olson (1983), Guoxiao (1989), and Summerer (1995), and for carbon isotopes by Tanihata (2016), to include Li, Be, B, and N isotopic chains for the first time. The new empirical formula, which explicitly includes isospin asymmetry I_P = (N-Z)/(N+Z), replaces the earlier A_P^2 formula of Guoxiao (1989) and provides a unified description for both stable and neutron-rich nuclei. This enables reliable estimation of production rates for exotic neutron-rich nuclides and may help constrain total transition strengths in charge-exchange reactions relevant to beta-decay quenching studies.
