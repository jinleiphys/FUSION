# Strangeness at high temperatures: from hadrons to quarks

> Appropriate combinations of up to fourth order cumulants of net strangeness fluctuations and their correlations with net baryon number and electric charge fluctuations, obtained from lattice QCD calculations, have been used to probe the strangeness carrying degrees of freedom at high temperatures. For temperatures up to the chiral crossover separate contributions of strange mesons and baryons can be well described by an uncorrelated gas of hadrons. Such a description breaks down in the chiral crossover region, suggesting that the deconfinement of strangeness takes place at the chiral crossover. On the other hand, the strangeness carrying degrees of freedom inside the quark gluon plasma can be described by a weakly interacting gas of quarks only for temperatures larger than twice the chiral crossover temperature. In the intermediate temperature window these observables show considerably richer structures, indicative of the strongly interacting nature of the quark gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The deconfinement of strangeness occurs at the chiral crossover temperature (T_c = 154(9) MeV), not at a higher temperature as previously suggested; however, the strangeness-carrying degrees of freedom in the quark-gluon plasma can only be described by a weakly interacting quark gas for temperatures larger than twice the chiral crossover temperature (T > 2T_c), with richer structure in the intermediate temperature window.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using (2+1) flavor QCD with O(a^2) improved gauge and Highly Improved Staggered Quark discretization schemes, with up and down quark masses corresponding to a Goldstone pion mass of 160 MeV and a physical strange quark mass. They computed up to fourth order cumulants of net strangeness fluctuations and their correlations with net baryon number and electric charge fluctuations on N_tau = 6 and 8 lattices using 3000-8000 gauge field configurations. From these susceptibilities they constructed observables that project onto partial pressures of strange mesons and baryons in an uncorrelated hadron gas, and compared results with the Hadron Resonance Gas model at low temperatures and with non-interacting massless quark gas and one-loop re-summed Hard Thermal Loop perturbation theory at high temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Chiral crossover temperature: T_c = 154(9) MeV
- Goldstone pion mass used in simulations: 160 MeV
- Temperature range for Hadron Resonance Gas model comparison: 130 MeV to 200 MeV, where Boltzmann approximation gives at most 3% corrections to full HRG model results
- Lattice temporal extents: N_tau = 6 and 8
- Second order correlations chi_11^{BS}/chi_2^S and chi_11^{QS}/chi_2^S differ from weakly interacting quark gas values at only a few percent level for T ~ 1.25 T_c
- Weakly interacting quark gas description valid only for T > 2 T_c

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of strangeness fluctuations (WHRG, hotQCDHRG) and baryon-strangeness correlations (Koch, Ejiri) to fourth order cumulants, and contradicts earlier suggestions (WB-Tc) that strange quark deconfinement may occur at a temperature larger than T_c. The results also address the open question of whether the QGP for moderately high temperatures is a strongly coupled medium lacking quasi-particle description (hic-rev) or contains colored bound states (Shuryak) or massive colored quasi-particles (quasipart1). The findings enable future studies of the strongly interacting nature of the QGP in the intermediate temperature window T_c < T < 2 T_c.
