# Low Energy Tests of Chiral Symmetry

> The present status of low energy test of chiral invariance via chiral perturbation theory is reviewed, both in the meson and baryon sectors, and future prospects are discussed.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory provides a successful framework for low-energy tests of QCD via chiral symmetry, but the convergence of the chiral expansion is slow in some baryonic channels (e.g., neutral pion photoproduction), and certain experimental discrepancies (e.g., the charged pion polarizability and radiative muon capture on hydrogen) remain unresolved.

## Method

The paper reviews the status of chiral perturbation theory (χpt) in both the meson and baryon sectors, comparing one-loop chiral predictions to experimental data for scattering lengths, decay constants, polarizabilities, and photoproduction multipoles. In the baryon sector, heavy baryon methods (equivalent to a Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation) are used to establish a consistent power counting scheme, with low-energy constants determined from resonance dominance or experimental input.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: \(F_\pi = 92.4\) MeV (used throughout as input).
- Weinberg ππ scattering length predictions: \(a_0^0 = 7m_\pi/(32\pi F_\pi^2)\), \(a_0^2 = -m_\pi/(16\pi F_\pi^2)\).
- Gasser-Leutwyler coefficients (example): \(L_1 = 0.65 \pm 0.28\), \(L_2 = 1.89 \pm 0.26\), \(L_3 = -3.06 \pm 0.02\) (from ππ scattering and \(K_{\ell4}\) decay).
- Charged pion polarizability: chiral prediction \(2.8 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\) vs. Serpukov value \(6.8 \pm 1.4 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\) and SLAC value \(2.2 \pm 1.1 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\).
- Goldberger-Treiman relation test: left side 1201 MeV vs. right side 1183 MeV (agreement to better than 2%).
- Muon capture ratio \(r_P\): prediction 7.0; experimental values \(7.4 \pm 2.0\) (³He), \(6.5 \pm 2.4\) (H), \(10 \pm 1\) (radiative H).
- Threshold \(E_{0+}\) for π⁺n photoproduction: theory 26.3 vs. experiment \(27.9 \pm 0.5\) and \(28.8 \pm 0.7\) (units \(10^{-3}/m_\pi\)).
- Threshold \(E_{0+}\) for π⁰p photoproduction: chiral prediction at \(O(p^4)\) is \(-1.2\) vs. experiment \(-1.31 \pm 0.08\) (units \(10^{-3}/m_\pi\)).
- P-wave parameter \(P_1/|\vec{q}|\) for π⁰p: theory 0.480 GeV\(^{-2}\) vs. experiment \(0.47 \pm 0.01\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
- Proton electric polarizability: chiral prediction \(10.5 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\) vs. experiment \(11.6 \pm 0.6 \pm 0.6 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\).
- Neutron electric polarizability: chiral prediction \(13.4 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\) vs. experiment \(12.6 \pm 1.5 \pm 2.0 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\).

## Context

This review by Holstein (1996) summarizes the state of chiral perturbation theory tests following the foundational work of Gasser and Leutwyler (1980s) on mesonic χpt and the heavy baryon program of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1990s). It highlights the resolution of the neutral pion photoproduction puzzle (where earlier Low Energy Theorems were shown incorrect by Bernard et al. in 1991) and identifies open questions including the charged pion polarizability discrepancy, the radiative muon capture anomaly, and the need to incorporate the Δ(1240) as an explicit degree of freedom. The paper enables future work at facilities such as DAΦNE, Fermilab, Mainz, and CEBAF to test χpt predictions at higher precision and lower momentum transfer.
