# Longitudinal dynamics for mesons on the light cone

> We survey a set of proposed light-front models for confinement in the valence quark sector of the mesons and portray similarities as well as differences. We present the spectroscopies for the light mesons that result from a selection of longitudinal confinement forms. We note that the Sturm-Liouville theory provides a unifying framework for many elements of this comparison.

## Key claim

The paper argues that incorporating longitudinal dynamics into light-front models for mesons is essential for correct state identification, chiral symmetry breaking (the GMOR relation), and proper endpoint behavior of wave functions, but notes that the true effective interaction between quark and antiquark may not be separable and that the separation-of-variables ansatz is only a first approximation valid for low-lying states.

## Method

The authors survey several proposed light-front longitudinal confinement models (the 't Hooft model, the Glazek-Trawiński harmonic oscillator, the Li-Maris-Zhao-Vary (LMZV) model, and the Shuryak-Zahed model) and compare their predictions for meson spectroscopy. They show that the Sturm-Liouville theory provides a unifying framework for constructing longitudinal confining potentials, with the LMZV model as a specific example of a Sturm-Liouville-type interaction.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass from IMA in the chiral limit: \(M_\pi^2 \approx 2m_q^2 (\ln \kappa^2/m_q^2-\gamma_\textsc{e})\) where \(\gamma_\textsc{e}\approx 0.577216\) is Euler's constant
- Ground-state mass in LMZV model near chiral limit: \(M^2_\textsc{lmzv} = \sigma (m_q+m_{\bar q}) + O(m_{q,\bar q}^2)\)
- Ground-state mass in 't Hooft model near chiral limit: \(M^2_\text{tH} = g\sqrt{\frac{\pi}{3}} (m_q+m_{\bar q}) + O(m_{q,\bar q}^2)\)
- Heavy quark limit confining strength: \(\sigma_\textsc{hq} \approx 0.24\,\text{GeV}\)
- Light sector confining strength: \(\sigma = \langle \bar q q\rangle /f_\pi^2 \approx 0.6\,\text{GeV}\)
- Predicted mass of \(a_0(980)\) in LMZV model: \(M_\textsc{lmzv} = 0.91\,\text{GeV}\)
- Predicted mass of \(a_0(980)\) in Ahmady et al. model: \(M_\textsc{amkms} = 1.39\,\text{GeV}\)
- LFH transverse confinement strength parameter: \(\kappa = 0.375\,\mathrm{GeV}\) (used for pion form factor calculations)
- Quark masses used for pion form factor: \(m_{\{u,d\}} = 50\,\mathrm{MeV}\) for LFH with IMA, \(m_{\{u,d\}} = 12\,\mathrm{MeV}\) for LMZV/BLFQ\(_0\)

## Context

This work extends the light-front holography (LFH) approach of Brodsky and de Téramond (2006) by addressing the need for longitudinal dynamics, which was absent in the original LFH with the invariant mass ansatz (IMA). It compares several proposed longitudinal confinement models including the 't Hooft model (Chabysheva and Hiller, 2012), the Glazek-Trawiński harmonic oscillator (2013-2014), the LMZV model (Li, Maris, Zhao, Vary, 2015), and the Shuryak-Zahed model (2021). The paper shows that the LMZV model reproduces the GMOR relation and resolves state identification issues in LFH, and that the Sturm-Liouville framework enables systematic construction of longitudinal confining potentials. This enables improved predictions for meson spectroscopy and hadronic observables such as form factors at large momentum transfer.
