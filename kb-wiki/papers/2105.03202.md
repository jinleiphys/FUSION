# Limits on active-sterile neutrino mixing parameters using heavy nuclei abundances

> The production of heavy-mass elements due to the rapid neutron-capture mechanism (r-process) is associated with astrophysical scenarios, such as supernovae and neutron-star mergers. In the r-process the capture of neutrons is followed by $β$-decays until nuclear stability is reached. A key element in the chain of nuclear weak-decays leading to the production of isotopes may be the change of the parameters controlling the neutrino sector, due to the mixing of active and sterile species. In this work we have addressed this question and calculated $β$-decay rates for the nuclei involved in the r-process chains as a function of the neutrino mixing parameters. These rates were then used in the calculation of the abundance of the heavy elements produced in core-collapse supernova and in neutron-star mergers, starting from different initial mass-fraction distributions. The analysis shows that the core-collapse supernova environment contributes with approximately $30\%$ of the total heavy nuclei abundance while the neutron-star merger contributes with about $70\%$ of it. Using available experimental data we have performed a statistical analysis to set limits on the active-sterile neutrino mixing angle and found a best-fit value $\sin^2 2θ_{14}=0.22$, a value comparable with those found in other studies reported in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper finds that including active-sterile neutrino mixing in r-process calculations yields a best-fit mixing angle of \(\sin^2 2\theta_{14}=0.22\) from comparison with observed heavy-element abundances, but this result is conditional on the assumption that only core-collapse supernovae and neutron-star mergers contribute to the r-process, with the latter dominating at about 70% of the total abundance.

## Method

The authors modified the r-java2.0 nuclear network code to include \(\beta\)-decay rates that depend on active-sterile neutrino mixing parameters (mixing angle \(\theta_{14}\) and mass-squared difference \(\Delta m^2_{14}\)) in a 2+1 flavor scheme. They solved the r-process network for two astrophysical environments: a neutrino-driven wind in a core-collapse supernova and a neutron-star merger, each with two different initial seed distributions (entropy-based \(s_{100}\) and nuclear statistical equilibrium). The calculated abundances were compared to observational data for 13 nuclei in the mass range \(120 < A < 200\) to perform a statistical analysis and extract constraints on the mixing angle.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit value for the active-sterile neutrino mixing angle: \(\sin^2 2\theta_{14} = 0.22^{+0.13}_{-0.15}\), obtained from a statistical analysis over 520 runs with fixed \(\Delta m^2_{14} = 1\ \mathrm{eV}^2\).
- Core-collapse supernova contributes approximately 30% of the total heavy nuclei abundance, while neutron-star merger contributes about 70%, extracted from a linear combination fit yielding \(\omega = 0.32 \pm 0.04\).
- For the neutron-star merger environment, the scaling factor \(F\) relating theoretical to observational abundances ranges from 3097 (for \(s_{100}\) initial condition) to 5709 (for NSE with \(Y_e=0.3\)).
- The neutron-star merger environment was modeled with initial temperature \(T_0 = 3 \times 10^9\ \mathrm{K}\), initial density \(\rho_0 = 10^{11}\ \mathrm{g/cm}^3\), and expansion velocity \(V_{\mathrm{exp}} = 1.9 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{km/s}\).
- The stability time adopted for decay to long-lived isotopes was \(t_{\mathrm{sta}} = 4.354 \times 10^{17}\ \mathrm{s}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of r-process nucleosynthesis in supernovae and neutron-star mergers (Cowan 2019, Kajino 2016, Wehmeyer 2015) by incorporating sterile neutrino mixing effects on \(\beta\)-decay rates, following anomalies reported by LSND (Athanassopoulos 1996), MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arevalo 2018), and reactor experiments (Mention 2011). The derived mixing angle \(\sin^2 2\theta_{14}=0.22\) is comparable to values from those experiments and from global fits (Conrad 2013, Giunti 2011), suggesting that r-process abundance patterns could serve as an indirect probe of sterile neutrinos. The results enable future studies that include additional astrophysical scenarios (e.g., accretion disks, collapsars) and improved nuclear network codes to refine constraints on the sterile neutrino sector.
