# Effects of phonon-phonon coupling on properties of pygmy resonance in $^{124-132}$Sn

> Starting from an effective Skyrme interaction we study effects of phonon-phonon coupling on the low-energy electric dipole response in $^{124-132}$Sn. The QRPA calculations are performed within a finite rank separable approximation. The inclusion of two-phonon configurations gives a considerable contribution to low-lying strength. Comparison with available experimental data shows a reasonable agreement for the low-energy $E1$ strength distribution.

## Key claim

The inclusion of phonon-phonon coupling (two-phonon configurations) in QRPA calculations, performed within a finite rank separable approximation using the SLy4 Skyrme interaction, gives a considerable contribution to the low-lying E1 strength in neutron-rich tin isotopes and yields reasonable agreement with available experimental data, although the agreement is notably worse for the pygmy dipole resonance in \(^{124}\)Sn.

## Method

The authors perform Hartree-Fock (HF) plus quasiparticle RPA (QRPA) calculations using the SLy4 Skyrme interaction in the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent zero-range pairing force in the particle-particle channel, with spherical symmetry assumed. The residual interaction is simplified using a finite rank separable approximation in Landau-Migdal form, and the coupling between one- and two-phonon components in the wave functions of excited states is taken into account by solving the corresponding secular equation. Two-phonon configurations are constructed from natural-parity phonons with multipolarities \(\lambda=1,2,3,4,5\), including all dipole excitations below 35 MeV and the 15 most collective phonons of higher multipolarity.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{124}\)Sn, the QRPA centroid energy of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) is 16.4 MeV; including phonon-phonon coupling (2PH) gives 16.3 MeV, while the experimental value is 15.3 MeV.
- For \(^{130}\)Sn, the GDR centroid energy is 15.8 MeV (QRPA) and 15.7 MeV (2PH), with an experimental value of 15.9(5) MeV; the GDR width is 4.8 MeV (2PH), compared to the experimental 4.8(1.7) MeV.
- For \(^{132}\)Sn, the GDR centroid energy is 15.5 MeV (QRPA) and 15.4 MeV (2PH), with an experimental value of 16.1(7) MeV; the GDR width is 5.0 MeV (2PH), compared to the experimental 4.7(2.1) MeV.
- For the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in \(^{124}\)Sn below 10 MeV, the QRPA mean energy is 9.7 MeV and the 2PH mean energy is 9.1 MeV, while the experimental value is 6.97 MeV; the summed \(B(E1)\) is 0.86 e\(^2\)fm\(^2\) (QRPA) and 0.59 e\(^2\)fm\(^2\) (2PH), compared to the experimental 0.379(45) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\).
- For \(^{130}\)Sn, the PDR centroid energy below 11 MeV is 10.0 MeV (both QRPA and 2PH), with an experimental value of 10.1(7) MeV; the summed \(B(E1)\) is 1.40 e\(^2\)fm\(^2\) (QRPA) and 1.80 e\(^2\)fm\(^2\) (2PH), compared to the experimental 2.4(7) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\).
- For \(^{132}\)Sn, the PDR centroid energy below 11 MeV is 9.9 MeV (both QRPA and 2PH), with an experimental value of 9.8(7) MeV; the summed \(B(E1)\) is 1.27 e\(^2\)fm\(^2\) (QRPA) and 1.42 e\(^2\)fm\(^2\) (2PH), compared to the experimental 1.3(8) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous QRPA studies of the pygmy dipole resonance in tin isotopes using Skyrme interactions (e.g., Tselyaev et al. (2007) and Auerbach et al. (2011)) by explicitly including phonon-phonon coupling beyond the standard QRPA. The results are compared with experimental data from Adrich et al. (2005), Klimkiewicz et al. (2007), and Ozel et al. (2007), and with other theoretical approaches such as the quasiparticle time blocking approximation (QTBA) of Auerbach et al. (2011), the relativistic QRPA (RQRPA) and relativistic QTBA (RQTBA) of Litvinova et al. (2008, 2009), and the relativistic RPA (RRPA) of Piekarewicz (2006). The work enables predictions for the GDR and PDR properties in \(^{126}\)Sn and \(^{128}\)Sn, where experimental data are not yet available.
