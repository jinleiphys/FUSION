# Global Conservation Laws and Femtoscopy of Small Systems

> It is important to understand, in detail, two-pion correlations measured in p+p and d+A collisions. In particular, one wishes to understand the femtoscopic correlations, in order to compare to similar measurements in heavy ion collisions. However, in the low-multiplicity final states of these systems, global conservation laws generate significant N-body correlations which project onto the two-pion space in non-trivial ways and complicate the femtoscopic analysis. We discuss a formalism to calculate and account for these correlations in collisions dominated by a single particle species (e.g. pions). We also discuss effects on two-particle correlations between non-identical particles, the understanding of which may be important in the study of femtoscopic space-time asymmetries.

## Key claim

Global conservation laws (energy and momentum) generate significant N-body correlations that project onto two-pion correlation functions in low-multiplicity p+p and d+A collisions, complicating femtoscopic analysis; the authors develop a formalism to calculate and account for these correlations, noting that the effects are present at all values of relative momentum and cannot be ignored in femtoscopic studies.

## Method

The authors use the GENBOD event generator to produce events with random momenta subject only to energy and momentum conservation, with event weights proportional to the phase-space integral. They analyze three-dimensional two-particle correlation functions using a spherical harmonic decomposition (SHD) in the Bertsch-Pratt (out-side-long) coordinate system, extracting harmonic moments A_{l,m}(Q) to characterize the correlation structure. They also develop an analytic formalism based on the multivariate central limit theorem to calculate energy and momentum conservation-induced correlations (EMCICs) from measured single-particle distributions.

## Key numbers

- For multiplicity-9 pion-only events with average kinetic energy per particle K̅=0.9 GeV, the correlation function shows significant non-unity values at all Q, with A_{2,0} and A_{2,2} moments deviating from zero
- For multiplicity-18 events with K̅=0.9 GeV, EMCIC strength decreases compared to multiplicity-9 events
- For multiplicity-6 events with K̅=0.9 GeV, EMCIC strength increases compared to higher multiplicities
- For multiplicity-18 events with K̅=0.5 GeV, EMCIC strength is larger than for K̅=0.9 GeV at the same multiplicity
- The ratio |A_{l+2,m}/A_{l,m}| is typically ~0.1, indicating only a few harmonic components are needed to characterize the 3D correlation function
- In a high-probability multiplicity-30 event versus a low-probability event, the probability difference is a factor of approximately one hundred million

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Bertsch et al. (1993) who included energy-momentum conservation in an analytically-solvable model in one spatial dimension with non-relativistic particles, and by Utyuzh et al. (2007) who estimated EMCIC effects using a numerical model of Bose-Einstein correlations from emitting cells. The formalism enables experimentalists to disentangle EMCICs from femtoscopic correlations in small systems like p+p and d+A collisions, which is crucial for comparing femtoscopic measurements to those in heavy ion collisions where bulk collective flow is observed. The spherical harmonic decomposition approach provides a more complete characterization of correlation functions than traditional one-dimensional Cartesian projections.
