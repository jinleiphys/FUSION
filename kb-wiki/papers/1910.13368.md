# Viscosity, non-conformal equation of state and sound velocity in Landau hydrodynamics

> We find an analytical solution to relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for a 1+1 dimensional Landau flow profile. We consider relativistic Navier-Stokes form of the dissipative hydrodynamic equation, for a non-conformal system with a constant speed of sound, and employ the obtained solution to fit rapidity spectrum of observed pions in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 200, 17.3, 12.3, 8.76, 7.62, 6.27, 4.29, 3.83, 3.28 and 2.63 GeV collision energies. We find that at the freeze-out hypersurface with improved Landau's freeze-out prescription, the viscous corrections do not affect the rapidity spectra. We demonstrate that the solution of the non-conformal Landau flow lead to a better agreement with the experimental data compared to the conformal ideal solution. We also extract speed of sound from fit to the rapidity spectra for various collision energies and find a monotonous decrease with decreasing collision energies. Appealing to the fact that viscosity has negligible effect on rapidity spectra for Landau's freeze-out scenario, we argue that our calculations provides a framework for extracting the average value of speed of sound in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors find an analytical solution to relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for a 1+1 dimensional Landau flow profile, but they demonstrate that for Landau's freeze-out prescription (with Wong's modification), viscous corrections do not affect the rapidity spectra of produced particles, meaning the extracted speed of sound is effectively independent of viscosity.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Navier-Stokes equations for a non-conformal system with a constant speed of sound, assuming a Landau flow profile where the fluid rapidity is expressed in terms of space-time rapidity. They use the method of characteristics to obtain an analytical solution for the energy density evolution, then apply Wong's modified freeze-out prescription to derive an expression for the rapidity distribution of pions, which they fit to experimental data across ten collision energies.

## Key numbers

- Fitted pion rapidity spectra at ten collision energies: $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 200, 17.3, 12.3, 8.76, 7.62, 6.27, 4.29, 3.83, 3.28, and 2.63 GeV.
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 200 GeV, the fitted squared speed of sound $c_s^2$ is slightly larger than 1/3.
- The freeze-out proper time is given by $\tau_{\rm FO} = \frac{1}{\gamma}\sqrt{\frac{1+c_s^2}{c_s^2}}$, where $\gamma$ is the Lorentz factor of the colliding nuclei.
- The variance of the Gaussian approximation to the rapidity distribution is $\sigma^2 = \frac{2\,c_s^2\,y_b'}{1-c_s^2}$, where $y_b' = \frac{1}{2}\ln[c_+/(4c_s^2)] + y_b$ and $y_b = \ln(\sqrt{s_{NN}}/m_p)$.

## Context

This work extends the classic Landau hydrodynamics (Landau, 1953) by including viscous corrections and a non-conformal equation of state, building on Wong's modified freeze-out prescription (Wong, 2008). It contradicts earlier calculations by Gazdzicki (2010) that predicted a minimum in the $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ dependence of $c_s^2$, finding instead a monotonic decrease with decreasing collision energy. The analytical solution provides a framework for extracting the average speed of sound in the QCD medium formed in heavy-ion collisions, which can serve as a benchmark for calibrating numerical hydrodynamic codes.
