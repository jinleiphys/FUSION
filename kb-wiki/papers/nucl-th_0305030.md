# The Quark-Gluon Plasma in Equilibrium

> Our current knowledge of the quark-gluon plasma in thermodynamical equilibrium is reviewed. The phase diagram of strongly interacting matter is discussed, with emphasis on the quark-hadron phase transition and the color-superconducting phases of quark matter. Lattice QCD results on the order of the phase transition, the thermodynamical functions, the heavy quark free energy, mesonic spectral functions, and recent results for nonzero quark chemical potential are presented. Analytic attempts to compute the thermodynamical properties of strongly interacting matter, such as perturbation theory, quasiparticle models, ``hard-thermal-loop''(HTL)-resummed perturbation theory, the Polyakov-loop model, as well as linear sigma models are discussed. Finally, color-superconducting quark matter is considered in the limit of weak coupling. The gap equation and the excitation spectrum are derived. The solution of the gap equation, gap parameters in various color-superconducting phases, and critical temperatures for the transition to normal-conducting quark matter are presented. A summary of gluon and photon properties in color superconductors is given.

## Key claim

This review summarizes the equilibrium properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) as known in 2003, with the caveat that most lattice QCD results are restricted to zero quark chemical potential and that analytic approaches become unreliable at temperatures near 150 MeV where the strong coupling constant is of order unity.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of thermodynamic functions, the heavy quark free energy, mesonic spectral functions, and the order of the QCD phase transition, alongside analytic approaches including perturbative QCD, hard-thermal-loop resummed perturbation theory, quasiparticle models, the Polyakov-loop model, and linear sigma models. For color-superconducting quark matter, the gap equation is derived and solved in the weak-coupling limit to obtain gap parameters and critical temperatures.

## Key numbers

- The transition temperature for pure gauge theory (N_f=0) is T_c ≈ 270 MeV
- The ground state of nuclear matter is at (T, μ)_0 = (0, 308) MeV, with baryon density n_{B,0} ≈ 0.17 fm^{-3} and energy per baryon (E/N_B)_0 ≈ 924 MeV
- The critical endpoint of the quark-hadron transition is approximately at (T, μ) ≈ (160, 240) MeV
- The Fermi momentum in the ground state of nuclear matter is k_{F,0} ≈ 250 MeV
- The QCD scale parameter is Λ_QCD ∼ 200 MeV
- The critical temperature for the nuclear liquid-gas transition is of order ∼ 10 MeV
- Matter in neutron star cores has quark chemical potential μ around 400–500 MeV
- Nuclear collisions at E_Lab ∼ 1 AGeV explore (T, μ) ∼ (70, 250) MeV; RHIC collisions at √s = 200 AGeV explore (T, μ) ∼ (170, 10) MeV

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Gross, Pisarski, and Yaffe (1981) on hot and dense strongly interacting matter, and complements textbooks by Kapusta (1989) and Le Bellac (1996). It synthesizes lattice QCD results from the Schladming lectures and the review by Laermann and Philipsen (2003), analytic resummation techniques reviewed by Blaizot, Iancu, and Rebhan (2001), and color superconductivity reviews by Rajagopal and Wilczek (2001). The paper enables researchers to connect equilibrium QGP theory with experimental signatures in heavy-ion collisions and with the physics of compact stellar objects.
