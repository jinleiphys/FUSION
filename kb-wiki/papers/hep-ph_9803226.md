# Optimized Perturbation Theory at Finite Temperature

> An optimized perturbation theory (OPT) at finite temperature T, which resums higher order terms in the naive perturbation, is developed in O(N) phi^4 theory. It is proved that (i) the renormalization of the ultra-violet divergences can be carried out systematically in any given order of OPT, and (ii) the Nambu-Goldstone theorem is fulfilled for arbitrary N and for any given order of OPT. The method is applied for the O(4) sigma model to study the soft modes associated with the chiral transition in quantum chromodynamics. Threshold enhancement of the spectral functions at finite T in the scalar and pseudo-scalar channels is shown to be a typical signal of the chiral transition.

## Key claim

The paper develops an optimized perturbation theory (OPT) at finite temperature that systematically resums higher-order terms in naive perturbation theory, proving that renormalization of ultraviolet divergences can be carried out order by order and that the Nambu-Goldstone theorem holds for any given order and any N, provided the mass decomposition is performed in an O(N)-invariant way and the counter terms are also decomposed.

## Method

The authors start with a renormalized Lagrangian and introduce a new mass parameter m^2 by rewriting μ^2 = m^2 - χ, where χ is treated as O(δ) in a modified loop expansion. They prove that all necessary counter terms for the operator insertion (1/2)χφ^2 are already present in the original Lagrangian's counter terms, establishing order-by-order renormalization. The method is then applied to the O(4) linear sigma model as an effective theory for QCD chiral dynamics, where the optimal mass parameter m is determined by a fastest apparent convergence (FAC) condition on the two-point functions, and the spectral functions for scalar (σ) and pseudoscalar (π) channels are computed at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass is fixed to m_π = 140 MeV and the pion decay constant to f_π = 93 MeV at T=0.
- The peak position of the σ spectral function at T=0 is taken as m_σ^(peak) = 550 MeV, 750 MeV, or 1000 MeV.
- The half width of the σ peak at T=0 is 260 MeV, 657 MeV, and 995 MeV for m_σ^(peak) = 550 MeV, 750 MeV, and 1000 MeV respectively.
- The continuum threshold in the σ channel at T=0 is 2 m_0π = 280 MeV.
- The continuum threshold in the π channel at T=0 is m_0π + m_0σ.

## Context

This work extends the optimized perturbation theory (OPT) previously applied at finite temperature by Oko (1994) and by Braaten and Mohapatra (1995), providing a general proof of renormalizability and the Nambu-Goldstone theorem in O(N) φ^4 theory. The application to the O(4) sigma model builds on earlier studies of soft modes in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1985) and on real-time dynamics in the linear sigma model by Chiku and Hatsuda (1998). The method enables systematic study of mode couplings among soft modes near the chiral transition, which is relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC.
