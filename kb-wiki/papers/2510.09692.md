# Revisiting the soft-hard separation in the transverse momentum spectra of $pp$ collisions

> We study the separation of soft and hard components in the transverse momentum spectra of charged particles as measured by ALICE in proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}= 2.76 TeV, 5.02 TeV and 13 TeV at the LHC. The soft component is described by a Boltzmann fit, while the residual spectra are identified as hard QCD-like fragmentation. After separation, the subtracted spectra show no evolution in the shape or peak position with multiplicity, supporting a two-component interpretation. Mean transverse momenta for both contributions remain nearly constant, while Pythia 8 Monte Carlo simulations confirm these trends. Our results support the two-component description as a robust alternative to hydrodynamical interpretations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed increase of mean transverse momentum with multiplicity in proton-proton collisions is driven by a growing contribution from hard QCD processes rather than by changes in the soft sector, and that a two-component description (soft Boltzmann plus hard fragmentation) is sufficient without invoking collective flow.

## Method

The authors fit a Boltzmann function to the low-transverse-momentum region of charged-particle spectra from ALICE data at 2.76, 5.02, and 13 TeV, using the chi-squared per degree of freedom to determine the optimal upper fit boundary p0 for each multiplicity class. They subtract the fitted Boltzmann component from the total spectra to isolate the hard fragmentation contribution, then extract mean transverse momenta for both components separately and compare with Pythia 8 Monte Carlo simulations using the Monash tune.

## Key numbers

- ALICE data at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV, 5.02 TeV, and 13 TeV in proton-proton collisions were analyzed across multiplicity classes
- The lowest multiplicity bin had N_ch approximately 2.9, the highest had N_ch approximately 54.1 at 13 TeV
- The Boltzmann fit described the lowest multiplicity bin well up to p_T greater than approximately 1.1 GeV/c
- The optimal p0 value for the highest multiplicity class was 0.60 GeV/c, while for the lowest it was 1.00 GeV/c (from figure captions)
- Mean transverse momenta for both soft and hard components showed nearly flat dependence on multiplicity, in contrast to the total mean p_T which increased monotonically

## Context

This work extends the two-component approach advocated by Trainor (2018, 2022) and challenges hydrodynamical interpretations such as the Blast Wave model used by Florkowski (2016) and others. The results support the view that proton-proton collisions can be described without invoking collective flow, and the agreement with Pythia 8 simulations validates the fitting method. The paper provides evidence against the need for intermediate sources like radial flow or recombination in small collision systems.
