# Local equilibrium and Lambda polarization in high energy heavy ion collisions

> The polarization of the $Λ$ hyperon has become an important probe of the Quark-Gluon Plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Recently, it has been found that polarization receives a substantial contribution from a local equilibrium term proportional to the symmetric derivative of the four-temperature vector, the thermal shear tensor. We show that, at very high energies, this term can restore the agreement between the experimental measurements and the predictions of the hydrodynamic model, provided that the hadronization hypersurface is isothermal. We review the theoretical derivation of this new term, discuss numerical computations at RHIC and LHC energies, and compare them with the experimental data. We also present the effect of feed-down corrections.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including a new local equilibrium term proportional to the thermal shear tensor, combined with an isothermal decoupling hypersurface assumption, can restore agreement between hydrodynamic model predictions and experimental measurements of Lambda hyperon polarization as a function of momentum, resolving the so-called "polarization sign puzzle" that existed when only the thermal vorticity contribution was considered.

## Method

The authors derived the spin polarization vector using linear response theory applied to the local equilibrium density operator, expanding to first order in gradients of the four-temperature vector. They then argued that for very high energy collisions (center-of-mass energies above 200 GeV per nucleon pair), the appropriate density operator is the isothermal local equilibrium operator, which leads to a modified expression for the spin vector involving kinematic vorticity and kinematic shear instead of thermal vorticity and thermal shear. The hydrodynamic evolution was simulated using the 3+1D vHLLE code with initial conditions from the Monte Carlo Glauber model (GLISSANDO v.2.702), and feed-down corrections from Sigma* and Sigma0 decays were included using fractions from prior work.

## Key numbers

- Decoupling temperature of 160 MeV yields good agreement with experimental data at both collision energies
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (RHIC), simulation used 20-60% centrality, eta/s = 0.08, and zeta/s = 0
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 5020 GeV (LHC/ALICE), simulation used 30-50% centrality, eta/s = 0.12, and a non-vanishing bulk viscosity (zeta/s from parametrization III of a reference)
- Feed-down reduces the polarization signal by about 20% compared to primary Lambdas only
- Production fractions used: n(FO)_Lambda = 0.243, n(Sigma*)_Lambda = 0.359, n(Sigma0)_Lambda = 0.165

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Becattini (2013) on spin polarization from thermal vorticity, which successfully explained global polarization data from STAR (Nature, 2017) but failed to describe local polarization measurements (STAR, 2019). The new thermal shear contribution was recently discovered by multiple groups (shear theory, shear num, yin theory, yin num). The paper enables future research into the role of bulk viscosity in spin physics, as a non-zero bulk viscosity was found necessary to match LHC data at 5020 GeV.
