# Effective Field Theory and High-Precision Calculations of Nuclear Electroweak Processes

> High-precision calculations of electroweak processes in light nuclei are of great importance for multiple reasons. Gerry Brown together with Dan-Olof Riska published in 1972 a famous calculation on radiative capture of a thermal neutron on a proton, and their work was a precursor of subsequent great developments in high-precision calculations of electroweak processes in light nuclei. The application of effective field theory to nuclear systems is a prominent example of these developments. We present an overview of it with main emphasis placed on those topics in which we ourselves have been involved.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a "more effective" effective field theory (MEEFT or EFT*), which combines SNPA wave functions with EFT-based transition operators and uses tritium beta-decay to fix the unknown low-energy constant, can achieve high-precision predictions for electroweak processes in light nuclei, but this approach is presented as a pragmatic strategy rather than a fully ab initio EFT calculation.

## Method

The authors review the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA), which uses phenomenological potentials fitted to NN scattering data to generate exact few-nucleon wave functions, and contrast it with chiral effective field theory (chiPT) in the Weinberg scheme, where irreducible diagrams define an effective potential and transition operators organized by chiral order. They then describe the hybrid EFT method, which uses SNPA wave functions with EFT-derived transition operators, and its refinement into MEEFT, where the unknown low-energy constant d_R is fixed by fitting the precisely known tritium beta-decay rate, enabling parameter-free predictions for other electroweak processes.

## Key numbers

- The S-factor for pp-fusion at threshold, S_pp(0), is required by solar models to be known with better than ~1% precision
- The experimental value of (g_A/g_V)_exp = 1.2695 ± 0.0029 (PDG08)
- Lattice QCD calculation at m_pi = 0.33-0.67 GeV found g_A/g_V = 1.19, which is 7% smaller than the experimental value
- The axial dipole mass M_A from lattice QCD was found to be 37% larger than M_A^exp
- The deuteron binding wave number gamma = 0.2316 fm^{-1}
- The cutoff range for MEEFT is chosen as 500 MeV less than or similar to Lambda_NN less than or similar to 800 MeV
- Schiavilla et al. (1998) achieved estimated uncertainties of ~0.3% for S_pp in SNPA after fine-tuning

## Context

This review extends the line of work initiated by Riska and Brown (1972), who resolved the ~10% discrepancy in thermal neutron radiative capture on protons by including exchange currents derived by Chemtob and Rho (1971). The paper contrasts the SNPA approach, which has been highly successful for light nuclei as reviewed by Carlson and Schiavilla (1998), with the EFT framework developed by Weinberg (1990, 1991) and others. The MEEFT approach enables reliable predictions for processes important to solar physics (pp-fusion, Hep reaction) and neutrino detection (nu-d reactions at SNO), where experimental cross sections are often impossible to measure directly.
