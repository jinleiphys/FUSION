# Pion couplings with low-lying nucleon resonances

> We have calculated coupling constants of a neutral pion with the lowest two nucleon resonances. This includes both the diagonal as well as non-diagonal coupling constants involving a nucleon resonance and a nucleon. For this, we first calculate vacuum-to-pion correlation function of the interpolating fields of two nucleons and then take its matrix elements with respect to a nucleon spinor and/or a nucleon resonance spinor(s). Using different QCD sum rules obtained from different matrix elements we eliminate unwanted coupling constants and solve for the desired ones. This is a simple extension of the projected correlation function approach used in the literature where we use multiple of states involving those of a nucleon and its resonances. We have also checked the stability of our results with respect to variation of different QCD and phenomenological input parameters.

# Pion couplings with low-lying nucleon resonances

## Key claim

The coupling constants of a neutral pion with the nucleon and the two lowest nucleon resonances N(1440) and N(1535) are extracted using a projected correlation function approach within QCD sum rules, with the results showing reasonable stability only for the Ioffe current parameter value t = -1.0, while values near t = -0.2 and t = -2.5 produce unstable or unreasonably large coupling constants.

## Method

The vacuum-to-pion correlation function of two nucleon interpolating fields is computed using operator product expansion (OPE) on the light-cone, including contributions up to twist-4. Projected correlation functions are formed by taking matrix elements of this correlation function with respect to nucleon and nucleon resonance spinors, yielding five algebraic equations that are solved to eliminate unwanted coupling constants and extract the diagonal and non-diagonal pion-nucleon-resonance couplings. The Borel transform is applied to improve convergence, and the continuum contribution is modeled by the OPE expression above a threshold parameter s_pi.

## Key numbers

- The diagonal coupling constant g_2 for pi^0 N(1440) N(1440) is found in the range 4.2 to 7.9, with central values around 6.0 to 7.0 for t = -1.0 and quark condensate -0.0117 GeV^3.
- The diagonal coupling constant g_3 for pi^0 N(1535) N(1535) is found in the range 11.25 to 15.95, with central values around 13.1 to 14.5 for t = -1.0 and quark condensate -0.0117 GeV^3.
- The non-diagonal coupling constant g_4 for pi^0 N N(1440) is found in the range -23.2 to -7.5, with central values around -10.4 to -8.8 for t = -1.0 and quark condensate -0.0117 GeV^3.
- The non-diagonal coupling constant g_5 for pi^0 N N(1535) is assumed in the range -1.1 to -0.6 based on estimates from An (2011) and Olbrich (2018).
- The coupling strengths of the interpolating field to the states are lambda^2 = 7.256 x 10^{-4} GeV^6 for the nucleon, lambda_1^2 = 6.027 x 10^{-4} GeV^6 for N(1440), and lambda_2^2 = 1.369 x 10^{-4} GeV^6 for N(1535), for quark condensate -0.0117 GeV^3.
- The Borel mass range where phenomenological and OPE sides match is typically 2.6 to 3.25 GeV^2, with continuum threshold s_pi varied between 2.7 and 3.0 GeV^2.
- The quark condensate is varied between -0.0117 and -0.0145 GeV^3, causing changes of 32% in g_2, 18% in g_3, and 78% in g_4.

## Context

This work extends the projected correlation function approach of Kondo (2003) and Doi (2004) from the pi NN coupling to include the two lowest nucleon resonances N(1440) and N(1535), addressing limitations of earlier three-point function methods by Azizi (2010) and light-cone sum rule approaches by Aliev (2011) that required derivatives of correlation functions or suffered from contamination by higher resonances. The results enable improved construction of NN potentials and phenomenological analyses of meson photoproduction and electroproduction reactions, complementing experimental efforts such as those by Burkert (2000) and theoretical estimates from phenomenological Lagrangians by Olbrich (2016, 2018).
