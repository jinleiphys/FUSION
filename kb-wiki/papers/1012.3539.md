# How to determine experimentally the K+ nucleus potential and the K+ N rescattering cross section in a hadronic environment?

> Comparing K+ spectra at low transverse momenta for different symmetric collision systems at beam energies around 1 AGeV allows for a direct determination of both the strength of the K+ nucleus potential as well as of the K+N rescattering cross section in a hadronic environment. Other little known or unknown quantities which enter the K+ dynamics, like the production cross sections of K+ mesons or the hadronic equation of state, do not spoil this signal as they cancel by using ratios of spectra. This procedure is based on transport model calculations using the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model which describes the available data quantitatively.

## Key claim

By comparing ratios of K⁺ transverse momentum spectra at low transverse momenta from symmetric collision systems of different sizes at beam energies around 1 AGeV, one can separately determine both the K⁺ nucleus potential (from light-to-medium system ratios) and the in-medium K⁺N rescattering cross section (from medium-to-heavy system ratios), because other unknown quantities like production cross sections and the equation of state cancel in the ratios.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) transport model to simulate K⁺ production in symmetric C+C, Ar+Ar, and Au+Au collisions at 1.0–1.25 AGeV incident energy. They computed ratios of transverse momentum spectra near midrapidity for different system pairs (C+C/Ar+Ar and Au+Au/Ar+Ar) and systematically varied the K⁺N rescattering cross section (by factors 0.5 to 2 relative to free space) and the K⁺ nucleus potential strength (via the parameter α in the linear density relation for the in-medium mass). They verified that variations in K⁺ production cross sections and the nuclear equation of state do not alter the slope of the ratios.

## Key numbers

- The in-medium K⁺ mass at zero momentum follows ω(k=0,ρ) = m_K⁺(ρ=0) × (1 + α_K⁺ ρ/ρ₀) with α_K⁺ = 0.08.
- The free-space K⁺p cross section is about 12 mb, with similar magnitude for K⁺n including charge-exchange.
- The maximum central density in symmetric systems at 1.25 AGeV rises strongly up to A≈40 and then only slightly for higher masses; the mean number of rescattering collisions per kaon rises moderately up to A=40 and then increases much stronger for heavier systems.
- The ratio of spectra for C+C and Ar+Ar is almost independent of the rescattering cross section (varied by factor 0.5–2) but depends strongly on the K⁺ nucleus potential strength; the opposite holds for Au+Au and Ar+Ar.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical efforts to determine meson properties in dense matter, which have been complicated by coupled-channels calculations and poorly known in-medium coupling constants (Eichstaedt 2007, Riek 2010, Korpa 2004, Tolos 2006). Previous attempts using πA reactions to extract the K⁺ nucleus potential (Benabderrahmane 2008) suffered from uncertainties in baryon-baryon → K⁺YN cross sections that do not cancel in ratios. The proposed method enables the first direct experimental determination of both the K⁺ in-medium mass and the K⁺N rescattering cross section in a hadronic environment, resolving a long-standing ambiguity where different combinations of potential strength and production cross sections could produce similar observables.
