# Importance of volume corrections on the net-charge distributions at the RHIC BES energies

> The paper presents my recent investigations of volume corrections on the cumulant products of net-charge distributions in statistical model, corresponding to the data reported by the STAR collaboration. The corrected statistical expectations, under simple Poisson approximations, can reasonably explain the data measured in experiment. The results indicate that volume corrections play crucial role in event-by-event multiplicity fluctuation studies.

# Importance of volume corrections on the net-charge distributions at the RHIC BES energies

## Key claim
Volume corrections from event-by-event fluctuations of the collision volume can explain the measured cumulant products Sσ and κσ² of net-charge distributions at RHIC BES energies, but only under the condition that the reference particle multiplicity and the net-charge fluctuation measures are treated as independent sub-events with Poisson and Skellam distributions in a fixed volume; the corrections fail to quantitatively reproduce the κσ² data, indicating additional correlations beyond volume effects.

## Method
The author derived a general formalism in a statistical model where the multiplicity distribution of reference particles (sub-event A, pseudorapidity 1.0 > |η| > 0.5) follows a Poisson distribution in a fixed volume, and the net-charge distribution in the fluctuation-measure sub-event B (|η| < 0.5) follows a Skellam distribution. Volume fluctuations were incorporated by integrating over the volume distribution, and the first four cumulants of the conditional net-charge distribution were expressed in terms of reduced cumulants κ₁ through κ₄. The resulting cumulant products ω, Sσ, and κσ² were then compared to STAR collaboration data from Au+Au collisions at beam energies from √sNN = 7.7 to 200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The corrected Sσ values are approximately 4 times the Skellam expectations, with the analytic approximation yielding Sσ ≃ 4(1-α)/(1+α) where α = M-/M+ ≃ 1.
- The corrected κσ² approximates to κσ² ≃ 4, compared to the Skellam expectation of 1.
- The scale variance ω ≃ (1+α)/(1-α) is close to the Skellam expectation, indicating volume corrections on ω can be neglected.
- The analysis covers collision energies from √sNN = 7.7 GeV to √sNN = 200 GeV.
- Results in the 0-5% centrality bin are deemed questionable due to non-trivial volume distribution features at most-central collisions.

## Context
This work extends the statistical model formalism developed by Xu (2016) for multiplicity fluctuation measurements at RHIC, and directly addresses the STAR collaboration data reported by Adamczyk (2014). The volume-corrected results are compared to both Skellam baselines and negative binomial distribution (NBD) baselines from the same STAR publication. The findings indicate that while volume corrections explain much of the deviation from Skellam expectations for Sσ, the failure to quantitatively reproduce κσ² suggests the need to incorporate correlations between positive and negative charges, as noted by Adamczyk (2014), and/or correlations between fluctuation measures and reference particles. Future work requires more precise modeling of volume distributions in relativistic heavy ion collisions, as well as inclusion of finite volume effects, quantum effects, resonance decays, and experimental acceptance corrections, particularly for net-proton distributions.
