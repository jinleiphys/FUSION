# Observation of the isospin breaking decay $ÎĄ(10860)\toÎĄ(1S)f_0(980)\toÎĄ(1S)Î·Ď€^0$ with the Belle II detector

> Using the Belle data on the decay $ÎĄ(10860)\to ÎĄ(1S)f_0(980)\toÎĄ(1S)Ď€^+Ď€^-$, we estimate the fraction of the process $ÎĄ(10860)\toÎĄ(1S)f_0(980)\to ÎĄ(1S)Î·Ď€^0$, caused by the mixing of $a^0_0(980)$ and $f_0(980)$ resonances that breaks the isotopic invariance due to the $K^+$ and $K^0$ meson mass difference. ## Key claim

The decay \(\Upsilon(10860)\to\Upsilon(1S)f_0(980)\to\Upsilon(1S)\eta\pi^0\) is predicted to be observable at Belle II, driven by \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing from the \(K^+\) and \(K^0\) mass difference, but the estimate relies on the central value of the mixing intensity measured by BESIII and assumes the Belle II luminosity is 40 times higher than Belle's.

## Method

The authors used the Belle measurement of the branching fraction for \(\Upsilon(10860)\to\Upsilon(1S)f_0(980)\to\Upsilon(1S)\pi^+\pi^-\) as a starting point. They then applied the BESIII central value for the \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing intensity \(\xi_{fa}\) to estimate the corresponding rate for the isospin-breaking \(\eta\pi^0\) final state. The \(\eta\pi^0\) mass spectrum near the \(K\bar K\) thresholds was calculated using a coupled-channel formalism with \(K\bar K\) loop mixing amplitudes and coupling constants extracted from earlier BESIII data.

## Key numbers

- The fraction of \(\Upsilon(10860)\to\Upsilon(1S)f_0(980)\) in the \(\pi^+\pi^-\) channel is \(f_{\Upsilon(1S)f_0(980)} = (6.9 \pm 1.6^{+0.8}_{-2.8})\%\) from Belle data.
- The \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing intensity from BESIII is \(\xi_{fa} \approx 0.009\).
- The estimated isospin-breaking decay fraction is \(f_{\Upsilon(1S)a^0_0(980)} \approx 6.2 \times 10^{-4}\).
- In the Belle experiment, \(2090 \pm 115\) \(\Upsilon(10860)\to\Upsilon(1S)\pi^+\pi^-\) events were collected, implying only one or two \(\eta\pi^0\) events could have been produced.
- With an instantaneous luminosity of \(8\times10^{35}\) cm\(^{-2}\cdot\)s\(^{-1}\) at SuperKEKB, Belle II could collect about a hundred \(\Upsilon(10860)\to\Upsilon(1S)\eta\pi^0\) events (50 to 150) in roughly three months.

## Context

This work extends the Belle amplitude analysis of \(e^+e^-\to\Upsilon(nS)\pi^+\pi^-\) (Garmash et al., 2015) by predicting a new isospin-breaking decay mode of \(\Upsilon(10860)\). It relies on the \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing intensity measured by BESIII in \(J/\psi\to\phi f_0(980)\to\phi a^0_0(980)\to\phi\eta\pi^0\) (Ablikim et al., 2011). The observation of this decay, together with studies of \(D_s^+\to\eta\pi^0\pi^+\) and \(D^0\to K_S\eta\pi^0\), would enable a thorough investigation of light scalar meson structure and the mixing phenomenon.
