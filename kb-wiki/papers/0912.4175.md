# Shear viscosity in antikaon condensed matter

> We investigate the shear viscosity of neutron star matter in the presence of an antikaon condensate. The electron and muon number densities are reduced due to the appearance of a $K^-$ condensate in neutron star matter, whereas the proton number density increases. Consequently the shear viscosity due to scatterings of electrons and muons with themselves and protons is lowered compared to the case without the condensate. On the other hand, the contribution of proton-proton collisions to the proton shear viscosity through electromagnetic and strong interactions, becomes important and comparable to the neutron shear viscosity.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of neutron star matter is significantly altered by the presence of an antikaon (K⁻) condensate: lepton (electron and muon) shear viscosities drop sharply after condensation onset, while the proton shear viscosity, previously negligible, becomes comparable to the neutron shear viscosity at higher densities. This result depends on the specific choice of antikaon optical potential depth (U_K̄ = -160 MeV at normal nuclear matter density) and the use of the Maxwell construction for the first-order phase transition.

## Method

The authors solve coupled Boltzmann transport equations for neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons in neutron star matter undergoing a first-order phase transition to a K⁻ condensed phase, using a relativistic field theoretical model for the equation of state. Effective relaxation times are obtained from a 4×4 matrix equation involving collision frequencies for electromagnetic scattering (with longitudinal and transverse plasmon exchange) and strong nucleon-nucleon scattering (using Dirac-Brueckner in-vacuum cross sections from Machleidt). Shear viscosities for each species are then computed from the relaxation times and Fermi momenta.

## Key numbers

- The K⁻ condensate sets in at 2.43 times the normal nuclear matter density (n₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³).
- The antikaon optical potential depth used is U_K̄ = -160 MeV at normal nuclear matter density, corresponding to a kaon-scalar meson coupling constant g_σK = 2.9937.
- The neutron Fermi momentum ranges from 1.3 to 2.03 fm⁻¹ and the proton Fermi momentum from 0.35 to 1.73 fm⁻¹, corresponding to baryon densities from ~0.5 n₀ to ~3.0 n₀.
- The shear viscosity calculations are performed at temperatures T = 10⁷, 10⁸, and 10⁹ K.
- The quantity S_pp is almost one order of magnitude larger than S_nn at lower baryon densities.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of shear viscosity in neutron star matter by Flowers and Itoh (1976, 1979) and by Shternin and Yakovlev (2008), which considered only nucleons, electrons, and muons, to include the effects of antikaon condensation. It also builds on studies of K⁻ condensation in neutron stars by Brown (1990), Pal et al. (2000), and Banik et al. (2004). The results enable future investigations of r-mode instability damping, pulsar glitches, and bubble nucleation rates in first-order phase transitions in neutron stars with exotic matter.
