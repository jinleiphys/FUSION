# Variation of transport coefficients for average fission dynamics with temperature and shape

> We study slow collective motion at finite thermal excitations on the basis of linear response theory applied to the locally harmonic approximation. The transport coefficients for average motion, friction γ, inertia M and the local stiffness C are computed along a fission path of Th-224 within a quasi-static picture. The inverse relaxation time β=γ/M and the effective damping rate η=γ/(2\sqrt{M|C|}) are found to increase with temperature, but do not change much with the collective variable. The values found for ηand βas well as their behavior with temperature are in accord with experimental findings.

## Key claim

The authors use linear response theory within a locally harmonic approximation to compute transport coefficients (friction γ, inertia M, and stiffness C) for average fission dynamics of ²²⁴Th, finding that the inverse relaxation time β = γ/M and effective damping rate η = γ/(2√(M|C|)) increase with temperature but show little variation with the collective coordinate along the fission path, in accord with experimental findings.

## Method

The authors compute the intrinsic response function χ(ω) from a two-center shell model Hamiltonian with collisional damping parametrized by a frequency- and temperature-dependent single-particle width Γ(ω,T). From this response, they extract the collective response χ_coll(ω) = χ(ω)/(1 + kχ(ω)) and fit its low-frequency peak to a damped oscillator form to obtain the self-consistent transport coefficients M, γ, and C. The calculations are performed along a symmetric fission path for ²²⁴Th defined by minimal potential energy in the two-center shell model, with the collective variable r₁₂ measuring the relative distance between fragment centers.

## Key numbers

- The single-particle width at the chemical potential is given by Γ(μ,T) = (1/Γ₀)π²T² ≈ 0.3 T², with Γ₀⁻¹ = 0.03 MeV⁻¹ and a cutoff parameter c = 20 MeV
- The Coulomb and surface free energies use temperature coefficients α = 0.000763 MeV⁻² and β = 0.00553 MeV⁻², respectively
- The two-center shell model uses oscillator frequency ħω₀ = 41A⁻¹/³ with A = 224, radius parameter r₀ = 1.2 fm, and basis states up to (N₀ + 3/2)ħω₀ with N₀ = 20
- The neck parameter is fixed to ε = 0.4, and the liquid-drop parameters are a = 0.65 fm, a_s = 21.836 MeV, K_s = 3.48

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic computations of friction, inertia, and stiffness by Yamaji et al. (1995) and Hofmann et al. (1995) using linear response theory, and addresses the discrepancy between macroscopic models where friction either decreases as T⁻² (two-body viscosity) or stays constant (wall formula). The results support experimental findings by Paul and Thoennessen (1995) showing η increasing markedly with temperature, and enable improved modeling of fission dynamics at finite thermal excitations by providing temperature-dependent transport coefficients along the fission path.
