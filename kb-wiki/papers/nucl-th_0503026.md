# Particle yield fluctuations and chemical non-equilibrium at RHIC

> We study charge fluctuations within the statistical hadronization model. Considering both the particle yield ratios and the charge fluctuations we show that it is possible to differentiate between chemical equilibrium and non-equilibrium freeze-out conditions. As an example of the procedure we show quantitatively how the relative yield ratio $Λ/K^-$ together with the normalized net charge fluctuation $v(Q)=\ave{ΔQ^2}/\ave{\Nch}$ constrain the chemical conditions at freeze-out. We also discuss the influence of the limited detector acceptance on fluctuation measurements, and show how this can be accounted for within a quantitative analysis.

## Key claim

The paper shows that combining measurements of particle yield ratios (specifically Λ/K⁻) with normalized net charge fluctuations v(Q) can distinguish between chemical equilibrium and chemical non-equilibrium freeze-out conditions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, provided that detector acceptance effects on resonance decay correlations are properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors use the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) in the Grand-Canonical ensemble, computing particle yields and fluctuations from the partition function with fugacities modified by phase-space occupancy factors γ_q and γ_s to describe chemical non-equilibrium. They evaluate v(Q) = ⟨ΔQ²⟩/⟨N_ch⟩ and the Λ/K⁻ yield ratio (corrected for weak decays from Ξ and φ) as functions of temperature T and γ_q, using the SHARE program suite expanded to include fluctuations, and then construct a two-dimensional constraint plane. They also derive analytic expressions for how limited detector pseudo-rapidity acceptance modifies the correlation term from resonance decays, computing acceptance fractions via Monte-Carlo integration of Lorentz-transformed decay phase space.

## Key numbers

- v(Q) is computed for T = 140 MeV and T = 170 MeV as a function of γ_q, with solid lines including resonance decays and dot-dashed lines showing only direct pion fluctuations; the curves terminate when fluctuations begin to diverge as in Eq. (divergence)
- The equilibrium scenario corresponds to γ_q = 1 and T = 170 MeV; the non-equilibrium scenario corresponds to γ_q = 1.62 and T = 140 MeV, shown as highlighted ellipses in the v(Q) versus Λ/K⁻ plane
- The Grand-Canonical correction parameter ζ_GC = (1/2)(T⟨N⟩/μ)k_{Vtot} is derived; for RHIC, one unit of rapidity corresponds to V/(2V_tot) ≈ 7%, which is argued to be a safe limit for the Grand-Canonical ensemble
- The acceptance fraction R_F for resonance decay pairs is computed as a function of inverse slope parameter b and detector pseudo-rapidity acceptance Δη for values Δη = 6, 4, 2, 1, 0.5, 0.1 (top to bottom in descending order)

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization approach of Fermi (1950), Pomeranchuk (1951), and Landau (1953) by incorporating charge fluctuations as an additional observable to break parameter degeneracies found in yield-only fits, such as the correlation between T and γ_q noted by Becattini (2003). The method enables experimental determination of the light-quark phase-space occupancy γ_q, which is motivated by the rapid hadronization of an entropy-rich deconfined state as argued by Rafelski (2000). The paper also provides a framework for correcting fluctuation measurements for detector acceptance effects, building on the formalism of Anisovich (1985) for resonance decay kinematics.
