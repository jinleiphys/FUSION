# Color superconducting quark matter core in the third family of compact stars

> We investigate first order phase transitions from $β$-equilibrated hadronic matter to color flavor locked quark matter in compact star interior. The hadronic phase including hyperons and Bose-Einstein condensate of $K^-$ mesons is described by the relativistic field theoretical model with density dependent meson-baryon couplings. The early appearance of hyperons and/or Bose-Einstein condensate of $K^-$ mesons delays the onset of phase transition to higher density. In the presence of hyperons and/or $K^-$ condensate, the overall equations of state become softer resulting in smaller maximum masses than the cases without hyperons and $K^-$ condensate. We find that the maximum mass neutron stars may contain a mixed phase core of hyperons, $K^-$ condensate and color superconducting quark matter. Depending on the parameter space, we also observe that there is a stable branch of superdense stars called the third family branch beyond the neutron star branch. Compact stars in the third family branch may contain pure color superconducting core and have radii smaller than those of the neutron star branch. Our results are compared with the recent observations on RX J185635-3754 and the recently measured mass-radius relationship by X-ray Multi Mirror-Newton Observatory.

# Color superconducting quark matter core in the third family of compact stars

## Key claim
The presence of hyperons and/or Bose-Einstein condensate of K⁻ mesons in hadronic matter delays the onset of the phase transition to color-flavor-locked (CFL) quark matter to higher densities, and depending on the parameter space (bag constant, superconducting gap, strange quark mass, and antikaon optical potential), a stable third family branch of superdense stars may exist beyond the neutron star branch, with maximum neutron star masses ranging from 1.464 to 1.649 solar masses.

## Method
The hadronic phase (including baryon octet, antikaon condensate, electrons, and muons) was described using a Density Dependent Relativistic Hadron (DDRH) model with density-dependent meson-baryon couplings from Dirac-Brueckner calculations using the Groningen nucleon-nucleon potential. The CFL quark matter phase (including K⁰ condensate from Goldstone bosons) was described using a thermodynamic potential to order Δ² with a bag constant, and the mixed phase between hadronic and CFL matter was constructed using Gibbs phase rules with global baryon number conservation and local electric/color charge neutrality. Static compact star structures were computed by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations.

- For NHK̅Q matter with B¹/⁴ = 185 MeV, Δ = 57 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV, U_K̅(n₀) = -160 MeV: maximum mass 1.582 M_solar at central energy density 803.32 MeV/fm³
- For NQ matter with B¹/⁴ = 180 MeV, Δ = 100 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV: maximum mass 1.649 M_solar (radius 9.48 km)

## Context
This work extends prior studies of hadron-quark phase transitions in compact stars by including both hyperons and antikaon condensate alongside CFL quark matter, which had not been treated together previously. It builds on the DDRH model for hadronic matter from Banik and Bandyopadhyay (2000, 2001, 2002) and on the CFL quark matter formalism of Alford and Reddy (2002) and Rajagopal and Wilczek (2001). The results are compared with observations of RX J185635-3754 (Walter and Lattimer 2002; Drake et al. 2002) and the gravitational redshift measurement z = 0.35 from EXO0748-676 by Cottam et al. (2002). The existence of a third family branch of compact stars with pure CFL quark matter cores and smaller radii than neutron stars enables future observational tests of color superconducting quark matter in dense stellar interiors.
