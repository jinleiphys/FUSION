# The quark mass gap in a magnetic field

> A magnetic field and the resulting Landau degeneracy enhance the infrared contributions to the quark mass gap. The gap does not grow arbitrarily, however, for models of asymptotic free interactions. For B -> infinity, the magnetic field decouples from the dimensionally reduced self-consistent equations, so that the gap behaves as ~ Lambda_QCD (or less), instead of ~ |eB|^{1/2}. On the other hand, the number of participants to the chiral condensate keeps increasing as ~ |eB| so that |<\barψ ψ>| ~ |eB| Lambda_QCD. After the mass gap stops developing, nothing tempers the growth of screening effects as B -> infinity. These features are utilized to interpret the reduction of critical temperatures for the chiral and deconfinement at finite B, recently found on the lattice. The structures of mesons are analyzed and light mesons are identified. Applications for cold, dense quark matter are also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The quark mass gap in a strong magnetic field does not grow arbitrarily as \( \sim \sqrt{|eB|} \), but instead saturates at a value of order \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) (or less), provided the gap is generated by nonperturbative infrared-enhanced gluon exchanges rather than by contact interactions.

## Method

The authors analyze the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quark self-energy in the large-\(N_c\) limit, using a gluon propagator of the Gribov-Zwanziger type that provides a linear rising potential. They work in the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation for asymptotically large magnetic fields, where the quark propagator reduces to a (1+1)-dimensional form. The self-consistent equations are then solved analytically, and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson states is similarly reduced to the 't Hooft model in (1+1) dimensions.

## Key numbers

* The chiral condensate at \(T=0\) behaves as \(|\langle \bar{\psi} \psi \rangle| \sim |eB| \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) for \(|eB| \ge 0.3\ {\rm GeV}^2 \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2 \ (\simeq 0.04\ {\rm GeV}^2)\).
* The quark mass gap saturates at \(\sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) (or less) as \(B \to \infty\), instead of growing as \(\sim \sqrt{|eB|}\).
* The critical magnetic field for deconfinement at \(T=0\) is estimated as \(|eB|^{1/2} \sim N_c^{1/2} \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\), by equating the numbers of virtual quark and gluon excitations: \(N_c |eB| \Lambda_{\rm QCD} \sim N_c^2 \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^3\).

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic catalysis (Suganuma 1990, Gusynin 1994) by showing that the quark mass gap does not grow with \(B\) in asymptotically free theories, contrary to NJL model predictions where the gap can scale as \(|eB|^{1/2}\) (Shovkovy 2012) or as the cutoff (Fukushima 2012). The results explain the lattice finding (Bali 2011) that chiral and deconfinement critical temperatures decrease with \(B\), despite the chiral condensate increasing. The analysis enables future studies of gluon screening effects at large \(B\) and provides analogies with cold dense quark matter near the Fermi surface (Kojo 2009, 2011).
