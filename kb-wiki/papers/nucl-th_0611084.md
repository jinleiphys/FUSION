# Working Group Summary: Chiral Dynamics in Few-Nucleon Systems

> We summarize the findings of our Working Group, which discussed progress in the understanding of Chiral Dynamics in the A=2, 3, and 4 systems over the last three years. We also identify key unresolved theoretical and experimental questions in this field.

## Key claim

The working group found that chiral effective theory (χET) calculations of few-nucleon systems have made significant progress, but fundamental disagreements remain about the power counting for short-distance operators in the NN potential, specifically whether strict renormalization-group invariance requires additional contact terms beyond those in Weinberg's counting when cutoffs exceed the expected breakdown scale of the theory (~m_ρ).

## Method

The working group convened panel discussions and reviewed recent theoretical and experimental results on chiral dynamics in A=2, 3, and 4 systems. Topics covered included NN potentials from chiral perturbation theory, lattice QCD calculations of NN scattering lengths, pionless effective field theory for low-energy three- and four-nucleon systems, three-nucleon scattering experiments and calculations, electromagnetic reactions on light nuclei, and symmetry-breaking effects including parity violation and isospin violation.

## Key numbers

- NN scattering lengths at physical pion mass: a(³S₁) = 5.4112(15) fm and a(¹S₀) = -23.7148(33) fm
- NPLQCD lattice results at lowest pion mass (m_π ≈ 350 MeV): a(³S₁) = (0.63 ± 0.74 ± 0.2) fm and a(¹S₀) = (0.63 ± 0.50 ± 0.2) fm
- Charged πNN pseudoscalar coupling from Nijmegen PWA: g²/4π = 13.54 ± 0.05
- Doublet nd scattering length: a_nd^(1/2) = 0.65 ± 0.04 fm (anticipated factor of 10 improvement from new experiment)
- Strong proton-proton scattering length: a_pp = -17.3 ± 0.4 fm
- Estimated impact of NNNN force on α-particle binding: 200–400 keV effect
- Neutron polarizabilities can be extracted from γd data with precision of about 15%

## Context

This working group summary from the 2006 Chiral Dynamics workshop reviews progress since the first quantitative chiral NN potential by Ordóñez et al. (1996) and addresses consistency questions raised by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996). The work extends previous studies of NN potentials at N³LO by Epelbaum and Meißner (2006) and Nogga et al. (2005), and discusses lattice QCD results from the NPLQCD collaboration (Beane et al., 2006). The summary identifies key unresolved questions including the power counting controversy for short-distance operators, the need for N³LO three-nucleon force calculations, and the importance of new experimental facilities (MAX-Lab, HIγS) for extracting neutron polarizabilities and resolving discrepancies in ⁴He photodisintegration data.
