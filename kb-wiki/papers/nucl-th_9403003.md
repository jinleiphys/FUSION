# Nuclear Breathing Mode in the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory

> The breathing-mode giant monopole resonance is studied within the framework of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory. Using a broad range of parameter sets, a systematic analysis of constrained incompressibility and excitation energy of isoscalar monopole states in finite nuclei is performed. A comparison is made with the incompressibility derived from the semi-infinite nuclear matter and with constrained nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations. Investigating the dependence of the breathing-mode energy on the nuclear matter incompressibility, it is shown that dynamical properties of surface respond differently in the RMF theory than in the Skyrme approach.

# Nuclear Breathing Mode in the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory

## Key claim

The paper shows that within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory, the incompressibility of a finite nucleus does not depend on the nuclear matter incompressibility in a simple monotonic way, unlike in the nonrelativistic Skyrme approach. The surface incompressibility in the RMF theory responds differently under compression, with the ratio of surface to bulk incompressibility being approximately 1.6–1.7, compared to a ratio of about 1 in the Skyrme ansatz.

## Method

The authors performed constrained relativistic mean-field calculations for spherical nuclei, solving the Dirac equation with a Lagrange multiplier term to fix the root-mean-square radius. They extracted the constrained incompressibility from the curvature of the energy as a function of radius, and computed the collective inertial mass parameter for the breathing mode by applying a local Lorentz boost to the constrained spinors and solving the continuity equation. Calculations were carried out using five RMF parameter sets (NL1, NL-SH, NL2, HS, and L1) with nuclear matter incompressibilities ranging from 211.7 to 626.3 MeV, and results were compared with nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations using SkM and SIII forces.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility values for the RMF parameter sets: NL1 = 211.7 MeV, NL-SH = 354.95 MeV, NL2 = 399.2 MeV, HS = 545 MeV, L1 = 626.3 MeV.
- For NL1 (K_NM = 211.7 MeV): constrained incompressibility K_C ranges from 74.0 MeV (¹⁶O) to 117.1 MeV (⁹⁰Zr) to 116.0 MeV (²⁰⁸Pb); breathing-mode energy ω_C ranges from 18.0 MeV (¹⁶O) to 12.2 MeV (²⁰⁸Pb).
- For NL-SH (K_NM = 354.95 MeV): constrained incompressibility K_C ranges from 105.6 MeV (¹⁶O) to 197.7 MeV (²⁰⁸Pb); breathing-mode energy ω_C ranges from 19.0 MeV (¹⁶O) to 15.6 MeV (²⁰⁸Pb).
- Collective mass parameter B_rel for ²⁰⁸Pb is about 0.9 MeV⁻¹, for ⁹⁰Zr about 0.5 MeV⁻¹, and for lighter nuclei about 0.3 MeV⁻¹.
- Ratio B_sr/B_rel for NL1 ranges from 0.742 (¹⁶O) to 0.991 (²⁰⁸Pb); for NL-SH ranges from 0.544 (¹⁶O) to 0.939 (²⁰⁸Pb).
- Empirical GMR energies: 13.9 ± 0.3 MeV for ²⁰⁸Pb and 16.4 ± 0.4 MeV for ⁹⁰Zr.
- Surface incompressibility K_S: −333.1 MeV for NL1 and −610.1 MeV for NL-SH; ratio of surface to bulk incompressibility is 1.58 for NL1 and 1.72 for NL-SH.

## Context

This work extends earlier constrained RMF calculations using the linear Walecka model (Maruyama and Suzuki, 1989; Boersma, Malfliet, and Scholten, 1991) by systematically examining the relationship between breathing-mode energies and nuclear matter incompressibility across multiple parameter sets. It contradicts the simpler monotonic relationship found in nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations (Blaizot, 1980; Treiner, Krivine, Bohigas, and Martorell, 1981), where the surface incompressibility scales approximately as −K_NM. The results enable a more nuanced interpretation of empirical GMR data in terms of nuclear matter properties, showing that the RMF theory can reproduce the ²⁰⁸Pb empirical energy for K_NM between 300–400 MeV, while the ⁹⁰Zr value is overestimated by 2–3 MeV.
