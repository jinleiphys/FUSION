# Reaction of deuteron with a heavy nucleus at low energies

> We extend the recently proposed mechanism for inducing low energy nuclear reactions (LENR) to compute the reaction rate of deuteron with a heavy nucleus. The process gets dominant contribution at second order in the time dependent perturbation theory and is assisted by a resonance. The reaction proceeds by breakdown of deuteron into a proton and a neutron due to the action of the first perturbation. In the second, nuclear perturbation, the neutron gets captured by the heavy nucleus. Both perturbations are assumed to be electromagnetic and lead to the emission of two photons, one at each vertex. The heavy nucleus is taken to be ${}^{58}$Ni although many other may be considered.The reaction rate is found to be very small unless assisted by some special conditions. In the present case we assume the presence of a nuclear resonant state. In the presence of such a state we find that the reaction rate is sufficiently large to be observable in laboratory even at low energies.

## Key claim

The authors extend a previously proposed second-order perturbation mechanism for low-energy nuclear reactions to compute the deuteron-${}^{58}$Ni fusion rate, finding that the rate is very small in free space unless assisted by a nuclear resonant state; with the presence of a 7 keV resonance in the neutron-nickel system, the rate becomes approximately 100 per second and observable in laboratory even at eV-scale incident energies.

## Method

The reaction proceeds via two electromagnetic perturbations: first, the deuteron breaks into a proton and neutron with emission of one photon; second, the neutron is captured by the ${}^{58}$Ni nucleus with emission of a second photon. The authors model the initial deuteron-nickel system as a molecular bound state with a Gaussian wave function, use a shell-model potential for the nickel nucleus, and model the neutron-nickel resonance via a parameterized phase shift expansion up to $k^6$ to match the observed 7 keV resonance with 7 eV width. The transition amplitude is computed at second order in time-dependent perturbation theory, summing over intermediate neutron states, and the rate is integrated over final-state phase space.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron-nickel reaction rate is approximately **100 per second** in the presence of the resonance.
- The neutron-nickel resonance is at **7 keV** with a width of approximately **7 eV**.
- The resonant neutron momentum $k_R$ corresponds to approximately **1140 atomic units**.
- The shell-model potential parameters are $V_0 = 50$ MeV, $a_X = 0.524$ fm, and $R_X = 1.25 A^{1/3}$, yielding the nickel $2p_{3/2}$ state at **-9.2 MeV** (observed value approximately -9 MeV for ${}^{59}$Ni).
- The deuteron potential parameters are $V_0 = 34.6$ MeV and $r_0 = 2.066$ fm.
- The standard neutron absorption cross section on ${}^{58}$Ni with photon emission is approximately **200 barns** at incident energy of $10^{-5}$ eV.
- The emitted photon energy $E_2$ shows a broad peak centered at approximately **2 MeV**.

## Context

This work extends the second-order perturbation mechanism previously applied to proton-deuteron fusion (Jain 2020, Jain 2021) and to weak-interaction proton-to-neutron conversion with capture by a heavy nucleus (Ramkumar 2024). It differs from standard first-order fusion processes suppressed by the Coulomb barrier (Coulomb 1968) and from other theoretical approaches such as electron screening (Assenbaum 1987, Ichimaru 1993), correlated states, electroweak interactions (Widom 2010), and phonon-induced reactions (Hagelstein 2015). The computed rate of 100 per second is much larger than that found for the weak interaction process in Ramkumar (2024), and the mechanism has also been used to explain experimentally observed transmutation of palladium with deuteron in medium (Gadly 2024). The result enables experimental searches for this specific two-photon emission channel in deuteron-nickel systems at low energies.
