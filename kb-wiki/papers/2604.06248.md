# Relativistic Barnett effect and Curie law in a rigidly rotating free Fermi gas

> By combining methods from thermal field theory and statistical mechanics, we reexamine the spin polarization caused by the relativistic Barnett effect in a rigidly rotating Fermi gas. We determine the pressure of this medium and show that it depends on an effective chemical potential, which includes contributions from orbital angular momentum-rotation and spin-rotation coupling. We introduce a specific regularization scheme to sum over the angular momentum quantum numbers. As a result, the thermal pressure and all thermodynamic quantities are separated into two parts that differ only in the spin fugacities of spin-up and spin-down fermions. We calculate the Fermi energy for both components and show that the Fermi energy of the spin-down fermions is lower than that of the spin-up ones. This difference arises from the spin-rotation coupling and leads to a spin polarization consistent with the Barnett effect. In particular, we introduce the spin-chemicorotational ratio $η\equiv Ω^{(0)}/2μ^{(0)}$, which adjusts the spin polarization of the Fermi gas. Here, $Ω^{(0)}$ and $μ^{(0)}$ represent the angular velocity and chemical potential at zero temperature, respectively. The factor $1/2$ accounts for the fermion's spin. We explore the temperature dependence of $μ$ and $Ω$, while assuming that the number of spin-up and spin-down fermions remains temperature independent. Our findings indicate that the spin-down component of the rotating Fermi gas dilutes at lower temperatures compared to the spin-up component. Additionally, we calculate the magnetic susceptibility arising from the Barnett magnetization and demonstrate that it is proportional to the moment of inertia $I$ of the rotating Fermi gas. Finally, we prove that $I$ exhibits a $1/T$ behavior in the high-temperature limit, similar to the Curie law of paramagnetism.

## Key claim

The spin polarization arising from the relativistic Barnett effect in a rigidly rotating free Fermi gas is controlled by the spin-chemicorotational ratio η ≡ Ω⁽⁰⁾/2μ⁽⁰⁾ (angular velocity over twice the chemical potential at zero temperature), and the moment of inertia of the gas follows a 1/T Curie-law behavior in the high-temperature limit, provided the number densities of spin-up and spin-down fermions remain temperature-independent.

## Method

The authors combine thermal field theory (imaginary-time formalism) with statistical mechanics to study a free Dirac fermion gas under rigid rotation, described by a metric in cylindrical coordinates with angular velocity Ω along the z-axis. They derive the pressure from the partition function, introduce a regularization scheme to sum over the angular momentum quantum number ℓ, and separate the thermodynamic quantities into spin-up and spin-down components characterized by fugacities z± = exp(β(μ ± Ω/2)). The analysis is performed in both nonrelativistic (NR) and ultrarelativistic (UR) limits, using Fermi integrals to compute number densities and thermodynamic relations to determine the temperature dependence of μ and Ω under the constraint that n± are temperature-independent.

## Key numbers

* The spin-chemicorotational ratio is defined as η ≡ Ω⁽⁰⁾/2μ⁽⁰⁾, where Ω⁽⁰⁾ and μ⁽⁰⁾ are the angular velocity and chemical potential at T = 0.
* For a spin polarization P = 0.5, the ratio η takes values η_nr = 0.35 in the nonrelativistic limit and η_ur = 0.18 in the ultrarelativistic limit.
* The Fermi energies of spin-up and spin-down fermions at T = 0 are given by ε_F^± = μ⁽⁰⁾(1 ± η), with ε_F⁻ < ε_F⁺ for Ω⁽⁰⁾ > 0.
* The number densities at T = 0 are n_{a,±} = (p_{a,F}³/6π²)(1 ± η_a)^{κ_a}, where κ_nr = 3/2 and κ_ur = 3, and p_{nr,F} = (2mμ_nr⁽⁰⁾)^{1/2}, p_{ur,F} = μ_ur⁽⁰⁾.
* The moment of inertia I of the rotating Fermi gas exhibits a 1/T behavior in the high-temperature limit, analogous to the Curie law of paramagnetism.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic generalization of the Barnett effect initially developed within chiral kinetic theory by Fukushima (2018) and the experimental observation of the nuclear Barnett effect in rotating water samples at 13.5 kHz by Arabgol and collaborators (2019). It also builds on recent studies of rotation effects on free bosonic and fermionic systems using the rigid-rotation metric from Yamamoto (2013). The results enable further investigation of spin polarization and magnetization in rotating quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy ion collisions, where angular velocities can reach 10²¹ rad/s, and connect to the observed polarization of Λ hyperons at RHIC and LHC.
