# On the use of a non-extensive distribution in the solar plasma

> We describe the physical motivations why we are urged to look for an ionic equilibrium distribution function slightly different from the Maxwellian to calculate nuclear reaction rates in the solar core plasma answering, at the same time, to a recent paper by Bahcall et al. (astro-ph/0010055).

# On the use of a non-extensive distribution in the solar plasma

## Key claim

The authors argue that the ionic equilibrium velocity distribution in the solar core plasma should deviate very slightly from the Maxwellian distribution, with the Tsallis non-extensive distribution providing a better description, and that this deviation can significantly affect nuclear reaction rates while leaving bulk properties such as sound speed and hydrostatic equilibrium unchanged. The claim is hedged by the condition that the deviation is small (|(1-q)/2| ≈ 0.003) and that the authors do not exclude the possibility of neutrino oscillation or new physics.

## Method

The authors present three physical motivations for adopting a non-extensive (Tsallis) equilibrium distribution in the solar plasma: (1) adding small corrections to the coefficients of the standard Fokker-Planck equation generates the Tsallis distribution; (2) time-spatial fluctuations of electric microfields in the solar plasma, with energy density of order 10⁻¹⁶ MeV/fm³, imply a deviation from Maxwellian; (3) memory effects and long-time correlations between velocities can produce solutions compatible with Tsallis distribution. The screening factor f₀ is taken as unity because its effect on reaction rates is negligible compared to the depletions or enhancements from the non-extensive distribution.

## Key numbers

- The plasma parameter Γ ≈ 0.05 to 0.1 in the solar core, where the mean Coulomb energy potential is not much smaller than the thermal kinetic energy.
- The inverse solar plasma frequency t_pl = ω_pl⁻¹ ≈ 10⁻¹⁷ sec, comparable to the collision time t_coll = ⟨σv n⟩⁻¹ ≈ 10⁻¹⁷ sec.
- The number of particles per Debye sphere is about 1.4 at the base of the convection zone and 2.2 at the core.
- The Tsallis parameter deviation in the solar core plasma is |(1-q)/2| ≈ 0.003 (q=1 corresponds to Maxwellian).
- The electric microfield energy density in the solar plasma is of order 10⁻¹⁶ MeV/fm³.

## Context

This paper responds to Bahcall et al. (2000), who argued for the correctness of the Salpeter screening formula, and extends the authors' prior work (Lavagno and Quarati, cited as npb) on reaction rates using a non-Maxwellian distribution. The work builds on the non-extensive thermostatistics developed by Tsallis (1988) and on an earlier ad hoc proposal by Clayton. The results aim toward better agreement with experimental solar neutrino flux measurements, and the authors note that the approach does not affect bulk solar properties such as sound speed, as shown in work by Innocenti et al. (cited as inno). The paper enables further investigation of non-Maxwellian effects in stellar plasmas and connects to ongoing molecular dynamics model work within Tsallis statistics.
