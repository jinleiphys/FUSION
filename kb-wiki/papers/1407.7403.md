# The $\bar{p} p \to ϕϕ$ reaction in an effective Lagrangian approach

> We investigate the $\bar{p} p \to ϕϕ$ reaction within an effective Lagrangian approach.We show that the inclusion of either a scalar meson $f_0$ or a tensor meson $f_2$ in the $s$-channel can lead to a fairly good description of the bump structure of the total cross section around the invariant $\bar{p} p$ mass $W \simeq 2.2$ GeV, which cannot be reproduced with only the "background" contributions from $t$- and $u$-channel $N^*(1535)$ resonance as studied in a previous work. From the fits, we infer the properties of the involved scalar or tensor resonances.

## Key claim

The bump structure in the total cross section of the \(\bar{p} p \to \phi \phi\) reaction around an invariant \(\bar{p} p\) mass \(W \simeq 2.2\) GeV can be reproduced by including either a scalar meson \(f_0\) or a tensor meson \(f_2\) in the \(s\)-channel, but the fit with the tensor meson is preferred (\(\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} = 1.4\) vs. \(2.1\) for the scalar), suggesting the reaction is dominated by a strange tensor meson with quantum numbers \(J^{PC} = 2^{++}\).

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach and the isobar model to compute tree-level Feynman diagrams for the \(\bar{p} p \to \phi \phi\) reaction. They included "background" contributions from \(t\)- and \(u\)-channel \(N^*(1535)\) resonance exchange (as in Shi et al. (2010)) and added an \(s\)-channel diagram with either a scalar meson \(f_0\) or a tensor meson \(f_2\). The masses, total decay widths, and product coupling constants (\(g_{f_0\bar{p}p}g_{f_0\phi\phi}\) or \(g_{f_2\bar{p}p}g_{f_2\phi\phi}\)) for these mesons were treated as free parameters and fitted to the total cross section data from the JETSET collaboration (Evangelista et al., 1998) using MINUIT.

## Key numbers

- The experimental total cross section at 1.2 GeV incident antiproton momentum is \(\sigma = 2.86 \pm 0.46\ \mu\)b (JETSET collaboration).
- Fit I (scalar meson \(f_0\)): \(M_{f_0} = 2174 \pm 3\) MeV, \(\Gamma_{f_0} = 167 \pm 27\) MeV, product coupling \(g_{f_0 \bar{p}p} g_{f_0 \phi \phi} = 0.45 \pm 0.08\), with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} = 2.1\).
- Fit II (tensor meson \(f_2\)): \(M_{f_2} = 2192 \pm 4\) MeV, \(\Gamma_{f_2} = 177 \pm 30\) MeV, product coupling \(g_{f_2 \bar{p}p} g_{f_2 \phi \phi} = -0.12 \pm 0.02\), with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} = 1.4\).
- The cutoff parameters were constrained: \(\Lambda_{f_0} = 0.6\) GeV, \(\Lambda_{f_2} = 0.65\) GeV, and \(\Lambda_{N^*(1535)} = 3.05\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of Shi et al. (2010), which included only \(t\)- and \(u\)-channel \(N^*(1535)\) resonance exchange and could not reproduce the bump structure below \(W = 2.3\) GeV. The large OZI rule violation in this reaction was previously interpreted via glueball candidates, four-quark states (Dover et al., 1989), instanton-induced interactions (Kochelev, 1995), or \(s\bar{s}\) admixture in the nucleon (Ellis et al., 1988, 1994, 1999). The present results enable future experimental measurements to better constrain the properties of poorly known strange scalar and tensor mesons with masses around 2.2 GeV.
