# Thermal photon emission from the pi-rho-omega system

> We investigate thermal photon emission rates in hot hadronic matter from a system consisting of pi, rho, and omega mesons. The rates are calculated using both relativistic kinetic theory with Born diagrams as well as thermal field theory at the two-loop level. This enables us to cross-check our calculations and to manage a pole contribution that arises in the Born approximation corresponding to the omega -> pi^0 gamma radiative decay. After implementing hadronic form factors to account for finite-size corrections, we find that the resulting photo-emission rates are comparable to existing results from pi rho -> pi gamma processes in the energy regime of 1-3 GeV. We expect that our new sources will provide a non-negligible contribution to the total hadronic rates, thereby enhancing calculated thermal photon spectra from heavy-ion collisions, which could improve the description of current direct-photon data from experiment.

## Key claim

The paper claims that thermal photon emission from the pi-rho-omega system provides a non-negligible contribution to total hadronic photon rates in heavy-ion collisions, potentially improving the description of current direct-photon data, but only after carefully excluding the omega radiative decay contribution to avoid double-counting.

## Method

The authors calculated thermal photon emission rates using both relativistic kinetic theory with Born scattering amplitudes and thermal field theory at the two-loop level for the processes pi rho -> gamma omega, pi omega -> gamma rho, and rho omega -> gamma pi. They employed vector meson dominance to relate the electromagnetic current to the rho meson self-energy, used a Wess-Zumino interaction Lagrangian for the pi-rho-omega vertex, and implemented hadronic dipole form factors with a cutoff parameter of 1 GeV to account for finite-size corrections. The thermal field theory calculations allowed them to separate unitarity and Landau cuts, providing a rigorous method to exclude the omega -> pi0 gamma radiative decay contribution that appears as a pole in the Born approximation.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants determined from experimental decay widths: g_rho = 5.98, g_pi-rho-omega = 21.6 GeV^-1, C_rho = 0.0611
- Input decay widths used: Gamma_rho->pi pi = 149.1 MeV, Gamma_omega->pi0 gamma = 0.703 MeV, Gamma_rho->e+e- = 7.04 keV
- Form factor cutoff parameter Lambda = 1 GeV
- Temperatures studied: 120, 150, and 180 MeV
- The u-channel form factor in the rho omega -> gamma pi process produces suppression up to a factor 4.5 at q_0 = 3.0 GeV for pion exchange, while the stc term with rho exchange produces suppression up to a factor 30 at q_0 = 3.0 GeV
- Photo-emission rates are comparable to existing results from pi rho -> pi gamma processes in the energy regime of 1-3 GeV
- Variations from including a finite rho meson width produced changes of order 10% or less in the photo-emission rates

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of hadronic photon emission from pi rho -> pi gamma processes by Turbide (2003) and Rapp (1999), which identified omega t-channel exchange as an important contributor. The new sources involving external omega mesons are motivated by the ongoing "photon puzzle" in heavy-ion collisions, where calculated photon spectra show discrepancies with experimental measurements from PHENIX and ALICE collaborations. The results enable more complete calculations of thermal photon spectra from heavy-ion collisions, potentially improving the description of direct-photon data that current models from van Hees (2011) and others have struggled to reproduce.
