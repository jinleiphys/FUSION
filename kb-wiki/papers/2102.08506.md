# Exploring the QCD phase diagram with fluctuations

> We report on recent progress concerning theoretical description of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions. Specifically we discuss a new Cooper-Frye particlization routine -- the subensemble sampler -- which is designed to incorporate effects of global conservation laws, thermal smearing and resonance decays on fluctuation measurements in various rapidity acceptances. First applications of the method to heavy-ion collisions at the LHC energies are presented and further necessary steps to analyze fluctuations from RHIC beam energy scan are outlined.

## Key claim

The subensemble sampler, a new Cooper-Frye particlization routine, can incorporate effects of global conservation laws, thermal smearing, and resonance decays on fluctuation measurements, but the results show that a reliable interpretation of experimental data will require control over these effects; for example, a measurement of a negative hyperkurtosis $\kappa_6[B-\bar{B}]/\kappa_2[B-\bar{B}]$ corrected for global baryon conservation via the subensemble acceptance method would indicate a negative grand-canonical hyperkurtosis $\chi_6^B / \chi_2^B$, which could be interpreted as an experimental signature of the chiral QCD crossover transition.

## Method

The authors partition the particlization hypersurface into contiguous subvolumes, each characterized by constant thermal parameters and sufficiently large compared to the correlation length. They sample hadron multiplicities from a joint probability distribution that combines an interacting hadron resonance gas model (with repulsive excluded volume interactions in the baryonic sector, excluded volume parameter $b = 1$ fm$^3$) with a Kronecker delta to enforce exact global conservation of baryon number, then sample momenta via the Cooper-Frye formula independently for each hadron from each subvolume. For the LHC application, the particlization hypersurface is approximated by a longitudinally boost-invariant blast-wave surface covering 9.6 units of space-time rapidity, with freeze-out temperature $T = 160$ MeV and vanishing chemical potentials, and the longitudinal axis is split into 96 slices each covering 0.1 units of rapidity.

## Key numbers

- In 0-5% central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76$ TeV, the predicted kurtosis of net baryon number in $\Delta Y_{\rm acc} = 1$ is $\kappa_4^B/\kappa_2^B \simeq 0.56$, while the net proton kurtosis is $\kappa_4^p/\kappa_2^p \simeq 0.83$, both differing from the grand-canonical net baryon kurtosis of $\chi_4^B/\chi_2^B \simeq 0.67$.
- The thermal smearing can be described analytically by assuming a Gaussian rapidity smearing with width $\sigma_y = 0.3$.
- The blast-wave surface covers 9.6 units of space-time rapidity $\eta_s$, and the parameter $\alpha$ (fraction of total volume covered by acceptance) is approximated as $\alpha = \Delta Y_{\rm acc} / 9.6$ for $p_T$-integrated acceptance in a range $\Delta Y_{\rm acc}$ around midrapidity.
- The excluded volume parameter in the interacting hadron resonance gas model is $b = 1$ fm$^3$.

## Context

This work extends the subensemble acceptance method (SAM) developed by Vovchenko et al. (2020) for correcting grand-canonical cumulants to account for global conservation of conserved charges. It provides a framework to connect lattice QCD susceptibilities (Bazavov et al., 2017; Borsanyi et al., 2018) with experimental fluctuation measurements at the LHC and RHIC beam energy scan, enabling quantitative analysis of net proton cumulants at RHIC BES when combined with three-dimensional hydro simulations (Shen et al., 2020). The results show that direct comparisons between grand-canonical cumulants and measured cumulants of net baryon or net proton distribution are unjustified, and that the method of Kitazawa and Asakawa (2011, 2012) can accurately relate net baryon and proton cumulants via binomial unfolding.
