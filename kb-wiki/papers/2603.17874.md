# Universal scaling of transport coefficients near the liquid-gas critical point

> We employ a novel real-time formulation of the functional renormalization group (FRG) to compute universal scaling functions of the thermal diffusivity and the shear viscosity in the vicinity of the liquid-gas critical point, i.e., for the dynamic universality class of Model H from the Halperin-Hohenberg classification. We map out the universal dependence of the transport coefficients on temperature, external magnetic field, and wavenumber, and provide a detailed comparison with the Kawasaki approximation, which is here obtained from a perturbative one-loop approximation to our real-time FRG flow. In contrast to the Kawasaki approximation, the non-perturbative scaling functions from the full real-time FRG flow show a mild dependence on the thermodynamic path towards the critical point. We further compare our FRG results for the universal wavenumber and temperature dependence of the thermal diffusivity with experimental data from critical fluids.

## Key claim

The non-perturbative real-time FRG scaling functions for thermal diffusivity and shear viscosity show a mild dependence on the thermodynamic path (symmetric vs. broken phase) toward the critical point, unlike the Kawasaki approximation which yields a path-independent scaling function.

## Method

The authors solve a truncated real-time functional renormalization group (FRG) flow for Model H, which describes a conserved order parameter coupled to transverse momentum density. They employ a derivative expansion with a wavenumber-dependent wave function renormalization and field-dependent effective potential, promoting the kinetic coefficients (heat conductivity and shear viscosity) to wavenumber-dependent functions. The FRG flow is solved numerically by expanding around the k-dependent minimum of the coarse-grained free energy, and the resulting transport coefficients are mapped as functions of reduced temperature, external field, and wavenumber.

## Key numbers

- Dynamic critical exponent ζ ≈ 3.06 from the FRG calculation, consistent with the numerical simulation result ζ = 3.013(58) from Chattopadhyay (2024)
- Critical exponent for shear viscosity xη ≈ 0.047 from magnetic scaling, compared to the experimental value xη = 0.0690(6) from the CVX experiment (PhysRevE.60.4079)
- Static critical exponents from the FRG: δ ≈ 4.39, β ≈ 0.358, with non-universal amplitudes Bc/H0^(1/δ) ≈ 2.03 and B ≈ 2.60, and H0 ≈ 2.99
- The FRG scaling functions for shear viscosity deviate from the Kawasaki approximation by less than 20% over the range of scaling variable x shown
- The systematic error from truncation is roughly the same order as the spread of experimental data in the hydrodynamic regime x ≲ 1, but larger than the data spread in the critical regime x ≳ 1

## Context

This work extends the real-time FRG framework developed by Roth (2024) for Model H to compute universal scaling functions of transport coefficients near the liquid-gas critical point, which belongs to the dynamic universality class of Model H from the Halperin-Hohenberg classification. The results are compared with the Kawasaki approximation (Kawasaki, 1970) and experimental data from various fluids compiled by Swinney (1973). The FRG approach provides a non-perturbative framework to assess the quality of the Kawasaki approximation, which neglects the weak power-law divergence of shear viscosity, and enables future studies with more sophisticated truncation schemes that could achieve the precision of static critical exponents from conformal bootstrap (Kos, 2016).
