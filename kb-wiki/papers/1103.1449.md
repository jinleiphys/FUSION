# Fusion Hindrance in the Heavy Ion Reactions -- Border Between the Normal and Hindered Fusions

> The fusion hindrance in heavy ion collisions is studied in the framework of the two-center liquid drop model. It appears that the neck and the radial degrees of freedom might both be hampered by an inner potential barrier on their path between the contact configuration to the compound nucleus. Heavy ion reactions with and without the two kinds of fusion hindrance are classified through systematic calculations. It is found that the number of reactions without radial fusion hindrance is much smaller than that without neck fusion hindrance, and for both kinds of fusion hindrance the number of reactions without fusion hindrance at small mass-asymmetry parameter $α$ is smaller than that at large $α$. In the formation of a given compound nucleus, if a reaction with $α_c$ is not hindered, then other reactions with $α> α_c$ are also not hindered as it is well known experimentally.

## Key claim

The paper claims that fusion hindrance in heavy ion reactions can arise from two distinct degrees of freedom (neck and radial), but that for most realistic reactions the neck degree of freedom is not hindered while the radial degree of freedom is the restrictive one; the border between hindered and non-hindered reactions depends on the mass-asymmetry parameter and the charge of the compound nucleus.

## Method

The authors used the two-center liquid drop model with three shape parameters (distance between centers z, mass-asymmetry α, and neck parameter ε) to calculate the LDM potential for composite systems. For neck fusion hindrance, they evaluated the slope of the potential at ε=1 at the contact configuration; for radial fusion hindrance, they solved Newtonian equations without random force to determine whether the system evolves toward the compound state or is blocked by a ridgeline. Systematic calculations were performed for various projectile-target combinations to map the borderlines between hindered and non-hindered reactions.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric reactions (α=0), the maximum proton number Z_max for reactions without radial fusion hindrance is Z_max = 87 (from the two-center LDM model), compared to Z_max = 86 from the parameterized LDM of Swiatecki et al.
- For α=0.6, Z_max = 90 from the two-center LDM model, compared to Z_max = 110 from the parameterized LDM of Swiatecki et al.
- The critical distance parameter z_c for 110Pd+110Pd is z_c = 1.67, where the slope f = dV/dε at ε=1 becomes zero.
- The contact point parameter z_0 for symmetric reactions is z_0 = 1.587.
- The equilibrium neck parameter ⟨ε⟩ is close to 0.1 for most reactions.

## Context

This work extends the two-step model of fusion (Abe 2000, Shen 2002) and the extra-push theory of Swiatecki (1982) by systematically classifying reactions according to both neck and radial fusion hindrance using the two-center parameterization. The results show that the two-center LDM gives a more restrictive borderline for radial hindrance at large asymmetry compared to the parameterized LDM of Swiatecki (1982). The paper enables future experimental tests to assess which parameterization is correct, and provides a foundation for calculating the amplitude of the extra-push energy needed for hindered reactions.
