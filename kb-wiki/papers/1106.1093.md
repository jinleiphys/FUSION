# A fully relativistic lattice Boltzmann algorithm

> Starting from the Maxwell-Juettner equilibrium distribution, we develop a relativistic lattice Boltzmann (LB) algorithm capable of handling ultrarelativistic systems with flat, but expanding, spacetimes. The algorithm is validated through simulations of quark-gluon plasma, yielding excellent agreement with hydrodynamic simulations. The present scheme opens the possibility of transferring the recognized computational advantages of lattice kinetic theory to the context of both weakly and ultra-relativistic systems.

## Key claim

The authors develop a fully relativistic lattice Boltzmann algorithm derived from the Maxwell-Juettner equilibrium distribution, capable of handling ultrarelativistic systems with flat but expanding spacetimes, with the caveat that the algorithm requires the temperature scaling parameter theta to satisfy theta less than 2 times (u^0 minus |u|) for mean convergence, which limits applicability for highly relativistic flows with Lorentz factors around 10 to small temperature regions.

## Method

The algorithm discretizes momentum space using Gauss-Laguerre quadrature for the radial momentum component and Gauss-Legendre quadrature plus uniform angular discretization for the angular components, requiring a minimum of 27 discrete momenta. The relativistic lattice Boltzmann equation is implemented through three sequential steps: streaming in configuration space (x-move), streaming in momentum space (p-move) to account for geometric forces via Christoffel symbols, and collisional relaxation toward the local equilibrium distribution expanded in orthogonal polynomials. The fluid 4-velocity and energy density are extracted at each step by computing the future-pointing eigenvector of the energy-momentum tensor.

## Key numbers

- Minimum of 27 discrete momenta required for the algorithm, comparable to non-relativistic lattice Boltzmann schemes
- The condition for mean convergence requires theta less than 2 times (u^0 minus |u|), where theta is the scaled temperature T/T_0
- For highly relativistic flows with Lorentz factor gamma approximately 10, theta = 0.1 ensures convergence
- The dynamic viscosity coefficient is given by eta = tau_R times (epsilon + P)/6, where tau_R = 6 times (eta/s) times T^{-1}
- The energy density is related to temperature by epsilon = 3 T^4 / pi^2 for the ultrarelativistic ideal gas equation of state

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic lattice Boltzmann methods developed over the previous two decades (Shan 1997, Grad) into the relativistic domain, addressing the realizability problem of previous top-down relativistic lattice Boltzmann formulations (RELB1, RELB2) that used moment-matching without an underlying microscopic model. The algorithm enables efficient simulation of event-by-event fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which have been shown to produce qualitatively different results compared to smooth initial conditions (Teaney 2010, Luzum 2010), and can validate the 3+1 dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamics code from the McGill group (Schenke 2010).
