# Study of spin-dependent structure functions of $^3{\rm He}$ and $^3{\rm H}$ at NNLO approximation and corresponding nuclear corrections

> We determine polarized parton distribution functions (PPDFs) and structure functions from recent experimental data of polarized deep inelastic scattering (DIS) on nuleons at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) approximation in perturbative quantum chromodynamic (pQCD). The nucleon polarized structure functions are computed using the Jacobi polynomial approach while target mass corrections (TMCs) are included in our fitting procedure. Having extracted the polarized spin structure functions, we extend our study to describe $^3{\mathrm He}$ and $^3{\mathrm H}$ polarized structure functions, as well as the Bjorken sum rule. We also explore the importance of the nuclear corrections on the polarized nuclear structure functions at small and large values of $x$. Our results are compared with the recent available and high precision polarized $^3{\mathrm He}$ and $^3{\mathrm H}$ experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors determine polarized parton distribution functions (PPDFs) and structure functions from recent polarized deep inelastic scattering (DIS) data at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in pQCD, then extend the analysis to compute the spin-dependent structure functions of $^3{\mathrm He}$ and $^3{\mathrm H}$ including nuclear corrections; the analysis is limited to the sum of quark and antiquark polarized PDFs (not individual valence and sea distributions) and focuses on the kinematic region where incoherent scattering dominates.

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD fit of 511 experimental data points for the polarized structure function $g_1$ on proton, neutron, and deuteron targets at NNLO approximation, using the Jacobi polynomial expansion method to reconstruct $x$-dependent quantities from their Mellin moments, with target mass corrections (TMCs) included. The polarized nuclear structure functions for $^3{\mathrm He}$ and $^3{\mathrm H}$ were then computed using a convolution approach within the weak binding approximation, where nucleon light-cone momentum distributions extracted from nuclear spectral functions are convolved with the off-shell polarized nucleon structure functions.

## Key numbers

- The global fit includes 511 experimental data points covering the kinematic range $0.0035 < x < 0.75$, $1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2 < Q^2 < 100\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$, and $W > 4\ \mathrm{GeV}$
- The total $\chi^2$ for the fit is 473.195 across all 511 data points
- The deuteron $D$-state probability is taken as $w_D = 0.05 \pm 0.01$
- The axial charges used as sum rule constraints are $a_3 = g_A = 1.269 \pm 0.003$ and $a_8 = 0.585 \pm 0.025$
- The Jacobi polynomial expansion parameters are set to $N_{\mathrm{max}} = 7$, $\alpha = 3$, $\beta = 0.5$ for optimal convergence

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NNLO analyses TKAA16 (Shahri et al., 2016) and KTA-I17 (Khanpour et al., 2017) by using a more general input parametrization for the sum of quark and antiquark polarized PDFs rather than separate valence and sea distributions, and by including the most recent COMPASS16 $g_1^p$ and $g_1^d$ data (Adolph et al., 2015, 2016). The nuclear structure function calculations for $^3{\mathrm He}$ and $^3{\mathrm H}$ enable comparison with recent high-precision experimental data from the E06-014 experiment at Jefferson Lab (Flay et al., 2016), and the framework can be applied to study nuclear corrections at intermediate and large Bjorken $x$ where incoherent scattering dominates.
