# Chiral dynamics with strange quarks: Mysteries and opportunities

> Recent developments and open issues in chiral dynamics with strange quarks are reviewed. Topics include: Order parameters of chiral symmetry breaking, the flavor dependence of these order parameters, speculations about the phase structure of QCD with varying number of flavors, OZI violation and its natural emergence in case of a small strange quark condensate, heavy kaon chiral perturbation theory, an update on pion-pion, pion-kaon and pion-nucleon sigma terms, the dynamics and nature of scalar mesons, the role of the scalar pion and kaon form factors, attempts to describe some strange baryons employing coupled channel dynamics tied to unitarity and chiral perturbation theory, and the role of final state interactions in Goldstone boson pair production in proton-proton collisions.

# Chiral dynamics with strange quarks: Mysteries and opportunities

## Key claim
The standard scenario of three-flavor chiral perturbation theory, where the strange quark mass is treated as a small perturbation on equal footing with up and down quark masses, may need to be reconsidered because the three-flavor quark condensate could be significantly suppressed relative to the two-flavor condensate, with the central value suggesting a suppression of about 54% but with large uncertainties that still allow marginal consistency with the standard scenario.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical developments in chiral dynamics with strange quarks, focusing on QCD inequalities that show chiral order parameters are suppressed with increasing number of flavors, and analyzes sum rules for the OZI-violating correlator Π_z(m_s) using spectral functions constructed from coupled-channel ππ/K̅K T-matrices and scalar form factors constrained by chiral perturbation theory. The author also examines the implications of a possible small strange quark condensate for the reordering of the chiral expansion, heavy kaon chiral perturbation theory, sigma terms from scattering data, and the nature of scalar mesons and strange baryons through coupled-channel dynamics.

## Key numbers
- The strange quark mass determination from scalar sum rules gives m_s(2 GeV) = 99 ± 16 MeV (at a scale of 1 GeV in the MSbar scheme)
- The Gell-Mann-Okubo relation M_η² = (4/3)M_K² - (1/3)M_π² is fulfilled within a few percent: (547.3 MeV)² ≈ (569.5 MeV)²
- The three-flavor condensate suppression is quantified as Σ(3) = Σ(2) × [1 - 0.54 ± 0.27], giving a central value of 54% suppression with ±27% uncertainty
- The sum rule analysis yields 2 ≲ 16π² Π_z ≲ 6 for the OZI-violating coupling
- In the standard scenario, the chiral scales satisfy Σ^{1/3}(3) ∼ 1.5 GeV ≫ F(3) ∼ 0.1 GeV
- Asymptotic freedom in QCD is lost for N_f ≥ 17, with a conformal window expected around N_f ≃ 6

## Context
This review extends the line of work on chiral perturbation theory initiated by Weinberg (1979) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985), and addresses the possibility raised by the Orsay group (Descotes, Girlanda, Stern, 2000) that the three-flavor quark condensate might be suppressed. The analysis builds on Moussallam's (2000) sum rule work using scalar form factors from chiral unitary approaches (Meißner and Oller, 2000) and earlier T-matrix inputs from Oller, Oset, and Pelaez (1999), as well as from Anisovich, Matveev, and Lapidus (1998) and Kaminski, Lesniak, and Loiseau (1999). The paper enables further lattice tests of the Leutwyler-Smilga sum rules (1992) extended to the three-flavor case, and motivates refined determinations of sigma terms from pion-pion, pion-kaon, and pion-nucleon scattering to distinguish between the standard and suppressed-condensate scenarios.
