# Particle production in proton-nucleus collisions beyond leading order

> We describe the origin of, and the solution to, the negativity problem which occurs in the perturbative calculation of the cross-section for single-inclusive particle production in proton-nucleus collisions at next-to-leading-order.

## Key claim

The negativity problem in the NLO cross-section for single-inclusive particle production in proton-nucleus collisions is an artifact of approximations made in hybrid factorization to obtain a result local in rapidity; a more general factorization scheme that is non-local in rapidity yields a manifestly positive cross-section to NLO accuracy.

## Method

The authors computed the quark multiplicity for forward particle production in pA collisions within the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, including NLO corrections to both the BK evolution kernel and the impact factor. They isolated the NLO correction to the impact factor by computing the primary gluon emission with exact kinematics (valid for any longitudinal momentum fraction x) and subtracting the eikonal contribution already included in the LO BK evolution. The resulting expression was compared to the hybrid factorization scheme of Chirilli, Xiao, and Yuan (2012), which makes additional approximations to obtain a result local in rapidity.

## Key numbers

- The LO quark multiplicity is given by dN/d²k dη|_LO = (1/(2π)²) x_p q(x_p) S(k, X_g), where x_p = (k_T/√s)e^η and X_g = (k_T/√s)e^(-η)
- The LO BK equation includes the coupling α̅ = α_s N_c/π
- The integral over the longitudinal momentum fraction x in the LO BK equation runs from X_g/X_0 to 1
- The NLO cross-section in the CXY scheme (Eq. toyCXY) turns negative when increasing k_T, as shown in numerical results from Ducloue et al. (2017)
- With running coupling α̅(k_T²), the subtracted version (Eq. toyNLOsub) turns negative at sufficiently large k_T, while the unsubtracted version (Eq. toyNLON) remains positive-definite

## Context

This work extends the NLO calculation of single-inclusive particle production in pA collisions by Chirilli, Xiao, and Yuan (2012), which exhibited a negativity problem first noted by Stasto, Xiao, and Zaslavsky (2013). The authors propose a more general factorization scheme within the CGC effective theory of Gelis, Iancu, Jalilian-Marian, and Venugopalan (2010) that avoids the approximations leading to negativity. The result enables phenomenological applications to forward particle production at RHIC and the LHC, where gluon saturation and multiple scattering are important.
