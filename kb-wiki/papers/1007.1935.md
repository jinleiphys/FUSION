# Entropy and light cluster production in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> The entropy production in medium energy heavy-ion collisions is analyzed in terms of ratio of deuteronlike to protonlike clusters ($d_{like}/p_{like}$) using \emph{quantum molecular dynamics} (QMD) model. The yield ratios of deuteronlike-to-protonlike clusters calculated as a function of participant proton multiplicity closely agree with experimental trends. Our model predictions indicate that full thermodynamical equilibrium may not be there even for the central geometry. The apparent entropy extracted from the yield ratios of deuteronlike-to-protonlike clusters, however, reflects the universality characteristics \emph{i.e.} it is governed by the volume of reaction independent of the target-projectile combination. Our calculations for apparent entropy produced in central collisions of Ca+Ca and Nb+Nb at different bombarding energies are in good agreement with $4π$ Plastic Ball data.

# Entropy and light cluster production in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

## Key claim
The apparent entropy extracted from deuteronlike-to-protonlike cluster ratios exhibits universality, it is governed by the participant volume (proton multiplicity) independent of the target-projectile combination, but full thermodynamical equilibrium is not achieved even for central collisions, meaning the extracted entropy is an *apparent* rather than a true baryonic entropy.

## Method
The authors used the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model with a soft equation of state (incompressibility κ = 200 MeV) and energy-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross sections to simulate symmetric heavy-ion collisions at incident energies between 400 and 1050 AMeV. Cluster yields were identified via the minimum spanning tree (MST) procedure with a coalescence radius of 4 fm, and the ratio of deuteronlike to protonlike clusters (d_like/p_like) was computed from the yields of A=1 through A=4 fragments. Apparent entropy was then extracted using the Kapusta formalism modified by Bertsch and Cugnon.

## Key numbers
- Incident energy range studied: 400–1050 AMeV for symmetric collisions of Ca+Ca and Nb+Nb.
- Soft equation of state incompressibility: κ = 200 MeV.
- Clusterization radius used in MST: r_C = 4 fm.
- Yield ratios were calculated at t ≈ 40 fm/c, when average nucleonic density saturates and nucleon-nucleon collisions practically cease.
- Anisotropy ratio ⟨R_A⟩ values at both 400 and 650 AMeV remain less than 1 at the expansion stage, indicating incomplete equilibrium.
- Apparent entropy values from QMD for central Ca+Ca and Nb+Nb collisions show close agreement with Plastic Ball experimental data (Doss et al., 1988), with nearly no effect of beam energy visible.

## Context
This work extends the coalescence-based entropy extraction formalism of Kapusta (1984) and Bertsch and Cugnon (1984) by applying it within a dynamical QMD framework rather than assuming thermal and chemical equilibrium. It confirms the participant-spectator picture of heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies, consistent with earlier Plastic Ball/Wall detector experiments by Doss et al. (1988). The results enable future studies of fireball formation and stopping using dynamical transport models, and suggest that secondary de-excitation of heavier clusters during the expansion stage modestly populates light cluster abundances, as previously noted by Dhawan et al. (2009) regarding light charged particle production and anisotropy ratios.
