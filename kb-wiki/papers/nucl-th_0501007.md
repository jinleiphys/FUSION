# Short Range Correlations and Spectral Functions in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> Dynamical correlations in asymmetric infinite nuclear matter are investigates in a transport theoretical approach. Self-energies due to short range correlations and their influence on the nucleon spectral functions are described in an approach accounting for a realistic treatment of mean-field dynamics and a self-consistently derived quasi-particle interaction. Landau-Migdal theory is used to derived the short range interaction from a phenomenological Skyrme energy density functional. The spectral functions in asymmetric nuclear matter are found to follow in their gross features closely the patterns observed previously in symmetric nuclear matter. An interesting sensitivity of dynamical self-energies and spectral functions on the momentum structure of the underlying interactions is found.

## Key claim

The spectral functions in asymmetric nuclear matter follow patterns similar to those in symmetric nuclear matter, but the authors find that the isovector mean-field introduces a gap between proton and neutron Fermi surfaces, causing the proton hole strength to shift toward the Fermi level while neutron hole strength is repelled deeper into the Fermi sea.

## Method

The authors use a transport theoretical approach with self-energies derived from Landau-Migdal theory, extracting the short-range interaction from a phenomenological Skyrme energy density functional (SLy230a parameter set) by subtracting the long-range pion exchange component. They calculate dynamical self-energies for both 2p1h and 1p2h configurations iteratively until convergence, treating mean-field effects through effective masses and potentials obtained from the Skyrme functional, and apply the formalism to asymmetric nuclear matter at various proton fractions.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3), the averaged matrix element is |M_bar(rho_0)| = 341 MeV fm^3, comparing well to the independently derived value of 320 MeV fm^3 from previous work.
- The averaged matrix element used in fixed-density calculations is (|M_qq'|^2)^(1/2) = 350 MeV fm^3.
- At asymmetry xi = 1/4 and momentum k = 31.3 MeV/c, the proton quasiparticle peak lies at omega_on^p = -28 MeV while the neutron quasiparticle peak lies at omega_on^n = -78 MeV.
- At asymmetry xi = 1/4 and momentum k = 312.5 MeV/c, the proton quasiparticle peak lies at omega(p) = 35 MeV and the neutron peak at omega(n) = 8 MeV.
- Inside the Fermi sea, occupancy is reduced by about 10% in both symmetric and asymmetric matter, with the same fraction of spectral strength shifted into high-momentum tails.

## Context

This work extends previous transport theoretical studies of symmetric nuclear matter by Lehr (2001, 2002) and Froemel (2003) to the asymmetric case, which had been rarely treated. It goes beyond the Brueckner Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations of Bozek (2003) and Hassaneen (2004) by including both 2p1h and 1p2h self-energies consistently, though at the expense of treating mean-field parts phenomenologically. The results enable predictions for spectral functions and momentum distributions in neutron-rich matter relevant to exotic nuclei and supernova physics, where the authors note that the short-range interaction matrix element derived from Landau-Migdal theory gives predictive power at densities and asymmetries previously inaccessible.
