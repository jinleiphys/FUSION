# Influence of the ab-initio nd cross sections in the critical heavy-water benchmarks

> The n-d elastic and breakup cross sections are computed by solving the three-body Faddeev equations for realistic and semi-realistic Nucleon-Nucleon potentials. These cross sections are inserted in the Monte Carlo simulation of the nuclear processes considered in the International Handbook of Evaluated Criticality Safety Benchmark Experiments (ICSBEP). The results obtained using thes ab initio n-d cross sections are compared with those provided by the most renown international libraries.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that *ab initio* neutron-deuteron (n-d) cross sections computed from realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials (AV18, INOY) can replace evaluated nuclear data library cross sections in Monte Carlo simulations of heavy-water criticality benchmarks, but the resulting multiplication factors (Keff) still systematically underestimate experimental values by 0.2 to 2.0 percent, depending on the benchmark series.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Faddeev equations in configuration space for n-d elastic and breakup scattering using three NN potentials: the semi-realistic MT I-III and the realistic AV18 (with Urbana UIX three-nucleon force) and INOY potentials. The resulting differential and integrated cross sections for incident neutron energies from 0 to 30 MeV were formatted into ENDF-6 evaluations and inserted into MCNP5 Monte Carlo simulations of 80 critical heavy-water benchmark experiments from the ICSBEP Handbook, with all other nuclear data taken from the ENDF/B-VII library and thermal scattering laws from SAB2002.

## Key numbers

- The n-d quartet (J=3/2) scattering length from AV18 and INOY is 6.34 fm, in agreement with the experimental value of 6.35 +/- 0.02 fm; the MT I-III quartet value is 6.44 fm, overestimating experiment by about 1 percent.
- The n-d doublet (J=1/2) scattering length from AV18 alone is 1.26 fm, but with the UIX three-nucleon force it becomes 0.595 fm, close to the experimental 0.65 +/- 0.04 fm; INOY gives 0.523 fm.
- The three NN potentials differ by about 2 percent in integrated elastic cross section at zero energy.
- The MT I-III potential underestimates experimental total n-d cross sections starting from about 15 MeV due to missing P-wave contributions.
- The Monte Carlo simulations achieved a standard deviation less than 25 pcm (0.025 percent) for each of the 90 selected benchmarks, using approximately 16 hours on 48 processors.
- The three evaluated libraries (ENDF/B-VII, JENDL-4, CENDL-3.1) lead to systematic underestimation of Keff values from about 0.2 to 2.0 percent for the HCM002 benchmark measurements.

## Context

This work extends the application of *ab initio* few-body calculations to reactor physics by inserting Faddeev-derived n-d cross sections into Monte Carlo neutron transport simulations, comparing with the evaluated libraries ENDF/B-VII, JENDL-4, and CENDL-3.1. The study builds on prior Faddeev calculations for the n-d system (Merkuriev 1971, Glockle 1976) and the established ICSBEP benchmark suite, and it enables future refinement of deuterium cross-section evaluations using first-principles nuclear theory rather than empirical fitting. The results show that realistic NN potentials (AV18, INOY) give better agreement with benchmark experiments than the semi-realistic MT I-III, but all approaches still underestimate reactivity, suggesting remaining deficiencies in either the n-d cross sections, the thermal scattering laws, or other nuclear data used in the simulations.
