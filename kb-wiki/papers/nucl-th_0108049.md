# Dynamical Study of the d(e,e' pi+) reaction

> The $d(e,e'π^+)$ reaction in the parallel kinematics has been investigated using a dynamical model of pion electroproduction on the nucleon. A unitary $πNN$ model has been used in order to examine the effects due to the final two-nucleon interactions, pion rescattering from the second nucleon, and the intermediate $NN$ and $NΔ$ interactions. It has been found that these $πNN$ mechanisms are small, but they can have significant contributions to the $d(e,e'π^+)$ cross sections through their interference with the dominant impulse term. For the longitudinal cross sections, the effects due to the interference between the pion pole term and other production mechanisms are also found to be very large. Our findings clearly indicate that these interference effects must be accounted for in any attempt to determine from the $d(e,e'π^+)$ data whether the pion form factor and/or $πNN$ vertex of the pion pole term are modified in the nuclear medium.

# Dynamical Study of the d(e,e' pi+) reaction

## Key claim

The hadronic final-state interactions (final two-nucleon interactions, pion rescattering, and intermediate NN and NDelta interactions) are individually small in the d(e,e'pi+) reaction under parallel kinematics, but they can produce significant contributions to cross sections through interference with the dominant impulse term. For longitudinal cross sections specifically, the interference between the pion pole term and other production mechanisms is very large, meaning that these interference effects must be accounted for before any determination of medium modifications to the pion form factor or piNN vertex can be made from the data.

## Method

The authors used the Sato-Lee (SL) dynamical model of pion electroproduction on the nucleon, combined with a unitary piNN model from Lee and Matsuyama (1987), to calculate the d(e,e'pi+) cross sections from four leading mechanisms: the impulse term (production on a single nucleon in the deuteron), final two-nucleon interactions (NN FSI), pion rescattering from the second nucleon, and intermediate baryon-baryon (NN and NDelta) interactions. The calculations were performed for parallel kinematics and were free of adjustable parameters, using the deuteron wavefunction and the SL model to generate the elementary production amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* At Saclay kinematics (Q^2 = 0.08 (GeV/c)^2, W = 1.16 GeV, E_e = 645 MeV, E_e' = 355 MeV), the piNN interaction mechanisms yield about a 10% effect in the region near the peak position of the cross section, mainly due to interference between the impulse amplitude and the FSI amplitude.
* At JLab kinematics (Q^2 = 0.4 (GeV/c)^2, W = 1.16 GeV, E_e = 844 MeV, E_e' = 395 MeV), the predicted cross sections are about 20% lower than the data.
* For the longitudinal cross section at the JLab kinematics, the piNN mechanisms reduce the cross section by only about 5%.
* The pion pole term alone gives only about 50% of the longitudinal cross section, with the remainder coming from interference with other production mechanisms.

## Context

This work extends the Sato-Lee (1996) dynamical model of pion electroproduction on the nucleon to the deuteron target, addressing theoretical questions raised by the Saclay experiment of Gilman et al. (1990) and the Jefferson Laboratory experiment of Gaskell et al. (2001). The study uses the piNN model developed by Lee and Matsuyama (1987) to calculate hadronic final-state interactions. The findings show that extracting medium modifications of the pion form factor or piNN vertex from d(e,e'pi+) data requires careful treatment of interference effects, particularly for longitudinal cross sections where the pion pole term alone accounts for only half of the observed strength.
