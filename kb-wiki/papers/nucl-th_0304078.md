# Spinodal in asymmetric nuclear matter

> The phase diagram of nuclear matter is quite rich - it shows such phenomena as phase-transitions, formation of condensates, clustering, etc. From the analysis of the spinodal instability, one can learn about the region of liquid-gas coexistence in nuclear matter at low densities and finite isospin asymmetries. In a recent paper, we have shown that asymmetric nuclear matter at sub-nuclear densities should undergo only one type of instability. The associated order parameter is dominated by the isoscalar density and so the transition is of liquid-gas type. The instability goes in the direction of a restoration of the isospin symmetry leading to a fractionation phenomenon.

## Key claim

Asymmetric nuclear matter at sub-nuclear densities exhibits only one type of spinodal instability, not two separate mechanical and chemical instabilities as previously claimed; the instability is dominated by isoscalar density fluctuations (liquid-gas type) and tends to restore isospin symmetry in the dense phase, leading to fractionation.

## Method

The authors performed a stability analysis of asymmetric nuclear matter by computing the curvature matrix of the free energy density with respect to baryon density and asymmetry density, using several Skyrme (SLy230a, SGII, SIII) and Gogny (D1, D1S, D1P) interactions. They diagonalized the 2×2 curvature matrix to obtain eigenvalues and eigenvectors, which define the direction and nature of the instability, and compared their results with simplified Skyrme interactions used by Baran et al. (2001). The analysis was extended to finite temperatures up to the critical point.

## Key numbers

- For the SLy230a interaction, the spinodal zone remains almost identical to the zero-temperature case up to a temperature of 5 MeV.
- The imaginary sound velocity in the spinodal region is larger than 0.06c for almost all of the spinodal zone, with the most internal contour corresponding to i0.09c.
- The k-effective mass (according to SLy230b) reduces the critical temperature in symmetric nuclear matter by about 1 MeV while increasing the critical temperature by 1 MeV in very asymmetric matter.
- The instability persists up to asymmetries where ρₚ < 3ρₙ without quantitative change in the density at which instability appears.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis (Margueron and Chomaz, 2003) showing that asymmetric nuclear matter has only one unstable mode, contradicting earlier claims by Müller and Serot (1995) and Bao et al. (1997) of separate mechanical and chemical instability regions. The results agree with Baran et al. (2001) on the isoscalar nature of the instability but show that simplified Skyrme interactions are insufficient for quantitative predictions. The findings enable more accurate modeling of fragment formation in heavy-ion collisions with asymmetric nuclei and clarify the phase diagram of nuclear matter for astrophysical applications.
