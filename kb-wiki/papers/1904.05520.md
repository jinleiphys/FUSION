# Non-static Analysis of the Anomalous Chiral Conductivities

> Given the intrinsic nonequilibrium nature of high-energy collisions the investigation of the dynamical properties of transport phenomena is important. The study of the real-time behavior of various conductivities and susceptibilities help refine the simulation tools we use to compare the theories and the experimental findings. In this contribution we take steps to give the chiral magnetic conductivity in case of magnetic field and chiral imbalance are both space-time dependent. Using linear response approximation we present the general 1-loop resummed expression for the electric current. We also suggest simple limiting cases in hope for possible implementation into a hydrodynamical framework.

### Key claim
The paper derives a general expression for the chiral magnetic conductivity when both the magnetic field and the chiral chemical potential are space-time dependent, using linear response theory; the static limit is ambiguous and depends on the order in which the external momenta are taken to zero, with the response current reaching at most 1/3 or 2/3 of the static value depending on which field is switched on first.

### Method
The authors compute the axial-vector–vector–vector (AVV) three-point function at one-loop order using the Schwinger–Keldysh formalism, expressing the vertex entirely in terms of the fermionic spectral function. They then evaluate the resulting electric current for homogeneous but time-dependent external fields, considering two limiting cases: a static chiral chemical potential with a decaying magnetic field, and a static magnetic field with a decaying chiral chemical potential. The calculation uses noninteracting chiral fermions and includes a Pauli–Villars regulator to maintain gauge invariance.

### Key numbers
- The maximal response current in the static-μ₅, decaying-B case is no larger than 1/3 of the static CME value.
- The maximal response current in the static-B, decaying-μ₅ case is no larger than 2/3 of the static CME value.
- The additional static contribution to the conductivity in the second case is g_stat(ω) = (1/3) ω/(ω + i0⁺), adding an extra (1/3) C_A B μ₅(0) e^{-t/τ} to the response current.
- The Dirac-fermion anomaly coefficient is C_A = e²/(2π²).

### Context
This work extends prior studies of non-static chiral magnetic effect (CME) by Kharzeev et al. (2010s) and by Hou and Ren (2011, 2017), which focused on time-dependent magnetic fields but treated the chiral chemical potential as static. The authors also reference kinetic-theory approaches to inhomogeneous μ₅ by earlier works (earlyMu5Kin1, earlyMu5Kin2). The results are intended for implementation into hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions, where the chiral charge density is inhomogeneous due to QCD vacuum fluctuations, and they plan to incorporate finite fermion lifetimes to resolve the ambiguity in the static limit, as suggested by CMErelaxFLTfermions.
