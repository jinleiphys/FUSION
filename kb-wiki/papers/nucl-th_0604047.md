# Tetrahedral correlations in $^{80}$Zr and $^{98}$Zr

> Axial octupole and tetrahedral correlations in $^{80}$Zr and $^{98}$Zr have been investigated using the generator coordinate method, applied to a basis generated by Skyrme HF+BCS calculations. We focus on the possible presence of states with tetrahedral symmetry and their stability with respect to octupole vibrations. We show that pairing significantly reduces the stability of the tetrahedral configuration and that a shallow mean-field tetrahedral minimum coexists with an axial octupole minimum. The contributions to the correlation energies coming from the tetrahedral degree of freedom and octupole axial deformation are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that tetrahedral correlations in $^{80}$Zr and $^{98}$Zr are slightly energetically favored over axial octupole correlations when dynamical effects beyond mean-field are included, but the authors hedge that the possible mixture between these modes cannot be excluded and that the results depend on a delicate balance between shell effects and pairing.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme Hartree-Fock + BCS (HFBCS) calculations with the SIII and SLy4 parametrizations, constraining the quadrupole moment to zero to probe octupole deformations along the axial ($Q_{30}$) and tetrahedral ($Q_{32}$) paths. They then applied the generator coordinate method (GCM) with particle-number and parity projection, using either $Q_{30}$ or $Q_{32}$ as the generator coordinate, to compute correlation energies and collective wave functions for the lowest positive- and negative-parity states.

## Key numbers

* For $^{80}$Zr with SIII, the correlation energy for the tetrahedral ($Q_{32}$) mode is 1.499 MeV for the positive-parity ground state, compared to 1.459 MeV for the axial ($Q_{30}$) mode.
* For $^{98}$Zr with SIII, the correlation energy for the tetrahedral mode is 1.485 MeV, compared to 1.387 MeV for the axial mode.
* The excitation energy of the first negative-parity state relative to the positive-parity ground state is 2.22 MeV for the tetrahedral mode in $^{80}$Zr and 0.784 MeV for the tetrahedral mode in $^{98}$Zr.
* The dynamical deformation $\tilde{\beta}_{32}$ for the positive-parity ground state is 0.12 in $^{80}$Zr and 0.14 in $^{98}$Zr, while $\tilde{\beta}_{30}$ is 0.13 and 0.11, respectively.
* In the HF approximation without pairing, the energy gained by tetrahedral deformation is around 2 MeV for $^{80}$Zr and 3 MeV for $^{98}$Zr, but pairing reduces these gains to below 1 MeV for $^{98}$Zr and eliminates the minima entirely for $^{80}$Zr.

## Context

This work extends prior predictions of tetrahedral magic numbers at Z=40 and N=40,56-58 by Dudek et al. (2002) and the macroscopic-microscopic calculations of the same group, applying for the first time a fully microscopic Skyrme HF+BCS plus GCM treatment to these candidates. The results contradict the expectation that tetrahedral shapes would be clearly identifiable, showing instead that pairing and quantum fluctuations make the tetrahedral minimum shallow and that both axial and tetrahedral octupole correlations have very similar characteristics. The study enables future multi-dimensional GCM calculations that simultaneously include quadrupole and both octupole degrees of freedom, which the authors identify as necessary for a definitive assessment.
