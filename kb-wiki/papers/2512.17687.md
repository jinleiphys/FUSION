# Reduced basis emulator for elastic scattering in continuum-discretized coupled-channel calculations

> I develop a reduced basis emulator for continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) calculations that achieves speedups of $\sim 10^2$ while maintaining sub-percent accuracy. The emulator is constructed using the proper orthogonal decomposition (POD) method applied to snapshots of CDCC solutions computed at sampled points in the optical potential parameter space. The prediction is performed via Galerkin projection onto the reduced basis. I demonstrate the method using deuteron scattering on $^{58}$Ni at 21.6 MeV as a test case, emulating 18 optical potential parameters simultaneously. The emulator reproduces elastic scattering cross sections with errors below 0.1\% across a wide parameter range. This development enables efficient uncertainty quantification and Bayesian parameter estimation for nuclear reaction calculations that were previously computationally prohibitive.

## Key claim

The reduced basis emulator for continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) calculations achieves speedups of approximately 10^2 while maintaining sub-percent accuracy, but this performance is demonstrated only for deuteron scattering on 58Ni at 21.6 MeV with 18 optical potential parameters, and the emulator requires an offline training phase using Latin hypercube sampling that involves solving the full CDCC problem at sampled parameter points.

## Method

The emulator is constructed using proper orthogonal decomposition (POD) applied to snapshots of CDCC solution vectors computed at sampled points in the optical potential parameter space, with the reduced basis extracted via singular value decomposition (SVD) truncation based on an energy criterion. Prediction for new parameter values is performed via Galerkin projection of the original linear system onto the reduced basis, exploiting the decomposition of the system matrix into parameter-independent kinetic and parameter-dependent potential contributions to enable rapid online evaluation. The method is demonstrated for deuteron scattering on 58Ni at 21.6 MeV, emulating 18 optical potential parameters (9 for proton-target and 9 for neutron-target interactions) simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- Speedup of approximately 10^2 (two orders of magnitude) for emulator evaluations compared to full CDCC calculations
- Emulator reproduces elastic scattering cross sections with errors below 0.1% across a wide parameter range
- Training uses either N_s = 200 or N_s = 400 Latin hypercube samples spanning the 18-dimensional parameter space
- The CDCC calculation uses J_max = 30 partial waves, N = 180 Lagrange mesh points with R_max = 100 fm, resulting in approximately 37 coupled channels and system dimension N_tot approximately 6660 per partial wave
- The reduced basis typically requires n_b = 5 to 50 basis vectors to capture more than 1 - epsilon_tol of the variance, where epsilon_tol is typically 10^-6 or smaller
- Relative errors in |S_11^J| remain below 1% for all J values and test cases, with most partial waves showing errors in the range 10^-4 to 10^-2 percent

## Context

This work extends reduced basis emulator techniques, previously applied to bound-state calculations via eigenvector continuation (Frame 2018, Konig 2020) and to scattering problems using the Kohn variational principle (Furnstahl 2020b, Zhang 2022) and other variational methods (Drischler 2021, Melendez 2021), to the more challenging CDCC framework involving tens of coupled channels. The emulator enables efficient uncertainty quantification and Bayesian parameter estimation for nuclear reaction calculations that would otherwise require 10^4 to 10^6 full CDCC evaluations, each taking seconds to hours, making such analyses computationally prohibitive with conventional solvers (Descouvemont 2016).
