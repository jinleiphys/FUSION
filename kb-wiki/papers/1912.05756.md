# Properties of exotic nuclei and their linkage to the nucleonic interaction

> The structure of exotic nuclei sheds new light on the linkage of the nuclear structure to the nucleonic interaction. The self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) theories are useful to investigate this linkage, which are applicable to many nuclei covering almost the whole range of the nuclear chart without artificial truncation of model space. For this purpose, it is desired to develop effective interaction for the SCMF calculations well connected to the bare nucleonic interaction. Focusing on ground-state properties, I show results of the SCMF calculations primarily with the M3Y-type semi-realistic interaction, M3Y-P6 and M3Y-P6a to be precise, and discuss in detail how the nucleonic interaction affects structure of nuclei including those far off the $β$-stability.

## Key claim
The paper argues that self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) calculations with semi-realistic interactions like M3Y-P6 and M3Y-P6a can reveal how the nucleonic interaction, particularly the tensor force, shapes ground-state properties of exotic nuclei, but the analysis is limited to ground-state properties and does not address excited states or dynamical correlations beyond the mean-field level.

## Method
The author applies axial Hartree-Fock (HF) and constrained HF (CHF) calculations using the M3Y-P6 semi-realistic interaction, which is based on the G-matrix and includes a realistic tensor force. For comparison, calculations with the D1M phenomenological interaction (which lacks explicit tensor force) are also performed. The center-of-mass corrections to nuclear densities and radii are treated via a cumulant expansion of the form factor, and the precision of this approximation is tested using a harmonic-oscillator model for the center-of-mass wave function.

## Key numbers
- For $^{16}$O, the ratio of $\langle\Phi|H_\mathrm{c.m.}^{(\mathrm{HO})}|\Phi\rangle$ to $\frac{1}{2}\mathrm{Tr}(\Xi^{1/2})$ is 1.008, with $\sqrt{\langle\vec{R}^2\rangle}=0.535$ fm and $\sqrt{\langle r^2\rangle_p}=2.599$ fm from Eq. (r2_p-MF_corr).
- For $^{208}$Pb, the same ratio is 1.022, with $\sqrt{\langle\vec{R}^2\rangle}=0.199$ fm and $\sqrt{\langle r^2\rangle_p}=5.429$ fm.
- The convergence condition for the $q$-integration in the density correction is $\langle\Phi|\vec{R}^2|\Phi\rangle < (1.12\ \mathrm{fm})^2$, derived from the Gaussian expansion method.
- For $^{96}$Zr, the tensor force enhances the spherical minimum stability, with the energy difference between spherical and deformed minima smaller in $[E-E^{(\mathrm{TN})}](q_0)$ than in $E(q_0)$ with M3Y-P6.

## Context
This work extends the SCMF approach using semi-realistic interactions developed by Nakada and collaborators (e.g., Nakada 2008, Suzuki-Nakada-Miyahara 2016) to study exotic nuclei far from stability. It builds on earlier studies of tensor-force effects in deformed nuclei by Bender et al. (2007) using Skyrme interactions, and on the island of inversion phenomena for $N=20$ and $N=28$ nuclei (Warburton-Becker-Brown 1990, Utsuno-Otsuka-Mizusaki-Honma 1999). The results enable systematic investigation of shape coexistence and magicity breakdown across the nuclear chart, particularly for Zr isotopes where shape evolution from spherical to prolate at $N\approx 60$ is reproduced.
