# Renormalization of ${}^1S_0$ NN scattering amplitude in effective field theory

> Cutoff regularized subleading order ${}^1S_0$ NN potential of effective field theory (EFT) is iterated using Lippmann-Schwinger equation. It is shown that the scattering amplitudes calculated in cutoff and subtractively renormalized EFT are equal up to the accuracy of performed calculations. Non-perturbative renormalization, where part of divergences are absorbed into two contact interaction coupling constants with subsequent removal of regularization is also performed. Cutoff and dimensional regularizations both lead to finite but different results within this scheme.

# Renormalization of ${}^1S_0$ NN scattering amplitude in effective field theory

## Key claim

The authors show that cutoff-regularized effective field theory (EFT) and subtractively renormalized EFT produce ${}^1S_0$ NN scattering amplitudes that are equal up to the order of accuracy of the performed calculations, provided the cutoff is chosen of the order of the large scale parameter governing the coupling constants. The formal inconsistency of Weinberg's power counting is argued to be irrelevant if power counting is applied to renormalized diagrams rather than to unrenormalized diagrams and counter-terms separately.

## Method

The subleading order ${}^1S_0$ NN potential, consisting of two contact interactions (C and C2) plus the one-pion exchange potential, is iterated using the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a factorized cutoff regulator function. The authors derive a closed expression for the scattering amplitude and perform non-perturbative renormalization by absorbing divergences into the two bare coupling constants, fixing them from finiteness conditions at p=0 and p=iμ. They compare this scheme with a BPHZ subtractive renormalization procedure where all divergences are subtracted, and with cutoff EFT where the cutoff is kept finite.

## Key numbers

- The regularization function used is χ(Λ,p) = Λ^4 / (Λ^2 + p^2)^2, with Λ the cutoff parameter.
- The one-pion exchange potential strength is given by α_π = g_A^2 m_π^2 / (8π f_π^2), where g_A is the axial coupling, m_π the pion mass, and f_π the pion decay constant.
- The divergent parts of the loop functions scale as: G1(p) ~ a0 Λ + a1 ln Λ + a2(p); G2(p) ~ b0 Λ^3 + b1 Λ^2 + b2(p)Λ + b3(p) ln Λ + b4(p); G3(p) ~ c0 Λ^5 + c1 Λ^4 + c2(p)Λ^3 + c3(p)Λ^2 + c4(p) ln Λ + c5(p); V2(p) ~ v Λ + d(p), where all coefficients are independent of Λ.
- The renormalization condition for the cutoff EFT requires C2/C^2 = C2R/CR^2 to ensure finiteness of the first-order term in the Λ → ∞ limit.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Gegelia 1999) on renormalization in Weinberg's EFT approach to nuclear forces, and addresses the formal inconsistency raised by Kaplan (1996) that higher-order counter-terms contribute to the renormalization of lower-order diagrams. The paper demonstrates that cutoff EFT, as used successfully by van Kolck (1999) and Epelbaum (2000, 2001) for few-nucleon systems, reproduces the results of subtractively renormalized EFT up to the working order, suggesting that the practical success of Weinberg's approach is not accidental. The results enable confidence in cutoff EFT calculations for nuclear systems where subtractive renormalization is technically infeasible.
