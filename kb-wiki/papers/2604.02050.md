# Gauge invariant momentum broadening of hard probes in glasma

> We compute the transport coefficient $\hat q$ which quantifies the transverse momentum broadening of hard probes passing through the evolving glasma from the earliest stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We use a proper-time expansion method which is designed to study the glasma at very early times. In our earlier calculations of $\hat q$ we used an approximation that greatly simplifies the complexity of the calculation but introduces a violation of gauge invariance. Based on these results we argued that the glasma plays an important role in jet quenching. In this paper we have used a gauge invariant formulation to calculate $\hat q$. The results for the momentum broadening coefficient are quantitatively very close to those of our previous simplified version of the calculation and confirm our earlier conclusion about the importance of the glasma contribution to jet quenching.

## Key claim

The gauge-invariant calculation of the transport coefficient \(\hat q\) for hard probes in glasma yields results that are "quantitatively very close" to the authors' previous simplified calculation where the Wilson link operator was set to unity, confirming that the glasma contribution to jet quenching is important despite the earlier gauge-violating approximation.

## Method

The authors compute \(\hat q\) from two-point correlators of chromodynamic fields with a Wilson line inserted for gauge invariance, using a proper-time expansion of glasma fields in the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) limit with infinite transverse nuclei. The glasma potentials are expanded in powers of proper time \(\tau\), the Yang-Mills equations are solved recursively, and averages are taken over Gaussian color charge distributions using the Glasma Graph approximation. The Wilson line is expanded in powers of the gauge potential and then in proper time, and the calculation is carried out to cumulative fourth order in the proper time expansion.

## Key numbers

* The average Wilson line \(\langle\!\langle W\rangle\!\rangle\) at second order is approximately 0.84, and the full fourth-order result shows \(1 - \langle\!\langle W\rangle\!\rangle_4\) is small over the range where the proper time expansion is valid (\(t \lesssim 0.06\) fm).
* The infrared cutoff is set to \(m = \Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 0.2\) GeV, the saturation scale \(Q_s = 2\) GeV, the coupling constant \(g = 1\), and \(N_c = 3\).
* The proper time expansion is considered valid for times \(t \lesssim 0.06\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of \(\hat q\) in glasma (Carrington 2020, 2022, 2021, 2023) which used the approximation \(W = \mathds{1}\) and thus violated gauge invariance. It also builds on the suggestion by Mrowczynski (2017) that the glasma phase contributes significantly to jet quenching. The results confirm earlier conclusions from analytic proper-time expansion calculations and numerical simulations by Ipp (2020) and Avramescu (2023, 2024). The gauge-invariant calculation enables more reliable predictions for jet quenching from the earliest stage of heavy-ion collisions, though the authors note that the fourth-order result is only partially gauge invariant due to the truncation of the proper-time expansion.
