# Accessing nucleon transversity with one-point energy correlators

> We propose a novel probe of the nucleon's transversity distribution, $h_1^q$, using the one-point energy correlator (OPEC), an infrared-and-collinear safe jet substructure observable. We demonstrate that in transversely polarized $p^{\uparrow}p$ collisions, the OPEC exhibits a single-spin asymmetry (SSA) with a clean $\sin(ϕ_s - ϕ_n)$ angular dependence. This method probes SSA over a much wider kinematic range in the angular scale $θ_n$ compared to traditional measurements of hadron transverse momentum~$j_\perp$, establishing a complementary and systematically distinct channel to study the nucleon's three-dimensional structure at RHIC and the future Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the one-point energy correlator (OPEC), an infrared-and-collinear safe jet substructure observable, provides a novel and direct probe of the nucleon's transversity distribution \(h_1^q\) in transversely polarized \(p^{\uparrow}p\) collisions, exhibiting a single-spin asymmetry with a \(\sin(\phi_s - \phi_n)\) angular dependence. The claim is hedged by the fact that the method is presented as complementary and systematically distinct from traditional hadron-in-jet measurements, and that current parametrizations do not yet allow resolution of TMD evolution effects, with the dominant uncertainty arising from the Collins fragmentation function rather than the transversity PDF itself.

## Method

The authors derive a factorization formula for the OPEC in inclusive jet production for transversely polarized proton-unpolarized proton scattering, expressing the spin-dependent structure function \(Z_{UT}\) in terms of the transversity PDF \(h_1^a\), the transversely polarized OPEC fragmenting jet function \(\mathcal{J}^{c}_{1,\perp}\), and spin-dependent hard functions. They match the OPEC fragmenting jet functions onto collinear fragmentation functions via an operator product expansion involving Bessel functions and perturbative Sudakov factors, and compute the single-spin asymmetry \(A_{UT}^{\sin(\phi_s - \phi_n)} = Z_{UT}/Z_{UU}\) at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy using two frameworks: a full TMD evolution framework and the JAM3D-22 Gaussian modeling approach.

## Key numbers

* At \(\sqrt{s}=510\) GeV and \(p_T=32.3\) GeV with a typical cut \(z_h=0.1\), the accessible \(j_\perp\) range \((0.1,1)\) GeV corresponds to \(\theta_n\simeq(0.03,0.3)\) rad, while modern detectors achieve resolutions of order \(10^{-3}\) rad and the EIC is expected to reach \(10^{-4}\) rad.
* The SSA \(A_{UT}^{\sin(\phi_s - \phi_n)}\) is evaluated at \(\sqrt{s}=510\) GeV with \(p_T=32.3\) GeV and at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV with \(p_T=13.3\) GeV, corresponding to kinematic settings studied by the STAR Collaboration.
* The non-perturbative Sudakov factor uses \(b_{\rm max} = 1.5\) GeV\(^{-1}\) in the \(b_*\)-prescription.
* The \(p_T\) distribution is obtained by integrating over \(\theta_n \in [0,0.1]\) rad.

## Context

This work extends the study of energy-energy correlators (EEC) into spin-dependent jet substructure, specifically targeting the transversity PDF which has previously been accessed via the Collins effect in SIDIS and proton-proton collisions (Collins 1992, Kang 2015, JAM3D-22 global analysis by Gamberg 2022). The OPEC approach offers a theoretically cleaner channel than traditional hadron-in-jet measurements because it reduces model dependence from non-perturbative fragmentation functions by projecting onto a single moment in the energy fraction \(z_h\), and it enables access to angular scales more than an order of magnitude finer than previously probed at RHIC and the future Electron-Ion Collider.
