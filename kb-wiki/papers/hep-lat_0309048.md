# Lattice study on kaon nucleon scattering length in the I=1 channel

> Using the tadpole improved clover Wilson quark action on small, coarse and anisotropic lattices, $KN$ scattering length in the I=1 channel is calculated within quenched approximation. The results are extrapolated towards the chiral and physical kaon mass region. Finite volume and finite lattice spacing errors are also analyzed and a result in the infinite volume and continuum limit is obtained which is compatible with the experiment and the results from Chiral Perturbation Theory.

## Key claim

Using quenched lattice QCD with tadpole improved clover Wilson quarks on small, coarse, anisotropic lattices, the authors obtain a kaon-nucleon scattering length in the I=1 channel that is compatible with experiment and chiral perturbation theory, but the final result carries a large statistical error and is stated as a preliminary study.

## Method

The authors used the tadpole improved clover Wilson quark action on anisotropic lattices with spatial lattice spacings between 0.2 fm and 0.4 fm and physical lattice sizes from 1.2 fm to 4.0 fm, within the quenched approximation. They computed KN correlation functions from quark propagators obtained via the Multi-mass Minimal Residual algorithm, extracted the energy shift of the two-particle state using Luscher's formula, and performed a two-step chiral extrapolation (first in up/down quark mass, then in strange quark mass) followed by infinite volume and continuum limit extrapolations.

## Key numbers

* The final continuum-extrapolated result for the quantity F = a0^(1) m_rho^2 / mu_KN is (-1.6 +/- 5.4).
* The corresponding KN scattering length in the I=1 channel is a0^(1) = (-0.17 +/- 0.59) fm.
* The experimental result for a0^(1) is (-0.33) fm.
* The chiral perturbation theory results for a0^(1) are (-0.398) fm at tree level, (+0.457) fm at next order, and (-0.389) fm at next-to-next order.
* The current algebra prediction for F is approximately -3.686, using m_rho ~ 770 MeV and f_K ~ 113 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice study of pion-pion scattering in the I=2 channel (Chuan et al., 2002) to the kaon-nucleon system, using the same anisotropic lattice technique. It contrasts with earlier lattice calculations of hadron scattering by Fukugita et al. (1995) which were performed at specific parameters without chiral or continuum extrapolation. The result enables future lattice QCD studies with higher statistics and finer lattices to obtain a more precise determination of the KN scattering length, which is needed because chiral perturbation theory predictions for this channel suffer from large corrections at each order.
