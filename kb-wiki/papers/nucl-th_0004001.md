# Generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn Sum Rule for the Proton-Neutron Difference in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Applications of the chiral expansion to generalize the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule for finite $Q^2$ are discussed. The observation of several authors that the corrections to the leading order contributions are large and limit the applicability to a very small range in $Q^2$, are valid when considering the sum rule for protons and neutrons, separately. When using the proton-neutron difference, the chiral expansion may be valid to considerably higher momentum transfers where hadronic degrees of freedom at large distances may connect up with quark and gluon degrees of freedom. This could mark the first time that nucleon structure is decribed by fundamental theory from large to small distances.

# Generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn Sum Rule for the Proton-Neutron Difference in Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim
The chiral expansion of the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule to finite $Q^2$ is severely limited to $Q^2 < 0.05$ GeV$^2$ when applied to protons and neutrons separately, but the proton-neutron difference yields a five times larger range of applicability, potentially allowing the connection of hadronic degrees of freedom at large distances with quark and gluon degrees of freedom at small distances.

## Method
The author applies heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to compute the next-to-leading order (NLO) $Q^2$ dependence of the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule, expressed through the spin-dependent structure function $G_1(\nu, Q^2)$ and the Compton amplitude $\bar{S}_1(0, Q^2)$. The proton-neutron difference is constructed to cancel the dominant $\Delta(1232)$ resonance contribution and other isospin 3/2 resonance contributions that are difficult to treat in HBChPT, yielding a much reduced $Q^2$ slope compared to the individual isospin channels.

## Key numbers
- The NLO slope at $Q^2=0$ for the proton is $6.85 Q^2$ (GeV$^2$) and for the neutron is $5.54 Q^2$ (GeV$^2$), while the proton-neutron difference slope is $1.31 Q^2$ (GeV$^2$), a factor of approximately five smaller.
- The chiral expansion for protons and neutrons separately is limited to $Q^2 < 0.05$ GeV$^2$, whereas the proton-neutron difference is valid to a five times larger $Q^2$ range.
- For the neutron, the NLO calculation predicts a sign change at $Q^2 \approx 0.16$ GeV$^2$, pointing opposite to the high $Q^2$ data.
- The isovector and isoscalar anomalous magnetic moments used are $\kappa_V = 3.706$ and $\kappa_S = -0.120$.

## Context
This work extends the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule calculations of Ji and Osborne (1999) and Ji, Kim, and Osborne (2000), who found large NLO corrections limiting the chiral expansion to very small $Q^2$ for individual nucleons. The proton-neutron difference approach is motivated by analogy with the Bjorken sum rule (1966), which has been experimentally tested with good accuracy, in contrast to the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule (1974) for protons and neutrons separately. The author suggests that combining this chiral expansion with higher twist operator product expansion of pQCD down to $Q^2 = 0.5$ GeV$^2$ and QCD lattice calculations could bridge the description of nucleon structure from large to small distances.
