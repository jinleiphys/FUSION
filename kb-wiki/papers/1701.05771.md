# Strongly screening on electron capture for Nuclides $^{52, 53, 59, 60}$Fe by the Shell-Model Monte Carlo method in pre-supernova

> The death of the massive stars due to supernova explosion is a key ingredient in stellar evolution, stellar population synthesis. The electron capture (EC) plays a vital role in supernovae explosions. According to the Shell-Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method, basing onthe Random Phase Approximation (RPA) and Linear Response Theory Model (LRTM), we study the strongly screening EC rates of nuclides $^{52, 53, 59, 60}$Fe in presupernova. The results show that the screening rates can decrease about 18.66\%. We compare our results with those of Fuller et al. (FFN), Aufderheide et al. (AUFD), and Nabi et al. (NKK)in the case with and without strong electron screening(SES). For the case without SES, our calculations are in very good agreement with those of AUFD in relatively high density surroundings and the maximum error is within 0.35\% at $ρ_7=100$ (e. g., even-even nuclei $^{60}$Fe). However, for odd-A nuclei$^{59}$Fe, our rates are close to one, one, two order magnitude smaller than those of FFN, AUFD, and NKK. For the case with SES, our screening results are about three, two orders magnitude, and 7.27\% lower than those of FFN, AUFD, NKK for $^{60}$Fe, respectively, and it is lower about two, two orders magnitude, and 12.42\% than those of FFN, AUFD, NKK for odd-A nuclide $^{59}$Fe.

## Key claim

The strong electron screening (SES) reduces electron capture (EC) rates for the iron isotopes $^{52,53,59,60}$Fe by up to about 18.66%, with the magnitude of the reduction depending strongly on the nuclear species (even-even vs. odd-A) and on the temperature and density conditions in the pre-supernova environment.

## Method

The authors computed EC rates without screening using the Shell-Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method to obtain Gamow-Teller strength distributions, combined with the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) for the cross sections. For the case with strong electron screening, they applied the Linear Response Theory Model (LRTM) to calculate the screening potential for a relativistic degenerate electron liquid, which modifies the electron energy and threshold in the rate integral. The resulting rates were compared with the earlier calculations of Fuller et al. (FFN), Aufderheide et al. (AUFD), and Nabi et al. (NKK), both with and without screening.

## Key numbers

* The screening rates for the studied Fe isotopes can decrease by about 18.66% relative to the unscreened rates.
* For the even-even nucleus $^{60}$Fe without SES, the authors' rates agree with those of AUFD to within a maximum error of 0.35% at a density $\rho_7 = 100$ (where $\rho_7$ is density in units of $10^7$ g/cm$^3$).
* For the odd-A nucleus $^{59}$Fe without SES, the rates of FFN, AUFD, and NKK are about one, one, and two orders of magnitude larger, respectively, than the authors' rates.
* With SES for $^{60}$Fe, the authors' screening rates are about three orders of magnitude lower than FFN's, two orders lower than AUFD's, and 7.27% lower than NKK's.
* With SES for odd-A $^{59}$Fe, the authors' screening rates are about two orders of magnitude lower than both FFN's and AUFD's, and 12.42% lower than NKK's.
* The total Gamow-Teller strength $B(\text{GT})_{\text{tot}}$ for the ground state ($0^+$) of $^{60}$Fe is 9.47 MeV, and for the first excited state ($2^+$) it is 8.19 MeV.
* The peak of the GT$^+$ strength distribution $S_{\text{GT}^+}$ for $^{60}$Fe reaches 1.562 MeV$^{-1}$ at 0.5 MeV excitation in the daughter $^{60}$Mn for the ground state, and 0.223 MeV$^{-1}$ at 3.40 MeV for the first excited state.

## Context

This work extends the study of electron capture on iron-group nuclei in pre-supernova conditions, which was pioneered by Fuller et al. (FFN) and Aufderheide et al. (AUFD) using the Brink Hypothesis, and by Nabi et al. (NKK) using the pn-QRPA method. The authors improve upon these earlier approaches by employing the SMMC method to obtain a more realistic average of the Gamow-Teller strength distribution, and by incorporating strong electron screening via the LRTM, which was previously applied by Bravo et al. and Liu et al. The results provide more accurate EC rates for stellar evolution and supernova simulations, particularly highlighting the significant suppression of rates for heavier, neutron-rich isotopes like $^{59,60}$Fe at lower temperatures and higher densities.
