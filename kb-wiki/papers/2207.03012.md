# gamma-UPC: Automated generation of exclusive photon-photon processes in ultraperipheral proton and nuclear collisions with varying form factors

> The automated generation of arbitrary exclusive final states produced via photon fusion in ultraperipheral high-energy collisions of protons and/or nuclei is implemented in the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO and HelacOnia Monte Carlo codes. Cross sections are calculated in the equivalent photon approximation using $γ$ fluxes derived from electric dipole and charge form factors, and incorporating hadronic survival probabilities. Multiple examples of $γγ$ cross sections computed with this setup, named gamma-UPC, are presented for proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) at the Large Hadron Collider and Future Circular Collider. Total photon-fusion cross sections for the exclusive production of spin-0,2 resonances (quarkonia, ditauonium, and Higgs boson; as well as axions and gravitons), and for pairs of particles ($J/ψJ/ψ$, WW, ZZ, Z$γ$, $t\bar{t}$, HH) are presented. Differential cross sections for exclusive dileptons and light-by-light scattering are compared to LHC data. This development paves the way for the upcoming automatic event generation of any UPC final state with electroweak corrections at next-to-leading-order accuracy and beyond.

## Key claim
The paper implements automated generation of arbitrary exclusive final states from photon fusion in ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) of protons and nuclei within the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO and HelacOnia Monte Carlo codes, using equivalent photon approximation fluxes that incorporate hadronic form factors and survival probabilities; the abstract glosses over the fact that the current MadGraph5_aMC@NLO implementation for proton-proton UPCs does not explicitly include proton survival probabilities, meaning those cross sections should be considered as upper values for high-mass exclusive processes.

## Method
The authors implemented two photon flux models (electric dipole form factor and charge form factor) with associated hadronic survival probabilities into the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO and HelacOnia event generators. For proton-proton collisions, the survival probability is computed from the elastic scattering amplitude using an exponential parametrization with energy-dependent slope fitted to experimental data; for proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions, the survival probability uses Glauber-model nuclear thickness functions and inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections. The code generates exclusive final states via photon fusion by convolving the elementary cross section with the two-photon differential luminosity including impact-parameter-dependent photon fluxes and the non-hadronic-overlap condition.

## Key numbers
- Maximum photon-photon center-of-mass energy for Pb-Pb UPCs at LHC (5.52 TeV nucleon-nucleon): 160 GeV
- Maximum photon-photon center-of-mass energy for p-Pb UPCs at LHC (8.8 TeV): 2.6 TeV
- Maximum photon-photon center-of-mass energy for pp UPCs at LHC (14 TeV): 4.5 TeV
- Maximum photon-photon center-of-mass energy for pp UPCs at FCC (100 TeV): 30.5 TeV
- Maximum photon energy for pp at LHC: 2.45 TeV (from proton with Lorentz factor 7450 and charge radius 0.7 fm)
- Maximum photon energy for Pb-Pb at LHC: 80 GeV (from lead ion with Lorentz factor 2960 and charge radius 7.1 fm)
- Integrated luminosity for Pb-Pb at LHC: 5 nb^-1; for pp at LHC: 150 fb^-1; for pp at FCC: 1 ab^-1
- Elastic slope fit parameters for pp: A = 9.81 GeV^-2, B = 0.211 GeV^-2, C = 0.0185 GeV^-2, predicting b0 = 20.6 GeV^-2 at LHC (14 TeV) and 24.5 GeV^-2 at FCC (100 TeV)

## Context
This work extends the capabilities of the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO and HelacOnia generators, which previously could only generate photon-induced processes using inclusive photon PDFs (such as LuxQED) or a simplified "improved Weizsacker-Williams" approach without survival probabilities for proton-proton UPCs. It enables automatic event generation of any exclusive photon-photon final state (including Standard Model processes like light-by-light scattering, dileptons, dibosons, and Higgs boson production, as well as beyond-Standard-Model signals such as axion-like particles and gravitons) that previously required dedicated generators like Starlight (Klein 2016) or Superchic (Harland-Lang 2020) with only pre-coded processes. The development paves the way for next-to-leading-order electroweak corrections and automatic event generation of any UPC final state.
