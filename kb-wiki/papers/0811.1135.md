# Isospin mixing of isospin-projected Slater determinants: formalism and preliminary applications

> We report on the development of a new theoretical tool that allows for isospin projection of Slater determinants and we present its first applications. In particular, we determine the isospin mixing in ground states of N=Z nuclei and discuss its dependence on the size of the harmonic-oscillator basis used in the calculations. We also discuss the unphysical contribution to the isospin mixing caused by the spurious isospin-symmetry breaking inherent to the mean-field approach. We show that these contributions may be as large as 30% of the value of the isospin-mixing parameter.

## Key claim

The paper reports a new theoretical tool for isospin projection of Slater determinants, but the isospin-mixing parameter alpha_C cannot be calculated with absolute precision greater than plus or minus 0.002 (or relative precision greater than plus or minus 4 percent) because the value does not stabilize at the maximum harmonic-oscillator basis size of N0=16 shells.

## Method

The authors perform Hartree-Fock calculations using Skyrme functionals plus exact Coulomb exchange to obtain isospin-symmetry-broken Slater determinants for N=Z nuclei. They then apply one-dimensional isospin projection operators to extract good-isospin states, and finally rediagonalize the total Skyrme-plus-Coulomb Hamiltonian in the basis of projected states to obtain the correct isospin mixing. The Coulomb interaction is decomposed into isoscalar, isovector, and isotensor components to compute both diagonal and off-diagonal matrix elements between projected states.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-mixing parameter alpha_C in 100Sn shows a significant variation with harmonic-oscillator basis size and does not stabilize at N0=16 shells, limiting absolute precision to plus or minus 0.002 and relative precision to plus or minus 4 percent.
- The spurious mean-field contribution to isospin mixing can be as large as 30 percent of the value of the isospin-mixing parameter in 100Sn.
- The isospin mixing in N=Z nuclei increases from a fraction of a percent in 16O to about 4-5 percent depending on the variant of the calculation.
- Calculations with N0=12 harmonic-oscillator shells provide a reasonable estimate for the total binding energy even for 100Sn.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent mean-field approach with isospin projection, building on earlier isospin-mixing calculations by Dobaczewski (1995) and the HFODD code development by Dobaczewski (2000, 2004). The formalism enables future simultaneous isospin and angular momentum projections, with the ultimate goal of calculating isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to the Fermi matrix element for superallowed 0+ to 0+ beta decays, as pursued by Hardy (2005) and Towner (2008).
