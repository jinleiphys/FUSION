# Muon capture rates within the projected QRPA

> The conservation of the number of particles within the QRPA plays an important role in the evaluation muon capture rates in all light nuclei with A \precsim 30 . The violation of the CVC by the Coulomb field in this mass region is of minor importance, but this effect could be quite relevant for medium and heavy nuclei studied previously. The extreme sensitivity of the muon capture rates on the 'pp' coupling strength in nuclei with large neutron excess when described within the QRPA is pointed out. We reckon that the comparison between theory and data for the inclusive muon capture is not a fully satisfactory test on the nuclear model that is used. The exclusive muon transitions are much more robust for such a purpose.

## Key claim

The number-projected QRPA (PQRPA) yields better agreement with experimental inclusive muon capture rates than the plain QRPA for light nuclei with A < 30, but the authors caution that agreement between theory and data for inclusive muon capture is not a fully satisfactory test of the nuclear model; exclusive muon transitions are much more robust for such a purpose.

## Method

The authors performed systematic calculations of muon capture rates for nuclei with masses 12 ≤ A ≤ 56 (12C, 20Ne, 24Mg, 28Si, 40Ar, 52Cr, 54Cr, 56Fe) using the number-projected quasiparticle random phase approximation (PQRPA). Single-particle energies were taken from relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations with DD-ME2 parametrization, the residual interaction was approximated by a delta-force with different coupling constants in the particle-hole, particle-particle, and pairing channels, and the particle-particle coupling strength ratio t was set to zero for these near-symmetric N≅Z nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For 56Fe, the total muon capture rate including the CVC-violating Coulomb correction is Λ = 4056 × 10^3 s^-1, compared to Λ = 4260 × 10^3 s^-1 without the correction
- For 12C, the PQRPA inclusive muon capture rate is Λ = 37 × 10^3 s^-1, compared to the experimental value of (38 ± 1) × 10^3 s^-1
- For 12C exclusive transitions: 1+_1 state Λ = 8.80 × 10^3 s^-1 (experiment: 6.00 ± 0.40 × 10^3 s^-1); 2+_1 state Λ = 0.20 × 10^3 s^-1 (experiment: 0.21 ± 0.10 × 10^3 s^-1); 2-_1 state Λ = 0.60 × 10^3 s^-1 (experiment: 0.18 ± 0.10 × 10^3 s^-1); 1-_1 state Λ = 0.85 × 10^3 s^-1 (experiment: 0.62 ± 0.20 × 10^3 s^-1)
- The Coulomb correction ΔE_Coul ranges from 3.8 MeV in 12C to 9.8 MeV in 56Fe
- For 208Pb, ΔE_Coul + E_B^μ ≅ 39.0 MeV, which would reduce the natural parity operator contribution to approximately 13% of the uncorrected value

## Context

This work extends previous PQRPA studies of weak observables in the 12B-12C-12N triad by Krmpotic et al. (2002) and Samana et al. (2011), and contrasts with the relativistic QRPA calculations of Marketin et al. (2009) and the RPA+BCS model of Zinner et al. (2006). The authors demonstrate that particle-number projection is essential for light nuclei, and that exclusive muon transitions provide a more stringent test of nuclear models than inclusive rates, enabling future extraction of the induced pseudoscalar coupling g_P from transitions such as 0+ → 0- in 16O and 40Ca.
