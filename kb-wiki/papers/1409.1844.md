# Simultaneous description of low-lying positive and negative parity bands in heavy even-even nuclei

> The low-lying spectra including the first few excited positive and negative parity bands of some heavy even-even nuclei from the rare earth and actinide mass regions are investigated within the framework of the symplectic Interacting Vector Boson Model with Sp(12,$R$) dynamical symmetry group. Symplectic dynamical symmetries allow the change of the number of excitation quanta or phonons building the collective states providing for larger representation spaces and richer subalgebraic structures to incorporate more complex nuclear spectra. The theoretical predictions for the energy levels and the electromagnetic transitions between the collective states of the ground state band and $K^π=0^{-}$ band are compared with experiment and some other collective models incorporating octupole and/or dipole degrees of freedom. The energy staggering which is a sensitive indicator of the octupole correlations in the even-even nuclei is also calculated and compared with experiment. The results obtained for the energy levels, energy staggering and transition strengths reveal the relevance of the used dynamical symmetry of the model for the simultaneous description of both positive and negative parity low-lying collective bands.

## Key claim

The symplectic Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM) with Sp(12,R) dynamical symmetry can simultaneously describe low-lying positive and negative parity bands in heavy even-even nuclei without introducing additional collective degrees of freedom, provided that the parity of each state is defined as \(\pi = (-1)^{N_n}\) (where \(N_n\) is the number of n-bosons) and the basis states are truncated to "stretched" SU(3) multiplets.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian diagonal in the basis of the Sp(12,R) dynamical symmetry chain, using invariant operators of the subgroups U(6), SU(3), U(2), and SO(3). The model space is truncated to "stretched" SU(3) states of the type \((\lambda,\mu)=(\lambda_0,\mu_0+k)\) with \(k=0,2,4,\ldots\); the parity of each state is assigned as \(\pi = (-1)^{N_n}\) by treating p-bosons as pseudovectors and n-bosons as vectors. Energy levels are obtained from the analytic formula \(E(N,L,T,T_0)= aN + bN^2 + \alpha_3 T(T+1) + \beta_3 L(L+1) + \alpha_1 T_0^2\), with parameters fitted to each nucleus. Transition probabilities for intraband E2 and interband E1 transitions are calculated using tensor operators constructed from the boson creation and annihilation operators.

## Context

This work extends the symplectic IVBM approach previously applied to ground state and \(K^\pi=0^-\) bands in Ganev (2013) to now include the first few positive parity bands (ground state, \(\beta\), \(\gamma\)) and negative parity bands (\(K^\pi=0^-\), \(1^-\), \(2^-\)) simultaneously. It provides an algebraic alternative to the spdf-IBM (which requires f and p bosons) and to geometrical models with octupole deformation \(\beta_3\); the results are compared with spdf-IBM calculations by Babilon (for \({}^{152}\)Sm, \({}^{154}\)Sm) and with critical point symmetry (CPS) approaches by Bizzeti and Bonatsos. The model successfully reproduces "beat patterns" in the energy staggering of \({}^{226}\)Ra, which the SU(3) limit of spdf-IBM cannot describe, and enables future studies of octupole correlations in heavy nuclei without introducing additional boson types.
