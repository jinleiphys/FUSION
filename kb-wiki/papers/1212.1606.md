# Few-baryon interactions from lattice QCD

> We report the recent progress on the determination of three-nucleon forces (3NF) in lattice QCD. We utilize the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function to define the potential in quantum field theory, and extract two-nucleon forces (2NF) and 3NF on equal footing. The enormous computational cost for calculating multi-baryon correlators on the lattice is drastically reduced by developing a novel contraction algorithm (the unified contraction algorithm). Quantum numbers of the three-nucleon (3N) system are chosen to be (I, J^P)=(1/2,1/2^+) (the triton channel), and we extract 3NF in which three nucleons are aligned linearly with an equal spacing. Lattice QCD simulations are performed using N_f=2 dynamical clover fermion configurations at the lattice spacing of a = 0.156 fm on a 16^3 x 32 lattice with a large quark mass corresponding to m(π)= 1.13 GeV. Repulsive 3NF is found at short distance.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first lattice QCD determination of three-nucleon forces (3NF) in the triton channel, finding a repulsive short-range 3NF, but only for a specific geometric configuration (three nucleons aligned linearly with equal spacing) and at a single heavy quark mass corresponding to m(π)=1.13 GeV, not at the physical point.

## Method

The authors employ the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function approach (HAL QCD method) to define energy-independent potentials in quantum field theory, extracting both two-nucleon forces (2NF) and three-nucleon forces (3NF) on equal footing from lattice QCD correlators. They use the time-dependent HAL QCD method to avoid ground-state saturation requirements, and apply a novel unified contraction algorithm that reduces computational cost for three-nucleon correlators by a factor of 192. The 3NF are extracted by subtracting the 2NF contributions from the total three-nucleon potential in the Schrödinger equation, using a channel that is anti-symmetric in spin/isospin to automatically eliminate parity-odd 2NF contributions.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.156 fm, on a 16^3 x 32 lattice, corresponding to a physical spatial box of (2.5 fm)^3
- Pion mass: m(π) = 1.13 GeV; nucleon mass: m(N) = 2.15 GeV; delta mass: m(Δ) = 2.31 GeV
- Inverse lattice spacing: a^(-1) = 1.269(14) GeV
- 598 configurations of N_f=2 dynamical clover fermions at β=1.95 used for the analysis
- Unified contraction algorithm reduces computational cost by a factor of 192 for ^3H and ^3He correlators, and a factor of 20736 for the ^4He correlator
- Sink time range analyzed: 5 ≤ (t-t_0)/a ≤ 10, with results shown at (t-t_0)/a = 7.5, 8.0, and 8.5

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method, previously applied to two-nucleon forces (Ishii 2006, Aoki 2008) and hyperon interactions (Inoue 2010), to three-nucleon systems for the first time. It directly addresses the phenomenological need for short-range repulsive 3NF components that are introduced ad hoc in models such as the Tucson-Melbourne model (Coon 2001) and Urbana/Illinois models (Pieper 2001), and which are required to explain properties of high-density nuclear matter (Akmal 1998, Nishizaki 2002). The results enable future first-principles calculations of nuclear structure and neutron star properties, though systematic studies at lighter quark masses and with finer lattices are needed before reaching the physical point.
