# Light-front CQM calculations of baryon electromagnetic form factors

> The parameter-free predictions for the $N - P_{11}(1440)$ and $N - P_{33}(1232)$ electromagnetic transition form factors, obtained within our light-front constituent quark model using eigenfunctions of a baryon mass operator which includes a large amount of configuration mixing, are reported. The effects due to small components in the baryon wave functions, such as S'- and D-wave, are also investigated.

## Key claim
The paper reports parameter-free predictions for the N–P11(1440) and N–P33(1232) electromagnetic transition form factors using light-front constituent quark model eigenfunctions that include substantial configuration mixing, but the authors acknowledge that an effective one-body current approximation cannot fully describe "small" form factors like E1 or S1 for the N–Δ transition, indicating the need for at least a two-body current term.

## Method
The authors solved the Capstick–Isgur mass operator (linear confinement plus one-gluon-exchange) by expanding baryon eigenstates onto a large harmonic oscillator basis (up to 20 HO quanta) and applying the Rayleigh–Ritz variational principle. They then computed electromagnetic form factors in the light-front formalism using the plus component of the current with q⁺ = 0, approximating the current as a sum of one-body constituent quark currents with quark form factors fixed from pion and nucleon data. For the N–Δ transition, two prescriptions (I and II) were used to extract the G₁, G₂, G₃ form factors from the light-front matrix elements.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark mass m_u = m_d = 0.220 GeV, taken from Capstick and Isgur (1986).
- The Roper resonance has a mixed-symmetry S' component probability P_{S'}^{Roper} = 9.3%.
- The Δ resonance has a D-wave component probability P_D^{Δ} = 1.1%.
- The E1/M1 ratio for N–Δ transition is approximately 5% for Q² up to a few GeV/c².
- The S1/M1 ratio for N–Δ transition is approximately 10% for Q² up to a few GeV/c².

## Context
This work extends the light-front constituent quark model approach previously developed by the same authors (Cardarelli et al., 1996) for nucleon and pion form factors, applying it to baryon resonance transitions. The results show significant improvement over non-relativistic calculations (Hybrid model) and relativistic calculations using gaussian wave functions (Capstick and Keister, 1995), bringing predictions closer to experimental data from the Particle Data Group and other analyses. The paper enables future work on two-body current contributions and suggests that the ratio G_M^{N–Δ}/(G_M^p – G_M^n) could probe SU(6) breaking effects in nucleon and Δ wave functions with reduced model dependence.
