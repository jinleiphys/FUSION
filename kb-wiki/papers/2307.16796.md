# Multi-particle correlations, cumulants, and moments sensitive to fluctuations in rare-probe azimuthal anisotropy in heavy ion collisions

> Correlations of two or more particles have been an essential tool for understanding the hydrodynamic behavior of the quark-gluon plasma created in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions. In this paper, we extend that framework to introduce a mathematical construction of multi-particle correlators that utilize correlations between arbitrary numbers of particles of interest (e.g. particles selected for their strangeness, heavy flavor, and conserved charges) and inclusive reference particles to estimate the azimuthal anisotropies of rare probes. To estimate the fluctuations and correlations in the azimuthal anisotropies of these particle of interest, we use these correlators in a system of cumulants, raw moments, and central moments. We finally introduce two classes of observables that can compare the fluctuations in the azimuthal anisotropies of particles of interest with reference particles at each order.

## Key claim

The paper extends the existing multi-particle correlation framework to construct observables that can measure not just the root-mean-square azimuthal anisotropy of rare probes (such as high-\(p_T\) jets, heavy-flavor hadrons, or particles selected by conserved charges), but also higher-order event-by-event fluctuations of those anisotropies, under the assumption that the particles of interest and reference particles are distributed symmetrically around their respective event planes and that the sum of positively and negatively correlated harmonics is zero.

## Method

The authors generalize the standard multi-particle correlator formalism to include an arbitrary number of particles of interest (POI) alongside reference particles, writing correlators as products of flow vectors \(V_n = v_n e^{in\Psi_n}\) and \(V'_n = v'_n e^{in\Psi'_n}\). They then define raw moments as event-by-event averages of these correlators, identify nontrivial stochastic variables by requiring that subsets of harmonics sum to zero, and introduce a normalization scheme that divides the raw moment by the product of the first moments of each stochastic variable. Finally, they propose two new classes of observables, central moments of arbitrary order in POI angle dependence and estimators for the contribution to a cumulant or moment for an arbitrary number of particles, to compare fluctuations in the azimuthal anisotropies of POI with those of reference particles at each order.

## Key numbers

- The paper defines the correlator \(\langle 1'+2k-1 \rangle = \langle e^{in(\psi_1 + \phi_1 .. \phi_{k-1} - \phi_{k} - ... - \phi_{2k-1})} \rangle\) with normalization factor \(\frac{(N-2k+1)!}{N'(N!)}\), which approximates the joint moment of order \(2k-2\) in \(v_n\) and first order in \(V'_n V_n\).
- The normalized Symmetric Cumulant is expressed as \(NSC(v_n^2,v_m^2) = \frac{\langle v_n^2 v_m^2 \rangle}{\langle v_n^2 \rangle \langle v_m^2 \rangle} - 1 = N\mu_{1,1}(v_n^2,v_m^2) - 1\), where the raw moment \(\mu_{1,1}\) is normalized by the product of the first moments.
- An example correlator \(\langle\langle 2'+2 \rangle\rangle_{2,4|3',3'} = \langle v_2 v_4 (v'_3)^2 \cos{(2\Psi_2 + 4\Psi_4 - 6\Psi'_3)} \rangle\) is given, showing a raw moment sensitive to a product of three flow harmonics with an event-plane angle dependence.

## Context

This work extends the differential cumulant framework developed by Borghini, Dinh, and Ollitrault (2000, 2001) and the multi-particle cumulant methods of Bilandzic, Voloshin, and collaborators (2010, 2013) to include an arbitrary number of particles of interest. It also generalizes the Symmetric Cumulants of Moravcova et al. (2020) and the Asymmetric Cumulants of Bilandzic et al. (2021) to rare probes. The formalism enables future experimental measurements of higher-order fluctuations in the azimuthal anisotropy of jets, heavy-flavor hadrons, and particles carrying conserved charges, which could constrain models of jet energy loss, heavy-quark diffusion, and baryon stopping in the quark-gluon plasma.
