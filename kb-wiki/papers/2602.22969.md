# The Delta-isobar masquerade: intrahadronic phase transitions and their quark-mimicking signatures in neutron stars

> We investigate the conditions under which $Δ(1232)$ isobars trigger a first-order phase transition within purely hadronic neutron-star matter, using the SW4L relativistic mean-field parametrization. For scalar-vector coupling differences $0.15 \lesssim x_{σΔ} - x_{ωΔ} \lesssim 0.2$ and $x_{σΔ} \gtrsim 1.3$, the onset of $Δ^-$ resonances produces a van der Waals-like instability driven by a self-amplifying feedback in the scalar meson sector, in which the $Δ^-$ particle fraction acts as the order parameter of a Landau-type transition. A Maxwell construction yields a sharp density discontinuity at baryon densities $n_b \sim (1.3$-$2)\,n_0$, separating a $Δ$-free outer core from a $Δ$-rich inner core. The resulting neutron-star sequences satisfy all current multimessenger constraints: maximum masses $M_{\rm max} \approx 2.15$-$2.25\,M_\odot$, radii $R_{1.4} \approx 11$-$12$ km, and tidal deformabilities $Λ_{1.4} \approx 190$-$480$, compatible with NICER observations and GW170817. We compute, for the first time for a $Δ$-induced interface, the $\ell = 2$ composition $g$-mode eigenfrequencies, obtaining $ν_g \sim 400$-$1100$ Hz with gravitational-wave damping times $τ_g \sim 10^3$-$10^9$ s. These frequencies overlap quantitatively with those predicted for hadron-quark phase-transition interfaces, demonstrating that the mass-radius ``knee'', reduced tidal deformability, and $g$-mode spectrum conventionally regarded as signatures of quark deconfinement can be reproduced by a purely intrahadronic mechanism. This extends the masquerade problem from static observables to the domain of gravitational-wave asteroseismology, implying that a future detection of a discontinuity $g$-mode alone would not suffice to identify quark matter in neutron-star cores.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a first-order phase transition driven by Delta(1232) isobars in purely hadronic neutron-star matter can produce mass-radius "knees," reduced tidal deformabilities, and g-mode oscillation spectra that quantitatively mimic the signatures conventionally attributed to quark deconfinement, extending the masquerade problem from static observables to gravitational-wave asteroseismology. This result holds specifically for scalar-vector coupling differences between 0.15 and 0.2 and scalar coupling ratios greater than 1.3 in the SW4L relativistic mean-field parametrization.

## Method

The authors employed the SW4L density-dependent relativistic mean-field parametrization, extending it to include all four Delta(1232) isobar charge states alongside nucleons, hyperons, and leptons under cold, beta-equilibrated, charge-neutral conditions. They performed a targeted scan of the poorly constrained meson-Delta coupling ratios (x_sigmaDelta and x_omegaDelta) to identify parameter space where Delta formation triggers a thermodynamic instability, then applied a Maxwell construction to determine the phase transition properties and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron-star structure. For the first time for a Delta-induced interface, they computed the l=2 composition g-mode eigenfrequencies and gravitational-wave damping times using linear perturbation theory.

## Context

This work extends the findings of De Oliveira (2007, 2016), Lavagno and Pigato (2019), and Raduta (2021), who first reported Delta-driven thermodynamic instabilities in different hadronic frameworks, by providing a Landau-type interpretation with the Delta- fraction as order parameter and by computing the previously unexplored g-mode spectrum. The results directly confront the masquerade problem identified by Alford (2004), showing that the oscillation spectrum cannot distinguish between a hadron-hadron Delta transition and a hadron-quark deconfinement transition, implying that a future detection of a discontinuity g-mode alone would not suffice to identify quark matter in neutron-star cores. The models are broadly consistent with recent model-agnostic Bayesian constraints from Gorda (2022), Brandes (2023), Essick (2023), and Komoltsev (2024), and the authors show that microphysics-informed correlations among transition properties can populate regions of equation-of-state space undersampled by generic parametrizations.
