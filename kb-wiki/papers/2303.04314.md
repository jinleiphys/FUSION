# Analogies between hadron-in-jet and dihadron fragmentation

> We describe the formal analogies in the description of the inclusive production in hard processes of hadron pairs (based on dihadron fragmentation functions) and of a single hadron inside a jet (based on hadron-in-jet fragmentation functions). Since several observables involving dihadron fragmentation functions have been proposed in the past, we are able to suggest new interesting observables involving hadron-in-jet fragmentation functions, in lepton-hadron deep-inelastic scattering and hadronic collisions.

# Analogies between hadron-in-jet and dihadron fragmentation

## Key claim

The formal analogies between dihadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs) and hadron-in-jet transverse-momentum-dependent fragmentation functions (jTMDFFs) allow the transfer of knowledge from one mechanism to the other, enabling the suggestion of new observables for hadron-in-jet fragmentation in lepton-hadron deep-inelastic scattering and hadronic collisions, but only in the specific kinematic regime where the jet is defined using the standard jet axis (not a recoil-free algorithm) and where the transverse momentum of the hadron inside the jet satisfies Λ_QCD ≲ |j_⟂| ≪ |P_T| r.

## Method

The authors compare the factorized cross sections for inclusive dihadron production (A + B → (C₁ C₂) + X) and inclusive hadron-in-jet production (A + B → (Jet C) + X) in unpolarized and transversely polarized proton-proton collisions, using leading-order collinear factorization for the dihadron case and hybrid factorization for the hadron-in-jet case. They establish a set of correspondence rules between the kinematic variables and fragmentation functions of the two processes by directly comparing the cross section expressions: the pair (z_J, z_h) maps onto (z_hh, ζ), the transverse momentum j_⟂ maps onto R_⟂, and the unpolarized and polarized jTMDFFs map onto the corresponding DiFFs up to a factor of 4π.

## Key numbers

- The hadron-pair invariant mass is defined as P² = M_hh², with the relative momentum R = (P₁ − P₂)/2 and R_⟂² = −R_⟂².
- The momentum fractions are defined as z_hh = (P₁⁻ + P₂⁻)/p⁻ = z₁ + z₂ and ζ = (z₁ − z₂)/z_hh for dihadron fragmentation, and z_J = J⁻/p⁻, z_h = P_h⁻/J⁻ for hadron-in-jet fragmentation.
- The kinematic condition for the hadron-in-jet factorization to apply is Λ_QCD ≲ |j_⟂| ≪ |P_T| r, where |P_T| is the hard scale and r is the jet radius.
- At leading order, the jTMDFF reduces to D₁^q(z_h, j_⟂²; |P_T|) multiplied by δ(1 − z_J), where D₁^q is the standard single-hadron TMD fragmentation function.

## Context

This work extends the formal understanding of fragmentation functions by drawing explicit analogies between the dihadron fragmentation formalism developed by Bacchetta, Radici, and collaborators (2002-2004) and the hadron-in-jet fragmentation formalism developed by Kang and collaborators (2017). The dihadron approach has been used to extract transversity via the interference fragmentation function H₁^∠ in SIDIS, e⁺e⁻ annihilations, and hadronic collisions (Radici 2015, 2018), while the hadron-in-jet approach enables access to TMD fragmentation functions in processes where TMD factorization is broken, such as hadronic collisions. The correspondence rules established here enable the authors to suggest new observables: the inclusive production of two back-to-back hadrons-in-jet in unpolarized proton-proton collisions (which could give access to jets initiated by linearly polarized gluons) and the inclusive production of a hadron-in-jet in SIDIS up to subleading twist (which could give access to the chiral-odd PDF e(x) related to the nucleon scalar charge).
