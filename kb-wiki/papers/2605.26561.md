# Spin-Dependent Nucleon-Nucleus Interactions Constrained by Neutron Observables and Their Impact on Near-Barrier Proton Fusion

> We investigate the role of spin-dependent nucleon-nucleus interactions in nuclear reactions. To this end, we use neutron spin observables to constrain the dominant central spin-spin form factors and then apply the corresponding like-channel interactions to near-barrier fusion in the $p+{}^{93}$Nb system. The interactions are constructed within a folding framework based on a finite-range effective nucleon-nucleon force and organized in terms of radial form factors associated with their spin-spin, tensor, and spin-orbit components. Neutron spin observables in the $n+{}^{27}$Al, $n+{}^{59}$Co, and $n+{}^{93}$Nb target systems are analyzed within a distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) framework to constrain the sign and normalization in the central spin-spin parts of the radial form factors and to examine the assembled operator conventions. The calculation reproduces the observed sign systematics of the neutron spin observables for the three targets, indicating that the essential spin-dependent structure is properly incorporated. The unlike-channel (neutron-proton) interaction constrained by neutron scattering is then reconstructed for the corresponding like-channel (proton-proton) interaction and applied to a coupled-channels description of near-barrier fusion for the $p+{}^{93}$Nb system. The resultant spin-dependent interactions lead only to a weak modification of the effective barrier and change the fusion cross section by about $0.01$-$0.03\%$ in the present calculation. These results show that the corresponding real spin-dependent correction in the like-channel is strongly suppressed in near-barrier fusion in $p+{}^{93}\mathrm{Nb}$. The present work thus connects the neutron-scattering constraints on the operator conventions with the fusion calculation in the proton channel, and quantifies the magnitude of the corresponding real spin-dependent correction in near-barrier fusion.

## Key claim

The real spin-dependent correction in the like-channel (proton-proton) interaction, when constrained by neutron spin observables and applied to near-barrier proton fusion in the p+93Nb system, is strongly suppressed and changes the fusion cross section by only about 0.01-0.03%, contrary to the possibility that such corrections might be significant.

## Method

The authors constructed spin-dependent nucleon-nucleus interactions within a folding framework using the finite-range Tokyo-4-Yukawa (T4Y) effective nucleon-nucleon force, organized into radial form factors for central spin-spin, tensor, and spin-orbit components. Neutron spin observables for n+27Al, n+59Co, and n+93Nb were analyzed within a distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) framework to constrain the sign and normalization of the central spin-spin form factors and to verify the operator conventions. The unlike-channel (neutron-proton) interaction constrained by neutron scattering was then reconstructed for the corresponding like-channel (proton-proton) interaction and applied to a coupled-channels description of near-barrier fusion for the p+93Nb system.

## Key numbers

- The spin-dependent interactions change the fusion cross section by about 0.01-0.03% for the p+93Nb system near the barrier.
- The maximal change in the neutron spin observable σss/(PtPb) from including the conventional projectile spin-orbit term in the distorting potential is about 2.4 mb in the low-energy peak region.
- The valence-proton separation energies used for the bound-state wave functions are 8.271 MeV for 27Al, 7.364 MeV for 59Co, and 6.043 MeV for 93Nb (Wang 2021).
- The Woods-Saxon geometry parameters for the bound-state wave functions are r0=1.20 fm and a0=0.65 fm.
- For 93Nb with the stretched g9/2 valence configuration, the ratio of form factors is F32(ss)=(3/2)F12(ss).

## Context

This work extends the folding-model studies of polarized-neutron scattering by McAbee (1990) and McAbee (1986), who established the operator basis and DWBA reduction for spin-dependent form factors, and applies those conventions to proton-induced fusion for the first time. The neutron scattering data for 27Al, 59Co, and 93Nb targets from Stamp (1967), Nagamine (1970), Heeringa (1977, 1989), Gould (1986), and Soderstrum (1992) provide the experimental constraints. The results enable future work to quantify whether the remaining low-energy residual in the 93Nb system arises from reaction dynamics, distortion, or absorption effects rather than from missing operator components.
