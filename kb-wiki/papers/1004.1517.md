# Coexistence of 'alpha+ 208Pb' cluster structures and single-particle excitations in 212Po

> Excited states in 212Po have been populated by alpha transfer using the 208Pb(18O,14C) reaction at 85MeV beam energy and studied with the EUROBALL IV gamma multidetector array. The level scheme has been extended up to ~ 3.2 MeV excitation energy from the triple gamma coincidence data. Spin and parity values of most of the observed states have been assigned from the gamma angular distributions and gamma -gamma angular correlations. Several gamma lines with E(gamma) < 1 MeV have been found to be shifted by the Doppler effect, allowing for the measurements of the associated lifetimes by the DSAM method. The values, found in the range [0.1-0.6] ps, lead to very enhanced E1 transitions. All the emitting states, which have non-natural parity values, are discussed in terms of alpha-208Pb structure. They are in the same excitation-energy range as the states issued from shell-model configurations.

## Key claim

The paper claims the first experimental evidence of "pure" alpha-cluster states in a heavy nucleus (²¹²Po), specifically states with non-natural parity that are connected to yrast states by enhanced E1 transitions; the authors hedge that these states are in the same excitation-energy range as shell-model configurations, meaning the cluster and single-particle structures coexist rather than being mutually exclusive.

## Method

Excited states in ²¹²Po were populated via the alpha-transfer reaction ²⁰⁸Pb(¹⁸O,¹⁴C) at 85 MeV beam energy using a thick ²⁰⁸Pb target, with gamma rays detected by the EUROBALL IV array. The level scheme was extended to ~3.2 MeV excitation energy using triple-gamma coincidence data, and spin-parity assignments were made from gamma angular distributions and gamma-gamma angular correlations. Lifetimes of states with gamma energies below 1 MeV were measured using the Doppler Shift Attenuation Method (DSAM), and conversion coefficients from intensity balances were used to determine multipolarities of low-energy transitions.

## Key numbers

- Lifetimes of several excited states measured in the range [0.1–0.6] ps via DSAM, leading to enhanced E1 transitions
- Ground state of ²¹²Po has half-life 0.299(2) μs (pure alpha emitter)
- Isomeric state at 2.9 MeV excitation energy has half-life 45 s and spin-parity 18⁺
- Velocity of recoiling ²¹²Po nuclei determined as v/c = 1% from Doppler shift of the 780.4 keV transition
- Cross section of the exit channel leading to ²¹²Po estimated at ~10–20 mb, ~10–20% of the fusion-fission cross section
- Half-life of the 8⁺ yrast state measured as 14(1) ns (adopting the most accurate previous value of 14.7(3) ns)
- Angular distribution attenuation coefficient α₂ ~ 0.7 determined from yrast transitions
- Theoretical ADO ratios of 0.85 and 1.30 expected for pure stretched dipole and quadrupole transitions, respectively

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of ²¹²Po from transfer reactions such as ²⁰⁸Pb(⁹Be,αn) by Po87 and from fusion-evaporation reactions using radioactive beams by Po03 and Ga05. The paper builds on the finding that shell-model calculations fail to reproduce the alpha-decay rate of the ground state by an order of magnitude, while cluster-model calculations by Va92 (which include ~0.3 alpha clustering in the ground state) succeed. The new non-natural parity states provide the first experimental evidence for "pure" alpha-cluster states in heavy nuclei, enabling future studies of the oscillatory motion of the alpha-core distance as a source of such states.
