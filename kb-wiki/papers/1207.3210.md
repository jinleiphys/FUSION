# Binding vs. scattering length: $η$ in light nuclei

> The possibility of etamesic nuclei remains an open problem in nuclear physics until now. Various calculations give contradictory predictions even for the lightest real nucleus $^3$He. In this paper we present the connection of the binding energy and width to the complex scattering length for heavier nuclei than this in the hope that, with knowledge of the final state interaction this could be useful in searches of possible bound states.

# Binding vs. scattering length: η in light nuclei

## Key claim
The paper presents a phenomenological connection between the complex scattering length and the binding energy/width of possible η-mesic nuclei for s-states in nuclei heavier than ³He, but the authors stress that the effective range must also be considered for a consistent analysis, and that the sign of the real part of the scattering length is crucial as a tell-tale of a bound state.

## Method
The authors use a simple optical model with a potential proportional to the nuclear density profile, freely varying the strength parameters to cover the complex scattering length plane. They solve the Schrödinger equation for scattering and search for poles in the homogeneous Lippmann-Schwinger integral equation for bound states, generating contour plots that connect binding energies and half-widths to the complex scattering length for ¹²C, ⁴He, and ²⁴Mg.

## Key numbers
- For ¹²C, the most "eastern" point of the zero binding curve is at scattering length a = (-2.525 + i 1.102) fm.
- For ⁴He, the turning point for weak and strong potentials is at about a = (-1.5 + i 0.9) fm, compared to (-2.5 + i 1.1) fm for carbon.
- For a real potential, the scattering length corresponding to 1 MeV binding changes from -7.9 fm for carbon to -7.4 fm for ⁴He.
- For ²⁴Mg, taking a claimed bound state at 13 MeV binding with half-width 5 MeV gives a complex scattering length of approximately a = (-3.1 + i 0.6) fm with effective range r₀ ≈ (1.6 - i 0.6) fm.
- The condition for binding is given by Re[a³(a* - r₀*)] > 0, which is more precise than the simpler rule |a_R| > a_I.

## Context
This work extends the investigation of Sibirtsev (2004) for ³He to heavier light nuclei (⁴He, ¹²C, ²⁴Mg), providing systematic contour maps that connect binding energies and widths to complex scattering lengths. The results are intended to help experimental searches for η-mesic nuclei, such as those by the GEM collaboration (Budzanowski 2009) and the MAMI experiment (Pfeiffer 2004), by allowing final-state interaction data to predict where bound states might be found. The paper also highlights the importance of including the effective range, as demonstrated by the differing results of Smyrski (2007) and Mersmann (2007) for the η³He final state.
