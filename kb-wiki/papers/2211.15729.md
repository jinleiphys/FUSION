# Directed flow in a baryonic fireball

> Directed flow of identified hadrons in a baryon rich fireball is an interesting observable as it is expected to probe several physics aspects: the initial three dimensional baryon profile in the thermalised fireball that can be treated as an input for the hydrodynamic evolution, the nature of baryon dissipation current and baryon transport coefficients, the QCD equation of state at finite baryon densities as well as the nature of phase transition between the quark gluon and hadronic phases. Particularly, the mid-rapidity slope of the rapidity dependence of directed flow of protons have been proposed as a sensitive observable to several of these physics aspects while a consistent description of the splitting in directed flow of baryon and its anti-particle has been a challenge. In this work, we propose a suitable ansatz of the initial condition for baryon deposition. When such a baryon deposition ansatz is coupled to a tilted fireball, we manage to find parameter space that can describe the directed flow of identified hadrons including the elusive baryon antibaryon splitting of directed flow. Further, we demonstrate that future measurements of baryon antibaryon directed flow at larger rapidities have the potential to constrain the baryon diffusion coefficient.

## Key claim
The authors propose a new ansatz for the initial transverse baryon density profile that includes a binary collision component, controlled by a free parameter omega, which allows independent tuning of the baryon tilt relative to the energy density tilt. This ansatz, when coupled to a tilted fireball model, can simultaneously describe the directed flow of pions, protons, and antiprotons including the baryon-antibaryon splitting, but the current experimental data cannot distinguish between different values of the baryon diffusion coefficient.

## Method
The authors initialize energy density using a two-component tilted fireball model (participant plus binary collision sources) and baryon density using a new two-component ansatz with a free parameter omega controlling the binary collision contribution. These initial conditions are evolved using the MUSIC relativistic hydrodynamic code with a lattice QCD equation of state at finite baryon density (NEoS-BQS), including shear viscosity and baryon diffusion current. The Cooper-Frye freeze-out is performed at constant energy density 0.26 GeV/fm^3, followed by hadronic transport with UrQMD. Parameters are tuned to reproduce charged particle pseudorapidity distributions, net proton rapidity distributions, and directed flow of identified hadrons in Au+Au collisions at 19.6 and 200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out energy density: epsilon_f = 0.26 GeV/fm^3
- Specific shear viscosity: C_eta = 0.08
- Transverse smearing width: 0.4 fm
- Hardness factor alpha = 0.1 for Au+Au at 19.6 GeV, alpha = 0.14 for Au+Au at 200 GeV
- Initial time tau_0 = 1.8 fm for 19.6 GeV, tau_0 = 0.6 fm for 200 GeV
- Initial energy density epsilon_0 = 1.55 GeV/fm^3 for 19.6 GeV, epsilon_0 = 8.0 GeV/fm^3 for 200 GeV
- Tilt parameter eta_m = 0.8 for 19.6 GeV, eta_m = 2.0-2.2 for 200 GeV
- Baryon profile parameter omega = 0.13-0.15 for 19.6 GeV, omega = 0.25-0.30 for 200 GeV
- Baryon diffusion coefficient parameter C_B tested at values 0 and 1

## Context
This work extends the tilted fireball approach of Bozek (2010) for energy density to include a separately tunable baryon density profile, motivated by microscopic models like LEXUS (Jeon, 1997; De, 2022). It addresses the challenge of describing baryon-antibaryon splitting in directed flow that previous approaches (Shen, 2020; Denicol, 2018) could not consistently reproduce. The results demonstrate that future measurements of baryon and antibaryon directed flow at larger rapidities (beyond |y| ~ 1.5) could constrain the baryon diffusion coefficient, which current data at mid-rapidity cannot distinguish.
