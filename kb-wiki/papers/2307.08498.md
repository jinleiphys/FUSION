# Illuminating early-stage dynamics of heavy-ion collisions through photons at RHIC BES energies

> Heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} \sim 10$ GeV probe the QCD phase diagram at large baryon densities. Because the longitudinal Lorentz contraction is small at these collision energies, understanding the dynamics during the early phase of the collision is essential for the subsequent modeling of the system evolution and for constraining the QGP transport properties at finite baryon densities. Direct photons provide undistorted information on early-stage dynamics. We model relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies with a hybrid dynamical approach consisting of a 3D-Glauber initial state followed by viscous hydrodynamics and hadronic transport (MUSIC + UrQMD). The implemented thermal photon emission takes into account the enhancement from finite baryon chemical potentials. We show that direct photon spectra and their anisotropic flow coefficients have a strong sensitivity to the early stage of heavy-ion collisions. Thus, they provide constraints on QGP dynamics complementary to those obtained from hadronic observables.

## Key claim

Direct photon spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies (specifically \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 19.6\) GeV) are much more sensitive to the early-stage dynamics of heavy-ion collisions than hadronic observables are, but this sensitivity is only revealed when using a dynamical initialization scheme that accounts for the finite crossing time of the nuclei (about 2 fm/\(c\)) rather than the conventional instantaneous initialization at a fixed proper time.

## Method

The authors used the iEBE-MUSIC hybrid framework with a (3+1)D dynamical initialization based on the 3D MC-Glauber model, which continuously deposits energy-momentum and net baryon charge into hydrodynamic fields as the two nuclei pass through each other. The subsequent evolution was modeled with viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC) followed by hadronic transport (UrQMD), and thermal photon emission was computed including enhancement from finite baryon chemical potentials (up to \(\langle \mu_B \rangle \sim 200\) MeV at mid-rapidity). The dynamical initialization results were compared to an instantaneous initialization scenario where hydrodynamic fields are set at a fixed proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.5\) fm/\(c\).

## Key numbers

- Central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 19.6\) GeV: the overlapping time between incoming nuclei reaches about 2 fm/\(c\).
- With dynamical initialization, the averaged fireball temperature rises from zero to a peak of about 240 MeV in central Au+Au collisions at 19.6 GeV.
- The mid-rapidity fireball at 19.6 GeV reaches a net baryon chemical potential \(\langle \mu_B \rangle \sim 200\) MeV.
- Finite \(\mu_B\) enhancements increase thermal photon production by 10-20% in central Au+Au collisions at 19.6 GeV.
- The switch from QGP to hadronic photon production rates occurs at a fixed temperature \(T_\mathrm{sw} = 155\) MeV.
- Thermal photons with transverse momenta below 1.5 GeV mostly come from the late-stage hadronic phase.
- Prompt photon production decreases faster than thermal radiation at high \(p_T\) (\(p_T \gtrsim 2-3\) GeV) as collision energy decreases.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid dynamical approach of Shen (2017) and Shen (2022) to lower RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, where longitudinal Lorentz contraction is small and the finite crossing time of the nuclei becomes important. It directly contrasts the dynamical initialization scheme with the conventional instantaneous initialization used in most prior hydrodynamic simulations. The results enable combined phenomenological analyses of hadron and photon measurements to constrain QGP transport properties at finite baryon chemical potential, complementing the hadron-only studies that dominate the field.
