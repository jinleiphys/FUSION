# Pomeranchuk instability and Bose condensation of scalar quanta in a Fermi liquid

> We study excitations in a normal Fermi liquid with a local scalar interaction. Spectrum of bosonic scalar-mode excitations is investigated for various values and momentum dependence of the scalar Landau parameter $f_0$ in the particle-hole channel. For $f_0 >0$ the conditions are found when the phase velocity on the spectrum of the zero sound acquires a minimum at a non-zero momentum. For $-1<f_0 <0$ there are only damped excitations, and for $f_0<-1$ the spectrum becomes unstable against a growth of scalar-mode excitations (a Pomeranchuk instability). An effective Lagrangian for the scalar excitation modes is derived after performing a bosonization procedure. We demonstrate that the Pomeranchuk instability may be tamed by the formation of a static Bose condensate of the scalar modes. The condensation may occur in a homogeneous or inhomogeneous state relying on the momentum dependence of the scalar Landau parameter. Then we consider a possibility of the condensation of the zero-sound-like excitations in a state with a non-zero momentum in Fermi liquids moving with overcritical velocities, provided an appropriate momentum dependence of the Landau parameter $f_0(k)>0$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for a scalar Landau parameter \(f_0 < -1\) the usual Pomeranchuk instability (exponential growth of density fluctuations) may be tamed not by a liquid-gas phase transition but by the formation of a static Bose condensate of scalar excitation modes, provided the condensate amplitude is stabilized by repulsive self-interaction.

## Method

The authors solve the Landau kinetic equation for the particle-hole scattering amplitude in a normal one-component Fermi liquid with a local scalar interaction, using the Lindhard function to describe the particle-hole propagator. They expand the scalar Landau parameter \(f_0(k)\) as \(f_0(x) \approx f_{00} + f_{02} x^2\) (with \(x = k/p_F\)) and derive the spectrum of bosonic scalar-mode excitations for repulsive (\(f_0 > 0\)), moderately attractive (\(-1 < f_0 < 0\)), and strongly attractive (\(f_0 < -1\)) regimes. They then perform a bosonization via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to derive an effective Lagrangian for the scalar modes, including a quartic self-interaction term, and analyze the possibility of static Bose condensation.

## Key numbers

* For \(f_{00} \ll 1\), the zero-sound phase velocity parameter \(s_0\) is given by \(s_0 = 1 + C[1 + (4+5f_{00})(C/2f_{00}) + (24+52f_{00}+29f_{00}^2)(C/2f_{00})^2]\) where \(C = (2/e^2)\exp(-2/f_{00})\); for \(f_{00} \gg 1\), the asymptotic solution is \(s_0 = \sqrt{f_{00}/3}\).
* The coefficient \(s_2\) in the zero-sound dispersion \(\omega_s(k) = v_F k (s_0 + s_2 x^2)\) becomes negative when \(f_{02} < -\alpha\) with \(\alpha = f_{00}^2/[12(s_0^2-1)^2]\); for \(f_{00}=1\) this requires \(f_{02} \lesssim -10\), for \(f_{00}=2\) requires \(f_{02} \lesssim -2.6\), and for \(f_{00}=3\) requires \(f_{02} \lesssim -1.7\).
* For the unstable regime \(f_{00} < -1\) with \(0 < z_f \ll 1\) (where \(z_f = 1 - 1/|f_{00}|\)), the instability increment is \(\omega_d(k) \approx i(2/\pi) v_F k [z_f - k^2/(12 p_F^2)]\), with maximum at \(k_m = 2 p_F \sqrt{z_f}\) and peak value \(-i\omega_m = (8/3\pi) v_F p_F z_f^{3/2}\).
* The spinodal instability growth rate for the hydrodynamic mode is \(-i\omega_m = v_F p_F (|f_{00}|-1)/(6 m_F \sqrt{c})\), and the zero-sound-like mode grows more rapidly when the surface tension coefficient satisfies \(c > \pi^2 |f_{00}|^{3/2} / [256 m_F^2 (|f_{00}|-1)]\).

## Context

This work extends the classic Fermi liquid theory of Landau (1956) and the Pomeranchuk stability analysis (1958) by proposing a novel stabilization mechanism for the scalar-channel instability (\(f_0 < -1\)) via Bose condensation, rather than the usual liquid-gas phase transition described by Maxwell construction or the pasta-phase formation studied by Ravenhall (1983) and Maruyama (2005) for neutron star crusts. The results enable future studies of zero-sound condensation in moving Fermi liquids (e.g., neutron stars, He-II, cold atomic gases) with overcritical velocities, building on the Landau critical velocity concept discussed by Pitaevskii (1984) and Voskresensky (1995).
