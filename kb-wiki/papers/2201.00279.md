# Influence of moments of inertia on transverse wobbling mode in odd-mass nuclei

> The reported transverse wobbling band in odd-mass $^{105}$Pd has been reinvestigated by the triaxial particle rotor model. Employing different parameter sets of moment of inertia (MOI), several calculated results could be in good agreement with the experimental data, which show distinct modes of rotational excitation, respectively. These modes are sensitive to the ratio between the MOI at intermediate and short axis. With the increase of this ratio, a wobble about the short axis of the total angular momentum is gradually changed to a wobble about the intermediate axis. In addition, it is exhibited that precession and tunneling are two aspects of the quantum wobbling motion. The tunneling aspect dominates in the yrare states of $^{105}$Pd. The present results in $^{105}$Pd show the complexity of the transverse wobbling mode.

# Influence of moments of inertia on transverse wobbling mode in odd-mass nuclei

## Key claim

The transverse wobbling band previously reported in odd-mass ¹⁰⁵Pd can be reproduced by the triaxial particle rotor model using multiple different parameter sets of moments of inertia, but the resulting rotational modes differ qualitatively depending on the ratio of the moment of inertia at the intermediate axis to that at the short axis (𝒥ₘ/𝒥ₛ). The paper shows that the available experimental data may not be sufficient to uniquely identify the transverse wobbling mechanism, and that the tunneling aspect of quantum wobbling dominates in the yrare states of ¹⁰⁵Pd, rather than pure precession.

## Method

The triaxial particle rotor model (PRM) was applied to ¹⁰⁵Pd assuming a ν(1h₁₁/₂)¹ configuration with fixed deformation parameters β = 0.27 and γ = 25°. Four different parameter sets of angular-momentum-dependent moments of inertia (MOI) were adopted, with the ratio 𝒥ₘ/𝒥ₛ taking values of 1:0.9 (rigid-body-like), 1:0.7, 1:0.5, and 1:0.3 (hydrodynamical-like). The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a strong-coupling basis, and the resulting energy spectra, wobbling energies, reduced transition probability ratios B(E2)ₒᵤₜ/B(E2)ᵢₙ and B(M1)/B(E2)ᵢₙ, and azimuthal probability distributions of the total and valence-nucleon angular momenta were calculated and compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters for ¹⁰⁵Pd: β = 0.27, γ = 25°
- Four MOI ratio sets used: 𝒥ₘ/𝒥ₛ = 1:0.9 (set A), 1:0.7 (set B), 1:0.5 (set C), 1:0.3 (set D)
- For γ = 25°, the ratio 𝒥ₘ/𝒥ₛ is approximately 1:0.9 for rigid-body MOI and 1:0.3 for hydrodynamical MOI
- Intrinsic quadrupole moment Q = 3.0 eb
- Gyromagnetic ratios: g_R = Z/A = 0.438, g_ν(h₁₁/₂) = −0.209, with a quenching factor of 0.36 applied to g
- Wobbling energies reproduced from I = 13/2 ħ to 29/2 ħ; increasing trend above I ≥ 33/2 ħ described with one-quasiparticle configuration using parameter sets C and D
- Classical trajectory calculations performed for spin 13 ħ with energies 1.48, 1.48, 1.49, and 1.51 MeV for four different MOI ratios

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate about transverse wobbling (TW) in odd-mass nuclei, which was first proposed by Frauendorf and Dönau (2014) to explain decreasing wobbling energies with increasing spin. The paper directly reinvestigates the TW band in ¹⁰⁵Pd previously reported by the experimental group (Pd105, 2015). It addresses theoretical disputes raised by Tanabe (2017, 2018) regarding the stability of TW and the alternative interpretation of tilted precession bands proposed by Lawrie (2020). The results show that the ratio of moments of inertia critically determines whether the system exhibits wobble about the short axis (TW), wobble about the intermediate axis, or planar tilted rotation, and that further experimental measurements such as lifetimes or g-factor measurements are needed to resolve the ambiguity.
