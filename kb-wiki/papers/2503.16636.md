# Decays of $N^*$ and $Δ^*$ resonances into $Nρ$, $Δπ$, and $Nσ$

> The decays of $N^*$ and $Δ^*$ resonances into $Nρ$, $Δπ$ and $Nσ$ final states are studied in a coupled-channel analysis of data on pion- and photo-induced reactions. Improvements in the fit were observed when new resonance contributions were introduced. Frequencies for the intermediate isobars $Δ(1232)π$, $Nρ$, $Nσ$ are reported.

# Decays of $N^*$ and $\Delta^*$ resonances into $N\rho$, $\Delta\pi$, and $N\sigma$

## Key claim

The paper reports branching ratios for decays of $N^*$ and $\Delta^*$ resonances into $N\rho$, $\Delta\pi$, and $Nf_0(500)$ from a coupled-channel analysis, but the authors note that branching ratios depend on the definition used, and the best definition requires integration over the mass distribution of the produced resonance rather than a simple ratio of partial widths.

## Method

The Bonn-Gatchina partial wave analysis performed a coupled-channel fit to a large database of pion- and photo-induced reactions, including data on $\gamma p \to \pi^+\pi^- p$, $\gamma p \to p\pi^0\pi^0$, $\pi^-p \to n\pi^0\pi^0$, and $\pi^-p \to n\pi^+\pi^-$ from multiple experiments. The energy-dependent amplitude was described using a D-matrix approach based on dispersion relations, with resonant and non-resonant terms including t- and u-channel exchanges, and the fit was performed via an event-based likelihood method for three-body final states combined with $\chi^2$ minimization for two-body data.

## Key numbers

- The database includes 400 million events from CLAS on $\gamma p \to \pi^+\pi^- p$ (CLAS 2018, CLAS 2024), with 1.842 million events available event-wise as Dalitz plots
- The analysis covers center-of-mass energies from 1320 to 1930 MeV for the pion-induced reactions from Manley et al. (1984), with bin widths varying from 20 to 40 MeV
- For resonances in the 1900 MeV region and above, the missing inelasticity channel can describe up to about 50% of the width of the observed states
- The Blatt-Weisskopf form-factors used a radius of 0.8 fm
- The $\pi N$ elastic scattering amplitudes used for constraints came from either the Karlsruhe analysis KA84 (Koch 1985) or the GWU analysis WI08 (Workman 2012)

## Context

This work extends previous coupled-channel analyses of $N^*$ and $\Delta^*$ resonances, particularly the Manley et al. (1984) analysis of bubble chamber data on $\pi^-p \to N\pi\pi$ which identified nine $N^*$ and five $\Delta^*$ resonances, and the earlier Bonn-Gatchina analyses of $\gamma p \to p\pi^0\pi^0$ (Sarantsev 2007, Thoma 2007, CBELSA/TAPS 2015). The new analysis incorporates recent high-statistics CLAS data on $\gamma p \to \pi^+\pi^- p$ (CLAS 2018, CLAS 2024) and polarized data (Crede 2024), and provides branching ratios for cascade decays that enable tests of three-quark wave function models where four $N^*$ resonances show evidence of two simultaneously excited oscillators.
