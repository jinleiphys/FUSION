# Full-folding optical potential for preequilibrium nucleon scattering at low energies

> The real part of the optical potential for the nucleon-nucleus scattering at lower energies (E_i<100MeV) has been calculated including nucleonic and mesonic form factors by a double folding approach. Realistic density- and energy-dependent effective NN-interactions DDM3Y, BDM3Y and HLM3Y based on the Reid and Paris potentials are used in this respect. The effects of the nucleon density distribution and the average relative momentum on the folded potential have been analysed. A good agreement with the phenomenological potential of Lagrange-Lejeune, as well as with the parametrization of Jeukenne-Lejeune-Mahaux for both neutron and proton double-folded potentials is obtained. The results indicate that the strongly simplified model interactions used in preequilibrium reaction theory neglect important dynamical details of such processes.

# Full-folding optical potential for preequilibrium nucleon scattering at low energies

## Key claim
The real part of the optical potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering at incident energies below 100 MeV can be reliably calculated using a double-folding approach that includes both nucleonic and mesonic form factors, provided that density- and energy-dependent effective NN-interactions (DDM3Y, BDM3Y, HLM3Y) are used. The strongly simplified single-Yukawa interactions commonly employed in preequilibrium reaction theory neglect important dynamical details, particularly the density dependence that is essential for reproducing phenomenological potentials.

## Method
The authors performed double-folding calculations of the real optical potential for neutron and proton scattering on 93Nb, using density- and energy-dependent effective NN-interactions (DDM3Y1, BDM3Y1, HLM3Y) derived from the Reid and Paris NN-potentials via g-matrix elements. They incorporated nucleonic form factors to regularize the short-range behavior and used the local momentum approximation with the Negele-Vautherin density-matrix expansion to treat exchange contributions, comparing different prescriptions for the kinetic-energy density (modified Thomas-Fermi and extended Thomas-Fermi approximations) and the average relative momentum.

## Key numbers
- Incident energy range considered: E_i < 100 MeV (with specific calculations shown for 20 MeV neutrons and protons on 93Nb)
- The energy-dependent factor g(E) = 1 - gamma*E, with gamma = 0.002 for M3Y-Reid interaction and gamma = 0.003 for M3Y-Paris interaction
- For the 1 fm range Yukawa interaction, V_0 = 40 MeV for n+93Nb and V_0 = 46.8 MeV for p+93Nb (from Watanabe et al.)
- Volume integrals J_V and rms radii <r^2>^{1/2} are tabulated for multiple interaction models (NM, SF, DF) for both neutrons and protons on 93Nb

## Context
This work extends the microscopic optical potential approach of Brieva and Rook (1977) and the JLM parametrization of Jeukenne, Lejeune and Mahaux (1977) by incorporating nucleonic form factors and realistic density-dependent interactions into a double-folding framework for low-energy nucleon scattering. It directly addresses the inadequacy of the simplified 1 fm range Yukawa interaction used in the Feshbach-Kerman-Koonin (FKK) model (Feshbach, 1980), where the strength V_0 is the only adjustable parameter. The results enable consistent microscopic treatment of both elastic scattering and preequilibrium reaction channels, reducing the uncertainties that arise from using different effective interactions for different reaction stages.
