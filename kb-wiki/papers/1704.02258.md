# Transition from vibrational to rotational characters in low-lying states of hypernuclei

> In order to clarify the nature of hypernuclear low-lying states, we carry out a comprehensive study for the structure of $^{144-154}_{~~~~~~~~Λ}$Sm-hypernuclei, which exhibit a transition from vibrational to rotational characters as the neutron number increases. To this end, we employ a microscopic particle-core coupling scheme based on a covariant density functional theory. We find that the positive-parity ground-state band in the hypernuclei shares a similar structure to that of the corresponding core nucleus. That is, regardless of whether the core nucleus is spherical or deformed, each hypernuclear state is dominated by the single configuration of the $Λ$ particle in the $s_{1/2}$ state ($Λs_{1/2}$) coupled to one core state of the ground band. In contrast, the low-lying negative-parity states mainly consist of $Λp_{1/2}$ and $Λp_{3/2}$ configurations coupled to plural nuclear core states. We show that, while the mixing amplitude between these configurations is negligibly small in spherical and weakly-deformed nuclei, it strongly increases as the core nucleus undergoes a transition to a well-deformed shape, being consistent with the Nilsson wave functions. We demonstrate that the structure of these negative-parity states with spin $I$ can be well understood based on the $LS$ coupling scheme, with the total orbital angular momentum of $L=[I\otimes 1]$ and the spin angular momentum of $S=1/2$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the low-lying negative-parity states in hypernuclei exhibit strong configuration mixing between $\Lambda p_{1/2}$ and $\Lambda p_{3/2}$ configurations coupled to core states, but only when the core nucleus undergoes a transition from spherical to well-deformed shape; for spherical or weakly-deformed cores, this mixing is negligibly small and the states remain nearly pure.

## Method

The authors employed a microscopic particle-core coupling scheme based on multi-reference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT). They first constructed the low-lying states of the even-even Sm core nuclei (144-154Sm) by projecting deformed mean-field reference states onto good angular momentum and particle number, then solving the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation. They then expanded the hypernuclear wave functions on these core states and solved coupled-channels equations in a relativistic framework using a point-coupling N-Lambda interaction with the PCY-S4 parametrization.

## Key numbers

- The energy ratio $R_{4/2} \equiv E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)$ ranges from 1.98 for 144Sm to 3.29 for 154Sm, spanning the harmonic oscillator limit (2.0) to near the rigid rotor limit (3.33)
- The probability of the dominant $[\Lambda s_{1/2} \otimes I^+_1]$ configuration in positive-parity states is around 99% for all Sm hypernuclei studied (0.998 for 145-Lambda-Sm down to 0.982 for 155-Lambda-Sm)
- For the $1/2^-_1$ state in 155-Lambda-Sm, the weight of the $[\Lambda p_{1/2} \otimes 0_1^+]$ configuration is 32.2% and the $[\Lambda p_{3/2} \otimes 2_1^+]$ configuration is 63.9%
- The energy splitting between the $1/2^-_1$ and $3/2^-_1$ states decreases from 68 keV in 145-Lambda-Sm to 4 keV in 155-Lambda-Sm
- The single-channel excitation energies for the $1/2^-_1$ and $3/2^-_1$ states are around 4.8 MeV, close to the estimate $\frac{2}{3} \times 41 A^{-1/3} \sim 5.14$ MeV for $A \sim 150$
- The ratio of E2 transition strength for $5/2_1^- \rightarrow 1/2_1^-$ to $9/2_1^- \rightarrow 5/2_1^-$ is 0.641 in 153-Lambda-Sm and 0.695 in 155-Lambda-Sm, both close to 0.7

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Motoba (1983), Bando (1983), and Hiyama (2000) on configuration mixing in hypernuclei, and the authors' own prior work (Mei 2014, 2015, 2016) on the microscopic particle-rotor model. The paper provides the first microscopic calculation for hypernuclear spectra in medium-heavy hypernuclei using a mean-field based approach with beyond-mean-field correlations. It enables future experimental interpretation of gamma-ray spectroscopy for hypernuclei in the mass region around A=150, particularly clarifying that the energy difference between $1/2^-$ and $3/2^-$ states cannot be naively interpreted as the spin-orbit splitting for p-orbits due to configuration mixing effects.
