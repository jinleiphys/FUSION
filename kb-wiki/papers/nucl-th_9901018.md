# Neutron Polarizabilities From Compton Scattering on the Deuteron?

> A calculation of deuteron Compton scattering using non-relativistic perturbation theory is presented,with the primary motivation of investigating the feasibility of determining the neutron polarizabilities from this type of experiment. This calculation is expected to be valid for energies below 100 MeV. Pion-exchange, relativistic, and recoil corrections are also included. The low-energy theorem for gauge invariance is shown to be satisfied. The relative effects of the different terms and their effects on the determinations of the polarizabilities are discussed at energies of 49, 69, and 95 MeV. The cross-section is dominated by the seagull, polarizability, and electromagnetic multipole interactions. Relativistic and pion-exchange terms are also important, while recoil corrections and multipoles of $L$=2 and greater are negligible. The calculation provides a reasonable description of the experimental data points at 49 and 69 MeV. The polarizabilities are difficult to determine at these energies. A more accurate determination of the polarizabilities may be possible at 95 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether neutron polarizabilities can be determined from deuteron Compton scattering, concluding that such determination is difficult at 49 and 69 MeV but may be more accurate at 95 MeV, with the caveat that the calculation is expected to be valid only for energies below 100 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed a non-relativistic perturbation theory calculation of deuteron Compton scattering, including seagull, polarizability, electromagnetic multipole, pion-exchange, relativistic, and recoil corrections. The transition matrix was computed using Fermi's golden rule with a Hamiltonian that includes electric and magnetic dipole interactions, and the differential cross-section was obtained by summing over final and averaging over initial polarizations. The calculation used the Reid93 np potential and the "B" wavefunctions of Machleidt (1989), with the inhomogeneous differential equation for intermediate states solved for outgoing spherical wave boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is expected to be valid for photon energies below 100 MeV
- Results are discussed at photon energies of 49, 69, and 95 MeV
- The Baldin sum rule gives alpha_n + beta_n = 15.8 +/- 0.5 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3) from older work (Damashun 1970, Scolar 1980), and 14.44 +/- 0.69 from a more recent experiment (Baldin 1998)
- The difference alpha_n - beta_n is estimated to be approximately 3.9 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3) from Holstein (1994)
- Chiral perturbation theory calculations give alpha_n = 13.4 +/- 1.5 and beta_n = 7.8 +/- 3.6 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3) from Meissner (1993, 1994)
- The neutron electric polarizability from Schmiedmayer et al. (1991) is alpha_n = 12.0 +/- 1.5 +/- 2.0 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)
- The photodisintegration cross-section comparison with Partovi (1964) shows reasonable agreement at all energies tested

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Weyrauch (1988, 1990) and the original suggestion by Baldin (1960) to use deuteron Compton scattering for neutron polarizability measurements. The calculation responds to experimental advances including the 1994 deuteron Compton scattering experiment by Lucas (1994) at 49 and 69 MeV, and ongoing experiments at Saskatoon and Lund. The paper enables future experimental determinations of neutron polarizabilities at higher energies (around 95 MeV) and provides a complete potential model calculation that can be compared with effective field theory approaches by Chen et al. (1998) and the intermediate approach by Beane, Malheiro, Phillips, and van Kolck (1999).
