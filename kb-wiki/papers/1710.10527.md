# Combined parametrization of the neutron electric form factor and the $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1232)$ quadrupole form factors

> Models based on $SU(6)$ symmetry breaking and large $N_c$ limit provide relations between the pion cloud contributions to the $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1232)$ quadrupole form factors, electric ($G_E$) and Coulomb ($G_C$), and the neutron electric form factor $G_{En}$, suggesting that those form factors are dominated by the same physical processes. Those relations are improved in order to satisfy a fundamental constraint between the electric and Coulomb quadrupole form factors in the long wavelength limit, when the photon three-momentum vanishes (Siegert's theorem). Inspired by those relations, we study alternative parametrizations for the neutron electric form factor. The parameters of the new form are then determined by a combined fit to the $G_{En}$ and the $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1232)$ quadrupole form factor data. We obtain a very good description of the $G_E$ and $G_C$ data when we combine the pion cloud contributions with small valence quark contributions to the $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1232)$ quadrupole form factors. The best description of the data is obtained when the second momentum of $G_{En}$ is $r_n^4 \simeq -0.4$ fm$^4$. We conclude that the square radii associated with $G_E$ and $G_C$, $r_E^2$ and $r_C^2$, respectively, are large, revealing the long extension of the pion cloud. We conclude also that those square radii are related by $r_E^2 - r_C^2 = 0.6 \pm 0.2$ fm$^2$. The last result is mainly the consequence of the pion cloud effects and Siegert's theorem.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a combined fit of the neutron electric form factor \(G_{En}\) and the \(\gamma^\ast N \to \Delta(1232)\) quadrupole form factors \(G_E\) and \(G_C\) can be achieved using pion cloud parametrizations that satisfy Siegert's theorem exactly, but only when small valence quark contributions are added; the best description of the data requires the second moment of \(G_{En}\) to be \(r_n^4 \simeq -0.4\) fm\(^4\), and the difference of the square radii of the quadrupole form factors is constrained to \(r_E^2 - r_C^2 = 0.6 \pm 0.2\) fm\(^2\).

## Method

The author uses relations derived from \(SU(6)\) symmetry breaking and the large \(N_c\) limit, improved to satisfy Siegert's theorem exactly, to express the pion cloud contributions to \(G_E\) and \(G_C\) in terms of \(\tilde G_{En} = G_{En}/Q^2\). A three-parameter rational-function parametrization of \(G_{En}\) is introduced, and a global chi-square fit is performed to data for \(G_{En}\), \(G_E\), and \(G_C\) (the latter restricted to \(Q^2 < 1.5\) GeV\(^2\)), with valence quark contributions from a covariant spectator quark model added to the quadrupole form factors.

## Key numbers

* The best description of the data is obtained when the second moment of \(G_{En}\) is \(r_n^4 \simeq -0.4\) fm\(^4\).
* The square radii of the electric and Coulomb quadrupole form factors satisfy \(r_E^2 - r_C^2 = 0.6 \pm 0.2\) fm\(^2\).
* The first moment of \(G_{En}\) is fixed to the experimental value \(r_n^2 \simeq -0.116\) fm\(^2\).
* The pseudothreshold for the \(\gamma^\ast N \to \Delta(1232)\) transition is at \(Q_{pt}^2 \simeq -0.086\) GeV\(^2\).
* The valence quark contributions to \(G_E\) and \(G_C\) near \(Q^2=0\) are only about 10% of the empirical data, based on \(G_E(0) = 0.076 \pm 0.015\) and \(\kappa G_C(0.04\ \text{GeV}^2) = 0.075 \pm 0.021\).
* The fit to the quadrupole form factors is restricted to \(Q^2 < 1.5\) GeV\(^2\), using 15 data points for \(G_E\) and 13 for \(G_C\).

## Context

This work extends previous relations between the neutron electric form factor and the \(\gamma^\ast N \to \Delta(1232)\) quadrupole form factors derived by Pascalutsa (2007) and Buchmann (2004, 2009), which were based on the large \(N_c\) limit and \(SU(6)\) symmetry breaking. The key improvement is the exact satisfaction of Siegert's theorem, which was violated in earlier parametrizations, and the inclusion of valence quark contributions from a covariant spectator quark model calibrated to lattice QCD results (Alexandrou 2008). The results enable more precise constraints on the shape of \(G_{En}\) and the spatial extension of the pion cloud in the \(\Delta(1232)\) transition, and they resolve a previous discrepancy with low-\(Q^2\) \(G_C\) data from Blomberg (2016).
