# SU(3) Chiral Effective Field Theories -- A Status Report --

> A personal overview on the present status of SU(3) chiral perturbation theory in the baryonic sector is given. Recent developments are presented and remaining challenges are pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while SU(3) chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) is a valid framework for hyperon properties and decays, the convergence of the chiral expansion is problematic due to large non-analytic loop contributions from kaons and etas, and that these convergence issues can be ameliorated by using a cutoff regulator rather than dimensional regularization.

## Method

The author reviews the construction of the SU(3) chiral effective Lagrangian for baryons, including the leading-order couplings (D and F for axial vectors) and the second-order mass terms with parameters b0, bD, bF. Calculations of baryon octet masses, sigma terms, axial vector couplings, nonleptonic weak hyperon decays, and radiative hyperon decays are presented at tree level and one-loop order, comparing results from dimensional regularization with those from a dipole cutoff regulator.

## Key numbers

- The axial vector couplings are fit as D = 0.80 and F = 0.46, giving g_A^{pn} = D+F = 1.26
- The Gell-Mann–Okubo mass relation is satisfied experimentally to about 3%
- The empirical pion-nucleon sigma term is sigma_{pi N}(0) = (45 +/- 8) MeV
- Using a cutoff regulator, the strangeness fraction in the nucleon is y = 0.25 +/- 0.05, and the strangeness contribution to the nucleon mass is m_s <p|bar s s|p> = 150 +/- 50 MeV
- The photon asymmetry parameter for Sigma+ -> p gamma is measured as alpha_{Sigma+ p} = -0.76 +/- 0.08, in contradiction with the SU(3) limit expectation of near zero

## Context

This status report reviews the state of baryon SU(3) chiral perturbation theory as of 2004, extending the mesonic ChPT framework developed by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) to include baryons via the heavy baryon approach of Jenkins and Manohar (1991). The paper highlights persistent puzzles including the s- and p-wave problem in nonleptonic hyperon decays and Hara's theorem violation in radiative hyperon decays, and points to the need for including higher-lying baryon resonances (such as 1/2^- states) or final state interactions to resolve these issues.
