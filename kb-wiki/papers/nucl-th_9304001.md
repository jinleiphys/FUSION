# Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Models Beyond the Mean Field Approximation

> Inspired by the model of Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, various Lagrangians are considered for a system of interacting quarks. Employing standard techniques of many-body theory, the scalar part of the quark self-energy is calculated including terms up to second-order in the interaction. Results obtained for the single-particle Green's function are compared with those which only account for the mean-field or Hartree-Fock term in the self-energy. Depending on the explicit form of the Lagrangian, the second-order contributions range between 4 and 90 percent of the leading Hartree-Fock term. This leads to a considerable momentum dependence of the self-energy and the effective mass of the quarks.

# Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Models Beyond the Mean Field Approximation

## Key claim

Second-order contributions to the quark self-energy in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models range between 4 and 90 percent of the leading Hartree-Fock term, depending on the explicit form of the Lagrangian chosen; this range is conditional on adjusting coupling constants so that all models yield the same constituent quark mass of 313 MeV for quarks at zero momentum.

## Method

The authors calculated the scalar part of the quark self-energy including all terms up to second order in the interaction, using standard many-body techniques with a three-momentum noncovariant cut-off regularization scheme. They considered four different NJL-type Lagrangians (labeled A through D), each with distinct direct interaction terms, and included exchange terms via Fierz transformation. The coupling constants for each model were adjusted so that the sum of Hartree-Fock and second-order contributions gave an effective mass of 313 MeV for quarks with zero three-momentum and energy equal to that mass.

## Key numbers

- Second-order contributions range between **4 percent** and **90 percent** of the leading Hartree-Fock term, depending on the Lagrangian chosen.
- The constituent quark mass used as reference is **313 MeV**, obtained with a cut-off parameter **Lambda = 653 MeV** (from Klevansky review).
- For model A (original NJL Lagrangian), the Hartree-Fock contribution dominates; for model B (pure vector-isoscalar-colorscalar direct interaction), more than **50 percent** of the effective mass comes from the Hartree-Fock term.
- The effective quark mass decreases with increasing momentum; the reduction can be as large as **20 percent** for momenta near the cut-off.
- The threshold for 2-particle-1-hole configurations contributing to the self-energy is at **p0 = 3 m* = 939 MeV** for quarks with zero three-momentum.
- Increasing the cut-off to **Lambda = 800 MeV** yields a slight decrease in the total second-order contribution relative to the Hartree-Fock term.

## Context

This work extends the standard mean-field (Hartree-Fock) treatment of NJL models, which had been the dominant approximation in most previous studies (Klevansky review, 1992). The second-order terms describe the coupling of a quark to a quark plus quark-antiquark excitation, representing the lowest-order contribution from meson exchange, in contrast to earlier work by Cao et al. that considered only pion-exchange effects. The results are a first step toward a consistent treatment beyond the mean-field approximation that would obey the Goldstone theorem, requiring both improved quark propagators and a consistent residual interaction for calculating polarization propagators in future work.
