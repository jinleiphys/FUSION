# $Q\bar Qqqq$ Quark System, Compact Pentaquark, and Gauge/String Duality (Part II)

> This is the second of two companion papers in which we continue to develop the construction of the doubly heavy pentaquark systems using the gauge/string duality. In this paper, we propose a stringy description of the $Q\bar Qqqq$ system in the case of two light flavors. Our goal is to explore the lower-lying Born-Oppenheimer potentials as a function of the separation distance between the heavy quark-antiquark pair. The analysis shows that the ground state Born-Oppenheimer potential is described in terms of both hadro-quarkonia and hadronic molecules. Meanwhile a standard pentaquark configuration, which describes a genuine five-quark interaction, makes the dominant contribution to a higher lying potential. This configuration has an antiquark-diquark-diquark structure $\bar Q[qq][Qq]$ for separations larger than $0.1\,\text{fm}$. The latter enables us to establish a relation among the masses of hadrons in the heavy quark limit. To describe the structure of the potentials more clear, we define some critical separations that are related to the processes of string reconnection, breaking and junction annihilation. Additionally, we consider the generalized baryon vertices, where more than three strings can meet, and explore their implications for the pentaquark systems.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the ground state Born-Oppenheimer potential for the \(Q\bar Qqqq\) system is described by both hadro-quarkonia and hadronic molecule configurations, while a genuine five-quark pentaquark configuration with an antiquark-diquark-diquark structure \(\bar Q[qq][Qq]\) makes the dominant contribution to a higher-lying potential, but only for heavy quark separations larger than \(0.1\,\text{fm}\).

## Method

The author uses gauge/string duality (AdS/QCD) with a five-dimensional Euclidean metric deformed by a parameter \(\s\), constructing string configurations that correspond to different quark arrangements. The Born-Oppenheimer potentials are computed by extremizing the total action, which includes Nambu-Goto strings, baryon vertices with action \(S_{\text{vert}}=\tau_v\int dt \, \ep^{-2\s r^2}/r\), and light quarks modeled as point particles with action \(S_{\text q}=\text{T}_0R\int dt \, \ep^{\frac{1}{2}\s r^2}/r\). The analysis considers disconnected configurations (hadro-quarkonium and hadronic molecules) and a connected pentaquark configuration, with the shape of the latter changing as a function of heavy quark separation, leading to piecewise definitions of the potential.

## Key numbers

* The pentaquark configuration exhibits an antiquark-diquark-diquark structure \(\bar Q[qq][Qq]\) for heavy quark separations larger than \(0.1\,\text{fm}\).
* The light quark masses are determined by fitting to lattice data, yielding \(m_{u/d}=46.6\,\text{MeV}\) for the parameter values used.
* The parameter \(\bar v = \s r_{\bar v}^2\) varies from 0 to \(\vs\) for small separations, then from \(\vs\) to \(\qs\) for slightly larger separations, and finally from \(\qs\) to \(\Vz\) and \(\Vo\) for intermediate and large separations, where \(\Vz\) and \(\Vo\) are solutions to specific equations (Eqs. (3.27) and (3.29)).
* The separation distance \(\ell\) becomes infinite when the parameter \(\lambda\) reaches 1, corresponding to the strings reaching the soft wall at \(r=1/\sqrt{\s}\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous development of doubly heavy pentaquark systems using gauge/string duality (Andreev 2023, Part I) and builds on the hadro-quarkonium picture of Voloshin. It provides a stringy realization of the antiquark-diquark-diquark scheme proposed by Maiani, and establishes a relation between the pentaquark and tetraquark energies: \(E_{\QQbqqq}(\ell)=E_{\QQqbqb}(\ell)+E_0\) for \(\ell\geq \ell(\vs)\). The results enable future lattice QCD comparisons and further exploration of exotic hadron spectra within the AdS/QCD framework.
