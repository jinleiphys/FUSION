# Correlated Emission of Hadrons from Recombination of Correlated Partons

> We discuss different sources of hadron correlations in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We show that correlations among partons in a quasi-thermal medium can lead to the correlated emission of hadrons by quark recombination and argue that this mechanism offers a plausible explanation for the dihadron correlations in the few GeV/c momentum range observed in Au+Au collisions at RHIC.

# Correlated Emission of Hadrons from Recombination of Correlated Partons

## Key claim
Correlations among partons in a quasi-thermal medium can produce correlated hadron emission via quark recombination, offering a plausible explanation for dihadron correlations at transverse momenta of a few GeV/c in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, provided that two-parton correlations of order 10% exist within a fixed correlation volume in the deconfined phase.

## Method
The authors compute dimeson production from three processes: recombination of four thermal partons into two mesons (SS-SS), double fragmentation from a jet (F-F), and fragmentation accompanied by soft-hard recombination (F-SH). They introduce two-parton correlations into the four-parton Wigner function as a product of single-particle distributions multiplied by a sum of Gaussian correlation functions in azimuthal angle and rapidity, then integrate over the hadronization hypersurface using Boltzmann distributions with temperature, radial flow, and a boost-invariant geometry. The resulting dimeson spectra are compared to PHENIX preliminary data after background subtraction, with the correlation strength parameter \(\hat c_0\) and azimuthal width \(\phi_0\) as adjustable inputs.

## Key numbers
- The amplification factor for correlations from partons to hadrons is \(Q = 4\) for a meson-meson pair, \(Q = 6\) for a meson-baryon pair, and \(Q = 9\) for a baryon-baryon pair.
- Trigger hadrons are selected with transverse momentum \(2.5\ \text{GeV}/c < p_T < 4.0\ \text{GeV}/c\); associated particles are selected with \(1.7\ \text{GeV}/c \le p_T \le 2.5\ \text{GeV}/c\), both within rapidity \(|y| < 0.35\).
- The azimuthal width \(\phi_0\) is set to 0.2 radians for all processes in the numerical examples.
- The correlation strength parameter \(\hat c_0 = 0.08\) is used for the SS-SS process in the most realistic scenario.
- For a fixed correlation volume scaling as \(\hat c = 0.08 \times 100/N_{\text{part}}\), the associated yield \(Y_{AB}\) varies weakly with centrality.
- The F-SH contribution with \(\hat v = 0.5\) yields negligible \(\pi-\pi\) correlations compared to SS-SS and F-F.

## Context
This work extends the recombination model of Fries et al. (2003) for inclusive hadron spectra and elliptic flow to include hadron correlations, addressing an apparent contradiction with jet-like correlations observed by STAR (2003) and Sickles (2004). The authors argue that correlations induced by energetic partons passing through the medium (a "wake effect") can produce the observed dihadron signals without abandoning the recombination framework. The results enable future comparisons with PHENIX data and motivate measurements in p-p collisions to distinguish between double fragmentation (F-F) and correlated recombination (SS-SS) as the dominant source.
