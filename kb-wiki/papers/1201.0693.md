# Interacting hadron resonance gas meets lattice QCD

> We present, in the framework of the interacting hadron resonance gas, an evaluation of thermodynamical quantities. The interaction is modelled via a correction for the finite size of the hadrons. We investigate the sensitivity of the model calculations on the radius of the hadrons, which is a parameter of the model. Our calculations for thermodynamical quantities as energy and entropy densities and pressure are confronted with predictions using the lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) formalism.

## Key claim

The interacting hadron resonance gas (HRG) model with excluded-volume corrections provides a realistic description of the hadronic phase up to temperatures near the critical temperature for deconfinement (T_c ~ 160 MeV), while the free (non-interacting) HRG becomes inappropriate above 130-140 MeV because the gas is no longer dilute; the Hagedorn limiting temperature is an artifact of using the free HRG at temperatures where the gas is manifestly dense.

## Method

The authors compute thermodynamic quantities (energy density, entropy density, pressure, trace anomaly, speed of sound) within the grand-canonical ensemble using a hadron resonance gas model that includes all known hadrons with masses up to 3 GeV from the 2008 PDG compilation. Short-range repulsive interactions are modeled via a thermodynamically consistent excluded-volume (Van-der-Waals-type) correction, where each hadron species is assigned an eigenvolume V_eigen = 4 * (4πR^3/3) based on a hard-core radius R. The calculations are performed for a range of radii R = 0.3 ± 0.05 fm for both mesons and baryons, and the results are compared to lattice QCD data from Borsanyi et al. and the HotQCD collaboration.

## Key numbers

- At T = 164 MeV and μ_b = 0.8 MeV, the energy density shows strong sensitivity to the hadron radius parameter; the default radius value is 0.3 fm.
- Total hadron densities exceed 0.5 fm^{-3} near T_c, and total baryon densities approach normal nuclear matter density of 0.15 fm^{-3}.
- The excluded-volume correction leads to up to 30% reduction of thermodynamic quantities at chemical freeze-out in nucleus-nucleus collisions.
- Good agreement between lattice QCD and the interacting HRG is found below T = 150 MeV for energy density, pressure, and entropy density.
- For the trace anomaly (ε-3P)/T^4, agreement between lattice QCD and the interacting HRG holds only up to T = 140 MeV for the data of Borsanyi et al.
- The adiabatic speed of sound squared exhibits a shallow minimum around T = 140-150 MeV for the interacting HRG, while lattice values show a more pronounced dip.

## Context

This work extends the hadron resonance gas approach of Andronic et al. (2008) by explicitly incorporating excluded-volume corrections based on the thermodynamically consistent formalism of Rischke et al. (1991). It addresses the approximation, used by Huovinen and Petreczky (2009) and Borsanyi et al. (2010), that the thermodynamics of the interacting HRG is well approximated by the non-interacting case via the Dashen-Ma-Bernstein theorem; the authors argue this approximation breaks down near T_c because the gas is not dilute. The results enable more realistic equations of state for hydrodynamic calculations of heavy-ion collisions and provide a baseline for identifying genuine quark-gluon degrees of freedom in lattice QCD results above T_c.
