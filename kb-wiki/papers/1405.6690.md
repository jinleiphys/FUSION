# Model-independent Analyses of Dark-Matter Particle Interactions

> A model-independent treatment of dark-matter particle elastic scattering has been developed, yielding the most general interaction for WIMP-nucleon low-energy scattering, and the resulting amplitude has been embedded in the nucleus, taking into account the selection rules imposed by parity and time-reversal. One finds that, in contrast to the usual spin-independent/spin-dependent (SI/SD) formulation, the resulting cross section contains six independent nuclear response functions, three of which are associated with possible velocity-dependent interactions. We find that current experiments are four orders of magnitude more sensitive to derivative couplings than is apparent in the standard SI/SD treatment, which necessarily associates such interactions with cross sections proportional to the square of the WIMP velocity relative to the nuclear center of mass.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard spin-independent/spin-dependent (SI/SD) treatment of WIMP-nucleus elastic scattering is incomplete, and that a model-independent effective field theory approach reveals six independent nuclear response functions rather than two, with velocity-dependent interactions being four orders of magnitude more sensitive than previously thought when nuclear finite-size effects are properly included.

## Method

The authors constructed the most general nonrelativistic WIMP-nucleon interaction Lagrangian to second order in momenta, constrained by Galilean invariance, parity, and time-reversal symmetry, yielding 14 independent operators (after eliminating one). They then embedded this interaction in the nucleus by summing over individual nucleons and performing a multipole decomposition of the nuclear response, identifying which multipole operators survive the parity and time-reversal selection rules imposed by the nuclear ground state.

## Key numbers

- Current experiments are four orders of magnitude more sensitive to derivative couplings than apparent in the standard SI/SD treatment, which associates such interactions with cross sections proportional to \(v_T^2 \sim 10^{-6}\)
- Velocity-dependent interactions lead to cross sections \(\sim q^2/m_N^2 \; G_F^2 \sim 10^{-2} \; G_F^2\), in contrast to the SI/SD result \(\sim v_T^2 \; G_F^2 \sim 10^{-6} \; G_F^2\)
- The enhancement factor for velocity-dependent interactions when properly treated is \(\sim (\mu_T/m_N)^2 \sim 10^4\), where \(\mu_T\) is the WIMP-nucleus reduced mass
- WIMP velocities today are \(v_T \sim 10^{-3}\), with momenta \(q \sim 100\) MeV/c and kinetic energies \(\epsilon \sim 50\) keV
- The effective theory has 28 parameters: 14 space/spin operators each with distinct couplings to protons and neutrons
- Six independent nuclear response functions are identified, with two interference terms yielding up to eight constraints on the low-energy constants from elastic scattering experiments

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to WIMP-nucleon interactions developed by Fan (2010), Fitzpatrick (2013), and Anand (2014), providing a complete treatment of nuclear finite-size effects that were neglected in the standard SI/SD formulation of Goodman (1985) and Lewin (1996). The paper enables experimentalists to design a suite of direct detection experiments using different nuclear targets (varying angular momentum, isospin, and shell structure) to separately measure the eight independent response functions, thereby extracting all information about dark matter particle interactions available from elastic scattering.
