# $γZ$-Exchange Contributions in Low-Energy Parity-Violating $ep$ Scattering

> In this work, the $γZ$-exchange contributions in the low-energy elastic parity-violating $ep$ scattering are discussed with the approximation $m_e=0$. By expanding the $γpp$ and $Zpp$ interactions on the momentum of photon and considering both the leading-order and the next-to-leading order interactions, we calculate the amplitudes of the $γZ$-exchange diagrams. After performing the loop integral, we expand the results in the low energy limit, and obtain the analytic expressions for the amplitudes. Numerical comparisons show that the analytic expressions are very close to the full results over a large region. We investigate the power behaviors of these contributions and find that some are enhanced by a kinematic factor in the low energy limit. Additionally, in some cases, the imaginary parts of the contributions from the next-to-leading-order interactions are at the same order as those from the leading-order interactions. Furthermore, the corresponding contributions to the physical observable quantity $A_{\textrm{PV}}$ are also discussed. Combining all the properties together, we conclude that these analytic expressions describe the leading-order contributions of all $γZ$-exchange helicity amplitudes in the region with $α_e\ll Q/M_N\sim δ/M_N^2\ll 1$, where $α_{e}$ is the fine structure constant, $M_N$ is the mass of proton, $Q$ and $δ$ are the small quantities related to the momentum transfer and the center-of-mass energy.

## Key claim
The authors derive analytic expressions for the γZ-exchange contributions to low-energy parity-violating elastic electron-proton scattering, valid in the region where the fine-structure constant α_e is much smaller than Q/M_N and δ/M_N^2, with Q the momentum transfer and δ the deviation from the minimum center-of-mass energy. They find that some γZ-exchange contributions are enhanced by a kinematic factor M_N/Q relative to the tree-level Z-exchange, and that the imaginary parts from next-to-leading-order interactions can be as large as those from leading-order interactions.

## Method
The authors expand the γpp and Zpp vertices to leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in the photon momentum, then calculate the box and crossed-box γZ-exchange diagrams using dimensional regularization with the NDR scheme for γ_5. After loop integration with FeynCalc and PackageX, they expand the amplitudes in the low-energy limit (Q → 0, δ → 0) independently, obtaining analytic expressions for the invariant amplitudes and helicity amplitudes. They separate the parity-violating part into vector and axial-vector components to avoid γ_5 ambiguities.

## Key numbers
- The analytic LO expressions for helicity amplitudes are compared to full numerical results at Q = 0.05, 0.1, 0.2, and 0.5 GeV, with δ in the range [0, 1] GeV², showing close agreement.
- The power behavior of Re[C_{γZ,111}^V] is α_e (2M_N Q + δ)/Q², while Re[C_{γZ,311}^V] behaves as α_e M_N/Q.
- The power behavior of Re[C_{γZ,113}^A] is α_e (2M_N Q + δ)/Q², and Re[C_{γZ,213}^A] and Re[C_{γZ,313}^A] both behave as α_e M_N/Q.
- The tree-level Z-exchange coefficients Re[F_{Z,1}^V] and Re[F_{Z,1}^A] are constant (order 1), independent of Q and δ.

## Context
This work extends the low-energy effective approach previously applied to two-photon-exchange (TPE) contributions in elastic ep and μp scattering (lp-lp-TPE-chiral) to the γZ-exchange case. It provides analytic expressions that complement existing methods for estimating γZ-exchange corrections, such as the zero-energy approximation (ep-ep-gammaZ-zero-energy), hadronic models (ep-ep-gammaZ-hadronic-model-method), general parton distributions (ep-ep-gammaZ-GPD-method), and dispersion relations (ep-ep-gammaZ-dispersion-relation-1, ep-ep-gammaZ-dispersion-relation-2). The results enable more precise extraction of the proton weak charge Q_W and weak form factors in upcoming experiments like P2, by providing a controlled low-energy expansion of the γZ-exchange contributions.
