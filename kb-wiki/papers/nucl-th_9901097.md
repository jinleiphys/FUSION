# The Landau-Migdal Parameters, g'_NN and g'_NΔand Pion Condensations

> The Landau-Migdal parameters for nucleon-nucleon (g'_NN) and nucleon-Δ(g'_NΔ) couplings are estimated from recent experimental data on the giant Gamow-Teller state of 90Nb. The observed quenching of the GT strength by 10% provides 0.16<g'_NΔ<0.23 for g'_ΔΔ<1.0, while the excitation energy g'_NN\sim 0.59. The critical density of pion condensations is much lower than those previously discussed with the universality ansatz.

## Key claim

The universality ansatz (assuming \(g'_{\mathrm{NN}} = g'_{\mathrm{N}\Delta} = g'_{\Delta\Delta}\)) does not hold; instead, the observed 10% quenching of the Gamow-Teller (GT) strength in \(^{90}\mathrm{Nb}\) constrains \(g'_{\mathrm{N}\Delta}\) to be about 0.2 (for \(g'_{\Delta\Delta} < 1.0\)), while \(g'_{\mathrm{NN}}\) is fixed near 0.59, leading to a much lower critical density for pion condensation than previously predicted.

## Method

The authors used the experimental excitation energy and the observed 10% quenching of the GT strength in \(^{90}\mathrm{Nb}\) (from Wakasa et al.) to solve the RPA equations in a combined nucleon and \(\Delta\)-hole space. They employed both the quark model and the Chew-Low model for the coupling constants, expressing the quenching factor \(Q\) and the spin-isospin interaction strength \(\kappa_{\tau\sigma}\) in terms of the Landau-Migdal parameters \(g'_{\mathrm{NN}}\), \(g'_{\mathrm{N}\Delta}\), and \(g'_{\Delta\Delta}\). The critical density for neutral and charged pion condensation was then computed from the pion propagator using these extracted parameters, without assuming universality.

## Key numbers

- Quenching factor of the GT strength in \(^{90}\mathrm{Zr}\): \(Q = 0.90 \pm 0.05\) (statistical error).
- Extracted range for \(g'_{\mathrm{N}\Delta}\) (quark model, for \(g'_{\Delta\Delta} < 1.0\)): \(0.18 < g'_{\mathrm{N}\Delta} < 0.23\).
- Extracted range for \(g'_{\mathrm{N}\Delta}\) (Chew-Low model, for \(g'_{\Delta\Delta} < 1.0\)): \(0.16 < g'_{\mathrm{N}\Delta} < 0.22\).
- Extracted value for \(g'_{\mathrm{NN}}\): \(0.591 < g'_{\mathrm{NN}} < 0.594\) (for \(g'_{\Delta\Delta} < 1.0\)).
- Critical density for \(\pi^0\) condensation in symmetric nuclear matter (for \(g'_{\Delta\Delta} = 1.0\), \(m^* = 0.8m\)): \(\rho_c \approx 1.6\,\rho_0\).
- Critical density for \(\pi^0\) condensation in pure neutron matter (for \(g'_{\Delta\Delta} = 1.0\), \(m^* = 0.8m\)): \(\rho_c \approx 2.5\,\rho_0\).
- Critical density for charged pion (\(\pi^c\)) condensation in neutron matter (for \(g'_{\Delta\Delta} = 1.0\)): \(\rho_c \approx 1.7\,\rho_0\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spin-isospin excitations and pion condensation that relied on the universality ansatz (e.g., Toki and Weise; Riska and Brown; Alberico et al.; Brown and Machleidt), which assumed all three Landau-Migdal parameters were equal and typically in the range 0.6–0.9. The new experimental data from Wakasa et al. (1998) on the GT strength of \(^{90}\mathrm{Zr}\) allowed the authors to break this degeneracy. The resulting small \(g'_{\mathrm{N}\Delta}\) (about 0.2) yields critical densities for pion condensation (around 1.6–2.5 \(\rho_0\)) that are much lower than those predicted under the universality ansatz, and comparable to values from a variational calculation by Pandharipande (1998). This opens the possibility of re-examining pion condensation in heavy-ion reactions and neutron star cooling scenarios.
