# Partial wave decomposition of the N3LO equation of state

> By means of a partial wave decomposition, we separate their contributions to the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter for the N3LO pseudo-potential. In particular, we show that although both the tensor and the spin-orbit terms do not contribute to the equation of state, they give a non-vanishing contribution to the separate (JLS) channels.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit partial-wave contributions to the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter for the N3LO Skyrme pseudo-potential, showing that while the tensor and spin-orbit terms vanish in the total equation of state, they produce non-zero contributions in individual (JLS) channels that could serve as additional constraints from *ab-initio* calculations.

## Method

The authors perform a partial-wave decomposition of the N3LO Skyrme pseudo-potential in symmetric nuclear matter within the Hartree-Fock approximation. They expand the central, tensor, and spin-orbit terms in spherical harmonics to isolate contributions to specific (JLS) channels, then derive analytic expressions for the potential energy per particle in each channel as a function of density and Fermi momentum. The decomposition follows the approach of Baldo et al. (1997) who used Brueckner-Hartree-Fock methods to calculate partial-wave contributions from realistic interactions.

## Key numbers

- Central term S-wave contributions: \(^1S_0\) channel gives \(\frac{3}{16}t_0(1-x_0)\rho + \frac{1}{32}t_3(1-x_3)\rho^{\alpha+1} + \frac{9}{160}t_1(1-x_1)\rho k_F^2 + \frac{9}{280}t_1^{(4)}(1-x_1^{(4)})\rho k_F^4 + \frac{1}{10}t_1^{(6)}(1-x_1^{(6)})\rho k_F^6\) per particle
- Central term P-wave: \(^1P_0\) channel gives \(\frac{3}{160}t_2(1-x_2)\rho k_F^2 + \frac{9}{560}t_2^{(4)}(1-x_2^{(4)})\rho k_F^4 + \frac{3}{50}t_2^{(6)}(1-x_2^{(6)})\rho k_F^6\) per particle
- Tensor term P-wave: \(^3P_0\) channel gives \(-\frac{3}{80}t_o\rho k_F^2 - \frac{9}{140}t_o^{(4)}\rho k_F^4 - \frac{17}{750}t_o^{(6)}\rho k_F^6\) per particle
- Tensor term D-wave: \(^3D_1\) channel gives \(-\frac{9}{1000}t_e^{(4)}\rho k_F^4 - \frac{7}{1500}\rho k_F^6\) per particle
- Spin-orbit term: \(^3P_0\) channel gives \(\frac{1}{40}W_o\rho k_F^2\) per particle
- The N3LO pseudo-potential introduces D-wave contributions (e.g., \(^1D_2\) channel) and F-wave contributions (e.g., \(^1F_3\) channel proportional to \(t_2^{(6)}\)), which are absent in standard Skyrme functionals

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Skyrme functionals beyond the standard S- and P-wave terms studied by Lesinski et al. (2006) and provides the necessary formalism to constrain higher-order derivative terms using partial-wave decomposition from *ab-initio* calculations. The results complement the linear response formalism developed by Pastore et al. (2013) and the N2LO analysis by Davesne et al. (2013), enabling future fits that use microscopic partial-wave contributions from methods such as Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (Baldo et al., 1997) or chiral effective field theory as pseudo-observables, avoiding the finite-size and shell effects that complicate fits to finite nuclei.
