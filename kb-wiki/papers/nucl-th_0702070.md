# Neutron-Proton pairing revisited

> We reexamine neutron-proton pairing as a phenomenon that should be explanable in a microscopic theory of nuclear binding energies. Empirically, there is an increased separation energy when both neutron and proton numbers are even or if they are both odd. The enhancement is present at some level in nearly all nuclei: the separation energy difference has the opposite sign in less than 1% of the cases in which sufficient data exist. We discuss the possible origin of the effect in the context of density functional theory (DFT) and its extensions. Neutron-proton pairing from the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory does not seem promising to explain the effect. We demonstrate that much of the increased binding in the odd-odd system might be understood as a recoupling energy. This suggests that the DFT should be extended by angular momentum projection to describe the effect.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the empirical enhancement of nuclear binding when both neutron and proton numbers are even or both odd is not well explained by standard Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with neutron-proton pairing, and that much of the effect may instead arise from a recoupling energy in odd-odd nuclei, suggesting density functional theory should be extended by angular momentum projection.

## Method

The authors analyzed neutron and proton separation energy differences \(S_{n2p}\) and \(S_{p2n}\) from the 2003 Audi-Wapstra mass tables across the nuclear mass table, counting how many nuclei showed the opposite sign from the expected enhancement. They then compared these empirical separation energy differences to a recoupling energy \(\delta_s\) computed from measured excitation energies of multiplets in odd-odd nuclei near doubly magic shells, using angular momentum recoupling formulas from the spherical shell model. Finally, they tested whether the effect is suppressed in deformed nuclei by fitting the \(A^{-2/3}\) parameterization to a subset of 92 strongly deformed even-even nuclei classified by a static deformation criterion.

## Key numbers

- Out of 1412 nuclei with significant \(S_{n2p}\) values, only 10 had the opposite sign from the expected enhancement; out of 1448 nuclei with significant \(S_{p2n}\) values, only 9 had the opposite sign.
- The least-squares fit to the magnitude of \(S_{p2n}\) gives \(\vert S_{p2n} \vert = 7.3 / A^{2/3}\) MeV.
- For the 92 strongly deformed nuclei, the fitted \(K\) value in the same parameterization is 5.7 MeV, compared to 7.3 MeV for the full dataset.
- 23% of deformed nuclei have \(S_{n2p}\) less than \(3.7 / A^{2/3}\) MeV, versus 9% for other nuclei.
- Table 1 lists specific recoupling energies \(\delta_s\) and corresponding \(S_{n2p}\) and \(S_{p2n}\) values for nuclei including \(^{40}\)K (0.44, 0.49, 0.32 MeV), \(^{58}\)Cu (0.65, 0.81, 0.78 MeV), and \(^{210}\)Bi (0.42, 0.36, 0.36 MeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the even-odd parity dependence of nuclear binding, including the parameterizations of Myers and Swiatecki (1975) and Moller and Nix (1981) using a \(\delta \sim \mod(N,2) \mod(Z,2) / A\) term, and the later \(A^{-2/3}\) parameterization by Madland (1988). The authors also reference the shell-based mass fits of Zeldes (1975) and the 9-point difference formula of Moller (1992). The paper suggests that standard density functional theory with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov pairing cannot explain the effect away from the \(N=Z\) line, and that angular momentum projection within DFT may be needed, while noting that the recoupling mechanism alone does not fully account for the data, especially in deformed nuclei.
