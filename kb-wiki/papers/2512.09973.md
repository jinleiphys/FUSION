# Phase transitions at high and low densities for a rotating QCD matter from holography

> We applied the exact Andreev soft-wall holographic model to investigate phase transitions in rotating strongly interacting matter at high and low densities. Using the dual description of hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma via thermal and charged black holes in five-dimensional AdS space with cylindrical symmetry, we find that for relativistic rotations exceeding 16\% of the speed of light, crossover transitions emerge in the low-density regime up to a critical baryon chemical potential $μ_{CPB}$. These smooth transitions, governed by the negative QCD $β$-function, describe a mixed phase of confined and deconfined matter with different angular momenta evolving into a pure plasma at very high temperatures. For $μ\geq μ_{CPB}$, first-order transitions dominate, following the critical-temperature curve of non-rotating matter. The critical point separating the low-density crossovers from high-density first-order transitions is numerically estimated as $(μ_{CPB}, T_{CP}) = (363.554, 58.507)\,\text{MeV}$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for relativistic rotations exceeding 16% of the speed of light, smooth crossover transitions emerge in the low-density regime of QCD matter up to a critical baryon chemical potential, while first-order transitions dominate at higher densities; this result is conditional on the specific choice of the exact Andreev soft-wall holographic model and the cylindrical symmetry assumption.

## Method

The authors applied the exact Andreev soft-wall holographic model, using the dual description of hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma via thermal and charged black holes in five-dimensional anti-de Sitter (AdS) space with cylindrical symmetry. They computed the regularized action density difference between rotating charged AdS black holes and thermal AdS space, solving the Hawking-Page transition equation numerically to map the QCD-like phase diagram in the temperature-chemical potential plane for various angular velocities.

## Key numbers

- Critical point separating low-density crossovers from high-density first-order transitions: (μ_CPB, T_CP) = (363.554, 58.507) MeV
- Crossover transitions emerge for relativistic rotations exceeding 16% of the speed of light (ωl ≳ 0.16)
- Maximum critical density at zero temperature for non-rotating matter: μ̄(T≈0) ≈ 1.067 (in dimensionless units)
- Maximum value of μ̄_EF (chemical potential below which no first-order transitions occur) is approximately 0.414 (in dimensionless units)

## Context

This work extends previous holographic QCD studies of rotating matter, particularly building on the exact Andreev soft-wall model (Andreev, 2010) and earlier work using the Reissner-Nordström approximation (Braga, 2025). The results enable mapping of the QCD phase diagram under rotation, relevant for understanding non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC where substantial angular momentum is generated. The findings contrast with the non-rotating case where only first-order transitions occur, and with lattice QCD results (Braguta, 2023) that also show modification of the deconfinement temperature under rotation.
