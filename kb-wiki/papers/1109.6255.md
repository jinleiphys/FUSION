# Beyond mean-field approach to heavy-ion reactions around the Coulomb barrier

> Dissipation and fluctuations of one-body observables in heavy-ion reactions around the Coulomb barrier are investigated with a microscopic stochastic mean-field approach. By projecting the stochastic mean-field dynamics on a suitable collective path, transport coefficients associated with the relative distance between colliding nuclei and a fragment mass are extracted. Although microscopic mean-field approach is know to underestimate the variance of fragment mass distribution, the description of the variance is much improved by the stochastic mean-field method. While fluctuations are consistent with the empirical (semiclassical) analysis of the experimental data, concerning mean values of macroscopic variables the semiclassical description breaks down below the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim

The stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach improves the description of the variance of fragment mass distributions in heavy-ion reactions compared to standard time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF), which severely underestimates this variance; however, while fluctuations are consistent with empirical semiclassical analysis, the semiclassical description of mean values of macroscopic variables breaks down below the Coulomb barrier.

## Method

The authors solve the TDHF equation for head-on collisions using a three-dimensional code with the SLy4d Skyrme effective force, then extract the nucleus-nucleus potential and friction coefficient by assuming the mean-field evolution obeys a classical equation of motion with a friction term. To include fluctuations, they employ the SMF approach, where the initial state is represented by an ensemble of single-particle density matrices with random Gaussian fluctuations, and each member of the ensemble evolves independently under its own self-consistent mean-field Hamiltonian. The SMF evolution is projected onto a one-dimensional macroscopic Langevin equation for the relative distance and for the fragment mass number, from which momentum and nucleon diffusion coefficients are extracted.

* For the ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca reaction at E_c.m. = 52.5 MeV, the asymptotic variance from TDHF is 0.004, from SMF is 1.718, and the number of exchanged nucleons is 1.441.
* For the ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca reaction at E_c.m. = 53.0 MeV, the asymptotic variance from TDHF is 0.008, from SMF is 3.790, and the number of exchanged nucleons is 3.634.
* The mesh sizes used in TDHF calculations are 0.8 fm in space and 0.45 fm/c in time.

## Context

This work extends the TDHF framework, which has been known since Koonin (1977), Davies (1978), and Dasso (1979) to severely underestimate fluctuations of collective variables, by introducing the SMF approach proposed by Ayik (2008). The extracted friction coefficients are compared with a microscopic model by Adamian (1997) and show similar radial dependence and magnitude. The SMF approach enables a practical microscopic description of fluctuations in low-energy nuclear reactions, and the diffusion coefficients obtained have the same form as those from the phenomenological nucleon exchange model of Feldmeier (1987).
