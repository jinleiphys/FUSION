# Level Densities of Heavy Nuclei by the Shell Model Monte Carlo Method

> The microscopic calculation of nuclear level densities in the presence of correlations is a difficult many-body problem. The shell model Monte Carlo method provides a powerful technique to carry out such calculations using the framework of the configuration-interaction shell model in spaces that are many orders of magnitude larger than spaces that can be treated by conventional methods. We present recent applications of the method to the calculation of level densities and their collective enhancement factors in heavy nuclei. The calculated level densities are in close agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method can microscopically calculate nuclear level densities for heavy rare-earth nuclei in the configuration-interaction shell model framework, and the resulting state densities are in close agreement with experimental level counting and neutron resonance data, provided the ground-state energy is accurately determined (for even-even nuclei) or fitted to experimental thermal energies (for odd-even nuclei).

## Method

The authors used the SMMC method to compute thermal observables (energy, spin-squared) for even-mass samarium and neodymium isotopes and odd-mass neodymium and samarium isotopes in a large model space spanning the 50-82 major shell plus the 1f7/2 orbital for protons and the 80-126 major shell plus the 0h11/2 and 1g9/2 orbitals for neutrons. State densities were obtained via the saddle-point approximation to the inverse Laplace transform of the partition function, using the calculated thermal energy and entropy. For odd-even nuclei, where direct SMMC ground-state energy extraction is unreliable due to a sign problem, the ground-state energy was determined by a one-parameter fit of the SMMC thermal excitation energy to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The SMMC state densities for even-mass 144-152Nd and 148-154Sm isotopes are shown in Fig. 3 and agree with level counting data and neutron resonance data at the neutron separation energy (open triangles).
- For odd-mass 143-149Nd and 149-155Sm isotopes, SMMC state densities agree with experimental data except for discrepancies with neutron resonance data in 153Sm and 155Sm.
- The collective enhancement factor K (ratio of SMMC state density to Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov density) decays to approximately 1 above the pairing phase transition energies in spherical nuclei and above the shape phase transition energy in deformed nuclei.
- The first excitation energy in even-even heavy deformed nuclei is approximately 100 keV, requiring propagation to much longer imaginary time beta compared to mid-mass nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the SMMC method, previously applied to mid-mass nuclei (Alhassid 2008), to heavy rare-earth nuclei where conventional diagonalization methods are prohibitively expensive due to combinatorial growth of the Hilbert space. The calculations demonstrate that the CI shell model can microscopically describe the crossover from vibrational to rotational collectivity in samarium and neodymium isotopes as neutron number increases from shell closure to mid-shell. The results enable microscopic determination of collective enhancement factors and their decay with excitation energy, addressing one of the least understood topics in level density modeling as noted in the RIPL reference.
