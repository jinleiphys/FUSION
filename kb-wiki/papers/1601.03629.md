# Event-by-event fluctuations in the medium-induced jet evolution

> We develop the event-by-event picture of the gluon distribution produced via medium-induced gluon branching by an energetic jet which propagates through a dense QCD medium. A typical event is characterized by the production of a large number of soft gluons which propagate at large angles with respect to the jet axis and which collectively carry a substantial amount of energy. By explicitly computing 2-gluon correlations, we demonstrate the existence of large event-by-event fluctuations, which reflect the stochastic nature of the branching process. For the two quantities that we have investigated -- the energy loss at large angles and the soft gluon multiplicity --, the dispersion is parametrically as large as the respective expectation value. We identify interesting scaling laws, which suggest that the multiplicity distribution should exhibit KNO (Koba-Nielsen-Olesen) scaling. A similar scaling is known to hold for a jet branching in the vacuum, but the medium-induced distribution is found to be considerably broader. We predict that event-by-event measurements of the di-jet asymmetry in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC should observe large fluctuations in the number of soft hadrons propagating at large angles and also in the total energy carried by these hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that event-by-event fluctuations in medium-induced jet evolution are parametrically as large as the expectation values themselves for both the energy loss at large angles and the soft gluon multiplicity, specifically within the regime where the branching process is treated without elastic collisions and for sufficiently high energies where the medium is characterized solely by the jet quenching parameter.

## Method

The authors develop a Markovian description of the medium-induced gluon branching process, using the BDMPSZ splitting rate to derive and solve transport equations for the gluon spectrum D(x) and the gluon pair density D^(2)(x,x'). They compute the dispersion in the energy lost by the jet at large angles and in the number of gluons N(x0) with energy fraction above an infrared cutoff x0, obtaining an exact result for the gluon pair density and using it to evaluate the variances.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic energy scale for medium-induced multiple branching is ω_br(L) ≡ ᾱ q̂ L², where ᾱ = α_s N_c/π; for ᾱ = 0.3, q̂ = 1 GeV²/fm, and L = 4 fm, ω_br ≈ 8 GeV
- For LHC phenomenology, typical values are E = 100 GeV and T = 0.5 GeV
- The typical transverse momentum acquired via medium rescattering is Q_s = √(q̂ L); for q̂ = 1 GeV²/fm and L = 4 fm, Q_s ≈ 2 GeV
- The branching time for a gluon of energy ω is t_br(ω) = (1/ᾱ)√(ω/q̂)
- The probability for a gluon to radiate a soft gluon with energy zω during time Δt is ΔP = 2ᾱ Δt / √(zω/q̂)

## Context

This work extends the mean-field studies of medium-induced jet evolution via multiple branching (Blaizot 2013, Fister 2014, Blaizot 2015) by computing for the first time the event-by-event fluctuations. It builds on the Markovian description of the branching process established in earlier works (Blaizot 2012, Blaizot 2013, Apolinario 2014) and predicts that the multiplicity fluctuations should exhibit KNO scaling, similar to vacuum jet branching (Dokshitzer 1991) but with a considerably broader distribution. The results enable experimental tests via event-by-event measurements of di-jet asymmetry in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC.
