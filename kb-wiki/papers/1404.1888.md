# Collective interaction of QCD strings and early stages of high multiplicity pA collisions

> We study the early stages of "central" pA and peripheral AA collisions. Several observables indicate that at a sufficiently large number of participant nucleons the system undergoes a transition into a new "explosive" regime. By defining a string-string interaction through the sigma meson exchange and performing molecular dynamics simulation, we argue that one should expect a strong collective implosion of the multi-string "spaghetti" state, creating significant compression of the system in the transverse plane. Another consequence is the collectivization of the "sigma clouds" of all strings into a chirally symmetric fireball. We find that these effects happen provided the number of strings $N_s > 30$ or so, as only such a number can compensate a small sigma-string coupling. These findings should help us to understand the subsequent explosive behavior, observed for the particle multiplicities roughly corresponding to this number of strings.

## Key claim

The authors argue that in high-multiplicity pA and peripheral AA collisions, when the number of QCD strings exceeds about 30, the strings cease to be independent and undergo a collective implosion due to sigma-meson-mediated attraction, creating a compressed, chirally symmetric fireball; this transition is conditional on the small string-string coupling being compensated by a sufficiently large number of strings.

## Method

The authors performed molecular dynamics simulations of parallel QCD strings (a "spaghetti" state) in the transverse plane, modeling the string-string interaction via a 2D Yukawa potential derived from sigma meson exchange. The interaction parameters were obtained from a fit to lattice data on the quark condensate around a single string, using a sigma mass of 600 MeV and an intrinsic string width of 0.176 fm. The simulations tracked the transverse motion of string segments in the nonrelativistic approximation, with initial positions sampled from the elastic scattering amplitude profile for central pA and peripheral AA geometries.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic string width from the fit to lattice data is \( s_{string} = 0.176 \, \mathrm{fm} \), matching the penetration length of the dual superconductor picture (0.175 fm) from other lattice data.
- The string-string coupling in units of string tension is in the range \( g_N \sigma_T \sim 10^{-1} - 10^{-2} \).
- The typical proper time for string breaking is \( \tau_{breaking} \sim 1 \, \mathrm{fm}/c \), and the yo-yo stage lasts \( \tau_{yoyo} \sim 0.5 \, \mathrm{fm}/c \), giving a total available time of about \( 1.5 \, \mathrm{fm}/c \) for collective effects.
- The average number of participant nucleons for a proton traversing a lead nucleus diameter is \( \langle N_p \rangle = 16 \), corresponding to about 30 strings.
- The diluteness of the spaghetti state is \( \langle \mathrm{diluteness} \rangle = 10^{-2} N_s \), which is 0.3 for \( N_s = 30 \).
- The sigma mass used in the fit is \( m_\sigma = 600 \, \mathrm{MeV} \).
- The inelastic NN cross section at LHC energies is \( \sigma_{in} \approx 100 \, \mathrm{mb} = 10 \, \mathrm{fm}^2 \), giving an average impact parameter \( \bar{b} \approx 1.5 \, \mathrm{fm} \).
- The string radius is \( r_s \approx 0.15 \, \mathrm{fm} \).

## Context

This work extends the study of collective effects in multi-string systems beyond earlier ideas of string fusion and color percolation (ropes, percolation models by Braun (1999) and others), focusing instead on the dynamical implosion of non-overlapping strings via sigma-mediated attraction. The authors connect their findings to the observed onset of radial and elliptic flows in high-multiplicity pA collisions at the LHC (CMS, ALICE, ATLAS) and dAu collisions at RHIC (PHENIX), where the transition to explosive behavior occurs near \( N_{tr} > 80 \). The results enable a microscopic explanation for how a dilute spaghetti state can transform into a hot, chirally symmetric QGP fireball, providing initial conditions for subsequent hydrodynamical evolution.
