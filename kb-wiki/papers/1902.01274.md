# The Golden Era of Neutron Stars: from Hadrons to Quarks

> Neutron stars were first posited in the early thirties, and discovered as pulsars in late sixties; however, only recently are we beginning to understand the matter they contain. This talk describes the continuing development of a consistent picture of the liquid interiors of neutron stars, driven by four advances: observations of two heavy neutron stars with masses $\simeq$ 2.0 solar masses; inferences of masses and radii simultaneously for an increasing number of neutron stars in low mass X-ray binaries, and future determinations via the NICER observatory; the observation of the binary neutron star merger, GW170817, through gravitational waves as well as across the electromagnetic spectrum; and an emerging understanding in QCD of how nuclear matter can turn into deconfined quark matter in the interior. We describe the modern quark-hadron crossover equation of state, QHC18, and the corresponding neutron stars, which agree well with current observations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a consistent picture of neutron star interiors is emerging from four observational advances combined with a theoretical quark-hadron crossover equation of state (QHC18), but the author acknowledges significant remaining uncertainties in the hadronic equation of state, the interpolation between hadronic and quark matter, and the lack of a full microscopic theory of the crossover regime.

## Method

The author reviews the construction of the QHC18 equation of state, which uses the APR hadronic equation of state (and more recently the Togashi equation of state) up to about twice nuclear saturation density (2 n0) and a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for three-flavor quark matter at high densities above 4-7 n0, with a simple polynomial interpolation in pressure versus baryon chemical potential between these two regimes. The NJL model includes a short-range repulsive universal vector interaction with coupling g_V and a BCS diquark pairing interaction with strength H, treated as phenomenological parameters constrained by the requirements of supporting two-solar-mass neutron stars and maintaining mechanical stability.

## Key numbers

- Two heavy neutron stars have masses 1.928 ± 0.017 M⊙ (PSR J1614-2230) and 2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙ (PSR J0348+0432)
- The GW170817 binary neutron star merger had a total mass of 2.73 (+0.04 -0.01) M⊙, with individual masses 1.36-1.60 M⊙ and 1.16-1.36 M⊙
- Nuclear saturation density n0 is approximately 0.16 fm^-3
- The merger produced approximately 0.1 M⊙ of elements heavier than iron via the r-process, including some 10-15 Earth masses of gold
- The QHC18 equation of state yields neutron star radii around 11.5 km for parameter sets that support 2 M⊙ stars
- The maximum neutron star mass for parameter set A is 1.94 M⊙, while sets B, C, and D can support 2 M⊙
- The pairing parameter H values above 1.5 G_s are larger than the conventionally included value of 3/4 G_s in the NJL model

## Context

This work extends the quark-hadron continuity concept first pointed out by Schafer and Wilczek, building on lattice QCD calculations by the Wuppertal-Budapest and HotQCD collaborations that show a continuous crossover with temperature. The QHC18 equation of state agrees remarkably well with the equation of state inferred by LIGO and Virgo from GW170817 (Abbott 2018), and the approach enables future modeling of neutron star mergers at non-zero temperatures for gravitational wave astrophysics, while open questions remain about the microscopic origin of the vector coupling and pairing strengths and the need to go beyond the phenomenological NJL model.
