# Comprehensive Review of 2$β$ Decay Half-Lives

> The double-beta (2$β$)-decay is the rarest nuclear physics process, and its experimental half-lives (T$_{1/2}$) exceed the age of the Universe from nine to fourteen orders of magnitude. Double-beta decay was observed, and its half-life was measured in 14 parent nuclei using direct, radiochemical, and geochemical methods. The decay observables are analyzed using the Evaluated Nuclear Structure Data File (ENSDF) procedures, and the recommended T$_{1/2}$ were deduced. Using the calculated values of phase factors, the effective nuclear matrix elements were extracted and compared with available data. Thousands of theoretical and experimental works have been dedicated to these topics in the last 85 years, and we present two data sets of recommended values to encapsulate the results.

## Key claim

This comprehensive review provides recommended half-life values for double-beta decay in 14 parent nuclei, derived from applying Evaluated Nuclear Structure Data File (ENSDF) evaluation procedures to compiled experimental data, but the authors note that multiple evaluation efforts can produce variant, time-dependent results that may confuse users, and the recommended values are presented as two data sets to encapsulate 85 years of theoretical and experimental work.

## Method

The authors compiled all relevant publications on double-beta decay half-lives before January 1, 2024, from nuclear bibliography databases, combining data from the Institute for Nuclear Research of NASU, Kyiv and the National Nuclear Data Center. They applied ENSDF evaluation practices and procedures developed by the international Nuclear Structure and Decay Data (NSDD) evaluators network to analyze decay observables from direct, radiochemical, and geochemical measurements. Using calculated phase factors, they extracted effective nuclear matrix elements and compared them with available data.

## Context

This work extends the comprehensive compilations of Tretyak and Zdesenko (1995, 2002) and the evaluation efforts of Barabash (2002-2020) and the Particle Data Group, combining them with ENSDF evaluation practices from the international NSDD evaluators network. The recommended half-lives and extracted nuclear matrix elements enable improved comparisons with nuclear structure models and provide a standardized reference for designing next-generation neutrinoless double-beta decay experiments, which currently have half-life limits four to five orders of magnitude higher than the two-neutrino mode half-lives.
