# Estimates of hadron azimuthal anisotropy from multiparton interactions in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV

> We estimate the amount of collective "elliptic flow" expected at mid-rapidity in proton-proton (p-p) collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC), assuming that any possible azimuthal anisotropy of the produced hadrons with respect to the plane of the reaction follows the same overlap-eccentricity and particle-density scalings as found in high-energy heavy ion collisions. Using a Glauber eikonal model, we compute the p-p eccentricities, transverse areas and particle-multiplicities for various phenomenological parametrisations of the proton spatial density. For realistic proton transverse profiles, we find integrated elliptic flow v2 parameters below 3% in p-p collisions at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that the integrated elliptic flow parameter \(v_2\) in proton-proton collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 14\) TeV will be below 3%, assuming that any azimuthal anisotropy follows the same eccentricity and particle-density scalings observed in high-energy heavy ion collisions, and using a Glauber eikonal model with various phenomenological proton spatial density parametrisations.

## Method

The authors used a Glauber eikonal model to compute the eccentricity, transverse overlap area, and particle multiplicity for proton-proton collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 14\) TeV, employing several phenomenological parametrisations of the proton spatial density (hard sphere, exponential, Fermi-I, Fermi-II). They then applied the scaling relation \(v_2/\varepsilon \propto (dN/dy)/A_\perp\) derived from heavy ion data, using fit parameters from the incomplete thermalisation model of Drescher et al. (2007), to estimate the elliptic flow \(v_2\) as a function of impact parameter.

## Key numbers

- Integrated elliptic flow \(v_2\) is below 3% in proton-proton collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 14\) TeV for realistic proton transverse profiles.
- The minimum-bias midrapidity multiplicity is expected to be \(dN_{\text{MB}}/dy \approx 10\) according to PYTHIA or PHOJET Monte Carlo extrapolations.
- The effective parton-parton cross section used is \(\sigma_{gg} = 5.5\) mb, taken from the fit to heavy ion data in Drescher et al. (2007).
- The hydrodynamic-limit ratio used is \((v_2^{\text{hydro}}/\varepsilon) = 0.22\), also from Drescher et al. (2007).
- The proton-proton inelastic cross section at LHC energies is approximately 80 mb.
- For the hard-sphere proton density, the maximum eccentricity \(\varepsilon_{\text{max}} = 1.0\) at \(b \approx 2 R_{\text{rms}}\); for the exponential density, \(\varepsilon_{\text{max}} = -0.4\); for Fermi-I, \(\varepsilon_{\text{max}} = 0.07\); for Fermi-II, \(\varepsilon_{\text{max}} = -0.05\).
- The overlap area at \(b = 0\) fm ranges from 0.58 fm\(^2\) (exponential) to 3.8 fm\(^2\) (Fermi-II).
- The number of binary parton-parton collisions at \(b = 0\) fm ranges from about 2 (Fermi-II) to about 100 (hard sphere and Fermi-I).

## Context

This work extends the scaling of elliptic flow observed in heavy ion collisions at RHIC, as parameterised by the incomplete thermalisation model of Drescher et al. (2007), to the much smaller system of proton-proton collisions at LHC energies. It builds on the observation that at LHC energies, proton-proton collisions may produce enough particles (up to hundreds) to potentially exhibit collective behaviour, similar to that seen in intermediate-size nuclear collisions such as Cu+Cu at RHIC (Adare et al., 2006). The results enable experimental searches for azimuthal anisotropy in high-multiplicity proton-proton events at the LHC, and provide a baseline for comparison with more sophisticated models such as percolation (Cunqueiro et al., 2008), colour-dipole saturation (Kopeliovich et al., 2008), and viscous hydrodynamics (Luzum and Romatschke, 2009).
