# From energy-density functionals to mean field potentials: a systematic derivation

> In this paper we present a systematic method to solve the variational problem of the derivation of a self-consistent Kohn-Sham field from an arbitrary local energy functional. We illustrate this formalism with an application in nuclear physics and give the general mean field associated to the widely used Skyrme effective interaction.

## Key claim

The paper presents a systematic method to derive the self-consistent Kohn-Sham mean-field potential from an arbitrary local energy-density functional by reducing the functional derivative to ordinary derivatives of the energy density with respect to generalized local densities, provided the functional is expressed in terms of those generalized densities.

## Method

The authors express any local energy-density functional in terms of generalized local one-body densities defined as \(\rho_{(A,B)}(\mathbf{r}) = \mathrm{tr}(\delta(\mathbf{\hat{r}}-\mathbf{r})\hat{A}\hat{\rho}\hat{B})\), where \(\hat{A}\) and \(\hat{B}\) are one-body operators. They then show that the variational derivative \(\delta E / \delta \hat{\rho}\) defining the mean-field Hamiltonian \(\hat{W}\) reduces to the sum \(\hat{W} = \sum_{(A,B)} \hat{B} \frac{\partial \mathcal{H}}{\partial \rho_{(A,B)}}(\mathbf{\hat{r}}) \hat{A}\), involving only ordinary derivatives of the energy density \(\mathcal{H}\). The method is applied to the Skyrme energy-density functional, decomposing each term (kinetic, zero-range, density-dependent, effective mass, finite-range, spin-orbit, and spin-gradient) into generalized densities and computing the corresponding mean-field contributions.

## Key numbers

- The Skyrme functional is decomposed into 7 terms: \(\mathcal{K}\), \(\mathcal{H}_0\), \(\mathcal{H}_3\), \(\mathcal{H}_{eff}\), \(\mathcal{H}_{fin}\), \(\mathcal{H}_{SO}\), and \(\mathcal{H}_{SG}\), with coefficients \(a_1\) through \(a_{11}\) expressed as linear combinations of the standard Skyrme parameters \(t_0, t_1, t_2, t_3, x_0, x_1, x_2, x_3, W_0\).
- The density-dependent term \(\mathcal{H}_3\) contains a power \(\alpha\) in the factor \(\rho^\alpha\).
- The effective mass for charge \(q\) is given by \(\frac{1}{2m(\mathbf{\hat{r}})} = \left[ \frac{1}{2m} + a_5 \rho(\mathbf{\hat{r}}) + a_6 \rho_q(\mathbf{\hat{r}}) \right]\).
- The spin-orbit potential is \(\mathbf{V}_q(\mathbf{r}) = a_9 (\mathbf{\nabla}\rho(\mathbf{\hat{r}}) + \mathbf{\nabla}\rho_q(\mathbf{\hat{r}})) + 2a_{10}\mathbf{J}(\mathbf{\hat{r}}) + 2a_{11}\mathbf{J}_q(\mathbf{\hat{r}})\).

## Context

This work extends the density functional theory (DFT) framework, originally established by Hohenberg and Kohn (1964) and Kohn and Sham (1965), to arbitrary local energy functionals beyond the local-density approximation (LDA). It provides a systematic derivation tool for nuclear physics applications using Skyrme effective interactions, as developed by Vautherin and Brink (1972) and later parameterized by Chabanat et al. (1997). The method enables straightforward incorporation of new terms into future generalized functionals, such as those involving higher-order gradients or additional currents.
