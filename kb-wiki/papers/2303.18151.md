# Microscopic calculation of the pinning energy of a vortex in the inner crust of a neutron star

> The structure of a vortex in the inner crust of a pulsar is calculated microscopically in the Wigner-Seitz cell approximation, simulating the conditions of the inner crust of a cold, non-accreting neutron star, in which a lattice of nuclei coexists with a sea of superfluid neutrons. The calculation is based on the axially deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov framework, using effective interactions. The present work extends and improves previous studies in four ways: i) it allows for the axial deformation of protons induced by the large deformation of neutrons due to the appearance of vortices; ii) it includes the effect of Coulomb exchange; iii) considers the possible effects of the screening of the pairing interaction; and iv) it improves the numerical treatment. We also demonstrate that the binding energy of the nucleus-vortex system can be used as a proxy to the pinning energy of a vortex and discuss in which conditions this applies. From our results, we can estimate the mesoscopic pinning forces per unit length acting on vortices. We obtain values ranging between $10^{14}$ to $10^{16}$ dyn/cm, consistent with previous findings.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the binding energy of the nucleus-vortex system can serve as a proxy for the pinning energy of a vortex in the inner crust of a neutron star, but only under specific conditions. The work improves upon previous microscopic calculations by allowing for axial deformation of protons induced by the vortex, including Coulomb exchange, and considering screening of the pairing interaction.

## Method

The authors solve the axially deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) equations in the Wigner-Seitz cell approximation for the inner crust of a cold, non-accreting neutron star. They use effective Skyrme interactions (SLy4 and SkM*) with a density-dependent pairing interaction, and compute the pinning energy as the difference between the energy of a vortex centered on a nucleus and a vortex located between nuclei. The calculation allows for self-consistent axial deformation of both neutrons and protons, includes Coulomb exchange via the Slater approximation, and tests the sensitivity to pairing strength by scaling the pairing gap with a factor beta.

## Key numbers

- Pinning forces per unit length acting on vortices range between 10^14 and 10^16 dyn/cm, consistent with previous findings.
- The critical distance rho* (defined as the nuclear radius plus the coherence length) ranges from 10.7 fm to 21.7 fm depending on the neutron sea density (from 0.001 fm^-3 to 0.038 fm^-3) and the pairing gap reduction factor beta (1 to 3).
- For the SLy4 interaction with beta=1, rho* values range from 11.1 fm at n_infinity = 0.008 fm^-3 to 14.5 fm at n_infinity = 0.037 fm^-3.
- For the SkM* interaction with beta=1, rho* values range from 10.7 fm at n_infinity = 0.008 fm^-3 to 12.3 fm at n_infinity = 0.038 fm^-3.
- The Wigner-Seitz cell radius R_WS ranges from 43.7 fm at the lowest density (0.001 fm^-3) to 21.4 fm at the highest density (0.037-0.038 fm^-3).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic HFB calculation of Avogadro et al. (2007, 2008) by removing the assumption of spherical proton symmetry, including Coulomb exchange, and improving numerical convergence. It contrasts with the semiclassical Local Density Approximation approach of Donati and Pizzochero (2004, 2006) and the hydrodynamic Ginzburg-Landau treatment of Epstein and Baym (1988). The results enable estimates of the mesoscopic pinning force landscape that defines the theoretical upper limit on the depinning threshold, which can be compared with observations of giant glitches in the Vela pulsar as discussed by Antonelli and Haskell (2020) and Antonelli, Montoli, and Pizzochero (2018).
