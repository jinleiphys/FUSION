# Simulation of fusion and quasi-fission in nuclear reactions leading to production of superheavy elements using the Constrained Molecular Dynamics model

> Fusion dynamics and the onset of quasi-fission in reactions, leading to production of superheavy nuclei are investigated using the constrained molecular dynamics model. Constraints on the parameters of the nuclear equation of state are derived from experimental fusion probabilities. The obtained constraint on the modulus of incompressibility of nuclear matter K0 = 240 - 260 MeV is consistent with the results of previous study using the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation and also with constraints derived using the recent neutron star binary collision event GW170817. Unlike the modulus of incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter, the stiffness of the density-dependence of symmetry energy influences the fusion probability only weakly.

## Key claim
The Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) model constrains the modulus of incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter to K0 = 240–260 MeV, consistent with prior Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) results and the neutron star merger event GW170817, while the stiffness of the density-dependence of symmetry energy has only a weak influence on fusion probability.

## Method
The authors performed CoMD simulations of central collisions (impact parameter 0.5 fm) for six representative reactions at beam energy 5 MeV/nucleon, using a four-dimensional parameter grid of incompressibility K0 (200–290 MeV), symmetry-energy stiffness γ (0.5–1), surface-energy coefficient Cs (0 to –2 MeV·fm²), and Gaussian wavepacket width σr (1.0–1.15 fm). Simulated fusion probabilities were compared to experimental data for reactions ranging from ⁴⁸Ca+²⁰⁸Pb (fusion dominant) to ⁶⁴Ni+²³⁸U (quasi-fission dominant), with 40 random seeds per parameter set and evolution up to 3000 fm/c.

## Key numbers
- Modulus of incompressibility constraint: K0 = 240–260 MeV, derived from experimental fusion probabilities.
- Two most successful parameter sets: (1) K0 = 245 MeV, Cs = 0 MeV·fm², σr = 1.085 fm; (2) K0 = 254 MeV, Cs = –1 MeV·fm², σr = 1.0 fm.
- For ⁴⁸Ca+²³⁸U, fusion probability constrained to 20–50% for central events; for ⁶⁴Ni+¹⁸⁶W, fusion probability constrained to 40–80% for central events.
- For ⁶⁴Ni+²⁰⁸Pb, ⁴⁸Ca+²⁴⁹Cf, and ⁶⁴Ni+²³⁸U, fusion suppression is 10⁻³–10⁻⁵ relative to total events.
- Simulations performed up to time 3000 fm/c; beam energy 5 MeV/nucleon for all reactions.

## Context
This work extends the prior BUU study by Veselsky et al. (2018) using a different transport model (CoMD) to verify constraints on the nuclear equation of state from fusion–quasi-fission competition. The CoMD results confirm the K0 range 240–260 MeV obtained from BUU and are consistent with constraints from the neutron star merger GW170817. The findings enable further microscopic studies of superheavy element formation and connect nuclear-matter properties to astrophysical observations, though the weak sensitivity to symmetry-energy stiffness requires additional investigation.
