# Phase diagram of two-color QCD matter at finite baryon and axial isospin densities

> We study the two-color QCD matter within two fundamental quark flavors via both chiral perturbation theory and Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model methods. The effective Lagrangian described by low lying meson and baryon, i.e., diquark, is derived, where the excitations locate in the extended $\mathrm{SU}(4)$ flavor symmetry space. We determine the leading order terms on the dependence of the baryon and axial isospin densities. Then, the two-color NJL model is employed to run the numerical simulation and the phase diagram in the plane of $μ-ν_{5}$ is plotted.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in two-color QCD with two fundamental quark flavors, the phase diagram in the plane of baryon chemical potential (μ) and axial isospin chemical potential (ν₅) contains four distinct phases including a mixed state of neutral pion and scalar diquark condensates, but this result is obtained within a simplified NJL model that neglects back-reaction on electromagnetic fields and Polyakov loop effects, and therefore cannot capture (de)confinement.

## Method

The authors first derive the leading-order effective Lagrangian in chiral perturbation theory within the extended SU(4) flavor symmetry space to identify the symmetry breaking patterns and determine which condensates (π₃, scalar diquark Δ, and axial vector diquark d) are favored at finite μ and ν₅. They then employ a two-color Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a gluon-inspired four-fermion interaction, apply the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce auxiliary meson and diquark fields, and compute the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field (Hartree) approximation at zero temperature. The gap equations are solved numerically to map the phase diagram.

## Key numbers

* The momentum cutoff Λ = 0.78 GeV, coupling constant G = H = 10.3 GeV⁻², and current quark mass m₀ = 4.5 MeV are used as the parameter set, borrowed from PhysRevD.70.054013.
* The diquark condensate onsets when the chemical potential μ exceeds half of m_Δ = m_π ≈ 0.14 GeV; the phase diagram is therefore studied starting from μ = 0.07 GeV.
* The phase diagram in the μ-ν₅ plane is plotted from μ = 0.07 GeV upward, revealing four phases: pion superfluid (π₃), scalar diquark (Δ), axial vector diquark (d), and a mixed state of π₃ and Δ.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of two-color QCD at finite baryon and isospin densities (Kogut et al., 2000; Hands et al., 2001) by introducing an axial isospin chemical potential ν₅ to mimic the effect of (anti)parallel electromagnetic fields and the chiral anomaly. The chiral rotation toward π₀ driven by the electromagnetic anomaly was previously found by Cao et al. (2016), and the present work confirms that neutral pion condensation (rather than charged pion condensation) occurs at nonzero ν₅. The results enable future lattice QCD simulations of two-color QCD at finite baryon and axial isospin densities, and support further investigation of low-temperature/high-density matter relevant to heavy-ion collisions and compact stars.
