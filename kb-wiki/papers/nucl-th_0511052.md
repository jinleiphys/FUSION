# Large Observed $v_2$ as a Signature for Deconfinement

> We present a new plot for representing RAA data that emphasizes the strong correlation between high-pt suppression and its elliptic anisotropy. We demonstrate that existing models cannot reproduce the centrality dependence of this correlation. Modification of a geometric energy loss model to include thermal absorption and stimulated emission can match the trend of the data, but requires dN/dy values inconsistent with the observed multiplicity. By including a small, outward-normal directed surface impulse opposing energy loss, $Δpt \hat{n}$, one can account for the centrality dependence of the observed Au+Au elliptic quench pattern. We also present predictions for Cu+Cu reactions.

## Key claim
Existing models of jet quenching cannot simultaneously reproduce the observed correlation between high-\(p_\perp\) suppression (\(R_{AA}\)) and its elliptic anisotropy (\(v_2\)) across centrality in Au+Au collisions; a small, outward-normal directed surface impulse of \(\Delta p_\perp \hat{n}\) opposing energy loss can account for the centrality dependence, but models including thermal absorption and stimulated emission require gluon densities inconsistent with measured multiplicities.

## Method
The author constructs a geometric radiative energy loss model (GREL) based on the first order in opacity (FOO) formula, with energy loss \(\epsilon = \Delta E_{rad}/E = \kappa I\), where \(I\) is a pathlength integral through a 1D Bjorken-expanding medium. Partons are distributed in the nuclear overlap region according to the nuclear overlap function \(T_{AA}\) and isotropically in azimuth; the survival probability uses a power-law spectrum Jacobian \((1-\epsilon)^n\) with \(n=4\). The model is extended to include thermal absorption (\(\epsilon = \kappa I - k I_2\)) and a momentum "punch" (\(\Delta p_\perp\)) applied after propagation. The \(v_2\) vs. \(R_{AA}\) correlation is evaluated by computing \(R_{AA}(\phi)\) at 24 azimuthal angles and extracting Fourier modes.

## Key numbers
- For the GREL model with thermal absorption, fitting the 20-30% centrality PHENIX \(\pi^0\) data point requires \(\kappa = 0.5\) and \(k = 0.25\).
- Inverting the absorption model for \(E = 6\) GeV, \(L = 5\) fm, and \(\alpha_s = 0.4\) gives \(dN_g^{rad}/dy \sim 1000\) and \(dN_g^{abs}/dy \sim 3000\) for most central collisions; for \(E = 10\) GeV, \(dN_g^{abs}/dy \sim 9000\).
- The momentum punch model fits the PHENIX 20-30% \(\pi^0\) data with \(\kappa = 0.18\) and \(\Delta p_\perp = 0.5\) GeV on a 10 GeV parton (a 5% impulse).
- The hard sphere geometry uses \(R_{HS} = 6.78\) fm to match the mean squared Woods-Saxon radius.

## Context
This work extends the geometric radiative energy loss approach of Drees, Feng, and Jia (2004) and the first-order in opacity formalism of Gyulavy, Levai, and Vitev (2001). It demonstrates that pure radiative energy loss models and the Molnar parton cascade (MPC) with extreme cross sections (\(\sigma_t \sim 45\) mb) cannot reproduce the centrality dependence of the \(v_2\) vs. \(R_{AA}\) correlation. The failure of the detailed balance (absorption) model to yield consistent gluon densities points to deconfinement effects (latent heat, bag constant, screening mass) as the origin of the required surface impulse. The paper provides predictions for Cu+Cu collisions and suggests that the \(p_\perp\) dependence of \(R_{AA}(\phi; p_\perp)\) should be calculated to test the deconfinement hypothesis.
