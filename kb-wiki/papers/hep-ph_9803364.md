# Scenario for Ultrarelativistic Nuclear Collisions: Space--Time Picture of Quantum Fluctuations and the Birth of QGP

> We study the dynamics of quantum fluctuations which take place at the earliest stage of high-energy processes and the conditions under which the data from e-p deep-inelastic scattering may serve as an input for computing the initial data for heavy-ion collisions at high energies. Our method is essentially based on the space-time picture of these seemingly different phenomena. We prove that the ultra-violet renormalization of the virtual loops does not bring any scale into the problem. The scale appears only in connection with the collinear cut-off in the evolution equations and is defined by the physical properties of the final state. In heavy-ion collisions the basic screening effect is due to the mass of the collective modes (plasmons) in the dense non-equilibrium quark-gluon system, which is estimated. We avoid the standard parton phenomenology and suggest a dedicated class of evolution equations which describe the dynamics of quantum fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions.

# Scenario for Ultrarelativistic Nuclear Collisions: Space-Time Picture of Quantum Fluctuations and the Birth of QGP

## Key claim

The dense quark-gluon system (QGP) can be formed only in a single quantum transition, and the scale for this transition is not introduced by ultraviolet renormalization but rather emerges from the collinear cut-off defined by the physical properties of the final state, specifically, the screening mass of collective modes (plasmons) in the dense non-equilibrium quark-gluon system. The authors claim that deep-inelastic scattering data can serve as input for heavy-ion collision calculations only if the evolution equations are modified to replace the universal running coupling with form-factors that depend on the type and scale of the emission process.

## Method

The authors derive evolution equations for gluon dynamics using the Keldysh-Schwinger technique adapted for inclusive observables, working in the light-cone gauge with a causal time-ordered picture of quantum fluctuations. They prove dispersion relations for retarded vertex functions and self-energies, showing that ultraviolet renormalization must be performed at a subtraction point corresponding to on-mass-shell conditions (p⁻=0) rather than at asymptotically high scales. The plasmon mass in the dense non-equilibrium system is computed using proper-time dynamics (with gauge A^τ=0) rather than null-plane dynamics, which the authors show cannot generate an effective mass for quasi-particles.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal size of Lorentz-contracted nuclei in the laboratory frame is R₀/γ ~ 0.1 fm
- The allowed range for the renormalization scale μ² is 0 < μ² < p_t², where p_t is the transverse momentum of the emission
- The formation time for states with transverse momentum p_t is τ ~ 1/p_t
- The saturation scale for the formation of new states is p_t ~ m_D, where m_D is the effective mass of partons in the collective system, with formation time τ_D ~ 1/m_D
- The cut-off mass for screening in the chemically non-equilibrated plasma is given by m² ~ g² n_gluon/T (citing Xiong)
- The plasmon mass is a function of the time of the distribution measurement, with the result that larger p_t emissions form earlier and have smaller effective mass

## Context

This work extends the space-time picture of quantum fluctuations originally developed by Gribov and the time-ordered approach of Lepage and Brodsky, while departing from the standard parton phenomenology and factorization-based scattering theory. The authors contrast their approach with the McLerran-Venugopalan model, which associated the scale with valence quark density in the initial state, and with the DGLAP and Altarelli-Parisi evolution equations, arguing that the running coupling in those equations must be replaced by process-dependent form-factors. The paper builds on earlier work by Shuryak and Xiong and by Eskola, Muller, and Wang on screening effects in nuclear collisions, but goes further by proving that screening parameters determine the collinear cut-off self-consistently within the evolution equations themselves. The authors also note that Jianwei Qiu first drew attention to the special role of static (contact) propagators in computing twist-4 corrections to DIS.
