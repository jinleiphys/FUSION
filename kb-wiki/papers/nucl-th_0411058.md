# Spectator theory for three-nucleon electromagnetic currents

> In this talk I present some of the more recent developments within the Covariant Spectator Theory. My focus will be on aspects of the derivation of gauge invariant electromagnetic three-nucleon currents which are consistent with the hadronic equations and with the basic assumptions of this framework. Important dynamical ingredients of the three-nucleon currents are also discussed, namely the three-nucleon bound state vertex function and the two-nucleon interaction model they are derived from.

## Key claim

The Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) provides a manifestly covariant framework for deriving gauge invariant electromagnetic three-nucleon currents that are consistent with the hadronic equations and the spectator-on-mass-shell constraint, resolving an apparent contradiction between earlier derivations by Kvinikhidze and Blankleider (1997) and Adam and Van Orden (2004) by showing their equivalence.

## Method

The author derives electromagnetic three-nucleon currents within the CST by applying a diagrammatic approach that systematically attaches photons to all propagators and momentum-dependent vertices while avoiding double counting. The "gauging of integral equations method" of Kvinikhidze and Blankleider (1997) is used to generate the infinite series of diagrams representing the current, and diagrams with off-shell spectators are shown to be equivalent to diagrams with all spectators on mass shell. The core diagrams for the current are identified, and separate expressions are given for elastic scattering and electrodisintegration (breakup) of the three-nucleon bound state.

## Key numbers

- The potential named W16 yields a triton binding energy of E_t = -8.491 MeV, closest to the experimental value of -8.48 MeV, with chi^2 = 1.895 for nu = 1.6 (nu_sigma = 4.16, nu_delta = -1.2).
- The preliminary model WJL19-1.1 with 19 free parameters achieves chi^2 = 1.254 and yields a triton binding energy of E_t = -9.116 MeV.
- The nonrelativistic limit calculation using a specially fitted potential gives a three-nucleon binding energy of -7.914 MeV.
- The family of NN potentials was fitted with only 13 adjustable parameters (for the W16 model) and used the Nijmegen np phase shift analysis of Stoks et al. (1993).

## Context

This work extends the Covariant Spectator Theory, previously applied successfully to the deuteron, nucleon-nucleon scattering (Gross, Van Orden, and Holinde, 1992), and the three-nucleon bound state (Stadler, 1997), to the derivation of electromagnetic currents for three-nucleon systems. The derived currents will enable calculations of elastic form factors and electrodisintegration of the three-nucleon bound state, which are currently in progress. The approach resolves a long-standing double-counting problem in covariant scattering theories that was known from work by Taylor (1963) and Phillips (1995).
