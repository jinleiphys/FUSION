# Collinear parton distributions and the structure of the nucleon sea in a light-front meson-cloud model

> The unpolarized, helicity and transversity parton distribution functions of the nucleon are studied within a convolution model where the bare nucleon is dressed by its virtual meson cloud. Using light-front time-ordered perturbation theory, the Fock states of the physical nucleon are expanded in a series involving a bare nucleon and two-particle, meson-baryon, states. The bare baryons and mesons are described with light-front wave functions (LFWFs) for the corresponding valence-parton components. Using a representation in terms of overlap of LFWFs, the role of the non-perturbative antiquark degrees of freedom and the valence quark contribution at the input scale of the model is discussed for the leading-twist collinear parton distributions. After introducing perturbative QCD effects through evolution to experimental scales, the results are compared with available data and phenomenological extractions. Predictions for the nucleon tensor charge are also presented, finding a very good agreement with recent phenomenological extractions.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a light-front meson-cloud model, where the nucleon is dressed by virtual meson-baryon fluctuations, can simultaneously describe unpolarized, helicity, and transversity parton distribution functions (PDFs) of the nucleon, but only after the model's hadronic scale is matched to perturbative QCD evolution at a specific input scale \(Q_0^2 = 0.186\) GeV\(^2\) and the results are evolved to experimental scales.

## Method
The authors construct the physical nucleon state as a superposition of a bare nucleon and two-particle meson-baryon Fock states using light-front time-ordered perturbation theory. The bare baryons and mesons are described by light-front wave functions (LFWFs) for their valence-parton components, with the nucleon LFWFs taken from two different quark models (a phenomenological fit to electroweak form factors and a relativistic hypercentral quark model). The leading-twist collinear PDFs are computed via convolution formulas that account for contributions from both the baryon and meson in the fluctuation, and the results are evolved to experimental scales using leading-order QCD evolution equations.

## Key numbers
- The probability for the proton to fluctuate into a nucleon-pion state is \(P_{p/N\pi} = 13.17\%\).
- The probability for the proton to fluctuate into a nucleon-rho state is \(P_{p/N\rho} = 2.17\%\).
- The input scale of the model is determined to be \(Q_0^2 = 0.186\) GeV\(^2\), where the valence quark momentum fraction \(\langle q_{\text{NS}}(Q_0^2)\rangle_2 = 0.94\).
- The cut-off parameter for the phenomenological vertex form factors is \(\Lambda_{BM} = 0.8\) GeV for all baryon-meson fluctuations.
- The coupling constants used are \(g^2_{NN\pi}/4\pi = 13.6\) and \(g^2_{NN\rho}/4\pi = 0.84\).
- For model 1 (phenomenological fit), the quark mass is \(m_q = 0.264\) GeV, the scale parameter \(\beta = 0.489\) GeV, and the power-law parameter \(\gamma = 3.21\).
- For the pion LFWF, the parameters are \(\alpha = 0.3659\) GeV and \(m_q = 0.22\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends the light-front meson-cloud model previously applied to unpolarized PDFs (Traini 2013, Traini 2011) and generalized parton distributions (Pasquini 2006, Pasquini 2007) to include longitudinally polarized and transversity PDFs for the first time in this framework. The transversity distribution has never been discussed in the context of meson-cloud models before. The approach builds on the Sullivan process (Sullivan 1971) and the meson-cloud model tradition (Speth 1996, Londergan 1998, Kumano 1997) that explains flavor-symmetry violation in the nucleon sea (Thomas 1983). The results enable predictions for the nucleon tensor charge and provide a framework for comparing non-perturbative sea-quark contributions with experimental data and phenomenological extractions.
