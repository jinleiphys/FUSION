# Evaluating chiral symmetry restoration through the use of sum rules

> We pursue the idea of assessing chiral restoration via in-medium modifications of hadronic spectral functions of chiral partners. The usefulness of sum rules in this endeavor is illustrated, focusing on the vector and axial-vector channels. We first present an update on constructing quantitative results for pertinent vacuum spectral functions. These spectral functions serve as a basis upon which the in-medium spectral functions can be constructed. A striking feature of our analysis of the vacuum spectral functions is the need to include excited resonances, dictated by satisfying the Weinberg-type sum rules. This includes excited states in both the vector and axial-vector channels. Preliminary results for the finite temperature vector spectral function are presented. Based on a rho spectral function tested in dilepton data which develops a shoulder at low energies, we find that the rho' peak flattens off. The flattening may be a sign of chiral restoration, though a study of the finite temperature axial-vector spectral function remains to be carried out.

## Key claim

The paper argues that sum rules, particularly Weinberg-type sum rules applied to chiral partners (vector and axial-vector mesons), can be used to assess chiral symmetry restoration in hot QCD matter, but the authors emphasize that a definitive conclusion requires quantitative knowledge of both the vector and axial-vector spectral functions in medium, which is not yet complete.

## Method

The authors construct vacuum spectral functions for the isovector vector (rho) and axial-vector (a1) channels using tau-decay data from ALEPH and OPAL, modeling the ground-state resonances, excited resonances, and a perturbative QCD continuum. They then apply finite-temperature QCD sum rules and Weinberg-type sum rules to constrain the in-medium modifications of the vector spectral function, using a hadron resonance gas model for the temperature dependence of condensates and a microscopic model for the rho meson spectral function that has been tested against dilepton data.

## Key numbers

* Quark condensate vacuum value: ⟨q̄q⟩ = (-0.25 GeV)³
* Gluon condensate vacuum value: ⟨(α_s/π) G²_μν⟩ = 0.022 ± 0.002 GeV⁴
* Factorization parameter κ = 2.1^{+0.3}_{-0.2} (assumed equal for vector and axial-vector 4-quark condensates)
* Excited axial-vector meson (a₁′) mass: approximately 1.8 GeV with width approximately 200 MeV
* Weinberg-type sum rule agreement after including a₁′: 0th sum rule -1.28%, 1st and 2nd sum rules ~0%, 3rd sum rule -96%
* Optimized average deviations for QCD sum rules: 0.24% for vector channel, 0.56% for axial-vector channel

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral symmetry restoration using sum rules, which are reviewed in Friman et al. (2011), by incorporating more realistic spectral functions with Breit-Wigner forms rather than delta-function approximations for ground-state resonances. The vacuum spectral functions are constrained by tau-decay data from ALEPH (Barate 1998) and OPAL (Ackerstaff 1998), and the in-medium rho spectral function builds on microscopic models by Urban (1999) and Rapp (1999, 2002) that successfully describe dilepton data from heavy-ion collisions (Arnaldi 2006, Adamova 2006). The finite-temperature QCD sum rules follow the formalism of Hatsuda (1992) and Kapusta (1993). The results enable future work to construct the finite-temperature axial-vector spectral function and test whether the vector and axial-vector channels become degenerate at high temperature, which would signal chiral restoration.
