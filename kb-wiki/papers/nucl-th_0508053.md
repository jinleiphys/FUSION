# Beam normal spin asymmetry of elastic $eN$ scattering in the leading logarithm approximation

> The beam normal spin asymmetry for the elastic $eN$ scattering is studied in the leading logarithm approximation. We derive the expression for the asymmetry, which is valid for any scattering angles. The result is compared with the results of other authors, obtained for the forward kinematics. We also calculate the numerical values of the asymmetry at intermediate energy and show that they are consistent with existing experimental data.

## Key claim

The beam normal spin asymmetry \(B_n\) for elastic \(eN\) scattering is calculated in the leading logarithm approximation, yielding an expression valid for any scattering angles that satisfy \(\sin^2(\theta/2) \ln(Q^2/m^2) \gg 1\), rather than only for forward kinematics as in prior work.

## Method

The asymmetry is derived from the unitarity relation for the two-photon exchange amplitude, isolating the double-logarithmic contribution \(\sim m \ln^2(Q^2/m^2)\) by systematically expanding the integrand around the singularities where the virtual photon momenta vanish. The hadronic tensor is expressed in terms of helicity amplitudes for intermediate hadronic states (baryon resonances and threshold pion production), and the leptonic tensor is evaluated in the leading-log approximation, keeping only the dominant electron mass dependence.

## Key numbers

- At electron lab energy \(E_{\text{lab}} = 0.2\) GeV (SAMPLE experiment), the calculated \(B_n\) for elastic \(ep\) scattering is consistent with experimental data.
- At \(E_{\text{lab}} = 0.3\) GeV (near the Delta resonance peak), the asymmetry shows a resonant shape.
- At \(E_{\text{lab}} = 0.57\) GeV and \(0.855\) GeV (MAMI experimental points), the calculated asymmetry agrees with experiment.
- For forward kinematics, the asymmetry scales as \(B_n \sim Q^3 \ln^2(Q^2/m^2)\).
- The double-logarithmic factor \(\ln^2(Q^2/m^2)\) is approximately 200 for \(Q^2 = 0.25\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of the beam normal spin asymmetry by Afanasev and Merenkov (2004) and Gorchtein (2004), which were restricted to forward scattering kinematics. The present result is valid for any scattering angle satisfying the condition \(\sin^2(\theta/2) \ln(Q^2/m^2) \gg 1\), and it differs from Gorchtein (2004) in the relation between the asymmetry and the hadron electromagnetic transition amplitudes (e.g., the magnetic form factor \(G_M\) enters the present result but is absent in Gorchtein's expression). The numerical predictions for the proton target agree with SAMPLE and MAMI experimental data, and predictions for the neutron target are provided for future experiments.
