# Systematics of low-lying states of even-even nuclei in the neutron-deficient lead region from a beyond-mean-field calculation

> Nuclei located in the neutron-deficient Pb region have a complex structure, rapidly evolving as a function of neutron and proton numbers. The most famous example is $^{186}$Pb where the three lowest levels are $0^+$ states, the two excited $0^+$ ones being located at low excitation energy around 600 keV. Coexisting structures with different properties are found in the neighboring nuclei. Many experiments have been performed over the last few years in which in-band and out-of-band $γ$ transition probabilities have been measured. A detailed interpretation of experimental data requires the use of a method going beyond a mean-field approach that permits to determine spectra and transition probabilities. Such methods have already been applied to selected isotopes in this mass region. Our aim is to provide a systematic investigation of this mass region in order to determine how well experimental data can be understood using a state-of-the-art method for nuclear structure. The starting point of our method is a set of mean-field wave functions generated with a constraint on the axial quadrupole moment and using a Skyrme energy density functional. Correlations beyond the mean field are introduced by projecting mean-field wave functions on angular-momentum and particle number and by mixing the symmetry restored wave functions as a function of the axial quadrupole moment. A detailed comparison with the available data is performed for energies, charge radii, spectroscopic quadrupole moments, $E0$ and $E2$ transition probabilities for the isotopic chains of neutron deficient Hg, Pb, Po and Rn. The connection between our results and the underlying mean field is also analyzed. Qualitative agreement with the data is obtained although our results indicate that the actual energy density functionals have to be improved further to achieve a quantitative agreement.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a beyond-mean-field method combining angular-momentum and particle-number projection with the generator coordinate method (GCM) using the SLy6 Skyrme energy density functional can achieve qualitative agreement with experimental data for low-lying states in neutron-deficient Hg, Pb, Po, and Rn isotopes, but the authors explicitly state that the energy density functionals must be improved further to achieve quantitative agreement.

## Method

The authors start with a set of Hartree-Fock (HF)+BCS mean-field wave functions generated with a constraint on the axial mass quadrupole moment using the SLy6 parametrization of the Skyrme interaction and a density-dependent zero-range pairing force. Correlations beyond the mean field are introduced by projecting these wave functions onto good angular momentum and particle number, then mixing the symmetry-restored wave functions as a function of the axial quadrupole moment using the generator coordinate method (GCM). The resulting correlated states are used to calculate excitation energies, charge radii, spectroscopic quadrupole moments, and E0 and E2 transition probabilities directly in the laboratory frame without effective charges.

## Key numbers

* For Pb isotopes, the oblate minimum excitation energy decreases from 0.76 MeV at N=112 to 0.50 MeV at N=108, then increases to 1.63 MeV at N=102.
* The prolate minimum excitation energy in Pb decreases from 1.47 MeV in 190Pb to 0.61 MeV in 184Pb, then rises to 0.82 MeV in 182Pb.
* The energy gain from angular-momentum projection onto J=0 reaches about 3 MeV around |β| ≈ 0.1 for nuclei such as 186Hg, 188Pb, and 190Po.
* For 200Po, the absolute minimum is obtained for a non-axial configuration with β ≈ 0.1 and γ ≈ 10°, with the prolate saddle located about 100 keV higher.
* The SLy6 parametrization has a surface energy coefficient a_surf = 17.7 MeV, while SLy4 has a_surf = 18.4 MeV, leading to different behavior for superdeformed configurations.

## Context

This work extends previous beyond-mean-field calculations that had been applied only to selected isotopes in the neutron-deficient lead region (Bender 2004, Duguet 2003) by providing a systematic investigation across four isotopic chains (Hg, Pb, Po, Rn). The results are compared with experimental data from multiple sources (Andreyev 2000, Julin 2001, Dewald 2003, Grahn 2008, Grahn 2009, Coc 2011) and with calculations using the Gogny D1s force (Guzman 2004, Bruyeres). The study enables future improvements of energy density functionals specifically fitted for beyond-mean-field calculations, as the authors note that the current parametrizations (SLy6 and SLy4) each have deficiencies in describing either the relative excitation energies or the stiffness of deformation energy curves.
