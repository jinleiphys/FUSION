# Phase space and quark mass effects in neutrino emissions in a color superconductor

> We study the phase space for neutrino emissions with massive quarks in direct Urca processes in normal and color superconducting quark matter. We derive in QCD and the NJL model the Fermi momentum reduction resulting from Fermi liquid properties which opens up the phase space for neutrino emissions. The relation between the Fermi momentum and chemical potential is found to be $p_{F}\approxμ(1-κ)$ with $κ$ depending on coupling constants. We find in the weak coupling regime that $κ$ is a monotonously increasing function of the chemical potential. This implies quenched phase space for neutrino emissions at low baryon densities. We calculate neutrino emissivities with massive quarks in a spin-one color superconductor. The quark mass corrections are found to be of the same order as the contributions in the massless case, which will bring sizable effects on the cooling behavior of compact stars.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in both QCD and the NJL model, the Fermi momentum reduction coefficient \(\kappa\) (where \(p_F \approx \mu(1-\kappa)\)) is a monotonically increasing function of chemical potential in the weak coupling regime, implying that the phase space for neutrino emissions is quenched at low baryon densities, a result the abstract presents as robust but which the full text qualifies as valid only under the assumption that the effective quark mass is much smaller than the chemical potential and that flavor dependence of the quark mass is weak and negligible.

## Method

The authors derived the quark-quark forward scattering amplitude via one-gluon exchange in QCD and via scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector channels in the NJL model, then used Landau Fermi liquid theory to obtain the relation \(p_F = \mu(1-\kappa)\) and the density dependence of \(\kappa(\mu)\). They extended their earlier formalism for neutrino emissivities in spin-one color superconductors (Schmitt 2005) by including quark masses, assuming the same form of the gap matrix as in the massless case and working in the collinear and small mass limit to compute mass corrections to the emissivity.

## Key numbers

- In the NJL model with scalar/pseudoscalar coupling \(G_S = 5.1 \times 10^{-6}\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-2}\) and \(\mu = 500\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\kappa \approx 0.052\) and the effective quark mass \(m \approx 160\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- In the NJL model with vector coupling \(G_V = 4.5 \times 10^{-5}\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-2}\), \(\kappa \approx 0.13\) and the effective quark mass \(m \approx 250\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The mass corrections to the neutrino emissivity in the CSL and Planar phases are about 26% and 15% of the massless contributions at low temperatures, respectively.
- The nuclear saturation density is given as \(\rho_0 \sim 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) or \(2.7 \times 10^{14}\ \mathrm{g/cm}^3\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of neutrino emissivity in spin-one color superconducting phases (Schmitt 2005) by incorporating quark mass effects, and builds on the Fermi liquid theory of Baym (1975) and Schafer (2004) which derived the Fermi momentum reduction via one-gluon exchange for massless quarks. The results enable more realistic modeling of compact star cooling, particularly for spin-one phases whose gaps of a few tens of keV (Popov 2005) can fit cooling data, and the mass corrections found here are predicted to have sizable effects on the cooling behavior of compact stars.
