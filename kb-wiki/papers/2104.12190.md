# The low energy inclusive $ν_μ (ν_{e})-^{12}$C scattering revisited

> We have reviewed the current status of the inclusive neutrino scattering from $^{12}$C in the low energy region corresponding to the neutrino beams from the pion, muon and kaon decaying at rest. The theoretical calculations of total cross sections in various nuclear models with special emphasis on the recent experiments with the monoenergetic neutrinos from KDAR [1] along with the older experiments from KARMEN and LSND collaborations have been discussed in the context of the recent works by Akbar et al. [2] and Nikolakopoulos et al. [3]. The inadequacy of the various theoretical models used to explain the experimental results on the inclusive neutrino scattering from nuclei at low energies has been highlighted and the need for a better understanding of the nuclear medium effects beyond the impulse approximation has been emphasized.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the persistent discrepancy between theoretical predictions and experimental measurements of inclusive neutrino-¹²C cross sections at low energies, seen in both the older LSND experiments and the recent MiniBooNE KDAR measurement, cannot be resolved within the impulse approximation and requires a better understanding of nuclear medium effects beyond it, such as nucleon-nucleon correlations and meson exchange currents.

## Method

The authors review the experimental results from KARMEN, LSND, and MiniBooNE collaborations for inclusive νₑ and ν_μ scattering on ¹²C at low energies (E_ν ≤ 280 MeV for ν_μ and E_ν ≤ 52.8 MeV for νₑ), as well as inclusive muon capture rates. They compare these data with theoretical predictions from two broad classes of nuclear models: microscopic shell-model-based calculations (including RPA, CRPA, QRPA, and large-basis shell models) and Fermi gas models (non-relativistic and relativistic, local and global, with and without RPA correlations and spectral functions). The comparison focuses on calculations that simultaneously address all three weak processes (νₑ capture, ν_μ capture, and muon capture) using the same nuclear model.

## Key numbers

* MiniBooNE KDAR measurement: inclusive ν_μ-¹²C cross section = (2.7 ± 0.9 ± 0.8) × 10⁻³⁹ cm²/neutron at E_ν = 236 MeV (monoenergetic from K⁺ → μ⁺ ν_μ decay).
* Monoenergetic KDAR ν_μ energy: 236 MeV from K⁺ → μ⁺ ν_μ decay.
* LSND ν_μ experiments: average neutrino energy above muon production threshold (E_th = 123.1 MeV) is approximately 170 MeV, with flux uncertainty of about 15% due to pion production cross section uncertainties.
* World average axial dipole mass M_A = 1.026 ± 0.016 GeV from quasielastic scattering, or 1.014 ± 0.016 GeV from threshold pion electroproduction.
* MiniBooNE anomaly required M_A = 1.35 ± 0.17 GeV to explain data without nuclear medium effects beyond impulse approximation.
* NOMAD collaboration on ⁵⁶Fe reported M_A = 1.05 ± 0.02 ± 0.06 GeV, consistent with world average.

## Context

This review extends the long-standing discussion of low-energy neutrino-¹²C scattering that began with experiments at Brookhaven National Laboratory approximately 40 years ago and continued with KARMEN and LSND collaborations. It directly addresses the recent MiniBooNE measurement with monoenergetic KDAR neutrinos (Aguilar-Arevalo 2018), which shows a discrepancy similar to that encountered in LSND experiments two decades ago. The paper contextualizes this within the broader MiniBooNE axial dipole mass anomaly, where calculations by Martini et al. (2010, 2011) and Nieves et al. (2011) showed that including 2p-2h, ph-Δh, and meson exchange current effects could resolve the anomaly using the world average M_A. The review emphasizes that similar calculations at lower energies (relevant to LSND and KDAR) are needed, and that future experiments at Fermilab and JPARC with ¹²C and ⁴⁰Ar targets will benefit from improved theoretical understanding.
