# Stable evolution of relativistic hydrodynamics order-by-order in gradients

> We provide a systematic framework for solving the initial value problem for relativistic hydrodynamics formulated as a gradient expansion. Secular growth is handled by a suitable covariant resummation scheme, which reorganises the degrees of freedom at each order in the expansion while preserving the sum. Our scheme can be applied to any order in the gradient expansion; we provide the explicit formulation at first and second orders. When working to first order, we find that the BDNK equations of motion emerge as an intermediate step in a calculation performed in the Landau frame. We show that non-hydrodynamic modes appear only in such intermediate calculations and cancel when evaluating solutions to the required order. Our procedure does not introduce any other fields or require any additional initial data beyond those appearing in the theory of ideal fluids.

## Key claim

The paper provides a systematic framework for solving the initial value problem in relativistic hydrodynamics using a gradient expansion, where secular growth is handled by a covariant resummation scheme that reorganizes degrees of freedom at each order while preserving the sum; crucially, the non-hydrodynamic modes that appear in intermediate calculations cancel out when evaluating the final physical solution to the required order in gradients.

## Method

The authors expand the hydrodynamic fields (temperature and fluid velocity) in a formal gradient expansion parameter epsilon, then apply a covariant field redefinition at each order that transfers secularly growing pieces from higher to lower orders while leaving the full physical fields invariant to the required order. This resummation modifies the equations of motion at each order by introducing source terms that couple the different orders, leading to a first-order formulation where the intermediate equations for the zeroth-order fields take the form of BDNK theory at first order. The final physical solution is obtained by reassembling the expanded fields after solving the coupled system, with the non-hydrodynamic modes canceling out in the final sum.

## Key numbers

- For diffusion at first order, the telegrapher's equation emerges: alpha^{-1} partial_t^2 rho_0 + partial_t rho_0 - epsilon D partial^2 rho_0 = 0, where alpha is a resummation parameter with dimensions of inverse length
- In the shear channel of first-order hydrodynamics, the non-hydrodynamic mode has gap omega_NH(k=0) = -i beta, where beta is a resummation parameter
- In the sound channel at first order, there are two non-hydrodynamic modes with gaps alpha and beta
- At second order in hydrodynamics, the shear channel contains one hydrodynamic mode and two gapped modes with gaps beta_1, beta_2; the sound channel contains two hydrodynamic modes and four gapped modes with gaps alpha_1, alpha_2, beta_1, beta_2
- At k=0 for the all-orders diffusion case, the spectrum eigenvalues are -i omega_n = -alpha_n for n = 0, 1, ..., with alpha_0 = 0

## Context

This work addresses the foundational problem of solving the initial value problem in relativistic hydrodynamics as an effective field theory, which has been approached previously through the Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) approach (Muller 1967, Israel 1976) that adds transient degrees of freedom explicitly, the Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun (BDNK) approach (Bemfica 2017, 2019, 2020; Kovtun 2019) that uses frame transformations and truncation to first order, and the density frame approach (Basar 2024, Bhambure 2024) that sacrifices covariance. The method enables causal and stable evolution without introducing additional fields or initial data beyond ideal hydrodynamics, and can be implemented with straightforward modifications of existing BDNK codes for applications to quark-gluon plasma modeling and neutron star mergers.
