# Nuclear fission as resonance-mediated conductance

> For 75 years the theory of nuclear fission has been based on the existence of a collective coordinate associated with the nuclear shape, an assumption required by the Bohr-Wheeler formula as well as by the R-matrix theory of fission. We show that it is also possible to formulate the theory without the help of collective coordinates. In the new formulation, fission is facilitated by individual states in the barrier region rather than channels over the barrier. In a certain limit the theory reduces to a formula closely related to the formula for electronic conductance through resonant tunneling states. In contrast, conduction through channels gives rise to a staircase excitation function that is well-known in nanoscale electronics but has never been seen in nuclear fission.

# Nuclear fission as resonance-mediated conductance

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that nuclear fission can be formulated without invoking collective coordinates or channel theory, instead treating fission as mediated by individual resonant states (bridge states) in the barrier region. The author acknowledges that large fluctuations seen experimentally in $^{236}$U fission above the barrier could also be ascribed to statistical fluctuations rather than individual bridge states.

## Method
The author constructs a discrete-basis Hamiltonian with two statistical reservoirs (left and right) connected by a single bridge state, using random matrix theory to model the reservoir level distributions. The time-dependent Schrödinger equation is solved numerically to compute survival probabilities and transition rates, with the right-hand reservoir treated via an imaginary potential $-i \Gamma_R/2$ added to the bridge state energy. The average decay rates are compared to the Breit-Wigner resonance formula for electronic conductance.

## Key numbers
- The characteristic time for uniform level spacing is $t_0 = 2\pi / \Delta E_L$, where $\Delta E_L$ is the level spacing in the left reservoir.
- For unit transmission coefficient ($T=1$), the decay rate is $W = \Delta E_L / 2\pi$.
- The average decay rate formula is $\overline \Gamma_c = (1/2\pi \rho_I) \cdot [\Gamma_R \Gamma_L / (E_b^2 + (\Gamma_R+\Gamma_L)^2/4)]$, where $\rho_I$ is the level density of the compound nucleus.
- For Gaussian-distributed coupling strengths, the average survival probability is $\overline P(t) = 1/(1 + 2\Gamma_0 t)^{1/2}$, where $\Gamma_0 = 2\pi v_0^2 \rho_I$.
- Large-amplitude fluctuations in suprabarrier fission of $^{236}$U were observed on the scale of 1 keV, with fluctuation amplitude of order 1.

## Context
This work extends the Bohr-Wheeler (1939) channel theory of fission by providing an alternative formulation based on resonant tunneling rather than collective coordinates. The author contrasts the staircase excitation function seen in quantum conductance (van Wees, 1988) with the absence of such structure in nuclear fission. The paper enables future calculations of coupling matrix elements to determine whether individual bridge states can be resolved experimentally, and notes that realistic treatments must include multiple bridge configurations, class II states between barrier humps (Brack, 1972), and pairing interactions (Rodriguez-Guzman, 2014).
