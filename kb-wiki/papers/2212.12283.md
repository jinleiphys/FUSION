# $Ξ$-nuclear constraints from $Ξ^-$ emulsion capture events

> All five KEK and J-PARC two-body $Ξ^-$+$^A$Z $\to$ $^{A'}_Λ$Z'+$^{A''}_Λ$Z'' capture events in light emulsion nuclei, including KISO and IBUKI in $^{14}$N, are consistent with Coulomb-assisted $1p_{Ξ^-}$ nuclear states. The underlying $Ξ$-nuclear potential is strongly attractive, with nuclear-matter depth $V_Ξ$ larger than 20 MeV. The recent $^{14}$N capture events KINKA and IRRAWADDY assigned by J-PARC E07 to $1s_{Ξ^-}$ nuclear states, and implying considerably shallower $V_Ξ$, have also another interpretation as $1p_{Ξ^0}$ nuclear states.

## Key claim

All five two-body Ξ⁻ capture events in light emulsion nuclei from KEK and J-PARC experiments, including KISO and IBUKI in ¹⁴N, are consistent with Coulomb-assisted 1p_Ξ⁻ nuclear states, but the recent ¹⁴N events KINKA and IRRAWADDY assigned by J-PARC E07 to 1s_Ξ⁻ nuclear states can instead be interpreted as 1p_Ξ⁰ nuclear states.

## Method

The authors used a standard tρ optical-potential formalism with a finite-size Coulomb potential to calculate Ξ⁻ atomic and nuclear bound states in ¹²C and ¹⁴N. They fitted the complex strength parameter b₀ to reproduce the measured 1p_Ξ⁻ binding energy in ¹²C from KEK E176 events, then applied the same potential to predict binding energies in ¹⁴N, including a quadrupole-quadrupole residual interaction to account for spin-parity splitting of the ¹⁴N ground state coupled to the 1p_Ξ⁻ state. They also introduced a density-dependent correction to b₀ accounting for Pauli exclusion correlations.

## Key numbers

- Ξ⁻ binding energy for 1p state in ¹²C: 0.82 ± 0.15 MeV (from two KEK E176 events)
- Ξ⁻ binding energies for ¹⁴N events: 1.03 ± 0.18 MeV (KISO), 1.27 ± 0.21 MeV (IBUKI), 1.18 ± 0.22 MeV (KEK E176 event 14-03-35)
- Nuclear-matter Ξ potential depth V_Ξ = 24.3 ± 0.8 MeV from density-independent fit to ¹²C data; reduced to 21.9 ± 0.7 MeV when Pauli correlations are included
- Calculated 1p_Ξ⁻ binding energy in ¹⁴N: 1.96 ± 0.26 MeV (Pauli-corrected), with the least bound Λ^π = 0⁻ state at 1.12 ± 0.26 MeV, consistent with the experimental average of 1.15 ± 0.20 MeV
- J-PARC E07 IRRAWADDY event: B_Ξ⁻^(1s) = 6.27 ± 0.27 MeV in ¹⁴N, yielding V_Ξ(ρ₀) = 13.8 MeV when used as input
- Skyrme-Hartree-Fock fit to KINKA (B_Ξ⁻^(1s) = 8.00 MeV) and mean of KISO/IBUKI (B_Ξ⁻^(1p) = 1.13 MeV) gives V_Ξ(ρ₀) = 13.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Friedman and Gal (2021) on Ξ-nuclear optical potentials, addressing the discrepancy between shallow potential depths (V_Ξ less than 10 MeV) predicted by recent free-space model evaluations such as HAL-QCD (2019), EFT at NLO (2019), and RMF (2021), and the deeper potential (V_Ξ greater than 20 MeV) required by the KEK E176 and E373 emulsion data. The proposed reinterpretation of KINKA and IRRAWADDY as 1p_Ξ⁰ states in ¹⁴C resolves the capture rate hierarchy problem noted by Zhu (1991) and Koike (2017), where 1s_Ξ⁻ capture rates are estimated at about 1% of 1p_Ξ⁻ capture rates. The results enable future J-PARC E07 emulsion analysis to distinguish between these scenarios by focusing on capture events in ¹²C and ¹⁶O.
