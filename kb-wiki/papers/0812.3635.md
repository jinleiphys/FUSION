# NeXSPheRIO results on azimuthal anisotropy in Au-Au collisions at 200A GeV

> In this work, we present the results obtained by the hydrodynamic code NeXSPheRIO on anisotropic flows. In our calculation, we made use of event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions, and chemical freeze-out was explicitly implemented. We studied directed flow, elliptic flow and forth harmonic coefficient for various hadrons at different centrality windows for Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV. The results are discussed and compared with experimental data from RHIC.

## Key claim

The NeXSPheRIO hydrodynamic code, using event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions and explicit chemical freeze-out for strange hadrons, produces anisotropic flow coefficients that are in qualitative agreement with STAR data for Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV, though the results for elliptic flow systematically undershoot pion data and slightly overestimate proton, antiproton, and Lambda data at low transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors combined the NeXus event generator (based on Gribov-Regge theory) to produce fluctuating initial conditions with the SPheRIO smoothed particle hydrodynamic code to solve three-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics. They used an equation of state with a first-order phase transition between a hadronic resonance gas (with finite volume corrections) and a MIT bag model quark-gluon plasma, and implemented chemical freeze-out for strange hadrons (Lambda, Xi, Omega, phi) at a temperature T_ch while thermal freeze-out at T_th was adjusted to reproduce transverse momentum spectra. Anisotropic flow coefficients v1, v2, and v4 were computed with respect to event planes defined using the PHOBOS subevent method.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow v2 for pions, protons, antiprotons, and Lambdas was computed for Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV across multiple centrality windows and for minimum bias, with results shown for transverse momenta up to approximately 2 GeV/c.
- Directed flow v1 for all charged particles was computed and showed reasonable agreement with STAR data in the small pseudorapidity region (|eta| less than about 1).
- Fourth harmonic coefficient v4 for all charged particles was computed and stayed "little below" the experimental data while demonstrating the correct trend.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch and thermal freeze-out temperature T_th were treated as centrality-dependent adjustable parameters, with T_ch tuned to reproduce Lambda yields.

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of anisotropic flow at RHIC (Author 2004, Author 2005) by incorporating event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions from NeXus rather than averaged smooth initial conditions, and by explicitly implementing chemical freeze-out for strange hadrons. The results enable future improvements by adopting continuous emission prescriptions (Author 2007, Author 2008) or by coupling the hydrodynamic model to hadronic transport codes (Author 2007), which may resolve the observed discrepancies in v2 at low transverse momentum.
