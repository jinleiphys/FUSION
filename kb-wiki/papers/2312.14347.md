# Pairing effects on pure rotational energy of nuclei

> By applying the angular-momentum projection (AMP) to the self-consistent axial mean-field solutions with the semi-realistic effective Hamiltonian M3Y-P6, the pairing effects on the pure rotational energy of nuclei, \textit{i.e.}, the rotational energy at a fixed intrinsic state, have been investigated. While it was shown at the Hartree-Fock (HF) level that the individual terms of the Hamiltonian contribute to the rotational energy with ratios insensitive to nuclides except for light or weakly-deformed nuclei, the pair correlations significantly change the contributions, even for the well-deformed heavy nuclei. The contribution of the interaction to the rotational energy is found to correlate well with the degree of proximity between nucleons, which is measured via the expectation value that two nucleons exist at the same position. While the nucleons slightly spread as the angular momentum increases at the HF level, accounting for the positive (negative) contribution of the attractive (repulsive) components of the interaction, the pair correlations reduce or invert the effect.

## Key claim

Pair correlations significantly alter the contributions of individual Hamiltonian terms to the pure rotational energy even in well-deformed heavy nuclei, contrary to the Hartree-Fock (HF) level where these contributions are insensitive to nuclides except for light or weakly-deformed systems; the effect correlates with the degree of spatial proximity between nucleons.

## Method

The authors applied angular-momentum projection (AMP) to self-consistent axial mean-field solutions obtained with the semi-realistic effective Hamiltonian M3Y-P6, using both Hartree-Fock+BCS (HF+BCS) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) methods. They decomposed the pure rotational energy into contributions from individual Hamiltonian terms (kinetic energy, central force, LS force, tensor force, density-dependent central force, and OPEP) and computed the degree of proximity (DoP) via the expectation value of the two-body delta operator. The cumulant expansion was used to analyze higher-order contributions beyond the Peierls-Yoccoz formula.

## Key numbers

- For $^{34}_{12}$Mg, the deformation parameter $a_{20}$ decreases from 0.47 (HF) to 0.43 (HFB); for $^{104}_{~40}$Zr, from 0.46 (HF) to 0.43 (HFB); for $^{24}_{12}$Mg, $a_{20}=0.54$ in both HF and HFB.
- The $E_{\mathrm{x}}(2^+)$ values for HFB solutions are about 1.5–2 times higher than the rigid-rotor value $E_{\mathrm{x}}^{(\mathrm{RR})}(J^+) = J(J+1)/(2\mathcal{I}^{\mathrm{(RR)}})$ with $\mathcal{I}^{\mathrm{(RR)}} \approx 0.0138\,A^{5/3}\,\mathrm{MeV}^{-1}$.
- For $^{80}_{40}$Zr and $^{100}_{~40}$Zr, the relative errors $\varepsilon^{(1)}_{\mathrm{g.s.c.}}$ and $\varepsilon^{(1)}_{\mathrm{x}}$ (neglecting $s_4$ terms) are less than 10%; for $^{34}_{12}$Mg, these errors become larger as the pairing strength $g$ increases.
- The ratios $\mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{x}}(4^+)/\mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{x}}(2^+)$ for constituent Hamiltonian terms are close to the rigid-rotor value $10/3$ for $^{80,100}_{~~~~40}$Zr, nearly independent of $g$ and $\hat{\mathcal{S}}$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study Abe and Nakada (2022) on the composition of rotational energy at the HF level by incorporating pair correlations via HF+BCS and HFB methods. It contrasts with the Belyaev formula (1959) and the cranking model of Mottelson and Valatin (1960), which treat pairing effects on the moment of inertia through different approximations. The results enable future investigations of how rotation affects intrinsic states (e.g., via variation-after-projection or cranking approaches) and provide insight into the role of spatial nucleon correlations (di-neutron/di-proton correlations) in rotational energy.
