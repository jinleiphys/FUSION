# Coherent Elastic Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering with directional detectors

> We study the sensitivity of detectors with directional sensitivity to coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE$ν$NS), and how these detectors complement measurements of the nuclear recoil energy. We consider stopped pion and reactor neutrino sources, and use gaseous helium and fluorine as examples of detector material. We generate Standard Model predictions, and compare to scenarios that include new, light vector or scalar mediators. We show that directional detectors can provide valuable additional information in discerning new physics, and we identify prominent spectral features in both the angular and the recoil energy spectrum for light mediators, even for nuclear recoil energy thresholds as high as $\sim 50$ keV. Combined with energy and timing information, directional information can play an important role in extracting new physics from CE$ν$NS experiments.

## Key claim

Directional detectors can provide valuable additional information for discerning new physics in CEνNS experiments, even for nuclear recoil energy thresholds as high as ~50 keV, by identifying prominent spectral features in both the angular and recoil energy spectra for light vector or scalar mediators.

## Method

The authors calculated the double differential event rate d²R/dE_r dΩ_r (Directional Recoil Spectrum) for CEνNS by integrating the differential cross section over neutrino energy, using the kinematic relation between neutrino energy and nuclear recoil angle. They considered stopped-pion sources (modeled after the SNS at Oak Ridge) and reactor neutrino sources (using Kopeikin spectral data), with gaseous helium-4 and fluorine-19 as detector materials at 1 tonne fiducial mass and 20 m distance. Standard Model predictions were compared to scenarios with new light vector or scalar mediators, assuming 100% detector efficiency and perfect energy and angular resolution with a 1 keV minimum energy threshold.

## Key numbers

- For SNS neutrinos with a 1 ton-year exposure, the total yield is approximately 2300 events for helium and 11200 events for fluorine detectors
- The SM angular spectrum peaks at θ_r ≈ 56° for helium and θ_r ≈ 59° for fluorine detectors with SNS neutrinos
- For reactor neutrinos, the maximum possible recoil energy is 43 keV for helium and 9 keV for fluorine detectors
- The monochromatic neutrino line from stopped pion decay is at E_ν ≈ 30 MeV, derived from (m_π² - m_μ²)/2m_π
- The maximum neutrino energy for a pion-at-rest source is E_ν^max = m_μ/2 = 52.825 MeV
- For reactor neutrinos, the flux is cut off at approximately 9 MeV
- The Helm form factor parameters used: s = 0.9 fm, R_min = 1.6755 fm for He and 2.8976 fm for F

## Context

This work extends the formalism for directional recoil spectra previously developed for solar neutrinos by O'Hare (2015) to terrestrial neutrino sources (stopped-pion and reactor), and builds on the CEνNS detection by COHERENT (Akimov 2017, 2020) which has been used to constrain non-standard neutrino interactions and light mediator models. The results are meant to guide both theoretical and experimental efforts in developing directional detectors for CEνNS, complementing existing work on energy and timing information from experiments like COHERENT, and leveraging ongoing research and development for directional dark matter detection (Mayet 2016, Battat 2016).
