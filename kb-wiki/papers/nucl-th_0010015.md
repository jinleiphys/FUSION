# Introduction to Effective Field Theory

> An introduction to methods of effective field theory is given. Examples are presented, including Rayleigh scattering from nonrelativistic quantum mechnics, chiral perturbation theory/QCD as well as electromagnetic and weak interactions of nucleons.

# Introduction to Effective Field Theory

## Key claim
Effective field theory (EFT) methods can describe nature in a limited energy range using a fully consistent quantum field theory, but almost any theory is an EFT except string theories that attempt to be a theory of everything; even quantum electrodynamics is an EFT in that it must break down at the very highest energies.

## Method
The paper presents a pedagogical introduction to EFT methods across three domains: Rayleigh scattering in nonrelativistic quantum mechanics, chiral perturbation theory for low-energy QCD, and nucleon-nucleon (NN) effective field theory for low-energy weak and electromagnetic processes. In each case, the author constructs effective interactions constrained by symmetry principles (gauge invariance, rotational invariance, parity, time reversal) and scale arguments, then fits undetermined counterterms to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Rayleigh scattering cross section: proportional to ω⁴, where ω is the photon energy, with atomic electric polarizability λ = α_em Σ (2|z_n0|²/(E_n − E_0))
- Chiral scale: Λ_χ ∼ 4πF_π ∼ 1 GeV, where F_π = 92.4 MeV is the pion decay constant
- Gasser-Leutwyler counterterm values (renormalized at scale μ): L₁ʳ = 0.65 ± 0.28, L₂ʳ = 1.89 ± 0.26, L₃ʳ = −3.06 ± 0.92, L₅ʳ = 2.3 ± 0.2, L₉ʳ = 7.1 ± 0.3, L₁₀ʳ = −5.6 ± 0.3
- Chiral prediction for charged pion polarizability α_E = 2.8 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Threshold cross section for n + p → d + γ: σ_exp = 334.5 ± 0.5 mb at incident thermal neutron velocity 2200 m/s; lowest-order one-body value = 297.2 mb
- Deuteron magnetic moment: 0.857 nm; simple one-body value: μ_p + μ_n = 0.88 nm
- ¹S₀ scattering length: a_s = −23.7 fm; deuteron binding momentum: γ = 45.6 MeV
- Experimental cross section for ν_e + d → p + p + e⁻: (53 ± 18) × 10⁻⁴² cm²; theoretical value: 52 × 10⁻⁴² cm²
- Four-nucleon axial counterterm L_1A expected range: −6 fm³ ≤ L_1A ≤ +6 fm³
- L_1A values characterizing prior calculations: 6.3 fm³ (Ying, Haxton, Henley); 1.0 fm³ (Kubodera and Nozawa)
- EFT prediction for quartet nd scattering length: a_{3/2}^{eft} = 6.32 ± 0.1 fm; experimental: a_{3/2}^{exp} = 6.35 ± 0.02 fm

## Context
This review extends the pedagogical tradition of effective field theory expositions, building on the foundational chiral perturbation theory work of Weinberg (1979) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1984). The paper demonstrates how EFT methods provide model-independent predictions for low-energy processes where traditional approaches (meson-exchange models, potential models) introduce uncontrolled uncertainties. It enables future work on few-nucleon systems (Bedaque, Hammer, van Kolck), solar neutrino reactions (Butler and Chen; Kong and Ravndal), and extensions to higher energies where counting schemes remain under debate.
