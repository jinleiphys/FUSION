# Gluon contribution to open heavy-meson production in heavy-ion collisions

> A sizable contribution to heavy quark production in high-energy hadronic and nuclear collisions comes from heavy quark-antiquark pair production from gluon splitting during the parton shower evolution. We investigate the effect of gluon-medium interaction on open heavy flavor spectra in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The interaction of hard gluons and heavy quarks with the hot QCD medium is simulated by utilizing a Langevin transport model that simultaneously incorporates contributions from collisional and radiative processes. It is found that while the gluon splitting channel has quite an important contribution to the single $D$ meson production cross section, its influence on the final heavy meson nuclear modification turns out to be quite modest because the average lifetime of hard gluons is short before splitting into heavy quark-antiquark pairs during the evolution and propagation of the parton shower.

## Key claim

While the gluon splitting channel contributes a sizable fraction of the final D meson production cross section, its influence on the nuclear modification factor R_AA is modest because the average lifetime of hard gluons before splitting into heavy quark-antiquark pairs is short, limiting their interaction with the QGP medium.

## Method

The authors used PYTHIA 6 to simulate gluon splitting in vacuum, extracting the splitting probability, gluon lifetime, and splitting function for g → c anti-c. They embedded these into a Langevin transport model that simultaneously incorporates collisional and radiative energy loss for hard partons propagating through a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic QGP fireball. The spatial diffusion coefficient D of heavy quarks was fixed to D = 5/(2π T), which provided the best description of D meson observables at the LHC and RHIC when only direct channels were considered.

## Key numbers

- For gluons with 400 GeV energy, the probability to split into heavy quark-antiquark pairs can exceed 10%
- The energy loss experienced by hard gluons is approximately twice that of charm quarks given the same propagation time inside the medium, due to different color factors
- A 300 GeV hard gluon loses only about 5 GeV energy before splitting into charm quark pairs, using realistic splitting times from PYTHIA
- The starting time of hydrodynamical evolution was set to τ_0 = 0.6 fm
- The charm quark mass was set to M = 1.27 GeV for in-medium transport calculations
- The spatial diffusion coefficient was fixed to D = 5/(2π T)

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Bedjidian (2004), Norrbin (2000), Aad (2012), and Huang (2013, 2015) that showed the gluon splitting contribution to heavy quark production is equally important as pair production and flavor excitation channels. It also builds on the Langevin-based transport approaches developed by Cao (2011, 2012, 2013, 2015) and others. The finding that gluon splitting has modest influence on R_AA despite large contributions to the cross section enables future work to focus on other mechanisms for heavy flavor modification, and the authors note the effect is even smaller for B mesons due to the shorter splitting time of g → b anti-b.
