# Floquet analysis of the modulated two-mode Bose-Hubbard model

> We study the tunneling dynamics in a time-periodically modulated two-mode Bose-Hubbard model using Floquet theory. We consider situations where the system is in the self-trapping regime and either the tunneling amplitude, the interaction strength, or the energy difference between the modes is modulated. In the former two cases, the tunneling is enhanced in a wide range of modulation frequencies, while in the latter case the resonance is narrow. We explain this difference with the help of Floquet analysis. If the modulation amplitude is weak, the locations of the resonances can be found using the spectrum of the non-modulated Hamiltonian. Furthermore, we use Floquet analysis to explain the coherent destruction of tunneling (CDT) occurring in a large-amplitude modulated system. Finally, we present two ways to create a NOON state (a superposition of $N$ particles in mode 1 with zero particles in mode 2 and vice versa). One is based on a coherent oscillation caused by detuning from a partial CDT. The other makes use of an adiabatic variation of the modulation frequency. This results in a Landau-Zener type of transition between the ground state and a NOON-like state.

# Floquet analysis of the modulated two-mode Bose-Hubbard model

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the self-trapping regime (where interaction energy dominates over tunneling), modulating the tunneling amplitude or the interaction strength enhances many-particle tunneling across a wide range of modulation frequencies, whereas modulating the energy difference between the modes (tilt) produces only narrow resonances; this difference is explained by the parity of the modulated operator and its coupling to Floquet eigenstates of the same or opposite parity.

## Method

The authors study the two-mode Bose-Hubbard Hamiltonian with time-periodic modulation of either the tunneling matrix element J(t), the on-site interaction U(t), or the energy difference between wells V(t). They compute the Floquet operator by time-evolving basis states over one modulation period, extract the Floquet eigenvalue phases, and relate the tunneling period (time for N particles to tunnel from one mode to the other and back) to the phase difference between the two highest-energy Floquet eigenstates. The positions of resonances are compared to the spectrum of the time-independent Hamiltonian H0 = -2J0 Sx + U0 Sz^2.

## Context

This work extends the finding of Catform (2010) that modulating the tunneling matrix element J can drastically reduce the tunneling period in the self-trapping regime, providing a detailed Floquet analysis that was not presented previously. The analysis explains why tilt modulation (V) produces only narrow resonances while J and U modulation produce wide resonances, due to the parity properties of the operators Sx (even parity) versus Sz (odd parity). The paper also presents two methods to create NOON states: one using partial coherent destruction of tunneling (CDT) from large-amplitude interaction modulation, and another using adiabatic frequency sweep across an avoided crossing to induce a Landau-Zener transition. The CDT phenomenon was originally discovered by Dunlap (1986) for charged particles in a lattice under an oscillating electric field.
