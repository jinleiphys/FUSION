# Current matrix element in HAL QCD's wave function equivalent potential method

> We give a formula to calculate a matrix element of a conserved current in the effective quantum mechanics defined by the wave function equivalent potentials proposed by HAL QCD collaboration. As a first step, a non-relativistic field theory with two channel coupling is considered as the original theory, with which a wave function equivalent HAL QCD potential is obtained in a closed analytic form. The external field method is used to derive the formula by demanding that the result should agree with the original theory. With this formula, the matrix element is obtained by sandwiching the effective current operator between the left and the right eigen functions of the effective Hamiltonian associated with the HAL QCD potential. In addition to the naive one-body current, the effective current operator contains an additional two-body term emerging from the degrees of freedom which has been integrated out.

## Key claim
The authors derive a formula for calculating a conserved current matrix element in the effective quantum mechanics defined by HAL QCD potentials, using a non-relativistic coupled-channel model as the original theory rather than full QCD, to obtain a closed analytic form with minimal approximation.

## Method
The authors employ a non-relativistic Galilei-covariant field theory with two-channel coupling (np-np$^*$) as the original theory, from which a wave-function equivalent HAL QCD potential is obtained in closed analytic form by integrating out the closed np$^*$ channel. They introduce an external U$_{\rm p}$(1) gauge field and use the external field method to derive a formula for the current matrix element in the effective quantum mechanics, demanding agreement with the original theory. The resulting effective current operator contains both a naive one-body term and an additional two-body term emerging from the integrated-out degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers
- The excitation energy of the proton (p$^*$) channel is denoted by $\Delta$, which defines the threshold below which the truncated subspace ${\mathcal F}_\Delta$ is constructed.
- The reduced mass $\widetilde m \equiv m/2$ is used in the center-of-mass frame Schr\"odinger equation.
- The energy eigenvalue decomposes as $E_n(\bm P^2) = \widetilde E_n + \frac1{4m}\bm P^2$, where $\widetilde E_n$ is the reduced energy in the center-of-mass frame.

## Context
This work extends the HAL QCD method (Ishii 2006, Aoki 2009, 2011) for constructing nuclear potentials from lattice QCD by addressing the previously unsolved problem of calculating matrix elements in the resulting effective quantum mechanics. It enables future calculations of QCD matrix elements such as form factors, nuclear electric dipole moments for physics beyond the Standard Model (Engel 2013), and the $np\to d\gamma$ reaction in big bang nucleosynthesis (Beane 2015), using conventional nucleon degrees of freedom. The approach contrasts with phenomenological phase-equivalent potentials (Machleidt 2000, Stoks 1994, Wiringa 1994) whose connection to QCD is unclear, and instead provides a path to derive explicit exchange current operators from the HAL QCD framework.
