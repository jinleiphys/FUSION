# Flashing coherently rotating carbon sticks in $^{24}$Mg+$^{24}$Mg collision

> The present discussion rises a number of the questions. For example, is rotational coherence of large molecules necessarily destroyed in the conventionally statistical limit of structureless non-selective continuum (for fixed total spin and parity values) under the conditions of complete intramolecular energy redistribution and vibrational dephasing in the regime of strong ro-vibrational coupling? For the slow cross-symmetry phase relaxation, quantum coherent superpositions of a large number of complex configurations with, e.g., many different total angular momenta produce image of a rotation of macroscopic object with classically fixed (single) total angular momentum. Suppose that the quantum coherent superpositions involving a very large number of different good quantum numbers play a role, in a hidden form, in a formation of macroscopic world. Then why these quantum superpositions are so stable against quick aging/decay of ordered complex structures preventing or slowing down tendencies towards uniform occupation of the available phase space as prescribed by the random matrix theory? And what kind of complex macroscopic phenomena may reveal traces of partially coherent quantum superpositions involving a huge number of quantum-mechanically different integrals of motion behind of what is referred to as conservation laws in classical physics employed for the description of the macroscopic world?

## Key claim

The paper claims that quasi-periodic energy structures observed in the angle-averaged excitation functions for the $^{24}$Mg+$^{24}$Mg collision are not statistical fluctuations but instead originate from coherent rotation of a highly excited hyper-deformed intermediate complex, with the coherence surviving despite strongly overlapping resonances and complete intramolecular energy redistribution.

## Method

The authors analyzed the normalized cross section energy autocorrelation functions $C(\varepsilon)$ constructed from summed deviation functions for the $^{24}$Mg($^{24}$Mg,$^{20}$Ne)$^{28}$Si reaction and $^{24}$Mg($^{24}$Mg,$^{24}$Mg)$^{24}$Mg elastic-inelastic scattering, using data measured on the energy interval $E_{cm}=44.86-47.76$ MeV. They fitted the autocorrelation functions with a formula derived from a diagonal approximation of the Bethe unitary $S$-matrix, which includes parameters for the angular velocity of coherent rotation ($\omega$), the spin off-diagonal phase relaxation width ($\beta$), and the total decay width ($\Gamma$) of the intermediate complex.

## Key numbers

* The quasi-period of the energy structures is $\simeq 0.81$ MeV, interpreted as the inverse half-period of coherent rotation.
* The coherent rotation period is $\simeq 5\times 10^{-21}$ sec.
* The lifetime of the intermediate complex is $\simeq 3.6\times 10^{-21}$ sec, corresponding to a total decay width $\Gamma = 0.2$ MeV.
* The length of the rotating chain is $\simeq 30$ fm.
* The fit parameters for the pure angular resolution case are $\hbar\omega = 0.41$ MeV, $\beta = 0.02$ MeV, and $\Gamma = 0.2$ MeV.
* The fit parameters for the poor angular resolution case ($\Delta\theta_{cm}\simeq 90^\circ\pm 25^\circ$) are $\hbar\omega = 0.404$ MeV, $\beta = 0.0205$ MeV, and $\Gamma = 0.17$ MeV.
* The spreading of angular orientation during one revolution due to the finite $\beta$-width is $2\pi\beta/\hbar\omega \simeq 15^\circ$.
* The coherent rotation persists for about 10 complete revolutions ($\simeq 10^{-19}$ sec), which is about three orders of magnitude longer than the energy relaxation time scale ($\simeq 10^{-22}$ sec).

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of quasi-periodic structures in $^{24}$Mg+$^{24}$Mg collisions previously reported by the same group (PRL99) for elastic-inelastic scattering on a longer energy interval ($E_{cm}=42-56$ MeV), now confirming the same period for $\alpha$-transfer channels on a shorter interval ($E_{cm}=44.86-47.76$ MeV). The analysis contradicts the statistical interpretation based on Ericson fluctuations and random matrix theory (RMW10), which would predict uncorrelated energy structures for different channels. The findings enable further tests of rotational coherence in heavy ion collisions, including the $^{12}$C+$^{24}$Mg system (PRC01), and raise fundamental questions about the survival of quantum coherence in the statistical limit relevant to the quantum-to-classical transition.
