# A soluble statistical model for nuclear fragmentation

> The statistical model of Chase and Mekjian, which offers an analytic solution for the canonical ensemble of non-interacting fragments, is investigated for it's thermodynamic behavior. Various properties of the model, which exhibits a first-order phase transition, are studied. The effects of finite particle number are investigated. Three extensions of the model are considered, excluded volume, Coulomb effects and inclusion of isospin degrees of freedom. A formulation of a microcanonical version of the model is also presented.

## Key claim

The Chase-Mekjian statistical model of non-interacting fragments exhibits a first-order phase transition, but the surface energy term is the most important parameter determining fragmentation and phase transition properties, and the phase transition appears as a discontinuity in energy density at constant volume rather than at constant pressure as in the standard liquid-gas picture.

## Method

The authors use the recursive relation of Chase and Mekjian to exactly calculate the canonical partition function for a system of A nucleons that can appear as monomers or composites, with fragment partition functions given by a liquid-drop formula including volume energy, surface tension, and Fermi-gas excitation energy. They compute thermodynamic quantities such as specific heat, pressure, chemical potential, and mass distributions, and extend the model to include excluded volume, Coulomb effects approximated by a screened liquid-drop formula, and isospin conservation via multi-dimensional recursion relations. A microcanonical formulation is also presented using either an integral over inverse temperature or discrete recursion relations for energy bins.

## Key numbers

- Surface tension at zero temperature: S(0) = 18 MeV; critical temperature for surface tension: T_c = 18 MeV
- Volume energy per nucleon: W_0 = 16 MeV; Fermi-gas parameter: epsilon_0 = 16 MeV
- Transition temperature for the basic model: approximately 6.25 MeV for the system sizes studied
- Coulomb energy addition to internal free energy: F_coul = 0.70 (1 - (rho/rho_0)^(1/3)) * (a_k^(5/3))/4 MeV
- System sizes studied for specific heat: A = 700, A = 1400, and A = 2800; also A = 100 for Coulomb effects and A = 200 for pressure-density diagrams

## Context

This work extends the analytic recursive technique of Chase and Mekjian (1995) for the canonical ensemble of non-interacting fragments, which itself built on earlier recursion relations applied by Lee and Mekjian (1992-1994) to a more algebraically motivated fragmentation model. The model reproduces features seen in percolation and lattice gas models (Pan and Das Gupta, 1995) and uses the liquid-drop parameterization of Bondorf, Botvina, Iljinov, Mishustin, and Sneppen (1995). The microcanonical formulation presented has yet to be implemented, and the authors note that for comparing to nuclear physics experiments, development of the microcanonical approaches is of greatest importance.
