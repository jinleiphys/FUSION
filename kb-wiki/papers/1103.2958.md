# A first search of excited states double beta and double electron capture decays of Pd110 and Pd102

> A search for double beta decays of the palladium isotopes 110Pd and 102Pd into excited states of their daughters was performed and first half-life limits for the 2νββ and 0νββ decays into first excited 0+ and 2+ states of 5.89e19 yr and 4.40e19 yr (95% CL) for the 110Pd decay were obtained. The half-life limits for the corresponding double electron capture transition of 102Pd are 7.64e18 yr and 2.68e18 yr (95% CL) respectively. These are the first measurements for 102Pd.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first experimental half-life limits for double beta decays of 110Pd and double electron capture decays of 102Pd into excited states of their daughters, but no signal was observed and the limits are valid for both the two-neutrino and neutrinoless decay modes combined, as the gamma-detection approach cannot distinguish between them.

## Method

A 802.35 g sample of palladium was placed in a Marinelli baker on a HPGe detector with 90% efficiency inside the Felsenkeller Underground Laboratory (120 mwe shielding). The search relied on detecting the de-excitation gamma rays from the first excited 0+ and 2+ states of the daughter nuclei, using 16.2 days of data (13.00 kg·d exposure). Efficiency calibration was performed with a SiO2 sample of identical geometry, corrected for self-absorption differences via Monte Carlo simulations with the AMOS code, and upper limits were extracted using the Feldman-Cousins method at 95% confidence level.

## Key numbers

- For 110Pd decay to the 0+1 state (815.35 keV gamma): lower half-life limit of 5.89 × 10^19 yr (95% CL)
- For 110Pd decay to the 2+1 state (657.76 keV gamma): lower half-life limit of 4.40 × 10^19 yr (95% CL)
- For 102Pd double electron capture to the 0+1 state (468.59 keV gamma): lower half-life limit of 7.64 × 10^18 yr (95% CL)
- For 102Pd double electron capture to the 2+1 state (475.05 keV gamma): lower half-life limit of 2.68 × 10^18 yr (95% CL)
- The 110Pd sample had natural abundance 11.27% and the 102Pd sample had natural abundance 1.02%
- The Q-value for 102Pd decay is 1172 keV

## Context

This work provides the first experimental limits for excited-state double beta transitions in 110Pd and the first experimental limits of any kind for 102Pd double beta decays. The results extend the search for double beta decay to palladium isotopes, which had previously only a weak limit of order 10^17 yr for 110Pd ground-state transitions from Winter (1952). Theoretical predictions for 110Pd excited-state transitions range from 0.62 × 10^25 yr to 8.37 × 10^25 yr for the 2+1 state (Domin 2005, Stoica 1994, Raduta 2007, Suhonen 2011) and from 2.4 × 10^26 yr to 9.1 × 10^23 yr for the 0+1 state (Domin 2005, Suhonen 2011); the new limits are still far from these predictions. No theoretical predictions exist for 102Pd, and the results enable future searches with larger exposures or enriched samples.
