# Deconfinement transition in protoneutron star cores: Analysis within the MIT Bag model

> We analyze the effect of color superconductivity in the transition from hot hadron matter to quark matter in the presence of a gas of trapped electron neutrinos. We adopt a two-phase picture in which the hadronic phase is described trough a nonlinear Walecka model and just deconfined matter through the MIT bag model including color superconductivity. We compare the present results with those obtained within the NJL model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that color superconductivity facilitates the deconfinement transition in protoneutron star cores by lowering the transition density, but only when the temperature is below the critical temperature \(T_c = 0.57 \Delta_0\); above this temperature the pairing gap vanishes and the effect disappears.

## Method

The authors adopt a two-phase picture: the hadronic phase is described by a nonlinear Walecka model (GM1nh parametrization) including nucleons, electrons, and trapped electron neutrinos, while the quark phase is described by the MIT Bag model with color superconductivity (2SC phase) for u, d, and s quarks. They impose Gibbs criteria for phase equilibrium (equal pressure, temperature, and Gibbs energy per baryon) together with flavor conservation and color neutrality, solving for the transition density as a function of temperature, neutrino chemical potential, and superconducting gap parameter \(\Delta_0\).

## Key numbers

- The hadronic model GM1nh has coupling constants \((g_{\sigma}/m_{\sigma})^2 = 11.79\) fm\(^2\), \((g_{\omega}/m_{\omega})^2 = 7.149\) fm\(^2\), \((g_{\rho}/m_{\rho})^2 = 4.411\) fm\(^2\), with \(b = 0.002947\) and \(c = -0.001070\), yielding a maximum hadronic star mass of 2.32 \(M_{\odot}\).
- The strange quark mass is set to \(m_s = 150\) MeV in the MIT Bag model.
- The critical temperature for color superconductivity is given by \(T_c = 0.57 \Delta_0\).
- Three bag constant values are used: \(B = 80\) MeV/fm\(^3\), \(B = 120\) MeV/fm\(^3\), and \(B = 160\) MeV/fm\(^3\).
- For comparison with NJL model results, bag constants \(B = 353\) MeV/fm\(^3\) and \(B = 337\) MeV/fm\(^3\) are used.
- The transition density decreases strongly for \(\Delta_0\) in the range 100–150 MeV compared to \(\Delta_0 = 0\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of deconfinement in deleptonized neutron stars (Lugones 2005) to hot, lepton-rich protoneutron star conditions with trapped neutrinos. It compares results with those obtained using the NJL model (Lugones and Carmo 2013), finding similar qualitative behavior for the temperature dependence of the transition density but opposite qualitative behavior regarding neutrino trapping. The analysis is performed in bulk without finite-size effects, and the results suggest that both cooling and deleptonization of a protoneutron star increase the probability of deconfinement when color superconductivity is included.
