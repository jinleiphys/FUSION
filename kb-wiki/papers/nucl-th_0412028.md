# Beta-decay rates of r-process nuclei in the relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation

> The fully consistent relativistic proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (PN-RQRPA) is employed in the calculation of beta-decay half-lives of neutron-rich nuclei in the N$\approx$50 and N$\approx$82 regions. A new density-dependent effective interaction, with an enhanced value of the nucleon effective mass, is used in relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculation of nuclear ground states and in the particle-hole channel of the PN-RQRPA. The finite range Gogny D1S interaction is employed in the T=1 pairing channel, and the model also includes a proton-neutron particle-particle interaction. The theoretical half-lives reproduce the experimental data for the Fe, Zn, Cd, and Te isotopic chains, but overestimate the lifetimes of Ni isotopes and predict a stable 132Sn.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by introducing a new density-dependent relativistic mean-field interaction (DD-ME1*) with an enhanced nucleon effective mass (achieved via an isoscalar tensor-coupling term for the omega meson), the relativistic proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (PN-RQRPA) can reproduce experimental beta-decay half-lives for several neutron-rich isotopic chains, but the model still overestimates lifetimes for Ni isotopes and predicts a stable 132Sn.

## Method

The authors employ the fully self-consistent relativistic proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (PN-RQRPA) in the canonical single-nucleon basis of the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model. A new density-dependent effective interaction (DD-ME1*) is used in the particle-hole channel, derived by adding an isoscalar tensor-coupling term for the omega meson to increase the nucleon effective mass from m*/m = 0.66 (DD-ME1) to m*/m = 0.76. The finite-range Gogny D1S interaction is used for T=1 pairing, and a Gaussian proton-neutron particle-particle interaction is included for T=0 pairing in open-shell nuclei. Beta-decay half-lives are calculated using the allowed Gamow-Teller approximation with an effective axial coupling constant g_A = 1.

## Key numbers

- The new DD-ME1* interaction has Dirac mass m_D = 0.67m and nucleon effective mass m*/m = 0.76, compared to DD-ME1 with m_D = 0.58m and m*/m = 0.66
- For 78Ni, the DD-ME1 interaction predicts T_{1/2} = 7 s, while the experimental value is T_{1/2} = 104+126-57 ms
- Nonrelativistic QRPA calculations with interaction DF3 give T_{1/2} ≈ 0.3 s for 78Ni, and with Skyrme interaction SkO' give T_{1/2} ≈ 0.6 s
- The theoretical half-lives reproduce experimental data for Fe, Zn, Cd, and Te isotopic chains
- The model overestimates lifetimes of Ni isotopes and predicts a stable 132Sn

## Context

This work extends the relativistic QRPA framework previously applied to charge-exchange modes (Paa. 2004) to the calculation of beta-decay half-lives for r-process nuclei. It addresses a limitation of standard relativistic mean-field interactions (like DD-ME1) which have low effective nucleon masses and therefore produce transition energies that are too low, resulting in half-lives an order of magnitude too long. The approach is compared to nonrelativistic self-consistent QRPA calculations using Skyrme interactions (Eng. 1999) and the HFB plus continuum QRPA framework (Bor. 2003), which have been applied to r-process waiting-point nuclei. The new interaction enables future systematic calculations of beta-decay rates for heavy neutron-rich nuclei along the r-process path.
