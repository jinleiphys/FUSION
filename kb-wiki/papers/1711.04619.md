# JOZSO, a renewed version of the computer code GAMOW for calculating broad neutron resonances in phenomenological nuclear potentials

> A renewed version of the computer code GAMOW ~\cite{[Ve82]} is given in which the difficulties in calculating broad neutron resonances are amended. New types of phenomenological neutron potentials with strict finite range are built in. Landscape of the S-matrix can be generated on a given domain of the complex wave number plane and S-matrix poles in the domain are localized. Normalized Gamow wave functions and trajectories of given poles can be calculated optionally.

# JOZSO, a renewed version of the computer code GAMOW for calculating broad neutron resonances in phenomenological nuclear potentials

## Key claim

The JOZSO program improves upon the earlier GAMOW code by using a different pole-finding strategy that searches for the absolute minimum of the summed modulus of logarithmic derivative differences over a radial interval, rather than relying on Newton iterations at a single matching radius, thereby resolving difficulties in calculating broad neutron resonances where the earlier method often converged poorly or failed.

## Method

The program solves the radial Schrödinger equation for spherically symmetric, strictly finite-range neutron potentials using adaptive step-size Runge-Kutta-Fehlberg integration with Cash-Karp parameters. Internal and external solutions are generated from boundary conditions at the origin and in the asymptotic region, respectively; the S-matrix pole is located by finding the absolute minimum of a real-valued function F that sums the modulus of the difference between internal and external logarithmic derivatives over a selected radial subinterval, using the Nelder-Mead algorithm. Normalized Gamow wave functions are computed using the regularization method of Gyarmati (1971), and pole trajectories as a function of potential strength can be calculated optionally.

## Key numbers

- The program reproduces pole positions from Ixaru et al. (1995) to 3–4 decimal digits of accuracy.
- The power series expansion for the internal solution near the origin uses up to 50 terms (j=0 to 50) in the form u_i(r,k) = Σ a_j r^(l+1+j).
- The radial interval for comparing logarithmic derivatives is selected based on potential values: the lower index i_1 corresponds to r_1 where V(r_1) ≈ V_or/10, and the upper index i_2 corresponds to r_2 where V(r_2) ≈ V_or/1000, where V_or is the potential value near the origin.
- The conversion factor between energy in MeV and k^2 in fm^(-2) is c_1 = 2μ/ħ^2, where μ is the reduced mass of the projectile-target system.

## Context

This work extends the earlier GAMOW code by Vertse, Pál, and Balogh (1982) and the ANTI code by Ixaru, Rizea, and Vertse (1995), which were limited in their ability to handle broad neutron resonances due to difficulties in choosing a proper matching radius for Newton iterations. The new approach enables reliable calculation of S-matrix poles across a domain of the complex wave number plane, including regions with large complex k-values, and has been demonstrated in studies by Baran et al. (2015) and Sahu et al. (2015). The program implements several strictly finite-range potential forms, cut-off Woods-Saxon (CWS), generalized Woods-Saxon (GWS), the SV form of Sahu et al. (2008), and the SS form of Sahu and Sahu (2012), and allows for external potential forms to be read in, enabling future studies of pole trajectories and resonance properties in phenomenological nuclear potentials.
