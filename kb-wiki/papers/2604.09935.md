# From credible shell model interactions to neutron-capture uncertainties

> Nuclear structure theory can provide nuclear astrophysics and nuclear technologies with bound state properties and transition rates. When describing nuclear reactions, the list can be extended to include statistical properties such as nuclear level densities (NLDs) and radiative strength functions (RSFs). We present the first uncertainty-quantified neutron-capture cross section for $^{27}$Al based on NLDs and RSFs computed with the shell model (SM). We find that the USDBUQ500 SM interaction predicts NLDs and RSFs with constant uncertainties of 6% and 9%, respectively. These, in turn, translate to a 5 to 25% uncertainty in the neutron-capture cross section, which exhibits a surprisingly non-Gaussian distribution.

## Key claim

The USDBUQ500 shell model interaction ensemble predicts nuclear level densities (NLDs) and M1 radiative strength functions (RSFs) with constant parametric uncertainties of 6% and 9% respectively, which propagate to a 5 to 25% uncertainty in the neutron-capture cross section for 27Al, but the resulting cross section distribution is surprisingly non-Gaussian and asymmetric, not a simple Gaussian as one might assume from the constant input uncertainties.

## Method

The authors sampled 560 realizations of the USDBUQ500 uncertainty-quantified shell model interaction from Gorton (2025) to compute ensembles of NLDs and M1 RSFs for 27Al and 28Al within the sd-valence space using large-scale shell model diagonalization. These statistical structure properties were then used as inputs to the Hauser-Feshbach reaction code YAHFC, with the Koning-Delaroche optical potential for neutron transmission coefficients and the simple modified Lorentzian (SMLO) model for the E1 RSF, to produce a distribution of neutron-capture cross sections on 27Al.

## Key numbers

- NLD uncertainty from the USDBUQ500 interaction ensemble is 6% (constant across excitation energy)
- M1 RSF uncertainty from the USDBUQ500 interaction ensemble is 9% (constant across gamma-ray energy)
- Neutron-capture cross section uncertainty ranges from 5% to 25% depending on incident neutron energy
- Pearson correlation coefficient between NLDs of 27Al and 28Al at their respective neutron separation energies is 0.6
- Neutron separation energy for 27Al is 13.058 MeV; for 28Al it is 7.725 MeV
- Energy of first negative parity state in 27Al is 4.055 MeV; in 28Al it is 3.465 MeV
- The ensemble contains 560 realizations of the USDBUQ500 interaction

## Context

This work extends the parametric uncertainty quantification framework for shell model interactions developed by Gorton (2025) to the domain of statistical reaction theory, providing the first uncertainty-quantified neutron-capture cross section from shell-model-based NLDs and RSFs. It replaces traditional phenomenological models for NLDs (such as the Gilbert-Cameron formula from 1965) and RSFs (such as Lorentzian-based functions from Kopecky (1990) and Goriely (2019)) with microscopic shell model calculations, enabling future large-scale studies of nuclear physics impacts on nucleosynthesis as demonstrated by Kedia (2026). The main limitations are the finite model space missing negative-parity states and core-excited configurations, and the use of a phenomenological E1 RSF model rather than a microscopic one.
