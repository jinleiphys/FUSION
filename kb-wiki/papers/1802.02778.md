# Deuteron: analytical forms of wave function and density distribution

> The deuteron wave function can be presented as the table through the respective massifs of numerically values of radial wave functions. In calculations such arrays of numbers in the texts of programs to operate quite difficult. Therefore a used simpler analytical forms is expedient. In the received analytical forms of deuteron wave function in coordinate representation it is necessary to calculate density distribution in the deuteron and transition density and compare them among themselves. On the received coefficients of the four analytical forms for deuteron wave function for the nucleon-nucleon potential Reid93 are calculated density distribution and transition density. For comparison, similar results are given for Moscow potential. Such calculations help to evaluate the correctness of the choice of the analytical form for approximation of the wave function, and also to obtain information about such characteristics of the deuteron as charge form factor, tensor polarization and momentum distribution.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that four different analytical forms for the deuteron wave function, when fitted to the Reid93 nucleon-nucleon potential, yield density distributions and transition densities that differ only in the region 0-0.25 fm, suggesting that the choice of analytical approximation primarily affects the description near the origin even when no spurious node is present.

## Method

The author took previously published coefficients for four analytical forms of the deuteron wave function in coordinate representation (Gaussian, Laguerre function, exponential with r^3, and exponential with r) fitted to numerical wave functions from the Reid93 potential. Using these analytical forms, the author computed the density distribution for projections M_d = 0 and M_d = +/-1, as well as the transition density, and compared the results among the four forms and with results from the Moscow potential.

## Key numbers

- The numerical wave function arrays used for fitting consisted of 839 x 2 values for radial wave functions in the interval r = 0-25 fm.
- The chi-squared minimization during fitting achieved values smaller than 10^{-4}.
- The density distributions and transition densities from the four analytical forms differ only in the region 0-0.25 fm.
- For the Moscow potential, the S-wave contains a spurious node at r = 0.65 fm, causing a shift of nodes and maxima in the density quantities.

## Context

This work extends previous parameterizations of the deuteron wave function by Dubovichenko and Berezhnoi-Korda-Gakh (2000s), as well as approximations using Laguerre functions and other analytical forms. The computed density distributions and transition densities can be used to obtain the charge form factor, tensor polarization, momentum distribution, and the cross section for d(e,e'p)n reactions in the one-photon exchange approximation, as well as to evaluate the spatial size of the toroidal structure in the deuteron.
