# $Œõ$ hypernuclear potentials beyond linear density dependence

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simple two-parameter Œõ-nucleus optical potential, with terms linear and quadratic in nuclear density, can reproduce all measured (1s_Œõ, 1p_Œõ) binding energies across the periodic table from A=12 to A=208, but only when the quadratic (ŒõNN) term is suppressed for interactions between core nucleons and excess neutrons in neutron-rich hypernuclei.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Friedman and Gal, 2023) that fitted only to ¬π‚Å∂_ŒõN binding energies, by now performing least-squares fits to the full dataset. The results confirm and refine earlier Skyrme-Hartree-Fock studies (Millener, Dover, and Gal, 1988; Hiyama et al., 2014) but with substantially reduced partial depths D_Œõ^(2) and D_Œõ^(3) due to the inclusion of Pauli correlations. The suppression of ŒõNN interactions between core nucleons and excess neutrons, traced to isospin-dependent pion-exchange models (Spitzer, 1958; Brown, Lee, and Niemi, 1967), provides a mechanism relevant to the hyperon puzzle in neutron-star matter, where the balance between attractive and repulsive terms at higher densities determines whether Œõ hyperons are expelled from dense matter.
