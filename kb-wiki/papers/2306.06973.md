# $Λ$ hypernuclear potentials beyond linear density dependence

> In a recent paper [PLB 837 (2023) 137669] we showed that all measured ($1s_Λ$, $1p_Λ$) pairs of $Λ$ binding energies in $Λ$-hypernuclei across the periodic table, $12\leq A \leq 208$, can be obtained from a $Λ$-nucleus optical potential with only two adjustable $ΛN$ and $ΛNN$ parameters, associated with leading linear and quadratic terms in the nuclear density, derived by fitting $^{16}_{~Λ}$N binding energies. Here we extend the previous analysis by performing least-squares fits to the full set of data points. Consequences of suppressing $ΛNN$ interactions between `core' nucleons and `excess' neutrons are studied and related predictions are made for ($1s_Λ$, $1p_Λ$) binding energies in $^{40,48}_{~~~~Λ}$K, obtainable from upcoming $^{40,48}$Ca($e,e'K^+$) JLab experiments. We find $Λ$-nucleus partial potential depths of $D^{(2)}_Λ= -38.6\pm 0.8$~MeV ($ΛN$) and $D^{(3)}_Λ= 11.3\pm 1.4$~MeV ($ΛNN$), with a total depth $D_Λ= -27.3\pm 0.6$~MeV at nuclear-matter density $ρ_0$=0.17~fm$^{-3}$, consistently with our previous results. Extrapolation to higher nuclear densities and possible relevance to the `hyperon puzzle' in neutron-star matter are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simple two-parameter Λ-nucleus optical potential, with terms linear and quadratic in nuclear density, can reproduce all measured (1s_Λ, 1p_Λ) binding energies across the periodic table from A=12 to A=208, but only when the quadratic (ΛNN) term is suppressed for interactions between core nucleons and excess neutrons in neutron-rich hypernuclei.

## Method

The authors performed least-squares fits of the two strength parameters b_0 (ΛN two-body term) and B_0 (ΛNN three-body term) to 18 experimental B_Λ values for 1s_Λ and 1p_Λ states in hypernuclei from ¹²_ΛB to ²⁰⁸_ΛPb. The optical potential included a Pauli correlation factor in the linear-density term and a suppression factor F = [(2Z)² + (N-Z)²]/A² applied to the ρ² term for medium-weight and heavy hypernuclei to account for the decoupling of excess neutrons from core nucleons in the three-body interaction.

## Key numbers

- Λ-nucleus partial potential depths at nuclear-matter density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³: D_Λ^(2) = -38.6 ± 0.8 MeV (ΛN attraction) and D_Λ^(3) = 11.3 ± 1.4 MeV (ΛNN repulsion), with total depth D_Λ = -27.3 ± 0.6 MeV
- Fitted strength parameters: b₀ = 1.437 ± 0.095 fm (attraction) and B₀ = 0.190 ± 0.024 fm (repulsion)
- Predicted binding energies for ⁴⁸_ΛK with suppression factor F=0.69: B_Λ^(1s) = 22.39 MeV and B_Λ^(1p) = 14.35 MeV
- Predicted binding energies for ⁴⁰_ΛK (F=1): B_Λ^(1s) = 18.70 MeV and B_Λ^(1p) = 10.70 MeV
- The suppression factor F varies between 0.85 and 0.67 for hypernuclei from ⁵¹_ΛV to ²⁰⁸_ΛPb

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Friedman and Gal, 2023) that fitted only to ¹⁶_ΛN binding energies, by now performing least-squares fits to the full dataset. The results confirm and refine earlier Skyrme-Hartree-Fock studies (Millener, Dover, and Gal, 1988; Hiyama et al., 2014) but with substantially reduced partial depths D_Λ^(2) and D_Λ^(3) due to the inclusion of Pauli correlations. The suppression of ΛNN interactions between core nucleons and excess neutrons, traced to isospin-dependent pion-exchange models (Spitzer, 1958; Brown, Lee, and Niemi, 1967), provides a mechanism relevant to the hyperon puzzle in neutron-star matter, where the balance between attractive and repulsive terms at higher densities determines whether Λ hyperons are expelled from dense matter.
