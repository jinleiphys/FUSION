# A spinless crystal for a high-performance solid-state $^{229}$Th nuclear clock

> Solid-state $^{229}$Th nuclear clocks require a host material whose band gap is larger than the 8.4 eV nuclear transition energy. As such, excitation of the $^{229}$Th nuclear state has so far only been demonstrated in metal fluorides, specifically CaF$_2$, LiSrAlF$_6$, and ThF$_4$, where the large electronegativity of the halogen leads to sufficient band gaps. However, it is expected that the nuclear magnetic moment of the fluorine gives rise to a leading order broadening mechanism that limits the clock stability. Here, we use concepts of molecular design to identify a polyatomic anion, SO$_4^{2-}$, that is both nuclear spin free and of sufficient electron affinity to result in a high band gap metal sulfate system. Using state-of-the-art calculations, we find that the band gap of Th(SO$_4$)$_2$ is approximately 9 eV, large enough for direct laser excitation of $^{229}$Th. Low concentrations of $^{229}$Th in the otherwise spinless $^{232}$Th(SO$_4$)$_2$ crystal mitigate $^{229}$Th-$^{229}$Th interactions. Furthermore, the introduction of $^{229}$Th does not modify the material band gap nor introduce electronic states associated with nuclear quenching. By removing one of the primary sources of nuclear line broadening in the crystal, the nuclear magnetic dipole-dipole interaction, a nuclear clock with instability as low as $σ= 4.6\times10^{-23}/\sqrtτ$, where $τ$ is the averaging time, may be realized. This is roughly six orders of magnitude lower than previously thought possible.

# A spinless crystal for a high-performance solid-state 229Th nuclear clock

## Key claim

Thorium sulfate (Th(SO4)2) is predicted to have a band gap of approximately 9 eV, sufficient for direct laser excitation of the 229Th nuclear transition, while being composed entirely of atoms with zero nuclear spin (when using isotopically pure 16O and 32S), potentially reducing nuclear clock instability by roughly six orders of magnitude compared to previously considered metal fluoride hosts, provided that the material can be synthesized with the required isotopic purity and that the calculated band gap is accurate within the typical 5-10% underestimation of GW methods.

## Method

The authors performed first-principles electronic structure calculations using density functional theory (DFT) with the PBE functional for structural optimization of Th(SO4)2, followed by G0W0R many-body perturbation theory (a cubic-scaling variant of G0W0) to compute the band gap. They validated the G0W0R method against standard G0W0 on ThO2, obtaining band gaps of 6.07 eV and 6.36 eV respectively, and tested convergence with respect to the number of frequency grid points (NOMEGA), choice of DFT orbitals (PBE vs LDA), and pseudopotential type. The nuclear linewidth contributions from magnetic dipole-dipole interactions were estimated via Monte Carlo simulations treating spinful nuclei as classical magnetic dipoles with random orientations, while temperature-dependent shifts (second-order Doppler shift, intrinsic isomer shift, and electric quadrupole shift) were estimated using the Debye model and literature values from Mössbauer spectroscopy.

## Key numbers

- Band gap of Th(SO4)2: 9.08 eV (computed with G0W0R, NOMEGA = 16, GW pseudopotentials for O and S, PBE orbitals)
- Validation on ThO2: G0W0R gave 6.07 eV band gap vs 6.36 eV from standard G0W0
- Minimum Th-Th distance in optimized Th(SO4)2 structure: 5.033 Å
- Electric field gradient at Th nucleus: Vzz = 200.4 V/Å² with asymmetry parameter η = 0.605
- Estimated magnetic dipole-dipole interaction energy between nearest Th nuclei: ~10 Hz; electric quadrupole-quadrupole interaction: ~0.001 Hz
- Magnetic dipole broadening with isotopically pure 16O and 32S: <0.01 Hz at 229Th fraction ~10⁻⁴%, rising to ~10 Hz at 100% 229Th
- Magnetic dipole broadening with natural-abundance S and O: ~1 Hz at low 229Th concentration
- Temperature-dependent broadening estimates at room temperature with 0.1 mK gradient: SODS negligible, intrinsic isomer shift ~0.5 Hz, electric quadrupole shift ~2.5 Hz
- Temperature-dependent broadening estimates at 4 K: intrinsic isomer shift ~0.1 Hz, electric quadrupole shift ~0.4 Hz
- Predicted clock instability: σ = 4.6 × 10⁻²³/√τ, where τ is averaging time

## Context

This work extends the search for suitable 229Th nuclear clock host materials beyond the metal fluorides (CaF₂, LiSrAlF₆, ThF₄) where nuclear excitation has been demonstrated (Tkalya 2015, Kazakov 2012, Dessovic 2014, Stellmer 2015, von der Wense 2016, Pineda 2025). The key limitation identified in those materials is the nuclear magnetic dipole-dipole interaction between 229Th and ¹⁹F nuclei, causing inhomogeneous broadening of order 1-10 kHz and predicted clock instabilities of σ ~ 10⁻¹⁵-10⁻¹⁷/√τ (Kazakov 2012, Pineda 2025). By proposing a spinless sulfate anion (SO₄²⁻) as the host, the authors aim to eliminate this broadening mechanism entirely. The work enables experimental synthesis and characterization of Th(SO₄)₂ doped with 229Th, and suggests that isotopically enriched ¹⁶O and ³²S would be needed to achieve the lowest predicted linewidths.
