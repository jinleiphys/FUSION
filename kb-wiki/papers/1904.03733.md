# Fission and the r-process nucleosynthesis of translead nuclei in neutron star mergers

> We study the impact of fission on the production and destruction of translead nuclei during the r-process nucleosynthesis occurring in neutron-star mergers. Abundance patterns and rates of nuclear energy production are obtained for different ejecta conditions using three sets of stellar reaction rates, one of which is based on microscopic and consistent calculations of nuclear masses, fission barriers, and collective inertias. We show that the accumulation of fissioning material during the r process can strongly affect the free neutron abundance after the r-process freeze-out. This leads to a significant impact on the abundances of heavy nuclei that undergo $α$ decay or spontaneous fission, affecting the radioactive energy production by the ejecta at timescales relevant for kilonova emission.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the accumulation of fissioning material during the r-process can strongly affect the free neutron abundance after freeze-out, which in turn significantly impacts the abundances of heavy nuclei that undergo alpha decay or spontaneous fission, affecting radioactive energy production at timescales relevant for kilonova emission, but this effect depends critically on the choice of nuclear reaction rates and fission models, particularly the height of fission barriers around the N=184 shell closure.

## Method

The authors performed r-process nucleosynthesis calculations for three different neutron star merger ejecta conditions (hot dynamical, cold dynamical, and accretion disk) using three sets of stellar reaction rates: FRDM+TF (based on finite-range droplet model masses and Thomas-Fermi fission barriers), HFB14 (based on Skyrme energy density functional calculations), and BCPM (based on the Barcelona-Catania-Paris-Madrid energy density functional with consistent microscopic calculations of masses, fission barriers, and collective inertias). They analyzed the evolution of abundances at four time stages (freeze-out, when neutron capture timescale equals beta-decay timescale, at 1 day, and at 1 Gyr) and studied the contribution of individual fission channels (neutron-induced, beta-delayed, and spontaneous fission) to the destruction of heavy nuclei.

## Key numbers

- In the hot dynamical ejecta, the initial neutron-to-seed ratio is n/s ~ 600; in the cold dynamical ejecta, n/s ~ 1200; in the accretion disk trajectory, n/s ~ 120.
- At freeze-out, nuclei with Z >= 84 constitute more than 95% of the A >= 252 abundances in the hot and accretion scenarios; in the cold scenario this range reduces to A >= 246.
- The BCPM and HFB14 models predict accumulation of material around A ~ 280 due to larger fission barriers at N=184, while FRDM+TF shows a peak at A ~ 260 due to a strong shell gap at N=172.
- At 10 seconds after freeze-out in the hot scenario, the free neutron abundance Y_n obtained with FRDM+TF is more than one order of magnitude smaller than in the HFB14 and BCPM cases.
- The spontaneous fission half-life of Cf-254 is 60.5 +/- 0.2 days.
- For BCPM and FRDM+TF, nuclei with A > 250 are destroyed by 1 day; for HFB14, nuclei up to A = 255 survive due to larger fission barriers.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on the role of fission in r-process nucleosynthesis (Thielemann 1983, Panov 2004, Panov 2008, Petermann 2012, Korobkin 2012, Goriely 2015, Eichler 2015, Mendoza-Temis 2015, Mumpower 2018, Vassh 2018, Holmbeck 2019) by using a consistent microscopic nuclear input (BCPM) and comparing it with the widely-used FRDM+TF and HFB14 rate sets. The results enable improved predictions for kilonova light curves at late times (greater than 10 days), particularly regarding the survival of Cf-254 and nuclei in the mass range A=222-225, which are relevant for interpreting future observations of kilonova emission from neutron star mergers.
