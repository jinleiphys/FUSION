# Pairing in finite systems: beyond the HFB theory

> The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation is very useful for treating both long- and short-range correlations in finite quantum fermion systems, but it must be extended in order to describe detailed spectroscopic properties. One problem is the symmetry-breaking character of the HFB approximation. We present a general and systematic way to restore symmetries and to extend the configuration space using pfaffian formulas for the many-body matrix elements. The advantage of those formulas is that the sign of the matrix elements is unambiguously determined. It is also helpful to extend the space of configurations by constraining the HFB solutions in some way. A powerful method for finding these constrained solutions is the gradient method, based on the generalized Thouless transformation. The gradient method also preserves the number parity of the Bogoliubov transformation, which facilitates the application of the theory to systems with odd particle number.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general and systematic method to restore symmetries and extend the configuration space in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory using pfaffian formulas for many-body matrix elements, but the method's practical implementation still faces technical difficulties in systems where time reversal symmetry is broken, particularly regarding the sign of matrix elements between general HFB wave functions.

## Method

The authors derive pfaffian-based formulas for computing overlaps between HFB wave functions, including multi-quasiparticle overlaps and those involving canonical transformation operators, which unambiguously determine the sign of matrix elements. They also develop a gradient method for solving the HFB equations in odd-A systems by introducing a "swapping" matrix that transforms the blocked quasiparticle vacuum into the standard even-parity formalism, allowing the same gradient method used for even-even systems to be applied to odd-A systems.

## Key numbers

- The number of contractions to be computed using the generalized Wick theorem for ten quasiparticles is 9!! = 945, while for fourteen quasiparticles it rises to 13!! = 135,135
- Differences between the equal filling approximation and real blocking in odd-A systems can amount to a few hundred keV, relevant for determining ground and excited state spins and parities

## Context

This work extends the HFB theory beyond mean-field approximations for finite fermion systems, building on the foundational BCS theory of Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer (1957) and its application to nuclei by Bohr et al. (1958). The pfaffian technique for overlap signs was first introduced by Robledo (2009) and later extended to odd-A systems by Robledo and Bertsch (2012), while the gradient method for HFB equations was previously developed by the Madrid group (2011). The work enables systematic calculations of nuclear structure across the entire nuclide chart, including theoretical mass table construction, and can be extended to finite temperature systems and quantum chemistry applications.
