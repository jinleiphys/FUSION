# From Quark-Gluon Universe to Neutrino Decoupling: 200<T<2MeV

> The properties of the quark and hadron Universe are explored. Kinetic theory considerations are presented proving that hadron abundances after phase transformation from quarks to hadrons remain intact till abundances of hadrons become irrelevant. The hadronization process and the evolution of hadron yields is described in detail.

## Key claim

The paper argues that contrary to naive expectations, hadron abundances established during the quark-gluon plasma to hadron gas phase transformation remain intact until abundances become irrelevant, because the background gas of photons and leptons keeps hadrons in chemical equilibrium throughout the Universe's evolution down to neutrino decoupling at approximately 2 MeV.

## Method

The authors solve a system of three coupled nonlinear equations for the chemical potentials μ_d, μ_e, and μ_ν at each temperature T, imposing charge neutrality, net lepton number equal to net baryon number, and constant entropy-per-baryon derived from the baryon-to-photon ratio. They use statistical partition functions for both the quark-gluon plasma and hadron gas phases, including all hadrons with mass less than 2 GeV, and apply the Levenberg-Marquardt method for numerical solution. For the mixed phase during the QGP-HG transformation, they parametrize the total partition function by the fraction f_HG of phase space occupied by the hadron gas phase.

## Key numbers

- The hadronization (Hagedorn) temperature is T_h ~ 160 MeV
- The phase transformation from QGP to HG takes τ_h ~ 10 μs in the early Universe
- At T = 35 MeV, matter-antimatter annihilation is nearly complete (approximately 1 ms after the Big Bang)
- Pion abundance exceeds nucleon abundance by a factor of 10^7, with pion density falling below nucleon density only at T ≃ 6 MeV
- Strangeness becomes subdominant in abundance to baryons only at T = 20 MeV
- The baryon chemical potential just before the phase transition is μ_B = 0.33^{+0.11}_{-0.08} eV
- All chemical potentials satisfy μ_f ≲ 0.5 eV for f = d, e, ν
- The entropy-per-baryon from BBN analysis is s/n_B = 3.42^{+0.42}_{-0.39} × 10^10
- The baryon-to-photon ratio from BBN best fit is η = n_B/n_γ = 6.27 ± 0.34 × 10^{-10}
- The confining vacuum energy density is B ≃ 0.19 GeV fm^{-3}
- Thermalization timescale in the HG phase at T = 1 MeV is τ_therm ≲ 10^{-14} s, compared to the Universe age t ~ 1 s
- The π^0 lifetime in vacuum is τ_0 = 8.4 × 10^{-17} s

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous decade-long effort to improve understanding of the early quark and hadron Universe, initiated in studies of QGP to HG transformation timescales (Letessier 2002) and later refined with kinetic theory showing chemical equilibrium prevails (Kuznetsova 2008, 2010). The paper applies statistical hadronization tools developed for relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC to the early Universe, addressing the question of whether baryon asymmetry could originate in the hadron Universe through CP violation in the kaon system. The results enable quantitative study of charge distillation during the mixed phase and its potential role in matter-antimatter separation, relevant to experiments such as AMS searching for anti-alpha particles.
