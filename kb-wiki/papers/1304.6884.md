# Combustion of a neutron star into a strange quark star: The neutrino signal

> There are strong indications that the process of conversion of a neutron star into a strange quark star proceeds as a strong deflagration implying that in a few milliseconds almost the whole star is converted. Starting from the three-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations of the combustion process which provide the temperature profiles inside the newly born strange star, we calculate for the first time the neutrino signal that is to be expected if such a conversion process takes place. The neutrino emission is characterized by a luminosity and a duration that is typical for the signal expected from protoneutron stars and represents therefore a powerful source of neutrinos which could be possibly directly detected in case of events occurring close to our Galaxy. We discuss moreover possible connections between the birth of strange stars and explosive phenomena such as supernovae and gamma-ray-bursts.

## Key claim

The neutrino signal from a neutron star converting into a strange quark star via a turbulent deflagration has a luminosity and duration similar to that of a protoneutron star, but the signal lasts shorter by a factor of 3-4; the results are presented as an order-of-magnitude calculation with substantial uncertainties from the high-density equation of state.

## Method

The authors used three-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations of the combustion process (based on the piecewise parabolic method with a level-set conversion front) to obtain temperature profiles inside the newly formed strange quark star, then solved the neutrino energy diffusion equation coupled with hydrostatic structure equations to compute the cooling and neutrino luminosity. The nucleonic matter equation of state was the Lattimer and Swesty table with incompressibility modulus K=220 MeV, and the strange quark matter equation of state was the MIT bag model with perturbative QCD corrections for two parameter sets (set1 and set2) that both allow a maximum strange star mass of 2 solar masses.

## Key numbers

- Initial neutrino luminosity: approximately 3 × 10^52 erg/s, dropping to 10^50 erg after 10-15 seconds
- For a 1.4 solar mass star, the central temperature reaches 40-50 MeV after combustion and drops to about 1 MeV after approximately 10 seconds
- For a 1.8 solar mass star, the central temperature reaches 40-50 MeV after combustion and drops to about 1 MeV after approximately 15 seconds
- Total energy released by the conversion process: of the order of 10^53 erg
- Conversion time scale: a few milliseconds for the turbulent deflagration to convert almost the whole star
- For set1 (B_eff^(1/4)=142 MeV, a_4=0.9): ground state energy per baryon E/A = 860 MeV
- For set2 (B_eff^(1/4)=141 MeV, a_4=0.65): ground state energy per baryon E/A = 930 MeV

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations of Herzog (2011) by calculating for the first time the neutrino signal expected from the conversion of a neutron star into a strange quark star, which had previously only been estimated in simple terms by Keranen (2004). The results are compared to protoneutron star cooling simulations by Janka (1991) and Pons (1998, 2000, 2001), showing that the strange star signal is shorter by a factor of 3-4 due to larger neutrino mean free paths in quark matter and the absence of deleptonization. The paper enables future studies connecting strange star formation to supernova explosions and gamma-ray-burst mechanisms, particularly the protomagnetar model of Metzger (2010) and the delayed conversion scenario proposed by Berezhiani (2002).
