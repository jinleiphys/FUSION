# Correlations in impact-parameter space in a hierarchical saturation model for QCD at high energy

> In order to get an estimate of the homogeneity of the distribution of matter in a fast hadron or nucleus, we compute the correlations of the saturation scales Q_s between different points in impact-parameter space, in some specific saturation models. We find that these correlations are quite strong: The saturation scale is nearly uniform in domains whose sizes scale like exp[const*ln^2(1/α_s^2)]/Q_s, which means that the density of gluons should not fluctuate significantly over regions of that typical size. We expect these conclusions as well as the explicit analytical expressions we obtain for the correlations to be true also for full QCD in appropriate limits.

## Key claim

The authors compute correlations of the saturation scale \(Q_s\) between different points in impact-parameter space in a simplified hierarchical toy model of QCD at high energy, finding that the saturation scale is nearly uniform over domains whose size scales as \(\exp[\text{const} \times \ln^2(1/\alpha_s^2)]/Q_s\), and they expect these conclusions and analytical expressions to hold for full QCD in appropriate limits.

## Method

The authors construct a discrete hierarchical toy model based on the QCD color dipole model, where dipoles evolve by splitting into half-size or double-size dipoles at fixed rates, with a saturation mechanism that vetoes splittings into bins already containing \(N\) dipoles. They then study the correlations of the front position (related to the logarithm of the saturation scale) between two points in impact-parameter space separated by a distance \(\Delta b\), using analytical calculations of the effect of fluctuations on front propagation in the Fisher-Kolmogorov-Petrovsky-Piscounov (FKPP) framework, and check their results against numerical simulations.

## Key numbers

- The size of domains where the saturation scale is nearly uniform scales like \(\exp[\text{const} \times \ln^2(1/\alpha_s^2)]/Q_s\).
- The front velocity in the mean-field approximation is \(v_0 = \chi'(\gamma_0)\), where \(\chi(\gamma)\) is the eigenvalue of the linearized evolution kernel.
- The front extension (width) is \(L_0 = \ln N / \gamma_0\), where \(N\) is the maximum number of dipoles per bin.
- The mean front velocity including discreteness corrections is \(v_\text{BD} = \chi'(\gamma_0) - \frac{\pi^2 \chi''(\gamma_0)}{2 \gamma_0 L_0^2}\).
- The mean front velocity including fluctuations is \(v = \chi'(\gamma_0) - \frac{\pi^2 \chi''(\gamma_0)}{2 \gamma_0 (L_0 + \frac{3}{\gamma_0} \ln L_0)^2}\).
- The diffusion constant of the front is \(D = \frac{\pi^4 \chi''(\gamma_0)}{3 \gamma_0^3 L^3}\).
- The time at which the front tip reaches level \(k_{\Delta b}\) is \(t_{\Delta b} = [-\log_2 \Delta b] / v\).

## Context

This work extends the study of saturation and fluctuations in QCD beyond the mean-field Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation, which neglects stochastic effects, and beyond the one-dimensional stochastic FKPP picture applied at fixed impact parameters in earlier work by Mueller, Shoshi, and others (2005). The authors build on the color dipole model of Mueller (1994) and the numerical study of Mueller, Shoshi, and Wong (2005) to provide analytical expressions for two-point correlations of the saturation scale, enabling future calculations of observables that probe multiple points in impact-parameter space simultaneously.
