# First observation of 20B and 21B

> The most neutron-rich boron isotopes 20B and 21B have been observed for the first time following proton removal from 22N and 22C at energies around 230 MeV/nucleon. Both nuclei were found to exist as resonances which were detected through their decay into 19B and one or two neutrons. Two-proton removal from 22N populated a prominent resonance-like structure in 20B at around 2.5 MeV above the one-neutron decay threshold, which is interpreted as arising from the closely spaced 1-,2- ground-state doublet predicted by the shell model. In the case of proton removal from 22C, the 19B plus one- and two-neutron channels were consistent with the population of a resonance in 21B 2.47+-0.19 MeV above the two-neutron decay threshold, which is found to exhibit direct two-neutron decay. The ground-state mass excesses determined for 20,21B are found to be in agreement with mass surface extrapolations derived within the latest atomic-mass evaluations.

## Key claim

The most neutron-rich boron isotopes 20B and 21B have been observed for the first time as unbound resonances, with the interpretation that the structure observed in 20B arises from a closely spaced 1-,2- ground-state doublet predicted by the shell model, and that 21B exhibits direct two-neutron decay.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the RIKEN RIBF using secondary beams of 22N and 22C produced by fragmentation of a 345 MeV/nucleon 48Ca primary beam. Proton removal reactions at energies around 230 MeV/nucleon on a carbon target populated 20B and 21B, which were reconstructed via invariant-mass spectroscopy using the SAMURAI spectrometer and NEBULA neutron array to detect 19B fragments and neutrons from the decay of the unbound nuclei.

## Key numbers

- 20B resonance structure at 2.44 ± 0.09 MeV above the one-neutron decay threshold, with width Γ = 1.2 ± 0.4 MeV, from two-proton removal from 22N
- Best fit for 20B as a doublet: resonances at 1.56 ± 0.15 MeV (Γ < 0.5 MeV), 2.50 ± 0.09 MeV (Γ = 0.9 ± 0.3 MeV), and 4.86 ± 0.25 MeV (Γ < 0.5 MeV)
- 21B resonance at 2.47 ± 0.19 MeV above the two-neutron decay threshold, with width Γ < 0.6 MeV, from proton removal from 22C
- 21B resonance energy from 19B+2n events: 2.4 ± 0.4 MeV with Γ < 3 MeV
- Beam energies at target mid-point: 225 MeV/nucleon for 22N and 233 MeV/nucleon for 22C
- Beam intensities: 6600 pps for 22N and 6 pps for 22C
- Experimental mass excesses: 20B = 69.40 ± 0.38 MeV; 21B = 78.38 ± 0.40 MeV

## Context

This work extends the mapping of the neutron dripline in the boron isotopic chain, following prior studies of unbound threshold states in 16,18B (Spyrou, 2010) and the two-neutron halo of 17B and 19B (Suzuki, 1999). The results provide the first experimental mass determinations for 20,21B, which agree with mass-surface extrapolations from the 2016 atomic-mass evaluation (AME16) but show that the 2012 evaluation (AME12) overbinds these isotopes by approximately 1-3 MeV. The identification of these nuclei opens the way to exploring structure and correlations beyond the dripline below 24O, including investigations of the N=16 shell closure and tests of ab initio models that incorporate the continuum.
