# Thermal photon $v_3$ at LHC from fluctuating initial conditions

> We calculate the triangular flow parameter $v_3$ of thermal photons for 0--40\% central collisions of Pb nuclei at LHC using an event-by-event hydrodynamic model with fluctuating initial conditions. Thermal photon $v_3$ with respect to the the participant plane angle is found to be positive and significant compared to the elliptic flow parameter $v_2$ of thermal photons. In addition, photon $v_3$ as a function of $p_T$ shows similar qualitative nature to photon $v_2$ in the region $1< p_T <6$ GeV/$c$. We argue that while $v_3$ originates from $ε_3$ deformations of the initial state density distribution, fast buildup of radial flow due to fluctuations is the main driving mechanism for the observed large value.

## Key claim
Thermal photon triangular flow \(v_3\) at LHC is positive and significant compared to elliptic flow \(v_2\), but unlike hadron \(v_3\) which is driven by initial triangular eccentricity \(\epsilon_3\), photon \(v_3\) is primarily caused by fast buildup of radial flow due to initial-state fluctuations rather than by the initial geometry deformation itself.

## Method
The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional event-by-event ideal hydrodynamic simulations with fluctuating initial conditions for 0–40% central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy 2.76A TeV. Thermal photon emission was calculated using complete leading-order plasma rates integrated over the hydrodynamic evolution, with initial formation time \(\tau_0 = 0.14\) fm/c and fluctuation size parameter \(\sigma = 0.4\) fm. The triangular flow \(v_3\) was computed with respect to the participant plane angle by averaging over many events.

## Key numbers
- Thermal photon \(v_3\)(PP) is about half the value of \(v_2\)(PP) at \(p_T = 1\) GeV/\(c\).
- For \(p_T > 3\) GeV/\(c\), the difference between \(v_3\)(PP) and \(v_2\)(PP) is less than 25\%.
- The \(p_T\) range studied for \(v_3\) is \(1 < p_T < 6\) GeV/\(c\).
- The initial formation time \(\tau_0\) is 0.14 fm/c and fluctuation size \(\sigma\) is 0.4 fm.
- Including NLO plasma rates enhances thermal photon production by about 10–20% for \(p_T < 2\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous event-by-event hydrodynamic studies of thermal photon spectra and elliptic flow at RHIC and LHC (Chatterjee et al., 2014) to the triangular flow observable. It builds on the recognition that nonzero \(v_3\) arises only from fluctuating initial conditions, unlike \(v_2\) which also appears from smooth geometry. The results show that photon \(v_3\) is driven by fast transverse flow buildup rather than initial eccentricity, contrasting with hadron \(v_3\) where \(\epsilon_3\) dominates (Alver et al., 2010). This enables using photon \(v_3\) as a more direct probe of initial-state fluctuations than hadronic observables.
