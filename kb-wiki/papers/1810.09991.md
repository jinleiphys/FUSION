# Controlling Excited-State Contributions with Distillation in Lattice QCD Calculations of Nucleon Isovector Charges $g_S^{u-d}$, $g_A^{u-d}$, $g_T^{u-d}$

> We investigate the application of the distillation smearing approach, and the use of the variational method with an extended basis of operators facilitated by this approach, on the calculation of the nucleon isovector charges $g_S^{u-d}$, $g_A^{u-d}$, and $g_T^{u-d}$. We find that the better sampling of the lattice enabled through the use of distillation yields a substantial reduction in the statistical uncertainty in comparison with the use of alternative smearing methods, and furthermore, appears to offer better control over the contribution of excited-states compared to use of a single, local interpolating operator. The additional benefit arising through the use of the variational method in the distillation approach is less dramatic, but nevertheless significant given that it requires no additional Dirac inversions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the distillation smearing method, even when used with a single interpolating operator, provides substantial reduction in statistical uncertainty and better control over excited-state contamination in lattice QCD calculations of nucleon isovector charges compared to standard Jacobi smearing; the additional variational method using an extended basis of distilled operators yields further but less dramatic improvement, with the advantage of requiring no additional Dirac inversions.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations on a 350-configuration ensemble of 2+1 flavor clover-Wilson fermions on a $32^3\times64$ lattice with lattice spacing $a=0.09840(4)$ fm and pion mass $m_\pi=356$ MeV. They compared four types of interpolating operators: a standard Jacobi-smeared nucleon interpolator, a single "local" distilled interpolator ($^2S_S\frac{1}{2}^+$), and variationally improved interpolators from a 3-operator basis ($\mathcal{B}_3$) and a 7-operator basis ($\mathcal{B}_7$) of distilled interpolators, using the variational method on two-point correlation functions to construct optimal interpolators for extracting the ground-state nucleon charges $g_S^{u-d}$, $g_A^{u-d}$, and $g_T^{u-d}$.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: $a=0.09840(4)$ fm, determined via the Wilson-flow scale $w_0$
- Pion mass: $m_\pi = 356$ MeV, with $am_\pi\sim0.176803$ and $am_{\pi}L\sim5.658$
- Lattice volume: $32^3\times64$ with periodic spatial and anti-periodic temporal boundary conditions
- Distillation space rank: 64 eigenvectors
- Variational method parameters: $\hat{\mathcal{P}}_3$ used $\lbrace t_0=3, t_Z=5\rbrace$; $\hat{\mathcal{P}}_7$ used $\lbrace t_0=6, t_Z=8\rbrace$ (in lattice time units)
- Jacobi smearing parameters: 60 hits with width $\sigma=5.0$; gauge links smeared via 20 applications of stout smearing with $\rho_{ij}=0.08$

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of nucleon structure that have systematically under-determined $g_A^{u-d}$ by roughly 10-15% compared to the experimental value of $1.2724\pm0.0023$ (Tanabashi 2018). The authors build on the distillation method introduced by Peardon (2009) and the variational method applied to Jacobi-smeared interpolators by Yoon (2016) and Owen (2012), and contrast their approach with the Feynman-Hellman inspired method of Chang (2018) that achieved agreement within 1% of experiment. The techniques developed here enable more reliable extraction of nucleon matrix elements at earlier source-sink separations where the signal-to-noise ratio is exponentially better, with implications for calculations of quasi-PDFs and pseudo-PDFs, as well as for constraining beyond-Standard-Model physics through precise determinations of $g_S^{u-d}$ and $g_T^{u-d}$.
