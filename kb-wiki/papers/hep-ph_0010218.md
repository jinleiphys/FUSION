# Virtual Compton Scattering off the Pseudoscalar Meson Octet

> We present a calculation of the virtual Compton scattering amplitude for the pseudoscalar meson octet in the framework of chiral perturbation theory at ${\cal O}(p^4)$. We calculate the electromagnetic generalized polarizabilities and compare the results in the real Compton scattering limit to available experimental values. Finally, we give predictions for the differential cross section of electron-meson bremsstrahlung.

## Key claim

The paper presents a calculation of the virtual Compton scattering (VCS) amplitude for the pseudoscalar meson octet (pions, kaons, eta) using chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order (O(p^4)), but the results for the real Compton scattering (RCS) polarizabilities are "remarkably smaller compared to the experimental values and the other theoretical results," indicating a significant discrepancy that the abstract does not mention.

## Method

The authors computed the VCS amplitude for charged and neutral pions and kaons using the effective chiral Lagrangian at O(p^4) in the momentum expansion, splitting the amplitude into separately gauge-invariant Born and non-Born terms. They extracted the generalized dipole polarizabilities (alpha_L, alpha_T, beta) from the non-Born amplitude in the limit of vanishing outgoing photon energy (omega' -> 0), evaluated in the pion Breit frame. The calculation used the low-energy constant combination L_9^r + L_10^r = (1.43 +/- 0.27) x 10^{-3}, determined from the decay pi^+ -> e^+ nu_e gamma, and the pion decay constant F_pi = 92.4 MeV.

## Key numbers

*   Charged pion RCS polarizability: alpha_{pi^+/-} = -beta_{pi^+/-} = (2.72 +/- 0.42) x 10^{-4} fm^3
*   Neutral pion RCS polarizability: alpha_{pi^0} = -beta_{pi^0} = -0.50 x 10^{-4} fm^3
*   Charged kaon RCS polarizability: alpha_{K^+/-} = -beta_{K^+/-} = (0.74 +/- 0.12) x 10^{-4} fm^3
*   Neutral kaon RCS polarizability: alpha_{K^0 (bar{K}^0)} = beta_{K^0 (bar{K}^0)} = 0
*   Low-energy constant combination: L_9^r + L_10^r = (1.43 +/- 0.27) x 10^{-3}
*   Pion decay constant used: F_pi = 92.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends chiral perturbation theory calculations of Compton scattering from pions (previously done by the ChPT group, e.g., in Ref. [chpt]) to the full pseudoscalar meson octet and to the virtual photon case. The results are compared to experimental values from the Serpukhov experiment (Antipov et al., 1983) and the SELEX collaboration (Aitala et al., 1999), as well as to other theoretical models including the chiral quark model (chiral QM), Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (NJL), and quark confinement model (QCM). The predictions for the differential cross section of electron-pion bremsstrahlung provide a framework for analyzing ongoing SELEX experiment data and for planning future experiments at CERN (COMPASS) to measure kaon polarizabilities.
