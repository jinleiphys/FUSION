# $γ^{*}NΔ$ Form Factors from a Relativistic Dynamical Model of Pion Electroproduction

> We obtain the electromagnetic form factors of the $γNΔ$ transition by analyzing recent pion-electroproduction data using a fully relativistic dynamical model. Special care is taken to satisfy Ward-Takahashi identities for the Born term in the presence of form factors thereby allowing the use of realistic electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon and pion. We parametrize the $Q^2$ dependence of the {\it bare} $γN Δ$ form factors by a three-parameter form which is consistent with the asymptotic behavior inferred from QCD. The parameters of the bare $γN Δ$ form factors are the only free parameters of the model and are fitted to the differential cross-section and multipole-analysis data up to $Q^2=4$ (GeV/c)$^2$ in the $Δ(1232)$-resonance region. This analysis emphasizes the significance of the pion-cloud effects in the extraction of the resonance parameters.

## Key claim

The authors extract the electromagnetic form factors of the gamma N Delta transition by fitting a fully relativistic dynamical model of pion electroproduction to data up to Q^2 = 4 (GeV/c)^2, but they emphasize that the separation of resonance excitation from background mechanisms (such as pion rescattering) is inherently model dependent, and that pion-cloud effects contribute about 50% of the M_{1+} strength near the photon point.

## Method

The model is based on a covariant quasipotential description of pion-nucleon scattering extended to pion electroproduction, with the amplitude including a Born term (using pseudo-vector pi N N coupling), t-channel rho and omega meson exchange, and Delta-isobar exchange. The pion-nucleon final state interaction is included via a coupled-channel equation solved to first order in the electromagnetic coupling, and gauge invariance is maintained by a procedure that allows arbitrary electromagnetic form factors to be inserted into the Born terms while satisfying Ward-Takahashi identities. The bare gamma N Delta form factors are parametrized with a three-parameter form consistent with perturbative QCD asymptotic behavior and fitted to differential cross-section data and MAID multipole analysis up to Q^2 = 4 (GeV/c)^2 in the Delta(1232) resonance region.

## Key numbers

- The M_{1+} multipole strength from pion-cloud mechanisms accounts for about 50% of the total near the photon point and about 25% near Q^2 = 4 (GeV/c)^2.
- At the real photon point, the ratio R_{EM} = Im E_{1+}/Im M_{1+} is approximately -2.7%.
- At the real photon point, the ratio R_{SM} = Im S_{1+}/Im M_{1+} is approximately -2.3%.
- The R_{EM} ratio shows a tendency to cross zero and change sign in the region between 3 and 4 (GeV/c)^2.
- The model is fitted to data up to Q^2 = 4 (GeV/c)^2 in the Delta(1232) resonance region.

## Context

This work extends the covariant quasipotential description of pion-nucleon interaction developed by Pascalutsa and collaborators (2001) from photo-production to electroproduction, and exploits recent experimental results from LEGS, MAMI, JLab (Frolov, Joo), and MIT-Bates, as well as the MAID multipole analysis. The approach differs from models by Sato and Lee (2001) and the DMT group by using a parametrization of the bare gamma N Delta form factors that satisfies the perturbative QCD asymptotic Q^{-4} falloff rather than an exponential falloff, and by finding that R_{EM} changes sign below Q^2 = 4 (GeV/c)^2 in contrast to the flat negative behavior reported by Sato and Lee (2001) and the JLab data analysis of Frolov (1999). The results enable further study of the Q^2 range where perturbative QCD becomes applicable and a thorough analysis of the existing pion electroproduction data base is underway.
