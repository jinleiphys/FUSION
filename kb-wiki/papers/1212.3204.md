# Importance of Fragmentation Functions in Determining Polarized Parton Densities

> New fragmentation functions (FFs) are extracted from a NLO QCD fit to the preliminary COMPASS data on pion and kaon multiplicities. It is shown that the new kaon FFs are very different from those of De Florian at al. (DSS) and Hirai et al. (HKNS). The sensitivity of the extracted polarized PDFs to the new FFs is discussed.

## Key claim

New kaon fragmentation functions extracted from preliminary COMPASS data are very different from the DSS and HKNS sets, and using them in a combined QCD analysis yields a polarized strange quark density that is negative for all x in the measured region, consistent with inclusive DIS analyses and resolving the strange quark polarization puzzle.

## Method

The authors performed a NLO QCD fit to preliminary COMPASS 2004 deuteron data on pion and kaon multiplicities in the 2D(x,z) presentation, using 90 experimental points (45 for K+ and 45 for K-) across four z-bins. The fit determined 13 free parameters attached to input parametrizations of kaon fragmentation functions at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2, assuming all unfavored kaon FFs are equal, and achieved a chi^2/DOF of 137.8/77 = 1.79. The new FFs were then used in a combined NLO QCD analysis of world polarized inclusive and semi-inclusive DIS data to study their impact on polarized parton densities.

## Key numbers

- The fit used 90 experimental points (45 for K+ and 45 for K-) from COMPASS 2004 deuteron data.
- The best fit yielded chi^2/DOF = 137.8/77 = 1.79.
- The input parametrizations for kaon FFs were set at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2.
- The new kaon FFs are presented at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2 for comparison with DSS and HKNS sets.
- The z-bins used were [0.2-0.3; 0.3-0.45; 0.45-0.65; 0.65-0.85].

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses (LSS10, LSS07) and addresses the strange quark polarization puzzle, where the DSS fragmentation functions (De Florian et al.) gave positive polarized strange quark densities in combined analyses, contradicting the negative values from inclusive DIS analyses alone. The new preliminary COMPASS and HERMES data disagree with the DSS FFs, and the authors show that their new FFs yield a negative polarized strange quark density consistent with pure DIS analyses, with final COMPASS and HERMES data on hadron multiplicities expected to be crucial for resolving the puzzle definitively.
