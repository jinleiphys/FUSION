# Electromagnetic current operators for phenomenological relativistic models

> Background: Phenomenological Poincaré invariant quantum mechanical models can provide an efficient description of the dynamics of strongly interacting particles that is consistent with spectral and scattering observables. These models are representation dependent and in order to apply them to reactions with electromagnetic probes it is necessary to have a consistent electromagnetic current operator. Purpose: The purpose of this work is to use local gauge invariance to construct consistent strong current operators. Method: Current operators are constructed from a model Hamiltonian by replacing momentum operators in the Weyl representation by gauge covariant derivatives. Results: The construction provides a systematic method to construct explicit expressions for current operators that are consistent with relativistic models of strong interaction dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper provides a systematic method to construct explicit electromagnetic current operators for phenomenological relativistic models by replacing momentum operators in the Weyl representation with gauge covariant derivatives, but the resulting current operator is not unique due to the absence of a unique relativistic position operator and the assumption that interacting particles are point charges rather than nucleons with finite size.

## Method

The author uses local gauge invariance at a fixed common time to construct current operators from model Hamiltonians expressed in the Weyl representation, where operators are ordered with momentum operators to the right of coordinate operators. The construction replaces momentum operators in the Weyl representation by gauge covariant derivatives, then extracts the coefficient of the term linear in the vector potential to obtain the current operator. The method is illustrated by applying it to the spin-orbit, \((\mathbf{L}\cdot\mathbf{S})^2\), and \(\mathbf{L}\cdot\mathbf{L}\) parts of the non-relativistic Argonne V18 interaction, and extended to translationally invariant non-local two-body interactions and square root kinetic energy operators.

## Key numbers

- The spin-orbit contribution to the current operator is given by \(\mathbf{J}(\mathbf{x},0) = -\frac{1}{2} v_{so}(|\mathbf{q}|) (\mathbf{S}\times\mathbf{q}) (e_1\delta(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{q}_1) - e_2\delta(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{q}_2))\), where \(e_i\) are the charges of the two nucleons.
- The momentum-space matrix element of the spin-orbit current at \(\mathbf{x}=0\) is \(\langle \mathbf{p}'_1, \mathbf{p}'_2 | \mathbf{J}(\mathbf{0},0) | \mathbf{p}_1, \mathbf{p}_2 \rangle = \frac{1}{(2\pi)^6} \int [(e_1-e_2)\cos(\mathbf{q}\cdot(\mathbf{P}'-\mathbf{P})/2) - i(e_1+e_2)\sin(\mathbf{q}\cdot(\mathbf{P}'-\mathbf{P})/2)] e^{-i\mathbf{q}\cdot(\mathbf{p}'-\mathbf{p})} v_{so}(|\mathbf{q}|) (\mathbf{S}\times\mathbf{q}) d\mathbf{q}\).
- The \((\mathbf{L}\cdot\mathbf{S})^2\) contribution to the current matrix element involves terms proportional to \(-\frac{1}{2} (\mathbf{S}\times\mathbf{q})\cdot(\mathbf{p}+\mathbf{p}')(\mathbf{S}\times\mathbf{q}) - \frac{i}{2} (\mathbf{S}^2\mathbf{q} - (\mathbf{S}\cdot\mathbf{q})\mathbf{S})\).
- The \(\mathbf{L}\cdot\mathbf{L}\) contribution to the current matrix element involves a term proportional to \(\frac{1}{2} \mathbf{q} \times (\mathbf{q} \times (\mathbf{p}+\mathbf{p}'))\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of constructing current operators for phenomenological Poincaré invariant quantum mechanical models, which have been developed by Terent'ev (1976), Berestetskii (1976), Glöckle (1986), Keister (1988), and others, by providing a systematic operator-level construction rather than a matrix-element-based method. The construction addresses the challenge that current covariance and current conservation cannot be satisfied without dynamical many-body contributions to the current, which previous approaches using impulse approximation could not consistently treat for reactions with different initial and final states. The method enables the use of electromagnetic probes in relativistic quantum models where many-body parts of the hadronic current operator can now be systematically constructed, though the resulting current operator is not unique and requires extension to include nucleon form factors for realistic applications.
