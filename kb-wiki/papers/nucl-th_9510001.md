# Electromagnetic Form Factors in a Collective Model of the Nucleon

> We study the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon in a collective model of baryons. Using the algebraic approach to hadron structure, we derive closed expressions for both elastic and transition form factors, and consequently for the helicity amplitudes that can be measured in electro- and photoproduction. Effects of spin-flavor symmetry breaking and of swelling of hadrons with increasing excitation energy are considered.

## Key claim

The paper presents a semi-phenomenological collective model of the nucleon, based on an algebraic approach, that can simultaneously describe both elastic and inelastic electromagnetic form factors and helicity amplitudes; the authors find that breaking of spin-flavor symmetry to account for the neutron electric form factor worsens the description of the proton electric form factor, suggesting that meson cloud corrections play an important role in \(G^{n}_{E}\).

## Method

The authors use the algebraic approach to baryon structure based on a \(U(7)\) algebra, which describes a collective string-like configuration of three constituents. They derive closed analytic expressions for elastic and transition form factors by evaluating matrix elements of exponentiated dipole operators in the large model-space limit (\(N \rightarrow \infty\)), folding the results with a probability distribution \(g(\beta) = \beta^2 e^{-\beta/a} / 2a^3\) to obtain a dipole form for the elastic form factor. They then compute helicity amplitudes for photo- and electroproduction and study the effects of spin-flavor symmetry breaking (by introducing flavor-dependent scale parameters \(a_u\) and \(a_d\)) and of swelling of hadrons with increasing excitation energy (by making the scale parameter energy-dependent via \(a = a_0 (1 + \xi (W-M)/M)\)).

## Key numbers

- Proton charge radius: \(\langle r^2 \rangle_{E}^{p} = 12a^2\), with \(a = 0.232\) fm from a simultaneous fit to proton and neutron charge radii and form factors up to \(Q^2 = 5\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- Constituent masses from magnetic moments: \(m_u = 0.338\) GeV and \(m_d = 0.322\) GeV, with \(g_u = g_d = 1\).
- Scale parameters from fit: \(a_u = 0.230\) fm and \(a_d = 0.257\) fm.
- Ratio \(G_M^n / G_M^p\) for \(k^2 \rightarrow \infty\) with spin-flavor breaking: \(-0.541\).
- Elastic form factor drops as \(k^{-4}\) for large \(k\); transition form factors for rotational excitations (\(v=0\)) drop as \(k^{-3}\); for vibrational excitations (\(v=1, L=0\)) drop as \(k^{-4}\).
- Stretchability parameter \(\xi \approx 1\) suggested by Regge trajectories.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach to baryon structure introduced by Bijker, Iachello, and Leviatan (1994) to compute electromagnetic form factors and helicity amplitudes, providing a unified framework that includes both the harmonic oscillator quark model and the collective model. The results contrast with nonrelativistic and relativized quark models (e.g., Copley, Koniuk, Isgur, Warns, Close, Li, Capstick) which give exponential fall-off of form factors, while the collective model yields power-law behavior. The analysis enables future experimental programs at CEBAF and MAMI to test the stretching mechanism and the role of meson cloud corrections, and suggests that vector dominance models (VMD) should be revisited using the intrinsic constituent form factors derived here.
