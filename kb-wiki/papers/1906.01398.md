# "Splitting" magnetic catalysis effect prevents vacuum superconductivity in strong magnetic fields

> By comparing the two- and three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) models, we demonstrate that the naively expected vacuum superconductivity (VSC) in constant magnetic field ${\bf B}=B\hat z$ is disfavored due to the splitting magnetic catalysis effect (MCE) to chiral condensates with different quark flavors. Based on the simple two-flavor NJL model, we illuminate, in the lowest Landau level approximation, the similar origins of $π^0$ and $\barρ^+_1$ ($ρ^+$ meson with spin $S_z=1$) mass reductions with smaller $B$ and their different features at larger $B$. With the full Landau levels, the two-flavor NJL model is found to be invalid to study the magnetic field effect to $\barρ^+_1$ meson with physical vacuum mass $775~{\rm MeV}$. Then, restricted to $ρ$ meson mass below two-quark threshold in vacuum, that is $m_ρ^v<2m_q^v$, it is found that $π^0$ mass decreases and then increases with $B$ slowly, and $\barρ^+_1$ mass vanishing point is delayed to larger $B$ compared to the point particle result. In the more realistic three-flavor NJL model, all the quark masses split in strong magnetic field as a combinatorial result of their different current masses and electric charges. By choosing a vacuum mass closer to the physical one, $\barρ^+_1$ meson mass is found to be consistent with the LQCD results semi-quantitatively in smaller $B$ region but increase in larger $B$ region. These features are mainly outcomes of the interplay between the $S_z-B$ coupling effect and splitting MCE to the composite $u$ and $d$ quarks, which definitely disfavors VSC when the latter dominates. Furthermore, mesonic flavor mixing is modified by $B$ among the neutral pseudoscalars: $π^0,η_0$ and $η_8$, which is very important to suppress the mass enhancement of the effective mass eigenstates at large $B$.

## Key claim

The naively expected vacuum superconductivity (VSC) in a constant magnetic field is disfavored because the "splitting magnetic catalysis effect" causes chiral condensates of different quark flavors to respond differently to the field, and this effect dominates over the spin-field coupling that would otherwise drive the charged rho meson mass to zero.

## Method

The author compares two-flavor and three-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) models, first using the lowest Landau level approximation for intuition and then full Landau level calculations with vacuum regularization. For the two-flavor model, the gap equation and inverse meson propagators for the neutral pion and the spin-1 charged rho meson are derived in random phase approximation, and the model parameters are fixed to vacuum observables with the rho meson mass set below the two-quark threshold. The three-flavor model includes the 't Hooft six-fermion interaction and allows flavor-dependent quark masses, enabling the splitting magnetic catalysis effect to be studied.

## Key numbers

* Neutral pion mass in the two-flavor NJL model decreases mildly and then slowly increases with magnetic field, never reaching zero; the vacuum pion mass is 0.134 GeV.
* Charged rho meson mass (spin component S_z=1) in the two-flavor NJL model decreases to zero at some magnetic field, favoring VSC, but the vanishing point is delayed to larger B compared to the point particle result m_V^B = sqrt(m_V^v^2 - |eB|).
* In the two-flavor NJL model, the vacuum rho meson mass is set to 0.6 GeV, smaller than twice the vacuum quark mass 0.626 GeV, to keep the model valid.
* In the three-flavor NJL model, the charged rho meson mass is consistent with lattice QCD results semi-quantitatively in the smaller B region but increases in the larger B region, disfavoring VSC.

## Context

This work extends earlier two-flavor NJL model studies that predicted vacuum superconductivity (Chernodub 2010, 2011; Liu 2014) by showing that the splitting magnetic catalysis effect, which is absent in the two-flavor model, prevents the charged rho meson mass from vanishing. It also addresses the contradiction with lattice QCD simulations (Hidaka 2012; Bali 2017) that found no evidence for VSC. The results enable future studies of mesonic flavor mixing among neutral pseudoscalars (pi^0, eta_0, eta_8) in strong magnetic fields, which suppresses mass enhancement at large B.
