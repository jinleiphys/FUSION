# Direct measurement of the $^7$Be $L/K$ capture ratio in Ta-based superconducting tunnel junctions

> We report a high-statistics measurement of the $L/K$ orbital electron capture (EC) ratio in $^7$Be embedded in cryogenic Ta. The thin Ta film formed part of a high-resolution superconducting tunnel junction (STJ) radiation detector that was used to identify the signals from different decay channels. The measured $L/K$ capture ratio of 0.070(7) is significantly larger than the only previous measurement of this quantity and the theoretical predictions that include in-medium effects. This value is a uniquely sensitive probe of the 1s and 2s orbital overlaps with the nucleus, and is of relevance to nuclear and atomic physics, as well as Li production in novae and other astrophysical scenarios. This is the first experiment that uses STJs for nuclear-recoil detection, opening a new experimental avenue for low-energy precision measurements with rare isotopes.

## Key claim

The measured $L/K$ orbital electron capture ratio in $^7$Be embedded in cryogenic Ta is 0.070(7), which is nearly a factor of two larger than the only previous measurement of 0.040(4) in HgTe (Voytas 2002) and more than two times larger than theoretical predictions that include in-medium effects (which yield 0.031(2) for Ta). The discrepancy likely points to deficiencies in the models used to correct for in-medium effects on the electron wavefunctions.

## Method

$^7$Be$^+$ ions were produced via spallation reactions at TRIUMF's ISAC facility, selectively laser ionized, mass-selected, and implanted at 25 keV into a Ta-based superconducting tunnel junction (STJ) radiation detector. The STJ, operated at approximately 0.1 K, measured the low-energy $^7$Li recoil spectrum following electron capture decay, resolving the four peaks from $K$-capture and $L$-capture to the ground state and excited state of $^7$Li. The $L/K$ ratio was extracted by fitting the calibrated recoil spectrum with Gaussian peaks, exponential tails for shake-up/shake-off effects, and models for Auger electron escape and gamma-ray background.

## Key numbers

- Measured $L/K$ capture ratio: 0.070(7), with statistical uncertainty 0.0003 and systematic uncertainty 0.0066
- Previous measurement in HgTe: 0.040(4) (Voytas 2002)
- Theoretical prediction for atomic $^7$Be in vacuum: 0.105(8)
- Theoretical prediction for $^7$Be in Ta including in-medium effects: 0.031(2) (using a reduction factor of 0.2986 from Ray 2002)
- Energy difference between L-GS and K-GS peaks: 49.27(6) eV
- $^7$Be laboratory half-life: 53.22(6) days
- $Q_{EC}$-value: 861.89(7) keV
- Branching fraction to excited state in $^7$Li: 10.44(4)%
- Mean implantation depth into Ta layer: 46 nm with straggle of 26 nm (from SRIM simulations)
- Energy resolution of STJ: 1.4 eV to 2.9 eV FWHM over 20-140 eV range
- Calibration accuracy: better than ±10 meV in the region of interest

## Context

This work extends the only previous direct measurement of the $^7$Be $L/K$ capture ratio (Voytas 2002) using a Si microcalorimeter with HgTe absorber, which suffered from low statistics and uncertain line-shape assumptions. The measured value is relevant for benchmarking theoretical calculations of electron capture rates in astrophysical environments (e.g., novae, RGB and AGB stars) where in-medium effects alter the $2s$ electron wavefunction overlap with the nucleus, as studied theoretically by Ray (2002) and Das (2005). This is the first experiment using STJs for nuclear-recoil detection, demonstrating a new experimental avenue for low-energy precision measurements with rare isotopes.
