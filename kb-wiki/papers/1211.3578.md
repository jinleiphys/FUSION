# Chiral Perturbation Theory and the $\bar B \bar B$ Strong Interaction

> We have calculated the potentials of the heavy (charmed or bottomed) pseudoscalar mesons up to $O(ε^2)$ with the heavy meson chiral perturbation theory. We take into account the contributions from the football, triangle, box, and crossed diagrams with the 2$ϕ$ exchange and one-loop corrections to the contact terms. We notice that the total 2$ϕ$-exchange potential alone is attractive in the small momentum region in the channel ${\bar B \bar B}^{I=1}$, ${\bar B_s \bar B_s}^{I=0}$, or ${\bar B \bar B_s}^{I=1/2}$, while repulsive in the channel ${\bar B \bar B}^{I=0}$. Hopefully the analytical chiral structures of the potentials may be useful in the extrapolation of the heavy meson interaction from lattice QCD simulation.

## Key claim

The paper derives the strong interaction potentials between two bottomed pseudoscalar mesons ($\bar B \bar B$) up to $O(\epsilon^2)$ using heavy meson chiral perturbation theory, but the authors note that the finite parts of the low-energy constants at $O(\epsilon^2)$ cannot be fixed due to lack of experimental data, so the numerical analysis focuses only on the two-pion-exchange part of the potential.

## Method

The authors used Weinberg's formalism to calculate the 2-particle-irreducible (2PI) parts of Feynman diagrams for $\bar B \bar B$ scattering, including tree-level contact terms at leading order $O(\epsilon^0)$ and one-loop corrections at $O(\epsilon^2)$ from football, triangle, box, and crossed diagrams with two-pion exchange, plus one-loop corrections to contact terms. The heavy vector $\bar B^*$ mesons were included as explicit degrees of freedom alongside the $\bar B$ and light pseudoscalar mesons, with the mass difference $\Delta$ between $\bar B$ and $\bar B^*$ counted as $O(\epsilon^1)$. Dimension regularization with modified minimal subtraction was used to handle divergences.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: $m_\pi = 139$ MeV; kaon mass: $m_K = 494$ MeV; eta mass: $m_\eta = \sqrt{(4 m_K^2 - m_\pi^2)/3}$
* Mass difference between $\bar B$ and $\bar B^*$ mesons: $\Delta = 46$ MeV
* Pion decay constant: $f = 92$ MeV; axial coupling constant from unquenched lattice QCD: $g = 0.52$
* Leading order potentials: $V_{{\bar B \bar B}^{1}}^{(0)} = -2 D_a - \frac{8}{3} E_a$, $V_{{\bar B \bar B}^{0}}^{(0)} = 0$, $V_{{\bar B_s \bar B_s}^{0}}^{(0)} = -2 D_a - \frac{8}{3} E_a$, $V_{{\bar B \bar B_s}^{1/2}}^{(0)} = -2 D_a - \frac{8}{3} E_a$
* The ratio of box diagram Y-functions satisfies $15 \lessapprox Y^{B.1}(m_{\phi_1},m_{\phi_2}) / Y^{B.1}(m_\pi,m_\pi) \lessapprox 50$

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory formalism developed by Weinberg (1990, 1991) for the two-nucleon system to the heavy meson sector, specifically for $\bar B \bar B$ interactions. It provides a model-independent framework complementary to earlier one-boson-exchange (OBE) model studies of heavy meson molecules, such as those for $X(3872)$ and $Z_b$ states. The analytical chiral structures of the potentials are intended to aid extrapolation of heavy meson interactions from lattice QCD simulations, and the authors note that future experimental data from SuperBelle or lattice QCD studies using Luscher's formalism could help determine the unknown low-energy constants.
