# Problem of Light Scalar Mesons

> The following topics are considered. 1. Confinement, chiral dynamics and light scalar mesons. 2. $ϕ$-meson radiative decays about nature of light scalar resonances. Arguments in favor of the four-quark model of the $a_0(980)$ and $f_0(980)$ mesons are given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the copious radiative decays $\phi\to\gamma a_0(980)$ and $\phi\to\gamma f_0(980)$, which appear to violate the Okubo-Zweig-Izuka (OZI) rule in a naive quark-antiquark picture, are instead naturally explained by a four-quark ($q^2\bar q^2$) structure for these scalar mesons, with the production mechanism proceeding via a $K^+K^-$ loop intermediate state.

## Method

The author reviews experimental branching ratios for $\phi\to\gamma\pi^0\eta$ and $\phi\to\gamma\pi^0\pi^0$ from the SND, CMD-2, and KLOE Collaborations, and analyzes the invariant mass spectra of the final-state mesons. He demonstrates that the observed spectral shapes require a smooth, nearly constant production form factor $|g_R(m)|^2$ in the range $m \leq 0.99$ GeV, which is naturally provided by the $K^+K^-$-loop model but cannot be explained by a simple isolated resonance with a photon-energy-dependent coupling. The analysis is extended using large $N_C$ expansion arguments to compare the OZI suppression factors for two-quark versus four-quark interpretations.

## Key numbers

- $BR(\phi\to\gamma\pi^0\eta) = (0.88 \pm 0.14 \pm 0.09) \times 10^{-4}$ (SND) and $(0.9 \pm 0.24 \pm 0.1) \times 10^{-4}$ (CMD-2)
- $BR(\phi\to\gamma\pi^0\pi^0) = (1.221 \pm 0.098 \pm 0.061) \times 10^{-4}$ (SND) and $(0.92 \pm 0.08 \pm 0.06) \times 10^{-4}$ (CMD-2)
- KLOE measured $BR(\phi\to\gamma\pi^0\eta(\to\gamma\gamma)) = (0.851 \pm 0.051 \pm 0.057) \times 10^{-4}$ and $BR(\phi\to\gamma\pi^0\pi^0) = (1.09 \pm 0.03 \pm 0.05) \times 10^{-4}$
- The photon energy at $m = 990$ MeV is $\omega(990\ \text{MeV}) = 29$ MeV
- In the $K^+K^-$-loop model, the function $|g(m)|^2$ is suppressed by the $(\omega(m))^2$ law in the region 950-1020 MeV for the physical kaon mass; shifting the kaon mass to 469 MeV or 444 MeV shifts the effective spectrum maximum to 935-950 MeV or 880-900 MeV respectively

## Context

This talk extends the author's earlier work (1994) showing that a negative background phase in the linear sigma model can "shield" the $\sigma(600)$ meson, and builds on the four-quark model proposal of Jaffe (1977) for the $a_0(980)$ and $f_0(980)$ states. The analysis of $\phi$ radiative decays provides a new experimental test that distinguishes between the four-quark and conventional $P$-wave $q\bar q$ interpretations, and the large $N_C$ expansion arguments show that the $K^+K^-$-loop mechanism corresponds to a four-quark transition that is OZI-allowed for $q^2\bar q^2$ states but OZI-forbidden for $q\bar q$ states. The results enable future work to treat the $a_0(980)$ and $f_0(980)$ as established four-quark states and to extend the same framework to the putative $\sigma(600)$ and $\kappa(700-900)$ members of the scalar nonet.
