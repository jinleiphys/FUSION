# Strong coupling expansion for finite temperature Yang-Mills theory in the confined phase

> We perform euclidean strong coupling expansions for Yang Mills theory on the lattice at finite temperature. After setting up the formalism for general SU(N), we compute the first few terms of the series for the free energy density and the lowest screening mass in the case of SU(2). To next-to-leading order the free energy series agrees with that of an ideal gas of glueballs. This demonstrates that in the confined phase the quasi-particles indeed correspond to the T=0 hadron excitations, as commonly assumed in hadron resonance gas models. Our result also fixes the lower integration constant for Monte Carlo calculations of the thermodynamic pressure via the integral method. In accord with Monte Carlo results, we find screening masses to be nearly temperature independent in the confined phase. This and the exponential smallness of the pressure can be understood as genuine strong coupling effects. Finally, we analyse Pade approximants to estimate the critical couplings of the phase transition, which for our short series are only ~25% accurate. However, up to these couplings the equation of state agrees quantitatively with numerical results on N_t=1-4 lattices.

## Key claim

The strong coupling expansion of the free energy density for SU(2) Yang-Mills theory on the lattice, to next-to-leading order, agrees with that of an ideal gas of glueballs, demonstrating from first principles that quasi-particles in the confined phase correspond to T=0 hadron excitations as assumed in hadron resonance gas models. The authors note that their Padé approximant estimates for the critical couplings of the phase transition are only approximately 25% accurate due to the short series length.

## Method

The authors perform Euclidean strong coupling expansions for SU(N) Yang-Mills theory on an Ω = N_s^3 × N_t lattice with the Wilson action, computing the first few terms of the series for the free energy density and the lowest screening mass for SU(2). They classify contributing graphs based on their temporal extent relative to N_t, sum basic polymers and their decorations (including higher representation plaquettes and geometric decorations) using the cluster expansion formalism, and analyze the resulting series using Padé approximants to estimate critical couplings.

## Context

This work extends strong coupling expansion techniques, previously applied to zero-temperature lattice gauge theory for quantities such as glueball masses (Sexton et al., 1990s) and energy density (Balian, Drouffe, 1970s), to finite temperature Yang-Mills theory in the confined phase. The results provide a first-principles justification for the hadron resonance gas model (Hagedorn, 1960s) and fix the lower integration constant for Monte Carlo calculations of thermodynamic pressure via the integral method (Boyd et al., 1996). The approach enables analytic study of the confined phase equation of state and screening masses, complementing lattice Monte Carlo simulations which face difficulties due to exponential suppression of pressure at low temperatures.
