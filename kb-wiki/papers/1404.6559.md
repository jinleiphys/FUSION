# Coupling highly excited nuclei to the atomic shell in dense astrophysical plasmas

> In dense astrophysical plasmas, neutron capture populates highly excited nuclear states close to the neutron threshold. The impact of additional low-energy nuclear excitations via coupling to the atomic shell on the ability of the so-formed compound nucleus to retain the captured neutron is investigated. We focus on the mechanism of nuclear excitation by electron capture in plasmas characterized by electron fluxes typical for the slow neutron capture process of stellar nucleosynthesis. The small effect of this further excitation on the neutron capture and gamma decay sequence relevant for nucleosynthesis is quantified and compared to the corresponding effect of an additional low-energy photoexcitation step.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC) can further excite compound nuclei formed by neutron capture in dense astrophysical plasmas, finding that for typical s-process nucleosynthesis conditions the effect is small, with stellar mitigation factor (SMF) values on the order of 10^{-9} rising to only 10^{-4} at plasma temperatures of about 1.2 × 10^9 K; at such higher temperatures typical for r-process nucleosynthesis, photoabsorption rather than NEEC is the favored excitation mechanism.

## Method

The authors adapted the NEEC formalism from Ref. Pa06 (originally developed for nuclear transitions close to the ground state) to describe excitation starting from highly excited compound states near the neutron threshold, using nuclear level density parametrizations from the back-shifted Fermi gas model and photon strength functions from the giant dipole resonance to estimate the reduced nuclear transition probabilities. They extended the theoretical NEEC treatment to account for the temperature domain of both neutron and electron fluxes and the role of multiple neutron resonances, then quantified the impact by defining a stellar mitigation factor (SMF) as the ratio of effective cross sections for the neutron capture and gamma decay sequence with and without the additional NEEC step.

## Key numbers

- For typical s-process nucleosynthesis conditions, SMF values are on the order of 10^{-9}
- At a plasma temperature of T ≃ 1.2 × 10^9 K, SMF values rise to 10^{-4}
- The total width Γ_tot of the excited state (2) was taken as a constant 100 eV, consistent with NEEC into higher free electronic shells and a single 1s electron in the K shell
- The neutron separation energy S_n for ¹⁸⁷Os is 6.3 MeV
- The photon strength function for ¹⁹³Ir is about two times larger than that for ¹⁸⁷Os

## Context

This work extends the investigation of additional excitation steps on compound nuclei formed by neutron capture, following the arguments of Bernstein and Bernstein (recent papers) who found that low-energy photon absorption after neutron capture could dominate over direct gamma decay above certain temperatures typical for r-process nucleosynthesis. The present study provides the counterpart for NEEC as the excitation mechanism instead of photoabsorption, building on prior work by Gosselin (2004, 2007, 2010), Morel (2010), and Gunst (2014) who investigated NEEC and NEET for isomer depletion under dense plasma conditions. The results enable a comparison of the relative importance of atomic-shell-mediated versus photoabsorption-mediated excitation of compound nuclei in nucleosynthesis models, showing that for s-process conditions NEEC has negligible impact while for r-process temperatures photoabsorption remains the dominant mechanism.
