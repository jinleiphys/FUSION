# Quarkonium-photoproduction prospects at a fixed-target experiment at the LHC (AFTER@LHC)

> We report on the potentialities offered by a fixed-target experiment at the LHC using the proton and ion LHC beams (AFTER@LHC project) regarding the study of J/psi exclusive-photoproduction in pA and AA collisions. The foreseen usage of polarised targets (hydrogen, deuteron, helium) gives access to measurements of the Single-Transverse-Spin Asymmetries of this exclusive process, therefore allowing one to access the helicity-flip Generalised Parton Distribution (GPD) Eg. We detail the expected yields of photoproduced J/psi in proton-hydrogen and lead-hydrogen collisions and discuss the statistical uncertainties on the asymmetry measurement for one year of data taking at the LHC.

## Key claim

AFTER@LHC, using a polarised hydrogen target with a storage cell, can measure the Single-Transverse-Spin Asymmetry (STSA) of exclusive J/psi photoproduction with statistical precision better than 10% in two pT bins, thereby providing the first access to the helicity-flip gluon Generalised Parton Distribution (GPD) Eg, contingent on achieving the projected yearly luminosities of 10 fb^-1 in proton-hydrogen collisions.

## Method

The authors used the STARLIGHT Monte Carlo generator to simulate 100,000 photoproduced J/psi events decaying into muon pairs in proton-hydrogen collisions at sqrt(s) = 115 GeV and lead-hydrogen collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 72 GeV. They applied LHCb-like forward detector acceptance cuts (2 < eta_lab^mu < 5, pT^mu > 0.4 GeV/c) and calculated expected yearly yields based on projected luminosities for three target technologies: gas-jet, storage cell, and bent crystal with solid target. The statistical uncertainty on the STSA (A_N) was derived from the expected yields, assuming the target polarisation and the modulation from the GPD Eg.

## Key numbers

*   In proton-hydrogen collisions with a storage cell, the expected yearly integrated luminosity is 10 fb^-1, yielding about 200,000 photoproduced J/psi per year in an LHCb-like acceptance.
*   In lead-hydrogen collisions with a storage cell, the expected yearly integrated luminosity is 100 nb^-1, yielding about 1,000 photoproduced J/psi per year in an LHCb-like acceptance.
*   The total J/psi photoproduction cross section in proton-hydrogen collisions is 1.18 x 10^3 pb; in lead-hydrogen collisions (with Pb as photon emitter) it is 276.77 x 10^3 pb.
*   The expected statistical precision on A_N in proton-hydrogen collisions is better than 10% for the two J/psi pT ranges 0.4 < pT < 0.6 GeV/c and 0.6 < pT < 0.8 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the physics programme of the proposed AFTER@LHC fixed-target experiment, previously outlined by Brodsky (2012), Lansberg (2012, 2013, 2014, 2016), and others, by demonstrating the feasibility of exclusive J/psi photoproduction for accessing gluon GPDs. It builds on the theoretical framework of Koempel (2011) linking STSA in photoproduction to the GPD Eg, and on the STARLIGHT Monte Carlo generator developed by Klein (1999), Baltz (2002, 2009), and others. The projected yields and precision would enable the first determination of Eg, a key quantity for understanding the total angular momentum of gluons in the nucleon, and complement spin-physics measurements at RHIC and other facilities.
