# Revisit of directed flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions from a multiphase transport model

> We have revisited several interesting questions on how the rapidity-odd directed flow is developed in relativistic $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 and 39 GeV based on a multiphase transport model. As the partonic phase evolves with time, the slope of the parton directed flow at midrapidity region changes from negative to positive as a result of the later dynamics at 200 GeV, while it remains negative at 39 GeV due to the shorter life time of the partonic phase. The directed flow splitting for various quark species due to their different initial eccentricities is observed at 39 GeV, while the splitting is very small at 200 GeV. From a dynamical coalescence algorithm with Wigner functions, we found that the directed flow of hadrons is a result of competition between the coalescence in momentum and coordinate space as well as further modifications by the hadronic rescatterings.

## Key claim

The slope of parton directed flow at midrapidity in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV changes from negative to positive during the partonic phase due to later dynamics, while at 39 GeV it remains negative because the partonic phase lifetime is too short to reverse the slope; this non-monotonic behavior is a condition that the abstract presents as a key finding rather than a universal feature.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT (a multiphase transport) model, which includes HIJING for initial conditions, Zhang's parton cascade (ZPC) for partonic evolution with a total cross section of 3 mb, a coalescence model for hadronization (both default spatial coalescence and a dynamical coalescence using Wigner functions), and the ART model for hadronic rescatterings. They simulated midcentral (impact parameter b = 5 fm) Au+Au collisions at 200 and 39 GeV and tracked the time evolution of the rapidity-odd directed flow for partons and hadrons.

## Key numbers

- At 200 GeV, the parton directed flow slope at midrapidity first drops below -0.3% and then increases to about +0.15% during the partonic evolution.
- At 39 GeV, the parton directed flow slope drops to about -0.6% and the later dynamics only slightly modifies the slope.
- The partonic strong scatterings mostly end around 4 to 6 fm/c at both energies.
- The integrated directed flow at forward and backward rapidities becomes saturated at about 8 fm/c at 200 GeV, while at 39 GeV it saturates at a later time.
- The RMS radius used in the dynamical coalescence Wigner function is 0.61 fm for pions and 0.877 fm for protons.
- The strong coupling constant was set to 0.47 and the parton screening mass to 3.2264 fm^-1, giving a total parton cross section of 3 mb.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of directed flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly the non-monotonic behavior observed by Liu (1999) and Steinheimer (2014), and the beam-energy-scan results from the RHIC-STAR Collaboration. The study clarifies how the directed flow develops at different stages (partonic phase, hadronization via coalescence, and hadronic rescatterings) and explains the splitting of directed flow between different quark species at lower energies. The results enable future work incorporating mean-field potentials and the equation of state to extract information about the hadron-quark phase transition and the QCD phase diagram.
