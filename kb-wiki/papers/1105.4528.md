# Phase diagrams in the Hadron-PNJL model

> The two-Equation of State (Two-EoS) model is used to describe the hadron-quark phase transition in dense-hot matter formed in heavy-ion collisions. The non-linear Walecka model is used to describe the hadronic phase. For the quark phase, the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model coupled to Polyakov-Loop fields (PNJL) is used to include both the chiral and (de)confinement dynamics. The phase diagrams are derived from the Gibbs conditions and compared with the results obtained in the Hadron-NJL model without confinement. As in the Hadron-NJL case a first order transition is observed, but with a Critical-End-Point at much higher temperature, consequence of the confinement mechanism that reduces the degrees of freedom of the quark matter in proximity of the phase transition. Particular attention is devoted to the phase transition in isospin asymmetric matter. Interesting isospin effects are found at high baryon density and reduced temperatures, in fact common also to other quark models, like MIT-Bag and NJL model. Some possible observation signals are suggested to probe in Heavy-Ion Collision (HIC) experiments at intermediate energies.

## Key claim

The Hadron-PNJL model produces a first-order hadron-quark phase transition with a Critical-End-Point (CEP) at much higher temperature than the Hadron-NJL model, because the confinement mechanism in the PNJL model reduces the quark degrees of freedom near the phase transition.

## Method

The authors used a Two-Equation of State (Two-EoS) approach, describing the hadronic phase with the non-linear Walecka model (RMF) and the quark phase with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model coupled to Polyakov-Loop fields (PNJL). The two phases were connected via Gibbs conditions requiring thermal, chemical, and mechanical equilibrium, with baryon number and isospin conservation enforced during the transition.

## Key numbers

- The CEP of the chiral transition in the pure PNJL model appears at (132.2, 296.6) MeV in the T-mu_q plane
- The critical temperature for the deconfinement phase transition at zero chemical potential was set to T_c = 171 MeV by rescaling T_0 = 210 MeV
- In the Hadron-NJL model, the Gibbs conditions have no solution for T approximately 75 MeV and rho/rho_0 approximately 1.8
- In the Hadron-PNJL model, the Gibbs conditions have no solution for T approximately 170 MeV and rho/rho_0 approximately 1.6
- The PNJL model parameters used: Lambda = 603.2 MeV, G*Lambda^2 = 1.835, K*Lambda^5 = 12.36, m_u,d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 140.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous Two-EoS studies by the same group that used the MIT-Bag model (Toro et al. 2009) and the NJL model (Shao et al. 2011) for the quark phase, where a CEP was found at about T=80 MeV and mu=900 MeV with the NJL model. The inclusion of Polyakov-Loop fields shifts the CEP to much higher temperature, bringing it closer to the critical temperature for crossover from lattice QCD at vanishing chemical potential. The results enable predictions for heavy-ion collision experiments at intermediate energies, particularly regarding isospin effects at high baryon density and reduced temperatures.
