# Linking the parameters of diquark-quark model to the Cabibbo angle

> From two different modifications of the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass relation for (1/2)^+ baryons: the first one given by a version of diquark-quark model and the second one being the optimal mass sum rule obtained by using quantum groups U_q(su_n) for the role of hadronic flavor symmetries, we find direct connection of the mass parameters of the diquark-quark model of Lichtenberg, Tassie and Keleman to the (proper value of) q-parameter and then to the Cabibbo angle.

## Key claim
The paper claims a direct mathematical connection between the mass parameters of the Lichtenberg-Tassie-Keleman (LTK) diquark-quark model for octet baryons and the Cabibbo angle, mediated by the deformation parameter q of quantum-group-based mass sum rules. This connection is conditional on using the optimized values of the LTK parameters that correspond to an all-order account of SU(3) symmetry breaking, rather than the values originally adopted by LTK.

## Method
The authors equate the correction term to the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass relation derived from the LTK diquark-quark model with the correction term from the most accurate q-deformed mass sum rule (corresponding to q_7 = exp(iπ/7)). By imposing consistency conditions between the two expressions, they derive a relation linking the ratio ξ_ts = γ_t/γ_s of LTK model parameters to the q-number [2]_7. Using the previously established relation between q_7 and the Cabibbo angle (π/7 = 2θ_C), they obtain a formula connecting cos(2θ_C) directly to the optimized LTK parameter ratio.

## Key numbers
- The most accurate q-deformed mass sum rule (for q_7 = exp(iπ/7)) reproduces octet baryon masses with 0.07% accuracy, compared to 0.58% for the standard Gell-Mann-Okubo relation.
- The deformation parameter q_7 = exp(iπ/7) gives [2]_7 = 2 cos(π/7) ≈ 1.80194.
- The two solutions for the optimized LTK parameter ratio are ξ_ts^(+) = 0.741 and ξ_ts^(-) = -6.705.
- The decuplet mass combination yields 8 μ_s = 2 m_Ξ* - m_Σ* - m_Ω = +9.8 MeV.
- The Cabibbo angle is given by the exact value π/14 from the relation π/7 = 2θ_C.

## Context
This work connects two distinct approaches to modifying the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass relation for octet baryons: the diquark-quark model of Lichtenberg, Tassie and Keleman (LTK), and the quantum-group-based approach using U_q(su_n) flavor symmetries developed by Gavrilik and collaborators. The LTK model had previously yielded a value ξ_ts = -3 that gave a correction to GMO in the wrong direction, while the quantum-group approach had already linked q_7 to the Cabibbo angle. The new connection enables interpreting the LTK model parameters in terms of the Cabibbo angle, and provides optimized values (ξ_ts = 0.741) that give positive corrections to GMO and imply a specific hierarchy among the diquark and quark mass parameters.
