# Mass Spectra of $Λ_Q\barΣ_Q$ Hexaquark States in QCD Sum Rules

> Recently, the BESIII Collaboration indicate that no $Λ_c\barΣ_c$ bound-state with a mass near threshold in the range $4715$--$4735~\mathrm{MeV}$ was observed. In order to determine the plausible mass region of the states in this structure, we calculate the mass spectrum of the $Λ_c\barΣ_c$ configuration with the method of QCD sum rules. Two linearly independent interpolating currents are constructed, and contributions from nonperturbative condensates up to dimension 12 are included in the numerical results. Consequently, we obtain the masses of the candidate states with quantum numbers $J^P = 0^-,\,0^+,\,1^-,\,1^+$. Our results show that the central values of the $Λ_c\barΣ_c$ ground-state masses lie around the $5.8~\mathrm{GeV}$ region, which do not support them as bound states and consistent with the findings reported by the BESIII Collaboration. Furthermore, we compute the mass spectrum of the $Λ_b\barΣ_b$ states with quantum numbers $J^P = 0^-,\,0^+,\,1^-,\,1^+$, which could be served as hidden-bottom candidates in the experimental detecting.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule calculations show that the central values of the $\Lambda_c\bar{\Sigma}_c$ ground-state masses lie around 5.8 GeV, which is significantly above the 4.71 GeV threshold and the 4715--4735 MeV range where BESIII searched, meaning these states are not bound states near threshold and are consistent with the BESIII non-observation.

## Method

The authors constructed two linearly independent interpolating currents (Type-I and Type-II) for the $\Lambda_Q\bar{\Sigma}_Q$ hexaquark system and computed two-point correlation functions using the QCD sum rules method. Nonperturbative condensate contributions up to dimension 12 were included in the operator product expansion, and Borel sum rules were used to extract ground-state masses for quantum numbers $J^P = 0^-,\,0^+,\,1^-,\,1^+$ after establishing Borel windows satisfying OPE convergence and pole dominance criteria.

## Context

This work addresses the discrepancy between a one-boson-exchange potential and Bethe-Salpeter equation study by Dong (2021) which predicted a $\Lambda_c\bar{\Sigma}_c$ bound state near 4.7--4.8 GeV, and the BESIII (2025) experimental search that found no such structure in the 4715--4735 MeV range. The results extend prior QCD sum rule studies of hidden-charm baryonium states by Chen (2016) and Wan (2019) which found $\Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c$ masses around 5.0 GeV, showing that $\Lambda_c\bar{\Sigma}_c$ masses are even higher at approximately 5.8 GeV. The predictions for $\Lambda_b\bar{\Sigma}_b$ states provide mass targets for future experimental searches for hidden-bottom hexaquark candidates.
