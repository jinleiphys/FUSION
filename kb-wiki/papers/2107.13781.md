# Critical properties of various sizes of cluster in the Ising percolation transition

> It is proposed that the $O(n)$ spin and geometrical percolation models can help to study the QCD phase diagram due to the universality properties of the phase transition. In this paper, correlations and fluctuations of various sizes of cluster in the Ising model are systematically studied. With a finite size system, we demonstrate how to use the finite size scaling and fixed point behavior to search for critical point. At critical point, the independency of system size is found from skewness and kurtosis of the maximum, second and third largest cluster and their correlations. It is similar to the Binder-ratio, which has provided a remarkable identification of the critical point. Through an explanation of the universal characteristic of skewness and kurtosis of the order parameter, a possible application to the relativistic heavy-ion collisions is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that skewness and kurtosis of the maximum, second, and third largest cluster sizes in the 2D Ising model exhibit a fixed-point behavior at the critical temperature, where their values become independent of system size, similar to the Binder-ratio; however, the sign-change and non-monotonic behaviors often used as critical signals are not always robust and depend on system size.

## Method

Monte Carlo simulations of the 2D Ising model were performed on square lattices of size N = L^2 with L = 50, 100, 150, 200 using the Wolff cluster algorithm with periodic boundary conditions and no external magnetic field. Clusters were constructed by linking nearest-neighbor spins with the same spin value, and the maximum, second, and third largest cluster sizes were recorded from 2 million thermalized configurations per system size. Finite-size scaling and fixed-point analyses were applied to study correlations (C12, C13, C23), skewness, and kurtosis of these differently ranked cluster sizes near the critical point.

## Key numbers

- Simulations performed on square lattices with L = 50, 100, 150, 200 (system sizes N = L^2)
- 40,010,000 Wolff Monte Carlo steps total per system size, with 2 million states used in calculations and initial 10,000 steps for equilibration
- One state used every 20 Monte Carlo steps to ensure independent configurations
- The percolation correlation length exponent nu_p equals the magnetization correlation length exponent nu_m = 1
- Values of C23 are one order of magnitude larger than those of C12 and C13
- Values of kurtosis are all one order of magnitude larger than the corresponding skewness for differently ranked cluster sizes
- Skewness and kurtosis of the second largest cluster are systematically larger than those of the maximum largest cluster

## Context

This work extends the application of finite-size scaling and fixed-point methods from the Binder-ratio (Binder) to skewness and kurtosis of multiple cluster sizes in the 2D Ising percolation transition, which belongs to the same universality class as the QCD critical point. The study connects to the Color String Percolation Model (CSPM) used in heavy-ion collisions, where percolation properties from Stauffer have been applied to infinite systems, and addresses the need for finite-size effects. The results enable future application of these fixed-point observables to relativistic heavy-ion collision data from RHIC/STAR, where non-monotonic signals in kurtosis times variance (kappa sigma^2) of net-proton distributions have been observed but the fixed-point behavior in skewness and kurtosis has not yet been seen.
