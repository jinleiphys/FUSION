# Initial Conditions in the One-Fluid Hydrodynamical Description of Ultrarelativistic Nuclear Collisions

> We present a phenomenological model for the initial conditions needed in a one-fluid hydrodynamical description of ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions at CERN--SPS. The basic ingredient is the parametrization of the baryon stopping, i.e. the rapidity distribution, as a function of the thickness of the nuclei. We apply the model to S + S and Pb + Pb collisions and find after hydrodynamical evolution reasonable agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a phenomenological parametrization of baryon stopping (initial baryon rapidity distribution) as a function of nuclear thickness, which, when used as initial conditions for one-fluid hydrodynamics, yields reasonable agreement with experimental hadron spectra for both S+S and Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS energies, but only after accounting for impact-parameter averaging via an effective nuclear size parameter and with the caveat that the stopping parameter beta_s depends on the equation of state used in the evolution.

## Method

The authors parametrize the initial baryon rapidity distribution using a functional form based on two exponential functions of third-order polynomials in scaled rapidity, with parameters a, b, and c that depend on the nuclear thickness function T_A(rho) and the inelastic pp cross section (32 mb). The initial energy density is described by a Gaussian in rapidity with width correlated to baryon stopping, and the initial longitudinal velocity profile is taken as linear in z with a transverse-position-dependent slope kappa(rho). The hydrodynamic evolution is solved on a 2+1 dimensional grid assuming azimuthal symmetry, using an equation of state with a phase transition to quark-gluon plasma at T_c = 165 MeV, and freeze-out at constant energy density 0.15 GeV/fm^3.

## Key numbers

- Inelastic pp cross section used: sigma_pp = 32 mb
- Stopping parameter beta_s = 2.25 (determined from fits to S+S data)
- Effective nuclear size parameter xi = 0.9 for both S+S and Pb+Pb
- Effective equilibration time tau_0^eff = 1.2 fm/c for S+S, 1.3 fm/c for Pb+Pb
- Central initial energy density: 7.1 GeV/fm^3 for S+S, 16.7 GeV/fm^3 for Pb+Pb
- Central initial baryon density: 0.59 fm^{-3} for S+S, 1.94 fm^{-3} for Pb+Pb
- Central initial temperature: 244 MeV for S+S, 300 MeV for Pb+Pb
- Central freeze-out time t_f: 6.1 fm/c for S+S, 11.4 fm/c for Pb+Pb
- Mean radial velocity at freeze-out (|y_z| < 0.25): 0.28 for S+S, 0.34 for Pb+Pb
- Energy density parameter c_epsilon = 0.75, alpha_epsilon = 0.13
- Phase transition temperature T_c = 165 MeV
- Freeze-out energy density epsilon_f = 0.15 GeV/fm^3
- Average freeze-out temperature T_f approximately 140 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier one-fluid hydrodynamic approaches (Schlei et al., 1996; Sollfrank et al., 1997) by incorporating a transverse-position-dependent baryon stopping parametrization, whereas previous treatments used uniform stopping across the transverse plane. The model enables systematic determination of initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies, reducing the arbitrariness of trial-and-error fitting. The results are compared with data from the NA35 collaboration (Bachler et al., 1994) for S+S and the NA49 collaboration (Jones et al., 1996) for Pb+Pb, and the extracted freeze-out parameters are compared with analyses from the NA44 collaboration (Bearden et al., 1996).
