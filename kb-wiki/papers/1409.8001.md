# Momentum scale dependence of the net quark number fluctuations near chiral crossover

> We investigate properties of the net baryon number fluctuations near chiral crossover in a hot and dense medium of strongly interacting quarks. The chirally invariant quark-antiquark interactions are modeled by an effective quark-meson Lagrangian. To preserve remnants of criticality in the O(4) universality class, we apply the functional renormalization group method to describe thermodynamics near chiral crossover. Our studies are focused on the influence of the momentum cuts on the critical behavior of different cumulants of the net quark number fluctuations. We use the momentum scale dependence of the flow equation to examine how the suppression of the momentum modes in the infrared and ultraviolet regime modifies generic properties of fluctuations expected in the O(4) universality class. We show, that the pion mass $m_π$ is a natural soft momentum scale at which cumulants are saturated at their critical values, whereas for scales larger than $2m_π$ the characteristic O(4) structure of the higher order cumulants get lost. These results indicate, that when measuring fluctuations of the net baryon number in heavy ion collisions to search for a partial restoration of chiral symmetry or critical point, a special care have to be made when introducing kinematical cuts on the fluctuation measurements.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the characteristic O(4) critical structure of higher-order net quark number fluctuations near the chiral crossover is preserved only when the infrared momentum scale is smaller than approximately twice the pion mass (2mπ), and that imposing momentum cuts above this scale suppresses the critical signatures, meaning experimental measurements of net baryon number fluctuations in heavy ion collisions must carefully account for kinematic cuts.

## Method

The authors employed the two-flavor quark-meson (QM) model with chirally invariant quark-antiquark interactions and applied the functional renormalization group (FRG) method within the local potential approximation to compute the scale-dependent thermodynamic potential. They solved the FRG flow equations numerically using a Taylor expansion method around the minimum of the effective potential, with optimized regulator functions for both bosonic and fermionic fields, and calculated the net quark number cumulants as derivatives of the pressure with respect to quark chemical potential at different momentum scales k.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum pion mass used in the primary calculations is mπ = 135 MeV, with a second set of calculations using mπ = 67.5 MeV for comparison
- The ultraviolet cutoff scale is Λ = 1.0 GeV, and the Yukawa coupling g = 3.2 is fixed to yield mq,0 = 300 MeV at T = 0
- At T = 0, the chiral symmetry breaking scale kch ≈ 900 MeV marks where the order parameter begins rapid growth toward its vacuum value
- The sixth-to-second order cumulant ratio R6,2 = χ6/χ2 becomes negative near Tpc only when the infrared cutoff k < 2mπ; for k > 2mπ the negative structure disappears
- At finite chemical potential (μpc = 300 MeV, Tpc = 125 MeV), all cumulant ratios saturate at their critical values when the momentum scale reaches the pion mass, with R5,1 showing the strongest scale dependence

## Context

This work extends previous studies of net baryon number fluctuations in the O(4) universality class using FRG methods (Stokic 2010, Friman 2011) and addresses the practical issue of how kinematic cuts in heavy ion experiments affect the critical signatures predicted by chiral effective models. The results are directly relevant to interpreting STAR Collaboration measurements of net proton fluctuations from the Beam Energy Scan program (Aggarwal 2010, STAR 2013), which were taken within the transverse momentum range 0.4 < pT < 0.8 GeV/c, and indicate that such cuts may suppress the O(4) critical structure if they exclude momentum modes below approximately 2mπ.
