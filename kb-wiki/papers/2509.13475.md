# Pairing Strength and Quadrupole-Soft Tin Isotopes

> Background: Understanding the experimental $B(E2)$ values for Sn isotopes around $^{110}$Sn has been a significant challenge in nuclear structure studies for over a decade. Both experimental data and many, though not all, calculations suggest a picture of the light Sn isotopes as being quadrupole-soft, that is, spherical, yet easy to deform. Purpose: To investigate the delicate interplay of quadrupole deformation and pairing correlations in these nuclides. In particular, by using slightly enhanced pairing, we ask: can we generate spherical mean-field solutions that describe the data? Method: First, we apply the standard spherical Skyrme HFBCS-QRPA calculation with default pairing parameters, allowing us to identify nuclides that are unstable against quadrupole deformation among Sn isotopes. Next, we moderately enhance the pairing strength to reproduce the experimental binding energy in the deformation-unstable isotopes. Result: Within our choice of Skyrme parameters and use of density-independent pairing, this moderate adjustment sufficiently stabilizes the HFBCS ground states against deformation, ensuring a successful QRPA calculation and, more importantly, leading to more realistic properties for the quadrupole $2^+$ states. Conclusion: Careful attention to the sensitive interplay of pairing and shell effects in deformation-soft nuclides can be crucial to their correct descriptions. This sensitivity can be exploited to optimize the treatment of pairing in phenomenological approaches such as the present Skyrme-QRPA.

## Key claim

By moderately enhancing the pairing strength to reproduce experimental binding energies, spherical HFBCS-QRPA calculations can be stabilized against quadrupole deformation in neutron-deficient Sn isotopes, yielding realistic properties for the quadrupole \(2^+\) states, but this result is conditional on the specific choice of Skyrme parameters and the use of density-independent pairing.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Skyrme HFBCS-QRPA calculations using the SLy5, SkM*, and SkP functionals with default density-independent contact pairing, first identifying Sn isotopes that are unstable against quadrupole deformation. They then increased the pairing strength parameter for the SLy5 functional to reproduce the experimental binding energy per nucleon of \(^{112}\)Sn, and recalculated the \(2^+_1\) energies and \(B(E2)\) transition strengths across the isotopic chain. Additionally, fully self-consistent spherical HFB-QRPA calculations with the Gogny D1S interaction were performed for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Default pairing strengths \(V_0^i\) (in MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\)): 265.5 for SLy5, 222.9 for SkM*, and 213.7 for SkP, fitted to the experimental neutron pairing gap in \(^{120}\)Sn (\(\Delta_n = 1.245\) MeV).
- For \(^{112}\)Sn with SLy5, increasing the pairing strength from 265.5 to 334.5 MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) changed the calculated binding energy per particle from \(-8.484\) MeV to \(-8.513\) MeV; the SkP functional yields \(-8.502\) MeV.
- The \(n0g_{7/2}\) and \(n1d_{5/2}\) levels are predicted by all three functionals to be separated by roughly 2 MeV.
- With default pairing, SLy5 predicts \(^{106-116}\)Sn (\(N = 56-66\)) as soft or unstable; SkM* predicts \(^{110-122}\)Sn as soft or unstable; SkP gives no deformed Sn isotopes.
- Gogny D1S HFB-QRPA calculations found instabilities between and including \(^{104}\)Sn and \(^{120}\)Sn.

## Context

This work extends the diagnosis of quadrupole softness in even-even nuclei from the authors' previous study (Anh et al., 2024) and addresses a longstanding challenge in understanding \(B(E2)\) values for Sn isotopes around \(^{110}\)Sn. The results contrast with shell-model calculations (Siciliano et al., 2020; Togashi et al., 2018) that suggested strong quadrupole components, and with constrained HFB calculations (Omura et al., 2023) that predicted near-degenerate \(n0g_{7/2}\) and \(n1d_{5/2}\) levels. The finding that moderate pairing adjustment stabilizes spherical solutions enables future optimization of pairing treatments in Skyrme-QRPA approaches and suggests that similar quadrupole softness issues may appear in Pb isotopes beyond \(N=126\).
