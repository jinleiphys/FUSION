# Structure of Lambda(1405) and construction of KbarN local potential based on chiral SU(3) dynamics

> We develop the single-channel local potential for the KbarN system, which is applicable to quantitative studies of Kbar bound states in nuclei. Because the high precision measurement of the kaonic hydrogen by SIDDHARTA reduces the uncertainty of the KbarN amplitude below the KbarN threshold, the local potential should be calibrated in a wide energy region. We establish a new method to construct the local potential focusing on the behavior of the scattering amplitude in the complex energy plane. Applying this method, we construct the KbarN potential based on the chiral coupled-channel approach with the SIDDHARTA constraint. The wave function from the new potential indicates the KbarN molecular structure of Lambda(1405).

## Key claim

The paper develops a method to construct a single-channel local potential for the KbarN system that reproduces the original chiral coupled-channel scattering amplitude not only on the real energy axis but also in the complex energy plane, enabling the correct reproduction of both poles of the Lambda(1405) resonance.

## Method

The authors extract an effective single-channel KbarN interaction from the chiral coupled-channel framework by eliminating the lower-energy piSigma channel, then construct an energy-dependent local potential in coordinate space using a Gaussian spatial distribution. The potential strength is parameterized as a polynomial in energy, and a complex correction term Delta V is introduced to improve reproduction of the original amplitude. The potential is calibrated by matching the scattering amplitude from the Schrodinger equation to the original chiral unitary amplitude, with the range parameter b determined at the KbarN threshold.

## Key numbers

- The previous potential (Hyodo 2007) gave only one pole at 1421-35i MeV, while the original chiral amplitude had two poles at 1428-17i MeV and 1400-76i MeV for the HNJH model
- Potential II reproduces both original poles at 1428-17i MeV and 1400-77i MeV, with an average deviation on the real axis Delta F_real = 4.0 x 10^-4
- The percentage of the precise region (Delta F < 0.2) in the complex energy plane (1332-1450 MeV real, -100 to 50 MeV imaginary) improved from 50% for the previous potential to 85% for Potential II
- The optimized upper boundary of the parameterized range was 1521 MeV, with the potential range parameter b = 0.46 fm

## Context

This work extends the single-channel local potential approach of Hyodo and Weise (2007) by incorporating the SIDDHARTA constraint from kaonic hydrogen measurements (Bazzi 2011, 2012), which reduced the uncertainty of the subthreshold KbarN amplitude. The method enables quantitative studies of Kbar bound states in nuclei (Kbar-nuclei) and provides a reliable potential for few-body calculations of systems like KbarNN, where previous calculations by Shevchenko (2006, 2007), Yamazaki (2007), Ikeda (2007, 2010), Dote (2008, 2014), and Barnea (2012) gave qualitatively consistent but quantitatively different results.
