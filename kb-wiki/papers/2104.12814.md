# A Beyond Mean Field Approach to Yang-Mills Thermodynamics

> We propose a beyond mean field approach to evaluate Yang-Mills thermodynamics from the partition function with n-body gluon contribution, in the presence of a uniform background Polyakov field. Using a path integral based formalism, we obtain, unlike the previous mean field studies within this model framework, physically consistent results with good agreement to the lattice data throughout the temperature range.

## Key claim
The authors propose a beyond-mean-field approach to Yang-Mills thermodynamics that, unlike previous mean-field studies within the same model framework (Sasaki 2012, Islam 2012), yields physically consistent results throughout the full temperature range, including below the transition temperature where mean-field treatments produced unphysical behavior.

## Method
The authors start from a partition function for thermal gluons in a uniform background Polyakov field, expressed as an integral over the SU(3) group parameters θ₃ and θ₈. Instead of using the saddle-point approximation to obtain mean-field values of the Polyakov loop, they evaluate the full configuration-space integral by discretizing space into N points and introducing a volume parameter δv, then compute thermodynamic observables directly from the logarithm of the partition function. An effective gluon mass with temperature-dependent parametrization is introduced to achieve quantitative agreement with lattice data.

## Key numbers
- The volume parameter is chosen as δv = (0.5 T_d)⁻³, where T_d is the deconfinement temperature.
- The effective gluon mass parametrization uses fitted parameters: α = 0.564, β = 0.176657, γ = 1.08526 for T/T_d > 1, and ζ = 2.70066 for T/T_d < 1.
- Pressure scaled by T⁴ shows agreement with lattice data from Giusti (2016) across the full temperature range.
- The thermal behavior of ⟨Φ⟩ shows a discontinuity at the transition point, signaling a first-order phase transition.

## Context
This work extends the Polyakov-loop effective model approach previously used by Sasaki (2012) and Islam (2012), which suffered from unphysical thermodynamic behavior below the transition temperature due to the mean-field approximation. The beyond-mean-field method enables a complete model description of the SU(3) pure gauge system that reproduces both the symmetry properties and thermodynamic observables from lattice simulations (Giusti 2016 for pressure and energy; Boyd 1996 for specific heat), providing a framework that can be extended to include dynamical quarks or applied to other gauge groups.
