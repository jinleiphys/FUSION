# Higher-Order Nuclear-Polarizability Corrections in Atomic Hydrogen

> Nuclear-polarizability corrections that go beyond unretarded-dipole approximation are calculated analytically for hydrogenic (atomic) S-states. These retardation corrections are evaluated numerically for deuterium and contribute -0.68 kHz, for a total polarization correction of 18.58(7) kHz. Our results are in agreement with one previous numerical calculation, and the retardation corrections completely account for the difference between two previous calculations. The uncertainty in the deuterium polarizability correction is substantially reduced. At the level of 0.01 kHz for deuterium, only three primary nuclear observables contribute: the electric polarizability, $α_E$, the paramagnetic susceptibility, $β_M$, and the third Zemach moment, $< r^3 >_{(2)}$. Cartesian multipole decomposition of the virtual Compton amplitude and its concomitant gauge sum rules are used in the analysis.

## Key claim

The paper calculates nuclear-polarizability corrections beyond the unretarded-dipole approximation for hydrogenic S-states, finding that retardation corrections contribute -0.68 kHz to the deuterium 1S-2S transition, yielding a total polarization correction of 18.58(7) kHz; the authors claim this result substantially reduces the uncertainty in the deuterium polarizability correction and completely accounts for the difference between two previous calculations.

## Method

The authors performed an analytic expansion of the virtual Compton amplitude using Cartesian multipole decomposition and gauge sum rules, reducing the nuclear-polarizability correction to a series depending on three primary nuclear observables: the electric polarizability α_E, the paramagnetic susceptibility β_M, and the third Zemach moment ⟨r³⟩_(2). They evaluated these quantities numerically for deuterium using multiple second-generation nucleon-nucleon potentials (Argonne V₁₈, Nijmegen variants, Reid Soft Core 93) and first-generation potentials for comparison, calculating the magnetic susceptibility both with and without meson-exchange currents (MEC). The retardation correction proportional to the third Zemach moment was computed by folding charge densities and performing spline fits to obtain the convoluted moments.

## Key numbers

- Total deuteron polarizability correction for the 1S-2S transition: 18.58(7) kHz
- Retardation correction contribution: -0.68 kHz (difference from leading-order α_E result of 19.26 kHz)
- Electric polarizability α_E = 0.6328(17) fm³ with log(2Ē/m_e) = 2.9620(5), contributing 19.26(6) kHz
- Paramagnetic susceptibility β_M = 0.0777(3) fm³ (with MEC, second-generation average) with log(2Ē/m_e) = 2.498(2), contributing -0.307(2)(6) kHz
- Δα_E correction: 0.106 kHz (with m_e² Δα_E = 0.0098 fm³)
- Retardation correction from third Zemach moment: Δ⟨r³⟩_(2) = -37.32(12) fm³, contributing -0.483(2) kHz
- Impulse-approximation β_M for Argonne V₁₈: 0.0678 fm³; full β_M (with MEC) for Argonne V₁₈: 0.0774 fm³
- ΔB (seagull and higher-order terms) estimated as less than 0.01 kHz

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of nuclear-polarizability corrections for deuterium by Schmidt et al. (various years) and by Friar and Payne (1995), specifically addressing the discrepancy between the unretarded-dipole results of Ref. [8] and the more comprehensive numerical calculation of Ref. [7] that included retardation, higher multipoles, nucleon finite size, seagulls, and meson-exchange currents. The authors show that their analytic series reproduces the full numerical results of Ref. [7] within 0.02 kHz for both Coulomb and transverse parts, and they note that substantially improving the uncertainty in Eq. (32) would require improvements in the nuclear parameter A_s. The result enables more precise extraction of nuclear charge radii from hydrogen isotope shift measurements, where the 1S-2S d-p isotope shift has a reported uncertainty of 2 kHz.
