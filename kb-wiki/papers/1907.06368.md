# Double charge-exchange phonon states

> We study double charge-exchange phonon states in neutron-rich nuclei, in particular the double isobaric analog states and the double Gamow-Teller excitations, induced by the double isospin operator $\sum_{i,j=1}^At_-(i) t_-(j)$ and spin-isospin operator $\sum_{i,j=1}^Aσ(i) t_-(i)σ(j) t_-(j)$, respectively. We employ quartic commutator relations to evaluate the average energies $E_{\rm DIAS} - 2E_{\rm IAS}$ and $E_{\rm DGTR}-E_{\rm DIAS} - 2(E_{\rm GTR}-E_{\rm IAS})$, and conventional double commutator relations to evaluate the average energies of $E_{\rm GTR}-E_{\rm IAS}$ and $E_{\rm IAS}$. We have found that the corrections due to quartic commutators follow the approximate laws: $E_{\rm DIAS} - 2E_{\rm IAS}\approx \frac{3}{2} A^{-1/3}$ MeV and $E_{\rm DGTR}-E_{\rm DIAS} - 2(E_{\rm GTR}-E_{\rm IAS})\approx 16 A^{-1}$ MeV. While the former is dominated by direct Coulomb effects, since Coulomb exchange cancels out to some extent with isospin symmetry breaking contributions originated form the nuclear strong force, the latter is sensitive to the difference in strength between the spin and spin-isospin chanels of the strong interaction.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic formulas for the average energies of double isobaric analog states (DIAS) and double Gamow-Teller resonances (DGTR) using commutator relations, finding that the corrections to the simple sum of single-excitation energies follow approximate mass-dependent laws: \(E_{\rm DIAS} - 2E_{\rm IAS} \approx \frac{3}{2} A^{-1/3}\) MeV and \(E_{\rm DGTR}-E_{\rm DIAS} - 2(E_{\rm GTR}-E_{\rm IAS}) \approx 16 A^{-1}\) MeV, with the former dominated by direct Coulomb effects and the latter sensitive to the difference between spin and spin-isospin strong interaction channels.

## Method

The authors employed quartic commutator relations to evaluate the average energy differences \(E_{\rm DIAS} - 2E_{\rm IAS}\) and \(E_{\rm DGTR}-E_{\rm DIAS} - 2(E_{\rm GTR}-E_{\rm IAS})\), and conventional double commutator relations for \(E_{\rm GTR}-E_{\rm IAS}\) and \(E_{\rm IAS}\). For the DIAS correction, they used an independent particle model with sharp-sphere neutron and proton distributions and a zero-range charge-independence-breaking (CIB) interaction (SAMi-ISB) fitted to Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations with the AV18 potential. For the DGTR correction, they adopted a separable Hamiltonian including spin-orbit, isospin, spin, and spin-isospin residual interactions, with coupling strengths adjusted to reproduce experimental single GTR energies in \({}^{48}\)Ca, \({}^{90}\)Zr, \({}^{112-124}\)Sn, and \({}^{208}\)Pb.

## Key numbers

* \(E_{\rm DIAS} - 2E_{\rm IAS} \approx \frac{3}{2} A^{-1/3}\) MeV from the liquid-drop-model estimate of the Coulomb direct term.
* \(E_{\rm DGTR}-E_{\rm DIAS} - 2(E_{\rm GTR}-E_{\rm IAS}) \approx 16 A^{-1}\) MeV from the quartic commutator analysis.
* For \({}^{48}\)Ca: \(\Delta E_{\rm Cd} = 0.385\) MeV (Coulomb direct, Eq. 12), \(\Delta E_{\rm Cex} = -0.056\) MeV (Coulomb exchange, exact), \(\Delta E_{\rm CIB} = 0.097\) MeV (CIB strong force), giving total correction \(E_{\rm DIAS} - 2E_{\rm IAS} \approx 0.426\) MeV; experimental \(E_{\rm IAS} = 7.182(8)\) MeV.
* For \({}^{208}\)Pb: \(\Delta E_{\rm Cd} = 0.235\) MeV, \(\Delta E_{\rm Cex} = -0.008\) MeV, \(\Delta E_{\rm CIB} = 0.026\) MeV; experimental \(E_{\rm IAS} = 18.826(10)\) MeV.
* Spin-orbit strength parameter \(V_{ls} = 34\) MeV and \(\kappa_{\sigma\tau} - \kappa_{\tau} = -4\) MeV reproduce experimental \(E_{\rm GT} - E_{\rm IAS}\) values.
* For \({}^{48}\)Ca: predicted \(E_{\rm DGTR} - E_{\rm DIAS} = 8.0\) MeV, with \(E_{\rm DGTR} - E_{\rm DIAS} - 2(E_{\rm GT} - E_{\rm IAS}) = 0.38\) MeV.
* For \({}^{208}\)Pb: predicted \(E_{\rm DGTR} - E_{\rm DIAS} = 0.92\) MeV, with the quartic correction = 0.08 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the sum-rule approach of Vogel (1986), Zamick (1986), and Muto (1991) for double Gamow-Teller strengths, and the IAS studies of Auerbach (1972) and Roca-Maza (2018), by providing analytic commutator-based formulas for average excitation energies of double charge-exchange phonon states. The results enable future experimental programs at RIKEN RIBF using the (\({}^{12}\)C, \({}^{12}\)Be(0\(^+_2\))) reaction at 250 MeV/nucleon to probe spin-isospin response in neutron-rich nuclei, and establish that DCX measurements of \(E_{\rm DGTR} - E_{\rm DIAS}\) with better than a few percent accuracy could provide a new probe of spin and spin-isospin properties in nuclei.
