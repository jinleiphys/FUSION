# Nuclear Responses to Two-Body External Fields Studied with the Second Random-Phase-Approximation

> This study investigates nuclear responses to two-body external fields, interpreted as double-phonon excitations, within the subtracted second random-phase approximation (SSRPA) for 16O. To clarify the underlying characteristics of these modes, Hartree-Fock (HF) and SSRPA with the diagonal approximation are first examined. The resulting strength distributions are nearly identical, indicating that residual interactions in the 1p-1h sector contribute only weakly. This behavior contrasts with that of one-body excitations, where coupling between 1p-1h and 2p-2h configurations is essential for generating collectivity. In the full SSRPA calculation, which incorporates the residual interaction among 2p-2h configurations, the strength distributions are substantially modified. The double IS 0+ and 2+ modes show pronounced redistribution, with peaks shifted to lower energies and additional strength emerging at higher energies, whereas the double IV $1^{-}$ mode shifts predominantly to higher energies due to a largely repulsive interaction. Analysis of single transition amplitudes reveals that low-lying resonances are formed coherently through constructive neutron-neutron, proton-proton, and neutron-proton configurations, while high-lying resonances are dominated by neutron-proton configurations, reflecting their higher state density. These results demonstrate that double-phonon excitations cannot be described by simple folding of one-body responses; a fully microscopic treatment of 2p-2h mixing, as provided by SSRPA, is essential.

# Nuclear Responses to Two-Body External Fields Studied with the Second Random-Phase-Approximation

## Key claim

Double-phonon excitations in 16O cannot be described by simple folding of one-body responses; a fully microscopic treatment of 2p-2h mixing via the subtracted second random-phase approximation (SSRPA) is essential, but the residual interactions in the 1p-1h sector contribute only weakly to two-body external field responses, contrasting with one-body excitations where 1p-1h to 2p-2h coupling is essential for collectivity.

## Method

The study applies the subtracted second random-phase approximation (SSRPA) to 16O using the Skyrme SGII force under spherical symmetry, with single-particle energies and wave functions from Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations and continuum states discretized in a 20 fm box with 0.1 fm steps. Three calculations are compared: HF (no residual interactions), SSRPA with diagonal approximation (SSRPA_D, neglecting coupling among 2p-2h configurations), and full SSRPA (SSRPA_F, including all residual interactions among 2p-2h configurations). The model space includes single-particle levels up to 100 MeV, with 2p-2h configurations included up to 100, 80, and 60 MeV for 0+, 1-, and 2+ states respectively.

## Key numbers

- Center energy of IS 0+ one-body mode: 23.3 MeV (RPA) vs 19.9 MeV (SSRPA_F), a shift of about 3.4 MeV
- Center energy of IV 1- one-body mode: 20.0 MeV (RPA) vs 17.2 MeV (SSRPA_F), a shift of about 2.8 MeV
- Center energy of IS 2+ one-body mode: 20.6 MeV (RPA) vs 19.0 MeV (SSRPA_F), a shift of about 1.6 MeV
- Width of IS 2+ one-body mode: 1.1 MeV (RPA) vs 4.8 MeV (SSRPA_F)
- HF center energies for two-body modes: double IS 0+ at 52.8 MeV, double IV 1- at 27.8 MeV, double IS 2+ at 53.5 MeV
- SSRPA_F first-group center energies: double IS 0+ at 25.3 MeV (width 3.7 MeV), double IS 2+ at 33.7 MeV (width 2.9 MeV)
- SSRPA_F second-group center energies: double IS 0+ at 57.7 MeV (width 44.1 MeV), double IS 2+ at 55.5 MeV (width 30.3 MeV)
- SSRPA_F double IV 1- center energy: 42.5 MeV (width 12.8 MeV)
- SSRPA_F centroid energies: double IS 0+ at 45.4 MeV, double IV 1- at 44.8 MeV, double IS 2+ at 50.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the SSRPA framework, previously successful for describing spreading widths of giant resonances (Gambacurta 2015, Yang 2021) and quenching of Gamow-Teller transitions (Gambacurta 2018, 2020, 2022; Yang 2022, 2023), to nuclear responses induced by two-body external fields interpreted as double-phonon excitations. The results contrast with multiphonon models (Catara 1992, Severyukhin 2004, Litvinova 2013, Gambacurta 2016, Arsenyev 2023) and semiclassical formalisms (Bertulani 1994) by demonstrating that coupling among 2p-2h configurations produces substantial redistribution not captured by folding one-body responses. The work enables future microscopic incorporation of meson-exchange two-body currents in muon capture and other weak and electromagnetic processes.
