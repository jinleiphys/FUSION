# $ψ(2S)$ photoproduction in high energy nuclear collisions

> We study coherent photoproduction of $ψ'$ and the subsequent suppression in high energy nuclear collisions. The hot medium effect on charmonium photoproduction is controlled by the competition between the fireball size and nuclear size. When the former is smaller than the later, the photoproduction is only slightly affected by the hot medium. We calculate the yield ratio of $ψ'$ to $J/ψ$ in semi-central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy, while the high $p_T$ limit is characterized by the fireball, its value in low $p_T$ limit can still reach the significant enhancement by photoproduction.

## Key claim

The yield ratio of ψ' to J/ψ at extremely low transverse momentum in semi-central Pb-Pb collisions can reach the coherent photoproduction limit (0.34) when the fireball size is smaller than the nuclear size (impact parameter b ≈ 12 fm), but is suppressed to about 0.1 when the fireball is larger (b = 8.4 fm), because the hot medium effect on photoproduced charmonia depends on the competition between fireball size and nuclear size.

## Method

The authors solve a Boltzmann transport equation for charmonium hadroproduction in the quark-gluon plasma, with suppression via gluon dissociation and regeneration via the inverse process using detailed balance. For photoproduction, they treat the electromagnetic fields of one nucleus as quasi-real photons (Weizsäcker-Williams method) that scatter coherently off the other nucleus via two-gluon exchange (photon-Pomeron fusion). The QGP evolution is described by ideal hydrodynamics with initial maximum temperatures of 485 MeV (√sNN = 2.76 TeV) and 510 MeV (√sNN = 5.02 TeV) fitted from final charge multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- Initial QGP maximum temperature T0 = 485 MeV for √sNN = 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions at b = 0
- Initial QGP maximum temperature T0 = 510 MeV for √sNN = 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions at b = 0
- QGP lifetime ≈ 10 fm/c at b = 0 and ≈ 6 fm/c at b = 8.4 fm for both collision energies
- QGP formation time τ0 ≈ 0.6 fm/c; ψ' formation time ≈ 1 fm/c; J/ψ formation time ≈ 0.5 fm/c
- Yield ratio NAAψ'/NAAJ/ψ ≈ 0.05 at high pT (6.5 < pT < 30 GeV/c) for both b = 8.4 fm and b = 12 fm events at √sNN = 5.02 TeV
- Yield ratio NAAψ'/NAAJ/ψ ≈ 0.1 at low pT for b = 8.4 fm events at √sNN = 5.02 TeV
- Yield ratio NAAψ'/NAAJ/ψ = 0.34 at low pT for b = 12 fm events at √sNN = 5.02 TeV (matching the ultra-peripheral collision limit from Adam (2015))
- Ratio of ψ' to J/ψ in pp collisions = 0.166 (from Acharya (2017))
- J/ψ dissociation temperature TJ/ψ ≈ 1.5 Tc; ψ' dissociation temperature Tψ' ≈ Tc

## Context

This work extends the study of coherent photoproduction in heavy ion collisions from ultra-peripheral collisions (where hadroproduction is absent) to semi-central collisions, following the observation by Adam (2015) of a J/ψ excess at very low pT in semi-central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC. The authors build on their previous transport model for charmonium evolution (Zhou (2014), Chen (2015)) and the photoproduction framework of Shi (2017). The calculation enables experimental discrimination between two competing scenarios for photoproduction in semi-central collisions: the "nucleus-nucleus" scenario (photoproduction before hadronic collisions, using the entire nuclear charge) versus the "spectator-spectator" scenario (photoproduction after hadronic collisions, using only spectator charges).
