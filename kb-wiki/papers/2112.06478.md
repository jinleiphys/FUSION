# Weinberg Operator Contribution to the Hadronic CP Violation

> The CP violating Weinberg operator (or the gluon chromo-electric dipole moment) is generated in many candidates of new physics beyond the standard model, and it contributes to observables such as the electric dipole moments (EDM) of the neutron, nuclei and atoms. In this proceedings contribution, we review the present understanding of the Weinberg operator as well as the result of the estimation of its effect to the EDM of $^3$He nucleus through the contact CP-odd nuclear force.

## Key claim

The Weinberg operator contributes to the EDM of the $^3$He nucleus through a contact CP-odd nuclear force, with a magnitude comparable to that from the intrinsic nucleon EDM, but the one-pion exchange CP-odd nuclear force is suppressed due to chiral symmetry conservation.

## Method

The author derives the hadron-level effective field theory of the Weinberg operator, calculating the nucleon EDM from both chiral rotation of the nucleon anomalous magnetic moment and irreducible (contact) diagrams using QCD sum rules and the nonrelativistic quark model with the Gaussian expansion method. The CP-odd contact nuclear force is then matched to a vector meson exchange potential, and the resulting $^3$He EDM is computed using both chiral perturbation theory and the vector meson exchange model.

## Key numbers

- Neutron EDM from chiral rotation: $d_n^{(a)}(w) = w \times (25 \pm 13) \, e \, {\rm MeV}$
- Proton EDM from chiral rotation: $d_p^{(a)}(w) = w \times (-23 \pm 12) \, e \, {\rm MeV}$
- Combined nucleon EDM: $d_n(w) = w \times (20 \pm 12) \, e \, {\rm MeV}$; $d_p(w) = w \times (-18 \pm 11) \, e \, {\rm MeV}$
- $^3$He EDM from contact CP-odd nuclear force via chiral perturbation theory: $d_{\rm He} = (25 \pm 12) \, w \, e \, {\rm MeV}$
- $^3$He EDM from contact CP-odd nuclear force via vector meson exchange model: $d_{\rm He} = [2-7] \, w \, e \, {\rm MeV}$
- $^3$He EDM from intrinsic nucleon EDM: $d_{\rm He} = (19 \pm 11) \, w \, e \, {\rm MeV}$

## Context

This proceedings contribution reviews and extends prior work on the Weinberg operator's contribution to hadronic CP violation, building on the QCD sum rule calculations of Demir (2002) and Haisch (2019) for the nucleon EDM, and on nuclear EDM calculations by Song (2012), Yamanaka (2015-2020), and Froese (2021). The key finding is that the contact CP-odd nuclear force contribution to the $^3$He EDM is comparable to the intrinsic nucleon EDM contribution, enabling future experimental searches for the Weinberg operator through nuclear EDMs. The author notes that a recent calculation by Osamura (2022) found that the long-range pion-exchange nuclear force induced by the Weinberg operator is not negligible for atomic EDMs, which was not included in this analysis.
