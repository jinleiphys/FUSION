# Intermediate mass dileptons as pre-equilibrium probes in heavy ion collisions

> The production of dileptons with an invariant mass in the range 1 GeV < M < 5 GeV provides unique insight into the approach to thermal equilibrium in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions. In this mass range, they are produced through the annihilation of quark-antiquark pairs in the early stages of the collision. They are sensitive to the anisotropy of the quark momentum distribution, and also to the quark abundance, which is expected to be underpopulated relative to thermal equilibrium. We take into account both effects based on recent theoretical developments in QCD kinetic theory, and study how the dilepton mass spectrum depends on the shear viscosity to entropy ratio that controls the equilibration time. We evaluate the background from the Drell-Yan process and argue that future detector developments can suppress the additional background from semileptonic decays of heavy flavors.

## Key claim

Intermediate mass dileptons (1 GeV < M < 5 GeV) produced in the early stages of ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions can serve as a probe of the pre-equilibrium dynamics and thermalization process, provided that both the anisotropy of the quark momentum distribution and the underpopulation of quark abundances relative to thermal equilibrium are taken into account using QCD kinetic theory.

## Method

The authors model dilepton production through quark-antiquark annihilation using a parametrization of the out-of-equilibrium phase-space distribution that accounts for momentum anisotropy and quark suppression. They employ a boost-invariant longitudinal expansion model with uniform transverse density, matching the energy density and longitudinal pressure to attractor solutions from QCD kinetic theory. The dilepton yield per unit invariant mass and rapidity is obtained by integrating the production rate over proper time, with the shear viscosity to entropy ratio η/s as the only free parameter controlling the equilibration time.

## Key numbers

* For central Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV with η/s = 0.16, the hydrodynamic time τ_hydro (defined by w̃ = 1) is approximately 1 fm/c.
* The constant τT³ = 8.24 fm⁻² in the hydrodynamic regime, derived from dN_ch/dη ≈ 1900 for 0-5% centrality and transverse area A = 110 fm².
* Transverse areas A for different centrality intervals in Pb+Pb collisions: 104 fm² (0-5%), 96 fm² (0-10%), 71 fm² (10-20%), 54 fm² (20-30%), 41 fm² (30-40%).
* Quark suppression decreases the dilepton yield by a large factor for M ≳ 3 GeV.
* The simplified uniform-density calculation underestimates the yield by a factor 1.5-2 for M = 5 GeV compared to a calculation with fluctuating initial profiles.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for pre-equilibrium dilepton production developed by Martinez and Strickland (2008) and the QCD kinetic theory attractor approach of Kurkela and collaborators (2018-2020). It contrasts with the three-dimensional hydrodynamic calculation of Kasmaei and Strickland (2018), which neglects quark suppression, and with the work of Churchill et al. (2020), which finds orders-of-magnitude smaller dilepton rates due to different modeling of the high-momentum tail of the initial gluon distribution. The results enable future experimental measurements at LHCb to constrain the shear viscosity to entropy ratio and the chemical equilibration time in the early stages of heavy ion collisions.
