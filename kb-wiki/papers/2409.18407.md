# The glue that binds us all -- Latin America and the Electron-Ion Collider

> The Electron-Ion Collider, a next generation electron-hadron and electron-nuclei scattering facility, will be built at Brookhaven National Laboratory. The wealth of new data will shape research in hadron physics, from nonperturbative QCD techniques to perturbative QCD improvements and global QCD analyses, for the decades to come. With the present proposal, Latin America based physicists, whose expertise lies on the theory and phenomenology side, make the case for the past and future efforts of a growing community, working hand-in-hand towards developing theoretical tools and predictions to analyze, interpret and optimize the results that will be obtained at the EIC, unveiling the role of the glue that binds us all. This effort is along the lines of various initiatives taken in the U.S., and supported by colleagues worldwide, such as the ones by the EIC User Group which were highlighted during the Snowmass Process and the Particle Physics Project Prioritization Panel (P5).

## Key claim

This white paper argues that Latin American theorists and phenomenologists have developed substantial expertise in nonperturbative QCD, perturbative QCD, and global QCD analyses that will be essential for interpreting data from the future Electron-Ion Collider, though the paper presents a community statement rather than a specific testable hypothesis.

## Method

The paper surveys the research activities of Latin American physicists working on EIC-related theory and phenomenology, organized by country (Brazil, Colombia, Mexico, Argentina) and by research area including Dyson-Schwinger equations, Bethe-Salpeter equations, light-front wave functions, parton distribution functions, transverse momentum distributions, generalized parton distributions, gluon saturation in the color glass condensate formalism, and higher-order QCD corrections. The authors also document community-building efforts through workshops and conferences held in Latin America between 2019 and 2024.

## Key numbers

* At the pion scale, 30% of the pion state is distributed beyond the valence component, as demonstrated by light-front projection of Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes (de Paula and Frederico, ITA).
* Predictions for pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors extend up to Q^2 of 40 GeV^2, corresponding to the projected EIC and JLab Q^2 range (Higuera-Angulo et al., 2024).
* Nuclear mass number A enhances gluon densities as approximately A^(1/3) in electron-nucleus collisions, providing access to saturation effects.

## Context

This white paper extends the community organization efforts documented in the EIC Yellow Report (AbdulKhalek et al., 2021), which was signed by over 400 experts including theorists and experimentalists from Argentina, Brazil, Chile, and Mexico. The paper documents Latin American contributions to the CTEQ-Tung global analysis collaboration (Ablat et al., 2024) and to the EIC User Group, and it enables future coordinated Latin American participation in EIC physics analysis, detector design, and phenomenology. The work builds on prior Dyson-Schwinger equation studies by Bashir (2012) and Roberts (1994), and on saturation physics developed by Gribov (1983) and Iancu (2003).
