# Reconciling Coulomb breakup and neutron radiative capture

> The Coulomb-breakup method to extract the cross section for neutron radiative capture at astrophysical energies is analyzed in detail. In particular, its sensitivity to the description of the neutron-core continuum is ascertained. We consider the case of 14C(n, $γ$)15C for which both the radiative capture at low energy and the Coulomb breakup of 15C into 14C+n on Pb at 68 MeV/nucleon have been measured with accuracy. We confirm the direct proportionality of the cross section for both reactions to the square of the asymptotic normalization constant of 15C observed by Summers and Nunes [Phys. Rev. C 78, 011601 (2008)], but we also show that the 14C-n continuum plays a significant role in the calculations. Fortunately, the method proposed by Summers and Nunes can be improved to absorb that continuum dependence. We show that a more precise radiative-capture cross section can be extracted selecting the breakup data at forward angles and low 14C-n relative energies.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that both Coulomb breakup and neutron radiative capture scale with the square of the asymptotic normalization constant (ANC) of the bound state, but shows that the description of the neutron-core continuum also significantly affects both reactions; the method proposed by Summers and Nunes (2008) can be improved by selecting breakup data at forward angles and low relative energies to absorb that continuum dependence.

## Method

The authors developed twelve different descriptions of 15C by combining three Woods-Saxon potentials for the 14C-n s-wave bound state (varying diffuseness to produce different ANCs) with four different p-wave continuum potentials (varying depth and diffuseness to produce different phase shifts). They used the Dynamical Eikonal Approximation (DEA) to compute the Coulomb breakup of 15C on Pb at 68 MeV/nucleon and the radiative capture 14C(n,γ)15C for each description, then scaled the breakup calculations to fit the experimental data of Nakamura (2009) and compared the resulting scaled radiative-capture cross sections to the direct measurements of Reifart et al. (2008).

## Key numbers

* One-neutron separation energy of 15C: Sn = 1.218 MeV, used to adjust the depth of the s-wave Woods-Saxon potential.
* Three s-wave diffuseness values used: as = 0.6 fm (ANC = 1.38 fm^{-1/2}), as = 1.5 fm (ANC = 2.29 fm^{-1/2}), and as = 0.3 fm (ANC = 1.17 fm^{-1/2}).
* The d-wave potential was adjusted to reproduce the 5/2+ excited state binding energy: E0d = -478 keV.
* At the lowest energy (E = 23.3 keV), the estimate of the radiative-capture cross section obtained from the improved method (forward angles, low energy) is 4.74 ± 0.27 μb, in excellent agreement with the direct measurement of 4.76 ± 0.34 μb from Reifart et al. (2008).
* The experimental angular cuts used were Θ < 6° (all angles) and Θ < 2.1° (forward angles); the low-energy cut was E < 0.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the ANC method proposed by Summers and Nunes (2008) for extracting radiative-capture cross sections from Coulomb-breakup measurements, which was tested on the 15C system. It also builds on earlier findings by Capel and Nunes (2006) that continuum effects can cause up to 40% variations in breakup cross sections, and on the work of Typel and Baur (2004, 2005) who showed the influence of the p-wave continuum through their dimensionless function S0^1(1). The improved method enables more reliable extraction of astrophysical radiative-capture cross sections from breakup experiments, particularly for reactions involving short-lived nuclei where direct measurements are difficult.
