# An explanatory study of $^{11}$Li $β$ decay into $^9$Li and deuteron

> The $β$-decay of $^{11}$Li into $^9$Li and {\it d} is studied using simple halo wave functions of $^{11}$Li. The sensitivity of the transition probability is elucidated on a description of the halo part and on a choice of the potential between $^9$Li and {\it d} .

# An explanatory study of $^{11}$Li $\beta$ decay into $^9$Li and deuteron

## Key claim
The $\beta$-decay of $^{11}$Li into $^9$Li and deuteron probes mainly the halo part of the $^{11}$Li wave function at distances R ≥ 4 fm, in contrast to the $^6$He case where cancellation occurs between internal and external parts. The study is qualitative only, because the understanding of $^{11}$Li structure is still imperfect and no $^9$Li+d scattering data exist to constrain the optical potential.

## Method
The authors calculated the Gamow-Teller transition probability for the direct $\beta$-delayed deuteron emission from $^{11}$Li to $^9$Li+d continuum, assuming s-wave relative motion between the fragments. They used three simple two-neutron reduced amplitudes for the $^{11}$Li halo, a shell model (SM) wave function, a di-neutron cluster model (CM) wave function, and a hybrid model (HM) wave function, and generated the $^9$Li-d relative motion function from a folding potential of Watanabe type with Woods-Saxon optical potentials.

## Key numbers
- The lower limit on the branching ratio for t+(d) from experiment is P$_{t+(d)}$ = (1.8 ± 0.3) × 10$^{-4}$, and a tentative value for the deuteron branch alone is P$_d$ = (1.0 ± 0.3) × 10$^{-4}$.
- The $^6$He $\beta$ decay into $\alpha$ and d has branching ratio P$_d$ = (7.6 ± 0.6) × 10$^{-6}$ for E$_d$ > 350 keV.
- The Q-values for the $^8$Li+t and $^9$Li+d decay channels are 4.96 MeV and 2.76 MeV, respectively.
- The three model wave functions yielded transition probabilities differing within a factor of ten at the peak values.
- A standard set of the optical potential yields a branching ratio of order 10$^{-4}$.

## Context
This work extends the approach used by Baye (1994) and others for the $^6$He $\beta$ decay into $\alpha$ and d, where a cancellation between internal and halo contributions was found. The authors note that the three-body $^9$Li+n+n configuration is important for $^{11}$Li structure, but the role of $^9$Li polarizability and the $^9$Li-n interaction remain unsettled. The hybrid model wave function used here was previously employed by Ogawa (1994) to analyze $^9$Li momentum distributions from $^{11}$Li reactions. The study enables future experimental measurements of the delayed deuteron spectrum to constrain the halo wave function and the $^9$Li-d interaction.
