# On spontaneous fission and alpha-decay half-lives of atomic nuclei

> It is shown, that the Gamow-like model with only one adjustable parameter - radius constant is able to reproduce well the alpha-decay half-lives for all even-even nuclei with the proton number larger than 50. The systematics for odd-A and odd-odd isotopes can be also well described when ones introduces an additional hindrance factor. Similar model based on the W. J. Swiatecki idea from 1955 is developed to reproduce the spontaneous fission half-lives of transactinide nuclei. The achieved accuracy of reproduction of the data is better than that obtained in more advanced theories.

## Key claim

The authors show that a Gamow-like model with only one adjustable parameter (the radius constant) can reproduce alpha-decay half-lives for all even-even nuclei with proton number greater than 50, and that a similar simple model based on Swiatecki's 1955 idea can reproduce spontaneous fission half-lives of transactinide nuclei with accuracy better than more advanced theories, though the spontaneous fission formula breaks down for nuclei whose masses have not yet been measured.

## Method

For alpha decay, the authors used a one-dimensional WKB tunneling model with a spherical square well potential, fitting the radius constant r0 to 127 even-even alpha emitters and introducing a hindrance factor h for odd-A and odd-odd nuclei. For spontaneous fission, they developed a semi-empirical formula following Swiatecki's 1955 approach, fitting the logarithms of spontaneous fission half-lives corrected by ground-state microscopic energy shifts (using experimental masses and the LSD macroscopic mass formula) as a function of proton number, with a fitted factor k = 7.7 MeV⁻¹ and hindrance factors for odd systems.

## Key numbers

- Radius constant for alpha decay: r0 = 1.23 fm, fitted to 127 even-even alpha emitters
- Root-mean-square deviation of log10(T1/2^α) for even-even nuclei: 0.39 (127 cases)
- Root-mean-square deviation of log10(T1/2^α) for odd-A (even-odd) nuclei: 0.66 (97 cases)
- Root-mean-square deviation of log10(T1/2^α) for odd-A (odd-even) nuclei: 0.54 (82 cases)
- Root-mean-square deviation of log10(T1/2^α) for odd-odd nuclei: 0.79 (54 cases)
- Hindrance factor h = 0.25 for odd-A alpha emitters, doubled to 0.5 for odd-odd emitters
- Factor k = 7.7 MeV⁻¹ for the spontaneous fission mass-shift correction
- Root-mean-square deviation of log10(T1/2^sf) for even-even actinides: 1.18 (35 isotopes)
- Root-mean-square deviation of log10(T1/2^sf) including odd-A and odd-odd nuclei: 1.57
- Root-mean-square deviation of log10(T1/2^sf) including super-heavy isotopes: 1.96
- Hindrance factor h = 2.5 for odd-A spontaneous fission, doubled to 5 for odd-odd
- Fitted formula for spontaneous fission half-lives in years: log10(T1/2^sf/y) = -4.1·min(Z,103) + 380.2 - 7.7·δM(Z,N) plus hindrance factors
- Root-mean-square deviation of fission barrier height estimates using the topographical theorem: 0.31 MeV (18 nuclei)

## Context

This work extends the simple Gamow-like model previously applied to cluster radioactivity by Zdeb, Warda, and Pomorski (2013) to a larger set of alpha emitters, and revives Swiatecki's 1955 phenomenological formula for spontaneous fission half-lives using the modern LSD (Lublin-Strasbourg Drop) macroscopic model from Pomorski and Dudek (2003). The authors show that their simple one-parameter (for even-even) spontaneous fission formula reproduces data better than the macroscopic-microscopic calculations with cranking inertia by Baran (1981) and more recent HFB calculations with Skyrme or Gogny forces. The work enables quick estimates of spontaneous fission half-lives for unknown super-heavy nuclei, provided their masses are known, and demonstrates that the success of Swiatecki's approach follows from the topographical theorem relating fission barrier heights to the difference between macroscopic and experimental masses.
