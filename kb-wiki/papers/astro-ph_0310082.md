# Primordial nuggets survival and QCD pairing

> We revisit the problem of boiling and surface evaporation of quark nuggets in the cosmological quark-hadron transition with the explicit consideration of pairing between quarks in a color-flavor locked (CFL) state. Assuming that primordial quark nuggets are actually formed, we analyze the consequences of pairing on the rates of boiling and surface evaporation in order to determine whether they could have survived with substantial mass. We find a substantial quenching of the evaporation + boiling processes, which suggests the survival of primordial nuggets for the currently considered range of the pairing gap $Δ$. Boiling is shown to depend on the competition of an increased stability window and the suppression of the rate, and is not likely to dominate the destruction of the nuggets. If surface evaporation dominates, the fate of the nuggets depend on the features of the initial mass spectrum of the nuggets, their evaporation rate, and the value of the pairing gap, as shown and discussed in the text.

# Primordial nuggets survival and QCD pairing

## Key claim

The authors find that quark pairing in a color-flavor locked (CFL) state substantially suppresses both boiling and surface evaporation of primordial quark nuggets, suggesting that such nuggets could survive with substantial mass for the currently considered range of the pairing gap Δ. However, the survival depends on the unknown initial mass spectrum of the nuggets, their evaporation rate, and the value of Δ; the authors state that nuggets "may survive" rather than definitively concluding they do.

## Method

The authors model the boiling of CFL nuggets by calculating the work required to nucleate hadronic bubbles inside the quark phase, including a term for the energy needed to break CFL pairs (3/2 n_B Δ per baryon). They relate the pressure difference between CFL and hadron phases to that between unpaired strange quark matter and hadrons via a shift proportional to Δ²μ², defining an effective bag constant B_eff. For surface evaporation, they scale the Alcock-Farhi (1985) evaporation rate by a Boltzmann factor exp(-3Δ/2T) to account for the energy cost of breaking CFL pairs, then solve the baryon number evolution equation analytically using exponential integrals.

## Key numbers

- Maximum baryon number of nuggets at formation: A_max = 10⁴⁹ × (100 MeV / T_QCD)², with T_QCD ~ 150 MeV
- Critical temperature for CFL pairing: T_Δ = 0.57 Δ
- Minimum baryon number for nuggets that freeze without evaporating: A_freeze = 3.38 × 10⁴⁴ × (100 MeV / Δ)¹²
- For Δ = 50 MeV, T_Δ ≈ 28.5 MeV; for Δ = 100 MeV, T_Δ ≈ 57 MeV
- The net shift h_Δ = (9μ² - m_n²)Δ²/(3π²) + (3/2) n_B Δ is positive, making boiling impossible below T_Δ
- Binding energy for evaporated hadrons: I = 20 MeV
- Surface evaporation rate parameter: σ₀ = 3.1 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻²

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quark nugget survival by Alcock and Farhi (1985), Madsen (1991, 1993), and Madsen, Heiselberg, and Riisager (1986), who found that surface evaporation could destroy nuggets unless they were extremely massive. The authors previously showed (Lugones and Horvath, 2002) that CFL pairing enlarges the stability window for strange quark matter. The present paper demonstrates that pairing also suppresses destruction mechanisms, enabling nuggets with baryon numbers as low as ~10⁴² to survive, whereas earlier work without pairing required A > 10³⁹ to 10⁴⁶. This enables future detailed numerical studies of the full nugget evolution and the resulting mass spectrum.
