# Connection between the high energy-scale evolution of the P- and T-odd $πN N$ coupling constant and the strong $πN N$ interaction

> The large energy-scale behaviour of the parity and time-reversal violating (PTV) pion-nucleon coupling constant is analyzed in a model combining renormalization-group techniques and the dressing of the PTV vertex with a pion loop. With the strong $πN N$ vertex as a mixture of the pseudovector and pseudoscalar couplings, we show that depending on the admixture parameter, two qualitatively distinct types of behaviour are obtained for the PTV coupling constant at high energy scales: an asymptotic freedom or a fixed-point. We find a critical value of the admixture parameter which delineates these two scenarios. Several examples of the high-energy scale behaviour of the PTV $πN N$ constant are considered, corresponding to realistic hadronic models of the strong pion-nucleon interaction.

## Key claim

The high-energy-scale behavior of the parity- and time-reversal violating (PTV) pion-nucleon coupling constant depends on the admixture parameter \(a\) that mixes pseudovector and pseudoscalar couplings in the strong \(\pi N N\) vertex; there exists a critical value \(a_{\text{crit}} \approx 0.63\) that separates the two possible regimes of asymptotic freedom (coupling vanishes at high energy) or a fixed point (coupling tends to a nonzero constant).

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop beta-function for the PTV \(\pi N N\) coupling constant using dimensional regularization and modified minimal subtraction, dressing the PTV vertex with a pion loop. The strong \(\pi N N\) vertex is taken as a linear combination of pseudovector and pseudoscalar couplings with a mixing parameter \(a\). The renormalization-group equation is integrated to determine the evolution of the PTV coupling constant \(c(\mu)\) between energy scales, and the sign of the beta-function dictates whether \(c\) flows to zero or to a fixed point at high energies.

## Key numbers

* Critical admixture parameter: \(a_{\text{crit}} = \sqrt{2/5} \approx 0.63\), which separates asymptotic-freedom behavior (\(a \le a_{\text{crit}}\)) from fixed-point behavior (\(a > a_{\text{crit}}\)).
* Fixed-point value for the PTV coupling constant: \(c^* = g \sqrt{(5a^2 - 2)/2}\), with the strong coupling constant \(g \approx 13\).
* For the relativistic model of Gross (1992) with \(a \approx 0.78\), the fixed point is \(c^* \approx 9.4\).
* For the non-relativistic model of Gross (1992) with \(a \approx 0.59\), the coupling flows to zero (\(c(\mu \to \infty) \to 0\)).
* For the model of Gross (1993) with \(a \approx 0.75\), the fixed point is \(c^* \approx 8.3\).
* For the model of Goudsmit et al. (1994) with \(a \approx 0.97\), the fixed point is \(c^* \approx 15.1\).
* For the model of Surya and Gross (1996) with \(a \approx 0.8\), the fixed point is \(c^* \approx 10.1\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier model of Kondratyuk (2007), which considered only purely pseudoscalar or purely pseudovector strong \(\pi N N\) vertices, to the more realistic case of a mixed vertex. The results connect the high-energy evolution of the PTV coupling constant to specific hadronic models of the strong interaction, such as those of Gross (1992, 1993), Goudsmit et al. (1994), and Surya and Gross (1996), which use constant mixing parameters. The analysis is limited to nucleon and pion degrees of freedom; at higher energies, heavier mesons and quark-gluon degrees of freedom are expected to become important, so the applicability of the models of Dressler et al. (1999) and Kondratyuk (2001), which have energy-dependent mixing parameters, remains uncertain.
