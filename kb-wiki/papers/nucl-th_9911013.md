# Two Lectures on Small x Physics and High Gluon Density

> In these lectures, I shall discuss small x physics and the consequences of the high gluon density which arises as x decreases. I argue that an understanding of this problem would lead to knowledge of the high energy asymptotics of hadronic processes. The high gluon density should allow a first principles computation of these asymptotics from QCD. This physics might be experimentally probed in heavy ion colliders or in high energy electron-nuclear collisions

# Two Lectures on Small x Physics and High Gluon Density

## Key claim
The growth of the gluon distribution at small x (as seen at HERA) would violate unitarity if it continued indefinitely, but the resolution scale for gluons grows at smaller x so that a probe of fixed resolution does not see all gluons, thereby avoiding contradiction with the Froissart bound. The high gluon density allows a first-principles computation of high-energy asymptotics from weakly coupled QCD, even though the physics is non-perturbative.

## Method
The author develops an effective action formalism in light-cone gauge where valence quarks at high rapidity act as recoilless color sources on a sheet of infinitesimal longitudinal thickness. The classical gluon fields are constructed as non-abelian Lienard-Wiechart potentials, and a renormalization group is generated by integrating out gluon degrees of freedom in successive rapidity intervals, leading to an evolution equation for the source strength. The theory is specified by the local density of color charge squared, which is gauge invariant and related to the gluon distribution function.

## Key numbers
- The gluon distribution parameterization gives $1/(\pi R^2) \, dN/dy \sim A^{1/3} / x^\delta$ where $\delta \sim 0.2 - 0.3$
- The saturation scale is defined as $\Lambda^2 = (1/\pi R^2) \, dN/dy$, and when $\Lambda^2 \gg \Lambda^2_{\text{QCD}}$ the theory is weakly coupled since $\alpha_S(\Lambda) \ll 1$
- The Froissart bound allows at most $\ln^2(1/x)$ growth of the total cross section, while exponential growth in rapidity corresponds to power-law growth in $1/x$
- For a large nucleus, the condition for the gluon to resolve the nucleus as a whole is $x \ll A^{-1/3}$

## Context
This paper extends the small-x physics program initiated by the BFKL approach of Lipatov and colleagues (1990s) and the GLR (Gribov, Levin, Ryskin) analysis of gluon recombination and shadowing. It builds on the McLerran-Venugopalan model (1994) and subsequent work by Jalilian-Marian, Kovner, McLerran, and Weigert (1997-1999) on the renormalization group for high-density QCD. The formalism enables computation of gluon distribution functions, quark and heavy quark distributions, and intrinsic transverse momentum distributions from first principles, with experimental tests possible at RHIC (nuclei comparable to HERA protons) and LHC (nuclei comparable to HERA nuclei).
