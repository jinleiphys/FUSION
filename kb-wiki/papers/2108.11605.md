# Near-threshold Spectrum from Uniformized Mittag-Leffler Expansion -Pole Structure of $Z(3900)$-

> We demonstrate how S-matrix poles manifest themselves as the physical spectrum near the upper threshold in the context of the two-channel uniformized Mittag-Leffler expansion, an expression written as a sum of pole terms under an appropriate variable where the S-matrix is made single-valued (uniformization). We show that the transition of the spectrum is continuous as a S-matrix pole moves across the boundaries of the complex energy Riemann sheets and that the physical spectrum peaks at or near the upper threshold when the S-matrix pole is positioned sufficiently close to it on the uniformized plane. There is no essential difference on which sheet the pole is positioned. What is important is the existence of a pole near the upper threshold and the distance between the pole and the physical region, not on which complex energy sheet the pole is positioned. We also point out that when the pole is close to the upper threshold, the complex pole does not have the usual meaning of the resonance. Neither the real part represents the peak energy, nor the imaginary part represents the half width. Subsequently, we try to understand the current status of $Z(3900)$ from the viewpoint of the uniformized Mittag-Leffler expansion reflecting in particular, Phys.Rev.Lett.117, 242001 (2016) in which they concluded that $Z(3900)$ is not a conventional resonance but a threshold cusp. We point out that their results turn out to indicate the existence of S-matrix poles near the $\bar D D^*$ threshold, which is most likely the origin of the peak found in their calculation of the near-threshold spectrum. In order to support our argument, we set up a separable potential model which shares common behavior of poles near the $\bar D D^*$ threshold to the above-mentioned reference and show in our model that the structures near the $\bar D D^*$ threshold are indeed caused by these near-threshold poles.

# Near-threshold Spectrum from Uniformized Mittag-Leffler Expansion - Pole Structure of $Z(3900)$

## Key claim

The physical spectrum peaks at or near the upper threshold when an S-matrix pole is positioned sufficiently close to it on the uniformized plane, regardless of which complex energy Riemann sheet the pole occupies; there is no essential difference between sheets, and when the pole is close to the upper threshold, the complex pole does not have the usual meaning of a resonance (neither the real part represents the peak energy nor the imaginary part represents the half width).

## Method

The authors employ the two-channel uniformized Mittag-Leffler expansion, in which the S-matrix or Green's function is expressed as a sum of pole terms in the uniformization variable $z$ (where $z = e^{1/2} + (e-1)^{1/2}$ and $e$ is the scaled energy). They numerically track the contribution of a single S-matrix pole as it moves across the [$bt$], [$tb$], and [$bb$] Riemann sheets near the upper threshold, and then apply this framework to reanalyze the HAL QCD results for $Z(3900)$ from Phys.Rev.Lett.117, 242001 (2016), supplemented by a separable potential model with off-diagonal interactions that reproduces the near-threshold pole behavior.

## Key numbers

- For poles $A$-$F$ (all with distance $\gamma_0 = 0.100$ from the nearest physical point): pole $A$ on [$bt$] sheet has $z_p = 0.869 + 0.233i$ and $e_p = 0.943 - 0.053i$; pole $C$ on [$tb$] sheet has $z_p = 0.908 - 0.038i$ and $e_p = 1.007 + 0.008i$; pole $F$ on [$bb$] sheet has $z_p = 1.300 - 0.100i$ and $e_p = 1.065 - 0.043i$.
- From the HAL QCD reanalysis: pole $3^*$ (conjugate to pole $3$) has $z_p = 0.86 - 0.45i$ and $e_p = 0.79 \mp 0.02i$; pole $2^*$ has $z_p = 0.74 - 0.53i$ and $e_p = 0.66 \mp 0.09i$; these are the nearest poles to the $\bar D D^*$ threshold.
- In the separable potential model with $\alpha = \gamma = 1.7$ and $\sqrt{\mu_1/\mu_2} = 0.606$: pole $1^*$ on [$tb$] sheet has $z_p = -0.754 - 0.229i$, residue $r_p = 0.118 - 0.042i$, and $e_p = 0.964 - 0.138i$; pole $2^*$ on [$bb$] sheet has $z_p = 1.198 - 1.076i$, residue $r_p = -0.147 - 0.254i$, and $e_p = 0.580 - 0.549i$.

## Context

This work extends the uniformized Mittag-Leffler expansion method previously applied to $\Lambda(1405)$ by Yamada et al. (2020) and Phys.Rev.C.102.055201, and directly addresses the interpretation of $Z(3900)$ from the HAL QCD collaboration (Phys.Rev.Lett.117, 242001, 2016), who concluded that $Z(3900)$ is a threshold cusp rather than a conventional resonance. The authors argue that the HAL QCD results actually indicate the existence of S-matrix poles near the $\bar D D^*$ threshold, and that the observed enhancement can be naturally understood as arising from such near-threshold poles. This enables a model-independent analysis of experimental data for $Z(3900)$ using the uniformized Mittag-Leffler expansion to determine pole positions and residues without relying on sheet assignments.
