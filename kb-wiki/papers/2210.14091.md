# Characterizing the initial and final state effects of relativistic nuclear collisions

> The Multi-Phase Transport model (AMPT) is used to study the final state effects on the Symmetric Correlations (SC), Asymmetric Correlations (ASC), Normalized Symmetric Correlations (NSC), and Normalized Asymmetric Correlations (NASC) in Au+Au collisions at 200~GeV. The correlators' sensitivity to non-flow effects associated with long- and short-range non-flow correlations are also discussed using the HIJING model. The results indicate that SC, ASC, NSC, and NASC can give accompanying constraints for initial and final state effects. In addition, conducting further detailed experimental measurements spanning a broad range of collision systems and beam energies will serve as an additional constraint for the theoretical models' calculations.

## Key claim

The Multi-Phase Transport model (AMPT) calculations show that Symmetric Correlations (SC), Asymmetric Correlations (ASC), Normalized Symmetric Correlations (NSC), and Normalized Asymmetric Correlations (NASC) can provide simultaneous constraints on both initial and final state effects in relativistic nuclear collisions, but only if accompanied by further detailed experimental measurements across a broad range of collision systems and beam energies.

## Method

The study uses the AMPT model (v2.26t9b) with two parameter sets (Set-1: mu = 3.35 fm^-1, sigma_pp = 2.8 mb, eta/s = 0.2; Set-2: mu = 4.20 fm^-1, sigma_pp = 1.8 mb, eta/s = 0.3) to simulate minimum bias Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, with approximately 10 million events per set. Multi-particle correlation methods using two-subevents (with Delta-eta > 0.7) for two-, three-, and four-particle correlations, and one-subevent for five- and six-particle correlations, are employed to calculate SC, ASC, NSC, and NASC for flow harmonics v_1 through v_6. The HIJING model (v1.411) is used separately to assess non-flow contributions from long-range (dijets) and short-range (resonance decays, Bose-Einstein correlations, jet fragments) sources.

## Key numbers

- Two AMPT parameter sets used: Set-1 with mu = 3.35 fm^-1, sigma_pp = 2.8 mb, eta/s = 0.2; Set-2 with mu = 4.20 fm^-1, sigma_pp = 1.8 mb, eta/s = 0.3
- Charged particles selected with 0.2 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c and |eta| < 1.0
- Two-subevent method uses Delta-eta > 0.7 between subevents A (eta > 0.35) and B (eta < -0.35)
- Approximately 10 million minimum bias Au+Au events at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV generated for each AMPT parameter set
- HIJING model parameters for Lund string fragmentation: a = 0.55 and b = 0.15 GeV^-2
- AMPT fixed alpha_s = 0.47 with varying mu values

## Context

This work extends prior investigations of SC, ASC, NSC, and NASC that were conducted in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies using hydrodynamic models (Mordasini 2019, Moravcova 2020, Li 2021, Bilandzic 2021) and event-plane angular correlations for harmonics 2-5 (Bilandzic 2020, Taghavi 2020). The study extends these analyses to Au+Au collisions at RHIC top energy (200 GeV) and includes flow harmonics v_1 through v_6, systematically investigating final state effects (medium properties eta/s, hadronization, and hadronic transport) using the AMPT model. The results enable future experimental measurements across different collision systems and beam energies to provide additional constraints for theoretical models and help discriminate between various approaches to initial and final state effects.
