# Vector Meson Decay of Baryon Resonances

> 8 pages, 2tables, 4 figures, invited talk at NSTAR 2001, Workshop on the Physics of Excited Nucleons, University of Mainz, Germany, March 7-10, 2001. To be published in World Scientific

## Key claim

Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) works "remarkably well" for the isovector channel of baryon resonance decay to vector mesons, but only within a factor of two for most resonances; for the isoscalar channel, the VMD approach yields predictions for the omega coupling strength that are consistent with hadronic data, though the analysis is limited by the fact that most strongly-coupled resonances lie below the N-omega threshold.

## Method

The authors decompose the electromagnetic coupling of nucleon resonances into isoscalar and isovector parts using linear combinations of proton and neutron helicity amplitudes, then extract the isoscalar and isovector coupling strengths (g_s and g_v) from experimental helicity amplitudes via the gamma-width. Using the VMD relation f_RNrho = g_v * m_rho * (2*g_rho/e) and f_RNomega = g_s * m_omega * (2*g_omega/e), they compute the hadronic coupling constants for rho and omega mesons to various nucleon resonances, and then use these couplings to calculate the in-medium spectral functions of rho and omega mesons in nuclear matter at saturation density via a low-density approximation for the selfenergy.

## Key numbers

- For the D13(1520) resonance, the VMD-derived coupling f_RNrho is 3.44 ± 0.18 (Arndt helicity amplitudes) compared to the hadronic fit value of 6.67 ± 0.78 (Manley)
- For the F15(1680) resonance, the VMD-derived coupling f_RNrho is 3.48 ± 0.39 (Arndt) compared to the hadronic fit value of 6.87 ± 1.57 (Manley)
- For the P33(1232) resonance, the VMD-derived coupling f_RNrho is 13.40 ± 0.2 (Arndt) compared to 11.96 (Feuster)
- For the F15(1680) resonance, the VMD-predicted coupling f_RNomega is 6.89 ± 1.38 (Arndt) and 6.52 ± 1.49 (PDG)
- For the D13(1520) resonance, the VMD-predicted coupling f_RNomega is 2.87 ± 0.76 (Arndt) and 3.42 ± 0.87 (PDG)
- The omega meson in nuclear matter at density rho = rho_0 shows a broadening of about 50 MeV and a repulsive mass shift of roughly 20 MeV
- The D13(2080) resonance has an omega decay width of about 70 MeV and contributes about 0.1 mb to the pi- p -> omega n cross-section, roughly 10% of the total

## Context

This work extends the VMD approach to baryon resonance decays into vector mesons, building on the formalism of Williams and the helicity amplitude analyses of Arndt et al. and Feuster et al. The results for the omega meson coupling are compared to the coupled-channel analysis of Friman and Lutz, which found f_RNomega ≈ 6.5 (nearly twice the authors' value), and to a quark model calculation by Brolli and Riska, which found f_RNomega ≈ 2.6 (close to the authors' result). The in-medium spectral functions are relevant for interpreting the CERES experiment's dilepton excess at invariant masses below the vector meson mass, and the omega in-medium results agree with those of Friman and Lutz, Klingl, and Effenberger. The work enables improved modeling of vector meson production in nuclear reactions and better understanding of in-medium modifications of hadron properties.
