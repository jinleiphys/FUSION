# Effective Field Theories and Lattice QCD for the X Y Z frontier

> Exotic states have been predicted before and after the advent of QCD. In the last decades they have been observed at accelerator experiments in the sector with two heavy quarks, at or above the quarkonium strong decay threshold and called X Y Z states. These states offer a unique possibility for investigating the dynamical properties of strongly correlated systems in QCD. I will show how an alliance of nonrelativistic effective field theories and lattice can allow us to address these states in QCD. In particular I will explain what are the opportunities and challenges of lattice QCD in this respect and which new tools should be developed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a systematic description of XYZ exotic states can be achieved by combining nonrelativistic effective field theories (NR EFTs) with lattice QCD, exploiting the large heavy-quark mass scale to factorize the problem into universal, gauge-invariant gluonic correlators that can be computed on the lattice, rather than attempting ab initio lattice calculations for each individual exotic state.

## Method

The author constructs a Born-Oppenheimer Effective Field Theory (BOEFT) that exploits the hierarchy of scales where the confinement scale Λ_QCD is much larger than the heavy-quark binding energy mv², allowing the light degrees of freedom (gluons, light quarks) to be treated as fast degrees of freedom that generate adiabatic static potentials for the heavy quarks. These static potentials are obtained from lattice calculations of generalized Wilson loops and gluonic static energies classified by the D∞h symmetry group (labeled by Λησ), and the heavy-quark dynamics is then solved via a Schrödinger equation on each potential surface. For quarkonium below threshold, the method uses strongly-coupled pNRQCD where the matching from NRQCD is performed nonperturbatively using lattice input for the singlet static potential and its relativistic corrections.

## Key numbers

- The heavy-quark relative velocity v² ∼ 0.1 for b-bbar systems and v² ∼ 0.3 for c-cbar systems, defining the nonrelativistic hierarchy m ≫ mv ≫ mv²
- The gluonic static energies E_Γ(r) are classified by rotational quantum number Λ = 0,1,2,... (corresponding to Σ, Π, Δ), CP eigenvalue η = g (gerade) or u (ungerade), and for Σ states the reflection eigenvalue σ
- In the r → 0 limit, the Π_u and Σ_u⁻ hybrid static energies become degenerate and correspond to a gluonic operator with quantum numbers K^PC = 1^+⁻
- The hybrid static energies are separated from the lowest Σ_g⁺ potential by an energy gap of order Λ_QCD at distances r ∼ Λ_QCD⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the successful application of NR EFTs and lattice QCD to ordinary quarkonium (pioneered by the TUMQCD collaboration) to the exotic XYZ sector, where states like the X(3872) discovered at BELLE in 2003 have been observed at or above the strong decay threshold. The BOEFT framework provides a unified theoretical description that encompasses hybrids, tetraquarks, molecules, and pentaquarks, complementing earlier model-based approaches such as molecular descriptions by Guo (2017), Braaten (2007), and Fleming (2011), as well as compact tetraquark and hadro-quarkonium pictures. The approach enables future lattice calculations of chromoelectric and chromomagnetic correlators (both local and nonlocal in time), generalized static Wilson loops with field insertions, and transport coefficients at finite temperature to sharpen predictions for quarkonium and exotic states in vacuum and in heavy-ion collisions.
