# Test of the heavy quark-light diquark approximation for baryons with a heavy quark

> We check a commonly used approximation in which a baryon with a heavy quark is described as a heavy quark-light diquark system. The heavy quark influences the diquark internal motion reducing the average distance between the two light quarks. Besides, we show how the average distance between the heavy quark and any of the light quarks, and that between the heavy quark and the center of mass of the light diquark, are smaller than the distance between the two light quarks, which seems to contradict the heavy quark-light diquark picture. This latter result is in agreement with expectations from QCD sum rules and lattice QCD calculations. Our results also show that the diquark approximations produces larger masses than the ones obtained in a full calculation.

## Key claim

The heavy quark-light diquark (HQLD) approximation, in which a baryon with a heavy quark is treated as a bound state of a heavy quark and a light diquark whose internal structure is unaffected by the heavy quark, produces larger masses than a full three-body calculation and yields quark distributions that contradict the picture of a compact diquark far from the heavy quark; the approximation works reasonably well for total masses but the heavy quark is actually closer to the light quarks than the light quarks are to each other.

## Method

The authors used a nonrelativistic quark model with the AL1 interquark potential (confinement plus Coulomb and hyperfine terms from one-gluon exchange) to compute ground-state heavy baryon masses and quark probability distributions. They compared a full variational three-body calculation (previously published in Albertus (2004)) with the HQLD approximation, in which the diquark internal wave function is determined by the light-quark Hamiltonian alone and the heavy-quark–diquark relative wave function is obtained variationally. The comparison was performed for baryons containing a charm or bottom quark and two light quarks (u, d, s) with total spin J=1/2 or 3/2 and zero orbital angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- Full calculation masses (in MeV) are smaller than HQLD approximation masses for all baryons; e.g., Λ_c: full 2295 vs. HQLD 2317 (experiment 2286.48±0.14); Λ_b: full 5643 vs. HQLD 5663 (experiment 5624±9).
- The squared projection |𝒫|² of the full wave function onto the HQLD wave function ranges from 0.921 (Ξ'_b) to 0.971 (Λ_c), indicating 3–8% discrepancy.
- For Λ_c, the full calculation mass is 2295 MeV, while the HQLD approximation gives 2317 MeV; for Σ_c, full 2469 MeV vs. HQLD 2521 MeV (experiment 2453.6±0.5).
- For Ω_c, full calculation mass is 2681 MeV vs. HQLD 2727 MeV (experiment 2697.5±2.6); for Ω_b, full 6033 MeV vs. HQLD 6081 MeV (lattice estimate 5990±70).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Cardarelli (1998), who used a light-front constituent quark model and found that collinear-type configurations (heavy quark close to the light-quark center of mass) dominate over diquark-like configurations when compared to QCD sum rules (Grozin 1992) and lattice QCD calculations (UKQCD 1998). The present results confirm that finding using a nonrelativistic quark model with a different interquark potential and a variational three-body solution that reproduces earlier Faddeev results from Silvestre-Brac (1996). The study enables future work to use full three-body calculations rather than the HQLD approximation for heavy baryons, while noting that for doubly heavy baryons the light quark–heavy diquark picture is clearly favored (as shown using wave functions from Albertus (2007)).
