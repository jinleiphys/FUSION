# Extraction of the strong coupling with HERA and EIC inclusive data

> Sensitivity to the strong coupling $α_S(M^2_Z)$ is investigated using existing Deep Inelastic Scattering data from HERA in combination with projected future measurements from the Electron Ion Collider (EIC) in a next-to-next-to-leading order QCD analysis. A potentially world-leading level of precision is achievable when combining simulated inclusive neutral current EIC data with inclusive charged and neutral current measurements from HERA, with or without the addition of HERA inclusive jet and dijet data. The result can be obtained with substantially less than one year of projected EIC data at the lower end of the EIC centre-of-mass energy range. Some questions remain over the magnitude of uncertainties due to missing higher orders in the theoretical framework.

## Key claim

A simultaneous NNLO QCD fit of HERA inclusive data with simulated EIC inclusive data can determine the strong coupling α_s(M²_Z) with a total precision better than 0.4%, potentially world-leading, but this result does not yet include an uncertainty due to missing higher orders beyond NNLO, which remains to be evaluated.

## Method

The analysis uses the HERAPDF theoretical framework within the xFitter program, performing NNLO DGLAP evolution to simultaneously fit parton distribution functions and α_s(M²_Z) via χ² minimization. Simulated EIC neutral current inclusive pseudodata are generated using HERAPDF2.0NNLO predictions with α_s(M²_Z)=0.116, smeared by Gaussian distributions with standard deviations from the ATHENA detector proposal, and combined with final combined H1 and ZEUS inclusive DIS cross sections and optionally with HERA inclusive jet and dijet data.

## Key numbers

- α_s(M²_Z) = 0.1160 ± 0.0004 (exp) +0.0003/-0.0002 (model+parameterisation) ± 0.0005 (scale) from the simultaneous NNLO fit to HERA inclusive and jet data plus EIC inclusive data at all five √s values
- α_s(M²_Z) = 0.1159 ± 0.0004 (exp) +0.0002/-0.0001 (model+parameterisation) from the fit using only HERA inclusive data plus EIC inclusive data, corresponding to a total precision better than 0.4%
- The HERAPDF2.0Jets NNLO result without EIC data gives α_s(M²_Z) = 0.1156 ± 0.0011 (exp) +0.0001/-0.0002 (model+parameterisation) ± 0.0029 (scale)
- When including only a single EIC data set with √s = 45 GeV, the experimental uncertainty is approximately ±0.0010
- EIC beam configurations span √s from 29 GeV to 141 GeV with integrated luminosities from 4.4 fb⁻¹ to 100 fb⁻¹
- Most EIC data points have a point-to-point uncorrelated systematic uncertainty of 1.9%, extending to 2.75% at lowest y values, with an additional normalisation uncertainty of 3.4%

## Context

This work extends previous HERA-only studies (H1 Collaboration, 2015; H1 Collaboration, 2021) that showed limited sensitivity to α_s from inclusive data alone but competitive precision when including jet production cross sections. The projected EIC precision significantly improves upon both the PDG world average and the lattice QCD average, and is enabled by the EIC's coverage of the high-x, moderate-Q² region not accessed at HERA, which decouples α_s from the gluon density. The results motivate further work to establish a consensus on estimating missing higher order uncertainties for inclusive DIS data, as discussed by the NNPDF collaboration (2019), and open the possibility of including EIC jet data and other observables in future analyses.
