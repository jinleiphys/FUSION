# Unitarity Analyses of $πN$ Elastic Scattering Amplitudes

> The pion - nucleon scattering phase shifts in $s$ and $p$ waves are analyzed using PKU unitarization approach that can separate the phase shifts into different contributions from poles and branch cuts. It is found that in $S_{11}$ and $P_{11}$ channels, there exist large and positive missing contributions when one compares the phase shift from known resonances plus branch cuts with the experimental data, which indicates that those two channels may contain sizable effects from $N^*(1535)$ and $N^*(1440)$ shadow poles. Those results are obtained using tree level results of the $πN$ amplitude.

# Unitarity Analyses of πN Elastic Scattering Amplitudes

## Key claim
The PKU unitarization approach reveals large and positive missing contributions in the S11 and P11 channels when comparing phase shifts from known resonances plus branch cuts to experimental data, suggesting that N*(1535) and N*(1440) may contain sizable shadow pole effects; however, these results are obtained using only tree-level πN amplitudes, and the missing contributions could also arise from one-loop effects, incorrect shadow pole approximations, hidden poles, or neglected branch cuts.

## Method
The PKU representation, a production representation of the partial wave S-matrix for 2-to-2 elastic scattering, was applied to s-wave and p-wave πN elastic scattering channels. The left-hand cut contribution was calculated using baryon chiral perturbation theory at tree level (O(p¹) and O(p²) Lagrangians in SU(2)), while pole positions from known resonances were taken from Anisovich (2011) and used as input parameters. The inelastic cut was omitted because the analyzed energy region is far from inelastic thresholds, and the tree-level K-matrix method was also examined for spurious pole issues.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon mass M = 0.9383 GeV, pion mass m = 0.1396 GeV, pion decay constant F = 0.0924 GeV, axial coupling g = 0.1267.
- Fitted O(p²) coupling constants from K-matrix fit to 20 data points per channel: c1 = -0.642, c2 = 1.559, c3 = -2.812, c4 = 1.560, with fit quality χ²/d.o.f = 3.624.
- Under the assumption of no hidden poles or extra cuts, extracted shadow pole positions: for N*(1535) mass M = 0.880 GeV and width Γ = 540 MeV (χ²/d.o.f = 0.017); for N*(1440) mass M = 0.896 GeV and width Γ = 14.6 MeV (χ²/d.o.f = 3.738).

## Context
This work extends the PKU representation method previously applied to ππ and πK elastic scatterings by Zheng (2004), Zhou (2006), and Xiao (2001) to the πN system, and checks the reliability of the traditional K-matrix unitarization approach. The analysis reveals that the K-matrix fit at tree level generates spurious poles on the first Riemann sheet off the real axis, making it unreliable for dynamically generating poles despite its widespread use for determining coupling constants. The findings point to unresolved dynamical properties of N*(1535) and N*(1440), motivating future work to examine one-loop contributions, more accurate shadow pole calculations, possible hidden poles below threshold, and additional branch cuts such as those proposed by Ceci (2011).
