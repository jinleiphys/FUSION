# Non-perturbative finite $T$ broadening of the $ρ$ meson and dilepton emission in heavy ion-collisions

> We study self-consistently the finite $T$ broadening of the $ρ$-meson and its implications for dilepton emission in heavy ion-collisions. For this purpose finite width effects at finite temperature due to the $ρ$-$π$ interaction are investigated in a self-consistent $Φ$-functional approach. The temperature dependence of the $ρ$-meson and pion spectral functions and self-energies is discussed. The spectral functions show considerable broadening in comparison with a perturbative calculation on the one-loop level. Using these spectral functions, we make a comparison to dilepton emission data from the CERES NA49 collaboration employing a parametrized fireball evolution model of collision. We demonstrate that these non-perturbative finite width effects are in-medium modifications relevant to the understanding of the enhancement of the low invariant mass spectrum of dileptons emitted in A-A collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that even considering only the π-ρ interaction, a self-consistent Φ-functional resummation (including off-shell contributions and the in-medium damping width of the pion) produces substantial broadening of the ρ-meson spectral function at finite temperature, contrary to earlier perturbative calculations which assumed stable pions and predicted negligible broadening from a purely mesonic medium.

## Method

The authors employ a self-consistent Φ-functional (Baym) approach to derive Dyson-Schwinger equations for the ρ-meson and pion propagators, using a vector meson dominance (VMD) inspired model with the ρ-meson treated in the Stückelberg formalism. They solve the coupled set of equations numerically for the imaginary parts of the self-energies and spectral functions, neglecting mass shifts (real parts) and considering only collisional broadening from the π-ρ interaction. Two approaches to enforce electromagnetic current conservation are compared: taking the Rξ-gauge limit ξ→0 (approach I) and explicitly enforcing transversality of the self-energy tensor (approach II).

## Key numbers

* The ρ-meson spectral function shows considerable broadening compared to perturbative one-loop calculations; at T=150 MeV, the width from the self-consistent calculation is substantially larger than the perturbative result (which gives zero width below the 2mπ threshold).
* The CERES collaboration observed a strong enhancement of low-mass dileptons in the range between 200 MeV and 600 MeV in central A-A collisions compared to p-A and p-p yields.
* The perturbative study by Gale (1991) predicted physical mass shifts from the π-ρ interaction only in the range of about 10% of the vacuum ρ-meson mass even for comparably high temperatures.

## Context

This work extends earlier perturbative studies of finite-temperature ρ-meson broadening by Gale (1991) and Urban (1998), which considered only stable pions or on-shell scattering processes (Haglin 1994, Gao 1998). The self-consistent treatment including off-shell contributions and the reciprocal dependence of ρ and pion widths is novel. The results are compared to dilepton data from the CERES NA49 collaboration (Agakishiev 1995, 1997; Lenkeit 1999) using a parametrized fireball evolution model (Renk 2004) that successfully reproduces charmonium suppression, direct photon emission, hadronic spectra, and HBT correlations. The work enables more realistic modeling of in-medium modifications of vector mesons in heavy-ion collisions, though it deliberately omits baryon scattering effects and chiral symmetry restoration considered in other approaches (Rapp 1999, Renk 2002, 2003).
