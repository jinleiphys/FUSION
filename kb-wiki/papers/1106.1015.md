# Structure of scalar mesons and the Higgs sector of strong interaction

> The scalar mesons $σ(600)$, $κ(800)$, $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ together with the pseudo Goldstone bosons $π$, $K$, and $η$ may be considered as the Higgs sector of strong interaction. After a long time of uncertainty about the internal structure of the scalar mesons there now seems to be consistency which is in line with the major parts of experimental observations. Great progress has been made by introducing the unified model of Close and Törnqvist. This model states that mesons below 1 GeV may be understood as $q^2\bar{q}^2$ in S-wave with some $q\bar{q}$ in P-wave in the center, further out they rearrange as $(q\bar{q})^2$ and finally as meson-meson states. The P-wave component inherent in the structure of the neutral scalar mesons can be understood as a doorway state for the formation of the scalar meson via two-photon fusion, whereas in nucleon Compton scattering these P-wave components serve as intermediate states. The masses of the scalar mesons are predicted in terms of spontaneous and explicit symmetry breaking.

## Key claim

The scalar mesons σ(600), κ(800), f₀(980) and a₀(980) together with the pseudo Goldstone bosons π, K and η may be considered as the Higgs sector of strong interaction, with the masses of scalar mesons predicted in terms of spontaneous and explicit symmetry breaking; the paper adopts the unified model of Close and Törnqvist but makes one essential refinement concerning the small q\bar{q} component in P-wave, interpreting it as a doorway state for two-photon fusion reactions and as intermediate states in nucleon Compton scattering.

## Method

The author applies the doorway mechanism from nuclear physics to neutral scalar mesons, decomposing the total wave function into ground state, doorway state (q\bar{q} in P-wave), and main complex structure ((q\bar{q})² in S-wave). The two-photon decay amplitudes of scalar mesons are calculated using the quark-level linear σ model (QLLσM) with the Delbourgo-Scadron relation, comparing predicted two-photon widths to experimental data from two-photon fusion reactions and t-channel contributions to nucleon Compton scattering. The masses of scalar mesons are derived from spontaneous symmetry breaking (giving a common component) and explicit symmetry breaking (giving mass differences via strange quark content in the (q\bar{q})² configurations).

## Key numbers

- σ meson pole position: M_σ = 441^{+16}_{-8} MeV, Γ_σ = 544^{+18}_{-25} MeV (from Caprini et al. 2006)
- Two-photon width of σ meson from dispersive studies: Γ(σ → γγ) = (2.3 ± 0.4) keV (average of recent evaluations)
- Two-photon width of σ meson from QLLσM prediction: Γ(σ → γγ) = 2.6 keV, using m_σ = 666 MeV
- Two-photon width of σ meson from t-channel part of proton electric polarizability: Γ(σ → γγ) = (2.6 ± 0.3) keV
- Bare mass of σ meson predicted by QLLσM: m_σ = 666 MeV, from m_σ = (16π²/3 f₀² + \hat{m}_π²)^{1/2} with f₀ = 89.8 MeV and \hat{m}_π = 138 MeV
- Constituent quark mass in chiral limit: M = 2π/√N_c f₀ = 326 MeV (with N_c = 3, f₀ = 89.8 MeV), giving m^{cl}_σ = 2M = 652 MeV
- Two-photon width of π⁰: Γ(π⁰ → γγ) = (7.74 ± 0.55) × 10^{-3} keV
- Two-photon width of η: Γ(η → γγ) = 0.510 ± 0.026 keV
- Two-photon width of η′: Γ(η′ → γγ) = 4.29 ± 0.15 keV
- Two-photon width of f₀(980): Γ(f₀ → γγ) = 0.29^{+0.07}_{-0.08} keV
- Two-photon width of a₀(980): Γ(a₀ → γγ) = 0.30 ± 0.10 keV
- Transition amplitude for σ → γγ: |ℳ(σ → γγ)| = (5/3)(α_e/π f_π) = 41.9 × 10^{-6} MeV^{-1}, with α_e = 1/137.04 and f_π = (92.42 ± 0.26) MeV

## Context

This work extends the unified model of Close and Törnqvist (2002) for scalar mesons below 1 GeV, which describes them as q²\bar{q}² in S-wave with some q\bar{q} in P-wave, by interpreting the P-wave component as a doorway state for two-photon fusion and Compton scattering. The paper builds on previous investigations by Schumacher (2005-2011) showing that the σ meson as a q\bar{q} state has properties of the Higgs boson of strong interaction, and supplements those studies by addressing the relationship between q\bar{q}, (q\bar{q})² and dimeson structures. The approach enables quantitative predictions of two-photon widths and meson masses through spontaneous and explicit symmetry breaking, connecting to earlier work on the four-quark configuration by Jaffe (1976) and to the quark-level linear σ model of Delbourgo and Scadron (1995).
