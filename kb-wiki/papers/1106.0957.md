# Three triton states in 9Li

> We focus on a characteristic non-alpha cluster structure in light neutron-rich nuclei; three triton structure in 9Li. This is an analogy to the case of three alpha state in 12C (Hoyle state). The alpha clusters behave as bosons. however tritons have Fermionic nature, and how the three cluster structure is different from 12C is an intriguing problem. For this purpose, we introduce three triton wave functions. In addition, alpha+t+n+n wave functions are prepared to describe other low-lying states of 9Li, and the coupling effect between them is taken into account. The states with dominantly the three triton components appear below the three triton threshold energy, where three triton correlation is important, however the root mean square radius is not enhanced contrary to the alpha gas states in 12C and 16O.

## Key claim

The paper claims that states with dominantly three-triton components appear in the low-lying states of 9Li below the three-triton threshold energy, but contrary to the alpha-gas states in 12C and 16O, the root mean square radius is not enhanced; the authors therefore characterize this as "three triton correlation" rather than "three triton cluster."

## Method

The authors constructed a microscopic model using two sets of basis states: alpha+t+n+n configurations and three-triton (t+t+t) configurations, each built from locally shifted Gaussian single-particle wave functions with a size parameter b = 1.46 fm. The Hamiltonian included the Volkov No.2 central interaction with Bartlet and Heisenberg parameters B = H = 0.08, the G3RS spin-orbit potential, and Coulomb interaction; the total wave function was obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian matrix after parity and angular momentum projection. The basis consisted of 150 alpha+t+n+n states and 150 three-triton states, and the coupling between the two configuration spaces was taken into account.

## Key numbers

- Ground state of 9Li converges at -40.59 MeV, which is 6.13 MeV below the alpha+t+n+n threshold (calculated at -34.46 MeV), compared with the experimental value of 8.22 MeV.
- The three-triton threshold is calculated at -20.63 MeV, corresponding to excitation energy Ex = 19.95 MeV, compared with the experimental value of 19.55 MeV.
- The third 3/2- state converges at -30.66 MeV with a root mean square radius of 2.39 fm, smaller than the ground state radius of 2.43 fm.
- The second 1/2- state converges at -29.06 MeV with an rms radius of 2.37 fm, and lies 8.43 MeV below the three-triton threshold.
- The squared isoscalar E0 transition strength from the ground 3/2- state to the 4th 3/2- state is enhanced at approximately 40 fm4, but the strengths to the third and 5th 3/2- states (which have large three-triton components) are very suppressed (0.01 fm4 and 2.11 fm4, respectively).
- The three-triton component C3t for the second 1/2- state is 1.00, for the third 3/2- state is 1.00, and for the 4th 1/2- and 5th 3/2- states is 0.99 and 0.96, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the study of non-alpha cluster structures in light neutron-rich nuclei, following earlier investigations of alpha+t+t structure in 10Be by Itagaki et al. (2008) and the coexistence of alpha+t+t and alpha+alpha+n+n configurations in that nucleus. The three-triton structure in 9Li is presented as an analogy to the three-alpha Hoyle state in 12C studied by Tohsaki, Horiuchi, Schuck, and Ropke (2001), but with the key difference that tritons are fermions rather than bosons. The results enable future development of an effective wave function for multi-triton (fermion) systems analogous to the THSR wave function for alpha clusters, and suggest that isoscalar monopole transitions may not be enhanced for states with three-triton correlation, in contrast to cluster states in 12C and 13C.
