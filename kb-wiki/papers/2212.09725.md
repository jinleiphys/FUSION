# Anisotropic fluctuations of angular momentum of heavy quarks in the Glasma

> We study the evolution of the angular momentum of the heavy quarks in the very early stage of high energy nuclear collisions, in which the background is made of evolving Glasma fields. Given the novelty of the problem, we limit ourselves to the use of toy heavy quarks with a large, unphysical mass, in order to implement the kinetic equations for the angular momentum in the non-relativistic limit. We find that as a consequence of the anisotropy of the background fields, angular momentum fluctuations are also anisotropic: we understand this in simple terms relating the fluctuations of the angular momentum, $L$, to those of linear momentum. While orbital angular momentum diffuses and develops substantial fluctuations and anisotropies, the spin does not. Hence, we can identify the fluctuations of $L$ with those of the total angular momentum $J=L + S$. Therefore, our study suggests that the total angular momentum of the heavy quarks in the early stage of high energy nuclear collisions will present anisotropic fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper finds that anisotropic fluctuations of heavy quark angular momentum arise in the evolving Glasma fields of the early stage of high-energy nuclear collisions, but this result is obtained using toy heavy quarks with a large, unphysical mass (10 GeV) in the non-relativistic limit and in a static box geometry with zero average angular momentum, leaving the full relativistic case and inclusion of finite angular momentum to future studies.

## Method

The authors initialize Glasma fields using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model for SU(2) color group and evolve them via classical Yang-Mills equations in temporal gauge. Heavy quarks with mass 10 GeV are initialized uniformly in a static box and evolved using non-relativistic classical equations of motion for position, kinetic momentum, color charge, spin, and orbital angular momentum derived from the non-relativistic Dirac equation with gauge-invariant coupling to gluon fields. The system has zero average angular momentum, and quantities are averaged over 100 events for each parameter set.

## Key numbers

- Heavy quark mass used: \(m = 10\) GeV (unphysical, for non-relativistic consistency); also tested with \(m = 5\) GeV (close to beauty quark mass)
- Glasma lifetime: up to approximately 0.6 fm/c for RHIC energy collisions and approximately 0.4 fm/c for LHC energy collisions
- Saturation scale used: \(Q_s = 2\) GeV (primary results) and \(Q_s = 1\) GeV (comparison)
- Box geometry: longitudinal half-side \(\ell_z = 0.5\) fm; transverse half-sides \(\ell = 0.5, 1, 2\) fm (corresponding to transverse areas 1, 4, 16 fm\(^2\))
- Anisotropy parameter \(\Delta_2\) for \(\ell = 1\) fm and \(\ell = 2\) fm lies between 40% and 60% within the realistic time range up to 0.6 fm/c (for \(p_z = 0\) initialization)
- For \(\ell = 0.5\) fm, \(\Delta_2\) remains smaller but still sizeable
- Geometric anisotropy contribution \(\Delta_2^\mathrm{geom} = 0\) for \(\ell = \ell_z = 0.5\) fm, 0.231 for \(\ell = 1\) fm, \(\ell_z = 0.5\) fm, and 0.306 for \(\ell = 2\) fm, \(\ell_z = 0.5\) fm
- Initial heavy quark transverse momentum: \(p_T = 0.5\) GeV; longitudinal momentum either 0 or \(|p_z| = 0.5\) GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark momentum diffusion in evolving Glasma fields (Ruggieri 2018, Ipp 2020, Liu 2020) to the novel topic of angular momentum fluctuations. The anisotropic momentum broadening of heavy quarks in the anisotropic gluon fields, previously established in those works, is shown here to produce anisotropic fluctuations of orbital angular momentum, while spin fluctuations remain isotropic and negligible in magnitude. The results enable future studies incorporating finite average angular momentum, relativistic dynamics, and realistic longitudinal expansion of the fireball.
