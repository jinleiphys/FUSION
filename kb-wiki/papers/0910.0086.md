# A phenomenological equation of state for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter

> A phenomenological momentum-independent (MID) model is constructed to describe the equation of state (EOS) for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter, especially the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy $E_{\text{\textrm{sym}}}(ρ)$. This model can reasonably describe the general properties of the EOS for symmetric nuclear matter and the symmetry energy predicted by both the sophisticated isospin and momentum dependent MDI model and the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach. We find that there exists a nicely linear correlation between $K_{\mathrm{sym}}$ and $L$ as well as between $J_{0}/K_{0} $ and $K_{0}$, where $L$ and $K_{\mathrm{sym}}$ represent, respectively, the slope and curvature parameters of the symmetry energy at the normal nuclear density $ρ_{0}$ while $K_{0}$ and $J_{0}$ are, respectively, the incompressibility and the third-order derivative parameter of symmetric nuclear matter at $ρ_{0}$. These correlations together with the empirical constraints on $K_{0}$, $L$ and $E_{\text{\textrm{sym}}}(ρ_{0}) $ lead to an estimation of -477 MeV $\leq K_{\mathrm{sat,2}}\leq -241 $ MeV for the second-order isospin asymmetry expansion coefficient for the incompressibility of asymmetric nuclear matter at the saturation point.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a phenomenological momentum-independent (MID) model that can reasonably describe the equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter and the symmetry energy predicted by both the sophisticated isospin and momentum dependent MDI model and the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach, with the hedge that the model's density functional for the symmetry energy is flexible enough to mimic very different density behaviors by varying only one parameter.

## Method

The author constructs a momentum-independent (MID) model where the potential energy density of cold asymmetric nuclear matter is parametrized with parameters alpha, beta, and sigma determined by the binding energy per nucleon (-16 MeV) and the incompressibility K0 at saturation density rho0 = 0.16 fm^-3, while the potential part of the symmetry energy is parametrized with a dimensionless y parameter and a gamma_sym parameter (default 4/3) to mimic various density dependences. The model is then used to examine correlations between the slope parameter L and curvature parameter K_sym of the symmetry energy, and between the third-order derivative parameter J0 and the incompressibility K0 of symmetric nuclear matter, leading to constraints on the second-order isospin asymmetry expansion coefficient K_sat,2 for the incompressibility of asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: rho0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: E0(rho0) = -16 MeV
- Kinetic part of symmetry energy at rho0: E_sym^kin(rho0) = 12.3 MeV
- Potential part of symmetry energy at rho0: E_sym^pot(rho0) = 17.7 MeV (from E_sym(rho0) = 30 MeV minus kinetic part)
- Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: K0 = 240 +/- 20 MeV from nuclear giant monopole resonances
- Empirical constraint on slope parameter L from heavy-ion collisions: 46 MeV <= L <= 111 MeV
- Empirical constraint on symmetry energy at rho0: 25 MeV <= E_sym(rho0) <= 35 MeV
- Estimated range for K_sat,2: -477 MeV <= K_sat,2 <= -241 MeV
- For gamma_sym = 4/3 with L = 46 MeV: -429 MeV <= K_sat,2 <= -281 MeV
- For gamma_sym = 4/3 with L = 111 MeV: -477 MeV <= K_sat,2 <= -289 MeV
- For gamma_sym = 5/3 with L = 46 MeV: -476 MeV <= K_sat,2 <= -298 MeV
- For gamma_sym = 5/3 with L = 111 MeV: -459 MeV <= K_sat,2 <= -241 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of isospin-dependent nuclear matter properties by constructing a flexible phenomenological model that can reproduce results from both the MDI interaction of Das (2003) and the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach with various Skyrme forces. The linear correlations found between K_sym and L, and between J0/K0 and K0, together with empirical constraints from heavy-ion collision data (isospin diffusion analyses using the IBUU04 transport model by Tsang (2004) and Chen (2005)) and from nuclear giant monopole resonances, enable a constrained estimate of the previously uncertain K_sat,2 parameter, which is relevant for understanding the isospin dependence of nuclear incompressibility and for interpreting giant monopole resonance measurements in neutron-rich nuclei.
