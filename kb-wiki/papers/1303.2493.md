# Symmetry energy and nucleon-nucleon cross sections

> The extension of the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck model of nucleus-nucleus collision is presented. The isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross sections are estimated using the proper volume extracted from the equation of state of the nuclear matter transformed into the form of the Van der Waals equation of state. The results of such simulations demonstrate the dependence on symmetry energy which typically varies strongly from the results obtained using only the isospin-dependent mean-field. The evolution of the n/p multiplicity ratio with angle and kinetic energy, in combination with the elliptic flow of neutrons and protons, provides a suitable set of observables for determination of the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The model thus provides an environment for testing of equations of state, used for various applications in nuclear physics and astrophysics.

# Symmetry energy and nucleon-nucleon cross sections

## Key claim
The isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross sections, estimated from the proper volume extracted by transforming the nuclear equation of state into Van der Waals form, produce a dependence on symmetry energy that varies strongly from results obtained using only the isospin-dependent mean field; the combination of n/p multiplicity ratio evolution with angle and kinetic energy together with elliptic flow of neutrons and protons provides a suitable set of observables for determining the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Method
The authors extended the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model by deriving in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections from the proper volume parameter of a Van der Waals equation of state, which was obtained by formally transforming the nuclear equation of state (constructed from a single-particle potential with a symmetry energy term) into Van der Waals form. Local temperature was estimated from the Maxwellian momentum distribution of nucleons in cubic cells of 1 fm side, and the resulting density- and temperature-dependent cross sections were implemented into the collision term of the BUU equation. Simulations were performed for reactions of 48Ca+48Ca at 400 AMeV and for 124Sn+124Sn and 112Sn+112Sn at 400 AMeV at impact parameters of 1 fm and 6 fm, using both soft (K=200 MeV) and stiff (K=380 MeV) equations of state with soft and stiff symmetry energy parametrizations.

## Key numbers
- Soft equation of state compressibility: K = 200 MeV (kappa = 7/6, a*rho0 = -356 MeV, b*rho0^kappa = 303 MeV)
- Stiff equation of state compressibility: K = 380 MeV (kappa = 2, a*rho0 = -124 MeV, b*rho0^kappa = 70.5 MeV)
- Symmetry energy kinetic term parameters: a_s1 = 12.5 MeV, gamma_1 = 2/3; potential term parameters: a_s2 = 17.5 MeV with gamma_2 = 2 (stiff) or gamma_2 = 1/2 (soft)
- Beam energy: 400 AMeV for all reactions
- Impact parameters used: 1 fm and 6 fm
- Temperature for cross section extraction: 20 MeV (Figure 1)
- Fireball temperature estimate from systematics of pre-equilibrium spectra: dotted line in Figure 2
- Stopping time for n/p multiplicity ratio analysis: 200 fm/c
- Density threshold for temperature evaluation: rho0/10
- Prior constraint on symmetry energy from UrQMD: gamma = 0.9 +/- 0.4 (Rus11)

## Context
This work extends the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model approach (BUU1, BUU2) by introducing a method to derive in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections directly from the nuclear equation of state, building on earlier theoretical investigations of density-dependent in-medium cross sections for symmetric nuclear matter (Machleidt, Alm) and on momentum-dependent interaction approaches using reduced nucleonic masses (LiBAnn). The results are compared to the standard parametrization of free nucleon-nucleon cross sections by Cugnon, Mizutani and Vandermeulen (Cugnon) and to G-matrix in-medium cross sections by Cassing et al. (Cassing). The work is motivated by ongoing efforts to determine the density dependence of the symmetry energy (Tsan04, Fami06, Tsan09, Kuma11a, Xiao09, Rus11, Cozm11, Gaut11) and is directly relevant to interpreting results from the FOPI+LAND experiment (Lei93, Lam94) and the proposed new experiment (Lem09) using LAND, CHIMERA, ALADIN, and Krakow telescope detectors. The approach enables testing of equations of state for applications in nuclear physics and astrophysics, particularly for neutron star and supernova studies.
