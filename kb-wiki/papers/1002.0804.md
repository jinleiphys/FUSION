# Hydrodynamics and the Detection of the QCD Axial Anomaly in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We consider the experimental implications of the axial current triangle diagram anomaly in a hydrodynamic description of high density QCD. We propose a signal of an enhanced production of spin-excited hadrons in the direction of the rotation axis in off-central heavy ion collisions.

# Hydrodynamics and the Detection of the QCD Axial Anomaly in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the axial anomaly in QCD hydrodynamics leads to an enhanced production of spin-excited hadrons (specifically narrow resonances such as Ω⁻) along the rotation axis in off-central heavy ion collisions, but the authors emphasize that the precise magnitude requires detailed numerical analysis beyond their linear approximation and that the effect depends on assumptions about the hadronization process that are not yet understood.

## Method
The authors estimate the axial charge density at early stages of heavy ion collisions using a linear expansion of the non-conservation equation for the axial current, with source terms from electromagnetic fields (E·B), vorticity divergence, and magnetic field divergence evaluated using the Glauber model with Woods-Saxon nuclear density distributions. They then relate the axial charge to enhanced production of spin-excited hadrons through a phenomenological model where aligned quark spins from non-zero axial charge can only bind into high-spin hadrons, and propose measuring the ratio of Ω⁻ to Ξ⁻ production in cones along the rotation axis versus the equatorial belt.

## Key numbers
- Chemical potentials: μ_B, μ_I, μ_S ∼ 10 MeV ∼ 0.05 fm⁻¹
- Gold ion parameters: Woods-Saxon radius R₀ = 6.4 fm, surface thickness a₀ = 0.54 fm, mass number A = 197
- Nucleon-nucleon cross section: σ_NN ∼ 40 mb
- Boost factor: γ = 100
- Freeze-out time estimate: Δt ≈ 10⁻²² sec = 30 fm/c
- Anomaly coefficients: C_ABB = 1/(2π²), C_AII = 3/(4π²), C_ASS = 3/(2π²), C_ABS = -1/(2π²), C_Aγγ = 1/π², C_ACC = 3/(4π²)
- Estimated ratio of fermion number to participating nucleons: O(10)
- Estimated ratio of chromo-magnetic to electromagnetic contribution: α_s/α ∼ 100
- Dominant source term (E·B) estimated larger than magnetic divergence term by factor ∼ 25 and larger than vorticity term by factor ∼ ζ·10⁵

## Context
This work extends the theoretical discovery of anomalous hydrodynamic transport coefficients from the gauge/gravity correspondence (Erdmenger 2008, Banerjee 2008, Torabian 2009) and the resolution of the second law of thermodynamics issue (Son 2009) to experimental observables in heavy ion collisions. It contrasts with the existing focus on charge separation effects from the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2007, Fukushima 2008) by proposing a charge-independent signal. The paper enables future numerical solutions of full hydrodynamic equations beyond the Bjorken flow ansatz and modifications to freeze-out distribution functions to incorporate axial charge effects, which could be tested against experimental data from RHIC and LHC.
