# On the discrepancies in the low energy neutron-deuteron breakup

> In view of recent neutron-deuteron (nd) breakup data for neutron-neutron (nn) and neutron-proton (np) quasi-free-scattering (QFS) arrangements and the large discrepancy found between theoretical predictions and measured nn QFS cross sections, we analyze the sensitivity of the QFS cross sections to different partial wave components of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction. We found that the QFS cross section is strongly dominated by the 1S0 and 3S1-3D1 contributions. Because the standard three-nucleon force (3NF) only weakly influence the QFS region, we conjecture, that it must be the nn 1S0 force component which is responsible for the discrepancy in the nn QFS peak. A stronger 1S0 nn force is required to bring theory and data into agreement. Such an increased strength of the nn interaction will, however, not help to explain the nd breakup symmetric-space-star (SST) discrepancy. Further experimental cross-checkings are required.

## Key claim

The authors conjecture that the large discrepancy (≈20%) between theoretical predictions and measured neutron-neutron (nn) quasi-free-scattering (QFS) cross sections in low-energy neutron-deuteron (nd) breakup is caused by an insufficiently strong nn ¹S₀ force component, but they note that such an increased strength would produce an unrealistic nn scattering length and would not resolve the separate symmetric-space-star (SST) discrepancy.

## Method

The authors performed momentum-space partial-wave Faddeev calculations for nd breakup at laboratory energies between 10.3 and 26 MeV, using realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials (AV18, CD Bonn, Nijm1, Nijm2) with and without the 2π-exchange TM99 three-nucleon force (3NF). They systematically varied the nn ¹S₀ force component by multiplying the CD Bonn ¹S₀ nn matrix element by a factor λ to test sensitivity of QFS and SST cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The nn QFS cross section data at E_n^lab = 26 MeV are underestimated by theoretical predictions by ≈20%.
- To remove the 18% discrepancy in nn QFS found in Siepe (2002) would require λ ≈ 1.08, which changes the nn ¹S₀ scattering length to a_nn(λ=1.08) = -135 fm.
- The nd SST cross section discrepancies reach up to ≈30% at E_n^lab = 10.3, 13, and 16 MeV from independent measurements.
- At E_n^lab = 26 MeV, the np QFS cross section data are well described by theory, contrasting with the nn QFS discrepancy.
- The QFS cross section is dominated by ¹S₀ and ³S₁-³D₁ partial waves, with negligible contributions from higher angular momentum states up to two-body total angular momentum j=5.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing discrepancies in low-energy nd breakup identified in the review by Glöckle et al. (1996) and subsequent measurements by Siepe (2002), Ruan (2004), and Lübcke (2005) for nn QFS, and by Strate (1989), Stephan (1997), and others for SST configurations. The authors show that standard 3NF models (e.g., TM99) cannot resolve the nn QFS discrepancy, and that the SST discrepancy remains unexplained even with a modified nn ¹S₀ force. The results call for further experimental cross-checking and suggest that the nn QFS configuration may serve as a tool to study the nn ¹S₀ interaction, though the required modification leads to an unrealistic two-neutron scattering length.
