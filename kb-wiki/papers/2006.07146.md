# A Statistical Description of Nuclear Reaction Models for Medical Radionuclides: the Paradigmatic Case of $^{47}$Sc Production with Thick Vanadium Targets

> We have introduced a tool to describe in a simple and efficient way the outcomes of known nuclear reaction codes. It differs from the customary use where typically a specific single model is selected and the remaining disregarded. The use of simple statistical procedures allows to introduce a more general theoretical evaluation with quantitative uncertainty, constructed on the variability of the built-in theoretical models. We apply the technique to study the production of $^{47}$Sc (a radio-nuclide with potential theranostic applications in nuclear medicine) with a proton beam impinging on a thick natural Vanadium target. We find an energy range with significant production of $^{47}$Sc, and a minimum co-production of $^{46}$Sc, the radioactive contaminant that has to be avoided as much as possible because of its much longer half life than $^{47}$Sc (83.79 d vs 3.3492 d).

## Key claim

The authors propose a "Best Theoretical Evaluation" (BTE) approach that uses the interquartile range of cross sections from multiple TALYS model combinations (pre-equilibrium and level density models) to produce a theoretical prediction with a quantitative uncertainty band, rather than selecting a single default or adjusted model configuration.

## Method

The authors used TALYS version 1.9 with 3 pre-equilibrium models and 6 level density models (18 total combinations, excluding one unphysical pre-equilibrium model) to calculate cross sections and yields for the $^{nat}$V(p,x)$^{47}$Sc reaction. For each energy point, they computed the first quartile (Q1) and third quartile (Q3) across all model results, defined the BTE cross section as (Q1+Q3)/2, and assigned an uncertainty of (Q3-Q1)/2. They applied the same statistical procedure to production yields, activities, and purities.

## Key numbers

- $^{47}$Sc half-life: 3.3492 days; $^{46}$Sc half-life: 83.79 days
- For a $^{nat}$V target of thickness 1080.81 $\mu$m with incoming beam energy 29.5 MeV and outgoing energy 19.9 MeV: $^{47}$Sc production yield = 122 $\pm$ 46 KBq/$\mu$Ah
- Under the same irradiation conditions: $^{46}$Sc production yield = 0.015 $\pm$ 0.006 KBq/$\mu$Ah
- The analysis used 18 model combinations (3 pre-equilibrium models times 6 level density models)

## Context

This work extends the approach of using statistical bands for theoretical model variability, similar to what was done by Lamere et al. (2018), and contrasts with the common practice of selecting a single default or adjusted TALYS configuration as used by Duchemin et al. (2015). The technique enables systematic uncertainty quantification for production routes of medical radionuclides, with applications to scandium, tin, and manganese radionuclides studied within the INFN PASTA project.
