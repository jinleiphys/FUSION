# Weak Response of Nuclear Matter

> The quantitative understanding of neutrino interactions with nuclei and nuclear matter is needed to the study of many different problems. In the astrophysics environment, neutrino-nucleon and neutrino-nucleus reaction rates are used as inputs in the simulations of phenomena like supernov$æ$ explosions and neutron star cooling. In the field of neutrino physics, the quantitative knowledge of neutrino-nucleus cross-section is critical to reduce the systematic uncertainty of the long baseline oscillation experiments. It is important to realize that, while neutrinos interacting in stellar matter typically have energies of the order of few MeV, the energies involved in long baseline oscillations experiments are much larger. For example, K2K experiment takes data in the region $E_ν =0.5-3$ GeV. In this thesis, we describe how nuclear many-body theory provide a scheme allowing for a consistent treatment of neutrino-nucleus interactions at both high and low energies. We will show our predictions of the neutrino-nucleus cross section in the high energy regime and the results of our calculations for the nuclear matter weak response in the low energy regime.

## Key claim

This thesis demonstrates that nuclear many-body theory, using correlated wave functions and an effective interaction derived from the cluster expansion formalism, can provide a consistent treatment of neutrino-nucleus interactions across both high-energy (GeV-scale) and low-energy (MeV-scale) regimes, though the applicability of the impulse approximation at high energies and the treatment of correlations at low energies require distinct formalisms.

## Method

The author employs a nonrelativistic nuclear many-body approach based on correlated wave functions and the cluster expansion formalism, using the Argonne v18 potential (simplified to an eight-operator version) and a three-nucleon interaction. For the high-energy regime, the impulse approximation is used with the spectral function obtained from nuclear many-body theory. For the low-energy regime, an effective interaction derived from correlated wave functions is combined with an effective Fermi transition operator, with correlations treated within the correlated Hartree-Fock and Tamm-Dancoff approximations.

## Context

This work extends the correlated basis function approach originally developed for quantum liquids by Feenberg (1969) and later applied to nuclear matter by various groups using Monte Carlo methods (Wiringa and Pieper, 2002; Carlson et al., 1999) and the Fermi Hypernetted Chain integral equations (Akmal et al., 1998). The effective interaction formalism was recently proposed by Shannon and Benhar (2007) and applied to neutrino mean free path calculations. The thesis aims to provide more realistic neutrino-nucleus cross sections for supernova simulations and long-baseline oscillation experiments, replacing the oversimplified nuclear dynamics used in existing programs (Bruenn, 1985; Horowitz, 2002).
