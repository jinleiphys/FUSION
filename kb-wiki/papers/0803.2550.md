# Origin of resonances in the chiral unitary approach

> We study the origin of the resonances associated with pole singularities of the scattering amplitude in the chiral unitary approach. We propose a "natural renormalization" scheme using the low-energy interaction and the general principle of the scattering theory. We develop a method to distinguish dynamically generated resonances from genuine quark states [Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson (CDD) poles] using the natural renormalization scheme and phenomenological fitting. Analyzing physical meson-baryon scatterings, we find that the Lambda(1405) resonance is largely dominated by the meson-baryon molecule component. In contrast, the N(1535) resonance requires a sizable CDD pole contribution, while the effect of the meson-baryon dynamics is also important.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a "natural renormalization" scheme to distinguish whether resonances in the chiral unitary approach are dynamically generated meson-baryon molecules or require explicit Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson (CDD) pole contributions from genuine quark states, finding that the Lambda(1405) is largely a meson-baryon molecule while the N(1535) requires a sizable CDD pole component.

## Method

The authors develop a natural renormalization scheme by fixing the subtraction constant of the meson-baryon loop function using two theoretical conditions: the loop function must be negative below threshold (consistent with quantum mechanical perturbation theory) and the full scattering amplitude must match the low-energy chiral interaction at a kinematic point. They then compare this natural scheme to phenomenological fits of experimental data; the difference between the phenomenological and natural subtraction constants reveals the presence and size of CDD pole contributions, which can be expressed as an explicit pole term in the effective interaction kernel.

## Key numbers

- The natural subtraction constant is uniquely fixed by requiring G(M_T) = 0, where M_T is the target baryon mass
- For the Kbar-N channel, the natural subtraction constant is calculated using M_T = 939 MeV, m = 496 MeV, and f = 106.95 MeV
- The effective interaction in the natural scheme contains a pole at mass M_eff = M_T - (16 pi^2 f^2)/(C M_T Delta a), where Delta a is the difference between phenomenological and natural subtraction constants
- The leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction is V_WT(sqrt(s)) = -C/(2f^2)[sqrt(s) - M_T], with C being a group theoretical factor

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach developed by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1998), and Oller (2000) for meson-baryon scattering, and addresses the question of resonance origin first raised by Oller (1998) regarding CDD poles in unitarized amplitudes. The method enables systematic classification of baryon resonances as either dynamically generated molecules or quark states, and can be applied to other systems such as the rho meson in pi-pi scattering, which earlier work by Oller (1998) and Pelaez (2003) identified as a CDD pole based on large N_c scaling.
