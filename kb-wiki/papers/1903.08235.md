# Convergence properties of Lévy expansions: implications for Odderon and proton structure

> We detail here the convergence properties of a new model-independent imaging method, the Lévy expansion, that seems to play an important role in the analysis of the differential cross section of elastic hadron-hadron scattering. We demonstrate, how our earlier results concerning the Odderon effects in the differential cross-section of elastic proton-proton and proton-antiproton scattering as well as those related to apparent sub-structures inside the protons were obtained in a convergent and stable manner.

## Key claim
The Lévy expansion method provides a convergent, model-independent imaging technique for elastic hadron-hadron scattering, but the convergence is order-dependent: fourth-order expansions are needed for proton-proton data at TeV energies, while third-order suffices for proton-antiproton data due to limited t-range and statistics.

## Method
The authors fit a Lévy series expansion of the elastic scattering amplitude to differential cross-section data, using orthonormal Lévy polynomials with a stretched-exponential weight function. The expansion coefficients are determined up to fourth order for proton-proton (pp) data from TOTEM and up to third order for proton-antiproton (pbar p) data from D0. Partial sums from zeroth to fourth order are compared to assess convergence of the differential cross-section, elastic slope B(t), shadow profile P(b), and nuclear phase φ₂(t).

## Key numbers
- For pp at √s = 13 TeV, a fourth-order Lévy expansion provides statistically acceptable description of TOTEM data with b₀ fixed to zero (consistent within errors).
- For pp at √s = 7 TeV, fourth-order expansion converges to TOTEM data; the diffractive minimum position and magnitude are correctly captured only at third order.
- For pbar p at √s = 1.96 TeV, third-order Lévy expansion is sufficient for D0 data due to limited t-range and reduced statistics.
- The zeroth-order term fixes the total cross-section at t=0 but is insufficient even at small t; first order describes B(t=0) and the start of the diffractive cone.
- The nuclear phase φ₂(t) at the diffractive minimum equals π, stable between third and fourth order for pp at both 7 TeV and 13 TeV.

## Context
This work extends the Lévy imaging technique introduced by Csörgő et al. (2018) for analyzing elastic hadron scattering, previously applied to TOTEM and D0 data to identify Odderon effects and proton substructures. The convergence analysis addresses a gap in the literature, as prior publications (Csörgő 2018, 2019) summarized results without detailing stability. The method enables model-independent extraction of Odderon signals via differences in B(t) between pp and pbar p reactions, and supports the quark-diquark picture of Bialas and Bzdak (2006) for proton structure at LHC energies.
