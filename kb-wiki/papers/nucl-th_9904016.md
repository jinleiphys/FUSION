# On Studying Charm in Nuclei through Antiproton Annihilation

> We examine the production of open charm in antiproton annihilation on finite nuclei. The enhancement of the subthreshold production cross section, even in a nucleus as light as carbon, should provide a clean signature of the reduction in the masses of these mesons in-medium. We also show that a careful analysis of the $D^+$ and $D^-$ spectra can yield important information on the cross section for $D^{\pm}N$ scattering.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the enhanced subthreshold production of open charm in antiproton annihilation on finite nuclei, even as light as carbon, should provide a clean signature of the reduction of D-meson masses in nuclear matter, but the authors caution that the effect must be disentangled from Fermi motion and multistep production processes through missing-mass reconstruction.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to calculate the in-medium masses and scalar/vector potentials for D⁺ and D⁻ mesons in finite nuclei (¹²C and ¹⁹⁷Au) and in nuclear matter. They then implemented a cascade model for antiproton-nucleus annihilation, using a quark-gluon string model for the elementary \(\bar{p}N \to D\bar{D}\) cross section and assuming the D⁻N elastic cross section equals the K⁺N cross section (20 mb) and the D⁺N inelastic cross section equals the K⁻N → Λπ cross section, scaled to the charm sector.

## Key numbers

- The threshold for the \(\bar{p}N \to D\bar{D}\) reaction in vacuum opens at an antiproton energy around 5.57 GeV.
- At normal nuclear matter density (\(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm⁻³), the threshold reduction for D⁺D⁻ production is around 164 MeV.
- The bare D-meson mass used in the calculations is \(m_D = 1.8693\) GeV.
- The current quark masses used are \(m_q \equiv m_u = m_d = 5\) MeV and \(m_c = 1300\) MeV.
- The bag radius of the nucleon in free space is \(R_N = 0.8\) fm.
- The D⁻N elastic cross section is taken as 20 mb.
- The matrix element for the K⁻N → Λπ transition used to scale the D⁺N cross section is \(|M| = 11.64\) GeV·fm.
- For \(\bar{p}Au\) annihilation at 5 GeV, around 40% of the initial D⁺ mesons are absorbed.

## Context

This work extends the QMC model, previously applied to kaon in-medium properties by Tsushima (1998) and to nuclear matter by Guichon (1996) and Saito (1997), to the charm sector. It builds on the proposal by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1991) that hadron masses are modified in dense matter via changes in the light quark condensates. The paper enables experimental searches for in-medium D-meson mass shifts using antiproton beams at facilities such as FAIR, and provides a method to extract the unknown D⁺N and D⁻N cross sections from the difference in D⁺ and D⁻ momentum spectra.
