# Turbulent meson condensation in quark deconfinement

> In a QCD-like strongly coupled gauge theory at large N_c, using the AdS/CFT correspondence, we find that heavy quark deconfinement is accompanied by a coherent condensation of higher meson resonances. This is revealed in non-equilibrium deconfinement transitions triggered by static, as well as, quenched electric fields even below the Schwinger limit. There, we observe a "turbulent" energy flow to higher meson modes, which finally results in the quark deconfinement. Our observation is consistent with seeing deconfinement as a condensation of long QCD strings.

## Key claim

Using the AdS/CFT correspondence in a large-N_c, strongly coupled gauge theory, the authors find that heavy quark deconfinement is accompanied by a coherent condensation of higher meson resonances, even for electric fields below the Schwinger limit in the case of a time-dependent quench.

## Method

The authors study the D7-brane action in an AdS_5 x S^5 geometry, which is the gravity dual of N=2 supersymmetric QCD. They expand the D7-brane action in fluctuations around a static solution and decompose these fluctuations into an infinite tower of meson eigenmodes, obtaining an effective action for the meson fields. They then solve the full nonlinear D7-brane equations of motion for both static electric fields and time-dependent quenched electric fields, and decompose the resulting solutions into the meson eigenmodes to study the energy distribution across the meson tower.

## Key numbers

- The Schwinger limit for the static electric field is given as E_Sch = 0.5759 m^2, where m is related to the quark mass m_q by m_q = (lambda/2pi^2)^(1/2) m.
- The meson eigenfrequencies are omega_n^2 = 4(n+1)(n+2) m^2 for meson level number n = 0, 1, 2, ...
- For the quenched electric field case, the final field strength is E_f / E_Sch = 0.2672 and the switch-on duration is m Delta V = 2, which leads to deconfinement below the Schwinger limit.
- The time evolution of the meson condensate is shown at times m t = 15, 40, and 49.3, where m t = 49.3 is just before deconfinement.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of dynamical deconfinement transitions (Hashimoto 2014) and connects to the interpretation of deconfinement as condensation of QCD strings, as discussed by Polyakov (1978), Lucini (2005), Hanada (2014), Pisarski (1982), and Patel (1983). The observed turbulent energy transfer to higher meson modes is a probe-brane version of the turbulent instability in AdS geometries found by Bizon and Rostworowski (2011). The results suggest a universal connection between quark deconfinement and higher meson condensation, which could be tested by lattice QCD and may have implications for understanding black hole formation and thermalization in heavy ion collisions.
