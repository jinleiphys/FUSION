# Photoproduction of heavy vector mesons in peripheral $PbPb$ collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> A comprehensive analysis of the photoproduction of $J/Ψ$ and $Υ$ mesons in peripheral $PbPb$ collisions at the center - of - mass energies of the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) is performed, considering distinct assumptions for the modeling of the nuclear photon flux, photon - nucleus cross - section, overlap function and dipole - proton scattering amplitude. The comparison of these predictions with the ALICE data is also performed. Our results indicate that a detailed analysis of the production of both mesons will be very useful to improve the description of photon - induced processes in peripheral collisions.

## Key claim
The photoproduction of J/Ψ and Υ mesons in peripheral PbPb collisions is sensitive to the modeling of the nuclear photon flux, photon-nucleus cross-section, overlap function, and dipole-proton scattering amplitude; a simultaneous analysis of both mesons will be very useful to improve the description of photon-induced processes in peripheral collisions, but the current ALICE data do not yet allow discrimination between the different models.

## Method
The cross-section for vector meson photoproduction in peripheral PbPb collisions was calculated using the equivalent photon approximation, with the nuclear photon flux estimated from the Wood-Saxon charge form factor. The photon-nucleus cross-section was computed in the color dipole formalism using the Glauber-Gribov approach, considering three models for the dipole-proton scattering amplitude (bCGC, IP-SAT, and IPnon-SAT) and two models for the vector meson wave function overlap (Boosted Gaussian and Gaus-LC). Four distinct effective nuclear photon flux models (N⁰, N¹, N², N³) were employed, and the photon-nucleus cross-section was also modified to exclude the overlapping nuclear region.

## Key numbers
- For J/Ψ production at √s = 5.02 TeV, centrality 50%-70%, the average rapidity distribution ⟨dσ/dy⟩ ranges from 183.70 μb (IP-SAT, N² flux, GLC overlap) to 377.76 μb (IPnon-SAT, N¹ flux, BG overlap), compared to the ALICE measurement of 216 ± 10 ± 12 μb.
- For J/Ψ production at √s = 5.02 TeV, centrality 70%-90%, ⟨dσ/dy⟩ ranges from 143.32 μb (bCGC, N⁰ flux, GLC overlap) to 240.04 μb (IPnon-SAT, N¹ flux, BG overlap), compared to the ALICE measurement of 167 ± 6 ± 12 μb.
- For J/Ψ production at √s = 2.76 TeV, centrality 50%-70%, ⟨dσ/dy⟩ ranges from 72.43 μb (IP-SAT, N² flux, GLC overlap) to 195.49 μb (IPnon-SAT, N¹ flux, BG overlap), compared to the ALICE measurement of 58 ± 16⁺⁸₋₁₀ ± 8 μb.
- For Υ production at √s = 5.02 TeV, centrality 50%-70%, ⟨dσ/dy⟩ ranges from 103.82 nb (bCGC, N² flux, GLC overlap) to 564.45 nb (IPnon-SAT, N¹ flux, BG overlap).
- The nuclear radius for lead is R_A = 6.62 fm, with diffuseness a = 0.549 fm and central density ρ₀ = 0.1603 fm⁻³.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of vector meson photoproduction in ultraperipheral collisions (reviewed in UPC reviews) to peripheral collisions, where the impact parameter b is less than R_A + R_B and nuclear overlap occurs. It builds on the approaches of Klusek-Gawenda (2015) and Gay Ducati (2017, 2018) for modeling the effective nuclear photon flux in peripheral collisions, and uses dipole models (bCGC from Kowalski, Motyka, and Watt; IP-SAT from Rezaeian et al.) previously applied to ultraperipheral data. The results enable future experimental discrimination between models through measurements of J/Ψ and Υ production ratios across different centralities and rapidity ranges.
