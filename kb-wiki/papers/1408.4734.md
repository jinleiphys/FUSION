# Determination of freeze-out conditions from fluctuation observables measured at RHIC

> We extract chemical freeze-out conditions via a thermal model approach from fluctuation observables measured at RHIC and compare with results from lattice QCD and statistical hadronization model fits. The possible influence of additional critical and non-critical fluctuation sources not accounted for in our analysis is discussed.

## Key claim

Chemical freeze-out conditions extracted from fluctuation observables measured at RHIC using a thermal model agree well with recent lattice QCD results but deviate significantly from freeze-out parameters deduced via standard statistical hadronization model fits to particle ratios, particularly at high beam energies; the authors note that their analysis assumes fluctuations originate from a primordially equilibrated source and that the influence of critical fluctuations in the considered observables is negligible.

## Method

The authors determined freeze-out temperature T_ch and baryo-chemical potential mu_B,ch as a function of beam energy sqrt(s) by analyzing efficiency-corrected STAR data for sigma^2/M of net-electric charge and net-proton multiplicity distributions for most central collisions, using a thermal model in the grand canonical ensemble that included hadrons and resonances with masses up to 2 GeV/c from the Particle Data Group. Experimentally employed kinematic cuts were applied on the level of primordial hadrons and resonances, resonance decay contributions were included with weak decay feed-down excluded, and the influence of isospin randomizing final state interactions was taken into account for the net-proton analysis.

## Key numbers

* Freeze-out parameters for sqrt(s)=200 GeV were found to be (mu_B,ch, T_ch) = (24.3, 146) MeV from the fluctuation analysis, compared to (24.3, 166) MeV from standard SHM fits (Cleymans 2005).
* The lattice QCD study (Borsanyi 2014) determined an upper limit T_ch <= (148 +/- 4) MeV for the freeze-out temperature.
* For sqrt(s)=200 GeV, the reduction in sigma^2/M for net protons due to exact baryon number conservation was found to be about 1%, within experimental error bars.
* The accepted transverse momentum window in the STAR data (Adamczyk 2014) was 0.2 GeV/c <= p_T <= 2 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using conserved charge fluctuations to probe freeze-out conditions (Karsch 2010, Karsch 2012) and directly compares with a recent lattice QCD determination (Borsanyi 2014) that extracted freeze-out parameters from the same STAR data for the first time. The results contradict the freeze-out parameters obtained from standard statistical hadronization model fits to particle ratios (Cleymans 2005, Andronic 2011) at high beam energies, and the analysis enables future investigations of whether deviations seen in higher-order cumulant ratios at lower beam energies could be of critical origin, possibly related to a critical point in the QCD phase diagram.
