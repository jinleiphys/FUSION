# Properties of infrared extrapolations in a harmonic oscillator basis

> We continue our studies of infrared (ir) and ultraviolet (uv) regulators of no-core shell model calculations. We extend our results that an extrapolation in the ir cutoff with the uv cutoff above the intrinsic uv scale of the interaction is quite successful, not only for the eigenstates of the Hamiltonian but also for expectation values of operators considered long range. The latter results are obtained with Hamiltonians transformed by the similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution. On the other hand, a suggested extrapolation in the uv cutoff when the ir cutoff is below the intrinsic ir scale is neither robust nor reliable.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that extrapolation in the infrared cutoff works reliably for ground-state energies and long-range operator expectation values only when the ultraviolet cutoff is kept above the intrinsic ultraviolet scale of the nuclear interaction; conversely, extrapolation in the ultraviolet cutoff when the infrared cutoff is below the intrinsic infrared scale is neither robust nor reliable.

## Method

The authors perform no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for light nuclei (²H, ³H, ⁶He, ⁶Li) using the Idaho N³LO and JISP16 nucleon-nucleon interactions, which are "smooth" potentials with exponential convergence in the basis truncation parameter. They characterize each finite harmonic oscillator basis by two momentum cutoffs: an ultraviolet cutoff Λ = √[m_N(N+3/2)ħω] and an infrared cutoff λ_sc = √[(m_Nħω)/(N+3/2)], then study the running of ground-state energies as one cutoff is held fixed while the other varies. Extrapolations to the infinite basis limit are performed by fitting an exponential plus constant to the energy as a function of the infrared cutoff λ_sc.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶He with JISP16, extrapolation using the infrared cutoff ħω gives -28.78(50) MeV, while extrapolation using λ_sc gives -28.68(22) MeV; both agree with the hyperspherical harmonics expansion value of -28.70(13) MeV and the traditional extrapolation of -28.69(5) MeV or -28.68(12) MeV from Maris (2009).
- For ³H with Idaho N³LO, six extrapolations with the infrared regulator ħω yield a mean of -7.832 MeV with standard deviation 0.020 MeV, while extrapolations with λ_sc yield a mean of -7.8523 MeV with standard deviation 0.0008 MeV; both agree with the consensus value of -7.855 MeV from a 34-channel Faddeev calculation and -7.854 MeV from a hyperspherical harmonics expansion.
- The intrinsic ultraviolet scale Λ^NN for JISP16 is estimated at 500-550 MeV/c from the behavior of ²H ground-state energy as a function of λ_sc at fixed Λ.
- For ²H with JISP16, when Λ is approximately 712 MeV/c and above, the running of |ΔE/E| versus λ_sc defines a universal curve that can be fit by |ΔE/E| = A exp(-B/λ_sc).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Coon 2012) that introduced extrapolation tools using both ultraviolet and infrared cutoffs on an equal footing, building on the effective field theory framework for NCSM introduced by the EFT-NCSM collaboration. The paper contrasts its approach with the traditional extrapolation in N_max at fixed ħω, which shows strong ħω dependence in the extrapolated ground-state energy (as demonstrated for ⁶Li with the Idaho N³LO potential). The results enable more reliable extrapolations for NCSM calculations using realistic interactions fitted to data, and parallel independent work by Furnstahl, Hagen, and Papenbrock (2012-2014) derived similar infrared extrapolation formulas for coupled-cluster calculations.
