# Stable vortex loops in two-species BECs

> We consider the creation of stable, stationary closed vortex loops, analogue to the vortons and superconducting cosmic strings, in cold atom BEC's. We explore the parameter region where these solutions are likely to exist and comment on methods to create them experimentally.

## Key claim

Stable, stationary closed vortex loops (vortons) can exist in two-species Bose-Einstein condensates when the second species condenses in the core of a vortex loop of the first species and carries non-zero vorticity along the loop, provided the equilibrium radius is large enough to preclude annihilation.

## Method

The authors model the two-species BEC using a mean-field Gross-Pitaevskii Hamiltonian with contact interactions characterized by scattering lengths. They assume the vorton radius R is much larger than its thickness δ, allowing them to compute the energy per unit length of a straight vortex with an internal current of the second species and multiply by the loop circumference. The total energy is minimized numerically with respect to the radial profiles f₁(r), f₂(r), and the radius R, while keeping the number N₂ of species-2 particles fixed, for parameters corresponding to ⁷Li-⁸⁷Rb and ³⁹K-⁸⁷Rb mixtures.

## Key numbers

- For a ⁷Li-⁸⁷Rb vorton with l=5, n₁=4×10¹³ cm⁻³, N₂=2×10⁴, a₁=100 a₀, a₂=40 a₀, a₁₂=5000 a₀: R/δ = 2.3, R = 7.9 μm
- For the same system at n₁=4×10¹² cm⁻³: R/δ = 2.5, R = 19 μm
- For the same system with N₂=2×10³: R/δ = 2.5, R = 4.4 μm
- For a ³⁹K-⁸⁷Rb vorton (M₁/M₂=2.12) with l=5, n₁=4×10¹³ cm⁻³, N₂=2×10⁴: R/δ = 1.7, R = 5.3 μm
- The healing length for ⁸⁷Rb at n₁=4×10¹³ cm⁻³ is estimated at 0.45 μm, while the numerical δ = 3.4 μm, giving a ratio of roughly 7.5
- For a Li-Rb mixture with equal densities (10¹³/cc) and R ≈ 10 μm with R/δ ≈ 2.5, N₂ is estimated to be on the order of 10⁴
- The Kibble-Zurek mechanism estimate for vorticity in an equal-density Rb/Li mixture gives l ≈ 7

## Context

This work extends the concept of vortons and superconducting cosmic strings from relativistic field theory (Davis 1989, Witten 1984) to cold atom BECs, where they have never been observed experimentally. The stabilization mechanism, where a second species condensing in the vortex core provides a kinetic energy term that scales as l²/R, balancing the tension term T R, was previously studied in relativistic contexts by Davis (1988) and Kaplan (2001) for quark matter. The paper enables experimental searches for vortons in existing two-species BEC traps, suggesting two creation methods: Raman scattering with Gauss-Laguerre beams (based on a suggestion by J.V. Porto) and the Kibble-Zurek mechanism previously demonstrated for single-species vortices in ⁸⁷Rb by Weiler (2008).
