# Radial and angular-momentum Regge trajectories: a systematic approach

> We present the analysis of Ref.[1] of the radial (n) and angular-momentum (J) Regge trajectories for all light-quark meson states listed in the Particle Data Tables. The parameters of the trajectories are obtained with linear regression, with weight of each resonance inversely proportional to its half-width squared, (Γ/2)^2. The joint analysis in the (n,J,M^2) Regge plane indicates, at the 4.5 standard deviation level, that the slopes in n are larger from the slopes in J. Thus no strict universality of slopes occurs in the light non-strange meson sector. We also extend our analysis to the kaon sector.

## Key claim

The joint analysis of radial and angular-momentum Regge trajectories for light unflavored mesons shows, at the 4.5 standard deviation level, that the slopes in the radial quantum number n are larger than the slopes in the angular momentum J, contradicting the strict universality of slopes assumed in prior work.

## Method

The authors performed linear regression fits to the squared masses of all light-quark meson states listed in the Particle Data Tables, using the half-width rule: each resonance was weighted by the inverse of its half-width squared, (Γ/2)². Separate fits were made for radial trajectories (mass squared versus radial quantum number n) and angular-momentum trajectories (mass squared versus J) for each meson family, and a joint fit was also performed using the formula M²(n,J) = M²(0,0) + n μ² + J β². The analysis was extended to the kaon sector for families with more than three states.

## Key numbers

- Weighted average slope for radial trajectories across all light unflavored meson families: μ² = 1.35(4) GeV²
- Weighted average slope for angular-momentum trajectories across all light unflavored meson families: β² = 1.16(4) GeV²
- Joint fit result for light unflavored mesons: M²(n,J) = (-1.25(4) + 1.38(4) n + 1.12(4) J) GeV², with a difference between slopes at 4.5 standard deviations
- Radial slope for kaon sector: μ²_K = 1.22(21) GeV² for K mesons and 1.12(21) GeV² for K* mesons
- Angular-momentum slope for kaon sector: β²_K = 1.36(6) GeV² for K mesons and 1.19(7) GeV² for K* mesons

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Anisovich (2000), who suggested radial Regge trajectories with slope μ² = 1.25(15) GeV², and contradicts the universal-slope assumption of Afonin (2006), who proposed M² = b + a(n + J) with a = 1.14 GeV². The results enable more precise tests of Regge theory in the light meson sector and provide a systematic benchmark for future studies of meson spectroscopy and quark-model predictions.
