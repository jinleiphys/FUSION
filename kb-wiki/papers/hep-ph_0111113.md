# A remark on non-Abelian classical kinetic theory

> It is known that non-Abelian classical kinetic theory reproduces the Hard Thermal/Dense Loop (HTL/HDL) effective action of QCD, obtained after integrating out the hardest momentum scales from the system, as well as the first higher dimensional operator beyond the HTL/HDL level. We discuss here its applicability at still higher orders, by comparing the exact classical effective action obtained in the static limit, with the 1-loop quantum effective potential. We remark that while correct types of operators arise, the classical colour algebra reproduces correctly the prefactor of the 4-point function $tr A_0^4$ only for matter in asymptotically high dimensional colour representations.

## Key claim

Non-Abelian classical kinetic theory reproduces the correct quadratic and cubic terms of the 1-loop quantum effective action for static gauge fields, but the classical colour algebra fails at the quartic level; the prefactor of the $\tr A_0^4$ term matches quantum field theory only for matter in asymptotically high dimensional colour representations.

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann equation with Wong's colour dynamics exactly for static background gauge fields, using an ansatz where the distribution function depends only on canonical momenta. They then computed the induced gauge current and integrated to obtain the effective action, comparing the colour traces $\int_Q Q^{a_1}\cdots Q^{a_n}$ from the classical colour measure with the symmetric parts of quantum traces $\tr T^{a_1}\cdots T^{a_n}$ for $n=0$ through $4$. For the "second formulation" based on moment equations, they solved iteratively to third order for constant $A_0$.

## Key numbers

- For SU(2), the classical colour integral gives $\int_Q A_0^4 = \frac{1}{5} d_R\, C_2^2(R) (A_0^a A_0^a)^2$, where $d_R$ is the representation dimension and $C_2(R)$ the quadratic Casimir.
- The quantum result for SU(2) is $\tr A_0^4 = \frac{1}{5} d_R \, C_2(R) (C_2(R)-\frac{1}{3}) (A_0^a A_0^a)^2$.
- For the "second formulation" with constant $A_0$, the classical result is too large by a factor of three for SU(2).
- The fermionic contribution to the effective Lagrangian density includes terms such as $\frac{g^2}{2} (\frac{T^2}{3} + \frac{\mu^2}{\pi^2}) A_0^2$ and $\frac{g^4}{12\pi^2} A_0^4$ (with colour integrals factored out).

## Context

This work extends the known result that classical kinetic theory reproduces the Hard Thermal/Dense Loop (HTL/HDL) effective action and the first higher-dimensional operator (a charge-conjugation-violating three-gluon interaction found by Blaizot and Iancu (2001)). It contradicts the expectation that the classical approach would continue to match quantum field theory at higher orders, showing a discrepancy at the quartic level for finite-dimensional colour representations. The finding motivates development of a more elaborate effective description for real-time dynamics in QCD plasma, analogous to the dimensionally reduced theory for static observables, and highlights the need for proper renormalisation scale fixing and continuum limit treatment as discussed by Braaten and Nieto (1996) and by Bodeker (2000).
