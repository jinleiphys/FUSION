# Propagation of Strange Quark Matter in the Atmosphere

> We propose model of propagation of lumps of Strange Quark Matter (strangelets) through the atmosphere, which accounts for their apparent strong penetrability and normal nuclear-type sizes at the same time. The mass spectrum of strangelets reaching the Earth predicted by this model agrees very well with the existing data on the abundance of different elemets in the Universe.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that strangelets (lumps of strange quark matter) observed deep in the atmosphere are not intrinsically highly penetrating objects with small geometrical sizes, but instead are the remnants of much heavier initial strangelets that fragment through successive collisions with air nuclei, thereby reconciling their apparent strong penetrability with normal nuclear-type sizes.

## Method

The authors model the propagation of strangelets through the atmosphere by considering two extreme collision scenarios with air nuclei (mean mass number A_t = 14.5): a "standard model" where the strangelet loses at most A_t baryons per collision, and a "tube-like model" where it loses A^(1/3) * A_t^(2/3) baryons per collision. They compute the mass number A(h) as a function of atmospheric depth h, and use experimental flux measurements at various depths to reconstruct the initial mass spectrum of strangelets entering the atmosphere.

## Key numbers

- Critical mass number for strangelet stability against neutron evaporation: A_crit ~ 300–400 (taken as 320 in numerical calculations)
- Interaction mean free path for strangelets reported in prior work: λ_S = 124 g/cm² for A = 100, decreasing to λ_S = 59 g/cm² for A = 1000 (compared to λ_proton = 60–70 g/cm² and λ_nucleus ≈ 3.8 g/cm² for A = 100)
- Rescaled radius r_0 for strangelets with m ≈ 150 MeV and μ ≈ 300 MeV is comparable to the normal nuclear value r_0 = 1.12 fm (corresponding to density ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³)
- Energy per baryon in strange matter: ε = 919 MeV; strangelet starts emitting neutrons when dE/dA exceeds m_N at A ≤ 320
- Observed candidate strangelet mass numbers: A ≈ 350 and 450 (Saito event), A ≈ 460 (Exotic Track), A > 1000 (Price event), ~200 baryons (Centauro event)
- Atmospheric depth for Exotic Track: ~200 g/cm²; Centauro events observed at ~500 g/cm²
- Initial strangelet mass numbers required: A_0 = 900 to reach depth 200 g/cm²; A_0 = 1800 to reach Centauro altitudes
- Best-fit power law for initial flux: A_0^(-7.5) for standard model; A_0^(-2.8) for tube-like model

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of cosmic ray anomalies such as Centauro events (Lattes 1973) and the Exotic Track event (Ichimura 1993) by challenging the prevailing view from JACEE (1993) and Concorde (Capdevielle 1994) that strangelets must have anomalously small geometrical cross sections. Instead, the authors argue that strangelets have normal nuclear densities, consistent with the theoretical framework of strange quark matter (Witten 1984, Farhi and Jaffe 1984), and that their deep penetration arises from fragmentation of much heavier primary strangelets. The predicted mass spectrum matches the observed abundance of normal elements in the Universe, suggesting a common fragmentation mechanism.
