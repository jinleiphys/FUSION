# Chiral Corrections to Baryon Masses Calculated within Lattice QCD

> Consideration of the analytic properties of pion-induced baryon self energies leads to new functional forms for the extrapolation of light baryon masses. These functional forms reproduce the leading non-analytic behavior of chiral perturbation theory, the correct non-analytic behavior at the $N π$ threshold and the appropriate heavy-quark limit. They involve only three unknown parameters, which may be obtained by fitting lattice QCD data. Recent dynamical fermion results from CP-PACS and UKQCD are extrapolated using these new functional forms. We also use these functions to probe the limit of applicability of chiral perturbation theory.

# Chiral Corrections to Baryon Masses Calculated within Lattice QCD

## Key claim

The paper proposes new functional forms for extrapolating lattice QCD baryon masses from high pion masses down to the physical pion mass, which correctly incorporate both the leading non-analytic behavior of chiral perturbation theory and the branch point at the Nπ threshold, but the authors acknowledge that model-independent fits require lattice data at pion masses approaching mπ² ~ 0.1 GeV², and that the cut-off parameter Λ may need to be constrained by external models when such light quark masses are unavailable.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for pion-induced self energies of the nucleon and delta baryons using a sharp cut-off form factor, incorporating contributions from NN, NΔ, ΔN, and ΔΔ intermediate states. They fitted these functional forms to two-flavor dynamical fermion lattice QCD results from CP-PACS and UKQCD, allowing the physical scale to float within 10% systematic errors. They compared their fits to conventional polynomial expansions including an mπ³ term, both with the coefficient fixed to the chiral perturbation theory value and with it as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- The leading non-analytic coefficient for the mπ³ term in chiral perturbation theory is -5.60, while a three-parameter polynomial fit to lattice data yields -0.761, differing by almost an order of magnitude
- The branch point in the NΔ self energy occurs at mπ = ΔM ≈ 300 MeV, beyond which chiral perturbation theory breaks down
- Model-independent fits require lattice simulations at pion masses approaching mπ² ~ 0.1 GeV²
- The self energy contributions vanish at least as fast as 1/mπ² in the heavy quark (large mπ) limit

## Context

This work extends the approach of Leinweber (1999) by applying analyticity arguments and heavy quark effective theory insights to derive functional forms for baryon mass extrapolation that go beyond the chiral perturbation theory regime. The method successfully describes predictions from the Cloudy Bag Model (Thomas, 1984) and recent dynamical fermion lattice QCD calculations from CP-PACS and UKQCD. The curvature around mπ = ΔM neglected in previous extrapolations leads to shifts in extrapolated masses of the same order as the departure of lattice estimates from experimental measurements, enabling more reliable connections between lattice QCD results and physical baryon masses.
