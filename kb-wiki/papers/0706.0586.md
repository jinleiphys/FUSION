# Extended Optical Model Analyses of Elastic Scattering and Fusion Cross Section Data for the 7Li+208Pb System at Near-Coulomb-Barrier Energies using the Folding Potential

> Simultaneous $χ^{2}$ analyses previously made for elastic scattering and fusion cross section data for the $^{6}$Li+$^{208}$Pb system is extended to the $^{7}$Li+$^{208}$Pb system at near-Coulomb-barrier energies based on the extended optical model approach, in which the polarization potential is decomposed into direct reaction (DR) and fusion parts. Use is made of the double folding potential as a bare potential. It is found that the experimental elastic scattering and fusion data are well reproduced without introducing any normalization factor for the double folding potential and that both the DR and fusion parts of the polarization potential determined from the $χ^{2}$ analyses satisfy separately the dispersion relation. Further, we find that the real part of the fusion portion of the polarization potential is attractive while that of the DR part is repulsive except at energies far below the Coulomb barrier energy. A comparison is made of the present results with those obtained from the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculations and a previous study based on the conventional optical model with a double folding potential. We also compare the present results for the $^7$Li+$^{208}$Pb system with the analysis previously made for the $^{6}$Li+$^{208}$Pb system.

## Key claim

The experimental elastic scattering and fusion data for 7Li+208Pb are well reproduced without introducing any normalization factor for the double folding potential, and both the direct reaction (DR) and fusion parts of the polarization potential satisfy the dispersion relation separately. However, the real part of the DR polarization potential is repulsive only at energies above approximately 29 MeV, becoming attractive between 19 MeV and 29 MeV, and the repulsiveness is considerably weaker than that previously found for the 6Li+208Pb system.

## Method

Simultaneous chi-squared analyses were performed on elastic scattering angular distributions, experimental fusion cross sections, and semi-experimental DR cross sections for the 7Li+208Pb system at near-Coulomb-barrier energies. The optical potential was constructed as a sum of a double folding bare potential (using the M3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction) and a complex polarization potential decomposed into volume-type fusion and surface-derivative-type DR components, with fixed geometrical parameters. The strength parameters of the DR and fusion potentials were determined in a two-step fitting procedure, and the real parts were subsequently generated from the imaginary parts via the dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

- The interaction distance d_I is 1.8 fm for 7Li+208Pb, compared to 1.9 fm for 6Li+208Pb.
- The threshold energy for DR is E_0,D^(semi-exp) = 19.3 MeV; the threshold energy for fusion is E_0,F^(exp) = 26.5 MeV.
- The imaginary DR strength W_D(E) is parameterized as 0.075(E-19.3) MeV for 19.3 < E <= 29.3 MeV, saturating at 0.75 MeV above 29.3 MeV.
- The imaginary fusion strength W_F(E) is parameterized as 0.588(E-26.5) MeV for 26.5 < E <= 29.9 MeV, saturating at 2.00 MeV above 29.9 MeV.
- The semi-experimental DR cross sections for 7Li+208Pb are smaller than those for 6Li+208Pb by a factor of 1.23 to 1.72.
- At the strong absorption radius R_sa = 12.4 fm, the contribution from the fusion part to the total imaginary potential is less than 6% for 7Li+208Pb and less than 15% for 6Li+208Pb.
- The contribution from the inner imaginary potential W_I to the total calculated fusion cross section ranges from 22% to 46%.

## Context

This work extends the simultaneous chi-squared analysis previously performed by So, Udagawa, Kim, Hong, and Kim (2007) for the 6Li+208Pb system to the 7Li+208Pb system. It contrasts with the conventional optical model study by Keeley et al. (1994), which required a normalization factor N approximately 0.5-0.6 for 6Li but N approximately 1 for 7Li, and with CDCC calculations by Keeley and Rusek (1998) that derived polarization potentials from breakup channel couplings. The results show that the energy dependence of the optical potential observed in the earlier conventional analysis by Keeley et al. (1994) is actually due to the DR potential rather than a genuine fusion threshold anomaly, and the present approach enables separate determination of the fusion and DR polarization potentials that satisfy the dispersion relation independently.
