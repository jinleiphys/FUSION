# Shear viscosity of rotating, hot, and dense spin-half fermionic systems from quantum field theory

> In this study, we calculate the shear viscosity for rotating fermions with spin-half under conditions of high temperature and density. We employ the Kubo formalism, rooted in finite-temperature quantum field theory, to compute the field correlation functions essential for this evaluation. The one-loop diagram pertinent to shear viscosity is analyzed within the context of curved space, utilizing tetrad formalism as an effective approach in cylindrical coordinates. Our findings focus on extremely high angular velocities, ranging from 0.1 to 1 GeV, which align with experimental expectations. Furthermore, we explore the inter-relationship between the chemical potential and angular velocity within the scope of this study.

## Key claim

The paper calculates shear viscosity for rotating spin-half fermions at high temperature and density using Kubo formalism, finding that both temperature and angular velocity increase shear viscosity while chemical potential reduces it; however, the authors explicitly restrict their analysis to extremely high angular velocities (0.1 to 1 GeV) where translational symmetry of the propagator is restored, and they note that chemical potential and angular velocity exert opposite effects on shear dissipation despite being functionally similar.

## Method

The authors employ the Kubo formalism within finite-temperature quantum field theory, computing the retarded two-point correlation function of the shear stress tensor at one-loop order. They model rotation using curved spacetime with tetrad formalism in cylindrical coordinates, deriving a momentum-space fermion propagator via the Fock-Schwinger method that incorporates angular velocity. The spectral function is obtained through analytic continuation of Matsubara frequency sums using the Saclay method, with a Breit-Wigner thermal width introduced to handle the Landau cuts that contribute to transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Angular velocity range considered: 0.1 to 1 GeV, described as "extremely high angular velocities" that align with experimental expectations
- The angular momentum in off-center heavy-ion collisions is cited as 10^3 to 10^5 ħ, based on STAR collaboration observations
- The supervortical temperature is given as 1.5 times the critical temperature for rotating QCD matter, from lattice SU(3) gauge theory results

## Context

This work extends the study of transport coefficients in rotating QCD matter, building on prior Kubo formula derivations for first-order spin hydrodynamics by Hu (2021) using Zubarev's non-equilibrium statistical operator formalism. The authors note that rotation induces background torsion similar to how magnetic fields break translational symmetry via the Schwinger phase factor, as studied by Kuznetsov (2004) and Ayala (2021). The results enable hydrodynamic simulations of rotating QGP in off-central heavy-ion collisions, particularly for understanding the interplay between angular velocity and chemical potential in fermionic systems.
