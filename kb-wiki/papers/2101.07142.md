# $α$-decay half-lives of superheavy nuclei with $Z=122-125$

> For $α$ decay half-life calculations in this work, the Coulomb and proximity potential model with a new semiempirical formula for diffuseness parameter developed in previous work [Phys. Rev. C 100, 024601 (2019)] is used. The present model in this work is compared with the generalized liquid-drop model (GLDM), universal decay law (UDL), and experimental half-lives in the region $Z=104-118$. Next, the predicted half-lives of 51 superheavy nuclei (SHN) with $Z=122-125$ by the present model are compared with those of GLDM, and UDL. The present model is revealed to be more accurate in reproducing experimental half-lives compared to GLDM and UDL. Moreover, it is found that the predictions of the present model and UDL are highly consistent while GLDM largely deviates from the other two. A study of the competition between $α$ decay and spontaneous fission (SF) shows that $α$ decay is the dominant mode. Among the studied SHN with $Z=122-125$, ${}^{295-307}122$ and ${}^{314-320}125$ are identified as potential candidates whose half-lives are relatively long enough to be experimentally detected in the future through their $α$-decay chains. The identified candidates are in good agreement with other recent work.

## Key claim

The Coulomb and proximity potential model with a new semiempirical formula for diffuseness parameter reproduces experimental alpha-decay half-lives for superheavy nuclei with Z=104-118 more accurately than the generalized liquid-drop model (GLDM) and universal decay law (UDL), and its predictions for Z=122-125 are highly consistent with UDL while GLDM deviates significantly due to its use of a constant diffuseness parameter of 0.99 fm.

## Method

The author used the Coulomb and proximity potential model with a semiempirical formula for the effective diffuseness parameter a_eff = -1.09535 + 0.012063Z + 0.0019759N, developed in prior work (Nagib and Abdul-latif, 2019). Half-lives were calculated using the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) approximation with a preformation factor dependent on magic numbers. The model was compared statistically against GLDM and UDL using experimental half-lives for nuclei with Z=104-118, then used to predict half-lives for 51 superheavy nuclei with Z=122-125, with competition between alpha decay and spontaneous fission evaluated via branching ratios.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation from experimental half-lives: Prox model 0.41, UDL 0.641, GLDM 0.714 (for nuclei with Z=104-118)
- Maximum error in log10(half-life): Prox model 0.926 (off by factor of about 8), UDL 2.671 (off by factor of 470), GLDM 3.197 (off by three orders of magnitude)
- For Z=122-125, the effective diffuseness parameter b_eff ranges from 1.06 fm to 1.17 fm, while GLDM uses a constant 0.99 fm
- Root-mean-square deviation between model predictions for Z=122-125: Prox/UDL 0.347, GLDM/UDL 0.969, Prox/GLDM 1.006
- Four superheavy nuclei out of 51 studied (309-310122, 311123, and 312124) have log10(branching ratio) less than or equal to zero, indicating they will not survive fission
- Candidates for experimental detection: 295-307122 and 314-320125, with half-lives of at least 100 ns

## Context

This work extends the Coulomb and proximity potential model with a variable diffuseness parameter previously developed by Nagib and Abdul-latif (2019) for 218 superheavy nuclei. It compares this model against the generalized liquid-drop model (GLDM) and universal decay law (UDL) for the first time in the Z=122-125 region, and identifies potential candidates for experimental detection that agree with recent work by Santhosh and colleagues (2020). The results enable future experiments to target specific superheavy nuclei for synthesis and detection through their alpha-decay chains.
