# A variational approach to the partition function of an interacting many body system

> For the calculation of the partition function $\mathcal{Z}$ of small, isolated and interacting many body systems an improvement with respect to previous formulations is presented. By including anharmonicities and employing a variational approach quantum effects can be treated even at very low temperatures. In order to test its accuracy the novel approach is applied to the exactly solvable Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model (LMGM). For thermodynamic properties and level densities good agreement with the exact calculations is found.

# A variational approach to the partition function of an interacting many body system

## Key claim

The paper presents a variational method for calculating the partition function of small, isolated interacting many-body systems that improves upon previous formulations by including anharmonic terms up to fourth order, enabling treatment of quantum effects even at very low temperatures. However, the specific heat exhibits some unphysical features at very low temperatures due to truncation of the reference action, and the free energy becomes smaller than the exact result at low temperatures, violating the variational bound when the action is truncated.

## Method

The authors start from the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation of the partition function and expand the Euclidean action around the static path in Fourier components of the auxiliary field. They introduce a reference action with variational parameters replacing the local RPA frequencies, then apply the Jensen-Peierls inequality to obtain an upper bound for the effective free energy, which is minimized with respect to the variational parameters. The method is tested on the exactly solvable Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model (LMGM) with a single collective mode, using up to 100 Fourier coefficients and truncating the action at fourth order in the fluctuating coordinates.

## Key numbers

- Effective coupling constant defined as κ = |k|g/ε, with g = 10 and ε = 5 MeV used for calculations
- Inverse crossover temperature for the PSPA breakdown: β₀ = 1.139 MeV⁻¹ for κ = 1.313
- Inverse critical temperature: β_crit = 0.4 MeV⁻¹ for κ = 1.313
- At β = 2.0 MeV⁻¹ (≈ 1.8 β₀), clear deviations between ePSPA and variational approach are visible
- Relative error of the variational approach at very low temperatures is of the order of 1% for the free energy
- The PSPA is applicable only for β < β₀ = 1.139 MeV⁻¹, while the variational approach works down to T = 0

## Context

This work extends the Static Path Approximation (SPA) and Perturbed Static Path Approximation (PSPA) methods developed by Alhassid et al. (1997) and others, which break down at a crossover temperature T₀ when the RPA frequency becomes imaginary. The approach is inspired by the Feynman-Kleinert variational method developed by Giachetti and Feynman and Kleinert (1986) for a particle in a one-dimensional potential. The method enables calculation of thermodynamic properties and level densities for interacting many-body systems at arbitrarily low temperatures, though the truncation of the action at fourth order introduces artifacts that could be resolved by including higher-order anharmonic terms.
