# Ab initio calculations of low-energy nuclear scattering using confining potential traps

> A recently modified method to enable low-energy nuclear scattering results to be extracted from the discrete energy levels of the target-projectile clusters confined by harmonic potential traps is tested. We report encouraging results for neutron--$α$ and neutron--$^{24}\mathrm{O}$ elastic scattering from analyzing the trapped levels computed using two different ab initio nuclear structure methods. The $n$--$α$ results have also been checked against a direct ab initio reaction calculation. The $n$--$^{24}\mathrm{O}$ results demonstrate the approach's applicability for a large range of systems provided their spectra in traps can be computed by ab initio methods. A key ingredient is a rigorous understanding of the errors in the calculated energy levels caused by inevitable Hilbert-space truncations in the ab initio methods.

# Ab initio calculations of low-energy nuclear scattering using confining potential traps

## Key claim
The method of extracting low-energy nuclear scattering phase shifts from discrete energy levels of projectile-target clusters confined in harmonic potential traps can produce results consistent with direct ab initio reaction calculations for neutron-alpha scattering, and can be extended to larger systems like neutron-24O where no direct ab initio treatment exists, provided the Hilbert-space truncation errors in the ab initio energy levels are rigorously understood and modeled.

## Method
The authors used two ab initio nuclear structure methods, the no-core shell model (NCSM) and the valence-space formulation of the in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG), to compute discrete energy levels of neutron-alpha and neutron-24O clusters confined in harmonic potential traps with varying trap frequencies. They then applied a modified quantization condition that relates the trapped eigenenergies to a generalized effective range expansion, modeling the regulator-induced errors from Hilbert-space truncation using a two-body model with a cutoff on radial excitation quanta. Bayesian inference was used to constrain the model parameters and extract phase shifts from the binned data sets.

## Key numbers
- For neutron-alpha scattering, the extracted low-energy phase shifts from both NCSM and IMSRG methods agree with direct NCSMC calculations within uncertainties; the NCSMC phase-shift uncertainty was estimated to be about 5%.
- For neutron-24O scattering, the extracted phase shift indicates (with 75% probability) a shallow bound state with binding energy at -1.4 ± 0.5 MeV, while experimental information indicates a resonance at 0.75 MeV with a width about 90 keV.
- By increasing the C_{0,0} parameter by 0.277 in the generalized effective range expansion at a UV cutoff of 950 MeV, the calculation produces a resonance at 0.75 MeV with a 135 keV width, compatible with experimental information.
- The UV cutoff scales used were 900 MeV for neutron-alpha and 950 MeV for neutron-24O, where the extracted coefficients appear to converge.
- The expansion convergence radius is E ≤ 22 MeV for neutron-alpha and E ≤ 4 MeV for neutron-24O, determined by the targets' lowest excitation energies.

## Context
This work extends the method of confining potential traps for extracting scattering observables, which was recently improved by Zhang (2019) to allow systematic control of theory errors. The approach is similar in spirit to the Luscher method used in Lattice QCD, which extracts hadronic scattering observables from energy spectra discretized by a spatial box with periodic boundary conditions. The results enable ab initio calculations of low-energy scattering for medium-mass nuclei where direct reaction methods like the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) are computationally infeasible, and provide a unified framework to treat continuum physics and shallow bound states as needed in low-energy nuclear physics.
