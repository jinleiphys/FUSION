# Truncation scheme of time-dependent density-matrix approach III

> The time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM) where the Bogoliubov-Born-Green-Kirkwood-Yvon hierarchy for reduced density matrices is truncated by approximating a three-body density matrix with one-body and two-body density matrices is applied to the Lipkin model. It is shown that in the large $N$ limit the ground state in TDDM approaches the exact solution. Various truncation schemes for the three-body density matrix are also tested for an extended three-level Lipkin model.

# Truncation scheme of time-dependent density-matrix approach III

## Key claim

The time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM), which neglects the three-body correlation matrix entirely, solves the two-level Lipkin model exactly in the large N (thermodynamic) limit when working in the symmetry-unbroken (spherical) basis; however, for the three-level Lipkin model the three-body correlation matrix cannot be neglected even in the large N limit, and a modified truncation scheme (TDDM1-b) that includes only the pph-pph type three-body correlation matrix gives good agreement with exact ground-state energies.

## Method

The authors apply the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM) and its extensions (TDDM1, TDDM2, and TDDM1-b) to the two-level and three-level Lipkin models. These approaches truncate the BBGKY hierarchy at the two-body level by approximating the three-body correlation matrix using either neglect (TDDM), a perturbative cumulant expansion (TDDM1), a normalized cumulant expansion (TDDM2), or a selective inclusion of only the pph-pph type three-body correlation matrix (TDDM1-b). Ground states are obtained using the adiabatic method starting from the non-interacting spherical Hartree-Fock state, and excitation energies are computed from the TDDM equations of motion.

## Key numbers

- For the two-level Lipkin model with N=12, TDDM2 improves the ground-state energy over TDDM and TDDM1 across the transition region around chi=1, where chi=|V|(N-1)/epsilon.
- For the two-level Lipkin model with N=50, TDDM ground-state energies agree well with exact solutions, and DHF energies also become close to exact values including the transition region.
- For the two-level Lipkin model with N=200, TDDM gives the almost exact ground state; the first excited state excitation energy decreases with increasing interaction strength beyond chi=1, and TDDM does not give an oscillating solution for chi>1.073.
- For the three-level Lipkin model with N=20, TDDM1-b ground-state energies agree well with exact values (dots) across the range of chi shown, while TDDM strongly overestimates two-body correlations.
- For the three-level Lipkin model with N=200, TDDM1-b results agree with exact values at least up to chi=10; DHF gives a good description of the ground-state energy but TDDM still strongly overestimates two-body correlations.
- The three-body correlation matrix element C_{11,02,13,11,12,03} in the three-level Lipkin model is evaluated as (1/8) cos^2 alpha sin^4 alpha using the DHF wavefunction.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigations of truncation schemes for the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM), specifically TDDM1 (Tohyama and Schuck, 2014) and TDDM2 (Tohyama and Schuck, 2017). The two-level Lipkin model (Lipkin, 1965) serves as a benchmark where TDDM becomes exact in the thermodynamic limit, while the three-level Lipkin model (Li, 1983) provides a more realistic test because it has more unoccupied states than occupied states and has been used to test extended mean-field theories (Holzwarth, 1977; Hagino, 2002; Delion, 2013). The findings enable improved treatment of three-body correlations in nuclear many-body systems where the simple neglect of the three-body correlation matrix fails, particularly for models with multiple unoccupied levels.
