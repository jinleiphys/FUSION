# Transverse Energy-Energy Correlator for Vector Boson-Tagged Hadron Production in $pp$ and $pA$ collisions

> We investigate the transverse energy-energy correlator (TEEC) event-shape observable for back-to-back $γ+ h$ and $Z + h$ production in both $pp$ and $pA$ collisions. Our study incorporates nuclear modifications into the transverse-momentum dependent (TMD) factorization framework, with resummation up to next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy, for TEEC as a function of the variable $τ= \left(1 + \cosϕ \right)/2$, where $ϕ$ is the azimuthal angle between the vector boson and the final hadron. We analyze the nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$ in $p\mathrm{Au}$ collisions at RHIC and $p\mathrm{Pb}$ collisions at the LHC. Our results demonstrate that the TEEC observable is a sensitive probe for nuclear modifications in TMD physics. Specifically, the changes in the $τ$-distribution shape provide insights into transverse momentum broadening effects in large nuclei, while measurements at different rapidities allow us to explore nuclear modifications in the collinear component of the TMD parton distribution functions in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the transverse energy-energy correlator (TEEC) for vector boson-tagged hadron production is a sensitive probe of nuclear modifications in transverse-momentum dependent (TMD) physics, specifically through changes in the τ-distribution shape that reveal transverse momentum broadening effects in large nuclei, though the universality of the nuclear broadening parameters across different observables remains an assumption that future measurements will test.

## Method

The authors compute the TEEC observable for back-to-back γ+h and Z+h production in pp and pA collisions using TMD factorization with resummation up to next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy. They incorporate nuclear modifications by substituting nuclear collinear PDFs (EPPS16 set) for vacuum PDFs and introducing a modified non-perturbative parameter g_{1,A}^q = g_1^q + a_N (A^{1/3} - 1) for TMD PDFs, with a_N = 0.016 GeV^2 fitted from SIDIS and Drell-Yan data. The TEEC jet function is constructed from TMD fragmentation functions using pion FFs (DSS 2021 parameterization) with a fitted non-perturbative function S_NP^{TEEC}(b) = N b^α (1 + r b^β).

## Context

This work extends the TEEC formalism previously developed for lepton-hadron production in lepton-proton scattering (Li 2020, Kang 2023) to hadronic collisions with nuclear targets, building on the TMD factorization framework for γ/Z-jet production at N^3LL accuracy (Gao 2023). The nuclear modification approach follows the methodology of Alrashed (2021) for SIDIS and Drell-Yan processes, using EPPS16 nuclear PDFs (Eskola 2016) and CT14nlo proton PDFs (Dulat 2015). The results enable future experimental measurements at RHIC (pAu collisions) and the LHC (pPb collisions) to probe transverse momentum broadening and nuclear modifications of TMD parton distributions, with the potential to test the universality of nuclear broadening parameters across different observables.
