# Electromagnetic Form Factors of Hadrons in Quantum Field Theories

> In this talk, recent results are presented of calculations of electromagnetic form factors of hadrons in the framework of two quantum field theories (QFT), (a) Dual-Large $N_c$ QCD (Dual-$QCD_\infty$) for the pion, proton, and $Δ(1236)$, and (b) the Kroll-Lee-Zumino (KLZ) fully renormalizable Abelian QFT for the pion form factor. Both theories provide a QFT platform to improve on naive (tree-level) Vector Meson Dominance (VMD). Dual-$QCD_\infty$ provides a tree-level improvement by incorporating an infinite number of zero-width resonances, which can be subsequently shifted from the real axis to account for the time-like behaviour of the form factors. The renormalizable KLZ model provides a QFT improvement of VMD in the framework of perturbation theory. Due to the relative mildness of the $ρππ$ coupling, and the size of loop suppression factors, the perturbative expansion is well defined in spite of this being a strong coupling theory. Both approaches lead to considerable improvements of VMD predictions for electromagnetic form factors, in excellent agreement with data.

## Key claim
Dual-Large Nc QCD (Dual-QCD∞) and the Kroll-Lee-Zumino (KLZ) renormalizable Abelian QFT both provide systematic frameworks to improve on naive tree-level Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) for hadron electromagnetic form factors, with the caveat that Dual-QCD∞ remains a tree-level improvement involving one free parameter, while KLZ is a perturbative QFT with no free parameters but relies on the relative mildness of the ρππ coupling and large loop suppression factors to maintain a meaningful expansion.

## Method
For Dual-QCD∞, the form factor is expressed as an Euler Beta function by summing over an infinite tower of zero-width vector meson radial excitations with masses Mn² = Mρ²(1+2n) and couplings fixed by a single parameter β that controls asymptotic power behavior; unitarization is then performed by shifting poles from the real axis using a Breit-Wigner form. For the KLZ theory, one-loop corrections to the pion form factor are calculated in dimensional regularization from vacuum polarization (renormalized on mass shell at q² = Mρ²) and vertex diagrams (renormalized at q²=0), using the known experimental values of masses and couplings as inputs.

## Key numbers
- Pion form factor asymptotic behavior: Fπ ∼ (−q²)^(−1.3) from βπ = 2.3
- Proton Dirac form factor F1 asymptotic: F1 ∼ (−q²)^(−2.0) from β1 = 2.95–3.03
- Proton Pauli form factor F2 asymptotic: F2 ∼ (−q²)^(−3.2) from β2 = 4.13–4.20
- Δ(1236) magnetic form factor G*M asymptotic: G*M ∼ (−q²)^(−3.7) from β*M = 4.6–4.8
- Δ(1236) Coulomb form factor G*C asymptotic: G*C ∼ (−q²)^(−5.1) from β*C = 6.0–6.2
- ρππ coupling strength: gρππ ∼ 5, with loop suppression factor 1/(4π)²
- KLZ theory produces the Gounaris-Sakurai momentum-dependent width: Γρ(s)|KLZ = (Mρ Γρ/√s) [(s − 4μπ²)/(Mρ² − 4μπ²)]^(3/2)

## Context
This work extends earlier attempts at improving VMD, such as Extended VMD which incorporated radial excitations of the rho meson without a renormalizable QFT foundation. The Dual-QCD∞ approach is inspired by the Veneziano Dual Resonance Model (Veneziano, 1968) but avoids its unwanted features (lack of unitarity, unphysical particles), while the KLZ theory (Kroll, Lee, and Zumino, 1967) provides a fully renormalizable framework. These results enable systematic calculations of form factors for the pion, proton, and Δ(1236) across both space-like and time-like regions, with the KLZ approach naturally reproducing the empirical Gounaris-Sakurai width formula.
