# Differential elastic nucleus-nucleus scattering in complete Glauber theory

> The differential elastic cross sections of $^{12}$C -- $^{12}$C and $^{11}$Li -- $^{12}$C nuclei are calculated in the complete Glauber theory. The role of the possible correlations connected to the shell effects in $^{11}$Li nucleus is considered.

## Key claim
The differential elastic cross sections of \(^{12}\)C–\(^{12}\)C and \(^{11}\)Li–\(^{12}\)C scattering are calculated in the complete Glauber theory using a generating function approach, with the caveat that the real part of the strong nucleon-nucleon amplitude is neglected (it is at most 10% of the imaginary part at the beam energy of about 1 GeV per nucleon).

## Method
The elastic scattering amplitude is computed from the Glauber theory expression involving the impact-parameter profile function \(S_{AB}(b)\), which is evaluated via a generating function \(Z(u,v)\) that allows analytical differentiation for light nuclei (\(A,B \lesssim 20\)). The nucleon-nucleon amplitude is parametrized with \(\sigma_{NN}^{tot}=43\) mb and slope \(\beta = 0.2\) fm\(^2\), and the nuclear densities are taken as harmonic-oscillator forms for \(^{12}\)C and as a sum of core and halo Gaussian forms for \(^{11}\)Li, with the Coulomb amplitude added coherently.

## Key numbers
- Total nucleon-nucleon cross section: \(\sigma_{NN}^{tot} = 43\) mb (average over \(pp\) and \(pn\) values).
- Slope parameter of NN amplitude: \(\beta = 0.2\) fm\(^2\).
- Mean square radius of \(^{12}\)C: \(R_{\mathrm{rms}} = 2.49\) fm (fitted from Monte-Carlo simulation).
- For \(^{11}\)Li: core mean square radius \(R_c = 2.50\) fm, halo mean square radius \(R_v = 5.04\) fm, with \(N_c = 9\) core nucleons and \(N_v = 2\) valence neutrons.
- The differential cross section for \(^{12}\)C–\(^{12}\)C shows 2 maxima and 2 minima in the transferred momentum interval \(-t = 0.001\) to \(0.15\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context
This work extends earlier Glauber-theory calculations for \(^{11}\)Li–\(^{12}\)C scattering by Alkhazov (2011, 2013) and uses the generating function approach developed by Shabelski (2021) to avoid the usual simplified approximations for nucleus-nucleus scattering with \(A>4\). The results enable a more sensitive test of nuclear density distributions, especially the halo structure of \(^{11}\)Li, by comparing differential cross sections from three different halo parametrizations taken from Hassan (2015).
