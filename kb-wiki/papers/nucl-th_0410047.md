# Phase Diagram of the Proton-Neutron Interacting Boson Model

> We study the phase diagram of the proton--neutron interacting boson model (IBM--2) with special emphasis on the phase transitions leading to triaxial phases. The existence of a new critical point between spherical and triaxial shapes is reported.

## Key claim
The paper reports the existence of a new critical point (labeled x*) in the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) that corresponds to a direct second-order phase transition from spherical to triaxial shapes, bypassing the axially deformed phase. This claim is conditional on the use of a simplified Consistent-Q Hamiltonian and the mean-field (intrinsic state) formalism in the limit of large boson number.

## Method
The authors used the intrinsic state (mean-field) formalism with a general proton-neutron boson condensate trial wave function to compute the energy expectation value of the Consistent-Q IBM-2 Hamiltonian. By minimizing the energy per boson in the large-N limit as a function of the shape parameters (βπ, γπ, βν, γν) and the relative orientation Ω, they mapped the phase diagram across the IBM-2 parameter space (x, χπ, χν). They determined the order of each phase transition by performing forward and backward calculations to check for coexistence of minima (first-order) versus convergence of spinodal, critical, and antispinodal points (second-order).

## Key numbers
- The transition from SU(3) to SU*(3) (axial to triaxial) occurs at χν = 0.4035 (with x=0 and χπ = -√7/2 fixed); this point is labeled "y".
- The transition from U(5) to SU*(3) (spherical to triaxial) occurs at x = 0.8 (with χπ = -χν = -√7/2 fixed); this point is labeled "x*".
- For a generic trajectory with χπ = -1.2 and χν = 0.5, a first-order transition from spherical to axial occurs at x ≈ 0.8, and a second-order transition from axial to triaxial occurs at x ≈ 0.48.
- The critical surface separating spherical and axially deformed shapes is approximately a sphere of radius ρ = 0.2 centered at U(5).

## Context
This work extends the phase diagram studies of the interacting boson model from IBM-1 (which has only spherical, γ-unstable, and axial phases) to the more realistic IBM-2 that includes proton-neutron degrees of freedom. It builds on the earlier IBM-1 phase diagram work by Dukelsky et al. (1980s) and the critical point symmetries E(5), X(5), and Y(5) proposed by Iachello (2000-2004). The new critical point x* enables future spectroscopic studies of nuclei that may exhibit a direct spherical-to-triaxial shape transition, analogous to how E(5) and X(5) were used to identify candidate critical nuclei experimentally.
