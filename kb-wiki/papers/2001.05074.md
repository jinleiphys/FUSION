# Heavy Quarks Embedded in Glasma

> Heavy quarks, which are produced at the earliest stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, probe the entire history of the quark-gluon plasma that is created in the collision. Initially the plasma is populated with chromodynamic fields which can be treated as classical. We study the transport of heavy quarks across such a system, which is called glasma, using a Fokker-Planck equation where the quarks interact with long wavelength chromodynamic fields. We compute field correlators which are used to calculate the collision terms of the transport equation. Finally, the energy loss and momentum broadening of heavy quarks in the glasma are studied. Both of these quantities are sizable and strongly directionally dependent.

## Key claim

Heavy quarks traversing the glasma, the early-stage non-equilibrium system of classical chromodynamic fields produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, experience sizable energy loss and momentum broadening that are strongly directionally dependent, but the authors' calculation is limited to first order in a proper-time expansion of the fields, and the zeroth- and first-order contributions are of similar magnitude, indicating that higher-order terms could be important.

## Method

The authors derive the collision terms of a Fokker-Planck equation for heavy quarks interacting with long-wavelength classical chromodynamic fields in the glasma, using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework. The fields are expanded in powers of the proper time τ, keeping the zeroth and first order terms, and the field correlators are computed from the correlators of the pure gauge potentials generated by the colliding nuclei. The tensor X^{ij}(v) obtained from these correlators is then used to compute the collisional energy loss dE/dx and the transverse momentum broadening parameter q̂.

## Key numbers

- The saturation momentum parameter is taken as Q_s = 2 GeV, the infrared regulator m ≈ Λ_QCD ≈ 200 MeV, and the QCD coupling g = 1 with N_c = 3.
- The numerical saturation value of q̂ at v = 0.9 and cosθ = 0 (quark moving perpendicular to the collision axis) equals 42 GeV²/fm when only the zeroth-order contribution is included, and is reduced to 8.5 GeV²/fm when both zeroth- and first-order contributions are summed.
- The zeroth-order contribution to the energy loss and momentum broadening is larger than the first-order contribution for transverse velocities v_⊥ ≳ 0.73.
- Saturation of the functions f_E^0, f_B^0, f_E^1, f_B^1 is reached before t = 1.0 fm/c for the velocities studied (v_⊥ = 0.6 and v_⊥ = 1.0).

## Context

This work extends the analytical approach of Mrowczynski (2017) by computing the Fokker-Planck collision terms within the CGC framework, using the proper-time expansion method of Chen (2015). It contrasts with numerical simulations of heavy-quark transport in glasma (Ruggieri 2018, Sun 2019, Liu 2019) by providing an analytically tractable treatment. The results suggest that the glasma phase can contribute significantly to jet quenching observed in heavy-ion collisions, with the computed q̂ values (8.5 GeV²/fm including first-order corrections) being comparable to or larger than the range 1.5–7.0 GeV²/fm inferred from jet quenching data (Prino 2016), but the authors note that higher-order terms and the regularization scheme need further study before firm phenomenological conclusions can be drawn.
