# Pion-Nucleon Scattering in Kadyshevsky Formalism and Higher Spin Field Quantization

> This thesis contains two parts. The first part deals with pion-nucleon/meson-baryon scattering in the Kadyshevsky formalism. This formalism is introduced and discussed. Problems may arise when derivative couplings and/or higher spin fields are used, especially when compared to the results in the Feynman formalism: unwanted contact terms pop-up. These terms are cancelled using the Gross and Jackiw or Takahashi and Umezawa method. The final results in both formalisms are therefore equal, causal and covariant. Formal incorporation of pair suppression in the baryon exchange sector is achieved using a method based on the Takahashi and Umezawa method. For the resulting tree level amplitudes, we have shown, to our knowledge for the first time, that they are causal, covariant and n-independent. Moreover, the amplitudes are just a factor 1/2 of the usual Feynman expressions. The amplitudes contain only posititve energy initial and final states, although it should be mentioned that negative energy is present inside an amplitude via the Delta propagator. The second part of this thesis deals with higher spin field quantization in the framework of Dirac's Constraint analysis. A full constraint analysis and quantization procedure is presented for the free spin 1,3/2 and 2 case and for the situation where these fields are coupled to auxiliary fields. The constructed propagators in the former case are shown to be non-covariant, as is well-known. Therefore, auxiliary fields coupled to gauge conditions of the free case are introduced in the Lagrangian. Now, the resulting propagators are covariant.

## Key claim

The thesis demonstrates that tree-level pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes derived in the Kadyshevsky formalism, after cancelling unwanted contact terms via the Gross and Jackiw or Takahashi and Umezawa methods, are causal, covariant, and independent of the auxiliary parameter n, but are exactly a factor of 1/2 of the usual Feynman expressions; the amplitudes contain only positive energy initial and final states, though negative energy is present inside an amplitude via the Delta propagator.

## Method

The first part applies the Kadyshevsky formalism to pion-nucleon/meson-baryon scattering, addressing derivative couplings and higher spin fields that produce unwanted contact terms compared to the Feynman formalism; these terms are cancelled using the Gross and Jackiw or Takahashi and Umezawa methods, and pair suppression in the baryon exchange sector is formally incorporated via a method based on Takahashi and Umezawa. The second part performs a full Dirac constraint analysis and quantization procedure for free massive spin-1, spin-3/2, and spin-2 fields, calculating all Lagrange multipliers and constraints, then introducing auxiliary fields coupled to gauge conditions to obtain covariant propagators.

## Key numbers

- The tree-level Kadyshevsky amplitudes are a factor of **1/2** of the usual Feynman expressions.
- For the spin-3/2 field, the class of Lagrangians has a parameter A with the constraint **A ≠ -1/2**.
- For the spin-2 field, the Fierz-Pauli Lagrangian is obtained with parameters B = 1/2(3A²+2A+1), C = 3A²+3A+1, and **A ≠ -1/2**.
- The Dirac bracket for the spin-3/2 constraints yields a non-vanishing Poisson bracket of **-3i/2 M_{3/2}² δ³(x-y)** between the transformed constraints.
- The Dirac bracket for the spin-2 constraints yields a non-vanishing Poisson bracket of **3M₂⁴ δ³(x-y)** between transformed constraints.
- The equal-time commutation relation for the spin-1 field gives **[Ȧⁿ(x), Aᵐ(y)]₀ = i(g^{nm} + ∂ⁿ∂ᵐ/M₁²) δ³(x-y)**.
- The equal-time anti-commutation relation for the spin-3/2 field gives **{ψⁿ(x), ψᵐ†(y)}₀ = -[g^{nm} - (1/3)γⁿγᵐ + (2/(3M_{3/2}²))∂ⁿ∂ᵐ + (1/(3M_{3/2}))(γⁿi∂ᵐ - i∂ⁿγᵐ)] δ³(x-y)**.

## Context

This work extends the Kadyshevsky formalism for pion-nucleon scattering by resolving contact term problems that arise with derivative couplings and higher spin fields, building on methods from Gross and Jackiw and Takahashi and Umezawa. The second part provides a complete Dirac constraint analysis for free spin-1, spin-3/2, and spin-2 fields, going beyond prior work by Baaklini (which did not determine all Lagrange multipliers and constraints) and by Pascual and Pasc-Theriault (which focused on the number of constraints rather than their specific forms), and obtains all equal-time (anti-)commutation relations rather than only spatial components. The introduction of auxiliary fields coupled to gauge conditions, inspired by Nakanishi (1972), yields covariant propagators for all three spin cases, enabling consistent quantization of higher spin fields for applications in meson-baryon scattering models and supergravity.
