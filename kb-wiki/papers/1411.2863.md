# Axial current generation by P-odd domains in QCD matter

> The dynamics of topological domains which break parity (${\cal P}$) and charge-parity (${\cal CP}$) symmetry of QCD are studied. We derive in a general setting that those local domains will generate an axial current and quantify the strength of the induced axial current. Our findings are verified in a top-down holographic model. The relation between the real time dynamics of those local domains and chiral magnetic effect is also elucidated. We finally argue that such an induced axial current would be phenomenologically important if topological domains are created in heavy-ion collisions experiment.

## Key claim

The paper derives that local, metastable topological domains in QCD matter which break parity (P) and charge-parity (CP) symmetry will generate an axial current proportional to the spatial gradient of an effective axion field θ(t,x), a result that holds provided the variation of θ is on scales larger than 1/T and its time variation is longer than the relaxation time but shorter than the domain lifetime.

## Method

The authors use a general hydrodynamic expansion of the retarded correlator of topological charge density q, combined with the axial anomaly relation ∂_μ J^μ_A = -2q, to derive constitutive relations for the axial current in the presence of a space-time dependent θ angle. They then verify these relations in a top-down holographic model, the Sakai-Sugimoto model, by solving the equations of motion for the dual gauge fields in the hydrodynamic regime (ω, k ≪ T) and extracting the transport coefficients from the near-boundary asymptotics.

## Key numbers

- Chern-Simons diffusion rate in the Sakai-Sugimoto model: Γ_CS = (8 λ^3 T^6) / (729 π M_KK^2), where λ is the 't Hooft coupling and M_KK is the mass gap.
- Transport coefficient κ_CS in the Sakai-Sugimoto model: κ_CS = (3 Γ_CS) / (8 π T^2).
- Diffusion constant in the Sakai-Sugimoto model: D = 1 / (2 π T).
- Sphaleron damping rate τ_sph defined via τ_sph ≡ (2 χ T) / Γ_CS, where χ is the axial susceptibility.
- Estimated axial current from θ domains: j_{A,θ} ∼ (μ_A T^2) (τ_sph / L_θ), where L_θ is the characteristic domain size.

## Context

This work extends the study of P- and CP-odd effects in QCD matter, particularly the chiral magnetic effect (CME) reviewed by Kharzeev (2013), by addressing the dynamical generation of chiral imbalance from topological domains rather than introducing it by hand. The derived axial current is new and complements the chiral charge separation effect (CCSE) of Son and Zhitnitsky (2004) and Metlitski and Zhitnitsky (2005). The results enable phenomenological estimates of axial current contributions in heavy-ion collisions, potentially dominating over diffusive axial currents if τ_sph / L_θ ≳ 1 and τ_sph / L_θ ≳ T / L.
