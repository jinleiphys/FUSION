# Influence of additional neutrons on the fusion cross-section beyond the N=8 shell

> Fusion enhancement for neutron-rich isotopes of oxygen on carbon nuclei was probed. To measure the fusion cross-section a $^{20}$O beam accelerated to E$_{lab}$/A=2.7 MeV bombarded the active-target detector MuSIC@Indiana with a fill gas of CH$_4$. Examination of the average fusion cross-section over the interval 12 MeV $\leq$E$_{c.m.}$$\leq$ 17 MeV for $^{16-20}$O + $^{12}$C reveals that while even isotopes of oxygen exhibit essentially the same cross-section, the cross-section for odd isotopes can be either enhanced or suppressed relative to the even A members of the isotopic chain. Theoretical models fail to explain the observed experimental results.

## Key claim

The average fusion cross-section for $^{20}$O + $^{12}$C over 12 MeV ≤ E_c.m. ≤ 17 MeV does not show the large increase previously observed for $^{19}$O, but is essentially the same as that of $^{18}$O; this contradicts the expectation that additional neutrons beyond N=8 would systematically enhance fusion, and reveals that odd-neutron isotopes ($^{17}$O, $^{19}$O) can be either suppressed or enhanced relative to even-neutron isotopes ($^{16,18,20}$O), a pattern that theoretical models fail to explain.

## Method

The fusion excitation function for $^{20}$O + $^{12}$C was measured using the active-target detector MuSIC@Indiana, operated with a CH$_4$ fill gas at pressures between 110 and 124 torr, at the SPIRAL1 facility at GANIL. A $^{20}$O beam accelerated to E_lab/A = 2.7 MeV bombarded the detector, and the ionization traces from segmented anodes were used to identify fusion events by their characteristic larger signal from evaporation residues. The average fusion cross-section over 12–17 MeV in the center-of-mass frame was compared for the isotopic chain $^{16-20}$O + $^{12}$C, using prior data for $^{16-19}$O and new data for $^{20}$O.

## Key numbers

- Average fusion cross-section for $^{20}$O + $^{12}$C over 12 MeV ≤ E_c.m. ≤ 17 MeV is essentially the same as that for $^{18}$O, and slightly decreased compared to $^{18}$O.
- The one-neutron separation energies for $^{17}$O and $^{19}$O are 4143 keV and 3956 keV, respectively, which are essentially the same.
- The $^{20}$O beam intensity was up to ≈3×10$^4$ ions/s.
- The fusion excitation function for $^{20}$O + $^{12}$C was measured in the energy interval 10.6 MeV ≤ E_c.m. ≤ 17.5 MeV.
- A sharp drop in cross-section is observed at E_c.m. ≈ 13 MeV.
- The reduced electric field in MuSIC@Indiana was ~0.7 kV/cm/atm, giving an electron drift velocity of ~10 cm/μs for CH$_4$.
- The veto detector allowed operation at 3×10$^4$ ions/s with a live time of ≈70%.

## Context

This work extends prior measurements of fusion for $^{12-15}$C + $^{12}$C (Carnelli 2015, Calderon 2015) and for $^{16-19}$O + $^{12}$C (Hudan 2020, Hudan 2023), where a significant increase in the above-barrier fusion cross-section was observed for $^{19}$O compared to $^{18}$O (Singh 2017). The present result for $^{20}$O contradicts the expectation of a systematic increase with neutron excess and instead reveals a dichotomy between even-N and odd-N isotopes. This motivates future high-resolution measurements of barrier distributions to investigate the role of initial structure and pairing on fusion for neutron-rich light nuclei.
