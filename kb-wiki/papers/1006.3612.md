# Nucleon Statistics in Holographic QCD : Aharonov-Bohm Effect in a Matrix Model

> We show that the Aharonov-Bohm effect in the nuclear matrix model derives the statistical nature of nucleons in holographic QCD. For N_c= odd (even), the nucleon is shown to be a fermion (boson).

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Aharonov-Bohm effect in the nuclear matrix model of holographic QCD determines the statistical nature of nucleons: for odd (even) number of colors \(N_c\), the nucleon is a fermion (boson). The hedge is that this result depends on the specific path chosen in the target space of the matrix model, combined with a gauge transformation, to isolate the phase.

## Method

The authors consider a \(2\pi\) spatial rotation in the target space of the nuclear matrix model for a single baryon (\(k=1\)). They isolate the effect on the \(w_{\dot{\alpha} i}\) field, which carries a spinor index, and combine the rotation with a gauge transformation to define a closed path in the moduli space. The Aharonov-Bohm phase for this path is computed from the gauge potential \(\widetilde{A}\) arising from the Chern-Simons term in the one-dimensional gauge theory, using the constant vacuum value \(\rho_0\) of the matrix model.

## Key numbers

- The Aharonov-Bohm phase \(\Phi = N_c \pi\) (Equation AB), derived from the gauge potential \(\widetilde{A}_\theta = -N_c / 2\rho_0\) and the path \(u+iv = \rho_0 e^{-i\theta}\) for \(0 \leq \theta \leq 2\pi\).
- The mass parameter \(M = \lambda N_c M_{\rm KK} / 27\pi\) for the hypothetical charged particle in the \(u\)-\(v\) space.
- The constant gauge field \(A_0 = -27\pi / 2 \lambda M_{\rm KK} \rho_0^2\), obtained from solving the equation of motion.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear matrix model of Hashimoto (2010) by addressing the statistics of baryons, which was not obvious from the bosonic variables of the model. It parallels the earlier demonstration by Witten (1983) that the Wess-Zumino term in chiral soliton models determines nucleon statistics, and it connects the holographic QCD framework of Sakai and Sugimoto (2004) to that result via the Ramond-Ramond flux generating a Chern-Simons term. The result enables the consistent treatment of multi-baryon systems in holographic QCD, including calculations of baryon spectra and nuclear forces.
