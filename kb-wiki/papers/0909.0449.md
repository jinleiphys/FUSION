# Neutron star, $β$-stable ring-diagram equation of state and Brown-Rho scaling

> Neutron star properties, such as its mass, radius, and moment of inertia, are calculated by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations using the ring-diagram equation of state (EOS) obtained from realistic low-momentum NN interactions $V_{low-k}$. Several NN potentials (CDBonn, Nijmegen, Argonne V18 and BonnA) have been employed to calculate the ring-diagram EOS where the particle-particle hole-hole ring diagrams are summed to all orders. The proton fractions for different radial regions of a $β$-stable neutron star are determined from the chemical potential conditions $μ_n-μ_p = μ_e = μ_μ$. The neutron star masses, radii and moments of inertia given by the above potentials all tend to be too small compared with the accepted values. Our results are largely improved with the inclusion of medium corrections based on Brown-Rho scaling where the in-medium meson masses, particularly those of $ω$, $ρ$ and $σ$, are slightly decreased compared with their in-vacuum values. Representative results using such medium corrected interactions are neutron star mass $M\sim 1.8 M_{\odot}$, radius $R\sim 9$ km and moment of inertia $\sim 60 M_{\odot}km^2$. The mass-radius trajectories given by the above four realistic NN potentials are by and large overlapping.

## Key claim

The authors find that neutron star masses, radii, and moments of inertia calculated from ring-diagram equations of state using realistic NN potentials alone are too small compared with accepted values, but results are largely improved when medium corrections based on Brown-Rho scaling are included, yielding a representative maximum neutron star mass of approximately 1.8 solar masses.

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations using a ring-diagram equation of state (EOS) derived from low-momentum NN interactions \(V_{low-k}\), where particle-particle hole-hole ring diagrams are summed to all orders. Four realistic NN potentials (CDBonn, Nijmegen, Argonne V18, and BonnA) are employed, and the proton fractions for different radial regions of a \(\beta\)-stable neutron star are determined from the chemical potential conditions \(\mu_n-\mu_p = \mu_e = \mu_\mu\). Medium corrections are incorporated via Brown-Rho scaling, where in-medium meson masses (\(\omega\), \(\rho\), \(\sigma\)) are decreased compared to their in-vacuum values, and this effect is simulated by a Skyrme-type three-body force with parameter \(t_3 = 2000\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^6\).

## Key numbers

- Representative maximum neutron star mass: \(M \sim 1.8 M_{\odot}\) with radius \(R \sim 9\) km and moment of inertia \(\sim 60 M_{\odot}\)km\(^2\), using medium-corrected interactions
- For symmetric nuclear matter with linear BR scaling (BR\(_1\)): \(E_0/A = -15.3\) MeV, saturation Fermi momentum \(k_F^0 = 1.33\) fm\(^{-1}\), compression modulus \(\kappa = 225\) MeV
- For symmetric nuclear matter with \(V_{low-k}\) plus three-body force: \(E_0/A = -14.7\) MeV, \(k_F^0 = 1.40\) fm\(^{-1}\), compression modulus \(\kappa = 140\) MeV
- BR scaling parameters for BonnA potential: \(C_{\rho} = 0.113\), \(C_{\omega} = 0.128\), \(C_{\sigma} = 0.102\)
- Decimation scale \(\Lambda\) chosen between 3.0 and 3.5 fm\(^{-1}\)
- Normal nuclear saturation density: \(n_0 \simeq 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)

## Context

This work extends the ring-diagram approach previously applied to neutron matter (Siu, 2008) and symmetric nuclear matter (Siu, 2009) to \(\beta\)-stable neutron star matter, using \(V_{low-k}\) interactions derived from multiple realistic NN potentials. The results improve upon earlier calculations that gave too small neutron star masses, and the authors show that Brown-Rho scaling, which has been essential in Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (Rapp, 1999) and in explaining the long lifetime of \(^{14}\)C \(\beta\)-decay (Holt, 2008), can be effectively simulated by a Skyrme-type three-body force. The work enables further testing of Brown-Rho scaling at high densities relevant to neutron star interiors and provides a nearly unique neutron-matter EOS that agrees well with the variational results of Friedman and Pandharipande.
