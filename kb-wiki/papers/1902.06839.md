# Nuclear electromagnetic currents to fourth order in chiral effective field theory

> Recently, we have shown that the continuity equation for the nuclear vector and axial current operators acquires additional terms if the latter depend on the energy transfer. We analyze in detail the electromagnetic single-nucleon four-current operators and verify the validity of the modified continuity equation for all one- and two-nucleon contributions up to fourth order in the chiral expansion. We also derive, for the first time, the leading contribution to the three-nucleon charge operator which appears at this order. Our study completes the derivation of the electroweak nuclear currents to fourth order in the chiral expansion.

## Key claim

The paper completes the derivation of nuclear electromagnetic currents at fourth order (N³LO) in chiral effective field theory, but the continuity equation for the vector current acquires additional terms when the current operators depend on the energy transfer, requiring a modified form of current conservation rather than the traditional one.

## Method

The authors employed the method of unitary transformation (UT) in the pion-nucleon Fock space to derive the electromagnetic single-nucleon, two-nucleon, and three-nucleon charge and current operators up to order Q⁴. They explicitly verified the validity of the modified continuity equation for all one- and two-nucleon contributions, including one-pion exchange (OPE), two-pion exchange, and short-range terms, using dimensional regularization to handle loop divergences and fixing the unitary phases β̄ᵢ to ensure renormalizability.

## Key numbers

- The chiral expansion parameter Q is defined as Q ∈ {Mπ/Λb, p/Λb} with breakdown scale Λb ∼ 600 MeV in the few-nucleon sector
- The single-nucleon charge operator at leading order (Q⁻³) is V¹N⁰⁽Q⁻³⁾ = (e/2)(1 + τ₃)
- Renormalizability of the one-loop contributions required β̄₁ = 1, β̄₄ − 3β̄₅ − 3β̄₆ = 2, and β̄₃ = 0, with the additional constraint β̄₆ = −1/2 from the single-nucleon current
- The leading three-nucleon charge operator appears at order Q⁴ (N³LO), completing the derivation of electroweak nuclear currents to fourth order

## Context

This work extends the earlier derivations of two-nucleon electromagnetic currents at N³LO by Kolling et al. (2009, 2011) and the axial current work by Krebs et al. (2016). It also builds on the nuclear force derivations up to N⁴LO by Entem et al. (2014, 2015, 2017), Epelbaum et al. (2014), and Reinert et al. (2017). The paper resolves the energy-transfer dependence of current operators identified in Krebs et al. (2016) and provides the first derivation of the three-nucleon charge operator, enabling complete electroweak current calculations for few-nucleon systems at N³LO.
