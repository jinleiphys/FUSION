# Octupole deformation in neutron-rich actinides and superheavy nuclei and the role of nodal structure of single-particle wavefunctions in extremely deformed structures of light nuclei

> Octupole deformed shapes in neutron-rich actinides and superheavy nuclei as well as extremely deformed shapes of the N~Z light nuclei have been investigated within the framework of covariant density functional theory. We confirmed the presence of new region of octupole deformation in neutron-rich actinides with the center around Z~96, N~196 but our calculations do not predict octupole deformation in the ground states of superheavy Z~108 nuclei. As exemplified by the study of 36Ar, the nodal structure of the wavefunction of occupied single-particle orbitals in extremely deformed structures allows to understand the formation of the alpha-clusters in very light nuclei, the suppression of the alpha-clusterization with the increase of mass number, the formation of ellipsoidal mean-field type structures and nuclear molecules.

## Key claim

The paper confirms the existence of a new region of octupole deformation in neutron-rich actinides centered around Z~96, N~196, but explicitly states that their calculations do not predict octupole deformation in the ground states of superheavy nuclei with Z≥108, contrary to some non-relativistic models.

## Method

The authors performed Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using four covariant energy density functionals (DD-PC1, NL3*, DD-ME2, and PC-PK1) with constraints on quadrupole and octupole moments, employing a separable form of finite range Gogny pairing interaction. For the study of extremely deformed shapes in 36Ar, they used the cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) framework with the NL3* functional to analyze single-particle density distributions and their nodal structure.

## Key numbers

- The new region of octupole deformation is centered around Z~96, N~196, with the size predicted by most CEDFs larger than the experimentally known region at Z~92, N~136
- For 36Ar, the megadeformed [31,31] configuration is predicted to become yrast at spin I≥21ℏ
- The weight of the dominant Nilsson component exceeds 75% of the total wavefunction for the majority of single-particle states in the [31,31] configuration of 36Ar, with exceptions for [440]1/2 (55%), [330]1/2 (62%), and [321]3/2 (54%)
- The observed superdeformed band in 36Ar terminates at spin I=16+

## Context

This work extends the global investigation of octupole deformation by Afanasjev et al. (2016) using the DD-PC1 and NL3* functionals, now adding DD-ME2 and PC-PK1 functionals and extending to superheavy nuclei. The results differ from Skyrme DFT calculations by Erler et al. (2012) which predict the octupole region centered at Z~100, N~190, and from microscopic+macroscopic calculations by Möller et al. (1995) which predict it at Z~100, N~184; Gogny DFT calculations by Ward and Hilaire (2012) do not suggest octupole deformation in very neutron-rich actinides. The study of single-particle nodal structure in 36Ar builds on systematic investigations of extremely deformed structures at high spin by Afanasjev and collaborators (2016, 2017) for N≈Z nuclei with Z=14-24, and enables understanding of the transition between ellipsoidal mean-field type structures and nuclear molecules through specific particle-hole excitations.
