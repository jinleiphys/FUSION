# A parton recombination approach to heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC

> This thesis discusses the phenomenological parton recombination approach to describe hadronization in heavy ion collisions. The very good agreement to RHIC data for the flow coefficients v_2 and v_4 is shown and extrapolations are used to make predictions for the LHC.

## Key claim

This thesis demonstrates that a phenomenological parton recombination (coalescence) model can reproduce RHIC data for elliptic flow coefficients \(v_2\) and \(v_4\) with very good agreement, and uses extrapolations to make predictions for the LHC; however, the model is explicitly valid only for transverse momenta \(P_T \gtrsim 1\ \text{GeV}\) due to an energy violation at low \(P_T\), though the flow coefficients may give valid results down to several hundred MeV.

## Method

The author develops an analytical colinear recombination formalism in which constituent quarks with colinear momenta recombine to form hadrons, using a light-cone wavefunction ansatz for mesons and baryons. The quark densities are modeled via a blast-wave parameterization with an ellipsoidal freeze-out hypersurface, and the invariant hadron yields are computed using the Cooper-Frye prescription with either constant freeze-out time or a \(\rho\)-dependent freeze-out time. The model is applied to compute transverse momentum spectra and flow coefficients \(v_2\) and \(v_4\) for RHIC energies, and extrapolated to LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- The proton-to-pion ratio from recombination is approximately 2, compared to about 0.2 from pQCD fragmentation.
- The critical temperature at zero chemical potential in the MIT bag model is \(T_C = 175\ \text{MeV}\).
- The model is applicable for transverse momenta \(P_T \gtrsim 1\ \text{GeV}\) for spectra, but flow coefficients may be valid down to several hundred MeV.

## Context

This work extends the recombination approach of Fries (2003) to include elliptic flow observables and predictions for higher collision energies. It contrasts with pQCD fragmentation, which fails to explain the large baryon/meson ratio at RHIC, and with thermal models that assume global equilibrium. The results enable predictions for the LHC and provide guidance for dynamical studies of hadronization from a Quark-Gluon Plasma.
