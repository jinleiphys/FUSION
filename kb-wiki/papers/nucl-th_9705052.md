# Single Photons from Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions and Quark-Hadron Phase Transition

> The present status of theoretical expectations of studies of single photons from relativistic heavy ion collisions is discussed. It is argued that the upper limit of single photon radiation from S+Au collisions at CERN SPS obtained by the WA80 collaboration perhaps rules out any reasonable description of the collision process which does not involve a phase transition to quark gluon plasma. Predictions for single photons from the quark-matter likely to be created in collision of two lead nuclei at RHIC and LHC energies are given with a proper accounting of chemical equilibration and transverse expansion. Finally, it is pointed out that, contrary to the popular belief of a quadrilateral dependence of electromagnetic radiations ($N_γ$) from such collisions on the number of charged particles ($N_{ch})$, we may only have $N_γ\propto N_{ch}^{1.2}$.

## Key claim

The upper limit of single photon radiation from S+Au collisions at CERN SPS obtained by the WA80 collaboration "perhaps rules out any reasonable description of the collision process which does not involve a phase transition to quark gluon plasma," though the author acknowledges that a hadronic description with very high initial hadronic densities (3-4 hadrons/fm^3) could still be consistent with the data if one accepts such densities as physically plausible.

## Method

The author calculates thermal photon spectra by convoluting photon emission rates from quark-gluon plasma (using Compton plus annihilation contributions corrected for infrared divergences) and from hadronic matter (considering reactions among pi, rho, omega, and eta mesons, plus vector meson decays) with a boost-invariant hydrodynamic expansion including transverse expansion. For S+Au collisions at SPS, initial conditions are fixed using dN_charge/dy = 150 and an initial time of 1 fm/c, with two scenarios: one assuming initial QGP formation at T_i = 203.4 MeV and one assuming initial hot hadronic gas at T_i = 407.8 MeV with the same entropy density but no phase transition. For Pb+Pb collisions at SPS, the analysis uses dN_charge/dy = 550 and considers two hadronic equations of state (light mesons only versus all hadrons from the particle data book), each with and without a phase transition at T_c = 160 MeV and freeze-out at 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For S+Au collisions at SPS: initial temperature T_i = 203.4 MeV if QGP phase is formed at tau_i = 1 fm/c; initial temperature T_i = 407.8 MeV if hot hadronic phase is formed at tau_i = 1 fm/c with same entropy density
- For Pb+Pb collisions at SPS: dN_charge/dy = 550 is used to fix initial conditions
- Transition temperature T_c = 160 MeV; freeze-out temperature T_f = 100 MeV
- For RHIC and LHC: thermalization occurs by tau = 0.25 fm/c, with the plasma far from chemical equilibrium
- Scaling relation: N_gamma proportional to N_ch^1.2, not the commonly assumed N_ch^2

## Context

This paper extends the theoretical framework of Srivastava and Sinha (1997) for single photon production in relativistic heavy ion collisions, building on earlier rate calculations by Kapusta, Lichard and Seibert (1991) for hadronic matter and by several authors for quark matter using Braaten-Pisarski resummation. The work directly confronts the WA80 upper limit data for S+Au collisions and provides predictions for Pb+Pb collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies. The analysis enables future experimental tests of QGP formation through single photon measurements, particularly at RHIC and LHC where the author argues QGP formation is "beyond doubt," and suggests that the scaling behavior N_gamma proportional to N_ch^1.2 could be used to determine the equation of state of hadronic matter.
