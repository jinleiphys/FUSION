# Weak sensitivity of three-body ($d,p$) reactions to $np$ force models

> Adiabatic distorted-wave approximation (ADWA) study of three-body $(d,p)$ transfer reactions [G.W. Bailey, N.K. Timofeyuk, and J.A. Tostevin, Phys. Rev. Lett. 117, 162502 (2016)] reported strong sensitivity of cross sections to the neutron-proton $(np)$ interaction model when the nucleon-nucleus optical potential is nonlocal. The verification of this unusual finding using more reliable methods is aimed for in the present work. A rigorous Faddeev-type three-body scattering theory is applied to the study of $(d,p)$ transfer reactions. The equations for transition operators are solved in the momentum-space partial-wave framework. Differential cross sections for $^{26}$Al$(d,p)^{27}$Al reactions are calculated using nonlocal nuclear optical potentials and a number of realistic $np$ potentials. Only a weak dependence on the $np$ force model is observed, typically one order of magnitude lower than in the previous ADWA study. The shape of the angular distribution of the experimental data is well reproduced. Cross sections of $(d,p)$ transfer reactions calculated using a rigorous three-body method show little sensitivity to the $np$ interaction model. This indicates a failure of the ADWA in the context of nonlocal potentials. Some evident shortcomings of the ADWA are pointed out.

## Key claim

Cross sections for \((d,p)\) transfer reactions calculated using a rigorous Faddeev-type three-body method show only weak sensitivity to the neutron-proton (\(np\)) interaction model, typically one order of magnitude lower than in the previous adiabatic distorted-wave approximation (ADWA) study by Bailey, Timofeyuk, and Tostevin (2016), indicating a failure of the ADWA in the context of nonlocal nucleon-nucleus optical potentials.

## Method

The author applied a rigorous Faddeev-type three-body scattering theory, solving the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for transition operators in the momentum-space partial-wave framework. Differential cross sections for \(^{26}\mathrm{Al}(d,p)^{27}\mathrm{Al}\) reactions at a deuteron beam energy of 12 MeV were calculated using nonlocal nuclear optical potentials (the Giannini and Ricco parametrization without spin-orbit part) and a number of realistic high-precision \(np\) potentials, including Argonne V18 (AV18), charge-dependent Bonn (CD Bonn), Reid93, chiral effective field theory potentials at N4LO, and a similarity renormalization group (SRG)-softened AV18 potential.

## Key numbers

- The spread of ADWA results with realistic \(np\) potentials at \(\Theta_{\mathrm{c.m.}} = 0\) deg for the \(^{27}\mathrm{Al}\) ground state was roughly a factor of 2.5, or about \(\pm 50\%\) from the central value.
- In contrast, rigorous Faddeev calculations for the same state at \(\Theta_{\mathrm{c.m.}} = 0\) deg exhibited a spread of about \(\pm 5\%\) from the central value.
- The magnitude of the Faddeev differential cross section at \(\Theta_{\mathrm{c.m.}} = 0\) deg for the ground state was lower by a factor of 2 (CD Bonn) to 4 (AV18) compared to ADWA.
- For the \(J^\pi = 9/2^+\), \(S_n = 5.25\) MeV excited state at \(\Theta_{\mathrm{c.m.}} = 0\) deg, the spread of Faddeev results was \(\pm 1\%\) for angular momentum transfer \(L=0\) and \(\pm 3\%\) for \(L=2\), while the ADWA spread was about \(\pm 7\%\) and \(\pm 25\%\), respectively.
- The deuteron \(D\)-state probability of the \(np\) potentials ranged from 2.53% (SRG) to 5.76% (AV18).

## Context

This work extends the rigorous Faddeev-type treatment of three-body reactions with nonlocal optical potentials, first implemented by Deltuva (2009), to directly test the surprising claim by Bailey, Timofeyuk, and Tostevin (2016) that ADWA with nonlocal potentials yields strong sensitivity to the \(np\) interaction model. The present results contradict that finding and show that the ADWA is inadequate for nonlocal potentials, while the rigorous method reproduces the shape of experimental angular distributions from a 2015 measurement. The author points out that the energy of the nucleon-nucleus subsystem in the Faddeev formalism has an upper limit of about 9 MeV in this study, whereas the ADWA requires an equivalent local optical potential evaluated at energies around 50–72 MeV, which is unnatural from the Faddeev perspective.
