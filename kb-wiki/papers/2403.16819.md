# A neural network approach for two-body systems with spin and isospin degrees of freedom

> We propose an enhanced machine learning method to calculate the ground state of two-body systems. By extending the original method [Naito, Naito, and Hashimoto, Phys. Rev. Research 5, 033189 (2023)], the present method enables consideration of the spin and isospin degrees of freedom by employing a non-fully connected deep neural network and the unsupervised machine learning technique. The validity of this method is verified by calculating the unique bound state of the deuteron.

## Key claim

The paper extends an unsupervised deep neural network method (Naito, Naito, and Hashimoto, 2023) to include spin and isospin degrees of freedom for two-body systems, but only demonstrates validity for the single bound state of the deuteron as a proof-of-principle, without addressing excited states or systems with more than two particles.

## Method

The authors discretize the relative coordinate of a two-body system into mesh points and represent the radial partial wave functions as outputs of a non-fully-connected deep neural network, where hidden layer nodes of different output channels are not connected to each other. The Hamiltonian matrix is constructed in coordinate space using finite-difference approximations for the kinetic term, and the ground-state energy is obtained by minimizing the energy expectation value as the loss function using the Adam optimizer. The method incorporates spin and isospin via a partial wave expansion with Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, and is tested on the deuteron using the Argonne V18, AV8', and AV4' nucleon-nucleon potentials.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of deuteron with AV18 potential: -2.2241 MeV (non-fully-connected DNN) and -2.2246 MeV (fully-connected DNN), both within 0.03% relative error of the benchmark from Wiringa (1995).
- Relative error of ground-state energy with AV18 potential using 18 partial waves: 0.05% of the benchmark value.
- Accuracy within 2 keV (0.1%) for both AV18 and AV8' calculations compared to benchmark.
- AV4' potential yields a final error of approximately 1%.
- With 500 mesh points in the AV18 potential, results produce less than 1% relative error.
- Single hidden layer with 16 units gives 2.857% relative error; increasing to 32 units or adding a layer keeps relative errors below 0.1%.

## Context

This work extends the unsupervised deep neural network method of Naito, Naito, and Hashimoto (2023), which previously handled bosonic and fermionic systems without spin or isospin, to include these degrees of freedom crucial for nuclear physics. It differs technically from the supervised pre-training approach of Keeble (2020) and the neural network analysis of Sarmiento (2024), as the present method requires no pre-training and shows stable convergence without overfitting. The ability to handle arbitrary spin and isospin via a non-fully-connected DNN structure enables future applications to more complex nuclear systems beyond the deuteron, such as three-body systems or nuclei with explicit spin-isospin dependence.
