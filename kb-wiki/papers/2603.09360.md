# Microscopic Investigation of Fusion and Quasifission Dynamics

> We introduce the application of Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory to two key aspects of heavy-ion reaction dynamics for producing superheavy elements: fusion and quasi-fission (QF). For fusion reactions $^{48}$Ca+$^{238}$U, the capture cross sections, fusion probabilities, and evaporation-residue cross sections are calculated using the inputs from TDHF simulations, and the results are found to be in reasonable agreement with available experimental data. For the QF process of $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Bk, we show the distribution of the fragments and investigate the impact of the tensor force, significantly enhancing the role of spherical shell effects.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory, when combined with coupled-channel and fusion-by-diffusion models, can reproduce experimental capture and evaporation-residue cross sections for the hot-fusion reaction 48Ca+238U, and that the tensor force in the Skyrme interaction significantly enhances the role of spherical shell effects (N=126, Z=82) in quasifission dynamics for 48Ca+249Bk.

## Method

The authors used the density-constrained frozen Hartree-Fock (DC-FHF) method to compute orientation-dependent internuclear potentials for the 48Ca+238U system, which were then input into the coupled-channels code CCFULL to calculate capture cross sections. Fusion probabilities were obtained from the fusion-by-diffusion (FbD) model with the injection point extracted from TDHF simulations, and evaporation-residue cross sections were computed using a statistical model for neutron emission and fission competition. For the 48Ca+249Bk quasifission study, TDHF calculations were performed with five initial target orientations and a range of impact parameters using both SLy5t (with tensor force) and SLy5 (without tensor force) Skyrme interactions.

## Key numbers

- For 48Ca+238U, the effective fusion probability Pfus was found to be in the range of (2-6) x 10^-4.
- For 48Ca+249Bk quasifission at Ec.m. = 234 MeV, the SLy5t calculation (with tensor force) produced a fragment yield peak centered at N = 126, while the SLy5 calculation (without tensor force) peaked at N ≈ 122.
- For 48Ca+249Bk at Ec.m. = 234 MeV, the SLy5t calculation produced a proton yield peak near Z = 82, while the SLy5 calculation peaked at Z ≈ 79.
- The capture cross section calculations for 48Ca+238U showed significant improvement over the empirical coupled-channels (ECC) model at sub-barrier energies below Ec.m. ≈ 190 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of TDHF theory to the complete hot-fusion reaction chain for superheavy element synthesis, building on prior TDHF studies of fusion (Guo 2012, Washiyama 2015, Sun 2022) and quasifission (Yu 2017, Guo 2018, Li 2022). The hybrid framework combining TDHF with coupled-channels and statistical models enables quantitative predictions for systems where experimental data are scarce, particularly for the synthesis of elements Z = 119 and 120 which have not yet been successfully produced. The tensor force effect on quasifission fragments provides microscopic input for improving phenomenological models of compound nucleus formation.
