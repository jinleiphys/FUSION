# Hydrodynamics with multiple charges and holography

> We establish the connection between thermodynamic and dynamical instabilities in relativistic hydrodynamics with multiple flavours of conserved U(1) charges. In theories with positive hydrodynamic entropy production, where the underlying perfect fluid has a positive speed of sound squared and satisfies the null energy condition, we show that hydrodynamic instabilities can arise only through negative diffusion coefficients associated with the U(1) charges. The onset of such instabilities is governed by the eigenvalues of the thermodynamic Hessian matrix, while the flavour-space polarisations of the unstable diffusion modes are determined by the corresponding eigenvectors. We illustrate this connection using strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory at finite densities of the three U(1) R-charges. In the dual holographic description, the five-dimensional STU black brane exhibits unstable quasinormal modes precisely at the onset of thermodynamic instability. We derive analytic expressions for the R-charge diffusion coefficients in several representative cases, including the configuration with three equal chemical potentials.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that in relativistic hydrodynamics with multiple conserved U(1) charges, hydrodynamic instabilities can arise only through negative diffusion coefficients associated with the charges, provided the underlying perfect fluid has a positive speed of sound squared and satisfies the null energy condition, and the hydrodynamic entropy production is non-negative.

## Method

The authors develop first-order relativistic hydrodynamics for systems with Nf flavours of conserved U(1) charges, deriving the linearised equations for small fluctuations around equilibrium and obtaining dispersion relations for shear, diffusion, and sound modes. They then apply this formalism to strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory at finite densities of the three U(1) R-charges, using the dual holographic description of the five-dimensional STU black brane to compute analytic expressions for the R-charge conductivity matrix in several representative cases (one non-zero chemical potential, two equal chemical potentials, and three equal chemical potentials).

## Key numbers

- The shear mode dispersion relation is ω_(i)^shear(q) = -i η/(ε+p) q² + ..., where η is shear viscosity and ε+p is enthalpy density
- The sound damping coefficient Γ = ((2d-2)/d η + ζ)/(ε+p) + (V±^a σ_ab V±^b)/((ε+p) det(X)² c_s²), where d is the number of spatial dimensions
- For the neutral equilibrium state with all μ_a=0, the diffusion coefficients are D_(a) = σ_(a)/χ_(a), where σ_(a) are charge conductivities and χ_(a) are static charge susceptibilities
- The speed of sound squared in the neutral state is c_s² = (ε+p)/(T (∂ε/∂T))

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier results in Gladden (2024) on the connection between thermodynamic and dynamic instabilities of the STU background, building on the Gubser-Mitra conjecture (Gubser and Mitra, 2000) about equivalence between thermodynamic and dynamic instabilities for black branes. The paper provides analytic expressions for the R-charge conductivity matrix of N=4 SYM theory, which was previously unknown, and demonstrates that R-charge diffusion coefficients become negative at the onset of thermodynamic instability, enabling the prediction of which perturbations of the gravitational background become unstable and at what parameter values.
