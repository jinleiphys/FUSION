# Last Call for RHIC Predictions

> 56 pages, psfig, epsf, epsfig, graphicx style files required, Proceedings of the XIV Int. Conf. on Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions, Quark Matter 99, Torino, Italy, May 10 - 15, 1999. Typographical mistakes corrected and figure numbers changed

## Key claim
This paper compiles pre-RHIC predictions from multiple theoretical approaches, with the explicit caveat that extrapolations from lower-energy AGS and SPS data to RHIC energies are "inevitably rigged with uncertainties" due to unknown nuclear effects such as parton shadowing, jet quenching, and the possible formation of a quark-gluon plasma.

## Method
The contributions use a variety of theoretical frameworks: lowest-order perturbative QCD with K-factors and saturation arguments (Eskola), quasi-classical Yang-Mills calculations resumming powers of α_s² A^{1/3} (Kovchegov), the HIJING event generator incorporating minijet production and string fragmentation (Wang), combined parton cascade (VNI) and hadronic transport (UrQMD) (Bass), ideal hydrodynamics with a first-order phase transition equation of state (Dumitru), the neXus model based on parton ladders with multiple scattering (Drescher), and the UrQMD microscopic transport model (Bleicher). Each approach treats initial conditions, parton evolution, hadronization, and final-state interactions differently.

## Key numbers
- Lower limit for final ⟨E_T⟩ in central Au+Au at RHIC: approximately 600–800 GeV per central rapidity unit, depending on the p_T cutoff p_0 (Eskola, using pQCD + hydrodynamics with K-factor 2.0)
- Lower limit for final N_ch in central Au+Au at RHIC: approximately 600–1000 charged particles per central rapidity unit, read off at p_0 ~ 1.5 GeV (Eskola)
- Charged particle multiplicity dN_ch/dη ≈ 1100 per unit rapidity at midrapidity for Au+Au at √s = 200 GeV, impact parameter b < 2 fm (Drescher, neXus)
- Transverse energy dE_T/dy|_(y=0) ≈ 850 GeV for hadrons, corresponding to energy density ≈ 7 GeV/fm³ (Drescher, neXus)
- Net proton multiplicity ≈ 5 per rapidity unit at y=0, indicating great transparency (Drescher, neXus)
- Net proton multiplicity ≈ 12 per rapidity unit at midrapidity (Bleicher, UrQMD)
- Charged pion multiplicity ≈ 750 per rapidity unit at midrapidity, with ≈ 100 additional charged kaons (Bleicher, UrQMD)
- Initial energy density estimate from pQCD + soft component: ε_i = E_T^i / V_i, with τ_i ~ 1/p_0 ~ 0.1–0.2 fm/c (Eskola)
- Hadron spectra ratio R_AA(p_T) at p_T = 4 GeV/c shows uncertainty of about 25% for p+A, significantly larger for Au+Au (Wang, HIJING)
- dN_ch/dη uncertainty of about a factor of 2 in HIJING, with lowest estimate dN_ch/dη = 550 from A-scaling of SPS Pb+Pb/pp ratio ≈ 250 (Wang)
- Average collective flow velocity increases from 0.35 at hadronization to 0.4–0.45 at freeze-out (Dumitru, hydrodynamics + UrQMD)
- Transverse energy dE_T/dy reduced by 40% from initial 1.2 TeV due to isentropic expansion (Dumitru, hydrodynamics)

## Context
This proceedings volume from Quark Matter 1999 compiles pre-RHIC predictions from many of the major theoretical groups active at the time, including pQCD-based approaches (Eskola, Kovchegov, Wang), transport models (Bass, Bleicher, Drescher, Sorge), and hydrodynamics (Dumitru). It extends earlier work at AGS and SPS energies (e.g., NA49 collaboration results for Pb+Pb at SPS) and provides a benchmark for comparison with the first RHIC data. The predictions enable tests of competing mechanisms for particle production, thermalization, and parton energy loss, and highlight the need for p+A data to constrain nuclear shadowing and for high-p_T spectra to detect jet quenching.
