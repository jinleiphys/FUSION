# Coupled-channels analyses for $^{9,11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb fusion reactions with multi-neutron transfer couplings

> We discuss the role of two-neutron transfer processes in the fusion reaction of the $^{9,11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb systems. We first analyze the $^{9}$Li + $^{208}$Pb reaction by taking into account the coupling to the $^{7}$Li + $^{210}$Pb channel. To this end, we assume that two neutrons are directly transferred to a single effective channel in $^{210}$Pb and solve the coupled-channels equations with the two channels. By adjusting the coupling strength and the effective $Q$-value, we successfully reproduce the experimental fusion cross sections for this system. We then analyze the $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb reaction in a similar manner, that is, by taking into account three effective channels with $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb, $^{9}$Li + $^{210}$Pb, and $^{7}$Li + $^{212}$Pb partitions. In order to take into account the halo structure of the $^{11}$Li nucleus, we construct the potential between $^{11}$Li and $^{208}$Pb with a double folding procedure, while we employ a Wood-Saxon type potential with the global Akyüz-Winther parameters for the other channels. Our calculation indicates that the multiple two-neutron transfer process plays a crucial role in the $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb fusion reaction at energies around the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim

The paper argues that multi-neutron transfer processes, specifically two-neutron transfers to excited states rather than ground states, can simultaneously explain the fusion cross sections for both the $^{9}$Li + $^{208}$Pb and $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb systems, provided the effective $Q$-values and coupling strengths are adjusted to fit the experimental data.

## Method

The authors solved coupled-channels equations for the $^{9}$Li + $^{208}$Pb system using a two-channel model coupling the elastic channel to a single effective two-neutron transfer channel ($^{7}$Li + $^{210}$Pb). For the $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb system, they used a three-channel model including $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb, $^{9}$Li + $^{210}$Pb, and $^{7}$Li + $^{212}$Pb partitions. The $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb potential was constructed via a double folding procedure using a dineutron cluster model for the halo nucleus, while all other channels used the global Akyüz-Winther Woods-Saxon potential.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{9}$Li + $^{208}$Pb system, the ground-state-to-ground-state two-neutron transfer $Q$-value is $Q_{gg}$ = +3.0 MeV, but the fitted effective $Q$-value for the transfer channel is $Q_{23}$ = -3.204 MeV.
- The fitted coupling form factor parameters for the $^{9}$Li + $^{208}$Pb system are: $F_t$ = 51.367 MeV fm, $r_{\rm coup}$ = 1.357 fm, and $a_{\rm coup}$ = 0.264 fm.
- For the $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb system, the fitted effective $Q$-value for the coupling between channels 1 and 2 is $Q$ = +8.346 MeV, with uncertainties $^{+1.163}_{-0.897}$ MeV, and $F_t$ = 40.227 fm, with uncertainties $^{+2.45}_{-2.136}$ fm.
- The two-neutron separation energy of $^{11}$Li used in the dineutron cluster model is $S_{2n}$ = 0.369 MeV.
- The Coulomb barrier height for the $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb channel is $V_b$ = 27.3 MeV, with barrier position $R_b$ = 12.1 fm and barrier curvature $\hbar\Omega$ = 3.04 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled-channels calculations for $^{9}$Li + $^{208}$Pb (PhysRevC.80.054609) and $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb (PhysRevC.87.044603) that failed to reproduce experimental data using only collective excitations. The authors build on the approach of Rowley (2001) for treating transfer to excited states via a single effective channel. The results enable future experimental measurements at more energy points, especially below the Coulomb barrier, to better constrain the role of transfer couplings in fusion of weakly bound halo nuclei.
