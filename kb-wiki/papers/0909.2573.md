# Resonant formation of Lambda(1405) by stopped-K- absorption in deuteron

> To solve the current debate on the position of the quasi-bound K^-p state, namely, "Lambda(1405) or Lambda*(1420)", we propose to measure the T_{21} = T_{Sigma-pi \leftarrow Kbar-N} Sigma-pi invariant-mass spectrum in stopped-K- absorption in deuteron, since the spectrum, reflecting the soft and hard deuteron momentum distribution, is expected to have a narrow quasi-free component with an upper edge of M = 1430 MeV/c^2, followed by a significant "high-momentum" tail toward the lower mass region, where a resonant formation of Lambda(1405) of any mass and width in a wide range is revealed. We introduce a "deviation" spectrum as defined by DEV = OBS (observed or calculated) / QF (non-resonant quasi-free), in which the resonant component can be seen as an isolated peak free from the QF shape.

# Resonant formation of Lambda(1405) by stopped-K- absorption in deuteron

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring the Sigma-pi invariant-mass spectrum from stopped-K- absorption in deuteron can resolve the debate over whether the quasi-bound K-p state is Lambda(1405) or Lambda*(1420), because the deuteron's momentum distribution produces a narrow quasi-free component followed by a tail where resonant formation of Lambda(1405) of any mass and width in a wide range will be clearly revealed. The authors introduce a "deviation" spectrum (DEV = observed/calculated divided by non-resonant quasi-free) to isolate the resonant component as an isolated peak free from the quasi-free shape.

## Method

The authors perform a coupled-channel analysis of the Kbar-N and Sigma-pi system using separable potentials with Yukawa-type form factors, treating the K-p quasi-bound state as a Feshbach resonance. They calculate the Sigma-pi invariant-mass spectra from stopped-K- absorption in deuteron using a neutron-spectator model, where the momentum distribution of the spectator neutron is taken from the realistic Av18 SC potential for the deuteron wavefunction. The transition matrix T_{21} = T_{Sigma-pi ← Kbar-N} is used to generate the spectra, which are then presented as DEV spectra by dividing by the non-resonant quasi-free component.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit values from reanalysis of Riley's He-4 bubble-chamber data: M = 1405.5^{+1.4}_{-1.0} MeV/c^2 and Gamma = 23.6^{+4}_{-3} MeV, with chi^2 ~ 11.5 for ndf = 6
- The Lambda*(1420) ansatz gives chi^2 ~ 92 for ndf = 8 for the same He-4 data, excluded by more than 99.9% statistical confidence
- Best-fit values from ANKE data (p + p → K+ + p + Y^0): M = 1406^{+19}_{-9} MeV/c^2 and Gamma = 40 ± 8 MeV
- Simulation spectra for stopped-K- on deuteron with Lambda(1405)-30 (M=1405, Gamma=30 MeV) yield fitted values M = 1405.4 ± 0.3 MeV/c^2 and Gamma = 29.6 ± 0.8 MeV
- Simulation spectra for Lambda*(1420)-30 yield fitted values M = 1419.6 ± 1.0 MeV/c^2 and Gamma = 27.9 ± 1.2 MeV
- The quasi-free peak has an upper edge at M = 1430 MeV/c^2
- The nuclear Auger process intensity is smaller than the neutron-spectator process by 2 orders of magnitude

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of stopped-K- absorption in He-4, He-3, and deuteron (Esmaili, 2009), where they showed that Sigma-pi invariant-mass spectra reflect resonant formation of a quasi-bound K-p state rather than non-resonant direct capture. It directly addresses the debate between the traditional Lambda(1405) identification (Dalitz and Tuan, 1960; Dalitz and Wong, 1967) and the chiral dynamics double-pole hypothesis (Oller, 2001; Hyodo and Weise, 2008) which places the first pole around 1420 MeV/c^2. The proposed experiment at J-PARC (Suzuki, 2009) aims to provide a decisive result by using the DEV method to extract the resonant T_{21} shape, which the authors argue is insensitive to the presence of any second pole in the Sigma-pi channel.
