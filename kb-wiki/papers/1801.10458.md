# The impact of $\mathbf{K^+Λ}$ photoproduction on the resonance spectrum

> The Jülich-Bonn coupled-channel framework is extended to $K^+Λ$ photoproduction. The spectrum of nucleon and $Δ$ resonances is extracted from simultaneous fits to several pion-induced reactions in addition to pion, eta and $K^+Λ$ photoproduction off the proton. More than 40,000 data points up to a center-of-mass energy of E$\sim$2.3 GeV including recently measured double-polarization observables are analyzed. The influence of the $γp\to K^+Λ$ channel on the extracted resonance parameters and the appearance of states not seen in other channels is investigated. The Jülich-Bonn model includes effective three-body channels and guarantees unitarity and analyticity, which is a prerequisite for a reliable determination of the resonance spectrum in terms of poles and residues.

## Key claim

The Jülich-Bonn coupled-channel framework is extended to include $K^+\Lambda$ photoproduction, and the extracted resonance spectrum shows that the inclusion of this channel can reveal states not seen in other reactions, but the authors caution that the decomposition into pole and non-pole parts is scheme-dependent and only the pole positions and residues of the full amplitude are physically well-defined.

## Method

The Jülich-Bonn dynamical coupled-channel (DCC) approach iterates a hadronic scattering potential, derived from effective Lagrangians using time-ordered perturbation theory, in a Lippmann-Schwinger equation to ensure two-body unitarity, with effective three-body channels ($\rho N$, $\sigma N$, $\pi\Delta$) included to approximate three-body unitarity. The photoproduction kernel for $\gamma p\to K^+\Lambda$ is parametrized by energy-dependent polynomials, while the hadronic final-state interaction is given by the full Jülich-Bonn T-matrix. Simultaneous fits are performed to more than 48,000 data points from pion-induced reactions ($\pi N\to\pi N$, $\pi^-p\to\eta n$, $K^0\Lambda$, $K^0\Sigma^0$, $K^+\Sigma^-$, $\pi^+p\to K^+\Sigma^+$) and photoproduction reactions ($\gamma p\to\pi^0p$, $\pi^+n$, $\eta p$, $K^+\Lambda$) up to a center-of-mass energy of about 2.3 GeV.

## Key numbers

* 48,050 total data points analyzed in simultaneous fits, including 5,936 data points for $\gamma p\to K^+\Lambda$ (differential cross section, recoil polarization $P$, beam asymmetry $\Sigma$, target asymmetry $T$, and beam-recoil observables $C_{x^\prime}$, $C_{z^\prime}$, $O_{x^\prime}$, $O_{z^\prime}$, $O_x$, $O_z$).
* 761 free parameters adjusted in the fit: 134 from hadronic s-channel poles (12 in isospin 1/2, 10 in isospin 3/2), 61 from hadronic contact terms, and 566 from photoproduction parameters.
* Fit performed up to center-of-mass energy $E_{\rm cm}\sim 2.3$ GeV.
* Expansion point for photoproduction polynomials set at $E_s=1077$ MeV (near $\pi N$ threshold).
* Nucleon pole renormalized to $E_0=m_N=938$ MeV and $\pi N$ coupling $f_{\pi NN}=0.964$.

## Context

This work extends the Jülich-Bonn DCC framework, previously applied to pion and eta photoproduction (Ronchen 2015), to include $K^+\Lambda$ photoproduction, building on the hadronic analysis of Ronchen (2012) that included $\pi N$, $\eta N$, $K\Lambda$, and $K\Sigma$ channels. The approach contrasts with single-channel isobar models (Mart 1999, Clymton 2017) and multi-channel K-matrix approaches (Anisovich 2007, Shklyar 2005) by preserving two- and three-body unitarity and analyticity, which is argued to be a prerequisite for reliable resonance extraction. The inclusion of kaon photoproduction enables the search for resonances that couple weakly to $\pi N$ or $\eta N$ but may decay dominantly to $K\Lambda$, potentially addressing the "missing resonance problem" identified by Koniuk (1979).
