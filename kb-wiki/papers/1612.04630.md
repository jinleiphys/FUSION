# Relativistic Chiral Kinetic Theory from Quantum Field Theories

> The chiral kinetic theory of Weyl fermions with collisions in the presence of weak electric and magnetic fields is derived from quantum field theories. It is found that the side-jump terms in the perturbative solution of Wigner functions play a significant role for the derivation. Moreover, such terms manifest the breaking of Lorentz symmetry for distribution functions. The Lorentz covariance of Wigner functions thus leads to modified Lorentz transformation associated with side-jump phenomena further influenced by background fields and collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a relativistic chiral kinetic theory (CKT) for Weyl fermions from quantum field theory, showing that the side-jump terms in the perturbative solution of Wigner functions are essential and that these terms imply the distribution function is not a Lorentz scalar, requiring modified Lorentz transformations that are further influenced by background fields and collisions.

## Method

The authors start from Dirac equations for non-interacting right-handed Weyl fermions, perform a Wigner transformation, and solve the resulting difference equations perturbatively up to order ℏ using a gradient expansion. They then incorporate collisions via the Dyson-Schwinger equation along the Schwinger-Keldysh contour, obtaining the CKT with collision terms, and analyze the Lorentz transformation properties of the distribution function by introducing a frame-dependent four-velocity.

## Key numbers

- The on-shell condition is modified to \(q^2 + \hbar \mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{q} / q_0 = 0\), leading to the energy shift \(q_0 = \epsilon_{\mathbf{q}} = |\mathbf{q}| \left(1 - \frac{\hbar \mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{q}}{2|\mathbf{q}|^3}\right)\).
- The effective velocity is \(\mathbf{\tilde{v}} = \frac{\mathbf{q}}{|\mathbf{q}|} - \frac{\hbar \mathbf{B}}{2|\mathbf{q}|^2} + \frac{\hbar (\mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{q}) \mathbf{q}}{|\mathbf{q}|^4}\).
- The particle number density is \(J^0 = \int \frac{d^3q}{(2\pi)^3} \left(1 + \hbar \mathbf{B} \cdot \boldsymbol{\Omega}_{\mathbf{q}}\right) f\), where \(\boldsymbol{\Omega}_{\mathbf{q}} = \mathbf{q}/(2|\mathbf{q}|^3)\) is the Berry curvature.
- The spatial current includes terms for the anomalous Hall effect, chiral magnetic effect, and side-jump, valid up to \(\mathcal{O}(\hbar)\).

## Context

This work extends the field-theory derivations of chiral kinetic theory by Son and Yamamoto (2012) and Gao, Liang, Pu, Wang, and Wang (2012) beyond the assumptions of predominant chemical potential or local equilibrium. It supports the modified Lorentz transformation with side-jump phenomena proposed by Chen, Son, Stephanov, Yee, and Yin (2014, 2015) from semi-classical approaches, and systematically incorporates collisions into the quantum field theory framework. The results enable further studies of anomalous transport in non-equilibrium systems such as heavy-ion collisions and Weyl semimetals, and provide a foundation for including higher-order quantum corrections.
