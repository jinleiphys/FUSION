# SU(5) Symmetry of spdfg Interacting Boson Model

> The extended interacting boson model with s-, p-, d-, f- and g-bosons being included (spdfg IBM) are investigated. The algebraic structure including the generators, the Casimir operators of the groups at the SU(5) dynamical symmetry and the branching rules of the irreducible representation reductions along the group chain are obtained. The typical energy spectrum of the Symmetry is given.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the SU(5) dynamical symmetry of the spdfg interacting boson model (IBM) can generate both vibrational bands with a ΔI = 4 angular momentum sequence and rotational bands with a ΔI = 2 sequence, and that the resulting ΔI = 4 bifurcation in normally deformed and superdeformed states may be attributed to nuclei possessing this SU(5) symmetry, but this is presented as a possibility rather than a proven fact.

## Method

The authors constructed the algebraic structure of the SU(5) dynamical symmetry for the spdfg IBM by identifying the group chain U(25) ⊃ U_sdg(15) ⊗ U_pf(10) ⊃ SU_sdg(5) ⊗ SU_pf(5) ⊃ SU_spdfg(5) ⊃ SO_spdfg(5) ⊃ SO_spdfg(3). They derived the generators, Casimir operators, and branching rules for irreducible representation reductions at each step of this chain. They then wrote a Hamiltonian as a linear combination of the Casimir operators and calculated the energy spectrum for a system with boson number N=3 using a chosen set of parameters.

## Key numbers

- Boson number N=3 was used for the example energy spectrum calculation.
- The single-boson space is 25-dimensional, summed as 1 + 3 + 5 + 7 + 9 = 25.
- Parameters chosen for the example spectrum: ε = 0.3 MeV, ε′ = 0.7 MeV, A = A′ = 0, B = -0.06 MeV, C = 0.05 MeV, D = 0.006 MeV.
- The SO(5) symmetry contribution to γ-ray energy E_γ is given as E_γ^(SO(5))([I] = 4k + 2) = 6C and E_γ^(SO(5))([I] = 4k) = (4[I/2] - 4)C.

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) symmetry of the spdfg IBM, which was previously investigated by Long (1998), by analyzing the alternative SU(5) dynamical symmetry. The paper builds on earlier studies showing that the sdg IBM describes superdeformed states with positive parity (OH 1991, Kuy 1996, Liu 1997, LSZ 1997) and that the spdf IBM describes octupole deformation (EI 1985, Kus 1990, Sun 1991, Liu 1994, Liu 1994, JL 1999). The results enable future calculation of electromagnetic transition rates and selection rules, which the authors state are too complicated to include in this work, and suggest applicability to describing shape coexistence and phase transitions in nuclei.
