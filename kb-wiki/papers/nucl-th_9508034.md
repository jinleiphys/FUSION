# Universal pattern in (e,e'p) at large missing momenta: quasi-deuteron or diffractive final state interactions?

> The intrinsic single particle momentum distributions in nuclei are supposed to show a universal behavior at large momenta, dominated by short-range correlated pairs, or quasi-deuterons. We discuss whether the quasi-deuteron universality survives the final state interaction effects, which are present in the missing momentum spectra measured in $A(e,e'p)$ experiments at GeV energies. We demonstrate that in the observed missing momentum spectra an approximate universality is present, but originating from the universal pattern of diffractive final state interactions of the struck proton independent of the target nucleus.

## Key claim

The observed approximate universality in missing momentum spectra from \(A(e,e'p)\) reactions at large missing momenta does not originate from the intrinsic quasi-deuteron short-range correlations in the nuclear ground state, but instead arises from the universal pattern of diffractive final state interactions of the struck proton, which are independent of the target nucleus.

## Method

The authors calculated the missing momentum distribution \(W(\vec{p}_m)\) for \((e,e'p)\) reactions on deuteron and \(^4\)He targets at GeV energies, including final state interactions via the Glauber model with no free parameters. For the deuteron, realistic Bonn and Paris wave functions were used; for \(^4\)He, a harmonic oscillator mean-field wave function with Jastrow-type correlations was employed. The calculations were performed for transverse, forward longitudinal, and backward longitudinal kinematics, and the results for the two targets were compared.

## Key numbers

* The total cross section for \(pN\) scattering in the GeV energy range is \(\sigma_{tot} \approx 40\) mb.
* The diffraction slope parameter is \(b_o \approx 0.5\) fm.
* The ratio of real to imaginary parts of the forward elastic \(pN\) scattering amplitude is \(\rho \approx 0.3\div 0.4\).
* The correlation radius used in the Jastrow-type wave function for \(^4\)He is \(r_c = 0.5\) fm.
* The harmonic oscillator parameter for \(^4\)He is \(R_o = 1.29\) fm, chosen to reproduce the experimental charge radius.
* The similarity between the FSI-inclusive distributions for \(^4\)He and deuteron is striking at missing momenta \(p_m\) larger than 1.5 fm\(^{-1}\).
* In transverse kinematics, the full distributions \(W(\vec{p}_m)\) at large \(p_m\) differ by one order of magnitude between transverse and longitudinal kinematics for the same nucleus, attesting to FSI dominance.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of final state interactions in \((e,e'p)\) on \(^4\)He (Helium4) and deuteron (Deuter) targets. It contradicts the expectation that the large-momentum tail of the single-particle momentum distribution, dominated by short-range correlated pairs or quasi-deuterons as discussed by Levinger and others, would be directly observable in \((e,e'p)\) experiments. The results show that testing short-range structures in the nuclear ground state is much more difficult than previously thought, especially in transverse kinematics, and that detailed studies of universal features of nuclear FSI in the diffractive regime are now enabled.
