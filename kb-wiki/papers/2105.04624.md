# Incoherent approximation for neutron up-scattering cross sections and its corrections for slow neutrons and low crystal temperatures

> The incoherent approximation (IA) is often used for calculating the one-phonon inelastic neutron scattering cross section for arbitrary solids. It is valid for thermal neutrons but for slow neutrons it requires a correction, which is significant for isotopes that are strong coherent scatterers. In this article, we present the extension of the Placzek--Van Hove corrections for slow neutrons in the limit of low temperatures using the example of solid \emph{ortho}-deuterium (sD$_2$). Our approach yields realistic one-phonon up-scattering cross sections for sD$_2$ and shows the IA to be a factor of 2 to 5 too high for ultracold neutron (UCN) up-scattering in sD$_2$. Our calculations are compared with previously published Monte Carlo calculations of the one-phonon cross section based on the dynamic structure function $S(q,ω)$ of polycrystalline \emph{ortho}-deuterium and are found to be consistent with them. Furthermore, we provide the means for easily replicable calculations of the one-phonon up-scattering cross sections of solid \emph{ortho}-deuterium for slow neutrons. These should from now on be used in calculations and simulations of UCN scattering in sD$_2$.

## Key claim

The incoherent approximation (IA) overestimates the one-phonon up-scattering cross section for ultracold neutrons (UCNs) in solid *ortho*-deuterium (sD₂) by a factor of 2 to 5, and the authors extend the Placzek–Van Hove corrections to the low-temperature limit to obtain realistic cross sections that should replace the uncorrected IA in future calculations.

## Method

The authors applied the Placzek–Van Hove (PVH) correction formalism, originally developed for thermal and slow neutrons at room temperature, to the low-temperature limit of solid *ortho*-deuterium. They calculated the interference correction term δS/S^inc by evaluating the coherent contribution from the two smallest reduced lattice vectors of an equivalent fcc structure, using a Debye temperature of 70 K for acoustic phonons and a correction factor for optical phonons. The corrected one-phonon up-scattering cross sections were obtained by multiplying the IA result by the correction factor χ = 1 + (σ^coh/σ^sc)(δS/S^inc).

## Key numbers

- The IA overestimates the one-phonon up-scattering cross section for UCNs in sD₂ by a factor of 2 to 5.
- At T = 5 K, the corrected cross section σ^-1ph = 0.04 barn per molecule (IA: 0.19 barn); at T = 18 K, σ^-1ph = 6.70 barn (IA: 12.9 barn), for a UCN velocity of 10 m/s.
- The correction factor χ_D2 ranges from 0.19 at 5 K to 0.52 at 18 K, compared to χ^Liu_D2 values of 0.22 and 0.54 from Monte Carlo calculations by Liu et al. (2010).
- The Stepanov equation yields cross sections about 40% above the full IA at T = 18 K (18.4 barn vs. 12.9 barn).
- For thermal neutrons, PVH corrections are marginal: -1.6% for copper and -0.1% for aluminum; for slow neutrons at room temperature, corrections are -8% for copper and +11% for aluminum.
- The Debye temperature for acoustic phonons in sD₂ is Θ_D = 70 K; the Debye vector q_D = 1.21 Å⁻¹; the fill factor f = 1.98.

## Context

This work extends the Placzek–Van Hove correction (Placzek and Van Hove, 1955) to the low-temperature limit, correcting the incoherent approximation that was previously used for UCN scattering in sD₂ by Atchison et al. (2005). The corrected cross sections agree within 14% with first-principles Monte Carlo calculations of the dynamic structure function S(q,ω) by Liu et al. (2010), mutually validating both approaches. The results enable more accurate calculations of UCN extraction rates from sD₂-based converters and shed new light on the role of crystal defects in such systems, as discussed in the PhD thesis of Döge (2019).
