# Octet Baryons in Large Magnetic Fields

> Magnetic properties of octet baryons are investigated within the framework of chiral perturbation theory. Utilizing a power counting for large magnetic fields, the Landau levels of charged mesons are treated exactly giving rise to baryon energies that depend non-analytically on the strength of the magnetic field. In the small-field limit, baryon magnetic moments and polarizabilities emerge from the calculated energies. We argue that the magnetic polarizabilities of hyperons provide a testing ground for potentially large contributions from decuplet pole diagrams. In external magnetic fields, such contributions manifest themselves through decuplet-octet mixing, for which possible results are compared in a few scenarios. These scenarios can be tested with lattice QCD calculations of the octet baryon energies in magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The magnetic polarizabilities of hyperons provide a testing ground for potentially large contributions from decuplet pole diagrams, which manifest through decuplet-octet mixing in external magnetic fields; these contributions can be disentangled from short-distance diamagnetic contributions by diagonalizing the magnetically coupled decuplet-octet system, and the resulting scenarios can be tested with lattice QCD calculations.

## Method

The authors used three-flavor heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) with a power counting that treats the magnetic field non-perturbatively relative to meson masses and momenta, summing charged-meson Landau levels exactly via Schwinger's proper-time method. They computed octet baryon self-energies to third order in the combined chiral and heavy baryon expansion, including tree-level magnetic moment and kinetic terms, decuplet pole diagrams from the magnetic dipole transition operator, and meson-loop contributions with intermediate octet and decuplet baryons. Partial resummation of SU(3)_V breaking was employed by using physical baryon mass splittings and distinct meson decay constants for each isospin multiplet.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic fields of order eB/m_π² ∼ 3–4 are required for extraction of polarizabilities from lattice QCD, assuming m_π L ∼ 4 and the 't Hooft quantization condition eB = 6π n / L².
- Charged-meson decay constants used: f_π = 130 MeV and f_K = 156 MeV from experiment.
- For charged baryons in the lowest Landau level with zero longitudinal momentum, the kinetic energy contribution is E = |QeB|/(2M_B).
- The decuplet pole diagram contributes to octet baryon energies at order (eB/2M_N)² with coefficient α_T μ_U²/Δ, where Δ is the decuplet-octet mass splitting.

## Context

This work extends the external-field chiral perturbation theory approach previously applied to the chiral condensate (Cohen 2007, Tiburzi 2008) and nucleon properties to the full octet of baryons. It addresses the well-known difficulty with nucleon magnetic polarizabilities, where large paramagnetic contributions from the delta-pole diagram (Mukhopadhyay 1993) have been problematic, by showing that such contributions can be systematically treated through decuplet-octet mixing in magnetic fields. The results enable future lattice QCD computations of octet baryon energies in magnetic fields to disentangle paramagnetic and diamagnetic contributions and test the role of U-spin and large-N_c symmetries in baryon magnetic properties.
