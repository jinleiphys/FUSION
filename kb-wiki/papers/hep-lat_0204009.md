# Quenched chiral logarithms in lattice QCD with exact chiral symmetry

> We examine quenched chiral logarithms in lattice QCD with overlap Dirac quark. For 100 gauge configurations generated with the Wilson gauge action at $ β= 5.8 $ on the $ 8^3 \times 24 $ lattice, we compute quenched quark propagators for 12 bare quark masses. The pion decay constant is extracted from the pion propagator, and from which the lattice spacing is determined to be 0.147 fm. The presence of quenched chiral logarithm in the pion mass is confirmed, and its coefficient is determined to be $ δ= 0.203 \pm 0.014 $, in agreement with the theoretical estimate in quenched chiral perturbation theory. Further, we obtain the topological susceptibility of these 100 gauge configurations by measuring the index of the overlap Dirac operator. Using a formula due to exact chiral symmetry, we obtain the $ η' $ mass in quenched chiral perturbation theory, $ m_{η'} = (901 \pm 64) $ Mev, and an estimate of $ δ= 0.197 \pm 0.027 $, which is in good agreement with that determined from the pion mass.

## Key claim

The paper confirms the presence of quenched chiral logarithms in lattice QCD using overlap Dirac quarks, and demonstrates that the coefficient δ extracted from the pion mass (δ = 0.203 ± 0.014) agrees with the value obtained independently from the topological susceptibility (δ = 0.197 ± 0.027), satisfying a consistency criterion that previous lattice fermion schemes failed to meet.

## Method

The authors generated 100 gauge configurations using the Wilson gauge action at β = 5.8 on an 8³ × 24 lattice. They computed quenched quark propagators for 12 bare quark masses using the overlap Dirac operator with exact chiral symmetry, employing Zolotarev optimal rational approximation for the inverse square root of H_w² and nested conjugate gradient loops. The pion mass and decay constant were extracted from pion propagators, and the index of the overlap Dirac operator was measured via the spectral flow method to obtain topological susceptibility and the η' mass.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.147(1) fm, determined from f_π a = 0.0984(3) at m_q a = 0, using the experimental value f_π = 132 MeV
- Coefficient of quenched chiral logarithm from pion mass: δ = 0.2034 ± 0.0140, with χ²/d.o.f. = 0.03
- η' mass in quenched chiral perturbation theory: m_η' = (901 ± 64) MeV
- Coefficient of quenched chiral logarithm from index susceptibility: δ = 0.197 ± 0.027
- Smallest pion mass: approximately 418 MeV
- Lattice size: approximately (1.2 fm)³ × 3.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the search for quenched chiral logarithms in lattice QCD, which had previously yielded smaller δ values (0.06-0.12) with Wilson-Dirac quarks (Aoki 1999, Bardeen 2000) and δ ≈ 0.06 with staggered quarks (Bernard 2001). A prior study using overlap fermions (Dong 2001) gave δ ≈ 0.41(9), which was too large compared to the theoretical expectation δ ≈ 0.2. The present paper establishes consistency between δ from the pion mass and from the index susceptibility, satisfying a basic criterion for lattice QCD to realize continuum chiral dynamics. The derivation of the η' mass from the index susceptibility provides a practical alternative to computing the hairpin diagram directly.
