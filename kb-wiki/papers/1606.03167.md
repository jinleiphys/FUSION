# Solutions of random-phase approximation equation for positive-semidefinite stability matrix

> It is mathematically proven that, if the stability matrix $\mathsf{S}$ is positive-semidefinite, solutions of the random-phase approximation (RPA) equation are all physical or belong to Nambu-Goldstone (NG) modes, and the NG-mode solutions may form Jordan blocks of $\mathsf{N\,S}$ ($\mathsf{N}$ is the norm matrix) but their dimension is not more than two. This guarantees that the NG modes in the RPA can be separated out via canonically conjugate variables.

## Key claim

The paper proves that when the stability matrix S is positive-semidefinite (rather than positive-definite), all solutions of the random-phase approximation equation are either physical or Nambu-Goldstone modes, and the Jordan blocks formed by the NG-mode solutions of the matrix N S have dimension at most two, contrary to prior examples where the dualities did not limit Jordan block dimensionality.

## Method

The author constructs the Hermitian matrix \(\tilde{\mathsf{S}} = \mathsf{S}^{1/2} \mathsf{N} \mathsf{S}^{1/2}\) whose eigenvalue problem is almost equivalent to that of \(\mathsf{N S}\). By diagonalizing \(\tilde{\mathsf{S}}\) and analyzing the kernel of \(\tilde{\mathsf{S}}\), the solutions are classified into physical modes (Class 1) with nonzero eigenvalues and NG modes (Class 5) with zero eigenvalues. For the NG-mode solutions, the proof uses the Jordan block condition \(\mathsf{S} \boldsymbol{\xi}_2^{(\nu)} = i c_1^{(\nu)} \mathsf{N} \boldsymbol{x}_\nu\) and the positive-semidefiniteness of S to show that the Jordan block dimension \(d_\nu\) cannot exceed two and that such blocks are self LR-dual.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionality of the stability matrix S is denoted by \(2D\).
- For \(\tilde{K} < \nu \leq D\), the eigenvalues satisfy \(0 \leq \omega_1 \leq \omega_2 \leq \cdots \leq \omega_D\), with \(\omega_1 = \omega_2 = \cdots = \omega_{\tilde{K}} = 0\) where \(\tilde{K}\) is the dimensionality of \(\mathrm{Ker}(\tilde{\mathsf{S}})\).
- The Jordan block dimension for NG-mode solutions is \(d_\nu = 2\) (not more than two).

## Context

This work extends the author's prior classification of RPA solutions into five categories using UL- and LR-dualities (Nakada, 2016) and reconfirms the earlier result that positive-definite S yields only physical solutions (Thouless, 1961). It provides the rigorous mathematical justification for the established method of handling NG modes via canonically conjugate variables (Thouless, 1961; Thouless and Valatin, 1962; Ring and Schuck, 1980), which had previously presumed two-dimensional Jordan blocks without proof. The result enables the clean separation of NG modes from physical excitations in self-bound systems like atomic nuclei where spontaneous symmetry breaking occurs.
