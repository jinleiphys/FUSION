# Quark rotation asymmetry and baryon magnetic moments

> Quark rotation asymmetry is proposed in calculating baryon magnetic moments. After taking into account interactions enforced on constituent quarks, assumed to be linear and Coulomb potentials, respectively, and the quark rotation asymmetry, we fit the theoretical values, based on two more hypotheses and several other reasonable assumptions, of baryon magnetic moments with those from experiments. The good fitting results show the necessity of the consideration of quark rotation asymmetry within baryon.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the rotation of constituent quarks within baryons is asymmetric due to mass differences between quarks, and that this asymmetry, combined with simple linear or Coulomb interaction potentials, can reproduce experimental baryon magnetic moments. The authors hedge that their assumptions about the interaction potentials are "too simple" and that the orbital contribution is a "quasiclassical approximation."

## Method

The authors start from the Naive Quark Model (NQM) expressions for baryon magnetic moments, expressed in terms of quark spin polarizations (Δq) and a modified set (δq) that differs in the sign of antiquark contributions. They introduce two hypotheses for the sea quark contribution (spin-zero meson cloud or gluon splitting) and then add an orbital angular momentum term ⟨Lz⟩ from revolving quarks, assuming the centripetal force is provided by either a linear potential (U=cr) or a Coulomb potential (U=c/r). The mass-dependent radius for each quark is derived from the force balance, and the resulting magnetic moment formulae are fit to experimental data using μu, ⟨Sz⟩, and ⟨Lz⟩ as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- For the linear potential with Hypothesis I (fit 2), the best fit gives χ²/DoF = 1.09, with fitted parameters μu = 2.28, ⟨Sz⟩ = -0.03, and ⟨Lz⟩ = 0.23.
- For the Coulomb potential with Hypothesis I (fit 1), the best fit gives χ²/DoF = 1.14, with fitted parameters μu = 2.22 and ⟨Sz⟩ = 0.17.
- The mass ratio used is λ = md/ms = 0.6, and for the Coulomb case λ* = (0.6)^(1/3).
- The experimental magnetic moment of the proton is 2.79 ± 0.10 nuclear magnetons; the linear potential fit 2 under Hypothesis I gives μL(p) = 2.66.
- The experimental magnetic moment of the neutron is -1.91 ± 0.10; the Coulomb potential fit 1 under Hypothesis I gives μC(n) = -1.80.

## Context

This work extends the collective rotation model of Casu and Sehgal (1997), who assumed an equilateral triangle configuration for quarks, by introducing quark rotation asymmetry due to mass differences. It also builds on the earlier "rotating proton" concept of Chou and Yang (1976) and Sehgal (1974), and addresses the "spin crisis" raised by the European Muon Collaboration (1988). The paper enables a simple, semi-classical method for calculating baryon magnetic moments that can be tested against future experimental data on nucleon structure, potentially from LHC experiments.
