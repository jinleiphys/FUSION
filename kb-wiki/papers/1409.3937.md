# Shell model and deformed shell model spectroscopy of $^{62}$Ga

> In the present work we have reported comprehensive analysis of recently available experimental data [H.M. David et al., Phys. Lett. B {\bf 726}, 665 (2013)] for high-spin states up to $17^+$ with $T=0$ in the odd-odd $N=Z$ nucleus $^{62}$Ga using shell model calculations within the full $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space and deformed shell model based on Hartee-Fock intrinsic states in the same space. The calculations have been performed using jj44b effective interaction developed recently by B.A. Brown and A.F. Lisetskiy for this model space. The results obtained with the two models are similar and they are in reasonable agreement with experimental data. In addition to the $T=0$ and $T=1$ energy bands, band crossings and electromagnetic transition probabilities, we have also calculated the pairing energy in shell model and all these compare well with the available theoretical results.

## Key claim

Shell model and deformed shell model calculations using the jj44b effective interaction in the full \(f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}\) model space reproduce the recently measured high-spin states up to \(17^+\) with \(T=0\) in \(^{62}\)Ga reasonably well, although the shell model predicts the lowest \(T=0\) level at 148 keV excitation energy compared to the experimental value of 571 keV, indicating a systematic compression of the \(T=0\)–\(T=1\) band-head separation.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed with the NuShell code using the jj44b effective interaction (developed by Brown and Lisetskiy) within the full \(f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}\) model space, taking \(^{56}\)Ni as an inert core. Deformed shell model calculations were carried out by first solving the Hartree-Fock equations self-consistently for prolate and oblate intrinsic states, then projecting states of good angular momentum and isospin from 44 low-lying intrinsic configurations, followed by band mixing. Both calculations used the same model space and effective interaction, with single-particle energies of -9.6566, -9.2859, -8.2695, and -5.8944 MeV for the \(p_{3/2}\), \(f_{5/2}\), \(p_{1/2}\), and \(g_{9/2}\) orbits respectively.

## Key numbers

- The shell model predicts the lowest \(T=0\) (\(1^+\)) level at 148 keV excitation energy, while the experimental value is 571 keV.
- The maximum matrix dimension in the \(M\)-scheme shell model calculation is 91,564 for \(0^+\) states.
- The \(B(E2,3_1^+ \rightarrow 1_1^+)\) for \(T=0\) is 6.46 W.u. from shell model and 24.7 W.u. from deformed shell model, compared to the experimental value of \(12^{+6}_{-3}\) W.u.
- The \(B(E2,2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+)\) for \(T=1\) is 42.57 W.u. from shell model and 51.6 W.u. from deformed shell model.
- The \(B(M1,0_1^+ \rightarrow 1_1^+)\) for \(T=1 \rightarrow T=0\) is 1.39 \(\mu_N^2\) from shell model and 1.2 \(\mu_N^2\) from deformed shell model.
- The \(17^+\) state in the lowest \(T=0\) band has a dominant configuration \(f_{5/2}^2 p_{3/2}^2 g_{9/2}^2\) with 68.87% probability.
- The number of \(T=0\) levels up to 1.7 MeV excitation is 7 from experiment, 17 from shell model, and 10 from deformed shell model.
- The pairing energy contributions for the lowest \(T=0\) band show isoscalar pairing of about 2 MeV and isovector pairing of about 1 MeV; for the lowest \(T=1\) band, isoscalar pairing is about 0.8 MeV and isovector pairing about 2.3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous shell model and deformed shell model studies of \(^{62}\)Ga by Juodagalvis and Aberg (2010) and earlier work by the same group (Srivastava et al., 2005), using the newer jj44b effective interaction rather than the Madrid-Strasbourg interaction. The calculations comprehensively explain the high-spin data up to \(17^+\) from David et al. (2013) obtained with the Gammasphere array, and also address the low level density observed in \(^{62}\)Ga compared to neutron-rich odd-odd Ga isotopes. The results enable future studies of \(T=0\) versus \(T=1\) pairing in heavier \(N=Z\) odd-odd nuclei and motivate improvements to effective interactions in the \(f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}\) space, including the possible inclusion of the \(1f_{7/2}\) orbital.
