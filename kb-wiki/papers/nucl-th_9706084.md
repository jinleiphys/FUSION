# Branching ratio change in K- absorption at rest and the nature of the Lambda(1405)

> We investigate in-medium corrections to the branching ratio in K- absorption at rest and their effect on the (positively and negatively) charged pion spectrum. The in-medium corrections are due to Pauli blocking, which arises if the Lambda(1405) is assumed to be a $\bar{K}$-nucleon bound state and leads to a density and momentum dependent mass shift of the Lambda(1405). Requiring that the optical potential as well as the branching ratio are derived from the same elementary T-matrix, we find that the in-medium corrected, density dependent T-matrix gives a better description of the K- absorption reaction than the free, density-independent one. This result suggests that the dominant component of the Lambda(1405) wave function is the $\bar{K}N$ bound state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the in-medium branching ratio change in K- absorption at rest on carbon-12 is better described when the Lambda(1405) is treated as a bound state of antikaon-nucleon (Kbar N) rather than a genuine three-quark state, with the caveat that this conclusion depends on requiring the optical potential and branching ratio to be derived from the same elementary T-matrix.

## Method

The authors calculated the in-medium T-matrix for the I=0 Kbar N channel using a coupled-channel Schrodinger equation with separable potentials, incorporating Pauli blocking effects that produce a density- and momentum-dependent mass shift of the Lambda(1405). They then performed a Monte Carlo cascade simulation of K- absorption at rest on carbon-12, including absorption point distributions from the optical potential, branching ratios, multiple scattering, Sigma conversion to Lambda, and hyperon mean-field potentials, comparing the resulting charged pion spectra with experimental data from Tamura (1989) and Kubota (1996).

## Key numbers

- The Lambda(1405) mass shift at half nuclear matter density (rho_0/2) with zero momentum produces an upward shift visible in the scattering amplitude peak position (Fig. 1)
- The K- optical potential changes from repulsive to attractive at a nucleon density of approximately 0.25 rho_0 in the bound-state model (KF)
- The extracted multiplicity ratio of quasi-free pi+ to Sigma+ particles is 0.554 from experiment, compared to 1.075 in the KF model (bound state) and 2.180 in the MFb model (Fermi smearing only)
- The branching ratio for K- p to Sigma+ pi- is 26.4% in the KF model versus 37.7% experimentally, while the free-space value (model M) is 4.4%
- The branching ratio for K- p to Sigma- pi+ is 17.5% in the KF model versus 16.8% experimentally, while the free-space value is 28.7%
- The Lambda(1405) model parameters used are Lambda = 0.78 GeV and g^2 = 17.9

## Context

This work extends the bound-state picture of the Lambda(1405) proposed by Dalitz, Wong, and Rajasekaran (1967) and by Siegel and Weise (1988), and builds on the in-medium treatment of Koch (1994) which showed that Pauli blocking shifts the Lambda(1405) mass upward. The paper contradicts the assumption that Fermi smearing and binding energy corrections alone (as in the work of Martin, 1981) can explain the branching ratio changes observed in bubble chamber data by Wilquet (1977) and in counter experiments by Tamura (1989) and Kubota (1996). The results enable future extraction of the density- and momentum-dependent mass shift of the Lambda(1405) in reactions such as (gamma, K+), (pi, K+), and (K-, pi) as suggested by Koch and Ohnishi (1997).
