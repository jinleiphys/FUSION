# Towards an extraction of $\hat{q}$ with an uncertainty controlled energy loss Monte-Carlo

> Recent progress in open-heavy-flavor measurements and future experimental upgrades are bringing heavy-flavor physics into the precision era, allowing for strong quantitative constraints on the transport properties of heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma. Starting from the LIDO transport model, which combines a matrix-element based linearized-Boltzmann transport and diffusion-based Langevin equation, we have made two essential improvements to increase both the flexibility and physical accuracy of the model. First, we have absorbed the pQCD scatterings with small momentum transfers to the medium into the diffusion part of the LIDO model, and we have restricted the use of vacuum matrix elements to large momentum transfer processes. This study allows us to construct a model that smoothly interpolates between a pure pQCD based approach and a radiation-improved Langevin equation by tuning a single scale parameter. Second, the Monte-Carlo implementation of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect of the original model has been improved to account for multiple scatterings of gluons. The simulated radiative energy loss can be tuned to quantitatively agree with semi-analytic theory calculations both for a static (finite / infinite) medium and for a dynamic expanding medium. With such improvements, the LIDO model will greatly facilitate the extraction of heavy-quark transport coefficient from a systematic model-to-data comparison.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their improved LIDO transport model, which separates soft and hard interactions by a scale parameter and implements a more sophisticated Monte-Carlo treatment of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect, can be tuned to quantitatively agree with semi-analytic theory calculations for radiative energy loss in both static and expanding media, thereby enabling a controlled extraction of the heavy-quark transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) from future model-to-data comparisons.

## Method

The authors modified the LIDO model by introducing a separation scale \(Q_{\mathrm{cut}}\) proportional to the screening mass \(m_D\) to divide perturbative interactions into soft and hard modes: soft interactions (momentum transfer below \(Q_{\mathrm{cut}}\)) are absorbed into a diffusion process with an associated \(1\rightarrow 2\) gluon radiation, while hard interactions use vacuum matrix elements. They also implemented an improved Monte-Carlo treatment of the LPM effect where preformed gluons are carried by the mother parton and only become formed (carrying away energy) when the formation time \(\tau_f \sim \omega/k_\perp^2\) is less than the elapsed time, with a probability \(P = \tilde{\lambda}/\tau_f\) where \(\tilde{\lambda} \sim m_D^2/\hat{q}\) is an effective mean-free-path.

## Key numbers

- The simulated gluon radiation spectra from a light quark in an infinite medium at temperature \(T = 0.5\) GeV with \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) quantitatively agree with next-to-leading-log AMY calculations in the LPM regime (\(2\pi T \lesssim \omega < E\)).
- The model reproduces BDMPS calculations for an expanding medium with power-law temperature profile \(T^3 \propto \tau^{1/\nu-2}\) at expansion rates \(\nu = 0.5\) (static), 0.75, 1.0 (Bjorken), and 1.5.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, natural choices of the medium scale parameter \(\mu = 1\) and \(\mu = 2\) (where the coupling runs as \(\alpha_s(\max\{Q, \mu\pi T\})\)) reasonably describe ALICE and CMS \(D\)-meson \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) data at \(p_T > 10\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the LIDO transport model originally developed for heavy quarks by Ke et al. (2018) and incorporates the soft/hard separation scheme proposed by Ghiglieri et al. (2015) for next-to-leading-order jet transport calculations. The improved LPM implementation is motivated by the approach of Zapp et al. (2011). The model enables future large-scale Bayesian calibration to extract the functional form of the heavy quark transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\), using event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations with parameters from Bernhard et al. (2018).
