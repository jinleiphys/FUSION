# Order-by-order Anisotropic Transport Coefficients of a Magnetised Fluid: a Chapman-Enskog Approach

> We derive the first and second-order expressions for the shear, the bulk viscosity, and the thermal conductivity of a relativistic hot boson gas in a magnetic field using the relativistic kinetic theory within the Chapman-Enskog method. The order-by-order off-equilibrium distribution function is obtained in terms of the associate Laguerre polynomial with magnetic field-dependent coefficients using the relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport equation. The order-by-order anisotropic transport coefficients are evaluated in powers of the dimensionless ratio of kinetic energy to the fluid temperature for finite magnetic fields. In a magnetic field, the shear viscosity (in all order) splits into five different coefficients. Four of them show a magnetic field dependence as seen in a previous study \cite{Ashutosh1} using the relaxation time approximation for the collision kernel. On the other hand, bulk viscosity, which splits into three components (in all order), is independent of the magnetic field. The thermal conductivity shows a similar splitting but is field-dependent. The difference in the first and second-order results are prominent for the thermal conductivities than the shear viscosity; moreover, the difference in the two results is most evident at low temperatures. The first and second-order results seem to converge rapidly for high temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper derives first- and second-order expressions for anisotropic transport coefficients of a relativistic hot boson gas in a magnetic field using the Chapman-Enskog method, finding that the difference between first- and second-order results is most evident at low temperatures (around 80-100 MeV) and that the two orders converge rapidly for high temperatures, with the qualification that the study is limited to a pion gas in the temperature range of 80-200 MeV and magnetic fields up to 10 times the pion mass squared.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport equation for a pion gas using the Chapman-Enskog method, expanding the off-equilibrium distribution function in terms of associate Laguerre polynomials with magnetic field-dependent coefficients. The linearized collision integral was expanded order-by-order in powers of the dimensionless ratio of kinetic energy to fluid temperature, and the transport coefficients were obtained by truncating the polynomial series at first and second order. The pion-pion scattering cross sections were taken from a parametrized form using phase shifts from sigma and rho meson resonances.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: 80-200 MeV (pion gas)
- Magnetic field strengths considered: \(m_\pi^2\), \(5m_\pi^2\), and \(10m_\pi^2\) (where \(m_\pi\) is the pion mass)
- All five shear viscous coefficients (\(\eta_0\) through \(\eta_4\)) increase monotonically with temperature for all magnetic fields considered
- \(\eta_0\) (the parallel component) has the largest value among the five shear coefficients in the temperature range studied
- The difference between first- and second-order results for shear viscosity is only visible for \(\eta_0\) at low temperature (approximately 80 MeV)
- Bulk viscosity splits into three components (\(\zeta_{||}\), \(\zeta_{\perp}\), \(\zeta_{\times}\)) with \(\zeta_{\times}=0\) at both first and second order
- Bulk viscosity is independent of the magnetic field
- For thermal conductivity, \(\lambda_0\) is independent of magnetic fields, while \(\lambda_1\) and \(\lambda_2\) increase with magnetic fields
- The difference between \([\lambda_2]_1\) and \([\lambda_2]_2\) is significant at low temperatures (approximately 100 MeV) and increases with larger magnetic fields

## Context

This work extends previous studies of anisotropic transport coefficients in magnetized fluids, specifically building on the relaxation time approximation (RTA) approach of Ashutosh (2021) by using the more rigorous Chapman-Enskog method with the full BUU collision kernel. The paper also follows the formalism of Davesne (1996) for Bose enhancement factors in the collision integral. The results enable more accurate modeling of the quark-gluon plasma and hadronic resonance gas phases produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where transient magnetic fields break the isotropic symmetry in the transverse plane.
