# Trapping Penguins with Entangled B Mesons

> The first direct observation of time-reversal (T) violation in the $B\bar{B}$ system has been reported by the BaBar collaboration, employing the method of Ba$\tilde {\rm n}\!$uls and Bernabéu. Given this, we generalize their analysis of the time-dependent T-violating asymmetry ($A_{T}$) to consider different choices of CP tags for which the dominant amplitudes have the same weak phase. As one application, we find that it is possible to measure departures from the universality of $\sin(2β)$ directly. If $\sin(2β)$ is universal, as in the Standard Model, the method permits the direct determination of penguin effects in these channels. Our method, although no longer a strict test of T, can yield tests of the $\sin(2β)$ universality, or, alternatively, of penguin effects, of much improved precision even with existing data sets.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that by generalizing the BaBar time-reversal (T) asymmetry method to use different CP-tagging final states that share a common dominant weak phase, the resulting asymmetry no longer tests T but instead provides a direct measurement of small deviations from $\sin(2\beta)$ universality or of penguin pollution, with improved statistical precision possible even using existing data sets.

## Method

The authors generalize the Bañuls and Bernabéu formalism for the time-dependent T-violating asymmetry $A_T$ in entangled $B\bar{B}$ pairs from $\Upsilon(4S)$ decay, replacing the standard CP tags $J/\psi K_S$ and $J/\psi K_L$ with generalized CP-odd and CP-even final states (e.g., $\phi K_S$, $\eta K_L$, $\rho^0 K_S$, $\omega K_S$, $\pi^0 K_L$ and their CP conjugates) that share the same dominant weak phase in the Standard Model. They derive expressions for the resulting asymmetries $A_T^{o\pm}$ and $A_T^{e\pm}$ in terms of the parameters $S_{o,e}$ and $C_{o,e}$, showing that the combination $S_e + S_o$ cancels the dominant $\sin(2\beta)$ term and isolates the difference of penguin-induced deviations $\delta S_o - \delta S_e$.

## Key numbers

* BaBar measured T-violating parameters $\Delta S_T^{+} = -1.37 \pm 0.14_{\rm stat} \pm 0.06_{\rm syst}$ and $\Delta S_T^{-} = 1.17 \pm 0.18_{\rm stat} \pm 0.11_{\rm syst}$, with an effective significance of $14\sigma$ for T violation.
* The CP-violation parameter $\sin(2\beta)$ from $B\to c\bar{c} K_S$ and $J/\psi K_L$ decays is $0.677 \pm 0.020$ (Bevan 2014).
* Current experimental results for other $\sin(2\beta)$ modes include $-\eta_{CP}^f S_{\pi^0 K_S} = 0.57 \pm 0.17$ and $-\eta_{CP}^f S_{\omega K_S} = 0.45 \pm 0.24$ (Bevan 2014).
* The CPLEAR experiment reported a T-violation asymmetry $\langle A_T^{exp} \rangle = (6.6 \pm 1.3_{\rm stat} \pm 1.0_{\rm syst}) \times 10^{-3}$, of only $4\sigma$ significance.

## Context

This work extends the method of Bañuls and Bernabéu (1999, 2000) and the BaBar T-violation analysis (Lees 2012) by purposefully breaking the interpretation of $A_T$ as a test of T through the choice of generalized CP tags. It addresses the difficulty of measuring penguin pollution in $\sin(2\beta)$ modes, which has been studied theoretically by Beneke, Boos (2004), Cheng, Buchalla, Li (2006), Dutta (2008), and Frings (2015), and through SU(3)-flavor approaches by Grossman (2003), Jung (2012), and Ligeti (2015). The method enables direct access to differences of penguin effects that have been difficult to quantify (Bevan 2014), and can yield improved precision from existing B-factory data sets, with further applications anticipated at Belle II.
