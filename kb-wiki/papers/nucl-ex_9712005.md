# Excited States in 52Fe and the Origin of the Yrast Trap at I=12+

> Excited states in 52Fe have been determined up to spin 10\hbar in the reaction 28Si + 28Si at 115 MeV by using γ-ray spectroscopy methods at the GASP array. The excitation energy of the yrast 10+ state has been determined to be 7.381 MeV, almost 0.5 MeV above the well known β+-decaying yrast 12+ state, definitely confirming the nature of its isomeric character. The mean lifetimes of the states have been measured by using the Doppler Shift Attenuation method. The experimental data are compared with spherical shell model calculations in the full pf-shell.

## Key claim

The yrast 12+ state in 52Fe is confirmed to be an isomeric yrast trap because it lies at a lower excitation energy than the 10+ state, not because it is simply long-lived; the 10+ state was measured at 7.381 MeV, almost 0.5 MeV above the 12+ state, which decays by beta+ emission rather than gamma decay.

## Method

High-spin states in 52Fe were populated via the 28Si + 28Si fusion-evaporation reaction at 115 MeV using the GASP array of 40 Compton-suppressed HPGe detectors. Gamma-gamma coincidence data were collected, and the level scheme was constructed up to spin 10hbar. Mean lifetimes were measured using the Doppler Shift Attenuation Method (DSAM) with the LINESHAPE code, and experimental results were compared to spherical shell model calculations in the full pf-shell using the KB3 effective interaction.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy of the yrast 10+ state: 7.381 MeV, almost 0.5 MeV above the 12+ state (which lies at 6820 ± 130 keV)
- The 12+ state has a measured half-life of 45.94 ± 1.0 seconds and an upper limit of 0.4% for gamma decay
- Total collected data: 7.8 × 10^8 two-fold and 5.4 × 10^7 three-fold coincidence events
- The 52Fe ground state population was less than 1% of the total gamma yield
- ADO ratio values: ~1.17 for stretched quadrupole transitions and ~0.85 for stretched dipole transitions at 60° in the GASP geometry
- Intrinsic quadrupole moment Q0 ≈ 90 e·fm² and deformation parameter β = 0.23 for the 2+ and 4+ states, derived from shell model calculations

## Context

This work extends previous studies of 52Fe that had only reached low spin states (below 6hbar) using reactions such as (3He,n) by Alford (1975) and (α,2n) by Evers (1977), and it confirms the predicted inversion of the 10+ and 12+ states suggested by Geesaman (1976). The results enable systematic comparison of yrast traps across the f7/2 shell, showing that unlike the band-terminating states in 44Ti and 48Cr studied by Lenzi (1996, 1997), the 12+ state in 52Fe retains quadrupole coherence from p3/2 orbital contributions, explaining its prolate deformation and isomeric character.
