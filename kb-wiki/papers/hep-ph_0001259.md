# $Λ$, $\barΛ$ Polarization and Spin Transfer in Lepton Deep-Inelastic Scattering

> The flavor and helicity distributions of the $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hyperons for both valence and sea quarks are calculated in a perturbative QCD (pQCD) based model. We relate these quark distributions to the fragmentation functions of the $Λ$ and $\barΛ$, and calculate the $z$-dependence of the longitudinal spin transfer to the $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ in lepton deep-inelastic scattering (DIS). It is shown that the spin transfer to the $Λ$ is compatible with the first HERMES results at DESY and further tests are suggested. We also make predictions for the $z$-dependence of the $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ longitudinal polarizations in neutrino (antineutrino) DIS processes. We investigate the sea contribution to the fragmentation functions, and we test a possible scenario where sea quarks in $Λ$ (or sea antiquarks in $\barΛ$) are negatively polarized, whereas sea antiquarks in the $Λ$ (or sea quarks in $\barΛ$) are positively polarized. The asymmetry of the polarized fragmentation functions of sea quarks and antiquarks to $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ provides a way to understand the different behaviour between the $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ spin transfers observed in the recent E665 experiment at FNAL.

# Λ, \(\bar{Λ}\) Polarization and Spin Transfer in Lepton Deep-Inelastic Scattering

## Key claim

The perturbative QCD (pQCD) based model, which assumes helicity retention for valence quarks at large \(x\), can describe the available \(\Lambda\) spin transfer data from HERMES, but the different behavior of \(\Lambda\) and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) spin transfers observed by E665 requires an additional assumption of asymmetric sea quark and antiquark helicity distributions in the \(\Lambda\) (sea quarks negatively polarized, sea antiquarks positively polarized) rather than arising from the valence sector alone.

## Method

The authors calculated the flavor and helicity distributions of valence and sea quarks in the \(\Lambda\) hyperon using a pQCD-based model with counting-rule behavior at large \(x\) and Regge behavior at small \(x\). They related these quark distributions to fragmentation functions via the Gribov-Lipatov reciprocity relation, then computed the \(z\)-dependence of the longitudinal spin transfer to \(\Lambda\) and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) in charged lepton deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) and the longitudinal polarizations in neutrino and antineutrino DIS processes. Two scenarios for sea quark helicities were tested: an asymmetric scenario (scenario I, with \(\Delta q_s = -0.3\) and \(\Delta \bar{q} = +0.2\)) and a symmetric scenario (scenario II, with \(\Delta q_s = \Delta \bar{q} = -0.05\)), using CTEQ5 parton distributions at \(Q^2 = 1.3\ \text{GeV}^2\).

## Key numbers

- Valence strange quark helicity: \(\Delta s = 0.7\); valence up/down quark helicity: \(\Delta u = -0.1\) (slightly different from the Burkardt-Jaffe values \(\Delta s = 0.6\), \(\Delta u = -0.2\)).
- For scenario I (asymmetric sea): \(\Delta q_s = -0.3\), \(\Delta \bar{q} = 0.2\), with momentum fractions \(\langle x_{q_s} \rangle = \langle x_{\bar{q}} \rangle \approx 0.03\); the free parameter \(C_{q_s}\) is constrained to \(1.714 < C_{q_s} < 1.718\) and taken as \(C_{q_s} = 1.715\); \(C_{\bar{q}}\) is constrained to \(0 < C_{\bar{q}} < 0.111\) and taken as \(C_{\bar{q}} = 0.1\).
- For scenario II (symmetric sea): \(\Delta q_s = \Delta \bar{q} = -0.05\) with \(\langle x_{q_s} \rangle = \langle x_{\bar{q}} \rangle \approx 0.03\); the free parameter \(C_q\) is constrained to \(0.422 < C_q < 0.914\) and taken as \(C_q = 0.6\).
- The \(x\) integration range for the lepton DIS predictions is \([0.02, 0.4]\); the averaged Bjorken variable for HERMES data is \(x = 0.1\), and for E665 data is \(x = 0.005\).

## Context

This work extends the pQCD-based model of Brodsky, Burkardt and Schmidt (1995), previously applied to nucleon spin structure and single-spin asymmetries in \(pp\) collisions, to the semi-inclusive production of \(\Lambda\) and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) in DIS. It directly addresses the different \(\Lambda\) and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) spin transfer behaviors reported by the E665 Collaboration at FNAL, which earlier calculations by Kotzinian (1998) (assuming constant or slowly varying \(\Delta D_q^\Lambda(z)/D_q^\Lambda(z)\) ratios) could not reproduce. The predictions for neutrino and antineutrino DIS processes are intended to guide upcoming analyses from the NOMAD experiment, and the proposed sea quark-antiquark asymmetry in fragmentation functions provides a testable mechanism for understanding the proton spin problem via baryon-meson fluctuation models of intrinsic sea pairs as discussed by Brodsky and Ma (1996).
