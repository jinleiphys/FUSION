# Solar Hydrogen Burning and Neutrinos

> We summarize the current status of laboratory measurements of nuclear cross sections of the pp chain and CN cycle. We discuss the connections between such measurements, predictions of solar neutrino fluxes, and the conclusion that solar neutrinos oscillate before reaching earth.

## Key claim

The paper summarizes the current status of laboratory measurements of nuclear cross sections for the pp chain and CN cycle, emphasizing that while solar neutrino oscillations are now established, the reliability of that conclusion depends critically on the quality of our understanding of the nuclear reaction rates governing these hydrogen-burning processes. The authors note that the \(^{3}\text{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^{7}\text{Be}\) reaction rate, due to an inconsistency between direct-capture and residual-activity measurements, has become the most important nuclear uncertainty in the solar neutrino problem.

## Method

The authors review laboratory measurements of nuclear cross sections for each reaction in the proton-proton chain and the CN cycle, describing how S-factors are extracted from data taken at energies above the solar Gamow peak and then extrapolated to astrophysical energies. They discuss the formalism for relating laboratory cross sections to stellar reaction rates, including corrections for electron screening in both terrestrial experiments and the solar plasma, and they highlight specific experimental techniques such as underground measurements (LUNA), direct-capture versus residual-activity methods, and the Trojan Horse Method.

## Key numbers

- \(S_{pp}(0) = (3.92 \pm 0.08) \times 10^{-25}\) MeV·b for the pp reaction, based on effective field theory constrained by tritium beta decay
- \(S(0) = 0.216 \pm 0.006\) eV·b and \(S'(0) = 0.0059 \pm 0.0004\) b for the d(p,\(\gamma\))\(^{3}\)He reaction, measured down to \(E_{cm} = 2.5\) keV by LUNA
- \(S(0) = 5.32 \pm 0.08\) MeV·b and \(S'(0) = -3.7 \pm 0.6\) b for the \(^{3}\text{He}(^{3}\text{He},2p)^{4}\text{He}\) reaction, directly measured at the solar Gamow peak (\(E_0 \pm \Delta/2 = 21 \pm 5\) keV) with a cross section of 20 fb at 16 keV
- \(S_{34}(0) = 0.53 \pm 0.05\) keV·b for the \(^{3}\text{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^{7}\text{Be}\) reaction, from a weighted mean of direct-capture and residual-activity data sets
- The pep reaction rate in the solar interior is \(R_{pep} \sim 2.4 \times 10^{-3} R_{pp}\)
- The \(^{3}\text{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^{7}\text{Be}\) reaction has a cross section of \(\sim 3 \times 10^{-5}\) pb at the Gamow peak (\(E_0 \sim 23\) keV)
- The \(^{7}\text{Be}(p,\gamma)^{8}\text{B}\) reaction has a cross section of \(\sim 1.5 \times 10^{-3}\) pb at \(E_0 \sim 18.4\) keV
- The \(^{14}\text{N}(p,\gamma)^{15}\text{O}\) reaction has a cross section of \(\sim 2.2 \times 10^{-7}\) pb at \(E_0 \sim 27.2\) keV

## Context

This review extends the work of the 1997 INT working group (summarized in Ref. 29) and earlier compilations by Fowler et al. (Ref. 13) and Angulo et al. (Ref. 14), providing an updated status of laboratory measurements for solar hydrogen-burning reactions. The paper connects these nuclear data to standard solar model predictions of neutrino fluxes, which were critical to the discovery of neutrino oscillations by the Homestake, SNO, and Super-Kamiokande experiments. The unresolved discrepancy between direct-capture and residual-activity measurements of the \(^{3}\text{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^{7}\text{Be}\) reaction motivates ongoing experiments using new direct-capture, activity, and Coulomb-dissociation approaches, and resolving this uncertainty would significantly improve the precision of solar neutrino flux predictions.
