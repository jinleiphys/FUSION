# Hagedorn States and Thermalization in Heavy Ion Collisions

> In recent years, Hagedorn states have been used to explain the equilibrium and transport properties of a hadron gas close to the QCD critical temperature. These massive resonances are shown to lower $η/s$ to near the AdS/CFT limit close to the phase transition. A comparison of the Hagedorn model to recent lattice results is made and it is found that the hadrons can reach chemical equilibrium almost immediately, well before the chemical freeze-out temperatures found in thermal fits for a hadron gas without Hagedorn states.

## Key claim

Hagedorn states (massive resonances following an exponential mass spectrum) can drive rapid chemical equilibration of hadrons in the hadron gas phase after a heavy-ion collision, making the final particle yields nearly independent of the initial conditions at the phase transition and consistent with experimental data, provided the Hagedorn temperature is set equal to the QCD critical temperature.

## Method

The authors model the hadron gas using a discretized Hagedorn spectrum with mass bins of 100 MeV, fitting the Hagedorn state parameters (degeneracy constant A, maximum mass M, and bag constant B) to lattice QCD results for the trace anomaly and energy density from two different collaborations (RBC-Bielefeld with T_c=196 MeV and BMW with T_c=176 MeV). They then solve coupled master equations for pions, Hagedorn states, and X\bar{X} pairs (proton-antiproton, kaon-antikaon, Lambda-antilambda, Omega-antigomega) under a Bjorken expansion with radial flow, using decay widths and branching ratios derived from a microcanonical model and fits to known resonances up to 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- For T_H=196 MeV (RBC-Bielefeld lattice): A=0.5 GeV^{3/2}, M=12 GeV, B=(340 MeV)^4
- For T_H=176 MeV (BMW lattice): A=0.1 GeV^{3/2}, M=12 GeV, B=(300 MeV)^4
- Hagedorn state decay width fit: Gamma_i [GeV] = 0.15 m_i [GeV] - 0.03, ranging from 250 MeV to 1800 MeV
- Average pion number from Hagedorn decay: <n_i> = 0.9 + 1.2 (m_i / m_p), ranging from approximately 3 to 34
- For Omega production at RHIC (T=170 MeV) without Hagedorn states: equilibration time tau_Omega approximately 10 fm/c, longer than the fireball lifetime of tau < 4 fm/c in the hadronic stage
- With Hagedorn states, the p/pi ratio at T_H=196 MeV matches experimental data (STAR p/pi^-=0.11, PHENIX p/pi^-=0.10) already at T approximately 190 MeV
- The Omega/pi ratio from the model reaches STAR values (Omega/pi^-=9.5 x 10^{-4}) only when using the full decay width; reducing the width to one-half or one-fourth gives insufficient production (25% or 50% of experimental yield, respectively)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Greiner and Leupold (2004) on multi-mesonic reactions for anti-baryon production, and by Rapp and Shuryak (2000) on multi-pion interactions forming anti-baryons at SPS energies. It addresses the problem identified by Koch (1986) that binary strangeness production reactions in a hadron gas require equilibration times of approximately 1000 fm/c, far longer than the fireball lifetime. The Hagedorn state mechanism provides an alternative to the suggestion by Stock (1999) and Heinz (2006) that hadrons are "born into equilibrium" at the phase transition. The results enable future work on strange and baryonic Hagedorn states, and on decay channels involving exotic states, using microscopic quark models to determine the full Hagedorn spectrum.
