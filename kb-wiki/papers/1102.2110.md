# Restoration of a Lost Symmetry -- Mass Reduction of the η' Mesons in 10^-22 Sec

> A particle called η' meson is found to reduce its mass in less than 10^-22 second in high energy heavy ion collisions at RHIC. Apparently this is the fastest mass reduction ever observed. This indicates the restoration of a lost symmetry of strong interactions in a hot and dense, hadronic matter.

# Restoration of a Lost Symmetry -- Mass Reduction of the η' Mesons in 10^-22 Sec

## Key claim

The η' meson exhibits a significant mass reduction of at least 200 MeV in the hot, dense hadronic medium created in high-energy heavy ion collisions at RHIC, with the mass reduction occurring in less than 10^-22 seconds. This observation indicates the restoration of the UA(1) symmetry of strong interactions at temperatures below the range of sQGP formation, in agreement with theoretical predictions based on quark model calculations and lattice QCD.

## Method

The analysis combined datasets from the STAR and PHENIX Collaborations at RHIC for √sNN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions. The η' meson mass reduction was inferred indirectly from the behavior of the pseudoscalar meson spectrum in the hot and dense hadronic medium formed after the quark-gluon plasma rehadronizes. The mass reduction was evaluated by comparing the in-medium η' mass to its vacuum value of 958 MeV, with the η(548) meson serving as the reference partner.

## Key numbers

- The η'(958) meson in vacuum has a mass of 958 MeV, while its partner η(548) has a mass of 548 MeV, giving a mass difference of 410 MeV between the two mesons.
- The observed mass reduction of the η' meson in the hot and dense hadronic medium is at least 200 MeV.
- The mass reduction occurs within a timescale of less than 10^-22 seconds.
- The collision energy was √sNN = 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions at RHIC.
- The rehadronization temperature is approximately 2 Terakelvin (2 × 10^12 K).

## Context

This work extends the search for UA(1) symmetry restoration in hot and dense hadronic matter, following theoretical predictions from quark model calculations and lattice quantum chromodynamics. The results are consistent with the expectation that the in-medium modified η' behaves as a "prodigal" Goldstone boson whose mass returns toward the η mass when the symmetry is restored. The observation enables further experimental studies of chiral and axial symmetry restoration in heavy ion collisions, and provides a benchmark for theoretical models of the QCD phase diagram at temperatures below the deconfinement transition.
