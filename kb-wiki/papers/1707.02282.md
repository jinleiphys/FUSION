# New theories of relativistic hydrodynamics in the LHC era

> The success of relativistic hydrodynamics as an essential part of the phenomenological description of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC has motivated a significant body of theoretical work concerning its fundamental aspects. Our review presents these developments from the perspective of the underlying microscopic physics, using the language of quantum field theory, relativistic kinetic theory, and holography. We discuss the gradient expansion, the phenomenon of hydrodynamization, as well as several models of hydrodynamic evolution equations, highlighting the interplay between collective long-lived and transient modes in relativistic matter. Our aim to provide a unified presentation of this vast subject -- which is naturally expressed in diverse mathematical languages -- has also led us to include several new results on the large-order behaviour of the hydrodynamic gradient~expansion.

## Key claim

This review argues that relativistic hydrodynamics, while phenomenologically successful at RHIC and the LHC, can be understood from underlying microscopic physics through quantum field theory, relativistic kinetic theory, and holography; the authors include new results on the large-order behaviour of the hydrodynamic gradient expansion, but these are presented within a unified review framework rather than as a standalone experimental discovery.

## Method

The authors survey theoretical developments in relativistic hydrodynamics by examining the gradient expansion, the phenomenon of hydrodynamization, and several models of hydrodynamic evolution equations, using the languages of quantum field theory, relativistic kinetic theory, and holography. They analyze the interplay between collective long-lived and transient modes in relativistic matter, and present new results on the large-order behaviour of the hydrodynamic gradient expansion. The review covers linear response theory, quasinormal modes of black branes in holography, and comparisons between different hydrodynamic frameworks such as MIS (Muller-Israel-Stewart), BRSSS (Baier-Romatschke-Son-Starinets-Stephanov), DNMR (Denicol-Molnar-Niemi-Rischke), and HJSW (Heller-Janik-Spalinski-Witaszczyk).

## Key numbers

* For N=4 SYM in the holographic regime, the central charge is c = N_c^2/4, and the entropy density is s = (pi^2/2) N_c^2 T^3, which differs from the ideal gas result by only 25% as noted by Gubser (1998).
* The Hawking temperature of the AdS-Schwarzschild black brane is T = sqrt(2)/(pi u_0), where u_0 is the horizon location.
* Quasinormal mode frequencies in strongly-coupled holographic CFTs are necessarily linear in T, with all but one mode decaying over a time scale of order 1/T; the remaining long-lived excitation at low momentum is the hydrodynamic sound wave.
* For Gauss-Bonnet gravity, the coupling parameter lambda_GB must satisfy lambda_GB in (-infinity, 1/4] for consistency, but the theory is fully trustworthy only for |lambda_GB| << 1.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical understanding of relativistic hydrodynamics beyond the Navier-Stokes equations, building on earlier work by Kovtun (2005) on quasinormal modes in holography and by Grozdanov (2016) on Gauss-Bonnet gravity. It synthesizes results from multiple approaches including the fluid-gravity duality and kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation, and enables future work on connecting hydrodynamic descriptions to microscopic dynamics in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC.
