# Evidence for a scalar kappa(900) resonance in pi-K scattering

> Motivated by the $1/N_c$ expansion, we study a simple model in which the pi-K scattering amplitude is the sum of a current-algebra contact term and resonance pole exchanges. This phenomenological model is crossing symmetric and, when a putative light strange scalar meson, kappa, is included, satisfies the unitarity bounds to well above 1 GeV. The model also features chiral dynamics, vector meson dominance and appropriate interference between the established scalar K*(1430) resonance and its predicted background. We briefly discuss the physical significance of the results and directions for further work.

## Key claim

The paper presents evidence for a light strange scalar resonance, the kappa(900), in pi-K scattering by showing that its inclusion in a crossing-symmetric model is necessary to satisfy unitarity bounds up to well above 1 GeV, but the authors note that the existence of the kappa is demonstrated through a model-dependent analysis of the background rather than a direct experimental observation.

## Method

The authors construct a phenomenological model for pi-K scattering where the amplitude is the sum of a current-algebra contact term and tree-level resonance pole exchanges, motivated by the 1/N_c expansion of QCD. They compute the real part of the J=0, I=1/2 partial wave amplitude and compare it to experimental data from Aston et al. (1988), fitting the kappa and K*(1430) parameters simultaneously using the MINUIT package while keeping the sigma and f0(980) couplings fixed from prior pi-pi scattering analyses.

## Key numbers

- Kappa meson mass m_kappa = 897 +/- 2.1 MeV (for the case gamma_sigma K anti-K = gamma_sigma pi pi)
- Kappa width parameter G'_kappa = 322 +/- 6.0 MeV (for the same case)
- Kappa coupling gamma_kappa K pi = 5.0 +/- 0.07 GeV^{-1} (for the same case)
- K*(1430) mass m_* = 1385 +/- 3.3 MeV (for the same case)
- K*(1430) width parameter G'_* = 266 +/- 9.5 MeV (for the same case)
- Ratio G_kappa / G'_kappa = 0.13, indicating deviation from pure Breit-Wigner shape (similar to the sigma meson ratio of 0.29)
- Background phase sin(2*delta) = 0.937 at the K*(1430) mass, corresponding to delta approximately pi/4
- Chi-squared value of 4.0 for the fit with gamma_sigma K anti-K = gamma_sigma pi pi

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to pi-pi scattering by Sannino and Schechter (1995) and Harada, Sannino, and Schechter (1996), where a light sigma(560) meson was found necessary to restore unitarity. The authors note that evidence for a kappa resonance has been found by van Beveren (1986) using a unitarized non-relativistic meson model and by Ishida (1997) using interfering Breit-Wigner amplitudes, but disputed by Tornqvist (1995) using a unitarized quark model. The existence of a light kappa would strengthen the case for a non-conventional scalar nonet lying below 1 GeV, as proposed by Jaffe (1977), and the authors reserve speculation on quark substructure for future work.
