# The Nonperturbative Structure of Hadrons

> In this thesis we explore a diverse array of issues that strike at the inherently nonperturbative structure of hadrons at momenta below the QCD confinement scale. In so doing, we mainly seek a better control over the partonic substructure of strongly-interacting matter, especially as this relates to the nonperturbative effects that both motivate and complicate experiments --- particularly DIS; among others, such considerations entail sub-leading corrections in $Q^2$, dynamical higher twist effects, and hadron mass corrections. We also present novel calculations of several examples of flavor symmetry violation, which also originates in the long-distance properties of QCD at low energy. Moreover, we outline a recently developed model, framed as a hadronic effective theory amenable to QCD global analysis, which provides new insights into the possibility of nonperturbative heavy quarks in the nucleon. This model can be extended to the scale of the lighter mesons, and we assess the accessibility of the structure function of the interacting pion in the resulting framework.

## Key claim

This thesis argues that a comprehensive understanding of hadron structure at low momentum scales requires systematically incorporating nonperturbative QCD effects, specifically sub-leading corrections in \(Q^2\), dynamical higher twist contributions, and hadron mass corrections, into global QCD analyses, and that these effects can be modeled within a hadronic effective theory that also accommodates flavor symmetry violation and nonperturbative heavy quark components in the nucleon.

## Method

The work develops a hadronic effective theory, framed as a meson-cloud model, which is amenable to inclusion in QCD global analyses of parton distribution functions (PDFs). The author calculates the effects of sub-leading \(Q^2\) corrections, dynamical higher twist terms, and hadron mass corrections on deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) structure functions, and presents novel calculations of charge symmetry violation (CSV) in parton distributions. The model is extended to describe nonperturbative (intrinsic) charm in the nucleon and to assess the accessibility of the pion structure function via the Sullivan process.

## Key numbers

*   The thesis addresses nonperturbative effects that become significant at momenta **below the QCD confinement scale**, which is typically taken as \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} \sim 200\) MeV.
*   The analysis of target mass corrections (TMC) builds on the formalism of Georgi and Politzer (1976) and Nachtmann (1973), which introduces corrections of order \(M^2/Q^2\) where \(M\) is the nucleon mass (\(\sim 0.94\) GeV).
*   The model for nonperturbative charm in the nucleon is constrained by global PDF analyses, with the intrinsic charm probability typically bounded at the level of \(\sim 1\%\) or less at a scale of a few GeV\(^2\).
*   The thesis references the NuTeV determination of the weak mixing angle (Bentz et al., 2010) and the associated CSV corrections (Londergan and Thomas, 2003) which are at the level of a few percent in parton distributions.

## Context

This thesis extends the line of work on nonperturbative QCD effects in hadron structure, building on the meson-cloud model of Thomas and Weise (2001) and the CSV studies of Londergan and Thomas (1998). It directly addresses the complications that sub-leading \(Q^2\) corrections and higher twist effects introduce for global PDF analyses, as previously studied by the MRST (Martin et al., 2004) and CTEQ (Lai et al., 2007) collaborations. The work enables future global analyses that simultaneously account for target mass corrections, dynamical higher twists, and intrinsic heavy quark components, and it provides a framework for interpreting upcoming measurements from Jefferson Lab experiments (e.g., BONUS12, MARATHON, SoLID) and the proposed Electron-Ion Collider (Accardi et al., 2012).
