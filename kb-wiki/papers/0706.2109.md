# Bremsstrahlung in alpha-Decay Reexamined

> A high-statistics measurement of bremsstrahlung emitted in the alpha decay of 210Po has been performed, which allows to follow the photon spectra up to energies of ~ 500 keV. The measured differential emission probability is in good agreement with our theoretical results obtained within the quasi classical approximation as well as with the exact quantum mechanical calculation. It is shown that due to the small effective electric dipole charge of the radiating system a significant interference between the electric dipole and quadrupole contributions occurs, which is altering substantially the angular correlation between the alpha particle and the emitted photon.

## Key claim

The measured differential emission probability for bremsstrahlung in alpha decay of 210Po agrees well with both the quasi-classical approximation and the exact quantum mechanical calculation, but only after accounting for a significant interference between electric dipole and quadrupole contributions that substantially alters the angular correlation between the alpha particle and the emitted photon.

## Method

A high-statistics coincidence measurement was performed using two 210Po alpha sources (100 kBq each) viewed by segmented silicon detectors for alpha particles and a high-purity germanium triple cluster detector (MINIBALL design) for bremsstrahlung photons. The experiment ran for 270 days, recording 4.3 × 10^11 alpha particles, and the differential emission probability was extracted from alpha-gamma coincidence events by projecting along the diagonal line defined by energy and momentum conservation, with careful correction for the E2/E1 interference effect on detection efficiency.

## Key numbers

- Total recorded alpha particles: 4.311(2) × 10^11 over 270 days of data taking
- Differential emission probability measured up to photon energies of ~500 keV
- Statistical uncertainty σ_sta = 2% and systematic uncertainty σ_sys = 8% at <E_γ> = 139 keV
- Statistical uncertainty σ_sta = 19% and systematic uncertainty σ_sys = 5% at <E_γ> = 373 keV
- Effective dipole charge Z_E1^eff = 0.40 and effective quadrupole charge Z_E2^eff = 1.95 for the 210Po system
- Interference parameter χ(E_γ) takes values of +0.09 at 100 keV and +0.22 at 500 keV
- E2 interference reduces detection efficiency by about 8% at 100 keV and 24% at 500 keV compared to pure dipole emission
- Absolute gamma full-energy peak efficiency at 803 keV: 8.09(26)% for isotropic emission
- Sommerfeld parameter η = 22 for 210Po alpha decay

## Context

This work extends earlier experimental attempts by Kasagi et al. (1997) and others that produced conflicting results and lacked sensitivity above ~200 keV. The measurement provides the first high-statistics test of the fully quantum mechanical calculation by Papenbrock and Bertsch (1998) and the quasi-classical approach by DyGo1996 and Dy1999, showing that both theories agree with each other to better than 2% and with the data overall. The results rule out classical Coulomb acceleration calculations and theoretical suggestions by Kasagi et al. (1997), MaOl2003, and BPZ1999, while small deviations of up to 20% below 200 keV suggest the need to refine the potential model used to describe the alpha-nucleus interaction at nuclear radii.
