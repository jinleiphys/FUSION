# Off-shell effects in dilepton production from hot interacting mesons

> The production of dielectrons in reactions involving a_1 mesons and pions is studied. We compare results obtained with different phenomenological Lagrangians that have been used in connection with hadronic matter and finite nuclei. We insist on the necessity for those interactions to satisfy known empirical properties of the strong interaction. Large off-shell effects in dielectron production are found and some consequences for the interpretation of heavy ion data are outlined. We also compare with results obtained using experimentally-extracted spectral functions.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that different phenomenological Lagrangians for the $\pi \rho a_1$ vertex, all fitted to the on-shell $a_1 \to \rho \pi$ decay width, produce dielectron production rates from $\pi + a_1 \to e^+ e^-$ that span two-and-a-half orders of magnitude at T = 150 MeV, revealing that off-shell effects dominate the uncertainty and that on-shell fits alone are insufficient to determine the correct interaction.

## Method
The authors calculated the dilepton production rate from the reaction $\pi + a_1 \to e^+ e^-$ in a hot meson gas at T = 150 MeV using relativistic kinetic theory with Boltzmann distributions. They compared results from five different effective Lagrangians for the $\pi \rho a_1$ vertex (Ko and Rudaz, Li, Gomm et al., Xiong et al., and Janssen et al.), each fitted to the experimental $a_1 \to \rho \pi$ decay width. They also computed rates using an experimentally-constrained spectral function extracted from $e^+ e^- \to 4\pi^\pm$ data via the method of Penshaw (1980) and compared all results to discriminate between models.

## Key numbers
- Dielectron production rates from $\pi + a_1 \to e^+ e^-$ at T = 150 MeV span two-and-a-half orders of magnitude across the different Lagrangians (Fig. 3, no explicit numerical rate values given in text).
- The $D/S$ ratio for $a_1 \to \rho \pi$ decay is measured experimentally as $-0.09 \pm 0.03$ (Particle Data Group, 1996).
- The model of Gomm et al. (parameter set II: $g=6.4483$, $\sigma=-0.2913$, $\xi=0.0585$) yields $D/S = -0.099$ and $\chi^2 = 1.8$ for on-shell hadronic properties.
- The model of Li yields $\Gamma(a_1 \to \rho \pi) = 331.7$ MeV and $D/S = -0.161$ with $\chi^2 = 3.1$.
- The model of Xiong et al. yields $\Gamma(a_1 \to \pi \gamma) = 1.940$ MeV (experimental value: $0.640 \pm 0.246$ MeV) and $D/S = 0.185$ with $\chi^2 = 37.3$.
- The model of Janssen et al. yields $D/S = 0.045$ with $\chi^2 = 7.3$.
- The Ko and Rudaz model parameter set I gives $\Gamma(a_1 \to \rho \pi) = 313.4$ MeV, $D/S = 0.078$, $\chi^2 = 11.8$; parameter set II gives $\Gamma(a_1 \to \rho \pi) = 579.1$ MeV, $D/S = -0.168$, $\chi^2 = 9.6$.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of dilepton production from $\pi + a_1$ reactions by Song, Ko, and Gale (1994) and by Kim, Ko, and Lee (1995), and addresses the need for reliable theoretical modeling of the low-mass dilepton excess observed by the CERES/NA45 collaboration (1996). The authors show that the model of Gomm et al. (1994), which also yields an excellent $\chi^2$ for on-shell hadronic properties, agrees best with the experimentally-constrained spectral function approach of Huang (1995) and Penshaw (1980). This enables future calculations of dilepton and photon emission from hot hadronic matter to be performed with a more reliable interaction.
