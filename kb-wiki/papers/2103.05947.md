# Electron exchange and polarization effects on electron captures and neutron emissions by nuclei in white dwarfs and neutron stars

> In dense stellar environments, nuclei may become unstable against electron captures and/or neutron emissions. These processes are of particular importance for determining the internal constitution of white-dwarf cores and neutron-star crusts. In this paper, the role of electron exchange and polarization effects is studied. In particular, the instability condition for the onset of electron captures and neutron emissions is extended so as to account for electron exchange and polarization. Moreover, general analytical expressions for the corresponding density and pressure are derived. The corrections to the electron-capture threshold in white-dwarf cores are found to be very small. Likewise, the neutron-drip density and pressure in the crusts of accreting and nonaccreting neutron stars are only slightly shifted. Depending on the nuclear mass model employed, electron polarization may change the composition of the crust of nonaccreting neutron stars. On the other hand, the current uncertainties in the masses of neutron-rich Kr and Sr isotopes are found to be more important than electron exchange and polarization effects.

## Key claim

Electron exchange and polarization corrections to the onset of electron captures in white-dwarf cores and neutron emissions in neutron-star crusts are very small (typically below 0.3%), but for one particular nuclear mass model (HFB-22) the inclusion of these effects changes the equilibrium nucleus at the neutron-drip transition from ¹²²Kr to ¹²⁸Sr, because the threshold electron Fermi energies for these two nuclei differ by only 0.037 MeV.

## Method

The authors extended the instability condition for electron captures and neutron emissions by nuclei in dense plasmas to include electron exchange (using the Salpeter (1961) ultrarelativistic correction) and electron polarization (using a rescaled Thomas-Fermi approximation based on Potekhin and Chabrier (2000)). They derived analytical expressions for the threshold density and pressure at the onset of electron captures in white-dwarf cores and neutron drip in both accreting and nonaccreting neutron-star crusts, and numerically minimized the Gibbs free energy per nucleon to determine the equilibrium crust composition for five different nuclear mass models (HFB-22, HFB-24, HFB-27, D1M, and DZ).

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶O in white-dwarf cores: electron-capture threshold density n_β = 1.240 × 10⁻⁵ fm⁻³ (with corrections) versus 1.244 × 10⁻⁵ fm⁻³ (without); threshold pressure P_β = 1.709 × 10⁻⁵ MeV fm⁻³ (with) versus 1.714 × 10⁻⁵ MeV fm⁻³ (without); corrections are about 0.3%.
- For nonaccreting neutron stars using HFB-22: neutron-drip density n_drip = 2.701 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³ (with corrections) versus 2.707 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³ (without); pressure P_drip = 5.004 × 10⁻⁴ MeV fm⁻³ (with) versus 4.989 × 10⁻⁴ MeV fm⁻³ (without); equilibrium nucleus changes from ¹²²Kr (Z=36) to ¹²⁸Sr (Z=38).
- For nonaccreting neutron stars using HFB-24: n_drip = 2.565 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³ (with) versus 2.564 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³ (without); P_drip = 4.871 × 10⁻⁴ MeV fm⁻³ (both); equilibrium nucleus remains ¹²⁴Sr.
- Threshold electron Fermi energies for HFB-22: μ_e^drip = 24.967 MeV for ¹²⁸Sr and 25.004 MeV for ¹²²Kr.
- For accreting neutron stars: deviations in neutron-drip density and pressure due to electron exchange and polarization are about 0.1% for all models.
- The rescaled Thomas-Fermi approximation for electron polarization has errors of about 0.2% at most for neutron-star crust conditions; the scaling factor b₁(Z) is about 0.5-0.6 for Z ~ 30-50 and about 0.3-0.4 for carbon and oxygen.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of electron-ion interactions on nuclear stability in white dwarfs (Chamel and Fantina 2015) and neutron stars (Chamel, Fantina, and Zdunik 2015; Chamel 2015) by adding electron exchange and polarization corrections that were previously neglected. The Thomas-Fermi approximation for electron polarization (Salpeter 1961) is shown to overestimate the correction by a factor of 2-3 for the elements in white-dwarf cores and neutron-star crusts. The results enable more precise modeling of the internal constitution of white-dwarf cores and neutron-star crusts, but also show that the current uncertainties in nuclear masses of neutron-rich Kr and Sr isotopes are more important than these electron corrections, pointing to the need for improved mass measurements.
