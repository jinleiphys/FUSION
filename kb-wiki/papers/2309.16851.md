# Extraction of the neutron F2 structure function from inclusive proton and deuteron deep-inelastic scattering data

> The available world deep-inelastic scattering data on proton and deuteron structure functions F2p, F2d, and their ratios, are leveraged to extract the free neutron F2n structure function, the F2n/F2p ratio, and associated uncertainties using the latest nuclear effect calculations in the deuteron. Special attention is devoted to the normalization of the proton and deuteron experimental datasets and to the treatment of correlated systematic errors, as well as the quantification of procedural and theoretical uncertainties. The extracted F2n dataset is utilized to evaluate the Q2 dependence of the Gottfried sum rule and the nonsinglet F2p - F2n moments. To facilitate replication of our study, as well as for general applications, a comprehensive DIS database including all recent JLab 6 GeV measurements, the extracted F2n, a modified CTEQ-JLab global PDF fit named CJ15nlo_mod, and grids with calculated proton, neutron and deuteron DIS structure functions at next-to-leading order, are discussed and made publicly available.

## Key claim

The paper extracts the free neutron F2 structure function from global proton and deuteron deep-inelastic scattering data using the CJ15 nuclear correction model, with the caveat that the extraction depends on the specific choice of deuteron correction model and that adopting alternative models (such as AKP17/21) would produce systematic shifts in the extracted neutron data.

## Method

The authors apply the theoretically calculated deuteron-to-nucleon structure function ratio R_d/N from the CJ15 global QCD analysis to convert measured deuteron F2^d data into a superposition of free proton and free neutron structure functions, then subtract the measured proton contribution to obtain F2^n. They cross-normalize experimental datasets from different experiments and targets to control systematic shifts, and propagate statistical, systematic, procedural, and theoretical uncertainties throughout the extraction.

## Key numbers

- The CJ15 fit without nuclear corrections is strongly disfavored, with a chi-squared increase of 262 units compared to the nuclear-corrected fit, driven by tensions between deuteron DIS and D0 W asymmetry data
- The extracted F2^n dataset covers the kinematic range of the world DIS data on protons and deuterons, including all recent JLab 6 GeV measurements
- The deuteron-to-nucleon ratio R_d/N is evaluated at x values where data have average invariant mass squared 2 < ⟨W^2⟩ < 3 GeV^2 for x ≳ 0.5
- The AKP17 68% CL uncertainty band was rescaled by a factor 1.646 to compare with the CJ15 90% CL band in the R_d/N comparison

## Context

This work extends the CJ15 global QCD analysis (Accardi, 2016) by providing a data-level extraction of the neutron structure function rather than PDF-level results, and contrasts with the AKP17/21 analyses (Alekhin, 2017; Alekhin, 2022) which yield a softer d/u ratio and different R_d/N. The extracted F2^n dataset enables new evaluations of the Gottfried sum rule with Q^2 dependence for the first time, and nonsinglet F2^p - F2^n moments for comparison with lattice QCD calculations from groups such as Lin (2017) and Constantinou (2020).
