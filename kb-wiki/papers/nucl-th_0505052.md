# Baryon-strangeness correlations: a diagnostic of strongly interacting matter

> The correlation between baryon number and strangeness elucidates the nature of strongly interacting matter, such as that formed transiently in high-energy nuclear collisions. This diagnostic can be extracted theoretically from lattice QCD calculations and experimentally from event-by-event fluctuations. The analysis of present lattice results above the critical temperature severely limits the presence of q-qbar bound states, thus supporting a picture of independent (quasi)quarks.

## Key claim

The correlation coefficient \(C_{BS} = -3\sigma_{BS}/\sigma_S^2\) between baryon number and strangeness can distinguish between a quark-gluon plasma of independent (quasi)quarks (where \(C_{BS}=1\)) and a hadron gas (where \(C_{BS}\approx 0.66\) at RHIC conditions), but the analysis of present lattice QCD results above the critical temperature only supports the independent-quark picture if the off-diagonal flavor susceptibilities remain small, which the authors note has not yet been fully verified for strange quarks at \(T_c\).

## Method

The authors define a correlation coefficient \(C_{BS} = -3\sigma_{BS}/\sigma_S^2\) using event-by-event fluctuations of baryon number \(B\) and strangeness \(S\). They compute this coefficient analytically for an ideal quark-gluon plasma (where it equals unity) and for a hadron gas at freeze-out using Poisson statistics and known hadron abundances. They extract the coefficient from quenched lattice QCD susceptibility data at \(T=1.5\,T_c\) from Gavai et al. (2002), and also estimate it for a bound-state model of the quark-gluon plasma using the 749 bound states parametrized by Shuryak et al. (2004). Finally, they calculate \(C_{BS}\) from HIJING and RQMD event generators for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\,A\,{\rm GeV}\).

## Key numbers

- For a hadron gas at freeze-out with \(T=170\,{\rm MeV}\) and zero chemical potential \(\mu_B=0\), \(C_{BS}=0.66\) (including hadrons up to \(\Omega^-\)).
- From quenched lattice QCD at \(T=1.5\,T_c\), the ratio \((\chi_{us}+\chi_{ds})/\chi_{ss} = 0.00(3)/0.53(1) \ll 1\), yielding \(C_{BS}\approx 1\).
- For the bound-state model at \(T=1.5\,T_c\), the estimated value is \(C_{BS}=0.62\).
- HIJING simulations for Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s}=200\,A\,{\rm GeV}\) give \(C_{BS}\approx 0.50\) at small rapidity acceptance.
- RQMD simulations for RHIC events yield \(C_{BS}\approx 0.55\).

## Context

This work extends the use of susceptibilities and fluctuations from lattice QCD (Gavai et al., 2002; Allton et al., 2005) and event-by-event fluctuation analyses (Jeon et al., 2000; Asakawa et al., 2000) to a new diagnostic for the nature of strongly interacting matter. The proposed correlation coefficient can test the bound-state quark-gluon plasma model of Shuryak et al. (2003, 2004) against lattice QCD results, and can be applied experimentally at RHIC to distinguish between a deconfined phase of independent quasiparticles and a hadron gas. The authors suggest that reconciling the lattice equation of state (which falls below an ideal gas) with the observed collective flow may require a quasiparticle description with effective masses, analogous to the independent-particle model of atomic nuclei.
