# Electromagnetic Structure and Reactions of Few-Nucleon Systems in $χ$EFT

> We summarize our recent work dealing with the construction of the nucleon-nucleon potential and associated electromagnetic currents up to one loop in chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT). The magnetic dipole operators derived from these currents are then used in hybrid calculations of static properties and low-energy radiative capture processes in few-body nuclei. A preliminary set of results are presented for the magnetic moments of the deuteron and trinucleons and thermal neutron captures on $p$, $d$, and $^3$He.

## Key claim

The authors present a chiral effective field theory (χEFT) framework for nucleon-nucleon potentials and electromagnetic currents up to one loop (N³LO), but the results for magnetic dipole observables in A=2–4 nuclei are obtained in a "hybrid" approach, using wave functions from realistic potentials (AV18/UIX and N3LO/N2LO) rather than fully consistent χEFT wave functions, and are described as "preliminary."

## Method

The authors constructed the NN potential up to N²LO (one- and two-pion exchange plus contact terms) and associated electromagnetic currents up to N³LO using time-ordered perturbation theory and non-relativistic Hamiltonians from chiral Lagrangians. They then evaluated matrix elements of the χEFT magnetic dipole (M1) operators between wave functions obtained from the Argonne v₁₈ (AV18) potential with Urbana-IX three-nucleon forces and from a chiral N³LO two-nucleon potential with a chiral N²LO three-nucleon potential. The low-energy constants in the M1 operators were determined by fitting to the experimental np capture cross section and the magnetic moments of the deuteron and trinucleons.

- Deuteron binding energy: 2.2244 to 2.2246 MeV (Λ=500–700 MeV) vs. experimental 2.224575(9) MeV
- Deuteron magnetic moment: 0.860 to 0.853 μ_N (Λ=500–700 MeV) vs. experimental 0.8574382329(92) μ_N
- Deuteron quadrupole moment: 0.275 to 0.279 fm² (Λ=500–700 MeV) vs. experimental 0.2859(3) fm²
- Thermal neutron capture cross section on ¹H: 332.6±0.7 mb (experimental reference)
- Thermal neutron capture cross section on ²H: 0.508±0.015 mb (experimental reference)
- Thermal neutron capture cross section on ³He: 0.055±0.003 mb (experimental reference)

## Context

This work extends the χEFT program for nuclear forces and currents to one-loop order, building on earlier derivations by Park et al. (1996) and Kolling et al. (2009), but using time-ordered perturbation theory with both irreducible and recoil-corrected reducible diagrams. The hybrid calculations enable comparison with experimental data for magnetic moments and radiative captures in few-nucleon systems, and the fully constrained N³LO M1 operator provides predictions for nd and n-³He capture cross sections that agree well with experiment, unlike earlier calculations by Song et al. (2009) and Lazauskas et al. which showed larger cutoff sensitivity and underpredicted cross sections.
