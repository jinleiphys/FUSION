# Kaonic hydrogen atom and kaon-proton scattering length

> Kaonic hydrogen is studied with various realistic potentials in an accurate numerical approach based on Sturmian functions. The kaon-proton scattering length extracted from the 1s energy shift of the kaonic hydrogen by applying the Deser-Trueman formula is severely inconsistent with the one derived by directly solving the scattering Schoedinger equation. We pay special attention to the recent measurement of the energy shift and decay width of the 1s kaonic hydrogen state by the DEAR Collaboration. After taking into account the large discrepancy between the extracted and directly-evaluated scattering lengths, we found theoretical predictions of most chiral SU(3) based models for the kaonic hydrogen decay width are consistent with the DEAR data. We warn the SIDDHARTA collaboration that it may not be reasonable to extract kaon-nucleon scattering lengths, by using the Coulomb-interaction corrected Deser-Truemab formula, from the planned measurement of kaonic hydrogen.

# Kaonic hydrogen atom and kaon-proton scattering length

## Key claim
The Deser-Trueman formula (both the lowest-order and Coulomb-corrected versions) is unreliable for extracting the kaon-proton scattering length from the 1s energy shift and decay width of kaonic hydrogen; after accounting for the large discrepancy between extracted and directly-evaluated scattering lengths, most chiral SU(3) based model predictions for the kaonic hydrogen decay width are actually consistent with the DEAR Collaboration data.

## Method
The author solves a coupled-channel Schrödinger equation (with relativistic modifications and the K⁻p–\(\bar K^0\)n mass difference) using a Sturmian function numerical approach, which accurately handles both the short-range strong interaction and the long-range Coulomb force. Five realistic \(\overline KN\) potentials are employed: one phenomenological potential (AY) fitted to free \(\bar KN\) scattering data, KEK kaonic hydrogen data, and the \(\Lambda(1405)\) binding energy; and four chiral SU(3) based effective potentials (HNJH, ORB, BNW, BMN). The directly-evaluated K⁻p scattering lengths from solving the scattering equation are compared with those extracted from the same potentials' energy shifts and decay widths via the Deser-Trueman formula.

## Key numbers
- For the AY potential: directly-evaluated scattering length \(a_{K^-p} = -1.012 + 0.499 i\) fm; extracted scattering length \(\tilde{a}_{K^-p} = -0.934 + 0.348 i\) fm; energy shift \(\Delta E_{1s} = -384\) eV; half decay width \(\Gamma_{1s}/2 = 144\) eV.
- For the HNJH potential: directly-evaluated \(a_{K^-p} = -0.778 + 1.084 i\) fm; extracted \(\tilde{a}_{K^-p} = -0.815 + 0.785 i\) fm; \(\Delta E_{1s} = -336\) eV; \(\Gamma_{1s}/2 = 324\) eV.
- For the ORB potential: directly-evaluated \(a_{K^-p} = -0.804 + 1.067 i\) fm; extracted \(\tilde{a}_{K^-p} = -0.845 + 0.784 i\) fm; \(\Delta E_{1s} = -348\) eV; \(\Gamma_{1s}/2 = 323\) eV.
- For the BNW potential: directly-evaluated \(a_{K^-p} = -0.625 + 1.068 i\) fm; extracted \(\tilde{a}_{K^-p} = -0.700 + 0.818 i\) fm; \(\Delta E_{1s} = -288\) eV; \(\Gamma_{1s}/2 = 337\) eV.
- For the BMN potential: directly-evaluated \(a_{K^-p} = -0.655 + 0.992 i\) fm; extracted \(\tilde{a}_{K^-p} = -0.721 + 0.755 i\) fm; \(\Delta E_{1s} = -297\) eV; \(\Gamma_{1s}/2 = 311\) eV.
- The imaginary part of the extracted scattering length is smaller than the directly-evaluated one by an average factor of 0.26 across all five potentials.
- The Coulomb correction to the lowest Deser-Trueman formula is less than 10%.
- DEAR Collaboration data: \(\Gamma_{1s}/2 = 125 \pm 56\) (stat) \(\pm 15\) (syst) eV.
- Using Martin's scattering data with the modified Deser-Trueman formula yields \(\Gamma_{1s} = 202 \pm 20\) eV.
- Extracted decay widths from chiral SU(3) models range from \(\Gamma_{1s}/2 = 112\) eV (Ivanov 2004) to 307 eV (Oller 2001).

## Context
This work extends the theoretical study of kaonic hydrogen following the precise DEAR Collaboration measurement, addressing the long-standing puzzle that energy shifts and decay widths extracted from chiral SU(3) based model scattering lengths (Oller 2001, Borasoy 2005, Oller 2005, Borasoy 2006, Ivanov 2004, Ivanov 2005) appeared inconsistent with the DEAR data. The author shows that this inconsistency is largely resolved once the failure of the Deser-Trueman formula for the \(\overline KN\) system is accounted for. The results warn the SIDDHARTA collaboration that extracting kaon-nucleon scattering lengths from their planned kaonic hydrogen measurements using the Coulomb-corrected Deser-Trueman formula may be unreliable, and suggest that direct solution of the scattering equation is necessary for accurate extraction.
