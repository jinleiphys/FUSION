# Pseudospin symmetry and octupole correlations for multiple chiral doublets in 131Ba

> A reflection-asymmetric triaxial particle rotor model with three quasiparticles and a reflection-asymmetric triaxial rotor is developed, and applied to investigate the observed multiple chiral doublets (M\c{hi}D) candidates with octupole correlations in 131Ba, i.e., two pairs of positive-parity bands D3-D4 and D5-D6, as well as one pair of negative-parity bands D7-D8. The energy spectra, the energy staggering parameters, the B(M1)/B(E2) ratios, and the B(E1)/B(E2) ratios are reproduced well. The chiral geometries for these M\c{hi}D candidates are examined by the azimuthal plots, and the evolution of chiral geometry with spin is clearly demonstrated. The intrinsic structure for the positive-parity bands is analyzed and the possible pseudospin-chiral quartet bands are suggested.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed multiple chiral doublet (MχD) candidates in ¹³¹Ba, specifically two pairs of positive-parity bands (D3-D4 and D5-D6) and one pair of negative-parity bands (D7-D8), can be reproduced by a reflection-asymmetric triaxial particle rotor model (RAT-PRM) with three quasiparticles, and that the positive-parity bands may form pseudospin-chiral quartet bands. The hedge is that the pseudospin-chiral quartet interpretation is suggested based on the intrinsic structure analysis, not definitively proven.

## Method

The authors developed a Reflection-Asymmetric Triaxial Particle Rotor Model (RAT-PRM) coupling three quasiparticles (two valence protons and one valence neutron) with a reflection-asymmetric triaxial rotor. The Hamiltonian includes a core term with moments of inertia for irrotational flow and a core parity splitting parameter, plus intrinsic Hamiltonians for valence protons and neutrons in a reflection-asymmetric triaxial Nilsson potential with pairing correlations treated by the BCS approach. The model was applied to ¹³¹Ba using deformation parameters β₂=0.22, γ=27.1°, and β₃=0.02 from configuration-fixed triaxial covariant density functional theory, with the total Hamiltonian diagonalized in a symmetrized strong-coupled basis to obtain energies, electromagnetic transition probabilities, and azimuthal plots.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation β₂ = 0.22 and triaxial deformation γ = 27.1° for the configuration πh₁₁/₂(g₇/₂,d₅/₂)⊗νh₁₁/₂ in ¹³¹Ba, from configuration-fixed triaxial covariant density functional theory with PC-PK1
- Octupole deformation β₃ = 0.02 adopted to investigate octupole correlations
- Moment of inertia parameter 𝒥₀ = 19 ħ²/MeV adjusted to experimental spectra
- Core parity splitting parameter E(0⁻) = 2.1 MeV adjusted to experimental spectra
- Proton Fermi energy λπ = 43.86 MeV and neutron Fermi energy λν = 48.76 MeV
- Pairing gap parameter Δ = 12/√A MeV from empirical formula
- For bands D3-D4 at spin I=29/2ħ, the azimuthal plot for band D3 has a single peak at (65°, 90°) and band D4 shows two peaks at (70°, 50°) and (70°, 130°)
- For bands D3-D4 at spin I=35/2ħ, band D3 shows two peaks at (80°, 30°) and (80°, 150°), band D4 shows two peaks at (70°, 50°) and (70°, 130°)
- For bands D3-D4 at spin I=41/2ħ, band D3 peaks at (90°, 0°) and (90°, 180°), band D4 peaks at (85°, 30°) and (85°, 150°)
- For bands D5-D6, chiral vibration at I=29/2ħ, static chirality at I=31/2ħ, tendency to principal axis rotation at I=33/2ħ
- For bands D7-D8 at I=25/2ħ, band D7 peaks at (70°, 90°), band D8 peaks at (60°, 0°) and (60°, 180°)
- For bands D7-D8, chiral vibration at I=31/2ħ, static chirality at I=35/2ħ, tendency to principal axis rotation at I=39/2ħ
- Changing β₃ by 10% changes B(E1) values by about 20% while leaving energies, staggering parameters, B(M1)/B(E2) ratios, and chiral geometry negligibly affected

## Context

This work extends the RAT-PRM previously developed for two quasiparticles (Wang, 2019) to three quasiparticles, enabling the description of the MχD candidates with octupole correlations recently observed in ¹³¹Ba by Guo (2019). The positive-parity bands D3-D4 and D5-D6 are suggested to be pseudospin-chiral quartet bands involving the pseudospin partners (g₇/₂,d₅/₂), a mechanism distinct from the yrast/excited chiral doublet mechanism proposed for ¹⁰³Rh by Kuti (2014). The results enable future studies of chiral geometry evolution with spin and octupole correlations in other mass regions where MχD candidates have been reported, such as A∼80, 100, 130, and 190.
