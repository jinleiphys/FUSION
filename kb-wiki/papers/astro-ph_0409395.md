# Evidence for nucleosynthesis in supernova $γ$-process: Universal scaling on p-nuclei

> With analyzing the solar system abundance, we have found two universal scaling laws concerning the p- and s-nuclei. They indicate that the gamma-process in supernova (SN) explosions is the most promising origin of the p-nuclei that has been discussed with many possible nuclear reactions and sites for about fifty years. In addition the scalings provide new concepts: an universality of the gamma-process and a new nuclear cosmochronometer. We have carried out gamma-process nucleosynthesis calculations for typical core-collapse SN explosion models and the results satisfy the observed scalings.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two universal scaling laws in solar system abundances, N(s)/N(p) ≈ 23 and N(1st p)/N(2nd p) ≈ 1, provide evidence that the gamma-process in supernova explosions is the most promising origin of p-nuclei, but the authors hedge that other astrophysical sites such as deflagrating white dwarf stars and supernova-driven supercritical accretion disks may also contribute, and that the scaling holds only after accounting for exceptions due to r-process contamination, weak s-process branches, and abundance uncertainties.

## Method

The authors analyzed solar system isotope abundances for 22 p-nuclei associated with pure s-nuclei, computing the abundance ratios N(s)/N(p) and N(1st p)/N(2nd p). They then performed gamma-process nucleosynthesis calculations for core-collapse supernova explosion models with 15, 25, and 40 solar mass progenitors at solar metallicity (Z = Z_sun) and at Z = 0.05 Z_sun, using an explosion energy of 10^51 ergs and s-processed initial abundances.

## Key numbers

- N(s)/N(p) ≈ 23 (constant value) for the ratio of pure s-nucleus abundance to pure p-nucleus abundance in a wide region of atomic number, with deviations for Ce, W, Cd, Sn, Gd, Mo, Ru, and heavy-mass elements.
- N(1st p)/N(2nd p) ≈ 1 for the ratio of two pure p-nuclei with the same atomic number, with a weak slope and a large deviation for Er attributed to beta-decay of 163Dy under stellar s-process conditions.
- The 25 solar mass progenitor model with explosion energy 10^51 ergs and solar metallicity was used for the primary calculations; 15 and 40 solar mass progenitors showed no drastic change in the two ratios.
- The proposed cosmochronometer uses 176Lu with half-life 37.8 Gyr, paired with 176Hf and 174Hf, for dating gamma-process events.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing search for the origin of p-nuclei, which had been debated for about fifty years with proposed mechanisms including the rp-process (Schatz 1998, 2001), cosmic-ray proton-induced reactions (Audouze 1970), the gamma-process (Arnould 1976, Woosley 1978, Howard 1991, Fujimoto 2003), and the neutrino-process (Woosley 1990, Hoffman 1996, Goriely 2001). The scaling laws provide a new tool to constrain supernova gamma-process models and enable a new cosmochronometer for dating pre-solar grains affected by single supernova events, complementing earlier chronometers like 146Sm and 92Nb (Audouze 1972, Harper 1996) which have half-lives shorter than the solar system age.
