# Rapidity bin multiplicity correlations from a multi-phase transport model

> The central-arbitrary bin and forward-backward bin multiplicity correlation patterns for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} $= $7.7-62.4$ GeV are investigated within a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model. An interesting observation is that for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} <19.6$ GeV Au+Au collisions, these two correlation patterns both have an increase with the pseudorapidity gap, while for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} >19.6$ GeV Au+Au collisions, they decrease. We mainly discuss the influence of different evolution stages of collision system on the central-arbitrary bin correlations, such as the initial conditions, partonic scatterings, hadronization scheme and hadronic scatterings. Our results show that the central-arbitrary bin multiplicity correlations have different responses to partonic phase and hadronic phase, which can be suggested as a good probe to explore the dynamical evolution mechanism of the hot dense matter in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the sign of the pseudorapidity-gap dependence of two-bin multiplicity correlations in Au+Au collisions changes at a threshold energy of approximately 19.6 GeV, with correlations increasing with gap below this energy and decreasing above it. This behavior is attributed to the competing effects of partonic scatterings (which weaken short-range correlations) and the quark coalescence hadronization scheme (which enhances short-range correlations), with the relative importance of each phase depending on collision energy.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with string melting mechanism to simulate Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 7.7 to 62.4 GeV. They computed two types of multiplicity correlation coefficients: forward-backward bin correlations (symmetric bins about midrapidity) and central-arbitrary bin correlations (fixed central bin versus varying bins), both as functions of pseudorapidity gap. To isolate the effects of different evolution stages, they compared results from the full AMPT simulation (including hadronic scatterings via the ART model) with results obtained just after quark coalescence but before hadronic scatterings, and also examined parton-level correlations at different times during the parton cascade.

## Key numbers

- The correlation patterns change behavior at a threshold of approximately \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6\) GeV: for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 19.6\) GeV (e.g., 7.7 and 11.5 GeV) correlations increase with pseudorapidity gap, while for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} > 19.6\) GeV (e.g., 27, 39, and 62.4 GeV) they decrease.
- The parton cross section used in the AMPT simulations was fixed at 10 mb.
- For Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV, the parton density at mid-rapidity right before hadronization was approximately half of that at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4\) GeV.
- The pseudorapidity bin width used for correlation measurements was \(\delta \eta = 0.2\), covering the region \([-2, 2]\) with 20 bins.
- Centrality classes studied were 0-10%, 30-40%, and 50-80% for selected energies (7.7, 19.6, and 39 GeV).

## Context

This work extends the study of rapidity correlations from proton-proton collisions (studied in the 1980s-1990s, e.g., by the authors of 1986 PRD and 1994 PRL) to heavy-ion collisions, where the presence of a deconfined QGP phase is expected. The authors build on previous observations that longitudinal correlations are sensitive to early-stage dynamics (e.g., the forward-backward correlation studies by the authors of FB-ex and FB-th) and that balance function narrowing implies delayed hadronization (narrow-bf1, narrow-bf2). The results enable future experimental comparisons with the STAR Beam Energy Scan program, suggesting that these correlation patterns can serve as a probe to distinguish partonic from hadronic dominance in the system evolution across different collision energies.
