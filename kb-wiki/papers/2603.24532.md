# Many-body perturbation theory for the nuclear equation of state up to fifth order

> We present an automated, GPU-accelerated framework for many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) calculations of the zero-temperature nuclear equation of state (EOS) based on chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions. Automated diagram generation and evaluation enable the computation of all diagrams up to fifth order in the MBPT expansion at the normal-ordered two-body level in infinite matter, with residual three-body contributions explicitly included up to third order. Multi-GPU acceleration of 3N normal ordering, a novel Monte Carlo integrator (called PVegas), and further advances in high-performance computing enable us to evaluate all 840 fifth-order diagrams with controlled numerical uncertainties. We investigate the MBPT convergence up to fifth order in pure neutron matter (PNM) and symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) for two sets of chiral interactions, study neutron star matter, and present fourth-order results for asymmetric matter including normal-ordered 3N forces. The framework enables systematic MBPT studies with harder interactions and benchmarks against nonperturbative methods. It can be further extended to finite-temperature EOS calculations and to improved uncertainty quantification using emulation and resummation techniques.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) calculations of the nuclear equation of state can be extended to fifth order with controlled numerical uncertainties, but the convergence behavior depends on the chiral interaction used and the density regime, with the expansion showing signs of asymptotic rather than convergent behavior at higher densities.

## Method

The authors developed an automated, GPU-accelerated framework that generates and evaluates all MBPT diagrams up to fifth order at the normal-ordered two-body level in infinite nuclear matter, using chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions. Residual three-body contributions were explicitly included up to third order, and a novel Monte Carlo integrator called PVegas was employed to evaluate all 840 fifth-order diagrams with controlled numerical uncertainties. The calculations were performed for pure neutron matter (PNM) and symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) using two sets of chiral interactions, with the Hartree-Fock single-particle spectrum used for energy denominators.

## Key numbers

- 840 fifth-order MBPT diagrams were evaluated, compared to only 3 and 39 diagrams at third and fourth order, respectively
- MBPT calculations were performed up to fifth order (MBPT(5)) at the normal-ordered two-body level
- Residual three-nucleon contributions were explicitly included up to third order (15 residual 3N diagrams)
- The framework enables calculations up to twice nuclear saturation density (approximately 0.32 fm^-3)

## Context

This work extends previous MBPT calculations that were limited to second or third order in infinite matter (Drischler 2017, Leonhardt 2019), where the first fourth-order MBPT calculations for infinite matter were carried out but with 3N contributions only included up to third order at the normal-ordered two-body level (Hebeler 2020). The automated framework enables systematic benchmarks against nonperturbative methods such as quantum Monte Carlo (Lonardoni 2019, Lovato 2022) and the in-medium similarity renormalization group (Zhen 2025), and can be extended to finite-temperature EOS calculations and improved uncertainty quantification using emulation and resummation techniques.
