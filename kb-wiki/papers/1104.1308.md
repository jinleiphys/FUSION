# Deuteron photodisintegration with polarized lasers

> A model independent theoretical analysis of recent experimental data on deuteron photodisintegration with polarized laser beams is presented. We find that it is important to distinguish between the three isovector E1 amplitudes $E1_v^j$ in reaction channels with total angular momentum $j=0,1,2$ and that the isoscalar M1 amplitude $M1_s$ is non-zero in the photon energy range $3.5 MeV < E_γ< 10 MeV$

# Deuteron photodisintegration with polarized lasers

## Key claim
The isoscalar M1 amplitude \(M1_s\) is non-zero in the photon energy range \(3.5\ \mathrm{MeV} < E_\gamma < 10\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and the three isovector E1 amplitudes \(E1_v^j\) in channels with total angular momentum \(j=0,1,2\) cannot all be equal, contrary to the traditional attribution of deuteron photodisintegration solely to an isovector E1 transition.

## Method
The authors performed a model-independent theoretical analysis of experimental data on deuteron photodisintegration using 100% linearly polarized photon beams from the Duke Free Electron Laser Laboratory (HIGS). They derived an expression for the differential cross section that includes a \(\cos\theta\) term, and extracted the ratio \(c/a\) from experimental data reported by Sawatzky and Blackston, where \(c\) is the coefficient of the \(\cos\theta\) term and \(a\) is the isotropic term. The coefficient \(c\) is expressed as a linear combination of the three isovector E1 amplitudes and the isoscalar M1 amplitude, allowing the authors to infer the non-zero nature of \(M1_s\) and the inequality of the \(E1_v^j\) amplitudes.

## Key numbers
- At \(E_\gamma = 3.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(c/a = 0.2325 \pm 0.0722\).
- At \(E_\gamma = 4\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(c/a = 0.1084 \pm 0.0391\).
- At \(E_\gamma = 6\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(c/a = 0.0160 \pm 0.0141\).
- At \(E_\gamma = 10\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(c/a = -0.1413 \pm 0.0074\).
- At \(E_\gamma = 14\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(c/a = -0.056 \pm 0.006\).
- At \(E_\gamma = 16\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(c/a = -0.077 \pm 0.006\).
- The isoscalar M1 amplitude \(M1_s\) is non-zero in the photon energy range \(3.5\ \mathrm{MeV} < E_\gamma < 10\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context
This work extends the long history of experimental and theoretical studies of the \(d+\gamma \rightleftharpoons n+p\) reaction, dating back to Chadwick and Bethe (1930s). It addresses the 10% discrepancy in the total cross section noted by Breit and Rustgi, who proposed a polarized-target-beam test to detect an isoscalar \(M1_s\) transition, which Riska and Brown later explained via meson exchange currents. The present model-independent analysis contradicts the traditional Rustgi parametrization and Partovi parametrization by showing that the \(\cos\theta\) term survives even when higher-order multipoles are neglected, and that the interference term \(c/a\) increases as \(E_\gamma\) decreases toward astrophysically relevant energies. This enables future work using polarized deuteron targets with both tensor and vector polarization to distinguish the three \(E1_v^j\) amplitudes, with implications for big bang nucleosynthesis predictions.
