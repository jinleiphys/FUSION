# Electromagnetic and Mass Difference Corrections in pion N Scattering

> Coulomb and mass difference corrections to low-energy pion-nucleon scattering are calculated and compared with previous work including potential models, dispersion relation methods and chiral perturbation theory calculations. Particular attention is paid to their role in testing isospin breaking.

# Electromagnetic and Mass Difference Corrections in $\pi N$ Scattering

## Key claim

The paper calculates Coulomb and mass-difference corrections to low-energy pion-nucleon scattering using a potential model (the GAK approach) and finds that while these corrections are in rough agreement with previous potential-model and dispersion-relation results, the mass-difference correction dominates over the Coulomb correction at low energies, and both corrections move the predicted charge-exchange cross section *away* from experimental data at forward angles, contrary to what would be expected if the corrections were purely electromagnetic in origin.

## Method

The authors solve coupled-channel wave equations for the $\pi^-p$ and $\pi^0n$ system, including the Coulomb potential in the charged channel and using physical masses for pions and nucleons (neutron-proton and $\pi^\pm$–$\pi^0$ mass differences). The hadronic (isospin-pure) potentials are assumed unchanged, and the resulting $K$-matrix is diagonalized to extract phase shifts $\delta_1$, $\delta_2$ and a mixing angle $\phi$; the corrections are defined as the differences between these quantities and their hadronic (isospin-symmetric) values. For $\pi^+p$ scattering, the correction is simply the difference between the nuclear phase shift (Coulomb included) and the hadronic phase shift. The results are compared with the potential-model work of Gashi et al. (2000), the dispersion-relation NORDITA results of Tromborg et al. (1978), and the chiral perturbation theory calculation of Fettes and Meissner (2001).

## Key numbers

- The isospin breaking in the extracted charged-pion scattering amplitudes was previously found to be of order **8%** in the amplitude.
- For the $P_{1/2}$ partial wave, the hadronic phase shifts cross near **60 MeV** laboratory kinetic energy, causing a non-perturbative behavior in the correction extraction.
- The forward charge-exchange cross section shows a deep minimum near **45 MeV**; the position of this minimum is sensitive to isospin breaking but does not depend on an absolute cross-section normalization.
- The difference between prediction and data for charge-exchange cross sections can be expressed as a difference in normalization of around **15%**.
- For the $P_{3/2}$ partial wave, the ratio $R_2$ from the hadronic models is a factor of **3–5** larger than the chiral perturbation theory result when the flux factor is removed.
- The GAK hadronic phase shifts are fitted with analytic forms valid from **5–100 MeV**; for example, the $S_{11}$ wave has $\delta = a\sqrt{T}/(1+bT+cT^2)$ with $a=1.0319$, $b=2.865\times10^{-3}$, $c=-8.0\times10^{-7}$ (T in MeV, $\delta$ in degrees).

## Context

This work extends the potential-model corrections of Gashi et al. (2000) and Zimmermann (1994) and the dispersion-relation NORDITA corrections of Tromborg et al. (1978) by presenting the previously unpublished GAK corrections and comparing them with chiral perturbation theory (Fettes and Meissner, 2001). The results enable improved tests of isospin breaking in low-energy pion-nucleon scattering, but the disagreement between hadronic-model corrections and chiral perturbation theory in the $P_{3/2}$ partial wave, where the Delta resonance dominates, suggests that either the hadronic corrections are systematically too large or the Delta degree of freedom must be treated differently in chiral perturbation theory. The paper also highlights a fundamental difficulty in defining corrections for the $P_{1/2}$ partial wave near the crossing point of the hadronic phase shifts, where the mixing angle $\phi$ changes by $\pi$ and the correction becomes non-perturbative.
