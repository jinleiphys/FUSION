# On the existence of a bound tetraneutron

> Following recent work in which events which may correspond to a bound tetraneutron ($^4$n) were observed, it is pointed out that from the theoretical perspective the two-body nucleon-nucleon force cannot by itself bind four neutrons, even if it can bind a dineutron. A very strong phenomenological four-nucleon (4N) force is needed in order to bind the tetraneutron. Such a 4N force, if it existed, would bind $^4$He by about 100 MeV. Alternative experiments such as ($^8$He,$^4$n) are proposed to search for the tetraneutron.

## Key claim

The two-body nucleon-nucleon force cannot by itself bind four neutrons, even if it can bind a dineutron; a very strong phenomenological four-nucleon (4N) force would be needed to bind the tetraneutron, and such a force, if it existed, would bind ⁴He by about 100 MeV.

## Method

The author performed hyperspherical functions method (HSFM) calculations of hyperradial potentials for the tetraneutron up to hyperangular momentum \(K_{max} = 16\), using the Reichstein-Tang (RT) central NN potential, the Volkov V1 effective NN potential, and the realistic Argonne \(v_4\) central NN interaction. The diagonalised hyperradial potentials were examined for attractive pockets that could indicate bound or resonant states, and the strength of a simulated 4N force needed to bind the tetraneutron was estimated by adding a Gaussian potential \(V_{4N}(\rho) = W_0 e^{-\alpha \rho}\) to the RT and V1 calculations.

## Key numbers

- With the RT potential and \(u = 1\) (no interaction in odd partial waves), the hyperradial potentials are purely repulsive and show no attractive pockets, excluding any bound or resonance state for the tetraneutron.
- To get a bound tetraneutron with the RT potential, the parameter \(u\) must be increased to 2.3; this would bind ⁴H and ⁵He by about 6.5 MeV and 32 MeV respectively, whereas in reality these nuclei are unbound by 3 MeV and 0.9 MeV.
- With the Volkov V1 potential, changing the Majorana parameter from its standard value of 0.6 to \(m = -0.2\) gives E(⁴n) = −1.2 MeV but binds ⁴H and ⁵He by 7 MeV and 25 MeV respectively.
- For a simulated 4N force with range \(\alpha = 0.7, 1.2, 1.5\) fm⁻¹, the tetraneutron becomes bound when \(W_0\) equals −410, −1460, and −2530 MeV respectively (with the RT potential); these strengths are two orders of magnitude larger than the 3N spin-orbit force values of −1, −10, and −20 MeV.
- If the same 4N force existed in ⁴He, the binding energy of ⁴He calculated with V1 would be −88, −82, and −134 MeV respectively.

## Context

This work responds to experimental events reported by Marques and Orr (2002) that may correspond to a bound tetraneutron from ¹⁴Be breakup. The author extends prior theoretical calculations by Bev (unbound by 18.5 MeV), Gorb (no bound state), Varga (no bound state), and others who found no bound tetraneutron with two-body forces, and specifically critiques the resonance claim by GNO (2001) as unconvincing due to lack of convergence. The paper proposes alternative experiments such as (⁸He,⁴n) transfer reactions to search for the tetraneutron, and suggests that if confirmed, the tetraneutron would require a strong T-dependent 4N force, which would challenge modern realistic models of the three-nucleon interaction.
