# Approximate bound state solutions of the deformed Woods-Saxon potential using asymptotic iteration method

> By using the Pekeris approximation, the Schrodinger equation is approximately solved for the nuclear deformed Woods-Saxon potential within the framework of the asymptotic iteration method. The energy levels are worked out and the corresponding normalized eigenfunctions are obtained in terms of hypergeometric function.

## Key claim
The paper claims to obtain approximate analytical bound-state solutions of the Schrodinger equation for the deformed Woods-Saxon potential for any orbital angular momentum, but only by applying the Pekeris approximation to the centrifugal term, which restricts the validity to cases where the nuclear radius is much larger than the diffuseness parameter (R >> a).

## Method
The authors start from the radial Schrodinger equation with the deformed Woods-Saxon potential and apply the Pekeris approximation to expand the centrifugal term 1/r^2 in a series around r=R, then re-express it as a sum of terms proportional to powers of the potential's Fermi-function form. After a change of variable z = (1+q exp((r-R)/a))^{-1}, the equation is transformed into a hypergeometric-type differential equation, which is solved using the asymptotic iteration method (AIM). The energy eigenvalues are obtained from the quantization condition of the AIM, and the normalized wave functions are expressed in terms of hypergeometric functions.

## Key numbers
- The potential depth is parameterized as V_o = (40.5 + 0.13 A_o) MeV, where A_o is the atomic mass number of the target nucleus.
- For the ground state (n=0) with parameters mu=1 fm^{-1}, V_o=0.3 fm^{-1}, R=7 fm, a=0.65 fm, the three states with l=0,1,2 have equal energy E approx -0.165 fm^{-1} at deformation parameter q approx 0.6.
- For the first excited state (n=1) with the same parameters, the three states have equal energy E approx -1.04 fm^{-1} at q approx 1.0.
- For n=0, the energy eigenvalues increase with increasing diffuseness a, becoming weakly attractive with ordering E_{02} > E_{01} > E_{00}.
- For n=0, at potential width R approx 5.1 fm, the energies of l=0,1,2 coincide; for R < 5.1 fm, E_{00} > E_{01} > E_{02}, and for R > 5.1 fm, E_{02} > E_{01} > E_{00}.
- For n=0, E_{02} becomes positive (repulsive) for reduced mass mu > 2.2 fm^{-1}, E_{01} becomes positive for mu > 2.5 fm^{-1}, and E_{00} becomes positive for mu > 3.0 fm^{-1}.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of the Woods-Saxon potential using the Nikiforov-Uvarov method by Badalov et al. (2010, 2011) and the authors' own previous relativistic treatments of the deformed Woods-Saxon potential in the Duffin-Kemmer-Petiau and Dirac equations (Falaye et al., 2012; Hamzavi et al., 2012). The analytical expressions for energy levels and wave functions enable systematic studies of single-particle states in deformed nuclei, particularly for applications to plutonium isotopes where the deformed Woods-Saxon parameterization at large deformations has been analyzed (Cwiok et al., 1994). The method also provides a framework for testing the sensitivity of bound-state energies to the deformation parameter q, diffuseness a, potential depth V_o, width R, and reduced mass mu.
