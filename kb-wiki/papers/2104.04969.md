# Stability of Neutron Stars with Dark Matter Core Using Three Crustal Types and the Impact on Mass-Radius Relations

> We investigate the effects of dark matter (DM) on the nuclear equation of state (EoS) and neutron star structure, in the relativistic mean field theory, both in the absence and presence of a crust. The $σ-ω$ model is modified by adding a WIMP-DM component, which interacts with nucleonic matter through the Higgs portal. This model agrees well with previous studies which utilized either a more complicated nuclear model or higher-order terms of the Higgs potential, in that DM softens the EoS, resulting in stars with lower maximum masses. However, instabilities corresponding to negative pressure values in the low-energy density regime of the DM-admixed EoS are present, and this effect becomes more prominent as we increase the DM Fermi momentum. We resolve this by confining DM in the star's core. The regions of instability were replaced by three types of crust: first by the Friedman-Pandharipande-Skyrme (FPS), Skyrme-Lyon (SLy) and BSk19 EoS from the Brussels-Montreal Group, which can be represented by analytical approximations. For a fixed value of the DM Fermi momentum $p_F^{DM}$, the DM-admixed neutron star does not have significant changes in its mass with the addition of the crusts. However, the entire mass-radius relation of the neutron star is significantly affected, with an observed increase in the radius of the star corresponding to the mass. The effect of DM is to reduce the mass of the star, while the crust does not affect the radius significantly, as the value of the $p_F^{DM}$ increases.

# Stability of Neutron Stars with Dark Matter Core Using Three Crustal Types and the Impact on Mass-Radius Relations

## Key claim

Dark matter softens the nuclear equation of state and reduces neutron star maximum masses, but instabilities (negative pressure) appear in the low-energy density regime of the DM-admixed EoS; these instabilities are resolved by confining DM to the star's core and replacing the unstable regions with crustal equations of state, which significantly affects the mass-radius relation by increasing the radius for a given mass.

## Method

The authors modify the relativistic mean field theory sigma-omega (Walecka) model by adding a fermionic WIMP dark matter component with mass 200 GeV that interacts with nucleonic matter through the Higgs portal, keeping only terms up to order h^2. They solve the mean-field equations for the sigma, omega, and Higgs fields simultaneously for nucleon Fermi momenta and DM Fermi momenta of 0.02, 0.04, and 0.06 GeV, then compute the energy density and pressure to obtain the parametric EoS. The unstable low-pressure regions of the DM-admixed EoS are replaced by three different crustal EoS (FPS, SLy, and BSk19) at the intersection points, and the resulting EoS are fed into the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute mass-radius relations for static, nonrotating neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- DM particle mass: M_chi = 200 GeV; Higgs boson mass: M_h = 125 GeV; nucleon mass: m_n ≈ 1 GeV; sigma meson mass: m_sigma = 520 MeV; omega meson mass: m_omega = 783 MeV
- Coupling constants: g_omega^2 = 190.4, g_sigma^2 = 109.6; Higgs-nucleon coupling parameter f = 0.3; Higgs vacuum expectation value v = 246 GeV
- DM Fermi momenta considered: p_F^DM = 0.02 GeV, 0.04 GeV, 0.06 GeV
- For p_F^DM = 0 (no DM), maximum mass M_lim/M_sun = 2.827 and limiting radius R_lim = 13.039 km for the bare sigma-omega-DM EoS; with FPS crust: M_lim/M_sun = 2.827, R_lim = 13.512 km; with SLy crust: M_lim/M_sun = 2.827, R_lim = 13.533 km; with BSk19 crust: M_lim/M_sun = 2.827, R_lim = 13.406 km
- For p_F^DM = 0.06 GeV, maximum mass M_lim/M_sun = 2.124 and R_lim = 9.447 km (bare); with FPS crust: M_lim/M_sun = 2.138, R_lim = 10.899 km; with SLy crust: M_lim/M_sun = 2.155, R_lim = 11.220 km; with BSk19 crust: M_lim/M_sun = 2.135, R_lim = 10.461 km
- Intersection energy densities between crust and DM-admixed core at p_F^DM = 0.06 GeV: FPS: epsilon = 8.79 × 10^14 g/cm^3, P = 7.78 × 10^34 dyne/cm^2; SLy: epsilon = 10.2 × 10^14 g/cm^3, P = 14.8 × 10^34 dyne/cm^2; BSk19: epsilon = 8.69 × 10^14 g/cm^3, P = 7.34 × 10^34 dyne/cm^2

## Context

This work extends previous studies of DM effects on neutron stars using relativistic mean field theory, particularly Panotopoulos (2017) who used the sigma-omega model without crust, and Das (2019) who added a Baym-Pethick-Sutherland crust with a polytropic connection to the core. The authors address the instability problem of negative pressure in the DM-admixed EoS by confining DM to the core and replacing unstable regions with three realistic crustal EoS (FPS, SLy, BSk19) from Haensel (2005) and Potekhin (2013). This enables more realistic modeling of neutron star structure with DM cores and allows investigation of how different crust types affect mass-radius relations, potentially constraining DM properties through future neutron star observations.
