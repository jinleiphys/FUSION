> Employing a kinetic framework, we calculate all transport coefficients for relativistic dissipative (second-order) hydrodynamics for arbitrary particle masses in the 14-moment approximation. # Relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics for particles of arbitrary mass

## Key claim

The authors calculate all second-order transport coefficients for relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics for arbitrary particle masses within the 14-moment approximation, and show that the relativistic theory reduces to the Grad equations in the non-relativistic limit. The derivation is performed within a kinetic framework using classical statistics, meaning the results are applicable to rarefied gases with weak coupling rather than strongly coupled systems.

## Method

The authors start from the Boltzmann equation and transform it into an infinite system of moment equations, then truncate at the 14-moment approximation (keeping moments corresponding to particle number, energy, momentum, bulk viscous pressure, particle diffusion current, and shear-stress tensor). They compute all transport coefficients appearing in the second-order equations of motion for bulk viscous pressure, particle diffusion current, and shear-stress tensor by evaluating linear and non-linear collision term contributions. The non-relativistic limit is taken by expanding thermodynamic integrals asymptotically for large mass (z = beta*m approaching infinity) and comparing the resulting equations to Grad's 13-moment formulation.

## Key numbers

- Bulk viscosity coefficient: zeta = (m^2/3) * alpha_0^(0) * tau_00^(0)
- Particle diffusion coefficient: kappa = alpha_0^(1) * tau_00^(1)
- Shear viscosity coefficient: eta = alpha_0^(2) * tau_00^(2)
- Relaxation time for bulk viscous pressure: tau_Pi = tau_00^(0)
- Relaxation time for particle diffusion: tau_V = tau_00^(1)
- Relaxation time for shear stress: tau_pi = tau_00^(2)
- Asymptotic non-relativistic pressure: P_0^(infinity) = e^(alpha - z) / ((2*pi)^(3/2) * beta^4) * z^(3/2)
- Asymptotic non-relativistic thermodynamic integral: I_nq^(infinity) = e^(alpha - z) / ((2*pi)^(3/2) * beta^(n+2)) * z^(n - q + 1/2)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of second-order transport coefficients that were mostly restricted to massless fluid constituents, such as those by Denicol (2012), Molnar (2013), and Wagner (2022). The results enable numerical simulations of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics that include the effects of non-zero particle masses on transport properties, which is relevant for describing systems like the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions or astrophysical plasmas. The authors note that in the massless case, exact transport coefficients can differ substantially from the 14-moment approximation results (Wagner, 2024), suggesting that extending the analysis beyond the 14-moment approximation for massive particles is a logical next step.
