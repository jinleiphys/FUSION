# Proton-proton forward scattering at the LHC

> Recently the TOTEM experiment at the LHC has released measurements at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV of the proton-proton total cross section, $σ_{tot}$, and the ratio of the real to imaginary parts of the forward elastic amplitude, $ρ$. Since then an intense debate on the $C$-parity asymptotic nature of the scattering amplitude was initiated. We examine the proton-proton and the antiproton-proton forward data above 10 GeV in the context of an eikonal QCD-based model, where nonperturbative effects are readily included via a QCD effective charge. We show that, despite an overall satisfactory description of the forward data is obtained by a model in which the scattering amplitude is dominated by only crossing-even elastic terms, there is evidence that the introduction of a crossing-odd term may improve the agreement with the measurements of $ρ$ at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV. In the Regge language the dominant even(odd)-under-crossing object is the so called Pomeron (Odderon).

## Key claim
Despite an overall satisfactory description of forward proton-proton and antiproton-proton data by a model with only crossing-even elastic amplitudes, the TOTEM measurements of the ratio ρ at √s = 13 TeV are lower than the model prediction, suggesting that a crossing-odd term (Odderon) may play a central role in soft and semihard interactions at high energies.

## Method
The authors use an eikonal QCD-based model where the scattering amplitude is decomposed into soft and semihard components, with the semihard eikonal computed from QCD parton-parton cross sections using CT14 parton distribution functions and an infrared finite QCD effective charge dependent on a dynamical gluon mass. The soft eikonal includes even and odd contributions based on Regge-Gribov formalism, with the odd term significant only at low energies. A global fit to forward total cross section σtot and ρ data for both pp and p̅p channels from √s = 10 GeV to LHC energies is performed, with analyticity ensured by the substitution s → se^{-iπ/2}.

## Key numbers
- σtot(pp) at √s = 13 TeV predicted by the model: 105.6^{+7.9}_{-6.3} mb
- ρ(pp) at √s = 13 TeV predicted by the model: 0.1182^{+0.0074}_{-0.0057}
- TOTEM measured σtot(pp) at √s = 13 TeV: 110.6 ± 3.4 mb (first measurement) and 110.3 ± 3.5 mb (second measurement)
- TOTEM measured ρ(pp) at √s = 13 TeV: 0.09 ± 0.01 and 0.10 ± 0.01
- χ²/ζ for the global fit: 1.30 with ζ = 166 degrees of freedom
- Dynamical gluon mass scale set to mg = 400 MeV; minimal momentum transfer Qmin = 1.3 GeV

## Context
This work extends the eikonal QCD-based model previously developed by Luna et al. (2010, 2015, 2017) to include the recent TOTEM measurements at √s = 13 TeV (Antchev et al., 2017, 2019). The model assumes crossing-even dominance (Pomeron) at high energies, in contrast to models that include an Odderon (Ewerz et al., 2010; Martynov and Nicolescu, 2019). The discrepancy between the model prediction for ρ at 13 TeV and the TOTEM data suggests that future measurements and analyses should consider the introduction of a crossing-odd term, potentially enabling discrimination between Pomeron-only and Odderon-inclusive scenarios for forward scattering at LHC energies.
