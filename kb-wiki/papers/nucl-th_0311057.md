# Quantum Rotational Spectra and Classical Rotors

> We consider the generalized rotor Hamiltonians capable of describing quantum systems invariant with respect to symmetry point-groups that go beyond the usual D_2-symmetry of a tri-axial rotor. We discuss the canonical de-quantisation procedure to obtain the classical analogs of the original quantum Hamiltonians. Classical and quantum solutions to the Hamiltonians relevant in the nuclear physics applications are illustrated and compared using the 'usual' (D_2) and an 'exotic' (T_d) symmetries.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that generalized rotor Hamiltonians invariant under point groups beyond the usual \(D_2\) symmetry (specifically tetrahedral \(T_d\) symmetry) can be constructed and de-quantized to obtain classical analogs, but the nuclear physics applications must focus on very low-spin limits because angular-momentum alignment phenomena are expected to destroy the delicate balance leading to such high symmetries.

## Method

The authors construct generalized rotor Hamiltonians using spherical-tensor operators built from angular-momentum operators via Clebsch-Gordan coupling, allowing expansion for any predefined point-group symmetry. They apply an inverse canonical quantization procedure (replacing commutators with Poisson brackets and operators with classical variables) to obtain classical Hamiltonians, then derive Hamilton equations for the Euler angles and analyze equilibrium points via the Jacobi matrix to classify trajectories as periodic (centers) or aperiodic (saddles/separatrices). The method is illustrated for both the standard \(D_2\)-symmetric rotor and an exotic \(T_d\)-symmetric rotor.

## Key numbers

- For the \(D_2\)-symmetric rotor illustration: quadrupole deformations \(\beta_2=0.25\) and \(\gamma=30^\circ\) (maximum triaxiality); Hamiltonian coefficients \(h_{00}=-0.016\), \(h_{20}=0.002\), \(h_{22}=0.001\); corresponding moments of inertia \(J_x=53.1\), \(J_y=67.4\), \(J_z=45.9\) in \(\hbar/\)MeV for angular momentum \(J=10\,\hbar\) (typical Rare Earth nucleus).
- For the \(T_d\)-symmetric rotor illustration: Hamiltonian coefficients \(h_{00}=-0.016\), \(h_{32}=i0.003003\); angular momentum \(J=16\,\hbar\).
- The \(T_d\)-symmetric rotor has eight equilibrium points: all combinations of \(\beta=\arccos(1/3),\,\pi-\arccos(1/3)\) with \(\gamma=\pi/4,\,3\pi/4,\,5\pi/4,\,7\pi/4\), plus four points at \(\beta=\pi/2\) with \(\gamma=0,\,\pi/2,\,\pi,\,3\pi/2\) (classified as saddles).

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of tri-axial rotors with \(D_2\) symmetry (the usual nuclear rotor Hamiltonian with three moments of inertia) to rotors with higher point-group symmetries such as tetrahedral \(T_d\), motivated by realistic microscopic calculations predicting that some excited nuclear states may be characterized by high tetrahedral and/or octahedral symmetries (Dudek, Gozdz, and Schunck, 2002). The de-quantization procedure is analogous to those used in molecular physics studies, but the authors emphasize that nuclear physics applications differ significantly because the total symmetries of nuclei and molecules are very different, and nuclear rotors must be studied at very low spins where the high symmetry is not destroyed by angular-momentum alignment. The work enables future comparison between classical trajectory features (such as separatrix energies) and quantum spectral patterns (such as multiplet degeneracies and state densities) for high-symmetry nuclear rotors.
