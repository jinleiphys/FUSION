# Progress on the three-particle quantization condition

> We report progress on extending the relativistic model-independent quantization condition for three particles, derived previously by two of us, to a broader class of theories, as well as progress on checking the formalism. In particular, we discuss the extension to include the possibility of 2->3 and 3->2 transitions and the calculation of the finite-volume energy shift of an Efimov-like three-particle bound state. The latter agrees with the results obtained previously using non-relativistic quantum mechanics.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previously derived relativistic, model-independent three-particle quantization condition to include 2→3 and 3→2 transitions, and they verify the formalism by reproducing the finite-volume energy shift of an Efimov-like three-particle bound state previously obtained using non-relativistic quantum mechanics, with the caveat that the extension to include transitions remains a conjecture pending completion of the full derivation.

## Method

The authors generalize the three-particle quantization condition by removing a Z₂ symmetry that forbids 2→3 and 3→2 transitions, conjecturing a block-matrix form that couples two- and three-particle on-shell finite-volume phase space. They then test the original formalism by calculating the leading finite-volume energy shift of an Efimov-like three-particle bound state in the unitary limit, using the known Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes derived from the non-relativistic wavefunction via the work of Faddeev and collaborators, and comparing the result to the non-relativistic quantum mechanics calculation of Meißner, Ríos, and Rusetsky (2015).

## Key numbers

* The leading finite-volume energy shift for a three-particle Efimov bound state at rest is ΔE₃(L) = c (κ²/m) (1/(κL)³/²) exp(−2κL/√3) [1 + O(κ/m, 1/(κL))], where κ is defined by the binding energy E_B = 3m − κ²/m and c is a known constant depending on the wavefunction.
* The corresponding two-particle bound state shift is ΔE₂(L) = −12 (κ²/m) (1/(κL)) e^{−κL} [1 + O(κ/m, 1/(κL))], from Beane et al. (2004).
* For a moving frame with total momentum P = 2π n_P/L, the three-particle shift becomes ΔE_{3,P}(L) = f₃[n_P] ΔE₃(L) with f₃[n_P] = (1/6) Σ_ŝ exp(i 2π ŝ·n_P/3), and f₃[(1,1,0)] = 0, so the leading volume dependence vanishes for that momentum.
* The two-particle moving-frame prefactor is f₂[n_P] = (1/6) Σ_ŝ exp(i 2π ŝ·n_P/2), from Davoudi and Savage (2011).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic three-particle quantization condition developed by Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015) to include 2→3 and 3→2 transitions, removing a major limitation of the original formalism that assumed a Z₂ symmetry (applicable to three pions via G-parity but not to most three-particle systems). The successful reproduction of the Efimov bound-state energy shift from non-relativistic quantum mechanics (Meißner, Ríos, and Rusetsky 2015) provides a nontrivial check of the formalism, complementing earlier threshold-energy checks by Hansen and Sharpe (2016). The extension to moving frames enables cancellation of leading exponential volume dependence by combining results from different momenta, analogous to the two-particle case (Davoudi and Savage 2011).
