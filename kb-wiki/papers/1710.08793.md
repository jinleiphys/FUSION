# Magnetic field in expanding quark-gluon plasma

> Intense electromagnetic fields are created in the quark-gluon plasma by the external ultra-relativistic valence charges. The time-evolution and the strength of this field are strongly affected by the electrical conductivity of the plasma. Yet, it has recently been observed that the effect of the magnetic field on the plasma flow is small. We compute the effect of plasma flow on magnetic field and demonstrate that it is less than 10\%. These observations indicate that the plasma hydrodynamics and the dynamics of electromagnetic field decouple. Thus, it is a very good approximation, on the one hand, to study QGP in the background electromagnetic field generated by external sources and, on the other hand, to investigate the dynamics of magnetic field in the background plasma. We also argue that the wake induced by the magnetic field in plasma is negligible.

## Key claim

The effect of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) expansion on the magnetic field created by external valence charges is less than 10%, indicating that plasma hydrodynamics and electromagnetic field dynamics effectively decouple; this conclusion holds under the assumption that the plasma flow is not affected by the magnetic field and is described by the phenomenological blast-wave model.

## Method

The authors solve Maxwell's equations in a conducting, expanding medium, treating the plasma flow velocity as a perturbation on the stationary-plasma solution. They derive the retarded Green's function for the electromagnetic field in a conducting medium, separating it into a pulse term and a wake term, and compute the vector potential and magnetic field for a point charge moving through the plasma. The total field from a nucleus is obtained by integrating over the nuclear charge distribution, using the Bjorken model for longitudinal expansion and a blast-wave model for transverse flow, with parameters fitted to experimental data (R_o = 7.5 fm, u_o = 0.55 from Teaney (2003)).

## Key numbers

- The relative contribution of plasma expansion to the magnetic field is less than 10% (right panel of Fig. Numerics).
- The electrical conductivity of the plasma is taken as σ = 5.8 MeV, based on Amato (2013) and Ding (2010).
- The wake term in the Green's function is negligible until t ~ 100 fm/c for γ = 100 and σ = 5.8 MeV.
- The initial time for plasma emergence is chosen as τ = 0.2 fm/c, following Kolb (2000) and Teaney (2000).
- The boost factor for the heavy-ion remnant is γ = 100, corresponding to √s = 0.2 TeV.
- The impact parameter is |s| = 3 fm.
- The nuclear radius parameter is R_o = 7.5 fm and the transverse flow velocity parameter is u_o = 0.55, from Teaney (2003).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of electromagnetic fields in QGP that assumed stationary plasma (Tuchin 2013, Tuchin 2015) by computing the perturbative effect of plasma expansion. It supports the conclusion of Roy (2017) that the effect of electromagnetic fields on QGP flow is small, and justifies the decoupling approximation used in earlier studies (Kharzeev 2007, Skokov 2009, Bzdak 2011). The results enable simplified magneto-hydrodynamic treatments of QGP where the magnetic field can be computed in a background plasma without solving the full coupled MHD equations, and pave the way for comprehensive calculations with quantum sources (Holliday 2016, Peroutka 2017).
