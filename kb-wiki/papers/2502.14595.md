# Neutron versus proton scattering on exotic nuclei: the $^9$He example

> Neutron scattering on exotic nuclides is a class of processes which can not be studied directly now and in any observable future. Resonance proton scattering of exotic nuclide on a thick target in inverse kinematics can be used to infer the properties of the low-energy neutron scattering of this nuclide assuming the isobaric symmetry. However, the results of such resonance proton scattering reactions are so far analyzed in theoretical approaches (optical, R-matrix models), which are missing important aspects of isospin dynamics, isospin violation in continuum and threshold dynamics. The isospin conserving coupled-channel model (ICM) is proposed, which provides a more reliable basis for understanding of such experimental studies. Qualitatively different phase shifts for the $^{8}$He+$p$ $T=5/2$ and $T=3/2$ resonances are predicted by ICM with quite unusual profile for the $T=5/2$ states. Alternative interpretation of the existing $^{8}$He+$p$ data is proposed. The observable properties of the $T=5/2$ resonances may be strongly affected by the isobaric-partner $T=3/2$ states. Crucial importance of studies of the neutron-emission channel for disentangling this possible influence is demonstrated.

## Key claim

The isospin-conserving coupled-channel model (ICM) predicts that the standard resonant phase-shift behavior (steep rise passing π/2) is characteristic of the T=3/2 states in the ⁸He+p system, not the T=5/2 states; this implies that previously identified T=5/2 resonances in ⁸He+p data may be misidentified, and the interpretation of such data is unreliable without simultaneous measurement of the neutron-emission channel.

## Method

The authors developed an isospin-conserving coupled-channel model (ICM) for the ⁸He+p and ⁸Li*(0⁺2)+n channels, using Gaussian-formfactor nuclear potentials with definite total isospin (T=3/2 and T=5/2) and a Coulomb potential for a homogeneously charged sphere. The coupled Schrödinger equations were solved to obtain cross sections, phase shifts, and isospin content of continuum states, with the potential radius fine-tuned so that resonant states in the decoupled channels have the same energy near the ⁸He+p threshold. The model was then applied to re-analyze existing ⁸He+p experimental data from Uberseder (2016) and Rogachev (2003a).

## Key numbers

- The threshold shift between the ⁸He+p and ⁸Li*(0⁺2)+n channels is ΔE = 0.941 MeV.
- The Coulomb interaction uses a radius of 2.53 fm, consistent with the ⁸He charge radius of 1.956(16) fm (Mueller 2007, Krauth 2021).
- For states broader than Γ ≳ 0.3 MeV, any realistic energy gap between T=5/2 and T=3/2 states is insufficient to make their interference negligible.
- The ICM fit to the Uberseder (2016) data uses a 1/2⁻ resonance at Er = 2.3 MeV and a 5/2⁺ resonance at Er = 4.7 MeV in the T=5/2 channel.
- The scattering length in the 1/2⁺ channel is constrained to as ≳ 0 fm, consistent with the theoretical limitation as ≳ 1 fm from Sharov (2014).
- The 1/2⁻ resonance in ⁹He from Golovkov (2009) is at Er = 2.0 ± 0.2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the isospin-conserving coupled-channel approach previously applied to ⁴He and ⁸Be systems (Grigorenko 2000) to the case of ⁸He+p scattering, which aims to infer properties of ⁹He via isobaric symmetry. The paper directly contradicts the interpretations of Rogachev (2003a) and Uberseder (2016), who used optical and R-matrix models that neglect isospin mixing and threshold dynamics. The results enable future experiments that simultaneously measure the ⁸He+p elastic channel and the ⁸Li*(0⁺2)+n neutron-emission channel to reliably determine the T=5/2 resonance properties in ⁹He.
