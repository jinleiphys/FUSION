# Energy Correlators: A Journey From Theory to Experiment

> Collider experiments offer a unique opportunity to explore the Standard Model (SM), and to search for new physics, new interactions, and new principles of nature. The theoretical abstraction of a collider, namely the study of correlations in asymptotic fluxes, provides one of the most basic examples of an observable in quantum field theory (QFT) and quantum gravity. Energy flux is described in QFT by energy flow operators, a particular example of light-ray operators. In addition to their central role in the theoretical description of collider physics, energy flow operators play an important role in diverse areas of formal QFT and gravity, providing a connection between real world collider phenomenology, and the deep underlying principles of QFT. Recently it has become possible to measure correlation functions of energy flow operators in a wide variety of collider experiments, providing an exciting new connection between collider physics and formal theory. In this review, we provide a survey of recent progress in our understanding of energy operators and their correlators, highlighting their importance in both formal theory and collider phenomenology, and in particular, their great potential for bridging these areas to provide new ways to understand the real world. We intend this article as a resource for both formal theorists interested in understanding how light-ray operators are being applied in particle and nuclear physics, as well as for experimentalists interested in the theoretical motivation for these observables. Most importantly, we aim to stimulate further interaction between the formal, phenomenological and experimental communities through the common lens of energy correlators.

## Key claim

This review argues that energy correlators, correlation functions of energy flow operators, serve as a bridge between formal quantum field theory (QFT) and collider phenomenology, but the authors qualify that their goal is not to present detailed derivations but rather to emphasize the underlying physics and connections between different areas of study, aiming to stimulate further interaction between formal, phenomenological, and experimental communities.

## Method

The authors survey the 50-year history of energy flux operators, from Sterman's (1975) introduction of the energy flow operator acting on on-shell particle states, through the operator definition relating it to the stress tensor by Sveshnikov, Tkachov, and Korchemsky (1995-1999), to the modern revival by Hofman and Maldacena (2008) who generalized energy correlators as observables for studying generic QFTs. They review perturbative QCD calculations at leading order through NNLO, resummation techniques for collinear and Sudakov logarithms, and the use of the AdS/CFT correspondence for strong-coupling calculations in planar N=4 sYM.

## Key numbers

- The PLUTO experiment measured the two-point energy correlator at 13 GeV, with poor agreement between perturbative calculations and data due to the low energy of the collider
- OPAL data at 91.2 GeV shows convergence of perturbative calculations at LO, NLO, and NNLO towards the data, using the CoLoRFulNNLO subtraction scheme
- For an N=1 super-conformal field theory in d=4, the one-point energy correlator in a state produced by the R-current takes the form: ⟨E(θ)⟩ = 1 + 3(c-a)/c (cos²θ - 1/3)
- The ANEC combined with the three-point function yields the constraint: 3c/2 ≥ a ≥ 0
- At strong coupling in planar N=4 sYM, the n-point energy correlator is uniform: ⟨E(ñ₁)···E(ñₙ)⟩ = (Q/(4π))ⁿ + O(1/λ), where λ is the 't Hooft coupling

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on energy flow operators by Sterman (1975) and the multi-point energy correlators introduced by Basham, Brown, Ellis, and Love (1977-1979), systematized by Hofman and Maldacena (2008) who highlighted their role as observables for studying generic QFTs. It connects to the conformal bootstrap program (Rattazzi 2008), the a-theorem proven by Komargodski and Schwimmer (2011), and the ANEC proven using causality by Hartman, Kundu, and Tajdini (2016) and using relative entropy by Faulkner, Leigh, Parrikar, and Wang (2016). The review enables future non-perturbative determination of energy-energy correlators in theories such as the 3d Ising model, 3d supersymmetric ABJM models, and N=4 sYM using conformal bootstrap results.
