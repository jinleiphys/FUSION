# Shell model study of high-spin states and band terminations in $^{67}$As

> In the present work, recently available experimental data for different bands of $^{67}$As [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 98}, 024313 (2018)] have been interpreted within the framework of the shell model in full $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space using JUN45 and jj44b effective interactions. The variation of $E$ - $E_{rot}$ energy versus spin for different bands is shown to obtain useful information about band termination. We have also reported the electromagnetic transition probabilities, quadrupole and magnetic moments of $^{67}$As. Except for some tentative high-spin states, the results are in good agreement with the available experimental data.The states $29/2^+$-$45/2^+$ are described by three particles in $g_{9/2}$, while $47/2^+$ and $51/2^+$ states are described by five particles in $g_{9/2}$. The shell model results corresponding to nine tentative states such as $41/2_1^+$ and $45/2_1^+$ in band-1b; $41/2_2^+$ and $45/2_2^+$ in band-1a; $43/2_2^+$, $47/2_2^+$ and $51/2_1^+$ in band-2a; $43/2_1^+$ and $47/2_1^+$ in band-2b are reported and discussed.

## Key claim

The shell model, using JUN45 and jj44b effective interactions in the full $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space, reproduces the experimental band structures and band termination in $^{67}$As, except for some tentative high-spin states where the agreement is poorer; the JUN45 interaction shows band termination more reasonably for band-1a and band-2a compared to jj44b.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model calculations for $^{67}$As using the NuShellX, Antoine, and KSHELL codes, with $^{56}$Ni as an inert core and the model space comprising the $1p_{3/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{1/2}$, and $0g_{9/2}$ orbitals for both protons and neutrons. They used two effective interactions: JUN45, based on the Bonn-C potential and empirically modified for the A=63-69 mass region, and jj44b, fitted to 600 experimental binding and excitation energies for nuclei with Z=28-30 and N=48-50. Bands were identified by connecting states with strong E2 transition matrix elements and similar wave function configurations, and the variation of $E - E_{rot}$ energy versus spin was analyzed to study band termination.

## Key numbers

* The experimental energy difference between the $33/2^+$ and $29/2^+$ states in band-1a is 1604 keV; the shell model gives 875 keV (JUN45) and 1873 keV (jj44b).
* For band-1b, the JUN45 prediction for the $41/2^+$ state differs from experiment by only 12 keV, while the jj44b difference is 741 keV.
* The $B(E2;29/2^{+}_{2} \rightarrow 25/2^{+}_{2})$ transition in band-1a is 0.12 $e^2fm^4$ (JUN45), indicating a drastic change in $g_{9/2}$ occupancy.
* The $B(E2;51/2^{+}_{1} \rightarrow 47/2^{+}_{2})$ transition in band-2a is 0.02 $e^2fm^4$ (JUN45) and 5 $e^2fm^4$ (jj44b), reflecting a configuration change from two to three neutrons in $g_{9/2}$.
* The calculated quadrupole moment for the $9/2^+$ band head of band-1a is -0.642 eb (JUN45) and -0.667 eb (jj44b); the magnetic moment is +4.744 $\mu_N$ (JUN45) and +4.805 $\mu_N$ (jj44b).

## Context

This work extends the experimental study of $^{67}$As by Wadsworth et al. (2018), which reported two new band structures using the $^{40}$Ca($^{36}$Ar, 2$\alpha$p)$^{67}$As reaction and interpreted them with cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) calculations. The shell model results support the CNS conclusion that high-spin positive-parity states up to $45/2^+$ involve three $g_{9/2}$ particles, while $47/2^+$ and $51/2^+$ states require five $g_{9/2}$ particles. The calculations provide predicted electromagnetic transition probabilities, quadrupole moments, and magnetic moments for future experimental comparison, and they highlight that the JUN45 interaction better describes band termination in band-1a and band-2a than jj44b.
