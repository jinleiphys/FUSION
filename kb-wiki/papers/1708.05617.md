# Enhancement of elliptic flow can signal a first order phase transition in high energy heavy ion collisions

> The beam energy dependence of the elliptic flow,$v_2$, is studied in mid-central Au+Au collisions in the energy range of $3\leq \sqrt{s_{NN}} \leq 30$ GeV within the microscopic transport model JAM. The results of three different modes of JAM are compared; cascade-,hadronic mean field-, and a new mode with modified equations of state, with a first order phase transition (1.O.P.T.) and with a crossover transition. The standard hadronic mean field suppresses $v_2$, while the inclusion of the effects of a 1.O.P.T. (and also of a crossover transition) does enhance $v_2$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}<30$ GeV. The enhancement or suppression of the scaled energy flow, dubbed "elliptic flow"is understood as being due to out of plane- flow, i.e. $v_2<0$, dubbed out of plane - "squeeze-out", which occurs predominantly in the early, compression stage. Subsequently, the in-plane flow dominates, in the expansion stage, $v_2 > 0$. The directed flow, dubbed "bounce- off", is an independent measure of the pressure, which quickly builds up the transverse momentum transfer in the reaction plane. When the spectator matter leaves the participant fireball region, where the highest compression occurs, a hard expansion leads to larger $v_2$. A combined analysis of the three transverse flow coefficients, radial $v_0$-, directed $v_1$- and elliptic $v_2$- flow, in the beam energy range of $3\leq\sqrt{s_{NN}}\leq10$ GeV, distinguishes the different compression and expansion scenarios: a characteristic dependence on the early stage equation of state is observed. The enhancement of both the elliptic and the transverse radial flow and the simultaneous collapse of the directed flow of nucleons offers a clear signature if 1.O.P.T. is realized at the highest baryon densities created in high energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The enhancement of elliptic flow \(v_2\) at beam energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 30\) GeV, when a first-order phase transition or crossover transition is included in the JAM transport model, can serve as a signature for a first-order phase transition at the highest baryon densities, but only when combined with simultaneous enhancement of radial flow and collapse of directed flow.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic transport model JAM to simulate mid-central Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 3 to 30 GeV. They compared three modes: standard cascade, hadronic mean field with momentum-dependent potentials (RQMD/S approach), and a new mode where the equation of state is modified by controlling two-body scattering angles via the virial theorem to implement either a first-order phase transition (EOS-Q model) or a crossover transition (chiral model EoS). Elliptic flow was extracted using both the event-plane method and cumulant methods with pseudorapidity gaps.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range studied: \(3 \leq \sqrt{s_{NN}} \leq 30\) GeV for mid-central Au+Au collisions with impact parameter \(4.6 < b < 9.4\) fm
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.4\) GeV, the hadronic mean field mode reduces \(v_2\) values significantly compared to cascade, while the first-order phase transition and crossover modes yield larger \(v_2\) values
- The passage time of two Au nuclei is approximately 5 fm/c at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) GeV and 1.25 fm/c at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 20\) GeV
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.74\) GeV, including vector potentials instead of scalar potentials reduces \(v_2\) by about 20%, but this effect is small at higher energies
- The eccentricity of the system reaches a maximum at time \(t = 4\) fm/c at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.4\) GeV, corresponding to the passage time of spectators

## Context

This work extends earlier transport studies by Sorge (1996) that demonstrated the sensitivity of elliptic flow to the equation of state at maximum compression, and by Danielewicz (1998, 2002) who showed that flow data up to top AGS energy (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 5\) GeV) can be described by repulsive mean field interactions with nuclear incompressibility \(K = 220-380\) MeV. The results contrast with the PHSD model predictions of Konchakovski (2012) which attribute smooth growth of elliptic flow to partonic degrees of freedom, and with UrQMD hybrid model results of Karpenko (2015) requiring doubled shear viscosity at lower energies. The combined analysis of radial, directed, and elliptic flow proposed here enables distinguishing different compression and expansion scenarios in the beam energy range \(3 \leq \sqrt{s_{NN}} \leq 10\) GeV.
