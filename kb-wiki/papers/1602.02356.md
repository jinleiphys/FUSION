# Analytic Bethe-Salpeter Description of the Lightest Pseudoscalar Mesons

> Within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism for instantaneous interactions, we describe, along a totally analytic route, the lightest pseudoscalar mesons by quark-antiquark bound states which show at least three indispensable general features, namely, the (almost) masslessness required for pions and kaons to be interpretable as (pseudo) Goldstone bosons, the suitable asymptotic behaviour in the limit of large spacelike relative momenta as determined by the relationship between quark mass function and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and a pointwise behaviour for finite spacelike relative momenta suited for guaranteeing colour confinement.

## Key claim

Within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism for instantaneous interactions, the authors demonstrate that light pseudoscalar mesons can be described as quark-antiquark bound states that simultaneously exhibit (almost) masslessness for Goldstone boson interpretation, correct asymptotic behavior from quark mass functions, and pointwise behavior suitable for color confinement, but only when the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude incorporates an inflexion point that violates reflection positivity, with the resulting confining potential depending critically on a mixing parameter η and exhibiting singularities for η outside the interval [-1,0].

## Method

The authors start from the Salpeter equation (the instantaneous limit of the Bethe-Salpeter equation) for pseudoscalar mesons, using a Fierz-symmetric linear combination of scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector Dirac structures for the interaction kernel. They impose the Goldstone boson condition (zero bound-state mass) which decouples the radial equations, then use an inversion technique to extract configuration-space potentials V(r) from assumed forms of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. The amplitude ansatz is constructed to incorporate both the ultraviolet behavior dictated by the quark mass function from Dyson-Schwinger equations and the confinement requirement enforced by violating reflection positivity through an inflexion point in the quark propagator.

- For massless quarks (m=0) with η=-1, the potential at the origin is V(0) = -2μ
- For massive quarks (m=μ) with η=-1, the potential at the origin is V(0) = -3πm/4 = -m × 2.35619449...
- The zero r₀ of the Salpeter component φ(r) for η=0.5 is r₀ = 11.51002562 (in units of 1/μ)
- The zero r₀ for η=2 is r₀ = 5.51940196 (in units of 1/μ)
- The zero r₀ for η=-1.25 is r₀ = 0.41415074 (in units of 1/μ)
- The zero r₀ for η=-2 is r₀ = 1.55265125 (in units of 1/μ)
- The switching value of η where the potential flips behavior is η̃ ≈ -1.520216412

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous inversion technique (Lucha and Schöberl, 2015) which related Salpeter solutions to configuration-space potentials, by now incorporating confinement constraints from axiomatic quantum field theory. The approach builds on the relationship between the Bethe-Salpeter equation and Dyson-Schwinger equations established by Maris and Roberts (1997), where the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity connects the quark propagator to pseudoscalar meson amplitudes. The confinement mechanism is implemented through violation of the Osterwalder-Schrader reflection positivity axiom, following criteria formulated by Chaichian, Deser, and Rothe (1992, 2008). This enables analytic derivation of confining potentials for light pseudoscalar mesons that satisfy both ultraviolet asymptotic freedom constraints and infrared confinement requirements.
