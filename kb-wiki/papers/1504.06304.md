# Reply to Comment on "New limits on intrinsic charm in the nucleon from global analysis of parton distributions"

> We reply to the Comment of Brodsky and Gardner on our paper "New limits on intrinsic charm in the nucleon from global analysis of parton distributions" [Phys. Rev. Lett. 114, 082002 (2015)]. We address a number of incorrect claims made about our fitting methodology, and elaborate how global QCD analysis of all available high-energy data provides no evidence for a large intrinsic charm component of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The authors argue that Brodsky and Gardner's criticism of their global QCD analysis is based on a misunderstanding of error estimation in chi-squared fits, and that the data provide no evidence for a large intrinsic charm component in the nucleon, with the momentum fraction carried by charm quarks at most 0.5% at the 4-sigma confidence level.

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD analysis of all available high-energy scattering data, including fixed-target experiments at high x and low Q^2, using standard chi-squared minimization with Delta-chi-squared = 1 for one-dimensional parameter errors. They included target mass corrections, phenomenological higher twists determined consistently within the fit, and nuclear corrections, and propagated all statistical and systematic errors including normalization for all data sets.

## Key numbers

- The momentum fraction carried by charm quarks (⟨x⟩_IC) is at most 0.5% at the 4-sigma confidence level from the global analysis
- For the EMC charm structure function data, the overall chi-squared per data point is ≥ 4, indicating strong tension with other measurements
- With partonic threshold factors alone, ⟨x⟩_IC would be < 0.1% at the 5-sigma confidence level

## Context

This reply addresses the Comment by Brodsky and Gardner on the authors' original paper (Jimenez-Delgado et al., 2015) which placed strong constraints on intrinsic charm in the nucleon. The work extends previous global PDF analyses by groups such as MSTW, CJ, CT, JR, HERAPDF, and ABM, and contradicts claims that the EMC charm structure function data provide evidence for large intrinsic charm. The analysis enables future studies of nucleon structure by demonstrating that modern QCD analyses consistently find no evidence for a sizable intrinsic charm component, and that the EMC data are incompatible with other global data sets within a QCD framework.
