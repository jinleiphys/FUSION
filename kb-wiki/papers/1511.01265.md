# Neutron $2p$ and $1f$ spin--orbit splittings in $^{40}$Ca, $^{36}$S, and $^{34}$Si $N=20$ isotones: tensor--induced and pure spin--orbit effects

> Neutron $2p$ and $1f$ spin--orbit splittings were recently measured in the isotones $^{37}$S and $^{35}$Si by $(d,p)$ transfer reactions. Values were reported by using the major fragments of the states. An important reduction of the $p$ splitting was observed, from $^{37}$S to $^{35}$Si, associated to a strong modification of the spin--orbit potential in the central region of the nucleus $^{35}$Si. We analyze $2p$ and $1f$ neutron spin--orbit splittings in the $N=20$ isotones $^{40}$Ca, $^{36}$S, and $^{34}$Si. We employ several Skyrme and Gogny interactions, to reliably isolate pure spin--orbit and tensor--induced contributions, within the mean--field approximation. We use interactions (i) without the tensor force; (ii) with the tensor force and with tensor parameters adjusted on top of existing parametrizations; (iii) with the tensor force and with tensor and spin--orbit parameters adjusted simultaneously on top of existing parametrizations. We predict in cases (ii) and (iii) a non negligible reduction of both $p$ and $f$ splittings, associated to neutron--proton tensor effects, from $^{40}$Ca to $^{36}$S. The two splittings are further decreased for the three types of interactions, going from $^{36}$S to $^{34}$Si. This reduction is produced by the spin--orbit force and is not affected by tensor--induced contributions. For both reductions, from $^{40}$Ca to $^{36}$S and from $^{36}$S to $^{34}$Si, we predict in all cases that the modification is more pronounced for $p$ than for $f$ splittings. The measurement of the centroids for neutron $2p$ and $1f$ states in the nuclei $^{36}$S and $^{34}$Si would be interesting to validate this prediction experimentally. We show the importance of using interactions of type (iii), because they provide $p$ and $f$ splittings in the nucleus $^{40}$Ca which are in agreement with the corresponding experimental values.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the reduction of neutron $2p$ and $1f$ spin-orbit splittings along the $N=20$ isotone chain from $^{40}$Ca to $^{34}$Si arises from two distinct mechanisms: neutron-proton tensor effects dominate the reduction from $^{40}$Ca to $^{36}$S, while pure spin-orbit effects (related to central charge-density depletion) dominate the reduction from $^{36}$S to $^{34}$Si, with the $p$ splitting always reduced more strongly than the $f$ splitting.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) mean-field calculations using three classes of Skyrme and Gogny effective interactions: (i) original forces without tensor terms (SLy5, D1S); (ii) forces with tensor parameters added on top of existing parametrizations without refitting the spin-orbit parameter (SLy5$_{T-2007}$, D1ST$_{2a-2012}$); and (iii) forces where tensor and spin-orbit parameters were adjusted simultaneously (SLy5$_{T-2013}$, D1ST$_{2c-2013}$). For unbound single-particle states, energies were estimated using box discretization with box radii of 20, 40, and 60 fm, combining criteria based on the weak dependence of resonant-state energies on box radius and the similarity of their radial wave-function profiles to bound states.

## Key numbers

- Experimental neutron $2p$ spin-orbit splitting in $^{37}$S: 2 MeV; in $^{35}$Si: 1.1 MeV (using major fragments), representing a 45% reduction (Bur 2014)
- Reduction of $p$ splitting from $^{36}$S to $^{34}$Si (pure spin-orbit effect): 40% with SLy5, 43% with D1S, 40% with SLy5$_{T-2007}$, 39% with D1ST$_{2a-2012}$, 43% with SLy5$_{T-2013}$, 42% with D1ST$_{2c-2013}$
- Reduction of $f$ splitting from $^{36}$S to $^{34}$Si (pure spin-orbit effect): 26% with SLy5, 20% with D1S, 27% with SLy5$_{T-2007}$, 18% with D1ST$_{2a-2012}$, 20% with SLy5$_{T-2013}$, 16% with D1ST$_{2c-2013}$
- Reduction of $p$ splitting from $^{40}$Ca to $^{36}$S (tensor-induced): 28% with SLy5$_{T-2007}$, 40% with D1ST$_{2a-2012}$, 39% with SLy5$_{T-2013}$, 27% with D1ST$_{2c-2013}$
- Reduction of $f$ splitting from $^{40}$Ca to $^{36}$S (tensor-induced): 14% with SLy5$_{T-2007}$, 28% with D1ST$_{2a-2012}$, 18% with SLy5$_{T-2013}$, 18% with D1ST$_{2c-2013}$
- Overlap reduction between spin-orbit potential and wave functions from $^{40}$Ca to $^{36}$S with SLy5$_{T-2013}$: 22% for $p$ states, 13% for $f$ states

## Context

This work extends experimental measurements by Bur 2014, who reported neutron $2p$ and $1f$ spin-orbit splittings in $^{37}$S and $^{35}$Si using $(d,p)$ transfer reactions, finding a 45% reduction of the $p$ splitting attributed to central charge-density depletion in $^{34}$Si predicted by Grasso 2009. The authors build on their own previous parametrizations of tensor forces for Skyrme and Gogny interactions (Anguiano 2012, Grasso 2013), which used a three-step fitting procedure on $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca, and $^{56}$Ni to determine spin-orbit and tensor parameters. The results enable experimental validation through measurement of centroids for neutron $2p$ and $1f$ states in $^{36}$S and $^{34}$Si, which would test the prediction that $p$ splittings are reduced more strongly than $f$ splittings in both the tensor-induced (from $^{40}$Ca to $^{36}$S) and pure spin-orbit (from $^{36}$S to $^{34}$Si) regimes.
