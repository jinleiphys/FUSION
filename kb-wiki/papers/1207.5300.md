# Dressed-quarks and the nucleon's axial charge

> The nucleon's axial charge, g_A, expresses features that are both fundamental to the strong interaction and crucial to its connection with weak interaction physics. We show that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) suppresses the axial-charge of a dressed-quark, g_A^q, at infrared momenta. Since this effect disappears as chiral symmetry is restored, one may argue that g_A vanishes with the restoration of chiral symmetry because no nucleon bound-state survives the associated transition. The suppression of g_A^q is shown to be part of an explanation for a 25% reduction of g_A from its nonrelativistic quark-model value. Critical too, however, is the presence of dressed-quark angular momentum within the nucleon. The value of g_A^q depends on the kernels of the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations. We find that incorporation of essentially nonperturbative effects associated with DCSB into these kernels inflates the value relative to that obtained at leading-order in a widely used truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations. Such corrections also affect the nucleon's axial radius. In both cases, however, agreement with experiment will require similar improvements to the Faddeev kernel and associated interaction current.

## Key claim

Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) suppresses the axial-charge of a dressed-quark, \(g_A^q\), at infrared momenta, and this suppression is part of an explanation for a 25% reduction of the nucleon's axial charge \(g_A\) from its nonrelativistic quark-model value; however, agreement with experiment also requires the presence of dressed-quark angular momentum within the nucleon and similar improvements to the Faddeev kernel and associated interaction current.

## Method

The authors solved QCD's gap equation for the dressed-quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion, using both a symmetry-preserving vector\(\times\)vector contact interaction and a sophisticated rainbow-ladder interaction (Qin:2011dd), as well as DCSB-improved kernels (denoted DB) that incorporate essentially nonperturbative effects such as a dressed-quark anomalous chromomagnetic moment (Chang:2011ei). They computed the scalar functions in the axial-vector vertex, particularly \(F_R(k;P=0) \equiv g_A^q(k^2)\), and evaluated the ratio of the axial-charge distribution for on-Euclidean-mass-shell dressed-quarks between the DB and rainbow-ladder truncations.

## Key numbers

*   For the contact interaction with DCSB (\(\alpha_{\rm IR}/\pi \simeq 1\)), \(g_{A_{\rm CN}}^q = 0.74\) (Eq. \eqref{gAcontact}), compared to \(g_{A_{\rm CN}}^q = 1\) when DCSB is absent.
*   For the rainbow-ladder interaction (RL), \(g_{A_{RL}}^q(k^2=0) = 0.81\) at a realistic light-quark current-mass.
*   For the DCSB-improved kernels (DB), \(g_{A_{DB}}^q(0) = 0.87 = 1.06\,g_{A_{RL}}^q(0)\) (Eq. \eqref{AMMonRL}).
*   The ratio \(|g_A^{E_q DB}(k^2)|/|g_A^{E_q RL}(k^2)|\) is \(1.064 \pm 0.003\).
*   The empirical value of the nucleon's axial charge is \(g_A = 1.27\); a rainbow-ladder Faddeev calculation (Eichmann:2011pv) yields \(g_{A_{RL}} = 0.99(2)\), underestimating the empirical value by 22%.

## Context

This work extends studies of the nucleon's axial charge using Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) and the Faddeev equation, particularly the rainbow-ladder truncation study by Eichmann (2011) which produced \(g_A = 0.99(2)\). It shows that the suppression of \(g_A^q\) is a generic consequence of DCSB, contrasting with the enhancement of the vector vertex scalar function. The results enable improved Faddeev equation calculations that incorporate DCSB-improved kernels, which are necessary to achieve agreement with the experimental value of \(g_A\) and to understand the connection between DCSB, quark orbital angular momentum, and parton distribution functions.
