# Two-body nucleon-nucleon correlations in Glauber models of relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the influence of the central two-body NN correlations on several quantities observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It is demonstrated with explicit Monte Carlo simulations, that the basic correlation measures, such as the fluctuations of participant eccentricity, initial size fluctuations, or the fluctuations of the number of sources producing particles, are all sensitive to the inclusion of the two-body correlations. The effect is at the level of about 10-20%. Moreover, the realistic (Gaussian) correlation function gives indistinguishable results from the hard-core repulsion, with the expulsion distance set to 0.9 fm. Thus, for investigations of the considered correlation measures, it is sufficient to use the Monte Carlo generators accounting for the hard-core repulsion.

# Two-body nucleon-nucleon correlations in Glauber models of relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The inclusion of central two-body nucleon-nucleon correlations in Glauber Monte Carlo simulations affects fluctuation measures (participant eccentricity fluctuations, multiplicity fluctuations, and initial size fluctuations) at the level of about 10-20%, but the realistic Gaussian correlation function yields results indistinguishable from the simpler hard-core repulsion model when the expulsion distance is set to 0.9 fm. This means that for practical modeling of the Glauber initial phase, Monte Carlo generators using hard-core repulsion are sufficient, provided the expulsion distance is tuned to this specific value.

## Method

The authors used the GLISSANDO Monte Carlo code to simulate Pb-Pb collisions at SPS energies (with the mixed wounded-nucleon/binary-collision model using alpha = 0.12), comparing three nucleon distribution schemes: uncorrelated Woods-Saxon distributions, distributions with realistic Gaussian central two-body correlations from the Alvioli-Drescher-Strikman method (2009), and distributions with hard-core repulsion at expulsion distance d = 0.9 fm. The one-body Woods-Saxon parameters were fitted to the correlated distributions (R = 6.59 fm, a = 0.549 fm) and the bare parameters for the hard-core case were adjusted (R = 6.44 fm, a = 0.549 fm) so that all calculations produced identical one-body distributions, isolating the effect of two-body correlations.

## Key numbers

- The Gaussian correlation function parameter b = 0.561(1) fm, extracted from fitting the central two-body NN radial correlation density for Pb-208.
- The Woods-Saxon fit parameters for Pb-208: R = 6.59(1) fm, a = 0.549(2) fm.
- The hard-core expulsion distance that reproduces the realistic correlation results: d = 0.9 fm.
- The effect of including two-body correlations on fluctuation measures is at the level of about 10-20%.
- For the participant eccentricity scaled standard deviation, the uncorrelated case shows up to 10% larger fluctuations at intermediate centralities compared to the correlated cases.
- For multiplicity fluctuations, the scaled variance omega is reduced by about 20% when two-body NN correlations are included.
- The mixed model parameters: alpha = 0.12 for collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV (highest SPS energy), alpha = 0.145 for sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, and extrapolated alpha = 0.2 for LHC energies.

## Context

This work extends the method of Alvioli, Drescher, and Strikman (2009), who generated nucleon distributions in nuclei accounting for central two-body NN correlations via a Metropolis search procedure, by testing those distributions in actual Glauber Monte Carlo simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The paper addresses a practical question for the Glauber Monte Carlo community (codes such as those by Wang, Werner, Broniowski, and Alver): whether the commonly used hard-core expulsion approximation is adequate compared to realistic Gaussian correlations. The results enable practitioners to continue using hard-core repulsion with confidence at the tuned expulsion distance, while also opening the door for future work on channel-dependent NN correlations and nuclear attraction effects from the more general Alvioli-Drescher-Strikman method.
