# Ab initio no-core full configuration calculations of light nuclei

> We perform no-core full configuration calculations for a set of light nuclei including $^{16}$O with a realistic NN interaction, JISP16. We obtain ground state energies and their uncertainties through exponential extrapolations that we demonstrate are reliable in $^2$H, $^3$H and $^4$He test cases where fully converged results are obtained directly. We find that $^6$He, $^6$Li and $^8$He are underbound by about 600 keV, 560 keV and 1.7 MeV, respectively. $^{12}$C is overbound by about 1.7 MeV and $^{16}$O is overbound by about 16 MeV. The first excited $0^+$ states in $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O are also evaluated but their uncertainties are significantly larger than the uncertainties for the ground states.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that no-core full configuration (NCFC) calculations using the JISP16 realistic NN interaction, combined with exponential extrapolations in the harmonic oscillator basis space, can produce ground state energies for light nuclei up to 16O with quantified uncertainties, but the results systematically deviate from experiment, underbinding 6He, 6Li, and 8He while overbinding 12C and 16O, indicating that three-nucleon (NNN) and higher-body interactions are likely needed for exact agreement.

## Method

The authors perform no-core full configuration (NCFC) calculations by diagonalizing the many-body Hamiltonian in a harmonic oscillator (HO) basis with a cutoff Nmax (maximum total oscillator quanta above the minimal configuration) and the oscillator energy ħΩ as parameters, using the JISP16 bare NN interaction with Coulomb. For nuclei where direct convergence is not achieved (A ≥ 6), they extrapolate ground state energies to the infinite basis limit using an exponential-plus-constant fit E(Nmax) = a exp(-c Nmax) + E(∞), employing two methods: a global extrapolation (method A) using four successive Nmax values with a common asymptote constraint and weighted chi-square minimization, and a fixed-ħΩ extrapolation (method B) using three successive Nmax values.

## Key numbers

* 2H ground state energy: Nmax = 20 curve reaches within 5 keV of the exact result; Nmax = 18 curve reaches within 9 keV
* 4He ground state energy with JISP16: converges directly without extrapolation, with experimental value at -28.296 MeV
* 6He underbound by about 600 keV relative to experiment
* 6Li underbound by about 560 keV relative to experiment
* 8He underbound by about 1.7 MeV relative to experiment
* 12C overbound by about 1.7 MeV relative to experiment
* 16O overbound by about 16 MeV relative to experiment
* Largest matrix diagonalized: 16O in Nmax = 8 space with basis dimension about 1 billion, solved in 4.5 hours on 12,090 processors
* 12C matrix dimension about 600 million for Nmax = 8, solved in 2.3 hours on 12,720 processors
* ESPM test for 12C: at optimal ħΩ = 7 MeV, Nmax = 8 yields total energy within 170 keV (0.25%) of exact result

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell model (NCSM) approach of Navratil et al. (2007) by removing the effective interaction renormalization and instead using the bare JISP16 interaction with systematic exponential extrapolation to infinite basis spaces, building on earlier extrapolation strategies by Zhan (2003) and Forssen (2008). The results enable benchmarking of realistic NN interactions against experiment across a range of light nuclei and establish a foundation for incorporating three-nucleon forces to resolve the systematic underbinding and overbinding discrepancies, as well as for extending NCFC calculations to nuclear reactions with improved predictive power.
