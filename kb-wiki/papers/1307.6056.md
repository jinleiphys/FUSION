# Review on the inclusion of isospin breaking effects in lattice calculations

> Isospin symmetry is explicitly broken in the Standard Model by the non-zero differences of mass and electric charge between the up and down quarks. Both of these corrections are expected to have a comparable size of the order of one percent relatively to hadronic energies. Although these contributions are small, they play a crucial role in hadronic and nuclear physics. In this review we explain how to properly define QCD and QED on a finite and discrete space-time so that isospin corrections to hadronic observables can be computed ab-initio. We then consider the different approaches to compute lattice correlation functions of QCD and QED observables. Finally we summarise the actual lattice results concerning the isospin corrections to the light hadron spectrum.

# Review on the inclusion of isospin breaking effects in lattice calculations

## Key claim
Isospin breaking corrections from QED and the up-down quark mass difference are of comparable size (about 1% relative to hadronic energies), and lattice QCD+QED simulations are now reaching the precision where these effects must be included to control the dominant systematic uncertainty; however, all current lattice results for Dashen's theorem corrections use the electro-quenched approximation, which introduces an uncontrolled O(10%) uncertainty on QED mass splittings.

## Method
The paper reviews two approaches to include QED in lattice QCD simulations: (1) a stochastic method where random EM fields are drawn from a Gaussian distribution (since pure-gauge QED is a free theory) and combined with QCD gauge configurations, typically in the electro-quenched approximation that neglects QED contributions to the fermion determinant; and (2) a perturbative expansion in the fine-structure constant α, where O(α) EM corrections to hadronic correlation functions are computed from pure-QCD four-point functions with two electromagnetic current insertions. Both methods require an infrared regularization of finite-volume QED, achieved by removing the zero mode of the photon field from the path integral while preserving gauge invariance.

## Key numbers
- The fine-structure constant α ≈ 0.007 gives the typical relative size of electromagnetic isospin breaking.
- The experimental nucleon mass splitting is ΔM_N = M_p − M_n = −1.2933322(4) MeV.
- The up quark mass is 2.3(+0.7−0.5) MeV and the down quark mass is 4.8(+0.7−0.3) MeV (PDG 2012).
- Leading-order finite-volume QED effects are O(L⁻¹) where L is the spatial extent, requiring volumes above approximately (6 fm)³ to control the infinite-volume limit.
- The electro-quenched approximation is estimated to be correct up to O(10%) corrections, based on SU(3) breaking effects of O(30%) divided by the number of colours N_c = 3.
- The FLAG 2011 review quoted ε = 0.7(5) for Dashen's theorem violations; post-2012 lattice results (especially deDivitiis 2013 and BMWc 2013) significantly reduce this uncertainty.
- Lattice results now exclude m_u = 0 with a significance of about 10 standard deviations.

## Context
This review extends the FLAG review (2011) on lattice determinations of quark masses and the earlier phenomenological calculations of Dashen's theorem violations from the 1990s (e.g., Bijnens, Donoghue, and others cited in the paper). It builds on the pioneering lattice work of Duncan et al. (1996, 1997) and the subsequent calculations by the RBC-UKQCD, MILC, BMWc, PACS-CS, and RM123 collaborations. The review enables future fully-unquenched QCD+QED calculations that will provide ab-initio predictions for the nucleon mass splitting (and thus nuclear matter stability), individual up and down quark masses, and isospin corrections to decay constants and hadronic matrix elements.
