# Isospin mixing and energy distributions in three-body decay

> The structure of the second 2$^+$ resonance in $^{6}$Li is investigated with special emphasis on its isospin 0 components. The wave functions are computed in a three-body model ($α$+$n$+$p$) using the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method combined with complex scaling. In the decay into three free particles the symmetry conserving short-range interaction dominates at short distance whereas the symmetry breaking Coulomb interaction dominates at intermediate and large distances resulting in substantial isospin mixing. We predict the mixing and the energy distributions of the fragments after decay. Computations are consistent with available experiments. We conjecture that nuclear three-body decays frequently produce such large isospin mixing at large distance where the energy distributions. are determined.

## Key claim
The isospin mixing in the second 2+ resonance of 6Li is predicted to be substantial at intermediate and large distances (up to 40% relative weight of T=0 components between 20 and 50 fm), even though the integrated isospin 0 contribution over all distances is only about 4% and the deuteron channel is populated by only about 10^-3% of the norm. This large mixing at large distance is a dynamic effect of the Coulomb interaction during decay, not a property of the resonance at small distances.

## Method
The wave functions of the 2+ resonance in 6Li were computed in a three-body alpha+n+p model using the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method combined with complex scaling. The calculation included components with relative two-body orbital angular momenta up to 4, with an extraordinarily large basis (Kmax up to 240) for the isospin-zero components. The resonance wave function was obtained by imposing a box boundary condition at rho_max=1000 fm, and the asymptotic behavior was matched to Coulomb functions beyond 60 fm.

## Key numbers
- The computed 2+ resonance in 6Li has energy and width (E_R, Gamma_R) = (1.67, 0.51) MeV, corresponding to 5.37 MeV excitation energy, agreeing with the experimental value of (1.67 +/- 0.02, 0.54 +/- 0.02) MeV
- Roughly 82% of the integrated weight comes from the first three components in the first Jacobi set: 38%, 20%, and 24% respectively
- The isospin-zero components (components 6-11) accumulate about 4.5% of the integrated weight, most from component 9
- The relative weight of T=0 components reaches up to 40% of the total between 20 and 50 fm, stabilizing at about 10% beyond 50 fm
- The contribution from the lowest adiabatic potential (holding deuteron at large distances) provides about 10^-3% of the norm

## Context
This work extends earlier investigations of isospin mixing in 6Li by Deb (1971) and Witten (1975), who found an upper limit of 8x10^-3 admixture from alpha-deuteron decay branching ratios, and by Werby (1981) and Gai (1988), who claimed substantial isospin mixing in reaction experiments. The authors show that the small deuteron branching ratio is not inconsistent with large isospin mixing at large distances, contradicting the suggestion by Werby (1981) that the mixing arises from direct reactions rather than resonance decay. The results enable interpretation of three-body decay energy distributions as sensitive probes of isospin mixing at large distances, and the authors conjecture that such dynamic isospin mixing is a common feature in decays of nuclear three-body resonances.
