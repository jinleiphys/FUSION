# A Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis Limit on the Neutral Fermion Decays into Neutrinos

> Using the primordial helium abundance, an upper limit to the magnetic moments for Dirac neutrinos had been provided by imposing restrictions on the number of the additional helicity states. Considering non-thermal photons produced in the decay of the heavy sterile mass eigenstates due to the neutrino magnetic moment, we explore the constraints imposed by the observed abundances of all the light elements produced during the Big Bang nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The authors show that observed light-element abundances from Big Bang nucleosynthesis can constrain sterile neutrino masses and their contributions to the neutrino magnetic moment, but they find that allowing a heavy sterile neutrino decay into active ones does not resolve the persistent discrepancy between predicted and observed primordial lithium-7 abundances unless one relaxes the requirement of using the cosmic microwave background value of the baryon-to-photon ratio or the relic neutrino abundance deduced from thermal freezeout.

## Method

The authors used Kawano's public BBN code with updated reaction rates from the JINA REACLIB Database V1.0 and adopted the method of Kusakabe (2006) to calculate nonthermal nucleosynthesis, including thermonuclear reactions simultaneously. They modeled the radiative decay of a heavy sterile neutrino mass eigenstate into a lighter state via a magnetic or electric dipole moment, producing nonthermal photons that induce electromagnetic cascade showers and potentially photodisintegrate background light elements. The relic abundance of the sterile neutrino was determined from its decoupling temperature, taken as the higher of 1 MeV or m_X/20, and the resulting nonthermal photon spectra were computed for cases where the emitted photon energy lies near or above photodisintegration thresholds.

## Key numbers

- The current direct laboratory limit on the neutrino magnetic moment is mu_nu < 2.9 x 10^{-11} mu_B at 90% C.L., where mu_B = e/2m_e is the Bohr magneton.
- The globular-cluster star limit gives mu_nu < 3 x 10^{-12} mu_B.
- The radiative decay rate of a sterile neutrino is tau_X^{-1} = 5.308 s^{-1} (mu_eff/mu_B)^2 ((m_i^2 - m_j^2)/m_i^2)^3 (m_i/eV)^3.
- The adopted observational constraint on primordial deuterium is 2.40 x 10^{-5} < D/H < 2.88 x 10^{-5} (2-sigma).
- The adopted constraint on helium-4 is 0.2345 < Y_p < 0.2777 (2-sigma).
- The adopted constraint on lithium-7 is 1.06 x 10^{-10} < Li-7/H < 2.35 x 10^{-10} (2-sigma).
- The adopted constraint on lithium-6 is Li-6/H < 1.3 x 10^{-11} (2-sigma).
- For a fixed lifetime tau_X = 10^{10} s, a constraint on the sterile neutrino mass m_nu < 4.4 MeV is derived from the deuterium abundance.
- A narrow-band allowed region exists at m_X ~ (3-5) MeV and |mu_eff| ~ (10^{-17} - 10^{-12}) mu_B when the CMB baryon-to-photon ratio requirement is relaxed.
- The photodisintegration threshold for beryllium-7 is 1.59 MeV and for deuterium is 2.22 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier BBN constraints on neutrino magnetic moments that used only primordial helium abundance (Morgan, 1981; Fukugita, 1987) by considering nonthermal photons from sterile neutrino decay and their effects on all light elements. It also builds on studies of nonthermal nucleosynthesis from decaying particles (Ellis, 1984; Kawasaki, 1994; Jedamzik, 2006; Kusakabe, 2006) and addresses the lithium-7 problem noted in standard BBN. The results enable future experimental searches for sterile neutrinos in the few-MeV mass range and provide a framework for constraining neutrino magnetic moments from cosmological data, complementing limits from globular-cluster stars (Raffelt, 1990) and supernova SN1987A (Lattimer, 1988).
