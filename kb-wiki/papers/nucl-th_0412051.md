# Jet Tomography of Quark Gluon Plasma

> Recent experimental measurements of high $p_T$ hadron spectra and jet correlation at RHIC are analyzed within a parton model which incoporates initial jet production and final propagation in heavy-ion collisions. The suppresion of single hadron spectra, back-to-back correlation, their centrality dependence and azimuthal anisotropy point to a dense matter with an initial parton density about 30 times of that in a cold heavy nucleus.

## Key claim

The observed suppression of high-$p_T$ hadron spectra and back-to-back correlations in Au+Au collisions at RHIC can be explained by parton energy loss in a dense medium, with the extracted average energy loss for a 10 GeV quark in an expanding medium being $\langle dE/dL\rangle_{1d}\approx 0.85 \pm 0.24$ GeV/fm, equivalent to $dE_0/dL\approx 13.8 \pm 3.9$ GeV/fm in a static uniform medium over 6.5 fm, though this value depends on the specific parton shadowing parameterization used (HIJING vs. EKS) and is about a factor of 2 larger than a previous estimate.

## Method

The paper uses a leading-order (LO) perturbative QCD parton model that incorporates initial jet production, nuclear parton distributions with shadowing, initial transverse momentum broadening (Cronin effect), and medium-modified fragmentation functions to calculate single hadron spectra and di-hadron correlations in heavy-ion collisions. The medium modification is modeled via an effective fragmentation function that depends on the parton energy loss, which is calculated using a parameterization derived from opacity expansion with detailed balance (gluon absorption) and a 1-dimensional expanding gluon density profile. The energy loss parameter $\epsilon_0$ is tuned to fit the observed $\pi^0$ suppression in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, and the same model is then applied to predict the centrality dependence, azimuthal anisotropy, and back-to-back correlations.

## Key numbers

- Extracted average parton energy loss for a 10 GeV quark in the expanding medium: $\langle dE/dL\rangle_{1d} \approx 0.85 \pm 0.24$ GeV/fm (with HIJING shadowing), equivalent to $dE_0/dL \approx 13.8 \pm 3.9$ GeV/fm in a static uniform medium over $R_A = 6.5$ fm.
- With EKS parton shadowing: $\langle dE/dL\rangle_{1d} \approx 0.99 \pm 0.24$ GeV/fm, equivalent to $dE_0/dL \approx 16.1 \pm 3.9$ GeV/fm.
- Quark energy loss inside a Au nucleus from DIS data: $dE/dL \approx 0.5$ GeV/fm, extracted from HERMES data with $\widetilde{C}(Q^2)=0.0060$ GeV$^2$ at $Q^2 \approx 3$ GeV$^2$ and $\langle x_B\rangle \approx 0.124$.
- Initial parton density in central Au+Au collisions estimated to be about 30 times that of a cold heavy nucleus.
- Parameters used to fit $\pi^0$ suppression: $\mu = 1.5$ GeV, $\epsilon_0 = 1.07$ GeV/fm, $\lambda_0 = 0.3$ fm.
- The energy loss parameterization: $\langle dE/dL\rangle_{1d} = \epsilon_0 (E/\mu - 1.6)^{1.2} / (7.5 + E/\mu)$ with $\mu = 1$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the jet quenching formalism developed by Wang (1996) and the opacity expansion approach of Gyulassy (2000) to a comprehensive phenomenological analysis of RHIC data from STAR and PHENIX collaborations. The model simultaneously describes single hadron suppression, back-to-back correlations, and azimuthal anisotropy, establishing that the observed jet quenching is due to parton energy loss rather than hadron absorption. The extracted energy loss values are about a factor of 2 larger than a previous estimate by Wang (2002), attributed to the variation of gluon density along the propagation path and more precise RHIC data. This enables future studies to constrain the initial gluon density and transport coefficient of the quark-gluon plasma produced at RHIC and the LHC.
