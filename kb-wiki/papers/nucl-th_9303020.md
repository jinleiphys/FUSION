# Exclusive Hadronic Reactions at High $Q^2$ (90°) and Polarization Phenomena, An Experimental Proposal

## Key claim

The large asymmetry observed in $\overline{p}p \rightarrow \pi^-\pi^+$ at low energies ($p_{lab} \lesssim$ 2.2 GeV/c) will not vanish at higher energies but will instead oscillate with energy at fixed 90° scattering angle when perturbative QCD becomes applicable, due to an energy-dependent QCD phase difference between Landshoff (independent quark-quark scattering) and short-distance processes.

## Method

The author analyzes the energy dependence of the two helicity amplitudes (helicity non-flip $f_{++}$ and helicity flip $f_{+-}$) for $\pi N \rightarrow \pi N$ scattering and the crossed-channel reaction $\overline{p}p \rightarrow \pi^-\pi^+$, which each have only two helicity amplitudes (unlike $pp$ elastic scattering which has five). The short-distance amplitude $f_{SD}$ is real and contributes only to $f_{+-}$ for the annihilation reaction, while the complex Landshoff amplitude $f_L$ contributes to both helicity amplitudes, with a phase derived from perturbative QCD calculations by Sen (1983) and Botts and Sterman (1989). The author argues that existing scaled cross section data for both reactions at 90° show potential oscillatory patterns that could be confirmed with additional measurements.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry $A_{0n}$ for $\overline{p}p \rightarrow \pi^-\pi^+$ is close to maximal ($\approx$ 1) for $p_{lab} \lesssim$ 2.2 GeV/c
- The QCD phase has the form $\Phi \sim (\pi/6) \ln \ln (Q^2/\Lambda^2)$ with $\Lambda \approx$ 100 MeV
- Ralston and Pire needed a constant $a \approx$ 50 (instead of $\pi/6$) in front of the double log to reproduce the observed period of energy oscillations in scaled $pp$ elastic 90° cross section
- The impact parameter $b$ has energy dependence $b\Lambda = (\sqrt{s}/\Lambda')^{-\tau}$ where $\tau \approx$ 0.7 for three quark flavors
- The short-distance amplitude scales as $f_{SD} \propto s^{-3}$ while the Landshoff amplitude scales as $f_L \propto s^{-2.85}$
- Data for $\overline{p}p \rightarrow \pi^-\pi^+$ exist up to $p_{lab}$ = 6.2 GeV/c
- Elastic $\pi N \rightarrow \pi N$ scattering at 90° has been measured up to $p_{lab}$ = 30 GeV/c (though the highest energy point has uncertainties too large to be useful)

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological analysis of Ralston and Pire (1982) and Carlson, Chachkhunashvili, and Myhrer (1992) who described energy oscillations in the scaled $pp$ elastic 90° cross section and the spin-correlation observable $A_{nn}$ using the interference between short-distance and Landshoff amplitudes. The author argues that the $\pi N \rightarrow \pi N$ and $\overline{p}p \rightarrow \pi^-\pi^+$ reactions are cleaner tests because they involve only two helicity amplitudes rather than five for $pp$ elastic scattering. The proposal suggests measurements at existing facilities (AGS at Brookhaven, Fermilab) or proposed facilities (SuperLEAR, KAON) to establish oscillatory patterns in scaled cross sections and asymmetries, which would confirm the perturbative QCD regime onset.
