# Transverse Momentum Balance and Angular Distribution of $b\bar{b}$ Dijets in Pb+Pb collisions

> The productions of inclusive b-jet and $b\bar{b}$ dijets in Pb+Pb collisions have been investigated by considering the heavy quark and the light quark in-medium evolution simultaneously. The initial hard processes of inclusive b-jet and $b\bar{b}$ dijets productions are described by a next-to-leading order (NLO) plus parton shower Monte Carlo (MC) event generator SHERPA which can be well-matched with the experimental data in p+p collisions. The framework combines the Langevin transport model to describe the evolution of the bottom quark also its collisional energy loss and the higher-twist description to consider the radiative energy loss of both the bottom and light quarks. We compare the theoretical simulation of inclusive jet and inclusive b-jet $R_{\rm AA}$ in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV with the experimental data, and then present the theoretical simulation of the momentum balance of the $b\bar{b}$ dijet in Pb+Pb collisions at $5.02$ TeV with the recent CMS data for the first time. A similar trend as that in inclusive dijets has been observed in $b\bar{b}$ dijets, the production distribution is shifted to smaller $x_J$ due to the jet quenching effect. At last, the prediction of the normalized azimuthal angle distribution of the $b\bar{b}$ dijet in Pb+Pb collisions at $5.02$ TeV has been reported. The medium induced energy loss effect of the $b\bar{b}$ dijets will overall suppress its production, but the same side ($Δϕ\to 0$ region) suffers more energy loss than away side ($Δϕ\to π$ region), therefore lead to the suppression on the same side and the enhancement on the away side in the normalized azimuthal angle distribution in A+A collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first theoretical calculation of the transverse momentum balance of \(b\bar{b}\) dijets in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV that is compared with CMS data, finding that the medium-induced energy loss shifts the \(x_J\) distribution to smaller values similarly to inclusive dijets, but with a smaller energy loss for \(b\bar{b}\) dijets than for inclusive dijets as centrality increases.

## Method

The authors use the NLO+parton shower event generator SHERPA to simulate the initial hard processes of inclusive b-jet and \(b\bar{b}\) dijet production in p+p collisions. The in-medium evolution of both heavy and light quarks is described by a modified Langevin transport equation that includes a radiative energy loss term based on the higher-twist formalism, with the spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s\) and jet transport parameter \(\hat{q}_0\) as free parameters. The bulk medium evolution is provided by the iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic model, and final-state jet reconstruction is performed using the anti-\(k_T\) algorithm in FASTJET.

## Key numbers

* Inclusive jet and inclusive b-jet \(R_{\rm AA}\) in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76\) TeV are compared with CMS data, using parameters \(2\pi TD_s=4.0\) and \(\hat{q}_0=1.2\) GeV\(^2\)/fm.
* The normalized \(x_J\) distribution of \(b\bar{b}\) dijets is calculated for Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV with leading jet \(p_T > 100\) GeV and sub-leading jet \(p_T > 40\) GeV, with an opening angle cut \(|\Delta\phi| > 2\pi/3\).
* The averaged \(x_J\) values for \(b\bar{b}\) dijets as a function of number of participants show that the imbalance increases with centrality, and in 30-100% Pb+Pb collisions the averaged \(x_J\) is compatible with the p+p reference.
* The normalized azimuthal angle distribution of \(b\bar{b}\) dijets in 0-10% central Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV shows suppression of the same-side peak (\(\Delta\phi \to 0\)) and enhancement of the away-side peak (\(\Delta\phi \to \pi\)).

## Context

This work extends the study of jet quenching from inclusive dijets to heavy-flavor tagged \(b\bar{b}\) dijets, providing a simultaneous treatment of heavy and light quark in-medium evolution. It builds on prior measurements by ATLAS (2016) and CMS (2018) of \(b\bar{b}\) dijet production in p+p collisions, and on theoretical frameworks for heavy quark transport using Langevin and higher-twist approaches (Cao 2013, Wang 2019). The results enable future comparisons of flavor-dependent energy loss in heavy-ion collisions and help isolate the role of parton mass in jet quenching.
