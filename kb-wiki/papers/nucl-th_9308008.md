# Momentum-Dependent Mean Field Based Upon the Dirac-Brueckner Approach for Nuclear Matter

> A momentum-dependent mean field potential, suitable for application in the transport-model description of nucleus-nucleus collisions, is derived in a microscopic way. The derivation is based upon the Bonn meson-exchange model for the nucleon-nucleon interaction and the Dirac-Brueckner approach for nuclear matter. The properties of the microscopic mean field are examined and compared with phenomenological parametrizations which are commonly used in transport-model calculations.

## Key claim
The paper derives a momentum-dependent mean-field potential for nuclear matter from the Dirac-Brueckner approach using the Bonn meson-exchange potential, but the authors note that the scalar and vector fields \(U_S\) and \(U_V\) are assumed to be momentum-independent (only density-dependent), which is a good approximation because the momentum dependence from these fields is typically a factor of five smaller than the dominant momentum dependence from the relativistic kinetic structure of the mean field.

## Method
The authors solve the Dirac equation for single-particle motion in nuclear matter together with the in-medium Thompson equation (relativistic Bethe-Goldstone equation) self-consistently, using the Bonn A one-boson-exchange potential. The scalar field \(U_S\) and vector field \(U_V\) are determined from the \(\tilde G\)-matrix via the relativistic analogue of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock definition of the single-particle potential, and the mean-field potential is then constructed as \(U(\rho,p) = U_V(\rho) + [(m+U_S(\rho))^2 + p^2]^{1/2} - (m^2 + p^2)^{1/2}\). The resulting mean field is parametrized in the same analytic form as the phenomenological SMD/HMD parametrization for convenient use in transport models.

## Key numbers
- At normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\), the scalar field is \(U_S = -355.7\) MeV and the vector field is \(U_V = 274.7\) MeV.
- The nucleon effective mass from DBHF using the Bonn A potential is \(m^*/m = 0.71\) at \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The momentum dependence of \(U_S\) and \(U_V\) is characterized by \(U_S^{(1)}/U_S^{(0)} \approx U_V^{(1)}/U_V^{(0)} \approx 0.05\), making it negligible compared to the dominant momentum dependence from the relativistic kinetic term.
- The phenomenological parametrizations compared include GBG (incompressibility \(K = 215\) MeV), SMD (\(K = 200\) MeV), and HMD (\(K = 380\) MeV).

## Context
This work extends the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach from static nuclear matter to a momentum-dependent mean field suitable for transport models of nucleus-nucleus collisions, following earlier DBHF studies by Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) and Machleidt (1989). It contrasts with non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using the Reid soft-core potential (Aichelin et al., 1987; Khoa et al., 1992), which cannot quantitatively reproduce nuclear matter saturation. The derived mean field enables fully self-consistent transport-model calculations when combined with the in-medium NN cross sections from Li and Machleidt (1993), reducing uncertainties from separate parametrizations of the mean field and NN collisions.
