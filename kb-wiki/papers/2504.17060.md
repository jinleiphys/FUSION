# Charge Radii Measurements of Exotic Tin Isotopes in the Proximity of $N=50$ and $N=82$

> We report nuclear charge radii for the isotopes $^{104-134}$Sn, measured using two different collinear laser spectroscopy techniques at ISOLDE-CERN. These measurements clarify the arch-like trend in charge radii along the isotopic chain and reveal an odd-even staggering that is more pronounced near the $N=50$ and $N=82$ shell closures. The observed local trends are well described by both nuclear density functional theory and valence space in-medium similarity renormalization group calculations. Both theories predict appreciable contributions from beyond-mean-field correlations to the charge radii of the neutron-deficient tin isotopes. The models, however, fall short of reproducing the magnitude of the known $B(E2)$ transition probabilities, highlighting the remaining challenges in achieving a unified description of both ground-state properties and collective phenomena.

## Key claim

The paper reports charge radii for the complete chain of tin isotopes from 104Sn to 134Sn, revealing an arch-like trend and enhanced odd-even staggering near the N=50 and N=82 shell closures; however, both nuclear density functional theory and valence space in-medium similarity renormalization group calculations, while describing the local radius trends well, fall short of reproducing the magnitude of known B(E2) transition probabilities, indicating that a unified description of ground-state properties and collective phenomena remains incomplete.

## Method

Two collinear laser spectroscopy techniques were employed at ISOLDE-CERN: the Collinear Resonance Ionization Spectroscopy (CRIS) setup for neutron-deficient isotopes (104Sn to 124Sn) using 284 nm and 281 nm atomic transitions, and the Collinear Laser Spectroscopy (COLLAPS) experiment for neutron-rich isotopes (108Sn to 134Sn) using 452.5 nm and 286.3 nm transitions. Changes in mean-square charge radii were extracted from measured isotope shifts via a standard King-plot analysis using muonic data and electron scattering form factors, with the 124Sn reference radius taken as 4.675(1) fm. The experimental results were compared to valence space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations using two chiral interactions (1.8/2.0 (EM) and DeltaN2LO_GO(394)) and to nuclear density functional theory (DFT) using the Fy(IVP) Fayans functional.

## Key numbers

- Charge radii measured for 31 tin isotopes from 104Sn to 134Sn, extending previous data by four isotopes on the neutron-deficient side (104-107Sn) and completing earlier reported radii with odd-even isotopes including 133Sn
- 104Sn charge radius: 4.512(5) fm (first measurement)
- 134Sn charge radius: 4.731(1) fm
- 124Sn reference charge radius: 4.675(1) fm (from prior electron scattering data)
- 133Sn charge radius: 4.718(2) fm (first measurement)
- Difference in squared radii between different valence-space VS-IMSRG calculations at N=82: 0.12 fm2
- DFT Fy(IVP) functional provides improved description of radii trend compared to its former variant Fy(Delta r,HFB) employed in Gorges (2019) for even-even tin isotopes

## Context

This work extends the available charge radius data for tin isotopes from previous measurements by Blanc (2005), Eberz (1987), and Anselment (1986), and completes the earlier reported radii in Gorges (2019) with odd-even isotopes including 133Sn. The measurements clarify the arch-like trend in charge radii along the isotopic chain and reveal odd-even staggering more pronounced near the N=50 and N=82 shell closures, similar to that observed at other major shell closures such as Ca (N=28) and Pb (N=126). The results enable testing of both nuclear density functional theory and ab initio VS-IMSRG calculations, with the finding that the arch-like trend is dominated by isotropic growth of the mean field rather than many-body correlations, in contrast to B(E2) values which require higher-order correlations and cross-shell excitations as previously suggested by large-scale shell model calculations of Togashi (2018).
