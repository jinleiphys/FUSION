# Holographic description of total hadronic cross sections at high energies

> We investigate the hadron-hadron total cross sections at high energies in the framework of holographic QCD. In our model setup, the involved strong interaction is described by the Brower-Polchinski-Strassler-Tan Pomeron exchange kernel, and the two form factors are obtained from the bottom-up AdS/QCD models. We show that the resulting nucleon-nucleon total cross section is consistent with the experimental data recently measured by the TOTEM collaboration at the LHC. As examples to see the general versatility of the model, we also present our analysis on the pion-nucleon and pion-pion cases, which can be predicted because all the adjustable parameters are fixed by the nucleon-nucleon data.

# Holographic description of total hadronic cross sections at high energies

## Key claim
The holographic QCD model, using the BPST Pomeron exchange kernel with bottom-up AdS/QCD form factors, reproduces nucleon-nucleon total cross sections consistent with TOTEM data at LHC energies, but only after including a confinement-effect modification to the conformal kernel. The model predicts pion-nucleon and pion-pion cross sections without additional free parameters, because all adjustable parameters are fixed by nucleon-nucleon data.

## Method
The authors compute total hadronic cross sections using the eikonal representation of the scattering amplitude, where the strong interaction is described by the Brower-Polchinski-Strassler-Tan (BPST) Pomeron exchange kernel in five-dimensional AdS space. The hadron density distributions (gravitational form factors) are obtained from bottom-up AdS/QCD models: the nucleon from Hong et al. (2006) and the pion from Erlich et al. (2005) and Abidin and Carlson (2008). A modified kernel incorporating confinement effects is used for numerical evaluation, with two adjustable parameters (rho and g0^2) fitted to pp and antiproton-proton data.

## Key numbers
- The nucleon-nucleon total cross section is calculated over the kinematic range 10^2 < sqrt(s) < 10^5 GeV.
- The predicted total cross section ratio sigma_tot^{pi N} / sigma_tot^{NN} = 0.63.
- The predicted total cross section ratio sigma_tot^{pi pi} / sigma_tot^{NN} = 0.45.
- The soft Pomeron intercept value from Donnachie and Landshoff (1992) is 1.0808, which is close to the constant intercept obtained in the hadron-hadron (IR-IR) scattering case.
- The Bjorken x value at sqrt(s) ~ 10 TeV is estimated as x ~ 10^{-8}, using the proton mass m_p ~ 1 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the holographic QCD approach, previously applied to deep inelastic scattering (DIS) at small x by Brower et al. (2007, 2010) and Watanabe et al. (2012, 2013), to hadron-hadron scattering where both participants are in the infrared region. It contrasts with the DIS case (UV-IR scattering) where the Pomeron intercept depends on Q^2, while here the intercept is constant. The predicted pion-nucleon cross section ratio agrees with the empirical value extracted from the soft Pomeron exchange analysis of Donnachie and Landshoff (1992). The framework enables predictions for any hadron pair at high energies, and could be tested at future facilities with high intensity pion beams.
