# Net-charge fluctuation in Au+Au collisions at energies available at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research using the UrQMD model

> We have studied the dynamical fluctuation of net-charge of hadrons produced in Au+Au collisions at energies that in near future will be available at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR). Data simulated by a microscopic transport model based on ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics are analyzed for the purpose. The centrality and pseudorapidity dependence of the net-charge fluctuation of hadrons are examined. Our simulated results are compared with the results available for nucleus-nucleus collision experiments held at similar energies. The gross features of our simulated results on net-charge fluctuations are found to be consistent with the experiment. At incident beam energy $E_{\rm lab}=10A$ GeV the magnitude of net-charge fluctuation is very large, and in comparison with the rest its centrality dependence appears to be a little unusual. The effect of global charge conservation is expected to be very crucial at FAIR energies. The charge fluctuations measured with varying pesudorapidity window size depend on the collision centrality. The dependence is however exactly opposite in nature to that observed in the Pb+Pb collision at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=2.76$ TeV.

## Key claim

The UrQMD model predicts that at the incident beam energy \(E_{\rm lab}=10A\) GeV the magnitude of net-charge fluctuation is very large and its centrality dependence appears unusual compared to higher energies, but this deviation is largely removed after correcting for global charge conservation, which is expected to be very crucial at FAIR energies.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD (v3.4) microscopic transport model to generate \(10^{6}\) minimum bias Au+Au collision events at laboratory energies \(E_{\rm lab}=10A\), \(20A\), \(30A\), and \(40A\) GeV. Collision centralities were determined via the Monte-Carlo Glauber model, and the dynamical net-charge fluctuation parameter \(\nu_{\pm,\rm dyn}\) was calculated from the correlation strengths between like-sign and opposite-sign charged particle pairs within a pseudorapidity window \(\Delta\eta = 1.0\) and transverse momentum range \(0.2 < p_t < 2.0\) GeV/\(c\). The results were corrected for global charge conservation using the relation \(\nu^{\rm corr}_{\pm,\rm dyn} = \nu_{\pm,\rm dyn} + 4/\langle N_{4\pi}\rangle\).

## Key numbers

* At \(E_{\rm lab}=10A\) GeV for the 0-5% most central collisions: \(\nu_{\pm,\rm dyn} = -0.0095 \pm 6.5\times10^{-6}\) and \(\nu^{\rm corr}_{\pm,\rm dyn} = -0.0017\), with \(\langle N_{\rm ch}\rangle = 143 \pm 0.065\) within \(|\eta|<0.5\) and \(0.2 \leq p_t \leq 2.0\) GeV/\(c\).
* At \(E_{\rm lab}=40A\) GeV for the 0-5% most central collisions: \(\nu_{\pm,\rm dyn} = -0.0052 \pm 0.6\times10^{-6}\) and \(\nu^{\rm corr}_{\pm,\rm dyn} = -0.0009\), with \(\langle N_{\rm ch}\rangle = 220 \pm 0.097\).
* For the 80-90% centrality bin at \(E_{\rm lab}=10A\) GeV: \(\nu_{\pm,\rm dyn} = -0.2433 \pm 1.2\times10^{-2}\) and \(\nu^{\rm corr}_{\pm,\rm dyn} = -0.2197\).
* The magnitude of \(dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta\,\nu_{\pm,\rm dyn}\) increases by approximately 40% from peripheral to central collisions for \(E_{\rm lab}=20A-40A\) GeV, and by about 60% for \(E_{\rm lab}=10A\) GeV.
* After GCC correction, the product \(\langle N_{\rm ch}\rangle\,\nu^{\rm corr}_{\pm,\rm dyn}\) for the 0-5% most central collisions shows little energy dependence within \(E_{\rm lab}=10A-40A\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends simulation studies of dynamical net-charge fluctuations to the FAIR energy regime (\(E_{\rm lab}=10A-40A\) GeV) using the UrQMD model, where no experimental data yet exist. The results are compared with STAR Au+Au data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) and 11.5 GeV (Sharma 2015) and with ALICE Pb+Pb data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV (Abelev 2013). The study provides a baseline for the upcoming CBM experiment at FAIR, showing that global charge conservation effects are significant at these lower energies and that the pseudorapidity dependence of fluctuations is opposite in nature to that observed at LHC energies.
