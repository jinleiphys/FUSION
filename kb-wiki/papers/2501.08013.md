# Halo Structures in p-Shell Hypernuclei with Natural Orbitals

> We extend the concept of natural orbitals as an optimized single-particle basis for ab initio nuclear many-body calculations to hypernuclei and show that their superior properties, in particular accelerated convergence and independence of the underlying harmonic-oscillator frequency, can be directly transferred to the hypernuclear regime as demonstrated in no-core shell model calculations for selected p-shell hypernuclei. Moreover, the radial single-particle wavefunctions associated with the natural-orbital basis yield important structural information with respect to the different particle species allowing us to identify a hyperon halo in ΛHe5. We further explore nucleonic and hyperonic halo structures in A=6 and A=7 singly-strange hypernuclei based on one-body densities and point-particle radii obtained from no-core shell model calculations with realistic interactions from chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

The authors extend the natural-orbital (NAT) single-particle basis, previously used in purely nucleonic ab initio calculations, to hypernuclei and demonstrate that the NAT basis provides accelerated convergence and independence of the harmonic-oscillator frequency for hypernuclear no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations, with the caveat that a residual frequency dependence remains for spatially extended states due to truncation of the underlying harmonic-oscillator basis at finite \(e_\mathrm{max}\).

## Method

The authors construct natural orbitals as eigenvectors of the one-body density matrix approximated via Hartree-Fock many-body perturbation theory (HF-MBPT) up to second order, using a single-determinant Hartree-Fock reference state in an equal-filling approximation for open-shell systems. They then perform importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM) calculations for selected p-shell hypernuclei using chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions at N\(^3\)LO with cutoff \(\Lambda_\mathrm{N}=500\) MeV and a chiral hyperon-nucleon (YN) interaction at LO with cutoff \(\Lambda_\mathrm{Y}=700\) MeV, all evolved via similarity renormalization group (SRG) to flow parameter \(\alpha=0.08\) fm\(^4\). The single-particle basis is truncated at \(e_\mathrm{max}=12\) with \(l_\mathrm{max}=8\), and three-body forces are included via a normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) approximation.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies of \(^5_{\Lambda}\mathrm{He}\) and \(^7_{\Lambda}\mathrm{He}\) are computed in the NAT basis and shown to be frequency-independent, converging to values in good agreement with HO-basis results including three-body forces; the NO2B error is small for small \(\hbar\Omega\) but increases significantly for larger \(\hbar\Omega\), more pronounced in \(^5_{\Lambda}\mathrm{He}\) than in \(^7_{\Lambda}\mathrm{He}\).
- For \(^5_{\Lambda}\mathrm{He}\), the neutron separation energy is \(S_n = 20.578\) MeV, while the hyperon separation energy is \(B_\Lambda = 3.12(2)\) MeV, supporting the interpretation of a hyperon halo around an \(\alpha\) core.
- In \(^6_{\Lambda}\mathrm{He}\), the additional neutron occupies the \(0p_{3/2}\) orbital with occupation number \(n_\mathrm{n} \approx 0.66\) to \(0.81\) depending on frequency; in \(^7_{\Lambda}\mathrm{He}\), this becomes \(n_\mathrm{n} \approx 1.69\) to \(1.76\).
- Point-particle rms radii for neutrons, protons, and \(\Lambda\) hyperons are reported for \(^5_{\Lambda}\mathrm{He}\), \(^6_{\Lambda}\mathrm{He}\), \(^7_{\Lambda}\mathrm{He}\), \(^6_{\Lambda}\mathrm{Li}\), \(^7_{\Lambda}\mathrm{Li}\), and \(^7_{\Lambda}\mathrm{Be}\) at \(\hbar\Omega=16\) MeV; the \(\Lambda\) radius in \(^5_{\Lambda}\mathrm{He}\) is much larger than the nucleonic radii, indicating a hyperon halo.
- Negative occupation numbers as low as \(-0.6\) occur in triply open-shell systems such as \(^7_{\Lambda}\mathrm{Li}\), slowing model-space convergence but with effects mitigated at larger \(\Nmax\).

## Context

This work extends the natural-orbital approach of Tichai (2019) and Hoppe (2021) from purely nucleonic systems to hypernuclei, enabling ab initio studies of halo structures in singly-strange p-shell hypernuclei. Prior cluster-model studies by Hiyama (1996, 2009), Cobis (1997), Hildenbrand (2019), and Zhang (2021) had indicated hyperon halo structures in light hypernuclei, but this work provides the first ab initio evidence using realistic chiral interactions and the NCSM framework. The results enable future ab initio calculations of medium-mass hypernuclei, for example within the in-medium NCSM, and motivate multi-reference treatments of natural orbitals to address negative occupation numbers in open-shell systems.
