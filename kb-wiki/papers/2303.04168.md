# Neutrinoless double-beta decay in the neutrino-extended Standard Model

> We investigate neutrinoless double-beta decay ($0νββ$) in the minimal extension of the standard model of particle physics, the $ν$SM, where gauge-singlet right-handed neutrinos give rise to Dirac and Majorana neutrino mass terms. We focus on the associated sterile neutrinos and argue that the usual evaluation of their contributions to $0νββ$, based on mass-dependent nuclear matrix elements, is missing important contributions from neutrinos with ultrasoft and hard momenta. We identify the hadronic and nuclear matrix elements that enter the new contributions, and calculate all relevant nuclear matrix elements for $^{136}$Xe using the nuclear shell model. Finally, we illustrate the impact on $0νββ$ rates in specific neutrino mass models and show that the new contributions significantly alter the $0νββ$ rate in most parts of the $ν$SM parameter space.

## Key claim

The usual evaluation of sterile neutrino contributions to neutrinoless double-beta decay, based solely on mass-dependent nuclear matrix elements from the neutrino potential, misses important contributions from neutrinos with ultrasoft and hard momenta; these new contributions can alter the decay rate by orders of magnitude for light sterile neutrinos and by factors of a few for heavier ones.

## Method

The authors perform a systematic effective field theory (EFT) derivation of the 0νββ amplitude in the νSM, identifying contributions from sterile neutrinos in three momentum regimes: potential (k ∼ k_F), hard (k ∼ Λ_χ), and ultrasoft (k ∼ k_F²/m_N). They calculate all relevant nuclear matrix elements for ¹³⁶Xe using the nuclear shell model with the GCN5082 effective Hamiltonian in a configuration space comprising the 0g₇/₂, 1d₅/₂, 2s₁/₂, 1d₃/₂, and 0h₁₁/₂ orbitals, using the shell-model code NATHAN. The ultrasoft contributions require intermediate-state energies of ¹³⁶Cs and matrix elements computed via the Lanczos strength function method with 60 iterations.

## Key numbers

- For ¹³⁶Xe, the phase-space factor G₀₁ = 1.4 × 10⁻¹⁴ y⁻¹
- The nuclear matrix element at zero neutrino mass is M(0) = 2.7 for ¹³⁶Xe
- The fit to the mass-dependent NME gives parameters m_a = 157 MeV and m_b = 221 MeV in the functional form M(m_i) = M(0) / (1 + m_i/m_a + (m_i/m_b)²)
- The hard-neutrino LEC at zero mass is g_ν^NN(0) = -1.01 fm², with the corresponding shell-model matrix element M_F,sd = -1.94
- The pion-pion LEC from lattice QCD is g₁^ππ = 0.36(2) at μ = 2 GeV
- For the 3+1 model with m₄ = 10 MeV, the new approach gives half-lives roughly three orders of magnitude shorter than the standard approach
- For the pseudo-Dirac scenario with mass splitting Δ/M₂ = 1%, the constraints on mixing angles are tighter by a factor of about 2.5 for m_i ≈ 400 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT framework for 0νββ decay developed by Cirigliano et al. (2018, 2019) to include sterile neutrinos in the νSM, correcting earlier treatments by Blennow et al. (2010), Faessler et al. (2014), and Asaka et al. (2016) that only replaced the neutrino propagator mass dependence. The new contributions enable more accurate predictions for 0νββ rates in seesaw models relevant to leptogenesis and dark matter, and the results can be refined by ongoing lattice QCD calculations of the relevant hadronic matrix elements.
