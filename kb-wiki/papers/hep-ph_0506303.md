# Anisotropic flow of strange particles at RHIC

> Space-time picture of the anisotropic flow evolution in Au+Au collisions at BNL RHIC is studied for strange hadrons within the microscopic quark-gluon string model. The directed flow of both mesons and hyperons demonstrates wiggle structure with the universal antiflow slope at |y| < 2 for minimum bias events. This effect increases as the reaction becomes more peripheral. The development of both components of the anisotropic flow is closely related to particle freeze-out. Hadrons are emitted continuously, and different hadronic species are decoupled from the system at different times. These hadrons contribute differently to the formation and evolution of the elliptic flow, which can be decomposed onto three components: (i) flow created by hadrons emitted from the surface at the onset of the collision; (ii) flow produced by jets; (iii) hydrodynamic flow. Due to these features, the general trend in elliptic flow formation is that the earlier mesons are frozen, the weaker their elliptic flow. In contrast, baryons frozen at the end of the system evolution have stronger v2.

# Anisotropic flow of strange particles at RHIC

## Key claim

The directed flow of all hadrons in Au+Au collisions at RHIC exhibits an antiflow slope at $|y| \leq 2$ that increases with peripheral collisions, but the signal is weak enough (less than 1% at $|\eta| \leq 2$) that systematic uncertainties prevent distinguishing between the quark-gluon string model and other microscopic models such as UrQMD or AMPT.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic quark-gluon string model (QGSM) to simulate Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies, generating approximately 20,000 events at $\sqrt{s} = 130$ AGeV with impact parameter $b = 8$ fm. They tracked the time evolution of directed flow $v_1$ and elliptic flow $v_2$ for strange hadrons (kaons, lambdas, phi mesons, and nucleons) by taking snapshots at fixed times after the collision, switching off all hadronic interactions at each snapshot and allowing resonances to decay according to their branching ratios.

## Key numbers

- Directed flow of charged hadrons at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ AGeV has magnitude less than 1% at $|\eta| \leq 2$, with antiflow slope at $|y| \leq 2$ for minimum bias events
- Elliptic flow of kaons at midrapidity reaches maximum $v_2^K (y=0) \approx 6\%$ at $t = 8$ fm/$c$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 130$ AGeV with $b = 8$ fm
- Kaons frozen at $t = 2$ fm/$c$ show the strongest elliptic anisotropy among all freeze-out time fractions, despite the apparent $v_2$ being weak at that time
- Lambdas decoupled after $t = 8$ fm/$c$ show stronger elliptic flow the later they freeze, opposite to the trend for kaons

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of anisotropic flow in microscopic models (Bleicher and Stoecker 2002, Li and others 1999) by focusing on the time evolution and freeze-out decomposition of strange hadron flow. The results contradict the simple hydrodynamic picture where elliptic flow develops only in the early dense phase, showing instead that continuous freeze-out produces different flow contributions for mesons versus baryons. The findings enable future studies that must couple collective flow development with the freeze-out chronology, as previously suggested for pions and nucleons (Erice workshop proceedings).
