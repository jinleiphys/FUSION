# A New Mechanism for J/psi Suppression in Nuclear Collisions

> We present new results based on an improved version of the Glauber model, used to describe the nuclear absorption of J/psi in reactions between nuclei at high energy. The excitation of the colour degrees of freedom of nucleons, due to collisions mediated by one-gluon exchange, is taken into account. It is found that the proposed mechanism leads to larger nuclear absorption of J/psi than previously considered.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that nucleons become coloured (octet or decuplet states) via one-gluon-exchange collisions during the early stage of a nuclear collision, and that this colour excitation leads to larger J/ψ absorption than predicted by the standard Glauber model. The author notes that the result overestimates suppression at small transverse energy because retardation and formation time effects are not yet included.

## Method
The author extends the standard Glauber model by replacing the fixed ψ-nucleon absorption cross section with an effective cross section that depends on the colour state of the nucleon (singlet, octet, or decuplet). Colour-dipole cross sections are computed within the Low-Nussinov model (two-gluon exchange with a gluon mass of 140 MeV), and the evolution of colour probabilities along the nuclear path is obtained from a master equation based on multiple Glauber collisions. The resulting ψ production cross section in Pb+Pb collisions is then evaluated and compared to NA50 data.

## Key numbers
- The effective absorption cross section for ψ on a singlet nucleon is set to σ^{abs}_{ψN} = 7.3 mb, matching p+A and S+Pb data.
- For an octet nucleon, the absorption cross section is 1.35 times the singlet value; for a decuplet nucleon, it is 1.7 times the singlet value.
- The inelastic NN cross section used is σ^{in}_{NN} = 30 mb.
- The strong coupling is fixed to α_s ≈ 0.65 to reproduce a πN cross section of about 23 mb.
- The ψ meson velocity is v_ψ ≈ 0.4, derived from x_F ≈ 0.15 and √s_{NN} ≈ 17 GeV at the SPS.
- The colour probabilities reach a statistical limit after about 4 fm of nuclear penetration: about 2/3 of nucleons are in octet state and 1/3 in decuplet, with negligible singlet fraction.

## Context
This work extends the standard Glauber model for J/ψ absorption in nuclear collisions, which with σ^{abs}_{ψN} = 7.3 mb accounts for p+A and S+Pb data but fails for Pb+Pb collisions (NA50 experiment). It builds on earlier attempts by Hüfner and Kopeliovich (1998) that included prompt gluon clouds but treated nucleons as non-interacting, and on the Dual Parton Model framework for one-gluon exchange. The new mechanism enables more detailed calculations of anomalous J/ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions, though the author notes that retardation effects and formation time effects (Hüfner, Hüfner, and Kopeliovich) are still needed for a complete description.
