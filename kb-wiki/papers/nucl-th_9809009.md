# Isospin breaking in the reaction np --> dpi^0 at threshold

> The model for charge symmetry breaking in the reaction np --> dpi^0 applied earlier around the Delta region is used to calculate the integrated forward-backward asymmetry of the cross section close to threshold. The mixing of the pi and eta mesons appears as strongly dominant at these energies. This contrasts elastic np scattering experiments, where the np mass difference in OPE dominates, or np --> dπ^0 closer to the Delta region.

# Isospin breaking in the reaction np → dπ⁰ at threshold

## Key claim

The integrated forward-backward asymmetry in np → dπ⁰ near threshold is strongly dominated by η-π meson mixing, in contrast to elastic np scattering where the neutron-proton mass difference in one-pion exchange dominates, and in contrast to the same reaction at higher energies near the Δ region where cancellations occur.

## Method

The author calculates the integrated forward-backward asymmetry of the unpolarized cross section by solving coupled Schrödinger equations for the initial np states, including isobar configurations with Δ(1232) intermediate states. Several charge symmetry breaking mechanisms are included: the np mass difference in one-pion exchange (both in the NN sector and in the extended two-baryon space), an isoscalar meson-like pion production vertex from the np mass difference, η and η' production followed by mixing into π⁰, and ηπ or η'π mixing potentials. The isospin-conserving amplitudes are generated using standard coupled-channel methods that reproduce pp → dπ⁺ cross sections and NN phase shifts to within a few degrees from threshold through the Δ region.

## Key numbers

- At laboratory energy 279.5 MeV, the η vertex contribution to the ¹P₁ → s amplitude is (-9.04, 4.78) in complex reduced matrix elements, compared to (-0.45, 0.24) from the π vertex and (0.25, 0.60) from the π potential (NN sector).
- The ηπ mixing matrix element used is ⟨η|H|π⟩ = -5900 MeV², with ηNN coupling G_η²/4π = 3.68 (corresponding to G_η²/4π = 4.8 at zero momentum).
- The η' mixing matrix element is -5500 MeV².
- At E_lab = 279.5 MeV, the total integrated forward-backward asymmetry is approximately -0.31% (sum of π vertex -0.009%, π potential +0.024%, η vertex -0.183%, ηπ potential -0.141%).
- At E_lab = 278 MeV (η = q/m_π⁰ = 0.138), the total asymmetry is approximately -0.26%; at E_lab = 350 MeV (η = 0.730), the total asymmetry is approximately -0.51%.
- The anticipated precision of the TRIUMF experiment E704 is 0.12%.
- The isospin-conserving s- and p-wave amplitudes at 279.5 MeV are about 100 times larger than the η-vertex column amplitudes.

## Context

This work extends the model of Niskanen (1997, Ref. nst) for charge symmetry breaking in np → dπ⁰ from the Δ region down to threshold, motivated by the TRIUMF experiment E704 (Ref. e704) which can detect asymmetries at the level of one part in a thousand. The results contrast with elastic np scattering experiments (Abegg et al., Knutson et al., Greeniaus et al.) where the np mass difference in one-pion exchange dominates, and with the same reaction at higher energies where ηπ mixing effects cancel significantly (Niskanen, 1997). The dominance of ηπ mixing suggests these measurements can serve as a probe of the ηNN coupling, which has large uncertainties (values ranging from 2 to 7 in various Bonn potentials, Elster et al.) and is controversial in photoproduction (Tiator et al.). The off-shell momentum dependence of ρω mixing (contested by Coon et al.) is noted as a related uncertainty that could affect ηπ mixing as well.
