# Laboratory Probes of the Neutron-Matter Equation of State

> To relate constraints from nuclear physics to the tidal deformabilities of neutron stars, we construct a neutron star model that accepts input from a large collection of Skyrme density functions to calculate properties of 1.4 solar-mass neutron stars. We find that restricting this set of Skyrme to density functions that describe nuclear masses, isobaric analog states, and low energy nuclear reactions does not sufficiently restrict the predicted neutron-star radii and the tidal deformabilities. However, pressure constraints on the EoS around twice saturation density ($2\times2.74\times10^{14}g/cm^3$), obtained from high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions, does constrain predicted tidal deformabilities with uncertainties smaller than those obtained from the analysis of GW170817. We also found that the density-pressure constraint on the EoS obtained from a recent analysis of the neutron-star merger event agree very well with the density pressure constraints obtained from nuclear physics experiments published in 2002.

## Key claim

Pressure constraints on the equation of state around twice saturation density, obtained from high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions, can constrain predicted tidal deformabilities with uncertainties smaller than those obtained from the analysis of GW170817, whereas restricting Skyrme density functions to those that describe nuclear masses, isobaric analog states, and low-energy nuclear reactions does not sufficiently restrict predicted neutron-star radii and tidal deformabilities.

## Method

The authors constructed a neutron star model that accepts input from 189 Skyrme density functions (selected from a collection of 207) to calculate properties of 1.4 solar-mass neutron stars. They incorporated EoS from different density regions: crust EoS from the literature, polytropic EoS for a relativistic electron Fermi gas, Skyrme interactions for the inner core region (approximately 0.5 to 3 times saturation density), and polytropic EoS above 3 times saturation density to support a maximum neutron star of 2.17 solar masses. They then compared the predicted tidal deformabilities and radii against constraints from nuclear physics experiments and from the GW170817 neutron-star merger event.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: $\rho_0 = 2.74 \times 10^{14} g/cm^3$
- Pressure constraints on the EoS were obtained around twice saturation density: $2 \times 2.74 \times 10^{14} g/cm^3$
- The range of tidal deformability from GW170817 analysis in Ref. [r7]: $\Lambda = 70-720$
- The more restricted region from Ref. [r8]: $\Lambda = 70-580$ and radius $R = 10.5-13.3 km$
- The maximum neutron star mass supported by the polytropic EoS above $3\rho_0$: 2.17 solar masses
- Hypothetical pressure constraint at $2\rho_0$ assuming a soft symmetry energy: $16-20 MeV/fm^3$, yielding $\Lambda$ values in the range 200-400

## Context

This work extends the connection between nuclear physics experiments and neutron-star astrophysics, building on prior constraints from collective flow measurements in Au+Au collisions (Tsang et al., 2002) and kaon production measurements (Fuchs et al., 2001; Sturm et al., 2001). The authors find that symmetry energy data extracted at sub-saturation densities from nuclear masses (Brown, 2000), isobaric analog states (Danielewicz and Lee, 2009), and isospin diffusion measurements (Tsang et al., 2004) do not correlate strongly with neutron-star properties. The work enables tighter predictions for tidal deformabilities and neutron-star radii by combining heavy-ion collision constraints at twice saturation density with future gravitational wave observations.
