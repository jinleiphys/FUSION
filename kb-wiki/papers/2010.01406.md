# Event-shaped-dependent cumulants in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV

> In this paper, we present a novel event-shaped cumulants (ESC) response approach, based on a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model simulations, to analyze p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 5.02 TeV. We find that the Pearson coefficients between the subset cumulants of the final harmonics $v_{2}\{2k\}~(k=1,2,3,4)$ and the subset cumulants of initial eccentricity $\varepsilon_{2}\{2k\}$ in the ESC basis are significantly enhanced. These Pearson coefficients are strongly-dependent on the charged multiplicity, the set number of events (SNE), and only weakly-dependent on the order of the multi-particle cumulants (two-particles, four-particles, and so on). Our results show that the ESC method can suppress the event-by-event fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a novel event-shaped cumulants (ESC) response approach, based on AMPT model simulations, significantly enhances the Pearson correlation coefficients between subset cumulants of final harmonic flow and initial eccentricity in p-Pb collisions, thereby suppressing event-by-event fluctuations. The hedge is that this enhancement is demonstrated only in AMPT model simulations, not in experimental data, and the authors explicitly state they do not attempt to compare with experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with string melting to simulate p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, with impact parameter b=0-3 fm and specific shear viscosity η/s=0.273. They introduced an event-shaped cumulants (ESC) method where events are sorted by initial eccentricity from minimum to maximum, then divided into subsets of events (SOE) with a fixed set number of events (SNE) per subset. For each SOE, they calculated multi-particle cumulants (2k-particle, k=1,2,3,4) for both final harmonic flow v₂{2k} and initial eccentricity ε₂{2k}, then computed Pearson coefficients between these subset cumulants across all SOEs.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: √s_NN = 5.02 TeV for p-Pb collisions
- Impact parameter range: b = 0-3 fm
- Specific shear viscosity: η/s = 0.273
- Charged pion selection: 0.3 < p_T < 3.0 GeV, |η| < 1.0, and multiplicity M in [80, 120] (except for multiplicity dependence studies)
- Event-by-event Pearson coefficient: C(ε, v) ≈ 0.04 for the standard event-by-event method
- ESC Pearson coefficient for SNE=500: C(ε, v) ≈ 0.560 for four-particle, six-particle, and eight-particle cumulants
- Linear response coefficient from least-square fitting: approximately 0.08
- Pearson coefficients reach maximum plateau at SNE ≈ 1000
- Total events used: up to 1.25×10⁷ for SNE=500 case

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of response relations in p-Pb systems with event-by-event simulations (Wei and Zhou, 2020) and addresses the problem that event-by-event Pearson coefficients are negligible in peripheral Pb-Pb collisions (Rao, 2019). The ESC method is presented as an improvement over standard subevent methods (Jia, 2017) which failed to suppress residual nonflow contributions in small collisions. The approach enables future studies of fluidlike QGP fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, though the authors note that nonlinear response relations in small systems remain under debate and require further investigation with experimental data.
