# Halo Nuclei from Ab Initio Nuclear Theory

> A realistic description of halo nuclei, characterized by low-lying breakup thresholds, requires a proper treatment of continuum effects. We have developed an ab initio approach, the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC), capable of describing both bound and unbound states in light nuclei in a unified way. With chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions as the only input, we can predict structure and dynamics of halo and other light nuclei and, by comparing to available experimental data, test the quality of chiral nuclear forces. We review NCSMC calculations of weakly bound states and resonances of exotic halo nuclei $^6$He, $^8$B, $^{11}$Be, and $^{15}$C. For the latter, we discuss its production in the capture reaction $^{14}$C(n,$γ$)$^{15}$C. We highlight challenges of a description of $^6$He as a Borromean n-n-$^4$He system. Finally, we present calculations of excited states in $^{10}$Be exhibiting a one-neutron halo structure and a large scale no-core shell model investigation of $^{11}$Li as a precursor of a full n-n-$^9$Li NCSMC study.

## Key claim
The no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) can predict both bound and unbound states of halo nuclei using only chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions as input, but the quality of these predictions depends on how well the chiral nuclear forces reproduce experimental data when compared.

## Method
The authors applied the NCSMC, which unifies the no-core shell model (NCSM) with the resonating-group method (RGM) to treat bound states, resonances, and scattering in light nuclei. Calculations were performed for the halo nuclei 6He, 8B, 11Be, 15C, and 10Be using chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces as the sole input. For 15C, the radiative capture reaction 14C(n,gamma)15C was also computed within the same framework.

## Key numbers
- 6He: described as a Borromean n-n-4He system, meaning no binary subsystems are bound (the di-neutron and n-4He are unbound).
- 15C: produced in the capture reaction 14C(n,gamma)15C, with the NCSMC calculation predicting the structure and dynamics of this weakly bound state.
- 10Be: excited states exhibiting a one-neutron halo structure were calculated.
- 11Li: a large-scale NCSM investigation was performed as a precursor to a full n-n-9Li NCSMC study.

## Context
This work extends the NCSMC approach previously developed by the same group (Navratil and Quaglioni, earlier works) to a broader set of halo nuclei, including Borromean systems like 6He and the neutron-rich 11Li. The calculations enable direct tests of chiral effective field theory interactions against experimental data for weakly bound and resonant states. The results for 15C and its capture reaction provide a pathway for ab initio predictions of astrophysical neutron-capture rates on light nuclei.
