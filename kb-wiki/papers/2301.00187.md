# Collective modes in the anisotropic collisional hot QCD medium at finite chemical potential

> We conducted a study on the collective modes within the hot QCD medium generated in heavy-ion collision experiments. These modes, whether real or imaginary, stable or unstable, play a crucial role in shaping the medium's evolution. To gain a deeper understanding, we considered several factors affecting the medium, including anisotropy, interactions among medium particles, and finite baryonic chemical potential. While the first two aspects have been thoroughly examined from various angles, the inclusion of finite chemical potential was previously overlooked. To provide a comprehensive analysis, we integrated these factors. The interactions among medium particles were accounted for using the BGK collisional kernel, while anisotropy and finite chemical potential were incorporated through the distribution functions of quarks, anti-quarks, and gluons. Our findings suggest that the presence of finite chemical potential amplifies the influence of unstable modes, potentially affecting the rapid thermalization of the hot QCD medium. Furthermore, exploring the implications of finite chemical potential in conjunction with other aspects of the created medium is intriguing, particularly in the context of low-energy heavy-ion collision experiments conducted at low temperatures and finite baryon density.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a finite baryonic chemical potential amplifies the influence of unstable collective modes in an anisotropic, collisional hot QCD medium, potentially affecting rapid thermalization. This claim is hedged by the condition that the analysis is performed in the small anisotropy limit (ξ < 1) and at a fixed temperature of 1.5 T_c, with the collision frequency set to ν = 0.3 ω_p.

## Method

The gluon polarization tensor was derived from the linearized Boltzmann transport equation using the BGK collisional kernel to account for medium particle collisions, with momentum anisotropy introduced by rescaling the isotropic distribution function along a direction n̂, and finite chemical potential incorporated through the equilibrium distribution functions of quarks, anti-quarks, and gluons. The gluon propagator was then constructed from the Yang-Mills equation in temporal gauge, and the dispersion relations for collective modes were obtained from the poles of this propagator, yielding three dispersion equations (A-mode, G1-mode, and G2-mode). The structure functions α, β, γ, and δ of the gluon self-energy were computed analytically up to linear order in the anisotropy parameter ξ.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass squared: m_D² = (2 α_s(μ,T) / 3π) (π² T² (2 N_c + N_f) + 3 μ² N_f), where N_c = 3 and N_f is the number of flavors.
- Temperature fixed at T = 1.5 T_c with T_c = 0.155 GeV.
- Collision frequency set to ν = 0.3 ω_p, where ω_p = m_D(μ=0)/√3.
- Anisotropy strength set to ξ = 0.3 for the main analysis.
- Angular dependence set to θ_n = π/6 between wave vector k and anisotropy direction n̂.
- Chemical potential values considered in the range 0 < μ < 1 GeV.
- For the k_max analysis, anisotropy strength was increased to ξ = 0.6 to observe survival of unstable modes; at ν = 0.3 ω_p, k_max is almost completely suppressed.
- The magnitude of structure functions γ and δ is almost one order less than α and β at different values of μ.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of collective modes in anisotropic QCD plasma by Romatschke (2003, 2004) and Schenke (2006) that considered anisotropy and collisions but omitted finite chemical potential. It also builds on the BGK collisional kernel approach used by Jiang (2016) and Carrington (2004). The inclusion of finite baryon chemical potential is motivated by low-energy heavy-ion collision experiments at facilities such as SPS at CERN, FAIR in Darmstadt, NICA in Dubna, and J-PARC in Tokai, where the QCD medium is created at moderate temperatures and finite baryon density. The results enable future studies incorporating a non-local BGK kernel, the non-abelian term of the field strength tensor, and the group velocity of these collective modes.
