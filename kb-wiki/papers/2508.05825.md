# Correlation functions for $n\,\bar{D}_{s1}(2460)$ and $n\,\bar{D}_{s1}(2536)$

> We study the interaction of the $n \bar {D}_{s1}(2460)$ and $n \bar {D}_{s1}(2536)$ systems from the perspective that the $D_{s1}(2460)$ and $D_{s1}(2536)$ states are molecular states of $K D^*$ and $K^*D$, respectively. We use an improved version of the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equations which fulfills exact elastic unitarity at the threshold of the systems. We predict the existence of resonant states below threshold and also determine the scattering length, effective range and correlation functions of these systems, showing that the results contain important information to test the assumed molecular nature of the two $D_{s1}$ states.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of resonant states below the thresholds of the \(n\bar{D}_{s1}(2460)\) and \(n\bar{D}_{s1}(2536)\) systems, but only under the assumption that the \(D_{s1}(2460)\) and \(D_{s1}(2536)\) mesons themselves are molecular states composed of \(K D^*\) and \(K^* D\), respectively.

## Method

The authors use an improved version of the fixed center approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations, which exactly fulfills elastic unitarity at the threshold of the systems. They model the \(n\bar{D}_{s1}(2460)\) system by considering the neutron scattering successively with the \(\bar{D}^*\) and \(\bar{K}\) constituents of the \(\bar{D}_{s1}(2460)\) cluster, and similarly for the \(n\bar{D}_{s1}(2536)\) system with \(\bar{D}\) and \(\bar{K}^*\) constituents. The two-body interactions \(n\bar{D}^*\), \(n\bar{K}\), \(n\bar{D}\), and \(n\bar{K}^*\) are described using chiral unitary approaches, and the full three-body amplitude is obtained by solving coupled equations that include elastic propagation of the neutron and the cluster.

## Key numbers

*   For the \(n\bar{D}_{s1}(2460)\) system: a resonant state is found at a mass around 3265 MeV, about 130 MeV below the \(n\bar{D}_{s1}(2460)\) threshold (3399 MeV), with a width of about 90 MeV.
*   For the \(n\bar{D}_{s1}(2536)\) system: a resonant state is found at a mass around 3426 MeV, about 50 MeV below the \(n\bar{D}_{s1}(2536)\) threshold (3475 MeV), with a width of about 60 MeV.
*   Scattering length for \(n\bar{D}_{s1}(2460)\): \(a = (0.59 - i0.21)\) fm.
*   Effective range for \(n\bar{D}_{s1}(2460)\): \(r_0 = (0.65 - i0.16)\) fm.
*   Scattering length for \(n\bar{D}_{s1}(2536)\): \(a = (0.71 - i0.18)\) fm.
*   Effective range for \(n\bar{D}_{s1}(2536)\): \(r_0 = (0.16 + i0.32)\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the line of research initiated by Encarnacion et al. (2025) on the \(p f_1(1285)\) correlation function and by Ikeno et al. (2025) on the \(n\bar{D}_{s0}(2317)\) system, where the improved unitary FCA was first developed. The present study applies this formalism to the \(n\bar{D}_{s1}(2460)\) and \(n\bar{D}_{s1}(2536)\) systems, assuming the molecular nature of the \(D_{s1}\) states as advocated by Gamermann et al. (2007) and supported by lattice QCD calculations. The predicted correlation functions and scattering parameters provide a testable signature that can distinguish the molecular interpretation of these resonances from alternative structures (e.g., tetraquarks or ordinary \(q\bar{q}\) states) in future femtoscopic measurements at facilities like ALICE.
