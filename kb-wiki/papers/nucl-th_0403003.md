# Isoscaling in the Lattice Gas Model

> The isoscaling behavior is investigated using the isotopic/isobaric yields from the equilibrated thermal source which is prepared by the lattice gas model for lighter systems with A = 36. The isoscaling parameters $Оұ$ and -$ОІ$ are observed to drop with temperature. The difference of neutron and proton chemical potential shows a turning point around 5 MeV where the liquid gas phase transition occurs in the model. The relative free neutron or proton density shows a nearly linear relation with the N/Z (neutron to proton ratio) of system and the isospin fractionation is observed.

## Key claim

Isoscaling parameters Оұ and ОІ drop with temperature only when the lattice gas model uses an asymmetric nucleon-nucleon potential (different interaction strength for like-pairs versus unlike-pairs); when a symmetric potential is used, the temperature dependence vanishes and the absolute values of Оұ and ОІ become smaller.

## Method

The authors performed lattice gas model (LGM) simulations for four isotonic sources with A = 36 (ВівҒ¶Ca, ВівҒ¶Ar, ВівҒ¶S, ВівҒ¶Si, covering N/Z = 0.8 to 1.57) at a freeze-out density of 0.563 ПҒвӮҖ on a 4Ві cubic lattice. They extracted isoscaling parameters Оұ and ОІ from the ratios of fragment yields (Z вүӨ 8, 2 вүӨ N вүӨ 9) for pairs of sources at various temperatures, using both an isospin-dependent potential (isoLGM: ОөвӮҡвӮҷ = вҲ’5.33 MeV, ОөвӮҷвӮҷ = ОөвӮҡвӮҡ = 0) and an isospin-independent potential (noisoLGM: all Оө = вҲ’5.33 MeV). The relative free neutron and proton densities were then deduced from the scaling parameters.

## Key numbers

- Four isotonic sources with A = 36 were simulated: ВівҒ¶Ca (N/Z = 0.8), ВівҒ¶Ar (N/Z = 1.0), ВівҒ¶S (N/Z = 1.25), and ВівҒ¶Si (N/Z = 1.57).
- Freeze-out density was set to ПҒ_f = 0.563 ПҒвӮҖ, corresponding to a 4Ві cubic lattice.
- The interaction strength for unlike nucleons was ОөвӮҡвӮҷ = вҲ’5.33 MeV in both potentials; for the isoLGM case, ОөвӮҷвӮҷ = ОөвӮҡвӮҡ = 0 MeV.
- A turning point in (О”ОјвӮҷ вҲ’ О”ОјвӮҡ)/2 was observed around T = 5 MeV, coinciding with the liquid-gas phase transition temperature for A вҲј 36 systems.
- For the isoLGM case at T = 5 MeV, the ratio ПҒвӮҷ/ПҒвӮҡ for neutron-rich sources (ВівҒ¶S, ВівҒ¶Si) was much larger than the initial homogeneous value, while for the proton-rich source (ВівҒ¶Ca) it was much smaller.

## Context

This work extends the isoscaling studies of Tsang et al. (2001) and the statistical multifragmentation model calculations of Botvina et al. (2002) to lighter systems (A = 36) using the lattice gas model. It connects the temperature dependence of isoscaling parameters to the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, complementing experimental observations from the TAMU-NIMROD data on A вҲј 36 disassembly (Ma et al., 2004) and the peripheral collision data of Veselsky et al. (2004). The results suggest that the temperature dependence of isoscaling parameters can be used to extract information on the asymmetric nucleon-nucleon potential and the nuclear equation of state, while the observed isospin fractionation provides a signal for chemical phase separation in asymmetric systems.
