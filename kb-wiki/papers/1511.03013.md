# Magnetic moments in odd-A Cd isotopes and coupling of particles with zero-point vibrations

> Background: The coupling of the last nucleon with configurations in the ground state of the even-even core is known to augment the single quasiparticle fragmentation pattern. In a recent experimental study by Yordanov \emph{et al.} the values of the magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments of the $11/2^-$ state in a long chain of Cd isotopes were found to follow a simple trend which we try to explain by means of incorporating long-range correlations in the ground state. Purpose: Our purpose is to study the influence of the ground-state correlations (GSC) on the magnetic moments and compare our results with the data for the odd-A Cd isotopes. Method: In order to evaluate if the additional correlations have bearing on the magnetic moments we employ an extension to the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) which takes into account quasiparticle$\otimes$phonon configurations in the ground state of the even-even core to the structure of the odd-A nucleus wave function. Results: It is shown that the values for the magnetic moments which the applied QPM extension yields deviate further from the Schmidt values. The latter is in agreement with the measured values for the Cd isotopes. Conclusions: The GSC exert significant influence on the magnetic dipole moments and reveal a potential for reproducing the experimental values for the studied cadmium isotopes.

## Key claim

The coupling of the last nucleon with quasiparticle⊗phonon configurations in the ground state of the even-even core (ground-state correlations, GSC) significantly affects the magnetic dipole moments of odd-A Cd isotopes, and including these correlations allows reproduction of experimental values that cannot be matched when such correlations are neglected. However, the calculations exhibit very high sensitivity to interaction parameters, which limits predictive power.

## Method

The authors employed an extension of the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) that includes backward-going amplitudes in the odd-A nucleus wave function, allowing nonzero probabilities for the last quasiparticle to interact with quasiparticle and quasiparticle⊗phonon configurations residing in the ground state of the even-even core. The Hamiltonian included mean-field (Woods-Saxon potential), monopole pairing, and separable multipole and spin-multipole residual interactions, with strengths fitted to experimental data. The equation-of-motion method was used to incorporate ground-state correlations beyond the phonon vacuum, with average values evaluated using the random phase approximation (RPA) with corrections for Pauli principle effects.

## Key numbers

- The interaction strength between the ν1h11/2 quasiparticle and the ν1h11/2⊗2+1 configuration varies between -1.5 MeV and -2.5 MeV along the Cd isotope chain (from 117Cd to 127Cd).
- The backward amplitude φJjλi for annihilating the two-quasineutron state [ν1h11/2⊗ν1h11/2]2 in the ground state lies between 0.3 and 0.5 across different isotopes.
- The pairing occupation factor v−ν1h11/2,ν1h11/2 drops from 0.4619 in 117Cd through 0.05 in 121Cd to -0.530 in 127Cd.
- The quantity v−ν1h11/2,ν2f7/2 attenuates from 0.61 in 121Cd to 0.36 in 127Cd.
- The dominant 1+ state contributing to the 11/2- state structure has an energy in the region of 13 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle-phonon model framework of Soloviev (1992) by incorporating ground-state correlations that were not included in earlier QPM treatments. It builds on the experimental study by Yordanov et al. (2013), which measured magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments of the 11/2- state in a long chain of Cd isotopes. The approach differs from earlier studies by Levon (1986) by explicitly including backward-going amplitudes, and it addresses limitations of the seniority model used by Yordanov et al. (2013) and the BCS approximation employed by Zhao (2014). The results enable future work using Extended RPA or variational methods for the ground state to overcome the inconsistency where the fitted interaction parameters produce less collective 2+1 states in even-even cores than experimental data indicate.
