# Production and Equilibration of the Quark-Gluon Plasma with Chromoelectric Field and Minijets

> Production and equilibration of quark-gluon plasma are studied within the color flux-tube model, at the RHIC and LHC energies. Non-Abelian relativistic transport equations for quarks, antiquarks and gluons, are solved in the extended phase space which includes coordinates, momenta and color. Before the chromoelectric field is formed, hard and semihard partons are produced via minijets which provide the initial conditions necessary to solve the transport equations. The model predicts that in spite of the vast difference between the RHIC and LHC incident energies, once the local equilibrium is reached, the energy densities, the number densities and the temperatures at the two machines may not be very different from each other. The minijet input significantly alters the evolution of the deconfined matter, unless the color field is too strong. For the input parameters used here the equilibration time is estimated to be $\sim 1$ fm at RHIC and $\sim 0.5$ fm at LHC, measured from the instant when the two colliding nuclei have just passed through each other. The temperature at equilibration is found to be $\sim 250$ MeV at RHIC and $\sim 300$ MeV at LHC.

## Key claim

The model predicts that despite the vast difference between RHIC and LHC incident energies, once local equilibrium is reached, the energy densities, number densities, and temperatures at the two machines may not be very different from each other, because the memory of the minijet input decays exponentially with time.

## Method

The authors solved non-Abelian relativistic transport equations for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons in an extended phase space (coordinates, momenta, and color) within the color flux-tube model. Hard and semihard partons (minijets) were calculated using perturbative QCD with a minimum transverse momentum of 2 GeV and a K-factor of 2, providing initial distribution functions at the instant the nuclei pass through each other; soft partons were then produced from the chromoelectric field via the Schwinger-like mechanism. The transport equations included convective flow, background field acceleration, color precession (Wong's equation), collision terms in the relaxation-time approximation (relaxation time 0.2 fm), and source terms, and were solved self-consistently with the Yang-Mills equations for the field evolution.

## Key numbers

- Equilibration time at RHIC: approximately 1 fm, measured from the instant the two colliding nuclei have just passed through each other.
- Equilibration time at LHC: approximately 0.5 fm, measured from the instant the two colliding nuclei have just passed through each other.
- Temperature at equilibration at RHIC: approximately 250 MeV.
- Temperature at equilibration at LHC: approximately 300 MeV.
- Initial minijet energy density at RHIC: approximately 3 GeV/fm^3.
- Initial minijet energy density at LHC: approximately 178 GeV/fm^3.
- Maximum parton number density at LHC: approximately 120 fm^{-3}; at RHIC: approximately 90 fm^{-3}.
- For the LHC energy, for tau >= 0.5 fm, the fitted exponents b for energy density, number density, and temperature were 1.23, 0.70, and 0.31 respectively (Bjorken hydrodynamics values: 1.33, 1, and 0.33).
- Initial field energy density used: either 0 or 300 GeV/fm^3.

## Context

This work extends the color flux-tube model studies of Bhalerao, Nayak, and collaborators (1998-1999) by adding minijet production via perturbative QCD, which was previously ignored in pre-equilibrium evolution calculations. It contrasts with the Parton Cascade Model of Geiger and Muller (1995) and the HIJING model of Wang et al. (1997), which neglect the background chromoelectric field entirely, and with the hydrodynamic approach of Eskola and Gyulassy (1999) which applies only after local equilibrium. The results enable more realistic initial conditions for hydrodynamic expansion and predictions for QGP signatures such as J/psi suppression, strangeness enhancement, and dilepton/photon production at RHIC and LHC.
