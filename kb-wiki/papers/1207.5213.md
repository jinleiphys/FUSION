# Schwinger Pair Production in Pulsed Electric Fields

> We numerically investigate the temporal behavior and the structure of longitudinal momentum spectrum and the field polarity effect on pair production in pulsed electric fields in scalar quantum electrodynamics (QED). Using the evolution operator expressed in terms of the particle and antiparticle operators, we find the exact quantum states under the influence of electric pulses and measure the number of pairs of the Minkowski particle and antiparticle. The number of pairs, depending on the configuration of electric pulses, exhibits rich structures in the longitudinal momentum spectrum and undergoes diverse dynamical behaviors at the onset of the interaction but always either converges to a momentum-dependent constant or oscillates around a momentum-dependent time average after the completion of fields.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the number of pairs produced by pulsed electric fields in scalar QED always either converges to a momentum-dependent constant or oscillates around a momentum-dependent time average after the fields complete, but this behavior depends on whether the gauge potential asymptotically vanishes or approaches a nonzero constant.

## Method

The authors employed an evolution operator formalism in scalar QED, expressing the Hamiltonian in terms of creation and annihilation operators of the Minkowski vacuum and representing the evolution operator in SU(1,1) algebra. They solved the time-dependent Schrodinger equation numerically for six different pulsed electric field configurations (mono-polarity and di-polarity types) to compute the exact quantum states evolved from the Minkowski vacuum and the number of produced pairs as a function of time and longitudinal momentum.

## Key numbers

- For the Sauter electric field with critical strength E0 = 1 and Compton time scale pulse tau = 1, the number of pairs is order of one and exhibits substructure in the longitudinal momentum spectrum.
- The longitudinal momentum spectrum for the Sauter field is symmetric around k_parallel = -1, where pairs are minimally produced.
- For the Gaussian electric field with E0 = 1 and tau = 1, the longitudinal momentum spectrum is symmetric around k_parallel = -sqrt(pi)/2, the channel for minimal pair production for small momentum.
- The Gaussian electric field produces relatively smaller pairs than the Sauter electric field for the same parameters E0 = 1 and tau = 1.

## Context

This work extends the numerical study of Schwinger pair production beyond the adiabatic basis approaches used in earlier kinetic approaches (SGD08, HAG08, HADG09) and Wigner formalism (HAG10, OHA11, HAG11). The evolution operator formalism confirms the substructure of the longitudinal momentum spectrum previously discovered by Hebenstreit et al (2009) in sinusoidal fields with Gaussian envelope and explained by Dumlu and Dunne (2010, 2011) via the Stokes phenomenon. The results enable direct computation of pair production for arbitrary pulsed field configurations and clarify the distinction between Minkowski and adiabatic particle counting.
