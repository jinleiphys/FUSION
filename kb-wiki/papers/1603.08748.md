# Decay width of $d^*(2380)\to NN 蟺蟺$ processes

> The decay widths of four-body double-pion decays $\ds\to pn 蟺^0蟺^0$, $\ds\to pn 蟺^+蟺^-$, and iso-scalar parts of $\ds\to pp 蟺^0蟺^-$ and $\ds\to nn 蟺^+蟺^0$ are explicitly calculated with the help of the $d^*$ wave function obtained in a chiral SU(3) quark model calculation. The effect of the dynamical structure on $\ds$'s width is analyzed both in the single $螖螖$ channel and coupled $螖螖$ and $CC$ channel approximations. It is found that in the coupled-channel approximation, the obtained partial decay widths of $\ds\to pn 蟺^0蟺^0$, $\ds\to pn 蟺^+蟺^-$, and those of $d^*$ to the iso-scalar parts of $pp 蟺^0蟺^-$ and $nn 蟺^+蟺^0$ are about $7.4$MeV, $16.4$MeV, $3.5$MeV and $3.5$MeV, respectively As a consequence, the total width is about $64.5$MeV. These widths are consistent with those estimated by using the corresponding cross section data in our previous investigation and also the observed data. But in the single $螖螖$ channel approximation, the widths are still almost 2-times larger than the measured values. Apparently, the explicitly calculated width together with the evaluated mass of $d^*$ in the coupled $螖螖$ and $CC$ channel approximation can well explain the observed data, which again supports our assertion that the $\ds$ resonance is a six-quark dominated exotic state.

## Key claim

The authors explicitly calculate the four-body double-pion decay widths of the $d^*(2380)$ resonance and find that the coupled $\Delta\Delta$ and hidden-color (CC) channel approximation yields partial widths consistent with experimental data, whereas the single $\Delta\Delta$ channel approximation gives widths almost 2-times larger than measured values.

## Method

The decay widths are calculated using the $d^*$ wave function obtained from a chiral SU(3) quark model within the Resonating Group Method (RGM). The transition matrix elements for the four-body decays are computed using a non-relativistic effective Hamiltonian for the quark-pion interaction, with the coupling constant $g_{qq\pi}$ extracted from the measured $\Delta \to \pi N$ decay width. The calculation is performed both in the single $\Delta\Delta$ channel approximation and in the coupled $\Delta\Delta$ and CC channel approximation, where the $d^*$ wave function contains 31.5% $\Delta\Delta$ component and 68.5% CC component.

## Key numbers

- Partial decay width of $d^* \to pn \pi^0\pi^0$: 7.4 MeV in the coupled-channel approximation (Case II), compared to 15.2 MeV in the single $\Delta\Delta$ channel (Case I)
- Partial decay width of $d^* \to pn \pi^+\pi^-$: 16.4 MeV in the coupled-channel approximation, compared to 33.5 MeV in the single $\Delta\Delta$ channel
- Partial decay widths of $d^* \to pp \pi^0\pi^-$ and $d^* \to nn \pi^+\pi^0$: 3.5 MeV each in the coupled-channel approximation
- Total width of $d^*$: 64.5 MeV in the coupled-channel approximation, compared to 119.1 MeV in the single $\Delta\Delta$ channel and the experimental value of 74.9 MeV
- Ratio $R = \Gamma(d^* \to pn \pi^+\pi^-) / \Gamma(d^* \to pn \pi^0\pi^0)$: 2.22 in the coupled-channel approximation
- Binding energy of $d^*$: approximately 62 MeV in the single $\Delta\Delta$ channel and approximately 84 MeV in the coupled $\Delta\Delta$ and CC channel

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of three-body decays $d^* \to d \pi\pi$ (Dong et al., 2015) to the four-body decays $d^* \to NN \pi\pi$, which were not explicitly computed before. The results support the interpretation that the $d^*$ resonance is a six-quark dominated exotic state with a dominant hidden-color (CC) configuration, consistent with the argument by Bashkanov, Brodsky, and Clement (2013) that a hidden-color structure is necessary for understanding its strong coupling. The calculation contradicts the single $\Delta\Delta$ resonance interpretation of Wang et al. (2011), which gave a width of about 150 MeV, and provides a unified explanation of the mass and width data from the CELSIUS/WASA and WASA@COSY Collaborations.
