# Generalized Richardson-Gaudin Nuclear Models

> The exact solvability of several nuclear models with non-degenerate single-particle energies is outlined and leads to a generalization of integrable Richardson-Gaudin models, like the $su(2)$-based fermion pairing, to any simple Lie algebra. As an example, the $so(5)\sim sp(4)$ model of T=1 pairing is discussed and illustrated for the case of $^{64}$Ge with non-degenerate single-particle energies.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes integrable Richardson-Gaudin models, previously limited to the $su(2)$ pairing case, to any simple Lie algebra, thereby allowing exact solutions for nuclear models with non-degenerate single-particle energies that break the dynamical symmetry but preserve integrability. The authors explicitly note that a prior claimed exact solution for the $so(5)$ proton-neutron isovector pairing model by Richardson was shown to be incorrect by Pan (2002).

## Method

The authors construct a set of commuting operators (integrals of motion) $R_i$ for an arbitrary simple Lie algebra $\mathcal{L}$, where each copy $i$ of the algebra has fixed parameters $z_i$ related to single-particle energies. The eigenvalues of these operators are given by generalized Richardson equations involving spectral parameters $e_{a,\alpha}$, and any linear combination of the $R_i$ yields an integrable Hamiltonian. As a concrete example, the formalism is applied to the $so(5) \sim sp(4)$ model of $T=1$ isovector pairing, and the resulting generalized Richardson equations are solved numerically for $^{64}$Ge with four protons and four neutrons, using single-particle energies from the $pf$ shell.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle energies (in MeV) used for $^{64}$Ge: $\epsilon_{3/2}=0.00$, $\epsilon_{5/2}=0.77$, $\epsilon_{1/2}=1.11$, and $\epsilon_{9/2}=3.00$.
- Two pn-pairing strengths were used: $g=0.1$ (weak) and $g=0.37$ (strong).
- The ground state of $^{64}$Ge corresponds to isospin $T=0$; excited states with $T=1$ and $T=2$ were also computed.
- For the $T=1$ state, a critical point occurs at $g_{\rm c}=0.326$ MeV where three $w$ parameters approach a real $e$ pairon at $-1.08$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the integrable Richardson-Gaudin models, originally formulated for $su(2)$ fermion pairing by Richardson (1963) and later generalized by Dukelsky (2001), to the full range of Lie algebras underlying nuclear dynamical-symmetry models, including Elliott's $su(3)$ model (1958), Flowers' $so(5)$ $T=1$ pairing model (1952), and the $so(8)$ model of isoscalar and isovector pairing (1964, 1981). The generalized formalism enables exact nuclear structure calculations with non-degenerate single-particle energies for higher-rank algebras like $sp(6)$ and $so(8)$, and could also be applied to $so(5)$ models of high-temperature superconductivity in condensed-matter physics.
