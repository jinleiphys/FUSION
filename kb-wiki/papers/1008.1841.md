# Hadron production in p+p, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions with the HIJING 2.0 model at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> The HIJING (Heavy-ion Jet Interaction Generator) Monte Carlo model is updated with the latest parton distributions functions (PDF) and new set of the parameters in the two-component mini-jet model that controls total $p+p$ cross section and the central pseudorapity density. We study hadron spectra and multiplicity distributions using the HIJING 2.0 model and compare to recent experimental data from $p+p$ collisions at the LHC energies. We also give predictions of hadron production in $p+p$, $p+Pb$ and $Pb+Pb$ collisions at the full LHC energy.

## Key claim

The HIJING 2.0 model, updated with modern parton distribution functions and energy-dependent parameters, can describe hadron production in p+p collisions at LHC energies, but the agreement depends on the event trigger definition and the model currently lacks double-diffractive events needed to match INEL>0 data.

## Method

The authors updated the HIJING Monte Carlo model by replacing the Duke-Owens parton distribution functions with the Gluck-Reya-Vogt (GRV) parameterization and introducing energy-dependent forms for the transverse momentum cut-off p0 and the soft parton cross section σsoft, fitted to total and inelastic cross sections and central rapidity density data from p+p/p̅p collisions. They then simulated hadron spectra and multiplicity distributions in p+p, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, incorporating nuclear shadowing via impact-parameter-dependent parton distribution functions with a gluon shadowing parameter sg constrained by RHIC data.

## Key numbers

- The energy-dependent cut-off p0 is parameterized as: p0 = 2.62 − 1.084 log(√s) + 0.299 log²(√s) − 0.0292 log³(√s) + 0.00151 log⁴(√s), where √s is in GeV.
- The soft parton cross section σsoft is parameterized as: σsoft = 55.316 − 4.1126 log(√s) + 0.854 log²(√s) − 0.0307 log³(√s) + 0.00328 log⁴(√s), in mb.
- In HIJING 1.0, a constant cut-off p0 = 2 GeV/c and σsoft = 57 mb were used.
- HIJING 2.0 results for charged hadron transverse momentum spectra at LHC energies show a maximum discrepancy of 50% with data at small pT ∼ 2 GeV.
- The gluon shadowing parameter sg is chosen in the range 0.17–0.22 to fit RHIC data, leading to larger uncertainties in multiplicity density at LHC energies.
- Predictions are given for p+p collisions at √s = 7 and 14 TeV, and for Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.75 and 5.5 TeV/n.

## Context

This work extends the HIJING model (Wang 1991, Gyulassy 1994) by updating the parton distribution functions from the outdated Duke-Owens parameterization to the GRV parameterization (Gluck 1994), which is necessary for the small-x region probed at LHC energies. The energy-dependent cut-off p0 and σsoft replace the constant values used in HIJING 1.0, and the increasing cut-off is interpreted as indirect evidence of gluon saturation. The model enables predictions for LHC heavy-ion collisions and can be compared to other approaches such as the color glass condensate or limiting fragmentation models (Abreu 2007), with the next upgrade planned to incorporate modern jet quenching theory (Gyulassy 1993, Baier 1996, Wiedemann 2000).
