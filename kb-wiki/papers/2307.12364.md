# Generalised description of Neutron Star matter with nucleonic Relativistic Density Functional

> In this work, we propose a meta-modelling technique to nuclear matter on the basis of a relativistic density functional with density-dependent couplings. Identical density dependence for the couplings both in the isoscalar and isovector sectors is employed. We vary the coupling parameters of the model to capture the uncertainties of the empirical nuclear matter parameters at saturation. Then, we construct a large ensemble of unified equations of state in a consistent manner both for clusterized and uniform matter in $β$-equilibrium at zero temperature. Finally, we calculate neutron star properties to check the consistency with astrophysical observations within a Bayesian framework. Out of the different sets of astrophysical data employed, constraint on tidal deformability from the GW170817 event was found to be the most stringent in the posteriors of different neutron star properties explored in the present study. We demonstrate in detail the impact of the isovector incompressibility ($K_{sym}$) on high-density matter that leads to a considerable variation in the composition of neutron star matter. A couple of selected models with extreme values of $K_{sym}$, which satisfy various modern nuclear physics and neutron star astrophysics constraints, are uploaded in the \textsc{CompOSE} \cite{Typel:2013rza} database for use by the community.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a relativistic meta-modelling technique for nuclear matter based on a density-dependent coupling Lagrangian, which allows exploration of a wider range of isovector nuclear matter parameters, particularly positive values of the isovector incompressibility ($K_{sym}$), than previous relativistic models, while the abstract's claim of capturing uncertainties is qualified by the finding that present astrophysical constraints are not very effective in pinning down the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic energy density functional with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings (sigma, omega, rho) using the GDFM functional form for the density dependence, with 12 independent parameters sampled uniformly. They generate a large ensemble of unified equations of state by combining a high-density core from the relativistic model with a low-density crust from the compressible liquid drop model, using consistent first- and second-order nuclear matter parameters. The models are filtered through low-density constraints from chiral effective field theory calculations and the AME2016 mass table, then through high-density astrophysical constraints including the mass of PSR J0348+0432 and the tidal deformability from GW170817 within a Bayesian framework.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density range explored: 0.135 to 0.195 fm$^{-3}$
- Saturation energy range: -17 to -14 MeV
- Incompressibility $K_{sat}$ range: 150 to 350 MeV
- Symmetry energy $E_{sym}$ range: 20 to 45 MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy $L_{sym}$ range: 20 to 180 MeV
- Two example models: Model I with $K_{sym} = -156$ MeV and Model II with $K_{sym} = 158$ MeV, both producing maximum neutron star masses of 2.31 $M_\odot$ and 2.30 $M_\odot$ respectively
- Only approximately 0.43% of the 6.5 million prior models were retained after all filters
- The tidal deformability for high-mass neutron stars can differ by up to a factor of 2-3 between models with extreme $K_{sym}$ values

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic nucleonic meta-modelling approach of Margueron (2017) to a relativistic formulation, addressing the limitation that causality had to be imposed a posteriori in the earlier work. It contrasts with recent Bayesian optimizations of relativistic density functionals by Malik (2022, 2023) and Beznogov (2022) that sought best-fit models, instead aiming to thoroughly explore the parameter space of nuclear matter parameters. The study enables future extensions to include non-nucleonic degrees of freedom (hyperons, mesons) consistently within the relativistic framework, and provides selected equation of state models uploaded to the CompOSE database for community use.
