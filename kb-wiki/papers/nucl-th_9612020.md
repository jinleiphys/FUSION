# Feedback from Freeze-out in Hydrodynamics

> Most hydrodynamical calculations used in heavy-ion physics ignore the effect of freeze-out matter carrying energy and momentum away from the expanding fluid. In a simple one-dimensional model we compare calculated energy density and velocity profiles, with and without interaction between fluid-like and freeze-out parts of the system, in order to estimate the importance of this effect.

# Feedback from Freeze-out in Hydrodynamics

## Key claim

In a simple one-dimensional model with massless pions, including the feedback from freeze-out matter (which carries energy and momentum away from the expanding fluid) significantly alters the trajectory of the freeze-out surface compared to traditional hydrodynamical calculations that ignore this coupling; the effect produces a more rapid "eating into" the thermalized fluid and larger fluctuations in energy density, though the authors note these fluctuations may partly arise from their simplifying assumptions about velocity distributions.

## Method

The authors extended the Godunov-type (HLLE) hydrodynamical method to handle three types of cell interfaces: between two thermalized fluid cells, between two freeze-out cells, and between one cell of each kind. For freeze-out matter, they parametrized the velocity distribution using three moments of the energy-momentum tensor (energy density E, momentum density M, and pressure P) rather than assuming an equation of state, and calculated flows by integrating over particle velocities crossing cell boundaries. They simulated a one-dimensional slab of thermalized massless pion gas initially at 200 MeV expanding into vacuum, with freeze-out set at 140 MeV, and compared results with and without the freeze-out coupling.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature: T₀ = 200 MeV for -2.7 fm ≤ x ≤ 2.7 fm, vacuum elsewhere
- Freeze-out temperature: T_fo = 140 MeV (corresponding to energy density e = 49 MeV/fm³)
- Timestep: δt = 0.03 fm; cell size: δx = 0.1 fm
- Most freeze-out matter moves outward at approximately 0.65c when feedback is included
- Energy density contours plotted from 5 to 70 MeV/fm³ in increments of 5 MeV/fm³

## Context

This work extends the traditional hydrodynamical approach used in heavy-ion physics, which typically assumes a sudden transition from fluid behavior to free-streaming at a freeze-out surface without accounting for the energy and momentum carried away by freeze-out particles. The authors build on analytical work by Bugaev (1995) on freeze-out surface trajectories and on the HLLE numerical method developed by Harten, Lax, van Leer, and Einfeldt. The generalized Godunov algorithm enables future applications to more complex geometries and equations of state, including three-fluid hydrodynamics and calculations of dilepton and photon spectra sensitive to freeze-out dynamics.
