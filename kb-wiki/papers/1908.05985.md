# One-proton and one-neutron knockout reactions from $N = Z = 28$ $^{56}$Ni to the $A = 55$ mirror pair $^{55}$Co and $^{55}$Ni

> We present a high-resolution in-beam $γ$-ray spectroscopy study of excited states in the mirror nuclei $^{55}$Co and $^{55}$Ni following one-nucleon knockout from a projectile beam of $^{56}$Ni. The newly determined partial cross sections and the $γ$-decay properties of excited states provide a test of state-of-the-art nuclear structure models and probe mirror symmetry in unique ways. A mirror asymmetry for the partial cross sections leading to the two lowest $3/2^-$ states in the $A = 55$ mirror pair was identified as well as a significant difference in the $E1$ decays from the $1/2^+_1$ state to the same two $3/2^-$ states. The mirror asymmetry in the partial cross sections cannot be reconciled with the present shell-model picture or small mixing introduced in a two-state model. The observed mirror asymmetry in the $E1$ decay pattern, however, points at stronger mixing between the two lowest $3/2^-$ states in $^{55}$Co than in its mirror $^{55}$Ni.

## Key claim

The paper reports a mirror asymmetry in the partial cross sections leading to the two lowest 3/2- states in the A=55 mirror pair 55Co and 55Ni, as well as a significant difference in the E1 decays from the 1/2+1 state to those same two 3/2- states; the cross-section asymmetry cannot be explained by the shell-model picture or small mixing in a two-state model, but the E1 decay pattern points at stronger mixing between the two lowest 3/2- states in 55Co than in its mirror 55Ni.

## Method

One-neutron and one-proton knockout reactions from a 56Ni secondary beam at approximately 85.9 MeV/u on a 188 mg/cm2 9Be target were used to populate excited states in 55Ni and 55Co, respectively. The reaction residues were identified event-by-event using the S800 spectrograph, and de-excitation gamma rays were detected with the SeGA array of 16 segmented HPGe detectors, enabling Doppler reconstruction. Partial cross sections to individual final states were extracted from gamma-ray yields using GEANT4 simulations, and the results were compared to large-scale shell-model calculations in the full pf space using the GXPF1A-cd-pn Hamiltonian combined with eikonal reaction theory.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive cross section for one-neutron knockout from 56Ni to all bound states of 55Ni: 38.0 +/- 0.2 (stat.) +/- 3.0 (sys.) mb
- Inclusive cross section for one-proton knockout from 56Ni to all bound states of 55Co: 126 +/- 2 (stat.) +/- 17 (sys.) mb
- Ground-state partial cross section in 55Ni: 29.1(7) mb (77(2)% of inclusive)
- Ground-state partial cross section in 55Co: 80(5) mb (63(4)% of inclusive)
- Phase-space corrected R(E1)3/2-2/3/2-1 ratios: 2.69(14) in 55Co and 3.9(3) in 55Ni
- Reduced B(E1; 1/2+ -> 3/2-1) transition strength in 55Co: 17.2(+1.2/-13.5) mW.u.
- Reduced B(E1; 1/2+ -> 3/2-2) transition strength in 55Co: 46(+4/-36) mW.u.
- Partial cross section for the (9/2-,11/2-) doublet: 1.42(10) mb (3.7(3)%) in 55Ni and 8.2(3) mb (6.5(2)%) in 55Co
- Ratio of spectroscopic factors C2S(3/2-1)/C2S(3/2-2) = 1.54(22) from 54Fe to 55Co transfer reactions

## Context

This work extends previous studies of mirror energy differences (MED) in the pf shell, such as those by Bentley (2007, 2015), and mirrored knockout reactions on mirror projectiles like those by Davies (2013) on (52Ni,52Cr) and Milne (2016) on (53Ni,53Mn). The use of a self-conjugate 56Ni projectile allows direct comparison of one-proton and one-neutron knockout to the mirror pair, enabling tests of isospin symmetry near the doubly-magic N=Z=28 nucleus. The results provide experimental constraints for ab-initio-type calculations that are becoming feasible for these nuclei, as noted by Hagen (2016) and Hergert (2017), and reveal unexpected mirror asymmetries that challenge current shell-model descriptions.
