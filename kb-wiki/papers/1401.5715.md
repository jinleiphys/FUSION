# Degree of Chemical Non-equilibrium in Central Au-Au Collisions at RHIC energies

> We investigate the difference between hadron resonance gas (HRG) calculations for chemical freeze-out parameters at fully and partly chemical equilibria. To this end, the results are compared with the particle ratios measured in central Au-Au collisions at a wide range of nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energies, \hbox{$\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7-200 $GeV} as offered by the STAR experiment. We restrict the discussion to STAR, because of large statistics and overall homogeneity of STAR measurements (one detector) against previous experiments. We find that the matter produced at these energies is likely in fully chemical equilibrium, which is consistent with recent lattice QCD results. The possible improvements by partial chemical equilibrium ($γ_S\neq 1$) are very limited. We also discuss these results with the ones deduced from $ϕ/π^-$ and $Ω^-/π^-$ ratios. These hadron ratios are sensitive to the degree of chemical equilibrium. Accordingly, the conclusion that the matter produced reaches fully chemical equilibrium in central Au-Au at RHIC energies is confirmed.

## Key claim

The matter produced in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies (√sNN = 7.7–200 GeV) is likely in fully chemical equilibrium, with only very limited improvements possible by introducing a strangeness phase-space occupation factor γS ≠ 1; the authors note that the slight χ² improvements from partial chemical equilibrium cannot be seriously taken as proof that partial equilibrium is more suitable.

## Method

The authors performed χ² fits of hadron resonance gas (HRG) model calculations to 10–11 independent particle ratios measured by the STAR experiment in central Au-Au collisions at six beam energies (7.7, 11.5, 39, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV). They compared fits assuming fully chemical equilibrium (γS = 1, two free parameters: Tch and μb) with fits assuming partial chemical equilibrium (γS ≠ 1, three free parameters: Tch, μb, γS), using the same set of hadron resonances, branching ratios, and excluded-volume correction (single hard-core radius rm = rb = 0.3 fm) as in their previous work (Tawfik 2013). They also examined the ϕ/π⁻ and Ω⁻/π⁻ ratios separately as sensitive probes of chemical non-equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- At 200 GeV, fully chemical equilibrium fit: Tch = 159.5 MeV, μb = 27.5 MeV, χ²/dof = 9.244/9; partial equilibrium fit: Tch = 167 MeV, μb = 30 MeV, γS = 0.9, χ²/dof = 7.101/8 (improvement of only 0.139 in χ²/dof)
- At 62.4 GeV, partial equilibrium fit: Tch = 161.5 MeV, μb = 68.5 MeV, γS = 0.89, χ²/dof = 6.91/8 (improvement of 0.30 over fully equilibrium χ²/dof = 10.512/9)
- At 7.7 GeV, partial equilibrium fit: Tch = 144.5 MeV, μb = 413 MeV, γS = 1.12, χ²/dof = 12.148/7
- At 130, 39, and 11.5 GeV, the χ²/dof values for fully chemical equilibrium were actually lower than those for partial chemical equilibrium
- The freeze-out parameters from fully chemical equilibrium were more consistent with lattice QCD predictions than those from partial chemical equilibrium

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Tawfik 2013) which assumed fully chemical equilibrium, by explicitly testing whether introducing γS as a free parameter improves fits to STAR Beam Energy Scan data. The results contradict earlier expectations (Becattini 2001, Kampfer 2002) that γS should remain smaller than unity and increase toward unity with energy; instead, the authors find a non-monotonic energy dependence of γS, including a value above unity (1.12) at 7.7 GeV. The conclusion that central Au-Au collisions at RHIC reach full chemical equilibrium is consistent with lattice QCD results and with previous studies (Manninen 2008, Kampfer 2002, Kaneta 2005) at 200 and 130 GeV. The work enables future studies to focus on fully chemical equilibrium HRG fits for central collisions, reserving γS for peripheral or elementary collisions where strangeness suppression is observed.
