# Stability windows at finite temperature

> The assumption underlying the existence of quark stars is based on the Bodmer-Witten conjecture. These authors have claimed that it is possible that the interior of a neutron-like star does not consist primarily of hadrons, but rather of the strange matter (SM). Strange matter is composed of deconfined quarks, including up, down and strange quarks, plus the leptons necessary to ensure charge neutrality and β-equilibrium. This possibility arises because a phase transition from hadronic to quark phase is possible at densities present in the interior of neutron stars. It has been argued that strange matter is the true ground state of all matter. If this is the case, as soon as the core of the star converts to the quark phase, the entire star converts. SM was first considered in calculations obtained within the MIT bag model framework. More sophisticated treatments for SM, based on the quark-mass density dependent model is considered in this work. We discuss next the following models for proto-quark stars: the QMDD model, and the MIT bag model.

# Stability windows at finite temperature

## Key claim
The authors argue that for proto-quark stars at finite temperature, the correct quantity to analyze for stability conditions is the free energy per baryon rather than the binding energy per baryon used at zero temperature, and that this choice leads to a wider range of model parameters satisfying the strange matter stability hypothesis.

## Method
The authors compute stability windows for strange quark matter using two models: the MIT bag model (with fixed quark masses and a bag constant) and the quark-mass density dependent (QMDD) model (where effective quark masses depend on baryon density). They evaluate the free energy per baryon for two-flavor and three-flavor quark matter at temperatures up to 40 MeV, imposing the conditions that two-flavor matter must be unstable (free energy per baryon above 930 MeV) and three-flavor strange matter must be stable (free energy per baryon below 930 MeV). For the MIT bag model, they also include the effect of a magnetic field of 7.2 × 10^18 G.

## Key numbers
- The stability condition threshold is 930 MeV, corresponding to the binding energy of iron (lead binding energy).
- Temperatures considered range up to 40 MeV, relevant for proto-neutron star evolution simulations.
- For the MIT bag model with magnetic field effects, a field strength of 7.2 × 10^18 G was applied.
- For the QMDD model, strange quark masses of 100 MeV and 150 MeV were used; a larger s quark mass yields a smaller range of possible parameters satisfying stability conditions.

## Context
This work extends the Bodmer-Witten conjecture (Bodmer, 1971; Witten, 1984) and prior stability window calculations at zero temperature by Lugones (1995) and Prakash (1996) to finite temperature systems relevant for proto-quark stars. The authors note that earlier finite-temperature studies by Chmaj (1989) and Lugones (1995) used binding energy per baryon, whereas the present work shows that using free energy per baryon shifts the stability windows to the right, allowing a wider range of constants. This enables more realistic modeling of proto-neutron star evolution and the possible formation of quark stars in the early stages of neutron star cooling.
