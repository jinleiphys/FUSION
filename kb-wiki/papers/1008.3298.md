# Relativistic Viscous Hydrodynamics for Multi-Component Systems with Multiple Conserved Currents

> We would like to formulate relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics for multi-component systems with multiple conserved currents. This is important for analyses of the hot matter created in relativistic heavy ion collisions because particle creations and annihilations of various particle species are frequently taking place there. We show that consistent formulation in such systems involves many non-trivialities, and derive constitutive equations that satisfy Onsager reciprocal relations and describe the systems without ambiguity.

# Relativistic Viscous Hydrodynamics for Multi-Component Systems with Multiple Conserved Currents

## Key claim
The authors derive second-order constitutive equations for relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics in multi-component systems with multiple conserved currents, showing that consistent formulation requires introducing new higher-moment equations for charge conservation that are absent in single-component Israel-Stewart theory. The derivation claims to satisfy Onsager reciprocal relations and to describe systems without ambiguity, but the constitutive equations contain many undetermined second-order transport coefficients whose values are not computed.

## Method
The authors generalize Grad's 14-moment method to systems with multiple conserved currents by expanding the phase space distribution function in terms of momentum moments that include both energy-momentum and charge-current degrees of freedom. They introduce higher-moment equations for both energy-momentum conservation and charge conservation, then determine the source terms from the law of increasing entropy. The resulting constitutive equations for bulk pressure, energy current, charge currents, and shear stress tensor are expressed as linear combinations of thermodynamic forces with first-order transport coefficients (bulk viscosity, energy conductivity, charge conductivity, shear viscosity) and second-order coefficients (relaxation times and coupling terms).

## Key numbers
- The system has 10+4N dissipative currents: bulk pressure Π, energy density distortion δe, energy current W^μ, shear stress tensor π^{μν}, type-J charge density distortion δn_J, and type-J charge current V_J^μ, where N is the number of conserved charges.
- There are 2+N equilibrium quantities: energy density e_0, hydrostatic pressure P_0, and type-J charge density n_{J0}.
- In ideal hydrodynamics, the number of unknowns is 5+N for systems with N conserved charges.
- The first-order transport coefficients include bulk viscosity ζ, energy conductivity κ_{WW}, charge conductivity κ_{V_J V_J}, and shear viscosity η.

## Context
This work extends the Israel-Stewart formalism (Israel, 1979) for relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics from single-component systems to multi-component systems with multiple conserved currents, motivated by the need to describe quark-gluon plasma created in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The authors note that most conventional formalisms do not consider Onsager cross terms carefully, and that their approach can describe systems in an arbitrary frame unlike formalisms requiring a specific choice of energy or particle frame. The work enables future calculations of chemical diffusion effects (Soret and Dufour effects) in heavy ion collisions and provides a framework for comparing with alternative approaches such as those of Baier (2007), Tsumura (2009), and Betz (2009).
