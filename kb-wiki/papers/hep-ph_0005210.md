# The Quark-Antiquark Asymmetry of the Nucleon Sea from $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ Fragmentation

> We present a general analysis of the spin transfer for $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ production in deep-inelastic scattering of polarized charged leptons on the nucleon, and find that the pattern of different behaviors of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ production observed by the E665 Collaboration suggests the possibility of quark-antiquark asymmetries either in the quark to $Λ$ fragmentation functions and/or in the quark and antiquark distributions of the target proton. We also point out that the strange-antistrange asymmetry of the nucleon sea may produce an observable contribution to the different behaviors of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ production. We find that a softer $\bar{s}(x)$ than $s(x)$ as predicted by the light-cone baryon-meson fluctuation model of intrinsic quark-antiquark pairs of the nucleon sea might lead to a reasonable picture. However, the magnitude is still too small to explain the E665 data and the conclusion has also strong model-dependence. This may suggest the importance of quark-antiquark asymmetry in the quark to $Λ$ fragmentation functions, provided that the E665 data are confirmed.

# The Quark-Antiquark Asymmetry of the Nucleon Sea from Λ and \(\bar{Λ}\) Fragmentation

## Key claim
The different behaviors of Λ and \(\bar{Λ}\) spin transfer observed by the E665 Collaboration could arise from quark-antiquark asymmetries either in the quark-to-Λ fragmentation functions or in the quark and antiquark distributions of the target proton. However, the magnitude of the effect from strange-antistrange asymmetry in the nucleon sea is still too small to fully explain the E665 data, and the conclusion has strong model-dependence.

## Method
The authors performed a general analysis of longitudinal spin transfer for Λ and \(\bar{Λ}\) production in deep-inelastic scattering of polarized charged leptons on an unpolarized nucleon target, using the quark parton model. They calculated the spin transfer \(A^{\Lambda}(x,z)\) using Eq. (1) with quark distributions from CTEQ5 parametrizations and quark-to-Λ fragmentation functions from a light-cone SU(6) quark-diquark model and a perturbative QCD based model. They then modified the strange and antistrange quark distributions at \(x=0.005\) according to the light-cone baryon-meson fluctuation model prediction of a softer \(\bar{s}(x)\) than \(s(x)\), and also tested cases with \(\bar{d}(x) > d(x)\).

## Key numbers
- The E665 data were measured in the Bjorken variable range \(0.0001 < x < 0.1\) with \(\left<x\right>=0.005\), and for \(z\) between 0.1 and 0.5.
- At \(x=0.005\), the CTEQ5 strange and antistrange distributions were modified from \(s_0=17.11\) and \(\bar{s}_0=17.12\) to \(s=17.11-8\) and \(\bar{s}=17.12+8\) (arbitrary units) to introduce strange-antistrange asymmetry.
- The difference in spin transfer between Λ and \(\bar{Λ}\) from strange-antistrange asymmetry alone was found to be of order 0.25.
- For the case with additional \(\bar{d}(x) > d(x)\), the distributions were modified to \(u=50.32+8\), \(d=45.41-8\), \(\bar{u}=33.55-8\), \(\bar{d}=35.29+8\), \(s=17.11-8\), \(\bar{s}=17.12+8\).

## Context
This work extends previous analyses of Λ production in DIS as a probe of the strange content of the nucleon, originally proposed by Lu and Ma (1995) and Ellis et al. (1996). It builds on recent progress in understanding quark-to-Λ fragmentation functions using the Gribov-Lipatov relation and the SU(6) quark-diquark model (Ma, Schmidt, and Yang, 1999; Ma et al., 2000). The authors compare their results with the E665 and HERMES data, and note that the strange-antistrange asymmetry predicted by the light-cone baryon-meson fluctuation model (Brodsky and Ma, 1996) and supported by analyses of Barone, Pascaud, and Zomer (1999) and Buccella, Pisanti, and Rosa (2000) can produce a trend but insufficient magnitude. The paper suggests that if the E665 data are confirmed, quark-antiquark asymmetry in the fragmentation functions (as explored in Ma et al., 2000) may be more important.
