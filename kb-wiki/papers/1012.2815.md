# Total J/Psi and Upsilon production cross section at the LHC: theory vs. experiment

> We evaluate the production cross section for direct J/Psi and Upsilon integrated in P_T for various collision energies in the QCD-based Colour-Singlet Model (CSM). We consider the LO contribution from gluon fusion whose P_T-integrated cross section shows a very good agreement with the Tevatron and LHC data, both for J/Psi and Upsilon. The rapidity distribution of this yield is evaluated in the central region relevant for the ATLAS and CMS detectors, as well as in the more forward region relevant for the ALICE and LHCb detectors. The results obtained here are compatible with those of other approaches within the range of the theoretical uncertainties which are admittedly very large. This suggests that the "mere" measurements of the yield at the LHC will not help disentangle between the different possible quarkonium production mechanisms. Yet, the comparison with the first LHC results by ALICE, ATLAS, CMS and LHCb confirms that the CSM correctly accounts for the P_T-integrated yield at sqrt(s)=7 TeV.

## Key claim
The Colour-Singlet Model (CSM) at leading order (LO) via gluon fusion correctly accounts for the P_T-integrated yield of direct J/psi and Upsilon at LHC energies, but the theoretical uncertainties are so large (up to a factor of ten) that measurements of the total yield alone cannot distinguish between different quarkonium production mechanisms.

## Method
The P_T-integrated cross sections for direct J/psi and Upsilon were evaluated using LO gluon fusion contributions in the Colour-Singlet Model, following the same procedure as a prior study by Brodsky and Lansberg (2009). The uncertainty bands were computed using the same values for the charm and bottom quark masses (m_c, m_b) and the renormalization and factorization scales (mu_R, mu_F). The rapidity distributions were evaluated both in the central region (relevant for ATLAS and CMS) and in the forward region (relevant for ALICE and LHCb).

## Key numbers
- For J/psi at sqrt(s)=7 TeV: predicted cross section range of 100 to 800 nb (differential in rapidity at y=0, times branching ratio)
- For J/psi at sqrt(s)=14 TeV: predicted cross section range of 200 to 1400 nb (differential in rapidity at y=0, times branching ratio)
- Colour Evaporation Model central (upper) values for J/psi: 140 nb (400 nb) at 7 TeV and 200 nb (550 nb) at 14 TeV
- Gluon tower model predictions for J/psi: 300 nb at 7 TeV and 480 nb at 14 TeV
- For Upsilon at sqrt(s)=7 TeV: good agreement with preliminary CMS data (explicit numerical values not given in the truncated text)
- Theoretical uncertainties on the J/psi yield at large energies approach one decade (factor of 10)

## Context
This work extends the Colour-Singlet Model calculations previously applied at RHIC energies by Brodsky and Lansberg (2009) to the higher energies of the LHC. The results are compatible with the Colour Evaporation Model (CEM) by Bedjidian et al. (2004) and the gluon tower model (GTM) by Khoze et al. (2004), but all approaches suffer from large theoretical uncertainties. The good agreement with data from PHENIX, CDF, ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, and LHCb confirms that colour-octet contributions have little impact at low P_T, consistent with recent e+e- analyses. The paper motivates studying other observables at the LHC, such as J/psi production in association with a single charm, a prompt isolated photon, or a pair of c cbar, to discriminate between production mechanisms.
