# Isospin asymmetry and neutron stars in V-QCD

> Isospin asymmetric nuclear matter is introduced to V-QCD, a bottom-up holographic Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) model. Using a small isospin chemical potential we extract the symmetry energy in the model, finding excellent agreement with experimental results for some of the potentials. Extending the calculation for finite and arbitrary sized isospin chemical potentials, we construct beta-equilibrated neutron stars via the usual Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations. We find, pleasingly, that the neutron stars passing the mass/radius and tidal deformability constraints are those with the potentials that also lead to excellent symmetry energies.

## Key claim

The paper introduces isospin asymmetric nuclear matter into the V-QCD holographic model for the first time, finding that the symmetry energy extracted at small isospin chemical potential shows excellent agreement with experimental results for some of the model potentials, and that the neutron stars which satisfy mass/radius and tidal deformability constraints are precisely those with potentials that also yield the best symmetry energies.

## Method

The authors extend the V-QCD bottom-up holographic model by introducing a nonvanishing isospin chemical potential via the holographic dictionary, turning on additional gauge field components (a non-Abelian temporal component and spatial components along the third isospin direction) beyond the symmetric homogeneous Ansatz. They linearize the equations of motion around the isospin-symmetric configuration to extract the symmetry energy at small isospin chemical potential, and then solve the fully nonlinear backreacted system for arbitrary isospin chemical potentials to construct beta-equilibrated neutron star matter. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations are used to compute mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities, with two matching procedures (a and b) to combine the holographic equations of state with low-energy nuclear matter models (Hebeler-Lattimer-Pethick-Schwenk variants and SLy4).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron stars in holographic QCD models, specifically the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model (Kovensky 2021, Bartolini 2023) and earlier V-QCD studies without isospin asymmetry (Jokela 2018, Chesler 2019, Ecker 2019, Järvinen 2023), by porting isospin asymmetric baryonic matter to the precision fit-to-LQCD V-QCD framework. It also builds on the determination of the Chern-Simons term in V-QCD (Järvinen 2022) and the homogeneous Ansatz for baryonic matter (Li 2015, Ishii 2019). The results enable future refinement of holographic neutron star modeling by identifying which potentials simultaneously satisfy symmetry energy constraints and neutron star observational bounds.
