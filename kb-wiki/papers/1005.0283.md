# A description of the f2(1270), rho3(1690), f4(2050), rho5(2350) and f6(2510) resonances as multi-rho(770) states

> In a previous work regarding the interaction of two $ρ(770)$ resonances, the $f_2(1270)$ ($J^{PC}=2^{++}$) resonance was obtained dynamically as a two-$ρ$ molecule with a very strong binding energy, 135~MeV per $ρ$ particle. In the present work we use the $ρρ$ interaction in spin 2 and isospin 0 channel to show that the resonances $ρ_3(1690)$ ($3^{--}$), $f_4(2050)$ ($4^{++}$), $ρ_5(2350)$ ($5^{--}$) and $f_6(2510)$ ($6^{++}$) are basically molecules of increasing number of $ρ(770)$ particles. We use the fixed center approximation of the Faddeev equations to write the multi-body interaction in terms of the two-body scattering amplitudes. We find the masses of the states very close to the experimental values and we get an increasing value of the binding energy per $ρ$ as the number of $ρ$ mesons is increased.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the resonances ρ₃(1690), f₄(2050), ρ₅(2350), and f₆(2510) can be described as molecules of increasing numbers of ρ(770) mesons, using the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equations. The hedge is that the description relies on the assumption that the two-ρ system with spin 2 and isospin 0 is so strongly bound that it forms a cluster (the f₂(1270)) which then serves as a building block for larger systems, and the results depend on a cutoff parameter Λ chosen to reproduce the f₂(1270) mass.

## Method

The authors use the fixed center approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations to compute the scattering amplitude of a ρ meson with a cluster of ρ mesons, starting from the two-ρ system (f₂(1270)) and iteratively building up to six ρ mesons. The two-ρ interaction is obtained from a hidden gauge symmetry Lagrangian, unitarized via the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a cutoff regularization, and the wave function of the f₂(1270) cluster is used to compute form factors needed in the FCA. For systems with four, five, and six ρ mesons, the interaction is treated as scattering between clusters (e.g., two f₂ clusters for the four-ρ system, a ρ with an f₄ for the five-ρ system, and an f₄ with an f₂ for the six-ρ system).

## Key numbers

- The f₂(1270) resonance is obtained as a two-ρ molecule with a binding energy of 135 MeV per ρ particle.
- The ρ₃(1690) mass from the full model is 1698 MeV, compared to the PDG value of 1689 ± 2 MeV.
- The f₄(2050) mass from the full model is 2051 MeV, compared to the PDG value of 2018 ± 11 MeV.
- The ρ₅(2350) mass from the full model ranges from 2330 to 2366 MeV (depending on the cutoff Λ'), compared to the PDG value of 2330 ± 35 MeV.
- The f₆(2510) mass from the full model ranges from 2607 to 2633 MeV, compared to the PDG value of 2465 ± 50 MeV.
- The binding energy per ρ for the six-ρ system is 337 to 341 MeV.
- The cutoff Λ used for the ρρ loop function is 875 MeV, chosen to reproduce the f₂(1270) mass.

## Context

This work extends the previous finding by Molina and Oset (2008) that the f₂(1270) resonance can be dynamically generated as a two-ρ molecule, and it applies the same chiral unitary approach to systems with more than two ρ mesons. The paper builds on the hidden gauge symmetry Lagrangian framework used in earlier studies of vector meson interactions (Molina 2008, Geng and Oset 2009, Nagahiro 2009) and uses the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equations, a technique previously applied to kaon-deuteron scattering (Kamalov 2000, Jido 2009). The results suggest that these high-spin meson resonances can be understood as multi-ρ molecules, and the increasing binding energy per ρ as more mesons are added raises the question of whether the mass of such systems saturates for a large number of ρ mesons, which could have implications for meson condensates.
