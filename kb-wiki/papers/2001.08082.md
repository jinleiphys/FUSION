# Lee-Yang-inspired energy-density functional including contributions from $p$-wave scattering

> The ELYO functional proposed in [M. Grasso, D. Lacroix, and C. J. Yang, Phys. Rev. C \textbf{95}, 054327 (2017)] belongs to the family of energy-density functionals (EDFs) inspired by effective-field theories (EFTs) and constrained by \textit{ab--initio} pseudo-data. We present here an extension of this EDF which also accounts for the first $p$-wave term appearing in the low-density expansion from which it derives. It is shown that this enrichment of the ansatz on which the functional is based leads to a significant improvement of the description of neutronic systems, especially in regimes besides the pseudo--data set employed to adjust the parameters. As an illustrative application, the mass-radius relation of neutron stars is considered. In contrast to its initial version, the new functional predicts values which are qualitatively consistent with recent observations.

# Lee-Yang-inspired energy-density functional including contributions from p-wave scattering

## Key claim
The extended ELYO functional that includes the first p-wave term from the low-density expansion significantly improves the description of neutronic systems compared to the original s-wave-only version, particularly in density regimes beyond the pseudo-data set used for parameter adjustment. The new functional predicts neutron star mass-radius relations that are qualitatively consistent with recent observations, whereas the initial version did not.

## Method
The authors extended the ELYO energy-density functional by adding a p-wave contribution derived from the Lee-Yang expansion, mapping it term-by-term onto a Skyrme-type EOS with a new t2 term related to the p-wave scattering length. For pure neutron matter, the s-wave scattering length was made density-dependent with a control parameter Λ=1, while the effective range rs was treated as a phenomenological parameter fitted to the SLy5 reference EOS, yielding rs^II = -7.754 fm. For finite neutron drops, two additional factors W1 and W2 were introduced to modulate the velocity-dependent terms and correct the neutron effective mass, with W1 = -0.163 and W2 = 0.499 fitted to reproduce ab-initio drop energies. Symmetric nuclear matter parameters t0, t3, and Θs were then fitted to the SLy5 SNM EOS.

## Context
This work extends the ELYO functional originally proposed by Grasso, Lacroix, and Yang (2017), which was an EFT-inspired EDF constrained by ab-initio pseudo-data but limited to s-wave contributions only. The new functional addresses severe limitations revealed in applications to neutron drops (Grasso et al., 2019), where the original version produced incorrect droplet energies and numerical instabilities for N>22. The inclusion of p-wave scattering enables better description of neutron matter at higher densities and eliminates the numerical instabilities, while the neutron star application demonstrates consistency with observational constraints. The authors note that the density profiles still exhibit unphysical bubble-like structures that may indicate finite-size instabilities, which they plan to analyze using linear response theory in future work.
