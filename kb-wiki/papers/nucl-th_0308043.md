# Analysis of Intermediate-Energy Nucleus-Nucleus Spallation, Fission, and Fragmentation Reactions with the LAQGSM code

> The LAQGSM code has been recently developed at Los Alamos National Laboratory to simulate nuclear reactions for proton radiography applications. We have benchmarked our code against most available measured data both for proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus interactions at incident energies from 10 MeV to 800 GeV and have compared our results with predictions of other current models used by the nuclear community. Here, we present a brief description of our code and show illustrative results obtained with LAQGSM for neutron spectra measured recently by Nakamura's groups for reactions induced by light and medium nuclei on targets from C to Pb at several incident energies from 95 to 600 MeV/nucleon and with the recent GSI measurements of spallation, fission, and fragmentation yields from A+p and A+A reactions at incident energies near and below 1 GeV/nucleon. Further necessary work is outlined.

## Key claim

The LAQGSM code, when merged with the GEM2 evaporation/fission model, describes a large variety of spallation, fission, and fragmentation reactions for both proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus interactions at intermediate energies without any refitted parameters, but it fails to reproduce mass distributions for some fission-fragment elements from the reaction 1 GeV/nucleon $^{238}$U + $^{208}$Pb, likely because it neglects electromagnetic-induced fission and angular momentum effects.

## Method

The authors used the Los Alamos version of the Quark-Gluon String Model (LAQGSM), which combines a time-dependent intranuclear cascade model (the Dubna Cascade Model) with the coalescence model for light composite particles, the preequilibrium and evaporation physics from the CEM2k code, and the Fermi break-up model for residual nuclei with A ≤ 12. To handle fission and production of light fragments heavier than $^4$He, LAQGSM was merged with the Generalized Evaporation Model code GEM2 by Furihata. The code was benchmarked against measured neutron double-differential cross sections from Nakamura's group and against GSI measurements of spallation, fission, and fragmentation yields from A+p and A+A reactions at incident energies near and below 1 GeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

* Neutron double-differential cross sections from 560 MeV/nucleon $^{40}$Ar beams on C, Cu, and Pb targets were calculated with LAQGSM and compared to data from Iwata et al. (2001); LAQGSM agreed better with measurements than the QMD and HIC models.
* For the reaction $^{238}$U(1 GeV/nucleon) + d, LAQGSM+GEM2 described spallation and fission product cross sections with an accuracy of a factor of two or better compared to data from Enqvist et al. (2002).
* For the reaction $^{238}$U(950 MeV/nucleon) + $^{64}$Cu, LAQGSM+GEM2 described most spallation yields with an accuracy of a factor of two or better compared to data from Junghans et al. (1997, 1998).
* For the reaction $^{40}$Ar (1.05 GeV/nucleon) + $^9$Be, LAQGSM+GEM2 reproduced measured neutron-rich product yields covering an interval of about six orders of magnitude compared to data from Ozawa et al. (2000).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the CEM2k code for nucleon-, pion-, and photo-induced reactions and the LAQGSM code for particle- and nucleus-induced reactions up to about 1 TeV/nucleon. The paper benchmarks LAQGSM against recent GSI measurements that provide "pure" individual isotope cross sections from inverse-kinematics experiments, which are easier to compare to models than the "camouflaged" data from gamma-spectrometry measurements. The results enable the use of LAQGSM as an event-generator in transport codes like MARS, LAHET, and MCNPX for simulating reactions with targets of arbitrary geometry and nuclide composition, while also identifying needed improvements for heavy-ion fission reactions involving angular momentum and electromagnetic-induced fission.
