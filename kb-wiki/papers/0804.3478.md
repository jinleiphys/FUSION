# U_A(1) anomaly and eta' mass from an infrared singular quark-gluon vertex

> The $U_A(1)$ problem of QCD is inevitably tied to the infrared behaviour of quarks and gluons with its most visible effect being the $η^\prime$ mass. A dimensional argument of Kogut and Susskind showed that the mixing of the pseudoscalar flavour-singlet mesons with gluons can provide a screening of the Goldstone pole in this channel if the full quark-quark interaction is strongly infrared singular as $\sim 1/k^4$. We investigate this idea using previously obtained results for the Landau gauge ghost and gluon propagator, together with recent determinations for the singular behaviour of the quark-gluon vertex. We find that, even with an infrared vanishing gluon propagator, the singular structure of the quark-gluon vertex for certain kinematics is apposite for yielding a non-zero screening mass.

# U_A(1) anomaly and eta' mass from an infrared singular quark-gluon vertex

## Key claim

Even with an infrared vanishing gluon propagator, the singular structure of the quark-gluon vertex for certain kinematics (specifically a soft collinear-like divergence dependent only on the external gluon momentum) is sufficient to yield a non-zero screening mass for the eta' in the chiral limit, supporting the Kogut-Susskind mechanism for resolving the U_A(1) problem.

## Method

The authors compute the diamond diagram (two anomalous AVV triangle diagrams linked by two gluon exchange) using Dyson-Schwinger equation solutions for the Landau gauge ghost and gluon propagators, together with a model for the quark-gluon vertex dressing that incorporates both a uniform infrared enhancement and a soft collinear-like divergence. The pseudoscalar Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for the eta is obtained from the rainbow-ladder approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation, and the resulting five-dimensional integral for the screening mass is evaluated numerically using adaptive quadrature routines.

## Key numbers

- The topological susceptibility obtained for the best parameter set is chi^2 = (169 MeV)^4, in qualitative agreement with lattice results.
- For the parameter set with d_1 = 1.67 GeV^2, d_2 = 0.5 GeV^2, d_3 = 2.6 GeV^2, the calculated anomalous mass squared is m_A^2 = 0.56 GeV^2, yielding m_eta = 479 MeV and m_eta' = 906 MeV with a mixing angle theta = -23.2 degrees.
- The pion decay constant used in the Witten-Veneziano formula is f_0 = 93 MeV.
- The infrared exponent for the ghost propagator is kappa = (93 - sqrt(1201))/98 = 0.595, giving the gluon dressing function Z(p^2) ~ (p^2)^{2kappa} and the ghost dressing G(p^2) ~ (p^2)^{-kappa}.
- The infrared fixed point of the running coupling is alpha_S(0) = 8.915/N_c.
- The renormalisation scale is mu^2 = 170 GeV^2 with alpha_mu = 0.2, and Lambda_QCD^2 = 0.5 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the Kogut-Susskind mechanism (Kogut and Susskind, 1973) for the U_A(1) problem by replacing earlier phenomenological ansatze for the gluon propagator (von Smekal, 1997; Mecke thesis) with self-consistent Dyson-Schwinger equation results for the Yang-Mills sector (Fischer, 2003; Alkofer, 2003) and recent determinations of the quark-gluon vertex infrared behaviour (Alkofer, 2008). The results are complementary to the Witten-Veneziano mechanism (Witten, 1979; Veneziano, 1979) which relates the eta' mass to the topological susceptibility without specifying the responsible degrees of freedom; the present work suggests that center vortices (Engelhardt, 2000) rather than instantons may be the relevant topologically non-trivial field configurations, since they provide both confinement and a non-vanishing topological susceptibility.
