# Tensor correlations in $^4$He and $^8$Be with antisymmetrized quasi cluster model

> In this paper, we extend the framework of improved version of simplified method to take into account the tensor contribution ($i$SMT) and propose AQCM-T, tensor version of antisymmetrized quasi cluster model (AQCM). Although AQCM-T is phenomenological, we can treat the $^3S$-$^3D$ coupling in the deuteron-like $T=0$ $NN$-pair induced by the tensor interaction in a very simplified way, which allows us to proceed to heavier nuclei. Also we propose a new effective interaction, V2m, where the triplet-even channel of the Volkov No.2 interaction is weakened to 60% so as to reproduce the binding energy of $^4$He after including the tensor term of a realistic interaction. Using AQCM-T and the new interaction, the significant tensor contribution in $^4$He is shown, which is almost comparable the central interaction, where $D$-state mixes by 8% to the major $S$-state. The AQCM-T model with the new interaction is also applied to $^8$Be. It is found that the tensor suppression gives significant contribution to the short-range repulsion between two α clusters.

## Key claim
The authors propose AQCM-T, a phenomenological extension of the antisymmetrized quasi cluster model that treats the tensor-induced \(^3S\)-\(^3D\) coupling in deuteron-like \(T=0\) \(NN\) pairs via complex Gaussian centroids, and introduce a new effective interaction V2m where the triplet-even central channel is weakened to 60% of the original Volkov No.2 strength to avoid double-counting the tensor contribution when a realistic tensor term is added.

## Method
The method uses Gaussian wave packets with complex centroids \(\mathbf{S}_{1,2} = \mathbf{R} \pm i\mathbf{K}/\nu\) for a correlated \(NN\) pair, giving a relative wave function \(\varphi_{\mathbf{k}}(\mathbf{r})\) with mean momentum \(\mathbf{k}=2\mathbf{K}\) that contains both \(S\)-wave and \(D\)-wave components after positive-parity projection. For \(^4\)He, the total wave function is a linear combination of basis states with \(k = 0.5, 1.0, \ldots, 5.5\) fm\(^{-1}\) (11 points) across three spin-isospin channels (\(^1S\), \(^3S\), \(^3D\)), totaling 34 basis states after diagonalization. For \(^8\)Be, one \(\alpha\) cluster is treated as correlated (with \(k = 1, 2, 3\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(^3S,^3D\) channels) and the other as a \((0s)^4\) configuration, with the inter-cluster distance \(d_\alpha\) fixed and the angle \(\theta_\alpha\) varied over five mesh points from 0 to \(\pi/2\).

## Key numbers
- The triplet-even channel of the Volkov No.2 central interaction is weakened to **60%** to form the new V2m interaction, reproducing the \(^4\)He binding energy after including the tensor term.
- In \(^4\)He, the \(D\)-state mixes by **8%** to the major \(S\)-state.
- For \(^4\)He, basis states with \(k\) values from **0.5 to 5.5 fm\(^{-1}\)** (11 points) are adopted for each of three channels.
- For \(^8\)Be, three \(k\) values (**1, 2, 3 fm\(^{-1}\)**) and five \(\theta_\alpha\) mesh points (**0, \(\pi/8\), \(\pi/4\), \(3\pi/8\), \(\pi/2\)**) are used, giving 31 basis states per distance \(d_\alpha\).
- The Gaussian width parameter \(\nu\) is set to **0.25 fm\(^{-2}\)** for all calculations.

## Context
This work extends the improved simplified method for tensor contribution (\(i\)SMT) of Itagaki et al. (2016) and the high-momentum AMD (HM-AMD) of Myo et al. (2017) by formalizing AQCM-T as a tensor version of the antisymmetrized quasi cluster model. It addresses the double-counting problem in earlier \(i\)SMT and HM-AMD calculations where the tensor interaction was simply added to effective Hamiltonians that already renormalized tensor effects in the central channel. The new V2m interaction and AQCM-T framework enable systematic studies of tensor correlations in heavier nuclei, starting here with \(^8\)Be where tensor suppression contributes to the short-range repulsion between two \(\alpha\) clusters, building on the Brueckner theory estimates of Bando (1970s) and the quantum Monte Carlo results for \(^8\)Be.
