# (Strange) Meson Interferometry at RHIC

> We make predictions for the kaon interferometry measurements in Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). A first order phase transition from a thermalized Quark-Gluon-Plasma (QGP) to a gas of hadrons is assumed for the transport calculations. The fraction of kaons that are directly emitted from the phase boundary is considerably enhanced at large transverse momenta K_T ~ 1 GeV/c. In this kinematic region, the sensitivity of the R_out/R_side ratio to the QGP-properties is enlarged. Here, the results of the 1-dimensional correlation analysis are presented. The extracted interferometry radii, depending on $K_T$, are not unusually large and are strongly affected by momentum resolution effects.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that kaon interferometry at RHIC, assuming a first-order phase transition from a thermalized Quark-Gluon-Plasma to hadrons, will show an increasing R_out/R_side ratio with transverse momentum K_T, but that these ratios (around 1.5-2 at large K_T) are considerably smaller than former expectations, and that the extracted one-dimensional radii are not unusually large and are strongly affected by finite momentum resolution effects.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic two-phase dynamical transport model that describes the early quark-gluon plasma phase by hydrodynamics with a bag model equation of state exhibiting a first-order phase transition, and the later hadronic phase by microscopic transport (UrQMD) including resonance (de)excitations and binary collisions. Kaon correlation functions are calculated from the freeze-out phase-space distributions using the correlation afterburner CRAB by Pratt, and Gaussian fits are performed to extract the one-dimensional invariant radius parameters R_inv and intercept parameters lambda_1 for various K_T bins in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)_NN = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- R_out/R_side ratio for kaons at RHIC reaches values on the order of approximately 1.5-2 at large K_T, which is considerably smaller than former expectations.
- About 30% of kaons at K_T ~ 1 GeV/c are directly emitted from the phase boundary (for T_c approximately 160 MeV).
- R_inv is on the order of 5.5-6.5 fm without finite momentum resolution effects and 4.5-6 fm with finite momentum resolution (approximately 2% of the center of each K_T bin) taken into account.
- The bag parameter B is varied from 380 MeV/fm^3 to 720 MeV/fm^3, corresponding to a variation of the critical temperature from T_c approximately 160 MeV to T_c approximately 200 MeV.
- Preliminary STAR KK data at sqrt(s)_NN = 130 GeV gives R_inv = 4.5 +/- 0.3 fm (at 150 MeV < K_T < 400 MeV and central 11% collisions) with lambda_1 = 0.92 +/- 0.13.
- For central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 17.4 GeV, experimental data yield R_inv = 6.2 +/- 0.4 +/- 0.9 fm at low transverse momenta (mean p_t = 250 MeV) and R_inv = 3.3 +/- 0.6 fm at high transverse momenta (mean p_t = 910 MeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions by Pratt (1986), Schlei, and Rischke (1995) that a first-order phase transition should produce large hadronization times and thus large interferometry radii and emission durations. The calculations show that the dissipative hadronic phase dominates the radii for pions (Soff, Bass, and Dumitru, 2001), and that kaons are less contaminated by resonance decays than pions (Gyulassy and Padula, 1989; Sullivan, 1993). The results enable comparison with first RHIC pion correlation data from STAR (2001) showing ratios smaller than unity, which would hint at a rather explosive scenario with very short emission times not compatible with a thermalized QGP hadronizing via a first-order phase transition.
