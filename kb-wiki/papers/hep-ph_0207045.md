# Diffractive Hard Dijets and Nuclear Parton Distributions

> Diffraction plays an exceptional role in DIS off heavy nuclei. First, diffraction into hard dijets is an unique probe of the unintegrated glue in the target. Second, because diffraction makes 50 per cent of total DIS off a heavy target, understanding diffraction in a saturation regime is crucial for a definition of saturated nuclear parton densities. After brief comments on the Nikolaev-Zakharov (NZ) pomeron-splitting mechanism for diffractive hard dijet production, I review an extension of the Nikolaev-Schafer-Schwiete (NSS) analysis of diffractive dijet production off nuclei to the definition of nuclear partons in the saturation regime. I emphasize the importance of intranuclear final state interactions for the parton momentum distributions.

## Key claim

Diffractive hard dijet production off nuclei provides a unique probe of the unintegrated gluon distribution in the target, but the interpretation of the saturation regime in terms of nuclear parton densities is questionable because coherent diffractive DIS makes 50% of total DIS off heavy nuclei and cannot be treated in terms of parton densities in the target.

## Method

The authors use the color dipole formulation of deep inelastic scattering (DIS) to analyze diffractive and inelastic DIS off heavy nuclei. They apply the Nikolaev-Schafer-Schwiete (NSS) representation for the nuclear profile function, which expresses the nuclear Weizsacker-Williams (WW) gluon distribution in terms of multiple convolutions of the nucleon's unintegrated gluon distribution. The analysis compares the transverse momentum distributions of quarks from diffractive and inelastic DIS in the saturation regime where the nuclear opacity criterion is satisfied for all color dipoles of the photon.

## Key numbers

- Coherent diffractive DIS makes precisely 50% of total DIS events for a very heavy nucleus
- For free nucleons, the diffractive fraction eta_D is less than or approximately 6-10%
- The saturation scale Q_A^2 = nu_A(b) Q_0^2, which scales approximately as A^{1/3}
- The plateau height for diffractive DIS sea quarks is d(qbar)/d^2b d^2p = N_c/(4 pi^4)
- The plateau height for inelastic DIS sea quarks is d(qbar)/d^2b d^2p = (N_c/(4 pi^4)) * (Q^2/Q_A^2)
- The inelastic plateau extends up to p^2 ~ Q_A^2

## Context

This work extends the Nikolaev-Schafer-Schwiete (NSS) analysis of diffractive dijet production off nuclei to the definition of nuclear partons in the saturation regime. It builds on the earlier Nikolaev-Zakharov (NZ) pomeron-splitting mechanism for diffractive hard dijet production and the Nikolaev-Zakharov-Zoller (NZZ) result that coherent diffractive DIS makes 50% of total DIS off heavy nuclei. The analysis contradicts claims by Frankfurt et al. (FMS) that the diffractive amplitude is proportional to the integrated gluon structure function, and it questions the interpretation of nuclear saturation in terms of parton densities, suggesting instead that intranuclear final state interactions strongly distort the observed quark momentum distributions.
