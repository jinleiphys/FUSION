# Multiple Scattering Theory for the Photoproduction of Vector Mesons off Nuclei

> The integrated cross section for the incoherent photoproduction of vector mesons on nuclei $γ^* A \to VX$, $X\not=A$, is calculated within Glauber theory and as a function of the photon energy. The inverse of the longitudinal momentum transfer is called coherence length $l_c$ and depends on the virtuality and the energy of the photon. Nuclear transmission factors strongly depend on $l_c/R_A$ ($R_A$ is the nuclear radius) and this effect may interfere with the search for color transparency effects.

## Key claim

The paper derives a fully quantum mechanical expression for the incoherent photoproduction cross section of vector mesons on nuclei within Glauber multiple scattering theory, showing that the nuclear transmission factor depends strongly on the ratio of the coherence length \(l_c = 1/q_L\) to the nuclear radius \(R_A\); this effect must be accounted for as a baseline before searching for color transparency effects, because the energy- and \(Q^2\)-dependence of the cross section can mimic or obscure unconventional phenomena.

## Method

The authors calculate the integrated cross section for the incoherent reaction \(\gamma^* A \to V X\) (where \(X\) is any nuclear state except the ground state) using Glauber's eikonal formalism. The transition operator includes a phase factor \(\exp(i q_L z)\) for the longitudinal momentum transfer \(q_L = (Q^2 + M_V^2 + (p_V^T)^2)/(2\nu)\), and the nuclear wave function is taken as a product of single-particle densities. The incoherent cross section is obtained by summing over all final nuclear states excluding the ground state, and analytical expressions are derived for the limiting cases \(q_L \to 0\) (high energy) and \(q_L \to \infty\) (low energy), as well as a general formula (Equation 14) that interpolates between them.

## Key numbers

* The coherence length is defined as \(l_c = 1/q_L\), where \(q_L = (Q^2 + M_V^2 + (p_V^T)^2)/(2\nu)\); the half-value between low- and high-energy limits is reached for \(q_L \cdot R_A \simeq 1.5\), with \(R_A = 1.2 A^{1/3}\) fm.
* For \(\rho\)-meson photoproduction, the total \(\rho N\) cross section is taken as \(\sigma^{\rho N}_{tot} = 25\) mb, and the elastic slope is \(B^{\rho N}_{el} \approx 8\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
* The mean free path of the vector meson in nuclear matter is estimated as \(l_{free} \approx 1/(\sigma_{in}^{VN} \rho_0) \approx 3\) fm for the \(\rho\)-meson.
* In the limit of small attenuation (\(\sigma^{VN}_{in} \langle T \rangle \ll 1\)), the nuclear transparency obeys \(Tr(q_L \to 0) \approx [Tr(q_L \to \infty)]^2\).

## Context

This work extends the standard Glauber multiple scattering theory (Glauber, 1959) to the photoproduction of vector mesons, incorporating the coherence length effect that had not been treated before in this context. The results provide a baseline calculation that must be compared with any search for color transparency effects, as suggested by Benhar (1996) for \(J/\psi\) photoproduction. The paper enables future experimental studies at CEBAF and HERA to disentangle conventional nuclear attenuation from genuine quantum chromodynamic effects by using the derived general formula (Equation 14) and the approximate relation (Equation 16).
