# Nuclear Scaling and the EMC Effect

> Results of recent EMC effect measurements and nuclear scaling measurements have both been attributed to local nuclear density effects and not properties of the bulk nuclear system. This lead us to the phenomenological observation that the ratio of the slopes in the 0.3 < x_B < 0.7 EMC data scale as the ratio of the x_B > 1 nuclear scaling plateaus. Using this correlation, we developed a phenomenological relation which reproduces the general trends and features of the EMC effect for nuclei from 3He to 56Fe.

# Nuclear Scaling and the EMC Effect

## Key claim
The ratio of the slopes in the EMC data for 0.3 < x_B < 0.7 scales as the ratio of the x_B > 1 nuclear scaling plateaus, suggesting both effects arise from local nuclear density and high-momentum components of the nuclear wave function rather than bulk nuclear properties. This is a phenomenological observation, not a derivation from first principles.

## Method
The authors used existing inclusive electron scattering data for nuclei from 3He to 56Fe, comparing the slopes of the EMC effect in the 0.3 < x_B < 0.7 region with the magnitudes of the x_B > 1 nuclear scaling plateaus. They modeled the high-momentum nucleon distribution for each nucleus as the deuteron momentum distribution (calculated with the Argonne v-18 potential) multiplied by a constant scaling factor C^A obtained from experimental nuclear scaling ratios. They then calculated the fraction of the momentum distribution kinematically forbidden to contribute to the cross section at each x_B, using the minimum missing momentum p_min from the D(e,e')pn reaction kinematics at Q^2 = 10 (GeV/c)^2.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear scaling ratios C^A to deuterium: 2 for 3He, 4 for 4He, 4.8 for 12C, and 5.7 for 56Fe
- At x_B = 0.70 (p_min = 250 MeV/c), the fraction of initial-state momentum above p_min is: deuteron 2.5%, 3He 5.0%, 4He 10%, 12C 12%, 56Fe 14.3%
- At x_B = 0.30 (p_min = 500 MeV/c), the fraction is: deuteron 0.65%, 3He 1.3%, 4He 2.6%, 12C 3.1%, 56Fe 3.7%
- The calculation uses Q^2 = 10 (GeV/c)^2 for the kinematic mapping between x_B and p_min
- The Fermi momentum threshold for the high-momentum tail is approximately 250 MeV/c

## Context
This work extends the observations of the Jefferson Lab EMC effect measurements on light nuclei (Seely, 2009) and nuclear scaling measurements from SLAC and Jefferson Lab (Egiyan, 2003; Fomin, 2008), which both suggested local density effects. The authors build on the theoretical framework of Kumano and Close (1989) who showed that local density effects and quark rescaling models are compatible. The phenomenological relation connects the x_B > 1 scaling plateaus (attributed to nucleon-nucleon correlations, particularly tensor-dominated proton-neutron pairs as shown by Piasetzky (2006) and Subedi (2008)) to the EMC effect slope, enabling a unified description of both phenomena using the same high-momentum nucleon distributions.
