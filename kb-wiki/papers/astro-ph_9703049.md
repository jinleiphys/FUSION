# Nuclear Matter in Relativistic Mean Field Theory with Isovector Scalar Meson

> Relativistic mean field (RMF) theory of nuclear matter with the isovector scalar mean field corresponding to the delta-meson [a_0(980)] is studied. While the delta-meson mean field vanishes in symmetric nuclear matter, it can influence properties of asymmetric nuclear matter in neutron stars. The RMF contribution due to delta-field to the nuclear symmetry energy is negative. To fit the empirical value, E_s=30 MeV, a stronger rho-meson coupling is required than in the absence of the delta-field. The energy per particle of neutron matter is then larger at high densities than the one with no delta-field included. Also, the proton fraction of beta-stable matter increases. Splitting of proton and neutron effective masses due to the delta-field can affect transport properties of neutron star matter.

## Key claim

Including the isovector scalar delta-meson in relativistic mean field (RMF) theory of nuclear matter produces a negative contribution to the nuclear symmetry energy, so that fitting the empirical symmetry energy of approximately 30 MeV requires a stronger rho-meson coupling than when the delta-field is absent; the resulting energy per particle of neutron matter at high densities is then larger than without the delta-field.

## Method

The authors extend the standard RMF model (Serot and Walecka 1986) by adding the isovector scalar delta-meson field with Yukawa coupling to nucleons, alongside the usual isoscalar sigma and omega fields and the isovector rho field. They adopt the sigma-field potential of Boguta and Bodmer (1977) and adjust the isoscalar parameters to fit symmetric nuclear matter saturation properties (density 0.145 fm^-3, binding energy -16 MeV per nucleon, compressibility modulus ~280 MeV). The isovector couplings C_rho^2 and C_delta^2 are constrained by requiring the nuclear symmetry energy to match the empirical value of 31 ± 4 MeV, using two parameter sets (soft and stiff equations of state) for the isoscalar sector.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: n_0 = 0.145 fm^-3
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: w_0 = -16 MeV
- Compressibility modulus: K_V ≈ 280 MeV
- Empirical nuclear symmetry energy: E_s = 31 ± 4 MeV (Myers and Swiatecki 1973; Pearson et al. 1991; Möller and Nix 1988; Möller et al. 1988; Myers et al. 1977)
- For the soft equation of state with no delta-field: C_rho^2 = 5.0 fm^2
- For the stiff equation of state with no delta-field: C_rho^2 = 4.29 fm^2
- Maximum delta-meson coupling from Bonn potential C: C_delta^2 = 2.6 fm^2 (corresponding to g_delta = 8.0; Machleidt 1989)
- For the stiff equation of state, neutron matter becomes self-bound for C_delta^2 ≥ 1.1 fm^2
- For C_delta^2 ≈ 1.0 fm^2, the symmetry energy lowers to about 20 MeV
- For C_delta^2 = 2.5 fm^2, the proton fraction in beta-stable neutron star matter exceeds the direct URCA threshold x_URCA ≈ 0.11

## Context

This work extends standard RMF models (Serot and Walecka 1986) by including the delta-meson, which is usually omitted because its mean field vanishes in symmetric nuclear matter. The study shows that for asymmetric matter in neutron stars the delta-field significantly affects the symmetry energy, neutron matter equation of state, and proton fraction, with implications for neutron star cooling via the direct URCA process. The authors note that inclusion of Fock terms (Bouyssy et al. 1987; Lopez-Quelle et al. 1988) could modify the quantitative results, and they plan to address this in future work (Kubis and Kutschera, in preparation).
