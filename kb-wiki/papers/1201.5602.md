# Thermal and transport properties of the neutron star inner crust

> We review the nuclear and condensed matter physics underlying the thermal and transport properties of the neutron star inner crust. These properties play a key role in interpreting transient phenomena such as thermal relaxation in accreting neutron stars, superbursts, and magnetar flares. We emphasize simplifications that occur at low temperature where the inner crust can be described in terms of electrons and collective excitations. The heat conductivity and heat capacity of the solid and superfluid phase of matter is discussed in detail and we emphasize its role in interpreting observations of neutron stars in soft X-ray transients. We highlight recent theoretical and observational results, and identify future work needed to better understand a host of transient phenomena in neutron stars.

## Key claim

This review argues that at low temperatures, the thermal and transport properties of the neutron star inner crust can be described by a simplified low-energy theory involving only electrons and collective excitations (phonons), with the complex nuclear ground state structure encoded in a few low-energy constants; however, the authors emphasize that the composition and properties remain uncertain due to poorly constrained nuclear symmetry energy and gradient energy, and that the neutron superfluid gap and entrainment effects significantly modify the standard picture.

## Method

The authors review the nuclear and condensed matter physics of the neutron star inner crust by analyzing the low-energy excitations of three components: electrons (treated as a relativistic degenerate Fermi gas), ions (treated as a Coulomb crystal with lattice phonons), and neutrons (treated as a superfluid with a gap and superfluid phonons). They derive analytic expressions for specific heat and thermal conductivity from these collective modes, and incorporate the effects of neutron entrainment by nuclei using an effective field theory framework with a gradient expansion. The review compares predictions for different models of the neutron superfluid gap and for different crust compositions (cold catalyzed matter versus accreted matter).

## Key numbers

- Neutron drip occurs at density $\rho \simeq 4-7 \times 10^{11}$ g/cm$^3$
- The melting temperature of the Coulomb solid is $T_M \simeq 3.4 \times 10^9 (Z/40)^{5/3} (\mu_e/30\ {\rm MeV})$ K
- The ion plasma frequency is $\omega_p = T_p \simeq 0.3 \sqrt{Z/A^*} (\mu_e/30\ {\rm MeV})^{3/2}$ MeV
- The electron specific heat is $C^e_v = (1/3) \mu_e^2 T$
- The lattice phonon specific heat at low temperature is $C^{\rm lph}_v = n_i (12\pi^4/5) (T/T_D)^3$ where $T_D \simeq 0.45 T_p$
- The neutron specific heat in the normal phase is $C^{\rm neutron}_v = (1/3) m_n k_{\rm Fn} T$
- The superfluid phonon specific heat is $C^{\rm sph}_v = (2\pi^2/15) T^3/v_\phi^3$
- The neutron $^1$S$_0$ superfluid gap reaches a maximum value $\approx 1$ MeV at density $\rho \simeq 10^{13}$ g/cm$^3$
- Conversion factors: $1\ {\rm MeV}^2 = 1.07 \times 10^{16}\ [{\rm ergs/(cm\ s\ K)}]$ and $1\ {\rm MeV}^3 = 1.81 \times 10^{16}\ [{\rm ergs/(cm^3\ K)}]$

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on neutron star crust structure by Baym, Bethe and Pethick (1971) and Negele and Vautherin (1973), and the comprehensive review by Chamel and Haensel (2008). It provides a simplified low-energy theoretical framework for thermal and transport properties that enables interpretation of transient phenomena such as thermal relaxation in accreting neutron stars, superbursts, and magnetar flares. The review identifies future work needed to better understand the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, the gradient energy in heterogeneous asymmetric matter, and the rates of pycno-nuclear fusion reactions, which remain poorly constrained and affect predictions for crust composition and thermal evolution.
