# Three-dimensional parton distribution functions $g_{1T}$ and $h_{1L}^\perp$ in the polarized proton-antiproton Drell-Yan process

> We present predictions of the unweighted and weighted double spin asymmetries related to the transversal helicity distribution $g_{1T}$ and the longitudinal transversity distribution $h_{1L}^\perp$, two of eight leading-twist transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs) or three-dimensional parton distribution functions (3dPDFs), in the polarized proton-antiproton Drell-Yan process at typical kinematics on the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR). We conclude that FAIR is ideal to access the new 3dPDFs towards a detailed picture of the nucleon structure.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the double spin asymmetries related to the T-even transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions (TMDs) \(g_{1T}\) and \(h_{1L}^\perp\) in the polarized proton-antiproton Drell-Yan process at FAIR are significantly large, making FAIR an ideal facility to access these distributions, but only under the specific kinematic conditions of \(s = 45~\mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(Q^2 = 12~\mathrm{GeV}^2\) and using a particular light-cone quark-diquark model for the calculations.

## Method

The authors calculated unweighted and weighted double spin asymmetries for the polarized proton-antiproton Drell-Yan process, focusing on the structure functions that involve the TMDs \(g_{1T}\) and \(h_{1L}^\perp\). They used two approaches: Approach 1 directly employed the light-cone quark-diquark model with a Brodsky-Huang-Lepage (BHL) wavefunction and summed only valence quark distributions; Approach 2 used the CTEQ6L parametrization for unpolarized parton distributions with a Gaussian transverse momentum dependence (\(k_{un}^2 = 0.25~\mathrm{GeV}^2\)) while retaining the Melosh-Wigner rotation factors from the quark-diquark model for the polarized distributions. The asymmetries were evaluated as functions of \(x_F\) at fixed \(s = 45~\mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(Q^2 = 12~\mathrm{GeV}^2\).

## Key numbers

- The unweighted double spin asymmetry \(A_{LL}^{\cos2\phi}\), which involves only \(h_{1L}^\perp\), is "significantly large" at \(s = 45~\mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(Q^2 = 12~\mathrm{GeV}^2\) (no explicit numerical value given in the text).
- The unweighted double spin asymmetry related to \(h_{1L}^\perp\) and \(h_{1T}^\perp\) (Fig. 4) is "about several percent" at the same kinematics.
- The expected antiproton beam polarizations are \(0.15\) to \(0.20\) (spin filtering with transverse target orientation) or \(0.35\) to \(0.40\) (longitudinal).
- Model parameters used: \(\alpha_D = 0.33~\mathrm{GeV}\), quark mass \(m_q = 0.33~\mathrm{GeV}\), diquark masses \(m_S = 0.60~\mathrm{GeV}\) and \(m_V = 0.80~\mathrm{GeV}\), mixing angle \(\theta_0 = \pi/4\).
- The Gaussian width for transverse momentum in Approach 2 is \(k_{un}^2 = 0.25~\mathrm{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the pretzelosity distribution \(h_{1T}^\perp\) in the same polarized proton-antiproton Drell-Yan process (Zhu, 2010) to the two remaining T-even TMDs, \(g_{1T}\) and \(h_{1L}^\perp\). It builds on the proposal by the PAX collaboration (Barone, 2005; Efremov, 2004) to measure transversity distributions via polarized antiproton beams, and on technical progress towards a polarized proton-antiproton collider at FAIR (Lenisa, 2010; Rathmann, 2005). The results enable experimental access to the three-dimensional parton structure of the nucleon, complementing measurements from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (She, 2009; Zhu, 2011) and pion-nucleon Drell-Yan processes (Lu, 2011).
