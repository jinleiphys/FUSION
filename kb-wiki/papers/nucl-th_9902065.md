# Beyond the Parton Cascade Model: Klaus Kinder-Geiger and VNI

> I review Klaus Kinder-Geiger's contributions to the physics of relativistic heavy ion collisions, in particular, the Parton Cascade Model. Klaus developed this model in order to provide a QCD-based description of nucleus-nucleus reactions at high energies such as they will soon become available at the Brookhaven Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. The PCM describes the collision dynamics within the early and dense phase of the reaction in terms of the relativistic, probabilistic transport of perturbative excitations (partons) of the QCD vacuum. I will present an overview of the current state of the numerical implementations of this model, as well as its predictions for nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The Parton Cascade Model (PCM) provides a QCD-based description of relativistic heavy ion collisions, but its original formulation depended critically on several ad hoc cut-off parameters that had to be determined from proton-proton collision data; recent advances incorporating in-medium effects have reduced this dependence significantly, possibly allowing application over a wider energy range.

## Method

The PCM describes the early, dense phase of nucleus-nucleus collisions via a relativistic Boltzmann equation with leading-log improved lowest-order collision terms, treating partons as perturbative excitations of the QCD vacuum. The model combines three main ingredients: an initial state of incoherent partons from nuclear parton distribution functions, a time evolution governed by binary interactions and DGLAP-style virtuality evolution, and a hadronization stage when parton virtuality falls below a critical value near 1 GeV. Two practical implementations are discussed: the self-screened parton cascade model (SSPCM), which calculates the color-electric screening scale self-consistently, and the Monte-Carlo code VNI, which uses heuristic rules linking parton virtuality to space-time evolution to suppress soft interactions in dense media.

## Key numbers

- Color-electric screening mass approaches about 1 GeV at low transverse momentum in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy (100 GeV/u) and 1.5 GeV at LHC energy (2.75 TeV/u).
- Total deposited energy within one unit of rapidity after a formation time of 0.25 fm/c is approximately 60 GeV/fm³ at RHIC and approximately 430 GeV/fm³ at LHC.
- The plasma cools to the critical temperature of QCD (Tc ≈ 150 MeV) after 5 fm/c at RHIC and 10 fm/c at LHC.
- Energy density in scattered partons at τ < 1 fm/c rises from about 2 GeV/fm³ in S+S collisions to 5 GeV/fm³ in Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS energies.
- The rapidity density of scattered partons grows as (A₁A₂)^(1/3) ln √s.

## Context

This review extends the original PCM work by Kinder-Geiger and Muller (1990-1991) and the subsequent development of the VNI code, building on earlier concepts including the inside-outside cascade model of Anisovich, Kobrinsky, and Mur (1980) and Bjorken's hydrodynamical model (1983). The paper discusses how in-medium screening effects, studied formally by Makhlin and Surdutovich (1998) and implemented in the SSPCM by Eskola, Muller, and Wang (1996), reduce the model's dependence on arbitrary cut-off parameters. The work enables future comparisons with experimental data from RHIC and LHC, particularly through the planned coupling of VNI with the UrQMD hadronic cascade model by Bass (1998) to describe final-state hadronic interactions.
