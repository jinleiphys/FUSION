# Comparative multi-probe study of jet energy-loss in QGP

> Jet-energy loss is an important sign of the creation of Quark-Gluon Plasma in heavy-ion collisions. High transverse momentum ($p_T$) partons are produced at the moment of initial hard scattering and are modified as a result of their propagation through the created medium. We study two models of low-virtuality radiative energy loss: CUJET and MARTINI. This is done using the JETSCAPE framework, which allows for an objective comparison. CUJET is integrated into the JETSCAPE workflow, and full jet simulations, including substructure observables, are computed for the first time using leading-order DGLV rates. Strongly-interacting probes (charged hadrons, jets, jet fragmentation functions and jet shape ratio) are considered along jet-medium photons for the first time in a dynamic QGP. We find that these photons make a significant contribution in the phenomenologically interesting intermediate $p_T$ domain of $4$-$12$ GeV.

## Key claim
The paper finds that jet-medium photons make a significant contribution to the total direct photon yield in the intermediate transverse momentum domain of 4-12 GeV, but the magnitude of this contribution depends strongly on the energy-loss model used: MARTINI predicts nearly half the total photon spectrum in this range while CUJET places the contribution at approximately 25%.

## Method
The authors integrated the CUJET model into the JETSCAPE framework and performed full jet simulations using leading-order DGLV rates for the first time. They constructed composite multi-stage models where MATTER handles high-virtuality energy loss and either MARTINI or CUJET handles low-virtuality energy loss, with hydrodynamic backgrounds generated by a TRENTO+Free-Streaming+VISHNU+UrQMD simulation. They computed charged hadron nuclear modification factors, jet nuclear modification factors, jet fragmentation function ratios, and direct photon spectra for lead-lead collisions at 2.76 ATeV, fitting CUJET and MARTINI parameters to charged hadron R_AA data at 0-5% centrality.

## Key numbers
- Charged hadron R_AA data at 0-5% centrality for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 ATeV was used to fit CUJET and MARTINI parameters
- The intermediate p_T domain where jet-medium photons make significant contribution is 4-12 GeV
- MARTINI jet-medium photons contribute nearly half (approximately 50%) of the total photon spectrum at p_T around 9-10 GeV
- CUJET jet-medium photons contribute approximately 25% of the total photon spectrum in the same p_T domain
- The freeze-out temperature was set at T_c = 160 MeV
- The momentum cutoff for hard partons was p_cut = 2 GeV
- The strong coupling was fixed to alpha_s = 0.3 for the conversion photon channel

## Context
This work extends comparative studies of jet energy-loss models by using the JETSCAPE framework to provide an objective comparison between CUJET and MARTINI, which had not been done previously with full jet simulations including substructure observables. The paper presents the first calculation of photons from jet-medium interactions within a realistic dynamic QGP and dynamic jet population for both models, building on prior work by Zhang (2010) on photon bremsstrahlung within the DGLV framework and by Gale and collaborators on multi-messenger photon spectra. The results enable future comprehensive analyses of jet-medium photons as a probe of partonic energy loss without hadronization modifications.
