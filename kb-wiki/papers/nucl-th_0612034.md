# Electromagnetic Dissociation and Space Radiation

> Relativistic nucleus-nucleus reactions occur mainly through the Strong or Electromagnetic (EM) interactions. Transport codes often neglect the latter. This work shows the importance of including EM interactions for space radiation applications.

# Electromagnetic Dissociation and Space Radiation

## Key claim

Electromagnetic (EM) dissociation cross sections for single neutron removal from an iron projectile on an aluminum target range from about 30% to 50% of the strong interaction cross section at typical space radiation energies (1–10 GeV/A), and reach about 80% at 50 GeV/A, meaning that EM interactions should not be neglected in space radiation transport codes despite being commonly omitted.

## Method

The authors calculate electromagnetic dissociation cross sections using the Weizsacker-Williams method of virtual quanta, which describes the virtual photon spectrum produced by a target nucleus, convolved with a Lorentzian parameterization of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) photonuclear cross section for the projectile. They compute single neutron removal cross sections for various projectile-target combinations (C, Si, Fe, Au projectiles on C, Al, Fe targets) at laboratory energies of 3, 5, 10, and 50 GeV/A, and compare these to the corresponding strong interaction cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For a 56Fe projectile on a 27Al target at 3 GeV/A: strong cross section = 102 mb, EM cross section = 27 mb (EM is ~26% of strong)
- For a 56Fe projectile on a 27Al target at 10 GeV/A: strong cross section = 102 mb, EM cross section = 47 mb (EM is ~46% of strong)
- For a 56Fe projectile on a 27Al target at 50 GeV/A: strong cross section = 102 mb, EM cross section = 77 mb (EM is ~75% of strong)
- For a 56Fe projectile on a 56Fe target at 50 GeV/A: strong cross section = 116 mb, EM cross section = 298 mb (EM exceeds strong by factor ~2.6)
- For a 12C projectile on a 12C target at 3 GeV/A: strong cross section = 64 mb, EM cross section = 0.6 mb (EM is negligible at ~0.9%)
- For a 197Au projectile on a 56Fe target at 50 GeV/A: strong cross section = 156 mb, EM cross section = 2108 mb (EM exceeds strong by factor ~13.5)

## Context

This work extends the understanding of nucleus-nucleus reactions for space radiation applications, where transport codes (both deterministic and Monte Carlo methods) have traditionally neglected electromagnetic dissociation. The paper builds on prior theoretical work on the Weizsacker-Williams method by Bertulani and others, and on experimental photonuclear cross sections, to demonstrate that for heavy projectile-target combinations relevant to Galactic cosmic ray shielding (e.g., Fe on Al), EM dissociation contributes significantly and must be included. The findings enable more accurate radiation dose estimates for long-duration human space missions, such as those planned for Mars, where the dose is estimated at about 1000 mSv/year in a conventional spacecraft, double the recommended limit for astronauts.
