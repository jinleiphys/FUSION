# Symmetry Problems in Low Energy Physics

> Some recent experimental and theoretical work on 1) charge symmetry-breaking, 2) parity non-conservation, and 3) searches for breaking of time reversal invariance are reviewed. The examples illustrate the uses of symmetry to learn about underlying dynamics and/or structure.

## Key claim

This review argues that symmetry violations in low-energy nuclear physics remain a powerful probe of underlying dynamics, but the interpretation of charge-symmetry-breaking experiments is complicated by unresolved theoretical uncertainties in the momentum dependence of rho-omega mixing and by competing mechanisms such as charge-dependent rho and omega couplings to nucleons.

## Method

The paper surveys three symmetry topics: charge symmetry breaking (CSB), parity nonconservation (PNC), and time reversal invariance (TRI). For CSB, the author examines the momentum dependence of rho-omega mixing from quark-loop calculations and its impact on nucleon-nucleon scattering asymmetries and mirror-nucleus mass differences. For PNC, the review covers isovector weak neutral current searches in light nuclei, compound-nuclear resonance enhancements of parity-violating asymmetries, and atomic parity violation including the nuclear anapole moment. For TRI, the author discusses beta-decay correlation measurements in A=8 nuclei, compound-nuclear enhancement of T-odd correlations, and electric dipole moment limits.

## Key numbers

* rho-omega mixing matrix element at q^2 = m_omega^2: -(4520 +/- 600) MeV^2, measured in e+e- -> pi+pi- cross sections
* Forward-backward asymmetry in np -> d pi^0 expected to be approximately 10^-3 to 10^-2 at about 300 MeV
* Cross section for d+d -> ^4He+pi^0 at 1.1 GeV: 0.97 +/- 0.20 +/- 0.15 pb/sr at theta_cm = 107 degrees
* Best quark model (DDH) prediction for neutral-current pion-nucleon parity-violating coupling f_pi^(0): approximately 5 x 10^-7; more recent value from Feldman et al.: 3 x 10^-7
* Charged-current (strangeness-changing) pion-nucleon parity-violating coupling f_pi^(+/-): approximately 4 x 10^-8
* R coefficient for T-odd correlation in ^8Li beta decay: (4 +/- 35) x 10^-4, limiting Im C_T to less than 0.010
* Neutron electric dipole moment upper limit: less than 8 x 10^-26 e-cm
* ^199Hg electric dipole moment upper limit: 8 x 10^-28 e-cm, limiting the strong CP parameter theta to less than 10^-10

## Context

This review extends the line of symmetry studies in nuclear physics summarized by Miller and Van Oers (1995) and by Henley and Miller (1979). It addresses the controversy sparked by Goldman, Henderson, and Thomas (1992) regarding the momentum dependence of rho-omega mixing, and discusses the DDH quark model calculations of Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980) for parity-violating couplings. The paper enables future experiments such as the TRIUMF np -> d pi^0 asymmetry measurement, the SAMPLE experiment at MIT to determine strange quark contributions to nucleon form factors, and improved searches for electric dipole moments in neutrons and atoms.
