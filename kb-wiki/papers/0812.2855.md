# On the QCD phase structure from effective models

> Some recent theoretical developments of the QCD phase diagram are summarized. Chiral symmetry restoration and the confinement/deconfinement transition at nonzero temperature and quark densities are analyzed in the framework of an effective linear sigma model with three light quark flavors. The sensitivity of the chiral transition as well as the existence of a critical end point in the phase diagram on the value of the sigma mass is explored. The influence of the axial anomaly on the chiral critical surface is addressed. Finally, the modifications by the inclusion of the Polyakov loop on the phase structure are investigated.

## Key claim

The existence and location of a critical end point (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram depend sensitively on the poorly known sigma meson mass and on the pion and kaon masses; for a sigma mass of 900 MeV the CEP disappears entirely, making the chiral transition a smooth crossover over the whole phase diagram.

## Method

The authors analyze the chiral phase transition using a three-flavor linear sigma model (quark-meson model) in mean-field approximation, where quantum and thermal meson fluctuations are neglected but quarks and antiquarks are retained as quantum fields. They then extend this to the Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model by coupling the Polyakov loop to the quark sector, using three different realizations of the Polyakov loop potential. The phase boundaries are extracted from the peak in the temperature derivative of the light nonstrange condensate and the Polyakov loop variable.

## Key numbers

- For a sigma mass of 600 MeV, the CEP is located at smaller temperatures and larger chemical potentials compared to recent lattice simulations; for m_sigma = 900 MeV the CEP disappears and the chiral transition becomes a smooth crossover over the entire phase diagram.
- With m_sigma = 800 MeV, when the pion mass is 1.36 times the physical value (m_pi* = 138 MeV, m_K* = 496 MeV), the CEP lies exactly on the mu-axis; for a pion mass below half the physical value the chiral transition becomes first-order for all densities.
- The chiral critical surface is evaluated as a function of pion and kaon masses for m_sigma = 800 MeV; the effect of the U(1)_A anomaly on the surface shape is marginal for kaon masses greater than 400 MeV.
- At mu = 0, the scaled pressure from the PQM model with three different Polyakov loop potentials shows reasonable agreement with 2+1-flavor lattice data for N_tau = 6 from Cheng (2007).

## Context

This work extends earlier two-flavor quark-meson model analyses by Schaefer (2007, 2006, 2004) to three flavors, enabling investigation of SU(3) x SU(3) chiral symmetry restoration including the axial U(1)_A anomaly. The results contradict the non-standard scenario predicted by de Forcrand and Philipsen (2002) where the first-order region shrinks with increasing chemical potential; instead the first-order region grows. The study also addresses the interplay between chiral and deconfinement transitions raised by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) regarding a possible quarkyonic phase, and the PQM model results are compared to lattice data from Cheng (2007).
