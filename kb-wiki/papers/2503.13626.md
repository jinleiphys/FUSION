# Comprehensive Analysis of Constructing Hybrid Stars with an RG-consistent NJL Model

> In this work, we investigate the properties of hadronic and quark matter that would allow for a first order phase transition between them within neutron stars. To this end, we use a parameterizable Relativistic Mean-Field (RMF) description for the hadronic phase and a Renormalization Group-consistent Nambu-Jona-Lasino (RG-NJL) model for the quark phase. This also enables us to consider sequential phase transitions involving a two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) and a color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase. We find large ranges for all parameters that facilitate a phase transition, even when constrained by current astrophysical data. We further attempt to filter out stars with a high chance of detectability by mass-radius measurement, i.e., stars with identical mass but different radii, so-called twin stars. However, we find that such configurations are outside the constrained parameter spaces. Instead, most of the mass-radius relations that feature a phase transition appear to be indistinguishable from a purely hadronic description.

## Key claim
The paper finds that while large parameter ranges for a first-order hadron-quark phase transition in neutron stars remain viable under current astrophysical constraints, twin star configurations (stars with identical mass but different radii) are effectively ruled out within their RG-consistent NJL model approach because the required parameter spaces are incompatible with the combined constraints from the 2 solar mass maximum mass requirement and the tidal deformability measurement from GW170817.

## Method
The authors use a parameterizable Relativistic Mean-Field (RMF) description for the hadronic phase and a Renormalization Group-consistent Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (RG-NJL) model for the quark phase, allowing for sequential phase transitions involving two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) and color-flavor-locked (CFL) phases. They model the hadron-quark transition with a Maxwell construction and systematically vary hadronic parameters (effective mass, slope parameter, symmetry energy, saturation density) and quark parameters (vector coupling, diquark coupling, bag constant) across over 700,000 combinations, filtering those that satisfy constraints from NICER, GW170817, and the 2 solar mass maximum mass requirement.

## Key numbers
- Over 700,000 parameter combinations were investigated; about 125,000 feature a possible transition point and 22,000 comply with astrophysical constraints.
- The effective mass range explored is m*/m = 0.55-0.75; the slope parameter L is varied between 40-60 MeV; the symmetry energy J between 30-32 MeV; saturation density n0 between 0.15-0.16 fm^{-3}.
- The vector coupling eta_V is varied from 0.0 to 1.5; the diquark coupling eta_D from 0.9 to 2.0; the bag constant B from 0 to 200 MeV/fm^3.
- For the twin star example PS2, the latent heat (energy density jump) at the hadron-2SC transition is Delta-epsilon = 370 MeV/fm^3.
- The RG-consistent cutoff scale is Lambda = 10 Lambda' = 6023 MeV.
- The tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 is Lambda_{1.4 M_sun} = 190^{+390}_{-120} at 90% credibility.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars using NJL models (Gholami 2024) and RMF parametrizations (Hornick 2018) by applying an RG-consistent regularization to avoid cutoff artifacts at high densities. It contradicts the possibility of observable twin stars within this framework, as found in earlier works using constant speed of sound approaches (Alford 2013) or other quark matter models. The results enable future studies to focus on the constrained parameter regions where hybrid stars remain indistinguishable from purely hadronic stars, and highlight the need for more precise radius measurements to detect phase transitions.
