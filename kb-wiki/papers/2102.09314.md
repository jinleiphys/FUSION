# Bayesian evaluation of charge yields of fission fragments of 239U

> Recent experiments [Phys. Rev. Lett. 123, 092503(2019); Phys. Rev. Lett. 118, 222501 (2017)] have made remarkable progress in measurements of the isotopic fission-fragment yields of the compound nucleus $^{239}$U, which is of great interests for fast-neutron reactors and for benchmarks of fission models. We apply the Bayesian neural network (BNN) approach to learn existing evaluated charge yields and infer the incomplete charge yields of $^{239}$U. We found the two-layer BNN is improved compared to the single-layer BNN for the overall performance. Our results support the normal charge yields of $^{239}$U around Sn and Mo isotopes. The role of odd-even effects in charge yields has also been studied.

## Key claim
The Bayesian neural network (BNN) evaluation of the charge yields of fission fragments from the compound nucleus 239U does not reproduce the abnormally small yields around Sn and Mo isotopes reported in one experiment (Wilson et al., 2017), instead supporting the normal yields seen in a later experiment (Ramos et al., 2019). This conclusion holds even when the BNN is trained without the odd-even input and without resampling the 238U data.

## Method
The authors applied a two-layer Bayesian neural network (BNN) with 16 neurons per hidden layer to learn 2303 evaluated charge-yield data points from the JENDL library for 29 fissioning nuclei, then used the trained network to predict the incomplete charge yields of 239U from two experimental datasets. The network inputs were the fragment charge number, the fissioning nucleus charge and mass numbers, and the excitation energy; an additional input variable δ = ±0.2 was used to indicate even or odd atomic numbers for studying odd-even effects. Predictions were made by Monte Carlo integration over the posterior distribution of network parameters, with 10^5 sampling iterations, and uncertainties were given as 95% confidence intervals.

## Key numbers
- The standard deviation χ²_N for the single-layer network (32 neurons) was 1.43×10⁻⁵, and for the double-layer network (16-16 neurons) it was 6.36×10⁻⁶, indicating improved learning performance.
- The normalization of the charge distributions was within 2% of the required value of 2.0.
- The BNN evaluation of the 2017 experiment (Wilson et al., 2017) at an excitation energy of 6.5 MeV and the 2019 experiment (Ramos et al., 2019) at 8.3 MeV both showed normal charge yields around Sn and Mo isotopes, contrary to the 600% deviation reported in the 2017 experiment.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous application of BNN to fission mass yields (Qiao et al., 2019) by using a multi-layer network to evaluate charge yields, which show distinct odd-even effects not present in mass distributions. The results resolve a discrepancy between two experiments on 239U: Wilson et al. (2017) reported abnormally small charge yields around Sn and Mo, while Ramos et al. (2019) found normal yields. The BNN evaluations support the latter and are comparable to the phenomenological GEF model evaluations, suggesting that machine learning can serve as a complementary tool for evaluating incomplete fission data with quantified uncertainties.
