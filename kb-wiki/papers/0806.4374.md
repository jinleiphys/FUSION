# Mach-like Angular Correlations Arise Only From the Head Zone of AdS/CFT String Jets

> We show that Mach-like azimuthal correlations of associated hadrons in the wake of heavy quark jets modeled in the AdS/CFT string drag picture originate only from the near-quark ``Head'' region. The contributions from the far zones lead only to a yield peaked in the opposite direction with respect to the trigger jet.

## Key claim

Mach-like double-peak azimuthal correlations in the wake of heavy quark jets modeled via the AdS/CFT string drag picture arise only from the non-equilibrium "head" region near the quark, not from the far hydrodynamic zones; once the head zone is excluded, the yield peaks only in the opposite direction relative to the trigger jet.

## Method

The authors used the trailing string solution of Herzog et al. (2006) to compute the energy-momentum tensor disturbances from a heavy quark moving at constant velocity \(v=0.9\) through a static, strongly-coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM plasma at temperature \(T_0\). They applied the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription to convert the flow velocity and temperature fields into an angular distribution of associated hadrons at mid-rapidity, subtracting the equilibrium background. The numerical stress tensor data from Gubser, Pufu, and Yarom (2008) were used, and the head zone was defined as the volume where the relative energy density disturbance \(\xi = \delta T_{00}/\varepsilon_0 > 0.3\).

## Key numbers

- Heavy quark velocity: \(v = 0.9\).
- 't Hooft coupling: \(\lambda = 5.5\); number of colors: \(N_c = 3\).
- Cooper-Frye volume boundaries: \(-14 < X_1\,(\pi T_0) < 1\), \(0 < X_\perp\,(\pi T_0) < 14\).
- Head zone defined by \(\xi > 0.3\), corresponding roughly to \(-1 < X_1\,(\pi T_0) < 1\) and \(0 < X_\perp\,(\pi T_0) < 2\).
- Transverse momentum bin: \(3 < p_T/(\pi T_0) < 4\).
- Mach angle: \(\phi_M = \cos^{-1}(c_s/v)\) with speed of sound \(c_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\).

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT trailing string model of heavy quark energy loss (Herzog et al., 2006) and the numerical computation of the stress tensor wake (Gubser, Pufu, and Yarom, 2008). It contradicts the expectation that Mach cone correlations arise from the near-equilibrium hydrodynamic wake; instead, the double-peak structure originates only from the non-equilibrium head zone. The results suggest that interpreting RHIC Mach-like correlations as evidence for fast thermalization of the medium requires careful subtraction of non-equilibrium jet regions, and motivate further study of the interplay between the head, neck, and far zones in the freeze-out of AdS/CFT jet wakes.
