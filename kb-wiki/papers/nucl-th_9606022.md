# Few-Body Quantum Problem in the Boundary-Condition Model

> Systems of three and four quantum particles in the boundary-condition model are considered. The Faddeev-Yakubovsky approach is applied to construct the Fredholm-type integral equations for these systems in framework of the Potential theory. The boundary-value problems are formulated for the Faddeev-Yakubovsky components of wave functions.

## Key claim

The Faddeev-Yakubovsky approach can be applied within the framework of Potential theory to construct Fredholm-type integral equations for three- and four-particle systems in the boundary-condition model, provided the singular diagonal part of the integral operator is extracted and inverted explicitly, thereby overcoming the mathematical difficulties that arise from the noncompact nature of the boundary surfaces.

## Method

The author formulates the few-body quantum problem with pair interactions described by boundary conditions (hard-core Dirichlet or third-type) on surfaces in configuration space, using the Faddeev-Yakubovsky decomposition of the wave function. For the three-body case, the Potential theory is used to derive integral equations for the density of a simple layer on the boundary surface; these equations are then transformed into Faddeev-type equations by extracting and inverting the diagonal part of the integral operator. For both three- and four-body systems, the formalism of generalized potentials (quasipotentials involving delta-functions and their normal derivatives) is employed to reformulate the boundary conditions, leading to differential equations for the Faddeev and Yakubovsky components of the wave function with explicit two-sided boundary conditions on the cylinders.

## Key numbers

- The configuration space for the N-body system is \(\mathbb{R}^{(N-1)n}\), where \(n\) is the dimension of particles (usually \(n=3\)).
- The cylinders \(\Gamma_\alpha\) are \([(N-1)n-1]\)-dimensional surfaces in \(\mathbb{R}^{(N-1)n}\), given by \(\Gamma_\alpha = \partial\omega_\alpha \times \mathbb{R}^{(N-2)n}\).
- The Green function of the Laplacian in \(L_2(\mathbb{R}^\nu)\) is given explicitly as \(R_0(X,X',z) = \frac{i}{4} \left(\frac{\sqrt{z}}{2\pi}\right) \frac{H_{(\nu-2)/2}^{(1)}(\sqrt{z}|X-X'|)}{|X-X'|^{(\nu-2)/2}}\), with \(H^{(1)}\) the Hankel function of the first kind.
- The normal derivative of the simple-layer potential at a smooth point \(S\) on the boundary satisfies \(\lim_{X\rightarrow S} \frac{\partial U}{\partial n_S} = \mp \frac{1}{2}\mu(S) + \int_{\partial\Omega} \frac{\partial}{\partial n_S} R_0(S,S',z)\mu(S') d\sigma_{S'}\), where the upper (lower) sign corresponds to the inside (outside) limit.

## Context

This work extends the earlier attempts by Efimov and Schultz (1976), Kim and Tubis (1974), and Brayshaw (1974) to regularize the three-body boundary-condition model, which had not yielded Fredholm-type equations. The author builds on the approach of Merkuriev and Motovilov (1990-1995) using Potential theory, and applies the Faddeev method (Faddeev, 1963) to transform the resulting integral equations into Fredholm form. The formalism enables rigorous treatment of scattering theory and bound-state problems for three- and four-body systems with hard-core or third-type boundary conditions, with concrete computational results for three-body scattering and bound-state energies reported in Motovilov (1995) and Efros, Chulkov, and Yakovlev (1995).
