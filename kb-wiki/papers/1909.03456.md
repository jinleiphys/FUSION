# $Δ$ Contribution to $ pp \to ppπ^0$

> A model independent discussion of $Δ-$production in $pp \to pp π^0$ is presented and the $Δ-$contributions to the 16 $pp \to ppπ^0$ partial wave amplitudes are identified taking the $Ds$ and $Sd$ channels also into consideration.

## Key claim

The paper provides a model-independent identification of how the nine partial-wave amplitudes for the intermediate process \(pp \to p\Delta^+\) map onto the sixteen partial-wave amplitudes for the full reaction \(pp \to pp\pi^0\), including contributions from the \(Ds\) and \(Sd\) channels that were neglected in earlier analyses.

## Method

The authors use irreducible tensor techniques to express the reaction matrix for \(pp \to p\Delta^+\) in terms of nine threshold partial-wave amplitudes \(F_1,\ldots,F_9\) (Table II), covering \(l_2 = 0,1\) for the \(\Delta\)-proton relative orbital angular momentum. They then derive the connection between these amplitudes and the sixteen partial-wave amplitudes \(f_1,\ldots,f_{16}\) for \(pp \to pp\pi^0\) (Table III) by recoupling the spherical harmonics describing the \(\Delta\) decay and the final-state proton momenta, using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and \(9j\) symbols. The mapping is summarized in Table IV, which shows which \(F_i\) contribute to each class of \(pp\pi^0\) amplitudes (\(Ss\), \(Ps\), \(Pp\), \(Sd\), \(Ds\)).

## Key numbers

- The number of non-zero irreducible tensor amplitudes for \(pp \to p\Delta^+\) is reduced to 16 from a possible 32 (Eq. 18 and surrounding text).
- Nine partial-wave amplitudes \(F_1,\ldots,F_9\) for \(pp \to p\Delta^+\) are listed in Table II, covering \(l_2 = 0,1\).
- Sixteen partial-wave amplitudes \(f_1,\ldots,f_{16}\) for \(pp \to pp\pi^0\) are listed in Table III, including the \(Ds\) and \(Sd\) channels.
- The mapping in Table IV shows that the \(Ss\) amplitude \(f_1\) derives contribution only from \(F_2\); \(Ps\) amplitudes derive contributions from \(F_1, F_2, F_4, F_5, F_7\); \(Pp\) amplitudes derive contributions from \(F_2, F_8\); and \(Sd\) and \(Ds\) amplitudes derive contributions from \(F_4, F_5\).

## Context

This work extends the model-independent approach of Venkataraya et al. (2015) and Ramachandran et al. (2004, 2006) by including the \(Ds\) and \(Sd\) channels that were neglected in the analysis of Meyer et al. (2004) for \(\vec{p}\vec{p} \to pp\pi^0\). It addresses the shortcoming noted in earlier comparisons with the Julich meson exchange model (2000), where the \(\Delta\) degree of freedom was found important but the phase ambiguity of the threshold \(Ss\) amplitude remained unresolved. The results enable future empirical extraction of the \(\Delta\)-production contributions from spin-observable measurements in \(pp \to pp\pi^0\).
