# Neutron density fluctuation and neutron-proton correlation from AMPT model

> Using the multiphase transport (AMPT) model, we study the relative neutron density fluctuation and neutron-proton correlation in matter produced by Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = $7.7-200 GeV. The rapidity, centrality, and energy dependence of these two observations are also discussed. The light nuclei yield ratio of proton, deuteron, and triton $N_tN_p/N_d^2$ calculated directly from the relative neutron density fluctuation and neutron-proton correlation, decreases with rapidity coverage and increases with collision centrality. Our study also found that the ratio does not exhibit any non-monotonic behavior in collision energy dependence. Since there is no first-order phase transition or critical physics in the AMPT model, our work provides a reference for extracting the relative neutron density fluctuation from light nuclei production in experiments.

# Neutron density fluctuation and neutron-proton correlation from AMPT model

## Key claim
Using the AMPT model, which contains no first-order phase transition or critical physics, the light nuclei yield ratio \(N_tN_p/N_d^2\) shows no non-monotonic behavior as a function of collision energy, providing a baseline reference for extracting the relative neutron density fluctuation from experimental light nuclei production.

## Method
The string melting version of the AMPT model was used to generate about one million Au+Au collision events at each of nine beam energies from \(\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 7.7\) to 200 GeV. Event-by-event neutron and proton multiplicities were extracted for different rapidity ranges and centrality classes (defined by charged particle multiplicity in \(|\eta| \leq 0.5\)), and the dimensionless relative neutron density fluctuation \(\Delta\rho_n = \sigma_n^2/\langle\rho_n\rangle^2\) and neutron-proton correlation \(C_{np} = \text{Cov}(\rho_n,\rho_p)/(\langle\rho_n\rangle\langle\rho_p\rangle)\) were computed directly from the nucleon densities. The light nuclei yield ratio was then calculated from these quantities using the coalescence model formula \(R = N_tN_p/N_d^2 = (1+\Delta\rho_n+2C_{np})/[2\sqrt{3}(1+C_{np})^2]\).

## Key numbers
- The relative neutron density fluctuations \(\sigma_p/\langle\rho_p\rangle\) and \(\sigma_n/\langle\rho_n\rangle\) decrease with increasing rapidity coverage for 0-10% central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 200\) GeV.
- The correlation coefficient \(r_{np}\) converges to about 0.6 in central collisions and about 0.85 in 60-80% peripheral collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 39\) GeV.
- The neutron-proton correlation \(C_{np}\) is approximately 0.45-0.5 in 60-80% peripheral Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 39\) GeV, independent of rapidity coverage.
- The light nuclei yield ratio from AMPT increases slightly with collision energy and shows no peak; experimental STAR data show a peak around \(\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 20-30\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends the coalescence model framework of Sun et al. (2018) and Shao et al. (2018) by explicitly computing the neutron-proton correlation \(C_{np}\) in the AMPT model, which previous studies often assumed to be negligible or energy-independent. The results provide a baseline without critical physics for interpreting the non-monotonic energy dependence of the light nuclei yield ratio observed by the STAR collaboration (2022) and NA49 collaboration (2004) in central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions. The study enables experimental extraction of the relative neutron density fluctuation from light nuclei ratios, particularly in central and mid-central collisions where \(C_{np}\) effects are small.
