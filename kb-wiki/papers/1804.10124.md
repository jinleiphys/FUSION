# Critical Exponents of Nonequilibrium Phase Transitions in AdS/CFT Correspondence

> We study critical phenomena of nonequilibrium phase transitions by using the AdS/CFT correspondence. Our system consists of charged particles interacting with a heat bath of neutral gauge particles. The system is in current-driven nonequilibrium steady state, and the nonequilibrium phase transition is associated with nonlinear electric conductivity. We define a susceptibility as a response of the system to the current variation. We further define a critical exponent from the power-law divergence of the susceptibility. We find that the critical exponent and the critical amplitude ratio of the susceptibility agree with those of the Landau theory of equilibrium phase transitions, if we identify the current as the external field in the Landau theory.

# Critical Exponents of Nonequilibrium Phase Transitions in AdS/CFT Correspondence

## Key claim

The critical exponent gamma (defined from the power-law divergence of a susceptibility with respect to current variation) and the critical amplitude ratio of that susceptibility agree with those of the Landau theory of equilibrium phase transitions, provided one identifies the electric current density as the external field. This agreement holds within numerical error for both the conductivity and the chiral condensate as order parameters, but the authors note that the physical definitions of the exponents should be distinguished from their equilibrium counterparts.

## Method

The authors employ the D3-D7 system in the AdS/CFT correspondence, where the D7-brane is embedded in a 5-dimensional AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background. They solve the Euler-Lagrange equation for the D7-brane embedding numerically, using the Dirac-Born-Infeld action, to obtain the current density J versus electric field E characteristics at various temperatures. A susceptibility is defined as the derivative of the order parameter (conductivity or chiral condensate) with respect to current density, and its power-law divergence near the critical point is fitted to extract the critical exponent gamma.

- Critical exponent gamma in NDC phase (from conductivity): 1.018 ± 0.043
- Critical exponent gamma in PDC phase (from conductivity): 1.014 ± 0.042
- Critical exponent gamma in crossover region (from conductivity): 1.022 ± 0.025

- Exponent alpha from conductivity average: 0.048 ± 0.111

## Context

This work extends the earlier holographic study of nonequilibrium phase transitions by Nakamura and collaborators (2010, 2011), who discovered first-order and second-order nonequilibrium phase transitions associated with nonlinear electric conductivity in the D3-D7 system and reported critical exponents beta and delta. The present paper adds the critical exponent gamma and the critical amplitude ratio, showing that all three exponents (beta = 1/2, delta = 3, gamma = 1) match the Landau theory of equilibrium phase transitions. This suggests that the current density plays a role analogous to the external magnetic field in equilibrium ferromagnets. The results enable future work on formulating a Landau-like theory for nonequilibrium phase transitions and on defining the critical exponent alpha, which remains an open question because the notion of heat capacity in nonequilibrium steady states is not clear.
