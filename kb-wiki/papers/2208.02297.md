# Continuum-extrapolated NNLO Valence PDF of Pion at the Physical Point

> We present lattice QCD calculations of valence parton distribution function (PDF) of pion employing next-to-next-leading-order (NNLO) perturbative QCD matching. Our calculations are based on three gauge ensembles of 2+1 flavor highly improved staggered quarks and Wilson--Clover valance quarks, corresponding to pion mass $m_π=140$~MeV at a lattice spacing $a=0.076$~fm and $m_π=300$~MeV at $a=0.04, 0.06$~fm. This enables us to present, for the first time, continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results for NNLO valence PDF of the pion at the physical point. Applying leading-twist expansion for renormalization group invariant (RGI) ratios of bi-local pion matrix elements with NNLO Wilson coefficients we extract $2^{\mathrm{nd}}$, $4^{\mathrm{th}}$ and $6^{\mathrm{th}}$ Mellin moments of the PDF. We reconstruct the Bjorken-$x$ dependence of the NNLO PDF from real-space RGI ratios using a deep neural network (DNN) as well as from momentum-space matrix elements renormalized using a hybrid-scheme. All our results are in broad agreement with the results of global fits to the experimental data carried out by the xFitter and JAM collaborations.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results for the NNLO valence PDF of the pion at the physical point, using both real-space and x-space matching, with results in broad agreement with global fits from the xFitter and JAM collaborations.

## Method

The authors computed bare matrix elements of bilocal quark-bilinear operators with Wilson-Clover valence quarks on three 2+1 flavor HISQ gauge ensembles: one at physical pion mass (140 MeV) with lattice spacing 0.076 fm, and two at 300 MeV pion mass with lattice spacings 0.04 and 0.06 fm. They extracted the ground-state matrix elements using two-state fits (Fit(2,3)) and summation methods, then formed renormalization-group-invariant ratios using a nonzero reference momentum to cancel UV divergences and reduce wrap-around effects. The NNLO leading-twist operator product expansion was applied to extract the 2nd, 4th, and 6th Mellin moments, and the x-dependence was reconstructed using a deep neural network from real-space RGI ratios as well as from hybrid-scheme renormalized matrix elements via LaMET matching.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: 140 MeV (physical point) at lattice spacing a = 0.076 fm; 300 MeV at a = 0.04 fm and 0.06 fm
- Maximum pion momentum achieved: 1.78 GeV for the physical mass ensemble, 2.42 GeV for the 300 MeV ensembles
- Lattice extent: 64 x 64^3 for the a = 0.076 fm ensemble
- Reference momentum P_z^0 = 0.25 GeV (2 pi / L_s) used for the physical mass ensemble
- First excited state energy E_1 consistent with dispersion relation using m_pi' approximately 1.3 GeV
- Wrap-around effects estimated at up to 3% for P_z = 0 with m_pi L_t less than or equal to 3.5

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of the pion PDF that used NLO perturbative matching and heavier-than-physical pion masses, such as those by Fan (2021), Gao (2020, 2021), and the JAM collaboration (2022). It also builds on the NNLO matching formalism developed by Li (2020) and Chen (2020), and on the ratio-scheme renormalization approach advocated by Gao (2020). The results enable more precise comparisons with experimental global fits and provide continuum-extrapolated NNLO results that can constrain future phenomenological analyses of pion structure.
