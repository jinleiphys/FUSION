# Cluster Expansion Model for QCD Baryon Number Fluctuations: No Phase Transition at $μ_B / T < π$

> A Cluster Expansion Model (CEM), representing a relativistic extension of Mayer's cluster expansion, is constructed to study baryon number fluctuations in QCD. The temperature dependent first two coefficients, corresponding to the partial pressures in the baryon number $B = 1$ and $B = 2$ sectors, are the only model input, which we fix by recent lattice data at imaginary baryochemical potential. All other coefficients are constructed in terms of the first two and required to match the Stefan-Boltzmann limit at $T \to \infty$. The CEM allows calculations of the baryon number susceptibilities $χ_k^B$ to arbitrary order. We obtain excellent agreement with available lattice data for the baryon fluctuation measures $χ_2^B$, $χ_4^B$, $χ_6^B$ and predict higher order susceptibilities, that are not yet available from Lattice QCD. The calculated susceptibilities are then used to extract the radius of convergence of the Taylor expansion of the pressure. The commonly used ratio test fails due to the non-trivial asymptotic behavior of the Taylor coefficients. At the same time, a more elaborate estimator provides finite convergence radii at all temperatures and in agreement with the singularities of Padé approximants. The associated singularities lie in the complex $μ_B/T$-plane and appear smoothly connected to the Roberge-Weiss transition at high temperatures and imaginary chemical potential. No evidence for a phase transition at $μ_B / T \lesssim π$ is found.

# Cluster Expansion Model for QCD Baryon Number Fluctuations: No Phase Transition at μ_B / T < π

## Key claim

The Cluster Expansion Model (CEM), constructed from only the first two Fourier coefficients of the baryon density expansion (fixed by lattice QCD data at imaginary chemical potential), shows no evidence for a phase transition or critical point at real baryochemical potential values satisfying μ_B / T ≲ π for temperatures above 135 MeV. The radius of convergence of the Taylor expansion of the QCD pressure is finite at all temperatures considered and is determined by singularities in the complex μ_B/T-plane that appear smoothly connected to the Roberge-Weiss transition at high temperatures and imaginary chemical potential, rather than by a real-axis phase transition.

## Method

The authors construct a Cluster Expansion Model (CEM) as a relativistic extension of Mayer's cluster expansion, where the QCD pressure is expressed as a fugacity series in baryon number sectors. The temperature-dependent first two Fourier coefficients b₁(T) and b₂(T) (corresponding to partial pressures in the |B|=1 and |B|=2 sectors) are taken as input from lattice QCD simulations at imaginary μ_B (CEM-LQCD) or from a hadron resonance gas with excluded-volume interactions (CEM-HRG). All higher-order coefficients b_k(T) are constructed from the first two using a recursion relation motivated by two-particle hard-core interactions, with temperature-independent coefficients α_k fixed by requiring the Stefan-Boltzmann limit of non-interacting quarks and gluons as T → ∞. The baryon number susceptibilities χ_{2n}^B at μ_B=0 are then computed to arbitrarily high order by summing the series, and the radius of convergence of the Taylor expansion of the pressure is extracted using the Mercer-Roberts estimator and cross-checked with Padé approximants.

## Key numbers

- The CEM-LQCD model reproduces lattice data for b₃ and b₄ quantitatively for all temperatures 135 ≤ T ≤ 230 MeV (Fig. 2).
- For χ₂^B at T = 230 MeV, k_max ≃ 80 terms are required in the sum to obtain the full result; at T ≲ 160 MeV, k_max = 2 suffices.
- The radius of convergence r_{μ/T} approaches the asymptotic value r_{μ/T} = π at higher temperatures T > 190 MeV.
- The Roberge-Weiss transition endpoint temperature from lattice studies is T_RW = 208 ± 5 MeV, where r_{μ/T} is already almost indistinguishable from π in CEM-LQCD.
- The CEM-HRG model reproduces CEM-LQCD results up to T ≃ 180-190 MeV for all considered observables, but diverges at higher temperatures.
- At μ_B = 0, the CEM-HRG convergence condition is satisfied only up to T ≃ 195 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the fugacity expansion approach to QCD thermodynamics previously applied in lattice studies at imaginary chemical potential (deForcrand 2002, D'Elia 2002) and in the canonical approach (Hasenfratz 1991, Alexandru 2005). The CEM builds on earlier hadron resonance gas with excluded-volume interactions (Vovchenko 2017) by adding the Stefan-Boltzmann constraint at high temperatures. The finding that the radius of convergence is determined by complex singularities connected to the Roberge-Weiss transition (Roberge and Weiss 1986) rather than a real-axis critical point contradicts various QCD critical point estimates from lattice reweighting (Fodor 2004), experimental finite-size scaling (Lacey 2014), Dyson-Schwinger (Fischer 2014), and holographic approaches (Critelli 2017, Li 2017). The CEM enables predictions for higher-order susceptibilities χ₈^B, χ₁₀^B, and χ₁₂^B that can be tested by future lattice simulations.
