# Towards scalable bound-to-resonance extrapolations for few- and many-body systems

> In open quantum many-body systems, the theoretical description of resonant states of many particles strongly coupled to the continuum can be challenging. Such states are commonplace in, for example, exotic nuclei and hadrons, and can reveal important information about the underlying forces at play in these systems. In this work, we demonstrate that the complex-augmented eigenvector continuation (CA-EC) method, originally formulated for the two-body problem with uniform complex scaling, can reliably perform bound-to-resonance extrapolations for genuine three-body resonances having no bound subsystems. We first establish that three-body bound-to-resonance extrapolations are possible by benchmarking different few-body approaches, and we provide arguments to explain how the extrapolation works in the many-body case. We furthermore pave the way towards scalable resonance extrapolations in many-body systems by showing that the CA-EC method also works in the Berggren basis, studying a realistic application using the Gamow shell model.

## Key claim

The complex-augmented eigenvector continuation (CA-EC) method, previously demonstrated for two-body systems, can reliably perform bound-to-resonance extrapolations for genuine three-body resonances that have no bound subsystems, and the method also works in the Berggren basis, paving the way toward scalable many-body applications.

## Method

The authors implemented CA-EC using two different numerical approaches for three-body systems: a configuration interaction method based on harmonic oscillator states in Jacobi coordinates, and a discrete variable representation in finite volume. They also demonstrated CA-EC in the Berggren basis using a two-body toy model with a Gaussian potential, and applied the method to a realistic Gamow shell model calculation for the oxygen isotope chain. Training bound states were generated by varying an attractive term in the potential, and the reduced basis included both the complex-scaled bound-state wave functions and their complex conjugates.

## Key numbers

* For the three-boson toy model with pairwise potential V(r) = 2 exp(-((r-3)/1.5)^2), a bosonic three-body resonance was identified near Re(E) ≈ 4.1 (arbitrary units) in the A1+ representation of the cubic group.
* The harmonic oscillator basis truncation used 2n1 + l1 + 2n2 + l2 ≤ 40 for the three-body L=0 state.
* The Berggren basis contour for the two-body toy model was discretized with 128 Gauss-Legendre quadrature points per segment, with segments defined by k0 = 0.0, k1 = 0.4 - 0.15i, k2 = 0.8, and k3 = 0.6.
* The Berggren basis for the two-body toy model included a resonant pole at E ≈ 0.168 - 0.041i (arbitrary units).

## Context

This work extends the CA-EC method introduced by Yapa et al. (2023) for two-body bound-to-resonance extrapolations to genuine three-body resonances, which exhibit more complex decay dynamics such as sequential two-body decays. The demonstration that CA-EC works in the Berggren basis, as used in the Gamow shell model, enables future scalable calculations of broad many-body resonances in exotic nuclei and hadrons, where direct calculations become increasingly difficult due to computational cost and stability issues.
