# Nuclear fragmentation induced by low-energy antiprotons within a microscopic transport approach

> Within the framework of the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model, the nuclear fragmentation induced by low-energy antiprotons has been investigated thoroughly. A coalescence approach is developed for constructing the primary fragments in phase space. The secondary decay process of the fragments is described by the well-known statistical code. It is found that the localized energy released in antibaryon-baryon annihilation is deposited in a nucleus mainly via pion-nucleon collisions, which leads to the emissions of pre-equilibrium particles, fission, evaporation of nucleons and light fragments etc. The strangeness exchange reactions dominate the hyperon production. The averaged mass loss increases with the mass number of target nucleus. A bump structure in the domain of intermediate mass for heavy targets appears owing to the contribution of fission fragments.

# Nuclear fragmentation induced by low-energy antiprotons within a microscopic transport approach

## Key claim
The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model, combined with a coalescence approach for primary fragments and the GEMINI statistical code for secondary decay, can reproduce available LEAR data on fragment mass and charge distributions from antiproton-induced reactions at 200 MeV/c, but the primary fragments in the mass region 100 < A < 160 are highly excited and their yields are overestimated before secondary decay is applied.

## Method
The LQMD transport model was used to simulate antiproton-nucleus collisions, including annihilation channels, charge-exchange reactions, and strangeness exchange, with mean-field potentials for antinucleons derived from G-parity transformation scaled by a factor ξ = 0.25. Primary fragments were constructed at freeze-out using a coalescence model with relative momentum cutoff P0 = 200 MeV/c and relative distance cutoff R0 = 3 fm. The de-excitation of these primary fragments was then treated with the statistical code GEMINI, assuming zero angular momentum.

## Key numbers
- The antiproton-nucleus optical potential at zero momentum and normal nuclear density is V_opt(p=0, ρ=ρ0) = -164 MeV after scaling with ξ = 0.25 (compared to -655 MeV from pure G-parity).
- Average excitation energies for target nuclei are 157 MeV for ⁹⁵Mo and 415 MeV for ¹⁶⁵Ho, evaluated from the fragment cross-section distribution.
- Average excitation energies for ¹²⁴Sn and ¹³⁸Ba are 238 MeV and 288 MeV, respectively.
- On average, 17.8 nucleons are emitted from the target ⁹⁵Mo, including 3.1 nucleons from the preequilibrium stage, leading to an estimated excitation energy of 161.7 MeV (assuming 8 MeV separation energy and 3 MeV kinetic energy per nucleon).
- The incident antiproton momentum used throughout is 200 MeV/c (kinetic energy of 21 MeV).

## Context
This work extends the LQMD transport model, previously applied to heavy-ion collisions and hadron-induced reactions (Feng 2011, 2013, 2014), to antiproton-induced fragmentation. It reproduces fragment yields measured at the LEAR facility at CERN (Lubkiewicz 2002; Møller 1989) and provides a framework for future antiproton physics at the PANDA facility at Darmstadt. The approach contrasts with earlier intranuclear cascade models (Cugnon 1989) and the GiBUU transport model (Larionov 2009; Gaitanos 2012) by incorporating a coalescence plus statistical decay treatment that captures both preequilibrium emission and fission contributions, particularly the bump structure around A ~ 90 from fission of heavy fragments.
