# A Lattice Study of Quark and Glue Momenta and Angular Momenta in the Nucleon

> We report a complete calculation of the quark and glue momenta and angular momenta in the proton. These include the quark contributions from both the connected and disconnected insertions. The quark disconnected insertion loops are computed with $Z_4$ noise, and the signal-to-noise is improved with unbiased subtractions. The glue operator is comprised of gauge-field tensors constructed from the overlap operator. The calculation is carried out on a $16^3 \times 24$ quenched lattice at $β= 6.0$ for Wilson fermions with $κ=0.154, 0.155$, and $0.1555$ which correspond to pion masses at $650, 538$, and $478$~MeV, respectively. The chirally extrapolated $u$ and $d$ quark momentum/angular momentum fraction is found to be $0.64(5)/0.70(5)$, the strange momentum/angular momentum fraction is $0.024(6)/0.023(7)$, and that of the glue is $0.33(6)/0.28(8)$. The previous study of quark spin on the same lattice revealed that it carries a fraction of $0.25(12)$ of proton spin. The orbital angular momenta of the quarks are then obtained from subtracting the spin from their corresponding angular momentum components. We find that the quark orbital angular momentum constitutes $0.47(13)$ of the proton spin with almost all of it coming from the disconnected insertions.

## Key claim

This work provides the first complete lattice calculation of both quark and glue momentum and angular momentum fractions in the proton, including disconnected insertions, on a quenched lattice; the results satisfy the momentum and angular momentum sum rules after perturbative renormalization to the \(\overline{MS}\) scheme at 2 GeV, but the calculation is performed at unphysically heavy pion masses (478–650 MeV) and requires chiral extrapolation.

## Method

The calculation uses a \(16^3 \times 24\) quenched lattice at \(\beta = 6.0\) with Wilson fermions at three hopping parameters corresponding to pion masses of 650, 538, and 478 MeV. Quark disconnected insertion loops are computed with \(Z_4\) noise and unbiased subtraction for variance reduction, while the glue operator is constructed from gauge-field tensors derived from the overlap Dirac operator. The form factors \(T_1(q^2)\) and \(T_2(q^2)\) for both quarks and glue are extracted from two-point and three-point correlation functions at non-zero momentum transfer and separately extrapolated to \(q^2 \rightarrow 0\) to obtain the momentum fraction \(\langle x \rangle = T_1(0)\) and the angular momentum fraction \(J = \frac{1}{2}[T_1(0) + T_2(0)]\).

## Key numbers

- Chirally extrapolated \(u\) and \(d\) quark momentum fraction: \(0.64(5)\)
- Chirally extrapolated \(u\) and \(d\) quark angular momentum fraction: \(0.70(5)\)
- Strange quark momentum fraction: \(0.024(6)\)
- Strange quark angular momentum fraction: \(0.023(7)\)
- Glue momentum fraction: \(0.33(6)\)
- Glue angular momentum fraction: \(0.28(8)\)
- Quark spin fraction from previous study on same lattice: \(0.25(12)\) of proton spin
- Quark orbital angular momentum fraction: \(0.47(13)\) of proton spin, with almost all from disconnected insertions
- Pion masses used: 650, 538, and 478 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of quark spin (Dong 1995) and connected-insertion orbital angular momenta (Mathur 1999, Gockeler 2003) by including disconnected insertions and the first calculation of glue angular momentum, addressing the "Dark Spin" conundrum where approximately 50% of the proton spin remained unaccounted for. The results enable future calculations with dynamical fermions at physical pion masses and provide lattice-QCD input for understanding the spin structure of the nucleon, complementing experimental constraints on gluon helicity from COMPASS, STAR, HERMES, and PHENIX.
