# Omega_bbb excited-state spectroscopy from lattice QCD

> Triply heavy baryons are very interesting systems analogous to heavy quarkonia, but are difficult to access experimentally. Lattice QCD can provide precise predictions for these systems, which can be compared to other theoretical approaches. In this work, the spectrum of excited states of the Omega_bbb baryon is calculated using lattice NRQCD for the b quarks, and using a domain-wall action for the u, d and s sea quarks. The calculations are done for multiple values of the sea-quark masses, and for two different lattice spacings. The energies of states with angular momentum up to J=7/2 are calculated, and the effects of rotational symmetry breaking by the lattice are analyzed. Precise results are obtained even for the small spin-dependent energy splittings, and the contributions of individual NRQCD interactions to these energy splittings are studied. The results are compared to potential-model calculations.

# Omega_bbb excited-state spectroscopy from lattice QCD

## Key claim

Lattice QCD can provide precise predictions for the excited-state spectrum of the Omega_bbb baryon, resolving even the smallest spin-dependent energy splittings that are neglected in potential models; however, the results are obtained using nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) for the b quarks, meaning only energy differences are physical, and the calculations include systematic uncertainties from the lattice spacing, sea-quark masses, and NRQCD interaction terms.

## Method

The spectrum of Omega_bbb excited states was calculated using lattice QCD with NRQCD for the b quarks and a domain-wall action for the u, d, and s sea quarks, on gauge-field configurations generated by the RBC/UKQCD collaboration with the Iwasaki gauge action. Baryon interpolating operators with up to two gauge-covariant derivatives were constructed and subduced into irreducible representations of the double cover of the octahedral group, allowing extraction of energies for states with angular momentum up to J=7/2 from Euclidean two-point functions. Calculations were performed at two lattice spacings (approximately 0.11 fm and 0.08 fm) and multiple sea-quark masses, with chiral extrapolations to the physical pion mass and simultaneous fits across different irreducible representations to account for rotational symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state Omega_bbb mass from prior lattice QCD work: 14.371 ± 0.004_stat ± 0.011_syst ± 0.001_exp GeV (Meinel, 2010)
- Lattice spacings used: approximately 0.11 fm (from ensembles with β=2.13) and 0.08 fm (from ensembles with β=2.25)
- Pion masses on the ensembles ranged from 0.2950(40) GeV (finer lattice, a m_u,d=0.004) to 0.641(15) GeV (coarser lattice, a m_u,d=0.03)
- For the negative-parity splitting with only the σ·B operator turned on: E_1(1/2^-) - E_1(3/2^-) = -12.97(45) MeV (at a≈0.11 fm, a m_u,d=0.005)
- Spin-dependent order-v^6 interactions change the bbb spin splittings by up to 30% when included

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD calculation of the Omega_bbb ground state (Meinel, 2010) to excited states, providing nonperturbative results that can be compared to potential-model calculations such as that of Silvestre-Brac (1996), which uses a sum of two-body interactions with spin-spin but no spin-orbit or tensor interactions. The lattice results show that degeneracies predicted by the potential model (e.g., E_2(1/2^+)=E_3(3/2^+)=E_1(5/2^+)=E_1(7/2^+)) are lifted in full QCD, and the study of individual NRQCD interaction contributions enables identification of spin-orbit effects. The results enable further theoretical work on triply heavy baryons and provide benchmarks for effective field theories and quark models.
