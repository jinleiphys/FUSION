# Construction of Consistent Meson Exchange Currents by Laplace Transform

> We propose a new method for the construction of a consistent meson exchange current in $r$-space for the spin-isospin dependent central and tensor part of phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potentials by using a Laplace transformation, which allows the representation by a finite number of Yukawa functions. This method is applied to the Paris and the recent Argonne $V_{18}$ potentials. Results are presented for electrodisintegration of the deuteron near threshold.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new method for constructing a consistent meson exchange current (MEC) in r-space for the spin-isospin dependent central and tensor parts of phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potentials, using a Laplace transform representation as a superposition of Yukawa functions; the method is not unique, as it requires an arbitrary modification of the short-range part to eliminate a delta-function term.

## Method

The authors represent the radial central and tensor parts of a spin-isospin dependent NN-potential as a continuous superposition of Yukawa functions via a Laplace transform, then discretize the integral using Gauss quadrature to obtain a finite sum. The coefficients of the Yukawa functions are determined by solving a set of linear equations from a chosen radial grid, and the potential is split into pi-like and rho-like parts for which the corresponding MEC operators are known. The method is applied to the Paris potential and the Argonne V18 potential, and the resulting MECs are tested by calculating the inclusive cross section for deuteron electrodisintegration near threshold.

## Key numbers

* For the Paris potential with N=16 Gauss points, the relative mean deviation of the Laplace transform representation from the original central potential is 0.54 x 10^-6, and for the tensor potential it is 0.72 x 10^-5.
* For the Argonne V18 potential with N=16 Gauss points, the relative mean deviation of the Laplace transform representation from the original central potential is 0.58 x 10^-4, and for the tensor potential it is 0.11 x 10^-3.
* For the Paris potential, the relative deviation of the inclusive cross section for deuteron electrodisintegration near threshold (E_np = 1.5 MeV, theta_e = 155 degrees) between the Laplace transform MEC for N=12 and the original MEC of Buchmann et al. (1985) does not exceed 1 percent; for N=16 it is less than 0.1 percent.
* For the Argonne V18 potential, the relative deviation of the same cross section between N=12 and N=20 is about 0.2 percent, and for N=16 it is less than 0.01 percent.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Buchmann et al. (1985) for constructing consistent MECs from the Paris potential, which required the potential to already be in Yukawa form, to any realistic r-space potential by using a Laplace transform representation. It also provides an alternative to the momentum-space method of Riska (1985). The method enables the construction of consistent MECs for modern high-precision phenomenological potentials like the Argonne V18, allowing r-space calculations of electromagnetic reactions on nuclei such as deuteron electrodisintegration near threshold, where MEC contributions are known to be dominant at higher momentum transfers.
