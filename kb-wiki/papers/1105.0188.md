# The Hawking-Page crossover in noncommutative anti-deSitter space

> We study the problem of a Schwarzschild-anti-deSitter black hole in a noncommutative geometry framework, thought to be an effective description of quantum-gravitational spacetime. As a first step we derive the noncommutative geometry inspired Schwarzschild-anti-deSitter solution. After studying the horizon structure, we find that the curvature singularity is smeared out by the noncommutative fluctuations. On the thermodynamics side, we show that the black hole temperature, instead of a divergent behavior at small scales, admits a maximum value. This fact implies an extension of the Hawking-Page transition into a van der Waals-like phase diagram, with a critical point at a critical cosmological constant size in Plank units and a smooth crossover thereafter. We speculate that, in the gauge-string dictionary, this corresponds to the confinement "critical point" in number of colors at finite number of flavors, a highly non-trivial parameter that can be determined through lattice simulations.

## Key claim
The paper shows that in a noncommutative-geometry-inspired Schwarzschild-anti-deSitter black hole, the Hawking-Page transition is extended into a van der Waals-like phase diagram with a critical point at a critical cosmological constant size in Planck units, and a smooth crossover thereafter; this is a qualitative extension of the standard first-order transition, not a replacement, and the crossover regime occurs only for a "fictitious charge" parameter \(q > q^\ast \approx 0.18243\).

## Method
The authors derive a noncommutative-geometry-inspired Schwarzschild-anti-deSitter (NCSchwAdS) metric by replacing the point-like mass source with a Gaussian energy-density profile of width \(\ell\) (the noncommutative minimal length) in the Einstein equations with a negative cosmological constant \(\Lambda = -3/L^2\). They then study the horizon structure, compute the Hawking temperature, heat capacity, entropy, and free energy as functions of the horizon radius, and classify the phase transitions by analyzing the temperature extrema and the sign of the heat capacity as a function of the dimensionless parameter \(q = 2\ell/L\).

## Key numbers
- The critical value of the fictitious charge is \(q^\ast \approx 0.18243\); for \(q < q^\ast\) the temperature has both a minimum and a maximum, for \(q = q^\ast\) the two extrema coalesce, and for \(q > q^\ast\) the temperature is monotonic.
- For \(q = 0.1\), the remnant (extremal) horizon radius is \(r_0 \approx 0.15 L = 3\ell\) and the remnant mass parameter is \(m_0 \approx 0.097\) (in units where \(m = MG/L\)).
- For the conventional SchwAdS case (\(q=0\)), the minimum temperature is \(T_{\mathrm{min}} = \sqrt{3}/(2\pi L) \approx 0.27 L^{-1}\) at \(r_{\mathrm{min}} = L/\sqrt{3} \approx 0.57 L\); these values are essentially unchanged for \(q < q^\ast\).
- For \(q = 1/15\), the remnant mass parameter is \(m_0 = 0.064\) and the remnant radius is \(x_0 = 0.10\) (in units of \(L\)); for \(q = 1/10\), \(m_0 = 0.097\) and \(x_0 = 0.15\); for \(q = 1/6\), \(m_0 = 0.168\) and \(x_0 = 0.24\); for \(q = 1/3\), \(m_0 = 0.393\) and \(x_0 = 0.40\); for \(q = 1\), \(m_0 = 2.689\) and \(x_0 = 0.70\).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of quantum-gravity-improved black holes from the deSitter case (Nicolini and Mann, 2011) to the anti-deSitter background, building on the noncommutative-geometry-inspired black hole program (Nicolini, 2009; Smailagic and Spallucci, 2003-2004). It modifies the classic Hawking-Page transition (Hawking and Page, 1983) by introducing a maximum temperature and a remnant, which enables a phase diagram analogous to a van der Waals fluid. The authors speculate that in the gauge-string correspondence, this crossover corresponds to a confinement "critical point" in the number of colors at finite number of flavors, a parameter that could be determined by lattice simulations.
