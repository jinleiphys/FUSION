# The Quark-Gluon Vertex and the QCD Infrared Dynamics

> The Dyson-Schwinger quark equation is solved for the quark-gluon vertex using the most recent lattice data available in the Landau gauge for the quark, gluon and ghost propagators, the full set of longitudinal tensor structures in the Ball-Chiu vertex, taking into account a recently derived normalisation for a quark-ghost kernel form factors and the gluon contribution for the tree level quark-gluon vertex identified on a recent study of the lattice soft gluon limit. A solution for the inverse problem is computed after the Tikhonov linear regularisation of the integral equation, that implies solving a modified Dyson-Schwinger equation. We get longitudinal form factors that are strongly enhanced at the infrared region, deviate significantly from the tree level results for quark and gluon momentum below 2 GeV and at higher momentum approach their perturbative values. The computed quark-gluon vertex favours kinematical configurations where the quark momentum $p$ and the gluon momentum $q$ are small and parallel. Further, the quark-gluon vertex is dominated by the form factors associated to the tree level vertex $γ_μ$ and to the operator $2 \, p_μ+ q_μ$. The higher rank tensor structures provide small contributions to the vertex.

## Key claim

The longitudinal form factors of the quark-gluon vertex are strongly enhanced in the infrared region and deviate significantly from tree-level results for quark and gluon momentum below 2 GeV, but the matching with the perturbative tail at high momentum is not perfect due to the Tikhonov regularisation and the parametrisation of the vertex.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson-Schwinger quark equation for the quark-gluon vertex using lattice data for the quark, gluon, and ghost propagators in Landau gauge, incorporating the full set of longitudinal tensor structures from the Ball-Chiu vertex. They employed Tikhonov linear regularisation to solve the inverse problem, which required solving a modified Dyson-Schwinger equation, and included a recently derived normalisation for the quark-ghost kernel form factors as well as the gluon contribution identified from lattice studies of the soft gluon limit.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal form factors deviate significantly from tree-level results for quark and gluon momentum below 2 GeV.
- The quark-gluon vertex is dominated by the form factors associated to the tree level vertex $\gamma_\mu$ and to the operator $2 p_\mu + q_\mu$.
- The enhancement occurs preferably at momenta of approximately $\Lambda_{QCD}$.
- The computed $X_0$ deviates only slightly from the normalisation condition $X_0(p, -p, 0) = 1$.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Rojas (2013) which solved the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation using a generalised Ball-Chiu vertex with a single unknown form factor depending only on gluon momentum, by including the full set of longitudinal form factors. The calculation also incorporates the normalisation of the quark-ghost kernel form factor $X_0$ derived by Aguilar (2014) and the gluon propagator contribution identified in Oliveira (2018) from lattice data analysis. The results enable improved modelling of the quark-gluon vertex for hadron phenomenology, particularly for understanding chiral symmetry breaking and confinement, and can help refine models like the Maris-Tandy model which fails to describe certain hadronic properties such as $\rho$ and $a_1$ mass splittings.
