# Neutrons and the New Standard Model

> Fundamental symmetry tests with neutrons can provide unique information about whatever will be the new Standard Model of fundamental interactions. I review two aspects of this possibility: searches for the permanent electric dipole moment of the neutron and its relation to the origin of baryonic matter, and precision studies of neutron decay that can probe new symmetries. I discuss the complementarity of these experiments with other low-energy precision tests and high energy collider searches for new physics.

## Key claim

Fundamental symmetry tests with neutrons can provide unique information about the new Standard Model, but the interpretation of results from neutron electric dipole moment (EDM) searches and precision neutron decay studies requires input from the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and other low-energy experiments to distinguish between possible new physics scenarios, such as electroweak baryogenesis or supersymmetry.

## Method

The paper reviews two thrusts of the worldwide neutron physics program: searches for the permanent electric dipole moment of the neutron and precision studies of neutron decay. For EDMs, the author relates the baryon asymmetry of the universe (BAU) and the neutron EDM to parameters of the new Standard Model, using the minimal supersymmetric Standard Model (MSSM) as an example to show how the required CP-violating phase depends on superpartner masses. For neutron decay, the author examines tests of CKM matrix unitarity using superallowed nuclear beta-decay and kaon decays, and discusses how correlation coefficients in neutron decay (such as the Fierz interference coefficient and the neutrino asymmetry parameter) can probe non-(V-A) interactions from supersymmetric box graphs.

## Key numbers

*   Baryon asymmetry of the universe: \(8.36 \times 10^{-11} < Y_B < 9.32 \times 10^{-11}\) (95% C.L.), from cosmic microwave background measurements.
*   For MSSM electroweak baryogenesis, the needed neutron EDM sensitivity is \(|d_n| \gtrsim 10^{-28}\) \(e\)-cm to accommodate sufficiently light charginos and neutralinos and a sufficiently large CP-violating phase \(\sin\phi_\mu\).
*   Neutron and electron EDM limits yield stringent limits on \(\sin\phi_\mu\) for light squarks and sleptons (\(m_{\tilde f} \lesssim 1\) TeV): \(|\sin\phi_\mu| \lesssim 0.01\) (95% C.L.).
*   CKM unitarity test results: \(|V_{ud}|^2+|V_{us}|^2+|V_{ub}|^2 = 0.9968 \pm 0.0014\) using chiral perturbation theory/large \(N_C\) for the kaon form factor, and \(0.9998 \pm 0.0015\) using lattice QCD.
*   For new physics at the electroweak scale, the combined relative experimental and theoretical uncertainty in neutron decay observables must be \(\lesssim 10^{-3}\) to discern deviations.
*   Supersymmetric box-graph contributions to the Fierz interference coefficient and the energy-dependent term in the neutrino asymmetry parameter can be of order \(10^{-3}\) when superpartner masses are of order the electroweak scale and scalar fermion mixing is nearly maximal.

## Context

This review extends the discussion of fundamental symmetry tests with neutrons found in two recent co-authored reviews (Erler and Ramsey-Musolf, 2004; Ramsey-Musolf and Su, 2006). It specifically addresses the complementarity between neutron EDM searches and LHC searches for testing electroweak baryogenesis, building on computations of quantum transport during a supersymmetric electroweak phase transition (Chung et al., 2008; Lee et al., 2004; Cirigliano et al., 2006). The paper also discusses the interplay of neutron decay studies with other low-energy precision tests, such as parity-violating electron scattering and neutrinoless double beta decay searches, and with high energy collider searches for new physics.
