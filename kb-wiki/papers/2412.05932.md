# Testing for isospin symmetry breaking with extensive calculations of isotope shift factors in potassium

> Precise evaluation of the isotope shift (IS) factors for seven low-lying potassium (K) states is achieved using relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) theory. The energies of these states are assessed and compared with experimental data to confirm the accuracy of the wave functions calculated at varying RCC theory approximations and highlight the significance of many-body and relativistic effects in determining the energies and IS factors of K. Various methods are used to compute the IS factors, with the finite-field (FF) approach yielding results that align with observed and semi-empirical data. This consistency is attributed to orbital relaxation effects that are naturally present in the FF method but emerge only through complex interactions in other techniques. Using the IS factors derived from FF, we review the mean square radius difference between $^{38m}$K and $^{39}$K. From this difference and muonic atom x-ray spectroscopy, we deduce the absolute radius of $^{38m}$K using an updated calculation of the nuclear polarizability effect. Finally, we evaluate the isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) in this isotriplet by integrating the radius of $^{38m}$K with an updated radius of $^{38}$Ca, concluding that the ISB is compatible with zero. This finding offers a stringent benchmark for nuclear model calculations of ISB corrections in nuclear beta decay, which play a key role in determining the $V_{ud}$ matrix element.

## Key claim

The isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) in the A=38 isotriplet (³⁸Ca, ³⁸ᵐK, ³⁸Ar) is compatible with zero, based on updated charge radii of ³⁸ᵐK and ³⁸Ca derived from new isotope shift calculations and nuclear polarizability corrections, providing a benchmark for nuclear model calculations of ISB corrections in superallowed beta decay.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) calculations of field-shift (FS), normal mass shift (NMS), and specific mass shift (SMS) factors for seven low-lying states of potassium (4S, 4P₁/₂;₃/₂, 5S, 5P₁/₂;₃/₂, and 6S) using three approaches: finite-field (FF), expectation-value evaluation (EVE), and analytical response (AR). They compared results at the Dirac-Hartree-Fock (DHF), second-order relativistic many-body perturbation theory (RMBPT(2)), RCC with singles and doubles (RCCSD), and RCC with singles, doubles, and triples (RCCSDT) levels, and validated wave function accuracy by computing energies and magnetic dipole hyperfine structure factors against experimental data. Using the FF-derived IS factors, they extracted the mean square radius difference between ³⁸ᵐK and ³⁹K, combined it with muonic atom x-ray spectroscopy data and an updated nuclear polarizability calculation to obtain the absolute radius of ³⁸ᵐK, and then evaluated ISB in the isotriplet using the radius of ³⁸Ca.

## Key numbers

- The FS, NMS, and SMS factors were calculated for seven low-lying states of K using RCC theory at the RCCSDT level with the FF approach
- The finite-field parameter λₒ = 10⁻⁵ a.u. was used for all states in the FF approach
- The semi-empirical relation F = -D Z A_hf / g_I uses D ≈ 6.16381 × 10⁻³ fm⁻²
- From the mean square radius difference between ³⁸ᵐK and ³⁹K and muonic atom x-ray spectroscopy, the absolute radius of ³⁸ᵐK was deduced using an updated nuclear polarizability calculation
- The ISB in the A=38 isotriplet was found to be compatible with zero

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of IS factors in potassium by Martensson and collaborators (1990s) using nonrelativistic CC with approximate relativistic corrections, Safronova and Johnson (2001) using SD method, RPA, and MBPT, and Berengut et al. using a FF approach with Brueckner effects. The results enable improved determination of the statistical rate function f and the ISB correction δ_C needed for extracting the CKM matrix element V_ud from superallowed nuclear beta decays, addressing the current ~3σ deficit in CKM unitarity reported by Cirigliano et al. (2022).
