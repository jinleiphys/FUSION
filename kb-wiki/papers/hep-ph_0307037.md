# Cronin Effect and High-p_T Suppression in pA Collisions

> We review the predictions of the theory of Color Glass Condensate for gluon production cross section in p(d)A collisions. We demonstrate that at moderate energies, when the gluon production cross section can be calculated in the framework of McLerran-Venugopalan model, it has only partonic level Cronin effect in it. At higher energies/rapidities corresponding to smaller values of Bjorken x quantum evolution becomes important. The effect of quantum evolution at higher energies/rapidities is to introduce suppression of high-p_T gluons slightly decreasing the Cronin enhancement. At still higher energies/rapidities quantum evolution leads to suppression of produced gluons at all values of p_T.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in the Color Glass Condensate framework, the Cronin enhancement of gluon production in pA collisions is only present at moderate energies within the McLerran-Venugopalan model; at higher energies/rapidities, quantum evolution introduces suppression of high-p_T gluons that gradually reduces the Cronin enhancement, and at still higher energies leads to suppression at all p_T values.

## Method

The authors calculate the gluon production cross section in proton-nucleus collisions using the McLerran-Venugopalan model for the quasi-classical approximation, then incorporate non-linear small-x quantum evolution via the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation. They analyze the ratio R^{pA} of gluon yields in pA collisions relative to pp collisions scaled by the number of binary collisions, studying its behavior across different transverse momentum regimes and energy scales.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale is given by Q_{s0}^2 = 4π α_s^2 ρ T(b), where ρ is the atomic number density and T(b) the nuclear profile function
- At high k_T in the quasi-classical approximation, R^{pA}(k_T) = 1 + (3/2)(Q_{s0}^2/k_T^2) ln(k_T^2/Λ^2) + ..., indicating Cronin enhancement approaching 1 from above
- The height of the Cronin maximum scales as R^{pA}(β Q_{s0}) ~ ln(Q_{s0}/Λ) + const ~ ln A + const'
- At very high energies when gluon production in pp is also in the extended geometric scaling region, R^{pA} saturates at R^{pA} ~ A^{-1/6}
- For the Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution in the saturation region (k_T ≪ Q_{s0}), φ^{WW}(x, k^2) ≈ (4 C_F S_A)/(α_s (2π)^2) ln(Q_{s0}/k_T)
- For the conventional gluon distribution in the saturation region (k_T ≪ Q_{s0}), φ(x, k^2) ≈ (2 C_F S_A)/(α_s (2π)^2) k_T^2/Q_{s0}^2

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (1994) and the gluon production calculations of Kovchegov and Mueller (1998) by incorporating non-linear small-x evolution from Balitsky (1996) and Kovchegov (1999). The paper provides a theoretical framework for interpreting the first d-Au data from RHIC (dAtaphen, dAtaphob, dAtastar, 2003), which showed Cronin enhancement up to k_T ≈ 6 GeV at mid-rapidity but no significant enhancement at forward rapidity. The results enable separation of initial-state saturation effects from final-state jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions, and predict that at sufficiently high energies the nuclear modification factor R^{pA} becomes a decreasing function of collision centrality, contrary to quasi-classical expectations.
