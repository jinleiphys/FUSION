# The Similarity Renormalization Group for Three-Body Interactions in One Dimension

> We report on recent progress of the implementation of the similarity renormalization group (SRG) for three-body interactions in a one-dimensional, bosonic model system using the plane wave basis. We discuss our implementation of the flow equations and show results that confirm that results in the three-body sector remain unchanged by the transformation of the Hamiltonian. We also show how the SRG transformation decouples low- from high-momentum nodes in the three-body sector and therefore simplifies the numerical calculation of observables.

## Key claim

The similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution of three-body interactions in a one-dimensional bosonic model system, implemented in a plane-wave basis, preserves three-body observables while decoupling low- from high-momentum modes, but the authors note that the induced three-body force is weak for small flow parameter \(s\) and only becomes stronger and more diagonal at larger \(s\), with the caveat that the results are for a simplified one-dimensional model rather than a full three-dimensional nuclear system.

## Method

The authors implemented the SRG flow equations for two- and three-body interactions in a one-dimensional bosonic system using a plane-wave basis with Jacobi momenta and partial-wave projection (only \(l=0\) states for bosons). They used the relative kinetic energy operator \(T_{\text{rel}}\) as the SRG generator, which drives the Hamiltonian toward diagonal form in momentum space. The flow equations were discretized and solved numerically using standard ODE solvers in Python and Matlab, with global splines used to handle off-grid momenta arising from permutation operators in the three-body sector.

## Key numbers

* Two-body binding energy for the separable potential (\(g=-1\), \(\Lambda=10\)): \(B_2 = 5.12880487\) at \(s=0\), changing to \(5.12880454\) at \(s=0.06\), confirming observable conservation to within \(3\times10^{-7}\).
* Two-body binding energy for potential \(V_\alpha\): \(B_2 = -0.920\) (independent of three-body strength \(c_E\)).
* Three-body binding energies for \(V_\alpha\) with \(c_E = -0.10\): \(B_3 = -3.226\); with \(c_E = 0.10\): \(B_3 = -2.027\).
* Three-body binding energies for \(V_\beta\) with \(c_E = -0.10\): \(B_3 = -2.570\); with \(c_E = 0.10\): \(B_3 = -0.952\).
* Two-body binding energy for \(V_\alpha\) becomes independent of momentum-space cutoff \(\Lambda_{\text{cut}}\) for \(\Lambda_{\text{cut}} \lesssim 3\) after evolution to \(s=0.1\), compared to requiring \(\Lambda_{\text{cut}} \gtrsim 7\) at \(s=0\).

## Context

This work extends the SRG approach for three-body interactions, previously implemented in a truncated harmonic oscillator basis by Jurgenson (2009, 2010) and Roth (2011), to a plane-wave basis that enables projection onto any basis and calculation of infinite matter observables. The results confirm that a consistently evolved three-body interaction can be obtained in plane waves, addressing a missing link in nuclear matter calculations by Bogner (2005) and Hebeler (2010) where phenomenological three-body forces were refitted alongside evolved two-body potentials. The plane-wave formulation opens the door to more consistent nuclear matter calculations, testing the importance of four-body forces as claimed by Roth (2011), and studying the Efimov effect in three-body systems with large scattering length as investigated by Bedaque (1998).
