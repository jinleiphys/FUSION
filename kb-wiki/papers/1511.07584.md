# Alpha Decay Width of $^{212}$Po from a quartetting wave function approach

> A microscopic calculation of $α$-cluster preformation probability and $α$ decay width in the typical $α$ emitter $^{212}$Po is presented. Results are obtained by improving a recent approach to describe $α$ preformation in $^{212}$Po [Phys. Rev. C 90, 034304 (2014)] implementing four-nucleon correlations (quartetting). Using the actually measured density distribution of the $^{208}$ Pb core, the calculated alpha decay width of $^{212}$Po agrees fairly well with the measured one.

## Key claim
Using the experimentally measured density distribution of the \(^{208}\)Pb core and a local-density approximation for the Pauli blocking, the calculated \(\alpha\) decay width of \(^{212}\)Po agrees fairly well with the measured value, but the result depends on the choice of the effective center-of-mass potential and the treatment of the chemical potential; the preformation factor is not uniquely determined from first principles.

## Method
The authors solve a quartetting wave function approach for the four-nucleon (two neutrons, two protons) system on top of a \(^{208}\)Pb core. The intrinsic part of the four-nucleon wave function is treated as an \(\alpha\)-like bound state where the baryon density is below the Mott density \(n_B^{\rm Mott}=0.02917\) fm\(^{-3}\), and as an uncorrelated four-nucleon state at higher densities. The center-of-mass motion is described by an effective potential that includes the intrinsic binding energy, Pauli blocking (from a power expansion in baryon density), and an external part from the M3Y double-folding potential with Coulomb interaction. The \(\alpha\) decay width is computed using the two-potential approach of Gurvitz (1988), separating the effective potential at \(r_{\rm sep}=15\) fm.

## Key numbers
- The Mott density for the \(\alpha\) cluster is \(n_B^{\rm Mott}=0.02917\) fm\(^{-3}\), occurring at a cluster radius \(r_{\rm cluster}=7.4383\) fm for the empirical \(^{208}\)Pb density.
- The experimental bound state energy (tunneling energy) is \(E_{\rm tunnel}=Q_\alpha-28.3=8.954-28.3=-19.346\) MeV.
- For potential B (fitted to the experimental half-life), the preformation factor is \(P_\alpha=0.142\) and the decay half-life is \(T_{1/2}=2.99\times 10^{-7}\) s.
- For potential A (Thomas-Fermi condition \(E_{\rm tunnel}=\mu_4\)), the preformation factor is \(P_\alpha=0.367\) and the half-life is \(T_{1/2}=2.91\times 10^{-8}\) s.
- For potential C (standard M3Y parametrization), the preformation factor is \(P_\alpha=0.268\) and the half-life is \(T_{1/2}=1.11\times 10^{-7}\) s.
- The empirical neutron density of \(^{208}\)Pb is \(n_n(r)=0.093776/\{1+\exp[(r-6.7\ {\rm fm})/0.55\ {\rm fm}]\}\) fm\(^{-3}\) and the proton density is \(n_p(r)=0.062895/\{1+\exp[(r-6.68\ {\rm fm})/0.447\ {\rm fm}]\}\) fm\(^{-3}\) (Tarbert 2014).

## Context
This work extends the quartetting wave function approach of Xu et al. (Phys. Rev. C 90, 034304, 2014) by replacing simple density parametrizations with the measured density distribution of \(^{208}\)Pb from Tarbert (2014) and using the M3Y double-folding potential instead of a Woods-Saxon potential. The approach is inspired by the THSR wave function concept (Tohsaki, Horiuchi, Schuck, Röpke) previously applied to light nuclei, and the results are compared with earlier microscopic calculations by Varga and collaborators. The method enables a consistent microscopic calculation of \(\alpha\) preformation and decay width in heavy nuclei, with the open question of improving the treatment of gradient terms and non-local potentials for finite nuclei.
