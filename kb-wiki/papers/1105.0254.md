# Warm and dense stellar matter under strong magnetic fields

> We investigate the effects of strong magnetic fields on the equation of state of warm stellar matter as it may occur in a protoneutron star. Both neutrino free and neutrino trapped matter at a fixed entropy per baryon are analyzed. A relativistic mean field nuclear model, including the possibility of hyperon formation, is considered. A density dependent magnetic field with the magnitude $10^{15}$ G at the surface and not more than $3\times 10^{18}$ G at the center is considered. The magnetic field gives rise to a neutrino suppression, mainly at low densities, in matter with trapped neutrinos. It is shown that an hybrid protoneutron star will not evolve to a low mass blackhole if the magnetic field is strong enough and the magnetic field does not decay. However, the decay of the magnetic field after cooling may give rise to the formation of a low mass blackhole.

## Key claim

Strong magnetic fields (up to $3\times 10^{18}$ G at the center) can prevent a hybrid protoneutron star from collapsing into a low-mass black hole, but only if the field does not decay; if the magnetic field decays after cooling, a low-mass black hole may still form.

## Method

The authors solve a relativistic mean-field model (non-linear Walecka model with the GM1 parameter set) for warm stellar matter in beta equilibrium, including the full baryon octet and leptons, at fixed entropy per baryon. They incorporate Landau quantization for charged particles and the anomalous magnetic moments (AMM) of baryons, considering both neutrino-free matter and matter with trapped neutrinos (lepton fraction $Y_{Le}=0.4$), under a density-dependent magnetic field ranging from $10^{15}$ G at the surface to $3\times 10^{18}$ G at the center.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field range: $10^{15}$ G at the surface to $3\times 10^{18}$ G at the center
- Electron critical field: $B_e^c = 4.414 \times 10^{13}$ G (used to define $B = B^* B_e^c$)
- Nuclear saturation density: $\rho_0 = 0.153$ fm$^{-3}$
- Entropy per baryon values considered: $\mathfrak{s} = 0, 1, 2$ (in units of the Boltzmann constant)
- Lepton fraction for trapped neutrino matter: $Y_{Le} = 0.4$
- Magnetic field strengths plotted: $B^* = 0, 10^5, 2\times 10^5$ (corresponding to $B = 0, 4.414\times 10^{18}$ G, $8.828\times 10^{18}$ G)
- Nucleon mass: $m_n = 939$ MeV
- Effective mass ratio at saturation: $M^*/M = 0.70$
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: $-B/A = 16.30$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature studies of Broderick (2000) and Aziz (2009) on strong magnetic field effects in dense matter to finite temperature, as relevant for protoneutron stars. It also builds on the finite-temperature work of Prakash (1997) on warm stellar matter without magnetic fields. The results enable more realistic modeling of protoneutron star evolution and black hole formation, showing that magnetic field decay is a crucial factor in determining whether a low-mass black hole forms after cooling.
