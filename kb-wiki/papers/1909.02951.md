# Collinear Factorization in Wide-Angle Hadron Pair Production in $e^+e^-$ Annihilation

> We compute the inclusive unpolarized dihadron production cross section in the far from back-to-back region of $e^+ e^-$ annihilation in leading order pQCD using existing fragmentation function fits and standard collinear factorization, focusing on the large transverse momentum region where transverse momentum is comparable to the hard scale (the center-of-mass energy). We compare with standard transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) fragmentation function-based predictions intended for the small transverse momentum region with the aim of testing the expectation that the two types of calculation roughly coincide at intermediate transverse momentum. We find significant tension, within the intermediate transverse momentum region, between calculations done with existing non-perturbative TMD fragmentation functions and collinear factorization calculations if the center-of-mass energy is not extremely large. We argue that $e^+ e^-$ measurements are ideal for resolving this tension and exploring the large-to-small transverse momentum transition, given the typically larger hard scales ($\gtrsim 10$ GeV) of the process as compared with similar scenarios that arise in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and fixed-target Drell-Yan measurements.

## Key claim

The paper finds significant tension between collinear factorization calculations and TMD-based predictions for dihadron production in $e^+e^-$ annihilation at intermediate transverse momentum when the center-of-mass energy is not extremely large (below about 50 GeV), contradicting the expectation that the two approaches should roughly coincide in that region.

## Method

The authors computed the leading-order (order $\alpha_s$) collinear factorization cross section for inclusive unpolarized dihadron production in $e^+e^-$ annihilation in the far-from-back-to-back region, using existing collinear fragmentation function fits from de Florian et al. (2014). They compared these results with predictions from TMD factorization using non-perturbative TMD fragmentation function parametrizations from Bacchetta et al. (2017), examining the ratio of the two calculations as a function of transverse momentum at various center-of-mass energies. The collinear calculation included six partonic channels at leading order, with sensitivity to gluon fragmentation already at the lowest non-vanishing order.

## Key numbers

* At center-of-mass energy $Q \sim 50$ GeV, the TMD-based and collinear calculations approach each other at intermediate transverse momentum, with the transition point between regimes occurring at $q_T/q_T^{\rm Max} \approx 0.3$ to $0.2$.
* At moderate $Q$ around 12 GeV, the disagreement between the two calculations at intermediate transverse momentum rises to a factor of several, with the fixed-order collinear calculation undershooting the TMD-based calculation.
* The hard scales considered for $e^+e^-$ annihilation are typically $\gtrsim 10$ GeV, larger than comparable scales in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and fixed-target Drell-Yan measurements where similar tension has been observed at $Q \lesssim 14$ GeV.
* The kinematical maximum of transverse momentum is given by ${q_{\rm T}^{\rm Max}}^2 \leq Q^2 (1 - z_A)(1-z_B) / [1 - (1-z_A)(1 - z_B)]$.

## Context

This work extends the study of factorization boundaries in QCD, following prior observations of tension between collinear factorization and data in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (Daleo et al., 2004; Kniehl et al., 2004; Boglione et al., 2014; Gonzalez-Hernandez et al., 2018) and Drell-Yan (Bacchetta et al., 2019). The paper argues that $e^+e^-$ annihilation measurements are ideal for resolving this tension and exploring the transition between large and small transverse momentum regimes, given the typically larger hard scales available compared to SIDIS and fixed-target Drell-Yan. The results motivate experimental studies of dihadron pair production that probe the intermediate transverse momentum transition region.
