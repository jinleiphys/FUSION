# Ground state properties and potential energy surfaces of $^{270}$Hs from multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean field model

> We study the ground state properties, potential energy curves and potential energy surfaces of the superheavy nucleus $^{270}$Hs by using the multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean-field model with the effective interaction PC-PK1. The binding energy, size and shape as well as single particle shell structure corresponding to the ground state of this nucleus are obtained. $^{270}$Hs is well deformed and exhibits deformed doubly magic feature in the single neutron and proton level schemes. One-dimensional potential energy curves and two-dimensional potential energy surfaces are calculated for $^{270}$Hs with various spatial symmetries imposed. We investigate in detail the effects of the reflection asymmetric and triaxial distortions on the fission barrier and fission path of $^{270}$Hs. When the axial symmetry is imposed, the reflection symmetric and reflection asymmetric fission barriers both show a double-hump structure and the former is higher. However, when triaxial shapes are allowed the reflection symmetric barrier is lowered very much and then the reflection symmetric fission path becomes favorable.

## Key claim

The superheavy nucleus \(^{270}\)Hs is well deformed and exhibits deformed doubly magic features, but the fission barrier heights and paths depend critically on whether triaxial and reflection-asymmetric shape degrees of freedom are allowed; when triaxial shapes are permitted, the reflection-symmetric fission barrier is lowered substantially, making the reflection-symmetric path energetically favorable over the reflection-asymmetric one.

## Method

The authors used the multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean-field (MDC-RMF) model with the PC-PK1 effective interaction, solving the Dirac equation in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis with a truncation of \(N_f = 20\) (or \(N_f = 16\) for the most demanding two-dimensional surfaces). Pairing correlations were treated via the BCS approach with a separable pairing force of finite range (strength \(G/G_0 = 1.1\)). One-dimensional potential energy curves (PECs) were obtained by constraining the axial quadrupole deformation \(\beta_{20}\) from 0 to 1.5, and two-dimensional potential energy surfaces (PESs) were calculated with various combinations of axial symmetry, reflection symmetry, and triaxiality imposed.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of \(^{270}\)Hs: \(E_\mathrm{B} = 1967.40\) MeV (MDC-RMF with PC-PK1), compared to the AME2016 empirical value of 1969.65 MeV.
- Ground state quadrupole deformation: \(\beta_{20} = 0.261\), \(\beta_{22} = 0\); hexadecupole deformation \(\beta_{40} = -0.056\).
- Root-mean-square charge radius: \(R_\mathrm{c} = 6.167\) fm.
- Single-particle shell gaps: 1.3 MeV for protons at \(Z=108\) and approximately 2 MeV for neutrons at \(N=162\).
- First fission barrier height (AS-RS): 5.4 MeV at \(\beta_{20} \approx 0.46\); second fission barrier height (AS-RS): 7.9 MeV at \(\beta_{20} \approx 0.66\).
- Lowering of the second fission barrier: by 2.1 MeV when reflection asymmetry is allowed (AS-RA), and by 2.4 MeV when triaxiality is allowed (TA-RS), relative to the AS-RS barrier.
- Basis truncation accuracy: \(N_f = 20\) gives 0.1 MeV accuracy in total binding energy; \(N_f = 16\) gives 0.5 MeV accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the MDC-CDFT framework, previously applied to actinide nuclei such as \(^{240}\)Pu (Lu 2012, Lu 2014), to the superheavy region for the first time. It provides the first potential energy surface for \(^{270}\)Hs that simultaneously considers both triaxial and reflection-asymmetric deformations. The results enable more realistic modeling of fission dynamics in superheavy nuclei and can be compared with macroscopic-microscopic models such as the finite range droplet model (Moeller 2016) and the Woods–Saxon–Strutinsky calculations (Chai 2018).
