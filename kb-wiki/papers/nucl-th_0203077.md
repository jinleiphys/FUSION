# Compton Scattering on the Proton

> A microscopic coupled-channels model for Compton and pion scattering off the nucleon is introduced which is applicable at the lowest energies (polarizabilities) as well as at GeV energies. To introduce the model first the conventional K-matrix approach is discussed to extend this in a following chapter to the "Dressed K-Matrix" model. The latter approach restores causality, or analyticity, of the amplitude to a large extent. In particular, crossing symmetry, gauge invariance and unitarity are satisfied. The extent of violation of analyticity (causality) is used as an expansion parameter.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a "Dressed K-Matrix" model for Compton and pion scattering on the nucleon that approximately restores analyticity (causality) while preserving crossing symmetry, gauge invariance, and unitarity; the extent of analyticity violation is used as an expansion parameter, meaning the model is not fully causal but improves upon the conventional K-matrix approach.

## Method

The authors first present the conventional K-matrix approach, which sums an infinite series of pole contributions from loop diagrams (on-shell intermediate particles) to enforce unitarity. They then extend this to the "Dressed K-Matrix" model, where dressed vertices and propagators are calculated self-consistently by iterating between imaginary parts (obtained via cutting rules) and real parts (obtained via dispersion relations). The dressing is applied to the \(\pi N N\) and \(\gamma N N\) vertices and nucleon propagator, with contact terms added via minimal substitution to maintain gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- Electric polarizability of the proton \(\alpha = 12.1 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3\) (dressed K-matrix, D) compared to \(15.5 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3\) (bare K-matrix, B).
- Magnetic polarizability of the proton \(\beta = 2.4 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3\) (dressed) compared to \(1.7 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3\) (bare).
- Spin polarizability \(\gamma_{E1} = -5.0 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^4\) (dressed) compared to \(-1.7 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^4\) (bare).
- Spin polarizability \(\gamma_{M1} = 3.4 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^4\) (dressed) compared to \(3.8 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^4\) (bare).
- Spin polarizability \(\gamma_0 = 2.4 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^4\) (dressed) compared to \(-0.9 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^4\) (bare).
- Spin polarizability \(\gamma_\pi = 11.4 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^4\) (dressed) compared to \(8.9 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^4\) (bare).

## Context

This work extends the conventional K-matrix approach used in earlier studies (e.g., Korchin 1998) by incorporating analyticity constraints through dressed vertices and propagators, addressing a known failure of such models in predicting nucleon polarizabilities. The model enables more reliable extraction of polarizabilities from low-energy Compton scattering data and provides a framework for testing sum rules; preliminary results indicate that most sum rules are obeyed except for the spin polarizability \(\gamma_0\), possibly due to incomplete dressing of the \(\Delta\) resonance.
