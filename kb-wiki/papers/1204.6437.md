# Non-Local Interactions For the Deuteron Using Spherical Bessel Functions

> Non-local interactions are assumed for the deuteron with form factors $g_C(k)= j_0(b_1k)\,j_0(b_2k)$ for the central part responsible for the S-state and $g_T(k)= j_1(b_1k)\,j_1(b_2k)$ for the tensor part responsible for the D-state, where $b_1$ and $b_2$ are range parameters for the proton and neutron, respectively. The analytically obtained wavefunctions in coordinate space have different forms in three different regions. The inner most region is between $r=0$ and $r=b_2-b_1$ (assuming $b_2>b_1$), followed by a region between $r=b_2-b_1$ and $b_1+b_2$ and finally the region $r>b_1+b_2$. The resulting wavefunctions and their derivatives are found to be continuous at the boundaries. The ensuing calculations are simplified by setting $b_1=b_2=b$. Good agreement is obtained for the quadrupole moment. Neutron-proton scattering calculations will follow in the next communication.

## Key claim

Non-local interactions for the deuteron using spherical Bessel function form factors yield analytically solvable wavefunctions that exhibit different functional forms in three distinct radial regions, with the simplification of equal range parameters (b1 = b2 = b) producing good agreement with the deuteron quadrupole moment to two significant figures, though the abstract's claim of "good agreement" is qualified by the text noting that more accuracy is expected when b1 is set different from b2.

## Method

The author assumed separable non-local potentials for the deuteron with central form factor g_C(k) = j0(b1 k) j0(b2 k) for the S-state and tensor form factor g_T(k) = j1(b1 k) j1(b2 k) for the D-state, where j_l are spherical Bessel functions and b1, b2 are range parameters for proton and neutron. The momentum-space wavefunctions were transformed analytically to coordinate space, yielding piecewise expressions in three radial regions defined by the range parameters. Calculations were simplified by setting b1 = b2 = b, and the resulting wavefunctions were used to compute the S- and D-state probabilities, root mean square radius, and quadrupole moment via numerical integration.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron binding energy parameter alpha = 0.23165 fm^-1, with range parameter b = 1.475 fm giving alpha b = 0.342
* S-wave probability approximately 96% and D-wave probability approximately 4%
* Normalization constants: A = 0.905 fm^-1/2 (S-state), B = 1.57 fm^-1/2 (D-state) to 3 significant figures
* Asymptotic normalizations: A_S = 0.941 fm^-1/2, A_D = 0.0208 fm^-1/2, giving D/S ratio eta = 0.022
* Root mean square radius r_rms = 2.08 fm
* Quadrupole moment Q = 0.286 fm^2

## Context

This work extends the non-local potential approach of Yamaguchi (1954) and Yamaguchi and Yamaguchi (1954) by using spherical Bessel functions as form factors rather than the original Yamaguchi form factors, which yields analytically tractable wavefunctions with piecewise structure in coordinate space. The results are compared with the Nijmegen I (NIJM I) potential model calculations of Stoks et al. (1994). The paper enables future neutron-proton scattering calculations and 3S1 phase shift analysis, which the author defers to a subsequent communication.
