# Dynamics of quantized vortices under quasi-periodic boundary conditions

> The Gross-Pitaevskii equation is widely used for vortex dynamics, but finite domains with hard walls or confining potentials distort bulk behavior through vortex-image effects or induced flows. Periodic boundaries reduce wall artifacts yet cannot realize finite net vorticity because of topological obstruction, so bulk simulations with non-zero circulation are typically unavailable. Hence, we impose quasi-periodic boundary conditions that keep the superfluid's density periodic while enforcing phase windings consistent with a net prescribed total vorticity. This setting conserves the net number of vortices and enables long-time tracking of vortex trajectories in settings that finite containers cannot capture. This allows us to study vortex depinning and nucleation leading to the creation of Kármán vortex streets and perfectly periodic vortex arrays. The framework also provides a toy model for studying vortex dynamics in the bulk of neutron stars, free of possible limitations induced by confining potentials.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quasi-periodic boundary conditions (QPBC) allow Gross-Pitaevskii equation simulations of superfluid vortex dynamics with non-zero net vorticity, free from the topological obstruction that prevents standard periodic boundary conditions from sustaining finite circulation, though the framework is presented as a toy model for neutron star interiors rather than a direct astrophysical simulation.

## Method

The authors solve the two-dimensional Gross-Pitaevskii equation with a phenomenological dissipation term on a rectangular Cartesian grid, imposing quasi-periodic boundary conditions that keep the superfluid density periodic while allowing phase windings consistent with a prescribed net vorticity. The wave function is decomposed into real and imaginary parts and evolved using a third-order Adams-Bashforth method with five-point stencils for spatial derivatives, with initial conditions constructed from Jacobi theta functions to imprint vortices at specified positions. The code, named GINTONIC, is validated by reproducing vortex lattice formation and studying vortex depinning from moving Gaussian potentials.

## Key numbers

- Healing length scale: \(\xi = \hbar/\sqrt{m \mu}\), with natural units \(\hbar = m = 1\) and \(\mu\) setting the energy scale
- Time resolution: \(\delta t = 5 \times 10^{-4}\,\tau\) where \(\tau = \hbar/\mu\) is the fundamental timescale
- Dissipation parameter: \(\gamma = 0.03\) for lattice formation simulations, \(\gamma = 0.01\) for Kármán street simulations
- Cell sizes: \(60\sqrt{3}\,\xi \times 60\,\xi\) for rectangular lattice formation, \(80\,\xi \times 80\,\xi\) for square cell, \(180\,\xi \times 60\,\xi\) for Kármán street simulations
- Spatial resolution: \(\delta l \simeq 0.3\,\xi\) for lattice formation, \(\delta l = 0.33\,\xi\) for Kármán street simulations
- Background potential width: \(\sigma / \xi = 0.5, 1, 2, 5\) tested for depinning simulations
- Background velocity ramp: \(v_0 = c_1\) (first sound velocity), with ramp time \(T/\tau = 1 \times 10^4\)
- Depinning occurs at \(t \simeq 5358\,\tau\) for \(\sigma = 2\,\xi\) case
- Chemical potential: \(\mu = 0.5\) for lattice formation simulations, \(\mu = 1\) otherwise

## Context

This work extends the quasi-periodic boundary condition approach introduced by Mingarelli (2016) and Wood (2019) for static vortex lattices to dynamical Gross-Pitaevskii simulations, building on point-vortex studies by Antonelli and Haskell (2020) that demonstrated transitions from pinned to unpinned vortex regimes. The framework enables revisiting phenomena such as Kármán vortex streets (previously studied by Sasaki with periodic boundary conditions and zero net vorticity) in systems with finite net vorticity and no confining potentials, and provides a toy model for vortex dynamics in neutron star interiors free from wall-induced artifacts that affect simulations using hard-wall domains (as in Bulgac 2013 or Warszawski 2011).
