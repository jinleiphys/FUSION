# Relativistic Hartree-Fock model for axial-symmetric nuclei with quadruple and octupole deformations

> \textbf{Background:} The initial observation of a negative-parity state in proximity to the ground state in the 1950s marked the advent of extensive research into octupole deformed nuclei. Since then, the physics of octupole deformed nuclei has consistently held a special interest within the field of nuclear physics. In the present era, with the advent of sophisticated radioactive ion beam (RIB) facilities and advanced detectors, coupled with the remarkable capabilities of high-performance computing, extensive and intensive explorations are being conducted from both experimental and theoretical perspectives to elucidate the physics of octupole deformed nuclei. \textbf{Results:} This work establishes the OD-RHF model, which provides a reliable tool for studying octupole nuclei over a fairly wide range. The reliability of the newly developed OD-RHF model is illustrated by taking the octupole nucleus $^{144}$Ba as an example. Furthermore, the octupole deformation effects in $^{144}$Ba is verified by using the RHF Lagrangians PKO$i$ ($i=1,2,3$) and the RMF one DD-ME2. The intrusion of the neutron $1i_{13/2}$ and proton $1h_{11/2}$ components is demonstrated to play an essential role in determining the notable octupole deformation of $^{144}$Ba using PKO$i$ ($i=1,2,3$) and DD-ME2. It is indicated that the Fock terms play an important role in stabilizing the octupole deformation. More specifically, due to the repulsive tensor coupling between the intrude components and the core of $^{144}$Ba, the tensor force component carried by the $π$-PV coupling, that contributes only via the Fock terms, plays an opposing role in the formation of the octupole deformation of $^{144}$Ba.

## Key claim
The OD-RHF model provides a reliable tool for studying octupole deformed nuclei over a fairly wide range, with the Fock terms playing an important role in stabilizing the octupole deformation; however, the tensor force component carried by the π-PV coupling, which contributes only via the Fock terms, plays an opposing role in the formation of the octupole deformation of ¹⁴⁴Ba due to repulsive tensor coupling between the intruder components and the core.

## Method
The authors developed an axially octupole-quadrupole deformed relativistic Hartree-Fock (OD-RHF) model by expanding Dirac spinors on a spherical Dirac Woods-Saxon (DWS) basis. The Lagrangian includes isoscalar (σ and ω) and isovector (ρ and π) meson fields, and the integro-differential Dirac equation is solved after incorporating both quadrupole and octupole deformations. For open-shell nuclei, pairing correlations are treated within the BCS scheme using the finite-range Gogny force D1S as the pairing force.

## Key numbers
- The spherical DWS basis was solved with a box size of 20 fm and a radial mesh step of 0.1 fm.
- For expanding the density-dependent coupling strengths, it is sufficient to set λ_p = 0, 1, 2, ..., 8 for the majority of nuclei.
- The maximum value of |κ| in the basis expansion is given by κ_max = m_max + K_m - 1/2, where m_max is the maximum projection of total angular momentum and K_m is the number of κ-blocks.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic Hartree-Fock theory for deformed nuclei, building upon the D-RHF and D-RHFB models developed by Geng (2020, 2022) which considered only quadrupole deformation. The new OD-RHF model incorporates both quadrupole and octupole deformations, enabling study of pear-shaped nuclei such as ¹⁴⁴Ba, which was confirmed experimentally to have octupole deformation by Bucher (2016). The model uses the RHF Lagrangians PKOi (i=1,2,3) from Long (2006, 2008) and the RMF one DD-ME2 from Lalazissis (2005), and enables future systematic theoretical investigations of octupole deformed nuclei across a wide range of the nuclear chart.
