# Direct Photons from a Hybrid Approach -- Exploring the parameter space

> Direct photon spectra are calculated within a transport+hydrodynamics hybrid approach, in which the high-density part of the transport evolution has been replaced by a 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamic calculation. We study the effects of changing the parameters of the two interfaces between the transport- and hydrodynamic descriptions on the resulting direct photon spectra.

## Key claim

The direct photon yield from the UrQMD transport+hydrodynamics hybrid model depends systematically on the fraction of the evolution described by hydrodynamics: a larger hydrodynamic phase increases the photon yield, but only when the initial transition to hydrodynamics is not set too early (small t_start) does the yield decrease.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD v3.3 transport model with its high-density stage replaced by a 3+1-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic calculation, employing three equations of state (hadron gas EoS, chiral EoS with crossover transition, and MIT-bag model EoS with first-order transition). They varied the transition time from transport to hydrodynamics (t_start from 0.25 t_0 to 4 t_0), the critical energy density for the transition back to transport (epsilon_crit from 2.5 epsilon_0 to 10 epsilon_0), and the transition scenario (gradual versus isochronous), for central Pb+Pb collisions at E_lab = 8, 45, and 158 AGeV and sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear ground state energy density: epsilon_0 = 146 MeV/fm^3
- Default critical energy density for hydro-to-transport transition: epsilon = 5 epsilon_0
- Minimal start time for hydrodynamic description: t_start = 0.6 fm, which equals 0.41 t_0 for Pb+Pb at E_lab = 158 AGeV and exceeds 4 t_0 at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Maximum direct photon yield observed for t_start approximately 0.75 t_0 across all systems studied
- Critical energy density varied between epsilon_crit = 2.5 epsilon_0 and epsilon_crit = 10 epsilon_0
- Calculations performed for impact parameter b < 5 fm

## Context

This work extends the hybrid model approach of Petersen et al. (2008) by systematically exploring the sensitivity of direct photon spectra to the interface parameters between transport and hydrodynamic descriptions, building on the direct photon emission model of Baeuchle and Bleicher (2010). The results enable future comparisons with experimental direct photon measurements from the SPS and RHIC, and help constrain the appropriate choice of interface parameters for hybrid model calculations of heavy-ion collisions.
