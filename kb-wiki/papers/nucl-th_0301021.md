# On rescattering effects in the reaction $π^- d \to π^- d$

> We discuss rescattering corrections to the impulse approximation for the processes $π^- d\toπ^- d$ and $γd\toπ^0 d$. It is shown that the rescattering effects (RE) give non-negligible contribution to the real part of these amplitudes. At the same time the contributions from the imaginary parts of impulse and rescattering corrections drastically cancel each other. This cancellation means that the processes $π^- d\to π^0 nn$ and $γd\to π^+ nn / π^- pp$ are strongly suppressed near threshold as required by the Pauli principle.

## Key claim

Rescattering effects give a non-negligible contribution to the real part of the pion-deuteron elastic scattering amplitude, but the imaginary parts of the impulse approximation and rescattering corrections drastically cancel each other; this cancellation means that processes such as π⁻ d → π⁰ nn are strongly suppressed near threshold as required by the Pauli principle, but the real parts do not cancel.

## Method

The authors used a separable S-wave pion-nucleon potential V_πN with a cutoff parameter c (varied between 2.5 μ and 3.5 μ) to solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation perturbatively up to second order. They calculated the pion-deuteron scattering amplitude by evaluating three contributions: the single-scattering Born term, the one-loop correction to single scattering (loop-corrected impulse approximation, LCIA), and the double-scattering (rescattering) diagram, including the charge-exchange process π⁻ p → π⁰ n → π⁻ p with an excess energy Δm = 3.3 MeV. The integrals were evaluated numerically and analytically in the limit of large c and small nucleon-to-pion mass ratio.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar and isovector pion-nucleon scattering lengths used are b₀ = −2.2 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹ and b₁ = 90.5 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹ (purely hadronic values from Ref. [7]).
- The excess energy for the charge-exchange process π⁻ p → π⁰ n in the intermediate state is Δm = 3.3 MeV.
- The cutoff parameter c characterizing the range of πN forces is varied in the range 2.5 μ ≤ c ≤ 3.5 μ (where μ is the pion mass).
- In the limit of large c (c ≫ μ) and μ/m ≪ 1, the real part of the double-scattering amplitude is Re f⁽²⁾_πd = 2 (b₀² − 2b₁²) ⟨1/r⟩_d, where ⟨1/r⟩_d is the expectation value of the inverse deuteron radius.
- In the same limit, the imaginary part of the total πd amplitude cancels to Im f_πd ≈ 4 k₀(Δm=0) b₀², where k₀ is the on-shell momentum of the intermediate πNN system.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of rescattering effects in coherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron (γ d → π⁰ d) to the analogous case of elastic π⁻ d scattering, directly addressing a claim by Ref. [1] that rescattering effects are totally compensated by loop corrections to the impulse approximation. The authors show that while the imaginary parts cancel (consistent with the Pauli principle argument of Ref. [1]), the real parts do not cancel, in agreement with earlier calculations by Refs. [2,3,4,Beane] which found rescattering effects important. The results enable a consistent treatment of off-shell effects and the charge-exchange channel in low-energy pion-deuteron scattering, and the authors note that the same conclusion applies to the γ d → π⁰ d reaction.
