# Charged $ρ$-meson condensation in neutron stars

> We extend relativistic mean-field models with hadron masses and meson-baryon coupling constants dependent on the scalar field $σ$, including hyperons and $Δ(1232)$ baryons, to incorporate a possibility of the charged $ρ$ meson condensation in neutron star matter. The influence of the $ρ^-$ condensation on the equation of state proves to be strongly model dependent. In our models of one type (KVORcut-based ones) the $ρ^-$ condensation arises by a second-order phase transition above a critical density and the maximum value of the neutron star mass diminishes only slightly. The matter composition changes more significantly. In our models of other type (MKVOR*-based ones), if the system is considered at fixed density, the $ρ^-$ condensation arises by a second-order phase transition at the baryon density $n=n_{c,ρ}^{(\rm II)}$ and at a slightly higher density $n=n_{c,ρ}^{(\rm I)}$ there occurs a first-order phase transition. In a neutron star matter starting with a density $n<n_{c,ρ}^{(\rm II)}$ there appears a region of a mixed phase, or the system is described by Maxwell construction, that results in a substantial decrease of the value of the maximum neutron star mass. Nevertheless in the models under consideration the observational constraint on the maximum neutron star mass is fulfilled. Besides, in MKVOR*-based models the appearance of the $ρ^-$ condensate is accompanied by a strong rearrangement of the matter composition. Dependence of the results on a choice of the $ρ$ meson scaling functions for the effective $ρ$ meson mass and coupling constants is also investigated.

# Charged ρ-meson condensation in neutron stars

## Key claim

Charged ρ⁻ condensation can occur in neutron star matter within relativistic mean-field models, but its impact on the equation of state and maximum neutron star mass is strongly model-dependent: in KVORcut-based models the condensation is a second-order phase transition with only a slight reduction of the maximum mass, whereas in MKVOR*-based models it triggers a first-order phase transition that substantially decreases the maximum mass, yet the observational constraint on the maximum neutron star mass (≥1.97 M⊙) is still fulfilled in all models considered.

## Method

The authors extended relativistic mean-field models with σ-field-dependent hadron masses and meson-baryon coupling constants, previously developed to include hyperons and Δ(1232) baryons, to incorporate charged ρ⁻ condensation via a non-Abelian gauge field ansatz for the ρ meson. They solved the coupled equations for baryon densities, lepton densities, the scalar field f, and the charged ρ condensate amplitude under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions, using two families of models (KVORcut03-based and MKVOR*-based) with different scaling functions η_ρ(f) for the ρ meson sector, and investigated the dependence on the choice of the ρ meson scaling tail at high scalar field values.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for ρ⁻ condensation in KVORcut03ρ model (no hyperons, no Δs): n_c,ρ ≈ 4.6 n₀ (where n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is saturation density)
- Maximum neutron star mass in KVORcut03 model without ρ⁻ condensate: 2.17 M⊙; with ρ⁻ condensate (KVORcut03ρ): 2.16 M⊙
- Critical density for second-order ρ⁻ condensation in MKVOR*HΔϕρ model: n_c,ρ^(II) = 2.74 n₀
- Densities at which the first-order phase transition to the new branch occurs in MKVOR*HΔϕρ model: n_c,ρ^(I) ≈ 2.76 n₀ (tail 1), 2.81 n₀ (tail 2), 2.82 n₀ (tail 3), 2.83 n₀ (tail 4)
- Hyperon potentials at saturation density used: U_Λ = −28 MeV, U_Σ = 30 MeV, U_Ξ = −15 MeV; Δ potential: U_Δ ≈ −50 MeV
- Observed neutron star masses used as constraints: PSR J1614-2230 at 1.928 ± 0.017 M⊙ (corrected from earlier 1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙) and PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of relativistic mean-field models with σ-scaled hadron masses and couplings, specifically the KVOR model (Kolomeitsev 2004), its KVORcut modification (Maslov 2015), and the MKVOR* model (Maslov 2015, MKVOR-Delta 2017), which were designed to satisfy constraints from nuclear matter properties, heavy-ion collision flow data, and the maximum neutron star mass constraint while including hyperons and Δ baryons. The charged ρ⁻ condensation possibility, originally suggested by Voskresensky (1997) and studied in earlier RMF frameworks (Kolomeitsev 2004), is now incorporated into these more complete models that already include hyperons and Δ resonances. The results enable assessment of whether the additional softening from ρ⁻ condensation violates the observational maximum mass constraint, and show that the outcome depends sensitively on the high-density behavior of the ρ meson scaling function, motivating further constraints on the ρ meson in-medium properties from nuclear theory and astrophysical observations.
