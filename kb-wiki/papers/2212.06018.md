# Interplay of longitudinal and transverse expansion in the kinetic dynamics of heavy-ion collisions

> The early dynamics in heavy-ion collisions involves a rapid, far from equilibrium evolution. This early pre-equilibrium stage of the dynamics can be modeled using kinetic equations. The effect of this pre-equilibrium stage on final observables derived from transverse momenta of emitted particles is small. The kinetic equations in the relaxation time approximation for a non-boost invariant system are solved. The asymmetry of the flow with respect to the reaction plane at different rapidities is found to be very sensitive to the degree of non-equilibrium in the evolution. This suggests that the rapidity odd directed flow could be studied to identify the occurrence of non-equilibrium effects and to estimate the asymmetry of the pressure between the longitudinal and transverse directions in the collision.

## Key claim

The rapidity-odd directed flow (the asymmetry of transverse flow with respect to the reaction plane at different rapidities) is very sensitive to the degree of non-equilibrium in the early evolution, even though standard transverse-plane flow observables show only a few percent difference between free-streaming, kinetic, and hydrodynamic treatments.

## Method

The author solves the kinetic equation in the relaxation time (isotropization time) approximation for massless particles in a 1+1+1 dimensional geometry (one longitudinal and one transverse direction, homogeneous in the second transverse direction), using Milne coordinates. The initial conditions correspond to a tilted fireball (Au-Au collisions at impact parameter 9.6 fm) with an initial pressure asymmetry \(P_L/P_T \simeq 0.2\), and the evolution is followed from \(\tau_0 = 0.2\) fm/c to 1.2 fm/c for three different relaxation times (free-streaming, \(\eta/s = 0.08\), and \(\eta/s = 0.16\)).

## Key numbers

* Initial pressure asymmetry: \(P_L/P_T \simeq 0.2\) (from the parametric distribution \(g(v_z)\) with \(w_z = 0.3\)).
* Initial time: \(\tau_0 = 0.2\) fm/c; final evolution time: \(\tau = 1.2\) fm/c.
* Relaxation time parameters: \(\tau_C = 0.2\) fm/c and \(\tau_C = 0.4\) fm/c, corresponding (for an initial central temperature of 400 MeV) to \(\eta/s = 0.08\) and \(\eta/s = 0.16\) respectively.
* At the center of the fireball (\(\eta=0, x=0\)) at \(\tau=1.2\) fm/c, the pressure ratio \(P_L/P_T\) reaches about 0.6 for \(\eta/s=0.08\), remains at \(0.2-0.4\) for \(\eta/s=0.16\), and increases rapidly for free-streaming.
* The average transverse flow \(\langle v_T \rangle\) at \(\eta=0\) increases linearly with \(\tau-\tau_0\) and is almost identical for all three relaxation times.
* The slope of the average velocity \(-d\langle v_x \rangle/d\eta\) at \(\tau=1.2\) fm/c differs significantly between the three cases, with the largest slope for \(\eta/s=0.08\) and the smallest for free-streaming.

## Context

This work extends the study of pre-equilibrium dynamics in heavy-ion collisions beyond the boost-invariant approximation used in earlier kinetic treatments (Kurkela 2018, Ambrus 2022, Liyanage 2022) and beyond the free-streaming pre-equilibrium stage used in hydrodynamic analyses (Bernhard 2019, JETSCAPE 2020, Nijs 2020). It builds on the observation by Bozek (2010) that rapidity-dependent directed flow may be sensitive to early-stage pressure asymmetry in hydrodynamics. The results suggest that experimental measurement of rapidity-odd directed flow could serve as a probe of the pressure asymmetry between longitudinal and transverse directions during the early non-equilibrium stage, which is not accessible from transverse-plane flow observables alone.
