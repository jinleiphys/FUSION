# The Dilatonic Dynamics of Baryonic Crystals, Branes and Spheres

> We systematically analyze the impact of dilatonic dynamics on Skyrme spheres, crystals and branes. The effects of the dilatonic model parameters, encompassing different underlying near-conformal dynamics, on the macroscopic properties of Skyrmions such as their mass and radius, are discussed. For spheres and crystals we identify special values of the ratio of the decay constants for which the second order differential equations reduce to a solvable first order system. Additionally, in the case of the crystals, the dilaton presence spatially separates the baryon and isospin charge distributions. For branes, we show how the dilaton smooths out their configurations. Our results are expected to have wide implications from the study of near-conformal dynamics stemming from QCD-like theories to phenomenological investigations of nuclear matter in extreme regimes.

## Key claim

The paper systematically analyzes how dilatonic dynamics, parameterized by the dilaton mass, the anomalous dimension of the chiral condensate, and the conformal dimension of the operator triggering the renormalization group flow, modifies the properties of Skyrme spheres, crystals, and branes; the abstract glosses over the finding that the dilaton decouples rapidly from the dynamics once its mass exceeds the pion mass, and that this decoupling happens faster when the anomalous dimension of the chiral condensate is large.

## Method

The authors augment the Skyrme Lagrangian for two flavors with a dilaton field, dressing each operator of mass dimension k with a factor e^{(k-4)σ/f} to restore conformal invariance, and model explicit conformal breaking via a dilaton potential V(σ) depending on parameters Δ (conformal dimension of the perturbing operator) and m_σ (dilaton mass). They numerically solve the coupled second-order equations of motion for the hedgehog ansatz (spherical Skyrmions) and for crystal configurations, using QCD-inspired values f=0.29, λ=1/4.75², m_π=140/93 in units of √K, with boundary conditions α(0)=π, α(∞)=0 and σ(∞)=σ_f determined by the pion mass term. For branes, they find the general analytic solution assuming fields depend only on time and one spatial coordinate.

## Key numbers

- For spherical Skyrmions with B=1, the baryon mass M_S first decreases with increasing dilaton mass m_σ until m_σ ~ 6 (in units of √K), then climbs toward the pure Skyrme model value M_Skyrme = 15.67 (in units of √K).
- The root mean square radius ⟨r²⟩^{1/2} increases with m_σ up to m_σ ~ 6, then decreases for higher dilaton masses; for m_σ=1,2,...,5 it grows monotonically with Δ, while for m_σ=7,8,9,10 the opposite holds.
- For m_σ=1, both M_S and ⟨r²⟩^{1/2} differ by more than 20% from their values without the dilaton, indicating rapid decoupling as m_σ increases.
- The dilaton profile χ(r) flattens toward its asymptotic value χ=1 as m_σ increases at fixed Δ, and as Δ decreases at fixed m_σ.
- For branes, a lower bound on the dilaton mass exists for any given topological charge such that the solution is real.

## Context

This work extends the study of Skyrmion matter in near-conformal gauge theories, building on the dilaton effective field theory framework developed by Appelquist (2020, 2022), Golterman (2016-2021), and others for modeling walking dynamics near the conformal window. It generalizes earlier analyses of Skyrmion crystals by Canfora (2018, 2022, 2023) and branes by Alvarez (2017) and Ayon-Beato (2015) to include dilatonic dynamics, and differs from prior numerical studies by Lee (2003) and Park (2008) by accounting for the dependence of the dilaton vacuum expectation value on the pion mass. The results enable phenomenological investigations of nuclear matter in extreme regimes, such as neutron star cores, within the generalized nuclear effective field theory (GnEFT) framework reviewed by Ma (2021, 2023).
