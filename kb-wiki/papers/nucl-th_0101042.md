# Self-energy Effects in the Superfluidity of Neutron Matter

> The superfluidity of neutron matter in the channel $^1 S_0$ is studied by taking into account the effect of the ground-state correlations in the self-energy. To this purpose the gap equation has been solved within the generalized Gorkov approach. A sizeable suppression of the energy gap is driven by the quasi-particle strength around the Fermi surface.

# Self-energy Effects in the Superfluidity of Neutron Matter

## Key claim

Self-energy corrections from ground-state correlations, particularly the quasi-particle strength (Z-factor) around the Fermi surface, cause a sizeable suppression of the $^1S_0$ pairing gap in neutron matter beyond what is captured by the standard BCS approach or by earlier treatments that considered self-energy only at the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) level. The inclusion of the second-order rearrangement term ($\Sigma^2$) produces a further non-negligible reduction beyond the BHF-only self-energy treatment of Baldo (unreferenced in text).

## Method

The generalized Gorkov gap equation was solved for pure neutron matter in the $^1S_0$ channel, coupled with the density closure equation, using the Argonne $V_{14}$ potential as the bare pairing interaction. The neutron self-energy was calculated at two levels of the hole-line expansion in Brueckner theory with the continuous choice: first-order (BHF, $\Sigma^1$) and second-order (extended BHF, $\Sigma^1 + \Sigma^2$), from which the quasi-particle strength Z and effective mass $m^*$ were extracted. The gap equation was solved in the static limit where the pairing interaction is taken as the bare force, with self-energy effects entering through the Z-factor and the quasi-particle energy spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum range studied: $0.5 \le k_F \le 1.3\ \text{fm}^{-1}$, corresponding to density range $0.0042$ through $0.074\ \text{fm}^{-3}$
- At $k_F = 0.8\ \text{fm}^{-1}$, the Z-factor from the BHF-only self-energy is approximately $0.9$, while including the rearrangement term ($\Sigma^1 + \Sigma^2$) gives $Z \approx 0.83$
- At $k_F = 0.8\ \text{fm}^{-1}$, the effective mass ratio including the rearrangement term is $m^*/m \approx 1.2$

## Context

This work extends the investigation of self-energy effects on pairing by Baldo (unreferenced in text), who considered self-energy only at the BHF level but whose approximations underestimated correlation effects. The authors note that most previous microscopic calculations of neutron matter superfluidity used the bare NN force in the gap equation (Lombardo, Migdal, Ring) or studied medium polarization via induced interaction theory (Brown, Clark, Ainsworth, Schuck), but self-energy corrections had attracted much less attention. The present study is an intermediate step toward a fully consistent treatment where self-energy and vertex corrections are treated on equal footing; the authors expect similar effects in symmetric nuclear matter and note that precise pairing gaps are important for understanding superfluidity in neutron stars and exotic nuclei.
