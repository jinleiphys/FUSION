# Gamma-Ray Constraint on Galactic Positron Production by MeV Dark Matter

> The Galactic positrons, as observed by their annihilation gamma-ray line at 0.511 MeV, are difficult to account for with astrophysical sources. It has been proposed that they are produced instead by dark matter annihilation or decay in the inner Galactic halo. To avoid other constraints, these processes are required to occur "invisibly," such that the eventual positron annihilation is the only detectable signal. However, electromagnetic radiative corrections to these processes inevitably produce real gamma rays (``internal bremsstrahlung"); this emission violates COMPTEL and EGRET constraints unless the dark matter mass is less than about 20 MeV.

# Gamma-Ray Constraint on Galactic Positron Production by MeV Dark Matter

## Key claim

Internal bremsstrahlung gamma rays inevitably accompany dark matter annihilation or decay into electron-positron pairs, and this emission violates COMPTEL and EGRET diffuse gamma-ray constraints unless the dark matter mass is less than about 20 MeV. The constraint is nearly independent of the specific new particle physics mediating the annihilation and would be strengthened if the assumptions about positron confinement, equilibrium, or annihilation at rest are relaxed.

## Method

The authors calculated the internal bremsstrahlung cross section for the process χχ → e⁺e⁻γ using the final-state radiation probability from the kinematically similar process e⁺e⁻ → μ⁺μ⁻, modified by replacing the muon mass with the electron mass. They normalized the internal bremsstrahlung gamma-ray flux to the observed 0.511 MeV positron annihilation flux from INTEGRAL, accounting for the positronium fraction (f = 0.93 ± 0.04), and compared the resulting spectra to COMPTEL and EGRET measurements of diffuse gamma-ray emission from the Galactic center region.

## Key numbers

- The observed 0.511 MeV flux from the Galactic center region is Φ₅₁₁ = (9.9⁺⁴·⁷₋₂·₁) × 10⁻⁴ photons cm⁻² s⁻¹.
- The 0.511 MeV emission region has a full width at half maximum of 9 degrees, with a 2-σ uncertainty range covering 6–18 degrees.
- The positronium fraction is f = 0.93 ± 0.04, meaning most positrons annihilate via positronium formation.
- The internal bremsstrahlung flux per steradian is (4.2⁺²·⁰₋₀·₉) × 10⁻² (1/σ_tot)(dσ_Br/dE) cm⁻² s⁻¹ sr⁻¹.
- The dark matter mass is constrained to m_χ ≲ 20 MeV; varying the emission region size from 6 to 18 degrees changes the upper limit from 10 to 60 MeV.

## Context

This paper directly tests the model of Boehm et al. (2004), who proposed that light (1–100 MeV) dark matter particles annihilating invisibly into e⁺e⁻ pairs could explain the Galactic positron excess observed by INTEGRAL. The work extends the standard argument that high-mass dark matter candidates (10 GeV–10 TeV) produce excessive gamma rays from secondary decays, showing that even MeV-scale dark matter is constrained by internal bremsstrahlung. The result also applies to dark matter decay models such as those of Picciotto and Hooper (2004) and Hooper (2004). Future gamma-ray data from COMPTEL and EGRET successors could improve the sensitivity of this constraint.
