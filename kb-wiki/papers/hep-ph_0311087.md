# The End of the Constituent Quark Model?

> In this conference summary talk at Hadron03, questions and challenges for Hadron physics of light flavours are outlined. Precision data and recent discoveries are at last exposing the limitations of the naive constituent quark model and also giving hints as to its extension into a more mature description of hadrons. These notes also pay special attention to the positive strangeness baryon $Θ^+(1540)$ and include a pedagogic discussion of wavefunctions in the pentaquark picture, their relation with the Skyrme model and related issues of phenomenology.

## Key claim

The naive constituent quark model is being exposed as inadequate by precision data and recent discoveries, particularly in the scalar meson sector and the positive strangeness baryon Θ⁺(1540), but the paper argues that the quark model and chiral approaches each serve as the best approximation for different hadronic systems depending on whether the leading Fock state component is S-wave or higher partial wave.

## Method

The paper reviews light hadron spectroscopy from the Hadron03 conference, analyzing the successes and failures of the non-relativistic quark model across different J^PC channels. It classifies hadronic states by whether the leading partial wave of the quark configuration is lower or higher than that of the hadronic decay channels, using this to determine whether the quark model or meson-meson dynamics dominates. The author develops explicit wavefunctions for the pentaquark Θ⁺(1540) in the 10-bar representation using a diquark-diquark-antiquark construction, and compares the mass splittings predicted by the pentaquark picture versus the Skyrme model.

## Key numbers

- The Θ⁺(1540) has a width below 10 MeV, possibly as low as 1 MeV
- The D_s states at 2.32 GeV and 2.46 GeV cannot be explained as simple c\bar{s} in potential models
- In the pentaquark picture, the exotic Ξ states with strangeness -2 are predicted around 1700 MeV, versus over 2 GeV in the Skyrme model of Diakonov, Petrov and Polyakov (1997)
- The mass gap from Θ to Ξ in the pentaquark picture is one unit of m_s (~150 MeV), while the Skyrme model requires three units (~540 MeV)
- The f_0(980) and a_0(980) are candidates for qq\bar{q}\bar{q} or dimeson dynamical structure

## Context

This conference summary extends the critique of the naive constituent quark model raised by Jaffe (1977) and Tornqvist (1995) regarding multiquark states in the scalar meson sector, and addresses the tension between the Skyrme model prediction of Diakonov, Petrov and Polyakov (1997) for the Θ⁺ and the pentaquark interpretation developed by Karliner and Lipkin (2003) and Jaffe and Wilczek (2003). The work enables future experimental tests at Jefferson Laboratory (planned for 2004) to distinguish between the Skyrme and pentaquark pictures through the masses of exotic Ξ states, and motivates further study of how S-wave hadronic thresholds distort the simple quark model spectrum across all light hadron sectors.
