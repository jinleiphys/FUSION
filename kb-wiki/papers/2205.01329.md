# Deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum with a point-coupling functional. II. Examples of odd Nd isotopes

> The aim of this work is to extend the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) based on the point-coupling density functionals to odd-$A$ and odd-odd nuclei and examine its applicability by taking odd-$A$ Nd isotopes as examples. In the DRHBc theory, the densities and potentials with axial deformation are expanded in terms of Legendre polynomials, and the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations are solved in a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis to include the continuum effects. For an odd-$A$ or odd-odd nucleus, the blocking effect of unpaired nucleon(s) is taken into account with the equal filling approximation. To determine its ground state, an automatic blocking procedure is adopted, in which the orbital with the lowest quasiparticle energy is blocked during the iteration. This procedure is justified by comparing with the results from the orbital-fixed blocking calculations, in which the blocked orbital near the Fermi surface is fixed during the iteration. The ground states for both light and heavy nuclei can be provided by the automatic blocking procedure as the orbital-fixed blocking procedure, but with considerably reduced computational cost. The numerical details for even-even nuclei are found to be valid for odd-$A$ and odd-odd nuclei as well. Taking Nd isotopes including both even-even and odd-$A$ ones as examples, the calculated ground-state properties with PC-PK1 are in good agreement with the available experimental data. This work paves the way to construct the DRHBc mass table including all even-even, odd-$A$ and odd-odd nuclei in the nuclear chart.

## Key claim

The deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with a point-coupling functional is extended to odd-A and odd-odd nuclei by incorporating the blocking effect via the equal filling approximation, and the automatic blocking procedure (blocking the lowest quasiparticle orbital in each iteration) yields ground states equivalent to the computationally more expensive orbital-fixed blocking procedure, as validated for both light and heavy nuclei.

## Method

The DRHBc equations are solved in a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis with axial deformation, where densities and potentials are expanded in Legendre polynomials. For odd-A and odd-odd nuclei, the blocking effect of unpaired nucleon(s) is treated with the equal filling approximation, and an automatic blocking procedure is adopted in which the orbital with the lowest quasiparticle energy is blocked during each iteration. The method is validated by comparing potential energy curves from the automatic procedure against those from orbital-fixed blocking calculations for the light nuclei 23Mg and 22Al and the heavy nuclei 175Nd and 174Pm.

## Key numbers

* Binding energies per nucleon for Nd isotopes from the proton drip line to N=82 increase gradually, then decrease from N=82 to the neutron drip line; the DRHBc results agree well with experimental data from Wang (2021).
* For 301Th, changing the energy cutoff E_cut from 300 to 350 MeV changes the total energy by 0.0007 MeV and the quadrupole deformation beta_2 by less than 0.0001.
* For 301Th, changing the angular momentum cutoff J_max from 23/2 to 27/2 hbar changes the total energy by 0.2583 MeV (0.01%) and beta_2 by 0.003.
* For 301Th, changing the Legendre expansion truncation lambda_max from 8 to 14 changes the total energy by 0.1076 MeV and beta_2 by 0.0001; at a constrained deformation beta_2=0.6, the total energy changes by 0.1777 MeV (0.01%).
* The pairing strength used is V_0 = -325.0 MeV fm^3 with a sharp pairing window of 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the DRHBc theory with point-coupling functionals, previously developed for even-even nuclei by Zhang (2020) and applied to 2583 even-even nuclei in the DRHBc mass table by Zhang (2022), to odd-A and odd-odd nuclei. It builds on the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory developed by Meng (1996, 1998) and the first nuclear mass table including continuum effects by Xia (2018). The results enable construction of a complete DRHBc mass table covering all even-even, odd-A, and odd-odd nuclei across the nuclear chart, providing predictions for exotic nuclei beyond current experimental capability.
