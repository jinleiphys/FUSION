# More on the relation between the two physically inequivalent decompositions of the nucleon spin

> The recent controversy on the nucleon spin decomposition problem is critically overviewed. We argue that there exist two and only two physically inequivalent gauge-invariant decompositions of the longitudinal nucleon spin, contrary to the rapidly spreading view in the QCD spin physics community that there are infinitely many decompositions of the nucleon spin.

## Key claim
The paper argues that there are exactly two physically inequivalent gauge-invariant decompositions of the longitudinal nucleon spin (called decomposition (I) and (II)), contrary to the view that there are infinitely many such decompositions. The author claims that the apparent non-uniqueness argued by Lorc'e (2012) and Ji et al. (2012) stems from overlooking the transversality condition that uniquely fixes the decomposition of the gauge field into physical and pure-gauge components once the Lorentz frame is chosen.

## Method
The author reviews the controversy by first establishing that the decomposition of the gluon field into physical and pure-gauge components is analogous to the standard transverse-longitudinal decomposition of the photon field in QED, which is unique once the Lorentz frame is fixed. The paper then demonstrates that the Stueckelberg-like transformation proposed by Lorc'e (2012) fails to preserve the transversality condition for the physical component, and that the gauge-invariant extension (GIE) approach of Ji et al. (2012) introduces path-dependent gauge links that do not yield true gauge-invariant observables. The analysis uses the QED case as a simpler analog, showing that the total angular momentum of a charged particle and photon system admits exactly two gauge-invariant decompositions: one where the potential angular momentum is combined with the photon orbital angular momentum (decomposition I, with "dynamical" OAMs) and one where it is combined with the charged particle orbital angular momentum (decomposition II, with generalized "canonical" OAMs).

## Key numbers
- The paper calculates the 1-loop anomalous dimension matrix for the quark and gluon spin operators in the nucleon, finding that it reproduces the standardly-known answer irrespectively of the choice of the constant 4-vector \(n^\mu\) that specifies the Lorentz frame for the transversality condition.
- The projection operator for the physical component of the gauge field is given as \(P^{\mu\nu}_{phys} = g^{\mu\nu} - \frac{\partial^\mu n^\nu + \partial^\nu n^\mu}{\partial \cdot n} + \frac{n^\mu n^\nu \Box}{(\partial \cdot n)^2}\), where \(n^\mu\) is an arbitrary constant 4-vector (time-like, light-like, or space-like).

## Context
This paper extends the author's prior work (Wakamatsu 2010, 2011, 2012) establishing two gauge-invariant decompositions of the nucleon spin, and directly contradicts the claims of Lorc'e (2012) that there are infinitely many decompositions due to a Stueckelberg-like symmetry, and of Ji et al. (2012) that the Chen decomposition is merely one of many gauge-invariant extensions of the Jaffe-Manohar decomposition. The paper argues that the dynamical OAMs in decomposition (I) can be extracted model-independently from combined analysis of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs), while the observability of the canonical OAM in decomposition (II) remains an open question requiring clarification of path-dependence in Wigner distributions and the status of transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions (TMDs).
