# Reassessment of the NuTeV determination of the Weinberg angle

> In light of the recent discovery of the importance of the isovector EMC effect for the interpretation of the NuTeV determination of sin^2 theta_W, it seems timely to reassess the central value and the errors on this fundamental Standard Model parameter derived from the NuTeV data. We also include earlier work on charge symmetry violation and the recent limits on a possible asymmetry between s and \bar{s} quarks. With these corrections we find a revised NuTeV result of sin^2 theta_W = 0.2232 \pm 0.0013(stat) \pm 0.0024(syst), which is in excellent agreement with the running of sin^2 theta_W predicted by the Standard Model.

## Key claim

After including corrections from the isovector EMC effect, charge symmetry violation (CSV), and a possible strange quark asymmetry, the NuTeV determination of the weak mixing angle is revised to sin^2 theta_W = 0.2221 ± 0.0013(stat) ± 0.0020(syst), which is in excellent agreement with the Standard Model prediction of 0.2227 ± 0.0004, removing the previously reported three-standard-deviation discrepancy.

## Method

The authors reassess the NuTeV extraction of sin^2 theta_W by combining three independent corrections: the isovector EMC effect from the neutron excess in iron, calculated using a nuclear matter approximation at 0.89 times nuclear matter density; charge symmetry violation from quark mass differences and QED splitting; and a possible asymmetry between s and anti-s quark distributions. For each correction, they compute the effect on the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio and then convert it to a correction to sin^2 theta_W using the NuTeV functional provided by the collaboration, combining the systematic errors in quadrature.

## Key numbers

- Revised NuTeV result: sin^2 theta_W = 0.2221 ± 0.0013(stat) ± 0.0020(syst)
- Original NuTeV result: sin^2 theta_W = 0.2277 ± 0.0013(stat) ± 0.0009(syst)
- Standard Model prediction: sin^2 theta_W = 0.2227 ± 0.0004 (on-shell scheme)
- Isovector EMC correction: Delta R^{rho^0} = -0.0019 ± 0.0006
- Total CSV correction: Delta R^{CSV} = -0.0026 ± 0.0011
- Strange quark correction (preferred value): Delta R^s = 0.0 ± 0.0018
- After corrections, the total chi^2 for R^nu and R^{anti-nu} compared with Standard Model values moves from 7.19 to 2.58

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Cloet et al. (2009) on the isovector EMC effect and incorporates earlier work on charge symmetry violation by Sather (1991) and Rodionov et al. (1994), as well as the Londergan and Thomas (2003) demonstration of model independence for the leading CSV contribution. The reassessment resolves the long-standing NuTeV anomaly that had been interpreted as possible new physics, and the authors note that future improvements in determining the strange quark asymmetry and the anti-neutrino ratio R^{anti-nu} would further constrain the result.
