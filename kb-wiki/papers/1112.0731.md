# Modifications to the Hadronic Regge Trajectories

> The effect of quark mass on the Regge trajectory is analysed. Modifications in the equations of Regge trajectories are shown for mesonic as well as baryonic systems. For mesonic systems, the Regge trajectories get modified, but still remain linear. Contrary to the mesonic case, the Regge trajectories for baryonic systems indicate non-linearity. It is shown that in low mass and angular momentum region two hadrons with different quark compositions can have same mass and angular momentum.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when finite quark masses are included in the string model of hadrons, mesonic Regge trajectories remain linear in form, while baryonic Regge trajectories become non-linear. The abstract's claim of non-linearity for baryons is conditional on the averaging over three equally probable string configurations for the baryon, and the linearity for mesons holds only when plotting angular momentum versus (mass minus a mass-dependent offset) squared, not versus mass squared directly.

## Method

The authors model mesons as a quark and antiquark connected by a rotating string of length \(l\) with linear energy density \(K\), and baryons as three quarks in three equally probable string configurations (one quark rotating about the other two). They derive analytic expressions for the classical mass and classical angular momentum of these systems, incorporating finite quark masses and a rotational speed \(f\) (fraction of the speed of light). The resulting modified Regge trajectory equations are then plotted for varying quark mass and rotational speed, and compared to nucleon resonance data.

## Key numbers

- The observed Regge slope parameter is \(\alpha' = 0.93\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), which gives a string tension \(K = 0.87\ \mathrm{GeV/fm}\).
- In computations, the authors use quark masses \(m_1 = 1.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (u quark), \(m_2 = 3.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (d quark), \(m_3 = 3.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (d quark), string tension \(K = 0.2\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), and string length \(l = 1\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- The nucleon resonance data compared include \(N^*(1238, \frac{3}{2}^+)\), \(N^*(1688, \frac{5}{2}^+)\), \(N^*(1920, \frac{7}{2})\), \(N^*(2190, \frac{9}{2})\), and \(N^*(2360, \frac{11}{2})\); the model's baryonic Regge trajectories are found in close agreement with these data.

## Context

This work extends the standard string model of hadrons, which assumes massless quarks and yields the linear Regge trajectory \(J = \alpha' M^2 + \alpha_0\) (Chew and Frautschi, 1960s), by incorporating finite quark masses. It builds on earlier studies of non-linearity in Regge trajectories by Inopin (2000), Hothi (2000), Bura (2001), Soloviev (2001), and others. The results enable future investigation of whether the non-linearity in baryonic trajectories is connected to the mass difference between quarks and diquarks, and whether position-dependent quark masses affect the trajectories; the authors also note that spin effects were neglected and may account for deviations from experiment.
