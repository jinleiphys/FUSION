# Superfluidity of $Λ$ hyperons in warm strange hadronic star matter

> In this work we evaluate the $^1S_0$ superfluidity of $Λ$ hyperons in $β$-stable strange hadronic matter. We investigate the equation of state (EOS) of hadronic matter at finite values of baryon density and temperature in the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. Effects of the introduced isoscalar-isovector cross-interaction term on the $Λ$ superfluidity are investigated systematically. In addition, the temperature effects on the superfluidity of $Λ$ hyperons in hadronic matter are discussed. It is found that the density region and the magnitude of the $Λ$ pairing gap are dependent on the cross-interaction term. The obtained maximal critical temperature of $Λ$ superfluid is around $10^9$ K.

## Key claim

The maximal critical temperature of $^1S_0$ $\Lambda$ superfluid in $\beta$-stable strange hadronic matter is around $10^9$ K, but this value depends strongly on the choice of the isoscalar-isovector cross-interaction term and the $\Lambda\Lambda$ pairing potential; the result is obtained within the relativistic mean field (RMF) model and is consistent with the weak-coupling approximation.

## Method

The authors solve the finite-temperature gap equation for $^1S_0$ $\Lambda$ pairing in $\beta$-equilibrium hadronic matter containing the full baryon octet (nucleons, $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, $\Xi$) and leptons, using the RMF model with nonlinear self-interactions and an isoscalar-isovector cross-interaction term ($\omega^2\rho^2$). The equation of state is computed self-consistently at finite temperature, and the $\Lambda$ pairing gap is obtained using several $\Lambda\Lambda$ potentials (ESC00, ND1, ND2, NSC97b/e/f/s, NFs) fitted to Nijmegen models or the Nagara event. The influence of the cross-interaction strength is studied by varying the parameter $\Lambda_\nu$ in the FSU and NL3* parameter sets.

## Key numbers

- Maximal $^1S_0$ $\Lambda$ energy gap at $T=0$ with ESC00 potential: 0.81 MeV (FSU parameter set) and 0.63 MeV (NL3* parameter set).
- Maximal critical temperature of $\Lambda$ superfluid with ESC00 potential: $5.34 \times 10^9$ K (FSU) and $4.19 \times 10^9$ K (NL3*).
- Threshold density for $\Lambda$ appearance in FSU: approximately 0.39 fm$^{-3}$.
- Baryon density corresponding to maximal $\Lambda$ pairing gap with FSU and ESC00: 0.439 fm$^{-3}$.
- $\Lambda$ fraction at maximal critical temperature (FSU, ESC00): about 4%.
- Maximal pairing gap with ND1 potential (FSU): 0.18 MeV; with ND2: 0.12 MeV; with NSC97b: $2.0 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV; with NFs: $1.7 \times 10^{-2}$ MeV.
- Critical temperature with NSC97s potential (FSU): $6.7 \times 10^7$ K; with NFs: $1.0 \times 10^8$ K.
- $\Lambda$ superfluid exists for $\Lambda$ fractions from $3.8 \times 10^{-3}\%$ up to $\sim 18.8\%$ at $T=0$ in FSU, corresponding to baryon densities from 0.39 fm$^{-3}$ to $\sim 0.71$ fm$^{-3}$.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of $\Lambda$ superfluidity in neutron star matter by Balberg and Barnea (using $G$-matrix effective interactions) and by Takatsuka and Tamagaki (using bare $\Lambda\Lambda$ interactions), who predicted critical temperatures around $10^9$ K and gap energies of several tenths of an MeV. It also builds on the RMF-based investigation of $\Lambda\Lambda$ pairing by Tanigawa et al. (2003) and the work of Wang and Shen (using Nijmegen $\Lambda\Lambda$ potentials). The novel contributions are the systematic study of the isoscalar-isovector cross-interaction term on $\Lambda$ superfluidity and the first finite-temperature treatment of $\Lambda$ pairing within the RMF model. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star cooling and glitch phenomena by providing temperature-dependent pairing gaps for $\Lambda$ hyperons.
