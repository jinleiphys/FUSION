# Quantum interference terms in nonmesonic weak decay of $Λ$-hypernuclei within a RPA formalism

> Single and double coincidence nucleon spectra in the $Λ$-hypernuclei weak decay are evaluated and discussed using a microscopic formalism. Nuclear matter is employed together with the local density approximation which allows us to analyze the $^{12}_ΛC$ hypernucleus non-mesonic weak decay. Final state interactions (FSI) are included via the first order (in the nuclear residual interaction) terms to the RPA, where the strong residual interaction is modelled by a Bonn potential. At this level of approximation, these FSI are pure quantum interference terms between the primary decay $(ΛN \to NN)$ and $(ΛN \to NN \to NN)$, where the strong interaction is responsible for the last piece in the second reaction. Also the Pauli exchange contributions are explicitly evaluated. We show that the inclusion of Pauli exchange terms is important. A comparison with data is made. We conclude that the limitations in phase space in the RPA makes this approximation inadequate to reproduce the nucleon spectra. This fact, does not allow us to draw a definite conclusion about the importance of the interference terms.

# Quantum interference terms in nonmesonic weak decay of $\Lambda$-hypernuclei within a RPA formalism

## Key claim

The first-order RPA contributions to final state interactions in the nonmesonic weak decay of $\Lambda$-hypernuclei are pure quantum interference terms between the primary decay $(\Lambda N \to NN)$ and the process $(\Lambda N \to NN \to NN)$, but the limitations in phase space within the RPA approximation make it inadequate to reproduce the nucleon spectra, preventing a definite conclusion about the importance of these interference terms.

## Method

The author employs nonrelativistic nuclear matter together with the local density approximation (LDA) to analyze the $^{12}_{\Lambda}C$ hypernucleus. Final state interactions are included via the first-order (in the nuclear residual interaction) terms to the RPA, where the strong residual interaction is modelled by a Bonn potential. The Pauli exchange contributions are explicitly evaluated alongside the direct (ring) terms, and the nucleon spectra are obtained by integrating over the $\Lambda$ momentum distribution and the nuclear density profile.

## Key numbers

- The ratio $\Gamma_{n/p} \equiv \Gamma_n / \Gamma_p$ is discussed: theory predicts a value smaller than 0.5, while experimental results from intranuclear cascade code (INC) analysis give $(\Gamma_{n/p})^{exp} \sim 0.4-0.6$.
- The total nonmesonic decay width $\Gamma_{NM}$ is defined as $\Gamma_{NM} = \Gamma_1 + \Gamma_2$, where $\Gamma_1 \equiv \Gamma_n + \Gamma_p$ and $\Gamma_2$ is the two-body induced decay width from processes such as $\Lambda NN \to nNN$.
- The Fermi momenta are defined as $k_{F_{n(p)}}(r) = \hbar c (3 \pi^2 \rho_{n(p)}(r))^{1/3}$, with $\rho_n(r) = \rho(r) N/(N+Z)$ and $\rho_p(r) = \rho(r) Z/(N+Z)$ for the nuclear core of the hypernucleus.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic model of Bauer (2007) for nucleon spectra in $\Lambda$-hypernuclei decay by incorporating Pauli exchange terms into the RPA formalism, which were absent in the earlier ring approximation. It contrasts with the intranuclear cascade code (INC) approach used by Ramos et al. (1997), Gaitanos et al. (2003-2004), and Bauer et al. (2006), which cannot include quantum interference terms between the primary weak decay and strong rescattering. The results enable future work to assess whether higher-order RPA contributions or alternative formalisms are needed to resolve the longstanding discrepancy between theoretical and experimental values of $\Gamma_{n/p}$.
