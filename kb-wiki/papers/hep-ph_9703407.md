# Implication of the Modified Gottfried Sum Rule

## Key claim

The modified Gottfried sum rule and its relatives can provide model-independent information about the mean quantum numbers of sea quarks in the nucleon, but the formalism relies on current anti-commutation relations on the null-plane and the Deser-Gilbert-Sudarshan representation, which incorporate causality and spectral conditions that are not fully justified by intuitive methods alone.

## Method

The author derives sum rules for the mean quantum numbers of light sea quarks using current anti-commutation relations on the null-plane, justified through the Deser-Gilbert-Sudarshan representation. The formalism separates non-perturbative contributions (dominating at small x, reflecting vacuum properties of the nucleon) from perturbative QCD contributions (dominating at larger x), with matching between the two regimes performed at the level of moments: the n=1 moment is treated non-perturbatively, while moments above n=2 are treated perturbatively. The hypercharge sum rule and mean charge sum rules for light sea quarks in the proton and neutron are then derived from the formalism.

## Key numbers

- The modified Gottfried sum rule gives the mean I3 of light sea quarks in the proton as 3(0.26-0.33)/4 ~ -0.053
- The mean hypercharge of light sea quarks in the proton is (2.12-1)/2 ~ 0.56
- The mean charge of light sea quarks in the proton is approximately 0.2
- The mean charge of light sea quarks in the neutron is approximately 0.3
- The intermediate integrals are Iπ ~ 5.17, IKp ~ 2.39, and IKn ~ 1.61
- The perturbative QCD prediction for the Q2 dependence of the Gottfried sum rule is roughly 0.01[αs(Q2)-αs(Q20)]

## Context

This work extends the author's previous formalism (Koretune, 1993; Koretune, 1995) for the modified Gottfried sum rule, which was developed in response to the surprising NMC experimental result that the Gottfried sum rule value departs largely from 1/3, a departure impossible to explain perturbatively. The approach is complementary to the perturbative QCD treatment of Ross and Sachrajda (1978), as the non-perturbative pomeron contribution (reflecting the vacuum property of the nucleon, following Weinberg, 1970) is argued to shield the perturbative Q2 dependence. The formalism enables model-independent constraints on the integrals of bilocal current matrix elements corresponding to the n=1 moment, and provides sum rules for the mean charge of light sea quarks in both the proton and neutron that can in principle be determined experimentally.
