# Hadron yields and fluctuations at the CERN Super Proton Synchrotron: system size dependence from Pb+Pb to p+p collisions

> The kaon to pion ratio $K^+/π^+$ and the scaled variance $ω^-$ for fluctuations of negatively charged particles are studied within the statistical hadron resonance gas (HRG) model and the Ultra relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model. The calculations are done for p+p, Be+Be, Ar+Sc, and Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN Super Proton Synchrotron energy range to reveal the system size dependence of hadron production. For the HRG calculations the canonical ensemble is imposed for all conserved charges. In the UrQMD simulations the centrality selection in nucleus-nucleus collisions is done by calculating the forward energy $E_{\rm F}$ deposited in the Projectile Spectator Detector, and the acceptance maps of the NA61/SHINE detectors are used. A comparison of the HRG and UrQMD results with the data of the NA61/SHINE Collaboration is done. To understand a difference of the event-by-event fluctuations in p+p and heavy ion collisions the centrality selection procedure in the sample of all inelastic p+p events is proposed and analyzed within the UrQMD simulations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed step-like behavior of hadron production observables (the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio and the scaled variance $\omega^-$) as a function of system size from p+p to Pb+Pb collisions can be largely explained by canonical ensemble suppression effects in the statistical hadron resonance gas (HRG) model and by centrality selection effects in the UrQMD transport model, but the HRG model fails to reproduce the large $\omega^- > 1$ values seen in p+p and Be+Be collisions at the highest SPS energy.

## Method

The authors performed calculations using two complementary models: the canonical ensemble (CE) statistical hadron resonance gas (HRG) model with fixed baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness, and the Ultra relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model. For the HRG calculations, the system radius was determined by assuming equal baryon densities across all colliding systems, and the strangeness suppression parameter $\gamma_S$ was varied. For the UrQMD simulations, the experimental centrality selection procedure of the NA61/SHINE Collaboration was implemented by calculating the forward energy $E_{\rm F}$ deposited in the Projectile Spectator Detector (PSD) and applying the detector acceptance maps; a novel centrality selection procedure for inelastic p+p events was also introduced.

## Key numbers

- The CE HRG model with $\gamma_S=1$ predicts a monotonous increase of $K^+/\pi^+$ and decrease of $\omega^-$ with system size; the difference between p+p and A+A collisions is strongest at the smallest collision energies.
- For p+p collisions at $p_{\rm lab}=158$ GeV/$c$, the UrQMD simulation gives $\omega^- \cong 1.15$ for all inelastic events and $\omega^- \cong 0.76$ for the 13% most central p+p collisions (defined by $E_{\rm F}=0$).
- The acceptance parameter $q$ in Eq. (acc) for Pb+Pb collisions is $q\cong 0.06$ at 30 $A$ GeV/$c$ and $q\cong 0.16$ at 158 $A$ GeV/$c$, compared to $q\cong 0.3$ and $q\cong 0.4$ for other systems at 30/31 and 150/158 $A$ GeV/$c$, respectively.
- In p+p inelastic collisions at $p_{\rm lab}=31$ GeV/$c$, about 48% of events have $E_{\rm F}=0$; at $p_{\rm lab}=158$ GeV/$c$, about 13% of events have $E_{\rm F}=0$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of hadron production systematics performed by the NA61/SHINE Collaboration (Gazdzicki 2017, Seryakov 2017) and the NA49 Collaboration (Alt 2008, Anticic 2011), which suggested a possible "onset of fireball" between Be+Be and Ar+Sc collisions. The paper uses the CE HRG approach (following Vovchenko 2015, Begun 2018) and the UrQMD transport model (Bass 1998, Bleicher 1999) to test whether these models can reproduce the observed system size dependence. The results enable a better understanding of how centrality selection affects fluctuation observables and suggest that future comparisons between p+p and A+A collisions should use matched centrality selection procedures rather than comparing all inelastic p+p events to central A+A events.
