# Hyperon Puzzle, Hadron-Quark Crossover and Massive Neutron Stars

> Bulk properties of cold and hot neutron stars (NSs) are studied on the basis of the hadron-quark crossover picture where a smooth transition from the hadronic phase to the quark phase takes place at finite baryon density. By using a phenomenological equation of state (EOS) "CRover" which interpolates the two phases at around 3 times the nuclear matter density, it is found that the cold NSs with the gravitational mass larger than 2-solarmass can be sustained. This is in sharp contrast to the case of the first-order hadron-quark transition. The radii of the cold NSs with the CRover EOS are in the narrow range which is insensitive to the NS masses. Due to the stiffening of the EOS induced by the hadron-quark crossover, the central density of the NSs is at most 4 times the nuclear matter density and the hyperon-mixing barely occurs inside the NS core. This constitutes a solution of the long-standing hyperon puzzle. The effect of color superconductivity (CSC) on the NS structures is also examined with the hadron-quark crossover. For the typical strength of the diquark attraction, a slight softening of the EOS due to two-flavor CSC (2SC) takes place and the maximum mass is reduced by about 0.2-solarmass. The CRover EOS is generalized to the supernova matter at finite temperature to describe the hot NSs at birth. The hadron-quark crossover is found to decrease the central temperature of the hot NSs under isentropic condition. The gravitational energy release and the spin-up rate during the contraction from the hot NS to the cold NS are also estimated.

## Key claim

The hadron-quark crossover picture, implemented via a phenomenological equation of state (EOS) called "CRover" that smoothly interpolates between hadronic and quark phases around three times nuclear matter density, can sustain neutron stars with gravitational mass above 2 solar masses and simultaneously resolve the hyperon puzzle by stiffening the EOS enough to suppress hyperon mixing in the core. The authors note that this result depends on the crossover occurring at a sufficiently low density (around 3ρ₀) and on the strength of the repulsive vector interaction in the quark phase.

## Method

The authors constructed the CRover EOS by interpolating between a hadronic EOS (TNI2u or TNI2, based on G-matrix calculations with three-body forces) and a quark EOS from the (2+1)-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, using a hyperbolic tangent weight function with crossover density \(\bar{\rho}\) and width \(\Gamma\). They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equation for spherically symmetric neutron stars in hydrostatic equilibrium, and extended the CRover EOS to finite temperature to model hot proto-neutron stars under isentropic conditions with trapped neutrinos.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass \(M_{\rm max} = 2.02 M_{\odot}\) for CRover EOS with TNI2u hadronic EOS, \(g_V=0\), no color superconductivity, and crossover window \((\bar{\rho},\Gamma) = (3\rho_0, \rho_0)\)
- Maximum mass \(M_{\rm max} = 2.59 M_{\odot}\) for the same setup but with \(g_V = 0.5 G_S\)
- Radii of cold neutron stars with CRover EOS (\(g_V = 0.5 G_S\)) lie in the narrow range \(R = (12.5 \pm 0.5)\) km for masses \(0.5 < M/M_{\odot} < 2.5\)
- Central density of a \(2 M_{\odot}\) neutron star with CRover EOS (\(g_V = 0.5 G_S\)) is \(\rho_c = 2.4 \rho_0\); for the maximum mass \(2.59 M_{\odot}\), \(\rho_c = 4.4 \rho_0\)
- Including two-flavor color superconductivity (2SC) reduces the maximum mass by about \(0.2 M_{\odot}\) (e.g., from 2.59 to 2.40 \(M_{\odot}\) for TNI2u with \(g_V = 0.5 G_S\))
- Threshold density for strangeness appearance in the quark EOS without CSC is \(\rho_{\rm th} \simeq 4 \rho_0\); with CSC it reduces to \(3.6 \rho_0\)
- For hadronic EOS TNI2u without crossover, the maximum mass with hyperons is only \(1.52 M_{\odot}\) (central density \(11.06 \rho_0\))

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Masuda et al., 2012, 2015) on hadron-quark crossover as a solution to the hyperon puzzle, which arises because conventional hadronic EOSs with hyperons (e.g., TNI2, AV18+TBF+ΛΣ, SCL3ΛΣ) produce maximum masses below \(2 M_{\odot}\) and cannot explain the observed massive neutron stars PSR J1614-2230 (\(1.97 \pm 0.04 M_{\odot}\)) and PSR J0348+0432 (\(2.01 \pm 0.04 M_{\odot}\)). The crossover approach contrasts with first-order hadron-quark transitions and with earlier attempts to include three-body hyperon forces (Nishizaki et al., 2001; Takatsuka et al., 2008). The results enable future comparisons with neutron star radius observations (Steiner, 2012; Ozel, 2015) and provide a framework for studying hot proto-neutron stars and their cooling evolution.
