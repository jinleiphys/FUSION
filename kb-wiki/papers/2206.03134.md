# Combined theoretical analysis of the parity-violating asymmetry for ${}^{48}$Ca and ${}^{208}Pb$

> The recent experimental determination of the parity violating asymmetry $A_{\rm pv}$ in ${}^{48}$Ca and ${}^{208}$Pb at Jefferson Lab is important for our understanding on how neutrons and protons arrange themselves inside the atomic nucleus. To better understand the impact of these measurements, we present a rigorous theoretical investigation of $A_{\rm pv}$ in ${}^{48}$Ca and ${}^{208}$Pb and assess the associated uncertainties. We complement our study by inspecting the static electric dipole polarizability in these nuclei. The analysis is carried out within nuclear energy density functional theory with quantified input. We conclude that the simultaneous accurate description of $A_{\rm pv}$ in ${}^{48}$Ca and ${}^{208}$Pb cannot be achieved by our models that accommodate a pool of global nuclear properties, such as masses and charge radii, throughout the nuclear chart, and describe -- within one standard deviation -- the experimental dipole polarizabilities $α_{\rm D}$ in these nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that current nuclear energy density functionals (EDFs) cannot simultaneously describe the measured parity-violating asymmetry \(A_{\rm pv}\) in both \({}^{48}\)Ca and \({}^{208}\)Pb, suggesting either a tension between the two experimental datasets (PREX-2 and CREX) or a fundamental limitation of the theoretical models, despite those same models successfully describing global nuclear properties like masses and charge radii across the nuclear chart.

## Method

The authors performed calculations of \(A_{\rm pv}\) and the static electric dipole polarizability \(\alpha_{\rm D}\) for \({}^{48}\)Ca and \({}^{208}\)Pb using three families of nuclear energy density functionals: a non-relativistic Skyrme type (SV), a generalized Skyrme type with rational density dependence (RD), and a point-coupling relativistic mean-field type (PC). All functionals were first optimized against a common set of ground-state data (masses, charge radii, surface thickness) for over 60 semi-magic spherical nuclei, without including \(A_{\rm pv}\) or \(\alpha_{\rm D}\) data. New parametrizations were then created by adding the experimental \(A_{\rm pv}\) and/or \(\alpha_{\rm D}\) data to the fitting protocol, while constraining nuclear matter properties (incompressibility, effective masses) to maintain agreement with giant resonance data in \({}^{208}\)Pb. The analysis used linear-regression-based uncertainty quantification and coefficient of determination (CoD) correlation matrices to assess relationships between model parameters and observables.

## Key numbers

* CREX experimental result for \({}^{48}\)Ca: \(A_{\rm pv} = 2668 \pm 106\) ppb at \(\langle Q^2\rangle = 0.0297 \pm 0.0002\) GeV\(^2\) and beam energy \(2182 \pm 0.5\) MeV.
* Neutron skin thickness \(\skin\) in \({}^{48}\)Ca predicted by PC-min: \(0.229 \pm 0.027\) fm; by SV-min: \(0.170 \pm 0.034\) fm; by SV-APV\(^1\alpha^2\): \(0.129 \pm 0.017\) fm.
* Symmetry energy slope \(L\) predicted by PC-min: \(82.5 \pm 17.2\) MeV; by SV-min: \(44.8 \pm 24.6\) MeV; by SV-APV\(^1\alpha^2\): \(15.5 \pm 11.0\) MeV.
* State-of-the-art EDFs describe charge radii within 0.015–0.02 fm average deviations and masses within 1–2 MeV across the nuclear chart.
* The CoD between \(\skin\) and \(A_{\rm pv}\) in \({}^{208}\)Pb is 99% for the models considered.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Reinhard (2021) on \(A_{\rm pv}\) in \({}^{208}\)Pb by adding the CREX measurement for \({}^{48}\)Ca and the dipole polarizability \(\alpha_{\rm D}\) for both nuclei. It directly addresses the tension noted by Piekarewicz (2021) between the large neutron skin from PREX-2 and measured \(\alpha_{\rm D}\) values, and contrasts with the extraction of \(\skin\) and symmetry energy parameters by Reed (2021) using a restricted set of relativistic functionals. The results call into question the reliability of using current \(A_{\rm pv}\) measurements to constrain neutron skins and the nuclear symmetry energy, and motivate future experiments such as the planned MREX at MESA (Thiel, 2019) as well as further development of EDFs beyond the forms used here, including those explored by Baldo (2008), Dobaczewski (2016), and others.
