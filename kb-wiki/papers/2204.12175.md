# Beyond the Standard Model with Lepton Flavor Universality Violation

> In recent years, exciting (indirect) hints for physics beyond the Standard Model (SM) have been accumulated. In particular, semi-leptonic $B$ decays show deviations from the SM predictions, which, due to the ratios $R(K^{(*)})$ and $R(D^{(*)})$ are obviously related to lepton flavour universality violation (LFUV). However, {we point out} there are more anomalies which admit an interpretation in terms of LFUV: The anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, the Cabibbo angle anomaly, the CMS measurements of non-resonant di-electrons, the difference of the forward-backward asymmetry in $B\to D^*\ellν$ and leptonic tau decays. In this letter we discuss the experimental and theoretical status of these anomalies, {compare their strength and weaknesses} and examine {and synthesize} how they can be explained in terms of possible extensions of the SM by new particles and interactions. Even though not all anomalies might be confirmed in the future, this unified view of the anomalies in terms of LFUV significantly strengthens their relevance, which is crucial in order to construct a convincing physics case for future colliders.

# Beyond the Standard Model with Lepton Flavor Universality Violation

## Key claim

The paper proposes that not only the well-known ratios R(K^(*)) and R(D^(*)) in semi-leptonic B decays indicate lepton flavor universality violation (LFUV), but that several other anomalies, including the muon anomalous magnetic moment, the Cabibbo angle anomaly, CMS non-resonant di-electron measurements, the forward-backward asymmetry difference in B→D*ℓν, and leptonic tau decays, can all be interpreted as manifestations of LFUV. The authors hedge that not all anomalies may be confirmed in the future, but argue that this unified view significantly strengthens the case for physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Method

The authors review the experimental and theoretical status of seven distinct anomalies (b→sℓ⁺ℓ⁻ transitions, b→cτν decays, a_μ, the Cabibbo angle anomaly, qq̅→e⁺e⁻ at CMS, ΔA_FB in B→D*ℓν, and τ→μνν decays), compare their statistical significance, experimental robustness, theoretical solidity, and viability of new physics explanations. They then examine how each anomaly can be explained by four classes of Standard Model extensions: leptoquarks, W' bosons, Z' bosons, and new scalars and fermions, including vector-like fermions.

## Key numbers

- Global fits to b→sℓ⁺ℓ⁻ data prefer new physics scenarios over the Standard Model hypothesis by more than 7σ, with destructive new physics at the 20% level relative to the Standard Model
- R(D^(*)) measurements show a 3σ tension with the Standard Model, with constructive new physics at the 10% level
- The combined result for the muon anomalous magnetic moment (a_μ) displays a 4.2σ tension with the Standard Model prediction
- First row CKM unitarity gives Σ_i|V_ui|² = 0.9985(5) and first column gives Σ_i|V_id|² = 0.9970(18), indicating a deficit
- CMS observed 44 electron events with invariant mass above 1.8 TeV versus 29.2±3.6 expected, a ≈4σ tension
- ΔA_FB extracted from BELLE B→D*ℓν data shows a ≈4σ tension
- τ→μνν decays show a ≈2σ preference for constructive new physics at the per-mille level

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of LFUV beyond the well-studied ratios R(K^(*)) and R(D^(*)) measured by LHCb, BaBar, and Belle, synthesizing anomalies that had previously been treated separately. The paper builds on global fit analyses by Descotes-Genon (2015), Capdevila (2017), and Alguero (2019) for b→sℓ⁺ℓ⁻, and on the HFLAV combination for b→cτν. It enables future model-building efforts by showing that common explanations via leptoquarks, Z' bosons, W' bosons, or new scalars and fermions are possible, and provides a unified framework for constructing a physics case for future colliders. The authors note that while no single new particle can explain all anomalies simultaneously, leptoquarks are a particularly promising solution.
