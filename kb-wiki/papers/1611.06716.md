# The extraction of parameters from final state interactions

> It is argued that final state enhancements in production reactions at large momentum transfers, such as pp -> K^+ Lambda p, are primarily sensitive to the position of a virtual bound state pole in the Lambda p system rather than the Lambda p scattering length and effective range. These arguments are supported by a study of the dispersion relation derived to describe such processes as a function of the cut-off energy. This shows that the position of the virtual bound state is independent of the cut-off energy.

## Key claim

Final state enhancements in high-momentum-transfer production reactions like \(pp\to K^+\Lambda p\) are primarily sensitive to the position of a virtual bound state pole in the \(\Lambda p\) system, rather than to the scattering length and effective range; the pole position is shown to be independent of the cut-off energy \(Q_{\rm max}\) in the dispersion integral, whereas the scattering length and effective range vary significantly with that cut-off.

## Method

The authors study the dispersion relation derived for the \(S\)-wave \(\Lambda p\) phase shift in the reaction \(pp\to K^+\Lambda p\), assuming a zero-range meson production operator so that the final-state interaction is expressed via the Jost function. They evaluate the dispersion integral numerically as a function of the cut-off energy \(Q_{\rm max}\) (the upper limit of integration over the missing mass), using as input the Jost function parameters from the COSY-HIRES collaboration (BUD2010) and also an analytic parameterization used by the COSY-TOF collaboration (HAU2016). From the extracted phase shifts they compute the scattering length and effective range via the effective range expansion, and then deduce the pole parameter \(\alpha\) (the imaginary momentum of the virtual state pole) by fitting the effective range expansion.

## Key numbers

* For the Jost function input with asymptotic parameters \(\alpha = -0.307\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) and \(\beta = 1.212\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) (from COSY-HIRES, BUD2010), the scattering length changes by about \(0.3\ \mathrm{fm}\) from its asymptotic value when \(Q_{\rm max}\) is reduced from infinity to \(40\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* At \(Q_{\rm max}=40\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the parameter \(\beta\) deviates from its asymptotic value by almost one third, but the change in \(\alpha\) is barely \(1\%\).
* For the analytic parameterization of Eq. (analytic) with \(C_1=348\ \mathrm{MeV}/c^2\) and \(C_2=2039.2\ \mathrm{MeV}/c^2\) (from COSY-TOF, HAU2016), the pole parameter changes from \(\alpha = -0.421\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) at \(Q_{\rm max}=100\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to \(\alpha = -0.417\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) at \(Q_{\rm max}=40\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The COSY-HIRES direct fit (BUD2010) gave \(\alpha = (-0.31 \pm 0.03)\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\), \(\beta = (1.21 \pm 0.14)\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\), scattering length \(a = -2.43 \pm 0.17\ \mathrm{fm}\), and effective range \(r = 2.21 \pm 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-relation approach of Gasparyan (2004, 2005) for extracting \(\Lambda p\) scattering parameters from final-state interactions in \(pp\to K^+\Lambda p\). While those earlier studies focused on determining the scattering length and effective range, the present paper shows that the pole position is far more stable against the choice of cut-off energy \(Q_{\rm max}\) and against variations in the production operator range. The result enables future analyses to extract the virtual-state pole position more reliably from experimental data, and it highlights that the difference between the COSY-HIRES (BUD2010) and COSY-TOF (HAU2014, HAU2016) results for \(\alpha\) (approximately \(-0.31\) vs. \(-0.42\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\)) likely reflects genuine experimental discrepancies rather than methodological artifacts. The conclusions are consistent with the review of low-energy \(\Lambda\)-nucleon interactions by Gal (2016), which reports nearby virtual-state poles in both spin-singlet and spin-triplet channels.
