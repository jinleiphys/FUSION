# Non-classical transport with angular-dependent path-length distributions. 2: Application to pebble bed reactor cores

> We describe an analysis of neutron transport in the interior of model pebble bed reactor (PBR) cores, considering both crystal and random pebble arrangements. Monte Carlo codes were developed for (i) generating random realizations of the model PBR core, and (ii) performing neutron transport inside the crystal and random heterogeneous cores; numerical results are presented for two different choices of material parameters. These numerical results are used to investigate the anisotropic behavior of neutrons in each case and to assess the accuracy of estimates for the diffusion coefficients obtained with the diffusion approximations of different models: the atomic mix model, the Behrens correction, the Lieberoth correction, the generalized linear Boltzmann equation (GLBE), and the new GLBE with angular-dependent path-length distributions. This new theory utilizes a non-classical form of the Boltzmann equation in which the locations of the scattering centers in the system are correlated and the distance-to-collision is not exponentially distributed; this leads to an anisotropic diffusion equation. We show that the results predicted using the new GLBE theory are extremely accurate, correctly identifying the anisotropic diffusion in each case and greatly outperforming the other models for the case of random systems.

## Key claim

The new generalized linear Boltzmann equation (GLBE) with angular-dependent path-length distributions predicts anisotropic diffusion coefficients in pebble bed reactor cores with extremely high accuracy, correctly identifying anisotropic effects in both crystal and random pebble arrangements and greatly outperforming the atomic mix model, Behrens correction, and Lieberoth correction for random systems.

## Method

Monte Carlo codes were developed to generate random realizations of model pebble bed reactor cores and to simulate neutron transport inside both crystal (face-centered cubic) and random heterogeneous cores. The mean free path, mean-squared free path, and mean-squared displacements in the x and z directions were computed numerically for two sets of material parameters, and these results were compared to diffusion coefficients predicted by the atomic mix model, the Behrens correction, the Lieberoth correction, the generalized linear Boltzmann equation (GLBE), and the new GLBE with angular-dependent path-length distributions.

## Key numbers

- For crystal structures with pebble diameter d and material parameter dΣt = 1.0 (Problem 1), the ratio ⟨z²⟩/⟨x²⟩ varied from approximately 0.998 at ε/d = 0.000 to 1.012 at ε/d = 0.125, indicating anisotropic diffusion
- For crystal structures with dΣt = 2.0 (Problem 2), the ratio ⟨z²⟩/⟨x²⟩ varied from approximately 0.999 at ε/d = 0.000 to 1.032 at ε/d = 0.125
- For random structures with dΣt = 1.0 (Problem 1), ensemble-averaged ⟨s⟩/d = 1.7053 with 0.095% statistical error, ⟨s²⟩/d = 6.2898 with 0.211% error, ⟨x²⟩/d = 209.56 with 0.201% error, and ⟨z²⟩/d = 210.01
- For random structures with dΣt = 2.0 (Problem 2), ensemble-averaged ⟨s⟩/d = 0.8527, ⟨s²⟩/d = 1.7016, ⟨x²⟩/d = 224.18, and ⟨z²⟩/d = 224.78
- The average packing fraction in the interior of 100 simulated random packings was ⟨Γ*⟩ = 0.5934 with standard deviation 0.0012
- Statistical error (with 95% confidence) was less than 0.037% for all crystal structure Monte Carlo results

## Context

This work extends the generalized linear Boltzmann equation theory developed in Vasques and Larsen (2013) to pebble bed reactor cores, addressing anisotropic neutron streaming effects that the atomic mix model (Larsen, 2005; Vasques, 2005) fails to capture when pebble sizes are on the order of one mean free path. The results enable more accurate neutronic modeling of generation IV pebble bed reactor designs such as the HTR-PM in China, the PBMR in South Africa, and the MPBR in the United States, and build upon earlier corrections by Behrens (1949) and Lieberoth and Stojadinovic (1980) for neutron streaming in heterogeneous systems.
