# Multipole decomposition of potentials in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> In relativistic heavy ion collisions an exact multipole decomposition of the Lorentz transformed time dependent Coulomb potentials in a coordinate system with equal constant, but opposite velocities of the ions, is obtained for both zero and different from zero impact parameter. The case of large values of $γ$ and the gauge transformation of the interaction removing both the $γ$ dependence and the $\ln b$ dependence are also considered.

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact multipole decomposition of the Lorentz-transformed, time-dependent Coulomb potentials for two colliding heavy ions in a center-of-mass coordinate system, for both zero and non-zero impact parameter, with the hedge that the asymptotic large-gamma forms are valid only when the impact parameter satisfies \(b \ll \gamma \hbar / m_e c\).

## Method

The author starts from the Liénard-Wiechert potentials for two ions moving on straight-line, classical paths with equal and opposite velocities. The time-dependent two-center Dirac equation is approximated by a one-center Dirac equation via multipole expansion of the two potentials. Four cases are treated: large gamma with non-zero impact parameter, exact multipole decomposition with non-zero impact parameter, exact multipole decomposition with zero impact parameter, and large gamma with zero impact parameter. A gauge transformation is also applied to remove the gamma dependence and the \(\ln b\) dependence from the interaction.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic large-gamma multipole expansion requires \(b \ll \gamma \hbar / m_e c\) (with \(\hbar/m_e c = 386\) fm) for validity.
- For the \(b=0\) case, sample calculations show that results at \(b=0\) and \(b=15\) fm differ by \(\le 1\%\).
- In the monopole approximation for large gamma and \(b=0\), the potential is \(V^{(0)} = (2eZ/r) \times \{ Q_0(t/r) \text{ for } r < t; \ln 2\gamma \text{ for } r > t \}\).
- In the dipole approximation for large gamma and \(b=0\), the potential includes terms with \(Q_1(t/r)\) for \(r < t\) and \((t/r)(\ln 2\gamma - 1)\) for \(r > t\).
- The gauge-transformed interaction for the \(m=0\) component yields \(M_l^0(r,t) = (\sqrt{\pi(2l+1)}/r) P_l(t/r) \times \{ 0 \text{ for } r < \sqrt{b^2+t^2}; -\ln[(r^2-t^2)/b^2] \text{ for } r > \sqrt{b^2+t^2} \}\).

## Context

This work extends the multipole decomposition methods developed by Baltz, Rhoades-Brown, and Weneser (1990s) for a single moving projectile potential to the two-center case relevant for relativistic heavy ion collisions. The derived forms enable coupled-channels calculations of electron-positron pair production and other electromagnetic processes, particularly by providing separable \(r\) and \(t\) dependence in matrix elements. The gauge transformation that removes both the \(\gamma\) dependence and the \(\ln b\) dependence builds directly on the prior work of Baltz, Rhoades-Brown, and Weneser, and facilitates calculations by eliminating the large positive and negative time contributions of the interaction.
