# Kaon Condensation in a Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) Model at High Density

> We demonstrate a fully self-consistent microscopic realization of a kaon-condensed colour-flavour locked state (CFLK0) within the context of a mean-field NJL model at high density. The properties of this state are shown to be consistent with the QCD low-energy effective theory once the proper gauge neutrality conditions are satisfied, and a simple matching procedure is used to compute the pion decay constant, which agrees with the perturbative QCD result. The NJL model is used to compare the energies of the CFLK0 state to the parity even CFL state, and to determine locations of the metal/insulator transition to a phase with gapless fermionic excitations in the presence of a non-zero hypercharge chemical potential and a non-zero strange quark mass. The transition points are compared with results derived previously via effective theories and with partially self-consistent NJL calculations. We find that the qualitative physics does not change, but that the transitions are slightly lower.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates a fully self-consistent microscopic realization of a kaon-condensed colour-flavour locked state (CFLK⁰) within a mean-field NJL model, showing that the qualitative physics of the phase transitions to gapless states does not change compared to previous effective-theory calculations, but the transition points are slightly lower.

## Method
The authors use an NJL model with a four-fermion contact interaction having the quantum numbers of single gluon exchange, imposing a hard momentum cutoff to mimic asymptotic freedom. They perform a variational mean-field calculation by introducing a quadratic Hamiltonian with all condensates and self-energy corrections required to close the gap equations, then numerically find stationary points of the self-consistent gap equations while imposing gauge charge neutrality conditions. The solutions are compared for two cases: finite hypercharge chemical potential and finite strange quark mass, for both the parity-even CFL phase and the kaon-condensed CFLK⁰ phase.

## Key numbers
- The critical hypercharge chemical potential for the CFL/gCFL transition is μ_Y^c = Δ₀ = 25 MeV, where Δ₀ is the physical gap in the absence of perturbations.
- The CFLK⁰/gCFLK⁰ transition occurs at a hypercharge chemical potential larger than the CFL/gCFL transition by a factor of 1.22.
- For finite strange quark mass, the CFL/gCFL transition occurs at M_s²/μ = 43.9 MeV, which is lower than the value of 46.8 MeV found in Alford (2003) and Alford (2004) and Fukushima (2004).
- The CFLK⁰/gCFLK⁰ transition for finite strange quark mass occurs at M_s²/μ = 52.5 MeV, a factor of 1.2 larger than the CFL/gCFL transition, compared to the factor of 4/3 found in Kryjevski (2004).

## Context
This work extends previous NJL-based calculations by Alford (2003), Alford (2004), Ruster (2004), and Fukushima (2004) that excluded kaon condensation, and effective-theory calculations by Kryjevski (2004) that did not consider the full condensate structure. The paper enables future work on finite temperature effects, analysis of the gapless CFLK⁰ phase, inclusion of instanton effects, up and down quark mass effects, and other forms of meson condensation.
