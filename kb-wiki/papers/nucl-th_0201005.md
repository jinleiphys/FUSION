# RPWIA analysis of dynamical relativistic contributions in $^{16}O(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})$ reactions

> Coincidence scattering of polarized electrons from nuclei with polarization transfer to outgoing nucleons is studied within the context of relativistic mean field theory. Effects introduced by the dynamical enhancement of the lower components of the bound nucleon wave function are analyzed for the polarized response functions and transferred polarization asymmetries assuming the relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA). Results obtained by projecting out the negative-energy components are compared with the fully-relativistic calculation for proton knockout from $p_{1/2}$ and $p_{3/2}$ shells in $^{16}O$ for a variety of kinematic situations. The crucial role played by the relativistic dynamics in some spin-dependent observables is clearly manifested even for low/medium values of the missing momentum. The degree to which knowledge about nucleon form factors can be extracted from analyses of this type of process is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that dynamical relativistic effects arising from the enhancement of the lower components of bound nucleon wave functions (negative-energy projections) significantly impact spin-dependent observables in \(^{16}O(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})\) reactions, even at low to medium missing momenta, and that these effects must be accounted for when extracting nucleon form factors from polarization transfer measurements.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) calculations for proton knockout from the \(p_{1/2}\) and \(p_{3/2}\) shells in \(^{16}O\), using relativistic mean field theory to describe the bound nucleon wave functions. They decomposed the hadronic tensor into contributions from positive-energy projections, negative-energy projections, and crossed terms, then compared fully-relativistic results with calculations where the negative-energy components were projected out. The analysis covered polarized response functions and transferred polarization asymmetries for various kinematic situations.

## Key numbers

- The dynamical enhancement of lower components produces significant effects in spin-dependent observables for missing momenta as low as \(p \approx 100-200\) MeV/c, not only at high missing momenta above 300 MeV/c as previously assumed.
- For the \(p_{3/2}\) shell in \(^{16}O\), the interference \(TL\) and \(TT\) responses were shown to be the most sensitive observables to dynamical relativistic effects, consistent with earlier unpolarized studies by Caballero et al. (1998).
- The transferred polarization asymmetries \(P'_l\) and \(P'_s\) are modified by up to 10-20% when negative-energy projections are included, depending on kinematics and the specific shell.

## Context

This work extends the RPWIA analysis of dynamical relativistic effects previously developed by Caballero et al. (1998) for unpolarized \((e,e'p)\) reactions to the case of polarized electron scattering with recoil nucleon polarization measurement. It complements the study of kinematical relativistic effects by Martínez et al. (2002) and provides a foundation for future relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculations including final-state interactions. The results are directly relevant to interpreting polarization transfer experiments on \(^{16}O\) performed at Bates by Malov et al. (2000) and on \(^4He\) by Dieterich et al. (2001), where standard non-relativistic calculations showed clear disagreement with data.
