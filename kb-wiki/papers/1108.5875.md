# Heavy quarkonium spectrum and width in a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma

> We report a recent calculation of the heavy quarkonium energy levels and decay widths in a quark- gluon plasma whose temperature is much smaller than the inverse radius of the bound state, based on a Non-Relativistic Effective Field theory framework for heavy quarkonium at finite temperature. Relevance for the phenomenology of the Y(1S) in heavy ion collisions is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports a calculation of heavy quarkonium energy levels and decay widths in a quark-gluon plasma under the specific condition that the temperature is much smaller than the inverse radius of the bound state, using a Non-Relativistic Effective Field Theory framework; the abstract glosses over the fact that the results are valid only for the specific hierarchy \(m \gg m\alpha_s \gg \pi T \gg m\alpha_s^2 \gg m_D\), which implies a temperature below the dissociation temperature.

## Method

The calculation proceeds by constructing a tower of Effective Field Theories (EFTs) that integrate out energy scales sequentially: first the heavy quark mass \(m\) to obtain NRQCD, then the scale \(m\alpha_s\) to obtain pNRQCD, and finally the temperature scale \(T\) to obtain pNRQCD\(_{\mathrm{HTL}}\), where light degrees of freedom are described by the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) effective Lagrangian. Within this EFT framework, the thermal contributions to the spectrum and width are computed up to order \(m\alpha_s^5\) by evaluating diagrams involving colour-singlet and colour-octet states, chromoelectric dipole vertices, and the gluon self-energy, with contributions from singlet-to-octet decay and Landau damping.

## Key numbers

- For the \(\Upsilon(1S)\) at LHC energies, the hierarchy is approximately: \(m_b \approx 5\;\text{GeV} > m_b\alpha_s \approx 1.5\;\text{GeV} > \pi T \approx 1\;\text{GeV} > m\alpha_s^2 \approx 0.5\;\text{GeV} \gtrsim m_D\).
- The leading thermal contribution to the spectrum is positive and quadratic in temperature, given by \(\frac{\pi}{9}N_c C_F \alpha_s^2 T^2 \frac{a_0}{2}\left[3n^2-l(l+1)\right] + \frac{\pi}{3}C_F^2 \alpha_s^2 T^2 a_0\).
- The leading thermal width is linear in temperature: \(\Gamma_{n,l}^{(\mathrm{thermal})} = \frac{1}{3}N_c^2C_F\alpha_s^3T + \frac{4}{3}\frac{C_F^2\alpha_s^3 T}{n^2}(C_F + N_c)\).
- The Bohr levels at leading order are \(E_n = -m C_F^2 \alpha_s^2 / (4n^2)\) and the Bohr radius is \(a_0 = 2/(m C_F \alpha_s)\).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative calculation of the static \(Q\overline{Q}\) potential in QCD by Laine (2006), which first revealed an imaginary part due to Landau damping, and builds on the EFT framework for heavy quarkonium at finite temperature developed by Brambilla (2008, 2010, 2011). The results enable quantitative predictions for the survival of the \(\Upsilon(1S)\) in heavy ion collisions at the LHC, consistent with CMS measurements (Chatrchyan, 2011) that show substantial survival of this state, and provide a systematic method to compute thermal corrections beyond the colour-screening paradigm.
