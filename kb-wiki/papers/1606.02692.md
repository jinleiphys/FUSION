# Rapidity Correlation Structure in Nuclear Collisions

> We show that measurements of the rapidity dependence of transverse momentum correlations can be used to determine the characteristic time $τ_π$ that dictates the rate of isotropization of the stress energy tensor, as well as the shear viscosity $ν= η/sT$. We formulate methods for computing these correlations using second order dissipative hydrodynamics with noise. Current data are consistent with $τ_π/ν\sim 10$, but targeted measurements can improve this precision.

## Key claim

Measurements of the rapidity dependence of transverse momentum correlations can determine both the shear viscosity $\nu = \eta/sT$ and, for the first time, the relaxation time $\tau_\pi$ that dictates the rate of isotropization of the stress energy tensor, but current data are only consistent with $\tau_\pi/\nu \sim 10$ and targeted measurements are needed to improve this precision.

## Method

The authors formulate methods for computing transverse momentum correlation functions using second order dissipative hydrodynamics with noise, specifically deriving a new partial differential equation for the correlation function $\Delta r_g = r_g - r_{g,\text{le}}$ that describes the deviation of momentum correlations from their local equilibrium value. They apply this formalism to Bjorken flow in nuclear collisions and compare the resulting rapidity correlation structure to STAR collaboration data, solving the second order viscous diffusion equation and contrasting it with first order diffusion theory.

## Key numbers

- Current data are consistent with $\tau_\pi/\nu \sim 10$, where $\tau_\pi$ is the relaxation time and $\nu = \eta/sT$ is the kinematic viscosity
- The effective diffusion coefficient for transverse momentum fluctuations is the kinematic viscosity $\nu = \eta/sT$, where $s$ is entropy density and $T$ is temperature
- The relaxation time for the variance $\Delta r_v$ in the Brownian motion analogy is $1/2\gamma$, half the value for relaxation of the mean $\langle v\rangle$

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier first-order treatment of viscous diffusion broadening of $p_t$ correlations (Gavin 2006) by incorporating second order hydrodynamics with noise, and refines a more schematic causal diffusion approach used to study net charge and baryon number diffusion (Aziz 2004). The method enables experimental extraction of the shear relaxation time $\tau_\pi$ from systematic changes in the shape of the rapidity dependence of $p_t$ correlations with centrality, complementing other pre-equilibrium probes such as dilepton, photon, and heavy quark production studied by groups including Broniowski (2008), Heller (2012), and Gelis (2013).
