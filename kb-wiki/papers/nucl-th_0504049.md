# Three Bosons in One Dimension with Short Range Interactions I: Zero Range Potentials

> We consider the three-boson problem with $δ$-function interactions in one spatial dimension. Three different approaches are used to calculate the phase shifts, which we interpret in the context of the effective range expansion, for the scattering of one free particle a off of a bound pair. We first follow a procedure outlined by McGuire in order to obtain an analytic expression for the desired S-matrix element. This result is then compared to a variational calculation in the adiabatic hyperspherical representation, and to a numerical solution to the momentum space Faddeev equations. We find excellent agreement with the exact phase shifts, and comment on some of the important features in the scattering and bound-state sectors. In particular, we find that the 1+2 scattering length is divergent, marking the presence of a zero-energy resonance which appears as a feature when the pair-wise interactions are short-range. Finally, we consider the introduction of a three-body interaction, and comment on the cutoff dependence of the coupling.

## Key claim

The authors find that for three identical bosons in one dimension interacting via zero-range delta-function potentials, the 1+2 scattering length is divergent regardless of the strength of the two-body interaction, marking a zero-energy resonance that appears as a universal feature when the pairwise interactions are of zero range.

## Method

The authors calculate the S-matrix element for scattering of one free boson off a bound pair using three independent approaches: an analytic ray-tracing method following McGuire, a variational calculation in the adiabatic hyperspherical representation with the eigenchannel R-matrix method, and a numerical solution to the momentum-space Faddeev equations. The phase shifts are interpreted within the effective range expansion, and a three-body contact interaction is introduced to study cutoff dependence of the coupling.

## Key numbers

- The two-body binding energy is given by \(B_2 = \frac{1}{ma_2^2}\), where \(a_2\) is the two-body scattering length.
- The exact 1+2 S-matrix element yields \(qa_2\tan{\delta} = 4\sqrt{\frac{2}{3}}\frac{(qa_2)^2}{(qa_2)^2-2}\), showing the inverse scattering length term is missing.
- The model supports one true three-body bound state at energy \(E = -4B_2\).
- A variational calculation with one adiabatic channel under-binds this state by about \(0.03B_2\).
- Without the diagonal coupling term \(Q_{00}\), a second bound state appears at \(-1.00213B_2\); including \(Q_{00}\) removes this state.
- The eigenchannel R-matrix method gives \(\lim_{qa_2 \rightarrow 0}{qa_2\tan{\delta}} \approx 0.065\) without diagonal coupling and \(\approx -0.008\) with it.
- For cutoff \(\Lambda a_2 = 5\), a three-body term \(V_3 = -0.0516\) brings \(K(0,0)\) to the exact result; for \(\Lambda a_2 = 2\), \(V_3 = -0.17595\); for \(\Lambda a_2 = 1\), \(V_3 = -1.907\).

## Context

This work revisits the classic one-dimensional three-boson problem with delta-function interactions, previously studied by McGuire and others, and extends it using modern effective field theory (EFT) and renormalization group methods. The authors explicitly compare their phase-shift results to those of Amaya-Tapia (2004), tracing discrepancies to differing definitions of the S-matrix element and the correct form of Levinson's theorem in one dimension. The paper enables future work on including finite-range effects (two-body effective range and shape parameter) and comparing EFT predictions to realistic phenomenological nucleon-nucleon interactions in a companion paper.
