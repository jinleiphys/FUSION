# Neutron emission during fission and its impact on fission-fragment mass distribution studied by Langevin model

> Actinide nuclei exhibit mass-asymmetric fission at low energy due to shell structure. The fission-fragment mass distributions produced at high energy tend to have a symmetric shape due to smearing of shell effects. On the other hand, the distribution can be changed by neutron emission occurring before fission, as this decreases the excitation energy of the fissioning nucleus, and thus revives the shell structure. In so called multichance fission, neutron emission is considered prior to fission at the initial nuclear shape, and competition between fission and neutron emission is determined with the framework of the statistical model. In the present work, we describe fission in the Langevin equations, and neutron emission is treated throughout the fission process. The calculation reproduces experimentally observed mass distributions, and for a wide range of initial compound-nucleus excitation energy up to 60 MeV. The results show that, while neutron emission dominates at the ground-state shape, it occurs along the shape evolution path to the scission point.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a Langevin model which allows neutron emission at any stage of the fission process, not just at the ground-state shape as in the conventional statistical model, can reproduce experimentally observed fission-fragment mass distributions for actinide nuclei with initial excitation energies up to 60 MeV. However, the agreement with experimental data is not as good as the previous GEF-based calculation of Tanaka (2019), particularly at high excitation energies where the present model shows larger peak-to-valley ratios than experiment.

## Method
The authors used three-dimensional Langevin equations with the two-center parametrization (deformation parameters z, delta, and alpha) to calculate the time evolution of nuclear shape from ground state to scission. Neutron emission was introduced at each time step using the neutron-decay width from the Weisskopf formula, with the probability determined by comparing the time step to the neutron lifetime. After each neutron emission, the potential energy was recalculated using updated shell corrections for the new fissioning nucleus (Z, N-1), and the excitation energy was reduced by the neutron separation energy plus the neutron kinetic energy.

## Key numbers
- Initial compound-nucleus excitation energy range: 15 to 55 MeV (10 MeV bins)
- Shell-damping energy E_d = 20 MeV, as proposed by Ignatyuk (1975)
- Average kinetic energy of emitted neutrons: 1.64 MeV (compared to 1.9 MeV in previous calculations by Tanaka (2019))
- For U-238 at E* = 45 MeV: first neutron emission occurs around 5 zeptoseconds, second neutron almost immediately after, third neutron at 55 zeptoseconds after overcoming the second saddle point
- Neutron multiplicity for U-238 at E* = 55 MeV: 4.176 neutrons
- Neutron multiplicity for Pu-244 at E* = 55 MeV: 4.476 neutrons
- Level-density parameter coefficient tilde_a_n varies by less than 4% up to the maximum deformation delta = 0.8

## Context
This work extends the Langevin model approach to fission by incorporating neutron emission throughout the shape evolution, rather than only at the ground-state shape as in the conventional statistical model framework used by Schmidt (2016) and Vandenbosch (1973). The results are compared to experimental FFMDs from multinucleon-transfer reactions at the JAEA tandem accelerator facility (Hirose 2017, Vermeulen 2020) and to the GEF code calculations of Tanaka (2019). The approach enables discussion of fission time scales through comparison with experimental pre-scission neutron data from Hinde (1992, 2021), and provides a method that does not require nuclide-dependent parameters such as fission barrier heights and level density parameters, making it applicable to unexplored regions of the nuclide chart.
