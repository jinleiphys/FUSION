# Influence of baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge fluctuations at the LHC

> At the Large Hadron Collider it is possible to generate BSQ (baryon, strangeness, and electric) charge density fluctuations from gluon splittings into quark/anti-quark pairs, generated within the ICCING model. In this work, we implement BSQ charge dynamics in a fully integrated framework. We propagate these conserved charges within an upgraded version of the v-USPhydro hydrodynamic model, which conserves the BSQ densities exactly. Our hydrodynamic simulation uses the full 4D equation of state $\left\{T,μ_B,μ_S,μ_Q\right\}$ from lattice Quantum Chromodynamics and includes decays from the Particle Data Group 2016+. We study the dynamical trajectories of fluid cells passing through the QCD phase diagram. We discuss future applications for this new framework.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that local fluctuations of baryon, strangeness, and electric charge densities, generated from gluon splittings into quark/anti-quark pairs, can be propagated through a full relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulation using a 4D lattice QCD equation of state; these fluctuations remain significant even at freeze-out, despite net BSQ charges being zero on average at LHC energies.

## Method

The authors use the TRENTo+ICCING+CCAKE framework: TRENTo generates initial entropy density profiles, ICCING samples gluons and splits them into up, down, and strange quark/anti-quark pairs to produce initial BSQ density fluctuations, and CCAKE (an upgraded version of v-USPhydro) solves the Israel-Stewart equations with three conserved charges using Smooth Particle Hydrodynamics. The simulation employs the full 4D lattice QCD equation of state in {T, μ_B, μ_S, μ_Q} space, with three back-up equations of state for cases where the lattice QCD table becomes unusable (affecting less than 5% of SPH particles in central collisions). Freeze-out is implemented along a hypersurface of constant energy density ε_FO = 266 MeV/fm^3, corresponding to T_FO = 150 MeV at vanishing densities, using the PDG2016+ particle list.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 0.047 (constant), with vanishing bulk viscosity ζ/s = 0
- Freeze-out energy density: ε_FO = 266 MeV/fm^3, corresponding to freeze-out temperature T_FO = 150 MeV at vanishing densities
- Lattice QCD equation of state Taylor series expansion breaks down for μ_X/T ≳ 2.5 (X = B, S, Q)
- Back-up equations of state are needed for less than 5% of SPH particles in central collisions
- Initial temperature cutoff for trajectory analysis: T_0 > 350 MeV (and T_0 > 300 MeV in the figure)

## Context

This work extends the TRENTo+ICCING framework (Carzon 2019, Carzon 2023) for initial conditions with BSQ charge fluctuations by integrating it with the v-USPhydro hydrodynamic code (Noronha-Hostler 2013, 2014), which previously did not include conserved charge densities. The new CCAKE code enables the first full hydrodynamic simulation using the 4D lattice QCD equation of state (Noronha-Hostler 2019) with BSQ conservation. This framework enables future studies of BSQ diffusion (Greif 2017), chemical potential-dependent transport coefficients (McLaughlin 2021), and equation of state studies at large densities.
