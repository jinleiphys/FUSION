# Diffractive Deep Inelastic Scattering in the Dipole Picture at Next-to-Leading Order

> We calculate the contribution from the $q \bar q g$ state production to the diffractive cross sections in deep inelastic scattering at high energy. The obtained cross section is finite by itself, and consists a part of the full next-to-leading order result for the diffractive structure functions. Our calculation for the diffractive structure functions is performed using exact kinematics, under the shockwave approximation of the scattering process. Once the calculation is completed, we show that the previously known behaviour at the high-$Q^2$ and large-$M_X^2$ regime can be extracted from our results by taking the appropriate limits. Furthermore, we discuss the steps required to obtain the complete next-to-leading order results for the structure functions in the color glass condensate (CGC) formalism, and the application of these results to phenomenology.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first complete calculation of the $q\bar{q}g$ contribution to diffractive deep inelastic scattering structure functions at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the dipole picture and color glass condensate (CGC) formalism, using exact kinematics under the shockwave approximation; however, this result constitutes only a part of the full NLO result, as loop corrections and final state radiation contributions are left for future work.

## Method

The authors calculated the diffractive cross section for production of a $q\bar{q}g$ Fock state by combining the NLO light-cone wavefunction for $\gamma^* \to q\bar{q}g$ (previously derived in Beuf et al. (2016, 2017)) with the scattering amplitude of the three-parton state off the target color field, imposing a color-singlet condition on the final state. The calculation was performed using exact kinematics in the shockwave approximation, with the momentum transfer $t$ and invariant mass $M_X$ dependencies factorizing into Bessel function factors. The authors then verified that their results reduce to previously known limiting cases: the large-$M_X$ limit and the large-$Q^2$ limit.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal structure function result is given by equation (1) in the paper, with prefactor $4 \frac{\alpha_s N_c C_F Q^4}{\beta} \sum_f e_f^2$ and integrals over momentum fractions $z_0, z_1, z_2$ satisfying $\delta(z_0 + z_1 + z_2 - 1)$.
- The transverse structure function result is given by equation (2) in the paper, with prefactor $2 \frac{\alpha_s N_c C_F Q^4}{\beta} \sum_f e_f^2$ and the same delta function constraint on momentum fractions.
- The $M_X$ dependence for the three-parton final state factorizes as ${\cal I}_{M_X}^{(3)} = 2 \frac{z_0 z_1 z_2}{(4\pi)^2} \frac{M_X}{Y_{012}} J_1(M_X Y_{012})$, where $Y_{012}^2$ is a combination of transverse coordinate differences weighted by momentum fractions.
- The $t$ dependence factorizes as ${\cal I}_{\Delta_T}^{(3)} = \frac{1}{4\pi} J_0\left( \sqrt{|t|} \| z_0 \mathbf{x}_{\bar{0}0} + z_1 \mathbf{x}_{\bar{1}1} + z_2 \mathbf{x}_{\bar{2}2} \| \right)$.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order dipole picture result for diffractive DIS (Nikolaev, 1991) by including the $q\bar{q}g$ contribution at NLO accuracy, which is necessary to describe the diffractive structure function $F_2^D$ at low $\beta$ where the quark-antiquark contribution vanishes (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1999). Previous calculations of this contribution relied on simplifying assumptions such as the large-$M_X$ limit (Munier, 2003) or the large-$Q^2$ limit (Wusthoff, 1997; Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1999). The results enable future numerical implementation for phenomenology at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and provide a foundation for completing the full NLO calculation by including the remaining loop and final state radiation contributions (Penttala, 2023).
