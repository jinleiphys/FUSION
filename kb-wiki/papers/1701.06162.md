# Collective excitations of hot QCD medium in a quasi-particle description

> Collective excitations of a hot QCD medium are the main focus of the present article. The analysis is performed within semi-classical transport theory with isotropic and anisotropic momentum distribution functions for the gluonic and quark-antiquark degrees of freedom that constitutes the hot QCD plasma. The isotropic/equilibrium momentum distributions for gluons and quarks are based on a recent quasi-particle description of hot QCD equations of state. The anisotropic distributions are just the extensions of isotropic ones by stretching or squeezing them in one of the directions. The hot QCD medium effects in the model adopted here enter through the effective gluon and quark fugacities along with non-trivial dispersion relations leading to an effective QCD coupling constant. Interestingly, with these distribution functions the tensorial structure of the gluon polarization tensor in the medium turned out to be similar to the one for the non-interacting ultra-relativistic system of quarks/antiquarks and gluons . The interactions mainly modify the Debye mass parameter and , in turn, the effective coupling in the medium. These modifications have been seen to modify the collective modes of the hot QCD plasma in a significant way.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the collective modes of a hot QCD medium are significantly modified when realistic, interacting equations of state (EOS) are used via a quasi-particle description, compared to the standard non-interacting ultra-relativistic gas approximation. This modification is conditional on the use of effective gluon and quark fugacities and non-trivial dispersion relations, which primarily alter the Debye mass parameter and effective coupling.

## Method

The analysis uses semi-classical transport theory (Boltzmann-Vlasov equation) within a linear response approximation to compute the gluon polarization tensor for both isotropic and anisotropic QCD plasmas. The isotropic equilibrium momentum distributions for gluons and quarks are taken from the Effective Quasi-Particle Model (EQPM), which maps hot QCD equations of state (EOS1, EOS2, and LEOS) into effective fugacities. Anisotropic distributions are generated by stretching or squeezing the isotropic ones along a single direction, parameterized by an anisotropy parameter ξ, following the approach of Romatschke and Strickland (2003) and Carrington, Deja, and Mrowczynski (2014).

## Key numbers

- The Debye mass squared for the interacting medium is given by \( {m_D^2}^{EOS(i)} = 4 \pi \alpha_s(T) T^2 \left( \frac{2 N_c}{\pi^2} \text{PolyLog}[2, z_g] - \frac{2 N_f}{\pi^2} \text{PolyLog}[2, -z_q] \right) \), where \( z_g \) and \( z_q \) are effective fugacities from the EQPM.
- In the non-interacting (ideal) limit (\( z_{g,q} = 1 \)), the Debye mass reduces to \( {m_D^2}^{LO} = \alpha_s(T) T^2 (N_c/3 + N_f/6) \).
- The normalization constant for the anisotropic distribution in the small ξ limit is \( C_\xi = 1 + \xi/3 + O(\xi^2) \).
- The analysis is performed at a temperature \( T = 0.25 \) GeV with a critical temperature \( T_c = 0.17 \) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Romatschke and Strickland (2003) and Carrington, Deja, and Mrowczynski (2014) by incorporating interacting QCD equations of state via the EQPM, rather than treating the QGP as a free ultra-relativistic gas. It builds on prior quasi-particle models for transport properties (e.g., shear and bulk viscosities by Bluhm, Chandra, and others) and enables more realistic studies of collective excitations, which are crucial for understanding quarkonia suppression and other signatures in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
