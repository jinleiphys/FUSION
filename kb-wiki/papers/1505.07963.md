# First measurement of target and beam-target asymmetries in the $γp\to π^0ηp$ reaction

> The first data on target and beam-target asymmetries for the $γp\toπ^0ηp$ reaction at photon energies from 1050 up to 1450 MeV are presented. The measurements were performed using the Crystal Ball and TAPS detector setup at the Glasgow tagged photon facility of the Mainz Microtron MAMI. The general assumption that the reaction is dominated by the $Δ3/2^-$ amplitude is confirmed. The data are in particular sensitive to small contributions from other partial waves.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the $\gamma p\to\pi^0\eta p$ reaction is dominated by the $\Delta 3/2^-$ amplitude, but the new polarization data are particularly sensitive to small contributions from other partial waves, which the abstract does not quantify.

## Method

The experiment used the Crystal Ball and TAPS detector setup at the Glasgow tagged photon facility of the Mainz Microtron MAMI with a transversely polarized frozen-spin butanol target. The authors measured four polarization observables ($P_x$, $P_y$, $P_x^\odot$, $P_y^\odot$) for the $\gamma p\to\pi^0\eta p$ reaction by extracting count rate asymmetries from events with different orientations of target spin and beam helicity, with background from carbon and oxygen nuclei subtracted using separate hydrogen and carbon target measurements.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range: 1050 to 1450 MeV, with an average resolution of 4 MeV
- Degree of circular photon polarization varied from 67% at 1050 MeV to 79% at 1450 MeV
- Average degree of target polarization during the beam periods was 70%
- Total systematic uncertainty of less than 8% (proton polarization uncertainty 4%, beam polarization uncertainty 2%, acceptance and background subtraction uncertainties 3%-6%)
- Average detection efficiency for the $\gamma p\to\pi\eta p$ reaction after all analysis steps is about 50% for beam energies from 1.05 to 1.45 GeV
- The beam-target asymmetry $P_x^\odot(\cos\Theta_\pi)$ shows a maximum amplitude of about 0.8, described by the approximation $P_x^\odot\approx -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\sin 2\Theta_\pi$

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of polarization observables for $\gamma p\to\pi^0\eta p$ reported by Aphi, Ajaka, Gutz1, Gutz2, and DorMeiss, and provides the first target and beam-target asymmetry data for this reaction. The results are compared with the isobar model of FKLO and the Bonn-Gatchina multichannel fit of Anis12 (solution BG2011-02), both of which describe the new data equally well. The data enable future detailed partial-wave analyses to disentangle small contributions from partial waves other than the dominant $\Delta 3/2^-$ amplitude.
