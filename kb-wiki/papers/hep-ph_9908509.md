# A Quark Model Calculation of gamma gamma to pi pi Including Final State Interactions

> A quark model calculation of the processes gamma gamma -> pi+ pi- and gamma gamma -> pi0 pi0 is performed. At tree level, only charged pions couple to the initial state photons and neutral pions are not expected in the final state. However, a small but significant gamma gamma -> pi0 pi0 cross section is observed. We demonstrate that this may be accounted for by a rotation in isospin space induced by final state interactions. The resulting pi+ pi- cross section is in good agreement with experiment while the pi0 pi0 cross section is in qualitative agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The observed cross section for $\gamma\gamma \to \pi^0\pi^0$, which cannot arise from tree-level scalar QED because neutral pions do not couple directly to low-energy photons, can be explained by a rotation in isospin space induced by final state interactions (FSI) between the outgoing pions, provided the difference between the I=0 and I=2 $\pi\pi$ potentials is taken into account.

## Method

The authors performed a quark model calculation of $\gamma\gamma \to \pi\pi$ using scalar QED at tree level for the basic electromagnetic interaction, then incorporated final state interactions via the distorted wave Born approximation. They solved a nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation with effective $\pi\pi$ potentials derived from the quark model (including one-gluon exchange and confinement) to obtain the distorted wavefunctions and phase shifts for the I=0 and I=2 channels. The FSI-corrected partial wave amplitudes were obtained by convolving the scalar QED amplitudes with these wavefunctions, and the resulting cross sections for $\pi^+\pi^-$ and $\pi^0\pi^0$ were computed by projecting onto the physical pion states using isospin Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The scalar QED cross section for $\gamma\gamma \to \pi^+\pi^-$ is given by $\sigma = \frac{\pi\alpha^2}{4m^2}\left[2x(1+x)\sqrt{1-x} - x^2(2-x)\ln\left(\frac{1+\sqrt{1-x}}{1-\sqrt{1-x}}\right)\right]$, where $x \equiv 4m^2/s$ and $m$ is the pion mass.
- The $\pi\pi$ potentials are parametrized as $V_{\pi\pi}(r)=V_0\:e^{ -\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{r}{r_0}\right)^2}$; for the I=0, L=0 channel the s-channel gluon exchange contribution has $V_0 = -1.75$ GeV and $r_0 = 1.48$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- The predicted cross section for $\gamma\gamma \to \pi^0\pi^0$ from $f_0(980)$ resonance contributions at threshold is 0.015 nb, from $f_0(1275)$ is 0.00005 nb, and from $f_2(1275)$ is 0.15 nb.
- The linear fit to the I=0 S-wave $\pi\pi$ phase shift is $\delta^{I=0}_{L=0}=0.0027$ rad/MeV $\times (\sqrt{s}-250$ MeV), and for I=2 it is $\delta^{I=2}_{L=0}=-0.00062$ rad/MeV $\times (\sqrt{s}-250$ MeV).
- The Born result for $\gamma\gamma \to \pi^+\pi^-$ agrees well with the dispersion analysis of Morgan and Pennington (1991) for $\sqrt{s} < 350$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier quark model studies of $\gamma\gamma \to \pi\pi$ by Barnes, Dooley, and Isgur (1987), which used the Fermi approximation for FSI and did not account for differences between I=0 and I=2 potentials, leading to failure for neutral pion production. The present calculation demonstrates that including the full isospin dependence of $\pi\pi$ interactions can qualitatively reproduce the $\pi^0\pi^0$ data, unlike the earlier approach. The results enable future application of the quark model to more complex processes such as $\gamma\gamma$ production of vector-vector states (e.g., $\rho\rho$), where chiral perturbation theory and dispersion methods are inapplicable.
