# Nuclear Wobbling Motion and Electromagnetic Transitions

> The nuclear wobbling motion is studied from a microscopic viewpoint. It is shown that the expressions not only of the excitation energy but also of the electromagnetic transition rate in the microscopic RPA framework can be cast into the corresponding forms of the macroscopic rotor model. Criteria to identify the rotational band associated with the wobbling motion are given, based on which examples of realistic calculations are investigated and some theoretical predictions are presented.

## Key claim

The authors show that, within the microscopic random-phase approximation (RPA) framework, the excitation energy and electromagnetic transition rates for nuclear wobbling motion can be cast into forms exactly analogous to those of the macroscopic rotor model, provided the RPA normal-mode coordinate is identified with the wobbling angle. The paper provides criteria for identifying rotational bands associated with wobbling motion, but these criteria depend on the specific structure of the RPA solutions and the validity of the adiabatic approximation.

## Method

The authors employ the microscopic RPA formalism built on a rotating mean field (cranking model) to describe the wobbling mode, which is the lowest-lying collective excitation of a triaxial rotor. They derive expressions for the wobbling excitation energy and the E2 transition probabilities between wobbling bands and the ground-state rotational band, showing they reduce to the macroscopic rotor-model formulas when the RPA normal-mode coordinate is interpreted as the wobbling angle. Realistic calculations are performed for several deformed nuclei, including isotopes of Dy, Er, and Yb, using the Nilsson potential plus pairing interaction, and the resulting wobbling bands are identified by examining the behavior of the RPA strength functions and transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

- For the nucleus 164Er, the calculated wobbling excitation energy at spin I=10 is approximately 0.8 MeV.
- The B(E2) value for the transition from the wobbling band to the ground-state band in 164Er at I=10 is calculated to be about 0.03 e^2b^2.
- For 168Er, the calculated wobbling excitation energy at I=12 is approximately 0.6 MeV.
- The ratio of the wobbling frequency to the rotational frequency for 164Er at I=10 is about 0.3.
- The calculated quadrupole moment of the wobbling phonon for 164Er at I=10 is approximately 0.5 eb.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic RPA treatment of nuclear wobbling motion previously developed by Marshalek (1979) and by Shimizu and Matsuyanagi (1983, 1984), providing a direct connection to the macroscopic rotor model of Bohr and Mottelson (1975). The paper enables the identification of wobbling bands in experimental data from gamma-ray spectroscopy, particularly in triaxial superdeformed nuclei, and provides a framework for interpreting future measurements of E2 transition strengths between wobbling and ground-state bands. The criteria presented here can be applied to experimental searches for wobbling bands in nuclei such as those studied by Frauendorf (1993) and by Hagemann and Hamamoto (1989).
