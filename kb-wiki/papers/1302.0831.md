# Quantum mechanical model for J/psi suppression in the LHC era

> We discuss the interplay of screening, absorption and regeneration effects, on the quantum mechanical evolution of quarkonia states, within a time-dependent harmonic oscillator (THO) model with complex oscillator strength. We compare the results with data for R_AA/R_AA(CNM) from CERN and RHIC experiments. In the absence of a measurement of cold nuclear matter (CNM) effects at LHC we estimate their role and interpret the recent data from the ALICE experiment. We also discuss the temperature dependence of the real and imaginary parts of the oscillator frequency which stand for screening and absorption/regeneration, respectively. We point out that a structure in the J/psi suppression pattern for In-In collisions at SPS is possibly related to the recently found X(3872) state in the charmonium spectrum. Theoretical support for this hypothesis comes from the cluster expansion of the plasma Hamiltonian for heavy quarkonia in a strongly correlated medium.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "wiggle" (dip) observed in the J/ψ suppression pattern for In-In collisions at SPS, near charged particle densities dN_ch/dη ~ 150–250, may indicate the true threshold for anomalous suppression due to coupling of charmonium to the D̄*0D0 channel involving the X(3872) state, rather than the traditional interpretation of a threshold at dN_ch/dη ~ 300.

## Method

The authors employ a time-dependent harmonic oscillator (THO) model with a complex squared oscillator frequency ω²(T) to describe the quantum mechanical evolution of quarkonia states in a cooling quark-gluon plasma. The temperature evolution of the medium follows Bjorken scaling with entropy conservation, using a lattice QCD-based fit for the entropy density. The survival probability (suppression factor) is computed from the evolution amplitude for a Gaussian initial Q̄Q state, and the real and imaginary parts of ω²(T) are parameterized to simultaneously account for screening, absorption, and regeneration effects.

## Key numbers

- The freeze-out temperature for the effective THO potential is T_f = 0.193 GeV, corresponding to T_f ≈ 1.25 T_c with T_c = 0.154 GeV (Borsanyi 2010).
- The initial time for quarkonium evolution is τ₀ = 1.0 fm/c.
- The transverse overlap area is parameterized as A_T = (4.8 fm²) × [(dN_ch/dη)_(η=0)]^0.57.
- The wiggle in In-In data is located in the temperature region 1.2 ≤ T/T_c < 1.5.
- The two LHC predictions diverge above dN_ch/dη ~ 700 depending on whether the wiggle is fitted or not.
- The entropy density fit is s(T) = 9.0 T³ × [1 + tanh((T − 0.189)/(0.534 T))].

## Context

This work extends the generalized Matsui approach (Matsui 1989) for J/ψ suppression by incorporating a complex potential within a THO model, building on earlier applications by Blaschke (2011) and Carlos (2011, 2012). It contrasts with statistical hadronization models (Braun-Munzinger 2000, Andronic 2010) and kinetic approaches (Grandchamp 2001, Thews 2000) by using a quantum mechanical evolution framework. The paper enables future use of realistic temperature-dependent complex potentials from lattice QCD and suggests measuring the heavy quark association degree n_Q̄Q / (n_Q̄q n_q̄Q) as a new diagnostic for QGP, analogous to plasma diagnostics in stellar atmospheres and semiconductor plasmas.
