# On the Equivalence of Three-Particle Scattering Formalisms

> In recent years, different on-shell $\mathbf{3}\to\mathbf{3}$ scattering formalisms have been proposed to be applied to both lattice QCD and infinite volume scattering processes. We prove that the formulation in the infinite volume presented by Hansen and Sharpe in Phys.~Rev.~D92, 114509 (2015) and subsequently Briceño, Hansen, and Sharpe in Phys.~Rev.~D95, 074510 (2017) can be recovered from the $B$-matrix representation, derived on the basis of $S$-matrix unitarity, presented by Mai {\em et al.} in Eur.~Phys.~J.~A53, 177 (2017) and Jackura {\em et al.} in Eur.~Phys.~J.~C79, 56 (2019). Therefore, both formalisms in the infinite volume are equivalent and the physical content is identical. Additionally, the Faddeev equations are recovered in the non-relativistic limit of both representations.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the infinite-volume three-to-three scattering amplitude derived from the Hansen-Sharpe (2015) and Briceno-Hansen-Sharpe (2017) formalism is equivalent to the B-matrix representation derived from S-matrix unitarity by Mai et al. (2017) and Jackura et al. (2019), establishing that both formalisms contain identical physical content despite superficial differences in the organization of short-range rescattering effects and symmetrization.

## Method

The authors compare two on-shell representations for the connected three-to-three scattering amplitude in the partial-wave basis for identical spinless particles: the B-matrix representation, which satisfies elastic unitarity via an integral equation involving a real R-matrix kernel, and the HS-BHS representation, which expresses the amplitude in terms of a divergence-free K-matrix and ladder series. They derive an integral equation relating the R-matrix of the B-matrix formalism to the K-matrix of the HS-BHS formalism, then demonstrate that this relation preserves the reality of both quantities, thereby proving the two representations yield identical connected amplitudes. The Faddeev equations are recovered in the non-relativistic limit of both representations.

## Key numbers

- The three-particle invariant mass squared s lies in the range (3m)^2 ≤ s < s_inel, where s_inel is the first inelastic threshold
- The pair invariant mass squared σ_p has physical region (2m)^2 ≤ σ_p ≤ (√s - m)^2
- The spectator momentum magnitude in the CMF is p = (1/(2√s)) λ^(1/2)(s, m^2, σ_p), where λ is the Källén triangle function
- The relative momentum of the pair in the helicity frame is q_p^* = (1/(2√σ_p)) λ^(1/2)(σ_p, m^2, m^2) = (1/2)√(σ_p - 4m^2)
- The two-body phase space factor ρ_p has diagonal elements (1/2!)(1/(16π))√(1 - 4m^2/σ_p)

## Context

This work extends the on-shell three-particle scattering formalisms developed by Hansen and Sharpe (2015) and Briceno, Hansen, and Sharpe (2017), which were derived from relativistic effective field theory in finite volume, and the B-matrix representation of Mai et al. (2017) and Jackura et al. (2019), which was constructed from S-matrix unitarity. The equivalence proof enables future lattice QCD analyses and phenomenological studies to use either formalism interchangeably, and the recovery of Faddeev equations in the non-relativistic limit connects these relativistic approaches to the established non-relativistic three-body scattering framework.
