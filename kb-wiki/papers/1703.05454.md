# High-quality two-nucleon potentials up to fifth order of the chiral expansion

> We present NN potentials through five orders of chiral effective field theory ranging from leading order (LO) to next-to-next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N4LO). The construction may be perceived as consistent in the sense that the same power counting scheme as well as the same cutoff procedures are applied in all orders. Moreover, the long-range parts of these potentials are fixed by the very accurate pi-N LECs as determined in the Roy-Steiner equations analysis by Hoferichter, Ruiz de Elvira and coworkers. In fact, the uncertainties of these LECs are so small that a variation within the errors leads to effects that are essentially negligible, reducing the error budget of predictions considerably. The NN potentials are fit to the world NN data below pion-production threshold of the year of 2016. The potential of the highest order (N4LO) reproduces the world NN data with the outstanding chi^2/datum of 1.15, which is the highest precision ever accomplished for any chiral NN potential to date. The NN potentials presented may serve as a solid basis for systematic ab initio calculations of nuclear structure and reactions that allow for a comprehensive error analysis. In particular, the consistent order by order development of the potentials will make possible a reliable determination of the truncation error at each order. Our family of potentials is non-local and, generally, of soft character. This feature is reflected in the fact that the predictions for the triton binding energy (from two-body forces only) converges to about 8.1 MeV at the highest orders. This leaves room for three-nucleon-force contributions of moderate size.

## Key claim

The authors present nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials through five orders of chiral effective field theory (LO to N4LO) that achieve the highest precision ever for a chiral NN potential, with the N4LO potential reproducing world NN data below pion-production threshold with a chi-squared per datum of 1.15; however, the construction is described as "may be perceived as consistent" because the same power counting scheme and cutoff procedures are applied across all orders, and the long-range parts rely on the very accurate pion-nucleon low-energy constants from the Roy-Steiner analysis by Hoferichter, Ruiz de Elvira and coworkers (2015), whose uncertainties are so small that variation within errors produces essentially negligible effects.

## Method

The authors construct NN potentials order by order from LO to N4LO using chiral effective field theory with Weinberg power counting, including one-pion exchange, two-pion exchange, and three-pion exchange contributions with spectral-function regularization at cutoff scales of 650 MeV (for orders up to N3LO) and 700 MeV (for N4LO). The short-range part is described by contact interactions up to sixth order (including F-wave contacts at N4LO), and the potentials are regularized with a nonlocal exponential regulator function and then iterated in the Blankenbeclar-Sugar equation (reduced to a Lippmann-Schwinger equation) to fit the world NN scattering data below pion-production threshold from the year 2016.

## Key numbers

- N4LO potential achieves chi-squared per datum of 1.15 when reproducing world NN data below pion-production threshold, the highest precision ever for any chiral NN potential to date.
- Triton binding energy from two-body forces only converges to about 8.1 MeV at the highest orders, leaving room for three-nucleon-force contributions of moderate size.
- Spectral-function regularization cutoff is set to 650 MeV for all orders except N4LO where 700 MeV is used.
- Pion-nucleon LECs used include c1 = -1.10(3) GeV^-1, c3 = -5.54(6) GeV^-1, and c4 = 4.17(4) GeV^-1 at N4LO, as determined from the Roy-Steiner analysis by Hoferichter et al. (2015).

## Context

This work extends the chiral NN potential program of Entem, Machleidt, and collaborators (2003, 2005) from N3LO to N4LO, and builds upon the Roy-Steiner equation analysis of pion-nucleon scattering by Hoferichter, Ruiz de Elvira and coworkers (2015) to fix the long-range parts with unprecedented precision. The consistent order-by-order family of potentials from LO to N4LO enables systematic ab initio calculations of nuclear structure and reactions with reliable truncation error estimates, and provides the necessary two-nucleon force component for studies of three-nucleon forces at N4LO that may address persistent problems such as the Ay puzzle in nucleon-deuteron scattering and the radius problem in nuclear structure.
