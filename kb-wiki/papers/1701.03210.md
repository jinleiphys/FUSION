# Search for a hidden strange baryon-meson bound state from $ϕ$ production in a nuclear medium

> We investigate the hidden strange light baryon-meson system. With the resonating-group method, two bound states, $η'-N$ and $ϕ-N$, are found in the quark delocalization color screening model. Focusing on the $ϕ-N$ bound state around 1950\,MeV, we obtain the total decay width of about 4\,MeV by calculating the phase shifts in the resonance scattering processes. To study the feasibility of an experimental search for the $ϕ-N$ bound state, we perform a Monte Carlo simulation of the bound state production with an electron beam and a gold target. In the simulation, we use the CLAS12 detector with the Forward Tagger and the BONUS12 detector in Hall B at Jefferson Lab. Both the signal and the background channels are estimated. We demonstrate that the signal events can be separated from the background with some momentum cuts. Therefore it is feasible to experimentally search for the $ϕ-N$ bound state through the near threshold $ϕ$ meson production from heavy nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a $\phi-N$ bound state (a hidden strange pentaquark candidate) exists with a mass around 1950 MeV and a narrow total decay width of about 4 MeV, but this result depends on the channel-coupling effect in the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM), without such coupling, the $N\phi$ channel alone is unbound.

## Method
The authors used the resonating-group method (RGM) within the QDCSM to solve for bound states in the hidden strange baryon-meson system, considering multiple coupled channels with quantum numbers $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^\pm$, $\frac{3}{2}^\pm$, and $\frac{5}{2}^\pm$. They calculated decay widths from phase shifts in resonance scattering processes, then performed a Monte Carlo simulation of electroproduction on a gold target using the CLAS12 detector with the Forward Tagger and BONUS12 detector at Jefferson Lab, estimating both signal and background channels.

## Key numbers
- The $\phi-N$ bound state mass varies from 1948.9 MeV to 1957.4 MeV across three parameter sets (QDCSM1, QDCSM2, QDCSM3).
- The total decay width of the $\phi-N$ bound state is about 4 MeV, with the $\phi$ meson decay channel contributing 3.619 MeV (QDCSM1), 3.892 MeV (QDCSM2), and 4.616 MeV (QDCSM3).
- The $\eta'-N$ bound state mass varies from 1872.7 MeV to 1881.0 MeV across the three parameter sets.
- The signal rate after momentum cuts ($p(K^\pm) < 350$ MeV and $p(p) < 500$ MeV) and assuming 50% detector efficiency is 0.75 events per hour.
- The momentum cuts remove only about 10% of signal events, reducing the rate from 1.64/h to 1.50/h before efficiency correction.

## Context
This work extends previous QDCSM studies of dibaryon candidates and hidden charm pentaquark states (Huang 2015) to the strange sector, predicting a $\phi-N$ bound state as a hidden strange pentaquark. The production mechanism follows the proposal by Gao, Lee, and Marinov (2000) that subthreshold $\phi$ meson production in nuclear media enhances bound state formation. The simulation demonstrates feasibility for experimental searches at Jefferson Lab, complementing lattice QCD calculations (Beane 2014) and chiral quark model studies (Huang 2005) that also support such bound states.
