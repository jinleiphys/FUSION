# Time Evolution of Temperature Fluctuation in a Non-Equilibrated System

> The evolution equation for inhomogeneous and anisotropic temperature fluctuation inside a medium is derived within the ambit of Boltzmann Transport Equation (BTE) for a hot gas of massless particles. Also, specializing to a situation created after heavy-ion collision (HIC), we analyze the Fourier space variation of temperature fluctuation of the medium using its temperature profile. The effect of viscosity on the variation of fluctuations in the latter case is investigated and possible implications for early universe cosmology, and its connection with HICs are also explored.

## Key claim

The paper derives an evolution equation for temperature (inverse-temperature) fluctuations in a non-equilibrated system of massless particles using the Boltzmann Transport Equation, but the derivation is limited to observation times much shorter than the relaxation time of the thermal bath; for arbitrary observation times, the authors instead analyze the Fourier-space variation of fluctuations using a specific temperature profile from heavy-ion collision simulations.

## Method

The authors start from the Boltzmann Transport Equation in Relaxation Time Approximation (RTA) with an ansatz for the distribution function that includes an anisotropic and inhomogeneous fluctuation \(\Delta\beta(\vec{r},\hat{p};t)\) in the inverse temperature \(\beta = 1/T\). They linearize the equation for small fluctuations, solve it in Fourier space for massless particles, and average over the solid angle to obtain the time evolution of the relative fluctuation \(\Delta\beta_{\mathrm{rel}}(\vec{k};t)\). For the case of arbitrary observation time, they take a radially varying temperature profile of a viscous medium from Baier et al. (2006), compute the Fourier transform of the inverse-temperature fluctuation, and evaluate the relative variance \(\mathcal{R}_{\beta}\) at different evolution times and viscosities.

## Key numbers

* Debye length \(r_D \sim 2\) fm for coupling \(g = 0.5\) and average temperature \(T = 200\) MeV.
* Relative fluctuation \(\Delta\beta_{\mathrm{rel}}(\vec{k};t)\) plotted for \((t-t^0) = 1, 2, 3\) fm with relaxation time \(t_{\mathrm{R}} = 3\) fm.
* Relative fluctuation \(\Delta\beta_{\mathrm{rel}}(\vec{k};t)\) plotted for \(t_{\mathrm{R}} = 3, 6, 9\) fm with \((t-t^0) = 0.1\) fm.
* For a viscous medium with \(\eta/s = 0.08\), the relative variance \(\mathcal{R}_{\beta}\) decreases from 0.047 at \(\tau = 2.2\) fm/c to 0.011 at \(\tau = 5.1\) fm/c to 0.002 at \(\tau = 9.1\) fm/c.
* At \(\tau = 5.1\) fm/c, \(\mathcal{R}_{\beta} = 0.012\) for \(\eta/s = 0.08\) and \(\mathcal{R}_{\beta} = 0.011\) for \(\eta/s = 0.3\).
* The \(\mathcal{R}_{\beta}\) value at the system boundary (\(\sim 0.01\)) is comparable to the experimentally obtained \((q-1) = 0.018 \pm 0.005\) for 0-10% central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC with \(\sqrt{s}_{NN} = 200\) GeV (from TBW).
* The Tsallis parameter \(q\) for cosmological temperature fluctuations is reported as \(q = 1.045 \pm 0.005\) (from Bernui-Tsallis-Villela).

## Context

This work extends the study of fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, previously applied to net-charge and multiplicity fluctuations (Jeon, Stephanov, NA49), to the specific case of temperature fluctuation. The authors connect their calculated relative variance \(\mathcal{R}_{\beta}\) to the Tsallis non-extensivity parameter \(q\) (Wilk, Tsallis), showing that the value at freeze-out is comparable to experimental fits. They also draw a parallel between the temperature fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions and those in the cosmic microwave background (CMB), noting that similar \(q\) values describe both systems, and suggest that the freeze-out surface in heavy-ion collisions may be analogous to the surface of last scattering in cosmology.
