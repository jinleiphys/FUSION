# Superdeformed rotational bands in the Mercury region; A Cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov study

> A study of rotational properties of the ground superdeformed bands in \Hg{0}, \Hg{2}, \Hg{4}, and \Pb{4} is presented. We use the cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with the {\skm} parametrization of the Skyrme force in the particle-hole channel and a seniority interaction in the pairing channel. An approximate particle number projection is performed by means of the Lipkin-Nogami prescription. We analyze the proton and neutron quasiparticle routhians in connection with the present information on about thirty presently observed superdeformed bands in nuclei close neighbours of \Hg{2}.

## Key claim

The cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with the SkM* Skyrme force and seniority pairing, augmented by the Lipkin-Nogami approximate particle-number projection, can reproduce the observed steady increase of the second moment of inertia in superdeformed bands of mercury-region nuclei, provided the pairing strength is renormalized downward from values that fit static properties.

## Method

The authors solved the cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations on a three-dimensional spatial mesh (1 fm spacing) using the SkM* parametrization of the Skyrme force for the particle-hole channel and a seniority (monopole) pairing force for the particle-particle channel. An approximate variation-after-projection on particle number was implemented via the Lipkin-Nogami prescription, and the quasiparticle routhians were analyzed to interpret the observed one- and two-quasiparticle excitations in about thirty superdeformed bands near ¹⁹²Hg.

## Key numbers

- The HF moment of inertia for ¹⁹²Hg is nearly constant at ≈ 115 ℏ²MeV⁻¹, contradicting the experimental increase from 90 to 135 ℏ²MeV⁻¹ over ℏω = 100–450 keV.
- With HFB (pairing strengths g_N = g_Z = 15.5 MeV), the second moment of inertia shows sudden drops at ℏω = 250 keV and 350 keV due to collapse of proton and neutron pairing, respectively.
- With HFBLN and reduced pairing strengths g_N = g_Z = 12.6 MeV, the neutron and proton pairing gaps at ℏω = 0 in ¹⁹²Hg are Δ_N = 0.557 MeV and Δ_Z = 0.647 MeV.
- For ¹⁹⁴Pb at J₀ = 52 ℏ, the pairing energies CEP_N and CEP_Z remain of order 0.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the cranked Hartree-Fock calculations of Chen (1992) on ¹⁹²Hg, ¹⁹⁴Hg, and ¹⁹⁴Pb by adding pairing correlations and particle-number projection, and it provides a microscopic alternative to the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky and Woods-Saxon approaches (e.g., Riley 1990, Satula 1991, 1993) that have been the standard tools for analyzing superdeformed bands in the A≈190 region. The results enable systematic microscopic studies of crossing frequencies, pairing strengths, and the relative roles of alignment and pairing reduction in the rise of the dynamical moment of inertia.
