# Process-independent effective coupling. From QCD Green's functions to phenomenology

> This article reports on a very recent proposal for a new type of process-independent QCD effective charge [Phys.Rev.D96(2017)054026] defined, as an anologue of the Gell-Mann-Low effective charge in QCD, on the ground of nothing but the knowledge of the gauge-field two-point Green's function, albeit modified within a particular computational framework; namely, the combination of pinch technique and background field method which makes possible a systematic rearranging of classes of diagrams in order to redefine the Green's function and have them obey linear QED-like Slavnov-Taylor identities. We have here calculated that effective charge, shown how strikingly well it compares to a process-dependent effective charge based on the Bjorken sum rule; and, finally, employed it in an exploratory calculation of the proton electromagnetic form factor in the hard scattering regime.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a process-independent QCD effective charge defined solely from the gauge-field two-point Green's function within the pinch technique–background field method framework, and shows that after properly accounting for ghost-gluon dynamics in the gluon vacuum polarisation, this coupling is nearly pointwise identical to the process-dependent effective charge based on the Bjorken sum rule, with the saturation value approaching \(\widehat{\alpha}_{\rm PI}(0) \approx \pi\).

## Method

The authors compute the process-independent effective charge \(\widehat{\alpha}_{\rm PI}(k^2)\) as the ratio of the renormalisation-group-invariant running interaction \(\widehat{d}(k^2)\) to the RGI function \(\mathpzc{D}(k^2)\) derived from the canonical gluon two-point function. They obtain \(\widehat{d}(k^2)\) from lattice-QCD inputs for the gluon propagator, and construct \(\mathpzc{D}(k^2)\) using an interpolating function that imposes the known asymptotic infrared behaviour of the gluon propagator including massless-ghost loop effects, with parameters fixed by lattice data and three-gluon vertex analyses. The resulting coupling is then applied to compute the proton electromagnetic form factor in the hard scattering regime using nucleon parton distribution amplitudes from Ref. Mezrag (2017).

## Key numbers

* Saturation value of the process-independent effective charge: \(\widehat{\alpha}_{\rm PI}(0)/\pi = 1.002\) (after correcting for ghost-gluon dynamics), compared to \(0.949\) from the earlier Padé approximant in Binosi (2016).
* Infrared mass scale: \(m_0 = 0.468\) GeV (updated value), compared to \(0.455\) GeV from the earlier Padé approximant.
* \(\widehat{d}(0) = 14.4\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
* Asymptotic expansion coefficients relative to \(\alpha_{\overline{\rm MS}}\): \(\widehat{\alpha}_{\rm PI}(k^2) = \alpha_{\overline{\rm MS}}(k^2) (1 + 1.09 \, \alpha_{\overline{\rm MS}}(k^2) + \ldots)\); for \(\alpha_{g_1}\): \(\alpha_{g_1}(k^2) = \alpha_{\overline{\rm MS}}(k^2) (1 + 1.14 \, \alpha_{\overline{\rm MS}}(k^2) + \ldots)\).
* Inflection point of the predicted running coupling at \(\sqrt{k^2} = 0.7\) GeV.
* Fit parameters for the ansatz Eq. (14): \(d_1 = 3.56\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(d_2 = 2.85\) GeV\(^{-4}\), \(b_1 = 7.25\) GeV\(^{-2}\), with \(\Lambda_T = 500\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the PT-BFM framework developed by Binosi (2014, 2016) and Aguilar (2009) to define a QCD analogue of the Gell-Mann-Low effective charge in QED, contrasting with process-dependent effective charges such as \(\alpha_{g_1}\) defined via the Bjorken sum rule (Grunberg 1982, Deur 2016). The near pointwise agreement between the process-independent coupling and \(\alpha_{g_1}\) at infrared momenta is a nontrivial result that supports the uniqueness of the QCD running coupling. The exploratory calculation of the proton electromagnetic form factor using this coupling with PDAs from Mezrag (2017) demonstrates a path toward phenomenological applications, though the authors note this is preliminary and aims only to sketch the rough impact.
