# Fixed-t analyticity as a constraint in single energy partial wave analyses of meson photoproduction reactions

> Partial wave amplitudes of meson photoproduction reactions are an important source of information in baryon spectroscopy. We investigate a new approach in single-energy partial wave analyses of these reactions. Instead of using a constraint to theoretical models in order to achieve solutions which are continuous in energy, we enforce the analyticity of the amplitudes at fixed values of the Mandelstam variable $t$. We present an iterative procedure with successive fixed-$t$ amplitude analyses which constrain the single-energy partial wave analyses and apply this method to the $γp \to ηp$ reaction. We use pseudo data, generated by the EtaMAID model, to test the method and to analyze ambiguities. Finally, we present an analytically constrained partial wave analysis using experimental data for four polarization observables recently measured at MAMI and GRAAL in the energy range from threshold to $\sqrt{s}=1.85$ GeV.

## Key claim
Fixed-t analyticity, enforced through an iterative Pietarinen expansion method, can replace model-dependent energy-continuity constraints in single-energy partial wave analyses of meson photoproduction, but the method's success depends on the quality and completeness of the experimental data base and still leaves residual ambiguities.

## Method
The authors perform separate fixed-t amplitude analyses (FT AA) and single-energy partial wave analyses (SE PWA) in an iterative loop. In the FT AA, invariant amplitudes at fixed Mandelstam t are represented by two Pietarinen expansions in conformally mapped variables, with a convergence penalty function suppressing higher expansion coefficients. The SE PWA at each energy uses a soft truncation penalty on higher multipoles; the two analyses constrain each other iteratively until convergence. The method is tested on pseudo data from the EtaMAID model and then applied to real experimental data for the γp → ηp reaction.

## Key numbers
- Energy range: from threshold to √s = 1.85 GeV (center-of-mass energy W).
- Fixed-t analyses are performed at t = -0.2 GeV² and t = -0.5 GeV².
- The η photoproduction threshold is W_thr = 1.486 GeV (for t = -0.2 GeV²) and W_thr = 1.554 GeV (for t = -0.5 GeV²).
- The unphysical cut in the t channel starts at t = 4m_π².
- The πN photoproduction threshold is at W_π,th = 1.077 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the fixed-t analyticity method originally developed by Höhler (1984) for πN elastic scattering partial wave analyses (the KH80 solution) to meson photoproduction reactions. It contrasts with prior approaches that enforce energy continuity by constraining fits to theoretical reaction models such as isobar models (EtaMAID, versions I-III from 2015-2017) or coupled-channels K-matrix methods (Shklyar 2006, Anisovich 2012). The method enables model-independent single-energy partial wave analyses that avoid the strong model dependence of previous approaches, though it still requires sufficient experimental data to resolve ambiguities.
