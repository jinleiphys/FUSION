# Transverse energy dependence of J/Psi suppression in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy

> Prediction for transverse energy dependence of $J/ψ$ to Drell-Yan ratio in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy was obtained in a model which assume 100% absorption of $J/ψ$ above a threshold density. The threshold density was obtained by fitting the NA50 data on $J/ψ$ suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy. At RHIC energy, hard processes may be important. Prediction of $J/ψ$ suppression with and without hard processes were obtained. With hard processes included, $J/ψ$'s are strongly suppressed.

## Key claim

The model predicts that in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy, the transverse energy dependence of J/Psi suppression depends critically on whether a hard scattering component is included in the transverse density; without the hard component, suppression is similar to that at SPS energy, but with a 37% hard component, J/Psi's are strongly suppressed and the characteristic second drop in the J/Psi to Drell-Yan ratio is washed out.

## Method

The author extended a model by Blaizot et al. (2000) that assumes 100% J/Psi absorption above a threshold density, incorporating fluctuations in the number of nucleon-nucleon collisions at fixed impact parameter via a geometric model for the transverse energy distribution. The threshold density and J/Psi-nucleon absorption cross-section were obtained by fitting NA50 data on J/Psi suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy. The model was then applied to Au+Au collisions at RHIC by scaling the SPS transverse energy distribution upward by 70% based on PHOBOS multiplicity data, and predictions were made both with and without a hard scattering component in the transverse density.

## Key numbers

- Threshold density for 100% J/Psi absorption: n_c = 3.8 fm^{-2} (with sigma_abs = 6.4 mb) or n_c = 3.42-3.44 fm^{-2} (with sigma_abs = 4 mb), obtained from fitting NA50 Pb+Pb data at SPS.
- Average transverse energy per nucleon-nucleon collision at SPS: beta/alpha = 0.1 GeV; at RHIC: beta/alpha = 0.16 GeV, a 60% increase.
- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross-section: 32 mb at SPS, 41 mb at RHIC.
- The second drop in the J/Psi to Drell-Yan ratio occurs at E_T ~ 100 GeV in Pb+Pb at SPS, shifting to E_T ~ 180 GeV in Au+Au at RHIC without the hard component.
- With a 37% hard component, suppression at the knee of the E_T distribution is 6 times greater than the corresponding suppression without the hard component.

## Context

This work extends the model of Blaizot et al. (2000) by including fluctuations in the number of nucleon-nucleon collisions at fixed impact parameter, which were neglected in the earlier approach. It also builds on the author's own prior analysis (Chaudhuri, 2001) of NA50 data. The predictions are intended to guide experimental planning at RHIC, where the PHOBOS collaboration had recently measured multiplicity. The results contrast with the predictions of Blaizot et al. (2001), though agreement is found at large transverse energy. The paper enables future experimental tests of whether hard processes significantly modify J/Psi suppression patterns at RHIC energies.
