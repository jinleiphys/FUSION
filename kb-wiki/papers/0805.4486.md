# Comment on "Polarizability of the pion: No conflict between dispersion theory and chiral perturbation theory"

> The statement of the authors of the article Phys. Rev. C 77, 065211 (2008) that spurious singularities occur in the dispersion relation approach, where imaginary parts of the amplitudes of the process γγ->ππare saturated by the contributions of meson resonances by using Breit-Wigner expressions, is analyzed. It is shown that there are no any additional singularities in this approach and the disagreement between the predictions of the dispersion relations and the chiral perturbation theory for (α_1-β_1)_π^{\pm} remains.

# Comment on "Polarizability of the pion: No conflict between dispersion theory and chiral perturbation theory"

## Key claim
The authors argue that no additional spurious singularities arise in the dispersion relation approach of Fil'kov and Kashevarov (2005-2006) when Breit-Wigner expressions with energy-dependent widths are used to saturate the imaginary parts of the amplitudes for γγ → ππ, and that the disagreement between dispersion relation predictions and chiral perturbation theory for the charged pion dipole polarizability difference (α₁−β₁)π± remains unresolved.

## Method
The authors constructed fixed-t dispersion relations with one subtraction at s=μ² for the helicity amplitudes M₊₊ and M₊₋ of the γγ→ππ process, which are identical via cross symmetry to dispersion relations with two subtractions. They also built dispersion sum rules for the dipole and quadrupole pion polarizabilities using fixed-u=μ² dispersion relations without and with one subtraction, respectively. The imaginary parts of the amplitudes were saturated by meson resonance contributions using Breit-Wigner expressions, and the authors tested the zero-width approximation by recalculating the contributions of all mesons except the σ meson to the dispersion sum rule for the dipole polarizability difference.

## Key numbers
- The full calculation of the charged pion dipole polarizability difference (α₁−β₁)π± from dispersion sum rules yields 13.60 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, with a total uncertainty ΔΣ of 2.15 × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- The zero-width approximation calculation of (α₁−β₁)π± gives 13.70 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, nearly identical to the full calculation, demonstrating the absence of spurious singularities.
- The neutral pion dipole polarizability difference (α₁−β₁)π⁰ from the full calculation is −3.49 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, with uncertainty 2.13 × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- A finite-energy dispersion sum rule calculation by another author (Petrun'kin, 2005) gave (α₁−β₁)π± = 10.3 ± 1.3 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, which practically coincides with the Fil'kov and Kashevarov result.
- The Pasquini, Drechsel, and Scherer (2008) calculation gave (α₁−β₁)π⁰ = 6.62 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, which differs from the chiral perturbation theory result of −1.90 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ both in quantity and sign.

## Context
This comment responds to Pasquini, Drechsel, and Scherer (2008), who claimed that spurious singularities appear in the dispersion relation approach of Radzhabov (2005) and Fil'kov and Kashevarov (2005-2006) due to energy-dependent Breit-Wigner widths, and that the polarizability values should be modified. The authors show that the zero-width approximation produces nearly identical results, confirming no additional singularities exist. They identify the main error in Pasquini et al. (2008) as using an incorrect form for the vector meson contributions (Eq. vect) instead of the proper pole form (Eq. vect-d), and note that the S-wave ππ interaction contribution strongly depends on the upper integration limit, requiring Λ greater than (5 GeV)² for stable results. The discrepancy between dispersion sum rules and chiral perturbation theory (Burgi, 1996; Gasser, 2002) for (α₁−β₁)π± persists, attributed to different treatments of the σ meson (only partially included in two-loop chiral perturbation theory) and vector mesons (poles in dispersion sum rules versus Born terms in chiral perturbation theory).
