# Relativistic NN scattering without partial wave decomposition

> We consider the covariant Spectator equation with a One Boson Exchange (OBE) kernel, and apply it to the NN system. Relativistic effects such as retardation and negative-energy state components are included in that equation. We develop a method, based on the Padé method, to solve the Spectator equation without partial wave decomposition. The convergence of the partial wave decomposition series is tested as a function of the energy. The on- and off-mass-shell amplitudes are calculated. The NN interaction was fitted to the differential cross section for NN scattering, in the energy range below the pion production threshold.

## Key claim

The authors develop a method to solve the covariant Spectator equation for nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering without partial wave decomposition, using a Padé method to handle the coupled integral equations; the method works for energies below the pion production threshold, but the convergence of the partial wave series is tested as a function of energy, and the authors note that the form factor exponent had to be increased from n=1 to n=2 to achieve convergence in the 3D method, unlike in previous partial wave decomposition calculations.

## Method

The authors solve the covariant Spectator equation with a One Boson Exchange (OBE) kernel including π, σ, ρ, and ω meson exchanges, using helicity and ρ-spin states to represent the 8-channel coupled system. They reduce the 3-dimensional integral equation to 2 dimensions by analytically integrating the azimuthal angle φ, then discretize the remaining momentum and polar angle variables using Gaussian quadrature and solve the resulting linear system via the Padé method (Born series summation). The kernel includes retardation effects and negative-energy state components, and the authors introduce an "on-mass-shell prescription" to handle spurious singularities in the exchange kernel.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon mass m is used as the energy scale; the momentum grid uses a transformation k_i = Λ x_i/(1-x_i) with Λ ≈ 0.5 m.
- The propagator pole residue factor is m² p̄/(4W) where p̄ is the on-shell momentum and W the total energy.
- The principal part integral S = - (1/W) log((W-2m)/(2m)) is used for the principal value treatment of the propagator singularity.
- The matrix dimension is n = 8(N_p+1)(N_u+1), where N_p and N_u are typically of order 20.
- The form factor exponent n=2 was required for convergence, whereas previous partial wave decomposition work used n=1 (Stadler 1997).

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic 3D methods for NN scattering without partial wave decomposition (Fac2, Fac3) to the relativistic Spectator equation framework developed by Gross (1969, 1992). The method enables future applications to photo- and electro-reactions at high energies where partial wave decomposition converges too slowly (above 300 MeV, as shown in Fac2), and can be extended to other quasi-potential equations beyond the Spectator equation. The approach prepares for later applications to three-nucleon systems and reactions probed by JLab data.
