# Probing the Frontiers of QCD

> With the energy scales opened up by RHIC and LHC the age of high-pT physics is upon us. This has created new opportunities and novel mysteries, both of which will be explored in this thesis. The possibility now exists experimentally to exploit these high momentum particles to uniquely probe the unprecedented state of matter produced in heavy ion collisions. At the same time naive theoretical expectations have been dashed by data. The first puzzle we confront is that of the enormous intermediate-pT azimuthal anisotropy, or v2, of jets observed at RHIC. The second puzzle is the surprisingly similar suppression of light mesons and nonphotonic electrons, which precludes perturbative predictions predicated on gluon bremsstrahlung radiation as the dominant energy loss channel. Near qualitative agreement results from including collisional energy loss and integrating over the fluctuating jet pathlengths. Another conjecture for heavy quark energy loss comes via explicit construction using the AdS/CFT correspondence; the momentum loss of a hanging dragging string moving through the deconfined plasma leads to qualitative agreement with heavy quark decay data. We propose a robust test to experimentally differentiate these two competing ideas: the ratio of charm to bottom suppression rapidly approaches 1 for pQCD but is independent of momentum and well below 1 for AdS/CFT. Finally as a warmup problem to calculating the photon bremsstrahlung associated with jet energy loss we quantify improvements to the perturbative estimates of the Ter-Mikayelian effect.

## Key claim

The thesis argues that two distinct puzzles in heavy ion collisions, the large intermediate-\(p_T\) azimuthal anisotropy (\(v_2\)) and the similar suppression of light mesons and nonphotonic electrons, can be brought into qualitative agreement with data by including collisional energy loss and integrating over fluctuating jet pathlengths, rather than relying solely on perturbative gluon bremsstrahlung. A robust experimental test to differentiate between pQCD and AdS/CFT descriptions of heavy quark energy loss is proposed: the ratio of charm to bottom suppression approaches 1 rapidly for pQCD but is independent of momentum and well below 1 for AdS/CFT.

## Method

The thesis develops a theoretical framework combining perturbative QCD energy loss mechanisms (including both radiative and collisional contributions) with pathlength fluctuations of jets in the expanding medium produced in heavy ion collisions at RHIC. It also constructs an explicit AdS/CFT model for heavy quark energy loss by treating a hanging dragging string moving through the deconfined plasma, and compares the resulting momentum loss predictions to heavy quark decay data. The work further quantifies improvements to perturbative estimates of the Ter-Mikayelian effect as a warmup to calculating photon bremsstrahlung associated with jet energy loss.

## Key numbers

- The string tension in the original hadronic string theory had a Regge slope \(\alpha' \sim 1.0\ \text{(GeV)}^{-2}\).
- The promotion of string theory to a theory of everything changed the string tension by 38 orders of magnitude, from \(\alpha'\sim 1\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) to \(\alpha'\sim 1/M_p^2\), where the Planck mass in 4D is \(M_p\simeq 1.22 \times 10^{18}\ \text{GeV}\).
- The strong coupling limit of the viscosity to entropy ratio is \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\), with corrections of order \(\mathcal{O}(1/\sqrt{\lambda})\).
- The entropy of weakly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) \(U(N)\) SYM is \(S = (2\pi^3/3) N^2 V_3 T^3\), while the black hole entropy from the supergravity picture is \(S_{BH} = (\pi^2/2) N^2 V_3 T^3\), giving a factor of \(4/3\) difference between the two limits.
- The temperature of the nonextremal \(D3\) brane geometry is \(T = r_0/(\pi R^2)\).
- The AdS/CFT prediction for the Debye screening length shows a kink at \(|\vec{x}| = 0.24/T\) for \(\lambda \to \infty\).

## Context

This work extends the program of applying AdS/CFT correspondence to heavy ion collisions, following the foundational conjecture of Maldacena (1997) and the subsequent derivation of the viscosity to entropy bound by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2004). It directly addresses the puzzles of intermediate-\(p_T\) azimuthal anisotropy and heavy flavor suppression observed at RHIC, which contradicted naive perturbative expectations based solely on gluon bremsstrahlung. The proposed test comparing charm-to-bottom suppression ratios between pQCD and AdS/CFT predictions enables experimental differentiation between these competing theoretical descriptions of heavy quark energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma.
