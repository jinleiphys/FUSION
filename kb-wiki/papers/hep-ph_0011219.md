# Initial Condition for QGP Evolution from NEXUS

> We recently proposed a new approach to high energy nuclear scattering, which treats the initial stage of heavy ion collisions in a sophisticated way. We are able to calculate macroscopic quantities like energy density and velocity flow at the end of this initial stage, after the two nuclei having penetrated each other. In other words, we provide the initial conditions for a macroscopic treatment of the second stage of the collision. We address in particular the question of how to incorporate the soft component properly. We find almost perfect "Bjorken scaling": the rapidity coincides with the space-time rapidity, whereas the transverse flow is practically zero. The distribution of the energy density in the transverse plane shows typically a very "bumpy" structure.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the NEXUS model provides almost perfect "Bjorken scaling" (rapidity coincides with space-time rapidity and transverse flow is practically zero) for heavy ion collisions, but this result is conditional on the specific proper time of 1 fm/c and the particular smearing width of 0.5 fm used in the calculation.

## Method

The authors use the parton-based Gribov-Regge model NEXUS to calculate the initial stage of nucleus-nucleus collisions, treating multiple scattering via Pomeron exchanges with soft, semi-hard, and hard contributions. They map the parton configurations from cut Pomerons onto kinky strings using the Nambu-Goto Lagrangian, then compute the energy-momentum tensor on a hyper-surface of constant proper time by summing over string segments with Gaussian smearing. The energy density and velocity fields are extracted from the energy-momentum tensor in a local comoving frame.

## Key numbers

- Energy density calculations are performed at proper time tau = 1 fm/c with a Gaussian smearing width sigma = 0.5 fm
- For a single string without kinks at 50 GeV, the energy density is peaked around x=0 and distributed evenly in eta between limits defined by the string energy
- For proton-proton scattering at 50 GeV, four strings with energies of 17.2 GeV, 9.3 GeV, 7.6 GeV and 15.8 GeV produce an energy density peak at eta=0 roughly 2.5 times larger than for a single string
- For central gold-gold collisions at 200 AGeV, the energy density shows large fluctuations in the transverse plane with pronounced peaks corresponding to fluctuations in nucleon density (measured in nucleons per fm^2)

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Regge theory approach (Gribov, 1968) to provide realistic initial conditions for hydrodynamic evolution of quark-gluon plasma, addressing the problem that previous models used simplified initial conditions like flat distributions in space-time rapidity. The NEXUS model improves upon earlier approaches by consistently treating energy conservation in both cross section and particle production calculations, which was not the case in models by Baker (1976) or Abramovsky (1992). The results enable more realistic hydrodynamic simulations of the later stages of heavy ion collisions, particularly at RHIC energies, by providing initial conditions that include the proper treatment of soft partons and the "bumpy" structure of energy density in the transverse plane.
