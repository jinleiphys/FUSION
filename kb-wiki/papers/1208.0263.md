# Microscopic DC-TDHF study of heavy-ion potentials and fusion cross sections

> We study heavy-ion fusion reactions at energies near the Coulomb barrier, in particular with neutron-rich radioactive ion beams. Dynamic microscopic calculations are carried out on a three-dimensional lattice using the Density-Constrained Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) method. New results are presented for the $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca system which are compared to $^{132}$Sn+$^{48}$Ca studied earlier. Our theoretical fusion cross-sections agree surprisingly well with recent data measured at HRIBF. We also study the near- and sub-barrier fusion of $^{24,16}$O on $^{12}$C which is important to determine the composition and heating of the crust of accreting neutron stars.

## Key claim

The DC-TDHF method reproduces measured fusion cross sections for neutron-rich systems like $^{132}$Sn+$^{40,48}$Ca without adjustable parameters, but the agreement depends critically on using energy-dependent potentials: the potential barrier height increases dramatically with increasing TDHF energy, and only the low-energy potential (e.g., $E_\mathrm{TDHF}=115$ MeV) explains the sub-barrier data, while the high-energy potential (e.g., $E_\mathrm{TDHF}=180$ MeV) describes above-barrier data.

## Method

The authors performed fully microscopic Density-Constrained Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) calculations on a three-dimensional Cartesian lattice using the Skyrme SLy4 interaction with all time-odd terms. At each time step during the TDHF evolution, the instantaneous density was used to perform a constrained static Hartree-Fock minimization, yielding the ion-ion potential $V(R)$ after subtracting the binding energies of the separated nuclei. Fusion cross sections were then obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with the Incoming Wave Boundary Condition (IWBC) method using the transformed potential that accounts for the coordinate-dependent mass parameter.

## Key numbers

- For $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca at $E_\mathrm{TDHF}=180$ MeV: barrier height $E_B=115.3$ MeV located at $R=11.8$ fm
- For $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca at $E_\mathrm{TDHF}=115$ MeV: barrier height $E_B=111.5$ MeV located at $R=12.4$ fm
- At $E_\mathrm{c.m.}=110$ MeV, experimental fusion cross section for $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca is approximately 6 mb, compared to 0.8 mb for $^{132}$Sn+$48$Ca
- For $^{12}$C+$^{16}$O: DC-TDHF barrier peak energy of 7.77 MeV
- For $^{12}$C+$^{24}$O: DC-TDHF barrier peak energy of 6.64 MeV
- Lattice grid spacing of 1.0 fm; time step $\Delta t = 0.4$ fm/c; initial separation of 22 fm for central collisions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DC-TDHF studies of systems including $^{132}$Sn+$^{64}$Ni (Umar and Oberacker, 2007), $^{16}$O+$^{208}$Pb (Umar and Oberacker, 2009), and tin+calcium isotopes (Oberacker and Umar, 2012) to the new system $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca, comparing with recent HRIBF data (Kohley and collaborators, 2012). The results contradict the frozen-density (sudden) approximation used in double-folding methods and the Sao Paulo barrier penetration model, which predict order-of-magnitude smaller cross sections for neutron-rich systems like $^{12}$C+$^{24}$O. The work enables microscopic predictions for fusion reactions relevant to neutron star crust composition and heating, and suggests that future Skyrme parametrizations should incorporate deformation and scattering information.
