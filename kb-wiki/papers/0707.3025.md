# Constraints on nuclear matter properties from QCD susceptibilities

> We establish the interrelation between the QCD scalar response of the nuclear medium and its response to a scalar probe coupled to nucleons, such as the scalar meson responsible for the nuclear binding. The relation that we derive applies at the nucleonic as well as at the nuclear levels. Non trivial consequences follow. In particular it opens the possibility of relating medium effects in the scalar meson exchange or three-body forces of nuclear physics to QCD lattice studies of the nucleon mass

# Constraints on nuclear matter properties from QCD susceptibilities

## Key claim

The QCD scalar response of nuclear matter and the nuclear response to a scalar probe coupled to nucleons are mutually related by a scaling coefficient involving the nucleon scalar charge, and this relation holds at both the nucleonic and nuclear levels; however, the linear sigma model predicts a nucleon scalar susceptibility too large in magnitude compared to lattice QCD results, and this failure is linked to the absence of confinement effects.

## Method

The authors derive the nuclear QCD scalar susceptibility from the derivative of the quark condensate with respect to quark mass at constant chemical potential, first in a relativistic mean-field chiral model with scalar and vector meson exchange. They then extend the analysis to incorporate confinement using a hybrid model of three constituent quarks bound by a harmonic confining potential, where the nucleon mass receives contributions from both the quark condensate and confinement. The relation between the scalar meson self-energy and the nucleon QCD scalar susceptibility is shown to persist in this extended framework.

## Key numbers

- The non-pionic part of the nucleon sigma commutator from lattice data is sigma_N^{non-pion} = 29 MeV (using a_2 = 1.5 GeV^{-1}, a_4 = -0.5 GeV^{-3}) or 20 MeV (using a_2 = 1 GeV^{-1}, a_4 = -0.23 GeV^{-3}).
- The non-pionic nucleon scalar susceptibility from lattice data is chi_S^{N, non-pion} = -5.4 GeV^{-1}.
- The scalar charge of the nucleon from the scalar field is Q_S^s = -2.4 (using a_2 = 1.5 GeV^{-1}) or Q_S^s = -1.66 (using a_2 = 1 GeV^{-1}).
- The mean scalar field at normal nuclear density is |bar s(rho_0)| = 21 MeV.
- The linear sigma model predicts -a_4 = 3.5 GeV^{-3} (using a_2 = 1.5 GeV^{-1}) or -a_4 = 1.2 GeV^{-3} (using a_2 = 1 GeV^{-1}), both much larger than the lattice value of 0.5 GeV^{-3} or 0.23 GeV^{-3}.
- The in-medium sigma mass decreases by only 6 percent at normal nuclear density (for m_sigma = 0.75 GeV, g_S = 10), much less than the chiral dropping alone.
- The three-body force contribution to the energy per nucleon is (E/A)^{three-body} = 20 (rho/rho_0)^2 MeV for C = 1, and about 50 percent larger for the lattice-derived value C = 1.3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Ericson, Chanfray, 2003; Ericson, Chanfray, 2005) on the increase of the QCD scalar susceptibility in nuclear matter due to mixing with nuclear excitations. It contradicts the linear sigma model prediction of too large a nucleon scalar susceptibility and links this failure to the absence of confinement, which also causes nuclear matter collapse in that model (Kerman, Miller, 1974). The results enable a description of nuclear matter saturation properties using lattice QCD parameters as inputs, as demonstrated in a companion paper (Ericson, Chanfray, 2007). The approach connects to the quark-meson coupling model (Guichon, 1988; Guichon, Saito, Rodionov, Thomas, 1996) and to lattice studies of the nucleon mass evolution with pion mass (Thomas et al., 2004).
