# Studying the $KΛ$ strong interaction with femtoscopic correlation functions and the $N^*(1535)$

> We investigate the $K Λ$ strong interaction dynamics around the energy region of $N^*(1535)$ resonance through femtoscopic correlation functions within the Koonin-Pratt formalism, where coupled-channel effects of $πN$, $KΛ$, $K Σ$ and $ηN$ channels are taken into account, by means of which the $N^*(1535)$ resonance can be dynamically generated in the chiral unitary approach. Two-body scattering amplitudes are calculated using the chiral unitary approach, and the corresponding wave functions and correlation functions are obtained. Our analysis shows that the $πN$ channel becomes important to the $K^+Λ$ correlation function through coupled channel effects, even if its mass threshold is far from the energy region of the $N^*(1535)$. Furthermore, with the picture that the $N^*(1535)$ is a dynamically generated state, we can get a good reproduction of the experimental data on the $K^+Λ$ correlation function. This work establishes femtoscopic correlation functions as a sensitive probe for coupled-channel dynamics and validates the Koonin-Pratt framework in multi-channel hadronic systems, offering critical insights into some exotic hadron states and strong interaction mechanisms.

# Studying the $K\Lambda$ strong interaction with femtoscopic correlation functions and the $N^*(1535)$

## Key claim

The $K^+\Lambda$ correlation function can be well reproduced by a chiral unitary approach where the $N^*(1535)$ resonance is dynamically generated from coupled channels including $\pi N$, $K\Lambda$, $K\Sigma$, and $\eta N$, but only if the $\pi N$ channel is included, even though its mass threshold is far from the $N^*(1535)$ energy region, it significantly smooths out cusp structures that would otherwise appear at the $K\Sigma$ threshold and disagree with ALICE data.

## Method

The authors compute two-body scattering amplitudes for the strangeness $S=0$, charge $+1$ channels ($K^0\Sigma^+$, $K^+\Sigma^0$, $K^+\Lambda$, $\pi^+ n$, $\pi^0 p$, $\eta p$) using the chiral unitary approach with the Bethe-Salpeter equation, then derive the $K^+\Lambda$ femtoscopic correlation function within the Koonin-Pratt formalism. The loop functions are regularized dimensionally with subtraction constants $\alpha_j(\mu)$ at scale $\mu = 1200$ MeV, and the source function uses a two-Gaussian parametrization with parameters from ALICE (2023). The model parameters are fitted to the ALICE $K^+\Lambda$ correlation function data under two scenarios: fixed subtraction constants from Inoue (2001) with only normalization free, or all subtraction constants free with the $N^*(1535)$ pole position constrained to PDG values.

## Key numbers

- $N^*(1535)$ pole position: Scenario I gives $\sqrt{s_{\rm pole}} = 1531 - i\,36.5$ MeV; Scenario II gives $\sqrt{s_{\rm pole}} = 1505 - i\,40.1$ MeV
- Branching fractions from Scenario I: $Br[N^*(1535) \to \pi N] = 41\%$, $Br[N^*(1535) \to \eta N] = 56\%$; from Scenario II: $33\%$ and $52\%$ respectively
- Ratio $\Gamma_{N^*(1535) \to N\eta} / \Gamma_{N^*(1535) \to N\pi} = 1.4$ (Scenario I) and $1.6$ (Scenario II)
- $\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f.} = 1.9$ for Scenario I (2 free parameters, 28 data points); $\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f.} = 1.1$ for Scenario II (6 free parameters, 28 data points)
- Source function parameters from ALICE (2023): $R_1 = 1.202^{+0.043}_{-0.042}$ fm, $R_2 = 2.330^{+0.050}_{-0.045}$ fm, $\lambda_S = 0.9806^{+0.0006}_{-0.0008}$, $\omega_S = 0.7993^{+0.0037}_{-0.0027}$
- $K^+\Lambda$ scattering length from Scenario I: $a_{K^+\Lambda} = 0.33 - i\,0.14$ MeV$^{-1}$; from Scenario II: $a_{K^+\Lambda} = 0.26 - i\,0.15$ MeV$^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends previous chiral unitary studies of $K\Lambda$ correlation functions by Molina (2023) and Li (2023), which did not include the $\pi N$ channel and consequently showed cusp structures at the $\eta N$ and $K\Sigma$ thresholds that were not observed in ALICE data (2020, 2023). By including the $\pi N$ channel, the present calculation produces smooth correlation functions that reproduce the experimental measurements. The results support the hadronic molecule picture of the $N^*(1535)$ resonance, consistent with earlier work by Inoue (2001) and Zou (2006, 2007, 2010) on its strong couplings to strangeness channels. This work enables femtoscopic correlation functions to be used as a sensitive probe of coupled-channel dynamics in multi-channel hadronic systems, particularly for studying exotic hadron states with molecular nature.
