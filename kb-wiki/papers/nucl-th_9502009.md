# Sensitivity of the nuclear equation of state towards relativistic effects

> We analyze relativistic effects in transverse momentum using Quantum Molecular Dynamics [QMD] and its covariant extension Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics [RQMD]. The strength of the relativistic effects is found to increase with the bombarding energy and with an averaged impact parameter. The variation in the intensity of the relativistic effects with variation in the mass of the colliding nuclei is not systematic. Furthermore, the hard EOS is affected drastically by the relativistic effects whereas the soft EOS is affected less. Our analysis shows that up to the bombarding energy of 1 GeV/nucl., the influence of relativistic effects is small. Whereas at higher energies, relativistic effects become naturally very important.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects in transverse momentum flow are small (below 20% in the defined SORE measure) for bombarding energies up to 1 GeV/nucleon, but become very important at higher energies; the hard equation of state is affected drastically by relativistic effects whereas the soft equation of state is affected less.

## Method

The authors compared transverse momentum flow from the non-covariant Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model with its covariant extension Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD) for heavy-ion collisions. They defined a "Size Of Relativistic Effects" (SORE) quantity as the absolute value of the fractional difference between RQMD and QMD results for the average in-plane transverse momentum. The analysis was performed for various bombarding energies, impact parameters, and nuclear masses, using both hard and soft equations of state while keeping the relativistic mean field absent by generalizing the Skyrme force as a Lorentz-scalar.

## Key numbers

- At 50 MeV/nucleon, RQMD and QMD show good agreement (relativistic effects are minimal).
- Up to 1 GeV/nucleon, the intensity of relativistic effects is very small (below the 20% threshold considered significant for flow).
- At 1.5 GeV/nucleon, the maximum SORE for the hard EOS is about 170%, while for the soft EOS it is less than 90%.
- The SORE as a function of impact parameter at 1.5 GeV/nucleon varies between 26% and 170%.
- Averaged over collision-dominated impact parameters (0 < b_scale < 0.5), the SORE is about 49% for the hard EOS and about 36% for the soft EOS.
- Averaged over mutual-interaction-dominated impact parameters (0.5 ≤ b_scale ≤ 1.0), the SORE is 135.8% for the hard EOS and 85.6% for the soft EOS.
- Averaged over the full impact parameter range (0.0 < b_scale ≤ 1.0), the hard EOS shows about a 90% effect, while the soft EOS shows less than 50%.
- Statistical error in RQMD simulations is about 2% for light systems (C-C) and about 4% for the heaviest system (Ca-Ca).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of transverse momentum flow by Molitoris (1987) and Aichelin (1991) by systematically quantifying the differences between covariant (RQMD) and non-covariant (QMD) treatments. The RQMD approach was originally developed by the Frankfurt group (Sorge, 1989) for ultra-relativistic energies; here it is applied at intermediate energies. The results enable researchers to determine when non-covariant models can be safely used (up to 1 GeV/nucleon) and show that relativistic effects cannot be estimated by simply rescaling non-covariant results, since hard and soft equations of state respond very differently to covariant treatment.
