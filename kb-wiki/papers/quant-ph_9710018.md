# Accidental Degeneracy and Berry Phase of Resonant States

> We study the complex geometric phase acquired by the resonant states of an open quantum system which evolves irreversibly in a slowly time dependent environment. In analogy with the case of bound states, the Berry phase factors of resonant states are holonomy group elements of a complex line bundle with structure group C*. In sharp contrast with bound states, accidental degeneracies of resonances produce a continuous closed line of singularities formally equivalent to a continuous distribution of "magnetic" charge on a "diabolical" circle, in consequence, we find different classes of topologically inequivalent non-trivial closed paths in parameter space.

## Key claim

The Berry phase of resonant states in open quantum systems is a complex geometric phase factor arising from holonomy in a complex line bundle with structure group C* (the multiplicative group of non-zero complex numbers), and accidental degeneracies of resonances produce a continuous closed line of singularities formally equivalent to a continuous distribution of "magnetic" charge on a "diabolical" circle, leading to topologically inequivalent non-trivial closed paths in parameter space.

## Method

The authors study the adiabatic evolution of resonant (Gamow) states of a Hermitian Hamiltonian with non-self-adjoint purely outgoing wave boundary conditions, using a rigged Hilbert space formalism to properly characterize the Gamow functions as generalized eigenvectors. They derive closed analytical expressions for the complex Berry phase by expanding the time-dependent wave function in terms of instantaneous energy eigenstates of the complete Hamiltonian, transforming the path integral into a surface integral via Stokes theorem, and showing that the adiabatic evolution condition is equivalent to parallel transport in a complex line bundle over parameter space.

## Key numbers

- The codimension of accidental degeneracy of n resonances differs significantly from those of bound states (no explicit numerical value given for the codimension)
- The paper references a numerical analysis of experimental data on the 2+ doublet of resonances with T = 0,1 in the energy spectrum of 8Be, showing that a true crossing of resonances mixed by a Hermitian interaction may be brought about by variation of only two real linearly independent parameters (no explicit numerical values from the data are provided in the text)
- The sum of geometric phases over all resonant states equals -2πc₁, where c₁ is the first Chern number of the complex vector bundle (topological invariant)

## Context

This work extends the geometric interpretation of Berry phase, first given by Simon (1983) for adiabatic evolution of closed quantum systems, to resonant states of open quantum systems. It builds on prior work by Dattoli et al. on Berry phase in optical supermode propagation, Miniatura et al. on geometric phase in atomic systems with metastable states, and the authors' own previous papers (Mondragón and Hernández, 1995-1996) giving explicit expressions for geometric phase of resonant states. The paper enables further study of topological properties of resonant state degeneracies and their observable consequences in nuclear physics experiments, such as peripheral collisions of 8Be with double magic nuclei like 208Pb.
