# Extended R-matrix description of two-proton radioactivity

> Two-proton ($2p$) radioactivity provides fundamental knowledge on the three-body decay mechanism and the residual nuclear interaction. In this work, we propose decay width formulae in the extended R-matrix framework for different decay mechanisms, including sequential $2p$ decay, diproton decay, tri-body decay, and sequential two-diproton decay. The diproton and tri-body formulae, combined with information on the two-nucleon transfer amplitude and Wigner single-particle reduced width, can reproduce well experimental $2p$ radioactivity half-lives. For the case of $^{67}$Kr, theoretical predictions for direct $2p$ decay give much larger half-lives than the recent measurement from RIKEN. A combination of direct and sequential $2p$ emission is analyzed by considering a small negative one-proton separation energy and a possible enhanced contribution from the $p$-wave component. The present method predicts that $^{71}$Sr and $^{74}$Zr may be the most promising candidates for future study on $2p$ radioactivity. Our model gives an upper limit of 55(4) keV for the decay width of $4p$ emission in recently found four-proton resonant nuclide, $^{18}$Mg, which agrees with the observed width of 115(100) keV.

## Key claim

The extended R-matrix framework can reproduce experimental two-proton radioactivity half-lives well when diproton and tri-body decay formulae are combined with two-nucleon transfer amplitude and Wigner single-particle reduced width information, but for the specific case of ⁶⁷Kr the theoretical predictions for direct 2p decay give much larger half-lives than the recent RIKEN measurement, requiring a combination of direct and sequential 2p emission with a small negative one-proton separation energy and possible enhanced p-wave component.

## Method

The authors derived decay width formulae within the extended R-matrix framework for sequential 2p decay, diproton decay, tri-body decay, and sequential two-diproton decay, treating three-body disintegration as a succession of two-body disintegrations. The diproton and tri-body formulae incorporate spectroscopic factors obtained from shell-model calculations via two-nucleon transfer amplitudes and Wigner single-particle reduced widths, with the channel radius set to 1.45(A₁¹ᐟ³ + A₁¹ᐟ³). For the tri-body case, the Hamiltonian assumes two emitted protons are spatially separated with negligible proton-proton interaction in the outer region, and the decay width depends explicitly on the resonance properties of the core+proton subsystem.

## Context

This work extends the R-matrix approach previously applied to 2p emission in ¹²O by Barker (1999, 2001), ⁴⁵Fe by Brown (2003), and ¹⁸Ne by Brown (2002), which had limited application due to simplified treatment of the decay process. It provides a realistic three-body extension that can consistently treat nuclear structure information via spectroscopic factors and combine with configuration-interaction calculations, in contrast to the three-body model by Grigorenko (2000, 2001) which considered only single-orbital wave functions. The method enables predictions for unobserved 2p radioactivity candidates in the region 22 < Z < 50, identifying ⁷¹Sr and ⁷⁴Zr as the most promising candidates for future experimental study, and can be extended to describe multi-proton emission such as the sequential 4p decay observed in ¹⁸Mg by Jin (2021).
