# Effect of limited statistics on higher order cumulants measurement in heavy-ion collision experiments

> We have studied the effect of limited statistics of data on measurement of the different order of cumulants of net-proton distribution assuming that the proton and antiproton distributions follow Possionian and Binomial distributions with initial parameters determined from experimental results for two top center of mass energies ($\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200$ and $62.4$ GeV) in most central ($0-5$%) Au$+$Au collisions at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). In this simulation, we observe that the central values for higher order cumulants have a strong dependence on event sample size and due to statistical randomness the central values of higher order cumulants could become negative. We also present a study on the determination of the statistical error on cumulants using delta theorem, bootstrap and sub-group methods and verified their suitability by employing a Monte Carlo procedure. Based on our study we find that the bootstrap method provides a robust way for statistical error estimation on high order cumulants. We also present the exclusion limits on the minimum event statistics needed for determination of cumulants if the signal strength (phase transition or critical point) is at a level of $5$% and $10$% above the statistical level. This study will help the experiments to arrive at the minimum required event statistics and choice of proper method for statistical error estimation for high order cumulant measurements.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that higher-order cumulants (up to \(C_7\)) of net-proton distributions in heavy-ion collisions can acquire negative central values purely due to limited event statistics and statistical randomness, even when the true underlying distribution has positive cumulants; this statistical artifact must be ruled out before any physical interpretation of negative cumulant signs as signals of a QCD crossover or critical point.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations generating net-proton distributions from two statistical models: a Poissonian model (Skellam distribution) and a Binomial model, using experimentally measured mean and variance of proton and antiproton distributions from STAR experiment for most central (0-5%) Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200\) and 62.4 GeV. They calculated cumulants up to \(7^{th}\) order as a function of event sample size (up to \(10^{10}\) events) and compared three statistical error estimation methods: delta theorem, bootstrap, and sub-group methods, verifying their reliability via a Monte Carlo procedure.

## Key numbers

- For the Poissonian model at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200\) GeV, \(C_6\) values were found to be \(-116.656\) for sample size \(10^5\) events and \(-2.201\) for sample size \(10^7\) events, while the true value was positive.
- Cumulants deviated from their true values in a range of \(0.005\sigma\) to \(2.1\sigma\) for the Poissonian model at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200\) GeV, and \(0.04\sigma\) to \(2.5\sigma\) at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=62.4\) GeV.
- For the Binomial model at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200\) GeV, cumulants deviated in a range of \(0.07\sigma\) to \(2.2\sigma\); at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=62.4\) GeV, deviations ranged from \(0.04\sigma\) to \(2.3\sigma\).
- In the sub-group method with 10000 sub-samples, the relative deviation from delta theorem errors reached nearly 150% for higher-order cumulants.
- For a sample size of \(10^6\) events, the delta theorem method satisfied Gaussian behavior with 67.9%, 69.2%, and 68.6% of samples lying within \(\pm1\sigma\) error for \(C_4\), \(C_6\), and \(C_8\) respectively.

## Context

This work extends the methodology used by the STAR experiment (STAR Collaboration, 2010-2014) for measuring higher-order cumulants of net-proton, net-charge, and net-kaon distributions in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. The study directly addresses the interpretation challenge raised by theoretical predictions from Friman et al. (2011) and Cheng et al. (2009) that negative sixth-order cumulants could signal a crossover phase transition, by showing that negative signs can arise from insufficient statistics alone. The results provide practical guidance for the high-statistics second phase of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan to determine minimum required event statistics and appropriate error estimation methods for cumulant measurements up to \(C_8\).
