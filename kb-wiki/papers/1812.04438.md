# Nuclear Astrophysics in the Multimessenger Era: A Partnership Made in Heaven

> On August 17, 2017 the LIGO-Virgo collaboration detected for the first time gravitational waves from the binary merger of two neutron stars (GW170817). Unlike the merger of two black holes, the associated electromagnetic radiation was also detected by a host of telescopes operating over a wide range of frequencies---opening a brand new era of multimessenger astronomy. This historical detection is providing fundamental new insights into the astrophysical site for the r-process and on the nature of dense matter. In this contribution we examine the impact of GW170817 on the equation of state of neutron rich matter, particularly on the density dependence of the symmetry energy. Limits on the tidal polarizability extracted from GW170817 seem to suggest that the symmetry energy is soft, thereby excluding models that predict overly large stellar radii.

## Key claim

GW170817's limits on the tidal polarizability suggest the symmetry energy is soft, thereby excluding models that predict overly large stellar radii, but this conclusion depends on the low-spin scenario assumed in the LIGO-Virgo analysis and on the specific set of relativistic models used.

## Method

The author computes neutron-star structure by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using equations of state from ten relativistic mean-field models that accurately describe finite nuclei and neutron stars. The tidal polarizability (dimensionless Lambda) is calculated from the second Love number and stellar compactness, then compared to the 90% probability contour extracted from the GW170817 discovery paper under the low-spin assumption.

## Key numbers

- Chirp mass of GW170817: 1.188 solar masses
- Upper limit on stellar radius for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 13.76 km
- Maximum stellar mass from combined electromagnetic and gravitational wave constraints: 2.17 solar masses
- Minimum radius of a 1.6 solar mass neutron star: 10.7 km
- Distance to GW170817: about 40 Mpc
- About 0.05 solar masses of r-process elements were synthesized in the event
- Peak gravitational strain measured: 10^{-21}

## Context

This work extends the analysis of GW170817 (Abbott 2017) to examine constraints on the equation of state of neutron-rich matter, particularly the density dependence of the symmetry energy. It builds on prior determinations of neutron-star radii that had large systematic uncertainties (Ozel 2010, Steiner 2010, Suleimanov 2010) and complements improved photometric techniques (Guillot 2013, Lattimer 2013, Heinke 2014, Ozel 2015, Watts 2016, Steiner 2017, Nattila 2017). The results enable future multimessenger observations, with the third LIGO-Virgo observing run scheduled for 2019 expected to provide many more neutron-star merger detections.
