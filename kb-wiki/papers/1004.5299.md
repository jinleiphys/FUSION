# Thermodynamics of dense matter in chiral approaches

> We discuss phases in dense hadronic and quark matter from chiral model approaches. Within PNJL models the phase diagram for various number of colors $N_c$ is studied. How phases are constrained in quantum field theories are also discussed along with the anomaly matching. An exotic phase with unbroken center symmetry of chiral group has a characteristic feature in the thermodynamics, which can be interpreted as one realization of the quarkyonic phase in QCD for $N_c=3$.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a chirally symmetric but confined phase (quarkyonic phase) may be realized in QCD for N_c=3, but this possibility is constrained by anomaly matching conditions and the chirality assignment to baryons, making it unlikely in standard QCD unless the physics around the Fermi surface alters the anomaly matching at finite density.

## Method
The author uses the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model to study the phase diagram of dense hadronic and quark matter for varying numbers of colors N_c, from the large N_c limit down to N_c=3. A Ginzburg-Landau free energy with 2-quark and 4-quark condensates is employed to explore a non-standard chiral symmetry breaking pattern where the center of the chiral group remains unbroken. The analysis also incorporates anomaly matching conditions to constrain possible phases.

## Key numbers
- For N_c=3, deconfinement and chiral crossover lines are on top of each other in a wide range of chemical potential μ.
- The pion decay constant is given by F_π = √(σ_0² + (8/3)χ_0²), where σ_0 and χ_0 are the expectation values of 2-quark and 4-quark scalar fields, respectively.
- Two tricritical points (TCPs) and one triple point appear in the phase diagram of the Ginzburg-Landau model: TCP₁ at (A,B)=(0,1/4) and TCP₂ at (A,B)=(1/4,-1/8), with the triple point T at (A,B)=(1/8,0).

## Context
This work extends the concept of the quarkyonic phase proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) using large N_c counting, and applies it to N_c=3 via PNJL models developed by Fukushima (2004) and others. The paper also discusses the non-standard chiral symmetry breaking pattern suggested by Stern (1998) and ruled out at zero density by Shifman (1984) due to QCD inequalities, but potentially viable at finite density. The results enable future studies of new critical points at low temperature and the role of 4-quark condensates in dense QCD, while raising open questions about baryon chirality assignment and the validity of PNJL model predictions.
