# Excitation energy of superdeformed bands in Relativistic Mean Field Theory

> Constrained Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) calculations have been carried out to estimate excitation energies relative to the ground state for superdeformed bands in the mass regions A $\sim$ 190 and A $\sim$ 150. It is shown that RMF theory is able to successfully reproduce the recently measured superdeformed minima in Hg and Pb nuclei.

# Excitation energy of superdeformed bands in Relativistic Mean Field Theory

## Key claim

Constrained Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory, using the NL3 parameter set, reproduces the experimentally measured excitation energies of superdeformed minima in \(^{194}\)Hg and \(^{194}\)Pb with higher precision than non-relativistic calculations (Skyrme, Gogny, Woods-Saxon), but the calculations stay strictly in the mean field approximation without subtracting spurious rotational contributions, which would affect the energy differences between minima.

## Method

Constrained RMF calculations were performed by imposing a quadratic constraint on the quadrupole moment to trace the potential energy surface as a function of deformation. Pairing correlations were included within the BCS formalism, with monopole pairing constants determined from experimental odd-even mass differences and applied self-consistently at each deformation. The calculations used the NL1 and NL3 parameter sets with an axially deformed code.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy \(E_x\) of the superdeformed minimum in \(^{194}\)Hg: 5.62 MeV (NL1), 6 MeV (NL3); experimental value is 6.017 MeV.
- Excitation energy \(E_x\) of the superdeformed minimum in \(^{194}\)Pb: 4.53 MeV (NL3); experimental value is 4.471(6) MeV.
- Depth of the superdeformed isomeric well in \(^{194}\)Hg: 2.66 MeV (NL1), 1.25 MeV (NL3).
- Depth of the superdeformed isomeric well in \(^{194}\)Pb: 2.2 MeV (NL3).
- Ground state binding energy for \(^{194}\)Hg: 1535.90 MeV (NL3), 1540.31 MeV (NL1); experimental value is 1535.50 MeV.
- Ground state binding energy for \(^{192}\)Hg: 1519.87 MeV (NL3), 1524.99 MeV (NL1); experimental value is 1519.20 MeV.
- Excitation energy \(E_x\) for \(^{146}\)Gd: 10.63 MeV (NL3), with a superdeformed minimum depth of 0.66 MeV.
- Excitation energy \(E_x\) for \(^{192}\)Hg: 3.93 MeV (NL1), 4.46 MeV (NL3).

## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic theoretical predictions for superdeformed excitation energies, including Hartree-Fock calculations with Skyrme forces (Krieger 1992) and Gogny interactions (Delaroche 1989), as well as Strutinsky calculations with a Woods-Saxon potential (Satula 1991). The RMF results improve upon these by achieving better agreement with the first direct measurements of superdeformed band excitation energies in \(^{194}\)Hg (Khoo 1996) and \(^{194}\)Pb (Brinkman 1996). The predictions for rare earth nuclei (Gd, Dy isotopes) provide benchmarks for future experiments (Lieder 1996) and enable comparisons of barrier heights and tunneling probabilities between superdeformed and normal deformed minima.
