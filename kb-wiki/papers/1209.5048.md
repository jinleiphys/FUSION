# Strength of reduced two-body spin-orbit interaction from chiral three-nucleon force

> The contribution of a chiral three-nucleon force to the strength of an effective spin-orbit coupling is estimated. We first construct a reduced two-body interaction by folding one-nucleon degrees of freedom of the three-nucleon force in nuclear matter. The spin-orbit strength is evaluated by a Scheerbaum factor obtained by the $G$-matrix calculation in nuclear matter with the two-nucleon interaction plus the reduced two-nucleon interaction. The problem of the insufficiency of modern realistic two-nucleon interactions to account for the empirical spin-orbit strength is resolved. It is also indicated that the spin-orbit coupling is weaker in the neutron-rich environment. Because the spin-orbit component from the three-nucleon force is determined by the low-energy constants fixed in the two-nucleon sector, there is little uncertainty in the present estimation.

## Key claim

The chiral three-nucleon force (3NF) contribution to the effective spin-orbit strength, evaluated via a reduced two-body interaction folded in nuclear matter, resolves the long-standing insufficiency of modern realistic two-nucleon interactions to account for the empirical spin-orbit strength; the additional spin-orbit coupling is predicted to be weaker in neutron-rich environments.

## Method

The author first constructs a reduced two-body interaction by folding one-nucleon degrees of freedom of the chiral 3NF in nuclear matter, using the lowest-order Brueckner theory (LOBT) with continuous intermediate spectra. The spin-orbit strength is then evaluated via the Scheerbaum factor \(B_S(\bar{q})\) obtained from \(G\)-matrix calculations in nuclear matter, combining the two-nucleon interaction (AV18, NSC97, CD-Bonn, or Jülich N\(^3\)LO) with the reduced two-nucleon interaction from the 3NF. Partial-wave decomposition of the spin-orbit components from the \(c_1\) and \(c_3\) terms of the chiral 3NF is performed, and a form factor with cutoff \(\Lambda = 550\) MeV is introduced for the reduced interaction.

## Key numbers

- Modern two-nucleon interactions alone give Scheerbaum factors \(B_S(\bar{q})\) of 88.4 (AV18), 88.6 (NSC97), 93.3 (CD-Bonn), and 87.1 (N\(^3\)LO) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\) at \(k_F = 1.35\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(\bar{q} = 0.7\) fm\(^{-1}\), about 25% below the empirical value of \(\sim 120\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\).
- Including the reduced 3NF contribution raises \(B_S(\bar{q})\) to 123.2 MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\) at \(k_F = 1.35\) fm\(^{-1}\) for the N\(^3\)LO+3NF combination.
- At half density (\(k_F = 1.07\) fm\(^{-1}\)), \(B_S(\bar{q})\) increases from 88.1 (N\(^3\)LO alone) to 110.8 MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\) (N\(^3\)LO+3NF).
- In pure neutron matter at \(k_F^n = 1.35\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(B_S(\bar{q})\) is 84.7 MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\) without and 93.5 MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\) with the reduced 3NF; at \(k_F^n = 1.07\) fm\(^{-1}\), the values are 87.0 and 94.6 MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations by Kaiser and collaborators (Kaiser et al., 2002; Kaiser, 2003; Kaiser, 2004) who studied nuclear spin-orbit coupling in chiral perturbation theory, finding large cancellations between iterated one-pion exchange and the 3NF. It also builds on the Scheerbaum formulation (Scheerbaum, 1976) and the Illinois group's demonstration (Pieper and Pandharipande, 1993) that their 3NF contributes substantially to spin-orbit splitting in \(^{15}\)N. The results enable more reliable predictions of shell structure in neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line, where a decreasing spin-orbit interaction is observed (Schiffer et al., 2004).
