# Orbital angular momenta of quarks and gluons in the nucleon -- model-dependent versus model-independent extractions

> We demonstrate that there exist two kinds of gauge-invariant decompositions of the nucleon spin, which are physically inequivalent. The quark and gluon orbital angular momenta (OAMs) appearing in one decomposition are basically the canonical OAMs, while the quark and gluon OAMs appearing in another decomposition are called the dynamical OAMs. It is shown that the dynamical OAMs of quarks and gluons in the nucleon can be related to definite high-energy deep-inelastic-scattering observables. On the other hand, we conjecture that the canonical OAMs of quarks and gluon in the nucleon are model-dependent quantities, which is meaningful only within a specific theoretical model of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper argues that there exist two physically inequivalent gauge-invariant decompositions of the nucleon spin, producing two distinct types of quark and gluon orbital angular momenta (OAMs): "canonical OAMs" (which are model-dependent and meaningful only within a specific theoretical model) and "dynamical OAMs" (which can be related to definite high-energy deep-inelastic-scattering observables). The author recommends the decomposition yielding dynamical OAMs as the model-independent, observable choice.

## Method

The author constructs two covariant gauge-invariant decompositions of the QCD angular-momentum tensor, based on splitting the gluon field into physical and pure-gauge parts following the approach of Chen et al. (2008, 2009). Decomposition (I) uses the full covariant derivative in the quark OAM operator and includes a "potential angular momentum" term in the gluon OAM; this decomposition is shown to connect to generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs). Decomposition (II) uses a pure-gauge covariant derivative and reduces, after gauge fixing, to the decompositions of Bashinsky-Jaffe (1999), Chen et al. (2008, 2009), and Jaffe-Manohar (1990), which the author argues are all gauge-equivalent but not directly observable. The author also analyzes the MIT bag model and the deuteron as analogies to illustrate the model dependence of canonical OAM.

## Key numbers

* Quark polarization was fairly precisely determined to be around 1/3 of the nucleon spin.
* In the MIT bag model, the quark OAM for the isoscalar combination (u+d) is given by ⟨L³_Q⟩ = (2/3) P_P, where P_P = ∫₀ᴿ [g(r)]² r² dr is the probability from the lower-component radial wave function.
* In the MIT bag model, the full nucleon spin decomposition yields ⟨J₃⟩ = ⟨L³_Q⟩ + (1/2)⟨Σ³_Q⟩ = (2/3) P_P + (1/2)(P_S - (1/3) P_P) = 1/2.
* For the deuteron, the angular momentum decomposition gives ⟨J₃⟩ = ⟨L₃⟩ + ⟨S₃⟩ = (3/2) P_D + (P_S - (1/2) P_D) = 1, where P_D is the D-state probability.

## Context

This work extends the controversy over gauge-invariant nucleon spin decompositions initiated by Chen et al. (2008, 2009) and builds on the author's own prior papers (Wakamatsu 2010, 2011A, 2011B). It contrasts the Jaffe-Manohar (1990) decomposition (canonical OAMs, not separately gauge-invariant) with the Ji (1997) decomposition (gauge-invariant but unable to separate gluon spin from gluon OAM). The paper enables a path to model-independent extraction of dynamical quark and gluon OAMs via combined analysis of GPD measurements and polarized DIS measurements, resolving a long-standing question about the gauge-invariance of the gluon spin operator.
