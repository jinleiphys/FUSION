# Heavy-Flavor Fragmentation: The QCD Portal to Exotic Matter

> We investigate the core dynamics behind exotic matter formation via the TQ4Q1.1 set of collinear fragmentation functions for fully charmed or bottomed tetraquarks in three quantum configurations: scalar ($0^{++}$), axial vector ($1^{+-}$), and tensor ($2^{++}$). We adopt leading-power single-parton fragmentation within a nonrelativistic QCD framework tailored to tetraquark Fock states. Initial-scale inputs are constructed from updated gluon- and heavy-quark channels, and evolved through threshold-consistent DGLAP within HF-NRevo. We present the first systematic propagation of uncertainties from color-composite long-distance matrix elements governing tetraquark hadronization. This study advances the connection between hadronic structure, precision QCD, and exotic matter.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first systematic propagation of uncertainties from color-composite long-distance matrix elements governing tetraquark hadronization, specifically for the TQ4Q1.1 set of collinear fragmentation functions for fully charmed or bottomed tetraquarks, but this is limited to leading-power single-parton fragmentation within a nonrelativistic QCD framework and does not include light-quark or nonconstituent heavy-quark channels.

## Method

The authors adopt leading-power single-parton fragmentation within a nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) framework tailored to tetraquark Fock states, constructing initial-scale inputs from updated gluon- and heavy-quark channels. These inputs are evolved through threshold-consistent DGLAP evolution using the HF-NRevo scheme, which handles the multi-threshold structure by first evolving the gluon fragmentation function analytically from 4m_Q to 5m_Q via the LO P_gg kernel, then matching to the NRQCD input for the (anti)quark channel at the heavy-quark threshold, followed by full NLO DGLAP evolution including all active parton species. Uncertainties from missing higher-order contributions in the fragmentation sector are estimated through variations of the initial evolution scale by a factor of two around its central value, with each variation independently evolved to produce an uncertainty envelope.

## Key numbers

- Initial scale for gluon fragmentation into fully heavy tetraquarks: μ_F,0(g → T_4c) = 4m_Q
- Initial scale for (anti)quark fragmentation into fully heavy tetraquarks: μ_F,0(Q → T_4c) = 5m_Q
- Fragmentation functions presented for μ_F values ranging from 30 to 90 GeV for the tensor tetraquark T_4c(2^++)
- The gluon FF is initialized at μ_F,0 = 4m_Q and evolved up to μ_F,0 = 5m_Q through single-channel dynamics

## Context

This work extends the NRQCD-based description of fragmentation from quarkonia and B_c mesons (Braaten 1993, Zheng 2019, Celiberto 2022) to exotic multiquark systems, building on initial NRQCD inputs for gluon fragmentation into fully heavy tetraquarks derived by Feng (2020). It follows the development of the TQHL1.0 sets for heavy-light tetraquarks (Celiberto 2023, 2024) and the TQ4Q1.x and TQHL1.1 releases (Celiberto 2024, 2025), which incorporate NRQCD-based inputs for both gluon and heavy-quark channels. The TQ4Q1.1 functions provide the first robust and publicly available description of fully heavy tetraquark fragmentation, enabling systematic exploration of exotic production at the LHC, its high-luminosity upgrade, and future colliders.
