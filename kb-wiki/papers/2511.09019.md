# The color force acting on a quark in the pion and nucleon

> In the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) of hard scattering amplitudes, the twist-3 operators describe local colored Lorentz forces acting on a quark, thereby providing a measure of the strength of the gluon fields. Its value is directly accessible from the nucleon twist-3 polarized $g_2$-parton distribution function. In the semiclassical (instanton-based) QCD vacuum models, the leading non-perturbative contribution stems from correlated instanton-anti-instanton pairs, or molecules. We analyze the magnitude of the color force on a struck quark in light hadrons (pion and nucleon), in the context of the instanton liquid model (ILM). We derive explicitly the pertinent form factors associated with the color Lorentz force and show that they are intimately related to the pertinent hadronic gravitational and transversity form factors. Using the ILM enhanced by molecules, we detail the ensuing colored force distribution in the transverse plane for the luminal pions and nucleons. The results for the nucleons are in good agreement with those recently reported from a lattice collaboration.

## Key claim

The color Lorentz force on a struck quark in the nucleon, extracted from twist-3 parton distribution functions, is much larger than earlier estimates (about 0.1 GeV/fm) and is instead comparable to or even exceeding the confining string tension, with values of 2–3 GeV/fm, but this result is model-dependent because the instanton liquid model (ILM) does not incorporate confinement explicitly and relies on phenomenological input for the instanton ensemble parameters.

## Method

The authors compute the color Lorentz force operator \(\bar{q} g G^{+i} \gamma^+ q\) in the semiclassical instanton liquid model (ILM) of the QCD vacuum, using both individual instantons/anti-instantons and correlated instanton-anti-instanton ("molecular") pairs. They construct explicit gauge field configurations via a ratio ansatz for the \(I\bar I\) molecules, evaluate the quark zero-mode overlap integrals \(T_{I\bar I}\) numerically, and perform Monte Carlo averaging over 4-dimensional orientations and positions of the molecules relative to the quark path to obtain the integrated color-electric field kick. The force is then related to the twist-3 parton distribution \(g_T(x)\) and its second Mellin moment \(d_2\), and connected to hadronic gravitational and transversity form factors.

## Key numbers

* The average instanton density in the ILM is \(n_{I+\bar{I}} \approx 1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-4}\) and the typical instanton size is \(\rho \sim 1/3\ \mathrm{fm}\) (from Eq. (4)).
* The molecular density from lattice studies is \(n_{\mathrm{mol}} \rho^4 \approx 0.10\)–\(0.15\) (Athenodorou 2018), leading to a force estimate \(F \approx (2\)–\(3)\ \mathrm{GeV/fm}\).
* The integrated color-electric field kick from Monte Carlo averaging is \(\langle \int E d\tau \rangle \approx (4.15 \pm 0.21)/\rho\).
* The lattice result from the Adelaide group (Crawford 2024) gives \(F_u \approx 3\ \mathrm{GeV/fm}\) and \(F_d = 0. \pm 0.05\ \mathrm{GeV/fm}\).
* The earlier large-\(N_c\) estimate (Burkardt 2008) was \(F_u = -F_d \sim 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV/fm}\), an order of magnitude smaller than the confining string tension \(\sigma \approx 1\ \mathrm{GeV/fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical instanton-based approach to QCD vacuum structure (Shuryak 1981, Diakonov 1985) by including correlated instanton-anti-instanton molecules, which were previously applied to pion form factors (Shuryak 2020) and heavy quark potentials (Shuryak 2021, Miesch 2024). The results agree with recent lattice extractions of twist-3 nucleon matrix elements (Crawford 2024, Adelaide group) and contradict the earlier large-\(N_c\) estimate of Burkardt (2008). The framework enables a direct connection between non-perturbative vacuum dynamics and experimentally accessible twist-3 parton distribution functions, and shows that the color Lorentz force is intimately related to hadronic gravitational and transversity form factors.
