# Lattice gauge theory with staggered fermions: how, where, and why (not)

> Many results from lattice QCD of broad importance to particle and nuclear physics are obtained with 2+1 flavors of staggered sea quarks. In the continuum limit, staggered fermions yield four species, called tastes. To reduce the number of tastes to one (per flavor), the simulation employs the fourth root of the four-taste staggered fermion determinant. This talk surveys evidence in favor of this procedure, refutes recent criticisms, and reviews recent algorithmic and technical improvements. Physics results are covered in other plenary talks.

## Key claim

The talk surveys evidence that the fourth-root procedure for staggered fermions is valid for lattice QCD simulations, while acknowledging that the procedure remains theoretically controversial and that critics have raised non-perturbative concerns that the author argues are not fatal flaws.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical foundations of staggered fermions, including their doubling symmetries, taste quantum numbers, and chiral properties, then examines the fourth-root procedure that reduces the four tastes per flavor to one. It refutes specific criticisms by Creutz (2006-2007) using arguments based on the Symanzik effective field theory, chiral perturbation theory, and numerical evidence from eigenvalue spectra and hadron spectrum calculations.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD calculations from 2003 achieved agreement with experiment at the 1-3% level for a wide variety of physical quantities
- The naive fermion action yields 16 species in four dimensions; staggered fermions reduce this to 4 species
- The beta-function for staggered fermions starts with β₀ = 11/3 N_c - 2/3 × 4 n_f, confirming the four-species interpretation
- Pseudoscalar meson masses follow m²_{aξ} = 2m_q B + a²Δ_ξ + μ²δ_{a0}δ_{ξI}, where Δ_P = 0 due to exact chiral symmetries
- The anomalous taste-singlet axial current extends over a hypercube of size 2⁴, with explicit construction involving sums over 4-dimensional diagonals

## Context

This plenary talk from Lattice 2007 surveys the status of staggered fermions following the successful 2003 HPQCD/MILC/Fermilab calculations that agreed with experiment at 1-3% level. It extends the theoretical framework developed by Sharatchandra (1981), Golterman and Smit (1984-1986), and the Symanzik effective field theory analysis by Lepage (1998) and Lee and Sharpe (1999). The talk refutes specific criticisms by Creutz (2006-2007) and responds to the broader controversy about the fourth-root procedure, arguing that the wider lattice community should engage in open scientific debate rather than sideline sniping. The work enables continued use of the fastest available technique for sea quarks during the LHC era, while motivating further theoretical understanding and development of cleaner fermion formulations.
