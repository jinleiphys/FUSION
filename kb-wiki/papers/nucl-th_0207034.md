# Low Energy Expansion in the Three Body System to All Orders and the Triton Channel

> We extend and systematise the power counting for the three-body system, in the context of the ``pion-less'' Effective Field Theory approach, to all orders in the low-energy expansion. We show that a sub-leading part of the three-body force appears at the third order and delineate how the expansion proceeds at higher orders. After discussing the renormalisation issues in a simple bosonic model, we compute the phase shifts for neutron-deuteron scattering in the doublet S wave (triton) channel and compare our results with phase shift analysis and potential model calculations.

## Key claim

The authors extend the power counting for the three-body system in pionless Effective Field Theory to all orders in the low-energy expansion, showing that a sub-leading three-body force appears at third order (NNLO) rather than at leading or next-to-leading order, and that the expansion proceeds with new three-body forces entering every two orders.

## Method

The authors first analyze renormalization in a bosonic model with a shallow two-body bound state, using a sharp momentum cutoff regulator and solving the Faddeev integral equation. They determine which three-body force terms are required at each order by demanding that the cutoff dependence of the amplitude cancels order by order in the low-energy expansion, exploiting the fact that the integral equation kernel has a nearly singular operator due to a zero mode. They then generalize to the three-nucleon system in the doublet S-wave (triton) channel, including spin and isospin degrees of freedom, and compute the neutron-deuteron phase shift at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) by solving the modified integral equation with the deuteron propagator expanded perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- The zero mode eigenvalue corresponds to $s_0 = 1.00624\dots$ (dimensionless), characterizing the asymptotic behavior of the three-body amplitude.
- The leading three-body force $H_0(\Lambda)$ runs with cutoff as $H(\Lambda) = \frac{\sin[s_0 \ln\frac{\Lambda}{\bar{\Lambda}} + \arctan s_0]}{\sin[s_0 \ln\frac{\Lambda}{\bar{\Lambda}} - \arctan s_0]}$.
- The effective range $\rho$ is assumed to be of natural size, $\rho \sim R$, where $R$ is the short-distance scale.
- The binding momentum of the shallow two-body bound state is $\gamma = \sqrt{M B_2}$, where $B_2$ is the two-body binding energy.

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT approach to three-body systems previously developed for leading order (Bedaque, Hammer, van Kolck, 1999) and next-to-leading order (Bedaque, Grießhammer, Hammer, 2000) calculations of the doublet S-wave neutron-deuteron phase shift and triton binding energy. It systematizes the power counting to all orders, enabling higher-precision calculations that can be compared with phase shift analysis (e.g., the work of the Bochum group) and potential model calculations. The results enable credible error estimates for three-nucleon observables and provide a framework for including external electroweak currents systematically.
