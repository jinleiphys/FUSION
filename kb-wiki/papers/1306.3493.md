# A Heavy Ion Fireball freeze-out Dipion Cocktail for Au-Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV $p_t$ dependence(Part 2)

> In this paper we use methods developed in Part 1 of The Dipion Cocktail, to fit the $p_t$ dependence of dipions for mid-central Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV. For the minijet fragmentation part we use PYTHIA fragmentation as described in Part 1. For the thermal resonance production we use an exponential growth behavior. The interference between the direct production of dipion pairs from non-resonance minijet fragmentation and re-scattering through resonance states gives a measure of the size of the re-scattering region. This size is contain in the $α$ parameter of Part1. We assumed a relationship between the $α$ parameter and the mass shift of the $ρ$ resonance. The data used for the fits comes from the RHIC collider as measured in the STAR experiment.

## Key claim
The paper applies the Dipion Cocktail formalism from Part 1 to fit the transverse momentum dependence of dipion pairs in mid-central Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV, assuming a relationship between the re-scattering parameter alpha and the mass shift of the rho resonance. The key hedge is that the sigma meson Breit-Wigner parameters were fixed because they were ill-determined, and the minijet fragmentation from PYTHIA was allowed to be free in the fits due to large uncertainties in other threshold effects.

## Method
The author used a two-component model combining thermal resonance production (with exponential Boltzmann behavior) and PYTHIA minijet fragmentation, with interference between direct non-resonance dipion production and re-scattering through resonance states. The formalism was applied to 19 dipion transverse momentum bins from 0.2 to 4.0 GeV/c for STAR data on Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV with 40% to 80% centrality, fitting Breit-Wigner parameters for sigma (l=0), rho(770) (l=1), and f2(1270) (l=2) resonances plus thermal terms for f0, Ks, and omega.

## Key numbers
- The rho width was found to be always 0.147 GeV with an error of +/- 0.007 GeV across all fits
- The rho mass decreases as alpha grows, reaching a minimum of 0.738 GeV at alpha=0.907, corresponding to a mass shift of 37 MeV from the vacuum value
- At alpha=0.504 (the smallest alpha found), the best fit rho mass is 0.775 GeV
- The f0 was fitted with mass = 0.9727 +/- 0.0039 GeV and width = 0.04512 +/- 0.01128 GeV
- The f2(1270) mass was fixed at 1.275 GeV and width at 0.185 GeV from PDG
- The sigma mass was fixed at 1.011 GeV and width at 1.015 GeV
- The re-scattering radius ranges from 0.739 fm at pt=0.3 GeV/c to 0.865 fm at pt=2.3 GeV/c and above
- The alpha parameter ranges from 0.907 +/- 0.028 at pt=0.3 GeV/c down to 0.504 +/- 0.016 at pt=2.3 GeV/c and above

## Context
This work extends the Dipion Cocktail formalism developed in Part 1 (Longacre, 2013) from the dipion mass spectrum to the transverse momentum dependence of dipions in heavy ion collisions. The approach builds on the concept that both thermal hadron production and minijet fragmentation contribute to the final state, with interference effects sensitive to the re-scattering region size. The results enable extraction of the rho meson in-medium mass shift as a function of transverse momentum, suggesting a density wave effect around dipion pt of 0.6 to 1.0 GeV/c that may relate to fireball expansion dynamics through Hubble flow.
