# Towards the Finite Temperature Gluon Propagator in Landau Gauge Yang-Mills Theory

> Yang-Mills theories undergo a deconfining phase transition at a critical temperature. In lattice calculations the temporal Wilson loop and Z_3 order parameter show above this temperature a behavior typical of deconfinement. A quantity of interest in the study of this transition is the gluon propagator and its evolution with temperature. This contribution describes the current status of an investigation of the finite temperature gluon propagator in Landau gauge. It analyzes the high temperature case. The resulting equations are compared to the corresponding ones of three-dimensional Yang-Mills theory. Under certain assumptions it is found that a kind of spatial "confinement" is still present, even at very high temperatures.

## Key claim

Under the assumption that the three-dimensional longitudinal part of the gluon propagator can be neglected at high temperature, the transverse part of the finite-temperature gluon propagator in Landau gauge becomes identical to the three-dimensional gluon propagator, implying that a kind of spatial "confinement" persists even at very high temperatures above the deconfinement transition.

## Method

The authors employ Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) truncated at the level of two-point functions with bare vertices, extended to finite temperature using the Matsubara formalism. They analyze the high-temperature limit by retaining only the lowest Matsubara frequency and compare the resulting equations for the transverse and longitudinal gluon dressing functions to the corresponding DSEs of three-dimensional Yang-Mills theory. Spurious quadratic divergences from gauge-symmetry violation are removed using generalized projectors with parameters ξ and ζ.

## Key numbers

- The zero-temperature ghost dressing function diverges with an exponent κ = 0.5953 in k², and the gluon dressing function vanishes at zero momentum with an exponent 2κ.
- The spurious quadratic divergences in the finite-temperature gluon DSE are removed for projector parameters ξ = 0 and ζ = 3.
- In the high-temperature limit, the three-dimensional coupling constant is identified as g²₃ = g²T, where T is the temperature.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature DSE calculations of von Smekal (1997) and Alkofer (2000) to finite temperature, where those calculations showed good agreement with lattice results from Langfeld (2002). The high-temperature analysis compares to three-dimensional Yang-Mills theory, which is known to confine (Zwanziger, 2001). The finding that spatial confinement may persist at high temperatures corresponds to lattice results from Bali (1993) showing that spatial Wilson loops remain confining even when temporal Wilson loops indicate deconfinement. The next step is to obtain full numerical solutions of the finite-temperature DSEs, which are stated to be under way.
