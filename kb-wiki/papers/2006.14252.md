# Relaxation time approximation with pair production and annihilation processes

> We extend the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation to explicitly include transitions between particles forming an interacting mixture. Using the detailed balance condition as well as conditions of energy-momentum and current conservation, we show that only two independent relaxation time scales are allowed in such an interacting system. Dissipative hydrodynamic equations and the form of transport coefficients is subsequently derived for this case. We find that the shear and bulk viscosity coefficients, as well as the baryon charge conductivity are independent of the transition time scale. However, the bulk viscosity and conductivity coefficients that can be attributed to the individual components of the mixture depend on the transition time.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) to explicitly include pair production and annihilation processes between quarks and gluons, showing that only two independent relaxation time scales are allowed in such an interacting system. The shear and bulk viscosity coefficients, as well as the baryon charge conductivity, are found to be independent of the transition time scale, while the bulk viscosity and conductivity coefficients attributed to individual components of the mixture depend on the transition time.

## Method

The authors formulate a set of coupled RTA Boltzmann equations for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons that include additional inelastic-interaction terms for the processes \(g \leftrightarrow q\bar{q}\). Using the detailed balance condition, energy-momentum conservation, and net baryon current conservation, they reduce the five apparent relaxation times to two independent scales: \(\tau_{\rm eq}\) (equilibration) and \(\tau_{\rm tr}\) (transition). They then derive first-order gradient corrections to the distribution functions by inverting the relaxation matrix and obtain the Navier-Stokes relations and transport coefficients for the interacting mixture.

## Key numbers

* The constraint from global equilibrium gives \(\tau^{q\rightarrow g}_{\rm tr} = 2\tau^{g\rightarrow q}_{\rm tr}\,\cosh{\alpha_0}\), where \(\alpha_0\) is the constant value of \(\mu/T\) in global equilibrium.
* The constraint from local equilibrium gives \(\tau^{q\rightarrow g}_{\rm tr} = 2\tau^{g\rightarrow q}_{\rm tr}\,\cosh{\alpha}\), with \(\alpha \equiv \mu/T\).
* The transition times are parameterized as \(\tau^{q\rightarrow g}_{\rm tr} = \tau_{\rm tr}\,\sinh{2\alpha}\) and \(\tau^{g\rightarrow q}_{\rm tr} = \tau_{\rm tr}\,\sinh{\alpha}\).
* The effective relaxation time for the distribution \(S^-\) is \(\tau^{\rm eff}_{\rm eq} = \tau_{\rm eq} \,\frac{\gamma}{\gamma + \frac{r}{\sinh\alpha} + \frac{1}{\sinh 2\alpha}}\), where \(\gamma = \tau_{\rm tr}/\tau_{\rm eq}\) and \(r = g_q/g_g\).
* The natural range for \(\alpha\) is \(|\alpha| < 4\), since for larger values the entropy density may become negative.

## Context

This work extends the standard RTA Boltzmann equation (Anderson 1974) to explicitly include inelastic transitions between quarks and gluons, building on earlier studies of anisotropic distribution functions in quark-gluon mixtures (Florkowski 2015). The approach enables the derivation of dissipative hydrodynamic equations and transport coefficients for systems where elastic and inelastic timescales are comparable, complementing earlier work on simplified space-time geometries (Florkowski 2013, Florkowski 2016). The framework can be extended to study the effects of inelastic processes on the hydrodynamic evolution of quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions.
