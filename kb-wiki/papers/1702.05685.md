# Highly magnetized neutron stars in a many-body forces formalism

> In this work, we study the effects of different magnetic field configurations in neutron stars described by a many-body forces formalism (MBF model). The MBF model is a relativistic mean field formalism that takes into account many-body forces by means of a meson field dependence of the nuclear interaction coupling constants. We choose the best parametrization of the model that reproduces nuclear matter properties at saturation and also describes massive neutron stars. We assume matter to be in beta-equilibrium, charge neutral and at zero temperature. Magnetic fields are taken into account both in the equation of state and in the structure of the stars by the self-consistent solution of the Einstein-Maxwell equations. We assume a poloidal magnetic field distribution and calculate its effects on neutron stars, showing its influence on the gravitational mass and deformation of the stars.

## Key claim

Magnetic field effects on the equation of state (Landau quantization) produce no significant impact on the gravitational mass and radius of magnetized neutron stars; only the magnetic field effects on the macroscopic structure of the stars are significant for determining these global properties, contrary to some earlier conclusions in the literature.

## Method

The authors used the many-body forces (MBF) relativistic mean-field model with a fixed parametrization (zeta = 0.040) that reproduces nuclear matter saturation properties and supports massive neutron stars. They solved the coupled Einstein-Maxwell equations assuming a stationary, axi-symmetric spacetime with a poloidal magnetic field distribution, including magnetic effects both in the equation of state (via Landau quantization of charged particle energy levels) and in the star structure, for beta-equilibrium, charge-neutral matter at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}, binding energy B/A = -15.75 MeV, symmetry energy J_0 = 32 MeV, slope L_0 = 97 MeV, compressibility K_0 = 297 MeV, effective nucleon mass m*_n = 0.66 m_n
- For a star with baryon mass M_b = 2.2 M_sun and current function j_0 = 3.5 x 10^15 A/m^2, the equatorial radius reaches r_eq = 1.7 r_p (polar radius)
- Surface magnetic fields B_s for M_b = 2.2 M_sun stars range from 1.03 x 10^17 G (j_0 = 1.0 x 10^15 A/m^2) to 3.80 x 10^17 G (j_0 = 3.5 x 10^15 A/m^2)
- Central magnetic fields B_c for the same stars range from 3.48 x 10^17 G (j_0 = 1.0 x 10^15 A/m^2) to 8.09 x 10^17 G (j_0 = 3.5 x 10^15 A/m^2)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetic effects in neutron stars by applying the MBF model to nucleonic matter, building on the formalism of Bonazzola (1993) and Bocquet (1995) for solving the Einstein-Maxwell equations with a poloidal field. The results agree with earlier findings for quark stars (Chatterjee, 2014) and hybrid stars (Franzon, 2015) that magnetic effects on the equation of state are negligible compared to structural effects, and the authors note that neglecting deformation for fields above about 10^17 G leads to considerable errors in mass and radius determinations.
