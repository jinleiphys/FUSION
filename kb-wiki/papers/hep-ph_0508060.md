# Electromagnetic Nucleon-to-Delta Transition in Chiral Effective-Field Theory

> We perform a relativistic chiral effective-field theory calculation of the pion electroproduction off the nucleon ($e^- N \to e^- N π$) in the Delta(1232)-resonance region. After fixing the three low-energy constants, corresponding to the magnetic (M1), electric (E2), and Coulomb (C2) $γNΔ$ couplings, our calculation provides a prediction for the momentum-transfer and pion-mass dependence of the $γNΔ$ form factors. The prediction for the pion-mass dependence resolves the discrepancy between the recent lattice QCD results and the experimental value for the "C2/M1 ratio" at low $Q^2$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the apparent discrepancy between lattice QCD results and experimental data for the C2/M1 ratio of the nucleon-to-Delta transition at low momentum transfer is resolved when non-analytic pion-mass dependencies are included, contrary to the linear extrapolation used in the lattice analysis.

## Method

The authors performed a relativistic chiral effective-field theory (chi-EFT) calculation of pion electroproduction off the nucleon (e- N → e- N π) in the Delta(1232)-resonance region, using the delta-expansion power-counting scheme where the Delta-nucleon mass splitting (Δ ≈ 0.3 GeV) is treated as a light scale. They computed the amplitude to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the delta expansion, including one-loop vertex corrections and self-energy diagrams, and fixed three low-energy constants (g_M, g_E, g_C) corresponding to the magnetic (M1), electric (E2), and Coulomb (C2) gamma-N-Delta couplings by fitting to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The Delta-nucleon mass splitting used as a light scale: Δ ≡ M_Δ - M ≈ 0.3 GeV, with heavy scale Λ ∼ 1 GeV, giving expansion parameter δ = Δ/Λ
- The pion decay constant: f_π ≈ 92.4 MeV
- The nucleon and Delta masses: M ≈ 0.939 GeV, M_Δ ≈ 1.232 GeV
- The axial coupling h_A fixed to h_A ≈ 2.85 from the experimental Delta-resonance width
- Best-fit low-energy constants: g_M = 2.88, g_E = -1.04, g_C = -2.36
- The cross section results shown at Q^2 = 0.127 GeV^2 and sqrt(s) = 1.232 GeV
- The theoretical uncertainty estimated as R_err = 2|R_av| · (1/3) · (δ^2 + m_π/M + Q^2/M^2)

## Context

This work extends previous chiral effective-field theory approaches by using the delta-expansion scheme (Pascalutsa and Phillips, 2003) which treats the Delta-nucleon mass splitting and pion mass at different orders, unlike earlier schemes (small-scale expansion) that counted them equally. The calculation reconciles lattice QCD results (Alexandrou et al., 2005) with experimental data from MAMI and MIT-Bates, showing that the linear extrapolation in m_π^2 used in the lattice analysis misses crucial non-analytic behavior at the physical pion mass where the Delta → π N decay channel opens. This enables future lattice QCD calculations to be properly connected to experimental observables through chiral extrapolation.
