# HBT with Space- vs. Time-like Hydrodynamic Freezeout

> Bose-Einstein correlations in relativistic heavy ion collisions and their dependence on the freeze-out condition in hydrodynamic models is compared to time-like freeze-out, where particles are emitted away only from the surface, i.e. space- vs. time-like freeze-out. The corresponding HBT radii are calculated for the two models emphasizing the difference in the outward HBT radius.

# HBT with Space- vs. Time-like Hydrodynamic Freezeout

## Key claim

The outward HBT radius differs significantly between the Cooper & Frye space-like freeze-out assumption and the Sinyukov time-like freeze-out assumption, but only when the freeze-out surface is static or slowly moving; for a long-lived mixed phase (as in some RHIC hydrodynamic calculations), the duration of emission dominates the outward radius and the choice of freeze-out assumption becomes less important.

## Method

The author compares two freeze-out prescriptions for hydrodynamic models of relativistic heavy ion collisions: the Cooper & Frye space-like freeze-out, where particles are emitted from a hypersurface defined at constant temperature or energy density, and the Sinyukov time-like freeze-out, which reflects the momenta of particles that would re-enter the fluid so that emission occurs only away from the surface. A toy model with cylindrical symmetry, Bjorken longitudinal scaling, and no transverse flow is constructed, consisting of a static surface component (time-like, using Sinyukov) and a rapid volume freeze-out component (space-like, using Cooper & Frye). HBT radii are calculated from the source emission functions using standard correlation function analysis with Gaussian parametrization.

## Key numbers

- The freeze-out temperature is taken as \(T_c \simeq 100-150\) MeV, determined from \(p_\perp\) slopes.
- For a static surface with time-like freeze-out, the outward HBT radius squared is \(R_o^2 = (1/2 - 4/\pi^2) \langle R(\tau)\rangle^2 + \beta_o^2 \sigma(\tau) + (1/2)\sigma(R(\tau)) - 2\beta_o (2/\pi) \langle (R(\tau)-\langle R(\tau)\rangle)(\tau-\langle\tau\rangle)\rangle\).
- For the two-component toy model, the sideward radius squared is \(R_s^2 = (1/4)(1+p) R^2\) and the outward radius squared is \(R_o^2 = \left( (1/4)(1+p) - (4/\pi^2)p^2 \right) R^2 + (p/18)\beta_o^2 \tau_f^2\), where \(p\) is the fraction of time-like emitted particles.
- Typical heavy ion source sizes are \(R \sim 5\) fm, so interference occurs predominantly when \(q \lesssim \hbar/R \sim 40\) MeV/c.
- Typical particle momenta are \(p_i \simeq p \sim 300\) MeV.
- The reduction factor \(\lambda\) is found to be \(\lambda \sim 0.5\) for pions and \(\lambda \sim 0.9\) for kaons.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of freeze-out conditions in hydrodynamic models initiated by Cooper & Frye (1974) and the correction for time-like surfaces proposed by Sinyukov (1994). It contrasts with the findings of Rischke (1996) at RHIC energies, where a long-lived mixed phase of quark-gluon plasma and hadronic matter produces a large outward HBT radius that is insensitive to the freeze-out assumption. At AGS and SPS energies, where measured \(R_o\) and \(R_s\) are very similar, the difference arising from the freeze-out assumption may be significant. The paper enables more careful interpretation of HBT data by showing that the choice of freeze-out prescription affects the outward radius, particularly for sources with a significant time-like (surface) emission component, and that this effect is reduced by transverse flow and diffuse surface layers as discussed in Opaque (1997) and Grassi (1997).
