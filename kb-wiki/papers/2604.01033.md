# Microscopic optical potential framework applied to neutron scattering on deformed $^{48,50}$Cr

> We formulate and implement a microscopic framework to derive an optical potential from the solution to an effective Hamiltonian and use it to calculate neutron scattering cross sections for the deformed nuclei $^{24}$Mg, $^{48}$Cr and $^{50}$Cr. This approach is based on a symmetry-restored multi-excitation generator coordinate method (GCM), enabling the consistent treatment of both nuclear structure and reaction observables. Through this method, non-local optical potentials corresponding to a Hamiltonian can potentially be constructed for any nucleus in the whole nuclide chart. We use this to perform reaction calculations employing quadrupole deformed triaxial configurations, obtaining results for $A\approx 50$ chromium isotopes, and study the properties of the calculated non-local optical potentials. This work further advances the unified treatment of structure and reaction, within a framework that exploits the intrinsic symmetries of nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors formulate a microscopic optical potential framework derived from a symmetry-restored multi-excitation generator coordinate method (GCM) solution to an effective Hamiltonian, enabling consistent treatment of nuclear structure and reaction observables for deformed nuclei without case-by-case phenomenological tuning, though the continuum is approximated by broadening discrete states with a finite imaginary parameter \(\eta\) rather than by a fully microscopic treatment of resonance widths.

## Method

The framework uses a GCM with triaxial quadrupole deformation, cranking frequency, and proton/neutron pairing strengths as generator coordinates to solve an effective Hamiltonian (SLy4-H) that reproduces a quadrupole-constrained density functional theory calculation. Spectroscopic amplitudes are computed from the GCM solutions for the \(A\pm1\) systems, and the Green's function formalism with the Dyson equation is used to construct the non-local optical potential, with the continuum approximated by a Breit-Wigner broadening parameter \(\eta\) that depends on the scattering energy relative to the Fermi energy.

## Key numbers

- Neutron scattering cross sections calculated for deformed nuclei \(^{24}\text{Mg}\), \(^{48}\text{Cr}\), and \(^{50}\text{Cr}\) using the microscopic optical potential framework.
- The imaginary broadening parameter \(\eta\) is parameterized as \(\eta = \frac{a}{\pi} \frac{(E-E_F)^2}{(E-E_F)^2 + b_\eta^2}\) with \(a = 12\ \text{MeV}\) and \(b_\eta = 22.36\ \text{MeV}\), taken from Waldecker (2011) and Brown (1981).
- The GCM basis includes states with HFB energies within \(E_\mathrm{cut}\) of the lowest energy state, with the number of kept states denoted \(n_\mathrm{grid}\).
- For odd-even systems, one quasiparticle is excited from each GCM basis state, chosen uniformly from the \(q_\mathrm{num}\) lowest-energy quasiparticle excitations with the correct signature (default \(\mathrm{sig} = +i\)).

## Context

This work extends the unified structure-reaction framework first applied to \(^{24}\text{Mg}\) in Bostrom (2025) to the chromium isotopes \(^{48}\text{Cr}\) and \(^{50}\text{Cr}\), which are of particular interest for nuclear reactor applications as components of stainless steel and are included in the NEA high-priority request list (Dupont, 2020). The approach contrasts with phenomenological optical potentials (Koning, 2003; Pruitt, 2023) that are fitted to data and have limited predictive power for exotic nuclei, and builds on recent microscopic efforts using coupled-cluster (Rotureau, 2017), symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (Burrows, 2024), and self-consistent Green's functions (Idini, 2019). The framework enables future application to any nucleus across the nuclide chart where GCM solutions can be obtained, and the authors note that reaction observables are increasingly recognized as essential constraints on nuclear structure itself (Idini, 2020; Bertulani, 2021).
