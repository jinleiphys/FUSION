# Sub-threshold $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ Production in $γA$ Collisions

> We study sub-threshold heavy quarkonium ($J/ψ$ and $Υ$) photo-productions in $γA$ collisions as an independent test of the universality of the nucleon-nucleon short range correlation (SRC) in nuclear scattering processes. Just below the $γp$ threshold, the cross section is dominated by the mean field contribution of nucleons inside the nucleus. The SRC contributions start to dominate at lower photon energies, depending on the fraction of the SRC pairs in the target nucleus. We give an estimate of the cross sections in the sub-threshold region both for $J/ψ$ and $Υ$. This may be helpful for future measurements at JLab as well as at the Electron-Ion Collider in the U.S., and especially in China.

## Key claim

Sub-threshold heavy quarkonium photoproduction in nuclear targets can serve as an independent test of the universality of nucleon-nucleon short range correlations (SRC) in the gluonic sector, but the SRC contribution only dominates over the mean field contribution at photon energies sufficiently far below the free-nucleon threshold, specifically below about 7 GeV for J/psi and below about 9.7 GeV center-of-mass energy for Upsilon.

## Method

The authors compute the cross section for sub-threshold J/psi and Upsilon photoproduction on deuteron and nuclear targets using the impulse approximation, convoluting the free-nucleon photoproduction cross section (parameterized from GlueX data for J/psi) with the nuclear momentum distribution (deuteron) or spectral function (Carbon-12). For the deuteron, the single-nucleon momentum distribution from Wiringa (2013) is used, separating mean field (k less than 300 MeV) and SRC (k greater than 300 MeV) contributions. For Carbon-12, the spectral function from the AV-18 potential is employed with nuclear contacts from Duer (2018) to separate mean field and SRC components, and the mean field momentum distribution is modeled as a Gaussian with a constant separation energy of 20 MeV.

## Key numbers

* For J/psi production on a deuteron target at E_gamma = 7 GeV, the cross section per nucleon is about 3.2 pb and is completely dominated by the SRC contribution.
* For J/psi production on a Carbon-12 target at E_gamma = 5.7 GeV (the energy of a previous JLab experiment), the cross section is about 0.05 pb, explaining the null observation in Bosted (2008).
* The ratio of J/psi photoproduction cross sections per nucleon between Carbon-12 and deuteron rises from about 2 near the gamma-p threshold (E_gamma = 8.2 GeV) to a plateau of about 4.2 around E_gamma = 7.4 GeV.
* For Upsilon production, the near-threshold cross section is estimated to be 10^{-3} to 10^{-2} nb, dropping to about 10^{-5} nb in the SRC-dominated region (W_gamma p less than 9.7 GeV).
* The ratio of Upsilon to J/psi photoproduction cross sections at high energy is taken as 1/200, and the exponent beta for Upsilon is estimated as 1.8 (compared to 1.3 for J/psi).

## Context

This work extends the earlier crude estimate by Xu (2019) by including both mean field and SRC contributions in a detailed calculation using nuclear spectral functions, and generalizes from deuteron to heavier nuclei. It provides a gluonic counterpart to the quark-sector SRC universality tests established by Schmookler (2019) and Segarra (2019) in the EMC effect, and offers predictions for upcoming measurements at JLab, the future Electron-Ion Collider in the U.S., and the proposed EicC in China. The paper also connects to the long history of sub-threshold hadron production in nuclear collisions, including strangeness production (Randrup 1980, Aichelin 1986, Schnetzer 1982) and antiproton production (Carroll 1988, Shor 1990).
