# Light Nuclei as Quantized Skyrmions

> We consider the rigid body quantization of Skyrmions with topological charges 1 to 8, as approximated by the rational map ansatz. Novel, general expressions for the elements of the inertia tensors, in terms of the approximating rational map, are presented and are used to determine the kinetic energy contribution to the total energy of the ground and excited states of the quantized Skyrmions. Our results are compared to the experimentally determined energy levels of the corresponding nuclei, and the energies and spins of a few as yet unobserved states are predicted.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a reparametrization of the Skyrme model (using lithium-6 to set parameters) improves the description of nuclei with baryon number \(B \ge 4\), but the model still fails to accurately fit masses for \(B=1,2,3\) and has unresolved problems with spin assignments for \(B=5\) and \(B=7\) nuclei.

## Method

The authors perform rigid-body quantization of Skyrmions with topological charges \(B=1\) to \(8\), using the rational map ansatz to approximate the angular dependence of the Skyrme field. They derive simplified expressions for the inertia tensors (rotational and isorotational) in terms of the rational map, compute these numerically with a new parameter set (\(e=3.26\), \(F_\pi = 75.2\) MeV, \(m_\pi=138\) MeV), and impose Finkelstein-Rubinstein constraints to determine allowed spin and isospin states. The kinetic energy contribution is added to the classical Skyrmion mass to obtain total energies for ground and excited states, which are compared to experimental nuclear energy levels.

## Key numbers

- New parameter set: \(e=3.26\), \(F_\pi = 75.2\) MeV, \(m_\pi=138\) MeV (implying dimensionless pion mass \(m=1.125\)); old parameters: \(e=4.84\), \(F_\pi = 108\) MeV, \(m=0.528\)
- For \(B=1\): inertia tensor component \(\lambda = 45.1\) (Skyrme units); nucleon (spin/isospin 1/2) total energy = \(986.2\) MeV (classical) + \(21.7\) MeV (spin energy) = \(1008\) MeV; delta resonance energy = \(1095\) MeV
- For \(B=2\): deuteron ground state (\(J=1\), \(I=0\)) total energy = \(1949.3\) MeV + \(16.3\) MeV = \(1966\) MeV; first excited state (\(J=0\), \(I=1\)) total energy = \(1976\) MeV; excitation energy of the \(^1S_0\) state relative to deuteron = \(11\) MeV
- Conversion factor for inertia tensors to physical units: \(e^3 F_\pi = 2613\) MeV

## Context

This work extends prior rigid-body quantization studies of Skyrmions for \(B=1\) (Adkins, Nappi and Witten, 1983), \(B=2\) (Kopeliovich and others, 1980s), \(B=3\) (Carson, 1991), and \(B=4\) (Walhout, 1990s) by applying a unified rational-map-based approach to baryon numbers up to \(B=8\). It builds on the reparametrization proposed by Manton and Wood (2006) using lithium-6, and incorporates Finkelstein-Rubinstein constraints via the method of Krusch (2006). The paper enables quantitative predictions for previously unquantized Skyrmions (especially \(B=6\) and \(B=8\)) and tests the viability of deformed Skyrmions for \(B=5\) and \(B=7\) to resolve spin disagreements noted by Irwin (2000).
