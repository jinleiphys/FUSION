# Revealing nucleon and nucleus flickering in pA collisions at the LHC

> We analyze $pA$ interactions at ultra-high energies within the semiclassical approximation for high energy processes accounting for the diffractive processes and a rapid increase with the incident energy of the coherence length. The fluctuations of the strength of interaction expected in QCD and momentum conservation are taken into account also. We evaluate the number of wounded nucleons in soft and hard processes, the multiplicity of jets in the proton fragmentation region as a function of the variance of the distribution over the interaction strengths directly measured in forward diffractive $pN$ scattering for RHIC and LHC energies. We argue that these results could be used to test whether parton configurations containing a parton carrying the $x\ge 0.5$ fraction of the projectile momentum interact significantly weaker than on average. We also study leading twist shadowing and the EMC effect for superdense nuclear matter configurations probed in the events with larger than average number of wounded nucleons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed pattern of forward jet production in LHC pA collisions is consistent with the hypothesis that parton configurations in the proton carrying a large momentum fraction x_p >= 0.5 interact with a strength approximately a factor of two smaller than the average, but this conclusion depends on the specific value of the variance of the distribution over interaction strengths (omega_sigma ≈ 0.1 at LHC energies) and on the assumption that the color fluctuation formalism correctly accounts for diffractive processes.

## Method

The authors employ the color fluctuation (CF) formalism, which generalizes the Gribov–Glauber model to include inelastic diffractive processes and the rapid increase of coherence length with energy, using a distribution over interaction strengths P_N(sigma) whose variance is determined by the forward diffractive cross section in pN scattering. They implement this in a Monte Carlo algorithm that generates multi-nucleon configurations in nuclei with short-range correlations and uses a profile function for the impact-parameter dependence of inelastic NN collisions, scaling the profile function with sigma to enforce black-disk behavior at small impact parameters. For hard processes, they combine the CF approach for soft accompanying interactions with the QCD factorization theorem for the hard subprocess, evaluating the distribution over the number of wounded nucleons N_coll for events with a hard trigger.

## Key numbers

- Total pp cross section at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV: sigma_tot = 93 mb, with variance omega_sigma = 0.1 (base value) and alternative value omega_sigma = 0.2 for sensitivity studies
- The variance omega_sigma ≈ 0.1 at LHC energies corresponds to a two-state model where cross sections differ by nearly a factor of two: sigma_i = sigma_tot (1 ± sqrt(omega_sigma)), giving approximately 130 mb and 70 mb
- At lower energies (sqrt(s) ≈ 100 GeV), the variance reaches omega_sigma ≈ 0.3, then decreases at higher energies
- In central pA collisions at the LHC, one typically selects N_coll ≈ 14
- The parametrization of P_N(sigma) uses sigma_0 = 86.4825 mb, gamma = 0.0263914, and Omega = 0.51285 for omega_sigma = 0.1

## Context

This work extends the color fluctuation approach of Heiselberg (1991) and Alvioli (2013) to include hard triggers and the connection between parton momentum fraction and interaction strength, building on the Gribov–Glauber model (Gribov, 1969) for nuclear shadowing. It directly addresses the interpretation of LHC pPb data from ATLAS and CMS, which showed that the Glauber model without fluctuations cannot describe the centrality dependence of jet production. The results enable experimental tests of whether high-x parton configurations in the proton are systematically smaller than average, and they predict enhanced nuclear shadowing and EMC effects in events with large N_coll, probing local nucleon densities comparable to those in neutron star cores.
