# Volume and surface contributions to the nuclear symmetry energy within the coherent density fluctuation model

> The volume and surface components of the nuclear symmetry energy (NSE) and their ratio are calculated within the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM). The estimations use the results of the model for the NSE in finite nuclei based on the Brueckner energy-density functional for nuclear matter. In addition, we present results for the NSE and its volume and surface contributions obtained by using the Skyrme energy-density functional. The CDFM weight function is obtained using the proton and neutron densities from the self-consistent HF+BCS method with Skyrme interactions. We present and discuss the values of the volume and surface contributions to the NSE and their ratio obtained for the Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopic chains studying their isotopic sensitivity. The results are compared with estimations of other approaches which have used available experimental data on binding energies, neutron-skin thicknesses, excitation energies to isobaric analog states (IAS) and also with results of other theoretical methods.

## Key claim

The volume and surface components of the nuclear symmetry energy and their ratio are calculated within the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) using both Brueckner and Skyrme energy-density functionals, with the key condition that the results depend on the specific Skyrme interaction used in the HF+BCS calculation of nuclear densities and on the isotopic chain considered.

## Method

The CDFM weight function was obtained from proton and neutron density distributions calculated using the self-consistent HF+BCS method with Skyrme interactions (SLy4, SGII, and Sk3). The symmetry energy for finite nuclei was calculated as an infinite superposition of asymmetric nuclear matter symmetry energies weighted by this function, using both the Brueckner energy-density functional and the Skyrme energy-density functional. The ratio of volume to surface symmetry energy coefficients was then derived from an expression involving the density dependence of the symmetry energy, and the individual volume and surface coefficients were extracted using the calculated symmetry energy and ratio.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of volume to surface symmetry energy coefficients \(\kappa = a_A^V / a_A^S\) obtained with the Brueckner EDF lies within the range \(2.10 \leq \kappa \leq 2.90\) for the Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopic chains.
- For the Brueckner EDF, the volume symmetry energy \(a_A^V\) ranges between 41.5 and 43 MeV across all isotopic chains and Skyrme forces used.
- For the Brueckner EDF, the surface symmetry energy \(a_A^S\) ranges between 14 and 20 MeV across all isotopic chains and Skyrme forces used.
- For the Ni isotopic chain with Brueckner EDF, average values are \(\bar a_A^V = 42.05\) MeV (SLy4), 42.1 MeV (SGII), 42.83 MeV (Sk3) and \(\bar a_A^S = 18.3\) MeV (SLy4), 19 MeV (SGII), 18.73 MeV (Sk3).
- For the Sn isotopic chain with Brueckner EDF, average values are \(\bar a_A^V = 41.9\) MeV (SLy4), 42.71 MeV (SGII), 42.02 MeV (Sk3) and \(\bar a_A^S = 16.27\) MeV (SLy4), 16.5 MeV (SGII), 16.92 MeV (Sk3).
- For the Pb isotopic chain with Brueckner EDF, average values are \(\bar a_A^V = 41.84\) MeV (SLy4), 41.92 MeV (SGII), 42.43 MeV (Sk3) and \(\bar a_A^S = 15.82\) MeV (SLy4), 16.33 MeV (SGII), 15.5 MeV (Sk3).
- The equilibrium nuclear matter density used in the Brueckner EDF is \(\rho_0 = 0.204\) fm\(^{-3}\), with \(r_0 = 1.054\) fm and symmetry energy at equilibrium \(s(\rho_0) = 35.07\) MeV.
- For the Skyrme EDF, the equilibrium densities are \(\rho_0 = 0.1583\) fm\(^{-3}\) (SGII), 0.1453 fm\(^{-3}\) (Sk3), 0.1595 fm\(^{-3}\) (SLy4), with corresponding symmetry energies at equilibrium \(s(\rho_0) = 26.84\) MeV (SGII), 28.17 MeV (Sk3), 32.01 MeV (SLy4).

## Context

This work extends previous CDFM studies of the nuclear symmetry energy in finite nuclei by Gaidarov et al. (2011, 2012) to explicitly separate volume and surface contributions, following the framework of Danielewicz et al. (2003, 2004, 2006) who related the ratio of volume to surface symmetry energy to neutron-skin thickness. The results are compared with empirical extractions from binding energies, neutron-skin thicknesses, and isobaric analog state excitation energies by Danielewicz (2003, 2004), as well as with liquid-drop model fits by Moller et al. (1995), Groote et al. (1976), and Koura et al. (2005). The CDFM approach enables future studies of the isotopic and shell-structure dependence of the symmetry energy components across the nuclear chart.
