# Suitable resolution for EOS tables in neutron star investigation

> Inasmuch as the hydrostatic structure of interior neutron stars uniquely depends on the equation of state (EOS), the inverse constraints on EOS from astrophysical observation have been important methods to reveal the properties of the high-density matter. Up to date, most of EOSs for neutron-star matter are given in tabulations, but these numeric tables could be quite different in resolution. To guarantee both the accuracy and efficiency in computing Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation, a concise standard for generating EOS tables with suitable resolution is investigated in this work. It is shown that the EOS tables with 50 points logarithmic-uniformly located at supra-nuclear density segment [$ρ_{0}, 10 ρ_{0} $], where $ρ_{0}$ is the nuclear saturation density) would correspond to the interpolation-induced errors at $\sim 0.02\%$ for the gravitational mass $M$ and $\sim 0.2\%$ for the tidal deformability $Λ$.

## Key claim
The paper claims that EOS tables with 50 points logarithmic-uniformly located in the supra-nuclear density segment [ρ₀, 10ρ₀] (where ρ₀ is nuclear saturation density) produce interpolation-induced errors of ~0.02% for gravitational mass M and ~0.2% for tidal deformability Λ, but this conclusion is conditional on the use of a logarithmic-uniform grid (U-grid) and is demonstrated primarily for the APR EOS, with additional checks on parameterized asymmetric nucleon matter EOSs.

## Method
The authors numerically integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method with adaptive step-size control to compute neutron star mass M and tidal deformability Λ. They generated EOS tables at various resolutions by sampling from a spline-fitted APR EOS (APR-a) using logarithmic-uniform grids (U-grid) and other grid types, then compared interpolation-induced errors from linear interpolation and Piecewise-Cubic-Hermite-Interpolating-Polynomial (PCHIP) interpolation against exact solutions.

## Key numbers
- EOS tables with 50 points at supra-nuclear density [ρ₀, 10ρ₀] (scale 50(150)) yield interpolation-induced errors of ~0.02% for gravitational mass M and ~0.2% for tidal deformability Λ.
- For EOS tables of scale 20(200) (typical in literature), interpolation-induced errors for stellar mass M are expected to be 0.1% to 1%.
- The relative error of radius R for the four EOSs in Table 1 is ~0.01%.
- For APR-20(200) and APR-20(400), the relative residual of the Love number is ~6 times the mass residual (resk₂ ~ 6 resM).
- The minimal scale for U-grid APR EOS is determined as 50(150), corresponding to the stated errors.

## Context
This work extends prior studies on neutron star structure and EOS constraints from gravitational wave observations such as GW170817, which provided constraints on tidal deformability (Λ₁.₄ = 190_{-120}^{+390}) and pressure at twice saturation density (p(2ρ₀) = 21.85_{-10.61}^{+16.85} MeV/fm³) as reported by the LIGO/Virgo collaboration. The paper addresses a practical issue in Bayesian statistical studies (e.g., those by Most et al. (2018) and Annala et al. (2018)) where large numbers of EOS tables are used: too many grid points make computation inefficient, while too few introduce interpolation errors. The authors recommend that future EOS tables contain more than 50 grid points in the supra-nuclear density segment, improving upon the common scale of ~20(200) used in tables such as SFHo, GShen, and LS EOSs.
