# Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay without Vacuum Majorana Neutrino Mass

> We present a proof-of-concept extension to the Standard Model that can generate a non-vanishing neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$) signal without the existence of Majorana neutrinos or lepton number violation in the zero-density vacuum-ground-state Lagrangian. We propose that the $0νββ$ can be induced by the capture of an ultralight scalar field, a potential dark matter candidate, that carries two units of lepton number. This makes the observable $0νββ$ spectrum indistinguishable from the usual $0νββ$ mechanisms by any practical means. We find that a non-zero $0νββ$ rate does not require neutrinos to be fundamentally Majorana particles. However, for sizeable decay rates within the range of next-generation experiments, the neutrino will, generally, acquire an (effective) Majorana mass as the scalar field undergoes a transition to the Bose-Einstein condensate phase. We also discuss the distinction between the aforementioned scenario and the case in which the emission of a lepton-number-two scalar leads to $0νββ$, exhibiting discernible qualitative features that make it experimentally distinguishable from the conventional scenario.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that neutrinoless double beta decay (0νββ) can occur without Majorana neutrinos or lepton number violation in the vacuum Lagrangian, instead being induced by capture of an ultralight scalar field carrying two units of lepton number. However, for decay rates within reach of next-generation experiments, the scalar field necessarily forms a Bose-Einstein condensate that induces an effective in-medium Majorana mass for neutrinos, restoring the connection between 0νββ and Majorana neutrino mass.

## Method
The authors introduce a scalar field φ with lepton number 2 and a right-handed neutrino νR, enforcing exact B-L symmetry to prevent vacuum Majorana masses. They calculate the 0νββ rate from scalar capture using existing nuclear matrix elements and phase-space factors for 136Xe, comparing the capture process to scalar emission and the standard light-Majorana-neutrino-exchange mechanism. They then analyze the transition to a Bose-Einstein condensate phase at high scalar number densities, where the condensate expectation value generates an effective Majorana mass for the right-handed neutrino, and compute the resulting 0νββ half-life using a 6×6 neutrino mixing framework.

## Key numbers
- For 136Xe, the phase-space factor for scalar-emission 0νββ is G0νββφ = 4.09×10−16 yr−1, compared to G0νββ = 1.458×10−14 yr−1 for the standard mechanism.
- The effective neutrino mass squared for the scalar process is mββφ2 ≃ 78 meV2 (assuming normal ordering, vanishing lightest neutrino mass, and zero CP phase).
- The half-life for φ-capture 0νββ in 136Xe is T1/2 ≃ 1.4×1028 yr × (mφ/10−20 eV)2 / (α g2), where α is the dark matter fraction and g the coupling.
- The ratio of emission to capture rates is Γem/Γcap ≃ 6.4×10−25 (mφ/10−20 eV)2 / α.
- The critical temperature for Bose-Einstein condensation is TC ≃ 1030 eV × α (10−20 eV / mφ)5/3.
- The half-life for cosmic relic neutrino capture on 100Mo is (Tννββ1/2)−1 ≃ 7×1047 yr × (nν / ⟨nν⟩)2, with ⟨nν⟩ = 56 cm−3.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of the Schechter-Valle black-box theorem (Schechter and Valle, 1982) by showing that 0νββ can occur without vacuum Majorana masses if an external lepton-number-carrying particle participates, similar to Majoron-emission models (Burgess and Cline, 1992; Carone, 1993) but with an experimentally indistinguishable signature. It contrasts with prior dark-matter-induced 0νββ studies (Huang and Urrutia, 2021; Herms et al., 2023) by explicitly conserving B-L and avoiding vacuum Majorana mass terms. The work enables experimental searches that can distinguish between scalar capture and standard mechanisms through energy resolution and isotope comparisons, and it connects to broader studies of Bose-Einstein condensate dark matter (Ferreira, 2020) and neutrino self-interactions relevant to the Hubble tension.
