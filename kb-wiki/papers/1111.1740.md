# Transverse Momentum Dependent Fragmentation and Quark Distribution Functions from the NJL-jet Model

> Using the model of Nambu and Jona-Lasinio to provide a microscopic description of both the structure of the nucleon and of the quark to hadron elementary fragmentation functions, we investigate the transverse momentum dependence of the unpolarized quark distributions in the nucleon and of the quark to pion and kaon fragmentation functions. The transverse momentum dependence of the fragmentation functions is determined within a Monte Carlo framework, with the notable result that the average $P_\perp^2$ of the produced kaons is significantly larger than that of the pions. We also find that $<P_\perp^2>$ has a sizable $z$ dependence, in contrast with the naive Gaussian ansatz for the fragmentation functions. Diquark correlations in the nucleon give rise to a non-trivial flavor dependence in the unpolarized transverse momentum dependent quark distribution functions. The $<k_T^2>$ of the quarks in the nucleon are also found to have a sizable $x$ dependence. Finally, these results are used as input to a Monte Carlo event generator for semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS), which is used to determine the average transverse momentum squared of the produced hadrons measured in SIDIS, namely $<P_T^2>$. Again we find that the average $P_T^2$ of the produced kaons in SIDIS is significantly larger than that of the pions and in each case $\la P_T^2 \ra$ has a sizable $z$ dependence.

# Transverse Momentum Dependent Fragmentation and Quark Distribution Functions from the NJL-jet Model

## Key claim
Using the NJL-jet model with a dipole regulator (LB-DIP) instead of a sharp cutoff, the authors find that the average transverse momentum squared of produced kaons is significantly larger than that of pions, and that these averages have a sizable dependence on the light-cone momentum fraction z, contradicting the naive Gaussian ansatz often used in phenomenological analyses.

## Method
The authors calculate elementary (one-step) transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) splitting functions for quarks fragmenting to pions and kaons within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, using a dipole regularization scheme (LB-DIP) that replaces the sharp Lepage-Brodsky cutoff. These elementary functions are then used as input to a Monte Carlo simulation of the quark-jet cascade, where at each emission vertex the hadron type, light-cone momentum fraction z, and transverse momentum are randomly sampled. The TMD quark distribution functions in the nucleon are calculated separately using the NJL model with proper-time regularization, including both scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations from a relativistic Faddeev equation. The TMD fragmentation functions and TMD quark distributions are then combined in a Monte Carlo event generator for semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) to compute the average transverse momentum squared of produced hadrons.

## Key numbers
- Light constituent quark mass: M = 0.4 GeV; strange constituent quark mass: M_s = 0.59 GeV (LB-DIP scheme)
- Three-momentum cutoff for LB-DIP regularization: Λ₃ = 0.773 GeV
- Quark-meson coupling constants: g_{πqQ} = 4.24, g_{KqQ} = 4.52
- Model scale for fragmentation functions: Q₀² = 0.2 GeV²
- Model scale for TMD PDFs (proper-time scheme): Q₀² = 0.16 GeV²
- Infrared cutoff for proper-time regularization: Λ_IR = 0.24 GeV
- The average ⟨k_T²⟩ of quarks in the nucleon shows a sizable x dependence, with values ranging from approximately 0.1 GeV² at x ≈ 0.2 to above 0.3 GeV² at x ≈ 0.8 for u quarks
- The average ⟨P_⟂²⟩ of produced hadrons in fragmentation has a strong z dependence, with values for pions ranging from approximately 0.05 GeV² at z ≈ 0.2 to above 0.2 GeV² at z ≈ 0.8

## Context
This work extends the NJL-jet model framework previously developed by Ito et al. (2009) and Matevosyan et al. (2010, 2011) for integrated fragmentation functions to include transverse momentum dependence. It provides the first microscopic calculation of spin-independent TMD quark distributions and TMD fragmentation functions where no parameters are adjusted to TMD data. The results contradict the Gaussian ansatz for TMD fragmentation functions used in phenomenological analyses such as Schweitzer et al. (2010), and the dipole regulator (LB-DIP) improves agreement with empirical parametrizations of fragmentation functions from Hirai et al. (2007) (HKNS) and de Florian et al. (2007) (DSS), especially near z = 1. The framework enables self-consistent Monte Carlo simulations of SIDIS that can be matched to experimental kinematics at COMPASS, HERMES, and JLab.
