# Triplet np Final State Interactions at Large Momentum Transfers

> Using the simple relation between the np scattering and bound state wave functions, the spin-triplet contributions to the pp -> pi+ (pn) and backward dp -> p(pn) reactions are estimated for low np excitation energies. The good agreement with the pion production data at 400 and 450 MeV over a range of angles shows that spin-singlet final states cannot be more than of the order of 10%. On the other hand it is seen that the spin-singlet states must provide about 30% of the strength in pion production at 1 GeV as well as deuteron-proton backward scattering at 1.6 GeV.

## Key claim

Using a simple relation between np scattering and bound state wave functions, the authors estimate that spin-singlet final states in the pp → π⁺(pn) reaction at 400 and 450 MeV cannot exceed about 10% of the spin-triplet contribution, while at 1 GeV (pion production) and 1.6 GeV (backward dp → p(pn) scattering) the spin-singlet states must provide about 30% of the strength, with the caveat that the model is only valid for low np excitation energies and short-range transition operators.

## Method

The authors exploit the fact that the S-wave triplet np scattering wave function, extrapolated to the deuteron pole, is proportional to the deuteron bound state wave function with a proportionality constant independent of the potential form. They use this relation to derive an expression (Eq. 5) that predicts the spin-triplet contribution to three-body cross sections (pp → π⁺(pn) and dp → p(pn)) in terms of the corresponding two-body deuteron production cross section, valid for low np excitation energies Q and high beam energies. The predictions are compared to existing experimental data on pp → π⁺pn at 400 and 450 MeV (TRIUMF data from Falk) and dp → p(pn) at 400 and 1600 MeV (Boudard data), with the singlet contribution estimated by fitting the remaining strength after subtracting the triplet prediction.

## Key numbers

- For pp → π⁺(pn) at 400 and 450 MeV, spin-singlet final states are less than about 10% of the spin-triplet contribution.
- For dp → p(pn) at T_d = 1600 MeV, the singlet final-state cross section is about 0.4 ± 0.05 of the triplet at large Q, consistent with a statistical population.
- For pp → π⁺(pn) at 1 GeV, the singlet/triplet ratio is also around 0.4 ± 0.05, consistent with the 1.6 GeV dp → p(pn) data after accounting for resolution (Gaussian smearing with σ = 2.60 MeV).
- For p(p,π⁺)X at 592 MeV and θ = 0°, the singlet/triplet ratio is 0.25 ± 0.05.
- The triplet contribution to the cross section ratio R (Eq. 7) is given by R = [p(x)/p(-1)] * [√x/(1+x)], where x = Q/ε and ε is the deuteron binding energy (α_t = 0.232 fm⁻¹).
- The singlet pole parameter is α_s = -0.104 fm⁻¹.
- The experimental resolution in Q for the dp → p(pn) data at 1600 MeV is taken as Gaussian with σ = 2.35 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the short-range approximation previously applied to np → npη and pp → pnπ⁺ near threshold (Faeldt and Wilkin, 1995) to higher energies, showing that the same simple model works for large momentum transfer reactions when the np system is at low excitation. The results contradict the expectation that spin-singlet contributions might be negligible at all energies; instead, the singlet/triplet ratio increases from ≤10% at 400-450 MeV to about 40% at 1-1.6 GeV, consistent with evidence from pion absorption on ³He (Summers, 1993). The approach enables a model-independent estimate of triplet contributions to semi-inclusive reactions, and the authors suggest that future experiments with polarised beam and target at forward angles could directly measure the singlet/triplet ratio via spin-spin correlations.
