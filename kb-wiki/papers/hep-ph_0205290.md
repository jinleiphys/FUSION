# No-go for detecting CP violation via neutrinoless double beta decay

> We present a necessary condition on the solar oscillation amplitude for CP violation to be detectable through neutrinoless double beta (0νββ) decay. It depends only on the fractional uncertainty in the ν_e-ν_e element of the neutrino mass matrix. We demonstrate that even under very optimistic assumptions about the sensitivity of future experiments to the absolute neutrino mass scale, and on the precision with which nuclear matrix elements that contribute to 0νββdecay are calculable, it will be impossible to detect neutrino CP violation arising from Majorana phases.

# No-go for detecting CP violation via neutrinoless double beta decay

## Key claim

Even under extremely optimistic assumptions about future experimental sensitivity to the absolute neutrino mass scale and the calculability of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta (0νββ) decay, it will be impossible to detect neutrino CP violation arising from Majorana phases. The necessary condition for detectability depends on the solar oscillation amplitude and the fractional uncertainty in the νe-νe element of the neutrino mass matrix, and this condition cannot be met in practice.

## Method

The authors derive a necessary condition relating the solar mixing angle sin²2θ and the combined experimental and theoretical fractional uncertainty x on Mee (the νe-νe element of the neutrino mass matrix) that must be satisfied for CP violation to be detectable via 0νββ decay. They then assume a set of seven highly optimistic experimental and theoretical developments achievable by 2020, including 25% experimental uncertainty on Mee, a reduction of the nuclear matrix element uncertainty to x=0.5, and precise measurements of the sum of neutrino masses from cosmology, and perform a quantitative analysis scanning over hypothetical measurements of Σ and Mee to determine whether the Majorana phase φ can be distinguished from CP-conserving values.

## Key numbers

- The necessary condition for CP violation detectability is sin²2θ > 1 − ((1−x)/(1+x))²; with current solar data requiring sin²2θ < 0.95 at 3σ C.L., x must be smaller than 0.63.
- The current factor-of-3 uncertainty on nuclear matrix elements corresponds to Mee(1⁺²·⁰₋₀·₇).
- For the LMA solar solution at 3σ C.L.: 0.56 ≤ sin²2θ₃ ≤ 0.95, with 2.0×10⁻⁵ ≤ Δs ≤ 2.3×10⁻⁴ eV².
- For the LOW solar solution at 3σ C.L.: 0.80 ≤ sin²2θ₃ ≤ 0.94, with 9.0×10⁻⁸ ≤ Δs ≤ 2.0×10⁻⁷ eV².
- Atmospheric neutrino data at 99% C.L.: sin²2θ₁ ≥ 0.85 and 1.1×10⁻³ eV² ≤ Δa ≤ 5×10⁻³ eV².
- CHOOZ constraint at 95% C.L.: sin²2θ₂ ≤ 0.1.
- Under optimistic assumptions, the authors consider hypothetical measurements with Σ = 0.24 ± 0.04 eV and Mee = 0.03(1 ± 0.5) eV, Σ = 0.51 ± 0.04 eV and Mee = 0.10(1 ± 0.5) eV, and Σ = 1.12 ± 0.04 eV and Mee = 0.30(1 ± 0.5) eV.

## Context

This paper extends the discussion of CP violation in the Majorana neutrino sector, building on the framework of Wolfenstein (1978) for CP parities of mass eigenstates and the formalism for 0νββ decay. The authors explicitly consider the two possible neutrino mass hierarchies (normal and inverted) and the LMA and LOW solar solutions from Barger (2002). The work demonstrates that even with the ambitious experimental programs envisioned for GENIUS, EXO, KATRIN, KamLAND, Borexino, and the JHF-Kamioka project, 0νββ decay cannot detect Majorana CP violation, establishing a fundamental limitation for this channel and highlighting the complementary role of long-baseline experiments for detecting CP violation from the Dirac phase δ.
