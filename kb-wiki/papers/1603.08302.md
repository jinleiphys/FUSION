# The Dubna-Mainz-Taipei Dynamical Model for $πN$ Scattering and $π$ Electromagnetic Production

> Some of the featured results of the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) dynamical model for $πN$ scattering and $π^0$ electromagnetic production are summarized. These include results for threshold $π^0$ production, deformation of $Δ(1232)$, and the extracted properties of higher resonances below 2 GeV. The excellent agreement of DMT model's predictions with threshold $π^0$ production data, including the recent precision measurements from MAMI establishes results of DMT model as a benchmark for experimentalists and theorists in dealing with threshold pion production.

## Key claim

The DMT dynamical model achieves excellent agreement with threshold π⁰ production data, including recent precision measurements from MAMI, but this success is contingent on the model's use of a chiral-invariant effective Lagrangian and the Cooper-Jennings propagator, which preserves soft-pion theorems and unitarity, rather than being a model-independent prediction.

## Method

The DMT model solves a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for πN scattering using a meson-exchange potential derived from a chiral-invariant Lagrangian with π, N, σ, ρ, and Δ(1232) fields, employing the Cooper-Jennings propagator. For electromagnetic production, the γπ transition amplitude is obtained from a Lippmann-Schwinger equation where the transition potential is derived by gauging the same Lagrangian, including Born terms and t-channel ρ and ω exchange. For energies up to 2 GeV, the model is extended by including the ηN channel and bare resonances dressed by meson clouds, with ππN channel effects accounted for via a phenomenological width in the resonance propagators.

## Key numbers

- Threshold π⁰ production: DMT model describes the real and imaginary parts of the E₀₊(pπ⁰) multipole near threshold, with two-loop corrections found to be small, justifying one-loop ChPT calculations.
- For π⁰ electroproduction at Q² = 0.05 GeV², Θπ = 90°, Φπ = 90°, and ε = 0.93, DMT model predictions for σ₀, σTT, σLT, and ALT agree well with MAMI data, outperforming heavy-baryon ChPT.
- For polarized differential cross sections σT of threshold π⁰ photoproduction at photon energies 168.5 and 183.7 MeV, DMT model predictions agree with Crystal Ball/TAPS data from MAMI.
- At W = 1232 MeV and near Q² = 0, the DMT model gives REM = E₁₊⁽³/²⁾/M₁₊⁽³/²⁾ = 0.24%, compared to the experimental summary value of -(2.5 ± 0.5)% from PDG (2006), indicating Δ(1232) deformation arises from pion cloud dressing.
- For the Δ(1232) resonance, DMT extracts a bare mass of 1425 MeV and a physical mass of 1233 MeV with a total width of 132 MeV, compared to PDG values of 1232 ± 1 MeV and 118 ± 2 MeV.
- For the S₁₁(1535) resonance, DMT extracts a bare mass of 1559 MeV and a physical mass of 1520 MeV with a total width of 130 MeV, compared to PDG values of 1535 ± 10 MeV and 150 ± 25 MeV.
- The fit to data requires four additional resonances not listed by PDG: S₁₁(1878), D₁₃(2152), P₁₃(2204), and P₃₁(2100), all with very large widths.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical approach to πN scattering and electromagnetic pion production originally proposed by Tanabe and Yang (1985) and Yang (1985), building on the Taipei-Argonne meson-exchange πN model developed by Hung et al. (1994, 2001). The DMT model provides a benchmark for threshold pion production that complements chiral perturbation theory calculations by Bernard et al. (1996) and relativistic ChPT by Hilt et al. (2013) and Scherer (2015), while offering a dynamical picture of Δ(1232) deformation where the bare Δ is spherical and deformation arises from pion cloud dressing. The extracted resonance properties enable comparison with lattice QCD results and highlight discrepancies with PDG listings that require further investigation, particularly for higher resonance widths.
