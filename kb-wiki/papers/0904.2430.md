# Influence of the eta exchange to the eta production in proton-proton scattering

> Eta meson production in the proton-proton scattering is dominated by the low-mass meson exchange. We present a brief study on how the type of the exchanged mesons within coupled-channel and multi-resonance model influences the scattering observables. We show under which circumstances the eta exchange may explain the shape of the observed cross sections, and present a few selected results: total cross section in the full energy range, and the proton-proton energy distribution at 15.5 MeV.

## Key claim

The shape of the total and differential cross sections for eta meson production in proton-proton scattering can be reproduced by including only pion and eta meson exchange with destructive interference between the two amplitudes, but the absolute size of the cross section still requires modification from the missing initial state interaction factor.

## Method

The authors used a coupled-channel multi-resonance model based on the Cutkosky CMB approach to obtain the two-body off-shell eta-production amplitude, considering only pion and eta meson exchange between the two colliding protons. The relative sign between the πN → ηN and ηN → ηN amplitudes was treated as a free parameter, and destructive interference was chosen. Final state interaction was calculated in a multiplicative approximation using the product of Jost functions for the pp and pη subsystems, while initial state interaction was set equal to one for the high-energy analysis.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section data for pp → ppη range from 5 MeV excess energy to well above 1 GeV.
- The proton-proton energy distribution was calculated at 15.5 MeV excess energy and compared with COSY-11 experiment data.
- Higher partial waves of the meson-nucleon amplitude affected results almost insignificantly below 200 MeV excess energy.
- A potentially interesting region with almost no data exists between roughly 100 and 500 MeV excess energy where higher partial waves start to play an important role.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses (Bat98, Cec06) of eta production in proton-proton scattering, where earlier models used only proton-proton initial and final state interactions and assumed constructive interference between amplitudes. The paper contradicts the earlier assumption of constructive interference by showing that destructive interference between πN and ηN amplitudes can describe the shape of total cross sections almost perfectly. The results enable future inclusion of rho meson exchange, proper initial state interaction treatment, and detailed analysis of related reactions such as pn → pnη and pn → dη.
