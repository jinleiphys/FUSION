# A model for the Delta(1600) resonance and gamma N -> Delta(1600) transition

> A covariant spectator constituent quark model is applied to study the gamma N -> Delta(1600) transition. Two processes are important in the transition: a photon couples to the individual quarks of the Delta(1600) core (quark core), and a photon couples to the intermediate pion-baryon states (pion cloud). While the quark core contributions are estimated assuming Delta(1600) as the first radial excitation of Delta(1232), the pion cloud contributions are estimated based on an analogy with the gamma N -> Delta(1232) transition. To estimate the pion cloud contributions in the gamma N -> Delta(1600) transition, we include the relevant intermediate states, pi-N, pi-Delta, pi-N(1440) and pi-Delta(1600). Dependence on the four-momentum transfer squared, Q2, is predicted for the magnetic dipole transition form factor, GM*(Q2), as well as the helicity amplitudes, A_1/2(Q2) and A_3/2(Q2). The results at Q2=0 are compared with the existing data.

## Key claim

The paper models the \(\gamma N \to \Delta(1600)\) transition using a covariant spectator constituent quark model, finding that the bare quark core contribution alone gives a negative magnetic dipole form factor at \(Q^2=0\) (\(-1.11\)), which is only brought into agreement with the experimental data (approximately \(+0.20\)) after including dominant pion cloud contributions from intermediate \(\pi N\), \(\pi \Delta\), \(\pi N(1440)\), and \(\pi \Delta(1600)\) states.

## Method

The authors applied a covariant spectator constituent quark model, treating the \(\Delta(1600)\) as the first radial excitation of the \(\Delta(1232)\) in an S-state approximation. The valence quark (bare) contributions to the magnetic dipole transition form factor \(G_M^*(Q^2)\) were calculated from the overlap integral of the nucleon and \(\Delta(1600)\) scalar wave functions, with the \(\Delta(1600)\) wave function fixed by orthogonality to the \(\Delta(1232)\) state. The pion cloud contributions were estimated using an effective parametrization based on the cloudy bag model formalism, including intermediate states \(\pi N\), \(\pi \Delta\), \(\pi N(1440)\), and \(\pi \Delta(1600)\), with coupling constants derived from decay branching ratios.

## Key numbers

*   The bare quark core contribution to the magnetic dipole transition form factor at \(Q^2=0\) is \(-1.11\).
*   The experimental data for the magnetic dipole transition form factor at \(Q^2=0\) is approximately \(+0.20\).
*   The pion cloud contribution to the \(\gamma N \to \Delta(1232)\) transition is 46.4% at \(Q^2=0\).
*   The cutoff parameter for the pion cloud parametrization is \(\Lambda_\pi^2 \simeq 1.22\) GeV\(^2\).
*   The nucleon dipole form factor used is \(G_D = (1 + Q^2/0.71)^{-2}\) with \(Q^2\) in GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model, previously applied to the \(\gamma N \to \Delta(1232)\) transition (Ramalho and Tsushima, earlier works), to the less-studied \(\Delta(1600)\) resonance. It addresses the "missing resonance problem" by providing a theoretical prediction for the \(\gamma N \to \Delta(1600)\) transition form factors, which can be tested by experiments at facilities like CLAS (Jefferson Lab), MAMI (Mainz), and ELSA (Bonn). The results enable future comparisons with data from double pion production experiments and with lattice QCD simulations (Leinweber 2004, Engel 2009) and QCD sum rules (Erkol 2008).
