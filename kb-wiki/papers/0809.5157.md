# Nuclear clusters with Halo Effective Field Theory

> After a brief discussion of effective field theory applied to nuclear clusters, I present the aspect of Coulomb interactions, with applications to low-energy alpha-alpha and nucleon-alpha scattering.

## Key claim

Halo effective field theory (EFT) can describe low-energy alpha-alpha and nucleon-alpha scattering with Coulomb interactions included, but the power counting for nuclear clusters often requires extra fine-tuning beyond the nucleon-nucleon case, and the alpha-alpha system shows a remarkable cancellation between strong and electromagnetic contributions that makes the scattering length an order of magnitude larger than its purely strong part.

## Method

The author represents each cluster as an elementary field with short-range forces described by contact interactions, introducing an auxiliary dimeron field for S-wave two-boson systems. Coulomb interactions are included by extending the Kong-Ravndal EFT formulation for two-proton systems to include resonances, with the Coulomb-modified strong amplitude expressed as a geometric series that requires resummation for narrow resonances. For alpha-alpha scattering, the low-energy expansion of the Coulomb function H(eta) is performed around the resonance pole, while for nucleon-alpha scattering the formalism is extended from S-wave to P-wave strong interactions.

## Key numbers

- Alpha-alpha resonance energy: E_R ≈ 92 keV, width Γ_R ≈ 6 MeV (the ⁸Be ground state)
- Alpha-alpha scattering length a_0 ≈ 2000 fm, nearly three orders of magnitude larger than the alpha matter radius
- Low-energy scale M_lo ≈ 20 MeV, high momentum scale M_hi ≈ 140 MeV (ratio roughly 1:7)
- At LO: a_0 = -1.80 × 10³ fm, r_0 = 1.083 fm
- At NLO: a_0 = -1.92 ± 0.09 × 10³ fm, r_0 = 1.098 ± 0.005 fm, P_0 = -1.46 ± 0.08 fm³
- ERE fit (author's): a_0 = -1.92 ± 0.09 × 10³ fm, r_0 = 1.099 ± 0.005 fm, P_0 = -1.62 ± 0.08 fm³
- If strong forces generated r_0 11% larger, the ⁸Be ground state would be bound

## Context

This work extends the halo EFT approach developed by Bedaque, Hammer, van Kolck, and others (2003-2007) for nuclear clusters, and the Coulomb formulation by Kong and Ravndal (1999-2000) for two-proton systems. The alpha-alpha results improve on the earlier ERE parameter extraction by Rasch (1967) with smaller error bars, though the author notes that the scattering data and resonance property measurements appear incompatible. The formalism enables future studies of more complex systems such as ⁶He halo nuclei, the Hoyle state in ¹²C, and the ½⁺ excited ⁹Be state, with a model study by Lim and collaborators (2007) providing a perturbative Coulomb treatment for three charged particles.
