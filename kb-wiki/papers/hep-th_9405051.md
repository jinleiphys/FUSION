# Higher-Order Derivative Susy in Quantum Mechanics with Large Energy Shifts

> Within the framework of second order derivative (one dimensional) SUSYQM we discuss particular realizations which incorporate large energy shifts between the lowest states of the spectrum of the superhamiltonian (of Schrödinger type). The technique used in this construction is based on the "gluing" procedure. We study the limit of infinite energy shift for the charges of the Higher Derivative SUSY Algebra, and compare the results with those of the standard SUSY Algebra. We conjecture that our results can suggest a construction of a toy model where large energy splittings between fermionic and bosonic partners do not affect the SUSY at low energies.

## Key claim

Within second-order derivative supersymmetric quantum mechanics, large energy shifts between the lowest states of partner Hamiltonians can be realized via a "gluing" procedure, and in the limit of infinite energy shift the higher-derivative SUSY algebra reduces effectively to standard first-order SUSY, but with an extra phase of π in scattering observables and a δ-type singularity in the potential.

## Method

The authors construct second-order supercharges as products of two first-order supercharges, each defined by a superpotential (W₁, W₂). They impose a "gluing" condition (Eq. glue) that relates the two superpotentials and a parameter λ > 0, producing a superhamiltonian H whose diagonal components are h⁽¹⁾ and h⁽²⁾ − λ. They then study specific choices of W₁ (constant, tanh) and solve the gluing condition for W₂, analyzing the spectrum, bound states, and scattering properties in the large-λ limit.

## Key numbers

- The gluing condition involves a parameter λ > 0; the superalgebra takes the form K = {Q⁺, Q⁻} = H(H + λ).
- In Example 1, with W₁ = ±α, the continuum spectrum starts at α², and the ground state has eigenvalue E = −λ with wave function Ψ₋λ⁽²⁾(x) = cosh⁻¹(κx + γ), where κ ≡ √(λ + α²).
- In the large-λ limit, the difference between superpotentials W₂(x) and W₂⁽⁰⁾(x) scales as O(κ⁻¹) (Eq. asymp).
- The limiting rescaled charges yield the algebra {Q̃⁺, Q̃⁻} = H + H²/λ (Eq. algebra).
- The potential V⁽²⁾(x) acquires an attractive δ-type singularity proportional to √λ (Eq. delta).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of higher-derivative SUSY quantum mechanics developed in Andrianov, Ioffe, and Spiridonov (1993) and Andrianov, Cannata, Dedonder, and Ioffe (1994). It provides toy models where large energy splittings between fermionic and bosonic partners can be introduced without breaking a generalized SUSY algebra at low energies, complementing the more standard mechanism of Nilles (1984) where levels are pushed up in energy. The results enable approximation schemes for the resolvent operator 1/(λ − H) and may be relevant for nonrelativistic limits of relativistic Hamiltonians.
