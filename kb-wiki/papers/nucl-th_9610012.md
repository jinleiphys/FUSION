# Spin Observables in Consistent Relativistic Models of (e, e'p) and (gamma, p) Reactions

> Single nucleon knockout reactions must be described in a consistent framework in order to extract information about nuclear structure and reaction mechanisms. Consistent relativistic models for the direct knockout contribution to the (e, e'p) and (gamma, p) reactions have been developed and used previously to examine existing momentum distribution and cross section data for the two reactions^{1}. We present results of calculations of spin observables obtained from these models of (e, e'p) and (gamma, p) reactions. For the (e, e'p) reaction we consider the importance of the choice of kinematics and show that perpendicular kinematics result in distributions with relatively large cross sections and comparatively large polarizations. In the (gamma, p) reactions the photon asymmetry and proton polarization are not very sensitive to changes in model ingredients when the incident photon energy is less than 100 MeV. For higher energies these observables show increasing sensitivity to modifications of both the bound and continuum wave functions.

# Spin Observables in Consistent Relativistic Models of (e, e'p) and (gamma, p) Reactions

## Key claim

The relativistic models for direct knockout in (e, e'p) and (gamma, p) reactions, which already describe cross section data well, predict that spin observables can distinguish between relativistic and non-relativistic approaches, but only under specific kinematic conditions: for (e, e'p) the proton polarization is large and measurable in perpendicular or constant q-omega kinematics, while for (gamma, p) the photon asymmetry and proton polarization become sensitive to model ingredients only when the incident photon energy exceeds 100 MeV.

## Method

The authors use fully relativistic Dirac equation solutions with strong vector and scalar potentials for both bound and continuum nucleon wave functions. For (e, e'p) the amplitude is calculated in the one-photon exchange approximation using the effective momentum approximation for Coulomb distortion, while for (gamma, p) only direct knockout is considered. The spectroscopic factor is determined by scaling (e, e'p) momentum distribution calculations to experimental data, and the same ingredients are then used as parameter-free predictions for (gamma, p) observables.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factors from relativistic analyses of (e, e'p) reactions are consistently larger by 10% to 20% than values from non-relativistic analyses.
- For an oxygen target with incident electron energy of 700 MeV and proton kinetic energy near 150 MeV in constant q-omega kinematics, the calculated proton polarizations at a missing momentum of 30 MeV/c differ by as much as 0.8 between relativistic and non-relativistic models.
- In this same kinematic region, the triple differential cross section is calculated to be about 0.6 nb / sr sr MeV.
- For (gamma, p) reactions, the photon asymmetry and proton polarization are not very sensitive to changes in model ingredients when the incident photon energy is less than 100 MeV.
- The analyzing power due to initially polarized electrons and the polarization of final state electrons in the (e, e'p) model are less than 1%.

## Context

This work extends the consistent relativistic models previously developed by Johansson, Sherif and Lotz (1996) for momentum distribution and cross section data, now calculating spin observables for the first time. The relativistic approach contrasts with non-relativistic analyses by Boffi et al. (1990) and others, which require large meson exchange current contributions to describe (gamma, p) data while relativistic analyses do not. The results suggest that measurements of spin observables, particularly at photon energies above 100 MeV for (gamma, p) and in perpendicular or constant q-omega kinematics for (e, e'p), could resolve the long-standing discrepancy between relativistic and non-relativistic descriptions of nucleon knockout reactions.
