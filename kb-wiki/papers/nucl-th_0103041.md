# The Continuum Spectrum of the 4N System. Results and Challenges

> The recent calculation of the four nucleon continuum spectrum are reviewed. Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in configuration space have been solved for the four nucleon system with special interest in their scattering states. We present results concerning the structure of the first $^4$He, scattering states in different (T,S) channels including the first inelatic threshold N+3N$\to$NN+NN and the n-t cross section using realistic potentials.

## Key claim

The four-nucleon continuum spectrum, particularly the n-³H resonant cross section, shows an unusually high sensitivity to NN P-waves, but this sensitivity may be model-dependent; a trivial S-wave-only NN potential (MT I-III) reproduces the resonance peak equally well, suggesting that the resonant structure can arise from exchange mechanisms without requiring NN P-waves.

## Method

Faddeev-Yakubovsky (FY) equations in configuration space were solved for the four-nucleon system, focusing on scattering states. Calculations used realistic NN potentials (AV14, AV18, Nijm II) with and without three-nucleon interactions (TNI), and were compared to results from the Correlated Hyperspherical Method (CHH) and AGS equations in momentum space. The partial wave expansion of FY amplitudes was limited to l_y,z ≤ 2 for the configuration-space FY calculations, while AGS calculations pushed to l_y,z ≤ 3.

## Key numbers

- n-³H singlet scattering length a₀ = 4.31 fm and triplet a₁ = 3.79 fm with AV14 potential (no TNI), giving zero-energy cross section σ(0) = 194 fm²
- n-³H zero-energy cross section with TNI (AV14 + hyperadial): σ(0) = 168 fm², compared to experimental value σ(0) = 170 ± 3 mb (note: text gives 170±3 mb, but table gives 170±3 in fm² units)
- n-³H scattering lengths with AV14 + Urbana VIII TNI: a₀ = 4.08 fm, a₁ = 3.59 fm, a_c = 3.71 fm, σ(0) = 174 fm²
- MT I-III model (S-wave only) gives B₃ = 8.53 MeV (target 8.48) and B₄ = 30.3 MeV (target 29.0), and reproduces the n-³H resonance peak
- p-³He triplet scattering length a₁ = 10.1 ± 0.5 fm (AV14 + Urbana VII, VMC method)
- Low-energy N+3N parameters from MT I-III: S=0,T=0 scattering length a = 14.75 fm, effective range r₀ = 6.75 fm; S=0,T=1: a = 4.13 fm, r₀ = 2.01 fm; S=1,T=0: a = 3.25 fm, r₀ = 1.82 fm; S=1,T=1: a = 3.73 fm, r₀ = 1.87 fm
- First ⁴He 0⁺ excitation: resonance at E_R = 0.40 MeV above p-³H threshold with width Γ = 0.5 MeV (experimental); CRM calculation gives E_R = 0.15 MeV, Γ = 0.23 MeV

## Context

This work reviews calculations of the A=4 continuum spectrum, extending earlier bound-state studies of ³H and ⁴He (GK 1993, VKR 1995, GWHKG 1996, PPCPW 1997) and recent scattering calculations by different groups (VRK 1998, CCG 1999, F 1999). The n-³H results show that unlike the n-d case (where TNI is unnecessary for low-energy cross sections), the four-nucleon system requires three-nucleon forces from the very beginning to reproduce zero-energy data. The paper identifies key open challenges: clarifying the role of NN P-waves in the n-³H resonance via independent calculations, describing the p-³H and n-³He thresholds dominated by the first ⁴He excitation (requiring coupled-channel four-body equations with Coulomb), and calculating weak and electromagnetic capture processes.
