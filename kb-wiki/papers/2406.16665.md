# Scale setting and hadronic properties in light quark sector with $(2+1)$-flavor Wilson fermions at the physical point

> We report scale setting and hadronic properties for our new lattice QCD gauge configuration set (HAL-conf-2023). We employ $(2+1)$-flavor nonperturbatively improved Wilson fermions with stout smearing and the Iwasaki gauge action on a $96^4$ lattice, and generate configurations of 8,000 trajectories at the physical point. We show the basic properties of the configurations such as the plaquette value, topological charge distribution and their auto-correlation times. The scale setting is performed by detailed analyses of the $Ω$ baryon mass. We calculate the physical results of quark masses, decay constants of pseudoscalar mesons and single hadron spectra in light quark sector. The masses of the stable hadrons are found to agree with the experimental values within a sub-percent level.

## Key claim

The authors generate 8,000 trajectories of (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD configurations at the physical point on a 96^4 lattice, and report that stable hadron masses agree with experimental values at the sub-percent level, though the "physical point" is defined using isospin-averaged pseudoscalar meson masses (m_pi = 138 MeV, m_K = 496 MeV) since QED effects are not included.

## Method

The authors employ (2+1)-flavor nonperturbatively O(a)-improved Wilson fermions with stout smearing and the Iwasaki gauge action at beta = 1.82 on a 96^4 lattice, generating configurations through 5 independent Markov chains using the hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm with domain-decomposed HMC, Hasenbusch mass preconditioning, and rational HMC for the strange quark. Scale setting is performed by detailed analysis of the Omega baryon mass, and hadron masses, decay constants, and quark masses are extracted from two-point correlation functions using wall source propagators with simultaneous fits of pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar and pseudoscalar-axial vector correlators.

## Key numbers

- Lattice cutoff: a^{-1} = 2338.8(1.5)(^{+0.2}_{-3.0}) MeV, determined from the Omega baryon mass
- Pion mass: m_pi = 137.1(0.3)(^{+0.0}_{-0.2}) MeV; Kaon mass: m_K = 501.8(0.3)(^{+0.0}_{-0.7}) MeV
- Plaquette value: 0.5039576(3) with bin size of 100 trajectories
- Reference scales: t_0/a^2 = 2.1047(4) and w_0/a = 2.0126(4)
- Topological susceptibility: chi_Q = 4.90(19) x 10^{-6}
- Integrated autocorrelation times: at most 2 MD trajectories for the plaquette and 6 MD trajectories for the topological charge
- Acceptance rate: 87% over 8,000 trajectories

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD Collaboration's previous calculations at (m_pi, m_K) = (146, 525) MeV on the K computer (K-conf) to the physical point, enabled by the Fugaku supercomputer. It builds upon parameter tuning by the PACS Collaboration (Ishikawa 2018, PACS 2019) who generated PACS10 configurations at similar parameters but with only 200 trajectories, whereas this work provides 8,000 trajectories sufficient for nuclear physics studies. The configurations enable next-step calculations of baryon-baryon interactions near the physical point, which is critical for studying bound/virtual dibaryon states previously predicted by the HAL QCD Collaboration such as Omega-Omega, N-Omega, and the exotic T_cc+ state.
