# Unitary coupled-channels model for three-mesons decays of heavy mesons

> A unitary coupled-channels model is presented for investigating the decays of heavy mesons and excited meson states into three light pseudoscalar mesons. The model accounts for the three-mesons final state interactions in the decay processes, as required by both the three-body and two-body unitarity conditions. In the absence of the Z-diagram mechanisms that are necessary consequences of the three-body unitarity, our decay amplitudes are reduced to a form similar to those used in the so-called isobar-model analysis. We apply our coupled-channels model to the three-pions decays of a1(1260), pi2(1670), pi2(2100), and D0 mesons, and show that the Z-diagram mechanisms can contribute to the calculated Dalitz plot distributions by as much as 30% in magnitudes in the regions where f0(600), rho(770), and f2(1270) dominate the distributions. Also, by fitting to the same Dalitz plot distributions, we demonstrate that the decay amplitudes obtained with the unitary model and the isobar model can be rather different, particularly in the phase that plays a crucial role in extracting the CKM CP-violating phase from the data of B meson decays. Our results indicate that the commonly used isobar model analysis must be extended to account for the final state interactions required by the three-body unitarity to reanalyze the three-mesons decays of heavy mesons, thereby exploring hybrid or exotic mesons, and signatures of physics beyond the standard model.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unitary coupled-channels model for three-meson decays of heavy mesons that satisfies both two-body and three-body unitarity conditions; the authors show that the commonly used isobar model neglects Z-diagram mechanisms required by three-body unitarity, and that these mechanisms can contribute up to 30% to Dalitz plot distributions in regions dominated by resonances such as f0(600), rho(770), and f2(1270).

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian with bare vertex interactions for heavy meson decays into a light meson plus a resonance (M* → cR) and for resonance decays into two pseudoscalars (R → ab), plus two-body interactions v_{c'R',cR}. They first determine the R → ab vertex parameters and bare masses by fitting empirical ππ and πK scattering amplitudes. Then they solve coupled-channels equations for cR → c'R' scattering driven by Z-diagram exchange amplitudes, with the transition potentials v set to zero so that only the three-body unitarity-required mechanisms are included. The full decay amplitude for M* → abc is then computed from the dressed M* → cR vertex that incorporates the cR scattering T-matrix, and the results are compared to the isobar model obtained by omitting the Z-diagram contributions.

## Key numbers

- Z-diagram mechanisms contribute up to 30% in magnitudes to calculated Dalitz plot distributions in regions where f0(600), rho(770), and f2(1270) dominate the distributions.
- The model is applied to three-pion decays of a1(1260), pi2(1670), pi2(2100), and D0 mesons.
- The ππ scattering model includes ππ and K̅K channels up to invariant mass W = 2 GeV.
- The bare vertex function for M* → cR uses a cutoff parameter Λ0 = 1 GeV as a scale factor.

## Context

This work extends the unitary three-body scattering formalism of MSL (Matsuyama, Sato, Lee, 2007) to heavy meson decays into three pseudoscalar mesons, addressing the lack of three-body unitarity in the isobar model commonly used in analyses of data from experiments such as E852, CLAS, COMPASS, BABAR, and Belle. The results indicate that the isobar model must be extended to include final state interactions required by three-body unitarity to reliably extract resonance parameters and the CKM CP-violating phase γ from B meson decays, and to search for exotic mesons such as the π1(1600) candidate.
