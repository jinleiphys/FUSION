# The roles of three-nucleon force and continuum coupling in mirror symmetry breaking of oxygen mass region

> With both three-nucleon force and continuum coupling included, we have developed a self-consistent {\it ab initio} Gamow shell model within the Gamow Hartree-Fock (GHF) basis obtained by the realistic interaction itself. With the chiral two-nucleon N$^3$LO and three-nucleon N$^2$LO interactions, the Gamow shell model has been applied to the mirror systems of $Z=8$ neutron-rich isotopes and $N=8$ proton-rich isotones, giving good agreements with data in binding energies, dripline positions and excitation spectra. The GHF calculated that the $0d_{3/2}$, $1s_{1/2}$ and $1p_{3/2}$ orbitals are resonances. The resonance states and their interplay with nonresonant continua play a crucial role in the descriptions of nuclei around driplines. Excitation spectra and Thomas-Ehrman shifts observed can be better described when both three-nucleon force and continuum coupling are considered in calculations. The three-nucleon force and continuum coupling produce a combined effect on the Thomas-Ehrman shift, e.g., for the ${1/2}^+$ resonance level of $^{19}$Na. The calculations help the understandings of related nuclear astrophysical processes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both three-nucleon force (3NF) and continuum coupling are necessary to accurately describe mirror symmetry breaking (Thomas-Ehrman shifts) in the oxygen mass region, but the abstract's claim of "good agreements with data" is qualified by the fact that the 3NF and continuum coupling produce a *combined* effect on the Thomas-Ehrman shift, and that the continuum effect is weak in proton-rich isotones due to the Coulomb barrier.

## Method

The authors developed a self-consistent *ab initio* Gamow shell model (GSM) using a Gamow Hartree-Fock (GHF) basis generated from the same chiral two-nucleon N³LO and three-nucleon N²LO interactions. The GHF basis, which includes bound, resonance, and continuum states, was used with a ¹⁶O core, and the effective Hamiltonian for the valence space was constructed using many-body perturbation theory (S-box and Q-box folded diagrams) in the complex-energy space. The model space included the orbitals {1s₁/₂, 0d₅/₂, 0d₃/₂, 1p₃/₂, 0f₇/₂} with resonance and continuum partial waves for the d₃/₂, p₃/₂, and πs₁/₂ channels.

## Key numbers

- The two-neutron separation energy of ²⁶O is calculated as S₂ₙ = -86 keV in the GSM with 3NF, compared to the experimental value of -18 keV and the real-energy SM value of -2.12 MeV.
- The GSM calculation gives an excitation energy of 4.45 MeV for the 3/₂₁⁺ state in ²³O, compared to the experimental value of 4.00(2) MeV and the real-energy SM value of 5.67 MeV.
- The GSM calculation predicts a two-proton separation energy of 674 keV for ²²Si.
- The calculated width of the 1/₂⁺ resonance level in ¹⁹Na is 0.28 MeV, compared to the experimental width of 0.10 MeV.
- The GSM calculation with 3NF gives S₂ₙ = -86 keV for ²⁶O, while the real-energy SM with 3NF gives S₂ₙ = -2.12 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the *ab initio* Gamow shell model framework by incorporating both chiral three-nucleon forces and continuum coupling self-consistently, building on prior GSM developments with realistic interactions (Sun 2017, Hu 2020) and earlier studies of Thomas-Ehrman shifts (Ogawa 1999, PhysRevC.89.044327). The calculations reproduce the oxygen neutron dripline at ²⁴O and the unbound nature of ²⁵,²⁶O, improving upon real-energy shell-model calculations and VS-IMSRG calculations (PhysRevLett.118.032502) that lack continuum effects. The work enables predictions for the proton-rich N=8 isotones, including ²²Si as a candidate with multiple Thomas-Ehrman shift levels, relevant for astrophysical rp-process reactions such as ¹⁸Ne(2p,γ)²⁰Mg.
