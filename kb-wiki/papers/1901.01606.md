# Open Heavy Flavors in Nuclear Collisions: Theory Overview

> I review the current status and some prospects of theoretical studies on open heavy flavor physics in nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim
The paper argues that while many theoretical models can qualitatively reproduce the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) of open heavy flavor mesons, quantitative agreement requires specific energy loss models and the inclusion of multiple extra ingredients (hadronic rescatterings, coalescence hadronization, temperature-dependent drag coefficients, and Boltzmann versus Langevin transport). The author cautions that the spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s\) alone is insufficient to characterize the interaction, as the momentum dependence of the drag coefficient also plays a crucial role.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical approaches to open heavy flavor production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, classifying models by their treatment of heavy quark interactions (elastic, radiative, or both) and transport implementation (Langevin, Boltzmann, or cross-section based). It compares model predictions for \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) of D mesons, evaluates the extraction of Fokker-Planck coefficients (drag \(\eta_D\) and diffusion \(\kappa_T\)), and discusses recent collective efforts to benchmark models using a common pQCD energy loss scaled by a factor of 5 and a uniform "brick" medium.

## Key numbers
- The spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s\) from lattice QCD around \(T_c\) is of order \(6 \pm 2\), leading to a relaxation time \(\tau_{\rm relax} \approx (3 \pm 1.5)\,{\rm fm}\).
- For c-quarks at freeze-out in a common pQCD (\(\alpha_s=0.4\)) energy loss scaled by a factor of 5, the \(R_{AA}\) lies in a 0.3–0.4 band for 0–10% centrality and 0.4–0.6 for 30–50% centrality in PbPb collisions.
- The uncertainty from hadronization on the ratio \(H_{AA} = dN_D/dp_T / dN_{c,{\rm final}}/dp_T\) is about 25% between models.
- Combined model uncertainties for \(R_{AA}(D)\) are about 15% and for \(v_2(D)\) about 1%, comparable to experimental uncertainties.
- For D mesons with \(p_T > 10\,{\rm GeV}/c\), finite energy corrections to radiative energy loss have at most a 5% influence on \(R_{AA}\).

## Context
This review extends the earlier comprehensive comparison by Andronic (2015) and the review by Prino (2016), which found that \(D_s\) values from models agreeing with data varied by a factor of 5. It discusses recent Bayesian extraction of \(D_s\) by the Duke group (Xu, 2017) and the multi-model comparison by Rapp (2018) that quantified uncertainties from bulk evolution, transport implementation, and hadronization. The paper enables future work by advocating for common baselines (e.g., state-of-the-art bulk evolution) and for testing models against lattice QCD results on charm correlations and meson correlators around \(T_c\).
