# Parameter dependence of the $β$-decay properties of neutron-rich Zr isotopes within the interacting boson model

> We investigate parameter dependence of the calculated $β$-decay properties, as well as low-lying states for the neutron-rich Zr isotopes within the neutron-proton interacting boson model (IBM-2) and interacting boson-fermion-fermion model (IBFFM-2). It is shown that the calculated $\log_{10}ft$ values for the transitions of the $0^+_1$ ground states of the parent even-even nuclei $^{96-102}$Zr into the $1^+_1$ states of the daughter odd-odd nuclei $^{96-102}$Nb consistently exhibit a strong dependence on those parameters associated with the quadrupole-quadrupole boson interaction, and with the residual interaction between an unpaired neutron and an unpaired proton in the IBFFM-2 Hamiltonian for the odd-odd Nb nuclei. By the reduction in magnitude of the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength by approximately a factor of 2, the calculated $\log_{10}ft$ values for the Zr$(0^+_1)\to$Nb$(1^+_1)$ transitions increase and agree with the experimental values. This points to a significant improvement over the previous study performed in the same mass region, that consists of the mapping from a relativistic energy density functional calculation onto the IBM-2 Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The calculated $\log_{10}ft$ values for the $\beta^-$ decay of neutron-rich even-even $^{96-102}$Zr nuclei into odd-odd $^{96-102}$Nb nuclei depend most strongly on the quadrupole-quadrupole boson interaction strength $\kappa_f$ in the daughter boson core and on the residual neutron-proton interaction parameters, rather than on the parent nucleus parameters; reducing the magnitude of $\kappa_f$ by approximately 55% from the mapped values brings the calculated $\log_{10}ft$ values into agreement with experiment, resolving a systematic underestimation found in the previous energy-density-functional-based mapping approach of Nomura (2024).

## Method

The authors employed the neutron-proton interacting boson model (IBM-2) for the even-even parent Zr nuclei and the interacting boson-fermion-fermion model (IBFFM-2) for the odd-odd daughter Nb nuclei, using as a starting point the Hamiltonian parameters previously determined by mapping relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) energy density functional calculations onto the IBM-2. They systematically varied the IBM-2 parameters ($\epsilon_d$, $\kappa$, $\chi_\nu$, $\chi_\pi$), the boson-fermion interaction strengths ($\Gamma_\rho$, $\Lambda_\rho$, $A_\rho$ for $\rho=\nu,\pi$), and the residual neutron-proton interaction parameters ($v_d$ for the $\delta$ interaction and $v_t$ for the tensor interaction) in the IBFFM-2 Hamiltonian, and computed the resulting Gamow-Teller transition matrix elements and $\log_{10}ft$ values for the $0^+_1 \to 1^+_1$ decays of $^{96-102}$Zr into $^{96-102}$Nb.

## Key numbers

- The experimental $\log_{10}ft$ values for the $0^+_1 \to 1^+_1$ transitions are 4.154 for $^{98}$Zr$\to^{98}$Nb, 4.6 for $^{100}$Zr$\to^{100}$Nb, and 4.71 for $^{102}$Zr$\to^{102}$Nb; no experimental value is available for $^{96}$Zr$\to^{96}$Nb.
- The mapped IBM-2 calculation of Nomura (2024) gave $\log_{10}ft \approx 3.5$ for these transitions, systematically underestimating the experimental values.
- Optimal $\kappa_f$ values that reproduce the experimental $\log_{10}ft$ are approximately $-0.180$ MeV for $^{98}$Nb, $-0.150$ MeV for $^{100}$Nb, and $-0.260$ MeV for $^{102}$Nb, corresponding to a reduction in magnitude of about 55% from the mapped values (which were $-0.41$, $-0.35$, and $-0.27$ MeV respectively).
- The $\delta$-interaction strength parameter $v_d = -0.08$ MeV was fixed for all nuclei; the tensor interaction strength $v_t$ varied from 0 MeV ($^{96}$Nb) to 0.500 MeV ($^{100,102}$Nb) in the mapped calculation.

## Context

This work extends the previous study by Nomura (2024), which mapped relativistic energy density functional (RHB with DD-PC1) calculations onto the IBM-2 and IBFFM-2 for neutron-rich nuclei near $N=60$ from Kr to Cd, but found that $\log_{10}ft$ values for Zr$\to$Nb decays were systematically underestimated by a factor of about 1.5. The present analysis identifies the quadrupole-quadrupole boson interaction strength in the daughter nucleus as the key parameter controlling this discrepancy, and demonstrates that a moderate reduction of this strength (rather than an unrealistically large quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant $g_A$) can resolve the disagreement. This enables improved predictions of $\beta$-decay properties for neutron-rich nuclei relevant to the rapid neutron-capture process and to double-$\beta$ decay nuclear matrix element evaluations.
