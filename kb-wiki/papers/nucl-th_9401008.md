# Analysis of Subthreshold Antiproton Production in p-Nucleus and Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions in the RBUU Approach

> We calculate the subthreshold production of antiprotons in the Lorentz-covariant RBUU approach employing a weighted testparticle method to treat the antiproton propagation and absorption nonperturbatively. We find that the pbar differential cross sections are highly sensitive to the baryon and antiproton selfenergies in the dense baryonic environment. Adopting the baryon scalar and vector selfenergies from the empirical optical potential for proton-nucleus elastic scattering and from Dirac-Brueckner calculations at higher density rho > rho_0 we examine the differential pbar spectra as a function of the antiproton selfenergy. A detailed comparison with the available experimental data for p-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus reactions shows that the antiproton feels a moderately attractive mean-field at normal nuclear matter density rho_0 which is in line with a dispersive potential extracted from the free annihilation cross section.

## Key claim

The antiproton feels a moderately attractive mean-field at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀, which is consistent with a dispersive potential extracted from the free annihilation cross section, but the real part of the antiproton selfenergy cannot be determined consistently across different models and must be treated as a free parameter in the transport model.

## Method

The authors employed the Lorentz-covariant Relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) approach with momentum-dependent baryon selfenergies, using a weighted testparticle method to propagate antiprotons and treat their annihilation nonperturbatively via individual rate equations. Antiproton production was calculated perturbatively by summing incoherently over all baryon-baryon collisions, assuming the elementary production cross section follows a parametrization of the free pp → p̄+X cross section. The baryon scalar and vector selfenergies were taken from the empirical optical potential for proton-nucleus elastic scattering and from Dirac-Brueckner calculations at higher densities ρ > ρ₀, while the antiproton selfenergy was treated as a free parameter to be determined by comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For an antiproton at rest at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, the real part of the antiproton selfenergy from the dispersive model ranges from -175 MeV to -100 MeV depending on the parametrization of the absorption cross section.
- The elementary antiproton production cross section parametrization used is σ_BB→p̄+X = 0.01 (√s − √s₀)^(1.846) mb, with √s₀ = 4m_N and m_N = 0.9383 GeV/c².
- For Si+Si at 2.1 GeV/u, the antiproton production cross section prior to annihilation is enhanced by approximately a factor of 70 compared to a relativistic cascade calculation with no in-medium effects.
- The antiproton production probability is enhanced by roughly one order of magnitude when using the NL3 parameter set (m*/m = 0.7 at ρ₀) compared to NL1 or NL2 (m*/m = 0.83 at ρ₀) for both Au+Au and Si+Si at 2.1 GeV/u.
- Almost all antiprotons are produced at densities ρ ≥ 3ρ₀ in the heavy Au+Au system, while the majority are created between 2ρ₀ and 3ρ₀ in the lighter Si+Si system at 2.1 GeV/u.
- Pauli-blocking effects reduce the antiproton production probability by about 10 to 20% for a central Au+Au collision at 2.1 GeV/u.

## Context

This work extends earlier nonequilibrium transport calculations by Batko et al. (1991) and Huang et al. (Faessl) that used the VUU transport equation, which suffered from an approximate geometrical treatment of the annihilation channel and neglect of momentum-dependent baryon selfenergies. It also improves upon preliminary relativistic transport results reported by Cassing et al. (1992) by including explicit antiproton propagation in time-dependent potentials and nonperturbative annihilation treatment. The study enables systematic determination of the antiproton selfenergy in dense nuclear matter through comparison with experimental data from p-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus reactions at JINR, BEVALAC, KEK, and GSI, and provides a framework for probing the nuclear equation of state via subthreshold antiproton production.
