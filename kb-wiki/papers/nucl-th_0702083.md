# Bjorken expansion with gradual freeze out

> The freeze out of the expanding systems, created in relativistic heavy ion collisions, will be discussed. We combine kinetic freeze out equations with Bjorken type system expansion into a unified model. Such a model is a more physical generalization of the earlier simplified non-expanding freeze out models. We shall see that the basic freeze out features, pointed out in the earlier works, are not smeared out by the expansion.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including Bjorken-type expansion simultaneously with gradual freeze out does not smear out the universal features of the final post-freeze-out particle distributions that were previously observed in non-expanding freeze out models, provided the freeze out layer thickness is several times the freeze out time scale.

## Method

The authors construct a kinetic freeze out model where the fireball undergoes Bjorken expansion (phase I) followed by a simultaneous expansion and gradual freeze out in a layer of finite thickness \(L\) between proper times \(\tau_1\) and \(\tau_1+L\) (phase II). They split the distribution function into interacting and frozen-out components, write coupled differential equations for their energy and baryon densities, and solve them analytically for the interacting component. The free component is then obtained by integrating the kinetic equation, and the final post-freeze-out spectra are evaluated for a massive pion gas with a Jüttner equilibrium distribution.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.05\ \mathrm{fm}\) and initial temperature \(T_i(\tau_0) = 820\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Freeze out start time \(\tau_1 = 5\ \mathrm{fm}\), at which the interacting temperature reaches \(T_{FO} = 180\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Freeze out time scale \(\tau_{FO} = 0.5\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- Gold nucleus radius \(R_{Au} = 7.685\ \mathrm{fm}\) used for the transverse area \(A_{xy} = \pi R_{Au}^2\).
- For a freeze out layer thickness \(L\) of several \(\tau_{FO}\), the final post-freeze-out distribution is very close to that for an infinitely long freeze out, with differences only at very small momenta.

## Context

This work extends earlier gradual freeze out models that did not include system expansion (old_SL_FO, old_TL_FO, Mo05a, Mo05b) by combining kinetic freeze out equations with Bjorken-type expansion on a simultaneous basis, rather than the sequential approach of old_TL_FO where expansion stops before freeze out. The model enables analytical treatment of freeze out in a layer of any thickness from zero to infinity, and shows that long gradual freeze out produces substantial entropy, which must be checked for physical consistency.
