# Chiral Dynamics from Dyson-Schwinger Equations

> A strongly momentum-dependent dressed-quark mass function is basic to QCD. It is central to the appearance of a constituent-quark mass-scale and an existential prerequisite for Goldstone modes. Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) studies have long emphasised these facts and are a natural way to exploit them.

# Chiral Dynamics from Dyson-Schwinger Equations

## Key claim
The Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework provides a nonperturbative, systematic, and symmetry-preserving truncation that proves Goldstone's theorem in QCD and yields a generalized Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation valid for all pseudoscalar mesons, including heavy-light and heavy-heavy systems, but only within a measurable domain of current-quark mass where a perturbative chiral expansion is uniformly valid.

## Method
The authors employ Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) and their symmetry-preserving truncations to study dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) in QCD. They analyze the gap equation and its DCSB solution, deriving a generalized relation between the leptonic decay constant, meson mass, and current-quark mass from the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity. The formalism is applied to pseudoscalar mesons, including radial excitations and heavy-light systems, and extended to scalar mesons and sigma terms.

## Key numbers
- The chiral expansion in current-quark mass around the chiral limit (hat m = 0) fails at a critical current-quark mass hat m_cr ~ 60–70 MeV.
- At the critical current-quark mass, the mass of a flavour-nonsinglet 0- meson constituted of equal mass quarks is m_0- ~ 0.45 GeV.
- For all pseudoscalar mesons with n >= 1 (radial excitations), the leptonic decay constant in the chiral limit is f_pi_n^0 = 0 when chiral symmetry is dynamically broken.
- In the absence of DCSB, the leptonic decay constant of all pseudoscalar mesons vanishes in the chiral limit: f_pi_n^0 = 0 for all n >= 0.
- The leading-order ultraviolet power-law behaviour of the transition form factor for excited-state pseudoscalar mesons in the chiral limit is O(1/Q^4).

## Context
This work extends the DSE-based approach to chiral dynamics established by Maris, Roberts, and Tandy (1998) and the symmetry-preserving truncation developed by Bhagwat and collaborators. It enables systematic studies of meson properties across the full range of quark masses, from the chiral limit to heavy quarks, and provides a framework for analyzing excited-state mesons and scalar mesons. The results for scalar mesons are compatible with a picture of the lightest 0++ as a dressed-quark–antiquark bound state supplemented by a pion cloud, as studied by Holl et al. (2005).
