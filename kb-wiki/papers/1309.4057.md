# Magnetic Moments of Bottom Baryons: Effective mass and Screened Charge

> We calculate the magnetic moments of low lying heavy flavor bottom baryons using effective quark mass and shielded quark charge scheme. We obtain the magnetic moments of both $J^P = 1/2^+$ and $J^P = 3/2^+$ baryon states. We compare our predictions with other theoretical approaches.

# Wiki Page: Magnetic Moments of Bottom Baryons

## Key claim
The magnetic moments of low-lying bottom baryons (both J^P = 1/2^+ and J^P = 3/2^+ states) are calculated using an effective quark mass scheme and a screened quark charge scheme, with the hedge that the non-relativistic constituent quark model approach is justified by its equivalence to a parametrization of relativistic field theory for static baryon properties, as argued by Morpurgo and by Durand et al.

## Method
The authors first extract constituent quark masses (u, d, s, c, b) and hyperfine interaction parameters b_ij from known baryon masses (N, Delta, Lambda, Lambda_c, Lambda_b, Sigma, Omega, Sigma_c, Sigma_b, Sigma*_b). Effective quark masses inside each baryon are then computed by adding spin-dependent hyperfine corrections to the bare quark masses, using coefficients alpha and beta determined from the spin structure of the baryon state. Magnetic moments are obtained by sandwiching the operator mu = sum_i (e_i / (2 m_i^eff)) sigma_i between the appropriate baryon wave functions, and the screened charge scheme further modifies the quark charge e_i by contributions from neighboring quarks using parameters fitted to the proton, neutron, and Lambda magnetic moments.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark masses used: m_u = m_d = 362 MeV, m_s = 539 MeV, m_c = 1710 MeV, m_b = 5043 MeV
- Hyperfine interaction parameters: b_uu = b_ud = b_dd = 196 MeV; b_us = b_ds = 118 MeV; b_ss = 76 MeV; b_uc = b_dc = 28 MeV; b_ub = b_db = 7 MeV; b_bb = 0.5 MeV
- Magnetic moment of Lambda_b^0: -0.062 nuclear magneton (effective mass scheme), -0.060 nuclear magneton (screened charge scheme)
- Magnetic moment of Sigma_b^+: 2.190 nuclear magneton (effective mass scheme), 2.177 nuclear magneton (screened charge scheme)
- Magnetic moment of Omega_b^-: -0.741 nuclear magneton (effective mass scheme), -0.863 nuclear magneton (screened charge scheme)
- Magnetic moment of Omega_bbb^{*-} (triply heavy, J^P = 3/2^+): -0.186 nuclear magneton (effective mass scheme), -0.198 nuclear magneton (screened charge scheme)
- Mass of Lambda_b: 5.620 GeV (calculated), compared to experimental value 5.619 GeV
- Mass of Sigma_b: 5.808 GeV (calculated), compared to experimental value 5.813 GeV

## Context
This work extends the effective quark mass and screened charge formalism previously applied by Kumar, Dhir and Verma to spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 charmed baryons, now applied to the bottom baryon sector. The predictions are compared with results from the MIT bag model (1981), hypercentral potential model (2008), relativistic three-quark model (2005), light cone QCD sum rules (2007, 2012), NRQM using AL1 potential (2005), and power-law potential model (1996). The calculations provide theoretical guidance for future experimental measurements of bottom baryon magnetic moments at facilities such as LHCb, where doubly and triply heavy baryons may be detected.
