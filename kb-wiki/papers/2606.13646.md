# Observable Dependence of Viscous Corrections in QGP: Heavy Quarks and Dileptons in Chapman--Enskog Theory

> We calculate, for the first time, heavy quark transport and thermal dilepton production from QGP using viscous correction up to second order in gradients. We use the form of viscous correction obtained from Chapman-Enskog like expansion of the Boltzmann transport equation in relaxation time approximation, and compare our results with that of Grad's 14-moment approximation. By employing the temperature and shear stress evolution profiles of QGP obtained from second-order causal relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, we study the heavy quark transport coefficients and thermal dilepton production from an evolving QGP. In the case of HQ transport, the CE corrections suppress the drag force substantially, induce a non-trivial momentum dependence in transverse momentum diffusion, and result in a comparatively less modification in longitudinal momentum diffusion. Whereas, for thermal dileptons, the CE corrections result in an enhanced early-time contribution which decreases and become converging to the first-order CE correction with the evolution of QGP, and remain well behaved compared to that of Grad's correction. Our results indicate that the modification of the observable due to viscous corrections is governed by the magnitude of the corrections as well as the interplay between their momentum dependence and momentum weighting of the transport and emission kernels. We demonstrate that the momentum structure of the various viscous corrections at the level of distribution function is not directly translated to the observables since the different observables are sensitive to distinct regions of momentum space.

# Observable Dependence of Viscous Corrections in QGP: Heavy Quarks and Dileptons in Chapman-Enskog Theory

## Key claim

The momentum structure of viscous corrections at the level of the distribution function does not directly translate to observables, because different observables (heavy quark transport coefficients versus thermal dilepton production) are sensitive to distinct regions of momentum space; the modification of each observable is governed by both the magnitude of the viscous corrections and the interplay between their momentum dependence and the momentum weighting of the transport and emission kernels.

## Method

The authors compute heavy quark transport coefficients (drag force, transverse and longitudinal momentum diffusion) and thermal dilepton production rates using viscous corrections to the quark and gluon distribution functions up to second order in gradients, obtained from a Chapman-Enskog-like iterative solution of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation. These results are compared with those from Grad's 14-moment approximation, using temperature and shear stress evolution profiles from second-order causal relativistic viscous hydrodynamics under longitudinal boost-invariant Bjorken flow, with a conformal equation of state for massless quarks and gluons.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to $m_c = 1.5$ GeV with degeneracy factor $\gamma_c = 6$
- Strong coupling fixed at $\alpha_s = 0.3$
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio fixed at $\eta/s = 1/4\pi$
- Number of colors $N_c = 3$ and number of flavors $N_f = 2$
- Debye mass given by $\mu_D = \sqrt{4\pi \alpha_s}\, T$
- Thermal masses: $m_q^2(T) = g^2 T^2 / 6$ for quarks, $m_g^2(T) = (1 + N_f/6) g^2 T^2 / 3$ for gluons
- Spatial diffusion coefficient defined as $D_s = m_c / [T A(p \to 0)]$, with thermalization time $\tau_{th} = m_c D_s / T$

## Context

This work extends prior studies of first-order viscous corrections to heavy quark transport (Das 2012, Kurian 2020, Singh 2019, Shaikh 2021) and thermal dilepton production (Dusling 2008, Bhatt 2011, Bhalerao 2013, Vujanovic 2013, Chandra 2015, Naik 2022) by incorporating second-order Chapman-Enskog corrections for the first time. It builds on the second-order CE correction formalism derived by Bhalerao (2013) and compares with the standard Grad 14-moment approximation (Grad 1949, Israel 1979). The results enable systematic understanding of how non-equilibrium corrections propagate from microscopic distribution functions to experimentally accessible QGP observables, establishing which viscous correction scheme is appropriate for different momentum-space-sensitive probes.
