# $Λ^{\ast}(1405)$-matter: stable or unstable?

> A recent suggestion [PLB 774 (2017) 522] that purely-$Λ^{\ast}(1405)$ nuclei provide the absolute minimum energy in charge-neutral baryon matter for baryon-number $A\gtrsim 8$, is tested within RMF calculations. A broad range of $Λ^{\ast}$ interaction strengths, commensurate with $(\bar K \bar K NN)_{I=0}$ binding energy assumed to be of order 100 MeV, is scanned. It is found that the binding energy per $Λ^{\ast}$, $B/A$, saturates for $A\gtrsim 120$ with values of $B/A$ considerably below 100 MeV, implying that $Λ^{\ast}(1405)$ matter is highly unstable against strong decay to $Λ$ and $Σ$ hyperon aggregates. The central density of $Λ^{\ast}$ matter is found to saturate as well, at roughly twice nuclear matter density. Moreover, it is shown that the underlying very strong $\bar K N$ potentials, fitted for isospin $I=0$ to the mass and width values of $Λ^{\ast}(1405)$, fail to reproduce values of single-nucleon absorption fractions deduced across the periodic table from $K^-$ capture-at-rest bubble chamber experiments.

## Key claim
The paper tests the 2017 claim by Akaishi and Yamazaki that purely-Λ*(1405) aggregates become absolutely stable for baryon number A ≳ 8, and finds that within relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations the binding energy per Λ* saturates at values considerably below 100 MeV, implying such matter is highly unstable against strong decay to Λ and Σ hyperon aggregates.

## Method
The authors performed RMF calculations of purely-Λ* nuclei using linear (HS) and nonlinear (NL-SH) RMF models, with Λ* baryons coupled to isoscalar-scalar σ and isoscalar-vector ω meson fields. The coupling strengths were scaled to reproduce a two-body Λ*Λ* binding energy of 40 MeV, derived from the (E-indep.)_a scenario of energy-independent K̅N potentials that identify the Λ*(1405) with a quasibound state 27 MeV below threshold. The resulting binding energies per baryon and density distributions were computed for closed-shell systems up to A=168.

## Key numbers
- The binding energy per Λ*, B/A, saturates for A ≳ 120 at values between roughly 30 to 80 MeV, depending on the RMF modeling details
- Central densities saturate at values about twice nuclear matter density, in the range 0.3–0.45 fm⁻³
- The radius parameter r₀ in Λ* nuclei saturates at values about 0.7–0.8 fm, compared to 0.9–1.0 fm in atomic nuclei
- The two-body Λ*Λ* binding energy of 40 MeV was used as input, derived from a (K̅K̅NN)_{I=0} binding energy of 93 MeV in the (E-indep.)_a scenario
- The energy-independent K̅N potentials (YA) produce χ² of 150 for 65 kaonic atom data points, significantly worse than chiral EFT models which achieve χ² of 110–120

## Context
This work directly contradicts the suggestion by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2017) that purely-Λ*(1405) nuclei become absolutely stable for A ≳ 8, extending their earlier conjecture from 2004. The paper also shows that the strong energy-independent K̅N potentials used by Yamazaki and Akaishi (2007) fail to reproduce single-nucleon absorption fractions from K⁻ capture-at-rest bubble chamber experiments, unlike chiral EFT models such as the Kyoto-Munich (KM) and Prague (P) models. The results are consistent with earlier RMF calculations of multi-K̅ nuclei by Gazda, Friedman, Gal, and Mareš (2008), which found saturation of binding energies and densities, and support the conclusion that SU(3)-octet hyperons provide the lowest energy strange hadronic matter configurations.
