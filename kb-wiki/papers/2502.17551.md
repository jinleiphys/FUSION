# Emergent Hydrodynamic Mode on SU(2) Plaquette Chains and Quantum Simulation

> We search for emergent hydrodynamic modes in real-time Hamiltonian dynamics of $2+1$-dimensional SU(2) lattice gauge theory on a quasi one dimensional plaquette chain, by numerically computing symmetric correlation functions of energy densities on lattice sizes of about $20$ with the local Hilbert space truncated at $j_{\rm max}=\frac{1}{2}$. Because of the Umklapp processes, we only find a mode for energy diffusion. The symmetric correlator exhibits transport peak near zero frequency with a width approximately proportional to momentum squared at small momentum, when the system is fully quantum ergodic, as indicated by the eigenenergy level statistics. This transport peak leads to a power-law $t^{-\frac{1}{2}}$ decay of the symmetric correlator at late time, also known as the long-time tail, as well as diffusion-like spreading in position space. We also introduce a quantum algorithm for computing the symmetric correlator on a quantum computer and find it gives results consistent with exact diagonalization when tested on the IBM emulator. Finally we discuss the future prospect of searching for the sound modes.

## Key claim

The authors find that only an energy diffusion mode emerges in the real-time Hamiltonian dynamics of SU(2) lattice gauge theory on a quasi-one-dimensional plaquette chain, and no sound modes are observed, because Umklapp processes break momentum conservation on the lattice at the couplings studied with the local Hilbert space truncated at j_max = 1/2.

## Method

The authors exactly diagonalize the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian of 2+1-dimensional SU(2) pure gauge theory on a plaquette chain, which is mapped onto a spin system when the local Hilbert space is truncated at j_max = 1/2. They compute the real-time symmetric correlation function of energy densities in frequency-momentum space and in position space on lattice sizes of N=17 and N=20, and fit the transport peaks and long-time tails to extract the energy diffusion coefficient.

## Key numbers

- On a periodic N=20 plaquette chain at coupling ag^2=1.2 and temperature T=20, the ratio of fitted widths b(n_k=2)/b(n_k=1) is 3.83, close to the diffusive prediction of 4.
- On the same N=20 chain at ag^2=0.8 and T=5, the ratio b(n_k=2)/b(n_k=1) is 3.91.
- The extracted energy diffusion coefficient D_epsilon at ag^2=1.2 ranges from 0.213 (T=20) to 0.243 (T=5) in lattice units.
- At ag^2=0.8, D_epsilon ranges from 0.647 (T=20) to 0.693 (T=5) in lattice units.
- The symmetric correlator at late time (t > 100) on an open N=17 chain exhibits power-law decay t^{-1/2}, with fitted coefficient c_1 = 1.83 at ag^2=1.2 and T=20, and c_1 = 0.891 at ag^2=0.8 and T=20.

## Context

This work extends the Hamiltonian lattice gauge theory approach to calculating shear viscosity and transport coefficients, previously studied by Cohen (2021) and Turro (2024), by demonstrating that hydrodynamic behavior (specifically energy diffusion) can emerge on lattice sizes of about 20 sites. The results contradict the expectation that continuum sound modes would appear at these lattice sizes; instead, Umklapp processes suppress momentum conservation as discussed by Arnold (1997). The work enables future quantum simulation of these correlators, as demonstrated on the IBM emulator, and motivates searches for sound modes on larger lattices with higher j_max truncation.
