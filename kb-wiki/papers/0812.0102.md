# Chiral effective field theory calculations of neutrino processes in dense matter

> We calculate neutrino processes involving two nucleons at subnuclear densities using chiral effective field theory. Shorter-range noncentral forces reduce the neutrino rates significantly compared with the one-pion exchange approximation currently used in supernova simulations. For densities rho < 10^{14} g cm^{-3}, we find that neutrino rates are well constrained by nuclear interactions and nucleon-nucleon scattering data. As an application, we calculate the mean-square energy transfer in scattering of a neutrino from nucleons and find that collision processes and spin-dependent mean-field effects dominate over the energy transfer due to nucleon recoil.

## Key claim

The paper claims that chiral effective field theory calculations of neutrino processes in dense matter show that shorter-range noncentral forces reduce neutrino rates significantly compared to the one-pion exchange (OPE) approximation currently used in supernova simulations, but this reduction is well constrained by nuclear interactions and nucleon-nucleon scattering data only for densities below 10^14 g cm^-3.

## Method

The authors calculated the spin relaxation rate C_sigma for neutrino-pair bremsstrahlung and absorption in neutron matter using chiral effective field theory interactions up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N^3LO). They employed a Landau Fermi liquid theory framework with a relaxation time approximation to compute the spin response function, evaluating the spin trace in two-body spin space using a partial-wave expansion. The strong interaction was treated in Born approximation, and results were compared with calculations based on phase shifts and renormalization group evolved low-momentum interactions (V_lowk).

## Key numbers

- The saturation density rho_0 = 2.8 x 10^14 g cm^-3
- For densities rho < 10^14 g cm^-3 (k_F < 1.2 fm^-1), the spin response is well constrained and all results lie within a band significantly reduced compared to OPE
- At k_F = 1.0 fm^-1, the NLO 2pi-exchange tensor force added to OPE increases C_sigma to 0.29 MeV^-1, but this is compensated by repulsive shorter-range noncentral contact interactions
- A fit to the results gives C_sigma/MeV = 0.86 (k_F/fm^-1)^3.6 / [1 + 10.9 (k_F/fm^-1)^3.6]
- The effective mass approximation used is m*/m approx 1.12 - 0.17 (k_F/fm^-1) over the relevant range

## Context

This work extends the one-pion exchange approximation used in supernova simulations by Raffelt (1996) and others, and builds on the Landau Fermi liquid approach to neutrino processes developed by Lykasov, Pethick, and Schwenk (2008). The results enable improved neutrino transport calculations in core-collapse supernova simulations by providing systematically constrained rates that replace the standard OPE-based rates from Friman and Maxwell (1979) and Hannestad and Raffelt (1998). Future work will need to include neutron-proton mixtures, charged currents, and three-nucleon interactions at higher densities.
