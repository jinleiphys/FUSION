# Properties of charmed and bottom hadrons in nuclear matter: A plausible study

> Changes in properties of heavy hadrons with a charm or a bottom quark are studied in nuclear matter. Effective masses (scalar potentials) for the hadrons are calculated using quark-meson coupling model. Our results also suggest that the heavy baryons containing a charm or a bottom quark will form charmed or bottom hypernuclei, which was first predicted in mid 70's. In addition a possibility of $B^-$-nuclear bound (atomic) states is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that heavy baryons containing a charm or bottom quark will form charmed or bottom hypernuclei in nuclear matter, as first predicted in the mid-1970s, and that the scalar potential felt by these hadrons follows a simple light-quark number scaling rule relative to the nucleon scalar potential.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, a mean-field Hartree approximation, to calculate the effective masses (scalar potentials) of charmed and bottom hadrons embedded in infinite, uniform symmetric nuclear matter. The Dirac equations for light (u,d) and heavy (c,b) quarks inside MIT bags were solved self-consistently with constant mean-field scalar and vector potentials, and hadron masses in medium were obtained from the bag stability condition. The same quark-meson coupling constants fitted to nuclear saturation were used for light quarks in all hadrons, with an enhanced vector coupling (factor 1.4^2) applied to mesons treated as pseudo-Goldstone bosons (K, D, B).

## Key numbers

- Current quark masses used: m_q = 5 MeV, m_s = 250 MeV, m_c = 1300 MeV, m_b = 4200 MeV.
- Free-space nucleon bag radius: R_N = 0.8 fm.
- The ratio of effective mass to free mass decreases with density in the order: hadrons with only light quarks > with one strange quark > with one charm quark > with one bottom quark.
- For QMC-II versus QMC-I, the difference in effective masses is typically ~10% or less, and less than ~8% for hyperon effective masses.
- The scalar potential for a hadron h follows V_s^h ≈ (n_q + n_qbar)/3 * V_s^N, where n_q (n_qbar) is the number of light quarks (antiquarks) in the hadron.

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model, previously applied successfully to hypernuclei (Tsushima 1998) and kaon systems (Tsushima 1998), to the first study of heavy baryons with charm or bottom quarks in nuclear matter. It builds on theoretical predictions of charmed hypernuclei from the mid-1970s (Tyapkin 1975, Dover 1977) and anticipates experimental searches at the Japan Hadron Facility (JHF), which will produce charmed hadrons profusely at 50 GeV beam energy. The results enable quantitative predictions for finite charmed and bottom hypernuclei and suggest that B- mesons will form nuclear bound (atomic) states complementary to D- nuclear bound states.
