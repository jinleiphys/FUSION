# A Momentum Transformation Connecting a NN Potential in the Nonrelativistic and the Relativistic Two-Nucleon Schroedinger Equation

> An analytical relation between center of mass momenta in a nonrelativistic and a relativistic two-nucleon Schrödinger equation is proposed which allows to analytically rewrite the two Schrödinger equations into each other. As a consequence a NN potential occurring in the relativistic Schrödinger equation can be gained from a nonrelativistic one by an analytical procedure. The S-matrices in the two equations are exactly identical and therefore the two-nucleon phase shifts.

# A Momentum Transformation Connecting a NN Potential in the Nonrelativistic and the Relativistic Two-Nucleon Schroedinger Equation

## Key claim
The authors propose an exact analytical transformation between momenta in the nonrelativistic and relativistic two-nucleon Schroedinger equations that allows a nonrelativistic NN potential to be converted into a relativistic one without refitting parameters, yielding exactly identical S-matrices and phase shifts. However, the resulting relativistic potential is not a fully relativistic NN potential derived from field theory, but merely a formal connection treating potentials as "black boxes" with no reference to whether they had relativistic background originally.

## Method
The authors define a relation between the nonrelativistic momentum q and the relativistic momentum p via 2*sqrt(m^2 + p^2) = 2m + q^2/m, which is solved to give p = q*sqrt(1 + q^2/(4m^2)). Using this momentum transformation together with a wave function scaling factor h(q) = sqrt((1 + q^2/(2m^2))*sqrt(1 + q^2/(4m^2))), they derive explicit formulas (Eqs. 15 and 16) that convert the nonrelativistic potential V(q,q') into the relativistic potential U(p,p') and vice versa for each partial wave (l,s,j). They then verify that the Lippmann-Schwinger equations transform correspondingly and that the S-matrices are identical.

## Key numbers
- The momentum transformation p = q * sqrt(1 + q^2/(4m^2)) relates relativistic momentum p to nonrelativistic momentum q, where m is the nucleon mass.
- For small momenta, p ≈ q(1 + q^2/(8m^2)) and q ≈ p(1 - p^2/(8m^2)).
- For large momenta, p ≈ q^2/(2m).
- The scaling factor h(q) = sqrt((1 + q^2/(2m^2))*sqrt(1 + q^2/(4m^2))) appears in the potential transformation.
- The deuteron wave function components show large differences between relativistic and nonrelativistic cases above about 5 fm^{-1}.

## Context
This work extends earlier approximate relations between nonrelativistic and relativistic potentials introduced by Gloeckle, Lee and Coester (1986) and by Coester, Pieper and Serduke (1975), and addresses formal relations using mass operators by Keister and Polyzou (1991) which did not allow explicit construction of the relativistic potential. The transformation enables existing nonrelativistic NN potentials such as CD Bonn (Machleidt, Sammarruca, Song 1996), Nijmegen (Stoks et al. 1994), and Argonne v18 (Wiringa, Stoks, Schiavilla 1995) to be used in relativistic few-nucleon calculations for systems with A > 2, as previously attempted by Carlson, Pandharipande, Schiavilla (1993) and Forest et al. (1995) through refitting.
