# Freeze-out from HBT and Coulomb Effects

> The freeze-out of hot and dense hadronic matter formed in relativistic nuclear collisions is probed by HBT interferometry of identical pions, kaons, etc. Coulomb repulsion/attraction of positive/negative particles show up at small particle momenta and is also very sensitive to the freeze-out conditions. The source sizes and times freeze-out are extracted from $π^-/π^+$ spectra and HBT radii and compared.

## Key claim

The freeze-out radii extracted from Coulomb effects on \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratios are compatible with those from HBT analyses, but the interpretation of the outward HBT radius \(R_o\) is highly model-dependent and cannot yet be used to extract the duration of emission because opacity and transverse flow effects can reduce \(R_o\) relative to \(R_s\).

## Method

The author models the Coulomb kick \(p_c\) received by charged pions from the net charge rapidity distribution in a longitudinally expanding Bjorken source, then fits the resulting \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratio formula to data at SIS, AGS, and SPS energies. Separately, HBT radii for transparent and opaque sources are derived from fluctuations in freeze-out position and time, and compared to experimental sideward (\(R_s\)), outward (\(R_o\)), and longitudinal (\(R_l\)) radii from NA44, NA49, and other collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb kick extracted from AGS data: \(p_c^{AGS} \simeq 20\) MeV/c; from SPS data: \(p_c^{SPS} \simeq 10\) MeV/c.
- Freeze-out radius from Coulomb effects at both AGS and SPS: \(R_f \simeq 10\) fm.
- At SIS energies, Coulomb energy \(V_c \simeq 27\) MeV and source radius \(R \simeq 8\) fm for Au+Au collisions with 14% centrality (\(Z=110\)).
- Sideward HBT radius \(R_s \simeq 5\)–\(6\) fm in central Au or Pb collisions at AGS and SPS.
- Longitudinal HBT radius \(R_l \simeq 5.5\) fm at AGS and SPS; corresponding freeze-out time \(\tau_f \simeq 8\) fm/c.
- Freeze-out radius from transverse flow (\(\beta \simeq 0.5\)–\(0.6c\)) and geometry: \(R_f \simeq 11\)–\(12\) fm.
- Geometrical radius for near-central Au or Pb collisions: \(R_{geom} \simeq 6\) fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier Coulomb-effect calculations by Barz (1997) and HBT analyses by Csorgo (1996) and Heinz (1996) to a unified comparison of freeze-out radii across SIS, AGS, and SPS energies. It enables future studies to disentangle opacity, transverse flow, and emission duration effects in HBT, and suggests that the NA44 data (\(R_o \lesssim R_s\)) may require opacity effects not needed for the NA49 data (\(R_o > R_s\)).
