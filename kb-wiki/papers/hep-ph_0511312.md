# Dropping rho and A_1 Meson Masses at Chiral Phase Transition in the Generalized Hidden Local Symmetry

> We study the chiral symmetry restoration using the generalized hidden local symmetry (GHLS) which incorporates the rho and A_1 mesons as the gauge bosons of the GHLS and the pion as the Nambu-Goldstone boson consistently with the chiral symmetry of QCD. We show that a set of parameter relations, which ensures the first and second Weinberg's sum rules, is invariant under the renormalization group evolution. Then, we found that the Weinberg's sum rules together with the matching of the vector and axial-vector current correlators inevitably leads to {\it the dropping masses of both rho and A_1 mesons} at the symmetry restoration point, and that the mass ratio as well as the mixing angle between the pion and A_1 meson flows into one of three fixed points.

## Key claim

The chiral symmetry restoration in the generalized hidden local symmetry (GHLS), when the first and second Weinberg sum rules are required to be satisfied through matching to QCD, can only be realized with masses of both rho and A_1 mesons vanishing at the restoration point, and the mass ratio between them flows into one of three fixed points (VM-like, Ginzburg-Landau-like, or Hybrid-like) rather than being uniquely determined.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral perturbation theory based on the generalized hidden local symmetry (GHLS), which incorporates rho and A_1 mesons as gauge bosons and the pion as a Nambu-Goldstone boson. They performed one-loop calculations using the background field gauge in 't Hooft-Feynman gauge, identified quadratic divergences via dimensional regularization with poles at n=2, and derived renormalization group equations (RGEs) for the parameters a, b, c, d and the gauge coupling g. They then matched the vector and axial-vector current correlators from the GHLS to those from the operator product expansion (OPE) in QCD, requiring the high-energy behavior to agree, which yielded the pole-saturated forms of the first and second Weinberg sum rules.

## Key numbers

* The masses of rho and A_1 mesons are given by M_rho = g F_sigma and M_A1 = g F_q, where g is the GHLS gauge coupling and F_sigma, F_q are decay constants; both masses vanish as g approaches the fixed point g* = 0.
* The matching condition relating the GHLS to QCD gives F_A1^2 M_A1^4 - F_rho^2 M_rho^4 = (32 pi alpha_s / 9) <bar{q} q>^2, which is proportional to the square of the quark condensate.
* The RGE for the gauge coupling g with a=b and d=0 is mu d g^2 / d mu = - (N_f / (4 pi)^2) (43/3) g^4, which has a fixed point at g* = 0.
* The mass ratio zeta = M_rho^2 / M_A1^2 = a / (a + c) is controlled by the parameters a and c, and the pion decay constant is F_pi^2 = (a c / (a + c)) F^2.

## Context

This work extends the vector manifestation (VM) framework of Harada and Yamawaki (2001), which previously assumed axial-vector mesons were decoupled near the phase transition, to include the A_1 meson as a dynamical degree of freedom via the GHLS. It contradicts earlier model analyses (such as those cited as rhoA1model) that found no significant reduction of axial-vector meson masses. The results enable systematic study of chiral symmetry restoration patterns in hot and/or dense QCD, providing a theoretical basis for interpreting experimental signals of dropping meson masses observed by KEK-PS E325, CBELSA/TAPS, STAR, and NA60 collaborations.
