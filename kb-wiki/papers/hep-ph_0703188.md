# Separable Dyson-Schwinger model at zero and finite T

> Theoretical understanding of experimental results from relativistic heavy-ion collisions requires a microscopic approach to the behavior of QCD n-point functions at finite temperatures, as given by the hierarchy of Dyson-Schwinger equations, properly generalized within the Matsubara formalism. The technical complexity of related finite-temperature calculations however mandates modeling. We present a model where the QCD interaction in the infrared, nonperturbative domain, is modeled by a separable form. Results for the mass spectrum of light quark flavors at finite temperature are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a separable-rank-2 Dyson-Schwinger model that reproduces the light pseudoscalar meson phenomenology and yields a chiral restoration temperature of 128 MeV, but the model's ultraviolet suppression is much stronger than the asymptotic falloff known from perturbative QCD and lattice studies, so the perturbative regime is not addressed.

## Method

The authors model the nonperturbative QCD interaction in the infrared by a separable Ansatz for the effective gluon propagator, consisting of two rank-2 terms with Gaussian form factors and strength parameters. They solve the Dyson-Schwinger (gap) equations for the quark propagator at zero and finite temperature using the Matsubara formalism, and then compute the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states in the rainbow-ladder truncation. The model parameters are fixed by fitting meson phenomenology, and the temperature dependence arises solely from the Matsubara sums without introducing explicit T-dependent parameters.

## Key numbers

* Chiral restoration temperature: T_Ch = 128 MeV, determined by the vanishing of the chiral-limit scalar propagator function B_0(0) and the chiral condensate.
* Nonstrange current quark masses: m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV; strange current quark mass: m_s = 115 MeV.
* Dynamical quark mass at p^2=0 for nonstrange quarks: m_u(0) = 398 MeV; for strange quarks: m_s(0) = 672 MeV.
* Model parameters: Lambda_0 = 758 MeV, Lambda_1 = 961 MeV, p_0 = 600 MeV; D_0 * Lambda_0^2 = 219, D_1 * Lambda_1^4 = 40.
* Gap equation coefficients: a_{u,d} = 0.672, b_{u,d} = 660 MeV; a_s = 0.657, b_s = 998 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous separable Dyson-Schwinger model studies (Burden 1996, Blaschke 2000) by including the strange quark flavor and presenting the temperature dependence of the quark mass spectrum. It builds on the Dyson-Schwinger approach reviewed by Roberts (2000) and Alkofer (2000), and the finite-temperature extension follows the work of Bender (1996). The model enables further studies of the light pseudoscalar meson nonet at finite temperature within the Bethe-Salpeter framework, and clarifies the connection between deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration in the Dyson-Schwinger approach.
