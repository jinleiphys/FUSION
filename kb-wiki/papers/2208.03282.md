# Constraining bosonic asymmetric dark matter with neutron star mass-radius measurements

> Neutron stars can accumulate asymmetric dark matter (ADM) in their interiors, which affects the neutron star's measurable properties and makes compact objects prime targets to search for ADM. In this work, we use Bayesian inference to explore potential neutron star mass-radius measurements, from current and future x-ray telescopes, to constrain the bosonic ADM parameters for the case where bosonic ADM has accumulated in the neutron star interior. We find that the current uncertainties in the baryonic equation of state do not allow for constraints on the ADM parameter space to be made. However, we also find that ADM cannot be excluded and the inclusion of bosonic ADM in neutron star cores relaxes the constraints on the baryonic equation of state space. If the baryonic equation of state were more tightly constrained independent of ADM, we find that statements about the ADM parameter space could be made. In particular, we find that the high bosonic ADM particle mass ($m_χ$) and low effective self-interaction strength ($g_χ/m_ϕ)$ regime is disfavored due to the observationally and theoretically motivated constraint that neutron stars must have at least a mass of $1 \, \mathrm{M_\odot}$. However, within the remaining parameter space, $m_χ$ and $g_χ/m_ϕ$ are individually unconstrained. On the other hand, the ADM mass-fraction, i.e., the fraction of ADM mass inside the neutron star, can be constrained by such neutron star measurements.

## Key claim

The authors find that current uncertainties in the baryonic equation of state prevent constraints on bosonic asymmetric dark matter (ADM) parameters from neutron star mass-radius measurements, but if the baryonic equation of state were more tightly constrained independently, future NICER and STROBE-X observations could constrain the ADM mass-fraction and the ratio of particle mass to effective self-interaction strength, though not the individual particle mass or self-interaction strength.

## Method

The authors used Bayesian inference with nested sampling to analyze simulated neutron star mass-radius measurements from two future scenarios: a "Future" scenario modeled after NICER's end-of-mission capabilities with six simulated sources, and a "Future-X" scenario modeled after six possible STROBE-X sources with tighter measurement uncertainties. They solved the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations using a piece-wise polytropic baryonic equation of state and the bosonic ADM equation of state from Nelson (2018), varying both baryonic and ADM parameters simultaneously to maintain constant ADM mass-fractions along mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- The high bosonic ADM particle mass ($m_\chi$) and low effective self-interaction strength ($g_\chi/m_\phi$) regime is disfavored by the constraint that neutron stars must have at least a mass of $1 \, \mathrm{M_\odot}$
- Newly born neutron stars have a temperature of approximately $5.8 \times 10^{11} \, \mathrm{K}$, which can lead to approximately $0.02 \, M_{NS}$ ADM accumulation in the interior
- For neutron stars with an age of approximately 10 Gyr, neutron conversion to scalar ADM allows ADM to reach masses of approximately $0.05 \, M_{NS}$
- The upper bound on accreted dark matter mass is between $10^{-5}$ and $10^{-8} \, \mathrm{M_\odot}$
- The coupling constant $g_B$ is constrained from supernova SN1987A measurements to be $g_B \leq 10^{-10}$
- For a $2.3 \, \mathrm{M_\odot}$ purely baryonic neutron star with radius 10.7 km compared to one with a $7\%$ ADM mass-fraction, the radius is reduced at the $6\%$ level and gravitational mass at the $9\%$ level

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of dark matter in neutron stars by Apran (2020) and Miao (2022), which used fixed baryonic equations of state, by instead allowing all baryonic equation of state parameters to vary simultaneously with ADM parameters. The study builds on the Nelson (2018) bosonic ADM model and uses the piece-wise polytropic parameterization from Raaijmakers (2019, 2020, 2021). The results enable future observational campaigns with NICER and STROBE-X to potentially constrain the ADM mass-fraction in neutron star cores, while demonstrating that current uncertainties in the baryonic equation of state preclude definitive statements about ADM parameters.
