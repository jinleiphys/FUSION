# Kaon quark distribution functions in the chiral constituent quark model

> We investigate the valence $u$ and $\bar{s}$ quark distribution functions of the $K^+$ meson, $v^{K(u)}(x,Q^2)$ and $v^{K(\bar{s})}(x,Q^2)$, in the framework of the chiral constituent quark model. We judiciously choose the bare distributions at the initial scale to generate the dressed distributions at the higher scale, considering the meson cloud effects and the QCD evolution, which agree with the phenomenologically satisfactory valence quark distribution of the pion and the experimental data of the ratio $v^{K(u)}(x,Q^2)/v^{π(u)}(x,Q^2)$. We show how the meson cloud effects affect the bare distribution functions in detail. We find that a smaller $SU(3)$ flavor symmetry breaking effect is observed, compared with results of the preceding studies based on other approaches.

## Key claim

The authors find that the $SU(3)$ flavor symmetry breaking effect in the kaon valence quark distributions is smaller than that reported in preceding studies based on other approaches, such as the nonlocal chiral quark model or the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach, after including meson cloud effects and QCD evolution.

## Method

The authors assume functional forms for the bare valence quark distributions of the $K^+$ meson at an initial scale $Q_0^2 = 0.25$ GeV$^2$, with parameters $\beta$ and $\gamma$ to be determined. They evaluate dressing corrections from meson cloud effects using the chiral constituent quark model, which involves convolution integrals of splitting functions and quark distribution functions of the Goldstone bosons. The dressed distributions are then evolved to higher $Q^2$ using the DGLAP equation at next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy, and the parameters are fixed by fitting to experimental data for the ratio $v^{K(u)}(x,Q^2)/v^{\pi(u)}(x,Q^2)$ at $Q^2 = 27$ GeV$^2$.

## Key numbers

*   The best-fit parameters for the bare kaon valence quark distributions are $\beta = 1.84 \pm 0.27$ and $\gamma = 2.01 \pm 0.19$.
*   The first moment of the pion splitting function is $\langle P_{\pi}\rangle = 0.16$, and the kaon splitting functions are $\langle P_{K(i=u)}\rangle = 0.09$ and $\langle P_{K(i=\bar{s})}\rangle = 0.14$.
*   The renormalization constants are $Z_u = 0.67$ and $Z_s = 0.63$ at the initial scale $Q_0^2 = 0.25$ GeV$^2$.
*   At $Q^2 = 27$ GeV$^2$, the first moment $\langle x \rangle$ for the kaon valence $u$ quark is 0.23, and for the kaon valence $\bar{s}$ quark it is 0.24.
*   The peak positions of the bare distributions $v^{K(u)}_{\rm bare}$ and $v^{K(\bar{s})}_{\rm bare}$ are located around $x=0.48$ and $x=0.52$, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the pion valence quark distribution within the chiral constituent quark model (Watanabe, 2016) to the kaon case, aiming to investigate meson cloud effects and $SU(3)$ flavor symmetry breaking. The results are compared with those from the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach (Chen, 2016) and the nonlocal chiral quark model (Nam, 2012), finding a smaller symmetry breaking effect. The predictions can be tested by future kaon-induced Drell-Yan experiments proposed at various facilities.
