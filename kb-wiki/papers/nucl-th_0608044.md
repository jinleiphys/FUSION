# Cold, dense nuclear matter in a SU(2) parity doublet model

> We study dense nuclear matter and the chiral phase transition in a SU(2) parity doublet model at zero temperature. The model is defined by adding the chiral partner of the nucleon, the N', to the linear sigma model, treating the mass of the N' as an unknown free parameter. The parity doublet model gives a reasonable description of the properties of cold nuclear matter, and avoids unphysical behaviour present in the standard SU(2) linear sigma model. If the N' is identified as the N'(1535), the parity doublet model shows a first order phase transition to a chirally restored phase at large densities, $ρ\approx 10 ρ_0$, defining the transition by the degeneracy of the masses of the nucleon and the N'. If the mass of the N' is chosen to be 1.2 GeV, then the critical density of the chiral phase transition is lowered to three times normal nuclear matter density, and for physical values of the pion mass, the first order transition turns into a smooth crossover.

## Key claim

The parity doublet model, which adds the chiral partner of the nucleon (N') to the linear sigma model, can describe saturated nuclear matter and avoids the instabilities of the standard SU(2) linear sigma model, but only if the explicit mass term parameter m0 is finite (300-800 MeV); the critical density and order of the chiral phase transition depend strongly on the assumed vacuum mass of the N'.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral SU(2) parity doublet model in the mirror assignment, coupling two nucleon fields (ψ1, ψ2) to σ, π, and ω mesons in a mean-field approximation at zero temperature. They fixed parameters by requiring nuclear matter saturation at binding energy -16 MeV and density 0.16 fm⁻³ at μB = 923 MeV, then varied the N' mass (1.2 or 1.5 GeV), the explicit mass parameter m0, the quartic vector coupling g4, and the sigma vacuum mass mσ to study the chiral phase transition at high density.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³ at μB = 923 MeV, with binding energy E/A - mN = -16 MeV
- For mN⁻ = 1.5 GeV (N'(1535) identification), first order chiral phase transition occurs at ρ ≈ 8-9 ρ0 (critical chemical potential μq^c ≈ 575 MeV for g4=0; μq^c ≈ 410 MeV for g4=3.8)
- For mN⁻ = 1.2 GeV, the chiral transition becomes a smooth crossover with critical density ρ ≈ 3 ρ0 (μq^c ≈ 360 MeV for g4=0; μq^c ≈ 340 MeV for g4=3.8)
- Nuclear incompressibility K ranges from 436 MeV (mN⁻=1.2 GeV, g4=0) to 511 MeV (mN⁻=1.5 GeV, g4=0); with g4=3.8, K decreases to 375 MeV and 441 MeV respectively
- The critical N' mass where first order transition turns into crossover is ≈ 1490 MeV for g4=3.8 and ≈ 1370 MeV for g4=0
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation mN*(ρ0)/mN = 0.83-0.86 for the parameter sets studied

## Context

This work extends the parity doublet model of DeTar and Kunihiro (1988) and Jido et al. (1998) to cold dense nuclear matter, addressing the long-standing failure of the standard SU(2) linear sigma model to describe nuclear matter saturation, first noted by Kerman and Miller (1974). The model avoids the unphysical bifurcations present in earlier extensions by Boguta (1982) and Mishustin et al. (1993), and provides a framework where the sigma field generates mass splitting between parity partners rather than the full nucleon mass. The results enable future studies of chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter, with implications for neutron star equations of state and heavy-ion collision phenomenology, and suggest that the true chiral partner of the nucleon may be lighter than the N'(1535) and thus experimentally elusive.
