# Effects of Longitudinal Asymmetry in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> In collisions of identical nuclei at a given impact parameter, the number of nucleons participating in the overlap region of each nucleus can be unequal due to nuclear density fluctuations. The asymmetry due to the unequal number of participating nucleons, referred to as longitudinal asymmetry, causes a shift in the center of mass rapidity of the participant zone. The information of the event asymmetry allows us to isolate and study the effect of longitudinal asymmetry on rapidity distribution of final state particles. In a Monte Carlo Glauber model the average rapidity-shift is found to be almost linearly related to the asymmetry. Using toy models, as well as Monte Carlo data for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV generated with HIJING, two different versions of AMPT and DPMJET models, we demonstrate that the effect of asymmetry on final state rapidity distribution can be quantitatively related to the average rapidity shift via a third-order polynomial with a dominantly linear term. The coefficients of the polynomial are proportional to the rapidity shift with the dependence being sensitive to the details of the rapidity distribution.Experimental estimates of the spectator asymmetry through the measurement of spectator nucleons in a Zero Degree Calorimeter may hence be used to further constrain the initial conditions in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the effect of longitudinal asymmetry (unequal number of participating nucleons from each nucleus) on final-state rapidity distributions can be quantitatively described by a third-order polynomial with a dominantly linear term, and that this relationship is sensitive to the details of the parent rapidity distribution, but the authors hedge that the method relies on the assumption that the rapidity shift of the participant zone survives through the particle production process and subsequent evolution to the observed final state.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo Glauber model to calculate the rapidity shift of the participant zone from the participant asymmetry in Pb-Pb collisions. They then generated toy-model simulations with Gaussian and Woods-Saxon rapidity distributions, as well as Monte Carlo events from HIJING, two versions of AMPT (Default and String Melting), and DPMJET for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV. For each event generator, events were classified by the sign of the rapidity shift, and the ratio of the rapidity distributions for positive-asymmetry to negative-asymmetry events was fitted to a third-order polynomial to extract the coefficients.

## Key numbers

* For 0-5% centrality Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, the mean absolute rapidity shift is 0.0144; for 65-70% centrality it is 0.1764.
* For a Gaussian rapidity distribution with sigma=3.6 and y0=0.1, the ratio c2/c1 is approximately 0.015 and c3/c1 is approximately 0.00015.
* The chi-squared per degree of freedom for the third-order polynomial fit to the ratio of rapidity distributions in the most central class is about 0.64 for Gaussian and 0.83 for Woods-Saxon rapidity distributions.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of longitudinal fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the wounded nucleon model of Bzdak (2012) and the fluid dynamical framework of Csernai (2011) which showed that longitudinal fluctuations affect azimuthal anisotropy coefficients. The method enables experimental determination of the event-by-event center-of-mass rapidity using spectator asymmetry measurements from Zero Degree Calorimeters, as recently demonstrated by the ALICE collaboration (Acharya 2017), which can help separate initial-state fluctuations from dynamical evolution effects and may be relevant for studies of vorticity and Lambda polarization.
