# Leading-order nucleon self-energy in relativistic chiral effective field theory

> We apply thermal field theory methods to compute microscopically the nucleon self-energy arising from one-pion exchange in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter. A self-consistent numerical scheme is introduced and its convergence is demonstrated. The repulsive contribution from the Fock exchange diagram to the energy per nucleon in symmetric nuclear matter is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper computes the leading-order nucleon self-energy from one-pion exchange using relativistic thermal field theory, but the results are limited to the Fock exchange diagram only and do not include the contact interaction required by chiral power counting, meaning the calculation is incomplete for a full leading-order description of nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors applied imaginary-time thermal field theory to compute the one-pion-exchange contribution to the nucleon self-energy in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter, using the leading-order chiral Lagrangian with pion-nucleon coupling. They performed a self-consistent iterative solution of the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the nucleon propagator, replacing free quantities with renormalized effective masses, momenta, and chemical potentials at each iteration step until convergence was achieved.

## Key numbers

- At density n = 0.1 fm⁻³ and T = 0, the self-consistent iteration procedure converges to a relative accuracy ≤ 10⁻⁶ after about 10 iterations
- The relativity parameter x = p_F/m ≈ 0.28 (n/n₀)^(1/3), where n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is nuclear saturation density
- The axial-vector coupling constant used is g_A = 1.2695 ± 0.0029
- In isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at n = 0.1 fm⁻³, the reduced self-energy at T = 20 MeV is larger at low momenta than at T = 0, but the difference diminishes at higher momenta
- The one-pion-exchange contribution to the energy per nucleon in symmetric nuclear matter is repulsive and agrees with the analytical expansion of Kaiser et al. (2002) given by E(T=0) = (3g_A² p_F³)/(16π² f_π²) (a₀ + a₂ x²)

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature analytical results of Fraga et al. (2004) for the nucleon self-energy from one-pion exchange to finite temperature and implements self-consistency through the Schwinger-Dyson equation. The results agree with the heavy-baryon expansion of Kaiser et al. (2002) for the energy per particle, but the authors note that chiral power counting becomes unreliable at non-zero density and temperature due to the appearance of new scales. The approach enables future computation of electro-weak processes in nuclear and neutron matter using fully relativistic self-consistent propagators, and the authors identify the need to include contact interactions, two-pion exchange, and Δ-isobar excitations for a complete phenomenology.
