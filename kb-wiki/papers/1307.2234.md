# Chiral and Gravitational Anomalies on Fermi Surfaces

> A Fermi surface threaded by a Berry phase can be described by the Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) term. After gauging, it produces a five-dimensional Chern-Simons term in the action. We show how this Chern-Simons term captures the essence of the Abelian, non-Abelian, and mixed gravitational anomalies in describing both in- and off-equilibrium phenomena. In particular we derive a novel contribution to the Chiral Vortical Effect that arises when a temperature gradient is present. We also discuss the issue of universality of the anomalous currents.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a five-dimensional Chern-Simons term derived from a gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten term on a Fermi surface captures Abelian, non-Abelian, and mixed gravitational anomalies for both in- and off-equilibrium phenomena, but the temperature corrections to the Chiral Vortical Effect are not universal and differ between chiral Fermi surfaces and chiral superfluids.

## Method

The authors start from a Berry phase induced by a momentum-space monopole on a chiral Fermi surface, represent it as a Wess-Zumino-Witten term, and then gauge it by minimal substitution to obtain a five-dimensional Chern-Simons action. They integrate over the Fermi sphere using a distribution function for particles and antiparticles, and extend the formalism to non-Abelian flavor charges, rotating frames with a gravitational potential, and spin connections for mixed gravitational anomalies.

## Key numbers

- The Berry monopole charge is \(g = 1/2\) centered in the Fermi sphere of radius \(k_F\).
- In equilibrium, the energy integral across the Fermi sphere yields \(\left(\sum_{s=\pm}\int_0^\infty {d(s\epsilon)}\,{\bf f}(\epsilon,s)\right) = k_F\).
- For the Chiral Vortical Effect in a rotating frame, the equilibrium integral gives \(\left(\sum_{s=\pm}\int_0^\infty \,d\epsilon\,\epsilon\,{\bf f}(\epsilon,s)\right) = \frac 12\left(k_F^2+\frac{(\pi T)^2}3\right)\).
- The novel contribution to the chiral vortical current from a temperature gradient is \({\bf J}_R = \frac{g}{4\pi^2}\,\frac{\Delta T}{T}\,\left(k_F^2+\frac{(\pi T)^2}3\right) \, {\bf{\Omega}}\).
- For the color-flavor locked superfluid phase of dense QCD, the temperature correction to the CVE current is \(-\frac 38\,\frac{{(\pi T)^2}}{12}\) relative to \(\mu^2\).
- The mixed gravitational anomaly coefficient is reproduced with semi-classical spin squared \({\bf s}^2 = (1/2)^2\); the quantum value \({\bf s}^2 = 3/4\) would give a coefficient larger by 3.

## Context

This work extends the five-dimensional Chern-Simons approach of Volovik (2003) and Zahed (2013) to describe off-equilibrium dynamics of chiral Fermi surfaces, building on prior treatments of chiral anomalies in dense QCD by Son and Zhitnitsky (2004) and in holography by Erdmenger et al. (2009) and Rebhan et al. (2010). The new temperature-gradient contribution to the Chiral Vortical Effect and the semi-classical treatment of mixed gravitational anomalies enable applications to inhomogeneous systems out of equilibrium, such as rotating quark-gluon plasmas or superfluid phases of dense QCD.
