# The role of the $qqqq\bar{q}$ components in the electromagnetic transition $γ^*N\to N^*(1535)$

> The helicity amplitudes $A^{p}_{1/2}$ and $S^{p}_{1/2}$ for the electromagnetic transition $γ^{*} N\to N^{*}(1535)$ are calculated in the quark model that is extended to include the lowest lying $qqqq\bar{q}$ components in addition to the $qqq$ component. It is found that with admixture of 5-quark components with a proportion of 20% in the nucleon and 25-65% in the $N^{*}(1535)$ resonance the calculated helicity amplitude $A^{p}_{1/2}$ decreases at the photon point, $Q^{2}=0$ to the empirical range. The $qqqq\bar{q}$ components contain $s\bar s$ pairs, which is consistent with the substantial width for $Nη$ decay of the $N^{*}(1535)$. The best description of the momentum dependence of the empirical helicity amplitudes is obtained by assuming that the $qqqq\bar{q}$ components are more compact than the $qqq$ component. However, this version of extended quark model still does not lead to a satisfactory simultaneous description of both $A^{p}_{1/2}$ and $S^{p}_{1/2}$ although with significant improvement.

## Key claim

The extended quark model that includes 5-quark ($qqqq\bar{q}$) components with a proportion of 20% in the nucleon and 25-65% in the $N^*(1535)$ resonance can bring the calculated helicity amplitude $A^{p}_{1/2}$ at the photon point ($Q^2=0$) into the empirical range, but the model still does not achieve a satisfactory simultaneous description of both $A^{p}_{1/2}$ and $S^{p}_{1/2}$ despite significant improvement.

## Method

The authors calculated the helicity amplitudes $A^{p}_{1/2}$ and $S^{p}_{1/2}$ for the electromagnetic transition $\gamma^{*}p \to N^*(1535)$ using a non-relativistic constituent quark model extended to include the lowest-lying $qqqq\bar{q}$ components in addition to the conventional $qqq$ component. They considered contributions from three parts: the direct $qqq\to qqq$ transition, diagonal transitions within the $qqqq\bar{q}$ components, and off-diagonal annihilation transitions $\gamma^{*}qqq \to qqqq\bar{q}$ (and the reverse), using harmonic oscillator wave functions with separate oscillator parameters $\omega_3$ for the three-quark component and $\omega_5$ for the five-quark component.

## Key numbers

- At the photon point $Q^2=0$, the pure $qqq$ model gives $A^{p}_{1/2}=0.147/\sqrt{\mathrm{GeV}}$ with $\omega_3=340$ MeV and $0.115/\sqrt{\mathrm{GeV}}$ with $\omega_3=246$ MeV, both larger than the experimental value $A^{p}_{1/2}=0.090\pm0.030/\sqrt{\mathrm{GeV}}$.
- With 20% $qqqq\bar{q}$ probability in the proton and 45% in $N^*(1535)$, using $\omega_3=340$ MeV and $\omega_5=600$ MeV, the model describes $A^{p}_{1/2}$ well at both the photon point and larger $Q^2$.
- The non-diagonal contribution to $A^{p}_{1/2}$ at $Q^2=0$ is about $-0.025/\sqrt{\mathrm{GeV}}$ with $\omega_3=340$ MeV and $\omega_5=600$ MeV.
- The calculated $S^{p}_{1/2}$ values change sign at $Q^2 \approx 1.8\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$, limiting the model's validity to $Q^2 < 1.8\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.
- For the neutron, the pure $qqq$ model gives $A^{n}_{1/2}=-0.123/\sqrt{\mathrm{GeV}}$ with $\omega_3=340$ MeV, much larger than the experimental value $A^{n}_{1/2}=-0.046\pm0.027/\sqrt{\mathrm{GeV}}$.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model of Isgur and Glozman by adding explicit $qqqq\bar{q}$ components to address the long-standing difficulty of describing the $N^*(1535)$ electromagnetic transition form factors, which had been better described by chiral dynamical approaches (Weise, Oset). The inclusion of $s\bar{s}$ pairs in the five-quark components is consistent with the substantial $N\eta$ decay width of $N^*(1535)$ and with previous indications of strong $N^*(1535)K\Lambda$ coupling (Zou, 2005) and $N^*(1535)N\phi$ coupling (Xie, 2007). The approach enables future studies of whether a fully relativistic treatment or different admixture ratios can achieve simultaneous agreement for both helicity amplitudes.
