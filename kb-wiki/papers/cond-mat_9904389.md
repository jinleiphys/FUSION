# Superdiffusion and Out-of-equilibrium Chaotic Dynamics with Many Degrees of Freedoms

> We study the link between relaxation to the equilibrium and anomalous superdiffusive motion in a classical N-body hamiltonian system with long-range interaction showing a second-order phase-transition in the canonical ensemble. Anomalous diffusion is observed only in a transient out-of-equilibrium regime and for a small range of energy, below the critical one. Superdiffusion is due to Lévy walks of single particles and is checked independently through the second moment of the distribution, power spectra, trapping and walking time probabilities. Diffusion becomes normal at equilibrium, after a relaxation time which diverges with N.

# Superdiffusion and Out-of-equilibrium Chaotic Dynamics with Many Degrees of Freedoms

## Key claim
Superdiffusion (anomalous diffusion with exponent α > 1) occurs in the Hamiltonian Mean Field (HMF) model only during a transient out-of-equilibrium regime in a narrow energy range below the critical energy, and the crossover time to normal diffusion coincides with the relaxation time of quasi-stationary states (QSS) to canonical equilibrium, which diverges with system size N.

## Method
The authors performed microcanonical molecular dynamics simulations of the HMF model, a system of N fully coupled classical rotors with Hamiltonian H = Σ p_i²/2 + (1/2N) Σ [1 - cos(θ_i - θ_j)]. They initialized the system in a far-from-equilibrium "water bag" condition (all θ_i = 0, uniform momenta centered at zero) and integrated the equations of motion using fourth-order symplectic algorithms. They computed the variance of the single-particle angle σ_θ²(t) as a function of time, the temperature T = 2⟨K⟩/N, and the total magnetization M, for various system sizes N and energies U = E/N, and extracted diffusion exponents α, trapping time exponents ν, and walking time exponents μ from power-law fits.

## Key numbers
- Critical energy of the second-order phase transition: U_c = 0.75 (corresponding critical temperature T_c = 0.5).
- Superdiffusion exponent α = 1.38 ± 0.05 for U = 0.6 and N = 500, observed over several decades before a crossover time τ_c ∼ 7 × 10⁴.
- Superdiffusion exponent α = 1.42 ± 0.05 for U = 0.69 and N = 500 and N = 2000, with the transient regime increasing linearly with N (almost 3 decades for N = 2000).
- Superdiffusion present only in the energy range 0.5 < U < 0.75; no diffusion (α = 0) for U ≤ 0.2; ballistic regime (α = 2) for U > 0.75.
- Walking time exponent μ = 2.14 ± 0.1 (U = 0.69) and μ = 1.98 ± 0.1 (U = 0.6); trapping time exponent ν = 1.58 ± 0.05 (U = 0.69) and ν = 1.34 ± 0.05 (U = 0.6).
- Derived superdiffusion exponent from Lévy walk model: α = 2 + ν - μ = 1.44 ± 0.05 (U = 0.69) and 1.36 ± 0.05 (U = 0.6), consistent with direct fits within ∼5% relative error.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of the HMF model by Antoni and Ruffo (1995) and Latora et al. (1998), which established the canonical phase transition and the existence of quasi-stationary states with slow relaxation. It contrasts with the mechanism proposed by Geisel (1985) for superdiffusion in "egg-crate" potentials and with results from Torcini et al. (1999) where superdiffusion arises from distinct dynamical classes in a first-order phase transition. The finding that superdiffusion coincides with QSS relaxation and that the crossover time diverges with N confirms a scenario conjectured by Tsallis (1998) for systems with long-term memory, and the Lévy walk exponents are similar to those observed in the fluid flow experiment of Solomon et al. (1993). This work enables further study of out-of-equilibrium dynamics in long-range interacting systems and connects anomalous transport to phase transitions in Hamiltonian systems with many degrees of freedom.
