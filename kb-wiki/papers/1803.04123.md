# Characterization of quark gluon plasma as seen through the energy loss of heavy quarks

> The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) of quark gluon plasma produced in ultra- relativistic heavy-ion collisions has been studied from the energy loss of heavy quarks in QGP medium. We have also studied the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio (ζ/s) and fluidity measure(F) of the medium using the obtained η/s values. In addition to that, we have estimated the heavy quark bound state potential (V) inside this medium. Our finding of η/s agrees well with the results obtained by Lattice QCD (LQCD) and functional renormalization group technique.

## Key claim
The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) of quark gluon plasma, extracted from heavy quark energy loss using the transport parameter q̂, agrees well with Lattice QCD and functional renormalization group results, but only when both collisional and radiative energy loss processes are included.

## Method
The authors calculated the collisional energy loss of charm quarks using the Peigne-Pashier (PP) formalism and the radiative energy loss using the Abir-Jamil-Mustafa-Srivastava (AJMS) generalized dead cone approach. These energy losses were averaged over the QGP lifetime using a hydrodynamic medium evolution with initial time τ₀ = 0.3 fm and freeze-out time τ_f = 6 fm. The transport parameter q̂ was extracted from the total energy loss, and then η/s was derived via the relation η/s ≈ 1.25 T³/q̂. Bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio (ζ/s) was parametrized from η/s using the velocity of sound from LQCD, and the fluidity measure F was computed from η/s and LQCD inputs.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature T_c ~ 155 MeV (from LQCD at zero baryonic chemical potential).
- Initial time τ₀ = 0.3 fm and freeze-out time τ_f = 6 fm used for hydrodynamic medium evolution.
- The transport parameter q̂ increases with temperature T; a distance d = 5 fm was chosen for its calculation.
- η/s is compared with LQCD calculations (Mayer 2007, Mayer 2009, Borsanyi 2014) and functional renormalization group calculations (Haas).
- ζ/s peaks near T_c ~ 155 MeV and decreases as T increases.
- The fluidity measure F is lower near T_c, indicating the QGP is closer to perfect fluidity near T_c, and increases with T.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of QGP transport coefficients by using heavy quark energy loss as a probe, specifically comparing with the charm quark energy loss results of Mazumder et al. (2015). The η/s results agree with LQCD calculations (Mayer 2007, Mayer 2009, Borsanyi 2014) and functional renormalization group calculations (Haas), while the Mazumder et al. results were lower than those calculations. The study enables a consistent characterization of QGP through η/s, ζ/s, and fluidity measure F, and shows how the medium-modified heavy quark bound state potential weakens with increasing temperature and η/s.
