# Enhancement of flow anisotropies due to magnetic field in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> It is known that the presence of background magnetic field in cosmic plasma distorts the acoustic peaks in CMBR. This primarily results from different types of waves in the plasma with velocities depending on the angle between the magnetic field and the wave vector. We consider the consequences of these effects in relativistic heavy-ion collisions where very strong magnetic fields arise during early stages of the plasma evolution. We show that flow coefficients can be significantly affected by these effects when the magnetic field remains strong during early stages due to strong induced fields in the conducting plasma. In particular, the presence of magnetic field can lead to enhancement in the elliptic flow coefficient $v_2$.

## Key claim

The presence of a strong magnetic field during the early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions can enhance the elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) by almost 30%, but this result depends on the magnetic field remaining approximately constant for the first few fm of plasma evolution due to induced currents, rather than decaying rapidly as the initial external field does.

## Method

The authors used HIJING to generate initial transverse energy density distributions for Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV/A center-of-mass energy, assuming hydrodynamic description becomes applicable at \(\tau = 1\) fm. They calculated Fourier coefficients \(F_n\) of spatial anisotropies and converted them to flow coefficients \(v_n^{rms}\) using a proportionality factor that incorporates the angle-dependent group velocity of fast magnetosonic waves from relativistic magnetohydrodynamics, replacing the usual sound speed with the magnetosonic wave velocity. The magnetic field was taken as uniform over the plasma region, constant for the first few fm, with magnitude proportional to impact parameter, and directed in the transverse plane normal to the impact parameter direction.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength parameterized as \(B_0 = 10^5 \times (b/10)~\mathrm{MeV}^2\), corresponding to a maximum of about \(10^{15}\) Tesla for Au-Au collisions at RHIC.
- The time scale over which the magnetic field remains approximately constant is \(\tau \simeq L^2 \sigma/4\); for \(L = 5-6\) fm and electrical conductivity \(\sigma \simeq 0.3~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) (at \(T \simeq 200\) MeV), this gives \(\tau \simeq 2-3\) fm.
- The ratio \(v_2(B)/v_2(0)\) reaches 1.3 for impact parameter \(b = 10\) fm, indicating a 30% enhancement of elliptic flow due to the magnetic field.
- The penetration depth of the initial magnetic field pulse (with \(\omega \simeq 30~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\)) is of order 3 fm.
- For zero magnetic field, the proportionality factor between spatial and flow Fourier coefficients is 0.2, using sound velocity \(c_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\).

## Context

This work extends the previously established correspondence between CMBR acoustic peak physics and flow anisotropies in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Mohapatra et al., 2007; referred to as cmbhic in the text), where root-mean-square flow coefficients \(v_n^{rms}\) were proposed as a probe of flow without event-plane determination. It draws on the known effect of background magnetic fields distorting CMBR acoustic peaks (referred to as cmbrB in the text) and applies it to the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions, where strong magnetic fields of order \(10^{15}\) Tesla arise from valence charges of the colliding nuclei. The work enables future full magnetohydrodynamical simulations to determine whether a larger value of \(\eta/s\) (shear viscosity to entropy density ratio) can be accommodated by RHIC data, and suggests that even stronger effects may be observable at LHC energies where magnetic fields are expected to be an order of magnitude larger.
