# About the inclusion of an infinite number of resonances in anomalous decays

> The extracted value for the $g^{eff}_{ωρπ}$ effective coupling from experimental data, considering only the $ρ$ meson, resumes not only the $ρ$ meson effect but also all its additional radial excitation modes. By explicitly adding the radial excitations of the $ρ$ meson, considering a particular form of the spectrum and relations among the couplings, we identify the single $g_{ωρπ}$ and the $ρ$ radial excitations effect in the $ω\rightarrow π^0 γ$ decay. We obtain that the individual coupling is in the range $g_{ωρπ}= 8.2 - 8.6 {\text GeV}^{-1}$, which is about 40% smaller than the effective $g^{eff}_{ωρπ}$. We verify the consistency with the chiral approach in the $π^0 \rightarrow γγ$ and $γ^* \rightarrow 3π$ processes. Besides the model dependence, our description succeeds in exhibiting how each contribution came into the game. In particular, we show that for the $γ^* \rightarrow 3 π$ decay, the usual relation $\mathcal{A}^{VMD}_{\gamma3π}=(3/2)\mathcal{A}^{WZW}_{\gamma3π}$, encodes all the vector contributions and not only the $ρ$ meson one. In addition, we find that there is an almost exact (accidental) cancelation between the radial excitations and the contact term contributions.

## Key claim

The commonly extracted effective coupling \(g^{eff}_{\omega\rho\pi}\) from experimental data does not represent the single \(\omega\)-\(\rho\)-\(\pi\) vertex but instead subsumes contributions from all radial excitations of the \(\rho\) meson. When these excitations are explicitly included using a harmonic-oscillator-inspired mass spectrum and KSFR-like relations, the true individual coupling \(g_{\omega\rho\pi}\) is found to be about 40% smaller than the effective value.

## Method

The authors model the radial excitation spectrum of the \(\rho\) meson as \(m_{\rho_n}^2 = m_\rho^2 (1 + a n)^2\) with \(a = 0.89\) fixed by the \(\rho'(1450)\) mass, and assume a KSFR-like relation \(g_{\rho_n\pi\pi} = m_{\rho_n}/(\sqrt{2}f_\pi)\) for each excitation. They compute the decay amplitudes for \(\omega \rightarrow \pi^0 \gamma\) and \(\omega \rightarrow 3\pi\) by summing over all \(\rho_n\) contributions, using SU(3) symmetry and the assumption that the ratio \(g_{\omega\rho_n\pi} g_{\rho_n}\) is constant across excitations. The individual coupling \(g_{\omega\rho\pi}\) is then extracted by matching the summed amplitudes to experimental decay widths, and consistency with the chiral anomaly is verified for \(\pi^0 \rightarrow \gamma\gamma\) and \(\gamma^* \rightarrow 3\pi\).

## Key numbers

*   \(g_{\omega\rho\pi} = 7.7 \pm 0.2\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\) from the \(\omega \rightarrow \pi^0 \gamma\) decay width using \(BR(\omega \rightarrow \pi^0 \gamma) = 8.28 \pm 0.28\%\).
*   \(g_{\omega\rho\pi} = 8.9 \pm 0.2\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\) from the \(\omega \rightarrow 3\pi\) decay width.
*   Global average: \(g_{\omega\rho\pi} = 8.3 \pm 0.8\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\).
*   The effective coupling \(g^{eff}_{\omega\rho\pi} = 14.5 \pm 1.4\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\) is obtained from the \(\pi^0 \rightarrow \gamma\gamma\) anomaly matching, about 40% larger than the individual coupling.
*   The contact term coupling for \(\gamma^* \rightarrow 3\pi\) is \(g^c_{3\pi} = -47\ \text{GeV}^{-3}\).
*   The effective coupling from radial excitations in \(\gamma^* \rightarrow 3\pi\) is \(g^{re}_{3\pi} = 61 \pm 6\ \text{GeV}^{-3}\).
*   The sum of radial excitation and contact term contributions in \(\gamma^* \rightarrow 3\pi\) amounts to \(\frac{1}{7} \mathcal{A}^{WZW}_{\gamma3\pi}\).

## Context

This work extends the vector meson dominance (VMD) approach by explicitly including the infinite tower of \(\rho\) radial excitations, which are usually neglected in standard extractions of the \(\omega\rho\pi\) coupling. It builds on the factorizable dual model of Cesareo (1994) and the KSFR relation of Kawarabayashi, Suzuki, Fayyazuddin, and Riazuddin (1966), and it reconciles the VMD description with the chiral Wess-Zumino-Witten anomaly for \(\pi^0 \rightarrow \gamma\gamma\) and \(\gamma^* \rightarrow 3\pi\). The results enable a cleaner determination of the fundamental \(\omega\rho\pi\) vertex and clarify that the usual relation \(\mathcal{A}^{VMD}_{\gamma3\pi} = (3/2)\mathcal{A}^{WZW}_{\gamma3\pi}\) encodes all vector contributions, not just the \(\rho\) meson, while the contact term remains purely axial.
