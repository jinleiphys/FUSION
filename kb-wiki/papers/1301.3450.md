# Unified ab initio approach to bound and unbound states: no-core shell model with continuum and its application to 7He

> We introduce a unified approach to nuclear bound and continuum states based on the coupling of the no-core shell model (NCSM), a bound-state technique, with the no-core shell model/resonating group method (NCSM/RGM), a nuclear scattering technique. This new ab initio method, no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC), leads to convergence properties superior to either NCSM or NCSM/RGM while providing a balanced approach to different classes of states. In the NCSMC, the ansatz for the many-nucleon wave function includes: i) a square-integrable A-nucleon component expanded in a complete harmonic oscillator basis; ii) a binary-cluster component with asymptotic boundary conditions that can properly describe weakly-bound states, resonances and scattering; and, in principle, iii) a three-cluster component suitable for the description of, e.g., Borromean halo nuclei and reactions with final three-body states. The Schroedinger equation is transformed into a system of coupled-channel integral-differential equations that we solve using a modified microscopic R-matrix formalism within a Lagrange mesh basis. We demonstrate the usefulness of the approach by investigating the unbound 7He nucleus.

## Key claim
The paper introduces the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC), a unified ab initio method that couples the no-core shell model (NCSM) with the no-core shell model/resonating group method (NCSM/RGM) to achieve superior convergence for both bound and unbound nuclear states, particularly for systems where the NCSM/RGM alone requires a large number of excited cluster pseudostates to account for short- and medium-range correlations.

## Method
The NCSMC ansatz for the many-nucleon wave function combines a square-integrable A-nucleon component expanded in a complete harmonic oscillator basis (NCSM eigenstates) with a binary-cluster component having asymptotic boundary conditions suitable for weakly-bound states, resonances, and scattering (NCSM/RGM states). The Schrödinger equation is transformed into a system of coupled-channel integral-differential equations, which are solved using a modified microscopic R-matrix formalism within a Lagrange mesh basis. The method is applied to the unbound 7He nucleus using an SRG-evolved chiral EFT nucleon-nucleon potential.

## Key numbers
- The NCSM/RGM calculations with soft SRG-evolved chiral EFT NN interactions typically require HO basis sizes with N_max ~ 10–14 to reach convergence.
- For the 7Be(p,γ)8B capture, the NCSM/RGM required taking into account the lowest five eigenstates of 7Be to achieve a good description.
- The NCSMC approach was applied to study the low-lying resonances of the exotic 7He nucleus using an SRG-evolved chiral EFT NN potential.

## Context
This work extends the NCSM/RGM approach previously developed by the same group (Quaglioni and Navratil, 2008-2009) and applied to reactions such as nucleon-4He scattering, 7Be(p,γ)8B capture, d-4He scattering, and 3H(d,n)4He and 3He(d,p)4He fusion. The NCSMC addresses a practical limitation of NCSM/RGM: the need to include many excited pseudostates of weakly-bound clusters (e.g., 2H, 3H, 3He) to account for virtual breakup, which dramatically increases computational complexity. The new method enables more efficient calculations of bound and unbound states in light nuclei, including exotic systems near the drip lines.
