# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of excited states in A = 6--8 nuclei

> A variational Monte Carlo method is used to generate sets of orthogonal trial functions, Psi_T(J^pi,T), for given quantum numbers in various light p-shell nuclei. These Psi_T are then used as input to Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of first, second, and higher excited (J^pi,T) states. Realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions are used. We find that if the physical excited state is reasonably narrow, the GFMC energy converges to a stable result. With the combined Argonne v_18 two-nucleon and Illinois-2 three-nucleon interactions, the results for many second and higher states in A = 6--8 nuclei are close to the experimental values.

## Key claim

Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations can produce stable and useful predictions for second and higher excited states with the same quantum numbers in light p-shell nuclei, provided the physical excited state is reasonably narrow; contrary to prior expectations, the propagation of higher states does not quickly collapse to the lowest state.

## Method

Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) trial functions were constructed from products of two- and three-body correlation operators acting on antisymmetric single-particle states, with mixing parameters determined by diagonalization in a basis of different spatial-symmetry states. These orthogonal trial functions were then used as input to GFMC calculations that projected out eigenstates by propagation in imaginary time, with the constrained path algorithm employed for A ≥ 8 nuclei and 10 to 20 unconstrained steps before energy evaluation.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation from 36 experimental energies with 6 ≤ A ≤ 8 using the AV18/IL2 Hamiltonian is only 0.60 MeV
- For ⁶He(2⁺) with AV18/UIX: GFMC excitation energy 1.9(1) MeV (VMC: 1.7(1) MeV)
- For ⁶Li(3⁺) with AV18/UIX: GFMC excitation energy 2.8(1) MeV (VMC: 2.9(1) MeV)
- For ⁶Li(2⁺) with AV18/UIX: GFMC excitation energy 4.0(1) MeV (VMC: 4.2(1) MeV)
- For ⁷Li(7/2⁻) with AV18/UIX: GFMC excitation energy 5.3(1) MeV (VMC: 5.8(3) MeV)
- For ⁷Li(5/2⁻, 2nd) with AV18/UIX: GFMC excitation energy 7.8(2) MeV (VMC: 9.3(3) MeV)
- For ⁷Li(3/2⁻, 2nd) with AV18/UIX: GFMC excitation energy 8.5(3) MeV (VMC: 10.2(3) MeV)
- For ⁷Li(5/2⁻, 3rd) with AV18/UIX: GFMC excitation energy 10.5(2) MeV (VMC: 12.5(3) MeV)
- ⁷Li first 5/2⁻ state has experimental width 918 keV; second 5/2⁻ state has width 80 keV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous VMC and GFMC calculations of ground and low-lying excited states in A = 6–10 nuclei (Pieper et al., 1995, 1997, 2000, 2001, 2002) to second and higher excited states with the same quantum numbers, which had not been attempted previously in nuclear physics. The results enable systematic GFMC predictions for multiple states of identical quantum numbers in light p-shell nuclei, and demonstrate that the AV18/IL2 Hamiltonian reproduces the experimental spectrum with high accuracy, while the AV18/UIX Hamiltonian shows larger deviations for higher excited states.
