# The Trojan Horse Method: an Indirect Technique in Nuclear Astrophysics

> The Trojan Horse (TH) method is a powerful indirect technique that provides information to determine astrophysical factors for rearrangement processes at astrophysically relevant energies. A short coming for understanding the reliability of the technique has been determining the importance of nuclear and Coulomb effects on the energy dependence of the yield. Using a simple model, we demonstrate that off-energy-shell and Coulomb effects in the entry channel and the final state nuclear interactions do not change the energy dependence of the astrophysical factor extracted from the TH reaction. Some examples are presented.

## Key claim
Using a simple model, the authors demonstrate that off-energy-shell effects, Coulomb effects in the entry channel, and final-state nuclear interactions do not change the energy dependence of the astrophysical factor extracted from the Trojan Horse method, though the absolute value must still be normalized to direct measurements at higher energies.

## Method
The authors employed a simple theoretical model comparing half-off-energy-shell (HOF) and on-shell (ON) reaction amplitudes for the binary subprocesses in the Trojan Horse method. They calculated the energy dependence of the astrophysical factor for the reactions 7Li(p,α)4He and 6Li(d,α)4He using plane wave approximations for the HOF amplitude and Coulomb-distorted waves for the ON amplitude, then varied parameters such as the virtuality parameter σx and included initial-state Coulomb interactions and final-state nuclear interactions to test their effects.

## Key numbers
- For the 7Li(p,α)4He reaction, the Q-value is 16.56 MeV; for the 6Li(d,α)4He reaction, the Q-value is 21.64 MeV.
- The half-off-shell and on-shell astrophysical factors were compared at energies ExA below 1 MeV, with normalization at ExA = 1 keV.
- For the 7Li(d,nα)4He TH reaction at Ed-7Li = 4.20 MeV, varying σp by 7% around the QF value σp = 2.224 MeV covers the energy region 0 < Ep-7Li ≤ 800 keV.
- The TH reaction 6Li(6Li,αα)4He was calculated at E6Li-6Li = 3.14 MeV; the TH reaction 7Li(d,nα)4He was calculated at Ed-7Li = 4.03 MeV.

## Context
This work addresses reservations about the Trojan Horse method raised by earlier studies regarding off-shell effects and initial/final state interactions, extending the method first suggested by Baur (1986). The results validate the standard analysis approach used by Spitaleri (2001) and Typel (2003), which neglects the virtual nature of the transferred particle and uses plane wave approximations. The method enables determination of astrophysical factors down to zero relative kinetic energy without distortion from electron screening, as demonstrated by comparison with direct measurements from Engstler (1992) and Elwyn (1979), and allows extraction of the screening potential when compared to direct results at low energies.
