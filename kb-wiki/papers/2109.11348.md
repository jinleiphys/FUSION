# Peripheral nucleon-nucleon scattering at next-to-next-to-leading order in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate the complete $T$ matrices of the elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering up to third order in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The phase shifts with orbital angular momentum $L\geq 2$ and the mixing angles with $J\geq 2$ are evaluated by using low-energy constants that were extracted from the meson-baryon analysis. It turns out that our prediction is consistent with the empirical phase shifts and mixing angles data, and also the results from SU(2) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The errors from the low-energy constants are analyzed in detail. Our calculation provides reliable evidence that the nucleon-nucleon interaction calculated in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory leads to reasonable predictions.

# Peripheral nucleon-nucleon scattering at next-to-next-to-leading order in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim
The authors calculate nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts for peripheral partial waves (L >= 2, J >= 2) up to next-to-next-to-leading order in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, finding that predictions are consistent with empirical data and with SU(2) results, but only when the large uncertainties from low-energy constants (estimated at +/- 20%) are taken into account; the central values deviate from data at higher energies, especially for D-waves.

## Method
The complete T matrices for elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering were calculated up to third order (NNLO) in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, including one-meson exchange (LO), two-meson exchange (NLO), and third-order contributions from relativistic 1/M0 corrections and vertices proportional to low-energy constants b_i. Phase shifts for L = 2 through 6 and mixing angles for J = 2 through 6 were extracted from the T matrices using partial wave projection formulas, with low-energy constants taken from previous meson-baryon scattering analyses (pion-nucleon and kaon-nucleon) rather than fitted to nucleon-nucleon data.

## Key numbers
- Phase shifts and mixing angles calculated up to T_lab = 250 MeV, with momentum transfers up to q = 4.9 m_pi = 685 MeV
- Physical parameters used: m_pi = 139.57 MeV, m_K = 493.68 MeV, m_eta = 547.86 MeV, f_pi = 92.07 MeV, f_K = 110.03 MeV, f_eta = 1.2 f_pi, M_N = 938.92 MeV
- Low-energy constants from pion-nucleon scattering at O(p^4): b_1 = 1.61 GeV^{-1}, b_2 = 0.10 GeV^{-1}, b_3 = -4.50 GeV^{-1}, b_4 = -1.34 GeV^{-1}, b_9 = 1.86 GeV^{-1}, b_10 = -0.38 GeV^{-1}, b_11 = 17.66 GeV^{-1}
- Chiral limit baryon mass M_0 = 963.58 +/- 153.97 MeV
- D and F axial coupling constants: D = 0.80, F = 0.47
- The mixing angle epsilon_2 is consistent with data below 100 MeV laboratory kinetic energy
- The ^3D_1 phase shift is in fair agreement with data only up to 100 MeV laboratory kinetic energy

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory studies of meson-baryon scattering (Huang et al., 2015, 2017, 2020) to the nucleon-nucleon sector, using low-energy constants extracted from those analyses rather than fitting to nucleon-nucleon data. It contrasts with the SU(2) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory results of Kaiser et al. (1997), where near-complete cancellation of irreducible two-pion exchange and iterated one-pion exchange occurs for the ^3D_1 wave; in the SU(3) case this cancellation is absent, leading to larger deviations. The results enable future extension to hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions at higher orders, which have been calculated only up to next-to-leading order by Haidenbauer et al. (2013, 2020) where convergence was not yet achieved.
