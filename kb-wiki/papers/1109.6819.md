# Jet color chemistry and anomalous baryon production in $AA$-collisions

> We study anomalous high-$p_T$ baryon production in $AA$-collisions due to formation of the two parton collinear $gq$ system in the anti-sextet color state for quark jets and $gg$ system in the decuplet/anti-decuplet color states for gluon jets. Fragmentation of these states, which are absent for $NN$-collisions, after escaping from the quark-gluon plasma leads to baryon production. Our qualitative estimates show that this mechanism can be potentially important at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that anomalous high-$p_T$ baryon production in nucleus-nucleus collisions can arise from collinear two-parton systems (gq in anti-sextet color state for quark jets, gg in decuplet/anti-decuplet color states for gluon jets) that escape from the quark-gluon plasma and fragment into baryons, but the authors emphasize that only qualitative estimates are presented and a reliable quantitative computation cannot yet be made.

## Method

The authors model the medium-modified fragmentation function for baryons as a convolution of a diquark fragmentation function (taken from the quark-gluon string model) with a diquark distribution derived from perturbative QCD calculations of the $q \rightarrow |qg\rangle_{\{\bar{6}\}}$ and $g \rightarrow |gg\rangle_{\{10\},\{\overline{10}\}}$ transitions. The diquark distribution is computed with an invariant mass cutoff $M_D$ of 1 or 1.5 GeV, treating the two fastest quarks in the prehadron states as the diquark, and assuming color randomization in the QGP gives a statistical weight factor of 6/24 for the anti-sextet state.

## Key numbers

- Diquark mass cutoff values used: $M_D = 1$ GeV and $M_D = 1.5$ GeV, motivated by Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations giving diquark masses $\sim 0.8-1$ GeV
- Gluon mass taken as $m_g = 0.75$ GeV, obtained from analysis of low-$x$ proton structure function $F_2$ within the dipole BFKL equation
- Quark mass taken as $m_q = 0.3$ GeV
- For the diquark to proton fragmentation function, the parametrization $D_{p/D}(z) = Az^m$ with $m = 5/2$ is used, obtained from fitting proton spectra in hadron reactions within the quark-gluon string model by Kaidalov and Piskunova
- The QGP lifetime/size is taken as $L \sim R_A \sim 6$ fm for RHIC and LHC conditions
- The minimal nonperturbative scale is $Q_0 \sim 1-2$ GeV
- The QGP formation time is $\tau_0 \sim 0.5$ fm
- For jet energies $E \lesssim 30-50$ GeV, the two parton system almost surely changes its color state in the QGP

## Context

This work extends the gluon mechanism of baryon number flow previously developed for $\bar{p}p$-annihilation and baryon stopping by Zakharov (1988, 1989) and collaborators, which explained the $\sigma_{\bar{p}p}^{ann} \propto s^{-1/2}$ dependence and the slow rapidity dependence $\propto \exp(-y/2)$ of baryon stopping observed at ISR. It contrasts with the string junction model of Kharzeev (1996) and the recombination/coalescence models, which are soft mechanisms, whereas the proposed color anomalous baryon fragmentation mechanism persists at high $p_T$. The paper enables future quantitative calculations of baryon production in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, though the authors note that such calculations remain difficult.
