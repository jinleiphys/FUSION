# Commentary on rainbow-ladder truncation for excited states and exotics

> Ground-state, radially-excited and exotic scalar-, vector- and flavoured-pseudoscalar-mesons are studied in rainbow-ladder truncation using an interaction kernel that is consonant with modern DSE- and lattice-QCD results. The inability of this truncation to provide realistic predictions for the masses of excited- and exotic-states is confirmed and explained. On the other hand, its application does provide information that is potentially useful in proceeding beyond this leading-order truncation, e.g.: assisting with development of projection techniques that ease the computation of excited state properties; placing qualitative constraints on the long-range behaviour of the interaction kernel; and highlighting and illustrating some features of hadron observables that do not depend on details of the dynamics.

## Key claim

The rainbow-ladder truncation, while providing realistic ground-state masses for flavoured-pseudoscalar and vector mesons, is confirmed to be incapable of producing quantitatively reliable predictions for excited and exotic meson masses; however, its simplicity can still yield useful qualitative information for developing more sophisticated truncation schemes.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) for light u/d and s quarks and the corresponding Bethe-Salpeter equations for meson bound states, using a symmetry-preserving rainbow-ladder truncation with an interaction kernel that combines an infrared Gaussian term with a one-loop perturbative-QCD running coupling. They computed masses and decay constants for ground-state, radially-excited, and exotic scalar, vector, and flavoured-pseudoscalar mesons, and analyzed the relative importance of different Dirac amplitudes in the Bethe-Salpeter wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Current-quark masses at renormalisation point ζ=19 GeV: m_u=d^ζ = 3.4 MeV, m_s^ζ = 82 MeV, corresponding to renormalisation-group-invariant masses m̂_u,d = 6 MeV, m̂_s = 146 MeV
- Ground-state results with Dω = (0.8 GeV)^3 and ω=0.5 GeV: m_π = 0.134 GeV, f_π = 0.093 GeV; m_K = 0.495 GeV, f_K = 0.11 GeV; m_ρ = 0.74 GeV, f_ρ = 0.15 GeV; m_φ = 1.08 GeV, f_φ = 0.19 GeV
- With Dω = (1.1 GeV)^3 and ω=0.6 GeV: first radial excitation masses m_π1 = 1.575 GeV, m_ρ1 = 1.303 GeV; exotic state masses m_0-- = 1.053 GeV, m_1-+ = 1.318 GeV
- The ratio m_s/m_u = 24 from the fitted current-quark masses

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter equation studies of meson spectroscopy by Qin (2011) and others, confirming the known inadequacy of rainbow-ladder truncation for states beyond ground-state pseudoscalar and vector mesons, as previously discussed by Chang (2009, 2011). The results enable development of projection techniques for computing excited state properties and provide qualitative constraints on the long-range behaviour of the QCD interaction kernel, pointing toward the need for truncation schemes that properly incorporate dynamical chiral symmetry breaking effects in the Bethe-Salpeter kernels, as illustrated in the work of Chang (2011).
