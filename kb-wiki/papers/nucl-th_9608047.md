# On the direct nucleon decay of high-spin subbarrier single-particle states in near-magic nuclei

> The description of the direct nucleon decay of high-spin subbarrier one-particle states in near-magic nuclei is attempted using a simple optical model and the simplest version of the coupled-channel approach. The branching ratios for the direct decay of the several single-neutron states in $^{209}Pb$ and $^{91}Zr$ to the ground state and to the low-lying collective states of $^{208}Pb$ and $^{90}Zr$, respectively, are evaluated. Results are compared with recent experimental data.

## Key claim
The authors attempt to describe the direct neutron decay branching ratios of high-spin subbarrier single-neutron states in near-magic nuclei using a simple optical model and the simplest version of the coupled-channel approach, finding qualitative agreement with experimental data for decays to the ground state and 3⁻ states but some disagreement for decays to the 5⁻ state.

## Method
The authors evaluate branching ratios for direct neutron decay of single-neutron quasistationary states in ²⁰⁹Pb and ⁹¹Zr using an optical model for decay to the ground state and a coupled-channel approach for decay to low-lying collective states (3⁻ and 5⁻). They employ a "combined" optical-model potential of Woods-Saxon type with parameters from Chepurnov (1967) and an energy-dependent imaginary part, and calculate the relevant Green functions and escape widths. The branching ratios are averaged over finite excitation energy intervals and compared with experimental data from the (α,³He) reaction reported by Fortier (1995) and Harakeh (1995).

## Key numbers
- For ²⁰⁹Pb, the calculated branching ratio ⟨b_{L^π}⟩_Δ for direct neutron decay to the 0⁺ ground state is in qualitative agreement with experiment; the value for the 3⁻ state (E=2.615 MeV, β₃=0.12) is also in qualitative agreement; the value for the 5⁻ state (E=3.198 MeV, β₅=0.06) shows some disagreement.
- For ⁹¹Zr, the calculated branching ratio ⟨b_{L^π}⟩_Δ for direct neutron decay to the 0⁺ ground state is in qualitative agreement with experiment; the value for the 3⁻ state (E=2.748 MeV, β₃=0.14) is also in qualitative agreement; the value for the 5⁻ state (E=2.319 MeV, β₅=0.08) shows some disagreement.
- The single-particle spreading width Γ_λ^↓ is defined as 2∫W(r,ε_λ)|χ_λ^(0)(r)|² dr, where W is the imaginary part of the optical potential.
- The substitution of ∂f_ws/∂r by f_ws in the vertex changes the ⟨b_{L^π}⟩_Δ value by less than 20%.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical description of one-nucleon transfer reactions (Chepurnov 1967, DWBA approach) to the direct decay of the excited single-particle states, building on the strength function formalism of Urin (1994). The method is applied to recent experimental data on direct neutron decay from ²⁰⁹Pb and ⁹¹Zr excited via the (α,³He) reaction (Fortier 1995, Harakeh 1995). The approach enables future application to direct proton decay of subbarrier single-proton states and to "soft" spherical nuclei where strong particle-2⁺-phonon coupling occurs, with the need for more advanced coupled-channel methods and microscopic transition potentials.
