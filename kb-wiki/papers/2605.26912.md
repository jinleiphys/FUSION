# Nuclear structure within the relativistic mean field approach including chiral symmetry and quark confinement effects

> The relativistic mean field approach, within a theoretical framework known as the chiral confining model incorporating chiral symmetry breaking and quark confinement effects, is applied for the first time to finite nuclei. Model parameters are calibrated through a Bayesian approach using nuclear empirical properties and doubly magic nuclei. The model provides a satisfactory description of binding energies and charge radii for medium and heavy nuclei, while larger discrepancies are observed in light nuclei. This behavior is linked to the constrained form of the chiral potential, which reduces flexibility away from saturation density. Charge radii are reproduced with very good accuracy, although density profiles remain slightly more diffuse than experimental ones. The extension to open-shell nuclei with a separable Gogny pairing interaction reveals enhanced pairing correlations associated with the large Dirac effective mass, reduced spin-orbit splittings, and increased single-particle level density around the Fermi surface. Finally, departures from the linear sigma model potential motivated by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio framework are explored. Allowing additional flexibility in the chiral potential improves the description of light nuclei and reduces the Dirac mass, which in turn suppresses the anomalous pairing. These results highlight the sensitivity of finite nuclei properties to the structure of the chiral potential and the associated single-particle spectrum.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean field approach incorporating chiral symmetry breaking and quark confinement effects (the chiral confining model, RMF-CC) is applied to finite nuclei for the first time, providing a satisfactory description of binding energies and charge radii for medium and heavy nuclei, but with larger discrepancies in light nuclei due to the constrained form of the chiral potential which reduces flexibility away from saturation density.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean field Lagrangian where the scalar field is identified with the chiral-invariant radial fluctuation of the quark condensate, and nucleon polarization from confinement effects is included via a scalar polarizability parameter. Model parameters (m_s, g_s, C_NS, g_omega, g_rho) are calibrated through a Bayesian Markov Chain Monte Carlo approach using nuclear empirical parameters (saturation density, energy, symmetry energy, incompressibility) and binding energies and charge radii of seven doubly magic nuclei (16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, 56Ni, 100Sn, 132Sn, 208Pb). Open-shell nuclei are then treated by adding a separable Gogny pairing interaction based on the D1S force.

## Key numbers

- Saturation energy E_sat = -16.26 (+0.07/-0.07) MeV for the RMF-CC1 parametrization
- Saturation density n_sat = 0.159 (+0.002/-0.001) fm^-3 for RMF-CC1
- Symmetry energy E_sym = 38 (+1/-1) MeV for RMF-CC1, about 20% larger than the nominal data constraint of 32 MeV
- Incompressibility modulus K_sat = 199 (+13/-16) MeV for RMF-CC1
- Dirac effective mass M*_D / M_N = 0.65 (+0.01/-0.01) for RMF-CC1, compared to 0.60 for NL3 and 0.57 for DD-ME2
- Non-relativistic effective mass M*_NR / M_N = 0.72 (+0.01/-0.01) for RMF-CC1
- Scalar meson mass m_s = 659.474 (+5.496/-5.605) MeV for RMF-CC1
- Charge radii reproduced with less than about 2% deviation from experimental values across all mass regions
- Reducing the pairing strength by a factor of about 0.9 for both protons and neutrons removes anomalous pairing in closed-shell nuclei for RMF-CC1; PDM-3 required rescaling by 0.4

## Context

This work extends the chiral confining model (CCM), previously applied to nuclear matter by Massot (2008), Rahul (2022), and Chamseddine (2023), to finite nuclei for the first time. The approach contrasts with phenomenological relativistic mean field models such as NL3 (Lalazissis, 1996) and DD-ME2 (Lalazissis, 2005), which have free parameters in the scalar potential or density-dependent couplings, and with the Parity Doublet Model (PDM-3) applied to finite nuclei by Kim (2023), which also uses a chiral potential but with higher-order terms and a different origin for the nucleon mass dependence. The results enable future exploration of how the structure of the chiral potential and the associated single-particle spectrum affect finite nuclei properties, and suggest that a density-dependent isovector coupling or additional isovector degrees of freedom may be needed for a quantitatively accurate isovector sector.
