# Magnetic field-dependence of the neutral pion mass in the linear sigma model coupled to quarks: The weak field case

> We compute the neutral pion mass dependence on a magnetic field in the weak field approximation at one-loop order. The calculation is carried out within the linear sigma model coupled to quarks and using Schwinger's proper-time representation for the charged particle propagators. We find that the neutral pion mass decreases with the field strength provided the boson self-coupling magnetic field corrections are also included. The calculation should be regarded as the setting of the trend for the neutral pion mass as the magnetic field is turned on.

## Key claim

The neutral pion mass decreases with magnetic field strength in the weak field approximation, but only when one-loop magnetic field corrections to the boson self-coupling are included; without these corrections, the naive calculation would predict an increase.

## Method

The authors computed the neutral pion self-energy at one-loop order within the Linear Sigma Model coupled to quarks (LSMq), using Schwinger's proper-time representation for charged particle propagators in a weak magnetic field. They included contributions from quark-antiquark loops, charged pion loops, neutral pion and sigma loops, and also computed the magnetic field correction to the boson self-coupling and the vacuum expectation value, keeping terms up to order (eB)².

## Key numbers

- The neutral pion mass decreases with magnetic field when the effective coupling λ^eff = λ [1 − (27λ/(160π²)) (eB)²/m_π⁴] is used, where m_π ≈ 140 MeV is the vacuum pion mass.
- The quark-loop contribution to the self-energy at q=0 is −i N_f g² (q_f B)² / (4π² M_f²), with M_f = g v the fermion mass.
- The charged pion loop contribution to the self-energy is i (λ/4) (eB)² / (96π² M_π²), where M_π is the pion mass.
- The magnetic field correction to the boson self-coupling is Δλ = −27 λ² (eB)² / (160π² M_π⁴).
- The vacuum expectation value correction is negligible in the weak field limit, with v_0^B ≈ v'_0.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson masses in magnetic fields using effective QCD models, including Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model calculations by Avancini (2017) and chiral perturbation theory by Andersen (2012). It addresses a disagreement between quenched Wilson and overlap fermion lattice QCD formulations resolved by Lattice QCD calculations (PhysRevD.97.034505, 2018), which showed the neutral pion mass decreases monotonically with |eB|. The calculation enables future extension to intermediate and strong magnetic fields for comparison with lattice QCD data.
