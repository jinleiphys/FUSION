# Correspondence between isoscalar monopole strengths and $α$ inelastic cross sections on $^{24}$Mg

> The correspondence between the isoscalar monopole (IS0) transition strengths and $α$ inelastic cross sections, the $B({\rm IS0})$-$(α,α')$ correspondence, is investigated for $^{24}$Mg($α,α'$) at 130 and 386 MeV. We adopt a microscopic coupled-channel reaction framework to link structural inputs, diagonal and transition densities, for $^{24}$Mg obtained with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics to the ($α,α'$) cross sections. We aim at clarifying how the $B({\rm IS0})$-$(α,α')$ correspondence is affected by the nuclear distortion, the in-medium modification to the nucleon-nucleon effective interaction in the scattering process, and the coupled-channels effect. It is found that these effects are significant and the explanation of the $B({\rm IS0})$-$(α,α')$ correspondence in the plane wave limit with the long-wavelength approximation, which is often used, makes no sense. Nevertheless, the $B({\rm IS0})$-$(α,α')$ correspondence tends to remain because of a strong constraint on the transition densities between the ground state and the $0^+$ excited states. The correspondence is found to hold at 386 MeV with an error of about 20%-30%, while it is seriously stained at 130 MeV mainly by the strong nuclear distortion. It is also found that when a $0^+$ state that has a different structure from a simple $α$ cluster state is considered, the $B({\rm IS0})$-$(α,α')$ correspondence becomes less valid. For a quantitative discussion on the $α$ clustering in $0^+$ excited states of nuclei, a microscopic description of both the structure and reaction parts will be necessary.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the often-used plane-wave limit with long-wavelength approximation explanation for the correspondence between isoscalar monopole (IS0) transition strengths and alpha inelastic cross sections is invalid, yet the correspondence tends to remain due to strong constraints on transition densities between the ground state and 0+ excited states. The correspondence holds at 386 MeV with an error of about 20%-30% but is seriously degraded at 130 MeV mainly by strong nuclear distortion.

## Method

The authors employed a microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) reaction framework for 24Mg(α,α') at 130 and 386 MeV, using diagonal and transition densities from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S interaction. They constructed α-nucleus potentials via the extended nucleon-nucleus folding model using the Melbourne g-matrix interaction, and compared results with plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA) using either the Franey-Love t-matrix or the Melbourne g-matrix, and with distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations to isolate the roles of nuclear distortion, in-medium modification, and coupled-channels effects.

## Key numbers

- The B(IS0) correspondence with α inelastic cross sections holds at 386 MeV with an error of about 20%-30%, while it is seriously stained at 130 MeV mainly by strong nuclear distortion.
- For the 0₃⁺ and 0₅⁺ states, the DWBA-g results deviate from B(IS0) ratios by less than about 20%-30% at 130 MeV and 10%-15% at 386 MeV.
- The coupled-channels effect reduces cross sections relative to DWBA by 30%-60% for the 0₂⁺, 0₃⁺, and 0₅⁺ states at both 130 and 386 MeV (e.g., ratio of 0.56 for 0₂⁺ at 130 MeV first peak, 0.69 at 386 MeV first peak).
- For the 0₇⁺ and 0₈⁺ states, the cross section ratios from PWBA-t differ from B(IS0) ratios by about 25%, and the correspondence does not hold well.
- The B(E0) value from AMD for the 0₂⁺ state is 30.32 e²fm⁴, compared to the experimental value of 44.9 ± 5.4 e²fm⁴, requiring a renormalization factor of 1.217.

## Context

This work extends the study of the B(IS0)-(α,α') correspondence for 24Mg reported by Kanada-En'yo (2015), who showed that AMD-calculated B(IS0) values correlated with those extracted from α inelastic scattering data. The present study uses the microscopic coupled-channel framework developed by Ogata et al. (2019-2021) to clarify that the simple plane-wave long-wavelength explanation is invalid, but the correspondence survives due to constraints on transition densities. The results enable future quantitative studies of α clustering in 0+ excited states of nuclei, but require microscopic descriptions of both structure and reaction parts, especially for states with different intrinsic configurations such as the ¹²C+¹²C structure found in the 0₇⁺ and 0₈⁺ states of 24Mg.
