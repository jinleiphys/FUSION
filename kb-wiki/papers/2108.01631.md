# Structural properties of nuclei with semi-magic number N(Z)=40

> Various ground state properties are explored for full isotonic(isotopic) chain of neutron number N(proton number Z)$=$40 using different families of Relativistic Mean-Field theory. Several properties such as nucleon separation energies, pairing energies, deformation, radii and nucleon density distributions are evaluated and compared with the experimental data as well as those from other microscopic and macroscopic models. N$=$40 isotonic chain presents ample of support for the neutron magicity and articulates double magicity in recently discovered $^{60}$Ca and $^{68}$Ni. Our results are in close conformity with recently measured value of charge radius of $^{68}$Ni [S. Kaufmann \textit{et al.}, Phys. Rev. Lett. 124, 132502 (2020)] which supports the N$=$40 magicity. Contrarily, Zr isotopes (Z$=$40) display variety of shapes leading to the phenomenon of shape transitions and shape co-existence. The role of 3s$_{1/2}$ state, which leads to central depletion if unoccupied, is also investigated. $^{56}_{16}$S and $^{122}_{40}$Zr are found to be doubly bubble nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the N=40 isotonic chain exhibits strong neutron magicity, with double magicity in \({}^{60}\)Ca and \({}^{68}\)Ni, and that the DD-ME2 parameter set of the Relativistic Mean-Field model provides results in close conformity with the measured charge radius of \({}^{68}\)Ni from Kaufmann et al. (2020). However, the authors note that for drip-line nuclei such as \({}^{80}\)Zr, the treatment of pairing becomes very crucial and leaves somewhat distinct results from different variants of theoretical approaches, indicating that the magicity claims are conditional on the specific theoretical framework and pairing treatment used.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic study of the full chain of N=40 isotones (proton number Z=16 to Z=42) and Z=40 (Zr) isotopes (neutron number N=38 to N=112) using three classes of Relativistic Mean-Field (RMF) models: density-dependent meson-exchange (DD-ME2 parameter), nonlinear meson-nucleon coupling (NL3* and FSU-Gold parameters), and density-dependent point-coupling (DD-PCX parameter). Ground state properties including deformation, separation energies, pairing energies, single particle energies, radii, and nucleon density distributions were calculated and compared with experimental data and other microscopic models such as FRDM, Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB-24), and Gogny D1S interactions.

## Key numbers

* Two-proton drip line for N=40 isotones is found at \({}^{82}\)Mo from DD-ME2 and DD-PCX parameters, whereas NL3* and FSU-Gold parameters give \({}^{80}\)Zr.
* Two-neutron drip line for Zr isotopes is reported as \({}^{122}\)Zr from density-dependent parameters, but extends to \({}^{138}\)Zr (NL3*) and \({}^{130}\)Zr (FSU-Gold) from nonlinear parameters.
* The gap between neutron pf-shell and \(\nu1g_{9/2}\) state responsible for N=40 shell closure varies from 4.4 MeV to 5.7 MeV for the considered nuclei.
* Symmetry energy J values are 32.30 MeV (DD-ME2), 38.6 MeV (NL3*), 32.6 MeV (FSU-Gold), and 31.12 MeV (DD-PCX); slope parameter L values are 51.26 MeV (DD-ME2), 122.6 MeV (NL3*), 60.5 MeV (FSU-Gold), and 46.32 MeV (DD-PCX).
* Excitation energy for shape coexistence in \({}^{72}\)Ge is 0.55 MeV (oblate secondary minima), in \({}^{76}\)Kr is 0.65 MeV (oblate) and 2.51 MeV (prolate), and in \({}^{98}\)Zr is 0.80 MeV (oblate secondary with prolate dominant).
* Pairing strength used in BCS calculations is V0 = 350 MeV fm\(^3\) with a cutoff energy E_cut = 8.0 MeV; for separable pairing force the parameters are G = 728 MeV fm\(^3\) and \(\alpha\) = 0.644 fm.

## Context

This work extends systematic covariant density functional studies of N=40 isotones by Wang et al. (2015) and shape coexistence investigations in Zr isotopes using interacting boson model with configuration mixing by Ramos et al. (2019) and Hartree-Fock calculations with M3Y-P6 interaction by Miyahara et al. (2018). The study is motivated by recent experimental discoveries including \({}^{60}\)Ca at RIKEN (Tarasov et al., 2018), shape-coexistence in \({}^{98}\)Zr (Purnima et al., 2018), and the first spectroscopy of \({}^{62}\)Ti (Cortes et al., 2020). The results enable further investigation of the island of inversion around N=40, the role of the 3s\(_{1/2}\) state in central depletion leading to doubly bubble nuclei (\({}^{56}_{16}\)S and \({}^{122}_{40}\)Zr), and the need for deeper study of pairing treatment for drip-line nuclei.
