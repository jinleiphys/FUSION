# Final-state interactions effects in neutral-current neutrino and antineutrino cross sections at MiniBooNE kinematics

> The analysis of the recent neutral-current elastic neutrino and antineutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections measured by the MiniBooNE Collaboration requires relativistic theoretical descriptions also accounting for the role of final-state interactions. In this work we investigate the sensitivity to final-state interactions and compare the MiniBooNE data with the results obtained in the relativistic Green's function model with different parameterizations for the phenomenological relativistic optical potential.

## Key claim

The relativistic Green's function (RGF) model can describe the MiniBooNE neutral-current elastic neutrino and antineutrino cross sections using the standard value of the nucleon axial mass (1.03 GeV/c²), without needing to increase it, but only when the full complex optical potential is used; the specific choice of optical potential parametrization produces visible differences at low Q².

## Method

The authors computed flux-averaged differential cross sections for neutral-current elastic neutrino-nucleus and antineutrino-nucleus scattering on carbon, using the relativistic Green's function model with three different parametrizations of the phenomenological relativistic optical potential (EDAD1, EDAI, and DEM). They compared these results with those from the relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA, no final-state interactions) and the relativistic mean field model (RMF, using only the real part of the optical potential), and with the MiniBooNE data.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon axial mass set to the standard value M_A = 1.03 GeV/c² throughout all calculations
- RPWIA results (no FSI) show satisfactory agreement with the magnitude of neutrino data, while rROP results (real part only of EDAI potential) underestimate the data except for Q² ≥ 0.6 (GeV/c)²
- RGF-DEM results provide the best agreement with both neutrino and antineutrino data across the entire Q² range
- For the ratio of antineutrino to neutrino cross sections, all RGF results are practically independent of the optical potential parametrization and describe the data reasonably for Q² ≤ 1 (GeV/c)², but slightly underestimate the data for Q² ≥ 1 (GeV/c)²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RGF calculations for neutrino-nucleus scattering (Meucci 2011, PhysRevC 2013) to the recently available MiniBooNE antineutrino data, using the correct antineutrino efficiency coefficients. The results contradict the need for an enhanced axial mass (M_A ~ 1.20-1.40 GeV/c²) that simpler impulse-approximation models require to fit the data, as noted by Amaro (2006), Antonov (2007), Benhar (2011), and Ankowski (2012). The RGF model recovers contributions beyond direct one-nucleon emission through the imaginary part of the phenomenological optical potential, but the authors note that a better determination of a relativistic optical potential that closely fulfills dispersion relations deserves further investigation.
