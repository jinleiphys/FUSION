# Pion re-scattering in pi0 production near threshold

> We compare the most used approximations to calculate the contribution from pion re-scattering mechanisms to pion production from proton-proton scattering, near threshold. Our calculation employs a pseudo-vector coupling for the pi NN vertex and realistic amplitudes for the pi N re-scattering and the NN transitions.

# Pion re-scattering in pi0 production near threshold

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the choice of approximation for the energy of the exchanged pion in the re-scattering vertex is crucial for the cross section strength, while the choice for the pion propagator is not very decisive; only the on-shell approximation to the exact pion propagator gives a larger cross section that practically coincides with the reference result when using the Bonn B potential.

## Method

The authors start from the full Feynman diagram for pion production in proton-proton scattering and perform a partial fraction decomposition of the energy integration to isolate the DWBA amplitude from stretched box contributions. They then compare three common approximations for the pion propagator, fixed kinematics, on-shell, and static approximation, using a pseudo-vector coupling for the πNN vertex, chiral perturbation theory πN-πN re-scattering amplitudes, and the Bonn B potential for the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the final state.

## Key numbers

- The fixed kinematics approximation freezes the exchanged pion energy at mπ/2, its value at threshold.
- The on-shell and fixed kinematics approximations coincide at threshold and their deviation increases with energy.
- The DWBA amplitude is dominant over stretched boxes for the physical case, even more pronounced than in the scalar toy model.

## Context

This work extends the toy model study of Hanhart et al. (2001) for scalar particles and interactions to a physical model with nucleons and pions. The paper addresses the need for controlled calculations of the re-scattering diagram before quantitative conclusions can be drawn about short-range mechanisms in pp → ppπ0, which are subsumed in low-energy constants that appear in two- and three-nucleon potentials (Epelbaum et al.) and have not yet been precisely determined. The results enable more reliable extraction of the unknown strength of short-range mechanisms from experimental data.
