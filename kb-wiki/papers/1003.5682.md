# QCD thermodynamics with nonzero chemical potential at $N_t=6$ and effects from heavy quarks

> We extend our work on QCD thermodynamics with 2+1 quark flavors at nonzero chemical potential to finer lattices with $N_t=6$. We study the equation of state and other thermodynamic quantities, such as quark number densities and susceptibilities, and compare them with our previous results at $N_t=4$. We also calculate the effects of the addition of the charm and bottom quarks on the equation of state at zero and nonzero chemical potential. These effects are important for cosmological studies of the early Universe.

# QCD thermodynamics with nonzero chemical potential at $N_t=6$ and effects from heavy quarks

## Key claim

The authors extend their previous 2+1 flavor QCD thermodynamics study at nonzero chemical potential from $N_t=4$ to $N_t=6$ lattices, finding small but significant discretization effects in Taylor expansion coefficients; however, the isentropic equation of state shows very little difference between the two lattice spacings, and the charm quark contribution to the energy density reaches about 20% at temperatures near 400 MeV, though heavy-quark discretization artifacts in the asqtad action may depress these lattice values below the continuum limit.

## Method

The authors employ the Taylor expansion method up to sixth order in $\mu_q/T$ to compute the pressure, interaction measure, quark number densities, and susceptibilities for 2+1 flavor QCD on $N_t=6$ lattices using asqtad improved staggered fermions with two degenerate light quarks ($m_l = m_s/10$) and a physical strange quark. They then add charm and bottom quarks in the heavy-quark-quenched approximation (valence quarks only, no sea loops), tuning the charm mass to the $D_s$ meson mass ($m_c/m_s = 10$) and the bottom mass to the $B_s$ meson mass ($m_b/m_s = 38$), and compute the resulting equation of state at zero and nonzero chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark contribution to the energy density reaches about 20% at the highest available temperature (approximately 400 MeV) for the 2+1+1 flavor case at zero chemical potential
- The charm quark contributions to the energy density and pressure are about 25%-30% lower than those of Cheng (2007) at temperatures around 400 MeV, attributed to larger discretization effects in the asqtad action
- Increasing the temporal extent from $N_t=4$ to $N_t=6$ lowers the light-quark density, light-light and strange-strange quark number susceptibilities by 4%-10%
- The $c_{002}$ coefficient (proportional to the charm quark number susceptibility at zero chemical potential) becomes slightly negative for temperatures up to about 220 MeV, an unphysical artifact of the asqtad action's Naik term
- The bottom quark contribution to the energy density at zero chemical potential is within one standard deviation at temperatures near 400 MeV
- The ratio $m_c/m_s = 10$ was determined with a 4% uncertainty; $m_b/m_s = 38$ was determined with a 5% uncertainty

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous 2+1 flavor thermodynamics studies at $N_t=4$ (Bernard 2007) to finer lattices, providing a step toward continuum extrapolation. The charm quark contribution at zero chemical potential was previously studied on the lattice by Cheng (2007) using the p4 fermion formulation at $N_t=4$, 6, and 8; the present results are qualitatively similar but quantitatively lower by 25%-30%. The heavy-quark-quenched approximation used here is motivated by the relevance of a full-flavor equation of state for cosmological studies of the early Universe (Laine 2006, McGuigan 2008), while the 2+1 flavor equation of state remains sufficient for heavy-ion collision phenomenology at RHIC due to the short thermalization timescale. The unphysical negative $c_{002}$ coefficient identified here motivates future work with tuned Naik terms or larger $N_t$ to control heavy-quark discretization effects.
