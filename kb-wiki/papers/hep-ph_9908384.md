# Covariance of Light-Front Models: Pair Current

> We compute the "+" component of the electromagnetic current of a composite spin-one two-fermion system for vanishing momentum transfer component $q^+=q^0+q^3$. In particular, we extract the nonvanishing pair production amplitude on the light-front. It is a consequence of the longitudinal zero momentum mode, contributing to the light-front current in the Breit-frame. The covariance of the current is violated, if such pair terms are not included in its matrix elements. We illustrate our discussion with some numerical examples.

## Key claim

The "+" component of the electromagnetic current of a composite spin-one two-fermion system, computed in the Breit-frame with vanishing momentum transfer component q^+=0, violates covariance if the longitudinal zero-momentum mode (pair production amplitude) is omitted from the light-front calculation; including this pair term restores the angular condition and reproduces the covariant result.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop triangle Feynman diagram for the electromagnetic current of a vector particle modeled as a composite fermion system, using two different fermion-vector couplings: gamma-mu coupling and derivative coupling. They perform the integration over the k^- momentum component using a "dislocation of pole" technique to extract the zero-mode contribution, which corresponds to virtual pair production by the photon at q^+=0. The light-front calculation with pair terms is compared to the full covariant calculation and to the naive light-front calculation that omits pair terms.

## Key numbers

- Vector particle mass m_v = 0.77 GeV, fermion mass m = 0.43 GeV, regulator mass m_R = 1.8 GeV (parameters from de Melo and Frederico (1997))
- For q^2 approaching 0, the zero-mode contribution to j^+_{xx} vanishes as q^2
- For small momentum transfer, the pair term in j^+_{zx} is proportional to q_x
- The angular condition Delta(q^2) = j^+_{yy} - j^+_{zz} is identically zero when pair terms are included, but violated when they are omitted; even at q_x=0, the zero-mode contribution to j^+_{zz} is nonvanishing

## Context

This work extends the analysis of de Melo and Frederico (1997), which showed that the current component j^+ of a spin-one system violates covariance, parity, and gauge invariance when naive Cauchy integration is performed. It builds on the "dislocation of pole" integration method developed by de Melo, Sales, Frederico, and Sauer (1998) and by Naus, de Melo, and Frederico (1998) for extracting zero-mode contributions. The results enable consistent light-front calculations of electromagnetic form factors for composite vector particles by demonstrating that pair terms must be included to restore rotational covariance, contradicting earlier assumptions that a description purely in terms of a two-particle light-front wave-function would suffice.
