# Model space truncation in shell-model fits

> We carry out an interacting shell-model study of binding energies and spectra in the $sd$-shell nuclei to examine the effect of truncation of the shell-model spaces. Starting with a Hamiltonian defined in a larger space and truncating to the $sd$ shell, the binding energies are strongly affected by the truncation, but the effect on the excitation energies is an order of magnitude smaller. We then refit the matrix elements of the two-particle interaction to compensate for the space truncation, and find that it is easy to capture 90% of the binding energy shifts by refitting a few parameters. With the full parameter space of the two-particle Hamiltonian, we find that both the binding energies and the excitation energy can be fitted with remaining residual error about 5% of the average error from the truncation. Numerically, the rms initial error associated with our Hamiltonian is 3.4 MeV and the remaining residual error is 0.16 MeV. This is comparable to the empirical error found in $sd$-shell interacting shell model fits to experimental data\cite{br06}.

# Model space truncation in shell-model fits

## Key claim

The authors show that truncating a shell-model Hamiltonian from a larger space to the *sd* shell produces binding energy shifts an order of magnitude larger than excitation energy shifts, and that refitting as few as two parameters (a contact interaction) can capture 90% of the binding energy shift; however, the residual error after a full 63-parameter refit (0.16 MeV) is comparable to the empirical error of the USDB fit to experimental data (0.13 MeV), suggesting that the truncation error may already be at the level of the experimental fit quality.

## Method

The authors define a "target" Hamiltonian in an extended space including all 2-ħω excitations into the *pf* and *sdg* shells, using the USDB interaction for the *sd* shell and a contact interaction for off-diagonal and higher-shell matrix elements. They compute binding energies and excitation energies for 90 binding energies and 559 excitation energies in *sd*-shell nuclei from ¹⁷O to ⁴⁰Ca, then refit the *sd*-shell Hamiltonian parameters (3 single-particle energies and 63 two-body matrix elements) to reproduce the target energies using singular value decomposition and simple model Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

- Initial rms energy difference between the *sd*-shell and extended-space Hamiltonians: 3.4 MeV for the full data set
- Binding energy rms difference: 9.2 MeV; excitation energy rms difference: 0.53 MeV
- After refitting with 63 parameters (full two-body space), residual rms error: 0.16 MeV
- After refitting with 2 parameters (contact interaction), residual rms error: 0.46 MeV
- After refitting with 11 parameters (full monopole interaction), residual rms error: 0.92 MeV (2-parameter) to 1.18 MeV (11-parameter)
- Rank-19 SV fit reduces binding energy rms error by a factor of 40 relative to initial 9.0 MeV
- Empirical error in USDB fit to experimental data: 0.13 MeV (Brown and Richter, 2006)

## Context

This work extends the methodology of shell-model fits by Brown and Richter (2006) for the *sd*-shell USDB interaction, explicitly quantifying the effect of truncating the model space to the *sd* shell. The authors find that the monopole interaction dominates the singular value decomposition of the sensitivity matrix, consistent with findings by Papenbrock and collaborators (2006) for fixed J,T spectra. The results enable systematic error estimation for shell-model truncation and suggest that simple contact or monopole interactions may suffice to capture the dominant truncation effects, with implications for improving configuration-interaction theory by treating higher shells.
