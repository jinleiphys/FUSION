# Superfluid extension of the self-consistent time-dependent band theory for neutron star matter: Anti-entrainment versus superfluid effects in the slab phase

> Background: The inner crust of neutron stars consists of a Coulomb lattice of neutron-rich nuclei, immersed in a sea of superfluid neutrons with background relativistic electron gas. A proper quantum mechanical treatment for such a system under a periodic potential is the band theory of solids. The effect of band structure on the effective mass of dripped neutrons, the so-called \textit{entrainment effect}, is currently in a debatable situation, and it has been highly desired to develop a nuclear band theory taking into account neutron superfluidity in a fully self-consistent manner. Purpose: The main purpose of the present work is twofold: 1) to develop a formalism of the time-dependent self-consistent band theory, taking full account of nuclear superfluidity, based on time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) extended for superfluid systems, and 2) to quantify the effects of band structure and superfluidity on crustal properties, applying the formalism to the slab phase of nuclear matter in the $β$ equilibrium. Results: Static calculations have been performed for a range of baryon (nucleon) number density ($n_b=0.04-0.07$ fm$^{-3}$) under the $β$-equilibrium condition with and without superfluidity, for various inter-slab spacings. From a dynamic response to an external potential, we extract the collective mass of a slab and that of protons immersed in neutron superfluid. From the results, we find that the collective mass of a slab is substantially reduced by 57.5--82.5\% for $n_b=0.04-0.07$ fm$^{-3}$, which corresponds to an enhancement of conduction neutron number density and, thus, to a reduction of the neutron effective mass, which we call the anti-entrainment effect. We discuss novel phenomena associated with superfluidity, quasiparticle resonances in the inner crust, which are absent in normal systems. *shortened due to the arXiv word limit.

## Key claim

The authors develop a fully self-consistent, microscopic time-dependent band theory for superfluid neutron star matter and find that band structure effects *reduce* the neutron effective mass by about 20–40% (the "anti-entrainment" effect) for baryon densities 0.04–0.07 fm⁻³, with superfluidity slightly *enhancing* this reduction, contradicting earlier non-self-consistent calculations that predicted an increase of the effective mass.

## Method

The formalism extends the time-dependent self-consistent band theory of Sekizawa, Kobayashi, and Matsuo (2022) to superfluid systems by implementing time-dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA) with a Skyrme-type energy density functional in coordinate space, imposing Bloch's boundary condition on quasiparticle wave functions. Static HFB calculations are performed for the slab (lasagna) phase of neutron star matter under β-equilibrium for baryon densities 0.04–0.07 fm⁻³ with various inter-slab spacings, and a real-time method is used to dynamically extract collective masses from the system's response to an external potential.

## Key numbers

- Collective mass of a slab is reduced by 57.5–82.5% for baryon densities n_b = 0.04–0.07 fm⁻³ due to band structure effects
- Neutron effective mass m_n^*/m_n = 0.58 at n_b = 0.07 fm⁻³ (with superfluidity included)
- The reduction of neutron effective mass is about 20–40% for n_b = 0.04–0.07 fm⁻³
- Static calculations performed for baryon number density range n_b = 0.04–0.07 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the fully self-consistent band theory calculations of Kashiwaba and Nakatsukasa (2019) and Sekizawa, Kobayashi, and Matsuo (2022) by including neutron superfluidity, which those earlier works neglected. It contradicts the earlier non-self-consistent results of Carter, Chamel, and Haensel (2005) and Chamel (2005, 2012), who found that band structure effects *increase* the neutron effective mass (entrainment), sometimes by factors of 10 or more. The formalism enables future extensions to 2D and 3D crystalline phases (rod and spherical nuclei) to provide conclusive values of the neutron effective mass throughout the inner crust of neutron stars.
