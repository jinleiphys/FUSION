# Repulsive Core of NN S-Wave Scattering in a Quark Model with a Condensed Vacuum

> We work in a chiral invariant quark model, with a condensed vacuum, characterized by only one parameter. Bound state equations for the nucleon and Delta are solved in order to obtain an updated value of their radii and masses. Nucleon-nucleon S-Wave scattering is studied in the RGM framework both for isospin T=1 and T=0. The phase shifts are calculated and an equivalent local potential, which is consistent with K-N scattering, is derived. The result is a reasonable microscopic short range repulsion in the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

# Repulsive Core of NN S-Wave Scattering in a Quark Model with a Condensed Vacuum

## Key claim

The authors derive a microscopic short-range repulsion in nucleon-nucleon S-wave scattering from a chiral invariant quark model with a condensed vacuum, using only one free parameter (the harmonic potential strength K0). The repulsive core is soft (comparable to the Nijmegen NijmI potential) and is obtained simultaneously with reasonable descriptions of meson and baryon spectra, vector meson decays, and KN scattering, though the model neglects pionic cloud contributions responsible for intermediate- and long-range forces.

## Method

The authors solve bound-state Salpeter equations for the nucleon and Delta using a variational harmonic oscillator wave function, with the chiral angle determined by the mass-gap equation from a Coulomb harmonic confining potential. Nucleon-nucleon S-wave scattering is studied in the Resonating Group Method (RGM) framework for both isospin T=1 and T=0 channels, using only the hyperfine quark-quark potential and neglecting spin-orbit and tensor terms. The RGM equation is reduced to a non-local Schrodinger-type equation with a separable potential, from which phase shifts and an equivalent local potential are derived analytically.

## Key numbers

- Harmonic potential strength: K0 = 247 MeV (fixed from previous KN scattering calculations)
- Oscillator parameter: alpha = 296.4 MeV
- Bare nucleon mean radius: 0.66 fm (with K0 = 247 MeV); reduces to 0.57 fm with K0 = 290 MeV
- Momentum-space matrix element: V00 = -0.0671 K0 = -16.6 MeV
- Beta parameter for chiral angle approximation: beta = 0.582 (from sin(phi(p)) = exp(-p^2/(2*beta^2))
- Equivalent local potential peak values from other quark models cited for comparison: 600-1300 MeV (Faessler, Oka), 500 MeV (Ribeiro square potential), 450 MeV (Liberman potential)

## Context

This work extends the chiral invariant quark model with BCS-type condensed vacuum developed by Le Yaouanc, Olivier, Pene, and Raynal (1984) and previously applied to KN scattering, hadronic spectra, and pion-nucleon couplings. It contrasts with earlier RGM calculations by Ribeiro (1980) and Oka and Yazaki (1981) which obtained harder repulsive cores, and with the skyrmion picture of NN repulsion. The model enables a unified description of low-energy hadronic phenomena including NN scattering, meson and baryon spectra, and exotic scattering cross sections within a single-parameter framework, though the authors note that inclusion of meson exchange contributions (omega, rho) and coupled N-pi channels is needed for a complete NN interaction.
