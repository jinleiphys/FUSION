# Calculable microscopic theory for $^{12}$C($α, γ$)$^{16}$O cross section near Gamow window

> $^{12}$C$(α, γ)^{16}$O radiative-capture process is a key reaction to produce the element of oxygen in stars. Measuring the cross section near the Gamow window is extremely hard because it is too small. To make a theoretical contribution towards resolving the long-standing problem, I present a microscopic formulation that aims at providing all materials needed to calculate the cross section. The states of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O relevant to the reaction are respectively described with fully microscopic 3 $α$-particle and 4 $α$-particle configurations, in which the relative motion among the $α$ particles is expanded in terms of correlated Gaussian basis functions. The configuration space has the advantage of being able to well describe the reduced $α$-width amplitudes of the states of $^{16}$O. Both electric dipole and electric quadrupole transitions are responsible for the radiative-capture process. The $α$ particle is described with a $(0s)^4$ configuration admixed with a small amount of an isospin $T=1$ impurity component, which is crucially important to account for the isovector electric dipole transition. The isoscalar electric dipole operators are also taken into account up to the first order beyond the long-wavelength approximation. All the necessary ingredients are provided to make the paper self-contained and ready for numerical computations.

## Key claim

This paper presents a fully microscopic theoretical framework for calculating the $^{12}$C($\alpha, \gamma$)$^{16}$O cross section near the Gamow window, but it does not yet produce numerical cross-section results; instead it provides all the formal ingredients needed for such a calculation, with the expectation that the conditions for reproducing experimental energies and transition rates can be met based on prior semi-microscopic and microscopic studies.

## Method

The author formulates the $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O states using fully microscopic 3$\alpha$-particle and 4$\alpha$-particle configurations, where the relative motion among $\alpha$ particles is expanded in correlated Gaussian basis functions with global-vector representation. The $\alpha$ particle is described by a $(0s)^4$ configuration with a small admixture of an isospin $T=1$ impurity component to account for the isovector electric dipole transition, while isoscalar electric dipole operators are included up to first order beyond the long-wavelength approximation. Matrix elements between Slater determinants of the $N\alpha$-particle model are derived analytically, and the basis conversion from Slater determinants to correlated Gaussians is performed to enable calculation of bound and scattering states.

## Key numbers

- The Gamow peak energy for the $^{12}$C($\alpha, \gamma$)$^{16}$O reaction is $E_0 = 0.3$ MeV
- The $1^-$ resonance at 9.585 MeV has a width of 420 keV
- The subthreshold states of $^{16}$O are at 6.917 MeV ($2^+$) and 7.117 MeV ($1^-$)
- The astrophysical $S$-factors at $E_0$ are estimated as: $S_{\rm DC}(E_0) \approx 5 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV b, $S_{9.585}(E_0) \approx 1.5 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV b, $S_{6.917}(E_0) \approx 0.2$ MeV b, $S_{7.117}(E_0) \approx 0.1$ MeV b, with total $S(E_0) \approx 0.3$ MeV b
- The $T=1$ impurity parameter $|\epsilon|$ for the $\alpha$ particle is on the order of $4 \times 10^{-3}$
- The wave number $k$ for the $E1$ transition from the 7.117 MeV $1^-$ state is 0.036 fm$^{-1}$, giving a ratio factor $f \approx 2 \times 10^{-3}$ for $r = 2.5$ fm
- The $1_1^-$ state is located 45 keV below the $\alpha$ threshold
- The $\beta$-delayed $\alpha$-spectrum of $^{16}$N constrains $S_{7.117}(E_0) \approx 86 \pm 22$ keV b
- The second $0^+$ state of $^{16}$O is 1.11 MeV below the $^{12}$C+$\alpha$ threshold

## Context

This work extends the microscopic $^{12}$C+$\alpha$ studies of Suzuki (1976a, 1976b) and the isospin-mixing treatment of Descouvemont and Baye (1987) by incorporating both isovector and isoscalar $E1$ operators in a fully microscopic 4$\alpha$ cluster model with correlated Gaussian basis functions. The framework is designed to enable reliable calculation of the $^{12}$C($\alpha, \gamma$)$^{16}$O cross section, which is a long-standing problem in nuclear astrophysics because the cross section near the Gamow window is too small to measure experimentally. The approach aims to improve upon the phenomenological $S$-factor of the NACRE compilation and potential model studies by Katsuma (2008a, 2008b, 2008c), with the goal of determining the carbon-to-oxygen abundance ratio in stars.
