# Gamow Shell Model description of $^7$Li and elastic scattering reaction $^4$He($^3$H, $^3$H)$^4$He

> Spectrum of $^7$Li and elastic scattering reaction $^4$He($^3$H, $^3$H)$^4$He are studied using the unified description of the Gamow shell model in the coupled-channel formulation (GSMCC). The reaction channels are constructed using the cluster expansion with the two mass partitions [$^4$He + $^3$H], [$^6$Li + n].

## Key claim

The Gamow Shell Model in the coupled-channel formulation (GSMCC) can describe both the spectrum of \(^7\)Li and the elastic scattering reaction \(^4\)He(\(^3\)H, \(^3\)H)\(^4\)He within a single unified framework, but the calculation uses a hybrid approach where the \(^3\)H projectile is described by a realistic N\(^3\)LO interaction while the \(^6\)Li target and \(^7\)Li compound system use an effective FHT Hamiltonian, because neither interaction alone can simultaneously describe both subsystems.

## Method

The GSMCC formalism decomposes the \(A\)-body wave function into reaction channels built from two mass partitions: [\(^4\)He + \(^3\)H] and [\(^6\)Li + n]. The coupled-channel equations are solved using a Berggren basis expansion of the Green's function, with the Hamiltonian kernels computed from Slater determinant expansions of the cluster wave functions. The effective Hamiltonian uses a \(^4\)He inert core with valence nucleons, employing a Wood-Saxon one-body potential and the FHT nucleon-nucleon interaction supplemented by Coulomb terms, while the \(^3\)H projectile intrinsic states are generated separately using the N\(^3\)LO interaction without three-body forces.

## Key numbers

- The ground state energy of \(^3\)H calculated with the N\(^3\)LO interaction in six harmonic oscillator shells with oscillator length \(b = 1.65\) fm is \(-7.14\) MeV, compared to the experimental value of \(-8.48\) MeV.
- The \(5/2^-_1\) resonance has a 26% amplitude from the open \(^4\)He+\(^3\)H channel [\(^4\)He(\(0^+_1\))\(\otimes\)\(^3\)H(\(5/2^-\))]\(^{5/2^-_1}\) and only about 2% from the open neutron channel [\(^6\)Li(\(1^+_1\))\(\otimes\)n(\(p_{3/2}\))]\(^{5/2^-_1}\).
- In the \(7/2^-_2\) resonance, the summed probability of [\(^6\)Li(\(3^+_1\))\(\otimes\)n(\(p_{3/2,1/2}\))]\(^{7/2^-_2}\) amounts to 85%, while the weight of the \(^4\)He+\(^3\)H cluster channel is totally negligible.
- The estimated error on experimental cross-section results varies with the \(^3\)H bombarding energy and amounts to approximately 10%.

## Context

This work extends the GSMCC formalism, previously applied to \(^6\)Li via deuteron-induced elastic scattering on \(^4\)He by Mercenne (2019), to the case of multiple mass partitions and to the description of \(^7\)Li. The analysis demonstrates the near-threshold alignment of many-body states with a nearby reaction channel, a phenomenon discussed by Okolowicz (2012, 2013) that is expected to influence reaction rates at stellar energies. The approach enables future unified descriptions of low-energy transfer and radiative capture reactions of astrophysical interest, and can be applied to heavy nuclei where ab initio approaches are impractical.
