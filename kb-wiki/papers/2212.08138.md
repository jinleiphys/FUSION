# Muon capture on the deuteron in chiral effective field theory

> We consider the capture of a muon on a deuteron. An uncertainty analysis of the dominant channels is important for a careful analysis of forthcoming experimental data. We quantify the theoretical uncertainties of chiral effective-field-theory predictions of the muon-deuteron capture rate from the relevant neutron-neutron partial wave channels in the final state. We study the dependence on the cutoff used to regularize the interactions, low-energy constants calibrated using different fitting data and strategies, and truncation of the effective-field-theory expansion of the currents. Combining these approaches gives as an estimate of $Γ^{1/2}_{μd} = 399.1 \pm 7.6 \pm 4.4$ s$^{-1}$ for capture from the atomic doublet state, and $Γ^{3/2}_{μd} = 12.31 \pm 0.47 \pm 0.04$ s$^{-1}$ for capture from the quartet state.

## Key claim

The paper provides chiral effective-field-theory predictions for muon-deuteron capture rates that include contributions from all neutron-neutron partial wave channels up to total angular momentum J=2, with quantified theoretical uncertainties from cutoff variation, low-energy constant fitting strategies, and truncation of the chiral expansion; the abstract's quoted uncertainties (±7.6 and ±4.4 s⁻¹ for the doublet state) represent separate systematic components rather than a single combined error.

## Method

The authors calculated muon capture on the deuteron (μ⁻ + d → ν_μ + n + n) using chiral effective field theory with nucleon-nucleon interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) from the NNLOsim family (42 interactions across seven cutoff values from 450 to 600 MeV) and from the RS450 family at LO, NLO, and NNLO. They evaluated transition matrix elements from the deuteron bound state to neutron-neutron scattering states in partial wave channels (¹S₀, ³P₀, ³P₁, ³P₂, ¹D₂, ³F₂) using one-body and two-body electroweak currents derived up to Q⁰ (NNLO) in chiral power counting, then integrated over the final-state phase space to obtain capture rates for both hyperfine states (f=1/2 doublet and f=3/2 quartet).

## Key numbers

- Doublet state capture rate: Γ^{1/2}_{μd} = 399.1 ± 7.6 ± 4.4 s⁻¹ (first uncertainty from cutoff and fitting variation, second from EFT truncation)
- Quartet state capture rate: Γ^{3/2}_{μd} = 12.31 ± 0.47 ± 0.04 s⁻¹ (same uncertainty structure)
- The ¹S₀ channel gives the dominant contribution to both doublet and quartet capture rates
- Previous theoretical results for comparison: Ando et al. (2001) gave 386±4 s⁻¹ for the doublet; Marcucci et al. (2011) gave 399±3 s⁻¹; Elmeshneb (2015) gave 401 s⁻¹
- Previous experimental results for the doublet: Wang et al. (1965) 365±96 s⁻¹; Bertin et al. (1973) 445±60 s⁻¹; Martino (1986) 470±29 s⁻¹; Cargnelli et al. (1989) 409±40 s⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral EFT calculation (PhysRevC.98.065506) that focused only on the ¹S₀ neutron-neutron final state channel, which is the dominant channel and the only one sensitive to the leading two-nucleon axial current. By including higher partial waves (³P₀, ³P₁, ³P₂, ¹D₂, ³F₂) up to J=2, the calculation now provides a complete prediction that can be compared to an ongoing experiment at the Paul Scherrer Institute aiming for 1.5% precision (Kammel 2021). The calibrated two-nucleon axial current operator from this process can then be applied to other electroweak observables such as the proton-proton fusion rate, which is important for astrophysical models but cannot be measured at relevant energies.
