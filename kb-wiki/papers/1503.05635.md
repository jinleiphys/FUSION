# Quantum Field Theoretic Treatment of Pion Production via Proton Synchrotron Radiation in Strong Magnetic Fields: Effects of Landau Levels

> We study pion production from proton synchrotron radiation in the presence of strong magnetic fields. We derive the exact proton propagator from the Dirac equation in a strong magnetic field by explicitly including the anomalous magnetic moment. In this exact quantum-field approach the magnitude of pion synchrotron emission turns out to be much smaller than that obtained in the semi-classical approach. However, we also find that the anomalous magnetic moment of the proton greatly enhances the production rate about by two order magnitude.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a fully quantum-field-theoretic treatment of pion synchrotron radiation from protons in strong magnetic fields yields a much smaller production rate than the semi-classical approach, but that including the proton's anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) enhances the rate by about two orders of magnitude, making it comparable to the semi-classical result.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation for a proton in a uniform magnetic field (along the z-direction) including the anomalous magnetic moment term, and derived the exact proton propagator using a Green's function method. They then calculated the pionic decay width from the imaginary part of the proton self-energy from one-pion exchange, using a pseudo-vector pion-nucleon coupling, and summed over final proton spin and Landau levels for initial proton kinetic energies of 1-2 GeV and magnetic field strengths of 1-8 x 10^18 G.

## Key numbers

* For a 1 GeV proton in B = 5 x 10^18 G, the semi-classical approach gives a decay width of approximately 180 MeV (Tokara & Kajino, 1999) and 210 MeV (Baring, 1995); the quantum-field result without AMM is much smaller, but with AMM included the width for spin s_i = -1 is enhanced by about a factor of 100.
* The peak height of the pion energy spectrum with AMM is about 59 times larger than without AMM for e_p = 1 GeV, 36 times for e_p = 1.5 GeV, and 30 times for e_p = 2 GeV, all at B = 5 x 10^18 G.
* The peak height ratio (with AMM / without AMM) is about 93 for B = 1 x 10^18 G, 84 for B = 2 x 10^18 G, and 56 for B = 5 x 10^18 G, at a fixed proton energy of 1 GeV.
* The curvature parameter chi = e B E_i / M_p^3 is 0.069 for the adopted conditions (1 GeV proton, B = 5 x 10^18 G).

## Context

This work extends earlier semi-classical calculations of pion synchrotron radiation by Ginzburg (1965), Zharkov (1965), Tokara and Kajino (1999), and Baring (1995), which did not include the anomalous magnetic moment or a full quantum treatment of Landau levels. The results show that the AMM effect is vital for pion production in strong magnetic fields, particularly at small curvature radii, and the angular distribution of emitted pions could affect gamma-ray beaming in gamma-ray burst models. Future work is needed to extend the calculation to magnetar-strength fields (B ~ 10^15 G) and to study neutrino pair production via Z^0 boson emission.
