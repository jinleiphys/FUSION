# Momentum Distribution in Nuclear matter within a Perturbation Approximation

> It is shown that the norm corrections, introduced to avoid the violation of the constraints on the depletion of the hole states in the standard perturbative 2p2h approach, leads in nuclear matter to a dependence of the momentum distribution with the total nucleon number. This unphysical behavior, which in turn makes the depletion to be non-extensive, arises from contributions of disconnected diagrams contained in the norm. It is found that the extensivity is again recovered when the 4p4h excitations in the ground state are included, and a reasonable value for the total number of nucleons promoted above the Fermi level is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including norm corrections in the standard perturbative 2p2h approach to nuclear matter ground-state correlations produces an unphysical dependence of the momentum distribution on the total nucleon number A, making the depletion non-extensive; this problem is resolved by including 4p4h excitations, which cancel the disconnected diagram contributions and restore extensivity.

## Method

The authors compute the momentum distribution n(k) in nuclear matter using three perturbative approximations: (1) the usual second-order 2p2h approximation without norm corrections, (2) a normalized second-order approximation including the norm factor as proposed by Van Neck et al., and (3) a fourth-order approximation within a 2p2h+4p4h configuration space. The residual interaction is parameterized using Landau-Migdal parameters with a πNN form factor, and the momentum-space integrals are evaluated for energies up to 300 MeV at normal nuclear matter density (k_F = 1.36 fm).

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum: k_F = 1.36 fm (normal nuclear matter density)
- Number of particles promoted above the Fermi level in the plain second-order approximation: N_> = 16.8
- Number of particles promoted above the Fermi level in the normalized second-order approximation: N_> = 1.8
- Number of particles promoted above the Fermi level in the fourth-order 2p2h+4p4h approximation: N_> = 1.9
- Empirical value from optical potential analysis for ⁴⁰Ca: N_>(⁴⁰Ca) ≈ 2.7
- Norm factor for ⁴⁰Ca in the normalized approximation: 𝒩² = 0.11; for A=90: 𝒩² = 0.05
- Landau-Migdal parameters used: f = -0.12, f' = 0.39, g = 0, g' = 0.5
- πNN form factor cutoff: Λ_π = 800 MeV/(ħc k_F)

## Context

This work extends the critique by Van Neck et al. (1990s) that standard perturbative 2p2h calculations overestimate ground-state correlation effects when norm corrections are neglected, and follows a previous finite-nuclei study by Mariano et al. (1995) of Gamow-Teller strength that included norm effects. The paper resolves the unphysical A-dependence problem by showing that 4p4h excitations cancel disconnected diagram contributions, and the resulting depletion number N_> = 1.9 is consistent with empirical optical potential analyses by Johnson (1990s) and with the bound N_> ≤ 2 from shell-model constraints. The results suggest that 2p2h and 4p4h correlations are the dominant degrees of freedom for describing the nuclear ground state, enabling improved calculations of the nuclear response function in the quasielastic region.
