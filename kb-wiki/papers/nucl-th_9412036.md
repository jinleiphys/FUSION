# Does the excited state of the $^3$H nucleus exist?

> The suggestion is made that the excited state of the $^3 H$ nucleus found out recently in the reaction $H$ ($^6 He$,$α$) ({\it Pis'ma JETPh\/} {\bf 59} (1994) 301) has spin and parity $1/2^+$ and the same configuration that the ground one of $^6 He$. An amplitude of electromagnetic transition to the triton ground state is strongly suppressed, therefore the excited state cannot be detected in radiative capture of neutrons by deuterons. It is shown that in an elastic nd-scattering a resonance associated with the exited state may be absent due to the destructive interference of potential and resonant scattering phases.

## Key claim

The excited state of the triton (³H) reported in the reaction H(⁶He,α) at 7.0±0.3 MeV with width 0.6±0.3 MeV can be interpreted as a 1/2⁺ state with the same configuration as the ground state of ⁶He, and its absence in elastic nd-scattering and radiative neutron-deuteron capture can be explained without contradicting existing data.

## Method

The author proposes a shell-model configuration for the excited ³H state analogous to the ⁶He ground state, where two neutrons occupy 1p-states with zero total angular momentum, equivalent to a superposition of 1s and 2s states in relative coordinates. A two-channel scattering model with spherical rectangular wells (radius 7 fm) is used to analyze elastic nd-scattering in the doublet channel, coupling an elastic channel to a closed inelastic channel representing the excited triton state. The model parameters are fitted to reproduce the doublet scattering length a₂=0.65 fm, and the resulting scattering phases and cross sections are computed.

## Key numbers

- Excited state energy from the H(⁶He,α) reaction: E* = 7.0±0.3 MeV, width Γ* = 0.6±0.3 MeV
- Triton break-up threshold to neutron+deuteron: E_b = 6.26 MeV
- Doublet nd-scattering length: a₂ = 0.65±0.03 fm
- Quartet nd-scattering length: a₄ = 6.34±0.02 fm
- Deuteron break-up threshold: E_d = 2.23 MeV
- Model resonance energy in doublet channel: E₂ = 0.76 MeV (with fitted parameters U₂⁽⁰⁾=5 MeV, U₂⁽¹⁾=U₄=7.58 MeV, W=3.68 MeV)
- Reduced width from model: γ₂ = 0.57 MeV; total resonance width: Γ₂⁰ = 1.24 MeV
- Pole energies on non-physical sheet: Re(E_r) = 0.54 MeV, Im(E_r) = ±0.76 MeV

## Context

This work addresses the interpretation of a maximum in the H(⁶He,α) reaction cross section reported in Pis'ma JETPh 59 (1994) 301 as an excited state of ³H, which had no prior evidence (Ref. [2]). The author reconciles this with the absence of a resonance in nd-scattering data (Refs. [3,4]) and in radiative neutron-deuteron capture (Ref. [5]), and with earlier theoretical discussions of a virtual nd-state (Ref. [6]). The analysis enables future three-body calculations of nd-scattering to follow the energy dependence of inelastic channel wave functions, and suggests investigating triton excitation via inelastic electron scattering (E0-transition) and extending the analysis to the p+d system and ³He nucleus.
