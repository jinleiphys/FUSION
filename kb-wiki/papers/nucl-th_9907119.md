# Quasifree Kaon Photoproduction on Nuclei

> Investigations of the quasifree reaction A$(γ, K Y)$B are presented in the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA). For this purpose, we present a revised tree-level model of elementary kaon photoproduction that incorporates hadronic form factors consistent with gauge invariance, uses SU(3) values for the Born couplings and uses resonances consistent with multi-channel analyses. The potential of exclusive quasifree kaon photoproduction on nuclei to reveal details of the hyperon-nucleus interaction is examined. Detailed predictions for the coincidence cross section, the photon asymmetry, and the hyperon polarization and their sensitivities to the ingredients of the model are obtained for all six production channels. Under selected kinematics these observables are found to be sensitive to the hyperon-nucleus final state interaction. Some polarization observables are found to be insensitive to distortion effects, making them ideal tools to search for possible medium modifications of the elementary amplitude.

## Key claim

The paper presents a distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework for exclusive quasifree kaon photoproduction on nuclei, finding that under selected kinematics some polarization observables are insensitive to distortion effects while the coincidence cross section and hyperon polarization are sensitive to the hyperon-nucleus final state interaction, with the caveat that these conclusions depend on the specific kinematic arrangement and the choice of elementary amplitude model.

## Method

The authors developed a revised tree-level isobar model for elementary kaon photoproduction on the nucleon, incorporating hadronic form factors consistent with gauge invariance via Haberzettl's method, using SU(3) values for Born couplings and including only resonances with significant branching ratios into KΛ and KΣ channels based on multichannel analyses (S11(1650), P11(1710), P13(1720) for KΛ; S31(1900) and P31(1910) for KΣ; plus K*(892) and K1(1270) in the t-channel). This elementary amplitude was then embedded in a DWIA framework for nuclear targets, using single-particle wave functions from electron scattering, distorted kaon wave functions, and a hyperon-nucleus optical potential to compute coincidence cross sections, photon asymmetries, and hyperon polarizations for all six production channels.

## Key numbers

- The formation probability of bound hypernuclear states via (γ,K) reactions was estimated at around 5-10% of the total (γ,K+) strength on nuclear targets (Cotanch 1986).
- The decay width for K*+(892) → K+γ is 50 ± 5 keV; for K*0(892) → K0γ it is 117 ± 10 keV.
- The ratio g_{K*0K0γ} / g_{K*+K+γ} = -1.53, derived from the quark model prediction of Singer and Miller.
- The predicted ratio of decay widths Γ_{K1^0→K0γ} / Γ_{K1^+→K+γ} = 0.068 ± 0.110.
- The fit produced a soft cut-off of about 800 MeV for non-resonant background terms and a resonance cut-off of 1.89 GeV.
- The Born terms contribute about 10-20% to the total cross sections in the present model.
- The range of applicability of the isobar model is limited to energies up to W = 2.2-2.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hypernuclear formation via electromagnetic probes (Bennhold 1989, Cotanch 1986, Rosenthal 1988, Cohen 1989) by focusing on the quasifree continuum region rather than bound hypernuclear states. It builds on the authors' prior DWIA framework applied to pion photo- and electro-production (Lee 1993) and eta photoproduction (Lee 1996), and responds to the suggestion by Piekarewicz (1999) that polarization observables in PWIA are sensitive to medium modifications of the elementary amplitude. The results enable experimental searches for medium modifications of the elementary kaon photoproduction amplitude and provide predictions for the approved Jefferson Lab Hall B experiment (Hyde 1991), while also establishing the kinematic range where polarization observables can serve as clean probes of the hyperon-nucleus interaction.
