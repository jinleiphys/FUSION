# A hitchhiker's guide to nuclear polarization in muonic atoms

> I consider the so-called nuclear polarization correction to the 1S-levels in light to intermediate muonic atoms. An easy to use recipe to compute it is given. The calculation includes the effect of the nucleon polarization, i.e. the contribution from inelastic states in the hadronic range, and Coulomb corrections beyond the leading logarithm approximation to both nuclear and nucleon polarization. I provide numerical estimates for $4\leq Z\leq41$, compare to the estimates in the literature and discuss the need for future improvements.

# A hitchhiker's guide to nuclear polarization in muonic atoms

## Key claim

The nuclear polarization correction to 1S-levels in muonic atoms with atomic numbers 4 ≤ Z ≤ 41 can be computed using a simple recipe that includes both nuclear and nucleon polarization contributions, but the leading-logarithm approximation for Coulomb corrections becomes inadequate for Z > 5 and must be expanded to higher orders. The nucleon polarization contribution, traditionally neglected for atoms heavier than helium, becomes comparable to experimental precision starting from calcium and must be included.

## Method

The author splits the two-photon exchange loop integral into two energy regions: the nuclear range below pion production threshold (ν < ν_π ≈ 140 MeV) treated nonrelativistically using the longitudinal response function in the retarded dipole approximation, and the hadronic range (ν ≥ ν_π) treated via dispersion relations using photoabsorption data scaled by atomic mass number A. For the nuclear part, the correction is expressed in terms of the electric dipole polarizability α_E1 and mean excitation energy ν̄, with the full β-dependence of the nuclear form factor retained rather than expanded. Coulomb corrections beyond the leading-logarithm approximation are included by computing the radial Coulomb integral K^(1) using a series expansion in the small parameter p = √ε₂, and finite nuclear size effects are accounted for via a reduction factor F_R that quantifies the variation of the atomic wave function over the nucleus volume.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon polarization contribution to μD 2S level: −28(2) μeV (used as normalization for scaling to other nuclei)
- Nuclear dipole polarizability scaling formula: α_E1 = 0.0518 MeV·fm³ · A² / [S_v(A^(1/3) − κ)] with S_v = 27.3(8) MeV and κ = 1.69(6)
- Total nuclear polarization correction for μ¹²C 1S level: 1.9^(+0.4)_(−0.2) eV (compared to 2.5(7) eV in Fricke (2004))
- Total nuclear polarization correction for μ¹⁶O 1S level: 7.0^(+1.4)_(−0.7) eV (compared to 5.0(1.5) eV in Fricke (2004))
- Total nuclear polarization correction for μ³⁵Cl 1S level: 93^(+18)_(−9) eV (compared to no prior entry)
- Total nuclear polarization correction for μ³⁹K 1S level: 136^(+28)_(−14) eV (compared to 119(36) eV in Fricke (2004))
- Nuclear dipole polarizability for ⁴⁰Ca: 1.75(18) fm³ (this work) vs. 1.92(17) fm³ from Fearick (2023)
- Nuclear dipole polarizability for ⁴⁸Ca: 2.25(23) fm³ (this work) vs. 2.07(22) fm³ from Birkhan (2016)
- Nuclear dipole polarizability for ⁵⁸Ni: 2.93(29) fm³ (this work) vs. 3.48(31) fm³ from Brandherm (2024)

## Context

This work extends the nuclear polarization calculations of Rinker and Speth (1978), which have been the state-of-the-art for extracting nuclear radii from muonic atom measurements of light and medium-light nuclei, and updates the review by Borie and Rinker (1982). It addresses discrepancies of 30-40% between different literature entries for Fe, Co, Ni, Cu, Zn isotopes found in Fricke (2004) and Shera (1976). The recipe enables practitioners to compute nuclear polarization corrections for upcoming experiments by the QUARTET collaboration (Ohayon 2023), RefRad potassium measurements (Adamczak 2022), and other planned muonic atom spectroscopy programs, with the explicit inclusion of nucleon polarization that was previously missing for atoms heavier than helium.
