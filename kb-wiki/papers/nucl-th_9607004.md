# Polarization Phenomena in Small-Angle Photoproduction of e+e- Pairs and the GDH Sum Rule

> We discuss a possibility to measure the spin-dependent part of the forward Compton scattering amplitude through interference effects of the Bethe-Heitler and virtual Compton scattering mechanisms in photoproduction of e+e- pairs at small angles.

## Key claim
The spin-dependent part of the forward Compton scattering amplitude, specifically the function \(f_2\) related to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule, can be accessed experimentally through the interference of Bethe-Heitler and virtual Compton scattering amplitudes in photoproduction of \(e^+e^-\) pairs at small angles, rather than only through direct measurement of total photoabsorption cross sections \(\sigma_{1/2}\) and \(\sigma_{3/2}\) over a wide energy range.

## Method
The authors derive approximate analytic expressions for the Bethe-Heitler and virtual Compton scattering amplitudes in the reaction \(\gamma N \to e^+ e^- N'\), keeping leading terms in an expansion in transverse momenta and neglecting the electron mass. They then compute the differential cross section and the \(e^+\)-\(e^-\) azimuthal asymmetry arising from the interference of the two amplitudes, showing how this asymmetry depends on the real and imaginary parts of the forward Compton amplitudes \(f_1\) and \(f_2\). The analysis is restricted to the kinematic region \(\omega \sim 1\) GeV, \(W \sim 10\) MeV, \(Q \sim 100\) MeV, and \(x_1 \sim x_2 \sim 0.5\), corresponding to small lepton angles.

## Key numbers
- The Bethe-Heitler cross section in the chosen kinematics equals 2.4 nb.
- The asymmetry proportional to the target polarization and \(\Im f_2\) (the GDH cross section \(\Delta\sigma\)) equals 14% for \(\Delta\sigma = 100\ \mu\)b in the kinematics of \(\omega \sim 1\) GeV, \(W \sim 10\) MeV, \(Q \sim 100\) MeV, independently of photon energy \(\omega\).
- The momentum transfer scale is \(Q^2 \sim 0.01\ \text{GeV}^2\), which is small compared to the typical hadronic scale \(m_\rho^2 \simeq 0.6\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The longitudinal photon contribution to the VCS amplitude is estimated to be of order \(q_\perp W/\omega^2 \sim 10^{-3}\) relative to the transverse contribution.

## Context
This work extends the method previously used by Alvensleben et al. (1973) to determine \(\Re f_1\) at 2.2 GeV via \(e^+e^-\) pair photoproduction, now proposing to extract both \(\Re f_2\) and \(\Im f_2\) using polarized beams and targets. The paper addresses the puzzling violation of the isovector (\(I=1\)) GDH sum rule, which appears to be violated despite known isovector Regge exchanges (such as the \(a_2\)-meson) having intercepts \(\alpha_R(0) \lesssim 0.5\) that should not spoil the sum rule convergence; the proposed measurement could help resolve this discrepancy by providing direct experimental access to the forward Compton amplitude \(f_2\).
