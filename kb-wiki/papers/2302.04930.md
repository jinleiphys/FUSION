# Model-independent Odderon results based on TOTEM data on elastic proton-proton scattering at 8 TeV

> We complete the model-independent analysis of the scaling properties of the differential cross section of elastic proton-proton cross sections, including new TOTEM data published in 2022 at $\sqrt{s} = 8$ TeV. We separate the signal and the background region with a new gating method. In the signal region, we find that the statistical significance of Odderon exchange from the combined 7.0 and 8.0 TeV $pp$ data of TOTEM and the 1.96 TeV $p\bar{p}$ data of D0 is at least 7.32$σ$. In the background region, the scaling functions of elastic proton-proton data at 7 and 8 TeV and that of elastic proton-antiproton scattering data at 1.96 TeV agree with a statistical significance not larger than 1.93$σ$.

## Key claim
The model-independent analysis of elastic proton-proton scattering data at 8 TeV, combined with 7 TeV pp data and 1.96 TeV proton-antiproton data, yields a statistical significance for Odderon exchange of at least 7.32 sigma in the signal region, while the scaling functions agree within 1.93 sigma in the background region.

## Method
The authors apply the H(x) scaling method, which compares the differential cross-section of elastic pp and pbar-p collisions after scaling by the nuclear slope parameter B. They use a new "closing gates" technique to separate signal and background regions by iteratively removing the leftmost or rightmost D0 datapoint that minimizes the increase in signal significance. Pairwise comparisons of H(x) scaling functions are performed between pp data at 2.76, 7.0, and 8.0 TeV from TOTEM and pbar-p data at 1.96 TeV from D0.

## Key numbers
- Combined Odderon signal significance from 7.0 and 8.0 TeV pp data versus 1.96 TeV pbar-p data: at least 7.32 sigma (with gate position n=3, m=2)
- Background region agreement between pp data at 7 and 8 TeV and pbar-p data at 1.96 TeV: 1.93 sigma or less
- Pairwise comparison of pp H(x) scaling functions at 8.0 TeV vs 7.0 TeV: confidence level at least 98%
- Pairwise comparison of pp H(x) scaling functions at 8.0 TeV vs 2.76 TeV: confidence level approximately 100%
- Odderon signal from 8.0 TeV pp vs 1.96 TeV pbar-p alone: at least 3.74 sigma
- Odderon signal from 7.0 TeV pp vs 1.96 TeV pbar-p alone: at least 6.26 sigma
- With gate position (n=6, m=1), 8.0 TeV vs 1.96 TeV signal increases to at least 4.55 sigma, background agrees at 0.13 sigma

## Context
This work extends the model-independent H(x) scaling analysis of Csörgő et al. (2021), which first reported a 6.26 sigma Odderon signal using 7 TeV TOTEM data and 1.96 TeV D0 data. It incorporates the newly published 8 TeV TOTEM data from 2022 (TOTEM, 2021) and introduces the closing gates method to separate signal and background regions. The results are consistent with model-dependent analyses using the Real Extended Bialas-Bzdak model (Csörgő et al., 2020; Szanyi et al., 2022) and the joint D0-TOTEM analysis (2021), which found at least 5.2 sigma significance. This work enables a fully model-independent confirmation of Odderon exchange at the TeV energy scale.
