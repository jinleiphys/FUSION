# Isospin asymmetry in the continuum of the A=14 mirror nuclei

> We study the isospin asymmetry in the isoscalar (IS) excitations in the mirror nuclei $^{14}$O and $^{14}$C by using the Hartree-Fock(HF)+random phase approximation (RPA) linear response function theory with a Skyrme interaction to take into account the continuum effect properly. The asymmetry in the IS monopole, dipole responses is pointed out in the continuum near the particle threshold with respect to the excitation energy and the sum rule strength. On the other hand, no clear sign of the asymmetry is found in the giant resonance (GR) region. In the quadrupole case, the calculated strengths of the mirror nuclei show almost the same energy dependence from the threshold to the GR region. It is found that the transition densities of the monopole response show an extended halo structure near the threshold, while those of GR region show a typical radial dependence of the compressional collective mode without any halo effect. Contrary to the transition densities of the monopole response, those of quadrupole response do not show any sign of the extended feature of wave functions neither near the threshold nor the GR energy region. Calculated strength distributions of the IS multipole states are compared with recent experimental data obtained by the multipole decomposition analysis of $α$ inelastic scattering on $^{14}$O.

## Key claim

Isospin asymmetry in isoscalar monopole and dipole responses is present in the continuum near the particle threshold of the A=14 mirror nuclei, but no clear sign of asymmetry is found in the giant resonance region; for the quadrupole case, the calculated strengths show almost the same energy dependence from threshold to the GR region.

## Method

The author used Hartree-Fock (HF) plus random phase approximation (RPA) linear response function theory with the Skyrme interaction SkM* to calculate isoscalar strength distributions for monopole, dipole, and quadrupole excitations in the mirror nuclei 14O and 14C. The spin-orbit strength of the Skyrme interaction was adjusted to reproduce the empirical neutron and proton separation energies (W_o = 150 MeV·fm^5 for 14C and W_o = 155 MeV·fm^5 for 14O, versus the original 130 MeV·fm^5). Transition densities and energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) fractions were computed and compared with experimental data from multipole decomposition analysis of α inelastic scattering on 14O.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energy in 14C: S_n = 8.11 MeV (adjusted calculation) versus empirical S_n(exp) = 8.18 MeV
- Proton separation energy in 14O: S_p = 4.66 MeV (adjusted calculation) versus empirical S_p(exp) = 4.63 MeV
- Monopole strength below 14 MeV exhausts 10.1% of EWSR in 14O and 8.1% of EWSR in 14C
- Integrated monopole strength up to Ex ≤ 25 MeV: 49.6% of EWSR in 14O and 48.5% of EWSR in 14C
- Lowest 1^- state observed at Ex = 5.17 MeV in 14O and Ex = 6.09 MeV in 14C; calculated energy difference ΔE = 1 MeV versus experimental ΔE(exp) = 0.92 MeV
- Threshold 1^- peak in 14O has 2.5% of EWSR; discrete lowest 1^- state in 14C has 1.1% of EWSR
- Integrated dipole strength up to Ex = 12 MeV: 17.3% of EWSR in 14O versus 10.7% of EWSR in 14C
- Dipole strength in GR region (12 MeV < Ex < 25 MeV): 23% of EWSR in both 14O and 14C
- Lowest 2^+ state observed at Ex = 6.59 MeV in 14O and Ex = 7.01 MeV in 14C; energy difference ΔE = 0.42 MeV
- IS quadrupole EWSR below Ex = 25 MeV: 85.3% in 14C and 85.5% in 14O
- Calculated monopole peak in 14O at Ex ∼ 8 MeV exhausts 10% of EWSR; experimental peak at Ex ∼ 7 MeV exhausts about 8% of EWSR up to 13.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends studies of isospin asymmetry in light nuclei, particularly the Thomas-Ehrman effect observed in A=13 and A=17 mirror nuclei (Ehrman 1951, Thomas 1951), to the continuum region of A=14 mirror nuclei. The large difference in threshold energies (S_n = 8.18 MeV in 14C versus S_p = 4.63 MeV in 14O) provides a unique test case for isospin symmetry breaking in unbound states. The calculated results for 14O are compared with recent experimental data from multipole decomposition analysis of α inelastic scattering (Baba, unpublished at time of writing). The work enables future experimental studies of 14C in the continuum region to confirm the predicted isospin asymmetry, and suggests that particle-vibration coupling effects (Colo, earlier work) may need to be incorporated for more sophisticated calculations of single-particle energies near threshold.
