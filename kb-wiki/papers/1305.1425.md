# Relativistic Langevin Dynamics in Expanding Media

> We study the consequences of different realizations of diffusion processes in relativistic Langevin simulations. We elaborate on the Ito-Stratonovich dilemma by showing how microscopically calculated transport coefficients as obtained from a Boltzmann/Fokker-Planck equation can be implemented to lead to an unambiguous realization of the Langevin process. Pertinent examples within the pre-point (Ito) and post-point (Hänggi-Klimontovich) Langevin prescriptions are worked out explicitly. Deviations from this implementation are shown to generate variants of the Boltzmann distribution as the stationary (equilibrium) solutions. Finally, we explicitly verify how the Lorentz invariance of the Langevin process is maintained in the presence of an expanding medium, including the case of an "elliptic flow" transmitted to a Brownian test particle. This is particularly relevant for using heavy-flavor diffusion as a quantitative tool to diagnose transport properties of QCD matter as created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the "Ito-Stratonovich dilemma" in relativistic Langevin simulations can be resolved by starting from microscopically calculated transport coefficients derived from a Boltzmann/Fokker-Planck equation, provided the proper equilibrium condition (generalized fluctuation-dissipation relation) is exactly fulfilled; both pre-point (Ito) and post-point (Hänggi-Klimontovich) schemes then yield the same Lorentz-invariant Boltzmann-Jüttner distribution as the stationary limit, even in the presence of expanding media with elliptic flow.

## Method

The authors start from a Fokker-Planck equation with drag and diffusion coefficients obtained from microscopic scattering matrix elements via momentum-transfer moments. They derive the general equilibrium condition (dissipation-fluctuation relation) that ensures the asymptotic solution converges to the Boltzmann-Jüttner distribution. They then work out explicit Langevin updating rules for the pre-point (ξ=0) and post-point (ξ=1) schemes, showing how the friction coefficient Γ(p) must be expressed in terms of the drag coefficient A(p) and diffusion coefficient D(p) differently for each scheme, such that the resulting Fokker-Planck equations are identical. Numerical simulations are performed for heavy quarks (m=1.5 GeV) diffusing in a QGP at T=0.18 GeV, first in a static medium, then in a constant 1-D flow (v_z=0.9), and finally in an elliptically expanding fireball with initial temperature T_i=0.33 GeV and final temperature T_f=0.18 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Heavy quark mass: m = 1.5 GeV
- Medium temperature: T = 0.18 GeV (static and 1-D flow cases)
- Flow velocity for 1-D flow: v_z = 0.9
- Fireball initial temperature: T_i = 0.33 GeV
- Fireball final temperature: T_f = 0.18 GeV
- Constant friction coefficient example: Γ = 20 fm⁻¹ (with D = Γ E T)
- Alternative friction coefficient example: Γ = 300(1+T/E)/E² GeV²/fm (with D = 300 T/E GeV²/fm)
- Simulation lifetime for static medium: 10 fm

## Context

This work extends prior studies of relativistic Brownian motion and the Ito-Stratonovich dilemma by Dunkel and Hänggi (2005, 2009) and addresses the debate on asymptotic phase-space distributions of relativistic Brownian particles (Cubero and Hänggi, 2007). It provides a practical resolution for heavy-quark diffusion simulations in quark-gluon plasmas created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on the Fokker-Planck approach of Moore and Teaney (2005) and the fireball model of van Hees (2005). The results enable quantitative use of heavy-flavor diffusion as a diagnostic tool for transport properties of QCD matter, by establishing that both pre-point and post-point Langevin schemes yield the correct Lorentz-invariant equilibrium distribution when the proper equilibrium condition is imposed, removing ambiguities in previous implementations.
