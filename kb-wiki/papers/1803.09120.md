# The nuclear symmetry energy and the breaking of the isospin symmetry: how do they reconcile with each other?

> We analyze and propose a solution to the apparent inconsistency between our current knowledge of the Equation of State of asymmetric nuclear matter, the energy of the Isobaric Analog State (IAS) in a heavy nucleus such as 208Pb, and the isospin symmetry breaking forces in the nuclear medium. This is achieved by performing state-of-the-art Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation calculations of the IAS that include all isospin symmetry breaking contributions. To this aim, we propose a new effective interaction that is successful in reproducing the IAS excitation energy without compromising other properties of finite nuclei.

# The nuclear symmetry energy and the breaking of the isospin symmetry: how do they reconcile with each other?

## Key claim
The apparent inconsistency between the nuclear symmetry energy, the Isobaric Analog State (IAS) energy in 208Pb, and isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) forces can be resolved by introducing a new effective interaction (SAMi-ISB) that includes all ISB contributions, provided one simultaneously fits both the experimental IAS energy in 208Pb and the ISB contributions to symmetric nuclear matter from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations based on the AV18 potential.

## Method
The authors performed Hartree-Fock plus charge-exchange Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations using a Skyrme-type effective interaction, with ground state properties computed in coordinate space using box boundary conditions (20 fm box radius) and a 60 MeV single-particle energy cutoff. The new SAMi-ISB interaction was fitted via a two-step χ² minimization: first, ISB parameters were determined to reproduce both the IAS energy in 208Pb and the ISB contributions to symmetric nuclear matter from BHF calculations; second, the remaining parameters were fine-tuned to maintain accuracy for nuclear masses and charge radii comparable to the original SAMi interaction.

## Key numbers
- IAS energy in 208Pb: predicted 18.80(5) MeV with SAMi-ISB, compared to experimental 18.826(10) MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation J = 30.8(4) MeV
- Symmetry energy slope parameter L = 50(5) MeV
- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.1613(6) fm⁻³
- Saturation energy e₀ = -16.03(2) MeV
- Nuclear matter incompressibility K₀ = 235(4) MeV
- IAS energy in ⁴⁸Ca: predicted 6.79(2) MeV (SAMi-ISB) vs. experimental 7.182(8) MeV
- IAS energy in ⁹⁰Zr: predicted 11.36(4) MeV (SAMi-ISB) vs. experimental 11.901(12) MeV
- For Sn isotopes, SAMi-ISB reduces the IAS energy discrepancy from ~600 keV (SAMi) to ~50-200 keV
- Numerical accuracy of the HF+RPA code for IAS energy is within 10 keV for the test case of ⁹⁰Zr

## Context
This work extends the SAMi interaction family (Roca-Maza et al., 2012) by adding ISB terms, reconciling mean-field approaches with benchmark BHF calculations based on the AV18 potential (Muther, 1999) and experimental IAS data (Antony, 1997). The new SAMi-ISB interaction enables improved predictions of IAS energies across nuclei from ⁴⁸Ca to ²⁰⁸Pb while preserving the accuracy of the original SAMi interaction for ground-state properties, and provides a framework for studying the relationship between the symmetry energy and isospin-breaking effects in finite nuclei.
