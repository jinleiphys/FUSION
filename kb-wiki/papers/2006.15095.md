# Fast-timing study of $^{81}$Ga from the $β$ decay of $^{81}$Zn

> The $β^{-}$ decay of $^{81}$Zn to the neutron magic $N=50$ nucleus $^{81}$Ga, with only three valence protons with respect to $^{78}$Ni, was investigated. The study was performed at the ISOLDE facility at CERN by means of $γ$ spectroscopy. The $^{81}$Zn half-life was determined to be $T_{1/2}=290(4)$ ms while the $β$-delayed neutron emission probability was measured as $P_n=23(4)\%$. The analysis of the $β$-gated $γ$-ray singles and $γ$-$γ$ coincidences from the decay of $^{81}$Zn provides 47 new levels and 70 new transitions in $^{81}$Ga. The $β^-$$n$ decay of $^{81}$Zn was observed and a new decay scheme into the odd-odd $^{80}$Ga nucleus was established. The half-lives of the first and second excited states of $^{81}$Ga were measured via the fast-timing method using LaBr$_3$(Ce) detectors. The level scheme and transition rates are compared to large-scale shell-model calculations. The low-lying structure of $^{81}$Ga is interpreted in terms of the coupling of the three valence protons outside the doubly-magic $^{78}$Ni core.

## Key claim

The low-lying structure of $^{81}$Ga is interpreted in terms of the coupling of three valence protons outside the doubly-magic $^{78}$Ni core, with the measured half-lives and transition rates providing more stringent tests of large-scale shell-model calculations than level energies alone, though the interpretation depends on the specific theoretical framework used for comparison.

## Method

The $\beta^{-}$ decay of $^{81}$Zn was studied at the ISOLDE facility at CERN using $\gamma$-ray spectroscopy with HPGe detectors for energy measurements and LaBr$_3$(Ce) detectors for fast-timing lifetime measurements via the Advanced Time Delayed $\beta\gamma\gamma$(t) method. $^{81}$Zn ions were produced by fission reactions induced by fast neutrons from 1.4-GeV protons on a UC$_2$/graphite target, selectively ionized by the RILIS laser ion source, mass-separated, and collected on an aluminum stopper foil. Coincidences between a plastic scintillator for $\beta$ detection, HPGe detectors, and LaBr$_3$(Ce) detectors were used to construct the level scheme and extract excited-state lifetimes down to the tens of picoseconds range.

## Key numbers

* $^{81}$Zn half-life: $T_{1/2} = 290(4)$ ms, measured from time spectra gated on the 351-, 452-, and 1341-keV transitions in $^{81}$Ga.
* $\beta$-delayed neutron emission probability: $P_n = 23(4)\%$, measured from the decay of $^{81}$Zn.
* $^{81}$Ga half-life: $T_{1/2} = 1.25(3)$ s, obtained from the 216- and 828-keV transitions in $^{81}$Ge.
* Apparent $^{80}$Ga half-life (combined ground state and 22-keV isomer): $T_{1/2} = 1.70(3)$ s, from gating on the 659-keV transition in $^{80}$Ge.
* 47 new levels and 70 new transitions in $^{81}$Ga were identified from $\beta$-gated $\gamma$-ray singles and $\gamma$-$\gamma$ coincidences.
* The strongest transitions in $^{81}$Ga are at 351.1 keV (relative intensity 100) and 451.6 keV (relative intensity 20.1).

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of $^{81}$Zn decay by Kratz (1991), Verney (2007), and Padgett (2010), providing a significantly expanded level scheme and the first lifetime measurements of excited states in $^{81}$Ga via fast-timing. The results enable comparison with large-scale shell-model calculations for nuclei near the doubly-magic $^{78}$Ni core, complementing recent spectroscopic studies of $^{79}$Cu by Olivier (2017) and $^{80}$Zn by Shiga (2016), and providing input for understanding the evolution of proton single-particle configurations and the strength of the $N=50$ shell closure as proton number increases.
