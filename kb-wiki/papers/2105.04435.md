# The $^{6}$H states studied in the $^2\text{He}(^8\text{He},^4\text{He})$ reaction and evidence of extremely correlated character of the $^{5}$H ground state

> The extremely neutron-rich system $^{6}$H was studied in the direct $^2\text{H}(^8\text{He},{^4\text{He}})^{6}$H transfer reaction with a $26 A$ MeV secondary $^{8}$He beam. The measured missing mass spectrum shows a broad bump at $\sim 4-8$ MeV above the $^3$H+$3n$ decay threshold. This bump can be interpreted as a broad resonant state in $^{6}$H at $6.8(5)$ MeV. The population cross section of such a presumably $p$-wave state (or may be few overlapping states) in the energy range from 4 to 8 MeV is $dσ/dΩ_{\text{c.m.}} \simeq 190^{+40}_{-80}$ $μ$b/sr in the angular range $5^{\circ}<θ_{\text{c.m.}}<16^{\circ}$. The obtained missing mass spectrum is practically free of the $^{6}$H events below 3.5 MeV ($dσ/dΩ_{\text{c.m.}} \lesssim 5$ $μ$b/sr in the same angular range). The steep rise of the $^{6}$H missing mass spectrum at $\sim 3$ MeV allows to derive the lower limit for the possible resonant-state energy in $^{6}$H to be $4.5(3)$ MeV. According to the paring energy estimates, such a $4.5(3)$ MeV resonance is a realistic candidate for the $^{6}$H ground state (g.s.). The obtained results confirm that the decay mechanism of the $^{7}$H g.s.\ (located at 2.2 MeV above the $^{3}$H+$4n$ threshold) is the "true" (or simultaneous) $4n$ emission. The resonance energy profiles and the momentum distributions of fragments of the sequential $^{6}$H$ \,\rightarrow \, ^5$H(g.s.)+$n\, \rightarrow \, ^3$H+$3n$ decay were analyzed by the theoretically-updated direct four-body-decay and sequential-emission mechanisms. The measured momentum distributions of the $^{3}$H fragments in the $^{6}$H rest frame indicate very strong "dineutron-type" correlations in the $^{5}$H ground state decay.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the ground state of $^{6}$H is not at the previously reported 2.6-2.9 MeV above the $^{3}$H+$3n$ threshold, but rather at a significantly higher energy of 4.5(3) MeV, with a broad resonant structure observed at 6.8(5) MeV; the authors hedge that the 6.8 MeV bump "can be interpreted as a broad resonant state" and that the 4.5 MeV candidate is "a realistic candidate for the $^{6}$H ground state" based on pairing energy estimates, not a definitive assignment.

## Method

The $^{6}$H system was produced via the direct $^{2}$H($^{8}$He,$^{4}$He)$^{6}$H transfer reaction using a 26 A MeV secondary $^{8}$He beam incident on a cryogenic deuterium gas target at the ACCULINNA-2 separator. The missing mass spectrum was reconstructed from $^{4}$He-$^{3}$H coincidence events detected by silicon telescope arrays, with additional triple $^{4}$He-$^{3}$H-$n$ coincidences from a neutron wall providing background-free confirmation. Monte Carlo simulations of the setup were performed to determine the missing mass resolution (0.8-1.7 MeV depending on kinematics) and detection efficiency, and the data were analyzed using both direct and advanced empty-target background subtraction procedures.

## Key numbers

- The $^{6}$H missing mass spectrum shows a broad bump at approximately 4-8 MeV above the $^{3}$H+$3n$ decay threshold, interpreted as a resonant state at 6.8(5) MeV.
- The population cross section for the 4-8 MeV energy range is $d\sigma/d\Omega_{\text{c.m.}} \simeq 190^{+40}_{-80}$ $\mu$b/sr in the angular range $5^{\circ}<\theta_{\text{c.m.}}<16^{\circ}$.
- The missing mass spectrum is practically free of $^{6}$H events below 3.5 MeV, with $d\sigma/d\Omega_{\text{c.m.}} \lesssim 5$ $\mu$b/sr in the same angular range.
- The lower limit for the possible resonant-state energy in $^{6}$H is derived to be 4.5(3) MeV from the steep rise of the spectrum at approximately 3 MeV.
- The $^{6}$H missing mass resolution of the experiment was approximately 0.8-1.7 MeV depending on the kinematic range, as determined by Monte Carlo simulations.
- The maximum detection efficiencies for double $^{4}$He-$^{3}$H and triple $^{4}$He-$^{3}$H-$n$ coincidences were approximately 65% and 4%, respectively, at $E_T \sim 5-7$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of extremely neutron-rich hydrogen isotopes beyond the neutron dripline, following recent reliable spectroscopic information on $^{7}$H from the same experimental campaign (Bezbakh 2020, Muzalevskii 2021). The results contradict earlier claims of a $^{6}$H ground state at 2.6-2.9 MeV from Aleksandrov (1984), Belozyorov (1986), and Caamano (2008), and instead agree with the higher-energy states reported by Gurov (2007) and the null results for low-lying states from Parker (1990) and Seth (1991). The measured momentum distributions of $^{3}$H fragments in the $^{6}$H rest frame indicate very strong "dineutron-type" correlations in the $^{5}$H ground state decay, highlighting the potential of sequential $^{6}$H$\rightarrow ^{5}$H(g.s.)+$n\rightarrow ^{3}$H+$3n$ decay as a source of information about the intermediate $^{5}$H system.
