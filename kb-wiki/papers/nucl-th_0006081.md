# On a q-analogue of the spin-orbit coupling

> Based on the tensor method, a q-analoque of the spin-orbit coupling is introduced in a q-deformed Schroedinger equation, previously derived for a central potential. Analytic expressions for the matrix elemnets of the representation j=l\pm 1/2 are derived. The spectra of the harmonic oscillator and the Coulomb potential are calculated numerically as a function of the deformation parameter, without and with the spin-orbit coupling. The harmonic oscillator spectrum presents strong analogies with the bound spectrum of an Woods-Saxon potential customarily used in nuclear physics. The Coulomb spectrum simulates relativistic effects. The addition of the spin-orbit coupling reinforces this picture.

## Key claim

The authors construct a q-deformed spin-orbit coupling operator that is a q-scalar in the coproduct algebra of the total angular momentum, using a hermitian form of the universal R matrix; this operator is entirely different from previous q-deformed spin-orbit couplings such as that of Raychev (1998), and its matrix elements for j = ℓ ± 1/2 are derived analytically.

## Method

The authors start from a previously derived q-deformed Schrödinger equation for a central potential (Micu 1999), where the angular momentum operator Λ_μ is a q-vector in the su_q(2) algebra. They introduce a spin operator σ_μ as a q-vector in the spin algebra, then construct a hermitian spin operator Σ_μ(R_u) in the coproduct algebra of the total angular momentum J_μ using a unitary version of the universal R matrix. The spin-orbit interaction is defined as a q-scalar product between Σ_μ(R_u) and Λ_μ, and its expectation values for states with j = ℓ ± 1/2 are calculated analytically using the state of maximum weight.

## Key numbers

- For the q-harmonic oscillator with q = e^(is) and 0 < s < 0.13, the q-deformed spectrum satisfies inequalities such as E_{1d} < E_{2s}, E_{1f} < E_{2p}, E_{1g} < E_{2d}, which correspond to a potential between a harmonic oscillator and a square well.
- The expectation value of the spin-orbit operator for j = ℓ + 1/2 is E_{ℓ+1/2} = [2ℓ]/[2]^2 × (q^{ℓ+5/2} + q^{-ℓ-5/2})/(q^{ℓ+1/2} + q^{-ℓ-1/2}).
- The expectation value for j = ℓ - 1/2 is E_{ℓ-1/2} = -[2ℓ+2]/[2]^2 × (q^{ℓ-3/2} + q^{-ℓ+3/2})/(q^{ℓ+1/2} + q^{-ℓ-1/2}).
- In the limit q → 1, these recover the non-deformed values ℓ/2 for j = ℓ + 1/2 and -(ℓ+1)/2 for j = ℓ - 1/2.
- For the q-Coulomb potential with α = 0.001, the level sequence 2p_{3/2} > 2p_{1/2} > 2s_{1/2} holds when s > 0.11, and 3d_{5/2} > 3d_{3/2} > 3p_{3/2} > 3p_{1/2} > 3s_{1/2} holds when s > 0.17.
- For the q-harmonic oscillator with spin-orbit coupling and α = -0.1, the level sequence is similar to neutron single-particle spectra from a Woods-Saxon potential.

## Context

This work extends the q-deformed Schrödinger equation approach of Micu (1999) by adding a spin-orbit coupling term, and contrasts with the boson realization method of Raychev (1996, 1998) which yields different eigenvalues. The q-harmonic oscillator spectrum without spin-orbit coupling reproduces the bound spectrum inequalities of a Woods-Saxon potential as described by Bohr and Mottelson (1969), while the q-Coulomb spectrum simulates relativistic effects seen in the Klein-Gordon equation (Graf 1991, 1994) and the Herbst equation (Lucha 1997). With spin-orbit coupling, the q-Coulomb spectrum mimics the Dirac equation level ordering for alkaline atoms, and the q-harmonic oscillator spectrum with negative α reproduces neutron single-particle spectra from Woods-Saxon potentials or Hartree-Fock calculations (Brink 1970). This enables a phenomenological modeling of nuclear single-particle spectra using a single deformation parameter s, though the authors do not attempt a quantitative fit to any particular system.
