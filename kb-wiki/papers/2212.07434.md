# Rigorous bounds on transport from causality

> We use causality to derive a number of simple and universal constraints on dispersion relations, which describe the location of singularities of retarded two-point functions in relativistic quantum field theories. We prove that all causal dissipative dispersion relations have a finite radius of convergence. We then give two-sided bounds on all transport coefficients in units of this radius, including an upper bound on diffusivity.

## Key claim

Causality alone, expressed as the condition that the imaginary part of the frequency cannot exceed the imaginary part of the wavenumber (Im ω(k) ≤ |Im k|), forces all causal dissipative dispersion relations to have a finite radius of convergence and yields two-sided bounds on all transport coefficients in units of that radius, including an upper bound on diffusivity. The abstract's claim of "universal constraints" is hedged by the requirement that the retarded Green's function be a tempered distribution and that the system be in a (linearly) stable phase.

## Method

The authors start from the necessary causality condition Im ω(k) ≤ |Im k| for dispersion relations describing singularities of retarded two-point functions in relativistic quantum field theories. They prove a general theorem bounding the Taylor coefficients a_n of ω(k) around k=0 using a real-part theorem adapted from Holland, which involves integrals of the imaginary part of ω over angular variables. They then specialize these bounds to sound modes (ω = v k - i (Γ_s/2) k² + ...) and diffusive modes (ω = -i D k² + ...), and also prove that poles and essential singularities are forbidden by the causality condition.

## Key numbers

- For diffusive modes: 0 ≤ D ≤ (16/(3π)) × (1/R), where R is the radius of convergence of the hydrodynamic series.
- For sound modes: |v| ≤ 1 and 0 ≤ Γ_s/2 ≤ (16/(3π)) × (1/R), plus a relational bound Γ_s/2 ≤ 4√(2/3) × (8/(√6 π) - |v|) × (1/R).
- The largest value of DR found in the literature is DR ≈ 0.78 for the shear mode in the dual of Reissner-Nordström-AdS₅ (Jansen 2020).
- A mathematical test function achieves DR ≈ 1.22.
- For the telegrapher's equation (MIS theory), the maximum diffusion constant satisfies DR = 1/2.

## Context

This work extends the study of analytic structure of dispersion relations initiated in Withers (2018) using holography, and generalizes case-by-case observations of finite radius of convergence for hydrodynamic modes in relativistic theories (Grozdanov 2019, Jansen 2020, Baggioli 2021, and many others). The results prove that nonhydrodynamic modes are unavoidable in any causal formulation of relativistic hydrodynamics, explaining the appearance of such modes in models like MIS theory (Israel 1976, 1979), BRSSS (Baier 2007), BDNK (Bemfica 2017, Kovtun 2019), and RTA kinetic theory (Anderson 1974, Romatschke 2015). The bounds are reminiscent of causality constraints on Wilson coefficients in effective field theories (Caron-Huot 2022, Carrillo-Gonzalez 2022).
