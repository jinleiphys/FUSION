# Raman Photoassociation of Bose-Fermi Mixtures and the Subsequent Prospects for Atom-Molecule Cooper Pairing

> We theoretically investigate Raman photoassociation of a degenerate Bose-Fermi mixture of atoms and the subsequent prospect for anomalous (Cooper) pairing between atoms and molecules. Stable fermionic molecules are created via free-bound-bound stimulated Raman adiabatic passage which, in contrast to purely bosonic systems, can occur in spite of collisions. With the leftover atomic condensate to enhance intrafermion interactions, the superfluid transition to atom-molecule Cooper pairs occurs at a temperature that is roughly an order of magnitude below what is currently feasible.

## Key claim

Raman photoassociation via fractional STIRAP can create an arbitrary number of stable fermionic molecules from a Bose-Fermi mixture, but the subsequent superfluid transition to atom-molecule Cooper pairs occurs at a temperature roughly an order of magnitude below what is currently feasible, even with the condensate-enhanced intrafermion attraction.

## Method

The authors model a degenerate Bose-Fermi mixture of atoms (bosonic 87Rb and fermionic 40K) coupled by heteronuclear photoassociation to electronically-excited fermionic molecules, which are then coupled by a second laser to stable fermionic molecules. They solve the Schrodinger equation numerically using Fock states for the fractional STIRAP process, then calculate the effective Fermi-Fermi scattering length including BEC phonon-mediated interactions and the resulting BCS critical temperature for atom-molecule Cooper pairing.

## Key numbers

- Boson density: rho_B = 5 x 10^14 cm^-3, consistent with N_B = 1.3 x 10^6 Bose-condensed 87Rb atoms in a trap with radial frequency omega_r = 100 x 2pi Hz and axial frequency omega_a = 10 x 2pi Hz
- Peak atom-molecule coupling: Omega_+ ~ Omega_0 = 2pi MHz
- Spontaneous decay rate: Gamma = 10 x 2pi MHz
- Fermi atom-molecule scattering length magnitude: |a_+-| = a_00 = 5.29 nm
- Fermion density: rho_F = 1.1 x 10^12 cm^-3, consistent with N_F = 5 x 10^3 40K atoms
- Fermi-Fermi interaction strength: Lambda_+- = 5.81 x 2pi Hz
- For weak interactions (k_F|a_+-| = 0.05), the critical temperature is unreasonably low; for marginally weak interactions (k_F|a_+-| = 0.2), T ~ 0.05 T_F, still out of reach of current technology

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Heiselberg (2000) on BEC-mediated fermion interactions to the case of atom-molecule Cooper pairing, where the boson-fermion fluctuation contribution can be negative. It contrasts with purely bosonic STIRAP systems (Hopfmann, 2001) where collisions prevent efficient conversion. The results suggest that next-generation technology must shed another order of magnitude in temperature from the first generation (DeMarco, 1999), or that a system with an attractive and strong-but-not-too-strong interaction must be found, making alternative approaches such as photoassociation or Feshbach resonances worth investigating.
