# Nuclear matter in the crust of neutron stars

> The properties of inhomogeneous nuclear matter are investigated considering the self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock approach with inclusion of pairing correlations. For a comparison we also consider a relativistic mean field approach. The inhomogeneous infinite matter is described in terms of cubic Wigner-Seitz cells, which leads to a smooth transition to the limit of homogeneous nuclear matter. The possible existence of various structures in the so-called pasta phase is investigated within this self-consistent approach and a comparison is made to results obtained within the Thomas-Fermi approximation. Results for the proton abundances and the pairing properties are discussed for densities for which clustering phenomena are obtained.

## Key claim

The paper investigates inhomogeneous nuclear matter in neutron star crusts using self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock and relativistic mean field approaches with cubic Wigner-Seitz cells, which allows for a smooth transition to homogeneous matter and the exploration of non-spherical pasta-phase structures that spherical cell calculations cannot capture.

## Method

The authors solved the Skyrme Hartree-Fock equations and the relativistic mean field Dirac equation on a discretized three-dimensional Cartesian mesh within cubic Wigner-Seitz cells, using the imaginary time step method for iterative convergence with periodic boundary conditions. Pairing correlations were included via the BCS formalism, and the calculations were performed for beta-stable matter at densities where clustering phenomena occur, with comparisons made to Thomas-Fermi approximations.

## Key numbers

- Mesh spacing of 1.0 fm was used, with the exponential operator truncated at 9th order and a time step of 2.0 × 10⁻²⁴ s for the imaginary time step method
- Box sizes considered ranged from 2 × 10 fm to 2 × 16 fm
- Up to 2600 wave functions had to be evolved to account for pairing correlations with occupation probabilities different from zero
- For the relativistic mean field calculations, baryon mass was set to 938.9 MeV, with meson masses m_σ = 520 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, and m_ρ = 770 MeV
- Coupling constants for the linear Horowitz-Serot model were g_σ = 10.4814, g_ω = 13.8144, and g_ρ = 8.08488

## Context

This work extends earlier self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations by Bonche and Vautherin (1981) and others, who used spherical Wigner-Seitz cells that could not describe non-spherical pasta-phase structures or achieve a satisfactory homogeneous matter limit. The cubic cell approach enables investigation of string-like and plate-like quasi-nuclear clusters predicted by Thomas-Fermi calculations, and provides a basis for microscopic studies of pairing phenomena, excitation modes, and finite-temperature effects beyond the equation of state.
