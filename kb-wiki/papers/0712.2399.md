# Parity nonconserving two-pion exchange in elastic proton-proton scattering

> Parity nonconserving two-pion exchange in elastic pp scattering is investigated in the presence of phenomenological strong distortions in various models. Parity violation is included in the nucleon-pion vertex considering NN and N Delta(1232) intermediate states in box and crossed box diagrams. Using the derived parity nonconserving two-pion exchange potential we calculate the longitudinal analyzing power A_L in elastic $pp$ scattering. The predicted effect is of the same order as vector meson exchanges.

## Key claim

Parity nonconserving two-pion exchange (PNC TPE) in elastic proton-proton scattering produces a longitudinal analyzing power of the same order as vector meson exchanges, contradicting the common assumption that TPE is negligible; however, the result depends on the choice of weak coupling constants, with the older DDH "best" values leading to an overestimate while the newer FCDH values give tolerable agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors derived a parity nonconserving two-pion exchange potential in momentum space using time-ordered perturbation theory, considering NN and NΔ(1232) intermediate states in box and crossed box diagrams with monopole form factors at each vertex. They then calculated the longitudinal analyzing power A_L in elastic pp scattering by incorporating this potential into a distorted-wave calculation using the Reid soft-core potential for strong distortions and empirical phase shifts, and compared the results to those from local parts of ρ and ω exchange potentials using two sets of weak coupling constants (DDH and FCDH).

## Key numbers

- At 221.3 MeV (TRIUMF experiment energy), the PNC TPE contribution to A_L is 0.48 × 10⁻⁷ in the static model, 0.26 × 10⁻⁷ in the nonstatic symmetric model, 0.28 × 10⁻⁷ in the nonstatic asymmetric model, and 0.70 × 10⁻⁷ using the coupled channels model, all using the DDH weak coupling h_π^(1) = 4.6 × 10⁻⁷
- The charged pion mass used is m_π = 139.6 MeV and the average nucleon mass M = 939 MeV
- The Δ-N mass difference δ = M_Δ - M is used in the energy denominators
- The weak coupling h_π^(1) from DDH is 4.6 × 10⁻⁷ and from FCDH is 2.7 × 10⁻⁷
- The weak ρpp coupling from DDH is −15.5 × 10⁻⁷ and from FCDH is −7.0 × 10⁻⁷
- The weak ωpp coupling from DDH is −3.0 × 10⁻⁷ and from FCDH is −7.2 × 10⁻⁷
- The strong πNN coupling constant g_π²/4π = 13.8 with a cutoff Λ_π = 1.0 GeV
- The strong ρ coupling g_ρ²/4π = 0.95 with χ_ρ = 6.1 and cutoff Λ_ρ = 1.3 GeV
- The strong ω coupling g_ω²/4π = 20 with χ_ω = 0 and cutoff Λ_ω = 1.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of parity violation in NN scattering by calculating the two-pion exchange contribution, which was previously assumed negligible compared to vector meson exchanges (Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein 1980; Adelberger and Haxton 1985). The results show that PNC TPE is comparable to vector meson contributions, contradicting the assumption that only ρ and ω exchanges are significant in pp scattering, and the authors note that experimental bounds from ¹⁸F parity violating measurements (Page 1987; Bini 1985) constrain |h_π^(1)| ≤ 1.5 × 10⁻⁷, suggesting the DDH and FCDH "best" values may be too large. The work enables more careful interpretation of the TRIUMF experiment E497 at 221.3 MeV (Berdoz 2001) and suggests that an experimental point between the TRIUMF and Los Alamos energies would be of interest.
