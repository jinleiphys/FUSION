# Chiral Restoration and the Scalar and Vector Correlations in Hot and Dense Matter

> First, it is pointed out that hadron/nuclear physics based on QCD should be regarded as ``condensed matter physics'' of the QCD vacuum. We indicate that phase shift analyses which respect chiral symmetry (ChS), analyticity and crossing symmetry of the scattering amplitude show the $σ$ meson pole in the s-channel in the low mass region as well as the $ρ$ meson pole in the $t$-channel in the \pipi scattering in the scalar channel. We review recent developments in exploring possible precursory phenomena of partial restoration of \chis in nuclear medium by examining the spectral function in the scalar and the vector channels. We emphasize that the wave function renormalization of the pion in the medium plays an essential role to induce the decrease of the pion decay constant as the order parameter of chiral transition. An emphasis is also put on the importance to examine the scalar and vector channels simultaneously for exploring the possible restoration of chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper argues that partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter can be observed through characteristic enhancements of the spectral function near the two-pion threshold in both the scalar (sigma) and vector (rho) channels, but this enhancement depends on the wave function renormalization of the pion in the medium and may only appear as a precursory phenomenon before full chiral restoration is achieved.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical calculations using both linear and nonlinear sigma models to examine the spectral functions in the scalar and vector channels at finite temperature and baryon density. The linear sigma model includes explicit sigma and pion degrees of freedom coupled to nucleons, while the nonlinear realization integrates out the sigma field to study how the in-medium pion decay constant and wave function renormalization affect the pi-pi scattering amplitude. The N/D method incorporating chiral symmetry, analyticity, unitarity, and crossing symmetry is applied to analyze pole structures of scattering matrices in hot and dense matter.

## Key numbers

- The chiral condensate at normal nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3} shows a reduction of almost 35% relative to the vacuum value
- The sigma meson pole in free space has real part Re m_sigma = 500-600 MeV with imaginary part Im m_sigma approximately equal to Re m_sigma
- The chiral transition temperature from lattice simulations is T_c ~ 150-175 MeV, depending on the number of active light flavors
- The pi-N sigma term Sigma_{pi N} has a semi-empirical value of 40-50 MeV
- The pion decay constant in the medium is given by f_pi^* = f_pi (1 - g rho / f_pi m_sigma^2), where rho is the baryon density

## Context

This work extends the earlier finite-temperature calculations of Chiku and Hatsuda (1998) to finite baryon density, building on the foundational concept of chiral symmetry breaking as a phase transition of the QCD vacuum introduced by Nambu. The paper reviews the Hatsuda-Shimizu-Kunihiro (HKS) calculation showing spectral enhancement near the 2m_pi threshold in association with partial chiral restoration, and the Jido-Hatsuda-Kunihiro (JHK) work demonstrating that the nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry can also produce this enhancement through pion wave function renormalization. The results are connected to experimental observations from the CHAOS collaboration showing increased pi+pi- yield near threshold in heavy nuclei, and to deeply bound pionic atom measurements from KEK that indicate a decrease of the effective pion decay constant in nuclear matter. The work enables further investigation using the N/D method applied to hot and dense matter by Yokokawa et al, which examines sigma and rho meson pole structures on equal footing.
