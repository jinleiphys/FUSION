# Coupled channel analysis of molecule picture of $P_{c}(4380)$

> We construct a potential obtained by one-pion exchange for the coupled channel $Σ_c^\ast$$\bar{D}$-$Σ_c$$\bar{D}^\ast$, and solve the coupled Schrödinger equations to determine the binding energy. We find that there exists one or two bound states with the binding energy of several MeV below the threshold of $Σ_c^\ast$ and $\bar{D}$, dominantly made from a $Σ_c^\ast$ baryon and a $\bar{D}$ meson, with the size of about $1.5$fm for wide parameter region. We also study the pentaquark states including a $b$ quark and/or an anti-$b$ quark. We show that there exist pentaquarks including $c\bar{b}$, $b\bar{c}$ and $b\bar{b}$, all of which lie at about $10$MeV below the corresponding threshold and have size of about $1.5$fm.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed LHCb pentaquark \(P_c(4380)\) can be understood as a loosely bound molecular state of a \(\Sigma_c^*\) baryon and a \(\bar{D}\) meson, but only when the coupled channel \(\Sigma_c^*\bar{D}\)-\(\Sigma_c\bar{D}^*\) is included, because a one-pion exchange potential alone cannot bind the \(\Sigma_c^*\bar{D}\) channel directly.

## Method

The authors constructed a one-pion exchange potential for the coupled channel \(\Sigma_c^*\bar{D}\)-\(\Sigma_c\bar{D}^*\) using heavy-quark symmetry and chiral Lagrangians, with a monopole-type form factor at each vertex. They solved the coupled Schrodinger equations for the S-wave with \(I(J^P)=\frac{1}{2}(\frac{3}{2}^-)\) to determine binding energies, mixing ratios, and mean square radii, varying the cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) from 0.8 GeV to 2.5 GeV and the baryon-pion coupling \(g_1\) among 0.75, 0.95, and 1.95.

## Key numbers

* The \(\Sigma_c^*\bar{D}\) threshold is 4385.3 MeV; the ground state binding energy is several MeV below this threshold for a wide parameter range.
* The \(\Sigma_c^*\bar{D}\) component of the ground state wave function is more than 99% for most parameter choices.
* The mean square radius of the bound state is about 1.5 fm for a wide parameter region.
* For the hidden-bottom system \(\Sigma_b B^*\)-\(\Sigma_b^* B\), the binding energy measured from the \(\Sigma_b^* B\) threshold of 11113.0 MeV is larger than for the charm case, with a mean square radius of about 1.5-1.7 fm.
* For the exotic-flavor systems \(\Sigma_b^*\bar{D}\) and \(\Sigma_c^* B\), binding energies are several MeV below thresholds of 7701 MeV and 7778 MeV respectively, with size about 1.5 fm.
* For some parameter choices (e.g., \(\Lambda\) above about 2200 MeV with \(g_1=0.95\)), a second bound state (first excited state) appears within a few MeV of the ground state.

## Context

This work extends the molecular picture of the \(P_c(4380)\) pentaquark discovered by LHCb (Aaij 2015) by addressing a gap noted by Chen (2016): the coupled channel effect of \(\Sigma_c^*\bar{D}\)-\(\Sigma_c\bar{D}^*\) had not been studied. Prior analyses by Chen (2015) and He (2015) considered single-channel molecular states using one-pion exchange or additional sigma/omega exchange, but the present work shows that one-pion exchange alone can bind the \(\Sigma_c^*\bar{D}\) system through the coupled channel mechanism. The results enable predictions for hidden-bottom and exotic-flavor pentaquarks that could be tested in future experiments.
