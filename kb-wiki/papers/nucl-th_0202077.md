# Microscopic three-body force for asymmetric nuclear matter

> Brueckner calculations including a microscopic three-body force have been extended to isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. The effects of the three-body force on the equation of state and on the single-particle properties of nuclear matter are discussed with a view to possible applications in nuclear physics and astrophysics. It is shown that, even in the presence of the three-body force, the empirical parabolic law of the energy per nucleon vs isospin asymmetry $β=(N-Z)/A$ is fulfilled in the whole asymmetry range $0\leβ\le 1$ up to high densities. The three-body force provides a strong enhancement of symmetry energy increasing with the density in good agreement with relativistic approaches. The Lane's assumption that proton and neutron mean fields linearly vary vs the isospin parameter is violated at high density in the presence of the three-body force. Instead the momentum dependence of the mean fields is rather insensitive to three body force which brings about a linear isospin deviation of the neutron and proton effective masses. The isospin effects on multifragmentation events and collective flows in heavy-ion collisions are briefly discussed along with the conditions for direct URCA processes to occur in the neutron-star cooling.

## Key claim

The paper extends Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations with a microscopic three-body force to isospin asymmetric nuclear matter, showing that the empirical parabolic law (energy per nucleon proportional to \(\beta^2\)) holds up to high densities even with the three-body force included, but that the three-body force causes a strong enhancement of symmetry energy at high density and breaks the linear Lane assumption for proton and neutron mean fields.

## Method

The authors used the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone formalism with the Argonne \(V_{18}\) two-body force and a microscopic three-body force based on meson-exchange currents, including contributions from \(\Delta(1232)\) and \(N(1440)\) resonance excitations and nucleon-antinucleon virtual excitations. The three-body force was converted into an effective two-body force by integrating over the third nucleon's degrees of freedom weighted by the correlation function, and this effective force was self-consistently coupled with the Brueckner procedure. Calculations spanned the full isospin asymmetry range \(0 \le \beta \le 1\) and densities up to \(0.45\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation density: 30.71 MeV with three-body force, 29.28 MeV without three-body force; both consistent with the empirical value \(30 \pm 4\) MeV from nuclear mass tables.
- Parametrization of symmetry energy with three-body force: \(E_{sym} = 30.71 u^{0.6}\) for \(u \le 1\) and \(E_{sym} = 30.71 + 18.42(u-1) + 9(u-1)^2\) for \(u > 1\), where \(u = \rho/\rho_0\) and \(\rho_0 = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- Maximum deviation from the \(\beta^2\) law is \(6\%\) up to the highest densities calculated.
- Symmetry energy curvature at saturation: \(-66\) MeV (with three-body force).

## Context

This work extends earlier Brueckner calculations for asymmetric nuclear matter (Bombaci and Lombardo, 1991; Zuo et al., 1999) by including a microscopic three-body force previously applied only to symmetric matter and pure neutron matter (Lejeune et al., 2000). The results enable more realistic modeling of isospin effects in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for multifragmentation and collective flows, and provide constraints for neutron-star cooling via direct URCA processes. The density dependence of symmetry energy is compared with relativistic mean-field theory (DiToro et al., 2000) and Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (Kuo et al., 1999), showing a slower rise at low densities and steeper rise at high densities.
