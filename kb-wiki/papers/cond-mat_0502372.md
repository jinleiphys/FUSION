# Critical temperature for fermion pairing using lattice field theory

> Dilute gases of 2-component fermions are of great interest in atomic and nuclear physics. When interactions are strong enough so that a bound state is at threshold, universal behavior is expected. Lattice field theory provides a first principles approach to the study of strongly interacting systems such as this through Monte Carlo simulation. Results of exploratory simulations are presented here. In particular, the finite temperature phase transition between superfluid and normal states is studied. We present first results for the critical temperature T_c and describe the future work necessary to determine T_c as a function of interaction strength.

## Key claim

Exploratory lattice field theory simulations demonstrate the feasibility of locating the finite-temperature phase transition between superfluid and normal states for dilute two-component fermions, but the critical temperature values obtained are preliminary because the scattering length varies significantly along the parameter-space paths used, so holding the scattering length fixed requires different simulation strategies.

## Method

The partition function for a two-component Fermi gas with an attractive contact interaction is rewritten as a path integral and discretized on a 4D lattice with spatial sites and imaginary-time sites. An auxiliary scalar field is introduced via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, and the fermion determinant is made nonnegative to enable Monte Carlo sampling. The hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm generates ensembles of scalar field configurations, and the pairing condensate is computed as a function of an external source J and extrapolated to J=0 to locate the superfluid-to-normal transition.

## Key numbers

- For m²=0.155, the critical temperature is T_c/T_F = 0.035 ± 0.004, with b_s/a varying from -0.10 at ξ=1.1 to 0.10 at ξ=1.2 across the transition region.
- For m²=0.175, the critical temperature is T_c/T_F = 0.036 ± 0.004, with b_s/a varying from -0.11 at ξ=1.2 to 0.08 at ξ=1.3 across the transition region.
- The zero-temperature density used for the T_c calculation is n_0 b_s³ = 0.2612(6) for (m²,ξ)=(0.155,1.2) and 0.2893(7) for (0.175,1.3), measured at T_c/4 with N_t=48.
- On a 6³×12 lattice at μ=0 with m²=0.1456 and ξ=1 (corresponding to 1/a=0), the density is n b_s³ = 0.0073(6), confirming the dilute regime.

## Context

This work extends zero-temperature fixed-node Green's function Monte Carlo calculations by Carlson (2003) and Chang (2004) and fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo by Astrakharchik (2004) to finite temperature, using a fundamentally different lattice field theory approach that avoids the sign problem and requires no ground-state assumptions. The results enable future first-principles determination of T_c as a function of interaction strength in the universal regime near unitarity, where the only length scale is the interparticle spacing, complementing the BCS regime calculations by Heiselberg (2000) and BEC regime calculations.
