# Bulk viscosity of QCD matter near the critical temperature

> Kubo's formula relates bulk viscosity to the retarded Green's function of the trace of the energy-momentum tensor. Using low energy theorems of QCD for the latter we derive the formula which relates the bulk viscosity to the energy density and pressure of hot matter. We then employ the available lattice QCD data to extract the bulk viscosity as a function of temperature. We find that close to the deconfinement temperature bulk viscosity becomes large, with viscosity-to-entropy ratio zeta/s about 1.

# Bulk viscosity of QCD matter near the critical temperature

## Key claim

Close to the deconfinement temperature, the bulk viscosity of QCD matter becomes large, with a viscosity-to-entropy ratio ζ/s ~ 1, but this result depends on an assumed ansatz for the spectral density at low frequencies and on the choice of the scale parameter ω₀.

## Method

The authors use Kubo's formula, which relates bulk viscosity to the retarded Green's function of the trace of the energy-momentum tensor, together with low-energy theorems (LET) of QCD that constrain this correlation function non-perturbatively. They derive a sum rule that expresses the bulk viscosity in terms of the interaction measure (energy density minus three times pressure) measured on the lattice, and then apply an ansatz for the spectral density to extract ζ from lattice data for SU(3) gluodynamics from Boyd et al. (1996).

## Key numbers

- The ratio of bulk viscosity to entropy density ζ/s is approximately 1 near the deconfinement temperature T_c.
- The scale parameter ω₀ is estimated as ω₀ ≈ (T/T_c) × 1.4 GeV in the region 1 < T/T_c < 3.
- The vacuum energy density used is |ε_v| = 0.62 T_c⁴, with T_c = 0.28 GeV, taken from Boyd et al. (1996).
- Away from T_c, the bulk viscosity is small, consistent with the perturbative result ζ/s ~ 10⁻³ from Arnold et al. (2006).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative evaluation of bulk viscosity by Arnold et al. (2006), which gave a very small value, by providing a non-perturbative extraction using lattice data near the critical temperature. It contrasts with the conformal N=4 SUSY Yang-Mills theory studied via AdS/CFT by Policastro et al. (2001) and Kovtun et al. (2004), where bulk viscosity vanishes due to exact conformal invariance. The result enables improved hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, suggesting that bulk viscosity, rather than shear viscosity, may be the dominant dissipative correction near the deconfinement phase transition.
