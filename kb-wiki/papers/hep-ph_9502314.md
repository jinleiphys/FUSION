# In-medium effective chiral lagrangians and the pion mass in nuclear matter

> We argue that the effective pion mass in nuclear matter obtained from chiral effective lagrangians is unique and does not depend on off-mass-shell extensions of the pion fields as e.g. the PCAC choice. The effective pion mass in isospin symmetric nuclear matter is predicted to increase slightly with increasing nuclear density, whereas the effective time-like pion decay constant and the magnitude of the density-dependent quark condensate decrease appreciably. The in-medium Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation as well as other in-medium identities are studied in addition. Finally, several constraints on effective lagrangians for the description of the pion propagation in isospin symmetric, isotropic and homogenous nuclear matter are discussed. (Talk presented at the workshop ``Hirschegg '95: Hadrons in Nuclear Matter'', Hirschegg, Kleinwalsertal, Austria, January 16-21, 1995)

## Key claim

The effective pion mass in isospin symmetric nuclear matter is unique and independent of the choice of off-mass-shell extensions of the pion fields (such as the PCAC choice), contrary to claims that such extensions lead to an effective repulsion that inhibits meson condensation.

## Method

The authors apply chiral perturbation theory at tree level to ${\cal O}(Q^2)$, using the functional integral formulation of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) extended to include nucleons. They evaluate nucleon operators in the mean field approximation for homogeneous, isotropic, isospin symmetric, spin-unpolarized nuclear matter, working to linear order in density. The pole position of the in-medium pion propagator is computed to determine the effective pion mass, and the in-medium Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation and PCAC relation are derived from the effective lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The empirical pion decay constant $f_\pi = 93$ MeV and pion mass $m_\pi = 139$ MeV are used as input.
- The sigma term is fixed at $\sigma \approx 45$ MeV, giving the nucleon mass $m_N = m_0 + \sigma$ with $m_0 \approx 890$ MeV.
- The empirical isospin-even scattering length $a_{\pi N}^{+} = -0.0083 \, m_\pi^{-1}$ (repulsive) yields $(c_2 + c_3) m_\pi^2 \approx -26$ MeV.
- The effective pion mass to linear order in density is $m_\pi^\ast(\rho) = m_\pi - 2 \pi a^+_{\pi N} \rho / m_R$, where $m_R$ is the reduced mass of the pion-nucleon system.
- The time-like pion decay constant $f_t^\star$ and the magnitude of the density-dependent quark condensate decrease to about two-thirds of their vacuum values at nuclear matter density, while $m_\pi^\star$ increases slightly.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous letter (Wirzba and Thorsson, 1995) which refuted claims by Delorme et al. (1992) and Schuck et al. (1993) that chiral effective lagrangians are inconsistent with current algebra and PCAC for describing meson condensation. The paper establishes that the in-medium GMOR relation holds with the time-component of the decay constant, and discusses how non-relativistic chiral lagrangians from Leutwyler (1994) can constrain the structure of in-medium effective theories. This enables a consistent framework for studying pion propagation in nuclear matter and addresses open questions about higher-order corrections and nuclear correlation effects.
