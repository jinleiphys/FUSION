# Binding energy shifts from heavy-ion experiments in a nuclear statistical equilibrium model

> Chemical constants extracted from $^{124}$Xe+ $^{124}$Sn collisions at 32 AMeV are compared to the predictions of an extended Nuclear Statistical Equilibrium model including mean-field interactions and in-medium binding energy shifts for the light ($Z\leq 2$) clusters. The ion species and density dependence of the in-medium modification is directly extracted from the experimental data. We show that the shift increases with the mass of the cluster and the density of the medium, and we provide a simple linear fit for future use in astrophysical simulations in the framework of the CompOSE data base. The resulting mass fractions are computed in representative thermodynamic conditions relevant for supernova and neutron star mergers. A comparison to the results of a similar analysis of the same data performed in the framework of a relativistic mean-field model shows a good agreement at low density, but significant discrepancies close to the Mott dissolution of clusters in the dense medium.

# Binding energy shifts from heavy-ion experiments in a nuclear statistical equilibrium model

## Key claim

The in-medium binding energy shift for light clusters (Z ≤ 2) increases with cluster mass and medium density, but the authors show that this shift can be approximated as isospin-independent based on the similar behavior observed for the isobars ³H and ³He, though they caution that this assumption might have important consequences for extrapolation to neutron-rich astrophysical matter.

## Method

The authors compared chemical equilibrium constants extracted from INDRA data on ¹²⁴Xe + ¹²⁴Sn collisions at 32 AMeV to predictions of an extended Nuclear Statistical Equilibrium (NSE) model that includes mean-field interactions and in-medium binding energy shifts for light clusters. The thermodynamic variables (temperature, baryonic density, proton fraction) were taken from the experimental analysis of Pais (2020), and the binding energy shifts were optimized using a Bayesian likelihood procedure with a three-parameter function ΔE = a₁ + a₂A^(a₃) fitted at each density point. The resulting shifts were then parametrized with a linear fit ΔE = m·n_B for use in astrophysical simulations.

## Key numbers

- The reaction studied was ¹²⁴Xe + ¹²⁴Sn at 32 AMeV, with baryonic densities extracted from the experiment ranging from 5.14×10⁻³ fm⁻³ to 6.18×10⁻² fm⁻³ and temperatures from 5.12 MeV to 9.06 MeV.
- The linear slope parameter m = ΔE/n_B for each cluster: ²H: -160.5 ± 23.3 MeV·fm³; ³H and ³He: -355.5 ± 42.7 MeV·fm³; ⁴He: -576.8 ± 56.3 MeV·fm³; ⁶He: -1075.8 ± 108.4 MeV·fm³.
- Clusters dissolve in dense matter at densities varying between approximately 2×10⁻² fm⁻³ and 5×10⁻² fm⁻³ depending on the isotope.
- The RMF model (DDME2 functional) with optimized scalar coupling x_s = 0.93 ± 0.02 shows good agreement with the NSE model at low density but significant discrepancies near the Mott dissolution density.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Pais (2020) and Pais (2020b), who extracted chemical constants from INDRA data and fitted them within relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, by using a different theoretical framework: the extended NSE model of Mallik (NuclAstro1) with the Sly5 functional. The results are compared to the RMF calculations using the DDME2 functional from Pais (2020b), showing agreement at low density but model-dependent differences near cluster dissolution. The parametrized binding energy shifts are intended for use in the CompOSE database for supernova and neutron star merger simulations, and the authors note that future data on more asymmetric systems would help confirm the isospin-independence assumption.
