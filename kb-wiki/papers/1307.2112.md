# Phase structure of hadronic and Polyakov-loop extended NJL model at finite isospin density

> It is believed that there exists a rich phase structure of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) at finite temperature and baryon density, namely, the deconfinement process from hadron gas to quark-gluon plasma, the transition from chiral symmetry breaking phase to the symmetry restoration phase, and the color superconductivity at low temperature and high baryon density. In the present work we study the hadron-quark phase transition by investigating the binodal surface and extending it to finite temperature in order to mimic the QCD phase diagram. In order to obtain these conditions we use different models for the two possible phases, namely the quark and hadron phases. The phase separation boundary (binodal) is determined by the Gibbs criteria for phase equilibrium. The boundaries of the mixed phase and the related critical points for symmetric and asymmetric matter are obtained. Isospin effects appear to be rather significant. The critical endpoint (CEP) and the phase structure are also studied in the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model.

## Key claim

The hadron-quark phase transition in the QCD phase diagram is studied by combining a two-phase model (Walecka model for hadrons and MIT bag model for quarks) with the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, finding that isospin effects significantly alter the binodal surface and that the critical endpoint (CEP) location depends on the bag parameter and the hadronic equation of state parameter set.

## Method

The authors construct a two-phase model using the relativistic non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) for the hadronic phase and the MIT bag model with gluons for the quark phase, determining the phase separation boundary (binodal) via the Gibbs criteria for phase equilibrium. They also study the PNJL model with two quark flavors, incorporating static gluonic degrees of freedom through an effective gluon potential in terms of Polyakov loops to simultaneously describe chiral symmetry breaking and deconfinement. The binodal surface is projected onto the asymmetry-pressure plane and the baryon density-temperature plane for symmetric and asymmetric matter, including hyperons with zero total strangeness.

## Key numbers

- For the bag parameter between \(B^{1/4} = 190\) MeV and \(B^{1/4} = 210\) MeV, the critical temperature \(T_c\) lies between 150 MeV and 170 MeV.
- At zero baryon density (zero chemical potential), the hadron-quark phase transition temperature lies between \(T = 150\) MeV and \(T = 170\) MeV.
- The region where the phase transition can be probed by intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions is located in the range \(n_B = 2 - 4 n_0\) and \(T = 50 - 65\) MeV.
- The chiral phase transition in the PNJL model at zero chemical potential takes place at 170 MeV for a given parametrization.

## Context

This work extends the two-phase model approach of Cavagnoli (2011) by systematically studying the binodal surface at finite temperature and including isospin asymmetry effects. The results are compared with the Gibbs criteria for phase equilibrium from Landau and Müller, and the critical endpoint analysis builds on prior studies of the CEP. The PNJL model calculations follow the formalism of Costa, enabling simultaneous study of chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement. The findings provide a framework for interpreting intermediate energy heavy-ion collision experiments and mapping the QCD phase diagram, particularly the region around the critical point.
