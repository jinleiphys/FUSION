# NMEs for $0νββ(0^+\rightarrow2^+)$ of two-nucleon mechanism for $^{76}$Ge

> In this work we present the first beyond closure calculation for the neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$) of $^{76}$Ge to the first $2^+$ states of $^{76}$Se. The isospin symmetry restored Quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) method with the CD-Bonn realistic force is adopted for the nuclear structure calculations. We analyze the structure of the two nucleon mechanism nuclear matrix elements, and estimate the uncertainties from the nuclear many-body calculations. We find $g_{pp}$ plays an important role for the calculations and if quenching is included, suppression for the transition matrix element $M_λ$ is found. Our results for the transition matrix elements are about one order of magnitude larger than previous projected Hatree-Fock-Boglyubov results with the closure approximation.

# NMEs for $0\nu\beta\beta(0^+\rightarrow2^+)$ of two-nucleon mechanism for $^{76}$Ge

## Key claim

The first beyond-closure calculation for neutrinoless double beta decay of $^{76}$Ge to the first $2^+$ state of $^{76}$Se yields nuclear matrix elements about one order of magnitude larger than previous Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov results with closure approximation, but this result depends sensitively on the quenching of the axial vector coupling constant $g_A$ and on the particle-particle interaction strength $g_{pp}$.

## Method

The isospin symmetry restored Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) method with the CD-Bonn realistic force was used for nuclear structure calculations. The calculation goes beyond the closure approximation by explicitly including contributions from all intermediate states, using both pn-QRPA for the intermediate nucleus and charge-conserving QRPA for the final $2^+$ state. The single particle energies came from a Coulomb-corrected Woods-Saxon potential with harmonic oscillator wave functions, and the particle-particle interaction strengths were fitted separately for isoscalar and isovector channels to reproduce experimental $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay and restore isospin symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Baseline calculation (CD-Bonn short range correlation, $g_A=1.27$, $N_{max}=6$): $M_\lambda = 0.150$, $M_\eta = 0.469$, $M'_\eta = 1.519$ (dimensionless NMEs for $^{76}$Ge)
- Individual operator contributions in baseline: $M_1 = 0.705$, $M_2 = -0.253$, $M_3 = -0.046$, $M_4 = -0.153$, $M_5 = -0.048$, $M_6 = 0.527$, $M_7 = -1.270$
- PHFB results from Tomoda (1988) for comparison: $M_\lambda = 0.002$, $M_\eta = 0.061$, $M'_\eta = 0.001$
- With quenched $g_A = 0.75 \times 1.27 = 0.953$: $M_\lambda = 0.008$, $M_\eta = 0.317$, $M'_\eta = 1.249$
- Model space variation: changing from $N_{max}=5$ to $N_{max}=6$ changes $M_1$ by about 10%; adding $N_{max}=7$ shell gives changes generally smaller than 20%
- Closure approximation with 7 MeV closure energy changes results by only several percent

## Context

This work extends the QRPA approach previously applied to $0\nu\beta\beta(0^+)$ decays (Simkovic et al., 1999; Suhonen and Civitarese, 1998) to the $2^+$ final state, which is dominated by helicity-changing mechanisms involving right-handed weak currents. It contradicts the earlier PHFB calculation by Tomoda (1988), which found much smaller NMEs and strong cancellations between $M_6$ and $M_7$ leading to negligible $M'_\eta$. The results enable experimental searches for $0\nu\beta\beta(2^+)$ to be interpreted as constraints on left-right symmetric models, potentially ruling out mechanisms without right-handed gauge bosons if the $2^+$ decay is observed. Further work is needed on the role of induced weak currents, anharmonicity beyond QRPA, and N* mediated decay mechanisms.
