# Dropping Sigma-Meson Mass and In-Medium S-Wave pion-pion Correlations

> The influence of a dropping sigma-meson mass on previously calculated in-medium pion-pion correlations in the J=I=0 (sigma-meson) channel is investigated. It is found that the invariant-mass distribution around the vacuum threshold experiences a further strong enhancement with respect to standard many-body effects. The relevance of this result for the explanation of recent $A(π,2π)X$ data is pointed out.

# Dropping Sigma-Meson Mass and In-Medium S-Wave pion-pion Correlations

## Key claim

The previously calculated strong enhancement of the in-medium pion-pion invariant-mass distribution near the $2m_{\pi}$ threshold, arising from standard many-body effects (p-wave coupling to $\Delta$-h and p-h configurations), is further increased when a dropping sigma-meson mass, linked to partial restoration of chiral symmetry, is included, with the magnitude of the enhancement depending on the parameter $\alpha$ governing the density dependence of the sigma mass.

## Method

The authors use the linear sigma model treated in leading order of the $1/N$-expansion, with the pion propagator and sigma condensate obtained from the Hartree-Bogoliubov approximation and the sigma meson from the Random Phase Approximation involving $\pi\pi$ scattering. The in-medium sigma-meson mass is parametrized as $m_{\sigma}(\rho) = m_{\sigma}(1 - \alpha \rho/\rho_0)$, with $\alpha$ estimated from model calculations or QCD sum rules, and the imaginary part of the sigma propagator and the $\pi\pi$ T-matrix are computed at normal nuclear matter density for different values of $\alpha$.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum sigma-meson mass $m_{\sigma}$ is used as the reference value, with the in-medium mass given by $m_{\sigma}(\rho) = m_{\sigma}(1 - \alpha \rho/\rho_0)$.
- The parameter $\alpha$ is estimated to lie in the range from 0.2 to 0.3.
- At normal nuclear matter density $\rho_0$, for $\alpha = 0.2$ the peak height of the sigma-mass distribution is increased by a factor of 2 relative to the $\alpha = 0$ case.
- At normal nuclear matter density $\rho_0$, for $\alpha = 0.3$ the peak height of the sigma-mass distribution is increased by a factor of 3 relative to the $\alpha = 0$ case.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the same group (Aouissat et al., 1997; Aouissat et al., 1998) which showed that standard p-wave coupling of the pion to $\Delta$-h and p-h configurations induces strong enhancement of the $\pi\pi$ invariant-mass distribution around $2m_{\pi}$ threshold, an effect independently confirmed by Rapp and Wambach (1996) and by Vicente Vacas and Oset (1999). The present note combines these standard many-body effects with the dropping sigma-meson mass proposed by Hatsuda et al. (1999) and Brown and Rho (1991) as a signature of partial chiral symmetry restoration, and suggests that the combined effect may explain the $A(\pi,2\pi)X$ knockout reaction data from the CHAOS collaboration (Bonutti et al., 1996). Further work is needed to incorporate vertex corrections and Pauli blocking effects, and to make quantitative comparisons with experimental data.
