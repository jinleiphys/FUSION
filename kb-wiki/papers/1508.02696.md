# Quasiparticle Theory of Transport Coefficients for Hadronic Matter at Finite Temperature and Baryon Density

> We develop a flexible quasiparticle theory of transport coefficients of hot hadronic matter at finite baryon density. We begin with a hadronic quasiparticle model which includes a scalar and a vector mean field. Quasiparticle energies and the mean fields depend on temperature and baryon chemical potential. Starting with the quasiparticle dispersion relation, we derive the Boltzmann equation and use the Chapman-Enskog expansion to derive formulas for the shear and bulk viscosities and thermal conductivity. We obtain both relaxation time approximation formulas and more general integral equations. Throughout the work, we explicitly enforce the Landau-Lifshitz conditions of fit and ensure the theory is thermodynamically self-consistent. The derived formulas should be useful for predicting the transport coefficients of the hadronic phase of matter produced in heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and at other accelerators.

# Quasiparticle Theory of Transport Coefficients for Hadronic Matter at Finite Temperature and Baryon Density

## Key claim

The paper derives general formulas for shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and thermal conductivity of hot hadronic matter using a quasiparticle model that includes both scalar and vector mean fields, with the key condition that thermodynamic self-consistency and the Landau-Lifshitz conditions of fit are explicitly enforced throughout the derivation. The authors claim their results put previous ansatzes (such as those in Chen et al. (2013) and Khvorostukhin et al. (2013)) on a firmer theoretical foundation and can determine which of those competing ansatzes are correct.

## Method

The authors begin with a hadronic quasiparticle model where quasiparticle masses and mean fields depend on temperature and baryon chemical potential, including both scalar (sigma) and vector (omega) mean fields. They derive the Boltzmann equation from the quasiparticle dispersion relation, then apply the Chapman-Enskog expansion to obtain formulas for shear and bulk viscosities and thermal conductivity. Both relaxation time approximation formulas and more general integral equations are derived, with classical statistics used in the main text and quantum statistics results provided in an appendix.

## Key numbers

- The crossover temperature from hadrons to quarks and gluons is approximately T ≈ 150 MeV at zero baryon chemical potential, based on lattice QCD calculations cited from Aoki et al. (2006, 2009), Borsanyi et al. (2010), Bazavov et al. (2012), and Borsanyi et al. (2014)
- The critical point is generally thought to be located at T < 160 MeV and mu_B equal to several hundreds of MeV
- The KSS lower bound on shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is eta/s >= 1/4pi for strongly-coupled conformal theories, cited from Kovtun et al. (2005)

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Chakraborty and Kapusta (2011), which developed a comprehensive theory of shear and bulk viscosities in hadronic gases with temperature-dependent masses and a scalar mean field but did not include chemical potentials or thermal conductivity. The paper also generalizes earlier work by Jeon (1995) and Jeon and Yaffe (1996) on transport in scalar field theory, and by Baym et al. (1990, 1991) and Arnold et al. (2000, 2003) on hot QCD and QED. The derived formulas are intended for predicting transport coefficients of the hadronic phase produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, the Super Proton Synchrotron (SHINE) at CERN, and the future Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR) at GSI, enabling improved hydrodynamic simulations of these collisions.
