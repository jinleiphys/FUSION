# Broken symmetries and dilepton production from gluon fusion in a quark gluon plasma

> The observational consequences of certain broken symmetries in a thermalised quark gluon plasma are elucidated. The signature under study is the spectrum of dileptons radiating from the plasma, through gluon fusion. Being a pure medium effect, this channel is non-vanishing only in plasmas with explicitly broken charge conjugation invariance. The emission rates are also sensitive to rotational invariance through the constraints imposed by Yang's theorem. This theorem is interpreted in the medium via the destructive interference between various multiple scattering diagrams obtained in the spectator picture. Rates from the fusion process are presented in comparison with those from the Born term.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that dilepton production via gluon fusion, which is forbidden in vacuum by Furry's theorem and Yang's theorem, becomes non-zero in a quark-gluon plasma with a finite net charge density (arising from a baryon chemical potential), and that the emission rate is sensitive to the breaking of rotational invariance through medium modifications of Yang's theorem.

## Method

The authors compute the three-loop photon self-energy in the imaginary time formalism at finite temperature and chemical potential, isolating the cut corresponding to the process of two gluons fusing into a virtual photon that decays to a dilepton pair. They evaluate the two-gluon-photon vertex (a quark triangle diagram) using contour integration, sum over Matsubara frequencies, and analytically continue the photon energy to real values to obtain the discontinuity of the self-energy. The spectator interpretation is extended to three loops to identify the relevant cut and to separate the contributions from the breaking of charge conjugation invariance (via a finite chemical potential) and from the breaking of rotational invariance (via medium modifications to Yang's theorem).

## Key numbers

- The entire contribution to the two-gluon-photon vertex is proportional to the difference of quark and antiquark distribution functions: Δñ(q⁰, μ) = [1/(exp(β(q⁰ − μ)) + 1) − 1/(exp(β(q⁰ + μ)) + 1)].
- The gluon propagator in Feynman gauge is taken as D_{ρζ}(k) = −i g_{ρζ} / k² for the massless case, and for massive gluons the propagator includes a thermal gluon mass m_g with the form D_{ρζ}(k) = −i (g_{ρζ} − k_ρ k_ζ / m_g²) / (k² − m_g²).
- The cut that yields the gluon fusion process corresponds to the condition E = k + E_{p−k} for the photon energy, where k is the gluon energy and E_{p−k} = |p − k| (or the massive equivalent √(|p − k|² + m_g²) when thermal masses are included).

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Majumder (2001) that a finite charge density in the QGP enables a new dilepton production channel via a two-gluon-photon vertex, which is forbidden in vacuum by Furry's theorem (Furry, 1937). The paper also addresses the medium interpretation of Yang's theorem (Yang, 1950), which constrains the fusion of two on-shell vector particles into a single vector particle based on rotational invariance; the authors show how this theorem is broken in the medium by two distinct effects. The results enable future calculations of the dilepton rate from this pure medium channel, which could be compared with the Born term (q\bar{q} annihilation) and other higher-order rates (Aurenche et al., 2002) to identify signatures of QGP formation in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC.
