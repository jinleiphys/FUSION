# Bulk Viscosity in Dense Nuclear Matter

> In this chapter, I describe bulk viscosity as a general concept, and then focus on bulk viscosity in the dense matter present in compact objects. While this review is focused on bulk viscosity in the conditions present in neutron star mergers, I present a history of bulk viscosity research in dense matter, from its role in damping radial oscillations in neutron stars through its current applications in neutron star mergers. The majority of the chapter consists of calculations of the bulk viscosity from Urca processes in generic neutron-proton-electron ($npe$) matter, and then in dense matter containing muons ($npeμ$ matter) as well. I make several approximations in these calculations to keep the focus on the concepts. More precise calculations exist in the literature, to which I refer the reader. One concept I attempt to elucidate is the thermodynamic behavior of a fluid element throughout an oscillation and how that leads to bulk-viscous dissipation. I conclude with a discussion of the recent research into the role of weak interactions and bulk viscosity in neutron star mergers.

# Bulk Viscosity in Dense Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

This review chapter argues that bulk viscosity from Urca processes in dense nuclear matter is likely the most important transport property in neutron star mergers, but the calculations presented are approximate and pedagogical, with the author explicitly noting that more precise calculations exist in the literature to which the reader is referred.

## Method

The author presents a pedagogical derivation of bulk viscosity from Urca processes, first in generic neutron-proton-electron (npe) matter and then extending to matter containing muons (npeμ matter), using several approximations to keep focus on conceptual understanding. The thermodynamic behavior of a fluid element throughout an oscillation is elucidated to show how bulk-viscous dissipation arises, and the chapter concludes with a discussion of recent research on weak interactions and bulk viscosity in neutron star merger simulations.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density is defined as n₀ ≡ 0.16 fm⁻³, used as the reference density for dense matter in neutron stars
- Neutron star merger shocks heat matter to temperatures of tens of MeV, where 1 MeV ≈ 1.2 × 10¹⁰ K in natural units (c = ħ = k_B = 1)
- Neutron stars contain matter at densities up to several times nuclear saturation density (n₀)

## Context

This review extends the long history of bulk viscosity research in dense matter, from its original role in damping radial oscillations in isolated neutron stars through its current applications in neutron star mergers. The work builds on prior studies of transport properties in dense matter, including reviews by Schmitt and Shternin (2017) on transport in cold dense matter, and connects to simulations of neutron star cooling, core-collapse supernovae, and neutron star mergers. The chapter enables future work by clarifying the conceptual framework for bulk-viscous dissipation, while directing readers to more precise calculations in the existing literature for quantitative applications.
