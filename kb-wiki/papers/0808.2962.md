# Is there an Ay problem in low-energy neutron-proton scattering?

> We calculate Ay in neutron-proton scattering for the interactions models WJC-1 and WJC-2 in the Covariant Spectator Theory. We find that the recent 12 MeV measurements performed at TUNL are in better agreement with our results than with the Nijmegen Phase Shift Analysis of 1993, and after reviewing the low-energy data, conclude that there is no Ay problem in low-energy np scattering.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the recent precise 12 MeV neutron-proton analyzing power measurements from TUNL (BR08) do not constitute a genuine "A_y problem" because the data are marginally consistent with the authors' own Covariant Spectator Theory models (WJC-2 in particular), and the apparent discrepancy with the Nijmegen PWA93 phase shifts is largely resolved when the data are properly treated with separate statistical and systematic errors.

## Method

The authors solved the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) integral equation for neutron-proton scattering using one-boson-exchange kernels, producing two models: WJC-1 (8 bosons, 27 parameters) and WJC-2 (6 bosons, 15 parameters). They then compared the predictions of these models and the Nijmegen PWA93 phase shift analysis to the BR08 12 MeV A_y data, using a chi-squared treatment that separates statistical and systematic errors via a scale factor Z, rather than combining them in quadrature as the experimentalists had done.

## Key numbers

- WJC-1 model: chi-squared per data point of 3.014 for the scaled BR08 data (15 points plus normalization error), with scale factor Z = 0.9690
- WJC-2 model: chi-squared per data point of 2.387 for the scaled BR08 data, with scale factor Z = 1.0029
- PWA93 model: chi-squared per data point of 3.849 for the scaled BR08 data, with scale factor Z = 0.9367
- The Nijmegen rejection criterion for 16 data points (15 plus normalization) is chi-squared per data point less than 2.26; WJC-2 at 2.387 comes close to meeting this criterion
- WJC-1 charged-pion coupling constant: g^2_{pi^pm}/4pi = 13.703; neutral-pion coupling: g^2_{pi^0}/4pi = 14.608
- WJC-2 uses equal charged and neutral pion couplings: g^2/4pi = 14.038
- PWA93 uses equal charged and neutral pion couplings: g^2/4pi = 13.567
- The full low-energy A_y database (111 data points below 20 MeV) gives chi-squared per data point of 0.85 for WJC-1 and 0.90 for both WJC-2 and PWA93

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Covariant Spectator Theory analysis of np scattering (Gross 2007, 2008) and directly addresses the claim by Braun et al. (2008) that their precise 12 MeV A_y measurements reveal a significant shortcoming in the Nijmegen PWA93 phase shifts and all potential models fitted to them. The paper contradicts the interpretation of Braun et al. (2008) that the data require increasing the charged-pion coupling constant, showing instead that the WJC models already have larger pion couplings than PWA93 and that the BR08 data do not alter the fits when included in the full database. The analysis enables future work by demonstrating that the low-energy np database is remarkably consistent, with no evidence for an A_y problem, and that the Covariant Spectator Theory provides a viable relativistic framework for describing NN scattering.
