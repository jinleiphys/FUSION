# What do we learn about vector interactions from GW170817?

> We analyze the role played by vector-isovector meson interaction in dense matter present in the interior of neutron stars in the light of new measurements made during the double neutron-star merger GW170817. These concern measurements of tidal deformability from gravitational waves and electromagnetic observations. Our study includes three different equations of state that contain different physical assumptions and matter compositions, namely the NL3 family, MBF, and CMF models. Other related quantities/relations analyzed are the neutron matter pressure, symmetry energy slope, stellar masses and radii, and Urca process threshold for stellar cooling.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including a mixed vector-isoscalar/vector-isovector (ωρ) meson self-interaction in three different neutron-star equations of state systematically reduces stellar radii, symmetry energy slope, and tidal deformability, bringing all models into better agreement with the GW170817 tidal deformability upper bound of 1045 (and a stricter bound of 800), but the specific coupling strength required to satisfy these constraints is model-dependent.

## Method

The authors employ three relativistic mean-field models (NL3 family, MBF, and CMF) that differ in degrees of freedom (nucleons only or full baryon octet), density dependence of couplings, and chiral symmetry treatment. For each model, they add a Lagrangian term coupling ω and ρ mesons (L_ωρ = g_ωρ g_ω² g_ρ² ω_μ ω^μ ρ_μ ρ^μ), refitting the ρ coupling to maintain fixed symmetry energy at a chosen density, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with crust matching to compute stellar masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

* Radius of a 1.4 M⊙ star decreases by approximately 7% when the ωρ coupling is increased from zero to its maximum studied value across all models.
* Maximum stellar mass decreases by approximately 2% over the same coupling range.
* Symmetry energy slope at saturation decreases by approximately 53% on average when the ωρ coupling is increased from zero to its maximum.
* Tidal deformability of a 1.4 M⊙ star decreases by approximately 30% over the same coupling range.
* The dimensionless tidal deformability range from GW170817 is 76-1045 with 90% confidence (De 2018), corresponding to a radius between 8.7 and 14.1 km for a 1.4 M⊙ star.
* A tidal deformability upper bound of 1045 constrains the ωρ coupling strength to be above 11 in the NL3 model and above 3 in the MBF model, but imposes no constraint on the CMF model.
* A stricter tidal deformability upper bound of 800 constrains the ωρ coupling to about 70 in the MBF model and above about 5 in the CMF model, but is not compatible with the NL3 model.
* The tidal deformability upper bound of 1045 constrains the symmetry energy slope to below 90 MeV in the MBF model and below 89 MeV in the NL3 model.
* The tidal deformability upper bound of 1045 constrains the radius of a 1.4 M⊙ star to below 14.3 km in the MBF model and below 14.2 km in the NL3 model.
* The stricter bound of 800 constrains the radius of a 1.4 M⊙ star to below about 13.5 km in the MBF model and below 13.7 km in the CMF model.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of ωρ vector interactions in neutron stars (Schramm 2003, Dexheimer 2008, Bednarek 2011, Steiner 2012, Dutra 2014, Dexheimer 2015, Pais 2016, Hornick 2018) by testing them against the first gravitational-wave constraint from GW170817 (LIGO/Virgo 2017). The results show that the ωρ coupling simultaneously reduces radii, symmetry energy slope, and tidal deformability, enabling future gravitational-wave detections and NICER radius measurements to discriminate between models and constrain the dense-matter equation of state at both low and high densities.
