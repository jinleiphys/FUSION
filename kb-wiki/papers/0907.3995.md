# Parity-violating polarization in $np \to dγ$ with a pionless effective field theory

> We consider the two-nucleon weak interaction with a pionless effective field theory. Dibaryon fields are introduced to facilitate calculations and ensure precision in the initial and final state propagators. Weak interactions are accounted for with the parity-violating dibaryon-nucleon-nucleon vertices, which contain unknown weak dibaryon-nucleon-nucleon coupling constants. We apply the model to the calculation of a parity-violating observable in the neutron-proton capture at threshold. Result is obtained up to the linear order in the unknown dibaryon-nucleon-nucleon coupling constants. We compare our result to the one obtained from a hybrid calculation, and discuss the extension to weak interactions in the few-body systems.

## Key claim

The parity-violating polarization \(P_\gamma\) in \(np \to d\gamma\) at threshold is calculated using a pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields, and the result depends on two unknown weak dibaryon-nucleon-nucleon coupling constants (\(\htzero\) and \(\hszero\)), meaning a single measurement cannot determine them uniquely.

## Method

The authors construct a parity-conserving Lagrangian with dibaryon fields for the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) states, and a parity-violating Lagrangian with \(\Delta I = 0\) and \(\Delta I = 1\) dibaryon-nucleon-nucleon vertices. They then compute the Feynman diagrams at leading order (\(Q^0\)) for the capture process at threshold, obtaining the parity-violating amplitude \(Z\) and the polarization \(P_\gamma\) in terms of the weak coupling constants \(\htzero\) and \(\hszero\). The result is compared to a hybrid calculation using the Argonne \(v18\) potential and pionless EFT weak interactions.

## Key numbers

*   Deuteron momentum \(\gamma = \sqrt{m_N B} = 45.7\) MeV.
*   Deuteron effective range \(\rho_d = 1.764\) fm.
*   Neutron-proton scattering length in the \(^1S_0\) state \(a_s = -23.732\) fm.
*   Neutron-proton effective range in the \(^1S_0\) channel \(r_0 = 2.70\) fm.
*   Isovector anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon \(\kappa_V = 3.706\).
*   The parity-violating polarization at leading order is \(P_\gamma = -(2.59 \htzero - 1.01 \hszero) \times 10^{-2}\).
*   The experimental value for the neutron-proton capture cross section at threshold is \(\sigma_{\rm exp} = 334.2 \pm 0.5\) mb.
*   The experimental value for \(P_\gamma\) is \((1.8 \pm 1.8) \times 10^{-7}\) (from 1984).

## Context

This work extends the application of pionless effective field theory to parity-violating observables in two-nucleon systems, following the formulation of weak \(NN\) interactions in EFT by Zhu et al. (2005). It contrasts with hybrid calculations, such as those by Liu et al. (2007) using the Argonne \(v18\) potential, and with conventional one-meson-exchange (DDH) potentials, which show strong model dependence. The result enables future determination of the weak dibaryon-nucleon-nucleon coupling constants when combined with measurements of other observables, such as the parity-violating asymmetry in \(\vec{n}p \to d\gamma\) or spin rotation in \(\vec{n}d\) scattering.
