# The Pion Form Factor Within the Hidden Local Symmetry Model

> We analyze a pion form factor formulation which fulfills the Analyticity requirement within the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) Model. We show that it implies an $s$--dependent dressing of the $ρ-γ$ VMD coupling and an account of several coupled channels. The corresponding function $F_π(s)$ provides nice fits of the pion form factor data from $s=-0.25$ to $s=1$ GeV$^2$. It is shown that the coupling to $K \bar{K}$ has little effects, while $\omg π^0$ improves significantly the fit quality below the $ϕ$ mass. All parameters, except for the subtraction polynomial coefficients are fixed from the rest of the HLS phenomenology. The fits show consistency with the expected behaviour of $F_π(s)$ at $s=0$ up to ${\cal O} (s^2)$ and with the phase shift data on $δ_1^1(s)$ from threshold to somewhat above the $ϕ$ mass. The $\omg$ sector is also examined in relation with recent data from CMD--2.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) Model, when extended to include one-loop corrections from both the non-anomalous and anomalous sectors, can describe the pion form factor data from s = -0.25 to s = 1 GeV² without requiring additional vector states like the ρ(1450), provided that the ρ-γ coupling acquires an s-dependent dressing and that coupled channels (especially ωπ⁰) are included.

## Method

The authors derived the pion form factor Fπ(s) within the HLS Model by dressing the ρ propagator via the Schwinger-Dyson equation at one-loop order and introducing an s-dependent ρ-γ coupling Πργ(s) from loop corrections. They included contributions from pion loops, kaon loops, and vector-pseudoscalar (VP) loops (particularly ωπ⁰) from the anomalous FKTUY Lagrangian, with subtraction polynomials of second degree in s as free parameters. All other parameters (a, g, z) were fixed from previous global fits to radiative and leptonic decays of light mesons, and the fits were performed on e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ data from Barkov et al. and CMD-2, combined with spacelike data from NA7 and Fermilab.

## Key numbers

- The HLS parameter a from global fits to radiative and leptonic decays is a = 2.51 ± 0.03, while fits to the pion form factor alone give a = 2.35 ± 0.01 (averaged from previous work)
- The universal vector coupling g = 5.65 ± 0.02 from fits to radiative and leptonic decays
- The ρ mass is fixed by the HLS-KSFR relation to mρ = 827 ± 4 MeV, with a corresponding width Γρ ≈ 135 MeV
- The SU(3) breaking parameter z is fixed to z = [fK/fπ]² = 3/2
- Adding the ωπ⁰ channel reduces the minimum χ² from approximately 184 to 174 without additional free parameters
- The ω → ππ branching fraction from fits to CMD-2 data is 1.33 ± 0.25% (compared to 2.21 ± 0.30% from earlier Barkov data)

## Context

This work extends the HLS Model analysis of the pion form factor beyond the tree-level treatment of Benayoun et al. (2000) and the CMD-2 collaboration fits, by incorporating one-loop corrections to satisfy analyticity requirements. It contrasts with approaches using Gounaris-Sakurai functions (Barkov et al., 1985) or the dispersive method of Yndurain (2002) and Pich (2002), and shows that the HLS Model can describe data up to 1 GeV² without invoking the ρ(1450) resonance needed in standard VMD fits. The results enable a consistent global phenomenology connecting the pion form factor with radiative and leptonic decays of light mesons, and provide a framework for extracting the hadronic contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment.
