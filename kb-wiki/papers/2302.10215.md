# The LPM effect in sequential bremsstrahlung: gluon shower development

> We give details of our study of whether high-energy gluon showers inside a QCD medium can be treated as a sequence of individual splitting processes $g \to gg$, or whether there is significant quantum overlap between where one splitting ends and the next begins (neglecting effects that can be absorbed into an effective value of the jet quenching parameter $\hat q$ that characterizes the medium). The study is carried out by imagining in-medium gluon shower development in the simplest theoretical situation, which includes imagining a very large, static, homogeneous medium and taking the large $N_{\rm c}$ limit. Along the way, we also show how in-medium shower evolution can be written in terms of a "net" splitting rate $[dΓ/dx]_{\rm net}$, and we provide a moderately simple analytic fit to our numerical results for the overlap effects included in that rate, which we hope may be of use to others wishing to study possible consequences of overlapping splittings.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether overlapping formation times between consecutive gluon splittings in a QCD medium produce significant corrections to the shape of the energy deposition distribution, beyond effects that can be absorbed into an effective value of the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\). The authors find that these overlap effects on the shape function \(S(Z)\) are very small, much smaller than related effects previously computed for large-\(N_f\) QED.

## Method

The authors compute the "net" splitting rate \([d\Gamma/dx]_{\rm net}\) for gluon showers in a large, static, homogeneous QCD medium in the large-\(N_c\) limit, using the \(\hat q\) approximation for transverse momentum transfer from the medium. They separate the net rate into leading-order (BDMPS-Z) and next-to-leading-order (overlap) pieces, factorize infrared divergences associated with soft radiative corrections, and provide an analytic fit to the numerical results for the overlap corrections. The energy deposition distribution \(\epsilon(z)\) is then analyzed via an integro-differential equation and its moments, with the shape function \(S(Z) \equiv \langle z \rangle \epsilon(\langle z \rangle Z)/E_0\) as the primary observable.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic shower length \(\langle z \rangle\) is of parametric order \(\alpha_s^{-1} \sqrt{E_0/\hat q}\).
- The leading-order \(g \to gg\) splitting rate is \([d\Gamma/dx]^{\rm LO} = \frac{\alpha_s P_{g\to gg}(x)}{2\pi} \sqrt{\frac{(1-x+x^2) \hat q_A}{x(1-x)E}}\).
- The small-\(y\) behavior of the NLO net rate integral is \(-\frac{C_A \alpha_s}{4\pi} [d\Gamma/dx]^{\rm LO} \int_{y \ll \min(x,1-x)} \frac{dy}{y} [\ln y + \bar s(x)]\), where \(\bar s(x)\) is given by an explicit formula involving \(\ln(16 x(1-x)(1-x+x^2))\) and terms with \(\ln x\) and \(\ln(1-x)\).
- Overlap corrections to the fourth cumulant of the shape function \(S(Z)\) are found to be very sensitive, while most other moments show very small corrections.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of overlapping formation time effects in QCD showers previously studied at leading-log order by Blaizot, Iancu, and Wu (2013-2014), who found large effects that could be absorbed into an effective \(\hat q\). The present paper investigates effects that cannot be absorbed into \(\hat q\), using the full NLO rate calculations developed in earlier works by Arnold and collaborators (2008-2022). The results enable future studies of whether overlapping splittings significantly modify shower development in quark-gluon plasmas, and provide a fit function for the net splitting rate that others may use. The authors note that their results are qualitatively different from the large-\(N_f\) QED case studied by Arnold and Elgedawy (2022), raising questions for future work.
