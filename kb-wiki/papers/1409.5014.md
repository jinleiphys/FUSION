# Constraint analysis for the interaction of the vector-meson octet with the baryon octet

> We describe a constraint analysis for the interaction of the vector-meson octet with the baryon octet. Applying Dirac's Hamiltonian method, we verify that the standard interaction in terms of two independent SU(3) structures is consistent at the classical level. We argue how the requirement of self consistency with respect to perturbative renormalizability may lead to relations among the renormalized coupling constants of the system.

# Constraint analysis for the interaction of the vector-meson octet with the baryon octet

## Key claim

The standard SU(3)-invariant Lagrangian for vector-meson–baryon-octet interactions with three independent coupling constants ($g$, $\text{G}_\text{F}$, $\text{G}_\text{D}$) is classically self-consistent via Dirac constraint analysis, but the requirement of perturbative renormalizability in the effective-field-theory sense *may* impose additional relations among these couplings, this remains an open question, not a demonstrated result.

## Method

The authors performed a classical Dirac Hamiltonian constraint analysis on the most general lowest-order Lagrangian for a massive vector-meson octet interacting with a massive baryon octet, including the vector-meson self-interaction already reduced to a single coupling $g$ from prior work. They identified primary constraints from the canonical momenta, derived secondary constraints via time conservation of the primary constraints, and verified that the total number of constraints (80) yields the correct number of physical degrees of freedom (112) without requiring any relation among $g$, $\text{G}_\text{F}$, and $\text{G}_\text{D}$. They then outlined how a perturbative renormalizability condition could be tested by comparing counter-term structures from different vertex functions.

## Key numbers

- Free vector fields: 64 total degrees of freedom, 16 constraints, 48 physical degrees of freedom.
- Free Dirac fields: 128 total degrees of freedom, 64 constraints, 64 physical degrees of freedom.
- Interacting theory (vector mesons + baryons): 192 total degrees of freedom, 80 constraints, 112 physical degrees of freedom.
- The vector-meson sector yields 8 primary constraints ($\theta_{Va}^1 \approx 0$) and 8 secondary constraints ($\theta_{Va}^2 \approx 0$).
- The baryon sector yields 64 primary constraints ($\chi_{\Psi a} \approx 0$, $\chi_{\Psi^\dagger a} \approx 0$).

## Context

This work extends the Dirac constraint analysis previously applied to the pure vector-meson sector by Djukanovic (2010), Neiser (2011), and Bijnens (2014), which reduced five independent vector-meson self-couplings to a single coupling $g$. It also follows the approach of Djukanovic (2004) for relating coupling constants via perturbative renormalizability in effective field theory. The results provide a foundation for testing whether universality relations (linking $g$ to a linear combination of $\text{G}_\text{F}$ and $\text{G}_\text{D}$) or a single-parameter description emerge at the quantum level, which would constrain models of low-energy QCD with vector mesons.
