# Relation between E2 transitions in even--even and odd-mass nuclei

> How is the B(E2;2_1^+ -> 0_1^+) value in an even-even nucleus related to corresponding B(E2;J_i -> J_f) values in a neighbouring odd-mass nucleus? If either neutrons or protons are confined to a single-j orbital and if the nucleon--nucleon interaction conserves seniority, a simple relation between the two properties is obtained, which may differ from what is found in the weak-coupling limit of the core-particle model. This single-j relation is substantially perturbed if several non-degenerate orbitals are considered. An application to recently measured B(E2) values in neutron-deficient tin isotopes is presented.

# Relation between E2 transitions in even–even and odd-mass nuclei

## Key claim

The paper derives a simple relation between B(E2) values in even–even and odd-mass nuclei under the condition that either neutrons or protons are confined to a single-j orbital and the nucleon–nucleon interaction conserves seniority, but this relation is substantially perturbed when several non-degenerate orbitals are considered. The weak-coupling core–particle model prediction that B(E2;J_i→J_f) equals B(E2;2_1^+→0_1^+) is shown to be valid only in the large-j limit, with important deviations for finite j.

## Method

The author derives analytic expressions for the ratio of B(E2) values between odd-mass and even–even nuclei using seniority-conserving wavefunctions for identical nucleons in a single-j orbital, relating three-nucleon to two-nucleon systems via coefficients of fractional parentage (CFPs). For the multi-orbital case, a schematic Hamiltonian with a surface delta interaction (SDI) and single-particle energies for the 1d5/2 and 0g7/2 orbitals is diagonalized, and the resulting B(E2) values are compared to experimental data in neutron-deficient tin isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the particle-core coupling model of de-Shalit (1961) and the E2 sum rule tests by Tuttle et al. (1976) and Gray et al. (2020), which compared E2 strength in odd-mass indium isotopes with even–even tin isotopes. The paper provides a theoretical framework for understanding deviations from the weak-coupling limit observed in recent lifetime measurements in 105Sn by Pasqualato et al. (2023). The results enable quantitative interpretation of B(E2) measurements in odd-mass semi-magic nuclei and motivate further experimental studies with reduced uncertainties in neutron-deficient tin isotopes.
