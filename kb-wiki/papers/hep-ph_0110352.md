# Collective modes in color superconducting matter

> 10 pages, 1 figure. v2 - minor corrections. The detailed version of the presentation given at "Statistical QCD", Bielefeld (Aug. 26-30, 2001), "Workshop on Quantum Field Theory under the Influence of External Conditions", Leipzig, (Sep 10-14, 2001) and "Strange Quarks in Matter", Frankfurt on Main, (Sep 24-29, 2001)

## Key claim

The color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of dense quark matter supports a rich spectrum of collective modes including plasmons, Nambu-Goldstone (NG) bosons, and gapless Carlson-Goldman (CG) modes, with the latter two exhibiting large widths near the critical temperature; additionally, when kaon condensation occurs due to nonzero quark masses, the number of gapless NG bosons can be smaller than the number of broken symmetry generators, a phenomenon that depends on the presence of effective chemical potentials and the breaking of Lorentz invariance.

## Method

The author studies the poles of current-current correlation functions for vector and axial color currents in the CFL phase at large densities where the color interaction is weak. The polarization tensor is decomposed into component functions \(\Pi_n(q)\) using a basis of four tensors \(O^{(n)}_{\mu\nu}(q)\), and the contribution from "would be" NG bosons is included via gauge invariance arguments. Dispersion relations for magnetic and electric collective modes are derived from the pole equations \(q^2 + \Pi_1(q) = 0\) and \([q^2 + \Pi_2(q)]\Pi_3(q) + [\Pi_4(q)]^2 = 0\), while pseudoscalar NG bosons are obtained from \(\Pi_3(q) = 0\). For the kaon condensation scenario, a toy model with a complex doublet field and an effective chemical potential is analyzed to study the spectrum of excitations.

## Key numbers

- The plasma frequency for both magnetic and electric modes is \(\omega_p \equiv g\mu/(\sqrt{2}\pi)\), which is much larger than the superconducting gap \(|\Delta_T|\).
- At zero temperature, the light plasmon mass is \(m_\Delta \approx 1.362 |\Delta_0|\), where \(|\Delta_0|\) is the zero-temperature gap.
- For NG bosons at low temperature (\(T \ll |\Delta_0|\)), the dispersion relation is \(q_0 = |\vec{q}|/\sqrt{3} [1 - i(5\sqrt{2}\pi)/(4(21-8\log 2)) \exp(-\sqrt{3/2} |\Delta_0|/T)]\).
- Near the critical temperature (\(T \to T_c - 0\)), NG bosons have dispersion \(q_0 = (|\Delta_T|/T)(\pm x_{ng}^* - i y_{ng}^*)|\vec{q}|\) with \(x_{ng}^* \approx 0.215\) and \(y_{ng}^* \approx 0.245\).
- Near \(T_c\), CG modes have dispersion \(q_0 = (|\Delta_T|/T)(\pm x_{cg}^* - i y_{cg}^*)|\vec{q}|\) with \(x_{cg}^* \approx 0.193\) and \(y_{cg}^* \approx 0.316\).
- In the kaon condensation toy model, the two gapless NG bosons have dispersion relations \(\omega_2 \simeq v^2 q^2/(2\mu)\) (quadratic) and \(\tilde{\omega}_2 \simeq \sqrt{(\mu^2 - m^2)/(3\mu^2 - m^2)} v q\) (linear), while the gapped excitations have masses \(m_1 = 2\mu\) and \(\tilde{m}_1 = \sqrt{2(3\mu^2 - m^2)}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of collective modes in the CFL phase of dense quark matter, building on the original CFL paper (Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek, 1999) and earlier studies of color superconductivity (e.g., Rapp, Schafer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky, 1998; Pisarski and Rischke, 1999). The light plasmon mode has no analogue in ordinary metals and was first identified in this work (Gusynin and Shovkovy, 2001). The kaon condensation scenario follows from the effective low-energy action of Casalbuoni and Gatto (1999) and Son and Stephanov (2000), modified by Bedaque and Schafer (2001). The finding that the number of gapless NG bosons can be less than the number of broken generators connects to known examples in non-relativistic systems such as ferromagnets and superfluid \(^3\)He in the A-phase (Volovik, 1987). The results enable future studies of transport properties and possible observational signatures of color superconducting matter in compact stars.
