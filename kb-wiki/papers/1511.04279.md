# The effective description of non-strange hadrons low-energy electro-weak transitions

> Starting with the general principles of global and local symmetries, the effective pion-nucleon lagrangian, essentially non-linear in pion field, to describe the non-strange hadrons low-energy electro-weak transitions is developed. We encounter no divergence summarizing properly all the infinite power series in pion field which occur in the course of treatment. Our consistent approach proves to be relevant in considering P-parity violation in pion-nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

Starting from general principles of global and local symmetries, the paper develops an effective pion-nucleon Lagrangian, essentially nonlinear in the pion field, that avoids divergences by properly summing all infinite power series in the pion field; this approach is claimed to be relevant for describing P-parity violation in pion-nucleon interactions, but the treatment relies on the specific choice of the parameter \(b=1\) and on dimensional regularization to handle ultraviolet divergences.

## Method

The authors construct a nonlinear realization of chiral \(SU(2)\times SU(2)\) symmetry for the pion-nucleon system, starting from a nucleon Lagrangian and introducing a compensating pseudoscalar field \(\boldsymbol{\varphi}\) via a matrix \(\mathbf{U} = e^{-i2f\gamma^5\boldsymbol{\tau\varphi}}\). The Lagrangian includes both derivative and non-derivative pion-nucleon couplings, with a parameter \(b\) chosen as \(b=1\) to retain only derivative couplings; pion mass and wavefunction renormalization are performed by summing infinite series in the pion field using dimensional regularization, yielding finite results for the renormalization constants \(\mathcal{K}\) and \(\mathcal{M}\). Conserved vector and partially-conserved axial currents are derived, and the electroweak interaction with photons, \(W^\pm\), and \(Z^0\) bosons is introduced via minimal coupling to gauge fields, with the parameter \(\mathsf{Z}_e\) and \(g'\) fixed by requiring correct electromagnetic and axial charges.

## Key numbers

- The renormalization constants in the physical limit (\(d\rightarrow 4\), \(\Delta\rightarrow\infty\), \(P\rightarrow 0\)) are \(\mathcal{K} = -1/3\) and \(2\mathcal{M}/m^2 = 1\).
- The electromagnetic charge renormalization factor is \(\mathsf{Z}_e = 3\) in the physical limit (from \(\langle 0|\sin^2 z|0\rangle = 1/2\)).
- The axial coupling parameter is \(g' = 3g_A\) in the physical limit, where \(g_A\) is the nucleon axial form factor.
- The pion mass renormalization yields \(m_r^2 = m^2(1 - 2\mathcal{K} - 2\mathcal{M}/m^2)\), which remains finite.
- The vacuum expectation \(\langle 0|\sin^2 z|0\rangle\) is given analytically as \(\frac{1}{2} - \frac{2P-1}{4P}\exp[-1/(4P)]\), reducing to \(1/2\) in the physical limit.

## Context

This work extends the nonlinear chiral Lagrangian approach developed by Weinberg (1968), Gasser and Leutwyler (1984), and others, providing a method to sum all infinite pion-field series without encountering divergences, in contrast to standard chiral perturbation theory where only a finite number of terms are kept and renormalization is required order by order. The resulting effective Lagrangian and currents enable the description of low-energy electroweak transitions of non-strange hadrons, including P-odd pion-nucleon interactions, and can be applied to processes such as neutron beta decay and neutral-current interactions mediated by \(Z^0\) bosons.
