# The European Muon Collaboration effect in Light-Front Hamiltonian Dynamics

> A rigorous light-front formalism for electron deep inelastic scattering on unpolarized nuclei, in Bjorken limit, is reported. It preserves Poincaré covariance, macroscopic locality, both number of particles and momentum sum rules. The scheme is applied to the A=3 iso-doublet, very relevant in view of the planned operation with unpolarized and polarized beams at the Electron-Ion Collider. At variance with previous light-front estimates, our procedure, including a realistic nuclear description and free-nucleon structure functions, predicts a sizeable European Muon Collaboration effect for $^3$He. This will allow to analyze deviations from the proposed baseline in terms of genuine QCD effects. The extension to heavier nuclei is straightforward, although numerically challenging.

# The European Muon Collaboration effect in Light-Front Hamiltonian Dynamics

## Key claim

A Poincaré-covariant light-front formalism for deep inelastic scattering on unpolarized nuclei, applied to the A=3 iso-doublet, predicts a sizeable European Muon Collaboration effect for $^3$He without any medium modification of the bound nucleons, at variance with previous light-front estimates that found $R^A_{EMC}(x) \ge 1$. The result is conditional on using a realistic nuclear description with nonsymmetric intrinsic variables that implement macroscopic locality, together with free-nucleon structure functions.

## Method

The authors construct the nuclear structure function $F^A_2(x)$ in the Bjorken limit within light-front Hamiltonian dynamics, using the impulse approximation and the light-front spectral function formalism. For $A=3$, they evaluate the unpolarized light-cone momentum distributions $f^N_1(\xi)$ from three-body wave functions obtained with the NN charge-dependent AV18 potential plus the NNN Urbana IX force, then extract the neutron-to-proton structure function ratio $r(x) = F_2^n(x)/F_2^p(x)$ via an iterative method applied to the MARATHON Collaboration's $^3$He/$^3$H cross-section ratio data. The EMC ratio for $^3$He is computed as $R^{^3He}_{EMC}(x) = R^{^3He}_2(x)/R^D_2(x)$, with the deuteron structure function evaluated consistently using the AV18 wave function.

## Key numbers

- The iterative extraction of $r(x)$ converges with a relative difference between iterations 100 and 200 of less than 4 per thousand (0.4%).
- For $0.2 \le x \le 0.75$, the calculated $^3$He EMC ratio becomes less than 1, producing the EMC effect signature without off-shell corrections.
- The quadratic interpolation of the extracted $r(x)$ is given by $r^q(x) = 1.02107 - 1.48834 x + 0.887864 x^2$; the cubic interpolation is $r^c(x) = 1.05835 - 1.99892 x + 2.31339 x^2 - 1.0557 x^3$.
- The effect of the NNN interaction on the EMC ratio is safely negligible for $x \le 0.7$.

## Context

This work extends the light-front Hamiltonian dynamics approach of Pace (2020) and Del Dotto (2016) to produce the first Poincaré-covariant calculation of the EMC effect for $A=3$ nuclei. It contradicts earlier light-front estimates by Smith (2002) and Miller (2001) that found no EMC effect, which the authors attribute to those calculations using a nuclear mean field without proper treatment of correlations and using symmetric intrinsic variables that only assured covariance for kinematical Lorentz transformations. The formalism provides a baseline for separating genuine nuclear effects from QCD effects in future Electron-Ion Collider measurements with polarized $^3$He beams, and the extension to heavier nuclei such as $^4$He is straightforward though numerically challenging.
