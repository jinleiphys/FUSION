# Neutron$-^{19}$C scattering: emergence of universal properties in a finite range potential

> The low-energy properties of the elastic $s-$wave scattering for the $n-^{19}$C are studied near the critical condition for the occurrence of an excited Efimov state in $n-n-^{18}$C. It is established to which extent the universal scaling laws, strictly valid in the zero-range limit, survive when finite range potentials are considered. By fixing the two-neutrons separation energy in $^{20}$C with available experimental data, it is studied the scaling of the real ($δ_0^R$) and imaginary parts of the $s-$wave phase-shift with the variation of the $n-^{18}$C binding energy. We obtain some universal characteristics given by the pole-position of $k\cot(δ_0^R)$ and effective-range parameters. By increasing the $n-^{18}$C binding energy, it was verified that the excited state of $^{20}$C goes to a virtual state, resembling the neutron-deuteron behavior in the triton. It is confirmed that the analytical structure of the unitary cut is not affected by the range of the potential or mass asymmetry of the three-body system.

## Key claim

The universal scaling laws derived from zero-range (Efimov) models for the $n-^{19}$C elastic $s$-wave scattering survive when finite-range potentials are used, but the sensitivity of observables to the $^{19}$C binding energy is weakened as the interaction range increases. The excited Efimov state of $^{20}$C transitions to a virtual state when the $n-^{18}$C binding energy exceeds approximately 167 keV (zero-range) to 190 keV (high-range), confirming that the general Efimov picture remains valid even with finite-range interactions.

## Method

The authors solved coupled Faddeev-type integral equations for the $n-^{19}$C elastic scattering amplitude using separable Yamaguchi potentials for the $n-n$ and $n-c$ interactions, with the $n-n$ virtual state fixed at -143 keV and the $^{20}$C ground-state binding energy fixed at -3.5 MeV. They varied the $n-^{18}$C binding energy ($E_{^{19}{\rm C}}$) from 200 to 850 keV and adjusted the range parameter $\beta_{nc}$ to keep the two-neutron separation energy in $^{20}$C constant, considering two regimes: high-$\beta$ (low-range, $r_{nc}\sim0.16-0.21$ fm) and low-$\beta$ (high-range, $r_{nc}\sim2.7-4.4$ fm). The scattering amplitude was extracted using an auxiliary function method to handle the unitarity cut, and the effective-range expansion parameters were obtained by fitting $k\cot\delta_0^R$ to the form $(-a^{-1}+bE_K+cE_K^2)/(1-E_K/E_0)$.

## Key numbers

- The $n-n$ virtual state energy is fixed at $E_{nn}= -143$ keV.
- The $^{20}$C ground-state binding energy is fixed at $E_{^{20}{\rm C}}=-3.5$ MeV.
- For the zero-range model, the excited Efimov state of $^{20}$C becomes a virtual state when $|E_{nc}|=167$ keV; for the high-range Yamaguchi potential ($r_{nc}\sim2.7$ fm), this threshold moves to $|E_{nc}|=190$ keV.
- For the low-range potential ($\beta_{nn}=24.5$ fm$^{-1}$, $r_{nn}=0.1228$ fm), the pole position $E_0$ in $k\cot\delta_0^R$ ranges from 1304 keV (at $|E_{^{19}{\rm C}}|=200$ keV) down to 28.98 keV (at $|E_{^{19}{\rm C}}|=830$ keV).
- For the high-range potential ($\beta_{nn}=1.34$ fm$^{-1}$, $r_{nn}=2.372$ fm), $E_0$ ranges from 881.91 keV (at $|E_{^{19}{\rm C}}|=200$ keV) down to 85.59 keV (at $|E_{^{19}{\rm C}}|=850$ keV).
- The ratio $\sqrt{E^B_{^{19}{\rm C}}/E^0_{^{19}{\rm C}}}$ is 0.44 for the low-range case and 0.45 for the high-range case, compared to the universal zero-range prediction of 0.419.
- The $n-c$ effective range $r_{nc}$ varies from 0.157 fm (at $|E_{^{19}{\rm C}}|=200$ keV, high-$\beta$) to 4.403 fm (at $|E_{^{19}{\rm C}}|=850$ keV, low-$\beta$).

## Context

This work extends previous zero-range calculations for the $n-^{19}$C system by Yamashita et al. (2008) and the $n-d$ system studies by van Oers and Seagrave (1967) and by Whiting and Fuda (1971), which established the pole structure in $k\cot\delta_0$ for three-body scattering. It complements studies of finite-range effects in three-boson systems near the unitary limit by Hammer et al. (2008) and by Kievsky et al. (2011, 2015, 2016). The results enable experimental investigation of Efimov physics in halo nuclei using the $^{19}$C(d,p)$^{20}$C reaction at FRIB, as proposed by Macchiavelli et al. (2015), by predicting the energy-dependent $s$-wave cross-section minima that signal the pole in $k\cot\delta_0^R$.
