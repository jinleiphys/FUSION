# Unified exact WKB framework for resonance -- Zel'dovich/complex-scaling regularization and rigged Hilbert space

> We develop a unified framework for analyzing quantum mechanical resonances using the exact WKB method. The non-perturbative formulation based on the exact WKB method works for incorporating the Zel'dovich regularization, the complex scaling method, and the rigged Hilbert space. While previous studies have demonstrated the exact WKB analysis in bound state problems, our work extends its application to quasi-stationary states. By examining the inverted Rosen--Morse potential, we illustrate how the exact WKB analysis captures resonant phenomena in a rigorous manner. We explore the equivalence and complementarity of different well-established regularizations à la Zel'dovich and complex scaling within this framework. Also, we find the most essential regulator of functional analyticity and construct a modified Hilbert space of the exact WKB framework for resonance, which is called the rigged Hilbert space. This offers a deeper understanding of resonant states and their analytic structures. Our results provide a concrete demonstration of the non-perturbative accuracy of exact WKB methods in unstable quantum systems.

## Key claim

The paper develops a unified exact WKB framework that incorporates Zel'dovich regularization, complex scaling, and rigged Hilbert space for analyzing quantum mechanical resonances, extending exact WKB analysis from bound states to quasi-stationary states. The authors demonstrate this framework specifically for the inverted Rosen-Morse potential, showing that the different regularization schemes reveal a unifying analytic structure governing resonance physics.

## Method

The authors formulate the exact WKB method for a generalized Riccati equation that includes a first-order derivative term arising from the Zel'dovich regularization, with the regularization parameter appearing as a Gaussian factor \(e^{-\varepsilon r^2}\). They construct the Stokes geometry for the inverted Rosen-Morse potential under the Zel'dovich transformation, derive connection formulas between Borel-summable regions, and impose normalizability conditions on the wave function to obtain quantization conditions for resonant states. The analysis uses Borel resummation of the formal WKB series and examines the Stokes phenomenon to determine analytically continuable regions.

## Key numbers

- The inverted Rosen-Morse potential is given by \(V(x) = U_0 / \cosh^2 \beta x\) with \(U_0 > 0\)
- The Zel'dovich-transformed Hamiltonian in one dimension acquires a term \(-\frac{\hbar^2\varepsilon}{m}(1 + 2x\frac{d}{dx})\) from the transformation
- The quantization condition for resonance is given by \(1 - A = 0\), where \(A = e^{\oint_{a_1}^{a_2} dx\, S_{\text{odd}}}\) is the non-perturbative cycle between turning points \(a_1\) and \(a_2\)
- The connection matrix between turning points involves the factor \(N_{a_1a_2} = \text{diag}(\exp(\int_{a_1}^{a_2} dx\, S_{\text{odd}} + \int_{a_1}^{a_2} dx\, f), \exp(-\int_{a_1}^{a_2} dx\, S_{\text{odd}} + \int_{a_1}^{a_2} dx\, f))\)

## Context

This work extends the exact WKB analysis previously applied to bound state problems by Sueishi (2020), Sueishi (2021), and Kamata (2021) to resonant (quasi-stationary) states. It builds upon the analysis of the inverted Rosen-Morse potential in Morikawa (2025) and connects the exact WKB framework to the well-established regularization schemes of Zel'dovich (1961) and Berggren (1968) as well as the complex scaling method of Aguilar (1971) and Balslev (1971). The construction of a rigged Hilbert space for resonance within the exact WKB framework opens a path for future investigations of non-Hermitian and open quantum systems within a unified analytic setting.
