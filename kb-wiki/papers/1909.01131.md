# QCD at finite isospin density: chiral perturbation theory confronts lattice data

> We consider the thermodynamics of three-flavor QCD in the pion-condensed phase at nonzero isospin chemical potential ($μ_I$) and vanishing temperature using chiral perturbation theory in the isospin limit. The transition from the vacuum phase to a superfluid phase with a Bose-Einstein condensate of charged pions is shown to be second order and takes place at $μ_I=m_π$. We calculate the pressure, isospin density, and energy density to next-to-leading order in the low-energy expansion. Our results are compared with recent high-precision lattice simulations as well as previously obtained results in two-flavor chiral perturbation theory. The agreement between the lattice results and the predictions from three-flavor chiral perturbation theory is very good for $μ_I<200$ MeV. For larger values of $μ_I$, the agreement between lattice data and the two-flavor predictions is surprisingly good and better than with the three-flavor predictions. Finally, in the limit $m_{s}\gg m_{u}=m_{d}$, we show that the three-flavor observables reduce to the two-flavor observables with renormalized parameters. The disagreement between the results for two-flavor and three-flavor $χ$PT can largely be explained by the differences in the measured low-energy constants.

## Key claim

The paper shows that three-flavor chiral perturbation theory (χPT) at next-to-leading order agrees very well with lattice QCD data for isospin chemical potentials below 200 MeV, but for larger values the two-flavor predictions actually match the lattice data better than the three-flavor results, with the discrepancy largely explained by differences in the measured low-energy constants.

## Method

The authors calculated the effective potential in three-flavor chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order in the low-energy expansion for QCD at finite isospin chemical potential and zero temperature, working in the isospin limit with degenerate up and down quark masses. They derived the pressure, isospin density, and energy density from the effective potential minimized with respect to the pion condensate rotation angle α, and compared these thermodynamic quantities with recent high-precision lattice simulations from Endrodi (2019) as well as with earlier two-flavor χPT results. The parameters of the chiral Lagrangian were determined using the physical meson masses and decay constants from the lattice simulations.

## Key numbers

- The transition from the vacuum phase to the superfluid pion-condensed phase occurs at μ_I = m_π (the critical isospin chemical potential equals the pion mass)
- Three-flavor χPT agrees very well with lattice data for μ_I < 200 MeV
- The lattice values used for input parameters: m_π = 131 ± 3 MeV, m_K = 481 ± 10 MeV, f_π = 128 ± 3 / √2 MeV, f_K = 150 ± 3 / √2 MeV
- The low-energy constants at scale μ = m_ρ: L_1^r = (1.0 ± 0.1)×10^{-3}, L_2^r = (1.6 ± 0.2)×10^{-3}, L_3^r = (-3.8 ± 0.3)×10^{-3}, L_4^r = (0.0 ± 0.3)×10^{-3}, L_5^r = (1.2 ± 0.1)×10^{-3}, L_6^r = (0.0 ± 0.4)×10^{-3}, L_8^r = (0.5 ± 0.2)×10^{-3}
- Using the one-loop χPT expression for f_K with the central values gives f_K = 113.9 MeV, which is about 7% above the lattice value of 106.1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the seminal leading-order analysis of Son and Stephanov (2001) on pion condensation at finite isospin density by computing the next-to-leading order corrections for three flavors, which had not been done previously. The results are compared with lattice simulations by Endrodi (2019) and with the two-flavor NLO results from Adhikari and Andersen (2019). In the limit where the strange quark mass is much larger than the light quark masses, the three-flavor results reduce to the two-flavor results with renormalized parameters, providing a nontrivial consistency check. This work enables more precise comparisons between chiral perturbation theory and lattice QCD at finite isospin density, and the accompanying long paper by the same authors provides results on kaon-condensed phases at finite strange and isospin chemical potentials.
