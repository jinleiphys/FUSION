# What can we learn from the directed flow in heavy-ion collisions at BES RHIC energies?

> Analysis of directed flow ($v_1$) of protons, antiprotons and pions in heavy-ion collisions is performed in the range of collision energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.7--39 GeV. Simulations have been done within a three-fluid model employing a purely hadronic equation of state (EoS) and two versions of the EoS with deconfinement transitions: a first-order phase transition and a smooth crossover transition. The crossover EoS is unambiguously preferable for the description of the most part of experimental data in this energy range. The directed flow indicates that the crossover deconfinement transition takes place in semicentral Au+Au collisions in a wide range of collision energies 4 \lsim\sqrt{s_{NN}}\lsim$ 30 GeV. The obtained results suggest that the deconfinement EoS's in the quark-gluon sector should be stiffer at high baryon densities than those used in the calculation. The latter finding is in agreement with that discussed in astrophysics.

# What can we learn from the directed flow in heavy-ion collisions at BES RHIC energies?

## Key claim

The crossover equation of state (EoS) is unambiguously preferable for describing most of the directed flow data in the energy range sqrt(s_NN) = 2.7–39 GeV, but the description is not perfect; the results suggest that the deconfinement EoS in the quark-gluon sector should be stiffer at high baryon densities than those used in the calculation, which is in agreement with astrophysical discussions.

## Method

The authors performed simulations within a three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model for mid-central Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b = 6 fm, using a purely hadronic EoS and two versions of EoS with deconfinement transitions: a first-order phase transition and a smooth crossover transition. The directed flow v1(y) was calculated for protons, antiprotons, and pions as a function of rapidity at collision energies sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV, with an acceptance cut of p_T < 2 GeV/c applied to all hadrons.

## Key numbers

- The crossover deconfinement transition takes place in semicentral Au+Au collisions in a wide range of collision energies 4 < sqrt(s_NN) < 30 GeV.
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 39 GeV, the directed flow of all considered species practically coincides within the first-order-transition and crossover scenarios, indicating the crossover transition has been practically completed at this energy.
- The antiproton multiplicity in mid-central (b = 6 fm) Au+Au collisions at 7.7 GeV is about 1 within the deconfinement scenarios and about 3 within the hadronic scenario.
- The freeze-out energy density used in the model was epsilon_frz = 0.4 GeV/fm^3.
- The total hadronic Au+Au cross-section within the 3FD model is sigma_AA^(3FD) approximately 580 barns, compared to the Glauber calculation value of sigma_AA approximately 720 barns.
- The mean impact parameter for the 3FD model was scaled to approximately 6 fm based on the cross-section ratio.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the STAR collaboration (2014) directed flow data previously studied in Konchakovski et al. (2014) and Ivanov (2014) within the 3FD model, now including simulations at sqrt(s_NN) = 39 GeV that were previously inaccessible due to computational limitations. The results contradict the findings of the Frankfurt group (SAP14) using a hybrid UrQMD approach, which found no sensitivity of directed flow to the EoS. The paper also connects to astrophysical discussions by Alford et al. (2004) on hybrid stars masquerading as neutron stars, and to pulsar mass measurements by Demorest et al. (2010) and Antoniadis et al. (2013) that constrain the QGP EoS at high baryon densities. The work enables future comparisons of various EoS predictions with v1 data to provide evidence on QGP formation.
