# Pion production in the MiniBooNE experiment

> Background: Charged current pion production gives information on the axial formfactors of nucleon resonances. It also introduces a noticeable background to quasi-elastic measurements on nuclear targets. Purpose: Understand pion production in neutrino interactions with nucleons and the reaction mechanism in nuclei. Method: The Giessen Boltzmann--Uehling--Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) model is used for an investigation of neutrino-nucleus reactions. Results: Theoretical results for integrated and differential cross sections for the MiniBooNE neutrino flux are compared to the data. Two sets of pion production data on elementary targets are used to obtain limits for the neutrino-nucleus reactions. Conclusions: The MiniBooNE pion production data are consistent with the BNL elementary data if a small flux renormalization is performed while the ANL input data lead to significantly too low cross sections. A final determination of in-medium effects requires new data on elementary (p,D) targets.

## Key claim

The MiniBooNE pion production data are approximately consistent with the Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL) elementary data if a small flux renormalization is performed, while the Argonne National Laboratory (ANL) input data lead to significantly too low cross sections; a final determination of in-medium effects requires new data on elementary (proton, deuterium) targets.

## Method

The Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model was used to simulate neutrino-nucleus reactions on a CH2 target, incorporating quasielastic scattering, Delta and higher resonance production, background 1-pion production, and deep inelastic scattering. The model employed the impulse approximation with in-medium modifications for the Delta resonance width from Oset and Salcedo (1987), and final state interactions were treated by solving the semi-classical BUU equation. Two different parametrizations for elementary pion production, tuned separately to ANL and BNL data, were used as lower and upper boundaries for the calculations.

## Key numbers

* The BNL elementary cross sections are about 30% larger than those from the ANL experiment.
* For 1-pi+ production after FSI, the lower boundary (ANL input) lies approximately 40-60% below the MiniBooNE data, with the discrepancy increasing with neutrino energy.
* For 1-pi+ production after FSI, the upper boundary (BNL input) is still about 15-20% below the data.
* For 1-pi0 production at 1 GeV, the lower boundary (ANL input) lies about 40% below the data, while the upper boundary (BNL input) is within about 20% at 1 GeV and within experimental uncertainties at higher energies.
* The MiniBooNE neutrino energy flux peaks at 0.6 GeV and becomes very small above 1.7 GeV.
* The calculated cross section after FSI is about 30% lower than the corresponding cross section before FSI for both inputs.
* Cross sections for pi- production appear to be around 10 to 20 times smaller than those for 1-pi+ production.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical attempts to describe neutrino-induced pion production on nuclei, such as those by Leitner et al. (2006) and Ahmad et al. (2006), and addresses the compatibility of old pion production data on protons or deuterium with new data obtained on nuclear targets. The study uses the GiBUU model, which has been validated against pion-nucleus and photon-nucleon reactions, including photoproduction data from Krusche et al. (2004). The results enable a more reliable interpretation of quasielastic-like data from MiniBooNE, which contain a background of stuck-pion events, and highlight the need for new elementary data on proton and deuterium targets to resolve remaining discrepancies.
