# On the flat transverse momentum dependence of the single-spin asymmetry in inclusive neutral pion production

> We discuss recent experimental results from RHIC where the flat transverse momentum dependence of a single-spin asymmetry has been found in inclusive production of neutral pions. This dependence takes place in a wide region of the transverse momenta up to $p_T=10$ GeV/c. We emphasize that similar dependence has been predicted in the nonperturbative spin filtering mechanism for the single-spin asymmetries in hadron interactions and present some implications for this mechanism from the new experimental data.

## Key claim

The flat transverse momentum dependence of the single-spin asymmetry (SSA) in inclusive neutral pion production, observed at RHIC up to \(p_T = 10\) GeV/c, is consistent with the nonperturbative spin filtering mechanism predicted earlier, but the interpretation is not unique and the current experimental data have significant statistical errors that prevent a completely unambiguous conclusion.

## Method

The authors apply a nonperturbative chiral quark model where a polarized proton's constituent quark (\(U\) or \(D\)) fluctuates into a \(\pi^0\) and a spin-flipped constituent quark, conserving total angular momentum by generating an orbital angular momentum shift \(\delta\tilde{\mathbf{b}}\) in the impact parameter. The inclusive cross-sections for \(p^\uparrow + p \to \pi^0 + X\) are expressed via the \(U\)-matrix formalism (generalized reaction matrix) that enforces unitarity, and the asymmetry \(A_N^{\pi^0}\) is derived from the difference of the spin-dependent functions \(I^\uparrow\) and \(I^\downarrow\) integrated over impact parameter \(b\). The model uses a semiclassical correspondence between transverse momentum and impact parameter to evaluate the \(p_T\) dependence.

## Key numbers

- The flat \(p_T\) dependence of SSA is observed up to \(p_T = 10\) GeV/c (RHIC data).
- The model predicts the flat region applies for \(p_T > 1\) GeV/c, based on the typical hadron interaction radius \(R(s) \simeq 1\) fm and \(x_F/R(s) \simeq 0.1\) GeV/c.
- The extracted value of the spin-flip quark interaction radius is \(r_Q^{\text{flip}} \simeq 0.05\) fm from comparison with RHIC data.
- The unpolarized inclusive cross-section follows a power-law \(p_T^{-6}\) at high \(p_T\), consistent with data.
- The parameter \(\zeta \simeq 2\) and the total constituent quark mass \(M = 6 m_Q \simeq 2\) GeV/c are used in the model.

## Context

This work extends the spin filtering mechanism proposed by Troshin and Tyurin (2005) to explain the flat \(p_T\) dependence of SSA, which contrasts with the decreasing \(p_T\) dependence predicted by perturbative QCD approaches based on collinear factorization. The new RHIC data (preliminary, from the STAR collaboration) cover a wider \(p_T\) range than earlier measurements of \(\Lambda\)-hyperon polarization, and the model also reproduces the linear increase of SSA with \(x_F\). The results enable future quantitative tests of nonperturbative mechanisms versus alternative explanations involving internal orbital angular momentum of constituent quarks, but final data with smaller error bars are needed for discrimination.
