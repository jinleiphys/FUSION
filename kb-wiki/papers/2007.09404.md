# Probing in-medium nucleon-nucleon inelastic scattering cross section by using energetic n/p ratio

> Based on the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, the in-medium nucleon-nucleon inelastic scattering ( which is dominated by pion production at low and intermediate energies) is explored. It is found that the in-medium modification of nucleon-nucleon inelastic scatterings appears to reduce the neutron to proton ratio n/p at higher kinetic energies. Although the in-medium modification of nucleon-nucleon inelastic scatterings, as expected, affects the value of $π^-/π^+$ ratio, considering a series of undetermined properties of delta resonance and $π$ in medium, the energetic neutron to proton ratio n/p is more suitable to be used to probe the in-medium correction of nucleon-nucleon inelastic scatterings.

## Key claim

The in-medium reduction of nucleon-nucleon inelastic scattering cross sections (dominated by pion production at low and intermediate energies) decreases the energetic neutron-to-proton ratio n/p by about 15%, and this observable is more suitable than the π⁻/π⁺ ratio for probing the in-medium correction because pion production involves additional undetermined properties of delta resonances and pions in medium.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate central collisions of ¹³²Sn + ¹²⁴Sn at beam energies of 270 and 400 MeV/nucleon. They implemented an effective-mass scaling model to reduce the in-medium baryon-baryon inelastic cross sections relative to free-space values, with the reduction factor depending on density, momentum, and isospin asymmetry. They compared observables (n/p ratio, resonance yields, π⁻/π⁺ ratio) calculated with free versus in-medium inelastic cross sections, while fixing the symmetry energy parameter to isolate the in-medium effect.

## Key numbers

- The in-medium reduction of the baryon-baryon inelastic scattering cross section decreases the energetic n/p ratio by about **15%** in central ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn collisions at beam energies of **400 MeV/nucleon** and **270 MeV/nucleon** (for nucleon kinetic energies in the 300–450 MeV range).
- The maximum effect of the in-medium elastic scattering cross section on the energetic n/p ratio (for nucleon kinetic energies above 300 MeV) is only about **one half** of the effect from the inelastic cross section.
- The in-medium reduction decreases the energetic π⁻/π⁺ ratio by about **15%** at 400 MeV/nucleon beam energy.
- The reduction factor R_medium for nn, np, and pp colliding pairs is shown for densities ρ/ρ₀ = **0.5, 1.0, 1.5, and 2.0** with asymmetry δ = **0.2**.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections (e.g., Li et al. (1993, 1994), Alm et al. (1995), Fuchs et al. (2005), Li et al. (2006), Yong et al. (2011), Zhang et al. (2012)) by focusing on the inelastic channel, which had received little attention. It contrasts with the commonly used π⁻/π⁺ ratio (proposed by Li et al. (2002) for constraining the high-density symmetry energy) by arguing that the energetic n/p ratio avoids complications from uncertain delta and pion in-medium properties. The results enable future experimental constraints at facilities such as FAIR (GSI), HIRFL-CSR (IMP), FRIB, RIBF (RIKEN), and RISP (Korea), which could then allow better determination of the density-dependent symmetry energy using the π⁻/π⁺ ratio.
