# The Role of Tensor Force in Heavy-Ion Fusion Dynamics

> The tensor force is implemented into the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory so that both exotic and stable collision partners, as well as their dynamics in heavy-ion fusion, can be described microscopically. The role of tensor force on fusion dynamics is systematically investigated for $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+\mathrm{^{40}Ca}$, $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+\mathrm{^{48}Ca}$, $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+\mathrm{^{48}Ca}$, $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+\mathrm{^{56}Ni}$, and $^{56}\mathrm{Ni}+\mathrm{^{56}Ni}$ reactions which vary by the total number of spin-unsaturated magic numbers in target and projectile. A notable effect on fusion barriers and cross sections is observed by the inclusion of tensor force. The origin of this effect is analyzed. The influence of isoscalar and isovector tensor terms is investigated with the T$IJ$ forces. These effects of tensor force in fusion dynamics are essentially attributed to the shift of low-lying vibration states of colliding partners and nucleon transfer in the asymmetric reactions. Our calculations of above-barrier fusion cross sections also show that tensor force does not significantly affect the dynamical dissipation at near-barrier energies.

## Key claim
The tensor force produces a notable effect on fusion barriers and cross sections for spin-unsaturated systems, but this effect originates primarily from modifications to low-lying vibrational states and nucleon transfer rather than from changes in dynamical dissipation at near-barrier energies.

## Method
The authors implemented the full tensor terms of the Skyrme energy density functional into the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) approach in a fully three-dimensional Cartesian space without symmetry restrictions. They performed both frozen Hartree-Fock (FHF) static calculations and TDHF dynamical calculations of fusion barriers for five reactions with varying numbers of spin-unsaturated magic numbers. They also computed fusion cross sections and analyzed low-lying quadrupole and octupole vibrational states as well as nucleon transfer in asymmetric collisions.

## Key numbers
- For $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$, the tensor force (SLy5t) shifts the $2_1^+$ state from 2.98 MeV to 4.03 MeV (experimental value: 3.83 MeV) and the $3_1^-$ state from 5.13 MeV to 6.46 MeV (experimental value: 4.51 MeV).
- For $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}$, the $3_1^-$ state shifts from 3.64 MeV (SLy5) to 3.51 MeV (SLy5t), a change of 0.13 MeV.
- For $^{56}\mathrm{Ni}$, the $2_1^+$ state shifts from 2.30 MeV (SLy5) to 2.76 MeV (SLy5t); the $3_1^-$ state shifts from 9.53 MeV to 9.68 MeV.
- In $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$ at 0.1 MeV below the TDHF barrier, average neutron transfer changes from -0.85 (SLy5) to -0.25 (SLy5t); proton transfer changes from +0.95 to +0.94.
- In $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+^{56}\mathrm{Ni}$ at 0.1 MeV below the TDHF barrier, average neutron transfer changes from +1.37 (SLy5) to +1.88 (SLy5t); proton transfer changes from -0.29 to -1.06.
- For $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+^{40}\mathrm{Ca}$, calculated fusion cross sections overestimate experimental data by about 20%.

## Context
This work extends the study of tensor force effects from nuclear structure (e.g., shell evolution in Otsuka (2006), spin-orbit splitting in Colo (2007), and charge-exchange excitations in Bai (2010)) into heavy-ion fusion dynamics for the first time. It builds on prior TDHF studies of fusion (Umar (2006), Guo (2007), Simenel (2011)) and vibration dynamics (Maruhn (2005), Simenel (2009)). The results enable future improvements to effective interactions by demonstrating that tensor force must be included to reproduce fusion barriers, particularly for spin-unsaturated systems, and that the T$IJ$ parametrizations (Lesinski (2007)) show distinct isoscalar and isovector tensor effects on fusion.
