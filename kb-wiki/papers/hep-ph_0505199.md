# Thermal Time Scales in a Color Glass Condensate

> In a model of relativistic heavy ion collisions wherein the unconfined quark-gluon plasma is condensed into glass, we derive the Vogel-Fulcher-Tammann cooling law. This law is well known to hold true in condensed matter glasses. The high energy plasma is initially created in a very hot negative temperature state and cools down to the Hagedorn glass temperature at an ever decreasing rate. The cooling rate is largely determined by the QCD string tension derived from hadronic Regge trajectories. The ultimately slow relaxation time is a defining characteristic of a color glass condensate.

## Key claim

The paper derives the Vogel-Fulcher-Tammann (VFT) cooling law for a color glass condensate formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions, showing that the unconfined quark-gluon plasma cools from an initial negative temperature state toward the Hagedorn glass temperature with ever-increasing relaxation times. The central hedge is that this glassy behavior only applies when the thermal relaxation times are not much shorter than the RHIC collision times, making the glass state relevant specifically for explaining heavy quark suppression.

## Method

The authors model the perturbative QCD vacuum as an excited state at negative temperature, analogous to a laser medium, using the color dielectric response function to derive a confining linear quark potential with string tension from hadronic Regge trajectories. They then compute the Hagedorn entropy for a bosonic QCD string using analytical number theory (Hardy-Ramanujan partition asymptotics) and relate the entropy to thermal relaxation times via phase-space averaging over initial and final states, yielding the VFT law for the temperature-dependent relaxation time.

## Key numbers

- String tension from experimental Regge trajectories: \(\hbar c \sigma \approx 0.18\ \text{GeV}^2\)
- Hagedorn glass temperature: \(k_B T_g = \sqrt{3\hbar c \sigma / 4\pi} \approx 207\ \text{MeV}\)
- Thermal activation energy: \(\Phi = 7k_B T_g / 2 \approx 725\ \text{MeV}\)

## Context

This work extends the color glass condensate (CGC) concept proposed by Gtulassy, Kharzeev, Iancu, Jalilian-Marian, Venugopalan, and others to explain heavy quark suppression at RHIC, by providing a concrete thermal relaxation mechanism based on QCD string dynamics. The derivation connects the Hagedorn temperature (Hagedorn 1965) to a glass transition temperature, enabling future calculations of thermalization timescales in heavy ion collisions and supporting the interpretation of RHIC data as evidence for a glassy rather than fluid phase of matter.
