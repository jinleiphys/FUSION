# Nonextensive statistical effects and strangeness production in hot and dense nuclear matter

> By means of an effective relativistic nuclear equation of state in the framework of the nonextensive statistical mechanics, characterized by power-law quantum distributions, we study the phase transition from hadronic matter to quark-gluon plasma at finite temperature and baryon density. The analysis is performed by requiring the Gibbs conditions on the global conservation of baryon number, electric charge fraction and zero net strangeness. We show that nonextensive statistical effects strongly influence the strangeness production during the pure hadronic phase and the hadron-quark-gluon mixed phase transition, also for small deviations from the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics.

## Key claim

The paper shows that nonextensive statistical effects (with the Tsallis parameter \(q>1\)) strongly enhance strangeness production in both the pure hadronic phase and the hadron-quark-gluon mixed phase, even for small deviations from standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics; this enhancement is most pronounced at moderate baryon densities and temperatures relevant for compressed baryonic matter experiments.

## Method

The authors employ an effective relativistic mean-field model for hadronic matter (including the full baryon octet, \(\Delta\)-isobars, and light mesons) and a MIT bag model for the quark-gluon plasma, both generalized with power-law quantum distributions from Tsallis nonextensive statistics. The phase transition is studied by imposing Gibbs conditions for global conservation of baryon number, electric charge fraction (\(Z/A=0.4\)), and zero net strangeness, allowing the charge densities to differ between phases in the mixed phase. The nonextensive parameter \(q\) is varied (focusing on \(q>1\)) to compare with the standard \(q=1\) case.

## Key numbers

- At \(T = 120\) MeV, the pressure as a function of baryon density is considerably increased for \(q>1\) compared to \(q=1\), with a stronger softening in the mixed phase and a larger range of baryon density involved in the mixed phase region.
- The critical maximum temperature at vanishing baryon density is significantly lowered for \(q>1\) relative to the standard case.
- At \(\rho_B = \rho_0\) (nuclear saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.153\) fm\(^{-3}\)), the strangeness chemical potential \(\mu_S\) shows a different behavior at lower and higher temperatures for \(q \ne 1\) compared to \(q=1\).
- At \(T = 120\) MeV and \(q=1.1\), the net particle concentrations show a strong reduction of neutron and proton fractions and a considerable increase in hyperon and meson concentrations compared to \(q=1\).
- At \(T = 120\) MeV, the strangeness fraction carried by anti-baryons becomes relevant and comparable to anti-mesons and baryons for \(q=1.1\), whereas for \(q=1\) the total strangeness is carried almost completely by mesons and baryons.
- The strange to anti-strange quark ratio \(\rho_s/\rho_{\overline{s}}\) in the quark-gluon phase shows a maximum excess of \(s\) over \(\overline{s}\) around \(\rho_B \approx 3\,\rho_0\) at the beginning of the mixed phase (\(\chi=0.1\)) for \(q=1\); this behavior is modified for \(q>1\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Lavagno and Pigato, 2010) that considered nonextensive effects in the hadron-to-quark-gluon phase transition without strangeness, by now including the full octet of baryons, \(\Delta\)-isobars, and strange mesons. The results contradict the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs picture (e.g., as used in Glenn (2001) and Schaffner (1996) for hyperon couplings) by showing that even small deviations (\(q=1.1\)) strongly enhance strange particle densities and modify the phase diagram. The findings enable future comparisons with experimental data from compressed baryonic matter experiments such as FAIR at GSI and NICA at JINR, and suggest that nonextensive statistics may be necessary to interpret strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions.
