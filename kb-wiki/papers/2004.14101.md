# Nuclear Mass Measurements Map the Structure of Atomic Nuclei and Accreting Neutron Stars

> We present mass excesses (ME) of neutron-rich isotopes of Ar through Fe, obtained via TOF-$Bρ$ mass spectrometry at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory. Our new results have significantly reduced systematic uncertainties relative to a prior analysis, enabling the first determination of ME for $^{58,59}{\rm Ti}$, $^{62}{\rm V}$, $^{65}{\rm Cr}$, $^{67,68}{\rm Mn}$, and $^{69,70}{\rm Fe}$. Our results show the $N=34$ subshell weaken at Sc and vanish at Ti, along with the absence of an $N=40$ subshell at Mn. This leads to a cooler accreted neutron star crust, highlighting the connection between the structure of nuclei and neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper presents mass excesses of neutron-rich isotopes from Ar through Fe with significantly reduced systematic uncertainties compared to a prior analysis, providing the first mass measurements for several nuclides. The results show that the N=34 subshell weakens at Sc and vanishes at Ti, and that the N=40 subshell is absent at Mn, which together lead to a cooler accreted neutron star crust than previously thought.

## Method

Time-of-flight magnetic-rigidity (TOF-Bρ) mass spectrometry was performed at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory using a 140 MeV/nucleon beam of 82Se impinging on a Be target, with fully-stripped fragments transmitted through the A1900 fragment separator and S800 spectrograph. An empirical relationship between m/q and TOF was determined from a fit to 27 calibration nuclides (including seven newly added high-precision Penning trap mass data from Moug18 and Leis18), and mass excesses were extracted for neutron-rich isotopes of elements from Ar through Fe (Z=18-26).

## Key numbers

- Mass excess of 55Sc: -31,090 (220) keV, compared to the prior value of -30,159 (454) keV from AME 2016
- Mass excess of 65Cr: -27,280 (780) keV, compared to the AME 2016 extrapolation of -28,220 (300*) keV
- QEC for 55Sc: 12.44 (0.27) MeV, compared to the prior value of 11.51 (0.48) MeV
- QEC for 65Mn: 13.69 (0.78) MeV, compared to the prior value of 12.75 (0.30) MeV
- First determination of mass excess for 58Ti: -30,890 (250) keV, 59Ti: -25,220 (270) keV, 62V: -25,340 (420) keV, 65Cr: -27,280 (780) keV, 67Mn: -33,960 (330) keV, 68Mn: -27,710 (1310) keV, 69Fe: -40,270 (400) keV, and 70Fe: -37,710 (490) keV
- Systematic uncertainty of 9.1 keV/q added to normalize reduced chi-squared to one

## Context

This work extends prior TOF-Bρ mass measurements reported in Meisel (2015) and Meisel (2016) by incorporating high-precision Penning trap data from Moug18 and Leis18 as additional calibration nuclides, which reduced systematic uncertainties by roughly a factor of two. The results provide model-independent confirmation of the N=34 subshell closure at Ca (previously shown by Mich18) and its weakening at Sc and absence at Ti, as well as confirming the disappearance of the N=40 subshell at Mn (previously indicated by spectroscopy in Naim12). The updated QEC values increase neutrino luminosity from urca cooling by 50% for 55Sc and provide the first experimental determination for 65Mn, leading to a cooler predicted neutron star crust and leaving 63V as the final nuclear mass important for urca cooling that still relies on theoretical estimates.
