# The effect of the spectator charge on the charged pion spectra in peripheral ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We estimate the electromagnetic effect of the spectator charge on the momentum spectra of charged pions produced in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies. We find a large effect which results in strongly varying structures in the $x_F$ dependence of the $π^+/π^-$ ratio, especially at low transverse momenta where a deep valley in the above ratio is predicted at $x_F \sim$ 0.15 -- 0.20. The effect depends on initial conditions. Thus, it provides new information on the space and time evolution of the non-perturbative pion creation process.

# The effect of the spectator charge on the charged pion spectra in peripheral ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The electromagnetic interaction between spectator nuclear remnants and charged pions produces large, strongly varying structures in the π⁺/π⁻ ratio as a function of Feynman-x (xF), especially at low transverse momenta, but the magnitude and shape of these structures depend on the initial conditions of the pion creation process, specifically the pion emission time relative to the nuclear collision.

## Method
The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of charged pion propagation in the electromagnetic fields generated by two moving spectator systems, modeled as uniform spheres of normal nuclear density (0.17/fm³) with total charge Q=70 elementary units each. The static electric fields of the spectators were Lorentz-transformed to the center-of-mass frame, and pion trajectories were computed from the relativistic equations of motion for peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (√sNN=17 GeV). The initial pion spectra were taken as identical for π⁺ and π⁻, and the final-state distributions were obtained after propagation through the time-dependent EM fields.

## Key numbers
- Spectator charge: Q=70 elementary units for each spectator system
- Nuclear density: ρ=0.17/fm³ assumed for both spectator spheres
- Deep valley in π⁺/π⁻ ratio predicted at xF ≈ 0.15–0.20 at low transverse momenta
- Transverse momentum values studied: pT = 25, 75, 125, 175, and 325 MeV/c (each with ±25 MeV/c bin width)
- Changes in pion emission time by 0.5 fm/c (in center-of-mass time) are sufficient to modify the observed distortion pattern
- Beam energy: 158 GeV/nucleon, corresponding to √sNN=17 GeV

## Context
This work extends earlier predictions of nuclear effects at SPS energies by Szczurek and collaborators (2004) and extends preliminary observations from the NA49 experiment (2006) which suggested a Coulomb effect due to spectator charge in peripheral collisions. The study demonstrates that the spectator-induced electromagnetic effect is sensitive to the space-time evolution of pion production, providing a new tool to probe the non-perturbative process of light meson creation. The results enable future experimental comparisons with NA49 data to constrain the formation time and spatial extent of the pion emission zone in heavy-ion collisions.
