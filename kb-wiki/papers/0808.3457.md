# A model comparison of resonance lifetime modifications, a soft equation of state and non-Gaussian effects on $π-π$ correlations at FAIR/AGS energies

> HBT correlations of $π^--π^-$ pairs at FAIR/AGS energies are investigated by using the UrQMD transport model and the CRAB analyzing program. Three different possible sources (treatment of resonance lifetimes, a soft equation of state and non-Gaussian effects) to understand the HBT $R_O/R_S$ puzzle are investigated. Firstly, we find that different treatments of the resonance decay time can not resolve the HBT time-related puzzle, however it can modify the HBT radii at low transverse momenta to some extent to explain the data slightly. Secondly, with a soft equation of state with momentum dependence, the measured transverse momentum dependent HBT radii and $R_O/R_S$ ratio can be described fairly well. Thirdly, non-Gaussian effects are visible in the calculated correlation function. Using the Edgeworth expansion, one finds that the non-Gaussian effect is strongest in the longitudinal direction and weakest in the sideward direction.

## Key claim

The paper investigates three possible sources (resonance lifetime modifications, a soft equation of state, and non-Gaussian effects) to address the HBT \(R_O/R_S\) puzzle at FAIR/AGS energies, finding that only the inclusion of a soft momentum-dependent equation of state (mean field potential) can resolve the puzzle, while resonance lifetime modifications and non-Gaussian effects cannot.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model (v2.2) to simulate central Au+Au collisions at beam energies of 2, 4, 6, and 8 A GeV, then fed the freeze-out phase-space coordinates of negatively charged pions into the CRAB analyzing program to compute two-pion correlation functions. They compared cascade-mode calculations (with mass-independent and mass-dependent \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance lifetimes, as well as widths of \(1/2\Gamma_0\), \(\Gamma_0\), and \(2\Gamma_0\)) against calculations with a soft momentum-dependent equation of state (SM-EoS) for baryons. The correlation functions were fitted with both a standard 3D Gaussian form and an Edgeworth expansion up to eighth order to quantify non-Gaussian effects.

## Key numbers

- The difference in \(R_{\rm inv}\) between no expansion and expansion up to 8th order is 1.18 fm in cascade mode, reduced to 0.83 fm in SM-EoS mode.
- In cascade mode, the radii in longitudinal, outward, and sideward directions change by 2.2 fm, 0.6 fm, and 0.52 fm respectively when Edgeworth expansion is applied; in SM-EoS mode, the changes are 1.45 fm, 0.51 fm, and 0.38 fm respectively.
- At \(E_b=2\) A GeV, varying the \(\Delta(1232)\) width from \(1/2\Gamma_0\) to \(2\Gamma_0\) modifies HBT radii at large \(k_T\) but cannot reproduce the experimental \(R_O/R_S\) ratio.
- The mass-dependent resonance lifetime treatment increases HBT radii at small \(k_T\) and low beam energies compared to the mass-independent treatment, but leaves the \(R_O/R_S\) ratio unchanged.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous UrQMD-based HBT studies (Li 2007, Li 2008) that identified the "HBT time-related puzzle" at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies, where cascade-mode calculations overpredicted the \(R_O/R_S\) ratio. It directly compares the effects of resonance decay dynamics (varying \(\Delta(1232)\) lifetimes) against mean-field potentials (SM-EoS from Isse 2005) as competing explanations. The finding that only the soft equation of state resolves the puzzle supports the conclusion from Li (2007) that early-stage interactions (potentials) are key, and it enables future work on non-Gaussian effects and vector meson broadening at higher SPS and RHIC energies, as well as studies of non-identical particle correlations.
