# In-medium vector mesons and low mass lepton pairs from heavy ion collisions

> The rho and omega meson self-energy at finite temperature and baryon density have been analysed for an exhaustive set of mesonic and baryonic loops in the real time formulation of thermal field theory. The large enhancement of spectral strength below the nominal rho mass is seen to cause a substantial enhancement in dilepton pair yield in this mass region. The integrated yield after space-time evolution using relativistic hydrodynamics with quark gluon plasma in the initial state leads to a very good agreement with the experimental data from In-In collisions obtained by the NA60 collaboration.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the large enhancement of spectral strength below the nominal rho mass, arising from both mesonic and baryonic loops in the self-energy, causes a substantial enhancement in dilepton pair yield in this mass region, and that after space-time evolution using relativistic hydrodynamics with quark gluon plasma in the initial state, the integrated yield leads to a very good agreement with the experimental data from In-In collisions obtained by the NA60 collaboration. The hedge is that this agreement depends on including baryonic loops (including contributions from antibaryons) which produce the required enhancement, whereas earlier approaches using a different baryon self-energy treatment do not produce sufficient enhancement to explain the data in the range 0.35 to 0.65 GeV.

## Method

The authors computed the rho and omega meson self-energies at finite temperature and baryon density using the real time formulation of thermal field theory, evaluating an exhaustive set of one-loop diagrams involving mesons (pi-pi, pi-omega, pi-h1, pi-a1) and baryons (nucleon and 4-star N* and Delta resonances up to spin 3/2). The imaginary part of the retarded self-energy was obtained from the Landau cut contribution (arising from scattering with medium particles) rather than the unitary cut, and the spectral functions were then used to compute the dilepton emission rate via vector meson dominance. The space-time evolution of the fireball was modeled using relativistic hydrodynamics with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and azimuthally symmetric transverse expansion, assuming a quark gluon plasma initial state transitioning to hadronic matter at T_c = 175 MeV, with the initial temperature constrained by hadron multiplicity.

## Key numbers

* Initial energy density taken as 4.5 GeV/fm^3 corresponding to a thermalisation time tau_i = 0.7 fm for In-In collisions at 17.3 AGeV
* QGP to hadronic matter transition temperature T_c = 175 MeV
* Freeze-out temperature T_f = 120 MeV
* Surface thickness parameter delta = 0.5 fm for the initial energy density profile
* Continuum threshold parameters omega_0 = 1.3 GeV for rho and 1.1 GeV for omega, with delta = 0.2 GeV for both
* The approach using baryon self-energy following the method of Eletsky et al. (dashed curve) does not produce the required enhancement to explain the data in the range 0.35 to 0.65 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the rho meson self-energy in hot mesonic matter (Ghosh et al., 2010) and baryonic matter (Ghosh et al., 2011) by applying the same formalism to the omega meson and comparing the full integrated dilepton yield with NA60 experimental data. The authors note that earlier calculations using the approach of Eletsky et al. (2001) do not produce sufficient enhancement to explain the data, whereas the present treatment including full relativistic baryon propagators and contributions from both baryons and antibaryons achieves good agreement. This enables more reliable extraction of in-medium vector meson properties from dilepton measurements and provides a benchmark for future studies at RHIC and LHC energies.
