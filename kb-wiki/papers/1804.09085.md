# On parametrised cold dense matter equation of state inference

> Constraining the equation of state of cold dense matter in compact stars is a major science goal for observing programmes being conducted using X-ray, radio, and gravitational wave telescopes. We discuss Bayesian hierarchical inference of parametrised dense matter equations of state. In particular we generalise and examine two inference paradigms from the literature: (i) direct posterior equation of state parameter estimation, conditioned on observations of a set of rotating compact stars; and (ii) indirect parameter estimation, via transformation of an intermediary joint posterior distribution of exterior spacetime parameters (such as gravitational masses and coordinate equatorial radii). We conclude that the former paradigm is not only tractable for large-scale analyses, but is principled and flexible from a Bayesian perspective whilst the latter paradigm is not. The thematic problem of Bayesian prior definition emerges as the crux of the difference between these paradigms. The second paradigm should in general only be considered as an ill-defined approach to the problem of utilising archival posterior constraints on exterior spacetime parameters; we advocate for an alternative approach whereby such information is repurposed as an approximative likelihood function. We also discuss why conditioning on a piecewise-polytropic equation of state model - currently standard in the field of dense matter study - can easily violate conditions required for transformation of a probability density distribution between spaces of exterior (spacetime) and interior (source matter) parameters.

## Key claim

The paper argues that direct Bayesian hierarchical inference of equation of state parameters (the Interior-Prior paradigm) is principled, tractable, and flexible, while the alternative indirect paradigm that transforms an intermediary posterior distribution of exterior spacetime parameters into an equation of state posterior is ill-defined and should generally be avoided, except when archival posterior constraints are repurposed as an approximative likelihood function.

## Method

The authors formally define and contrast two Bayesian inference paradigms for parametrised cold dense matter equations of state: the Interior-Prior paradigm, where a prior is defined on interior parameters (equation of state parameters, central densities, and rotation frequencies) and posterior sampling proceeds directly conditioned on observational data; and the Exterior-Prior paradigm, where an intermediary posterior distribution of exterior spacetime parameters (such as gravitational masses and coordinate equatorial radii) is first computed and then transformed into a posterior on equation of state parameters. They examine the mathematical conditions required for valid probability density transformations between these parameter spaces, particularly highlighting how conditioning on a piecewise-polytropic equation of state model can violate the necessary conditions for such transformations.

## Key numbers

* The paper identifies that for a fixed equation of state, equilibrium solutions to the Einstein field equations form a two-parameter family naturally understood in terms of central energy density ρ and coordinate rotation frequency Ω.
* The Hartle-Thorne perturbative solution is discussed as a pragmatic analytic exterior solution valid at second-order in a small dimensionless rotation frequency, yielding exterior parameters including rotationally perturbed gravitational mass M, coordinate equatorial radius Req, angular momentum J, mass quadrupole moment Q, and boundary coordinate ellipticity e.
* The authors note that a continuous set of stable non-rotating interior solutions parametrised solely by central energy density ρ for a fixed equation of state maps to a unique curve M(R; θ), where M is the exterior Schwarzschild gravitational mass and R is the circumferential stellar radius.

## Context

This work generalises and examines the Bayesian inference framework of Ozel (2009), which was based on post-processing an intermediary joint posterior distribution of exterior spacetime parameters, and extends the later work of Steiner (2010), Steiner, Lattimer and Brown (2013), Lattimer and Steiner (2014), Ozel (2016), Nattila (2016), Raithel (2017), and Alsing (2017), who addressed probabilistic expression of knowledge conditioned on observational data. The discussion complements that of Lackey (2015) by providing a framework that can be merged with the Interior-Prior paradigm for synergistic multi-messenger constraints, helping bridge the gap between gravitational wave and high-energy electromagnetic equation of state inference communities. The paper enables principled large-scale collaborative analyses combining X-ray, radio, and gravitational wave observations to constrain the dense matter equation of state.
