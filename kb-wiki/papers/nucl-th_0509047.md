# Helicity amplitudes and electromagnetic decays of hyperon resonances

> We present results for the helicity amplitudes of the lowest-lying hyperon resonances Y*, computed within the framework of the Bonn constituent-quark model, which is based on the Bethe-Salpeter approach. The seven parameters entering the model were fitted to the best known baryon masses. Accordingly, the results for the helicity amplitudes are genuine predictions. Some hyperon resonances are seen to couple more strongly to a virtual photon with finite Q^2 than to a real photon. Other Y*'s, such as the S_{01}(1670) Lambda resonance or the S_{11}(1620) Sigma resonance, couple very strongly to real photons. We present a qualitative argument for predicting the behaviour of the helicity asymmetries of baryon resonances at high Q^2.

## Key claim

The helicity amplitudes of the lowest-lying hyperon resonances are genuine predictions from the Bonn constituent-quark model, with the seven model parameters fitted exclusively to baryon masses rather than to electromagnetic data; some resonances couple more strongly to virtual photons at finite \(Q^2\) than to real photons, while others such as the \(S_{01}(1670)\) \(\Lambda\) resonance or the \(S_{11}(1620)\) \(\Sigma\) resonance couple very strongly to real photons.

## Method

The authors compute helicity amplitudes for electromagnetic decays of hyperon resonances (\(Y^* \to \gamma^{(*)} Y\)) within the Bonn constituent-quark model, which is based on the Bethe-Salpeter approach with instantaneous interactions. The three-quark bound-state Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are obtained by solving the Salpeter equation using a confinement potential and the 't Hooft instanton-induced interaction, and the current matrix elements are evaluated at lowest order with the photon coupling to a point-like constituent quark. The helicity amplitudes \(A_{1/2}\), \(A_{3/2}\), and \(C_{1/2}\) are then extracted from the current matrix elements for transitions between excited and ground-state hyperons.

## Key numbers

- The helicity amplitudes are computed for \(0 \leq Q^2 \leq 6\) GeV\(^2\) for the lowest-lying \(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma\) resonances.
- The \(S_{01}(1670)\) \(\Lambda\) resonance and the \(S_{11}(1620)\) \(\Sigma\) resonance are identified as coupling very strongly to real photons (\(Q^2 = 0\)).
- The electromagnetic decay width formula uses \(\alpha = e^2/(4\pi) \simeq 1/137\) and the photon three-momentum \(|\mathbf{q}| = (m^{*2} - m^2)/(2m^{*})\) in the rest frame of the initial resonance.
- The computed mass spectrum for \(\Lambda^*\) resonances is compared with the PDG (2004) listing, covering states with spins up to \(J = 13/2\) and masses up to 3000 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Bonn constituent-quark model, previously applied to mesons and nonstrange baryons (Koll 2000, Merten 2002), to hyperon resonances, providing the first parameter-free predictions for their \(Q^2\)-dependent helicity amplitudes. Prior theoretical studies of hyperon resonance electromagnetic properties were restricted to static properties (e.g., decay widths) and low-lying states, using nonrelativistic quark models, bag models (Darewych and Kaxiras 1984), chiral bag models (Umino and Myhrer 1993), lattice QCD (Leinweber 1993), heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (Butler 1993), the bound-state soliton model (Schat 1995), the Skyrme model (Abada and Haberichter), and the chiral constituent quark model (Wagner). The results enable improved isobar-model descriptions of kaon electroproduction processes such as \(p(e,e'K^+)Y\) and \(p(\gamma,K^+)Y\), where the \(\gamma^*\)-\(Y^{(*)}\) coupling in the \(u\)-channel is a major source of theoretical uncertainty.
