# Does deuteron-induced fission on actinide nuclei prevails over the breakup at low incident energies?

> An analysis of the $^{231}$Pa$(d,3n)$$^{230}$U reaction excitation function at energies around the Coulomb barrier has taken into account the pre-equilibrium and compound-nucleus cross sections corrected for the deuteron-breakup decrease of the total reaction cross section, as well as the inelastic breakup enhancement. The analysis reveals the dominance of the deuteron breakup mechanism unlike a former assessment in this respect of the deuteron-induced fission process.

# Does deuteron-induced fission on actinide nuclei prevails over the breakup at low incident energies?

## Key claim
Deuteron breakup, not deuteron-induced fission, is the dominant reaction mechanism for deuterons on actinide nuclei at energies around the Coulomb barrier, contrary to a prior assessment by Morgenstern et al. that fission was the dominant decay channel. The claim is conditional on the use of empirical parameterizations for breakup fractions and a specific deuteron optical potential (Han et al., 2006) that yields larger total reaction cross sections than the default TALYS potential.

## Method
The authors analyzed the measured excitation function of the 231Pa(d,3n)230U reaction between 11.2 and 19.9 MeV using the TALYS-1.2 code with pre-equilibrium and compound-nucleus models. They applied a reduction factor (1 − σBU/σR) to account for the decrease of the total reaction cross section due to deuteron breakup, and added an inelastic breakup enhancement term calculated by convolving the measured 231Pa(p,2n)230U cross section ratio with a Gaussian distribution of breakup-proton energies from the Kalbach (2008) systematics.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy: Bd = 2.224 MeV
- Elastic breakup cross section for deuterons on 232Th at E = 15 MeV: σEB = 119 mb, corresponding to fEB = 0.164, used for 231Pa over the whole incident energy range
- For deuteron incident energies above ~13 MeV, the total nucleon-emission breakup cross sections from both the Avrigeanu (2009) and Kalbach (2003) parameterizations are around ~50% of the deuteron total reaction cross section
- The measured fission probability for 231Pa(d,pf) at 15 MeV was found lower than 40%
- The (d,3n) cross section calculated without breakup effects overestimated data; after including breakup reduction, data were underestimated by a factor up to 3 at E ~ 20 MeV

## Context
This work extends the systematic analysis of deuteron breakup effects by Avrigeanu (2009, 2010, 2011) and Bem (2009) to actinide targets, specifically 231Pa. It contradicts the earlier conclusion of Morgenstern et al. (the experimental group that measured the (d,3n) data) that fission dominates over breakup. The analysis enables improved predictions for deuteron-induced reactions relevant to fusion technology, nuclear medicine, and the surrogate nuclear reaction method, where (d,xf) reactions are used as surrogates for (n,f) reactions. The authors note that only the internal surrogate ratio method (Jandel et al., 2009) was shown to be valid in the presence of deuteron breakup without assuming specific breakup mechanisms.
