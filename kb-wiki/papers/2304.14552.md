# Superfast Quarks in Deuterium

> An extension to our previous study on Nuclear Parton Distribution Functions (nPDFs) using Light-Front Holographic Quantum Chromodynamics (LFHQCD) is presented. We focus on applying the effects of nucleon motion inside the nucleus (Fermi motion/smearing) to deuterium, extending our nPDFs (and hence the DIS $F_2$ structure function for deuterium, $F_2^D$) to the superfast, $x > 1$, region. We utilize four different deuteron wavefunctions (AV18, NijmI, NijmII, Nijm93) in this study. We find that our model, with no additional new parameters, is in excellent agreement with deuterium EMC ratio data obtained from the BONuS experiment. Looking beyond conventional nuclear physics, and in anticipation of ongoing 12 GeV experiments at Jefferson Lab, we use a LFHQCD ansatz to predict the contributions of an exotic six-quark state to $F_2^D$ in the superfast region. Our results are that the effects of using other potentials are about the same magnitude as six-quark effects - both have small effects in $x < 1$, but have significant contributions at $x > 1$.

# Superfast Quarks in Deuterium

## Key claim
The authors extend their previous Light-Front Holographic QCD (LFHQCD) model for nuclear parton distribution functions to deuterium in the superfast (x > 1) region, finding that the model, with no additional free parameters, agrees well with BONuS experiment data for the deuterium EMC ratio. The effects of using different nucleon-nucleon potentials (AV18, NijmI, NijmII, Nijm93) are comparable in magnitude to the predicted contributions from an exotic six-quark state, with both having small effects at x < 1 but significant contributions at x > 1.

## Method
The authors use a light-cone convolution model that connects the deuteron structure function F2^D to bound-nucleon structure functions via a light-cone density matrix derived from non-relativistic deuteron wavefunctions (AV18, NijmI, NijmII, Nijm93). The bound-nucleon structure functions are obtained from the authors' previous nLFHQCD model, which incorporates medium modifications through a virtuality-dependent parameter δr_N/A(α,k⊥) = -η V_N/A(α,k⊥) θ(1-α) with η = 0.4 ± 0.1. For the six-quark contribution, they construct a LFHQCD ansatz for a six-quark hadronic state PDF, using q_6(x/2,Q^2) with the same universal scale √λ = 0.548 GeV, and add it to the convolution result weighted by a six-quark probability P_6q.

## Key numbers
- The matching scale between LFHQCD and pQCD is Q_0 = 1.06 ± 0.15 GeV.
- The flavor-independent parameter a = 0.531 ± 0.037 in the function q_τ(x,Q^2_o).
- The constant η in the virtuality ansatz is taken as 0.4 ± 0.1.
- The convolution model nLFHQCD result for the deuterium EMC ratio gives χ^2 = 1.03 when compared to BONuS data at Q^2 = 1.12 GeV^2.
- Simply applying Fermi smearing to free nucleon structure functions yields χ^2 = 1.18 for the same data.
- Differences between results using different nucleon-nucleon potentials (AV18 vs Nijmegen) are as large as 50% in the x > 1 region.
- The six-quark probability upper bound is P_6q = 0.15%, motivated by a previous study of the b_1 structure function (Miller, 2013).
- With P_6q = 0.15%, the six-quark contribution enhances F2^D by more than 25% for x > 1.6 at Q^2 = 1.12 GeV^2.
- Results are also presented at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2 (within reach of 12 GeV JLab experiments) using DGLAP evolution.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous nLFHQCD study (Kim and Miller, 2022) from the EMC region (0.3 ≤ x ≤ 0.7) into the superfast (x > 1) region for deuterium, using the light-cone convolution formalism developed by Frankfurt and Strikman (1981, 1988). The model reproduces BONuS experiment data (Fenker, 2008; CLAS Collaboration, 2011, 2014; Griffioen, 2015) and predicts six-quark contributions using a LFHQCD ansatz adapted from Gutsche et al. (2016). The predictions are intended for testing against upcoming 12 GeV Jefferson Lab experiments (Arrington, 2006; Hen, 2014), where deviations could indicate new physics in the superfast region.
