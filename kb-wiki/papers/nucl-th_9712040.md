# Nuclear transparency in quasielastic A(e,e'p): intranuclear cascade versus eikonal approximation

> The problem of nuclear propagation through the nuclear medium in quasielastic A(e,e'p) reactions is discussed in the kinematic range 1<Q^2<7 (GeV/c)^2. The coefficient of nuclear transparency is calculated for each Q^2 in the framework of the intranuclear cascade model (INC) and of the eikonal approximation (EA). The predictions of both models are in good agreement with each other and with experimental data recently obtained at SLAC, BATES and TJNAF. The EA gives an explanation of the Q^2 behaviour of the transparency coefficient as a kinematic effect related to the superposition of contributions from each target shell.

# Nuclear transparency in quasielastic A(e,e'p): intranuclear cascade versus eikonal approximation

## Key claim

The observed Q^2 dependence of nuclear transparency in quasielastic A(e,e'p) reactions can be explained as a kinematic effect related to the superposition of contributions from each target shell, without invoking color transparency, provided that the range of explored missing momenta changes with Q^2 according to the experimental setup.

## Method

The authors calculated nuclear transparency using two independent theoretical approaches: the intranuclear cascade model (INC), a quasiclassical numerical solution of the multiple scattering series that includes Pauli blocking and directly implements detector acceptances; and the eikonal approximation (EA), based on the Glauber method with a Woods-Saxon optical potential, which contains explicit information about the target shell structure. Both models were applied to quasielastic A(e,e'p) reactions in the kinematic range 1 < Q^2 < 7 (GeV/c)^2 for carbon, iron, gold, and calcium targets, and compared with experimental data from SLAC, BATES, and TJNAF.

## Key numbers

- The INC model reproduces experimental data for carbon, iron, and gold targets across Q^2 from 1 to 7 (GeV/c)^2 without free parameters
- For carbon at Q^2 = 1.04 (GeV/c)^2, E_e = 2.015 GeV, the transparency T_INC is approximately 0.6 to 0.7 for proton angles 34 degrees <= theta_p <= 54 degrees
- For gold at Q^2 = 1.04 (GeV/c)^2, E_e = 2.015 GeV, the transparency T_INC is approximately 0.25 to 0.35 for proton angles 34 degrees <= theta_p <= 56 degrees
- The EA calculation for carbon uses W = 50 * p'/1400 MeV, which reproduces the damping observed in the NE18 experiment for 12C at p' approximately q = 1.4 GeV/c
- The ratio R = N_resc / N_dir of indirect to direct protons reaching the detector is always small under NE18 experimental conditions at Q^2 = 1.04 and 6.77 (GeV/c)^2

## Context

This work extends the conventional understanding of nuclear transparency in quasielastic A(e,e'p) reactions, previously studied experimentally by the NE18 collaboration at SLAC (1995) and at TJNAF, and theoretically within the Glauber model by Nikolaev et al. (1994-1996). The authors show that the INC model and EA give results in good agreement with each other and with data, contradicting earlier expectations from Nikolaev et al. that transparency should increase with transverse missing momentum. The analysis enables future searches for color transparency by establishing that conventional mechanisms (Pauli blocking, shell structure effects) can account for the observed Q^2 dependence, meaning that any onset of color transparency would need to be identified as a deviation from these baseline predictions.
