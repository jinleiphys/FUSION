# Functional medium-dependence of the nonrelativistic optical model potential

> By examining the structure in momentum and coordinate space of a two-body interaction spherically symmetric in its local coordinate, we demonstrate that it can be disentangled into two distinctive contributions. One of them is a medium-independent and momentum-conserving term, whereas the other is functionally --and exclusively-- proportional to the radial derivative of the reduced matrix element. As example, this exact result was applied to the unabridged optical potential in momentum space, leading to an explicit separation between the medium-free and medium-dependent contributions. The latter does not depend on the strength of the reduced effective interaction but only on its variations with respect to the density. The modulation of radial derivatives of the density enhances the effect in the surface and suppresses it in the saturated volume. The generality of this result may prove to be useful for the study of surface-sensitive phenomena.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the nonrelativistic optical model potential can be separated exactly into a medium-independent, momentum-conserving term and a medium-dependent term that is functionally proportional to the radial derivative of the reduced effective interaction, with the medium dependence enhanced in the nuclear surface and suppressed in the saturated volume.

## Method

The authors examine the structure of a two-body effective interaction that is spherically symmetric in its local coordinate, applying a Wigner transform to separate the free-space and medium-dependent contributions. They then apply this "asymptotic separation" to the unabridged optical potential in momentum space, expressing it as the sum of a term depending only on the free-space t matrix and a term depending on the gradient of the reduced g matrix. The analysis uses the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone reaction matrix as the effective interaction and evaluates the medium sensitivity through the amplitude Gamma(Z';E,Z), which accounts for the Fermi average of the gradient of the effective interaction.

## Key numbers

- The profile function Phi_1(t) = j_1(t)/t has its peak value of 1/3 at the origin and is mainly contained within the region t less than approximately 4
- For Pb-208, medium effects from partial derivative g / partial derivative rho become dominant in the region between 5.5 fm and 9 fm, where the local Fermi momentum k_F varies between 0.3 fm^-1 and 1.2 fm^-1
- The Gamma amplitude was evaluated on-shell at forward angles for nucleon energies of 65 MeV, 100 MeV, 200 MeV, and 300 MeV, using the density of Pb-208
- The strongest medium dependence occurs for the real pn amplitude at 65 MeV, while the weakest occurs for the absorptive pp amplitude

## Context

This work extends the momentum-space folding optical potential approach developed by Arellano, Brieva and Love (ABL) (Arellano et al., 1995, 2002), which uses Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone g matrices folded with the target ground-state local density. The result provides a rigorous derivation showing that the ABL approach can be obtained as a limit case of the unabridged optical potential under simplifying assumptions (Slater approximation for the mixed density and weak Q-dependence of the g matrix). The authors contrast their result with the rho(partial derivative/partial derivative rho) rearrangement term proposed by Cheon et al. (1984) for inelastic scattering, noting that their own result for elastic scattering produces surface-enhanced rather than volume-uniform medium effects. The work enables future studies of surface-sensitive phenomena, particularly for rare isotope beams at intermediate energies (30A-100A MeV).
