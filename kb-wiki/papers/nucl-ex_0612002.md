# Elastic Scattering and Total Reaction Cross Section for the 6He + 27Al System

> The elastic scattering of the radioactive halo nucleus 6He on 27Al target was measured at four energies close to the Coulomb barrier using the RIBRAS (Radioactive Ion Beams in Brazil) facility. The Sao Paulo Potential(SPP) was used and its diffuseness and imaginary strength were adjusted to fit the elastic scattering angular distributions. Reaction cross-sections were extracted from the optical model fits. The reduced reaction cross-sections of 6He on 27Al are similar to those for stable, weakly bound projectiles as {6,7}Li, 9Be and larger than stable, tightly bound projectile as 16O on 27Al.

## Key claim

The reduced total reaction cross sections for the halo nucleus 6He on 27Al are similar to those for stable weakly bound projectiles (6,7Li, 9Be) within error bars, indicating that for light systems the halo effect on reaction cross sections may be much smaller than observed in heavier systems; however, the authors note that higher quality data are needed to observe the effect of the Borromean nature of 6He.

## Method

Elastic scattering angular distributions for 6He + 27Al were measured at four energies near the Coulomb barrier using the RIBRAS facility, with a 6He secondary beam produced via the 9Be(7Li,6He)10B reaction. The Sao Paulo Potential (SPP) was used as the optical model, with the diffuseness of the nuclear density and the normalization of the imaginary potential adjusted to fit the angular distributions. Reaction cross sections were extracted from the best-fit optical potentials, and reduced reaction cross sections were compared with those for 9Be, 6Li, 7Li, and 16O on the same 27Al target, analyzed using the same SPP procedure.

## Key numbers

- 6He secondary beam energies in the middle of the 27Al target: 9.5, 11.0, 12.0, and 13.4 MeV; Coulomb barrier for 6He + 27Al is about 8.0 MeV in the laboratory system
- Best-fit diffuseness of the 6He matter distribution: a = 0.56(2) fm; deconvoluted point nucleon distribution diffuseness: 0.52(2) fm
- Best imaginary normalization factors N_I: 0.8(5), 0.8(4), 0.6(4), and 0.7(5) for energies 9.5, 11.0, 12.0, and 13.4 MeV respectively
- Estimated nuclear break-up cross section for 6He + 27Al: about 150 mb at E_lab = 10 MeV (dipole contribution ~30 mb, quadrupole contribution ~120 mb)
- 6He secondary beam intensity: about 0.7 x 10^5 pps per microampere equivalent of 7Li primary beam
- Energy resolution (FWHM) with thick Al target: about 400 keV

## Context

This work presents the first experimental results from the RIBRAS facility at Sao Paulo and extends studies of halo-induced reactions from heavy targets (e.g., 6He + 209Bi by Aguilera (2000) and 6He + 238U by Trotta (2000)) to a light target (27Al). The comparison with stable weakly bound projectiles (6,7Li, 9Be) and tightly bound 16O on the same target follows the methodology of Gomes (2004) for the 64Zn target. The results enable future investigations of whether the Borromean nature of 6He affects reaction cross sections at low energies, and the estimated nuclear break-up cross section provides a benchmark for Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) calculations by Moro (2006).
