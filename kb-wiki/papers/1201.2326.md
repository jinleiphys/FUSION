# Momentum-space calculation of four-boson recombination

> The system of four identical bosons with large two-boson scattering length is described using momentum-space integral equations for the four-particle transition operators. The creation of Efimov trimers via ultracold four-boson recombination is studied. The universal behavior of the recombination rate is demonstrated.

# Momentum-space calculation of four-boson recombination

## Key claim

The four-boson recombination rate for identical bosons with large two-boson scattering length exhibits universal behavior for highly excited Efimov trimers (n ≥ 3), but only when finite-range effects become negligible; for low-lying states (n ≤ 1) the results depend on the short-range details of the interaction.

## Method

The author solves the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) four-particle integral equations in momentum-space partial-wave representation, using a separable two-boson S-wave potential with two different form factors to verify universality. The four-cluster breakup amplitude at zero energy is related to the two-cluster AGS transition operators via time-reversal symmetry, and the resulting coupled integral equations in two continuous variables (k_y, k_z) are discretized by Gaussian quadrature and solved by direct matrix inversion.

## Key numbers

- Universal ratio for the deeper tetramer emergence: a_{n,1}^0 / a_n^0 = 0.4254(2) for n ≥ 3
- Universal ratio for the shallower tetramer emergence: a_{n,2}^0 / a_n^0 = 0.9125(2) for n ≥ 3
- Universal ratio of successive trimer zero-energy points: a_{n+1}^0 / a_n^0 → e^{π/s_0} ≈ 22.6944 for large n, with s_0 ≈ 1.00624
- Universal product a_n^0 √(m b_n^u) / ħ = -1.5077(1) for n ≥ 3
- For n=0: a_{0,1}^0 / a_0^0 = 0.4435, a_{0,2}^0 / a_0^0 = 0.8841, a_1^0 / a_0^0 = 17.752
- For n=1: a_{1,1}^0 / a_1^0 = 0.4412, a_{1,2}^0 / a_1^0 = 0.9162, a_2^0 / a_1^0 = 21.935

## Context

This work extends the author's previous momentum-space AGS calculations for bosonic atom-trimer scattering (Deltuva, 2010) and dimer-dimer scattering (Deltuva, 2011) to the four-boson recombination process. It improves upon coordinate-space adiabatic hyperspherical calculations by Stecher et al. (2009) and Mehta et al. (2009), which were limited to n=1 and could not resolve the universal intersections of shallow tetramers with atom-trimer thresholds. The results are consistent with experimental data on ultracold ¹³³Cs atoms by Ferlaino et al. (2009), though those data show larger deviations from universality due to deep non-Efimov-like bound states. The work enables future experimental tests in a truly universal regime and constitutes a step toward solving momentum-space four-body scattering equations above the breakup threshold.
