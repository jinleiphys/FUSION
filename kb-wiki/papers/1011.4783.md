# QCD under magnetic field: chiral magnetic effect at heavy ion collisions

> In this talk, we report our recent studies for the chiral magnetic effect, which signals the P- and CP-violations at the heavy ion collisions. We compute the electric current and its correlations, induced by the external magnetic field, inside the hot and dense QCD matter created in the heavy ion collisions. For this purpose, we employ the instanton-liquid model, modified by the Harrington-Shepard caloron at finite T. We observe that the chiral magnetic effect current and its correlations increase with respect to the magnetic field, whereas they decrease as functions of T, due to the diluting instanton ensemble. It turns out that the numerical results are in good agreement with those from the model-independent analyses and lattice QCD simulations. We also reproduce the charge separations, observed in the STAR experiment, qualitatively well, considering the simplified Lienard-Wiechert potential, screening and size effects.

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic effect (CME) current and its correlations increase with the external magnetic field but decrease as temperature increases due to the diluting instanton ensemble, and the numerical results show qualitatively good agreement with model-independent analyses and lattice QCD simulations, though the charge separations observed by the STAR experiment are only reproduced "qualitatively well" after tuning model parameters and including screening and size effects.

## Method

The authors employ the instanton-liquid model modified by the Harrington-Shepard caloron to describe the QCD vacuum at finite temperature, and include the external magnetic field via the linear Schwinger method. They compute the vacuum expectation value of the electric current (CME current) and its correlations by functionally differentiating the effective action derived from the instanton vacuum with P- and CP-violation, then extend these quantities to finite temperature using the fermionic Matsubara formula. For the charge separation observable, they incorporate a simplified Lienard-Wiechert potential for the magnetic field spatial configuration, screening effects, and a size effect factor of 1/N^2_nucl where N_nucl is the atomic mass of the projectile nucleus.

## Key numbers

- CME current increases linearly with magnetic field B_0, as given by the expression ⟨j⟩ ≈ (1/2π²) μ_χ B_0
- Ratio of transverse to longitudinal CME current components: |j_⟂/j_∥| = (3/2)(|A_2,1|/|A_4,3|)δ, with δ ≪ 1 indicating longitudinal component dominates
- Charge separations computed at T = 200 MeV for Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions as functions of centrality
- Size effect gives factor 1/N²_nucl, where N_nucl is the atomic mass of the projectile nucleus

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of the chiral magnetic effect, originally proposed by Kharzeev (2004) and Voloshin (2004), by using the instanton-liquid model to compute CME currents and correlations in hot QCD matter. The results are compared with lattice QCD simulations by Buividovich (2009, 2010) and with charge separation data from the STAR experiment at RHIC. The approach enables future calculations of additional physical quantities related to the CME and provides a framework for understanding how the instanton ensemble dilution at high temperature suppresses the effect.
