# Period of the gamma-ray staggering in the 150Gd superdeformed region

> It has been previously proposed to explain gamma-ray staggerings in the deexcitation of some superdeformed bands in the $^{150}$Gd region in terms of a coupling between global rotation and intrinsic vortical modes. The observed 4$\hbar$ period for the phenomenon is suggested from our microscopic Routhian calculations using the Skyrme SkM* effective interaction.

## Key claim

The observed 4ħ period of γ-ray staggering in superdeformed bands of the ¹⁵⁰Gd region is explained by pairing correlations fine-tuning the ratio of angular momentum I to Kelvin circulation J to approximately 2, rather than the ratio being intrinsically close to 2 in the pure Hartree-Fock solutions.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations using the Skyrme SkM* effective interaction, solving a generalized Routhian variational problem with two Lagrange multipliers (Ω for global rotation and ω for intrinsic vortical modes). They computed the dynamical moment of inertia 𝒥⁽²⁾ and the Kelvin circulation operator J for angular momentum values from I=40ħ to I=66ħ, then varied J at fixed I to study the effect of pairing-like correlations modeled through intrinsic vortical excitations.

## Key numbers

- The yrast value of Kelvin circulation as a function of angular momentum follows J_yrast(I) ≃ 0.8 I + 1.0 ħ, giving a ratio I/J far from 2 around I=50ħ
- For I=50ħ, the Hartree-Fock dynamical moment of inertia is well below the experimental value; pairing correlations raise 𝒥⁽²⁾ to approximately 90 ħ² MeV⁻¹
- When constraining the intrinsic vortical mode to reproduce 𝒥⁽²⁾ ∼ 90 ħ² MeV⁻¹, the Kelvin circulation reaches ∼ 25 ħ, yielding I/J close to 2 and thus the 4ħ staggering period
- The two-point derivative for 𝒥⁽²⁾ used calculations differing by ΔħΩ ∼ 0.07 keV around I=50ħ

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal (Samsoen, Quentin, Mikhailov, 1997) that γ-ray staggering arises from coupling between global rotation and intrinsic vortical modes (Chandrasekhar S-ellipsoids). It addresses the specific question of why the staggering period is 4ħ, which earlier harmonic oscillator mean-field calculations (Samsoen, Quentin, Mikhailov, 1997) had suggested would require hyperdeformation rather than the observed superdeformation. The results show that pairing correlations, which act similarly to intrinsic vortical modes as shown by Durand (1998), raise the dynamical moment of inertia from its Hartree-Fock value to the experimental range, thereby tuning the I/J ratio to the required value of 2. The authors note that fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations are needed to confirm this indirect estimate.
