# Rescaling of Nuclear Structure Functions

> It is shown that nucleonic structure functions are $x-$ and $Q^{2}-$rescaled in nuclei. The $x-$rescaling accounts for nuclear effects in the case of exact scaling, while the $Q^{2}-$rescaling is responsible for a corresponding modification of quantum corrections. This result is obtained in the leading order for all flavour combinations and connects the two known models for the EMC-effect. Electroproduction and gluonic nuclear structure functions are calculated.

## Key claim
Nucleonic structure functions in nuclei undergo both an $x$-rescaling (accounting for nuclear effects in the tree approximation for intrinsic partons) and a $Q^{2}$-rescaling (accounting for modification of quantum corrections), and these two types of rescaling are self-complementary and must both be taken into account, contrary to earlier claims that they are equivalent or that one alone suffices.

## Method
The author uses the operator product expansion (OPE) and renormalization group (RG) techniques to derive the combined $(x,Q^{2})$-rescaling of nuclear structure functions in leading order for all flavour combinations. The bound nucleon four-momentum distribution is taken from $(e,e'p)$ experiments, with a Fermi momentum cutoff of 260 MeV and a separation energy of approximately 35 MeV for medium nuclei. The free nucleon structure function parametrization from Duke and Owens (Set 1) is used, and the rescaling parameter $\xi$ is derived from the bound nucleon off-shellness via $\sigma = (p^2/m_N^2)^{1/2}$.

## Key numbers
- Fermi momentum cutoff for medium nuclei: $|\vec{p}| \leq p_F = 260$ MeV
- Nucleon separation energy for medium nuclei: $\epsilon \approx 35$ MeV
- Average rescaling parameter $\sigma$ for iron nucleus: $\sigma \approx 0.94$ (from Eq. 9), compared to $\sigma' \approx 0.87$ from the $Q^{2}$-rescaling model of Jaffe, Close, Roberts, and Ross, i.e. $(1-\sigma') \approx 2(1-\sigma)$
- The rescaling parameter $\xi$ in leading order: $\xi = \sigma^{-2\alpha_s(m_N^2)/\alpha_s(Q^2)}$, independent of operator spin and flavour

## Context
This work extends the $Q^{2}$-rescaling model of Jaffe, Close, Roberts, and Ross (1984) and the $x$-rescaling model of Akulinichev, Kulagin, and Vagradov (1985) by proving that both types of rescaling must be combined, confirming the ansatz of Ciofi degli Atti and Treleani (1988). The author shows that the $x$-rescaling alone cannot explain the EMC-effect, contrary to some earlier conclusions, and that the combined rescaling can explain the bulk of the EMC-effect. The calculated nuclear structure functions are applied to estimate the nuclear effectiveness $\alpha$ in bottomonium production in proton-nucleus collisions at 800 GeV, where the EMC-effect explains only a small fraction of the observed bottomonium suppression but is not negligible.
