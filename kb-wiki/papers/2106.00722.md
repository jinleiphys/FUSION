# Electroproduction of heavy vector mesons using holographic QCD: from near threshold to high energy regimes

> We develop a non-perturbative analysis of the electro-production of heavy vector mesons ($ϕ$, $J/Ψ$) from threshold to high energy. We use the holographic construction with bulk confinement enforced through a soft wall. Using Witten diagrams, we evaluate the pertinent cross sections for heavy vector mesons ($ϕ$, $J/Ψ$) production and study their dependence on both the incoming virtual photon polarization as well as the outgoing polarization of the heavy meson. Our results for $J/Ψ$ electro-production compares well with the available HERA data at low and intermediate $Q^2$, and for a wide range of momentum transfer. We also predict the quasi-real electro-production of $J/Ψ$ near threshold.

## Key claim

The paper develops a non-perturbative holographic QCD framework for electroproduction of heavy vector mesons (phi, J/Psi) that works from near threshold to high energy, with the caveat that the scalar dilaton exchange decouples from the diffractive process, leaving only tensor graviton exchange as the dominant mechanism.

## Method

The authors use a holographic soft-wall AdS/QCD construction with bulk confinement, computing Witten diagrams for diffractive electroproduction of heavy vector mesons on a bulk Dirac fermion representing the nucleon. The amplitude involves exchange of a tensor graviton (dual to a Reggeized 2++ glueball) near threshold, with higher spin exchanges resummed via Reggeization at high energies, using bulk-to-boundary propagators for the virtual photon and normalizable wave functions for the vector mesons.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon mass radius from tensor glueball exchange is computed as <r^2_Mass> = (0.57 fm)^2, derived from the gravitational form factor A(t) with fixed kappa_N = 0.350 GeV
- The tensor form factor A(t) is well parametrized by a dipole: A(t)/A(0) ≈ 1/(1 - t/1.124^2)^2
- The radial Regge spectrum for tensor and scalar glueballs is m^2_{T,S} = 8 kappa_N^2 (n + 2)
- The nucleon mass trajectory gives m_N(0) ≈ 990 MeV and rho mass trajectory gives m_rho(0) ≈ 700 MeV from the relation m^2(n) = 4 kappa_N^2 (n + tau_N - 1)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous holographic photoproduction analysis of heavy mesons (Mamo and Zahed, 2019) to the electroproduction case with virtual photons, and builds on earlier holographic Pomeron formulations by Brower (2006) and Stoffers (2012). The results enable comparison with existing HERA data for J/Psi electroproduction at low and intermediate Q^2, and provide predictions for quasi-real electroproduction of J/Psi near threshold that can be tested by ongoing Jefferson Lab experiments from the GlueX and J/Psi-007 collaborations.
