# Cascade emission of $γ$-quanta by highly excited nuclei

> The thermodynamics of the electromagnetic radiation from heated nuclei is developed on basis of the Landau theory of a Fermi liquid [1]. The case of non-spherical nuclei is considered, in which the quasiparticle energy spectrum is not distorted by the residual interactions that affect the thermodynamic behavior of the system. The number of quanta per cascade and mean-square fluctuation are calculated; the $γ$-quantum spectrum of the whole cascade is also obtained. The formulae can be used to determine the entropy and temperature of the initial nucleus by various methods. The effective nucleon (quasiparticle) mass in nuclear matter is determined by comparison with the experimental data. The region of validity of the theory and some possibilities of its extension on the basis of new experiments are discussed.

## Key claim

The thermodynamics of gamma-ray cascades from highly excited non-spherical nuclei can be described using Landau Fermi-liquid theory, where the quasiparticle energy spectrum is not distorted by residual interactions; the theory's validity is limited to cases where the number of cascade quanta satisfies ν² ≫ 4, and the softest part of the spectrum is excluded.

## Method

The authors derived the probability distribution for gamma-quantum emission from a heated nucleus using Fermi-liquid theory, assuming electric-dipole radiation from quasiparticles reflecting off the nuclear surface. They calculated the number of quanta per cascade, the mean-square fluctuation, and the full cascade spectrum, then compared these predictions with experimental data from thermal neutron capture on ten heavy non-spherical nuclei (samarium through rhenium). The temperature of each compound nucleus was extracted from the position of the maximum in the energy-weighted spectrum, and the effective nucleon mass was determined from the entropy via the specific heat relation.

## Key numbers

- Average energy of a single emitted quantum: \(\bar{\eps} \simeq 4.91 T\) (equation 6)
- Mean-square fluctuation of the number of cascade quanta: \(\overline{(\Delta\nu)^2} \simeq 0.0433 S\) (equation 12)
- Relation between fluctuation and number of quanta: \(\sqrt{\overline{(\Delta\nu)^2}} \simeq 0.461\sqrt{\nu}\) (equation 13)
- Energy at the maximum of the cascade spectrum: \(\eps_{max} \simeq 2.89 T\) (equation 21)
- Quantum energy averaged over the full cascade spectrum: \(\overline{\overline{\eps}} \simeq 2.45 T\) (equation 19)
- Temperatures extracted for ten compound nuclei range from 0.66 MeV (Ta\(^{182}\)) to 1.00 MeV (Er\(^{168}\)), with excitation energies from 5.46 MeV (W\(^{187}\)) to 8.53 MeV (Gd\(^{156}\))
- Effective nucleon mass ratio: \(m^*/m_n = 0.87 \pm 0.04\) (equation 33), averaged over six nuclei with ν = 3.5–4.1
- Individual \(m^*/m_n\) values range from 0.51 (Dy\(^{165}\) and W\(^{187}\)) to 1.01 (Sm\(^{150}\))

## Context

This work extends the Landau theory of a Fermi liquid (Landau, 1957) to the electromagnetic radiation of heated non-spherical nuclei, providing a thermodynamic alternative to the detailed-balance method used for evaporation neutron spectra (Weisskopf, 1937). The theory enables determination of nuclear entropy and temperature from gamma-cascade measurements, and the effective mass result (0.87 ± 0.04) can be compared with values from other nuclear matter studies. The authors identify the need for experiments at higher excitation energies (above the 8 MeV thermal neutron capture energy) and with anticoincidence techniques to avoid neutron evaporation, which would extend the region where the criterion ν² ≫ 4 is satisfied and allow refinement of the effective mass determination.
