# Imaginary-time theory for triple-alpha reaction rate

> Using imaginary-time theory, it is shown that the triple-alpha reaction rate can be reliably calculated without the need to solve scattering problems involving three charged particles. The calculated reaction rate is found to agree well with the empirical NACRE rate, which is widely adopted in stellar evolution calculations. The reason for this is explained using $R$-matrix theory. Extremely slow convergence is found to occur when a coupled-channel expansion is introduced, which helps to explain the very different reaction rates obtained using different theoretical approaches.

# Imaginary-time theory for triple-alpha reaction rate

## Key claim

The triple-alpha reaction rate calculated using imaginary-time theory agrees well with the empirical NACRE rate without any enhancement at low temperatures, but the calculated rate is smaller than the NACRE rate over the entire temperature region by a factor of up to six times.

## Method

The authors solve the imaginary-time evolution equation $-\partial \Psi(\beta)/\partial \beta = H \Psi(\beta)$ starting from an initial wave function $\Psi(\beta=0)=M^{\dagger}_{\lambda\mu}\Phi_f$, where $\beta = 1/k_B T$ is inverse temperature, using a Taylor expansion method with step size $\Delta \beta = 0.004$ MeV$^{-1}$. The three-alpha Hamiltonian uses the Ali-Bodmer potential for alpha-alpha interactions (modified to reproduce the $^8$Be ground state resonance at 92.08 keV) plus a three-body potential adjusted to place the Hoyle state at 379.8 keV above the three-alpha threshold, with radial grids up to $R_{\rm max}=r_{\rm max}=400$ fm and grid spacing 0.5 fm.

## Key numbers

- The $^8$Be ground state resonance energy is 92.08 keV above the two-alpha threshold.
- The Hoyle state ($0_2^+$ of $^{12}$C) resonance energy is 379.8 keV above the three-alpha threshold.
- At $T=0.01$ GK, the Gamow peak energy estimated from the Coulomb potential is $E_0 \simeq 43$ keV, and the calculated average energy is $\bar E = 55$ keV.
- The calculated reaction rate is smaller than the NACRE rate by a factor of up to six over the entire temperature region.
- Using a coupled-channel expansion with only 14 channels (states below the $^8$Be ground state), the reaction rate at $T=0.01$ GK is 24 orders of magnitude higher than the fully converged result.
- Even with 400 channels, the reaction rate at $T=0.01$ GK is still 13 orders of magnitude larger than the fully converged result.

## Context

This work extends the imaginary-time theory previously proposed by the same group (Yabana 2012) for radiative capture reactions, applying it specifically to the triple-alpha process where no rigorous scattering theory for three charged particles exists. The results contradict the earlier CDCC calculation by Ogata (2009) which found a rate 26 orders of magnitude larger than NACRE at $T=0.01$ GK, and help explain the large scatter among theoretical rates reported by various groups (Garrido 2011, Ishikawa 2012, Nguyen 2012, Ishikawa 2013, Nguyen 2013, Suzuki 2013). The analytic derivation using R-matrix theory (Lane 1958) provides quantum-mechanical support for the empirical NACRE formula (Angulo 1999), and the slow convergence of coupled-channel expansions explains why different theoretical approaches yield widely different rates at low temperatures.
