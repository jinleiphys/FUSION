# Studies of the Three-Body Breakup in Deuteron-Deuteron Collisions near the Quasi-Free Limit at 160 MeV

> A set of differential cross section of the three-body $^{2}$H($d$,$dp$)$n$ breakup reaction at 160 MeV deuteron beam energy are presented for 147 kinematically complete configurations near the quasi-free scattering kinematics. The experiment was performed at KVI in Groningen, the Netherlands using the BINA detector. The cross-section data have been normalized to the $^{2}$H($d$,$d$)$^{2}$H elastic scattering cross section. The data are compared to the recent single-scattering approximation (SSA) calculations for three-cluster breakup in deuteron-deuteron collisions. Confronting the SSA predictions with the experimental data shows that SSA provides the correct order of magnitude of the cross-section data. The studied energy is probably too low to meet the SSA assumptions which prevents better accuracy of the description.

## Key claim

The single-scattering approximation (SSA) calculations provide the correct order of magnitude for the differential cross sections of the three-body breakup in deuteron-deuteron collisions near the quasi-free scattering limit at 160 MeV, but the beam energy is probably too low to meet the SSA assumptions, which prevents better accuracy of the description.

## Method

The experiment was performed at KVI in Groningen using the BINA detector with a 160 MeV deuteron beam impinging on a liquid deuterium target. Differential cross sections were measured for 147 kinematically complete configurations of the 2H(d,dp)n breakup reaction near quasi-free scattering kinematics, and the data were normalized to the 2H(d,d)2H elastic scattering cross section. The results were compared to single-scattering approximation (SSA) calculations for three-cluster breakup in deuteron-deuteron collisions, including both one-term and four-term SSA predictions.

## Key numbers

- 147 kinematically complete configurations were measured near the quasi-free scattering kinematics
- Deuteron beam energy: 160 MeV
- Forward Wall covers polar angles theta in the range of 10 degrees to 35 degrees with full azimuthal coverage
- Energy resolution of the forward detectors: about 2%
- Angle reconstruction accuracy: 0.3 degrees for theta and between 0.6 degrees and 3 degrees for phi
- MWPC efficiency for weak tracks: about 98%
- Inefficiency of the Delta E detector: below 5%
- For p+d breakup, the total cross section calculated in SSA is higher than the exact calculation by 30% at 95 MeV and by 20% at 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the experimental database for four-nucleon (4N) systems, which previously consisted of limited breakup measurements at low energies (Val et al. 1972, Kluge et al. 1978, Cowley et al. 1974, Yuan et al. 1977, Wielinga et al. 1981, Myers et al. 1983) and new-generation data at 130 MeV from the same KVI group (Ramazani-Moghaddam-Arani et al. 2011, 2013). The data are compared to recent SSA calculations by Deltuva and Fonseca (2016), which represent the first theoretical estimates for the d+d system at higher energies. The results enable testing of the SSA approach and will motivate more complete four-body calculations beyond the single-scattering approximation.
