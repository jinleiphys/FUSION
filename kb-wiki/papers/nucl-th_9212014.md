# Dynamics of antibaryon-baryon annihilation in the Skyrme model

> We examine the dynamics of a baryon number zero lump in the Skyrme picture, as a model for annihilation in the $\bar{N}N$ system. We find that radiation propagates at the causal limit as a localized pulse and that the linearized theory gives a good approximation. Both of these results are contrary to findings in the Sine-Gordon model.

## Key claim

The authors find that a baryon-number-zero lump in the Skyrme model decays rapidly, with radiation propagating as a localized pulse at the causal limit, and that the linearized Skyrme equation gives a good approximation to the full nonlinear evolution, both results contrary to findings in the 1+1 Sine-Gordon model, where the lump "rings" for a long time and radiates slowly.

## Method

The authors construct a spherically symmetric "lump" with baryon number zero in the Skyrme model, using a hedgehog ansatz for the SU(2) field, and solve the classical equations of motion numerically with an implicit difference scheme. They compare the full nonlinear evolution to the linearized equation, and also perform analogous calculations in the 1+1 Sine-Gordon model for comparison. Initial conditions are chosen so that the lump has twice the energy of a single skyrmion, modeling an antinucleon-nucleon system at threshold.

## Key numbers

- The initial lump in the Skyrme model has parameters h=49.6 and a=2 (in scaled Skyrme units), giving twice the energy of a single skyrmion.
- The initial lump in the Sine-Gordon model has parameters h=39 and a=2, also giving twice the energy of a B=1 soliton.
- In the Skyrme model, the pion mass squared in scaled units is 0.27, which accounts for the coherent propagation observed.
- The total number of pions expected from the decay is small compared to 2 GeV/m_pi ≈ 14, as borne out by experiment.

## Context

This work extends the comprehensive numerical calculations of Sommermann et al. (1991) on antiskyrmion-skyrmion collisions, which found rapid baryon number disappearance but offered no explanation for the dynamics. The authors construct a simplified spherically symmetric system to extract dynamical insight, and find that the linearized Skyrme equation describes the decay well, a result that contrasts sharply with the behavior of the Sine-Gordon model, which has often been used as a toy model for skyrmions following Skyrme and Perring (1961). The findings suggest that annihilation in the antinucleon-nucleon system near threshold can be modeled by rapid decay into a small number of relativistic, non-interacting pions, rather than a thermodynamic limit.
