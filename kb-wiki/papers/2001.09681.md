# Evolution of Octupole Deformation in Radium Nuclei from Coulomb Excitation of Radioactive $^{222}$Ra and $^{228}$Ra Beams

> There is sparse direct experimental evidence that atomic nuclei can exhibit stable pear shapes arising from strong octupole correlations. In order to investigate the nature of octupole collectivity in radium isotopes, electric octupole ($E3$) matrix elements have been determined for transitions in $^{222,228}$Ra nuclei using the method of sub-barrier, multi-step Coulomb excitation. Beams of the radioactive radium isotopes were provided by the HIE-ISOLDE facility at CERN. The observed pattern of $E$3 matrix elements for different nuclear transitions is explained by describing $^{222}$Ra as pear-shaped with stable octupole deformation, while $^{228}$Ra behaves like an octupole vibrator.

## Key claim

The observed pattern of electric octupole (E3) matrix elements for different nuclear transitions is explained by describing 222Ra as pear-shaped with stable octupole deformation, while 228Ra behaves like an octupole vibrator; the distinction relies on the pattern of E3 matrix elements across multiple transitions, not solely on the magnitude of a single matrix element.

## Method

Sub-barrier, multi-step Coulomb excitation was performed using radioactive 222Ra and 228Ra beams at 4.31 MeV/nucleon from HIE-ISOLDE, incident on 60Ni and 120Sn targets. Gamma rays were detected with the Miniball array of 24 segmented HPGe detectors in coincidence with scattered particles detected in a segmented silicon detector. The yields of gamma-ray transitions were measured for four ranges of recoil angle and fitted using the GOSIA code to extract E2, E3, and E1 matrix elements, with E3 matrix elements between higher-lying states coupled to lower-lying ones assuming the rotational model.

## Key numbers

- 222Ra: intrinsic octupole moment Q3 from the 0+ to 3- transition = 3030 ± 240 efm3
- 228Ra: intrinsic octupole moment Q3 from the 0+ to 3- transition = 2300 ± 400 efm3
- 222Ra: intrinsic quadrupole moment Q2 from the 2+ to 4+ transition = 590 ± 30 efm2
- 228Ra: intrinsic quadrupole moment Q2 from the 2+ to 4+ transition = 770 ± 40 efm2
- 222Ra: Q3 from rotational model fit = 3120 ± 190 efm3
- 228Ra: Q3 from rotational model fit = 2230 ± 290 efm3
- 222Ra: Q2 from rotational model fit = 578 ± 18 efm2
- 228Ra: Q2 from rotational model fit = 798 ± 21 efm2
- 228Ra: the 2+ to 3- E3 matrix element is -0.06 (+0.23/-0.16) eb3/2, consistent with zero
- 228Ra: the 4+ to 1- E3 matrix element is 0.4 (+0.7/-1.1) eb3/2, consistent with zero

## Context

This work extends previous Coulomb excitation studies of 224Ra and 226Ra (Gaffney 2013, Wollersheim 1993) that showed evidence for static octupole deformation, and contrasts with the octupole vibrator behavior observed in 148Nd (Ibbotson 1997). The results confirm theoretical predictions (Nazarewicz 1984, Robledo 2013, Agbemava 2016) that the boundary of stable octupole deformation lies at Z approximately 88 and N approximately 138, and that even-even radon isotopes with similar neutron numbers behave as octupole vibrators (Butler 2019). The findings are relevant for searches for permanent electric dipole moments in radium atoms that would indicate CP violation beyond the Standard Model.
