# Z=110--111 Elements and the Stability of Heavy and Superheavy Elements

> The recent discovery of isotopes with Z=110--111 suggests evidence for (1) a monopole--monopole interaction that does not appear explicitly in Nilsson--Strutinsky mass systematics, and (2) a competition between $SU(2)$ and $SU(3)$ dynamical symmetries that has been predicted for this region. Our calculations suggest that these new isotopes are near spherical, and may represent a true island of superheavy nuclei, but shifted downward in neutron number by these new physical effects.

# Z=110–111 Elements and the Stability of Heavy and Superheavy Elements

## Key claim
The newly discovered isotopes with Z=110–111 may represent the first examples of the originally conjectured island of superheavy nuclei, but shifted downward in neutron number by approximately 15–20 units relative to traditional predictions, provided that the commonly used Woods–Saxon single-particle spectrum is reliable.

## Method
The authors applied the Fermion Dynamical Symmetric Model (FDSM) to calculate nuclear masses for Z=82–126 and N=126–184, using a Hamiltonian consisting of spherical single-particle energies plus two-body interactions (monopole pairing, quadrupole pairing, and monopole–monopole interactions). The shell correction was computed as the sum of a spherical single-particle shell correction and the expectation value of two-body residue interactions, with the Fermi Gas Model describing the smooth part. Alpha decay energies and lifetimes were estimated using a one-dimensional barrier penetration model.

## Key numbers
- The r.m.s. error for all available masses above Z=82 and N=126 is approximately 0.22 MeV.
- The center of the predicted superheavy island is around Z=114 and N=164.
- The beta stability line is given by A = Z/0.390 and the fission stability line by A = Z/0.409.
- The newly discovered isotopes have Z=110 (A=269) and Z=111 (A=272), both lying on the predicted fission stability line.
- The monopole–monopole interaction parameters for SU(2) symmetry include: a = -13.75, b = -4.572, c = 0.4293, d = -4.889, e = 0.3306, f = -0.2915 (units not specified in text).
- The monopole–monopole interaction parameters for SU(3) symmetry include: a = -5.570, b = -5.790, c = 0.3713, d = -6.806, e = 0.3587, f = -0.1095 (units not specified in text).
- The predicted alpha decay energies show a maximum at Z=109 and a minimum half-life at Z=109.

## Context
This work extends the FDSM mass calculations previously reported by Wu (1987) and Han (1992) to interpret the recent experimental discoveries of elements 110 and 111 by Hofmann (1995). The predictions differ fundamentally from the traditional Nilsson–Strutinsky deformed mean field calculations by Nilsson (1969) and Greiner, which placed the superheavy island near N=184. The FDSM predicts no stable nuclides near the traditional superheavy island location, and instead suggests that elements 110–114 near N=160–165 may be spherical or deformation soft due to competition between SU(2) and SU(3) dynamical symmetries. This work enables experimental tests through further searches for isotopes in the Z=110–114 region and continued failure to find superheavy elements at their historically expected location.
