# Momentum Distribution and Contact of the Unitary Fermi gas

> We calculate the momentum distribution n(k) of the Unitary Fermi Gas using Quantum Monte Carlo calculations at finite temperature T/ε_F as well as in the ground state. At large momenta k/k_F, we find that n(k) falls off as C/k^4, in agreement with the Tan relations. From the asymptotics of n(k), we determine the contact C as a function of T/ε_F and present a comparison with theory. At low T/ε_F, we find that C increases with temperature, and we tentatively identify a maximum around T/ε_F \simeq 0.4. Our calculations are performed on lattices of spatial extent up to N_x = 14 with a particle number per unit volume of \simeq 0.03 - 0.07.

## Key claim
The paper reports a Quantum Monte Carlo calculation of the momentum distribution and contact of the Unitary Fermi Gas, finding that the contact at zero temperature is \(C/(Nk_F) \simeq 2.95 \pm 0.10\), which is lower than the value of \(\simeq 3.4\) from earlier Diffusion Monte Carlo calculations; the authors tentatively identify a maximum in the contact around \(T/\epsilon_F \simeq 0.4\), but note that finite-density effects and the need for larger lattices leave this conclusion uncertain.

## Method
The authors use an auxiliary-field Quantum Monte Carlo method on a \((3+1)\)-dimensional Euclidean space-time lattice, with a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to represent the zero-range interaction and a Hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm for global updates of the auxiliary field. The bare lattice coupling constant \(g \simeq 5.14\) for the unitary limit is determined using Lüscher’s formula, and calculations are performed for spatial lattice sizes up to \(N_x = 14\) with particle numbers per unit volume of \(\simeq 0.03\)–\(0.07\), corresponding to 40–50 particles at \(N_x = 10\) and 70–80 particles at \(N_x = 12\). The momentum distribution \(n(k)\) is extracted by averaging over angular directions and imaginary-time slices, and the contact \(C\) is obtained from the asymptotic \(C/k^4\) tail of \(n(k)\).

## Key numbers
- Ground-state contact: \(C(T=0)/(N k_F) \simeq 2.95 \pm 0.10\).
- Maximum contact: \(C_{\mathrm{max}} \simeq 3.4\) at \(T/\epsilon_F \simeq 0.4\) (tentative).
- Asymptotic \(k^{-4}\) regime sets in at \(k \simeq 2 k_F\) at the lowest temperatures.
- Lattice coupling constant for unitary limit: \(g \simeq 5.14\).
- Imaginary-time lattice spacing: \(\tau \simeq 0.05\).
- Particle number per unit volume: \(\simeq 0.03\)–\(0.07\).
- Lattice spatial extents: \(N_x = 10, 12, 14\).
- Finite-temperature results accurate to \(\simeq 7\%\); zero-temperature results accurate to \(\leq 5\%\).

## Context
This work extends prior Quantum Monte Carlo studies of the Unitary Fermi Gas, notably the Diffusion Monte Carlo calculations of Lobo (2006) and Stefano (2009) which found \(C/(Nk_F) \simeq 3.4\) at zero temperature, and the analytic predictions of Yu (2009) for the temperature dependence of the contact. The results are compared with \(t\)-matrix calculations by Palestini (2010) and Enss (2010), the virial expansion of Hu (2010), and the diagrammatic Monte Carlo result of Goulko (2010). The paper provides the first fully non-perturbative calculation of the momentum distribution free of uncontrolled approximations, and enables future comparisons with ultracold atom experiments via the virial expansion and Local Density Approximation.
