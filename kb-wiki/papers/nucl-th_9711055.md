# Chemical freezeout in relativistic A+A collisions: is it close to the QGP?

> Preliminary experimental data for particle number ratios in the collisions of Au+Au at the BNL AGS (11$A$ GeV/$c$) and Pb+Pb at the CERN SPS (160$A$ GeV/$c$) are analyzed in a thermodynamically consistent hadron gas model with excluded volume. Large values of temperature, T=140--185 MeV, and baryonic chemical potential, $μ_b=590$--270 MeV, close to the boundary of the quark-gluon plasma phase are found from fitting the data. This seems to indicate that the energy density at the chemical freezeout is tremendous which would be indeed the case for the point-like hadrons. However, a self-consistent treatment of the van der Waals excluded volume reveals much smaller energy densities which are very far below a lowest limit estimate of the quark-gluon plasma energy density.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out parameters extracted from fitting particle ratios in Au+Au (AGS) and Pb+Pb (SPS) collisions yield temperatures and baryonic chemical potentials close to the quark-gluon plasma boundary, but the energy densities calculated with a thermodynamically consistent van der Waals excluded volume hadron gas model are far below the lowest estimate of QGP energy density, contradicting the impression given by ideal gas models.

## Method

The authors fit preliminary experimental particle number ratios from Au+Au at 11A GeV/c (AGS) and Pb+Pb at 160A GeV/c (SPS) using a grand-canonical hadron gas model with van der Waals excluded volume repulsion, where the pressure is given by a self-consistent equation that modifies chemical potentials. They include all known hadron resonances up to 2 GeV mass with subsequent decays, assume zero strangeness, and allow different hard-core radii for pions versus other hadrons to improve fits.

## Key numbers

- AGS freeze-out: T ≈ 140 MeV, μ_b ≈ 590 MeV
- SPS freeze-out: T ≈ 185 MeV, μ_b ≈ 270 MeV
- Ideal gas energy density at AGS: ε^{id} ≈ 0.72 GeV/fm³
- VDW energy density at AGS: ε^{VDW} = 0.25–0.08 GeV/fm³ (range depending on hard-core radii)
- Ideal gas energy density at SPS: ε^{id} ≈ 1.58 GeV/fm³
- VDW energy density at SPS: ε^{VDW} = 0.43–0.10 GeV/fm³ (range depending on hard-core radii)
- Ideal gas baryon density at AGS: n_b^{id} ≈ 0.40 fm⁻³
- VDW baryon density at AGS: n_b^{VDW} = 0.13–0.04 fm⁻³
- Ideal gas baryon density at SPS: n_b^{id} ≈ 0.40 fm⁻³
- VDW baryon density at SPS: n_b^{VDW} = 0.10–0.04 fm⁻³
- Hard-core radii that fit both AGS and SPS simultaneously: r_π ≈ 0.62 fm, r ≈ 0.8 fm
- With r_π = 0, best-fit r = 0.50 fm (AGS) and r = 0.46 fm (SPS)
- Pion-to-other-hadron ratio increase from r_π < r: factor 2.0 for AGS, 2.6 for SPS (direct); 25% (AGS) and 33% (SPS) for total ratios

## Context

This work extends the thermal model analyses of Braun-Munzinger et al. (1995) and Stachel (1996) by showing that the large T and μ_b values they found near the QGP boundary do not imply high energy density when a thermodynamically consistent excluded volume treatment is used. The authors contrast their self-consistent van der Waals approach with the ad hoc excluded volume treatments used in prior thermal model fits (Refs. 7–10), which leave particle ratios unchanged from ideal gas values. The results enable a consistent picture where chemical freeze-out occurs at energy densities far below the QGP phase, consistent with a large latent heat in the hadron-to-QGP transition.
