# Spin-orbit coupling in nuclei and realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials

> We analyze the spin-orbit coupling term in the nuclear energy density functional in terms of a zero-range NN-contact interaction and finite-range contributions from two-pion exchange. We show that the strength of the spin-orbit contact interaction as extracted from high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials is in perfect agreement with that of phenomenological Skyrme forces employed in non-relativistic nuclear structure calculations. Additional long-range contributions from chiral two-pion exchange turn out to be relatively small. These explicitly density-dependent contributions reduce the ratio of the isovector to the isoscalar spin-orbit strength significantly below the Skyrme value 1/3. We perform a similar analysis for the strength function of the $(\vec \nabla ρ)^2$-term and find values not far from those of phenomenological Skyrme parameterizations.

## Key claim

The strength of the short-range spin-orbit contact interaction extracted from high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials (which fit NN-scattering data) agrees quantitatively with the phenomenological Skyrme spin-orbit parameter used in non-relativistic nuclear structure calculations, with the caveat that additional long-range contributions from chiral two-pion exchange reduce the isovector-to-isoscalar spin-orbit strength ratio significantly below the Skyrme value of 1/3.

## Method

The author decomposes the spin-orbit term in the nuclear energy density functional into a zero-range NN-contact interaction (characterized by the low-energy constant C5) and finite-range contributions from chiral two-pion exchange. Using the density-matrix expansion of Negele and Vautherin, the isoscalar and isovector spin-orbit strength functions F_so(kf) and G_so(kf) are computed diagrammatically from Hartree and Fock diagrams, with the contact contribution extracted from spectroscopic low-energy constants of high-precision NN-potentials via the linear combination C5 = (1/16π)[2C(³P0)+3C(³P1)-5C(³P2)].

## Key numbers

- The phenomenological Skyrme spin-orbit strength parameter 3W0/4 averages approximately 90 MeV fm⁵ across various Skyrme forces (SIII: 90.0, SkM: 97.5, SkP: 75.0, Sly4-7: 93.2, MSk1-6: 87.6, SkI1-5: 92.7 MeV fm⁵)
- For high-precision NN-potentials, the short-range spin-orbit strength 3C5/8 equals: CD-Bonn 89.6, Nijm-II 87.7, AV-18 88.9 MeV fm⁵ (all marked with asterisks)
- At nuclear matter saturation density ρ0 ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³, the ratio G_so(kf0)/F_so(kf0) is about 1/8, substantially less than the Skyrme value 1/3
- The contact contribution to the (∇ρ)² strength function from high-precision potentials gives mean value F_∇^(ct)(kf) = 81.1 MeV fm⁵ (CD-Bonn: 82.4, Nijm-II: 78.4, AV-18: 82.5 MeV fm⁵)
- One-pion exchange contributes negligibly to spin-orbit strength: at ρ0/2 ≈ 0.08 fm⁻³, F_so^(1π)(kf) ≈ -0.79 MeV fm⁵ (less than 1% of the empirical 90 MeV fm⁵)

## Context

This work extends the connection between free NN-scattering observables and nuclear structure phenomenology, building on the Skyrme force parameterizations (Reinhard, 1991) and the spectroscopic low-energy constants of Epelbaum et al. (2003). The analysis shows that the spin-orbit interaction needed for nuclear structure is consistent with that from realistic NN-potentials, while the two-pion exchange contributions (which reduce the isovector spin-orbit strength) are favorable for describing isotope shifts in Pb nuclei as noted by Reinhard and Flocard (1995). The method enables a microscopic foundation for the spin-orbit part of nuclear energy density functionals and suggests that the isospin dependence of the spin-orbit interaction is a property where pion-exchange effects play a role.
