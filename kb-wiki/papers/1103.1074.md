# An effective theory for jet propagation in dense QCD matter: jet broadening and medium-induced bremsstrahlung

> Two effects, jet broadening and gluon bremsstrahlung induced by the propagation of a highly energetic quark in dense QCD matter, are reconsidered from effective theory point of view. We modify the standard Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) Lagrangian to include Glauber modes, which are needed to implement the interactions between the medium and the collinear fields. We derive the Feynman rules for this Lagrangian and show that it is invariant under soft and collinear gauge transformations. We find that the newly constructed theory SCET$_{\rm G}$ recovers exactly the general result for the transverse momentum broadening of jets. In the limit where the radiated gluons are significantly less energetic than the parent quark, we obtain a jet energy-loss kernel identical to the one discussed in the reaction operator approach to parton propagation in matter. In the framework of SCET$_{\rm G}$ we present results for the fully-differential bremsstrahlung spectrum for both the incoherent and the Landau-Pomeranchunk-Migdal suppressed regimes beyond the soft-gluon approximation. Gauge invariance of the physics results is demonstrated explicitly by performing the calculations in both the light-cone and covariant $R_ξ$ gauges. We also show how the process-dependent medium-induced radiative corrections factorize from the jet production cross section on the example of the quark jets considered here.

## Key claim

The authors construct a modified Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET$_{\rm G}$) that includes Glauber modes to describe jet propagation in dense QCD matter, and they demonstrate that this effective theory recovers the general result for transverse momentum broadening and, in the soft-gluon limit, yields a jet energy-loss kernel identical to the reaction operator approach, but only under the condition that the static scattering center approximation is used (which they show is within 15% of the exact result even for low energy jets).

## Method

The authors modify the standard SCET Lagrangian by adding Glauber modes with momentum scaling $q \sim [\lambda^2, \lambda^2, \bm{\lambda}]$ as an effective potential term, analogous to the NRQCD potential. They derive Feynman rules for the coupling of collinear quarks and gluons to Glauber gluons in three gauges (covariant $R_\xi$, light-cone $A^+=0$, and light-cone $A^-=0$) for three types of sources (collinear, static, and soft). They then compute the transverse momentum broadening and the fully-differential bremsstrahlung spectrum for both incoherent and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppressed regimes, performing calculations in both light-cone and covariant $R_\xi$ gauges to demonstrate gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- The integrated scattering cross section difference between a recoil mass of 1000 GeV and 1 GeV is approximately 13% for RHIC (incident parton energy 10 GeV) and approximately 2% for LHC (incident parton energy 100 GeV)
- More than 90% of the scattering cross section comes from configurations where the jet is not deflected more than 15% from its original direction of propagation
- The static scattering center approximation is within 15% of the exact result for low energy jets
- The typical jet transverse energies at RHIC are in the range 10 GeV to 50 GeV, and at LHC extend to several hundred GeV
- The mass of nucleons in cold nuclear matter is $m_N = 0.94$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the Soft Collinear Effective Theory framework, previously applied to semi-inclusive deeply inelastic scattering by Idilbi and Majumder (2008), to a general description of jet propagation in dense QCD matter including both collisional and radiative processes. The authors compare their results to prior calculations of parton broadening by Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev (2002) and Qiu and Vitev (2003), and to energy loss calculations by Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, and Schiff (2000), Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev (2000), Wang and Guo (2001), and Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2002). The construction enables improved accuracy beyond the soft-gluon approximation and demonstrates factorization of medium-induced radiative corrections from the hard jet production cross section.
