# Roles of Quarks in Strong and Weak YN Interactions

> The short range parts of the strong and weak hyperon-nucleon interactions are studied with quark substructure of the baryons taken into account. A summary of the quark cluster model calculations of the baryon-baryon interactions is presented. It is pointed out that the spin-flavor symmetry structure determines the qualitative behaviors of the short-range YN interactions. We also show that the spin-orbit YN forces give characteristic behaviors, which are distinct from the meson exchange forces. Weak decays of $Λ$ in hypernuclei are described by the direct quark mechanism. We find that the neutron induced nonmesonic decay is enhanced due to the direct quark transition. Importance of the $ΔI=3/2$ decay amplitudes is emphasized both for the nonmesonic weak decays and the $π^+$ emission in the $Λ$ decay.

# Roles of Quarks in Strong and Weak YN Interactions

## Key claim

The short-range parts of both strong and weak hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions exhibit characteristic features arising from quark substructure that are distinct from meson-exchange descriptions; in particular, the antisymmetric spin-orbit (ALS) forces in YN systems are predicted to be as strong as the symmetric spin-orbit (SLS) forces in the quark cluster model, contrary to the weak ALS forces expected from meson exchange, and the direct quark mechanism for nonmesonic weak decay of Λ in hypernuclei enhances the neutron-induced decay rate and introduces significant ΔI=3/2 amplitudes.

## Method

The authors employ a quark cluster model with SU(3) constituent quarks, one-gluon exchange interactions, and confining forces to compute baryon-baryon interactions, including hyperon-nucleon systems. For weak decays, they evaluate an effective weak Hamiltonian for quarks (including one-loop perturbative QCD corrections to the W-exchange diagram) in six-quark wave functions of two-baryon systems to derive a direct quark transition potential for ΛN → NN, and compare it with conventional one-pion exchange (OPE) and other meson-exchange potentials. The soft-pion theorem is used to relate π⁺ emission from hypernuclei to ΔI=3/2 two-body transition amplitudes.

## Context

This work extends quark cluster model studies of baryon-baryon interactions (Oka and Yazaki, 1980s) to hyperon-nucleon systems, building on earlier findings that quark exchange forces produce state-dependent repulsion in NN and YN channels. The authors contrast their quark-exchange ALS predictions with meson-exchange models (Nijmegen group, Bonn potential, Jülich group) where ALS forces are much weaker. The direct quark mechanism for nonmesonic weak decay addresses discrepancies between meson-exchange theory and experimental data for the neutron/proton decay ratio in light hypernuclei, and the soft-pion analysis of π⁺ emission provides a new probe of ΔI=3/2 weak amplitudes. The results motivate further experimental studies of ΣN scattering and π⁺ decay branches in hypernuclei to distinguish quark substructure effects from meson-exchange descriptions.
