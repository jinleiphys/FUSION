# Twist-2 matching of transverse momentum dependent distributions

> We systematically study the large-$q_T$ (or small-$b$) matching of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) distributions to the twist-2 integrated parton distributions. Performing operator product expansion for a generic TMD operator at the next-to-leading order (NLO) we found the complete set of TMD distributions that match twist-2. These are unpolarized, helicity, transversity, pretzelosity and linearly polarized gluon distributions. The NLO matching coefficients for these distributions are presented. The pretzelosity matching coefficient is zero at the presented order, however, it is evident that it is non-zero in the following orders. This result offers a natural explanation of the small value of pretzelosity found in phenomenological fits. We also demonstrate that the cancellation of rapidity divergences by the leading order soft factor imposes the necessary requirement on the Lorentz structure of TMD operators, which is supported only by the TMD distributions of leading dynamical twist. Additionally, this requirement puts restrictions on the $γ_5$-definition in the dimensional regularization.

# Twist-2 matching of transverse momentum dependent distributions

## Key claim

The complete set of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) distributions that match to twist-2 integrated parton distributions at next-to-leading order (NLO) via operator product expansion are unpolarized, helicity, transversity, pretzelosity, and linearly polarized gluon distributions; however, the pretzelosity matching coefficient is zero at NLO and only becomes non-zero at higher orders, which offers a natural explanation for the small value of pretzelosity found in phenomenological fits.

## Method

The authors performed an operator product expansion for generic TMD quark and gluon operators near the light-cone at NLO, using dimensional regularization combined with the delta-regularization scheme for rapidity divergences. They projected out the specific Lorentz structures corresponding to each TMD distribution and extracted the matching coefficients as the epsilon-finite parts of the renormalized TMD operator matrix elements, evaluating both in the 't Hooft-Veltman-Breitenlohner-Maison (HVBM) scheme and a modified Larin scheme (Larin+) for gamma-5.

## Key numbers

- The NLO matching coefficient for the pretzelosity distribution is proportional to epsilon-squared: delta-perp C_{q<-q} = -4 a_s C_F B^epsilon Gamma(-epsilon) x-bar epsilon^2, yielding zero at finite epsilon (NLO).
- The helicity matching coefficient Delta C_{q<-q} at small-b contains a term delta(bar x)(-L_mu^2 + 2 L_mu l_zeta - zeta_2) + O(a_s^2), where zeta_2 = pi^2/6.
- The transversity matching coefficient delta C_{q<-q} at small-b contains delta(bar x)(-L_mu^2 + 2 L_mu l_zeta - zeta_2) + O(a_s^2).
- The linearly polarized gluon matching coefficients are delta^L C_{g<-g} = -4 a_s C_A (bar x/x) + O(a_s^2) and delta^L C_{g<-q} = -4 a_s C_F (bar x/x) + O(a_s^2).
- The finite renormalization constant Z_{qq}^5 for helicity TMD matching is delta(bar x) + 2 a_s C_F B^epsilon Gamma(-epsilon)(1 - epsilon - (1+epsilon) H_sch) bar x, where H_sch depends on the gamma-5 scheme.

## Context

This work extends previous NLO calculations of TMD matching for unpolarized distributions (Echevarria 2016, Gehrmann 2014) and for helicity, transversity, and linearly polarized gluon distributions (Bacchetta 2013, Echevarria 2015) by providing a uniform treatment across all twist-2 TMD distributions including pretzelosity, which had been overlooked. The authors demonstrate that the cancellation of rapidity divergences by the leading-order soft factor imposes conditions on the Lorentz structure of TMD operators that define the leading dynamical twist, and they show that the Larin scheme for gamma-5 is inapplicable for TMD calculations, proposing a modified Larin+ scheme instead. This work enables the extension of polarized TMD matching to NNLO and provides a theoretical basis for the small numerical value of pretzelosity observed in HERMES and COMPASS measurements of sin(3phi_h - phi_S) asymmetries (Lefky 2014).
