# An Approach to the Computation of Few/Many-Body Multichannel Reactions

> A method to calculate reactions in quantum mechanics is outlined. It is advantageous, in particular, in problems with many open channels of various nature i.e. when energy is not low. In the method there is no need to specify reaction channels in a dynamics calculation. These channels come into play at merely the kinematics level and only after a dynamics calculation is done. This calculation is of the bound--state type while continuum spectrum states never enter the game.

## Key claim

The paper outlines a method for calculating multichannel reactions that avoids specifying reaction channels during the dynamics calculation and never computes continuum spectrum states; the method is advantageous specifically when many open channels of various nature exist (i.e., when energy is not low), but it has not yet been applied to reactions induced by strong interaction, only to perturbation-induced (electromagnetic or weak) processes.

## Method

The approach expresses reaction observables as quadratures of "response-like" functions of the form \(R(E)=\sum_n\langle Q'|\Psi_n\rangle\langle\Psi_n|Q\rangle\delta(E-E_n)+\sum\!\!\!\!\!\!\int d\gamma\langle Q'|\Psi_\gamma\rangle\langle\Psi_\gamma|Q\rangle\delta(E-E_\gamma)\). An integral transform of this function is performed using a kernel \(K(\sigma,E)\), yielding a "continuum sum rule" \(\Phi(\sigma)=\langle Q'|K(\sigma,H)|Q\rangle\) that is evaluated via a bound-state type calculation (either by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian on a finite basis or by solving an inhomogeneous Schrödinger-like equation). The resulting integral equation is then inverted to recover the response function, from which reaction cross sections are obtained.

## Key numbers

- The approach was successfully applied in nuclear reaction problems with \(3\le A\le7\) and recently for \(A=12\) and \(16\) using NN or NN+NNN forces.
- In a benchmark comparison for \(^3\)H photoabsorption total cross section using the Argonne V18 NN interaction and Urbana IX NNN potential, the LIT results and Faddeev results showed complete agreement when only the NN force was retained, and agreement everywhere except the peak region when the NNN force was added.
- For the \(^3\)He(\(\gamma\),p)d reaction in the threshold region calculated with the MT NN potential, complete agreement was observed between LIT results and direct continuum wave function calculations.
- A resonance in \(^4\)He with a width of about 200 keV was reproduced with reasonable accuracy in the framework of the present approach.

## Context

This work extends the integral transform approach introduced by Efros (1985) for general-type reactions and by Efros (1980) for inclusive perturbation-induced reactions using the Stieltjes transform, and later the Lorentz transform introduced by Efros, Leidemann, and Orlandini (1994). The method contrasts with conventional approaches that deal explicitly with continuum wave functions, which become impractical for \(A>3\) when many channels are open. The approach enables calculations for heavier nuclei (up to \(A=16\) in recent work) and can incorporate existing bound-state techniques for solving the multiparticle scattering problem as reviewed by Carbone (year not specified in text).
