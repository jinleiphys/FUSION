# Progress in the determination of the $J/ψ-π$ cross section

> Improving previous calculations, we compute the $J/ψπ\to {charmed mesons}$ cross section using QCD sum rules. Our sum rules for the $J/ψπ\to \bar{D} D^*$, $D \bar{D}^*$, ${\bar D}^* D^*$ and ${\bar D} D$ hadronic matrix elements are constructed by using vaccum-pion correlation functions, and we work up to twist-4 in the soft-pion limit. Our results suggest that, using meson exchange models is perfectly acceptable, provided that they include form factors and that they respect chiral symmetry. After doing a thermal average we get $<σ^{πJ/ψ} v>\sim 0.3$ mb at $T=150\MeV$.

## Key claim

The authors compute the $J/\psi\pi$ dissociation cross section using QCD sum rules with a three-point function and OPE expansion up to twist-4, and find that the cross section grows monotonically with center-of-mass energy but not as steeply near threshold as previous meson-exchange model calculations, provided chiral symmetry is respected. The abstract's claim that "using meson exchange models is perfectly acceptable" is conditioned on those models including form factors and respecting chiral symmetry.

## Method

The authors construct QCD sum rules for the hadronic matrix elements of $J/\psi\pi \to \bar{D}D^*$, $D\bar{D}^*$, $\bar{D}^*D^*$, and $\bar{D}D$ using vacuum-pion correlation functions in a three-point function approach, working up to twist-4 in the soft-pion limit. They perform a single Borel transformation on all external momenta and extract the hadronic amplitudes by fitting the QCD sum rule results to a quadratic form and extrapolating to zero Borel mass. The cross sections are then computed from these amplitudes and thermally averaged over a Bose-Einstein distribution of pions.

## Context

This work improves upon the previous QCD sum rule calculation by the same group (Duraes et al., 2002) which used a four-point function at the pion pole, by including twist-3 and twist-4 contributions in a three-point function approach. The results confirm the smooth threshold behavior found in the chiral meson exchange model of Nielsen et al. (2001) and contradict the steep rise near threshold seen in earlier meson exchange models by Haglin (2000), Lin and Ko (2000), and others. The calculation enables more reliable estimates of $J/\psi$ dissociation in hadron gas at temperatures of 100-300 MeV, relevant for interpreting charmonium suppression as a quark-gluon plasma signature.
