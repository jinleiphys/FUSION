# Global-Vector Representation of the Angular Motion of Few-Particle Systems II

> The angular motion of a few-body system is described with global vectors which depend on the positions of the particles. The previous study using a single global vector is extended to make it possible to describe both natural and unnatural parity states. Numerical examples include three- and four-nucleon systems interacting via nucleon-nucleon potentials of AV8 type and a 3$α$ system with a nonlocal $αα$ potential. The results using the explicitly correlated Gaussian basis with the global vectors are shown to be in good agreement with those of other methods. A unique role of the unnatural parity component, caused by the tensor force, is clarified in the $0^-_1$ state of $^4$He. Two-particle correlation function is calculated in the coordinate and momentum spaces to show different characteristics of the interactions employed.

## Key claim

The paper extends the global-vector representation (GVR) to describe both natural and unnatural parity states in few-body systems, demonstrating that the explicitly correlated Gaussian basis with two global vectors achieves accuracy comparable to state-of-the-art calculations, but the method's performance depends on the specific potential model and the inclusion of unnatural parity components is essential for precise energies when tensor forces are present.

## Method

The authors use an explicitly correlated Gaussian (CG) basis with two global vectors (GVs) to represent the orbital part of the wave function, where natural parity states use one GV and unnatural parity states use two GVs. The basis parameters (matrix A and vectors u1, u2) are selected via the stochastic variational method (SVM), with random sampling of nonlinear parameters followed by refinement. Matrix elements for local and nonlocal operators are calculated using a generating function technique that relates the CG basis to exponential generating functions, enabling analytic evaluation of integrals.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: -2.242 MeV (AV8'), -2.277 MeV (G3RS), -2.202 MeV (MN)
- Deuteron D-state probability P(2,1): 5.77% (AV8'), 4.78% (G3RS)
- 3H binding energy with AV8': -7.76 MeV (GVR), -7.76 MeV (PWE), -7.767 MeV (Faddeev reference)
- 4He ground state energy with AV8': -25.08 MeV (GVR), -25.05 MeV (PWE)
- 4He ground state unnatural parity probability P(1,1): 0.36% (AV8'), 0.25% (G3RS)
- Energy gain from unnatural parity mixing in 4He: about 0.8 MeV (G3RS) and 1.6 MeV (AV8')
- 4He 0^-_1 state energy: -6.40 MeV (full calculation), compared to approximately -1 MeV without the (2,2) channel
- Coupling matrix element between (1,1) and (2,2) channels in 4He 0^-_1: -13.50 MeV
- Unnatural parity admixture in 4He 0^-_1 state: 4.5%

## Context

This work extends the single-global-vector approach of Suzuki (1995) and the correlated Gaussian method described in the book by Suzuki and Varga (1998) to handle unnatural parity states, which were previously inaccessible with a single GV. The method is tested against partial-wave expansion (PWE) calculations from the benchmark study of Kamimura (2001) and Faddeev calculations by Nogga (2002), showing excellent agreement. The results enable accurate treatment of nonlocal potentials such as the resonating group method (RGM) alpha-alpha potential, and clarify the role of tensor-force-induced unnatural parity components in states like the 0^-_1 state of 4He, where the tensor coupling between channels provides over 13 MeV of attraction.
