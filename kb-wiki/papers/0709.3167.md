# Giant Resonances using Correlated Realistic Interactions: The Case for Second RPA

> Lately we have been tackling the problem of describing nuclear collective excitations starting from correlated realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions. The latter are constructed within the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM), starting from realistic NN potentials. It has been concluded that first-order RPA with a two-body UCOM interaction is not capable, in general, of reproducing quantitatively the properties of giant resonances (GRs), due to missing higher-order configurations and long-range correlations as well as neglected three-body terms in the Hamiltonian. Here we report results on GRs obtained by employing a UCOM interaction based on the Argonne V18 potential in Second RPA (SRPA) calculations. The same interaction is used to describe the Hartree-Fock (HF) ground state and the residual interactions. We find that the inclusion of second-order configurations -- which effectively dress the underlying HF single-particle states with self-energy insertions -- produces sizable corrections. The effect appears essential for a realistic description of GRs when using the UCOM. We argue that effects of higher than second order should be negligible. Therefore, the UCOM-SRPA emerges as a promising tool for consistent calculations of collective states in closed-shell nuclei. This is an interesting development, given that SRPA can accommodate more physics than RPA (e.g., fragmentation). Remaining discrepancies due to the missing three-body terms and self-consistency issues of the present SRPA model are pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper reports that including second-order configurations via Second RPA (SRPA) produces sizable corrections to first-order RPA results for giant resonances when using a correlated realistic interaction (UCOM based on Argonne V18), and that these corrections are essential for a realistic description; however, remaining discrepancies, particularly for the isoscalar giant monopole resonance, are attributed to missing three-body terms and self-consistency issues in the present SRPA model.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations using the correlated Argonne V18 interaction (V_UCOM) to obtain the ground state and single-particle energies, then employed Second RPA (SRPA) in a harmonic-oscillator basis of 11 oscillator shells to compute the strength distributions for the isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole, and isoscalar quadrupole resonances in ^16O and ^40Ca. The SRPA equations were solved using a Lanczos procedure, with the 2p2h subspace treated in a diagonal approximation where couplings among 2p2h states were neglected.

## Key numbers

- The total first moment m1 in SRPA is smaller than in RPA by more than 20% for the nuclei studied.
- The spurious center-of-mass state appears at about 5 MeV in the SRPA calculations.
- The SRPA centroid energies are much lower than the RPA ones for all giant resonances examined.
- The agreement of SRPA results with experiment is very good for the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (IS GQR) in both ^16O and ^40Ca.
- The isovector giant dipole resonance (IV GDR) centroid energy is somewhat underestimated in SRPA.
- The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (IS GMR) energies are underestimated within SRPA.
- The convergence of the GR sum rules m0 and m1 and centroids is within about 1 MeV for the centroids with the 11-shell basis.
- The total number of eigenvalues is 10^4-10^5 for the cases presented, but less than 300 eigenstates are sufficient to describe the region of the giant resonances.

## Context

This work extends previous UCOM-RPA calculations (Papakonstantinou 2006, Papakonstantinou and Roth 2007) which found that first-order RPA with a two-body UCOM interaction could not quantitatively reproduce giant resonance properties, overestimating the energies of the IV GDR and IS GQR by several MeV. The SRPA approach addresses the missing higher-order configurations and long-range correlations that were identified as problematic. The results confirm earlier perturbation-theory findings (Roth 2006) that corrections beyond second order are small, and point toward the need for a three-body term to improve the description of the isoscalar monopole resonance. This enables future studies of giant resonance decay properties, heavier nuclei, and low-lying states within the UCOM-SRPA framework.
