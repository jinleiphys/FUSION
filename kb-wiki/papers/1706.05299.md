# In-medium bound states and pairing gap

> The propagator of two nucleons in infinite nuclear matter is evaluated by a diagonalization of the $pphh$ RPA Hamiltonian. This effective Hamiltonian is non-Hermitian and, for specific density domains and partial waves, yields pairs of complex conjugated eigenvalues representing in-medium bound states of two nucleons. The occurrence of these complex poles in the two-particle Greens function is tightly related to the well known BCS pairing approach. It is demonstrated that these complex eigenvalues and the corresponding bound state wavefunctions contain all information about the BCS gap function. This is illustrated by calculations for $^1S_0$ and $^3PF_2$ pairing gaps in neutron matter which essentially coincide with the corresponding gap functions extracted from conventional solutions of the gap equation. Differences between the bound states in the conventional BCS approach and the $pphh$ RPA are arising in the case of $^3SD_1$ channel in symmetric nuclear matter at low densities. These differences are discussed in the context of transition from BEC for quasi-deuterons to the formation of BCS pairing.

## Key claim

The complex eigenvalues and eigenfunctions of the non-Hermitian \(pphh\) RPA Hamiltonian contain all information about the BCS pairing gap function \(\Delta(k)\), not only at the Fermi surface but for all momenta, provided one uses the mean-field (independent particle) limit for the single-particle Green's function at zero temperature.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the \(pphh\) RPA Hamiltonian in a discrete stationary wave-packet (SWP) basis, using the CD-Bonn potential and kinetic-energy-only single-particle energies. They extract the complex eigenvalue \(E_b = E_0 + i\Gamma_0\) and its eigenfunction \(\psi(k)\), then reconstruct the momentum-dependent gap function via \(|\Delta(k)| \approx |\psi(k)| / |\psi(k_F)| \cdot \sqrt{(2e_k - E_0)^2 + \Gamma_0^2}\). This reconstruction is compared to direct solutions of the nonlinear BCS gap equation for neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^1S_0\) channel in neutron matter at \(k_F = 0.8\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(k_F = 1.2\) fm\(^{-1}\), the bound-state wave function \(\psi(k)\) and the characteristic function \(A\phi(k)\) (built from the conventional BCS gap) are "almost indistinguishable" (Fig. 1).
- For the \(^3PF_2\) channel in neutron matter at \(k_F = 2.5\) fm\(^{-1}\), the agreement between the reconstructed gap and the direct gap-equation solution is "perfect" (Fig. 4).
- In symmetric nuclear matter for the \(^3SD_1\) channel, a second real bound state appears below the \(hh\) threshold at \(k_F \sim 0.14\) fm\(^{-1}\); the two real states merge at a critical Fermi momentum \(k_F^C \approx 0.182\) fm\(^{-1}\), above which they become a complex-conjugate pair.
- At \(k_F = 1.2\) fm\(^{-1}\) in symmetric nuclear matter, the gap \(\Delta(k_F)\) from the imaginary part of the eigenvalue and from the BCS equation coincide (Fig. 6), and the momentum-dependent gaps also agree (Fig. 7).

## Context

This work extends the stabilization technique of Ramirez et al. (2012) and Ramirez et al. (2015), which used only the imaginary part of the complex eigenvalue to find the gap at the Fermi surface. The present paper shows that the full eigenfunction yields the momentum dependence of the gap, providing a simpler alternative to solving the nonlinear gap equation, especially for coupled channels like \(^3PF_2\). The approach also reveals a BEC–BCS transition for the \(^3SD_1\) channel in symmetric nuclear matter at very low densities, which differs from conventional BCS results and may explain the absence of strong proton–neutron pairing in finite nuclei. The method is intended as a step toward self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) calculations with dressed single-particle propagators.
