# Super-asymmetric fission of heavy nuclei induced by intermediate energy protons

> In this work we present the results for the investigation of intermediate-mass fragment (IMF) production with the proton-induced reaction at 660 MeV on 238U and 237Np target. The data were obtained with the LNR Phasotron U-400M Cyclotron at Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR), Dubna, Russia. A total of 93 isotopes, in the mass range of 30 < A < 200, were unambiguously identified with high precision. The fragment production cross sections were obtained by means of the induced-activation method in an off-line analysis. Mass-yield distributions were derived from the data and compared with the results of the simulation code CRISP for multimodal fission. A discussion of the super-asymmetric fragment production mechanism is also given.

## Key claim

The paper claims that intermediate-mass fragments (IMF) produced in 660 MeV proton-induced reactions on heavy targets (²³⁸U and ²³⁷Np) are predominantly formed through a binary fission process, specifically a super-asymmetric fission mode, rather than through multifragmentation or spallation. The authors hedge that they cannot totally exclude the possibility that other mechanisms (such as evaporation with associated spallation and emission of fragments heavier than alpha-particles) could also describe the data.

## Method

The authors measured fragment production cross sections for 93 isotopes in the mass range 30 < A < 200 using the induced-activation method with off-line gamma-ray spectroscopy, irradiating ²³⁸U and ²³⁷Np targets with 660 MeV protons from the LNR Phasotron U-400M Cyclotron at JINR, Dubna. They compared the experimental mass-yield distributions with simulations from the CRISP Monte Carlo code, which models the intranuclear cascade followed by evaporation/fission competition using the Weisskopf model for evaporation and the Bohr-Wheeler model for fission. To describe the IMF region, they added a fourth super-asymmetric fission mode (S3) to the existing multimodal random neck rupture model (MM-NRM) framework, which already included symmetric (S) and two asymmetric (S1, S2) modes.

## Key numbers

- Total fission cross section for ²³⁸U target: 1140 mb
- Total fission cross section for ²³⁷Np target: 1360 mb
- Super-asymmetric (S3) mode contributes 0.6% of total fission cross section for ²³⁸U and 1.2% for ²³⁷Np
- The super-asymmetric mode produces fragments around 60 a.m.u. away from the symmetric fragment mass
- 93 isotopes were unambiguously identified in the mass range 30 < A < 200
- Proton beam energy: 660 MeV; irradiation time: 27 min; beam intensity: about 3 × 10¹⁴ protons per min
- Quoted errors in measured cross sections include statistical significance (2-3%), target thickness (3%), and detector efficiency (10%)

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of mass distributions for p+²³⁷Np and p+²³⁸U systems in the mass range 70 < A < 150 (Deppman 2013) by adding new data in the IMF region (30 < A < 70) and the heavy region (150 < A < 200). The results agree with findings by Ricciardi et al. (2005) who identified binary decay products of a fully equilibrated compound nucleus in inverse-kinematics reactions of ²³⁸U on proton at 1 AGeV, contradicting the earlier classification by Hufner (1985) that would attribute IMF formation to multifragmentation. The work enables further investigation of the continuous transition between evaporation and fission as manifestations of a single binary decay mechanism, as conjectured by Moretto and collaborators.
