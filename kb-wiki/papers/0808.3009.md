# Accuracy of BCS-based approximations for pairing in small Fermi systems

> We analyze the accuracy of BCS-based approximations for calculating correlation energies and odd-even energy differences in 2-component fermionic systems with a small number of pairs. The analysis is focused on comparing BCS and projected BCS treatments with the exact solution of the pairing Hamiltonian, considering parameter ranges appropriate for nuclear pairing energies. We find that the projected BCS is quite accurate over the entire range of coupling strengths in spaces of up to about 20 doubly degenerate orbitals. It is also quite accurate for two cases we considered with a more realistic Hamiltonian, representing the nuclei around 117Sn and 207Pb. However, the projected BCS significantly underestimates the energies for much larger spaces when the pairing is weak.

## Key claim

Projected BCS (PBCS) is quite accurate for correlation energies and odd-even energy differences over the entire range of coupling strengths in spaces of up to about 20 doubly degenerate orbitals, and also for two realistic cases representing nuclei around 117Sn and 207Pb; however, PBCS significantly underestimates the energies for much larger spaces (e.g., 80 orbitals) when the pairing is weak.

## Method

The authors compared exact solutions of the reduced BCS pairing Hamiltonian (with constant pairing strength g and equally spaced single-particle levels) with BCS and number-projected BCS (PBCS) approximations for systems of 4 to 40 pairs in spaces of 8 to 80 doubly degenerate orbitals. They also performed exact configuration-interaction diagonalizations for two realistic cases (116Sn and 206Pb) using a delta-function pairing interaction with single-particle energies and wave functions from Skyrme SLy4 mean-field calculations, and compared those results to BCS and PBCS approximations.

## Key numbers

- For the constant-g Hamiltonian with Ω=8 and Npair=4, PBCS errors in correlation energy are well under 10% across the coupling range g=0.1-0.8 (in units of level spacing).
- For Ω=80 and Npair=40, PBCS errors in correlation energy become unacceptably large (exceeding 20% in the weak-coupling regime).
- For the realistic 116Sn case with Ω=16 orbitals, PBCS tracks the exact correlation energy accurately at the nominal interaction strength v0 ≈ 450 MeV·fm³; BCS systematically underpredicts the correlation energy.
- For 207Pb, the empirical neutron pairing gap is Δ(3) = 0.32 MeV; BCS errors in the gap are 100-200 keV, while PBCS remains remarkably accurate.
- For the constant-g model with Npair=8 and Ω=16, the exact pairing gaps are approximately 0.6, 1.0, and 5.0 (in level-spacing units) at g=0.32, 0.42, and 0.87 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the critical analysis of BCS approximations in finite Fermi systems originally performed by Richardson (1960s) using the exactly solvable pairing Hamiltonian. It addresses the question of how much improvement number-projected BCS (PBCS) provides over standard BCS, which was known to strongly underestimate pairing correlations. The study also relates to recent work on metallic grains (Duke, 2000; von Delft, 2001) and a nuclear physics study by Dussel (2007) that found significant differences between exact and PBCS results. The findings enable reliable use of PBCS for nuclear structure calculations within single-major-shell truncations, while identifying the need for renormalization procedures when larger orbital spaces are employed.
