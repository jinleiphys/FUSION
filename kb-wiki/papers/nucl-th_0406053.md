# On kaonic deuterium. Quantum field theoretic and relativistic covariant approach

> We study kaonic deuterium, the bound K^-d state A_(K d). Within a quantum field theoretic and relativistic covariant approach we derive the energy level displacement of the ground state of kaonic deuterium in terms of the amplitude of K^-d scattering for arbitrary relative momenta. Near threshold our formula reduces to the well-known DGBT formula. The S-wave amplitude of K^-d scattering near threshold is defined by the resonances Lambda(1405), Sigma(1750) and a smooth elastic background, and the inelastic channels K^- d -> NY and K^- d -> NY pion, with Y = Sigma^(+/-), Sigma^0 and Lambda^0, where the final-state interactions play an important role. The Ericson-Weise formula for the S-wave scattering length of K^-d scattering is derived. The total width of the energy level of the ground state of kaonic deuterium is estimated using the theoretical predictions of the partial widths of the two-body decays A_(Kd) -> NY and experimental data on the rates of the NY-pair production in the reactions K^-d -> NY. We obtain Gamma_{1s} = (630 +/-100) eV. For the shift of the energy level of the ground state of kaonic deuterium we predict epsilon_(1s) = (353 +/-60)eV.

## Key claim

The paper derives the energy level displacement of the ground state of kaonic deuterium using a quantum field theoretic and relativistic covariant approach, obtaining a total width of Gamma_{1s} = (630 +/- 100) eV and a shift of epsilon_{1s} = (353 +/- 60) eV, with the caveat that these predictions rely on theoretical estimates of partial widths for two-body decays combined with experimental data on NY-pair production rates.

## Method

The authors derive a general formula for the energy level displacement of kaonic deuterium in terms of the K^-d scattering amplitude for arbitrary relative momenta, which near threshold reduces to the DGBT formula. The S-wave K^-d scattering amplitude near threshold is computed from the contributions of the Lambda(1405) and Sigma(1750) resonances, a smooth elastic background, and inelastic channels K^- d -> NY and K^- d -> NY pi (where Y = Sigma^+/-, Sigma^0, Lambda^0), including final-state interactions. The Ericson-Weise formula for the S-wave scattering length of K^-d scattering is derived, and the total width is estimated by combining theoretical predictions for partial widths of two-body decays A_{Kd} -> NY with experimental data on NY-pair production rates in K^-d -> NY reactions.

## Key numbers

* Bohr radius of kaonic deuterium: a_B = 69 fm, calculated from reduced mass mu = 391 MeV (with m_K = 494 MeV, m_d = 1876 MeV) and fine-structure constant alpha = 1/137.036
* Binding energy of kaonic deuterium ground state: E_{1s} = -10.41 keV
* Predicted total width of ground state: Gamma_{1s} = (630 +/- 100) eV
* Predicted shift of ground state energy level: epsilon_{1s} = (353 +/- 60) eV (with an alternative value of (325 +/- 60) eV also given in the text)
* Real part of S-wave K^-n scattering amplitude from resonance contribution: Re f^{K^-n}_0(0)_R = 0.037 fm
* Elastic background parameter for K^-n scattering from current algebra: A^{K^-n}_B = (0.200 +/- 0.024) fm, with F_K = 113 MeV
* Smearing correction factor: delta^{(sm)}_{1s} approximately -10^{-2}

## Context

This work extends the DGBT formula (Deser, Goldberger, Baumann, Thirring, 1954) for kaonic deuterium to a fully relativistic quantum field theoretic framework, and derives the Ericson-Weise formula (Ericson and Weise, 1988) for the K^-d S-wave scattering length. The approach builds on previous work by Ivanov et al. on kaonic hydrogen and uses the Effective quark model with chiral U(3)xU(3) symmetry (Ivanov et al., 1999) for the elastic background. The results enable experimental analysis of kaonic deuterium data from the DEAR collaboration and provide predictions that can be tested by future measurements of kaonic deuterium X-rays.
