# Resonant contributions to polarized proton structure functions

> Nucleon resonance contributions to the polarized proton $g_1$ and $g_2$ structure functions are computed from resonance electroexcitation amplitudes extracted from CLAS exclusive meson electroproduction data. Including resonances in the mass range up to 1.75 GeV, and taking into account the interference between excited states, we compare the resonant contributions with the polarized proton structure function and polarization asymmetry data from Jefferson Lab 6 GeV measurements. All resonance-like structure observed in the polarized structure functions and asymmetries can be attributed to the resonant contributions, confirming their essential role in the behavior of $g_1$ and $g_2$ in the resonant region over the entire range $Q^2 < 7.5$ GeV$^2$ covered by the measurements. Comparing the resonance contributions with the $g_1$ and $g_2$ structure functions computed from parton distribution functions extrapolated from the deep-inelastic region, we also quantify the degree to which quark-hadron duality holds for $g_1$ and $g_2$ and their moments.

## Key claim

All resonance-like structure observed in the polarized proton structure functions and asymmetries can be attributed to resonant contributions, confirming their essential role in the behavior of \(g_1\) and \(g_2\) in the resonant region over the entire range \(Q^2 < 7.5\) GeV\(^2\) covered by the measurements, though the comparison is limited to \(Q^2 \lesssim 5\) GeV\(^2\) where resonance electrocouplings are currently available.

## Method

The resonant contributions to the polarized proton \(g_1\) and \(g_2\) structure functions were computed from resonance electroexcitation amplitudes (\(A_{1/2}^R\), \(A_{3/2}^R\), \(S_{1/2}^R\)) extracted from CLAS exclusive meson electroproduction data, using a coherent sum over resonances with spin \(J\), isospin \(I\), and parity \(\eta\) in the mass range up to 1.75 GeV. The formalism included interference between excited states and used interpolation functions fitted to the \(Q^2\) evolution of electrocouplings determined from analyses of exclusive \(\pi^+ n\), \(\pi^0 p\), \(\eta p\), and \(\pi^+ \pi^- p\) electroproduction channels. The computed resonant contributions were compared with polarized structure function and polarization asymmetry data from Jefferson Lab 6 GeV measurements, including CLAS, RSS, and SANE experiments.

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers the mass range up to \(W \leq 1.75\) GeV and \(Q^2 \leq 5\) GeV\(^2\), where resonance electrocouplings are currently available.
- The \(\Delta(1232) 3/2^+\) resonance contribution to \(g_1\) becomes negative and to \(g_2\) positive over the entire range \(Q^2 \lesssim 5\) GeV\(^2\), with the absolute values of \(A_{3/2}\) remaining larger than \(A_{1/2}\).
- For the \(g_1\) structure function, the third resonance peak is mostly driven by the \(N'(1720) 3/2^+\) state over the entire \(Q^2\) range studied.
- The SANE \(g_2\) data were taken at beam energy \(E = 4.7\) GeV with \(Q^2\) running between approximately 3.4 and 4.0 GeV\(^2\), and at \(E = 5.9\) GeV with \(Q^2\) between approximately 5.0 and 5.7 GeV\(^2\).
- The RSS \(A_1\) and \(A_2\) data were taken at \(E = 5.8\) GeV with \(Q^2\) between approximately 1.2 and 1.4 GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological studies of resonant contributions to spin-averaged structure functions (Blin 2019, Blin 2021) to the spin-dependent sector, using the same resonance electrocoupling framework. It builds on the experimental program of exclusive meson electroproduction with CLAS at Jefferson Lab (Aznauryan 2011, Mokeev 2015, Carman 2020) and enables quantitative evaluation of quark-hadron duality for spin-dependent structure functions, comparing resonant contributions with PDF-based calculations from the JAM15 global QCD analysis (Sato 2016) to quantify the degree to which duality holds for \(g_1\) and \(g_2\) and their moments.
