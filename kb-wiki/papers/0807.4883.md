# K pi vector form factor, dispersive constraints and tau -> nu_tau K pi decays

> Recent experimental data for the differential decay distribution of the decay $τ^-\toν_τK_Sπ^-$ by the Belle collaboration are described by a theoretical model which is composed of the contributing vector and scalar form factors $F_+^{Kπ}(s)$ and $F_0^{Kπ}(s)$. Both form factors are constructed such that they fulfil constraints posed by analyticity and unitarity. A good description of the experimental measurement is achieved by incorporating two vector resonances and working with a three-times subtracted dispersion relation in order to suppress higher-energy contributions. The resonance parameters of the charged $K^*(892)$ meson, defined as the pole of $F_+^{Kπ}(s)$ in the complex $s$-plane, can be extracted, with the result $M_{K^*}=892.0 \pm 0.9 $MeV and $Γ_{K^*}=46.2 \pm 0.4 $MeV. Finally, employing the three-subtracted dispersion relation allows to determine the slope and curvature parameters $λ_+^{'}=(24.7\pm 0.8)\cdot 10^{-3}$ and $λ_+^{''}=(12.0\pm 0.2)\cdot 10^{-4}$ of the vector form factor $F_+^{Kπ}(s)$ directly from the data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a model for the $K\pi$ vector form factor incorporating analyticity and elastic unitarity via a three-times subtracted dispersion relation provides a good description of Belle data for $\tau^-\to\nu_\tau K_S\pi^-$, but the slope parameter $\lambda_+^{'}$ is almost 100% correlated with the total branching fraction and thus requires fixing the latter to an experimental value for a precise determination.

## Method

The authors fit Belle data for the differential decay distribution of $\tau^-\to\nu_\tau K_S\pi^-$ using a theoretical model for the vector form factor $F_+^{K\pi}(s)$ that satisfies an $n$-subtracted Omnès dispersion relation, with the elastic $K\pi$ P-wave phase shift derived from a resonance parametrization. They investigate one, two, three, and four subtractions, with a cutoff $s_{\rm cut}$ varied from $1.8\;\mathrm{GeV}$ to infinity, and include one or two vector resonances ($K^*(892)$ and $K^*(1410)$) together with the scalar form factor $F_0^{K\pi}(s)$ from Jamin et al. (2006). The physical resonance parameters are extracted from the pole of $F_+^{K\pi}(s)$ in the complex $s$-plane.

## Key numbers

- $M_{K^*} = 892.0 \pm 0.9\;\mathrm{MeV}$ and $\Gamma_{K^*} = 46.2 \pm 0.4\;\mathrm{MeV}$ for the charged $K^*(892)$ meson, extracted from the pole position of $F_+^{K\pi}(s)$ in the complex $s$-plane.
- $\lambda_+^{'} = (24.7 \pm 0.8) \times 10^{-3}$ and $\lambda_+^{''} = (12.0 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-4}$ for the slope and curvature of the vector form factor $F_+^{K\pi}(s)$, determined from the three-subtracted dispersion relation fit with the total branching fraction fixed to the experimental value.
- $M_{K^{*'}} = 1276^{\,+\,72}_{\,-\,77}\;\mathrm{MeV}$ and $\Gamma_{K^{*'}} = 198^{\,+\,61}_{\,-\,87}\;\mathrm{MeV}$ for the second vector resonance $K^*(1410)$, from the pole position.
- $\chi^2/\mathrm{n.d.f.} = 77.3/79$ for the best two-resonance fit with $s_{\rm cut} \to \infty$ and fixed branching fraction.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Guerrero and Pich (1997) and Jamin et al. (2006, 2008) for the $K\pi$ vector form factor by enforcing strict elastic unitarity through a denominator-resummed form factor rather than an exponential resummation, and by using $n$-subtracted dispersion relations to suppress higher-energy contributions. The results enable a more model-independent extraction of the $K^*(892)$ pole parameters compared to the Breit-Wigner parameters obtained in the Belle analysis (2007) and Jamin et al. (2008), and provide slope parameters for the vector form factor that can be compared with chiral perturbation theory predictions and used in future analyses of $\tau$ decays and $K_{\ell 3}$ decays.
