# Covariant density functional analysis of shape evolution in $N =40$ isotones

> The structure of low-lying excitation states of even-even $N=40$ isotones is studied using a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian with the collective parameters determined from the relativistic mean-field plus BCS method with the PC-PK1 functional in the particle-hole channel and a separable paring force in the particle-particle channel. The theoretical calculations can reproduce not only the systematics of the low-lying states along the isotonic chain but also the detailed structure of the spectroscopy in a single nucleus. We find a picture of spherical-oblate-prolate shape transition along the isotonic chain of $N=40$ by analyzing the potential energy surfaces. The coexistence of low-lying excited $0^+$ states has also been shown to be a common feature in neutron-deficient $N=40$ isotones.

## Key claim
The paper claims a spherical-oblate-prolate shape transition along the N=40 isotonic chain and that low-lying excited 0+ states are a common feature in neutron-deficient N=40 isotones, but the commonly used covariant density functional without tensor coupling fails to reproduce the onset of ground-state deformation in neutron-rich N=40 isotones, requiring future work with the PKA1 interaction that includes tensor components.

## Method
The authors performed constrained relativistic mean-field plus BCS calculations using the PC-PK1 functional in the particle-hole channel and a separable pairing force in the particle-particle channel to determine collective parameters. These parameters were then used in a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) solved by expanding eigenfunctions on a basis in the collective space of quadrupole deformations (beta, gamma) and Euler angles. The Dirac equation was solved by expanding spinors on eigenfunctions of a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator in Cartesian coordinates with 12 major shells.

## Key numbers
- The root-mean-square deviation from experimental two-proton separation energies was reduced from 1.82 MeV to 1.18 MeV when dynamical correlation energies were included by the 5DCH.
- The axial barriers between coexisting spherical and prolate minima in 78Sr and 80Zr are approximately 6 MeV and 8 MeV, respectively; after including the gamma degree of freedom, barrier heights lowered to 3.5 MeV and 4.5 MeV.
- The average deformation values for the ground state in 78Sr are beta approximately 0.48 and gamma approximately 11 degrees, remaining almost constant with increasing angular momentum.
- The average deformation values for the second 0+ state in 78Sr are beta approximately 0.42 and gamma approximately 22 degrees.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of shape evolution in N=40 isotones using the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian based on the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model with the Gogny force D1S (PhysRevC.80.064313). The present calculations using the covariant density functional PC-PK1 give results similar to those obtained with the Gogny force D1S and reproduce the systematics of low-lying states for isotones with Z greater than 28. The study demonstrates that covariant density functional theory can describe both single-configuration-dominated structures and coexistence structures of distinctly different shapes, but the commonly used functional without tensor coupling fails to reproduce the onset of deformation in neutron-rich N=40 isotones, motivating future work with the density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock theory plus BCS using the PKA1 effective interaction that includes tensor components.
