# Electrical Conductivity of Hot QCD Matter

> We study the electric conductivity of hot QCD matter at various temperatures T within the off-shell parton-hadron-string dynamics transport approach for interacting partonic, hadronic or mixed systems in a finite box with periodic boundary conditions. The response of the strongly interacting system in equilibrium to an external electric field defines the electric conductivity sigma_0. We find a sizable temperature dependence of the ratio sigma_0/T well in line with calculations in a relaxation time approach for Tc<T<2.5Tc. The ratio drops in the hadronic phase with T, shows a minimum close to Tc, and becomes approximately constant (~0.3) above ~5Tc. Our findings imply that the QCD matter even at T=Tc is a much better electric conductor than Cu or Ag (at room temperature).

## Key claim

The ratio σ₀/T of hot QCD matter shows a minimum near the critical temperature T_c and becomes approximately constant (~0.3) above ~5 T_c, but the authors note that energy densities corresponding to T > 5 T_c are not reached in present heavy-ion experiments at RHIC or LHC.

## Method

The authors simulated infinite QCD matter in a cubic box of volume V = 9³ fm³ with periodic boundary conditions using the off-shell Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach. They applied a constant external electric field E_z to the equilibrated system and extracted the electric conductivity σ₀ from the ratio of the induced equilibrium current density j_eq to the field strength. The system was studied at temperatures from T = 100 MeV up to T = 350 MeV, covering hadronic, mixed, and partonic phases.

## Key numbers

- The ratio σ₀/T in the partonic phase for T_c ≤ T ≤ 2.2 T_c follows σ₀(T)/T ≈ 0.01 + 0.16 (T − T_c)/T_c, where T_c = 158 MeV.
- Above ~5 T_c, the ratio σ₀/T becomes approximately constant at ~0.3.
- The sQGP at its minimum (T ≈ T_c) is a better electric conductor than Cu or Ag at room temperature by about a factor of 500 (using 2πℏ/e² = 2.58 × 10⁴ Ω).
- The damping time of the electromagnetic propagator τ_e(T) = (ℏc)/σ₀(T) becomes shorter than 2 fm/c for T > 2.8 T_c.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of transport coefficients in hot QCD matter, specifically shear and bulk viscosities from PHSD simulations (Vitalii et al., 2012) and lattice QCD calculations of the electromagnetic correlator (various lattice groups, 2006-2012). The results enable more accurate modeling of electromagnetic field evolution in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for studying the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) proposed by Kharzeev et al. (2008), as the conductivity determines the screening and decay of electromagnetic fields in the quark-gluon plasma.
