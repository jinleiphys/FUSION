# Modified Fragmentation Function and Jet Quenching at RHIC

> Medium modification of jet fragmentation functions and parton energy loss in cold and hot matter are reviewed. The predicted nuclear modification of the jet fragmentation function agrees well with the recent HERMES data with a resultant energy loss dE/dx approximately 0.5 GeV/fm. From the the recent PHENIX data of high p_T pi_0 spectra in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130 GeV, one extracts an energy loss for a 10 GeV parton that is equivalent to dE/dx=7.3 GeV/fm in a static medium with the same gluon density as in the initial stage of the collision at tau_0=0.2 fm/c. Constraints on jet quenching by the central rapidity density of charged hadrons is also discussed.

# Modified Fragmentation Function and Jet Quenching at RHIC

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed suppression of high-pT hadron spectra in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC can be explained by parton energy loss in dense matter, but the actual energy loss extracted from data depends on converting an expanding-system average to an equivalent static-medium value using the factor R/2τ0, and the effective model used to fit data underestimates the true pQCD energy loss by a factor of about 1.6 due to unitarity corrections.

## Method

The author derives medium-modified fragmentation functions using a twist-four pQCD framework (LQS framework) that incorporates double parton scattering and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) interference effect, leading to modified DGLAP evolution equations with new splitting functions. For DIS on nuclear targets, the calculation is compared to HERMES data to extract the single free parameter C̃α²s. For heavy-ion collisions, the effective rescaling model D̃(z) ≈ (1/(1-ΔE/E)) D(z/(1-ΔE/E)) is used to fit PHENIX π0 spectra, with the extracted energy loss then corrected for expansion and unitarity effects.

## Key numbers

- Energy loss in cold nuclear matter (DIS): dE/dx ≈ 0.5 GeV/fm for a Au nucleus target
- Extracted energy loss for a 10 GeV parton in central Au+Au collisions at √s = 130 GeV: equivalent to dE/dx = 7.3 GeV/fm in a static medium with the same gluon density as the initial stage at τ0 = 0.2 fm/c
- Parameter from HERMES fit: C̃α²s = 0.00065 GeV² (for ¹⁴N target)
- Independent estimate from Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening: C̃α²s = 0.00057 GeV² (with αs = 0.21 at M² = 40 GeV²)
- Quark energy loss formula for DIS: ΔE ≈ C̃α²s mN RA² (CA/Nc) 3 ln(1/2xB)
- Total background energy within jet cone (δη=1, δφ=1) in central Au+Au: ΣET ≈ 80 GeV with fluctuation δET ≈ 10 GeV
- Total charged multiplicity density in central Au+Au at √s = 130 GeV: dN/dη ≈ 900
- Total transverse energy density: dET/dη ≈ 500 GeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of parton energy loss developed by Gyulassy and Wang (1994) and Baier et al. (BDMPS) by incorporating the LPM interference effect into a modified DGLAP evolution equation for fragmentation functions in a medium. The predictions are compared to HERMES DIS data on nuclear targets, showing agreement with the quadratic A²/³ nuclear size dependence. The extracted energy loss in hot matter (7.3 GeV/fm) is about 15 times larger than in cold nuclear matter (0.5 GeV/fm), implying a correspondingly higher gluon density in the initial stage of heavy-ion collisions. The paper also discusses constraints from the HIJING model and the two-component model for charged multiplicity, showing that the energy dependence of dNch/dη at RHIC requires stronger gluon shadowing than quark shadowing. This work enables quantitative extraction of medium properties from jet quenching measurements at RHIC and LHC.
