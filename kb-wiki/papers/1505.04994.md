# Viscosities of Gluon Dominated QGP Model within Relativistic Non-Abelian Hydrodynamics

> Based on the first principle calculation, a Lagrangian for the system describing quarks, gluons, and their interactions, is constructed. Ascribed to the existence of dissipative behavior as a consequence of strong interaction within quark-gluon plasma (QGP) matter, auxiliary terms describing viscosities are constituted into the Lagrangian. Through a "kind" of phase transition, gluon field is redefined as a scalar field with four-vector velocity inherently attached. Then, the Lagrangian is elaborated further to produce the energy-momentum tensor of dissipative fluid-like system and the equation of motion (EOM). By imposing the law of energy and momentum conservation, the values of shear and bulk viscosities are analytically calculated. Our result shows that, at the energy level close to hadronization, the bulk viscosity is bigger than shear viscosity. By making use of the conjectured values $η/ s \sim 1 / 4π$ and $ζ/ s \sim 1$, the ratio of bulk to shear viscosity is found to be $ζ/ η> 4 π$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, for a gluon-dominated quark-gluon plasma near the hadronization temperature, the bulk viscosity is larger than the shear viscosity, with the ratio ζ/η > 4π when using the conjectured values η/s ∼ 1/4π and ζ/s ∼ 1. This result is derived from a first-principles Lagrangian approach rather than from lattice QCD or kinetic theory, and the authors note that the calculation applies only near the phase transition energy level.

## Method

The authors constructed a Lagrangian for a viscous QCD fluid by combining an ideal part (based on SU(3)⊗U(1) gauge symmetry with quarks and gluons) with a dissipative part modeled on the relativistic hydrodynamic energy-momentum tensor of Landau. They redefined the gluon field as U^a_μ = u_μ φ^a, where u_μ is the four-velocity and φ^a a scalar field, interpreting this as a "phase transition" between hadronic and QGP states. By imposing energy-momentum conservation on the derived energy-momentum tensor, they obtained analytic expressions for shear viscosity η and bulk viscosity ζ, then evaluated these numerically for a temperature range of 150–250 MeV using parameters appropriate for a gluon-dominated plasma.

## Key numbers

- The hadronization temperature is taken as 150 MeV, with temperatures above 200 MeV considered for lattice QCD comparison.
- The quark mass m_Q is set to 0.5 GeV.
- The gauge field energy A is set to 100 GeV.
- The ratio of weak to strong coupling constants g_G/g_F is 0.0072.
- The time step Δt = 1 × 10^{-13} s.
- The space variable x ranges from 10^{-5} to 10^{-3} nm.
- Bulk viscosity ζ ∼ O(10) and shear viscosity η ∼ O(10^0) in the units used.
- The linear relation between viscosities near hadronization is ζ ≈ c η with c ∼ 50.
- Using the conjectured bound η/s ≥ 1/4π, the entropy s ∼ O(10) and ζ/s ≳ O(10^0).

## Context

This work extends the hybrid fluid-field approach of Bambah (2006) and the non-commutative fluid analogy of Jackiw and collaborators, applying it specifically to a gluon-dominated QGP. The result that bulk viscosity exceeds shear viscosity near the critical temperature is compared to the findings of Tuchin and collaborators for QCD plasma (where ζ/s ∼ 1) and to lattice QCD computations by Gottlieb (2007) and Petreczky (2007). The paper enables future connections between hydrodynamic transport coefficients and gluon cross-sections via kinetic theory, as noted by Plumari and collaborators.
