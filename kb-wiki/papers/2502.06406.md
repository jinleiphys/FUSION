# Probing the QCD $\bar θ$ term with paramagnetic molecules

> The experimental search for CP violation in paramagnetic atomic and molecular systems has made impressive progress in recent years. This has led to strong upper limits on the electron electric dipole moment. The same measurements can also be used to constrain hadronic sources of CP violation through CP-violating interactions between the electrons and the nucleus. We employ heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory to compute such CP-violating semileptonic electron-nucleus interactions arising from the QCD theta term. We sharpen earlier results by determining the relevant short-distance effects and by an explicit two-loop calculation of meson-photon diagrams. We derive a bound of $|\barθ| < 1.5 \cdot 10^{-8}$ at $90\%$ confidence, based on HfF$^+$ experiments at JILA. A further experimental improvement of one to two orders of magnitude would make paramagnetic molecular electric dipole moment experiments competitive with the neutron and diamagnetic atom program in constraining strong CP violation and higher-dimensional CP-odd quark-gluon operators.

## Key claim

The authors compute the CP-violating semileptonic electron-nucleus interactions arising from the QCD theta term using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, deriving a bound of \(|\bar{\theta}| < 1.5 \times 10^{-8}\) at 90% confidence based on HfF\(^+\) experiments at JILA, but note that this bound is currently weaker than the neutron EDM bound of \(|\bar{\theta}| < 10^{-10}\) and that further experimental improvement of one to two orders of magnitude would make paramagnetic molecular EDM experiments competitive with neutron and diamagnetic atom programs.

## Method

The authors employ heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT) to compute the CP-odd scalar-pseudoscalar semileptonic couplings \(C_{\mathrm{SP}}^{p,n}\) induced by the QCD \(\bar{\theta}\) term. They calculate both leading-order (LO) meson-exchange diagrams involving \(\pi^0\) and \(\eta\) mesons and next-to-leading-order (NLO) two-loop pion-loop diagrams, using the Wess-Zumino Lagrangian for anomalous meson-photon couplings and counterterms fitted to \(\pi^0 \rightarrow e^+ e^-\) and \(\eta \rightarrow \mu^+ \mu^-\) branching ratios to renormalize ultraviolet divergences. The CP-odd low-energy constants \(\bar{g}_0\), \(\bar{g}_1\), and \(\bar{g}_{0\eta}\) are expressed in terms of \(\bar{\theta}\) using lattice QCD results for nucleon sigma terms and mass splittings.

## Key numbers

*   Bound on the QCD vacuum angle: \(|\bar{\theta}| < 1.5 \times 10^{-8}\) at 90% confidence, derived from HfF\(^+\) experiments at JILA.
*   CP-odd pion-nucleon coupling: \(\bar{g}_0(\bar{\theta}) = -17.2[2.0] \times 10^{-3}\,\bar{\theta}\), based on \(\delta m_N = 2.54[21]\,\mathrm{MeV}\) from lattice QCD.
*   CP-odd eta-nucleon coupling: \(\bar{g}_{0\eta}(\bar{\theta}) = -117[36] \times 10^{-3}\,\bar{\theta}\), using \(\sigma_{Ns} = 44.9[6.4]\) MeV and \(\sigma_{Nl}=59.1[3.5]\) MeV.
*   CP-odd isospin-breaking coupling: \(\bar{g}_{1}(\bar{\theta}) = 3.4[1.5] \times 10^{-3}\,\bar{\theta}\).
*   Nucleus-averaged meson-exchange contribution: \(C^\text{ME}_{\mathrm{SP}} = 0.78[42] \times 10^{-2}\,\bar{\theta}\) for a typical heavy atom with \(Z/A = 0.4\) and \(N/A = 0.6\).
*   Fitted counterterm for pion: \(\chi_\text{fin}^{\pi}(\mu=m_\rho) = 2.6[1.4]\) from \(\mathrm{BR}(\pi^0 \rightarrow e^+ e^-, \text{ no rad}) = 6.22[0.39] \times 10^{-8}\).
*   Fitted counterterm for eta: \(\chi_\text{fin}^{\eta}(\mu=m_\rho) = 1.6[1.0]\) from \(\mathrm{BR}(\eta \rightarrow \mu^+ \mu^-, \text{ no rad}) = 5.9[0.8] \times 10^{-6}\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Pospelov and Ritz (2014) for paramagnetic molecules, sharpening results by determining short-distance effects and performing a complete two-loop calculation of meson-photon diagrams, whereas the earlier work used a factorized approach that led to divergent amplitudes. The derived bound on \(\bar{\theta}\) from HfF\(^+\) is currently weaker than the neutron EDM bound of \(|\bar{\theta}| < 10^{-10}\) from Abel et al. (2020) and lattice QCD calculations by Dragos et al. (2019) and Liang et al. (2023), but the authors argue that paramagnetic molecular experiments are improving faster than neutron and diamagnetic atom searches, potentially providing a competitive and independent constraint on strong CP violation and higher-dimensional CP-odd quark-gluon operators.
