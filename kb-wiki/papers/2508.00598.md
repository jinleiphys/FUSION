# Coupled-channel contributions to the GDH sum rule from the Jülich-Bonn approach

> We study the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule within a dynamical coupled-channel approach, the Jülich-Bonn model for light baryon resonances based on fits to an extensive data base of pion and photon induced data. Recently published photoproduction data for different observables with $πN$ and $ηN$ final states are analyzed simultaneously with older data for the reactions $πN\to πN$, $ηN$, $KΛ$, $KΣ$ and $γp\toπN$, $ηN$, $KΛ$, $KΣ$. The impact of the new data on the resonance spectrum is investigated and the contribution of the individual channels to the GDH integral is determined.

## Key claim

The Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule for the proton is evaluated using the Jülich-Bonn dynamical coupled-channel model, with the condition that the predictions rely on fits to an extensive data base of pion- and photon-induced reactions and that contributions from channels for which no direct data on the helicity-difference cross section exist are included via the coupled-channel framework.

## Method

The Jülich-Bonn dynamical coupled-channel approach solves a Lippmann-Schwinger-like equation for the hadronic T-matrix in a partial-wave basis up to J=9/2, including channels πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ, σN, ρN, and πΔ, with the latter three parameterizing the ππN three-body channel. Photoproduction multipole amplitudes are obtained by coupling the hadronic T-matrix to a photoproduction potential that includes polynomial parameterizations for non-pole contributions and bare s-channel resonance vertices. A χ² minimization using MINUIT fits 959 free parameters to 73,171 data points, including recent CLAS double-polarization data for π⁰p and π⁺n, LEPS2/BGOegg ηp data, and CLAS K⁰Σ⁺ polarization data, with weights applied to smaller data sets and systematic uncertainties treated via normalization factors.

## Key numbers

- Total data base: 73,171 data points fitted simultaneously across all channels
- New CLAS double-polarization E data for γp→π⁰p: 495 data points with χ²/point = 1.62 (weight 60)
- New CLAS double-polarization G data for γp→π⁰p: 198 data points with χ²/point = 1.57 (weight 100)
- New CLAS double-polarization G data for γp→π⁺n: 217 data points with χ²/point = 3.02 (weight 130)
- New LEPS2/BGOegg differential cross section for γp→ηp: 320 data points with χ²/point = 0.85 (weight 60)
- New LEPS2/BGOegg beam asymmetry Σ for γp→ηp: 80 data points with χ²/point = 0.63 (weight 90)
- Dynamically generated N(1875)3/2⁻ pole position: 1914(1) − i 343(2) MeV, corresponding to a width of 686 MeV
- Elastic πN partial-wave data from GWU/SAID WI08 energy-dependent solution used from πN threshold up to W = 2400 MeV in steps of 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Jülich-Bonn coupled-channel analyses of Rönchen et al. (2022) (JüBo2022) by including recent photoproduction data from CLAS and LEPS2/BGOegg, updating the resonance spectrum and providing channel-by-channel GDH sum rule contributions. The approach is complementary to other dynamical coupled-channel frameworks such as the ANL/Osaka model of Kamano et al. (2013, 2016) and the Bonn-Gatchina model of Anisovich et al. (2011), and it enables future extensions to ωN and η′N channels as well as comparisons with generalized GDH integrals from chiral effective field theory.
