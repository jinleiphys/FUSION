# Photons as a viscometer of heavy ion collisions

> The viscous correction to thermal photon production at leading log order is calculated and integrated over the space-time evolution of a hydrodynamic simulation of heavy-ion collisions. The resulting transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow can be reliably calculated within a hydrodynamic framework up to transverse momenta of $q_\perp$ = 2.5 GeV and $q_\perp$ = 1.5 GeV respectively. A non-vanishing viscosity leads to a larger thermalization time when extracted from the experimentally measured inverse slope (T_eff) of photon $q_\perp$ spectra. A precise, O(20 MeV), measurement of photon T_eff can place stringent bounds on $τ_0$ and $η/s$.

# Photons as a viscometer of heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The viscous correction to thermal photon production at leading log order, when integrated over a hydrodynamic simulation, shows that a non-vanishing shear viscosity leads to a larger extracted thermalization time from the experimentally measured inverse slope of photon transverse momentum spectra. A measurement of the photon effective temperature to within 20 MeV can place stringent bounds on both the thermalization time and the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, but the calculation is only reliable up to transverse momenta of 2.5 GeV for spectra and 1.5 GeV for elliptic flow.

## Method
The viscous correction to the quark distribution function was computed using a one-parameter ansatz for the off-equilibrium distribution, and the leading log photon production rates from Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation were derived. These rates were then integrated over the space-time evolution of a 2+1 dimensional boost-invariant viscous hydrodynamic model with a bag model equation of state and Glauber model initial conditions, using an impact parameter of 6.5 fm and a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of 1/4π. The viscous corrections to the flow and temperature profiles were neglected, with the entire viscous effect coming from the modification to the photon emission rates.

## Key numbers
- The viscous correction to the photon transverse momentum spectra becomes of order one at q⊥ ≈ 2.5 GeV, setting the reliability limit for spectra calculations.
- The photon elliptic flow can be reliably calculated up to q⊥ ≈ 1.5 GeV, above which the viscous correction expansion breaks down.
- A measurement of the photon effective temperature (Teff) to within 20 MeV is required to distinguish between ideal and viscous results.
- For a fixed Teff, an ideal hydrodynamic evolution starting at τ0 = 0.6 fm/c gives the same result as a viscous evolution starting at τ0 = 1.0 fm/c.
- The leading log contribution comprises about fifty percent of the thermal QGP photon yields in the kinematic regions of interest (1.5 to 2.5 GeV).

## Context
This work extends the study of electromagnetic probes from viscous media, following earlier work by Dusling (2008) on dilepton emission that found viscous corrections to hydrodynamic variables modified yields but left the spectral shape largely undistorted. The calculation uses the same hydrodynamic model and initial conditions as Teaney (2001) and builds on the photon rate formalism of Wong and Kapusta (1991) and Kapusta (1991). The results enable the use of direct photons as a complementary observable to hadronic flow for constraining the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma, addressing the ambiguity that hadrons are sensitive to later stages of the evolution while photons are emitted throughout the entire space-time evolution without final state interactions.
