# Exclusive photoproduction of $π^0$ up to large values of Mandelstam variables $s, t$ and $u$ with CLAS

> Exclusive photoproduction cross sections have been measured for the process $γp \rightarrow pπ^0(e^+e^-(γ))$ with the Dalitz decay final state using tagged photon energies in the range of $E_γ = 1.275-5.425$ GeV. The complete angular distribution of the final state $π^0$, for the entire photon energy range up to large values of $t$ and $u$, has been measured for the first time. The data obtained show that the cross section $dσ/dt$, at mid to large angles, decreases with energy as $s^{-6.89\pm 0.26} $. This is in agreement with the perturbative QCD quark counting rule prediction of $s^{-7} $. Paradoxically, the size of angular distribution of measured cross sections is greatly underestimated by the QCD based Generalized Parton Distribution mechanism at highest available invariant energy $s=11$ GeV$^2$. At the same time, the Regge exchange based models for $π^0$ photoproduction are more consistent with experimental data.

## Key claim

The cross section for exclusive photoproduction of π⁰ off protons, measured via the Dalitz decay channel, decreases with energy as s⁻⁶·⁸⁹±⁰·²⁶ at mid to large angles, consistent with the perturbative QCD quark counting rule prediction of s⁻⁷, yet the absolute magnitude of the angular distribution is greatly underestimated by the QCD-based Generalized Parton Distribution mechanism at the highest available invariant energy s = 11 GeV².

## Method

The CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab was used with a tagged photon beam from a 5.72 GeV electron beam incident on a liquid hydrogen target, covering photon energies Eγ = 1.275–5.425 GeV. The reaction γp → pπ⁰ was identified through the Dalitz decay π⁰ → e⁺e⁻γ, requiring three charged tracks (p, e⁺, e⁻) in the final state; kinematic fitting and cuts on missing energy (E_X > 75 MeV) and missing mass squared of the proton were applied to suppress background from π⁺π⁻ events. Differential cross sections dσ/dt were extracted over the full angular range up to large values of t and u, and compared to Regge pole models, the Handbag/GPD mechanism, and constituent counting rule predictions.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range: Eγ = 1.275–5.425 GeV, corresponding to center-of-mass energies W = 1.81–3.33 GeV
- Power-law fit at 90° c.m. angle yields n = 6.89 ± 0.26 for dσ/dt ∝ s⁻ⁿ, consistent with the s⁻⁷ counting rule prediction
- Highest available invariant energy: s = 11 GeV²
- π⁰ mass squared from fit: M_π⁰² = 0.0179 GeV² with Gaussian width σ = 0.0049 GeV²
- Integrated background after cuts: no more than 1.05%
- Total systematic uncertainty: 9%–12% as a function of energy
- Sector-to-sector systematic uncertainty: 4.4%–7.1%; flux determination uncertainty: 5.7%; 1-C pull probability cut uncertainty: 1.6%–6.1%

## Context

This work extends the database for γp → pπ⁰ above Eγ = 2 GeV by a factor of four, bridging the nucleon resonance region and high-energy regime over a wide angular range for the first time. It provides the first complete angular distributions up to large t and u, enabling detailed tests of Regge exchange models (Goldstein and Owens 1973; Mathieu et al. 2015; Donnachie and Kalashnikova 2015; Laget et al. 2005) and the Handbag/GPD mechanism (Kroll et al. 2000). The results show that Regge-based models are more consistent with the data than the GPD-based approach, and the observed s⁻⁶·⁸⁹ scaling at 90° supports the constituent counting rules of Brodsky and Farrar (1973) and Matveev et al. (1972), while structures at smaller angles indicate higher energies are needed for full applicability of the counting rule.
