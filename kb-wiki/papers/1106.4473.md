# Identity Method for Particle Number Fluctuations and Correlations

> An incomplete particle identification distorts the observed event-by-event fluctuations of the hadron chemical composition in nucleus-nucleus collisions. A new experimental technique called the {\em identity method} was recently proposed. It eliminated the misidentification problem for one specific combination of the second moments in a system of two hadron species. In the present paper this method is extended to calculate all the second moments in a system with arbitrary number of hadron species. Special linear combinations of the second moments are introduced. These combinations are presented in terms of single-particle variables and can be found experimentally from the event-by-event averaging. The mathematical problem is then reduced to solving a system of linear equations. The effect of incomplete particle identification is fully eliminated from the final results.

# Identity Method for Particle Number Fluctuations and Correlations

## Key claim
The identity method, previously limited to one specific combination of second moments for two hadron species, is extended to reconstruct all second moments (variances and covariances) of the multiplicity distribution for an arbitrary number of hadron species, provided the determinant of a constructed matrix is nonzero, a condition that fails only in the extreme case of random identification where all mass distributions have identical shape.

## Method
The method uses experimentally measured mass distributions ρ_j(m) for each hadron species j, averaged over all particles from all events, to define identity variables w_j(m) = ρ_j(m) / Σ_i ρ_i(m). Event-by-event averages of squared and cross-term sums of these identity variables, ⟨W_j²⟩ and ⟨W_pW_q⟩, are computed; these are linear combinations of the unknown true second moments ⟨N_i²⟩ and ⟨N_iN_l⟩ plus known first-moment terms. The problem reduces to solving a system of k + k(k−1)/2 linear equations for the k variances and k(k−1)/2 covariances, with coefficients determined entirely from the ρ_j(m) functions.

## Key numbers
- For k = 2 hadron species, the solution for the three second moments (⟨N₁²⟩, ⟨N₂²⟩, ⟨N₁N₂⟩) is given explicitly by three equations involving the 2×2 matrix of u_ji coefficients and the measured b-vector; the denominator is (u₁₁u₂₂ − u₁₂u₂₁)².
- In the random identification limit where ρ_j(m) = ⟨N_j⟩ f(m) for all j, the determinant of the system matrix A becomes zero, reducing the information to a single relation: Σ_j ⟨N_j²⟩ + 2 Σ_{p<q} ⟨N_pN_q⟩ = ⟨N²⟩.
- For complete identification (non-overlapping mass distributions), the matrix A has determinant = 1, and the solution reduces to ⟨W_j²⟩ = ⟨N_j²⟩ and ⟨W_pW_q⟩ = ⟨N_pN_q⟩.

## Context
This work extends the identity method of Gazdzicki (2011), which solved the misidentification problem for only one fluctuation measure (Ψ) in two-species systems, to all second moments for any number of species (k ≥ 2). The method is directly applicable to experimental data from the NA49 Collaboration (Afanasev 2000, Alt 2008, Kresan 2009) and STAR Collaboration (Abelev 2009), which previously used measures like σ_dyn and ν_dyn in limited acceptance. The approach enables model-independent extraction of chemical fluctuation moments in full phase space, though the author notes that practical applicability requires further study of experimental error propagation.
