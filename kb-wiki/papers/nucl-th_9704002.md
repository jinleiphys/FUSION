# Phi Meson Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions at SIS Energies

> Phi meson production in heavy-ion collisions at SIS/GSI energies ($\sim 2$ GeV/nucleon) is studied in the relativistic transport model. We include contributions from baryon-baryon, pion-baryon, and kaon-antikaon collisions. The cross sections for the first two processes are obtained in an one-boson-exchange model, while that for the last process is taken to be of Breit-Wigner form through the phi meson resonance. The dominant contribution to phi meson production in heavy ion collisions at these energies is found to come from secondary pion-nucleon collisions. Effects due to medium modifications of kaon masses are also studied and are found to reduce the phi meson yield by about a factor of two, mainly because of increased phi decay width as a result of dropping kaon-antikaon masses. In this case, the $ϕ/K^-$ ratio is about 4%, which is a factor of 2-3 below preliminary experimental data from the FOPI collaboration at GSI. Including also the reduction of phi meson mass in medium increases this ratio to about 8%, which is then in reasonable agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The dominant contribution to phi meson production in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies (∼2 GeV/nucleon) comes from secondary pion-nucleon collisions, not from baryon-baryon or kaon-antikaon channels. The predicted φ/K⁻ ratio agrees with preliminary FOPI data only when both kaon and phi meson in-medium mass modifications are included simultaneously, yielding a ratio of about 8%.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic transport model (RVUU) to simulate Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 AGeV. Elementary cross sections for phi meson production from baryon-baryon, pion-baryon, and kaon-antikaon collisions were calculated using a one-boson-exchange model with parameters fitted to available experimental data, with the kaon-antikaon channel treated via a Breit-Wigner form. Phi mesons, kaons, and antikaons were treated perturbatively using the test particle method, while nucleons, deltas, and pions were propagated with mean-field potentials and stochastic two-body collisions.

## Key numbers

- The φ/K⁻ ratio is about 4% when only kaon medium effects are included, which is a factor of 2-3 below preliminary FOPI data
- The φ/K⁻ ratio increases to about 8% when both kaon and phi meson medium effects are included, in reasonable agreement with FOPI data
- The phi meson yield without medium effects is 3.3 × 10⁻⁴, with K⁻ yield about 1.4 × 10⁻³, giving a φ/K⁻ ratio of about 25%
- Including kaon medium effects reduces the phi meson yield by about a factor of 2 to about 1.6 × 10⁻⁴
- Including both kaon and phi meson medium effects gives a final phi meson yield of about 3 × 10⁻⁴
- The maximum baryon density reached in central Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 AGeV is about three times normal nuclear density
- The inverse slope parameter for nucleons is about 180 MeV, while that of pions is about 120 MeV
- The phi meson mass in medium follows m_φ*/m_φ ≈ 1.0 - 0.0225 ρ/ρ₀ from QCD sum rule calculations by Hatsuda and Lee
- The kaon mass at normal nuclear density increases by about 10 MeV, while the antikaon mass decreases by about 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of phi meson production at SIS energies by Ko and collaborators (1995) that only included the K K̅ → φ channel and predicted a φ/K⁻ ratio less than 1%, an order of magnitude below the FOPI data. The inclusion of baryon-baryon and pion-baryon production channels, particularly the dominant πN channel, brings the predicted ratio closer to experiment. The study also builds on QCD sum rule calculations by Hatsuda and Lee (1992) for in-medium phi meson mass and chiral perturbation theory by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) for kaon in-medium masses. The results enable further experimental tests at SATURNE for pp → ppφ cross sections near threshold and suggest that the φ/K⁻ ratio can serve as a probe of in-medium hadron properties.
