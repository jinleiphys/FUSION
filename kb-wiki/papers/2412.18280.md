# Heavy-Quark Spin Symmetry Violation effects in Charmed Baryon Production

> In this work, we investigate the Heavy-Quark Spin Symmetry (HQSS) exhibited in the effective Lagrangians governing the three-point interactions of $D$ mesons, charmed baryons, and nucleons. We first construct the effective Lagrangians, and there are 12 distinct terms. As a result, we observe that the invariant Lagrangian under HQSS manifests exclusively in the pseudoscalar $D$ mesons coupling to nucleons and $Λ_c$ baryons, whereas nucleons and $Σ_c$ ($Σ_c^*$) baryons only couple with vector $D$ mesons. By taking into account the violated heavy-quark spin transformation, one can recover all interactions from the effective Lagrangians. Furthermore, we compute the differential cross-sections of the $p\bar p \to Y_c\bar{Y}_c'$ scatterings, where $Y_c,\bar{Y_c}' = Λ_c,~Σ_c,~Σ_c^*$, to reveal the residue of the violating HQSS (VHQSS) on charmed baryon production. Ultimately, by accounting for VHQSS, we aim for precise predictions of production rates, which are essential for the High-Energy Storage Ring (HESR) experiments at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR).

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that Heavy-Quark Spin Symmetry (HQSS) is violated in charmed baryon production from antiproton-proton annihilation, and that incorporating violating-HQSS (VHQSS) terms into the effective Lagrangian framework is necessary to recover all 12 interaction terms, enabling more precise predictions for production rates at FAIR's HESR experiments.

## Method
The authors constructed SU(2)_f effective Lagrangians for D mesons, nucleons, and charmed baryons (Λ_c, Σ_c, Σ_c^*), identifying 12 low-energy constants. They decomposed these into conserving-HQSS and violating-HQSS terms using super-multiplet heavy-quark fields, then derived heavy-quark sum rules that reduce the parameters to 9. Coupling constants were estimated using SU(4)_f symmetry breaking with a 20% deviation relative to SU(3)_f, and the remaining parameters were fixed by matching relations from the HQSS sum rules. Differential cross-sections for nine p p̅ → Y_c Y̅_c' channels were computed at tree level with phenomenological form factors.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of p p̅ → Λ_c Λ̅_c using the Quark-Gluon String Model and Regge approach by Kaidalov (1994) and Titov (2008), as well as meson exchange frameworks by Haidenbauer (2010, 2016). It builds on the effective Lagrangian approach of Shyam (2014) and Sangkhakrit (2020) by incorporating HQSS and its violations explicitly. The results enable refined predictions for the PANDA experiment at FAIR, which aims for high-precision spectroscopy of charmed hadrons. The paper also provides heavy-quark sum rules that relate the 12 coupling constants, reducing them to 9 independent parameters for future theoretical and experimental studies.
