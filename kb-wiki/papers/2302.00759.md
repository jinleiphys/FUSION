# Searching for a dark matter particle with anti-protonic atoms

> A wide range of dark matter candidates have been proposed and are actively being searched for in a large number of experiments, both at high (TeV) and low (sub meV) energies. One dark matter candidate, a deeply bound $uuddss$ sexaquark, $S$, with mass $\sim 2$ GeV (having the same quark content as the hypothesized H-dibaryon, but long lived) is particularly difficult to explore experimentally. In this paper, we propose a scheme in which such a state could be produced at rest through the formation of $\bar{p}-^3$He antiprotonic atoms and their annihilation into $S$ + $K^+K^+π^-$, identified both through the unique tag of a S=+2, Q=+1 final state, as well as through full kinematic reconstruction of the final state recoiling against it.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a stable sexaquark dark matter candidate (a deeply bound $uuddss$ state with mass $\sim 2$ GeV) could be produced at rest via antiproton-³He annihilation into $S + K^+K^+\pi^-$, and that this signal would be identifiable through both the unique strangeness +2, charge +1 final state and full kinematic reconstruction, provided the relative production rate is at least $10^{-9}$ of all annihilations.

## Method

The authors simulate antiproton capture and annihilation on ³He using the Geant4 framework with the FTFP_BERT_HP physics list, generating $10^9$ annihilation events to search for $K^+K^+\pi^-$ final states. They then apply detector response assumptions (momentum thresholds of 10 MeV/$c$ for pions, 25 MeV/$c$ for kaons, 40 MeV/$c$ for protons, 80 MeV/$c$ for deuterons; 0.5% tracking inefficiency; 1% momentum resolution; 0.5% pion-kaon misidentification probability) to evaluate background contributions from misidentified particles and undetected spectator nucleons.

## Key numbers

- No $K^+K^+\pi^-$ events were found in $10^9$ simulated annihilations, consistent with the absence of multi-nucleon annihilation processes in the simulation code
- The proposed scheme should be sensitive to relative production rates down to $10^{-9}$ of all annihilations
- The total energy budget for the $\bar{p}$-³He system at rest is approximately 3750 MeV
- The sexaquark mass must satisfy $m_S < m_\Lambda + m_p + m_e = 2.054$ GeV for the particle to be stable against strong decay
- The geometric probability for a six-quark cluster configuration in ³He is 16%, and for a nine-quark cluster is 1%
- The process $\bar{p}d \rightarrow p\pi$ (requiring all nine quarks and antiquarks to interact) has a branching ratio of $10^{-5}$
- The OBELIX experiment observed $2K^+2\pi^-$ final states from $\bar{p}$⁴He annihilations with a yield of $(0.172 \pm 0.038) \times 10^{-4}$
- In the mass region around the deuteron, the expected background is 60-70 counts per 0.052 GeV/$c^2$ for $10^{10}$ annihilations

## Context

This work extends the search strategy for sexaquark dark matter proposed by Farrar (2018) and addresses the difficulty of detecting this candidate that would evade both high-energy accelerator searches and low-mass quantum sensor experiments. The approach contrasts with prior H-dibaryon searches (Jaffe, 1977) that relied on decays into $\Lambda\Lambda$, $\Lambda p\pi$, or $\Lambda n$ final states, or on exclusive co-production with $\bar{\Lambda}\bar{\Lambda}$ in $\Upsilon$ decays (PhysRevLett.110.222002, 2013). The proposed method enables a clean experimental tag for sexaquark production at low energy, complementing high-multiplicity searches at the LHC (Farrar, 2017; Farrar, 2018) and potentially resolving questions about the hadron vacuum polarization contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment.
