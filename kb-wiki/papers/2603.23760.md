# Meson mixing effects on the speed of sound in isospin-imbalanced matter

> We explore isospin imbalanced strongly interacting matter within the two-flavor Linear Sigma Model with quarks, an effective model for low-energy QCD. At one loop order, including quark, pion, and sigma fluctuations while respecting chiral symmetry, we find that the formation of an isospin condensate necessarily gives rise to a Goldstone mode. This mode enforces a nontrivial relation between the chiral and isospin condensates through the mixing of charged pions and the sigma field in the condensed phase. From the resulting thermodynamic potential, we compute the speed of sound and observe a pronounced peak as a function of the isospin chemical potential. Although the peak of the speed of sound may be described at tree-level and including only quarks in the analysis, meson dynamics introduces further constraints that influence the position and width of the peak which making it to align well with lattice QCD simulations. Therefore we identify that the shape and position of the peak is a consequence of the Goldstone mode dynamics and of the associated charged pion sigma mixing.

# Meson mixing effects on the speed of sound in isospin-imbalanced matter

## Key claim

The peak in the speed of sound as a function of isospin chemical potential arises from the mixing of charged pions and the sigma field in the condensed phase, mediated by the Goldstone mode dynamics; although a peak can be obtained at tree-level with only quarks, the one-loop meson dynamics (including the nontrivial Goldstone condition) are necessary to align the peak position and width with lattice QCD simulations.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Linear Sigma Model with quarks (LSMq) at one-loop order, including quark, pion, and sigma fluctuations while respecting chiral symmetry. They compute the effective potential from fermion and boson contributions, where the boson sector requires diagonalizing a mixed propagator matrix for the sigma field and charged pions in the condensed phase. The isospin condensate is constrained by requiring a massless Goldstone mode from the broken U(1)_{I_3} symmetry, yielding two possible solutions (Δ₁ and Δ₂), of which Δ₁ represents the true Goldstone mode. The speed of sound is then computed from the thermodynamic potential as c_s² = ∂P/∂ε.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum quark masses m_f are varied between 200 MeV and 300 MeV, calibrated against lattice QCD results from Brandt et al. (2022) and Abbott et al. (2025)
- For m_f = 200 MeV: λ = 8.33, m_σ = 423.792 MeV (from JHEP 07, 055 (2022) calibration)
- For m_f = 300 MeV: λ = 18.74, m_σ = 616.117 MeV (from Phys. Rev. Lett. 134, 1 (2025) calibration)
- The isospin chemical potential is explored up to μ_I/m_π ∼ 4
- The speed of sound squared exceeds the conformal bound c_s² = 1/3 for both parameter sets
- The lower quark mass (m_f = 200 MeV) shifts the peak to slightly lower μ_I values and reduces its magnitude compared to m_f = 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pion condensation in isospin-asymmetric matter using effective models, particularly the LSMq (quark-meson model) where Chiba (2024) showed that quantum corrections from quark loops produce saturation of the isospin condensate and qualitative agreement with lattice QCD. The present paper goes beyond that by explicitly including meson loops and the charged pion-sigma mixing, which was absent in earlier treatments. The results enable calibration of effective model parameters against lattice QCD data from Brandt et al. (2022) and Abbott et al. (2025), and the authors note that incorporating additional degrees of freedom such as the rho meson will be essential for extending validity to μ_I ≥ 4m_π.
