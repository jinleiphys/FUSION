# Mean-field calculations of quasi-elastic responses in 4He

> We present calculations of the quasi-elastic responses functions in 4He based upon a mean-field model used to perform analogous calculations in heavier nuclei. The meson exchange current contribution is small if compared with the results of calculations where short-range correlations are explicitly considered. It is argued that the presence of these correlations in the description of the nuclear wave functions is crucial to make meson exchange current effects appreciable.

# Mean-field calculations of quasi-elastic responses in 4He

## Key claim

Within a mean-field model, the meson exchange current (MEC) contribution to quasi-elastic response functions in 4He is small (a few percent at peak energies), similar to results previously obtained for medium-heavy nuclei such as 12C and 40Ca. The authors argue that this smallness is due to the absence of short-range correlations (especially their tensor components) in the mean-field description, and that such correlations are crucial for making MEC effects appreciable.

## Method

The authors applied a mean-field model previously used for 12C and 40Ca (Anguiano 1994) to 4He. The ground state was described as a Slater determinant of single-particle wave functions from a Woods-Saxon potential; excited states were built as 1p-1h and 2p-2h excitations with continuum particle wave functions from the same potential. The longitudinal response was calculated with the one-body charge operator, while the transverse response included one-body convection and magnetization currents plus two-body MEC (seagull, pion-in-flight, and Delta-isobar terms). Three Woods-Saxon parametrizations were tested: WS1 (fit to single-particle energies), WS2 (fit to charge density), and WS3 (fit to longitudinal response data).

## Key numbers

- MEC contribution to the transverse response at peak energies in 4He is of the order of a few percent, rather independent of the Woods-Saxon potential used.
- For momentum transfers above 400 MeV/c, the MEC contribution at peak energies (including the Delta-isobar current) becomes negative.
- The effect of the spin-orbit potential on the calculated responses is less than 1%.
- In comparison, Leidemann and Orlandini (1990) found that adding tensor pieces of short-range correlations increases the relative MEC contribution to 10-15% at peak energy in 4He.
- In a relativistic Fermi gas calculation for 40Ca by Blunden and Butler (1989), MEC effects were evaluated to be of the order of 10% (without Delta-isobar current), matching the authors' own results when that component is switched off.
- The Gent group (Slu 1995) found MEC effects of 20-30% of the quasi-elastic peak strength within a non-relativistic Hartree-Fock-RPA model.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous mean-field calculations of quasi-elastic responses in 12C and 40Ca (Anguiano 1994) to the light nucleus 4He, to test whether the small MEC effects seen in medium-heavy nuclei might be due to cancellations among many particle-hole excitations rather than the absence of short-range correlations. The results contradict the pattern seen in few-body systems (deuteron, 3He, 4He) studied with Faddeev equations (Fri 1991), hyperspherical functions (Ros 1990), or Green function Monte Carlo (Car 1991), where MEC produce large effects. The authors conclude that explicit treatment of short-range correlations in medium-heavy nuclei is needed to resolve the discrepancy, and note that RPA correlations (Slu 1995) may also enhance MEC contributions, though earlier continuum RPA calculations (Anguiano 1993, Bub 1991) showed little difference from mean-field results.
