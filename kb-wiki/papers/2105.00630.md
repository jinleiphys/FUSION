# QCD equation of state and the structure of neutron stars in NJL model

> The chiral and pion superfluidity phase transitions are studied within NJL model. The model parameters are fitted by pion mass and decay constant together with recent isospin density from lattice QCD. The dense and cold QCD matter suffered the first order chiral phase transition during the quark chemical potential increase. Based on the studies of phase structures, the equation of states~(EoS) of dense QCD matter are calculated with the conditions of $β$ equilibrium and electric charge neutrality. Take the EoS as input, the mass-radius relation of neutron stars are predicted, which is consistent with recent observations of heaviest mass and radius of $1.4M_\odot$.

## Key claim
The NJL model, with parameters fitted to lattice QCD isospin density data, predicts a maximum neutron star mass around \(2M_\odot\) and a radius for a \(1.4M_\odot\) star consistent with the PREX collaboration's recent upper limit of \(14.26~\mathrm{km}\), but the model's chiral phase transition is first-order at a specific quark chemical potential, a detail the abstract does not emphasize.

## Method
The author uses a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation to compute the thermodynamic potential at zero temperature. Model parameters (bare quark mass \(m=0.005\) GeV, cutoff \(\Lambda=0.66\) GeV, coupling \(G=4.8~\mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\)) are fitted to the pion mass, decay constant, and lattice QCD isospin density data. The equation of state (EoS) is then derived under \(\beta\)-equilibrium and electric charge neutrality conditions, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain the mass-radius relation for neutron stars.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass used for parameter fitting: \(m_\pi = 0.135~\mathrm{MeV}\) (stated as \(0.135~\mathrm{MeV}\) in text, likely a typo for \(0.135~\mathrm{GeV}\)).
- Critical quark chemical potential for chiral phase transition at \(\mu_I=0\): \(\mu_q^c = 0.333~\mathrm{GeV}\).
- Onset of nonzero quark number density at \(\mu_I=0\): \(\mu_q = 0.31~\mathrm{GeV}\).
- Tricritical point in the \(\mu_q-\mu_I\) plane: \((\mu_q, \mu_I) = (0.270, 0.158)~\mathrm{GeV}\).
- Maximum neutron star mass predicted: roughly \(2M_\odot\).
- Radius constraint for a \(1.4M_\odot\) neutron star from PREX collaboration, quoted as consistent with the model: \(13.25~\mathrm{km} \lesssim R^{1.4M_\odot} \lesssim 14.26~\mathrm{km}\).
- Observed mass of pulsar J0348+0432: \(2.01 \pm 0.04~M_\odot\).
- Observed mass of pulsar J1614-2230: \(1.97 \pm 0.04~M_\odot\).

## Context
This work extends previous NJL model studies of dense QCD by fitting parameters to recent lattice QCD isospin density data (PhysRevD.98.094510), rather than only to vacuum observables. It enables a direct comparison of the predicted mass-radius relation with observations of heavy pulsars such as PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013) and PSR J1614-2230 (Arzoumanian 2018), as well as the radius constraint from the PREX collaboration (PhysRevLett.126.172503). The results are also compared with NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 (Riley 2019, Miller 2019).
