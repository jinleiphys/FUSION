# Subthreshold K+ Meson Production in Proton-Nucleus Reactions

> The inclusive K+ mesons production in proton-nucleus collisions in the near threshold and subthreshold energy regimes is analyzed with respect to the one-step (pN->K+YN, Y=Lambda,Sigma) and the two-step (pN->NNpi, NN2pi; piN->K+Y) incoherent production processes on the basis of an appropriate new folding model, which takes properly into account the struck target nucleon removal energy and momentum distribution (nucleon spectral function), extracted from recent quasielastic electron scattering experiments and from many-body calculations with realistic models of the NN interaction. Comparison of the model calculations of the K+ double differential cross sections for the reaction p+C at 1.2, 1.5 and 2.5 GeV beam energies with the existing experimental data from the SATURNE experiment is given, illustrating both the relative role of the primary and secondary production channels at considered incident energies and those features of the cross sections which are sensitive to the high momentum and high removal energy part of the nucleon spectral function that is governed by nucleon-nucleon short-range and tensor correlations. In--medium modifications of the available for pion and hyperon production invariant energies squared due to the respective optical potentials are needed to account for the experimental data on K+ production in the energy region under consideration.

# Subthreshold K+ Meson Production in Proton-Nucleus Reactions

## Key claim

The two-step production mechanism (pN → NNπ, NN2π; πN → K+Y) dominates K+ production at subthreshold beam energies, but the high-momentum and high-removal-energy part of the nucleon spectral function (generated by nucleon-nucleon short-range and tensor correlations) contributes only weakly to inclusive K+ yields, making inclusive subthreshold kaon production in pA collisions unsuitable for studying these high-momentum components. In-medium modifications of the invariant energies squared for pion and hyperon production via optical potentials are required to reproduce experimental data.

## Method

The authors developed a folding model that incorporates the full nucleon spectral function P(p_t, E) (including both the single-particle uncorrelated part P_0 and the correlated part P_1 from NN correlations) to compute inclusive K+ double differential cross sections for p+C12 reactions. They considered one-step processes (pN → K+YN, Y=Λ,Σ) and two-step processes (pN → NNπ, NN2π followed by πN → K+Y), using three-body and four-body phase space calculations normalized to total cross sections for elementary reactions, and included in-medium modifications via momentum-dependent optical potentials for the proton, nucleons, and hyperons.

## Key numbers

- The free K+ production threshold in nucleon-nucleon collisions is 1.58 GeV.
- The free threshold energies for pion production reactions pN → NNπ and pN → NN2π are 0.29 GeV and 0.60 GeV, respectively.
- The free threshold energies for kaon production via pions πN → K+Λ and πN → K+Σ are 0.76 GeV and 0.89 GeV, respectively.
- The effective number of nucleons for the pN → K+YN reaction on C12 is 6.9 (for a Gaussian nuclear density).
- The single-particle part P_0 of the spectral function was taken as 0.8 times the harmonic oscillator spectral function, with s-shell and p-shell binding energies of 34 MeV and 16 MeV, respectively.
- NN correlations deplete states below the Fermi sea by 20 ± 5%.
- The high-momentum region where correlations dominate is for nucleon momenta p_t ≥ 2 fm^{-1}.
- The contribution to K+ production from the correlated part P_1 alone is an order of magnitude smaller than contributions from mixed uncorrelated/correlated parts in the two-step channels.
- At 2.5 GeV beam energy, the one-step process dominates; at subthreshold energies (≤ 1.5 GeV), the two-step mechanism dominates.
- The two-step model with only the uncorrelated spectral function reproduces total K+ cross sections above about 900 MeV but underestimates data at lower bombarding energies.

## Context

This work extends earlier folding models by Debowski et al. (1995) and Sibirtsev et al. (1996) that used nucleon spectral functions for subthreshold kaon production, but provides a unified treatment of both total and differential cross sections within the same approach. It contradicts the expectation that high-momentum components from NN correlations could be probed via inclusive subthreshold kaon production, finding instead that the uncorrelated part of the spectral function dominates. The authors suggest that the π+ + A → K+ + X reaction in the subthreshold regime is more promising for studying short-range correlations, as shown in their earlier work (Paryev, 1995). The results enable future experiments at COSY-Julich and CELSIUS to focus on exclusive measurements or different reaction channels to access nuclear correlation effects.
