# Progress in Nonequilibrium Quantum Field Theory

> We review recent developments for the description of far-from-equilibrium dynamics of quantum fields and subsequent thermalization.

## Key claim

This review argues that two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action techniques, particularly the 2PI 1/N-expansion and coupling-expansion, can describe far-from-equilibrium dynamics and subsequent thermalization from first principles without further assumptions, but the paper notes that these methods have not yet been successfully extended to gauge fields due to Ward identity violations.

## Method

The authors review the 2PI effective action formalism for scalar and fermionic quantum field theories, which is parametrized by connected one- and two-point functions and contains all contributions beyond one-loop order as a sum over closed 2PI diagrams. They discuss systematic approximation schemes including the 2PI 1/N-expansion (with LO scaling as N, NLO as 1, NNLO as 1/N) and the 2PI coupling-expansion, and compare results from these approximations to exact classical statistical field theory solutions obtained via Monte Carlo techniques.

## Key numbers

- For the 2PI 1/N-expansion, LO contributions scale as ~N, NLO as ~1, and NNLO as ~1/N (Section 3)
- In the classical field limit with N=4, NLO results follow exact (MC) solutions closely for both early-time far-from-equilibrium dynamics and late-time behavior (Figure 3)
- For N=1, the three-loop contribution to the damping rate in a coupling expansion is ~(lambda/N)^2[1+2/N], while the NLO 1/N-expansion gives a factor of three less for the latter (Section 4)
- For parametric resonance with lambda=10^-6, early-time exponential amplification of modes near momentum p0 occurs with rate ~2*gamma0, followed by enhanced growth with rate ~6*gamma0 for a broad momentum range (Figure 5)
- For the chiral quark-meson model in 3+1 dimensions, late-time thermalization yields temperature T=0.94m (in units of scalar thermal mass) for both fermions and scalars (Figure 8)

## Context

This review extends the line of work on nonequilibrium quantum field theory that began with the 2PI effective action formalism of Cornwall, Jackiw, and Tomboulis (1974) and the closed time path method of Kadanoff and Baym (1962) and Chou et al. (1985). It contrasts with earlier mean-field type approximations (Hartree-Fock, leading-order large-N) and 1PI 1/N-expansions, which either fail to describe exponential damping and thermalization or become secular in time. The work enables quantitative first-principles descriptions of early-universe preheating, relativistic heavy-ion collision dynamics, and out-of-equilibrium chiral phase transitions, though the extension to gauge fields remains an open problem.
