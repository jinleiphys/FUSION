# Possible chiral symmetry in $^{138}$Nd

> The pheomenological Generalized Coherent State Model Hamiltonian is amended with a many body term describing a set of nucleons moving in a shell model mean-field and interacting among themselves with paring, as well as with a particle-core interaction involving a quadrupole-quadrupole and a hexadecapole-hexdecapole force and a spin-spin interaction. The model Hamiltonian is treated in a restricted space consisting of the core projected states associated to the bands ground, $β, γ,\widetildeγ, 1^+$ and $\widetilde{1^+}$ and two proton aligned quasiparticles coupled to the states of the ground band. The chirally transformed particle-core states are also included. The Hamiltonian contains two terms which are not invariant to the chiral transformations relating the right handed trihedral $({\bf J_F}, {\bf J_p}, {\bf J_n})$ and the left handed ones $(-{\bf J_F}, {\bf J_p}, {\bf J_n})$, $({\bf J_F}, -{\bf J_p}, {\bf J_n})$, $({\bf J_F}, {\bf J_p}, -{\bf J_n})$ where ${\bf J_F}, {\bf J_p}, {\bf J_n}$ are the angular momenta carried by fermions, proton and neutron bosons, respectively. The energies defined with the particle-core states form four chiral bands, two of them being degenerate. Electromagnetic properties of the chiral bands are investigated. Results are compared with the experimental data on $^{138}$Nd.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new type of chiral symmetry can exist in even-even nuclei, specifically in $^{138}$Nd, where the chiral bands arise from a system consisting of a triaxial core and two aligned proton quasiparticles, rather than from the more common particle-hole configuration. The chiral symmetry is not exact but is identified through the near-degeneracy of bands that result from breaking the symmetry via terms in the Hamiltonian that are not invariant under chiral transformations.

## Method

The authors extended the Generalized Coherent State Model (GCSM) by adding a particle-core Hamiltonian that includes quadrupole-quadrupole, hexadecapole-hexadecapole, and spin-spin interactions between two proton quasiparticles and a core described by six collective bands (ground, beta, gamma, tilde-gamma, 1+, and tilde-1+). They constructed a restricted basis of particle-core states by coupling two aligned proton quasiparticles (with maximum angular momentum projection) to the ground band and dipole band states of the core, and also included chirally transformed states. The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in this basis, and the energies and electromagnetic properties of the resulting four chiral bands (two of which are degenerate) were calculated and compared to experimental data on $^{138}$Nd.

## Key numbers

- The order parameter $\epsilon = \frac{({\bf J_p}\times {\bf J_n})\cdot{\bf J_F}}{\sqrt{J_p(J_p+1)J_n(J_n+1)J_F(J_F+1)}}$ reaches its maximum value of 1 (indicating mutually orthogonal angular momenta) for total angular momentum I=11 and I=12.
- The angle between core proton and neutron angular momenta reaches $\pi/2$ (orthogonality) at a critical rotational frequency, enabling optimal conditions for large magnetic dipole transitions.
- The spin-spin interaction term $-X_{sS}{\bf J_F}\cdot{\bf J_c}$ produces eigenvalues $\lambda_{JI}$ that are mirror images about zero, with the chiral partner bands having eigenvalues $+\lambda_{JI}$ and $-\lambda_{JI}$.
- The ratio ${\cal I}^{(2)}/B(E2)$ for magnetic dipole bands in nearly spherical nuclei can take large values of order 100 (eb)$^{-2}$MeV$^{-1}$, and typical dipole bands have $B(M1)\sim 1-3 \mu^2_N$ with $B(E2)\sim 0.1$ (eb)$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of chiral symmetry in triaxial nuclei (Raduta 2014, 2015) which were applied to $^{192}$Pt, $^{188}$Os, and $^{190}$Os. The present paper introduces a new type of chiral motion specific to even-even nuclei, using $^{138}$Nd as a test case where experimental data are available from Petrache and Li. The formalism enables the identification of four chiral bands (two degenerate) and provides a framework for predicting electromagnetic transition properties that can be tested against future experimental measurements in the A=130-140 mass region.
