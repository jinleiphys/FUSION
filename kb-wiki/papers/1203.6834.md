# Using effective field theory to analyse low-energy Compton scattering data from protons and light nuclei

> Compton scattering provides important insight into the structure of the nucleon. For photons up to about 300 MeV, it is parameterised by six dynamical dipole polarisabilities which characterise the response of the nucleon to a monochromatic photon of fixed frequency and multipolarity. Their zero-energy limit yields the well-known static electric and magnetic dipole polarisabilities αand β, and the four dipole spin polarisabilities. Chiral Effective Field Theory (ChiEFT) describes nucleon, deuteron and 3-He Compton scattering, using consistent nuclear currents, rescattering and wave functions. It can thus also be used to extract useful information on the neutron amplitude from Compton scattering on light nuclei. We summarise past work in ChiEFT on all of these reactions and compare with other theoretical approaches. We also discuss all proton experiments up to about 400 MeV, as well as the three modern elastic deuteron data sets, paying particular attention to precision and accuracy of each set. Constraining the Delta(1232) parameters from the resonance region, we then perform new fits to the proton data up to omega(lab)=170 MeV, and a new fit to the deuteron data. After checking in each case that a two-parameter fit is compatible with the respective Baldin sum rules, we obtain, using the sum-rule constraints in a one-parameter fit, α=10.7\pm0.3(stat)\pm0.2(Baldin)\pm0.8(theory), β=3.1\mp0.3(stat)\pm0.2(Baldin)\pm0.8(theory), for the proton polarisabilities, and α=10.9\pm 0.9(stat)\pm0.2(Baldin)\pm0.8(theory), β=3.6\mp 0.9(stat)\pm0.2(Baldin)\pm0.8(theory), for the isoscalar polarisabilities, each in units of 10^(-4) fm^3. We discuss plans for polarised Compton scattering, their promise as tools to access spin polarisabilities, and other future avenues for theoretical and experimental investigation.

## Key claim

Chiral Effective Field Theory (ChiEFT) provides a unified framework for describing Compton scattering on protons, deuterons, and 3-He from the Thomson limit up to the pion-production threshold, but the extraction of nucleon polarisabilities from deuteron data requires including both rescattering contributions and an explicit Delta(1232) degree of freedom to achieve the accuracy needed for meaningful results.

## Method

The authors performed new fits to proton Compton scattering data up to omega(lab)=170 MeV and to deuteron data, using ChiEFT with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom and a unified treatment of two kinematic regimes (Regime I near the Thomson limit and Regime II at higher energies). For the deuteron, the Compton amplitude was computed as a convolution of irreducible photonuclear kernels with deuteron wave functions, including both single-nucleon and two-nucleon contributions, with rescattering effects treated perturbatively. The fits used the Baldin sum rule as a constraint, first checking that two-parameter fits were compatible with the sum rule, then performing one-parameter fits with the sum-rule constraint applied.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric dipole polarisability: alpha = 10.7 +/- 0.3(stat) +/- 0.2(Baldin) +/- 0.8(theory) in units of 10^(-4) fm^3
- Proton magnetic dipole polarisability: beta = 3.1 -/+ 0.3(stat) +/- 0.2(Baldin) +/- 0.8(theory) in units of 10^(-4) fm^3
- Isoscalar electric dipole polarisability: alpha = 10.9 +/- 0.9(stat) +/- 0.2(Baldin) +/- 0.8(theory) in units of 10^(-4) fm^3
- Isoscalar magnetic dipole polarisability: beta = 3.6 -/+ 0.9(stat) +/- 0.2(Baldin) +/- 0.8(theory) in units of 10^(-4) fm^3
- The deuteron database covers photon energies from 49 to 95 MeV in the lab frame
- The Thomson limit cross section is reproduced to better than 0.2% accuracy in the unified approach
- Wave function dependence of deuteron cross section predictions is reduced from about +/-5% to less than +/-0.5% at 100 MeV when the Thomson limit constraint is applied

## Context

This work extends the ChiEFT calculations of Beane et al. (1999, 2004) and Hildebrandt et al. (2004, 2005) for deuteron Compton scattering by providing a unified treatment of both low-energy and higher-energy regimes, and by performing new fits to both proton and deuteron data. The inclusion of an explicit Delta(1232) resonance resolves discrepancies between earlier calculations and the Hornidge (2000) SAL data at 94.5 MeV. The results enable future extraction of spin polarisabilities from polarised Compton scattering experiments, and the framework can be extended to other light nuclei such as 3-He.
