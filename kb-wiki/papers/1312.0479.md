# The strength of beta-decays to the continuum

> The beta-strength in beta-delayed particle decays has up to now been defined in a somewhat ad hoc manner that depends on the decay mechanism. A simple, consistent definition is presented that fulfils the beta strength sum rules. Special consideration is given to the modifications needed when employing R-matrix fits to data. As an example the 11Be(beta-p) decay is investigated through simple models.

# The strength of beta-decays to the continuum

## Key claim

The paper proposes a consistent definition of beta strength for beta-delayed particle decays that fulfills the Gamow-Teller sum rule, but notes that this definition differs from the strength used in R-matrix formalism and that for broad resonances or large Q-windows the simple approximation of integrating only near the resonance peak may be insufficient.

## Method

The author defines the beta strength for unbound final states via the decay rate expression w(E)dE = ln2 (g_A^2/K) f(Q-E) B_GT(E) dE, which separates the weak interaction part from the strong interaction part. The definition is examined through the Gamow-Teller sum rule using Berggren's formalism for continuum states, and compared to existing R-matrix treatments of sequential decays through resonances. A simple discretized continuum direct decay (DCDD) model is applied to the 11Be(beta-p) decay as an illustrative example, using square well and Woods-Saxon potentials with a confining quantization volume.

## Key numbers

- The 11Be neutron separation energy used in the model is 501.64(25) keV, taken from mass measurements (mas12).
- The Q-window for 11Be(beta-p) decay is 280.7 keV.
- The square well potential radius used is 4.0 fm, with potential strength adjusted to 33.819 MeV for the initial state.
- For the final state, potential depths in the range 33-34 MeV produce significantly higher overlaps (resonance-like behavior).
- The correction factor (1+gamma^2 dS/dE)^{-1} for R-matrix parameters decreases from approximately 1/2.5 at 50 keV to 1/3 at 200 keV in the 11Be case.
- The Wigner limit for gamma^2 is taken as hbar^2/(m a^2) with channel radius a = 1.4(1+10^{1/3}) fm.
- For 12B and 12N decays, summed strength to identified states up to the 12.71 MeV state is 1.0-1.1, while bin-by-bin summation gives about 0.6 for 12N.

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions by Barker (1969, 1988, 1996) and Nyman (1990) on how beta strength should be assigned to unbound levels, providing a consistent definition independent of the decay mechanism. The paper builds on Berggren's formalism (1968, 1993) for continuum states and the R-matrix framework of Lane and Thomas (1958). The proposed definition enables consistent comparison between experimental beta-strength determinations and theoretical calculations, particularly for halo nuclei decays such as those reviewed by Pfutzner (2012), and resolves the discrepancy in sum-rule violations seen in 12N decays studied by Hyldegaard (2009, 2010).
