# On the possibility of revealing the transition of a baryon pair state to a six-quark confinement state

> Proton-proton collisions are considered to find favourable conditions for searching for the transition of a baryon pair state to a hexa-quark confinement state $(3q)+(3q)\rightarrow(6q)_\mathrm{cnf}$. It is admitted that central $pp$ collisions in a definite range of the initial energy can lead to creation of an intermediate compound system where the hexa-quark dibaryon can be formed. Criteria for selection of central collision events and for manifestation of the quark-structure dibaryon production are proposed.

## Key claim

Central proton-proton collisions in a specific energy range may create an intermediate compound system where a hexa-quark dibaryon can form, but the paper hedges that this transition requires both spatial overlap of the nucleon quark cores (impact parameter < 0.4 fm) and sufficient energy to overcome the short-range repulsion between nucleons, conditions not met in previous searches.

## Method

The author analyzes the kinematic conditions for the transition (3q)+(3q)→(6q)_cnf in proton-proton collisions, deriving criteria for centrality: impact parameter less than 0.4 fm, transverse momentum transfer above 500 MeV/c, and interaction volume radius constrained by produced mass M ≥ 400 MeV/c². The paper proposes studying the inclusive reaction p+p→d at 90° + X in the energy range √s ≈ 2.5–6 GeV (T_lab ≈ 2–20 GeV), where the deuteron is detected at 90° in the center-of-mass system along with an accompanying meson system.

## Key numbers

- Impact parameter requirement: R_imp < 0.4 fm for central NN collision
- Transverse momentum transfer needed: q_⊥ > 500 MeV/c
- Mass threshold for constraining interaction volume: M ≥ 400 MeV/c²
- Short-range repulsion potential V(0) estimated at 0.5–1.0 GeV, corresponding to √s^min = 2.4–2.9 GeV and T_lab^min ≈ 1.1–2.5 GeV
- Upper energy boundary: √s^max ≈ 5.9–6.1 GeV, T_lab^max ≈ 16.5–19.8 GeV
- Baryon density in intermediate six-quark system: ρ_NN ≈ 4.3 fm⁻³, exceeding critical deconfinement density ρ_c = 0.85 fm⁻³
- Energy density in intermediate system: ε_NN ≈ 4 GeV fm⁻³, exceeding critical ε_c = 1 GeV fm⁻³
- Width of previously observed isoscalar NN resonance: Γ ≈ 70 MeV (Bashkanov 2009, Adlarson 2014)
- Quark momentum threshold for leaving six-quark space: 0.84 GeV/c

## Context

This paper extends the long search for dibaryon states that began with SU(3) symmetry considerations (Oakes 1963) and SU(6) classification (Dyson 1964), and later quark-bag model predictions (Aerts 1978, Mulders 1980). It argues that previously observed resonances in pp collisions at 0.7 GeV (Komarov 2016) and pn collisions at 1.14 GeV (Bashkanov 2009, Adlarson 2014) are likely meson-baryon in nature rather than genuine quark-structure dibaryons, because they did not satisfy the centrality criteria. The proposed reaction p+p→d at 90°+X enables next-step searches for hexa-quark confinement states and could provide experimental input for nonperturbative confinement theory, while also offering a general kinematic test to distinguish genuine elementary hadrons from composite states, extending the approach of Weinberg (1965).
