# Long-range chiral dynamics of $Λ$-hyperon in nuclear media

> We extend a chiral effective field theory approach to the $Λ$-nuclei interaction with the inclusion of the decuplet baryons. More precisely, we study the contributions due to the long-range two-pion exchange, with $Σ$ and $Σ^*$ baryons in the internal baryonic lines considering Nh and $Δ$h excitations. In particular, central and spin-orbit potentials are studied. For the former, regularization is needed and physical values of the cut-off give a large attraction, becoming necessary to include the repulsion of other terms not considered here. For the latter, in a model-independent framework, the inclusion of the decuplet supports the natural explanation of the smallness of the $Λ$-nuclear spin-orbit term and shows the importance of the $Σ^*$ and $Δ$ degrees of freedom for the hyperon-nucleus interactions.

## Key claim

The inclusion of decuplet baryons (Δ and Σ*) in a chiral effective field theory approach to the Λ-nucleus interaction shows that the natural cancellation between long-range and short-range pieces still explains the smallness of the Λ-nuclear spin-orbit term, but only because the Σ* contributions (positive sign) cancel against the Δ contributions (negative sign), restoring the result previously obtained with only Σ intermediate states.

## Method

The authors computed the two-pion exchange contributions to the Λ-nucleus central and spin-orbit potentials using chiral effective field theory, including Σ and Σ* baryons in internal baryonic lines and considering both nucleon-hole (Nh) and Δ-hole (Δh) excitations of nuclear matter. They performed a non-relativistic reduction expanding the potentials in powers of the average baryon mass M_B, regularizing ultraviolet-divergent integrals with a momentum cut-off Λ̄ = 0.6 GeV. The spin-orbit potentials were obtained by considering the Λ-hyperon scattering from a weakly inhomogeneous medium and extracting the antisymmetric spin-dependent part of the self-energy.

## Key numbers

- Central potential from long-range two-pion exchange at saturation density: U_Λ^(l-r)(ρ₀) ≈ -250 MeV (strongly attractive, requiring short-range repulsion for a realistic depth)
- Coupling constants used: D = 0.84, F = 0.46 (SU(3) axial-vector constants), g_A = D + F = 1.3, f_π = 92.4 MeV
- Decuplet coupling constants: C_Σ* = 1.7 (from Σ* → πΛ decay), C_Δ = 2.0 (from Δ → πN decay)
- Mass splittings: M_Σ - M_Λ = Δ²/M_B ≈ 77 MeV, M_Σ* - M_Λ = Δ*²/M_B ≈ 292 MeV, M_Δ - M_N = Δ_f²/M_B ≈ 270 MeV
- Pion mass: m_π = 138 MeV
- Short-range spin-orbit parametrization: U_Λls^(shell)(k_f) = 21.3 C_l MeV fm² ρ/ρ₀, with C_l between 1/2 and 2/3, based on U_Nls^(shell)(ρ₀) = 30 MeV fm³

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach of Kaiser (2004) for the Λ-nucleus interaction by including decuplet baryons (Δ and Σ*), motivated by the known importance of Δ-hole excitations in pion physics (Oset, 1981) and by previous studies showing the relevance of decuplet intermediate states in two-meson exchange YN potentials (Holzenkamp, 1989; Sasaki, 2006). The results show that while the Δ-hole contributions would spoil the natural cancellation found by Kaiser (2004), the Σ* contributions restore it, supporting the explanation of the weak Λ-nuclear spin-orbit force. The central potential requires inclusion of short-range correlations (as in Tolos, 2006) to obtain realistic depths.
