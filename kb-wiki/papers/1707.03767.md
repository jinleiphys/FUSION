# Probing medium-induced jet splitting and energy loss in heavy-ion collisions

> The nuclear modification of jet splitting in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC energies is studied based on the higher twist formalism. Assuming coherent energy loss for the two splitted subjets, a non-monotonic jet energy dependence is found for the nuclear modification of jet splitting function: strongest modification at intermediate jet energies whereas weaker modification for larger or smaller jet energies. Combined with the smaller size and lower density of the QGP medium at RHIC than at the LHC, this explains the CMS-STAR groomed jet puzzle -- strong nuclear modification of the momentum sharing $z_g$ distribution at the LHC whereas no obvious modification of the $z_g$ distribution at RHIC. In contrast, the observed nuclear modification pattern of the groomed jet $z_g$ distribution cannot be explained solely by independent energy loss of the two subjets. Our result may be tested in future measurements of groomed jets with lower jet energies at the LHC and larger jet energies at RHIC, for different angular separations between the two subjets.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nuclear modification of the jet splitting function in heavy-ion collisions exhibits a non-monotonic dependence on jet energy, with the strongest modification at intermediate jet energies and weaker modification at both larger and smaller jet energies, which can explain the apparent contradiction between CMS and STAR measurements of the groomed jet momentum sharing \(z_g\) distribution.

## Method

The authors use the higher twist formalism to compute both vacuum and medium-induced contributions to the parton splitting function, coupling the medium-induced splitting to (3+1)-dimensional and (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic models (CLVisc and VISHNew) that provide the space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma. The observed \(z_g\) distribution is obtained by convolving the splitting function with initial parton spectra from leading-order perturbative QCD and jet energy loss calculated using the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model, assuming coherent energy loss for the two subjets. The nuclear modification factor \(R_{p(z_g)}\) is defined as the ratio of the medium-modified \(p(z_g)\) in A+A collisions to the vacuum \(p(z_g)\) in p+p collisions.

## Key numbers

* The initial quark transport coefficient \(\hat{q}_0\) is taken as 4 GeV\(^2\)/fm for central Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and 2 GeV\(^2\)/fm for central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV.
* The maximal nuclear modification of the groomed jet momentum sharing \(z_g\) distribution is predicted at around 50-60 GeV at RHIC and at around 70-90 GeV at the LHC.
* The medium-induced splitting function shape transitions from \(1/x^3\) at large jet energies to \(1/x\) at small jet energies.
* The fractional contribution from medium-induced splitting to the total jet splitting probability becomes very small for jet \(p_\mathrm{T} > 400\)-500 GeV at the LHC.

## Context

This work extends the higher twist formalism for jet splitting in heavy-ion collisions to address the "CMS-STAR groomed jet puzzle": CMS observed strong nuclear modification of the \(z_g\) distribution at the LHC (with strength diminishing with increasing jet \(p_\mathrm{T}\)), while STAR observed no obvious modification at lower jet energies at RHIC. The study contradicts the expectation from independent (incoherent) energy loss of the two subjets, which cannot explain the observed modification pattern, and instead supports coherent energy loss. The results enable future tests with groomed jets at lower energies at the LHC and larger energies at RHIC, and for different angular separations between subjets.
