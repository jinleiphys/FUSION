# Isospin sum rules for bottom-baryon weak decays

> Isospin symmetry, as the most precise flavor symmetry, can be used to extract information about hadronic dynamics. The effective Hamiltonian operators of bottom quark weak decays are zero under a series of isospin lowering operators $I_-^n$, which permits us to generate isospin sum rules without the Wigner-Eckart invariants. In this work, we derive hundreds of isospin sum rules for the two- and three-body non-leptonic decays of bottom baryons. They provide hints for new decay modes and the isospin partners of pentaquark states.

## Key claim

The authors derive hundreds of isospin sum rules for two- and three-body non-leptonic decays of bottom baryons using a systematic approach that does not require Wigner-Eckart invariants, based on the property that effective Hamiltonian operators of bottom quark weak decays are zero under a series of isospin lowering operators \(I_-^n\).

## Method

The authors apply the isospin lowering operator \(I_-\) and its powers \(I_-^n\) to the effective Hamiltonian of bottom quark weak decays, exploiting the fact that \(I_-^n H = 0\) for specific \(n\) depending on the quark-level transition (e.g., \(n \geq 3\) for \(b \to c\overline u d\)). They then act with \(I_-^n\) on the initial and final hadron states, generating linear combinations of decay amplitudes that must sum to zero, producing isospin sum rules. The coefficient matrices for \(I_-\) acting on various hadron multiplets (bottom baryon anti-triplet, charm meson anti-triplet, pseudoscalar meson octet, light baryon octet, charmed baryon sextet, light baryon decuplet) are derived and tabulated.

## Key numbers

* Isospin breaking is naively expected to be \(\delta_I \simeq (m_u-m_d)/\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\sim 1\%\).
* The branching fraction of \(\Lambda_b^0\to pJ/\psi K^-\) is \((3.2^{+0.6}_{-0.5})\times 10^{-4}\), and the branching fraction of \(\Lambda_b^0\to nJ/\psi\overline{K}^0\) is predicted to be the same value.
* The ratio \(\mathcal{B}r(\Lambda_b^0\to\Xi^-J/\psi K^+)/\mathcal{B}r(\Lambda_b^0\to\Lambda^0 J/\psi) = (1.17\pm 0.14\pm 0.08)\times 10^{-2}\), and the ratio \(\mathcal{B}r(\Lambda_b^0\to\Xi^0J/\psi K^0)/\mathcal{B}r(\Lambda_b^0\to\Lambda^0 J/\psi)\) is predicted to be \((1.17\pm 0.16)\times 10^{-2}\).
* The ratio \(\mathcal{B}r(\Xi_b^0\to\Xi^-J/\psi\pi^+)/\mathcal{B}r(\Xi_b^-\to\Xi^-J/\psi) = (11.9\pm 1.4\pm 0.6)\times 10^{-2}\).
* Predicted ratios for other \(\Xi_b\to\Xi J/\psi\pi\) modes relative to \(\mathcal{B}r(\Xi_b^-\to\Xi^-J/\psi)\): \(\mathcal{B}r(\Xi_b^0\to\Xi^0J/\psi\pi^0) = (6.0\pm 0.8)\times 10^{-2}\), \(\mathcal{B}r(\Xi_b^-\to\Xi^0J/\psi\pi^-) = (12.6\pm 1.6)\times 10^{-2}\), \(\mathcal{B}r(\Xi_b^-\to\Xi^-J/\psi\pi^0) = (6.3\pm 0.8)\times 10^{-2}\).
* The breaking induced by \(I_-^n H_{u\overline us} \neq 0\) is naively predicted to be \((V_{ub}V_{us})/(V_{cb}V_{cs})\sim \mathcal{O}(1\%)\) and by \(I_-^n H_{u\overline ud} \neq 0\) is \((V_{ub}V_{ud})/(V_{cb}V_{cd})\sim \mathcal{O}(10\%)\).

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to heavy hadron decays in Wang (2023), Luo (2023), and Wang (2024) to bottom baryon decays specifically. It derives many new isospin sum rules for the first time, while some rules (e.g., those labeled r5, r6, r1, r2, r3, r4) were previously found in Roy (2019), Roy (2020), and He (2018). The isospin sum rules can be used to test isospin symmetry, predict branching fractions and Lee-Yang parameters of unobserved decay modes, extract relative strong phases between different modes, and study isospin multiplets of exotic hadrons such as pentaquark states in bottom baryon decays.
