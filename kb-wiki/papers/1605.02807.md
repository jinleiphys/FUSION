# Scattering solutions of Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski and Euclidean spaces

> We shortly review different methods to obtain the scattering solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space. We emphasize the possibility to obtain the zero energy observables in terms of the Euclidean scattering amplitude.

## Key claim

The paper reviews methods for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation for scattering states in Minkowski space, and emphasizes that the scattering amplitude at zero energy obeys a purely Euclidean equation, decoupled from the Minkowski amplitude, a condition that holds only in the zero-energy limit.

## Method

The authors review two approaches to obtain scattering solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The first uses a Light-Front projection combined with the Nakanishi integral representation of the BS amplitude to regularize singularities and obtain an equation for the Nakanishi weight function. The second directly solves the singular four-dimensional integral equation in Minkowski space by analytically transforming all singular terms into smooth integrands, handling pole contributions from propagators and kernels. For the zero-energy case, the authors derive a purely Euclidean integral equation for the S-wave amplitude by showing that the additional term coupling Euclidean and Minkowski amplitudes vanishes in the limit \(k_s \to 0\).

## Key numbers

* Scattering length \(a_0 = 12.3\) (in units where \(m=1\)) for a scalar system with parameters \(m=1\), \(\mu=0.15\), and \(\alpha=2.5\), extracted from the Euclidean amplitude at the origin via \(a_0 = -F_0(0,0)\).
* The scattering length formula: \(a_0 = -\frac{1}{m} F_0(k_4=0, k=0)\).
* For the same parameters (\(m=1\), \(\mu=0.15\), \(\alpha=2.5\)), the two-body system has two bound states, which produce structure in the amplitude.

## Context

This work extends prior methods for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation for bound states in Minkowski space (Kusaka, 1990s; Carbonell and Karmanov, 2010) to scattering states, building on earlier direct solutions (Carbonell and Karmanov, 2013; Frederico, Salme, and Viviani, 2014-2015). The key result, that zero-energy scattering can be obtained from a purely Euclidean equation, provides an alternative to the Luscher method (Luscher, 1986) for extracting scattering lengths from lattice QCD calculations, which currently rely on finite-volume energy shifts. The approach is contrasted with the Maiani-Testa theorem (Maiani and Testa, 1990), which forbids extracting non-zero-energy scattering amplitudes from Euclidean correlators in infinite volume.
