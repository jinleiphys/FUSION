# Studies of beauty hadron and non-prompt charm hadron production in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}$=13 TeV within a transport model approach

> In high-energy proton proton ($pp$) collisions at the LHC, non-prompt charm hadrons, originating from beauty hadron decays, provide a valuable probe for beauty quark dynamics, particularly at low transverse momentum where direct beauty measurements are challenging. We employ A Multi-Phase Transport Model (AMPT) of string melting version coupled with PYTHIA8 initial conditions to study the beauty hadron and non-prompt charm hadron productions in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV. In this work, the beauty quark mass during the generation stage has been increased to reproduce the measured $b\bar{b}$ cross section, and a beauty flavor specific coalescence parameter $r_{BM}^b$ is introduced to match LHCb measurements of beauty baryon to meson ratios. With these refinements, AMPT achieves a reasonable agreement with experimental data on beauty hadron yields and non-prompt charm hadron production from ALICE and LHCb. We present the transverse momentum and multiplicity dependence of non-prompt to prompt charm hadron ratios, providing new insights into the interplay between beauty quark production and hadronization process. We emphasize that the multiplicity dependence of the non-prompt to prompt charm hadron productions can be useful to constrain the flavor dependences of the coalescence dynamics. This work establishes a unified framework for future studies of heavy quark transport and collective flow behavior in small collision systems.

## Key claim

The AMPT transport model, with an increased beauty quark mass (to 6.6 GeV/c²) and a beauty-specific coalescence parameter (r_BM^b = 1.2), achieves reasonable agreement with LHCb and ALICE data on beauty hadron yields and non-prompt charm hadron production in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV, but the abstract's claim of "reasonable agreement" glosses over a noticeable excess at low pT in forward-rapidity B+ spectra and a weaker saturation trend in the multiplicity-dependent Λ_b^0/B^0 ratio compared to data.

## Method

The authors employed the string melting version of the A Multi-Phase Transport Model (AMPT) with PYTHIA8 initial conditions, Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) for partonic interactions, and a spatial coalescence hadronization scheme. They increased the effective beauty quark mass from 4.8 to 6.6 GeV/c² during the PYTHIA8 generation stage to reproduce the measured b b̅ cross section, and introduced a beauty flavor specific coalescence parameter r_BM^b = 1.2 (compared to r_BM^c = 1.4 for charm) to match LHCb measurements of the Λ_b^0/B^0 ratio.

## Key numbers

- Beauty quark mass increased from default 4.8 GeV/c² to 6.6 GeV/c² during PYTHIA8 generation to reproduce the b b̅ cross section at √s = 13 TeV
- Beauty flavor specific coalescence parameter set to r_BM^b = 1.2, slightly smaller than the charm value r_BM^c = 1.4 used in prior work
- Parton rescattering cross section σ = 0.15 mb employed, giving satisfactory description of elliptic flow in pp collisions
- ALICE B+ data derived by scaling b b̅ cross section with fragmentation fraction 0.399 (from DELPHI 2011)
- Non-prompt D+/D0 ratios from both AMPT and ALICE close to 0.5 across the measured pT range, consistent with isospin symmetry

## Context

This work extends the AMPT framework previously applied to prompt charm hadrons (Zhang 2025) to beauty hadron and non-prompt charm hadron production in small collision systems. It addresses the challenge that default PYTHIA8 with Monash tune significantly overestimates b b̅ production at LHC energies, and that earlier AMPT versions performed poorly for beauty hadrons. The study enables future investigations of heavy quark transport and collective flow in small systems, and the multiplicity dependence of non-prompt to prompt ratios can help constrain flavor dependences of coalescence dynamics, complementing statistical hadronization models (He 2019) and hydrodynamics-based simulations such as EPOS4HQ (Zhao 2023).
