# Ab initio informed 20Ne(p, p$־±$)16O reaction elucidates the emergence of alpha clustering from chiral potentials

> We report on the first \textit{ab initio} informed $־±$ knock-out reaction in the intermediate-mass region, with the aim to probe the underlying chiral potential and its impact on the emergence of alpha clustering in this mass region. The theoretical predictions of the $־±+^{16}$O clustering in the $^{20}$Ne ground state, based on the \textit{ab initio} symmetry-adapted no-core shell model, yield a triple differential cross section for $^{20}$Ne(p, p$־±$)$^{16}$O that is in a remarkable agreement with the data. This allows us to examine predictions of surface and in-medium $־±$-cluster features from a chiral potential and to compare these to the successful antisymmetrized molecular dynamics approach.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first *ab initio* informed alpha knock-out reaction in the intermediate-mass region, showing that the theoretical predictions of alpha+O-16 clustering in the Ne-20 ground state, based on the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model with a chiral potential, yield a triple differential cross section for Ne-20(p, p alpha)O-16 that agrees remarkably with experimental data, with the caveat that the agreement depends on using the NNLO_opt chiral potential without three-nucleon forces and on matching interior wavefunctions to Coulomb functions at large distances.

## Method

The authors calculated the Ne-20 ground-state wavefunction using the *ab initio* symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) with the NNLO_opt chiral nucleon-nucleon potential in 13 harmonic oscillator shells. They projected this wavefunction onto the alpha+O-16 cluster channel to obtain spectroscopic amplitudes (reduced width amplitudes), which were matched to Coulomb Whittaker functions at large distances using an R-matrix framework to determine asymptotic normalization coefficients. These spectroscopic amplitudes were then used as input to distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations of the Ne-20(p, p alpha)O-16 reaction, employing the EDAD1 global optical potential for proton scattering and the Michel optical potential for alpha-O-16 scattering.

## Key numbers

* The asymptotic normalization coefficient for the alpha+O-16 cluster in the Ne-20 ground state is C_0 = 3760 +/- 470 fm^{-1/2}, determined from the infinite-space limit using model spaces with hw=11 MeV (upper limit) and hw=13 MeV (lower limit).
* The SA-NCSM calculation yields a Ne-20 ground-state charge radius of 2.94(9) fm, in agreement with the experimental value of 3.0055(21) fm.
* The maximum of the knockout cross section occurs at a proton kinetic energy T_p^L ~ 67 MeV, corresponding to the recoil-less condition that probes surface alpha clustering.
* The surface peak location for the spectroscopic amplitude is 4.32 fm for the SA-NCSM (upper limit) and 4.4 fm for the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) approach.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of alpha clustering using the SA-NCSM (Dreyfuss et al., 2020) and AMD (Kanada-Enyo, 1995, 1998) by providing the first *ab initio* informed (p, p alpha) reaction calculation in the intermediate-mass region, directly probing the emergent clustering features from chiral effective field theory potentials. The results validate the NNLO_opt chiral potential for predicting collective and clustering features beyond light nuclei, and demonstrate that the SA-NCSM approach can describe localized clustering from first principles, enabling future studies of alpha clustering in other intermediate-mass nuclei and comparisons with experimental knockout data.
