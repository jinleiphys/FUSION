# The proton-antiproton mass threshold structure in psi(3686) radiative decay revisited

> The near-threshold behavior of the ppbar invariant mass spectrum from the psi(3686) -> gamma ppbar decay reported recently by the BESIII Collaboration is analyzed. The enhancement in the ppbar invariant mass spectrum near threshold is nicely reproduced by the ppbar final-state interaction based on the isospin averaged 1S0 partial-wave amplitude as predicted by the Julich nucleon--antinucleon model. Contributions from the f_2(1910) or f_2(1950) mesons, as promoted in earlier works, are not needed.

## Key claim

The near-threshold enhancement in the psi(3686) -> gamma p pbar invariant mass spectrum can be reproduced solely by the p pbar final-state interaction using the isospin-averaged 1S0 partial-wave amplitude from the Juelich nucleon-antinucleon model, without requiring contributions from the f2(1910) or f2(1950) tensor mesons that earlier works had invoked.

## Method

The authors applied the Watson-Migdal factorization approach, where the total reaction amplitude for the decay is approximated as a constant production amplitude multiplied by the p pbar scattering amplitude from the Juelich NbarN model A(OBE). They computed the differential decay rate for psi(3686) -> gamma p pbar using the isospin-averaged 1S0 partial-wave amplitude and compared the resulting invariant mass spectrum to BESIII and CLEO data, evaluating the fit quality via chi-squared.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-averaged amplitude (T_I=1 + T_I=0)/2 yields a chi-squared of 8.7 for 15 data points from BESIII, compared to 22.2 for the pure I=1 amplitude and 11.9 for the pure I=0 amplitude.
- The pure phase-space curve gives a chi-squared of 60 for the same 15 BESIII data points, indicating the measured spectrum does not follow phase-space behavior near threshold.
- The FSI-based results reproduce the BESIII data from the p pbar threshold up to excess energies of about 150 MeV.
- For the CLEO data, all FSI results yield chi-squared/data approximately 20/30, while pure phase space gives 30/30.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis of the J/psi -> gamma p pbar decay (Sibirtsev 2005), where the I=1 component alone explained the stronger near-threshold enhancement. It contradicts the interpretation by the BESIII Collaboration (2012) and the CLEO Collaboration (2010), who invoked the f2(1910) or f2(1950) tensor mesons to describe the psi(3686) decay data. The results enable future dedicated microscopic calculations to clarify the different isospin admixtures in J/psi versus psi(3686) decays into gamma p pbar.
