# Faddeev calculations of the $\bar{K}NN$ system with chirally-motivated $\bar{K}N$ interaction. I. Low-energy $K^- d$ scattering and antikaonic deuterium

> A chirally-motivated coupled-channel $\bar{K}N$ potential, reproducing all low-energy experimental data on $K^- p$ scattering and kaonic hydrogen and suitable for using in accurate few-body calculations, was constructed. The potential was used for calculations of low-energy amplitudes of the elastic $K^- d$ scattering using Faddeev-type AGS equations with coupled $\bar{K}NN$ and $πΣN$ channels. A complex $K^- - d$ potential reproducing the three-body $K^- d$ amplitudes was constructed and used for calculation of $1s$ level shift and width of kaonic deuterium. The predicted shift $ΔE_{1s}^{K^- d} \sim -830$ eV and width $Γ_{1s}^{K^- d} \sim 1055$ eV are close to our previous results obtained with phenomenological $\bar{K}N$ potentials.

## Key claim

A chirally-motivated coupled-channel $\bar{K}N$ potential, which reproduces all low-energy $K^- p$ scattering data and kaonic hydrogen observables with the same accuracy as previously constructed phenomenological potentials, yields low-energy $K^- d$ elastic amplitudes and kaonic deuterium characteristics that are rather close to those obtained with the phenomenological models, meaning that comparison with future kaonic deuterium experiments could hardly discriminate between the models.

## Method

The authors constructed a three-channel, isospin-dependent, separable chirally-motivated $\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma - \pi \Lambda$ potential with Yamaguchi form-factors, fitted to elastic and inelastic $K^- p$ cross-sections, threshold branching ratios, and kaonic hydrogen data. They solved Faddeev-type Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations in momentum representation for the $\bar{K}NN$ system with coupled $\pi \Sigma N$ channels, using the new potential together with a two-term TSA-B $NN$ potential and an exact optical $\Sigma N (-\Lambda N)$ potential. A complex separable $K^- - d$ potential was then fitted to the three-body $K^- d$ amplitudes and used in a two-body Lippmann-Schwinger equation with Coulomb interaction to compute the $1s$ level shift and width of kaonic deuterium.

## Key numbers

- $K^- d$ scattering length from the chirally-motivated potential: $a_{K^- d} = -1.59 + i \, 1.32$ fm.
- Effective range of $K^- d$ scattering from the chirally-motivated potential: $r^{eff}_{K^- d} = 0.50 - i \, 1.17$ fm.
- Predicted $1s$ level shift of kaonic deuterium: $\Delta E_{1s}^{K^- d} \sim -828$ eV (rounded to $-830$ eV in the abstract).
- Predicted $1s$ level width of kaonic deuterium: $\Gamma_{1s}^{K^- d} \sim 1055$ eV.
- Kaonic hydrogen $1s$ level shift from the chirally-motivated potential: $\Delta E_{1s}^{K^- p} = -313$ eV (experiment: $-283 \pm 36 \pm 6$ eV from SIDDHARTA).
- Kaonic hydrogen $1s$ level width from the chirally-motivated potential: $\Gamma_{1s}^{K^- p} = 561$ eV (experiment: $541 \pm 89 \pm 22$ eV from SIDDHARTA).
- $K^- p$ scattering length from the chirally-motivated potential: $a_{K^- p} = -0.77 + i \, 0.84$ fm.
- Isospin-zero $\bar{K}N$ scattering length: $a_{\bar{K}N,0} = -1.65 + i \, 1.26$ fm.
- Isospin-one $\bar{K}N$ scattering length: $a_{\bar{K}N,1} = 0.52 + i \, 0.48$ fm.
- Poles of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance from the chirally-motivated potential: $z_1 = 1417 - i \, 33$ MeV, $z_2 = 1406 - i \, 89$ MeV.
- Corrected Deser formula applied to the chirally-motivated $a_{K^- d}$ gives $\Delta E^{Chiral}_{K^- d, cD} = -878$ eV and $\Gamma^{Chiral}_{K^- d, cD} = 724$ eV, underestimating the width by about $30\%$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Faddeev calculations of the $\bar{K}NN$ system (Shevchenko and Revai, 2012) by replacing the phenomenological $\bar{K}N$ potentials (one-pole and two-pole versions) with a new chirally-motivated potential that reproduces the same low-energy $K^- p$ data and kaonic hydrogen results from the SIDDHARTA collaboration. The results are compared to other Faddeev calculations by Deloff (2000), the TDD and TGE groups, and the more recent MFST calculation (2013). The predicted kaonic deuterium shift and width are intended for direct comparison with the upcoming SIDDHARTA-2 experiment. The authors also demonstrate that the corrected Deser formula, which is often used to extract scattering lengths from atomic data, introduces a large error (about $30\%$ in the width) for the $K^- d$ system, consistent with their earlier findings for phenomenological potentials.
