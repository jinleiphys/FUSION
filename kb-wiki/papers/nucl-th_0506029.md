# Relativistic Equation of state with short range correlations

> Short range correlations are introduced using unitary correlation method in a relativistic approach to the equation of state of the infinite nuclear matter in the framework of the Hartree-Fock approximation. The effect of the correlations in the ground state properties of the nuclear matter is studied.

# Relativistic Equation of state with short range correlations

## Key claim

Short range correlations introduced via the unitary operator method in a relativistic Hartree-Fock framework produce a softer equation of state (EOS) than either Hartree or Hartree-Fock calculations alone, with the explicit inclusion of correlations yielding a larger effective mass than both approaches. The authors note that while correlation effects may be partially absorbed into the coupling constants of relativistic mean field models, the explicit treatment has distinct consequences such as softening the EOS.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic Hamiltonian with scalar (sigma) and vector (omega) meson exchange interactions and expand the nucleon field in plane waves with a variational angle chi(k) that defines the effective mass and effective momentum. Short range correlations are introduced via a unitary operator acting on the Slater determinant, approximated by a Jastrow-type correlation function f(r) = 1 - (alpha + beta r) exp(-gamma r), with the normalization constraint that the integral of (f^2 - 1) vanishes. The correlated ground state energy density is computed in the Hartree-Fock approximation, with the interaction matrix elements replaced by correlated matrix elements including kinetic energy contributions, and the parameters (couplings g_sigma, g_omega, and correlation parameters alpha, beta, gamma) are fitted to reproduce saturation properties of nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density fixed at k_F0 = 1.3 fm^{-1} with binding energy E_B = -15.75 MeV
- Nucleon mass M = 939 MeV, sigma meson mass m_sigma = 550 MeV, omega meson mass m_omega = 783 MeV
- Hartree-Fock with correlations (HF+corr): g_sigma = 4.4559, g_omega = 2.6098, alpha = 13.855, beta = -2252.448 MeV, gamma = 1000 MeV (at k_F = k_F0)
- Compressibility K = 429 MeV for HF+corr, compared to 540 MeV (Hartree) and 585 MeV (HF)
- Effective mass ratio M*/M = 0.625 for HF+corr, compared to 0.540 (Hartree) and 0.515 (HF)
- Kinetic energy contribution (T/rho_B - M) = 15.95 MeV for HF+corr
- Direct potential energy V_d/rho_B = -73.12 MeV, exchange potential energy V_e/rho_B = 20.46 MeV, correlation kinetic energy T^C/rho_B = 19.96 MeV for HF+corr

## Context

This work extends the unitary correlation method, previously applied in nonrelativistic calculations by Villars and by Jastrow, to the relativistic treatment of nuclear matter for the first time. The authors compare their results with the quark-meson-coupling model (QMC) and the nonlinear Walecka model NL3, finding that the correlated EOS is softer than both Hartree and Hartree-Fock calculations and, around twice saturation density, softer than NL3. The approach enables future studies of how explicitly including short range correlations modifies the density dependence of the effective mass and the equation of state beyond what can be absorbed into phenomenological coupling constants.
