# Nuclear suppression of azimuthal asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off polarized targets

> We extend the study of nuclear dependence of the transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions and azimuthal asymmetries to semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) off polarized nuclear targets. We show that azimuthal asymmetries are suppressed for SIDIS off a polarized nuclear target relative to that off a polarized nucleon due to multiple scattering inside the nucleus. Using the value of transport parameter inside large nuclei extracted from jet quenching analyses in SIDIS off nuclear targets, we also present a numerical estimate of the nuclear suppression of the azimuthal asymmetry that might be useful to guide the future experimental studies of SIDIS off polarized nuclear targets.

## Key claim

The paper shows that azimuthal asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) off polarized nuclear targets are suppressed relative to those off polarized nucleons, with the suppression arising from multiple parton scattering inside the nucleus; the effect includes an additional dilution factor \(2J_A/A\) beyond the unpolarized case, and the numerical estimates depend on the assumption that all Gaussian widths for nucleon TMD parton distributions are equal.

## Method

The authors extend the formalism of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions to polarized nuclear targets, assuming each nucleon in the nucleus carries equal polarization \(J_A/A\). Under a "maximal two-gluon" or random-walk approximation, the nuclear TMD distributions are obtained as a convolution of the nucleon TMD distributions with a Gaussian broadening of width \(\Delta_{2F}\), which is the total transverse momentum broadening squared from multiple scattering. Using a Gaussian ansatz for the nucleon TMD distributions, the nuclear dependence of various azimuthal asymmetries (leading-twist and twist-3) is derived analytically, and numerical estimates are made using the quark transport parameter \(\hat q_0\) extracted from jet quenching analyses in SIDIS off nuclear targets.

## Key numbers

- Quark transport parameter at the center of a large nucleus: \(\hat q_0 \approx 0.024 \pm 0.008\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, from analyses of jet suppression in SIDIS (Wang 2009, Chang 2014).
- Gaussian width for nucleon TMD quark transverse momentum distribution: \(\alpha = 0.25\) GeV\(^2\) (taken as representative from the range \(0.2 \sim 0.3\) GeV\(^2\)).
- Nuclear suppression factor for azimuthal asymmetry: \(f_s \approx (1 + 0.114 A^{1/3})^{-1}\), derived assuming \(\alpha = 0.25\) GeV\(^2\) and using the nuclear radius \(r_0 \approx 1.12\) fm.
- For equal Gaussian widths, the ratio of azimuthal asymmetries for polarized targets relative to nucleon targets is \(\frac{2J_A}{A} \frac{\alpha}{\alpha + \Delta_{2F}}\) for leading-twist asymmetries, and \(\frac{2J_A}{A}\) for certain twist-3 asymmetries after integration over \(k_\perp\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of nuclear dependence of azimuthal asymmetries in SIDIS off unpolarized targets (Song 2013) to polarized nuclear targets, motivated by the possibility of future experiments at Jefferson Laboratory (Harut Avakian, private communication). The formalism builds on earlier work on nuclear broadening of TMD distributions (Liang 2008) and on azimuthal asymmetries in SIDIS (Gao 2010). The numerical estimates use the quark transport parameter extracted from jet quenching analyses in SIDIS (Wang 2009, Chang 2014) and are compared qualitatively with HERMES data on transverse momentum broadening (Airapetian 2009, 2011). The results provide guidance for future experimental studies of SIDIS off polarized nuclear targets.
