# Baryonic contributions to the dilepton spectrum of nucleon-nucleon collisions

> We study the production of dileptons in relativistic nucleon-nucleon collisions. Additionally to the traditional dilepton production channels (vector meson decays, meson and Delta(1232) Dalitz decays) we included in our model as new dilepton sources the Dalitz decay of higher unflavored baryon resonances with spin<=5/2 and mass<=2.25 GeV/c^2. The contributions of these new channels are estimated using experimental information about the Ngamma decays of the resonances and have large uncertainties. The obtained dilepton spectra are compared to the experimental data by the DLS collaboration. Predictions for the HADES detector (SIS, GSI) are also discussed. In spite of the large uncertainties of the higher resonance Dalitz decay contributions we are able to draw the conclusion that these contributions are negligible compared to the other dilepton sources and do not influence the detectability of the phi and omega vector meson peaks.

## Key claim

The Dalitz decays of higher unflavored baryon resonances (spin ≤ 5/2, mass ≤ 2.25 GeV/c²) contribute negligibly to the dilepton spectrum in nucleon-nucleon collisions, despite large uncertainties in their estimated contributions, and do not obscure the detectability of the φ and ω vector meson peaks.

## Method

The authors constructed a Monte Carlo resonance model for proton-proton collisions, including all unflavored baryon resonances with mass ≤ 2.25 GeV/c² and at least three-star status from the Particle Data Group. They calculated the Dalitz-decay widths of spin-3/2 and spin-5/2 resonances using a generalized Rarita-Schwinger formalism with three independent form factors, setting g₂ = g₃ = 0 and fitting the remaining coupling g₁ to experimental photonic decay widths. The model was fitted to pion-induced reaction data and to one-pion, two-pion, η, ω, ρ, and kaon production cross sections in pp and pn collisions, then used to compute dilepton invariant mass spectra for comparison with DLS data and predictions for HADES.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: 1.04, 1.27, 1.61, 1.85, 2.09, and 4.88 GeV (DLS comparison); 3.0 and 3.5 GeV (HADES predictions)
- Resonance mass upper limit: 2.25 GeV/c²; maximum spin: 5/2
- At beam energies E_b ≥ 1.61 GeV, the η Dalitz-decay gives the dominant contribution around 0.2 GeV dilepton mass
- The model underestimates η production cross section at E_b = 2.02 GeV (p_lab = 2.81 GeV) compared to experimental data
- At 4.88 GeV beam energy, model results are an order of magnitude below experimental data due to missing multi-pion final states
- For HADES predictions at E_b = 3 and 3.5 GeV (above φ production threshold of 2.59 GeV), the N(1535) and N(1520) channels exceed the Δ(1232) contribution in the φ mass region but remain well below the φ meson contribution

## Context

This work extends earlier studies that considered only Δ(1232) and N(1440) Dalitz decays (the latter giving negligible contribution) by including higher baryon resonances up to spin 5/2. The model is compared to DLS collaboration data (1990s) and provides predictions for the HADES detector at SIS/GSI. The authors note that other models (Frankfurt group, Giessen group) using string fragmentation describe the 4.88 GeV data better, and that a strong pp bremsstrahlung contribution has been suggested as a possible explanation for discrepancies around M = 0.6 GeV at E_b = 2.09 GeV. The model can serve as input to transport models of heavy ion collisions for studying in-medium modifications of vector mesons and baryon resonances.
