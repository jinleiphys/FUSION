# Weak decays of $\bar B_s$ mesons

> In the present work we study the semileptonic decays of $\bar B_s$ mesons in the context of nonrelativistic constituent quark models. We estimate the uncertainties of our calculation using different interquark potentials to obtain the meson wave functions. We check the results from our model against the predictions of Heavy Quark Symmetry, in the limit of infinite heavy quark mass. We also study the nonleptonic decays of $\bar B_s$ mesons within the factorization approximation.

## Key claim
The paper calculates semileptonic and nonleptonic decay rates of $\bar B_s$ mesons using nonrelativistic constituent quark models, finding that the results are consistent with Heavy Quark Symmetry predictions in the infinite heavy quark mass limit, but only within the uncertainties estimated from using different interquark potentials.

## Method
The author computed the weak decay form factors for $\bar B_s$ mesons by solving the Schrodinger equation with several nonrelativistic interquark potentials to obtain the meson wave functions. Semileptonic decay widths were then evaluated from these form factors, and nonleptonic decays were treated within the factorization approximation.

## Key numbers
- The differential decay rate $d\Gamma/dq^2$ for $\bar B_s \to D_s l \nu_l$ (with $l=e$) is plotted on a scale up to $0.45 \times |V_{cb}|^2\ 10^{-13} \rm GeV^{-1}$ at $q^2=0$.
- The differential decay rate $d\Gamma/dq^2$ for $\bar B_s \to D_{s0}^*(2371) l \nu_l$ (with $l=e$) is plotted on a scale up to $0.1 \times |V_{cb}|^2\ 10^{-13} \rm GeV^{-1}$ at $q^2=0$.
- The differential decay rate for the tau lepton channel ($l=\tau$) is shown to be significantly smaller than the electron channel for both $\bar B_s \to D_s$ and $\bar B_s \to D_{s0}^*(2371)$ decays.

## Context
This work extends the application of nonrelativistic constituent quark models, previously used for similar calculations in other heavy-light systems, to the specific case of $\bar B_s$ mesons. The results provide a theoretical benchmark for future experimental measurements of $B_s$ decays at facilities like LHCb, and the comparison with Heavy Quark Symmetry (e.g., the work of Isgur and Wise) validates the model in the heavy quark limit. The study enables refined predictions for $|V_{cb}|$ extraction from $B_s$ semileptonic decays.
