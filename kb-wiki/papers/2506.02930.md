# High-order anisotropic flow and nonlinear hydrodynamic responses

> In this work, we study the charged hadron anisotropic flow coefficients $v_n$ (up to $n = 12$) using high-statistics event-by-event simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 5.02$ and $5.36$~TeV, employing the IP-Glasma + MUSIC + UrQMD hybrid approach. The power spectra of anisotropic flow coefficients ($v_n\{2\}$ vs. $n$) are compared with the ALICE measurements from central to 50% centrality. To understand the various sources contributing to the high-order $v_n$ coefficients, we analyze the nonlinear mode coefficients for high-order anisotropic flow coefficients using different approximations and make comparisons with available measurements.

## Key claim

The IP-Glasma + MUSIC + UrQMD hybrid framework can describe ALICE measurements of charged hadron anisotropic flow coefficients $v_n\{2\}$ from $n=2$ up to $n=7$ across central to 50% centrality using only elliptic flow calibration, but the model underestimates $v_8\{2\}$ and $v_9\{2\}$ in semi-peripheral collisions, and the ALICE data for $v_8\{2\}$ and $v_9\{2\}$ saturate around $10^{-3}$ while the model continues to decay exponentially.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 5.02$ and $5.36$ TeV using the IP-Glasma initial-state model, MUSIC relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, and UrQMD hadronic afterburner, with 300k minimum-bias hydrodynamic events at 5.02 TeV. Anisotropic flow vectors were constructed from charged hadrons with $p_T \in [0.2, 3]$ GeV using two sub-events with a rapidity gap $|\Delta \eta| \ge 0.8$, and nonlinear mode decomposition was performed for $V_4$ through $V_9$ by solving matrix equations for the nonlinear mode coefficients, including off-diagonal correlation terms.

## Key numbers

- Anisotropic flow coefficients $v_n\{2\}$ computed up to $n = 12$ in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 5.02$ and $5.36$ TeV
- For $n \ge 7$, $v_n\{2\}$ decays exponentially with $n$ in 0-5% central collisions, consistent with expectations from Hatta (2015) and Hatta (2016)
- The nonlinear mode coefficient $\chi_{4,22}$ is underestimated by 15% in 0-10% central Pb+Pb collisions compared to ALICE data
- The ratio $\sqrt{\langle |V_{nL}|^2 \rangle}/v_n\{2\}$ reaches a maximum mixture at $n = 6$, where the linear flow mode is about half of $v_n\{2\}$
- For $v_{10}\{2\}$ to $v_{12}\{2\}$ at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 5.36$ TeV, values saturate around $10^{-4}$
- Relative numerical uncertainty in $v_n\{2\}$ is below 10% based on grid spacing and rotation convergence tests with $dx = 0.067$ fm

## Context

This work extends previous IP-Glasma + MUSIC results from McDonald (2016) and the ALICE Collaboration (2020) by including sub-nucleonic structures from HERA diffractive $J/\Psi$ measurements and improved QGP viscosities as calibrated by Mantysaari (2024). The study enables future experimental comparisons for nonlinear mode coefficients of $V_8$ and $V_9$, and tests the proposal from Staig (2010, 2011), Lacey (2013), and Shuryak (2017) that $v_n/|\mathcal{E}_n| \propto \exp[-bn^2]$, as well as the exponential decay prediction from Hatta (2015, 2016).
