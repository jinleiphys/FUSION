# Charge dependent directed flow splitting from baryon inhomogeneity and electromagnetic field

> This work aims to understand the recent experimental data from the STAR collaboration on the system size dependence of directed flow splitting between oppositely charged hadrons [arXiv:2412.18326]. Previously, we have studied the role of baryon inhomogeneity on charge dependent directed flow. We now incorporate the effects of the electromagnetic (EM) field albeit perturbatively, as implemented in Ref. [arXiv:1806.05288]. This enables us to compare the relative contributions between baryon inhomogeneity and EM field on charge dependent directed flow. Our model calculation describes the experimental data on the centrality and system size dependence of the mid-rapidity directed flow slope splitting, $Δdv_1/dy$, between protons and anti-protons. Our results indicate that in central collisions, where the EM field strength is negligible, the inclusion of EM field effects does not influence the splitting between protons and anti-protons. This suggests that the observed system size dependence of $Δdv_1/dy (p-\bar{p})$ in central collisions arises solely from enhanced baryon stopping in larger collision systems. However, in semi-central and peripheral collisions, both baryon diffusion and EM field effects contribute to the splitting. Furthermore, the centrality dependence of $Δdv_1/dy (p-\bar{p})$ is highly sensitive to the electrical conductivity of the medium, making it a potential probe for extracting this transport coefficient in the QCD medium through model-to-data comparisons. However, achieving this requires a precise determination of the background baseline originating from baryon diffusion. Additionally, further investigation is needed to understand $Δdv_1/dy$ for oppositely charged kaons and pions, particularly by incorporating the diffusion of other conserved charges.

## Key claim
The observed system size dependence of the directed flow slope splitting between protons and anti-protons in central collisions arises solely from enhanced baryon stopping in larger collision systems, while in semi-central and peripheral collisions both baryon diffusion and electromagnetic field effects contribute. The centrality dependence of this splitting is highly sensitive to the electrical conductivity of the medium, but extracting this transport coefficient requires precise determination of the background baseline from baryon diffusion.

## Method
The authors employ a hydrodynamic model using the MUSIC code with net baryon density evolution and baryon diffusion, incorporating a tilted initial condition for energy density and a two-component baryon deposition model. The electromagnetic field effects from spectator nucleons are included perturbatively by solving Maxwell's equations in a conducting medium and computing a charge-dependent drift velocity from the force balance equation, which is then used in the Cooper-Frye particlization to obtain final hadron distributions. Model parameters are tuned to reproduce bulk observables including charged particle yields, net-proton yields, and directed flow of pions, protons, and anti-protons at 10-40% centrality.

## Key numbers
- At 40-50% centrality in Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, incorporating baryon diffusion with C_B=1 changes the magnitude of Delta dv1/dy(p-anti-p) by 46%; further including EM field effects with sigma=0.023 fm^-1 results in an additional 43% change in magnitude.
- The initial magnetic field eB_y is calculated at (x,y,z)=(0,0,0) at proper time tau=0.6 fm assuming electrical conductivity sigma=0.023 fm^-1, presented in units of m_pi^2.
- For C_B=1, the tilt parameters are set to eta_m=2.0 and omega=0.2; for C_B=0, eta_m=2.0 and omega=0.31.
- The freeze-out energy density is epsilon_f=0.26 GeV/fm^3, and the specific shear viscosity is c_eta=0.08.
- The drag coefficient in the force balance equation is taken as mu m = (pi sqrt(6 pi)/2) T^2.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Parida et al. (2022, 2023) on baryon inhomogeneity effects on charge-dependent directed flow and incorporates electromagnetic field effects following the approach of Gursoy et al. (2018). The authors aim to understand recent STAR collaboration data (2024) on system size dependence of directed flow splitting across U+U, Au+Au, and Zr+Zr collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. The results suggest that comparing model calculations with precise measurements of p_T-differential Delta v1(p-anti-p) in central collisions could constrain the baryon diffusion coefficient, while the sensitivity to electrical conductivity makes this observable a potential probe for extracting this transport coefficient of the QCD medium.
