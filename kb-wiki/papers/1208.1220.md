# Freeze-out Conditions in Heavy Ion Collisions from QCD Thermodynamics

> We present a determination of chemical freeze-out conditions in heavy ion collisions based on ratios of cumulants of net electric charge fluctuations. These ratios can reliably be calculated in lattice QCD for a wide range of chemical potential values by using a next-to-leading order Taylor series expansion around the limit of vanishing baryon, electric charge and strangeness chemical potentials. From a computation of up to fourth order cumulants and charge correlations we first determine the strangeness and electric charge chemical potentials that characterize freeze-out conditions in a heavy ion collision and confirm that in the temperature range 150 MeV < T < 170 MeV the hadron resonance gas model provides good approximations for these parameters that agree with QCD calculations on the (5-15)% level. We then show that a comparison of lattice QCD results for ratios of up to third order cumulants of electric charge fluctuations with experimental results allows to extract the freeze-out baryon chemical potential and the freeze-out temperature.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that ratios of cumulants of net electric charge fluctuations, calculated in lattice QCD using a next-to-leading order Taylor expansion, can determine the chemical freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential in heavy ion collisions, but the extracted freeze-out temperature may be about 5% lower than hadron resonance gas model predictions if the QCD calculation of the ratio \(R_{31}^Q\) is used instead of the HRG value.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action for (2+1)-flavor QCD with a light to strange quark mass ratio of 1/20, corresponding to a pion mass of about 160 MeV. They computed up to fourth order cumulants and charge correlations on lattices with temporal extent \(N_\tau = 6, 8, 12\) and extrapolated to the continuum limit, then used a next-to-leading order Taylor expansion in the baryon, electric charge, and strangeness chemical potentials to determine the freeze-out parameters by comparing ratios of cumulants of net electric charge fluctuations with experimental data.

## Key numbers

* The leading order expansion coefficients for the strangeness and electric charge chemical potentials were computed in the temperature range \(150\ {\rm MeV} \le T \le 250\ {\rm MeV}\) on lattices with \(N_\tau = 6, 8, 12\).
* The hadron resonance gas model agrees with QCD calculations for the strangeness and electric charge chemical potentials at the (5-15)% level in the temperature range \(150\ {\rm MeV} < T < 170\ {\rm MeV}\).
* The next-to-leading order corrections to the leading order results for the chemical potentials are below 10% in the temperature interval \(T \simeq (160 \pm 10)\ {\rm MeV}\).
* For \(T = 160\ {\rm MeV}\), the ratio \(R_{12}^Q = 0.102(5) \hat{\mu}_B + 0.002(1) \hat{\mu}_B^3\).
* At \(T = 165\ {\rm MeV}\), the QCD calculation gives \(R_{31}^Q \simeq 1.2\) while the HRG model gives \(R_{31}^Q \simeq 2.0\).
* For a freeze-out temperature near \(T = 160\ {\rm MeV}\), \(\mu_B^f = (20-30)\ {\rm MeV}\) corresponds to \(R_{12}^Q\) in the range \(0.012-0.020\), \(\mu_B^f = (50-70)\ {\rm MeV}\) for \(0.032 \le R_{12}^Q \le 0.045\), and \(\mu_B^f = (80-120)\ {\rm MeV}\) for \(0.05 \le R_{12}^Q \le 0.08\).

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD calculations of charge fluctuations by the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov 2012) to determine freeze-out conditions directly from QCD rather than relying solely on hadron resonance gas model fits as done by Cleymans (2006). The results enable experimental determination of freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential from measurements of net electric charge cumulant ratios at the RHIC Beam Energy Scan, and provide a baseline for searching for critical behavior through higher order cumulant calculations.
