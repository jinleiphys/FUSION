# Dynamical fragmentation of flux tubes in the Friedberg-Lee model

> We present two novel dynamical features of flux tubes in the Friedberg-Lee model. First the fusion of two (anti-)parallel flux tubes, where we extract a string-string interaction potential which has a qualitative similarity to the nucleon-nucleon potential in the Friedberg-Lee model obtained by Koepf et al. Furthermore we show the dynamical breakup of flux tubes via $q\bar{q}-$particle production and the disintegration into mesons. We find, as a shortcoming of the present realization of the model, that the full dynamical transport approach presented in a previous publication fails to provide the disintegration mechanism in the semiclassical limit. Therefore, in addition, we present here a molecular dynamical approach for the motion of the quarks and show, as a first application, the space-time development of the quarks and their mean-fields for Lund-type string fragmentation processes.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate two dynamical features of flux tubes in the Friedberg-Lee model, fusion and breakup, but note that the full dynamical transport approach fails to provide the disintegration mechanism in the semiclassical limit, requiring instead a molecular dynamical approach to describe Lund-type string fragmentation processes.

## Method

The authors solve the Friedberg-Lee model's field equations (for the scalar σ-field and the color-electric field) coupled to a semiclassical Vlasov transport equation for quarks and antiquarks, using a test-particle method. For the fusion of flux tubes, they adiabatically separate quark-antiquark pairs at constant velocity (0.1c) and extract a string-string interaction potential from the field energies. For the breakup, they insert qq̄ pairs into a dynamically evolving flux tube and simulate the space-time evolution, then switch to a molecular dynamical approach where each physical quark is represented by a single test particle with a meson groundstate distribution to avoid self-interaction artifacts.

## Key numbers

- String tension τ = 1 GeV/fm, obtained with strong coupling constant α_s = 1.92
- Flux tube length of 8 fm and width of approximately 1 fm
- Fusion velocity v_f = 0.1c used for adiabatic fusion of flux tubes
- Quark current mass m_0 ≈ 10 MeV
- Fermi energy μ = 1.768 fm⁻¹ for baryon soliton and μ = 1.9582 fm⁻¹ for meson soliton
- Meson soliton energy 798 MeV (compared to experimental mean of 465 MeV)
- Baryon soliton energy 1099 MeV (compared to experimental mean of 1087 MeV)
- Meson RMS radius 0.653 fm (experimental 0.66 fm)
- Baryon RMS radius 0.693 fm (experimental 0.83 fm)
- Glueball mass 1.045 GeV
- Test particle number N_T = 50000 for each quark in the transport dynamical model
- String breakup occurs at times such as t = 2.0 fm/c (pair insertion), t = 2.6 fm/c (screening), t = 3.4 fm/c and t = 7.0 fm/c (free propagation)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Friedberg-Lee model by Koepf et al. (1994) on nucleon-nucleon potentials and by Vetter et al. (1995) on dynamical confinement, providing a dynamical treatment of flux tube fusion and fragmentation. The string-string interaction potential shows qualitative similarity to the nucleon-nucleon potential obtained by Koepf et al. (1994) in the same model. The approach enables a fully dynamical space-time description of Lund-type string fragmentation processes, which are normally treated purely phenomenologically in models like Fritjof (Andersson et al., 1987), Venus (Werner, 1989), RQMD (Sorge et al., 1989), and HIJING (Wang and Gyulassy, 1991).
