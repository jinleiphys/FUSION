# Matter-wave interference originates mass-angle correlation in fusion-fission

> Mass-angle correlation of fission fragments has been understood as manifestation of quasifission. We show that this is not so: the effect can originate from correlation between fusion-fission amplitudes with different total spins signifying matter-wave interference in compound nucleus processes. This resolves the well-known puzzle with the mass-angle correlation in the complete fusion sub-barrier reaction $^{16}$O+$^{238}$U. Our finding is important for more reliable predictions of production cross sections for superheavy elements. Matter-wave interference also produces quantum-classical transition to the time-orientation localization of the coherently rotating dinucleus in quasifission.

## Key claim

The authors claim that mass-angle correlation (MAC) of fission fragments, previously understood as a signature of quasifission, can instead originate from spin off-diagonal correlations in compound nucleus fusion-fission amplitudes, signifying matter-wave interference. This claim is hedged by the condition that the effect depends on a finite ratio of the correlation length in energy space to the compound nucleus total decay width, and that the interpretation applies specifically to the sub-barrier reaction ¹⁶O+²³⁸U where quasifission has been shown to be insignificant.

## Method

The authors extend the transition-state statistical model (TSSM) of fission-fragment angular distributions by introducing compound nucleus spin off-diagonal correlations between resonance eigenstates of different total angular momentum J. They represent the fission-fragment angular distribution as a coherent sum over J₁ and J₂ contributions weighted by transmission coefficients, potential phase shifts in the entrance channel, and a Lorentzian correlation factor [1+(β/Γ)|J₁-J₂|]⁻¹, where β is the correlation length in energy space and Γ is the compound nucleus total decay width. The model is applied to the sub-barrier reaction ¹⁶O+²³⁸U at center-of-mass energies 72.8 MeV and 75.6 MeV, using a fission barrier of 4.15 MeV instead of the standard 1.5 MeV to reproduce the experimental evaporation residue to fission cross-section ratio.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶O+²³⁸U at E_cm=72 MeV, the experimental ratio σ_ER(4n)/σ_fiss ≈ 6×10⁻⁵; a statistical model with B_f=1.5 MeV predicts ≈10⁻¹¹, while B_f=4.15 MeV reproduces the experimental value.
- The experimental anisotropy A=W(π)/W(π/2) increases from ≈1.85 to ≈2.6 as E_cm decreases from 75.6 MeV to 72.8 MeV; the TSSM predicts a decrease from ≈1.16 to ≈1.1.
- For E_cm=72.8 MeV, <J²>=80 and K₀²=200; for E_cm=75.6 MeV, <J²>=128 and K₀²=200.
- The mass-summed FFAD anisotropy A=2.6 at 72.8 MeV is reproduced with β/Γ=1.66; at 75.6 MeV, A=1.85 is reproduced with β/Γ=1.66 and |Φ̇|=3.6°.
- With Γ≈2.5 keV (T_b=1 MeV, B_f=4.15 MeV), β≈4 keV and ℏ/β≈1.65×10⁻¹⁹ sec, about 3 orders of magnitude longer than the CN thermalization time ℏ/Γ_spr≈1.3×10⁻²² sec (Γ_spr≈5 MeV).
- The light-to-heavy fragment yield ratio r=1.8 at E_cm=72.8 MeV (integrated over 110°≤θ_cm≤155°) is reproduced with β_(π₁≠π₂)/Γ=3.36 while keeping β_(π₁=π₂)/Γ=1.66.

## Context

This work extends the transition-state statistical model of Halpern and Strutinski (1958) by introducing compound nucleus spin off-diagonal correlations, and contradicts the interpretation of Hinde et al. (1996) that mass-angle correlation in ¹⁶O+²³⁸U originates from orientation-dependent quasifission. The authors rely on the experimental finding of Nishio et al. (2004) that complete fusion is the main process in the sub-barrier region for this reaction, refuting the quasifission interpretation. The result enables more reliable predictions of production cross sections for superheavy elements, as demonstrated by the observation of ²⁷¹Hs via the reaction ²⁶Mg+²⁴⁸Cm reported by Dvorak et al. (2008).
