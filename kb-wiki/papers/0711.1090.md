# TDDFT with Skyrme Forces: Effect of Time-Odd Densities on Electric Giant Resonances

> Time-odd densities and their effect on electric giant resonances are investigated within the self-consistent separable random-phase-approximation (SRPA) model for various Skyrme forces (SkT6, SkO, SkM*, SIII, SGII, SLy4, SLy6, SkI3). Time-odd densities restore Galilean invariance of the Skyrme functional, violated by the effective-mass and spin-orbital terms. In even-even nuclei these densities do not contribute to the ground state but can affect the dynamics. As a particular case, we explore the role of the current density in description of isovector E1 and isoscalar E2 giant resonances in a chain of Nd spherical and deformed isotopes with A=134-158. Relation of the current to the effective masses and relevant parameters of the Skyrme functional is analyzed. It is shown that current contribution to E1 and E2 resonances is generally essential and fully determined by the values and signs of the isovector and isoscalar effective-mass parameters of the force. The contribution is the same for all the isotope chain, i.e. for both standard and exotic nuclei.

# TDDFT with Skyrme Forces: Effect of Time-Odd Densities on Electric Giant Resonances

## Key claim

The contribution of the time-odd current density to isovector E1 and isoscalar E2 giant resonances is fully determined by the values and signs of the isovector and isoscalar effective-mass parameters of the Skyrme force, and this contribution is the same across the entire isotope chain from standard to exotic nuclei, but no single Skyrme force can simultaneously describe both E1(T=1) and E2(T=0) resonances equally well.

## Method

The authors used the self-consistent separable random-phase-approximation (SRPA) model with factorized Skyrme forces to compute electric giant resonances in a chain of Nd isotopes with A=134-158. They compared results obtained with and without the contribution of the time-odd current density for eight different Skyrme parametrizations (SkT6, SkO, SkM*, SIII, SGII, SLy4, SLy6, SkI3). The calculations used a Lorentz smoothing with averaging parameter Delta = 2 MeV and a large configuration space including up to 247 proton and 307 neutron single-particle levels.

## Key numbers

- The E1(T=1) and E2(T=0) resonances were computed in Nd isotopes with A=134-158.
- For the force SLy6 in 150Nd, the configuration space included 247 proton and 307 neutron single-particle levels up to approximately +20 MeV, yielding 13000 2-quasiparticle configurations for E1(T=1) and 22000 for E2(T=0) excitations with energies up to 65 MeV.
- Energy-weighted sum rules (EWSR) were exhausted by 93-99% for E2(T=0) and 95-97% for E1(T=1), depending on the force.
- The averaging parameter for the Lorentz strength function was Delta = 2 MeV in all calculations.
- The empirical estimation for E2(T=0) resonance energy is E = 62 A^{-1/3} MeV; for E1(T=1) the empirical estimation is E = 81 A^{-1/3} MeV.
- For Skyrme forces with m*/m less than 0.8, no single force can describe both E1(T=1) and E2(T=0) simultaneously.
- The spin-orbital density contribution to EWSR can reach 7-8% for forces with low m* such as SkI3.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Nesterenko et al. (2006, 2007) to a much larger set of nuclei and Skyrme forces, making the exploration systematic. The paper classifies Skyrme forces into three groups based on their isoscalar parameter B0 and isovector parameter B1, which determine the current-density contribution to giant resonances. The results enable future work on upgrading and unifying Skyrme forces by showing that time-odd density effects, which restore Galilean invariance violated by velocity-dependent time-even densities as noted by Engel (1975) and Dobaczewski, can be studied in standard nuclei and then transferred to exotic nuclei.
