# Non-radiative synthesis of Be7 in solar plasma

> The nuclear reaction e + He3 + He4 ---> e + Be7 is considered at thermonuclear energies. The motion of the electron is treated within the adiabatic approximation and the He3-He4 scattering state is calculated using an effective He3-He4 potential constructed via the Marchenko inverse scattering method. The reaction rate thus obtained for solar interior conditions is approximately 10^{-4} of the corresponding rate for the radiative capture He3 + He4 ----> Be7 + gamma. However, at the conditions corresponding to the early stages of the primordial nucleosynthesis, the non-radiative fusion is five times faster than the radiative one. The importance of the non-radiative processes for the energy balance in the sun, which for electrons is totally opaque, is discussed.

# Non-radiative synthesis of Be7 in solar plasma

## Key claim
The three-body reaction e + He3 + He4 → e + Be7 proceeds at a rate approximately 10^{-4} of the radiative capture He3 + He4 → Be7 + gamma under solar interior conditions, but under early primordial nucleosynthesis conditions the non-radiative fusion is five times faster than the radiative one. The authors caution that while the triple collision frequency is much lower than binary collision frequency, the transition probability for three-body reactions can be higher because triple collisions are kinematically less restricted.

## Method
The authors reduced the eight-body problem to a three-body system (electron, He3, He4) and treated the electron motion within the adiabatic approximation, separating the nuclear and electron interactions. The He3-He4 scattering state was calculated using an effective potential constructed via the Marchenko inverse scattering method, which reproduces Resonating Group Model phase shifts and the correct binding energies of Be7. The electron-nucleus interaction was treated in the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) with Debye-Huckel screening for the solar plasma environment, and the Coulomb potentials included finite nuclear charge distributions with Gaussian form factors using experimental rms radii.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of non-radiative three-body fusion reactions in stellar and primordial plasmas, following studies of e+n+p→e+d by Picker (1984) and e+p+d→e+He3 and e+p+Be7→e+B8 by Rakityansky et al. (1997) and Monakhov et al. (1998). The authors address the Be7 paradox in the context of the solar neutrino problem, where combined neutrino flux measurements suggest Be7 production must be suppressed relative to standard solar model predictions (Bahcall 1989; Degl'Innocenti et al. 1994). The results enable inclusion of non-radiative processes in solar energy balance calculations and primordial nucleosynthesis models, where the authors note the current uncertainty in Be7 production is assumed to be about 16% (Fiorentini, Lisi, Sarkar, and Villante 1998).
