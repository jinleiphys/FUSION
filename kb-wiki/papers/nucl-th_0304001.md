# Pseudo + quasi SU(3): Towards a shell-model description of heavy deformed nuclei

> The pseudo-SU(3) model has been extensively used to study normal parity bands in even-even and odd-mass heavy deformed nuclei. The use of a realistic Hamiltonian that mixes many SU(3) irreps has allowed for a successful description of energy spectra and electromagnetic transition strengths. While this model is powerful, there are situations in which the intruder states must be taken into account explicitly. The quasi-SU(3) symmetry is expected to complement the model, allowing for a description of nucleons occupying normal and intruder parity orbitals using a unified formalism.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that combining the pseudo-SU(3) model (for normal parity bands) with the quasi-SU(3) symmetry (for intruder parity orbitals) can provide a unified shell-model description of heavy deformed nuclei, but this remains a programmatic goal rather than a demonstrated result, the actual calculations shown are limited to normal parity states only.

## Method

The authors employed a realistic Hamiltonian containing spherical Nilsson single-particle energies, quadrupole-quadrupole interactions, monopole pairing interactions, and additional terms (K_J^2, J^2, and C_2) with strengths taken from known systematics, diagonalized in a strongly truncated pseudo-SU(3) Hilbert space. For even-even nuclei, configurations with pseudo-spin zero were used; for odd-mass nuclei, pseudo-spin 1/2 and in some cases 3/2 states were included. The quasi-SU(3) analysis examined the interplay of quadrupole-quadrupole and spin-orbit interactions in a SU(3) basis for light nuclei (sd-shell) to establish truncation schemes.

## Key numbers

- For $^{166}$Er: B(E2; 0_gs → 2_gs) = 580 × 10^{-2} e²b² (experimental 580 ± 27); B(E2; 2_gs → 4_gs) = 299 × 10^{-2} e²b² (experimental 303 ± 20); B(E2; 2_γ → 4_gs) = 1.485 × 10^{-2} e²b² (experimental 0.363 ± 0.027)
- For $^{166}$Er: effective charges used were 1.25e (neutrons) and 2.25e (protons)
- For $^{167}$Er: B(E2; 1/2^-_1 → 3/2^-_1) = 275 × 10^{-2} e²b²; B(E2; 1/2^-_1 → 5/2^-_1) = 415 × 10^{-2} e²b²; effective charges were 1.3e (neutrons) and 2.3e (protons)
- For $^{22}$Ne: the J=0 ground state wave function has 54% (8,2) irrep, 23% (9,0) irrep, and 11% (7,1) irrep from the Q·Q + l·s Hamiltonian; the overlap with full shell-model calculations (Retamosa 1990) sums to 67% for these components
- For the quasi-SU(3) analysis of systems with four protons and four neutrons in pf and sdg shells, the overlap between full-space and truncated-space calculations was always better than (0.95)²

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-SU(3) model developed by Hecht (1969) and Ratcliff (1973), which had been successfully applied to normal parity bands in even-even rare earth isotopes ($^{154}$Sm, $^{156,158,160}$Gd, $^{160,162,164}$Dy, $^{164,166,168}$Er) by Beuschel (1998, 2000), Popa (2000), and Draayer (2001), and to odd-mass nuclei ($^{157}$Gd, $^{159,161}$Tb, $^{159,163}$Dy, $^{159}$Eu, $^{161,169}$Tm, $^{165,167}$Er) by Vargas (2000a, 2000b, 2001). The quasi-SU(3) approach builds on shell-model studies by Zuker (1995) in the pf and sdg shells, and the authors' own prior work (Vargas 1998) on SU(3) truncation schemes. The combined pseudo+quasi SU(3) formalism aims to enable realistic shell-model calculations for deformed nuclei throughout the periodic table, including both normal and intruder parity orbitals.
