# Analysis of the low-energy $π^\pm p$ differential cross sections of the CHAOS Collaboration

> This paper presents the results of an analysis of the low-energy $π^\pm p$ differential cross sections, acquired by the CHAOS Collaboration at TRIUMF \cite{chaos,denz}. We first analyse separately the $π^+ p$ and the $π^- p$ elastic-scattering measurements on the basis of standard low-energy parameterisations of the $s$- and p-wave $K$-matrix elements. After the removal of the outliers, we subject the truncated $π^\pm p$ elastic-scattering databases into a common optimisation scheme using the ETH model \cite{glmbg}; the optimisation failed to produce reasonable values for the model parameters. We conclude that the problems we have encountered in the analysis of these data are due to the shape of the angular distributions of their $π^+ p$ differential cross sections.

## Key claim

The CHAOS Collaboration's low-energy π±p differential cross sections (DENZ04) are incompatible with the rest of the low-energy pion-nucleon database; the authors conclude that the problems arise specifically from the shape of the angular distributions of the π⁺p differential cross sections, not from the π⁻p data.

## Method

The authors first analyzed the π⁺p and π⁻p elastic-scattering measurements separately using standard low-energy parameterizations of the s- and p-wave K-matrix elements, removing outliers one at a time until the data sets became consistent. They then subjected the truncated combined π±p databases to a common optimization using the ETH model (a Feynman-diagram-based model with seven parameters), and finally reproduced the DENZ04 data using the ZUAS12 phase-shift solution as reference to identify the kinematical regions of disagreement.

## Key numbers

- The DENZ04 π⁺p database contains 275 data points at five energies between 19.90 and 43.30 MeV; the π⁻p database contains 271 data points at the same energies.
- After outlier removal, the common fit of the K-matrix parameterizations to the truncated DENZ04 DB₊/₋ yielded a χ² of 751.5 for 521 degrees of freedom.
- The common fit of the ETH model to the truncated DENZ04 DB₊/₋ yielded a χ² of 783.3 for 528 degrees of freedom; the model parameters drifted to unreasonable values and the Hessian matrix failed, preventing uncertainty estimation.
- The F-test comparing the truncated DENZ04 DB₋ with the truncated ZUAS12 DB₋ gave a score of 1.515 for 268 and 321 degrees of freedom (p-value ≈ 1.9·10⁻⁴); for the π⁺p databases, the F-test score was 1.944 for 267 and 333 degrees of freedom (p-value ≈ 4.8·10⁻⁹).
- The scale factors for the DENZ04 π⁺p data at forward and medium angles showed discrepancies reaching 35%, with an average around 15%.
- The value of the isoscalar s-wave scattering length from the DENZ04 DB₋ was about 0.041 μc⁻¹, differing by a factor of 2 from the ZUAS12 result of 0.0803(11) μc⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous phase-shift analyses (UZH06 and ZUAS12) and directly addresses the self-consistency of the CHAOS Collaboration data (DENZ04) which had not been included in those analyses. The study contradicts the SAID group's treatment of these data (WI08 solution), which yielded large χ² values (703.22 for 274 π⁺p points, 503.30 for 271 π⁻p points). The authors conclude that the DENZ04 π⁺p data cannot be reconciled with the established low-energy pion-nucleon database, and that including them in future phase-shift analyses would introduce bias. The work enables a clearer separation of reliable π⁻p data from problematic π⁺p data in the low-energy regime.
