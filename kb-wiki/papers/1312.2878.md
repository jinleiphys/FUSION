# Status of three-neutrino oscillation parameters, circa 2013

> The standard three-neutrino (3nu) oscillation framework is being increasingly refined by results coming from different sets of experiments, using neutrinos from solar, atmospheric, accelerator and reactor sources. At present, each of the known oscillation parameters [the two squared mass gaps (delta m^2, Delta m^2) and the three mixing angles (theta_12}, theta_13, theta_23)] is dominantly determined by a single class of experiments. Conversely, the unknown parameters [the mass hierarchy, the theta_23 octant and the CP-violating phase delta] can be currently constrained only through a combined analysis of various (eventually all) classes of experiments. In the light of recent new results coming from reactor and accelerator experiments, and of their interplay with solar and atmospheric data, we update the estimated N-sigma ranges of the known 3nu parameters, and revisit the status of the unknown ones. Concerning the hierarchy, no significant difference emerges between normal and inverted mass ordering. A slight overall preference is found for theta_23 in the first octant and for nonzero CP violation with sin delta < 0; however, for both parameters, such preference exceeds 1 sigma only for normal hierarchy. We also discuss the correlations and stability of the oscillation parameters within different combinations of data sets.

## Key claim

The global fit of all neutrino oscillation data circa 2013 shows a slight overall preference for the first octant of theta_23 (theta_23 < pi/4) and for nonzero CP violation with sin(delta) < 0, but these preferences exceed 1 sigma only for normal hierarchy; no significant difference emerges between normal and inverted mass ordering.

## Method

The authors performed a global chi-squared analysis of neutrino oscillation data, combining long-baseline accelerator data from T2K and MINOS (both appearance and disappearance spectra), solar neutrino data, long-baseline reactor data from KamLAND, short-baseline reactor data from Daya Bay and RENO, and Super-Kamiokande atmospheric neutrino data (phases I-IV). The six oscillation parameters (delta m^2, Delta m^2, theta_12, theta_13, theta_23, delta) were left free at fixed hierarchy (normal or inverted), and parameter ranges at N standard deviations were defined through N sigma = sqrt(chi^2 - chi^2_min).

## Key numbers

- delta m^2 = 7.54 x 10^-5 eV^2 (best fit), with 1 sigma range 7.32-7.80 x 10^-5 eV^2 (NH or IH)
- sin^2 theta_12 = 0.308 (best fit), with 1 sigma range 0.291-0.325 (NH or IH)
- Delta m^2 = 2.43 x 10^-3 eV^2 (NH best fit), 1 sigma range 2.37-2.49 x 10^-3 eV^2
- Delta m^2 = 2.38 x 10^-3 eV^2 (IH best fit), 1 sigma range 2.32-2.44 x 10^-3 eV^2
- sin^2 theta_13 = 0.0234 (NH best fit), 1 sigma range 0.0215-0.0254
- sin^2 theta_13 = 0.0240 (IH best fit), 1 sigma range 0.0218-0.0259
- sin^2 theta_23 = 0.437 (NH best fit), 1 sigma range 0.414-0.470
- sin^2 theta_23 = 0.455 (IH best fit), 1 sigma range 0.424-0.594
- delta/pi = 1.39 (NH best fit), 1 sigma range 1.12-1.77
- delta/pi = 1.31 (IH best fit), 1 sigma range 0.98-1.60
- Delta chi^2(I-N) = -0.3 for the global fit (no significant hierarchy preference)

## Context

This work updates the previous global analysis by the same group (Fogli et al., 2012) by including new 2013-2014 data from Daya Bay, RENO, T2K, and MINOS, which significantly reduce the theta_13 uncertainty and provide new constraints on theta_23 and delta. The results are compared with other contemporary global fits (Gonzalez-Garcia et al., 2013) and with official Super-Kamiokande analyses (Wendell et al., 2012; Himmel et al., 2013), noting that the theta_23 octant preference remains unstable across different analyses. The work enables future studies to track how upcoming accelerator and reactor data will resolve the current hints for CP violation and the theta_23 octant.
