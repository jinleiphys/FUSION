# How to measure the parity of the $Î˜^+$ in $\vec p\vec p$ collisions

> Triggered by a recent paper by Thomas, Hicks and Hosaka, we investigate which observables can be used to determine the parity of the $Î˜^+$ from the reaction $\vec p\vec p \to Î£^+Î˜^+$ near its production threshold. In particular, we show that the sign of the spin correlation coefficient $A_{xx}$ for small excess energies yields the negative of the parity of the $Î˜^+$. The argument relies solely on the Pauli principle and parity conservation and is therefore model--independent.

## Key claim

The sign of the spin correlation coefficient \(A_{xx}\) in the reaction \(\vec p\vec p \to \Sigma^+\Theta^+\) near threshold yields the negative of the parity of the \(\Theta^+\), but this identification holds only if the final-state interaction between the \(\Sigma^+\) and \(\Theta^+\) is weak, which the authors argue is justified given the known properties of the \(\Theta^+\).

## Method

The authors construct the most general transition amplitude for \(pp \to \Sigma^+\Theta^+\) near threshold, respecting parity conservation and the Pauli principle, and expand it in powers of the final-state center-of-mass momentum \(p'\). They derive expressions for the spin correlation coefficient \(A_{xx}\) in terms of the partial-wave amplitudes for both positive and negative parity of the \(\Theta^+\), keeping terms up to order \(p'^2\). They then estimate the energy range where the threshold formulas apply by identifying the scale \(\Lambda^2\) with the momentum transfer \(t \sim -m_x M_N\).

## Key numbers

- The threshold formulas for \(A_{xx}\) should be applicable for excess energies \(Q < 50\) MeV, based on the condition \((p'/\Lambda)^2 \sim 0.1\) and the relation \(p'^2/\Lambda^2 \sim 2 Q / M_N\).
- The estimated \(\Theta^+\) production cross section for \(pp \to \Sigma^+\Theta^+\) at \(Q \le 100\) MeV is about 80â€“120 nb, assuming a \(\Theta^+\) width of 5 MeV.
- A 5\(\sigma\) signal in the \(K^0 p\) invariant mass distribution was observed in the reaction \(pp \to \Sigma^+ K^0 p\) at a beam momentum of 2.95 GeV/c, with a very preliminary cross section estimate of a few hundred nb and a peak width of about 25 MeV (experimental resolution of TOF).

## Context

This work extends the model-independent parity-determination method proposed by Thomas, Hicks, and Hosaka (2003) for the \(\Theta^+\) pentaquark, which itself was based on an earlier method by Pak and Rekalo for the kaon. The paper provides a detailed analysis of the energy and angular dependence of the observable \(A_{xx}\) and discusses experimental feasibility at the COSY facility in Julich, enabling a practical measurement that can distinguish between the positive parity predicted by chiral soliton models (e.g., Diakonov, Petrov, Polyakov) and the negative parity indicated by lattice calculations.
