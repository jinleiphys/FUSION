# Perturbation Theory for Spin Ladders Using Angular-Momentum Coupled Bases

> We compute bulk properties of Heisenberg spin-1/2 ladders using Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation theory in the rung and plaquette bases. We formulate a method to extract high-order perturbative coefficients in the bulk limit from solutions for relatively small finite clusters. For example, a perturbative calculation for an isotropic $2\times 12$ ladder yields an eleventh-order estimate of the ground-state energy per site that is within 0.02% of the density-matrix-renormalization-group (DMRG) value. Moreover, the method also enables a reliable estimate of the radius of convergence of the perturbative expansion. We find that for the rung basis the radius of convergence is $λ_c\simeq 0.8$, with $λ$ defining the ratio between the coupling along the chain relative to the coupling across the chain. In contrast, for the plaquette basis we estimate a radius of convergence of $λ_c\simeq 1.25$. Thus, we conclude that the plaquette basis offers the only currently available perturbative approach which can provide a reliable treatment of the physically interesting case of isotropic $(λ=1)$ spin ladders. We illustrate our methods by computing perturbative coefficients for the ground-state energy per site, the gap, and the one-magnon dispersion relation.

# Perturbation Theory for Spin Ladders Using Angular-Momentum Coupled Bases

## Key claim

The plaquette basis provides a perturbative approach to Heisenberg spin-1/2 ladders that converges for isotropic couplings (lambda=1), whereas the commonly used rung basis has a radius of convergence of only lambda_c approximately 0.8, making it unsuitable for the physically interesting isotropic case. The authors extract high-order perturbative coefficients for bulk systems from calculations on relatively small finite clusters by exploiting the connectedness of perturbative energy contributions.

## Method

The authors apply Rayleigh-Schrodinger perturbation theory to Heisenberg spin-1/2 ladders using two different angular-momentum coupled bases: the rung basis, where the unperturbed Hamiltonian contains intra-rung interactions and the perturbation couples adjacent rungs, and the plaquette basis, where the unperturbed Hamiltonian contains intra-plaquette interactions and the perturbation couples adjacent plaquettes. They extract bulk-limit perturbative coefficients by exploiting the connectedness of energy contributions, which ensures that at nth order, the largest cluster contributing contains n+1 clusters (rungs or plaquettes), so calculations on finite ladders with N clusters yield coefficients up to order N-1 for the ground-state energy and spin gap, and up to order N-2 for the one-magnon dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

- For an isotropic 2x12 ladder, the 11th-order estimate of the ground-state energy per site is within 0.02% of the DMRG value
- The radius of convergence in the rung basis is lambda_c approximately 0.8, where lambda = J_parallel/J_perp
- The radius of convergence in the plaquette basis is lambda_c approximately 1.25 (for the triplet state) to 1.4 (for the ground state)
- The unperturbed ground-state energy per site is -3/8 in the rung basis and -1/2 in the plaquette basis
- The spin gap starts at Delta = 1 in both bases at zeroth order
- At 7th order in the rung basis, the perturbative spin gap agrees with DMRG to about 0.07% for lambda=0.25
- At 4th order in the plaquette basis, the perturbative spin gap agrees with DMRG to better than 10% for the isotropic case

## Context

This work extends perturbative treatments of Heisenberg spin ladders beyond the standard rung-basis approach used by Barnes (1993), Reigrotzki (1994), and Gopalan (1994), which are routinely assumed to be meaningful even at isotropic couplings. The authors had previously developed the plaquette basis in Piekarewicz and Shepard (1997). The method avoids the more complicated diagrammatic connected-graph expansions of Gelfand (1990) by exploiting the simplicity of quasi-one-dimensional systems. The results enable reliable perturbative calculations for isotropic spin ladders, which are of interest for understanding high-temperature superconductivity as discussed by Dagotto and Rice (1996), and open the door to mean-field treatments and studies of doped ladders using the plaquette basis.
