# From Brueckner Approach to Skyrme-type Energy Density Functional

> A Skyrme-like effective interaction is built up from the equation of state of nuclear matter. The latter is calculated in the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation with two and three body forces. A complete Skyrme parametrization requires a fit of the neutron and proton effective masses and also the Landau parameters. The new parametrization is probed on the properties of a set of closed-shell and closed-subshell nuclei, including binding energies and charge radii.

# From Brueckner Approach to Skyrme-type Energy Density Functional

## Key claim

A Skyrme-type effective interaction can be derived from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations of infinite nuclear matter including three-body forces, but the resulting parametrization (LNS) systematically underestimates charge radii by 2-4% and deviates from empirical binding energies by up to 5% because the BHF saturation density (0.18 fm⁻³) is larger than the empirical value.

## Method

The authors performed extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (EBHF) calculations of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter including two- and three-body forces, extracting the equation of state, nucleon effective masses, symmetry energy, and Landau parameters. They then fitted a standard Skyrme interaction (with density-dependence exponent σ=1/6 fixed) to reproduce these infinite-matter properties, using the effective masses to determine the velocity-dependent combinations Θₛ and Θᵥ, the equation of state to determine t₀, t₃, x₀, x₃, and the G₀ Landau parameter plus a surface coefficient αₛ to determine t₁, x₁, t₂, x₂. The spin-orbit parameter W₀ was adjusted to the 1p1/2-1p3/2 splitting in ¹⁶O. The resulting LNS parametrization was tested in spherical Hartree-Fock calculations of nine closed-shell and closed-subshell nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.1746 fm⁻³ (from LNS parametrization; BHF value was 0.18 fm⁻³)
- Saturation energy E/A = -15.32 MeV (LNS)
- Compression modulus K∞ = 210.85 MeV (LNS)
- Isoscalar effective mass m*/m = 0.825 (LNS at saturation)
- Isovector effective mass m*/m = 0.727 (LNS at saturation)
- Symmetry energy aₛ(ρ₀) = 33.4 MeV (LNS; BHF value was 34 MeV)
- Θₛ = 400.8 MeV fm⁵ (from fit to symmetric matter effective masses)
- Θᵥ = 356.4 MeV fm⁵ (from fit to asymmetric matter effective masses)
- G₀ Landau parameter = 0.83 (EBHF value at ρ₀ = 0.18 fm⁻³)
- Spin-orbit strength W₀ = 96.00 MeV fm⁵ (LNS)
- Charge radii underestimated by 2-4% across the tested nuclei
- Binding energy deviations within 5% across the tested nuclei

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts by Baldo et al. (2004) to relate Brueckner results to Skyrme parameters, but improves upon them by including constraints from effective masses and Landau parameters to fully determine the velocity-dependent part of the force. The approach contrasts with purely phenomenological Skyrme parametrizations such as SLy4 (Chabanat et al., 1998) and the force of Goriely et al. (2003), which are fitted directly to nuclear data. The LNS force reproduces the EBHF prediction that neutron effective mass increases with neutron excess while proton effective mass decreases, opposite to the behavior of some Skyrme forces but consistent with Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations. The work enables future improvements by showing that if BHF calculations could yield a saturation point closer to the empirical value (0.16 fm⁻³, -16 MeV), the resulting Skyrme parametrization would likely give much better finite-nuclei results.
