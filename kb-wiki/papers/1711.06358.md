# Partial restoration of chiral symmetry in cold nuclear matter: the $ϕ$-meson case

> The work presented at this workshop is divided into two parts. In the first part, the mass and decay width of the $ϕ$-meson in cold nuclear matter are computed in an effective Lagrangian approach. The medium dependence of these properties are obtained by evaluating kaon-antikaon loop contributions to the $ϕ$-meson self-energy, employing medium-modified kaon masses calculated using the quark-meson coupling model. The loop integral is regularized with a dipole form factor, and the sensitivity of the results to the choice of cutoff mass in the form factor is investigated. At normal nuclear matter density, we find a downward shift of the $ϕ$ mass by a few percent, while the decay width is enhanced by an order of magnitude. Our results support the literature which suggest that one should observe a small downward mass shift and a large broadening of the decay width. In the second part, we present $ϕ$-meson--nucleus bound state energies and absorption widths for four selected nuclei, calculated by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with complex optical potentials. The attractive potential for the $ϕ$-meson in the nuclear medium originates from the in-medium enhanced KK loop in the $ϕ$-meson selfenergy. The results suggest that the $ϕ$-meson should form bound states with all the nuclei considered. However, the identification of the signal for these predicted bound states will need careful investigation because of their sizable absorption widths.

## Key claim

The authors compute that at normal nuclear matter density, the phi-meson mass shifts downward by only a few percent (approximately 2%) while its decay width broadens by an order of magnitude, but these results are sensitive to the choice of cutoff parameter in the dipole form factor used to regularize the kaon-antikaon loop integral.

## Method

The phi-meson self-energy in cold nuclear matter was computed from kaon-antikaon loop contributions using an effective Lagrangian with phi-K-antiK and phi-phi-K-antiK couplings, regularized by a dipole form factor with cutoff parameter Lambda_K. In-medium kaon and antikaon masses were calculated using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, and the phi-meson mass and width were determined self-consistently from the real and imaginary parts of the self-energy. For finite nuclei, complex optical potentials were constructed via local density approximation, and the Klein-Gordon equation was solved in momentum space to obtain bound state energies and absorption widths for 4He, 12C, 16O, and 208Pb.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3, the in-medium kaon Lorentz scalar mass decreases by about 13%.
- For cutoff Lambda_K = 3000 MeV, the in-medium phi-meson mass is 994.9 MeV (without the phi-phi-K-antiK term) or 1004.3 MeV (with that term), compared to the vacuum mass of approximately 1019.5 MeV.
- For Lambda_K = 3000 MeV, the in-medium phi-meson decay width at rho_0 is 32.8 MeV (without the phi-phi-K-antiK term) or 36.0 MeV (with that term), versus the vacuum width of about 4.3 MeV.
- For 208Pb with Lambda_K = 4000 MeV, the 1s bound state energy is -25.8 MeV with half-width Gamma/2 = 16.0 MeV; for 4He with the same cutoff, the 1s bound state energy is -1.0 MeV with half-width 8.3 MeV.
- For 12C with Lambda_K = 4000 MeV, the 1s bound state energy is -9.8 MeV with half-width 11.2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical literature on in-medium phi-meson properties by using the QMC model to compute kaon masses self-consistently, rather than relying on chiral perturbation theory as in Ko (1992) or QCD sum rules as in Hatsuda and Lee (1991, 1996). The results support the consensus from Klingl (1997), Cabrera (2002), and Gubler (2015) that the phi-meson experiences a small downward mass shift (less than 2-3%) and a large broadening (30-45 MeV) at normal nuclear density. The bound state calculations directly address the experimental proposals at J-PARC (E16 and E29 collaborations) and JLab to search for phi-meson-nucleus bound states, showing that while bound states should form, their sizable absorption widths (8-17 MeV half-widths) will make experimental identification challenging.
