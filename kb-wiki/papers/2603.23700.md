# Semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering on a polarized spin-1 target. II. Deuteron and spectator nucleon tagging

> We develop the theoretical framework for semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering on a polarized spin-1 target and apply it to scattering on the polarized deuteron with spectator nucleon tagging. In Part I (previous article) we present the general form of the semi-inclusive cross section and polarization observables for the spin-1 target. In Part II (this article) we consider deep-inelastic scattering on the polarized deuteron with spectator nucleon tagging as a special case of target fragmentation. Methods of light-front quantization are employed to separate nuclear and hadronic structure in the high-energy process and achieve a composite description. The light-front wave function of the polarized deuteron is obtained from a rotationally covariant 3-dimensional wave function in the center-of-mass frame of the proton-neutron system. The tagged structure functions are computed in the impulse approximation. The momentum and spin distribution of the active nucleon are controlled by the deuteron polarization and the detected spectator momentum ($D/S$ wave ratio). The cross section and spin asymmetries are evaluated for general deuteron polarization (vector and tensor, longitudinal and transverse) as functions of the spectator momentum. Tensor-polarized spin asymmetries of order unity are achieved for spectator momenta $\sim$ 300 MeV, which select configurations with large $D$-wave. Sum rules for the tagged spin structure functions are derived. The results can be used for simulations of spectator tagging in future polarized fixed-target experiments (Jefferson Lab) or at the Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that tensor-polarized spin asymmetries of order unity can be achieved in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering on the polarized deuteron with spectator nucleon tagging at spectator momenta around 300 MeV, which select configurations with large D-wave, whereas inclusive DIS without tagging yields tensor-polarized asymmetries much smaller than unity.

## Method

The authors employ light-front quantization methods to separate nuclear and hadronic structure in the high-energy process, computing the tagged structure functions in the impulse approximation. The light-front wave function of the polarized deuteron is obtained from a rotationally covariant three-dimensional wave function in the center-of-mass frame of the proton-neutron system. Two formulations are used, light-front quantum mechanics and the virtual nucleon scheme, and their comparison illustrates theoretical uncertainties of the method.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy is ε_D = 2.2 MeV, and the typical nucleon momenta in the deuteron are of order the binding momentum √(ε_D m) = 45 MeV.
- Tensor-polarized spin asymmetries of order unity are achieved for spectator momenta ∼ 300 MeV, which select configurations with large D-wave.
- The D-wave becomes prominent at nucleon momenta ≳ 200 MeV.
- The upper limit on the proton plus momentum in the DIS limit is α_p < 2 − x, neglecting power corrections of order O(x² m²/Q²).
- The invariant polarization parameters for a pure spin state with longitudinal polarization are {T_LL, T_LT, T_TT} = W(Λ) × {1/3, 0, 0}, and for transverse polarization {T_LL, T_LT, T_TT} = W(Λ) × {−1/6, 0, 1/2}.

## Context

This work extends the general formalism for semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering on polarized spin-1 targets developed in Part I (Cosyn and Weiss, previous article) to the specific case of the polarized deuteron with spectator nucleon tagging. It builds on the theoretical framework of Frankfurt and Strikman (1983, 1981) for spectator tagging in DIS on the deuteron, and on prior work by Cosyn, Sargsian, and others on neutron structure extraction and short-range correlations. The results enable simulations of polarized spectator tagging experiments at Jefferson Lab (BONuS, ALERT, Deeps, BAND, LAD) and at the Electron-Ion Collider, and illustrate the unique spin structures appearing in SIDIS on a spin-1 target where the dynamics is well understood.
