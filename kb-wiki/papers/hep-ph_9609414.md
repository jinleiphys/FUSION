# On the Physics of a Cool Pion Gas

> At finite temperature, the Nambu-Goldstone bosons of a spontaneously broken chiral symmetry travel at a velocity $v < 1$. This effect first appears at order $\sim T^4$ in an expansion about low temperature, and can be related to the appearence of two distinct pion decay constants in a thermal bath. We discuss some consequences on the thermodynamics of a gas of massless pions.

## Key claim

At finite temperature, the Nambu-Goldstone bosons of spontaneously broken chiral symmetry travel at a velocity v < 1, an effect that first appears at order ~ T^4 in a low-temperature expansion; the abstract glosses over the condition that this result is derived in the chiral limit (massless pions) and that the effect is related to the appearance of two distinct pion decay constants in a thermal bath.

## Method

The authors use chiral perturbation theory (chiPT) and a weakly coupled linear sigma model with a light sigma particle (m_sigma^2 = 2 lambda sigma^2, f_pi = sigma) to compute the temperature-dependent temporal and spatial pion decay constants f_pi^t and f_pi^s. From current conservation, the ratio f_pi^s / f_pi^t gives the pion velocity squared. The calculation is performed to one-loop order in the linear sigma model, expanding in powers of T/m_sigma, and the results are compared with the three-loop chiPT calculation of the energy density by Gerber and Leutwyler (1990s).

## Key numbers

- The vacuum pion decay constant is f_pi ~ 93 MeV.
- To leading order in low-temperature expansion, f_pi(T) = f_pi (1 - T^2 / (12 f_pi^2)), with f_pi^t = f_pi^s to this order.
- The velocity squared is v^2 ~ 1 - 8 t_2, where t_2 = (pi^2 T^4) / (45 f_pi^2 m_sigma^2).
- The energy density of a massless pion gas is u = (1/10) pi^2 T^4 [1 + (T^4 / (108 f_pi^4)) (7 ln(Lambda_p / T) - 1) + O(T^6)], with Lambda_p ~ 275 MeV.
- From the energy density, the quasi-pion mean velocity is v ~ 1 - (1/3) (T^4 / (108 f_pi^4)) (7 ln(Lambda_p / T) - 1).
- For massive pions, Schenk (1990s) estimates v(0) ~ 0.87 at T ~ 150 MeV.
- In the linear sigma model near T_c, f_pi(T) = sigma(T) = sigma (1 - T^2 / (4 sigma^2)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Pisarski and Tytgat, 1996) and connects to the broader understanding of pion propagation in a thermal medium. The result that v < 1 at order T^4 is implicit in the three-loop chiPT calculation of the energy density by Gerber and Leutwyler (1990s), and the dispersion relation with v < 1 has been advocated by Shuryak (1990s) and by Gale and Kapusta (1990s) in the context of dilepton production from pion annihilation. The authors draw an analogy to spin waves in ferromagnets, where Dyson (1950s) found that magnon interactions produce a T^4 correction to magnetization with no T^3 term, similar to the absence of a T^6 term in the pion energy density. The work enables further study of pion dispersion relations near the critical temperature and the critical exponents of f_pi and the order parameter sigma.
