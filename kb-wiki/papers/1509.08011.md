# Jets and Centrality in p(d)-A Collisions

> The production of jets, and high-$p_{T}$ leading pions from jets, in d-Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) and p-Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) are studied. Using a modified version of the event generator PYTHIA, in conjunction with a nuclear Glauber Monte-Carlo event generator, we demonstrate how events with a hard jet may be simulated, in such a way that the parton distribution function of the projectile nucleon is frozen during its interaction with the extended nucleus. Using our hybrid Monte-Carlo event generator, we demonstrate that the enhancement in $R_{pA}$ seen in peripheral events at RHIC and at LHC, as well as the depletion in central or semi-central events, is mainly due to "mis-binning" of central and semi-central events with a jet, as peripheral events. This occurs due to the reduction of soft particle production caused by a depletion of energy available in a nucleon (of the deuteron in the case of d-Au collisions), after the production of a hard jet. This represents a form of "color transparency" of the projectile nucleon, which has fluctuated to a state with fewer and harder partons, in events which lead to jet production. We conclude with discussions of the form of multi-parton correlations in a nucleon which may be responsible for such a startling effect.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement in the nuclear modification factor \(R_{pA}\) in peripheral events and the depletion in central or semi-central events at both RHIC and LHC is mainly due to "mis-binning" of central and semi-central events that contain a jet as peripheral events, caused by a reduction of soft particle production when a nucleon's parton distribution function is frozen in a state with a hard parton.

## Method

The authors constructed a hybrid Monte-Carlo event generator combining a nuclear Glauber Monte-Carlo (using Woods-Saxon distributions for heavy nuclei and a Hulthén wavefunction for the deuteron) with a modified version of PYTHIA. The key modification was to "freeze" the parton distribution function of the projectile nucleon during its interaction with the extended nucleus, treating the entire event as a single proton impact on a "superproton" rather than as a sum of independent nucleon-nucleon collisions, and incorporating event-by-event nuclear shadowing. They then compared the simulated centrality-binned \(R_{dAu}\) for pions at RHIC and \(R_{CP}\) for jets and charged hadrons at LHC to experimental data from PHENIX and ATLAS.

## Key numbers

*   Woods-Saxon parameters for gold: \(R = 6.38\) fm, \(a = 0.535\) fm; for lead: \(R = 6.62\) fm, \(a = 0.546\) fm.
*   Hulthén wavefunction parameters for deuteron: \(a = 0.228\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(b = 1.18\) fm\(^{-1}\).
*   Nucleon exclusion volume diameter: 1.12 fm.
*   Woods-Saxon distribution sampled out to 13.3 fm; Hulthén distribution sampled out to 38.9 fm to ensure error less than \(3.67 \times 10^{-11}\).
*   Impact parameter sampled from an \(r^2\) distribution out to 10 fm.
*   For LHC jet analysis, the anti-\(k_T\) algorithm was used with \(R = 0.4\).
*   For LHC charged hadron analysis, particles with \(p_T < 10\) GeV were used for centrality binning.

## Context

This work extends the study of centrality-dependent nuclear modification factors in p(d)-A collisions, addressing the unexpected enhancement in peripheral events and suppression in central events reported by PHENIX (Sahlmueller, 2012) and ATLAS (ATLAS, 2014a; ATLAS, 2014b). The authors propose a "color transparency" mechanism where the projectile nucleon fluctuates to a state with fewer and harder partons in jet-producing events, which reduces soft particle production and shifts events to more peripheral centrality bins. This enables future investigation of multi-parton correlations in nucleons that could produce such an effect.
