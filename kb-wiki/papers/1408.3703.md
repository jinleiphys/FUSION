# Spectroscopy of heavy baryons with breaking of heavy-quark symmetry

> Transition decay widths by one-pion emissions for excited heavy-baryons with a single heavy (charm and bottom) quark are investigated by following the heavy-quark symmetry and its breaking effects at ${\cal O}(1/M)$ for a heavy-baryon mass $M$. Based on the heavy-baryon effective theory, interaction Lagrangian for the heavy baryons with axial-vector current induced by a pion is constructed. It is presented that the transition decay widths up to ${\cal O}(1/M)$ in several channels are constrained. The results will be useful in experimental study of excited heavy-baryons.

## Key claim

Transition decay widths for one-pion emissions between excited heavy baryons with arbitrary brown-muck spin are constrained by relations that hold up to \({\cal O}(1/M)\) in the heavy-baryon mass expansion, but the paper does not provide concrete numerical values for the decay widths because the coupling constants are not known.

## Method

The author constructs effective Lagrangians for heavy baryons with arbitrary brown-muck spin \(j\) using heavy-baryon effective theory, including both the leading-order heavy-quark symmetric terms and the \({\cal O}(1/M)\) breaking terms. The breaking terms are introduced via the spin-operator (Pauli-Lubanski vector) acting on the heavy-quark spin, and the invariance under velocity rearrangement is imposed. The resulting Lagrangians are used to derive relations among the dimensionless reduced decay widths \(\check{\Gamma}\) for \((j,j+1)\) transitions (between different HQS doublets) and \((j,j)\) transitions (within the same brown-muck spin) by eliminating the unknown coupling constants.

## Key numbers

* For \((j,j+1)\) transitions (case i), the general relation for any \(j\ge1\) is: \(\frac{j+1}{j} \check{\Gamma}[\Psi^{(j+1)}_{j+1/2} \rightarrow \Psi^{(j)}_{j-1/2}\pi] - (2j+2) \check{\Gamma}[\Psi^{(j+1)}_{j+1/2} \rightarrow \Psi^{(j)}_{j+1/2}\pi] = \check{\Gamma}[\Psi^{(j+1)}_{j+3/2} \rightarrow \Psi^{(j)}_{j+1/2}\pi] + {\cal O}(1/M^{2})\).
* For \((j,j+1)\) transitions (case ii), the general relation for any \(j\ge1\) is: \(\check{\Gamma}[\Psi^{(j)}_{j-1/2} \rightarrow \Psi^{(j+1)}_{j+1/2}\pi] = (2j+2) \check{\Gamma}[\Psi^{(j)}_{j+1/2} \rightarrow \Psi^{(j+1)}_{j+1/2}\pi] + \frac{j+1}{j+2} \check{\Gamma}[\Psi^{(j)}_{j+1/2} \rightarrow \Psi^{(j+1)}_{j+3/2}\pi] + {\cal O}(1/M^{2})\).
* For \((j,j)\) transitions, the general relation for any \(j\ge1\) is: \(\check{\Gamma}[\Psi^{(j)}_{j-1/2} \rightarrow \Psi'^{(j)}_{j+1/2}\pi] = \frac{j+1}{j} \check{\Gamma}[\Psi^{(j)}_{j+1/2} \rightarrow \Psi'^{(j)}_{j-1/2}\pi] + {\cal O}(1/M^{2})\).
* The kinematic factor used to define \(\check{\Gamma}\) is \(K^{(j+1,j)}_{J_{2},J_{1}} = \frac{1}{2\pi f_{\pi}^{2}} \left( (\Delta^{(j+1,j)})^{2} - m_{\pi}^{2} \right)^{3/2}\), where \(\Delta^{(j+1,j)} = m_{\pi}^{2}/(2M^{(j+1)}_{J_{2}}) + M^{(j+1)}_{J_{2}} - M^{(j)}_{J_{1}}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy-baryon decays in the heavy-quark limit (Isgur and Wise, 1991) and the \({\cal O}(1/M)\) corrections for ground states with brown-muck spins zero and one (Cheng, 1993) to excited heavy baryons with arbitrary brown-muck spin. The derived relations are weaker than those in the exact heavy-quark limit and are analogous to the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass formula for light-flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking. The results enable experimental tests of heavy-quark symmetry breaking in charm and bottom baryon decays, and the formalism is applicable to heavy mesons and exotic hadrons with a single heavy quark.
