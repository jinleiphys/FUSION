# Radiative heavy quark energy loss in an expanding viscous QCD plasma

> We study viscous effects on heavy quark radiative energy loss in a dynamically screened medium with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion. We calculate, to first order in opacity, the energy loss by incorporating viscous corrections in the single-particle phase-space distribution function within relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics. We consider the Grad's 14-moment and the Chapman-Enskog-like methods for the nonequilibrium distribution functions. Our numerical results for the charm quark radiative energy loss show that, as compared to static fluid, an expanding ideal (nonviscous) fluid causes a much smaller energy loss. Viscosity in the evolution lead to somewhat enhanced energy loss which is insensitive to the underlying viscous hydrodynamic models used. Further inclusion of viscous correction induces larger energy loss, the magnitude and pattern of this enhancement crucially depend on the form of viscous corrections used.

# Radiative heavy quark energy loss in an expanding viscous QCD plasma

## Key claim

Viscous corrections to the phase-space distribution function of the QCD medium significantly enhance heavy quark radiative energy loss, but the magnitude and pattern of this enhancement depend crucially on which form of viscous correction (Grad's 14-moment versus Chapman-Enskog-like method) is used, while the energy loss is insensitive to the underlying viscous hydrodynamic model (Muller-Israel-Stewart versus Chapman-Enskog) when only the dynamical evolution includes viscosity without distribution function corrections.

## Method

The authors computed heavy quark radiative energy loss to first order in opacity for charm quarks in a dynamically screened QCD medium with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, using relativistic second-order viscous hydrodynamics based on the Muller-Israel-Stewart framework and the Chapman-Enskog-like approach. Viscous corrections were incorporated in the single-particle phase-space distribution function via Grad's 14-moment approximation and the Chapman-Enskog method, and the energy loss was obtained by summing contributions from Feynman diagrams at first order in opacity with hard thermal loop gluon propagators.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass: M = 1.2 GeV
- Initial temperature: T0 = 400 MeV
- Initial proper time: tau0 = 0.4 fm/c
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: eta/s = 1/(4pi) (approximately 0.08) for the main results, with a range of eta/s = 0.08-0.24 explored
- Strong coupling constant: alpha_s = 0.3
- Effective number of degrees of freedom: N_f = 2.5
- Viscous effects from dynamics only (without distribution function corrections) caused approximately 5% larger energy loss for charm quarks with momentum p >= 10 GeV compared to ideal fluid at tau = 1.2 fm/c
- Total integration time for cumulative energy loss: tau_f = 5 fm/c

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of radiative energy loss in dynamically screened QCD media by Djordjevic (2007, 2008, 2009) from ideal fluids to viscous fluids, and is the first calculation incorporating viscosity both in the medium evolution and in the phase-space distribution function. The results are crucial for interpreting D-meson nuclear modification factor measurements at LHC, and the finding that Grad's 14-moment approximation gives larger viscous corrections than the Chapman-Enskog method is consistent with similar observations in hadron spectra and elliptic flow by Romatschke (2007), Song (2007), and Luzum (2008).
