# Event-shape fluctuations and flow correlations in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> I review recent measurements of a large set of flow observables associated with event-shape fluctuations and collective expansion in heavy ion collisions. First, these flow observables are classified and experiment methods are introduced. The experimental results for each type of observables are then presented and compared to theoretical calculations. A coherent picture of initial condition and collective flow based on linear and non-linear hydrodynamic responses is derived, which qualitatively describe most experimental results. I discuss new types of fluctuation measurements that can further our understanding of the event-shape fluctuations and collective expansion dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a coherent picture of initial conditions and collective flow based on linear and non-linear hydrodynamic responses can qualitatively describe most experimental results, but this description is not perfect everywhere, particularly for dipolar flow at LHC and higher-order harmonics in ultra-central collisions.

## Method

The author reviews and classifies flow observables into three types: those involving only flow amplitudes, those involving only event-plane angles, and those involving both amplitudes and phases. Experimental results for each observable type are presented and compared to theoretical calculations from hydrodynamic models, including IP-Glasma and MC Glauber initial conditions, using multi-particle correlation methods and event-shape selection techniques.

## Key numbers

- Flow harmonics up to at least 6th order are observed in the azimuthal distribution of produced particles
- In ultra-central Pb+Pb collisions (0-0.2% centrality), v3 is comparable or larger than v2
- v2(pT) peaks at around 1.5 GeV, while other harmonics peak at 3-4 GeV
- v2 breaks the factorization relation at 20-30% level in ultra-central collisions
- The first four cumulants of flow distributions are expressed as v_n{2}^2 = ⟨v_n^2⟩, v_n{4}^4 = -⟨v_n^4⟩+2⟨v_n^2⟩^2

## Context

This review extends the understanding of event-by-event fluctuations that emerged around 2010 (Alver 2010), showing that each collision produces a different fireball with its own shape and hydrodynamic expansion. The work builds on earlier measurements of harmonic flow at RHIC and LHC (Adare 2011, Adamczyk 2013, ALICE 2011, Aad 2012, Chatrchyan 2013) and compares with hydrodynamic calculations from Gale (2012), Gardim (2012), and Song (2013). The review enables future detailed mapping of flow observables and improved constraints on transport properties such as the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density.
