# Evolution of deformations in medium-mass nuclei

> Evolution of quadrupole deformations in $sd$ and $pf$ shell nuclei with mass A= 18$\sim$56 is studied by using deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock (HF) model with pairing correlations. We point out that the quadrupole deformations of the nuclei with the isospin T=0 and T=1 show strong mass number dependence as a clear manifestation of dynamical evolution of deformation in nuclear many-body systems. The competition between the deformation driving particle-vibration coupling and the closed shell structure is shown in a systematic study of the ratios between the proton and neutron deformations in nuclei with T=$|$T$_z|$=1. Calculated quadrupole and hexadecapole deformations are compared with shell model results and available experimental data. A relation between the skin thickness and the intrinsic Q$_2$ moments is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the mass number dependence of quadrupole deformations in sd and pf shell nuclei with isospin T=0 and T=1 is a clear manifestation of dynamical evolution of deformation, but the calculated Q2p moments are about 10-20% smaller than empirical values in nuclei with A=20-36, while agreement is better in pf shell nuclei.

## Method

Deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock (HF) plus BCS calculations were performed with two Skyrme interactions (SGII and SIII) and a density-dependent surface pairing interaction for nuclei with mass A=18-56. The axial symmetry was assumed for the HF deformed potential, and the constraint was imposed on the axial mass quadrupole moment operator. Intrinsic proton and neutron quadrupole moments Q2p and Q2n, as well as hexadecapole moments Q4p and Q4n, were calculated from the HF wave functions and compared with shell model results and experimental B(E2) data.

## Key numbers

* Calculated Q2p for 22Mg is approximately 50 fm^2, compared with empirical value 60±11 fm^2 (Raman 2001)
* Calculated Q2p for 22Ne is 43 fm^2, compared with empirical value 48.1±1.0 fm^2 (Raman 2001)
* Experimental B(E2) for 46Ti is (950±50) e^2fm^4; for 46Cr is (929±199) e^2fm^4; calculated with SGII gives 582.1 e^2fm^4 and 913.7 e^2fm^4 respectively
* Experimental B(E2) for 50Fe is (1359±261) e^2fm^4; calculated with SGII gives 1160 e^2fm^4
* Experimental B(E2) for 54Ni is (590±168) e^2fm^4; calculated with SGII gives 373 e^2fm^4
* Experimental B(E2) for 50Cr is (1080±60) e^2fm^4; calculated with SGII gives 1097 e^2fm^4
* Experimental B(E2) for 54Fe is (620±50) e^2fm^4; calculated with SGII gives 390 e^2fm^4
* The ratio (Q2p/Q2n)/(Z/N) is more than 2 in the A=18 system for both HF calculations and experiments
* For 46Cr, the pairing gain energy is about -16 MeV and the calculated total binding energy is -380 MeV, close to the empirical value -382 MeV
* In 44Ti, the prolate and oblate minima are almost degenerate in energy with small deformations

## Context

This work extends previous deformed HF calculations by Quentin, Flocard, and others, as well as Nilsson-Strutinsky type calculations by Ragnarsson and Sheline (1970s-1980s) and the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) calculations by Moller and collaborators. The results are compared with shell model calculations by Brown and Wildenthal (1982) for sd shell nuclei and by Honma et al. (2004) for pf shell nuclei. The study enables systematic understanding of deformation evolution across closed shells in medium-mass nuclei and provides predictions for proton-rich unstable nuclei where new experimental data from Coulomb excitation experiments by Yurkewicz et al. (2004) and Yamada et al. (2004) are becoming available.
