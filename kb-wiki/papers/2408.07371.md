# Survival Probabilities of Compound Superheavy Nuclei Towards Element 119

> To synthesize superheavy element 119 is becoming highly concerned as several experimental projects in major laboratories are being pursued. This work studied the survival probabilities of compound superheavy nuclei after multiple neutron emissions based on microscopic energy dependent fission barriers, demonstrating a significant role of triaxial deformation in decreasing the first fission barriers in the heaviest region. Together with the fusion cross sections by the dinuclear system model, the optimal energy and the residual cross section of $^{243}$Am($^{48}$Ca, 3$n$)$^{288}$Mc are reproduced. Finally the cross sections and optimal beam energies of $^{243}$Am+$^{54}$Cr and $^{249}$Bk+$^{50}$Ti reactions are estimated, providing clues for the synthesis of new elements.

# Survival Probabilities of Compound Superheavy Nuclei Towards Element 119

## Key claim
The survival probabilities of compound superheavy nuclei after multiple neutron emissions, calculated using microscopic energy-dependent fission barriers that include triaxial deformation, show that the $^{249}$Bk+$^{50}$Ti reaction with a lower beam energy is promising for synthesizing element 119, but the estimated cross section for the $^{243}$Am+$^{54}$Cr reaction is only about 11 fb, which is extremely low for experiments.

## Method
The authors calculated energy-dependent fission barriers using the finite-temperature Hartree-Fock+BCS (FT-BCS) approach with the Skyrme force UNEDF1, including triaxial and octupole deformations, for a cascade of superheavy isotopes. They combined these with fusion cross sections from the dinuclear system (DNS) model to compute residual cross sections after multiple neutron evaporation, using the hybrid model (Bohr-Wheeler plus imaginary free energy method) for fission probabilities and adjusting the level density parameter ratio $a_f/a_0$ to reproduce the benchmark $^{243}$Am($^{48}$Ca, 3$n$)$^{288}$Mc reaction.

## Key numbers
- For $^{291}$Mc, the fission barrier height is 5.72 MeV with triaxial deformation, compared to about 8.16 MeV with axial symmetry.
- The neutron separation energy $S_n$ of $^{291}$Mc is 6.77 MeV, which is larger than the barrier height of 5.72 MeV.
- For the $^{249}$Bk+$^{50}$Ti reaction, the estimated cross section at the 3$n$ channel is about 0.22 pb at an excitation energy $E^{*}$ of about 35 MeV.
- For the $^{243}$Am+$^{54}$Cr reaction, the estimated cross section is about 11 fb at $E^{*}$ of about 37.5 MeV in the 3$n$ evaporation channel.
- The calculated optimal beam energies $E_{\rm cm}$ are 226.8 MeV for $^{249}$Bk+$^{50}$Ti and 245.3 MeV for $^{243}$Am+$^{54}$Cr reactions.
- The optimal excitation energy for the $^{243}$Am+$^{48}$Ca 3$n$ channel is around 33 MeV, close to the experimental value of about 34 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous microscopic studies of first-chance survival probabilities based on energy-dependent fission barriers by Qiao et al. (2023) to include multiple neutron emissions and practical cross section calculations for element 119 synthesis. It addresses the discrepancy that microscopic fission barriers from self-consistent calculations are much higher than those from macroscopic-microscopic (MM) models by Moller et al. in the heaviest region, showing that triaxial deformation significantly reduces the first barriers. The results enable experimental planning for the $^{249}$Bk+$^{50}$Ti and $^{243}$Am+$^{54}$Cr reactions at facilities such as the SHE factory in Dubna and CAFE2 in Lanzhou, and contradict earlier predictions by Nasirov et al. and Zhang et al. that the optimal reaction for element 119 might be at the 4$n$ channel.
