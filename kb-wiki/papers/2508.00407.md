# Thermoelectric figure of merit and the deconfinement phase transition

> Thermoelectric phenomena are traditionally associated with the interconversion of thermal and electrical energy in many-body systems. In the context of high-temperature quantum chromodynamics (QCD) matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, thermoelectric responses can provide insight into the evolving microscopic dynamics and the redistribution of effective degrees of freedom across the phase transition region. In this work, for the first time, we present a phenomenological study of the thermoelectric figure of merit (\( ZT \)) in hot QCD matter, with a particular focus on its behavior across the hadronic and quark-gluon plasma phases. Using model-based calculations for the electrical conductivity, Seebeck coefficient, and thermal conductivity, we analyze the temperature dependence of \( ZT \) and identify characteristic features near the QCD phase transition temperature. Our results indicate that \( ZT \) exhibits nontrivial behavior near the transition region, reflecting the changing transport properties and active degrees of freedom in the medium. This phenomenological study of the thermoelectric figure of merit provides a complementary perspective to traditional transport studies and may provide critical insights for advancing the understanding of QCD matter through the transition region.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first phenomenological study of the thermoelectric figure of merit (\( ZT \)) in hot QCD matter, showing that \( ZT \) exhibits a peak near the QCD phase transition temperature, which the authors interpret as a sensitive probe of the deconfinement transition. The claim is hedged by the fact that \( ZT \) is not directly accessible in experiments and the study relies on model-based calculations using the quasiparticle model (QPM) for the QGP phase and the ideal Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model for the hadronic phase.

## Method

The authors calculate the Seebeck coefficient (\( S \)), electrical conductivity (\( \sigma_{el} \)), and thermal conductivity (\( \kappa_0 \)) by solving the linearized Boltzmann transport equation under the relaxation time approximation. For the QGP phase, they use a quasiparticle model (QPM) where partons have temperature-dependent effective thermal masses, with a two-loop QCD running coupling constant and a momentum-independent relaxation time. For the hadronic phase, they use the ideal Hadron Resonance Gas model with thermal-averaged relaxation times computed from hard-sphere scattering cross-sections, including all hadrons and resonances from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

* The peak in \( ZT \) occurs around \( T \simeq 160 \) MeV for lower values of baryon chemical potential (\( \mu_B \)), corresponding to the lattice QCD predicted deconfinement phase transition temperature.
* The QCD scale parameter used in the QPM is \( \Lambda_T = 0.115 \) GeV.
* For the HRG phase, the hard-sphere scattering cross-section is \( \sigma = 4\pi r_h^2 \), independent of temperature and baryon chemical potential.
* The relaxation time for quarks in the QPM is given by \( \tau_R = 1/[5.1T\alpha_s^{2}(T, \mu_B)\log\{1/\alpha_s(T, \mu_B)\}[1+0.12(2N_f+1)]] \).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of transport coefficients in QCD matter, including electrical conductivity (Singh 2023, Dey 2020, Ghosh 2019), thermal conductivity (Singh 2023), and the Seebeck coefficient (Singh 2024, Li 2019). It is motivated by observations in condensed matter systems where \( ZT \) shows nontrivial behavior near phase transitions, such as in silver selenide (Ag2Se), copper selenide (Cu2Se), tin selenide (SnSe), and germanium telluride (GeTe). The study enables a new phenomenological signature for probing the QCD deconfinement transition through combined thermoelectric transport properties, complementing traditional studies of individual transport coefficients.
