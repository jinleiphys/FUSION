# Temperature dependence of cluster decay

> A universal decay law (UDL) for light cluster decay of excited nuclei is formulated by fitting the UDL half-lives to those evaluated within an excitation energy-dependent double folding model (DFM). The half-lives are evaluated within a preformed cluster model. The excitation energy dependence is introduced both in the energy of the emitted cluster and in the interaction potential through the density distributions of the interacting nuclei. The half-lives are found to decrease when the difference between the excitation energies of the parent and daughter nuclei, $ΔE^{*} > $ 0, increases, with the reduction being a few orders of magnitude for higher values of $ΔE^{*}$. This can be of importance for nucleosynthesis calculations of heavy elements formed in extremely hot environments, as well as in highly energetic heavy-ion collisions. The UDL for excited nuclei is used to provide an estimate of the enhancement in the cluster decay rates of thermally excited nuclei such as those produced in r-process nucleosynthesis.

# Temperature dependence of cluster decay

## Key claim
The authors formulate an excitation-energy-dependent universal decay law (UDL) for light cluster decay, finding that half-lives decrease by a few orders of magnitude when the parent nucleus is more excited than the daughter nucleus (ΔE* > 0), but only within a specific temperature window between approximately 1 GK and 10 GK where thermal effects become significant.

## Method
The authors extend the double folding model (DFM) by introducing temperature-dependent nuclear density distributions for the daughter nucleus and an effective Q-value that accounts for parent and daughter excitation energies. They calculate half-lives within a preformed cluster model using the JWKB approximation for tunneling through the Coulomb barrier, then fit a single parameter (a₁) to produce an excitation-energy-dependent UDL that can be factorized into a ground-state term plus an excitation-energy-dependent correction.

## Key numbers
- The fitted parameter a₁ = 0.1724 ± 0.0006 MeV^(1/2), obtained from 36 transitions (6 parent states × 6 daughter states) for 8 heavy nuclei
- The UDL approximation is valid for decays where |χ' × ΔE*/Q| ≲ 20 MeV^(-1/2)
- The assault frequency prefactor ν₀ ≈ 1.4714 × 10²² s⁻¹
- The half-life reduction (enhancement) occurs in a temperature window between about 1 GK and 10 GK
- The nuclear radius temperature dependence: R(T) = R₀(1 + 0.0005 T²) where R₀ = 1.07A^(1/3) fm
- The surface diffuseness temperature dependence: ã(T) = ã₀(1 + 0.01 T²) where ã₀ = 0.54 fm
- The excitation energy-temperature relation: E_d*(T) = (A/9)T²

## Context
This work extends the universal decay law (UDL) formalism of Qi et al. (2009) for ground-state cluster decays to include excitation energy effects, building on the Geiger-Nuttall law (1911) and Viola-Seaborg systematics (1966). The temperature-dependent densities follow the approach of Antonov (1989) and Gupta, Singh, and Greiner. The results suggest that cluster decay channels, typically neglected in r-process nucleosynthesis calculations, may become important at temperatures around 1-10 GK, potentially requiring modification of existing nucleosynthesis codes that rely on laboratory-measured half-lives.
