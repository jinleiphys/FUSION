# On the maximum compactness of neutron stars

> The stellar compactness, that is, the dimensionless ratio between the mass and radius of a compact star, $\mathcal{C} := M/R$, plays a fundamental role in characterising the gravitational and nuclear-physics aspects of neutron stars. Yet, because the compactness depends sensitively on the unknown equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter, the simple question: ``how compact can a neutron star be?'' remains unanswered. To address this question, we adopt a statistical approach and consider a large number of parameterised EOSs that satisfy all known constraints from nuclear theory, perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), and astrophysical observations. Next, we conjecture that, for any given EOS, the maximum compactness is attained by the star with the maximum mass of the sequence of nonrotating configurations. While we can prove this conjecture for a rather large class of solutions, its general proof is still lacking. However, the evidence from all of the EOSs considered strongly indicates that it is true in general. Exploiting the conjecture, we can concentrate on the compactness of the maximum-mass stars and show that an upper limit appears for the maximum compactness and is given by $\mathcal{C}_{\rm max} = 1/3$. Importantly, this upper limit is essentially independent of the stellar mass and a direct consequence of perturbative-QCD constraints.

## Key claim

The authors conjecture that for any given equation of state, the neutron star with the maximum mass is also the one with the maximum compactness; while a general mathematical proof is lacking, numerical evidence from all considered equations of state supports this claim, and the resulting upper limit on maximum compactness is C_max = 1/3, which is essentially independent of stellar mass and a direct consequence of perturbative QCD constraints.

## Method

The authors generated approximately 3 × 10^5 parameterized equations of state by randomly sampling the speed of sound as a function of chemical potential using piecewise-linear segments, imposing constraints from nuclear theory at low densities (Baym-Pethick-Sutherland crust, polytropes from Hebeler et al. (2013) up to n/n_s = 1.1) and perturbative QCD at high densities (baryon chemical potential μ = 2.6 GeV). They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to construct approximately 2 × 10^8 nonrotating stellar equilibrium models, which were filtered by astrophysical constraints including minimum maximum mass (M_TOV > 2.18 M_sun from pulsar mass measurements), NICER radius constraints (R > 10.75 km at M = 2.0 M_sun, R > 10.8 km at M = 1.1 M_sun), and binary tidal deformability from GW170817 (Λ̃ < 720), yielding approximately 1.6 × 10^7 stellar models for statistical analysis.

## Key numbers

- Maximum compactness found in the ensemble: C_max ≈ 0.3329, which is less than 1/3
- The upper limit C_max = 1/3 is essentially independent of stellar mass for maximum masses ranging from M_TOV ∼ 2.2 to M_TOV ∼ 3.0 M_sun
- Minimum compactness in the ensemble: C_min ≈ 0.2294
- The logarithmic derivative d ln R / d ln M is less than or approximately 0.18 across all relevant masses, supporting the conjecture that the most massive star is also the most compact
- The lower bound on maximum-mass compactness follows the quadratic relation C_TOV,min = 0.100 + 0.031 M_bar_TOV + 0.012 M_bar_TOV^2, where M_bar_TOV = M_TOV / M_sun
- The maximum mass lower bound imposed is M_TOV > 2.18 M_sun, based on pulsar measurements including J0348+0432 (M = 2.01 ± 0.04 M_sun), J0740+6620 (M = 2.08 ± 0.07 M_sun), and PSR J0952-0607 (M = 2.35 ± 0.17 M_sun)
- Radius constraints imposed: R < 10.75 km at M = 2.0 M_sun and R < 10.8 km at M = 1.1 M_sun from NICER observations
- Binary tidal deformability constraint: Λ̃ > 720 at chirp mass M_chirp = 1.186 M_sun for mass ratios q > 0.73 from GW170817

## Context

This work extends previous studies on neutron star compactness limits, including the Buchdahl bound (C ≤ 4/9) and the causality bound of C = 0.3543 from Haensel and Zdunik (1989) and Koranda et al. (1997). The authors use the statistical approach of Altiparmak et al. (2022) for generating parameterized equations of state and improve upon the quasi-universal relation for maximum-mass compactness from Most et al. (2020). The results enable future observational tests: the observation of a neutron star with compactness exceeding 1/3 would challenge the perturbative QCD constraints and the assumptions underlying this bound.
