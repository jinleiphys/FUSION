# A study of shear viscosity coefficient of quark matter in Polyakov quark meson model for three flavors

> We have computed shear viscosity coefficient for hot and dense QCD matter within Polyakov loop extended quark meson(PQM) model for three flavors. Viscosity coefficient is estimated using Boltzmann equation within the relaxation time approximation. Relaxation times for quarks are calculated from elastic scattering of quarks and antiquarks via gluon exchange. Also the mean free paths of strange and light quarks are estimated for this cases. The calculations are performed at finite chemical potential. Thermodynamic properties like energy, pressure, entropy densities are also computed and compared with lattice QCD data.

## Key claim
The shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio (η/s) for hot and dense QCD matter is computed within the Polyakov loop extended quark meson (PQM) model for three flavors, but the calculation is limited to quark scatterings only and does not include mesonic contributions, so the true minimum near the critical temperature is not obtained.

## Method
The shear viscosity coefficient is calculated using the Boltzmann equation within the relaxation time approximation. Medium-dependent quark masses for up, down, and strange quarks are obtained from the PQM model in the mean-field approximation by minimizing the thermodynamic potential. Relaxation times are computed from elastic scattering of quarks and antiquarks via gluon exchange in perturbative QCD, using an effective gluon mass as an infrared regulator. The calculation is performed at finite chemical potential, and thermodynamic quantities (energy, pressure, entropy densities) are also computed and compared with lattice QCD data.

## Key numbers
- The effective gluon mass used as a regulator is given by \( m_g^2 = 2\pi\alpha_s (1 + \frac{1}{6}N_f)T^2 \).
- The specific shear viscosity η/s is plotted for chemical potentials μ = 0 MeV and μ = 100 MeV; at finite chemical potential, η/s is lower than at vanishing μ.
- The value of η/s remains well above the Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) bound of 1/4π.
- Thermodynamic quantities (s/T³, P/T⁴, ε/T⁴) are compared with lattice QCD results from Borsanyi (2010) for temporal extent N_t = 10.

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of transport coefficients within two-flavor PQM models by Abhishek (2017) and Singha (2017) to the three-flavor case. It also builds on earlier effective model calculations of shear viscosity using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Marty 2013, Deb 2016) and quasiparticle models (Sasaki 2008, Bluhm 2010, Soloveva 2019). The results are relevant for hydrodynamic simulations of quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, LHC, and future facilities such as FAIR and NICA. The author notes that future work should include quark-meson scatterings to obtain improved viscosity coefficients.
