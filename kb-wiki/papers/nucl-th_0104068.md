# Asymmetries in phi photoproduction and the OZI violation

> We study the vector meson density matrix and the associated polarization asymmetries in $ϕ$ photoproduction near threshold. In order to study the OZI violating processes, we consider the direct $ϕNN$ coupling as well as the knockout processes arising from the non-vanishing $s\bar{s}$ sea quarks in the nucleon. The polarization asymmetries in $ϕ$ meson decays are found to be very useful to constrain the $ϕNN$ coupling constant. The hidden strangeness of the nucleon can also be studied by these asymmetries, but mostly at large $|t|$ region.

## Key claim

The polarization asymmetries in phi photoproduction are strongly dependent on the phase and magnitude of the phi-NN coupling constant, and can therefore be used to constrain this coupling and test the vector meson dominance hypothesis, but the direct knockout mechanisms probing hidden strangeness can only be distinguished at lower energies near threshold and mostly at large |t|.

## Method

The authors model phi photoproduction using a combination of Pomeron exchange (Donnachie-Landshoff model), pseudoscalar meson (pi, eta) exchange, nucleon pole terms with adjustable phi-NN coupling constants, and direct knockout mechanisms parameterizing the strange sea quark content of the nucleon. They compute the vector meson density matrix elements and the associated photon polarization asymmetry Sigma_phi and parity asymmetry P_sigma in the helicity frame, fitting the phi-NN coupling to reproduce recent CLAS differential cross section data.

## Key numbers

- The phi-NN vector coupling constant required to reproduce CLAS data is |g_phiNN| = 3.0, consistent with the Regge trajectory study of Laget (2000)
- The phi-NN coupling from OZI-consistent kaon loop estimates is g_phiNN = -0.24 (Meissner et al., 1997), while analyses of nucleon form factors and baryon-baryon scattering favor g_phiNN = -2.3 to -4.7
- The Pomeron model parameters used: mu_0^2 = 1.1 GeV^2, beta_0 = 2.05 GeV^{-1}, beta_s = 1.60 GeV^{-1}, s_0 = 4 GeV^2, with trajectory alpha_P(t) = 1.08 + 0.25 t
- Pseudoscalar coupling constants: g_piNN^2/4pi = 14, g_etaNN^2/4pi = 0.99, g_phigammapi = 0.14, g_phigammaeta = 0.704
- Cutoff parameters: Lambda_pi = 0.9 GeV, Lambda_phigammapi = 0.9 GeV, Lambda_eta = 1.0 GeV, Lambda_phigammaeta = 0.9 GeV, Lambda_N = 0.5 GeV
- The hidden strangeness content B^2 of the proton is varied between 0%, 1%, and 2% in the asymmetry calculations at E_gamma = 2.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of Titov, Oh, Yang, and Morii (1997, 1998) on polarization asymmetries in phi photoproduction, and reanalyzes the CLAS data (Anciant et al., 2000) which show increasing differential cross sections at large |t|. The results support a large phi-NN coupling consistent with analyses of electromagnetic nucleon form factors (Genz and Hohler, 1976; Hohler et al., 1976; Jaffe, 1989) and baryon-baryon scattering (Nagels et al., 1975, 1977, 1979), but contradict the OZI-consistent small coupling from kaon loop estimates (Meissner et al., 1997). The polarization asymmetries are shown to differ from predictions including nucleon resonances decaying to phi N in an SU(3) quark model (Zhao, Saghai, and Al-Khalili, 2001), enabling experimental tests at Jefferson Lab and LEPS at SPring-8.
