# Strong decays of $N^{*}(1535)$ in an extended chiral quark model

> The strong decays of the $N^{*}(1535)$ resonance are investigated in an extended chiral quark model by including the low-lying $qqqq\bar{q}$ components in addition to the $qqq$ component. The results show that these five-quark components in $N^{*}(1535)$ contribute significantly to the $N^{*}(1535)\to Nπ$ and $N^{*}(1535)\to Nη$ decays. The contributions to the $Nη$ decay come from both the lowest energy and the next-to-lowest energy five-quarks components, while the contributions to the $Nπ$ decay come from only the latter one. Taking these contributions into account, the description for the strong decays of $N^{*}(1535)$ is improved, especially, for the puzzling large ratio of the decays to $Nη$ and $Nπ$.

## Key claim

The puzzlingly large ratio of the $N\eta$ to $N\pi$ decay widths of the $N^{*}(1535)$ resonance can be explained by including low-lying $qqqq\bar{q}$ (five-quark) components in addition to the conventional $qqq$ (three-quark) component, provided the five-quark admixture in $N^{*}(1535)$ is in the range 45-65%.

## Method

The authors extended the chiral quark model by adding wave functions for low-lying $qqqq\bar{q}$ components in both the nucleon and the $N^{*}(1535)$ resonance, using the same oscillator parameters ($\omega_3=340$ MeV, $\omega_5=600$ MeV) that successfully described the electromagnetic transition $\gamma^{*}N\rightarrow N^{*}(1535)$ in prior work (An and Zou, 2009). They computed the decay widths for $N^{*}(1535)\to N\pi$ and $N^{*}(1535)\to N\eta$ from three types of transitions: diagonal transitions among three-quark components, diagonal transitions among five-quark components, and non-diagonal annihilation transitions between three-quark and five-quark components, using the non-relativistic reduction of the chiral quark Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- For the $N\pi$ decay, the three-momentum of the pion is $k_{\pi}=467$ MeV and the final nucleon energy is $E'=1048$ MeV.
- For the $N\eta$ decay, the three-momentum of the eta meson is $k_{\eta}=188$ MeV and the final nucleon energy is $E'=957$ MeV.
- With only the three-quark component and $g_A^q=0.75$, the calculated $N\pi$ width is 156.9 MeV (experimental range: 52.5-82.5 MeV), the $N\eta$ width is 50.7 MeV (experimental range: 67.5-90.0 MeV), and the ratio $R=\Gamma_{N\eta}/\Gamma_{N\pi}$ is 0.32 (experimental range: 0.82-1.71).
- Including both the lowest and next-to-lowest energy five-quark components with a total five-quark proportion of 55% in $N^{*}(1535)$ yields $\Gamma_{N\pi}=48.4$ MeV, $\Gamma_{N\eta}=47.0$ MeV, and $R=0.97$, all within the experimental ranges.
- With a five-quark proportion of 65%, the ratio $R$ reaches 1.31, also within the experimental range of 0.82-1.71.

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model approach previously applied to the $\Delta(1232)$ and $N^{*}(1440)$ resonances by Li and Riska (2006) to the $N^{*}(1535)$ resonance, which is known for its anomalously large $N\eta$ decay rate despite its mass being near threshold. The study builds on the finding by An and Zou (2009) that five-quark components with 25-65% admixture in $N^{*}(1535)$ can describe the electromagnetic transition $\gamma^{*}N\rightarrow N^{*}(1535)$, and shows that the same model and parameters also describe the strong decays, supporting the interpretation of large $s\bar{s}$ components in this resonance as previously suggested by Xie, Zou, and Chiang (2008) and Liu and Zou (2006).
