# The Four-Boson System with Short-Range Interactions

> We consider the non-relativistic four-boson system with short-range forces and large scattering length in an effective quantum mechanics approach. We construct the effective interaction potential at leading order in the large scattering length and compute the four-body binding energies using the Yakubovsky equations. Cutoff independence of the four-body binding energies does not require the introduction of a four-body force. This suggests that two- and three-body interactions are sufficient to renormalize the four-body system. We apply the equations to 4He atoms and calculate the binding energy of the 4He tetramer. We observe a correlation between the trimer and tetramer binding energies similar to the Tjon line in nuclear physics. Over the range of binding energies relevant to 4He atoms, the correlation is approximately linear.

## Key claim

Cutoff independence of the four-body binding energies does not require the introduction of a four-body force, suggesting that two- and three-body interactions are sufficient to renormalize the four-body system at leading order in the large scattering length, though the authors note this conclusion is based on the specific cutoff range and regulator used.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective interaction potential at leading order in the large scattering length using a momentum cutoff regularization with Gaussian regulator functions, then solved the Yakubovsky equations for the four-boson system to compute binding energies. They applied the equations to ⁴He atoms, using the two-body binding energy and one three-body binding energy as input, and studied the cutoff dependence of the four-body binding energies to determine whether a four-body force is required for renormalization.

## Key numbers

- The scattering length of ⁴He atoms is approximately 100 Å, with effective range r_e ≈ 7 Å.
- The transcendental number s₀ ≈ 1.00624 determines the period of the three-body limit cycle.
- The three-body coupling constant limit cycle has period exp(nπ/s₀) ≈ (22.7)ⁿ for integer n.
- The universal constant c in the three-body coupling limit cycle was determined as c = 0.074 ± 0.003.
- The three-body parameter L₃ = 23.3√B₂ was used in the fit for Λ ≥ 245√B₂.

## Context

This work extends the effective theory approach for short-range interactions from the two- and three-boson systems (Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck, 1998-1999) to the four-boson system, confirming earlier suggestions by Amado and Greenwood that a four-body parameter should not be required at leading order. The observed correlation between trimer and tetramer binding energies is similar to the Tjon line in nuclear physics (Tjon, 1975), and the results enable systematic calculations of four-body observables in atomic systems such as ⁴He clusters and provide a foundation for extending the approach to nuclear four-body problems with spin and isospin.
