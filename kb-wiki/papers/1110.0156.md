To see the effect of symmetry energy and cross-section in more effective way relative yield of $^{197}Au_{79}$ and $^{20}Ne_{10}$ was studied. # Assessment of multifragmentation under the effect of symmetry energy and cross-section

## Key claim

The relative yield of free nucleons and light mass fragments at isospin-dependent cross-section is almost the same for different symmetry energy values, while for fixed cross-section the relative yield is influenced by symmetry energy. The authors compare the stiffness of symmetry energy suggested by different groups with their own value and find that a parameterization with $E^{0}_{sym} \approx 31-32$ MeV and $\gamma \approx 0.55-0.69$ shows close agreement with prior work by Shetty et al.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate central collisions ($\hat{b}=0.3$) of symmetric systems $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}_{10} + ^{20}\mathrm{Ne}_{10}$ and $^{197}\mathrm{Au}_{79} + ^{197}\mathrm{Au}_{79}$ at incident energies from 50 to 1000 MeV/nucleon. They employed a soft equation of state and three forms of symmetry energy characterized by stiffness parameter $\gamma = 0, 0.66, 2$ with $E^{0}_{sym}=32$ MeV at normal density. Fragment production was analyzed using the minimum spanning tree algorithm, and the relative yields of free nucleons (A=1), light mass fragments (2 ≤ A ≤ 4), and intermediate mass fragments (5 ≤ A ≤ $A_{tot}/3$ or $A_{tot}/6$) were compared under isospin-dependent cross-section and fixed cross-sections of 20 mb and 55 mb.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at normal density: $E^{0}_{sym} \approx 31-32$ MeV, with stiffness parameter $\gamma \approx 0.55-0.69$ showing agreement with Shetty et al.
- For Ne+Ne at 50 MeV/nucleon, relative ratio of FN:LMF:IMF is 8:3:1; at 1000 MeV/nucleon the ratio is 7:2:1.
- For Au+Au at low energy (50 MeV/nucleon), relative ratio of FN:LMF:IMF is 9:3:1; at high energy (1000 MeV/nucleon) the ratio is 83:9:1.
- For free nucleons at high energy, the relative yield ratio at $\sigma_{iso}$ and $\sigma_{55}$ is approximately 10, while at $\sigma_{20}$ the value decreases.
- For light mass fragments, the relative ratio at $\sigma_{iso}$ and $\sigma_{55}$ is approximately 5 at low energy and 8 at high energy.

## Context

This work extends studies of multifragmentation using the IQMD model, building on prior work by Puri and co-workers who analyzed fragment production for symmetric Ca+Ca, Xe+Xe, and Au+Au systems. The authors compare their symmetry energy parameterization with results from Zhao, Chen, and Shetty (various years), and validate their calculations against experimental ALADIN data from Schüttauf and Tsang. The study enables further investigation of how the density dependence of symmetry energy and isospin-dependent cross-sections affect fragment isotopic yields in heavy-ion collisions, with implications for understanding nuclear matter at extreme conditions relevant to supernova explosions and neutron stars.
