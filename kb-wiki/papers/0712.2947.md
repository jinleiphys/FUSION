# Stability in the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter formalism: reduced exact-propagator bound-state equation with harmonic interaction

> Several numerical investigations of the Salpeter equation with static confining interactions of Lorentz-scalar type revealed that its solutions are plagued by instabilities of presumably Klein-paradox nature. By proving rigorously that the energies of all predicted bound states are part of real, entirely discrete spectra bounded from below, these instabilities are shown, for confining interactions of harmonic-oscillator shape, to be absent for a reduced version of an instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter formalism designed to generalize the Salpeter equation towards an approximate inclusion of the exact propagators of all bound-state constituents.

## Key claim
The paper rigorously proves that for confining interactions of harmonic-oscillator shape, the "reduced" version of an instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter formalism that approximately includes exact propagators of bound-state constituents yields bound-state energies that are real, entirely discrete, and bounded from below, thereby showing that the instabilities (of Klein-paradox type) observed in numerical studies of the Salpeter equation with static Lorentz-scalar confining interactions are absent in this generalized formalism.

## Method
The authors start from the exact-propagator instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation proposed by Lucha and Schöberl (2005) and truncate it to a reduced bound-state equation. For pseudoscalar states (quantum numbers J^PC = 0^{-+}) and for several Dirac structures of the interaction kernel (Lorentz-scalar, time-component Lorentz-vector, Lorentz-vector, Lorentz-pseudoscalar, and BJK), they reduce the integral equation to radial eigenvalue equations. Assuming a harmonic-oscillator confining potential V(r) = a r^2 in configuration space, these integral equations become second-order ordinary differential equations, which are then transformed into Schrödinger eigenvalue equations for zero eigenvalue. The spectral properties of the resulting Schrödinger operators are analyzed using the theorem that a Schrödinger operator with an infinitely rising potential has a purely discrete spectrum.

## Key numbers
- For light-quark propagators from Dyson-Schwinger equations (Maris 1997), the paper quotes parameter values: a = 0.745 GeV, b = (0.744 GeV)^4, m_0 = 0.0055 GeV, c = 0.545, d = (1.85508 GeV)^2.
- For the Lorentz-vector Dirac structure, the mass function m(p) may approach zero at p → 0 if it behaves like m(p) ∝ p^d with exponent d in (0, 1/2] ∪ {1} ∪ [2, ∞), i.e., d not in (1/2, 1) ∪ (1, 2).
- For the Lorentz-scalar kernel, stability requires the harmonic-oscillator coupling a < 0; for time-component Lorentz-vector, Lorentz-vector, and BJK kernels, stability requires a > 0.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier stability analysis (Lucha et al. 2007) of the reduced Salpeter equation with harmonic-oscillator interactions to the generalized formalism that includes exact propagators (Lucha and Schöberl 2005). The instabilities addressed were observed in numerical studies of the Salpeter equation by Parramore et al. (1995, 1996) and Uzzo (1999). The paper shows that for the BJK Dirac structure (Böhm, Joos, and Krammer 1973; Gross 1991) and for the other structures with appropriate sign of the coupling, all bound-state masses form a real discrete spectrum bounded from below, enabling future numerical studies of this improved formalism without fear of Klein-paradox instabilities.
