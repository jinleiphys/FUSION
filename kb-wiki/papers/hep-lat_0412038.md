# Thermodynamic Properties of Strongly Interacting Matter at Non-zero Baryon Number Density

> We present recent lattice results on QCD thermodynamics at non-vanishing baryon number density obtained from a 6th order Taylor expansion in the chemical potential. Results for bulk thermodynamic observables, in particular for fluctuations in the baryon number density, are found to be well described by a hadron resonance gas model at low temperature and an ideal quark gluon gas at high temperature. We also analyze the radius of convergence of the Taylor series and discuss the information it provides on the occurrence of a second order phase transition point in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim
The Taylor expansion of the QCD partition function to sixth order in the quark chemical potential provides a reliable description of bulk thermodynamic observables for small baryon chemical potential, but the large statistical errors on the sixth-order coefficient prevent a firm conclusion on the existence of a second-order chiral critical point in the QCD phase diagram.

## Method
The pressure of 2-flavor QCD was expanded in a Taylor series around zero baryon chemical potential up to sixth order, with expansion coefficients computed from lattice simulations at quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of about 770 MeV. The ratios of successive expansion coefficients were used to estimate the radius of convergence of the series, and the resulting thermodynamic observables were compared to predictions from a hadron resonance gas model at low temperature and an ideal quark-gluon gas at high temperature.

## Key numbers
- Transition temperature for (2+1)-flavor QCD: T_c = (172 ± 11 ± 7) MeV, consistent with the 2-flavor result T_c = (173 ± 8 ± sys. err.) MeV from simulations with the p4-action.
- At T ≃ 2 T_0, all expansion coefficients c_2, c_4, and c_6 reach their ideal gas values within about 20%.
- The critical baryon chemical potential for the chiral critical point is estimated as μ_B^{crit} ∼ 400 MeV from recent calculations.
- The ratio c_4/c_2 and c_6/c_4 change drastically across T_0, with c_6 positive only below T_0 and changing sign near T_0.
- For T ≲ 0.95 T_0, all expansion coefficients analyzed are positive, consistent with a radius of convergence due to a zero on the real (μ_q/T)-axis.

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD thermodynamics at non-zero baryon density using Taylor expansion methods developed by Allton et al. (2002-2004) and Gavai et al. (2003), and complements reweighting approaches by Fodor and Katz (2002) and simulations with imaginary chemical potential by Philipsen and Lombardo. The good agreement with the hadron resonance gas model, previously applied to heavy-ion collision data by Braun-Munzinger et al., shows that higher-order calculations at smaller quark masses are needed to detect deviations from resonance gas behavior and confirm the existence of a chiral critical point.
