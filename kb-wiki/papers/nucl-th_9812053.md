# Hadronic Vacuum Polarization and the Lamb Shift

> Recent improvements in the determination of the running of the fine-structure constant also allow an update of the hadronic vacuum-polarization contribution to the Lamb shift. We find a shift of -3.40(7) kHz to the 1S level of hydrogen. We also comment on the contribution of this effect to the determination by elastic electron scattering of the r.m.s. radii of nuclei.

## Key claim

The hadronic vacuum-polarization contribution to the 1S Lamb shift in hydrogen is -3.40(7) kHz, updated using a more accurate determination of the running of the fine-structure constant; the abstract does not mention that this result relies on the parameter \(\pi_h^\prime(0) = 9.3055 (\pm 2.2\%) \cdot 10^{-3} \, \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\) derived from \(e^+e^-\) collision data.

## Method

The authors computed the hadronic vacuum-polarization energy shift for S-states of hydrogenic ions using the relation \(E_{\rm VP} = -4(Z\alpha)^4 \pi^\prime(0) \mu^3 / n^3\), where \(\pi^\prime(0)\) is the slope of the polarization function at zero momentum transfer. They obtained \(\pi_h^\prime(0)\) from a dispersion integral over \(e^+e^-\) hadronic cross-section data, using the parameter \(\ell_1\) and error from Figure 7 of a recent compilation (Ref. [1] in the paper). The result was expressed relative to the muon-pair vacuum-polarization contribution, giving \(\delta_h = \pi_h^\prime(0) / \pi_\mu^\prime(0) = 0.671(15)\).

## Key numbers

- Hadronic vacuum-polarization shift for the 1S level of hydrogen: -3.40(7) kHz.
- \(\pi_h^\prime(0) = 9.3055 (\pm 2.2\%) \cdot 10^{-3} \, \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\).
- Ratio \(\delta_h = \pi_h^\prime(0) / \pi_\mu^\prime(0) = 0.671(15)\).
- Muon-pair vacuum-polarization shift for the 1S state of hydrogen: -5.07 kHz.
- Tau-lepton vacuum-polarization shift for the 1S state of hydrogen: -0.02 kHz.
- Hadronic contribution to the effective nuclear radius correction: \(6\pi_h^\prime(0) = 0.0022 \, \mathrm{fm}^2\).

## Context

This work updates the hadronic vacuum-polarization correction to the Lamb shift using improved data on the running of the fine-structure constant, extending earlier estimates that relied primarily on the \(\rho\)-meson contribution (e.g., Ref. [5] and [9] in the paper). The result is consistent with previous values from Ref. [6] (0.68), Ref. [7] (0.719(54)), and Ref. [8] (0.659(26)). The paper also notes that elastic electron scattering determinations of nuclear radii typically omit hadronic vacuum-polarization corrections, which could cause a systematic inconsistency when comparing with radii from optical measurements that include such corrections.
