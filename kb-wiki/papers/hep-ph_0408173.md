# Generalized parton distributions from nucleon form factor data

> We present a simple empirical parameterization of the x- and t-dependence of generalized parton distributions at zero skewness, using forward parton distributions as input. A fit to experimental data for the Dirac, Pauli and axial form factors of the nucleon allows us to discuss quantitatively the interplay between longitudinal and transverse partonic degrees of freedom in the nucleon ("nucleon tomography"). In particular we obtain the transverse distribution of valence quarks at given momentum fraction x. We calculate various moments of the distributions, including the form factors that appear in the handbag approximation to wide-angle Compton scattering. This allows us to estimate the minimal momentum transfer required for reliable predictions in that approach to be around |t|~3 GeV^2. We also evaluate the valence contributions to the energy-momentum form factors entering Ji's sum rule.

## Key claim

The authors present an empirical parameterization of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at zero skewness, fitted to nucleon form factor data, which allows them to extract the transverse impact parameter distribution of valence quarks as a function of longitudinal momentum fraction \(x\); however, the exponential \(t\)-dependence assumed in the ansatz has no rigorous theoretical backing and the results are only reliable for momentum transfers \(-t\) not much larger than \(1/f_q(x)\), where the GPDs become exponentially small.

## Method

The authors parameterize the valence quark GPDs \(H_v^q(x,t)\) and \(\widetilde{H}_v^q(x,t)\) at zero skewness using an exponential form \(H_v^q(x,t) = q_v(x) \exp[t f_q(x)]\), where \(q_v(x)\) are forward parton distributions from CTEQ6M at \(\mu = 2\) GeV, and the profile function \(f_q(x)\) is constrained by Regge behavior at small \(x\) and by power-law behavior at large \(x\). They fit the free parameters of \(f_q(x)\) to experimental data on the Dirac, Pauli, and axial form factors of the nucleon, exploiting sum rules that relate the \(x\)-integrals of GPDs to these form factors, and then compute the average impact parameter \(\langle \mathbf{b}^2 \rangle_q(x) = 4 f_q(x)\) and the transverse distribution of valence quarks via Fourier transform.

## Key numbers

* The fitted Regge slope parameter \(\alpha' = (1.38 \pm 0.01)~\text{GeV}^{-2}\) for the default parameterization at \(\mu = 2\) GeV, which is significantly larger than the value \(\alpha' \approx 0.88\)–\(0.95~\text{GeV}^{-2}\) from meson trajectories.
* The minimal momentum transfer required for reliable predictions in the handbag approximation to wide-angle Compton scattering is estimated to be \(|t| \simeq 3~\text{GeV}^2\).
* The average impact parameter \(\langle \mathbf{b}^2 \rangle_q(x)\) diverges like \(\log(1/x)\) at small \(x\) and vanishes like \((1-x)^n\) at large \(x\), with \(n=1\) or \(n=2\) explored in different fits.
* The fit to the Dirac form factors using the profile \(f_q(x) = \alpha' \log(1/x) + (A_q - \alpha')(1-x)\) yields \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.34\) with three free parameters.

## Context

This work extends earlier modeling of GPDs from light-cone wave functions (Diehl et al., 2003; Brodsky et al., 2001) and from Regge phenomenology (Goeke et al., 2001; Burkardt, 2000, 2002), providing a quantitative extraction of the transverse structure of valence quarks from form factor data. It enables nucleon tomography by mapping the joint density of partons in longitudinal momentum fraction \(x\) and transverse impact parameter \(\mathbf{b}\), and it provides input for predictions of wide-angle Compton scattering in the handbag approximation, which can be tested against upcoming Jefferson Lab data (Bogdan, 2004). The approach is limited to valence quarks at zero skewness and does not constrain sea quark GPDs or the GPD \(\widetilde{E}\).
