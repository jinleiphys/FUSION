# Extended Cluster Model for Light, and Medium Nuclei

> The structures, the electromagnetic transitions, and the beta decay strengths of exotic nuclei are investigated within an extended cluster model. We start by deriving an effective nuclear Hamiltonian within the $S_2$ correlation operator. Tensor forces are introduced in a perturbative expansion which includes up to the second order terms. Within this Hamiltonian we calculate the distributions and the radii of A=3,~4 nuclei. For exotic nuclei characterized by n valence protons/neutrons we excite the structure of the closed shell nuclei via mixed modes formed by considering correlations operators of higher order. Good results have been obtained for the calculated transitions and for the beta decay transition probabilities.

# Extended Cluster Model for Light and Medium Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper claims that an extended, non-perturbative cluster correlation model using higher-order correlation operators (S3 through Sn) can successfully describe structures, electromagnetic transitions, and beta decay strengths of exotic nuclei, but this success is conditional on including long-range three-body correlations which compensate for the absence of genuine three-body interactions used in no-core shell model approaches.

## Method

The authors derive an effective nuclear Hamiltonian using the S2 correlation operator via the e^{iS} method, separating the nucleon-nucleon interaction into short-range and long-range components following the separation method of Moszkowski and Scott (1960). Tensor forces are introduced through a perturbative expansion including second-order terms, and the resulting eigenvalue equations are solved using equation-of-motion methods with generalized linearization approximations and cluster factorization theory. For complex nuclei, higher-order correlation operators (S3 through Sn) are applied to Slater determinants, with amplitudes calculated via the equation-of-motion method including up to ((n+1)p1h) effective diagrams.

## Context

This work extends the Dynamic Correlation Model and Boson Dynamic Correlation Model previously developed by Tomaselli and collaborators (2004, 2006) by incorporating higher-order correlation operators beyond the two-body level. The approach builds on the unitary-model operator method introduced by Villars (1963) and implemented by Shakin (1966) for G-matrix calculations from hard-core interactions. The separation method shows strong analogy with the v_{low-k} approach of Kuo (2002), and the calculated two-body matrix elements are nearly independent of the radial shape of different potentials. The model enables calculations for exotic nuclei including ⁶He, ¹¹Be, ¹⁴C, ¹⁵O, and ¹⁷O, with applications to charge distributions, electromagnetic transitions, and beta decay strengths from ¹⁴N to excited states of ¹⁴C.
