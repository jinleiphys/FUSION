# Critical stability of three-body relativistic bound states with zero-range interaction

> For zero-range interaction providing a given mass M_2 of the two-body bound state, the mass M_3 of the relativistic three-body bound state is calculated. We have found that the three-body system exists only when M_2 is greater than a critical value M_c (approx. 1.43m for bosons and approx. 1.35m for fermions, m is the constituent mass). For M_2=M_c the mass M_3 turns into zero and for M_2<M_c there is no solution with real value of M_3.

## Key claim

The three-body relativistic bound state exists only when the two-body bound state mass \(M_2\) exceeds a critical value \(M_c\) (approximately \(1.43m\) for bosons and \(1.35m\) for fermions, where \(m\) is the constituent mass); for \(M_2 < M_c\) there is no solution with a real value of the three-body mass \(M_3\), which the authors interpret as a relativistic counterpart of the Thomas collapse.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body bound state problem for three equal-mass bosons and three equal-mass fermions using the explicitly covariant formulation of Light-Front Dynamics, with a zero-range two-body interaction kernel replaced by a constant in momentum space. For bosons, the Faddeev equation for the vertex function was reduced to a single integral equation in the variables \((\vec{R}_{\perp}, x)\) without imposing any cutoff on the integration domain. For fermions, a simplified factorized kernel was used, and the equation for the Faddeev component was solved by decomposing the spin-dependent amplitude into scalar functions.

## Key numbers

- For three bosons, the critical two-body mass is \(M_c \approx 1.43\,m\) (where \(m\) is the constituent mass, set to 1 in calculations).
- For three fermions, the critical two-body mass is \(M_c \approx 1.35\,m\).
- In the two-body zero binding limit, the three-boson binding energy is \(B_3 \approx 0.012\,m\).
- When \(M_2 \to 0\), the three-body mass squared \(M_3^2\) tends to approximately \(-11.6\) (in units of \(m^2\)).
- For bosons, the three-body bound state exists for \(M_2\) in the range \(M_c \approx 1.43\,m \leq M_2 \leq 2\,m\); for fermions, the range is \(M_c \approx 1.35\,m \leq M_2 \leq 1.9\,m\).

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic three-body calculations with zero-range interaction by Noyes (year not given) and Tobias (year not given), which had implicitly introduced a cutoff and found finite binding energies, but left unclear whether the finiteness was due to relativistic repulsion or the cutoff. By removing the cutoff, the authors show that the Thomas collapse (unbounded nonrelativistic binding energy as the interaction radius tends to zero) is replaced by a relativistic collapse where the three-body mass vanishes at a critical two-body mass. The results for bosons were reproduced in a later paper by Bicudo, Marques, Fiolhais, and Wagenbrunn (year not given). This work enables further study of the limits of zero-range models in relativistic few-body physics and raises open questions about the dependence of the fermion critical value on the specific spin coupling used in the two-body kernel.
