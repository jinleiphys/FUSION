# Nucleon-$Δ$ elastic cross section in isospin-asymmetric nuclear medium with inclusion of scalar-isovector $δ$ meson field

> The production, dynamic evolution, and decay of $Δ$ particles play a crucial role in understanding the properties of high baryon density nuclear matter in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions. In this work, the energy-, density-, and isospin-dependent nucleon-$Δ$ elastic cross section ($σ^{*}_{N Δ}$) is studied within the relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck framework, in which the $δ$ meson field is further considered. The results show that the $δ$ and $ρ$ meson related exchange terms have a nonnegligible contribution to the $σ^{*}_{N Δ}$ compared to only considering the $ρ$ meson exchange terms, although there is a significant cancellation on the cross section among these meson exchange terms. In addition, due to the different effects of the medium correction on the effective mass of neutrons, protons, and differently charged $Δ$s, the individual $σ^{*}_{N Δ}$ exhibits an ordered isospin-asymmetry ($α$) dependence, and $σ^{*}_{nΔ}$ and $σ^{*}_{pΔ}$ have opposite $α$ dependencies. And the $α$ dependence of the ratio $R(α)=σ^{*}(α)/σ^{*}(α=0)$ for $nΔ$ reaction channels follow $nΔ^{++}>nΔ^{+}>nΔ^{0}>nΔ^{-}$, while for $pΔ$ it is $pΔ^{-}>pΔ^{0}>pΔ^{+}>pΔ^{++}$. Moreover, the results also indicate that the isospin effect on the $σ^{*}_{N Δ}$, which is dominantly caused by the isovector $ρ$ and $δ$ meson fields, is still pronounced at densities up to 3 times normal nuclear density. Finally, a parametrization of the energy-, density-, and isospin-dependent $NΔ$ elastic cross section is proposed based on the microscopic calculated results, and the in-medium $σ^{*}_{N Δ}$ in the energy range of $\sqrt{s}$=2.3$\sim$3.0 GeV can be well described.

## Key claim

The scalar-isovector \(\delta\) meson field, when added to the \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), and \(\rho\) meson fields in the relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) framework, produces a nonnegligible contribution to the in-medium nucleon-\(\Delta\) elastic cross section, although significant cancellation occurs among the \(\delta\)- and \(\rho\)-related exchange terms; the isospin effect on the cross section remains pronounced up to three times normal nuclear density.

## Method

The authors calculated the energy-, density-, and isospin-dependent nucleon-\(\Delta\) elastic cross section within the RBUU transport theory using an effective Lagrangian that includes \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), \(\rho\), and \(\delta\) meson fields with density-dependent coupling constants. The transition probability for \(N\Delta \rightarrow N\Delta\) scattering was derived from the sum of ten meson-exchange terms (including \(\sigma-\sigma\), \(\omega-\omega\), \(\rho-\rho\), \(\delta-\delta\), and their cross terms), and the cross section was obtained by averaging over isospin and spin matrices with phenomenological form factors. The effective masses of nucleons and \(\Delta\) particles were split by the \(\delta\) field in isospin-asymmetric matter, leading to ordered differences among individual charged channels.

## Key numbers

* The in-medium nucleon-\(\Delta\) elastic cross section was calculated for center-of-mass energies \(\sqrt{s} = 2.3\) to \(3.0\) GeV, with a parametrization proposed for this energy range.
* At \(\sqrt{s} = 2.5\) GeV and symmetric matter (\(\alpha = 0\)), the total cross section decreases with increasing reduced density \(u = \rho_b/\rho_0\) for \(u = 0.5, 1, 2, 3\).
* The ratio \(R(\alpha) = \sigma^*(\alpha)/\sigma^*(\alpha=0)\) for \(n\Delta\) channels at \(u = 1\) and \(\sqrt{s} = 2.5\) GeV follows the ordering \(n\Delta^{++} > n\Delta^{+} > n\Delta^{0} > n\Delta^{-}\).
* The ratio \(R(\alpha)\) for \(p\Delta\) channels at \(u = 1\) and \(\sqrt{s} = 2.5\) GeV follows the ordering \(p\Delta^{-} > p\Delta^{0} > p\Delta^{+} > p\Delta^{++}\).
* The isospin effect on the cross section is still significant at densities up to \(u = 3\) (three times normal nuclear density).

## Context

This work extends prior RBUU calculations of in-medium \(N\Delta\) elastic cross sections that included only \(\sigma\) and \(\omega\) meson exchanges (Mao 1996) and later added the \(\rho\) meson (Nan 2023). It also builds on earlier studies of \(NN \rightarrow N\Delta\) and \(N\pi \rightarrow \Delta\) cross sections within the same framework (Li 2016, Li 2017). The inclusion of the \(\delta\) meson field is motivated by its role in describing strongly isospin-asymmetric matter in neutron stars (Kubis 1997, Dutra 2014) and its effect on the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The parametrization of the cross section enables more self-consistent treatment of \(N\Delta\) elastic channels in transport simulations of heavy-ion collisions, which is needed to improve the description of charged-pion yields measured by the HADES and STAR collaborations.
