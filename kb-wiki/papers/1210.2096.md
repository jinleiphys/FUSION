# Progress in Euclidean relativistic few-body quantum mechanics

> We discuss recent progress in the Euclidean formulation of relativistic few-body quantum mechanics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Euclidean relativistic few-body quantum mechanics can be formulated using truncated Euclidean Green functions that satisfy reflection positivity, but notes that reflection positivity is not stable under small Euclidean invariant perturbations, requiring special integral representations to ensure positivity.

## Method

The author constructs a model Hilbert space using collections of functions of Euclidean space-time variables with support for positive relative Euclidean times, and defines the quantum mechanical inner product in terms of Euclidean invariant Green functions. The dynamics is formulated through truncated Euclidean Green functions rather than a Hamiltonian with few-body interactions, and scattering theory is treated using a two-Hilbert space formulation with the Cook condition to test existence of wave operators. The S-matrix is computed using the invariance principle, replacing the Hamiltonian with a function of the transfer matrix that can be calculated using quadratures.

## Key numbers

- The transfer matrix element ⟨f|T(0,nβ)|g⟩ = ⟨f|e^{-nβH}|g⟩ can be calculated using only quadratures
- Test calculations demonstrate that the method can accurately calculate GeV-scale scattering cross sections

## Context

This work extends the Osterwalder-Schrader reconstruction theorem from local field theory to few-body quantum mechanics, where only a finite number of Green functions are retained rather than the infinite hierarchy of field theory. The approach addresses the challenge that reflection positivity is not stable under small perturbations, as noted in prior work (reference 4 in the paper), and builds on Widder's (reference 5) demonstration that the most general solution to a positivity condition has a structure similar to the Källén-Lehmann representation. The formalism enables construction of relativistic few-body models that satisfy cluster separability along with a spectral condition, and provides a practical method for computing scattering observables.
