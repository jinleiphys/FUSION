# Vector Meson Dominance and Rho-Omega Mixing

> The scale of a phenomenologically successful charge-symmetry-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction, that attributed to meson exchange with a $ΔI =1$ rho-omega transition, is set by the Coleman-Glashow SU(2) breaking tadpole mechanism. A single tadpole scale has been obtained from symmetry arguments, electromagnetic meson and baryon measured mass splittings, and the observed isospin violating ($ΔI=1$) decay $ω\to π^+π^-$. The hadronic realization of this tadpole mechanism lies in the $I = 1$ $a_0$ scalar meson. We show that measured hadronic and two-photon widths of the $a_0$ meson, with the aid of the Vector Meson Dominance model, recover the universal Coleman-Glashow tadpole scale.

# Vector Meson Dominance and Rho-Omega Mixing

## Key claim
The Coleman-Glashow tadpole scale of approximately -5200 MeV², which underlies the charge-symmetry-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction via rho-omega mixing, is recovered from measured hadronic and two-photon widths of the a₀(980) scalar meson using the Vector Meson Dominance model, provided the a₀ is assigned a conventional quark-antiquark structure rather than a tetraquark state.

## Method
The authors extract the Coleman-Glashow tadpole scale from diagonal meson mass splittings (pseudoscalar π and K, vector K* and ρ) using SU(6) symmetry relations, and compare it to the off-diagonal ρ⁰-ω transition obtained from the ω→π⁺π⁻ decay data. They then apply Vector Meson Dominance to relate the measured ratio Γ(a₀→γγ)/Γ(a₀→πη) to the ratio of tadpole matrix elements ⟨ρ⁰|H³_tad|ω⟩ and ⟨π⁰|H³_tad|η_NS⟩, using the a₀ meson as the hadronic realization of the tadpole mechanism.

## Key numbers
- The effective electromagnetic ρ⁰-ω transition from ω→π⁺π⁻ decay: ⟨ρ⁰|H_em|ω⟩ ≈ -4520 MeV²
- The photonic (photon exchange) contribution to this transition via VMD: ⟨ρ⁰|H_JJ|ω⟩ ≈ +644 MeV²
- The Coleman-Glashow tadpole contribution to ρ⁰-ω mixing: ⟨ρ⁰|H³_tad|ω⟩ ≈ -5164 MeV² (from data plus VMD)
- The SU(3) prediction for the same tadpole from vector meson mass splittings: ⟨ρ⁰|H³_tad|ω⟩ = Δm²_K* - Δm²_ρ ≈ -5120 MeV² (using 1996 PDG values: m_K*+ ≈ 891.6 MeV, m_K*0 ≈ 896.1 MeV, m_ρ+ ≈ 766.9 MeV, m_ρ0 ≈ 768.8 MeV)
- The SU(6) prediction from pseudoscalar meson data: ⟨ρ⁰|H³_tad|ω⟩ = Δm²_K - Δm²_π ≈ -5220 MeV² (Δm²_K ≈ -3960 MeV², Δm²_π ≈ -1260 MeV²)
- The diagonal pseudoscalar tadpole scale: (H³_tad)_ΔK = Δm²_K - Δm²_π ≈ -5220 MeV²
- The diagonal vector meson tadpole scale: (H³_tad)_ΔK* = Δm²_K* - Δm²_ρ ≈ -5120 MeV²
- Measured a₀(980) two-photon width: Γ(a₀→γγ) = 0.24 ± 0.08 keV
- Measured a₀(980) hadronic width: Γ(a₀→πη) ≈ 75 MeV (midpoint of PDG range 50-100 MeV)
- Ratio r = Γ(a₀→γγ)/Γ(a₀→πη) ≈ 3.2 × 10⁻⁶
- VMD-predicted amplitude ratio from a₀ data: F̃(a₀ρ⁰ω)/F(a₀π⁰η_NS) ≈ 1.0
- VMD couplings from e⁺e⁻ decay rates: g_ρ ≈ 5.03, g_ω ≈ 17.05 (with g_ρ/e ≈ 16.6, g_ω/e ≈ 56.3)
- VMD prediction for ω→π⁰γ: F(ωπ⁰γ) ≈ 0.704 GeV⁻¹, in agreement with measured value
- VMD prediction for ρ→π⁰γ: F(ρπ⁰γ) ≈ 0.208 GeV⁻¹, compared to measured 0.222 GeV⁻¹

## Context
This work extends the Coleman-Glashow tadpole mechanism (Coleman and Glashow, 1964) for SU(2) breaking to connect the charge-symmetry-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction (based on ρ⁰-ω mixing from the work of Coon, Scadron, and collaborators in the 1970s) with the measured decays of the a₀(980) scalar meson. The authors argue against recent proposals for a direct ω→2π contact term (Kim, 1998; Maltman, O'Connell, and Williams, 1996; Shakin and Wang, 1997; Tandy and collaborators, 1997), showing that such a term would raise the ρ⁰-ω mixing parameter to approximately -6830 MeV², inconsistent with the universal tadpole scale of approximately -5200 MeV² obtained from meson mass splittings, baryon mass differences, and the a₀ decay data. The analysis enables a consistent picture of charge symmetry violation in nuclear physics, including the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly and isospin-forbidden beta decays.
