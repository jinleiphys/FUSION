# Constraints on spin observables in pbar p → Lambdabar Lambda

## Key claim

The paper derives model-independent inequalities that constrain the allowed values of spin observables in the reaction pbar p → Lambdabar Lambda, showing that many pairs and triplets of observables are restricted to smaller domains than the naive square [-1, +1] × [-1, +1], based solely on the positivity of the generalized density matrix.

## Method

The authors apply a generalized density matrix formalism to the six independent amplitudes of the strangeness-exchange reaction pbar p → Lambdabar Lambda, enforcing positivity conditions that yield inequalities involving sub-determinants. They compare the predicted allowed domains for pairs and triplets of spin observables against actual data from the PS185 collaboration at CERN, using randomly generated unphysical amplitudes as a contrast.

## Key numbers

- For individual observables, the normalization is such that -1 ≤ O_i ≤ +1, which is checked against published and preliminary PS185 data.
- For many pairs of observables, the allowed domain is restricted to a unit disk O_i² + O_j² ≤ 1, as shown for polarisation and C_ll.
- For other pairs, the allowed domain is the inner part of a triangle O_i² − (1 + O_j)² ≤ 0, as shown for C_nn and C_lm.
- For triplets of observables, allowed volumes include the inner part of the unit sphere, the inner part of a cone, and the volume inside a symmetric cubic (a "twisted cushion") where no pair restriction applies.

## Context

This work extends the earlier systematic study by Richard and Elchikh (2003) on inequalities relating pairs of observables in pbar p → Lambdabar Lambda, using a more powerful generalized density matrix method. The reaction has been studied experimentally by the PS185 collaboration, with first data reproduced by both K, K* exchange models and quark models (Alberg, 1996). The framework developed here can also be applied to transverse-momentum dependent helicity and transversity quark distributions in nucleons, recovering existing inequalities from the structure function literature.
