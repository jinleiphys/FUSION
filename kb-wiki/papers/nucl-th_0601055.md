# Continuum coupling and spectroscopic properties of nuclei

> The nucleus is a correlated open quantum many-body system. The presence of states that are unbound to particle emission may have significant impact on spectroscopic properties of nuclei, especially those close to the particle drip lines. In the framework of the continuum shell model in the complex momentum-plane, we discuss salient effects of the continuum coupling on the spectroscopic factors, one-neutron overlap integrals, and energies of excited states in $^{6}$He and $^{18}$O. We demonstrate the presence of non-perturbative threshold effects in many-body wave functions of weakly-bound and unbound states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that coupling to the non-resonant scattering continuum produces non-perturbative threshold effects in spectroscopic factors and energy splittings of weakly bound and unbound nuclei, effects that are absent in traditional closed quantum system calculations. The authors show that spectroscopic factors exhibit a characteristic cusp at the one-neutron emission threshold, with the behavior depending on the orbital angular momentum of the single-particle state.

## Method

The authors performed Gamow Shell Model (GSM) calculations in the complex momentum plane using a Berggren basis of Gamow resonant states and non-resonant scattering continuum states. They computed spectroscopic factors and one-neutron overlap integrals for the ground state of ⁶He and the excited 0⁺₃ state of ¹⁸O, and calculated the spin-orbit splitting in ⁵He and ⁷He, comparing full GSM results with the pole approximation (GSM-p) and harmonic oscillator shell model (HO-SM) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor for ⁶He ground state in channel [⁵He(g.s.) ⊗ p₃/₂]⁰⁺ shows a cusp at the one-neutron emission threshold e₀p₃/₂ = 0, with the real part below threshold behaving as (-eⱼₗ)^(ℓ-1/2) and above threshold as (eⱼₗ)^(ℓ+1/2)
- For ¹⁸O excited 0⁺₃ state in channel [¹⁷O(3/2₁⁺) ⊗ d₃/₂]⁰⁺, the spectroscopic factor derivative exhibits a cusp at the 0d₃/₂ threshold, with Re(S²) below threshold behaving as (-eⱼₗ)^(3/2) and above threshold as (eⱼₗ)^(3/2)
- The Berggren basis contours used k_max = 3.27 fm⁻¹ for ⁶He calculations and k_max = 1.5 fm⁻¹ for ¹⁸O calculations, with discretization of up to 60 points and precision on energies and widths better than 0.1 keV
- The relative difference in squared radii between GSM overlap integrals and single-particle wave functions is around 1% for the d₃/₂ channel in ¹⁸O, and several percent for the p₃/₂ channel in ⁶He

## Context

This work extends the Gamow Shell Model formalism developed by Michel (2002, 2004) and Betan (2002) to study threshold effects in spectroscopic properties of weakly bound nuclei. The results are consistent with Wigner's (1948) early estimate of cross section behavior near threshold energy and relate to the Thomas-Ehrman shift and the helium anomaly observed by Ogloblin (1974). The paper demonstrates that standard shell model calculations using harmonic oscillator bases or pole approximations miss important non-perturbative continuum coupling effects, while the single-particle approximation for one-neutron overlap integrals works surprisingly well.
