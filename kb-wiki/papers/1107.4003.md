# Selected highlights from the study of mesons

> We provide a brief review of recent progress in the study of mesons using QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations. Along the way we touch on aspects of confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking but in the main focus upon: exact results for pseudoscalar mesons, including aspects of the eta-eta' problem; a realisation that the so-called vacuum condensates are actually an intrinsic, localised property of hadrons; an essentially nonperturbative procedure for constructing a symmetry-preserving Bethe-Salpeter kernel, which has enabled a demonstration that dressed-quarks possess momentum-dependent anomalous chromo- and electromagnetic moments that are large at infrared momenta, and resolution of a longstanding problem in understanding the mass-splitting between rho- and a1-mesons such that they are now readily seen to be parity partners in the meson spectrum; features of electromagnetic form factors connected with charged and neutral pions; and computation and explanation of valence-quark distribution functions in pseudoscalar mesons. We argue that in solving QCD, a constructive feedback between theory and extant and forthcoming experiments will enable constraints to be placed on the infrared behaviour of QCD's beta-function, the nonperturbative quantity at the core of hadron physics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) provide a systematically improvable, symmetry-preserving framework for studying mesons, and that comparison between DSE-based computations and experimental data can constrain the infrared behavior of QCD's beta-function, which is the central nonperturbative quantity governing hadron physics.

## Method

The authors review recent progress in applying DSEs and Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSEs) to meson physics, focusing on the gap equation for the dressed-quark propagator and the inhomogeneous BSE for the axial-vector vertex. They describe a nonperturbative truncation scheme that preserves the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity, enabling exact results for pseudoscalar mesons including a mass formula, Goldberger-Treiman-like relations, and the demonstration that vacuum condensates are intrinsic hadron properties rather than vacuum phenomena.

## Key numbers

- The dressed-quark mass function shows that less than 2% of a nucleon's mass comes from current-quark masses, with DCSB responsible for 98% of a proton's mass
- The pion mass formula is exact for flavour non-diagonal pseudoscalar mesons: f_{H_{0^-}} m_{H_{0^-}}^2 = (m_{f_1}^zeta + m_{f_2}^zeta) rho_{H_{0^-}}^zeta
- In the chiral limit, all radially-excited pseudoscalar mesons (n >= 1) have vanishing leptonic decay constant: f_{pi_n} = 0 for all n >= 1
- Lattice-QCD results at pion mass squared ~0.4 GeV^2 show |f_{pi_1}/f_{pi_0}| ~ 0.4 for unimproved fermion actions with poor chiral symmetry

## Context

This review extends earlier DSE work by Roberts (2000), Maris (2003), and Fischer (2006), and complements lattice-QCD studies of the gluon and quark propagators by Bowman (2005) and the pure Yang-Mills sector reviewed by Pepe (2011). The paper enables future constraints on QCD's infrared beta-function through comparison with experimental data from facilities including JLab, FAIR, and J-PARC, and addresses the longstanding problem of understanding the rho-a1 meson mass splitting as parity partners in the meson spectrum.
