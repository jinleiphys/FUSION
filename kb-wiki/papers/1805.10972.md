# New spectrum of negative-parity doubly charmed baryons: Possibility of two quasistable states

> The discovery of $Ξ_{cc}^{++}$ by the LHCb Collaboration triggers predictions of more doubly charmed baryons. By taking into account both the $P$-wave excitations between the two charm quarks and the scattering of light pseudoscalar mesons off the ground state doubly charmed baryons, a set of negative-parity spin-1/2 doubly charmed baryons are predicted already from a unitarized version of leading order chiral perturbation theory. Moreover, employing heavy antiquark-diquark symmetry the relevant low-energy constants in the next-to-leading order are connected with those describing light pseudoscalar mesons scattering off charmed mesons, which have been well determined from lattice calculations and experimental data. Our calculations result in a spectrum richer than that of heavy mesons. We find two very narrow $J^P=1/2^-$ $Ω_{cc}^P$, which very likely decay into $Ω_{cc}π^0$ breaking isospin symmetry. In the isospin-1/2 $Ξ_{cc}^P$ sector, three states are predicted to exist below 4.2~GeV with the lowest one being narrow and the other two rather broad. We suggest to search for the $Ξ_{cc}^{P}$ states in the $Ξ_{cc}^{++}π^-$ mode. Searching for them and their analogues are helpful to establish the hadron spectrum.

# New spectrum of negative-parity doubly charmed baryons: Possibility of two quasistable states

## Key claim
The authors predict that, under the assumption that the coupling constant λ is less than about 0.45 (which is likely given its natural scale of O(Λ_QCD/m_c) ≈ 0.2), there exist two very narrow J^P = 1/2^- Ω_cc^P states whose only strong decay mode is the isospin-breaking Ω_ccπ^0 channel, making them quasistable. This prediction depends on the inclusion of P-wave excitations between the two charm quarks as dynamical degrees of freedom, which had not been done in previous studies.

## Method
The authors construct a unitarized coupled-channel scattering amplitude for S-wave interactions between light pseudoscalar mesons (π, K, η) and ground-state doubly charmed baryons (Ξ_cc, Ω_cc) using chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order. They employ heavy antiquark-diquark symmetry (HADS) to relate the low-energy constants in the doubly charmed baryon sector to those already determined from lattice QCD and experimental data in the charmed meson sector. The P-wave excitations of the cc diquark are included as explicit s-channel exchange poles, with the bare masses taken from quark model predictions.

## Key numbers
- The P-wave diquark excitation energy is estimated as (M_h_c − M_J/ψ)/2 = 214 MeV, leading to a bare mass of M_Ξ_cc^P = 3838 MeV and M_Ω_cc^P ≈ 3942 MeV.
- The ground-state Ξ_cc mass is taken as 3621.4 MeV from LHCb (Aaij 2017), and the Ω_cc mass is fixed at 3725 MeV using the HADS relation M_Ω_cc − M_Ξ_cc = M_D_s − M_D.
- The subtraction constant for the loop function is a^{ψ_ccφ}(1 GeV) = −2.79^{+0.04}_{−0.05}, translated from the charmed meson sector.
- For λ ≲ 0.45, both 1/2^- Ω_cc^P states lie below the Ξ_ccK̅ threshold; the lower state (Ω_cc^{P,L}) has an isospin-breaking width of a few keV, while the higher state (Ω_cc^{P,H}) has a width larger than 30 keV.
- In the (S,I) = (0,1/2) sector, three Ξ_cc^P states are predicted below 4.2 GeV: the lowest has a width less than 40 MeV, while the other two are rather broad.
- The higher Ω_cc^P state (Ω_cc^{P,H}) contains 55%–80% Ξ_ccK̅ molecular component when below threshold.

## Context
This work extends the successful description of positive-parity charmed mesons (D_s0^*(2317), D_0^*(2400), D_1(2430)) as hadronic molecules from unitarized chiral perturbation theory (Liu 2012, Albaladejo 2016, Du 2017) to the doubly charmed baryon sector, following the discovery of Ξ_cc^{++} by LHCb (Aaij 2017). It goes beyond earlier studies of doubly charmed baryons (Guo 2011, Guo 2017, Dias 2018) by including P-wave diquark excitations as explicit degrees of freedom and using NLO chiral potentials. The predicted spectrum is richer than that of heavy mesons and differs from quark model predictions. The results enable experimental searches for these states, particularly in the Ξ_cc^{++}π^- decay mode at LHCb, and suggest analogous patterns for doubly bottom and charm-bottom baryons.
