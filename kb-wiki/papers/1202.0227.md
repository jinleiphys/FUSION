# Gamma-ray production in neutral-current neutrino-oxygen interactions at energies above 200 MeV

> We report the results of a calculation of the neutrino- and antineutrino-induced gamma-ray production cross section for oxygen target. Our analysis is focused on the kinematical region of neutrino energy larger than ~200MeV, in which single-nucleon knockout is known to be the dominant reaction mechanism. The numerical results have been obtained using a realistic model of the target spectral function, extensively tested against electron-nucleus scattering data. We find that at neutrino energy 600 MeV the fraction of neutral-current interactions leading to emission of gamma-rays of energy larger than 6 MeV is ~41%, and that the contribution of the p_{3/2} state is overwhelming.

# Gamma-ray production in neutral-current neutrino-oxygen interactions at energies above 200 MeV

## Key claim

At a neutrino energy of 600 MeV, approximately 41% of neutral-current interactions on oxygen lead to emission of gamma-rays with energy above 6 MeV, with the contribution from the p_{3/2} shell-model state being overwhelming; however, this fraction exhibits significant energy dependence below 1 GeV and the result applies specifically to the quasielastic regime where single-nucleon knockout dominates.

## Method

The authors computed the neutrino- and antineutrino-induced gamma-ray production cross section for oxygen by summing over shell-model states the product of the neutral-current quasielastic cross section (calculated using a realistic spectral function of oxygen obtained via the local density approximation, combining experimental data from Saclay with theoretical calculations of nucleon-nucleon correlations in nuclear matter) and the branching ratios for deexcitation of the residual nuclei by photon emission, taken from Ejiri and Kamyshkov. The spectral function describes the probability of removing a nucleon of given momentum and energy from the target, and the calculation includes contributions from the 1p_{1/2}, 1p_{3/2}, and 1s_{1/2} single-particle states for both protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

- At neutrino energy 600 MeV, the fraction of neutral-current interactions leading to emission of gamma-rays of energy larger than 6 MeV is ~41%
- Spectroscopic strengths for oxygen hole states: p_{1/2} = 0.632, p_{3/2} = 0.703, s_{1/2} = 0.422
- Branching ratios for deexcitation by photon emission with E_gamma > 6 MeV: p_{1/2} = 0%, p_{3/2} = 100%, s_{1/2} = 16 ± 1%
- Removal energies of proton states: 1p_{1/2} = 12.1 MeV, 1p_{3/2} = 18.4 MeV, 1s_{1/2} = ~42 MeV; neutron levels are more deeply bound by 3.54 MeV
- The neutrino-induced gamma-production cross section reaches its maximum at E_nu ~ 1.9 GeV
- The p_{3/2} hole deexcites through photon emission with half-life 0.146 ± 0.008 fs (proton) and less than 1.74 fs (neutron)
- Uncertainties in the gamma-ray production cross section arise from spectroscopic strengths (5.4%), Pauli blocking treatment (1%), and s_{1/2} branching ratio (1%)

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of gamma-ray production from neutral-current neutrino-oxygen interactions by Donnelly, Kolbe, and others, which covered the neutrino energy range 10–500 MeV and focused on inelastic excitation of discrete nuclear states. The present calculation addresses the higher-energy regime (above ~200 MeV) where quasielastic nucleon knockout becomes dominant, using a realistic spectral function extensively tested against electron-nucleus scattering data from Saclay, NIKHEF, and Jefferson Lab. The results provide an accurate estimate of a signal that can be exploited to identify neutral-current events in water-Cherenkov detectors such as Super-Kamiokande, where gamma-rays above the observational threshold of 5 MeV can contribute up to ~5% of the total event number independent of neutrino oscillations, and where ~90% of neutral-current interactions in water take place in oxygen.
