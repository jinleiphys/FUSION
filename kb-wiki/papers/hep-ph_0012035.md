# The color dipole picture of the Drell-Yan process

> At high energies, Drell-Yan (DY) dilepton production viewed in the target rest frame should be interpreted as bremsstrahlung and can be expressed in terms of the same color dipole cross section as DIS. We compute DY cross sections on a nucleon target with the realistic parameterization for the dipole cross section saturated at large separations. The results are compared to experimental data and predictions for RHIC are presented. The transverse momentum distribution of the DY process is calculated and energy growth is expected to be steeper at large than at small transverse momenta. We also calculate the DY angular distribution and investigate deviations from the 1+cos^2(θ) shape.

## Key claim

The Drell-Yan (DY) process at high energies can be described in the target rest frame as bremsstrahlung using the same color dipole cross section as deep-inelastic scattering (DIS), but this approach is only valid at small values of the target momentum fraction \(x_2\) (below 0.1) and does not include annihilation with valence quarks from the target.

## Method

The authors compute DY cross sections on a nucleon target by expressing the process in impact-parameter space using light-cone wavefunctions for the photon-quark fluctuation and the dipole cross section parameterization of Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (2000), which is fitted to HERA DIS data. The hadronic cross section is obtained by convoluting the partonic cross section with the projectile proton's structure function \(F_2^p\) at large \(x_{Bj}\), using the parameterization from Mils (1999). The transverse momentum distribution and angular distribution of the lepton pair are also calculated from the dipole cross section via Fourier integrals.

## Key numbers

* The dipole cross section parameterization uses \(\sigma_0 = 23.03\) mb, \(x_0 = 0.0003\), and \(\lambda = 0.288\), with \(Q_0 = 1\) GeV.
* The DY cross section at RHIC (\(\sqrt{s} = 500\) GeV) is about four times larger than at Fermilab (\(\sqrt{s} = 38.8\) GeV).
* The angular distribution parameter \(\lambda\) deviates from unity by typically about 5% as a function of dilepton mass, and by up to about 10% as a function of transverse momentum \(q_\perp\).
* The calculation uses a quark mass \(m_f = 200\) MeV, which has virtually no influence on the numerical results in proton-proton collisions.

## Context

This work extends the color dipole approach to DY production, previously applied to nuclear effects by Kopeliovich (1995) and Kopeliovich et al. (1995), to proton-proton collisions for the first time. It provides a parameter-free comparison to E772 data (1994) at low \(x_2\) and makes predictions for RHIC. The approach enables calculations of transverse momentum and angular distributions that are not divergent at \(q_\perp = 0\), in contrast to first-order perturbative QCD corrections to the parton model, and predicts violations of the Lam-Tung relation that can be tested at RHIC.
