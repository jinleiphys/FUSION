# Further explorations of Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass formulas. XII: Stiffness and stability of neutron-star matter

> We construct three new Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mass models, labeled HFB-19, HFB-20, and HFB-21, with unconventional Skyrme forces containing $t_4$ and $t_5$ terms, i.e., density-dependent generalizations of the usual $t_1$ and $t_2$ terms, respectively. The new forces underlying these models are fitted respectively to three different realistic equations of state of neutron matter for which the density dependence of the symmetry energy ranges from the very soft to the very stiff, reflecting thereby our present lack of complete knowledge of the high-density behavior of nuclear matter. All unphysical instabilities of nuclear matter, including the transition to a polarized state in neutron-star matter, are eliminated with the new forces. At the same time the new models fit essentially all the available mass data with rms deviations of 0.58 MeV and give the same high quality fits to measured charge radii that we obtained in earlier models with conventional Skyrme forces. Being constrained by neutron matter, these new mass models, which all give similar extrapolations out to the neutron drip line, are highly appropriate for studies of the $r$-process and the outer crust of neutron stars. Moreover, the underlying forces, labeled BSk19, BSk20 and BSk21, respectively, are well adapted to the study of the inner crust and core of neutron stars. The new family of Skyrme forces thus opens the way to a unified description of all regions of neutron stars.

## Key claim

Three new Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass models (HFB-19, HFB-20, HFB-21) with generalized Skyrme forces containing density-dependent \(t_4\) and \(t_5\) terms can simultaneously fit nuclear masses with rms deviations of 0.58 MeV and eliminate unphysical spin-polarized instabilities in neutron-star matter, but only by relaxing the cancellation conditions between the \(t_4\) and \(t_5\) terms that were imposed in the earlier HFB-18 model.

## Method

The authors constructed three Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass models using generalized Skyrme forces that include \(t_4\) and \(t_5\) terms (density-dependent generalizations of the usual \(t_1\) and \(t_2\) terms). Each force was fitted to a different realistic equation of state for neutron matter: BSk19 to the Friedman-Pandharipande (FP) EOS, BSk20 to the Akmal et al. (APR) EOS, and BSk21 to the LS2 EOS. The models were fitted to 2149 measured masses from the 2003 Atomic Mass Evaluation, with constraints on the isoscalar effective mass (0.8M at equilibrium density), symmetry coefficient (30 MeV), and incompressibility (240 \(\pm\) 10 MeV), while the conditions that made the \(t_4\) and \(t_5\) terms cancel in unpolarized homogeneous matter were released to gain flexibility.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation of 0.58 MeV for all three models (HFB-19, HFB-20, HFB-21) when fitting the 2149 measured masses of nuclei with \(N\) and \(Z \ge 8\) from the 2003 Atomic Mass Evaluation
- Symmetry coefficient \(J = 30\) MeV imposed as a constraint on all three forces
- Isoscalar effective mass \(M_s^* = 0.8M\) imposed at the equilibrium density of symmetric nuclear matter
- Incompressibility \(K_v\) constrained to fall in the experimental range 240 \(\pm\) 10 MeV
- For the subset of most neutron-rich measured nuclei (neutron separation energy \(S_n \le 5.0\) MeV), the FRDM gives an rms deviation of 0.910 MeV for comparison

## Context

This work extends the authors' series of HFB mass models (previously HFB-17 and HFB-18) by addressing the lack of flexibility in fitting the neutron-matter equation of state that limited the earlier HFB-18 model. The new forces cover three different realistic neutron-matter EOSs (FP, APR, and LS2) that span from very soft to very stiff density dependence of the symmetry energy, reflecting the present uncertainty in high-density nuclear matter behavior. The models enable a unified description of all regions of neutron stars (outer crust, inner crust, and core) using a single effective force, which was not possible with earlier models due to unphysical spin-polarized ground states. The work also improves the distribution of potential energy among the four spin-isospin channels compared to conventional Skyrme forces, as discussed in the authors' previous publications (Goriely et al. 2009, Chamel et al. 2009).
