# Comparative study of fusion barriers using Skyrme interactions and the energy density functional

> Using different Skyrme interactions, we have carried out a comparative analysis of fusion barriers for a wide range of interacting nuclei in the framework of semiclassical Skyrme energy density formalism. The results of our calculations reveal that SVI, SII, and SIII Skyrme forces are able to reproduce the empirical values of barrier heights with higher accuracy than the other considered forces in this formalism. It is also shown that the calculated nucleus-nucleus potentials derived from such Skyrme interactions are able to explain the fusion cross sections at energies near and above the barrier.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the SVI, SII, and SIII Skyrme interactions, when used with Fermi density distributions whose parameters are fitted to experimental data, reproduce empirical fusion barrier heights with higher accuracy than other Skyrme forces tested; however, this conclusion is conditional on using the specific semiclassical energy density formalism and the particular Fermi density parameterizations labeled case (a) and case (b).

## Method

The authors computed the total ion-ion interaction potential by combining the nuclear part from the Skyrme energy density functional (using the semiclassical extended Thomas-Fermi approximation to second order) with the Coulomb potential from a double-folding integral of the proton densities. They used two-parameter Fermi density distributions for the target and projectile nuclei, with half-density radii and surface thickness parameters taken from two polynomial fits (case (a) for A=4-209 and case (b) extended to A=238), and tested 18 different Skyrme force parametrizations (SI, SII, SIII, SIV, SV, SVI, SGI, SGII, SkM*, SkT1, SkT5, SKT9, SkP, SLy4, SLy7, BSK2, BSK14). Barrier heights and positions were extracted from the total potential by finding the maximum, and the accuracy was quantified by a relative error formula comparing calculated barrier heights to empirical values for a large set of fusion reactions.

## Key numbers

* The SVI Skyrme interaction gave a relative error of chi = 1.5079% for barrier height calculations across the studied systems.
* The SII and SIII Skyrme interactions gave relative errors of chi = 1.5144% and chi = 1.5147%, respectively, for barrier height calculations.
* Using the SVI, SII, and SIII forces, barrier heights were predicted with an accuracy of about +/-5% and barrier positions with an accuracy of about +/-10%.
* The Fermi density parameters for case (a) were given by polynomials in mass number A for half-density radius R0i and surface thickness ai, with coefficients such as R0i = 0.90106 + 0.10957Ai - 0.0013Ai^2 + 7.71458e-6 Ai^3 - 1.62164e-8 Ai^4.

## Context

This work extends prior studies that used self-consistent Hartree-Fock or Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov densities with Skyrme forces in the energy density formalism (Author (2015) and Author (2015) are cited in the text as Refs. [15,22,23]), by instead using Fermi density distributions with parameters fitted to experimental data as proposed by Author (2015) (Ref. [21]) and extended by Author (2015) (Ref. [24]). The results enable a systematic selection of Skyrme parametrizations for fusion barrier calculations and show that the SVI, SII, and SIII forces, combined with these simple density profiles, can reproduce fusion cross sections near and above the barrier, providing a computationally efficient alternative to fully microscopic density calculations.
