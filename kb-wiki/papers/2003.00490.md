# Neutron-rich matter in atomic nuclei and neutron stars

> The proper understanding of the equation of state (EoS) of highly asymmetric nuclear matter is essential when studying systems such as neutron stars (NSs). Using zero-range Skyrme interactions and finite-range interactions such as Gogny forces, momentum-dependent interactions (MDI) and simple effective interactions (SEI), we analyze the properties of the EoS and the influence they may have on the calculations for NSs. We study the convergence properties of the Taylor series expansion of the EoS in powers of the isospin asymmetry and the accuracy of the results for $β$-stable nuclear matter when it is computed using the Taylor expansion of the EoS. The NS mass-radius relation for Skyrme and finite-range interactions is also analyzed. As none of the existing parametrizations of the standard Gogny D1 interaction are able to provide an NS inside the observational constraints, we propose a new parametrization, which we name D1M$^*$, that is able to provide NSs of $2 M_\odot$ while still providing the same good description of finite nuclei as D1M. A parametrization D1M$^{**}$ is also presented, which is fitted similarly as D1M$^*$ and provides NSs up to $1.91 M_\odot$. An accurate determination of the core-crust transition point is necessary for the modeling of NSs for astrophysical purposes. We estimate the core-crust transition in NSs by finding where the nuclear matter in the core is unstable against fluctuations of the density. To do that, we employ two methods, the thermodynamical method and the dynamical method for zero-range and finite-range interactions. To apply the dynamical method to finite-range forces, we have explicitly derived the expression of the energy curvature matrix in momentum space. Finally, the NS crustal properties, moment of inertia and tidal deformability are thoroughly analyzed using Skyrme and finite-range interactions.

## Key claim

The paper systematically evaluates the convergence of the Taylor series expansion of the equation of state (EoS) in powers of isospin asymmetry for neutron-rich matter, finding that the expansion converges slowly for interactions with large values of the slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy, and that using the full EoS expression is necessary for accurate neutron star (NS) calculations.

## Method

The author employed non-relativistic mean-field models using zero-range Skyrme interactions and finite-range interactions (Gogny forces, momentum-dependent interactions (MDI), and simple effective interactions (SEI)) to compute the EoS of asymmetric nuclear matter. The core-crust transition in NSs was determined using both the thermodynamical method (mechanical and chemical stability conditions) and the dynamical method (including bulk, surface, and Coulomb effects), with the latter requiring a derivation of the energy curvature matrix in momentum space for finite-range forces. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations were integrated to obtain the mass-radius relation, and NS crustal properties (mass, thickness, moment of inertia, tidal deformability) were analyzed.

## Key numbers

- The new Gogny parametrization D1M\(^*\) provides NSs of \(2 M_\odot\) while maintaining the same good description of finite nuclei as D1M.
- The parametrization D1M\(^{**}\) provides NSs up to \(1.91 M_\odot\).
- The outer crust extends from densities around \(7.5\) g cm\(^{-3}\) to approximately \(4.3 \times 10^{11}\) g cm\(^{-3}\), where neutron drip occurs.
- The core reaches densities of \(2-3\) times nuclear saturation density (\(\sim 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\)).
- NS radii are in the range of \(10-16\) km, with masses of \(1-2 M_\odot\).
- The tidal deformability results are compared with constraints from the GW170817 event detected by LIGO and Virgo.
- The moment of inertia results are compared with constraints from the double pulsar system PSR J0737-3039 (Landry, 2018).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the EoS of asymmetric nuclear matter using Skyrme and Gogny interactions, particularly addressing the inability of standard Gogny D1 parametrizations to produce NSs meeting observational constraints (Sellahewa, 2014). The new D1M\(^*\) and D1M\(^{**}\) parametrizations enable simultaneous description of finite nuclei and NSs. The analysis of core-crust transition properties using both thermodynamical and dynamical methods, including the derivation for finite-range interactions, improves upon prior work (Xu, 2010) and provides a foundation for more accurate modeling of NS crustal properties such as glitches and r-mode oscillations.
