# Neutron stars with large quark cores

> We describe charge-neutral neutron star matter in $β-$equilibrium using hybrid equations of state, where a first-order phase transition from hadronic to quark matter is realized. The hadronic matter is described in a model-independent way by a Taylor expansion around saturation density $n_0$, while the three-flavor NJL model is used for the quark matter. Exploring the present uncertainty on the empirical parameters of nuclear matter and the parameter space of the NJL model, we construct two datasets of thermodynamically consistent and causal hybrid EoSs, compatible with astrophysical observations. We conclude that, to sustain a considerable quark core size, the intensity of the phase transition from hadron to quark matter cannot be strong, having a energy density gap below $200$ MeV/fm$^3$, and must occur at baryon densities not above four times the saturation density. A non zero but not too strong quark vector-isoscalar term and a weak vector isovector quark term are required. Large quark cores carrying almost half of the star mass are possible inside neutron stars with a maximum mass $\approx 2.2 M_\odot$. To get a considerable number of hybrid EoS predicting quark matter already inside neutron stars with a mass $\sim 1.4 M_\odot$, we require that the onset of quarks occurs in the range $1.3n_0$ and $2.5n_0$. Neutron stars with large quark cores corresponding to more than one fourth of the total star mass, are possible if the energy density gap and the pressure at transition are below 100 MeV/fm$^3$. However, under these constraints, the maximum neutron star mass is limited to $ \lesssim 2.06 M_{\odot}$. No strong signatures from quark matter were found on the radius and the tidal deformability for neutron star masses below $1.8\, M_\odot$.

# Neutron stars with large quark cores

## Key claim

Large quark cores carrying up to almost half of the star mass are possible inside neutron stars with a maximum mass around 2.2 solar masses, but only if the hadron-to-quark phase transition is weak (energy density gap below 200 MeV/fm³) and occurs at baryon densities not above four times saturation density; under the stricter condition that the quark core exceeds one quarter of the total star mass, the maximum neutron star mass is limited to approximately 2.06 solar masses.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid equations of state by connecting a hadronic phase (described by a fourth-order Taylor expansion around saturation density n₀, with parameters randomly sampled from multivariate Gaussian distributions) to a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) quark phase through a Maxwell first-order phase transition, imposing beta equilibrium and global charge neutrality. The quark phase was parametrized by vector-isoscalar coupling ξ_ω, vector-isovector coupling ξ_ρ, and an effective bag constant B, with values explored on a fixed grid; hybrid EoSs were accepted only if they satisfied thermodynamic stability, causality, a maximum mass above 1.97 solar masses, and a transition density above 0.2 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass with large quark cores: approximately 2.2 M_⊙ (for cores carrying almost half the star mass)
- Maximum neutron star mass under the constraint that quark cores exceed one quarter of total star mass: less than or approximately 2.06 M_⊙
- Energy density gap required for large quark cores: below 200 MeV/fm³; for cores exceeding one quarter of star mass, below 100 MeV/fm³
- Onset density for quarks to appear in 1.4 M_⊙ neutron stars: between 1.3 n₀ and 2.5 n₀ (where n₀ = 0.155 fm⁻³)
- Transition density upper bound for sustaining considerable quark cores: not above 4 n₀
- Mean transition mass M_t over 23723 hybrid EoSs: approximately 2.14 M_⊙
- Mean quark branch length ΔM: 0.03 M_⊙ (range 10⁻⁴ M_⊙ to 0.79 M_⊙)
- Only 29 out of 23723 hybrid EoSs predict quark matter in a 1.4 M_⊙ neutron star, all with bag constant B = 10 MeV/fm³
- Radius range for all neutron stars with M > 1 M_⊙: 10.7 km to 13.5 km
- Tidal deformability Λ at 1.4 M_⊙: mean 558, standard deviation 74, range 311 to 724; 62% of EoSs satisfy the GW170817 constraint 70 < Λ < 580
- Hadronic parameter means for accepted EoSs: K_sat = 236.52 MeV, Q_sat = 241.38 MeV, Z_sat = 362.11 MeV, E_sym = 33.17 MeV, L_sym = 49.99 MeV

## Context

This work extends the hybrid EoS approach of Pagliara et al. (2007), Benic et al. (2014), and Pereira et al. (2016) by using a model-independent Taylor expansion for the hadronic phase rather than a specific relativistic mean-field model, allowing systematic exploration of nuclear matter parameter uncertainties. The results are consistent with the finding of Alford et al. (2013, 2015) that quark branches are typically very short (ΔM less than 10⁻³ M_⊙), but show that larger quark cores become possible with weak phase transitions. The study enables future work on distinguishing hybrid stars from purely hadronic stars through multi-messenger observations, though it finds no strong quark-matter signatures in radius or tidal deformability for neutron stars below 1.8 M_⊙.
