# Determination of the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the ImQMD model by nuclear reaction at the Fermi energy region

> The nucleon-nucleon interaction is investigated by using the ImQMD model with the three sets of parameters IQ1, IQ2 and IQ3 in which the corresponding incompressibility coefficients of nuclear matter are different. The charge distribution of fragments for various reaction systems are calculated at different incident energies. The parameters strongly affect the charge distribution below the threshold energy of nuclear multifragmentation. The fragment multiplicity spectrum for $^{238}$U+$^{197}$Au at 15 AMeV and the charge distribution for $^{129}$Xe+$^{120}$Sn at 32 and 45 AMeV, and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at 35 AMeV are reproduced by the ImQMD model with the set of parameter IQ3. It is concluded that charge distribution of the fragments and the fragment multiplicity spectrum are good observables for studying N-N interaction, the Fermi energy region is a sensitive energy region to explore the N-N interaction, and IQ3 is a suitable set of parameters for the ImQMD model.

# Determination of the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the ImQMD model by nuclear reaction at the Fermi energy region

## Key claim

The charge distribution of fragments and the fragment multiplicity spectrum are sensitive observables for probing the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the Fermi energy region, but only below the threshold energy of nuclear multifragmentation; above that threshold, the charge distribution becomes insensitive to the ImQMD model parameters. The IQ3 parameter set (with an incompressibility coefficient of 226 MeV) reproduces experimental data for several reaction systems, while IQ1 and IQ2 do not.

## Method

The authors used the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model with three parameter sets (IQ1, IQ2, IQ3) that differ in their Skyrme-type effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and corresponding nuclear matter incompressibility coefficients. They simulated central collisions (500 events per system) for 40Ca+40Ca at incident energies from 10 to 45 AMeV, 129Xe+120Sn at 32 and 45 AMeV, 197Au+197Au at 35 and 50 AMeV, and 238U+197Au at 15 AMeV (4000 events from central to peripheral collisions), calculating the charge distribution of fragments and fragment multiplicity spectrum without combining a statistical model.

## Key numbers

- Incompressibility coefficients: IQ1 = 165 MeV, IQ2 = 195 MeV, IQ3 = 226 MeV
- Saturation density for all three parameter sets: ρ0 = 0.165 fm−3
- For 40Ca+40Ca, significant differences between IQ1, IQ2, and IQ3 appear at incident energies 10–30 AMeV; results converge at 35–45 AMeV
- For 129Xe+120Sn, significant differences appear at 32 AMeV but not at 45 AMeV
- For 197Au+197Au, significant differences appear at 35 AMeV but not at 50 AMeV
- For 238U+197Au at 15 AMeV, experimental data show two-body events exhaust about 5% and three- and four-body events exhaust approximately 83% of total reaction events; IQ3 reproduces this multiplicity spectrum

## Context

This work extends the ImQMD model development by Li, Tian, and Wang (2010–2012) that proposed the IQ1, IQ2, and IQ3 parameter sets based on Skyrme forces (such as SkP, SkM*, and Sly1-7 from earlier work by Vautherin and Brink (1972) and others) and experimental fusion reaction data. The study identifies the Fermi energy region (below the multifragmentation threshold) as a sensitive regime for probing the nucleon-nucleon interaction, in contrast to higher energies where the charge distribution becomes insensitive to the interaction parameters. This enables future use of the IQ3 parameter set for heavy-ion reaction simulations near the Fermi energy, and suggests that charge distribution and fragment multiplicity are useful observables for constraining the nuclear equation of state.
