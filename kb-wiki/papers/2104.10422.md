# Investigations on mixed harmonic cumulants in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC

> A series of new flow observables mixed harmonic multi-particle cumulants (MHC), which allow for the first time to quantify the correlations strength between different order of flow coefficients with various moments, was investigated using hydrodynamic model. These new observables are constructed based on multi-particle cumulants, and thus by design will be less sensitive to the non-flow contaminations. In addition to the previous study of correlation involving two flow coefficients with their second moments, both correlations of three flow coefficients and the correlations of higher order moments of $v_2$ and $v_3$ are systematically investigated using iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model with two different initial conditions, AMPT and TRENTo, respectively. These systematic studies using hydrodynamic models will significantly improve the understanding on the correlations between different orders (and moments) of flow coefficients. The hydrodynamic predictions shown in this paper and the future comparisons to experimental measurements will provide more constraints on theoretical models and extract more information about the transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Mixed harmonic cumulants (MHC) allow for the first time to quantify the correlation strength between different orders of flow coefficients with various moments, and the hydrodynamic predictions show that the linear response of final-state flow to initial-state eccentricity breaks down for higher-order moments of \(v_3\) and for correlations involving three flow coefficients, meaning these observables can provide new constraints on initial conditions and transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV using the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model, which combines (2+1)-d viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) with the UrQMD hadron cascade. Two different initial condition models were used: AMPT (with a Gaussian smearing factor) and TRENTo, and for AMPT initial conditions two values of specific shear viscosity (\(\eta/s = 0.08\) and \(0.2\)) were compared. Mixed harmonic cumulants (MHC) were computed via a generic recursive algorithm for 4-particle, 6-particle, and 8-particle correlations, and normalized mixed harmonic cumulants (nMHC) were defined to evaluate relative correlation strengths.

## Key numbers

* For \(nMHC(v_2^2,v_3^2)\) (equivalent to \(NSC(3,2)\)), results from two different \(\eta/s\) values (0.08 and 0.2) are consistent in the centrality region 0-50%, showing insensitivity to \(\eta/s\).
* For \(nMHC(v_2^4,v_3^2)\), the ratio \(nMHC(v_2^4,v_3^2)/nMHC(\epsilon_2^4,\epsilon_3^2)\) is close to 1 within error, demonstrating linear response holds for this observable.
* For \(nMHC(v_2^2,v_3^4)\), the ratio deviates from 1, indicating linear response breaks down for this case.
* The ordering of \(nMHC(v_2^k,v_3^l)\) signs is: negative for \((k+l=4)\), positive for \((k+l=6)\), and negative for \((k+l=8)\), independent of initial conditions.
* The absolute values show \(\lvert nMHC(v_2^2,v_3^2)\rvert > \lvert nMHC(v_2^2,v_3^4)\rvert > \lvert nMHC(v_2^2,v_3^6)\rvert\) for \((k=2)\) and \(\lvert nMHC(v_2^4,v_3^2)\rvert > \lvert nMHC(v_2^4,v_3^4)\rvert\) for \((k=4)\) in the centrality region 10-50%.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of symmetric cumulants \(SC(m,n)\) and normalized symmetric cumulants \(NSC(m,n)\) (ALICE Collaboration, 2016) that only quantified correlations between two flow coefficients with their second moments. The mixed harmonic cumulants (MHC) were developed via the generic recursive algorithm of Moravcova et al. (2020). The results enable future comparisons to experimental measurements at the LHC, providing more constraints on theoretical models and extracting information about the transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma, particularly through the sensitivity of \(nMHC(v_2^2,v_4^2)\), \(nMHC(v_3^2,v_5^2)\), and \(nMHC(v_2^2,v_3^2,v_4^2)\) to both \(\eta/s\) and initial conditions.
