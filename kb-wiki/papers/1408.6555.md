# Ab Initio Multi-Reference In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group Calculations of Even Calcium and Nickel Isotopes

> We use the newly developed Multi-Reference In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group to study all even isotopes of the calcium and nickel isotopic chains, based on two- plus three-nucleon interactions derived from chiral effective field theory. We present results for ground-state and two-neutron separation energies and quantify their theoretical uncertainties. At shell closures, we find excellent agreement with Coupled Cluster results obtained with the same Hamiltonians. Our results highlight the importance of the chiral 3N interaction to obtain a correct reproduction of experimental energy trends, and their subtle impact on the location of the neutron drip lines in the Ca and Ni chains. At the same time, we uncover and discuss deficiencies of the input Hamiltonians which need to be addressed by the next generation of chiral interactions.

## Key claim

The Multi-Reference In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (MR-IM-SRG) provides ground-state and two-neutron separation energies for even calcium and nickel isotopes that agree well with Coupled Cluster results at shell closures, but the location of the neutron drip lines and the quantitative reproduction of experimental trends depend sensitively on the inclusion of initial chiral three-nucleon (3N) forces and on the truncation of the three-body model space.

## Method

The authors perform MR-IM-SRG(2) calculations, truncating the flow equations at the two-body level, using Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) quasiparticle vacua projected on proton and neutron numbers as reference states. The input Hamiltonians are chiral N$^3$LO two-nucleon (NN) interactions by Entem and Machleidt with cutoff $\Lambda_\text{NN}=500$ MeV/c, optionally combined with local N$^2$LO 3N interactions with cutoffs $\Lambda_\text{3N}=350$ or $400$ MeV/c, softened by free-space SRG evolution to resolution scales $\lambda_\text{SRG}=1.88$ to $2.24$ fm$^{-1}$. The 3N matrix elements are truncated in the total energy quantum number $E_\text{3max}$, and the normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) approximation is used to discard the residual 3N interaction term.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state overbinding for the NN+3N-full Hamiltonian increases from 8% for $^{36}$Ca to 12% for $^{54}$Ca.
- The difference between CCSD and CR-CC(2,3) two-neutron separation energies for $^{54}$Ca is only 150 keV, almost independent of $\Lambda_\text{3N}$ and $\lambda_\text{SRG}$.
- Increasing $E_\text{3max}$ from 14 to 16 changes $S_{2n}$ by less than 100 keV for $^{38-50}$Ca and by 200-500 keV for $^{52-60}$Ca.
- For the NN+3N-induced Hamiltonian, $^{62}$Ca is consistently unbound by 5-6 MeV with respect to $^{60}$Ca across the range of $\lambda_\text{SRG}$.
- For the NN+3N-full Hamiltonian, $^{56-60}$Ca are unbound with respect to $^{54}$Ca by only 1-2 MeV.
- The difference between CCSD and CR-CC(2,3) two-neutron separation energies for $^{60,62}$Ni is 300-350 keV.
- Variation of $\Lambda_\text{3N}$ moves the theoretical neutron drip line for Ni from $^{86}$Ni to $^{78}$Ni.

## Context

This work extends the MR-IM-SRG method (Hergert 2013) to open-shell even isotopes of calcium and nickel, building on prior Coupled Cluster studies (Binder 2014) and Self-Consistent Green's Functions calculations (Soma 2014) that used the same chiral Hamiltonians. The results confirm the crucial role of chiral 3N forces previously demonstrated for oxygen isotopes (Otsuka 2010, Hagen 2012, Hergert 2013) and enable systematic uncertainty quantification for neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line, while identifying deficiencies in current chiral interactions that must be addressed by next-generation Hamiltonians.
