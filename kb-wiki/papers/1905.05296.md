# Thermodynamic Geometry of Nambu -- Jona Lasinio model

> The formalism of Riemannian geometry is applied to study the phase transitions in Nambu -Jona Lasinio (NJL) model. Thermodynamic geometry reliably describes the phase diagram, both in the chiral limit and for finite quark masses. The comparison between the geometrical study of NJL model and of (2+1) Quantum Chromodynamics at high temperature and small baryon density shows a clear connection between chiral symmetry restoration/breaking and deconfinement/confinement regimes.

## Key claim

Thermodynamic geometry, using the scalar curvature R derived from Ruppeiner's metric, reliably describes the phase diagram of the Nambu-Jona Lasinio (NJL) model, but the geometric criterion for the transition depends on whether confinement is present: the NJL crossover is identified by a maximum of |R|, whereas in QCD the deconfinement transition is identified by R = 0, revealing a geometrical interplay between chiral symmetry restoration and confinement.

## Method

The authors computed the thermodynamic scalar curvature R from the Hessian of the entropy density, using the potential phi = P/T (pressure over temperature) for the NJL model in the mean-field approximation. They solved the gap equations for the dynamically generated quark mass M(T, mu) for two-flavor and three-flavor NJL models, both in the chiral limit (m=0) and with finite quark masses, then evaluated R and its derivatives up to third order to locate phase transitions. The transition temperature was identified either by the divergence of |R| (second-order), by a discontinuity in R (first-order), or by the local maximum of |R| (crossover), and compared with the chiral susceptibility.

## Key numbers

- For two flavors in the chiral limit, the critical point between first-order and second-order phase transitions occurs at mu* = 290 MeV and T* = 58 MeV.
- For two flavors with physical quark mass m0 = 5.5 MeV, the critical point between crossover and first-order transition is at mu* = 329 MeV and T* = 32 MeV.
- For three flavors (m = 5.5 MeV, ms = 135.7 MeV, Lambda = 631.4 MeV), the critical point is at mu* = 335 MeV and T* = 35 MeV.
- The dynamically generated mass at T = mu = 0 in the chiral limit is M0 = 300 MeV, corresponding to Lambda = 650 MeV and G = 5.01 x 10^-6 MeV^-2.
- For two flavors with m0 = 5.5 MeV, the same cutoff Lambda = 650 MeV and coupling G = 5.01 x 10^-6 MeV^-2 were used.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic geometry approach previously applied to QCD deconfinement by Castorina (2018) to the NJL model, which lacks confinement. The authors compare the geometric description of chiral symmetry restoration in NJL with the QCD crossover studied in Castorina (2018), where the R = 0 condition matched lattice data and the freeze-out curve within 10%. The results suggest that in the effective chiral limit (ms/ml >> 1), thermodynamic geometry may recover a "true" chiral phase transition at a lower temperature, as recently discussed by Ding (2018).
