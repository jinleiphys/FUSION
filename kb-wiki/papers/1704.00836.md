# Relativistic effects in heavy-ion Coulomb scattering

> The role of relativistic corrections in Coulomb scattering of heavy ions at intermediate energy collisions ($E_{lab}\gtrsim 50$ MeV/n) is investigated by numerically solving a full set of coupled equations. We compare two methods, (a) one involving an exact account of interaction retardation with (b) a method based on the expansion of effective Lagrangians in powers of the ion velocities, $v/c$.Our study makes it possible to infer the relevance of kinematic corrections, retardation, and magnetic interactions such as the Darwin force. We show that analytical formulas are able to describe all aspects of experimental interest in relativistic effects in heavy-ion Coulomb scattering at intermediate energies without having to solve numerically the coupled equations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that analytical formulas can describe all aspects of relativistic effects in heavy-ion Coulomb scattering at intermediate energies (E_lab ≥ 50 MeV/nucleon) without solving the full set of coupled equations numerically, but this conclusion is qualified by the finding that the method of Matzdorf et al. (1987) is superior to that of Aguiar et al. (1990) because it includes full retardation effects, which constitute the largest relativistic correction beyond the relativistic mass increase.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the full covariant equations of motion for two charged particles using an adaptive Runge-Kutta method, comparing two approaches: (a) the exact retarded-field method of Matzdorf et al. (1987) using Lienard-Wiechert potentials, and (b) the effective Lagrangian expansion method of Aguiar et al. (1990) with corrections up to order (v/c)^4. They computed scattering angles and differential cross sections for symmetric (208Pb + 208Pb) and asymmetric (17O + 208Pb) systems at laboratory energies from 50 to 250 MeV/nucleon, and compared these numerical results against analytical formulas proposed in both prior works.

## Context

This work extends the classical trajectory studies of Matzdorf et al. (1987) on retardation effects in Coulomb scattering and the effective Lagrangian approach of Aguiar et al. (1990), which expanded the relativistic Lagrangian in powers of v/c. The paper resolves a discrepancy between these two prior approaches by showing that the Matzdorf method is superior due to its full inclusion of retardation, and that the analytical formulas from both works are accurate within their respective domains. The findings enable experimentalists at radioactive beam facilities (RIKEN, GANIL, GSI, NSCL, FAIR, FRIB) to use simple analytical formulas for calibrating Coulomb scattering experiments and extracting excitation amplitudes without performing complex numerical calculations.
