# MARTINI: An event generator for relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We introduce the Modular Algorithm for Relativistic Treatment of heavy IoN Interactions (MARTINI), a comprehensive event generator for the hard and penetrating probes in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. Its main components are a time evolution model for the soft background, PYTHIA 8.1 and the McGill-AMY parton evolution scheme including radiative as well as elastic processes. This allows us to generate full event configurations in the high p_T region that take into account thermal QCD and QED effects as well as effects of the evolving medium. We present results for the neutral pion nuclear modification factor in Au+Au collisions at RHIC as a function of p_T for different centralities, and also as a function of the angle with respect to the reaction plane for non-central collisions. Furthermore, we study the production of high transverse momentum photons incorporating a complete set of photon-production channels.

## Key claim

MARTINI is the first Monte Carlo event generator to combine AMY bremsstrahlung with elastic processes and in-medium photon production for hard probes in heavy-ion collisions, but the strong coupling constant \(\alpha_{\rm s}\) must be adjusted (to 0.3 or 0.33 depending on the hydrodynamic background) to match the neutral pion nuclear modification factor data, introducing a 10–20% uncertainty in \(\alpha_{\rm s}\).

## Method

The generator uses PYTHIA 8.1 to produce hard partons from individual nucleon-nucleon collisions and to perform final hadronization via the Lund string fragmentation model. Hard partons are evolved through a hydrodynamic background medium using the McGill-AMY formalism, which solves Fokker-Planck type rate equations for radiative processes (gluon bremsstrahlung including the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect) and elastic scattering, with transition rates tabulated as functions of parton energy, temperature, and coupling. The soft background is provided by 2+1 or 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic models that supply local temperature and flow velocity, and the simulation treats each parton individually on an event-by-event basis, sampling the type and energy of interactions from the computed rates.

## Key numbers

* Neutral pion nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) in central (0-10%) Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV described with \(\alpha_{\rm s}=0.3\) using 3+1D hydrodynamics and \(\alpha_{\rm s}=0.33\) using 2+1D hydrodynamics, with \(\tau_0=0.6\) fm/\(c\) for both.
* For radiative-only processes, \(\alpha_{\rm s}\simeq 0.39\) is needed to describe the central \(R_{AA}\) data, higher than the 0.33 used in prior work (Qin 2007).
* Photon production in p+p and Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV: including all vacuum shower photons gives an effective \(K\)-factor of approximately 1.8 in the regarded \(p_T\) range; excluding pre-equilibrium vacuum shower photons reduces the photon \(R_{AA}\) by approximately 20%.
* Impact parameters used for centrality classes: \(b=2.4\) fm for 0-10% central and \(b=7.5\) fm for 20-30% mid-central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the McGill-AMY parton evolution formalism (Arnold 2001, 2002; Qin 2007) by implementing it as a full event generator, enabling event-by-event studies of correlations and differential observables. It contrasts with other Monte Carlo simulations for heavy-ion collisions (Wang 1991, Dainese 2004, Lokhtin 2005, Renk 2008, Zapp 2008, Armesto 2009) by being the first to include AMY bremsstrahlung combined with elastic processes and in-medium photon production. The results enable future studies of many-body observables and correlations, as well as implementation of heavy quark evolution.
