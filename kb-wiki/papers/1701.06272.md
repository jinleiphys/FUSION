# Hyperon-nucleon Scattering In A Covariant Chiral Effective Field Theory Approach

> A recently proposed covariant chiral effective field theory approach is applied to study strangeness $S=-1$ hyperon-nucleon interactions at leading order. 12 low energy constants are introduced by Lorentz invariance, which is different from the heavy baryon approach, where only five appear. The Kadyshevsky equation is employed to iterate the chiral potentials. A quite satisfactory description of the 36 hyperon-nucleon scattering data is obtained with $χ^2\simeq 17$, which is comparable with the next-to-leading order heavy baryon approach. The results hint at a more efficient way to construct the chiral potentials.

# Hyperon-nucleon Scattering In A Covariant Chiral Effective Field Theory Approach

## Key claim

A covariant chiral effective field theory (χEFT) approach at leading order (LO) describes 36 hyperon-nucleon (YN) scattering data with χ² ≈ 17, comparable to the next-to-leading order (NLO) heavy baryon (HB) approach, but with only 12 low energy constants (LECs) instead of 23. The improvement is qualified by noting that renormalization group invariance is still not achieved, as shown by the variation of χ² with the cutoff.

## Method

The authors construct LO chiral potentials for the strangeness S = −1 ΛN–ΣN system using covariant chiral Lagrangians, retaining the full Dirac spinor structure of the baryons to preserve Lorentz invariance. The potentials include nonderivative four-baryon contact terms (with strict SU(3) symmetry, yielding 12 independent LECs) and one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges (with SU(3) broken only by meson mass differences). The Kadyshevsky equation is used to iterate the potentials, and the LECs are fitted to 36 YN scattering data points, with constraints from Λp ¹S₀, ³S₁ and Σ⁺p ³S₁ scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- χ² = 16.7 (minimum value) at cutoff Λ_F = 600 MeV for the LO covariant χEFT fit to 36 YN scattering data.
- 12 independent LECs are introduced in the covariant approach, compared to 5 in the LO HB approach and 23 in the NLO HB approach.
- The LECs are reported in units of 10⁴ GeV⁻² at Λ_F = 600 MeV; for example, C^{ΛΛ}_{1S0} = −0.0223, C^{ΣΣ}_{1S0} = −0.0528, C^{ΛΛ}_{3S1} = 0.0032, C^{ΣΣ}_{3S1} = 0.0842, C^{ΛΣ}_{3S1} = 0.0232, and Ĉ^{ΛΛ}_{1S0} = 3.9185.
- The pseudoscalar meson decay constant is taken as f₀ = 93 MeV, and D+F = 1.26 with F/(F+D) = 0.4.

## Context

This work extends the covariant χEFT approach previously applied to nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering by Ren et al. (2016) to the YN sector, building on the heavy baryon χEFT studies of Polinder et al. (2006) and Haidenbauer et al. (2013). It also relates to the EG approach of Epelbaum and Gegelia (2012, 2015), which uses the Kadyshevsky equation to mitigate cutoff dependence. The results enable future relativistic hypernuclear few- and many-body calculations, and suggest a more efficient path for constructing baryon-baryon chiral potentials with fewer LECs than the NLO HB approach.
