# Calculations of the K$^{+}$-Nucleus Microscopic Optical Potential and of the Corresponding Differential Elastic Cross Sections

> Calculations are made of the $K^{+}+^{12}$C, $^{40}$Ca differential elastic scattering cross sections at the beam momenta 0.635, 0.715, and 0.8 GeV/c. To this end the microscopic optical potential derived in the high-energy approximation was used where existing data on the kaon-nucleon amplitude and on the point-like density distributions of target-nuclei were utilized. The effect of different methods of relativization was studied and shown to play an important role. A good agreement with the experimental data on differential elastic cross sections was obtained.

## Key claim

The microscopic optical potential derived in the high-energy approximation, with no free parameters, yields good agreement with experimental differential elastic cross sections for K+ scattering on 12C and 40Ca at beam momenta 0.635, 0.715, and 0.8 GeV/c, but only when relativistic corrections are included; the non-relativistic treatment fails. The different methods of relativization produce very similar results to each other, but all are essential for agreement.

## Method

The authors constructed a microscopic optical potential using the high-energy approximation (optical limit of multiple scattering theory), incorporating existing data on the kaon-nucleon total cross sections, the ratio of real to imaginary forward scattering amplitude, and the kaon-nucleon form factor, together with point-like nucleon density distributions of the target nuclei derived from electron scattering data. They solved the Klein-Gordon equation reduced to a Schrodinger-like form with an effective potential that includes a relativization factor gamma(r), and tested four different prescriptions for this factor. The DWUCK4 code was used to compute differential elastic cross sections for K+ on 12C and 40Ca at three beam momenta.

## Key numbers

- Beam momenta studied: 0.635, 0.715, and 0.8 GeV/c for K+ on 12C; 0.8 GeV/c for K+ on 40Ca.
- Relativization factors gamma(r) for K+ on 12C at 0.8 GeV/c range from 1.7421 (gamma(r1)) to 1.8381 (gamma(r3)); for K+ on 40Ca at 0.8 GeV/c they range from 1.8496 to 1.8824.
- Calculated total reaction cross sections for K+ on 12C at 0.635 GeV/c are 121-125 mb/sr, compared to an estimated experimental value of about 140 mb/sr from Friedman.
- Calculated total reaction cross sections for K+ on 12C at 0.715 GeV/c are 126-128 mb/sr, compared to an estimated experimental value of about 150 mb/sr from Friedman.

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts to describe K+ nucleus scattering using the Glauber multiple scattering theory (Abgrall) and a local version of the Kisslinger nonlocal potential (Ebrahim1), which required increasing the K+N S11 phase shift by 10-15% to match data. The present parameter-free microscopic optical potential approach reproduces differential elastic cross sections well but underestimates total reaction cross sections by 10-15% compared to estimates from Friedman, indicating a remaining discrepancy that also appeared in the earlier Kisslinger-type calculations. The results enable further refinement of the K+N amplitude input and suggest that a unified description of both elastic and reaction cross sections may require additional physics beyond the high-energy approximation.
