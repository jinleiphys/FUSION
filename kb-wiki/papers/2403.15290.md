# Contact interactions, self-adjoint extensions, and low-energy scattering

> Low-energy scattering is well described by the effective-range expansion. In quantum mechanics, a tower of contact interactions can generate terms in this expansion after renormalization. Scattering parameters are also encoded in the self-adjoint extension of the Hamiltonian. We briefly review this well-known result for two particles with s-wave interactions using impenetrable self-adjoint extensions, including the case of harmonically trapped two-particle states. By contrast, the one-dimensional scattering problem is surprisingly intricate. We show that the families of self-adjoint extensions correspond to a coupled system of symmetric and antisymmetric outgoing waves, which is diagonalized by an SU(2) transformation that accounts for mixing and a relative phase. This is corroborated by an effective theory computation that includes all four energy-independent contact interactions. The equivalence of various one-dimensional contact interactions is discussed and scrutinized from the perspective of renormalization. As an application, the spectrum of a general point interaction with a harmonic trap is solved in one dimension.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the most general one-dimensional point interaction, described by self-adjoint extensions of the Hamiltonian on the punctured line, corresponds to a coupled system of symmetric and antisymmetric outgoing waves that can be diagonalized by an SU(2) transformation, and that this description is equivalent to an effective field theory computation including all four energy-independent contact interactions, provided the relevant delta-function potential is included to ensure renormalizability.

## Method

The authors first review the impenetrable self-adjoint extension for s-wave scattering in three dimensions, where Robin boundary conditions at the origin encode the scattering length. They then analyze the general self-adjoint extension on the one-dimensional punctured line, deriving the joining conditions for the wavefunction and its derivative across the origin in terms of a transfer matrix with four real parameters and a phase. The scattering problem is solved to obtain the S-matrix in the partial-wave basis, and the results are corroborated by a momentum-space effective field theory computation using dimensional regularization with all four energy-independent contact interactions (delta, delta-prime, and second-derivative delta interactions).

## Key numbers

* The self-adjoint extension parameter beta for s-wave scattering is related to the scattering length by beta = -1/a (Eq. (12)), where a is the scattering length using the nuclear physics sign convention.
* The spectrum condition for two harmonically trapped particles with s-wave interactions is given by -1/a = -2 sqrt(m omega) * Gamma(3/4 - E/(2 omega)) / Gamma(1/4 - E/(2 omega)) (Eq. (17)), where m is the reduced mass, omega is the trap frequency, and E is the energy.
* The roots of the denominator for the one-dimensional scattering amplitudes are kappa_plus/minus = [-(alpha+gamma) +/- sqrt((alpha-gamma)^2 + 4)] / (2 delta) (Eq. (26)), where alpha, gamma, and delta are real self-adjoint extension parameters.
* The mixing angle Theta satisfies k cot Theta = -(beta + k^2 delta) / sqrt((alpha-gamma)^2 + 4 sin^2 phi) (Eq. (31)), where phi is the time-reversal odd phase parameter and k is the scattering momentum.

## Context

This work extends the well-known connection between self-adjoint extensions and contact interactions reviewed by Jackiw (1991) and van Kolck (1998), and builds on the analysis of one-dimensional point interactions by Seba (1986), Chernoff (1993), and Coutinho (1997). The paper reconciles the self-adjoint extension approach with effective field theory, showing that the general point interaction requires all four energy-independent contact interactions to be renormalizable, and that the delta-prime interaction alone (a marginal operator) generates a delta-function potential under renormalization group evolution, confirming the quantum scale anomaly discussed by Bergman (1991) and Camblong (2000). The results enable the solution of the harmonically trapped two-particle problem with a general point interaction in one dimension, extending the trapped s-wave result of Busch (1998) to include parity mixing and relative phase effects.
