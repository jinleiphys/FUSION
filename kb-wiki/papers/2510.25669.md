# Minijet thermalization and jet transport coefficients in QCD kinetic theory

> We apply weakly coupled QCD kinetic theory to investigate the thermalization of high-momentum on-shell partons (minijets) in a Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). Our approach incorporates isotropic hard thermal loop screening to model soft quark and gluon exchanges, allowing us to verify consistency with established analytic results of jet transport coefficients. We perform kinetic simulations of minijets propagating through a thermal gluon plasma, incorporating both collinear radiation and elastic scatterings. The resulting evolution is compared to predictions from jet transport coefficients, including the longitudinal and transverse jet-quenching parameters $\hat{q}$, energy loss, and the drag coefficient. We find that standard definitions of jet transport coefficients neglect the contributions from recoiling medium particles. Including these contributions restores consistency with the kinetic evolution. Finally, we show that the minijet thermalization time scales remarkably well with $\hat{q}$ and we produce a phenomenological estimate of the minijet quenching time in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors show that standard definitions of jet transport coefficients (such as the jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$) neglect contributions from recoiling medium particles, and that including these contributions restores consistency between transport coefficient predictions and full kinetic theory evolution of minijets.

## Method

The authors apply weakly coupled QCD effective kinetic theory (EKT) with isotropic hard thermal loop (HTL) screening to simulate the evolution of high-momentum on-shell gluonic perturbations (minijets) propagating through a thermal gluon plasma at constant temperature. They solve the linearized Boltzmann equation including both elastic $2\leftrightarrow2$ scatterings and collinear $1\leftrightarrow2$ inelastic processes, and compare the resulting evolution to predictions from jet transport coefficients defined via a single-hit (opacity expansion) approximation.

## Key numbers

- The isotropic HTL screening prescription reproduces analytical results for longitudinal and transverse momentum diffusion in the infinite jet-momentum limit, with the gluon-to-quark conversion rate also matching known results.
- For finite minijet momentum, the additional term ${\k'_\perp}^2 - \k_\perp^2$ in the jet quenching parameter expression (arising from medium recoil) is identified as the difference between the full kinetic theory result and standard definitions.
- The minijet thermalization time scales remarkably well with $\hat{q}$, enabling a phenomenological estimate of the minijet quenching time in heavy-ion collisions.

## Context

This work extends previous applications of QCD kinetic theory to minijet thermalization in static and expanding plasmas (Schlichting 2020, Mehtar-Tani 2022, Boguslavski 2023, Zhou 2024) by using the more complete isotropic HTL screening prescription instead of the approximated Debye-like prescription. It also connects to the extensive literature on jet transport coefficients computed at leading and next-to-leading order in QCD kinetic theory (Arnold 2002, Ghiglieri 2015), non-perturbative lattice QCD (Panero 2013, Moore 2019), and holography (Liu 2006, Casalderrey-Solana 2011). The results enable a clearer connection between transport coefficient descriptions and full kinetic theory evolution of jet quenching, and provide a compact parametrization for minijet thermalization in the medium.
