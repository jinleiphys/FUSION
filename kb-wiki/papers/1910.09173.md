# Effects of Pauli blocking on pion production in central collisions of neutron-rich nuclei

> Pauli blocking is carefully investigated for the processes of $NN \rightarrow N Δ$ and $Δ\rightarrow N π$ in heavy-ion collisions, aiming at a more precise prediction of the $π^-/ π^+$ ratio which is an important observable to constrain the high-density symmetry energy. We use the AMD+JAM approach, which combines the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics for the time evolution of nucleons and the JAM model to treat processes for $Δ$ resonances and pions. As is known in general transport-code simulations, it is difficult to treat Pauli blocking very precisely due to unphysical fluctuations and additional smearing of the phase-space distribution function, when Pauli blocking is treated in the standard method of JAM. We propose an improved method in AMD+JAM to use the Wigner function precisely calculated in AMD as the blocking probability. Different Pauli blocking methods are compared in heavy-ion collisions of neutron-rich nuclei, ${}^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+{}^{124}\mathrm{Sn}$, at 270 MeV/nucleon. With the more accurate method, we find that Pauli blocking is stronger, in particular for the neutron in the final state in $NN \rightarrow N Δ$ and $ Δ\to Nπ$, compared to the case with a proton in the final state. Consequently, the $π^-/π^+$ ratio becomes higher when the Pauli blocking is improved, the effect of which is found to be comparable to the sensitivity to the high-density symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that improving the treatment of Pauli blocking in transport simulations, specifically by using the Wigner function from the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model rather than the standard smeared test-particle method, leads to stronger Pauli blocking for neutrons in the final state of \(NN \rightarrow N\Delta\) and \(\Delta \rightarrow N\pi\) processes, which increases the predicted \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratio by an amount comparable to the sensitivity of this ratio to the high-density symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors combine the AMD model for nucleon time evolution with the JAM hadronic cascade model for \(\Delta\) resonances and pions, treating \(\Delta\) and pion production as a perturbation on the AMD nucleon dynamics. They compare four Pauli blocking methods: the standard JAM method using smeared test particles [PB(jam)], a new method using the AMD Wigner function [PB(amd)], a mixed method [PB(amd, jam)], and an artificially weakened blocking [PB(\(\frac14\)jam)], applied to central collisions of \({}^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+{}^{124}\mathrm{Sn}\) at 270 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 270 MeV/nucleon for \({}^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+{}^{124}\mathrm{Sn}\) central collisions (impact parameter \(0<b<1\) fm).
- The \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratio increases when Pauli blocking is improved from PB(jam) to PB(amd), with the effect comparable to the sensitivity to the high-density symmetry energy (asy-soft with \(L=46\) MeV vs. asy-stiff with \(L=108\) MeV).
- The momentum of the final nucleon in \(NN \rightarrow N\Delta\) processes is distributed around 0.2 GeV/\(c\) in the center-of-mass frame.
- The \((N/Z)^2\) ratio for the total system is 2.4336, used as a reference line for \(\Delta^-/\Delta^{++}\) and \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratios.
- The Husimi function [PB(amd-h)] yields blocking probabilities between 0 and 1, with values lower than the Wigner function [PB(amd)] in regions where \(f\) is large.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMD+JAM studies (Ikeno 2016, Ikeno 2016 erratum) by addressing the known problem from the transport-code comparison project (Zhang 2018, Ono 2019) that Pauli blocking is systematically underestimated in QMD-type codes due to fluctuations from finite test-particle sampling. The improved method enables more reliable predictions of the \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratio as a probe of the high-density symmetry energy, and the results show that Pauli blocking effects are comparable in magnitude to the sensitivity from different symmetry energy parametrizations (asy-soft vs. asy-stiff).
