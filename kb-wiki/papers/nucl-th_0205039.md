# Estimation of Admixture of Twelve Quark Bag State in He4 Nucleus

> The $p{}^4He$ elastic scattering at the energy range from 0.695 to 393 GeV is analyzed in the framework of the Glauber theory. The Glauber amplitudes were evaluated using isospin-averaged nucleon-nucleon amplitudes and the ${}^4He$ wave function as a superposition of the Gaussian functions. The values of the calculated differential cross sections usually exceed the experimental ones. In order to overcome the discrepancy, it is assumed following to the paper by L.G. Dakno and N.N. Nikolaev (Nucl. Phys. {\bf A436} (1985) 653) that the ground state wave function of ${}^4He$ has an admixture of a twelve quark bag. Neglecting all transition amplitudes, the proton - 12q bag scattering amplitude was chosen in a simple gaussian form. The inclusion of the 12q bag leads to decreasing the $p{}^4He$ differential cross section and to a shift of the dip position to a large values of $t$ what is needed for a successful description of the experimental data. While fitting the data it is found that the weight of the 12q bag state in the ground state of the ${}^4He$ nucleus is $\sim 10.5$%, $σ^{tot}_{p-12q}\sim 34$ mb, and the slope parameter of the $p-12q$ bag elastic scattering is $\sim$ 23 (GeV/c)$^{-2}$. Inelastic shadowing is not taken into account at the calculations.

## Key claim

The authors assume that the ground state wave function of He-4 contains an admixture of a twelve-quark bag configuration, and by fitting this admixture to p-He-4 elastic scattering data they find a weight of approximately 10.5%, which is slightly lower than the 12% admixture originally proposed by Dakno and Nikolaev (1985). Inelastic shadowing corrections are not taken into account in the calculations.

## Method

The authors analyze p-He-4 elastic scattering in the energy range from 0.695 to 393 GeV within the Glauber diffraction theory framework. They evaluate Glauber amplitudes using isospin-averaged nucleon-nucleon amplitudes and the He-4 wave function expressed as a superposition of Gaussian functions, with four different parametrizations (A through D) taken from Dakno and Nikolaev (1985). To resolve discrepancies between calculated and experimental differential cross sections, they introduce a twelve-quark bag component in the He-4 ground state, modeling the proton-12q bag scattering amplitude in a simple Gaussian form and fitting the bag weight, total cross section, and slope parameter to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Weight of the 12q bag state in the He-4 ground state: approximately 10.5%
- Total cross section for p-12q scattering: approximately 34 mb
- Slope parameter of p-12q bag elastic scattering: approximately 23 (GeV/c)^-2
- Energy range of analyzed p-He-4 elastic scattering data: 0.695 to 393 GeV
- Charge radius parameters used: r_e = 1.67 fm, r_p = 0.812 fm
- Coulomb phase parameter B = 29 (GeV/c)^-2

## Context

This work extends the hypothesis of Dakno and Nikolaev (1985), who first proposed a 12% admixture of a twelve-quark bag in He-4 to explain irregularities in high-energy proton-He-4 elastic scattering. The authors note that inelastic screening corrections, which many authors have attempted to include (citing Abers, Gribov, Pump, Alberi, Harri, and Quigg), only produce corrections at the 2-5% level and shift the diffraction minimum to lower momentum transfer, whereas the data require a shift to larger t values. The results enable future calculations of elastic and inelastic scattering of He-4 on other nuclei such as deuteron, carbon, and oxygen, which are considered as strongly clustering alpha-particle nuclei where the He-4 structure may manifest.
