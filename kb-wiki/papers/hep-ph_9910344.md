# Shear viscosity in $œï^4$ theory from an extended ladder resummation

> We study shear viscosity in weakly coupled hot $œï^4$ theory using the CTP formalism . We show that the viscosity can be obtained as the integral of a three-point function. Non-perturbative corrections to the bare one-loop result can be obtained by solving a decoupled Schwinger-Dyson type integral equation for this vertex. This integral equation represents the resummation of an infinite series of ladder diagrams which contribute to the leading order result. It can be shown that this integral equation has exactly the same form as the Boltzmann equation. We show that the integral equation for the viscosity can be reexpressed by writing the vertex as a combination of polarization tensors. An expression for this polarization tensor can be obtained by solving another Schwinger-Dyson type integral equation. This procedure results in an expression for the viscosity that represents a non-perturbative resummation of contributions to the viscosity which includes certain non-ladder graphs, as well as the usual ladders. We discuss the motivation for this resummation. We show that these resummations can also be obtained by writing the viscosity as an integral equation involving a single four-point function. Finally, we show that when the viscosity is expressed in terms of a four-point function, it is possible to further extend the set of graphs included in the resummation by treating vertex and propagator corrections self-consistently. We discuss the significance of such a self-consistent resummation and show that the integral equation contains cancellations between vertex and propagator corrections.

## Method

The authors use the CTP (real-time) formalism of finite-temperature field theory to compute the shear viscosity via the Kubo formula, expressing it as the integral of a three-point function. They derive a decoupled Schwinger-Dyson type integral equation for the retarded three-point vertex that resums an infinite series of ladder diagrams, and show this equation reduces to the Boltzmann equation in the limit of small external momentum. They then re-express the viscosity in terms of a four-point function, which allows them to perform extended resummations that include non-ladder graphs and self-consistent treatments of vertex and propagator corrections, using HTL effective propagators to regulate infrared divergences.

## Context

This work extends the ladder resummation approach of Jeon (1995) from the imaginary-time formalism to the more efficient CTP (real-time) formalism, providing a compact derivation of the integral equation that resums planar ladder diagrams contributing to shear viscosity. The authors show that their three-point function integral equation has the same form as the Boltzmann equation, and they further develop techniques to include non-ladder graphs and self-consistent resummations that treat vertex and propagator corrections on equal footing. This enables future work on transport coefficients in gauge theories such as scalar QED, where the authors expect cancellations between vertex and propagator corrections to simplify the integral equations.
