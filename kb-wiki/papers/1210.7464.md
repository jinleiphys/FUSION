# The Tsallis Distribution at the LHC

> The Tsallis distribution has been used widely in high energy physics to describe the transverse momnetum distributions of particles. In this note we show that the use of a thermodynamically consistent form of this distribution leads to a description of identified particles with the same values of the temperature $T$ and the parameter $q$.

## Key claim

Using a thermodynamically consistent form of the Tsallis distribution (with an extra power of \(q\) in the integrals) allows a simultaneous description of identified pions, kaons, and protons in \(p-p\) collisions at 900 GeV with the same values of temperature \(T\) and parameter \(q\), contrary to the parameterizations used by experimental collaborations which break \(m_T\) scaling.

## Method

The author fits the transverse momentum spectra of identified hadrons (\(\pi^\pm\), \(K^\pm\), \(K^0_S\), \(p\), \(\bar{p}\), \(\Lambda\), \(\Xi^-\)) from ALICE and CMS \(p-p\) collisions at 900 GeV using the thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution \(\frac{d^2N}{dp_T dy} = gV \frac{p_T m_T}{(2\pi)^2} [1+(q-1) m_T/T]^{-q/(q-1)}\) at mid-rapidity with zero chemical potential. The fits extract a single temperature \(T\) and non-extensivity parameter \(q\) for each particle species, and the results are compared across species to test consistency.

## Key numbers

- For \(\pi^+\): \(q = 1.154 \pm 0.036\), \(T = 0.0682 \pm 0.0026\) GeV (68.2 MeV)
- For \(\pi^-\): \(q = 1.146 \pm 0.036\), \(T = 0.0704 \pm 0.0027\) GeV (70.4 MeV)
- For \(K^+\): \(q = 1.158 \pm 0.142\), \(T = 0.0690 \pm 0.0223\) GeV (69.0 MeV)
- For \(K^-\): \(q = 1.157 \pm 0.139\), \(T = 0.0681 \pm 0.0217\) GeV (68.1 MeV)
- For \(K^0_S\): \(q = 1.134 \pm 0.079\), \(T = 0.0923 \pm 0.0139\) GeV (92.3 MeV)
- For proton: \(q = 1.107 \pm 0.147\), \(T = 0.0730 \pm 0.0425\) GeV (73.0 MeV)
- For \(\bar{p}\): \(q = 1.106 \pm 0.158\), \(T = 0.0764 \pm 0.0464\) GeV (76.4 MeV)
- For \(\Lambda\): \(q = 1.114 \pm 0.047\), \(T = 0.0698 \pm 0.0148\) GeV (69.8 MeV)
- For \(\Xi^-\): \(q = 1.110 \pm 0.218\), \(T = 0.0440 \pm 0.0752\) GeV (44.0 MeV)
- Global approximate values: \(T \approx 70\) MeV, \(q \approx 1.15\)

## Context

This work extends the use of Tsallis statistics in high-energy physics, which has been applied by the ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, STAR, and PHENIX collaborations to fit transverse momentum spectra, but using a parameterization (Eq. 5 in the paper) that breaks thermodynamic consistency and \(m_T\) scaling. The author shows that the thermodynamically consistent form (Eq. 4) yields a single freeze-out temperature and \(q\) for all identified particles in \(p-p\) collisions at 900 GeV, enabling a unified thermal interpretation. The results are comparable to those from the Hagedorn-Tsallis distribution proposed by Deppman and Sena (2012).
