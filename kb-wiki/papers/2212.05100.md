# Mesons on the light front

> This lecture note is written for 'Courses on Light-Cone Techniques applied to QCD', Nov 21-25, 2022, IGFAE. It is intended to provide basic knowledge and selective perspectives on the application of light-front Hamiltonian approach to mesons in two 1.5-hour lectures.

## Key claim

This lecture note provides a pedagogical introduction to the light-front Hamiltonian approach to mesons as QCD bound states, but it is a review-style tutorial rather than presenting new numerical results or a specific calculation of meson properties.

## Method

The paper derives the light-front QCD Hamiltonian through canonical quantization on the light-front surface \(x^+=0\), starting from the QCD Lagrangian and applying the Legendre transformation. The derivation separates dynamical and constrained fields using light-cone gauge \(A_a^+=0\), expresses the constrained fields \(\Psi_-\) and \(A_a^-\) in terms of dynamical degrees of freedom, and obtains the Hamiltonian \(P_{QCD}^-\) containing kinetic terms, three-gluon, four-gluon, gluon-emission, instantaneous-gluon, and instantaneous-fermion interactions.

## Key numbers

- The metric tensor nonzero components are \(g^{+-}=g^{-+}=2\) and \(g_{+-}=g_{-+}=1/2\), with \(g^{ii}=g_{ii}=-1\) for \(i=1,2\).
- The determinant of the metric tensor is \(\det g_{\mu\nu} = -1/2\).
- The four-dimensional integral transforms as \(\int d^4 x = \frac{1}{2}\int dx^+ dx^- d^2 x_\perp\).
- The Lorentz-invariant momentum integral is \(\int \frac{d^4 p}{(2\pi)^4}\theta(p^+)(2\pi)\delta(p^+ p^- - \vec{p}_\perp^2 - m^2) = \int \frac{d^2 p_\perp dp^+}{(2\pi)^3 2p^+}\theta(p^+)\).

## Context

This lecture note is written for the course "Courses on Light-Cone Techniques applied to QCD" (November 2022, IGFAE) and is based primarily on the review by Brodsky, Pauli, and Pinsky (1997) and the author's own work Li (2019). It extends the pedagogical literature on light-front quantization by providing a self-contained derivation of the QCD Hamiltonian in the front form, following Dirac's (1949) classification of relativistic dynamics. The work enables students and researchers to understand the basis for light-front bound-state calculations of mesons, though it does not itself present numerical results or comparisons with experiment.
