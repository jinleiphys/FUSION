# Parity-Violating Three Nucleon Interactions at Low Energies and Large-$N_{C}$

> The parity-violating (PV) nucleon-nucleon ($N\!N$) interaction in the three-nucleon system is investigated using pionless effective field theory ($\mathrm{EFT}(/\!\!\!π)$). This work shows that a next-to-leading order (NLO) PV three-body force is necessary in contradiction with a previous claim [Griesshammer and Schindler in Eur. Phys. J. A 46, 73 (2010)]. Including three-body $P$ to $D$-wave transitions PV three-nucleon observables are calculated to higher energies than previously considered. Using the recent large-$N_{C}$ analysis of the PV $N\!N$ interaction in $\mathrm{EFT}(/\!\!\!π)$ the current understanding of low energy PV few-body measurements is reassessed. The recent measurement of the asymmetry $A_γ$ in $\vec{n}p\to dγ$ from the NPDGamma collaboration [D. Blyth et al. (NPDGamma), Phys. Rev. Lett. 121, 242002 (2018)], gives the value $g_{4}^{(N_{C}^{-1})}=(-1.4\pm 0.63(stat.)\pm 0.09(syst.))\times 10^{-10}~\mathrm{MeV}^{-1}$ for a next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^{2}$LO) in large-$N_{C}$ low energy constant (LEC). Using the large-$N_{C}$ hierarchy of LECs the sizes of the leading order (LO) in large-$N_{C}$ LECs are estimated using an experimental bound on a parity violating asymmetry in $\vec{p}d$ scattering at $E_{\mathrm{lab}}=15$ MeV and a measurement of $\vec{pp}$ scattering at $E_{\mathrm{lab}}=13.6$ MeV. Comparing the size of the resulting LO in large-$N_{C}$ LECs to the N$^{2}$LO in large-$N_{C}$ LEC $g_{4}^{(N_{C}^{-1})}$ shows they are roughly the same size in contradiction with current large-$N_{C}$ counting.

## Key claim

This work demonstrates that a next-to-leading order (NLO) parity-violating three-body force is necessary in pionless effective field theory, contradicting a previous claim by Griesshammer and Schindler (2010) that no such force appears up to NLO; the earlier argument relied on Fierz rearrangements that are not invariant under the required spin-isospin structure.

## Method

The parity-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction in the three-nucleon system was analyzed using pionless effective field theory (EFT(/π)), with coupled integral equations for the parity-violating scattering amplitude projected into spin, isospin, and angular momentum channels. The asymptotic behavior of the scattering amplitudes was examined in the Wigner basis to determine the necessity of three-body forces, and three-body P to D-wave transitions were included to extend calculations to higher energies than previously considered. The large-N_C hierarchy of low-energy constants was applied to reassess existing parity-violating few-body measurements.

## Key numbers

- The NPDGamma collaboration measurement of the photon asymmetry in n⃗p → dγ gives A_γ = (-3.0 ± 1.4(stat.) ± 0.2(sys.)) × 10^{-8}
- From the NPDGamma measurement, the N^2LO large-N_C low-energy constant g_4^{(N_C^{-1})} = (-1.4 ± 0.63(stat.) ± 0.09(syst.)) × 10^{-10} MeV^{-1}
- The longitudinal asymmetry in p⃗p scattering at E_lab = 13.6 MeV is A_L^{p⃗p} = (-0.93 ± 0.20 ± 0.05) × 10^{-7}
- The longitudinal asymmetry in p⃗p scattering at E_lab = 15 MeV is A_L^{p⃗p} = (-1.7 ± 0.8) × 10^{-7}
- The experimental bound on the parity-violating asymmetry in p⃗d scattering at E_lab = 15 MeV was used to estimate LO large-N_C low-energy constants
- The deuteron binding momentum γ_t = 45.7025 MeV and the ^1S_0 virtual bound state binding momentum γ_s = -7.890 MeV
- The doublet S-wave nd scattering length a_nd = 0.65 fm was used to fit the three-body forces

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT treatment of parity violation in few-nucleon systems by Griesshammer and Schindler (2010) and Vanasse (2011), correcting the earlier claim that no NLO three-body force is needed. The large-N_C analysis builds on the hierarchy established by Phillips et al. (2014) and Schindler et al. (2015), and the results contradict the expected large-N_C counting when comparing LO and N^2LO low-energy constants. The findings enable improved theoretical calculations for ongoing and proposed parity-violation experiments, including the photodisintegration asymmetry dγ⃗ → np that could be measured at the High Intensity Gamma Ray Source at Triangle Universities Nuclear Laboratory.
