# Influence of Small Impurities on Low-Energy Electron Dynamics in Two-Dimensional Microscopic Bounded Region

> In order to give some insight into a role of small impurities on the electron motion in microscopic devices, we examine from a general viewpoint, the effect of small obstacles on a particle motion at low energy inside microscopic bounded regions. It will be shown that the obstacles disturb the electron motion only if they are weakly attractive.

## Key claim

Small impurities in two-dimensional microscopic bounded regions disturb low-energy electron motion only if they are weakly attractive; the effect is negligible for both strongly repulsive and strongly attractive impurities.

## Method

The authors first construct an exactly solvable model using self-adjoint extension theory to handle a pointlike impurity in a two-dimensional bounded region with Dirichlet boundary conditions, deriving the transition matrix and eigenvalue condition. They then extend this to a finite-size impurity modeled as a constant potential over a small area Ω, relating the physical coupling strength to the formal pointlike strength through a basis truncation at the energy scale set by the impurity size. The condition for strong coupling is derived analytically and confirmed by numerical diagonalization of the Schrödinger equation in a rectangular domain with a small rectangular impurity.

## Key numbers

- The average level density of the two-dimensional system is ρ_av = M S / 2π, where M is the particle mass and S is the area of the bounded region.
- The strong coupling condition for a finite-size impurity is |1/(U₁ M Ω) – (1/2π) ln(ω M Ω)| < π/4, where U₁ is the potential height, Ω is the impurity area, and ω is the electron energy.
- The width Δ of the strong coupling region is Δ ≃ πM/2.
- Numerical test uses a rectangular outer region with side lengths (l_x, l_y) = (π/3, 3/π), particle mass M = 2π (giving ρ_av = 1), and a small rectangular impurity of area Ω = 1/900 with side lengths (3.53830 × 10⁻², 3.14023 × 10⁻²).
- The unperturbed energies between which the tested eigenstates lie are E_{1,2} = 4.16 and E_{2,2} = 6.31.
- Strong coupling is demonstrated for weak attraction with v₁ = –0.25 (where v₁ = U₁Ω), while strong repulsion (v₁ = 10) and strong attraction (v₁ = –3.33) produce negligible wave function mixing.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies (Shigehara 1994, Shigehara and Cheon 1996, Shigehara and Mizoguchi 1998) on pointlike and finite-size impurities in two-dimensional quantum systems, providing a general quantitative condition for when small impurities significantly affect electron dynamics. The result contradicts the naive expectation that stronger impurities always produce larger effects; instead, only weakly attractive impurities cause substantial wave function mixing at low energies. The findings are relevant for understanding electron transport in microscopic devices such as single-electron memories, where small impurities are unavoidable, and enable predictions of which impurity strengths will most strongly distort device performance.
