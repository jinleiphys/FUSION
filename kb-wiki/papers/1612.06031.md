# Nucleon spin structure studies at Jefferson Lab

> We report on the low and moderate Q^2 nucleon spin structure measurements done at Jefferson Lab, examining specifically the inclusive program. We discuss what the data teach us about quark confinement and the emergence of the effective hadronic degrees of freedom from the fundamental partonic ones. We show how this experimental program has reached its goal by providing a precise mapping at low, intermediate and moderately high Q^2 which has followed in many advances, e.g., with Chiral Perturbation Theory. Another example of a recent advance imputable to the JLab spin data is the improved understanding of α_s at low energy, which allowed Light-Front Holographic QCD, an approximation to non-perturbative QCD, to derive the hadron spectrum from Λ_s.

# Nucleon spin structure studies at Jefferson Lab

## Key claim

The Jefferson Lab inclusive spin program has provided a precise mapping of nucleon spin structure at low, intermediate, and moderately high Q² that has enabled advances in non-perturbative QCD, including the extraction of the QCD coupling α_s at low energy and the derivation of the hadron spectrum from Λ_s via Light-Front Holographic QCD, but the LFHQCD result is a semiclassical approximation that incorporates basic aspects of non-perturbative QCD dynamics, not a full solution of QCD itself.

## Method

The paper reports on inclusive polarized lepton scattering experiments at Jefferson Lab using the 6 GeV accelerator, where only the scattered lepton is detected. The first moment of the spin structure function g₁, defined as Γ₁ = ∫₀¹ g₁(x,Q²)dx, was measured for the proton, neutron (from deuterium and ³He targets), and the neutron-proton difference (Bjorken sum) over a Q² range spanning from very low values (≈0.01 GeV²) up to moderately high values (few GeV²). The data were used to extract the effective charge α_{g₁} via the Bjorken sum rule, and this coupling was compared to the LFHQCD prediction α_s(Q²) = π exp(-Q²/4κ²) with κ determined from hadron masses.

## Key numbers

- The twist-4 element f₂^{p-n} extracted from Bjorken sum measurements at Q²=1 GeV² has value f₂^{p-n} = -0.064(35), compared to the leading twist value of the Bjorken sum Γ₁^{p-n, LT} = 0.137(13)
- The LFHQCD prediction for the strong coupling is α_s(Q²) = π exp(-Q²/4κ²), with κ = M_ρ/√2
- The leading-order analytical relation between Λ_{MS-bar} and M_ρ is Λ_{MS-bar} = M_ρ e^{-a}/√a, where a ≈ 0.55 for n_f=3 quark flavors
- At N³LO, the numerical relation is Λ_{MS-bar} = 0.440 M_ρ
- The extracted value Λ_{MS-bar}^{n_f=3} = 0.339(19) GeV agrees with the world average of 0.339(17) GeV

## Context

This work extends the spin structure measurements previously performed at CERN, SLAC, and DESY high-energy facilities by providing high-precision data at lower Q² values that bridge the hadronic and partonic domains. The data have motivated improvements in Chiral Perturbation Theory calculations (particularly addressing the δ_{LT} puzzle), improvements in pQCD techniques such as Analytic or Massive Perturbation Theories, and the calculation of α_s using the AdS/CFT duality approach. The overlap of LFHQCD and pQCD applicability near Q² ≈ 1 GeV² allows the analytical determination of the hadron spectrum from Λ_s, representing progress toward understanding how hadronic degrees of freedom emerge from fundamental partonic ones.
