# Self-consistent quantum effects in the quark meson coupling model

> We derive the equation of state of nuclear matter including vacuum polarization effects arising from the nucleons and the sigma mesons in the quark-meson coupling model which incorporates explicitly quark degrees of freedom with quark coupled to the scalar and vector mesons. This leads to a softer equation of state for nuclear matter giving a lower value of incompressibility than would be reached without quantum effects. The {\it in-medium} nucleon and sigma meson masses are also calculated in a self-consistent manner.

# Self-consistent quantum effects in the quark meson coupling model

## Key claim

Including vacuum polarization effects from both nucleons and sigma mesons within the quark-meson coupling model produces a softer equation of state for nuclear matter than the mean-field treatment, but the softening is conditional on the value of the quartic sigma self-coupling lambda_R; higher lambda_R yields a further softening, while the effective nucleon mass at saturation actually increases relative to the mean-field QMC result.

## Method

The authors start from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, in which nucleons are treated as static MIT bags containing quarks that couple to scalar (sigma) and vector (omega) mesons. They add vacuum polarization corrections from the nucleon fields via renormalized one-loop diagrams (the relativistic Hartree approximation) and from the sigma meson field via a self-consistent treatment that includes a quartic scalar self-interaction and multi-loop effects, solving the resulting gap equation for the sigma meson mass. The coupling constants g_sigma^q and g_omega are fitted to reproduce saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter for given values of the renormalized sigma mass m_R and quartic coupling lambda_R.

## Key numbers

- Effective nucleon mass at saturation: M* = 0.817 M for lambda_R = 3.0, and M* = 0.83 M for lambda_R = 4.5; compared to M* = 0.775 M in the normal QMC model and M* = 0.793 M in the relativistic Hartree approximation within QMC.
- The incompressibility is lowered (softer equation of state) when quantum effects are included, with the softening increasing for higher lambda_R.
- The in-medium sigma meson mass M_sigma increases with density for positive lambda_R, with a larger increase for higher lambda_R.

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model (QMC, originally formulated by Guichon and collaborators) by incorporating vacuum polarization corrections previously applied in Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD, Walecka 1974) and in the work of Chin (1977) and Mishustin et al. The approach is similar to the self-consistent treatment of scalar field fluctuations in QHD by Bielich et al. (BCB). The results suggest that quantum fluctuations may prevent both chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement at high densities, which the authors indicate will be studied further in a subsequent work examining the role of lambda_R on chiral symmetry behavior.
