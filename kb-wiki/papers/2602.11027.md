# Strong potential in a box for applications to femtoscopy

> Understanding the short-range nucleon-nucleon interaction is essential for the interpretation of correlation femtoscopy measurements in high-energy hadronic and nuclear collisions. We present an analytical treatment of the strong interaction in two-nucleon systems by modelling it with a square-well potential and solving the Schroedinger equation in the presence of the Coulomb interaction. The resulting pair wave function is regular at small relative distances and allows for the inclusion of multiple partial waves. We apply this framework to proton-proton femtoscopy and compute theoretical correlation functions for realistic source sizes. We demonstrate that the commonly used Lednicky-Lyuboshits asymptotic approximation overestimates the correlation signal for small sources. Comparisons with numerical calculations using the CATS framework and the Argonne v18 potential show good agreement within current experimental uncertainties. The proposed analytical approach provides a practical and flexible tool for femtoscopic analyses of nucleon and baryon pairs.

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytical solution for the two-nucleon wave function using a square-well strong potential plus Coulomb interaction, and claims that the commonly used Lednicky-Lyuboshits asymptotic approximation overestimates the correlation signal for small source sizes, but the authors qualify that the discrepancy may be too small to be significant for current experimental data given that total uncertainties in measured proton-proton correlation functions range from approximately 6.7% at low relative momentum to approximately 3.1% at higher relative momentum.

## Method

The authors model the short-range strong interaction as a square-well potential with different widths and depths for different partial waves, solve the radial Schroedinger equation analytically in the presence of the Coulomb interaction, and match the interior and exterior solutions at the square-well boundary. They then compute proton-proton femtoscopic correlation functions using the Koonin-Pratt formula with Gaussian source profiles for effective source sizes of 1 fm, 2 fm, and 4 fm, and compare their results against the Lednicky-Lyuboshits model and against numerical calculations using the CATS framework with the Argonne v18 potential.

## Key numbers

- The Lednicky-Lyuboshits model overestimates the correlation function compared to the square-well model, with the overestimation more pronounced for smaller sources (1 fm effective source size shows the largest deviation).
- Including p-wave contributions in addition to s-wave changes the correlation function by up to 6% for an effective source size of 1 fm in the momentum region 100 < k < 180 MeV/c.
- The discrepancy between the square-well model and the CATS calculation with Argonne v18 is up to approximately 4% for an effective source size of 1 fm.
- Total uncertainties of the proton pair correlation function reported in ALICE (2018) range from approximately 6.7% at k* = 22 MeV/c to approximately 3.1% at k* = 98 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the analytical treatment of nucleon-nucleon femtoscopy beyond the Lednicky-Lyuboshits model (Lednicky and Lyuboshits, 1981, 2005), which is limited to s-wave and has a singular wave function at small relative distances. The authors compare their results with numerical calculations from the CATS framework (Mihaylov, 2018) using the Argonne v18 potential (Wiringa, 1995), which has been used in recent femtoscopic analyses by ALICE (2018, 2025). The analytical approach enables future applications to systems where numerical solutions are computationally demanding, such as correlations involving rare baryons, and could allow fitting measured correlation functions to extract effective strong potential parameters.
