# Time Reversal Violation in Light Nuclei

> Time Reversal Violation (TRV) interactions between quarks which appear in Standard Model (SM) and beyond-SM theories can induce TRV components in the nucleon-nucleon potential. The effects of these components can be studied by measuring the electric dipole moment (EDM) of light nuclei. In this work we present a complete derivation of the TRV nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon potential up to next-to-next-to leading order (N2LO) in a chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) framework. The TRV interaction is then used to evaluate the EDM of 2H, 3H and 3he focusing in particular on the effects of the TRV three-body force and on the calculation of the theoretical errors. In case of a measurement of the EDM of these nuclei, the result of present work would permit to determine the values of the low energy constants and to identify the source of TRV.

## Key claim

The paper derives the complete time-reversal-violating (TRV) nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon potential up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) in chiral effective field theory without assuming the chiral order of the low-energy constants, enabling source-independent study of all possible TRV sources; the contribution of the TRV three-body force to the electric dipole moments of tritium and helium-3 is found to be sizably larger than previously reported by Bsaisou et al. (2015).

## Method

The authors constructed the TRV Lagrangian terms up to order Q², including isoscalar, isovector, and isotensor components, and derived the corresponding NN and NNN potentials using time-ordered perturbation theory. The potentials were regularized with a cutoff function in the range 450–550 MeV and used to evaluate the electric dipole moments of ²H, ³H, and ³He via wave functions obtained from the strong-interaction NN potential at N4LO by Entem and Machleidt (2015) and the N2LO three-nucleon potential.

## Key numbers

- The experimental upper bound on the neutron EDM is |d_n| < 2.9 × 10⁻¹³ e fm (Baker et al., 2006)
- The experimental upper bound on the proton EDM is |d_p| < 7.9 × 10⁻¹² e fm, inferred from ¹⁹⁹Hg measurements (Griffith et al., 2009)
- The experimental upper bound on the electron EDM is |d_e| < 8.7 × 10⁻¹⁶ e fm, derived from ThO molecule measurements (Baron et al., 2014)
- Proposed storage-ring experiments aim to reach an accuracy of ~10⁻¹⁶ e fm for light-nuclei EDM measurements
- The cutoff parameter Λ for the TRV potential regularization is taken in the range 450–550 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT treatment of TRV nuclear forces beyond the NLO calculations of de Vries et al. (2011) and Bsaisou et al. (2013, 2015), which included only pion-nucleon and contact interactions, by adding the complete N2LO contributions including three-pion exchange terms and relativistic corrections. The results provide a framework for interpreting future EDM measurements of ²H, ³H, and ³He from proposed storage-ring experiments, enabling determination of the low-energy constants and identification of the TRV source.
