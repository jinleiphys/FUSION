# A new analysis of the "hep" S-factor and the "hen" cross section

> We present a new accurate analysis of the $^3$He$(p,e^+ν_e)$${}^4$He (''hep'') reaction at astrophysical energies. The S-factor is computed using a state-of-the-art method to calculate the four-nucleon scattering and bound-state wave functions (the hyperspherical harmonic expansion), and by using nuclear interactions and accompanying electroweak nuclear currents obtained within the chiral effective field theory framework. Our analysis includes a detailed examination of the theoretical uncertainties coming from two different sources: the truncation of the interaction and current chiral expansions, and the model dependence. Our recommended final theoretical value for the hep S-factor at zero energyis $S(0)=(8.7\pm 0.9)\times 10^{-20}$ keV b. We provide also the energy spectrum of the outgoing hep positrons which may be measured in future experiments. We include also an analysis of the ''sister'' reaction $^3$He$(n,γ)$${}^4$He (''hen'') at low energies, showing that the calculation well reproduce the total cross section from thermal energies to few MeV, validating our results on the hep reaction.

## Key claim

The authors compute the hep S-factor using a fully consistent chiral effective field theory (χEFT) framework for both nuclear interactions and weak currents, finding S(0) = (8.7 ± 0.9) × 10⁻²⁰ keV b, where the uncertainty includes contributions from truncation of the chiral expansion and model dependence across seven different interaction models.

## Method

The four-nucleon scattering and bound-state wave functions for the p+³He and ⁴He systems were calculated using the hyperspherical harmonic (HH) expansion method. Nuclear interactions and electroweak currents were derived within χEFT, using two families of interactions: the Entem-Machleidt-Nosyk (EMN) momentum-space potentials at orders LO through N4LO with cutoff values 450, 500, and 550 MeV, and the Norfolk (NV) local potentials at N3LO including Δ-isobar degrees of freedom. The LEC d_R appearing in the axial current at N3LO was fixed by simultaneously fitting the ³H binding energy and the Gamow-Teller matrix element of tritium β-decay. Theoretical uncertainties from chiral truncation were estimated using the Epelbaum et al. (2014) prescription, and model dependence was quantified by averaging over the seven interaction models.

## Key numbers

- S(0) = (8.7 ± 0.9) × 10⁻²⁰ keV b for the hep reaction at zero energy
- The hep reaction produces solar neutrinos with an endpoint energy of 18.8 MeV
- The hen reaction Q-value Δm = M_n + M_3 - M_4 = 20.577 MeV
- The hep reaction Q-value Δm = M_p + M_3 - M_4 = 19.287 MeV
- The expansion parameter x = Q/Λ_b ≈ 0.3, with Q = m_π and Λ_b = 500 MeV
- Experimental binding energies: B_exp(³He) = 7.72 MeV, B_exp(⁴He) = 28.30 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Marcucci et al. (2000, 2001) and Park et al. (2002), which used phenomenological AV18/UIX interactions with either phenomenological or hybrid χEFT currents, by employing fully consistent χEFT interactions and currents for the first time. The analysis simultaneously validates the hep calculation against the sister reaction ³He(n,γ)⁴He (hen), for which experimental data exist from thermal energies to a few MeV. The results provide updated predictions for the SNO+ experiment and address recommendations from the SF3 review for a new analysis of the hep process.
