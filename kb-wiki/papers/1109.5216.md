# Solar Neutrino Matter Effects Redux

> Following recent low-threshold analysis of the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory and asymmetry measurements of the BOREXINO Collaboration of the solar neutrino flux, we revisit the analysis of the matter effects in the Sun. We show that solar neutrino data constrains the mixing angle $θ_{13}$ poorly and that subdominant Standard Model effects can mimic the effects of the physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

Solar neutrino data alone constrains the mixing angle θ₁₃ poorly, and subdominant Standard Model effects (such as non-zero θ₁₃ or loop corrections) can mimic the effects of physics beyond the Standard Model, meaning that apparent non-standard interactions may be explained without invoking new physics.

## Method

The authors performed a two-flavor analysis of the solar electron-neutrino survival probability using the SNO low-threshold data (effective electron kinetic energy threshold of 3.5 MeV) and the Borexino day-night asymmetry measurement for 0.86 MeV ⁷Be neutrinos. They expanded the survival probability around Eν = 10 MeV and compared analytical expressions for the LMA and LOW regions of neutrino parameter space, then extended the analysis to include three-flavor mixing and Standard Model loop corrections parameterized as effective ε₁₁ and ε₁₂ terms in the matter potential.

## Key numbers

- SNO low-threshold analysis reports daytime survival probability coefficients: c₀ = 0.3435, c₁ and c₂ consistent with zero (energies in MeV)
- For LMA solution with cos 2θv = 0.36 and δm²₂₁ = 7.6 × 10⁻⁵ eV², the initial matter angle gives cos 2θ₀ = −0.87, corresponding to average initial density Ne ∼ 100 NA/cm³
- For LOW solution, hopping probability Phop⁰ ∼ 0.065 yields δ = 1.36, or for δm²₂₁ ∼ 8.2 × 10⁻⁸ eV², r₀ = R⊙/10.6
- SNO day-night asymmetry at Eν = 10 MeV: A = 0.0325 (consistent with zero within errors)
- Borexino day-night asymmetry for 0.86 MeV ⁷Be neutrinos: A = 0.001 ± 0.012 (stat) ± 0.007 (syst)
- SNO low-threshold analysis reports θ₁₃ = 7.2⁺²·⁰₋₂·₈ degrees (non-Gaussian errors)
- KamLAND global analysis: sin² θ₁₃ = 0.009⁺⁰·⁰¹³₋₀·₀₀₇ at 79% C.L.
- T2K: 0.03 < sin² 2θ₁₃ < 0.28 for normal hierarchy, 0.04 < sin² 2θ₁₃ < 0.34 for inverted hierarchy (both for δCP = 0, maximal θ₂₃)
- MINOS: 0 < 2 sin² 2θ₁₃ sin² θ₂₃ < 0.12 for normal hierarchy, 0 < 2 sin² 2θ₁₃ sin² θ₂₃ < 0.19 for inverted hierarchy

## Context

This work revisits solar neutrino matter effects following the SNO low-threshold analysis (Aharmim 2009) and Borexino day-night asymmetry measurement (Bellini 2011). It extends earlier analyses by Balantekin (2003), Fogli (2008), and Gonzalez-Garcia (2010) by showing that the degeneracy between LMA and LOW solutions persists in SNO data alone, and that Borexino's day-night asymmetry measurement rules out the LOW region using only solar neutrino data. The paper demonstrates that Standard Model loop corrections (Botella 1986) and non-zero θ₁₃ can mimic non-standard interactions, building on the parameterization of Friedland (2004) and the warning by Palazzo (2009) about confusion between oscillation effects and new physics. Future experiments such as SNO+ (Kraus 2010) could probe the few-MeV region where ε₁₁ effects become more visible.
