# Polarization observables in elastic electron deuteron scattering including parity and time reversal violating contributions

> The general formalism for polarization observables in elastic electron deuteron scattering is extended to incorporate parity and time reversal violating contributions. Parity violating effects arise from the interference of $γ$ and $Z$ exchange as well as from the hadronic sector via a small parity violating component in the deuteron. In addition we have allowed for time reversal invariance violating contributions in the hadronic sector. Formal expressions for the additional structure functions are derived, and their decomposition into the various multipole contributions are given explicitly.

## Key claim
The paper extends the general formalism for polarization observables in elastic electron-deuteron scattering to include parity-violating contributions from both gamma-Z interference and a small parity-violating component in the deuteron wave function, as well as time-reversal-violating contributions in the hadronic sector. The abstract does not emphasize that the formalism is restricted to the one-boson-exchange approximation and low momentum transfer where Z exchange is strongly suppressed.

## Method
The authors derive general expressions for polarization observables in elastic electron-deuteron scattering using the one-boson-exchange approximation including both virtual photon and Z exchange. They classify hadronic current contributions according to their parity and time-reversal transformation properties, decompose them into multipole expansions (charge C_L, electric E_L, and magnetic M_L multipoles), and express observables in terms of structure functions F and F-tilde that incorporate beam and target polarization dependence.

## Key numbers
- The Z exchange contribution is suppressed at low momentum transfer where $-q_\mu^2 \ll M_Z^2$ (no specific numerical bound given)
- The lepton vector and axial coupling constants are $g^e_v = -1/2 + 2\sin^2\theta_W$ and $g^e_a = 1/2$
- The Mott cross section is given by $\sigma_{\rm{Mott}} = \frac{\alpha^2 \cos^2(\theta_e^{lab}/2)}{4 \sin^4(\theta_e^{lab}/2)} \frac{k_2^{lab}}{(k_1^{lab})^3}$
- The kinematic variable $\eta = Q^2/(4M_d^2)$ appears in the kinematic functions $v_\alpha$ and $v_\alpha'$

## Context
This work extends the formalism of Gourdin and Piketty (1964) and Schildknecht (1965) for polarization observables in elastic electron-deuteron scattering, which treated parity and time-reversal invariant electromagnetic currents. It also builds on previous treatments of parity violation from weak neutral currents by Ram (1967), Raskin and Scheck (1978), and Musolf and Riska (1979), and on time-reversal violation studies by Schildknecht (1966) and Duclos and Cline (1966). The paper provides a systematic framework for future experimental searches for parity and time-reversal violation in electron-deuteron scattering, enabling identification of which polarization observables are sensitive to specific symmetry-violating multipole contributions.
