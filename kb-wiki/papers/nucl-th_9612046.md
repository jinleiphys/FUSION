# Damping mechanisms of the Delta resonance in nuclei

> The damping mechanisms of the Delta(1232) resonance in nuclei are studied by analyzing the quasi-free decay reactions 12C(pi+,pi+ p)11B and 12C(3He,t pi+ p)11B and the 2p emission reactions 12C(pi+,pp)10B and 12C(3He,t pp)10B. The coincidence cross sections are calculated within the framework of the isobar-hole model. It is found that the 2p emission process induced by the decay of the Delta resonance in the nucleus can be consistently described by a pi+rho+g' model for the Delta+N -> N+N decay interaction.

# Damping mechanisms of the Delta resonance in nuclei

## Key claim

The 2p emission process induced by the decay of the Delta(1232) resonance in the nucleus can be consistently described by a pi+rho+g' model for the Delta+N -> N+N decay interaction, but the Landau-Migdal parameter g'_NDelta must be extracted from data and lies in the range 0.25-0.35, with the zero-range Landau-Migdal term dominating over the finite-range pion and rho exchange contributions.

## Method

The authors applied the isobar-hole model to calculate coincidence cross sections for quasi-free decay reactions (12C(pi+,pi+p)11B and 12C(3He,t pi+p)11B) and 2p emission reactions (12C(pi+,pp)10B and 12C(3He,t pp)10B). The Delta+N -> N+N decay interaction was modeled using a pi+rho+g' framework with antisymmetrization of the two outgoing protons for the finite-range pion and rho exchange potentials, while the zero-range Landau-Migdal term was not antisymmetrized. Distortion effects on incoming and outgoing particles were treated using optical model wave functions, with the Delta propagation described within the isobar-hole model.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant for the pi N Delta interaction is fixed at f^2_pi N Delta / 4pi = 0.324, derived from pion-nucleon scattering data.
- For the 12C(pi+,p pi+)11B reaction at T_pi = 245 MeV, inclusion of the residual interaction V_Delta N,Delta N reduces the quasi-free decay cross section by a factor of approximately 4, and additional distortion effects on outgoing proton and pion wave functions reduce it further by a factor of approximately 3.
- For the 12C(pi+,pp)10B reaction at T_pi = 165 MeV, inclusion of the residual interaction reduces the cross section by a factor of approximately 4, and distortion effects on outgoing protons reduce it further by a factor of approximately 2.
- The Landau-Migdal parameter extracted from the 12C(3He,tpp) reaction data is g'_NDelta = 0.28, consistent with the range g'_NDelta approximately 0.25-0.35 found from the (pi+,pp) reactions.
- The minimal Landau-Migdal parameter g'_NDelta = 1/3 (in units of hbar c f_pi NN f_pi NDelta / m_pi^2 approximately 800 MeV fm^3) gives good agreement with the (pi+,pp) data.

## Context

This work extends the consistent Delta-hole model analysis previously applied to pion and photon scattering and charge exchange reactions by Koerfgen (1994), Udagawa (1990, 1994), Delorme (1991), and Oltmanns (1993). The calculations build on earlier studies of the coherent pion decay reaction 12C(3He,t pi+)12C(g.s.) by Hennino (1992, 1993) and spin observables in (p,n) reactions by Prout (1994, 1996). The extracted Landau-Migdal parameter values are consistent with those found in microscopic G-Matrix calculations by Krewald (1988) and can be directly compared with values from nuclear structure calculations by Meyer (1981) and Towner (1987). The results enable a consistent description of both inclusive and exclusive reactions involving the Delta resonance in nuclei, with the 2p emission channel providing a direct probe of the short-range Delta+N -> N+N interaction.
