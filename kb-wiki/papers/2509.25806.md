# Pre-equilibrium charm quark dynamics and their impact on D-Meson observables

> We study the impact of pre-equilibrium evolution on the charm quark $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV. We observe that there is significant diffusion in the pre-equilibrium evolution, but there is no measurable effect on the final state observables.

# Pre-equilibrium charm quark dynamics and their impact on D-Meson observables

## Key claim
While charm quarks undergo significant diffusion during the pre-equilibrium stage of Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, this pre-equilibrium evolution produces no measurable effect on the final D-meson observables $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$, contrary to earlier suggestions that heavy-quark observables could provide insight into the pre-equilibrium stage.

## Method
The authors model the bulk medium using the IP-Glasma initial state followed by relativistic viscous hydrodynamic evolution with MUSIC and hadronic afterburner UrQMD. Charm quarks are produced at binary collision positions using PYTHIA 8.2 with EPS09 nuclear parton distribution functions, and their propagation through both pre-equilibrium and hydrodynamic phases is treated via the Langevin framework within MARTINI, using drag and diffusion coefficients from lattice QCD and perturbative calculations. Hadronization of charm quarks into D-mesons and charmed baryons is performed using a hybrid coalescence plus fragmentation approach with the Duke coalescence model and Peterson fragmentation function.

## Key numbers
- Collision system: Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV
- Pre-equilibrium evolution begins at $\tau = 0.4$ fm, with charm quark positions shown at $\tau = 1.5$ fm at midrapidity
- Freezeout occurs at constant energy density of 0.18 GeV/fm$^3$
- Hadronization temperature for charm quarks: 165 MeV
- Net coalescence probability for a charm quark at rest in the medium: 0.2
- Pre-equilibrium evolution slightly modifies $R_{AA}$ in the $p_T$ range 2-6 GeV, while its effect on $v_2(p_T)$ remains negligible

## Context
This work extends the IP-Glasma + MUSIC + UrQMD + MARTINI framework (Singh 2025) to include charm quark dynamics during the pre-equilibrium phase, which had been proposed as a potential probe of early-stage bulk evolution by Das and Carrington (various years). The results contradict the expectation that strong interactions in the high-energy-density pre-equilibrium stage would leave an observable imprint on final heavy-flavor observables. The study enables future work to focus on other potential signatures of pre-equilibrium charm dynamics or to refine the simplified isotropic temperature assumption used for the pre-equilibrium phase.
