# Understanding $X(3862)$, $X(3872)$, and $X(3930)$ in a Friedrichs-model-like scheme

> We developed a Friedrichs-model-like scheme in studying the hadron resonance phenomenology and present that the hadron resonances might be regarded as the Gamow states produced by a Hamiltonian in which the bare discrete state is described by the result of usual quark potential model and the interaction part is described by the quark pair creation model. In a one-parameter calculation, the $X(3862)$, $X(3872)$, and $X(3930)$ state could be simultaneously produced with a quite good accuracy by coupling the three P-wave states, $χ_{c2}(2P)$, $χ_{c1}(2P)$, $χ_{c0}(2P)$ predicted in the Godfrey-Isgur model to the $D\bar D$, $D\bar D^{*}$, $D^*\bar D^*$ continuum states. At the same time, we predict that the $h_c(2P)$ state is at about 3902 MeV with a pole width of about 54 MeV. In this calculation, the $X(3872)$ state has a large compositeness. This scheme may shed more light on the long-standing problem about the general discrepancy between the prediction of the quark model and the observed values, and it may also provide reference for future search for the hadron resonance state.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the three observed charmonium-like states X(3862), X(3872), and X(3930) can be simultaneously reproduced with good accuracy in a one-parameter calculation by coupling the three P-wave bare states from the Godfrey-Isgur quark model to open-charm continuum channels, but the X(3872) emerges as a dynamically generated bound state with large molecular compositeness rather than as a shifted bare charmonium state.

## Method

The authors construct a Friedrichs-model Hamiltonian where the discrete bare states are the P-wave charmonium states chi_c2(2P), chi_c1(2P), and chi_c0(2P) from the Godfrey-Isgur quark model, and the continuum states are the D anti-D, D anti-D*, and D* anti-D* channels. The coupling between discrete and continuum states is modeled using the quark pair creation (QPC) model with a single adjustable parameter gamma, the quark pair production strength. The poles of the scattering amplitude are extracted by analytically continuing the eta(z) function to the unphysical Riemann sheet, and the compositeness of bound states is computed from the Friedrichs model wave function.

## Key numbers

* The chi_c2(2P) pole is found at 3920-4i MeV (Breit-Wigner mass 3920 MeV, width 10 MeV), compared to the experimental mass 3927.2 +/- 2.6 MeV and width 24 +/- 6 MeV.
* The X(3872) appears as a bound state pole at 3871-0i MeV (Breit-Wigner mass 3871 MeV, width 0 MeV), consistent with the experimental mass 3871.69 +/- 0.17 MeV and width less than 1.2 MeV.
* The chi_c0(2P) pole is found at 3878-5i MeV (Breit-Wigner mass 3878 MeV, width 11 MeV), compared to the experimental candidate X(3862) with mass 3862 (+66/-45) MeV and width 201 (+242/-149) MeV.
* The h_c(2P) state is predicted at about 3902 MeV with a pole width of about 54 MeV (Breit-Wigner mass 3895 MeV, width 37 MeV).
* For X(3872) as a bound state, the relative ratio of c anti-c to D anti-D* components is about 1:3.1 to 1:9.3 when tuning gamma to place X(3872) within 3.8710 to 3.8717 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Friedrichs model formalism previously developed by the same authors (Xiao 2016) to hadron resonance phenomenology, addressing the long-standing discrepancy between quark potential model predictions and observed charmonium masses above open-flavor thresholds. The approach contrasts with earlier dispersion relation methods combined with the QPC model (Zhou 2013) that could not obtain explicit resonance wave functions, and with pure molecular interpretations of X(3872) that struggle with radiative decays. The scheme enables future searches for the h_c(2P) state in negative C-parity channels such as eta_c gamma, and provides a general framework for incorporating hadron interaction corrections to quark model spectra for other states above open-flavor thresholds.
