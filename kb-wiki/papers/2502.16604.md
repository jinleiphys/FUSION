# Proton spin from small-$x$ with constraints from the valence quark model

> We apply the valence quark model recently calculated for polarized proton to constrain the non-perturbative initial condition for the small-$x$ helicity evolution. The remaining free parameters are constrained by performing a global analysis to the available polarized small-$x$ deep inelastic scattering data. A good description of the world data is obtained with only 8 free parameters. The model parameters are tightly constrained by the data, allowing us to predict the proton polarized structure function $g_1^p$ to be negative at small $x$. Furthermore, we obtain the small-$x$ quark and gluon spins to give a contribution $\int_{10^{-5}}^{0.1} dx \left( \frac{1}{2}ΔΣ+ ΔG \right) = 0.76 \pm 0.13$ or $1.70\pm 0.20$ to the proton spin, depending on the applied running coupling prescription.

## Key claim

The valence quark model constrains the non-perturbative initial condition for small-\(x\) helicity evolution sufficiently that the sign of the proton polarized structure function \(g_1^p\) is predicted to be negative at small \(x\), and the total small-\(x\) contribution to the proton spin is determined to be positive, though the exact magnitude depends on the running coupling prescription.

## Method

The authors apply the valence quark model of Dumitru (2024) to fix 16 of the 24 parameters in the Born-level parametrization of the polarized dipole amplitudes, leaving only 8 free constant terms. They then perform a global fit to 226 data points of polarized deep inelastic scattering and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering asymmetries from SLAC, EMC, SMC, COMPASS, and HERMES, within the kinematic range \(0.005 \leq x \leq 0.1\) and \(1.69\ \text{GeV}^2 \leq Q^2 \leq 10.4\ \text{GeV}^2\). The small-\(x\) helicity evolution equations in the large-\(N_c\) and large-\(N_f\) Veneziano limit are solved using the daughter-dipole prescription for the running coupling, with the coordinate-space coupling \(\alpha_s(r_\perp)\) following the form of Albacete (2010).

## Key numbers

* The fit yields \(\chi^2/N_{\text{pts}} = 1.26\) with \(C^2 = 1\) and \(\chi^2/N_{\text{pts}} = 1.28\) with \(C^2 = e^{-2\gamma_E} \approx 0.3\), using only 8 free parameters.
* The total small-\(x\) contribution to the proton spin, \(\int_{10^{-5}}^{0.1} dx \left( \frac{1}{2}\Delta\Sigma + \Delta G \right)\), is \(0.63 \pm 0.10\) for \(C^2 = 1\) and \(1.35 \pm 0.16\) for \(C^2 = e^{-2\gamma_E}\).
* The expectation value \(\overline{x^{-1}} = 3.64\) from the Harmonic oscillator model of Brodsky (1994) is used for the valence quark wave function.
* The infrared regulator \(\Lambda\) is set to \(1\ \text{GeV}\), and the initial evolution rapidity corresponds to \(x_0 = 0.1\).

## Context

This work extends the global analysis of Adamiak (2023), which used a flexible 24-parameter initial condition and obtained \(\chi^2/N_{\text{pts}} = 1.03\) but could not determine the sign of \(g_1^p\) at small \(x\) or the small-\(x\) spin contribution. By incorporating the valence quark model calculation of Dumitru (2024), the number of free parameters is reduced by a factor of three, yielding definite predictions for the sign of \(g_1^p\) and a positive small-\(x\) spin contribution. The results enable future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider to test these predictions and constrain the running coupling scale dependence.
