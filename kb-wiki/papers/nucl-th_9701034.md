# Hadronic Annihilation Decay Rates of P-wave Heavy Quarkonia with Both Relativistic and QCD Radiative Corrections

> Hadronic annihilation decay rates of P-wave heavy quarkonia are given to next-to-leading order in both $α_s$ and $v^2$. They include ten nonperturbative parameters, which can be rigorously defined as the matrix elements of color-singlet and color-octet operators in NRQCD. We expect these papameters will be determined from lattice calculations in future.

## Key claim
The paper provides complete expressions for hadronic annihilation decay rates of P-wave heavy quarkonia accurate to next-to-leading order in both the strong coupling constant α_s and the quark relative velocity squared v², but these expressions involve ten nonperturbative parameters that cannot yet be determined from existing experimental data and require future lattice calculations.

## Method
The author uses the Bodwin-Braaten-Lepage (BBL) factorization formalism within NRQCD, adding 4-fermion operators to the NRQCD Lagrangian to account for annihilation effects. Short-distance coefficients are computed by matching perturbative amplitudes for Q¯Q scattering in NRQCD with corresponding amplitudes in full QCD, while the long-distance matrix elements are defined as matrix elements of color-singlet and color-octet operators. The decay widths for the four P-wave states (χ₀, χ₁, χ₂, and h) are expressed in terms of these coefficients and matrix elements, with four new first-order operators (𝒫₁(³P₀), 𝒫₁(³P₂), 𝒫₈(¹S₀), and 𝒫₈(³S₁)) added compared to leading order.

## Key numbers
- For charmonium system, the estimated theoretical error from neglecting α_s²Γ terms is 9%, from v⁴Γ terms is 4%, and from α_sv²Γ terms is 6%, combining to a total theoretical error of about 12%.
- In the c¯c system, v² ≈ 0.2 and α_s(m_c) ≈ 0.3, showing these corrections are comparable in size.
- The coefficient Img₁(³P₂) is calculated to be 0 at leading order in α_s.
- The coefficient Imf₁(¹P₁) is given as (N_c²-4)C_Fα_s³/(3N_c²) × ((7π²-118)/48 - ln(μ/2m)).

## Context
This work extends the BBL factorization formalism (Bodwin, Braaten, and Lepage) for heavy quarkonia decay by adding relativistic corrections to previous nonrelativistic results including QCD radiative corrections for P-wave decay rates (Hagiwara, Barbieri, and Huang). The case of S-wave relativistic corrections had been studied by Kuang-Ta Chao. The paper enables future lattice QCD calculations to determine the ten nonperturbative parameters, which would allow strong theoretical predictions for P-wave quarkonia decay, particularly for bottomonium where the theoretical error is much smaller than for charmonium.
