# Competing quasifission and asymmetric fusion-fission in neutron-deficient sub-lead nuclei

> To disentangle the role of shell effects and dynamics, fission fragment mass distributions of $^{191}$Au, a nucleus in the newly identified island of mass asymmetric fission in the sub-lead region, have been measured down to excitation energy of $\approx$20 MeV above the fission barrier via two different entrance channels, viz. $^{16}$O+$^{175}$Lu and $^{37}$Cl+$^{154}$Sm reactions. Apart from having signature of the shell effects in both the cases, clear experimental evidence of quasifission has been observed in the mass distributions of the Cl induced reaction, that has also been substantiated by the theoretical calculations. This crucial evidence along with a systematic analysis of available experimental data has revealed that the dynamics in the entrance channel has significant influence on most of the reactions used earlier to explore the persistence of recently discovered mass asymmetry in $β$-delayed fission at low energy in this mass region, ignoring which might lead to ambiguity in interpreting the heavy-ion data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in heavy-ion induced reactions used to study mass-asymmetric fission in neutron-deficient sub-lead nuclei, the dynamics of the entrance channel (specifically quasifission) has a significant influence that was not anticipated in earlier studies; ignoring this effect can lead to ambiguity in interpreting the data, and the shell effects responsible for asymmetric fission are weaker than theoretical predictions.

## Method

Fission fragment mass distributions of $^{191}$Au were measured at excitation energies down to approximately 20 MeV above the fission barrier using two different entrance channels: $^{16}$O+$^{175}$Lu (charge product ZpZt = 568) and $^{37}$Cl+$154$Sm (ZpZt = 1054). Fragment masses were deduced using the time-of-flight difference method with position-sensitive multiwire proportional counters, and the experimental distributions were compared with predictions from the GEF code, a 4D Langevin dynamical model, and the dinuclear system (DNS) model for quasifission.

## Key numbers

* Fission fragment mass distributions of $^{191}$Au were measured down to excitation energy of approximately 20 MeV above the fission barrier.
* The mass resolution (sigma) was estimated from the elastic peak to be 2.8 u.
* The GEF code predicted 60%, 49%, and 45% asymmetric compound nuclear contributions for E*_CN = 39.6, 47.0, and 49.7 MeV, respectively, in the $^{16}$O+$^{175}$Lu system.
* The quasifission contribution in the $^{37}$Cl+$^{154}$Sm system was about 20% of the total counts at all three energies studied.
* The DNS model predicted 22% quasifission for the $^{37}$Cl+$^{154}$Sm reaction and negligibly small quasifission for the $^{16}$O+$^{175}$Lu reaction.
* For the $^{16}$O+$^{175}$Lu system, the statistical relation fit gave lambda = (2.77 +/- 0.08) x 10^{-3} and kappa = (1.23 +/- 0.24) x 10^{-6}.
* The estimated average angular momentum values for the $^{37}$Cl+$^{154}$Sm system were about 6 hbar higher than those for the $^{16}$O+$^{175}$Lu system at similar E*_CN.

## Context

This work extends studies of mass-asymmetric fission in the sub-lead region, which began with observations in $^{180}$Hg by Andreyev (2010) and multimodal fission in $^{194,196}$Po and $^{202}$Rn by Ghys (2014) using beta-delayed fission at ISOLDE-CERN. Previous heavy-ion induced fusion-fission studies of $^{179}$Au by Tripathi (2015), $^{180,190}$Hg by Nishio (2015), and $^{182}$Hg by Prasad (2015) attributed deviations from single Gaussian mass distributions to shell effects, but did not account for quasifission contributions. The present work contradicts the assumption that quasifission is negligible in reactions with ZpZt in the range 1054-1200, and enables more accurate modeling of the excitation energy dependence of shell effects in fission by disentangling compound nuclear contributions from entrance channel dynamics.
