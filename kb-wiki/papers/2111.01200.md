# Medium modification of jet shape observables in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 2.76 TeV using EPOS and JEWEL event generators

> The substructure of charged jets gets modified due to the presence of the medium in heavy-ion collisions which is an indication of redistribution of energy inside the jet cone. It helps to understand the energy loss mechanisms of jets in the medium. A model dependent study of the modification of differential jet shape $(ρ(r))$ and the angularity (g) due to medium, has been performed in the most central Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV in the jet range of 20-40 GeV/c.

## Key claim
The paper claims that EPOS-3 reproduces the experimentally observed broadening of jets at the periphery in central Pb-Pb collisions, while JEWEL with recoil off does not explain the distribution of lost energy at higher radii from the jet axis. This is a model-dependent study limited to the 20-40 GeV/c jet momentum range and the most central (0-10%) collisions.

## Method
Charged jets were reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with resolution parameters R = 0.2 and R = 0.3 in the Fastjet package, using tracks with |eta| < 0.9 and pT,min = 0.15 GeV/c. The differential jet shape rho(r) and angularity (g) were computed for 20 < pT,ch^jet < 40 GeV/c in 0-10% central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV using the EPOS-3 and JEWEL (recoil OFF) event generators, and compared to minimum bias pp results from JEWEL representing no medium effect.

## Key numbers
- Jet momentum range: 20-40 GeV/c for charged jets in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- Track selection: |eta| < 0.9 and pT,min = 0.15 GeV/c for jet reconstruction
- Jet pseudorapidity selection: |eta_jet| < 0.7 for R = 0.2 and |eta_jet| < 0.6 for R = 0.3
- Background reduction: jets required to have at least one particle with pT > 5 GeV/c
- Centrality: 0-10% most central Pb-Pb collisions

## Context
This work extends studies of jet-medium interactions in heavy-ion collisions by comparing two event generators (EPOS-3 and JEWEL) for jet shape observables at lower jet pT. The study is motivated by prior experimental observations of jet modification in central heavy-ion collisions compared to pp collisions (r8, r9). The comparison between EPOS-3 and JEWEL (recoil OFF) enables understanding of how different implementations of partonic energy loss and secondary hard-soft interactions affect jet substructure observables, particularly the radial distribution of energy inside the jet cone.
