# Bridging Quantum Computing and Nuclear Structure: Atomic Nuclei on a Trapped-Ion Quantum Computer

> We demonstrate quantum simulations of strongly correlated nuclear many-body systems on the RIKEN-Quantinuum Reimei trapped-ion quantum computer, targeting ground states of oxygen, calcium, and nickel isotopes. By combining a hard-core-boson representation of the nuclear shell model with a pair-unitary coupled-cluster doubles ansatz, we achieve sub-percent relative error in the ground-state energies compared to noise-free statevector simulations. Our approach leverages symmetry-aware state preparation and particle-number post-selection to efficiently capture pairing correlations characteristic of systems with same-species nucleons. These findings highlight the viability of high-fidelity trapped-ion platforms for nuclear physics applications and provide a foundation for scaling to more complex nuclear systems.

# Bridging Quantum Computing and Nuclear Structure: Atomic Nuclei on a Trapped-Ion Quantum Computer

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that ground-state energies of oxygen, calcium, and nickel isotopes computed on the RIKEN-Quantinuum Reimei trapped-ion quantum computer agree with noise-free statevector simulations within sub-percent relative error, but only when using a hard-core-boson representation combined with a pair-unitary coupled-cluster doubles ansatz and symmetry-aware post-selection; the approach is restricted to systems with even numbers of same-species nucleons where pairing correlations dominate.

## Method
The authors map nuclear shell-model Hamiltonians for oxygen, calcium, and nickel isotopes onto a hard-core-boson representation, reducing the number of qubits by grouping time-reversed single-particle pairs into boson-like sites. They prepare the ground state using a pair-unitary coupled-cluster doubles (pUCCD) ansatz implemented as a sequence of Givens rotation gates, optimize the variational parameters on a statevector simulator using the Adam optimizer in PennyLane, and measure energy expectation values on the trapped-ion device using basis rotations that preserve particle-number symmetry for effective post-selection.

## Key numbers
- One-qubit gate error on the Reimei device: 4.22 × 10⁻⁵ (uncertainty 5.23 × 10⁻⁶)
- Two-qubit gate error on the Reimei device: 1.39 × 10⁻³ (uncertainty 5.92 × 10⁻⁵)
- SPAM error for state 0: 2.73 × 10⁻³ (uncertainty 1.57 × 10⁻⁴)
- SPAM error for state 1: 5.25 × 10⁻³ (uncertainty 2.18 × 10⁻⁴)
- Number of qubits in HCB representation: 6 for oxygen isotopes (sd shell), 10 for calcium isotopes (pf shell), 11 for nickel isotopes (jj45 shell)
- Number of variational parameters for pUCCD: 5 for ¹⁸O and ²⁶O, 9 for ²²O, 25 for ⁵⁰Ca, 30 for ⁶⁶Ni and ⁶⁸Ni
- FCI dimension in M=0 sector: 14 for ¹⁸O and ²⁶O, 17,276 for ⁵⁰Ca, 23,884 for ⁶⁶Ni and ⁶⁸Ni

## Context
This work extends previous quantum computing studies of nuclear systems that achieved relative errors of 4-13% for ⁶Li (Kiss, 2022) and 3-13% for oxygen isotopes (Sarma, 2023) on trapped-ion hardware, and improves upon the authors' own prior work (Yoshida, 2024) that achieved sub-0.1% error for ⁶He and ¹⁸O but was limited to valence two-neutron configurations on IBM superconducting devices. The hard-core-boson mapping and pUCCD ansatz build on theoretical developments in quantum chemistry (Elfving, 2021; Google, 2023) and nuclear pairing Hamiltonians (Guzman, 2022; Zhang, 2024), and the results establish a new experimental benchmark for trapped-ion platforms in nuclear-structure calculations, enabling future scaling to more complex nuclear systems with larger valence spaces.
