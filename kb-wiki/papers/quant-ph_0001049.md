# A Generalized Jaynes-Cummings Hamiltonian and Supersymmetric Shape-Invariance

## Key claim

The paper introduces a class of shape-invariant bound-state problems that represent two-level systems, showing that the resulting coupled-channel Hamiltonians generalize the Jaynes-Cummings Hamiltonian. The generalization holds for any shape-invariant system where the parameters are related by a translation or a scaling, not only for the harmonic oscillator case that yields the standard Jaynes-Cummings model.

## Method

The authors construct an operator $\hat S = \sigma_+\hat A + \sigma_-\hat A^\dagger$ using Pauli matrices and the ladder operators $\hat A$, $\hat A^\dagger$ that satisfy the shape-invariance condition $\hat A(a_1)\hat A^\dagger(a_1) = \hat A^\dagger(a_2)\hat A(a_2) + R(a_1)$. They diagonalize $\hat S$ and $\hat S^2$ by introducing a unitary transformation operator $\hat T$ that maps parameters $a_1$ to $a_2$, then construct the Hamiltonian $\hat H = \hat S^2 + \sqrt{\hbar\Omega}\,\hat S$ whose eigenstates are two-component states built from the shape-invariant eigenstates. The formalism is then applied explicitly to the harmonic oscillator and Morse potential cases.

## Key numbers

- For the Morse potential realization, the energy eigenvalues are given by $E_m = \sqrt{V_0}\frac{\hbar\lambda}{\sqrt{2M}}(m+1)\left[2-\frac{\hbar\lambda}{\sqrt{2MV_0}}(m+2)\right] \pm \left\{\hbar\Omega\sqrt{V_0}\frac{\hbar\lambda}{\sqrt{2M}}(m+1)\left[2-\frac{\hbar\lambda}{\sqrt{2MV_0}}(m+2)\right]\right\}^{1/2}$, where $m=0,1,2,\ldots$, $V_0$ is the potential depth, $\lambda$ is the range parameter, and $M$ is the mass.
- For the harmonic oscillator case, the commutator $[\hat A,\hat A^\dagger] = \hbar\omega$ where $\omega$ is the oscillator frequency, and the Hamiltonian reduces to the standard Jaynes-Cummings Hamiltonian.
- The ground state of the generalized Hamiltonian has eigenvalue 0 and is given by $\mid \Psi_0\rangle = [0, \mid 0\rangle]^T$.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic formulation of shape invariance developed by Balantekin (1998) and Balantekin, Ribeiro, and Aleixo (1999) to coupled-channel problems representing two-level systems. It generalizes the Jaynes-Cummings Hamiltonian (Jaynes and Cummings, 1963) beyond the harmonic oscillator case to any shape-invariant potential, including the Morse potential and potentials where parameters are related by scaling (Khare and Sukhatme, 1993). The results enable future generalization to continuum problems such as alpha particle scattering (Baye, 1987), Coulomb scattering (Amado, 1988), and quantum tunneling calculations (Aleixo, Balantekin, and Ribeiro, 1999).
