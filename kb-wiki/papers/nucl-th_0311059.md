# Hadronization and final state interaction effects in semi-exclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering off nuclei

> Recent calculations of the effects of hadronization and final state interaction (FSI) in semi-exclusive deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) $A(e,e'(A-1))X$ processes are reviewed. The basic ingredient underlying these calculations, {\it viz} the time-dependent effective debris-nucleon cross section is illustrated, and some relevant results on complex nuclei and the deuteron are presented. In the latter case, particular attention is paid to the choice of the kinematics, for such a choice would in principle allow one to investigate both the structure function of a bound nucleon as well as the hadronization mechanisms. It is stressed that a planned experiment at Jlab on the process $D(e,e'p)X$ could be very useful in that respect.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the semi-exclusive deep inelastic scattering process \(A(e,e'(A-1))X\) can be an effective tool to study the initial stage of hadronization, but only if final state interaction (FSI) effects are carefully accounted for; the authors stress that a planned experiment at JLab on the deuteron process \(D(e,e'p)X\) could be very useful in this respect, provided the kinematics are chosen to either minimize FSI (to study bound nucleon structure) or maximize them (to study hadronization mechanisms).

## Method

The authors review calculations of FSI effects in semi-exclusive DIS off nuclei, using a time-dependent effective debris-nucleon cross section \(\sigma_{eff}(t)\) derived from the color string model and gluon radiation mechanisms. For complex nuclei, a Glauber-type approach is employed to compute distorted momentum distributions of the recoiling \((A-1)\) nucleus, incorporating the rescattering of the nucleon debris with the nuclear medium. For the deuteron, the distorted momentum distribution of the spectator nucleon is calculated using an S-matrix formalism that includes the debris-nucleon interaction, and the ratio \(n_D^{FSI}/n_D\) is evaluated as a function of spectator momentum and emission angle.

## Key numbers

* The effective debris-nucleon cross section \(\sigma_{eff}\) is calculated at \(Q^2=100\ GeV^2\) and \(x=0.2\) for complex nuclei, and at \(Q^2=5\ (GeV/c)^2\) and \(x=0.2\) for the deuteron.
* For the deuteron, the ratio \(n_D^{FSI}/n_D\) is shown for spectator momenta up to \(p_s \sim 0.2\ GeV/c\) and emission angles \(\theta_s\) from \(0^\circ\) to \(180^\circ\), with the largest FSI effects at \(\theta_s \sim 90^\circ\).
* The nuclear transparency for \(^{16}O\) is presented at \(Q^2=100\ GeV^2\), comparing results from the color string model alone versus including gluon bremsstrahlung.
* The average number of grey tracks \(<n_g>\) in semi-inclusive muon-Xenon scattering at \(490\ GeV\) was calculated using \(\sigma_{eff}\) and compared to experimental data from Adams et al.

## Context

This work extends the quark re-interaction model of Kopeliovich (2001) and the semi-exclusive process proposed by Ciofi degli Atti et al. (2000) to include detailed FSI calculations for both complex nuclei and the deuteron. It contrasts with earlier plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) calculations for the deuteron by Simula (1996) and others, and with the constant cross section approach of Wally (2001). The results enable planned JLab experiments on \(D(e,e'p)X\) to distinguish between different hadronization models and to investigate the structure function of bound nucleons, particularly the neutron, in kinematic regions where FSI are minimized.
