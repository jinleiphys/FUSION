# Dynamics of non-Gaussian fluctuations in model A

> Motivated by the experimental search for the QCD critical point we perform simulations of a stochastic field theory with purely relaxational dynamics (model A). We verify the expected dynamic scaling of correlation functions. Using a finite size scaling analysis we obtain the dynamic critical exponent $z=2.026(56)$. We investigate time dependent correlation functions of higher moments $M^n(t)$ of the order parameter $M(t)$ for $n=1,2,3,4$. We obtain dynamic scaling with the same critical exponent $z$ for all $n$, but the relaxation constant depends on $n$. We also study the relaxation of $M^n(t)$ after a quench, where the simulation is initialized in the high temperature phase, and the dynamics is studied at the critical temperature $T_c$. We find that the evolution does not follow simple scaling with the dynamic exponent $z$, and that it involves an early time rise followed by late stage relaxation.

# Dynamics of non-Gaussian fluctuations in model A

## Key claim
The dynamic critical exponent z = 2.026(56) governs not only the two-point function but also higher moments M^n(t) of the order parameter for n = 1,2,3,4, but the relaxation constant depends on n; however, after a quench from the high-temperature phase to T_c, the evolution does not follow simple scaling with z and involves an early-time rise followed by late-stage relaxation.

## Method
The authors performed simulations of a stochastic field theory with purely relaxational dynamics (model A) on a three-dimensional lattice using a Metropolis algorithm with fixed time step size. They discretized the Hamiltonian on a spatial lattice with periodic boundary conditions, set the relaxation rate Gamma = 1, and used a trial update phi_new = phi_old + sqrt(2 Delta t Gamma) xi with Gaussian noise xi, accepted with probability P = min(1, e^{-Delta H}). They determined the critical mass parameter m_c^2 via Binder cumulant crossing with the universal value U_c = 0.4658, then measured time-dependent correlation functions G_n(t) = <M^n(0) M^n(t)> for n = 1,2,3,4 and studied relaxation after a quench from T > T_c to T_c.

## Key numbers
- Dynamic critical exponent z = 2.026(56) from finite size scaling analysis of the decay time tau versus linear box size L
- Critical mass parameter m_c^2 = -2.28587(7) for lambda = 4, determined by extrapolating Binder cumulant crossings to infinite volume using critical exponents omega = 0.8303 and nu = 0.62999(5)
- Best fit for the decay time scaling gave ln f_tau(0) = -3.13 +/- 0.16, where tau = L^z f_tau(Delta T L^{1/nu})
- G_2(t) decays about five times faster than G_1(t) based on exponential fit of asymptotic decay rates

## Context
This work extends studies of critical dynamics in model A (Hohenberg and Halperin classification) to higher-order correlation functions, motivated by the experimental search for the QCD critical point in heavy ion collisions. Previous work by Stephanov (2017), Akamatsu (2018), and others focused on the two-point function, while Mukherjee (2015), Nahrgang (2018), An (2020), and Sogabe (2021) used approximations to truncate the hierarchy of correlation functions or solved dynamics in restricted geometry. The results enable more realistic modeling of non-Gaussian fluctuation observables (skewness, kurtosis) in heavy ion collisions, and the methods can be generalized to model B (conserved order parameter) or model H (coupling to conserved momentum density) which are closer to the QCD critical point universality class.
