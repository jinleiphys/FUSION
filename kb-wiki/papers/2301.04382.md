# Phase Transitions in Particle Physics -- Results and Perspectives from Lattice Quantum Chromo-Dynamics

> Phase transitions in a non-perturbative regime can be studied by ab initio Lattice Field Theory methods. The status and future research directions for LFT investigations of Quantum Chromo-Dynamics under extreme conditions are reviewed, including properties of hadrons and of the hypothesized QCD axion as inferred from QCD topology in different phases. We discuss phase transitions in strong interactions in an extended parameter space, and the possibility of model building for Dark Matter and Electro-Weak Symmetry Breaking. Methodological challenges are addressed as well, including new developments in Artificial Intelligence geared towards the identification of different phases and transitions.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the status and future directions of lattice field theory investigations of QCD under extreme conditions, covering phase transitions, hadron properties, and the QCD axion, while acknowledging that many results remain preliminary or depend on extrapolations to the chiral and continuum limits.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD simulations at finite temperature and chemical potential, using ab initio methods to study the QCD phase diagram, including the chiral crossover, the nature of the deconfinement transition, and topological properties. It also covers functional approaches (Dyson-Schwinger equations, functional renormalization group), effective field theories, and machine learning techniques for phase identification, synthesizing results from multiple collaborations (HotQCD, Wuppertal-Budapest, FASTSUM) and theoretical developments.

## Key numbers

- The chiral crossover temperature in (2+1)-flavor QCD at zero chemical potential is approximately 156-158 MeV, as reported by the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov et al., 2019) and the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi et al., 2020).
- The chiral phase transition temperature in (2+1)-flavor QCD with physical quark masses is determined to be T_c = 132^{+3}_{-6} MeV by the HotQCD collaboration (Ding et al., 2019).
- The topological susceptibility in pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory at zero temperature is approximately (191 MeV)^4, as reported by Alles, D'Elia, and Di Giacomo (1997).

## Context

This review extends previous lattice QCD reviews by Philipsen (2021) and Guenther (2021) on the QCD phase diagram, and by Fischer (2019) and Fu (2022) on functional approaches. It enables future work on the nature of the chiral transition in the limit of light quarks, the properties of the QCD axion from topological susceptibility calculations, and the exploration of conformal phases in many-flavor QCD for beyond-Standard-Model model building.
