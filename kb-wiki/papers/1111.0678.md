# Comment on `Controversy concerning the definition of quark and gluon angular momentum' by Elliot Leader (arXiv:1101.5956, PRD 83, 096012 (2011))

> It is argued by the author that the canonical form of the quark energy-momentum tensor with a partial derivative instead of the covariant derivative is the correct definition for the quark momentum and angular momentum fraction of the nucleon in covariant quantization. Although it is not manifestly gauge invariant, its matrix elements in the nucleon will be non-vanishing and are gauge invariant. We test this idea in the path-integral quantization by calculating correlation functions on the lattice with a gauge-invariant nucleon interpolation field and replacing the gauge link in the quark lattice momentum operator with unity, which corresponds to the partial derivative in the continuum. We find that the ratios of three-point to two-point functions are zero within errors for both the u and d quarks, contrary to the case without setting the gauge links to unity.

# Comment on "Controversy concerning the definition of quark and gluon angular momentum" by Elliot Leader

## Key claim

The authors test Leader's assertion that the canonical form of the quark energy-momentum tensor (using a partial derivative instead of a covariant derivative) yields non-vanishing, gauge-invariant matrix elements in the nucleon. Using lattice QCD with a gauge-invariant nucleon interpolation field, they find that when the gauge link in the lattice momentum operator is set to unity (corresponding to the partial derivative in the continuum), the ratios of three-point to two-point functions are zero within errors for both u and d quarks, contradicting Leader's claim.

## Method

The authors perform a lattice QCD calculation on a 2+1+1-flavor HISQ lattice with Wilson-clover valence fermions, using 127 configurations (each with 4 sources) on a 24^3 × 64 lattice. They compute the ratio of three-point to two-point correlation functions for the nucleon with the quark momentum operator, comparing two forms: the standard Bellinfante form with covariant derivative (gauge links included) and the canonical form with partial derivative (gauge links set to unity). The ratio is extracted as a function of operator insertion time t, with the nucleon source at t0 = 0 and sink at tf = 12.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: 24^3 × 64 with lattice spacing approximately 0.12 fm
- Pion mass: 310 MeV
- Number of configurations: 127, each with 4 sources (total 508 measurements)
- Sink timeslice: tf = 12
- For the canonical form (partial derivative, gauge links set to unity), the ratio of three-point to two-point functions is zero within errors for both u and d quarks at practically all insertion times t

## Context

This work directly tests the claim by Leader (2011) that the canonical form of the quark energy-momentum tensor, despite lacking manifest gauge invariance, yields non-vanishing gauge-invariant matrix elements in the nucleon. The result contradicts Leader's assertion and supports the standard use of the Bellinfante form with covariant derivatives, as employed in prior lattice calculations such as those by Deka et al. (2009). The finding also relates to the broader controversy over gauge-invariant decomposition of gluon angular momentum into spin and orbital components, discussed by Chen et al. (2008), Wakamatsu (2010), and Ji (2010). This lattice test provides a numerical check that can help resolve the theoretical debate about the correct operator definition for quark momentum and angular momentum fractions in QCD.
