# NACRE II: an update of the NACRE compilation of charged-particle-induced thermonuclear reaction rates for nuclei with mass number $A < 16$

> An update of the NACRE compilation [Angulo et al., Nucl. Phys. A 656 (1999) 3] is presented. This new compilation, referred to as NACRE II, reports thermonuclear reaction rates for 34 charged-particle induced, two-body exoergic reactions on nuclides with mass number $A<16$, of which fifteen are particle-transfer reactions and the rest radiative capture reactions. When compared with NACRE, NACRE II features in particular (1) the addition to the experimental data collected in NACRE of those reported later, preferentially in the major journals of the field by early 2013, and (2) the adoption of potential models as the primary tool for extrapolation to very low energies of astrophysical $S$-factors, with a systematic evaluation of uncertainties. As in NACRE, the rates are presented in tabular form for temperatures in the $10^{6}$ $\simeq\leq$ T $\leq$ $10^{10}$ K range. Along with the 'adopted' rates, their low and high limits are provided. The new rates are available in electronic form as part of the Brussels Library (BRUSLIB) of nuclear data. The NACRE II rates also supersede the previous NACRE rates in the Nuclear Network Generator (NETGEN) for astrophysics. [http://www.astro.ulb.ac.be/databases.html.]

## Key claim

NACRE II provides updated thermonuclear reaction rates for 34 charged-particle-induced two-body exoergic reactions on nuclides with mass number A < 16, using potential models as the primary tool for extrapolating astrophysical S-factors to very low energies, with the caveat that the compilation relies on experimental data taken "at face value" and omits data points that deviate very much from other measurements or that show the dramatic low-energy rise attributed to laboratory screening.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental cross section data from the original NACRE compilation and from literature published preferentially in major refereed journals by early 2013. For particle-transfer reactions (15 reactions), they used the zero-range Distorted-Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) with a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential plus surface absorption, fitting up to three adjustable potential parameters and a combined spectroscopic-factor/zero-range-strength parameter. For radiative capture reactions (19 reactions), they used a potential model (PM) with a real Woods-Saxon potential, fitting three potential parameters and a spectroscopic factor to reproduce resonances and non-resonant contributions simultaneously. The resulting Maxwellian-averaged reaction rates were tabulated for temperatures from 10^6 K to 10^10 K, with adopted values and low/high limits.

## Key numbers

- 34 two-body exoergic reactions compiled (15 particle-transfer, 19 radiative capture)
- Temperature range: 10^6 K ≤ T ≤ 10^10 K
- Cut-off energy for DWBA fit on the high-energy side normally set at E_cm ≈ 1 MeV
- Radiative capture cross sections "measured rarely below E_cm ~ 0.1 MeV"
- Rate expression constant: N_A<σv> = 3.73×10^10 μ̂^{-1/2} T_9^{-3/2} ∫ E σ(E) exp[-11.605 E/T_9] dE (with E in MeV, σ in barn)
- Sommerfeld parameter: η = 0.1575 Z_A Z_a (μ̂/E)^{1/2}

## Context

This work updates the NACRE compilation (Angulo et al., 1999) by adding experimental data reported through early 2013 and adopting potential models rather than the R-matrix method used in some other re-evaluations (Descouvemont, 2004). It complements the Monte-Carlo-based re-evaluation of reactions on nuclei with A = 14-40 (Iliadis and Coc, 2010-2011) by focusing on lighter nuclides (A < 16). The rates supersede the previous NACRE rates in the Nuclear Network Generator (NETGEN) for astrophysics and are available electronically as part of the Brussels Library (BRUSLIB).
