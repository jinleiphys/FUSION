# T864 (MiniMax): A Search for Disoriented Chiral Condensate at the Fermilab Collider

> A small test/experiment has been performed at the Fermilab Collider to measure charged particle and photon multiplicities in the forward direction, $η\approx 4.1$. The primary goal is to search for disoriented chiral condensate (DCC). The experiment and analysis methods are described, and preliminary results of the DCC search are presented.

## Key claim

The MiniMax experiment searched for disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) in forward proton-antiproton collisions at the TeVatron, but the preliminary results show no significant deviation from generic pion production; the data are consistent with at most a 10–20 percent DCC admixture, pending better understanding of systematic errors and more detailed DCC modeling.

## Method

The experiment used a 24-plane multiwire proportional chamber (MWPC) tracking telescope with a 28-element lead-scintillator electromagnetic calorimeter, placed at pseudorapidity η ≈ 4.1 in the proton hemisphere. A remotely movable lead converter (typically 1 radiation length) was inserted behind the eighth MWPC plane to allow photon conversion detection in the rear 16 planes. The analysis used bivariate normalized factorial moments of charged-track and converted-photon multiplicities, constructing robust ratios Rij that are insensitive to uncorrelated detection efficiencies; these ratios are predicted to be near unity for generic pion production and significantly smaller for pure DCC.

## Key numbers

- Trigger cross section: 35–40 mb, out of a total nondiffractive inelastic p-pbar cross section of 50 mb.
- Data acquisition rate: about 50 Hz; approximately 8 million events recorded in production running (1995–early 1996).
- Mean occupancy per wire: 10 percent to 30 percent depending on location.
- Photon conversion efficiency: about 65 percent per conversion above a gamma laboratory energy of 3 GeV; below that the efficiency drops rapidly.
- dN/dη at η = 4: about 3, consistent with smooth extrapolation of UA5 data.
- Charged-particle multiplicity distribution fit with a negative-binomial k-parameter in the neighborhood of 3.
- Measured R11 = 0.98 ± 0.01, R21 = 0.99 ± 0.02, R31 = 1.03 ± 0.04 (data); pure DCC prediction: R11 = 0.500, R21 = 0.333, R31 = 0.250; PYTHIA/GEANT simulation: R11 = 1.01 ± 0.02, R21 = 1.02 ± 0.05, R31 = 1.09 ± 0.14.

## Context

This work extends the search for disoriented chiral condensate motivated by cosmic-ray Centauro and anti-Centauro events reported by the JACEE collaboration (Lord and Iwai). The MiniMax experiment is a reduced-scope successor to the MAX proposal (rejected by Fermilab in 1992) and was designed to demonstrate forward-direction experimentation in collider mode. The results enable future DCC searches with larger acceptance detectors, though the paper notes that the characteristic coherence length of the chiral order parameter may be no larger than one or two lego units, complicating analyses for experiments with acceptance much larger than MiniMax.
