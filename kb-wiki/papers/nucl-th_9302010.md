# Determination of two-body potentials from n-body spectra

> We show how the two-body potential may be uniquely determined from n-body spectra where the hypercentral approximation is valid. We illustrate this by considering an harmonic oscillator potential which has been altered by changing the energy or normalisation constant of the ground state of the n-body system and finding how this modifies the two-body potential. It is shown that with increasing number of particles the spectrum must be known more precisely to obtain the two-body potential to the same degree of accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the two-body potential can be uniquely determined from n-body spectra when the hypercentral approximation (HCA) is valid, but that with increasing number of particles the spectrum must be known more precisely to obtain the two-body potential to the same degree of accuracy; this implies a rapid loss of information on the underlying two-particle potential with increasing system size.

## Method

The authors derive an exact inversion relation connecting the hypercentral potential (the rotationally invariant part of the sum of two-body central potentials in the hyperspherical harmonic expansion method) to the underlying two-body potential, separately for bosons and fermions. They then apply this inversion to a schematic n-body harmonic oscillator system, using supersymmetric quantum mechanics transformations to alter either the ground-state energy or the ground-state normalisation constant by small amounts, and compute the resulting changes in the reconstructed two-body potential for n = 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6 particles.

## Key numbers

- For the four-body case, the ground-state energy is shifted by ±5% and ±10% to study the induced changes in the potential.
- A reduction of the bound-state normalisation constant by 5% is applied to three-, four-, five-, and six-body systems, and the corresponding changes in the two-body potential are shown.
- The ground-state energy is lowered by 5% (without change in normalisation constant) for three-, four-, five-, and six-body systems, and the resulting changes in the two-body potential are presented.
- The hypercentral potential for the n-body harmonic oscillator has the form V = ρ²/b_n⁴, with the ground-state energy given by ε₀ = (2ℒ + 3)/b_n², where ℒ = l + L_m + (D-3)/2 and D = 3(n-1).

## Context

This work extends the inversion procedure for baryon spectra previously investigated by Leeb and Gavin (1992) and Gavin (1992) to general n-body systems, providing the first exact solution of the n-body inverse spectral problem in terms of two-body forces within the hypercentral approximation. The results show that the n-body spectrum becomes less sensitive to details of the two-body interaction with increasing particle number, which the authors relate to an intuitive understanding of the increasing difficulty of extracting the contribution of a single pair from the total interaction of n(n-1)/2 pairs accounted for only in an average way. The method is currently restricted to weakly correlated systems where the HCA is valid, leaving the extension to strongly correlated systems as a formidable open problem.
