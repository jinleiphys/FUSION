# Relativistic Pseudospin Symmetry in Nuclei

> We review recent developments that show that pseudospin symmetry is an approximate relativistic symmetry of the Dirac Hamiltonian with realistic nuclear mean field potentials.

# Relativistic Pseudospin Symmetry in Nuclei

## Key claim
Pseudospin symmetry in nuclei is an approximate relativistic symmetry of the Dirac Hamiltonian that arises when the scalar and vector potentials satisfy \(V_S \approx -V_V\), but the exact symmetry limit (\(V_S = -V_V\)) produces no bound Dirac valence states, so the symmetry must be broken for nuclei to exist; realistic mean fields nevertheless yield approximate pseudospin symmetry in both energy spectra and wave functions.

## Method
The authors review the SU(2) algebra of pseudospin generators for the Dirac Hamiltonian with external scalar and vector potentials, showing that in the symmetry limit the generators commute with the Hamiltonian. They compare relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations to experimental data for spherical nuclei (\({}^{208}\)Pb) and axially deformed nuclei, examining the radial wave functions of upper and lower Dirac components for pseudospin doublet partners. They also derive relations connecting magnetic dipole and Gamow-Teller transition strengths between pseudospin doublet states.

## Key numbers
- Pseudospin binding energy splittings in \({}^{208}\)Pb for \(\tilde{\ell}=4\) (\(0h_{9/2}-1f_{7/2}\)): experimental 1.073 MeV, RMF 2.575 MeV
- Pseudospin binding energy splittings in \({}^{208}\)Pb for \(\tilde{\ell}=3\) (\(0g_{7/2}-1d_{5/2}\)): experimental 1.791 MeV, RMF 4.333 MeV
- Pseudospin binding energy splittings in \({}^{208}\)Pb for \(\tilde{\ell}=2\) (\(1f_{5/2}-2p_{3/2}\)): experimental -0.328 MeV, RMF 0.697 MeV
- Pseudospin binding energy splittings in \({}^{208}\)Pb for \(\tilde{\ell}=1\) (\(1d_{3/2}-2s_{1/2}\)): experimental 0.351 MeV, RMF 1.247 MeV
- The anomalous magnetic moment for neutrons used in M1 transition relations: \(\mu_{A,\nu} = -1.913 \mu_0\)
- Ratio of scalar to vector self-energies from QCD sum rules: \(V_S/V_V \approx -\sigma_N/(8 m_q)\)

## Context
This review extends the foundational work on pseudospin doublets introduced by Arima and collaborators (1969) and Hecht and Adler (1969), which described near degeneracies of shell-model orbitals with quantum numbers \((n_r, \ell, j=\ell+1/2)\) and \((n_r-1, \ell+2, j=\ell+3/2)\). The authors show that pseudospin symmetry is a relativistic symmetry of the Dirac Hamiltonian, building on earlier work by Ginocchio (1997) and Leviatan (1999). The results enable predictions for M1 and Gamow-Teller transitions between pseudospin doublet states and suggest a possible connection to chiral symmetry breaking in QCD via the sigma term. Future work is needed to investigate whether pseudospin symmetry improves for neutron-rich nuclei and to explore the fundamental link to chiral symmetry.
