# Deuteron-Deuteron Correlation Function in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> A formula of the $D$-$D$ correlation function is derived. The deuterons are treated either as elementary particles or as neutron-proton bound states. In the first case the deuterons are directly emitted from a source and in the second one the deuteron formation is a final-state process simultaneous with a generation of $D$-$D$ correlation. The source radius of deuterons formed due to final-state interactions is bigger by the factor of $\sqrt{2}$ than that of directly emitted deuterons. To check how sizable is the effect we compute the $D$-$D$ correlation function taking into the Bose-Einstein statistics of deuterons, the $s$-wave scattering due to strong interaction and the Coulomb repulsion. The correlation function is shown to be sensitive to the source radius for sources which are sufficiently small with RMS radii smaller than 3.5 fm. Otherwise the correlation function is dominated by the Coulomb repulsion and weakly depends on the source radius. Measurements which can make use of our finding are discussed.

## Key claim

The source radius of deuterons formed via final-state interactions (coalescence) is larger by a factor of √2 than that of directly emitted deuterons, but this difference is only experimentally distinguishable for sources with RMS radii smaller than 3.5 fm; for larger sources the correlation function is dominated by Coulomb repulsion and becomes almost independent of the source radius.

## Method

The authors derived the deuteron-deuteron (D-D) correlation function using two formalisms: first treating deuterons as elementary particles directly emitted from a Gaussian source, and second treating deuterons as neutron-proton bound states formed at freeze-out via final-state interactions. They computed the correlation function including Bose-Einstein statistics, s-wave strong scattering with complex scattering lengths from Filikhin (2000), and Coulomb repulsion approximated by the Gamow factor, then spin-averaged over the S=0, 1, and 2 channels with weights 1/9, 3/9, and 5/9 respectively.

## Key numbers

- The source radius of deuterons formed via final-state interactions is larger by a factor of √2 (approximately 1.41) than that of directly emitted deuterons.
- The correlation function is sensitive to the source radius for sources with RMS radii smaller than 3.5 fm; beyond this the function is dominated by Coulomb repulsion.
- The Bohr radius of the deuteron-deuteron system is a_B = 28.8 fm.
- The singlet (S=0) scattering length used is a_0 = (10.2 + 0.2i) fm and the quintet (S=2) scattering length is a_2 = 7.5 fm, from Filikhin (2000).
- The deuteron mass used in calculations is m_D = 1876 MeV.
- The correlation functions were computed for source radii R_s = 1.00, 1.41, 2.00, and 2.83 fm, chosen so that each pair of neighboring curves corresponds to the √2 factor difference between direct emission and coalescence scenarios.
- The proton source radius at the LHC varies from R_s ≈ 1.1 fm in p-p collisions to R_s ≈ 4.0 fm in central Pb-Pb collisions (Adam 2015, Acharya 2020).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of the proton-deuteron correlation function (Mrowczynski 2019), where the source radius enlargement factor was √(4/3) rather than √2. The paper provides a method to distinguish between direct emission of deuterons from the fireball (as assumed in thermal models by Andronic 2017) and coalescence formation via final-state interactions, by comparing p-p, p-D, and D-D correlation functions. The results are relevant for midrapidity deuteron production at LHC energies, where the origin of light nuclei is under debate, and for relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions at a few GeV per nucleon where direct emission is suppressed.
