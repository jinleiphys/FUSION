# The Nucleon Spectral Function at Finite Temperature and the Onset of Superfluidity in Nuclear Matter

> Nucleon selfenergies and spectral functions are calculated at the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures. In particular, the behaviour of these quantities at temperatures above and close to the critical temperature for the superfluid phase transition in nuclear matter is discussed. It is shown how the singularity in the thermodynamic T-matrix at the critical temperature for superfluidity (Thouless criterion) reflects in the selfenergy and correspondingly in the spectral function. The real part of the on-shell selfenergy (optical potential) shows an anomalous behaviour for momenta near the Fermi momentum and temperatures close to the critical temperature related to the pairing singularity in the imaginary part. For comparison the selfenergy derived from the K-matrix of Brueckner theory is also calculated. It is found, that there is no pairing singularity in the imaginary part of the selfenergy in this case, which is due to the neglect of hole-hole scattering in the K-matrix. From the selfenergy the spectral function and the occupation numbers for finite temperatures are calculated.

## Key claim

The singularity in the thermodynamic T-matrix at the critical temperature for superfluidity (Thouless criterion) directly produces a corresponding singularity in the imaginary part of the nucleon selfenergy and spectral function, but this pairing singularity is absent in Brueckner theory because it neglects hole-hole scattering.

## Method

The authors calculated the retarded nucleon selfenergy and spectral function at the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter using Matsubara Green function theory within the ladder approximation, employing a quasiparticle approximation for the two-particle T-matrix. They compared these results with a finite-temperature extension of Brueckner theory (K-matrix) that uses a modified Pauli operator excluding hole-hole propagation. The bare nucleon-nucleon interaction was approximated by separable potentials (Yamaguchi rank-one and Mongan rank-two), restricted to S-waves.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for superfluidity in the \(^3\)S\(_1\)-channel at saturation density is \(T_c = 4.02\;\MeV\) for the Yamaguchi potential and \(T_c = 1.58\;\MeV\) for the Mongan potential.
- The pairing singularity in the imaginary part of the selfenergy occurs at energy \(\omega_0 = 2\mu - \epsilon_1 = 35.6\;\MeV\) for the Yamaguchi potential at saturation density.
- The chemical potential at \(T = 4.1\;\MeV\) is \(\mu = -23.5\;\MeV\) for the Yamaguchi potential at saturation density.
- The chemical potential at \(T = 1\;\MeV\) is \(\mu = -22.2\;\MeV\) for the Yamaguchi potential at saturation density.
- For the Mongan potential at saturation density, the chemical potential is \(\mu = 14.68\;\MeV\) and the pairing peak is at \(\omega_0 = 82.46\;\MeV\).

## Context

This work extends finite-temperature calculations of the nucleon selfenergy by Grangé et al. (1987), Röpke et al. (1983), and Köhler (1994) to temperatures near the superfluid phase transition, building on the authors' previous demonstration of precursor effects in the T-matrix (Alm et al., 1994). It contrasts with zero-temperature Green function calculations by Baldo et al. (1990), Köhler (1993), and Vonderfecht et al. (1993), and with Brueckner theory results, showing that the pairing singularity is a unique signature of the onset of superfluidity that is absent when hole-hole scattering is neglected. The results enable future calculations that consistently include the BCS gap below \(T_c\).
