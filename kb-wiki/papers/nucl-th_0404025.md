# Comparisons of Model Predictions of Pion Electrioproduction Amplitudes in the low $Q^2$ Region

> Pion electroproduction amplitudes predicted by three theoretical models in the $Q^2 \leq 0.5$ (GeV/c)$^2$ region are compared. The objective is to facilitate the analyses of the data from new experiments on investigating the pion cloud effects on the $γN \to Δ$ transition form factors.

# Comparisons of Model Predictions of Pion Electroproduction Amplitudes in the low Q² Region

## Key claim

The three models (Sato-Lee, Dubna-Mainz-Taiwan, and Mainz Unitary Isobar Model) agree well only on the imaginary part of the M1 multipole amplitude at the Δ resonance; their predictions for the R_SM ratio and the resonant E1+ and S1+ multipoles differ significantly in the Q² ≤ 0.5 (GeV/c)² region, with the Sato-Lee model deviating from the other two models and from existing data at Q² ≈ 0.127 (GeV/c)².

## Method

The authors compared the multipole amplitudes and the derived R_EM and R_SM ratios (electric E2 to magnetic M1, and Coulomb C2 to magnetic M1 transition ratios) for the γN → Δ transition, as predicted by the Sato-Lee (SL) dynamical model, the Dubna-Mainz-Taiwan (DMT) model, and the Mainz Unitary Isobar Model (MAID2003). The comparison was performed at the Δ resonance energy W = 1232 MeV for Q² values up to 0.5 (GeV/c)², using the imaginary parts of the multipole amplitudes M_{1+}, E_{1+}, and S_{1+} to compute the ratios.

## Key numbers

- The comparison covers the momentum transfer region Q² ≤ 0.5 (GeV/c)².
- The Δ resonance energy is taken at W = m_Δ = 1232 MeV.
- At Q² = 0, the SL model prediction for Im[S_{1+}]/Im[E_{1+}] is close to 1 (the long wavelength limit), while the DMT model approaches 2 and MAID2003 approaches 3.
- The SL model bare C2 transition strength is fixed as G_C(0) = -0.238, derived from G_E(0) = +0.025 using the long wavelength limit relation.
- A data point at Q² ≈ 0.127 (GeV/c)² from MIT-Bates and Mainz disagrees with the SL model prediction for R_SM but agrees with DMT and MAID predictions.
- The pion cloud effect is estimated to enhance the γN → Δ C2 transition by a factor of about 2 at Q² ≈ 0.2 (GeV/c)².
- Tables 1-4 list SL model multipole amplitudes at Q² = 0, 0.05, 0.1, 0.2, and 0.5 (GeV/c)² for four reaction channels (γp → π⁰p, γp → π⁺n, γn → π⁰n, γn → π⁻p), with values given in units of 10⁻³/m_π.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model comparisons initiated by Sato and Lee (2001) and the DMT collaboration (Kamalov and Yang, 1999; Kamalov et al., 2001), motivated by new experiments at MIT-Bates (Mertz et al., 2001), Mainz (Beck et al., 2000; Pospischil et al., 2001), and Jefferson Laboratory (Frolov et al., 1999; Joo et al., 2002) investigating pion cloud effects on the γN → Δ transition form factors. The comparison enables experimentalists to identify which multipole amplitudes and interference cross sections (σ_LT, σ_TT, σ'_LT) are most sensitive to the different model treatments of pion cloud effects, particularly the large discrepancies in the C2 transition predictions that can be tested by new data in the low Q² region.
