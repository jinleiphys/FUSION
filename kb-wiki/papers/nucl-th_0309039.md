# Final state interaction effects in $η$ photoproduction on two- and three-body nuclei

> The role of final state interaction in $η$ photoproduction on a deuteron as well as on three-body nuclei is investigated within few-body scattering theory. Deviation of the theory from the available experimental results is briefly discussed.

# Final state interaction effects in $\eta$ photoproduction on two- and three-body nuclei

## Key claim

Final state interaction (FSI) effects are essential for describing $\eta$ photoproduction on deuteron and $^3$He near threshold, but even after including exact three-body FSI calculations, systematic deviations from experimental data remain, suggesting that pion rescattering mechanisms (neglected in this work) may be responsible for the discrepancy.

## Method

The authors performed few-body scattering calculations using a separable representation of the driving $\eta N$ and $NN$ scattering amplitudes, reducing the three-body problem to a set of integral equations in one variable analogous to a two-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation. For the $\eta N$ interaction, an isobar ansatz including only the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance was used, with parameters adjusted to reproduce an $\eta N$ scattering length of $a_{\eta N} = (0.5 + i0.32)$ fm and to describe $\pi N \to \pi N$, $\pi^- p \to \eta n$, and $\pi N \to \pi\pi N$ processes. The $NN$ interaction was treated only in dominant $s$-wave scattering states using the BEST3 and BEST4 separable potentials.

## Key numbers

- $\eta N$ scattering length used: $a_{\eta N} = (0.5 + i0.32)$ fm, described as an approximate average of modern $\eta N$ analyses.
- For $\gamma d \to \eta X$, the three-body calculation yields a sizeable improvement over the impulse approximation but still fails to achieve quantitative agreement with the data of Kru95.
- For $^3$He$(\gamma,\eta)^3$He, the theoretical cross section shows a very rapid rise just above threshold due to a virtual state in the $\eta\,^3$He system, in contrast to the much flatter plane-wave approximation.
- The squared production amplitude for $^3$He$(\gamma,\eta)^3$He was arbitrarily normalized to the $d(p,\eta)^3$He data of May96 at $p = 0.25$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-body Faddeev-type calculations for $\eta$ production on the deuteron (Fix and Arenhoevel, 2000) and four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for $\eta\,^3$He elastic scattering (Fix and Arenhoevel, 2002). The results contradict the not-yet-published TAPS data for $^3$He$(\gamma,\eta)^3$He (Pfeiffer, 2002), which show a much stronger energy dependence than the theory predicts. The authors suggest that the energy dependence of the cross section near threshold should be universal across different entrance channels according to Migdal-Watson theory (Migdal, 1977), and note that the $d(p,\eta)^3$He data of May96 agree with this universal form, implying the TAPS data may require a new long-range production mechanism. The work enables future investigations of pion rescattering contributions, which were neglected here but may resolve the systematic discrepancies.
