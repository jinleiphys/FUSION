# On the Approximation in the Hermitian Treatment of Dyson Boson Expansion Theory

> We discuss about the Hermitian treatment of Dyson-type boson expansion theory. We show that the basic assumption of the conventional treatment does not hold in general and the method is only approximately valid. We also show that the approximation is the same order as that of truncation of the expansion usually done in the Hermitian type boson expansion theory.

# On the Approximation in the Hermitian Treatment of Dyson Boson Expansion Theory

## Key claim

The conventional Hermitian treatment of Dyson-type boson expansion theory is only approximately valid, and its basic assumption, that properly-normalized D-type basis states are proportional to ideal boson states, holds only when the norm matrix of multi-phonon states is diagonal, which is hardly expected in realistic nuclear systems. The approximation inherent in the Hermitization formula is of the same order as the truncation of the infinite expansion usually performed in Hermitian (Holstein-Primakoff-type) boson expansion theory.

## Method

The authors derive the exact relation between the D-type boson image and the Holstein-Primakoff (HP)-type boson image for any fermion operator, showing that the D-type image is related to the HP-type image by a similarity transformation involving the square root of the multi-phonon norm matrix. They then expand the norm operator in terms of a small parameter representing the non-orthonormality of multi-phonon states due to the Pauli effect, and expand the Hermitization formula to first order in this parameter to demonstrate the cancellation of first-order terms.

## Key numbers

- The norm matrix element is expressed as (Z²)ᵢⱼ = δᵢⱼ − Yᵢⱼ, where Yᵢⱼ represents the non-orthonormality of multi-phonon states and serves as a small parameter when the multi-phonon subspace is truncated.
- The expansion of the norm operator yields Z_B^{±1} = 1_B ∓ (1/2) Y_B + O(Y_B²), where Y_B is the operator form of the non-orthonormality matrix.
- The Hermitization formula produces the HP-type matrix element plus terms of O(Y_B²), meaning the first-order terms in the non-orthonormality parameter cancel exactly.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical foundation of Dyson-type boson expansion theory (BET) as developed by Dyson (1956), Holstein and Primakoff (1940), and later by Takada (1990s) who proposed the Hermitian treatment. The paper directly addresses the claims of Takada (HERMITEa, HERMITEb) that the Hermitian treatment could be used as an exact method, and contradicts the proposition of Janssen et al. (JDFJ) regarding the equivalence of boson images for number-conserving operators. The analysis enables practitioners to understand that solving both right and left eigenvalue problems remains necessary to preserve the advantage of finite expansion in D-type BET, and clarifies the relationship between the Hermitian treatment and conventional truncated HP-type calculations.
