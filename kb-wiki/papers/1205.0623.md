# Effects of four-body breakup on 6Li elastic scattering near the Coulomb barrier

> We investigate projectile breakup effects on 6Li+209Bi elastic scattering near the Coulomb barrier with the four-body version of the continuum-discretized coupled-channel method (four-body CDCC). This is the first application of four-body CDCC to 6Li elastic scattering. The elastic scattering is well described by the p+n+4He+209Bi four-body model. We propose a reasonable three-body model for describing the four-body scattering, clarifying four-body dynamics of the elastic scattering.

## Key claim

The four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channel method (four-body CDCC) applied to 6Li+209Bi elastic scattering near the Coulomb barrier reproduces measured cross sections, whereas previous three-body CDCC calculations could not. The key condition is that deuteron breakup is strongly suppressed in this scattering, so a three-body model with a single-folding deuteron-target potential (which omits deuteron breakup effects) can simulate the four-body result.

## Method

The authors performed four-body CDCC calculations treating the system as p+n+4He+209Bi, using Gaussian basis functions to discretize the continuum states of 6Li into pseudostates with total spins Iπ = 1+, 2+, and 3+ up to 20 MeV excitation energy. The internal Hamiltonian of 6Li used the Bonn-A interaction for the p-n subsystem and the KKNN interaction for nucleon-alpha subsystems. Coupled-channel equations were solved for the relative motion between 6Li and 209Bi, with nuclear optical potentials for n, p, and α interacting with 209Bi, and the Coulomb interaction approximated as e2ZLiZBi/R.

## Key numbers

- 6Li+209Bi elastic scattering at 29.9 MeV: four-body CDCC with breakup effects reproduces experimental angular distribution; three-body CDCC with deuteron optical potential UdOP underestimates the measured cross section
- 6Li+209Bi elastic scattering at 32.8 MeV: same conclusion as at 29.9 MeV
- d+209Bi elastic scattering at 12.8 MeV: three-body CDCC with deuteron breakup reproduces data; without breakup the calculation fails
- The neutron optical potential UnOP was fitted to n+209Bi elastic scattering data at 5 MeV, with parameters aV = 0.55 fm, WV = 0 MeV, and WD = 4.0 MeV
- The ground-state energy of 6Li is -1.47 MeV and the 3+-resonance state energy is 0.71 MeV in the d-α potential used

## Context

This work extends four-body CDCC, previously applied only to 6He scattering (Matsumoto 2006), to 6Li scattering for the first time. It resolves a failure of three-body CDCC calculations by Keeley (2003) that could not reproduce 6Li+209Bi elastic scattering data without normalization factors. The finding that deuteron breakup is suppressed in 6Li scattering near the Coulomb barrier, while significant in free d+209Bi scattering, clarifies the four-body dynamics and enables a simplified three-body model using a single-folding potential. The authors indicate future application to n+6Li scattering for nuclear engineering.
