# Diffractive excitation in $pp$ and $pA$ collisions at high energies

> In this paper we consider the Good - Walker approach for the diffractive excitation and updated the Miettinen -- Pumplin (MP) model for $pp/\bar{p}p$ collisions considering the recent LHC data for the total and elastic $pp$ cross sections. The behavior of the total, elastic and diffractive cross sections is analyzed and predictions for the energies of Run 3 of the LHC and those of the Cosmic Rays experiments are derived. Our results demonstrate that the MP model is able to describe the current data and that it implies that the cross section for the diffraction excitation in $pp$ collisions is almost constant in the energy range probed by the LHC and slowly decreases at higher energies. Our results indicate that the Pumplin bound is not reached at the LHC and Cosmic Ray energies. Moreover, the implications of the diffractive excitation in $pA$ collisions is discussed. In particular, the MP model, constrained by the $pp$ data, is used to derive the main quantities present in the treatment of the diffractive excitation in $pA$ collisions. Predictions for the total, elastic and diffractive $pA$ cross sections are presented considering different nuclei. We demonstrate that the effect of fluctuations decreases at larger energies and heavier nuclei. The energy dependence of the diffractive excitation cross section in $pA$ collisions is estimated for different nuclei and compared with the predictions for the proton dissociation induced by photon interactions.

## Key claim

The Miettinen-Pumplin (MP) model, updated with LHC data for total and elastic pp cross sections up to 13 TeV, predicts that the diffractive excitation cross section in pp collisions is almost constant in the LHC energy range and slowly decreases at higher energies, and that the Pumplin bound (R <= 1/2) is not reached at LHC or cosmic ray energies.

## Method

The authors updated the Miettinen-Pumplin model by fitting its two free parameters (G^2, the average number of partons, and beta, the width in impact parameter) to experimental total and elastic pp and pbar-p cross sections from 10 GeV to 13 TeV, using data from the PDG and LHC experiments. They derived analytical parametrizations for the energy dependence of G^2 and beta, then used these to compute the diffractive cross section in pp collisions and to determine the cross-section fluctuation parameter omega_sigma needed for pA collisions. For pA collisions, they applied the Good-Walker-based formalism of Heiselberg (1991) and Blaettel (1993) to compute total, elastic, and diffractive cross sections for proton-lead and proton-nitrogen collisions.

## Key numbers

- Total pp cross section at 13 TeV: 110.6 +/- 3.4 mb (experimental input)
- Elastic pp cross section at 13 TeV: 31.0 +/- 1.7 mb (experimental input)
- Predicted diffractive pp cross section at 8 TeV: 9.7 mb
- Predicted diffractive pp cross section at 14 TeV: 9.6 mb
- Predicted diffractive pp cross section at 57 TeV: 9.0 mb
- Predicted diffractive pp cross section at 95 TeV: 8.8 mb
- Predicted total pp cross section at 14 TeV: 114.1 mb
- Predicted total pp cross section at 57 TeV: 144.8 mb
- Predicted total pp cross section at 95 TeV: 157.1 mb
- Ratio R = (sigma_el + sigma_diff)/sigma_tot at LHC energies: approximately 0.35
- Fitted parameter beta(s) = 0.1809 + 0.0115 ln(s/s_0) fm^2, with s_0 = 1 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the Miettinen-Pumplin model (Miettinen, 1978) by incorporating recent LHC data from TOTEM and other experiments, updating earlier analyses by Sapeta (2004, 2005). The results for pp collisions are consistent with predictions from Fagundes (2016, 2017) for total and elastic cross sections. For pA collisions, the work applies the fluctuation formalism of Heiselberg (1991) and Blaettel (1993), and the omega_sigma parameter is compared with estimates by Guzey and Strikman (2006). The predictions enable future comparisons with LHC Run 3 data and cosmic ray experiments, and demonstrate that the black disc limit is not approached at current energies.
