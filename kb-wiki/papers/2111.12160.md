# ${\cal N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills thermodynamics from effective field theory

> The free energy density of ${\cal N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory in four space-time dimensions is derived through second order in the 't Hooft coupling $λ$ at finite temperature using effective-field theory methods. The contributions to the free energy density at this order come from the hard scale $T$ and the soft scale $\sqrtλ T$. The effects of the scale $T$ are encoded in the coefficients of an effective three-dimensional field theory that is obtained by dimensional reduction at finite temperature. The effects of the electric scale $\sqrtλ T$ are taken into account by perturbative calculations in the effective theory.

## Key claim

The free energy density of ${\cal N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory is derived through second order in the 't Hooft coupling $\lambda$ using effective field theory methods, confirming the result of Ref. [resumsuper] up to a small correction to one term at ${\cal O}(\lambda^2)$ due to an incorrect assignment of the spacetime dimension in that prior work.

## Method

The authors perform a two-step dimensional reduction: first, they use supersymmetric dimensional reduction from ten-dimensional ${\cal N}=1$ SYM to obtain the four-dimensional ${\cal N}=4$ theory; second, they apply high-temperature dimensional reduction to obtain a three-dimensional effective field theory (ESYM) for the static (zero Matsubara) modes. The hard scale $T$ contributions are encoded in the coefficients of the effective theory via matching calculations in strict perturbation theory, while the soft scale $\sqrt{\lambda} T$ contributions are computed perturbatively in the three-dimensional effective theory using dimensional regularization with the regularization by dimensional reduction (RDR) scheme.

## Key numbers

- The hard contribution to the free energy density through ${\cal O}(\lambda^2)$ is given by ${\cal F}^{\rm hard} = -d_A \frac{\pi^2 T^4}{6} \left\{ 1 - \frac{3}{2} \frac{\lambda}{\pi^2} + \left[ 3 \log \frac{\Lambda}{4\pi T} + \frac{39}{16} + \frac{3}{2} \gamma_E + \frac{3}{2} \frac{\zeta'(-1)}{\zeta(-1)} - \frac{1}{2} \log 2 \right] \left( \frac{\lambda}{\pi^2} \right)^2 \right\}$, where $d_A = N_c^2 - 1$ and $\Lambda$ is the factorization scale.
- The one-loop soft contribution to the free energy density is $f_{M,1} = -\frac{d_A}{12\pi} \left[ m_E^3 + 6 m_S^3 \right]$, where $m_E^2 = 8\lambda(d-2)(b_1 - f_1)$ and $m_S^2 = 8\lambda(b_1 - f_1)$ are the Debye and scalar mass parameters, respectively.
- The two-loop soft contribution after renormalization is $f_{M,2} + \delta f_E = \frac{d_A N_c}{(4\pi)^2} \left[ \frac{3}{4} + \log \frac{\Lambda}{2 m_E} \right] g_E^2 m_E^2 + \frac{d_A N_c}{(4\pi)^2} \left[ \frac{9}{2} + 6 \log \frac{\Lambda}{2 m_S} \right] g_E^2 m_S^2 + \frac{3 d_A N_c}{(4\pi)^2} h_E m_E m_S + \frac{15 d_A N_c}{2 (4\pi)^2} g_3^2 m_S^2$.
- The strong-coupling limit of the entropy density ratio for large $N_c$ is $\frac{\cal S}{{\cal S}_{\rm ideal}} = \frac{3}{4} \left[ 1 + \frac{15}{8} \zeta(3) \lambda^{-3/2} + {\cal O}(\lambda^{-3}) \right]$, from the AdS/CFT correspondence (Gubser 1998).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to ${\cal N}=4$ SYM thermodynamics previously applied through ${\cal O}(\lambda^{3/2})$ by Tytgat (2000) and through ${\cal O}(\lambda^2)$ by the resummation method of Arnold and Zhai (1994, 1995). The EFT framework provides a systematic way to separate hard ($T$) and soft ($\sqrt{\lambda} T$) scale contributions, enabling straightforward extension to higher orders in the 't Hooft coupling. The corrected result agrees with the prior calculation of Ref. [resumsuper] after accounting for a dimensional regularization issue, and the methods developed here can be applied to compute thermodynamic functions beyond ${\cal O}(\lambda^2)$ and to other supersymmetric gauge theories.
