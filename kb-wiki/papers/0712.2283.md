# Dynamical Coupled-Channels Effects on Pion Photoproduction

> The electromagnetic pion production reactions are investigated within the dynamical coupled-channels model developed in {\bf Physics Reports, 439, 193 (2007)}. The meson-baryon channels included in this study are $γN$, $πN$, $ηN$, and the $πΔ$, $ρN$ and $σN$ resonant components of the $ππN$ channel. With the hadronic parameters of the model determined in a recent study of $πN$ scattering, we show that the pion photoproduction data up to the second resonance region can be described to a very large extent by only adjusting the bare $γN \to N^*$ helicity amplitudes, while the non-resonant electromagnetic couplings are taken from previous works. It is found that the coupled-channels effects can contribute about 10 - 20 % of the production cross sections in the $Δ$ (1232) resonance region, and can drastically change the magnitude and shape of the cross sections in the second resonance region. The importance of the off-shell effects in a dynamical approach is also demonstrated. The meson cloud effects as well as the coupled-channels contributions to the $γN \to N^*$ form factors are found to be mainly in the low $Q^2$ region. For the magnetic M1 $γN \to Δ$ (1232) form factor, the results are close to that of the Sato-Lee Model. Necessary improvements to the model and future developments are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that pion photoproduction data up to the second resonance region (invariant mass W = 1.1–1.65 GeV) can be described to a very large extent by adjusting only the bare γN → N* helicity amplitudes, while keeping all hadronic parameters fixed from a prior πN scattering study and non-resonant electromagnetic couplings from previous works; however, the fit quality is limited by uncertainties in hadronic parameters associated with unstable particle channels (πΔ, ρN, σN) that were not fully constrained by πN data alone.

## Method

The dynamical coupled-channels model (MSL) solves coupled integral equations for partial-wave amplitudes including channels γN, πN, ηN, and the πΔ, ρN, σN resonant components of ππN. The hadronic parameters were fixed from a prior fit to πN scattering data (JLMS, 2007), and non-resonant electromagnetic couplings were taken from previous works; only the bare γN → N* helicity amplitudes were adjusted via χ²-fits using MINUIT to describe γp → π⁰p and γp → π⁺n differential cross sections and photon asymmetries up to W = 1.65 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Coupled-channels effects contribute about 10–20% of the production cross sections in the Δ(1232) resonance region
- Coupled-channels effects can change cross sections by as much as 50% in the second resonance region (W > 1400 MeV)
- The study covers the invariant mass range W = 1.1 GeV – 1.65 GeV
- Bare helicity amplitudes determined for 10 bare N* states (e.g., S11-1: A1/2 = 69 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²; P33-1: A1/2 = −78 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/², A3/2 = −132 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²; F15-1: A1/2 = −97 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/², A3/2 = −63 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²)
- The predicted meson cloud contributions to γN → N* form factors are mainly in the low Q² region (Q² < 1 (GeV/c)²)

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels model developed in Physics Reports, 439, 193 (2007) (the MSL model) and uses hadronic parameters from a recent πN scattering study by JLMS (2007). It contrasts with on-shell K-matrix models used by groups such as SAID, MAID, Giessen, KVI, and Bonn, which avoid explicit treatment of short-range reaction mechanisms. The results enable future analysis of pion electroproduction data in the first and second resonance regions, and provide a starting point for extracting bare γN → N* form factors that can be compared with constituent quark model predictions (Capstick, 1992) and Dyson-Schwinger Equation calculations (Roberts, 2000).
