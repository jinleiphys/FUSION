# Study of spin polarization dependence on rapidity, transverse momentum, and azimuthal angle

> We investigate the spacetime evolution of spin polarization within a hydrodynamic framework based on the de Groot--van Leeuwen--van Weert expressions for energy-momentum and spin tensors. The system's deviation from boost invariance results in the interplay of different spin polarization components, impacting spin observables. We specifically examine the transverse momentum, azimuthal angle, and rapidity dependence of the mean spin polarization vector of $Λ$ hyperons. Our results qualitatively align with other models and experimental data on global spin polarization rapidity dependence. While the quadrupole structure is absent in the longitudinal component at midrapidity, our analysis reveals non-trivial signal at forward rapidities that differs from predictions based on the Bjorken expansion.

## Key claim

The paper shows that breaking boost invariance in a hydrodynamic framework with spin leads to coupling between different spin polarization components, producing a non-trivial longitudinal spin signal at forward rapidities that differs from predictions based on the Bjorken expansion, even though the quadrupole structure is absent in the longitudinal component at midrapidity.

## Method

The authors solve relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamics for baryon current and energy-momentum conservation in (1+1)-dimensional spacetime (longitudinal proper time and space-time rapidity), assuming transverse homogeneity. They then evolve six independent components of the spin polarization tensor using the de Groot-van Leeuwen-van Weert (GLW) spin tensor conservation equations, with an initial condition where only the transverse spin component C_omega Y is non-zero, and compute the mean spin polarization vector of Lambda hyperons at freeze-out using the Cooper-Frye formalism.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamics-with-spin formalism of Florkowski (2017, 2018) beyond the boost-invariant Bjorken expansion scenario studied in Florkowski (2019), addressing discrepancies between spin-thermal model predictions and experimental data from STAR (2017, 2019) and ALICE (2019) on the transverse-momentum dependence of spin polarization. The results qualitatively align with other models and experimental data on global spin polarization rapidity dependence, and the non-trivial signal at forward rapidities offers predictions for future measurements at low and mid collision energies where the Bjorken model assumptions become inappropriate.
