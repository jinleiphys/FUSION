# Low Momentum Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions and Shell-Model Calculations

> In the last few years, the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction V-low-k derived from free-space NN potentials has been successfully used in shell-model calculations. V-low-k is a smooth potential which preserves the deuteron binding energy as well as the half-on-shell T-matrix of the original NN potential up to a momentum cutoff Lambda. In this paper we put to the test a new low-momentum NN potential derived from chiral perturbation theory at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order with a sharp low-momentum cutoff at 2.1 fm-1. Shell-model calculations for the oxygen isotopes using effective hamiltonians derived from both types of low-momentum potential are performed. We find that the two potentials show the same perturbative behavior and yield very similar results.

## Key claim

The new chiral low-momentum potential N³LOW, constructed from chiral perturbation theory at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order with a sharp cutoff at 2.1 fm⁻¹, shows the same perturbative behavior as the numerically-derived V-low-k potential and yields very similar results in shell-model calculations for oxygen isotopes, though both potentials overbind the ground states unless the single-particle spectrum is empirically shifted.

## Method

The authors constructed a new low-momentum NN potential (N³LOW) from chiral perturbation theory at N³LO with a sharp momentum cutoff at 2.1 fm⁻¹, given in analytic form. They derived shell-model effective Hamiltonians for oxygen isotopes using the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded-diagram expansion, with the Q-box computed through third order in the interaction and summed to all orders via the Lee-Suzuki iteration method. Calculations were performed for even-mass oxygen isotopes beyond the ¹⁶O core using both N³LOW and a V-low-k potential derived numerically from the "hard" N³LO chiral potential, with a harmonic oscillator parameter of ℏω = 14 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff momentum Λ = 2.1 fm⁻¹ used for both V-low-k and N³LOW potentials
- N³LOW reproduces NN phase shifts up to 200 MeV laboratory energy
- Ground-state energy of ¹⁸O relative to ¹⁶O from V-low-k at third order with N_max=16: -14.523 MeV
- Ground-state energy of ¹⁸O relative to ¹⁶O from N³LOW at third order with N_max=16: -14.049 MeV
- Difference between second- and third-order results for ¹⁸O at N_max=16: approximately 4% for V-low-k, less than 1% for N³LOW
- Experimental ground-state energy of ¹⁸O relative to ¹⁶O: -12.187 MeV
- Shift of 1.1 MeV upward in V-low-k single-particle spectrum and 1 MeV upward in N³LOW single-particle spectrum needed to reproduce experimental ¹⁸O binding energy
- Calculated absolute single-particle energy of ν0d5/2: -5.425 MeV (V-low-k), -4.909 MeV (N³LOW); experimental value: -4.144 MeV

## Context

This work extends the V-low-k approach of Bogner et al. (2001-2003) by constructing a low-momentum potential directly from chiral perturbation theory rather than by renormalizing a "hard" potential. It contrasts with the "hard" N³LO potential of Entem and Machleidt (2003) which requires a regulator at higher cutoff. The results enable future calculations with consistent chiral three-nucleon forces, which the authors suggest may resolve the overbinding problem in oxygen isotopes that has been noted by Brown (2001) and Ellis et al. (2005). The analytic form of N³LOW allows proper definition of consistent three-body forces, unlike the purely numerical V-low-k.
