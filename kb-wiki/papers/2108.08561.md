# Transport properties and equation-of-state of hot and dense QGP matter near the critical end-point in the phenomenological dynamical quasi-particle model

> We extend the effective dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) - constructed for the description of non-perturbative QCD phenomena of the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (QGP) - to large baryon chemical potentials, $μ_B$, including a critical end-point and a 1st order phase transition. The DQPM description of quarks and gluons is based on partonic propagators with complex selfenergies where the real part of the selfenergies is related to the quasiparticle mass and the imaginary part to a finite width of their spectral functions. In DQPM the determination of complex selfenergies for the partonic degrees of freedom at zero and finite $μ_B$ has been performed by adjusting the entropy density to the lQCD data. The temperature-dependent effective coupling (squared) $g^2(T/T_c)$, as well as the parton effective masses and widths are based on this adjustment. The novel extended dynamical quasiparticle model, named "DQPM-CP", makes it possible to describe thermodynamical and transport properties of quarks and gluons in a wide range of $T$ and $μ_B$, and reproduces the equation-of-state (EoS) of lQCD calculations in the crossover region of finite $T, μ_B$. We apply a scaling ansatz for the strong coupling constant near the CEP, located at ($T^{CEP}$, $μ^{CEP}_B) = (0.100, 0.960)$ GeV. We show the EoS as well as the speed of sound for $T>T_c$ and for a wide range of $μ_B$, which can be of interest for hydrodynamical simulations. Furthermore, we consider two settings for the strange quark chemical potentials (I) $μ_s=μ_B/3$ and (II) $μ_s=0$. The isentropic trajectories of the QGP matter are compared for these two cases. The phase diagram of DQPM-CP is close to PNJL calculations. The leading order pQCD transport coefficients of both approaches differ. This elucidates that the knowledge of the phase diagram alone is not sufficient to describe the dynamical evolution of QGP.

## Key claim

The paper extends the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) to large baryon chemical potentials by introducing a scaling ansatz for the strong coupling constant near a hypothetical critical end-point (CEP) located at (T^CEP, μ_B^CEP) = (0.100, 0.960) GeV, but the authors explicitly note that the exact location of the CEP remains an open question and that their results are model-dependent, providing only qualitative predictions.

## Method

The authors generalize the DQPM by parametrizing the effective coupling constant α_S as a function of scaled temperature T/T_c(μ_B) with an additional factor σ(μ_B) to account for the decrease of coupling with baryon chemical potential, and add a "critical" term α_S^crit near the CEP to describe second-order phase transition behavior. The quasiparticle pole masses and widths for gluons and quarks are computed from Eqs. (polemass_g_dqpm) and (polemass_q_dqpm) using the temperature- and μ_B-dependent coupling, and thermodynamic observables (entropy density, pressure, energy density) are derived from the quasiparticle propagators in a Φ-derivable two-loop approximation. The model is adjusted by fitting the entropy density to lattice QCD data at finite μ_B from the BMW collaboration and to PNJL model results near the CEP.

## Key numbers

- CEP location: (T^CEP, μ_B^CEP) = (0.100, 0.960) GeV
- Critical temperature at zero chemical potential: T_c(0) = 0.158 GeV
- Critical line curvature parameter: κ_PNJL = 0.00989
- DQPM-CP coupling constant coefficients (at μ_B=0): a_0 = 0.25, a_2 = 1.77, a_3 = 2.17, a_4 = 2.13, a_6 = 0.85
- σ(μ_B) parameters: σ_2 = 0.45 GeV^{-2}, σ_4 = 0.15 GeV^{-4}
- Smoothing function width: δT = 0.002 GeV
- Ratio m_i/γ_i at μ_B=0: approximately 9 for quarks and 6 for gluons; at μ_B=1 GeV: approximately 16 for quarks and 11 for gluons
- Strange quark mass offset: Δm ≈ 30 MeV

## Context

This work extends the DQPM framework (Peshier 2005, Cassing 2007, Moreau 2019) to include a critical end-point and first-order phase transition, building on the scaling hypothesis introduced by Cassing (2007) for finite baryon chemical potential. The model reproduces lattice QCD results from the BMW collaboration (Borsanyi 2012, 2013) at zero and moderate μ_B, and its phase diagram is close to PNJL model calculations (Fuseau 2019). The results are intended for use in hydrodynamical simulations and for implementation in the PHSD transport approach (Cassing 2009, Moreau 2019), enabling studies of QGP evolution in heavy-ion collisions at moderate and high baryon densities.
