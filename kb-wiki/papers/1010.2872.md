# Proton Timelike Form Factors Near Threshold via Antiproton-Nucleus Electromagnetic Annihilation

> In the reaction of the antiproton-deuteron electromagnetic annihilation pbar + d --> e+e-n the value of the invariant mass M of the (e+e-) pair can be near or below the (pbar p) mass even for enough high momentum of incident antiproton. This allows to access the proton electromagnetic form factors in the time-like region of q^2 near the (pbar p) threshold. We estimate the cross section dsigma(pbar + d --> e+e-n)/dM for an antiproton beam momentum of 1.5 GeV/c. We find that near the (pbar p) threshold this cross section is about 1 pb/MeV. The case of heavy nuclei target is also discussed. Elements of experimental feasibility are presented for the process pbar + d --> e+e-n in the context of the Panda project. We conclude that this process has a chance to be measurable at Panda.

## Key claim

The reaction \(\bar{p} + d \to e^+ e^- n\) can access proton electromagnetic form factors in the timelike region near and below the \(p\bar{p}\) threshold (\(q^2 < 4m^2\)), a domain otherwise kinematically forbidden in free \(p\bar{p}\) annihilation, because the off-shellness of the proton in the nucleus allows the invariant mass of the \(e^+e^-\) pair to reach near-threshold values even with a fast antiproton beam.

## Method

The cross section \(d\sigma(\bar{p} + d \to e^+ e^- n)/dM_{e^+e^-}\) was calculated in the impulse approximation, where the total amplitude squared is proportional to the free \(\bar{p}p \to e^+e^-\) annihilation amplitude squared and to the square of the deuteron wave function (including S- and D-waves). The calculation used an antiproton beam momentum of 1.5 GeV/c and a deuteron target at rest, with the proton form factor omitted for the nuclear effect estimate and later included via interpolation of measured data from Aubert (2005). For heavier nuclei (\({}^{12}\)C, \({}^{56}\)Fe, \({}^{197}\)Au), the deuteron momentum distribution was replaced by nuclear momentum distributions from Antonov (2004, 2006), multiplied by the number of protons \(Z\), and absorption was estimated in the Glauber approach.

## Key numbers

* Differential cross section at the \(p\bar{p}\) threshold (\(M_{e^+e^-} = 2m = 1880\) MeV) for a deuteron target: \(d\sigma/dM = 1\) pb/MeV, for an antiproton beam momentum of 1.5 GeV/c.
* Integrated cross section over a 100 MeV bin centered on the threshold (1830–1930 MeV) for deuteron: approximately 100 pb.
* Total cross section for \(\bar{p}d \to e^+e^- n\) integrated over all \(M_{e^+e^-}\), without form factors: 43 nb; with form factors included (from Aubert 2005 data): approximately 1 nb.
* For heavy nuclei near threshold (\(M_{e^+e^-} = 1880\) MeV): \(d\sigma/dM \approx 6.5 \times Z\) pb/MeV, giving 39 pb for \({}^{12}\)C (\(Z=6\)), 0.17 nb for \({}^{56}\)Fe (\(Z=26\)), and 0.5 nb for \({}^{197}\)Au (\(Z=79\)) over a 1 MeV interval; absorption reduces these by a factor of 2.
* The minimal proton momentum in the deuteron needed to reach the \(p\bar{p}\) threshold is \(k_{\text{min}} \approx 360\) MeV/c, corresponding to an off-shell proton effective mass \(m^* \le 0.85 m\).

## Context

This work revives the idea of Dalkarov (1980) to use a nuclear target for accessing under-threshold timelike form factors, now in the context of the PANDA project at FAIR-GSI. It extends the authors' earlier study (Karmanov and Fonvieille, 2009) and contrasts with direct \(p\bar{p} \to e^+e^-\) experiments at LEAR (Bardin, 1994) and \(e^+e^- \to p\bar{p}\) at BaBar (Aubert, 2005), which cannot reach below the \(p\bar{p}\) threshold. The results enable future experimental searches for predicted \(N\bar{N}\) quasinuclear bound states (Shapiro, 1978; Dalkarov, 1989) and for \(\Lambda\bar{\Lambda}\) threshold structures (Carbonell, 1993) in the unexplored under-threshold region.
