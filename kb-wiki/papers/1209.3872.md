# Electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon in effective field theory

> We calculate the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon to third chiral order in manifestly Lorentz-invariant effective field theory. The rho and omega mesons as well as the Delta(1232) resonance are included as explicit dynamical degrees of freedom. To obtain a self-consistent theory with respect to constraints we consider the proper relations among the couplings of the effective Lagrangian. For the purpose of generating a systematic power counting, the extended on-mass-shell renormalization scheme is applied in combination with the small-scale expansion. The results for the electric and magnetic Sachs form factors are analyzed in terms of experimental data and compared to previous findings in the framework of chiral perturbation theory. The pion-mass dependence of the form factors is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper calculates nucleon electromagnetic form factors to third chiral order using a manifestly Lorentz-invariant effective field theory that includes the rho and omega mesons and the Delta(1232) resonance as explicit dynamical degrees of freedom, but the quality of the fit to experimental data depends on the range of momentum transfer and on whether the Delta resonance is included; fits including the Delta show worse reduced chi-square values as momentum transfer increases compared to fits with only vector mesons.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian including pions, nucleons, rho and omega mesons, and the Delta(1232) resonance, with coupling constants constrained by requirements for a self-consistent theory with respect to constraints and perturbative renormalizability. They apply the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme combined with the small-scale expansion to generate a systematic power counting, then compute all Feynman diagrams up to and including order q^3. The renormalized low-energy constants are fixed by fitting the four Sachs form factors to experimental proton cross section data from MAMI and neutron form factor data from multiple experiments.

## Key numbers

* Proton anomalous magnetic moment: F_2^p(0) = 1.793; neutron anomalous magnetic moment: F_2^n(0) = -1.913
* Pion-decay constant: F_pi = 92.2 MeV; axial-vector coupling constant: g_A = 1.27
* Rho-meson coupling constant from KSRF relation: g = 5.93; Delta-N coupling constant: g = 1.13
* Fitted low-energy constants for the calculation with Delta, rho, omega at Q^2_max = 0.2 GeV^2: g_omega = -1.06, f_omega * g_omega = -0.106, G_rho = -4.84 GeV^{-1}, d_6 = 1.67 GeV^{-2}, d_7 = -0.282 GeV^{-2}, d_x = -0.506, with reduced chi-square = 1.50
* Fitted low-energy constants for the calculation with only rho, omega at Q^2_max = 0.2 GeV^2: g_omega = 5.13, f_omega * g_omega = 0.513, G_rho = -16.90 GeV^{-1}, d_6 = 0.629 GeV^{-2}, d_7 = 0.0909 GeV^{-2}, d_x = -0.134, with reduced chi-square = 1.45
* Pion loop contributions to isovector anomalous magnetic moment: expanded value 1.98, full value 1.35; pion-Delta loop contributions: expanded value -0.222, full value -0.150

## Context

This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory calculations of nucleon form factors, which typically described data only for Q^2 less than or equal to 0.1 GeV^2, by including vector mesons and the Delta resonance as explicit degrees of freedom to improve the description at higher momentum transfer. It builds on the manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon ChPT calculations of Kubis (2000) and Fuchs (2003) up to O(q^4), and the O(q^3) calculation including the Delta by Ledwig (2011), while incorporating the consistency conditions for vector meson Lagrangians derived by Djukanovic (2004, 2010). The approach enables a controlled, order-by-order calculation that can describe form factors up to intermediate Q^2 values, though the Delta resonance inclusion does not improve the fit quality at higher Q^2 compared to the vector-meson-only calculation.
