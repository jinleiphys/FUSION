# Effects of nuclear deformation and neutron transfer in capture process, and origin of fusion hindrance at deep sub-barrier energies

> The roles of nuclear deformation and neutron transfer in sub-barrier capture process are studied within the quantum diffusion approach. The change of the deformations of colliding nuclei with neutron exchange can crucially influence the sub-barrier fusion. The comparison of the calculated capture cross section and the measured fusion cross section in various reactions at extreme sub- barrier energies gives us information about the fusion and quasifission.

# Effects of nuclear deformation and neutron transfer in capture process, and origin of fusion hindrance at deep sub-barrier energies

## Key claim
The observed sub-barrier capture enhancement is mainly related to quadrupole deformation of the colliding nuclei and to two-neutron transfer with large positive Q-value, but only when the neutron transfer changes the deformations of the interacting nuclei; if deformations do not change or decrease after transfer, the neutron transfer weakly influences or even suppresses the capture process.

## Method
The quantum diffusion approach, treating the relative distance between colliding nuclei as a single collective variable with fluctuation and dissipation effects, was applied to calculate capture cross sections for many heavy-ion reactions. The nucleus-nucleus potential was calculated using a double-folding formalism with Skyrme-type density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, incorporating quadrupole deformation parameters from the literature or extracted by fitting experimental data. The calculated capture cross sections were compared with measured fusion cross sections to extract information about fusion hindrance and quasifission at deep sub-barrier energies.

## Key numbers
- Quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ = 0.341 for ¹⁵⁴Sm, used in calculations for reactions ¹⁶O, ⁴⁸Ca, ⁴⁰Ar + ¹⁵⁴Sm
- Quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ = 0.2825 for ⁷⁴Ge, used in ⁷⁴Ge + ⁷⁴Ge reaction
- Two-neutron transfer Q-value Q₂ₙ = 5.7 MeV for ⁴⁰Ca + ²⁰⁸Pb reaction
- Two-neutron transfer Q-value Q₂ₙ = 5.5 MeV for ⁴⁰Ca + ⁹⁶Zr reaction
- Two-neutron transfer Q-value Q₂ₙ = 5.6 MeV for ⁵⁸Ni + ²⁰⁷Pb reaction
- Extracted β₂ = 0.30 for ²⁸Si, β₂ = 0.125 for ³⁴S, β₂ = 0.25 for ⁴⁰Ar, β₂ = 0.087 for ⁶⁴Ni, β₂ = 0.08 for ⁹⁶Zr from fitting experimental capture cross sections
- For ⁴⁰Ca + ⁴⁸Ca, ⁶⁴Ni + ⁶⁴Ni, and ³⁶S + ⁴⁸Ca,⁶⁴Ni reactions: at energies above and just below the Coulomb barrier σ_cap = σ_fus, but the difference between sub-barrier capture and fusion cross sections becomes larger with decreasing bombarding energy

## Context
This work extends the quantum diffusion approach previously applied by the same group (Sargsyan, Adamian, Antonenko, Scheid, 2009-2011) to study capture reactions above and below the Coulomb barrier. It addresses the long-standing debate about the role of neutron transfer in sub-barrier fusion, following earlier suggestions by Broglia (1980s) that two-neutron transfer with positive Q-value enhances fusion, and contrasting with Scarlassara (2000s) who found weak influence in ⁶⁰Ni + ¹⁰⁰Mo. The paper also addresses fusion hindrance at deep sub-barrier energies, first identified by Jiang (2000s), and proposes that quasifission, previously associated with massive systems studied at GSI (1990s), may also occur in medium-mass and light nuclei at deep sub-barrier energies. The results enable future experimental searches for quasifission components in mass and charge distributions at low bombarding energies.
