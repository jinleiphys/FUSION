# Spin observables of proton-deuteron elastic scattering at SPD NICA energies within the Glauber model and pN amplitudes

> A systematic analysis of nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes is available up to a laboratory energy of $3$~GeV in case of the $pp$ system and up to $1.2$ GeV for $pn$. At higher energies there is only incomplete experimental information on $pp$ elastic scattering, whereas data for the $pn$ system are very scarce. We apply the spin-dependent Glauber theory to calculate spin observables of $pd$ elastic scattering at $3$-$50$ GeV/c using $pp$ amplitudes available in the literature and parametrized within the Regge formalism. The calculated vector $A_y^p$, $A_y^d$ and tensor $A_{xx}$, $A_{yy}$ analyzing powers and the spin-correlation coefficients $C_{y,y}$, $C_{x,x}$, $C_{yy,y}$, $C_{xx,y}$ can be measured at SPD NICA and, thus, will provide a test of the used $pN$ amplitudes.

## Key claim

The authors apply spin-dependent Glauber theory to calculate spin observables of proton-deuteron elastic scattering at laboratory momenta from 3 to 50 GeV/c, using proton-proton amplitudes parametrized within the Regge formalism by Sibirtsev et al. (2010) and assuming, as a first approximation, that the proton-neutron amplitudes are identical to the proton-proton ones.

## Method

The spin-dependent Glauber theory, as developed by Platonova and Kukulin (2016) and Temerbayev et al. (2015), is used to compute the transition amplitude for proton-deuteron elastic scattering. The input proton-nucleon amplitudes are taken from the Regge-type parametrization of Sibirtsev et al. (2010) and re-expressed as sums of Gaussian functions in the squared four-momentum transfer. The authors then calculate the differential cross section, vector analyzing powers \(A_y^p\) and \(A_y^d\), tensor analyzing powers \(A_{xx}\) and \(A_{yy}\), and spin-correlation coefficients \(C_{y,y}\), \(C_{x,x}\), \(C_{yy,y}\), and \(C_{xx,y}\) at laboratory momenta of 4.68 and 45 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The Regge-type parametrization of Sibirtsev et al. (2010) covers the laboratory momentum range 3 to 50 GeV/c, corresponding to center-of-mass energies \(2.77 < \sqrt{s} < 10\) GeV.
- The Gaussian parametrization of the proton-proton amplitudes is provided explicitly for \(p_{lab} = 45\) GeV/c in Table 1, with coefficients and slope parameters for the real and imaginary parts of the amplitudes \(A_p\), \(C_p\), \(G_p\), and \(H_p\).
- The differential cross section for proton-deuteron elastic scattering is calculated at \(p_{lab} = 4.8\) GeV/c and 45 GeV/c and compared to data from Dalkhazhav et al. (1969) at 4.8 GeV/c and Beznogikh et al. (1973) at 48.9 GeV/c.
- The vector analyzing power \(A_y^p\) decreases significantly in absolute value with increasing energy from 4.8 to 45 GeV/c, while the spin correlation coefficients \(C_{x,x}\) and \(C_{y,y}\) show the opposite tendency.
- The tensor analyzing powers \(A_{xx}\) and \(A_{yy}\) depend only weakly on energy and do not change qualitatively in the forward direction if all spin-dependent amplitudes are excluded.

## Context

This work extends the application of spin-dependent Glauber theory, previously validated against proton-deuteron scattering data at energies around 1 GeV using SAID partial-wave amplitudes (Arndt et al., 2007), to the higher energy range of 3 to 50 GeV/c where experimental information on nucleon-nucleon amplitudes is incomplete. The calculations provide predictions for spin observables that can be measured at the future SPD NICA facility, enabling a test of the Regge-type parametrizations of proton-nucleon amplitudes by Sibirtsev et al. (2010) and potentially constraining the spin-dependent proton-neutron amplitudes, which are currently very poorly known.
