# Impossibility of Spin Polarized States for Neutron Star / Proto-neutron Star Matter in $β$-Equilibrium Condition

> It is shown explicitly that a ferromagnetic transition of neutron star (NS) / proto-neutron star (PNS) matter in the $β$-equilibrium condition with $σ-ω-ρ$ exchange type of mean field approximation can actually occur if and only if the neutrinos remain trapped within the system, and perhaps it is also necessary for the neutrinos / anti-neutrinos to carry some finite non-zero mass. It is further shown that the electrons also play a significant role in this transition process. It is therefore, very much unlikely that such a transition of spin polarization can really take place in old neutron stars of very low temperature, whereas, the possibility of spontaneous ferromagnetic transition cannot be ruled out in a newly born PNS.

# Impossibility of Spin Polarized States for Neutron Star / Proto-neutron Star Matter in β-Equilibrium Condition

## Key claim

A spontaneous ferromagnetic transition in neutron star matter under β-equilibrium is absolutely forbidden in old neutron stars where neutrinos are non-degenerate and leave the system immediately; however, such a transition may be possible in proto-neutron stars only if neutrinos remain trapped, are degenerate, and carry a finite non-zero mass to allow helicity flipping.

## Method

The authors employ a σ-ω-ρ meson exchange mean field approximation for the hadronic sector, with non-interacting electrons and neutrons, to study spin polarization under β-equilibrium conditions. They derive chemical equilibrium conditions separately for the case where neutrinos escape freely (old neutron stars) and for the case where neutrinos are trapped and degenerate (proto-neutron stars), then compare the resulting spin-dependent chemical potential relations to determine whether spontaneous ferromagnetic ordering is possible.

## Key numbers

- For old neutron stars, the residual magnetic field from ferromagnetic transition is estimated to be too low: the upper limit of polar magnetic field strength from crustal ferromagnetic material (iron) is approximately **10 Tesla** (equivalent to ~10^5 Gauss), assuming crustal density of **10^10 gm cm^{-3}**.
- The typical magnetic field decay time scale for neutron stars is **10^7 to 10^9 years**, with residual magnetic field of order **10^8 Gauss** for millisecond pulsars.
- The fossil remnant field from progenitor stars is of order **10^12 Gauss** for some neutron stars, with progenitor magnetic field strength of **~10^4 Gauss**.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of ferromagnetic transitions in neutron star matter (referenced as R10 in the text) by explicitly incorporating β-equilibrium conditions and the role of neutrino trapping. It contradicts the possibility that spontaneous ferromagnetic ordering in the core could explain the residual magnetic fields of millisecond pulsars, which are believed to be very old neutron stars. The paper enables future work on the role of neutrino mass in magnetic field generation during proto-neutron star evolution and suggests that crustal ferromagnetic materials (e.g., iron) are insufficient to produce the observed field strengths of 10^7-10^8 Gauss.
