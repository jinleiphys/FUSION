# Nuclear shape transitions and elastic magnetic electron scattering

> Backward elastic electron scattering from odd-A nuclear targets is characterized by magnetic form factors containing precise information on the nuclear structure. We study the sensitivity of the magnetic form factors to structural effects related to the evolution and shape transitions in both isotopic and isotonic chains. Calculations of magnetic form factors are performed in the plane-wave Born approximation. The nuclear structure is obtained from a deformed self-consistent mean-field calculation based on a Skyrme HF+BCS formalism. Collective effects are included in the cranking approximation, whereas nucleon-nucleon correlations are taken into account in the coherent density fluctuation model. The evolution of the magnetic form factors is found to exhibit signatures of shape transitions that show up in selected isotopic and isotonic chains involving both stable and unstable nuclei. Several cases are identified as suitable candidates for showing such fingerprints of shape transitions. A new generation of electron scattering experiments involving electron-radioactive beam colliders will be available in the near future, leading to a renewed interest in this field.

## Key claim

The magnetic form factors in odd-A nuclei, calculated within a deformed self-consistent mean-field framework using the plane-wave Born approximation, exhibit clear signatures of shape transitions along isotopic and isotonic chains, but the sensitivity is conditional on the ground-state spin/parity changing due to structural changes rather than merely adding neutrons without altering the odd-nucleon orbital.

## Method

The authors performed deformed self-consistent Skyrme HF+BCS calculations (using the SLy4 interaction) for odd-A nuclei, generating the mean field within the equal filling approximation. Magnetic form factors were computed in the plane-wave Born approximation, with collective rotational effects included via the cranking approximation and nucleon-nucleon correlations incorporated through the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) for spherical nuclei. The calculations were applied to selected isotopic chains (vanadium, aluminum, fluorine, manganese, iodine, cesium) and isotonic chains (N=9, 11, 25, 57), comparing the resulting form factors across each chain to identify signatures of shape transitions.

## Key numbers

- Vanadium isotopes (49,51,53V): spherical, Iπ=7/2−; charge radii agree with experiment to within a few per thousand, with errors below 2% in the lightest cases.
- Manganese isotopes: 51Mn (prolate, βp not given numerically but labeled prolate), 53Mn (spherical, magic N=28), 55Mn (prolate); charge radii decrease from 51Mn to 53Mn and increase from 53Mn to 55Mn.
- Fluorine isotopes: 17F (spherical, Iπ=5/2+), 19F (prolate, Iπ=1/2+), 21F (oblate, Iπ=5/2+); excited 5/2+ state in 19F at 197 keV, excited 1/2+ states in 17F at 495 keV and in 21F at 280 keV.
- Manganese isotopes: excited 7/2− states in 51Mn at 237 keV and in 55Mn at 126 keV; excited 5/2− state in 53Mn at 378 keV.
- Iodine isotopes: 125I (oblate, Iπ=5/2+), 127I (oblate, Iπ=5/2+), 129I (spherical, Iπ=7/2+); excited 7/2+ states in 125I at 114 keV and in 127I at 58 keV; excited 5/2+ state in 129I at 28 keV.
- For 51V, experimental magnetic form factor data from Donnelly (year not given), Arita (1981), and Platchkov (1983) are well reproduced by the calculations.
- For 27Al, experimental data from Donnelly (year not given), Lapikas (1973), and Li (year not given) are well reproduced except at high q where data are underestimated.
- For 19F, experimental data from Williamson (year not given) and Donnelly (1986) show a three-peaked structure reproduced by the calculations, though the third peak is underestimated.

## Context

This work extends the deformed mean-field approach previously applied to stable deformed nuclei (Merino, year not given) to predict magnetic form factors for unstable nuclei along isotopic and isotonic chains, identifying candidates for future experiments at electron-radioactive beam colliders such as ELISe at FAIR-GSI and SCRIT at RIKEN. The study complements earlier shell-model calculations (Radhi 2003, 2007; Jassim, year not given; Samma, year not given) and relativistic mean-field work (Wang, year not given) by focusing on shape-transition signatures in magnetic scattering, and it enables experimental tests of structural evolution in nuclei near stability using the new generation of electron-ion colliders.
