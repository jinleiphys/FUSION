# A systematic formulation of chiral anomalous magnetohydrodynamics

> We present a new way of deriving effective theories of dynamical electromagnetic fields in general media. It can be used to give a systematic formulation of magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) with strong magnetic fields, including systems with chiral matter and Adler-Bell-Jackiw (ABJ) anomaly. We work in the regime in which velocity and temperature fluctuations can be neglected. The resulting chiral anomalous MHD incorporates and generalizes the chiral magnetic effect, the chiral separation effect, the chiral electric separation effect, as well as recently derived strong-field MHD, all in a single coherent framework. At linearized level, the theory predicts that the chiral magnetic wave does not survive dynamical electromagnetic fields. A different chiral wave, to which we refer as the chiral magnetic electric separation wave, emerges as a result of dynamical versions of the chiral electric separation effect and the chiral magnetic effect. We predict its wave velocity. We also introduce a simple, but solvable nonlinear model to explore the fate of the chiral instability.

## Key claim

The paper presents a systematic effective field theory formulation of chiral anomalous magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) in the strong magnetic field regime, under the condition that velocity and temperature fluctuations can be neglected and that the anomaly coefficient is sufficiently small so that chiral charge density can be treated as approximately conserved.

## Method

The authors derive an effective action for dynamical electromagnetic fields in general media using the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism, incorporating the Adler-Bell-Jackiw anomaly through a Wess-Zumino-type term. They assign derivative weights appropriate to the MHD regime (where magnetic diffusion dominates, with spatial derivatives weight 1 and time derivatives weight 2) and impose dynamical KMS symmetry, unitarity constraints, and discrete symmetries to construct the most general local action up to weight 2. The resulting equations of motion give constitutive relations for the electric field and chiral current, which are then linearized around homogeneous equilibrium solutions to study wave modes and instabilities.

## Key numbers

* The chiral magnetic wave does not survive dynamical electromagnetic fields; instead a "chiral magnetic electric separation wave" emerges, with its wave velocity predicted by the theory.
* The decay rate of a nonzero chiral chemical potential in the presence of a magnetic field is given by Gamma = (2c B)^2 r_parallel / chi_5, evaluated at zero chiral chemical potential, where c is the anomaly coefficient, B is the magnetic field strength, r_parallel is a transport coefficient, and chi_5 is the chiral susceptibility.
* The diffusion constant for chiral charge density is D_5 = (kappa - r (lambda^2 - gamma^2)) / chi_5, where kappa, r, lambda, and gamma are transport coefficients, and positivity requires kappa - r (lambda^2 - gamma^2) > 0.
* The magnetic diffusion constant is D_B = r / mu, where mu is the magnetic permeability.

## Context

This work extends the one-form symmetry formulation of MHD developed by Grozdanov (2016) and Vardhan (2022) to include chiral matter with the ABJ anomaly, generalizing previous treatments of the chiral magnetic effect, chiral separation effect, and chiral electric separation effect into a single coherent framework. The formulation corrects and extends the strong-field MHD constitutive relations of Vardhan (2022) by including a topological term (b_0) that was missing in that work, and provides a systematic way to incorporate dynamical electromagnetic fields that is challenging in the one-form symmetry approach. The results enable future studies of chiral plasma instabilities and nonlinear dynamics in systems such as heavy-ion collisions, neutron stars, and the early universe.
