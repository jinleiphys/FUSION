# Light (anti-)nuclei and (anti-)hypertriton production in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} =0.90, 2.76$ and $7$ TeV

> Production of light (anti-)nuclei and (anti-)hypertriton within midrapidity ($|y|<0.5$) and $p_T<3.0$ GeV/c in $pp$ interactions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 0.90, 2.76 and 7 TeV is investigated by the dynamically constrained phase space coalescence model (DCPC), combined with {\footnotesize{PACIAE}} model. The ALICE data for yields, ratios, as well as transverse momentum distributions of $\overline{d}$ and $d$ are well reproduced by the model simulations, meanwhile the three basic characters of $\rm^3{\overline{He}}$, $\rm^3{He}$, $\rm{{^3_{\overline Λ}\overline H}}$, and $\rm{^3_ΛH}$ are also predicted. Besides, we found the yields of light (anti-)nuclei produced are dependent upon their mass number $A$, namely, their yields sharply decrease with the increasing of $A$. The strangeness population factor $s_3=\rm{({^3_ΛH}/{^3{He}})/(Λ/p)}$ is found to be about 0.7 $\sim$ 0.8, and is comparable with the available experimental results.

## Key claim

The dynamically constrained phase space coalescence model (DCPC) combined with the PACIAE transport model reproduces ALICE data for deuteron and anti-deuteron yields, ratios, and transverse momentum distributions in pp collisions at three LHC energies, and predicts the same observables for helium-3, anti-helium-3, hypertriton, and anti-hypertriton. The authors note that the model parameters (parj(1), parj(2), parj(3), and K factor) were roughly fitted to ALICE data for (anti-)protons and (anti-)Lambda, and that the mass uncertainty parameter Delta m was tuned separately for deuterons (0.003 GeV) and for three-body nuclei (0.0075 GeV) by fitting to ALICE data at 7 TeV.

## Method

The authors used the PACIAE transport model (based on PYTHIA 6.4) to generate final-state hadrons from non-single diffractive pp collisions at center-of-mass energies of 0.90, 2.76, and 7 TeV. They then applied the dynamically constrained phase space coalescence (DCPC) model, which coalesces nucleons and hyperons into light nuclei and hypernuclei if the relative distances and invariant masses satisfy constraints derived from the uncertainty principle. The yields of deuterons, anti-deuterons, helium-3, anti-helium-3, hypertriton, and anti-hypertriton were computed within midrapidity (|y| < 0.5) and transverse momentum pT < 3.0 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron yield in pp at 0.90 TeV: 1.06E-4 (PACIAE+DCPC) vs. (1.12 +/- 0.13)E-4 (ALICE)
- Deuteron yield in pp at 2.76 TeV: 1.41E-4 (PACIAE+DCPC) vs. (1.53 +/- 0.14)E-4 (ALICE)
- Deuteron yield in pp at 7 TeV: 2.04E-4 (PACIAE+DCPC) vs. (2.02 +/- 0.17)E-4 (ALICE)
- Anti-deuteron yield in pp at 0.90 TeV: 9.83E-5 (PACIAE+DCPC) vs. (1.11 +/- 0.13)E-4 (ALICE)
- Anti-deuteron yield in pp at 2.76 TeV: 1.35E-4 (PACIAE+DCPC) vs. (1.37 +/- 0.13)E-4 (ALICE)
- Anti-deuteron yield in pp at 7 TeV: 1.98E-4 (PACIAE+DCPC) vs. (1.92 +/- 0.15)E-4 (ALICE)
- Helium-3 yield in pp at 7 TeV: 1.16E-7 (PACIAE+DCPC); ALICE data not available for comparison
- Anti-helium-3 yield in pp at 7 TeV: 1.10E-7 (PACIAE+DCPC) vs. (1.10 +/- 0.63)E-7 (ALICE)
- Hypertriton yield in pp at 7 TeV: 5.62E-8 (PACIAE+DCPC); no ALICE data available
- Anti-hypertriton yield in pp at 7 TeV: 5.23E-8 (PACIAE+DCPC); no ALICE data available
- Strangeness population factor s3 = (hypertriton/helium-3)/(Lambda/proton): approximately 0.7 to 0.8, comparable with experimental results from STAR, ALICE, and E864

## Context

This work extends the DCPC model, previously applied to Pb-Pb and Au-Au collisions by Chen and collaborators, to small-system pp collisions at LHC energies. It builds on ALICE measurements of light (anti-)nuclei in pp collisions at 0.90, 2.76, and 7 TeV (ALICE Collaboration, 2015-2017). The results enable predictions for helium-3, anti-helium-3, hypertriton, and anti-hypertriton production in pp collisions, which can be tested by future ALICE measurements. The calculated strangeness population factor s3 provides a baseline for comparing baryon-strangeness correlations across collision systems, complementing measurements from STAR (2010) in Au-Au collisions and ALICE (2016) in Pb-Pb collisions.
