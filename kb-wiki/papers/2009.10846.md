# Phenomenological quark-hadron equations of state with first-order phase transitions for astrophysical applications

> In the current work an equation of state model with a first-order phase transition for astrophysical applications is presented. The model is based on a two-phase approach for quark-hadron phase transitions, which leads by construction to a first-order phase transition. The resulting model has already been successfully used in several astrophysical applications, such as cold neutron stars, core-collapse supernova explosions and binary neutron star mergers. Main goal of this work is to present the details of the model, discuss certain features and eventually publish it in a tabulated form for further use.

## Key claim

The paper presents a phenomenological two-phase equation of state model that assumes a first-order phase transition at high baryon densities as a working hypothesis, explicitly noting that both the position and order of the hadron-quark transition at low temperatures are currently speculative.

## Method

The model uses a relativistic density functional (RDF) formalism derived from an effective Lagrangian, treating hadronic and quark phases independently and merging them via Gibbs conditions of phase equilibrium. The hadronic component uses the DD2 or DD2F Walecka-type model with density-dependent couplings, while the quark component employs a density functional incorporating confinement via a string-flip model, vector repulsion with four- and eight-fermion interactions, and an isovector mean field. Nuclear clusters at sub-saturated densities are described using a nuclear statistical equilibrium model with excluded volume, and leptons are added separately after the phase transition is constructed.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical temperature from lattice QCD at zero baryon chemical potential: T(μ_B = 0) = 156.5 ± 1.5 MeV (Bazavov 2018)
- DD2/DD2F nuclear saturation density: n_sat = 0.149 fm⁻³
- DD2/DD2F binding energy at saturation: E_B = 16.02 MeV
- DD2/DD2F incompressibility at saturation: K = 255.3 MeV
- DD2/DD2F symmetry energy at saturation: E_Sym = 31.674 MeV
- DD2/DD2F liquid-gas critical point: n_crit = 0.0448 fm⁻³, T_crit = 13.71 MeV
- DD2 maximum neutron star mass: M_max^DD2 = 2.44 M_⊙
- DD2F maximum neutron star mass: M_max^DD2F = 2.08 M_⊙
- DD2F flow constraint correction parameters: k = 0.04, p = 0.07, m = 2.25, with reference density n_ref = n_sat = 0.149 fm⁻³
- String tension parameter: √D_0 = 240 MeV (used in confinement model)

## Context

This work extends the original two-phase model of Kaltenborn (2017), which studied cold neutron stars, to dynamic phenomena including core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers. The model has been applied to binary neutron star mergers in Bauswein (2018), suggesting a gravitational wave signal characteristic of a first-order phase transition, and to core-collapse supernova simulations in Fischer (2017), predicting a second shock wave that successfully explodes a 50 M_⊙ progenitor star. The tabulated equation of state data enables systematic studies of observable signals such as gravitational waves, neutrino signals, and electromagnetic counterparts, and allows probing the hypothetical case of multiple critical endpoints at various temperatures.
