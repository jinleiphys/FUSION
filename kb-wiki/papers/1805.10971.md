# Weak decay of magnetized pions

> The leptonic decay of charged pions is investigated in the presence of background magnetic fields. In this situation Lorentz symmetry is broken and new fundamental decay constants need to be introduced, associated with the decay via the vector part of the electroweak current. We calculate the magnetic field-dependence of both the usual and a new decay constant non-perturbatively on the lattice. We employ both Wilson and staggered quarks and extrapolate the results to the continuum limit. With this non-perturbative input we calculate the tree-level electroweak amplitude for the full decay rate in strong magnetic fields. We find that the muonic decay of the charged pion is enhanced drastically by the magnetic field. We comment on possible astrophysical implications.

## Key claim

The leptonic decay of charged pions is drastically enhanced by strong background magnetic fields, but this enhancement is only observable in astrophysical environments (magnetars) and not in heavy-ion collisions, because the magnetic field lifetimes in the latter are too short by 14-15 orders of magnitude.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using both quenched Wilson fermions (with a heavier-than-physical pion mass of about 415 MeV) and dynamical staggered fermions (with physical quark masses and a pion mass of about 135 MeV), extrapolating results to the continuum limit from four lattice spacings each. They extracted the magnetic field-dependent pion mass and two independent decay constants (the usual axial decay constant fπ and a new vector decay constant f'π) from fits to Euclidean correlators, then used these as non-perturbative input to a tree-level electroweak calculation of the full decay rate in the lowest Landau level approximation.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the slope of fπ/fπ(0) at the origin is -16.9(3) GeV^{-2} for staggered fermions and -1.7(2) GeV^{-2} for Wilson fermions, consistent with the squared pion mass ratio (415/135)^2
- The new decay constant at low fields is f'π/fπ(0) = 0.8(2) GeV^{-2} for staggered and 1.2(3) GeV^{-2} for Wilson fermions
- At eB ≈ 0.3 GeV^2, the muonic decay rate is enhanced by almost a factor of 50 compared to B=0
- The pion lifetime at B ≈ 0.3 GeV^2/e ≈ 5×10^15 T is τπ = 5×10^{-10} s
- The ratio of electronic to muonic decay rates becomes (m_e/m_μ)^2 ≈ 2.27×10^{-5}, independent of magnetic field, which is about 5.4 times smaller than the B=0 fraction of 1.23×10^{-4}
- The kaon decay rate is enhanced only by a factor of about two at eB ≈ 0.3 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pion properties in magnetic fields, which had investigated the pion mass using chiral perturbation theory (Agasian 2001, Andersen 2012), lattice QCD (Bali 2011, Hidaka 2012, Luschevskaya 2014, Bali 2017), and model approaches (Fayazbakhsh 2012, Avancini 2016, Zhang 2016, Mao 2017, Gomez Dumm 2017, Coppola 2018), and the neutral pion decay constant in chiral perturbation theory (Agasian 2001, Andersen 2012) and models (Fayazbakhsh 2012, 2013, Avancini 2016, Zhang 2016, Mao 2017, Gomez Dumm 2017). The charged pion decay constant had only been discussed in chiral perturbation theory (Andersen 2012), and the authors demonstrate that previous studies were incomplete because they missed a second independent decay constant that becomes available in magnetic fields. The results enable improved modeling of cooling mechanisms in magnetars, where pion decay competes with inverse Compton scattering and affects the neutrino spectrum (Zhang 2002), and can be extended to study magnetic field effects on beta-decay rates and nucleon electroweak transition form factors (Matese 1969, Fassio-Canuto 1970, Shinkevich 2004, Giunti 2014).
