# Functional Renormalization Group Study of Thermodynamic Geometry Around the Phase Transition of Quantum Chromodynamic

> We investigate the thermodynamic geometry of the quark-meson model at finite temperature, $T$, and quark number chemical potential, $μ$. We extend previous works by the inclusion of fluctuations exploiting the functional renormalization group approach. We use recent developments to recast the flow equation into the form of an advection-diffusion equation. We adopt the local potential approximation for the effective average action. We focus on the thermodynamic curvature, $R$, in the $(μ,T)$ plane, in proximity of the chiral crossover, up to the critical point of the phase diagram. We find that the inclusion of fluctuations results in a smoother behavior of $R$ near the chiral crossover. Moreover, for small $μ$, $R$ remains negative, signaling the fact that bosonic fluctuations reduce the capability of the system to completely overcome the fermionic statistical repulsion of the quarks. We investigate in more detail the small $μ$ region by analyzing a system in which we artificially lower the pion mass, thus approaching the chiral limit in which the crossover is actually a second order phase transition. On the other hand, as $μ$ is increased and the critical point is approached, we find that $R$ is enhanced and a sign change occurs, in agreement with mean field studies. Hence, we completely support the picture that $R$ is sensitive to a crossover and a phase transition, and provides information about the effective behavior of the system at the phase transition.

## Key claim

The inclusion of quantum fluctuations via the functional renormalization group approach results in a smoother behavior of the thermodynamic curvature near the chiral crossover compared to mean field calculations, while the sign change of the curvature near the critical endpoint remains consistent with mean field studies.

## Method

The authors studied the quark-meson model at finite temperature and quark number chemical potential using the functional renormalization group approach within the local potential approximation. They recast the flow equation into an advection-diffusion equation form and solved it for the effective potential, then computed the thermodynamic curvature from the second and third derivatives of the thermodynamic potential. The results were compared with mean field calculations obtained in the large-N_c limit.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of thermodynamic geometry in the quark-meson model by Zhang (2019) and Castorina (2020) by incorporating quantum fluctuations through the functional renormalization group approach rather than the gaussian approximation used in earlier work. The results support the picture that the thermodynamic curvature is sensitive to both crossovers and phase transitions, and the approach enables future studies of the correlation volume near the QCD critical point using only thermodynamic potential information.
