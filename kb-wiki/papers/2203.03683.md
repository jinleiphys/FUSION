# 3D structure of jet-induced diffusion wake in an expanding quark-gluon plasma

> The diffusion wake accompanying the jet-induced Mach cone provides a unique probe of the properties of quark-gluon plasma in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. It can be characterized by a depletion of soft hadrons in the opposite direction of the propagating jet. We explore the 3D structure of the diffusion wake induced by $γ$-triggered jets in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energy within the coupled linear Boltzmann transport and hydro model. We identify a valley structure caused by the diffusion wake on top of a ridge from the initial multiple parton interaction (MPI) in jet-hadron correlation as a function of rapidity and azimuthal angle. This leads to a double-peak structure in the rapidity distribution of soft hadrons in the opposite direction of the jets as an unambiguous signal of the diffusion wake. Using a two-Gaussian fit, we extract the diffusion wake and MPI contributions to the double peak. The diffusion wake valley is found to deepen with the jet energy loss as characterized by the $γ$-jet asymmetry. Its sensitivity to the equation of state and shear viscosity is also studied.

## Key claim

The diffusion wake in the opposite direction of a jet produces a valley structure on top of a ridge from initial multiple parton interaction (MPI) in the 2D jet-hadron correlation as a function of rapidity and azimuthal angle, leading to a double-peak structure in the rapidity distribution of soft hadrons that serves as an unambiguous signal of the diffusion wake, provided the MPI contribution can be separated.

## Method

The authors used the coupled linear Boltzmann transport and hydro (CoLBT-hydro) model to simulate gamma-triggered jet events in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energy. The model combines the microscopic linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model for jet and recoil parton propagation with the event-by-event (3+1)D CCNU-LBNL viscous (CLVisc) hydrodynamic model for the bulk medium and soft modes of the jet-induced medium response, coupled through a source term from energy-momentum deposited by jet shower and recoil partons as well as particle holes from backreaction. A two-Gaussian fit was applied to extract the diffusion wake valley and MPI ridge contributions to the double-peak structure in the rapidity distribution of soft hadrons.

## Key numbers

- The freeze-out temperature used in the model is T_f = 137 MeV, with specific shear viscosity eta/s = 0.15 and initial time tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c.
- The effective strong coupling constant alpha_s = 0.24 was used to reproduce CMS data on gamma-jet asymmetry distributions.
- The gamma-jet asymmetry x_{jgamma} = p_T^{jet}/p_T^{gamma} was studied in three ranges: x_{jgamma} < 0.6, x_{jgamma} in (0.6, 1.0), and x_{jgamma} > 1, with the diffusion wake valley deepening for smaller x_{jgamma} values.
- Soft hadrons were analyzed in the p_T range 0-2 GeV/c and 1-2 GeV/c, with the latter used for detailed analyses.
- The jet cone size used for jet reconstruction was R = 0.3 with anti-k_T algorithm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of jet-induced Mach cones and diffusion wakes in quark-gluon plasma, which had not unambiguously observed the diffusion wake signal in final hadron spectra due to complications from soft gluon radiations and averaging over propagation paths (Li 2010, Tachibana 2020). Prior work by Yang (2021) showed that medium modification of partons from initial multiple parton interaction produces a uniform soft hadron enhancement that can overwhelm the diffusion wake depletion, requiring a mixed-event procedure for separation. The present study demonstrates that the 3D structure of the diffusion wake in rapidity and azimuthal angle provides a distinctive double-peak signature that can be extracted using a two-Gaussian fit, and shows sensitivity to the equation of state and shear viscosity of the QGP, enabling future combined constraints from experimental data at RHIC and LHC.
