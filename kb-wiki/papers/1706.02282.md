# Proxy-SU(3) symmetry in heavy deformed nuclei

> Background: Microscopic calculations of heavy nuclei face considerable difficulties due to the sizes of the matrices that need to be solved. Various approximation schemes have been invoked, for example by truncating the spaces, imposing seniority limits, or appealing to various symmetry schemes such as pseudo-SU(3). This paper proposes a new symmetry scheme also based on SU(3). This proxy-SU(3) can be applied to well-deformed nuclei, is simple to use, and can yield analytic predictions. Purpose: To present the new scheme and its microscopic motivation, and to test it using a Nilsson model calculation with the original shell model orbits and with the new proxy set. Method: We invoke an approximate, analytic, treatment of the Nilsson model, that allows the above vetting and yet is also transparent in understanding the approximations involved in the new proxy-SU(3). Results: It is found that the new scheme yields a Nilsson diagram for well-deformed nuclei that is very close to the original Nilsson diagram. The specific levels of approximation in the new scheme are also shown, for each major shell. Conclusions: The new proxy-SU(3) scheme is a good approximation to the full set of orbits in a major shell. Being able to replace a complex shell model calculation with a symmetry-based description now opens up the possibility to predict many properties of nuclei analytically and often in a parameter-free way. The new scheme works best for heavier nuclei, precisely where full microscopic calculations are most challenged. Some cases in which the new scheme can be used, often analytically, to make specific predictions, are shown in a subsequent paper.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new proxy-SU(3) symmetry scheme for heavy deformed nuclei, claiming that replacing intruder orbitals (such as the 1h$_{11/2}$ orbitals in the 50-82 shell) with their 0[110] Nilsson partners from the next lower shell (the 1g$_{9/2}$ orbitals) yields a Nilsson diagram very close to the original, with the caveat that the approximation works best for well-deformed nuclei with deformation parameter $\epsilon > 0.15$ and fails completely for $\epsilon \leq 0.1$.

## Method

The authors used an approximate analytic treatment of the Nilsson model Hamiltonian with cylindrical symmetry, employing asymptotic wave functions suitable for large deformations. They calculated matrix elements of the ${\bf l} \cdot {\bf s}$ and ${\bf l}^2$ operators in the $|n_z r s \Sigma\rangle$ basis for both the original shell model orbits and the proxy set, then diagonalized the full Hamiltonian to compare the resulting Nilsson diagrams for the 50-82 shell (protons) against the proxy sdg shell, and similarly for the 28-50, 82-126, and 126-184 shells.

## Key numbers

* For the 50-82 proton shell at deformation $\epsilon = 0.3$, the diagonal matrix elements of the full Hamiltonian range from 5.06 to 6.30 in units of $\hbar\omega_0$ for the original orbitals, while the proxy sdg shell diagonal elements range from 5.73 to 6.28 in the same units.
* The off-diagonal matrix elements introduced by the proxy replacement (shown in boldface in Tables III-V) are small, with the largest new off-diagonal ${\bf l} \cdot {\bf s}$ matrix element being -1.732 (in units of $\hbar\omega_0$) between 3/2[422] and 5/2[422] in the sdg shell.
* The ${\bf l} \cdot {\bf s}$ and ${\bf l}^2$ matrix elements are multiplied by coefficients $v_{ls} = -0.127$ and $v_{ll} = -0.0382$ respectively for the 50-82 region, reducing their contributions to the Hamiltonian by a considerable factor.
* The 1h$_{11/2}$ 11/2[505] orbit is the sole orbit dropped in the 50-82 shell proxy scheme, and the analogous 13/2[606] orbit is dropped in the 82-126 shell.

## Context

This work extends the concept of SU(3) symmetry, originally developed by Elliott (1958) for sd shell nuclei, to heavy deformed nuclei where the LS coupling scheme breaks down. It is inspired by the pseudo-SU(3) scheme (Arima, 1969; Hecht and Adler, 1969; Ratcliff, 1971) but uses a different substitution based on 0[110] Nilsson orbit pairs, whose high overlaps were previously shown by Cakirli (2010) and Karampagia (2015) to play a key role in nuclear deformation. The proxy-SU(3) scheme enables analytic, parameter-free predictions of collective properties in heavy nuclei, which are demonstrated in a subsequent paper by the same authors.
