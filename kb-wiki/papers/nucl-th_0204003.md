# Thermal Lepton Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The current status of evaluating thermal production of lepton pairs in high-energy collisions of heavy nuclei is discussed. After a brief survey of emission rates from hot and dense matter, we address applications to (and interpretations of) recent SPS data including 40 AGeV CERES results, as well as prospects for NA60. Emphasis is put on predictions for RHIC. In particular, implications of hadronic observables and first single-electron measurements at $\sqrt{s}=130$ AGeV for upcoming pair spectra from PHENIX at $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV are assessed.

## Key claim

Thermal dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions can be used to probe hot and dense strongly interacting matter, but the detailed understanding and interpretation of the underlying dynamics remain ambiguous, particularly regarding whether in-medium modifications of vector mesons manifest as dropping masses or spectral broadening.

## Method

The author evaluates equilibrium dilepton production rates using the electromagnetic current-current correlation function (photon selfenergy) and convolves these rates over the space-time history of the collision using thermal fireball simulations. Medium effects in the hadron gas phase are calculated using effective chiral Lagrangians with vector dominance, including finite-temperature and finite-baryon-chemical-potential field theory, while the quark-gluon plasma phase is treated with perturbative QCD and hard-thermal-loop resummed calculations. Chemical off-equilibrium effects are incorporated by conserving particle numbers after chemical freezeout via effective chemical potentials for pions, kaons, and antibaryons.

## Key numbers

- The electromagnetic decay width of the rho meson is 6.8 keV, compared to 0.6 keV for the omega meson, giving a naive quark-model ratio of 9:1.
- At RHIC energies, the charged particle multiplicity dN_ch/dy at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV is a factor of 2 larger than at SPS (sqrt(s)=17 AGeV).
- For RHIC conditions with chemical off-equilibrium, antibaryon chemical potentials reach approximately 270 MeV and pion-chemical potentials approximately 70 MeV at a temperature of 130 MeV.
- The initial temperature extracted from intermediate-mass NA50 data is approximately 220 MeV, well inside the QGP phase.
- At the highest temperature of 180 MeV in the RHIC scenario, approximately 40% of the medium effects on vector mesons stem from antibaryons.

## Context

This paper extends the theoretical framework for thermal dilepton production developed in earlier work by Rapp and Wambach (2000) and applies it to SPS data from CERES (NA45) and NA50, as well as making predictions for RHIC experiments including PHENIX. The work builds on prior calculations of in-medium vector meson spectral functions by Rapp (2001) and addresses the open-charm background problem identified by Gavai et al. (1996). The results enable future experiments at GSI, CERN, and RHIC to discriminate between different scenarios of chiral symmetry restoration, particularly the "Vector Manifestation" proposed by Harada and Yamawaki (2001) versus broadening-dominated approaches such as that of Urban, Buballa, and Wambach (2002).
