# On the relation between $E(5)-$models and the interacting boson model

> The connections between the $E(5)-$models (the original E(5) using an infinite square well, $E(5)-β^4$, $E(5)-β^6$ and $E(5)-β^8$), based on particular solutions of the geometrical Bohr Hamiltonian with $γ$-unstable potentials, and the interacting boson model (IBM) are explored. For that purpose, the general IBM Hamiltonian for the $U(5)-O(6)$ transition line is used and a numerical fit to the different $E(5)-$models energies is performed, later on the obtained wavefunctions are used to calculate B(E2) transition rates. It is shown that within the IBM one can reproduce very well all these $E(5)-$models. The agreement is the best for $E(5)-β^4$ and reduces when passing through $E(5)-β^6$, $E(5)-β^8$ and E(5), where the worst agreement is obtained (although still very good for a restricted set of lowest lying states). The fitted IBM Hamiltonians correspond to energy surfaces close to those expected for the critical point. A phenomenon similar to the quasidynamical symmetry is observed.

## Key claim

The interacting boson model (IBM) can reproduce the spectra and B(E2) transition rates of all E(5)-type models (E(5), E(5)-β⁴, E(5)-β⁶, E(5)-β⁸) with excellent agreement for the β⁴ case and progressively worse agreement for the others, but only for a restricted set of lowest-lying states in the worst case (E(5) itself). The fitted IBM Hamiltonians correspond to energy surfaces close to those expected for the critical point, and a phenomenon similar to quasidynamical symmetry is observed.

## Method

The authors used the general IBM Hamiltonian for the U(5)-O(6) transition line with κ₂=0 and κ₄=0, leaving ε_d, κ₀, κ₁, and κ₃ as free parameters. They performed a χ² fit of the IBM energy levels to the energies of 17 states from each E(5)-model (E(5), E(5)-β⁴, E(5)-β⁶, E(5)-β⁸) for boson numbers N ranging from small values up to the large-N limit. After fixing the IBM Hamiltonian from the energy fit, they calculated B(E2) transition rates using the fitted wavefunctions with no additional free parameters (the effective charge was fixed to give B(E2; 2₁⁺→0₁⁺)=100).

## Key numbers

- For N=60, the χ² value for the energy fit to E(5)-β⁴ is approximately χ²(E(5))/1000, meaning the agreement is roughly 1000 times better for β⁴ than for the original E(5) model.
- For the E(5) model, the fitted IBM Hamiltonian produces r₁=0 (the critical point condition) for N≈7; for E(5)-β⁸ this occurs at N≈25; for E(5)-β⁶ at N≈70; and for E(5)-β⁴ at very large N.
- For N=60, the B(E2; 2₁⁺→0₁⁺) is normalized to 100 for all models; the IBM gives B(E2; 4₁⁺→2₁⁺)=180.5 for E(5)-β⁴ compared to the model value of 183.2, and B(E2; 6₁⁺→4₁⁺)=248.7 compared to 256.4.
- For N=60, the B(E2; 2₃⁺→0₂⁺) from IBM is 107.9 for E(5)-β⁴ versus the model value of 112.6, while for E(5) the IBM gives 90.2 versus the model value of 75.2.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Arias and collaborators (2003, 2005) who first analyzed the connection between the U(5)-O(6) IBM critical point and the E(5) model, finding that IBM at the critical point gives results close to E(5) for small boson numbers (N≈5) but reproduces a β⁴ potential for large N. The paper also builds on the work of Bonatsos and collaborators (2004) who provided numerical solutions for γ-independent Bohr Hamiltonians with β²ⁿ potentials. The results enable future studies of X(5)-type models (mentioned as forthcoming work by the same authors) and establish that experimental data cannot discriminate between an E(5)-model and its IBM counterpart.
