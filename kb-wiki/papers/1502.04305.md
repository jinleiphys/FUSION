# Low-energy electric dipole response in 120Sn

> The electric dipole strength in 120Sn has been extracted from proton inelastic scattering experiments at E_p = 295 MeV and at forward angles including 0 degree. Below neutron threshoild it differs from the results of a 120Sn(gamma,gamma') experiment and peaks at an excitation energy of 8.3 MeV. The total strength corresponds to 2.3(2)% of the energy-weighted sum rule and is more than three times larger than what is observed with the (gamma,gamma') reaction. This implies a strong fragmentation of the E1 strength and/or small ground state branching ratios of the excited 1- states.

## Key claim

The electric dipole (E1) strength in 120Sn below the neutron threshold, extracted from proton inelastic scattering, is more than three times larger than that observed in a previous (gamma,gamma') experiment, implying a strong fragmentation of the E1 strength and/or small ground-state branching ratios of the excited 1- states.

## Method

The experiment used a 295 MeV polarized proton beam at the Grand Raiden spectrometer at RCNP Osaka, with spectrometer angles of 0°, 2.5°, and 4°. A multipole decomposition analysis (MDA) of the cross-section angular distributions was performed using theoretical angular distributions from quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) calculations, restricting the fit to E1, M1, and E2 multipoles. The E1 strength distribution was extracted from the (p,p') data in the semiclassical approximation, exploiting the dominance of relativistic Coulomb excitation at very forward angles.

## Key numbers

- Total E1 strength below neutron threshold (up to 9.0 MeV) corresponds to 2.3(2)% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR).
- The (p,p') experiment finds a peak in E1 strength at an excitation energy of 8.3 MeV, which is not seen in the (gamma,gamma') data.
- The summed B(E1) strength between 4 and 9 MeV from the (p,p') experiment is 1.169(12) e²fm², compared to 0.164(31) e²fm² from the (gamma,gamma') experiment.
- The integrated B(E1) strength in the 6-8 MeV range is 0.57(4) e²fm², lower than the empirical prediction of 0.93 e²fm² from Masuda (2014).
- The Lorentzian fit to the GDR has parameters: sigma_max = 252 mb, E_c = 15.0 MeV, Gamma = 4.2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the benchmark study of 208Pb by Tamii (2011) and Poltoratska (2012), which established relativistic Coulomb excitation in proton scattering as a method to measure complete E1 strength. The results contradict the earlier (gamma,gamma') experiment on 120Sn by Ozel-Tashenov (2014), which found a local minimum of integrated strength in this isotope. The findings suggest that systematic studies of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) based solely on ground-state transitions from (gamma,gamma') experiments should be viewed with caution, and call for further systematic (p,p') studies across different shell closures and deformed nuclei.
