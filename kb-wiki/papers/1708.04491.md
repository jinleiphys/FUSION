# Open charm production in central Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS: statistical model estimates

> Charm particle production in nucleus-nucleus collisions at the CERN SPS energies is considered within a statistical approach. Namely, the Statistical Model of the Early Stage is used to calculate mean multiplicity of charm particles in central Pb+Pb collisions. A small number of produced charm particles necessitates the use of the exact charm conservation law. The model predicts a rapid increase of mean charm multiplicity as a function of collision energy. The mean multiplicity calculated for central Pb+Pb collisions at the center of mass energy per nucleon pair $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3$ GeV exceeds significantly the experimental upper limit. Thus, in order to describe open charm production model parameters and/or assumptions should be revised.

## Key claim

The Statistical Model of the Early Stage (SMES) predicts a mean multiplicity of charm-anticharm pairs in central Pb+Pb collisions at the top CERN SPS energy that significantly exceeds the experimental upper limit, indicating that model parameters and/or assumptions must be revised to describe open charm production.

## Method

The authors apply the Statistical Model of the Early Stage (SMES) to central Pb+Pb collisions, assuming that newly created matter has zero conserved charges and is produced in a longitudinally contracted fireball whose volume and energy depend on collision energy and participant number. They introduce charm degrees of freedom using a canonical ensemble (CE) with exact charm conservation, calculating the mean number of charm-anticharm pairs from the CE number density of charm and anti-charm particles in either the confined (hadron) phase, deconfined (quark-gluon) phase, or mixed phase. The model uses fixed parameters: bag constant chosen for transition temperature T_c = 200 MeV, r_0 = 1.30 fm, and inelastic energy fraction η = 0.67.

## Key numbers

- For central Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 17.3 GeV, the SMES predicts ⟨N_cȯ⟩ ≈ 20 for charm quark mass m_Q^c = 1.3 GeV and ⟨N_cȯ⟩ ≈ 8 for m_Q^c = 1.5 GeV.
- The experimental upper limit from the NA49 Collaboration gives ⟨N_cȯ⟩ < 3.6, derived from the measured upper limit of 2.4 for D^0 + anti-D^0 meson mean multiplicity.
- The mixed phase region in the SMES spans from √s_NN,1 = 7.42 GeV to √s_NN,2 = 10.83 GeV.
- For the full spectrum of charm mesons, results are higher by a factor of 1.9 at the beginning of the mixed phase compared to the g_W^c = 10 case, and lower by a factor of 5.8 compared to the g_W^c = 50 case.

## Context

This work extends the SMES framework, previously applied to strangeness production (Poberezhnyuk 2015), to charm production in heavy-ion collisions at CERN SPS energies. The SMES predictions for charm multiplicity at √s_NN = 17.3 GeV (⟨N_cȯ⟩ ≈ 8–20) are significantly larger than the experimental upper limit from NA49 (1999) and also exceed estimates from perturbative QCD (⟨N_cȯ⟩ ≈ 0.17), the hadron resonance gas model (⟨N_cȯ⟩ = 0.3–0.45), and the ALCOR hadronization model (⟨N_cȯ⟩ ≈ 3.6). The results demonstrate that the SMES cannot describe open charm production without revising its parameters or assumptions, motivating future work to reconcile the model with experimental constraints.
