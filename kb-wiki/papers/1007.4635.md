# Nonrelativistic scale anomaly, and composite operators with complex scaling dimensions

> It is demonstrated that a nonrelativistic quantum scale anomaly manifests itself in the appearance of composite operators with complex scaling dimensions. In particular, we study nonrelativistic quantum mechanics with an inverse square potential and consider a composite s-wave operator O=ψψ. We analytically compute the scaling dimension of this operator and determine the propagator <0|T O O^{\dagger}|0>. The operator O represents an infinite tower of bound states with a geometric energy spectrum. Operators with higher angular momenta are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a nonrelativistic quantum scale anomaly manifests itself in composite operators acquiring complex scaling dimensions, specifically in the overcritical regime where the inverse-square coupling exceeds the critical value \(\kappa > \kappa_{cr} = (D-2)^2/4\), which causes the quantum field theory to lose conformality and the renormalization group flow to develop a limit cycle.

## Method

The author studies nonrelativistic quantum mechanics with an inverse-square potential \(V(r) = -\kappa/r^2\) in \(D > 2\) spatial dimensions, using a second-quantized field-theoretic formulation. The two-particle s-wave composite operator \(\mathcal{O} = \psi\psi\) is analyzed by computing its two-point correlation function \(iG_{\mathcal{O}}(\omega, \vec{p}) = \langle 0| T \mathcal{O} \mathcal{O}^\dagger |0\rangle\) in momentum space, relating it to the energy Green function of a single particle with reduced mass in the external inverse-square potential, and evaluating the radial Green function in the overcritical regime using regularization via a phase shift boundary condition at small radius.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling for the s-wave operator is \(\kappa_{cr} = (D-2)^2/4\); for \(\kappa > \kappa_{cr}\) the scaling dimensions become complex.
- The scaling dimensions of the s-wave composite operator are \(\Delta_{\pm} = (D+2)/2 \pm \sqrt{(D-2)^2/4 - \kappa}\).
- The renormalized two-particle propagator in the overcritical regime is \(iG^{\text{ren}}_{\mathcal{O}}(\omega) = i \tan\left( \theta_0 \ln \frac{\sqrt{-\omega - i\epsilon}}{\zeta^*_0} \right)\), where \(\theta_0 = \sqrt{\kappa - (D-2)^2/4}\).
- The bound-state energies form a geometric spectrum: \(\omega_n = -\zeta^{*2}_0 \exp\left( -\frac{2\pi n}{\theta_0} + \frac{\pi}{\theta_0} \right)\) for \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\), with ratio \(\omega_{n+1}/\omega_n = \exp(-2\pi/\theta_0)\).
- For higher angular momenta \(l\), the geometric ratio becomes \(\exp(-2\pi/\theta_l)\) with \(\theta_l = \sqrt{\kappa - (l + (D-2)/2)^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic conformal field theory framework developed by Nishida and Son (2007) by demonstrating that the quantum scale anomaly, previously studied in the context of the Efimov effect by Efimov (1970) and Bedaque et al. (1999), leads to composite operators with complex scaling dimensions in the inverse-square potential problem. The result provides a universal form for the two-point correlator of such operators, which the author expects to apply to other systems exhibiting the Efimov effect, such as three identical bosons at unitarity, and connects to a holographic AdS/CFT calculation by Moroz (2010) that yields the same functional form.
