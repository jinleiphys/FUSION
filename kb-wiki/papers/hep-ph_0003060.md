# Virtual Color Superconductivity and Nucleon Structure

> The observed electromagnetic (EM) properties of a nucleon at high energy are studied to search for a possible metastable color superconducting phase, called a virtual phase, that has an energy density close to the true ground state of the vacuum. The discussion is based on a mechanism of spontaneous partial breaking of the EM U(1) gauge symmetry inside a color superconductor. Favorable evidences for such a scenario are found.

# Virtual Color Superconductivity and Nucleon Structure

## Key claim
The observed electromagnetic properties of nucleons at high energy provide favorable but not conclusive evidence for a metastable color superconducting phase (called a virtual phase) with energy density close to the true ground state of the vacuum. The claim is hedged by the statement that more work is needed to firmly establish this scenario, and the evidence is described as qualitative rather than quantitative.

## Method
The author assumes the existence of a virtual color superconducting phase for the strong interaction vacuum, where diquark condensation breaks the global U(1) symmetry corresponding to nucleon number. The electromagnetic vertex is decomposed into a core contribution and a spread component carried by Goldstone bosons; using Ward identities, the lepton-nucleon scattering T-matrix is shown to contain an induced strong interaction component from the lack of Goldstone boson exchange between leptons and quarks in the superconducting phase. This formalism is then applied to deep inelastic scattering observables including structure functions, the GDH sum rule, and the Froissart bound.

## Key numbers
- The GDH sum rule violation is estimated at approximately 10%, which is still not saturated by current data.
- The total cross section for gamma*-N interaction extracted from semi-leptonic DIS grows as sigma ~ s^0.4, corresponding to a hard pomeron intercept of alpha_{P'} ≈ 1.4.
- The polarized structure functions at small x before the Bjorken limit behave as g_1(x) ~ x^{-1.4} and g_2(x) ~ x^{-2.4}, consistent with SMC data at the smallest x ~ 10^{-4}.
- The soft pomeron intercept for hadron-hadron collisions is alpha_P ~ 1.08, which satisfies the Froissart bound sigma ~ const × ln^2 s.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of color superconductivity (reviewed in Ying's prior work) to nucleon structure and deep inelastic scattering. It attempts to explain several puzzles simultaneously: the possible violation of the GDH sum rule, the rapid rise of structure functions F_2 and g_1 at small x measured at HERA and SMC, and the difference between F_2 from charged lepton DIS versus neutrino DIS. The hard pomeron hypothesis is contrasted with perturbative QCD explanations (Ball; Reya), which the author argues cannot simultaneously explain the spin content of the nucleon. The work enables further tests through predictions for the energy dependence of electro-production of rho, phi, and J/psi mesons under the hard pomeron hypothesis (Mprod).
