# Consistent Skyrme parametrizations constrained by GW170817

> The high-density behavior of the stellar matter composed of nucleons and leptons under $β$~equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions is studied with the Skyrme parametrizations shown to be consistent (CSkP) with the nuclear matter, pure neutron matter, symmetry energy and its derivatives in a set of $11$ constraints [Dutra {\it et al.}, Phys. Rev. C 85, 035201 (2012)]. The predictions of these parametrizations on the tidal deformabilities related to the GW170817 event are also examined. The CSkP that produce massive neutron stars give a range of $11.86~\mbox{km} \leqslant R_{1.4} \leqslant 12.55~\mbox{km}$ for the canonical star radius, in agreement with other theoretical predictions. It is shown that the CSkP are compatible with the region of masses and radii obtained from the analysis of recent data from LIGO and Virgo Collaboration (LVC). A correlation between dimensionless tidal deformability and radius of the canonical star is found, namely, $Λ_{1.4} \approx 3.16\times10^{-6}R_{1.4}^{7.35}$, with results for the CSkP compatible with the recent range of $Λ_{1.4}=190_{-120}^{+390}$ from LVC. An analysis of the $Λ_1\timesΛ_2$ graph shows that all the CSkP are compatible with the recent bounds obtained by LVC. Finally, the universal correlation between the moment of inertia and the deformability of a neutron star, named as the \mbox{$I$-Love} relation, is verified for the CSkP, that are also shown to be consistent with the prediction for the moment of inertia of the \mbox{PSR J0737-3039} primary component pulsar.

## Key claim

The 16 Skyrme parametrizations previously shown to satisfy 11 nuclear-matter constraints (CSkP) are compatible with the tidal deformability bounds from GW170817, but only five of them (GSkI, KDE0v1, MSL0, NRAPR, Ska35s20) simultaneously produce neutron star masses near 2 solar masses and give a canonical-star radius range of 11.86 km to 12.55 km.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using Skyrme-model equations of state for neutron star matter under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality, with a BPS outer crust and a polytropic inner crust matched at the core-crust transition density determined by the thermodynamical spinodal method. They computed the tidal Love number k2 from a coupled differential equation and the dimensionless tidal deformability Lambda for each CSkP, then compared the resulting mass-radius profiles, Lambda_1.4 versus R_1.4, and binary tidal deformabilities (Lambda_1, Lambda_2) against the LIGO/Virgo Collaboration constraints from GW170817.

## Key numbers

- Canonical star radius range for the five high-mass CSkP: 11.86 km (KDE0v1) to 12.55 km (Ska35s20)
- Fitted correlation for the canonical star: Lambda_1.4 approximately 3.16 x 10^-6 R_1.4^7.35
- LVC range for Lambda_1.4: 190 with lower bound -120 and upper bound +390
- Chirp mass used for binary analysis: 1.188 solar masses
- Maximum neutron star masses for the five high-mass CSkP: GSkI 1.974 solar masses, KDE0v1 1.970 solar masses, MSL0 1.956 solar masses, NRAPR 1.939 solar masses, Ska35s20 1.964 solar masses
- Range of mass-weighted tidal deformability tilde-Lambda for GSkI: 420 to 427
- Range of mass-weighted tidal deformability tilde-Lambda for SQMC650: 119 to 120

## Context

This work extends the systematic constraint analysis of Dutra et al. (2012), who identified 16 Skyrme parametrizations satisfying 11 nuclear-matter constraints, by testing them against the new astrophysical constraints from GW170817. The results are compared with similar studies using Skyrme and RMF models by Malik et al. (2018), Fattoyev et al. (2018), and the chiral EFT analysis of Lim et al. (2018). The paper confirms the I-Love universal relation for all CSkP, previously shown by P. P. et al. (2018) for a smaller set of Skyrme parametrizations, and enables future use of these parametrizations in neutron star modeling with confidence that they satisfy both nuclear and gravitational-wave constraints.
