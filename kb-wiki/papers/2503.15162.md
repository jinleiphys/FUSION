# Fine-tunings in nucleosynthesis and the emergence of life: Status and perspectives

> We discuss the fine-tunings of nuclear reactions in the Big Bang and in stars and draw some conclusions on the emergence of the light elements and the life-relevant elements carbon and oxygen. We also stress how to improve these calculations in the future. This requires a concerted effort of different communities, especially in nuclear reaction theory, lattice QCD for few-nucleon systems, stellar evolution calculations, particle physics and philosophy.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the fine-tuning of nuclear reactions in Big Bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) and stellar carbon/oxygen production is real but not yet conclusively quantified, with the strongest constraints coming from BBN rather than from stellar processes. The authors stress that the formation of life-enabling elements is a necessary but not sufficient condition for the emergence of life.

## Method

The authors review and compare five publicly available BBN network codes (NUC123, AlterBBN, PArthENoPE, PRIMAT, PRyMordial) to estimate systematic errors, then implement novel parametrizations of the leading 17 reaction rates based on available experimental data and pionless effective field theory (EFT) for the n+p→d+γ reaction. For stellar nucleosynthesis, they analyze the triple-alpha process and the 12C(α,γ)16O reaction using Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) and Halo-EFT calculations, varying the energy difference between the Hoyle state and the 3α threshold in stellar simulations.

## Key numbers

- From BBN: |δα_EM| < 1.8% from 4He abundance and |δα_EM| < 0.8% from deuterium abundance
- From BBN Higgs VEV variation: -0.07% ≤ δv/v ≤ -0.02% (2σ bound from deuterium abundance); -0.7% ≤ δv/v ≤ +0.4% (from 4He abundance)
- From stellar carbon/oxygen production: ΔE_R ≥ -300 keV and ΔE_R ≤ 300 keV for low metallicity stars; bounds narrower by a factor of 2 for solar metallicity
- From NLEFT analysis of triple-alpha process: δm_q/m_q between 0.4% and 5%; |δα_EM/α_EM| < 7.5%
- Upper bound on strange quark mass variation: |Δm_s/m_s| ≤ 5.1%
- QCD θ-parameter limit from nuclear reactions: θ ≲ 0.1

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of Livio (1989) and Oberhummer (2000) on stellar fine-tuning, and the NLEFT analysis of Epelbaum (2012, 2013) that first showed the correlation between the 8Be and Hoyle state fine-tunings. It improves on the BBN quark mass variation studies of Bedaque (2010) and Berengut (2013) by incorporating lattice QCD data for the two-nucleon system combined with low-energy theorems from Baru (2015, 2016). The paper enables future work combining lattice QCD simulations at lower pion masses, improved reaction theory calculations using Halo-EFT and NLEFT, and studies of interrelations between fundamental parameters in unified theories as in Coc (2006).
