# IBM: parameter symmetry, hidden symmetries and transformations of boson operators

> A symmetry of the parameter space of interacting boson models IBM-1 and IBM-2 is studied. The symmetry is associated with linear canonical transformations of boson operators, or, equivalently, with the existence of different realizations of the symmetry algebras of the models. The relevance of the parameter symmetry to physical observables is discussed.

## Key claim

The parameter space of the interacting boson models IBM-1 and IBM-2 possesses a symmetry that makes different sets of Hamiltonian parameters produce identical spectra; this symmetry is associated with linear canonical transformations of boson operators and, in the IBM-2 case, yields multiple alternative realizations of the combined proton-neutron subalgebras.

## Method

The authors study the parameter symmetry of the IBM-1 Hamiltonian expressed as a sum of Casimir operators of U(5), SO(5), SO(3), SO(6), and SU(3) subalgebras of U(6). They show that gauge transformations \(R(\varphi)\) and particle-hole conjugations \(\tilde R(\varphi)\) of the boson operators generate isospectral Hamiltonians, leading to explicit transformation rules for the Hamiltonian parameters. For IBM-2, they apply independent transformations to proton and neutron boson operators, obtaining a set of standard parameter symmetry relations and identifying 2 realizations of U\(_{\pi\nu}\)(5), 2 of SO\(_{\pi\nu}\)(6), 2 of \(\overline{\text{SO}}_{\pi\nu}\)(6), and 8 of SU\(_{\pi\nu}\)(3).

## Key numbers

* For IBM-1 with \(k_6 \neq 0\), the parameter symmetry transformation is: \(k'_1 = k_1 + 2k_6\), \(k'_2 = k_2 + 2k_6\), \(k'_3 = k_3 - 6k_6\), \(k'_4 = k_4 + 2k_6\), \(k'_5 = k_5 + 4k_6\), \(k'_6 = -k_6\).
* For IBM-1 with \(k_6 = 0\), the transformation is: \(H'_0 = H_0 + 10k_5 N\), \(k'_1 = k_1 + 4k_5(N+2)\), \(k'_2 = k_2 - 4k_5\), \(k'_3 = k_3 + 2k_5\), \(k'_4 = k_4\), \(k'_5 = -k_5\), \(k'_6 = 0\).
* For \(^{196}\)Pt (\(N=6\)), the SO(6) DS limit parameters are \(k_1=k_2=k_6=0\), \(k_3=50\) keV, \(k_4=10\) keV, \(k_5=-42.75\) keV; the parameter-symmetry-transformed set is \(k'_1=-1368\) keV, \(k'_2=171\) keV, \(k'_3=-35.5\) keV, \(k'_4=10\) keV, \(k'_5=42.75\) keV, \(k'_6=0\).
* The IBM-2 Hamiltonian is characterized by 21 independent parameters \(\{k_i^\pi, k_i^\nu, k_i\}\).

## Context

This work extends the parameter symmetry concept previously established for IBM-1 (Shirokov et al., 1998; Goslar, 1998) to the proton-neutron version IBM-2. It shows that the standard dynamical symmetry limits of IBM-2 are incomplete and that alternative embeddings of subalgebras (e.g., SU\(^{01}_{\pi\nu}\)(3) and SU\(^{11}_{\pi\nu}\)(3)) provide additional independent Casimir operators needed to express the general IBM-2 Hamiltonian. The results enable the identification of hidden symmetries in transitional Hamiltonians and clarify the ambiguity in fitting Hamiltonian parameters to nuclear spectra, with implications for distinguishing parameter sets via electromagnetic transitions or isotopic chains.
