# Global performance of multireference density functional theory for low-lying states in $sd$-shell nuclei

> We present a comprehensive study of low-lying states in even-even Ne, Mg, Si, S, Ar isotopes with the multireference density functional theory (MR-DFT) based on a relativistic point-coupling energy density functional (EDF). Beyond mean-field (BMF) effects are taken into account by configuration mixing of both particle-number and angular-momentum projected axially deformed states with generator coordinate method (GCM). Global performance of the MR-DFT for the properties of both ground state and of the first $2^+, 4^+$ states is examined, in comparison with previous studies based on nonrelativistic EDFs and available data. Our results indicate that an EDF parameterized at the BMF level is demanded to achieve a quantitative description.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that multireference density functional theory (MR-DFT) with axial symmetry and relativistic point-coupling energy density functionals provides a reasonable global description of low-lying states in sd-shell nuclei, but the authors explicitly state that an energy density functional parameterized at the beyond-mean-field level is required to achieve a truly quantitative description, as functionals optimized at the mean-field level systematically overbind neutron-rich nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic mean-field calculations using the PC-F1 and PC-PK1 point-coupling energy density functionals with BCS pairing, generating axially deformed configurations via quadratic constraints on the mass quadrupole moment. Beyond-mean-field effects were incorporated through configuration mixing of particle-number and angular-momentum projected states using the generator coordinate method (GCM) with the axial deformation parameter β as the collective coordinate. The Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation was solved to obtain the energies and wave functions of the ground state and first 2⁺, 4⁺ states for even-even Ne, Mg, Si, S, and Ar isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The rms deviation of binding energies for 56 sd-shell nuclei increased from 2.94 MeV at mean-field level to 3.61 MeV after beyond-mean-field calculations using the PC-F1 force.
- The rms deviation for 51 two-neutron separation energies improved from 2.07 MeV at mean-field level to 1.53 MeV after configuration-mixing calculations with the PC-F1 force.
- The largest discrepancy in dynamical correlation energy between the cranking prescription and GCM calculations was found in ³²S, with an energy difference of 3.5 MeV.
- The calculated excitation energy of the first 2⁺ state in ⁴⁴S was approximately 30% lower than experiment using both PC-PK1 and PC-F1 forces.
- The rms charge radius of ²⁸Si was overestimated by approximately 0.1 fm after including beyond-mean-field effects.

## Context

This work extends previous MR-DFT studies of carbon (Yao, 2011) and magnesium isotopes (Yao, 2011) to a comprehensive set of sd-shell nuclei, and provides a systematic comparison with nonrelativistic calculations using the Skyrme SLy4 force (Bender, 2006) and Gogny D1S/D1M forces (Rodríguez-Guzmán, 2014). The results enable future development of energy density functionals specifically parameterized at the beyond-mean-field level, similar to what was done for the Gogny D1M force (Goriely, 2009), and motivate inclusion of triaxial degrees of freedom for nuclei like ⁴⁴S where axial-only calculations show limitations.
