# An overview of heavy quark energy loss puzzle at RHIC

> We give a theoretical overview of the heavy quark tomography puzzle posed by recent non-photonic single electron data from central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ AGeV. We show that radiative energy loss mechanisms alone are not able to explain large single electron suppression data, as long as realistic parameter values are assumed. We argue that combined collisional and radiative pQCD approach can solve a substantial part of the non-photonic single electron puzzle.

## Key claim

Radiative energy loss mechanisms alone cannot explain the large suppression of non-photonic single electrons in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) AGeV, as long as realistic values of gluon rapidity density (around \(dN_g/dy = 1000\)) are assumed; a combined collisional and radiative pQCD approach may solve a substantial part of the puzzle.

## Method

The author computes initial heavy quark \(p_\perp\) distributions for charm and bottom using the MNR code with CTEQ5M parton distributions, assuming masses \(M_c = 1.2\) GeV and \(M_b = 4.75\) GeV. Medium-induced radiative energy loss is calculated using a finite-mass generalization of the GLV multigluon fluctuation approach, with Debye mass \(\mu \approx 0.5\) GeV, mean free path \(\lambda_g \approx 1\) fm, and medium thickness \(L \approx 5\) fm. Single electron suppression is obtained by convolving the quenched heavy quark spectra with fragmentation functions and decay spectra, and the results are compared to PHENIX and STAR non-photonic single electron data.

## Key numbers

- Single electron nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}^e\) from radiative energy loss alone is \(0.7 \pm 0.1\) for \(dN_g/dy = 1000\), in disagreement with data.
- To approach the measured suppression with radiative loss only, an excessive gluon rapidity density \(dN_g/dy \sim 3500\) is needed, yielding \(R_{AA}^e \sim 0.5 \pm 0.1\) but violating bulk entropy bounds.
- With combined collisional and radiative energy loss at \(dN_g/dy = 1000\), single electron \(R_{AA}^e \approx 0.5 \pm 0.1\) is achievable, consistent with data within errors.
- Bottom quark contribution to single electrons becomes comparable to charm at \(p_\perp \sim 5.5\) GeV in vacuum, and at \(p_\perp \sim 3.5\) GeV for \(dN_g/dy = 1000\).
- Charm and bottom masses used: \(m_c \approx 1.2\) GeV, \(m_b \approx 4.75\) GeV.
- Constant \(\alpha_S = 0.3\) assumed throughout.

## Context

This work addresses the "heavy quark tomography puzzle" raised by non-photonic single electron data from PHENIX and STAR (2005-2006), which showed suppression similar to light hadrons despite the expected dead-cone effect for heavy quarks. The author extends prior radiative-only calculations by Djordjevic, Gyulassy, Vogt, and Wicks (2005-2006) and incorporates collisional energy loss studies by Mustafa (2005), Dutt-Mazumder (2005), and Peigne (2006). The combined approach enables simultaneous consistency with both the single electron suppression and the well-established pion quenching data (\(R_{AA}^{\pi^0} \approx 0.2 \pm 0.1\)) without violating entropy constraints from PHOBOS (2004). Future work should relax the fixed \(\alpha_S\) approximation and allow running coupling.
