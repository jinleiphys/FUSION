# Dynamical anomalies and the role of initial conditions in the HMF model

> We discuss the role of the initial conditions for the dynamical anomalies observed in the quasi-stationary states of the Hamiltonian Mean Field (HMF) model.

## Key claim

The paper shows that dynamical anomalies in the HMF model, such as fractal-like structures in phase space, power-law decay of correlations, and superdiffusion, are present for a large class of initial conditions with initial magnetization greater than zero, contradicting conjectures that such anomalies exist only for very special initial conditions. The case of zero initial magnetization is a limiting situation that produces a qualitatively different quasi-stationary state.

## Method

The authors performed numerical simulations of the Hamiltonian Mean Field (HMF) model for N=1000 and N=10000 particles at energy density U=0.69, preparing initial conditions with varying initial magnetization M(0) in the range [0,1] by distributing spin angles uniformly over a variable portion of the unit circle. They computed the time evolution of temperature, velocity autocorrelation functions, and mean square displacement of angular motion, fitting the correlation decay with q-exponential functions from Tsallis thermostatistics.

## Key numbers

- Energy density U=0.69 was used for all simulations, where anomalies are most evident
- For N=1000, the equilibrium temperature is Teq=0.476, while the infinite-size limiting QSS temperature is T(N=∞)=0.38
- The critical energy density is Uc=3/4, corresponding to critical temperature Tc=1/2
- For initial magnetization M≥0.4, the velocity autocorrelation decay is fitted by a q-exponential with q=1.5; for M=0.2, q=1.2; for M=0, q=1.1
- The anomalous diffusion exponent α ranges from 1.4-1.5 for 0.4<M(0)<1 down to α=1.2 for M(0)=0, with the M(0)=0 case tending to normal diffusion (α=1) for N=10000

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the HMF model by Latora et al. (2001-2003) that had focused on M1 initial conditions (all spins aligned), and directly contradicts the conjecture by Yamaguchi et al. (2003-2004) that dynamical anomalies exist only for very special initial conditions. The results show that anomalous behavior is more ubiquitous than previously supposed, and the relationship between the entropic index q and the diffusion exponent α follows the theoretical prediction α=2/(3-q) from Bukman and Beck (1998).
