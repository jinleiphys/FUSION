# Electromagnetic contribution to charge symmetry violation in parton distributions

> We report a calculation of the combined effect of photon radiation and quark mass differences on charge symmetry violation (CSV) in the parton distribution functions of the nucleon. Following a recent suggestion of Martin and Ryskin, the initial photon distribution is calculated in terms of coherent radiation from the proton as a whole, while the effect of the quark mass difference is based on a recent lattice QCD simulation. The distributions are then evolved to a scale at which they can be compared with experiment by including both QCD and QED radiation. Overall, at a scale of 5 GeV$^2$, the total CSV effect on the phenomenologically important difference between the $d$ and $u$-quark distributions is some 20\% larger than the value based on quark mass differences alone. In total these sources of CSV account for approximately 40\% of the NuTeV anomaly.

# Electromagnetic contribution to charge symmetry violation in parton distributions

## Key claim
The combined effect of coherent photon radiation from the proton and quark mass differences produces a total charge symmetry violation (CSV) effect on the difference between the d and u-quark distributions that is approximately 20% larger at a scale of 5 GeV² than the effect from quark mass differences alone. Together these sources account for roughly 40% of the NuTeV anomaly, though the authors note that adding the isovector EMC effect would bring the total correction to -0.0041 ± 0.0007, which would resolve the discrepancy with the Standard Model.

## Method
The authors calculated the initial photon distribution using coherent radiation from the elastic proton following the approach of Martin and Ryskin (2014), with the Dirac form factor of the proton parametrized by a dipole form. The effect of the quark mass difference was taken from lattice QCD simulations by Horsley et al. (2010) and Shanahan et al. (2013). The distributions were then evolved from low starting scales (0.26 GeV² for LO and 0.40 GeV² for NLO) to higher scales using combined QCD and QED evolution via the APFEL program, treating radiation above the model scale as incoherent emission from individual quarks.

## Key numbers
- At Q² = 10 GeV², the pure QED contribution to the second moment of δu_v is -0.00107 (LO starting scale 0.26 GeV²) and -0.00095 (NLO starting scale 0.40 GeV²)
- At Q² = 10 GeV², the pure QED contribution to the second moment of δd_v is -0.00003 (LO starting scale 0.26 GeV²) and -0.00007 (NLO starting scale 0.40 GeV²)
- The lattice QCD values for the second moments at Q² = 4 GeV² are δU_v = -0.0023(7) and δD_v = 0.0017(4)
- The total CSV correction to Δs²_W from valence quark charge symmetry violation is -0.0022 ± 0.0004
- The QED contribution to Δs²_W is -0.00039(6) and the QCD contribution is -0.00176(35)
- The momentum fraction carried by the photon at the starting scale is 0.00105 at Q₀² = 0.26 GeV² and 0.00113 at Q₀² = 0.40 GeV²

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of CSV in parton distributions by Sather (1991), Rodionov et al. (1994), and Londergan et al. (1994) using the MIT bag model, and by Martin et al. (MRST, 2005) and Gluck et al. (2005) who included photon distributions at the starting scale. The key improvement is the use of coherent photon radiation from the elastic proton at low scales, following the suggestion of Martin and Ryskin (2014), rather than the large logarithms associated with mass singularities used in earlier QED treatments. The results are consistent with the earlier calculation by Bentz et al. (2009) but with significantly improved uncertainty estimates for the QED contribution. This work enables a more precise determination of the CSV correction to the NuTeV anomaly and identifies the potential asymmetry between s and sbar distributions as the major remaining issue to resolve.
