# Low-lying continuum structures in B8 and Li8 in a microscopic model

> We search for low-lying resonances in the B8 and Li8 nuclei using a microscopic cluster model and a variational scattering method, which is analytically continued to complex energies. After fine-tuning the nucleon-nucleon interaction to get the known 1+ state of B8 at the right energy, we reproduce the known spectra of the studied nuclei. In addition, our model predicts a 1+ state at 1.3 MeV in B8, relative to the Be7+p threshold, whose corresponding pair is situated right at the Li7+n threshold in Li8. Lacking any experimental evidence for the existence of such states, it is presently uncertain whether these structures really exist or they are spurious resonances in our model. We demonstrate that the predicted state in B8, if it exists, would have important consequences for the understanding of the astrophysically important Be7(p,gamma)B8 reaction.

# Low-lying continuum structures in B8 and Li8 in a microscopic model

## Key claim
The microscopic three-cluster model predicts an additional 1+ resonance in B8 at 1.28 MeV relative to the Be7+p threshold and a corresponding 1+ state in Li8 essentially at the Li7+n threshold, but the author states it is presently uncertain whether these structures really exist or are spurious resonances in the model, as no experimental evidence supports them.

## Method
The author used a microscopic three-cluster model with a (He4+He3+p) wave function for B8 and (He4+H3+n) for Li8, employing a variational scattering method (Kohn-Hulthén) with analytic continuation of the scattering matrix to complex energies to search for poles of the S-matrix. The modified Hasegawa-Nagata nucleon-nucleon interaction was fine-tuned (about 5% modification in the central force) to reproduce the known 1+ state of B8 at 632 keV, and the same interaction was used for both nuclei.

## Key numbers
- B8 1+1 state: resonance energy Er = 0.632 MeV, width Γ = 0.034 MeV (theory); experimental Er = 0.632 ± 0.01 MeV, Γ = 0.037 ± 0.005 MeV
- B8 predicted 1+2 state: Er = 1.278 MeV, Γ = 0.564 MeV (relative to Be7+p threshold)
- B8 3+ state: theory Er = 2.98 MeV, Γ = 0.808 MeV; experiment Er = 2.183 ± 0.03 MeV, Γ = 0.35 ± 0.04 MeV
- B8 2+ ground state: theory binding energy -0.215 MeV; experiment -0.137 MeV
- Li8 1+2 predicted state: Er = 0.037 MeV, Γ = 0.006 MeV (essentially at the Li7+n threshold)
- Li8 3+ state: theory Er = 0.937 MeV, Γ = 0.327 MeV; experiment Er = 0.222 ± 0.003 MeV, Γ = 0.033 ± 0.006 MeV
- Li8 1+3 state: theory Er = 2.29 MeV, Γ = 1.0 MeV; experiment Er = 1.18 MeV, Γ ∼ 1.0 MeV
- Three-body breakup threshold in the model: 1.34 MeV for B8, 2.13 MeV for Li8 (experimental: 1.59 MeV and 2.47 MeV respectively)

## Context
This work extends the author's previous microscopic cluster model calculations for B8 ground states and the nonresonant Be7(p,γ)B8 cross section (Csoto 1993, 1997). It addresses a potential model calculation by Kim (1987) that suggested an additional M1 state around 1.4 MeV, which was criticized by Barker (1988) as unphysical. The predicted 1+2 state, if real, would complicate the separation of E1 and M1 components in the astrophysically important Be7(p,γ)B8 reaction, which is the dominant source of high-energy solar neutrinos via B8 beta decay. The results call for further theoretical and experimental work to confirm or refute the existence of these predicted states.
