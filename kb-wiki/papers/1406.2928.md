# Theoretical analysis of the astrophysical S-factor for the alpha+d -->6Li + gamma capture reaction in the two body model

> Theoretical estimations for the astrophysical S-factor and the d(alpha,gamma)6Li reaction rates are obtained on the base of the two-body model with the alpha-d potential of a simple Gaussian form, which describes correctly the phase-shifts in the S-, P-, and D-waves, the binding energy and the asymptotic normalization constant in the final S-state. Wave functions of the bound and continuum states are calculated by using the Numerov algorithm of a high accuracy. A good convergence of the results for the E1- and E2- components of the transition is shown when increasing the upper limit of effective integrals up to 40 fm. The obtained results for the S-factor and reaction rates in the temperature interval 10E+6 K < T < 10E+10 K are in a good agreement with the results of Ref. A.M. Mukhamedzhanov, et.al., Phys. Rev., C 83, 055805 (2011), where the authors used the known asymptotical form of wave function at low energies and a complicated potential at higher energies.

## Key claim

The authors show that a simple Gaussian alpha-d potential, when slightly modified to reproduce the correct asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC), can yield astrophysical S-factors and reaction rates for the d(alpha,gamma)6Li capture reaction that agree well with the more complicated potential model of Mukhamedzhanov (2011), provided the radial integrals are extended to at least 40 fm for convergence.

## Method

The two-body radial Schrodinger equation was solved using the Numerov algorithm (accuracy O(h^6)) with a fixed step of 0.05 fm for both bound and continuum states. The initial Gaussian potential from Dubovichenko (1994) was modified in the S-wave to reproduce the empirical ANC of 2.30 fm^{-1/2} from Blokhintsev (1993) while keeping the binding energy fixed at 1.474 MeV, and the phase shifts were checked against the later data of Jenny (1983). The E1 and E2 transition matrix elements were computed by integrating the product of initial scattering and final bound state wave functions up to 40 fm to ensure convergence.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the alpha+d bound state: 1.474 MeV
- ANC from Blokhintsev (1993): C_{alpha d} = 2.30 +/- 0.12 fm^{-1/2}
- Modified potential V_M(r) = -92.44 exp(-0.25 r^2) MeV reproduces ANC = 2.31 fm^{-1/2}
- Initial potential V_D(r) = -76.12 exp(-0.2 r^2) MeV yields ANC = 2.53 fm^{-1/2}, overestimating the empirical value by 0.23 fm^{-1/2}
- Convergence of E2 transition integrals requires upper limit of 35-40 fm; E1 integrals converge faster
- Dominant E1 contribution comes from the ^3P_2 entrance channel; dominant E2 contribution comes from the ^3D_3 channel below the resonance
- Reaction rates calculated for temperature interval 10^6 K <= T <= 10^10 K (0.001 <= T_9 <= 10)

## Context

This work extends the two-body model approach of Dubovichenko (1994, 2010) by using a high-accuracy Numerov algorithm instead of a Gaussian expansion for the wave functions, which poorly describes the asymptotics beyond 10-15 fm. The results are consistent with the more complicated phase-equivalent potential approach of Mukhamedzhanov (2011), which used integro-differential transformations to correct the ANC. The paper enables future studies of the d(alpha,gamma)6Li reaction at astrophysical energies (50-400 keV) where experimental data remain poorly defined, and demonstrates that a simple local potential suffices if the ANC is correctly reproduced.
