# Neutron Star Masses, Radii and Equation of State

> A review of current neutron star masses and radius measurements is given and the resulting constraints on modern equation of states for nuclear matter. Phase transitions in particular to quark matter is discussed and the novel color superconducting mixed phases.

# Neutron Star Masses, Radii and Equation of State

## Key claim
Modern microscopic equations of state are converging and predict that neutron stars in the mass range 0.8–1.8 solar masses all have radii around 11.5 km, but this prediction applies only up to a few times nuclear saturation density and breaks down at higher densities where phase transitions may occur. The narrow observed mass range of double neutron star binaries (1.36 ± 0.08 solar masses) may be a selection effect in forming such systems rather than a universal neutron star birth mass.

## Method
The paper reviews observational determinations of neutron star masses from binary pulsars, X-ray binaries, and quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs), and radius measurements from thermal emission, X-ray bursts, burst oscillations, and absorption lines. It then compares these observations to theoretical equations of state derived from nucleon-nucleon interactions constrained by scattering experiments, nuclear binding energies, and many-body calculations, and discusses possible phase transitions to quark matter, hyperon matter, and color-superconducting phases.

## Key numbers
- Double neutron star binary masses: 1.36 ± 0.08 solar masses (six systems)
- PSR J0437-4715 mass: 1.58 ± 0.18 solar masses (2σ error bars)
- Vela X-1 mass: 1.87 (+0.23, -0.17) solar masses
- Cygnus X-2 mass: 1.8 ± 0.4 solar masses
- 4U 1820-30 QPO implies neutron star mass ≈ 2.3 solar masses
- RX J1856.5-3754 surface temperature: 57 eV; distance ≈ 61 pc; radius from one-temperature fit: R∞ ≈ 7 km; reanalysis gives R∞ ≈ 15 km corresponding to R ≈ 12 km for M = 1.4 solar masses
- CXOU 132619.7-472910.8 in NGC 5139: T = 66 ± 5 eV, R∞ = 14.3 ± 2.1 km (90% confidence)
- 4U 1636-53 burst oscillations give M/R < 0.16 and R > 12–13 km for M = 1.4 solar masses
- Modern EOS prediction: neutron stars of 0.8–1.8 solar masses have radius ≈ 11.5 km
- Maximum mass from causality: M ≲ 2.0–2.3 solar masses
- Strange quark mass: m_s ≈ 150–200 MeV; quark chemical potentials: μ ≈ 400–600 MeV; m_s²/2μ ≈ 10–25 MeV
- Electron chemical potential in pure β-equilibrium nuclear matter: μ_e ≈ 100–200 MeV; in bulk quark matter: μ_e ≈ m_s²/4μ ≈ 5–10 MeV
- Neutron star temperatures after cooling: T ≲ 10⁶ K ≈ 10⁻⁴ MeV

## Context
This review summarizes the observational and theoretical status of neutron star structure as of 2002, extending earlier reviews by Lattimer, Balb, and the author's own previous work (annrev, physrep). It discusses the modern EOS of Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998) and compares with the older FPS EOS. The paper enables future work by identifying which observational methods (QPOs, burst oscillations, gravitational lensing, absorption lines with Chandra and XMM) can discriminate between competing EOS and phase transitions, and by outlining the conditions under which color-superconducting phases (CFL, 2CS, LOFF) might appear in neutron star cores.
