# Pion Form Factor in the Light-Front

> The pion electromagnetic form factor is calculated with a light-front quark model. The "plus" and "minus" component of the electromagnetic current are used to calculate the electromagnetic form factor in the Breit frame with two models for the q\bar{q} vertex. The light front constituent quark models describes very well hadronic wave function for pseudo-scalar and vector particles. Symmetry problems arinsing in the light-front approach are solved by the pole dislocation method. The results are compared with new experimental data and with other quark models.

## Key claim

The light-front constituent quark model, using both "plus" and "minus" components of the electromagnetic current with the pole dislocation method to restore Lorentz covariance, can describe the pion electromagnetic form factor at both low and high momentum transfers, though differences exist between the nonsymmetric and symmetric vertex models that remain under investigation.

## Method

The pion electromagnetic form factor was calculated in the Breit frame using the "plus" and "minus" components of the electromagnetic current within a light-front constituent quark model. Two models for the pion-quark-antiquark vertex were employed: a nonsymmetric vertex (NSY) and a symmetric vertex (SY), with the pole dislocation method used to handle non-valence (pair term) contributions that arise for the minus component. The calculations were performed with a constituent quark mass of 0.220 GeV and regulator masses chosen to fit either the experimental pion radius or the pion decay constant.

## Key numbers

- Pion experimental radius: \( r_{exp} = 0.67 \pm 0.02 \) fm (Amendolia 1984), used to fix the regulator mass \( m_R = 0.946 \) GeV for the nonsymmetric vertex model
- Pion decay constant experimental value: \( f_{\pi}^{exp} = 92.4 \) MeV, used to fix the regulator mass \( m_R = 0.6 \) GeV for the symmetric vertex model
- Constituent quark mass: \( m = 0.220 \) GeV for both vertex models
- Pion mass: \( m_{\pi} = 0.140 \) GeV
- Momentum transfer range extended up to 10 \( (GeV/c)^2 \)

## Context

This work extends previous light-front models for the pion (Pacheco 1999, Pacheco 2002) to higher momentum transfers and compares results with new experimental data, QCD sum rules (Nesterenko 1982), and vector meson dominance models (Conne 1997). The approach addresses symmetry problems in light-front calculations through the pole dislocation method and enables further investigation of the differences between symmetric and nonsymmetric vertex models, with a more complete study in preparation (Pacheco 2004).
