# Meson-exchange currents and quasielastic predictions for neutrino-nucleus scattering

> We review some recent progress in the study of electroweak interactions in nuclei within the SuSAv2-MEC model. The model has the capability to predict (anti)neutrino scattering observables on different nuclei. The theoretical predictions are compared with the recent T2K $ν_μ-^{16}$O data and good agreement is found at all kinematics. The results are very similar to those obtained for $ν_μ-^{12}$C scattering, except at low energies, where some differences emerge. The role of meson-exchange currents in the two-particle two-hole channel is analyzed in some detail. In particular it is shown that the density dependence of these contributions is different from what is found for the quasielastic response.

## Key claim

The SuSAv2-MEC model, which combines superscaling analysis with meson-exchange current contributions, predicts neutrino-nucleus scattering observables on oxygen and carbon that agree well with T2K data, but the 2p2h MEC effects are smaller (approximately 15-20% of the total cross section at the peak) than those observed in MiniBooNE and MINERvA analyses due to the narrower T2K neutrino flux distribution.

## Method

The SuSAv2 model incorporates both the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach for low-to-intermediate momentum transfer and the relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) for high momentum transfer, combined through a q-dependent blending function. Two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) excitations induced by meson-exchange currents (MEC) are treated within the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model, allowing exact and fully relativistic calculations. The model predictions are compared with inclusive electron scattering data on oxygen and with T2K charged-current muon neutrino data on oxygen, using a Fermi momentum of k_F = 230 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The 2p2h MEC contribution to the T2K flux-folded double differential cross section on oxygen is approximately 15% of the total at the maximum of the peak, and about 20% at forward angles.
- The total integrated cross section per neutron for carbon and oxygen differs by at most 2-3% when including both QE and 2p2h MEC contributions.
- The Fermi momentum used for oxygen is fixed to k_F = 230 MeV/c.
- The reduced 2p2h MEC response scales approximately as k_F^2 when represented as a function of the scaling variable psi'_QE.

## Context

This work extends the SuSAv2-MEC model, previously applied to carbon (Megias 2016), to oxygen targets relevant for the T2K experiment, and builds on the scaling and superscaling properties of electron scattering data established by Amaro (2004). The analysis of the density dependence of 2p2h MEC contributions, showing a scaling law different from the quasielastic response, enables extrapolation of results between different nuclei without performing explicit calculations, which is particularly relevant for future applications to argon targets.
