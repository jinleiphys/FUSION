# Two photon couplings of the lightest isoscalars from BELLE data

> Amplitude Analysis of two photon production of $ŽĆŽĆ$ and ${\overline K}K$, using S-matrix constraints and fitting all available data, including the latest precision results from Belle, yields a single partial wave solution up to 1.4 GeV. The two photon couplings of the $Žā/f_0(500)$, $f_0(980)$ and $f_2(1270)$ are determined from the residues of the resonance poles.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a single partial wave solution for two-photon production of ŽĆŽĆ and K╠ģK up to 1.4 GeV can be obtained by combining S-matrix constraints with all available data, including the latest Belle results, but this solution is only valid where ŽĆŽĆ and K╠ģK saturate unitarity (roughly up to 1.4-1.5 GeV), and the analysis cannot be extended higher because multi-meson production information is missing.

## Method

The authors performed a coupled-channel amplitude analysis of ╬│╬│ ŌåÆ ŽĆŌü║ŽĆŌü╗, ŽĆŌü░ŽĆŌü░, and K╠ģK data up to 1.4 GeV, using unitarity constraints that link these two-photon reactions to the corresponding meson-meson scattering amplitudes. The hadronic T-matrix elements, constructed from dispersive analyses of ŽĆŽĆ scattering data (including NA48-2, CERN-Munich, Argonne, and Brookhaven experiments), were embedded in a K-matrix framework, while the two-photon coupling functions ╬▒(s) were parametrized as polynomials after dividing out the zeros of the hadronic scattering amplitudes. The fit included approximately 3000 data points for ŽĆŽĆ channels and 350 data points for K╠ģK, with systematic uncertainties in absolute normalization treated as a possible shift beyond 700 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier amplitude analyses of two-photon to ŽĆŽĆ data from SLAC and DESY (Mark II, CELLO, Crystal Ball) performed 25 years ago by the same group, which had yielded multiple distinct solutions (A-E in MRP90, 1 and 2 in MRP98). The addition of Belle's ŽĆŌü░ŽĆŌü░ and KŌéøKŌéø data, particularly the latter with accurate coverage from threshold upwards and angular information out to cos╬Ė = 0.6-0.8, reduces the range of solutions to a single one. The resulting amplitudes are a key input for the newly developed dispersive approach to calculating hadronic light-by-light scattering for the muon (g-2), needed to interpret the BNL measurement and assess prospects for the future Fermilab experiment. The results differ from earlier model calculations by Achasov and collaborators (favoring tetraquark composition for fŌéĆ(980) with ~270 eV width) and by Hanhart et al. (predicting ~220 eV for a K╠ģK molecular composition), though all are reasonably close to the extracted 320 ┬▒ 50 eV.
