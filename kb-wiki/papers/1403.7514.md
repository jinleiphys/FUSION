# Two photon couplings of the lightest isoscalars from BELLE data

> Amplitude Analysis of two photon production of $ππ$ and ${\overline K}K$, using S-matrix constraints and fitting all available data, including the latest precision results from Belle, yields a single partial wave solution up to 1.4 GeV. The two photon couplings of the $σ/f_0(500)$, $f_0(980)$ and $f_2(1270)$ are determined from the residues of the resonance poles.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a single partial wave solution for two-photon production of ππ and K̅K up to 1.4 GeV can be obtained by combining S-matrix constraints with all available data, including the latest Belle results, but this solution is only valid where ππ and K̅K saturate unitarity (roughly up to 1.4-1.5 GeV), and the analysis cannot be extended higher because multi-meson production information is missing.

## Method

The authors performed a coupled-channel amplitude analysis of γγ → π⁺π⁻, π⁰π⁰, and K̅K data up to 1.4 GeV, using unitarity constraints that link these two-photon reactions to the corresponding meson-meson scattering amplitudes. The hadronic T-matrix elements, constructed from dispersive analyses of ππ scattering data (including NA48-2, CERN-Munich, Argonne, and Brookhaven experiments), were embedded in a K-matrix framework, while the two-photon coupling functions α(s) were parametrized as polynomials after dividing out the zeros of the hadronic scattering amplitudes. The fit included approximately 3000 data points for ππ channels and 350 data points for K̅K, with systematic uncertainties in absolute normalization treated as a possible shift beyond 700 MeV.

## Key numbers

- σ/f₀(500) pole position: 0.441 − i0.272 GeV, with two-photon coupling |g| = 0.26 ± 0.01 GeV and phase 105° ± 3°, corresponding to Γ(γγ) = 2.05 ± 0.21 keV
- f₀(980) pole position: 0.998 − i0.021 GeV, with two-photon coupling |g| = 0.16 ± 0.01 GeV and phase −175° ± 5°, corresponding to Γ(γγ) = 0.32 ± 0.05 keV
- f₂(1270) pole position: 1.267 − i0.108 GeV, with helicity-0 coupling |g| = 0.35 ± 0.03 GeV (phase 168° ± 6°) and helicity-2 coupling |g| = 1.13 ± 0.08 GeV (phase 173° ± 6°), total Γ(γγ) = 2.93 ± 0.40 keV with 8.7% ± 1.7% from helicity-0
- Pole position uncertainties: ±10 MeV for σ and f₂, ±3 MeV for f₀(980)
- Belle charged pion data have systematic shift of approximately ±5%

## Context

This work extends the earlier amplitude analyses of two-photon to ππ data from SLAC and DESY (Mark II, CELLO, Crystal Ball) performed 25 years ago by the same group, which had yielded multiple distinct solutions (A-E in MRP90, 1 and 2 in MRP98). The addition of Belle's π⁰π⁰ and KₛKₛ data, particularly the latter with accurate coverage from threshold upwards and angular information out to cosθ = 0.6-0.8, reduces the range of solutions to a single one. The resulting amplitudes are a key input for the newly developed dispersive approach to calculating hadronic light-by-light scattering for the muon (g-2), needed to interpret the BNL measurement and assess prospects for the future Fermilab experiment. The results differ from earlier model calculations by Achasov and collaborators (favoring tetraquark composition for f₀(980) with ~270 eV width) and by Hanhart et al. (predicting ~220 eV for a K̅K molecular composition), though all are reasonably close to the extracted 320 ± 50 eV.
