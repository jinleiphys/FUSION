# Triaxial rigidity of $^{166}$Er and its Bohr-model realization

> The triaxial nature of low-lying rotational bands of $^{166}$Er is presented from the viewpoint of the Bohr Hamiltonian and from that of many-fermion calculations by the Monte Carlo shell model and the constrained Hartree-Fock method with projections. A recently proposed novel picture of those bands suggests definite triaxial shapes of those bands, in contrast to the traditional view with the prolate ground-state band and the $γ$-vibrational excited band. Excitation level energies and E2 transitions can be described well by the Bohr Hamiltonian and by the many-fermion approaches, where rather rigid triaxiality plays vital roles, although certain fluctuations occur in shell-model wave functions. Based on the potential energy surfaces with the projections, we show how the triaxial rigidity appears and what the softness of the triaxiality implies. The excitation to the so-called double $γ$-phonon state is discussed briefly.

## Key claim

The low-lying rotational bands of $^{166}$Er, traditionally interpreted as a prolate ground-state band with a gamma-vibrational excited band, can instead be understood as arising from a common triaxial intrinsic shape with rigidity, although some softness appears in the shell-model wave functions and the triaxial rigidity manifests as a rigid intrinsic state that remains the major component of gamma-soft wave functions spreading around gamma = 9 degrees.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo shell model (MCSM) calculations and constrained Hartree-Fock (CHF) calculations with angular-momentum and parity projections after variation, using the same Hamiltonian for both approaches. They also solved the Bohr Hamiltonian with a square-well potential in the gamma degree of freedom (gamma = 4-14 degrees) and with a rigid triaxial approximation (delta function at gamma = 9 degrees), fitting the inertia parameter B to reproduce the observed excitation energy of the second 2+ state and the E2 strength parameter t*beta_2 to reproduce the B(E2; 2+_1 -> 0+_1) value.

## Key numbers

- The minimum of the unprojected potential energy surface occurs at beta_2 ~ 0.3 and gamma ~ 9 degrees, indicating a triaxial shape.
- The T-plot analysis shows MCSM basis vectors concentrated in the range gamma = 4-14 degrees up to 99%, with beta_2 confined narrowly to around 0.29.
- The angular-momentum and parity projection lowers the 0+ PES minimum by 0.63 MeV at gamma_0 ~ 9.5 degrees, making the minimum more profound than in prior works where the lowering was less than about 0.2 MeV.
- The square-well Bohr Hamiltonian (gamma = 4-14 degrees) and the rigid triaxial model (gamma = 9 degrees) produce excitation energies and B(E2) values in "surprisingly good" and "almost perfect" agreement with experiment and MCSM results, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the recent MCSM study of Otsuka (2019) that proposed a triaxial picture for $^{166}$Er, contradicting the traditional prolate-plus-gamma-vibration interpretation dating to Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and textbook presentations such as Ring and Schuck (1980). The authors compare with prior constrained Hartree-Fock calculations by Li (2010) and Chen (2017) that found shallow PES minima deviating from prolate shape but too shallow (less than about 0.2 MeV) to drive the ground state off prolate, and with the angular-momentum projected calculations of Hayashi (1984) for $^{168}$Er and $^{188}$Os. The results enable a unified description of the lowest two rotational bands as consequences of common triaxiality, and suggest that the Davydov rigid triaxial model (1958, 1959) can describe E2 matrix elements even when states exhibit some gamma softness.
