# Quark-Model Baryon-Baryon Interaction Applied to the Neutron-Deuteron Scattering (II) Polalization Observables of the Elastic Scattering

> The neutron-deuteron (nd) scattering is solved in the Faddeev formalism, employing the energy-independent version of the quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2. The differential cross sections and the spin polarization of the elastic scattering up to the neutron incident energy $E_n=65$ MeV are well reproduced without reinforcing fss2 with the three-body force. The vector analyzing-power of the neutron, $A_y(θ)$, in the energy region $E_n$ < 25 MeV is largely improved in comparison with the predictions by the meson-exchange potentials, thus yielding a partial solution of the long-standing $A_y$-puzzle owing to the nonlocality of the short-range repulsion produced by the quark-model baryon-baryon interaction. The large Coulomb effect in the vector and tensor analyzing-powers in $E_n$ < 10 MeV is also analyzed based on the Vincent and Phatak method and recent detailed studies by other authors.

## Key claim

The quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2, without any three-body force, yields a partial solution to the long-standing nucleon analyzing-power puzzle in neutron-deuteron scattering at incident energies below 25 MeV, though a difference of about 15% remains in the magnitude of the maximum peaks of \(A_y(\theta)\).

## Method

The authors solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations in the Faddeev formalism using the Noyes-Kowalski method for the deuteron singularity and a spline interpolation technique for the free three-body Green function singularity. They employed the energy-independent renormalized resonating-group method (RGM) kernel from the quark-model interaction fss2, represented as a Gaussian nonlocal potential that preserves both on-shell properties and nonlocality. For proton-deuteron scattering at low energies, the Vincent and Phatak method with a cut-off Coulomb potential was applied to include Coulomb effects.

## Key numbers

- The spin-doublet \(nd\) scattering length is \({}^{2}a_{nd}=0.66\) fm and the quartet scattering length is \({}^{4}a_{nd}=6.30\) fm, calculated without three-body force and without charge dependence of the nucleon-nucleon force.
- Differential cross sections and spin polarization are reproduced up to neutron incident energy \(E_n=65\) MeV.
- The vector analyzing-power \(A_y(\theta)\) shows improvement in the energy region \(E_n \leq 25\) MeV compared to meson-exchange potential predictions.
- Large Coulomb effects in vector and tensor analyzing-powers are analyzed for \(E_n \leq 10\) MeV.
- The Gaussian nonlocal potential reproduces phase shift parameters with accuracy less than 0.1 degree.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Fukukawa and Fujiwara, 2011, referred to as Paper I) on neutron-deuteron elastic scattering differential cross sections to include all spin polarization observables. It addresses the long-standing nucleon analyzing-power puzzle, where rigorous three-nucleon calculations using meson-exchange potentials failed to reproduce measured \(A_y(\theta)\) values. The improvement arises from the nonlocal description of short-range repulsion in the quark-model interaction, which yields a more attractive \({}^{2}S_{1/2}\) phase shift. The Coulomb analysis for low-energy proton-deuteron scattering builds on the Vincent and Phatak method and comparisons with studies by Berthold (1990), Alt (2002), Kievsky (1996, 2001), Deltuva (2005), and Ishikawa (2009).
