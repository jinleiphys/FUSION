# Emission of intermediate mass fragments from hot $^{116}$Ba$^*$ formed in low-energy $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni reaction

> The complex fragments (or intermediate mass fragments) observed in the low-energy $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni$\to ^{116}$Ba$^*$ reaction, are studied within the dynamical cluster decay model for s-wave with the use of the temperature-dependent liquid drop, Coulomb and proximity energies. The important result is that, due to the temperature effects in liquid drop energy, the explicit preference for $α$-like fragments is washed out, though the $^{12}$C (or the complementary $^{104}$Sn) decay is still predicted to be one of the most probable $α$-nucleus decay for this reaction. The production rates for non-$α$ like intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) are now higher and the light particle production is shown to accompany the IMFs at all incident energies, without involving any statistical evaporation process in the model. The comparisons between the experimental data and the (s-wave) calculations for IMFs production cross sections are rather satisfactory and the contributions from other $\ell$-waves need to be added for a further improvement of these comparisons and for calculations of the total kinetic energies of fragments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including temperature-dependent liquid drop energy in the dynamical cluster decay model washes out the explicit preference for alpha-like fragments in the decay of hot $^{116}$Ba$^*$, though the $^{12}$C (or complementary $^{104}$Sn) decay remains one of the most probable alpha-nucleus decays. This result is conditional on using s-wave ($\ell=0$) calculations only, with the authors noting that contributions from other partial waves need to be added for further improvement.

## Method

The authors applied the dynamical cluster decay model (an adaptation of the preformed cluster model) to the $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni$\rightarrow ^{116}$Ba$^*$ reaction, using a complete temperature-dependent potential including the liquid drop energy from Davidson et al. (1994), Coulomb energy, and proximity potential. They solved the stationary Schrodinger equation in the mass asymmetry coordinate at the first turning point R$_a$=C$_t$+$\overline{\Delta R}$, with the neck-length parameter $\overline{\Delta R}$ as the only adjustable parameter, and calculated preformation probabilities via a Boltzmann-like function and penetrabilities via the WKB approximation for s-wave only.

## Key numbers

- Compound nucleus excitation energies E$_{CN}^*$ = 96.4 to 130.9 MeV for the $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni reaction at laboratory energies 348, 371, 394, and 630 MeV
- Compound nucleus temperatures T = 2.91, 3.06, 3.20, and 4.42 MeV corresponding to the four experimental center-of-mass energies E$_{c.m.}$ = 174, 185.5, 197, and 315 MeV
- Neck-length parameter values $\overline{\Delta R}$ = 0.226, 0.252, 0.281, and 0.466 fm for the four respective temperatures
- Effective temperature of the emitting system estimated at about 2.2 MeV, approximately half the compound nucleus temperature of 4.42 MeV
- Total excitation energy (TXE) in the exit channel observed to be about 50 MeV, compared to available compound nucleus excitation energy of 96.4 to 130.9 MeV
- Entrance-channel Q-value Q$_{in}$ = -67.59 MeV for $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni$\rightarrow ^{116}$Ba

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier cluster decay model (Gupta et al., 2002) by incorporating temperature-dependent liquid drop energy from Davidson et al. (1994), whereas the previous work used temperature-independent macroscopic liquid drop energy. The model provides an alternative to the Hauser-Feshbach formalism used in codes such as BUSCO (del Campo et al., 1991) and the statistical fission model of Moretto (1975) used in GEMINI (Charity et al., 1988), and is applied to experimental data from del Campo et al. (1991, 1988) at 630 MeV and from Commara et al. (2000) at lower energies. The results enable future inclusion of higher partial waves for improved comparisons with experimental cross sections and total kinetic energy calculations.
