# Origin of the Strong Toroidal Magnetic Field in Magnetars

> A simple model of chiral asymmetry is proposed to interpret the origin of the strong toroidal magnetic field. The electrons relevant to dynamics forming the the field are in a quantume degenerate state with ultra-relativistic Fermi energy. The system is described by Dirac Hartree Fock method using scaled h-bar method. Neutron stars are rotating and have large angular momentum which is formed by cranking model and breaks time reversal. Dirac current is decomposed into convection and spin currents due to Clifford number. The strong toroidal magnetic field is formed by the spin like current resulted by the chiral asymmetry brought about electron capture caused by the parity-violating weak interaction.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the strong toroidal magnetic field in magnetars (≈1×10¹² T) originates from a spin-like current produced by chiral asymmetry in the electron system, where positive-chirality electrons remain after parity-violating weak interaction capture of negative-chirality electrons by protons during gravitational collapse, and this current is sustained by its interaction with the vector potential of the toroidal field.

## Method

The authors model the electron system in a rotating neutron star using the Dirac Hartree-Fock method with a scaled-ℏ approach (ℏ⋆=10¹⁸ℏ) to handle the ≈3×10⁵⁵ particles. They employ the cranking model to describe the rotational motion and the breaking of time-reversal symmetry, and decompose the Dirac current into convection and spin currents using the Gordon decomposition with Clifford numbers. The chiral asymmetry is introduced through selective electron capture via parity-violating weak interactions, and the resulting spin current is calculated using helicity representation and projection operators.

## Key numbers

- Toroidal magnetic field strength suggested by hard X-ray data: ≈1×10¹² T (1 TT)
- Poloidal (dipole) field strength: 0.013 TT
- Electron density in the magnetar: ≈0.004 fm⁻³, which is 2×10¹² times higher than in iron metal
- Estimated magnetization from aligned electrons: μ₀μBρe ≈ 50 TT
- Neutron star mass and radius used in the example: 1.5 M⊙ and 12 km
- Average baryon density: 0.25 fm⁻³ (1.4 times nuclear density)
- Neutron Fermi energy: 76 MeV
- Scaling factor γ values used: 1 and 0.5
- Electric field maximum at rmx=10.6 km: 1.4×10⁴ V/m
- Polarization charge density at rmx: 2.4×10⁻¹¹ C/m³, corresponding to 1.5×10⁸ m⁻³ electrons
- Ratio of polarization charge to electron density: 3.4×10⁻³⁵

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion of toroidal magnetic field generation in magnetars by Ohnishi and Yamamoto (2014), who used magnetic helicity concepts but did not show the structure or formation mechanism, and addresses criticism by Grabowska et al. (2014) regarding rapid decay due to chiral mixing through the mass term. The present study provides a more practical demonstration using the spin current from the Gordon decomposition of the Dirac current, and enables future systematic studies including hadron treatment with general relativity.
