# Di-nucleons do not form bound states at heavy pion mass

> We perform a high-statistics lattice QCD calculation of the low-energy two-nucleon scattering amplitudes. In order to address discrepancies in the literature, the calculation is performed at a heavy pion mass in the limit that the light quark masses are equal to the physical strange quark mass, $m_π= m_K \simeq 714 $ MeV. Using a state-of-the-art momentum space method, we rule out the presence of a bound di-nucleon in both the isospin 0 (deuteron) and 1 (di-neutron) channels, in contrast with many previous results that made use of compact hexaquark creation operators. In order to diagnose the discrepancy, we add such hexaquark interpolating operators to our basis and find that they do not affect the determination of the two-nucleon finite volume spectrum, and thus they do not couple to deeply bound di-nucleons that are missed by the momentum-space operators. Further, we perform a high-statistics calculation of the HAL QCD potential on the same gauge ensembles and find qualitative agreement with our main results. We conclude that di-nucleons do not form bound states at heavy pion masses and that previous identification of deeply bound di-nucleons must have arisen from a misidentification of the spectrum from off-diagonal elements of a correlation function.

## Key claim

Using a high-statistics lattice QCD calculation at a heavy pion mass of approximately 714 MeV, the authors rule out the presence of bound di-nucleons in both the isospin 0 (deuteron) and isospin 1 (di-neutron) channels, contradicting many previous results that had identified deeply bound states. The authors conclude that earlier identifications of bound di-nucleons arose from a misidentification of the spectrum due to off-diagonal elements of correlation functions when using compact hexaquark creation operators.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations on a 48^3 x 96 lattice with spacing a approximately 0.086 fm, using N_f = 2 + 1 isotropic clover-Wilson action with non-perturbative O(a) improvement and Luscher-Weisz improved gluon action. They constructed two-nucleon operators using the stochastic Laplacian Heaviside (sLapH) method with 384 eigenvectors, enabling momentum-space creation operators, and determined the finite-volume spectrum using a Hermitian correlator matrix method with a single-pivot generalized eigenvalue problem. A "conspiracy model" was developed for the correlator analysis, where the two-nucleon correlation function energies are parameterized as sums of single-nucleon energies plus interaction energies, with the number of NN states derived from the number used for single nucleons.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: m_pi = m_K approximately 714 MeV, set in the SU(3) flavor limit with light quark masses equal to the physical strange quark mass
* Lattice spacing: a approximately 0.086 fm, with volume 48^3 x 96 and m_pi L approximately 15
* Total of 1490 gauge configurations used from four replica
* Di-nucleons ruled out with greater than or equal to 5 sigma confidence for the deuteron channel and greater than or equal to 2.5 sigma for the di-neutron channel
* Number of Laplacian eigenvectors used: N_ev = 384

## Context

This work directly addresses a long-standing discrepancy in lattice QCD calculations of two-nucleon systems at heavy pion masses, where earlier calculations by NPLQCD (2012) and Yamazaki (2012) found deeply bound di-nucleons while HAL QCD results by Iritani (2016, 2017, 2018) found no bound states. The authors use the same gauge ensembles as their previous work by Horz (2020) and extend the analysis with improved methods, including the conspiracy model for correlator fitting and direct comparison with the HAL QCD potential method on identical ensembles. This result enables more reliable calculations of two-nucleon scattering amplitudes and removes a systematic uncertainty that has hindered progress toward calculations at the physical pion mass.
