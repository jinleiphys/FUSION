# The D coefficient in neutron beta decay in effective field theory

> In this paper we explore the time-reversal-odd triple-correlation coefficient in neutron beta decay, the so-called "D coefficient", using heavy-baryon effective field theory with photon degrees of freedom. We find that this framework allows us to reproduce the known results for the contribution which comes from final-state interactions, and also to discuss higher-order corrections. In particular we are able to show that in the heavy-baryon limit all electromagnetic contributions vanish. By calculating the leading correction to the known result, we give a final expression which is accurate to better than 1%. Hence we extend downwards the range over which the D coefficient could be used to explore time-violation from new physics.

# The D coefficient in neutron beta decay in effective field theory

## Key claim

The authors show that the Callan-Treiman result for the D coefficient (a time-reversal-odd triple correlation in neutron beta decay) is reproduced as the leading non-vanishing contribution in a heavy-baryon effective field theory, and that all electromagnetic contributions vanish in the heavy-baryon limit, meaning corrections to the known result are higher-order and the final expression is accurate to better than 1%.

## Method

The authors employ a pionless heavy-baryon effective field theory (HBχPT) with photon degrees of freedom, treating α/2π and Q̄/mN as expansion parameters of comparable magnitude (~10⁻³). They calculate the D coefficient from interference between tree-level amplitudes and the imaginary parts of one-loop diagrams at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), and explicitly verify that two-photon-loop diagrams and higher-order electromagnetic contributions vanish in the heavy-baryon limit. The leading higher-order correction from the pseudoscalar form factor of the nucleon (enhanced by 1/mπ²) is also computed.

## Key numbers

- The Callan-Treiman result for the D coefficient is D_CT = (0.228(p_e^max/p_e) + 1.083(p_e/p_e^max)) × 10⁻⁵
- The pseudoscalar correction is D_π = -5.88(p_e^max/p_e) × 10⁻⁸, giving a correction of between -0.5% and -2.5%
- The Standard Model prediction for D is D_SM ≤ 10⁻¹², while the MSSM prediction is D_MSSM ~ 10⁻⁷
- Experimental bounds: D = [-0.6 ± 1.2(stat) ± 0.5(syst)] × 10⁻³ from the emiT experiment, and D = [-2.8 ± 6.4(stat) ± 3.0(syst)] × 10⁻⁴ from the Trine collaboration
- The expansion parameters are α/2π ~ Q̄/mN ~ 10⁻³
- The isovector magnetic moment used is μV = μp - μn = 4.706
- The axial coupling constant used is gA = 1.2695 ± 0.0029

## Context

This work extends the calculation of the D coefficient originally performed by Callan and Treiman (1967) using a modern effective field theory framework, confirming their result and establishing its accuracy. The authors show that the heavy-baryon EFT approach, previously applied to other correlation coefficients in neutron decay by Ando et al. (2004), allows systematic inclusion of higher-order corrections. The result enables experimental searches for time-reversal violation from new physics (such as MSSM or leptoquark models) at the level of 10⁻⁷ or below, by providing a Standard Model background known to better than 1%.
