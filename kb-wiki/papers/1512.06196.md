# A Complete Equation of State for Astrophysical Simulations

> We construct a complete equation of state (EOS) covering a wide range of temperature, proton fraction, and baryon density for the use of astrophysical simulations. We employ the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory to describe nuclear interactions and adopt the Thomas-Fermi approximation to describe the nonuniform nuclear matter. The uniform matter and nonuniform matter are studied consistently using the same RMF theory.

# A Complete Equation of State for Astrophysical Simulations

## Key claim

The paper constructs a complete equation of state (EOS) for astrophysical simulations using relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with the TM1 parameter set and the Thomas-Fermi approximation, covering a wider range of temperature, proton fraction, and baryon density than the previous Shen EOS (EOS1), with the hedge that the improved EOS tables (EOS2 and EOS3) are recalculations of the earlier work with refined grids and, in the case of EOS3, the inclusion of hyperons.

## Method

The authors employ the RMF theory with the TM1 parameter set to describe nuclear interactions, treating both uniform matter and nonuniform matter consistently within the same theoretical framework. For nonuniform matter, they adopt the Thomas-Fermi approximation, assuming each heavy nucleus sits at the center of a charge-neutral Wigner-Seitz cell containing a vapor of nucleons, electrons, and alpha-particles, with nucleon density distributions parameterized by a specific functional form. At fixed temperature, proton fraction, and baryon mass density, the thermodynamically favorable state is determined by minimizing the free energy density with respect to the nuclear structure parameters.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range: EOS2 and EOS3 cover \(-1.0 \leq \log_{10}(T) \leq 2.6\) (MeV), compared to EOS1 which covered \(-1.0 \leq \log_{10}(T) \leq 2.0\) (MeV)
- Temperature grid spacing: EOS2 and EOS3 use \(\Delta \log_{10}(T) = 0.04\), while EOS1 used \(\Delta \log_{10}(T) \simeq 0.1\)
- Proton fraction range: EOS2 and EOS3 cover \(0 \leq Y_p \leq 0.65\) with \(\Delta Y_p = 0.01\), while EOS1 covered \(-2 \leq \log_{10}(Y_p) \leq -0.25\) with \(\Delta \log_{10}(Y_p) = 0.025\)
- Baryon mass density range: EOS2 and EOS3 cover \(5.1 \leq \log_{10}(\rho_B) \leq 16\) (g cm\(^{-3}\)), while EOS1 covered \(5.1 \leq \log_{10}(\rho_B) \leq 15.4\) (g cm\(^{-3}\))
- Constituents: EOS2 includes neutrons, protons, and alpha-particles; EOS3 additionally includes \(\Lambda\) hyperons

## Context

This work extends the earlier Shen EOS (Shen et al., 1998) by recalculating the equation of state with improved grid spacing and range according to user requirements, and by adding hyperonic degrees of freedom in EOS3. It provides an alternative to the Lattimer-Swesty EOS (Lattimer and Swesty, 1991), which used a compressible liquid-drop model with Skyrme forces, by employing a consistent RMF treatment for both uniform and nonuniform matter. The improved EOS tables are intended for use in numerical simulations of supernovae and neutron star studies.
