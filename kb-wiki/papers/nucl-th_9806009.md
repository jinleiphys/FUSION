# Relativistic Mean Field Approach and the Pseudo-Spin Symmetry

> Based on the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach the existence of the broken pseudo-spin symmetry is investigated. Both spherical RMF and constrained deformed RMF calculations are carried out employing realistic Lagrangian parameters for spherical and for deformed sample nuclei. The quasi - degenerate pseudo-spin doublets are confirmed to exist near the fermi surface for both spherical and deformed nuclei.

# Relativistic Mean Field Approach and the Pseudo-Spin Symmetry

## Key claim
Quasi-degenerate pseudo-spin doublets exist near the Fermi surface for both spherical and deformed nuclei, but the pseudo-spin symmetry is necessarily broken in realistic nuclei because the exact symmetry limit (where attractive scalar and repulsive vector potentials are equal in magnitude) produces only unphysical Dirac sea states with no normalizable bound valence states. The symmetry is restored better as orbitals approach the continuum.

## Method
Spherical relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations in coordinate space were performed for 208Pb, and constrained deformed RMF calculations using an anisotropic oscillator basis were carried out for 154Dy. The Lagrangian parameter set NL3 was used for both systems, and pairing correlations in the deformed case were treated in the constant gap approximation. The second-order differential equations for the large and small components of the Dirac spinors were analyzed to identify the term responsible for pseudo-spin splitting.

## Key numbers
- For 154Dy, the calculated ground state deformation parameter beta2 = 0.202 (compared to experimental value 0.237).
- For 154Dy, the calculated ground state binding energy is 1262.95 MeV, differing from experiment by 1.2 MeV.
- The energy denominator in the splitting term (S-V)-E involves (S-V) approximately 50 MeV for bound states with binding energy E less than 50 MeV.
- For the neutron pseudo-spin doublet [404] in 154Dy, a crossing of the partners occurs at approximately beta = 0.3.

## Context
This work extends the observation by Ginocchio (1997) that pseudo-spin symmetry may arise from near equality of scalar and vector fields in relativistic theories, moving from square well potentials and spherical RMF solutions to realistic deformed nuclei. It confirms the findings of Ginocchio (1997) and Ginocchio and Leviatan (1997) while demonstrating that the symmetry breaking pattern persists in deformed systems. The results enable systematic study of pseudo-spin symmetry across the nuclear chart using realistic RMF parameter sets like NL3, and connect to earlier non-relativistic treatments by Bohr, Hamamoto and Mottelson (1982) who studied pseudo-spin in the Nilsson scheme.
