# Equation of state for strange quark matter: Linking the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model to perturbative QCD

> Neutron star constraints and {\it ab initio} pQCD evaluations require the EoS representing cold quark matter to be stiff at intermediate baryonic densities and soft at high-$n_B$. Here, I suggest that the three flavor NJL model with a density dependent repulsive coupling, $G_V(μ)$, can generate an EoS which interpolates between these two regimes. Such an interpolation requires repulsion to start decreasing with the chemical potential just after chiral transition takes place. The conjecture behind this mechanism is that repulsion should be necessary only as long as the quark condensates, which dress the effective masses, have non-vanishing values. This assumption guarantees that an initially hard EoS suffers a conspicuous change of slope at ${\cal E} \simeq 0.7 \,{\rm GeV fm^{-3}}$ converging to the pQCD results at higher energy densities. Then, the speed of sound naturally reaches a non-conformal maximum at $n_B = 3.23 \, n_0 = 0.52 \, {\rm fm}^{-3}$ while the trace anomaly remains positive for all densities, in agreement with recent investigations. These non-trivial results {\it cannot} be simultaneously obtained when $G_V$ vanishes or has a fixed value. Therefore, the simple model proposed here is able to link the (non-perturbative) region of intermediate densities to the region where pQCD becomes reliable.

# Equation of state for strange quark matter: Linking the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model to perturbative QCD

## Key claim

The three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a density-dependent repulsive vector coupling G_V(μ) can generate an equation of state (EoS) for cold strange quark matter that is stiff at intermediate baryonic densities and soft at high densities, provided that the repulsive coupling decreases with chemical potential just after chiral transition occurs. This interpolation requires that repulsion is necessary only as long as the quark condensates have non-vanishing values.

## Method

The author extends the two-flavor NJL model with a running vector coupling G_V(μ) to the three-flavor (u, d, s) case at zero temperature, using a mean-field approximation with a sharp non-covariant cutoff Λ = 602.3 MeV. The repulsive coupling is modeled by a sigmoid function G_V(μ) = G_V(0) / [1 + e^{(μ - μ_0)/δ}] that interpolates between G_V(0) = G_S/3 at low chemical potential and zero at high chemical potential, with μ_0 = 485 MeV and δ = 10 MeV. The quark number density is evaluated numerically before integration to obtain pressure, ensuring thermodynamic consistency with the density-dependent coupling.

## Key numbers

- Speed of sound squared reaches a non-conformal maximum V_s^2 = 0.38 at baryonic density n_B = 3.23 n_0 = 0.52 fm^{-3}, corresponding to energy density ℰ = 0.59 GeV fm^{-3}
- The EoS shows a conspicuous change of slope at ℰ ≃ 0.7 GeV fm^{-3}, where softening begins
- The chiral first-order phase transition occurs at μ_B = 1.083 GeV for G_V = 0 and at μ_B = 1.104 GeV for G_V = G_S/3 and G_V(μ)
- The onset of strangeness takes place at n_B ≃ 5.50 n_0
- For fixed G_V = G_S/3, the conformal measure becomes negative at n_B/n_0 ≃ 14
- The cutoff parameter Λ = 602.3 MeV; bare quark masses m = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 140.7 MeV; vacuum condensates σ_u = σ_d = -(241.9 MeV)^3, σ_s = -(257.7 MeV)^3

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor NJL model with running G_V(μ) proposed in a prior letter by the same author to include strangeness, and addresses the problem that fixed G_V produces an EoS that remains too stiff at high densities to match perturbative QCD (pQCD) predictions, while G_V = 0 produces an EoS too soft for neutron star constraints. The results support recent simulations by Sinan and collaborators, Michal and collaborators, and Weis and collaborators showing a non-monotonic speed of sound, and agree with the claim by Fukushima and collaborators that a non-conformal peak in V_s^2 is not necessarily in tension with a positive trace anomaly at all densities. The model enables linking the non-perturbative intermediate density region (2 n_0 to 8 n_0) to the region where pQCD becomes reliable (n_B ≳ 40 n_0).
