# Muon capture by a proton in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory

> The matrix element for muon capture by a proton is calculated to O(p^3) within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory using the new O(p^3) Lagrangian of Ecker and Mojzis. External nucleon fields are renormalized using the appropriate definition of the wave function renormalization factor Z_N. Our expression for Z_N differs somewhat from that found in existing literature, but is the one which is consistent with the Lagrangian we use and the one which ensures, within our approach, the nonrenormalization of the vector coupling as required by the conserved vector current. Expressions for the standard muon capture form factors are derived and compared to experimental data and we determine three of the coefficients of the Ecker - Mojzis Lagrangian, namely, b_7, b_{19}, and b_{23}.

# Muon capture by a proton in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim
The muon capture form factors are calculated to O(p^3) using the Ecker-Mojzis Lagrangian, but the wave function renormalization factor Z_N differs from previous literature because it must be consistent with the specific Lagrangian to ensure the vector coupling is not renormalized as required by the conserved vector current. Three coefficients of the Ecker-Mojzis Lagrangian (b_7, b_19, and b_23) are determined from experimental data, but the results carry significant uncertainty from truncation of the chiral expansion.

## Method
The authors compute the matrix element for muon capture by a proton (muon + proton → neutron + neutrino) to O(p^3) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory using the complete O(p^3) Lagrangian of Ecker and Mojzis. They derive the wave function renormalization factor Z_N from the nucleon two-point function, then calculate the renormalized W-nucleon vertex, pion-nucleon vertex, and W-pion vertex, combining them to obtain the four muon capture form factors G_V, G_M, G_A, and G_P. The form factors are compared to experimental data to extract numerical values for the Lagrangian parameters b_7, b_19, and b_23.

## Key numbers
- The momentum transfer at the muon capture point is q_*^2 = -0.88 m_mu^2 (where m_mu is the muon mass)
- The Fermi constant combination G_beta/sqrt(2) = (0.8030 ± 0.0008) × 10^{-5} GeV^{-2}
- The pion decay constant F = 92.4 ± 0.3 MeV
- The axial coupling at zero momentum transfer G_A(0) = 1.2601 ± 0.0025 from neutron decay
- The parameter a_6 = 1.661 ± 0.004, determined from the nucleon anomalous magnetic moments (kappa_p = 1.7928, kappa_n = -1.9130)
- The CHPT prediction for the magnetic radius squared is ⟨r^2⟩_M = 0.523 ± 0.004 fm^2, about 30% smaller than the dipole estimate of 0.72 fm^2
- The parameter b_7^r(m_N) = -0.53 ± 0.02, determined using the dipole prediction for the vector radius squared ⟨r^2⟩_V = 0.41 fm^2
- The parameter b_23 = -3.1 ± 0.3, determined from the axial radius squared ⟨r^2⟩_A = 0.42 ± 0.04 fm^2 from antineutrino-nucleon scattering
- The parameter b_19 = -0.7 ± 0.4, determined from the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy using g_piNN = 13.0 ± 0.1
- The predicted pseudoscalar form factor at the muon capture point is G_P(q_*^2) = 8.21 ± 0.09, in agreement with the experimental value 8.7 ± 1.9 from Bardin

## Context
This work extends previous heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations of muon capture form factors (Bernard, Kaiser, Meissner 1995; Bernard, Kaiser, Kambor, Meissner) by using the complete O(p^3) Lagrangian of Ecker and Mojzis (1996), which had not been applied to this process before. The derived parameters are directly transferable to future calculations of radiative muon capture by a proton, for which a recent TRIUMF experiment obtained interesting results and a heavy baryon CHPT study is in progress. The authors note that the 30% reduction in a_6 when omitting the O(p^3) term provides an indication of the convergence of the chiral expansion, and the errors quoted do not include uncertainties from truncation of the chiral expansion.
