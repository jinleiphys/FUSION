# Fluctuations driven isotropization of the quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions

> Averaged over ensemble of initial conditions kinetic transport equations of weakly coupled systems of quarks and gluons are derived. These equations account for the correlators of fluctuations of particles and classical gluon fields. The isotropization of particle momenta by field fluctuations at the early prethermal stage of matter evolution in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is discussed. Our results can be useful for understanding under what conditions isotropization of the quark-gluon plasma in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions can be reached within phenomenologically observed time scales.

## Key claim

The paper analytically demonstrates that fluctuations in the initial conditions of the quark-gluon plasma can drive isotropization of particle momenta through diffusion in momentum space, even before chromo-Weibel instabilities fully develop, provided the amplitudes of fluctuations remain below the nonAbelian scale where nonlinear gauge field self-interactions become important.

## Method

The author derives averaged kinetic transport equations for weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma by performing ensemble averages over initial conditions of the collisionless Vlasov-type transport equations for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons, coupled to the Yang-Mills equation. The resulting collision terms are expressed through correlators of fluctuations of particles and classical gluon fields, and are evaluated analytically in the Abelian approximation (below the nonAbelian scale) where nonlinear gauge field self-interactions can be neglected, yielding a diffusion term in momentum space.

## Key numbers

- The paper discusses isotropization proper times \(\tau_{i} < 1\) fm/c required by hydrodynamic models to describe RHIC data, compared to perturbative thermalization estimates near \(3\) fm/c.
- The nonAbelian scale is defined by the condition \(A \sim |\mathbf{k}|/g\), where \(|\mathbf{k}|\) is the characteristic momentum scale of unstable gauge field modes and \(g\) is the coupling constant (\(g \ll 1\)).
- The hard-loop effective theory approximation is justified when \(|\mathbf{k}| \ll |\mathbf{p}|\), where \(|\mathbf{p}|\) is the typical momentum of plasma particles.

## Context

This work extends analytical methods developed for Abelian plasmas by Kl (year not specified) and Ts (year not specified) to nonAbelian plasmas, and builds on numerical simulations of chromo-Weibel instabilities using hard-loop effective theory by Arnold (year not specified), Arnold (year not specified), and Bodeker (year not specified). The results also relate to classical Vlasov transport simulations by Dum (year not specified) which found rapid isotropization for strong initial random fields, and to Yang-Mills simulations by Rom (year not specified) with Color Glass Condensate initial conditions. The paper enables a theoretical framework for understanding how initial fluctuations can accelerate isotropization in heavy ion collisions, potentially resolving the puzzle of early thermalization times observed at RHIC.
