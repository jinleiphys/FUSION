# The longitudinal and transverse responses in the inclusive electron scattering: a functional approach

> The splitting between the charge-longitudinal and spin-transverse responses is explained in a model whose inputs are the effective interactions in the particle-hole channels in the frame of the first order boson loop expansion. It is shown that the interplay between $ω$-meson exchange and box diagrams (two-meson exchange with simultaneous excitation of one or two nucleons to $Δ$'s) mainly rules the longitudinal response, while in the transverse one the direct $Δ$ excitations almost cancel the one-loop correction and the response is mainly governed by the $ρ$-meson rescattering inside the nucleus. It is also shown that a small variation in the nuclear densities may explain the observed discrepancies between different nuclei.

## Key claim

The splitting between the longitudinal and transverse nuclear responses is explained by a model where the interplay of ω-meson exchange and box diagrams (two-meson exchange with simultaneous excitation of one or two nucleons to Δ resonances) mainly governs the longitudinal response, while in the transverse channel direct Δ excitations almost cancel the one-loop correction and the response is primarily governed by ρ-meson rescattering; a small variation in nuclear densities may explain observed discrepancies between different nuclei.

## Method

The authors employ a first-order boson loop expansion (BLE) applied to a system of nucleons and Δ resonances interacting via correlated π, ρ, and ω meson exchanges in nuclear matter. The effective interactions in the particle-hole channels are parametrized with Landau-Migdal parameters and momentum-dependent cut-offs, and the response functions are computed from the imaginary parts of Feynman diagrams at the one-boson-loop level, summing over all allowed spin-isospin channels and including self-energy, exchange, and correlation diagrams with nucleon and Δ internal lines.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb sum rule for Carbon was quenched by about 10% in Saclay data, while for Calcium it was quenched by about 40%; for Lead the sum rule was reduced to about 40% of the total charge.
- Bates data for ⁴⁰Ca showed a quenching of the sum rule of about 10%, in sharp contrast with the Saclay results.
- Jourdan (1995) showed that the corrected sum rule derived from the world data set is compatible with Z within a 1% uncertainty at q = 570 MeV/c.
- The ρ-meson coupling constant in the isovector spin-transverse channel is C_ρ ≈ 2.3, while in the scalar isovector channel it is C_ρ ≈ 0.05.
- The Δ magnetic moment is taken as μ_Δ = 2.95 according to the constituent quark model.
- The Δ radius is taken as 27% higher than the nucleon radius from the constituent quark model.
- A complete calculation with one set of parameters on a last-generation α-VAX required about one day of computing time.

## Context

This work extends the boson loop expansion approach previously developed by Cenni and Saracco (1994) and Alzetta et al. (1987, 1990), which was initially applied only to pion exchange and gave qualitatively unsatisfactory results for the longitudinal response. The present model incorporates π, ρ, and ω meson exchanges with Δ-resonance excitations, addressing the long-standing discrepancy between Saclay data (Meziani et al., 1984, 1985) showing strong quenching and Bates data (Yates et al., 1993) showing milder quenching. The approach contrasts with continuum RPA plus two-body currents calculations (Varga et al., 1995) and FHNC variational calculations (Fantoni and Pandharipande, 1987), which either obscure the link with underlying dynamics or cannot easily include pions and Δ's. The results enable future work to resolve the experimental controversy between Saclay and Bates data through small variations in nuclear density parametrization.
