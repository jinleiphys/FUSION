# Calculation of Doublet Capture Rate for Muon Capture in Deuterium within Chiral Effective Field Theory

> The doublet capture rate of the negative muon capture in deuterium is calculated employing the nuclear wave functions generated from accurate nucleon-nucleon potentials constructed at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order of heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory and the weak meson exchange current operator derived within the same formalism. All but one of the low-energy constants that enter the calculation were fixed from pion-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon scattering data. The low-energy constant d^R (c_D), which cannot be determined from the purely two-nucleon data, was extracted recently from the triton beta-decay and the binding energies of the three-nucleon systems. The calculated values of the doublet capture rates show a rather large spread for the used values of the d^R. Precise measurement of the doublet capture rate in the future will not only help to constrain the value of d^R, but also provide a highly nontrivial test of the nuclear chiral EFT framework. Besides, the precise knowledge of the constant d^R will allow for consistent calculations of other two-nucleon weak processes, such as proton-proton fusion and solar neutrino scattering on deuterons, which are important for astrophysics.

## Key claim

The doublet capture rate for muon capture in deuterium shows a rather large spread depending on the value of the low-energy constant d^R (c_D), with calculated values falling into two distinct intervals; a precise future measurement would help constrain this constant and provide a nontrivial test of the chiral effective field theory framework, but the current calculations cannot uniquely predict the rate without additional input from three-nucleon systems.

## Method

The authors calculated the doublet capture rate using nuclear wave functions generated from next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) chiral potentials: the Entem and Machleidt (EM) potential and two versions of the Epelbaum, Gloeckle, and Meissner (EGM) potential with cutoffs of 450 MeV and 600 MeV. The weak axial meson exchange current operator was derived within the same heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory formalism, and the low-energy constant d^R (c_D) was taken from previous determinations using triton beta-decay and three-nucleon binding energies. The nn wave functions in the continuum were generated using the K-matrix method, and the weak axial pion potential current required by the PCAC constraint was also included.

## Key numbers

- Doublet capture rate for EM potential with c_4=3.4 GeV^-1: ranges from 383.8 s^-1 to 387.2 s^-1 depending on c_D value
- Doublet capture rate for EM potential with c_4=5.4 GeV^-1: ranges from 389.4 s^-1 to 392.4 s^-1 depending on c_D value
- Doublet capture rate for EGM(204) potential: ranges from 386.4 s^-1 to 389.1 s^-1 depending on c_D value
- Doublet capture rate for EGM(205) potential: ranges from 410.1 s^-1 to 419.1 s^-1 depending on c_D value
- Inclusion of weak axial pion potential current suppresses the capture rate by up to 0.5% (e.g., from 389.4 s^-1 to 387.2 s^-1 for EM^b with c_D=-0.3)
- The nn scattering length from the EM potential is -18.89 fm, and from EGM(204) is -18.90 fm, compared to the experimental value of -18.9 +/- 0.4 fm
- The deuteron binding energy from the EM potential is -2.224575 MeV, from EGM(204) is -2.218923 MeV, and from EGM(205) is -2.223491 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to muon capture in deuterium, using consistent N3LO potentials and currents, building on previous calculations by the Pisa group (2010, 2011) which obtained capture rates of 389.7-394.3 s^-1 and 400+/-3 s^-1, and on the determination of c_D from triton beta-decay by Gazit et al. (2009). The results show stronger model dependence than admitted in those earlier works, with the EGM(205) potential giving values about 6% larger than the EM and EGM(204) potentials. A precise measurement by the MuSun Collaboration, planned with approximately 1.5% accuracy, would help constrain the low-energy constant d^R (c_D), which also enters calculations of proton-proton fusion and solar neutrino scattering on deuterons important for astrophysics.
