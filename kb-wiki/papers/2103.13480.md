# Enlighting the transverse structure of the proton via double parton scattering in photon-induced interactions

> In the present paper we address double parton scattering (DPS) in quasi-real photon-proton interactions. By using electromagnetic and hadronic models of the photon light cone wave functions, we compute the so-called effective cross-section, $σ_{eff}^{γp}$ which allows us to calculate the DPS contribution to these processes under dedicated assumptions. In particular, for the four-jet photoproduction in HERA kinematics we found a sizeable DPS contribution. We show that if the photon virtuality $Q^2$ could be measured and thus the dependence of $σ_{eff}^{γp}$ on such a parameter exposed, information on the transverse distance between partons active in proton could be extracted. To this aim, we set lower limits on the integrated luminosity needed to observe such an effect which would allow the extraction of novel information on the proton structure.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the mean transverse distance between partons in the proton can be extracted by measuring the dependence of the double parton scattering (DPS) effective cross section on photon virtuality \(Q^2\) in quasi-real photon-proton interactions, but this extraction requires sufficient integrated luminosity and is contingent on the photon virtuality being experimentally measurable.

## Method

The authors computed the effective cross section \(\sigma_{eff}^{\gamma p}\) for DPS in photon-proton interactions using Light-Front wave functions of the photon, considering both a pure electromagnetic (QED) model and a hadronic model (spectral quark model). They then estimated the DPS cross section for four-jet photoproduction at HERA kinematics using a pocket formula that factorizes the cross section into single parton scattering (SPS) cross sections divided by \(\sigma_{eff}^{\gamma p}\), and compared it to the leading-order SPS four-jet background calculated with ALPGEN.

## Key numbers

*   The experimental four-jet photoproduction cross section for \(x_\gamma < 0.75\) at HERA is 135 pb.
*   For the hadronic photon model (arriola) with proton eff G1, the DPS cross section for \(Q^2 \leq 1\) GeV\(^2\) is 53.7 pb, corresponding to 40% of the experimental cross section.
*   For the QED photon model (brodfrank) with proton eff G1, the DPS cross section for \(Q^2 \leq 1\) GeV\(^2\) is 142.1 pb, exceeding the experimental cross section (101%).
*   The ratio \(R\) of DPS cross sections in two \(Q^2\) bins (\(Q^2 \leq 10^{-2}\) GeV\(^2\) and \(10^{-2} \leq Q^2 \leq 1\) GeV\(^2\)) is predicted to be 1.89 for the hadronic model (G1) and 1.62 for the QED model (G1), compared to a reference value of 2.1 for a \(Q^2\)-independent \(\sigma_{eff}^{\gamma p}\).
*   The asymptotic value of \(\sigma_{eff}^{\gamma p}\) at high \(Q^2\) (100 GeV\(^2\)) for the QED model with proton eff G1 is 7.52 mb, in agreement with the gluon splitting prediction of 7.5 mb from Gaunt (2012).

## Context

This work extends studies of DPS in hadronic collisions, where \(\sigma_{eff}\) in \(pp\) collisions has been measured in the range 8-35 mb for four-jet production (CMS 2021), to the photon-proton sector. It builds on the treatment of photon structure by Klasen (2002) and on the formalism for DPS with a perturbatively splitting photon developed by Gaunt (2012) and Blok (2014). The proposed method avoids intrinsic limitations in extracting partonic transverse distances from \(\sigma_{eff}\) in \(pp\) collisions discussed by Rinaldi (rapid, jhepc). The results suggest that four-jet photoproduction at HERA could provide a new window on proton transverse structure, provided higher-order corrections to the SPS background are controlled.
