# Holographic calculation of the QCD crossover temperature in a magnetic field

> Lattice data for the QCD equation of state and the magnetic susceptibility computed near the crossover transition at zero magnetic field are used to determine the input parameters of a five dimensional Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton holographic model. Once the model parameters are fixed at zero magnetic field, one can use this holographic construction to study the effects of a magnetic field on the equilibrium and transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma. In this paper we use this model to study the dependence of the crossover temperature with an external magnetic field. Our results for the pressure of the plasma and the crossover temperature are in quantitative agreement with current lattice data for values of the magnetic field $0 \le eB \lesssim 0.3$ GeV$^2$, which is the relevant range for ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision applications.

## Key claim

The holographic model reproduces the lattice QCD crossover temperature as a function of magnetic field quantitatively for \(0 \le eB \lesssim 0.3\) GeV\(^2\), but only after the dilaton potential and Maxwell-Dilaton gauge coupling are fixed using zero-field lattice data for the equation of state and magnetic susceptibility, respectively.

## Method

A five-dimensional Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton (EMD) holographic model with a negative cosmological constant is constructed, using an anisotropic black brane Ansatz that breaks rotational symmetry to \(SO(2)\) in the presence of a constant external magnetic field. The dilaton potential \(V(\phi)\) and the gravitational constant \(\kappa^2\) are fixed by fitting the holographic equation of state at zero magnetic field to \((2+1)\)-flavor lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration, while the Maxwell-Dilaton gauge coupling \(f(\phi)\) is determined by matching the holographic magnetic susceptibility at zero field to the same lattice collaboration's results. The model is then used to compute the temperature and magnetic field dependence of thermodynamic quantities and the crossover temperature.

## Key numbers

* The crossover temperature at zero magnetic field is matched to the lattice QCD minimum of the speed of sound squared at \(T \approx 143.8\) MeV, yielding a conversion factor \(\lambda \approx 831\) MeV between black hole units and physical units.
* The dilaton potential is \(V(\phi) = -12\cosh(0.606\,\phi) + 0.703\,\phi^2 - 0.1\,\phi^4 + 0.0034\,\phi^6\).
* The gravitational constant is \(\kappa^2 = 8\pi G_5 = 12.5\).
* The model agrees with lattice data for the pressure and crossover temperature for magnetic fields in the range \(0 \le eB \lesssim 0.3\) GeV\(^2\), which is the relevant range for ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Context

This work extends the nonconformal holographic approach of Gubser et al. (2008) and Finazzo et al. (2015), which previously treated finite temperature and baryon chemical potential at zero magnetic field, by adding a constant external magnetic field. The model builds on the top-down magnetic field construction of D'Hoker and Kraus (2009) for \(\mathcal{N}=4\) super Yang-Mills, but uses a bottom-up approach to quantitatively mimic \((2+1)\)-flavor QCD near the crossover. The results enable future holographic calculations of transport coefficients and other observables in the \((T,B)\)-plane that are currently beyond lattice QCD, such as second-order hydrodynamic coefficients.
