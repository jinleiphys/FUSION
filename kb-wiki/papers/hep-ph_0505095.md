# Classical chromodynamics and heavy ion collisions

> This paper is a slightly modified version of the introductory part of a doctoral dissertation also containing the articles hep-ph/0303076, hep-ph/0409328 and hep-ph/0409058. The paper focuses on the calculation of particle production in a relativistic heavy ion collision using the McLerran-Venugopalan model. The main part of the paper summarizes the background of these numerical calculations. First we relate this calculation of the initial stage af a heavy ion collision to our understanding of the whole collision process. Then we discuss the saturation physics of the small x wavefunction of a hadron or a nucleus. The classical field model of Kovner, McLerran and Weigert is then introduced before moving to discuss the numerical algorithms used to compute gluon and quark pair production in this model. Finally we shortly review the results on gluon and quark-antiquark production obtained in the three articles mentioned above.

# Classical chromodynamics and heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper argues that bulk observables in relativistic heavy ion collisions (such as total multiplicity and transverse energy) can be calculated from first principles using weak coupling methods based on the QCD Lagrangian, provided the saturation scale Q_s is large enough to justify a classical field approximation; the actual hedge is that this approach is presented as a "conjecture" that has so far achieved only a posteriori explanations for RHIC data rather than predictive calculations.

## Method

The paper reviews the McLerran-Venugopalan model for the nuclear wavefunction at small x, where the high density of gluons justifies treating the color fields as classical. It then describes the numerical algorithms used to compute gluon and quark pair production in this model, specifically the classical field approach of Kovner, McLerran and Weigert, where the collision of two nuclei is simulated by solving the Yang-Mills equations with initial conditions given by the classical color fields of the two nuclei before the collision.

## Key numbers

- Transverse energy at midrapidity in central AuAu collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 A GeV: dE_T/deta ≈ 620 GeV
- Ratio of transverse energy to charged multiplicity at sqrt(s) = 200 A GeV: E_T/N_ch ≈ 0.86 GeV
- Total multiplicity at midrapidity at sqrt(s) = 200 A GeV: dN_tot/deta ≈ 1100
- Energy density estimate at tau = 1 fm: ε(tau = 1 fm) ≲ 9 GeV/fm^3 (upper limit from hydrodynamical evolution) and ε(tau = 1 fm) ≳ 4 GeV/fm^3 (lower limit from free streaming)
- Saturation scale parameters from Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff fit to HERA data: σ_0 = 23.03 mb, x_0 = 3.04 × 10^{-4}, λ = 0.288

## Context

This work extends the saturation physics developed in deep inelastic scattering at HERA (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1998) and the classical field model of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) to the calculation of particle production in heavy ion collisions. It builds on numerical calculations of gluon production in the classical field framework initiated by Krasnitz (1998) and aims to provide a weak-coupling, first-principles description of bulk observables at RHIC and the LHC, in contrast to traditional nonperturbative stringy models (Wang, 1991) or perturbative calculations with phenomenological saturation modifications (Kharzeev, 2001-2004).
