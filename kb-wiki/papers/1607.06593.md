# Properties of isocalar-pair condensates

> It is pointed out that the ground state of n neutrons and n protons in a single-j shell, interacting through an isoscalar (T=0) pairing force, is not paired, J=0, but rather spin-aligned, J=n. This observation is explained in the context of a model of isoscalar P (J=1) pairs, which is mapped onto a system of p bosons, leading to an approximate analytic solution of the isoscalar-pairing limit in jj coupling.

# Properties of isoscalar-pair condensates

## Key claim

The ground state of n neutrons and n protons in a single-j shell interacting through an isoscalar (T=0) pairing force is not the expected J=0 paired state but rather a spin-aligned state with J=n, provided the spin-orbit splitting exceeds a critical value of approximately 3.5g for the f7/2-f5/2 pair; this result is derived under the simplifying assumptions of a truncated shell-model space and a boson mapping that neglects Pauli corrections beyond two-body interactions.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations using the OXBASH code with an effective two-body force mixing isovector (T=1, J=0) and isoscalar (T=0, J=1) pairing interactions, parameterized by a mixing parameter x, for systems of 2n particles in single-j shells (j=7/2 through 15/2). They then developed a nucleon-pair shell model (NPSM) truncation to P-pair (J=1, T=0) states and mapped these onto a system of p bosons using the Otsuka-Arima-Iachello (OAI) mapping, yielding an approximate analytic solution via a U(3) ⊃ SO(3) dynamical symmetry.

## Key numbers

- For the f7/2-f5/2 pair, the critical spin-orbit splitting at which the jj coupling takes over is v*ℓs/g ≈ 3.5 (finite-j) compared to v*ℓs/g ≈ 5 in the large-j limit.
- The two-body boson matrix elements for an attractive isoscalar pairing interaction are repulsive: vᵇ₀ = [6(j²+j-1)]/[j(j+1)(2j+1)] g and vᵇ₂ = [3(4j⁴+6j³+j²+7j+12)]/[j(j+1)(2j+1)(5j²+7j+3)] g.
- In the large-j limit, vᵇ₀ → (3/j + O(1/j²))g and vᵇ₂ → (6/(5j) + O(1/j²))g.
- For j=7/2, the exact fermion energy of the aligned state E_f(4,4) = -2.767g compared to the paired state E_f(4,0) = -2.080g, confirming the aligned state is lower.
- For j=7/2, the exact fermion energy E_f(6,6) does not exist (state not present) while E_f(6,0) = -2.798g.
- The energy difference between paired and spin-aligned states in the boson approximation is Δ_b(n) ≈ g[3(n-n₂)(n+1+n₂)]/(10j), where n₂ = n mod 2.

## Context

This work extends the study of isoscalar (T=0) versus isovector (T=1) pairing correlations in N≈Z nuclei, a topic previously investigated by Bohr, Mottelson, and Pines (1958) and reviewed in the context of exotic nuclei. The authors note that group-theoretical solutions exist for the isoscalar and isovector limits in LS coupling (Flowers, 1964; Pang, 1969; Evans, 1981) and for the isovector limit in jj coupling (Racah, 1952; Kerman, 1961), but this work provides the first approximate analytic formulae for the isoscalar limit in jj coupling. The results enable future studies of spin-triplet pairing phases, which recent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Gezerlis (2011) and Bulthuis (2016) suggest may be favored over spin-singlet phases in the mass region A≈130 with Z≈64 (e.g., ¹³²Gd), and may be realizable in ultracold atomic traps with tunable spin-orbit coupling.
