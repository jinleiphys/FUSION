# Relativistic $O(q^4)$ two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential: parametrized version

> The chiral two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction has nowadays a rather firm conceptual basis, but depends on low-energy constants, which may be extracted from fits to data. In order to facilitate this kind of application, we present here a parametrized version of our relativistic expansion of this component of the force to $O(q^4)$, performed recently.

## Key claim

The paper provides a parametrized version of the relativistic chiral two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential up to order \(O(q^4)\), designed to be numerically accurate for distances larger than 1 fm, in order to facilitate the extraction of low-energy constants (LECs) from fits to data.

## Method

The authors start from their previously derived relativistic \(O(q^4)\) two-pion exchange potential in configuration space, expressed in terms of covariant loop integrals and LECs (\(c_i\) and \(d_i\)). They parametrize the dimensionless functions \(Z_i \equiv (F_i, G_i)\) that arise from Fourier transforms of Feynman loop integrals as a sum of powers of \(x = \mu r\) multiplied by an exponential factor \(e^{-2x}/x^2\), with coefficients \(\gamma_i^n\) given in tables. The resulting profile functions for the central, spin-orbit, tensor, and spin-spin components are then written explicitly in terms of these parametrized functions and the LECs.

## Key numbers

* The parametrization is more than 1% accurate in the range 0.8 fm \(\leq r \leq\) 10 fm.
* The potential is expressed in terms of the pion mass \(\mu\), nucleon mass \(m\), pion decay constant \(f_\pi\), axial coupling constant \(g_A\), and LECs \(c_i\) and \(d_i\).
* The dimensionless functions \(Z_i\) are parametrized as \(Z_i = -\frac{\mu}{(4\pi)^{5/2}} \left( \frac{\mu}{f_\pi} \right)^4 \left[ \sum \gamma_i^n x^n \right] \frac{e^{-2x}}{x^2}\), with coefficients \(\gamma_i^n\) tabulated for functions \(H_i\), \(G_i\), and \(I_i\).
* The potential is valid for distances larger than 1 fm, as the theoretical expressions are not reliable for distances smaller than 1 fm.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic chiral two-pion exchange potential developed by the same group (Higa, Robilotta, da Rocha, 2004) by providing a numerically efficient parametrized form. It is intended to facilitate the extraction of LECs from fits to nucleon-nucleon phase shifts, following the approach of the Nijmegen group (Nij) which used the one-pion exchange potential as input for phase shift analyses. The parametrized potential enables comparison with empirical phase shifts, particularly for peripheral waves where the Born approximation can be used, and for more important waves when combined with regularized potentials as done by the group of Epelbaum (Epe).
