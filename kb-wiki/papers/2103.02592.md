# Spin polarization dynamics in the Bjorken-expanding resistive MHD background

> Evolution of spin polarization in the presence of external electric field is studied for collision energies $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=27\,{\rm GeV}$ and $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\,{\rm GeV}$. The numerical analysis is done in the perfect-fluid Bjorken-expanding resistive magnetohydrodynamic background and novel results are reported. In particular, we show that the electric field plays a significant role in the competition between expansion and dissipation.

## Key claim

The electric field plays a significant role in the competition between expansion and dissipation in spin polarization dynamics, but only under the condition that the fluid is electrically neutral and the electric and magnetic fields are either parallel or antiparallel to each other, with the modification of the chemical potential allowing the electric field to exist in equilibrium.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann-Vlasov equation in the collisionless limit to obtain a stationary distribution function modified by the presence of an external electric field, then use this distribution to derive modified thermodynamic variables (energy density, pressure, baryon density) for a perfect fluid of massive quark-like quasi-particles. These are plugged into the resistive magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) equations under Bjorken flow assumptions, using the analytic solution for electromagnetic fields from Shokri (2017), and the resulting background dynamics are used in the GLW spin tensor conservation law to study the evolution of six spin polarization coefficients.

## Key numbers

* Collision energies studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 27 GeV and sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
* Initial electromagnetic field strengths are of order eE/m_pi^2 ~ eB/m_pi^2 ~ O(1), with m_pi being the pion mass
* The magnetic field evolves as B^mu = B_0 (tau_0/tau) Y^mu, where tau_0 is the initial proper time
* The electric field evolves as E^mu = l E_0 (tau_0/tau) exp[-sigma(tau - tau_0)] Y^mu, where l = +/- 1 for parallel/antiparallel configurations and sigma is the electric conductivity
* The modified fugacity parameter is xi_i = mu/T - l q_i R_RMS (E_0/T) (tau_0/tau) exp[-sigma(tau - tau_0)], where R_RMS is the nucleon root-mean-square charge radius

## Context

This work extends the spin hydrodynamics formalism of Florkowski (2017, 2018, 2019) and Bhadury (2020) by incorporating external electromagnetic fields into the background evolution, building on the resistive MHD solutions for Bjorken flow derived by Shokri (2017). The results enable future studies of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions where large electromagnetic fields are present, particularly at lower collision energies where the fields may decay more slowly and modify the competition between hydrodynamic expansion and dissipative effects.
