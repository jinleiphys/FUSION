# Comparison among Hamiltonian light-front formalisms at q+ = 0 and q+ <> 0: space-like elastic form factors of pseudoscalar and vector mesons

> The electromagnetic elastic form factors of pseudoscalar and vector mesons are analyzed for space-like momentum transfers in terms of relativistic quark models based on the Hamiltonian light-front formalism elaborated in different reference frames (q+ 0 and q+ <> 0). As far as the one-body approximation for the electromagnetic current operator is concerned, it is shown that the predictions of the light-front approach at q+=0 should be preferred, particularly in case of light hadrons, because of: i) the relevant role played by the Z-graph at q+ <> 0, and ii) the appropriate elimination of spurious effects, related to the orientation of the null hyperplane where the light-front wave function is defined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for space-like elastic form factors of pseudoscalar and vector mesons, the Hamiltonian light-front formalism at \(q^+ = 0\) should be preferred over the approach at \(q^+ \neq 0\) when using the one-body approximation for the electromagnetic current, because the \(q^+ \neq 0\) formalism ignores the \(Z\)-graph contribution and introduces spurious effects related to the orientation of the null hyperplane, which are particularly significant for light hadrons.

## Method

The author compares two Hamiltonian light-front formalisms: the approach of Ref. [DS] at \(q^+ = 0\) and the approach of Ref. [LPS] at \(q^+ \neq 0\), both using the one-body approximation for the electromagnetic current operator with point-like constituent quarks. Elastic form factors are computed for pseudoscalar mesons (pion, kaon, D, B) and vector mesons using the same light-front wave functions derived from the quark potential model of Godfrey and Isgur (1985), with constituent quark masses taken from that model. The comparison is performed by evaluating the form factors in Breit frames specific to each formalism and analyzing the contribution of the \(Z\)-graph term from the Feynman triangle diagram.

## Key numbers

- Pion charge radius at \(q^+ = 0\): \(\simeq 0.46\) fm (point-like constituents); at \(q^+ \neq 0\): \(\simeq 5\) fm (point-like constituents).
- Kaon charge radius at \(q^+ = 0\): \(\simeq 0.43\) fm; at \(q^+ \neq 0\): \(\simeq 2\) fm.
- Constituent quark masses used: \(m_u = m_d = 0.220\) GeV, \(m_s = 0.419\) GeV, \(m_c = 1.628\) GeV, \(m_b = 4.977\) GeV.
- Meson masses from PDG: \(M_\pi = 0.1396\) GeV, \(M_\rho = 0.767\) GeV, \(M_K = 0.4937\) GeV, \(M_{K^*} = 0.892\) GeV, \(M_D = 1.869\) GeV, \(M_{D^*} = 2.010\) GeV, \(M_B = 5.279\) GeV, \(M_{B^*} = 5.325\) GeV.
- Experimental charge radii: \(r_{ch}^\pi = 0.660 \pm 0.024\) fm, \(r_{ch}^K = 0.58 \pm 0.04\) fm, \(r_{ch}^p = 0.883 \pm 0.014\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of light-front formalisms previously studied for the \(\rho\)-meson by Cardarelli et al. (1995) and Karmanov (1996), and for pseudoscalar mesons by the author and collaborators. It directly contradicts the approach of Lev, Pace, and Salme (2000) at \(q^+ \neq 0\) by showing that their formalism maximizes the impact of many-body currents and produces unphysical results for light hadrons. The findings enable future work to properly include \(Z\)-graph contributions in time-like processes via analytic continuation and dispersion approaches, as developed by Melikhov and collaborators (1998).
