# Baryons as Hybrids of Solitons and Three Quark Bound States

> A hybrid model for baryons based on a dynamical interplay between relativistic three--quark bound states and soliton configurations of mesons is constructed. The Bethe-Salpeter equation for diquarks and the Faddeev equation for diquark-quark bound states in the background of a soliton is solved. The results show that baryons are very much like hybrids containing both, solitonic meson clouds and three--quark correlations.

## Key claim
The paper provides a practical "map" for when the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model can be reliably used to compute astrophysical reaction rates, based on a lower temperature limit derived from the number of resonances in the effective energy window. The key hedge is that the method assumes an average of 5–10 contributing resonances is sufficient, and the derived temperature limits are not highly sensitive to this threshold because of the exponential dependence of level density on excitation energy.

## Method
The authors used a back-shifted Fermi-gas model with an energy-dependent level density parameter, incorporating microscopic corrections from the FRDM mass formula and pairing gaps, to compute nuclear level densities. They then determined the effective energy window for neutron-induced reactions at a given stellar temperature (the peak of the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution at \(E_0 = kT\)), calculated the number of resonances in that window using the level density, and derived a lower temperature limit for statistical model applicability by requiring at least 5–10 resonances.

## Key numbers
- Level density parameter fit values: \(\alpha = 0.1336\), \(\beta = -0.06712\), \(\gamma = 0.04862\), yielding an averaged global deviation of 1.5 from experimental s-wave neutron resonance spacings.
- The effective energy window for s-wave neutrons coincides with the peak of the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution at \(E_0 = kT\).
- An average number of 5–10 contributing resonances is sufficient for statistical model applicability, based on numerical test calculations.

## Context
This work extends the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model approach used in nuclear astrophysics (e.g., Cowan et al., Bethe) by improving the level density description with an energy-dependent parameter from Ignatyuk et al. and microscopic corrections from the FRDM mass formula (Möller et al.). It enables users of astrophysical reaction rates to quickly identify which reactions require special attention or experimental investigation, and the method can be generalized to other projectiles and incident energy distributions.
