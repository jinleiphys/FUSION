# Search for New Physics with Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

> Neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$) is one of the most sensitive approaches to test particle physics beyond the standard model. During the last years, besides the most restrictive limit on the effective Majorana neutrino mass, the analysis of new contributions by the Heidelberg group led to bounds on left-right-symmetric models, leptoquarks and R-parity violating models competitive to recent accelerator limits, which are of special interest in view of the HERA anomaly at large $Q^2$ and $x$. These new results deduced from the Heidelberg-Moscow double beta decay experiment are reviewed. Also an outlook on the future of double beta decay, the GENIUS proposal, is given.

# Search for New Physics with Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

## Key claim
The Heidelberg-Moscow experiment's limits on neutrinoless double beta decay provide constraints on left-right-symmetric models, leptoquarks, and R-parity violating supersymmetry that are competitive with accelerator limits, but these bounds depend on nuclear matrix element calculations with uncertainties of about a factor of two.

## Method
The Heidelberg-Moscow experiment operated five p-type HPGe detectors with an active mass of 10.96 kg, grown from 19.2 kg of 86% enriched ⁷⁶Ge, in low-level cryostats at the Gran Sasso laboratory under 3500 m of water equivalent shielding. The experiment collected data over 31.8 kg·years of measuring time and applied pulse shape analysis to a subset of 15.3 kg·years to distinguish single-site events from background.

## Key numbers
- Half-life limit for neutrinoless double beta decay: T₁/₂⁰νββ > 1.2 × 10²⁵ y (90% C.L.) from 31.8 kg·y of data
- Half-life limit from pulse shape analysis data (15.3 kg·y): T₁/₂⁰νββ > 1.1 × 10²⁵ y
- Effective Majorana neutrino mass limit: ⟨m_ν⟩ ≤ 0.45 eV (90% C.L.)
- Two-neutrino double beta decay half-life: T₁/₂²νββ = (1.77⁺⁰.⁰¹₋₀.₀₁(stat.)⁺⁰.¹³₋₀.¹¹(syst.)) × 10²¹ y, from 21115 events in 500-2040 keV
- Lower limit on right-handed W boson mass: m_WR ≥ 1.2 TeV
- Limit on R-parity violating coupling: λ'₁₁₁ ≤ 3.2 × 10⁻⁴ (m_˜q/100 GeV)² (m_˜g/100 GeV)¹/²
- Projected sensitivity over next five years: half-life up to 6 × 10²⁵ y, corresponding to neutrino mass limit of 0.1-0.2 eV

## Context
This paper reviews results from the Heidelberg-Moscow experiment, which at the time held the leading position among double beta decay experiments and was the first to yield neutrino mass limits in the sub-eV range. The work extends earlier theoretical predictions by Staudt (year not given) for two-neutrino double beta decay and provides constraints on models proposed to explain the HERA anomaly at large Q² and x. The GENIUS proposal, operating 288 enriched ⁷⁶Ge detectors with a total mass of 1 ton in liquid nitrogen, would improve sensitivity to neutrino masses down to 0.01 eV, enabling tests of the atmospheric neutrino problem, hot dark matter scenarios, and SUSY models at levels comparable to the LHC.
