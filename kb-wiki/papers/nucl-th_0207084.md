# The hypernuclear Auger effect within the density dependent relativistic hadron field theory

> The hypernuclear Auger effect, given by the de-excitation of a Lambda hypernucleus by means of the transition of a Lambda hyperon from an initial to a lower lying final single particle state in conjunction with neutron emission from the core nucleus is studied in relativistic DDRH field theory. Baryonic interactions are obtained from the Bonn NN potential by Dirac-Brueckner theory and theoretically derived scaling laws for the meson-hyperon vertices. The model is applied to the ^{209}_Lambda Pb hypernucleus. Lambda and nucleon bound states as well as scattering states are calculated self-consistently in mean-field approximation. The Auger spectra of the emitted neutrons are of complex structure due to a huge combinatorial number of possible hypernuclear transitions. The sensitivity of the neutron Auger spectra to changes in the Lambda sigma and Lambda omega vertices are investigated. The theoretical results show that experimental applications of the hypernuclear Auger effect will require special efforts, e.g. by tagging on the energy of the initially created hyperon.

## Key claim

The hypernuclear Auger effect in $^{209}_{\Lambda}$Pb produces neutron spectra of such high complexity that experimental applications will require special efforts, such as tagging on the energy of the initially created hyperon, and even then the sensitivity of the total spectral strength to the $\Lambda$ spin-orbit interaction strength is disappointingly small.

## Method

The authors calculated hypernuclear Auger transition rates in $^{209}_{\Lambda}$Pb using density dependent relativistic hadron field (DDRH) theory, with baryonic interactions derived from the Bonn NN potential via Dirac-Brueckner theory and scaling laws for meson-hyperon vertices. $\Lambda$ and nucleon bound states as well as unbound neutron scattering states were computed self-consistently in relativistic mean-field approximation, with the continuum discretized by enclosing the system in a box of size R = 150 fm. The transition operator was the one-boson-exchange parametrization of the Dirac-Brueckner G-matrix, with only $\sigma$ and $\omega$ mesons contributing due to the $\Lambda$ being electrically neutral and isoscalar.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda$ 1s$_{1/2}$ binding energy in $^{209}_{\Lambda}$Pb: -27.16352 MeV
- $\Lambda$ 1g$_{9/2}$ binding energy: -7.40907 MeV; $\Lambda$ 1g$_{7/2}$ binding energy: -5.80963 MeV
- Neutron 1i$_{13/2}$ binding energy: -9.41419 MeV; neutron 1h$_{11/2}$ binding energy: -18.56635 MeV
- Transition width for $1g_{9/2}^\Lambda \rightarrow 1s_{1/2}^\Lambda$ with $1h_{11/2}$ neutron-hole: 57.1 $\times 10^{-2}$ keV
- Transition width for $1g_{7/2}^\Lambda \rightarrow 1s_{1/2}^\Lambda$ with $1h_{11/2}$ neutron-hole: 77.3 $\times 10^{-2}$ keV
- Transition width for $1g_{9/2}^\Lambda \rightarrow 1p_{3/2}^\Lambda$ with $1i_{13/2}$ neutron-hole: 137.8 $\times 10^{-2}$ keV
- Transition width for $1g_{7/2}^\Lambda \rightarrow 1p_{3/2}^\Lambda$ with $1i_{13/2}$ neutron-hole: 147.6 $\times 10^{-2}$ keV
- Scaling factor for $\Lambda\sigma$ vertex ($R_{\Lambda\sigma}$): 0.49 (from the Julich model)
- Scaling factor for $\Lambda\omega$ vertex ($R_{\Lambda\omega}$): 0.553 (from fit to $\Lambda$ separation energies)
- Spin-orbit variations explored: C = 0.6 (reduced by factor ~2), C = 1.0 (standard), C = 1.3 (increased by factor ~2)

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal of the hypernuclear Auger process by Likar et al. (1986) and supports a planned experiment at JLAB (Margaryan) by providing realistic model predictions for neutron spectral distributions in heavy hypernuclei. The DDRH approach had previously reproduced $\Lambda$ single particle spectra with percent-level accuracy (Keil, 2000), and the present calculations show that while the $1f^\Lambda$ shell may yield a clean spin-orbit doublet via $1i_{13/2}$ neutron knockout, the general case in heavy nuclei like lead produces spectra too complex for unambiguous interpretation without coincidence tagging. The authors suggest that hyperisotopes with $0^+$ core ground states, such as $^{91}_{\Lambda}$Zr and $^{119}_{\Lambda}$Sn, would be more favorable for resolving $\Lambda$ spin-orbit splitting than the $^{89}_{\Lambda}$Y and $^{51}_{\Lambda}$V nuclei used in prior experiments (Hotchi, 2001).
