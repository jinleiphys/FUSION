# The small-x gluon distribution in centrality biased pA and pp collisions

> The nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}(p_T)$ provides information on the small-$x$ gluon distribution of a nucleus at hadron colliders. Several experiments have recently measured the nuclear modification factor not only in minimum bias but also for central $pA$ collisions. In this paper we analyze the bias on the configurations of soft gluon fields introduced by a centrality selection via the number of hard particles. Such bias can be viewed as reweighting of configurations of small-$x$ gluons. We find that the biased nuclear modification factor ${\cal Q}_{pA}(p_T)$ for central collisions is above $R_{pA}(p_T)$ for minimum bias events, and that it may redevelop a "Cronin peak" even at small $x$. The magnitude of the peak is predicted to increase approximately like $1/{A_{\perp}}^ν$, $ν\sim0.6\pm0.1$, if one is able to select more compact configurations of the projectile proton where its gluons occupy a smaller transverse area $A_\perp$. We predict an enhanced ${\cal Q}_{pp}(p_T)-1 \sim 1/(p_T^2)^ν$ and a Cronin peak even for central $pp$ collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the experimentally observed increase of the nuclear modification factor in central pA collisions, and the reappearance of a Cronin peak, can be explained by a bias on the ensemble of small-x gluon configurations introduced by centrality selection, rather than solely by an increased number of binary collisions; the biased nuclear modification factor Q_pA(p_T) for central collisions is predicted to lie above the minimum-bias R_pA(p_T) and may redevelop a Cronin peak even at small x.

## Method

The authors analyze the effect of centrality selection on the small-x gluon distribution by modeling the bias as a reweighting of configurations in the JIMWLK ensemble, where the centrality selector is the number of hard gluons above the extended geometric scaling scale Q_gs,A. They derive analytic expressions for the biased nuclear modification factor using a Gaussian approximation for the distribution of the covariant gauge field A^+, and perform numerical Monte-Carlo simulations of the JIMWLK evolution at fixed coupling, selecting the 5% of configurations with the most gluons at high p_T > Q_gs to illustrate the bias effect.

## Key numbers

* The magnitude of the Cronin peak is predicted to increase approximately like 1/A_perp^nu, with nu ~ 0.6 ± 0.1, when selecting more compact configurations of the projectile proton with smaller transverse area A_perp.
* The biased nuclear modification factor Q_pp(p_T) - 1 is predicted to scale as 1/(p_T^2)^nu for central pp collisions.
* The ratio of initial saturation scales for a heavy ion versus a proton target is set to Q^2_s,A(Y=0)/Q^2_s,p(Y=0) = mu^2_A / mu^2_p = 6, corresponding to the thickness of the nucleus relative to a nucleon.
* The suppression probability p_r for a trial distortion of the gluon distribution is related to the parameters via eta_0 q_0^2 approximately equal to (8 pi log p_r^{-1})/(N_c^2 A_perp log(Q^2/Q^2_s,A)).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of nuclear modification factors beyond minimum-bias collisions, addressing the discrepancy between the naive expectation of stronger suppression in central pA collisions and experimental observations from ALICE (Adam 2014) and ATLAS (Aad 2016) showing an increase with centrality and a Cronin peak. The analysis builds on the JIMWLK framework and the concept of color charge fluctuations previously applied to A+A collisions (AA_color_flucs), and it enables future calculations that account for biased averages over small-x gluon fields when interpreting centrality-selected data from RHIC and LHC experiments.
