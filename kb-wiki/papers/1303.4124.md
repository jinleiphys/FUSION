# Phase space factors for $־²^+־²^+$ decay and competing modes of double-$־²$ decay

> A complete and improved calculation of phase space factors (PSF) for $2־½־²^+־²^+$ and $0־½־²^+־²^+$ decay, as well as for the competing modes $2־½EC־²^+$, $0־½EC־²^+$, and $2־½ECEC$, is presented. The calculation makes use of exact Dirac wave functions with finite nuclear size and electron screening and includes life-times, single and summed positron spectra, and angular positron correlations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete and improved calculation of phase space factors (PSF) for positron-emitting and electron-capture double-beta decay modes, using exact Dirac wave functions with finite nuclear size and electron screening, but the results depend on the approximation of replacing the excitation energy of the intermediate odd-odd nucleus with an average value \(\tilde{A} = 1.12 A^{1/2}\) MeV, which introduces an error of about 1% except near threshold.

## Method

The authors solved the radial Dirac equations for positron scattering wave functions and electron bound state wave functions using the RADIAL subroutine package, incorporating a Coulomb potential with finite nuclear size (uniform charge distribution in a sphere of radius \(R = 1.2 A^{1/3}\) fm) and electron screening via the Thomas-Fermi approximation with boundary conditions appropriate for each decay mode (\(\beta^+\beta^+\), \(EC\beta^+\), \(ECEC\)). They then computed the phase space factors for the two-neutrino and neutrinoless modes of these processes using the formulation of Doi and Kotani, evaluating the positron wave functions at the nuclear radius and using the closure approximation with an average intermediate-nucleus energy.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{78}\)Kr \(2\nu\beta^+\beta^+\) decay, the phase space factor \(G_{2\nu}^{\beta^+\beta^+}\) is \(9770 \times 10^{-29}\) yr\(^{-1}\) (this work), compared to \(13600 \times 10^{-29}\) yr\(^{-1}\) from Doi and Kotani and \(16000 \times 10^{-29}\) yr\(^{-1}\) from Boehm and Vogel.
- For \(^{106}\)Cd \(2\nu\beta^+\beta^+\) decay, \(G_{2\nu}^{\beta^+\beta^+}\) is \(2000 \times 10^{-29}\) yr\(^{-1}\) (this work), compared to \(1970 \times 10^{-29}\) yr\(^{-1}\) from Doi and Kotani and \(2420 \times 10^{-29}\) yr\(^{-1}\) from Boehm and Vogel.
- For \(^{124}\)Xe \(2\nu EC\beta^+\) decay, \(G_{2\nu}^{EC\beta^+}\) is \(1530 \times 10^{-24}\) yr\(^{-1}\) (this work), compared to \(1720 \times 10^{-24}\) yr\(^{-1}\) from Doi and Kotani and \(1408 \times 10^{-24}\) yr\(^{-1}\) from Boehm and Vogel.
- For \(^{124}\)Xe \(2\nu ECEC\) decay, \(G_{2\nu}^{ECEC}\) is \(17200 \times 10^{-24}\) yr\(^{-1}\) (this work), compared to \(20200 \times 10^{-24}\) yr\(^{-1}\) from Doi and Kotani and \(3500 \times 10^{-24}\) yr\(^{-1}\) from Boehm and Vogel.
- The nuclear radius used in the calculation is \(R = 1.2 A^{1/3}\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic calculation of phase space factors for \(\beta^-\beta^-\) decay (Kotila 2012) to the positron-emitting and electron-capture modes (\(\beta^+\beta^+\), \(EC\beta^+\), \(ECEC\)), which have recently gained experimental interest due to their distinctive signatures. It improves upon earlier calculations by Primakoff and Rosen (1950s-60s), Haxton and Stephenson, Doi and Kotani (1990s), and Boehm and Vogel by using exact Dirac wave functions with finite nuclear size and electron screening, rather than approximating positron wave functions at the nucleus or omitting screening. The results enable more accurate predictions of half-lives and decay spectra for these modes, which are needed for experimental searches for neutrinoless double-beta decay and for measuring the absolute neutrino mass scale.
