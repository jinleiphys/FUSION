# The Self-energy of Nucleon in the Pseudovector Coupling Pion-nucleon System for the Electromagnetic Form Factor of Proton

> The electromagnetic form factor of proton is studied following our previous calculation of the anomalous magnetic moment. The non-perturbative term is incorporated to correct the $Q^2$ dependence.

# The Self-energy of Nucleon in the Pseudovector Coupling Pion-nucleon System for the Electromagnetic Form Factor of Proton

## Key claim

The non-perturbative term incorporated into the pion-nucleon-nucleon vertex breaks the relation F1(Q2) ≡ 1 and introduces Q2 dependence into the Dirac form factor, but the resulting charge radius of the proton (1.55 fm) is about twice the experimental value, indicating that the interaction strength is too large when using the exact form of the self-energy.

## Method

The author extends the electromagnetic vertex of the nucleon by adding a non-perturbative term to the pion-nucleon-nucleon three-point vertex, modifying the pseudovector coupling interaction. The self-energy of the nucleon propagator is expressed in closed form with coefficients α(k2) and β(k2), which are approximated by their on-shell values α(M2) and β(M2) to apply dimensional regularization to the k-integral. The resulting expression for the pion-current contribution to the electromagnetic vertex is evaluated, and the electric form factor GE(Q2) is computed from the Dirac form factor F1(Q2) and the anomalous part F2(Q2).

## Key numbers

- Anomalous magnetic moment values from previous calculation: κp = 1.745 for proton and κn = −3.003 for neutron, in units of the nuclear magneton, using pseudovector coupling constant fπ = 1.0.
- Charge radius of proton from lowest-order perturbative expansion: √⟨r2⟩ = 0.34 fm, compared to 0.81 fm from the dipole form factor GD(Q2) = (1 − Q2/0.71)−2.
- Charge radius of proton after including the non-perturbative term with exact self-energy: √⟨r2⟩ = 1.55 fm.
- Charge radius of proton using the lowest-order form of the self-energy Σ(k) ≈ M (M2 + mπ2)−1 (γ·k − M)2: √⟨r2⟩ = 0.41 fm.
- The coefficient α(M2) from the exact self-energy is α(M2) = −2M2/mπ2.
- A decrease of the variable k2 in α(k2) from M2 by only 9% achieves the experimental value of √⟨r2⟩.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculation of the anomalous magnetic moment (Kinpara, 2019) by incorporating a non-perturbative term to correct the Q2 dependence of the electromagnetic form factor. The approach uses the Ward-Takahashi relation analogous to quantum electrodynamics to connect the self-energy and vertex function. The results contradict the dipole form factor parameterization of Gayou (2003) and the Rosenbluth (1950) separation data for electron-proton elastic scattering, showing that the lowest-order perturbative expansion gives too slow a decrease with Q2 while the non-perturbative term gives too fast a decrease. The work enables further investigation of self-consistent equations and higher-order vertex corrections to accurately describe the role of the pion cloud in the electromagnetic structure of the nucleon.
