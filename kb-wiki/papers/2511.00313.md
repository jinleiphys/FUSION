# Challenges for first-principles nuclear structure: $^{11}$Li and $^{29}$F

> Ab initio calculations of atomic nuclei have had many successes in recent years. Nonetheless, important challenges that resist even brute-force calculation remain. As archetypal examples of these challenges, we consider $^{11}$Li and $^{29}$F, well known halo nuclides situated on islands of inversion. The deformed intruder levels, which are primarily two-particle, two-hole neutron excitations with respect to naive spherical shell model configurations, are slow, with respect to increases of the model space, to take their rightful place among, and potentially mix with, the lowest levels. We suggest these systems prototype the challenges for other important intruder states, and can serve as useful testbeds for potential approaches.

## Key claim

In no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations of the halo nuclides $^{11}$Li and $^{29}$F, the deformed intruder states, primarily two-particle, two-hole neutron excitations, converge slowly with increasing model space size $N_\mathrm{max}$, remaining artificially high in energy relative to the normal spherical states unless very large model spaces are used; this slow convergence, rather than a failure of perturbation theory, constitutes the current challenge for first-principles nuclear structure.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations in an M-scheme basis of Slater determinants, using two interactions: a chiral N3LO interaction softened by SRG evolution to $\lambda = 2.0\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1}$, and the Daejeon16 interaction (SRG-evolved to $\lambda = 1.5\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ with phase-shift-equivalent transformations). Wave functions were decomposed into Elliott SU(3) irreps and into $N_\mathrm{ex}\hbar\omega$ components to identify normal versus intruder character, and the dimensionless ratio $Q/r^2$ was computed for quadrupole moments and radii using bare operators.

## Key numbers

- For $^{11}$Li with the Daejeon16 interaction at $N_\mathrm{max}=8$ and $\hbar\omega=12.5\,\mathrm{MeV}$, the normal $3/2^-$ state has less than 5% $0\hbar\omega$ contribution in the intruder state, while the intruder $3/2^-$ state has its largest contributions from $2\hbar\omega$ configurations.
- The normal and intruder $1/2^-$ states in $^{11}$Li (Daejeon16) approach to within approximately $2\,\mathrm{MeV}$ by $N_\mathrm{max}=12$, suggestive of an avoided crossing.
- For $^{11}$Li, the proton ratio $Q_p/r_p^2 \approx -0.6$ is nearly identical for normal and intruder $3/2^-$ states; the neutron ratio $Q_n/r_n^2$ is approximately $-0.2$ for the normal state and approximately $-1.3$ for the intruder state (N3LO interaction).
- For $^{29}$F with the Daejeon16 interaction, the intruder $1/2^+$ and normal $1/2^+$ states approach to within approximately $2\,\mathrm{MeV}$ by $N_\mathrm{max}=6$, with a mixing matrix element estimated below $1\,\mathrm{MeV}$.
- The $^{29}$F NCSM calculations reached a maximum $N_\mathrm{max}=6$, with an M-scheme dimension of $4.7 \times 10^9$.

## Context

This work extends the known challenge of intruder states from the earlier perturbative "intruder crisis" (Barrett 1975) to the modern NCSM framework, where the problem manifests as slow convergence rather than perturbative failure. The paper builds on prior NCSM studies of intruder states in light nuclei (Navratil 2003, Caprio 2019, Caprio 2022, McCoy 2024) and on the recognition of islands of inversion (Warburton 1990, Caurier 2014). The authors propose $^{11}$Li and $^{29}$F as testbeds for advanced methods such as symmetry-adapted configuration interaction (Dytrych 2008, Launey 2013) and angular-momentum-projected coupled cluster (Hu 2024).
