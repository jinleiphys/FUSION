# On the Observation of Phase Transitions in Collisions of Elementary Matter

> We investigate the excitation function of directed flow, which can provide a clear signature of the creation of the QGP and demonstrate that the minimum of the directed flow does not correspond to the softest point of the EoS for isentropic expansion. A novel technique measuring the compactness is introduced to determine the QGP transition in relativistic-heavy ion collisions: The QGP transition will lead to higher compression and therefore to higher compactness of the source in coordinate space. This effect can be observed by pion interferometry. We propose to measure the compactness of the source in the appropriate principal axis frame of the compactness tensor in coordinate space.

## Key claim

The minimum of directed flow in heavy-ion collisions does not correspond to the softest point of the equation of state for isentropic expansion; instead, the compactness of the source in coordinate space, measurable via pion interferometry, provides a clearer signature of the quark-gluon plasma phase transition.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic calculations using both one-fluid and three-fluid models for Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b=3 fm. They employed two equations of state: a relativistic mean field hadron fluid and the same hadron fluid supplemented by a Bag Model EoS with bag constant B^{1/4}=235 MeV for the quark-gluon phase, with phase coexistence constructed via Gibbs' condition. The three-fluid model treated projectile nucleons, target nucleons, and newly produced particles as separate fluids with a dynamical local unification procedure when the combined pressure ratio exceeded 90%.

## Key numbers

- The phase transition critical temperature at zero baryon density is T_C ≈ 170 MeV
- At zero temperature, phase coexistence sets in at baryon density ρ ≈ 4.6ρ₀
- The directed flow shows a local minimum at E_Lab^kin ≈ 5A GeV and a second local maximum around E_Lab^kin ≈ 10-20A GeV
- The three-fluid model predicts an increase of p_x^dir/N toward E_Lab^kin ≈ 40A GeV if phase coexistence occurs
- At the energy corresponding to the minimum of directed flow, the specific entropy is s/ρ ≤ 10
- The compactness tensor is nearly a factor of two flatter in the model with phase transition than without
- The ratio of in-plane radii √(f₂/f₁) remains much smaller in the case with phase transition after the compression stage

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic studies of directed flow as a signature of the QGP phase transition (Yaris; Hung) and addresses the discrepancy between the directed flow minimum and the softest point of the EoS noted by Joergcs. The compactness measurement technique builds on interferometry methods proposed by Lisa and provides a new observable that complements momentum-space flow angles. The results enable experimental searches at AGS and SPS energies, particularly around 40A GeV where data has recently been taken, to determine the onset of a first-order phase transition.
