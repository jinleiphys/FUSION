# Quartetting in Nuclear Matter

> A general theory for the condensation of strongly bound quartets in infinite nuclear matter is presented. Critical temperatures for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter are evaluated. A fully nonlinear theory for the quartet order parameter, based on an analogy of the Gorkov approach to pairing, is presented and solved. The strong qualitative difference with pairing is pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general theory for quartet (alpha-particle) condensation in infinite nuclear matter, showing that such condensation exists only in the Bose-Einstein condensed (BEC) phase and lacks a weak-coupling regime analogous to Cooper pairing, because the three-hole level density vanishes at the Fermi surface for positive chemical potential.

## Method

The authors derive an in-medium four-body wave equation using the Equation of Motion Method (EMM) analogous to RPA, incorporating Hartree-Fock self-energies and Pauli blocking factors. They solve this equation for the critical temperature of alpha condensation using a momentum-projected mean-field ansatz with a separable two-body interaction, and compare with exact Faddeev-Yakubovsky solutions using the Malfliet-Tjon interaction. For the quartet order parameter at zero temperature, they develop a Gorkov-type formalism extending the BCS gap equation to four-particle correlations, analyzing the three-hole level density to understand the analytic structure.

## Key numbers

- The alpha particle dissolves at a Mott density of approximately rho_alpha^Mott ≈ rho_0/10, where rho_0 is nuclear matter saturation density.
- The maximum critical temperature for alpha condensation in symmetric matter occurs at chemical potential μ = 5.5 MeV, with alpha condensation possible up to μ_max = 11 MeV.
- The separable interaction parameters are λ = -992 MeV fm^3 and k_0 = 1.43 fm^{-1} for the alpha particle, fitted to binding energy -28.3 MeV and radius 1.71 fm.
- For deuteron condensation, the fitted parameters are λ = -1305 MeV fm^3 and k_0 = 1.46 fm^{-1}, fitted to binding energy -2.2 MeV and radius 1.95 fm.
- The critical density at which the alpha condensate disappears in symmetric matter is estimated to be rho_0/3.
- For asymmetry δ = 0.9, alpha condensation exists up to n_total = 0.02 fm^{-3} (μ_total = 9.3 MeV), while deuteron condensation exists only up to n_total = 0.005 fm^{-3} (μ_total = 6.0 MeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of alpha clustering in finite nuclei, particularly the THSR wave function of Tohsaki, Horiuchi, Schuck, and Ropke (2001) for the Hoyle state in 12C, to infinite nuclear matter relevant for compact stellar objects. The authors compare their results with prior work on in-medium cluster binding energies by Ropke (1998) and Beyer et al. (2000), and with the Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink (1985) approach for including correlations in the density. The theory enables future investigation of the transition from quartetting to pairing in nuclear matter, and may be applicable to other quartet-correlated systems such as bi-excitons in semiconductors or four-component fermion gases in cold atom physics.
