# Approach to equilibrium of quarkonium in quark-gluon plasma

> We calculate the dissociation and recombination rates of $Υ(1S)$ in quark-gluon plasma by using potential non-relativistic QCD. We then study the dynamical in-medium evolution of the $b\bar{b}-Υ$ system in a periodic box via the Boltzmann equation and explore how the system reaches equilibrium. We find that interactions between the free heavy quarks and the medium are necessary for the system to reach equilibrium. We find that the angular distribution of $Υ(1S)$ probes the stages at which recombination occurs. Finally, we study the system under a longitudinal expansion and show that different initial conditions evolve to distinct final ratios of hidden and open $b$ flavors. We argue that experimental measurements of the ratio could address open questions in the quarkonium production in heavy ion collisions.

# Approach to equilibrium of quarkonium in quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim
The system of bottomonium (Υ(1S)) and unbound b-quarks in quark-gluon plasma can reach chemical equilibrium only if interactions between free heavy quarks and the medium (heavy quark diffusion) are included; without such interactions, a non-thermal initial distribution does not equilibrate. The angular distribution of Υ(1S) can serve as a probe of the stage at which recombination occurs, and different initial conditions for the fraction of initially bound versus free b-quarks evolve to distinct final ratios of hidden to open bottom flavor.

## Method
The authors calculated dissociation and recombination rates of Υ(1S) in quark-gluon plasma using potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) to lowest order, considering transitions between color-singlet Υ(1S) and color-octet unbound b-quark pairs via absorption or emission of on-shell gluons. They solved the Boltzmann transport equation for b, anti-b, and Υ(1S) distribution functions by stochastic Monte-Carlo simulation in a periodic box of QGP, implementing dissociation, recombination, and heavy quark diffusion (via a relaxation-time approximation with rate Γ_r = T²/M). The system was studied at constant temperature T = 350 MeV and under a boost-invariant longitudinal expansion (Bjorken model) with initial temperature T₀ = 350 MeV at t₀ = 1 fm/c and speed of sound c_s² = 1/3.

## Key numbers
- b-quark mass M = 4.65 GeV; relative velocity v ≈ 0.3 for b-quark pair inside Υ(1S); strong coupling α_s = 0.3 throughout.
- At T = 350 MeV with N_b,tot = 50, three initial conditions were simulated: (1) N_b = N_anti-b = 40, N_Υ = 10 with thermal momenta; (2) N_b = N_anti-b = 50, N_Υ = 0 with uniform momenta in range -1 GeV < p_x,p_y,p_z < 1 GeV and no heavy quark diffusion; (3) same as (2) but with heavy quark diffusion.
- The Υ(1S) fraction in proton-proton collisions is approximately 1.76 × 10⁻³.
- The QGP temperature range considered is T_C < T < 2.5 T_C, with T_C = 155 MeV.
- The periodic box side length L = 10 fm; the Gaussian width σ for recombination position dependence was chosen as the Υ(1S) Bohr radius.
- Degeneracy factors: g_b = g_anti-b = 6, g_Υ = 4 (hyperfine splitting not considered, η_b and Υ(1S) treated as degenerate).

## Context
This work extends the pNRQCD-based calculation of dissociation rates (Brambilla 2008, Brambilla 2011, Brambilla 2013) to include recombination rates consistently without assuming the quarkonium distribution is near equilibrium, unlike coalescence models based on Wigner functions (Young 2008, Chen 2017) or detailed balance approaches (Du 2017) that are only valid near chemical and thermal equilibrium. The study enables experimental measurements of the hidden-to-open heavy flavor ratio at the CERN Large Hadron Collider and BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider to discriminate between different initial quarkonium production mechanisms in heavy ion collisions, addressing open questions about the relative importance of initial production versus in-medium recombination.
