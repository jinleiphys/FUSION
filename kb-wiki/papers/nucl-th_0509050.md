# Neutron Dripline in Odd and Even Mass Calcium and Nickel Nuclei

> Neutron rich Ca and Ni nuclei have been studied in spherical Relativistic Mean Field formalism in co-ordinate space. A delta interaction has been has been adopted to treat the pairing correlations for the neutrons. Odd nuclei have been treated in the blocking approximation. The effect of the positive energy continuum and the role of pairing in the stability of nuclei have been investigated using the resonant-BCS (rBCS) approach. In Ca isotopes, N=50 is no longer a magic number while in Ni nuclei, a new magic number emerges at N=70. There is a remarkable difference in the relative positions of the drip lines for odd and even isotopes. In Ca isotopes, the last bound even and odd nuclei are found to be $^{72}$Ca and $^{59}$Ca, respectively. In Ni isotopes, the corresponding nuclei are $^{98}$Ni and $^{97}$Ni, respectively. The origin of this difference in relative positions of the dripline in even and odd isotopes in the two chain is traced to the difference in the single particle level structures and consequent modification in the magic numbers in the two elements. Pairing interaction is seen to play a major role. The effect of the width of the resonance states on pairing has also been investigated.

# Neutron Dripline in Odd and Even Mass Calcium and Nickel Nuclei

## Key claim

The relative positions of the neutron dripline for odd versus even isotopes differ dramatically between Ca and Ni nuclei: in Ca, the last bound odd nucleus (⁵⁹Ca) is far lighter than the last bound even nucleus (⁷²Ca), whereas in Ni the last bound odd (⁹⁷Ni) and even (⁹⁸Ni) nuclei differ by only one neutron. This difference arises from the single-particle level structure and the role of pairing, which stabilizes even-even Ca isotopes near the continuum but is insufficient to bind odd Ca isotopes beyond N=40.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) calculations in coordinate space with exact boundary conditions, using the NLSH force. Pairing correlations for neutrons were treated with a delta interaction in a resonant-BCS (rBCS) approach that includes the width of positive-energy resonance states. Odd-mass nuclei were treated using the blocking approximation, and the calculations included all bound states plus resonant continuum states up to N=82 for Ca and N=126 for Ni.

## Key numbers

- Last bound even Ca isotope: ⁷²Ca; last bound odd Ca isotope: ⁵⁹Ca
- Last bound even Ni isotope: ⁹⁸Ni; last bound odd Ni isotope: ⁹⁷Ni
- In Ca, odd isotopes are stable only below N=40; even isotopes range up to N=52
- In Ni, both even and odd isotopes are stable up to the new magic number N=70
- The delta interaction strength used was V₀ = -350 MeV
- The coordinate-space grid size was 0.08 fm; nuclear interaction was assumed to vanish beyond 20 fm

## Context

This work extends previous RMF and nonrelativistic mean-field studies of neutron-rich Ca and Ni isotopes (e.g., Fayans et al., Im et al., Yadav et al., and Zhang et al. using RCHB) by systematically including odd-mass nuclei, which had not been studied in detail near the dripline. The finding that N=50 is no longer magic in Ca while N=70 emerges as a new magic number in Ni contrasts with earlier expectations and parallels the halo phenomenon seen in light nuclei such as ¹¹Li and ¹⁴Be, where pairing stabilizes the last neutron pair but the corresponding odd nuclei are unbound. The results enable future experimental searches for the dripline nuclei ⁵⁹Ca, ⁷²Ca, ⁹⁷Ni, and ⁹⁸Ni, and highlight the need to treat continuum effects and resonance widths in drip-line calculations.
