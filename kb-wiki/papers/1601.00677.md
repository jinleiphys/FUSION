# Pairing Nambu-Goldstone Modes within Nuclear Density Functional Theory

> We show that the Nambu-Goldstone formalism of the broken gauge symmetry in the presence of the $T=1$ pairing condensate offers a quantitative description of the binding-energy differences of open-shell superfluid nuclei. We conclude that the pairing-rotational moments of inertia are excellent pairing indicators, which are free from ambiguities attributed to odd-mass systems. We offer a new, unified interpretation of the binding-energy differences traditionally viewed in the shell model picture as signatures of the valence nucleon properties. We present the first systematic analysis of the off-diagonal pairing-rotational moments of inertia and demonstrate the mixing of the neutron and proton pairing-rotational modes in the ground states of even-even nuclei. Finally, we discuss the importance of mass measurements of neutron-rich nuclei for constraining the pairing energy density functional.

## Key claim

The Nambu-Goldstone formalism of broken gauge symmetry in the presence of T=1 pairing condensates can quantitatively describe binding-energy differences of open-shell superfluid nuclei, provided that the pairing-rotational moments of inertia are computed at the self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation level rather than at the simpler Belyaev level.

## Method

The authors employed nuclear density functional theory with the UNEDF1-HFB energy density functional, computing Thouless-Valatin moments of inertia for pairing rotations using the finite amplitude method variant of the quasiparticle random-phase approximation. They performed systematic calculations for chains of semimagic nuclei (Sn, Pb, Ca isotopes and N=50 isotones) and doubly-open-shell nuclei (Er isotopes and N=100 isotones), comparing three types of pairing interactions: volume-type (eta=0), mixed-type (eta=0.5), and surface-type (eta=1). The pairing strengths for volume and surface pairing were adjusted to reproduce the average neutron pairing gap in 120Sn and average proton pairing gap in 92Mo.

## Key numbers

- For volume pairing: V0^n = -146.07 MeV fm^3 and V0^p = -161.72 MeV fm^3, adjusted to reproduce average pairing gaps in 120Sn and 92Mo respectively
- For surface pairing: V0^n = -474.32 MeV fm^3 and V0^p = -551.37 MeV fm^3, adjusted to reproduce average pairing gaps in 120Sn and 92Mo respectively
- The reference nucleus for Sn isotope calculations was 116Sn with N0=66
- For very neutron-rich Ca isotopes beyond 56Ca, volume-type pairing yields 1.5 to 2 times larger values of J_nn than mixed-pairing interaction
- The proton pairing strength required for agreement with experiment is significantly larger than the neutron strength, with V0^p/V0^n approximately 1.1
- The pairing density cutoff rho_c = 0.16 fm^-3 was used in the pairing functional

## Context

This work extends the Nambu-Goldstone formalism of pairing rotations originally developed by Bayman (1969), Brink and Broglia, and others, providing the first systematic analysis of off-diagonal pairing-rotational moments of inertia J_np in doubly-open-shell nuclei. The authors demonstrate that the pairing-rotational inertia tensor can be directly expressed in terms of binding-energy differences delta_2n, delta_2p, and delta_V_pn, which have traditionally been interpreted in the shell model picture as signatures of valence nucleon properties. This offers a unified interpretation of these quantities in terms of gauge symmetry breaking associated with collective T=1 pairing phases, and questions the findings of Qi (2012) that pairing energy plays a relatively minor role in understanding delta_V_pn. The results enable future constraints on the pairing energy density functional through mass measurements of neutron-rich nuclei, particularly Ca isotopes beyond N=36.
