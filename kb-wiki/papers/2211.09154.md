# Rethinking the $P_c(4457)^+$ as the $P_ψ^{Δ^+}(4457)$ isoquartet $\bar{D}^* Σ_c$ molecule

> The nature of the $P_{c}(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ pentaquarks is a fascinating theoretical question. Within the molecular picture their more usual interpretation is that of $I=\tfrac{1}{2}$ $\bar{D} Σ_c$ and $\bar{D}^* Σ_c$ bound states. Here we argue in favor of interpreting the $P_c(4457)$ pentaquark as a $I=\tfrac{3}{2}$ $\bar{D}^* Σ_c$ bound state (with spin $J=\tfrac{1}{2}$) instead. Owing to isospin symmetry breaking effects, with this identification the partial decay width of the $P_c(4457)^+$ into $J/ψp$ will be of the same order of magnitude as the $P_{c}(4312)^+$ and $P_c(4440)^+$, in contrast with the considerably larger partial decay width in the $I=\tfrac{1}{2}$ scenario. In turn, this leads to a different hidden-charm molecular pentaquark spectrum, in which there are only four or five $P_ψ^N$ bound states instead of the usual seven, which might explain why the predicted $J=\tfrac{1}{2}$ and $\tfrac{3}{2}$ ($I=\tfrac{1}{2}$) $\bar{D}^* Σ_c^*$ molecular partners of the $P_c(4312)$ and $P_c(4440)$ have not been observed.

## Key claim

The $P_c(4457)^+$ pentaquark may be better interpreted as an $I=\tfrac{3}{2}$ (decuplet) $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ bound state with spin $J=\tfrac{1}{2}$, rather than the usual $I=\tfrac{1}{2}$ (octet) assignment. This identification resolves the problem of the predicted $J/\psi p$ partial decay width being one order of magnitude larger than observed, provided isospin symmetry breaking effects are included and the state is close to threshold.

## Method

The authors perform a contact-range effective field theory (EFT) analysis of the $\bar{D}^{(*)} \Sigma_c^{(*)}$ molecular pentaquark system, using leading-order (LO) potentials with Gaussian regularization and a cutoff of $\Lambda = 0.75$ GeV. They compute partial decay widths into $J/\psi N$ and $\bar{D}^{(*)} \Lambda_c$ from the light- and heavy-quark spin decomposition of the meson-baryon pairs, and solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to determine bound state masses. The octet couplings $C_a^O$ and $C_b^O$ are calibrated from the $P_c(4312)$ and $P_c(4440)$ masses, while the decuplet coupling $C^D$ is fixed by reproducing the $P_c(4457)$ mass with explicit isospin breaking in the $\bar{D}^{*0} \Sigma_c^{+}$-${D}^{*-} \Sigma_c^{++}$ basis.

## Key numbers

- Masses of the three LHCb pentaquarks: $M(P_{c1}) = 4311.9 \pm 0.7^{+6.8}_{-0.6}$ MeV, $\Gamma = 9.8 \pm 2.7^{+3.7}_{-4.5}$ MeV; $M(P_{c2}) = 4440.3 \pm 1.3^{+4.1}_{-4.7}$ MeV, $\Gamma = 20.6 \pm 4.9^{+8.7}_{-10.1}$ MeV; $M(P_{c3}) = 4457.3 \pm 0.6^{+4.1}_{-1.7}$ MeV, $\Gamma = 6.4 \pm 2.0^{+5.7}_{-1.9}$ MeV (LHCb, Aaij 2019)
- Ratio of $J/\psi N$ partial decay widths for octet assignment: $1 : 1.8 : 11.5$ for $P_{c1}$, $P_{c2}$, $P_{c3}$ respectively (with Gaussian regulator, $\Lambda = 0.75$ GeV)
- Ratio of $J/\psi N$ partial decay widths for decuplet assignment (with isospin breaking): $1 : 1.83 : 0.44^{+3.64}_{-0.41}$ (central value with cutoff variation $(0.26-0.66)$)
- Experimental production fraction ratios: $\mathcal{F}_2/\mathcal{F}_1 = 3.7^{+2.5}_{-2.3}$, $\mathcal{F}_3/\mathcal{F}_1 = 1.8 \pm 1.2$ (LHCb, Aaij 2019)
- Predicted $\bar{D}^{(*)} \Lambda_c$ decay width for $P_{c3}$ as octet: $\Gamma_{P_{c3}} \simeq 81^{+38}_{-43}$ MeV; as decuplet: $\Gamma_{P_{c3}} \simeq 7.0^{+3.3}_{-3.7}$ MeV (for $\theta_I = 20.1^\circ$) or $0.2 \pm 0.1$ MeV (for $\theta_I = 35.3^\circ$)
- Octet couplings from set (i): $C_a^O = -1.19^{+0.17}_{-0.26}$ fm$^2$, $C_b^O = -0.38^{+0.08}_{-0.15}$ fm$^2$ (at $\Lambda = 0.75$ GeV)
- Decuplet coupling $C^D = -0.97^{+0.11}_{-0.14}$ fm$^2$ (at $\Lambda = 0.75$ GeV), with isospin angle $\theta_I = (26.6^{+7.6}_{-28.4})^\circ$
- Predicted $J=\tfrac{3}{2}$ $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c^*$ octet mass: $4523.8^{+0.5}_{-7.7}$ MeV; decuplet mass: $4530.3^{+7.0}_{-4.6} - (1.9_{-1.9}^{+10.2})i$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the molecular pentaquark program following the LHCb discovery of the $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$, and $P_c(4457)$ (Aaij 2019). It addresses inconsistencies noted by Burns (2021, 2022) and Kuang (2020) regarding the octet interpretation of $P_c(4457)$, particularly its problematic $J/\psi p$ decay width (Sakai 2019) and the non-observation of predicted $J=\tfrac{1}{2}$ and $\tfrac{3}{2}$ $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c^*$ partners. The decuplet assignment reduces the predicted number of bound $P_{\psi}^N$ states from seven to four or five, potentially explaining why certain HQSS-predicted partners remain unseen. The work enables future experimental tests via improved measurements of $P_c(4457)$ production rates in $\Lambda_b$ decays and $J/\psi p$ branching ratios, which could distinguish between the octet and decuplet hypotheses.
