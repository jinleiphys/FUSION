# Superdense Matter

> We review recent work on the phase structure of QCD at very high baryon density. We introduce the phenomenon of color superconductivity and discuss the use of weak coupling methods. We study the phase structure as a function of the number of flavors and their masses. We also introduce effective theories that describe low energy excitations at high baryon density. Finally, we study the possibility of kaon condensation at very large baryon density.

# Superdense Matter

## Key claim

At very high baryon density, QCD matter is expected to form a color superconductor; for three degenerate light flavors, the high-density color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase and the low-density hadronic phase may be continuously connected without an intervening phase transition, though a gauge-invariant U(1) order parameter related to superfluidity distinguishes them.

## Method

The paper reviews weak-coupling methods for dense QCD, including the solution of the Eliashberg-type gap equation with one-gluon exchange to compute the superconducting gap. It then develops effective field theories, the CFL chiral theory (CFLχTh) and the high-density effective theory (HDET), to describe low-energy excitations and the effects of non-zero quark masses, and uses these to compute Goldstone boson masses and the condition for kaon condensation.

## Key numbers

- The gap on the Fermi surface for two-flavor QCD is approximately Δ₀ ≃ 512π⁴(2/N_f)^{5/2} μ g⁻⁵ exp(−3π²/(√2 g)), with the pre-exponent containing only an estimate.
- Extrapolating perturbative calculations to densities ρ ≃ 5ρ₀ yields gaps Δ ≃ 100 MeV.
- The pion decay constant in the CFL phase is f_π² = (21 − 8 log(2))/18 × (p_F²/(2π²)).
- The coefficients A₁ and A₂ in the CFL chiral theory are A₁ = −A₂ = 3Δ²/(4π²), with A₃ = 0.
- The critical strange quark mass for kaon condensation is m_s|_{crit} = 3.03 · m_d^{1/3} Δ^{2/3}.
- The Debye screening mass squared is m_D² = (21 − 8 log(2)) p_F²/(36π²).

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on color superconductivity by Alford, Rapp, and collaborators (1998) and the CFL phase proposed by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999). It builds on weak-coupling gap equation calculations by Son (1999), Pisarski, and Hong (2000), and on the effective chiral theory of the CFL phase developed by Casalbuoni and Gatto (1999). The results enable quantitative predictions for the phase diagram of dense QCD relevant to neutron star interiors, including the possibility of kaon condensation and the location of the unlocking transition between the CFL and less symmetric phases.
