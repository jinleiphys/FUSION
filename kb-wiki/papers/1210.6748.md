# The relativistic correction of the quarkonium melting temperature with a holographic potential

> The relativistic correction of the AdS/CFT implied heavy quark potential is examined within the framework of the potential model. For the typical range of the coupling strength appropriate to heavy-ion collisions, we find the correction is significant in size and lowers the dissociation temperature of quarkonia.

## Key claim
The relativistic correction to the AdS/CFT implied heavy quark potential is significant in size and lowers the dissociation temperature of quarkonia, with the magnitude of the correction ranging from 8% for lambda=5.5 to 30% for lambda=6pi for J/psi, though the results remain incomplete because the gravity dual of spin-dependent forces is not available.

## Method
The authors used a two-body Dirac equation and performed a Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation to obtain the leading relativistic correction (order v^4) to the holographic heavy quark potential. They applied this to the truncated Coulomb approximation of the holographic potential and computed the corrected dissociation temperatures for charmonium and bottomonium states using first-order perturbation theory, separating the kinetic energy correction (p^4 term) from the Darwin and spin-orbit coupling contributions.

## Key numbers
- For J/psi (1s state of c\bar{c}) at lambda=5.5: non-relativistic dissociation temperature T_d = 143 MeV (truncated Coulomb), corrected to 130.79 MeV (including all v^4 corrections)
- For J/psi (1s state of c\bar{c}) at lambda=6pi: non-relativistic dissociation temperature T_d = 265 MeV (truncated Coulomb), corrected to 188.65 MeV (including all v^4 corrections)
- For Upsilon (1s state of b\bar{b}) at lambda=5.5: corrected dissociation temperature = 385.63 MeV
- For Upsilon (1s state of b\bar{b}) at lambda=6pi: corrected dissociation temperature = 555.58 MeV
- For 2s state of c\bar{c} at lambda=5.5: corrected dissociation temperature = 26.71 MeV
- For 1p state of c\bar{c} at lambda=5.5: corrected dissociation temperature ranges from 30.96 MeV (J=2) to 32.65 MeV (J=0) depending on total angular momentum
- The screening length r_0 = rho_0/(pi T) is about 0.25 fm at T=200 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Hou, 2012) which examined heavy quarkonium dissociation using the holographic potential with a non-relativistic Schroedinger equation and found dissociation temperatures systematically lower than lattice predictions. The relativistic correction further lowers the dissociation temperatures, moving them further from lattice results, which the authors attribute to the short screening length and sharp cutoff nature of the AdS/CFT potential. The paper notes that a phenomenological two-body Dirac approach by Wong (2007) and applied by Zhuang (2012) to the same problem uses a different method to generate spin-dependent forces, but the authors question its applicability beyond weak coupling.
