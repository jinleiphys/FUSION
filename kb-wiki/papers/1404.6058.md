# The non-ordinary Regge behavior of the $f_0(500)$ meson

> We review how the Regge trajectory of an elastic resonance can be obtained just from its pole position and coupling, by means of a dispersive formalism. This allows to deal correctly with the finite widths of resonances in Regge trajectories. For the $ρ(770)$ meson this method leads to the ordinary linear Regge trajectory with a universal slope. In contrast, for the $f_0(500)$ meson, the resulting Regge trajectory is non-linear and with much smaller slope. This is another strong indication of the non-ordinary nature of the lightest scalar meson.

# The non-ordinary Regge behavior of the $f_0(500)$ meson

## Key claim
The Regge trajectory of the $f_0(500)$ meson, extracted from its pole position and coupling via a dispersive formalism that correctly handles finite resonance widths, is non-linear and has a slope about two orders of magnitude smaller than ordinary quark-antiquark trajectories, providing strong support for a non-ordinary nature of the lightest scalar meson. The result is robust within the uncertainties of the input pole parameters, and imposing a typical linear trajectory on the $f_0(500)$ deteriorates the fit to the pole and coupling, producing an amplitude qualitatively different from observations.

## Method
The authors solve a coupled system of dispersion relations for the Regge trajectory $\alpha(s)$ and residue $\beta(s)$, derived from elastic unitarity and analyticity in the complex angular momentum plane. The three free parameters ($\alpha_0$, $\alpha'$, $b_0$) are fixed by fitting only the real and imaginary parts of the resonance pole position $s_M \simeq (M_R - i\Gamma/2)^2$ and the absolute value of the pole residue $|g_M|$, taken from a precise dispersive representation of $\pi\pi$ scattering data (GarciaMartin 2011). The resulting Regge amplitudes are then compared to the empirical partial waves on the real axis.

## Key numbers
- $\rho(770)$ trajectory parameters: $\alpha_0 = 0.520 \pm 0.002$, $\alpha' = 0.902 \pm 0.004$ GeV$^{-2}$, $b_0 = 0.52$.
- $f_0(500)$ trajectory parameters: $\alpha_0 = -0.090^{+0.004}_{-0.012}$, $\alpha' = 0.002^{+0.050}_{-0.001}$ GeV$^{-2}$, $b_0 = 0.12$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- The $f_0(500)$ slope is about two orders of magnitude smaller than the linear trajectories of ordinary quark-antiquark resonances such as $\rho$, $a_2$, $f_2$, or $\pi_2$.
- From the Yukawa potential comparison, the estimated range parameter $a \simeq 0.5$ GeV$^{-1}$, compared to the S-wave $\pi\pi$ scattering length $\simeq 1.6$ GeV$^{-1}$.

## Context
This work extends the dispersive formalism developed by the authors (Londergan 2013) to extract Regge trajectories from resonance poles, and applies it to the $\rho(770)$ and $f_0(500)$ mesons in elastic $\pi\pi$ scattering. The $\rho(770)$ yields an ordinary linear trajectory consistent with prior determinations (Pelaez 2003, PDG, Anisovich 2000, Masjuan 2012), while the $f_0(500)$ trajectory is non-linear with a much smaller slope, explaining why the lightest scalar meson must be excluded from ordinary linear Regge fits of ordinary mesons. The results suggest the $f_0(500)$ resembles a low-energy resonance of a short-range potential (e.g., between pions) rather than a bound state of a confining quark-antiquark force, and the tiny slope excludes the possibility that any known isoscalar resonance lies on its trajectory.
