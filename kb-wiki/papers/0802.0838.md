# Nuclear Energy Density Functionals Constrained by Low-Energy QCD

> A microscopic framework of nuclear energy density functionals is reviewed, which establishes a direct relation between low-energy QCD and nuclear structure, synthesizing effective field theory methods and principles of density functional theory. Guided by two closely related features of QCD in the low-energy limit: a) in-medium changes of vacuum condensates, and b) spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry; a relativistic energy density functional is developed and applied in studies of ground-state properties of spherical and deformed nuclei.

# Nuclear Energy Density Functionals Constrained by Low-Energy QCD

## Key claim

A relativistic nuclear energy density functional can be constructed that bridges low-energy QCD and nuclear structure phenomenology, but the framework is developed as a review of a novel program rather than a definitive derivation, and the functional is applied only to ground-state properties of spherical and deformed nuclei without claims of universal predictive power for all nuclear phenomena.

## Method

The paper reviews the integration of effective field theory (EFT) methods with density functional theory (DFT) to construct a relativistic nuclear energy density functional. The approach is guided by two features of low-energy QCD: in-medium changes of vacuum condensates and spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry, with long- and intermediate-range interactions generated by one- and two-pion exchange derived from in-medium chiral perturbation theory. The resulting functional is translated into a relativistic point-coupling model with density-dependent vertices, including second-order gradient corrections to the local density approximation, and applied to self-consistent calculations of ground-state properties across a range of spherical and deformed nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: fπ = 92.4 MeV, determined from the decay π+ → μ+ νμ + μ+ νμ γ
- Chiral condensate vacuum value: ⟨q̄q⟩ ≈ −(240 MeV)³ ≈ −1.8 fm⁻³, obtained from the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation using mu + md ≈ 12 MeV and fπ = 92.4 MeV
- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³
- Pion mass: ≈ 140 MeV, compared to hadronic scales
- Light quark masses at scale of 1 GeV: mu = 4 ± 2 MeV, md = 8 ± 4 MeV
- Strange quark mass: ms ∼ 150 MeV
- ρ meson mass: mρ ≈ 0.77 GeV; a1 meson mass: ma1 ≈ 1.23 GeV

## Context

This review extends the program initiated by Furnstahl, Serot, and collaborators (2002-2007) that integrates chiral effective field theory with density functional theory for nuclear systems. It contrasts with purely phenomenological approaches such as the Skyrme energy functional (with over one hundred parameterizations developed over thirty years) and the Gogny interaction, which are not necessarily related to any realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. The framework enables a systematic path from low-energy QCD to nuclear structure calculations, though the author notes that available data can only constrain a subset of parameters in the general functional form, and additional criteria are needed for selecting the optimal energy density functional.
