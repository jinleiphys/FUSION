# Toward extracting scattering phase shift from integrated correlation functions V: complex $ϕ^4$ field model in $3+1$ dimensions

> In Ref.~\cite{Guo:2024zal} and associated studies, a relativistic finite-volume formalism in $1+1$ dimensions is proposed to extract infinite-volume scattering phaseshift. It is based on the difference of integrated correlation functions (ICF) rather than energy spectrum in the finite volume, and can be regarded as complementary to the well-known L\"{uscher} formalism. In the present work, the formalism is further extended into $3+1$ dimensional spacetime. The aim is to explore and demonstrate the challenges in applying the formalism to more practical settings. Specifically, Monte Carlo simulations of a complex $ϕ^4$ relativistic field model are carried out in both 2+1 and 3+1 dimensions on lattices of varying sizes, and phaseshifts for the contact interaction are extracted from the formalism using modest computing resources.

## Key claim

The paper extends the integrated correlation function (ICF) formalism for extracting infinite-volume scattering phase shifts from finite-volume simulations from 1+1 dimensions to 3+1 dimensions, demonstrating feasibility using Monte Carlo simulations of a complex φ⁴ field model, but with the caveat that the signal quality on large lattices (L=24, 28) shows significant fluctuations at larger Euclidean times, limiting the precision of the extracted phase shift.

## Method

Monte Carlo simulations of a complex φ⁴ relativistic field model were performed on Euclidean lattices of size L³ × T using the Hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm, with spatial extents ranging from L=4 to L=28 and temporal extent T=60. The difference between interacting and non-interacting integrated correlation functions (ICF) in the A₁⁺ irreducible representation of the cubic symmetry group was computed, and the renormalized contact interaction strength V_R(0) was extracted by fitting the ICF difference to a perturbative expression, from which the S-wave phase shift was obtained via the quantization condition.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle mass in the non-interacting case (κ=0.0618, λ=0): m = 0.299 ± 0.004 (lattice units)
- Single-particle mass in the interacting case (κ=0.0666, λ=0.03): m = 0.17084 ± 0.0003 (lattice units), with finite-volume fit parameter c = 1.92 ± 0.02
- Renormalized contact interaction strength extracted from L=24 and L=28 lattices: V_R(0) = 2.5 ± 0.5
- Lattice sizes used for interacting simulations: L = 12, 16, 20, 24, 28, with T = 60 and statistics of approximately 200k measurements per case

## Context

This work extends the ICF formalism previously developed for 1+1 dimensions by Guo et al. (2024) to 3+1 dimensions, providing an alternative to the well-known Lüscher formalism that avoids the need for extracting energy spectra from finite-volume correlation functions. The approach is tested on a complex φ⁴ model with contact interaction, and the results show convergence to the infinite-volume limit as lattice size increases, though with statistical uncertainties that limit precision. The method is intended to be applicable to more complex theories such as lattice QCD, where it may help overcome signal-to-noise problems and challenges with dense energy spectra at large volumes.
