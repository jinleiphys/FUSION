# Hagedorn's Hadron Mass Spectrum and the Onset of Deconfinement

> A brief history of the observation of the onset of deconfinement - the beginning of the creation of quark gluon plasma in nucleus-nucleus collisions with increasing collision energy - is presented. It starts with the measurement of hadron mass spectrum and the Hagedorn's hypothesis of the limiting temperature of hadronic matter (the Hagedorn temperature). Then the conjecture that the Hagedorn temperature is the phase transition temperature was formulated with the crucial Hagedorn participation. It was confirmed by the observation of the onset of deconfinement in lead-lead collisions at the CERN SPS energies.

## Key claim

The Hagedorn temperature (approximately 160 MeV) is not a true limiting temperature of hadronic matter but rather the transition temperature from hadron gas to quark-gluon plasma (QGP), resolving the apparent contradiction between Hagedorn's statistical bootstrap model and the Fermi-Pomeranchuk-Landau picture of unlimited temperature growth with collision energy.

## Method

The authors review the historical development from Hagedorn's 1965 Statistical Bootstrap Model, which predicted an exponentially increasing hadron mass spectrum and a limiting temperature, through the formulation of the Statistical Model of the Early Stage (SMES) in the mid-1990s. The SMES extended Fermi's statistical model by assuming statistical production of confined matter at low collision energies and statistical QGP creation at high energies, predicting a rapid change in hadron production properties as a signal of the onset of deconfinement. This prediction was tested by the NA49 Collaboration at CERN SPS through an energy scan program measuring hadron production properties in lead-lead collisions.

## Key numbers

- Hagedorn temperature estimated from 1965 data: Th ≈ 160 MeV, defined as Th = 1/b where b is the exponential slope parameter in the mass spectrum ρ(m) = const × m^(-a) × exp(b×m)
- Pomeranchuk's estimated freeze-out temperature: ≈150 MeV, close to the pion mass
- The onset of deconfinement was located at the low CERN SPS energies, identified through the "horn" structure in the K⁺/π⁺ ratio and the "step" structure in the inverse slope parameter of K⁻ transverse mass spectra as functions of collision energy

## Context

This work extends the Hagedorn-Rafelski conjecture (1980) and the Gorenstein-Petrov-Zinovjev quark-gluon bag model, which reinterpreted Hagedorn's massive states as quark-gluon bags rather than point-like objects. It contradicts the earlier Fermi-Pomeranchuk-Landau model (1950-1953) which assumed unlimited temperature increase with collision energy. The discovery of the onset of deconfinement by the NA49 Collaboration at CERN SPS, motivated by SMES predictions, enables further study of QGP properties at higher energies at RHIC and LHC, and opens questions about the detailed nature of the phase transition between hadronic matter and QGP.
