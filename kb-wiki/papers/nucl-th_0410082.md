# Self-consistent Green's function calculation of 16O at small missing energies

> Calculations of the one-hole spectral function of 16O for small missing energies are reviewed. The self-consistent Green's function approach is employed together with the Faddeev equations technique in order to study the coupling of both particle-particle and particle-hole phonons to the single-particle motion. The results indicate that the characteristics of hole fragmentation are related to the low-lying states of 16O and an improvement of the description of this spectrum, beyond the random phase approximation, is required to understand the experimental strength distribution. A first calculation in this direction that accounts for two-phonon states is discussed.

## Key claim

The characteristics of hole fragmentation in 16O are related to the low-lying excited states of the nucleus, and a proper description of the experimental spectral strength requires an improvement of the excitation spectrum beyond the random phase approximation (RPA). The authors identify the poor description of the excitation spectrum by standard (D)RPA equations as the main impediment to obtaining a good theoretical description of the single-particle spectral function.

## Method

The self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) approach was employed together with the Faddeev equations technique to study the coupling of both particle-particle and particle-hole phonons to single-particle motion in 16O. The Dyson equation was solved in a model space of harmonic oscillator states (b = 1.76 fm, ℏω = 13.4 MeV) including the first four major shells plus the 1g9/2 orbital, using a Brueckner G-matrix derived from the Bonn-C potential as the effective interaction. The ph-DRPA (dressed Tamm-Dancoff/RPA) equations were solved first, then the lowest DRPA solutions for the 0+, 3− and 1− channels were shifted to their experimental energies and used to generate two-phonon contributions via the extended RPA (ERPA) formalism.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor for the main p1/2 quasihole peak: 0.775 (TDA), 0.745 (RPA), converging to 0.776 after four iterations including fragmentation
- Spectroscopic factor for the main p3/2 quasihole peak: 0.766 (TDA), 0.725 (RPA), converging to 0.750 after four iterations including fragmentation
- A second smaller p3/2 fragment appears at -26.3 MeV missing energy with a spectroscopic factor of 2.6% when the lowest 0+ excitation is shifted to its experimental energy
- A d5/2 hole peak obtained at a missing energy of -17.7 MeV when both the 3− and 1− ph-DRPA solutions are shifted to match experimental values
- The first isoscalar 0+ state in DRPA is found at approximately 17 MeV; the two-phonon ERPA produces a lower 0+ solution at approximately 11 MeV that is predominately a two-phonon state
- Low-lying 3− and 1− states in ERPA appear at 9.23 MeV and 10.90 MeV respectively, remaining above experimental energies
- A triplet of states at about 12 MeV (0+, 2+, 4+) is generated by the two-phonon ERPA, made almost exclusively of 3− ⊗ 3− configurations

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent Green's function calculations for 16O (Barbieri and Dickhoff, 2004) and addresses the substantial disagreement between experimental spectroscopic factors from (e,e'p) reactions (Leuschner et al., 1994; Udias et al., 1995) and theoretical calculations (Müther et al., 1995; Geurts et al., 1996; Faddeev RPA for 16O, 2001). The results show that coupling two-phonon states via the ERPA formalism can explain several excited states not obtained by RPA calculations, but a full solution likely requires considering up to four-phonon states and interactions in the pp and hh channels as done by Iachello (1981). This enables future work toward a consistent description of both the excitation spectrum and the single-particle spectral function of 16O.
