# Electromagnetic and neutral-weak response functions of 4He and 12C

> Ab initio calculations of the quasi-elastic electromagnetic and neutral-weak response functions of 4He and 12C are carried out for the first time. They are based on a realistic approach to nuclear dynamics, in which the strong interactions are described by two- and three-nucleon potentials and the electroweak interactions with external fields include one- and two-body terms. The Green's function Monte Carlo method is used to calculate directly the Laplace transforms of the response functions, and maximum-entropy techniques are employed to invert the resulting imaginary-time correlation functions with associated statistical errors. The theoretical results, confirmed by experiment in the electromagnetic case, show that two-body currents generate excess transverse strength from threshold to the quasi-elastic to the dip region and beyond. These findings challenge the conventional picture of quasi-elastic inclusive scattering as being largely dominated by single-nucleon knockout processes.

## Key claim

Two-body electroweak currents generate significant excess transverse strength in the quasi-elastic response of 4He and 12C, from threshold through the quasi-elastic peak and into the dip region, challenging the conventional picture that inclusive quasi-elastic scattering is dominated by single-nucleon knockout. The theoretical results are confirmed by experiment in the electromagnetic case, but the neutral-weak predictions await experimental verification.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations of the Laplace transforms (Euclidean response functions) of the electromagnetic and neutral-weak response functions for 4He and 12C, using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential and Illinois-7 three-nucleon potential for strong interactions, and including one- and two-body terms in the electroweak currents. They then employed maximum-entropy (MaxEnt) techniques to invert the imaginary-time correlation functions, which carry statistical errors from the GFMC propagation, to obtain the energy-dependent response functions R(q,ω). The calculations used variational wave functions as starting points and required approximately 45 million core hours on the Mira supercomputer.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfers studied: q ≤ 0.5 GeV (stated as q ≲ 0.5 GeV) for the main calculations, with specific results shown at q = 570 MeV for 12C and q = 600 MeV for 4He
- For 4He at q = 600 MeV, the quasi-elastic peak position from the full calculation appears near ω_qe + ΔE ≈ 195 MeV (with ΔE ≈ 20 MeV for the 3+1 cluster separation energy), compared to a naive nonrelativistic estimate of q²/(2m) + ΔE ≈ 211 MeV
- The GFMC calculations used a grid of τ points uniformly distributed between 0 and 0.05 MeV⁻¹ with Δτ = 0.0005 MeV⁻¹, and approximately 2500 independent GFMC estimates for the 4He Euclidean response
- Two-body currents in the neutral-weak transverse response enhance both the vector-vector and axial-axial contributions about equally at q = 570 MeV, with the axial-axial enhancement decreasing significantly for τ ≳ 0.01 MeV⁻¹ at lower q

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous ab initio sum-rule calculations for 12C (Lovato et al., 2013, 2014) by providing the full energy-dependent response functions rather than only integrated strength, and by including 4He alongside 12C. The results contradict the conventional quasi-elastic scattering picture assumed in many neutrino-nucleus event generators and mean-field studies (Martini et al., 2009; Nieves et al., 2011; Amaro et al., 2011), which only partially account for two-body current effects. The calculations enable direct comparison with inclusive electron scattering data (Jourdan, 1996; Carlson et al., 2002) and provide predictions for neutral-current neutrino scattering that can be tested by future experiments, though parity-violating electron scattering measurements of the relevant interference response are extremely difficult due to the small factor (1 − 4 sin²θ_W).
