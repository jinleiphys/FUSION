# Thermodynamic phases and mesonic fluctuations in a chiral nucleon-meson model

> Studies of the QCD phase diagram must properly include nucleonic degrees of freedom and their thermodynamics in the range of baryon chemical potentials characteristic of nuclear matter. A useful framework for incorporating relevant nuclear physics constraints in this context is a chiral nucleon-meson effective Lagrangian. In the present paper, such a chiral nucleon-meson model is extended with systematic inclusion of mesonic fluctuations using the functional renormalization group approach. The resulting description of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition shows a remarkable agreement with three-loop calculations based on in-medium chiral effective field theory. No signs of a chiral first-order phase transition and its critical endpoint are found in the region of applicability of the model, at least up to twice the density of normal nuclear matter and at temperatures T<100 MeV. Fluctuations close to the critical point of the first-order liquid-gas transition are also examined with a detailed study of the chiral susceptibility.

## Key claim

The authors find no evidence of a chiral first-order phase transition or its critical endpoint within the model's region of applicability (up to twice normal nuclear matter density and temperatures below 100 MeV), but this conclusion is conditional on the specific parametrization of the chiral nucleon-meson model and the inclusion of mesonic fluctuations via the functional renormalization group.

## Method

The authors extend a chiral nucleon-meson effective Lagrangian, which includes nucleons, pions, sigma and omega meson fields, by incorporating mesonic fluctuations beyond the mean-field approximation using the functional renormalization group (FRG) approach with Wetterich's equation. The flow equations for the scale-dependent effective potential are solved numerically using a grid method, with the omega field treated as a background field and computed self-consistently at each momentum scale. The model parameters are readjusted to reproduce empirical nuclear matter properties at zero temperature and saturation density after fluctuations are included.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.156 fm^-3 (model) compared to 0.16 fm^-3 (empirical)
* Binding energy: B = 16.3 MeV (model) compared to 16 MeV (empirical)
* Surface tension: Sigma = 1.11 MeV/fm^2 (model) compared to 1.1 MeV/fm^2 (empirical)
* Compression modulus: K = 330 MeV (model) compared to 240 +/- 30 MeV (empirical)
* Critical temperature of liquid-gas transition: Tc = 18.3 MeV (FRG) compared to 15.1 MeV from chiral effective field theory (Fiorilla 2012)
* Chemical potential at liquid-gas transition at T=0: mu_c = 923 MeV, derived from nucleon mass m_N = 939 MeV minus binding energy B = 16 MeV
* Pion mass: m_pi = 139 MeV; pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV
* Sigma boson mass with fluctuations: m_sigma approximately 770 MeV
* Ultraviolet cutoff scale: Lambda = 1.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the mean-field study of the chiral nucleon-meson model by Floerchinger (2012) by systematically including mesonic fluctuations through the functional renormalization group. The results are compared with three-loop in-medium chiral effective field theory calculations by Fiorilla (2012), showing remarkable agreement for the liquid-gas phase transition boundary and equation of state despite the different treatment of pionic fluctuations. The study enables further investigation of the QCD phase diagram at moderate baryon chemical potentials where nucleonic degrees of freedom dominate, and suggests that the chemical freeze-out trajectory from heavy-ion collision data (Andronic 2009) is not related to a chiral phase transition at chemical potentials around 700-900 MeV.
