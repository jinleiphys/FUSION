# Angular structure of the in-medium QCD cascade

> We study the angular broadening of a medium-induced QCD cascade. We derive the equation that governs the evolution of the average transverse momentum squared of the gluons in the cascade as a function of the medium length, and we solve this equation analytically. Two regimes are identified. For a medium of a not too large size, and for not too soft gluons, the transverse momentum grows with the size of the medium according to standard momentum broadening. The other regime, visible for a medium of a sufficiently large size and very soft gluons, is a regime dominated by multiple branchings: there, the average transverse momentum saturates to a value that is independent of the size of the medium. This structure of the in-medium QCD cascade is, at least qualitatively, compatible with the recent data on dijet asymmetry.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the average transverse momentum squared of gluons in a medium-induced QCD cascade saturates to a medium-size-independent value in the regime dominated by multiple branchings (very soft gluons and/or large medium), while it follows standard momentum broadening proportional to medium length for not-too-soft gluons and not-too-large media. This saturation is a genuine property of the in-medium cascade and is qualitatively compatible with dijet asymmetry data from the LHC.

## Method

The authors derive an evolution equation for the inclusive gluon distribution function \(D(x,\mathbf{k},t)\) that combines a diffusion term for transverse momentum broadening (controlled by the jet-quenching parameter \(\hat q\)) with a branching term using the BDMPSZ splitting kernel. They then study the first two moments of this equation: the zeroth moment (energy distribution \(D(x,t)\)) and the first moment (average transverse momentum squared \(\langle k_\perp^2\rangle_{t,x}\)). The equation for the first moment is solved analytically via a convolution integral using the known exact solution for the energy distribution, and the resulting mean transverse momentum is evaluated in two limiting regimes.

## Key numbers

* For standard momentum broadening (short times, not-too-soft gluons): \(\langle k_\perp^2\rangle \approx \frac{1}{2}\hat q L\, (1+x^2)\), where \(\hat q\) is the jet-quenching parameter, \(L\) is the medium length, and \(x\) is the energy fraction.
* For the multiple-branching regime (large medium, very soft gluons): \(\langle k_\perp^2\rangle \approx k_{\rm br}^2(x)/(4\bar\alpha) = \sqrt{xE\hat q}/(4\bar\alpha)\), where \(\bar\alpha = \alpha_s N_c/\pi\) and \(E\) is the initial jet energy; this value is independent of the medium size \(L\).
* The transition between regimes occurs at \(x \sim x_s = \bar\alpha \hat q L^2/E\).
* For a medium with \(\hat q = 1\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, \(\bar\alpha = 0.3\), and \(E = 100\) GeV, the leading-particle broadening angle is \(\Delta\Theta = \sqrt{\hat qL}/E \simeq 0.01\)–\(0.02\) after traveling \(L = 4\) fm.
* For the ideal cascade (\(\omega_{\rm BH} = 0\)) with the same parameters, about 70% of the energy is contained within a cone of angle \(\Theta = 0.1\), increasing only to 75% at \(\Theta = 1\); for \(\omega_{\rm BH} = 0.5\) GeV, only 10% of the total energy is missing at \(\Theta \simeq 1\).

## Context

This work extends the probabilistic branching framework for medium-induced QCD cascades developed by Blaizot et al. (2013) and Blaizot et al. (2013), which treated multiple emissions as independent branching processes with the BDMPSZ spectrum as the elementary rate. It contrasts with an earlier study by Salgado and Wiedemann (2003) that concluded medium-induced gluon energy remains collimated. The results enable a qualitative comparison with CMS dijet asymmetry data (2014) and provide a framework for estimating the angular distribution of energy lost by jets in heavy-ion collisions, including the fraction of energy recoverable within a given cone size.
