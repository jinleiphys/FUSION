# Finite Volume Effects on the Extraction of Form Factors at Zero Momentum

> Hadronic matrix elements that depend on momentum are required for numerous phenomenological applications. Probing the low-momentum regime is often problematic for lattice QCD computations on account of the restriction to periodic momentum modes. Recently a novel method has been proposed to compute matrix elements at zero momentum, for which straightforward evaluation of the matrix elements would otherwise yield a vanishing result. We clarify an assumption underlying this method, and thereby establish the theoretical framework required to address the associated finite volume effects. Using the pion electromagnetic form factor as an example, we show how the charge radius and two higher moments can be calculated at zero momentum transfer, and determine the corresponding finite volume effects. These computations are performed using chiral perturbation theory to account for modified infrared physics, and can be generalized to ascertain finite volume effects for other hadronic matrix elements extracted at zero momentum.

## Key claim

The paper clarifies that the Taylor series expansion of correlation functions in powers of momenta, used to extract form factors at zero momentum, is mathematically justified on a finite lattice only by imposing twisted boundary conditions on the active quark, differentiating with respect to the twist angle, and evaluating at vanishing twist; the method's finite volume effects can then be assessed using partially twisted chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The author derives the zero-momentum method by imposing twisted boundary conditions on the active quarks in a lattice QCD correlation function, enabling a continuous momentum variable and a rigorous Taylor expansion. Using the pion electromagnetic form factor as an example, the charge radius and two higher moments (<r^4> and <r^6>) are expressed as ratios of momentum derivatives of the temporal vector-current three-point function, evaluated at zero momentum. Finite volume corrections to these derivatives are computed using partially twisted chiral perturbation theory, where the initial- and final-state quarks carry twist angles while the spectator and sea quarks remain periodic.

## Context

This work extends the zero-momentum method proposed by de Divitiis et al. (2012) for computing hadronic matrix elements at zero momentum transfer, which was motivated by the proton charge radius puzzle from muonic hydrogen measurements (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013). The paper addresses the finite volume effects that arise when applying this method on a finite lattice, using partially twisted boundary conditions as a theoretical framework (Bedaque 2004, de Divitiis 2004, Sachrajda 2004). The results enable lattice QCD practitioners to compute the pion charge radius and two higher moments of the charge distribution without modeling the momentum dependence of form factors, and the framework can be generalized to other hadronic matrix elements.
