# Excitation function of elastic $pp$ scattering from a unitarily extended Bialas-Bzdak model

> The Bialas-Bzdak model of elastic proton-proton scattering assumes a purely imaginary forward scattering amplitude, which consequently vanishes at the diffractive minima. We extended the model to arbitrarily large real parts in a way that constraints from unitarity are satisfied. The resulting model is able to describe elastic $pp$ scattering not only at the lower ISR energies but also at $\sqrt{s}=$7 TeV in a statistically acceptable manner, both in the diffractive cone and in the region of the first diffractive minimum. The total cross-section as well as the differential cross-section of elastic proton-proton scattering is predicted for the future LHC energies of $\sqrt{s}=$8, 13, 14, 15 TeV and also to 28 TeV. A non-trivial, significantly non-exponential feature of the differential cross-section of elastic proton-proton scattering is analyzed and the excitation function of the non-exponential behavior is predicted. The excitation function of the shadow profiles is discussed and related to saturation at small impact parameters.

## Key claim

The Bialas-Bzdak model is extended to allow arbitrarily large real parts of the forward scattering amplitude while satisfying unitarity constraints, enabling statistically acceptable descriptions of elastic proton-proton scattering at both ISR energies and LHC energy of 7 TeV, but only when the two TOTEM data sets at 7 TeV are fitted separately rather than simultaneously.

## Method

The authors generalize the Bialas-Bzdak quark-diquark model by introducing an imaginary part of the opacity function proportional to the inelastic scattering probability, which determines the real part of the forward scattering amplitude via S-matrix unitarity. The model is fitted to elastic differential cross-section data at ISR energies (23.5, 30.7, 52.8, 62.5 GeV) and at LHC energy of 7 TeV, using four free parameters: three scale parameters (R_q, R_d, R_qd) and the parameter alpha that controls the imaginary part of the opacity. At 7 TeV, the two TOTEM data sets (below and above |t_sep| = 0.375 GeV^2) are fitted separately due to systematic differences.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, the fit in the low-|t| region (0 to |t_sep|) yields chi^2/NDF = 109.9/83 with CL = 2.6% (statistically acceptable)
- At sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, the fit in the dip region (|t_sep| to 2.5 GeV^2) yields chi^2/NDF = 120.42/73 with CL = 0.04% (statistically marginal)
- The total cross-section from the low-|t| fit at 7 TeV is sigma_tot = 99.6 +/- 0.5 mb, consistent with the TOTEM measured value of 98.0 +/- 2.5 mb
- The rho parameter from the ReBB model at 7 TeV is rho = 0.103 +/- 0.001, consistent with the measured value of 0.145 +/- 0.091
- At ISR energies, the alpha parameter ranges from 0.02 to 0.04, while at 7 TeV it is 0.12
- The quark radius R_q increases from 0.27 fm at ISR energies to 0.43-0.45 fm at 7 TeV
- The diquark radius R_d increases from 0.72-0.75 fm at ISR to 0.91-0.94 fm at 7 TeV

## Context

This work extends the Bialas-Bzdak model (Bialas, 2006) and its earlier alpha-generalized version (Csorgo, 2013) by incorporating unitarity constraints explicitly, addressing the failure of the previous model to describe the dip region at 7 TeV. The model predicts differential and total cross-sections for future LHC energies (8, 13, 14, 15 TeV) and 28 TeV, and analyzes the non-exponential behavior of the differential cross-section at low |t|, related to the TOTEM observation at 8 TeV (Simone, 2014) and t-channel unitarity (Jenkovszky, 2014). The shadow profile functions show saturation at small impact parameters at LHC energies, while at ISR energies they exhibit Gaussian shapes.
