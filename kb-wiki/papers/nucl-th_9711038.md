# Light Nuclei near Neutron and Proton Drip Lines in the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory

> We have made a detailed study of the ground-state properties of nuclei in the light mass region with atomic numbers Z=10-22 in the framework of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory. The nonlinear $σω$ model with scalar self-interaction has been employed. The RMF calculations have been performed in an axially deformed configuration using the force NL-SH. We have considered nuclei about the stability line as well as those close to proton and neutron drip lines. It is shown that the RMF results provide a good agreement with the available empirical data. The RMF predictions also show a reasonably good agreement with those of the mass models. It is observed that nuclei in this mass region are found to possess strong deformations and exhibit shape changes all along the isotopic chains. The phenomenon of the shape coexistence is found to persist near the stability line as well as near the drip lines. It is shown that the magic number N=28 is quenched strongly, thus enabling the corresponding nuclei to assume strong deformations. Nuclei near the neutron and proton drip lines in this region are also shown to be strongly deformed.

# Light Nuclei near Neutron and Proton Drip Lines in the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with the NL-SH force provides a good overall description of ground-state properties for light nuclei (Z=10-22), including those near the drip lines, but shows a slight disagreement of about 1-2% with experimental binding energies for some nuclei close to the stability line, particularly for sulfur isotopes.

## Method

The authors performed axially deformed relativistic mean-field calculations using the nonlinear sigma-omega model with scalar self-interaction and the NL-SH force parameter set, solving the Dirac equation via the oscillator expansion method with 12 harmonic-oscillator shells. Pairing was included using the BCS formalism with constant pairing gaps taken from empirical particle separation energies or the Moller-Nix prescription for unknown nuclei, and the center-of-mass correction was applied using the zero-point energy of a harmonic oscillator.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for isotopic chains Z=10-22 show agreement with experimental values within 1-2% for most chains, with slightly larger deviations for sulfur isotopes
- Quadrupole deformation beta_2 values reach approximately 0.40 for prolate shapes in Ne and Mg isotopes away from N=8
- The neutron-excess (N-Z) required to produce Mg and S nuclei near the neutron drip line is about 16 and 24 respectively, approximately a factor of 4 larger than the corresponding proton-excess for proton drip line nuclei
- For nuclei near the proton drip line, the proton-excess values are only 4 for 20Mg and 6 for 26S
- The pairing gaps used for unknown nuclei follow Delta_n = 4.8/N^(1/3) and Delta_p = 4.8/Z^(1/3)

## Context

This work extends earlier RMF studies of deformed nuclei in the Sr, Kr, and Zr regions (Lalazissis and Sharma, 1995) and rare-earth chains (Lalazissis and Sharma, 1996) to the light mass region near both drip lines. The results are compared with predictions from the Finite-Range Droplet Model (FRDM) of Moller et al. (1995) and the Extended Thomas-Fermi with Strutinsky Integral (ETF-SI) of Aboussir et al. (1992), showing general agreement for binding energies but some differences in deformation predictions. The study enables systematic comparison of RMF predictions with experimental data becoming available from radioactive beam facilities for light neutron-rich nuclei such as 38,40,42S and 44,46Ar, where recent BE(2) measurements by Scheit et al. (1996) provide new deformation data.
