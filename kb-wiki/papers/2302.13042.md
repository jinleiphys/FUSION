# Effect of time-varying electromagnetic field on Wiedemann-Franz law in a hot hadronic matter

> We have estimated the electrical and thermal conductivity of a hadron resonance gas for a time-varying magnetic field, which is also compared with constant and zero magnetic field cases. Considering the exponential decay of electromagnetic fields with time, a kinetic theory framework can provide the microscopic expression of electrical and thermal conductivity in terms of relaxation and decay times. In the absence of the magnetic field, only a single time scale appears, and in the finite magnetic field case, their expressions carry two-time scales, relaxation time and cyclotron time period. Estimating the conductivities for HRG matter in three cases -- zero, constant, and time-varying magnetic fields, we have studied the validity of the Wiedemann-Franz law. We noticed that at a high-temperature domain, the ratio saturates at a particular value, which may be considered as Lorenz number of the hadron resonance gas. With respect to the saturation values, the deviation of the Wiedemann-Franz law has been quantified at the low-temperature domain. For the first time, the present work sketches this quantitative deviation of the Wiedemann-Franz law for hadron resonance gas at a constant and a time-varying magnetic field.

## Key claim

The Wiedemann-Franz law, which predicts a constant Lorenz number for the ratio of thermal to electrical conductivity, is found to be violated at low temperatures in a hadron resonance gas, but saturates to a constant value at high temperatures; this is the first quantitative study of such deviation for hadronic matter under both constant and time-varying magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors used a kinetic theory framework with the relaxation time approximation to solve the Boltzmann transport equation for a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, considering all hadron resonances from the Particle Data Group. They derived expressions for electrical and thermal conductivity tensors under three scenarios: zero magnetic field, constant magnetic field, and an exponentially time-decaying magnetic field with decay parameter tau_B. The relaxation times for hadrons were computed using thermal-averaged cross sections for 2-to-2 elastic scattering with hard-sphere radii of 0.2 fm for mesons and 0.62 fm for baryons.

## Key numbers

- For the time-varying magnetic field case with decay parameter tau_B = 9 fm, the Ohmic electrical conductivity sigma_e/T decreases with temperature, and the Hall component sigma_H/T is shown as a function of temperature.
- The constant magnetic field case uses eB = 0.03 GeV^2 for comparison with the time-varying and zero-field cases.
- The Lorenz number (ratio of thermal to electrical conductivity divided by temperature) saturates at a constant value in the high-temperature domain, with the deviation from this saturation value quantified in the low-temperature domain.
- The initial magnetic field for a typical gold-gold collision with impact parameter ~7 fm is of order ~10 m_pi^2 (where m_pi is the pion mass).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of transport coefficients in hadronic matter under magnetic fields, such as those by PhysRevD.100.114004, PhysRevD.102.016016, and PhysRevC.106.044914, which considered constant magnetic fields. It also builds on recent work by PhysRevD.106.034008 and PhysRevD.104.094037 that studied time-varying electromagnetic fields in the QGP phase using quasiparticle models. The present study enables future investigations of magneto-hydrodynamic simulations for heavy-ion collisions by providing the first quantitative deviation of the Wiedemann-Franz law for hadron resonance gas under time-varying magnetic fields.
