# Improving the Volume Dependence of Two-Body Binding Energies Calculated with Lattice QCD

> Volume modifications to the binding of two-body systems in large cubic volumes of extent L depend upon the total momentum and exponentially upon the ratio of L to the size of the boosted system. Recent work by Bour et al determined the momentum dependence of the leading volume modifications to nonrelativistic systems with periodic boundary conditions imposed on the single-particle wavefunctions, enabling them to numerically determine the scattering of such bound states using a low-energy effective field theory and Luscher's finite-volume method. The calculation of bound nuclear systems directly from QCD using Lattice QCD has begun, and it is important to reduce the systematic uncertainty introduced into such calculations by the finite spatial extent of the gauge-field configurations. We extend the work of Bour et al from nonrelativistic quantum mechanics to quantum field theory by generalizing the work of Luscher and of Gottlieb and Rummukainen to boosted two-body bound states. The volume modifications to binding energies can be exponentially reduced from ~ e^{-kappa L}/L to ~ e^{-2 kappa L}/L in nonrelativistic systems (where kappa is the binding momentum of the state) by forming particular combinations of the binding energies determined in the four lowest-lying boosted systems. Relativistic corrections to this combination, and others, that violate the exponential reduction are determined. An analysis of what can be expected from Lattice QCD calculations of the deuteron is performed, the results of which are representative of a generic loosely bound system.

# Improving the Volume Dependence of Two-Body Binding Energies Calculated with Lattice QCD

## Key claim

By forming particular linear combinations of binding energies determined in the four lowest-lying boosted two-body systems, volume modifications to binding energies can be exponentially reduced from approximately e^{-kappa L}/L to approximately e^{-2 kappa L}/L in nonrelativistic systems, though relativistic corrections to this combination violate the exponential reduction and must be accounted for.

## Method

The authors extend the nonrelativistic quantum mechanical work of Bour et al (2011) to quantum field theory by generalizing the finite-volume formalisms of Luscher (1986, 1990) and of Gottlieb and Rummukainen (1995) to boosted two-body bound states of spinless particles with s-wave interactions. They derive the asymptotic behavior of the Luscher zeta function for negative squared center-of-momentum momentum, obtaining explicit expressions for the finite-volume functions F^{(d)}(kappa L) for the four lowest-lying boost vectors |d|^2 = 0, 1, 2, 3, and then construct linear combinations of the binding momenta kappa^{(d)} that cancel the leading exponential volume corrections in the nonrelativistic limit.

## Key numbers

- For the deuteron, the infinite-volume binding energy is B = 2.224644(34) MeV, corresponding to a binding momentum kappa_0 approximately 45.70 MeV using the isospin-averaged nucleon mass M_N = 938.92 MeV.
- The deuteron scattering length is a^{(3S1)} = 5.425(1) fm and the effective range is r^{(3S1)} = 1.749(8) fm.
- For the deuteron with L greater than or approximately equal to 12 fm, the volume modification to the binding energy from the improved combinations is Delta B^{(vol)} less than or approximately equal to 20 keV, orders of magnitude smaller than in the ground state energy of the |d| = 0 system alone.
- The central values of the scattering length and effective range give a deuteron binding energy of approximately 2.212 MeV with kappa_0 approximately 45.58 MeV, within approximately 0.5 percent of the actual deuteron binding parameters.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic analysis of Bour et al (2011) to quantum field theory, building on the finite-volume formalisms of Luscher (1986, 1990) for systems at rest and of Rummukainen and Gottlieb (1995) for moving systems. The results enable Lattice QCD calculations of bound nuclear systems such as the deuteron and the H-dibaryon (observed by Beane et al in 2010 and 2011, and by Inoue et al in 2010) to achieve exponentially reduced volume systematic uncertainties using a single ensemble of gauge-field configurations, rather than requiring multiple lattice volumes for extrapolation.
