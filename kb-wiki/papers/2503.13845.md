# Investigations of symmetry breakings by multiple condensates on exotic chiral condensed phases

> The Nambu-Goldstone modes on the exiotic chiral condensed phase with chiral and tensor-type quark-antiquark condensates are investigated by using of the two-point vertex functions. It is shown that one of the Nambu-Goldstone modes appears as a result of meson-mixing. As is well known, another method to find the Nambu-Goldstone modes is given by the use of the algebraic commutation relations between broken generators and massless modes obtained through the spontaneous symmetry breaking. This method is adopted to the cases of the chiral symmetry breakings due to the tensor-type condensate and the inhomogeneous chiral condensate. The result obtained by the use of the meson two-point vertex functions is obviously reproduced in the case of the tensor-type condensate. Furthermore, we investigate the general rules for determining the broken symmetries and the Nambu-Goldstone modes algebraically. As examples, the symmetry breaking pattern and the Nambu-Goldstone modes due to the tensor-type condensate or the inhomogeneous chiral condensate are shown by adopting the general rules developed in this paper in the algebraic method.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when both the chiral condensate and the tensor-type (spin-polarized) quark-antiquark condensate coexist, one Nambu-Goldstone mode arises from meson mixing between the neutral pion and the tensor auxiliary field, rather than appearing as a simple independent mode; the algebraic commutation-relation method reproduces this result and is extended to inhomogeneous chiral condensates where translational and rotational symmetries are also broken.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with an added tensor interaction term, introducing auxiliary fields for the scalar, pseudoscalar, tensor, and pseudotensor channels via functional Gaussian integrals. They derive the effective potential and gap equations for the chiral condensate \(\sigma_0\) and the tensor condensate \(t_0\), then compute the two-point vertex functions (inverse meson propagators) at zero momentum to identify massless modes. Separately, they apply the algebraic method based on commutation relations between broken symmetry generators and the corresponding field operators to identify Nambu-Goldstone modes, and they develop a general algebraic framework for symmetry breaking by multiple condensates.

## Key numbers

* The mass matrix for the \(\pi_3\) and \(\varpi^{12}\) system at zero momentum is proportional to \(32K\) times a \(2\times2\) matrix, where \(K\) is a loop integral defined in Eq. (4.32) of the paper; the diagonalized mass matrix yields one zero eigenvalue and one eigenvalue \(32K (t_0^2 + \sigma_0^2)\).
* The gap equations for coexisting condensates are given by Eqs. (2.27) and (2.28), which set two momentum integrals equal to \(1/(48 G_S)\) and \(1/(96 G_T)\) respectively, with the integrands depending on \(\sigma_0\), \(t_0\), and the momentum components \(k_1, k_2\).
* The Nambu-Goldstone modes identified are \(\pi_1\), \(\pi_2\), \(t_1^{12}\), \(t_2^{12}\), and the linear combination \(-(\sigma_0 \pi_3 + t_0 \varpi^{12}_h)/\sqrt{t_0^2 + \sigma_0^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis by Hayashi et al. (2023) on pseudovector condensates to the case of tensor-type condensates, and it generalizes the algebraic method for determining Nambu-Goldstone modes from commutation relations. The results for the inhomogeneous chiral condensate reproduce the findings of Low and Manohar (2002) and show the coupling of internal and translational symmetries as discussed by Kobayashi and Nitta (2014). The counting rule for Nambu-Goldstone modes follows the criterion of Watanabe and Murayama (2012), and the dispersion law follows Nambu (1960).
