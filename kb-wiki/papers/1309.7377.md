# Flavor Oscillation Modes In Dense Neutrino Media

> We study two-flavor neutrino oscillations in homogeneous neutrino gases in which neutrinos and anti-neutrinos are in nearly pure weak interaction states initially. We find that the monopole and dipole oscillation modes can trigger flavor instabilities in the opposite neutrino mass hierarchies in a nearly isotropic neutrino gas. For a class of simple neutrino systems we are able to identify the normal modes of neutrino oscillations in the linear regime. Our results provide new insights into the recently discovered multi-azimuthal angle (MAA) instability of neutrino oscillations in supernovae.

# Flavor Oscillation Modes In Dense Neutrino Media

## Key claim
The monopole and dipole oscillation modes can trigger flavor instabilities in opposite neutrino mass hierarchies in a nearly isotropic neutrino gas, but only in the linear regime where perturbations remain small; all higher multipole modes are stable in this regime. The results provide new insights into the multi-azimuthal angle (MAA) instability of neutrino oscillations in supernovae, but the author notes that the generalized bulb model used in prior work is not entirely self-consistent because MAA modes break axial symmetry.

## Method
The author studies two-flavor neutrino oscillations in homogeneous neutrino gases using the neutrino flavor isospin formalism, where neutrinos and anti-neutrinos are initially in nearly pure weak interaction states. Linear stability analysis is performed on the equations of motion for three specific monochromatic, symmetric, bipolar systems: a two-beam system (two colliding beams), an axial ring of beams, and an isotropic-flux system. The normal modes are identified by decomposing the flavor isospin perturbations into multipole components (spherical harmonics for isotropic systems, Fourier modes for axial systems) and solving the decoupled linearized equations for each mode.

## Key numbers
- For the two-beam system, the plus mode is unstable only in the inverted hierarchy (IH) when the dimensionless interaction strength μ > 1, with exponential growth rate κ = √(μ - 1).
- For the two-beam system, the minus mode is unstable only in the normal hierarchy (NH) when μ > 1.
- For the axial-ring system, the m = 0 mode behaves like the plus mode with effective coupling μ, while the m = ±1 modes behave like the minus mode with effective coupling -μ/2.
- For the isotropic-flux system, the monopole mode (l = 0) has effective coupling μ, and the dipole modes (l = 1) have effective coupling -μ/3.
- For a non-symmetric bipolar gas with anti-neutrino fraction α, the monopole mode is unstable in IH when 4/(1+√α)² < μ < 4/(1-√α)².
- For the same non-symmetric system, the dipole modes are unstable in NH when 12/(1+√α)² < μ < 12/(1-√α)².

## Context
This work extends the linear stability analysis of collective neutrino oscillations previously applied to homogeneous neutrino gases (Banerjee 2011, Sarikas 2012, Vaananen 2013) and provides a mathematical framework for understanding the multi-azimuthal angle (MAA) instability discovered by Raffelt (2013) in supernova neutrino models. The author shows that the bipolar/bimodal mode and multi-zenith-angle (MZA) mode have m = 0, while the MAA modes have m = ±1, and that these modes are unstable in opposite mass hierarchies. This enables future work to go beyond the spherical supernova model when azimuthal-angle-dependent collective oscillations become important, as the spherical symmetry breaks down once axial symmetry about any radial direction is lost.
