# Kinetic energy and spin-orbit splitting in nuclei near neutron drip line

> Two important ingredients of nuclear shell-structure, kinetic energy and spin-orbit splitting, are studied as a function of orbital angular momenta \ell and binding energies, when binding energies of neutrons decrease towards zero. If we use the standard parameters of the Woods-Saxon potential in βstable nuclei and approach the limit of zero binding energy from 10 MeV, the spin-orbit splitting for n=1 orbitals decreases considerably for \ell=1, while for \ell > 2 little decreasing is observed in the limit. In contrast, the kinetic energy decreases considerably for \ell \simleq 3. The smaller the \ell values of orbitals, the larger the decreasing rate of both kinetic energy and spin-orbit splitting. The dependence of the above bservation on the diffuseness of potentials is studied.

## Key claim

The kinetic energy and spin-orbit splitting of neutron orbitals decrease as binding energies approach zero, but the rate of decrease depends strongly on orbital angular momentum and potential diffuseness; for Woods-Saxon potentials with standard parameters, the spin-orbit splitting for n=1 orbitals decreases considerably only for ℓ=1, while for ℓ>2 little decrease is observed, whereas kinetic energy decreases considerably for ℓ≲3.

## Method

The authors computed expectation values of operators proportional to kinetic energy (r dU/dr) and spin-orbit splitting (1/r dU/dr) for neutron orbitals in Woods-Saxon potentials with standard parameters (r₀=1.27 fm, a=0.67 fm) and with doubled diffuseness (a=1.34 fm), as functions of the one-particle energy eigenvalue Eₙℓ from -10 MeV to 0 MeV. They compared these results with those for finite and infinite square-well potentials, for orbitals 1p, 2s, 1d, 2p, and 1f, without including the spin-orbit potential in the one-body potential itself.

## Key numbers

- For the standard Woods-Saxon potential (a=0.67 fm), the spin-orbit splitting for 1p orbitals decreases considerably as Eₙℓ goes from -5 to 0 MeV; for 1d orbitals little decrease is observed in the same range.
- For the Woods-Saxon potential with doubled diffuseness (a=1.34 fm), the spin-orbit splitting of 1d orbitals also decreases appreciably as Eₙℓ changes from -10 to 0 MeV.
- For the square-well potential at Eₙℓ=0, the expectation value of (r/U₀)(dU/dr) (proportional to kinetic energy) is 2/3 for ℓ=1, 6/5 for ℓ=2, and 10/7 for ℓ=3, independent of radial node n.
- For Woods-Saxon potentials at Eₙℓ=0, the same expectation value is almost a factor two smaller for a=0.67 fm and a factor four smaller for a=1.34 fm compared to the square-well values.
- The crossing of 1d₅/₂ and 2s₁/₂ eigenvalues occurs at Eₙℓ=-0.71 MeV for a=0.67 fm and at Eₙℓ=-7.21 MeV for a=1.34 fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of shell-structure variation near the neutron drip line beyond self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations (e.g., Skyrme-type HF by Hamamoto, Sagawa, and Zhang (1996) and by Hamamoto and Sagawa (1999)) and relativistic mean-field studies (e.g., Lalazissis, Vretenar, and Ring (1998)). It provides systematic single-particle matrix elements for kinetic energy and spin-orbit splitting as functions of binding energy and orbital angular momentum, which are needed to improve input parameters for shell-model calculations of neutron-rich nuclei, following the suggestion of Karataglidis and Satchler (1993) on isospin dependence of kinetic energies.
