# Particle multiplicities and fluctuations in 200 GeV Au-Au collisions

> We use the statistical hadronization model (SHM) to describe hadron multiplicity yields and fluctuations. We consider 200 GeV Au-Au collisions, and show that both event averaged yields of stable particles and resonances, and event-by-event fluctuation of the $K/π$ ratio can be described within the SHM using the same set of thermal parameters, provided that the phase space occupancy parameter value is significantly above chemical equilibrium, and the freeze-out temperature is $\sim 140$ MeV. We present predictions that allow to test the consistency of our results.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model (SHM) can describe both event-averaged yields and event-by-event fluctuations of the K/π ratio in 200 GeV Au-Au collisions using the same thermal parameters, but only if the phase space occupancy parameter γ_q is significantly above chemical equilibrium and the freeze-out temperature is approximately 140 MeV, consistent with a supercooled, sudden hadronization scenario.

## Method

The authors used the Grand Canonical ensemble implementation of the SHM (via the open-source SHARE software) to compute first and second cumulants of particle yields and fluctuations, fitting to STAR experimental data including particle yields, resonance abundances (K*(892), Λ(1520)), and the dynamical fluctuation σ^{dyn}_{K/π}. The fit parameters included freeze-out temperature T, chemical fugacities λ_q, λ_s, λ_{I3}, and phase space occupancy parameters γ_q and γ_s, with constraints enforcing strangeness neutrality and the Au-Au charge-to-baryon ratio.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature T ≈ 140 MeV from the best fit allowing chemical nonequilibrium
- Light quark phase space occupancy γ_q ≈ 1.6 from the best fit (significantly above the equilibrium value of 1)
- Strange quark phase space occupancy γ_s ≈ 2 from the best fit
- The chemical equilibrium fit (γ_q = 1, T = 155 MeV) misses the measured σ^{dyn}_{K/π} by many standard deviations
- The isospin chemical potential λ_{I3} ≈ 0.96 was required to reproduce the difference between σ^{dyn}_{K+/π+} and σ^{dyn}_{K-/π-}
- The mid-rapidity slice observed by RHIC experiments comprises roughly 1/8 of the total hadron multiplicity

## Context

This work extends the application of the statistical hadronization model from describing only average particle yields (as done extensively at SPS and RHIC energies by Rafelski and collaborators) to also describing event-by-event fluctuations, which had previously been studied theoretically by Jeon and Koch and experimentally as a search for new physics. The authors argue that comparing σ^{dyn}_{K/π} fluctuations to directly measured K*(892)/K- ratios provides a decisive test of the sudden hadronization picture versus equilibrium models where chemical and thermal freeze-out are separated, and they present predictions for resonances (ρ^0, f_0(950), Δ^{++}, Σ*(1385), Ξ*(1530)) and fluctuations of their decay product ratios to enable further experimental tests.
