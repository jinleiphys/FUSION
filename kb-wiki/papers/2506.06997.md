# Dense matter in a holographic hard-wall model of QCD

> A deeper understanding of QCD matter at strong coupling remains challenging due to its non-perturbative nature. To this end, we study a two-flavor holographic hard-wall model to investigate the properties of QCD at finite-density and zero temperature with a nonvanishing quark mass. A dense matter phase is described by a classical solution of the equations of motion in a homogeneous Ansatz. We apply holographic renormalization to formulate the holographic dictionary that relates UV boundary data in the bulk with the physical quantities in QCD. We emphasize a role played by an IR boundary action on the hard-wall when analyzing the QCD phase structures in this holographic setup. It is found that a baryonic matter phase is manifested in this model with a high baryon number density and a nearly vanishing chiral condensate. We derive the equation of state for the resulting phase and use it to work out the mass-radius relation for neutron stars. We find that the maximum mass of neutron stars can exceed two solar masses for a wide range of free parameters in this model. We also comment on an alternative scenario about the phase structure such that the baryonic matter phase arises at a baryon number chemical potential greater than a critical value.

## Key claim
The holographic hard-wall model with two-flavor massive quarks produces a baryonic matter phase with high baryon number density and nearly vanishing chiral condensate, and the resulting equation of state yields neutron star maximum masses exceeding two solar masses for a wide range of free parameters, though the results depend on the choice of IR boundary conditions and the parameters \(B\), \(L\), and \(k_2\) which lack direct experimental constraints.

## Method
The authors study a two-flavor holographic hard-wall model of QCD at finite density and zero temperature using a homogeneous ansatz for the bulk gauge fields, with a nonvanishing quark mass \(m = 3\ \mathrm{MeV}\). They apply holographic renormalization to formulate the GKP-W dictionary relating UV boundary data to QCD quantities, and impose IR boundary conditions (Neumann or Dirichlet) on the fields \(\hat{a}_0\), \(H\), and \(\omega_0\) at the hard-wall cutoff \(z = z_{\mathrm{IR}}\) to define distinct QCD phases. The equations of motion are solved numerically via the shooting method, and the grand potential is computed from the renormalized on-shell action to determine the energetically favored phase as a function of baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- Quark mass fixed to \(m = 3\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (up and down quark masses).
- Chiral condensate input: \(\xi_0 = (251\ \mathrm{MeV})^3\) from lattice calculations (JLQCD, 2007).
- Two choices for the AdS radius inverse: \(L^{-1} = 323\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (best fit to meson sector from Erlich (2005)) and \(L^{-1} = 170\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (reproduces critical baryon density \(n_0 = 0.17\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) at \(\mu_B = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\)).
- IR boundary value for \(\hat{a}_0\) set to \(A = 4\) in units of \(L^{-1}\).
- Sample values of \(H\) at the IR boundary: \(B = 0.4/L,\ 0.6/L,\ 0.8/L\).
- Maximum neutron star mass can exceed two solar masses for a wide range of free parameters.

## Context
This work extends the holographic hard-wall model approach of Bartolini (2022), which studied finite-temperature and finite-density QCD with massless quarks using the homogeneous ansatz first proposed by Rozali (2007), by incorporating massive quarks and a detailed treatment of IR boundary conditions. The results enable further studies of neutron star structure using holographic equations of state, complementing lattice QCD (which suffers from the sign problem at high density) and perturbative QCD (which is inapplicable at strong coupling). The model also connects to earlier holographic neutron star studies by de Boer (2009), Hoyos (2016), and Kovensky (2021).
