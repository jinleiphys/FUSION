# Thermodynamics of the parity-doublet model: Asymmetric and neutron matter

> We consider isospin-asymmetric matter in the parity-doublet model within an extended mean-field calculation, increasing continuously the neutron excess all the way to pure neutron matter. We compute the liquid-gas and the chiral phase transitions occurring at zero to moderate temperatures, but put special emphasis on the phase structure of matter at zero temperature and large baryon densities. The calculation of the free energy involves the solution of gap equations. This is achieved by transforming these gap equations into ordinary differential equations that control the flow with increasing baryon density of various physical quantities: the isoscalar condensate, the densities of protons and neutrons, as well as those of their respective chiral partners. In this formulation, the initial conditions for the differential equations determine the entire phase structure. It is further demonstrated that the threshold for the onset of the population of the chiral partners is exclusively determined by the fermionic parameters, most notably by the chiral-invariant mass of the nucleon. We underline the role of a parity symmetry energy in driving the equilibration of the nucleons and their parity partners across the chiral transition. We provide a detailed analysis of the changes in the matter properties as one varies the neutron excess, including a special discussion of the chiral limit, and we compare systematically the parity-doublet model to its corresponding singlet model, where the chiral partner of the nucleon is neglected. Finally, we focus on neutron matter and compute the equation of state and the speed of sound. The results are confronted to those of other calculations as well as to recent Bayesian analyses of neutron-star observations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the parity-doublet model, the threshold for the onset of the population of chiral partners (negative-parity baryons) is exclusively determined by the fermionic parameters, most notably by the chiral-invariant mass of the nucleon \(m_0\), and that a "parity symmetry energy" drives the equilibration of nucleons and their parity partners across the chiral transition, with the entire phase structure determined by the initial conditions of differential equations that replace the gap equations.

## Method

The authors perform an extended mean-field calculation of isospin-asymmetric matter in the parity-doublet model, treating meson fields (sigma, omega, and rho mesons) as classical fields while keeping fermion fluctuations to one-loop order. They transform the gap equations into ordinary differential equations that control the flow of the isoscalar condensate, the densities of protons and neutrons, and those of their respective chiral partners as functions of increasing baryon density. The model parameters are kept from a previous study of symmetric matter (Eser 2023), except the rho-meson coupling constant which is adjusted to reproduce the empirical symmetry energy of nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The chiral-invariant mass \(m_0\) is the mass at which the parity doublet members become degenerate when the sigma field vanishes; the positive-parity nucleon mass \(M_+\) exhibits a minimum at \(\sigma_{\rm min}^2 = m_0^2 (y_a - y_b)^2 / [y_a y_b (y_a + y_b)^2]\)
- The negative-parity baryon is identified with the \(N^*(1535)\) with a mass of 1510 MeV (ParticleDataGroup 2022)
- The pion decay constant in vacuum is \(f_\pi\), and the explicit symmetry breaking term is \(h = m_\pi^2 f_\pi\) for the physical pion mass \(m_\pi\)
- The vector meson coupling constants are defined as \(G_v \equiv g_v^2 / m_v^2\) and \(G_w \equiv g_w^2 / m_v^2\), with the same vector meson mass \(m_v\) for both omega and rho fields
- The sigma meson mass is 340 MeV for the doublet model and 640 MeV for the singlet model

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of the parity-doublet model for symmetric matter (Eser 2023) to isospin-asymmetric matter and pure neutron matter, building on the mirror assignment framework of Detar and Kunihiro (1988) and Jido et al. (2000). The results are compared to the singlet model (which neglects the chiral partner of the nucleon) and to calculations from Brandes et al. (2021) and Alford et al. (2022), as well as to recent Bayesian analyses of neutron-star observations. The study enables future confrontation of the parity-doublet model predictions with neutron-star data, particularly regarding the speed of sound and equation of state at high baryon densities.
