# Single- and two-nucleon momentum distributions for local chiral interactions

> We present quantum Monte Carlo calculations of the single- and two-nucleon momentum distributions in selected nuclei for $A\le16$. We employ local chiral interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order. We find good agreement at low momentum with the single-nucleon momentum distributions derived for phenomenological potentials. The same agreement is found for the integrated two-nucleon momentum distributions at low relative momentum $q$ and low center-of-mass momentum $Q$. We provide results for the two-nucleon momentum distributions as a function of both $q$ and $Q$. The large ratio of $pn$ to $pp$ pairs around $q=2\,\rm fm^{-1}$ for back-to-back $(Q=0)$ pairs is confirmed up to $^{16}$O, and results are compatible with those extracted from available experimental data.

## Key claim

Quantum Monte Carlo calculations using local chiral interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) reproduce the low-momentum behavior of single- and two-nucleon momentum distributions found with phenomenological potentials, but the high-momentum tails are systematically reduced because chiral EFT interactions have different short-range physics than phenomenological potentials like AV18+UIX.

## Method

The authors performed variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations of single- and two-nucleon momentum distributions in 4He, 12C, and 16O using trial wave functions optimized for local chiral interactions at N2LO with coordinate-space cutoffs R0 = 1.0 fm and 1.2 fm. The momentum distributions were computed by Metropolis Monte Carlo integration with Gauss-Legendre quadrature over the relative coordinate x, using symmetrized shifts of particle positions in the left- and right-hand wave functions to reduce statistical errors. For 12C, a reduced set of 13 Slater determinants was used instead of the full 119-determinant p-shell wave function to make the calculation computationally feasible.

## Key numbers

- At q = 2 fm^-1 for back-to-back (Q = 0) pairs, the ratio of pn to pp pairs is greater than or equal to 20 in 4He and greater than or equal to 10 in 12C and 16O
- At k = 2 fm^-1, the integrated strength of the proton momentum distribution for chiral interactions is 95.1(1)% in 4He, 96.3(4)% in 12C, and 97.0(4)% in 16O
- At q = 2 fm^-1, 97.3(2)% of pn pairs and 98.6(1)% of pp pairs are accounted for in 4He; for 12C these values are 98.8(7)% for pn and 99.1(3)% for pp; for 16O they are 99.1(8)% for pn and 99.4(4)% for pp
- VMC charge radii for the Eτ potential with R0 = 1.0 fm are 1.67(1) fm for 4He, 2.48(2) fm for 12C, and 2.77(3) fm for 16O
- Local chiral interactions produce approximately 20% to 35% less kinetic energy than phenomenological potentials
- In 16O, AFDMC results for the proton momentum distribution are approximately 35% higher than VMC results in the high-momentum region (k greater than or equal to 2 fm^-1)

## Context

This work extends previous VMC calculations of momentum distributions using phenomenological AV18+UIX potentials in A ≤ 12 nuclei (Schiavilla 1986, Wiringa 2014) and cluster VMC calculations in 16O and 40Ca (Alvioli 2005, Lonardoni 2017) to the framework of local chiral effective field theory interactions. The results confirm the large pn-to-pp pair ratio around q = 2 fm^-1 for back-to-back pairs observed experimentally in 4He (Korover 2014) and 12C (Subedi 2008), and show that this ratio is nearly independent of the choice of local chiral interaction. The calculations enable future studies of how two-nucleon currents impact back-to-back exclusive measurements in electron scattering, which are known to provide 30-40% constructive interference in inclusive transverse quasielastic scattering (Lovato 2016).
