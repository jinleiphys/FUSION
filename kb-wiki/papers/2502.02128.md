# Emission processes in a self-consistent field

> We present a microscopic description of cluster emission processes within the Cluster--Hartree--Fock (CHF) self--consistent field (SCF) theory. The starting point is a Woods--Saxon (WS) mean field (MF) with spin--orbit and Coulomb terms. Pairing is treated through standard Bardeen--Cooper--Schrieffer (BCS) quasiparticles. The residual two--body interaction is given by a density--dependent Wigner force having a Gaussian shape with a center of mass (com) correction located in a region of low nuclear density slightly beyond the geometrical contact radius of a system comprised from a nucleus and a surface cluster. We show that such a description adequately reproduces the ground state (gs) shape of a spherical nucleus while the surface correction enhances the radial tail of single particle orbitals, thus allowing for an adequate description of the $α$-decay width for unstable systems.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a self-consistent Cluster-Hartree-Fock (CHF) procedure, using a density-dependent Wigner force with a Gaussian shape and a surface correction, can simultaneously reproduce the ground state shape of a spherical nucleus and the alpha-decay width for unstable systems, but only when the surface correction is tuned to reproduce the experimental decay width and the plateau condition is satisfied over a narrow radial range.

## Method

The authors start from a Woods-Saxon mean field with spin-orbit and Coulomb terms, treat pairing via standard BCS quasiparticles, and introduce a residual two-body interaction of the Wigner type with a Gaussian shape and a center-of-mass correction located beyond the geometrical contact radius. They solve the CHF equations in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with 12 major shells, iterating the self-consistent field until convergence, and match the alpha-particle formation amplitude to an outgoing Coulomb wave to compute the decay width for the process Rn-216 to Po-212 plus alpha.

## Key numbers

- The decay width for the process Rn-216 to Po-212 plus alpha is given as Gamma_0 = 1.0 x 10^-17 MeV.
- The geometrical touching radius for the system is R_0 = 9.06 fm, with r_0 = 1.2 fm.
- The neutron skin thickness of Rn-216 from the self-consistent calculation is delta r_np = 0.34 fm, compared to 0.55 fm from the mean-field approximation.
- The spectroscopic factor (probability of the parent ground state being configured as a daughter core plus alpha-particle) is slightly over 3%.
- The harmonic oscillator basis uses N = 12 major shells, allowing wave function calculation up to approximately 13 fm.
- The surface Gaussian interaction parameters for protons are: interaction constant = 41.713 MeV, b = 1.3 fm, B = 1 fm, r_0 = 1.26 fm, x_c = 11.3; for neutrons: interaction constant = 241.467 MeV, b = 0.6 fm, B = 1 fm, r_0 = 1.30 fm, x_c = 16.7.

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent calculations by Delion (2013) and Dumitrescu (2023) by presenting a full CHF procedure with a surface Gaussian interaction that avoids the complicated cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method. The approach enables systematic investigation of alpha-clustering in deformed nuclei and provides a framework for predicting decay properties of superheavy or exotic nuclei, building on the R-matrix theory of Lane and Thomas (1958) and the phenomenological linear relationship between reduced decay width and fragmentation potential established by Delion (2009).
