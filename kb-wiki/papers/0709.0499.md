# Scattering Models for Ultracold Atoms

> We present a review of scattering models that can be used to describe the low-energy behavior of identical bosonic atoms. In the simplest models, the only degrees of freedom are atoms in the same spin state. More elaborate models have other degrees of freedom, such as atoms in other spin states or diatomic molecules. The parameters of the scattering models are specified by giving the S-wave phase shifts for scattering of atoms in the spin state of primary interest. The models are formulated as local quantum field theories and the renormalization of their coupling constants is determined. Some of the parameters can be constrained by renormalizability or by the absence of negative-norm states. The Green's functions that describe the evolution of two-atom states are determined analytically. They are used to determine the T-matrix elements for atom-atom scattering and the binding energies of diatomic molecules. The scattering models all exhibit universal behavior as the scattering length in a specific spin state becomes large.

## Key claim

This review paper systematically presents scattering models for ultracold identical bosonic atoms, showing that all such models exhibit universal behavior when the scattering length in a specific spin state becomes large, but the universality is restricted to energies much smaller than the van der Waals energy scale (approximately 6.4 × 10⁻⁹ eV for rubidium atoms).

## Method

The authors formulate scattering models as local quantum field theories with an ultraviolet cutoff, specifying the physical parameters by giving the S-wave phase shifts for the spin state of primary interest. They determine the renormalization of coupling constants, derive analytic Green's functions for two-atom states, and use these to compute T-matrix elements for atom-atom scattering and binding energies of diatomic molecules. The models considered include the Zero-Range Model (with only the scattering length as a parameter), the Effective Range Model (adding a second interaction parameter), the Two-Channel Model (atoms in two spin states), and the Resonance Model (including a diatomic molecule degree of freedom).

## Key numbers

- For rubidium-87 atoms: hyperfine splitting E_hf = 2.83 × 10⁻⁵ eV; van der Waals energy scale E_vdW = 6.4 × 10⁻⁹ eV (corresponding to 7.4 × 10⁻⁵ K); van der Waals length ℓ_vdW defined by (m C_6 / ℏ²)^{1/4}
- For alkali atoms: first electronic excitation energy scale ≈ E_Rydberg/n², where E_Rydberg = 13.6 eV and n = 1,2,3,4,5,6,7 for H, Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, Fr respectively
- For a large positive scattering length a, the shallow dimer binding energy is E_D = 1/(m a²)
- Examples of large scattering lengths at zero magnetic field: ⁶Li (a_t ≈ -2160 a₀), ⁸⁵Rb (a_s ≈ +2800 a₀), ¹³³Cs (a_t ≈ +2400 a₀)
- Feshbach resonance examples: 853 G and 907 G for ²³Na atoms in the |1, -1⟩ hyperfine level; 155 G for ⁸⁵Rb atoms in the |2, -2⟩ hyperfine level

## Context

This review extends the treatment of scattering models for ultracold atoms beyond the simple Zero-Range Model, providing a unified quantum field theory framework that includes the Effective Range Model, Two-Channel Model, and Resonance Model. The work builds upon the fundamental theory of alkali atoms with Born-Oppenheimer potentials and hyperfine structure described in the paper, and it references the universal properties review by Braaten (2004) and the Feshbach resonance review by Koehler, Goral, and Julienne (2006). The paper enables systematic construction of simplified models that accurately reproduce low-energy observables, particularly for systems near Feshbach resonances where the scattering length becomes large.
