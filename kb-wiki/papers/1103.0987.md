# Nuclear effects in neutral current quasi-elastic neutrino interactions

> The interpretation of the charged (CCQE) and neutral (NCE) current quasi elastic events collected by the MiniBooNE collaboration involves a number of unresolved issues. While it has been suggested that the data can be explained in terms of an effective nucleon axial mass, $M_A$, the results of our theoretical calculations suggest that the CCQE and NCE samples cannot be described by the same value of $M_A$. We argue that the disagreement between theory and data may arise from the uncertainties associated with the flux average procedure. We also analyze the role of the strange quark in NCE interactions and find that, due to a cancellation between proton and neutron contributions, it turns out to be negligible.

## Key claim

The CCQE and NCE data samples collected by MiniBooNE cannot be described by the same value of the effective nucleon axial mass \(M_A\), and the disagreement between theory and data may arise from uncertainties associated with the flux average procedure rather than from nuclear effects alone.

## Method

The authors computed the flux-averaged \(Q^2\)-distribution for neutral current quasi-elastic neutrino interactions on a Carbon target using the impulse approximation and a realistic nuclear spectral function obtained within the Local Density Approximation, which combines information from \((e,e^\prime p)\) measurements with theoretical calculations of uniform nuclear matter. The hadronic neutral current was parametrized using vector form factors from electron scattering and dipole axial form factors, with the strange quark contribution \(F_A^s\) expressed in terms of \(\Delta s\) and \(M_A\). Pauli blocking was included by modifying the spectral function with a step function using an average Fermi momentum of 225 MeV for Carbon.

## Key numbers

- The average Fermi momentum for Carbon is \(\bar{p}_F = 225\) MeV, derived from the nuclear density profile.
- The value of the axial mass yielding a good fit of the MiniBooNE CCQE distribution is \(M_A = 1.6\) GeV, but this value does not reproduce the NCE data.
- The world average axial mass from deuterium data is \(M_A = 1.03\) GeV; the RFG model requires \(M_A \gsim 1.35\) GeV to match MiniBooNE data.
- The difference between the \(Q^2\)-distributions obtained from the RFG model and the spectral function approach for CCQE and NCE does not exceed 2% over the whole \(Q^2\) range.
- The lowest value of \(\Delta s\) found in the literature is \(\Delta s = -0.19\); the NCE \(Q^2\)-distribution is nearly independent of \(\Delta s\) for a Carbon target.

## Context

This work extends the spectral function approach previously applied to CCQE interactions by Benhar and collaborators (2010) to the NCE channel, contradicting the suggestion that nuclear effects can be absorbed into an effective axial mass as used in the RFG model by the MiniBooNE collaboration (BooNECCQE, BooNENCE). The results enable future studies to focus on flux average uncertainties and multi-nucleon knockout contributions involving two-body currents, as proposed by Barbaro (2010), rather than on strange quark effects for nuclei with equal numbers of protons and neutrons.
