# In-medium electron-nucleon scattering

> In-medium nucleon electromagnetic form factors are calculated in the quark meson coupling model. The form factors are typically found to be suppressed as the density increases. For example, at normal nuclear density and $Q^2 \sim 0.3 { GeV}^2$, the nucleon electric form factors are reduced by approximately 8% while the magnetic form factors are reduced by only 1 - 2%. These variations are consistent with current experimental limits but should be tested by more precise experiments in the near future.

# In-medium electron-nucleon scattering

## Key claim
In the quark meson coupling (QMC) model, in-medium nucleon electromagnetic form factors are suppressed with increasing nuclear density, but the predicted changes are consistent with current experimental limits from y-scaling and the Coulomb sum-rule. The model predicts that electric form factors are significantly more sensitive to density than magnetic form factors, with the latter being nearly one order of magnitude less sensitive.

## Method
The authors use the quark meson coupling (QMC) model, where scalar (sigma) and vector (omega) meson fields couple directly to quarks confined in MIT bags, rather than to point nucleons as in Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD). The in-medium nucleon form factors are calculated in the Breit frame using the Peierls-Thouless projection method for center-of-mass corrections, together with Lorentz contraction of the bag, and include pion cloud corrections up to one-loop order within the cloudy bag model (CBM). The mean scalar and vector fields are determined self-consistently from the coupled equations of motion for symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers
- At normal nuclear density (rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3) and Q^2 ~ 0.3 GeV^2, the proton and neutron electric form factors are reduced by approximately 8% relative to free space.
- At the same density and Q^2, the proton magnetic form factor is reduced by 1.5% and the neutron magnetic form factor by 0.9%.
- At half nuclear density (0.5 rho_0) and Q^2 ~ 0.3 GeV^2, the proton and neutron charge form factors are reduced by roughly 5% and 6%, respectively.
- At half nuclear density, the proton and neutron magnetic form factors are 1% and 0.6% smaller than in free space.
- The calculated increase in the root-mean-square radius of the magnetic form factors is less than 0.8% at normal nuclear density.
- The free nucleon mass is taken as 939 MeV, and the free-space bag radius is chosen as R = 1 fm.
- The pi NN coupling constant used is f^NN ~ 3.03, corresponding to f^2_piNN ~ 0.081.

## Context
This work extends the quark meson coupling model of Guichon (1988), Saito and Thomas (1994), and Guichon, Saito, Rodionov, and Thomas (1996) to calculate density-dependent electromagnetic form factors, representing the first quantitative density-dependent study within a quark model that reproduces the saturation energy, density, and compressibility of nuclear matter. The results are consistent with experimental constraints from y-scaling analysis by Sick (1986) and the Coulomb sum-rule analysis by Jourdan (1995), which limit magnetic radius variations to less than 3% and electric form factor variations to less than 4%, respectively. The predicted variations motivate forthcoming quasielastic electron-nucleus scattering experiments at facilities such as Mainz, MIT-Bates, and TJNAF.
