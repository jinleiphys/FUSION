# Complete fusion of $^9$Be with spherical targets

> The complete fusion of $^9$Be with $^{144}$Sm and $^{208}$Pb targets is calculated in the coupled-channels approach. The calculations include couplings between the 3/2$^-$, 5/2$^-$, and 7/2$^-$ states in the $K=3/2$ ground state rotational band of $^9$Be. It is shown that the $B(E2)$ values for the excitation of these states are accurately described in the rotor model. The interaction of the strongly deformed $^9$Be nucleus with a spherical target is calculated using the double-folding technique and the effective M3Y interaction, which is supplemented with a repulsive term that is adjusted to optimize the fit to the data for the $^{144}$Sm target. The complete fusion is described by in-going-wave boundary conditions. The decay of the unbound excited states in $^9$Be is considered explicitly in the calculations by using complex excitation energies. The model gives an excellent account of the complete fusion (CF) data for $^9$Be+$^{144}$Sm, and the cross sections for the decay of the excited states are in surprisingly good agreement with the incomplete fusion (ICF) data. Similar calculations for $^9$Be+$^{208}$Pb explain the total fusion data at high energies but fail to explain the CF data, which are suppressed by 20%, and the calculated cross section for the decay of excited states is a factor of three smaller than the ICF data at high energies. Possible reasons for these discrepancies are discussed.

# Complete fusion of $^9$Be with spherical targets

## Key claim

The coupled-channels model, which includes couplings between the 3/2$^-$, 5/2$^-$, and 7/2$^-$ states in the $K=3/2$ ground state rotational band of $^9$Be and treats the decay of unbound excited states via complex excitation energies, gives an excellent account of complete fusion data for $^9$Be+$^{144}$Sm but fails to explain the complete fusion data for $^9$Be+$^{208}$Pb, which are suppressed by 20% relative to the calculation, while the calculated cross section for decay of excited states is a factor of three smaller than the incomplete fusion data at high energies for the lead target.

## Method

The double-folding technique with the effective M3Y interaction, supplemented by a repulsive contact term adjusted to optimize the fit to $^{144}$Sm target data, was used to calculate the Coulomb plus nuclear interaction between the deformed $^9$Be nucleus and spherical targets. Coupled-channels calculations were performed in the rotating frame approximation with ingoing-wave boundary conditions imposed at the minimum of the entrance channel potential pocket, and the decay of unbound excited states in $^9$Be was included explicitly by using complex excitation energies $E_I - i\Gamma_I/2$.

## Key numbers

- Intrinsic quadrupole moment of $^9$Be: $Q_0 = 26.45(20)$ fm$^2$, derived from the measured ground state quadrupole moment of 5.29(4) fm$^2$
- Deformation parameter for $^9$Be density: $\beta_2 = 1.183$, with radius $R = 2.08$ fm and diffuseness $a = 0.375$ fm, calibrated to reproduce both $Q_0$ and the RMS charge radius of 2.52(1) fm
- Excitation energies and widths of the $K=3/2$ rotational band: $E(5/2^-) = 2.429$ MeV with $\Gamma = 0.78$ keV; $E(7/2^-) = 6.38$ MeV with $\Gamma = 1210$ keV
- Measured $B(E2)$ values for transitions from the $3/2^-$ ground state: $B(E2, 3/2^- \rightarrow 5/2^-) = 40.7(6)$ $e^2$fm$^4$; $B(E2, 3/2^- \rightarrow 7/2^-) = 18.9(3)$ $e^2$fm$^4$; sum = 73.6(11) $e^2$fm$^4$
- Best-fit repulsive strength for the effective $NN$ interaction: $v_r \approx 410$ MeV fm$^3$, determined from $\chi^2$ minimization against $^9$Be+$^{144}$Sm data
- For $^9$Be+$^{208}$Pb: complete fusion data suppressed by 20% compared to calculation at high energies; calculated absorption cross section is a factor of three smaller than measured incomplete fusion data at high energies
- For $^9$Be+$^{144}$Sm: complete fusion suppressed by about 10% compared to total fusion at high energies, consistent between experiment and calculation
- Neutron skin thickness adopted for $^{208}$Pb: $\delta_{np} = \langle r^2 \rangle_n^{1/2} - \langle r^2 \rangle_p^{1/2} \approx 0.16(6)$ fm

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels treatment of fusion for weakly bound, strongly deformed nuclei, building on the rotor model description of $^9$Be and the double-folding method previously applied by Misicu and Greiner to fusion between spherical and deformed nuclei. The calculations address the experimental data for $^9$Be+$^{144}$Sm from Anuradha et al. (2006) and $^9$Be+$^{208}$Pb from Anuradha et al. (2004), where the earlier analysis using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential without decay effects found a 30% suppression of complete fusion relative to calculation. The present work shows that including the decay of excited states accounts for a 10% reduction, leaving a residual 20% discrepancy for the lead target that points to additional reaction mechanisms such as neutron transfer from $^9$Be, which recent experiments by Rafiei et al. show dominates the total breakup yield. The approach enables future theoretical studies of the competition between breakup, neutron transfer, and fusion in reactions with weakly bound projectiles.
