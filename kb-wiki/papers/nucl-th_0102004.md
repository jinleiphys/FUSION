# Realistic models of pion-exchange three-nucleon interactions

> We present realistic models of pion-exchange three-nucleon interactions obtained by fitting the energies of all the 17 bound or narrow states of 3 <= A <= 8 nucleons, calculated with less than 2% error using the Green's function Monte Carlo method. The models contain two-pion-exchange terms due to pi-N scattering in S- and P-waves, three-pion-exchange terms due to ring diagrams with one Delta in the intermediate states, and a phenomenological repulsive term. The models have five parameters: the four strengths and the short-range cutoff. The 17 fitted energies are insufficient to determine all of them uniquely. We consider five models; they reproduce the observed energies with an rms error < 1% when used together with the Argonne v18 two-nucleon interaction. In one of the models the pi-N S-wave scattering interaction is set to zero; in all others it has the strength suggested by chiral effective field theory. One model also assumes that the pi-N P-wave scattering interaction has the strength suggested by effective field theories, and the cutoff is adjusted to fit the data. In all other models the cutoff is taken to be the same as in the AV18 interaction. The effect of relativistic boost correction is estimated. Results obtained with only Argonne v8' and v18 interactions are also reported. In addition, we present results for 7- and 8-body neutron drops in external potential wells.

## Key claim

The paper presents five parameterized models (Illinois-1 through Illinois-5) of pion-exchange three-nucleon interactions that, when combined with the Argonne v18 two-nucleon interaction, reproduce the observed energies of 17 bound or narrow states of nuclei with A between 3 and 8 with an rms error below 1%, but the 17 fitted energies are insufficient to uniquely determine all five model parameters, so each model fixes two parameters to theoretically plausible values and adjusts the remaining three.

## Method

The authors constructed three-nucleon interaction models containing two-pion-exchange terms from pi-N scattering in S- and P-waves, three-pion-exchange ring diagrams with one Delta in intermediate states, and a phenomenological repulsive term. They used the Green's function Monte Carlo method to calculate binding energies of 17 states in nuclei with A between 3 and 8 with less than 2% error, then adjusted the interaction strengths and short-range cutoff parameters to fit the experimental energies. The models were designed for use with the Argonne v18 two-nucleon interaction, and the effect of relativistic boost corrections to the two-body interaction was estimated perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- The 17 fitted states include all bound or narrow states of nuclei with 3 ≤ A ≤ 8 nucleons, calculated with less than 2% error using the Green's function Monte Carlo method
- The five models reproduce observed energies with an rms error less than 1% when used together with the Argonne v18 two-nucleon interaction
- The Fujita-Miyazawa model predicts Atpip ≈ -0.04 MeV for the two-pion-exchange P-wave strength, while the Tucson-Melbourne model gives Atpip = -0.063 MeV and the Texas model gives Atpip = -0.09 MeV
- The Tucson-Melbourne value a' = -1.03/m_pi gives Atpis ≈ -0.8 MeV for the S-wave scattering strength
- The cutoff parameter c = 2.1 fm^{-2} is used in all Illinois models except IL3, where it is adjusted to fit the data
- The boost correction to the two-body interaction increases the triton energy by approximately 0.4 MeV away from experiment

## Context

This work extends the Urbana series of three-nucleon interaction models, particularly the Urbana IX (UIX) model which contained only two-pion-exchange P-wave and repulsive terms and still underbound p-shell nuclei, especially those with large neutron excess like 8He. The new Illinois models add S-wave two-pion-exchange and three-pion-exchange ring diagram terms to address these failures, and they enable more accurate ab initio calculations of light nuclei up to A=8. The paper also provides results for 7- and 8-body neutron drops in external potential wells to constrain energy-density functionals for neutron-rich nuclei, complementing prior work by Pieper et al. (1997) and Wiringa et al. (2000) on GFMC calculations of light nuclei.
