# Upcgen: a Monte Carlo simulation program for dilepton pair production in ultra-peripheral collisions of heavy ions

> Ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs) of heavy ions can be used as a clean environment to study two-photon induced interactions such as dilepton pair photoproduction. Recently, precise data on lepton pair production in UPCs were obtained by the ATLAS experiment at the LHC where significant deviations, of up to 20%, from available theoretical predictions were observed. In this work, we present a Monte Carlo event generator, Upcgen, that implements a refined treatment of the photon flux allowing us to improve the agreement with ATLAS data at large dilepton rapidities. Besides, the new generator offers a possibility to study photon polarization effects and set arbitrary values of the lepton anomalous magnetic moment that can be used in the future studies of tau g-2 via ditau production measurements in UPCs.

## Key claim

Upcgen improves agreement with ATLAS dimuon data at large dilepton rapidities by implementing a refined photon flux treatment based on realistic nuclear form factors, including contributions from photons emitted at impact parameters smaller than the nuclear radius, which STARlight's point-like approximation with a hard cut-off excludes.

## Method

The program computes the dilepton production cross section for \(AA \to AA + \ell\ell\) by folding the elementary \(\gamma\gamma \to \ell\ell\) cross section with the two-photon luminosity. The elementary cross section for arbitrary lepton anomalous magnetic moment is calculated using a generalized vertex formalism, while the two-photon luminosity is obtained by integrating photon fluxes over impact parameters, using either a point-like source approximation or a realistic Woods-Saxon form factor. Photon polarization effects are included by decomposing the cross section into parallel and perpendicular polarization components with separate two-photon luminosities.

## Key numbers

- ATLAS data show deviations of up to 20% from STARlight theoretical predictions for dimuon production in Pb–Pb UPCs at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV.
- The total cross section for \(\mathrm{PbPb} \to \mathrm{PbPb} + \tau\tau\) with \(a_\tau=0\) at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV is 1.06 mb, agreeing with calculations by Dyndal et al.
- At central rapidities (\(|y_{\mu\mu}| < 0.8\)), Upcgen and SuperChic overestimate the ATLAS cross section by about 10%, while at intermediate rapidities (\(1.6 < |y_{\mu\mu}| < 2.4\)), STARlight underestimates by about 20% and Upcgen stays closer to data.
- Photon polarization effects result in up to 1% differences in tau pair production cross sections compared to calculations ignoring polarization.
- The best limits on the tau anomalous magnetic moment from DELPHI are \(-0.052 < a_{\tau} < 0.013\) at 95% CL.

## Context

This work extends the line of Monte Carlo generators for UPCs, specifically addressing discrepancies between STARlight (Klein, 2016) and ATLAS data, and complementing SuperChic (Harland-Lang, 2018, 2020) which overshoots measurements by about 10% at large rapidities. The generator enables future studies of the tau anomalous magnetic moment via ditau production in UPCs, where \(a_\tau\) may be up to 280 times more sensitive to beyond-Standard-Model effects than \(a_\mu\) based on the Muon g-2 experiment results.
