# Energy Correlators Taking Charge

> The confining transition from asymptotically free partons to hadrons remains one of the most mysterious aspects of Quantum Chromodynamics. With the wealth of high quality jet substructure data we can hope to gain new experimental insights into the details of its dynamics. Jet substructure has traditionally focused on correlations, $\langle \mathcal{E}(n_1) \mathcal{E}(n_2) \cdots \mathcal{E}(n_k) \rangle$, in the energy flux of hadrons. However, significantly more information about the confinement transition is encoded in how energy is correlated between hadrons with different quantum numbers, for example electric charge. In this Letter we develop the field theoretic formalism to compute general correlations, $\langle \mathcal{E}_{R_1}(n_1) \mathcal{E}_{R_2}(n_2) \cdots\mathcal{E}_{R_k}(n_k) \rangle$, between the energy flux carried by hadrons with quantum numbers $R_i$, by introducing new universal non-perturbative functions, which we term joint track functions. Using this formalism we show that the strong interactions introduce enhanced small angle correlations between opposite-sign hadrons, relative to like-sign hadrons, identifiable as an enhanced scaling of $\langle \mathcal{E}_+(n_1) \mathcal{E}_-(n_2) \rangle$ relative to $\langle \mathcal{E}_+(n_1) \mathcal{E}_+(n_2) \rangle$. We are also able to compute the scaling of a $C$-odd three-point function, $\langle \mathcal{E}_\mathcal{Q}(n_1) \mathcal{E}_\mathcal{Q}(n_2) \mathcal{E}_\mathcal{Q}(n_3) \rangle$. Our results greatly extend the class of systematically computable jet substructure observables, pushing perturbation theory deeper into the parton to hadron transition, and providing new observables to understand the dynamics of confinement.

## Key claim

The authors develop a field-theoretic formalism to compute correlations between energy flux carried by hadrons with different quantum numbers (e.g., electric charge), showing that strong interactions produce enhanced small-angle correlations between opposite-sign hadrons relative to like-sign hadrons, with the caveat that these predictions rely on new universal non-perturbative functions called joint track functions whose moments enter the factorization theorems.

## Method

The authors introduce joint track functions, which simultaneously measure the energy fractions carried by hadrons of multiple distinct quantum numbers (here positive and negative electric charge), and derive factorization theorems for generalized energy correlators of the form ⟨ℰ_R1(n1) ℰ_R2(n2) ... ℰ_Rk(nk)⟩. They compute the renormalization group evolution of these joint track functions and extract their moments from the Pythia parton shower, then use these to derive the asymptotic small-angle scaling behavior of charged two-point and three-point correlators at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy.

## Key numbers

- The two-point correlator ⟨ℰ_Q ℰ_Q⟩ for up-type quarks scales as dσ/dθ ∼ θ^(−2) up to logarithmic corrections, compared to the familiar dσ/dθ ∼ θ^(−1) of standard energy correlators.
- The leading non-perturbative power correction to the energy correlators scales as Λ/θ^3, where Λ is universal up to multiplication by moments of the (joint) track functions.
- The covariance of the joint track function, σ^(+-)(1,1;μ), decreases monotonically as it is evolved to higher energies, as shown by the renormalization group evolution with anomalous dimension matrix eigenvalues that are positive.

## Context

This work extends the energy correlator program (Basham 1979, Dixon 2019, Chen 2020) and the track function formalism (Chang 2013, Li 2021, Jaarsma 2022) to correlators that distinguish hadrons by quantum number, building on the observation that only integer moments of non-perturbative functions enter as matching coefficients (Chen 2020). It enables systematic perturbative calculations of observables sensitive to the confinement transition, complementing earlier work on leading-hadron correlations (Chien 2021) and providing a framework for experimental measurements at colliders.
