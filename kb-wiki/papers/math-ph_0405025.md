# Relativistic N-boson systems bound by pair potentials V(r_{ij}) = g(r_{ij}^2)

> We study the lowest energy E of a relativistic system of N identical bosons bound by pair potentials of the form V(r_{ij}) = g(r_{ij}^2) in three spatial dimensions. In natural units hbar = c = 1 the system has the semirelativistic `spinless-Salpeter' Hamiltonian H = \sum_{i=1}^N \sqrt{m^2 + p_i^2} + \sum_{j>i=1}^N g(|r_i - r_j|^2), where g is monotone increasing and has convexity g'' >= 0. We use `envelope theory' to derive formulas for general lower energy bounds and we use a variational method to find complementary upper bounds valid for all N >= 2. In particular, we determine the energy of the N-body oscillator g(r^2) = c r^2 with error less than 0.15% for all m >= 0, N >= 2, and c > 0.

## Key claim
The paper derives rigorous lower and upper energy bounds for the ground state of N identical bosons with a semirelativistic spinless-Salpeter Hamiltonian and pair potentials of the form V(r) = g(r^2), where g is monotone increasing and convex (g'' >= 0). For the specific case of the harmonic oscillator V(r) = c r^2, the bounds are separated by less than 0.15% for all masses m >= 0, all N >= 2, and all couplings c > 0, but this precision relies on the assumption that the one-body oscillator energy e(m) is known numerically to arbitrary accuracy.

## Method
The authors use envelope theory to construct lower energy bounds by exploiting the convexity of g: the potential V(r) = g(r^2) lies above its tangent potentials a(t) + b(t) r^2, reducing the N-body problem to a scaled one-body oscillator problem whose ground-state energy e(m) is computed numerically. Complementary upper bounds are obtained variationally using a translation-invariant Gaussian trial wave function symmetric in Jacobi relative coordinates, with the kinetic-energy expectation expressed in terms of modified Bessel functions of the second kind K_1(z). For the harmonic oscillator, the reduction is exact in the sense that the N-body lower bound is given by the ground state of a one-body Hamiltonian with scaled parameters.

## Key numbers
- For the harmonic oscillator (q=2) at m=1 and N=8, the lower bound is E_8^L(1) = 35.86383 and the upper bound is E_8^U(1) = 35.89953; the average determines the energy with error less than 0.05%.
- For the harmonic oscillator (q=2), the bounds are separated by less than 0.15% for all m >= 0, N >= 2, and c > 0.
- For the potential V(r) = c r^(5/2), the maximum percentage separation between bounds (at m=0) is less than 1.43% for all N >= 2.
- For the cubic oscillator V(r) = c r^3 (q=3), the maximum percentage separation between bounds (at m=0) is 3.6% for all N >= 2.
- In the ultrarelativistic limit m -> 0, the lower bound involves the first zero of the Airy function, z_0 ≈ 2.33810741.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier results for the N-body harmonic oscillator (Hall, Lucha, Schoeberl 2002) to a broader class of potentials that are smooth convex transformations of the oscillator, V(r) = g(r^2). It also sharpens the upper bounds from that earlier work by avoiding the loosening incurred by Jensen's inequality, following a strategy already applied to the massless case m=0 (Hall, Lucha, Schoeberl 2003). The reduction of the N-boson problem to a scaled two-body problem builds on the permutation-symmetry approach of Hall and Post (1967). The results enable rigorous energy estimates for semirelativistic many-boson systems with a wide range of attractive pair potentials, and the bounds become exact in the nonrelativistic (Schrodinger) limit m -> infinity.
