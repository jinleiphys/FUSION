# One-body Langevin dynamics in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> We present a new framework to treat the dissipation and fluctuation dynamics associated with nucleon-nucleon scattering in heavy-ion collisions. The two-body collision processes are effectively described in terms of the diffusion of nucleons in viscous nuclear media, governed by a set of Langevin equations in momentum space. The new framework combined with the usual mean field dynamics can be used to simulate heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies. As a proof of principle, we simulate Au + Au reactions and obtain results consistent with other existing codes under the same constrained conditions. We also study the formation of fragments in Sn + Sn reactions at 50 MeV/nucleon, and results are discussed and compared with two other models commonly employed for collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new framework that replaces the two-body collision integral in the Boltzmann equation with a set of Langevin equations in momentum space, treating dissipation and fluctuation dynamics simultaneously and consistently rather than through an ad-hoc inclusion of fluctuations. The authors demonstrate that this Brownian motion model yields results consistent with other existing codes for one-body observables, but the approach is presented as a proof of principle rather than a fully validated replacement for existing methods.

## Method

The authors replace the Boltzmann collision integral with a Fokker-Planck equation derived from an expansion over scattering angle, then simulate the corresponding Langevin equation for nucleon wave-packets in momentum space. Test particles are partitioned into nucleon wave-packets using k-means clustering, and within spherical scattering regions of radius approximately 2 fm, the Langevin equation coefficients (drag coefficients R and R̃, and diffusion matrix D) are evaluated on a three-dimensional momentum lattice. The momentum transfer is applied to each nucleon wave-packet, followed by a collective recoil adjustment of the nuclear medium to conserve momentum and energy, and a Pauli-blocking procedure that rejects unphysical Brownian motions.

## Key numbers

- Mean-field parameters: A = −209.2 MeV, B = 156.4 MeV, C = −6 MeV, D = 1.35, S_iso = 18 MeV, ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, chosen to reproduce binding energy of 16 MeV/nucleon, incompressibility of 240 MeV, and symmetry energy of 30.3 MeV at normal density
- Lattice spacing for momentum space: typically between 100 MeV/c and 140 MeV/c
- Time step size: Δt ∼ 0.25 – 0.5 fm/c
- Metric parameters for k-means clustering: d_r = 1.2 fm, d_p = 130.5 MeV/c
- For single ground state nuclei, over 97% of attempted Brownian motions are blocked by the Pauli-blocking procedure
- Simulations performed for Au + Au at 100 MeV/nucleon and 400 MeV/nucleon at impact parameter b = 7 fm, and for Sn + Sn at 50 MeV/nucleon at impact parameter b = 0.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the Boltzmann-Langevin approach originally derived by Ayik and Gregoire and the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) model by Colonna et al., and is related to the recent Boltzmann-Langevin-One-Body (BLOB) dynamics by Napolitani and Colonna. The authors compare their results for Au + Au collisions with a transport code comparison project (CodeComparison, 2016) and for Sn + Sn fragmentation with the SMF and Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) models from a separate study (SMFAMD). The framework enables future extensions to incorporate elastic and inelastic collisions between different nucleon species and to study multifragmentation processes in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions.
