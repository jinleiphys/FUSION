# Small-$x$ physics at the LHeC

> The Large Hadron-electron Collider LHeC is a proposed upgrade of the LHC. It would add an electron beam to the LHC, and make it possible to study electron-proton and electron-nucleus collisions at very high energies. We present some of the highlights of the LHeC physics program related to the studies of partonic structure of protons and nuclei, and to the non-linear QCD phenomena visible at small $x$.

## Key claim

The LHeC would enable precise tests of non-linear QCD dynamics at small Bjorken-\(x\) down to \(x\sim 10^{-6}\) at perturbative scales \(Q^2\gtrsim 1\ \textrm{GeV}^2\), but the ability to distinguish non-linear saturation effects from linear resummation depends on the large kinematic lever arm and the precision of pseudodata, which in the nuclear case still suffers from underestimated PDF uncertainties at \(x\lesssim 10^{-3}\).

## Method

The paper reviews the proposed LHeC physics program for small-\(x\) QCD by presenting pseudodata for inclusive structure functions (e.g., \(F_2\) and \(F_L\)), dihadron azimuthal correlations, and exclusive vector meson production in electron-proton and electron-nucleus collisions. These observables are compared to predictions from the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory (using BK/JIMWLK evolution) and from collinear factorization with and without small-\(x\) resummation, with nuclear effects modeled via the IPsat and IPnonsat parametrizations.

## Key numbers

* Kinematic reach down to \(x\sim 10^{-6}\) at \(Q^2\gtrsim 1\ \textrm{GeV}^2\) for the LHeC.
* Nuclear suppression factor \(R = F_2/(A F_p)\) at \(Q^2=5\ \textrm{GeV}^2\) shown as LHeC pseudodata; CGC predictions give \(R\sim 0.5\) deep in the saturation region, rising to \(R=1\) in the dilute region.
* Nuclear suppression for exclusive vector meson production: light mesons are more heavily suppressed than heavier \(J/\Psi\); the ratio is approximately 1 for the linear IPnonsat parametrization.
* Dihadron azimuthal correlation: the back-to-back peak almost disappears when the proton target is replaced by a heavy nucleus, with the effect larger at higher center-of-mass energies.

## Context

This work extends the HERA program (Abramowicz 2015) by proposing a collider that reaches smaller \(x\) and includes nuclear targets, where no DIS experiment at collider energies has been performed. It directly builds on the LHeC design study (Abelleira Fernandez 2012) and on CGC calculations (Lappi 2013, Mantysaari 2018) to predict observables that can test saturation versus linear resummation (Ball 2017). The results would enable improved nuclear PDF extractions (Paukkunen 2017) and reduce PDF uncertainties for LHC searches.
