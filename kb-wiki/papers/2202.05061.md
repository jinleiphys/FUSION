# Quark deconfinement in compact stars through sexaquark condensation

> In this contribution, we present for the first time a scenario according to which early quark deconfinement in compact stars is triggered by the Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of a light sexaquark (S) with a mass $m_S<2054$ MeV, that has been suggested as a candidate particle to explain the baryonic dark matter in the Universe. The onset of S BEC marks the maximum mass of hadronic neutron stars and it occurs when the condition for the baryon chemical potential $μ_b=m_S/2$ is fulfilled in the center of the star, corresponding to $M_{\rm onset}\lesssim 0.7~M_\odot$. In the gravitational field of the star the density of the BEC of the S increases until a new state of the matter is attained, where each of the S-states got dissociated into a triplet of color-flavor-locked (CFL) diquark states. These diquarks are the Cooper pairs in the color superconducting CFL phase of quark matter, so that the developed scenario corresponds to a BEC-BCS transition in strongly interacting matter. For the description of the CFL phase, we develop here for the first time the three-flavor extension of the density-functional formulation of a chirally symmetric Lagrangian model of quark matter where confining properties are encoded in a divergence of the scalar self-energy at low densities and temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper presents a scenario where early quark deconfinement in compact stars is triggered by Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of a light sexaquark with mass \(m_S < 2054\) MeV, but only if the sexaquark mass is held constant at the upper bound of its stability range (2054 MeV) and no density-dependent mass shift occurs; otherwise, a stable hybrid star sequence would not form.

## Method

The authors combine a generalized relativistic density functional (GRDF) model for hadronic matter (DD2 parameterization) with hyperons and sexaquarks, and a newly developed three-flavor extension of a chirally symmetric density-functional Lagrangian for quark matter that includes confining properties via a divergent scalar self-energy. The hadronic and quark matter equations of state are connected via a Maxwell construction, and the resulting hybrid star configurations are computed by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations. The sexaquark BEC onset condition is \(\mu_b = m_S/2\), and the CFL quark matter phase is described with diquark pairing gaps and vector repulsion.

## Key numbers

- Sexaquark mass upper bound: \(m_S < 2054\) MeV, with stability against decay on cosmological timescales for \(m_S \leq 2054.466\) MeV.
- Onset of sexaquark BEC occurs at baryon density \(n_b^{\rm onset, S} = 0.25\) fm\(^{-3}\) (above 1.5 times nuclear saturation density) for \(m_S = 2054\) MeV.
- Onset star mass for sexaquark BEC: \(M_{\rm onset} \lesssim 0.7 M_\odot\).
- Maximum neutron star mass in pure nucleonic DD2 model: \(2.4 M_\odot\).
- Quark matter EoS coefficients used: \(a_4 = 0.22\), \(a_2 = -(299.6\ {\rm MeV})^2\), \(B_{\rm eff}^{1/4} = 174.2\) MeV.
- Momentum cutoff for quark model: \(\Lambda = 538\) MeV, derived from vacuum chiral condensate \(\langle\overline{f}f\rangle_0 = -(251\ {\rm MeV})^3\).
- Dark matter to baryon ratio reproduced: \(\Omega_{DM}/\Omega_B = 5.3 \pm 0.1\) for \(m_S = 1860\) MeV at \(T_{\rm eff} = 156\) MeV, or for \(m_S = 1876.54\) MeV at \(T_{\rm eff} = 150\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier two-flavor density-functional approach to quark matter (Kaltenborn 2017, Ivanytskyi 2021, 2022) to three flavors, incorporating color-flavor-locked (CFL) pairing. It contradicts the "No-Go" conjecture for absolute stability of strange quark matter in NJL-type models (Klahn 2017) and reopens the possibility of strangeness in compact stars, which had been argued against (Blaschke 2010). The scenario enables hybrid stars with cores of CFL quark matter that satisfy the \(2 M_\odot\) pulsar constraint (PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0348+0432) and the radius constraints from GW170817 and NICER measurements.
