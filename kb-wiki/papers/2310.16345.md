# Constructing the Equation of State of QCD in a functional QCD based scheme

> We construct the equation of state (EoS) of QCD based on the finite chemical potential information from the functional QCD approaches, with the assistance of the lattice QCD EoS. The obtained EoS is consistent with the up-to-date estimations of the QCD phase diagram, including a phase transition temperature at zero chemical potential of $T=155$ MeV, the curvature of the transition line $κ=0.016$ and also a critical end point at $(T,μ_B)=(118, 600)$ MeV. In specific, the phase diagram mapping is achieved by incorporating the order parameters into the EoS, namely the dynamical quark mass for the chiral phase transition together with the Polyakov loop parameter for the deconfinement phase transition. We also implement the EoS in hydrodynamic simulations to compute the particle yields, ratios and collective flow, and find that our obtained EoS agrees well with the commonly used one based on the combination of lattice QCD simulation and hadron resonance gas model.

# Constructing the Equation of State of QCD in a functional QCD based scheme

## Key claim

The authors construct a QCD equation of state that incorporates a critical end point at (T, μ_B) = (118, 600) MeV and a phase transition curvature κ = 0.016, but the construction relies on a specific mapping of order parameters (dynamical quark mass and Polyakov loop) using an Ising parametrization with free parameters ω, ρ, and M₀ that are set to values from prior work rather than derived from first principles.

## Method

The authors combine lattice QCD equation of state data at zero chemical potential with functional QCD results for the phase transition line and critical end point location. They express the quark number density analytically using a vanishing-momentum approximation for the quark propagator, incorporating the dynamical quark mass as the chiral order parameter and the Polyakov loop as the deconfinement order parameter. The order parameters are mapped to the (T, μ_B) plane using an Ising parametrization with a nonlinear mapping function that ensures consistency with the known phase transition line. The resulting equation of state is then implemented in the MUSIC hydrodynamic model with AMPT initial conditions to compute particle yields, ratios, and elliptic flow.

## Key numbers

- Phase transition temperature at zero chemical potential: T_c(0) = 155 MeV
- Curvature of the transition line: κ = 0.016
- Critical end point location: (T, μ_B) = (118, 600) MeV
- Vacuum dynamical quark mass for light flavors: M₀ = 350 MeV
- Minimum speed of sound squared at small chemical potentials: c²_s ∼ 0.12 at the phase transition point
- Ising parametrization exponents: β = 0.326, δ = 4.80, with h₀ = 0.394
- Mapping angles: α₁ = 7.0°, α₂ = 97.0°
- Mapping parameters: ω = 1.0, ρ = 2.0, M₀ = 0.75
- Polyakov loop fit parameters: l₁ = 2.732, m₁ = 0.5495, m₂ = 1.831
- Lattice trace anomaly fit parameters: h₀ = 0.1396, h₁ = -0.1800, h₂ = 0.0350, f₀ = 2.76, f₁ = 6.79, f₂ = -5.29, g₁ = -0.47, g₂ = 1.04 (with t = T/(200 MeV))
- Estimated first order phase transition region reached at s/n_B ∼ 10

## Context

This work extends functional QCD studies of the phase diagram by Dyson-Schwinger equations and functional renormalization group approaches (Gunkel 2021, Fu 2019, Gao 2020) that predicted a critical end point at μ_B ≈ 600-650 MeV, but had not provided a complete equation of state suitable for hydrodynamic simulations. The construction uses the lattice QCD equation of state at zero chemical potential from Borsanyi (2010) and the Ising parametrization mapping approach from Parotto (2018). The resulting equation of state enables hydrodynamic simulations to explore the first order phase transition region at high baryon density, which was not accessible with the commonly used NEOS-B equation of state based on lattice QCD and hadron resonance gas model (Monnai 2019).
