# Non local lagrangians(I): the pion

> We define a family of non local and chirally symmetric low energy lagrangians motivated by theoretical studies on Quantum Chromodynamics. These models lead to quark propagators with non trivial momentum dependencies. We define the formalism for two body bound states and apply it to the pion. We study the coupling of the photon and W bosons with special attention to the implementation of local gauge invariance. We calculate the pion decay constant recovering the Goldberger-Treiman and the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relations. We recover a form of the axial current consistent with PCAC. Finally we study the pion form factor and we construct the operators involved in its parton distribution.

## Key claim

This paper defines a family of non-local, chirally symmetric low-energy Lagrangians that produce quark propagators with non-trivial momentum dependencies, and applies the formalism to the pion as a two-body bound state, recovering the Goldberger-Treiman and Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relations while showing that the conventional impulse approximation for the pion form factor is not consistent with local gauge symmetry.

## Method

The author constructs a phenomenological Lagrangian with non-local scalar, pseudoscalar, and momentum currents that generate momentum-dependent quark mass and wave function renormalization via the Dyson equation. The pion is obtained as a Bethe-Salpeter bound state, and the coupling of photons and W bosons is implemented through path-ordered exponentials to enforce local gauge invariance, yielding dressed vertices that include additional transverse contributions beyond the Ball-Chiu ansatz.

## Key numbers

* Quark condensate: -303 MeV (scenario S1) and -285 MeV (scenario S2), compared to experimental range -250 to -300 MeV
* Pion mass: 137 MeV (S1) and 139 MeV (S2), compared to experimental range 135-140 MeV
* Pion decay constant: 82 MeV (S1) and 81 MeV (S2), compared to experimental value 92 MeV
* Pion mean square radius: 0.41 fm^2 (S1) and 0.36 fm^2 (S2) for full vertices, with Ball-Chiu ansatz giving 0.41 fm^2 and 0.40 fm^2 respectively, compared to experimental value 0.44 fm^2
* Current quark mass: 2.3 MeV (S1) and 3.0 MeV (S2), compared to experimental range 1.5-8 MeV
* Scenario S1 uses mass function parameters Lambda_m = 0.767 GeV and alpha_m = 0.343 GeV from Dyakonov and Petrov (1986)
* Scenario S2 uses mass function parameters Lambda_m = 0.719 GeV and alpha_m = 0.302 GeV, and wave function parameters alpha_z = -0.5 and Lambda_z = 1.183 GeV from Bowman et al. (2002, 2003)

## Context

This work extends the non-local Nambu-Jona Lasinio (NJL) model approach of Birse (1995, 1998, 2004) and Osipov (1995, 2006) by incorporating full momentum dependence of the quark propagator from fundamental QCD studies, including both mass and wave function renormalization. The formalism enables consistent calculation of pion electromagnetic properties and parton distributions while preserving chiral, Poincare, and local gauge symmetries, addressing frame-dependence issues noted in relativistic quantum mechanics calculations by Desplanques, Noguera, and Theussl (2003).
