# Flavor content of nucleon form factors in a VMD approach

> The strange form factors of the nucleon are studied in a two-component model consisting of a three-quark intrinsic structure surrounded by a meson cloud. A comparison with the available experimental world data from the SAMPLE, PVA4, HAPPEX and G0 collaborations shows a good overall agreement. It is shown that the strangeness contribution to the electric and magnetic form factors is of the order of a few percent. In particular, the strange quark contribution to the charge radius is small (0.005 fm**2) and to the magnetic moment it is positive (0.315 n.m.).

# Flavor content of nucleon form factors in a VMD approach

## Key claim
The strange quark contribution to nucleon form factors is of order a few percent, with a positive strange magnetic moment (0.315 μ_N) and a small positive strangeness charge radius (0.005 fm²), in agreement with available parity-violating electron scattering data. This positive sign for the strange magnetic moment contradicts most theoretical studies, which favor a negative value, but is consistent with the experimental evidence from SAMPLE, PVA4, HAPPEX, and G0 collaborations.

## Method
The author extends the two-component model of Bijker and Iachello (2004) for electromagnetic nucleon form factors to the strange sector using a vector meson dominance (VMD) approach. The model combines an intrinsic three-quark structure described by a dipole form factor g(Q²) with a meson cloud contribution mediated by ρ, ω, and φ mesons; the strange form factors are determined via the coupling of the strange current to the ω and φ mesons following the method of Jaffe (1989). Five coefficients (γ from the intrinsic form factor, β_ω and α_φ from isoscalar couplings, β_ρ and α_ρ from isovector couplings) are fitted to the same electromagnetic form factor data set used in Bijker and Iachello (2004), and the strange couplings are then derived from the isoscalar couplings using the ω-φ mixing angle ε = 0.053 rad (3.0°).

## Key numbers
- Strange magnetic moment: μ_s = 0.315 μ_N (positive)
- Strangeness charge radius: ⟨r²_s⟩_E = 0.005 fm²
- Strangeness magnetic radius: ⟨r²_s⟩_M = 0.410 fm²
- Strange Dirac form factor couplings: β_φ^s = -β_ω^s = 0.202
- Strange Pauli form factor couplings: α_φ^s = 0.648, α_ω^s = -0.018
- ω-φ mixing angle: ε = 0.053 rad (3.0°)
- Experimental strange electric form factor at Q² = 0.091 (GeV/c)²: G_E^s = -0.038 ± 0.042 ± 0.010 (HAPPEX, Aniol 2005)
- Experimental strange electric form factor at Q² = 0.1 (GeV/c)²: G_E^s = -0.002 ± 0.017 (Paschke)
- Experimental strange magnetic form factor at Q² = 0.1 (GeV/c)²: G_M^s = 0.37 ± 0.20 ± 0.26 ± 0.07 (SAMPLE, Spayde)
- Experimental strange magnetic form factor at Q² = 0.1 (GeV/c)²: G_M^s = 0.12 ± 0.24 (Paschke)
- Combined fit result at Q² ~ 0.1 (GeV/c)²: G_M^s(0.1) = 0.55 ± 0.28, G_E^s(0.1) = -0.01 ± 0.03 (Aniol 2005)
- Lattice QCD result: μ_s = G_M^s(0) = -0.046 ± 0.019 μ_N (Leinweber)
- Lattice QCD result: G_E^s(0.1) = -0.009 ± 0.028 (Leinweber)
- Good agreement with experimental data for 0 < Q² < 1 (GeV/c)²

## Context
This work extends the two-component model of Bijker and Iachello (2004) for electromagnetic nucleon form factors by incorporating the method of Jaffe (1989) to determine strangeness content via ω and φ meson couplings. The positive strange magnetic moment contradicts most theoretical studies (which favor negative values, as reviewed by Beck) but agrees with experimental evidence from SAMPLE, PVA4, HAPPEX, and G0 collaborations; it also differs from lattice QCD results of Leinweber (μ_s = -0.046 μ_N) and from most other theoretical approaches (Jaffe, Park, Garvey, Forkel, Hammer, Lyubovitskij) that give negative F₂^s, with exceptions being the meson-exchange model of Meissner (values two orders of magnitude smaller) and the SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model of Silva (positive at small Q² but changing sign around Q² = 0.1-0.3 (GeV/c)²). The model enables a consistent simultaneous description of electromagnetic and weak vector form factors of the nucleon and can be tested by future parity-violating electron scattering to backward angles and neutrino scattering experiments.
