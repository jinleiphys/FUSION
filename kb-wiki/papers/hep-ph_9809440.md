# Thermal Dileptons from a Nonperturbative Quark-Gluon Phase

> Assuming that gluon condensates are important even above the deconfining phase transition, we develop a model for the dilepton yield from a quark gluon plasma. Using a simple fire ball description of a heavy ion collision, and various estimates of the strengths of the gluon condensates, we compare our predicted dilepton yields with those observed in the CERES and HELIOS experiments at CERN. The simple model gives an adequate description of the data, and in particular it explains the observed considerable enhancement of the yield in the low mass region.

# Thermal Dileptons from a Nonperturbative Quark-Gluon Phase

## Key claim

Assuming that gluon condensates (chromoelectric, chromomagnetic, and $A_4^2$) persist above the deconfining phase transition, the dilepton yield from a quark-gluon plasma can be adequately described using a simple fireball model, and the observed enhancement in the low-mass region at CERES and HELIOS is explained, but the model is explicitly phenomenological and relies on estimated condensate values rather than a first-principles calculation.

## Method

The authors compute the dilepton emission rate from a thermalized quark-gluon plasma by adding nonperturbative corrections to the perturbative Born term (quark-antiquark annihilation) using operator product expansion techniques. The $A_4^2$ condensate contribution is obtained by treating a constant $A_4$ background as an imaginary color chemical potential and averaging over color directions; the chromoelectric and chromomagnetic condensate contributions are calculated using fixed-point gauge methods from earlier work (Hansson and Zahed, 1990). The emission rates are then integrated over a simple expanding fireball model with parameters taken from transport calculations (Li, Rapp), using detector acceptance cuts for the CERES and HELIOS-3 experiments.

## Key numbers

- The chromoelectric and chromomagnetic condensates are estimated as $\langle \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} E^2 \rangle \sim \langle \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} B^2 \rangle \sim (200\ \mathrm{MeV})^4$, based on lattice analysis (LAT2).
- The $A_4^2$ condensate is estimated as $\beta \equiv \langle \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} A_4^2 \rangle / T^2 \sim 0.4$ for $\alpha_s \sim 0.2$, supported by a lattice calculation in pure Yang-Mills (AANP) giving $a^2 \langle (g A_4)^2 \rangle \sim 1/3$ on a $4 \times 8^3$ lattice.
- The perturbative Born term for three light flavors gives $R_{\ge 3} = 2$ at zero temperature.
- The fireball parameters are: initial temperature $T_i = 170\ \mathrm{MeV}$, freeze-out temperature $T_\infty = 110\ \mathrm{MeV}$, expansion time constant $\tau = 8\ \mathrm{fm}/c$, $t_0 = 10\ \mathrm{fm}/c$, freeze-out time $t_{f.o.} = 10\ \mathrm{fm}/c$, and normalization $N_0 V_0 = 6.76 \times 10^{-7}\ \mathrm{fm}^3$.
- The condensate contributions to the emission rate are sizable in the 500 MeV region and dominate the perturbative part by orders of magnitude in the Dalitz region (below 200 MeV); effects are negligible above 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Hansson and Zahed (1990) that gluon condensates of dimension 2 ($A_4^2$) and 4 ($E^2$, $B^2$) persist above $T_c$, and applies it to dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with purely perturbative calculations of the quark-gluon plasma, which fail to explain the low-mass dilepton enhancement observed by CERES and HELIOS. The approach is similar in spirit to the calculation by Patkos and Sakai of nonperturbative magnetic contributions to $e^+e^-$ annihilation, but uses QCD sum-rule techniques rather than the Copenhagen vacuum picture. The results enable future work incorporating higher-order condensates, mixed condensates, and more realistic mixed-phase descriptions including hadronic matter.
