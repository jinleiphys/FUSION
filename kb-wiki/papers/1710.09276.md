# Nucleon matter equation of state, particle number fluctuations, and shear viscosity within UrQMD box calculations

> Properties of equilibrated nucleon system are studied within the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model. The UrQMD calculations are done within a finite box with periodic boundary conditions. The system achieves thermal equilibrium due to nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering. For the UrQMD equilibrium state, nucleon energy spectra, equation of state, particle number fluctuations, and shear viscosity $η$ are calculated. The UrQMD results are compared with both, statistical mechanics and Chapman-Enskog kinetic theory, for a classical system of nucleons with hard-core repulsion.

## Key claim

The UrQMD transport model, when run in a box with periodic boundary conditions for nucleons at low energies (temperatures below 50 MeV), produces an equilibrium state whose pressure and particle number fluctuations match an ideal gas, not a hard-sphere excluded volume gas, despite the presence of an effective nucleon size from the collision mechanism; the shear viscosity, however, agrees with the Chapman-Enskog result for hard spheres in the frequent collision regime.

## Method

The authors placed 400 nucleons (equal numbers of protons and neutrons) in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions and evolved the system using the standard UrQMD model with only isotropic elastic nucleon-nucleon scatterings, a fixed energy-independent cross section, and no mean-field interactions. After thermal equilibrium was reached (within 150 fm/c for all parameter combinations), they extracted the nucleon energy spectra, pressure via the stress-energy tensor, particle number fluctuations in a sub-volume cell, and shear viscosity via the Green-Kubo formalism from the time-correlation of off-diagonal stress-energy tensor components. The results were compared to the ideal gas law, the excluded volume (van der Waals) equation of state, and the Chapman-Enskog kinetic theory for hard spheres.

## Key numbers

- System parameters: N = 400 nucleons, box side L = 9.3 to 34.2 fm, nucleon density n = 0.01 to 0.5 fm^-3, mean energy per nucleon <epsilon> = 15 to 75 MeV, elastic cross section sigma_int = 10 to 80 mb, equilibration time t_eq < 150 fm/c.
- The UrQMD pressure P equals the ideal gas pressure P = n T, with T defined by <epsilon> = 3T/2, showing no excluded volume effects even at the highest density n = 0.5 fm^-3.
- The scaled variance of particle number fluctuations in a cell follows the binomial distribution omega[N_cell] = 1 - q, where q = (L_cell/L)^3, matching pointlike particles and not the excluded volume prediction omega[N] = (1 - b n)^2.
- The shear viscosity from UrQMD is eta ≈ 0.58 sqrt(m T) / sigma_int, with a numerical factor 0.58 close to the Chapman-Enskog factor 5 sqrt(pi)/16 ≈ 0.55.
- At low densities, the shear viscosity deviates from the Chapman-Enskog result and follows eta_RC ∝ n^(2/3) in the rare collision regime.

## Context

This work extends earlier UrQMD box studies (Belkacem 1998) and comparisons of transport codes (Xu 2016, Zhang 2017) by providing a controlled test of equilibrium properties for a pure nucleon system. It contradicts the expectation that the effective nucleon size from the UrQMD collision mechanism would produce excluded volume effects in the equation of state and fluctuations, as predicted by the van der Waals model (Rischke 1991, Cleymans 1992, Gorenstein 1999). The results enable a clearer separation of which transport coefficients (shear viscosity) are robust to the details of the scattering implementation and which (pressure, fluctuations) are not, guiding future improvements to transport codes for heavy-ion collisions.
