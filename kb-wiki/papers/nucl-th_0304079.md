# Blast-wave snapshots from RHIC

> I present fits with the so-called blast-wave model to single-particle spectra and HBT correlations from Au+Au collisions at a CMS energy of 130 AGeV. There is only little choice of freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity for which the model fits both the identified spectra and the correlation radii just well enough not to be excluded. The observed steep M_t dependence of R_side leads to a temperature which it is problematic to interpret. The applicability of the model for the freeze-out description is thus questioned.

# Blast-wave snapshots from RHIC

## Key claim
The blast-wave model can fit both single-particle spectra and HBT correlation radii from Au+Au collisions at 130 AGeV only marginally, with the best-fit parameters lying near the 95% confidence level contour of both fits; the steep M_t dependence of R_side forces a temperature parameter that cannot be interpreted as a physical freeze-out temperature, questioning the model's applicability for freeze-out description.

## Method
The author fitted identified single-particle p_T spectra of pions, kaons, and protons from PHENIX, and HBT correlation radii from both STAR and PHENIX, using a blast-wave emission function with assumptions of sudden freeze-out at uniform proper time, uniform radial density, boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, and transverse rapidity linear in the radial coordinate. Each spectrum was fitted individually to test the simultaneous freeze-out assumption, and HBT radii were fitted separately; statistical and systematic errors were combined linearly for robust confidence-level contours.

## Key numbers
- The overlap region between fits to spectra and HBT radii at 95% confidence level is located at T ≈ 106 MeV, ⟨v_t⟩ = 0.53, R_B = 12.36 fm, τ_0 = 4.54 fm/c, Δτ = 4.57 fm/c.
- The best fit to π⁺π⁺ correlations alone gives T = 33 MeV, ⟨v_t⟩ = 0.73, R_B = 24.11 fm, τ_0 = 21.32 fm/c, Δτ = 1.09 fm/c.
- Fits to different identified particle spectra show no overlap at the 1σ level, but overlap is found at the 95% confidence level.
- The analysis uses Au+Au collisions at a center-of-mass energy of √s = 130 AGeV.

## Context
This work extends the blast-wave model fitting approach advocated by Csörgő and Lörstad (no year given) for simultaneous determination of freeze-out temperature and transverse flow from spectra and HBT. The results contradict the assumption that a single freeze-out temperature and flow velocity can describe both observables, and are consistent with indications from cascade generators (Bleicher, no year given) and pion scattering rate studies (no author given, no year given) that freeze-out is continuous rather than sudden. The findings motivate development of more realistic freeze-out descriptions, as momentum spectra produced at freeze-out are the basis for searching for signatures of collective behavior.
