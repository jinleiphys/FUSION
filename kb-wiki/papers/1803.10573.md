# Influence of anisotropic $Λ/Σ$ creation on the $Ξ^-$ mutiplicity in subthreshold proton-nucleus collisions

> We present an analysis of $Ξ^-$ baryon production in subthreshold proton--nucleus ($p+A$) collisions in the framework of a BUU type transport model. We propose a new mechanism for $Ξ$ production in the collision of a secondary $Λ$ or $Σ$ hyperon and a nucleon from the target nucleus. We find that the $Ξ^-$ multiplicity in $p+A$ collisions is sensitive to the angular distribution of hyperon production in the primary $N+N$ collision. Using reasonable assumptions on the unknown elementary cross sections we are able to reproduce the $Ξ^-$ multiplicity and the $Ξ^-/(Λ+Σ^0)$ ratio obtained in the HADES experiment in $p$+Nb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = $ 3.2 GeV energy.

# Influence of anisotropic Λ/Σ creation on the Ξ⁻ multiplicity in subthreshold proton-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

The Ξ⁻ multiplicity in subthreshold p+Nb collisions at √sNN = 3.2 GeV is sensitive to the angular distribution of Λ and Σ hyperon production in primary N+N collisions, and using reasonable assumptions on unknown elementary cross sections the experimental HADES results can be reproduced; however, the anisotropy coefficient ξ at the relevant energy is extrapolated from lower-energy COSY-TOF data and is treated as a free parameter, and the absolute magnitude of the Ξ production cross section in hyperon-nucleon collisions is not predicted but instead tuned to match the experimental multiplicity.

## Method

The authors implemented a new Ξ production mechanism via hyperon-nucleon collisions (Y+N → NΞK) in a BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport model, where a secondary Λ or Σ hyperon from the first energetic p+N collision subsequently collides with a second target nucleon. The elementary cross section for Y+N → NΞK was estimated using a resonance model with 8 hyperon resonances (Λ* and Σ*), assuming a 3% branching ratio to the ΞK channel for each resonance and treating the squared matrix element as a free parameter tuned to reproduce the HADES Ξ⁻ multiplicity. Anisotropic hyperon production was implemented using an angular distribution dσ/dcosθ = σ[1/2 + ξ/4(3cos²θ − 1)] with the anisotropy coefficient ξ extrapolated from COSY-TOF data on pp → pΛK⁺ at lower energies.

## Key numbers

- Ξ⁻ multiplicity in the full model: 1.999 × 10⁻⁴, reproducing the HADES experimental value of 2.0 × 10⁻⁴ in p+Nb collisions at √sNN = 3.2 GeV (Ekin = 3.5 GeV)
- Ξ⁻/(Λ+Σ⁰) ratio in the full model: 1.015 × 10⁻², within the experimental range [1.2 ± 0.3(stat) ± 0.4(syst)] × 10⁻² from HADES
- Λ+Σ⁰ multiplicity in the full model: 1.969 × 10⁻², compared to the HADES experimental value of 0.017 ± 0.003
- Anisotropy coefficient ξ at the HADES energy: approximately 1.6, extrapolated from COSY-TOF data at excess energies 0.2–0.3 GeV
- Turning off hyperon anisotropy reduces the Ξ⁻ multiplicity by a factor of about 2.5 (from 1.999 × 10⁻⁴ to 0.785 × 10⁻⁴)
- Hyperon rescattering and absorption (YN → NN\bar{K}) reduces the Ξ⁻ multiplicity by almost 40%
- Hyperon potentials (Λ, Σ, Ξ) affect the Ξ⁻ multiplicity by about 20%
- The pp → pΛK⁺ cross section at √s = 3.2 GeV was scaled to 38.12 μb (from 51 μb) based on HADES partial wave analysis, using a scaling factor of 0.75
- The Y* creation cross section in YN collisions reaches a maximum of about 30 mb at √sYN ≈ 3.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends transport model studies of subthreshold strangeness production in hadron-nucleus collisions, specifically addressing the factor-of-8 discrepancy between the THERMUS statistical model and HADES data for Ξ⁻ yields in p+Nb collisions (HADES collaboration, 2014). The proposed Y+N → NΞK mechanism differs from prior approaches that required collisions of two secondary particles (such as \bar{K}Y → πΞ in rVUU1 or YY → ΞN in rVUU2) or the decay of heavy non-strange resonances to ΞKK as implemented in UrQMD. The work enables further investigation of subthreshold particle production mechanisms in both p+A and A+A collisions, and highlights the need for improved experimental knowledge of hyperon resonance decay modes and angular distributions of hyperon production in N+N collisions at higher energies.
