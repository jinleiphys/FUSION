# New Skyrme parametrizations to describe finite nuclei and neutron star matter with realistic effective masses. II. Adjusting the spin-dependent terms

> Many common Skyrme functionals present ferromagnetic instabilities or unrealistic density dependence of the spin-dependent Landau parameters. To solve these problems, we consider the Skyrme interaction as a density-functional rather than a density-dependent two-body force. This allows us to adjust the spin-dependent terms of the new extended Skyrme functionals of our previous paper [M. Duan and M. Urban, Phys. Rev. C 110, 065806 (2024)] independently without altering the properties of spin saturated matter. The parameters of the spin-dependent terms are determined by fitting the Landau parameters $G_0$ and $G'_0$ in neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter and the effective-mass splitting of up and down particles in spin polarized matter to the results of microscopic calculations. Using the new parametrizations, called Sky3s and Sky4s, the spin-related properties of nuclear matter are in good agreement with the microscopic results. As an application, we compute response functions and neutrino scattering rates of neutron-star matter with the new functionals having realistic effective masses and Landau parameters.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by treating the Skyrme interaction as a density functional rather than a density-dependent two-body force, the spin-dependent terms can be adjusted independently to solve ferromagnetic instabilities and unrealistic density dependence of spin-dependent Landau parameters, but this requires refitting the spin-orbit parameter \(W_0\) to maintain good binding energies and radii of finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors treat the Skyrme energy density functional as a general density functional, allowing independent adjustment of spin-dependent terms without altering spin-independent properties. They determine the coefficients \(C_0^{sT}\) and \(C_1^{sT}\) by fitting the effective-mass splitting of spin-up and spin-down nucleons in spin-polarized symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter to microscopic BHF and LOCV calculations. The coefficients \(C_0^s\) and \(C_1^s\) are determined by fitting the Landau parameters \(G_0\) and \(G_0'\) in symmetric nuclear matter to BHF results, with a correction applied to \(G_0\) in pure neutron matter. The spin-orbit parameter \(W_0\) is then refitted to maintain good agreement with binding energies and radii of doubly-magic nuclei.

## Key numbers

*   The Landau parameter \(G_0'\) at saturation density is approximately 1.20 for Sky3s and 1.19 for Sky4s, agreeing with the value of about 1.2 from Gamow-Teller resonances in finite nuclei.
*   The \(\chi^2\) of binding energies and radii is 4.48 for Sky3s and 5.55 for Sky4s, compared to 4.08 and 5.07 for the unconstrained Sky3 and Sky4.
*   The maximum central densities of neutron stars predicted by the unconstrained interactions are \(\rho_{\text{max}} = 0.9248\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) for Sky3 and \(\rho_{\text{max}} = 1.1264\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) for Sky4.
*   For the new constrained interactions Sky3s and Sky4s, unphysical ferromagnetic instabilities in spin-1 channels appear only at densities near the maximum central density and momentum transfers higher than about \(2\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
*   The new parameters for Sky3s include \(C^{sT}_{00} = -43.6\ \text{MeV fm}^5\), \(C^{sT}_{01} = 42.2\ \text{MeV fm}^6\), \(C^{sT}_{10} = -15.0\ \text{MeV fm}^5\), \(C^{sT}_{11} = 33.0\ \text{MeV fm}^6\), and \(W_0 = 117.4\ \text{MeV fm}^5\).
*   The new parameters for Sky4s include \(C^{sT}_{00} = -43.6\ \text{MeV fm}^5\), \(C^{sT}_{01} = 42.2\ \text{MeV fm}^6\), \(C^{sT}_{10} = -15.0\ \text{MeV fm}^5\), \(C^{sT}_{11} = 33.0\ \text{MeV fm}^6\), and \(W_0 = 114.1\ \text{MeV fm}^5\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous paper (Duan and Urban, 2024) which introduced the Sky3 and Sky4 parametrizations to solve unrealistic effective-mass problems, but which exhibited ferromagnetic instabilities. It addresses the spin-dependent terms that were left unconstrained in those functionals, following the density-functional philosophy of Bender (2002) where spin-independent and spin-dependent terms can be adjusted independently. The new functionals Sky3s and Sky4s enable reliable computation of response functions and neutrino scattering rates in neutron-star matter, which are essential for simulations of supernova explosions, neutron star mergers, and proto-neutron star evolution.
