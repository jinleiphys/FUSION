# Emergent Viscous Hydrodynamics From a Single Quantum Particle

> We investigate an explicit example of how spatial decoherence can lead to hydrodynamic behavior in the late-time, long-wavelength regime of open quantum systems. We focus on the case of a single non-relativistic quantum particle linearly coupled to a thermal bath of noninteracting harmonic oscillators at temperature $T$, a la Caldeira and Leggett. Taking advantage of decoherence in the position representation, we expand the reduced density matrix in powers of the off-diagonal spatial components, so that high-order terms are suppressed at late times. Truncating the resulting power series at second order leads to a set of dissipative transient hydrodynamic equations similar to the non-relativistic limit of equations widely used in simulations of the quark-gluon plasma formed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. Transport coefficients are directly determined by the damping constant $γ$, which quantifies the influence of the environment. The asymptotic limit of our hydrodynamic equations reduces to the celebrated Navier-Stokes equations for a compressible fluid in the presence of a drag force. Our results shed new light on the onset of hydrodynamic behavior in open quantum systems where a system with few degrees of freedom is coupled to a large thermal environment.

## Key claim

Spatial decoherence in an open quantum system consisting of a single non-relativistic particle coupled to a thermal bath can produce viscous hydrodynamic behavior at late times and long wavelengths, even though the system has only one degree of freedom. The hydrodynamic equations emerge from a truncation of the reduced density matrix expansion in powers of off-diagonal spatial components, and the asymptotic limit reduces to the Navier-Stokes equations for a compressible fluid with a drag force.

## Method

The authors start from the Caldeira-Leggett master equation for a single quantum particle linearly coupled to a bath of noninteracting harmonic oscillators at temperature T. They expand the reduced density matrix in powers of the off-diagonal spatial components, exploiting decoherence in the position representation which suppresses high-order terms at late times. Truncating this power series at second order yields a set of dissipative transient hydrodynamic equations, which are then linearized around equilibrium to compute dispersion relations. The same hydrodynamic equations are also derived from a truncation of an effective kinetic theory using the method of moments applied to the Wigner function.

## Key numbers

* The diffusion constant for the gapless hydrodynamic mode is D = k_B T / (2 m gamma), where gamma is the damping constant quantifying the influence of the environment.
* The non-hydrodynamic modes have a gap omega(k -> 0) ~ -i gamma, in contrast to the gapless behavior found in Hamiltonian systems.
* The thermal de Broglie wavelength is lambda_th = hbar / sqrt(2 m k_B T), which sets the scale for decoherence: off-diagonal elements with |x - x'| >> lambda_th decay as exp(-gamma (|x - x'|/lambda_th)^2 t).

## Context

This work extends the Madelung hydrodynamic formulation of isolated quantum systems (Madelung, 1927) to open quantum systems with dissipation and decoherence, where the Madelung representation no longer applies because the reduced density matrix cannot be written as a product of wavefunctions. The resulting transient hydrodynamic equations are similar to those used in simulations of the quark-gluon plasma formed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (Denicol, 2012; Rocha, 2023). The results provide a concrete example of how hydrodynamic behavior can emerge from systems with very few degrees of freedom, which is relevant for understanding the apparent hydrodynamic signatures observed in small systems such as proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions (Noronha, 2024).
