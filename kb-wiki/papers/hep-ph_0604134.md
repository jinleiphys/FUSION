# The Stability of Strange Star Crusts and Strangelets

> We construct strangelets, taking into account electrostatic effects, including Debye screening, and arbitrary surface tension sigma of the interface between vacuum and quark matter. We find that there is a critical surface tension sigma_crit below which large strangelets are unstable to fragmentation and below which quark star surfaces will fragment into a crystalline crust made of charged strangelets immersed in an electron gas. We derive a model-independent relationship between sigma_crit and two parameters that characterize any quark matter equation of state. For reasonable model equations of state, we find sigma_crit typically of order a few MeV/fm^2. If sigma <= sigma_crit, the size-distribution of strangelets in cosmic rays could feature a peak corresponding to the stable strangelets that we construct.

## Key claim

The paper shows that there is a critical surface tension sigma_crit below which neutral bulk quark matter and large strangelets are unstable to fragmentation into smaller, positively charged strangelets; this critical value depends only on two parameters (n_Q and chi_Q) that characterize any quark matter equation of state, and for reasonable models sigma_crit is typically of order a few MeV/fm^2.

## Method

The authors construct spherically symmetric strangelet profiles by solving the Poisson equation in the Thomas-Fermi approximation, using a second-order expansion of the quark matter equation of state in powers of mu_e/mu that depends only on the charge density n_Q and charge susceptibility chi_Q. They compute the Gibbs free energy per quark for strangelets of varying radius R, including Debye screening effects and surface tension sigma, and compare it to that of uniform neutral quark matter at zero pressure to determine stability against fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- The critical surface tension is sigma_crit = 0.1325 * n_Q^2 * lambda_D / chi_Q, where lambda_D = 1/sqrt(4 pi alpha chi_Q) is the Debye screening length
- The radius of the marginally stable strangelet at the critical surface tension is R_* = 1.606 lambda_D
- The volume average of mu_e in the critical strangelet is reduced from its neutral bulk value by a factor f = 0.5051
- The surface tension above which there is no fusion barrier for strangelets is sigma_no-barrier = 0.1699 * n_Q^2 * lambda_D / chi_Q
- The Debye screening length in quark matter is typically about 5 fm

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding by Jaikumar, Reddy, and Steiner (2005) that neglecting Debye screening and surface tension leads to fragmentation of strange star surfaces into charged strangelets immersed in an electron gas. The authors include both Debye screening (following Heiselberg 1993) and surface tension, and derive a model-independent relationship for the critical surface tension. The results enable quantitative predictions for whether strange stars have crystalline crusts and whether strangelets in cosmic rays would exhibit a peaked size distribution.
