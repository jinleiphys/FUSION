# Generalized dipole polarizabilities and the spatial structure of hadrons

> We present a phenomenological discussion of spin-independent, generalized dipole polarizabilities of hadrons entering the virtual Compton scattering process gamma* h -> gamma h. We introduce a new method of obtaining a tensor basis with appropriate Lorentz-invariant amplitudes which are free from kinematical singularities and constraints. We then motivate a gauge-invariant separation into a generalized Born term containing ground-state properties only, and a residual contribution describing the model-dependent internal structure. The generalized dipole polarizabilities are defined in terms of Lorentz-invariant residual amplitudes. Particular emphasis is laid on a physical interpretation of these quantities as characterizing the spatial distributions of the induced electric polarization and magnetization of hadrons. It is argued that three dipole polarizabilities, namely the longitudinal electric alpha_L(q^2), the transverse electric alpha_T(q^2), and the magnetic beta(q^2) ones are required in order to fully reconstruct local polarizations induced by soft external fields in a hadron. One of these polarizabilities, alpha_T(q^2), describes an effect of higher order in the soft final-photon momentum q'. We argue that the associated spatial distributions obtained via the Fourier transforms in the Breit frame are meaningful even for such a light particle as the pion. The spatial distributions are determined at large distances r ~ 1/m_pi for pions, kaons, and octet baryons by use of ChPT.

## Key claim

The paper argues that three generalized dipole polarizabilities, longitudinal electric α_L(q^2), transverse electric α_T(q^2), and magnetic β(q^2), are required to fully reconstruct the local electric and magnetic polarizations induced in a hadron by soft external fields, with α_T(q^2) describing an effect of higher order in the soft final-photon momentum q' that was omitted in previous analyses.

## Method

The authors construct a new tensor basis for the virtual Compton scattering (VCS) amplitude γ* h → γ h using Lorentz-invariant amplitudes free from kinematical singularities and constraints, then perform a gauge-invariant separation into a generalized Born term (containing only ground-state properties) and a residual contribution describing internal structure. They define the generalized dipole polarizabilities from the Lorentz-invariant residual amplitudes and interpret them via Fourier transforms in the Breit frame to obtain spatial distributions of induced electric polarization and magnetization. For illustration, they use chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) predictions to determine these spatial distributions at large distances for pions, kaons, and octet baryons.

## Key numbers

- The first experimental results for generalized polarizabilities of the proton were obtained at MAMI for a four-momentum squared of Q^2 = 0.33 GeV^2 (Roche et al., 2000).
- The spatial distributions are determined at large distances r ~ 1/m_π for pions, kaons, and octet baryons using ChPT, where m_π is the pion mass.
- The fine-structure constant is given as e^2/(4π) ≈ 1/137.
- The determinant of the transformation between the two amplitude bases (A_i and B_i) is 1/32 ≠ 0, independent of the kinematical variables.

## Context

This work extends the framework of generalized polarizabilities introduced by Guichon et al. (1995) for parametrizing structure-dependent effects in VCS to leading order in the final-photon momentum q'. It provides a physical interpretation of these quantities as spatial distributions of induced polarization, which was only insufficiently covered in previous studies. The paper enables future work on sum rules and dispersion relations for generalized polarizabilities, and its results can be applied to spin-averaged VCS amplitudes for targets of arbitrary spin, as well as to the crossed reaction γγ → ππ.
