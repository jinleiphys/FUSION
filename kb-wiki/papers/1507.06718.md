# Covariance Analysis of Symmetry Energy Observables from Heavy Ion Collision

> Using covariance analysis, we quantify the correlations between the interaction parameters in a transport model and the observables commonly used to extract information of the Equation of State of Asymmetric Nuclear Matter in experiments. By simulating $^{124}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn, $^{124}$Sn+$^{112}$Sn and $^{112}$Sn+$^{112}$Sn reactions at beam energies of 50 and 120 MeV per nucleon, we have identified that the nucleon effective mass splitting are most strongly correlated to the neutrons and protons yield ratios with high kinetic energy from central collisions especially at high incident energy. The best observable to determine the slope of the symmetry energy, L, at saturation density is the isospin diffusion observable even though the correlation is not very strong ($\sim$0.7). Similar magnitude of correlation but opposite in sign exists for isospin diffusion and nucleon isoscalar effective mass. At 120 MeV/u, the effective mass splitting and the isoscalar effective mass also have opposite correlation for the double n/p and isoscaling p/p yield ratios. By combining data and simulations at different beam energies, it should be possible to place constraints on the slope of symmetry energy (L) and effective mass splitting with reasonable uncertainties.

## Key claim

Using covariance analysis with 12 parameter sets in a transport model, the authors quantify that the isospin diffusion observable has the strongest correlation (approximately 0.7) with the slope of the symmetry energy L at saturation density, but this correlation is explicitly described as "not very strong," and the authors note that no attempt was made to optimize interaction parameters to fit experimental observables, meaning the reported correlation coefficients carry systematic uncertainties from the chosen parameter ranges.

## Method

The authors used the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamic model with Skyrme interactions (ImQMD-Sky) to simulate collisions of 124Sn+124Sn, 124Sn+112Sn, and 112Sn+112Sn at beam energies of 50 and 120 MeV per nucleon. They constructed 12 parameter sets by separately varying five nuclear matter properties (K0, S0, L, m_s*, and f_I) and calculated linear correlation coefficients between these parameters and five coalescence-invariant observables: single n/p yield ratios, double n/p ratios, isoscaling n/n and p/p ratios, and the isospin diffusion ratio R_diff.

## Key numbers

- The correlation coefficient between the slope of symmetry energy L and the isospin diffusion observable R_diff is approximately 0.7 at both 50 and 120 MeV per nucleon.
- The correlation coefficient between the isoscalar effective mass m_s* and R_diff ranges from approximately -0.64 to -0.70.
- The correlation between the effective mass splitting parameter f_I and the single and double n/p yield ratios is larger than 0.8 at both incident energies.
- The average values of the parameter space used were: <K0>=242.5 MeV, <S0>=32 MeV, <L>=54.5 MeV, <m_s*/m>=0.7375, and <f_I>=-0.1835.
- Simulations were performed for impact parameters of 2 fm for nucleon yield observables and 6 fm for isospin diffusion calculations, with 10,000 events per system.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to constrain the symmetry energy from heavy ion collision data, such as the compilation of constraints by Tsang et al. (2012) and the BUU calculations by Chen et al. (2005) and Rizzo et al. (2008) that showed R_diff decreases with increasing m_s*. The covariance analysis approach is introduced as a first step toward a global chi-square analysis of transport model parameters, which is currently computationally prohibitive. The results enable future combined constraints on the slope of symmetry energy L and the effective mass splitting by using data at different beam energies, and suggest that the isospin diffusion observable and high-energy nucleon yield ratios can disentangle the effects of isoscalar effective mass and effective mass splitting.
