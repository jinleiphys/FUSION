# Global statistical model calculations and the role of isospin

> An improved code for the calculation of astrophysical reaction rates in the statistical model is presented. It includes the possibility to study isospin effects. Such effects heavily affect capture rates involving self-conjugate nuclei and may also be found in reactions on other intermediate and heavy targets.

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved statistical model code (NON-SMOKER) that explicitly includes isospin effects, which the abstract notes "heavily affect capture rates involving self-conjugate nuclei." The hedge is that while isospin effects are significant for self-conjugate nuclei and some intermediate/heavy targets, the authors also find that for lighter targets (Z ≤ 30) isospin mixing can be nearly complete at the neutron threshold, meaning isospin effects may be negligible in those cases.

## Method

The authors developed the NON-SMOKER code based on the earlier SMOKER code, implementing global optical potentials for neutrons, protons, and alpha particles, with deformed nuclei treated via an effective spherical potential. They explicitly included isobaric analog states (T^> = T^< + 1) and introduced an isospin mixing parameter μ↓ that determines the fraction of T^> state width leading to T^< transitions, using experimental excitation energies and spreading widths where available and a level density description above the last known T^> level. The code also incorporates updated experimental level information, nuclear masses, and a modified parity distribution treatment based on shell model Monte Carlo results.

## Key numbers

- For (α,γ) reactions on self-conjugate (N=Z) targets, cross sections are "heavily suppressed" because T=1 states cannot be populated due to isospin conservation; previous phenomenological treatments divided by factors of 5 for (α,γ) reactions and 2 for nucleon capture reactions
- In the ⁵¹V(p,γ)⁵²Cr experiment, complete isospin mixing (μ↓=1) closely reproduced measured cross sections when width fluctuation corrections were considered
- The first T^> state in ⁵²Cr lies almost 1 MeV below the neutron threshold, indicating isospin mixing should be rather complete already at that energy
- For reactions on nuclei with Z > 30, the neutron and proton thresholds lie in a region of incomplete isospin mixing, making isospin effects potentially detectable

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach of Thielemann (1987) and Cowan (1991) by replacing their phenomenological suppression factors (dividing γ-widths by 5 or 2) with a fundamental treatment of isospin via explicit T^< and T^> state accounting. The code enables more accurate astrophysical reaction rate calculations for network simulations, particularly for reactions involving self-conjugate nuclei and proton-induced reactions on heavier targets (Z > 30), while also identifying where isospin effects can be neglected (lighter targets with complete mixing).
