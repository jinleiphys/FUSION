# The phase diagram of a gauge theory with fermionic baryons

> The fermion-sign problem at finite density is a persisting challenge for Monte-Carlo simulations. Theories that do not have a sign problem can provide valuable guidance and insight for physically more relevant ones that do. Replacing the gauge group SU(3) of QCD by the exceptional group G2, for example, leads to such a theory. It has mesons as well as bosonic and fermionic baryons, and shares many features with QCD. This makes the G2 gauge theory ideally suited to study general properties of dense, strongly-interacting matter, including baryonic and nuclear Fermi pressure effects. Here we present the first-ever results from lattice simulations of G2 QCD with dynamical fermions, providing a first explorative look at the phase diagram of this QCD-like theory at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential.

# The phase diagram of a gauge theory with fermionic baryons

## Key claim
This paper presents the first lattice simulations of G₂ gauge theory with dynamical fermions, providing an exploratory phase diagram at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential. The authors caution that the results are preliminary and potentially affected by lattice artifacts, including a bulk transition and proximity to an unphysical Aoki phase, meaning the phase diagram requires further systematic investigation before firm conclusions can be drawn.

## Method
The authors performed Monte-Carlo simulations of G₂ gauge theory with a single flavor of dynamical Wilson fermions on lattices of size 5×16³, 6×16³, 8×16³, and 16⁴, using a local hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm. They measured the Polyakov loop, chiral condensate (normalized to its vacuum value), and baryon number density (normalized to saturation) as functions of temperature (controlled by lattice coupling β and number of time slices) and baryon chemical potential μ. The scale was set by fixing the critical temperature at μ=0 to 160 MeV, yielding lattice spacings between 0.181 fm and 0.25 fm.

## Key numbers
- The plaquette average at saturation density is 0.5647(1), compared to 0.5724(5) for the pure gauge theory.
- The first excited diquark mass varies from 2.3_{-4}^{+5} to 1.65^{+1}_{-5} in lattice units for β=0.9 to 1.0 at κ=0.15625.
- Lattice spacings range from 0.25^{+5}_{-4} fm to 0.181^{+1}_{-20} fm for β=0.9 to 1.0.
- At zero temperature, the Polyakov loop peaks at around μ≈1.5 GeV.
- The lightest diquark mass is around 300–400 MeV, placing the silver-blaze point at μ≈150–200 MeV.
- The onset of large density increase at T=0 occurs at μ≈1 GeV.
- Saturation corresponds to a maximum of 14 quarks per lattice site for one flavor in the 7-dimensional fundamental representation.

## Context
This work extends the study of QCD-like theories without a sign problem, following two-color QCD (Kogut 2000, Hands 2000) which has bosonic baryons, to a theory with fermionic baryons via the G₂ gauge group (Holland 2003). The G₂ theory shares many features with QCD including linear confinement and a similar hadronic spectrum, but avoids the sign problem due to its real color representations. The results enable future comparisons with functional methods and effective models, and may eventually allow a continuous deformation toward QCD via Higgs breaking (Holland 2003, Wellegehausen 2011). The phase diagram shows qualitative similarities to two-color QCD but with the crucial difference of fermionic baryons, making it relevant for understanding nuclear matter and compact stellar objects.
