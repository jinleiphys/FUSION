# Exploring the axion potential and axion walls in dense quark matter

> We study the potential of the Quantum Chromodynamics axion in hot and/or dense quark matter, within a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-like model that includes the coupling of the axion to quarks. Differently from previous studies, we implement local electrical neutrality and $β-$equilibrium, which are relevant for the description of the quark matter in the core of compact stellar objects. Firstly we compute the effects of the chiral crossover on the axion mass and self-coupling. We find that the low energy properties of axion are very sensitive to the phase transition of Quantum Chromodynamics, in particular, when the bulk quark matter is close to criticality. Then, for the first time in the literature we compute the axion potential at finite quark chemical potential and study the axion domain walls in bulk quark matter. We find that the energy barrier between two adjacent vacuum states decrease in the chirally restored phase: this results in a lower surface tension of the walls. Finally, we comment on the possibility of production of walls in dense quark matter.

# Exploring the axion potential and axion walls in dense quark matter

## Key claim

The axion mass drops and the axion self-coupling is enhanced near the QCD chiral phase transition, particularly when bulk quark matter is close to the critical endpoint; however, these results are obtained within a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-like model and depend on the implementation of local electrical neutrality and beta-equilibrium, which are relevant for compact stellar object cores.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that includes a U(1)_A-breaking term coupling the axion to quarks via instanton-induced interactions. They compute the thermodynamic potential at one-loop order, imposing local electrical neutrality and beta-equilibrium by fixing the electron chemical potential self-consistently, and solve the gap equations for the chiral condensate sigma and the pseudoscalar condensate eta as functions of temperature T and quark chemical potential mu. The axion mass and self-coupling are obtained from the second and fourth derivatives of the thermodynamic potential with respect to the axion field at a=0, and the axion domain wall profiles and surface tensions are computed from the full axion potential.

## Key numbers

- The critical endpoint is located at (mu_CP, T_CP) = (336 MeV, 79 MeV); at T=0 the critical chemical potential is mu_C = 393 MeV.
- At T=mu=0, m_a f_a = 6.38 x 10^3 MeV^2, corresponding to a topological susceptibility chi = (79 MeV)^4.
- At T=mu=0, the quartic self-coupling satisfies lambda_a f_a^4 = -(55.63 MeV)^4.
- The surface tension at T=mu=0 is kappa_0 = 1.9 x 10^16 MeV^3.
- For f_a = 10^9 GeV, the axion mass is approximately 6.4 meV in the chiral symmetry broken phase and approximately 2.4 meV in the chiral symmetry restored phase; for cold dense matter at mu=400 MeV and T=10 MeV, m_a is approximately 1.48 meV.
- The wall thickness in the chiral restored phase is of order 10^{-4} meters.

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model studies of axion properties (Lu 2018, Lopes 2022, Bandyopadhyay 2019, Das 2020, Mohapatra 2022) by implementing electrical neutrality and beta-equilibrium, making the results applicable to compact stellar object cores. It goes beyond Chiral Perturbation Theory (GrillidiCortona 2015), which cannot describe the QCD phase transition at finite temperature and density. The calculation of axion domain walls in dense quark matter and their surface tension is presented as a first in the literature, and the authors suggest that axion walls may form easily in bulk quark matter because the energy cost of adding a wall is zero in the thermodynamic limit, potentially enabling studies of axion structures in neutron star cores.
