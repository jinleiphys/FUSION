# Gauge symmetric delta(1232) couplings and the radiative muon capture in hydrogen

> Using the difference between the gauge symmetric and standard pi-N-delta couplings, a contact pi-pi-N-N term, quadratic in the pi-N-delta coupling, is explicitly constructed. Besides, a contribution from the delta excitation mechanism to the photon spectrum for the radiative muon capture in hydrogen is derived from the gauge symmetric pi-N-delta and gamma-N-delta couplings. It is shown for the photon spectrum, studied recently experimentally, that the new spectrum is for the photon momentums k > 60 MeV by 4-10 % smaller than the one obtained from standardly used couplings with the on-shell deltas.

# Gauge symmetric delta(1232) couplings and the radiative muon capture in hydrogen

## Key claim

The gauge symmetric pi-N-delta and gamma-N-delta couplings produce a photon spectrum for radiative muon capture in hydrogen that is 4-10% smaller than the spectrum obtained from standardly used couplings with on-shell deltas, for photon momenta k greater than 60 MeV. However, this suppression means the new couplings cannot resolve the "g_P puzzle" either.

## Method

The authors constructed a contact pi-pi-N-N term, quadratic in the pi-N-delta coupling, by using the algebraic difference between the gauge symmetric and standard pi-N-delta couplings. They then derived the delta excitation contribution to the photon spectrum for radiative muon capture in hydrogen using the gauge symmetric pi-N-delta and gamma-N-delta Lagrangians proposed by Pascalutsa and Timmermans, and compared the resulting spectrum to that obtained from traditional couplings with on-shell deltas.

## Key numbers

- The new photon spectrum is 4-10% smaller than the standard one for photon momenta k greater than 60 MeV
- The new form factor D g_3^{g.s.} differs from the on-shell form factor D g_3^0 by the factor (M/M_D)^2 approximately equal to 0.58
- The TRIUMF experimental spectrum is given as S_T = 0.061 S_s + 0.854 S_o + 0.085 S_p, where S_s, S_o, and S_p correspond to the muon-hydrogen singlet system and ortho- and paramolecular p mu p states respectively

## Context

This work extends the gauge symmetric delta coupling approach of Pascalutsa and Timmermans (2001) to the radiative muon capture process, which had previously been calculated by several authors including BHM (2000) using small scale expansion and heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, and by TK (2003) using Lagrangians with hidden local SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R symmetry. The result shows that the damping factor (M/M_D)^2 appears naturally in meson exchange current operators with delta excitation, which may help explain the Gamow-Teller matrix element for triton beta decay where a suppression factor of 0.8 was found to be needed by Schiavilla (1994), but the comparison with existing data on weak and electromagnetic reactions in few-nucleon systems does not allow a unique decision on whether this factor is needed.
