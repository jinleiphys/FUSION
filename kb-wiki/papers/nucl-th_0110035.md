# Centrality Dependence of Thermal Parameters in Heavy-Ion Collisions at CERN-SPS

> The centrality dependence of thermal parameters, characterizing the hadron multiplicities, is determined phenomenologically for lead-on-lead collisions at CERN-SPS for a beam energy of 158 AGeV. The strangeness equilibration factor shows a clear, approximately linear, increase with increasing centrality, while the freeze-out temperature and chemical potential remain constant.

# Centrality Dependence of Thermal Parameters in Heavy-Ion Collisions at CERN-SPS

## Key claim

The strangeness equilibration factor γ_s increases approximately linearly with centrality in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV, while the freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential remain constant across centrality classes, but this conclusion is based on a restricted data set (only π±, K±, and antiprotons) and the authors emphasize that the analysis focuses on trends rather than absolute parameter values.

## Method

The authors performed a statistical-thermal model fit to fully integrated hadron multiplicities (π±, K±, antiprotons) from the NA49 collaboration for six centrality bins of Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV beam energy. They used a grand-canonical ideal gas of all hadrons and resonances from the Particle Data Group, with Breit-Wigner resonance shapes and full decay chains, fitting four free parameters (temperature T, baryon chemical potential μ_B, strangeness suppression factor γ_s, and effective volume V_eff) via chi-square minimization with MINUIT. The number of participants N_part was identified with the baryon number of the hadron gas.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 158 AGeV for Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS
- Six centrality bins with mean impact parameters: 2.2 fm (bin I), 4.3 fm (bin II), 6.0 fm (bin III), 7.4 fm (bin IV), 8.9 fm (bin V), and 10.7 fm (bin VI)
- Temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ_B show no noticeable dependence on centrality, consistent with values from Becattini (2001) for central collisions
- The strangeness equilibration factor γ_s increases approximately linearly with centrality and then saturates
- The radius R of the hadron gas increases approximately linearly with N_part
- The Wroblewski factor λ_s increases nearly linearly with increasing centrality
- Energy per hadron ⟨E⟩/⟨N⟩, energy density, baryon density, and entropy per baryon S/B all remain fairly independent of centrality

## Context

This work extends the statistical-thermal model approach of Becattini (2001) and Heppe (1999) by analyzing the centrality dependence of thermal parameters, rather than only central collisions. The results contradict the possibility that freeze-out conditions change with system size at fixed beam energy, and enable predictions for other hadron yields (K⁰_s, φ, Ξ±, Λ, Λ̅) that could be tested by further NA49 data analyses. The paper builds on the observation that electromagnetic signals from central SPS collisions are well described by thermal fireball models, as shown by the authors' earlier work on electromagnetic signals.
