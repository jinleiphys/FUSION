# Multiquark contributions to charm baryon spectroscopy

> We study possible multiquark contributions to the charm baryon spectrum by considering higher order Fock space components. For this purpose we perform two different calculations. In a first approach we do a coupled-channel calculation of the $ND$ system looking for molecular states. In a second step we allow for the coupling to a heavy baryon--light meson two-hadron system looking for compact exotic five--quark structures. Both calculations have been done within the framework of a chiral constituent quark model. The model, tuned in the description of the baryon and meson spectra as well as the $NN$ interaction, provides parameter-free predictions for charm $+1$ molecular or compact two-hadron systems. Unlike the $N \bar D$ system, no sharp quark-Pauli effects are found. However, the existence of different two-hadron thresholds for the five-quark system will make the coupled-channel dynamics relevant. Only a few channels are candidates to lodge molecular or compact hadrons with a five-quark structure, being specially relevant the $(T)J^P=(0)1/2^-$ and $(T)J^P=(2)5/2^-$ channels. The identification of molecular states and/or compact hadrons with multiquark components either with or without exotic quantum numbers is a challenge of different collaborations like $\bar {\rm P}$ANDA, LHCb, ExHIC or J-PARC.

# Multiquark contributions to charm baryon spectroscopy

## Key claim

The authors use a chiral constituent quark model to predict that only a few specific channels in the charm +1 baryon-meson system are candidates to lodge molecular or compact five-quark states, with the $(T)J^P=(0)1/2^-$ and $(T)J^P=(2)5/2^-$ channels being especially relevant. Unlike the $N \bar D$ system, no sharp quark-Pauli effects are found, but the existence of different two-hadron thresholds for the five-quark system makes coupled-channel dynamics relevant.

## Method

The authors performed two calculations within a chiral constituent quark model that was previously tuned to describe baryon and meson spectra as well as the nucleon-nucleon interaction. First, they solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for negative energies using the Fredholm determinant method for a coupled-channel calculation of the $ND$ system (baryon $N$ or $\Delta$ with meson $D$ or $D^*$) to look for molecular states. Second, they allowed coupling to a heavy baryon-light meson two-hadron system (such as $\Sigma_c \pi$ or $\Lambda_c^+ \eta$) to search for compact exotic five-quark structures, using the same integral equation approach with all physical states contributing to each set of quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- The chiral coupling constant $g^2_{\rm ch}/4\pi$ and the scale-dependent strong coupling constant $\alpha_s(\mu)$ give $\alpha_s \sim 0.54$ for the light-quark sector and $\alpha_s \sim 0.43$ for $uc$ pairs.
- The $(T)J^P=(0)1/2^-$ channel is the most attractive for molecular $ND$ states, with a bound state of around 14.4 MeV reported in the hadronic model of Yamaguchi et al. (2013) for the model including only pion exchanges.
- The $(T)J^P=(2)5/2^-$ channel may correspond to the $\Theta_c(3250)$ pentaquark, associated with an unexplained structure at a mass of 3250 MeV/c$^2$ in the $\Sigma_c^{++} \pi^- \pi^-$ invariant mass reported by the BABAR Collaboration (Lee et al., 2012).
- The Fredholm determinant for the $(T)J^P=(0)1/2^-$ channel shows a bound state when only $ND$ channels are considered, but the bound state disappears when the lowest break apart threshold ($\Sigma_c\pi$) is included.

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model approach previously applied to the interaction between two charm mesons (Carames and Valcarce, 2009) and between anticharm mesons and nucleons (Carames and Valcarce, 2012) to the charm baryon sector. The results are compared to the hadronic model of Yamaguchi et al. (2013) for the $ND$ system, which used Lagrangians satisfying heavy quark symmetry and chiral symmetry and arrived at the same conclusion that the $(T)J^P=(0)1/2^-$ channel is the most attractive. The study enables future experimental searches by collaborations such as $\bar {\rm P}$ANDA, LHCb, ExHIC, and J-PARC for molecular states and compact hadrons with multiquark components, and suggests that the $\Sigma_c(2800)$ could be an $S$ wave $DN$ molecular state with $J^P=1/2^-$ and the $\Lambda_c(2940)^+$ an $S$ wave $D^*N$ state with $J^P=3/2^-$, as proposed by Zhang et al. (2014).
