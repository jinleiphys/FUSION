# Recent Developments in Few-Nucleon Systems

> N-d elastic scattering is studied at different energies using one of the modern NN interactions, the Argonne v_{18} which explicitly includes the magnetic moment interaction between two nucleons. This interaction, which has been often neglected in the description of the few-nucleon continuum, produces sizable modifications in some elastic observables. Its effects, as well as those produced by the Coulomb potential, are analyzed as a function of energy. The magnetic moment interaction produces appreciable effects in $p-d$ scattering at low energies butthey are very small above 10 MeV. Above 65 MeV Coulomb effects can be observed only in specific observables as for example $T_{21}$.

## Key claim

The magnetic moment (MM) interaction between two nucleons, often neglected in few-nucleon continuum calculations, produces sizable modifications in some elastic observables for N-d scattering, but its effects are energy-dependent: appreciable in p-d scattering at low energies but very small above 10 MeV, while Coulomb effects above 65 MeV can be observed only in specific observables such as T_{21}.

## Method

The authors solved the three-nucleon scattering problem using the complex Kohn variational principle with an expansion of the three-nucleon scattering wave function in a pair correlated hyperspherical harmonic basis. They employed the Argonne v_{18} (AV18) NN potential, including its nuclear, Coulomb, and magnetic moment interactions, and for some calculations added the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential. The transition matrix was constructed by splitting the partial-wave sums into a low-L part from a full three-body calculation and a high-L part treated in Born approximation using the N-d magnetic moment interaction, with the slowly converging high-L series summed analytically.

## Key numbers

- For n-d scattering at E_n = 1.2 MeV and 1.9 MeV, the nuclear part of the transition matrix converges already for L_{max} = 3.
- For p-d scattering, the partial-wave series was summed up to L_{max} = 4 at E_p = 1 MeV and L_{max} = 6 at E_p = 3 MeV.
- The MM interaction produces appreciable effects in p-d scattering at low energies but effects are very small above E_lab = 10 MeV.
- At E_lab = 65 MeV, Coulomb effects are very reduced in most elastic observables with the exception of T_{21} where an appreciable effect is observed in the peak.
- The relative difference between experimental and theoretical A_y at the peak for both n-d and p-d scattering is around 25% in the energy interval below 16 MeV when the MM interaction is included; at 18 MeV the difference is reduced to 20%.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of N-d elastic scattering that used AV18 with and without three-body forces (Brune, Wood, Kievsky, and collaborators, various years) but neglected the magnetic moment interaction. The authors show that the MM interaction produces modifications of similar size to those from three-nucleon interactions like the Urbana IX potential, meaning that complete electromagnetic NN potentials must be included when using three-nucleon continuum data to study the three-nucleon interaction. The paper also clarifies the energy-dependent role of Coulomb effects, showing they remain important in T_{21} even at 65 MeV, where earlier work by Walter and others had sometimes used n-d calculations to describe p-d scattering.
