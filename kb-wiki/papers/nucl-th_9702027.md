# Fixed-Velocity Chiral Sum Rules for Nuclear Matter

> Infinite sets of sum rules involving the excitations of infinite nuclear matter are derived using only completeness, the current algebra implicit in QCD, and relativistic covariance. The sum rules can be used for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, including neutron matter. They relate the chiral condensate and the isospin density to weighted sums over states with fixed velocity relative to the nuclear matter ground state.

# Fixed-Velocity Chiral Sum Rules for Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

The authors derive infinite sets of sum rules for infinite nuclear matter that relate the chiral condensate and isospin density to weighted sums over excited states with a fixed velocity relative to the nuclear matter ground state, using only completeness, current algebra, and relativistic covariance. Unlike previous zero-momentum sum rules, these hold for any value of the quark mass and provide information about the momentum dependence of excitations, though they do not constrain modes with phase velocity greater than the speed of light.

## Method

The authors start from the QCD current algebra identities involving double commutators of axial charges with the Hamiltonian and the commutator of an axial charge with an axial current component. They sandwich these identities between states of translationally invariant nuclear matter moving with constant velocity, insert a complete set of covariantly normalized intermediate states, and then boost to the rest frame of the medium. This yields sum rules where the spectral functions are evaluated along lines of fixed velocity \(\beta = |\vec{q}|/E\) in the \((\omega, |\vec{q}|)\) plane, with the integration variable being the excitation energy \(E\).

## Key numbers

- The sum rules relate the chiral condensate \(- m_u \langle \overline{u}u \rangle_C - m_d \langle \overline{d}d \rangle_C\) to an integral over the neutral spectral function \(\sigma^0_C(E, \vec{q}=\vec{\beta}E)\) weighted by \(1/E\).
- The isospin density \(\rho_{I=1}\) is related to the difference of positive and negative isospin spectral functions via \(-\rho_{I=1} = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{dE}{E^2} \left( \sigma^+_C(E, \vec{q}=\vec{\beta}E) - \sigma^-_C(E, \vec{q}=\vec{\beta}E) \right)\).
- In a toy model with \(\rho\)-meson exchange and the KSFR relation \(2 g_\rho^2 = m_\rho^2/F_\pi^2\), the charged pion propagator is \(G^\pm(q_0, \vec{q}) = q_0^2 \mp \rho_{I=1}/F_\pi^2 q_0 - \vec{q}^2 - m_\pi^2\), and the excitation energies are \(E^\pm(\vec{q}) = \pm \rho_{I=1}/(2 F_\pi^2) + \sqrt{(\rho_{I=1}/(2 F_\pi^2))^2 + m_\pi^2 + \vec{q}^2}\).
- As \(\beta \to 1\), the positive isospin mode saturates both sum rules (II) and (III) in the toy model.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-momentum sum rules (Cohen and Broniowski, 1996) and related work by Lutz, Steiner and Weise (1993) by exploiting relativistic covariance to obtain sum rules sensitive to nonzero momentum excitations. The sum rules reduce to the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation in the vacuum limit and are exact for any quark mass, making them applicable to kaonic excitations in dense matter relevant to kaon condensation (Kaplan and Nelson, 1986; Brown et al., 1992). The results provide constraints for model builders, particularly for testing whether approximations that include short-range correlations and form factors violate chiral Ward identities.
