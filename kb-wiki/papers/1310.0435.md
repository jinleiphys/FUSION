# Polarization corrections to single-particle energies studied within the energy-density-functional and QRPA approaches

> Background: Models based on using perturbative polarization corrections and mean-field blocking approximation give conflicting results for masses of odd nuclei. Purpose: Systematically investigate the polarization and mean-field models, implemented within self-consistent approaches that use identical interactions and model spaces, so as to find reasons for the conflicts between them. Methods: For density-dependent interactions and with pairing correlations included, we derive and study links between the mean-field and polarization results obtained for energies of odd nuclei. We also identify and discuss differences between the polarization-correction and full particle-vibration-coupling (PVC) models. Numerical calculations are performed for the mean-field ground-state properties of deformed odd nuclei and then compared to the polarization corrections determined by using the approach that conserves spherical symmetry. Results: We have identified and numerically evaluated self-interaction (SI) energies that are at the origin of different results obtained within the mean-field and polarization-correction approaches. Conclusions: Mean-field energies of odd nuclei are polluted by the SI energies, and this makes them different from those obtained by using polarization-correction methods. A comparison of both approaches allows for the identification and determination of the SI terms, which then can be calculated and removed from the mean-field results, giving the self-interaction-free energies. The simplest deformed mean-field approach that does not break parity symmetry is unable to reproduce full PVC effects.

## Key claim

Mean-field energies of odd nuclei are polluted by self-interaction (SI) energies that arise from density-dependent interactions, and these SI terms make mean-field results differ from those obtained using polarization-correction methods; the simplest deformed mean-field approach that does not break parity symmetry cannot reproduce full particle-vibration-coupling (PVC) effects.

## Method

The authors derived and studied links between mean-field and polarization results for energies of odd nuclei using density-dependent interactions with pairing correlations included, within the Hartree-Fock (HF) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) frameworks. They performed numerical calculations for mean-field ground-state properties of deformed odd nuclei and compared them to polarization corrections determined using an approach that conserves spherical symmetry, using the same particle-hole energy-density functionals (EDFs), pairing interactions, and model spaces in both the mean-field and (Q)RPA calculations.

## Key numbers

- Self-interaction (SI) energies were identified and numerically evaluated as the origin of different results between mean-field and polarization-correction approaches; the SI term is given by \(E^\lambda_{\text{SI}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\tilde{\tilde{h}}^\lambda_{\lambda\lambda}\) (Eq. 88).
- The polarization correction to single-particle energies is expressed as \(\delta e_\lambda = \mp \sum_{\omega>0} \frac{|\sum_{ph} h^{\lambda*}_{ph} X_{ph}^\omega + h^\lambda_{ph} Y_{ph}^\omega|^2}{\hbar\omega}\) (Eq. 117), where \(\hbar\omega\) are positive RPA eigenvalues.
- For density-dependent interactions, the polarization correction to single-particle energies takes the form \(\delta e_\lambda = \pm (\delta E^\lambda_{\text{SIF}} + E^\lambda_{\text{SI}})\) (Eq. 68), where \(\delta E^\lambda_{\text{SIF}}\) is the self-interaction-free polarization correction.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing discussion of the link between mean-field and perturbative methods for odd nuclei, originally proposed by Brown (1970), Blaizot (1986), and Lipkin (1987, 1989). It directly addresses the discrepancy between blocked mean-field calculations and perturbative PVC calculations noted in the review by Colò (2010) and Indelicato (2012). The results enable the identification and removal of self-interaction terms from mean-field calculations, providing a path toward self-interaction-free energies for odd nuclei and clarifying the limitations of deformed mean-field approaches that do not break parity symmetry.
