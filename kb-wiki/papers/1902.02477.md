# Heavy flavour Langevin diffusion with the chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations in the quark-gluon plasma

> The chromo-electromagnetic field is produced due to the motion of partons in a quark-gluon plasma created by relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The fluctuations in the produced chromo-electromagnetic field are important, since they cause heavy quarks to gain energy in the low velocity limit. We study the effect of such fluctuations on heavy quark diffusion in quark-gluon plasma within the framework of Langevin dynamics under the background matter described by the ($3+1$)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamics. Theoretical calculations of the nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) of heavy mesons ($D$ and $B$ mesons), with the effect of these fluctuations, are compared with experimental measurements in $Au-Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV by the STAR experiment at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and $Pb-Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ and $5.02$ TeV by the ALICE and CMS experiments at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC). We find a significant effect of these fluctuations in describing the the measured $R_{AA}$ of $D$ and $B$ mesons in both RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations in the quark-gluon plasma cause heavy quarks to gain energy in the low velocity limit, and including this effect in Langevin simulations significantly improves the description of the measured nuclear modification factor of D and B mesons at lower transverse momentum, particularly at LHC energies, though the effect is less pronounced at RHIC energy.

## Method

The authors solve the Langevin equation for heavy quark diffusion in a quark-gluon plasma background described by (3+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamics using the vHLLE code. The drag coefficients for charm and bottom quarks are estimated from collisional energy loss (using the Peigne-Pashier derivation) and radiative energy loss (using a generalized dead cone approach), and the leading-log contribution of chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations is added as an energy gain term. Heavy quarks are initially produced at leading order with EPS09 nuclear parton distribution functions, evolved through the medium until the temperature drops to 150 MeV, and then hadronized via the Peterson fragmentation function to obtain D and B meson spectra.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to 1.25 GeV; bottom quark mass set to 4.2 GeV
- Hydrodynamic evolution starts at initial time tau_0 = 0.6 fm with critical temperature T_c = 150 MeV
- Shear viscosity eta/s = 0.08 and bulk viscosity zeta/s = 0.04 used in the hadronic phase
- Peterson fragmentation parameters: epsilon_c = 0.016 for c quarks to D mesons; epsilon_b = 0.0012 for b quarks to B mesons
- Calculations compared with Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (STAR), and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV (ALICE and CMS)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma by incorporating chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations, which were previously considered only with simple 1D expansion models by the same group (Sheikh and Ahammed, 2018). It builds on the Langevin framework used extensively by Moore (2005), Hees (2008), Cao (2013), and others, and uses the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics code vHLLE developed by Karpenko (2014). The results enable more precise comparisons between theoretical calculations and experimental measurements of heavy meson suppression at both RHIC and LHC energies, particularly in the low transverse momentum region where fluctuations have the largest effect.
