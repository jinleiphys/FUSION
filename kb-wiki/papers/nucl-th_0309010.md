# Pairing effect on the giant dipole resonance width at low temperature

> The width of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) at finite temperature T in Sn-120 is calculated within the Phonon Damping Model including the neutron thermal pairing gap determined from the modified BCS theory. It is shown that the effect of thermal pairing causes a smaller GDR width at T below 2 MeV as compared to the one obtained neglecting pairing. This improves significantly the agreement between theory and experiment including the most recent data point at T = 1 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a thermal pairing gap from modified-BCS theory (which does not vanish at the BCS critical temperature) reduces the calculated giant dipole resonance (GDR) width in Sn-120 at temperatures below 2 MeV, significantly improving agreement with experimental data including the most recent point at T = 1 MeV. The hedge is that this improvement holds specifically at low temperatures (T < 2.5 MeV), where pairing effects are significant, while at higher temperatures the results with and without pairing nearly coincide.

## Method

The authors incorporated the neutron thermal pairing gap from modified-BCS theory into the Phonon Damping Model (PDM) for Sn-120. The GDR width was calculated as the sum of a quantal width (from coupling to particle-hole configurations) and a thermal width (from coupling to particle-particle and hole-hole configurations), using quasiparticle energies and occupation numbers derived from the modified-BCS equations with Woods-Saxon single-particle energies spanning 7 major shells. The three temperature-independent PDM parameters (unperturbed phonon energy, and coupling strengths F1 and F2) were fixed by reproducing the ground-state GDR energy and width, then calculations were performed for temperatures from 0 to 5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The modified-BCS pairing gap at T=0 is approximately 1.4 MeV, obtained with pairing parameter G_nu = 0.13 MeV
- The conventional BCS gap collapses at T_c approximately 0.79 MeV, while the modified-BCS gap persists up to approximately 5 MeV
- At T = 0.1 MeV with parameter set A, the GDR energy drops to 14 MeV (1.4 MeV below the ground-state value) and the width increases to 5.3 MeV (compared to 4.9 MeV at ground state)
- With parameter set B, the GDR energy at T = 0.1 MeV is 16.6 MeV, agreeing with the experimental value of 16.5 +/- 0.7 MeV extracted at T = 1 MeV
- The GDR width at T = 1 MeV from the calculation with renormalized gap and parameter set B is approximately 4 MeV, matching the experimental value from Heckman (2003)
- The quantal width at T=0 is 4.9 MeV, decreasing to approximately 2.5 MeV at T = 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Phonon Damping Model (PDM) developed by Dang and Arima (PDM1, PDM2, PDM3) by including thermal pairing effects that were previously neglected in hot GDR calculations. It addresses the discrepancy noted by Heckman (2003) that the narrow GDR width observed in Sn-120 at low temperature was not understood. The modified-BCS theory used here (MBCS, MBCS1) extends earlier work by Moretto (showing thermal fluctuations smear out the superfluid-normal phase transition) and the modified Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory (MHFB). The results contradict predictions of the thermal shape-fluctuation model (TFM), which significantly overestimates the GDR width at T < 1.3 MeV. This work enables future inclusion of thermal pairing in other models (such as the thermal shape-fluctuation model) and suggests that CASCADE calculations using PDM strength functions with pairing should be performed for direct comparison with experimental divided spectra.
