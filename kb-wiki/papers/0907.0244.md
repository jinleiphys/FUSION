# Binding of D, D-bar and J/Psi mesons in nuclei

> We estimate the $D$, $\overline{D}$ and $J/╬©$ meson potentials in nuclei. $J/╬©$-nuclear potential (self-energy) is calculated based on the color-singlet mechanism, by the $DD$ and $DD^*$ meson loops. This consistently includes the in-medium properties of $D$ and $D^*$ mesons. The potential obtained for the $J/╬©$ meson is attractive, and it is complementary to the attraction obtained from the color-octet gluon-based mechanism. We predict that the $D^-$ and $J/╬©$ mesons will be bound in nuclei under proper conditions.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the $D^-$ and $J/\Psi$ mesons will be bound in nuclei under proper conditions, but the $J/\Psi$ binding relies on combining the color-singlet mechanism attraction (estimated here) with the color-octet gluon-based attraction and an additional expected attraction from the $D^*D^*$ loop, meaning the prediction is conditional on the sum of multiple contributions.

## Method

The in-medium properties of $D$ and $\overline{D}$ mesons in symmetric nuclear matter and in a Pb nucleus were calculated using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, a quark-based relativistic mean field model. The $J/\Psi$-nuclear potential (self-energy) was then calculated based on the color-singlet mechanism, using $DD$ and $DD^*$ meson loops with dipole form factors, consistently including the in-medium properties of the $D$ and $D^*$ mesons obtained from the QMC model.

## Key numbers

- The $D^-$ meson single-particle energy in $^{208}$Pb for the 1s state is -10.6 MeV (with $\tilde{V}^q_\omega$ vector potential) and -35.2 MeV (with $V^q_\omega$ vector potential).
- The $D^-$ meson single-particle energy in $^{208}$Pb for the 1p state is -10.2 MeV (with $\tilde{V}^q_\omega$) and -32.1 MeV (with $V^q_\omega$).
- The $D^-$ meson single-particle energy in $^{208}$Pb for the 2s state is -7.7 MeV (with $\tilde{V}^q_\omega$) and -30.0 MeV (with $V^q_\omega$).
- The $D^0$ meson single-particle energy in $^{208}$Pb for the 1s state is -96.2 MeV (with $V^q_\omega$).
- The $J/\Psi$ potential in symmetric nuclear matter at normal nuclear matter density is about 7 MeV attraction (with cut-off 1500 MeV) and about 9 MeV attraction (with cut-off 2000 MeV).
- The coupling constants used for the $J/\Psi$-$D$-$D$ and $J/\Psi$-$D$-$D^*$ vertexes are $g_{J/\Psi DD} = g_{J/\Psi DD^*} = 7.7$.

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, previously applied to finite nuclei and hadron properties in a nuclear medium by Guichon and others, to charmed mesons and charmonium. It builds on prior QMC studies of $D$ and $\overline{D}$ mesons in symmetric nuclear matter and nuclei (Tsushima, 2004; Tsushima, 2005) and on earlier estimates of the $J/\Psi$ potential based on gluon-induced mechanisms such as the effective field theory approach (Luke, 1992), QCD Stark effect (Lee, 1994), and chromo-polarizability (Voloshin, 1999). The results enable experimental searches for $D^-$-nuclear and $J/\Psi$-nuclear bound states at future facilities like JLab after the 12 GeV upgrade, J-PARC, and FAIR.
