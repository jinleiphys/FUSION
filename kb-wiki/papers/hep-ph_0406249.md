# Pairing in Many-Fermion Systems: An Exact Renormalisation Group Treatment

> We study the application of the exact renormalisation group to a many-fermion system with a short-range attractive force. We introduce a boson field to describe pairing effects, and take a simple ansatz for the effective action. We derive a set of approximate flow equations for the effective coupling including boson and fermionic fluctuations. The initial conditions are obtained by renormalising the interaction to fit the scattering length in vacuum. At some critical value of the running scale, the numerical solutions show a phase transition to a gapped phase. Standard results are recovered if we omit the boson loops. When boson fluctuations are included, we find that their contributions are significant only in the small-gap regime.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an exact renormalisation group (ERG) approach to pairing in many-fermion systems can recover standard mean-field BCS results when boson loops are omitted, but that including boson fluctuations produces significant corrections only in the small-gap regime, with effects on the gap itself being at most ~1% for the parameter range studied.

## Method

The authors introduce a boson field via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to describe Cooper pairing in a many-fermion system with a short-range attractive contact interaction. They derive approximate flow equations for the effective action parameters (potential coefficients, wave-function renormalisations) using a one-loop ERG equation, truncating the potential to quartic order in the boson field. The initial conditions are set by matching to vacuum scattering length, and the evolution is run from a high momentum scale down to k=0 at fixed fermion density, allowing the chemical potential to run.

## Key numbers

- Critical running scale for infinite scattering length: k_crit ≈ 1.2 fm⁻¹ (for p_F = 1.37 fm⁻¹)
- Boson loop effects on the gap are at most ~1% for 1/|p_F a_0| < 1
- Starting scale K > 5 fm⁻¹ (about 4p_F) gives results independent of K
- For neutron matter parameters: M = 4.76 fm⁻¹, p_F = 1.37 fm⁻¹, with |a_0| ≳ 1 fm
- Weak-coupling gap formula: Δ = (8/e²) ε_F exp(-π/(2 p_F |a_0|)) for p_F a_0 → 0⁻
- Gap in neutron matter from realistic interactions is at most 5 MeV (~0.1 ε_F), while the current calculation gives a gap comparable to ε_F (~30 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to pairing pioneered by Papenbrock and Bertsch (1999) and the BCS-BEC crossover studies of Marani et al. (1998), using the exact renormalisation group framework developed by Wetterich (1993) and applied to condensed matter by Tetradis and Wetterich (1994) and to particle physics by Jungnickel and Wetterich (1996). The method enables systematic inclusion of many-body effects beyond mean field, and the authors identify that future work should incorporate effective range corrections (to capture the suppression seen in neutron matter calculations by Dean, Hjorth-Jensen, and others) and treatment of particle-hole channels to remove the Fierz ambiguity.
