# Charmed mesons in nuclear matter

> We obtain the properties of charmed mesons in dense matter using a coupled-channel approach which accounts for Pauli blocking effects and meson self-energies in a self-consistent manner. We study the behaviour of dynamically-generated baryonic resonances together with the open-charm meson spectral functions in this dense nuclear environment. We discuss the implications of the in-medium properties of open-charm mesons on the $D_{s0}(2317)$ and the predicted X(3700) scalar resonances, and on the formation of $D$-mesic nuclei.

## Key claim

The in-medium properties of open-charm mesons are obtained using a self-consistent coupled-channel approach that accounts for Pauli blocking and meson self-energies, but the actual results depend strongly on whether the interaction model respects heavy-quark symmetry (SU(8) scheme) or uses a simpler SU(4) t-vector meson exchange model, with the SU(8) scheme generating a richer spectrum of baryonic resonances and narrower widths that make D-mesic nuclei feasible.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels for D and D* mesons interacting with nucleons, using effective Lagrangians derived from either a broken SU(4) Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction or an SU(8) spin-flavor symmetric interaction that incorporates heavy-quark symmetry. The meson self-energy is obtained by summing the in-medium T-matrix over the nucleon Fermi distribution at finite temperature, and the spectral function is computed from the imaginary part of the self-energy. The resulting in-medium D-meson self-energy is then used as input to study the modification of dynamically-generated scalar resonances (D_s0(2317) and X(3700)) and to solve the Klein-Gordon equation for D^0-nucleus bound states.

## Key numbers

- The D_s0(2317) resonance develops a width of approximately 100 MeV at normal nuclear matter density (rho_0), compared to its near-zero width in vacuum.
- The X(3700) hidden charm scalar resonance develops a width of approximately 200 MeV at normal nuclear matter density.
- For D^0-nucleus bound states in ^208Pb, the 1s state has binding energy B = -11.54 MeV and width -Gamma/2 = -2.89 MeV.
- For D^0-nucleus bound states in ^12C, the 1s state has binding energy B = -8.51 MeV and width -Gamma/2 = -4.64 MeV.
- For D^0-nucleus bound states in ^40Ca, the 1s state has binding energy B = -11.12 MeV and width -Gamma/2 = -3.86 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of open-charm mesons in nuclear matter by Tolos et al. (2007) using SU(4) models, and by Tsushima et al. (1999) using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model which predicted much stronger binding for D-mesic nuclei. The self-consistent treatment with heavy-quark symmetry (SU(8) scheme) generates a wider spectrum of charmed baryonic resonances (including J=3/2 states such as Lambda_c(2660), Lambda_c(2941), Sigma_c(2554), and Sigma_c(2902)) compared to SU(4) models. The results enable future experimental searches for D-mesic nuclei at FAIR/GSI and suggest using transparency ratios to measure the in-medium widths of scalar resonances.
