# Enhanced collectivity of gamma vibration in neutron-rich Dy isotopes with N=108 - 110

> Background: The $γ$ vibrational mode of excitation is an acknowledged collective mode in deformed nuclei. The collectivity depends on the details of the shell structure around the Fermi levels, in particular the presence of the orbitals that have the enhanced transition matrix elements of the non-axial quadrupole excitation. Quite recently, a sudden decrease in the excitation energy of the $γ$ vibration was observed at RIKEN RIBF for the neutron-rich Dy isotopes at $N=106$. Purpose: In the present work, by studying systematically the microscopic structure of the $γ$ vibration in the neutron-rich Dy isotopes with $N=98-114$, we try to understand the mechanism of the observed softening. Methods: The low-frequency modes of excitation in the neutron-rich rare-earth nuclei are described based on nuclear density-functional theory. We employ the Skyrme energy-density functionals (EDF) in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation for the ground states and in the Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA) for the excitations. Results: The lowering of the excitation energy around $N=106$ is reproduced well by employing the SkM* and SLy4 functionals. We find the similar isotopic dependence of the excitation energy in the neutron-rich Er and Yb isotopes as well. Conclusions: The microscopic framework of the Skyrme-EDF based QRPA describes well the isotopic dependence of the energy of the $γ$ vibration in the well-deformed neutron-rich rare-earth nuclei. The strong collectivity at $N=108-110$ is expected as the Fermi level of neutrons lies just among the orbitals that play an important role in generating the collectivity around $N=106$.

# Enhanced collectivity of gamma vibration in neutron-rich Dy isotopes with N=108 - 110

## Key claim

The Skyrme-EDF based QRPA reproduces the observed lowering of the gamma-vibration excitation energy at N=106 in neutron-rich Dy isotopes, and predicts even stronger collectivity at N=108-110 where the neutron Fermi level lies among the orbitals that generate the collectivity, though the RPA overestimates the collectivity at these neutron numbers due to the small-amplitude approximation.

## Method

The authors performed axially deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations in cylindrical-coordinate space using the SkM* and SLy4 Skyrme energy-density functionals, with volume-type pairing. They then solved the Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA) in the matrix formulation on top of the HFB ground states to describe the K-pi = 2+ gamma-vibrational mode in neutron-rich Dy, Er, and Yb isotopes with N=98-114.

## Key numbers

- The matter axial-deformation parameter beta for Dy isotopes with N=98-114 ranges from 0.26 to 0.33 using the SkM* functional
- The QRPA frequency drops to a few hundred keV at N=108-110 with the SkM* functional
- The sum of backward-going RPA amplitude is 1.90 for N=108 and 2.59 for N=110, indicating overestimation of collectivity
- The electric reduced probabilities calculated with SkM* and SLy4 are about 10-20 Weisskopf units (W.u.) except for N=106-110 with SkM*
- The neutron transition strength obtained with SkM* in 174Dy is 7670 fm^4 and in 176Dy is 10830 fm^4
- The 2qp excitation amplitude of the pi[411]1/2 ⊗ pi[411]3/2 configuration in 164Dy is 0.20, and the pi[411]1/2 ⊗ pi[413]5/2 amplitude is 0.16
- The nu[510]1/2 ⊗ nu[512]5/2 excitation amplitude in 170Dy is 0.25
- The nu[512]3/2 ⊗ nu[510]1/2 and nu[512]3/2 ⊗ nu[514]7/2 excitations in 172Dy contribute with amplitudes of 0.10 and 0.06 respectively

## Context

This work extends the systematic Skyrme-EDF QRPA study of gamma vibrations in rare-earth nuclei by Terasaki and collaborators (2011) to the neutron-rich side beyond N=106. It explains the sudden decrease in gamma-vibration energy observed at RIKEN RIBF for 172Dy (N=106) by Watanabe and collaborators (2016). The finding that similar enhanced collectivity appears in neighboring Er and Yb isotopes at N=108-110 provides predictions for future experimental studies at RIBF and other facilities, and suggests that large-amplitude collective motion methods may be needed for higher-Z nuclei where deformation is weaker.
