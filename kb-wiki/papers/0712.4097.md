# Ambiguities of neutrino(antineutrino) scattering on the nucleon due to the uncertainties of relevant strangeness form factors

> Strange quark contributions to neutrino(antineutrino) scattering are investigated on the nucleon level in the quasi-elastic region. The incident energy range between 500 MeV and 1.0 GeV is used for the scattering. All of the physical observable by the scattering are investigated within available experimental and theoretical results for the strangeness form factors of the nucleon. In specific, a newly combined data of parity violating electron scattering and neutrino scattering is exploited. Feasible quantities to be explored for the strangeness contents are discussed for the application to neutrino-nucleus scattering.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a newly combined data set from parity-violating electron scattering and neutrino scattering constrains the strangeness form factors of the nucleon, but that significant ambiguities remain in neutrino(antineutrino) scattering observables due to the uncertainties in these form factors. Crucially, the authors find that the strange magnetic moment \(\mu_s = G_M^s(0)\) must be positive according to the new data, contradicting the negative values previously used in neutrino-nucleus scattering calculations.

## Method

The authors calculated neutral-current (NC) and charged-current (CC) neutrino and antineutrino scattering cross sections on free nucleons in the quasi-elastic region (incident energies 500 MeV to 1.0 GeV), using a standard dipole parameterization for the strangeness vector and axial-vector form factors. They adjusted the parameters \(F_1^s\), \(\mu_s\), and \(g_A^s\) to fit the combined experimental data set of Pate (2006), which includes parity-violating electron scattering results from HAPPEX (Aniol 2004) and G0 (Armstrong 2005) together with BNL neutrino scattering data (Ahrens 1987). They then computed differential cross sections, cross-section ratios, and various asymmetries (between neutrino and antineutrino, and between NC and CC channels) as functions of \(Q^2\) to identify observables most sensitive to strangeness.

## Key numbers

* The strange magnetic moment \(\mu_s = G_M^s(0)\) is constrained to be positive, in the range \(0.0 \sim 0.4\), contradicting previous negative values (e.g., \(-0.40\) used in earlier calculations).
* The strange axial coupling constant \(g_A^s\) is constrained to the range \(-0.21 \sim 0.0\).
* The parameter \(F_1^s\) (related to the strangeness charge radius) is constrained to \(0 \sim 0.53\).
* Ambiguities in cross sections due to \(F_1^s\), \(\mu_s\), and \(g_A^s\) are at most 3%, 5%, and 15%, respectively.
* The asymmetry \(A_{NC}\) on the proton is maximally decreased by about 15% in the region \(Q^2 \sim 0.6\) GeV\(^2\) due to \(g_A^s\).
* The BNL flux-averaged ratio \(R_{\bar{\nu}/\nu}^{BNL} = \langle \sigma(\bar{\nu}p \rightarrow \bar{\nu}p) \rangle / \langle \sigma(\nu p \rightarrow \nu p) \rangle\) is about 0.32, corresponding to a flux-averaged asymmetry \(\langle A_{NC} \rangle\) of 0.5 on the nucleon level.
* CC cross sections are about 4 to 6 times larger than NC cross sections.
* Change of the axial mass \(M_A\) (between 1.026 and 1.032 GeV) affects results within only about 1%.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of strangeness in the nucleon by Garvey (1993), Alberico (2002), and others, which relied primarily on the single BNL neutrino scattering data set. The authors exploit the combined data set of Pate (2006) that incorporates parity-violating electron scattering results from HAPPEX (Aniol 2004) and G0 (Armstrong 2005), providing tighter constraints on the strangeness form factors. The paper enables more reliable calculations of neutrino-nucleus scattering for applications in neutrino oscillation experiments and supernova physics, and identifies the asymmetry between NC and CC scattering as a promising observable for determining the \(Q^2\) dependence of strangeness form factors. The authors note that their preliminary nuclear calculations show strong cancellation of \(g_A^s\) effects in nuclei.
