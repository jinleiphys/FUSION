# Description of the superdeformed band of $^{36}$Ar with the Gogny force

> The superdeformed band of $^{36}$Ar is studied with the Gogny force D1S and the angular momentum projected generator coordinate method for the quadrupole moment. The band head excitation energy, moments of inertia, $B(E2)$ transition probabilities and stability against quadrupole fluctuations at low spin are studied. The Self Consistent Cranking method is also used to describe the superdeformed rotational band. In addition, properties of some normal deformed states are discussed.

## Key claim

The superdeformed band of \(^{36}\)Ar is stable against quadrupole fluctuations at low spin despite the very shallow depth (only 67 keV) of the superdeformed minimum in the mean-field energy landscape, but the calculated band-head excitation energy of 7.086 MeV (including basis-size correction) is far from the experimental value of 4.33 MeV, a discrepancy that also occurs with the Skyrme SLy6 interaction.

## Method

The authors used the Gogny force D1S with the Angular Momentum Projected Generator Coordinate Method (AMP-GCM) for the axial quadrupole moment, restricted to \(K=0\) configurations, to describe the superdeformed band of \(^{36}\)Ar. Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wave functions were generated in an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis with 10 major shells (220 states), and the Hill-Wheeler equation was solved for each angular momentum. Self Consistent Cranking (SCC) calculations were also performed to include triaxiality effects.

## Key numbers

- HFB superdeformed minimum at \(q_{20}=1.4\) b (\(\beta_2=0.52\)) with excitation energy \(E^{HFB}_x(SD)=8.09\) MeV; depth of the SD well is only 67 keV.
- After including 18 major shells (1140 states), the HFB excitation energy of the SD minimum is reduced by 390 keV to \(E^{HFB}_x(SD)=7.7\) MeV.
- AMP-GCM excitation energy of the SD band head (including 390 keV shift) is 7.086 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 4.33 MeV.
- Binding energy from AMP-GCM (with Coulomb exchange and 906 keV basis correction) is 307.606 MeV, versus experimental 306.715 MeV and HFB value of 304.020 MeV.
- For the normal deformed oblate band: calculated \(2^+_1\) excitation energy 1.45 MeV (experiment 1.97 MeV) and \(B(E2,2^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_1)=72.1\;e^2\text{fm}^4\) (experiment \(60\pm6\;e^2\text{fm}^4\)).
- Hexadecapole deformation at the SD minimum: \(\beta_4=0.24\).
- Occupancies for the SD intrinsic state: 15.04 particles in the \(N=2\) major shell and 4.42 in the \(N=3\) major shell.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the superdeformed band in \(^{36}\)Ar that used the Cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) approach, shell model (Svensson 2000, Svensson 2001), the Projected Shell Model (Long 2001), and angular momentum projected techniques with the Skyrme SLy6 interaction (Bender 2003). The key advance is the use of the Gogny force D1S with the AMP-GCM to assess the stability of the shallow SD minimum against quadrupole fluctuations at low spin, which previous studies had questioned. The results enable future work incorporating triaxiality and dynamical pairing to resolve the discrepancy in the SD band-head energy and improve the description of the \(B(E2)\) transition probabilities.
