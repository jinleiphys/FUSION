# Decay behavior of the strange and beauty partners of $P_c$ hadronic molecules

> We extend our previous study on the decay behavior of $P_c$ hadronic molecules to their strange and beauty partners. While $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ locate just below the $\bar DΣ_c^*$ and $\bar D^*Σ_c$ thresholds, respectively, their proposed strange partners $N(1875)$ and $N(2080)$ sit just below the $KΣ^*$ and $K^*Σ$ thresholds, respectively. Using the effective Lagrangian approach as the same as for the study of $P_c$ hadronic molecular states and with the couplings determined by the $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ flavor symmetry, the decay patterns of $N(1875)$ and $N(2080)$ are obtained assuming them to be the $S$-wave $KΣ^*$ and $K^*Σ$ molecules, respectively. It is found that the measured decay properties of $N(1875)$ and $N(2080)$ are reproduced well. Our results suggest that both $N(1875)$ and $N(2080)$ can be ascribed as the hadronic molecular pentaquark states in the hidden strangeness sector with quantum number $I(J^P)=1/2({3/2}^-)$. Further checks on their hadronic molecule nature by various experiments are proposed. With the same approach, we also give our predictions for the decay behavior of the possible beauty partners of the $P_c$ hadronic molecular states. The $B^*Λ_b$ is found to be the largest decay mode for both $BΣ_b^*$ and $B^*Σ_b$ hadronic molecules, hence can be used to look for the hidden beauty pentaquark states in forthcoming experiments.

## Key claim

The paper extends the hadronic molecular interpretation of the hidden-charm pentaquark states \(P_c(4380)\) and \(P_c(4450)\) to their strange and beauty partners, finding that the measured decay properties of \(N(1875)\) and \(N(2080)\) are reproduced well under the assumption that they are \(S\)-wave \(K\Sigma^*\) and \(K^*\Sigma\) molecules, respectively, with quantum number \(I(J^P)=1/2({3/2}^-)\). The authors hedge that the SU(3) flavor symmetry used to determine couplings is broken by mass differences among \(u\), \(d\), and \(s\) quarks, so the results are a basic estimation rather than a precise prediction.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach with couplings determined by the Weinberg compositeness criterion for pure \(S\)-wave hadronic molecules and by SU(3) flavor symmetry for other vertices. They calculated partial decay widths for two-body decays via triangle diagrams and three-body decays via sequential decays of unstable constituents, employing a Gaussian regulator with cutoff \(\Lambda_0\) from 0.6 to 1.4 GeV and a monopolar form factor with cutoff \(\Lambda_1\) from 0.8 to 2.0 GeV to render loop amplitudes finite. For the beauty sector, parameters were obtained through heavy quark symmetry from the previous study of \(P_c\) states.

## Key numbers

- Mass of \(N(1875)\): \(1875 \pm 20\ \mathrm{MeV}\); total decay width: \(250 \pm 70\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (from PDG).
- Mass of \(N(2080)\) (old name for \(N(2120)\)): taken as \(2080\ \mathrm{MeV}\); total decay width: \(250 \pm 130\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (from PDG).
- For \(N(1875)\) as \(K\Sigma^*\) molecule with \(\Lambda_0=1.0\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(\Lambda_1=1.2\ \mathrm{GeV}\): total width \(228.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\); dominant channel \(\pi\Delta\) at \(201.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(N(2080)\) as \(K^*\Sigma\) molecule with \(J^P={3/2}^-\) and same cutoffs: total width \(181.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\); dominant channels \(\pi\Delta\) at \(82.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\phi p\) at \(19.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\omega p\) at \(11.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(N(2080)\) as \(K^*\Sigma\) molecule with \(J^P={1/2}^-\) and same cutoffs: total width \(216.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\); \(\pi N\) channel at \(22.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (much larger than the \(0.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the \({3/2}^-\) case).
- For beauty partners with binding energy \(90\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\Lambda_0=1.0\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(\Lambda_1=2.0\ \mathrm{GeV}\): \(B\Sigma_b^*\) molecule total width \(299.9\ \mathrm{MeV}\) with \(B^*\Lambda_b\) at \(271.1\ \mathrm{MeV}\); \(B^*\Sigma_b\) molecule with \(J^P={3/2}^-\) total width \(27.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\) with \(B^*\Lambda_b\) at \(19.9\ \mathrm{MeV}\); \(B^*\Sigma_b\) molecule with \(J^P={1/2}^-\) total width \(303.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\) with \(B^*\Lambda_b\) at \(167.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of \(P_c\) hadronic molecules (Lin, 2017) to strange and beauty sectors, following the suggestion by He (2017) that \(N(1875)\) and \(N(2080)\) are strange partners of the \(P_c\) states. The results support the hadronic molecular pentaquark interpretation and are consistent with multichannel partial-wave analyses by Shrestha (2012) and Sokhoyan (2015). For the beauty sector, the predictions improve on earlier calculations by Wu (2010) and Xiao (2013) by including pion exchange contributions, which were found to be non-negligible. The work enables experimental searches for hidden-beauty pentaquarks at future facilities such as the electron-ion collider (EIC) or EicC, and suggests specific channels (e.g., \(J/\psi \to \pi\Delta \bar p + c.c.\) at BESIII, \(\gamma p\) and \(\pi p\) reactions at JLab and JPARC) to test the molecular nature of the strange partners.
