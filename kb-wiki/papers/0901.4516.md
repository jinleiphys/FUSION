# The in-medium scale evolution in jet modification

> The in-medium modification of the scale dependence of the fragmentation function in dense matter, brought about by higher twist corrections to the Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi (DGLAP) evolution equations, is derived. A phenomenologically motivated resummation is outlined which incorporates the next-to-leading twist single gluon emission kernel along with the vacuum emission kernel and provides an in-medium virtuality evolution of the final fragmentation function of a hard jet propagating through dense matter. The concept of a fragmentation function is generalized to include a dependence on distance traveled in the medium. Following this, numerical implementations are carried out and compared to experimental results on the single inclusive suppression observed in Deep-Inelastic scattering (DIS) off a large nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper derives an in-medium modification of the DGLAP evolution equations for fragmentation functions, incorporating next-to-leading twist single gluon emission kernels alongside vacuum emission kernels, but the factorized form of the cross section on which this derivation rests is an assumption that has not yet been proven to hold in the presence of a medium.

## Method

The author generalizes the fragmentation function to include a dependence on the distance traveled in the medium, then derives an in-medium evolution equation by resumming strongly-ordered (planar) emissions where each emission is accompanied by a single scattering off the medium. The resulting evolution equation is solved numerically for deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) on large nuclei, using a hard-sphere nucleon density distribution with a single fit parameter for the gluon density, and the results are compared to experimental nuclear attenuation data for neon, krypton, and xenon targets.

## Key numbers

- The quark jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}_0\) extracted from fits to all three data sets lies in the range 0.06 to 0.18 GeV\(^2\)/fm.
- The perturbative regime at RHIC is believed to start above transverse momenta \(p_T \sim 6-7\) GeV.
- The final exiting parton is required to have virtuality \(m^2 \gtrsim 1\) GeV\(^2\) for jets punching through large nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the vacuum DGLAP evolution equations (Altarelli-Parisi, 1977) to include medium effects, providing the first analytic in-medium evolution equation for fragmentation functions. It contrasts with the single-emission, single-scattering approach of Guo and Wang (2000) and Majumder (2004), showing that multiple emissions are necessary to describe data from larger nuclei. The formalism enables improved calculations for jet modification in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and has been applied to RHIC data in a hydrodynamically expanding medium by Bass et al. (2008).
