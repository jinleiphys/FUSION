# Prediction of the neutron drip line in oxygen isotopes using quantum computation

> In the noisy intermediate-scale quantum era, variational algorithms have become a standard approach to solving quantum many-body problems. Here, we present variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) results of selected oxygen isotopes within the shell model description. The aim of the present work is to locate the neutron drip line of the oxygen chain using unitary coupled cluster (UCC) type ansatze with different microscopic interactions (DJ16, JISP16, and N3LO), in addition to a phenomenological USDB interaction. While initially infeasible to execute on contemporary quantum hardware, the size of the problem is reduced significantly using qubit tapering techniques in conjunction with custom circuit design and optimization. The optimal values of ansatz parameters from classical simulation are taken for the DJ16 interaction, and the tapered circuits are run on IonQ's Aria, a trapped-ion quantum computer. After applying gate error mitigation for three isotopes, we reproduced exact ground state energies within a few percent error. The post-processed results from hardware also clearly show $^{24}$O as the drip line nucleus of the oxygen chain. Future improvements in quantum hardware could make it possible to locate drip lines of heavier nuclei.

## Key claim
Using variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) with unitary coupled cluster (UCC) ansatze and qubit tapering, the authors locate the neutron drip line of oxygen at 24O, but only for the DJ16, JISP16, and USDB interactions; the N3LO interaction fails to establish 24O as the most bound nucleus, likely due to the lack of three-nucleon force.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations for even-even oxygen isotopes (18O to 26O) using valence neutrons in the sd-model space with four interactions (USDB, DJ16, JISP16, N3LO). They applied the Jordan-Wigner transformation to map the shell-model Hamiltonian to a 12-qubit Hamiltonian, then used qubit tapering to reduce the problem to 5 qubits with 52 Pauli terms. Custom-designed circuits with UCCD-type ansatze were optimized classically using AdamOptimizer, and the optimal parameters for the DJ16 interaction were executed on IonQ's Aria trapped-ion quantum computer with gate error mitigation via zero-noise extrapolation.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy of 18O with USDB interaction: -11.932 MeV (exact shell model), reproduced exactly by both original and tapered circuits.
- Ground state energy of 24O with DJ16 interaction: -44.132 MeV (exact shell model), -43.906 MeV (original circuit), -43.905 MeV (tapered circuit), with error less than 0.52%.
- Ground state energy of 26O with N3LO interaction: -53.779 MeV (exact shell model), reproduced exactly by both original and tapered circuits.
- Tapered circuits used 5 qubits instead of 12, with 2-qubit gate counts ranging from 8 (18O, 26O) to 36 (24O).
- Hardware gate fidelities: 99.95% for single-qubit gates, 99.6% for two-qubit gates on IonQ Aria.

## Context
This work extends prior VQE applications to nuclear physics, particularly building on Sarma et al. (2023) which studied 20O and 22O using UCC ansatz. The authors confirm the established result that 24O is the heaviest bound oxygen isotope, previously determined experimentally more than twenty years ago. The paper demonstrates that quantum computation can reproduce shell-model results for the oxygen drip line, and suggests that future hardware improvements could enable drip line predictions for heavier nuclei such as fluorine and neon, which were recently confirmed at RIKEN Radioactive Isotope Beam Factory.
