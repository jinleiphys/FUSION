# Centrality Dependence of Bulk Fireball Properties at RHIC

> We explore the centrality dependence of properties of the dense hadronic matter created in \sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 GeV Au--Au collisions at RHIC. Using the statistical hadronization model we fit particle yields known for 11 centrality bins. We present the resulting model parameters, rapidity yields of physical quantities and the physical properties of bulk matter at hadronization as function of centrality. We discuss the production of strangeness and entropy.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model parameters describing particle yields in Au–Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV are nearly independent of collision centrality for systems with more than about 20 participating nucleons, with the notable exception of the strangeness phase-space occupancy γs, which increases systematically with centrality; this suggests that a deconfined quark liquid phase is already formed for A ≳ 20.

## Method

The authors fitted the statistical hadronization model (SHM) to the measured rapidity yields of π±, K±, p, and p̅ from PHENIX across 11 centrality bins, supplemented by STAR results for K*(892)/K− and φ/K− ratios. They performed χ² minimization using the SHARE numerical package under three chemical assumptions: full chemical non-equilibrium (γq ≠ 1, γs ≠ 1), strange-quark non-equilibrium (γs ≠ 1, γq = 1), and chemical equilibrium (γq = γs = 1), while enforcing strangeness conservation, charge-to-baryon ratio matching, and isospin asymmetry constraints.

## Key numbers

- Baryochemical potential μB = 25 ± 1 MeV across the 10 most central centrality bins (A = 13.4 to 351.4 participants).
- Strangeness chemical potential μS = 5.5 ± 0.5 MeV across the same centrality range.
- Freeze-out temperature T = 155 ± 8 MeV for the chemical equilibrium and strangeness non-equilibrium models; T = 141 ± 7 MeV for the full chemical non-equilibrium model.
- For the most central bin (A = 351.4), net baryon yield per unit rapidity = 14.9 ± 1.5 baryons.
- Entropy yield per unit rapidity dS/dy = 5000 ± 500 for the most central bin.
- Strangeness per net baryon s/B = 9.6 ± 1 for the most central reactions.
- Strangeness per entropy s/S rises from s/S ≲ 0.02 for peripheral collisions to s/S ≃ 0.03 for the most central collisions.
- For the chemical non-equilibrium model, pressure P = 92 MeV/fm³ and energy density ε = 0.5 GeV/fm³ at hadronization.
- Volume normalization factor dV/dy = 969 fm³ for the most central bin (A = 351.4) in the full non-equilibrium model.

## Context

This work extends prior statistical hadronization analyses at RHIC, notably the inconclusive centrality study at √sNN = 130 GeV by Cleymans (2004) and the chemical semi-equilibrium analysis at √sNN = 200 GeV by Kaneta (2004), by including K*(892) and φ yields to anchor the temperature and γs values. The finding that γs exceeds unity for central collisions and that s/S saturates near the value expected for a chemically equilibrated quark-gluon plasma supports the interpretation that thermal gluon fusion to strangeness (Rafelski and Müller, 1982) is active in a deconfined phase. The centrality-independent temperature and chemical potentials for A ≳ 20, combined with the rapid adjustment of γq and γs, enable future studies of the QCD phase boundary and the onset of deconfinement as a function of system size.
