# Coherent $η$-photoproduction on $^4$He and $^{12}$C in the near-threshold region

> Coherent $η$ meson photoproduction on $^4$He and $^{12}$C is considered in the near-threshold region. The elementary $η$ photoproduction operator includes contributions from the $S_{11}(1535)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$ resonances as well as $t$-channel vector meson exchange and the nucleon pole terms. Due to the suppression of the dominant $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance for spin and isospin saturated nuclei, the reaction is mainly governed by $ω$ exchange. Furthermore, the influence of Fermi motion and of different prescriptions for the choice of the invariant reaction energy $W_{γN}$ in the elementary amplitude is studied.

## Key claim

Coherent η photoproduction on spin- and isospin-saturated nuclei (⁴He and ¹²C) is dominated by ω exchange in the t-channel, not by the S₁₁(1535) resonance, because the dominant S₁₁(1535) contribution is suppressed for J=T=0 nuclei; the cross section is therefore very small (of order 10⁻³ relative to the incoherent process) and is sensitive to the ωNN coupling constant.

## Method

The elementary γN → ηN amplitude was constructed using an effective Lagrangian approach including S₁₁(1535) and D₁₃(1520) resonances, t-channel vector meson (ρ and ω) exchange, and nucleon pole terms, with parameters fitted to Mainz γp → ηp data. The nuclear coherent amplitude was computed in the impulse approximation using the isoscalar non-spin-flip part of the elementary operator, with nuclear wave functions for ⁴He (a phenomenological s-wave N–3N cluster form) and ¹²C (a phenomenological charge form factor), and the influence of Fermi motion was studied by comparing exact integration with the factorization approximation.

## Key numbers

- The coherent cross section on J=T=0 nuclei is of relative order 10⁻³ compared to the incoherent process (no absolute cross section value given in the text).
- The isoscalar part of the S₁₁(1535) amplitude used in the calculation is T⁽⁰⁾/T_p = 0.076.
- For the D₁₃(1520) resonance in a nuclear medium, an effective width Γ* = 265 MeV was adopted from analysis of total photoabsorption on ⁹Be and ¹²C.
- The ωNN vector coupling constant used was gᵥ_ωNN = 10.3; using gᵥ_ωNN = 17.5 increases the ⁴He(γ,η)⁴He cross section by about a factor of 3.
- For ¹²C, the charge form factor parameters are R = 2.179 fm and b = 0.503 fm.
- For ⁴He, the wave function parameter α = 0.846 fm⁻¹.
- The cross section from Bennhold and Tanabe (1991) is a factor of 6 larger than the present result for ⁴He(γ,η)⁴He.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of coherent η photoproduction on spin-isospin saturated nuclei by Bennhold and Tanabe (1991), who used a pure isobar model without background terms and found the D₁₃(1520) resonance to dominate. The present calculation includes t-channel vector meson exchange and nucleon pole terms, and finds instead that ω exchange dominates, with the D₁₃(1520) contribution strongly reduced by medium broadening. The results enable future experiments at continuous-wave electron machines like MAMI to determine the ωNN coupling constant from precise measurements of coherent η photoproduction on ⁴He or ¹²C, provided two-body effects are small.
