# Divergence of the Chapman-Enskog expansion in relativistic kinetic theory

> In this letter we show for the first time that the relativistic Chapman-Enskog series for a massless gas undergoing Bjorken expansion diverges. In order to fix this problem, we propose a novel type of expansion that includes non-perturbative contributions in the Knudsen number that are not considered in Chapman-Enskog theory. This approach is in good agreement with exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation for a wide range of values of Knudsen number and does not display the clear signs of divergence exhibited by the Chapman-Enskog series.

## Key claim
The relativistic Chapman-Enskog series for a massless gas undergoing Bjorken expansion diverges, with coefficients growing factorially (approximately m!) at large order. The authors propose a generalized expansion that includes non-perturbative contributions of the form exp(-1/K_N), which agrees well with exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation and does not display the divergence of the standard series.

## Method
The Boltzmann equation with the relaxation time approximation (RTA) collision kernel is rewritten as an infinite set of coupled ordinary differential equations for the moments ρ_{n,ℓ} of the distribution function, using the relativistic method of moments developed by Denicol (2012). The Chapman-Enskog expansion is implemented by expanding the dimensionless moments M_{n,ℓ} in powers of the inverse Knudsen number 1/τ̂ = τ_R/τ, and the coefficients α_p^{(n,ℓ)} are obtained iteratively from the moment equations. A generalized expansion is then introduced where the coefficients β_p^{(n,ℓ)}(τ̂) are allowed to depend on τ̂, leading to differential equations that generate non-perturbative exponential terms.

## Key numbers
- The first-order Chapman-Enskog coefficient for the shear stress is α_1^{(1,1)} = -16/15, giving the Navier-Stokes result π/P ≈ 16/(15 τ̂).
- For large m, the coefficients α_m^{(1,ℓ)} grow approximately as m!, independent of ℓ, indicating zero radius of convergence of the Chapman-Enskog series.
- The generalized expansion at second order, π/P ≈ -β_1^{(1,1)}/τ̂ - β_2^{(1,1)}/τ̂², shows excellent agreement with the exact solution at τ̂₀ = 2.
- The generalized expansion appears to converge numerically already at second order for τ̂₀ = 1, and up to 15th order the approximation remains stable.

## Context
This work extends the study of Chapman-Enskog series divergence, previously demonstrated for uniform shear flow in hard spheres by Dufty (1980s), to the relativistic regime and to expanding systems relevant to heavy ion collisions. It contradicts the implicit assumption that the relativistic Chapman-Enskog series converges for Bjorken flow, and provides a new expansion method that captures both early-time (large Knudsen number) and late-time (small Knudsen number) dynamics. The results enable systematic improvement of hydrodynamic descriptions at moderate to large Knudsen numbers, as needed for early-time dynamics in heavy ion collisions, and connect to studies of gradient expansion divergence in holography by Heller (2013) and Buchel (2016).
