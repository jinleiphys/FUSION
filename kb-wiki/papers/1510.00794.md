# 3D Structure and Nuclear Targets

> Recent experimental and theoretical ideas are laying the ground for a new era in the knowledge of the parton structure of nuclei. We report on two promising directions beyond inclusive deep inelastic scattering experiments, aimed at, among other goals, unveiling the three dimensional structure of the bound nucleon. The 3D structure in coordinate space can be accessed through deep exclusive processes, whose non-perturbative content is parametrized in terms of generalized parton distributions. In this way the distribution of partons in the transverse plane will be obtained, providing a pictorial view of the realization of the European Muon Collaboration effect. In particular, we show how, through the generalized parton distribution framework, non nucleonic degrees of freedom in nuclei can be unveiled. Analogously, the momentum space 3D structure can be accessed by studying transverse momentum dependent parton distributions in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering processes. The status of measurements is also summarized, in particular novel coincidence measurements at high luminosity facilities, such as Jefferson Laboratory. Finally the prospects for the next years at future facilities, such as the 12~GeV Jefferson Laboratory and the Electron Ion Collider, are presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that measuring generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs) in nuclear targets can reveal the three-dimensional partonic structure of bound nucleons and non-nucleonic degrees of freedom, but the experimental feasibility depends on high-luminosity facilities and the ability to separate coherent and incoherent channels, which the HERMES collaboration could not definitively achieve.

## Method

The authors review theoretical frameworks for accessing nuclear 3D parton structure: deep exclusive processes (e.g., deeply virtual Compton scattering, DVCS) to extract GPDs in coordinate space, and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) to extract TMDs in momentum space. They summarize calculations using the impulse approximation (IA) with nuclear spectral functions, off-shell nucleon modifications, meson degrees of freedom, and medium-modified form factors, comparing predictions for coherent and incoherent DVCS observables such as beam spin asymmetries and charge asymmetries across various nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For coherent DVCS on spin-0 nuclei, the D-term contribution to the real part of the DVCS amplitude is predicted to grow with atomic number as \(A^{4/3}\), compared to \(\sim A\) in impulse approximation (Section 2).
- In the liquid drop model, the form factor \(d(0)\) scales as \(d(0) \sim A^{7/3}\) (Section 2).
- Guzey and Siddikov (2005) predict ratios of nuclear to free proton charge asymmetries ranging from 4.61 for \(^{12}\)C to 6.12 for \(^{208}\)Pb, and spin asymmetry ratios from 2.49 for \(^{12}\)C to 0.31 for \(^{208}\)Pb (Table 1).
- For \(^3\)He, nuclear effects in the GPD \(H\) are evaluated at \(t = -0.25\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\xi_3 = 3\xi = 0.2\), showing larger deviations from the forward limit than in inclusive DIS (Section 4.1).

## Context

This review extends earlier work on nuclear parton distributions from inclusive DIS (e.g., the EMC effect observed by Aubert (1983), SLAC E139, CERN NMC, and JLab E03103) by proposing 3D probes via GPDs and TMDs. It builds on theoretical developments by Berger (2001) on deuteron GPDs, Polyakov (2002) on the energy-momentum tensor and shear forces, and Liuti (2005) and Guzey (2005) on nuclear GPD models. The work enables future experimental tests at the 12 GeV Jefferson Laboratory and the proposed Electron-Ion Collider, aiming to resolve the microscopic origin of the EMC effect and access gluon distributions in nuclei.
