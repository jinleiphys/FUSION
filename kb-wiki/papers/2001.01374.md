# Atomic nuclei from quantum Monte Carlo calculations with chiral EFT interactions

> Quantum Monte Carlo methods are powerful numerical tools to accurately solve the Schrödinger equation for nuclear systems, a necessary step to describe the structure and reactions of nuclei and nucleonic matter starting from realistic interactions and currents. These ab-initio methods have been used to accurately compute properties of light nuclei -- including their spectra, moments, and transitions -- and the equation of state of neutron and nuclear matter. In this work we review selected results obtained by combining quantum Monte Carlo methods and recent Hamiltonians constructed within chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

Quantum Monte Carlo methods, when combined with local chiral effective field theory (χEFT) Hamiltonians, can accurately solve the Schrödinger equation for light nuclei, but the applicability of these methods is limited to nearly local nuclear potentials, which has only recently been addressed by the development of local χEFT potentials that are suitable for QMC techniques.

## Method

The authors review the combination of variational Monte Carlo (VMC), Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC), and auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) methods with local chiral nuclear interactions derived within χEFT. The local chiral potentials include both Δ-less and Δ-full formulations, with contact terms regularized in coordinate space using cutoff functions that depend only on the relative distance between nucleons, and low-energy constants are fitted to nucleon-nucleon scattering data and properties of light nuclei. The trial wave functions in VMC are constructed as a product of two- and three-body correlation operators acting on a mean-field state, with expectation values evaluated via Metropolis Monte Carlo integration over the 3A-dimensional configuration space.

## Key numbers

- The Argonne v18 potential fits 1787 pp and 2514 np observables for laboratory energies up to 350 MeV with a final χ²/datum = 1.1
- The AV8' simplified potential over-binds the deuteron by 18 keV due to omission of electromagnetic terms
- The local chiral NN interactions of Gezerlis et al. (2013, 2014) use cutoffs R0 in the range of 1.0-1.2 fm and fit NN phase shifts up to 150 MeV lab energy
- The NV2 class I potentials fit about 2700 data points with χ²/datum ≤ 1.1, while class II fits about 3700 data points with χ²/datum ≤ 1.4
- The NV2 potentials use short-range regulator RS = 0.8 fm or 0.7 fm and long-range regulator RL = 1.2 fm or 1.0 fm
- Four-nucleon interactions may contribute at the level of only about 100 keV in ⁴He or pure neutron matter

## Context

This review extends the line of work using phenomenological Hamiltonians such as Argonne v18 plus Illinois-7 three-body forces, which accurately predict ground- and excited-state energies up to A=12 but fail to provide sufficient repulsion for neutron-star equations of state (Maris, 2013). The development of local χEFT potentials by Gezerlis et al. (2013, 2014) and Piarulli et al. (2014, 2016, 2017) enables QMC methods to be applied with systematically improvable interactions that include consistent three-nucleon forces and allow estimation of theoretical uncertainties, which was not possible with phenomenological models. This work enables future calculations of nuclear structure and reactions with quantified uncertainties, and provides a pathway to consistently derive electroweak currents alongside the nuclear Hamiltonians.
