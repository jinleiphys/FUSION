# Two-Nucleon Spectral Function in Infinite Nuclear Matter

> The two-nucleon spectral function in nuclear matter is studied using Correlated Basis Function perturbation theory, including central and tensor correlations produceded by a realistic hamiltonian. The factorization property of the two-nucleon momentum distribution into the product of the two single nucleon distributions shows up in an analogous property of the spectral function. The correlated model yields a two-hole contribution quenched whith respect to Fermi gas model, while the peaks acquire a quasiparticle width that vanishes as the two momenta approach $k_F$. In addition, three-hole one-particle and more complicated intermediate states give rise to a background, spread out in energy and absent in the uncorrelated models. The possible connections with one- and two-nucleon emission processes are briefly discussed.

# Two-Nucleon Spectral Function in Infinite Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

The two-nucleon spectral function in infinite nuclear matter, computed using Correlated Basis Function (CBF) perturbation theory with a realistic Hamiltonian including central and tensor correlations, exhibits a factorization property analogous to that of the two-nucleon momentum distribution, but only in the infinite-matter limit where linked cluster contributions vanish as 1/A. The correlated model yields a two-hole contribution quenched relative to the Fermi gas model, with peaks acquiring a quasiparticle width that vanishes as both momenta approach the Fermi momentum k_F.

## Method

The authors employed Correlated Basis Function (CBF) perturbation theory, constructing correlated basis states by applying a symmetrized product of two-body correlation operators (including central, spin, isospin, and tensor components) to Fermi gas states, with correlation functions variationally determined using the Urbana v_14 potential plus the TNI three-nucleon interaction. The two-nucleon spectral function was computed at zero-th (variational) order from correlated two-hole (2h) and three-hole one-particle (3h1p) intermediate states, then corrected by perturbative contributions from 2h2p admixtures to the ground state and 2h1p admixtures to the one-hole intermediate states, using cluster expansion techniques to evaluate overlap matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density (k_F = 1.33 fm^{-1}), the two-nucleon relative momentum distribution n_rel(q) shows a peak at |q| ~ 0.7–0.8 fm^{-1}, quenched relative to the Fermi gas model, with a tail extending to |q| ~ 4 fm^{-1}.
- The k_1, k_2 < k_F component provides about 72% of the normalization of n_rel(q); pairs with at least one nucleon momentum above k_F contribute 26% (one above) and 2% (both above) of the normalization.
- The variational 4h2p intermediate state contribution is numerically negligible, being several orders of magnitude smaller than the 3h1p term.
- The two-hole spectral function retains a delta-function peak in energy at the variational level, located at E = -e^v_{h1} - e^v_{h2}, with the peak quenched via the 2h overlap matrix element.

## Context

This work extends the CBF perturbation theory approach previously applied to the single-nucleon spectral function (Benhar, Fabrocini, and Fantoni, 1994) and the nucleon momentum distribution (Fabrocini and Fantoni, 1993) to the two-nucleon spectral function. It provides a theoretical foundation for interpreting (e,e'NN) experiments, where the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation cross section factorizes in terms of the two-nucleon spectral function, building on earlier G-matrix calculations for finite nuclei (Muther, 1995) and the 12C(e,e'pp) reaction (Rosner, 1992). The results enable quantitative estimates of correlation observables in two-nucleon knockout reactions, though extension to finite nuclei is needed for direct comparison with experiment.
