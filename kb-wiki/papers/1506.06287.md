# The Low-Energy Quadrupole Mode of Nuclei

> The phenomenological classification of collective quadrupole excitations by means of the Bohr Hamiltonian is reviewed with focus on signatures for triaxility. The variants of the microscopic Bohr Hamiltonian derived by means of the Adiabatic Time Dependent Mean Field theory from the Pairing plus Quadrupole-Quadrupole interaction, the Shell Correction Method, the Skyrme Energy Density Functional, the Relativistic Mean Field Theory, and the Gogny interaction are discussed and applications to concrete nuclides reviewed. The Generator Coordinate Method for the five dimensional quadrupole deformation space and first applications to triaxial nuclei are presented. The phenomenological classification in the framework of the Interacting Boson Model is discussed with a critical view on the boson number counting rule. The recent success in calculating the model parameters by mapping the mean field deformation energy surface on the bosonic one is discussed and the applications listed. A critical assessment of the models is given with focus on the limitations due to the adiabatic approximation. The Tidal Wave approach and the Triaxial Projected Shell Model are presented as practical approaches to calculate spectral properties outside the adiabatic region.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that while the Bohr Hamiltonian and Interacting Boson Model provide useful phenomenological classifications of collective quadrupole excitations, their applicability is fundamentally limited by the adiabatic approximation, which restricts them to the low-energy region where collective motion remains coherent and decoupled from quasiparticle excitations.

## Method

The paper reviews multiple theoretical approaches to describing low-energy quadrupole modes: the Geometric Collective Model (GCM) as a parametrized Bohr Hamiltonian; the microscopic 5-Dimensional Bohr Hamiltonian derived from various mean-field frameworks including Pairing plus Quadrupole-Quadrupole (PQQ), Skyrme Energy Density Functional, Relativistic Mean Field Theory, and Gogny interactions; the Generator Coordinate Method for the five-dimensional quadrupole deformation space; and the Interacting Boson Model with its mapping from mean-field deformation energy surfaces. It also presents the Tidal Wave approach and Triaxial Projected Shell Model as methods for treating regions outside the adiabatic approximation where quasiparticle-collective coupling becomes important.

## Key numbers

- For $^{102}$Pd as an E(5) nucleus: GCM parameters $d=-43$ and $\hbar^2S=24\times10^{-22}=56\times10^{42}(\mathrm{MeV}s)^{-2}\hbar^2$, corresponding to $S/S_>=0.9$
- For $^{152}$Sm as an X(5) nucleus: GCM parameters $d=-1.75$ and $\hbar^2S=71\times10^{-26}=18\times10^{39}(\mathrm{MeV}s)^{-2}\hbar^2$, corresponding to $S/S_>=0.25$
- For $^{124}$Xe calculated with 5DBH-PQQ: calculated staggering parameters $S(5)=498$ keV and $S(6)=-475$ keV compared to experimental values 208 keV and -92 keV respectively; calculated $\gamma$ band head energy $E(2^+_\gamma)=1097$ keV versus experimental 847 keV; calculated $E(0^+_2)=1099$ keV versus experimental 1269 keV
- For $^{124}$Xe: ratio $B(E2, 3^+_\gamma \rightarrow 2^+_g)/B(E2, 3^+_\gamma \rightarrow 2^+_\gamma)=0.024$ experimental and 0.064 calculated; ratio $B(E2, 3^+_\gamma \rightarrow 4^+_g)/B(E2, 3^+_\gamma \rightarrow 2^+_\gamma)=0.26$ experimental and 0.19 calculated
- For $^{76}$Ge: observed staggering $S(8)-S(9)=0.5$, an order of magnitude smaller than the rigid rotor value of 12
- For Ru chain: staggering amplitude $S(8)-S(9)=1.2$ in $^{112}$Ru and $S(8)-S(9)=0.6$ in $^{114}$Pd
- The PQQ model quadrupole coupling constant $\kappa$ changes smoothly $\propto A^{-1.4}$ through regions of major restructuring
- The IB mass parameters in the PQQ model require a common scaling factor $F_B\sim2$ to 3 to obtain experimental energy scale
- Quadrupole pairing increases rotational moment of inertia by about 30% when calculated by self-consistent cranking, and the LQRPA method finds 20% to 30% increase of all mass parameters

## Context

This review extends the phenomenological classification work of Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and the Geometric Collective Model of Gneuss and Greiner (1971), while critically examining the boson number counting rule of the Interacting Boson Model developed by Iachello and Arima (1987). It builds on the systematic GCM parameter space analysis by Caprio (2005) and the staggering parameter systematics of Zamfir and Casten (1991) and McCutchan et al. (2007). The microscopic derivations reviewed extend the pioneering work of Baranger and Kumar (1968) on the Pairing plus Quadrupole model, and the paper discusses recent developments including the Local QRPA method of Matsuo, Matsuyanagi, and Nakatsukasa (2010-2014) and the Triaxial Projected Shell Model. The review enables future work by identifying the limitations of the adiabatic approximation and pointing to the Tidal Wave approach and Triaxial Projected Shell Model as practical methods for calculating spectral properties outside the adiabatic region.
