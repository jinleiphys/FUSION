# Fluid QCD approach for quark-gluon plasma in stellar structure

> The quark-gluon plasma in stellar structure is investigated using the fluid-like QCD approach. The classical energy momentum tensor relevant for high energy and hot plasma having the nature of fluid bulk of gluon sea is calculated within the model. The transition of gluon field from point particle field inside stable hadrons to relativistic fluid field in hot plasma and vice versa is briefly discussed. The results are applied to construct the equation of state using the Tolman--Oppenheimer--Volkoff equation to describe the hot plasma dominated stellar structure.

# Fluid QCD approach for quark-gluon plasma in stellar structure

## Key claim

The paper argues that the gluon field inside a quark-gluon plasma can be described as a relativistic fluid field (characterized by its velocity) rather than as a point-particle field (characterized by its polarization vector), and that this fluid-like QCD model yields an energy-momentum tensor that can be used with the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to describe stellar structure. However, the actual equation of state is only given in formal integral form depending on an unspecified distribution function φ(r) and temperature T, with no numerical solution presented.

## Method

The authors adopt a previously developed fluid QCD Lagrangian (Sulaiman et al.) in which the gluon field U_μ^a is written as u_μ^a φ, where u_μ^a is a relativistic four-velocity and φ is a scalar field. They derive the classical energy-momentum tensor from this Lagrangian by varying the action with respect to the metric, then specialize to the Schwarzschild geometry appropriate for a static spherically symmetric star. By assuming homogeneous gluon fields across all eight color states and isotropic plasma distribution, they obtain expressions for the energy density and pressure in terms of φ(r) and temperature T, and substitute these into the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation.

## Key numbers

- The phase transition from deconfined QGP to hadronic matter is predicted by lattice QCD to occur at approximately 175 MeV (citing Gottlieb and Petreczky).
- The electromagnetic force on quarks is suppressed relative to the strong force by a factor of e/g = sqrt(α/α_s) ~ O(10^{-1}).
- The pressure is given by P(r) = (4 g_s f_Q m_Q / T) ∫ dV [1 - (g_s f_g^2)/(16 f_Q m_Q) φ(r)^3] φ(r).
- The energy density is given by ρ(r) = (4 g_s f_Q m_Q / T) ∫ dV [1 + (5 g_s f_g^2)/(16 f_Q m_Q) φ(r)^3] φ(r).

## Context

This work extends the fluid QCD model developed by Sulaiman et al. (2005) and related approaches by Bambah et al. (2006) that hybridize charge fields with flow fields for describing quark-gluon plasma. It contrasts with lattice QCD calculations (Gottlieb, Petreczky) that treat QGP perturbatively, and with dissipative ideal hydrodynamics models used to fit RHIC data (Teaney, Huovinen, Kolb, Hirano, Baier). The paper enables future numerical analysis of the equation of state for hot QGP-dominated stellar interiors without assuming a particular relation between pressure and density, though the authors state that such numerical work is still in progress.
