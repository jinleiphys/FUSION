# Triangular singularity and a possible $ϕp$ resonance in the $Λ^+_c \to π^0 ϕp$ decay

> We study the $Λ^+_c \to π^0 ϕp$ decay by considering a triangle singularity mechanism. In this mechanism, the $Λ^+_c$ decays into the $K^* Σ^*(1385)$, the $Σ^*(1385)$ decays into the $π^0 Σ$ (or $Λ$), and then the $K^* Σ$ (or $Λ$) interact to produce the $ϕp$ in the final state. This mechanism produces a peak structure around $2020$ MeV. In addition, the possibility that there is a hidden-strange pentaquark-like state is also considered by taking into account the final state interactions of $K^* Λ$, $K^* Σ$, and $ϕp$. We conclude that it is difficult to search for the hidden-strange analogue of the $P_c$ states in this decay. However, we do expect nontrivial behavior in the $ϕp$ invariant mass distribution. The predictions can be tested by experiments such as BESIII, LHCb and Belle-II.

## Key claim

The triangle singularity mechanism, rather than a genuine hidden-strange pentaquark resonance, can produce a peak structure around 2020 MeV in the φp invariant mass distribution of the Λc+ → π0 φp decay, and the presence of such kinematic singularities makes it difficult to identify a possible Ps state in this channel.

## Method

The authors calculated the triangle diagram contribution where Λc+ decays into K* Σ*(1385), the Σ*(1385) decays into π0 Σ (or Λ), and then K* Σ (or Λ) rescatter into φp in the final state. They considered three models: Model I included final state interactions from a coupled-channel unitary approach (Ramos 2013) that produces a resonance around 2 GeV; Model II used a constant for the rescattering T-matrix representing no Ps resonance; Model III was pure phase space. The loop integral was evaluated nonrelativistically with finite widths for Σ* and K*.

## Key numbers

- The triangle singularity region for the Λ-exchange diagram (B) is [1384.8, 1394.3] MeV for the Σ* mass, while the measured Σ*0 mass is (1383.7 ± 1.0) MeV, almost reaching the lower bound.
- The φp invariant mass range for the decay is [1957.7, 2141.5] MeV.
- The K*+Λ threshold is at approximately 2007 MeV, and the triangle singularity produces a peak around 2020 MeV.
- The Belle Collaboration upper limit on the branching fraction is B(Λc+ → π0 p φ) < 15.3 × 10−5 at 90% confidence level.
- The estimated branching fraction for Model II is O(10−4), of the same order as the Belle upper limit.
- The Λc+ is above the π0φp three-body threshold by only 193 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of triangle singularities previously applied to the hidden-charm Pc pentaquark structures observed by LHCb (Aaij 2015) in Λb0 → K− J/ψ p decays, where a triangle singularity was found near the χc1p threshold at about 4.45 GeV (Guo 2015). The authors consider the analogous hidden-strange sector, where models predict φN bound states (Gao 2000, Huang 2005, Gao 2017) and a coupled-channel unitary approach (Oset 2009, Ramos 2013) produces a resonance around 2 GeV coupling to K*Σ, K*Λ, and φp. The results enable experimental tests by BESIII, LHCb, and Belle-II to distinguish triangle singularity effects from possible genuine pentaquark signals.
