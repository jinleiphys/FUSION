# The $Œ£$(1385) photoproduciton from proton within a Regge-plus-resonance approach

> The interaction mechanism of the $Œ£$(1385) photoproduction from proton is investigated within a Regge-plus-resonance approach based on the experimental data released by CLAS Collaboration recently. The t-channel and u-channel contributions are responsible to the behaviors of the differential cross sections at forward and backward angles, respectively. The contributions from nucleon resonances including $N^*$ and $Œî^*$, which are determined by the predicted decay amplitudes in constituent quark model, are found small but essential to reproduce the differential cross section especially the contribution from $F_{35}$ state $Œî(2000)$.

## Method
The reaction is modeled using a Regge-plus-resonance approach with Born terms (s-channel nucleon, t-channel K exchange, u-channel Œõ hyperon, and a contact term) plus nine nucleon resonances (N* and Œî*) selected by a criterion based on the product of radiative and strong decay amplitudes from the constituent quark model. The t-channel K exchange is Reggeized with a weighting function that interpolates between low and high energies, and gauge invariance is restored via a generalized contact term. The free parameters (cutoffs, Regge parameters, and the Reggeized coupling constant) are fitted to CLAS differential cross section data using the MINUIT code.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical study of kaon photoproduction with strange baryon resonances, following the release of high-precision CLAS data (Moriya 2013) for Œ£(1385), Œõ(1520), and Œõ(1405). It improves upon an earlier effective Lagrangian approach by Oh (2007) that used preliminary CLAS data and showed large discrepancies at low energies. The constituent quark model predictions by Capstick (1998, 1992) for decay amplitudes of nucleon resonances to Œ£(1385)K and NŒ≥ channels are used to select the nine resonances considered. The results enable future studies of the u-channel interaction mechanism, which requires higher-precision data at extreme backward angles to determine whether Reggeized treatment of the u-channel is needed.
