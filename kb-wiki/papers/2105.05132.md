# Multimessenger constraints for ultra-dense matter

> Recent rapid progress in neutron-star (NS) observations offers great potential to constrain the properties of strongly interacting matter under the most extreme conditions. In order to fully exploit the current observational inputs and to study the impact of future observations of NS masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities, we analyze a large ensemble of randomly generated viable NS-matter equations of state (EoSs) and the corresponding rotating stellar structures. We discuss the compatibility and impact of various hypotheses and measurements on the EoS, including those involving the merger product of the gravitational-wave (GW) event GW170817, the binary merger components in GW190814, and radius measurements of the pulsar PSR J0740+6620. We obtain an upper limit for the dimensionless spin of a rigidly rotating NS, $|χ| < 0.81$, an upper limit for the compactness of a NS, $GM/(Rc^2) < 0.33$, and find that the conservative hypothesis that the remnant in GW170817 ultimately collapsed to a black hole strongly constrains the EoS and the maximal mass of NSs, implying $M_\text{TOV} < 2.53 M_\odot$ (or $M_\text{TOV} < 2.19 M_\odot$ if we assume that a hypermassive NS was created). Additionally, we derive a novel lower limit for the tidal deformability as a function of NS mass and provide fitting formulae that can be used to set priors for parameter estimation and to discern whether neutron stars or other compact objects are involved in future low-mass GW events. Finally, we find that the recent NICER results for the radius of the massive NS PSR J0740+6620 place strong constraints for the behavior of the EoS, and that the indicated radius values $R(2M_\odot) \gtrsim 11$ km are compatible with moderate speeds of sound in NS matter and thus with the existence of quark matter cores in massive NSs.

## Key claim

The paper derives multimessenger constraints on the neutron-star equation of state by analyzing a large ensemble of randomly generated EoSs, finding that the conservative hypothesis that the GW170817 remnant collapsed to a black hole implies a maximum TOV mass M_TOV < 2.53 solar masses (or M_TOV < 2.19 solar masses if a hypermassive neutron star was created), with the caveat that these limits depend on assumptions about the critical rest mass for collapse and the rotational state of the remnant.

## Method

The authors generated approximately 1,500,000 equations of state by interpolating between low-density Chiral Effective Theory results (up to 1.1 times nuclear saturation density) and high-density perturbative QCD results (at baryon chemical potentials above 2.6 GeV), using piecewise-linear speed-of-sound-squared functions in the intermediate density region. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for non-rotating neutron stars and used a general relativistic code for hydrostationary equilibrium to construct Kepler sequences for rotating configurations, parallelizing the computation across thousands of cores. The EoS ensemble was then filtered through observational constraints including the existence of two-solar-mass neutron stars and the GW170817 binary tidal deformability limit.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit for dimensionless spin of a rigidly rotating neutron star: |chi| < 0.81 (without GW170817 constraint) or |chi| < 0.78 (with GW170817 constraint)
- Upper limit for neutron star compactness: GM/(Rc^2) < 0.33
- Maximum TOV mass under conservative BH formation hypothesis: M_TOV < 2.53 solar masses
- Maximum TOV mass under hypermassive neutron star assumption: M_TOV < 2.19 solar masses
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star ranges from 9.6 km to 13.4 km after imposing only the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint and the lower bound on maximum mass
- Maximum TOV mass without additional constraints: M_TOV less than approximately 3.0 solar masses
- All Kepler frequencies for stars with masses above 1.4 solar masses exceed 716 Hz (the frequency of PSR J1748-2446ad)
- A future measurement of rotation frequency f(1.4 solar masses) above 775 Hz would eliminate some allowed EoSs

## Context

This work extends the model-independent EoS interpolation approach of Annala (2019) by significantly enlarging the EoS ensemble to approximately 1,500,000 EoSs and incorporating rotating stellar configurations without relying on quasi-universal relations, which had been shown to be violated by hybrid hadron-quark EoSs in studies by Lau (2017), Bandyopadhyay (2017), and Han (2018). The analysis enables more robust constraints on the maximum neutron star mass from the GW170817 remnant collapse hypothesis than previous works by Margalit (2017), Rezzolla (2017), and Ruiz (2017), and provides fitting formulae for tidal deformability that can be used to distinguish neutron stars from other compact objects in future low-mass gravitational wave events.
