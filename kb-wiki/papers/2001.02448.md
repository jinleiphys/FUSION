# Systematics of direct-$α$ production with weakly and strongly bound projectiles

> The production of $α$-particles in reactions using both the strongly and weakly bound projectiles at energies around the Coulomb barrier show several interesting features. To understand these, the role of various reaction mechanisms responsible for $α$-production, such as non-capture breakup, capture of only one of the fragments subsequent to projectile breakup and their contribution to reaction cross sections have been investigated. A systematic study of the $α$-particle production based on available data for various projectile target systems have been performed and a classification based on projectile type is obtained.

# Systematics of direct-α production with weakly and strongly bound projectiles

## Key claim
Non-compound α-particle production cross sections exhibit a universal systematic behavior as a function of reduced energy for each projectile type (strongly bound, stable weakly bound, and radioactive ion beams), but the saturation energy and magnitude differ significantly between categories. For ⁶Li-induced reactions, the sum of complete fusion and non-compound α production fully accounts for the reaction cross section, whereas for ⁷Li and strongly bound projectiles, additional non-α-emitting channels contribute substantially.

## Method
The authors compiled inclusive α-particle production data from the literature for many projectile-target systems, subtracted the compound-fusion (evaporation) contribution estimated using the statistical model code PACE2, and plotted the resulting non-compound α cross sections against a reduced energy scale (center-of-mass energy divided by the Coulomb barrier parameter). For weakly bound projectiles, they compared these data with CDCC-absorption model calculations of fragment-capture cross sections (d-capture for ⁶Li, t-capture for ⁷Li, α-capture for ⁹Be) and with measured reaction-minus-fusion cross sections.

## Key numbers
- For ⁶Li and ⁷Li projectiles, non-capture breakup (NCBU) contributes approximately 10% of the non-compound inclusive α cross section for medium and heavy mass targets.
- The non-compound α cross section saturates at approximately 2V_B for strongly bound projectiles, compared to approximately 1.2V_B for stable weakly bound projectiles and radioactive ion beams.
- For ⁶Li+²⁰⁹Bi, the calculated σ_Reac − σ_CF (using the Sao-Paulo potential and CF cross sections from VVP16) matches the measured non-compound α cross section across all ⁶Li target systems.
- For ⁷Li+²⁰⁹Bi, the calculated σ_Reac − σ_CF is much larger than the measured non-compound α cross section, indicating significant contributions from inelastic and other transfer processes.

## Context
This work extends the systematic studies of α-particle production reported by Kolata (2001) for weakly bound projectiles on heavy targets (²⁰⁸Pb, ²⁰⁹Bi) and by earlier authors (Britt 1961, Siwek 1979, Hugi 1981, Tricoire 1982, Balster 1987, Parker 1987, Li 1988) for strongly bound projectiles. The classification by projectile type (SBP, stable WBP, RIB) and the comparison with CDCC-absorption model calculations (VVP16, VVP18, VVP18b) provide a framework for predicting α-production yields in reactions with radioactive ion beams, where the smaller binding energies and extended radial shapes produce the largest α cross sections. The results enable disentangling the contributions of breakup, transfer, and incomplete fusion to inclusive α yields in future exclusive measurements.
