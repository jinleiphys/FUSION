# Saturation scale fluctuations and multi-particle rapidity correlations

> We study the effect of intrinsic fluctuations of the proton saturation momentum scale on event-by-event rapidity distributions. Saturation scale fluctuations generate an asymmetry in the single particle rapidity distribution in each event resulting in genuine n-particle correlations having a component linear in the rapidities of the produced particles, $y_1\cdots y_n$. We introduce a color domain model that naturally explains the centrality dependence of the two-particle rapidity correlations recently measured by ATLAS while constraining the probability distribution of saturation scale fluctuations in the proton. Predictions for n = 4, 6 and 8 particle correlations find that the four and eight-particle cumulant change sign at an intermediate multiplicity, a signature which could be tested experimentally.

## Key claim

Saturation scale fluctuations in the proton generate an asymmetry in the single-particle rapidity distribution on an event-by-event basis, producing genuine n-particle correlations with a component linear in the rapidities of the produced particles; the authors introduce a color domain model that explains the centrality dependence of the two-particle rapidity correlations measured by ATLAS while constraining the probability distribution of saturation scale fluctuations in the proton, with the caveat that the variance sigma is treated as an energy-independent parameter fixed for LHC energies.

## Method

The authors use the KLN (Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi) model for single inclusive particle production, where the multiplicity depends on the saturation scales of the two colliding protons, each evolving with rapidity according to Q^2 = Q_o^2 exp(plus or minus lambda y). The saturation scales of each proton fluctuate independently on an event-by-event basis according to a log-normal probability distribution with variance sigma^2, and the expectation values of observables are computed by integrating over these fluctuations. A color domain model is introduced where the proton consists of N_cd color domains of size 1/Q_s, with the saturation scale fluctuating independently in each domain, leading to a suppression of the effective variance sigma^2 proportional to 1/N_cd and thus inversely proportional to the charged particle multiplicity N_ch.

## Key numbers

- The parameter lambda quantifying the rapidity dependence of the saturation scale is constrained to the range 0.25 less than or approximately equal to lambda less than or approximately equal to 0.35 from phenomenological fits of deep inelastic scattering data at small-x (Golec-Biernat 1998, Praszalowicz 2012, Praszalowicz 2015).
- For minimum-bias p+p collisions, the ATLAS measurement gives sqrt( <a_1^2> ) approximately equal to 0.1, consistent with sigma approximately 0.5-1.
- The minimum bias charged particle multiplicity used is N_ch^mb = 17.6 from ATLAS.
- For small sigma and even n, the moments are given by <a_1^n> approximately equal to (lambda sigma)^n [ n! / (2^n (n/2)!) - n (n/2)! sigma / sqrt(pi) + ... ].
- The cumulants for small sigma are: <a_1^2>_[2] approximately equal to lambda^2 sigma^2 / 2; <a_1^4>_[4] approximately equal to -2 lambda^4 sigma^5 / sqrt(pi); <a_1^6>_[6] approximately equal to 3 lambda^6 sigma^7 / (2 sqrt(pi)); <a_1^8>_[8] approximately equal to -3 lambda^8 sigma^9 / sqrt(pi).
- For n=4 and n=8, the cumulant becomes negative for sigma less than or approximately equal to 0.5 (independent of lambda).
- The variance scales with multiplicity as sigma^2 = (N_ch^mb / N_ch) sigma_mb^2.
- The ATLAS data shows sqrt( <a_1^2> ) approximately proportional to 1 / N_ch^0.5.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Bzdak 2015) of two-particle rapidity correlations from saturation scale fluctuations in p+p collisions to multi-particle correlations for n=4, 6, and 8. It builds on the Color Glass Condensate framework where gluon number fluctuations lead to dispersion in the saturation scale event-by-event, as studied in numerical simulations of the Langevin BK equation (Soyez 2005) and stochastic reaction-diffusion models (Brunet 2005, Marquet 2006). The color domain model is similar in spirit to the flux-tube interpretation of the near-side ridge (Dumitru 2008) and provides a natural explanation for the centrality dependence observed by ATLAS; the predictions for sign changes in the four- and eight-particle cumulants at intermediate multiplicities could be tested experimentally and would constrain the probability distribution of saturation scale fluctuations in the proton.
