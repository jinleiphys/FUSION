# Elliptic flow of thermal dileptons in relativistic nuclear collisions

> We calculate the transverse momentum and invariant mass dependence of elliptic flow of thermal dileptons for Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. The system is described using hydrodynamics, with the assumption of formation of a thermalized quark-gluon plasma at some early time, followed by cooling through expansion, hadronization and decoupling. Dileptons are emitted throughout the expansion history: by annihilation of quarks and anti-quarks inthe early quark-gluon plasma stage and through a set of hadronic reactions during the late hadronic stage. The resulting differential elliptic flow exhibits a rich structure, with different dilepton mass windows providing access to different stages of the expansion history. Elliptic flow measurements for dileptons,combined with those of hadrons and direct photons, are a powerful tool for mapping the time-evolution of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The elliptic flow of thermal dileptons exhibits a rich structure as a function of transverse momentum and invariant mass, with different mass windows providing access to different stages of the fireball expansion history; however, the analysis does not include non-thermal sources such as Drell-Yan, jet fragmentation, or jet-plasma interactions, which must be accounted for before quantitative comparison with future data.

## Method
The authors used the boost-invariant hydrodynamic code AZHYDRO to model Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 A GeV, assuming formation of a thermalized quark-gluon plasma at initial time tau_0=0.2 fm/c. They integrated thermal dilepton emission rates from the QGP (using the Born term) and from hadronic reactions (using the rates of Kvasnikova et al.) over the space-time history of the fireball, with initial conditions from a Glauber model (75% wounded nucleon, 25% binary collision scaling) and equation of state EOS Q (Maxwell construction at T_c=164 MeV).

## Key numbers
- Initial peak temperature in central collisions: T_0=520 MeV, corresponding to initial peak entropy density s_0=351 fm^{-3}
- Critical temperature for QGP-hadron gas transition: T_c=164 MeV, with energy densities epsilon_q=1.6 GeV/fm^3 (QGP) and epsilon_h=0.45 GeV/fm^3 (hadron gas)
- Hadron freeze-out energy density: epsilon_f=0.075 GeV/fm^3
- For dileptons with invariant mass M=2 GeV, the total elliptic flow peaks near p_T~2 GeV/c at a value of about 0.5%
- For semiperipheral collisions at impact parameter b=7 fm, the total dilepton flow at the rho and phi meson masses follows the hadronic contribution up to p_T~2 GeV/c, peaking near 3 GeV/c before decreasing
- The p_T-integrated elliptic flow for dileptons with masses above 1.5 GeV is of order 1% or less

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic calculation of thermal photon elliptic flow by Chatterjee et al. (2006) to the case of thermal dileptons (virtual photons), where the invariant mass provides an additional continuously tunable parameter. The results complement hadronic flow measurements (Teaney, 2003; Heinz and Kolb, 2002) and direct photon studies, and the authors suggest that combining dilepton elliptic flow with two-photon Hanbury Brown-Twiss correlations could provide a complete experimental characterization of the QGP phase-space structure during early fireball expansion. Future work should include viscous effects and non-thermal sources such as Drell-Yan and jet-plasma interactions.
