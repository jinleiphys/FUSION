# Bulk viscosity of resonantly interacting fermions in the quantum virial expansion

> We consider two-component fermions with a zero-range interaction both in two and three dimensions and calculate the bulk viscosity for an arbitrary scattering length in the high-temperature regime. We evaluate the Kubo formula for the bulk viscosity using an expansion with respect to the fugacity, which acts as a small parameter at high temperatures. In the zero-frequency limit of the Kubo formula, pinch singularities emerge that reduce the order of the fugacity by one. These singularities can turn higher-order vertex corrections at nonzero frequencies into the leading order at zero frequency, so that all such contributions have to be resummed. We present an exact microscopic computation for the bulk viscosity in the high-temperature regime by taking into account these pinch singularities. For negative scattering lengths, we derive the complete bulk viscosity at second order in fugacity and show that a self-consistent equation to resum the vertex corrections is identical to a linearized kinetic equation. For positive scattering lengths, a new type of pinch singularity arises for bound pairs. We show that the pinch singularity for bound pairs leads to a first-order contribution to the bulk viscosity, which is one order lower than that for negative scattering lengths, and that the vertex corrections also provide first-order contributions. We propose a new kinetic equation for bound pairs that derives from a self-consistent equation to resum the vertex corrections.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact microscopic computation of the bulk viscosity for two-component fermions with zero-range interaction in the high-temperature regime, showing that pinch singularities in the Kubo formula reduce the order of fugacity by one and require resummation of vertex corrections; for positive scattering lengths, a new type of pinch singularity for bound pairs leads to a first-order contribution in fugacity that is one order lower than the second-order contribution for negative scattering lengths.

## Method

The authors evaluate the Kubo formula for the bulk viscosity using a quantum virial expansion in the fugacity, which serves as a small parameter at high temperatures. They identify two types of pinch singularities: the fermion pinch singularity arising from products of retarded and advanced fermion propagators, and a new pair pinch singularity arising from products of retarded and advanced pair propagators for bound states at positive scattering length. The resummation of vertex corrections is performed through self-consistent equations that are shown to be equivalent to linearized kinetic equations for both fermions and bound pairs.

## Key numbers

- For negative scattering lengths in three dimensions, the bulk viscosity is of second order in fugacity: $\zeta \sim z^2/\lambda_T^3$, where $\lambda_T \equiv \hbar\sqrt{2\pi\beta/m}$ is the thermal de Broglie wavelength.
- The fermionic contribution at resonant interaction (unitarity) is $\lambda_T^3 \zeta_{\text{fermion}}(v\to0) = z^2 v^2 / 12\sqrt{2}$, where $v \equiv \lambda_T/(\sqrt{2\pi}a)$ is the dimensionless interaction parameter.
- For positive scattering lengths, the bound-pair contribution to the bulk viscosity appears at first order in fugacity as $\zeta \sim z e^{\beta/(ma^2)}/\lambda_T^3$, where $\epsilon_B = 1/(ma^2)$ is the binding energy.
- The second virial coefficient is given by $b_2(\beta,a) = -1/2^{d/2+1} + \delta b_2^{(\text{bound})}(\beta,a) + \delta b_2^{(\text{scat})}(\beta,a)$, with $\delta b_2^{(\text{bound})} = \sqrt{2}e^{\beta\epsilon_B}$ in three dimensions for positive scattering length.

## Context

This work extends the quantum virial expansion approach previously applied to shear viscosity and thermal conductivity (Enss 2011, Nishida 2019, Hofmann 2020, Frank 2020) to the bulk viscosity, which is fundamentally different because it involves two-body operator correlation functions rather than one-body operators. The exact resummation of pinch singularities is shown to be equivalent to solving the linearized Boltzmann equation, generalizing the method developed by Fujii (2021) for shear viscosity. The results enable a consistent microscopic description of transport in strongly correlated Fermi gases with bound states, combining the Boltzmann equation for fermions with a new kinetic equation for bound pairs.
