# Jet quenching effects on the anisotropic flow at RHIC

> In this article we investigate how the energy and momentum deposited by partonic dijets in the quark-gluon plasma may perturb the geometry-induced hydrodynamic expansion of the bulk nuclear matter created in heavy ion collisions at RHIC. The coupling between the jets and the medium is done through a source term in the energy-momentum conservation equations for ideal hydrodynamics. We concentrate our attention at mid-rapidity and solve the equations event-by-event imposing boost-invariance. For $p_T \gtrsim 1$ GeV the anisotropic flow is found to be considerably enhanced, if the dijets deposit on average more than 12 GeV in the medium (or equivalently 6 GeV for each jet of the pair), which corresponds, in our model, to an average suppression greater than $65\%$ of the initial jet transverse energy.

## Key claim
The energy and momentum deposited by partonic dijets in the quark-gluon plasma can considerably enhance the anisotropic flow coefficients for transverse momenta above 1 GeV, but only if the dijets deposit on average more than 12 GeV in the medium (6 GeV per jet), corresponding to an average suppression greater than 65% of the initial jet transverse energy in the model.

## Method
The authors used a 2+1 ideal hydrodynamic model with boost-invariance, solving the energy-momentum conservation equations with a source term representing energy and momentum deposited by a single dijet per event. The initial conditions were generated using a Monte Carlo Glauber model, and the dijet energy loss was parameterized by a reference energy loss rate dE/dl|0 varied from 5 to 20 GeV/fm, with a Gaussian spatial profile of width 0.6 fm. The particle spectra were computed using the Cooper-Frye prescription at a freeze-out temperature of 0.14 GeV for positively charged pions at mid-rapidity in Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV in the (0-5)% centrality window.

## Key numbers
- The reference entropy density s0 was set to 70 fm^-3, corresponding to the maximum of the average entropy density distribution in the (0-5)% centrality window.
- The Gaussian source width sigma was set to 0.6 fm.
- The freeze-out temperature T_fo was set to 0.14 GeV.
- The initial time for hydrodynamic evolution tau0 was 1 fm.
- For dE/dl|0 = 20 GeV/fm, the average suppression of the initial jet transverse energy reached 83%.
- The anisotropic flow enhancement was found to be important for intermediate pT between 1 and 3 GeV, with negligible effects below 1 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of jet-medium interactions beyond the usual focus on jet modification, instead investigating how the medium's hydrodynamic expansion is perturbed by energy and momentum deposition from dijets. It builds on prior work using source terms in hydrodynamic equations (Chaudhuri 2005, Betz 2009, 2010, Torrieri 2009) and the Monte Carlo Glauber model for initial conditions (Drescher 2006, 2007). The results enable future studies to calibrate jet energy loss parameters using anisotropic flow measurements and to assess whether jet-induced flow constitutes a non-negligible fraction of the total anisotropic flow observed at RHIC.
