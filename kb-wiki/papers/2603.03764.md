# Understanding the Structure of Doubly-Heavy Tetraquarks based on the Diquark Model

> We investigate the $T_{cc}$ tetraquark, treating it as a bound state of a heavy diquark and a light antidiquark. Using the Silvestre-Brac potential and solving the Schrödinger equation via the Gaussian Expansion Method, we find that the excitation energy between the heavy diquark and light antidiquark is unexpectedly larger than that between the two light anti-quarks within the anti-diquark -- contrary to the naive expectation where the former is smaller than the latter. We trace this inversion of the mass hierarchy to the centrifugal force acting on the light degree of freedom. Applying the same framework to other systems ($T_{bb}, Λ_b, Λ_c$) yields qualitatively identical behavior, demonstrating the robustness of the mechanism. These results provide new insights into diquark dynamics and the mass structure of exotic hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the $T_{cc}$ tetraquark treated as a bound state of a heavy diquark and a light antidiquark, the excitation energy between the heavy diquark and light antidiquark (the $\lambda$-mode) is unexpectedly larger than that between the two light antiquarks within the antidiquark (the $\rho$-mode), contrary to the naive harmonic oscillator expectation where the $\rho$-mode should be higher. The authors attribute this inversion to the centrifugal force acting on the light degree of freedom, and demonstrate that the same inverted hierarchy appears in $T_{bb}$, $\Lambda_c$, and $\Lambda_b$ systems.

## Method

The authors model the $T_{cc}$ tetraquark using a diquark ansatz (the $\overline{\text{LD}}$HD picture), grouping the two light antiquarks ($\bar{u}\bar{d}$) and the two charm quarks ($cc$) into separate diquark clusters. They solve the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation for each diquark subsystem and for the two-body diquark-antidiquark system using the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) with the Silvestre-Brac AL1 potential, which includes Coulomb, linear confinement, and hyperfine spin-spin terms. The diquark masses are computed first from the two-body quark-quark Hamiltonian, then used as inputs for the tetraquark calculation.

## Key numbers

- The constituent quark masses used are: $m_u = m_d = 0.315$ GeV, $m_c = 1.836$ GeV, $m_b = 5.227$ GeV (from the AL1 parameter set).
- The linear confinement strength is $\lambda = 0.1653$ GeV$^2$; the Coulomb strength is $\kappa = 0.5069$; the hyperfine strength is $\kappa' = 1.8609$.
- The naive harmonic oscillator hierarchy can be restored in the $T_{cc}$ system by tuning the light-diquark mass to a threshold value of $m_{ud}(0^-, 1^-, 2^-) = 1.225$ GeV, above which the $\rho$-mode surpasses the $\lambda$-mode energy.
- The $T_{cc}^+$ tetraquark observed by LHCb has an exceptionally narrow decay width of about 410 keV.

## Context

This work extends the diquark model approach previously applied to singly heavy baryons by Kim et al. (2020), where chiral effective field theory was used to estimate light diquark masses with lattice QCD inputs. The present study instead uses the standard quark model Hamiltonian with the Silvestre-Brac potential and GEM to determine diquark masses numerically. The inverted mass hierarchy contradicts the naive harmonic oscillator expectation found in earlier works by Copley (1979), Nagahiro (2016), Glozman (1996), and Tecocoatzi (2023). The results imply that the $\rho$-mode cannot be identified from the mass spectrum alone, motivating future calculations of decay modes (e.g., $\eta$ or $\pi\pi$ emission) to distinguish $\rho$-mode from $\lambda$-mode excitations experimentally. The authors suggest extending the analysis to three-body configurations and alternative potentials such as those by Yoshida et al. (2015) or Barnes et al. (2005).
