# Nuclear Deformation Effects on Charmonium Suppression in Au+Au and U+U Collisions

> We investigate the impact of intrinsic nuclear deformation and orientation on the yield suppression and momentum anisotropy of charmonia in Au+Au and U+U collisions at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider. The anisotropic nucleon density within the nucleus is parameterized using a modified Woods-Saxon distribution, which is incorporated into the initial distributions of both the heavy quarkonia and the bulk medium energy density. The well-established Boltzmann-type transport equation is utilized to describe the dynamical evolution of quarkonium in the anisotropic bulk medium. Treating quarkonium suppression in Au+Au collisions as a baseline, we find that the momentum-integrated charmonium yield suppression is relatively insensitive to the initial nuclear geometry in deformed U+U collisions. In contrast, the anisotropic flow coefficients ($v_n$) of the charmonium is more sensitive to the nuclear deformation. Furthermore, these observables are also connected with the collision configuration, particularly when distinguishing between tip-tip and body-body orientations in U+U collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 193$ GeV. This effect is more pronounced for the excited state due to its smaller binding energy and heightened sensitivity to the initial energy density of the hot QCD medium.

# Nuclear Deformation Effects on Charmonium Suppression in Au+Au and U+U Collisions

## Key claim

The momentum-integrated charmonium yield suppression is relatively insensitive to initial nuclear geometry in deformed U+U collisions, while the anisotropic flow coefficients ($v_n$) are more sensitive to nuclear deformation, particularly for the excited $\psi(2S)$ state due to its smaller binding energy; these effects are further connected to collision configuration when distinguishing between tip-tip and body-body orientations.

## Method

The authors parameterize the anisotropic nucleon density within the nucleus using a modified Woods-Saxon distribution with a tunable Bohr triaxiality angle $\gamma$, incorporated into an optical Glauber model to generate geometry-dependent initial conditions for both heavy quarkonia and the bulk medium. The bulk medium evolution is simulated with the viscous hydrodynamic model MUSIC, while the dynamical evolution of charmonium in the anisotropic medium is described by a Boltzmann-type transport equation, with the decay rate $\alpha_\Psi$ calculated from inelastic gluo-dissociation cross sections using the Operator Product Expansion method. The regeneration term $\beta_\Psi$ is neglected as the study focuses on high-$p_T$ charmonia where coalescence is not the dominant production mechanism.

## Key numbers

- $^{238}$U nuclear deformation parameters: $R_0 = 6.81$ fm, $a_A = 0.55$ fm, $\beta_2 = 0.28$, $\beta_4 = 0.093$
- $^{197}$Au (deformed) nuclear parameters: $R_0 = 6.42$ fm, $a_A = 0.41$ fm, $\beta_2 = -0.13$, $\beta_4 = -0.03$
- U+U collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 193$ GeV; Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV
- $J/\psi$ initial $p_T$ distribution parameters: $n = 6$, $\langle p_T^2\rangle_{pp}(y=0) = 4.14$ (GeV/c)$^2$
- In U+U collisions, increasing $\gamma$ from $0^\circ$ to $60^\circ$ decreases the elliptic eccentricity $\varepsilon_2$ by around 0.2
- $J/\psi$ $v_2$ and $R_{AA}$ calculated for centrality bins: 0-10%, 10-40%, 40-80% (for $v_2$); 0-20%, 20-40%, 40-60% (for $R_{AA}$)
- Representative impact parameters: $\langle b\rangle = 2.2$ fm (0-10%), 6.7 fm (10-40%), 10.7 fm (40-80%) for $v_2$ bins; 3.3 fm (0-20%), 7.9 fm (20-40%), 10.4 fm (40-60%) for $R_{AA}$ bins

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear deformation effects from light hadrons, as demonstrated by the STAR Collaboration (2024) linking intrinsic nuclear shapes to final-state momentum anisotropies, and from isobar collision studies by Jia (2022) disentangling deformation and neutron-skin effects, to heavy quarkonium observables. The Boltzmann-type transport model used has previously successfully described nuclear modification factors for charmonium and bottomonium from RHIC to LHC energies by Zhu (2004), Yan (2006), Liu (2009), Zhou (2014), Chen (2016), and Zhao (2022), and has been employed to study $J/\psi$ triangular flow in Pb+Pb collisions by Zhao (2021). The results enable using charmonium momentum anisotropy as a probe of initial bulk geometry originating from nuclear deformation, complementing light-hadron flow measurements at RHIC.
