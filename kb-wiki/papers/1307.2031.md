# Composite and elementary nature of a resonance in the sigma model

> We analyze the mixing nature of the low-lying scalar resonance consisting of the pipi composite and the elementary particle within the sigma model. A method to disentangle the mixing is formulated in the scattering theory with the concept of the two-level problem. We investigate the composite and elementary components of the sigma meson by changing a mixing parameter. We also study the dependence of the results on model parameters such as the cut-off value and the mass of the elementary sigma meson.

## Key claim

The physical σ meson is a mixture of a ππ composite state and an elementary σ field, and the mixing nature depends on the choice of flow parameter (x or X) used to connect the two seed states; at the physical point (x=1 or X=1) the elementary component can become predominant, contrary to the expectation from the pole trajectory at small mixing.

## Method

The authors use the nonlinear sigma model with a finite-mass elementary σ field and a four-pion contact interaction that generates a dynamically produced ππ composite σ resonance. They split the tree-level s-wave ππ scattering amplitude into a contact term (v_con, containing the four-pion interaction and t/u-channel σ exchange) and a σ-pole term (v_pole), then unitarize via the Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell factorization. The mixing between the composite and elementary σ seeds is analyzed by introducing a mixing parameter X (or x) and computing the full propagator matrix D̂, whose diagonal residues z¹¹ and z²² give the probabilities of finding the composite and elementary components in the physical state.

## Key numbers

- The elementary σ mass is set to m₀ = 550 MeV, with f_π = 92.4 MeV and m_π = 138 MeV (isospin-averaged).
- At X=0 (pure composite), the composite σ pole lies at √s = 390.7 − 308.4i MeV; at X=1 (physical point), the physical σ pole lies at √s = 420.5 − 128.0i MeV.
- At x=0 (parameterization-I), the naive-composite σ pole is at √s = 360.8 − 354.6i MeV and the elementary σ pole at √s = 550 − 0i MeV; at x=1 the physical pole is at √s = 420.5 − 128.0i MeV.
- At the physical point X=1, the modulus of the residue for the elementary component |z²²| is larger than that for the composite component |z¹¹|, indicating predominant elementary nature.

## Context

This work extends the two-level mixing method previously applied to the a₁(1260) meson (Nagahiro 2011) to the σ meson in the sigma model. It contrasts with the approach of Hyodo (2010), who isolated the σ-pole in the linear representation where the four-pion interaction is repulsive and no composite σ pole exists. The results enable future studies of compositeness conditions (Weinberg 1965, Lurie 1964) and the dependence of the mixing on the elementary σ mass and cutoff parameters.
