# Configuration mixing in the neutron-deficient $^{186-196}$Pb isotopes

> In this article we report the results of detailed interacting boson model calculations with configuration mixing for the neutron-deficient Pb isotopes. Calculated energy levels and $B(E2)$ values for $^{188-196}$Pb are discussed and some care is suggested concerning the current classification on the basis of level systematics of the $4_1^+$ and $6_1^+$ states in $^{190-194}$Pb. Furthermore, quadrupole deformations are extracted for $^{186-196}$Pb and the mixing between the different families (0p-0h, 2p-2h, and 4p-4h) is discussed in detail. Finally, the experimental and the theoretical level systematics are compared.

# Configuration mixing in the neutron-deficient $^{186-196}$Pb isotopes

## Key claim

The interacting boson model with configuration mixing calculations suggest that the $4_1^+$ and $6_1^+$ states in $^{190-194}$Pb, previously classified as spherical states based on level systematics, may instead have a major or even dominant 2p-2h intruder character, or be strongly mixed with spherical states. The authors advise caution regarding the current classification of these states.

## Method

The authors performed interacting boson model (IBM) calculations with configuration mixing, treating simultaneously three boson configurations corresponding to 0p-0h, 2p-2h, and 4p-4h proton excitations across the Z=82 shell gap. The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a U(5) basis for $^{186-196}$Pb, with parameters adjusted from Fossion et al. (2003) to reproduce recently measured B(E2) values in $^{186,188}$Pb. Quadrupole deformations were extracted from calculated spectroscopic quadrupole moments assuming K=0 band structure.

## Key numbers

- $\Delta^{N+2}$ values range from 1.744 MeV ($^{192}$Pb) to 2.129 MeV ($^{186}$Pb); $\Delta^{N+4}$ values range from 3.488 MeV ($^{192}$Pb) to 4.258 MeV ($^{186}$Pb)
- Mixing parameters $w_0^{i,i+2}=w_2^{i,i+2}=0.018$ MeV for all isotopes
- Effective boson charges: $e_N=0.110$ eb, $e_{N+2}=0.140$ eb, $e_{N+4}=0.170$ eb
- In $^{188}$Pb, the calculated B(E2) ratio for $4_2^+\rightarrow 4_1^+$ interband transition is 96.0 (experimental: 87.9), normalized to the in-band $4_2^+\rightarrow 2_2^+$ transition
- In $^{188}$Pb, the calculated B(E2) ratio for $2_2^+\rightarrow 2_1^+$ interband transition is 1947.2 (experimental: 14742.6), normalized to $2_2^+\rightarrow 0_1^+$
- The regular $2^+$ state in $^{188}$Pb occurs at a calculated excitation energy of 1061 keV
- The experimental $0_2^+$ state in $^{190}$Pb at 658 keV is interpreted as a 2p-2h intruder state

## Context

This work extends earlier schematic IBM calculations by Fossion et al. (2003) and previous studies on $^{186}$Pb by Pakarinen et al. (2007) and on isotopic shifts by De Witte et al. (2007). The results complement beyond mean-field calculations by Bender et al. (2004) and Rodriguez et al. (2004) that also find oblate deformation for 2p-2h bands and prolate deformation for 4p-4h bands. The calculations enable a re-evaluation of the classification of low-lying states in $^{190-194}$Pb, suggesting that experimental B(E2) measurements would resolve the ambiguity between classification by level systematics versus classification by wave function content.
