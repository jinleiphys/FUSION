# SU(2,1) Dynamics of Multiple Giant Dipole Resonance Coulomb Excitation

> We construct a three-dimensional analytically soluble model of the nonlinear effects in Coulomb excitation of multiphonon Giant Dipole Resonances (GDR) based on the SU(2,1) algebra. The full 3-dimensional model predicts further enhancement of the Double GDR (DGDR) cross sections at high bombarding energies. Enhancement factors for DGDR measured in thirteen different processes with various projectiles and targets at different bombarding energies are well reproduced with the same value of the nonlinearity parameter with the exception of the anomalous case of $^{136}$Xe which requires a larger value.

# SU(2,1) Dynamics of Multiple Giant Dipole Resonance Coulomb Excitation

## Key claim

The paper presents a three-dimensional analytically soluble model based on SU(2,1) algebra that reproduces the enhancement factors for Double Giant Dipole Resonance (DGDR) cross sections measured in thirteen different processes using a single average nonlinearity parameter x=0.29, with the exception of the 136Xe case which requires a larger value; the model predicts that enhancement factors grow again at high bombarding energies, contrary to the behavior at lower energies.

## Method

The authors construct a nonlinear extension of the harmonic phonon model for Coulomb excitation of multiphonon Giant Dipole Resonances by introducing a single-parameter nonlinearity in the dipole transition operators, which are expanded to include a leading nonlinear term proportional to the parameter x. The time-dependent Schrodinger equation is solved analytically using the SU(2,1) algebra formed by boson operator combinations, reducing the problem to two coupled nonlinear Riccati equations for the amplitudes alpha and beta. The excitation probabilities are then obtained from the asymptotic solutions of these equations, yielding a non-Poissonian expression that reduces to the harmonic Poisson distribution in the limit x goes to 0.

## Key numbers

- Nonlinearity parameter: average value x = 0.29 used to reproduce enhancement factors for twelve excitation processes (with 136Xe requiring a larger value)
- Enhancement factors for DGDR are found to be about 1.3-2 times larger than predicted by linear theory
- The enhancement factor r_2 for Pb+Pb collisions shows a minimum at gamma approximately 1.3-1.5 (corresponding to bombarding energies around 0.3-0.5 GeV per nucleon for heavy colliding nuclei), then grows again at higher energies
- Bombarding energy range studied: 70-700 MeV per nucleon for the Pb+Pb system in the truncated two-dimensional model
- The grazing impact parameter used in cross section integration: 1.2(A_ex^{1/3} + A_sp^{1/3})

## Context

This work addresses the well-known "enhancement factor problem" in multiphonon Giant Dipole Resonance Coulomb excitation, where experimental DGDR cross sections exceed predictions of the linear harmonic phonon model (Emlin, 1990s). The model extends previous attempts to explain this discrepancy through higher-order perturbation theory (BZ, 1993), anharmonic effects (CH-FR, 1990s; B-D, 1990s), and the concept of hot phonons (hot-phonon1, hot-phonon2, 1990s). The authors' earlier truncated two-dimensional model (HPV1, 1999) using SU(1,1) algebra neglected longitudinal response and predicted decreasing enhancement with energy, while the full three-dimensional SU(2,1) solution presented here predicts enhancement growth at high bombarding energies, enabling experimental discrimination between nonlinearity effects and damping/Brink-Axel mechanism effects proposed in other works.
