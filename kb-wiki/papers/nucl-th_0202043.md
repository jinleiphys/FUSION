# pion-rho-omega vertex in nuclear matter

> Medium modifications of the pion-omega-rho vertex are analyzed in context of the omega -> pi gamma* and rho -> pi gamma* decays in nuclear matter. A relativistic hadronic model with mesons, nucleons, and Delta(1232)isobars is applied. A substantial increase of the widths for the decays omega -> pi gamma* and rho -> pi gamma* is found for photon virtualities in the range 0.3-0.6 GeV. This enhancement has a direct importance for the description of dilepton yields obtained in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

# pion-rho-omega vertex in nuclear matter

## Key claim
The in-medium modification of the pion-rho-omega vertex leads to a substantial enhancement of the decay widths for omega → pi gamma* and rho → pi gamma* at photon virtualities between 0.3 and 0.6 GeV, but only when the decaying particle is at rest with respect to the medium and at leading baryon density and zero temperature.

## Method
A relativistic hadronic model with mesons, nucleons, and Delta(1232) isobars was used to compute the in-medium pion-rho-omega vertex at leading baryon density. The calculation included diagrams where exactly one nucleon line uses the density part of the in-medium nucleon propagator, with the loop integration replaced by a factor proportional to the baryon density. The effective coupling constant was evaluated for the decays omega → pi0 gamma* and rho → pi gamma* with the vector mesons on mass shell and the pion at rest relative to the medium.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum coupling constants: g(omega → rho pi) = -1.13 and g(rho → omega pi) = -0.76, obtained from experimental partial decay widths Gamma(omega → pi0 gamma) = 717 keV and Gamma(rho → pi gamma) = 79 keV.
- At nuclear saturation density and M ≈ 400 MeV, the effective width for omega → pi0 gamma* is approximately 900 keV and for rho → pi gamma* is approximately 250 keV.
- The square of the effective coupling constant is enhanced by a factor of approximately 2 for the omega and approximately 5 for the rho around M = 0.4 GeV.
- The vacuum value of the coupling from pi0 decay is g(pi → omega rho) = -1.36, inferred from the pi0 decay constant F_pi = 93 MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of in-medium hadronic couplings, such as the enhanced rho pi pi coupling found by Song (1998) and others, to the pion-rho-omega vertex. The results directly impact calculations of dilepton production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly the longstanding problem of excess dilepton yield in the mass range 0.3-0.5 GeV observed by the CERES and HELIOS experiments. A more detailed fireball evolution model is left for a further study by the same authors.
