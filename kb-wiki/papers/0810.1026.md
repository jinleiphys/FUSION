# High-energy jet quenching in weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasmas

> $\hat q$ is the average squared transverse momentum transfer per unit length to a high-energy particle traversing a QCD medium such as a quark-gluon plasma. We find the (UV-regulated) value of $\hat q$ to leading order in the weak coupling limit, $\alphas(T) \ll 1$. We then use this value to generalize previous analytic results on the gluon bremsstrahlung and pair production rates for massless high-energy particles in a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma, at next-to-leading logarithmic order.

## Key claim

The paper computes the UV-regulated jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\) to leading order in the weak coupling limit (\(\alpha_s(T) \ll 1\)), and then uses this result to generalize previous next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) analytic results for gluon bremsstrahlung and pair production rates to the regime \(E \gg T / [g^4 \ln(1/g)]\), where the assumption \(q_\perp \ll T\) used in prior work breaks down.

## Method

The authors first compute the differential elastic scattering rate \(d\Gamma_{\rm el}/d^2q_\perp\) for a high-energy particle traversing a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma, interpolating between the known small-\(q_\perp\) (screened) and large-\(q_\perp\) (unscreened) limits using a double-sum representation of thermal Bose and Fermi distributions. They then integrate this rate with a UV cutoff \(\Lambda\) to obtain \(\hat q(\Lambda)\) analytically. Finally, they insert this \(\hat q(\Lambda)\) into the implicit NLL formula for the transverse momentum scale \(\mu_\perp\) that controls the bremsstrahlung and pair production rates, extending the earlier NLL calculation of Arnold and Dogan (2008) to energies where the typical momentum transfer \(Q_\perp \sim (\hat q E)^{1/4}\) exceeds the temperature \(T\).

## Key numbers

* \(\hat q(\Lambda) = [\num_{\rm b} {\cal I}_+(\Lambda) + \num_{\rm f} {\cal I}_-(\Lambda)] \, g^4 T^3 / \pi^2\) for \(\Lambda \gg T\), with \({\cal I}_\pm(\Lambda) \simeq [\zeta_\pm(3)/(2\pi)] \ln(\Lambda/m_{\rm D}) + \Delta {\cal I}_\pm\), where \(m_{\rm D}\) is the Debye mass.
* The constants \(\sigma_+ = 0.386043817389949\cdots\) and \(\sigma_- = 0.011216764589789\cdots\) appear in the subleading term \(\Delta {\cal I}_\pm\).
* The Debye mass is \(m_{\rm D}^2 = (1 + \tfrac16 N_{\rm f}) g^2 T^2\) for \(N_{\rm f}\)-flavor QCD.
* The condition for the new regime is \(E \gg T / [g^4 \ln(1/g)]\); the previous regime required \(E \ll T / [g^4 \ln(1/g)]\).
* The leading-log bremsstrahlung rate is \(d\Gamma_{s\to{\rm g}s}/dx = [\alpha \mu_\perp^2 P_{s\to{\rm g}}(x)] / [4\pi\sqrt{2} \, x(1-x)E]\), with \(\mu_\perp^2\) given by an implicit equation involving \(\hat q\).

## Context

This work extends the NLL analytic calculation of jet quenching rates by Arnold and Dogan (2008), which used the small-\(q_\perp\) approximation \(\hat q(\Lambda) \propto \ln(\Lambda/m_{\rm D})\) valid only for \(q_\perp \ll T\). The present paper provides the exact weak-coupling \(\hat q(\Lambda)\) for all \(\Lambda\), enabling the NLL formulas to be applied at energies where \(Q_\perp \gg T\). This complements the leading-order numerical calculations of Jeon and Moore (2005) and the leading-log analysis of Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, and Schiff (BDMPS, 1997). The result also provides a baseline for comparison with the next-to-leading order in coupling calculation of \(\hat q\) by Caron-Huot (2008).
