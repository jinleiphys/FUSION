# The relationship between undularity and L dependence of the nuclear optical model potential

> The contribution of collective or reaction channels to a local optical model potential, OMP, can be readily calculated as a dynamical polarization potential, DPP. The resulting local DPPs commonly have undulatory (`wavy') features, often including local regions of emissivity in the imaginary component. We show here that this undularity arises from $l$-dependence of the underlying formal non-local and $l$-dependent DPP. The $l$-independent proton OMPs, that have the same $S$-matrix $S_{lj}$ as phenomenological $l$-dependent potentials, exhibit undulations that are qualitatively similar to undulations of local DPPs generated by channel coupling. The $l$-dependent phenomenological potentials studied are the potentials that give the best existing fits to the relevant elastic scattering data and the undulatory potentials presented here, being $S$-matrix equivalent (i.e.\ having the same $S$-matrix, $S_{lj}$) give exactly the same scattering. In addition, we present calculations strongly suggesting that undularity (`waviness') is a generic property of $l$-independent potentials that are $S$-matrix equivalent to $l$-dependent potentials. Implications for the validity of folding models based on a local density model are noted.

## Key claim

The undulatory (wavy) features and local emissive regions commonly found in local dynamical polarization potentials (DPPs) arise from the underlying angular-momentum dependence of the formal non-local DPP; any l-independent potential that is S-matrix equivalent to an l-dependent potential will generically exhibit such undulations, meaning that the undularity is not an artifact of a particular parameterization but a generic property.

## Method

The author used S-matrix inversion (the iterative-perturbative IP algorithm implemented in the code IMAGO) to construct l-independent central and spin-orbit potentials that are exactly S-matrix equivalent to previously published l-dependent phenomenological proton optical model potentials for 30.1–30.3 MeV protons on 16O, 40Ca, 58Ni, and 208Pb, and for 35.8 MeV protons on 40Ca. The l-dependent potentials were those that gave the best existing fits to elastic scattering data. Additional model calculations were performed using a Koning-Delaroche potential with a simple uniform renormalization of the real or imaginary term for low partial waves, to test whether undularity is a generic consequence of l-dependence.

## Key numbers

- For 30.1 MeV p + 16O, the full l-dependence changed the real volume integral by ΔJR = −27.25 MeV fm³ and the imaginary volume integral by ΔJI = 9.4 MeV fm³, with a change in reaction cross section ΔCS = 55.41 mb.
- For 30.3 MeV p + 40Ca, the full l-dependence gave ΔJR = −69.21 MeV fm³, ΔJI = 13.649 MeV fm³, and ΔCS = 53.13 mb.
- For 30.3 MeV p + 58Ni, the full l-dependence gave ΔJR = −38.85 MeV fm³, ΔJI = 8.346 MeV fm³, and ΔCS = 29.3 mb.
- For 30.3 MeV p + 208Pb, the full l-dependence gave ΔJR = −6.82 MeV fm³, ΔJI = 1.98 MeV fm³, and ΔCS = 10.1 mb.
- For 35.8 MeV p + 40Ca, the full l-dependence gave ΔJR = −69.75 MeV fm³, ΔJI = 13.74 MeV fm³, and ΔCS = 10.67 mb.
- For the model calculation with 30 MeV protons on 40Ca (real part reduced by 10% for low l, Δ = 1), the inverted l-independent potential had ΔJR = −41.12 MeV fm³ and ΔJI = 10.37 MeV fm³, with ΔCS = 0.52 mb.
- For the same model at 45 MeV (Δ = 2), the inverted potential had ΔJR = −32.67 MeV fm³ and ΔJI = 2.96 MeV fm³, with ΔCS = 0.206 mb.
- For the 30.3 MeV p + 40Ca case, the rms radius of the real central potential increased by 0.1568 fm and the rms radius of the imaginary central potential decreased by 0.2112 fm.

## Context

This work extends the line of investigation into the radial form of the nucleon optical model potential, connecting three previously separate observations: (i) that precise fits to elastic scattering data require undulatory potentials, (ii) that local DPPs generated by channel coupling are undulatory and often emissive, and (iii) that l-dependent potential models exist which fit data that smooth Woods-Saxon forms cannot. The paper directly builds on the l-dependent phenomenological potentials of Kobos and Mackintosh (1979, 1981) and on the DPP studies of Mackintosh (1976), Kobos and Mackintosh (1983), and others. The results imply that folding models based on a local density approximation may be of limited validity, since the S-matrix equivalent l-independent potentials that reproduce the scattering have undulatory forms not captured by such models.
