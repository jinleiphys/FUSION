# QCD equation of state with Tsallis statistics for heavy-ion collisions

> Nonextensive statistics has attracted attention as a description of particle spectra in nuclear collisions at QCD energies. First, we construct the equation of state by incorporating Tsallis statistics based on the hadron resonance gas and parton gas models. Thermodynamic conditions are found to impose constraints on the $q$-parameter of Tsallis distribution. Next, we apply the equation of state to the relativistic hydrodynamic modeling of nuclear collisions. The Cooper-Frye prescription is consistently modified. Numerical demonstrations indicate that the model may describe charged particle spectra at Large Hadron Collider in the transverse momentum range up to 6-8 GeV. Elliptic flow, on the other hand, suggests a narrower range of applicability.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a QCD equation of state using Tsallis statistics and embeds it in relativistic hydrodynamics, finding that the model may describe charged particle spectra at LHC up to 6-8 GeV in transverse momentum, but elliptic flow suggests a narrower range of applicability. The thermodynamic conditions for the equation of state impose constraints on the q-parameter, meaning q and the freeze-out temperature are not free parameters for fitting experimental data.

## Method

The authors constructed a nonextensive QCD equation of state by incorporating Tsallis statistics into the hadron resonance gas model for the hadronic phase and a parton gas model for the QGP phase, matching the two around the crossover temperature using an exponential damping procedure. They then embedded this equation of state in a (2+1)-dimensional inviscid relativistic hydrodynamic model with a consistently modified Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription, and performed numerical simulations for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV using Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle spectra in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV are described up to 6-8 GeV in transverse momentum with q=1.07 and T_f=140 MeV, compared to 2-3 GeV for the thermal case q=1
- The maximum allowed q value is around q ~ 1.07 for T_f around 140 MeV, based on thermodynamic constraints from the equation of state
- Pion contribution to total hadronic pressure at q=1.07 is 18.1% at T=120 MeV, 11.5% at T=140 MeV, and 7.8% at T=160 MeV; at q=1 the corresponding values are 64.4%, 48.6%, and 34.8%
- The energy density and pressure diverge at q=4/3 in the massless Boltzmann limit; the particle number density diverges at q=3/2
- The elliptic flow v_2 is suppressed when q increases, with possible applicability range extending from p_T < 2 GeV to p_T < 4 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous applications of Tsallis statistics to heavy-ion collisions by constructing a consistent hydrodynamic framework with a q-modified equation of state, going beyond simple fits of Tsallis distributions or blast-wave models to particle spectra (Wilk 1999, Osada 2008, Cleymans 2011). The approach enables quantitative testing of whether nonextensive statistics can be justified as a description of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and the authors note that the typical q value extracted from data is around 1.1 (Zheng 2015, Biro 2020), though their results are slightly smaller due to full inclusion of collective dynamics. Future work should investigate the interplay of nonextensive statistics with fluctuations and viscosity, and examine higher-order flow harmonics.
