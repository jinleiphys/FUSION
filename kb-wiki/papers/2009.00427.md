# Constraining Nuclear Symmetry Energy parameters from Neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca

> In the present work, we use a finite range effective interaction to calculate the neutron skin thickness in $^{48}$Ca and correlate these quantities with the parameters of nuclear symmetry energy. Available experimental data on the neutron skin thickness in $^{48}$Ca are used to deduce information on the density slope parameter and the curvature symmetry parameter of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation and at subsaturation densities. We obtained the constraints such as $54.5\leq L(ρ_0) \leq 97.5$ MeV and $47.3\leq L(ρ_c) \leq 57.1$ MeV for the density slope parameter. The constraints on the curvature symmetry energy parameter are obtained as $-170.7\leq K_{sym}(ρ_0) \leq -43.4$ MeV and $-80.8\leq K_{sym}(ρ_c) \leq 23.8$ MeV. A linear relation between the neutron skin thickness in $^{48}$Ca and in $^{2088}$Pb is obtained.

## Key claim

Using a finite range effective interaction (SEI) within the droplet model, the authors constrain the density slope parameter and curvature symmetry parameter of nuclear symmetry energy at both saturation and subsaturation densities from experimental neutron skin thickness data of $^{48}$Ca, but the constraints depend on which experimental measurement (Osaka-RCNP or RIKEN) is used, and the RIKEN results are only compatible with SEI sets having $E_s(\rho_0) \geq 34$ MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using a finite range simple effective interaction (SEI) with a Yukawa form factor, fixing interaction parameters to reproduce standard saturation properties (saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.1658$ fm$^{-3}$, incompressibility $K = 240$ MeV, effective mass $m^*/M = 0.67$). They calculated the neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca within the droplet model framework of Myers and Swiatecki, including surface contributions following Warda et al. (2009), and correlated these values with the symmetry energy slope parameter $L$ and curvature parameter $K_{sym}$ at both $\rho_0$ and a subsaturation density $\rho_c \approx 0.11$ fm$^{-3}$.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca predicted by the SEI ranges from $0.128$ to $0.169$ fm, with a spread of about $0.04$ fm.
- From Osaka-RCNP measurements (Birkhan 2017), the slope parameter at saturation is constrained as $54.5 \leq L(\rho_0) \leq 102$ MeV; from RIKEN measurements (Tanaka 2020), $21.3 \leq L(\rho_0) \leq 97.5$ MeV.
- From Osaka-RCNP, the curvature parameter at saturation is constrained as $-170.7 \leq K_{sym}(\rho_0) \leq -29.2$ MeV; from RIKEN, $-269.8 \leq K_{sym}(\rho_0) \leq -43.4$ MeV.
- At subsaturation density $\rho_c \approx 0.11$ fm$^{-3}$, Osaka-RCNP gives $47.3 \leq L(\rho_c) \leq 59.1$ MeV; RIKEN gives $39 \leq L(\rho_c) \leq 57.1$ MeV.
- At subsaturation density, Osaka-RCNP gives $-80.8 \leq K_{sym}(\rho_c) \leq -17.6$ MeV; RIKEN gives $-125 \leq K_{sym}(\rho_c) \leq -23.8$ MeV.
- A linear relation is obtained: $\Delta r_{np}(^{48}\text{Ca}) = 0.0416 + 0.6169 \, \Delta r_{np}(^{208}\text{Pb})$ fm.
- Using the PREX central value of $0.33$ fm for $^{208}$Pb, the predicted CREX result is $\Delta r_{np}(^{48}\text{Ca}) = 0.245 \pm 0.02$ fm.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to constrain nuclear symmetry energy parameters from neutron skin measurements, particularly following the coupled-cluster calculations of Hagen et al. (2015) which gave $L(\rho_0)$ in the range $37.8-47.7$ MeV, and the experimental determinations of $\Delta r_{np}(^{48}\text{Ca})$ by Birkhan et al. (2017) at RCNP Osaka and Tanaka et al. (2020) at RIKEN. The linear correlation found between neutron skin thickness in $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb enables cross-constraints between the ongoing CREX experiment at Jefferson Lab and the PREX/PREX-II results, though the predicted CREX value of $0.245$ fm from the PREX central value is larger than current experimental estimates from Osaka-RCNP and RIKEN.
