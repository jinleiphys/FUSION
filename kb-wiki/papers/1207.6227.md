# Upsilon Suppression in PbPb Collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV

> We suggest that the combined effect of screening, gluon-induced dissociation, collisional damping, and reduced feed-down explains most of the sequential suppression of Y(nS) states that has been observed in PbPb relative to pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV. The suppression is thus a clear, albeit indirect, indication for the presence of a qgp.

## Key claim

The combined effect of screening, gluon-induced dissociation, collisional damping, and reduced feed-down explains most of the sequential suppression of Y(nS) states observed in PbPb relative to pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, but the calculated suppression of the excited 2S state relative to the 1S ground state is not as large as the CMS value, leaving room for additional suppression mechanisms.

## Method

The authors calculated gluodissociation cross sections for the Y(1S,2S,3S) and chi_b(1P,2P) states using a screened Cornell potential with a complex-valued contribution for collisional damping, solving the Schrodinger equation for singlet wave functions and using a modified Bhanot-Peskin approach with finite string tension. They then thermally averaged these cross sections over a Bose-Einstein gluon distribution, obtained dissociation widths by multiplying with the gluon density, and performed a dynamical fireball evolution with a temperature dependence proportional to volume^{-1/4} to obtain suppression factors prior to feed-down, followed by a radiative and hadronic feed-down cascade calculation.

## Key numbers

- The Y(1S) ground state melts at 4.10 T_c, while the 2S (10.02 GeV) and 3S (10.36 GeV) states melt at about 1.6 and 1.2 T_c, respectively
- For the 2S state, the rms radius increases from 0.50 fm at T=0 to 0.72 fm at T=200 MeV; for the 3S state, the rms radius increases from 0.73 fm to 1.61 fm
- Thermal gluon densities at temperatures 170, 200, 300 and 400 MeV with m_g=0 are n_g = 1.25, 2.03, 6.85 and 16.23 fm^{-3}, respectively
- For an Y(1S) formation time of t_F=0.1 fm/c and QGP lifetime of t_QGP ~ 8 fm/c, the calculated R_AA(1S) ~ 0.44 is consistent with the CMS experimental value of R_AA(1S) = 0.62 +/- 0.11(stat) +/- 0.10(sys)
- The calculated ratio Y(2S)/Y(1S)_PbPb = 0.35 +0.15/-0.13 is larger than the CMS measured value of 0.12 +/- 0.03(stat) +/- 0.01(sys)
- CMS reports an upper limit of 0.07 for the ratio Y(3S)/Y(1S)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions by combining screening, gluon-induced dissociation (Bhanot and Peskin 1979), collisional damping (Laine 2007, Burnier et al. 2008), and feed-down effects (CDF results from Affolder et al. 2000) into a single calculation for bottomium states at LHC energies. The results are compared to CMS measurements from the 2010 run (Chatrchyan et al. 2011) and preliminary 2011 run data (Toneev 2012), and the finding that the calculated 2S suppression is not as strong as observed suggests that additional mechanisms beyond those considered here may be active in PbPb collisions.
