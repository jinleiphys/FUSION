# Deuteron Electromagnetic Form Factors in the Intermediate Energy Region

> Based on a Perturbative QCD analysis of the deuteron form factor, a model for the reduced form factor is suggested. The numerical result is consistent with the data in the intermediate energy region.

# Deuteron Electromagnetic Form Factors in the Intermediate Energy Region

## Key claim
The authors propose a model for the deuteron reduced form factor based on perturbative QCD, but restrict its validity to the intermediate energy region (Q² ≥ 1 GeV²) where the helicity-zero to zero amplitude dominates; the model cannot be applied at very large Q² where hidden-color state contributions become important, nor at low Q² below 1 GeV².

## Method
The authors define the reduced form factor f_d(Q²) by removing nucleon compositeness from the deuteron form factor F_d(Q²). They model the hard scattering kernel using a one-boson exchange approximation, replacing gluon exchange with a massive vector boson of effective mass M_b and an effective coupling constant g_eff. The body distribution amplitude is derived from a harmonic oscillator wave function using the Brodsky-Huang-Lepage prescription, and the reduced form factor is computed as a convolution of the body distribution amplitude with the hard scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers
- The effective boson mass is set to M_b = 0.5 GeV, consistent with the empirical scaling law value m_0² = 0.28 GeV².
- The wave function parameter α = 0.21 GeV.
- The effective coupling constant α_eff = 0.15, obtained by normalizing at the Q² = 2.5 GeV² data point.
- The model is applicable for Q² ≥ 1 GeV², with the domain for leading-power pQCD predictions given by Q² ≫ 2M_d Λ_QCD ∼ 0.8 GeV².
- The Paris potential explains data well for Q² < 1 GeV² but shows explicit deviation above that region.

## Context
This work extends the perturbative QCD analysis of deuteron form factors by Brodsky and Lepage (1979) and the reduced form factor approach of Brodsky and Ji (1985). It addresses the difficulty noted by Farrar, Huleihel, and Zhang (1991) that full perturbative calculations require over 300,000 Feynman diagrams and yield results much smaller than data. The model enables phenomenological predictions in the intermediate energy region without full six-quark evolution, and the authors suggest it could be generalized to other light nuclei.
