# The role of the stochastic color field fluctuations on $J/ψ$ suppression in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We consider the effect of the stochastic color field fluctuations in addition to the collisional as well as the radiative energy losses in the propagation of charm quarks in the hot and dense deconfined medium of quarks and gluons created in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. These fluctuations lead to energy gain of the propagating charm quarks. We construct and solve Langevin transport equations for charm quarks under the evolving background matter described by the ($3+1$)-D relativistic viscous hydrodynamics. Considering the energy gain of charm quarks, the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ of $J/ψ$ is calculated. Interestingly, the experimental measurements for $J/ψ$ suppression in $Au-Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV by PHENIX Collaboration and $Pb-Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV by ALICE and CMS Collaborations, can be nicely described with the effect of these field fluctuations without invoking the regeneration phenomena.

# The role of the stochastic color field fluctuations on $J/\psi$ suppression in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The inclusion of stochastic chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations, which cause an energy gain for charm quarks, allows the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ of $J/\psi$ to describe experimental data from PHENIX, ALICE, and CMS without invoking $J/\psi$ regeneration phenomena. The effect is most significant at low transverse momentum, and the semiclassical approximation used is equivalent to the hard thermal loop approximation in the weak coupling limit.

## Method
Charm quarks are produced via leading-order hard scattering with EPS09 nuclear parton distribution functions, and their initial positions are distributed according to the nuclear overlap function from the Glauber model. The authors solve Langevin transport equations for charm quark propagation through a (3+1)-D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic medium (vHLLE), incorporating collisional energy loss (Peigne and Pashier formalism) and radiative energy loss (with dead cone corrections) alongside the energy gain from stochastic color field fluctuations. Hadronization of charm quarks to $J/\psi$ is performed using leading-order fragmentation functions, and the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ is computed as a function of transverse momentum and number of participant nucleons.

## Key numbers
- Initial time $\tau_{0} = 0.6$ fm and critical temperature $T_{c} = 150$ MeV used for hydrodynamic evolution.
- Shear viscosity $\eta/s = 0.08$ and bulk viscosity $\zeta/s = 0.04$ in the hadronic phase for both $Au-Au$ and $Pb-Pb$ collisions.
- Fractional energy gain of charm quarks due to field fluctuations is shown for momenta $p = 5$, $p = 10$, and $p = 20$ GeV over a path length $L = 5$ fm as a function of temperature.
- $R_{AA}$ results are presented for $Pb-Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV (compared to CMS and ALICE data) and for $Au-Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV (compared to PHENIX data).

## Context
This work extends prior studies of heavy quark energy loss in QGP that considered only collisional and radiative mechanisms (e.g., Peigne and Pashier; Dokshitzer; Armesto; Wicks; Vitev; Mustafa and collaborators) by adding the stochastic field fluctuation effect previously described by Florkowski (2004). The authors had earlier incorporated these fluctuations in heavy quark propagation (Sheikh et al., earlier works), but this is the first application to $J/\psi$ suppression. The results challenge the necessity of regeneration models (e.g., Thews and collaborators; Braun-Munzinger and Stachel) that had been invoked to explain LHC $J/\psi$ data, and they also account for cold nuclear matter effects. The spatial diffusion coefficient $2\pi T D_s$ is compared with lattice QCD calculations, Bayesian analysis, and quasiparticle models.
