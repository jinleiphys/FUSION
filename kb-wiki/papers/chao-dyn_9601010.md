# Wave Chaos in Quantum Billiards with Small but Finite-Size Scatterer

> We study the low energy quantum spectra of two-dimensional rectangular billiards with a small but finite-size scatterer inside. We start by examining the spectral properties of billiards with a single pointlike scatterer. The problem is formulated in terms of self-adjoint extension theory of functional analysis. The condition for the appearance of so-called wave chaos is clarified. We then relate the pointlike scatterer to a finite-size scatterer through the appropriate truncation of basis. We show that the signature of wave chaos in low energy states is most prominent when the scatterer is weakly attractive. As an illustration, numerical results of a rectangular billiard with a small rectangular scatterer inside are exhibited.

## Key claim

Wave chaos in low-energy quantum spectra of two-dimensional rectangular billiards with a small but finite-size scatterer is most prominent when the scatterer is weakly attractive, and the effect disappears for repulsive scatterers; the condition for wave chaos depends on a logarithmic energy relation derived from self-adjoint extension theory.

## Method

The authors formulate the problem of a pointlike scatterer using self-adjoint extension theory of functional analysis, deriving the Green's function and T-matrix for the perturbed system. They then relate the pointlike scatterer to a finite-size scatterer by truncating the unperturbed basis at an energy cutoff determined by the scatterer area, approximating the finite-size potential as a Dirac delta function with strength \(v = V\Omega\) within a restricted basis up to \(E_{N(\Omega)} \simeq (2M\Omega)^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

- The average level density of the unperturbed system is \(\rho_{av} = MS/(2\pi)\), and the parameter \(\alpha = \rho_{av} \langle \varphi_n(\vec{x}_0)^2 \rangle \simeq M/(2\pi)\).
- The width of the wave-chaotic strip is \(\Delta \simeq \alpha\).
- Wave chaos occurs in the energy region satisfying \(\exp\{1/(\alpha \bar{v}_\theta) - 1\} \lesssim z \lesssim \exp\{1/(\alpha \bar{v}_\theta) + 1\}\) for a pointlike scatterer with renormalized coupling constant \(\bar{v}_\theta\).
- For a finite-size scatterer, the condition for wave chaos is \(\left| v(\Omega)^{-1} - \alpha \ln \frac{z}{E_{N(\Omega)}-z} \right| \lesssim \Delta \simeq \alpha\), where \(v(\Omega) = V\Omega\) and \(E_{N(\Omega)} \simeq (2M\Omega)^{-1}\).
- The zero-size limit requires \(V \rightarrow -\infty\) as \(\Omega \rightarrow 0\), with \(V = 1/(\bar{v}_\theta^{-1}\Omega + \alpha \Omega \ln(2M\Omega))\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of wave chaos in billiards with pointlike scatterers (Shigehara 1994) by showing that the phenomenon survives for finite-size scatterers, bridging the gap between mathematical abstraction and physical realizability. It contradicts the naive expectation that classical integrability implies quantum integrability, and it enables experimental or numerical studies of wave chaos using realistic finite-size obstacles. The results are consistent with Berry's (1981) numerical observation in Sinai's billiard that no wave chaos appears when the scatterer is repulsive.
