# Thermodynamic Potential of the Polyakov Loop in SU(3) Quenched Lattice QCD

> Using SU(3) lattice QCD, we study for the first time the effective potential of the Polyakov loop $\langle P \rangle$ at finite temperature, i.e., the thermodynamic potential, in the field-theoretical way. In the framework of the reweighting method in lattice QCD, we express the effective potential $V_{\rm eff}(\langle P \rangle)$ using the expectation value without a source term. In particular, we consider the most difficult and interesting case of vacuum coexistence at the critical temperature $T_c$. We adopt SU(3) quenched lattice QCD on $48^3 \times 6$ at $β$= 5.89379, which corresponds exactly to the critical temperature $T_c$ of the deconfinement phase transition, and use 200,000 Monte Carlo configurations. After categorizing the gauge configurations into one $Z_3$-symmetric and three $Z_3$-broken vacua each, we perform a vacuum-associated reweighting method, using the gauge configurations around each vacuum separately. Finally, we obtain the Polyakov-loop effective potential, which is well depicted around the $Z_3$-symmetric and $Z_3$-broken vacua.

## Key claim

The authors claim to compute, for the first time, the effective potential (thermodynamic potential) of the Polyakov loop directly in SU(3) lattice QCD using a field-theoretical reweighting method, but only for the specific case of vacuum coexistence at the critical temperature \(T_c\) in quenched QCD, and only after categorizing gauge configurations into separate \(Z_3\)-symmetric and \(Z_3\)-broken vacua to avoid the artifact of naive averaging.

## Method

The authors use SU(3) quenched lattice QCD with the standard plaquette action on a \(48^3 \times 6\) lattice at \(\beta = 5.89379\), corresponding to the critical temperature \(T_c \simeq 280\) MeV. They generate 200,000 Monte Carlo gauge configurations at zero source \(J=0\) and apply a vacuum-associated reweighting method: each configuration is assigned to the closest among one \(Z_3\)-symmetric and three \(Z_3\)-broken vacua, and the effective potential \(V_{\text{eff}}(\langle P \rangle)\) is computed separately around each vacuum using the reweighting formalism that expresses quantities at finite source \(J\) in terms of expectation values at \(J=0\).

## Key numbers

* Lattice size: \(48^3 \times 6\), with lattice spacing \(a \simeq 0.12\) fm.
* Coupling \(\beta = 5.89379\), corresponding exactly to the critical temperature \(T_c \simeq 280\) MeV of the deconfinement phase transition in quenched QCD.
* 200,000 Monte Carlo gauge configurations were used.
* 901 different points for the Polyakov-loop source \(J = J_{\text{Re}} + i J_{\text{Im}} \in \mathbb{C}\) were used to map the effective potential.

## Context

This work extends prior attempts to compute the Polyakov-loop effective potential using strong-coupling expansion of QCD (FLLP 2011, FS 2017) and from histograms of the Polyakov loop in lattice QCD (WHOT 2019). The authors provide the first direct field-theoretical lattice QCD calculation of this potential at \(T_c\). The result enables quantitative estimation of the thermal transition probability between confined and deconfined vacua and provides a lattice-QCD-driven input for modeling the Polyakov loop in hot QCD. The method is generally applicable to future studies at various temperatures in both quenched and full QCD, including the effect of dynamical quarks which explicitly breaks \(Z_3\) symmetry.
