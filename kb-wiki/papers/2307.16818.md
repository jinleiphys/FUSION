# Predicted Measurements of the Tensor-to-Scalar Transition in the CLAS12 Nuclear Targets Experiment

> Short-range correlated (SRC) nucleon pairs, which are strongly interacting nucleons at short inter-particle distances, can reveal properties of the effective nucleon-nucleon (\textit{NN}) interaction at short distance scales. The relative abundance of proton-proton (\textit{pp}) pairs and proton-neutron (\textit{pn}) pairs, for example, is sensitive to the tensor contribution to the \textit{NN} interaction. Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) theory, when used with realistic phenomenological \textit{NN} potential models, predicts a transition from a tensor-dominated regime -- at relative momenta of approximately 400~MeV/\textit{c} where \textit{pp} pairs are suppressed relative to \textit{pn} pairs -- to a scalar-dominated regime at higher momenta with no preferred isospin projection. While an increase in the prevalence of \textit{pp} pairs with increasing momentum has been observed in a few experiments, difficulties associated with neutron detection have so far hindered the observation of a corresponding reduction in the abundance of \textit{pn} pairs. High-precision measurements showing a simultaneous increase in the abundance of \textit{pp} pairs and change in the abundance of \textit{pn} pairs with increasing momentum would conclusively demonstrate the existence of the tensor-to-scalar transition. In this work, we study the potential impact of the recently conducted Nuclear Targets Experiment at the CLAS12 detector at Jefferson Lab, using GCF simulations. We model the expected yields and relevant observables for a carbon target with a beam energy of 6 GeV and show that sufficient statistical precision can be obtained from the experimental data, both for \textit{pp} and \textit{pn} pairs, to observe the tensor-to-scalar transition.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that data from the CLAS12 Nuclear Targets Experiment on a carbon target with a 6 GeV beam can achieve sufficient statistical precision to observe the tensor-to-scalar transition in the nucleon-nucleon interaction, but only if both the increase in proton-proton pair abundance and the simultaneous change in proton-neutron pair abundance are measured together, since the proton-proton channel alone is susceptible to inflation from single charge exchange reactions.

## Method

The authors used Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) simulations with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential model to generate Monte Carlo events for quasielastic electron scattering reactions (e,e'p), (e,e'pp), and (e,e'pn) on a carbon-12 target at a beam energy of 6 GeV. They applied event selection criteria for antiparallel kinematics, including requirements on Bjorken x_B > 1.1, missing momentum above 0.3 GeV/c, and polar angle constraints, then modeled detector response using both a Fast Monte Carlo integration over approximate acceptance and a full Geant4 simulation with CLAS12 reconstruction software. The effects of nuclear transparency and single charge exchange reactions were estimated using Glauber calculations with a 5% single charge exchange probability.

## Key numbers

- The tensor-to-scalar transition is predicted to occur from approximately 400 MeV/c to approximately 1000 MeV/c relative momentum, where the abundance of pp pairs increases dramatically while pn pairs decrease
- Single charge exchange probability was taken as P_SCX = 5% for both leading and recoiling nucleons, based on Glauber calculations for carbon
- The ratio of electron-proton to electron-neutron cross sections in the experimental kinematics is sigma_ep/sigma_en approximately 2.5
- Nuclear transparency values used: T^N = 0.53 for (e,e'p) yields and T^NN = 0.44 for (e,e'pN) yields
- The center-of-mass momentum distribution width sigma_CM^a was taken as 150 +/- 20 MeV/c
- The residual excitation energy E* for the A-2 system was varied uniformly from 0-30 MeV
- Event selection requires missing momentum p_miss > 0.3 GeV/c, Bjorken x_B > 1.1, and Q^2 at least 1.5 GeV/c^2

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of short-range correlations from the CLAS detector, which observed an increase in pp/p ratios but could not simultaneously measure pn/p with sufficient precision to confirm the tensor-to-scalar transition. Prior studies on helium-4 (LabHallA 2014) and carbon-12 (Korover 2021) extracted pn/p ratios but with large uncertainties that precluded definite conclusions. The CLAS12 detector offers a factor of 10 higher luminosity than the original CLAS, larger angular acceptance, and a dedicated Central Neutron Detector with efficiency up to about 10% for neutrons from 0.2 to 1.0 GeV/c, enabling the first simultaneous high-precision measurement of both pp/p and pn/p ratios needed to conclusively demonstrate the transition and test different nucleon-nucleon interaction models within the Generalized Contact Formalism framework.
