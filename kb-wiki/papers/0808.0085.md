# Quark--hadron duality in lepton scattering off nuclei

> A phenomenological study of quark--hadron duality in electron and neutrino scattering on nuclei is performed. We compute the structure functions $F_2$ and $xF_3$ in the resonance region within a framework that includes the Dortmund-group model for the production of the {f}{i}rst four lowest-lying baryonic resonances and a relativistic mean-field model for nuclei. We consider four-momentum transfers between 0.2 and 2.5 GeV$^2$. The results indicate that nuclear effects play a different role in the resonance and DIS region. We find that global but not local duality works well. In the studied range of four-momentum transfers, the integrated strength of the computed nuclear structure functions in the resonance region, is considerably lower than the DIS one.

## Key claim

The paper finds that global quark–hadron duality (averaging over the full resonance region) works well for lepton scattering off nuclei, but local duality (matching at individual resonance peaks) does not; the integrated strength of the computed nuclear structure functions in the resonance region is considerably lower than the deep inelastic scattering (DIS) one over the studied four-momentum transfer range of 0.2 to 2.5 GeV².

## Method

The authors compute the nuclear structure functions F₂ and xF₃ in the resonance region using the Dortmund-group model for the first four lowest-lying baryonic resonances (P₃₃(1232), P₁₁(1440), D₁₃(1520), S₁₁(1535)) and a relativistic mean-field (Walecka–Serot) model for the nuclear bound-state wave functions and momentum distributions. The nuclear structure functions are obtained via a convolution of the one-nucleon structure functions with the nucleon momentum distributions for each occupied shell in carbon and iron, assuming the impulse approximation and neglecting final-state interactions. The resonance-region results are compared to DIS data from the BCDMS collaboration (for electron-carbon scattering at Q² = 30–50 GeV²) and from the CCFR and NuTeV collaborations (for neutrino-iron scattering).

## Key numbers

- Four-momentum transfer range studied: 0.2 to 2.5 GeV²
- Binding energies for carbon 1s^{1/2} protons: 47.76 MeV; for carbon 1p^{3/2} protons: 16.76 MeV
- Binding energies for iron 1s^{1/2} protons: 57.19 MeV; for iron 1f^{7/2} protons: 12.11 MeV
- Resonance widths used: Γ_Δ⁰ = 0.12 GeV, Γ_{P1440}⁰ = 0.350 GeV, Γ_{D1520}⁰ = 0.125 GeV, Γ_{S1535}⁰ = 0.150 GeV
- Vector dipole mass M_V = 0.84 GeV; axial dipole mass M_A = 1.05 GeV
- For an isoscalar nucleon target at Q² > 0.5 GeV², Bloom–Gilman duality holds at the level of 20%

## Context

This work extends the study of quark–hadron duality from free nucleon targets (as investigated in Lalakulich (2006) and earlier by Bloom and Gilman (1970)) to nuclear targets, motivated by the need for accurate neutrino-nucleus cross sections in oscillation experiments. The results confirm earlier experimental findings from Jefferson Laboratory (Niculescu (2000) for deuterium, Arrington (2003) for iron) that global duality holds for nuclear targets, while showing that nuclear effects differ between the resonance and DIS regions. The approach enables using DIS results to estimate neutrino-nucleus cross sections in the resonance region, which is relevant for upcoming experiments such as MINERvA and SciBooNE that use carbon, iron, and lead targets.
