# Lattice study of K pi scattering in I = 3/2 and 1/2

> We report the first lattice QCD results of the scattering amplitudes of the $Kπ$ system for $I = 1/2$ channel together with $I=3/2$ case. We investigate all quark diagrams contributing to these iso-spin states, and find that the scattering amplitudes are expressed as combinations of only three diagrams after setting the masses of $u$-quark and $d$-quark to be the same. The lattice simulations are performed in the quenched approximation at $β= 2.23$ on a 12$^3 \times 24$ lattice with an improved Iwasaki gauge action. We employ a new dilution-type noise method to get accuracy of data with reasonable CPU time. A simple method is proposed and applied to eliminate lattice artefact due to the finite extent of lattice along the time direction. A clear difference in the quark mass dependence between $I=3/2$ and $I=1/2$ channels is observed. Although the chiral extrapolation is subtle, we assume $E_{Kπ}^2 \propto m_{u,d}^2 $, and obtain the $S$-wave scattering lengths as $a_0(I=3/2) m_π =-0.084^{+0.051}_{-0.064}$ and $a_0(I=1/2) m_π = -0.625 \pm0.012$. We show all necessary formulas which make the calculation possible. We argue that $ΛN$ is the most appropriate target of the Lüscher's formula for baryonic system because it has no $π$ exchange diagrams and has a scattering length suitable for a lattice QCD simulation.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first lattice QCD results for the scattering amplitudes of the Kπ system in the I = 1/2 channel, alongside the I = 3/2 case, but the chiral extrapolation is subtle and the results rely on the assumption that E_Kπ^2 is proportional to m_u,d^2.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations at β = 2.23 on a 12^3 × 24 lattice using an improved Iwasaki gauge action. They computed hadron four-point functions for the Kπ system, reduced the 22 possible quark diagrams to only three independent diagrams (A, H, and X) by assuming equal u and d quark masses, and employed a dilution-type noise method to evaluate quark propagators efficiently. A simple subtraction method was applied to remove lattice artifacts from the finite time extent.

## Key numbers

- S-wave scattering length for I = 3/2: a_0 m_π = -0.084^{+0.051}_{-0.064}
- S-wave scattering length for I = 1/2: a_0 m_π = -0.625 ± 0.012
- Lattice spacing a corresponds to 0.8144 GeV^{-1}
- Hopping parameters used: κ_ud = 0.1560, 0.1580, 0.1600 and κ_s = 0.1570
- Lattice size: 12^3 × 24

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of hadron scattering using Lüscher's formula, which had been applied to meson-meson, meson-baryon, and baryon-baryon systems by groups such as Fukugita (1995), Aoki (2003), and others. The authors had previously calculated scattering lengths in the Λp system (Muroya 2004). The results provide a test of chiral perturbation theory with strangeness and are directly related to the scalar meson κ, as discussed by Wada (2007). The method enables future lattice studies of baryonic systems, particularly ΛN scattering, which the authors argue is the most appropriate target for Lüscher's formula in the baryonic sector because it has no π exchange diagrams.
