# Bayesian inference of in-medium baryon-baryon scattering cross sections from HADES proton flow data

> Within a Bayesian statistical framework using a Gaussian Process emulator for an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model simulator of heavy-ion reactions at intermediate energies, we infer from the HADES proton flow data the posterior probability distribution functions of in-medium baryon-baryon scattering cross section modification factor X with respect to free-space and the corresponding incompressibility K of nuclear matter as well as their correlation function. The mean value of X is found to be $X=1.32^{+0.28}_{-0.40}$ at 68\% confidence level assuming the nuclear incompressibility K will not exceed 400 MeV, providing circumstantial evidence for enhanced baryon-baryon scattering cross sections in hot and dense nuclear matter.

# Bayesian inference of in-medium baryon-baryon scattering cross sections from HADES proton flow data

## Key claim

The mean value of the in-medium baryon-baryon scattering cross section modification factor X (defined as σ_med/σ_free) is found to be X = 1.32^{+0.28}_{-0.40} at 68% confidence level, but this result assumes the nuclear incompressibility K will not exceed 400 MeV; the posterior PDFs of both X and K depend on the chosen prior ranges, and the qualitative conclusion of enhanced cross sections is robust while the quantitative enhancement depends on precise knowledge of nuclear incompressibility.

## Method

The authors used a Gaussian Process emulator trained on 80 sets of X-K parameters generated on a Latin Hyperlattice using the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model simulator. They performed Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling with the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm to infer posterior probability distribution functions of X and K from HADES proton flow data (directed flow slope F1 and elliptical flow v2) for mid-central Au+Au collisions at E_beam/A = 1.23 GeV. The Skyrme-type momentum-independent mean-field potential was used, with uniform priors of X = 0.5–2.0 and K = 180–400 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Mean value of in-medium baryon-baryon scattering cross section modification factor: X = 1.32^{+0.28}_{-0.40} at 68% confidence level, assuming K does not exceed 400 MeV
- Mean value of nuclear incompressibility: K = 346^{+29}_{-31} MeV at 68% confidence level
- Most probable value (MPV) of X = 1.1 and MPV of K = 375 MeV from the posterior PDFs
- 68% confidence highest posterior density intervals: X = 0.92–1.60 and K = 315–375 MeV
- Experimental data used: proton directed flow slope F1 = 0.46 ± 0.03 at mid-rapidity and elliptical flow v2 = -0.06 ± 0.01 for |y_cm| ≤ 0.05 and p_t ≥ 0.3 GeV/c in 10–30% centrality Au+Au collisions at E_beam/A = 1.23 GeV from HADES Collaboration

## Context

This work addresses the longstanding degeneracy between nuclear EOS stiffness (incompressibility K) and in-medium baryon-baryon scattering cross sections in describing collective flow observables, which previous forward-modeling approaches (e.g., Bertsch 1988, Xu 1991, Westfall 1993, Klakow 1993, Danielewicz 2002) could only constrain qualitatively without quantified uncertainties. The Bayesian framework established here enables simultaneous inference of both parameters with quantified uncertainties, and the authors note that while most prior studies (e.g., Li 2018, Li 2022) found indications of reduced in-medium cross sections, this work finds circumstantial evidence for enhanced cross sections. The framework can be extended to more model parameters (e.g., Gogny-type momentum-dependent potentials, density-dependent symmetry energy) and additional data in future work, and the authors note parallel efforts by other groups (Rei, Oma, Oli) using different dynamical models.
