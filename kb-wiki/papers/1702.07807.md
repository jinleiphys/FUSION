# Three-particle correlations in relativistic heavy ion collisions in a multiphase transport model

> Using a multiphase transport model, we study three-particle mixed harmonic correlations in relativistic heavy ion collisions by considering the observable $C_{m,n,m+n}=\langle\negmedspace\langle \cos(mϕ_1+nϕ_2-(m+n)ϕ_3)\rangle\negmedspace\rangle$, where $ϕ_{1,2,3}$ are azimuthal angles of all particle triplets. We find that except for $C_{123}$, our results on the centrality dependence of $C_{112}$, $C_{224}$ and $C_{235}$ as well as the relative pseudorapidity dependence of $C_{123}$ and $C_{224}$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=$200 GeV agree reasonable well with the experimental data from the STAR Collaboration. We discuss the implications of our results.

## Key claim

The AMPT model with string melting and parameter set B reproduces the centrality and pseudorapidity dependence of three-particle mixed harmonic correlations $C_{112}$, $C_{224}$, and $C_{235}$ measured by STAR in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, but fails to describe $C_{123}$ for mid-central collisions, indicating a weaker correlation between the reaction-plane angles for directed, elliptic, and triangular flows in the model than in data.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (a multiphase transport) model with parameter set B (Lund string fragmentation parameters $a=0.5$, $b=0.9$ GeV$^{-2}$; parton cross section parameters $\alpha_s=0.33$, $\mu=3.2$ fm$^{-1}$) to simulate Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV. They computed two-particle cumulant anisotropic flows $v_n\{2\}$ and three-particle mixed harmonic correlations $C_{m,n,m+n}$ for mid-pseudorapidity ($|\eta|<1$) charged particles with $p_T>0.2$ GeV/$c$, using the analytic reduction of the three-particle sum to order $M$ terms for computational efficiency.

## Key numbers

- $C_{112}$ values are negative for all centralities in both AMPT and STAR data; AMPT agrees with data except for some discrepancy in more central collisions.
- $C_{123}$ values are essentially zero for mid-central collisions in AMPT, smaller than STAR data for those centralities; AMPT agrees within error bars for most-central collisions.
- $C_{224}$ shows "extremely well" agreement with STAR data for all centralities, with large values indicating strong correlation between $\Psi_2$ and $\Psi_4$ reaction-plane angles.
- $C_{235}$ from AMPT shows similar trend and magnitude as STAR data, overestimating in most-central collisions and underestimating in mid-central collisions.
- For 20-30% centrality, $C_{123}$ changes little with $|\eta_1-\eta_2|$ but decreases strongly with $|\eta_1-\eta_3|$; $C_{224}$ changes little with $|\eta_1-\eta_2|$ and decreases slightly with $|\eta_1-\eta_3|$.

## Context

This work extends the study of anisotropic flow from two-particle correlations to three-particle mixed harmonic correlations, which provide information on correlations among different flow harmonics and their reaction-plane angles. The results are compared with experimental data from the STAR Collaboration (Adamczyk 2017) and build on the AMPT model framework of Lin et al. (2005). The findings indicate that the AMPT model with fluctuating initial conditions and strong partonic scatterings captures the essential collision dynamics, and the three-particle correlations can serve as more stringent constraints on initial-state fluctuations and final-state interactions than two-particle correlations alone.
