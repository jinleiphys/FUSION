# Algebraic model for single-particle energies of $Λ$ hypernuclei

> A model is proposed for the spectrum of $Λ$ hypernuclei based on the $u(3)\times u(2)$ Lie algebra, in which the internal degrees of freedom of the spin-1/2 $Λ$ particle are treated in the Fermionic $u(2)$ scheme, while the motion of the hyperon inside a nucleus is described in the Bosonic $u(3)$ harmonic oscillator scheme. Within this model, a simple formula for single-particle energies of the $Λ$ particle is obtained from the natural dynamical symmetry. The formula is applied to the experimental data on the reaction spectroscopy for the $^{89}_Λ$Y and $^{51}_Λ$V hypernuclei, providing a clear theoretical interpretation of the observed structures.

# Algebraic model for single-particle energies of Λ hypernuclei

## Key claim

The authors propose that the single-particle spectrum of Λ hypernuclei can be described by a simple algebraic formula based on a u(3)×u(2) dynamical symmetry, and they demonstrate that this formula fits the experimental data for ⁸⁹_ΛY and ⁵¹_ΛV with fewer parameters than the previous empirical fit, though the fit quality is limited by experimental energy resolution and the model does not yet resolve the fine spin-orbit splitting (which is known from other work to be only 0.05–0.2 MeV).

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian for the hyperon using a bosonic u(3) algebra for the three-dimensional harmonic oscillator motion of the Λ particle inside the nucleus, coupled to a fermionic u(2) algebra for the Λ spin-1/2 degree of freedom. The energy eigenvalues are given by a formula E_Hyp = αN + βN(N+2) + γL(L+1) + E_nΛ, where N is the number of harmonic oscillator quanta, L is the orbital angular momentum, and E_nΛ depends on the number of Λ particles. They fit this formula to the experimental (π⁺,K⁺) reaction cross-section spectra for ⁸⁹_ΛY and ⁵¹_ΛV using a superposition of Gaussian functions with the centroid energies determined by the algebraic formula.

## Key numbers

- For ⁸⁹_ΛY: α = 5.39547 MeV, β = 0.506972 MeV, γ = −0.321663 MeV, E_Λ = −22.6373 MeV (fit parameters from Table 1)
- For ⁵¹_ΛV: α = 7.2807 MeV, β = 0.495241 MeV, γ = −0.476428 MeV, E_Λ = −19.003 MeV (fit parameters from Table 2)
- The experimental energy resolution for the ⁸⁹_ΛY measurement was 1.65 MeV (stated in text)
- The bin width ΔE_bin used in the fit was 0.25 MeV (stated in text)
- The spin-orbit splitting in Λ hypernuclei is known from other experiments and shell-model calculations to be of the order 0.05–0.2 MeV (stated in text)

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach previously applied to ground-state masses of Λ hypernuclei by L.C. Iachello and collaborators (1998) to instead describe excitation spectra. The experimental data reanalyzed here come from the (π⁺,K⁺) reaction spectroscopy measurements by Hotchi et al. (2001) for ⁸⁹_ΛY and ⁵¹_ΛV, which had previously been fitted empirically with 18 parameters. The present model provides a theoretical interpretation with quantum number assignments based on dynamical symmetry. The authors note that shell-model calculations by Motoba et al. (2008) and other experiments on lighter hypernuclei indicate that the spin-orbit splitting is much smaller than what a naive fit would suggest, and that future higher-resolution experiments could test the model's predictions for finer structure.
