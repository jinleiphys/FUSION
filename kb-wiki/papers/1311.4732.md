# Possible $H$-like dibaryon states with heavy quarks

> Possible $H$-like dibaryon states $Λ_{c}Λ_{c}$ and $Λ_{b}Λ_{b}$ are investigated within the framework of quark delocalization color screening model. The results show that the interaction between two $Λ_{c}$'s is repulsive, so it cannot be bound state by itself. However, the strong attraction in $Σ_{c}Σ_{c}$ and $Σ^{*}_{c}Σ^{*}_{c}$ channels and the strong channel coupling, due to the central interaction of one-gluon-exchange and one-pion-exchange, among $Λ_{c}Λ_{c}$, $Σ_{c}Σ_{c}$ and $Σ^{*}_{c}Σ^{*}_{c}$ push the energy of system below the threshold of $Λ_{c}Λ_{c}$ by $22$ MeV. The corresponding system $Λ_{b}Λ_{b}$ has the similar properties as that of $Λ_{c}Λ_{c}$ system, and a bound state is also possible in $Λ_{b}Λ_{b}$ system.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a bound $H$-like dibaryon state exists in the $\Lambda_{c}\Lambda_{c}$ system with $IJ^{P}=00^{+}$ and in the $\Lambda_{b}\Lambda_{b}$ system, but only when channel-coupling effects among $\Lambda_{c}\Lambda_{c}$, $\Sigma_{c}\Sigma_{c}$, $\Sigma^{*}_{c}\Sigma^{*}_{c}$, and $N\Xi_{cc}$ are included; the single $\Lambda_{c}\Lambda_{c}$ channel alone is repulsive and cannot form a bound state.

## Method

The authors used the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) with a Hamiltonian including one-gluon-exchange, chiral meson-exchange (pion, kaon, eta), and a color-screening confinement potential. They performed a dynamical calculation using the resonating group method (RGM) to solve for bound states of the $\Lambda_{c}\Lambda_{c}$ system with $IJ^{P}=00^{+}$, including channel coupling to $\Sigma_{c}\Sigma_{c}$, $\Sigma^{*}_{c}\Sigma^{*}_{c}$, $N\Xi_{cc}$, and their $D$-wave counterparts. The same procedure was applied to the $\Lambda_{b}\Lambda_{b}$ system with its analogous bottom-flavor channels.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy of the $\Lambda_{c}\Lambda_{c}$ system with channel coupling is $22$ MeV below the $\Lambda_{c}\Lambda_{c}$ threshold.
- The single-channel binding energy of $\Sigma_{c}\Sigma_{c}$ is $157$ MeV below its threshold.
- The single-channel binding energy of $\Sigma^{*}_{c}\Sigma^{*}_{c}$ is $91$ MeV below its threshold.
- The binding energy of the $\Lambda_{b}\Lambda_{b}$ system with channel coupling is $19$ MeV below the $\Lambda_{b}\Lambda_{b}$ threshold.
- The single-channel binding energy of $\Sigma_{b}\Sigma_{b}$ is $162$ MeV below its threshold.
- The single-channel binding energy of $\Sigma^{*}_{b}\Sigma^{*}_{b}$ is $79$ MeV below its threshold.

## Context

This work extends the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) previously applied to the $H$ dibaryon ($\Lambda\Lambda$ system) by the same group (QDCSM4) to the heavy-flavor sector. It contradicts the hadron-level one-boson-exchange potential model of Lee (no reference year given) which found no $\Lambda_{c}\Lambda_{c}$ bound state, but agrees with the one-pion-exchange potential model of Meguro (no reference year given) that found a molecular bound state. The results enable further investigation of the difference between quark-level and hadron-level approaches to heavy dibaryons, and suggest experimental searches for $\Lambda_{c}\Lambda_{c}$ and $\Lambda_{b}\Lambda_{b}$ bound states.
