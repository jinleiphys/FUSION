# Angela and the electric dipole response -- giant and pygmy, hot and cold, isoscalar and isovector

## Key claim
The paper argues that Angela Bracco's work on the electric dipole response of nuclei has been instrumental in addressing three current nuclear structure problems, but none of these topics are solved; her contributions have shaped progress toward understanding the damping of the giant dipole resonance (GDR), the validity of the Brink-Axel hypothesis, and the nature of the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR).

## Method
The paper reviews experimental studies of the GDR fine structure using high-resolution inelastic proton scattering at extreme forward angles with wavelet analysis to extract quantitative information on damping mechanisms. For the Brink-Axel hypothesis, the method combines ground-state photoabsorption measured with relativistic Coulomb excitation with quasicontinuum gamma decay data analyzed using the Oslo method and the shape method. The nature of the PDR is investigated through combined analysis of high-resolution (gamma,gamma'), (e,e'), and (p,p') experiments to distinguish E1 and M1 transitions and identify toroidal E1 candidates.

## Key numbers
- In Sn isotopes with mass A = 111-124, the low-energy E1 strength integrated over 4-10 MeV shows a resonance around 8 MeV with about 2% of the TRK sum rule, and for A >= 118 a second resonance peaked at about 6.5 MeV with strength <= 0.5% of the TRK sum rule.
- The wavelet analysis of the GDR fine structure in Ca-40 and Ca-48 uses a complex Morlet wavelet with k0 = 5 and a continuous wavelet transform to extract characteristic energy scales.
- For Sn-120, the gamma strength function extracted from the Oslo method agrees with photoabsorption data from (p,p') scattering within error bands, and the shape method applied to decay to the ground state and first excited 2+ state shows very good agreement after normalization.
- In Ni-58, dipole transitions in the excitation energy range 8-10 MeV were identified as candidates for the toroidal E1 mode based on combined (p,p'), (gamma,gamma'), and (e,e') data.

## Context
This review extends the work of Bracco and collaborators (Bracco 1988, 1989; Bortignon 1991) on giant resonance damping and the temperature dependence of the GDR, and builds on the fine structure studies of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (Olorunfunmi 2022; Bahini 2024), GDR (Poltoratska 2014; Jingo 2018; Donaldson 2021), and GQR (Shevchenko 2004, 2009). The Brink-Axel hypothesis tests extend conflicting results from Guttormsen (2016), Martin (2017), Crespo Campo (2018), Scholz (2020) claiming confirmation and Angell (2012), Isaak (2013, 2019), Netterdon (2015) claiming violation. The PDR discussion builds on the work of Bracco (2019), Wieland (2009, 2018), Crespi (2014, 2021), and Pellegri (2014), and the toroidal mode interpretation follows predictions by Repko (2013, 2019) and Nesterenko (2018). The first experimental evidence for low-energy toroidal E1 transitions in Ni-58 (Brandherm 2024; von Neumann-Cosel 2024) opens the possibility to resolve the long-standing debate on PDR nature through transverse electron scattering form factor measurements at the S-DALINAC facility (Pietralla 2018; Hesbacher 2024).
