# Tensor Analyzing Power and Current Conservation in the d+p->3He+g reaction

> The tensor analyzing power in p+ \vec{d} radiative capture is investigated in a covariant and gauge invariant approach.The results of the present calculation are compared with those of other theoretical approaches and with experiment. Predictions for Ayy at Ed = (150-350) MeV are given. At forward and backward angles T20, T22 and Ayy are sensitive to the effects of current conservation. The interplay between effects coming from the nuclear and current structures is discussed.

# Tensor Analyzing Power and Current Conservation in the d+p→³He+γ Reaction

## Key claim

The tensor analyzing power (TAP) in deuteron-proton radiative capture is sensitive to current conservation effects at forward and backward angles, and the observed asymmetry in T₂₀ between forward and backward directions reported in prior Faddeev calculations disappears when a fully gauge-invariant treatment of external and internal radiation is applied, regardless of the NN interaction model used.

## Method

The authors constructed a covariant, gauge-invariant field-theoretical amplitude for the d+p→³He+γ reaction by inserting photons minimally into the strong tdp vertex (³He→p+d) at the tree level, generating external radiation (proton, deuteron, and ³He pole diagrams) and an internal contact current that ensures current conservation via Ward-Takahashi identities. They also included one-loop isovector contributions from spin-singlet pn-pair intermediate states (d↔γ+{pn}_S). Strong form factors for the tdp and dpn vertices were expressed through overlap integrals of ³He and deuteron wave functions from Faddeev solutions using the Reid Soft Core (RSC) and Argonne-V18+Urbana-IX (V18) potentials.

## Key numbers

- At E_d = 19.6 MeV, the present gauge-invariant calculation (with all multipole transitions) reproduces the experimental T₂₀ data and shows a symmetric behavior around 90°, unlike the asymmetric (positive/negative) sign change at forward/backward angles seen in the non-gauge-invariant Faddeev calculation of ref. [4] (Bonn potential).
- At E_d = 29.0, 45.0, and 95.0 MeV, the difference between the covariant and non-relativistic calculations increases at forward/backward angles with increasing deuteron energy; at 95 MeV disagreement appears at all angles.
- Predictions for A_yy are given for deuteron energies from 150 MeV to 350 MeV.
- Cross section calculations for the ³He+γ→p+d reaction at E_γ = 240 MeV and 340 MeV are compared with experimental data and with the diagrammatic approach of Laget (2000), showing general agreement.

## Context

This work extends the covariant and gauge-invariant approach developed earlier by the same authors for photo- and electro-disintegration of few-body systems (Nagorny and Dieperink, 1997; 1999) to the tensor analyzing power in radiative capture. It contradicts the non-gauge-invariant Faddeev calculations of ref. [4] (which used the Siegert hypothesis and found asymmetric T₂₀ at extreme angles) by showing that current conservation restores symmetry. The results enable future measurements at facilities such as KVI, IUCF, and TRIUMF to test the balance of external and internal radiation mechanisms and to extract information on tensor forces in the NN interaction.
