# Progress in nonequilibrium quantum field theory II

> We review recent developments for the description of nonequilibrium quantum fields, continuing hep-ph/0302210.

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent progress in nonequilibrium quantum field theory, arguing that systematic approximations based on n-particle irreducible (nPI) effective actions can overcome both the secularity problem (spurious time-growing terms in perturbative expansions) and the requirement of late-time universality (insensitivity to initial conditions as thermal equilibrium is approached), which standard 1PI resummation schemes fail to achieve.

## Method

The authors analyze the secularity problem in a real scalar field theory with quartic self-interaction, showing that standard perturbative expansions and 1PI resummations produce secular terms at higher orders. They demonstrate that reorganizing the expansion in terms of the full propagator via the two-particle-irreducible (2PI) effective action yields nonlinear, self-consistent equations that automatically resum secular terms and allow thermalization. The renormalization procedure for the 2PI resummed effective action is developed using BPHZ subtraction on diagrams with resummed propagators, with counterterms fixed by renormalization conditions for the two-point and four-point functions. An equivalence hierarchy between nPI effective actions is established for loop expansions.

## Key numbers

- The two-loop self-energy contribution to the particle number gives a secular term proportional to time: \( \R^{<,>}_p(t) \simeq \bar\sigma^{<,>}_p \times t \) at large times.
- The on-shell production rate from two-body scatterings is: \( \bar\sigma^<_p = \frac{\lambda^2}{4\omega_p} \int \tilde{dq}\tilde{dk}\tilde{dr}\,(2\pi)^4\delta^{(4)}(Q+K-R-P) \times n^0_q\,n^0_k\,(1+n^0_r) \).
- The resummed two-loop self-energy is given by: \( \Sigma[G] = -\frac{\lambda^2}{6} G^3 \).
- The late-time equilibrium solution for the particle number is the Bose-Einstein distribution: \( n^{\rm eq}_p = \frac{1}{e^{(\omega_p-\mu)/T}-1} \).
- Renormalization conditions are imposed at zero momentum: \( Z\,\Gamma^{(2)}(p^2)|_{p=0} = -m_R^2 \), \( Z\,\frac{d}{d p^2}\Gamma^{(2)}(p^2)|_{p=0} = -1 \), and \( Z^2\,\Gamma^{(4)}(p_1,p_2,p_3)|_{p_1=p_2=p_3=0} = -\lambda_R \).

## Context

This work extends the review Berges and Serreau (2003) (hep-ph/0302210) and builds on the 2PI effective action formalism of Cornwall, Jackiw, and Tomboulis (1974). It addresses the need for nonperturbative methods beyond kinetic descriptions in cosmology (reheating after inflation) and heavy-ion collisions (early thermalization puzzle). The techniques enable first-principles studies of far-from-equilibrium particle production and thermalization, including prethermalization (Berges 2004), and provide systematic approaches to compute transport coefficients (Aarts 2004) and thermodynamic quantities (Berges 2004) in hot field theories, as well as critical phenomena near second-order phase transitions (Alford 2004).
