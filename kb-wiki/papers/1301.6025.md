# Application of the sextic oscillator with centrifugal barrier and the spheroidal equation for some X(5) candidate nuclei

> The eigenvalue equation associated to the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian is considered in the intrinsic reference frame and amended by replacing the harmonic oscillator potential in the $β$ variable with a sextic oscillator potential with centrifugal barrier plus a periodic potential for the $γ$ variable. After the separation of variables, the $β$ equation is quasi-exactly solved, while the solutions for the $γ$ equation are just the angular spheroidal functions. An anharmonic transition operator is used to determine the reduced E2 transition probabilities. The formalism is conventionally called the Sextic and Spheroidal Approach (SSA) and applied for several X(5) candidate nuclei: $^{176,178,180,188,190}$Os, $^{150}$Nd, $^{170}$W, $^{156}$Dy, $^{166,168}$Hf. The SSA predictions are in good agreement with the experimental data of the mentioned nuclei. The comparison of the SSA results with those yielded by other models, such as X(5) \cite{Iache9}, Infinite Square Well (ISW) \cite{Raduta}, and Davidson (D) like potential \cite{Raduta} for the $β$, otherwise keeping the spheroidal functions for the $γ$, and the Coherent State Model (CSM) \cite{Rad1,Rad2,Rad3,Rad4,RaSa,Rad5} respectively, suggests that SSA represents a good approach to describe nuclei achieving the critical point of the U(5)$\rightarrow$SU(3) shape phase transition.

# Application of the sextic oscillator with centrifugal barrier and the spheroidal equation for some X(5) candidate nuclei

## Key claim

The Sextic and Spheroidal Approach (SSA), which replaces the harmonic oscillator potential in the beta variable with a sextic oscillator potential with centrifugal barrier and uses spheroidal functions for the gamma variable, provides a good description of nuclei at the critical point of the U(5) to SU(3) shape phase transition, with predictions in good agreement with experimental data for ten candidate nuclei. The claim is qualified by the fact that the beta equation is only quasi-exactly solved, meaning solutions exist only for a limited set of quantum numbers.

## Method

The Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian was considered in the intrinsic reference frame with a potential separable into beta-dependent and gamma-dependent parts. The beta equation was solved using a sextic oscillator potential with a centrifugal barrier, yielding quasi-exact solutions, while the gamma equation was reduced to the spheroidal equation by expanding the rotational term around gamma equals zero and using a periodic potential. An anharmonic transition operator was used to compute reduced E2 transition probabilities, and the model parameters were fitted by least squares to experimental data for ten nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of excitation energies of the 4+ and 2+ ground band members for the candidate nuclei is close to 2.9, the X(5) critical point value.
- The beta wave functions for the sextic potential are given by: phi_n_beta,L^(M)(beta) = N_n_beta,L P_n_beta,L^(M)(beta^2) beta^(L+1) exp(-a/4 beta^4 - b/2 beta^2), with n_beta = 0,1,2,...,M.
- The beta excitation energy formula is: E_beta(n_beta,L) = (hbar^2/2B)[b(2L+3) + lambda_n_beta^(M)(L) + u_0^+-], with n_beta = 0,1,2,...,M.
- The gamma equation eigenvalue parameter lambda_m_gamma,n_gamma = (1/9)[epsilon_tilde_gamma - u_1/2 - (11/27)D + (1/3)L(L+1)] + 2L(L+1)/27, where D = L(L+1) - K^2 - 2.
- The nuclei studied were: 176Os, 178Os, 180Os, 188Os, 190Os, 150Nd, 170W, 156Dy, 166Hf, and 168Hf.

## Context

This work extends previous approaches by Raduta and collaborators (2007, 2011) that used spheroidal functions for the gamma variable, replacing the earlier Davidson potential or infinite square well for the beta variable with a sextic oscillator potential. The SSA results are compared with the X(5) model of Iachello (2001), the Infinite Square Well (ISW) model of Raduta (2011), the Davidson (D) like potential model of Raduta (2011), and the Coherent State Model (CSM) of Raduta (1991, 1993, 1995, 1996, 1999, 2000). The approach enables improved description of nuclei at the U(5) to SU(3) shape phase transition critical point, addressing the drawback that earlier approximations for the gamma variable produced non-periodic functions and lost hermiticity due to improper scalar products.
