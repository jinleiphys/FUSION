# Pion-nucleon scattering in chiral perturbation theory II: Fourth order calculation

> We analyse elastic-pion nucleon scattering to fourth order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, extending the third order study published in Nucl. Phys. A640 (1998) 199. We use various partial wave analyses to pin down the low-energy constants from data in the physical region. The S-wave scattering lengths are consistent with recent determinations from pionic hydrogen and deuterium. We find an improved description of the P-waves. We also discuss the pion-nucleon sigma term and problems related to the prediction of the subthreshold parameters.

## Key claim

The fourth-order heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation provides a complete one-loop representation of elastic pion-nucleon scattering, improving the description of P-waves compared to the third-order study, but the pion-nucleon sigma term cannot be predicted without further input because a new combination of low-energy constants appears that is not pinned down by scattering data.

## Method

The authors constructed the complete one-loop amplitude for elastic pion-nucleon scattering in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to order q^4, including tree, counterterm, and loop contributions. They fitted the 13 low-energy constants (plus one related to the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy) to S-wave and P-wave partial wave amplitudes from three different sets of pion-nucleon phase shifts in the physical region at low energies, using pion laboratory momenta between 40 and 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Fits were performed using pion laboratory momenta in the range of 40 to 100 MeV
- The fourth-order calculation contains 13 low-energy constants plus one related to the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Lambda_chi is approximately 1 GeV
- The S-wave scattering lengths are consistent with recent determinations from pionic hydrogen and deuterium (PSI, 1999)
- The fourth order corrections are in general not large, but they improve the description of most partial waves

## Context

This work extends the third-order study published in Nucl. Phys. A640 (1998) 199 (referred to as (I)) to fourth order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. It contrasts with the manifestly Lorentz invariant approach of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) and with the dispersive representation method of Paul (1999) that fits inside the Mandelstam triangle. The results enable future investigation of isospin symmetry violation in low-energy pion-nucleon scattering, building on earlier work by Meissner and Steininger (1998) and Fettes, Meissner, and Steininger (1998).
