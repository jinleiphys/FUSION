# Correlations imposed by the unitary limit between few-nucleon systems, nuclear matter and neutron stars

> The large values of the singlet and triplet two-nucleon scattering lengths locate the nuclear system close to the unitary limit. This particular position strongly constrains the low-energy observables in the three-nucleon system as depending on one parameter, the triton binding energy, and introduces correlations in the low energy sector of light nuclei. Here we analyze the propagation of these correlations to infinite nuclear matter showing that its saturation properties, the equation of state of $β$-stable nuclear matter and several properties of neutron stars, as their maximum mass, are well determined solely by a few number of low-energy quantities of the two- and three-nucleon systems. In this way we make a direct link between the universal behavior observed in the low-energy region of few-nucleon systems and fundamental properties of nuclear matter and neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the saturation properties of nuclear matter and the maximum mass of neutron stars are well determined solely by a few low-energy quantities of the two- and three-nucleon systems (the singlet and triplet scattering lengths, effective ranges, and the triton binding energy), because the nuclear system is naturally located close to the unitary limit. The hedge is that this holds for a leading-order (LO) effective field theory (EFT) inspired potential, particularly when the one-pion-exchange potential (OPEP) regulator is large (making the OPEP small), and that the three-body range \(r_3\) must be tuned independently of the two-body ranges to achieve the results.

## Method

The authors constructed a LO EFT-inspired two-nucleon potential consisting of a short-range contact interaction (regularized with a gaussian) plus a regularized one-pion-exchange potential (OPEP), with strengths and ranges fixed to reproduce the \(np\) scattering lengths and effective ranges in the singlet and triplet channels. A contact three-nucleon force with a gaussian regulator of range \(r_3\) was added, with its strength fixed to reproduce the triton binding energy \(B(^3\mathrm{H})\). Using this potential, they calculated the energy per particle of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and pure neutron matter within the Brueckner–Hartree–Fock (BHF) approximation, and then derived the equation of state (EoS) of \(\beta\)-stable nuclear matter to compute neutron star properties by integrating the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

*   The singlet and triplet two-nucleon scattering lengths are both large compared to the range of the nuclear force, \(r_N \approx 1.4\) fm.
*   The empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter is \(\rho_0 = 0.16 \pm 0.01~\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and \(E/A|_{\rho_0} = -16.0 \pm 1.0~\mathrm{MeV}\).
*   For the interaction model with \(\beta = \infty\) and \(r_3 = 1.4\) fm, the calculated saturation density is \(\rho_0 = 0.151~\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and the saturation energy is \(E/A|_{\rho_0} = -16.11\) MeV.
*   For the same model (\(\beta = \infty\), \(r_3 = 1.4\) fm), the symmetry energy at saturation is \(E_{sym}^0 = 35.20\) MeV, the symmetry energy slope parameter is \(L = 70.2\) MeV, and the SNM incompressibility is \(K_\infty = 251\) MeV.
*   For the interaction model with \(\beta = \infty\) and \(r_3 = 1.40\) fm, the calculated maximum neutron star mass is \(M_{max} = 2.52~M_\odot\) with a corresponding radius \(R = 11.64\) km and central nucleonic density \(\rho_c = 0.84~\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
*   The calculated values of \(K_\infty\) for the best models range from 203 to 251 MeV, in agreement with the empirical value \(K_\infty = 240 \pm 20\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of universal behavior in few-nucleon systems (Koenig 2017, Kievsky 2016) to infinite nuclear matter and neutron stars, showing that the unitary limit imposes constraints that propagate from the two- and three-body sectors to many-body observables. It builds on the pionless EFT framework (Bedaque 1999, Bedaque 2002) and the Efimov physics of three-nucleon systems, and it connects to studies of correlations between the triton and nuclear matter (Delfino). The results enable a direct link between low-energy few-nucleon observables and fundamental properties of neutron stars, such as their maximum mass, and are compatible with the unitary gas bounds on the symmetry energy proposed by Tews (2017).
