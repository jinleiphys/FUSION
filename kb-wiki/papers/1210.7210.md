# Quantitative sum rule analysis of low-temperature spectral functions

> We analyze QCD and Weinberg-type sum rules in a low-temperature pion gas using vector and axial-vector spectral functions following from the model-independent chiral-mixing scheme. Toward this end we employ recently constructed vacuum spectral functions with ground and first-excited states in both channels and a universal perturbative continuum; they quantitatively describe hadronic tau-decay data and satisfy vacuum sum rules. These features facilitate the implementation of chiral mixing without further assumptions, and lead to in-medium spectral functions which exhibit a mutual tendency of compensating resonance and dip structures, suggestive for an approach toward structureless distributions. In the sum rule analysis, we account for pion mass corrections, which turn out to be significant. While the Weinberg sum rules remain satisfied even at high temperatures, the numerical evaluation of the QCD sum rules for vector and axial-vector channels reveals significant deviations setting in for temperatures beyond ~140 MeV, suggestive of additional physics beyond low-energy chiral pion dynamics.

# Quantitative sum rule analysis of low-temperature spectral functions

## Key claim

The authors show that QCD sum rules for vector and axial-vector channels remain reasonably well satisfied up to temperatures of approximately 120–140 MeV when using chirally mixed spectral functions with finite pion mass corrections, but beyond ~140 MeV significant deviations emerge that suggest additional physics beyond low-energy chiral pion dynamics is required.

## Method

The authors construct finite-temperature vector and axial-vector spectral functions using a model-independent chiral mixing scheme, where in-medium spectral functions are linear combinations of vacuum spectral functions weighted by a mixing parameter ε(T) proportional to the scalar pion density. They then evaluate both Weinberg-type sum rules and QCD sum rules numerically, incorporating leading-order temperature dependence of condensates (quark, gluon, and four-quark) and nonscalar twist-2 and twist-4 operators, while accounting for finite pion mass corrections that are significant at the temperatures considered.

## Key numbers

- The mixing parameter ε(T) takes values: 0.06 at T=100 MeV, 0.16 at T=140 MeV, and 0.32 at T=180 MeV
- Vacuum QCD sum rule deviations: d_V = 0.24% and d_A = 0.56% over Borel windows of 0.085 GeV < M < 1.47 GeV (vector) and 0.089 GeV < M < 1.48 GeV (axial-vector)
- At T=140 MeV, d_V = 0.85% and d_A = 1.08% over the in-medium Borel window
- At T=180 MeV, d_V = 2.29% and d_A = 2.53% over the in-medium Borel window
- Vacuum gluon condensate value: ⟨(α_s/π)G²⟩ = 0.022 GeV⁴
- Vacuum four-quark condensate parameters: κ_V = κ_A = 2.1
- Twist-2 moments used: A₂^π = 0.97, A₄^π = 0.255
- Weinberg sum rule WSR-0 deviates by -1.28% in vacuum; WSR-3 deviates by -96% in vacuum

## Context

This work extends the chiral mixing approach of Dey (1990) for finite-temperature spectral functions, using updated vacuum spectral functions from Hohler (2012) that include excited states (ρ' and a₁') and a universal perturbative continuum, which quantitatively describe ALEPH τ-decay data. Previous QCD sum rule analyses by Hatsuda (1992) and others typically used ground-state-plus-continuum ansätze with different continua in vector and axial-vector channels, which caused complications for chiral mixing; the present work resolves this by employing spectral functions with a universal separation of nonperturbative and perturbative regimes. The results enable future studies of how additional physics beyond low-energy chiral dynamics (such as contributions from heavier mesons or four-pion interactions) might restore QCD sum rule satisfaction at higher temperatures.
