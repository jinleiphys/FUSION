# Growth of Interaction Between Antiprotons (Negative Hyperons) and Nuclei in Polarized Matter: The Possibility to Study the Spin-Dependent Part of the Forward Scattering Amplitude in the Range of Low-Energies

> The influence of Coulomb interaction on the phenomenon of "optical" spin rotation of negatively charged particles (antiprotons, etc.) moving in matter with polarized nuclei is considered. It is shown that because the density of the antiproton (negative hyperon) wave function on the nucleus increases, the spin precession frequency grows as the particle decelerates. As a result, spin rotation of negatively charged particles becomes observable despite their rapid deceleration. This provides information about the spin--dependent part of the amplitude of coherent elastic zero--angle scattering in the range of low energies, where scattering experiments are practically impossible to perform.

# Growth of Interaction Between Antiprotons (Negative Hyperons) and Nuclei in Polarized Matter

## Key claim

The Coulomb attraction between negatively charged particles (antiprotons, negative hyperons) and nuclei causes the density of the particle wave function at the nucleus to increase as the particle decelerates, making the spin precession frequency grow as ~1/v; this growth compensates for rapid deceleration and renders spin rotation observable at low energies where direct scattering experiments are practically impossible.

## Method

The paper derives the forward scattering amplitude for negatively charged particles in polarized matter by separating Coulomb and nuclear contributions using the distorted-wave approximation (Eqs. a8-a12). The Coulomb distortion factor g_aa, which gives the probability to find the incident particle at the nucleus, is evaluated analytically for the attractive Coulomb case (Eq. a15), yielding g_aa^att = 2π/(κ(1-e^{-2π/κ})) which simplifies to 2παZc/v at low velocities. This factor multiplies the purely nuclear forward scattering amplitude to give the full amplitude (Eq. a13), which is then used to compute the effective pseudomagnetic field and spin precession frequency (Eqs. a34-a35).

## Key numbers

- For antiprotons with energy of 10^2 eV in a polarized hydrogen target, the spin rotation angle is estimated as ϑ ≃ 6×10^{-2} P_t (where P_t is the target polarization)
- For antiproton energies of 20 eV, the spin rotation angle is ϑ ∼ 3×10^{-1} P_t
- The cross section for antiproton capture by hydrogen forming protonium at energies above 10 eV is σ_pr ≤ 10^{-18} cm^2
- The liquid hydrogen number density used for normalization is N_l ≃ 4.25×10^{22} atoms/cm^3
- For n-p scattering, the scattering lengths are b^+ = 5.39×10^{-13} cm and b^- = -2.37×10^{-12} cm, giving A_1^{nuc} ≈ 1.46×10^{-12} cm
- The spin precession frequency scales as Ω_pr ∼ (2παc/v) × 6×10^7 (N/N_l) P_t, where 2παc/v ≫ 1 for v < 10^9 cm/s

## Context

This work extends the theory of "optical" spin rotation in nuclear pseudomagnetic fields, originally predicted for slow neutrons by Baryshevsky (1970s) and experimentally observed by Abragam et al. (1980s), to negatively charged particles. The key difference from neutrons is that Coulomb attraction enhances rather than suppresses nuclear interaction at low energies, in contrast to positively charged particles where Coulomb repulsion suppresses the effect. The paper shows that the Facility for Low-Energy Antiproton and Ion Research (FLAIR) and the availability of polarized antiprotons via spin-filtering methods enable measurement of the spin-dependent forward scattering amplitude at energies where direct scattering experiments are impossible. The analysis also suggests that long-lived antiproton-helium systems (∼10^{-6} s lifetime) could serve as probes of the nuclear pseudomagnetic field using laser-microwave techniques.
