# Multi-pomeron exchange model for $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ collisions at ultra-high energy

> A new variant of the effective pomeron exchange model is proposed for the description of the correlation, observed in $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ collisions at center-of-mass energy from SPS to LHC, between mean transverse momentum and charged particles multiplicity. The model is based on the Regge-Gribov approach. Smooth logarithmic growth with the collision energy was established for the parameter k, the mean rapidity density of charged particles produced by a single string. It was obtained in the model by the fitting of the available experimental data on charged particles rapidity density in $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ collisions. The main effect of the model, a gradual onset of string collectivity with the growth of collision energy, is accounted by a free parameter β that is responsible in an effective way for the string fusion phenomenon. Another free parameter, t, is used to define string tension. We extract parameters β and t from the available experimental results on <pt>-multiplicity correlation at nucleon collision energy $\sqrt{s}$ from 17 GeV to 7 TeV. Smooth dependence of both β and t on energy allows to make predictions for the correlation behavior at the collision energy of 14 TeV. The indications to the string interaction effects in high multiplicity events in $pp$ collisions at the LHC energies are also discussed.

# Multi-pomeron exchange model for $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ collisions at ultra-high energy

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a two-parameter effective pomeron exchange model, based on the Regge-Gribov approach, can describe the observed correlation between mean transverse momentum and charged particle multiplicity in $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ collisions from SPS to LHC energies, with the caveat that the string tension parameter $t$ splits into two subsets depending on the experimental data analysis procedure used.

## Method

The model starts from the probability distribution for $n$ cut-pomeron exchanges, with each cut-pomeron corresponding to a pair of strings that emit particles according to a Poisson distribution. The transverse momentum distribution from each string follows a modified Schwinger mechanism where the effective string tension scales as $n^\beta t$, with $\beta$ controlling the string fusion collectivity and $t$ setting the baseline string tension. The parameters $k$ (mean rapidity density from a single string) is first extracted from charged particle rapidity density data, then $\beta$ and $t$ are fitted to experimental $\langle p_t \rangle$-multiplicity correlation data across collision energies from 17 GeV to 7 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Parameter $k$ (mean rapidity density of charged particles from one string) follows $k = 0.255 + 0.0653 \ln \sqrt{s}$, extracted from experimental data on charged particle pseudorapidity density
- Parameter $\beta$ follows $\beta = 1.16[1 - (\ln\sqrt{s} - 2.52)^{-0.19}]$ from fitting $\langle p_t \rangle$-multiplicity correlations
- String tension parameter $t$ splits into two subsets: one around $t = 0.566 \text{ GeV}^2$ and another around $t = 0.428 \text{ GeV}^2$
- Predictions for $\langle p_t \rangle$-multiplicity correlation at $\sqrt{s} = 14 \text{ TeV}$ are made using three assumptions for $t$: no energy dependence ($t = 0.566 \text{ GeV}^2$), parabolic fit ($t = 0.731 \text{ GeV}^2$), and same as at 7 TeV ($t = 0.676 \text{ GeV}^2$)
- Fixed pomeron parameters: $\Delta = 0.139$, $\alpha' = 0.21 \text{ GeV}^{-2}$, $\gamma = 1.77 \text{ GeV}^{-2}$, $R_0^2 = 3.18 \text{ GeV}^{-2}$, $C = 1.5$

## Context

This work extends the multi-pomeron exchange model previously proposed by Armesto and collaborators, reducing the number of free parameters to two by establishing a smooth logarithmic growth of the single-string multiplicity parameter $k$ with energy. The model provides numerical agreement with the string fusion model predictions, where the ratio $k/(\langle n \rangle^\beta t)$ remains approximately constant across energy, supporting the hypothesis that string fusion is the source of collectivity in $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ collisions. The results enable predictions for the $\langle p_t \rangle$-multiplicity correlation at the future LHC energy of 14 TeV, which could help determine the energy dependence of the string tension parameter $t$.
