# Systematic Uncertainties in Theoretical Predictions of Jet Quenching

> We find that the current radiative energy loss kernels obtained from the opacity expansion dramatically violate the collinear approximation used in their derivation. By keeping only the lowest order in collinearity terms, models based on the opacity expansion have ~50% systematic uncertainty in the calculation of pi^0 R_AA in 0-5% most central RHIC collisions resulting in a systematic uncertainty of ~200% in the extracted medium density. Surprisingly, the inclusion of a thermal gluon mass on the order of the Debye screening scale affects R_AA at only about the 5% level due to non-intuitive coherence effects. For some observables such as R_AA, the effect of these uncertainties decreases with increasing jet energy; for others, such as the average number of radiated gluons, the effect is energy independent. We note that it is likely that the differences reported in the extracted values of medium parameters such as qhat by various jet energy loss models will fall within this collinear approximation systematic uncertainty; it is imperative for the quantitative extraction of medium parameters or the possible falsification of the hypothesis of weak coupling between the hard probes and soft modes of the quark gluon plasma medium that future radiative energy loss research push beyond the lowest order collinear approximation.

# Systematic Uncertainties in Theoretical Predictions of Jet Quenching

## Key claim

The radiative energy loss kernels derived from the opacity expansion dramatically violate the collinear approximation used in their derivation, leading to approximately 50% systematic uncertainty in the calculation of neutral pion R_AA in 0-5% most central RHIC collisions and approximately 200% systematic uncertainty in the extracted medium density. The authors note that differences in extracted values of medium parameters such as qhat by various jet energy loss models likely fall within this collinear approximation systematic uncertainty.

## Method

The authors quantitatively evaluate the systematic theoretical uncertainty in the opacity expansion (GLV) formalism for radiative energy loss by varying the ultraviolet cutoff in the transverse momentum (k_T) integration of the single inclusive gluon radiation spectrum, and by comparing two different collinearly-equivalent definitions of the momentum fraction x (x_+ versus x_E). They also compute the effect of including a non-zero thermal gluon mass at the level of the gluon propagators rather than as a phenomenological infrared cutoff, and assess how these uncertainties propagate into the extracted medium gluon rapidity density dN_g/dy from fits to PHENIX 0-5% most central pi^0 R_AA data.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 50% systematic uncertainty in the calculation of pi^0 R_AA in 0-5% most central RHIC collisions from keeping only lowest order collinear terms
- Approximately 200% systematic uncertainty in the extracted medium density (dN_g/dy) from the collinear approximation
- Inclusion of a thermal gluon mass on the order of the Debye screening scale affects R_AA at only about the 5% level
- Extracted dN_g/dy values (radiative only, times 1000) range from 2.0^{+0.4}_{-0.3} to 5.9^{+1.1}_{-1.0} depending on the choice of x definition and theta_max cutoff, demonstrating nearly a factor of 3 difference
- Extracted dN_g/dy values (radiative plus elastic, times 1000) range from 0.9^{+0.2}_{-0.2} to 1.5^{+0.3}_{-0.3} across the same variations

## Context

This work extends previous qualitative estimates of theoretical uncertainty in jet quenching calculations, including studies of the running coupling by Horowitz (2006) and Wicks (2007), probability leakage in Poisson convolution by Dainese (2004) and Eskola (2004), and phenomenological infrared cutoffs by Baier (2001). The paper focuses on the opacity expansion (GLV) formalism developed by Gyulassy (1999, 2000), Wiedemann (2000), and Djordjevic (2003, 2008), and notes that similar uncertainties likely affect other pQCD radiative energy loss formalisms including BDMPS-Z-ASW, higher-twist (HT), and thermal field theory (AMY). The results imply that quantitative extraction of medium parameters or falsification of the weak-coupling hypothesis for the quark-gluon plasma requires future radiative energy loss research to push beyond the lowest order collinear approximation.
