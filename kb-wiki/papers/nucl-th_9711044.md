# Strangelets and Strange Quark Matter

> The properties of finite lumps of strange quark matter (strangelets) with emphasis on the two scenarios of producing strange matter in relativistic heavy ion collisions are summarized. As an outlook, the possibility of short-lived strange composites and charmed matter are discussed for coming heavy ion experiments.

# Strangelets and Strange Quark Matter

## Key claim
Finite lumps of strange quark matter (strangelets) with baryon numbers between 6 and 30 may be produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but their stability and detectability depend critically on the bag parameter and on shell effects; the paper argues that a "valley of stability" for highly negatively charged strangelets exists at A=10-16 with Z ≤ -4, but only if strange quark matter is at least metastable and has a local minimum at some strangeness fraction.

## Method
The paper reviews the MIT bag model calculations for strangelets, including volume energy, zero-point energy, kinetic energy, and color magnetic and electric interactions, for baryon numbers up to A=40. It examines two production scenarios: coalescence of hyperons (using penalty factors extracted from experiment) and distillation from a quark-gluon plasma. Shell-model filling is applied to compute single-particle energies and identify magic numbers for strangelets.

## Key numbers
- At AGS (14 AGeV): coalescence penalty factors q ≈ 0.03, λ ≈ 0.13; at SPS (200 AGeV): q ≈ 0.0075, λ ≈ 0.35.
- Assuming sensitivity of 10⁻⁸, clusters up to A ≤ 6 can be produced via coalescence at AGS, and A ≤ 4 at SPS.
- Bag parameter values: B¹/⁴ = 145 MeV (absolutely stable strange matter), B ≥ 210 MeV (unstable), B¹/⁴ = 170 MeV and 180 MeV used in calculations.
- For B¹/⁴ = 170 MeV, shell effects are ~100 MeV/A; spin-orbit splitting between 1p3/2 and 1p1/2 is ~70 MeV.
- Magic strangelet candidates: A=10 with Z=-4, A=12 with Z=-6, A=14 with Z=-8, A=16 with Z=-10.
- For B¹/⁴ = 180 MeV or larger, no long-lived strangelet candidates exist.
- Long-lived strangelets have lifetimes τ = 10⁻⁴–10⁻⁵ s; short-lived strangelets decay via weak hadronic decay at τ = 10⁻⁵–10⁻¹⁰ s.
- Coalescence estimates for dihyperons: at AGS, 0.2 per central AuAu collision for S=-2 and 0.03 for S=-3; at SPS, 0.4 for S=-2 and 0.1 for S=-3.
- For charmed matter: pentaquark production estimated at every 10⁵ events at SPS; charmlets with A+|C| ≤ 4 at every 10⁶ events at RHIC.

## Context
This paper extends the earlier speculation by Bodmer (1971) about collapsed nuclei and Witten's (1984) scenario of absolutely stable strange quark matter, and builds on the MIT bag model of DeGrand et al. (1975). It contrasts the coalescence model of Dover (1993) with the distillation model of Greiner (1991) for strangelet production. The work enables experimental searches at AGS (E864, E895, E896, E910) and SPS (NA49) for short-lived and long-lived strangelets, and opens the possibility of exploring charmed matter at RHIC and LHC.
