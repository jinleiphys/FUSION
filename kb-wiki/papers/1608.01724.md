# A new approach for calculating nuclear symmetry energy

> By using the functional path integral method, we obtain a model-independent formula for nuclear symmetry energy, which explicitly shows the relation between nuclear symmetry energy and isospin susceptibility. The latter one is found to be a probe to the QCD chiral phase transition. We further found that, the nuclear symmetry energy has an abrupt change at the critical nuclear density where the chiral symmetry restores partially, which could be detected from the experiments.

## Key claim

The paper derives a model-independent formula for nuclear symmetry energy that links it to isospin susceptibility, and claims that the symmetry energy undergoes an abrupt decrease (42%–60%) at the critical nuclear density where chiral symmetry is partially restored, but this result depends on combining the NJL model with the relativistic mean field (RMF) model using a specific force parameter (NL-B1).

## Method

The authors use the functional path integral method to derive a model-independent expression for nuclear symmetry energy in terms of isospin susceptibility and baryon density. They then compute the isospin susceptibility as a function of baryon chemical potential using the two-flavor Nambu–Jona–Lasinio (NJL) model with parameters m = 5 MeV, g = 5.02 GeV⁻², and cutoff Λ = 653 MeV. To convert the critical baryon chemical potential to nuclear matter density, they employ the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with the NL-B1 force parameter.

## Key numbers

- The isospin susceptibility increases by 72% at edge A and 150% at edge B of the critical interval (baryon chemical potential ranging from 1023 MeV to 1044 MeV).
- The critical baryon chemical potential interval for chiral phase transition is 1023 MeV to 1044 MeV.
- The corresponding critical nuclear matter density interval is 0.305 fm⁻³ to 0.323 fm⁻³.
- The nuclear symmetry energy is suddenly decreased by 42% to 60% at the density region of the chiral phase transition.

## Context

This work extends the quasi-particle model approach of Xu (2009, 2010) by providing a model-independent formula for nuclear symmetry energy derived from first principles via functional path integrals. The result connects nuclear symmetry energy to QCD chiral phase transition, which has been studied using the NJL model by Klevansky (1992) and others. The finding that symmetry energy drops abruptly at the chiral transition density could be tested in future nuclear physics experiments and provides a link between nuclear physics (nucleon degrees of freedom) and low-energy QCD (quark and gluon degrees of freedom).
