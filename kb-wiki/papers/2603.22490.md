# Analyzing Fermionic Dark Matter scenarios with anomalous compact objects

> In this paper, we consider three compact objects (HESS J1731-347, PSR J1231-1411, XTE J1814-338) with anomalous mass-radius relation to analyze the possibility of being dark matter admixed neutron stars. We try to infer the dark matter particle properties, under the assumption of behaving as a free Fermi gas. The main novelty relies on the use of a baryonic equation of state obtained from first principles in the whole density range, that allows to eliminate the model dependence of the baryonic part of the calculation. Once the possible Dark Matter Admixed Neutron Star configurations are obtained, we check their stability and whether it is feasible for a Neutron Star to capture the necessary dark matter fraction. We show that two of the anomalous compact objects (HESS J1731-347 and PSR J1231-1411) can be explained with a small fraction of fermionic dark matter content in the star. The other compact object (XTE J1814-338) cannot be explained as a dark matter admixed neutron star, and becomes a potential candidate for a twin star.

## Key claim

Two of the three anomalous compact objects (HESS J1731-347 and PSR J1231-1411) can be explained as dark matter admixed neutron stars containing a small fraction of fermionic dark matter, but only for dark matter particle masses of 1 GeV or lower; the third object (XTE J1814-338) cannot be explained as a dark matter admixed neutron star and is instead a candidate for a twin star.

## Method

The authors used a set of 14 baryonic equations of state derived from first-principles calculations (regulator-independent effective field theory at low densities and QCD constraints at high densities) combined with a free Fermi gas model for fermionic dark matter. They solved the two-fluid Tolmann-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations assuming only gravitational interaction between baryonic and dark matter, considering six dark matter particle masses from 1 MeV to 100 GeV. For each combination of central pressures, they performed a stability analysis using the determinant of the particle number variation matrix to identify stable configurations, then computed chi-squared values comparing the interpolated mass-radius curves to the observed masses and radii of the three compact objects.

## Key numbers

- HESS J1731-347: radius = 10.4 km (with errors -0.78/+0.86 km), mass = 0.77 solar masses (with errors -0.17/+0.20 solar masses)
- PSR J1231-1411: radius = 9.91 km (with errors -0.86/+0.86 km), mass = 1.12 solar masses (with errors -0.07/+0.07 solar masses)
- XTE J1814-338: radius = 7.0 km (with errors -0.4/+0.4 km), mass = 1.21 solar masses (with errors -0.05/+0.05 solar masses)
- For dark matter mass = 1 GeV and 9% dark matter fraction: minimum chi-squared = 1.04 for PSR J1231-1411 (first scenario reaching 1-sigma agreement)
- For dark matter mass = 1 GeV and 10% dark matter fraction: minimum chi-squared = 0.02 for HESS J1731-347 and 0.89 for PSR J1231-1411
- For dark matter mass = 100 GeV: minimum chi-squared = 0.84 for HESS J1731-347, 3.01 for PSR J1231-1411, and 126.36 for XTE J1814-338
- For dark matter mass = 100 MeV with 1% dark matter fraction: minimum chi-squared = 0.75 for HESS J1731-347, 2.83 for PSR J1231-1411, and 124.60 for XTE J1814-338
- Dark matter particle masses considered: 1 MeV, 10 MeV, 100 MeV, 1 GeV, 10 GeV, and 100 GeV
- For dark matter masses of 100 MeV and below, the dark matter component forms a halo extending beyond the baryonic radius, with total gravitational masses up to 600 solar masses (for 1 MeV dark matter)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter admixed neutron stars by using a baryonic equation of state derived from first principles (Alarcon 2024), eliminating the model dependence of the baryonic component that affected earlier analyses using phenomenological mean-field models such as BSk22 or relativistic mean field models like DDME2 and DD2 parameterizations. The results enable future observational constraints on fermionic dark matter particle properties from anomalous compact objects, and identify XTE J1814-338 as a potential twin star candidate requiring further investigation beyond the dark matter admixed neutron star scenario.
