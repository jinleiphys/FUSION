# Final state interaction in spin asymmetry and GDH sum rule for incoherent pion production on the deuteron

> The contribution of incoherent single pion photoproduction to the spin response of the deuteron, i.e., the asymmetry of the total photoabsorption cross section with respect to parallel and antiparallel spins of photon and deuteron, is calculated over the region of the $Δ$-resonance with inclusion of final state $NN$- and $πN$-rescattering. Sizeable effects, mainly from $NN$-rescattering, are found leading to an appreciable reduction of the spin asymmetry. Furthermore, the contribution to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn integral is explicitly evaluated by integration up to a photon energy of 550 MeV. Final state interaction reduces the value of the integral to about half of the value obtained for the pure impulse approximation.

# Final state interaction in spin asymmetry and GDH sum rule for incoherent pion production on the deuteron

## Key claim
Final state interaction, predominantly from NN-rescattering, reduces the spin asymmetry and the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn integral for incoherent single pion photoproduction on the deuteron to about half the value obtained in the pure impulse approximation, demonstrating that a simple extraction of the neutron spin asymmetry from deuteron measurements is not possible.

## Method
The authors calculated the total photoabsorption cross sections for circularly polarized photons on a vector-polarized deuteron target with spins parallel and antiparallel, for incoherent single pion photoproduction channels (γd → ppπ⁻, γd → nnπ⁺, γd → npπ⁰) in the Δ-resonance region. They included final state interaction via NN-rescattering (all partial waves with J ≤ 3, using the Paris potential) and πN-rescattering (S- through D-waves) using separable representations of realistic two-body interactions, and integrated the spin asymmetry to obtain the GDH integral up to 550 MeV.

## Key numbers
- For π⁰-production, final state interaction reduces the spin asymmetry by about 170 μb at the Δ-peak; for charged pions, the reduction is about 35 μb at the Δ-peak.
- The GDH integral for γd → npπ⁰ at 550 MeV: 287 μb (IA), 220 μb (IA+NN), 216 μb (IA+NN+πN).
- The GDH integral for γd → ppπ⁻ at 550 MeV: −73 μb (IA), −87 μb (IA+NN), −88 μb (IA+NN+πN).
- The GDH integral for γd → nnπ⁺ at 550 MeV: −27 μb (IA), −39 μb (IA+NN), −41 μb (IA+NN+πN).
- Total GDH integral for all incoherent pion channels at 550 MeV: 187 μb (IA), 94 μb (IA+NN), 87 μb (IA+NN+πN).
- The sum of free nucleon GDH integrals at 550 MeV from the model is 197 μb, compared to the total deuteron value of 87 μb with full FSI.
- The coherent π⁰-production contribution to the GDH integral at 550 MeV is 63 μb (from prior work).
- The photodisintegration channel GDH integral at 550 MeV is −413 μb (from prior work).
- The total deuteron GDH integral from explicit integration up to 550 MeV is −263 μb, compared to the sum rule prediction of 0.65 μb.
- The MAID 2000 analysis gives I^{GDH}_{n+p}(550 MeV) = 282 μb, larger than the model value of 197 μb.

## Context
This work extends the earlier impulse approximation calculations of Arenhoevel et al. (1997) for the deuteron spin asymmetry and GDH sum rule by including final state interaction effects. The authors had previously shown in Darwish et al. (2003) that FSI reduces unpolarized cross sections by about 5% for charged pions and about 35% for neutral pions. The present results demonstrate that even for charged pion production, where the impulse approximation works reasonably for unpolarized cross sections, the spin asymmetry is significantly modified by FSI. The findings contradict the assumption that pion production on proton and neutron in the deuteron adds incoherently, and show that a measurement on the deuteron cannot provide direct access to the neutron spin asymmetry, though it may serve as a check observable for models of pion photoproduction on the neutron.
