# Rapidity Divergences and Deep Inelastic Scattering in the Endpoint Region

> The deep inelastic scattering cross section in the endpoint region, $x \sim 1$, has been subjected to extensive analysis. We revisit this process, and show that in the endpoint individual factors in the factorized hadronic tensor have rapidity divergences. We regulate these divergences using a recently introduced rapidity regulator, and find that each factor requires a different scale to minimize large rapidity logarithms. Interestingly, in this case running in rapidity is non-perturbative. In addition, we give an operator definition of the parton distribution function in the endpoint region, and remark on the issues that should be considered in constructing this function.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the endpoint region of deep inelastic scattering (DIS), the collinear factor and soft function each contain rapidity divergences that require different rapidity scales to minimize large logarithms (ν ∼ Q for the collinear factor and ν ∼ Q(1−x) for the soft function), and that the resulting running in rapidity is non-perturbative and must be absorbed into the definition of the parton distribution function.

## Method

The authors use soft collinear effective theory (SCET) to factorize the DIS hadronic tensor in the endpoint region (x ∼ 1) into a hard coefficient, a jet function, a collinear factor, and a soft function. They perform one-loop calculations of the collinear and soft operator matrix elements using the rapidity regulator introduced by Chiu et al. (2011, 2012), including zero-bin subtractions for both collinear and soft contributions. The collinear function is shown to have only virtual contributions due to label momentum conservation and zero-bin subtraction, while the soft function receives both virtual and real contributions with zero-bin subtractions that remove overlap with collinear regions.

## Key numbers

- The collinear factor has rapidity logarithms minimized at ν ∼ bn·p ≈ Q, where Q is the large energy scale of the virtual photon.
- The soft function has rapidity logarithms minimized at ν ∼ κ ∼ Q(1−x), which is much smaller than Q in the endpoint region.
- The one-loop collinear counter term contains a term (1/ε)(3/4 + ln(ν/(bn·p))) in the α_s C_F/π expansion.
- The one-loop soft counter term contains a term (1/ε)[1/(ℓ)_+ − ln ν δ(ℓ)] in the α_s C_F/π expansion.
- The μ anomalous dimension for the collinear function is γ^μ_n = (2α_s C_F/π)(3/4 + ln(ν/(bn·p_p))).
- The ν anomalous dimensions are γ^ν_n = (α_s C_F/π) ln(μ²/m_g²) and γ^ν_s = −(α_s C_F/π) ln(μ²/m_g²), which sum to zero but depend on the gluon mass m_g, indicating non-perturbative running.

## Context

This work extends prior SCET analyses of DIS in the endpoint region by Manohar (2003), Pecjak (2005), Chay (2005), Idilbi (2006, 2007), Chen (2006), and Becher (2006), and earlier QCD factorization studies by Sterman (1986), Korchemsky (1988, 1992), and Catani (1989). The paper identifies for the first time that a single large logarithm of the ratio Q/(Q(1−x)) remains after combining the collinear and soft functions into the PDF, and that the rapidity running is non-perturbative, implying that models of the PDF in the endpoint may need to include logarithmically enhanced parameters. The results also show that the soft function can be expressed solely in terms of initial-state Wilson lines, ensuring PDF universality.
