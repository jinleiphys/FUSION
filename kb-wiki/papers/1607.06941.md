# Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation of ground state properties of even-even and odd Mo and Ru isotopes

> In a previous work [Int. J. Mod. Phys. E 24, 1550073 (2015)], hereafter referred as paper I, we have investigated the ground-state properties of Nd, Ce and Sm isotopes within Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with SLy5 skyrme force in which the pairing strength has been generalized with a new proposed formula. However, that formula is more appropriate for the region of Nd. In this work, we have studied the ground-state properties of both even-even and odd Mo and Ru isotopes. For this, we have used Hartree- Fock-Bogoliubov method with SLy4 skyrme force, and a new formula of the pairing strength which is more accurate for this region of nuclei. The results have been compared with available experimental data, the results of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations based on the D1S Gogny effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and predictions of some nuclear models such as Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) and Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their new generalized formula for the pairing strength, \( V_0^{n,p} = 155.88\,A^{1/6} \), when used with the SLy4 Skyrme force in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations, reproduces ground-state properties of even-even and odd Mo and Ru isotopes with accuracy comparable to or better than the Finite Range Droplet Model, Relativistic Mean Field theory, and HFB calculations with the D1S Gogny force, but they note that the formula is specifically tuned for this region of nuclei and is not universal.

## Method

The authors used the HFBTHO (v2.00d) code, which diagonalizes the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Hamiltonian in an axial Transformed Harmonic Oscillator basis, with the SLy4 Skyrme functional and a mixed surface-volume zero-range pairing interaction. For each isotope, the pairing strength \( V_0^{n,p} \) (identical for protons and neutrons) was iteratively adjusted to reproduce the experimental ground-state energy, and the resulting values were fitted to a new formula \( V_0^{n,p} = 155.88\,A^{1/6} \). For odd isotopes, the blocking of quasi-particle states was implemented in the equal filling approximation, conserving time-reversal symmetry.

## Key numbers

*   The maximal error in binding energy per nucleon (BE/A) between calculation and experiment is 0.04772 MeV for Mo and 0.03415 MeV for Ru.
*   The mean absolute error in total binding energy (BE) between calculation and experiment is 1.5777 MeV for Mo and 0.9790 MeV for Ru.
*   The mean absolute error in two-neutron separation energy (\( S_{2n} \)) between calculation and experiment is 0.090 MeV for Mo and 0.075 MeV for Ru.
*   The mean absolute error in one-neutron separation energy (\( S_n \)) between calculation and experiment is 0.061 MeV for Mo and 0.028 MeV for Ru.
*   The difference between neutron and proton rms radii reaches 0.560 fm for \( ^{140} \)Mo and 0.516 fm for \( ^{144} \)Ru.
*   The two-neutron drip-line nucleus is predicted to be \( ^{132} \)Mo for Mo isotopes and \( ^{138} \)Ru for Ru isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Bassem and Oulne, 2015) on Nd, Ce, and Sm isotopes, where a different pairing-strength formula was used with the SLy5 force. The present study applies the HFB method with the SLy4 force and a new pairing formula to the Mo and Ru isotopic chains, comparing results against experimental data, HFB calculations with the D1S Gogny force (Amedee, year not given), the Finite Range Droplet Model (Moller, 1997), and Relativistic Mean Field theory (Lalazissis, year not given). The results enable improved predictions for ground-state properties in this mass region and suggest candidates for neutron halo and shape phase transitions.
