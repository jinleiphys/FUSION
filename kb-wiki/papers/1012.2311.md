# PS-meson form factors in relativistic quantum mechanics and constraints from covariant space-time translations

> The role of Poincaré covariant space-time translations is investigated in the case of the pseudoscalar-meson charge form factors calculated within a relativistic quantum mechanics framework. It is shown that this role extends beyond the standard energy-momentum conservation, which is accounted for in all works based on this general approach. It implies constraints that have been largely ignored until now but should be nevertheless fulfilled to ensure the full Poincaré covariance. The violation of these constraints, which is more or less important depending on the form of relativistic quantum mechanics that is employed, points to the validity of using a single-particle current, which is generally assumed in calculations of form factors. In short, these constraints concern the relation of the momentum transferred to the constituents to the one transferred to the system, which most often differ in relativistic quantum mechanics while they are equal in field theory. How to account for the constraints, as well as restoring the equivalence of different relativistic quantum mechanics approaches in estimating form factors, is discussed. It is mentioned that the result so obtained can be identified to a dispersion-relation one. A short conclusion relative to the underlying dynamics is given in the pion case.

# PS-meson form factors in relativistic quantum mechanics and constraints from covariant space-time translations

## Key claim

Poincaré covariant space-time translations impose constraints on pseudoscalar-meson charge form factors beyond standard energy-momentum conservation, requiring that the squared momentum transferred to the constituents equal the squared momentum transferred to the system; these constraints have been largely ignored in relativistic quantum mechanics calculations but must be fulfilled to ensure full Poincaré covariance, and when accounted for, they restore the equivalence of different relativistic quantum mechanics approaches.

## Method

The authors derived constraints from the double commutator of the total momentum operator with the current operator, leading to the condition \(q^2 = (p_i - p_f)^2\) for a single-particle current. They implemented these constraints by introducing a multiplicative factor \(\alpha\) on the momentum transfer variable \(Q\) in the single-particle current, determined by solving the equation that equates the squared momentum transferred to the system with that transferred to the constituents. The resulting form factor expression was then integrated over the spectator momentum to obtain a dispersion-relation-like form in terms of Mandelstam variables \(s_i\) and \(s_f\).

## Key numbers

- Pion charge form factor calculated at low \(Q^2\) (left panel) and intermediate \(Q^2\) (right panel) up to approximately 10 GeV\(^2\), using a Gaussian wave function with string tension 0.2 GeV\(^2\)
- The factor \(2e_k/M\) determining the size of constraint effects can be as large as 6 for the pion at low \(Q^2\)
- For the kaon, the factor \(2e_k/M\) is smaller than for the pion due to the larger kaon mass
- At \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) and \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\), the integrand of the form factor was shown as a function of spectator momentum \(p = |\vec{p}|\) for different approaches

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study with scalar constituents (Desplanques 2008) to spin-1/2 constituents (quarks) for pseudoscalar mesons. It addresses the long-standing problem that different forms of relativistic quantum mechanics, instant form (Bakamjian and Thomas 1953), front form (Dirac 1949), and point form, give different form factor results when using a single-particle current approximation. The constraints from space-time translations, previously noted by Lev (1993), are shown to resolve these discrepancies. The resulting form factor expression agrees with a dispersion-relation approach by Melikhov (2001) but disagrees by a factor \((s_i + s_f + Q^2)/(2\sqrt{s_i s_f})\) with the result of Krutov and Troitsky (2001). This work enables reliable comparison of form factor calculations to measurements by removing the dependence on the chosen hypersurface, and opens the way to treating inelastic and time-like processes as well as non-zero spin systems such as the nucleon and its resonances.
