# Change of shape in the yrast sequence in 50Cr

> In shell model calculations for the yrast even spin states of 50Cr, the static quadrupole moments of the low spin states 2+_1 and 4+_1 are negative but those of the high spin states 10+_1, 12+_1 and 14+_1 are positive. Even spin states beyond the single j shell limit, J_max=14, again have negative moments. While the B(E2)'s for the J -> J-2 transitions are strongest along the yrast path for J <= 8, it is found that the transition from the second J=10 state to the first J=8 state (10+_2 -> 8+_1) is much stronger than the 10+_1 -> 8+_1 transition. We also note that while the 16+_1 -> 14+_1 transition is weak, the 16+_1 -> 14+_2 is quite strong.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations in the full $fp$ space for $^{50}\text{Cr}$ show that the yrast band undergoes multiple shape changes: from prolate at low spin ($J \le 8$) to oblate at intermediate spin ($10^+_1$, $12^+_1$, $14^+_1$) and back to prolate for very high spin ($J \ge 16$), with the $10^+_2$ state rather than the $10^+_1$ state belonging to the prolate ground-state band.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for $^{50}\text{Cr}$ using the FPD6 interaction in model spaces of increasing size, from the single $f_{7/2}$ shell ($t=0$) up to the complete $fp$ shell ($t=10$), where $t$ is the maximum number of nucleons allowed to be excited from the $f_{7/2}$ orbit. Static quadrupole moments $Q$ and $B(\text{E2})$ transition strengths were computed for yrast and near-yrast even-spin states using effective charges $e_p=1.5$ and $e_n=0.5$, with separate tabulation of proton and neutron contributions.

## Key numbers

- Static quadrupole moment of $2^+_1$: $-7.79\; e\,\text{fm}^2$ (single $j$ shell, $t=0$); $-22.01\; e\,\text{fm}^2$ ($t=2$)
- Static quadrupole moment of $10^+_1$: $+19.66\; e\,\text{fm}^2$ ($t=0$); $+45.70\; e\,\text{fm}^2$ ($t=3$)
- Static quadrupole moment of $16^+_1$: $+4.64\; e\,\text{fm}^2$ ($t=1$); $-9.88\; e\,\text{fm}^2$ ($t=4$)
- $B(\text{E2})$ for $10^+_1 \rightarrow 8^+_1$: $60.1\; e^2\,\text{fm}^4$ ($t=0$); $28.7\; e^2\,\text{fm}^4$ ($t=3$)
- $B(\text{E2})$ for $10^+_2 \rightarrow 8^+_1$: $25.1\; e^2\,\text{fm}^4$ ($t=0$); $140.0\; e^2\,\text{fm}^4$ ($t=3$)
- $B(\text{E2})$ for $16^+_1 \rightarrow 14^+_1$: $4.8\; e^2\,\text{fm}^4$ ($t=1$); $4.5\; e^2\,\text{fm}^4$ ($t=4$)
- $B(\text{E2})$ for $16^+_1 \rightarrow 14^+_2$: $103.5\; e^2\,\text{fm}^4$ ($t=2$); $122.8\; e^2\,\text{fm}^4$ ($t=4$)
- Maximum spin in complete $fp$ space: $J=22$ (even), $J=23$ (overall)

## Context

This work extends the single $j$ shell calculations of McCullen, Bayman, and Zamick (1964) and Ginocchio and French (1963) for $^{50}\text{Cr}$ by allowing nucleons to be excited from the $f_{7/2}$ shell into the full $fp$ space, following the approach of Caurier et al. (1994) who showed that such extended calculations are necessary to capture rotational behavior in $^{48}\text{Cr}$. The results demonstrate that the simple single-$j$ picture fails for quadrupole moments and $B(\text{E2})$ strengths even where it reproduces energies, and that the yrast band in $^{50}\text{Cr}$ exhibits multiple shape changes with spin. The identification of $10^+_2$ as a member of the prolate ground-state band and the weak $16^+_1 \rightarrow 14^+_1$ transition due to different dominant configurations open questions about the proper truncation scheme for describing collective motion in the $fp$ shell, where the large $p_{3/2}-f_{7/2}$ splitting prevents simple SU(3) approaches as noted by Bhatt and McGrory (1971).
