# Sensitivity of neutron to proton ratio toward the high density behavior of symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions

> The symmetry energy at sub and supra-saturation densities has a great importance in understanding the exact nature of asymmetric nuclear matter as well as neutron star, but, it is poor known, especially at supra-saturation densities. We will demonstrate here that the neutron to proton ratios from different kind of fragments is able to determine the supra-saturation behavior of symmetry energy or not. For this purpose, a series of Sn isotopes are simulated at different incident energies using the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model with either a soft or a stiff symmetry energy for the present study. It is found that the single neutron to proton ratio from free nucleons as well as LCP's is sensitive towards the symmetry energy, incident energy as well as isospin asymmetry of the system. However, with the double neutron to proton ratio, it is true only for the free nucleons. It is possible to study the high density behavior of symmetry energy by using the neutron to proton ratio from free nucleons.

## Key claim
The neutron-to-proton ratio from free nucleons can serve as a probe of the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy, but only when using the double ratio; the single ratio from light charged particles (LCPs) and intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) is not sensitive to supra-saturation densities because those fragments are not produced in the high-density region.

## Method
The authors used the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate collisions of three Sn isotope systems (112Sn+112Sn, 124Sn+124Sn, and 132Sn+132Sn) at incident energies from 50 to 600 MeV/nucleon at semicentral geometry. They employed a soft equation of state with either a soft (γi=0.5) or stiff (γi=1.5) symmetry energy and analyzed the time evolution of average density, fragment production, and single and double neutron-to-proton ratios at the time of maximum compression and at freeze-out time (t=200 fm/c). Cluster yields were determined using a coalescence model with coalescence parameters R0=3.5 fm and PFermi=268 MeV/c.

## Key numbers
- The soft symmetry energy corresponds to γi=0.5 and the stiff symmetry energy to γi=1.5, with the symmetry potential energy coefficient Cs,p=35.19 MeV.
- The soft equation of state uses parameters α=-356 MeV, β=303 MeV, and γ=7/6, giving an isoscalar compressibility κ=200 MeV.
- For the 132Sn+132Sn system at b=2 fm, the average density reaches maximum at t=18 fm/c for E=50 MeV/nucleon and at t=10 fm/c for E=600 MeV/nucleon.
- The difference between the time of maximum compression and return to normal density is approximately 15 fm/c at E=50 MeV/nucleon and 10 fm/c at E=600 MeV/nucleon.
- LCPs are produced in the supra-saturation density region only after the incident energy reaches 200 MeV/nucleon; no IMFs are produced in the supra-saturation density region at any energy studied.
- The double neutron-to-proton ratio from LCPs becomes constant above 200 MeV/nucleon, indicating insensitivity to the high-density symmetry energy.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities by the MSU group (Zhang 2008) and others (Sun 2010, Kumar 2011) that used double neutron-to-proton ratios and isospin diffusion for Sn isotopes at 35-50 MeV/nucleon. It also builds on studies of supra-saturation density probes such as pion ratios (Li 2002, 2003, 2009; Feng 2010) and kaon ratios (Wolter 2009), where conflicting predictions for the stiffness of the symmetry energy were obtained from different models (IBUU04 predicting soft, ImIQMD predicting stiff). The results enable future experimental comparisons of free nucleon neutron-to-proton ratios with FOPI data to constrain the high-density symmetry energy, similar to recent pion ratio comparisons.
