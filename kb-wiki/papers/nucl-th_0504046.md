# Systematics of g factors of 2_1^+ states in even-even nuclei from Gd to Pt: A microscopic description by the projected shell model

> The systematics of g factor of first excited 2^+ state vs neutron number N is studied by the projected shell model. The study covers the even-even nuclei of all isotopic chains from Gd to Pt. g factors are calculated by using the many-body wavefunctions that reproduces well the energy levels and B(E2)'s of the ground-state bands. For Gd to W isotopes the characteristic feature of the g factor data along an isotopic chain is described by the present model. Deficiency of the model in the g factor description for the heavier Os and Pt isotopes is discussed.

## Key claim

The projected shell model (PSM) can reproduce the observed systematic trends of g(2_1^+) factors across isotopic chains from Gd to W, but fails for heavier Os and Pt isotopes because the axially deformed quasiparticle vacuum state lacks sufficient correlations to describe these transitional, gamma-soft nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed angular-momentum-projected shell model calculations for 61 even-even nuclei from Gd (Z=64) to Pt (Z=78) with neutron numbers N=88 to 120. They constructed deformed Nilsson+BCS quasiparticle vacuum states using fixed quadrupole deformation parameters for each nucleus, then projected onto good angular momentum and diagonalized a pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian with standard interaction strengths. The same wavefunctions that reproduced experimental g-band energies and B(E2) values were used to compute g factors via the magnetic dipole operator with standard g-factors (g_s damped by 0.75).

## Key numbers

- For Gd isotopes: experimental g(2_1^+) ranges from 0.48(4) in 152Gd to 0.36(2) in 160Gd; calculated values range from 0.43 to 0.26
- For Dy isotopes: experimental g(2_1^+) ranges from 0.36(2) in 154Dy to 0.34(1) in 164Dy; calculated values range from 0.47 to 0.26
- For Er isotopes: experimental g(2_1^+) ranges from 0.40(6) in 156Er to 0.317(7) in 170Er; calculated values range from 0.49 to 0.26
- For W isotopes: experimental g(2_1^+) ranges from 0.25(5) in 168W to 0.31(2) in 186W; calculated values range from 0.32 to 0.26
- For Os isotopes: experimental g(2_1^+) rises from 0.26(2) in 186Os to 0.40(1) in 192Os; calculated values remain nearly constant at 0.25-0.26
- For Pt isotopes: experimental g(2_1^+) is nearly constant at 0.28-0.32; calculated values range from 0.11 to 0.23
- B(E2, 2→0) values range from 0.35(3) e^2b^2 in 152Gd to 1.11(2) e^2b^2 in 164Dy, with the calculation using effective charges e^π=1.5e and e^ν=0.5e

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of g(2_1^+) factors initiated by Berant et al. (2004), who compiled new and accumulated data for rare-earth and heavier nuclei. The PSM results are contrasted with the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), which gives overly strong particle-number dependence and fails to reproduce the flat midshell behavior, and with the phenomenological model of Zhang et al. (2006). The calculation successfully describes the downsloping trend in Gd, Dy, Er; the flat behavior in Yb, Hf; and the upsloping trend in W, but the failure for Os and Pt indicates that axially deformed basis states are insufficient for these gamma-soft transitional nuclei, motivating future inclusion of D-pair correlations or generator coordinate method approaches.
