# Electric dipole polarizability from first principles calculations

> The electric dipole polarizability quantifies the low-energy behaviour of the dipole strength and is related to critical observables such as the radii of the proton and neutron distributions. Its computation is challenging because most of the dipole strength lies in the scattering continuum. In this paper we combine integral transforms with the coupled-cluster method and compute the dipole polarizability using bound-state techniques. Employing different interactions from chiral effective field theory, we confirm the strong correlation between the dipole polarizability and the charge radius, and study its dependence on three-nucleon forces. We find good agreement with data for the 4He, 40Ca, and 16O nuclei, and predict the dipole polarizability for the rare nucleus 22O.

## Key claim

The authors compute the electric dipole polarizability for \(^{4}\text{He}\), \(^{16}\text{O}\), \(^{40}\text{Ca}\), and \(^{22}\text{O}\) using coupled-cluster theory combined with integral transforms, finding good agreement with experimental data for the stable nuclei but a significant discrepancy for the neutron-rich \(^{22}\text{O}\) (theory 0.86(4) fm\(^3\) vs. experiment 0.43(4) fm\(^3\) over the full energy range), which they attribute to the need for improved treatment of loosely bound neutrons.

## Method

The authors combine the Stieltjes integral transform with the coupled-cluster method, using an equation-of-motion approach to solve a Schrödinger-like equation with a source term on a bound-state basis. They employ the Lanczos algorithm for non-symmetric matrices to evaluate the continued-fraction representation of the polarizability, truncating the cluster operator \(\hat{T}\) and the excitation operator \(\hat{\mathcal{R}}\) at singles-and-doubles excitations. Calculations are performed with the NNLO\(_{\text{sat}}\) interaction from chiral effective field theory, using a Hartree-Fock basis built on 15 major harmonic oscillator shells with model-space sizes up to \(N_{\text{max}}=14\).

## Key numbers

* \(^{4}\text{He}\): \(\alpha_D = 0.0735(1)\) fm\(^3\) with NNLO\(_{\text{sat}}\) at \(N_{\text{max}}=14\); experiment is 0.074(9) fm\(^3\).
* \(^{16}\text{O}\): \(\alpha_D = 0.57(1)\) fm\(^3\) with NNLO\(_{\text{sat}}\) at \(N_{\text{max}}=14\); experiment is 0.585(9) fm\(^3\).
* \(^{22}\text{O}\): \(\alpha_D = 0.86(4)\) fm\(^3\) with NNLO\(_{\text{sat}}\) at \(N_{\text{max}}=14\); experiment is 0.43(4) fm\(^3\) over the full energy range.
* \(^{22}\text{O}\) low-lying strength: theory gives 0.05(1) fm\(^3\) for the first 3 MeV; experiment gives 0.07(2) fm\(^3\).
* \(^{4}\text{He}\) convergence: uncertainty at \(N_{\text{max}}=14\) across different \(\hbar\Omega\) values is about 0.1%.
* \(^{16}\text{O}\) conservative error: 2.8% for \(\alpha_D\) and 0.7% for the charge radius at \(N_{\text{max}}=14\).
* \(^{22}\text{O}\) conservative uncertainty: about 8% at \(N_{\text{max}}=14\).

## Context

This work extends the coupled-cluster approach for dipole response from Bacca et al. (2013) and Hagen et al. (2015) to include three-nucleon forces from chiral effective field theory, confirming the strong correlation between dipole polarizability and charge radius found in earlier ab initio studies. The method enables predictions for rare neutron-rich nuclei such as \(^{22}\text{O}\), where the discrepancy with the Coulomb excitation data from Leistenschneider et al. (2001) highlights the need for improved treatment of loosely bound systems and suggests that future experiments on unstable nuclei could further constrain the neutron equation of state.
