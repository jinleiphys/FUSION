# Dilepton excess from local parity breaking in baryon matter

> We propose that local parity breaking induced by a large-scale fluctuation of topological charge at large temperatures and/or condensation of pseudoscalar mesons in the isotriplet channel for large baryon densities may be responsible for the substantial dilepton excess that is found for low invariant masses and moderate values of $p_T$ in central heavy ions collisions. This insofar unexplained enhancement could be understood by a combination of two effects leading both to an excess of $e^+e^-$ and $μ^+ μ^-$ pairs with respect to theoretical predictions based on conventional hadronic processes: (a) a modification of the dispersion relation of photons and vector mesons propagating in such a nuclear medium due to local parity breaking; (b) the appearance of new decay channels, forbidden by parity conservation in QCD in the usual vacuum. Possible signatures of this effect and perspectives for its detection are discussed.

# Dilepton excess from local parity breaking in baryon matter

## Key claim
Local parity breaking (LPB) induced by a large-scale fluctuation of topological charge at high temperatures, or by pseudoscalar meson condensation in the isotriplet channel at large baryon densities, may explain the substantial dilepton excess observed in central heavy ion collisions for low invariant masses (M < 1.2 GeV) and moderate transverse momenta (p_T < 1 GeV). The authors propose that this excess arises from a combination of modified dispersion relations for photons and vector mesons in the nuclear medium, plus new decay channels forbidden by parity conservation in vacuum, but they explicitly postpone a complete comparison with experimental data to a subsequent publication.

## Method
The authors construct a Vector Dominance Model (VDM) Lagrangian for the lightest vector mesons (rho, omega, phi) in SU(3) flavor, adding a parity-odd Chern-Simons term that couples to a time-dependent pseudoscalar background field zeta (derived from an axial chemical potential mu_5). They derive modified mass-shell equations for vector mesons in the presence of this LPB background, compute the polarization-dependent mass splitting for transverse modes, and use a thermal Boltzmann average to estimate the dilepton production rate from rho and omega decays, implementing PHENIX experimental cuts and an effective temperature T = 220 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The topological charge fluctuation is assumed to survive for a fireball lifetime of tau_fireball = 5-10 fm, with a formation time tau_heating < 0.5 fm.
- For u,d quarks, the left-right mixing time is 1/m_q ~ 40 fm, much larger than tau_fireball; for strange quarks, 1/m_s ~ 1 fm, much smaller than tau_fireball, so strange quarks decouple from the LPB effect.
- The Chern-Simons coupling zeta is related to the axial chemical potential by zeta = 1.5 mu_5; natural estimates give zeta in the range 150-400 MeV.
- For zeta = 400 MeV, the in-medium rho yield is enhanced by a factor of 1.8 relative to the omega yield, based on PHENIX data.
- The estimated partial width for the parity-forbidden decay eta -> pi pi is Gamma ~ 100 MeV for zeta = 200 MeV, comparable to the rho -> pi pi width of 150 MeV.
- The effective temperature used in the thermal average is T = 220 MeV, matching PHENIX conditions.

## Context
This work extends the concept of local parity breaking previously discussed in connection with the Chiral Magnetic Effect (Kharzeev, 2008) and spontaneous parity violation at finite density (Andrianov et al., 2010). It addresses the unexplained dilepton excess observed by PHENIX (2007), NA60, and CERES experiments, which conventional mechanisms such as thermal vector resonance broadening or mass dropping (Rapp, 2000; Renk, 2004; Cassing, 2004; Zahed, 2005) have failed to fully explain. The paper enables future event-by-event measurements of dilepton circular polarization as a direct signature of LPB, and motivates detailed calculations of additional hadronic processes (eta, eta' Dalitz decays, eta -> pi pi) that could further contribute to the excess.
