# Manifestation of α-clustering in $^{10}$Be via α-knockout reaction

> Background: Proton-induced α-knockout reactions empower direct experimental manifestations of α-clustering in nuclei. This is obtained by relating the theoretical descriptions of clustering states with experimental reaction observables. It is desired to introduce microscopic structure models into the theoretical frameworks for α-knockout reactions. Purpose: Our goal is to probe the α-clustering in $^{10}$Be nucleus by proton-induced α-knockout reaction observables. Method: We adopt an extended version of the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave function of $^{10}$Be and integrate it with the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework for the calculation of (p,pα) knockout reactions. Results: We make the first calculation for the $^{10}$Be(p,pα)$^{6}$He reaction at 250 MeV implementing a microscopic α-cluster wave function and predict the triple differential cross sections (TDX). Furthermore, by constructing artificial states of the target nucleus $^{10}$Be with compact or dilute spatial distributions, the TDX is found to be highly sensitive to the extent of clustering in the target nuclei. Conclusions: These results provide reliable manifestation of the α-clustering in $^{10}$Be.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the triple differential cross section (TDX) of the $^{10}$Be(p,pα)$^{6}$He reaction at 250 MeV is highly sensitive to the degree of α-clustering in the target nucleus, but this sensitivity is conditional on the peripheral nature of the knockout reaction, which selectively probes the surface region where the approximated reduced width amplitude (RWA) is reliable.

## Method
The authors integrated an extended Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave function for $^{10}$Be and $^{6}$He into the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework to calculate the (p,pα) knockout reaction. The α-cluster wave function was extracted from the THSR wave function by approximating the reduced width amplitude (RWA) using the method of Kanada-En'yo (1998). Three states of $^{10}$Be were constructed by varying the β_{α,z} parameter: a compact shell-model-like state (β_{α,z}=1.0 fm), the physical molecular-like ground state (β_{α,z}=2.6 fm), and a dilute gas-like cluster state (β_{α,z}=6.0 fm).

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy of $^{10}$Be: -61.4 MeV from the optimized THSR wave function.
- Root-mean-square charge radius of $^{10}$Be: 2.31 fm, compared to the experimental value of 2.36 fm from Nortershauser (2009).
- Variational parameters for the physical ground state: β_{α,xy}=0.1 fm and β_{α,z}=2.6 fm.
- Kinematical conditions: incident proton energy 250 MeV, outgoing proton kinetic energy fixed at 180 MeV with emission angle (θ₁,φ₁)=(60.9°,0°), α-particle φ₂ fixed at 180° and θ₂ varied around 51° with kinetic energy around 62.5 MeV; p-α scattering angle θ_{pα} around 76° and scattering energy E_{pα} around 242 MeV.
- Giant ratio of about 10 for the TDX around the peak between the shell-model-like state and the molecular-like state, and similarly between the molecular-like state and the gas-like cluster state.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of Yoshida (2016, 2017) by replacing phenomenological α-cluster wave functions with microscopic THSR wave functions, enabling direct connection between clustering structure and reaction observables. It builds on the microscopic cluster model studies of $^{9,10}$Be by Itagaki (2000), Lyu (2015, 2016), and others, and the THSR wave function development by Tohsaki (2001) and Zhao (2017). The results enable future experimental measurements at RIBF (Yan 2017) to directly distinguish between shell-model, molecular-like, and gas-like cluster states in $^{10}$Be.
