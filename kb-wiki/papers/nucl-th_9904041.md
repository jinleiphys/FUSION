# Constructing Hybrid Baryons with Flux Tubes

> Hybrid baryon states are described in quark potential models as having explicit excitation of the gluon degrees of freedom. Such states are described in a model motivated by the strong coupling limit of Hamiltonian lattice gauge theory, where three flux tubes meeting at a junction play the role of the glue. The adiabatic approximation for the quark motion is used, and the flux tubes and junction are modeled by beads which are attracted to each other and the quarks by a linear potential, and vibrate in various string modes. Quantum numbers and estimates of the energies of the lightest hybrid baryons are provided.

## Key claim

The lightest hybrid baryons have masses substantially higher (1870 ± 100 MeV) than the roughly 1500 MeV estimated by bag models and QCD sum rules, and their internal structure and quantum numbers differ from those predicted by the constituent-gluon picture, because the gluon degrees of freedom collectively condense into flux tubes rather than behaving as a single colored gluon.

## Method

The authors model the glue in a baryon as three flux tubes meeting at a junction, motivated by the strong coupling limit of Hamiltonian lattice gauge theory. They use the adiabatic approximation for quark motion and represent the flux tubes and junction as beads attracted by a linear potential, then solve for the ground and first excited adiabatic surfaces by allowing the junction to move while the strings follow without excitation. Hybrid baryon masses are found by allowing quarks to move in a confining potential given by the linear potential plus the difference between the first excited and ground state string energies, with quark wavefunctions expanded in a large oscillator basis.

## Key numbers

- Lightest hybrid baryon mass (with quark orbital angular momentum L_q=0): 1980 MeV spin-averaged, reduced to 1870 MeV for N hybrids after hyperfine splitting, with model error estimated at less than ±100 MeV
- Hybrids with L_q=1 and L_q=2 have masses 2340 MeV and 2620 MeV respectively (spin-averaged)
- The spin-averaged mass of the N and Δ used as baseline: 1085 MeV
- String tension b = 0.18 GeV²
- Quark mass m_q = 0.33 GeV used in the junction effective mass calculation
- Lowest three string excitation frequencies for symmetric quark configuration (0.5, 0.5, 0.5 fm): 0.607, 0.607, and 0.828 GeV (exact diagonalization)
- The quark core of hybrid baryons is roughly 20% larger than for conventional baryons

## Context

This work extends the flux-tube model previously applied to mesons by Isgur and Paton (1985) and Barnes, Close, and Swanson (1995) to the baryon sector, and contradicts the lower mass estimates of about 1500 MeV from bag model calculations (Barnes and Close, 1983; Golowich, Haqq, and Karl, 1983) and QCD sum rules (Kisslinger and Li, 1998). The results enable experimental searches for hybrid baryons in the 2 GeV mass region at TJNAF Hall B and BNL, particularly in electro- and photoproduction of ρ and ω mesons, and in ψ decays to pω or pη' final states where gluonic hadron production is expected to be enhanced.
