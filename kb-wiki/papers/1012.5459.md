# Effective moment of inertia for several fission reaction systems induced by nucleons, light particles and heavy ions

> Compound nucleus effective moment of inertia has been calculated for several fission reaction systems induced by nucleons, light particles, and heavy ions. Determination of this quantity for these systems is based upon the comparison between the experimental data of the fission fragment angular distributions as well as the prediction of the standard saddle-point statistical model (SSPSM). For the systems, the two cases, namely with and without neutron emission corrections were considered. In these calculations, it is assumed that all the neutrons are emitted before reaching the saddle point.It should be noted that the above method for determining of the effective moment of inertia had not been reported until now and this method is used for the first time to determine compound nucleus effective moment of inertia. Hence, our calculations are of particular importance in obtaining this quantity, and have a significant rule in the field of fission physics. Afterwards, our theoretical results have been compared with the data obtained from the rotational liquid drop model as well as the Sierk model, and satisfactory agreements were found. Finally, we have considered the effective moment of inertia of compound nuclei for the systems that formed similar compound nuclei at similar excitation energies.

## Key claim

The paper presents a novel method for determining the compound nucleus effective moment of inertia by comparing experimental fission fragment angular distributions with predictions of the standard saddle-point statistical model (SSPSM), claiming this approach had not been reported previously. The method assumes all neutrons are emitted before reaching the saddle point, which is a necessary simplification since experimental separation of pre-saddle and post-saddle neutron emission is not straightforward.

## Method

The effective moment of inertia at the saddle point was extracted by fitting the SSPSM expression for fission fragment anisotropy, which relates anisotropy to the mean-square angular momentum, effective moment of inertia, and saddle-point temperature. For each reaction system, the effective moment of inertia was parameterized as a linear function of center-of-mass energy, with separate fits for cases of zero, one, and two emitted pre-fission neutrons, assuming each neutron removes 0.5 units of angular momentum and carries an average energy of 5 MeV for light projectiles or 9-10 MeV for heavy ions.

## Key numbers

- Level density parameter was taken as a = 0.094 * A_CN for all reaction systems, where A_CN is the mass number of the compound nucleus
- For the p+185Re system forming 186Os, the effective moment of inertia coefficients without neutron emission were a_0 = 0.362 and b_0 = 22.291, over a projectile energy range of 42-66.5 MeV
- For the 12C+232Th system forming 244Cm, the coefficients without neutron emission were a_0 = 1.155 and b_0 = 55.016, over a projectile energy range of 64-78 MeV
- For the 11B+235U system forming 246Bk, the coefficients without neutron emission were a_0 = 10.637 and b_0 = -538.514, over a projectile energy range of 60-70 MeV
- For the 16O+232Th system forming 248Cf, the coefficients without neutron emission were a_0 = 2.087 and b_0 = -54.563, over a projectile energy range of 93-136 MeV
- For the three reaction systems forming 186Os (p+185Re, tau+183W, alpha+182W), the average errors in effective moment of inertia were less than 5% for the first two reactions compared to the third, and approximately 29% for the tau+183W system compared to alpha+182W
- For systems forming 248Cf, the average errors for 12C+236U and 16O+232Th were about 6% and less than 3%, respectively, compared to the 11B+237Np system
- The contribution of non-compound nucleus fission for the 16O+232Th system was less than 10% over the energy range investigated
- Varying the level density parameter from a = A_CN/8 to A_CN/11 instead of a = 0.094*A_CN changed the effective moment of inertia by at most 6-7%

## Context

This work extends the standard saddle-point statistical model (SSPSM) approach of Vandenbosch (1973) by introducing a systematic method to extract effective moments of inertia from angular distribution data for 18 fission reaction systems induced by nucleons, light particles, and heavy ions. The calculated values were compared with the rotational liquid drop model (RLDM) and the rotating finite range model (RFRM) of Sierk, finding satisfactory agreement. The method enables future studies to determine effective moments of inertia for additional fission systems and to investigate entrance channel effects, particularly the role of non-compound nucleus fission in systems with mass asymmetry below the Businaro-Gallone point.
