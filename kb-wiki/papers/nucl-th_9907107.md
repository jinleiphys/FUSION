# Effective Widths and Effective Number of Phonons of Multiphonon Giant Resonances

> We discuss the origin of the difference between the harmonic value of the width of the multiphonon giant resonances and the smaller observed value. Analytical expressions are derived for both the effective width and the average cross-section. The contribution of the Brink-Axel mechanism in resolving the discrepancy is pointed out.

## Key claim

The observed width of multiphonon giant resonances is smaller than the harmonic value of n times the single-phonon width because the Brink-Axel mechanism introduces incoherent fluctuation contributions that produce an energy-dependent effective width; the effective width approaches the harmonic value only at high bombarding energies.

## Method

The authors use a multistep distorted wave series combined with statistical averaging over the fine-structure states (q₁) that couple to the doorway states (d₁). They derive closed-form analytical expressions for the inclusive n-phonon cross-section by separating the coherent "harmonic" contribution from the fluctuation contribution arising from the Brink-Axel mechanism, where the GDR decays into complicated background states before a subsequent collective excitation occurs. The effective width is then extracted by taking a Breit-Wigner form for each component of the averaged cross-section and evaluating at the peak.

## Key numbers

- The single-phonon doorway state width Γ(d₁) is set to 4.0 MeV for the ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb system.
- For two-phonon excitation, the ratio of the total cross-section to the coherent cross-section is given by σ⁽²⁾/σ_c⁽²⁾ = (1 + Γ_eff/(2Γ₁)) / (2 Γ_eff/(2Γ₁)), a relation independent of the system.
- The effective width Γ_eff⁽ⁿ⁾(E) = [1 / (1 + Σ (k/(n-k)) σ_fl⁽ⁿ⁾(k) / σ⁽ⁿ⁾)] × (n Γ_d₁), showing the reduction factor relative to the harmonic value nΓ_d₁.
- The fluctuation cross-section for two phonons is σ_fl⁽²⁾ = (1/2) (Γ_d₁↓ τ_c(E) / ħ) σ_c⁽²⁾, where Γ_d₁↓ is the spreading width and τ_c(E) is the correlation time.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous publications (Carlson et al., 1999; Carlson et al., 1998) that introduced the direct-plus-fluctuation model for multiphonon giant resonance excitation in heavy-ion collisions. It provides analytical formulas that complement the numerical solutions of the evolution equation for the density matrix reported in Carlson et al. (1999). The results connect to anharmonic oscillator models (Hussein et al., 1999; Bortignon and Dasso, 1997; Bertsch and Feldmeier, 1997) by defining an effective phonon number n_eff smaller than n, and the framework is also relevant to preequilibrium reaction processes.
