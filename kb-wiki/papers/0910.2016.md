# Large Q2 Electrodisintegration of the Deuteron in Virtual Nucleon Approximation

> The two-body break up of the deuteron is studied at high $Q^2$ kinematics, with main motivation to probe the deuteron at small internucleon distances. Such studies are associated with the probing of high momentum component of the deuteron wave function. For this, two main theoretical issues have been addressed such as electromagnetic interaction of the virtual photon with the bound nucleon and the strong interaction of produced baryons in the final state of the break-up reaction. Within virtual nucleon approximation we developed a new prescription to account for the bound nucleon effects in electromagnetic interaction. The final state interaction at high $Q^2$ kinematics is calculated within generalized eikonal approximation (GEA). We studied the uncertainties involved in the calculation and performed comparisons with the first experimental data on deuteron electrodisintegration at large $Q^2$. We demonstrate that the experimental data confirm GEA's early prediction that the rescattering is maximal at $\sim 70^0$ of recoil nucleon production relative to the momentum of the virtual photon. Comparisons also show that the forward recoil nucleon angles are best suited for studies of the electromagnetic interaction of bound nucleons and the high momentum structure of the deuteron. Backward recoil angle kinematics show sizable effects due to the $Δ$-isobar contribution. The latter indicates the importance of further development of GEA to account for the inelastic transitions in the intermediate state of the electrodisintegration reactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) prediction that final state rescattering is maximal at approximately 70 degrees of recoil nucleon production relative to the virtual photon momentum is confirmed by experimental data, but only under the specific condition that the recoil nucleon momentum is restricted to below 700 MeV/c to satisfy the virtual nucleon approximation's validity conditions.

## Method

The author developed a theoretical model for exclusive deuteron electrodisintegration at high momentum transfer using the virtual nucleon approximation, where the scattering amplitude is represented in covariant form using effective Feynman diagram rules. The final state interaction was calculated within the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA), which extends beyond the distorted wave impulse approximation by applying nonfactorized approximations to both the electromagnetic and NN rescattering parts. The total scattering amplitude was constructed as the sum of plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA), forward elastic final state interaction, and charge-exchange rescattering amplitudes, with the deuteron wave function modeled using the Paris potential and the nucleon electromagnetic current using phenomenological form-factor parameterizations.

## Key numbers

- The optimal kinematics for probing the deuteron at small internucleon distances requires Q² ≥ 1 GeV², with the knocked-out proton momentum approximately equal to the virtual photon momentum and the recoil nucleon momentum ≥ 300 MeV/c
- The virtual nucleon approximation restricts the recoil neutron momentum to p_r ≤ 700 MeV/c to satisfy the condition that negative energy projections of the virtual nucleon propagator give negligible contribution
- The kinetic energy of the recoil nucleon is restricted to T_N < 500 MeV to neglect inelastic initial state transitions involving baryonic resonances such as Δ(1232) and N*(1525)
- The experimental data confirm GEA's early prediction that rescattering is maximal at approximately 70 degrees of recoil nucleon production relative to the virtual photon momentum
- The off-shell part of the electromagnetic current in the PWIA amplitude introduces a correction term proportional to (E_i^{off} - E_i^{on})/(2m), where E_i^{off} = M_d - sqrt(m² + p_r²) and E_i^{on} = sqrt(m² + p_r²)

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical models from the mid-1990s (such as those by Frankfurt et al. and Sargsian et al.) that assumed factorization of electromagnetic and final state interactions, by developing a more elaborate covariant approach within the generalized eikonal approximation that accounts for off-shell effects and nonfactorized contributions. The calculations are compared with the first experimental data from Jefferson Lab experiments (Ulmer, Kim, Werner) at high Q², and the results enable future studies of the deuteron's high-momentum structure and short-range NN interactions using the upcoming 11 GeV upgrade at Jefferson Lab, while also indicating the need for further development of GEA to include Δ-isobar contributions in the intermediate state.
