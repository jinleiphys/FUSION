# Sharpening Low-Energy, Standard-Model Tests via Correlation Coefficients in Neutron Beta-Decay

> The correlation coefficients a, A, and B in neutron beta-decay are proportional to the ratio of the axial-vector to vector weak coupling constants, g_A/g_V, to leading recoil order. With the advent of the next generation of neutron decay experiments, the recoil-order corrections to these expressions become experimentally accessible, admitting a plurality of Standard Model (SM) tests. The measurement of both a and A, e.g., allows one to test the conserved-vector-current (CVC) hypothesis and to search for second-class currents (SCC) independently. The anticipated precision of these measurements suggests that the bounds on CVC violation and SCC from studies of nuclear beta-decay can be qualitatively bettered. Departures from SM expectations can be interpreted as evidence for non-V-A currents.

## Key claim

The paper argues that upcoming precision measurements of the neutron beta-decay correlation coefficients \(a\) and \(A\) can test the conserved-vector-current (CVC) hypothesis and search for second-class currents (SCC) independently, without the assumptions about nuclear matrix elements that limit existing nuclear beta-decay tests.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the recoil-order corrections to the correlation coefficients \(a\) and \(A\) in free neutron beta-decay, including the weak magnetism form factor \(f_2\) and the induced tensor form factor \(g_2\). They show that the energy-dependent (\(x^0\) and \(x^1\)) terms in \(a\) and \(A\) provide four independent empirical constraints on three unknowns (\(\lambda\), \(\tilde f_2\), and \(\tilde g_2\)), making the system overconstrained and allowing independent determination of each coupling constant.

## Key numbers

* The recoil-order corrections to the correlation coefficients are of order \(R \sim 1.4 \times 10^{-3}\), where \(R = E_e^{\rm max}/M\) is the ratio of the endpoint energy to the neutron mass.
* Using \(\lambda = 1.2670\) and \(\tilde f_2 = 1.8529\) (from CVC), the recoil corrections to \(a\) are roughly a factor of two larger than those to \(A\).
* A 0.1% measurement of \(a\) yields a 2.5% measurement of \(\tilde f_2\) from the \(x^1\) term.
* A 0.1% measurement of \(a\) combined with its \(x^0\) term yields an uncertainty in \(\tilde g_2\) of order \(0.22\lambda/2\).
* A 0.1% measurement of \(A\) combined with an earlier determination of \(\tilde f_2\) yields an uncertainty in \(\tilde g_2\) of \(0.26\lambda/2\).
* The current experimental values are \(a = -0.102 \pm 0.005\), \(A = -0.1162 \pm 0.0013\), and \(B = 0.983 \pm 0.004\) (Particle Data Group 2000).
* The mass 12 system yields \(2M f_T/f_A = 0.04 \pm 0.16 ({\rm stat}) \pm 0.04 ({\rm syst})\) (Minamisono 2000), and with \(\Delta y = 0.10 \pm 0.05\) gives the combined constraint \(0.01 \le 2M f_T/f_A \le 0.34\) at 90% CL.

## Context

This work extends the program of testing the CVC hypothesis and searching for second-class currents, previously pursued in nuclear beta-decay studies of the mass 12 system (Commins and Bucksbaum 1983; Grenacs 1985; Minamisono 1998, 2000) and mass 8 system (Snover 2000; Tribble and Garvey 1975). The authors show that neutron decay avoids the theoretical uncertainties from differences in axial matrix elements between mirror nuclear transitions, which complicate the nuclear analyses. The results enable crisp, independent tests of CVC and SCC using upcoming experiments that can measure \(a\) as a function of electron energy (Wietfeldt 2000) and \(A\) to 0.1% or better (Filippone, private communication).
