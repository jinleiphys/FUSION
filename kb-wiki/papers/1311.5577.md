# Effects of Fluid Velocity Gradients on Heavy Quark Energy Loss

> We use holographic duality to analyze the drag force on, and consequent energy loss of, a heavy quark moving through a strongly coupled conformal fluid with non-vanishing gradients in its velocity and temperature. We derive the general expression for the drag force to first order in the fluid gradients. Using this general expression, we show that a quark that is instantaneously at rest, relative to the fluid, in a fluid whose velocity is changing with time feels a nonzero force. And, we show that for a quark that is moving ultra-relativistically, the first order gradient "corrections" become larger than the zeroth order drag force, suggesting that the gradient expansion may be unreliable in this regime. We illustrate the importance of the fluid gradients for heavy quark energy loss by considering a fluid with one-dimensional boost invariant Bjorken expansion as well as the strongly coupled plasma created by colliding sheets of energy.

# Effects of Fluid Velocity Gradients on Heavy Quark Energy Loss

## Key claim

The authors use holographic duality to derive the general expression for the drag force on a heavy quark moving through a strongly coupled conformal fluid to first order in fluid velocity and temperature gradients, showing that the gradient expansion may become unreliable for ultrarelativistic quarks because the first-order gradient "corrections" become larger than the zeroth-order drag force by a factor of order gamma^(1/2) in the large Lorentz factor limit.

## Method

The authors construct the gravitational dual metric for a strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills fluid with first-order gradients in temperature and velocity, following the work of Bhattacharyya et al. (2008). They then introduce a heavy quark as a string endpoint at the AdS boundary, solve the Nambu-Goto equations of motion for the string profile to first order in fluid gradients, and compute the flux of momentum down the string to determine the drag force. The calculation is performed in the instantaneous fluid rest frame and then boosted to general frames, with the integration constants fixed by requiring regularity of the string profile at the worldsheet horizon at radial position sigma = sqrt(gamma)/b.

## Key numbers

- The worldsheet horizon for the string in the static fluid calculation is located at radial position sigma = sqrt(gamma)/b, where gamma = (1 - beta^2)^(-1/2) is the Lorentz factor of the heavy quark and b = 1/(pi T) is the inverse temperature.
- For a quark moving ultrarelativistically, the first-order gradient correction to the drag force exceeds the zeroth-order term by a factor of order gamma^(1/2), indicating breakdown of the gradient expansion in this regime.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio for the fluid is eta/s = 1/(4pi), following from the holographic dual description.
- The hydrodynamization time for the colliding sheets of energy density system is at most around (0.7-1)/T_hydro, where T_hydro is the effective temperature at the hydrodynamization time.

## Context

This work extends the holographic calculations of heavy quark drag force in static equilibrium plasmas performed by Herzog et al. (2006), Gubser et al. (2006), and Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2006) to the case of fluids with non-vanishing velocity and temperature gradients. It builds upon the gradient expansion framework for the gravitational dual of hydrodynamic fluids developed by Bhattacharyya et al. (2008). The results explain the deviations between actual drag force and equilibrium expectations observed in the far-from-equilibrium collision simulations of Chesler et al. (2013), and disagree with the earlier gradient-based approach of Abbasi et al. (2012, 2013) regarding the dependence of the worldsheet horizon position on fluid gradients. The findings enable quantitative modeling of heavy quark energy loss in expanding quark-gluon plasmas with Bjorken flow and motivate investigation of higher-order gradient effects for ultrarelativistic quarks.
