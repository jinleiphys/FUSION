# Measurement of neutron capture on 50Ti at thermonuclear energies

> At the Karlsruhe and Tuebingen 3.75 MV Van de Graaff accelerators the thermonuclear 50Ti(n,gamma)51Ti(5.8 min) cross section was measured by the fast cyclic activation technique via the 320.852 and 928.65 keV gamma-ray lines of the 51Ti-decay. Metallic Ti samples of natural isotopic composition and samples of TiO2 enriched in 50Ti by 67.53 % were irradiated between two gold foils which served as capture standards. The capture cross-section was measured at the neutron energies 25, 30, 52, and 145 keV, respectively. The direct capture cross section was determined to be 0.387 +/- 0.011 mbarn at 30 keV. We found evidence for a bound state s-wave resonance with an estimated radiative width of 0.34 eV which destructively interfers with direct capture. The strength of a suggested s-wave resonance at 146.8 keV was determined. The present data served to calculate, in addition to the directly measured Maxwellian averaged capture cross sections at 25 and 52 keV, an improved stellar 50Ti(n,gamma)51Ti rate in the thermonuclear energy region from 1 to 250 keV. The new stellar rate leads at low temperatures to much higher values than the previously recommended rate, e.g., at kT=8 keV the increase amounts to about 50 %. The new reaction rate therefore reduces the abundance of 50Ti due to s-processing in AGB stars.

## Key claim

The direct capture component of the 50Ti(n,γ)51Ti cross section was measured at 30 keV as 0.387 ± 0.011 mbarn, but this value is significantly larger than a 1/v extrapolation to thermal energy would predict (0.421 ± 0.012 barn versus the measured 0.179 ± 0.003 barn), implying destructive interference from a bound s-wave resonance at -16.8 keV with an estimated radiative width of 0.34 eV.

## Method

The thermonuclear 50Ti(n,γ)51Ti cross section was measured at the Karlsruhe and Tübingen 3.75 MV Van de Graaff accelerators using the fast cyclic activation technique, detecting the 320.852 and 928.65 keV gamma-ray lines from 51Ti decay (5.8 min half-life). Metallic Ti samples of natural isotopic composition and TiO2 samples enriched to 67.53% in 50Ti were irradiated between two gold foils serving as capture standards, with neutron energies of 25, 30, 52, and 145 keV produced via the 7Li(p,n) and T(p,n) reactions. The capture cross sections were determined relative to the 197Au(n,γ)198Au standard reaction, and the direct capture component was isolated at 30 keV where resonance contributions were negligible.

## Key numbers

- Direct capture cross section at 30 keV: 0.387 ± 0.011 mbarn
- Average Maxwellian averaged capture cross section at kT = 25 keV: 3.612 ± 0.095 mbarn
- Average Maxwellian averaged capture cross section at kT = 52 keV: 2.736 ± 0.113 mbarn
- Average capture cross section at 145 ± 16 keV: 0.650 ± 0.029 mbarn
- Estimated radiative width of bound s-wave resonance at -16.8 keV: 0.34 eV
- Resonance strength (gΓγΓn/Γ) for the 146.8 keV s-wave resonance: 0.37 ± 0.24 eV
- At kT = 8 keV, the new stellar rate is about 50% higher than the previously recommended rate

## Context

This work extends and corrects earlier time-of-flight measurements by Allen et al. (1977, 1979), which had required large corrections for prompt capture gamma-ray background and had revised the radiation widths of s-wave resonances. The new direct capture measurement and improved resonance parameters resolve discrepancies between the previously recommended Maxwellian averaged cross section (4.0 ± 0.5 mbarn at kT = 30 keV from Bao and Kappeler 1987 and Beer et al. 1992) and the present data. The increased reaction rate at low temperatures (especially at kT = 8 keV, where the 13C(α,n) neutron source operates in low-mass AGB stars) reduces the calculated abundance of 50Ti in AGB models, bringing predictions closer to the measured titanium isotopic anomalies in presolar SiC grains reported by Hoppe et al. (1994) and Gallino et al. (1994).
