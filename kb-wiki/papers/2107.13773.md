# New results on proton-induced reactions on Vanadium for $^{47}$Sc production and the impact of level densities on theoretical cross sections

> New data for the $^\mbox{nat}$V(p,x) reactions have been measured in the range 26-70 MeV, with production of the nuclides $^{47}$Sc, $^{43}$Sc, $^{44m}$Sc, $^{44g}$Sc, $^{46}$Sc, $^{48}$Sc, $^{42}$K, $^{43}$K, $^{48}$V, $^{48}$Cr, $^{49}$Cr, and $^{51}$Cr. The focus is on the production of $^{47}$Sc, a $β^-$-emitter suitable for innovative radiotheranostic applications in nuclear medicine. The measured cross sections for this radionuclide and its contaminants are compared with the theoretical excitation functions calculated with the TALYS code. In view of novel radiopharmaceutical applications, it is essential to accurately describe these cross-sections for the evaluation of yields, purities, and dose releases. Hence, we optimize the level-density parameters of the microscopic models in the TALYS code to obtain the best possible descriptions of the new data. We consider different irradiation conditions to estimate the production yields from the cross sections determined in this work.

## Key claim

The authors claim that by optimizing the level-density parameters (c and p) of the microscopic models in the TALYS code, they achieve an excellent reproduction of the measured cross sections for the natV(p,x) reactions in the 26-70 MeV range, without relying on parametric-fit extrapolations of data at low energies, and that this optimized theoretical model provides a more accurate prediction of yields and contamination by stable isotopes than simple fit extrapolation.

## Method

The authors measured cross sections for twelve radionuclides produced by proton-induced reactions on natural vanadium targets using the stacked-foils technique at the ARRONAX facility, with proton beam energies from 26 to 70 MeV. They compared the experimental data with theoretical excitation functions calculated using the TALYS code, employing the JLM semi-microscopic optical potential and the exciton model for preequilibrium emission, while optimizing the nuclear level density parameters (c and p) for the microscopic ldmodel 4 (Skyrme force from Goriely's tables) through a grid search followed by a Minuit-based chi-square minimization procedure.

## Key numbers

* The cross section for natV(p,x)47Sc at 70.0 ± 0.6 MeV is 8.0 ± 0.4 mb.
* The cross section for natV(p,x)47Sc at 67.8 ± 0.7 MeV is 7.7 ± 0.5 mb.
* The cross section for natV(p,x)47Sc at 65.5 ± 0.8 MeV is 6.8 ± 0.4 mb.
* The cross section for natV(p,x)46Sc at 70.0 ± 0.6 MeV is 16.4 ± 0.9 mb.
* The cross section for natV(p,x)48V at 70.0 ± 0.6 MeV is 72.4 ± 6.7 mb.
* The maximum beam energy uncertainty, including energy straggling, was 0.8 MeV.
* The 47Sc half-life is 3.3492 days, with a beta-minus mean energy of 162.0 keV and gamma emission at 159.381 keV with 68.3% intensity.

## Context

This work extends the experimental data for natV(p,x) reactions into the 26-70 MeV range, complementing earlier measurements by Michel et al. (1979, 1985) covering 18.5-45 MeV and 50-200 MeV, and by Ditroi et al. (2016) covering 36-64 MeV. The optimized theoretical description enables accurate estimation of 47Sc production yields and contaminant levels for radiotheranostic applications, particularly addressing the need to predict the co-production of stable isotopes like 45Sc that cannot be measured by gamma spectroscopy, and builds upon the PASTA project framework at INFN-Legnaro National Laboratories.
