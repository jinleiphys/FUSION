# Two-pole structures in a relativistic Friedrichs-Lee-QPC scheme

> A general appearance of two-pole structures is exhibited in a relativistic Friedrichs-Lee model combined with a relativistic quark pair creation model in a consistent manner. This kind of two-pole structure could be found when a $q\bar q$ state couples to the open-flavor continuum state in the $S$ partial wave. We found that many enigmatic states, such as $f_0(500)/σ$, $K_0^*(700)/κ$, $a_0(980)$, $f_0(980)$, $D_0^*(2300)$, $D_{s0}^*(2317)$, and $X(3872)$, together with another higher state for each, all result from this kind of two-pole structures. Furthermore, an interesting observation is that this kind of two-pole structure will contribute roughly a total of 180$^\circ$ phase shift for the scattering process in a single channel approximation. This relativistic scheme may provide more insights into the understanding of the properties of non-$q\bar q$ state. It is also suggested that such two-pole structure could be a common phenomenon which deserves studying both from theoretical and experimental perspectives.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a single bare quark-antiquark (q\bar q) seed state coupling to an open-flavor continuum state in the S partial wave generically produces two S-matrix poles, one from the shifted seed state and one dynamically generated, which together account for many enigmatic hadron states across light and heavy quark sectors. The authors hedge that this is demonstrated in a simplified single-channel approximation with only one continuum per bare state, and that the universal quark pair creation strength parameter gamma may need adjustment for heavy quark systems to match experimental values precisely.

## Method

The authors combine a relativistic Friedrichs-Lee model with a relativistic quark pair creation (RQPC) model to study the coupling between a discrete q\bar q seed state and a two-meson continuum state. The resolvent function eta(s) is constructed from the self-energy Pi(s), and its zero points on the complex s-plane are found to locate the S-matrix poles. The coupling form factor alpha(s) is computed using the RQPC model with wave functions from the Godfrey-Isgur potential model, and nine cases are studied ranging from light scalar mesons to charmonium-like states.

## Key numbers

- For the scalar (u\bar u+d\bar d)/sqrt(2) state with gamma=4.3 GeV: poles at sqrt(s_r1)=1.34-0.29i GeV (f_0(1370)) and sqrt(s_r2)=0.39-0.26i GeV (f_0(500))
- For the scalar u\bar s state: poles at sqrt(s_r1)=1.41-0.17i GeV (K_0^*(1430)) and sqrt(s_r2)=0.66-0.34i GeV (K_0^*(700))
- For the scalar s\bar s state coupling to K\bar K: poles at sqrt(s_r1)=1.71-0.16i GeV (f_0(1710)), a bound state at sqrt(s_b)=0.98 GeV (f_0(980)), and a virtual state at sqrt(s_v)=0.19 GeV
- For the scalar c\bar s state with gamma=4.3 GeV: poles at sqrt(s_r1)=2.80-0.23i GeV and a bound state at sqrt(s_b)=2.24 GeV (D_{s0}^*(2317))
- For the c\bar c(2^3P_1) state with gamma=4.3 GeV: pole at sqrt(s_r1)=4.01-0.049i GeV (X(3940)) and a bound state at sqrt(s_b)=3.785 GeV (X(3872))
- With gamma=3.0 GeV for heavy mesons: c\bar s dynamical bound state at sqrt(s_b)=2.32 GeV; c\bar c(2^3P_1) dynamical bound state at sqrt(s_b)=3.84 GeV
- The single-channel phase shift sum rule: delta(infinity)-delta(s_th)=180 degrees

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic Friedrichs model studies of two-pole structures (Zhou 2017) to a fully relativistic framework, allowing consistent treatment of both light and heavy meson systems. The results contradict the simple quark potential model assignment of the light scalar nonet, supporting instead the interpretation that states like f_0(500), K_0^*(700), a_0(980), and f_0(980) are dynamically generated non-q\bar q states, consistent with earlier work by Tornqvist (1995) and Jaffe (1976). The scheme enables future multi-channel studies that could refine the pole positions and connect to lattice QCD simulations and unitarized heavy quark chiral perturbation theory calculations (Guo 2015, Albaladejo 2016).
