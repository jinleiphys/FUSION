# Triaxial projected shell model study of $γ$-bands in atomic nuclei

> A systematic study of $γ$-bands observed in atomic nuclei is performed using the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach. The staggering phase between the even and odd spin members of the $γ$-band for most the nuclei investigated in the present work is found to have even-I-below-odd-I, which in the framework of the collective model is considered as a signature of $γ$-softness. It is observed that out of twenty-three systems studied, only four nuclei, namely, $^{76}$Ge, $^{112}$Ru, $^{170}$Er and $^{232}$Th depict staggering phase with odd-I-below-even-I, which is regarded as an indication of the static $γ$-deformation in the collective model picture. The inclusion of the quasiparticle excitations in the framework of configuration mixing is shown to reverse the staggering phase from odd-I-down to the even-I-down for all the studied nuclei, except for the aforementioned four nuclei. Furthermore, by fitting a collective Bohr Hamiltonian to the TPSM energies, the differences between the two models are delineated through a comparison of the transition probabilities.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the even-I-below-odd-I staggering phase of gamma-bands, traditionally interpreted as a signature of gamma-softness in collective models, can instead arise from quasiparticle excitations within the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) which assumes a fixed triaxial deformation. The reversal of the staggering phase from odd-I-down to even-I-down occurs for all 23 studied nuclei except four (76Ge, 112Ru, 170Er, and 232Th), demonstrating that the staggering phase alone does not uniquely distinguish between gamma-rigid and gamma-soft shapes.

## Method

The authors performed triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) calculations for 23 even-even nuclei across different mass regions, using a fixed triaxial Nilsson mean-field with pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole interactions. They compared results from angular-momentum projection of only the 0-quasiparticle vacuum state (representing a rigid triaxial rotor) with full configuration mixing that includes 2-proton, 2-neutron, and 4-quasiparticle excitations. Additionally, they fitted a collective Bohr Hamiltonian to the TPSM transition energies and compared the resulting staggering parameters and B(E2) transition probabilities for 104,106,108Mo and 108,110,112Ru.

## Key numbers

- Out of 23 systems studied, only 4 nuclei (76Ge, 112Ru, 170Er, 232Th) show odd-I-below-even-I staggering phase, indicating static gamma-deformation in the collective model picture
- Triaxial deformation parameters epsilon' range from 0.080 (114Ru) to 0.160 (76Ge), corresponding to gamma values from 17.7 degrees (114Ru) to 38.6 degrees (76Ge)
- For 108Ru and 112Ru, the quasiparticle admixture magnitudes are very similar, yet 108Ru shows even-I-down staggering while 112Ru shows odd-I-down staggering
- For 112Ru, the triaxial deformation parameter epsilon' = 0.13 (gamma = 24 degrees) is needed to reproduce the observed staggering phase
- The ratio E(2+_2)/E(2+_1) ranges from 1.9 (76Ge experimental) to 21.2 (238U experimental), with TPSM values from 2.1 to 20.7

## Context

This work extends previous TPSM studies (Jehangir et al., 2011; Bhat et al., 2014, 2015) to systematically investigate gamma-band staggering across the nuclear chart. It contradicts the traditional interpretation from collective models (Davydov-Filippov model for rigid triaxiality and Wilets-Jean model for gamma-softness) that the staggering phase directly indicates gamma-rigidity versus gamma-softness. The study shows that quasiparticle excitations can reverse the staggering phase even with fixed triaxial deformation, as previously discussed in the review by Frauendorf (2015). The results enable future investigations into the role of specific quasineutron states at the Fermi level in determining the staggering phase, and suggest that 76Ge, 112Ru, 170Er, and 232Th are candidates for genuinely gamma-rigid nuclei.
