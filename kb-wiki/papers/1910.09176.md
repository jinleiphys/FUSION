# A new probe to study symmetry energy at low density by deuteron breakup reaction

> The reactions of nucleon and polarized deuteron scattered off a heavy target at large impact parameter with intermediate energies have been investigated by using the improved quantum molecular dynamics model. It is found that, due to the difference effect of isovector potential on proton and neutron, there is a significant difference between the angle distribution of elastic scattering protons and neutrons. To overcome the lack of monochromatic neutron beam, the reaction of polarized deuteron peripherally scattered off the heavy target is used to replace the reaction of individual proton and neutron scattered off heavy target to study the isospin effect. It is found that the distributions of elastic scattering angle of proton and neutron originating from the breakup of deuteron are very similar to the results of the individual proton- and neutron-induced reaction. A new probe more effective and more clean, namely the difference between elastic scattering angle of proton and neutron originating from the breakup of polarized deuteron, is promoted to constrain the symmetry energy at subsaturation density.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the difference between the elastic scattering angle of the proton and neutron originating from the breakup of a polarized deuteron in peripheral collisions with a heavy target can serve as a new, highly sensitive probe to constrain the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities (below about 0.3 times saturation density), provided the impact parameter is large enough to avoid the dominance of the isoscalar potential.

## Method

The authors used the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model to simulate peripheral collisions of nucleons and polarized deuterons with a heavy target (124Sn) at an incident energy of 100 MeV per nucleon. They initialized the deuteron semiclassically with a pre-oriented long symmetric axis parallel to the beam axis and tracked the elastic scattering angles of the resulting proton and neutron. The angular distributions were fitted with Gaussian functions to extract the peak location and width, and the sensitivity of these observables to the stiffness of the symmetry energy (parameterized by γ in the symmetry energy formula) was evaluated for impact parameters from 6 to 8.5 fm.

## Key numbers

- The sensitivity of the peak location difference Δθ_c (proton minus neutron) to the stiffness parameter γ (from 0.5 to 2.0) in nucleon-induced reactions is about 200%.
- For the deuteron-induced reaction at impact parameters b = 6.5-8.5 fm, the sensitivities of the peak location δθ_c and the width W of the δθ distribution to γ are about 400% and 200%, respectively.
- The initial distance between neutron and proton in the deuteron is set to 3 ± Δr fm, where Δr is a random value in the range of 0-0.25 fm.
- The root mean square radius of the deuteron is kept at 2.1 ± 0.5 fm during initialization.
- The probed density window is asserted to have an upper limit below 0.3ρ₀, where ρ₀ is the saturation density (0.160 fm⁻³ for the MSL0 parameter set used).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Ou 2008, Ou 2015) which first identified the isospin effect in nucleon scattering and proposed the correlation angle from deuteron breakup as a symmetry energy probe. It directly addresses the difficulty of performing monochromatic neutron beam experiments by using a polarized deuteron beam as a surrogate for simultaneous proton and neutron beams. The proposed probe is intended to provide cleaner constraints on the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities than heavy-ion collision observables (such as the π⁻/π⁺ ratio), which are complicated by transport model uncertainties and sensitivity to multiple density regimes.
