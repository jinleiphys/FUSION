# Dielectric Functions and Dispersion Relations of Ultra-Relativistic Plasmas with Collisions

> In the present paper we calculate the dielectric functions of an ultra-relativistic plasma, such as an electron-positron or a quark-gluon plasma. We use classical transport theory and take into account collisions within the relaxation time approximation. From these dielectric functions we derive the dispersion relations of longitudinal and transverse plasma waves.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic expressions for the longitudinal and transverse dielectric functions of an ultra-relativistic plasma including collisions via a BGK collision term, but the collision rate \(\nu\) is treated as a free phenomenological parameter rather than computed from first principles; the resulting dispersion relations show only small deviations from the collisionless case, with the real part being remarkably insensitive to \(\nu\).

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Vlasov equation and add a BGK collision term with a constant, velocity-independent collision frequency \(\nu\). They linearize around the equilibrium Fermi-Dirac distribution for an ultra-relativistic electron-positron plasma (\(m \ll T\)), solve for the induced current, and extract the longitudinal and transverse dielectric functions analytically by exploiting the decoupling of radial and angular integrals at \(v=1\). The dispersion relations for longitudinal and transverse plasma waves are then obtained numerically from the conditions \(\epsilon^l(\omega,k)=0\) and \(\omega^2\epsilon^t(\omega,k)-k^2=0\).

## Key numbers

* The effective photon mass is defined as \(m_\gamma = eT/3\), which sets the scale for all dimensionful quantities.
* The Debye screening mass squared is \(m_D^2 = 3m_\gamma^2\), independent of the collision rate \(\nu\).
* The plasma frequency (at \(k=0\)) is reduced from \(m_\gamma\) at \(\nu=0\) to about \(0.9\,m_\gamma\) at \(\nu = m_\gamma\).
* The collision frequency \(\nu\) is varied as a parameter from 0 to 1 in units of \(m_\gamma\).
* The integral \(I_1 = \int_0^\infty dy\, y/(e^y+1) = \pi^2/12\) appears in the analytic dielectric functions.

## Context

This work extends the collisionless ultra-relativistic plasma results obtained from the Vlasov equation by Silin and from thermal field theory by Klimov and Weldon, and also parallels the non-relativistic degenerate plasma treatment of Alexandrov. The analytic dielectric functions enable numerical computation of dispersion relations with collisions, which are relevant for understanding damping mechanisms (beyond Landau damping) in hot electron-positron plasmas in astrophysical settings and, with appropriate replacement of the effective mass, provide a rough estimate for chromo-electromagnetic waves in a quark-gluon plasma. The results open the possibility of comparing phenomenological collision models with perturbative QCD calculations of damping rates by Thoma and others.
