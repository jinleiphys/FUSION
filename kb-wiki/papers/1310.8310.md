# Separation Between d$_{5/2}$ and s$_{1/2}$ Neutron Single Particle Strength in $^{15}$N

> The separation between single particle levels in nuclei plays the dominant role in determining the location of the neutron drip line. The separation also provides a test of current crossed shell model interactions if the experimental data is such that multiple shells are involved. The present work uses the $^{14}$N(d, p)$^{15}$N reaction to extract the 2s$_{1/2}$, and 1d$_{5/2}$ total neutron single particle strengths and then compares these results with a shell model calculation using a p-sd crossed shell interaction to identify the J$^π$ of all levels in $^{15}$N up to 12.8 MeV in excitation.

## Key claim

The 2s₁/₂ and 1d₅/₂ neutron single-particle orbits in ¹⁵N are roughly degenerate, with a centroid separation of only 0.164 MeV at 16 MeV bombarding energy, contradicting the expectation from neighboring nuclei where the splitting is much larger (760 keV in ¹³C and 870 keV in ¹⁷O).

## Method

The ¹⁴N(d,p)¹⁵N reaction was measured at a deuteron bombarding energy of 16 MeV using the FSU tandem-linac accelerator, with a silicon ΔE-E telescope covering laboratory angles from 10° to 35° in 5° steps. Finite-range Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) calculations using the code Fresco were compared to the measured angular distributions to extract spectroscopic factors for states up to 12.8 MeV excitation. Shell model calculations using an unrestricted p-sd valence space with the Utsuno-Chiba interaction (Utsuno 2001) were performed with the code COSMO for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Centroid of 2s₁/₂ strength: 7.904 MeV (16 MeV data), 8.138 MeV (9 MeV data from Phillips and Jacobs 1969), 7.835 MeV (theory)
- Centroid of 1d₅/₂ strength: 8.068 MeV (16 MeV data), 7.918 MeV (9 MeV data), 8.016 MeV (theory)
- Energy splitting Δ(d₅/₂ − s₁/₂): 0.164 MeV (16 MeV data), −0.220 MeV (9 MeV data), 0.181 MeV (theory)
- Summed s₁/₂ strength for J^π = 1/2⁺: 0.910 (16 MeV), 1.230 (9 MeV), 0.760 (theory)
- Summed d₅/₂ strength for J^π = 7/2⁺: 1.050 (16 MeV), 0.870 (9 MeV), 0.731 (theory)
- Spectroscopic factor for the 7.155 MeV 5/2⁺ state: 0.98 ± 0.020 (16 MeV), 0.88 ± 0.03 (9 MeV), 0.65 (theory)
- Spectroscopic factor for the 7.300 MeV 3/2⁺ state (L=0): 1.10 ± 0.024 (16 MeV), 0.89 ± 0.04 (9 MeV), 0.72 (theory)
- Absolute cross section uncertainty: ±20%

## Context

This work extends the earlier ¹⁴N(d,p) studies at 7, 8, and 9 MeV by Phillips and Jacobs (1969) and the shell model calculations of Lie and Engeland (1976) to higher excitation energies using a 16 MeV deuteron beam. The results confirm that the (d,p) reaction is direct even at lower energies, contrary to concerns about compound nucleus contributions. The near-degeneracy of the s₁/₂ and d₅/₂ orbits in ¹⁵N, combined with the known splittings in ¹³C (760 keV) and ¹⁷O (870 keV), shows that the energy splitting changes rapidly across this mass region, and modern p-sd shell model calculations reliably reproduce this trend.
