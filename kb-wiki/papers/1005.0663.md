# Color Transparency at COMPASS energies

> Pionic quasielastic knockout of protons from nuclei at 200 GeV show very large effects of color transparency as -t increases from 0 to several GeV^2. Similar effects are expected for quasielastic photoproduction of vector mesons.

## Key claim

Pionic quasielastic knockout of protons from nuclei at 200 GeV shows very large effects of color transparency as -t increases from 0 to several GeV², but this prediction depends on the assumption that the produced point-like configuration (PLC) does not expand while traversing the nucleus, which is valid only at the high energies available at COMPASS.

## Method

The authors compute the nuclear transparency T_A for the reaction π + A → π + p + (A-1)* on ²⁰⁸Pb using a quantum diffusion model for the effective cross section of the expanding PLC. The transparency is evaluated as the ratio of the nuclear cross section to A times the free nucleon cross section, integrating over the nuclear density with a Woods-Saxon distribution and including survival probabilities for both the incoming pion and outgoing proton. The pion PLC cross section is taken from perturbative QCD as σ_PLC ∝ n²⟨k_t²⟩/Q²_eff, with n=2 for the pion and ⟨k_t²⟩^{1/2} ≈ 0.35 GeV, while the proton's effective cross section includes expansion effects via the quantum diffusion length l_h = 2p/ΔM² with ΔM² = 0.7 GeV².

## Key numbers

- The pion beam momentum at COMPASS is 200 GeV/c, giving p_c² ≈ 100 GeV², so -t ranges from 0 to 10 GeV² as the center-of-mass scattering angle θ_c changes from 0 to about 0.35 radians.
- The proton emission angle θ_em remains large in the whole -t range discussed, approximately given by θ_em ≈ tan⁻¹(p_t/2α m_N), with α typically within |α - 1| ≤ 0.2 and the struck nucleon's initial transverse momentum k_t ≤ 0.2 GeV/c.
- The nuclear density parameters used are R = 1.1 A^{1/3} fm and a = 0.54 fm for ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The formation length for pions is l_h = 2p/ΔM² with ΔM² = 0.7 GeV², which at 200 GeV/c is much larger than the diameter of any stable nuclear target.
- For the γ + p → ρ⁰ + p reaction, data are consistent with the cross section being energy independent starting at -t ≥ 0.7 to 0.8 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the color transparency program from lower-energy experiments at Jefferson Lab and BNL (EVA experiment, Aclander 2004) to the much higher energies available at COMPASS, where expansion effects that complicated earlier measurements are suppressed. The authors build on the quantum diffusion model of Farrar (1988) and the dipole cross section formalism of Frankfurt (1993, 2000), and note that previous evidence for color transparency has been observed in diffractive di-jet production by pions (Dannyref), J/ψ photoproduction (E691), and vector meson production at HERA (Ha). The proposed measurement would allow direct study of the pionic PLC cross section as a function of -t and could also be applied to quasielastic photoproduction of vector mesons in muon-nucleus interactions at COMPASS.
