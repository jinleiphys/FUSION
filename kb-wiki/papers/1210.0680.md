# $β$-decay half-lives of neutron-rich nuclei and matter flow in the $r$-process

> The $β$-decay half-lives of neutron-rich nuclei with $20 \leqslant Z \leqslant 50$ are systematically investigated using the newly developed fully self-consistent proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA), based on the spherical relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) framework. Available data are reproduced by including an isospin-dependent proton-neutron pairing interaction in the isoscalar channel of the RHFB+QRPA model. With the calculated $β$-decay half-lives of neutron-rich nuclei a remarkable speeding up of $r$-matter flow is predicted. This leads to enhanced $r$-process abundances of elements with $A \gtrsim 140$, an important result for the understanding of the origin of heavy elements in the universe.

## Key claim
The paper claims that including an isospin-dependent proton-neutron pairing interaction in the RHFB+QRPA model reproduces experimental β-decay half-lives for neutron-rich nuclei with Z=20-50, and that the resulting shorter half-lives for r-process bottleneck nuclei near N=82 speed up r-matter flow to about 1.00 s, compared to 4.83 s predicted by FRDM+QRPA. The key qualifier is that this speeding up mainly applies to spherical or nearly spherical nuclei near N=82, and the spherical approximation does not affect this conclusion.

## Method
The authors implemented a fully self-consistent proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) based on the spherical relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) framework, using the PKO1 effective interaction. They introduced an isospin-dependent strength for the isoscalar (T=0) proton-neutron pairing interaction, with parameters fitted to reproduce known half-lives of even-even nuclei with 20 ≤ Z ≤ 50 and 8 ≤ N-Z ≤ 36. The calculated half-lives were then used in a classical r-process model with the waiting-point approximation, employing nuclear masses from the mass evaluation (Audi 2003) when available and the RMF mass model (Geng 2005) otherwise.

## Key numbers
- The isospin-dependent T=0 pairing strength parameters: V_L = 134.0 MeV, V_D = 121.1 MeV, a = 8.5, b = -0.4
- For 84Ge, the calculated half-life is 1.3 s, compared to the experimental value of 0.954 ± 0.014 s
- RHFB+QRPA half-life of 130Cd: 0.1685 s (experimental: 0.162 ± 0.007 s); FRDM+QRPA: 1.1232 s
- RHFB+QRPA half-life of 134Sn: 0.7530 s (experimental: 1.050 ± 0.011 s); FRDM+QRPA: 3.5410 s
- Summed half-lives of r-process bottleneck nuclei near N=82: 1.00 s (RHFB+QRPA) versus 4.83 s (FRDM+QRPA)
- RHFB+QRPA half-life of 124Mo: 0.0108 s; 126Ru: 0.0205 s; 128Pd: 0.0486 s

## Context
This work extends previous self-consistent QRPA calculations based on the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model (Niksic 2005, Marketin 2007) by incorporating the fully self-consistent RHFB framework including exchange terms, and by introducing an isospin-dependent T=0 pairing strength rather than a constant value. The results contradict the FRDM+QRPA calculations (Moller 1997) which systematically overestimate half-lives for Cd isotopes and other nuclei near N=82, and also improve upon RHB+QRPA and SHFB+QRPA (Engel 1999) calculations that used constant pairing strengths. The faster r-matter flow predicted here enables enhanced r-process abundances for A ≳ 140, and the authors note that future work should include deformation degrees of freedom for quantitative reproduction of abundances across the whole region.
