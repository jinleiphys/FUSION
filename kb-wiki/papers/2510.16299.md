# D-meson production via sequential hadronization in high-energy nuclear collisions

> Heavy flavor production serves as an ideal probe of the hadronization mechanism of the quark-gluon plasma created in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We study charm quark hadronization by taking Langevin equations for the charm quark transport in the medium and a sequential coalescence model for the hadronization. Since the $D_s$ formation is earlier than $D^0$, obtained from the Dirac equation with hot potential extracted from lattice QCD, the $D_s$ elliptic flow $v_2$ is smaller than the $D^0\ v_2$ in the intermediate $p_T$ region, which explains well the recent ALICE data. The sequential coalescence also predicts a hill in the yield ratio $D_s/D^0$ at low $p_T$, which can be tested in future heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a sequential hadronization mechanism, where $D_s$ mesons form at a higher temperature ($T_{D_s} \simeq 1.2 T_c$) than $D^0$ mesons (which form at $T_c$), can explain the ALICE observation that $D_s$ elliptic flow $v_2$ is smaller than $D^0$ $v_2$ in the intermediate $p_T$ region, a result that contradicts simultaneous hadronization models.

## Method

The authors simulate charm quark transport in the quark-gluon plasma using Langevin equations with both elastic scattering and inelastic gluon radiation, with the QGP evolution described by (3+1)D CLVisc hydrodynamics. Hadronization is modeled via a sequential coalescence approach where the formation temperatures for different charmed hadrons are determined by solving the Dirac equation with a lattice-simulated hot potential, giving $T_{D_s} = 1.2 T_c$ for $D_s$ mesons and $T_c$ for all other charmed hadrons. Charm quarks that do not coalescence at either hypersurface undergo fragmentation via the Peterson fragmentation function.

## Key numbers

- $D_s$ formation temperature: $T_{D_s} = 1.2 T_c$, where $T_c$ is the phase transition temperature
- Spatial charm quark diffusion coefficient: $2\pi T{\cal D}_s = 2.5$ (constant)
- Jet transport parameter at LHC: $\hat{q}_0 = 1.5$ GeV$^2$/fm
- Quark masses used: $m_{u,d} = 0.2$ GeV, $m_s = 0.3$ GeV, $m_c = 1.5$ GeV
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature: $137$ MeV
- Fragmentation parameter: $\epsilon = 0.01$ for charmed mesons, $\epsilon = 0.02$ for charmed baryons
- Mass cut for charmed meson states: $m < 2.6$ GeV; for charmed baryon states: $m < 3.1$ GeV
- Collision systems: Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV and $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.36$ TeV

## Context

This work extends the sequential hadronization framework previously developed for quarkonia by Zhao and collaborators (2018, 2020) to open charm mesons, and directly addresses the recent high-precision ALICE measurement (Wu, 2025) showing $v_2(D_s^+) < v_2(D^0)$ in intermediate $p_T$, which contradicts simultaneous hadronization predictions from transport models such as those by Zhao (2023). The results enable future experimental tests of the predicted hill in the $D_s/D^0$ yield ratio at low $p_T$ and provide a new method for constraining heavy-quark energy loss and hadronization dynamics.
