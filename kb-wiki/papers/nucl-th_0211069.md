# The Proton-Deuteron Break-Up Process in a Three-Dimensional Approach

> The pd break-up amplitude in the Faddeev scheme is calculated by employing a three-dimensional method without partial wave decomposition (PWD). In a first step and in view of higher energies only the leading term is evaluated and this for the process d(p,n)pp. A comparison with the results based on PWD reveals discrepancies in the cross section around 200 MeV. This indicates the onset of a limitation of the partial wave scheme. Also, around 200 MeV relativistic effects are clearly visible and the use of relativistic kinematics shifts the cross section peak to where the experimental peak is located. The theoretical peak height, however, is wrong and calls first of all for the inclusion of rescattering terms, which are shown to be important in a nonrelativistic full Faddeev calculation in PWD.

## Key claim

The leading term of the pd break-up amplitude in the Faddeev scheme, calculated using a three-dimensional method without partial wave decomposition (PWD), shows discrepancies in the cross section compared to PWD-based results around 200 MeV, indicating the onset of limitations of the partial wave scheme; however, the theoretical peak height remains wrong even with relativistic kinematics, requiring inclusion of rescattering terms.

## Method

The authors computed the leading term (first order in the NN t-matrix) of the proton-deuteron break-up amplitude for the process d(p,n)pp using a three-dimensional (3D) approach that employs relative momentum vectors and a helicity representation of total spin, avoiding partial wave decomposition entirely. They used the realistic NN potentials Bonn-B and AV18, and compared their 3D results to PWD-based calculations that included NN t-matrix for total angular momenta j=5 and j=7, with total 3N angular momenta up to J=31/2. They also investigated the effect of switching from nonrelativistic to relativistic kinematics by employing Lorentz boosted momenta.

## Key numbers

- The comparison between 3D and PWD results was performed at a laboratory energy of 197 MeV for the process d(p,n)pp.
- The PWD calculation included NN t-matrix for total NN angular momenta j=5 and j=7, and states of total 3N angular momenta up to J=31/2.
- At lower energies, around 100 MeV, the PWD agrees perfectly well with the 3D results.
- The spin averaged differential cross section and analyzing power A_y were shown at angles of 13 degrees and 24 degrees for the 197 MeV data.

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional approach previously developed for NN scattering (Fachruddin, Elster, Gloeckle, 2000) and the deuteron (Fachruddin, Elster, Gloeckle, 2001) to the three-nucleon break-up process. It reveals that at energies around 200 MeV, the standard partial wave decomposition method reaches its limits of reliability, as demonstrated by discrepancies with the 3D calculation. The results indicate that a complete description of the pd break-up cross section at these energies requires three ingredients: a 3D treatment, rescattering effects (which were shown to be important in nonrelativistic full Faddeev calculations in PWD), and relativistic kinematics. The experimental data used for comparison were from Prout et al. (2002).
