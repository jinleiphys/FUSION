# Finite temperature spectral functions of the linear O(N)-model at large N applied to the $π-σ$ system

> The thermal evolution of the spectral densities derivable from the two-point functions of the elementary and the quadratic composite fields of the O(N) model is studied in the isosinglet channel and in the broken symmetry phase at infinite N. The results are applied with realistic parameter values to the N=4 case. They provide a reasonable description of the $σ$ meson at T=0. Threshold enhancement is observed around $T\sim 1.07m_π$. For higher temperatures the maximum of the spectral function in the single meson channel decreases and becomes increasingly rounded.

# Finite temperature spectral functions of the linear O(N)-model at large N applied to the π-σ system

## Key claim

The leading-order large N expansion of the O(4) linear sigma model provides a reasonable description of the σ meson at T=0 and predicts threshold enhancement around T ~ 1.07 m_π, but the restrictions on the scalar self-coupling λ_R arising from the requirement of keeping distance from the tachyonic pole prevent finding a fully realistic σ-particle mass at T=0 when the physical pion mass is the input.

## Method

The authors study the thermal evolution of spectral densities from two-point functions of elementary and quadratic composite fields in the O(N) model, working in the broken symmetry phase at infinite N using the leading-order large N approximation to the Schwinger-Dyson equations. They analytically continue the finite-temperature bubble diagram into the lower half-plane to locate the complex pole of the σ propagator as a function of temperature, and apply the results with realistic parameter values to the N=4 case. The equation of state determining the condensate is solved self-consistently, and the spectral functions are computed for both the single-meson channel and the composite (φ^a)^2 channel.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature in the chiral limit is T_c = 161 MeV, obtained using the experimental value of f_π, compared to lattice simulations yielding (173 ± 8) MeV (Karsch, 2001).
- For the representative value λ_R = 310, the ratio M_σ/Γ = 1 at T=0, with M_σ estimated to be 7 Φ_0 ~ 350 MeV.
- Threshold enhancement in the σ-channel spectral function is observed in the close neighbourhood of T = 1.074 m_G0 (where m_G0 is the zero-temperature pion mass).
- The ratio m^2_G0 / Φ_0^2 ≈ 9.06 is fixed by phenomenology for the explicit symmetry breaking case.
- For λ_R ≤ 400, the tachyonic pole mass satisfies M_L / M_0 > 4, ensuring the effective approach is well justified.
- In the explicit symmetry breaking case with λ_R = 400, the phenomenologically preferred range gives M_σ = 3.95 f_π and M_σ/Γ ~ 1.4.

## Context

This work extends the large N analysis of the O(N) model to finite-temperature spectral functions in the isosinglet channel, building on earlier studies of hybridisation in scalar theories by Kondor (1974) and Szepfalusy (1976), and on applications to superfluid helium by Griffin (1993). The results are relevant for interpreting π-π correlations measured in heavy ion collisions, where temperature-induced shifts in the σ pole location are expected to be reflected in the spectra. The authors note that next-to-leading order corrections are needed to achieve a fully realistic description, and that the dynamical scaling analysis yields a dynamical exponent z = d/2 for finite N in the true critical region, while at N=∞ the precritical region with z̃ = 1 dominates, consistent with earlier work by Sasvari (1974, 1975) and Szepfalusy (1974).
