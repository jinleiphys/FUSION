# $D\bar{D}$ production and their interactions

> S and P wave $D\bar{D}$ scatterings are studied in a meson exchange model with the coupling constants obtained in the heavy quark effective theory. With the extracted P wave phase shifts and the separable potential approximation, we include the $D\bar{D}$ rescattering effect and investigate the production process $e^+e^-\to D\bar{D}$. We find that it is difficult to explain the anomalous line shape observed by the BES collaboration with this mechanism. Combining our model calculation and the experimental measurement, we estimate the upper limit of the nearly universal cutoff parameter to be 1.7 GeV. With this number, the upper limits of the binding energies of the S wave $D\bar{D}$ and $B\bar{B}$ bound states are obtained. Assuming that the S wave and P wave interactions rely on the same cutoff, our study provides a way of extracting the information about S wave molecular bound states from the P wave meson pair production.

## Key claim

The authors find that the $D\bar{D}$ rescattering mechanism, even with strong P-wave attraction, cannot explain the anomalous line shape observed by the BES Collaboration in $e^+e^-\to D\bar{D}$ production, contrary to what might be expected if final state interactions were responsible for the structure.

## Method

The authors study S- and P-wave $D\bar{D}$ scatterings using a meson exchange model with $\sigma$, $\rho$, and $\omega$ exchange, where coupling constants are derived from heavy quark effective theory and vector meson dominance. They extract P-wave phase shifts from the scattering calculation, then use the Yamaguchi separable potential approximation to incorporate multiple $D\bar{D}$ rescattering effects into the production process $e^+e^-\to D\bar{D}$ via $\psi(3770)$.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit of the nearly universal cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ estimated to be around 2 GeV, combining model calculation and experimental measurement.
- S-wave $D\bar{D}$ scattering lengths range from 0.0091 fm (NV, $m_\sigma=600$ MeV, $\Lambda=0.8$ GeV) to -1.14 fm (VC, $m_\sigma=600$ MeV, $\Lambda=2.0$ GeV, bound state).
- S-wave $D\bar{D}$ binding energies reach -29.4 MeV ($m_\sigma=600$ MeV, $\Lambda=2.0$ GeV, VC) and -31.8 MeV ($m_\sigma=400$ MeV, $\Lambda=2.0$ GeV, VC).
- P-wave $D\bar{D}$ scattering volumes range from 0.0014 fm$^3$ (NV, $m_\sigma=600$ MeV, $\Lambda=0.8$ GeV) to 0.087 fm$^3$ (VC, $m_\sigma=400$ MeV, $\Lambda=2.0$ GeV).
- S-wave $B\bar{B}$ binding energies reach -186.0 MeV ($m_\sigma=600$ MeV, $\Lambda=2.0$ GeV, VC) and -190.5 MeV ($m_\sigma=400$ MeV, $\Lambda=2.0$ GeV, VC).
- P-wave $B\bar{B}$ scattering volumes range from 0.0040 fm$^3$ (NV, $m_\sigma=600$ MeV, $\Lambda=0.8$ GeV) to 4.55 fm$^3$ (VC, $m_\sigma=600$ MeV, $\Lambda=2.0$ GeV).

## Context

This work extends previous meson exchange model studies of heavy meson-antimeson systems, such as those by Tornqvist and by Liu et al. (2007, 2009), and addresses the anomalous line shape observed by the BES Collaboration in $e^+e^-\to$ hadrons and $D\bar{D}$ production. The results enable future constraints on the cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ from lattice QCD simulations or experimental measurements, and provide a method to extract information about S-wave molecular bound states from P-wave meson pair production, assuming the same cutoff applies to both partial waves.
