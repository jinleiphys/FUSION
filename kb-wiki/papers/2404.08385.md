# $Ab$-$initio$ nucleon-nucleon correlations and their impact on high energy $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions

> Investigating nucleon-nucleon correlations inherent to the strong nuclear force is one of the core goals in nuclear physics research. We showcase the unique opportunities offered by collisions of $^{16}$O nuclei at high-energy facilities to reveal detailed many-body properties of the nuclear ground state. We interface existing knowledge about the geometry of $^{16}$O coming from \textit{ab-initio} calculations of nuclear structure with transport simulations of high-energy $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions. Bulk observables in these processes, such as the elliptic flow or the fluctuations of the mean transverse momentum, are found to depend significantly on the input nuclear model and to be sensitive to realistic clustering and short-range repulsive correlations, effectively opening a new avenue to probe these features experimentally. This finding demonstrates collisions of oxygen nuclei as a tool to elucidate initial conditions of small collision systems while fostering connections with effective field theories of nuclei rooted in quantum chromodynamics (QCD).

## Key claim

Bulk observables in high-energy 16O+16O collisions, such as elliptic flow and mean transverse momentum fluctuations, depend qualitatively on the resolution of nucleon-nucleon correlations in the input nuclear model: low-resolution models (pion-less EFT, clustered mean-field densities) enhance fluctuations relative to a spherical independent-nucleon baseline, while models including short-range repulsive effects suppress them.

## Method

The authors sampled nucleon configurations for 16O from three ab-initio frameworks: Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) with a pion-less EFT Hamiltonian, Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) with an N2LO chiral EFT Hamiltonian, and an ab-initio Projected Generator Coordinate Method (PGCM) with an N3LO chiral EFT Hamiltonian. These configurations were used as input to the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) framework to simulate 16O+16O collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. Final-state observables (mean transverse momentum, elliptic flow, and their fluctuations and correlations) were computed as a function of collision centrality and normalized to results from a baseline three-parameter Fermi (3pF) charge density model of independent nucleons.

## Key numbers

- The 3pF baseline fit to the measured charge density of 16O used parameters R = 2.608 fm, a0 = 0.513 fm, and w = -0.051.
- The NLEFT calculation used a lattice spacing a = 1.3155 fm and a resolution scale Lambda = 465 MeV, with Gaussian smearing of width 0.84 fm at each lattice site.
- The VMC and PGCM calculations both used a resolution scale Lambda = 500 MeV.
- Fitted 3pF parameters for the VMC one-body density were R = 1.84 fm, a0 = 0.46 fm, and w = 1.76; for the NLEFT one-body density they were R = 1.65 fm, a0 = 0.53 fm, and w = 0.87.
- The uncertainty on the size of the interaction region from the nuclear model is estimated at about 0.1% for quantities probing the size, such as mean transverse momentum.
- Uncertainties on collective flow characterizations are estimated at order 10-20% from the nuclear inputs.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of 16O structure using ab-initio methods (NLEFT by Summerfield (2021), VMC by Lim (2018), PGCM by Frosini (2021)) and connects them to high-energy collision phenomenology. It builds on earlier assessments of alpha-clustered geometries in 16O (He (2021), Rybczynski (2019)) and on the use of heavy-ion collisions to image nuclear ground-state structure (STAR (2024), Bally (2022)). The results enable experimental tests of nuclear Hamiltonians via flow measurements at RHIC and the LHC, and provide a baseline for interpreting upcoming 16O+16O data from the STAR collaboration.
