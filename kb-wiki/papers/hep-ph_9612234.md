# Thermal Photon Emission from QGP fluid

> We compare the numerical results of thermal photon distribution from the hot QCD matter produced by high energy nuclear collisions, based on hydrodynamical model, with the recent experimental data obtained by CERN WA80. Through the asymptotic value of the slope parameter of the transverse momentum distribution, we discuss the characteristic temperature of the QCD fluid.

## Key claim

The paper claims that only a QGP fluid model with a phase transition can consistently reproduce the CERN WA80 thermal photon data for S+Au 200 GeV/nucleon collisions, while a hot hadron gas model without phase transition fails. The key hedge is that the QGP phase region's contribution to the photon distribution is "negligibly small", the observed thermal photons come almost entirely from the hadron phase region of the QGP fluid model, not directly from the QGP itself.

## Method

The authors solve (3+1)-dimensional relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamics for S+Au 200 GeV/nucleon collisions, using two equations of state: a QGP fluid model with a phase transition (parameters: A=1.89, M=200 MeV, T_c=160 MeV, d=2 MeV) and a hot hadron gas model without phase transition. They first tune the hydrodynamical parameters to reproduce the measured hadron spectra (pseudo-rapidity and transverse momentum distributions), then compute the thermal photon production rate from a quantum Langevin equation approach with a damping parameter gamma(T)=cT (c=0.01), and integrate this rate over the space-time evolution of the fluid to obtain the photon transverse momentum distribution.

## Key numbers

* Initial temperature T_i = 195 MeV for the QGP fluid model with phase transition; T_i = 400 MeV for the hot hadron gas model without phase transition.
* Critical temperature T_c = 160 MeV and freeze-out temperature T_f = 140 MeV for both models.
* The damping parameter in the photon production rate is c = 0.01, where gamma(T) = cT.
* The asymptotic slope parameter T_s of the transverse momentum distribution at k_T = 20 GeV is compared with the maximum effective temperature T_eff from hydrodynamical simulation (values given in Table 1, but the specific numbers from Table 1 are not reproduced in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hydrodynamical analyses of S+Au collisions (Muroya 1995) by adding thermal photon observables to the hadron spectra already studied. It contrasts with earlier theoretical analyses by Srivastava, Arbex, Neumann, and Dumitru (all cited without years in the text), which fitted thermal photon data without simultaneously analyzing hadron spectra. The paper enables future use of the asymptotic slope parameter of the photon p_T distribution as a probe of the characteristic temperature (critical temperature versus initial temperature) of the QCD fluid produced in nuclear collisions.
