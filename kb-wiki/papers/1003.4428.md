# Mean field approach to flavor susceptibilities with a vector interaction

> We show that flavor diagonal and off-diagonal susceptibilities of light quarks at vanishing chemical potential can be calculated consistently assuming the baryon density and isospin density dependence of QCD to be expressed by a vector-isoscalar and a vector-isovector coupling, respectively. At the mean field level, their expression depends only on the effective medium-dependent couplings and quark thermodynamic potential. The strength of the couplings can be then estimated from the model using lattice QCD data as an input.

## Key claim

The paper shows that flavor diagonal and off-diagonal susceptibilities of light quarks at vanishing chemical potential can be calculated consistently using a mean-field approach with vector-isoscalar and vector-isovector couplings, but the extraction of the coupling strengths from lattice QCD data requires additional assumptions about the quark thermodynamic potential, leading to upper and lower bounds rather than unique values.

## Method

The authors construct an effective partition function for the light quark doublet (up and down quarks) that includes vector-isoscalar and vector-isovector mean-field interactions, with medium-dependent couplings and a medium-dependent quark thermodynamic potential. They impose thermodynamic consistency conditions (stationarity of the partition function with respect to densities and other variables) and derive expressions for flavor susceptibilities at zero chemical potential. Using lattice QCD data from Allton (2005) on flavor susceptibilities, they invert these relations to extract the vector couplings, considering two extreme cases: one where the vector-isovector coupling vanishes (lower bound) and one where all deviation from the Stefan-Boltzmann limit is attributed to vector interactions (upper bound).

## Key numbers

- The difference between vector-isoscalar and vector-isovector couplings, given by (gf - gfv) T^3, is large for T/T_c < 1 and vanishes quickly above the critical temperature, as extracted from lattice QCD data with bare quark mass m/T = 0.4.
- The susceptibilities chi_q and chi_I reach approximately 80% of the Stefan-Boltzmann limit at high temperature.
- Assuming T_c = 0.17 GeV, the vector-isoscalar coupling gi ranges between 4 GeV^{-2} and 19 GeV^{-2} at T = T_c.
- The upper bound for the quark thermodynamic potential Phi^M at T = 2 T_c is set equal to the Stefan-Boltzmann limit for massless particles, 12 T^3 / pi^2.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle approach to the Quark Gluon Plasma, which was shown by Koch (2009) to be consistent with vanishing flavor correlations for T > 1.2 T_c, and builds on the observation by Kunihiro (1991) that lattice data on baryon number susceptibility can be described by a temperature-dependent repulsive vector coupling in the Nambu Jona-Lasinio model. The method enables estimation of vector coupling strengths from lattice QCD data, which is relevant for understanding the QCD phase diagram and the possible existence of a critical point, as discussed by Fukushima (2008) and deForcrand (2007). The results provide constraints that can be compared with effective models such as the Polyakov loop extended NJL model and the Polyakov-quark-meson model of Schaefer (2009).
