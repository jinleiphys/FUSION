# (3+1)-dimensional framework for leading-order non conformal anisotropic hydrodynamics

> In this work I develop a new framework for anisotropic hydrodynamics that generalizes the leading order of the hydrodynamic expansion to the full (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic massive case. Following previous works, my considerations are based on the Boltzmann kinetic equation with the collisional term treated in the relaxation time approximation. The momentum anisotropy is included explicitly in the leading term, allowing for a large difference between the longitudinal and transverse pressures as well as for non trivial transverse dynamics. Energy and momentum conservation is expressed by the first moment of the Boltzmann equation. The system of equations is closed by using the zeroth and second moments of the Boltzmann equation. The close-to-equilibrium matching with second-order viscous hydrodynamics is demonstrated. In particular, I show that the coupling between shear and bulk pressure corrections, recently proved to be important for an accurate description of momentum anisotropy and bulk viscous dynamics, does not vanish in the close-to-equilibrium limit.

## Key claim

This work develops a leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics framework for the full (3+1)-dimensional massive case that includes non-trivial transverse dynamics and finite particle masses, with the claim that the coupling between shear and bulk pressure corrections does not vanish in the close-to-equilibrium limit, contrary to what the abstract's mention of "demonstrated" matching might suggest without this qualifier.

## Method

The framework is based on the Boltzmann kinetic equation with the collision term treated in the relaxation time approximation (RTA). The momentum anisotropy is included explicitly at leading order through an anisotropic phase-space distribution function characterized by a momentum scale parameter λ, a rank-two traceless tensor ξ^{μν} for shear anisotropy, and a scalar φ for isotropic pressure correction. Energy and momentum conservation is enforced via the first moment of the Boltzmann equation, while the system is closed using the zeroth and second moments, with the traceless part of the second moment providing five equations for ξ^{μν} and three candidate scalar equations (zeroth moment, trace of the second moment, or four-velocity projection of the second moment) for φ.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium energy density is given by ε_eq = 4π \tilde{N} T^4 (m/T)^2 [3K_2(m/T) + (m/T) K_1(m/T)], where \tilde{N} = N_dof/(2π)^3, T is the effective temperature, and K_n are modified Bessel functions.
- The equilibrium particle density is n_eq = 4π \tilde{N} T^3 (m/T)^2 K_2(m/T).
- The equilibrium pressure is P_eq = 4π \tilde{N} T^4 (m/T)^2 K_2(m/T).
- In the close-to-equilibrium limit, the shear pressure correction relates to the anisotropy tensor as π^{μν} = -ϖ_ξ(m,T) ξ^{μν} and the bulk pressure correction as Π = -ϖ_Π(m,T) φ, where ϖ_ξ and ϖ_Π are functions defined in the appendix.
- The close-to-equilibrium reduction yields shear evolution equations with transport coefficients τ_π, η, τ_ππ, δ_ππ, λ_πΠ, φ_6, φ_7 and bulk evolution equations with τ_Π, ζ, δ_ΠΠ, λ_Ππ, φ_1, φ_3, all of which include non-vanishing shear-bulk coupling terms.

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework from the (2+1)-dimensional boost-invariant case of Bazow, Heinz, and Strickland (2013) and the (1+1)-dimensional cylindrically symmetric case of Tinti (2014) to the full (3+1)-dimensional massive case with general transverse dynamics. It also generalizes the Romatschke-Strickland form used in earlier aHydro formulations by allowing two independent anisotropy parameters, following the approach of Nopoush, Ryblewski, and Strickland (2014). The framework enables systematic comparison with exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation in RTA, as done by Florkowski et al. (2013), and provides a foundation for testing which choice of the bulk equation (zeroth moment, trace of second moment, or four-velocity projection) best reproduces kinetic theory results in numerical studies.
