# Giant Octupole Resonance Simulation

> Using a pseudo-particle technique we simulate large-amplitude isoscalar giant octupole excitations in a finite nuclear system. Dependent on the initial conditions we observe either clear octupole modes or over-damped octupole modes which decay immediately into quadrupole ones. This shows clearly a behavior beyond linear response. We propose that octupole modes might be observed in central collisions of heavy ions.

## Key claim

Using a pseudo-particle Vlasov simulation, the authors show that isoscalar giant octupole resonances can appear in finite nuclei only when the initial excitation deposits sufficient collective energy through correlated spatial and momentum perturbations; otherwise the octupole mode is over-damped and decays immediately into a quadrupole mode, demonstrating behavior beyond linear response.

## Method

The authors solve the Vlasov kinetic equation using a pseudo-particle technique with Gaussian test particles (75 per nucleon) and a Skyrme-type mean field with compression modulus K=200 MeV. They initialize the system in two ways: (1) a pure momentum-space deformation corresponding to an octupole mode, and (2) a spatial splitting of the nucleus into two pieces of mass ratio 3:7 accelerated toward each other, mimicking a central heavy-ion collision. Multipole moments of the mass distribution are extracted via spherical harmonic decomposition and tracked in time.

## Key numbers

- Compression modulus of the Skyrme interaction: K = 200 MeV
- Skyrme parameters: a = -356 MeV, b = 303 MeV, exponent s = 7/6
- Pseudo-particle width: σ_r = 0.53 fm
- Pseudo-particles per nucleon: 75
- Ground state density for ²⁰⁸Pb: ρ₀ = 0.162 fm⁻³
- Background collective energy from finite-width correlations: 54 MeV
- Adiabaticity index η = 0.12 for the momentum-only excitation (figure X100)
- Adiabaticity index η = 0.30 for the spatial-splitting excitation (figure X088)
- Radius elongation in coordinate space: about 10%

## Context

This work extends earlier hypotheses by Jaroszwicz and Swiatecki (1993) and Blocki and Shlomo (1993) that the octupole mode is over-damped due to negative curved surface and chaotic damping. The authors show that the damping depends critically on initial conditions, contradicting the linear-response expectation of damping-rate independence. The results suggest that isoscalar giant octupole resonances might be observable in central heavy-ion collisions with mass ratio near 3:7, and enable future experimental searches for such modes beyond the linear-response regime.
