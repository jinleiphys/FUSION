# Global fluid fits to identified particle transverse momentum spectra from heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> Transverse momentum spectra of identified particles produced in heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider are described with relativistic fluid dynamics. We perform a systematic comparison of experimental data for pions, kaons and protons up to a transverse momentum of 3 GeV$/c$ with calculations using the FluiduM code package to solve the evolution equations of fluid dynamics, the TrENTo model to describe the initial state and the FastReso code to take resonance decays into account. Using data in five centrality classes at the center-of-mass collision energy per nucleon pair $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}=2.76\,\text{TeV}$, we determine systematically the most likely parameters of our theoretical model including the shear and bulk viscosity to entropy ratios, the initialization time, initial density and freeze-out temperature through a global search and quantify their posterior probability. This is facilitated by the very efficient numerical implementation of FluiduM and FastReso. Based on the most likely model parameters we present predictions for the transverse momentum spectra of multi-strange hadrons as well as identified particle spectra from Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}=5.02\,\text{TeV}$.

## Key claim
The paper claims that transverse momentum spectra of identified particles alone can provide non-trivial constraints on the shear and bulk viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma, contrary to the previous belief that anisotropic flow measurements were necessary for such constraints. The authors hedge that this conclusion depends on the high quality of current experimental data and the assumption that their theoretical model captures the essential physics, noting that the goodness-of-fit is very small (Q=1.8e-8), indicating the model is incomplete.

## Method
The authors combined the TrENTo initial condition model with the FluiduM relativistic viscous fluid dynamics solver and the FastReso resonance decay code to compute transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and protons in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV. They performed a global chi-squared minimization over nine free parameters (shear viscosity to entropy ratio eta/s, maximum bulk viscosity to entropy ratio (zeta/s)_max, initialization time tau_0, freeze-out temperature T_fo, and five centrality-dependent normalization constants Norm_i) using a discretized hypercubic lattice of 10^5 parameter combinations per centrality class with order-7 spline interpolation and Minuit minimization.

## Key numbers
- Best fit yields chi^2/N_dof = 1.37 for 555 data points minus 9 parameters (546 degrees of freedom)
- Transverse momentum range fitted: p_T < 3 GeV/c for pions (41 p_T intervals), kaons (36 intervals), and protons (34 intervals)
- Five centrality classes fitted: 0-5%, 5-10%, 10-20%, 20-30%, and 30-40% at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV
- Freeze-out temperature T_fo scanned in range 130-180 MeV with 0.5 MeV increments
- Initialization time tau_0 varied in range 0.1-0.6 fm/c
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio eta/s varied in range 0.08-0.25
- Maximum bulk viscosity to entropy ratio (zeta/s)_max varied in range 0.005-0.1
- Bulk viscosity Lorentzian peak temperature T_peak=175 MeV with width Delta T=24 MeV
- Correlation between eta/s and (zeta/s)_max is 0.97 in the Gaussian approximation

## Context
This work extends previous multi-observable model-to-data fits by Bernhard (2016) that focused on momentum-integrated quantities like multiplicity and mean transverse momentum, by instead fitting differential transverse momentum spectra. The authors use the TrENTo initial condition model from Moreland (2014) and the FluiduM code from Floerchinger (2018), with an enlarged resonance list of approximately 700 states based on Alba (2017) and the Particle Data Group 2016 edition. The results enable predictions for multi-strange hadron spectra and identified particle spectra at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, and demonstrate that high-quality spectra data alone can constrain transport coefficients, though the authors caution that systematic uncertainties from model incompleteness must be estimated through variations of the fitting scheme.
