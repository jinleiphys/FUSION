# Selfconsistent effective interactions and symmetry restoration

> In this paper we propose a general framework for deriving the effective interactions for many-body systems. We show how the selfconsistent interaction can be constructed on the quantum level for both local and nonlocal potentials. In particular the relation between the selfconsistent and symmetry restoring interaction is derived. The difference between the two is related to the coupling of the perturbing field to the kinetic energy operator.

# Selfconsistent effective interactions and symmetry restoration

## Key claim

The paper proposes a general quantum-level framework for deriving effective interactions in many-body systems, showing that the difference between selfconsistent and symmetry-restoring coupling constants arises from whether the perturbing field commutes with the kinetic energy operator, not from shell effects as previously assumed.

## Method

The authors construct the selfconsistent interaction by requiring that a one-body perturbation generated by a field operator \(\hat{A}\) induces the same change in the density as in the potential \(\hat{V}\). They derive the coupling constant \(\chi^{self}\) from the double commutator \(\langle [[\hat{A},\hat{V}],\hat{A}] \rangle_0\) and compare it to the symmetry-restoring coupling constant \(\chi^{rest}\) obtained from the response function at zero frequency. The formalism is applied to both local and nonlocal potentials, including the harmonic oscillator as an explicit example.

## Key numbers

- For the three-dimensional spherical harmonic oscillator, the perturbed hamiltonian under a quadrupole field yields frequency shifts: \(\delta\omega_1 = \delta\omega_2 = -\frac{1}{2}\alpha\omega + O(\alpha^2)\) and \(\delta\omega_3 = \alpha\omega + O(\alpha^2)\), where \(\alpha = 4\theta\).
- The selfconsistency condition for the harmonic oscillator takes the form \(\omega_i \Sigma_i = \text{const.}\), with \(\Sigma_i = m\omega_i Tr( x_i^2 \hat{\rho} )\).
- For dipole distortions, the operators \(\hat{A}_{1\mu}\) are proportional to momentum components (e.g., \(\hat{A}_{10} \sim \hat{p}_z\)), giving \(\delta\hat{T}=0\) and thus \(\chi^{rest}_{1\mu} = \chi^{self}_{1\mu}\).

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical approaches of Bohr and Mottelson (spherical nuclei) and Marshalek (deformed systems, Nilsson potential) by providing a quantum-level derivation of selfconsistent interactions. It also connects to the symmetry restoration method of Pyatov and collaborators, which had not previously been linked to the selfconsistent force concept. The formalism enables treatment of both local and nonlocal potentials, including momentum-dependent effects not captured by earlier work such as Sakamoto and Kishimoto (double-stretched multipole interaction) or Kubo et al. (momentum-dependent potentials).
