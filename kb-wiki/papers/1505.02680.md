# Evaluation of the proton charge radius from e-p scattering

> In light of the proton radius puzzle, the discrepancy between measurements of the proton charge radius from muonic hydrogen and those from electronic hydrogen and electron-proton scattering measurements, we reexamine the charge radius extractions from electron scattering measurements. We provide a recommended value for the proton RMS charge radius, $r_E = 0.879 \pm 0.011$ fm, based on a global examination of elastic e-p scattering data. The uncertainties include contributions to account for tension between different data sets and inconsistencies between radii using different extraction procedures.

## Key claim

The paper provides a recommended proton RMS charge radius of \(r_E = 0.879 \pm 0.011\) fm from a global reexamination of elastic e-p scattering data, but this value includes additional uncertainties to account for tension between different data sets and inconsistencies between extraction procedures, and the authors note that even with this conservative approach a 3.5\(\sigma\) discrepancy with muonic hydrogen remains.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed existing elastic electron-proton scattering data by separately examining the Mainz high-precision data set and other world data, applying hadronic two-photon exchange corrections, and evaluating systematic uncertainties including normalization factors, correlated systematic errors, and model dependence from fitting functions. They then combined the Mainz and world data results using an unweighted average and added an additional systematic uncertainty based on the observed discrepancy in the magnetic radius, scaled by the relative sensitivity of the charge and magnetic radii to systematic effects.

## Key numbers

- Recommended proton RMS charge radius from combined e-p scattering: \(r_E = 0.879 \pm 0.011\) fm
- Updated Mainz data charge radius: \(r_E = 0.875 \pm 0.015\) fm
- Updated world data charge radius: \(r_E = 0.881 \pm 0.011\) fm
- Updated Mainz data magnetic radius: \(r_M = 0.799 \pm 0.028\) fm
- Updated world data magnetic radius: \(r_M = 0.867 \pm 0.020\) fm
- Muonic hydrogen charge radius from Antognini (2013): \(r_E = 0.8409 \pm 0.0004\) fm
- CODATA 2012 combined e-p scattering and atomic hydrogen value: \(r_E = 0.8775 \pm 0.0051\) fm
- Discrepancy between e-p scattering and muonic hydrogen: 3.5\(\sigma\)
- Discrepancy between combined e-p scattering plus atomic hydrogen and muonic hydrogen: 5.7\(\sigma\)

## Context

This work addresses the proton radius puzzle, the discrepancy between the muonic hydrogen result from Antognini (2013) (\(r_E = 0.84087 \pm 0.00039\) fm) and values from electronic hydrogen and electron scattering. The authors extend earlier extractions by Sick (2003, 2006, 2012), Zhan (2011), and the Mainz A1 collaboration (Bernauer, 2013), applying more conservative uncertainty estimates and accounting for tensions between data sets. The recommended value enables a more reliable comparison between scattering results, atomic hydrogen measurements from Mohr (2012), and muonic hydrogen results, while highlighting the need for further investigation of systematic uncertainties in low-\(Q^2\) form factor measurements.
