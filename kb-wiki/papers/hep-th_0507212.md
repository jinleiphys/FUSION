# Chiral dynamics from the hadronic string: general formalism

> QCD at long distances can be described by the chiral Lagrangian. On the other hand there is overwhelming evidence that QCD and all non-abelian theories admit an effective string description. Here we review a derivation of the (intrinsic) parity-even chiral Lagrangian by requiring that the propagation of the QCD string takes place on a background where chiral symmetry is spontaneously broken. Requiring conformal invariance leads to the equation of motion of the chiral Lagrangian. We then proceed to coupling the string degrees of freedom to external gauge fields and we recover in this way the covariant equations of motion of the gauge-invariant chiral Lagrangian at p^2 order. We consider next the parity-odd part (Wess-Zumino-Witten) action and argue that this require the introduction of the spin degrees of freedom (absent in the usual effective action treatment). We manage to reproduce the Wess-Zumino-Witten term in 2D in an unambiguous way. In 4D the situation is considerably more involved. We outline the modification of boundary interaction that is necessary to induce the parity-odd part of the chiral Lagrangian.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the parity-even chiral Lagrangian can be derived from the QCD string by requiring conformal invariance when the string propagates on a background where chiral symmetry is spontaneously broken, but this derivation only works if the string is perturbed around the correct (non-perturbative) vacuum rather than the trivial vacuum that leads to tachyons and wrong chiral properties.

## Method

The authors couple a bosonic string action in four Euclidean dimensions to boundary Grassmann variables ("quarks") that interact with a chiral field U(x) living on the string boundary. They expand U(x) around a constant background, compute one-loop and two-loop divergences in the boundary field theory using dimensional regularization, and require that the beta-functional for the chiral couplings vanishes to preserve conformal invariance. The vanishing beta-function condition is interpreted as the equation of motion for the chiral field, from which the effective chiral Lagrangian is reconstructed.

## Key numbers

- The Regge trajectory slope is taken as alpha' = 0.9 GeV^{-2}, related to the inverse string tension
- The weak pion decay constant is f_pi = 90 MeV
- The predicted low-energy constants at order p^4 are: 2 L_1 = L_2 = -1/2 L_3 = f_pi^2 alpha' / 8 = f_pi^2 / (16 pi T) = 10^{-3} = xi
- The unique solution for the renormalized couplings is g_{1,r} = -g_{2,r} = -g_{3,r} = 2 and g = i
- The condition for unitarity and CP symmetry in the parity-even sector requires z^2 = -1
- In the two-dimensional QCD toy model, the anomaly coefficient requires g_A = 1 and z = 0 to reproduce the correct dimension-2 anomaly

## Context

This work extends the approach of Andrianov, Espriu, and collaborators (2004) that derived the parity-even chiral Lagrangian from string propagation in a chirally broken background. It contrasts with earlier string amplitudes such as the Veneziano amplitude and the Lovelace-Shapiro amplitude, which fail to reproduce the chiral properties of QCD because they correspond to expansion around the wrong vacuum. The paper enables a systematic derivation of both parity-even and parity-odd (Wess-Zumino-Witten) terms in the chiral Lagrangian from string theory, though the four-dimensional parity-odd case requires further development of the spin-flavor coupling algebra.
