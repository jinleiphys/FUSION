# Boson-conserving one-nucleon transfer operator in the interacting boson model

> The boson-conserving one-nucleon transfer operator in the interacting boson model (IBA) is reanalyzed. Extra terms are added to the usual form used for that operator. These new terms change generalized seniority by one unit, as the ones considered up to now. The results obtained using the new form for the transfer operator are compared with those obtained with the traditional form in a simple case involving the pseudo-spin Bose-Fermi symmetry $U^{B}(6) \otimes U^F(12)$ in its $U^{BF}(5) \otimes U^F(2)$ limit. Sizeable differences are found. These results are of relevance in the study of transfer reactions to check nuclear supersymmetry and in the description of (β)-decay within IBA.

## Key claim

The standard boson-conserving one-nucleon transfer operator in the interacting boson model (IBA) is incomplete; adding extra terms that change generalized seniority by one unit, terms of the form \([(d^\dagger \times \tilde{d})^{(\lambda)} \times a^\dagger_{j'}]^{(j)}_m\), produces sizeable differences in calculated spectroscopic intensities compared to the traditional operator, particularly in the \(U^{BF}(5) \otimes U^F(2)\) limit of the pseudo-spin Bose-Fermi symmetry.

## Method

The authors derived the new transfer operator by requiring equality of matrix elements of the fermion creation operator \(C^\dagger_j\) and the IBFA operator \(\mathcal{T}^{\dagger\prime}_j\) between states of generalized seniority \(\tilde{v}=2\) and \(\tilde{v}=3\) in the shell-model and boson-fermion spaces, respectively. They constructed non-orthonormal \(\tilde{v}=3\) fermion states from seniority-one and seniority-three components, then used the "democratic mapping" procedure, diagonalizing the fermion overlap matrix \(\Theta^J\) and applying the same eigenvector transformation to both fermion and boson states, to obtain orthonormal mapped states. The coefficients \(\phi^J_{jj'}\) for the new terms were then extracted from the mapping condition.

## Key numbers

* For the state \(|[2,0] \langle 0,0\rangle (0,0) 0;1/2\rangle\) in the odd-even nucleus, the new terms give zero contribution to transfers from the \(|2_B\rangle\) state in the even-even nucleus, while the traditional operator gives non-zero values for \(j=3/2\) and \(j=5/2\) transfers.
* For the state \(|[1,1] \langle 1,1\rangle (1,1) 1;1/2\rangle\), the new terms contribute constructively for one component and destructively for the other, with the wavefunction having components \(\sqrt{3/5} |(2_B \times a^\dagger_{3/2})^{(1/2)}\rangle + \sqrt{2/5} |(2_B \times a^\dagger_{5/2})^{(1/2)}\rangle\).
* The occupation probabilities used in the example were \(v_{1/2}^2 = 0.5\), \(v_{3/2}^2 = 0.25\), and \(v_{5/2}^2 = 0\), satisfying \(\sum_{j_k} (2j_k+1) v_{j_k}^2 = 2N\) with \(N=1\) boson.

## Context

This work extends the original boson-conserving one-nucleon transfer operator derived by Scholten and Dieperink (1981, 1985) using the generalized seniority scheme and the Otsuka-Arima-Iachello (OAI) mapping method. The new terms, which change seniority by one unit, arise naturally in alternative approaches such as nuclear field theory techniques (Sorensen, 1989) and the generalized Holstein-Primakoff mapping (Alhassid and collaborators, 1992, 1994). The results are relevant for testing nuclear supersymmetry in transfer reactions and for describing beta-decay within the IBA, where the new operator may significantly affect calculated matrix elements.
