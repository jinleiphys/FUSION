# Theoretical investigation of the antimagnetic rotation in $^{104}$Pd

> The particle-number-conserving method based on the cranked shell model is used to investigate the antimagnetic rotation band in $^{104}$Pd. The experimental moments of inertia and reduced $B(E2)$ transition probabilities are reproduced well. The $J^{(2)}/B(E2)$ ratios are also discussed. The occupation probability of each orbital close to the Fermi surface and the contribution of each major shell to the total angular momentum alignment with rotational frequency are analyzed. The backbending mechanism of the ground state band in $^{104}$Pd is understood clearly and the configuration of the antimagnetic rotation after backbending is clarified. In addition, the crossing of a four quasiparticle states with this antimagnetic rotation band is also predicted. By examining the the closing of the four proton hole angular momenta towards the neutron angular momenta, the "two-shears-like" mechanism for this antimagnetic rotation is investigated and two stages of antimagnetic rotation in $^{104}$Pd are seen clearly.

## Key claim

The particle-number-conserving cranked shell model (PNC-CSM) reproduces the experimental moments of inertia and reduced B(E2) transition probabilities for the antimagnetic rotation band in 104Pd, but only when pairing correlations are included and when the deformation parameter is allowed to decrease with rotational frequency at higher spins; the configuration of the band after backbending is clarified as πg9/2−4 ⊗ ν[h11/22,(g7/2,d5/2)6] when pairing is neglected, consistent with recent TAC-CDFT calculations by Jia (2018).

## Method

The particle-number-conserving method based on the cranked shell model (PNC-CSM) was used, in which the monopole pairing interaction is diagonalized directly in a large Fock space of cranked many-particle configurations (CMPCs) with dimension 1000 for both protons and neutrons, ensuring exact particle-number conservation and exact treatment of Pauli blocking. The Nilsson Hamiltonian with deformation ε2 = 0.18 was adopted, and effective monopole pairing strengths were set to Gp = 0.5 MeV for protons and Gn = 0.8 MeV for neutrons. The kinematic and dynamic moments of inertia, B(E2) values, occupation probabilities, and angular momentum alignments were calculated as functions of rotational frequency.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameter ε2 = 0.18 was taken as the experimental value from Rather (2014).
- Effective pairing strengths: Gp = 0.5 MeV for protons, Gn = 0.8 MeV for neutrons.
- The backbending in the ground state band occurs at rotational frequency ħω ≈ 0.4 MeV.
- At ħω ≈ 0.4 MeV, the occupation probability of the neutron h11/2 orbital ν1/2−[550] increases suddenly from about 0.4 to 2.0.
- At ħω ≈ 0.4 MeV, the occupation probability of the proton g9/2 orbital π7/2+[413] drops from about 0.6 to about 0.1.
- At ħω ≈ 0.8 MeV, the occupation of π1/2+[431] increases from nearly zero to about 0.6, and the occupation of π5/2+[422] drops from 1.9 to 1.3, indicating termination of the AMR band.
- The deformation shows a sharp reduction at ħω > 0.65 MeV and decreases to zero rapidly at ħω ≈ 0.75 MeV when fitting experimental B(E2) values.

## Context

This work extends the PNC-CSM approach previously applied to antimagnetic rotation bands in 105Cd, 106Cd (Zhang, 2013) and 101Pd (Zhang, 2016) to the case of 104Pd, which has a larger deformation (ε2 = 0.18) than those isotopes. It addresses a discrepancy between two prior configuration assignments for the AMR band in 104Pd: the semiclassical particle rotor model by Rather (2014) assumed πg9/2−2 ⊗ ν[h11/22,(g7/2,d5/2)2], while TAC-CDFT calculations by Jia (2018) with the PC-PK1 interaction assigned πg9/2−4 ⊗ ν[h11/22,(g7/2,d5/2)6]; the present work supports the latter configuration when pairing is neglected. The results enable future investigations of the predicted crossing of a four-quasiparticle state with the AMR band and the two-stage nature of the antimagnetic rotation mechanism in 104Pd.
