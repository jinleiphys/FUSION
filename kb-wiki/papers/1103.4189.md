# Coulomb dissociation of the deformed halo nucleus 31Ne

> The recently observed large cross sections for the Coulomb dissociation of $^{31}$Ne nucleus indicate that this nucleus takes a halo structure in the ground state. We analyse these experimental data using the particle-rotor model, that takes into account the rotational excitation of the core nucleus $^{30}$Ne. We show that the experimental data can be well reproduced when the quadrupole deformation parameter of $^{30}$Ne is around $Î²_2=0.2\sim0.3$, at which the ground state takes the spin and parity of $I^Ï€$=3/2$^-$. ## Method

The authors solve the particle-rotor model Hamiltonian for the 30Ne + n system without the adiabatic approximation, coupling the valence neutron to the rotational states of a deformed 30Ne core. The single-particle potential is a deformed Woods-Saxon potential expanded to linear order in Î²2, and the wave functions are expanded on a spherical basis with continuum states discretized in a 60 fm box. The Coulomb dissociation cross sections are computed from the E1 strength multiplied by the virtual photon number, summing over all final states above the neutron threshold.

## Context

This work extends the Nilsson model analysis of 31Ne by Hamamoto (2010) by including the non-adiabatic coupling between the valence neutron and the rotational states of the 30Ne core, which was neglected in the earlier adiabatic treatment. The particle-rotor model had previously been applied successfully to the halo nucleus 11Be by Eloirdi et al. (1995) and Nunes et al. (1996), and to neutron-rich carbon isotopes 15,17,19C by Tostevin and Howell (2004). The results enable future comparisons with momentum distribution and interaction cross section measurements recently obtained at the RIBF facility at RIKEN, and provide constraints on the deformation and ground-state spin-parity of 31Ne.
