# Energy Dependence of Jet Quenching and Life-time of the Dense Matter in High-energy Heavy-ion Collisions

> Suppression of high $p_T$ hadron spectra in high-energy heavy-ion collisions at different energies is studied within a pQCD parton model incorporating medium induced parton energy loss. The $p_T$ dependence of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}(p_T)$ is found to depend on both the energy dependence of the parton energy loss and the power-law behavior of the initial jet spectra. The high $p_T$ hadron suppression at $\sqrt{s}=62.4$ GeV and its centrality dependence are studied in detail. The overall values of the modification factor are found to provide strong constraints on the lifetime of the dense matter.

# Energy Dependence of Jet Quenching and Life-time of the Dense Matter in High-energy Heavy-ion Collisions

## Key claim

The flat $p_T$ dependence of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}(p_T)$ observed at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV is a coincidence arising from the combined effect of the energy dependence of parton energy loss and the power-law behavior of initial jet spectra; at other collision energies, the $p_T$ dependence is predicted to be monotonic (decreasing at 62.4 GeV, increasing at 5.5 TeV). The overall values of the modification factor provide strong constraints on the lifetime of the dense matter, but only if that lifetime is comparable to or smaller than the system size.

## Method

A leading-order pQCD parton model was used to calculate inclusive high-$p_T$ hadron cross sections in $A+A$ collisions, incorporating medium-induced parton energy loss through an effective modified fragmentation function. The parton energy loss was parameterized according to numerical results from Ref. Wang (2001) that include thermal gluon absorption, and the initial gluon density at different collision energies was assumed proportional to measured hadron multiplicity. The lifetime of the dense matter was treated as a free parameter by truncating the path integral for energy loss at $\tau_{\rm max} = \min(\Delta L, \tau_f)$, where $\tau_f$ is the lifetime before breakup.

## Key numbers

- At $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, the extracted energy loss parameter $\epsilon_0 = 1.07$ GeV/fm, with $\mu = 1.5$ GeV and $\lambda_0 = 0.3$ fm, corresponding to a static-medium energy loss $dE_0/dL \approx 14$ GeV/fm (about 30 times higher than in cold nuclei).
- The initial gluon density in central $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV is about 30/fm$^3$ at an initial time $\tau_0 = 0.2$ fm, giving a lower bound on initial energy density of about 25 GeV/fm$^3$.
- The lifetime of the plasma in central $Pb+Pb$ collisions at SPS ($\sqrt{s}=17.3$ GeV) is estimated as $\tau_s \sim 2-3$ fm/$c$, and the data point to $\tau_f < 2$ fm/$c$.
- The ratio of charged hadron multiplicities between LHC and RHIC is assumed as $(N_{ch}/d\eta)_{5500}/(N_{ch}/d\eta)_{200} \approx 2.4$.
- At $\sqrt{s}=62.4$ GeV, the nuclear modification factor for charged hadrons and neutral pions is predicted to diverge at $p_T > 8$ GeV/$c$ where recombination effects become negligible.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier parton model (Wang 1998) and energy-loss calculations (Wang 2001, 2003) to study the energy dependence of jet quenching from SPS ($\sqrt{s}=17.2$ GeV) through RHIC ($\sqrt{s}=62.4$ and 200 GeV) to LHC ($\sqrt{s}=5.5$ TeV). It contrasts the strong suppression observed at RHIC with the absence of suppression in WA98 data at SPS, and explains this via the finite lifetime of the dense matter rather than solely by differences in initial gluon density. The results enable experimental constraints on the plasma lifetime from measurements of $\pi^0$ and high-$p_T$ charged hadrons at $\sqrt{s}=62.4$ GeV, complementing elliptic flow measurements that are also sensitive to the thermalization time.
