# Antineutrino Opacity in Neutron Stars in the Models Constrained by Recent Terrestrial Experiments and Astrophysical Observations

> In the present paper, we investigate neutral current (NC) antineutrino scattering with the constituents of neutron star (NS) matter at zero temperature. The modeling of standard matter in NS is constructed within the framework of both extended relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) and nonrelativistic Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU energy density functional (KIDS-EDF) models. In the E-RMF model, we use a new parameter, G3(M), which was constrained by the recent PREX II experiment measurement of neutron distribution in $^{208}\rm{Pb}$, while the KIDS-EDF models are constrained by terrestrial experiments, gravitational-wave signals, and astrophysical observations. Using both realistic and well-constrained matter models, we then calculate the antineutrino differential cross-section (ADCS) and antineutrino mean free path (AMFP) for the interaction between antineutrinos and neutron star (NS) matter constituents using linear response theory. It is found that the AMFP for the KIDS0 and KIDSA models are smaller compared to the SLy4 model and the E-RMF model with the G3(M) parameter. The AMFP result of the Skyrme model with the SLy4 parameter set is found to have a prediction almost similar to that of the E-RMF model with the G3(M) parameter. Contributions of each nucleon to the total AMFP are also presented for the G3(M) model.

## Key claim

The antineutrino mean free path (AMFP) in neutron star matter depends on the nuclear model used, with the KIDS0 and KIDSA models predicting smaller AMFP values than the SLy4 model and the E-RMF model with the G3(M) parameter, though the abstract does not specify that this difference becomes pronounced only above a baryon density of approximately \(n_B/n_0 = 3.5\).

## Method

The author modeled neutron star matter using both the extended relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) model with the G3(M) parameter (constrained by the PREX II experiment's measurement of the neutron distribution in \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\)) and the nonrelativistic Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU energy density functional (KIDS-EDF) models (KIDS0, KIDSA, and SLy4, constrained by terrestrial experiments, gravitational-wave signals, and astrophysical observations). Using linear response theory at zero temperature, the antineutrino differential cross-section (ADCS) and antineutrino mean free path (AMFP) were calculated for neutral-current scattering of antineutrinos with neutrons and protons in the nuclear medium.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy per nucleon reproduced by both models is \(E_B/A = 15\) MeV at a saturation density \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- For the KIDS0 model, the isoscalar effective mass \(\mu^*_s = 0.991 M\) and isovector effective mass \(\mu^*_v = 0.819 M\); for the KIDSA model, \(\mu^*_s = 1.004 M\) and \(\mu^*_v = 0.827 M\).
- The nuclear symmetry energy \(J\) is 32.8 MeV for KIDS0 and 33 MeV for KIDSA; the slope parameter \(L\) is 49.1 MeV for KIDS0 and 66 MeV for KIDSA.
- The compressibility \(K_0\) is 240 MeV for KIDS0 and 230 MeV for KIDSA.
- The AMFP for the KIDS0 model decreases as \(n_B/n_0\) increases, and starting at around \(n_B/n_0 = 3.5\), the AMFP of the KIDS0 model is lower than that of the KIDSA and SLy4 models.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino and antineutrino opacity in neutron stars, notably following Horowitz (2003) and Hutauruk et al. (2006), who found that antineutrino differential cross-sections are considerably smaller than those for neutrinos when weak magnetism is included. The present study uses nuclear matter models constrained by the PREX II experiment (2021), LIGO/Virgo gravitational-wave observations (2017, 2018), and NICER measurements (2019), enabling more realistic predictions for antineutrino transport in neutron stars and supernova simulations. The results provide inputs for supernova simulations as noted by Mezzacappa (2000) and Rampp (2000).
