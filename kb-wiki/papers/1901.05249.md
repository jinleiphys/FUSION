# Thermal-FIST: A package for heavy-ion collisions and hadronic equation of state

> Thermal-FIST (Thermal, Fast and Interactive Statistical Toolkit) is a C++ package designed for a convenient general-purpose physics analysis within the family of hadron resonance gas (HRG) models. This mainly includes the statistical analysis of particle production in heavy-ion collisions and the phenomenology of hadronic equation of state. Notable features include fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges, effects of probabilistic decay, chemical non-equilibrium, and inclusion of van der Waals hadronic interactions. Calculations are possible within the grand canonical ensemble, the canonical ensemble, as well as in mixed-canonical ensembles combining the canonical treatment of certain conserved charges with the grand-canonical treatment of other conserved charges. The package contains a fast thermal event generator, which generates particle yields in accordance with the HRG chemistry, and particle momenta based on the Blast Wave model. A distinct feature of this package is the presence of the graphical user interface frontend -- QtThermalFIST -- which is designed for fast and convenient general-purpose HRG model applications.

## Key claim

Thermal-FIST provides a unified, user-friendly C++ framework for calculating hadron resonance gas (HRG) observables across multiple ensemble formulations and interaction models, but exact analytic calculations for fluctuations with probabilistic decays are limited to second-order moments in most cases (except the ideal HRG in the grand canonical ensemble, where fourth-order moments are available).

## Method

The package implements a class-based library that computes HRG observables for a specified particle list, statistical ensemble (grand canonical, canonical, or mixed-canonical), interaction model (ideal, excluded-volume, or van der Waals), and thermal parameters. Systems of transcendental equations arising from excluded-volume or van der Waals interactions are solved numerically using Broyden's method, while a Monte Carlo event generator produces particle yields and momenta based on HRG chemistry and the Blast Wave model. A graphical user interface frontend (QtThermalFIST) provides interactive access to all typical calculations including equation of state properties and thermal fits via chi-squared minimization.

## Key numbers

- Running time ranges from milliseconds (ideal HRG in GCE) to several minutes (thermal fit with energy-dependent widths, van der Waals interactions, or exact charge conservation)
- The thermal event generator takes "few milliseconds" to generate an event with about a thousand particles in the GCE
- At T = 155 MeV, V = 4000 fm^3, and zero chemical potentials, as much as 70% of all final pions come from resonance decays
- The package version described is Thermal-FIST 1.2, licensed under GPLv3

## Context

This package extends the capabilities of existing HRG implementations such as SHARE (Torrieri, 2004; Petran, 2013), THERMUS (Wheaton, 2004), and THERMINATOR (Kisiel, 2005; Chojnacki, 2011) by adding van der Waals interactions, chemical non-equilibrium effects simultaneously with exact charge conservation, and a graphical user interface. It enables systematic studies of fluctuation observables relevant to comparisons with lattice QCD data (Borsanyi, 2011; Bazavov, 2012; Bellwied, 2015) and heavy-ion collision experiments, particularly for investigating the role of repulsive baryon-baryon interactions in explaining deviations from the ideal HRG baseline (Vovchenko, 2016; Huovinen, 2017).
