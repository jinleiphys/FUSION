# The Twist Mode in Atomic Fermi Gases

> The quantum-kinetic energy of a finite number of trapped fermionic atoms provides a restoring force for shear motion due to a distortion of the momentum distribution. In analogy to the twist mode of nuclear physics it is proposed that counter-rotating the upper and lower hemisphere of a spherical atomic cloud yields a finite-frequency mode closely related to transverse zero-sound waves in bulk Fermi liquids.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a "twist mode", a collective oscillation where the upper and lower hemispheres of a trapped atomic Fermi gas counter-rotate, should exist as a finite-frequency excitation in dilute, degenerate Fermi gases, closely analogous to the twist mode previously studied in atomic nuclei and alkali metal clusters. The mode is driven by quantum-kinetic energy as the restoring force, and the paper's calculations show that the twist frequency depends only weakly on s-wave interactions (typically a 10% effect), with the main influence coming through the density-dependent mass parameter rather than the restoring force.

## Method

The authors use a Thomas-Fermi approach to describe a trapped two-component Fermi gas (equal numbers in two hyperfine states) with a contact s-wave interaction. They compute the total kinetic energy of the system under a twist deformation generated by the operator \( T = e^{-i\alpha z l_z} \), which induces a rotation proportional to \( z \) (clockwise for \( z>0 \), counterclockwise for \( z<0 \)). The restoring force \( C \) is obtained from the second derivative of the kinetic energy with respect to the twist angle \( \alpha \), and the mass parameter \( B \) is computed from the fluid-dynamical integral over the density times the square of the displacement field \( \vec{u} = (yz, -xz, 0) \). The twist frequency is then \( \hbar\Omega_T = \sqrt{C/B} \).

## Context

This work extends the concept of the twist mode, previously studied in spherical nuclei (Holzwarth and Eckart, 1980s) and in medium-to-heavy spherical alkali metal clusters (Nest, 1990s), to trapped atomic Fermi gases. The mode is closely related to transverse zero-sound waves in bulk Fermi liquids (Baym, 1960s) and to the elastic-body modes of finite Fermi systems (Lamb, 1930s; Bertsch, 1970s). The paper enables future experimental searches for the twist mode in trapped fermionic atoms, using techniques such as rotating laser fields to induce counter-rotation of the hemispheres, and opens questions about the influence of p-wave interactions, superfluidity, and the possibility of instabilities.
