# The proton radius (puzzle?) and its relatives

> We review determinations of the electric proton charge radius from a diverse set of low-energy observables. We explore under which conditions it can be related to Wilson coefficients of appropriate effective field theories. This discussion is generalized to other low-energy constants. This provides us with a unified framework to deal with a set of low-energy constants of the proton associated with its electromagnetic interactions. Unambiguous definitions of these objects are given, as well as their relation with expectation values of QCD operators. We show that the proton radius obtained from spectroscopy and lepton-proton scattering (when both the lepton and proton move with nonrelativistic velocities) is related to the same object of the underlying field theory with ${\cal O}(α)$ precision. The model dependence of these analyses is discussed. The prospects of constructing effective field theories valid for the kinematic configuration of present, or near-future, lepton-proton scattering experiments are discussed.

## Key claim

The proton charge radius measured in spectroscopy (Lamb shift of hydrogen and muonic hydrogen) and in lepton-proton scattering (when both lepton and proton move with nonrelativistic velocities) is related to the same object of the underlying field theory with O(alpha) precision, but this equivalence only holds in a specific kinematic regime where the momentum transfer Q^2 is much smaller than the pion mass squared and the lepton energy is nonrelativistic.

## Method

The authors construct effective field theories (NRQED for muon-proton and electron-proton systems, and HBET for relativistic kinematics) to describe the low-energy observables that determine the proton radius. They match the Wilson coefficients of these EFTs to the underlying QCD and QED dynamics, showing how the proton radius appears as a quasi-low-energy constant that is infrared divergent in pure QED but becomes well-defined when combined with appropriate Feynman diagrams of the effective theory. The relation between the proton radius measured in different experiments (Lamb shift of regular hydrogen, Lamb shift of muonic hydrogen, elastic electron-proton scattering, and elastic muon-proton scattering) is established by requiring that the same effective theory describes all these observables in the appropriate kinematic limits.

## Key numbers

- The proton anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_p = 1.792847356(23) (from Agashe 2014)
- The hadronic vacuum polarization derivative: Pi_h' = 9.3 x 10^(-3) GeV^(-2) (from Jegerlehner 1996)
- The Wilson coefficient c_F^(mu) = 1 + alpha/(2pi) + O(alpha^2) for the muon magnetic moment term
- The Wilson coefficient c_D^(mu)(nu) contains a logarithmic term (4alpha/(3pi)) ln(m_mu^2/nu^2) at O(alpha), with O(alpha^2) corrections including ln^2(m_mu/m_e) and ln(m_mu/m_e) terms
- The three-loop expression for c_F^(e) is given with coefficients up to alpha^3/pi^3 involving zeta(3), zeta(5), pi^2, and ln 2 terms

## Context

This review extends the earlier reviews of the proton radius puzzle by Pohl (2013) and Carlson (2015) by placing the problem in a unified effective field theory framework. The work clarifies the long-standing issue, previously discussed by Pachucki (1995) and Pineda (2004) in the context of muonic hydrogen Lamb shift, that the proton radius definition becomes ambiguous once electromagnetic corrections are incorporated. The framework enables future lattice QCD computations to be compared with experimental determinations, and provides a path to construct EFTs valid for the kinematic configurations of present and near-future lepton-proton scattering experiments such as MUSE.
