# Microscopic description of oblate-prolate shape mixing in proton-rich Se isotopes

> The oblate-prolate shape coexisting/mixing phenomena in proton-rich 68,70,72Se are investigated by means of the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method. The one-dimensional collective path and the collective Hamiltonian describing the large-amplitude shape vibration are derived in a fully microscopic way. The excitation spectra, B(E2) and spectroscopic quadrupole moments are calculated by requantizing the collective Hamiltonian and solving the collective Schroedinger equation. The basic properties of the coexisting two rotational bands in low-lying states of these nuclei are well reproduced. It is found that the oblate-prolate shape mixing becomes weak as the rotational angular momentum increases. We point out that the rotational energy plays a crucial role in causing the localization of the collective wave function in the (beta,gamma) deformation space.

## Key claim

The oblate-prolate shape mixing in proton-rich 68,70,72Se becomes weaker as rotational angular momentum increases, with the rotational energy playing a crucial role in localizing the collective wave function in the (beta,gamma) deformation space.

## Method

The authors applied the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method to derive a one-dimensional collective path and collective Hamiltonian describing large-amplitude shape vibrations in a fully microscopic way, using a pairing-plus-quadrupole force model with quadrupole pairing. They requantized the collective Hamiltonian and solved the collective Schroedinger equation to calculate excitation spectra, B(E2) values, and spectroscopic quadrupole moments for 68,70,72Se.

## Key numbers

- The oblate HB solution is lowest in energy for all three isotopes, with energy differences between oblate and prolate HB local minima of only 0.3 to 0.6 MeV
- For 68Se: oblate minimum at beta=0.296, gamma=60 degrees; prolate minimum at beta=0.260, gamma=0 degrees; potential energy difference V=0.41 MeV
- For 70Se: oblate minimum at beta=0.313, gamma=60 degrees; prolate minimum at beta=0.325, gamma=0 degrees; potential energy difference V=0.55 MeV
- For 72Se: oblate minimum at beta=0.268, gamma=60 degrees; prolate minimum at beta=0.381, gamma=0 degrees; potential energy difference V=0.32 MeV
- Rotational moment of inertia at prolate minimum increases from 4.60 MeV-1 in 68Se to 10.25 MeV-1 in 72Se
- Monopole-pairing strength G0 ranges from 0.320 MeV (68Se) to 0.302 MeV (72Se); quadrupole particle-hole interaction strength chi' ranges from 0.248 MeV (68Se) to 0.225 MeV (72Se)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous ASCC method applications to 68Se and 72Kr (Hinohara et al., 2004, 2005) by constructing a four-dimensional collective Hamiltonian coupling one-dimensional shape vibration with three-dimensional rotation, and applying it to the full isotopic chain 68,70,72Se. The results are compared with experimental data from lifetime measurements (Ljungvall et al., 2008) that indicated gradual oblate-to-prolate character change with increasing angular momentum, and with earlier mean-field calculations predicting oblate-prolate shape coexistence (Takami et al., 1998; Yamagami et al., 2001). The approach enables microscopic description of shape mixing beyond static mean-field approximations, with the collective mass including time-odd contributions from the moving mean-field that are ignored in the Inglis-Belyaev cranking formula.
