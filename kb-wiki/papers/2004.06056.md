# Evolution of strange and multi-strange hadron production with relative transverse multiplicity activity in underlying event

> In this work, the relative Underlying Event (UE) transverse multiplicity activity classifier ($R_{\rm {T}}$) is used to study the strange and multi-strange hadron production in proton-proton collisions. Our study with $R_{\rm {T}}$ would allow to disentangle these particles, which are originating from the soft and hard QCD processes. We have used the PYTHIA 8 Monte-Carlo (MC) with a different implementation of color reconnection and rope hadronization models to demonstrate the proton-proton collisions data at $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV. The relative production of strange and multi-strange hadrons are discussed extensively in low and high transverse activity regions. In this contribution, the relative strange hadron production is enhanced with increasing $R_{\rm {T}}$. This enhancement is significant for the strange baryons as compared to mesons. In addition, the particle ratios as a function of $R_{\rm {T}}$ confirm the baryon enhancement in new Color Reconnection (newCR), whereas the Rope model confirms the baryon enhancement only with strange quark content. Experimental confirmation of such results will provide more insight into the soft physics in the transverse region, which will be useful to investigate various tunes based on hadronization and color reconnection schemes.

# Evolution of strange and multi-strange hadron production with relative transverse multiplicity activity in underlying event

## Key claim

The relative production of strange and multi-strange hadrons in proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV, as modeled by PYTHIA 8 with different color reconnection and rope hadronization schemes, increases with the relative transverse multiplicity activity classifier (R_T), with the enhancement being significantly stronger for strange baryons than for strange mesons; however, these results are predictions from Monte Carlo models and require experimental confirmation to validate the underlying hadronization and color reconnection mechanisms.

## Method

The authors used the PYTHIA 8 Monte Carlo event generator (version 8.301) with three different physics models: the Monash 2013 tune as a reference, a new Color Reconnection (newCR) model incorporating junction fragmentation for baryon enhancement, and a Rope hadronization model describing interactions between overlapping Lund strings. Events were generated for proton-proton collisions at center-of-mass energy 13 TeV, and the relative transverse multiplicity activity classifier R_T was defined as the ratio of charged-particle multiplicity in the transverse region (60° < |Δφ| < 120° relative to the leading charged particle) to its event-averaged value, with the leading particle transverse momentum restricted to the plateau region (5 < p_T^lead < 40 GeV/c). Strange and multi-strange hadrons (K_S^0, K*^0, φ, Λ, Ξ, Ω) with p_T > 0.15 GeV/c and |η| < 0.8 were analyzed as functions of leading p_T and R_T.

## Key numbers

- The event-averaged multiplicity ⟨N_inc⟩ in the transverse region for Monash, newCR, and Rope models is 7.72, 7.97, and 7.65, respectively, with widths of 4.94, 4.94, and 4.85.
- The production rate of Ω (|S| = 3) in the plateau region for newCR and Rope models is approximately 10 and 3 times higher, respectively, compared to Monash.
- The Λ/K_S^0 ratio in the high R_T region is approximately 2.5 times higher for the Rope model and approximately 2 times higher for the newCR model compared to Monash.
- The average number of multiple parton interactions increases by 10-15% for newCR and Rope models relative to Monash at log10(R_T) beyond 0.
- The mean transverse momentum ⟨p_T⟩ of charged particles is underestimated by all three models by less than 10% for R_T < 0.5, with agreement for R_T > 3 when compared to ALICE data.
- Strange meson production increases by 10-20% in the Rope model relative to Monash in the p_T region 0.5-1.5 GeV/c.
- The Ω production rate is higher by up to a factor of 10 for the Rope model and 4 for the newCR model in the p_T region 0-3 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strange hadron production in minimum-bias pp collisions at LHC energies (ALICE Collaboration, 2017; Acharya et al., 2018) which showed enhancement as a function of multiplicity density but could not be reproduced by most QCD-based Monte Carlo models. The novel R_T classifier, which exhibits Koba-Nielsen-Olesen scaling (Antonio et al.), allows disentanglement of soft and hard QCD processes in the underlying event, building on earlier CMS measurements of neutral strange particle production in the underlying event at 7 TeV (CMS Collaboration, 2011). Experimental confirmation of these predictions would enable tuning of hadronization and color reconnection schemes in PYTHIA 8 to better describe particle production in proton-proton collisions.
