# Revisiting $ν_μ(\barν_μ)$ and $ν_e(\barν_e) $ Induced Quasielastic Scattering from Nuclei in Sub-GeV Energy Region

> We present the results of charged current quasielastic(CCQE) scattering cross sections from free as well as bound nucleons like in $^{12}C$, $^{16}O$, $^{40}Ar$ and $^{208}Pb$ nuclear targets in $E_ν(_{\barν})~\le~ $1 GeV energy region. The results are obtained using local Fermi gas model with and without RPA effect. The differences those may arise in the electron and muon production cross sections due to the different lepton mass, uncertainties in the axial dipole mass $M_A$ and pseudoscalar form factor, and due to the inclusion of second class currents have been highlighted for neutrino/antineutrino induced processes.

## Key claim

The paper calculates charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) cross sections for neutrinos and antineutrinos on nuclear targets in the sub-1 GeV region, finding that nuclear medium effects (Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, and RPA correlations) significantly suppress cross sections compared to free nucleon targets, with the suppression being larger for antineutrinos and for heavier nuclei. The differences between electron and muon production cross sections due to lepton mass, axial dipole mass uncertainty, pseudoscalar form factor, and second class currents are all shown to be small (typically less than 1% for axial mass variations) but non-negligible at low energies.

## Method

The authors use a local Fermi gas (LFG) model with and without random phase approximation (RPA) correlations to compute CCQE cross sections for neutrino and antineutrino scattering on free nucleons and on bound nucleons in 12C, 16O, 40Ar, and 208Pb targets. The hadronic current includes isovector vector form factors (from BBBA05 parameterization), an axial form factor with dipole parameterization (M_A = 1.026 GeV as default), a pseudoscalar form factor from the Goldberger-Treiman relation, and second class current form factors F_3^V and F_3^A with fixed proportionality constants. Cross sections are integrated over the nuclear volume using the Lindhard function for the imaginary part of the polarization propagator.

## Key numbers

- Default axial dipole mass M_A = 1.026 GeV (referred to as "WA" value); modified values of 0.9 GeV and 1.2 GeV are also used for sensitivity studies
- Axial form factor at Q^2=0: F_A(0) = -1.267
- Second class vector form factor: F_3^V(Q^2) = 4.4 × F_1^V(Q^2)
- Second class axial form factor: F_3^A(Q^2) = 0.15 × F_A(Q^2)
- Percentage difference in electron and muon production cross sections due to uncertainty in axial dipole mass is always less than 1% for the cases studied
- For neutrino-induced reactions at E_nu = 0.2 GeV, the sensitivity parameter Delta_F3V is 3% for free nucleon, approximately 4% for 12C and 40Ar, and approximately 2% for 208Pb
- For antineutrino-induced reactions at E_nu = 0.2 GeV, Delta_F3V is approximately 9% for free nucleon and approximately 12% for 12C, 40Ar, and 208Pb targets
- Sensitivity due to second class currents (both F_3^V and F_3^A) becomes almost negligible beyond E_nu = 0.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous CCQE calculations by Day et al. (2012) and others by systematically comparing electron and muon neutrino/antineutrino cross sections on multiple nuclear targets (12C, 16O, 40Ar, 208Pb) using a local Fermi gas model with RPA correlations. The results are relevant for current and future neutrino oscillation experiments operating in the sub-GeV energy region, particularly those studying CP violation and mass hierarchy, as they quantify the small but non-negligible differences between electron and muon production channels that must be accounted for in precision measurements.
