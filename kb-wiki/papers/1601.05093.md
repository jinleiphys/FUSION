# Bottomonium spectrum revisited

> We revisit the bottomonium spectrum motivated by the recently exciting experimental progress in the observation of new bottomonium states, both conventional and unconventional. Our framework is a nonrelativistic constituent quark model which has been applied to a wide range of hadronic observables from the light to the heavy quark sector and thus the model parameters are completely constrained. Beyond the spectrum, we provide a large number of electromagnetic, strong and hadronic decays in order to discuss the quark content of the bottomonium states and give more insights about the better way to determine their properties experimentally.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a nonrelativistic constituent quark model (CQM) with parameters fully constrained by light and heavy quark sectors provides a global description of the bottomonium spectrum, but the model overestimates the total decay width of the $\eta_{b}(1S)$ state by roughly a factor of two due to the regularization of the spin-spin contact hyperfine interaction.

## Method
The authors employed a nonrelativistic constituent quark model (CQM) based on Vijande et al. (2004), with parameters fine-tuned to reproduce the highest excited states in the light quark sector. The model includes one-gluon exchange (OGE) and a confining interaction, while Goldstone-boson exchanges are absent in the heavy quark sector due to explicit chiral symmetry breaking. They computed masses of bottomonium states up to spin $J=3$ and calculated electromagnetic, strong, and hadronic decay widths using the $^{3}P_{0}$ strong decay model and standard radiative transition formulas.

## Key numbers
- Hyperfine mass splitting for $\eta_{b}(1S)$: predicted $47\,{\rm MeV}$, compared to experimental $62.3\pm3.2\,{\rm MeV}$ (PDG 2014) and Belle result $57.9\pm2.3\,{\rm MeV}$ (Mizuk 2012).
- Hyperfine mass splitting for $\eta_{b}(2S)$: predicted $25\,{\rm MeV}$, in excellent agreement with experimental $24.3^{+4.0}_{-4.5}\,{\rm MeV}$ (Mizuk 2012) and lattice QCD range $(23.5-28.0)\,{\rm MeV}$ (Meinel 2010).
- Predicted mass of $\chi_{b1}(3P)$: $10513\,{\rm MeV}$, in perfect agreement with LHCb measurement $10515.7^{+2.2+1.5}_{-3.9-2.1}\,{\rm MeV}$ (Aaij 2014).
- Intra-multiplet splittings for $\chi_{bJ}(3P)$: $m_{\chi_{b2}(3P)}-m_{\chi_{b1}(3P)}=8\,{\rm MeV}$ and $m_{\chi_{b1}(3P)}-m_{\chi_{b0}(3P)}=13\,{\rm MeV}$.
- Total decay width of $\eta_{b}(1S)$: predicted $20.2\,{\rm MeV}$, compared to experimental $(10.8^{+4.0+4.5}_{-3.7-2.0})\,{\rm MeV}$ (Mizuk 2012).
- Total decay widths of $h_{b}(1P)$, $h_{b}(2P)$, $h_{b}(3P)$: predicted about $78.92\,{\rm keV}$, $91.14\,{\rm keV}$, and $95.76\,{\rm keV}$, respectively.
- Branching fraction for $h_{b}(1P)\to \eta_{b}(1S)\gamma$: predicted $55.32\%$, compared to experimental $49.2\pm5.7^{+5.6}_{-3.3}\%$ (Mizuk 2012).
- Branching fraction for $h_{b}(2P)\to \eta_{b}(2S)\gamma$: predicted $19.31\%$, compared to experimental $47.5\pm10.5^{+6.8}_{-7.7}\%$ (Mizuk 2012).

## Context
This work extends the constituent quark model approach of Vijande et al. (2004) and Segovia et al. (2008) to the bottomonium sector, motivated by recent experimental progress including observations of $\eta_{b}(1S)$, $\eta_{b}(2S)$, $h_{b}(1P)$, $h_{b}(2P)$, $\chi_{bJ}(3P)$, and the exotic $X(10610)^{\pm}$ and $X(10650)^{\pm}$ states. The results are compared with effective field theory predictions (Kniehl 2003) and lattice QCD calculations (Meinel 2010), and the model provides decay predictions that can guide future experimental searches for missing states such as $\eta_{b}(3S)$, $h_{b}(3P)$, and $\eta_{b2}(1D,2D)$.
