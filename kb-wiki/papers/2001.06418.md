# Chiral effective Lagrangian for heavy-light mesons from QCD

> We derive the chiral effective Lagrangian for heavy-light mesons in the heavy quark limit from QCD under proper approximations. The low energy constants in the effective Lagrangian are expressed in terms of the light quark self-energy. With typical forms of the running coupling constant of QCD and the quark self-energy obtained from Dyson-Schwinger equations as well as lattice QCD, we estimate the low energy constants in the model and the strong decay widths. A comparison with data and some discussions of the numerical results are presented.

## Key claim

The authors derive the chiral effective Lagrangian for heavy-light mesons directly from QCD, expressing the low-energy constants (LECs) in terms of the light-quark self-energy, but the derivation relies on the chiral limit, the heavy-quark limit, and the large-\(N_c\) limit, and omits non-“Tr ln” terms, so the numerical results are leading-order estimates that suffer from corrections of order \(\mathcal{O}(m_{u/d})\), \(\mathcal{O}(1/m_Q)\), and \(\mathcal{O}(1/N_c)\).

## Method

The authors start from the QCD generating functional with external sources, integrate in the chiral field and heavy-light meson fields, and then integrate out the gluon and quark fields to obtain an effective action in the form of a functional trace of a logarithm. By expanding this action to second and third order in the heavy-light meson fields and the chiral-rotated external source, they derive integral expressions for the LECs (masses \(m_H, m_G\) and coupling constants \(g_H, g_G, g_{HG}\)) in terms of the light-quark self-energy \(\Sigma(-p^2)\). They then evaluate these integrals numerically using the quark self-energy obtained from two models of the running coupling constant in Dyson-Schwinger equations (Model A and Model B) and from lattice QCD fits.

## Key numbers

*   Spin-averaged masses from PDG: \(m_H \simeq 1970\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_G \simeq 2400\ \text{MeV}\), with mass splitting \(\Delta m = 430\ \text{MeV}\).
*   Using DSE Model A with parameter \(a=0.79\): \(m_H = 0.555\ \text{GeV}\), \(m_G = 0.979\ \text{GeV}\), \(\Delta m = 0.424\ \text{GeV}\); after adding charm quark mass \(m_c \approx 1.27\ \text{GeV}\), \(\tilde{m}_H \approx 1.825\ \text{GeV}\) and \(\tilde{m}_G \approx 2.249\ \text{GeV}\).
*   Using DSE Model B with parameter \(a_0=0.58\): \(m_H = 0.663\ \text{GeV}\), \(m_G = 1.103\ \text{GeV}\), \(\Delta m = 0.440\ \text{GeV}\); after adding \(m_c \approx 1.27\ \text{GeV}\), \(\tilde{m}_H \approx 1.933\ \text{GeV}\) and \(\tilde{m}_G \approx 2.373\ \text{GeV}\).
*   From DSE Model B (\(a_0=0.58\)): coupling \(g_H = 0.408\), leading to decay width \(\Gamma(D^{\ast +}\to D\pi) = 48\ \text{KeV}\) (experimental value \(83.4 \pm 1.8\ \text{KeV}\)).
*   From DSE Model B (\(a_0=0.58\)): coupling \(g_{HG} = 0.432\), leading to decay width \(\Gamma(D_0^{\ast 0}\to D\pi) = 183\ \text{MeV}\) (experimental value \(274 \pm 40\ \text{MeV}\)).
*   Using lattice QCD fits with \(Z(-p^2)=1\): \(m_H = 0.537\ \text{GeV}\), \(m_G = 1.125\ \text{GeV}\), \(\Delta m = 0.588\ \text{GeV}\); with fitted \(Z(-p^2)\): \(m_H = 0.583\ \text{GeV}\), \(m_G = 0.883\ \text{GeV}\), \(\Delta m = 0.299\ \text{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Wang (1999), who derived a relation between QCD and chiral perturbation theory for pions, to the heavy-light meson sector by incorporating both heavy quark symmetry and chiral symmetry. It provides a direct QCD-based calculation of the LECs in the chiral effective theory for the \(H\) doublet (\(J^P = (0^-, 1^-)\)) and its chiral partner \(G\) doublet (\(J^P = (0^+, 1^+)\)), which were previously free parameters in models such as the chiral doublet model of Nowak (1992) and Bardeen (1993). The results enable the calculation of the quark-condensate dependence of these LECs, which can be used to study the intrinsic density and temperature dependence of heavy-light meson properties in medium, as discussed by Ma (2019).
