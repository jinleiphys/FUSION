# Observation of a first-order pairing phase transition in atomic nuclei

> Experimental nuclear level densities at excitation energies below the neutron threshold follow closely a constant-temperature shape. This dependence is unexpected and poorly understood. In this work, a fundamental explanation of the observed constant-temperature behavior in atomic nuclei is presented for the first time. It is shown that the experimental data portray a first-order phase transition from a superfluid to an ideal gas of non-interacting quasiparticles. Even-even, odd-$A$, and odd-odd level densities show in detail the behavior of gap- and gapless superconductors also observed in solid-state physics. These results and analysis should find a direct application to mesoscopic systems such as superconducting clusters.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the experimentally observed constant-temperature shape of nuclear level densities below the neutron threshold is the microcanonical signature of a first-order phase transition from a superfluid to an ideal gas of non-interacting quasiparticles, rather than the second-order transition predicted by standard BCS theory. The authors hedge that this interpretation follows from a shift in perspective from the canonical to the microcanonical ensemble, and that the BCS treatment, despite predicting a second-order transition, comes close to predicting the observed first-order behavior when fluctuations are accounted for.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental nuclear level densities for rare-earth and actinide nuclei measured at the Oslo Cyclotron Laboratory, fitting them with the constant-temperature model ρ(E) ∝ exp(E/T) for excitation energies above approximately 2 MeV. They compared the extracted constant temperatures T_CT to BCS critical temperatures T_cr derived from the pair-gap parameter Δ_0 using the relation T_cr = 2Δ_0/3.53, and compared the extracted gap parameters Δ_CT to the global Bohr-Mottelson formula Δ_BM ≈ 12A^{-1/2}. They also examined the horizontal and vertical shifts between level densities of adjacent even-even and odd-A nuclei to extract the energy cost and entropy per quasiparticle.

## Key numbers

- For 20 nuclei from A=148 to A=239, extracted constant temperatures T_CT range from 0.34(1) MeV (²³²Th) to 0.60(1) MeV (¹⁶⁰Dy and ¹⁶²Dy), with uncertainties of 0.01-0.02 MeV.
- Extracted pair-gap parameters Δ_CT range from 0.60(2) MeV (²³²Th) to 1.05(1) MeV (¹⁶⁰Dy), compared to Δ_BM values from 0.78 MeV (²³⁷U) to 0.99 MeV (¹⁴⁸Sm).
- The experimental entropy excess ΔS = ln[ρ_eo(E)/ρ_ee(E)] between odd-A and even-even nuclei ranges from 1.7(2) k_B (²³²Pa) to 2.5(5) k_B (²³⁹U), with an average of 2.0(1) k_B.
- The predicted entropy per quasiparticle from BCS theory is ΔS/ΔQ = 3.53/2 = 1.77 k_B.
- The number of available states per quasiparticle from the experimental average ΔS is exp(2.0) ≈ 8(1), compared to the theoretical value exp(1.77) ≈ 6.

## Context

This work extends the constant-temperature level density formula proposed by Ericson (1959) and Gilbert and Cameron (1965), who did not provide a fundamental explanation for the relation. It contradicts the expected Fermi-gas behavior first outlined by Bethe (1936), which predicts a square-root dependence of level density on excitation energy. The analysis enables prediction of low-energy nuclear level densities throughout the nuclear chart for regions away from magic proton/neutron numbers using only the even-odd mass difference, and should find direct application to other mesoscopic systems such as superconducting clusters differing by just one electron.
