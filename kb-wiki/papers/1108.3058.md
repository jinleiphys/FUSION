# Comment on "High-Precision Determination of the Electric and Magnetic Form Factors of the Proton"

> In a recent Letter, Bernauer, et al. present fits to the proton electromagnetic form factors, GEp(Q^2) and GMp(Q^2), along with extracted proton charge and magnetization radii based on large set of new, high statistical precision (<0.2%) cross section measurements. The Coulomb corrections they apply differ dramatically from more modern and complete calculations, implying significant error in their final results.

## Key claim

The Coulomb corrections applied by Bernauer et al. (2010) in their extraction of proton form factors and radii use the \(Q^2 \to 0\) limit of the full calculation, which differs dramatically from more modern and complete two-photon exchange calculations, implying significant error in their final results.

## Method

Arrington compares the Coulomb correction formula used by Bernauer et al. (2010), which is based on McKinley and Feshbach (1948), to the full \(Q^2\)-dependent Coulomb correction from Arrington (2004) and to two-photon exchange calculations from Blunden et al. (2005) and Borisyuk (2006). He evaluates the difference between these corrections at the mean \(Q^2\) of the experiment and estimates the impact on the extracted electric and magnetic form factors via a direct Rosenbluth separation, assuming dipole form factors and cross section data covering \(0.3 < \varepsilon < 0.9\).

## Key numbers

- The full Coulomb correction is outside the 50% uncertainty assumed by Bernauer et al. (2010) for all data above \(Q^2 = 0.06\) GeV\(^2\).
- By \(Q^2 = 0.1\) GeV\(^2\), the small-\(\varepsilon\) correction has changed by 1%, which will modify the magnetic form factor and its \(Q^2\) dependence.
- The full \(\delta_{CC}\) is 2–3% lower for \(Q^2 > 0.3\) GeV\(^2\) and low \(\varepsilon\), a change several times the total uncertainties on the individual cross sections (which are <0.2% statistical).
- The estimated impact of the full correction shifts the magnetic form factor results by more than 2–3 standard deviations, bringing the ratio \(\mu_p G_E / G_M\) into better agreement with recent high-precision polarization measurements (Zhan et al., 2011).

## Context

This comment extends the ongoing discussion of two-photon exchange corrections in proton form factor extractions, which have been shown to be important for both the form factors (Arrington, 2007) and the charge radius (Blunden et al., 2005). The work contradicts the analysis of Bernauer et al. (2010) by showing that their use of the \(Q^2 \to 0\) limit for Coulomb corrections introduces systematic errors larger than their assumed uncertainties, and it enables improved extraction of the proton charge and magnetization radii by applying the full \(Q^2\)-dependent corrections.
