# The $m$'bottom-up parton system with two momentum scales

> One possible evolutionary scenario of the dense gluon system produced in an ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision is the bottom-up thermalization scenario, which describes the dynamics of the system shortly after the collision via the decay of originally produced hard gluons to soft ones through QCD branching processes. The soft gluons form a thermal bath that subsequently reaches thermalization and/or equilibration. There is a scaling solution to the bottom-up problem that interpolates between its early stage, which has a highly anisotropic gluon distribution, and its final stage of equilibration which occurs later. Such a solution depends on a single parameter, the so called momentum asymmetry parameter $δ$. With this scaling solution, the bottom-up scenario gets modified and the evolving parton system, referred to as the $m$'bottom-up parton system throughout this paper, is described by this modification. The time evolution of the system in the original bottom-up ansatz is driven by the saturation scale, $Q_{s}$. However, for the $m$'bottom-up we generalize the ansatz of the evolution by introducing two additional momentum scales, which give a thermalization time and temperature of the soft gluon bath somewhat different from those obtained when the $m$'bottom-up matches onto the final stage of the original bottom-up scenario.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the "m'bottom-up" modification of the original bottom-up thermalization scenario can be described by two momentum scales, Ω and Ω_s, analogous to the scales Λ and Λ_s used in the Glasma picture, and that these scales yield a thermalization time and temperature that depend on the momentum asymmetry parameter δ, matching the original bottom-up results only when δ > 1/3.

## Method

The authors start from the scaling solution of the Boltzmann equation for the m'bottom-up scenario, which depends on a single parameter δ (the momentum asymmetry parameter). They introduce two dynamical momentum scales, Ω (ultraviolet) and Ω_s (infrared), and derive their time dependence by equating the soft gluon number density and Debye mass from the scaling solution to expressions written in terms of Ω and Ω_s. The thermalization time is then obtained from the condition that at thermalization Ω_s ~ α_s Ω, and the thermalization temperature is derived by matching the scaling solution to the final stage of the original bottom-up scenario.

## Key numbers

- The thermalization time in the m'bottom-up scenario is given by Q_s τ_therm ~ α_s^{-15/(5 + 3δ)}, where δ is the momentum asymmetry parameter in the range 0 < δ < 10/21.
- For δ > 1/3, the scaling solution matches onto the final stage of the original bottom-up at Q_s τ ~ α_s^{-13/5}, giving a thermalization temperature T_therm ~ Q_s α_s^{2/5} that is independent of δ.
- The two momentum scales as functions of proper time are Ω ~ Q_s (1/Q_s τ)^{(5 - 6δ)/15} and Ω_s ~ Q_s (1/Q_s τ)^{(10 - 3δ)/15}.
- The relation between the m'bottom-up asymmetry parameter δ and the Glasma asymmetry parameter δ' is δ = (10 - 15δ')/21, valid for 0 < δ' < 1/3 corresponding to 5/21 < δ < 10/21.

## Context

This work extends the original bottom-up thermalization scenario of Baier (2001) by incorporating a scaling solution that interpolates between initial plasma instabilities (Arnold 2003, Mrowczynski 2006, 2007) and final equilibration, as proposed by Mueller (2006). The two-scale description is analogous to the Glasma dynamical scales Λ and Λ_s introduced by Blaizot (2011), and the authors show that the m'bottom-up scales Ω and Ω_s are parametrically equivalent to the Glasma scales when the asymmetry parameters are related by δ = (10 - 15δ')/21. The results are intended to enable calculations of direct photon and di-electron yields, as well as elliptic flow of direct photons, in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV measured by the PHENIX collaboration (Adare 2008, 2009, 2011).
