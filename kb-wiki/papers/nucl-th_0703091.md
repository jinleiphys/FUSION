# The Friedrichs-Model with fermion-boson couplings II

> In this work we present a formal solution of the extended version of the Friedrichs Model. The Hamiltonian consists of discrete and continuum bosonic states, which are coupled to fermions. The simultaneous treatment of the couplings of the fermions with the discrete and continuous sectors of the bosonic degrees of freedom leads to a system of coupled equations, whose solutions are found by applying standard methods of representation of bound and resonant states.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formal, general solution of the extended Friedrichs model with fermion-boson couplings, going beyond the schematic solution of a prior paper (Civitarese et al., 2006) by using a T-matrix formalism to solve the system of coupled equations, provided that a certain determinant condition (Delta not equal to zero) holds for all energies.

## Method

The authors start from the extended Friedrichs model Hamiltonian, which includes discrete and continuum bosonic states coupled to fermions via three interaction terms (H_II, H_III, H_IV). They write the total wavefunction as a sum over fermion states, discrete boson-fermion states, and continuum boson-fermion states, then derive a system of three coupled integral equations for the amplitudes. By choosing separable form factors (f_{lk}(omega) = alpha_l alpha_k^* rho(omega), h_{lk} = beta_l beta_k^*, g_k(omega) = 0), they reduce the system to a matrix equation of T-matrix form, which they invert using a series expansion and algebraic manipulation to obtain explicit expressions for the fermion amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The condition for existence of the inverse matrix T^{-1} is given by 1 - B(E) tau(beta, beta^*, E) not equal to 0 (Equation 38), where tau is defined as sum_k (alpha_k beta_k)/(c_k - E).
* Nontrivial solutions for the coefficient a_{ks} exist if and only if Delta not equal to 0 (Equation 44), where Delta = 1 - A(E) tau(alpha, alpha^*, E) - B(E) tau(beta, beta^*, E) + A(E)B(E)[tau(alpha, alpha^*, E) tau(beta, beta^*, E) - tau(alpha, beta^*, E) tau(beta, alpha^*, E)].
* The explicit inverse matrix T^{-1}_{ks} is given by Equation 47, which contains terms proportional to A(E)[1 - B(E) tau(beta, beta^*, E)], B(E)[1 - A(E) tau(alpha, alpha^*, E)], and A(E)B(E) tau(alpha, beta^*, E) divided by (c_k - E)(c_s - E) Delta.

## Context

This work extends the standard Friedrichs model, originally introduced by Friedrichs (1948) and previously solved for boson-boson couplings only, by including fermion-boson couplings as proposed in a prior paper by the same authors (Civitarese et al., 2006, referred to as I). The formal solution generalizes the schematic model of that earlier work, where only a single discrete fermion state was considered, to a more general fermionic subspace. The T-matrix formalism borrowed from reaction theory (Newton, 1966) allows identification of resonant behavior in both fermion and boson sectors, and the authors state that further applications to physical situations are in progress.
