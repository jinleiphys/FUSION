# Kilonova Emissions from Neutron Star Merger Remnants: Implications for Nuclear Equation of State

> Multi-messenger observation of binary neutron-star mergers can provide valuable information on the nuclear equation of state (EoS). Here, we investigate to which extent electromagnetic observations of the associated kilonovae allow us to place constraints on the EoS. For this, we use state-of-the-art three-dimensional general-relativistic magneto-hydrodynamics simulations and detailed nucleosynthesis modeling to connect properties of observed light curves to properties of the accretion disk, and hence, the EoS. Using our general approach, we use multi-messenger observations of GW170817/AT2017gfo to study the impact of various sources of uncertainty on inferences of the EoS. We constrain the radius of a $\rm{1.4 M_\odot}$ neutron star to lie within $\rm{10.19\leq R_{1.4}\leq 13.0}$~km and the maximum mass to be $\rm{M_{TOV}\leq 3.06 M_\odot}$.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that kilonova observations can constrain the nuclear equation of state (EoS), but the resulting constraints have larger uncertainties than typical Bayesian analyses because the authors propagate multiple sources of systematic uncertainty from disk mass predictions, ejecta fractions, and nuclear physics through the inference chain, rather than treating them as fixed.

## Method
The authors compiled 112 numerical relativity simulation data points from 11 sources to derive a new fitting formula for remnant disk mass as a function of the lighter neutron star's compactness. They used 3D general-relativistic magnetohydrodynamics simulations to estimate that 30-40% of the disk becomes unbound as ejecta, then connected ejecta mass to kilonova light-curve observables using nucleosynthesis models from Zhu (2021). Finally, they applied their disk mass formula to 2000 chiral effective field theory informed EoSs for binaries consistent with GW170817 to identify which EoSs produce disk masses matching the inferred range from AT2017gfo observations.

## Key numbers
- Disk mass fitting formula RMS error: 0.042, compared to 0.065 (Radice 2018), 0.056 (Dietrich 2020), and 0.048 (Kruger 2020)
- Inferred disk ejecta mass from AT2017gfo: (2.926-3.645) x 10^-2 solar masses, based on peak luminosity of 1.23 x 10^41 erg/s at 4.8 days post-merger
- Implied disk mass range: (7.32-12.2) x 10^-2 solar masses
- Constrained radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 10.30 to 13.0 km
- Constrained maximum TOV mass: less than or equal to 3.06 solar masses
- EoS sample range before constraints: 8.7 to 15.1 km for R_1.4, 1.89 to 3.99 solar masses for M_TOV

## Context
This work extends the line of research connecting neutron star merger observations to the nuclear EoS, building on disk mass fits from Radice (2018), Coughlin (2019), Dietrich (2020), and Kruger (2020) by using a larger simulation dataset. It enables future work to incorporate more robust statistical methods and additional simulation data, particularly from asymmetric binaries, into frameworks like that of Pang (2023). The results are consistent with but less precise than Bayesian analyses such as Koehn (2025) (R_1.4 = 12.19 +0.71 -0.63 km) and Breschi (2024) (R_1.4 = 12.30 +0.81 -0.56 km), highlighting the importance of propagating systematic uncertainties rather than relying on point estimates.
