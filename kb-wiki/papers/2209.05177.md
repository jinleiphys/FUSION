# Nucleonic metamodelling in light of multimessenger, PREX-II and CREX data

> The need of reconciling our understanding of the behavior of hadronic matter across a wide range of densities, especially at the time when data from multimessenger observations and novel experimental facilities are flooding in, has provided new challenges to the nuclear models. Particularly, the density dependence of the isovector channel of the nuclear energy functionals seems hard to pin down if experiments like PREX-II (or PREX) and CREX are required to be taken on the same footing. We put to test this anomaly in a semi-agnostic modelling technique, by performing a full Bayesian analysis of static properties of neutron stars, together with global properties of nuclei as binding energy, charge radii and neutron skin calculated at the semi-classical level. Our results show that the interplay between bulk and surface properties, and the importance of high order empirical parameters that effectively decouple the subsaturation and the supersaturation density regime, might partially explain the tension between the different measurements and observations. If the surface behaviors, however, are decoupled from the bulk properties, we found a rather harmonious situation among experimental and observational data.

## Key claim

The tension between PREX-II and CREX measurements of neutron skin in lead and calcium can be partially resolved if high-order empirical parameters of the nuclear equation of state are allowed to decouple the subsaturation and supersaturation density regimes, and if surface properties are treated independently from bulk properties.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian analysis using a nuclear metamodel that expands the energy per particle of homogeneous nuclear matter in terms of empirical parameters up to fourth order around saturation density, with separate parameters below and above saturation. They combined this with an analytical extended Thomas-Fermi (aETF) model to compute binding energies, charge radii, and neutron skins for finite nuclei, and a compressible liquid drop model for neutron star crust properties. The posterior distributions were obtained by applying sequential filters: reproduction of the AME2016 mass table and charge radii for nine spherical nuclei, chiral effective field theory constraints at low densities, astrophysical constraints from the maximum neutron star mass and the GW170817 tidal deformability, and finally the PREX-II and CREX neutron skin measurements.

## Key numbers

* The PREX-II neutron skin for 208Pb is constrained with an uncertainty of 0.07 fm in the likelihood function.
* The GW170817 chirp mass is taken as 1.186 +/- 0.001 solar masses.
* The maximum neutron star mass constraint uses a mean of 2.01 solar masses with a variance of 0.04 solar masses.
* The charge radius likelihood uses sigma_ch = 0.02 fm for 7 nuclei and 0.1 fm for 2 nuclei.
* The surface stiffness Q is sampled between 5 and 70 in the uncorrelated case, with a Pearson correlation coefficient of -0.77 between Q and the symmetry slope L_sym across mean field models.

## Context

This work extends the metamodeling approach of Margueron (2018) and the analytical ETF method of Aymard (2016) to simultaneously address neutron star observables and finite nuclear properties. It directly confronts the anomaly identified by Reinhard (2021, 2022) and Yuksel (2022) that density functional theory struggles to simultaneously reproduce PREX-II and CREX results. The study enables a systematic assessment of whether the tension between these parity-violating electron scattering experiments and other constraints from heavy ion collisions and astrophysical observations can be resolved within a semi-agnostic framework, rather than requiring beyond-mean-field physics as suggested by Typel (2010, 2014) and Tanaka (2021).
