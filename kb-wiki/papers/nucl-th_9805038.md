# Self consistent random phase approximation within the O(5) model and Fermi transitions

> Self Consistent Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (SCQRPA) is considered in application to the Fermi transitions within the O(5) model. It is demonstrated that SCQRPA improves on renormalized QRPA (RQRPA), a method that has recently become rather popular in this context. The analytical form of the SCQRPA vacuum is used to evaluate all the matrix elements. The SCQRPA results show a general trend similar to the exact solutions. The necessity to change the single particle basis beyond the transition point, and to include the proton-proton and neutron-neutron channels in the QRPA operator, in addition to the proton-neutron one, is pointed out.

## Key claim

The Self Consistent Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (SCQRPA) improves on the Renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) for Fermi transitions within the O(5) model, but only when the charge-exchange mode is decoupled from the conserving modes and the generalized BCS equations are solved self-consistently with the RPA equations; the SCQRPA results show a general trend similar to the exact solutions, though the necessity to change the single particle basis beyond the transition point and to include proton-proton and neutron-neutron channels in the QRPA operator is pointed out.

## Method

The authors constructed the SCQRPA vacuum explicitly for the O(5) model Hamiltonian, which describes protons and neutrons occupying a single level with pairing and particle-particle interactions. They derived the SCQRPA equations of motion for the charge-exchange (Fermi transition) mode, decoupled from the like-particle modes, and solved them self-consistently with generalized BCS equations that couple the quasiparticle mean field to the charge-exchange excitation. The RQRPA was formulated as the limit of SCQRPA where the ground state two-body density is approximated by products of one-body densities.

## Key numbers

- For case (A) with Omega=5, N=8, Z=2, G_p=G_n=0.4 MeV, F_pn=0.4 MeV: the QRPA collapses at s_crit=2.10, while RQRPA and SCQRPA show no collapse and omega approaches zero asymptotically as s goes to infinity.
- For case (B) with Omega=10, N=14, Z=6, G_p=G_n=0.3 MeV, F_pn=0.3 MeV: the QRPA collapses at s_crit=1.41.
- For case (C) with Omega=25, N=30, Z=20, G_p=G_n=0.2 MeV, F_pn=0.2 MeV: the QRPA collapses at s_crit=1.10, and this case mimics a nucleus with A=50 using the Kisslinger and Sorensen (1963) estimate for the pairing coupling constant (G approximately 25/A MeV).
- The sum rule S- minus S+ = N-Z is fulfilled in both RQRPA and SCQRPA when the correct particle number condition is used and only J=0 ground state pn correlations are included.

## Context

This work extends the study of Delion et al. (1997) by avoiding the approximations made there, which had led to poorly reproduced excitation energies and matrix elements for beta-beta decay. It addresses the violation of charge-exchange sum rules in RQRPA, previously identified by Krmpotic et al. (1996, 1997) and Toivanen et al. (1997), by incorporating the scattering (anomalous) terms in the excitation operators as suggested by Dukelsky et al. (1996). The results enable a direct comparison between exact and approximate results for Fermi transitions and double-beta decay matrix elements within the O(5) model, and point toward the need for including proton-proton and neutron-neutron channels in realistic applications.
