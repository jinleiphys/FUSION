# A first prediction of the electromagnetic rare decays $η^\prime\toπ^0γγ$ and $η^\prime\toηγγ$

> The branching ratio of the electromagnetic rare decays $η\toπ^0γγ$ and $η^\prime\to (π^0,η)γγ$ are analysed in terms of scalar and vector meson exchange contributions using the frameworks of the Linear Sigma Model and Vector Meson Dominance, respectively. The measured $η\toπ^0γγ$ process serves as a test of our approach while the non yet measured $η^\prime\to (π^0,η)γγ$ reactions are predicted for the first time. Our prediction for the $η\toπ^0γγ$ decay agrees with recent experimental reported values, thus supporting the validity of our framework. Therefore, our predictions for the $η^\prime\toπ^0γγ$ and $η^\prime\toηγγ$ decays should be taken as a first indication of the possible values of the associated branching ratios. We hope these predictions to be interesting and useful for experiments such as KLOE-2, Crystal Ball, WASA, and BES-III where these processes are expected to be measured in the next future.

# A first prediction of the electromagnetic rare decays η′→π0γγ and η′→ηγγ

## Key claim
The branching ratios for the unmeasured decays η′→π0γγ and η′→ηγγ are predicted for the first time, but the author states these should be taken only as a first indication of possible values, not as precise calculations, because the analysis includes only the two main contributions (vector meson exchange and scalar meson effects via the Linear Sigma Model) rather than a full detailed calculation.

## Method
The analysis uses Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) to compute vector meson exchange contributions and the Linear Sigma Model (LσM) to incorporate scalar meson resonance effects, with the chiral-loop predictions from Chiral Perturbation Theory as a baseline. The measured η→π0γγ decay is used as a test case to validate the approach before applying the same framework to predict the η′→π0γγ and η′→ηγγ decays. The total decay widths are obtained from the coherent sum of the LσM and VMD contributions.

## Key numbers
- For η→π0γγ: predicted branching ratio 2.1 × 10⁻⁴, compared to the PDG 2010 fit of (2.7 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴
- For η′→π0γγ: predicted branching ratio 65 × 10⁻⁴, compared to the existing experimental upper bound of < 8 × 10⁻⁴ at 90% CL from GAMS-2000
- For η′→ηγγ: predicted branching ratio 2.6 × 10⁻⁴, with no experimental value available
- The VMD contribution to η′→π0γγ is dominated by the ω meson at 80.2% of the total VMD signal, with the ρ contributing 4.6%
- For η′→ηγγ, the ρ, ω, and φ mesons contribute 59.9%, 15.8%, and 1.6% respectively to the VMD signal
- The η-η′ mixing angle used is φP = (40.4 ± 0.6)°, with fπ = 92.2 MeV and |g| = 4.2 from ρ→ππ decay

## Context
This work extends the theoretical analysis of η→π0γγ, previously studied in Chiral Perturbation Theory by Ametller (1991), constituent quark models, and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, to the unmeasured η′→π0γγ and η′→ηγγ decays for the first time. The predictions are intended to guide upcoming measurements at experiments including KLOE-2, Crystal Ball, WASA, and BES-III, where these processes are expected to be measured in the near future. The predicted branching ratio for η′→π0γγ is an order of magnitude larger than the old experimental upper bound from GAMS-2000, suggesting a new measurement would be particularly valuable.
