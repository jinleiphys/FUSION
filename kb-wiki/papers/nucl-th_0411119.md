# Theta^+ baryon production from gamma N and NN scattering

> We investigate Theta^+ production via gamma N and NN reactions in order to obtain information on the structure of Theta^+, especially its parity. We observe that the positive parity. Theta^+production provides about ten times total cross sections than those of the negative parity one in both photon and nucleon induced reactions due to P-wave enhancement of the KNTheta vertex. We also consider the model independent method in the nucleon induced reaction to determine the parity of Theta^+ and show clearly distinguishable signals for the two parities.

## Key claim
The authors find that positive-parity Theta^+ production provides total cross sections about ten times larger than those of negative-parity Theta^+ in both photon-induced and nucleon-induced reactions, but they note that this factor arises from P-wave enhancement of the KNTheta vertex and that determining parity from gamma N reactions alone remains difficult based on angular distributions.

## Method
The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach at tree level for gamma N and NN scattering, using pseudoscalar (PS) and pseudovector (PV) coupling schemes for the KNTheta vertex. For gamma N reactions, they included K and K* exchange with gauge-invariant form factors, fixing cutoff parameters by fitting to the known gamma p -> K+ Lambda process. For NN reactions, they used K and K* exchange in the t-channel with form factors from the Nijmegen and Julich-Bonn potentials, and also analyzed polarized pp scattering near threshold to determine parity via spin observables.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section for gamma n -> K- Theta^+ (positive parity): ~44 nb in the photon energy range 1.73 GeV < E_gamma < 2.6 GeV
- Total cross section for gamma p -> anti-K^0 Theta^+ (positive parity): ~13 nb in the same photon energy range
- Total cross section for np -> Lambda Theta^+_+ (positive parity, Nijmegen potential): ~40 microbarns in the CM energy range from threshold (2656 MeV) to 3.5 GeV
- Total cross section for np -> Sigma^0 Theta^+_+ (positive parity, Nijmegen potential): ~2.0 microbarns in the same CM energy range
- Total cross section for np -> Lambda Theta^+_- (negative parity, Nijmegen potential): ~5.0 microbarns in the same CM energy range
- Total cross section for np -> Sigma^0 Theta^+_- (negative parity, Nijmegen potential): ~0.3 microbarns in the same CM energy range
- Total cross section for np -> Lambda Theta^+_+ (positive parity, Julich-Bonn potential): ~100 microbarns in the same CM energy range
- Total cross section for np -> Sigma^0 Theta^+_+ (positive parity, Julich-Bonn potential): ~20 microbarns in the same CM energy range
- Total cross section for np -> Lambda Theta^+_- (negative parity, Julich-Bonn potential): ~6.0 microbarns in the same CM energy range
- Total cross section for np -> Sigma^0 Theta^+_- (negative parity, Julich-Bonn potential): ~2.0 microbarns in the same CM energy range
- Absolute total cross section for polarized pp scattering near threshold: order 1 microbarn for positive parity Theta^+, order 0.1 microbarn for negative parity Theta^+

## Context
This work extends the theoretical study of Theta^+ pentaquark production following the LEPS observation at SPring-8 (Nakano, 2003) and the prediction by Diakonov (1997). The authors build on earlier effective Lagrangian calculations by Liu (2003), Oh (2003), and Yu (2003) for photoproduction, and by Polyakov (1999) for hadron-induced reactions. The polarized pp method follows the proposal by Hanhart (2003) and Thomas (2003) for unambiguous parity determination, and the results enable experimental tests at facilities like COSY-TOF, which the authors note was planned for 2005.
