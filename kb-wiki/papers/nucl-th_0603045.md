# How low-energy weak reactions can constrain three-nucleon forces and the neutron-neutron scattering length

> We show that chiral symmetry and gauge invariance enforce relations between the short-distance physics that occurs in a number of electroweak and pionic reactions on light nuclei. Within chiral perturbation theory this is manifested via the appearance of the same axial isovector two-body contact term in pi- d -> n n gamma, p-wave pion production in NN collisions, tritium beta decay, pp fusion, nu d scattering, and the hep reaction. Using a Gamow-Teller matrix element obtained from calculations of pp fusion as input we compute the neutron spectrum obtained in pi- d -> n n gamma. With the short-distance physics in this process controlled from p p -> d e+ nu_e the theoretical uncertainty in the nn scattering length extracted from pi- d -> n n gamma is reduced by a factor larger than three, to <~0.05 fm.

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry and gauge invariance enforce that the same axial isovector two-body contact term appears in multiple electroweak and pionic reactions on light nuclei; using the Gamow-Teller matrix element from pp fusion as input reduces the theoretical uncertainty in the neutron-neutron scattering length extracted from π⁻d → nnγ by more than a factor of three, to ≤0.05 fm when only the final-state interaction peak is fitted.

## Method

The authors employ chiral perturbation theory (χPT) to compute the operator for reactions involving two-nucleon systems, using wave functions derived consistently with chiral symmetry from one-pion-exchange potentials matched to spherical well potentials at a radius R. They extract the low-energy constant d̂ (parameterizing short-distance physics in ³S₁↔¹S₀ transitions) by requiring that the Gamow-Teller matrix element for pp fusion matches the value obtained from the Argonne v₁₈ potential (4.898 fm), then use this d̂ to compute the π⁻d → nnγ neutron spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The Gamow-Teller matrix element for pp fusion used as input: 4.898 fm, obtained from the Argonne v₁₈ potential
- The asymptotic normalization of the deuteron S-state: A_S = 0.8846 fm⁻¹/²
- The deuteron binding momentum: γ = 45.7 MeV
- The pp scattering length with respect to the Coulomb potential: a^C_pp = -7.8196 ± 0.0026 fm
- The R-related a_nn uncertainty reduced from ±1.0 fm to ±0.14 fm when including the d̂ contribution and fitting the entire spectrum
- Theoretical uncertainty in a_nn ≤ 0.3 fm if the entire spectrum is fitted, and ≤ 0.05 fm if only the FSI peak is fitted
- The accepted value of a_nn: -18.59 ± 0.40 fm from radiative pion capture experiments

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Weinberg (1990) and applied to electroweak processes by Park et al. (2003) and to νd scattering by Butler and Chen (2000). It establishes a direct correlation between pp fusion and π⁻d → nnγ, enabling model-independent constraints on the three-nucleon force (3NF) depicted in Fig. 1 of the paper. The reduction in theoretical uncertainty for a_nn extraction from π⁻d → nnγ addresses a long-standing problem identified in prior extractions by Gårdestig et al. (2004) and by Gibbs et al. (1975), where short-distance physics contributed ±0.3 fm of the total ±0.4 fm uncertainty. This work enables future measurements of γd → nnπ⁺ to help determine the strength of the chiral 3NF.
