# The quark mass gap in strong magnetic fields

> Quarks in strong magnetic fields (|eB|>>Lambda_QCD^2 ~ 0.04 GeV^2) acquire enhanced infrared phase space proportional to |eB|. Accordingly they provide larger chiral condensates and stronger backreactions to the gluon dynamics. Confronting theories with lattice data at various values of |eB|, one can test theoretical ideas as well as validity of various approximations, domain of applicability of the effective models, and so on. The particularly interesting findings on the lattice are inverse magnetic catalysis and linear growth of the chiral condensate as a function of |eB|, which pose theoretical challenges. In this talk we propose a scenario to explain both phenomena, claiming that the quark mass gap should stay at around ~ Lambda_QCD, instead of ~|eB|^{1/2} which has been supposed from dimensional arguments and/or effective model calculations. The contrast between infrared and ultraviolet behaviors of the interaction is a key ingredient to obtain the mass gap of ~Lambda_QCD.

## Key claim

The authors claim that in strong magnetic fields (|eB| >> Lambda_QCD^2), the dynamically generated quark mass gap should remain approximately constant at around Lambda_QCD, rather than scaling as |eB|^(1/2) as predicted by dimensional arguments and effective model calculations. This claim is conditional on the infrared enhancement of nonperturbative QCD forces dominating over ultraviolet contributions.

## Method

The authors analyze the Schwinger-Dyson equation for quarks in strong magnetic fields using the Ritus basis, which diagonalizes the quark propagator in Landau levels. They focus on the lowest Landau level (LLL) as the dominant source for dynamical mass generation, factorizing the four-dimensional equation into a two-dimensional quark propagator and a two-dimensional effective force obtained by integrating the four-dimensional gluon propagator over transverse momentum with a Gaussian form factor from the Ritus basis. They compare the resulting mass gap for different interaction types: contact interactions, QED-type (1/q^2) forces, and nonperturbative QCD forces with infrared enhancement.

## Key numbers

- The strong magnetic field regime is defined as |eB| >> Lambda_QCD^2 ~ 0.04 GeV^2
- Lattice results show critical temperatures decrease by 10-20% at |eB| ~ 1 GeV^2
- Lattice results show chiral condensate grows as ~ |eB| * Lambda_QCD for |eB| > 0.1-0.3 GeV^2
- The LLL approximation is valid for |eB| > 0.3 GeV^2
- Lattice results show 10-30% changes in gluonic quantities (gluon condensates, string tensions) at |eB| ~ 1 GeV^2

## Context

This work addresses two lattice QCD findings that contradict effective model predictions: inverse magnetic catalysis (decreasing critical temperature with increasing B) found by Bali et al. (2011) and the linear growth of the chiral condensate with |eB| found by Bali et al. (2012). The authors propose that effective models using contact interactions or perturbative gluons overestimate the quark mass gap, while nonperturbative QCD forces with infrared enhancement yield a mass gap of order Lambda_QCD. This scenario enables explanation of both lattice phenomena and suggests that QCD in strong magnetic fields can serve as a theoretical laboratory for understanding cold dense QCD matter, as the enhanced infrared phase space resembles effects at finite quark density.
