# Hadronic Signatures of Deconfinement in relativistic nuclear collisions

> We describe the remarkable accomplishments of the current heavy ion Pb--Pb collision experiments involving strange particle production, carried out at 158A GeV at CERN--SPS. These experimental results, together with the earlier 200A GeV S-induced reactions, imply that, at central rapidity, a novel mechanism of strangeness production arises, accompanied by excess entropy formation. We argue that: These results are consistent with the formation of a space-time localized, highly excited, dense state of matter; The freeze-out properties of strange hadrons are suggestive of the formation of a color-deconfined, thermally and nearly chemically equilibrated phase, which provides at present the only comprehensive framework to describe all experimental data; The matter fireball is undergoing a transverse expansion with nearly the velocity of sound of relativistic matter; longitudinal expansion is not in the scaling regime. We present a first analysis of the recent Pb-Pb results and discuss several alternative reaction scenarios. We evaluate quantitatively strangeness production in the deconfined quark-gluon phase and obtain yields in agreement with the experimental observations made in 200A GeV S--W and 158A GeV Pb-Pb interactions. We also present a qualitative discussion of J/Psi results consistent with our understanding of strange particle results.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed enhancement of strange particle production in central rapidity Pb-Pb collisions at 158A GeV and S-induced reactions at 200A GeV is consistent with the formation of a color-deconfined, thermally and nearly chemically equilibrated quark-gluon plasma phase, but the authors hedge that this framework is "at present the only comprehensive framework to describe all experimental data" rather than a definitively proven conclusion.

## Method

The authors analyzed strange baryon and antibaryon ratios measured by the WA97 and NA49 collaborations in Pb-Pb collisions at 158A GeV, comparing them to earlier S-W and S-S data from experiments WA85 and WA94. They evaluated strangeness production rates in the deconfined quark-gluon phase using two-particle collision processes with running QCD coupling constant α_s and running quark masses, integrating renormalization group equations with α_s(M_Z)=0.118 and m_s(M_Z)=90±18 MeV to compute thermal production rates and relaxation times.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier comprehensive presentation (Rafelski, Letessier, Tounsi, 1996) of strangeness and entropy as signatures of the deconfined phase in relativistic nuclear collisions. The results build upon the theoretical framework of Hagedorn (1965) for hadronic gas and lattice QCD simulations, and the strangeness enhancement prediction of Rafelski (1982). The analysis enables quantitative comparison between QGP model predictions and experimental data from CERN-SPS experiments, supporting the interpretation that a deconfined phase has been observed in these collisions.
