# Pseudospin symmetry: Recent progress with supersymmetric quantum mechanics

> It is an interesting and open problem to trace the origin of the pseudospin symmetry in nuclear single-particle spectra and its symmetry breaking mechanism in actual nuclei. In this report, we mainly focus on our recent progress on this topic by combining the similarity renormalization group technique, supersymmetric quantum mechanics, and perturbation theory. We found that it is a promising direction to understand the pseudospin symmetry in a quantitative way.

# Pseudospin symmetry: Recent progress with supersymmetric quantum mechanics

## Key claim
The combination of the similarity renormalization group technique, supersymmetric quantum mechanics, and perturbation theory provides a promising direction for understanding pseudospin symmetry in a quantitative way, but the origin of pseudospin symmetry and its breaking mechanism in realistic nuclei remains an open problem that has not been fully understood.

## Method
The authors start from the Dirac Hamiltonian for nucleons and apply the similarity renormalization group technique to transform it into a diagonal form, keeping leading-order terms to obtain a Schrodinger-like equation with a spin-orbit term. They then construct supersymmetric partner Hamiltonians using first-order differential operators with superpotentials, and decompose the partner Hamiltonian into pseudospin-symmetry-conserving and pseudospin-symmetry-breaking terms, where the breaking term is required to be proportional to the quantum number kappa. The pseudospin-orbit potential is then uniquely determined from the reduced superpotentials for a given pair of pseudospin doublets.

## Key numbers
- The indirect effect of the spin-orbit term on the pseudospin-orbit potential via reduced superpotentials is only around 0.1 to 0.2 MeV.
- The spin-orbit term reduces the pseudospin-orbit splittings systematically by 0.15 to 0.3 MeV.
- The pseudospin-orbit potential shows a node at the surface region (around 5 fm), being negative at smaller radius and positive at larger radius.

## Context
This work extends the pseudospin symmetry concept introduced by Arima (1969) and Hecht (1969), and builds on the relativistic symmetry condition d(S+V)/dr=0 proposed by Meng (1998) and Sugawara-Tanabe (1998). The authors combine the similarity renormalization group technique from Guo (2012) and Li (2013), supersymmetric quantum mechanics from Typel (2008), and perturbation theory from Liang (2011) to provide a quantitative framework for understanding pseudospin symmetry breaking. This approach enables systematic interpretation of pseudospin-orbit splitting patterns in realistic nuclei such as 132Sn, and can explain the general tendency of pseudospin-orbit splittings to decrease with increasing single-particle energies and potentially reverse near the single-particle threshold.
