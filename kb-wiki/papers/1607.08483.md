# Large-scale deformed quasiparticle random-phase approximation calculations of the $γ$-ray strength function using the Gogny force

> Valuable theoretical predictions of nuclear dipole excitations in the whole chart are of great interest for different nuclear applications, including in particular nuclear astrophysics. Here we present large-scale calculations of the $E1$ $γ$-ray strength function obtained in the framework of the axially-symmetric deformed QRPA based on the finite-range Gogny force. This approach is applied to even-even nuclei, the strength function for odd nuclei being derived by interpolation. The convergence with respect to the adopted number of harmonic oscillator shells and the cut-off energy introduced in the 2-quasiparticle (2-$qp$) excitation space is analyzed. The calculations performed with two different Gogny interactions, namely D1S and D1M, are compared. A systematic energy shift of the $E1$ strength is found for D1M relative to D1S, leading to a lower energy centroid and a smaller energy-weighted sum rule for D1M. When comparing with experimental photoabsorption data, the Gogny-QRPA predictions are found to overestimate the giant dipole energy by typically $\sim$2 MeV. Despite the microscopic nature of our self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov plus QRPA calculation, some phenomenological corrections need to be included to take into account the effects beyond the standard 2-$qp$ QRPA excitations and the coupling between the single-particle and low-lying collective phonon degrees of freedom. For this purpose, three prescriptions of folding procedure are considered and adjusted to reproduce experimental photoabsorption data at best. All of them are shown to lead to rather similar predictions of the $E1$ strength, both at low energies and for exotic neutron-rich nuclei. Predictions of $γ$-ray strength functions and Maxwellian-averaged neutron capture rates for the whole Sn isotopic chain are also discussed and compared with previous theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that large-scale, axially-symmetric deformed quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculations using the finite-range Gogny force can predict the E1 gamma-ray strength function across the nuclear chart, but the raw theoretical predictions systematically overestimate the giant dipole resonance (GDR) energy by typically about 2 MeV compared to experimental photoabsorption data, requiring phenomenological folding corrections to achieve agreement.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) plus QRPA calculations using the finite-range Gogny interaction (parameter sets D1S and D1M) for even-even nuclei, with single-particle wave functions expanded in a harmonic oscillator basis. The calculations were restricted to axially-symmetric deformed nuclei, and the discrete B(E1) strength distribution was folded with a Lorentzian function to account for effects beyond 2-quasiparticle excitations, including particle-vibration coupling. Three different folding prescriptions were considered and adjusted to reproduce experimental photoabsorption data, and the strength function for odd nuclei was derived by interpolation from neighboring even-even nuclei.

## Key numbers

* The raw Gogny-QRPA predictions overestimate the GDR energy by typically about 2 MeV when compared with experimental photoabsorption data.
* For the D1M interaction, the centroid energy shift between calculations with a 30 MeV cut-off and converged values is of the order of 0.5 to 1 MeV, while a 60 MeV cut-off gives shifts of only 100 to 200 keV.
* The centroid energy of the QRPA B(E1) distributions for both D1S and D1M interactions follows approximately the mass dependence E = 41 A^{-1/6} MeV.
* The computation time for a single K^pi=0^- calculation with N_sh=17 and no energy cut-off is about 286 days using 1024 CPUs, while with a 60 MeV cut-off it reduces to 7 days.
* The change in peak energies between N_sh=13 and N_sh=15 for ^92Zr is about 100 keV, and between N_sh=15 and N_sh=17 for ^238U is also about 100 keV.

## Context

This work extends previous large-scale calculations of gamma-ray strength functions that used zero-range Skyrme forces (Goriely 2002, Goriely 2004) by implementing a fully consistent axially-symmetric deformed QRPA approach based on the finite-range Gogny interaction, which provides an adequate treatment of deformation and pairing correlations without additional parameters. The approach was first applied to study giant resonances in Si and Mg isotopes (Peru and Goutte 2008) and later to dipole excitations in Ne isotopes and N=16 isotones (Martini 2011), as well as electromagnetic excitations of ^238U (Peru 2011). The present systematic comparison with experimental photoabsorption data from the RIPL library enables the tuning of phenomenological folding corrections, and the resulting predictions for the Sn isotopic chain provide input for Hauser-Feshbach calculations of Maxwellian-averaged neutron capture rates relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis.
