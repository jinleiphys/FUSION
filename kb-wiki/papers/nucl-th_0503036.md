# Continuum effects on the pairing in neutron drip-line nuclei studied with the canonical-basis HFB method

> The canonical-basis HFB method provides an efficient way to describe pairing correlations involving the continuum part of the single-particle spectrum in coordinate-space representations. It can be applied to super-conducting deformed drip-line nuclei as easily as to stable or spherical nuclei. This method is applied to a simulation of the approach to the neutron drip line. It turns out that the HFB solution has a stronger pairing and a smaller deformation as the Fermi level is raised. However, such changes are smooth and finite. No divergences or discontinuities of the radius or other quantities are found in the limit of zero Fermi energy. The nuclear density continues to be localized even a little beyond the drip line.

# Continuum effects on the pairing in neutron drip-line nuclei studied with the canonical-basis HFB method

## Key claim

The canonical-basis HFB method produces smooth and finite changes in pairing, deformation, and radius as the Fermi level approaches zero, with no divergences or discontinuities; however, the localized HFB solutions for positive Fermi energies obtained in this formalism may only serve as rough approximations to nuclei just beyond the neutron drip line, because the true ground state must be a dislocalized state according to general properties of HFB solutions.

## Method

The author applied the canonical-basis Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method to a simulated approach to the neutron drip line for an N=Z=14 system, using the Skyrme SIII force without spin-orbit or Coulomb terms. Instead of increasing N-Z, the Skyrme force parameter t0 was increased toward zero from below while t3 was decreased to keep symmetric nuclear matter saturation density unchanged. A three-dimensional Cartesian mesh with 0.8 fm spacing and a 40 fm cubic cavity was used, with 70 canonical basis states in each of the four spin-isospin sectors.

## Key numbers

- The pairing gap is enhanced by almost a factor of two at the drip line (where the Fermi level lambda = 0 MeV) compared with the solution for the original SIII force (lambda = -11.7 MeV)
- The nucleus has a large prolate deformation at lambda = -11.7 MeV but becomes spherical for lambda > -5.5 MeV
- The root-mean-square radius increase is only 35% even at the drip line
- Dislocalization of the density occurs not at lambda = 0 MeV but at higher lambda (0.8 MeV)
- Positive-energy localized s orbitals begin to spread over the cavity only for lambda > 300 keV

## Context

This work extends the canonical-basis HFB method originally introduced for spherical nuclei by Bennaceur et al. (1997) and later implemented for deformed nuclei by Tajima (1998, 2004). It addresses the difficulty of treating deformed neutron-rich nuclei with continuum pairing, which previous approaches handled using transformed oscillator basis (Stoitsov et al., 2003) or coordinate meshes for axially symmetric nuclei (Terasaki et al., 2003). The finding that canonical orbitals above the Fermi level are roughly bound eigenstates of the pairing Hamiltonian rather than the HF Hamiltonian helps understand shell structure in the continuum spectrum and enables future studies of deformed drip-line nuclei without the computational burden of treating huge numbers of quasiparticle states.
