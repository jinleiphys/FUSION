# Electromagnetic moments of quasi-stable particle

> We deal with the problem of assigning electromagnetic moments to a quasi-stable particle (i.e., a particle with mass located at particle's decay threshold). In this case, an application of a small external electromagnetic field changes the energy in a non-analytic way, which makes it difficult to assign definitive moments. On the example of a spin-1/2 field with mass $M_{*}$ interacting with two fields of masses $M$ and $m$, we show how a conventionally defined magnetic dipole moment diverges at $M_{*}=M+m$. We then show that the conventional definition makes sense only when the values of the applied magnetic field $B$ satisfy $|eB|/2M_{*}\ll|M_{*}-M-m|$. We discuss implications of these results to existing studies in electroweak theory, chiral effective-field theory, and lattice QCD.

## Key claim

For a quasi-stable particle whose mass lies exactly at a decay threshold, the conventionally defined magnetic dipole moment diverges, and the standard linear-response definition of magnetic moments only makes sense when the applied magnetic field satisfies \(|eB|/2M_{*}\ll|M_{*}-M-m|\); otherwise the energy shift is non-analytic in the field (typically \(\Delta E\sim |B|^{1/2}\)).

## Method

The authors study a toy model of three fields: a scalar \(\varphi\) and two Dirac spinors \(\psi\) and \(\Psi\) with masses \(m\), \(M\), and \(M_{*}\), coupled via a Yukawa interaction. They compute the one-loop electromagnetic vertex corrections to obtain the anomalous magnetic moment \(\kappa_{*}\) of the \(\Psi\) field, and separately compute the self-energy of \(\Psi\) in a constant external magnetic field to obtain the full energy shift \(\Delta E\). The two results are compared as functions of the mass parameters and the magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers

* The neutron mass lies less than 1 MeV above the threshold: \(M_n - M_p - m_e \approx 0.8\) MeV, which is huge compared to the nuclear magneton \(\mu_N \simeq 3 \times 10^{-14}\) MeV/Tesla.
* For the \(\Delta(1232)\) isobar, the condition for a valid magnetic moment definition is \(\left|\frac{eB}{2M_\Delta(M_\Delta-M_N-m_\pi)}\right| \ll 1\); with lattice parameters \(1/a = 2\) GeV and \(|eB| = 0.00108/a^2\) to \(0.00864/a^2\), and \(M_\Delta - M_N = 0.293\) GeV, this condition can be violated for certain pion masses.
* The dimensionless magnetic field variable used is \(\tilde{B} = eB_z / M_*^2\), with the energy shift \(\Delta\tilde{E} = \Delta E / M_* + \frac{1}{2}\tilde{B}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of singularities in electromagnetic moments previously observed in studies of the W-boson as a function of bottom- and top-quark masses (Couture et al.; Argyres et al.) and in chiral perturbation theory calculations of hadron moments (Pascalutsa 2004; Jiang 2009). The results show that those singularities are unphysical and arise because the conventional perturbative expansion in the magnetic field breaks down near decay thresholds. The findings are directly relevant to lattice QCD calculations of hadron electromagnetic moments using the background field method (Bernard 1982; Damgaard 1988), particularly for the \(\Delta(1232)\) isobar (Lee 2005; Aubin 2008), where current and future calculations approaching physical pion masses may violate the condition derived here.
