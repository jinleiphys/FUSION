# Quantum response of finite Fermi systems and the relation of Lyapunov exponent to transport coefficients

> Within the frame of kinetic theory a response function is derived for finite Fermi systems which includes dissipation in relaxation time approximation and a contribution from additional chaotic processes characterized by the largest Lyapunov exponent. A generalized local density approximation is presented including the effect of many particle relaxation and the additional chaotic scattering. For small Lyapunov exponents relative to the product of wave vector and Fermi velocity in the system, the largest Lyapunov exponent modifies the response in the same way as the relaxation time. Therefore the transport coefficients can be connected with the largest positive Lyapunov exponent in the same way as known from the transport theory in relaxation time approximation.

# Quantum response of finite Fermi systems and the relation of Lyapunov exponent to transport coefficients

## Key claim

For small Lyapunov exponent relative to the product of wave vector and Fermi velocity (λ << q v_F), the largest Lyapunov exponent modifies the linear response of a finite Fermi system in the same way as the relaxation time, so that transport coefficients can be expressed in terms of the Lyapunov exponent as an alternative to the relaxation time approximation.

## Method

The author derives a polarization function for finite Fermi systems starting from a quasiclassical kinetic equation with a relaxation time collision term, then linearizes and integrates along particle trajectories using the Kirschnitz formula. An additional chaotic component is introduced into the trajectory by adding an irregular part that grows exponentially with the largest Lyapunov exponent λ, and the resulting polarization is evaluated under a local density approximation with gradient expansion and first-order history expansion of trajectories.

## Key numbers

- The condition for equivalence is λ << q v_F, where v_F = p_f/m is the Fermi velocity and q is the wave vector.
- The damping mechanisms are additive according to the Matthiessen rule: Γ = 1/τ + λ.
- The quasiclassical Lindhard result for infinite matter is given as Π_0^inf(q, p_f, ω) = -(m g p_f)/(4 π^2 ℏ^3) {1 - 2k ln[(1+k)/(1-k)] + i k π [sgn(1+k) + sgn(1-k)]}, where k = mω/(q p_f).
- The local density approximation polarization including relaxation time is Π_τ^LDA(q, R, ω) = Π_0^inf(q, p_f(R), ω + i/τ).

## Context

This work extends the connection between Lyapunov exponents and transport coefficients previously explored for hard sphere gases by Evans, Dorfman, and others, and for the Lorentz-Boltzmann equation by van Beijeren and Dorfman (1997). The author derives a simple relation showing that the Lyapunov exponent acts as an additive damping term alongside the relaxation time, enabling transport coefficients from relaxation time approximation to be reinterpreted in terms of chaotic dynamics. This provides a bridge between many-particle kinetic theory and deterministic chaos approaches to irreversibility in finite Fermi systems.
