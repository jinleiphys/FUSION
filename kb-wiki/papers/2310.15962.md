# Acceptance effect on the $N_{\rm t} N_{\rm p}/N_{\rm d}^2$ ratio of light nuclei coalescence yields as a probe of nucleon density fluctuations

> We employ a coalescence model to form deuterons ($\rm d$), tritons (${\rm t}$) and helium-3 ($^3{\rm He}$) nuclei from a uniformly distributed volume of protons ({\rm p}) and neutrons ({\rm n}). We study the ratio $N_{\rm t} N_{\rm p}/N_{\rm d}^2$ of light nuclei yields as a function of the neutron density fluctuations. We investigate the effect of finite transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) acceptance on the ratio, in particular, the "extrapolation factor" ($f$) for the ratio as functions of the $p_{\rm T}$ spectral shape and the magnitude of neutron density fluctuations. It is found that $f$ is monotonic in $p_{\rm T}$ spectra "temperature" parameter and neutron density fluctuation magnitude; variations in the latter are relatively small. We also examine $f$ in realistic simulations using kinematic distributions of protons measured in heavy ion collision data. It is found that $f$ is smooth and monotonic as a function of beam energy. We conclude that extrapolation from limited $p_{\rm T}$ ranges would not create, enhance, or reduce a local peak of the $N_{\rm t} N_{\rm p}/N_{\rm d}^2$ ratio in beam energy. Our study provides a necessary benchmark for light nuclei ratios as a probe of nucleon density fluctuations, an important observable in the search for the critical point of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The extrapolation factor \(f\) (the ratio of the \(N_{\rm t} N_{\rm p}/N_{\rm d}^2\) ratio from total yields to that from a limited transverse momentum acceptance) is monotonic in both the \(p_{\rm T}\) spectral temperature parameter and the neutron density fluctuation magnitude, and variations due to the latter are relatively small; therefore, extrapolation from limited \(p_{\rm T}\) ranges would not create, enhance, or reduce a local peak of the ratio in beam energy, contrary to what might be inferred from the STAR data.

## Method

The authors used a coalescence model with a Wigner function formalism to form deuterons, tritons, and helium-3 from a uniformly distributed volume of protons and neutrons in a cylinder of 10 fm radius and 10 fm length. They varied the neutron multiplicity fluctuations using negative binomial distributions (parameterized by \(\theta\), the ratio of variance to mean relative to Poisson) and studied the ratio \(N_{\rm t} N_{\rm p}/N_{\rm d}^2\) as a function of these fluctuations and of the \(p_{\rm T}\) acceptance. They also performed more realistic simulations using kinematic distributions of protons measured in STAR heavy ion collision data, parameterizing the chemical freeze-out temperature, kinetic freeze-out temperature, and average radial velocity as functions of charged hadron multiplicity density.

## Key numbers

* The slope of the \(N_{\rm t} N_{\rm p}/N_{\rm d}^2\) ratio as a function of \(\Delta n_{\rm n}\) (neutron density fluctuation beyond Poisson) is about 0.477, with an intercept of about 0.439, for a system with average \(\bar{N}_{\rm p}=\bar{N}_{\rm n}=20\) at temperature \(T=150\) MeV.
* For the fiducial range \(\pt/A=0.3\)–0.4 GeV/\(c\), the extrapolation factor \(f\) varies by about 1% for \(\theta=2\) (a fluctuation magnitude of a factor of 2 of Poisson) relative to the case with no extra fluctuations.
* The deuteron RMS size used is \(R_{\rm d}=1.96\) fm, giving \(\sigma_r=3.20\) fm; the triton RMS size is \(R_{\rm t}=1.59\) fm, giving \(\sigma_r=2.75\) fm.
* The STAR non-monotonic bump in the ratio is observed in the energy region \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=20\)–30 GeV, with the non-monotonic effect in the extrapolation factor on the order of 10%.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model predictions of Sun et al. (2017, 2018) that the \(N_{\rm t} N_{\rm p}/N_{\rm d}^2\) ratio is a probe of neutron density fluctuations and the critical point of nuclear matter. It directly addresses the STAR experiment (2022) observation of a non-monotonic beam-energy dependence of this ratio in Au+Au collisions, which showed a stronger peak in the extrapolated total yield ratio than in the measured fiducial \(p_{\rm T}/A\) ranges. The study provides a necessary benchmark showing that the acceptance effect alone cannot produce the observed non-monotonic behavior, enabling future quantitative comparisons between coalescence model predictions and experimental data in the search for the critical point.
