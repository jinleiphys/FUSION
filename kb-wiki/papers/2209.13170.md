# Local position-space two-nucleon potentials from leading to fourth order of chiral effective field theory

> We present local, position-space chiral NN potentials through four orders of chiral effective field theory ranging from leading order (LO) to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO, fourth order) of the Delta-less version of the theory. The long-range parts of these potentials are fixed by the very accurate pi-N LECs as determined in the Roy-Steiner equations analysis. At the highest order (N3LO), the NN data below 190 MeV laboratory energy are reproduced with the respectable chi^2/datum of 1.45. A comparison of the N3LO potential with the phenomenological Argonne v_18 (AV18) potential reveals substantial agreement between the two potentials in the intermediate range ruled by chiral symmetry, thus, providing a chiral underpinning for the phenomenological AV18 potential. Our chiral NN potentials may serve as a solid basis for systematic ab initio calculations of nuclear structure and reactions that allow for a comprehensive error analysis. In particular, the order by order development of the potentials will make possible a reliable determination of the truncation error at each order. Our new family of local position-space potentials differs from existing potentials of this kind by a weaker tensor force as reflected in relatively low D-state probabilities of the deuteron (P_D less or equal 4.0% for our N3LO potentials) and predictions for the triton binding energy above 8.00 MeV (from two-body forces alone). As a consequence, our potentials may lead to different predictions when applied to light and intermediate-mass nuclei in ab initio calculations and, potentially, help solve some of the outstanding problems in microscopic nuclear structure.

## Key claim

The authors construct local, position-space chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials from leading order (LO) to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) in the Delta-less version of chiral effective field theory, achieving a chi-squared per datum of 1.45 for NN data below 190 MeV laboratory energy at N3LO; however, the potentials differ from existing local chiral potentials by having a weaker tensor force, as reflected in deuteron D-state probabilities of 4.0% or less and triton binding energies above 8.00 MeV from two-body forces alone.

## Method

The long-range parts of the potentials are constructed from one- and two-pion exchange contributions up to N3LO, with the pion-nucleon low-energy constants (LECs) fixed by the Roy-Steiner equations analysis of pi-N scattering. The short-range parts are built from contact interactions up to fourth order, using a local Gaussian regulator with cutoff radius R_ct, and the contact LECs are fitted to NN scattering data and deuteron properties. The potentials are defined in position space and satisfy the Blankenbeclar-Sugar equation, which is reduced to a nonrelativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation for phase shift and bound state calculations.

## Key numbers

- chi-squared per datum of 1.45 for NN data below 190 MeV laboratory energy at N3LO
- Deuteron D-state probability P_D less than or equal to 4.0% for the N3LO potentials
- Triton binding energy above 8.00 MeV from two-body forces alone
- Pion-nucleon LECs used: c1 = -1.07(2) GeV^-1, c2 = 3.20(3) GeV^-1, c3 = -5.32(5) GeV^-1, c4 = 3.56(3) GeV^-1 at N3LO (from Roy-Steiner analysis)
- Average pion mass used in two-pion exchange: 138.0390 MeV
- Axial-vector coupling constant g_A = 1.29, pion-decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the development of local chiral NN potentials beyond the previous next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) potentials of Gezerlis et al. (2014) and the hybrid NNLO/N3LO potentials of Piarulli et al. (2015, 2016), providing a consistent order-by-order family from LO to N3LO for systematic uncertainty quantification. The potentials show substantial agreement with the phenomenological Argonne v_18 (AV18) potential in the intermediate range, providing a chiral underpinning for AV18, and are designed for use in quantum Monte Carlo methods and other ab initio calculations of nuclear structure and reactions.
