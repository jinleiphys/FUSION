# Neutron star structure and collective excitations of finite nuclei

> We study relationships between properties of collective excitations in finite nuclei and the phase transition density $n_t$ and pressure $P_t$ at the inner edge separating the liquid core and the solid crust of a neutron star. A theoretical framework that includes the thermodynamic method, relativistic nuclear energy density functionals and the quasiparticle random-phase approximation is employed in a self-consistent calculation of $(n_t,P_t)$ and collective excitations in nuclei. The covariance analysis shows that properties of charge-exchange dipole transitions, isovector giant dipole and quadrupole resonances and pygmy dipole transitions are correlated with the core-crust transition density and pressure. A set of relativistic nuclear energy density functionals, characterized by systematic variation of the density dependence of the symmetry energy of nuclear matter, is used to constrain possible values for $(n_t,P_t)$. By comparing the calculated excitation energies of giant resonances, energy weighted pygmy dipole strength, and dipole polarizability with available data, we obtain the weighted average values: $n_t = 0.0955 \pm 0.0007$ fm$^{-3}$ and $P_t = 0.59 \pm 0.05$ MeV fm$^{-3}$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that properties of collective excitations in finite nuclei (charge-exchange dipole transitions, isovector giant dipole and quadrupole resonances, and pygmy dipole transitions) are correlated with the core-crust transition density and pressure of neutron stars, and uses these correlations to constrain the transition density to \(n_t = 0.0955 \pm 0.0007\) fm\(^{-3}\) and pressure to \(P_t = 0.59 \pm 0.05\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\). The analysis is limited to a single family of relativistic nuclear energy density functionals, and the quoted uncertainties reflect only the experimental errors of the collective excitation data used.

## Method

The authors employed a theoretical framework combining the thermodynamic method for determining the liquid-to-solid phase transition in neutron-rich matter, relativistic nuclear energy density functionals (RNEDFs) for nuclear matter and finite nuclei, and the relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (RQRPA) for collective excitations in spherical nuclei. A set of RNEDFs with systematic variation of the density dependence of the symmetry energy (spanning \(J = 30-38\) MeV and \(L = 30-110.8\) MeV) was used to compute the transition density \(n_t\) and pressure \(P_t\) via the condition \(C(n_t)=0\) from the thermodynamic stability inequality, and simultaneously to calculate excitation energies and strengths of collective modes in \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{68}\)Ni. Covariance analysis based on the DDME-min1 parameterization identified correlations between \((n_t, P_t)\) and nuclear observables, and linear correlations between calculated excitation properties and the symmetry energy parameters \(J\) and \(L\) were used to constrain \((n_t, P_t)\) by comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Weighted average transition density at the inner edge of the neutron star crust: \(n_t = 0.0955 \pm 0.0007\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Weighted average transition pressure: \(P_t = 0.59 \pm 0.05\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\)
- Weighted average symmetry energy at saturation: \(J = 32.5 \pm 0.5\) MeV
- Weighted average slope parameter of the symmetry energy: \(L = 49.9 \pm 4.7\) MeV
- Range of symmetry energy values spanned by the RNEDF set: \(J = 30-38\) MeV
- Range of slope parameter values spanned by the RNEDF set: \(L = 30-110.8\) MeV
- Neutron-skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb from PREX experiment: \(r_{np} = 0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18}\) fm (cited for context)
- Comparison value from Friedman and Pandharipande equation of state: \(n_t = 0.096\) fm\(^{-3}\) (Lor.93)
- Comparison value from \(A18+\delta v+UIX^*\) interaction: \(n_t = 0.087\) fm\(^{-3}\) (Akm.98)

## Context

This work extends the correlation between neutron-skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb and the core-crust transition density first identified by Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001) to a broader set of collective excitation observables, including charge-exchange dipole transitions, isovector giant resonances, and pygmy dipole strength. The results are consistent with previous constraints from the nonrelativistic microscopic equation of state of Friedman and Pandharipande (1981) and provide tighter constraints than earlier studies using modified Gogny interactions (Xu.09, Kra.13) or Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (Sam.09). The approach enables future refinement of neutron star crust properties through more accurate measurements of collective excitations in nuclei, particularly charge-exchange modes and pygmy dipole strength, and through extension to a wider range of non-relativistic and relativistic functionals.
