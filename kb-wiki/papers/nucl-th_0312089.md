# Response in the continuum for light deformed neutron-rich nuclei

> The time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculation with a full Skyrme energy functional has been carried out on the three-dimensional Cartesian lattice space to study E1 giant dipole resonances (GDR) in light nuclei. The outgoing boundary condition for the continuum states is treated by the absorbing complex potential. The calculation for GDR in O-16 suggests a significant influence of the residual interaction associated with time-odd densities in the Skyrme functional. We also predict a large damping for superdeformed Be-14 at the neutron drip line.

## Key claim
The time-odd density components in the Skyrme energy functional, which are not constrained by ground-state properties, significantly influence the isovector giant dipole resonance strength distribution in light nuclei; the calculation for 16O suggests that neglecting these components produces results similar to earlier continuum RPA calculations, while including them yields a single-peak structure.

## Method
The authors performed time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations on a three-dimensional Cartesian lattice using a full Skyrme energy functional with the SGII parameter set. The outgoing boundary condition for continuum states was treated by an absorbing complex potential (ABC) active in a non-interacting region outside the nuclear volume. The initial state was the HF ground state perturbed by an instantaneous isovector dipole field, and the time evolution was computed up to T=30 ℏ/MeV, with the strength function obtained via Fourier transform of the time-dependent expectation value.

## Key numbers
- For 16O, the TDHF+ABC calculation with the full Skyrme functional shows a single peak at approximately 20 MeV excitation energy.
- For 8Be, the low-energy resonance is located around 7 MeV and the high-energy resonance around 22 MeV, with the low-energy peak sharper and the high-energy peak broader.
- For 14Be at the neutron drip line, the average peak positions for low- and high-energy resonances are similar to those in 8Be, but excess neutrons significantly increase the peak width of both resonances.
- The model space used a 3D sphere of radius R0 = 22 fm with mesh spacing approximately 1 fm in the interacting region, increasing to approximately 3 fm in the non-interacting region.

## Context
This work extends the time-dependent approach with absorbing boundary conditions previously applied to molecular photoabsorption by Nakatsukasa and Yabana (2001) to nuclear giant dipole resonances. It contradicts earlier continuum RPA calculations for 16O by Liu and Giai (1976) that showed a two-peak structure but were not fully self-consistent, as they neglected residual Coulomb, spin-orbit, and σ·σ interactions. The results enable future constraints on the time-odd part of the Skyrme energy functional, which is not well determined by ground-state properties, and predict large damping for superdeformed 14Be at the neutron drip line.
