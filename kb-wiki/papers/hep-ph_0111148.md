# Small quark stars in the chromodielectric model

> Equations of state for strange quark matter in beta equilibrium at high densities are used to investigate the structure (mass and radius) of compact objects. The chromodielectric model is used as a general framework for the quark interactions, which are mediated by chiral mesons, $σ$ and $\vec π$, and by a confining chiral singlet dynamical field, $χ$. Using a quartic potential for $χ$, two equations of state for the same set of model parameters are obtained, one with a minimum at around the nuclear matter density $ρ_0$ and the other at $ρ\sim 5 ρ_0$. Using the latter equation of state in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations we found solutions corresponding to compact objects with $R\sim 5 - 8$ km and $M\sim M_\odot$. The phenomenology of recently discovered X-ray sources is compatible with the type of quark stars that we have obtained.

# Small quark stars in the chromodielectric model

## Key claim

Using a quartic potential for the confining chiral singlet field in the chromodielectric model yields two distinct equations of state for the same set of model parameters; only the second solution, corresponding to a chiral restored phase with nearly massless quarks, produces pure quark stars with radii of 5–8 km and masses near one solar mass, which are compatible with recently discovered X-ray sources such as RX J1856.5-3574.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using equations of state derived from the chromodielectric model with a quartic potential for the confining field χ. The model includes quark interactions mediated by chiral mesons (σ and π) and the χ field, with strange quark matter in beta equilibrium including an electron gas. A mean-field variational method was applied to the energy density, yielding two stable solutions for the same set of model parameters: solution I (chiral broken phase with massive quarks) and solution II (chiral restored phase with nearly massless quarks).

## Key numbers

- For solution II with η = 0.115 (yielding B^{1/4} ∼ 0.195 GeV), the maximum radius is R ∼ 6 km with a corresponding mass M ∼ 0.9 M⊙.
- The central density of the maximum-radius star is 10 ρ₀ (where ρ₀ is nuclear matter density), and the central energy density is ε ∼ 3 × 10¹⁵ g/cm³.
- At the edge of that star, the density drops to 5 ρ₀ and ε ∼ 1.35 × 10¹⁵ g/cm³.
- The maximum rotation period for these stars is ∼ 0.4 ms.
- For solution II with η ∼ 0.12, the saturation density is ρ ∼ 5 ρ₀.
- The bag pressure range considered is 0.150 ≤ B^{1/4} ≤ 0.250 GeV, corresponding to 0.08 ≤ η ≤ 0.15 with M = 1.7 GeV.
- Solution I (quadratic-like) yields stars with R ∼ 10–12 km and masses M ∼ 1–2 M⊙.

## Context

This work extends the chromodielectric model calculations of Drago et al. (2000), who used a quadratic potential for the χ field and obtained hybrid neutron stars with masses 1–2 M⊙ and radii of order 10 km plus a 2 km hadron crust. The quartic potential introduced here produces a second family of pure quark stars that are smaller and denser. The equations of state for both solutions are consistent with perturbative QCD results from Fraga (2001). The predicted mass and radius for η = 0.115 match the fitted values for the isolated compact object RX J1856.5-3574, supporting the conjecture that such sources may be strange quark stars rather than neutron stars.
