# Quark-hadron duality in a relativistic, confining model

> Quark-hadron duality is an interesting and potentially very useful phenomenon, as it relates the properly averaged hadronic data to a perturbative QCD result in some kinematic regions. While duality is well established experimentally, our current theoretical understanding is still incomplete. We employ a simple model to qualitatively reproduce all the features of Bloom-Gilman duality as seen in electron scattering. In particular, we address the role of relativity, give an explicit analytic proof of the equality of the hadronic and partonic scaling curves, and show how the transition from coherent to incoherent scattering takes place.

## Key claim

Using a relativistic, confining model with scalar quarks, the authors provide an explicit analytic proof that the hadronic scaling curve obtained from transitions between bound states coincides with the partonic scaling curve from a bound-to-free transition, thereby demonstrating quark-hadron duality holds in this model under the condition that a complete set of relativistic states (including negative energy solutions) is included.

## Method

The authors model a meson with an infinitely heavy antiquark and a light scalar quark confined by a linear potential, solving the Klein-Gordon equation to obtain relativistic harmonic oscillator wave functions and energy eigenvalues. They calculate the longitudinal and transverse response functions for electron scattering from the ground state to excited bound states, then derive the continuum limit analytically using Stirling's approximation and compare it to the parton model result for a quark transitioning from a bound state to a plane-wave final state. The Coulomb sum rule is evaluated to examine the transition from coherent to incoherent scattering, and the approach to scaling is studied numerically for different scaling variables.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass used throughout: \( m = 0.33 \) GeV.
- Relativistic string tension parameter: \( \beta = 0.4 \) GeV, chosen to give reasonable mass splitting and charge radius.
- The Coulomb sum rule is exactly satisfied: \( S(\vec q) = 1 \) for the single-charge case, verified analytically using the form factor expression \( F_{0,N}(\vec q) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{N!}} i^N \left( \frac{|\vec q|}{\sqrt{2} \beta} \right)^N \exp\left(-\frac{\vec q^2}{4 \beta^2}\right) \).
- The scaling function for the parton model is derived analytically: \( F_2(u) = \frac{m^2 u^2}{\pi^{1/2} \beta E_0} \exp\left(-\frac{(E_0 - m u)^2}{\beta^2}\right) \), where \( u = Q^2/(2m\nu) \).
- The continuum limit of the bound-state response functions yields the identical scaling function as the parton model in the limit \( Q^2 \rightarrow \infty \).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier scalar-electron model (Jeschonnek and Van Orden, 2001) to physical electrons and photons, providing a more detailed treatment of relativity, the Coulomb sum rule, and the transition from coherent to incoherent scattering. It builds on prior studies of duality in confining models by Close and Isgur (2001), and by Greenberg (1969), Gurvitz and Rinat (1994), and others who found scaling in bound-state models but did not prove the equality of the hadronic and partonic scaling curves. The results enable future extension to more realistic three-quark systems and help clarify the conditions under which Bloom-Gilman duality holds at moderate \( Q^2 \), which is relevant for the planned 12 GeV upgrade of CEBAF at Jefferson Lab.
