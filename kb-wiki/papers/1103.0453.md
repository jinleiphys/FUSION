# {Once more about astrophysical $S$ factor for the $α+ d \to {}^{6}{\rm Li} + γ$ reaction

> Recently to study the radiative capture $α+ d \to {}^{6}{\rm Li} + γ$ process a new measurement of the ${}^{6}{\rm Li}({\rm A\,150\,MeV})$ dissociation in the field of ${}^{208}{\rm Pb}$ has been reported in [F. Hammache {\it et al.} Phys. Rev ${\bf C 82}$, 065803 (2010)]. However, the dominance of the nuclear breakup over the Coulomb one prevented from obtaining the information about the $α+ d \to {}^{6}{\rm Li} + γ$ process from the breakup data. The astrophysical $S_{24}(E)$ factor has been calculated within the $α-d$ two-body potential model with potentials determined from the fits to the $α-d$ elastic scattering phase shifts. However, the scattering phase shift itself doesn't provide a unique $α-d$ bound state potential, which is the most crucial input when calculating the $S_{24}(E)$ astrophysical factor at astrophysical energies. In this work we emphasize an important role of the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for ${}^{6}{\rm Li} \to α+ d$, which controls the overall normalization of the peripheral $α+ d \to {}^{6}{\rm Li} + γ$ process and is determined by the adopted $α-d$ bound state potential. We demonstrate that the ANC previously determined from the $α-d$ elastic scattering $s$-wave phase shift in [Blokhintsev {\it et. al} Phys. Rev. {\bf C 48}, 2390 (1993)] gives $S_{24}(E)$, which is at low energies about 38% lower than the one reported in [F. Hammache {\it et al.} Phys. Rev ${\bf C 82}$, 065803 (2010)]. We recalculate also the reaction rates, which are also lower than those obtained in [F. Hammache {\it et al.} Phys. Rev ${\bf C 82}$, 065803 (2010)].

## Key claim

The astrophysical S-factor for the α + d → ⁶Li + γ reaction is not uniquely determined by fitting α-d elastic scattering phase shifts alone; the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for ⁶Li → α + d, which controls the overall normalization of the peripheral capture process, must also be fixed. Using the ANC previously determined from s-wave elastic scattering by Blokhintsev (1993) gives an S-factor at low energies about 38% lower than that reported by Hammache (2010).

## Method

The authors calculated the astrophysical S-factor S₂₄(E) for the α + d → ⁶Li + γ radiative capture using a two-body potential model, adopting the same α-d scattering potential as Hammache (2010) for the continuum partial waves (l=1,2). For the bound state, they modified the Woods-Saxon potential from Hammache (2010) using an inverse scattering theorem to produce a phase-equivalent potential that yields the ANC value C_{αd(01)} = 2.3 ± 0.12 fm^{-1/2} from Blokhintsev (1993), rather than the value C_{αd(01)} = 2.7 fm^{-1/2} from the unmodified Hammache potential. The resulting S-factor and reaction rates were then computed and compared.

## Key numbers

- The ANC from the Hammache (2010) potential is C_{αd(01)} = 2.7 fm^{-1/2}, while the ANC from Blokhintsev (1993) is C_{αd(01)} = 2.3 ± 0.12 fm^{-1/2}.
- The square of the ANC from Hammache (2010) is C² = 7.29 fm^{-1}, compared to C² = 5.29 fm^{-1} from Blokhintsev (1993), a difference of about 38%.
- At astrophysically relevant energies E ≤ 300 keV, the S-factor from this work is lower than that from Hammache (2010) by 38%.
- At E = 70 keV (the most effective energy), the E1 transition contributes about 60% to the total S₂₄(E); at E = 100 keV, the E1 contribution is about 52%.
- The resonance energy is E_R = 0.7117 MeV.
- The reaction rate at T₉ = 0.001 is N_A⟨σv⟩ = 6.467 × 10^{-30} cm³ mol^{-1} s^{-1}; at T₉ = 10.00 it is 8.664 × 10^{+00} cm³ mol^{-1} s^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the α + d → ⁶Li + γ reaction, which is the only process producing ⁶Li in big bang nucleosynthesis, and addresses the discrepancy between the observed ⁶Li/⁷Li ratio and calculated values. It directly contradicts the S-factor and reaction rates reported by Hammache (2010), who used a bound-state potential determined solely from elastic scattering phase shifts without fixing the ANC. The authors call for more accurate measurements of the s-wave α-d elastic scattering phase shift at lower energies to enable better extrapolation to the bound state pole and determination of the ANC, which would then enable more reliable calculations of the astrophysical S-factor.
