# Universality in nuclear dependence coefficient $α(q_T)$

> We derive the nuclear dependence coefficient $α(q_T)$ for Drell-Yan and J/$ψ$ production. We show that at small $q_T$, the $α(q_T)$ is given by an universal functional form: $α(q_T)=a+b q_T^2$, and the parameters $a$ and $b$ are completely determined by either perturbatively calculable or independently measurable quantities. This universal functional form $α(q_T)$ is insensitive to the $A$, and is consistent with existing data.

# Universality in nuclear dependence coefficient α(q_T)

## Key claim

The nuclear dependence coefficient α(q_T) for Drell-Yan and J/ψ production in the small transverse momentum region (q_T < 2.5 GeV) follows a universal quadratic form α(q_T) = a + b q_T^2, where the parameters are determined by perturbatively calculable or independently measurable quantities, and this form is insensitive to the atomic number A of the target nucleus.

## Method

The authors derive the Drell-Yan q_T-spectrum in hadron-nucleon and hadron-nucleus collisions using a Gaussian-like distribution for small q_T, with the width and normalization related to the total cross section and the average transverse momentum squared ⟨q_T²⟩. For hadron-nucleus collisions, multiple scattering contributions are included via quark-gluon correlation functions T_qg, and the transverse momentum broadening Δ⟨q_T²⟩ is calculated perturbatively. The nuclear dependence coefficient α(q_T) is then obtained by substituting the Gaussian forms into the defining relation dσ^{hA}/dQ² dq_T² = A^{α(q_T)} × dσ^{hN}/dQ² dq_T². The same approach is extended to J/ψ production, with additional treatment of nuclear suppression in the total cross section via a parameterization R^A_{J/ψ} = e^{-β A^{1/3}}.

## Key numbers

- The Drell-Yan q_T-spectrum is divided into three regions: small q_T (q_T < q_T^S ~ 1 GeV), intermediate (q_T^S ≤ q_T ≤ q_T^L with q_T^L = κQ and κ ~ 1/3-1/2), and large q_T (q_T > q_T^L).
- Measured ⟨q_T²⟩^{hN} = 1.8 GeV² for Drell-Yan production.
- Measured transverse momentum broadening Δ⟨q_T²⟩^{hA} = 0.022 A^{1/3} GeV² for Drell-Yan production, with b_DY ≈ 0.022 GeV².
- For J/ψ production: ⟨q_T²⟩^{hN}_{J/ψ} ≈ 1.68 GeV² and b_{J/ψ} ≈ 0.06 GeV².
- The parameter C for double-hard scattering has a factor of 20 difference between the maximum value (geometric factor with r_0 ≈ 1.1-1.25 fm) and minimum value (from saturation region with [xG(x)]_{x≈0} ≈ 3).
- The small q_T region for the universal form applies for q_T < q_T^L = κQ with κ ~ 1/3-1/2.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of nuclear dependence in Drell-Yan and J/ψ production (S. Gavin and M. Gyulassy, 1988; J. Hüfner, Y. Kurihara, and H.J. Pirner, 1988; J.P. Blaizot and J.-Y. Ollitrault, 1989) by providing a derivation valid in the small q_T region where most experimental data exist, rather than only the large q_T region treated in prior perturbative QCD calculations by Luo, Qiu, and Sterman (1992, 1994) and Guo (1998). The universal functional form enables comparison with existing data from the E772 and E866 collaborations without free fitting parameters, and the results are consistent with measured Drell-Yan and J/ψ data. The work also enables future extraction of the double-hard correlation function T^{DH} through angular dependence measurements of Drell-Yan pairs, as suggested by Fries, Müller, Schäfer, and Stein (1999).
