# Entropy scaling from chaotically produced particles in p-p collisions at LHC energies

> Scaling of information entropy obtained from chaotically produced particles in p-p collisions, has been shown to be valid up to the highest available collision energy at LHC. Results from Monte Carlo simulation model PYTHIA 6.135 have also been compared. Based on the two com- ponent model and collision energy dependence of the chaoticity, charged particle multiplicities at proposed higher collision energies have been predicted.

## Key claim
The scaling of information entropy derived from chaotically produced particles in p-p collisions holds up to the highest LHC energies, but only when the entropy is calculated from the chaotic fraction of the multiplicity distribution using a two-component model; the scaling fails if coherently produced particles are included.

## Method
The authors applied a two-component model (chaotic plus coherent sources) to multiplicity distributions from p-p collisions. They extracted the chaotic fraction of the average multiplicity using the second moment of the distribution, computed the information entropy, and plotted the scaled entropy \(S/\eta_{\text{max}}\) versus \(\xi = \eta_c/\eta_{\text{max}}\) for collision energies from SPS to LHC. They also compared results with Monte Carlo simulations from PYTHIA 8.135 and fitted power laws to predict multiplicities at higher energies.

## Key numbers
- Predicted average total multiplicity in p-p collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV: \(6.678 \pm 0.242\) for the most central pseudorapidity bin.
- Predicted average total multiplicity in p-p collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 14\) TeV: \(8.658 \pm 0.404\) for the most central pseudorapidity bin.
- The chaoticity parameter \(\tilde{P}\) reaches very close to unity at \(\sqrt{s} = 14\) TeV.
- The coherent multiplicity shows non-monotonous behavior as collision energy increases beyond 1 TeV.

## Context
This work extends the entropy scaling approach previously demonstrated from ISR (\(\sqrt{s} = 19\) GeV) up to SPS (\(\sqrt{s} = 900\) GeV) by Raha et al. to the full LHC energy range. It uses the two-component model of Fowler et al. to separate chaotic and coherent particle production. The results enable predictions of charged particle multiplicities at proposed higher LHC collision energies and suggest that collective phenomena may appear in hadronic collisions at TeV energies, complementing earlier trace-of-deconfinement studies by Porile et al.
