# High-spin structures of $^{136}_{54}$Xe, $^{137}_{55}$Cs, $^{138}_{56}$Ba, $^{139}_{57}$La, and $^{140}_{58}$Ce : A shell model description

> In the present work recently available experimental data [A. Astier et al, Phys. Rev. C 85, 064316 (2012)] for high-spin states of five N=82 isotones, $^{136}_{54}$Xe, $^{137}_{55}$Cs, $^{138}_{56}$Ba, $^{139}_{57}$La, and $^{140}_{58}$Ce have been interpreted with state-of-the-art shell model calculations. The calculations have been performed in the 50-82 valence shell composed of $1g_{7/2}$, $2d_{5/2}$, $1h_{11/2}$, $3s_{1/2}$, and $2d_{3/2}$ orbitals. We have compared our results with the available experimental data for excitation energies including high-spin states, occupancy numbers and transition probabilities. As expected the structure of these isotones are due to proton excitations across Z=50 shell. The structure of the positive-parity states are mainly from $(πg_{7/2}πd_{5/2})^n$ and $(πg_{7/2}πd_{5/2})^{n-2}(πh_{11/2})^2$ configurations, while the negative-parity states have $(πg_{7/2}πd_{5/2})^{n}(πh_{11/2})^1$ configuration. Additionally, for the $^{136}_{54}$Xe, $^{137}_{55}$Cs and $^{138}_{56}$Ba isotones the excitation of the neutrons across N=82 gap is important.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations using the SN100PN interaction, derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, can reproduce the recently measured high-spin states of five N=82 isotones, but the agreement degrades for heavier isotones and for the highest-spin states, indicating that the SN100PN interaction needs further tuning for heavier N=82 isotones and that neutron excitations across the N=82 gap are important for intermediate-high-spin states in the lighter isotones.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in the 50-82 valence shell (1g7/2, 2d5/2, 1h11/2, 3s1/2, 2d3/2 orbitals) using the SN100PN interaction with the NuShell code. Excitation energies, proton occupancy numbers, and transition probabilities (B(E2) and B(E3)) were calculated for five N=82 isotones (136Xe, 137Cs, 138Ba, 139La, 140Ce) and compared with experimental data from Astier et al. (2012) and earlier shell-model results from Coraggio et al. (2009) and Astier et al. (2012).

## Key numbers

- For 136Xe: the calculated 8_1+ level differs from experiment by only 22 keV; the 10_1+ level at 3483 keV is predicted at 3220 keV.
- For 138Ba: the 2_1+ and 4_1+ levels are predicted with only 4 keV and 27 keV difference from experiment, respectively.
- For 137Cs: the highest state (37/2_1+) is predicted 725 keV lower than the experimental value.
- For 136Xe: B(E2; 2_1+ -> 0_1+) = 7.4 W.u. (experiment: 9.7(4) W.u.).
- For 138Ba: B(E2; 2_1+ -> 0_1+) = 11.4 W.u. (experiment: 10.8(5) W.u.).
- For 140Ce: B(E2; 2_1+ -> 0_1+) = 14.9 W.u. (experiment: 13.8(3) W.u.); B(E3; 3_1- -> 0_1+) = 9.3 W.u. (experiment: 27(6) W.u.).
- Proton occupancy numbers for 136Xe ground state: calculated g7/2 = 2.97, d5/2 = 0.59, h11/2 = 0.29 (experiment: g7/2 = 3.5(4), d5/2 = 0.5(2), h11/2 = 0.0(7)).

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model studies of N=82 isotones by Holt et al. (1997) and Coraggio et al. (2009) to include newly measured high-spin states from Astier et al. (2012). The calculations use the SN100PN interaction (Brown et al., 2005) rather than the empirical interaction used by Astier et al. (2012). The results show that for heavier isotones (139La, 140Ce) the SN100PN interaction needs further tuning, and that for 136Xe, 137Cs, and 138Ba, neutron excitations across the N=82 gap must be included to properly describe intermediate-high-spin states, motivating future work with an enlarged model space including the nu f7/2 orbital.
