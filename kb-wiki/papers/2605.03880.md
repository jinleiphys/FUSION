# Exclusive photoproduction of a di-meson pair with large invariant mass

> The exclusive photoproduction of a pair of light mesons is studied within the framework of collinear factorisation. The amplitude factorises into a process-dependent perturbatively calculable hard part, a generalised parton distribution (GPD) and two distribution amplitudes (DAs). We focus on the production of any combination of $ρ$ and $π$ mesons (of any charge and polarisation) that do not involve neutral $C=+$ exchanges with the nucleon. This gives a total of 26 distinct channels, which are sensitive to quark GPDs only. We calculate the amplitude for these di-meson processes at leading order in $α_s$ and at leading twist, in a fully-automated way. Depending on the choice of mesons in the final state, some of these processes are sensitive to chiral-odd (helicity-flip) GPDs. Particular attention is given to the treatment of poles in the 3-dimensional convolution integral of the momentum fractions connecting the hard part with the different non-perturbative components. These poles are regularised by usual Feynman $i ε$ factors, but lead to numerical instabilities if not dealt with properly. We also discuss in detail the construction of the phase space. Importantly, we propose a resolution for the inconsistency of the kinematics of the hard part of the process, where hadron masses and other soft scales are neglected, with the rest of the process. As a proof of concept, we explicitly evaluate the cross section, for a subset of processes whose amplitudes have been constructed, at energies typical of the CLAS12 experiment at JLab. Our results indicate that exclusive di-meson photoproduction processes have very good statistics, which can be a factor of up to a hundred more than the exclusive photon-meson photoproduction process. Therefore, the family of processes that we study here represents a great opportunity for GPD extraction.

## Key claim
The exclusive photoproduction of a pair of light mesons (ρ and π) with large invariant mass provides access to chiral-odd (helicity-flip) GPDs at leading twist, and the cross sections for these processes can be up to a factor of a hundred larger than those for exclusive photon-meson photoproduction, offering a statistically rich opportunity for GPD extraction. However, this claim is conditional on the processes not involving neutral C=+ exchanges with the nucleon, which would break collinear factorisation due to Glauber gluon exchanges.

## Method
The authors calculate the amplitude for 26 distinct di-meson photoproduction channels at leading order in α_s and leading twist within the collinear factorisation framework. The amplitude factorises into a perturbative hard part, a generalised parton distribution (GPD), and two distribution amplitudes (DAs), requiring a 3-dimensional convolution integral. The hard part is generated automatically using FeynArts and FeynCalc, and the Fierz projection is applied to connect the perturbative diagrams to the non-perturbative GPDs and DAs, with particular attention given to the regularisation of poles in the convolution integral via Feynman iε factors.

## Key numbers
*   The study covers **26 distinct channels** for the exclusive photoproduction of any combination of ρ and π mesons that do not involve neutral C=+ exchanges with the nucleon.
*   The cross section for exclusive di-meson photoproduction can be a factor of **up to a hundred** more than the exclusive photon-meson photoproduction process.
*   Kinematic cuts are applied requiring the Mandelstam variables for the two-body subprocess to satisfy `-u' > 1 GeV^2` and `-t' > 1 GeV^2`, and the momentum transfer to the nucleon to satisfy `-t < 0.5 GeV^2`.
*   The pion decay constant used is `f_π = 131 MeV`, and the ρ meson decay constants are `f_ρ^⟂ = 160 MeV` and `f_ρ^|| = 216 MeV`.

## Context
This work extends the proposal by El Beiyad et al. (2010) to study exclusive ρ⁰_T π⁺ photoproduction and subsequent studies of 3-body final states like γρ (Boussarie et al., 2016; Duplancic et al., 2023) and γπ (Duplancic et al., 2018, 2022; Crnkovic et al., 2025). It builds on the factorisation proof for 2→3 exclusive processes by Qiu et al. (2022) and addresses the breakdown of factorisation for channels allowing two-gluon exchanges, as discovered by Nabeebaccus et al. (2023, 2024). This work enables future phenomenological studies and provides a new family of processes for GPD extraction at facilities like JLab (CLAS12) and the EIC.
