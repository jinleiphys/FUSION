# D meson mass increase by restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter

> Spectral functions of the pseudoscalar $D$ meson in the nuclear medium are analyzed using QCD sum rules and the maximum entropy method. This approach enables us to extract the spectral functions without any phenomenological assumption, and thus to visualize in-medium modification of the spectral functions directly. It is found that the reduction of the chiral condensates of dimension 3 and 5 causes the masses of both $D^+$ and $D^-$ mesons to grow gradually at finite density. Additionally, we construct charge-conjugate-projected sum rules and find a $D^+$-$D^-$ mass splitting of about -15 MeV at nuclear saturation density.

## Key claim

The reduction of chiral condensates of dimension 3 and 5 causes the masses of both D+ and D- mesons to increase gradually at finite density, with a D+-D- mass splitting of about -15 MeV at nuclear saturation density; this result is obtained without assuming any specific functional form for the spectral function, unlike previous QCD sum rule studies that relied on a "pole + continuum" ansatz.

## Method

The authors applied QCD sum rules combined with the maximum entropy method (MEM) to extract spectral functions of pseudoscalar D mesons in nuclear matter, using Gaussian sum rules with two controllable parameters (ŝ and τ). They constructed charge-conjugate-projected sum rules to separate D+ and D- spectra, incorporating operator product expansion (OPE) terms up to dimension-5 condensates, including both q0-even and q0-odd contributions that break charge symmetry in the nuclear medium.

## Key numbers

- D meson peak position in vacuum: 1.74 GeV (consistent with experimental value of 1.87 GeV, with ~10% systematic error typical for QCD sum rules)
- D+ meson mass shift at nuclear saturation density ρ0: about +23 MeV
- D- meson mass shift at nuclear saturation density ρ0: about +38 MeV
- D+-D- mass splitting at ρ0: about -15 MeV (defined as mD+ - mD-)
- Gaussian parameter window used: 1.67 < ŝ < 3.21 GeV² and 0.50 < τ < 0.62 GeV⁴
- Charm quark pole mass: mc(μ=mc) = 1.67 ± 0.07 GeV
- πN sigma term used: σπN = 45 ± 15 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of D mesons in nuclear matter that gave inconsistent results: Hayashigaki (2000) found a -50 MeV mass shift at ρ0 using condensates up to dimension-4, while Hilger et al. (2008) obtained +45 MeV at ρ0 with dimension-5 condensates but with significant ambiguity from the phenomenological threshold parameter. The MEM approach removes uncertainties from assumed functional forms and threshold parameters. The results qualitatively agree with Hilger et al. (2008) for the average mass shift but give a smaller D+-D- splitting (-15 MeV versus -60 MeV). The mass increase is interpreted as a shift toward degeneracy of chiral partners (pseudoscalar D and scalar D0 mesons), with the D0 meson mass expected to decrease at finite density. The findings enable future experimental investigations at J-PARC, CBM, and PANDA facilities.
