# Three-body forces, single diffraction dissociation and shadow corrections to hadron-deuteron total cross-sections

> The relationships between the fundamental dynamics and diffraction phenomena in scattering from two-body composite system are discussed. A new simple formula for the shadow corrections to the total cross-section in scattering from deuteron has been derived and new scaling characteristics with a clear physical interpretation have been established. The effect of weakening the inelastic screening at super-high energies is theoretically discovered. A comparison with the experimental data on proton(antiproton)-deuteron total cross-sections has been performed. It is shown that there is quite a remarkable correspondence of the theory with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the total shadow correction to hadron-deuteron total cross sections can be decomposed into elastic and inelastic parts, each expressed through scaling functions with clear physical interpretation, and that the inelastic screening effect weakens at super-high energies, a result derived under the assumption of a specific Gaussian model for three-body forces.

## Method

The author uses a single-time formalism in quantum field theory to derive dynamic equations for scattering from a two-body bound state (deuteron). The total shadow correction is split into an elastic part (from elastic rescattering of the incident hadron on nucleons) and an inelastic part (from three-body forces related to inclusive multiparticle production). The inelastic shadow correction is linked to the total single diffractive dissociation cross section via a derived relation, and the model is fitted to antiproton-deuteron total cross section data using a single free parameter \(R_d^2\).

## Key numbers

- Fitted value for the deuteron size parameter: \(R_d^2 = 66.61 \pm 1.16\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) from antiproton-deuteron total cross section data.
- Fit parameters for the total single diffraction dissociation cross section formula: \(A_0 = 28.05 \pm 0.66\ \text{mb}\,\text{GeV}^{-2}\), \(A_2 = 4.99 \pm 0.57\ \text{mb}\,\text{GeV}^{-2}\).
- The inelastic structure function \(a^{inel}\) reaches its maximum value \(2/3\sqrt{3}\) at the point \(x^{max}_{inel} = \sqrt{2}\).
- The energy where the inelastic shadow correction has a maximum is calculated as \(\sqrt{s_m} = 9.01 \times 10^8\ \text{GeV} = 901\ \text{PeV}\).
- The fitted \(R_d^2\) satisfies \(R_d^2 = \frac{2}{3} r^2_{d,m}\) with the deuteron matter radius \(r_{d,m} = 1.963(4)\ \text{fm}\) (corresponding to \(r^2_{d,m} = 3.853\ \text{fm}^2 = 98.96\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\)).
- For a proton considered as a quark-diquark system, the equation \(R_0^2(s_p) = R_p^2\) gives \(\sqrt{s_p} = 1681\ \text{GeV}\) (Tevatron energy), and at \(\sqrt{s_0} = 20.74\ \text{GeV}\) one finds \(R_0(s_0) = 0.45\ \text{fm}\) (half the proton charge radius).

## Context

This work extends the Glauber theory of shadow corrections (Glauber, 1959) and the Gribov approach to inelastic screening (Gribov, 1969) by deriving a new formula for the total shadow correction that includes both elastic and inelastic parts expressed via scaling functions. It addresses the long-standing discrepancy between earlier Regge-based calculations (which overestimated inelastic shadow corrections) and experimental data, as discussed by Alberi and Baldracchini (1980). The formalism enables a connection between three-body forces and single diffraction dissociation, and suggests that at asymptotically high energies the hadron-deuteron total cross section reduces to twice the inelastic hadron-nucleon cross section, which the author interprets as supporting quark counting rules for nuclei.
