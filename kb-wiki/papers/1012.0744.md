# Abnormal dilepton yield from parity breaking in dense nuclear matter

> At finite density parity can be spontaneously broken in strong interactions with far reaching implications. In particular, a time-dependent pseudoscalar background would modify QED by adding a Chern-Simons term to the lagrangian. As a striking consequence we propose a novel explanation for the dilepton excess observed in heavy ion collisions at low invariant masses. The presence of local parity breaking due to a time-dependent pseudoscalar condensate substantially modifies the dispersion relation of photons and vector mesons propagating in such a medium, changing the $ρ$ spectral function and resulting in a potentially large excess of dileptons with respect to the predictions based in a `cocktail' of known processes.

## Key claim

Local parity breaking (LPB) due to a time-dependent pseudoscalar condensate in dense nuclear matter can explain the dilepton excess observed in heavy ion collisions at low invariant masses, specifically by modifying the dispersion relation of vector mesons and broadening their resonant contribution away from the nominal vacuum position.

## Method

The authors model QCD at finite baryon density using a generalized sigma model with two isomultiplets of scalars and pseudoscalars, coupled to chiral quark fields. They introduce a time-dependent pseudoscalar background field \(a(t)\) and define a 4-vector \(\zeta_\mu \simeq \partial_\mu a\), then add a parity-odd Chern-Simons term to the Lagrangian within the Vector Dominance Model (VDM) framework containing \(\rho_0\) and \(\omega\) mesons. The resulting modified dispersion relations for vector mesons are used to compute dilepton production rates, which are compared to NA60 experimental data for central heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- The Chern-Simons parameter \(\zeta\) is estimated to be \(\sim 1\) MeV, based on a pseudoscalar condensate formation time \(\tau = 1\) fm and condensate magnitude of order \(f_\pi\).
- For an isosinglet background, vector mesons acquire modified masses \(m^2_{V,\pm} \simeq m_V^2 \pm 360 \zeta |\vec k|\), where \(g_\omega \simeq g_\rho \equiv g \simeq 6\).
- The best fit to NA60 data uses \(\zeta = 1.9\) MeV and effective temperature \(T = 270\) MeV for central collisions.
- The \(\rho/\omega\) ratio needs to be enhanced by a factor 1.6 for NA60 and approximately 1.8 for PHENIX in semi-central and central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation (Andrianov et al., 2010) of spontaneous parity violation in QCD at finite baryon density, which differs from the older idea of pion condensation proposed by Migdal. The proposed mechanism offers an alternative to explanations based on dropping mass (Brown, 1991) or broadening resonance (van Hees and Rapp, 2006; Renk, 2004) that have struggled to account for the dilepton enhancement observed by CERES, PHENIX, and NA60 experiments. If confirmed, LPB would provide a new framework for understanding heavy ion collision data and could be tested through additional experimental signatures such as parity-violating resonance decays and modifications to the nuclear equation of state.
