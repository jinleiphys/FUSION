# Photoproduction $γp \to K^+Λ(1520)$ in an effective Lagrangian approach

> The data on differential cross sections and photon-beam asymmetries for the $γp \to K^+Λ(1520)$ reaction have been analyzed within a tree-level effective Lagrangian approach. In addition to the $t$-channel $K$ and $K^\ast$ exchanges, the $u$-channel $Λ$ exchange, the $s$-channel nucleon exchange, and the interaction current, a minimal number of nucleon resonances in the $s$ channel are introduced in constructing the reaction amplitudes to describe the data. The results show that the experimental data can be well reproduced by including either the $N(2060)5/2^-$ or the $N(2120)3/2^-$ resonance. In both cases, the contact term and the $K$ exchange are found to make significant contributions, while the contributions from the $K^\ast$ and $Λ$ exchanges are negligible in the former case and considerable in the latter case. Measurements of the data on target asymmetries are called on to further pin down the resonance contents and to clarify the roles of the $K^\ast$ and $Λ$ exchanges in this reaction.

## Key claim
The available differential cross-section and photon-beam-asymmetry data for $\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda(1520)$ can be well reproduced by including either the $N(2060)5/2^-$ or the $N(2120)3/2^-$ resonance in the $s$ channel, but the data cannot distinguish which of these two resonances is the correct one, and the roles of $K^\ast$ and $\Lambda$ exchanges differ between the two solutions.

## Method
The authors performed a tree-level effective Lagrangian analysis of the $\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda(1520)$ reaction, constructing amplitudes from $t$-channel $K$ and $K^\ast$ exchanges, $u$-channel $\Lambda$ exchange, $s$-channel nucleon and nucleon-resonance exchanges, and an interaction current modeled by a generalized contact term. Gauge invariance was maintained via an auxiliary current, and an interpolated Regge treatment was used for the $t$-channel $K$ and $K^\ast$ exchanges to smoothly connect low-energy Feynman amplitudes to high-energy Regge amplitudes. The model parameters were fitted to simultaneously describe differential cross-section data from CLAS and LEPS and photon-beam-asymmetry data from LEPS.

## Key numbers
- The threshold of $K^+\Lambda(1520)$ photoproduction is about 2.01 GeV.
- The coupling constant $g_{\Lambda^\ast NK} = 10.5$ was determined from the $\Lambda(1520) \to NK$ decay width of 7.079 MeV.
- The $\Lambda(1520) \to \Lambda\gamma$ radiative decay width is 0.133 MeV, used to constrain the $\gamma\Lambda\Lambda^\ast$ coupling constants.
- The coupling constant $g_{\gamma K K^{\ast}} = 0.413$ was calculated from the $K^\ast\to K\gamma$ radiative decay width.
- The coupling constant $g_{\Lambda NK} \approx -14$ was determined from flavor SU(3) symmetry using $g_{NN\pi}=13.46$.
- The anomalous magnetic moments used are $\kappa_p=1.793$ and $\kappa_n=-1.913$.
- The Regge trajectory slopes are 0.7 GeV$^{-2}$ for $K$ and 0.85 GeV$^{-2}$ for $K^\ast$.

## Context
This work extends previous effective Lagrangian studies of $\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda(1520)$ by Nam et al. (2005-2010), Xie et al. (2007-2014), He and Chen (2012-2014), and Yu and Kong (2017), which could describe differential cross-section data but failed to reproduce the photon-beam-asymmetry data reported by the LEPS Collaboration in 2010. The present combined analysis resolves the sign discrepancy in beam asymmetries that plagued earlier models and clarifies that either the $N(2060)5/2^-$ or $N(2120)3/2^-$ resonance is needed, while four other near-threshold resonances ($N(2000)5/2^+$, $N(2040)3/2^+$, $N(2100)1/2^+$, $N(2190)7/2^-$) are excluded. The work calls for target-asymmetry measurements to distinguish between the two viable resonance scenarios and to determine the importance of $K^\ast$ and $\Lambda$ exchanges.
