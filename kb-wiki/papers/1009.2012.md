# Multi-fermion states for heavy fermions bound via Higgs exchange

> A possibility to produce bound states of several heavy fermions, which are bound together due to the Higgs exchange, is examined. It is shown that for 12 fermions, 6 fermions and 6 antifermions, occupying the lowest S_{1/2} shell this bound state cannot exist if the fermion mass is below the critical value, which depends on the Higgs mass and is found to be restricted to 320 < m_{cr} < 410 Gev/c^2 for the Higgs mass in the interval 100 < m_{H}< 200 Gev/c^2. This estimate is derived in the relativistic mean field approximation. The corrections are estimated to be not able to reduce significantly the critical value for the fermion mass. Consequently there exist no bound state for 12 top quarks, and the only feasible hope to observe a bag of 12 fermions experimentally should rely on possible existence of heavy fermions of the next, fourth generations.

# Multi-fermion states for heavy fermions bound via Higgs exchange

## Key claim
The paper claims that a bound state of 12 fermions (6 fermions and 6 antifermions) occupying the lowest S_{1/2} shell cannot exist if the fermion mass is below a critical value that depends on the Higgs mass; for Higgs masses between 100 and 200 GeV/c^2, the critical fermion mass lies between 320 and 410 GeV/c^2, which is well above the top quark mass, making such a bound state impossible for top quarks.

## Method
The author employs a relativistic mean field approximation to solve the coupled Dirac and Higgs field equations for a spherically symmetric system of 12 fermions occupying the same S_{1/2} shell. The mean field equations are derived from the Standard Model Lagrangian in the unitary gauge, neglecting interactions with gluons, Z^0 and W bosons. Numerical solutions are obtained for the fermion wave functions (large and small Dirac components) and the Higgs field profile, from which the total energy and binding energy per fermion are computed as functions of the fermion mass.

## Key numbers
- Critical fermion mass for Higgs mass m_H = 100 GeV/c^2: m_cr = 324.2 GeV/c^2 (from Fig. 1, binding energy per fermion versus fermion mass)
- For Higgs mass in the interval 100 <= m_H <= 200 GeV/c^2, the critical fermion mass lies in the interval 320 < m_cr < 410 GeV/c^2
- Higgs vacuum expectation value v = 246 GeV
- The fermion mass range studied is 170 < m < 1000 GeV/c^2

## Context
This work extends the author's previous calculations (Kuchiev 2008) and those of Richard (2008) which indicated that 12 top quarks cannot form a bound state via Higgs exchange, contradicting claims by Froggatt and Nielsen (2008) that such a bound state exists and is strongly bound. The paper also addresses earlier work by Froggatt et al. that neglected the Higgs mass and used a simplified hydrogen-atom model. The results rule out bound states of top quarks and suggest that only hypothetical fourth-generation heavy fermions with masses above 320 GeV/c^2 could potentially form such multi-fermion bags. The author notes that radiative corrections studied by Dimopoulos (1990), Bagger (1991), Farhi (1998), and Crichigno (2009) are expected to be repulsive and thus cannot reduce the critical mass estimate.
