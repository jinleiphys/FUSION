# Hypermatter - Properties and Formation in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> The extension of the Periodic System into hitherto unexplored domains - antimatter and hypermatter - is discussed. Starting from an analysis of hyperon and single hypernuclear properties we investigate the structure of multi-hyperon objects (MEMOs) using an extended relativistic meson field theory. These are contrasted with multi-strange quark states (strangelets). Their production mechanism is studied for relativistic collisions of heavy ions from present day experiments at AGS and SPS to future opportunities at RHIC and LHC. It is pointed out that absolutely stable hypermatter is unlikely to be produced in heavy ion collisions. New attention should be focused on short lived metastable hyperclusters ($τ\propto 10^{-10}$s) and on intensity interferometry of multi-strange-baryon correlations.

# Hypermatter - Properties and Formation in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

Absolutely stable hypermatter is unlikely to be produced in heavy ion collisions, but short-lived metastable hyperclusters with lifetimes on the order of 10^{-10} seconds may exist and should be searched for using intensity interferometry of multi-strange-baryon correlations. The paper qualifies that even the most optimistic parameter sets for strangelets predict absolute stability only for baryon numbers A > 25, while lighter strange clusters are metastable.

## Method

The authors extended a relativistic mean field (RMF) meson-baryon Lagrangian, previously successful for normal nuclei, to include hyperons (Λ, Σ, Ξ, Ω) by fitting coupling constants to single-Λ hypernuclear data and SU(6) symmetry relations. A second model added scalar σ* (f0(975)) and vector φ (1020) meson fields to account for the attractive ΛΛ interaction. Strange quark matter (strangelets) was described using a bag model equation of state with first-order QCD corrections, and production mechanisms were studied via microscopic transport models (FRITIOF 7.02, RQMD, QMD) with phase-space coalescence for cluster formation.

## Key numbers

- The Λ potential depth in saturated nuclear matter is approximately U_Λ ≈ 1/2 U_N, with U_Λ ≈ -28 MeV relative to the nucleon potential
- Binding energies of light single-Λ hypernuclei (A < 16) are predicted within 10% of experimental values without adjusting free parameters
- The double-Λ hypernucleus ^6He_ΛΛ shows an enhanced interaction radius with Λ lifetime τ ≈ 10^{-10} s
- Pure hyperon clusters (Y) in model 2 have strangeness fraction f_s ≈ 1.7, charge fraction f_q ≈ -0.3, and binding energy per baryon E/A ≈ 3-8 MeV
- Superheavy hypernuclei with a ^180Th core and added strangeness reach binding energies E/A ≈ -20 MeV
- For strangelets in the bag model with B^{1/4}=145 MeV and m_s=150 MeV, the binding energy curve has a local minimum at baryon number A_B=6 with f_s=1.0
- Absolute stability for strangelets requires A > 25 even with the most optimistic parameter set
- At LHC energies (s^{1/2}=6500 AGeV), the probability for net-baryon number fluctuations larger than ±20 at midrapidity is about 15%
- The critical temperature for the chiral transition in the RMF model is T_C ≈ 170 MeV

## Context

This work extends the periodic system into the strangeness domain, building on prior studies of single hypernuclei by Boguta (1981) and Schaffner (1992, 1993) and on strange quark matter calculations by Chin and Kerman (1979) and Bjorken and McLerran (1979). The paper contrasts two forms of strange matter: metastable exotic multistrange objects (MEMOs) described by hadronic RMF theory, and strangelets described by quark bag models. It enables experimental searches at AGS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC for short-lived hyperclusters with cm-scale flight paths, and motivates intensity interferometry of multi-strange baryon correlations as a new observable. The authors note that previous experiments focusing on long flight paths and large masses may have missed the metastable clusters predicted here.
