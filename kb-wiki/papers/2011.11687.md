# A Comprehensive Study of the Three- and Four-Neutron Systems at Low Energies

> This work presents further analysis of the three- and four-neutron systems in the low energy regime using adiabatic hyperspherical methods. In our previous Phys. Rev. Lett. article (Phys. Rev. Lett. 125, 052501 (2020)), the low-energy behavior of these neutron systems was treated in the adiabatic approximation, neglecting the off-diagonal non-adiabatic couplings. A thorough analysis of the density of states through a multi-channel treatment of the three-and four-neutron scattering near the scattering continuum threshold is performed, showing no evidence of a 4n resonance at low energy. A detailed analysis of the long-range behavior of the lowest few adiabatic hyperspherical potentials shows there is an attractive $ρ^{-3}$ universal behavior which dominates in the low-energy regime of the multi-channel scattering. This long-range behavior leads to a divergent behavior of the density of state for $E\rightarrow0$ that could account for the low-energy signal observed in the 2016 experiment by Kisamori et al. (Phys. Rev. Lett. 116, 052501 (2016)).

## Key claim

The paper finds no evidence of a tetraneutron (4n) resonance at low energy; instead, the low-energy signal observed in the 2016 experiment by Kisamori et al. can be explained by a divergent behavior of the density of states near zero energy caused by an attractive universal $\rho^{-3}$ long-range behavior in the adiabatic hyperspherical potentials, not by a resonant state.

## Method

The authors solved the three-neutron (3n) and four-neutron (4n) systems using the adiabatic hyperspherical framework with a multi-channel treatment that includes off-diagonal non-adiabatic couplings, going beyond their earlier adiabatic approximation. They computed the adiabatic hyperspherical potentials and non-adiabatic coupling matrix elements using both an explicitly correlated Gaussian (CGHS) basis and a hyperspherical harmonic (HH) basis, with several nucleon-nucleon interaction models including AV18, AV8', and chiral effective field theory potentials (NVIa, NVIb). A multi-channel scattering analysis of the density of states near the scattering continuum threshold was performed using R-matrix propagation with the slow-variable discretization method.

## Key numbers

- The 2016 Kisamori et al. experiment reported a possible 4n resonance at an energy of $0.83\pm0.65(\mathrm{stat.})\pm1.25(\mathrm{sys.})~\mathrm{MeV}$ above the 4-body continuum.
- The singlet $s$-wave scattering length for the $nn$ system is approximately $a_s \approx -18.5$ fm, giving a ratio $|a_s/r_0| \approx 10$ relative to the range of the nuclear interaction.
- The asymptotic form of the lowest adiabatic potentials is $W_{\nu}(\rho) \rightarrow \frac{\hbar^2}{2\mu}\big(\frac{l_{\mathrm{eff}}(l_{\mathrm{eff}}+1)}{\rho^2}+\frac{C_{3,\nu}}{\rho^3}\big)$, where $C_{3,\nu}=C_{\nu}a$ with $a$ being the $s$-wave scattering length.
- The effective angular momentum quantum number is $l_{\mathrm{eff.}}=30$ for the lowest potential of the 4n system and $l_{\mathrm{eff.}}=5/2$ for the lowest 3n potential.
- The limit $C_\nu(\rho \rightarrow \infty)=C_\nu$ is reached only for $\rho>500$ fm.
- The tensor and spin-orbit interactions provide extra attraction at short-range hyperradii $0<\rho<5$ fm in the 4n system.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous adiabatic approximation study (Higgins et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 125, 052501, 2020) by including off-diagonal non-adiabatic couplings in a full multi-channel treatment. It contradicts the interpretation of the 2016 Kisamori et al. experiment (Phys. Rev. Lett. 116, 052501, 2016) that claimed evidence of a 4n resonance, instead attributing the observed signal to a threshold enhancement from universal $\rho^{-3}$ behavior. The study also builds on earlier theoretical work by Shirokov and Vary (2016), Gandolfi (2017), and Li, Michel, and Hu (2019) that suggested possible 4n states, while supporting the conclusions of Hiyama and Lazauskas (2016), Fossez, Deltuva (2018), and Gandolfi and Hammer (2019) that no bound or resonant 4n state exists with current nuclear models. The universal behavior observed connects to Efimov physics in atomic and nuclear three-body systems and to BCS-BEC crossover in few-fermion atomic systems.
