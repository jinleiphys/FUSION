# The QCD Mixed Phase: Shaken, not Stirred

> Baryon fluctuations exceeding Poisson expectations can signal a nearly first order phase transition at RHIC. We show how these fluctuations can be measured, and apply a dissipative-hydrodynamic formulation used in condensed matter physics to simulate their evolution.

# The QCD Mixed Phase: Shaken, not Stirred

## Key claim

Baryon number fluctuations exceeding Poisson expectations can signal a nearly first order phase transition at RHIC, but the effect is conditional on the expansion time scale relative to the droplet growth time scale; the super-Poissonian variance grows appreciably only when the system is driven into the unstable (spinodal) region by rapid longitudinal expansion, and the signal survives post-hadronization diffusion only if the ratio τ₀/τ_R is favorable.

## Method

The authors model the evolution of baryon density fluctuations in the QCD mixed phase using a Ginzburg-Landau free energy (model B in the condensed matter classification) with a conserved order parameter, adding a Bjorken longitudinal expansion term to describe nuclear collisions. They perform 2+1 dimensional numerical simulations of the dissipative diffusion equation with thermal noise, seeding phase separation at the time when the average density reaches the critical density, and compute the variance of baryon number in a rapidity interval from 5000 simulated events.

## Key numbers

- The baryon susceptibility χ at μ = 0 can increase suddenly near T_h ~ 150 MeV, where the chiral order parameter and energy density change sharply.
- The time scale for spinodal decomposition (droplet formation from runaway density fluctuations) is τ_R = 8ξ²/D, where ξ is the correlation length and D is the baryon diffusion coefficient.
- In the simulations, τ_R and ξ are each taken to be 1 fm, giving D ~ 8 fm, consistent with calculations in Prakash.
- The expanding system reaches the critical density ρ_c at τ₀ = 5 fm.
- The system is unstable only for τ < 2.3 τ₀ (i.e., τ < 11.5 fm), but fluctuations in rapidity survive well past the freezeout time of order 10–30 fm.
- The enhanced variance is computed from a sample of 5000 simulated events.

## Context

This work extends the argument by Gavin that baryon number conservation and rapid longitudinal expansion limit the erosion of fluctuations in a rapidity interval, and applies a dissipative-hydrodynamic formulation (model B of Bray) used in condensed matter physics to simulate phase separation. The authors note that convection, viscosity, and collision-geometry effects can reduce the observable Ω_p, and that the effect may be compensated to some extent by the plasma-versus-hadron-gas fluctuation difference discussed by Koch and Asakawa in the same proceedings. The exploratory calculations invite further work on experimental measurement of Ω_p = (V_p − ⟨N_p⟩)/⟨N_p⟩², which equals the total Ω_B for a range of thermal and Glauber models that respect isospin symmetry.
