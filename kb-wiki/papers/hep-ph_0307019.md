# Interpretation of the Theta+ as an isotensor pentaquark with weakly decaying partners

> The Theta+(1540), recently observed at LEPS, DIANA and CLAS, is hypothesized to be an isotensor resonance. This implies the existence of a multiplet where the Theta++, Theta+ and Theta0 have isospin-violating strong decays, and the Theta+++ and Theta- have weak decays and so are long-lived. Production mechanisms for these states are discussed. The J^P assignment of the Theta is most likely 1/2^- or 3/2^-.

# Interpretation of the Theta+ as an isotensor pentaquark with weakly decaying partners

## Key claim

The Theta+(1540) resonance, recently observed at LEPS, DIANA and CLAS, is hypothesized to be an isotensor (I=2) pentaquark rather than an isoscalar or isovector state, which would explain its anomalously narrow width because its strong decay to nK+ would be isospin-violating. The authors note that this interpretation depends on the precept that a pentaquark 110 MeV above threshold should have a decay width of order 500 MeV unless suppressed by symmetry, and they consider high-J assignments (J >= 5/2 for positive parity or J >= 7/2 for negative parity) unlikely for such a light state.

## Method

The authors estimate the isospin-violating decay width of an isotensor Theta+ by assuming isospin mixing coefficients in the Theta+ and nucleon wave functions of order epsilon = -0.015 (the known Lambda0-Sigma0 mixing coefficient), and multiplying the squared mixing coefficient by a typical isospin-conserving fall-apart decay width of 500 MeV. They then construct the full isotensor pentaquark multiplet (Theta---, Theta0, Theta+, Theta++, Theta+++) from the quark content uudd\bar{s} and its isospin partners, and identify which members can decay strongly (Theta0, Theta+, Theta++) via isospin-conserving modes and which must decay weakly (Theta--- and Theta+++). Production mechanisms for the weakly-decaying states are discussed for pp, K+p, and photonuclear reactions at various facilities.

## Key numbers

- Theta+ mass: 1.54 +/- 0.01 GeV (LEPS), 1539 +/- 2 MeV (DIANA), 1542 +/- 5 MeV (CLAS)
- Theta+ width upper limits: less than 25 MeV (LEPS), less than 9 MeV (DIANA), less than 22 MeV (CLAS)
- Estimated isospin-violating decay width upper bound for isotensor Theta+: 0.45 MeV, derived from 4 x (0.015)^2 x (500 MeV)
- Center-of-mass decay momentum for Theta+ -> nK+: p = 270 MeV
- Typical energy scale for hadron size: beta = 400 MeV
- Theta+ is approximately 190 MeV below the Delta K threshold
- Expected mass splitting within the Theta multiplet: less than 10 MeV, based on known splittings (n-p = 1.3 MeV, pi+/- - pi0 = 4.6 MeV, Delta0 - Delta++ = 2.3-2.7 MeV)
- K+ momentum for formation reaction: 440 MeV incident on Xe target at rest

## Context

This paper extends the chiral soliton model prediction by Diakonov, Petrov and Polyakov (1997) of a narrow isoscalar Z+ at 1530 MeV as the top member of an SU(3)f anti-decuplet, but proposes an alternative isotensor interpretation that avoids the width problems of the chiral soliton model (where the predicted 15 MeV width for Z+ would increase if the N(1710) width were adjusted to PDG values). The work contrasts with the isoscalar or isovector pentaquark interpretation of Stancu and Riska (2003) using a flavor-spin interaction from Goldstone boson exchange, which yields positive parity ground states. The isotensor assignment predicts the existence of the first triply charged hadron (Theta+++) and a negatively charged partner (Theta-), both weakly decaying and long-lived, with no analogue in known meson and baryon multiplets. The paper enables experimental searches for these exotic states at facilities including JLab, SPring-8, ITEP, JHF, COSY, Mainz, SAPHIR, and HERA.
