# NN scattering with NDelta coupling: Dibaryon resonances without "dibaryons"?

> It is known that at their threshold intermediate two-baryon NDelta states can produce resonance-like structures in some isospin one states, often interpreted as more exotic manifest six-quark states. This paper applies the coupled-channel method to study details of the NDelta effect in isospin one NN scattering with interactions constrained to exclude the influence of such extraneous hypothetical particles in two respects. Firstly, the calculated phase parameters are fitted below pion inelasticity, i.e. far below the region of "dibaryons". Secondly, the strength of the NN --> NDelta transition is constrained to agree with the pion production reaction NN --> dpi, fairly independent of the details of the pure "diagonal" NN potential and its effect practically as strong as the NN itself. The results may be considered as a necessary background for searches of additional dibaryon effects. Optimal conditions for resonant NDelta appearances involve a decrease of the orbital angular momentum in the transition NN --> NDelta, as is the case in the 1D2 and 3F3 waves. Also the effective NDelta threshold as well as its width are state dependent. Detailed complex phase shift results are presented for isospin one partial waves up to J=6, where NDelta excitation is still found to be comparable to or even larger than one pion exchange.

## Key claim
The paper argues that resonance-like structures observed in isospin-one NN scattering, often interpreted as exotic six-quark "dibaryons," can instead be explained by coupled-channel NDelta dynamics alone, provided the NN→NDelta transition strength is externally constrained by the pp→dπ⁺ reaction rather than fitted to NN scattering data. The author hedges that these results constitute a "necessary background" for searches of additional dibaryon effects, not a definitive exclusion of them.

## Method
The author solves coupled Schrödinger equations for NN and NDelta channels in isospin-one partial waves up to J=6, using a one-pion-exchange transition potential supplemented by rho exchange and dipole form factors with cutoff masses Λπ=1000 MeV and Λρ=1050 MeV. The NN→NDelta transition strength is fixed by reproducing the total cross section of pp→dπ⁺, and the diagonal NN potential is modified by repulsive Yukawa terms to avoid double-counting the attraction from iterated NDelta excitation, with phase shifts fitted only below pion inelasticity (below 300 MeV). The NDelta channel width is calculated self-consistently from the free Δ width using the momentum-space wave function, rather than treated as a free parameter.

## Key numbers
- Δ mass difference: ΔM = 274 MeV (real part of the Δ pole position)
- Pion coupling constants: f²/4π = 0.076 (πNN), f*²/4π = 0.35 (πNΔ from Δ width)
- pp→dπ⁺ total cross section peak: 3.148 mb at 575.2 MeV laboratory energy (interpolation gives 3.157 mb at 577 MeV); calculation yields 3.285 mb between 583 and 588 MeV
- Free Δ width formula: Γ(q) = 142 × (0.81 q/μ)³ / [1 + (0.81 q/μ)²] MeV
- Cutoff masses: Λπ = 1000 MeV, Λρ = 1050 MeV
- ¹D₂ wave: resonance-like behavior arises above 400 MeV due to coupling to ⁵S₂(NDelta); data fitted below 300 MeV
- ³F₃ wave: higher angular momentum NDelta states give minor attraction of 0.5–1.0 degrees above 500 MeV

## Context
This work extends the coupled-channel NDelta approach previously applied to pp→dπ⁺ (Niskanen, 1980s–2010s) into the inelastic NN scattering regime, contrasting with dibaryon interpretations such as the d'(2380) observed by WASA@COSY. It differs from earlier coupled-channel studies by VerWest (1980), Kloet and Tjon (1980s), and van Faassen and Kloet (1987) by using the pp→dπ⁺ reaction as an external constraint on the transition strength, and from the Bonn potential extensions by Elster et al. (1990s) by explicitly generating NDelta wave functions rather than only NN→NN amplitudes. The results provide a baseline against which any additional dibaryon contributions must be measured, and suggest that the ¹D₂ and ³F₃ resonances can be understood as threshold cusps from NDelta channels with decreased orbital angular momentum.
