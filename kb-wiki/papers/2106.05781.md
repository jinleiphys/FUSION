# $^{50}$Ti($d$,$p$)$^{51}$Ti, single neutron energies in the $N=29$ isotones and the $N=32$ subshell closure

> A measurement of the $^{50}$Ti($d$,$p$)$^{51}$Ti reaction at 16 MeV was performed using a Super Enge Split Pole Spectrograph to measure the magnitude of the $N=32$ subshell gap in Ti. Seven states were observed that had not been observed in previous ($d$,$p$) measurements, and the \textit{L} transfer values for six previously measured states were either changed or measured for the first time. The results were used to determine single neutron energies for the $p_{3/2}$, $p_{1/2}$ and $f_{5/2}$ orbitals. The resulting single neutron energies in $^{51}$Ti confirm the existence of the $N=32$ gap in Ti. These single neutron energies and those from previous measurements in $^{49}$Ca, $^{53}$Cr and $^{55}$Fe are compared to values from a covariant density functional theory calculation.

# $^{50}$Ti($d$,$p$)$^{51}$Ti, single neutron energies in the $N=29$ isotones and the $N=32$ subshell closure

## Key claim

The measurement confirms the existence of the $N=32$ subshell gap in Ti, but the extracted gap size of 1459(365) keV carries significant uncertainty because the spins of three high-lying $L=1$ states (4567, 5109, and 5231 keV) could not be determined, and the $f_{5/2}$ single neutron energy relies on the assumption that four $L=3$ states with unknown $J^\pi$ are $5/2^-$ rather than $7/2^-$.

## Method

A 16 MeV deuteron beam from the 9 MV Super FN Tandem Van de Graaff Accelerator at Florida State University was used to bombard a 0.45 mg/cm$^2$ $^{50}$Ti target enriched to 90%. Reaction protons were momentum-analyzed with a Super Enge Split Pole Spectrograph rotated from 10$^\circ$ to 50$^\circ$ in 5$^\circ$ increments. Angular distributions were fitted with FRESCO calculations using both adiabatic (ADW) and standard DWBA approaches to extract $L$ transfer values and spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

- $N=32$ subshell gap in $^{51}$Ti: 1459(365) keV, determined as the difference between $p_{3/2}$ and $p_{1/2}$ single neutron energy centroids
- $p_{3/2}$ single neutron binding energy: 5800(149) keV (centroid 572(149) keV above ground state, ground state binding energy 6372 keV)
- $p_{1/2}$ single neutron binding energy: 4341(216) keV (centroid 2031(216) keV above ground state)
- $f_{5/2}$ single neutron binding energy: 2629 keV (centroid 3743 keV above ground state, assuming all four high-lying $L=3$ states are $5/2^-$)
- $p_{3/2}-f_{5/2}$ energy difference in $^{51}$Ti: 3171(149) keV
- $p_{3/2}-p_{1/2}$ gap in $^{53}$Cr: 1488(326) keV
- $p_{3/2}-p_{1/2}$ gap in $^{55}$Fe: 358 keV (from existing data, considered anomalously small)
- $p_{3/2}-f_{5/2}$ energy difference in $^{53}$Cr: 1424(165) keV
- $f_{5/2}$ centroid in $^{55}$Fe: 1360 keV above ground state (779 keV above $p_{3/2}$ single neutron energy)
- Spectroscopic factor for ground state $p_{3/2}$ in $^{51}$Ti: 0.48(6) (DWBA) / 0.47(6) (ADW)
- Spectroscopic factor for 2144 keV $f_{5/2}$ state: 0.22(3) (DWBA) / 0.21(3) (ADW)

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of $N=32$ subshell closure along the $N=29$ isotonic chain, following the $^{48}$Ca($d,p$)$^{49}$Ca measurement by Uozumi et al. (1994) which established a 1.7 MeV $p_{3/2}-p_{1/2}$ gap in Ca. The results contradict the earlier $^{50}$Ti($d,p$)$^{51}$Ti measurement by Barnes et al. (1964), which reported a single strong $L=3$ state at 5139 keV; the present work instead finds $f_{5/2}$ strength distributed among multiple states and does not observe that state. The comparison with covariant density functional theory (FSUGarnet functional) supports the observed narrowing of the $p_{3/2}-f_{5/2}$ gap with increasing proton number but predicts a constant $p_{3/2}-p_{1/2}$ splitting, motivating a remeasurement of the $^{54}$Fe($d,p$)$^{55}$Fe reaction to resolve the anomalously small 358 keV spin-orbit splitting reported in existing data.
