# Fission Fragment Mass and Total Kinetic Energy Distributions of Spontaneously Fissioning Plutonium Isotopes

> The fission-fragment mass and total kinetic energy (TKE) distributions are evaluated in a quantum mechanical framework using elongation, mass asymmetry, neck degree of freedom as the relevant collective parameters in the Fourier shape parametrization recently developed by us. The potential energy surfaces (PES) are calculated within the macroscopic-microscopic model based on the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD), the Yukawa-folded (YF) single-particle potential and a monopole pairing force. The PES are presented and analysed in detail for even-even Plutonium isotopes with $A=236 -246$. They reveal deep asymmetric valleys. The fission-fragment mass and TKE distributions are obtained from the ground state of a collective Hamiltonian computed within the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, in the WKB approach by introducing a neck-dependent fission probability. The calculated mass and total kinetic energydistributions are found in good agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The calculated fission-fragment mass and total kinetic energy distributions for even-even plutonium isotopes (A=236-246) show good agreement with experimental data, but the theoretical approach remains "quite crude" due to the use of Wigner functions instead of true quantum mechanical density distributions, the absence of extremely elongated shapes, and the neglect of dynamical coupling between fission and perpendicular modes.

## Method

The potential energy surfaces were calculated within the macroscopic-microscopic model using the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) macroscopic part, a Yukawa-folded single-particle potential, and monopole pairing with BCS theory including approximate particle-number projection. The collective Hamiltonian was solved in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation using three Fourier deformation parameters (elongation q2, mass asymmetry q3, neck degree of freedom q4), with the fission probability obtained from a WKB approach incorporating a neck-size-dependent rupture probability. The mass and TKE yields were derived from the probability distribution of the collective wave function on the (q3, q4) plane near the scission configuration (q2 ≈ 2.3).

## Key numbers

- The pairing strength was set to G·N^(2/3) = 0.28 ħω0, with ħω0 = 41/A^(1/3) MeV, adjusted to experimental mass differences in the region.
- The Strutinsky shell-correction method used an 8th-order correctional polynomial with smearing width γS = 1.2 ħω0.
- The half-width parameter for the neck-rupture probability was fixed at d/R0 = 0.15, roughly corresponding to the nucleon radius.
- The zero-point energy parameter E0 in the Wigner function was taken as 1 MeV.
- The maximum of the TKE yield for 240Pu corresponds to TKE ≈ 179 MeV, in line with measured data from Wag.
- Experimental resolution values used for folding were σ_TKE = 10 MeV and σ_Af_h = 1.5 a.m.u.

## Context

This work extends the Fourier shape parametrization developed by Pomorski et al. (2015, 2017) to describe fission fragment distributions, building on the macroscopic-microscopic approach of Bohr and Wheeler (1939) and the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop model. The results are compared to spontaneous fission yields measured by Dematte (reference in text) and TKE data from Wag and OBER2017. The approach enables future calculations for low-energy fission of other isotopes and the development of a Langevin code for fission at higher energies using the new Fourier deformation space.
