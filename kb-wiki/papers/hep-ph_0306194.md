# The Proton Electromagnetic Form Factor $F_2$ and Quark Orbital Angular Momentum

> We analyze the proton electromagnetic form factor ratio $R(Q^{2})=QF_2(Q^{2})/F_1(Q^{2})$ as a function of momentum transfer $Q^{2}$ within perturbative QCD. We find that the prediction for $R(Q^{2})$ at large momentum transfer $Q$ depends on the exclusive quark wave functions, which are unknown. For a wide range of wave functions we find that $ QF_2/F_1 \sim\ const$ at large momentum transfer, in agreement with recent JLAB data.

# The Proton Electromagnetic Form Factor $F_2$ and Quark Orbital Angular Momentum

## Key claim

The ratio $R(Q^2) = QF_2(Q^2)/F_1(Q^2)$ can scale as a constant at large momentum transfer if the effective transverse separation between quarks remains constant rather than scaling as $1/Q$, but the actual scaling behavior cannot be determined model-independently because the exclusive quark wave functions are unknown non-perturbative objects.

## Method

The authors analyze the proton electromagnetic form factors within perturbative QCD using the Brodsky-Lepage factorization scheme, working in impact parameter space where transverse quark momenta are Fourier-conjugated to transverse separation coordinates ${\bf b_i}$. They decompose the proton wave function into Dirac bilinear covariants, including operators that depend on transverse coordinates and encode quark orbital angular momentum, and compute the scaling behavior of $F_2$ by examining the interplay between the $x$ and $b$ dependence in the wave functions using a Gaussian model and the COZ parameterization.

## Key numbers

- The ratio $R(Q^2) = QF_2/F_1$ scales as a constant at large $Q$ when the effective transverse separation $b \sim \text{constant}$, yielding $F_2 \sim 1/Q^5$ in that limit.
- If instead $b \sim 1/Q$ (the asymptotically short-distance region), then $F_2/F_1 \sim 1/Q^2$.
- For the pion form factor, the condition $\sqrt{xx'}Q b < 1$ defines the dominant integration region; assigning average values $x \sim 1/2$ for the pion or $x \sim 1/3$ for the proton converts this to $Q b < 1$.
- The moment $\langle b(Q) \rangle_\pi$ scales as constant when the wave function parameter $r < A$, where $A$ is the power of $b$ in the Gaussian model and $r$ controls the $\xi$ dependence near $\xi \rightarrow 1$.

## Context

This work addresses the puzzling JLAB measurement (Jones, Gayou, Perdrisat) showing $QF_2/F_1 \sim \text{constant}$ at large $Q$, which contradicts the asymptotically short-distance (asd) formalism of Brodsky and Lepage (1980) that predicts $F_2/F_1 \sim 1/Q^2$ and enforces hadron helicity conservation. The authors extend their earlier analysis (Jain 2003) and the work of Buniy and collaborators, showing that quark orbital angular momentum contributions cannot be ruled out by pQCD alone. The result enables a reinterpretation of form factor data as evidence for substantial quark orbital angular momentum in the proton, challenging the non-relativistic quark model assumption that disfavors such contributions.
