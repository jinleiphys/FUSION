# Revisited nuclear magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments of polonium isotopes

> We revisited the electronic structure parameters used to interpret the hyperfine structure of neutral polonium. We used a computational scheme that treats relativistic and high-order electronic correlation effects within the coupled cluster with single, double, triple and perturbative quadruple excitations CCSDT(Q) method, as well as estimate the contribution of quantum electrodynamics and finite nuclear size effects. A systematic study of the uncertainty is carried out. This allowed us to obtain significantly refined values for the nuclear magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments of a wide range of odd-mass polonium isotopes. For $^{205}$Po and $^{207}$Po we extracted both the magnetic moment and the nuclear magnetization distribution parameter in a nuclear model-independent way. To assess the accuracy of the calculations, we also computed the ionization potential (IP), excitation energies (EE) of the $6p^4~{}^1D_2$ and $6p^3 7s^1~{}^5S_2$ electronic states and the electronic $g_J$ factor in the same theoretical framework. A good agreement of the theory and experiment for IP, EEs and $g_J$ confirms the reliability of the computational scheme and uncertainty estimation for the Po electromagnetic moments. We identify the $6p^4~{}^1D_2$ electronic level as a potentially promising state for further studies of the nuclear moments of polonium isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper provides significantly refined values for nuclear magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments of odd-mass polonium isotopes by using high-level relativistic coupled cluster calculations, but the results for the magnetic moments of \(^{205}\)Po and \(^{207}\)Po are extracted in a nuclear model-independent way only by combining the computed electronic parameters with experimental hyperfine structure constants for two electronic states, not from a direct measurement.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic coupled cluster calculations up to the CCSDT(Q) level (single, double, triple, and perturbative quadruple excitations) using the Dirac-Coulomb Hamiltonian with the AAE4Z basis set extended with additional diffuse and tight functions. They computed the electronic parameters \(\tilde{A}_0\), \(\tilde{A}_{\text{BWel}}\), and \(\tilde{A}_{\text{QED}}\) that enter the parametrization of the magnetic dipole hyperfine constant \(A\), separating purely electronic effects from the nuclear magnetization distribution parameter \(B_s\). For \(^{205}\)Po and \(^{207}\)Po, where hyperfine constants were measured for two electronic states, the ratio of these constants was used to extract \(B_s\) and then the nuclear magnetic moment without assuming any nuclear magnetization model; for other isotopes, the magnetic moments were derived using the computed electronic parameters and the experimental hyperfine constants. The electric quadrupole moments were obtained from the measured electric quadrupole hyperfine constants \(B\) and the computed electric field gradient \(q\) at the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Ionization potential (IP) of neutral Po: calculated total 67878(107) cm\(^{-1}\), experiment 67896.310(44) cm\(^{-1}\) (Po_IPexp:2019).
- Excitation energy of the \(6p^4\;^1D_2\) state: calculated 21696(111) cm\(^{-1}\), experiment 21679.11(1) cm\(^{-1}\) (Charles:66).
- Excitation energy of the \(6p^3 7s^1\;^5S_2\) state: calculated 39053(102) cm\(^{-1}\), experiment 39081.19(1) cm\(^{-1}\) (Charles:66).
- Electronic \(g_J\) factor for the ground state \(6p^4\;^3P_2\): calculated 1.39637(59), experiment 1.39609(4) (axensten1961nuclear).
- Previously reported magnetic moments for \(^{205}\)Po and \(^{207}\)Po from nuclear magnetic resonance on oriented nuclei: 0.760(55) \(\mu_N\) and 0.793(55) \(\mu_N\), respectively (herzog1983nuclear).
- Previously reported spectroscopic quadrupole moment for \(^{207}\)Po: 0.28 b with only a lower limit of 10% uncertainty (olsmats1961hyperfine).

## Context

This work resolves the long-standing indeterminacy in the interpretation of hyperfine structure data for polonium isotopes, where previous semiempirical calculations (olsmats1961hyperfine) gave magnetic moments that were off by a factor of about three compared to direct NMR measurements (herzog1983nuclear). The refined moments enable more reliable tests of nuclear theory models for shape coexistence and deformation in the lead region (Seliverstov:2014_Po), and provide accurate input for studies of bound-state quantum electrodynamics in highly-charged ions (Shabaev:01a). The approach of separating electronic and nuclear magnetization effects, previously demonstrated for Ra\(^+\) (Skripnikov:2020e) and \(^{229}\)Th (Porsev:2021), is now extended to polonium, and the \(6p^4\;^1D_2\) electronic level is identified as a promising state for future nuclear moment studies.
