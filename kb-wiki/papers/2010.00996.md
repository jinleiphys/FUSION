# 3-Dimensional QCD Phase Diagrams for Strange Matter

> In this work, we examine in detail the difference between constraining the electric charge fraction and isospin fraction when calculating the deconfinement phase transition in the presence of net strangeness. We present relations among charge and isospin fractions and the corresponding chemical potentials and draw 3-dimensional QCD phase diagrams for matter out of weak equilibrium. Finally, we briefly discuss how our results can be applied to comparisons of matter created in heavy ion collisions and binary neutron star mergers.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that constraining the electric charge fraction versus the isospin fraction produces significantly different 3-dimensional QCD phase diagrams for matter with net strangeness, particularly when the system is not in weak equilibrium; the authors caution that comparisons between heavy-ion collision and hot astrophysical scenarios regarding the deconfinement phase transition must be interpreted carefully due to these differences.

## Method

The authors used the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) formalism, a non-linear realization of the SU(3) linear sigma model for hadronic matter that also includes quark contributions, to calculate the deconfinement phase transition. They varied the charge fraction between \(Y_Q = 0\) and \(0.5\) and the isospin fraction between \(Y_I = -0.5\) and \(0\), constructing 3-dimensional phase diagrams with temperature, baryon chemical potential (or free energy), and either charge or isospin fraction as axes, without imposing weak equilibrium. The hadronic sector was fitted to nuclear saturation properties, symmetry energy, and hyperon potentials, while the quark sector reproduces expectations from heavy-ion collisions and lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- The baryon chemical potential can change by up to 130 MeV at zero temperature on the hadronic side when varying the charge or isospin fraction by 0.5.
- The free energy can change by up to 50 MeV at zero temperature under the same variation.
- The charge or isospin chemical potential can change by up to 330 MeV at zero temperature on the hadronic side.
- Phase diagrams were calculated up to a temperature of \(T = 160\) MeV, around the critical temperature for the first order phase transition.
- The charge fraction was varied between \(Y_Q = 0\) and \(0.5\); the isospin fraction was varied between \(Y_I = -0.5\) and \(0\).

## Context

This work extends the 3-dimensional phase diagrams previously presented for non-strange matter by Aryal (2020) to include net strangeness, which is relevant for astrophysical processes occurring over timescales longer than weak interactions. It contrasts with typical 2-dimensional QCD phase diagrams that fix either the charge fraction at \(Y_Q = 0.3\) or the lepton fraction at \(Y_L = 0.4\), as done by Hempel (2013) and Roark (2018). The results enable more accurate comparisons between matter created in heavy-ion collisions and that found in binary neutron star mergers, where recent simulations by Most (2019) suggest that neutron star mergers cannot reach the high charge fractions typical of heavy-ion collisions.
