# Probing the hidden-bottom pentaquark resonances in photonuclear bottomonium production near threshold: differential observables

> We study the near-threshold $Υ(1S)$ meson photoproduction from protons and nuclei by considering incoherent direct non-resonant ($γp \to {Υ(1S)}p$, $γn \to {Υ(1S)}n$) and two-step resonant ($γp \to P^+_{bi} \to {Υ(1S)}p$, $γn \to P^0_{bi} \to {Υ(1S)}n$, $i=1$, 2, 3; $P^{+,0}_{b1}=P^{+,0}_{b}(11080)$, $P^{+,0}_{b2}=P^{+,0}_{b}(11125)$, $P^{+,0}_{b3}=P^{+,0}_{b}(11130)$) bottomonium production processes with the main goal of clarifying the possibility to observe the non-strange hidden-bottom pentaquark states $P^{+,0}_{bi}$ in this production via differential observables. We calculate the absolute excitation functions, energy and momentum distributions for the non-resonant, resonant and for the combined (non-resonant plus resonant) production of $Υ(1S)$ mesons on protons, on carbon and tungsten target nuclei at near-threshold incident photon beam energies by assuming the spin-parity assignments of the hypothetical hidden-bottom resonances $P^{+,0}_{b}(11080)$, $P^{+,0}_{b}(11125)$ and $P^{+,0}_{b}(11130)$ as $J^P=(1/2)^-$, $J^P=(1/2)^-$ and $J^P=(3/2)^-$ within four different realistic choices for the branching ratios of their decays to the $Υ(1S)p$ and $Υ(1S)n$ modes (0.125, 0.25, 0.5 and 1\%) as well as for two options for the background contribution. We demonstrate that the measurements of these combined observables on proton and nuclear targets in the near-threshold energy region in future experiments at the planned high-luminosity electron-ion colliders EIC and EicC in the US and China should provide evidence for the existence of the above hidden-bottom pentaquark resonances as well as clarify their decay rates.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that measuring differential observables (energy and momentum distributions) of Υ(1S) mesons from photonuclear production near threshold could reveal the existence of hidden-bottom pentaquark states P_b(11080), P_b(11125), and P_b(11130), but only if their branching ratios to Υ(1S)p(n) are sufficiently large, the authors previously found that resonant production dominates over non-resonant background only for branching ratios of about 2% or higher, whereas the present work explores smaller branching ratios (0.125–1%) and two background options.

## Method
The author calculates incoherent direct non-resonant (γp → Υ(1S)p, γn → Υ(1S)n) and two-step resonant (γp → P⁺_bi → Υ(1S)p, γn → P⁰_bi → Υ(1S)n) photoproduction of Υ(1S) mesons on protons, carbon, and tungsten targets. The calculations use a spectral function approach for nuclear targets, assuming spin-parity assignments Jᴾ = (1/2)⁻ for P_b(11080) and P_b(11125), and Jᴾ = (3/2)⁻ for P_b(11130), with four branching ratio scenarios (0.125, 0.25, 0.5, 1%) and two background parametrizations. The non-resonant cross section is based on a scaling relation to J/ψ photoproduction data from the GlueX experiment, with the Υ(1S) t-slope parameter fixed at approximately 2.2 GeV⁻².

## Key numbers
- The threshold photon energy for direct non-resonant Υ(1S) photoproduction on a free proton at rest is approximately 57.2 GeV.
- The resonant photon energies for producing P_b(11080), P_b(11125), and P_b(11130) on a free target proton at rest are 64.952 GeV, 65.484 GeV, and 65.544 GeV, respectively.
- At the incident photon energy of 65.484 GeV, the allowed laboratory momentum range for Υ(1S) mesons from the direct process is 48.594 to 63.774 GeV/c, and the allowed energy range is 49.507 to 64.472 GeV.
- The maximum laboratory polar production angles for Υ(1S) and the final proton at 65.484 GeV are 1.308° and 13.307°, respectively.
- The exponential t-slope parameter b_Υ(1S) is approximately 2.2 GeV⁻² in the photon energy range 57.2–68.8 GeV (center-of-mass energy 10.4–11.4 GeV).
- The non-resonant total cross section from the author's parametrization (based on GlueX data) is larger by factors of about 5 and 15 than predictions from the dipole Pomeron model and other parametrizations at energies around 11 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Paryev, 2023) of total cross sections for Υ(1S) photoproduction, which found that resonant pentaquark signals are only visible for branching ratios of about 2% or higher. The present paper provides differential observables (energy and momentum distributions) that could be more sensitive to smaller branching ratios (0.125–1%). The hidden-bottom pentaquark masses (11080, 11125, 11130 MeV) are predicted from a hadronic molecular approach by replacing charm with bottom quarks in the observed hidden-charm pentaquarks P_c(4312), P_c(4440), and P_c(4457) (Ref. [42] in the paper). The calculations are designed to guide future experiments at the planned electron-ion colliders EIC (US) and EicC (China).
