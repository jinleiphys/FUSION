# The QCD Pomeron in Ultraperipheral Heavy Ion Collisions: I. The Double J/Psi Production

> The contribution of the QCD Pomeron to the process AA -> AA J/Psi J/Psi is discussed. We focus on the photon-photon collision, with the quasi-real photon coming from the Weizsacker - Williams spectrum of the nuclei. We calculate the cross section for this process considering the solution of the LLA BFKL equation at zero momentum transfer using a small-t approximation for the differential cross section of the subprocess. Furthermore, the impact of non-leading corrections to the BFKL equation is also analyzed. In both cases the cross section is found to increase with the energy, predicting considerable values for the LHC energies. Moreover, we compare our results with the Born two-gluon approximation, which is energy independent at the photon level. Our results indicate that the experimental analyzes of this process can be useful to discriminate the QCD dynamics at high energies.

## Key claim

The cross section for double J/Psi production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions is enhanced by the QCD Pomeron, but the large next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections to the BFKL equation reduce the ratio between the BFKL and the two-gluon Born cross section to approximately 1.7 at LHC energies, making discrimination between the two mechanisms difficult without accurate measurements.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross section for AA → AA J/Psi J/Psi via photon-photon collisions, using the Weizsacker-Williams equivalent photon spectrum for the nuclei. They compute the gamma-gamma → J/Psi J/Psi subprocess cross section using the leading logarithmic approximation (LLA) BFKL solution at zero momentum transfer with a small-t approximation for the differential cross section, and also simulate NLO effects by modifying the Pomeron intercept to omega_Pom = 0.37. The total cross section for the heavy ion reaction is obtained by convolving the gamma-gamma cross section with the photon-photon luminosity from the Cahn (1990) parameterization.

## Key numbers

- The Born two-gluon exchange cross section for gamma-gamma → J/Psi J/Psi is 3.6 pb using running alpha_s and s_0 = 0.16 GeV^2.
- The LO BFKL effective behavior is W^(4*lambda) with lambda = 0.54; the NLO-modified BFKL gives lambda = 0.29.
- For PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5500 A GeV, the ratio between the LO BFKL prediction and the two-gluon Born cross section is approximately 40; with NLO corrections this ratio is reduced to approximately 1.7.
- Expected number of events per month (10^6 s) at LHC for PbPb: approximately 30 (Born), 50 (BFKL with NLO modifications), and 1100 (LO BFKL).
- The slope parameter B for double J/Psi production is estimated as B = m_c^(-2), with m_c = 1.55 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of QCD Pomeron dynamics from photon collider proposals (Kwiecinski and Motyka, 1998) to ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions, where the cross section is enhanced by the Z^4 factor from the nuclear charge. The results are compared with the Born two-gluon approximation (Ginzburg et al., 1987) and with the dipole-dipole approach of Donnachie, Dosch, and Rueter (1999), which predicts cross sections one to two orders of magnitude lower. The paper enables future experimental tests of BFKL dynamics at LHC, though it notes that NLO corrections may make discrimination from the Born level challenging.
