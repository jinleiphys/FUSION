# Study of transition form factors of the lightest pseudoscalars

> In this paper, we study the transition form factors of the lightest pseudoscalar mesons, $π^0$, $η$, and $η'$, within the framework of resonance chiral theory. Our analysis is performed based on the data of time-like and space-like singly-virtual and space-like doubly-virtual form factors, as well as the relevant cross sections and latest invariant mass spectra of $e^+e^-$ pair for the process of $P\toγe^+ e^-$. The transition form factors of these pseudoscalars are obtained. Also, we evaluate their contributions to the light-by-light part of the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. Our two Fits give similar results, where Fit-A gives $a_μ^{π^0 }=(61.6\pm 1.8)\times10^{-11}$, $a_μ^{η}=(15.2\pm1.7)\times10^{-11}$, $a_μ^{η'}=(16.0\pm 1.2)\times10^{-11}$, and the total contribution of neutral pseudo-scalar meson poles is $a_μ^{π^0+η+η'}=(92.8\pm2.9)\times10^{-11}$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that resonance chiral theory (RChT) with U(3) flavor symmetry, including heavier vector resonance multiplets and high-energy matching constraints, can simultaneously describe the transition form factors of π⁰, η, and η' across time-like and space-like regions, yielding a total neutral pseudoscalar pole contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment of aμ^(π⁰+η+η') = (92.8 ± 2.9) × 10⁻¹¹, with the caveat that the analysis is limited to energies up to approximately 2.3 GeV and relies on the δ-expansion to combine large-Nc and chiral counting.

## Method

The authors construct the transition form factors of π⁰, η, and η' within U(3) resonance chiral theory, including the Wess-Zumino-Witten term, vector meson propagators with ρ-ω mixing, and heavier vector resonance multiplets V' and V'' via extended Breit-Wigner propagators. They perform a combined fit to experimental data including time-like singly-virtual form factors from P → γ e⁺ e⁻ decays (A2, NA62, NA60, BESIII), e⁺ e⁻ annihilation cross sections (SND, CMD-2), space-like singly-virtual form factors (CELLO, CLEO, BaBar, Belle, BESIII), space-like doubly-virtual form factors (BaBar for η'), and lattice QCD results (BMW, ETM). Unknown couplings are reduced by matching to QCD at high energies and to the Brodsky-Lepage limit, and the space-like form factors are extended to high energies by matching to pQCD.

## Key numbers

- Total neutral pseudoscalar pole contribution to aμ^(HLbL): aμ^(π⁰+η+η') = (92.8 ± 2.9) × 10⁻¹¹ (Fit-A)
- Pion-pole contribution: aμ^(π⁰) = (61.6 ± 1.8) × 10⁻¹¹ (Fit-A)
- Eta-pole contribution: aμ^(η) = (15.2 ± 1.7) × 10⁻¹¹ (Fit-A)
- Eta-prime-pole contribution: aμ^(η') = (16.0 ± 1.2) × 10⁻¹¹ (Fit-A)
- The analysis covers the energy region from threshold up to approximately √s ≈ 2.3 GeV for time-like form factors
- The U_A(1) anomaly parameter is fixed at M₀ = 900 MeV
- The pion decay constant is taken as F ≈ 92.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous SU(3) RChT analyses by Wang et al. (2023) to U(3) flavor symmetry, enabling proper treatment of η-η' mixing and including the ninth Goldstone boson. It incorporates the latest experimental data from BESIII (2024) for η and η' Dalitz decays with NLO radiative corrections, and new SND (2024) measurements of e⁺ e⁻ → η' γ cross sections. The results enable improved calculations of the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon g-2, complementing lattice QCD studies by BMW (2023) and ETM (2022, 2023) and dispersion relation approaches by Colangelo et al. (2014) and Hoferichter et al. (2018).
