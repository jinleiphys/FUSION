# Gluon-mediated inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering from Regge to Bjorken kinematics

> We revisit high energy factorization for gluon mediated inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) for which we propose a new semi-classical approach that accounts systematically for the longitudinal extent of the target in contrast with the shockwave limit. In this framework, based on a partial twist expansion, we derive a factorization formula that involves a new gauge invariant unintegrated gluon distribution which depends explicitly on the Feynman $x$ variable. It is shown that both the Regge and Bjorken limits are recovered in this approach. We reproduce in particular the full one loop inclusive DIS cross-section in the leading twist approximation and the all-twist dipole factorization formula in the strict $x=0$ limit. Although quantum evolution is not discussed explicitly in this work, we argue that the proper treatment of the $x$ dependence of the gluon distribution encompasses the kinematic constraint that must be imposed on the phase-space of gluon fluctuations in the target to ensure stability of small-$x$ evolution.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new semi-classical approach for gluon-mediated inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering that systematically accounts for the longitudinal extent of the target, going beyond the shockwave approximation, and they show that both the Regge and Bjorken limits are recovered within this framework, though these two limits do not commute in the semi-classical descriptions of the Regge regime.

## Method

The authors develop a background field method for high energy scattering where the target gluon field retains a generic dependence on light cone time \(x^+\), in contrast to the standard shockwave approximation where the field is assumed to be peaked around \(x^+=0\). They derive a scalar propagator satisfying a Klein-Gordon equation in a potential, which is then used to compute the hadronic tensor for gluon-mediated inclusive DIS via the optical theorem. The cross-section is factorized using a partial twist expansion in powers of \(x_{\rm Bj}/Q^2\), yielding a new gauge invariant unintegrated gluon distribution with explicit dependence on the Feynman \(x\) variable.

## Key numbers

- The Bjorken variable is defined as \(x_{\rm Bj} = Q^2/(2P\cdot q)\), with \(Q^2 = -q^2\) and \(s = (P+q)^2\).
- The longitudinal polarization vector is \(\varepsilon_L = (1/Q)(q^+ n_1 + (Q^2/(2q^+)) n_2)\).
- The transverse polarization sum satisfies \(\sum_{\lambda=1\ldots d} \varepsilon^\mu_\lambda \varepsilon^{\ast\nu}_\lambda = -g_\perp^{\mu\nu}\) in \(D = d+2 = 4+2\epsilon\) dimensions.
- The one-loop inclusive DIS cross-section is reproduced in the leading twist approximation, and the all-twist dipole factorization formula is recovered in the strict \(x=0\) limit.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory framework by relaxing the shockwave approximation, which assumes the target field is peaked around \(x^+=0\) due to Lorentz contraction. The approach addresses instability issues at Next-to-Leading Logarithmic (NLL) accuracy previously observed in BFKL and BK evolution equations (notably by Fadin and Lipatov (1998) and Ciafaloni and Camici (1998)), where the kernel appeared more singular than its Leading-Logarithmic part in collinear and anti-collinear regions. The new factorization formula enables a systematic treatment of the collinear regions and provides a top-down understanding of how the \(Q \rightarrow \infty\) limit of the Regge result compares to the \(x_{\rm Bj}\rightarrow 0\) limit of the Bjorken result, with implications for stabilizing small-\(x\) evolution.
