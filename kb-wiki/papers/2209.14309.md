# Symmetry energy in holographic QCD

> We study the symmetry energy (SE), an important quantity in nuclear physics, in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model and in a much simpler hard-wall model of holographic QCD. The SE is the energy contribution to the nucleus due to having an unequal number of neutrons and protons. Using a homogeneous Ansatz representing smeared instantons and quantizing their isospin, we extract the SE and the proton fraction assuming charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium, using quantization of the isospin zeromode. We also show the equivalence between our method adapted from solitons and the usual way of the isospin controlled by a chemical potential at the holographic boundary. We find that the SE can be well described in the WSS model if we allow for a larger 't Hooft coupling and lower Kaluza-Klein scale than is normally used in phenomenological fits.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy (SE) in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto (WSS) model can be brought into agreement with phenomenological constraints if one allows a larger 't Hooft coupling and lower Kaluza-Klein scale than the standard mesonic fit values, rather than using the phenomenologically fitted parameters that overestimate the SE by about a factor of 2.4.

## Method

The authors use a homogeneous Ansatz representing smeared instantons in both the WSS model and a hard-wall (HW) model of holographic QCD, then quantize the isospin zero-mode to extract the symmetry energy as a function of baryon density. They solve the coupled ordinary differential equations for the gauge fields in the holographic coordinate, imposing boundary conditions that relate the infrared value of the field H(z) to the baryon density, and compute the symmetry energy from the moment of inertia obtained via canonical quantization of the SU(2) collective coordinate.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation density in WSS model with standard fit (λ=16.63, M_KK=949 MeV): S_0 = 74.9 × (M_KK/949 MeV) MeV
- Slope parameter L in WSS with standard fit: L = 113.3 × (M_KK/949 MeV) MeV
- Curvature parameter K_sym in WSS with standard fit: K_sym = -35.9 × (M_KK/949 MeV) MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation density in HW model (M_5 = N_c/(12π^2), L^{-1}=150 MeV): S_0 = 70.4 × (L^{-1}/150 MeV) MeV
- Slope parameter L in HW model: L = 132.5 × (L^{-1}/150 MeV) MeV
- Curvature parameter K_sym in HW model: K_sym = -218.8 × (L^{-1}/150 MeV) MeV
- Saturation density in WSS model with λ=16.63: ρ_0 = 0.436 × (M_KK/949 MeV)^3 fm^{-3} (about 2.9 times too large)
- Saturation density in HW model: ρ_0 = 0.183 × (L^{-1}/150 MeV)^3 fm^{-3} (about 22% too large)

## Context

This work extends previous attempts to extract the symmetry energy from holographic QCD models, particularly the WSS model studied by Kovensky (2021) which found a symmetry energy that was too large. The authors improve upon that calculation by including the Abelian field component (which dominates the small-λ behavior) and by choosing a different N_c scaling for nucleon states that preserves the Gell-Mann-Nishijima formula. The method is equivalent to the chemical potential approach used by Kovensky (2021) but offers advantages in identifying the correct Chern-Simons action and resolving ambiguities from boundary terms. The results enable more reliable predictions for neutron star properties from holographic equations of state, building on the neutron star work of Bartolini (2022).
