# Quark Distributions of Octet Baryons from SU(3) Symmetry

> SU(3) symmetry relations between the octet baryons are introduced in order to connect both the unpolarized and polarized quark distributions of the octet baryons with those of the nucleon. Two different parametrizations of the nucleon quark distributions are used. A new scenario of quark flavor and spin structure of the $Λ$ is found and compared with two other models: a perturbative QCD based analysis and a quark diquark model. The $u$ and $d$ quarks inside the $Λ$ are predicted to be positively polarized at large Bjorken variable $x$ in the new scenario. By using an approximate relation connecting the quark fragmentation functions with the quark distributions, the hadron polarizations of the octet baryons in $e^+e^-$-annihilation, polarized charged lepton deep inelastic scattering (DIS) processes, and neutrino (antineutrino) DIS processes are predicted. The predictions for $Λ$ polarizations in several processes are compatible with the available data at large fragmentation momentum fraction $z$, and support the prediction of positively polarized $u$ and $d$ quarks inside the $Λ$ at large $x$. Predictions for Drell-Yan processes from $Σ^{\pm}$ and $Ξ^-$ beams on an isoscalar target are also given and discussed.

## Key claim

SU(3) symmetry relations can connect both unpolarized and polarized quark distributions of all octet baryons to those of the nucleon, but the resulting flavor structure of the Lambda hyperon at large Bjorken x depends on whether SU(3) symmetry is assumed or broken, and the predictions are only reliable at large x where valence quark structure dominates.

## Method

The authors applied SU(3) flavor symmetry relations to map the known unpolarized and polarized quark distributions of the nucleon onto the other octet baryons (Lambda, Sigma, Xi), using two different parametrization sets for the nucleon distributions (Set-1 from GRV/GRSV and Set-2 from MRST/GS). They then used the Gribov-Lipatov relation as an approximate connection between quark distributions and fragmentation functions near z approaching 1 to predict baryon polarizations in e+e- annihilation, polarized charged lepton DIS, and neutrino/antineutrino DIS processes. For the Sigma and Xi hyperons, they directly computed Drell-Yan cross section ratios using the SU(3)-derived quark distributions.

## Key numbers

- The ratio u^Lambda(x)/s^Lambda(x) approaches 0.25 as x goes to 1 under SU(3) symmetry, compared to 0 in the quark-diquark model and 0.5 in the perturbative QCD analysis
- The ratio d_v/u_v approaches 0 as x goes to 1, consistent with the quark-diquark model prediction
- Delta u(x)/u(x) is less than 0.8 as x goes to 1 in both parametrization sets, differing from the perturbative QCD prediction of 1
- Delta d(x)/d(x) lies in the range [-0.4, -0.2] as x goes to 1 for both sets, roughly consistent with the quark-diquark model prediction of -1/3
- Predictions for Lambda polarizations in e+e- annihilation at LEP I (sqrt(s) approximately 91 GeV) and LEP II (sqrt(s) approximately 200 GeV) are compatible with available data from ALEPH (1996), DELPHI (1995), and OPAL (1997)
- Predictions for Lambda polarization in neutrino DIS are compatible with NOMAD collaboration data at large fragmentation momentum fraction z

## Context

This work extends SU(3) symmetry relations previously applied to valence quarks (Ma et al., 2000) to include sea quark distributions for all octet baryons, and provides a systematic comparison with two existing models: the perturbative QCD based counting rule analysis and the quark spectator diquark model. The predictions enable experimental tests of SU(3) symmetry in baryon structure through Drell-Yan processes with Sigma and Xi beams on isoscalar targets, which can directly probe the sea quark content of hyperons and distinguish between different theoretical scenarios for the flavor and helicity structure at large x.
