# Two neutrino double $β$ decay of 94v$\leq A\leq $110 nuclei for $0^+ \to 0^+$ transition

> The two neutrino double beta decay of $ ^{94,96}$Zr$,^{98,100}$Mo$,^{104}$% Ru and $ ^{110}$Pd nuclei for $0^{+}\to 0^{+}$ transition is studied in the PHFB model in conjunction with the summation method. In the first step, the reliability of the intrinsic wave functions has been established by obtaining an overall agreement between a number of theoretically calculated spectroscopic properties and the available experimental data for $ ^{94,96}$% Zr$,^{94,96,98,100}$Mo, $^{98,100,104}$Ru, $^{104,110}$Pd and $^{110}$Cd isotopes. Subsequently, the PHFB wave functions of the above mentioned nuclei are employed to calculate the nuclear transition matrix elements $M_{2ν}$ as well as half-lives $T{_{1/2}^{2ν}}$. Further, we have studied the effects of deformation on the $M_{2ν}$.

## Key claim

The Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) model, using a pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole (PPQQ) interaction and the summation method (avoiding the closure approximation), can reproduce the experimentally observed half-lives of two-neutrino double beta decay for nuclei in the mass range 94 ≤ A ≤ 110, provided the intrinsic wave functions are first validated against spectroscopic properties such as yrast spectra, B(E2) values, quadrupole moments, and g-factors.

## Method

The authors employed the PHFB model with a PPQQ effective two-body interaction, treating the doubly even nucleus ⁷⁶Sr as an inert core with a valence space spanning seven orbits (1p₁/₂, 2s₁/₂, 1d₃/₂, 1d₅/₂, 0g₇/₂, 0g₉/₂, and 0h₁₁/₂) for both protons and neutrons. The strength of the proton-neutron component of the QQ interaction (χ_pn) was varied to reproduce the experimental 2⁺ excitation energies of the nuclei of interest, after which the HFB wave functions were used to calculate nuclear transition matrix elements M₂ν and half-lives T₁/₂²ν via the summation method. The role of deformation on M₂ν was studied by varying the strength of the QQ interaction through an arbitrary parameter χ_qq.

## Key numbers

- For ⁹⁴Zr → ⁹⁴Mo: predicted half-life T₁/₂²ν = 7.51 × 10²² yr (g_A = 1.25) and 1.834 × 10²³ yr (g_A = 1.0); experimental lower limit is > 1.1 × 10¹⁷ yr (Arnold, 1999)
- For ⁹⁶Zr: predicted half-life favors experimental values of NEMO and Wieser et al. (2001) for g_A = 1.25
- For ⁹⁸Mo → ⁹⁸Ru: predicted half-life T₁/₂²ν = 1.49 × 10³⁰ yr (g_A = 1.0)
- For ¹⁰⁰Mo: predicted M₂ν and T₁/₂²ν for g_A = 1.25 favor results from UC-Irvine (Elliott et al., 1991), ELEGANTS V (1991), LBL (1993), NEMO (1995), LBL collaboration (1997), and ITEP+INFN (2001)
- For ¹⁰⁴Ru → ¹⁰⁴Pd: predicted half-life T₁/₂²ν = 5.73 × 10²² yr (g_A = 1.0)
- For ¹¹⁰Pd → ¹¹⁰Cd: predicted half-life T₁/₂²ν = 1.41 × 10²⁰ yr (g_A = 1.25) and 3.44 × 10²⁰ yr (g_A = 1.0); experimental lower limit is > 6.0 × 10¹⁶ yr (Winter, 1952)
- Phase space factors G₂ν used: 2.304 × 10⁻²¹ yr⁻¹ (⁹⁴Zr), 1.927 × 10⁻¹⁷ yr⁻¹ (⁹⁶Zr), 9.709 × 10⁻²⁹ yr⁻¹ (⁹⁸Mo), 9.434 × 10⁻¹⁸ yr⁻¹ (¹⁰⁰Mo), 9.174 × 10⁻²¹ yr⁻¹ (¹⁰⁴Ru), and 3.984 × 10⁻¹⁹ yr⁻¹ (¹¹⁰Pd)

## Context

This work extends previous PHFB calculations on ¹⁰⁰Mo (Dixit et al., 2002; Chandra et al., 2003) to a broader set of nuclei in the A ≈ 100 mass region, using the summation method instead of the closure approximation. The study builds on the observation by Vogel and Zirnbauer (1986) that QRPA can explain the suppression of M₂ν, and addresses the need for models that incorporate both pairing and deformation on equal footing, as suggested by earlier work on ¹⁰⁰Mo and ¹⁵⁰Nd (Griffith et al., 1992; Suhonen et al., 1994). The results enable comparisons with QRPA calculations (Staudt et al., 1990; Engel et al., 1988), the operator expansion method (Hirsch et al., 1994), and the single state dominance hypothesis (Civitarese et al., 1998; Semenov et al., 2000), and provide predictions for nuclei such as ⁹⁸Mo and ¹⁰⁴Ru where no experimental half-lives are yet available.
