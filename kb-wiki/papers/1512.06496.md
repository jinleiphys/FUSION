# An estimate of the bulk viscosity of the hadronic medium

> The bulk viscosity ($ζ$) of the hadronic medium has been estimated within the ambit of the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model including the Hagedorn density of states. The HRG thermodynamics within a grand canonical ensemble provides the mean hadron number as well as its fluctuation. The fluctuation in the chemical composition of the hadronic medium in the grand canonical ensemble can result in non-zero divergence of the hadronic fluid flow velocity, allowing us to estimate the $ζ$ of the hadronic matter upto a relaxation time. We study the influence of the hadronic spectrum on $ζ$ and find its correlation with the conformal symmetry breaking (CSB) measure, $ε-3P$. We estimate $ζ$ along the contours with constant, $S/N_B$ (total entropy/net baryon number) in the $T-μ$ plane (temperature-baryonic chemical potential) for $S/N_B=30, 45$ and 300. We also assess the value of $ζ$ on the chemical freezeout curve for various centre of mass energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$) and find that the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio, $ζ/s$ is larger at FAIR than LHC energies.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of the hadronic medium is estimated within the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model up to a relaxation time, assuming a constant cross section for all hadronic processes and a single average relaxation time for the system, because the required microscopic cross sections for all hadronic reactions are not known.

## Method

The bulk viscosity is derived from the fluctuation in chemical composition of the hadronic medium in the grand canonical ensemble, which leads to a non-zero divergence of the fluid flow velocity. The ratio ζ/τ_R is computed using thermodynamic derivatives of pressure, energy density, number density, and entropy density for each hadron species, summed over all particles with masses up to 2.5 GeV from the Particle Data Book. The calculation is extended by including the Hagedorn density of states (HDS) with parameters a0=0.744 GeV^(3/2), m0=0.529 GeV, and TH=180 MeV, and by incorporating a temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent relaxation time estimated from a constant cross section σ=π fm^2.

## Key numbers

- The bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio ζ/s is larger at FAIR than at LHC energies, as evaluated along the chemical freeze-out curve parameterized by T(μ)=0.166-0.139μ^2-0.053μ^4 and μ=1.308(1+0.273√s_NN)^(-1).
- For a single-particle system, ζ/τ_R ~ (m/T)^2 in the limit m/T << 1 and ζ/τ_R ~ e^(-(m-μ)/T) for m/T >> 1.
- The conformal symmetry breaking measure (ε-3P)/T^4 peaks at T/m_l ~ 0.5 for a single species, and for a two-species system with m_h/m_l=10, distinct peaks appear at T/m_l ~ 0.5 and T/m_l ~ 5.
- The normalized bulk viscosity R_ζ is evaluated along constant S/N_B contours for S/N_B = 30, 45, and 300, corresponding to AGS/FAIR, SPS, and RHIC collision conditions respectively.
- The relaxation time τ_R decreases with temperature and chemical potential; τ_R^(-1) ∝ T^2 e^(-m/T) for constant cross sections.

## Context

This work extends prior estimates of bulk viscosity in hadronic matter using the HRG model, comparing with results from the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation with excluded volume effects (Guru) and the Chapman-Enskog approximation for interacting pion gas (Sukanya). The inclusion of the Hagedorn density of states improves agreement with lattice QCD data for the conformal symmetry breaking measure up to T ~ 1.1 m_π. The results indicate that bulk viscosity plays a more important role at lower beam energies (FAIR) than at LHC energies, enabling improved hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions across different collision energies.
