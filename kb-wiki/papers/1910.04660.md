# New solutions of viscous relativistic hydrodynamics

> Relativistic hydrodynamics represents a powerful tool to investigate the time evolution of the strongly interacting quark gluon plasma created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. The equations are solved often numerically, and numerous analytic solutions also exist. However, the inclusion of viscous effects in exact, analytic solutions has received less attention. Here we utilize Hubble flow to investigate the role of bulk viscosity, and present different classes of exact, analytic solutions valid also in the presence of dissipative effects.

## Key claim
The paper presents exact, analytic solutions of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics under Hubble flow that include bulk viscosity, but only for specific assumptions about the temperature or density dependence of the bulk viscosity coefficient; two of the five solution classes are unphysical because they produce diverging temperatures rather than cooling.

## Method
The authors impose Hubble flow (four-velocity \(u^\mu = x^\mu/\tau\)) on the first-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics equations in the Eckart frame, which causes shear viscosity and thermal conductivity terms to cancel. They then solve the resulting ordinary differential equation for pressure as a function of proper time \(\tau\) under five different combinations of assumptions about the equation of state (with or without a conserved charge) and the functional form of the bulk viscosity coefficient \(\zeta\) (constant, proportional to entropy density \(s\), or proportional to conserved charge density \(n\)).

## Key numbers
- For cases A and B (constant \(\zeta\)): \(p(\tau) = \left[p_0 - \frac{d^2}{(\kappa+1)d - \kappa}\frac{\zeta_0}{\tau_0}\right]\left(\frac{\tau_0}{\tau}\right)^{d\frac{\kappa+1}{\kappa}} + \frac{d^2}{(\kappa+1)d - \kappa}\frac{\zeta_0}{\tau}\), where \(d\) is the number of spatial dimensions and \(\kappa\) is the EoS parameter.
- For case C (no conserved charge, \(\zeta \propto s\)): when \(\kappa = d\), \(p(\tau) = p_0\left[1 + \frac{\kappa}{\kappa+1}\frac{\zeta_0}{p_0\tau_0}\ln\frac{\tau}{\tau_0}\right]\left(\frac{\tau_0}{\tau}\right)^{\kappa+1}\).
- For case D (conserved charge, \(\zeta/n =\) const): when \(\kappa = d\), \(p(\tau) = p_0\left[1 + \frac{\zeta_0\kappa}{p_0\tau_0}\ln\frac{\tau}{\tau_0}\right]\left(\frac{\tau_0}{\tau}\right)^{\kappa+1}\).
- For case E (conserved charge, \(\zeta \propto s\)): when \(\kappa = d\), \(p(\tau) = p_0\left(\frac{\tau_0}{\tau}\right)^{\kappa+1}\left\{1 - \kappa(\kappa-1)\frac{\zeta_0}{p_0\tau_0}\ln\frac{\tau}{\tau_0}\right\}^{-\frac{1}{\kappa-1}}\).
- Cases B and E are unphysical: in case B the temperature diverges because \(p = nT\) and \(n\) decreases faster than \(p\); in case E the pressure diverges if \(d > \kappa\) or if \(\frac{d^2(\kappa-1)}{\kappa-d}\frac{\zeta_0}{p_0\tau_0} > 1\).

## Context
This work extends the family of exact analytic solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics beyond the perfect-fluid case, which previously included the Landau-Khalatnikov solution (Khalatnikov, 1954) and the Hwa-Bjorken solution (Hwa, 1974; Bjorken, 1982). By focusing on Hubble flow, which was shown by Csorgo et al. (2003) to be compatible with many heavy-ion observables, the authors isolate the effects of bulk viscosity from shear viscosity and thermal conductivity. The new solutions enable analytic studies of how bulk viscosity modifies the cooling of the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions, and can serve as testbeds for numerical simulations.
