# $Λ^+_c$- and $Λ_b$-hypernuclei

> $Λ^+_c$- and $Λ_b$-hypernuclei are studied in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. Comparisons are made with the results for $Λ$-hypernuclei studied in the same model previously. Although the scalar and vector potentials felt by the $Λ$, $Λ_c^+$ and $Λ_b$ in the corresponding hypernuclei multiplet which has the same baryon numbers are quite similar, the wave functions obtained, e.g., for $1s_{1/2}$ state, are very different. The $Λ^+_c$ baryon density distribution in $^{209}_{Λ^+_c}$Pb is much more pushed away from the center than that for the $Λ$ in $^{209}_Λ$Pb due to the Coulomb force. On the contrary, the $Λ_b$ baryon density distributions in $Λ_b$-hypernuclei are much larger near the origin than those for the $Λ$ in the corresponding $Λ$-hypernuclei due to its heavy mass. It is also found that level spacing for the $Λ_b$ single-particle energies is much smaller than that for the $Λ$ and $Λ^+_c$.

# Wiki Page: Λ⁺_c- and Λ_b-Hypernuclei

## Key claim

The scalar and vector potentials felt by the Λ, Λ⁺_c, and Λ_b in hypernuclei with the same baryon number are quite similar, yet the single-particle wave functions and density distributions differ dramatically, the Λ⁺_c is pushed outward by the Coulomb force while the Λ_b is pulled inward by its heavy mass, and the level spacing for Λ_b single-particle energies is much smaller than for Λ or Λ⁺_c.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Dirac equations for a closed-shell nucleus plus one heavy baryon (Λ⁺_c or Λ_b) in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, using a Hartree, mean-field approximation. The σ, ω, ρ, and Coulomb fields were determined self-consistently from the scalar, baryon, isovector, and proton densities, with the σ-baryon coupling strengths computed from the underlying MIT bag model. Spin-orbit corrections were added perturbatively, and an effective Pauli-blocking plus ΣN–ΛN channel-coupling potential was included phenomenologically.

## Key numbers

- Bag radii in free space: R_N = 0.800 fm, R_Λ = 0.806 fm, R_Λ⁺_c = 0.846 fm, R_Λ_b = 0.930 fm.
- Current quark masses used: m_u,d = 5 MeV, m_s = 250 MeV, m_c = 1300 MeV, m_b = 4200 MeV.
- Bag parameters: z_N = 3.295, z_Λ = 3.131, z_Λ⁺_c = 1.766, z_Λ_b = −0.643; B^{1/4} = 170 MeV.
- Hadron-level coupling constants: g²_σ/4π = 3.12, g²_ω/4π = 5.31, g²_ρ/4π = 6.93; meson masses m_σ = 418 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, m_ρ = 770 MeV.
- Contribution from the spin-orbit potential with the quark-model correction is typically of order 0.01 MeV, and at most ≈ 0.1 MeV.
- The Λ⁺_c single-particle energy levels are higher than the corresponding levels for the Λ and Λ_b, an effect that strengthens as the proton number in the core nucleus increases.

## Context

This work extends the QMC model, previously applied to Λ-hypernuclei by Tsushima et al. (1998), to charmed and bottom hypernuclei, which were first predicted in the mid-1970s by Tyapkin (1975) and Dover (1977). The study builds on the authors’ earlier nuclear-matter calculations for heavy hadrons (Tsushima and Khanna, 2002) and on QMC studies of D- and D̅-nuclear bound states (Tsushima, 2001). The results suggest that Λ⁺_c- and Λ_b-hypernuclei should exist under realistic experimental conditions and open the possibility for experiments at facilities such as the Japan Hadron Facility (JHF) to study heavy-quark interactions in finite nuclei. The authors note that the small level spacing for Λ_b would make state identification difficult experimentally but may lead to new phenomena such as enhanced trapping probability in heavy nuclei.
