# Equation of state of nuclear matter from collective flows and stopping in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions

> The equation of state of nuclear matter, momentum dependence of the effective interaction and in-medium modification of elastic nucleon-nucleon cross-sections are studied by comparing theoretical predictions for stopping, directed and elliptic flows of protons and light clusters in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions of beam energy between 150 and 800 MeV/nucleon to experimental data gathered by the FOPI Collaboration. A multivariate analysis that takes into account systematic uncertainties induced on model predictions by the coalescence afterburner leads to the following constraint for the equation of state at 68 percent confidence level: compressibility modulus of isospin symmetric matter $K_0=230^{+9}_{-11}$ MeV and slope of the symmetry energy $L=63^{+10}_{-13}$ MeV. The momentum dependence of the isoscalar potential is found to be similar to that of the empirical optical potential, with an effective isoscalar mass $m^*=0.695^{+0.014}_{-0.018}$. The isovector potential displays a momentum dependence corresponding to a positive neutron-proton effective mass difference $Δm^*_{np}=(0.17^{+0.10}_{-0.09})δ$, close to the world average for this quantity. A suppression of elastic nucleon-nucleon cross-sections in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation by about 60$\%$ relative to vacuum values is deduced, in qualitative agreement with microscopical results. A strong dependence of the suppression factor on isospin asymmetry is evidenced, experimental data for isospin symmetric systems proving crucial for this last conclusion.

## Key claim

The paper constrains the equation of state of nuclear matter, momentum dependence of the effective interaction, and in-medium modification of elastic nucleon-nucleon cross-sections simultaneously from a multivariate analysis of FOPI heavy-ion collision data, finding that the compressibility modulus of symmetric nuclear matter is \(K_0=230^{+9}_{-11}\) MeV and the slope of the symmetry energy is \(L=63^{+10}_{-13}\) MeV at 68% confidence level, but these results are conditional on the specific transport model (dcQMD) and the restriction to nucleonic observables at beam energies up to 800 MeV/nucleon to avoid complications from baryonic resonance degrees of freedom.

## Method

The dcQMD transport model was upgraded with medium modifications of differential elastic nucleon-nucleon cross-sections guided by microscopical model calculations, including a medium modification factor that depends on local density, isospin asymmetry, and isospin projection of the scattering particles, as well as a MST coalescence algorithm applied at local freeze-out rather than at the end of simulation. Theoretical predictions for stopping (varxz), directed flow (v1), and elliptic flow (v2) of protons and light clusters in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies between 150 and 800 MeV/nucleon were compared to experimental data from the FOPI Collaboration, with a multivariate analysis that accounted for systematic uncertainties induced by the coalescence afterburner.

## Key numbers

* Compressibility modulus of isospin symmetric matter: \(K_0 = 230^{+9}_{-11}\) MeV at 68% confidence level
* Slope of the symmetry energy: \(L = 63^{+10}_{-13}\) MeV at 68% confidence level
* Effective isoscalar mass: \(m^* = 0.695^{+0.014}_{-0.018}\)
* Neutron-proton effective mass difference: \(\Delta m^*_{np} = (0.17^{+0.10}_{-0.09})\delta\), where \(\delta\) is the isospin asymmetry
* Suppression of elastic nucleon-nucleon cross-sections in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation: about 60% relative to vacuum values
* Beam energy range of experimental data used: 150 to 800 MeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends the quantitative analysis of FOPI data by LeFevre (2015), which used only elliptic flow data and found \(K_0 = 190 \pm 30\) MeV, and addresses the tension between directed flow constraints favoring a soft equation of state (\(K_0\) 167-210 MeV from Danielewicz (2002)) and elliptic flow data supporting a stiffer equation of state (\(K_0 \approx 300\) MeV). The study also builds on the earlier dcQMD analysis by Cozma (2017) and enables future simultaneous extraction of the equation of state, momentum dependence, and in-medium cross-sections from heavy-ion collision data, with planned extensions to include pion production and higher beam energies using data from ASYEOS2 and HADES collaborations.
