# QCD evolution of the gluon Sivers function in heavy flavor dijet production at the Electron-Ion Collider

> Using Soft-Collinear Effective Theory, we develop the transverse-momentum-dependent factorization formalism for heavy flavor dijet production in polarized-proton-electron collisions. We consider heavy flavor mass corrections in the collinear-soft and jet functions, as well as the associated evolution equations. Using this formalism, we generate a prediction for the gluon Sivers asymmetry for charm and bottom dijet production at the future Electron-Ion Collider. Furthermore, we compare theoretical predictions with and without the inclusion of finite quark masses. We find that the heavy flavor mass effects can give sizable corrections to the predicted asymmetry.

## Key claim

The paper develops a transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization formalism for heavy flavor dijet production in polarized-proton-electron collisions using Soft-Collinear Effective Theory, and finds that heavy flavor mass effects can give sizable corrections to the predicted gluon Sivers asymmetry at the future Electron-Ion Collider, though the analysis is performed at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy and the factorization applies only in the back-to-back region where the dijet transverse momentum imbalance is much smaller than the leading jet transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors derive the TMD factorization formula for heavy flavor dijet production in electron-proton collisions in the Breit frame, working in the back-to-back configuration where the dijet imbalance q_T is much smaller than the jet transverse momentum p_T, and in the kinematic regime where the heavy quark mass m_Q is comparable to or smaller than p_T R (with R the jet radius). They calculate the one-loop expressions for the massive quark jet functions, collinear-soft functions, soft function, and gluon TMD PDFs, incorporating heavy flavor mass corrections, and then use the renormalization group equations to perform resummation at NLL accuracy. The spin-dependent cross section is obtained by replacing the unpolarized gluon TMD PDF with the f-type gluon Sivers function, and the authors generate predictions for the gluon Sivers asymmetry for charm and bottom dijet production.

## Key numbers

- The factorization applies in the kinematic regime where m_Q is less than or approximately equal to p_T R, with the scale hierarchy q_T R << q_T less than or approximately equal to m_Q less than or approximately equal to p_T R << p_T
- The one-loop cusp anomalous dimension coefficient is gamma^{cusp}_0 = 4, and the two-loop coefficient is gamma^{cusp}_1 = (268/9 - 4pi^2/3)C_A - (40/9)C_F n_f
- The one-loop single logarithmic anomalous dimensions are gamma^q_0 = -3C_F and gamma^g_0 = -beta_0, where beta_0 = (11/3)C_A - (4/3)T_F n_f
- The one-loop rapidity anomalous dimension for the gluon TMD PDF is gamma_nu^{f_g}(alpha_s) = (alpha_s/pi) C_A ln(mu^2/mu_b^2) + O(alpha_s^2)
- The one-loop single logarithmic anomalous dimension for the soft function is gamma^s_0 = 8 C_F ln(4 c_{bJ}^2) + 4(C_A - 2C_F) ln(hat{s}/p_T^2) + 4 C_A ln(hat{s}/Q^2)

## Context

This work extends the TMD factorization framework developed for light flavor dijet production at the EIC by del Castillo et al. (2020) to include heavy flavor mass effects, building on the SCET-based approach used by Buffing et al. (2018), Liu et al. (2018, 2020), Chien et al. (2019), and Kang et al. (2020). The paper enables future experimental measurements of the gluon Sivers function at the EIC through heavy flavor dijet production, where the quark Sivers function contribution is further suppressed compared to light flavor dijet production, and provides the necessary theoretical framework including QCD evolution that was absent in earlier leading-order parton model studies by Zheng et al. (2018).
