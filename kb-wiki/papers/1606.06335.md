# Local chiral potentials and the structure of light nuclei

> We present fully local versions of the minimally non-local nucleon-nucleon potentials constructed in a previous paper [M.\ Piarulli {\it et al.}, Phys.\ Rev.\ C {\bf 91}, 024003 (2015)], and use them in hypersperical-harmonics and quantum Monte Carlo calculations of ground and excited states of $^3$H, $^3$He, $^4$He, $^6$He, and $^6$Li nuclei. The long-range part of these local potentials includes one- and two-pion exchange contributions without and with $Δ$-isobars in the intermediate states up to order $Q^3$ ($Q$ denotes generically the low momentum scale) in the chiral expansion, while the short-range part consists of contact interactions up to order $Q^4$. The low-energy constants multiplying these contact interactions are fitted to the 2013 Granada database in two different ranges of laboratory energies, either 0--125 MeV or 0--200 MeV, and to the deuteron binding energy and $nn$ singlet scattering length. Fits to these data are performed for three models characterized by long- and short-range cutoffs, $R_{\rm L}$ and $R_{\rm S}$ respectively, ranging from $(R_{\rm L},R_{\rm S})=(1.2,0.8)$ fm down to $(0.8,0.6)$ fm. The long-range (short-range) cutoff regularizes the one- and two-pion exchange (contact) part of the potential.

# Local chiral potentials and the structure of light nuclei

## Key claim
The authors construct fully local versions of previously developed minimally non-local chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials by dropping terms proportional to the squared relative momentum operator, and demonstrate that these local potentials, when used in hyperspherical-harmonics and quantum Monte Carlo calculations, provide an adequate description of ground and excited states of light nuclei up to A=6, though the N3LO calculations reported are incomplete because three-body forces, which start at N2LO, are not yet included.

## Method
The long-range part of the potential includes one- and two-pion exchange contributions up to order Q^3 in the chiral expansion, with and without Delta-isobar intermediate states, while the short-range part consists of contact interactions up to order Q^4 regularized by a Gaussian cutoff. The 26 low-energy constants multiplying the contact interactions were fitted to the 2013 Granada database of pp and np scattering observables in two laboratory energy ranges (0-125 MeV or 0-200 MeV), along with the deuteron binding energy and nn singlet scattering length, for three models with different long-range and short-range cutoffs (R_L, R_S) ranging from (1.2, 0.8) fm down to (0.8, 0.6) fm. The resulting potentials were then used in hyperspherical-harmonics calculations for A=3 and 4 nuclei and in variational Monte Carlo and Green's function Monte Carlo calculations for A=3, 4, and 6 nuclei.

- Total chi-squared per datum at N3LO for model c fitted up to 125 MeV: 1.11 (with 2666 pp+np data points)

## Context
This work extends the development of local chiral interactions previously constructed up to N2LO by Gezerlis et al. (2013, 2014) and the minimally non-local N3LO potentials of Piarulli et al. (2015) by producing fully local versions suitable for quantum Monte Carlo methods. The local potentials enable ab initio calculations of light nuclei using variational Monte Carlo and Green's function Monte Carlo techniques, which previously relied on phenomenological Hamiltonians based on the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential and Urbana/Illinois three-nucleon potentials. The N3LO two-body potentials reported here provide the basis for future calculations that will include chiral three-body forces, which are required for a complete description at this order.
