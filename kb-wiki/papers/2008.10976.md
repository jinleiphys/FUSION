# Dissipative Spin Dynamics in Relativistic Matter

> Using classical description of spin degrees of freedom, we extend recent formulation of the perfect-fluid hydrodynamics for spin-polarized fluids to the case including dissipation. Our work is based on the analysis of classical kinetic equations for massive particles with spin-1/2, with the collision terms treated in the relaxation time approximation. The kinetic-theory framework determines the structure of viscous and diffusive terms and allows to explicitly calculate a complete set of new kinetic coefficients that characterize dissipative spin dynamics.

## Key claim

Using a classical kinetic theory with spin-1/2 degrees of freedom and the relaxation time approximation for collisions, the authors extend the perfect-fluid hydrodynamics for spin-polarized fluids to include dissipation, explicitly calculating a complete set of new kinetic coefficients that characterize dissipative spin dynamics, but only in the linear order in the spin polarization tensor \(\omega_{\mu\nu}\) and for Boltzmann statistics.

## Method

The authors start from classical kinetic equations for massive spin-1/2 particles, treating the collision term in the relaxation time approximation (RTA). They expand the equilibrium distribution functions, which include the spin polarization tensor \(\omega_{\mu\nu}\) as an independent variable, to linear order in \(\omega\). By taking moments of the kinetic equation, they derive the dissipative corrections to the charge current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor, and express the new kinetic coefficients in terms of thermodynamic integrals.

## Key numbers

* The charge density in equilibrium is \(n = 4 \sinh(\xi) n_0(T)\), where \(n_0(T) = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} T^3 z^2 K_2(z)\) with \(z = m/T\).
* The equilibrium energy density is \(\varepsilon = 4 \cosh(\xi) \varepsilon_0(T)\), with \(\varepsilon_0(T) = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} T^4 z^2 [3 K_2(z) + z K_1(z)]\).
* The equilibrium pressure is \(P = 4 \cosh(\xi) P_0(T)\), with \(P_0(T) = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} T^4 z^2 K_2(z) = n_0(T) T\).
* The spin tensor coefficient \({\cal B}_0 = -\frac{2}{z^2} \frac{\varepsilon_0(T) + P_0(T)}{T} = -\frac{2}{z^2} s_0(T)\), where \(s_0\) is the entropy density of spinless neutral massive Boltzmann particles.
* The spin tensor coefficient \({\cal A}_0 = \frac{6}{z^2} s_0(T) + 2 n_0(T) = -3{\cal B}_0 + 2 n_0(T)\).

## Context

This work extends the perfect-fluid formulation of spin-polarized hydrodynamics developed by Florkowski et al. (2017-2018) and Becattini et al. (2018) to include dissipation, building on the initial dissipative treatment by Bhadury et al. (2020). It provides the kinetic coefficients needed to couple the non-equilibrium spin tensor to thermodynamic forces such as the expansion scalar, shear flow tensor, and gradients of chemical potential and spin polarization, enabling future numerical simulations of dissipative spin dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
