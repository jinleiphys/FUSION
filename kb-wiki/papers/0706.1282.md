# Folding model study of the isobaric analog excitation: isovector density dependence, Lane potential and nuclear symmetry energy

> A consistent folding model analysis of the ($ΔS=0, ΔT=1$) charge exchange \pn reaction measured with $^{48}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, $^{120}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb targets at the proton energies of 35 and 45 MeV is done within a two-channel coupling formalism. The nuclear ground state densities given by the Hartree-Fock-Bogoljubov formalism and the density dependent CDM3Y6 interaction were used as inputs for the folding calculation of the nucleon optical potential and \pn form factor. To have an accurate isospin dependence of the interaction, a complex isovector density dependence of the CDM3Y6 interaction has been carefully calibrated against the microscopic Brueckner-Hatree-Fock calculation by Jeukenne, Lejeune and Mahaux before being used as folding input. Since the isovector coupling was used to explicitly link the isovector part of the nucleon optical potential to the cross section of \pn reaction exciting the 0$^+$ isobaric analog states in $^{48}$Sc, $^{90}$Nb, $^{120}$Sb and $^{208}$Bi, the newly parameterized isovector density dependence could be well tested in the folding model analysis of the \pn reaction. The isospin- and density dependent CDM3Y6 interaction was further used in the Hartree-Fock calculation of asymmetric nuclear matter, and a realistic estimation of the nuclear symmetry energy has been made.

## Key claim

The isovector density dependence of the CDM3Y6 interaction, calibrated against the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculation by Jeukenne, Lejeune and Mahaux (JLM), provides a reliable microscopic prediction for the Lane potential and the charge exchange form factor in (p,n) reactions to isobaric analog states, but the description of the (p,n) cross sections depends on the choice of the imaginary optical potential and the treatment of surface absorption.

## Method

The authors performed a two-channel coupling analysis of charge exchange (p,n) reactions exciting 0+ isobaric analog states in 48Sc, 90Nb, 120Sb, and 208Bi, measured at proton energies of 35 and 45 MeV. They used Hartree-Fock-Bogoljubov ground state densities and a complex isovector density dependence of the CDM3Y6 interaction, parameterized to reproduce the JLM nucleon optical potential in nuclear matter, as input for single-folding calculations of the nucleon optical potential and (p,n) form factor. The Lane potential U1 was used to construct the charge exchange form factor, and the coupled-channel equations were solved with the code ECIS97.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density was estimated from the Hartree-Fock calculation of asymmetric nuclear matter using the isospin- and density dependent CDM3Y6 interaction, with the saturation density taken as ρ0 ≈ 0.17 fm−3.
- The isoscalar density dependence of the CDM3Y6 interaction was parameterized with a nuclear incompressibility K ≈ 252 MeV.
- The intrinsic energy dependence of the isoscalar interaction is given by g(E) ≈ 1 − 0.0026E, where E is the incident nucleon energy in MeV.
- The Yukawa strengths of the isovector exchange interaction (YEX01) are much larger than the direct terms (YD01), e.g., for the first Yukawa (Rν = 4.0 fm−1): YD01 = 313.625 MeV and YEX01 = −4118.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the folding model analysis of (p,n) reactions to isobaric analog states previously applied to the 6He(p,n) reaction by Khoa (2005), now applied to heavier targets (48Ca, 90Zr, 120Sn, 208Pb). The authors compare their microscopic Lane potential with the isovector parts of three global nucleon optical potentials: Becchetti and Greenlees (1969), Varner et al. (CH89, 1991), and Koning and Delaroche (2003), finding that the CH89 potential gives the best description of the (p,n) data among the global systematics. The study enables a link between BHF calculations of nuclear matter and experimental charge exchange reaction data, and provides a method to probe the nuclear symmetry energy through the (p,n) reaction strength.
