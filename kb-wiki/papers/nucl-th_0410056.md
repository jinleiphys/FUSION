# Interaction of the $Θ^+$ with the nuclear medium

> We study the selfenergy of the $Θ^+$ pentaquark in nuclei associated with two types of interaction: the KN decay of the $Θ^+$ and two meson baryon decay channels of the $Θ^+$. Whereas the potential related to the first source is quite weak, the second kind of interaction produces a large and attractive potential that could lead to the existence of $Θ^+$ nuclear bound states.

## Key claim

The $\Theta^+$ pentaquark experiences a weak potential from its $KN$ decay channel (too small to bind it in nuclei), but a second interaction from two-meson decay channels produces a large attractive potential of order 50–100 MeV at normal nuclear density, which could lead to bound $\Theta^+$ nuclear states; however, this conclusion depends on several assumptions (the $\Theta^+$ having $J^P = 1/2^+$ and belonging to an $SU(3)$ antidecuplet, the $N^*(1710)$ coupling strongly to the same antidecuplet, and the use of specific Lagrangians with coupling constants fixed from PDG data with large uncertainties).

## Method

The authors calculated the $\Theta^+$ selfenergy in infinite nuclear matter from two sources: (1) the $KN$ decay channel, using an in-medium kaon propagator with $s$-wave and $p$-wave selfenergies and a nucleon propagator modified by Pauli blocking and a Thomas-Fermi potential; (2) two-meson decay channels (e.g., $\Theta^+ \to N \pi \pi$), where coupling constants were determined from $N^*(1710)$ decay data using $SU(3)$ symmetry, and medium effects were included via pion and kaon selfenergies from $ph$ and $\Delta h$ excitations. The optical potential was obtained by subtracting the free $\Theta^+$ selfenergy from the in-medium one.

## Key numbers

- The $KN$ decay channel produces a $\Theta^+$ potential in the medium of order 1 MeV or less, insufficient to bind the $\Theta^+$ in nuclei.
- For the two-meson cloud contribution at normal nuclear density $\rho = \rho_0$, the attractive potential ranges from $-70$ to $-120$ MeV depending on the cutoff used in the selfenergy evaluation.
- Assuming a free $\Theta^+$ width of 15 MeV, the width from the $KN$ decay channel for 20 MeV binding at $\rho = \rho_0$ is less than 6 MeV.
- The width from the two-meson decay mechanism is smaller than 5 MeV for bound states with binding of about 20 MeV, and negligible for binding energies of about 40 MeV or higher.
- The total $\Theta^+$ width in the medium is below 8 MeV assuming a free width of 15 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation of Kim (2004), which found a weak $\Theta^+$ selfenergy from $KN$ interactions, by adding new selfenergy contributions from two-meson decay channels. The results contradict the schematic quark-pair model of Miller (2004) that suggested stable $\Theta^+$ hypernuclei, but instead predict bound states with small widths. The findings enable searches for $\Theta^+$ nuclear bound states in light and intermediate nuclei, with widths smaller than the separation between bound levels as estimated by Nagahiro (2004).
