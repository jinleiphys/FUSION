# Symmetry and Supersymmetry in Nuclear Pairing: Exact Solutions

> Pairing plays a crucial role in nuclear spectra and attempts to describe it has a long history in nuclear physics. The limiting case in which all single particle states are degenerate, but with different s-wave pairing strengths was only recently solved. In this strong coupling limit the nuclear pairing Hamiltonian also exhibits a supersymmetry. Another solution away from those limits, namely two non-degenerate single particle states with different pairing strengths, was also given. In this contribution these developments are summarized and difficulties with possible generalizations to more single particle states and d-wave pairing are discussed.

# Symmetry and Supersymmetry in Nuclear Pairing: Exact Solutions

## Key claim
The paper presents exact solutions for the nuclear s-wave pairing Hamiltonian in two specific limits: the degenerate case where all single-particle states have the same energy but different pairing strengths, and the case of two non-degenerate single-particle states with different pairing strengths. The authors note that generalizing the Bethe ansatz method to three or more non-degenerate orbitals appears very difficult, and that explicit solutions of the Bethe ansatz equations have been developed only in a limited number of cases.

## Method
The authors formulate the s-wave pairing problem using quasi-spin SU(2) algebras and derive Bethe ansatz equations for the degenerate separable pairing Hamiltonian, where the eigenstates are constructed as products of generalized pair-creation operators acting on the vacuum. For the two non-degenerate single-particle level case, they introduce a Hamiltonian with single-particle energies and separable pairing, and obtain energy eigenvalues and Bethe ansatz equations using step operators that depend on the single-particle energies and pairing strengths. The supersymmetry connecting particle and hole states is demonstrated by constructing a transformation operator that maps the empty state to the fully occupied state.

## Key numbers
- The maximum number of pairs that can occupy a level j is given by Omega_j = j + 1/2, where the quasi-spin representation has s_j = Omega_j/2.
- For the degenerate case with N pairs, the energy eigenvalue is E_N = -|G| (sum_j Omega_j |c_j|^2 - sum_{k=1}^{N-1} 2/z^{(N)}_k), where z^{(N)}_k satisfy the Bethe ansatz equations.
- For the two single-particle level case, the energy eigenvalues are E_N = -sum_{n=1}^N (delta x_n)/(beta - x_n), where beta = 2(epsilon_{j1} - epsilon_{j2})/(|c_{j1}|^2 - |c_{j2}|^2) and delta = 2(epsilon_{j2}|c_{j1}|^2 - epsilon_{j1}|c_{j2}|^2)/(|c_{j1}|^2 - |c_{j2}|^2).

## Context
This work extends the exact solutions for nuclear pairing Hamiltonians that began with the quasi-spin limit of Kerman (1961) and Richardson's solution (1963) for the case with single-particle energies. The degenerate separable pairing solution builds on earlier work by Talmi (1970s) and the authors' own previous studies (Pan 1998, Balantekin 2007). The paper discusses difficulties in generalizing to three non-degenerate orbitals but suggests that the Bethe ansatz method may be generalizable to d-wave pairing situations, particularly through connections to the Ginocchio model (1978) and Gaudin's algebraic approach. The techniques may also apply to other Hamiltonians, such as those describing dense electron gases in core-collapse supernovae.
