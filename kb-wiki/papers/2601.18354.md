# Chiral Properties of $(2\!+\!1)$-Flavor QCD in Magnetic Fields at Zero Temperature

> We present a lattice QCD study of the chiral properties of $(2\!+\!1)$-flavor QCD in background magnetic fields at zero temperature with physical pion masses. Simulations are performed using the highly improved staggered quark action across four different lattice spacings to enable a controlled continuum extrapolation. We compute the renormalized chiral condensates together with pseudoscalar meson masses and decay constants for pions, kaons, and the fictitious $η^0_{s\bar{s}}$ pseudoscalar as functions of the magnetic-field strength $eB$ up to $eB\simeq1.2$ $\mathrm{GeV}^2$. The chiral condensates exhibit clear magnetic catalysis, increasing monotonically with the field strength. In the meson sector, neutral pseudoscalar masses decrease steadily with $eB$, whereas charged pseudoscalar masses display a nonmonotonic response: They rise at small fields, consistent with the lowest-Landau-level expectation, but then saturate and slightly decrease at larger fields, signaling sizable internal-structure effects. At the same time, neutral pseudoscalar decay constants are strongly enhanced by the magnetic field. To quantify deviations from chiral symmetry relations, we isolate the magnetic-field-induced shift in the Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner corrections and find it to remain small for the neutral pion but to become sizable for the neutral kaon. To elucidate the origin of the magnetic response, we separately analyze the sea- and valence-quark contributions to both neutral and charged meson masses, finding that valence effects dominate at zero temperature. These results provide new insights into the interplay between QCD chiral symmetry breaking and strong magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in (2+1)-flavor QCD at zero temperature with physical pion masses, neutral pseudoscalar meson masses decrease monotonically with magnetic field strength, while charged pseudoscalar meson masses show a nonmonotonic response, rising at small fields but saturating and slightly decreasing at larger fields, indicating that internal-structure effects compete with the lowest-Landau-level expectation for pointlike charged particles.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action with physical quark masses across four lattice spacings (a = 0.112, 0.084, 0.067, and 0.056 fm) to enable controlled continuum extrapolations. They computed renormalized chiral condensates, pseudoscalar meson masses, and decay constants for pions, kaons, and the fictitious η⁰_s\bar{s} pseudoscalar as functions of magnetic field strength up to eB ≃ 1.2 GeV², extracting ground-state masses using both AICc-selected multistate fits and the oblique Lanczos method.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths studied: eB from 0 to 1.22 GeV², corresponding to flux quanta N_b = 0, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12
- Four lattice spacings used: a = 0.112 fm (24³ × 48), 0.084 fm (32³ × 64), 0.067 fm (40³ × 80), and 0.056 fm (48³ × 96)
- Spatial extent fixed at L ≃ 2.7 fm across all ensembles
- Light-to-strange quark mass ratio fixed at m_l/m_s = 1/27
- Coarsest lattice discretization error from magnetic field: N_b/N_σ² ≈ 2.1% at largest field (N_b = 12, N_σ = 24), well below the 5% benchmark
- Number of gauge configurations per ensemble ranges from 216 to 1401 depending on lattice size and magnetic field strength

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of mesons in magnetic fields that were limited by quenched approximations, valence-only magnetic fields, or single lattice spacings with heavier-than-physical pion masses (Ding 2020). It provides continuum-extrapolated benchmarks at the physical point that enable quantitative tests of the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation in magnetic fields and allow separation of sea- and valence-quark contributions to the magnetic response, which is relevant for understanding the inverse magnetic catalysis phenomenon observed at finite temperature (Bali 2011, Bali 2012).
