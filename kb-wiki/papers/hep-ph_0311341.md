# Meson production in nucleon--nucleon collisions close to the threshold

> The last decade has witnessed great experimental progress that has led to measurements of cross sections--polarized as well as unpolarized--of of high accuracy in several $NN \to NNx$ reaction channels. These data, naturally, pose challenges to theorists to develop methods by which they can be understood and explained in commensurate detail. In this work we review the status of the theory with special emphasis on model--independent methods. We discuss in detail not only the many observables involved in the reactions, but also the physical questions that can be addressed by studying them in the various reaction channels. The special advantages of nucleon--nucleon induced reactions are stressed. Foremost among these is the use of the initial and final states as a spin/isospin filter. This opens, for example, a window into the spin dependence of the hyperon--nucleon interaction and the dynamics of the light scalar mesons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions near threshold, despite the large momentum transfer that complicates theoretical analysis, offers unique advantages for studying strong interaction physics because the initial and final states can serve as spin/isospin filters, and the production operator's weak energy dependence enables model-independent extraction of final-state interaction parameters via dispersion relations.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical status of near-threshold meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, focusing on model-independent methods. The review covers both perturbative approaches (distorted wave Born approximation with a perturbatively constructed production operator) and non-perturbative approaches (solving integral equations for the coupled NN/NNx system), and discusses chiral perturbation theory counting schemes adapted to the large momentum transfer regime. The paper also presents counting rules within both time-ordered perturbation theory and covariant schemes for estimating the chiral order of loop diagrams contributing to pion production.

## Key numbers

- The typical momentum transfer for pion production near threshold gives a reaction volume size parameter R ~ 0.5 fm, which becomes even smaller for heavier mesons
- The threshold initial momentum for meson production is given by |p_i^(thres)|^2 = m_x M_N + m_x^2/4, where m_x is the meson mass and M_N the nucleon mass
- For the diagram analyzed in the chiral counting appendix, the estimated matrix element in both TOPT and covariant schemes is proportional to (1/f_pi^3)(m_pi/M_N), using the relation 4*pi*f_pi ~ M_N and p_i ~ sqrt(M_N*m_pi)
- The maximum angular momentum allowed near threshold can be estimated as l_max ~ q'/m_x ~ eta, where eta is the maximum pion momentum in units of the pion mass

## Context

This review extends the experimental summaries of Machner (2003) and Oelert (2003) by focusing on theoretical developments and the potential of polarization observables, which those earlier reviews did not discuss in detail. The work contrasts with traditional electromagnetic probe experiments by emphasizing that hadronic reactions allow isospin filtering not possible with photons, and that the large momentum transfer enables model-independent extraction of low-energy scattering parameters (e.g., hyperon-nucleon scattering lengths) via dispersion relations, unlike low-momentum-transfer reactions such as K^- d -> gamma Lambda n studied by Gibbs (1995) where results are sensitive to short-range physics. The review enables future systematic studies of charge symmetry breaking, three-body forces relevant for pd scattering, and the spin dependence of the hyperon-nucleon interaction.
