# Shell model versus liquid drop model for strangelets

> An ansatz for the curvature contribution to the density of states for massive quarks in a bag is given and shown to reproduce exact mode-filling calculations. A mass-formula for spherical lumps of 3-flavor quark matter is derived self-consistently from an asymptotic expansion within the MIT bag model, taking into account bulk, surface, and curvature contributions. Shell model calculations are performed for a variety of strange quark masses and bag constants, and the results shown to match nicely with the asymptotic expansion.

## Key claim

The paper presents an ansatz for the curvature contribution to the density of states for massive quarks in a bag, which is shown to reproduce exact mode-filling calculations; the claim is hedged by noting that the curvature term has not been derived from the multiple reflection expansion, but that small changes in the ansatz lead to clearly visible errors in fits to mode-filling calculations, so it seems fair to conclude this is the proper term.

## Method

The author derived a mass-formula for spherical lumps of 3-flavor quark matter within the MIT bag model using an asymptotic expansion that includes bulk, surface, and curvature contributions, with the curvature term for massive quarks given by a new ansatz for the density of states. Shell model calculations were performed for a variety of strange quark masses and bag constants by mode-filling in a spherical MIT bag, and the results were compared to the liquid drop model predictions from the asymptotic expansion.

## Context

This work extends the liquid drop model for strangelets by Madsen (1993a, 1993b) which previously only included curvature for massless quarks, and the surface tension treatment of Berger and Jaffe (1987) which lacked curvature contributions. The paper enables systematic study of strangelet properties across a range of parameters without requiring tedious shell model calculations, and facilitates generalizations to finite temperature, studies of phase transitions, and mixed phase structures in heavy-ion physics, cosmology, and neutron stars.
