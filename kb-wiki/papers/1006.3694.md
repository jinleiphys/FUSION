# Shape mixing dynamics in the low-lying states of proton-rich Kr isotopes

> We study the oblate-prolate shape mixing in the low-lying states of proton-rich Kr isotopes using the five-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian. The collective Hamiltonian is derived microscopically by means of the CHFB (constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov) + Local QRPA (quasiparticle random phase approximation) method, which we have developed recently on the basis of the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate method. The results of the numerical calculation show the importance of large-amplitude collective vibrations in the triaxial shape degree of freedom and rotational effects on the oblate-prolate shape mixing dynamics in the low-lying states of these isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the oblate-prolate shape mixing in proton-rich Kr isotopes is governed by large-amplitude collective vibrations in the triaxial shape degree of freedom and rotational effects, but the shape transition is not a simple function of the mean-field potential minima alone: dynamical effects beyond the mean field, including the rotational alignment, can cause the ground-state wave function to localize on the prolate side even when the collective potential has an oblate absolute minimum, as seen in ⁷⁶Kr.

## Method

The authors derive a five-dimensional (5D) quadrupole collective Hamiltonian microscopically using the constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) plus local quasiparticle random phase approximation (LQRPA) method, which is an approximation of the two-dimensional adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method. They solve the CHFB equations at each point on a 60 × 60 mesh in the (β, γ) deformation plane, then solve the LQRPA equations on top of each CHFB state to obtain the vibrational inertial masses, rotational moments of inertia, and collective potential, using a pairing-plus-quadrupole (P+Q) interaction including quadrupole pairing. The collective Schrödinger equation is then solved to obtain excitation spectra, B(E2) values, spectroscopic quadrupole moments, and monopole transition matrix elements for ⁷²Kr, ⁷⁴Kr, and ⁷⁶Kr.

## Key numbers

- The collective potential for ⁷²Kr shows the prolate minimum is approximately 600 keV higher than the oblate absolute minimum.
- The two-dimensional mesh consists of 60 × 60 points specified by β_i = (i − 0.5) × 0.01 (i = 1, …, 60) and γ_j = (j − 0.5) × 1° (j = 1, …, 60).
- The polarization charge used is δe_pol = 0.834e, determined such that the calculated B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) reproduces the experimental data for ⁷⁴Kr from Clement (2007).
- For ⁷²Kr, the 0₂⁺ excitation energy obtained with LQRPA masses is in much better agreement with experimental data than that obtained with Inglis-Belyaev cranking masses.
- For ⁷⁴Kr, the calculation reproduces the significant enhancement of B(E2; 0₂⁺ → 2₁⁺), B(E2; 2₂⁺ → 0₂⁺), and B(E2; 2₂⁺ → 2₁⁺) found in experiments.
- The spectroscopic quadrupole moments for ⁷⁴Kr are in excellent agreement with experimental data from Clement (2007), aside from a minor disagreement for the 2₃⁺ state.

## Context

This work extends the earlier (1+3) dimensional ASCC calculations for ⁷²Kr by Hinohara (2008) and for neighboring ⁶⁸⁻⁷²Se by Hinohara (2009) to a full five-dimensional treatment of the quadrupole collective Hamiltonian. It improves upon the generator coordinate method (GCM) calculation by Girod (2009) and the axial GCM calculation by Bender (2006) by including the time-odd components of the mean field in both vibrational and rotational inertial masses, which the widely-used Inglis-Belyaev cranking masses neglect. The results enable future studies of shape coexistence dynamics in other mass regions and provide a benchmark for comparing the CHFB+LQRPA method with other beyond-mean-field approaches.
