# Hollowness in $pp$ and $p \bar p$ scattering in a Regge model

> The proton-proton and proton-antiproton inelasticity profiles in the impact parameter display very interesting and sensitive features which cannot be deduced solely from the current large body of high-energy scattering data. In particular, phenomenological studies exhibit a link between the ratio of the real to imaginary parts of the elastic scattering amplitude at a finite momentum transfer, and the corresponding change of character of the inelastic processes from central to peripheral collisions. We describe how a theoretical model, accommodating the existing data, based on the Regge hypothesis including both the Pomeron and odderon as double poles, and $ω$ and $f$ mesons as single poles in the complex-$J$ plane, generates a hollow in the inelasticity at low impact parameters. The hollowness effect, which generally may be sensitive to model details, does unequivocally take place both for $pp$ and $p \bar p$ collisions within the applied Regge framework, indicating inapplicability of inelasticity-folding geometric approaches.

## Key claim
Within a Regge model that includes the Pomeron and odderon as double poles and ω and f mesons as single poles, the hollowness effect (a minimum of the inelasticity profile at zero impact parameter) occurs unequivocally for both pp and p\(\bar p\) collisions at LHC energies, but the authors note that the effect may generally be sensitive to model details and cannot be deduced solely from existing high-energy scattering data without theoretical input.

## Method
The authors constructed a spin-averaged Regge model with linear trajectories, where the Pomeron and odderon are treated as double poles in the complex-J plane and the ω and f mesons as single poles. They performed a joint fit to pp and p\(\bar p\) data on the differential elastic cross section, total cross section, and the ρ parameter (ratio of real to imaginary parts of the forward amplitude) over a range of energies from 20 GeV to 57 TeV, using the MIGRAD algorithm of MINUIT 2. The inelasticity profile in impact-parameter space was then obtained via Fourier-Bessel transform of the fitted amplitude.

## Key numbers
- The hollowness onset occurs at collision energies around 3 TeV for both pp and p\(\bar p\).
- At \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV, the inelasticity profile shows a clear minimum at b=0 for both systems, with the effect slightly stronger for p\(\bar p\) than for pp.
- The fit yields \(\chi^2/\text{NDF}=1.4\) with 159 degrees of freedom.
- The Pomeron intercept is \(1+\delta_P\) with \(\delta_P = 0.0293 \pm 0.0005\); the odderon intercept is \(1+\delta_O\) with \(\delta_O = 0.275 \pm 0.005\).
- The Pomeron slope \(\alpha'_P = 0.5069 \pm 0.0028\) GeV\(^{-2}\); the odderon slope \(\alpha'_O = 0.2309 \pm 0.0017\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
- The condition \(2p\,\text{Im}\,h(b=0,s) > 1\) is approximately equivalent to the hollowness criterion; the Pomeron contribution at b=0 scales as \(0.64 (s/\text{GeV}^2)^{0.028}\) and the odderon contribution as \(0.88 (s/\text{GeV}^2)^{0.27}\).

## Context
This work extends earlier phenomenological studies of hollowness by Broniowski and Ruiz Arriola (2017) and Dremin (2016) that used empirical parameterizations, by implementing a theoretically motivated Regge framework that satisfies fixed-t dispersion relations and crossing symmetry. The model contradicts geometric folding approaches (e.g., Chou and Yang (1968), Block (2006)) that assume maximal inelasticity at b=0, and instead supports the hollowness picture also found in hot-spot models by Albacete (2016). The results enable future comparisons with other Regge models and unitarization schemes, and highlight the need for improved theoretical input to resolve the phase ambiguity that prevents a model-independent determination of hollowness from elastic data alone.
