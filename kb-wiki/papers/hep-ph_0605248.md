# Heavy meson semileptonic differential decay rate in two dimensions in the large Nc

> We study QCD in 1+1 dimensions in the large $N_c$ limit using light-front Hamiltonian perturbation theory in the $1/N_c$ expansion. We use this formalism to exactly compute hadronic transition matrix elements for arbitrary currents at leading order in $1/N_c$. We compute the semileptonic differential decay rate of a heavy meson, $dΓ/dx$, and its moments, $M_N$, using the hadronic matrix elements obtained previously. We put some emphasis in trying to understand parity invariance. We also study with special care the kinematic region where the operator product expansion ($1/N \sim 1-x \sim 1$) or non-local effective field theories ($1/N \sim 1-x \sim Λ_{QCD}/m_Q$) can be applied. We then compare with the results obtained using an effective field theory approach based on perturbative factorization, with the focus to better understand quark-hadron duality. At the end of the day, using effective field theories, we have been able to obtain expressions for the moments with relative accuracy of $O(Λ_{QCD}^2/m_Q^2)$ in the kinematic region where the operator product expansion can be applied, and with relative accuracy of $O(Λ_{QCD}/m_Q)$ in the kinematic region where non-local effective field theories can be applied. These expressions agree, within this precision, with those obtained from the hadronic result using the layer-function approximation plus Euler-McLaurin expansion. Very good numerical agreement for the moments is obtained between the exact result and the result using effective field theories.

# Heavy meson semileptonic differential decay rate in two dimensions in the large Nc

## Key claim

The authors claim that in QCD in 1+1 dimensions in the large Nc limit, effective field theory expressions for the moments of the semileptonic differential decay rate of a heavy meson agree with the exact hadronic result within the stated precision, but only in specific kinematic regimes: the operator product expansion regime yields relative accuracy of O(Λ_QCD^2/m_Q^2), while the non-local effective field theory regime yields relative accuracy of O(Λ_QCD/m_Q).

## Method

The authors study QCD in 1+1 dimensions in the large Nc limit using light-front Hamiltonian perturbation theory in the 1/Nc expansion. They compute hadronic transition matrix elements for arbitrary currents at leading order in 1/Nc, then compute the semileptonic differential decay rate dΓ/dx and its moments MN from these matrix elements. They compare these exact hadronic results with expressions obtained from effective field theory approaches based on perturbative factorization, specifically soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) and heavy quark effective theory (HQET), using the layer-function approximation plus Euler-McLaurin expansion for the hadronic side.

## Key numbers

- The moments of the differential decay rate obtained from effective field theories have relative accuracy of O(Λ_QCD^2/m_Q^2) in the OPE kinematic region (where 1/N ~ 1-x ~ 1)
- The moments obtained from effective field theories have relative accuracy of O(Λ_QCD/m_Q) in the kinematic region where non-local effective field theories can be applied (where 1/N ~ 1-x ~ Λ_QCD/m_Q)
- The bound state masses in the asymptotic limit n→∞ satisfy M_n^2 ≃ nπ^2 β^2, where β^2 = g^2Nc/(2π)
- The 't Hooft wavefunctions satisfy the endpoint behavior φ_n^ij(x) → x^{β_i} as x→0, where m_{i,R}^2 + πβ_i cot πβ_i = 0
- The static limit sum rules give ⟨t⟩ = ε_n and ⟨t^2⟩ = (4/3)⟨t⟩^2 - (1/3)(m_s^2 - β^2)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quark-hadron duality violations in the 't Hooft model, following the definition of duality violations from Shifman (2000). It builds on earlier computations of transition matrix elements by Einhorn (1976) and Callan (1976), and on the work of Bigi et al. (1998) who studied inclusive heavy meson decay. The authors compare their results with the effective field theory approach of SCET as developed by Bauer (2000, 2001), Beneke (2002), and Neubert (2002), and aim to provide a controlled theoretical setup to better understand the structure of SCET and quark-hadron duality. The work enables systematic comparison between exact hadronic results and effective field theory predictions in a simplified model, which can inform the development of SCET in four dimensions.
