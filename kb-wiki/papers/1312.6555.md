# Universal fluctuation-driven eccentricities in proton-proton, proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We show that the statistics of fluctuation-driven initial-state anisotropies in proton-proton, proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions is to a large extent universal. We propose a simple parametrization for the probability distribution of the Fourier coefficient $\varepsilon_n$ in harmonic $n$, which is in good agreement with Monte-Carlo simulations. Our results provide a simple explanation for the 4-particle cumulant of triangular flow measured in Pb-Pb collisions, and for the 4-particle cumulant of elliptic flow recently measured in p-Pb collisions. Both arise as natural consequences of the condition that initial anisotropies are bounded by unity. We argue that the initial rms anisotropy in harmonic $n$ can be directly extracted from the measured ratio $v_n\{4\}/v_n\{2\}$: this gives direct access to a property of the initial density profile from experimental data. We also make quantitative predictions for the small lifting of degeneracy between $v_n\{4\}$, $v_n\{6\}$ and $v_n\{8\}$. If confirmed by future experiments, they will support the picture that long-range correlations observed in p-Pb collisions at the LHC originate from collective flow proportional to the initial anisotropy.

# Universal fluctuation-driven eccentricities in proton-proton, proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions

## Key claim
The probability distribution of fluctuation-driven initial-state anisotropies in small collision systems is universal and follows a power-law distribution bounded by unity, which explains observed nonzero 4-particle cumulants in p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions without invoking symmetry breaking or a mean eccentricity parameter.

## Method
The authors propose a one-parameter power-law distribution P(ε)=2αε(1-ε²)^(α-1) for the Fourier coefficient ε_n of the initial anisotropy, which satisfies the constraint ε_n<1 by construction. They fit this distribution to Monte-Carlo Glauber simulations of p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and compare with Gaussian and Bessel-Gaussian parameterizations. They then derive analytic expressions for eccentricity cumulants ε{2}, ε{4}, ε{6}, and ε{8} from this distribution and test the resulting parameter-free relation between ε{4} and ε{2} against Monte-Carlo results for p-Pb, p-p, and Pb-Pb collisions.

## Key numbers
- The power-law fit to Monte-Carlo Glauber simulations of p-Pb collisions yields α ≈ 5.64 for ε₂
- For the power-law distribution, ε{2} = 1/√(1+α) and ε{4} = [2/((1+α)²(2+α))]^(1/4)
- The parameter-free relation ε{4} = ε{2}^(3/2) × (2/(1+ε{2}²))^(1/4) is tested; full Monte-Carlo Glauber results lie above this line by ~0.015 for both ε₂ and ε₃
- For Pb-Pb collisions, Monte-Carlo Glauber and Monte-Carlo KLN results for ε₃ agree with the relation within ~0.01
- The ratio v₃{4}/v₃{2} ≈ 0.5 in mid-central Pb-Pb collisions predicts v₃{6}/v₃{4} ≈ 0.84 and v₃{8}/v₃{6} ≈ 0.94
- The ratio v₂{4}/v₂{2} ≈ 0.7 measured in p-Pb collisions predicts v₂{6}/v₂{4} ≈ 0.93 and v₂{8}/v₂{6} ≈ 0.98
- From the p-Pb ratio, the extracted rms ε₂{2} ≈ 0.37; from Pb-Pb, ε₃{2} ≈ 0.17

## Context
This work extends the understanding of fluctuation-driven anisotropies beyond the Gaussian approximation (Alver 2006, Alver 2010) and the Bessel-Gaussian parameterization (Bzdak 2013), which fail to describe small systems because they do not enforce the ε_n<1 bound. The power-law distribution was originally derived for pointlike sources (Ollitrault 1992) but is shown here to be universal across p-p, p-Pb, and Pb-Pb collisions regardless of model details. The results explain the nonzero v₂{4} observed by ATLAS (2013) and CMS (2013) in p-Pb collisions and provide predictions for higher-order cumulants v_n{6} and v_n{8} that can be tested experimentally to confirm the collective-flow interpretation of long-range correlations.
