# Medium effects on the flow of strange particles in heavy ion collisions

> Strange particles such as the kaon and lambda are useful in probing the properties of dense matter formed in heavy ion collisions. We review in this talk the theoretical understandings of their properties in nuclear medium based on both the effective chiral Lagrangian and the phenomenological hadronic model. We further review the effects due to changes in their in-medium properties on their collective flow in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical evidence that the in-medium properties of strange particles (kaon, antikaon, lambda) significantly affect their collective flow in heavy ion collisions, and that experimental flow data at GSI and AGS energies are consistent with the repulsive kaon potential and attractive antikaon potential predicted by the chiral Lagrangian, though the lambda potential strength cannot be determined from existing data due to large experimental errors.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical predictions for kaon, antikaon, and lambda properties in nuclear matter based on the effective chiral Lagrangian and the phenomenological hadronic (meson-exchange) model, using mean-field approximations to compute in-medium energies and potentials. These predictions are then compared with experimental flow data from heavy ion collisions at GSI (SIS), AGS (BNL), and SPS (CERN) energies, using results from various transport model calculations (ART, RQMD, and others) that incorporate or omit the in-medium potentials.

## Key numbers

- Kaon mass at normal nuclear matter density increases by about 30 MeV (from chiral Lagrangian with Sigma_KN ~ 350 MeV), while antikaon mass decreases by about 100 MeV.
- The kaon-nucleon sigma-term Sigma_KN is in the range 370-405 MeV for nucleon strangeness content y ~ 0.1-0.2, or about 450 MeV from lattice gauge calculations with y ~ 0.33.
- The lambda potential at normal nuclear matter density is empirically about -30 MeV from hypernuclear structure.
- Dirac-Bruckner-Hartree-Fock calculations give lambda potential ranging from -25 to -40 MeV.
- At AGS energies (6 A GeV Au+Au), proton flow data are well described by a soft nuclear equation of state with incompressibility K = 200 MeV, while a stiff equation of state with K = 380 MeV gives too large a proton flow.
- The kaon-nucleon scattering cross section is about 10 mb.

## Context

This review extends earlier work by Randrup and Ko (1980) on kaons as messengers of dense matter, and by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) on antikaon mass reduction in dense matter leading to possible kaon condensation in neutron stars. It builds on transport model studies by Li et al. (1995) showing kaon directed flow sensitivity to the kaon potential, and compares with experimental data from the FOPI collaboration at GSI, the E895 collaboration at AGS, and the WA98 collaboration at SPS. The paper enables future experimental and theoretical work to determine the lambda potential more precisely and to study antikaon flow at AGS and SPS energies.
