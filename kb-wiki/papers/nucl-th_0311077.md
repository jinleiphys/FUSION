# Theory of the radiative width of a highly excited nucleus

> A microscopic theory of the electromagnetic radiation emitted by a highly excited nucleus is developed on the basis of the Landau theory of a Fermi liquid. Closed formulae are obtained for the mean radiative width and its mean square fluctuation from level to level. The temperatures of many nuclei are found from the observed widths. The relaxation time is estimated from the experimental data on the radiative-width fluctuations. The regions of applicability of the various types of relations between the relaxation time and the lifetime of the compound nucleus, as well as the relevant physical consequences, are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives a closed-form microscopic expression for the mean radiative width of a highly excited nucleus based on the Landau theory of a Fermi liquid, but the result is strictly valid only when the temperature satisfies \(T \ll \hbar v_f / 2R \sim 5\) MeV, corresponding to the regime where the quasiparticle transit time across the nucleus is much shorter than the radiation period.

## Method

The authors treat the nucleus as a Fermi liquid of quasiparticles moving along straight-line chords between collisions with the nuclear surface. Using the correspondence principle, they convert the classical electric-dipole radiation intensity from a single quasiparticle into a quantum emission probability, then integrate over the Fermi distribution of quasiparticle energies and over the thermal distribution of emitted gamma-ray energies. The mean-square fluctuation of the radiative width is computed from the instantaneous occupation-number fluctuations of fermion states, and the relaxation time is extracted by relating these fluctuations to the observable level-to-level variance through a time-correlation function that accounts for the finite lifetime of the compound nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Characteristic energy \(\eps_0 = \hbar v_f / 2R \sim 5\) MeV, setting the upper temperature limit for the theory's validity
- Mean radiative width formula: \(\overline{\Gamma}_\gamma \propto T^5\) with numerical coefficient \((1024/105\pi^2)\zeta(5) \cong 1\)
- For nonspherical lanthanide compound nuclei, extracted temperatures range from \(T = 0.39\) MeV (\({}^{182}_{73}\)Ta) to \(T = 0.52\) MeV (\({}^{163}_{66}\)Dy)
- For nonspherical actinide compound nuclei, extracted temperatures range from \(T = 0.30\) MeV (\({}^{233}_{90}\)Th, \({}^{239}_{92}\)U) to \(T = 0.35\) MeV (\({}^{234}_{91}\)Pa, \({}^{244}_{95}\)Am)
- For spherical nuclei: \({}^{198}_{79}\)Au gives \(T = 0.45\) MeV, \({}^{202}_{80}\)Hg gives \(T = 0.56\) MeV, \({}^{204}_{81}\)Tl gives \(T = 0.62\) MeV
- Relaxation time estimates from radiative-width fluctuations: \(\hbar/\tau = 26\) eV for \({}^{156}_{64}\)Gd (10 levels, \(\overline{\Gamma}_\gamma = 0.11\) eV, rms fluctuation 0.016 eV); \(\hbar/\tau = 52\) eV for \({}^{158}_{64}\)Gd (11 levels, \(\overline{\Gamma}_\gamma = 0.089\) eV, rms fluctuation 0.0087 eV); \(\hbar/\tau = 39\) eV for \({}^{166}_{67}\)Ho (21 levels, \(\overline{\Gamma}_\gamma = 0.091\) eV, rms fluctuation 0.0099 eV); corresponding to \(\tau \sim 10^{-17}\) s

## Context

This work extends the semi-phenomenological treatment of Nosov (1969) by providing a fully microscopic derivation of the \(\overline{\Gamma}_\gamma \propto T^5\) law using Landau Fermi-liquid theory (Landau, 1957; Lifshitz and Pitaevskii, 1978). The extracted nuclear temperatures from observed radiative widths enable systematic comparison across the lanthanide and actinide regions, while the fluctuation analysis provides the first direct experimental estimates of the nuclear relaxation time. The authors note that for compound-nucleus total widths \(\Gamma \geq 100\) eV, the condition \(\Gamma\tau/\hbar \ll 1\) may break down, raising questions about the validity of thermal-equilibrium assumptions in neutron-evaporation temperature determinations (e.g., Ericson fluctuations, 1963).
