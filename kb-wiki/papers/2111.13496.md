# $D^+D^-$ hadronic atom and its production in $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ collisions

> There must be Coulomb bound states of a pair of hadrons, which are stable against the strong interaction, with opposite electric charges. Such bound states are hadronic atoms. We study the properties and the production of the ground-state $D^+D^-$ hadronic atom $A_{D^+D^-}$, called dionium, with quantum numbers $J^{PC}=0^{++}$. Using a nonrelativistic effective field theory for the $D^0\bar{D}^0$-$D^+D^-$ coupled-channel system, the mass of the ground-state dionium is predicted to be $(3739.3 \pm 0.1)~\text{MeV}$, with the binding energy reduced by about 10% compared to the Coulomb binding energy due to the strong interaction. Its width for the decay into the neutral $D^0\bar D^0$ channel is predicted to be $1.8^{+1.4}_{-0.6}$ keV using lattice inputs for the $D\bar D$ strong interaction. The cross section for the inclusive prompt production of the dionium at CMS and LHCb and that for the direct production $p\bar p\to A_{D^+D^-}$ at PANDA are estimated at an order-of-magnitude level. In particular, we expect that $\mathcal{O}(10^3\sim 10^5)$ events of the reaction chain $p\bar p\to A_{D^+D^-}\to D^0\bar D^0 \to K^-π^+K^+π^-$ can be collected at PANDA, and valuable information on the charmed meson interaction and on understanding charmoniumlike states will be obtained.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that a Coulomb-bound $D^+D^-$ hadronic atom (dionium) must exist regardless of whether a strongly-bound $D\bar{D}$ molecular state exists, with its binding energy reduced by about 10% from the pure Coulomb value due to strong interaction corrections, and its production cross sections at LHC and PANDA are estimated at order-of-magnitude level.

## Method

The authors use a nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT) for the $D^0\bar{D}^0$-$D^+D^-$ coupled-channel system, solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a contact interaction potential at leading order. The strong interaction low-energy constants are fixed using lattice QCD inputs for the isoscalar $D\bar{D}$ scattering length and binding energy from Prelovsek (2020), and the Deser-Goldberger-Baumann-Thirring (DGBT) formula is applied to extract the dionium binding energy and decay width. For production estimates, the inclusive $pp$ cross section uses PYTHIA8 Monte Carlo simulations combined with NREFT final-state interactions, while the $p\bar{p}$ direct production cross section at PANDA is estimated using a flavor SU(4) model with $\Sigma_c$ exchange.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state dionium mass: $(3739.3 \pm 0.1)$ MeV
- Dionium binding energy: $22.9^{+0.3}_{-0.4}$ keV (compared to pure Coulomb binding energy of 24.9 keV)
- Dionium decay width to $D^0\bar{D}^0$: $1.8^{+1.4}_{-0.6}$ keV
- Bohr radius of dionium: $28.91$ fm
- Inclusive prompt production cross section at CMS ($\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV, $10<p_T<50$ GeV): $1^{+7}_{-1}$ pb (cutoff $\Lambda=0.5$ GeV) or $49^{+76}_{-33}$ pb (cutoff $\Lambda=1.0$ GeV)
- Expected events at PANDA for the reaction chain $p\bar{p}\to A_{D^+D^-}\to D^0\bar{D}^0 \to K^-\pi^+K^+\pi^-$: $\mathcal{O}(10^3\sim 10^5)$

## Context

This work extends the NREFT framework previously applied to pionic atoms by Gasser (2007, 2009) and to the $X$ atom (a $D^*D$ hadronic atom) by Zhang (2020) into the charmed meson sector with nonperturbative strong interactions. It uses lattice QCD inputs from Prelovsek (2020) that indicate a possible $D\bar{D}$ bound state with $J^{PC}=0^{++}$ and binding energy $4.0^{+5.0}_{-3.7}$ MeV. The production estimates enable experimental searches at LHCb, CMS, and PANDA, where the dionium decay to $D^0\bar{D}^0$ can provide information on charmed meson interactions and help understand the nature of charmoniumlike states such as the $X(3872)$.
