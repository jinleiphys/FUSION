# The Pauli principle, QRPA and the two-neutrino double beta decay

> We examine the violation of the Pauli exclusion principle in the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculation of the two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements, which has its origin in the quasi-boson approximation. For that purpose we propose a new renormalized QRPA with proton-neutron pairing method (full-RQRPA) for nuclear structure studies, which includes ground state correlation beyond the QRPA. This is achieved by using of renormalized quasi-boson approximation, in which the Pauli exclusion principle is taken into account more carefully. The full-RQRPA has been applied to two-neutrino double beta decay of $^{76}Ge$, $^{82}Se$, $^{128}Te$ and $^{130}Te$. The nuclear matrix elements have been found significantly less sensitive to the increasing strength of particle-particle interaction in the physically interesting region in comparison with QRPA results. The strong differences between the results of both methods indicate that the Pauli exclusion principle plays an important role in the evaluation of the double beta decay. The inclusion of the Pauli principle removes the difficulties with the strong dependence on the particle-particle strength $g_{pp}$ in the QRPA on the two-neutrino double beta decay.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the strong sensitivity of the two-neutrino double beta decay nuclear matrix elements to the particle-particle interaction strength in standard QRPA calculations is an artifact of the quasi-boson approximation's violation of the Pauli principle; the proposed full-RQRPA method, which includes ground state correlations beyond QRPA via a renormalized quasi-boson approximation, yields matrix elements that are significantly less sensitive to this interaction strength in the physically interesting region.

## Method

The authors developed a renormalized QRPA with proton-neutron pairing (full-RQRPA) by replacing the uncorrelated HFB ground state with the correlated RPA ground state in the commutator of bifermion operators, introducing renormalization factors D that account for Pauli blocking. The method requires solving a doubly iterative system: the full-RQRPA eigenvalue equation for all multipolarities J^pi and the nonlinear equation for the renormalization factors D, which depend on the backward-going amplitudes Y. The method was applied to calculate the Gamow-Teller matrix element M^{2nu}_{GT} for the two-neutrino double beta decay of 76Ge, 82Se, 128Te and 130Te using a G-matrix derived from the Bonn one-boson exchange potential with a Wood-Saxon single-particle basis.

## Key numbers

- For 76Ge, agreement with the experimental M^{2nu}_{GT} was achieved at g_pp approximately 0.95
- For 82Se, agreement with the experimental M^{2nu}_{GT} was achieved at g_pp approximately 1.00
- For 128Te and 130Te, agreement required higher values of the renormalization parameter g_pp approximately 1.3
- The combination of 1+, 2+, 2- and 3- multipolarities was found to exhaust approximately the most of the correlations beyond RPA, showing results as good as the full set of 12 multipolarities ranging from 0+ to 5-
- The physically acceptable region of the g_pp parameter was taken as 0.8 to 1.2 (or up to 1.3 for Te isotopes)

## Context

This work extends the renormalized RPA approach of Catara et al. (1994) by incorporating proton-neutron pairing, and is similar to a method proposed by Toivanen and Suhonen (1995) which did not include proton-neutron pairing. The paper addresses the well-known problem in QRPA calculations of double beta decay, first identified by Vogel and Zirnbauer (1986), where the nuclear matrix elements show extreme sensitivity to the particle-particle interaction strength. The full-RQRPA enables more reliable predictions of two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements and could be extended to studies of neutrinoless double beta decay, single beta decay, and pion double charge exchange reactions.
