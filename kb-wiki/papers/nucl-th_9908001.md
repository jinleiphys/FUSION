# Information Theory, Quark Clusters in Nuclei, and Parton Distributions

> The procedure of maximization of information entropy can be used to improve our knowledge of parton distributions. This method has been applied in order to achieve improved description of the nuclear effect in Y production due to gluon distribution modification in nuclei.

## Key claim

The procedure of maximizing information entropy can be used to improve parton, specifically gluon, distributions in nuclei, under the constraint of fixed total gluon momentum fraction, in order to achieve an improved description of the nuclear effect in Upsilon production that the original quark-cluster model could not describe.

## Method

The author applied the principle of maximum information entropy to modify the functional form of gluon momentum distributions in nucleons (3q clusters) and 6q clusters, adding a linear term in x to the standard (1-x)^c form while keeping the total gluon momentum fraction fixed (z_3^(g)=0.57, z_6^(g)=0.60). The new parameters C_N and C'_N were determined by maximizing the information entropy S_N with respect to C_N, with the exponents c_3=6 and c_6=10 held fixed. The resulting corrected gluon distributions were then used to recalculate the Upsilon suppression exponent alpha in proton-nucleus collisions and compared with data from Fermilab.

## Key numbers

- Corrected gluon distribution parameters: (C_3, C'_3) = (1.163, 0.812) and (C_6, C'_6) = (1.411, 0.987), compared to uncorrected (C_3, C'_3)_uncorr = (4.130, 0.0) and (C_6, C'_6)_uncorr = (6.624, 0.0)
- Fixed gluon momentum fractions: z_3^(g) = 0.57 for nucleons, z_6^(g) = 0.60 for 6q clusters
- Fixed exponents: c_3 = 6, c_6 = 10
- J/Psi absorption cross section: sigma^(3)_abs ≈ 3.5 mbarn/nucleon
- Upsilon absorption cross section assumed: ≈ 3 mbarn/nucleon
- Quark masses used: m_D = 1.864 GeV, m_B = 5.278 GeV
- 6q cluster probability f ranges: 0.040-0.052 for deuterium
- Best-fit exponents for NMC data: (b_3^u, a_3, b_6^u, a_6) = (3, 9, 10, 11); direct gamma data suggest (c_3, c_6) = (6, 10)

## Context

This work extends the quark-cluster model of the EMC effect, previously applied to J/Psi suppression in hadron-nucleus collisions by Sato (1988) and others, to the case of Upsilon suppression where the original model proved inadequate. The information entropy maximization approach follows the method of Plastino (1994) for evaluating probability distributions under constraints. The corrected gluon distributions maintain good agreement with J/Psi data from Alde (1991) while improving agreement with Upsilon data from the same experiment, and the author notes that an alternative explanation by Liuti (1995) attributes the smaller Upsilon suppression to Q^2 evolution effects. The model enables predictions for quarkonium suppression at RHIC at sqrt(s)=200 GeV/nucleon.
