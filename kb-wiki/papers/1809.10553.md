# A beam-beam monitoring detector for the MPD experiment at NICA

> The Multi-Purpose Detector (MPD) is to be installed at the Nuclotron Ion Collider fAcility (NICA) of the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR). Its main goal is to study the phase diagram of the strongly interacting matter produced in heavy-ion collisions. These studies, while providing insight into the physics of heavy-ion collisions, are relevant for improving our understanding of the evolution of the early Universe and the formation of neutron stars. In order to extend the MPD trigger capabilities, we propose to include a high granularity beam-beam monitoring detector (BE-BE) to provide a level-0 trigger signal with an expected time resolution of 30 ps. This new detector will improve the determination of the reaction plane by the MPD experiment, a key measurement for flow studies that provides physics insight into the early stages of the reaction. In this work, we use simulated Au+Au collisions at NICA energies to show the potential of such a detector to determine the event plane resolution, providing further redundancy to the detectors originally considered for this purpose namely, the Fast Forward Detector (FFD) and the Hadron Calorimeter (HCAL). We also show our results for the time resolution studies of two prototype cells carried out at the T10 beam line at the CERN PS complex.

## Key claim
The proposed BE-BE detector can achieve a time resolution sufficient for a level-0 trigger (target 30 ps) and provide event-plane resolution for flow studies, but the final time resolution depends on optimizing the geometry, number of SiPM sensors, and the number of charged particles hitting the active volume; the 30 ps target is an extrapolation from single-cell prototype measurements and simulations, not a directly measured value.

## Method
The authors simulated 95000 minimum bias Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 9 GeV using UrQMD event generation and the MPD-ROOT offline framework with GEANT-3 transport, computing the event-plane resolution for n=1 from the multiplicity per hexagonal cell. They also performed beam tests at the CERN PS T10 facility with 5 GeV pions, measuring time resolution of two BC-404 plastic scintillator hexagonal cell prototypes (5 cm high, 2 cm wide) coupled to either a Hamamatsu PMT R6249 or a SensL C-60035-4P-EVB SiPM, using the ALICE V0 front-end electronics and HPTDC for timing. Additional GEANT-4 simulations varied scintillator material (BC404, BC422), cell thickness (1 cm, 2 cm), and number of photosensors (1, 2, 4) to extrapolate achievable resolution.

## Key numbers
- Best measured time resolution for the PMT-coupled prototype (BBp1): 68 ± 5 ps at 800 V operational voltage.
- Best measured time resolution for the SiPM-coupled prototype (BBp2): 45 ± 2 ps for ADC range 830-880 (arbitrary units).
- Extrapolated time resolution for a 5 cm high BC404 hexagon coupled to two SiPM sensors: 31 ps (central value, assuming 1/√N scaling).
- Maximum event-plane resolution from simulation: 48% for centrality range 25-45% in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 9 GeV.
- Simulated average number of charged particles hitting the innermost ring of the detector: 7 per event for minimum bias Au+Au at √sNN = 11 GeV.
- FEE time resolution used in analysis: 100 ps (reported by ALICE V0 group).

## Context
This work extends the beam-beam counter concept from the MPD Conceptual Design Report (CDR) and is inspired by the PHENIX-RHIC beam-beam counter geometry, the ALICE V0 detector (using BC404/BC408 scintillator with PMTs and optical fibers), and the upcoming ALICE FIT detector (targeting 30 ps time resolution with quartz radiators and MCPs). The BE-BE is designed to complement the Fast Forward Detector (FFD) and Hadron Calorimeter (HCAL) for event-plane determination at NICA. The results enable further optimization of the detector geometry and front-end electronics to achieve the 30 ps trigger requirement for the MPD experiment, with planned future tests using cosmic rays and radiation sources.
