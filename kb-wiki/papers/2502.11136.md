# Heavy-flavor multimodal fragmentation to $S$-wave pentacharms at next-generation hadron colliders

> We investigate the leading-power fragmentation of fully charmed pentaquark states ($S$-wave $|c\bar{c}ccc\rangle$ pentacharms) at hadron colliders. We introduce a new set of multimodal collinear fragmentation functions, named PQ5Q1.0. They rely on an enhanced calculation of the initial-scale input for the constituent heavy-quark fragmentation channel, making them well suited to describe the short-distance emission of either a compact multicharm state or a dicharm-charm-dicharm configuration. To explore phenomenological implications, we use the (sym)JETHAD multimodular interface to study NLL/NLO$^+$ semi-inclusive production rates for pentacharm-plus-jet systems at the forthcoming HL-LHC and the future FCC. Our analysis represents a further step toward bridging the domains of hadronic structure, precision QCD, and exotic matter.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new set of multimodal collinear fragmentation functions (PQ5Q1.0) for fully charmed pentaquark states, claiming that these functions can describe the short-distance emission of either a compact multicharm state or a dicharm-charm-dicharm configuration, but the analysis is exploratory and the initial-scale input relies on a free parameter (the average squared transverse momentum) that must be determined phenomenologically.

## Method

The author derives initial-scale fragmentation functions for charm-to-pentacharm fragmentation using a Suzuki-model framework that incorporates transverse-momentum dependence, then takes the collinear limit by replacing the squared transverse momentum with its average value. These initial-scale inputs are combined with DGLAP evolution equations to produce the PQ5Q1.0 fragmentation function set, and the (sym)JETHAD multimodular interface is used to compute NLL/NLO+ semi-inclusive production rates for pentacharm-plus-jet systems at HL-LHC and FCC energies.

## Key numbers

* Pentacharm mass set to M_P5c = 5 m_c, with charm-quark mass m_c = 1.5 GeV
* Hadron decay constant f_B = 0.25 GeV used in the normalization factor
* Center-of-mass energies studied: 14 TeV (HL-LHC) and 100 TeV (FCC)
* Average squared transverse momentum parameter set to <q_T^2> = 1 GeV^2 as an upper-bound estimate

## Context

This work extends the author's previous development of fragmentation functions for tetraquarks (TQHL1.1 and TQ4Q1.1 families, Celiberto 2024) to the pentacharm sector, building on the Suzuki-model approach previously applied to doubly heavy tetraquarks by Nejad (2021) and to fully charmed tetraquarks by Feng (2020). The PQ5Q1.0 functions enable future studies of pentacharm production at next-generation colliders, providing a framework to discriminate between compact multicharm and molecular-like exotic formation dynamics.
