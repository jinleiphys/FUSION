# Equivalent Photon Approach to Simultaneous Excitation in Heavy Ion Collision

> We apply the Equivalent Photon Approximation to calculate cross sections for the simultaneous excitation of two heavy ions in relativistic collisions. We study especially the excitation of two nuclei to a 1- - state and show that the equations are symmetric with respect to both ions. We also examine the limit in which the excitation energy of one of the nuclei goes to zero, which gives the elastic case. Finally a few remarks about the limits of this approach are made.

# Equivalent Photon Approach to Simultaneous Excitation in Heavy Ion Collision

## Key claim

The Equivalent Photon Approximation (EPA) can be applied to calculate cross sections for the simultaneous excitation of both projectile and target nuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, yielding a symmetric result that depends on the product of the nuclear transition strengths and the nuclear size, but only under the long-wavelength approximation (kR < 1) and for excitation energies low enough that ω ≈ Δ < 1/R.

## Method

The authors use the Equivalent Photon Approximation, where the cross section for simultaneous excitation of two nuclei is expressed as an integral over the equivalent photon spectrum of one nucleus (emitting a virtual photon during its own excitation) multiplied by the photoabsorption cross section of the other nucleus. The matrix elements for the nuclear transitions are expressed in terms of B(EJ) and B(MJ) values under the long-wavelength limit, and the transverse momentum integration is cut off by nuclear form factors, using either a Gaussian form factor or a simple cutoff at 1/R.

## Key numbers

- The cross section for simultaneous excitation of two nuclei to a 1⁻ (GDR) state, using a simple cutoff form factor and keeping only the leading term for large γ, is σ = (4πα²)/(81R²) × B(E1,Δ₁) × B(E1,Δ₂), where R is approximately the sum of the nuclear radii
- The equivalent photon number for the GDR case, using a Gaussian form factor, is n_Δ(ω) = (α B(E1))/(9π) × [λ²/2 × exp(-2q_min²/λ²) - q_min² × E₁(2q_min²/λ²)], where λ = (1/λ₁² + 1/λ₂²)^(-1/2) and q_min = Δ₁²/γ² + 2Δ₁ω/γ + ω²/γ²
- The simple cutoff approximation for the equivalent photon number gives n(ω) = (α B(E1,Δ₁))/(9πR²) for ω < γ/R
- In the Goldhaber-Teller model, B(E1,0⁺→1⁻,Δ) = (9/(2m_NΔ)) × (NZ/A)
- For M1 transitions, the equivalent photon number is n_Δ₁(ω) = (α/(9π)) × B(M1)/R² for ω < γ/R, and the cross section is σ = (4πα²)/(81R²) × B(M1,Δ₁) × B(M1,Δ₂)
- The static magnetic moment limit gives B(M1) = (9/(4π))μ²

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Hencken (1995) for calculating equivalent photon spectra corresponding to nuclear transitions, and simplifies the results previously derived by Benesh and Friar (1994) for simultaneous projectile and target excitation, making the physics more transparent. The approach enables calculation of cross sections for mutual excitation of both nuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, with the result depending on nuclear size R (unlike the earlier Benesh and Friar result), and opens the possibility to study higher multipolarities and transitions with different multipolarities for the two ions, though these are expected to have smaller cross sections.
