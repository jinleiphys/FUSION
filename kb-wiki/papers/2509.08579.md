# Lattice calculation of the Sn isotopes near the proton dripline

> We present the first $\textit{ab initio}$ lattice calculations of the proton-rich tin isotopes $^{99}$Sn to $^{102}$Sn using nuclear lattice effective field theory with high-fidelity two- and three-nucleon forces. For a given set of three-nucleon couplings, we reproduce binding energies with $\sim 1\%$ accuracy for the even-even systems, and obtain energy splitting and two-nucleon separation energies in agreement with experiment. Our results confirm the $N=50$ shell closure and reveal that the binding energy of $^{99}$Sn lies below values extrapolated from heavier isotopes.

## Key claim

The first *ab initio* lattice calculations of proton-rich tin isotopes (⁹⁹Sn–¹⁰²Sn) reproduce experimental binding energies and two-neutron separation energies, but only after a percent-level retuning of two three-nucleon force couplings; without this adjustment, the original N³LO Hamiltonian systematically underbinds by about 5%.

## Method

The authors used Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) on a 12³ × Lₜ lattice with spatial spacing 1.32 fm, employing high-fidelity chiral interactions at N³LO with non-locally regulated two-nucleon forces and locally smeared three-nucleon interactions. Ground-state energies were extracted via Euclidean time projection with wavefunction matching, using a simplified Hamiltonian for evolution and treating the full chiral Hamiltonian perturbatively. Simulations were performed for even–even (¹⁰⁰Sn, ¹⁰²Sn) and odd-A (⁹⁹Sn, ¹⁰¹Sn) isotopes, with the even–even systems benefiting from a suppressed sign problem.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of ¹⁰⁰Sn: 825.2 ± 3.0 MeV (N³LO*), compared to experimental 825.16 ± 0.24 MeV
- Binding energy of ⁹⁹Sn: 804 ± 8 MeV (N³LO*), compared to experimental 807.9 ± 0.6 MeV (extrapolated)
- Binding energy of ¹⁰²Sn: 848.1 ± 3.4 MeV (N³LO*), compared to experimental 849.09 ± 0.10 MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy S₂ₙ(¹⁰²Sn): 22.9 ± 4.6 MeV (N³LO*), compared to experimental 23.9 ± 0.3 MeV
- ¹⁰⁰Sn–⁹⁹Sn splitting: 21.2 ± 8.6 MeV (N³LO*), compared to experimental 17.3 ± 0.7 MeV
- In ¹⁰⁰Sn: kinetic plus two-nucleon contribution = 717.9 MeV, Coulomb contribution = -366.8 MeV, three-nucleon contribution = 435.6 MeV
- Lattice spacing a = 1.32 fm, corresponding to maximum momentum 470 MeV
- Computational cost: approximately 36 × 10⁶ core hours (500 × 10³ GPU hours) on JUPITER

## Context

This work extends NLEFT calculations from lighter systems (up to A=58, as in Elhatisari (2022)) into the A≈100 region for the first time, targeting the proton-rich tin isotopes near the doubly magic ¹⁰⁰Sn. It confirms the N=50 shell closure and the doubly magic nature of ¹⁰⁰Sn predicted by coupled-cluster calculations (Morris, 2017), while also showing that three-nucleon forces calibrated up to ⁴⁰Ca require modest retuning for heavier nuclei. The results establish a foundation for systematic lattice studies of the entire tin isotopic chain, including excited states, charge radii, and beta decays.
