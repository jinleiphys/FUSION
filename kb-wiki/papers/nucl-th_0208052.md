# Fluctuations of the Elliptic Flow

> We suggest to perform systematic measurements of the elliptic flow fluctuations which are sensitive to the early stage dynamics of heavy-ion collisions at high-energies. Significant flow fluctuations are shown to be generated due to the formation of topological clusters and development of the filamentation instability. The statistical noise and hydrodynamic fluctuations are also estimated.

# Fluctuations of the Elliptic Flow

## Key claim

Systematic measurements of elliptic flow fluctuations on an event-by-event basis can reveal early-stage dynamics of heavy-ion collisions, including exotic phenomena such as topological cluster formation and filamentation instability, provided that statistical noise and impact-parameter variations are properly subtracted.

## Method

The authors analyze the variance of the second Fourier harmonic $v_2$ using the standard method of reaction-plane reconstruction, where the impact parameter direction is determined from one set of particles and the Fourier coefficients are computed from a separate set. They derive analytic expressions for the statistical noise contribution to $v_2$ fluctuations, then estimate additional contributions from impact-parameter variation, hydrodynamic multiplicity fluctuations, cluster formation, and filamentation instability using analytic models and parameterizations of experimental data from Au-Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- Statistical noise estimate: $\delta v_2 = 1/(\langle R_2 \rangle \sqrt{2 \langle N \rangle})$, where $\langle R_2 \rangle$ is the reaction-plane resolution factor and $\langle N \rangle$ is the mean multiplicity.
- For Au-Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV, $\langle v_2 \rangle$ parameterized as a polynomial in impact parameter $b$ (in fm): $\langle v_2 \rangle = a_1 b + a_2 b^2 + a_3 b^3 + a_4 b^4 + a_5 b^5 + a_6 b^6$ with $a_1 = -3.94 \times 10^{-4}$, $a_2 = 2.1 \times 10^{-3}$, $a_3 = -7.06 \times 10^{-5}$, $a_4 = -3.2 \times 10^{-5}$, $a_5 = 3.58 \times 10^{-6}$, $a_6 = -1.17 \times 10^{-7}$, valid for $b < b_{\rm max} = 14.7$ fm.
- At $b = 5$ fm ($\langle v_2 \rangle \approx 0.03$), impact-parameter fluctuations give $\delta v_2 \approx 8 \times 10^{-4} \, \delta N_p$; for $\delta N_p = 30$ and $\langle N \rangle = 500$, this equals the statistical noise.
- Ratio of hydrodynamic fluctuations to statistical noise is $\sqrt{2} \langle R_2 \rangle \langle v_2 \rangle P_h$, which for $\langle R_2 \rangle = 0.6$, $\langle v_2 \rangle = 0.07$, and $P_h = 0.4$ gives 0.02.
- Hydrodynamic index for pions: $P_\pi \approx 0.4$ from logarithmic slope of $v_2$ vs. $dN/dy$ in Teaney (2001).
- Cluster production upper bound: up to about 70 clusters per unit rapidity in central Au-Au at RHIC, with $f \le 0.5$ and $k \approx 4$ hadrons per cluster, potentially doubling multiplicity fluctuations relative to Poissonian.
- Filamentation instability maximal $v_2$: $v_2 = 1/(\pi - 1)$ from the idealized azimuthal distribution $P(\phi) \sim [1 - \delta(\phi - \pi/2) - \delta(\phi - 3\pi/2)]/(2(\pi - 1))$.

## Context

This paper extends the standard elliptic flow analysis of Voloshin (1994) and Poskanzer (1998) by proposing event-by-event fluctuation measurements, motivated by the success of hydrodynamic descriptions of mean $v_2$ at RHIC (Ollitrault, Kolb 1999, Teaney 2001) and the puzzle of short equilibration times below 1 fm/c (Heinz 2001). The work builds on earlier studies of filamentation instability by Mrowczynski (1996) and topological cluster formation by Shuryak (2002), and responds to the first inconclusive fluctuation measurement by STAR (Adler 2002). The proposed analysis enables discrimination between hydrodynamic flow, statistical noise, and non-flow correlations from clusters and instabilities, with specific predictions for centrality dependence and particle-species dependence that can be tested with existing RHIC data.
