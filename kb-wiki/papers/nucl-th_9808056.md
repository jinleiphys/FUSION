# Effects of hyperons on the dynamical deconfinement transition in cold neutron star matter

> The influence of the presence of hyperons in dense hadronic matter on the quantum nucleation of quark matter is examined at low temperatures relevant to neutron star cores. We calculate the equation of state and the composition of matter before and after deconfinement by using a relativistic mean-field theory and an MIT bag model, respectively; the case in which hyperons are present in the hadronic system is considered, together with the case of the system without hyperons. We find that strangeness contained in hyperons acts to reduce a density jump at deconfinement as well as a lepton fraction in the hadronic phase. As a result of these reductions, a quark matter droplet being in a virtual or real state has its effective mass lightened and its electric charge diminished into nearly zero. The Coulomb screening of leptons on the droplet charge, which has significance to the droplet growth after nucleation in the absence of hyperons, is thus shown to be of little consequence. If the effective droplet mass is small enough to become comparable to the height of the potential barrier, the effect of relativity brings about an exponential increase in the rate of droplet formation via quantum tunneling, whereas the role played by energy dissipation in decelerating the droplet formation, dominant for matter without hyperons, becomes of less importance. For matter with and without hyperons, we estimate the overpressure needed to form the first droplet in the star during the compression due to stellar spin-down or mass accretion from a companion star. The temperature at which a crossover from the quantum nucleation to the Arrhenius-type thermal nucleation takes place is shown to be large compared with the temperature of matter in the core.

## Key claim

The presence of hyperons in dense hadronic matter reduces the density jump at deconfinement and the lepton fraction, which lightens the effective mass of a quark matter droplet and diminishes its electric charge to nearly zero; consequently, Coulomb screening by leptons becomes negligible and the quantum nucleation rate increases exponentially if the effective droplet mass becomes comparable to the potential barrier height, while energy dissipation becomes less important than in matter without hyperons.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean-field theory (with parameters incorporating the binding energy of Λ in saturated nuclear matter) to calculate the equation of state and composition of β-equilibrated hadronic matter with and without hyperons, and an MIT bag model for quark matter. They then applied a quantum nucleation theory based on Lifshitz and Kagan (1967), extended to include relativistic effects, Ohmic energy dissipation from collisions of low-energy excitations, and dynamical compressibility, to compute the droplet formation time and critical overpressure for neutron star compression scenarios.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density: n₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³
- The static deconfinement transition from uniform hadronic to uniform quark matter was expected to occur in the density range ∼5–10 n₀
- The crossover temperature from quantum tunneling to Arrhenius-type thermal nucleation is shown to be large compared with typical neutron star core temperatures (below ∼0.1 MeV)
- In the weak dissipation regime, the underbarrier action at zero energy is A(E₀=0) = (5√2 π²/16) |1 − n₂/n₁| √(σₛ ρ₁) R_c^(7/2)
- The compressibility correction term A_C(T) is proportional to (T/T₀)⁴ for T ≪ T₀

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Iida and Sato, 1997, 1998) on two-flavor quark matter droplet formation in nuclear matter without strangeness, which found that dissipation effects significantly increase the overpressure needed for droplet formation. It incorporates hyperons, whose presence was predicted in dense matter by earlier work (Glendenning, 1985; Heiselberg et al., 1993), and uses a relativistic mean-field model with Λ binding energy parameters from earlier nuclear physics. The results enable estimates of whether quark matter can appear in neutron stars during spin-down or accretion, and constrain the bag-model parameter space (bag constant, QCD fine structure constant, strange quark mass) where deconfinement is expected.
