# Holography in Quark-Gluon Plasma and Neutron Stars

> In this thesis, QCD is studied from three different directions, with one overarching theme: holography. The holographic duality allows certain strongly coupled QFTs to be described in terms of much simpler classical gravity in one dimension more. The first direction from which QCD is studied in this thesis is by examining the effects of an external magnetic field on a particular holographic model of QCD, yielding interesting qualitative insight. The second approach examines how, in the same model, one can describe dense baryonic configurations, providing a new way to study the matter composing neutron stars. Indeed, the equation of state produced in this way is subsequently used to compute several neutron star properties which are observable, or will be in the near future. The last direction contains no holographic computations per se, but does incorporate several qualitative insights from holography into a new heavy ion code called Trajectum. This will in the near future be used to perform a Bayesian analysis, whereby it is hoped that these qualitative insights from holography can be tested on experimental data, to see how well the ideas coming from holography match up with experiment.

## Key claim
The thesis demonstrates that holographic duality can provide qualitatively reasonable descriptions of strongly coupled QCD phenomena across three regimes, external magnetic fields, dense baryonic matter for neutron stars, and heavy-ion collisions, but the results are explicitly approximate and rely on uncontrolled expansions, such as truncating the non-Abelian gauge field action to first order and assuming a homogeneous baryon distribution.

## Method
The author uses the V-QCD holographic model, a bottom-up approach with potentials tuned to match lattice QCD at zero chemical potential, to study three problems. First, the effects of an external magnetic field and an independent anisotropy on the chiral condensate are computed by solving the background geometry with a judicious choice of the parameter \(c\) in the \(w\)-potential. Second, baryons are incorporated via a homogeneous approximation that reduces the degrees of freedom to a single field \(h\), with a discontinuity at a bulk coordinate \(r_c\) related to baryon number density, and the resulting equation of state is matched to the SLy nuclear matter equation of state at low densities to form a hybrid neutron star model. Third, a new heavy-ion simulation framework called Trajectum is introduced, which reimplements existing models with modifications inspired by AdS/CFT for fluid initialization, and is tested against maximum a posteriori values from a previous Bayesian analysis.

## Key numbers
- The chiral condensate in V-QCD increases with magnetic field \(B\) at small temperatures, but decreases in a region between the chiral phase transition and the deconfinement transition, with inverse magnetic catalysis disappearing at larger \(B\).
- The region of inverse magnetic catalysis at finite baryon chemical potential \(\mu\) extends only to a finite \(\mu\), after which it disappears.
- The chiral transition temperature for \(B \parallel a\) crosses that of \(B \perp a\) roughly where \(B \sim a\).
- The distance into the holographic direction where baryons are located is \(\mathcal{O}(1)\), indicating the approximation is reasonable.
- The zero-temperature equation of state from holography exceeds the conformal value for the speed of sound in two places.
- The hybrid equation of state (holographic high-density part + SLy low-density part) is compatible with existing constraints on maximum mass, mass-to-radius relation, and tidal deformability.
- The post-merger spectrum frequency \(f_1\) from the hybrid equation of state is compatible with a universal relation, while \(f_2\) shifts to lower frequencies as the holographic part is incorporated up to lower densities.
- Trajectum yields excellent agreement when tested against maximum a posteriori values from Bernhard (2018).

## Context
This thesis extends prior holographic studies of QCD, such as the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model (Witten 1998, Sakai 2004, Sakai 2005) and earlier V-QCD work (Jokela 2018), by incorporating baryonic matter for neutron star physics and by introducing a new heavy-ion simulation framework. It directly addresses the conjecture by Giataganas (2017) that inverse magnetic catalysis is caused by anisotropy, naming this "inverse anisotropic catalysis." The results enable future Bayesian analyses of heavy-ion data with Trajectum to test holographic predictions against experiment, and provide a holography-based neutron star equation of state that can be compared with upcoming observational data from mergers and gravitational waves.
