# The Beta Decay of 44V

> We have calculated the $β^+$ decay of the proton rich nuclei $^{44}$V in a full fp-shell valence space. We obtain a theoretical half-life of 71~ms compared with the experimental value t$_{1/2}$ = 90 $\pm$ 25 ms. Besides, we make predictions for the Gamow-Teller strength functions corresponding to the decays of the 2$^+$ ground state and 6$^+$ isomer state.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the beta-plus decay of 44V using a full fp-shell valence space and obtain a theoretical half-life of 71 ms, which agrees with the experimental value of 90 +/- 25 ms only after applying an effective axial-vector coupling ratio (g_A/g_V)_eff = 0.77 times the bare value, indicating that the bare coupling overpredicts the Gamow-Teller strength in low-lying states.

## Method

The authors performed full fp-shell shell-model calculations without truncations using a slightly modified Kuo-Brown effective interaction (KB3). They computed the Gamow-Teller strength functions for both the 2+ ground state and the 6+ isomer state of 44V decaying to 44Ti, and validated their wave functions by comparing calculated level schemes and electromagnetic transitions in the mirror nucleus 44Sc against experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Theoretical half-life of 44V: 71 ms using (g_A/g_V)_eff = 0.77 times the bare value; 49 ms using the bare (g_A/g_V) value
- Experimental half-life of 44V: 90 +/- 25 ms
- Q_EC value for 44V decay to 44Ti: 13.7 MeV
- Predicted half-life of the 6+ isomer state of 44V via beta decay: 66 ms using the bare (g_A/g_V) value
- Predicted half-life of the 6+ isomer state via E4 transition to ground state: 1.08 days
- Predicted half-life of the 6+ isomer state of 44Sc via E4 transition: 2.96 days (experimental: 2.44 days)
- Predicted half-life of 44Sc ground state: 1.13 h (experimental: 3.9 h)
- Approximately 80% of the predicted Gamow-Teller strength for the ground state decay falls within the beta-plus energy window

## Context

This work extends the debate on Gamow-Teller strength quenching in nuclei, following studies in the sd shell by Brown and Wildenthal (1985) that suggested a quenching factor of about 0.5 relative to the Ikeda sum rule, and conflicting results from beta decay experiments in 33Ar (Borge et al., 1989) and 37Ca (Adelberger et al., 1991) that found no need for renormalization. The calculations provide predictions for an upcoming experiment at Ganil (Baumann et al., 1992) and aim to clarify whether the apparent quenching observed in (p,n) reactions and sd-shell beta decays is genuine or results from incomplete sampling of the strength function.
