# Gamow Shell Model Description of Weakly Bound Nuclei and Unbound Nuclear States

> We present the study of weakly bound, neutron-rich nuclei using the nuclear shell model employing the complex Berggren ensemble representing the bound single-particle states, unbound Gamow states, and the non-resonant continuum. In the proposed Gamow Shell Model, the Hamiltonian consists of a one-body finite depth (Woods-Saxon) potential and a residual two-body interaction. We discuss the basic ingredients of the Gamow Shell Model. The formalism is illustrated by calculations involving {\it several} valence neutrons outside the double-magic core: $^{6-10}$He and $^{18-22}$O.

## Key claim

The Gamow Shell Model (GSM) provides a unified description of weakly bound and unbound nuclear states by treating bound states, resonances, and the non-resonant continuum on equal footing through the complex Berggren ensemble, but the non-resonant continuum contribution is essential for accurate results and cannot be neglected even when narrow resonances dominate the expansion.

## Method

The authors construct a shell model Hamiltonian consisting of a one-body Woods-Saxon potential and a zero-range surface delta interaction, then diagonalize it in the complex Berggren basis which includes bound single-particle states, Gamow (resonant) states with outgoing wave boundary conditions, and discretized scattering states along a complex contour in the momentum plane. Matrix elements involving Gamow and scattering states are regularized using complex rotation techniques, and the resulting complex-symmetric Hamiltonian is solved to obtain many-body energies and wave functions for systems with several valence neutrons.

## Key numbers

- The Woods-Saxon potential used has radius R0 = 5.3 fm, diffuseness d = 0.65 fm, and spin-orbit strength Vso = 5.0 MeV, with central depth varied to simulate different situations
- Energies of all Poschl-Teller-Ginocchio resonances with width up to 90 MeV are reproduced with precision of at least 10^-6 MeV
- The 2p3/2 single-particle resonance at 0.25-i0.20 MeV (from a potential with V0=58 MeV) is expanded in a basis where the 2p3/2 resonance lies at 0.16-i0.09 MeV (V0^B=59 MeV)
- A bound 2p3/2 state at -0.91 MeV (V0=64 MeV) is expanded in a basis where the 2p3/2 state lies at -0.33 MeV (V0^B=62 MeV)
- The contour in the complex k-plane is discretized with n=60, 80, 100, 120, and 160 points, with final results obtained using Richardson extrapolation to achieve energy precision of order 1 keV

## Context

This work extends the continuum shell model (CSM) framework, including the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) of Bennaceur (1999, 2000), which previously could only handle one nucleon in the continuum and thus could not describe Borromean systems. The GSM enables treatment of multiple valence particles in the continuum, building on the Berggren completeness relation (Berggren, 1968) and the complex rotation regularization method of Gyarmati and Vertse (1971). The formalism is applied to the oxygen isotope chain (18-22O) with an inert 16O core and the helium chain (6-10He) with an inert 4He core, enabling future studies of weakly bound and unbound multi-neutron systems that were inaccessible to earlier continuum shell model approaches.
