# Longitudinal fluctuations and decorrelations of anisotropic flows at the LHC and RHIC energies

> We perform a systematic study on the decorrelation of anisotropic flows along the pseudorapidity in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at the LHC and RHIC energies. The dynamical evolution of the QGP fireball is simulated via the CLVisc (ideal) (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics model, with the fully fluctuating initial condition from A-Multi-Phase-Transport (AMPT) model. Detailed analysis is performed on the longitudinal decorrelations of elliptic, triangular and quadrangular flows in terms of flow vectors, flow magnitudes and flow orientations (event planes). It is found that pure flow magnitudes have smaller longitudinal decorrelation than pure flow orientations, and the decorrelation of flow vectors is a combined effect of both flow magnitudes and orientations. The longitudinal decorrelation of elliptic flow has a strong and non-monotonic centrality dependence due to the initial elliptic collision geometry: smallest decorrelation in mid-central collisions. In contrast, the decorrelations of triangular and quadrangular flows have weak centrality dependence, slightly larger decorrelations in more peripheral collisions. Our numerical results for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC are in good agreement with the ATLAS data, while our RHIC results predict much larger longitudinal decorrelations as compared to the LHC. We further analyze the longitudinal structures of the AMPT initial conditions and find that the final-state longitudinal decorrelation effects are strongly correlated with the lengths of the initial string structures in the AMPT model. The decorrelation effects are typically larger at lower collision energies and in more peripheral collisions due to shorter lengths of the string structures in the initial states.

## Key claim

The longitudinal decorrelation of anisotropic flows is a combined effect of both flow magnitude and flow orientation (event plane) decorrelations, with pure flow orientations exhibiting larger decorrelation than pure flow magnitudes; the decorrelation of elliptic flow shows a strong and non-monotonic centrality dependence, being smallest in mid-central collisions, while triangular and quadrangular flow decorrelations have weak centrality dependence.

## Method

The dynamical evolution of the quark-gluon plasma fireball was simulated using the CLVisc (ideal) (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics model, with fully fluctuating initial conditions from the A-Multi-Phase-Transport (AMPT) model (string-melting version). The longitudinal decorrelations of elliptic, triangular, and quadrangular flows were analyzed in terms of flow vectors, flow magnitudes, and flow orientations (event planes) using correlation functions between different pseudorapidity bins, including both three-rapidity-bin and four-rapidity-bin observables.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02A TeV and 2.76A TeV at the LHC, the initial proper time was taken as tau_0 = 0.2 fm; for Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV at RHIC, tau_0 = 0.4 fm.
- The freezeout temperature was taken as T_f = 137 MeV.
- The Gaussian smearing widths in transverse and pseudorapidity directions were sigma_r = 0.6 fm and sigma_eta_s = 0.6.
- The scale factor K was fixed as K = 1.6 for Pb+Pb at 2.76A TeV, K = 1.44 for Pb+Pb at 5.02A TeV, and K = 1.45 for Au+Au at 200A GeV from charged hadron multiplicity in central collisions.
- For LHC analysis, charged hadrons were selected with 0.5 < p_T < 3.0 GeV; for RHIC analysis, 0.3 < p_T < 3.0 GeV.
- The reference rapidity window was taken as 4.0 < |eta_r| < 4.9 for LHC and 3.0 < |eta_r| < 4.0 for RHIC.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of longitudinal flow decorrelations by the CMS Collaboration (2015) using the three-rapidity-bin method and the ATLAS Collaboration (2017) using multiple-particle correlations, providing a systematic comparison of flow vector, flow magnitude, and flow orientation decorrelations. The results show that the longitudinal decorrelation effects are strongly correlated with the lengths of initial string structures in the AMPT model, with larger decorrelations at lower collision energies and in more peripheral collisions due to shorter string lengths. The numerical results for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC agree well with ATLAS data, while predictions for RHIC show much larger longitudinal decorrelations compared to the LHC.
