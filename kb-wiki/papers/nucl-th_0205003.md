# Vortex Excitations in $α$ Cluster Condensed Nuclei

> We point out an intriguing possibility that there exist topological soliton-like excitations in nuclei with $α$ cluster condensation. The excitations are vortices in superfluid, a property possessed by such Bose condensed nuclei. We show that the vortex-like states possess quantum numbers $J^{P}=n^{(-1)^n}$ and energies $\sim $ several MeV, measured from the ground state energies of the $n α$ condensed states. We point out actual candidates, for instance, $2^{+}(3 {MeV})$ for $^{8}$Be, $3^{-}(9.6 {MeV})$ for $^{12}$C, etc, although the topological nature as a vortex is obscure in such light nuclei.

# Vortex Excitations in α Cluster Condensed Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper proposes that vortex-like topological excitations, analogous to superfluid vortices, may exist in nuclei that exhibit α-particle cluster condensation, with the caveat that the topological nature becomes obscure in light nuclei with only a few α clusters (e.g., 2–5 clusters), where the excitations are better described as "vortex-like" rather than true topological solitons.

## Method

The author models α-cluster condensed nuclei using a complex boson field φ representing α particles, with a Hamiltonian containing a kinetic term, a contact interaction (λ|φ|² − ρ)², and a Coulomb term. Vortex solutions are obtained by solving the field equation for an axially symmetric ansatz φ = √ρ f(r) e^{iθ}, imposing boundary conditions f(0)=0 and f(∞)=1, and computing the excitation energy relative to the non-vortex condensed ground state. The Coulomb energy difference between vortex and non-vortex states is treated perturbatively and found to be less than 1 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Coherent length of the vortex: approximately 0.4 fm, derived from 1/(√(2λMρ))
- Vortex excitation energy formula: E_v ≈ 6.2 MeV × √(n/3) × (4 fm / R₀)^{3/2} × (S_v/7.1)(log(S_v/7.1)+1), where n is the number of α clusters
- For ¹²C (3 α clusters, radius ~4 fm): vortex-like excitation energy ~6 MeV measured from the condensed state
- For ¹⁶O: vortex-like excitation energy ~9 MeV
- General scaling: E_v ~ 6 (n/3)^{1/3} MeV with number n of α clusters
- Radius ratio R_v/R₀ ≈ 1.05 (vortex state radius slightly larger than non-vortex radius)
- Angular momentum of vortex state: J_z = n (all clusters occupy a p state with l_z = 1)
- Quantum numbers predicted: J^P = n^{(-1)^n} for nucleus A = 4n
- Candidate states: 2⁺(3 MeV) for ⁸Be, 3⁻(9.6 MeV) for ¹²C, 4⁺(16 MeV) for ¹⁶O

## Context

This work extends the concept of vortex excitations from infinite superfluid systems (e.g., liquid helium, superconductors, Bose-Einstein condensates) to finite nuclear systems with α-cluster condensation, which had been previously argued to exist near threshold energies by Tohsaki and collaborators (2001). The paper builds on the idea that the second 0⁺ state in ¹²C is a 3α condensed state with rms radius ~4.3 fm, much larger than the ground state radius of ~2.5 fm. The vortex-like states are identified with known molecular states in light nuclei (e.g., the 2⁺ state in ⁸Be and the 3⁻ state in ¹²C), offering a new interpretation of these states as manifestations of superfluid-like behavior. The work enables experimental searches for vortex-like states in heavier α-conjugate nuclei (e.g., ²⁰Ne) and suggests that observation of such states would confirm superfluidity in α-cluster condensed nuclei.
