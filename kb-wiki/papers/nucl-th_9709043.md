# Warm nuclei: The transition from independent particle motion to collisional dominance

> We study large scale collective dynamics of isoscalar type and examine the influence of interactions residual to independent particle motion. It is argued that for excitations which commonly are present in experimental situations such interactions must not be neglected. They even help to justify better the assumption of locality, both in time as well as in phase space, which is necessary not only for such classic approaches to collective motion as the "cranking model" but also for the more general picture of a transport process. With respect to dissipation, our results are contrasted with those of wall friction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for the large-scale collective dynamics of warm nuclei, residual interactions beyond independent particle motion cannot be neglected, and that these interactions help justify the locality assumptions needed for transport models like the Fokker-Planck equation, but the transition from independent particle motion to collisional dominance is only fully realized at higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors use linear response theory within a locally harmonic approximation (LHA) to derive transport coefficients for collective motion, starting from a Hamiltonian for nucleons in a deformed mean field. They compute the energy transfer and friction coefficient for forced quadrupole and octupole vibrations of independent particles in a Woods-Saxon potential, then introduce collisional damping by dressing single-particle states with self-energies having both real and imaginary parts. The friction coefficient is evaluated along a fission path of 224Th for different temperatures, and the contribution from the "heat pole" (a singular term in the correlation function at zero frequency) is analyzed separately.

## Key numbers

- For the independent particle model with quadrupole excitations, the parameter eta is approximately 0.02269 times hbar omega.
- The averaging interval for Strutinsky smoothing of the microscopic energy transfer results was varied: 5 MeV, 10 MeV, and 20 MeV.
- The range of values for the collisional damping parameter Gamma_0^{-1} is 0.03 MeV^{-1} to 0.06 MeV^{-1}, and the cut-off parameter c ranges from 15 MeV to 30 MeV.
- For the heat pole contribution, the width Gamma_T follows the rule Gamma_T approximately equals 2 Gamma(mu,T) approximately equals 2 T, valid up to about 10 MeV.
- The friction-to-inertia ratio gamma/M along the fission path of 224Th does not change much with collective variables when T is of order 2 MeV or larger, and increases with T.

## Context

This work extends the linear response approach to collective motion developed by Hofmann (1997) and contrasts its results with the wall friction model of Blocki, Skalski, and Swiatecki (1995). The paper shows that the independent particle model cannot justify a local friction force without averaging procedures, and that residual interactions become essential at higher temperatures. The results enable a microscopic understanding of the transition from shell-model behavior to dissipative, hydrodynamical-like motion, and open the question of whether pairing correlations alone can account for the behavior at lower temperatures, as currently under investigation by Ivanyuk and Hofmann.
