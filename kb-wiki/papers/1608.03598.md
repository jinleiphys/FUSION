# Ab initio and phenomenological studies of the static response of neutron matter

> We investigate the problem of periodically modulated strongly interacting neutron matter. We carry out ab initio non-perturbative auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations using an external sinusoidal potential in addition to phenomenological two- and three-nucleon interactions. Several choices for the wave function ansatz are explored and special care is taken to extrapolate finite-sized results to the thermodynamic limit. We perform calculations at various densities as well as at different strengths and periodicities of the one-body potential. Our microscopic results are then used to constrain the isovector term from energy-density functional theories of nuclei at many different densities, while making sure to separate isovector contributions from bulk properties. Lastly, we use our results to extract the static density-density linear response function of neutron matter at different densities. Our findings provide insights into inhomogeneous neutron matter and are related to the physics of neutron star crusts and neutron-rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first *ab initio* non-perturbative calculation of the static density-density linear response function of neutron matter, using auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) with phenomenological two- and three-nucleon interactions; the results are used to constrain the isovector gradient coefficient of energy-density functionals (EDFs), but the extracted coefficient is found to be density-dependent, meaning no single value works across all densities.

## Method

The authors performed auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations for 66 neutrons in a periodic box, using the Argonne v8' two-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential, with an external sinusoidal potential \(v(\mathbf{r}) = 2v_q \cos(\mathbf{q} \cdot \mathbf{r})\). They optimized the trial wave function by using Mathieu-function single-particle orbitals (solutions of the non-interacting problem with the same external potential) and a Jastrow factor, then extrapolated finite-size results to the thermodynamic limit using a correction based on non-interacting energies. The linear density-density response function was extracted by fitting the energy per particle as a function of potential strength \(v_q\) to an even-powered polynomial, and the isovector gradient coefficient \(C^{\Delta n}_1\) in Skyrme EDFs (SLy4, SLy7, SkM*) was adjusted to match the AFDMC response at low potential strengths.

## Key numbers

- AFDMC energy per particle for 66 neutrons at density \(n_0 = 0.10 \, \text{fm}^{-3}\) with external potential strength \(2v_q = 0.5 E_F = 21.363 \, \text{MeV}\) and periodicity \(q = 4\pi/L\): \(8.15 \, \text{MeV}\) (using optimized orbitals with \(\beta = 2v_q\)).
- Modified isovector gradient coefficient for SLy4 at density \(n_0 = 0.10 \, \text{fm}^{-3}\): \(C^{\Delta n}_1 = -29 \, \text{MeV fm}^5\) (fitted to \(2v_q = 0.25\) and \(0.3 E_F\)).
- Modified isovector gradient coefficient for SLy4 at density \(n_0 = 0.04 \, \text{fm}^{-3}\): \(C^{\Delta n}_1 = 9 \, \text{MeV fm}^5\).
- Original isovector gradient coefficients for SLy4, SLy7, and SkM*: \(-16\), \(-6\), and \(-17 \, \text{MeV fm}^5\) respectively.
- AFDMC energies for two different orbital fillings at \(n = 0.08 \, \text{fm}^{-3}\) with \(2v_q = 0.5 E_F\): \(7.072\) and \(7.062 \, \text{MeV}\); at \(n = 0.04 \, \text{fm}^{-3}\) with \(2v_q = 0.75 E_F\): \(1.818\) and \(1.822 \, \text{MeV}\).
- Quartic term in the energy expansion is on the order of \(10^{-4} \, \text{MeV}^{-3}\) or smaller.

## Context

This work extends the study of inhomogeneous neutron matter from earlier approximations (e.g., Iwamoto (1982), Olsson (2004), Chamel (2011, 2013, 2014), Kobyakov (2013), Pastore (2014), Davesne (2015)) to a fully non-perturbative *ab initio* quantum Monte Carlo approach, following the authors' earlier Letter (Buraczynski and Gezerlis, 2016). The results provide synthetic data to constrain the isovector gradient term in Skyrme energy-density functionals (such as SLy4, SLy7, SkM*), which are used to model neutron-star crusts and neutron-rich nuclei; the density dependence of the extracted coefficient suggests that future EDF fits should incorporate response data at multiple densities and periodicities simultaneously.
