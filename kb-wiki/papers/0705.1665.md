# Spin-dependent three-nucleon force effects on nucleon-deuteron scattering

> We construct a phenomenological three-nucleon force (3NF) model that gives a good description of polarization observables in elastic nucleon-deuteron (N-$d$) scattering at a low energy together with a realistic nucleon-nucleon force and a 3NF arising from the exchange of two pions. Parameters of the model, which consists of spin-independent, spin-orbit, and tensor components, are determined to reproduce the three-nucleon binding energy and polarization observables in N-d scattering at 3 MeV. Predictions of the model 3NF on N-d polarization observables at higher energies are examined, and effects of each component on the observables are investigated.

## Key claim

A phenomenological three-nucleon force (3NF) model with spin-independent, spin-orbit, and tensor components, added to the Argonne V18 two-nucleon force and the Brazil two-pion-exchange 3NF, can reproduce the triton binding energy and polarization observables in nucleon-deuteron scattering at 3 MeV, but its predictions at higher energies up to 30 MeV show that the tensor component's effect is favorable in some observables yet remains inconclusive due to limited experimental data and unresolved Coulomb effects.

## Method

The author solved Faddeev equations in coordinate space for three-nucleon (3N) scattering states, using the Argonne V18 model for the two-nucleon force and the Brazil model for the two-pion-exchange 3NF. A phenomenological 3NF with central spin-independent (C), tensor (T), and spin-orbit (SO) components was added, with strengths determined by fitting the triton binding energy and polarization observables in proton-deuteron scattering at 3 MeV. Calculations were performed for 3N partial waves with total two-nucleon angular momenta j ≤ 3 for scattering states and total 3N angular momentum J ≤ 19/2, with 3NFs switched off for J > 9/2.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy: empirical 8.482 MeV; AV18 alone gives 7.626 MeV; AV18+BR800 gives 9.380 MeV; AV18+BR680 gives 8.493 MeV; AV18+BR800+C+T+SO gives 8.478 MeV; AV18+BR680+SO gives 8.444 MeV.
- For the phenomenological 3NF (AV18+BR800+C+T+SO), the strengths are (V0, VT, Vls) = (+25 MeV, -40 MeV, -16 MeV), with the range parameter rG = 1.0 fm and α = 1.5 fm^-1.
- The C-3NF contributes about 0.62 MeV repulsion to the total repulsion of about 0.90 MeV from the C+T+SO-3NF; the T-3NF contributes about 0.24 MeV; the SO-3NF contributes 0.05 MeV.
- For the AV18+BR680+SO model, the SO term strength is Vls = -21 MeV.
- The Brazil 3NF cutoff mass Λ = 800 MeV ≈ 5.8 mπ (BR800) overshoots the triton binding energy by about 0.9 MeV; Λ = 680 MeV ≈ 4.9 mπ (BR680) reproduces the binding energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Ki99, Is03, and Is04, which introduced phenomenological 3NFs with artificial functional forms to explain polarization observables in nucleon-deuteron scattering. The paper addresses the known deficiency that combinations of realistic two-nucleon forces (like AV18) and two-pion-exchange 3NFs (like the Brazil model) that reproduce the 3N binding energy do not necessarily explain polarization observables such as vector and tensor analyzing powers. The results provide hints for constructing more realistic 3NFs from chiral effective field theory or heavier-boson-exchange mechanisms, and the author notes that further experimental studies of spin-dependent cross section differences and polarization transfer coefficients are needed to confirm the tensor component's role.
