# Ab initio quantum dynamics using coupled-cluster

> The curse of dimensionality (COD) limits the current state-of-the-art {\it ab initio} propagation methods for non-relativistic quantum mechanics to relatively few particles. For stationary structure calculations, the coupled-cluster (CC) method overcomes the COD in the sense that the method scales polynomially with the number of particles while still being size-consistent and extensive. We generalize the CC method to the time domain while allowing the single-particle functions to vary in an adaptive fashion as well, thereby creating a highly flexible, polynomially scaling approximation to the time-dependent Schrödinger equation. The method inherits size-consistency and extensivity from the CC method. The method is dubbed orbital-adaptive time-dependent coupled-cluster (OATDCC), and is a hierarchy of approximations to the now standard multi-configurational time-dependent Hartree method for fermions. A numerical experiment is also given.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the coupled-cluster (CC) method to the time domain while allowing single-particle functions to vary adaptively, creating a polynomially-scaling approximation to the time-dependent Schrödinger equation that inherits size-consistency and extensivity from the CC method; the method is a hierarchy of approximations to the multi-configurational time-dependent Hartree method for fermions (MCTDHF), with the caveat that the bivariational framework requires complex analytic parametrization and even-dimensional approximation manifolds to guarantee unique solutions.

## Method

The author derives the orbital-adaptive time-dependent coupled-cluster (OATDCC) method by inserting the standard CC exponential ansatz (with cluster operator T and de-excitation operator Lambda) into a bivariational action functional, then allowing both the T and Lambda amplitudes and the biorthogonal orbital sets (phi_p and tilde-phi_p) to vary independently according to the time-dependent bivariational principle. The equations of motion are obtained from stationarity of the bivariational functional, yielding a system where the singles amplitudes become redundant when orbitals are varied freely, and truncation of the cluster operators at doubles, triples, etc. produces a hierarchy (OATDCCD, OATDCCDT, ...) with time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) at one end and full MCTDHF at the other. A numerical experiment is performed to demonstrate the method.

## Key numbers

- The method scales polynomially with the number of particles N, overcoming the exponential "curse of dimensionality" that limits MCTDHF; the polynomial scaling is achieved by truncating the cluster operator at a fixed excitation level (e.g., singles and doubles).

## Context

This work extends the stationary coupled-cluster method, as formulated in the bivariational framework by Arponen (1983), to the time domain, and generalizes the standard CC Lagrangian approach to linear response theory developed by Dalgaard (1983), Helgaker (1989), and Koch (1990). The OATDCC method is a hierarchy of approximations to the multi-configurational time-dependent Hartree method for fermions (MCTDHF) of Meyer (1990, 2000, 2009), and is related to earlier time-dependent CC proposals by Schonhammer and Gunnarsson (1978) and Hoodbhoy and Negele (1978, 1979), who considered time-dependent orbitals with explicit time-dependence. The method enables polynomially-scaling ab initio quantum dynamics for systems with more particles than MCTDHF can handle, while maintaining size-consistency.
