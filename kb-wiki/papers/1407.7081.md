# Co-existence of states in quantum systems

> Co-existence of different states is a profound concept, which possibly underlies the phase transition and the symmetry breaking. Because of a property inherent to quantum mechanics (cf. uncertainty), the co-existence is expected to appear more naturally in quantum-microscopic systems than in macroscopic systems. In this paper a mathematical theory describing co-existence of states in quantum systems is presented, and the co-existence is classified into 9 types.

## Key claim

The paper presents a mathematical theory for the co-existence of different quantum states, classifying it into 9 types based on bifurcation analysis of a nonlinear elliptic partial differential equation; the classification depends on the signs of the first and second derivatives of the eigenvalue parameter at the bifurcation point, which are determined by the specific form of the nonlinear interaction.

## Method

The author studies the stationary boundary-value problem of a nonlinear elliptic equation \(-\nabla^2 u - m u - V(u) u = 0\) on a closed domain \(\Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^3\) with Dirichlet boundary conditions, where \(u = \psi - \bar{\psi}\) represents the difference between an unknown state \(\psi\) and a reference state \(\bar{\psi}\). Linearizing around the trivial solution \(u=0\) yields an eigenvalue problem for the operator \(-\nabla^2\), and the Crandall-Rabinowitz bifurcation theory is applied to show that the smallest eigenvalue \(\lambda_0\) is a bifurcation point. By expanding the solution in a power series in a parameter \(s\) and evaluating inner products with the eigenfunction \(u_0\), the signs of \(\mu_s(0) = \lambda_s(0)\) and \(\mu_{ss}(0) = \lambda_{ss}(0)\) are derived, leading to a classification of the co-existence into 9 types based on the signs of these two quantities.

## Key numbers

- The smallest eigenvalue \(\lambda_0\) of \(-\nabla^2\) is simple and satisfies \(0 < \lambda_0 < \lambda_1 \le \lambda_2 \le \cdots\).
- For the \(\psi^4\)-interaction theory (\(k=4\)), the co-existence type is (i) if \(\eta > 0\), (ii) if \(\eta = 0\), and (iii) if \(\eta < 0\); when \(\eta > 0\), co-existence emerges only if \(m > \lambda_0\).
- For the \(\psi^3\)-interaction theory (\(k=3\)), all 9 types are possible depending on the signs of \(\mu_s(0)\) and \(\mu_{ss}(0)\).
- For \(k \ge 5\), both \(\mu_s(0) = 0\) and \(\mu_{ss}(0) = 0\), giving only type (ii).
- For the free particle case (\(\eta=0\)) and the linear interaction case (\(\psi^2\)-interaction), the co-existence is classified as type (ii).

## Context

This work extends the mathematical theory of bifurcation from the Crandall-Rabinowitz framework (Crandall and Rabinowitz, 1971, 1973) to classify the co-existence of states in quantum systems described by nonlinear Schrödinger and nonlinear Klein-Gordon equations. The classification into 9 types provides a systematic way to characterize phase transitions and symmetry breaking in quantum field theories with polynomial interactions, such as \(\psi^3\) and \(\psi^4\) theories. The results enable future studies to identify the type of nonlinearity from the observed co-existence pattern, and to apply the framework to more complex many-body systems where statistical properties are included.
