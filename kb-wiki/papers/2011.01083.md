# An effective field theory approach to fermionic rotational bands in nuclei

> We extend an effective field theory developed to describe rotational bands in even-even nuclei to the odd-mass case. This organizes Bohr and Mottelson's treatment of a particle coupled to a rotor as a model-independent expansion in powers of the angular velocity of the overall system. We carry out this expansion up to fourth order in the angular velocity and present results for $^{99}$Tc, ${}^{159}$Dy, ${}^{167, 169}$Er, ${}^{167, 169}$Tm, ${}^{183}$W, ${}^{235}$U and ${}^{239}$Pu. In each case, the accuracy and breakdown scale of the effective field theory can be understood based on the single-particle and vibrational energy scales in that nucleus.

## Key claim

The authors extend an effective field theory (EFT) for rotational bands in even-even nuclei to odd-mass nuclei, organizing Bohr and Mottelson's particle-rotor model as a model-independent expansion in powers of the angular velocity of the overall system up to fourth order. The EFT's accuracy and breakdown scale depend on the single-particle and vibrational energy scales in each nucleus, and the expansion is systematic but the coefficients of operators at each order are not computed from first principles but instead fit to data.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian for a rotor coupled to a fermion, expanding in powers of the angular velocity vector v. They carry out the expansion up to fourth order in v, deriving energy-level formulas for rotational bands in odd-mass nuclei. The coefficients of operators at each order are treated as free parameters fit to experimental data for seven nuclei: 99Tc, 159Dy, 167,169Er, 167,169Tm, 183W, 235U, and 239Pu.

## Key numbers

* For 99Tc, 159Dy, 167,169Er, 167,169Tm, 183W, 235U, and 239Pu: the EFT expansion is carried out up to fourth order in the angular velocity, yielding energy-level formulas with accuracy scaling as (E_rot/E_high)^3, where E_high is of order either E_sp or E_vib.
* The correction at N^nLO scales as E_rot * epsilon_sp^(n-1) * I^n, where I is the total angular momentum quantum number and epsilon_sp = E_rot/E_sp.
* The error in the nth-order EFT energy-level formula scales as I^(n+1).

## Context

This work extends the rotor EFT developed by Coello Perez and Papenbrock (2015) for even-even nuclei to odd-mass systems, building on the particle-rotor model of Bohr and Mottelson. It contrasts with the approach of Chen (2020), which assumed a deformed harmonic oscillator potential for the fermion, and with Papenbrock and Weidenmuller (2020), who derived a similar particle-rotor EFT but with a different interpretation of the Coriolis operator coefficient. The EFT enables systematic, model-independent description of rotational bands in odd-mass nuclei, with the potential to connect to shell-model or ab initio calculations as noted by Caprio (2019).
