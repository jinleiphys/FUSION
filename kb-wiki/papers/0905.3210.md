# Distortion of the HBT images by meson clouds

> We study the effects of mesonic final state interactions on the Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) intensity interferometry for mesons in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. Modification of the one-body amplitude of emitted mesons while going through a cloud of other mesons is estimated in the semiclassical approximation with a mesonic optical potential which incorporates both coherent forward scattering with other mesons and the absorption due to the incoherent scattering in the meson clouds. We show how these effects results in the distortion of the HBT images.

## Key claim

The paper shows that final-state interactions between emitted pions and a surrounding meson cloud distort the HBT source image extracted from two-particle correlations, but only under the assumption that the initial source is incoherent and the two-particle density matrix factorizes; the distortion arises from the phase shift and absorption encoded in a mesonic optical potential.

## Method

The authors model the propagation of pions through a meson cloud using a semiclassical (WKB) approximation with a complex optical potential, where the real part describes coherent forward scattering and the imaginary part accounts for absorption via incoherent scattering. They compute the modification of the single-particle amplitude by solving classical trajectories and evaluating the action integral, then derive the effective source distribution from the two-particle correlation function. The numerical calculations use a schematic two-range Gaussian potential for the real part and a single-range Gaussian for the imaginary part, with parameters chosen to mimic a dense meson source surrounded by a halo.

## Key numbers

* Source size parameter: \(\lambda_1 = 5\) fm (same as the Gaussian width of the source distribution).
* Halo thickness parameter: \(\lambda_2 = 5\) fm, with halo center at \(\xi = 10\) fm.
* Real potential strengths: \(\mathcal{V}_1 = 10\) MeV (short-range), \(\mathcal{V}_2 = 2\) MeV (long-range).
* Imaginary potential strength: \(\mathcal{V}_i = 0.1\) MeV (absorptive).
* Initial pion momentum distribution: thermal Bose-Einstein with temperature \(T_0 = 140\) MeV.
* Effective source images computed at \(k = 100\) MeV/c and \(k = 150\) MeV/c.

## Context

This work addresses the "RHIC HBT puzzle" (STAR, PHENIX, 2000s), where hydrodynamic models overpredict the source size compared to data. It extends earlier studies by Cramer, Miller, Wu, and Yano (CMWY) and Pratt (2006) on mean-field effects in HBT, and shows that the distortion of the source image can be understood via the momentum dependence of the phase shift rather than classical trajectory deflection. The results suggest that including such final-state interactions may reduce the discrepancy between data and hydrodynamic simulations at small \(k\), though the authors note that improvements (relativistic treatment, time-dependent source, realistic optical potential) are needed before direct comparison with experiment.
