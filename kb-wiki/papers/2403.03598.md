# Controlling volume fluctuations for studies of critical phenomena in nuclear collisions

> We generalize and extend the recently proposed method to account for contributions of system size (or volume/participant) fluctuations to the experimentally measured moments of particle multiplicity distributions. We find that in the general case there are additional biases which are not directly accessible to experiment. These biases are, however, parametrically suppressed if the multiplicity of the particles of interest is small compared to the total charged-particle multiplicity, e.g., in the case of proton number fluctuations at top RHIC and LHC energies. They are also small if the multiplicity distribution of charged particles per wounded nucleon is close to the Poissonian limit, which is the case at low energy nuclear collisions, e.g., at GSI/SIS18. We further find that mixed events are not necessarily needed to extract the correction for volume fluctuations, albeit it can help if event statistics is small, which is typically the case for reconstructing the higher-order cumulants. We provide the formulas to correct pure and mixed cumulants of particle multiplicity distributions up to any order together with their associated biases.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the event-mixing method for correcting volume fluctuations in nuclear collision experiments, finding that the original method (Rustamov 2022) implicitly assumed a Poissonian multiplicity distribution per wounded nucleon, which introduces additional bias terms that are not directly accessible to experiment but are parametrically suppressed when the particles of interest are a small fraction of total charged-particle multiplicity or when the per-wounded-nucleon multiplicity is nearly Poissonian.

## Method

The authors work within the wounded-nucleon model to derive analytical expressions for cumulants and factorial cumulants of particle multiplicity distributions up to any order, separating contributions from fixed-volume (true) fluctuations from those due to volume (wounded-nucleon number) fluctuations. They express the volume fluctuation corrections in terms of factorial cumulants of the total charged-particle multiplicity distribution, identify the bias terms arising from non-Poissonian per-wounded-nucleon emission, and provide formulas for corrected cumulants and their associated biases up to fourth order. The method is tested with two Monte Carlo simulations (Model A with independent particle production and Model B with cluster correlations) using Glauber-model wounded-nucleon distributions for Au+Au collisions at 2.4 GeV, STAR at 3 GeV, and ALICE at 2.76 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the event-mixing method proposed by Rustamov (2022) for correcting volume fluctuations in cumulant measurements of conserved charges in heavy-ion collisions. The paper identifies and quantifies bias terms that were implicitly assumed to vanish in the original approach, showing they are suppressed at high energies (top RHIC, LHC) where proton multiplicity is small compared to total charged-particle multiplicity, and at low energies (GSI/SIS18) where the per-wounded-nucleon multiplicity distribution is nearly Poissonian. The results enable more reliable extraction of true density fluctuations from experimental data, which is essential for comparing with lattice QCD calculations (HotQCD 2017, Borsanyi 2018) and for searching for critical phenomena in the QCD phase diagram as studied by STAR (2021, 2022), HADES (2020), and ALICE (2019, 2022).
