# Spectroscopy of doubly charmed baryons

> We study the mass spectrum of baryons with two and three charmed quarks. For double charm baryons the spin splitting is found to be smaller than standard quark-model potential predictions. This splitting is not influenced either by the particular form of the confining potential or by the regularization taken for the contact term of the spin-spin potential. We consistently predict the spectra for triply charmed baryons.

# Spectroscopy of doubly charmed baryons

## Key claim

The spin splitting between the J^P=1/2^+ and J^P=3/2^+ states in doubly charmed baryons is predicted to be much smaller (around 20 MeV) than earlier potential-model estimates of approximately 80 MeV, and this small splitting is robust against changes in the confining potential form and the regularization of the spin-spin contact term, provided the model correctly reproduces the Delta-N mass difference.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrodinger equation exactly using the Faddeev method for the three-quark system, including orbital angular momentum configurations up to l=5 and lambda=5. They employed a constituent quark model with a scale-dependent strong coupling constant (alpha_s(qq)=0.54, alpha_s(qc)=0.44, alpha_s(cc)=0.29) and compared results using both linear and screened confining potentials, as well as two different values of the regularization parameter r_0 for the one-gluon exchange spin-spin interaction. For the triply charmed baryon, they also used the hyperspherical harmonic expansion method up to K=24 for cross-check.

## Key numbers

- The J^P=1/2^+ - J^P=3/2^+ spin splitting in ccq systems is approximately 20-25 MeV across all four parameter sets tested (Table 3: values of 23, 22, 25, and 21 MeV for sets A, B, C, D respectively)
- The ground state mass of the Xi^+_cc (J^P=1/2^+) is tuned to 3524 MeV/c^2 using parameter set E with charm quark mass 1550 MeV, matching the SELEX experimental value of 3520 MeV/c^2
- The first radial excitation (J^P=1/2^+)* lies at 3749 MeV (225 MeV above ground state) and the first negative parity state (J^P=1/2^-) at 3692 MeV (168 MeV above ground state) for parameter set E
- For the ccc system, the J^P=3/2^+ ground state is at 4632 MeV, with the J^P=1/2^+ state at 4915 MeV (283 MeV higher) and the J^P=1/2^- state at 4808 MeV (176 MeV higher)
- The strength of the spin-spin force for cc pairs ranges from 45.31 fm^-2 (sets C and D with r_0=0.45) to 149.79 fm^-2 (sets A and B with r_0=0.25)

## Context

This work extends the quark-model approach of Vijande et al. (2004) that successfully described light baryon and heavy-light meson spectra to the doubly and triply charmed baryon sectors. It contradicts earlier predictions by Fleck and Richard (1989), Rosner (1995), Stong (1995), Roncaglia et al. (1995), and Itoh et al. (2000) who found spin splittings around 80 MeV for ccq systems. The results enable experimental searches for the J^P=3/2^+ partner of the Xi^+_cc near 3548 MeV/c^2, and provide predictions for the ccc ground state at 4632 MeV that can guide future searches at facilities like BELLE and FOCUS.
