# Electrophobic Scalar Boson and Muonic Puzzles

> A new scalar boson which couples to the muon and proton can simultaneously solve the proton radius puzzle and the muon anomalous magnetic moment discrepancy. Using a variety of measurements, we constrain the mass of this scalar and its couplings to the electron, muon, neutron, and proton. Making no assumptions about the underlying model, these constraints and the requirement that it solve both problems limit the mass of the scalar to between about 100 keV and 100 MeV. We identify two unexplored regions in the coupling constant-mass plane. Potential future experiments and their implications for theories with mass-weighted lepton couplings are discussed.

## Key claim

A new scalar boson coupling to muons and protons can simultaneously resolve both the proton radius puzzle and the muon anomalous magnetic moment discrepancy, but only if its mass lies between about 100 keV and 100 MeV; the authors make no prior assumptions about the signs or magnitudes of its couplings to different fermions, unlike earlier work that assumed equal couplings or mass-weighted couplings.

## Method

The authors introduce a scalar boson φ with coupling strengths ε_f to each fermion species f, parameterized as e ε_f in the Lagrangian. They use the measured Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen and the muon anomalous magnetic moment to determine the required values of ε_μ and ε_p as functions of m_φ. They then constrain ε_n/ε_p using neutron-208Pb scattering, muonic deuterium and muonic helium Lamb shifts, and the neutron-neutron scattering length difference, and constrain ε_p using nuclear binding energy in infinite nuclear matter and the ³He-³H binding energy difference. Finally, they constrain ε_e using the electron anomalous magnetic moment, Bhabha scattering, beam dump experiments (E137, E141, Orsay), and the Lamb shift in ordinary hydrogen.

## Key numbers

- The muon anomalous magnetic moment discrepancy used is Δa_μ = 287(80) × 10⁻¹¹ (2σ allowed range).
- The Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen required to explain the proton radius puzzle is δE_L^{μH} = -0.307(56) meV (2σ allowed range).
- The scalar mass is constrained to 100 keV ≲ m_φ ≲ 100 MeV.
- The neutron-neutron scattering length difference constraint requires Δa_φ < 1.6 fm (2σ).
- The ³He-³H binding energy difference constraint requires the scalar contribution not exceed 30 keV.
- The electron anomalous magnetic moment constraint requires Δa_e < 1.5 × 10⁻¹² (2σ).
- The Lamb shift in ordinary hydrogen constraint requires δE_L^{H} < 14 kHz (2σ).
- At m_φ = 73 MeV, allowed couplings include |ε_e| < 9.1 × 10⁻⁸ (with a second allowed range 3.3 × 10⁻⁶ to 1.8 × 10⁻³), ε_μ = 1.96(27) × 10⁻³, ε_p = 0.39, and ε_n from -0.29 to 5.6 × 10⁻⁴.

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Tucker-Smith and Yavin (2010) by allowing ε_p ≠ ε_μ, which widens the allowed mass range from a narrow region around 1 MeV to 100 keV–100 MeV, and by carefully treating the neutron coupling ε_n instead of neglecting it. The authors also improve constraints on ε_e by two orders of magnitude at m_φ = 1 MeV compared to Tucker-Smith and Yavin (2010) using electron beam dump data. The results identify two unexplored regions in the (m_φ, ε_e) plane (Region A: 10–70 MeV, ε_e ~ 10⁻⁶–10⁻³; Region B: 100 keV–1 MeV, ε_e ~ 10⁻⁸–10⁻⁵) that could be probed by future experiments such as APEX, HPS, DarkLight, VEPP-3, MAMI/MESA, and nuclear de-excitation searches as proposed by Izaguirre et al. (2014).
