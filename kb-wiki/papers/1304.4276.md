# Pions: Experimental Tests of Chiral Symmetry Breaking

> Based on the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry, chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) is believed to approximate confinement scale QCD. Dedicated and increasingly accurate experiments and improving lattice calculations are confirming this belief, and we are entering a new era in which we can test confinement scale QCD in some well chosen reactions. This is demonstrated with an overview of low energy experimental tests of ChPT predictions of $ππ$ scattering, pion properties, $π$N scattering and electromagnetic pion production. These predictions have been shown to be consistent with QCD in the meson sector by increasingly accurate lattice calculations. At present there is good agreement between experiment and ChPT calculations, including the $ππ$ and $π$N s wave scattering lengths and the $π^{0}$ lifetime. Recent, accurate pionic atom data are in agreement with chiral calculations once isospin breaking effects due to the mass difference of the up and down quarks are taken into account, as was required to extract the $ππ$ scattering lengths. In addition to tests of the theory, comparisons between $ππ$ and $π$N interactions based on general chiral principles are discussed. Lattice calculations are now providing results for the fundamental, long and inconclusively studied, $π$N $σ$ term and the contribution of the strange quark to the mass of the proton. Increasingly accurate experiments in electromagnetic pion production experiments from the proton which test ChPT calculations (and their energy region of validity) are presented. These experiments are also beginning to measure the final state $π$N interaction. This paper is based on the concluding remarks made at the Chiral Dynamics Workshop CD12 held at Jefferson Lab in Aug. 2012.

# Pions: Experimental Tests of Chiral Symmetry Breaking

## Key claim
Chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) calculations of low-energy QCD observables are in good agreement with experiment and lattice QCD, but the paper emphasizes that this agreement is conditional on including isospin breaking effects from the up-down quark mass difference, and that some experimental results (notably the π⁰ lifetime) still show discrepancies that exceed the theoretical precision.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental tests of ChPT predictions across multiple sectors: ππ scattering (measured via Kₑ₄ decays by NA48 at CERN), πN scattering (measured via pionic hydrogen and deuterium level shifts at PSI), the π⁰ lifetime (measured via Primakoff effect at Jefferson Lab, direct decay at CERN, and e⁺e⁻ colliders), and electromagnetic pion production from the proton (measured with tagged polarized photons at the A2 experiment at Mainz). Comparisons are made between ChPT calculations at various orders, dispersion relation approaches, and lattice QCD results from the FLAG collaboration.

## Key numbers
- Weinberg's leading-order ChPT prediction for ππ s-wave scattering lengths: aⁱ⁼⁰_ππ = 0.16/m_π, aⁱ⁼²_ππ = -0.0042/m_π
- Weinberg's leading-order ChPT prediction for πN scattering lengths: a⁻_πN = 0.0795/m_π, a⁺_πN = 0
- Next-to-leading order ChPT increases aⁱ⁼⁰_ππ to 0.20/m_π (25% increase); next-to-next-to-leading order gives 0.216/m_π
- Extracted πN scattering lengths from pionic atoms (including isospin breaking): a⁻_πN = 0.0861 ± 0.0009/m_π, a⁺_πN = 0.0076 ± 0.0031/m_π
- π⁰ lifetime: PrimEx experiment error 2.8%, CERN direct experiment error 3.1%, but the two results differ by 7.5%; ChPT prediction has 1% theoretical accuracy
- ChPT predicts a 4.5 ± 1% increase to the π⁰ decay rate from isospin breaking (η, η' mixing)
- σ resonance parameters: mass 441⁺¹⁶₋₈ MeV, width 544⁺¹⁸₋₂₅ MeV
- ρ resonance: W = 776 MeV, width ≈ 150 MeV
- Δ resonance pole: W = 1210 MeV, approximately 270 MeV above nucleon mass
- ChPT calculations for γp → π⁰p agree with A2 data up to maximum photon energy of approximately 165-170 MeV (about 25 MeV above threshold)
- Lattice average for strange quark contribution to nucleon mass: f_s = 0.043 ± 0.011

## Context
This paper extends and compares the ChPT program across multiple sectors, building on Weinberg's (1966) current algebra predictions for π-hadron scattering lengths. It reviews the NA48 results for ππ scattering (Bizzeti, CD12 workshop), the PSI pionic atom measurements (Gotta), and the A2 photo-pion experiments at Mainz (Hornidge). The work contrasts the ππ and πN sectors, noting that the πN sector has a lower-lying resonance (Δ at 1210 MeV) compared to ππ (ρ at 776 MeV), which affects the relative importance of s-wave and p-wave interactions near threshold. The paper enables future tests of isospin breaking in electromagnetic pion production and extraction of the πN charge-exchange scattering length via unitary cusp measurements.
