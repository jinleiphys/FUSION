# Interactions of $ω$-Mesons in Nuclear Matter and with Nuclei

> In--medium interactions of $ω$--mesons are investigated microscopically by coupling to $NN^{-1}$ and $N^*N^{-1}$ particle--hole excitations of the medium, including elativistic mean--field dynamics by self--consistent scalar and vector fields. The resulting self--energies are transmitted to finite nuclei in local density approximation. Real and imaginary parts of longitudinal and transversal self--energies are discussed. The relation of the present approach to meson cloud models is addressed and an ambiguity is pointed out. Applications to recent data on the in--medium width of $ω$ mesons scattered on a Niobium target serve to determine unknown $N^*Nω$ in-medium coupling constants. The data are well described by $N^*N^{-1}$ self--energies containing S--wave and P--wave $N^*$ resonances. Exploratory investigations, however, show that the spectroscopic composition of self--energies depends crucially on the near--threshold properties of the width which at present is known only within large error bars. The calculations predict the prevalence of transversal self--energies, implying that vector current conservation is still maintained in the nuclear medium by slightly more than 90\%. Schrödinger--equivalent potentials are derived and scattering lengths and effective range parameters are extracted for the longitudinal and transversal channel. Longitudinal and transversal spectral distributions are discussed and the dependencies on momentum and nuclear density are investigated. Schrödinger--type $ω+{}^{93}$Nb optical potentials are constructed. Low--energy parameters are determined, are used to study the pole structure of the S--matrix at threshold. The effective range expansion of the omega--nucleus K--matrix led to a $ω+{}^{93}$Nb bound states with binding energy $Re(\varepsilon_B)=-448$~keV but of width $Γ_B=4445$~keV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the in-medium width of ω mesons in nuclear matter can be described by coupling to nucleon and nucleon-resonance particle-hole excitations, but the spectroscopic composition of the self-energies depends crucially on the near-threshold properties of the width, which is known only within large error bars.

## Method

The ω-meson self-energies in nuclear matter were computed microscopically from nuclear polarization tensors describing NN⁻¹ and N*N⁻¹ particle-hole excitations, including relativistic mean-field dynamics through self-consistent scalar and vector fields. The self-energies were decomposed into longitudinal and transversal components, transmitted to finite nuclei via local density approximation, and the unknown in-medium N*Nω coupling constants were determined by fitting to experimental ω+⁹³Nb width data from Friedrich et al. (2016).

## Key numbers

- The ω+⁹³Nb bound state from effective range expansion has binding energy Re(ε_B) = -448 keV and width Γ_B = 4445 keV
- The central isoscalar density of ⁹³Nb is ρ_A = 0.140 fm⁻³, with proton density ρ_p(0) = 0.063 fm⁻³ and neutron density ρ_n(0) = 0.077 fm⁻³
- The unconstrained fit yields a threshold width Γ_thr = 48.41 MeV, with medium-dependent width Γ⁽ᴬ⁾_0 = 39.73 MeV
- Transversal self-energies account for about 90% of the total yield, implying vector current conservation is maintained by slightly more than 90%
- The free ω-meson width is 8.68 MeV and its mass is 782.7 MeV
- The ω-meson lifetime is cτ = 22.73 fm or τ ≈ 7.58 × 10⁻²³ seconds
- The incident photon energies in the experiment ranged from 1.2 to 2.9 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of vector meson self-energies by Celenza (1991) and Peters (1997) for ω and ρ mesons, and contrasts with meson-loop approaches by Klingl (1997), Lutz (2001), and Ramos (2013). The results enable determination of in-medium N*Nω coupling constants from experimental data, and the predicted ω+⁹³Nb bound state provides a testable prediction for future experiments on meson-nucleus interactions near threshold.
