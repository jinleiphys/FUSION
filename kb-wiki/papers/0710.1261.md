# Coulomb distortion effects in quasi-elastic (e,e') scattering on heavy nuclei

> The influence of the Coulomb distortion for quasi-elastic (e,e') scattering on highly charged nuclei is investigated in distorted wave Born approximation for electrons. The Dirac equation is solved numerically in order to obtain exact electron continuum states in the electrostatic field generated by the charge distribution of an atomic nucleus. Different approximate models are used to describe the nucleon current in order to show that, at high electron energies and energy-momentum transfer, the influence of Coulomb distortions on (e,e') cross sections can be reliably described by the effective momentum approximation, irrespective of details concerning the description of the nuclear current.

# Coulomb distortion effects in quasi-elastic (e,e') scattering on heavy nuclei

## Key claim

The effective momentum approximation (EMA) provides a reliable description of Coulomb distortion effects on (e,e') cross sections for heavy nuclei at high electron energies and momentum transfer, irrespective of the details of the nuclear current model used, contrary to claims by the Ohio group that significant corrections beyond EMA were necessary.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation numerically to obtain exact electron continuum states in the electrostatic field generated by the charge distribution of a 208Pb nucleus, using distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA). They compared cross sections calculated with two different nuclear current models: a single-particle shell model with harmonic oscillator wave functions for bound nucleons and plane waves for knocked-out nucleons, and an eikonal approximation for final-state nucleons using a Woods-Saxon optical potential with Coulomb potential for protons.

## Key numbers

- The EMA works with an accuracy better than 1% for describing Coulomb distortion effects when the energy of scattered electrons and momentum transfer are sufficiently high, confirming the earlier findings of the Trento group
- For 208Pb, the Woods-Saxon potential parameters used were: range r_nuc = 7.371 fm, diffusivity a_nuc = 0.646 fm, with potential depth parameters for protons v1 = 67.2, v2 = 7.9 x 10^-3, v3 = 2.0 x 10^-5, v4 = 7 x 10^-9
- The Coulomb potential for the final-state nucleus used a homogeneous sphere with radius R_C = 1.220 A^(1/3) = 7.228 fm for 208Pb
- The EMA starts to fail when the final-state electron energy becomes significantly smaller than 150 MeV and the four-momentum transfer is below (300 MeV)^2
- The average binding energy of protons in the single-particle shell model calculations was 21 MeV, while correlated nucleons have binding energies around 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the debate between the Trento group (Traini, 1990s), who found EMA accuracy better than 1%, and the Ohio group (Kim, 1990s), who derived significant corrections beyond EMA. The paper also corrects earlier eikonal distorted wave Born approximation calculations by the Basel group that overestimated Coulomb-corrected cross sections due to poor approximations for electron focusing near the nucleus. The findings support the use of EMA for analyzing upcoming Jefferson Lab experiments (E01-016) on 4He, 12C, 56Fe, and 208Pb targets in the momentum transfer range 0.55 GeV to 1.0 GeV, particularly for studying the Coulomb sum rule and in-medium nucleon form factor modifications.
