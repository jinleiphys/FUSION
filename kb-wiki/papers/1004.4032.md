# Possible heavy molecular states composed of a pair of excited charm-strange mesons

> The P-wave charm-strange mesons $D_{s0}(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ lie below the $DK$ and $D^\ast K$ threshold respectively. They are extremely narrow because their strong decays violate the isospin symmetry. We study the possible heavy molecular states composed of a pair of excited charm strange mesons. As a byproduct, we also present the numerical results for the bottonium-like analogue.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether loosely bound molecular states can form from a pair of excited P-wave charm-strange mesons (the narrow \(D_{s0}(2317)\) and \(D_{s1}(2460)\)), finding that such states may exist if the hadronic coupling constants are large enough, but the numerical results are highly sensitive to the values of these poorly known couplings and the cutoff parameter in the form factor.

## Method

The authors employed a meson exchange model, deriving effective potentials from an effective Lagrangian that respects heavy quark symmetry and chiral symmetry, including exchanges of pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar mesons. They solved the Schrodinger equation using the MATSLISE package with a monopole-type form factor at each vertex, varying the cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) (expected in the range 1-3 GeV) and the coupling constants (taken from crude QCD sum rule estimates) to search for bound state solutions for the \(D^*_0-\bar{D}^*_0\), \(D_1-\bar{D}_1\), and \(D^*_0-\bar{D}_1\) systems.

## Key numbers

*   The P-wave charm-strange mesons \(D_{s0}(2317)\) and \(D_{s1}(2460)\) lie below the \(DK\) and \(D^*K\) thresholds respectively, with masses of 2.317 GeV and 2.46 GeV.
*   For the \(D_1-\bar{D}_1\) system with only pseudoscalar exchange and \(g'=0.80\), a \(J^P=0^+\) bound state (\(\Phi^{**}\)) appears with binding energy \(E=-5.4\) MeV and \(r_{rms}=1.33\) fm at cutoff \(\Lambda=1.9\) GeV.
*   For the \(D^*_0-\bar{D}_1\) system with only pseudoscalar exchange and \(g'=1.06\), a \(J^{PC}=1^{++}\) state (\(\Phi^{*}_{s1}\)) appears with binding energy \(E=-18.3\) MeV and \(r_{rms}=0.73\) fm at cutoff \(\Lambda=2.0\) GeV.
*   The predicted masses for the loosely bound molecular states are approximately 4.61 GeV (from \(D_{s0}\bar{D}_{s0}\)), 4.9 GeV (from \(D_{s1}\bar{D}_{s1}\)), and 4.75 GeV (from \(D_{s0}\bar{D}_{s1}\)).
*   The coupling constants used include \(\beta'=0.84\), \(g'_\sigma=0.761\), and \(\lambda'=0.42\) for one set of calculations, with \(g'\) varied from 0.80 to 1.32.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (Liu et al., 2009) that investigated molecular states composed of S-wave heavy mesons and S-wave plus P-wave heavy mesons, now focusing on the system of two P-wave heavy mesons. The study is motivated by the observation of many charmonium-like states (e.g., \(X(3872)\), \(Y(3940)\), \(Z^+(4430)\)) that are difficult to accommodate in the conventional quark model and are often speculated to be molecular states. The results enable future experimental searches at facilities like B factories, RHIC, Tevatron, and LHCb for these predicted narrow molecular states, which could help understand the nature of \(X(3872)\).
