# Impact of NNLO QED corrections on lepton-proton scattering at MUSE

> We present the complete next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) pure pointlike QED corrections to lepton-proton scattering, including three-photon-exchange contributions, and investigate their impact in the case of the MUSE experiment. These corrections are computed with no approximation regarding the energy of the emitted photons and taking into account lepton-mass effects. We contrast the NNLO QED corrections to known next-to-leading order corrections, where we include the elastic two-photon exchange (TPE) through a simple hadronic model calculation with a dipole ansatz for the proton electromagnetic form factors. We show that, in the low-momentum-transfer region accessed by the MUSE experiment, the improvement due to more sophisticated treatments of the TPE, including inelastic TPE, is of similar if not smaller size than some of the NNLO QED corrections. Hence, the latter have to be included in a precision determination of the low-energy proton structure from scattering data, in particular for electron-proton scattering. For muon-proton scattering, the NNLO QED corrections are considerably smaller.

## Key claim

The complete next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) pure pointlike QED corrections to lepton-proton scattering, including three-photon-exchange contributions, are comparable in size to the next-to-leading order two-photon-exchange (TPE) corrections in the low-momentum-transfer region accessed by the MUSE experiment, meaning that NNLO QED corrections must be included alongside improved TPE descriptions for precision extractions of low-energy proton structure, particularly for electron-proton scattering.

## Method

The authors computed the complete NNLO pure pointlike QED corrections to lepton-proton scattering using the McMule framework, adapting recent NNLO computations for muon-electron scattering with adapted masses. The elastic TPE contribution was implemented through a simple hadronic model with a dipole ansatz for the proton electromagnetic form factors, using on-shell proton form factors and varying the dipole parameter Lambda^2 between 0.60 and 0.86 GeV^2. All contributions were computed with no approximation regarding the energy of emitted photons and including lepton-mass effects, with phase-space integrations performed numerically using the FKS^l subtraction method.

## Key numbers

- The MUSE experiment accesses a low momentum-transfer region with Q^2 less than 0.08 GeV^2 for a beam momentum of 210 MeV
- The inelastic TPE contribution is estimated at delta_2gamma ~ 5 x 10^-4, smaller than the anticipated 1% accuracy of MUSE cross-section measurements
- The hadronic model calculation and dispersive evaluation agree with 5 x 10^-4 relative accuracy for muon scattering kinematics at MUSE
- The standard dipole parameter used is Lambda^2 = 0.71 GeV^2, varied within the range 0.60 GeV^2 to 0.86 GeV^2 to illustrate form factor uncertainties
- Three beam momenta are considered for MUSE: p_beam = 115, 153, and 210 MeV

## Context

This work extends the NLO radiative corrections from Gramolin (2014) applied in recent MUSE analyses and adapts the NNLO computation for muon-electron scattering from Broggio (2022) and Engel (2022) to lepton-proton scattering. It enables a critical assessment of the relative impact of NNLO QED corrections versus TPE corrections for the MUSE experiment, which aims to compare proton charge radius extractions from electron and muon scattering using the same experimental setup, and to experimentally determine TPE corrections using both beam polarities. The inelastic TPE, estimated through a dispersive approach by Tomalak (2015) relating it to forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering amplitudes, is neglected as it is smaller than the anticipated measurement accuracy.
