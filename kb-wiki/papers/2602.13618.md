# Probing Rotational Dynamics of Quark Gluon Plasma via Global Vorticity

> The findings on the spin polarization of $Λ$, $Ξ$, and $Ω$ hyperons and spin alignment of $K^{*0}$, $ϕ$, and $D^{*+}$ mesons in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments at the RHIC and LHC facilities propose the emergence of a strong vorticity field produced in these collisions. Contemplating the potential impact of vorticity on the space-time evolution of deconfined QCD matter and its freeze-out properties, we aim to investigate its characteristics within the medium. We introduce a complementary and data-driven approach to quantify the global vorticity field by extracting it directly from the transverse momentum spectra of produced hadrons. Employing the experimental data for $Λ$, $Ξ$, $Ω$, $K^{*0}$, $K^{*\pm}$, $ϕ$, $ρ$, and $D^{*+}$ at mid-rapidity in Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions over a wide range of beam energies, $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=7.7$ GeV-5.02 TeV, and centrality classes, we systematically examine spin-vorticity coupling in the medium. Our finding on the magnitude of the extracted vorticity is consistent with values deduced from $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ polarization measurements using statistical thermal models under the non-relativistic limit. Notably, we observe a prominent particle-species dependence of the vorticity, as well as a non-trivial variation with collision centrality and beam energy. These results indicate that vorticity-driven spin phenomena are sensitive to hadron structure and freeze-out dynamics, providing new constraints on the rotational properties of the QCD matter.

# Probing Rotational Dynamics of Quark Gluon Plasma via Global Vorticity

## Key claim

The authors introduce a data-driven method to extract the global vorticity field directly from the transverse momentum spectra of produced hadrons, rather than from spin polarization measurements. The extracted vorticity magnitude is consistent with values deduced from Λ and anti-Λ polarization measurements using statistical thermal models under the non-relativistic limit, but the authors observe a prominent particle-species dependence of vorticity that the abstract does not emphasize as a central finding.

## Method

The authors analyze mid-rapidity transverse momentum spectra of Λ, anti-Λ, Ξ⁻, anti-Ξ⁺, Ω⁻, anti-Ω⁺ hyperons and K*⁰, K*±, φ, ρ, D*⁺ vector mesons measured by STAR and ALICE collaborations in Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions. They fit these spectra using a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis non-extensive distribution modified to incorporate rigid-body rotation, where the global vorticity Ω is extracted as a fit parameter from the energy shift induced by spin-orbit coupling in a rotating frame. The analysis covers beam energies from √s_NN = 7.7 GeV to 5.02 TeV across multiple centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- Global vorticity magnitude from Λ and anti-Λ polarization measurements: Ω ≃ (9 ± 1) × 10²¹ s⁻¹ (quoted from STAR Collaboration, 2017)
- Beam energy range analyzed: √s_NN = 7.7 GeV to 5.02 TeV
- For Λ and anti-Λ hyperons in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 7.7–64 GeV, Ω decreases toward peripheral collisions across most beam energies
- At LHC energies (√s_NN = 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb), the centrality dependence of Ω for Λ and Ξ⁻ hyperons is approximately flat up to 80% centrality
- The extracted vorticity is significantly larger at LHC energies than at RHIC energies for Λ and anti-Λ hyperons in 30–40% and 20–40% centrality classes
- For vector mesons at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb, in peripheral collisions (40–70%), K*⁰, K*±, φ, and D*⁺ all have a similar value of Ω

## Context

This work extends the study of vorticity in relativistic heavy-ion collisions beyond the standard approach of measuring hyperon spin polarization, as pioneered by the STAR Collaboration (2017) at RHIC and the ALICE Collaboration (2019, 2021) at LHC. The authors build on the Tsallis non-extensive distribution framework developed by Cleymans (2011) and Bhattacharyya (2015) for describing hadron spectra, and incorporate rotational effects following the formalism of Landau and Mashhoon (1988). The extracted global vorticity values are compared with those inferred from statistical thermal model analyses of Λ polarization measurements. This method enables future studies of the QCD phase diagram under rotation, including coupling to baryon number, chiral dynamics, and critical fluctuations, and can be applied to data from upcoming programs at NICA, FAIR, and the EIC.
