# Relevant energy ranges for astrophysical reaction rates

> Effective energy windows (Gamow windows) of astrophysical reaction rates for (p,gamma), (p,n), (p,alpha), (alpha,gamma), (alpha,n), (alpha,p), (n,gamma), (n,p), and (n,alpha) on targets with 10<=Z<=83 from proton- to neutron-dripline are calculated using theoretical cross sections. It is shown that widely used approximation formulas for the relevant energy ranges are not valid for a large number of reactions relevant to hydrostatic and explosive nucleosynthesis. The influence of the energy dependence of the averaged widths on the location of the Gamow windows is discussed and the results presented in tabular form (also at http://download.nucastro.org/astro/gamow/).

## Key claim

The widely used approximation formulas for Gamow windows (Eqs. e0approx, deltaapprox) are not valid for a large number of reactions relevant to hydrostatic and explosive nucleosynthesis, because the energy dependence of the cross section is not always dominated by the Coulomb barrier penetration of the projectile; instead, the smallest width among the entrance and exit channels governs the energy dependence, which can shift the effective energy window significantly.

## Method

The author computed the reaction rate integrand F(E) = σ(E) E exp(-E/kT) for 20540 charged-particle reactions and 8862 neutron-induced reactions on targets with charge numbers 10 ≤ Z ≤ 83 from proton- to neutron-dripline, using theoretical cross sections from the FRDM set. For each case, the energy of the maximum Ẽ₀ and the width Δ̃ (defined as the energy range contributing 90% of the total integral) were determined numerically, and the shift δ = Ẽ₀ − E₀ relative to the standard Gamow peak energy was calculated.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁴Mg(α,γ) at T=2.5 GK: effective window upper end Ẽ_hi = 2.36 MeV, width Δ̃ = 1.05 MeV, shift δ = −1.16 MeV
- For ²⁷Al(p,γ) at T=3.5 GK: Ẽ_hi = 1.47 MeV, Δ̃ = 1.12 MeV, δ = −0.89 MeV
- For ⁴⁰Ca(α,γ) at T=2.0 GK: Ẽ_hi = 3.62 MeV, Δ̃ = 1.39 MeV, δ = −0.63 MeV; at T=4.0 GK: Ẽ_hi = 4.66 MeV, Δ̃ = 1.97 MeV, δ = −1.97 MeV
- For ¹⁶⁹Tm(α,γ) at T=2.0 GK: Ẽ_hi = 9.20 MeV, Δ̃ = 2.94 MeV, δ = −0.54 MeV; at T=5.0 GK: Ẽ_hi = 13.20 MeV, Δ̃ = 4.27 MeV, δ = −4.79 MeV
- For ¹⁰⁶Cd(α,γ) at T=3.5 GK: Ẽ_hi = 10.07 MeV, Δ̃ = 3.44 MeV, δ = −1.17 MeV
- For ¹²⁰Sn(n,α) at T=5.0 GK: Ẽ_hi = 9.54 MeV, Δ̃ = 4.16 MeV, δ = +6.49 MeV
- For ⁶⁰Fe(n,γ) at T=5.0 GK: Ẽ_hi = 1.20 MeV, Δ̃ = 1.20 MeV, δ = −0.30 MeV
- The 1/e width of the numerically derived Gamow windows contains only 55–70% of the total contribution to the integral

## Context

This work extends the criticism of standard Gamow window approximations previously noted by Iliadis (2007) and Newton (2007), who found that resonances below the conventionally computed Gamow window can contribute significantly to reaction rates for narrow-resonance capture on light targets. The paper provides a comprehensive numerical table of effective energy windows for 20540 charged-particle and 8862 neutron-induced reactions, enabling experimentalists to design measurements at the correct energies and theorists to improve reaction rate calculations for hydrostatic and explosive nucleosynthesis. The results are available in tabular form at the author's website and as EPAPS supplementary material.
