# Universal Efimov spectra and fermionic doublets in highly mass-imbalanced cold-atom mixtures with van der Waals and dipole interactions

> We study the Efimov states in highly mass-imbalanced three-body systems composed of two identical heavy atoms and one light atom, focusing on the Er-Er-Li and Dy-Dy-Li cold-atom mixtures with strong dipole-dipole interactions between the heavy atoms. By solving the Born-Oppenheimer equation for varying $s$-wave scattering lengths between the heavy and light atoms, we demonstrate for both bosonic and fermionic systems that the Efimov spectra and hence the three-body parameters are universal even with the dipole interaction comparable in strength to the van der Waals interaction. While the bosonic systems exhibit Efimov states only in the $M_z=0$ channel, the fermionic systems show a characteristic doublet of the Efimov states in the $M_z=0$ and $M_z = \pm 1$ channels due to the interplay of finite angular momentum and the anisotropy of the dipole interaction. Both numerical results and analytical formula obtained with the first-order perturbation show that the ratio of the three-body parameters between these two fermionic channels exhibits universality, particularly well in the limit of large mass imbalance. Leveraging this universality, we provide quantitative predictions for the values and ratios of the three-body parameters for experimentally relevant Er-Li and Dy-Li isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Efimov spectra and three-body parameters in highly mass-imbalanced cold-atom mixtures remain universal even when dipole-dipole interactions between heavy atoms are comparable in strength to van der Waals interactions, but this universality holds with the caveat that it applies primarily to excited Efimov states and breaks down for the ground state and near avoided crossings with higher partial-wave channels.

## Method

The authors solved the Born-Oppenheimer equation for three-body systems of two identical heavy atoms (Er or Dy) and one light atom (Li), incorporating both van der Waals and dipole-dipole interactions between the heavy particles. They discretized the radial coordinate with 3000-5000 grid points up to a maximum radius of 400-12000 times the van der Waals length, using a hard-wall boundary condition at short range to represent the quantum defect parameter, and solved coupled-channel equations for angular momentum channels up to L=8-9.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Oi and Endo, 2024) that demonstrated universality of Efimov states at the unitary limit of heavy-light scattering length, now covering the full range of scattering lengths. It builds on the established understanding that three-body parameters are universally determined by the van der Waals length scale for identical bosons (PhysRevLett.107.120401, 2011) and bosonic mixtures (PhysRevLett.109.243201, 2012). The results enable experimental identification of fermionic Efimov states in Er-Li and Dy-Li mixtures through a characteristic doublet structure in three-body loss spectra, with two loss peaks per limit cycle for fermions versus one peak for bosons.
