# Quadrupole collective variables in the natural Cartan-Weyl basis

> The matrix elements of the quadrupole collective variables, emerging from collective nuclear models, are calculated in the natural Cartan-Weyl basis of O(5) which is a subgroup of a covering $SU(1,1)\times O(5)$ structure. Making use of an intermediate set method, explicit expressions of the matrix elements are obtained in a pure algebraic way, fixing the $γ$-rotational structure of collective quadrupole models.

## Key claim

The matrix elements of the quadrupole collective variables \(\alpha_\mu\) can be calculated purely algebraically in the natural Cartan-Weyl basis of \(O(5)\) without explicit construction of wavefunctions, provided the basic commutation relations of the collective variables and the \(O(5)\) generators are known; the derivation fixes the \(\gamma\)-rotational structure but leaves the radial \(\beta\) dependence as a free parameter.

## Method

The authors exploit the \(SU(1,1)\times O(5)\) group structure of the five-dimensional quadrupole harmonic oscillator, focusing on the \(O(5)\) subgroup. They rotate the \(O(5)\) generators into the Cartan-Weyl basis \(SU(2)_X \times SU(2)_Y\) and classify the five collective variables \(\alpha_\mu\) as a \(\{\frac12,\frac12\}\) bispinor plus a biscalar under this product group. Using the Wigner-Eckart theorem for the direct product group, they derive double reduced matrix elements for the \(T\) bitensor (the \(O(5)\) generators) and then, from the commutation relations between \(T\) and \(\alpha\), obtain recurrence relations that determine all matrix elements of \(\alpha\) in terms of the biscalar reduced matrix element, which is fixed by the \(O(5)\)-scalar condition \(\alpha\cdot\alpha = \beta^2\).

## Key numbers

* The quadratic Casimir operator of \(O(5)\) has eigenvalue \(v(v+3)\) for a representation with seniority quantum number \(v\).
* The \(SU(2)_X\) and \(SU(2)_Y\) Casimir operators share the eigenvalue \(X(X+1)\) due to the identity \(X^2 - Y^2 \equiv 0\).
* The double reduced matrix element of the \(T\) bitensor satisfies \(|\langle vX||T||vX+\frac12\rangle|^2 = \frac14 (v-2X)(v+2X+3)(2X+1)(2X+2)\).
* The biscalar reduced matrix element is \(\langle v,X||\alpha^0||v+1,X\rangle = \sqrt{\frac{(v-2X+1)(v+2X+3)}{(2v+3)(2v+5)}} (2X+1)\beta\).
* The biscalar reduced matrix element for the downward step is \(\langle v,X||\alpha^0||v-1,X\rangle = \sqrt{\frac{(v-2X)(v+2X+2)}{(2v+1)(2v+3)}} (2X+1)\beta\).

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach to the Bohr collective model pioneered by Hecht (1964) for fractional parentage coefficients of spin-2 phonons and by the Frankfurt group (Gneuss and Greiner, 1971) in the General Collective Model. It provides an alternative to the vector coherent state formalism of Rowe (1994, 1995) and the algebraic tractable model of Rowe (2004, 2005), which construct bases with good \(O(3)\) angular momentum. The derived matrix elements enable diagonalization of general Bohr Hamiltonians in the Cartan-Weyl basis, with the \(\gamma\)-rotational structure fully fixed algebraically, leaving only the radial \(\beta\) dynamics to be handled via the \(SU(1,1)\) part of the covering group.
