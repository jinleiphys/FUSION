# Lattice simulations of QCD-like theories at non-zero density

> One way of avoiding the complex action problem in lattice QCD at non-zero density is to simulate QCD-like theories with a real action, such as two-colour QCD. The symmetries of two-colour QCD with quarks in the fundamental and in the adjoint representation are described, and the status of lattice simulations is reviewed, with particular emphasis on comparison with predictions from chiral perturbation theory. Finally, we discuss how the lessons from two-colour QCD may be carried over to physical QCD.

## Key claim

Lattice simulations of two-colour QCD with fundamental quarks reproduce chiral perturbation theory predictions for the chiral condensate, diquark condensate, and baryon density, except at very large chemical potentials where saturation effects appear; however, for two-colour QCD with one flavour of adjoint staggered quarks, simulations restricted to the positive determinant sector also reproduce those predictions despite the model having no diquark Goldstone modes, and only when configurations with negative determinants are included does the early onset transition appear to be cancelled.

## Method

The author reviews lattice simulations of two-colour QCD with staggered fermions in both the fundamental and adjoint representations, using Hybrid Monte Carlo (HMC) and two-step multibosonic (TSMB) algorithms. For adjoint quarks, the TSMB algorithm includes a reweighting factor and sign determination for each configuration, allowing observables to be computed separately in positive and negative determinant sectors. The simulations are compared with predictions from chiral perturbation theory (χPT) for the chiral condensate, diquark condensate, and baryon number density as functions of chemical potential μ and quark mass m.

## Key numbers

- For two-colour QCD with fundamental staggered quarks at m=0.2 and diquark source j=0.02, the chiral condensate, diquark condensate, and baryon density agree with χPT predictions from equation (12) across lattice sizes.
- For adjoint staggered quarks with HMC, data for the scaled chiral condensate y and scaled baryon density ñ collapse onto a universal curve up to x≈1.5, where x=2μ/mπ0.
- For adjoint staggered quarks with TSMB at μ=0.36 and μ=0.4, the average chiral condensate and baryon density return to values consistent with μ=0 values when negative determinant configurations are included.
- The onset transition for fundamental quarks occurs at μ≈mπ/2, where mπ is the pion mass.
- For adjoint staggered quarks with HMC, the pion mass appears to agree with the χPT prediction mπ=2μ at x>2.

## Context

This review extends the line of work using QCD-like theories with real actions to avoid the complex action problem in lattice QCD at non-zero density, following earlier studies by Kogut (2000) and Hands (2000). The simulations with fundamental quarks confirm predictions from chiral perturbation theory by Kogut (2000), while the adjoint quark results address the "premature onset" problem that has affected previous attempts to simulate physical QCD at non-zero density. The work enables future studies of gluodynamics in SU(2) and SU(3) at non-zero μ, and may help clarify the deconfinement transition at high μ and low T.
