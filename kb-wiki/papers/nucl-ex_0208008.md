# A Search for Neutral Baryon Resonances Below Pion Threshold

> The reaction p(e,e^{prime}pi^+)X^0 was studied with two high resolution magnetic spectrometers to search for narrow baryon resonances. A missing mass resolution of 2.0 MeV was achieved. A search for structures in the mass region of 0.97 < M_{X^0} < 1.06 GeV yielded no significant signal. The yield ratio of p(e,e^{prime}pi^+)X^0/p(e,e^{prime}pi^+)n was determined to be (-0.35 +/- 0.35) times 10^{-3} at 1.004 GeV and (0.34 +/- 0.42) times 10^{-3} at 1.044 GeV.

## Key claim

The search for narrow neutral baryon resonances in the mass region 0.97 to 1.06 GeV via the p(e,e'pi+)X0 reaction yielded no significant signal, but the authors explicitly note that this null result does not necessarily contradict the earlier claims of Tatischeff et al. (1997) because the hadronic reaction could have allowed more exotic production channels and the sensitivity of this measurement was not much improved compared to that earlier work.

## Method

The experiment used two high-resolution magnetic spectrometers at Jefferson Lab Hall A with a 1.722 GeV electron beam on a liquid hydrogen target, detecting scattered electrons in coincidence with pi+ particles. The missing mass technique was employed, with the spectrometer pair set to kinematics corresponding to an invariant mass W of approximately 1.44 GeV and four-momentum transfer squared of 0.2 GeV^2. Yields were corrected for reconstruction efficiencies, data acquisition dead times, and pion decay, with accidental coincidences subtracted using sideband sampling, and phase space volumes were calculated via Monte Carlo simulation.

## Key numbers

- Missing mass resolution of 2.0 MeV was achieved, demonstrated in calibration kinematics
- Yield ratio K_s = p(e,e'pi+)X0/p(e,e'pi+)n determined to be (-0.35 +/- 0.35) x 10^{-3} at 1.004 GeV
- Yield ratio K_s = (0.34 +/- 0.42) x 10^{-3} at 1.044 GeV
- For the reported state at 0.987 GeV from Fil'kov et al. (2001), K_s = (-0.94 +/- 0.44) x 10^{-3}
- Electron beam energy was 1.722 GeV with average current 33 microamps
- Four-momentum transfer squared was 0.2 GeV^2
- Invariant mass of the pi+X0 system was W approximately 1.44 GeV
- A third-order polynomial fit to the Kinematics-B yield had reduced chi-squared of 1.2 for 90 data points

## Context

This work extends the search for narrow baryon resonances below pion threshold, originally claimed by Tatischeff et al. (1997) in the pp to p pi+ X0 reaction at masses 1.004, 1.044, and 1.094 GeV, into the electromagnetic p(e,e'pi+)X0 channel. The null result is consistent with the arguments of L'vov and Workman (1998) that such states are excluded by real Compton scattering data, and with the null result of Tamii et al. (2002) in the pd to ppX0 reaction, but contrasts with the positive claims of Fil'kov et al. (2001) who reported states at 0.966, 0.987, and 1.003 GeV. The authors propose that future dedicated experiments with improved timing resolution to 0.5 ns and missing mass resolution to 0.5 MeV could set a tighter limit of K_s less than or equal to 1.0 x 10^{-4}.
