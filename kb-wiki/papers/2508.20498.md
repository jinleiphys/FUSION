# $ΞNN$ three-baryon force from SU(3) chiral effective field theory: A femtoscopic study

> Background: The development of SU(3) chiral effective field theory has opened the way to a systematic exploration of three-baryon forces (3BFs), a key ingredient in hypernuclear and dense matter physics. However, $ΞNN$ 3BF based on SU(3) chiral EFT has not been studied until now. Purpose: We apply SU(3) chiral EFT to derive $ΞNN$ potentials in momentum space. Then, we investigate how the $ΞNN$ 3BF affects the correlation function of deuteron--$Ξ^-$ pair created through heavy-ion collisions. Methods: To reduce the number of low-energy constants involved in the $ΞNN$ potentials, we employ the decuplet saturation approximation, by which only two of them remain unconstrained. The deuteron--$Ξ^-$ scattering is treated as an effective two-body problem with the $ΞNN$ 3BF incorporated into the potential between the deuteron and $Ξ^-$. Results: We found that the effect of the $ΞNN$ 3BF on the deuteron--$Ξ^-$ correlation function is at most about 4\%. This small effect is not primarily due to the loosely-bound nature of the deuteron. Instead, this is because the deuteron and $Ξ^-$ interact with each other mainly at low momentum, corresponding to peripheral scattering, where the influence of the $ΞNN$ 3BF is limited. Conclusions: Since the correlation function shows limited sensitivity to the short-range 3BF, complementary approaches may be necessary.

## Key claim

The effect of the leading SU(3) chiral EFT $\Xi NN$ three-baryon force on the deuteron–$\Xi^-$ correlation function is at most about 4%, and this small effect arises not primarily from the loosely-bound nature of the deuteron but because the deuteron and $\Xi^-$ interact mainly at low momentum (peripheral scattering), where the short-range 3BF influence is limited.

## Method

The authors derived the $\Xi NN$ three-baryon force (3BF) potentials in momentum space from SU(3) chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order, comprising two-pion exchange, one-pion exchange plus contact, and pure contact terms. To reduce the number of low-energy constants, the decuplet saturation approximation was employed, leaving only two unconstrained LECs. The deuteron–$\Xi^-$ scattering was treated as an effective two-body problem, with the $\Xi NN$ 3BF folded into the deuteron–$\Xi^-$ potential using the deuteron wave function from the Argonne $V4'$ interaction, and only the central component of the 3BF was retained for the correlation function calculation.

## Key numbers

- The effect of the $\Xi NN$ 3BF on the deuteron–$\Xi^-$ correlation function is at most about 4%.
- The pion decay constant used is $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV, and the average pion mass is $m_\pi = 138.04$ MeV.
- The axial-vector coupling constants are given by $g_A = D+F$ and $g_B = -D+F$ with $D \approx 0.8$ and $F \approx 0.5$.

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) chiral EFT framework for three-baryon forces, previously applied to $\Lambda NN$ systems (Le 2025, Gerstung 2020), into the strangeness $S=-2$ sector for the first time. It contrasts with a recent study (PhysRevC.110.054004, 2024) where phenomenological $\Lambda NN$ 3BFs significantly reduced the $pp\Lambda$ correlation function, showing that the $\Xi NN$ 3BF has much smaller effects on the deuteron–$\Xi^-$ correlation function. The results suggest that complementary approaches, such as high-momentum deuteron–$\Xi^-$ scattering, may be necessary to probe the $\Xi NN$ 3BF.
