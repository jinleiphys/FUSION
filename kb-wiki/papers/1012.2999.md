# Nuclear suppression of dileptons at forward rapidities

> Data from E772 and E866 experiments on the Drell-Yan process exhibit a significant nuclear suppression at large Feynman xF. We show that a corresponding kinematic region does not allow to interpret this as a manifestation of coherence or a Color Glass Condensate. We demonstrate, however, that this suppression can be treated alternatively as an effective energy loss proportional to initial energy. To eliminate suppression coming from the coherence, we perform predictions for nuclear effects also at large dilepton masses. Our calculations are in a good agreement with available data. Since the kinematic limit can be also approached in transverse momenta pT, we present in the RHIC energy range corresponding predictions for expected large-pT suppression as well. Since a new experiment E906 planned at FNAL will provide us with more precise data soon, we present also predictions for expected large-xF nuclear suppression in this kinematic region.

## Key claim

The observed nuclear suppression of Drell-Yan dileptons at large Feynman xF in fixed-target experiments (E772, E866) cannot be interpreted as a signature of coherence effects or the Color Glass Condensate because the kinematic region does not allow such phenomena; instead, the suppression can be treated as an effective energy loss proportional to initial energy, arising from energy conservation constraints in multiple parton rescatterings.

## Method

The authors use the color dipole approach in the target rest frame, treating Drell-Yan production as bremsstrahlung of a virtual photon from a projectile quark. Nuclear effects are calculated in both the short coherence length (SCL) and long coherence length (LCL) regimes, with finite coherence length corrections applied via linear interpolation using the nuclear longitudinal form factor. The effective energy loss mechanism is implemented through a modified parton distribution function that includes a survival probability factor S(x) ~ 1-x for large rapidity gap processes, with the nuclear thickness function and a fixed effective cross section sigma_eff = 20 mb.

## Key numbers

- The effective cross section used in the energy-loss suppression factor is sigma_eff = 20 mb.
- For the E772 data, predictions are shown for dilepton invariant mass bins 6 < M < 7 GeV and 7 < M < 8 GeV on tungsten and deuterium targets.
- At RHIC energy sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, predictions for d+Au collisions show isotopic effects giving R_d+Au^IE ~ 0.73 to 0.79 at large pT, depending on xF.
- The new E906 experiment at Fermilab will operate at E_lab = 120 GeV, with predictions for the ratio R_DY(W/D) in the same mass bins 6 < M < 7 GeV and 7 < M < 8 GeV.
- The mean-square charge radius of the proton used in the quark density matrix is <r_ch^2> = 0.79 +/- 0.03 fm^2.

## Context

This work extends the energy-loss mechanism proposed by Kopeliovich et al. (2005) to Drell-Yan production, demonstrating that the same suppression pattern seen at RHIC energies also explains fixed-target data from E772 and E866 at Fermilab without invoking coherence or Color Glass Condensate effects. The predictions for the upcoming E906 experiment at Fermilab, where the lower beam energy (120 GeV) excludes coherence interpretations, will provide a crucial test distinguishing this energy-loss mechanism from alternative explanations. The approach is universal, applicable to any reaction at any energy where the kinematic limit is approached.
