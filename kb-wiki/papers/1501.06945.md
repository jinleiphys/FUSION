# Nuclear equation of state and finite nucleon volumes

> It is shown how the Equation of State (EoS) depends on nucleon properties inside Nuclear Matter (NM). We propose to benefit from the concept of enthalpy in order to include volume corrections to the nucleon rest energy, which are proportional to pressure and absent in a standard Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) with point-like nucleons. As a result, the nucleon mass can decrease inside NM, making the model nonlinear and the EoS softer. The course of the EoS in our RMF model agrees with a semi-empirical estimate and is close to the results obtained from extensive DBHF calculations with a Bonn A potential, which produce an EoS stiff enough to describe neutron star properties (mass--radius constraint), especially the masses of PSR J1614_2230 and PSR J0348_0432, known as the most massive ($\sim 2 M_\odot$) neutron stars. The presented model has proper saturation properties, including a good value of compressibility.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including a pressure-dependent volume correction to the nucleon rest energy (via enthalpy) in a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model softens the nuclear equation of state (EoS) and reduces compressibility, but only when the nucleon radius is held constant (case B); the effect is negligible if the nucleon mass is held constant (case A).

## Method

The author modifies the standard linear (σ,ω) RMF model by replacing the constant nucleon mass with a pressure-dependent effective mass M_pr(ρ) = M_N − p_H Ω_N, derived from a bag model where the nucleon enthalpy H_N = M_pr + p_H Ω_N is kept constant. The coupled RMF equations are solved self-consistently with the pressure p_H = ρ² ε'(ρ)/(1 − ρ Ω_N) for densities above saturation, using two parameter sets (S1 and S2) and nucleon radii R₀ = 0.55, 0.6, 0.7, 0.8 fm.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear compressibility K_A⁻¹ is reduced from 540 MeV (standard linear RMF, set S2) to 328, 252, and 172 MeV for R₀ = 0.6, 0.7, and 0.8 fm respectively (case B).
- The estimated nucleon compressibility from alpha scattering experiments is K_N⁻¹ = 1.4 ± 0.3 GeV.
- The bag constant B(ρ₀) ≈ 60 MeV fm⁻³; in case B the bag constant disappears at p_H ≈ 100 MeV fm⁻³.
- For case B with R₀ = 0.7 fm and set S2, the EoS matches the DBHF Bonn A result up to density ρ ≈ 0.6 fm⁻³.
- The saturation densities used are ρ₀ ≈ 0.149 fm⁻³ (set S1) and ρ₀ ≈ 0.193 fm⁻³ (set S2).
- The Hugenholtz-van Hove relation is satisfied with 0.1–3% numerical accuracy.

## Context

This work extends earlier excluded-volume RMF models (Costa et al., Rocha et al.) that assumed constant nucleon volume, by adding the pressure-volume work term to the nucleon rest energy. It also contrasts with the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model (Jennings, Hua) where the bag radius can increase with density. The resulting softened EoS is close to Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations with the Bonn A potential (Bonn group) and satisfies the flow constraint from heavy-ion collisions (Danielewicz et al.). The model is intended for studying neutron star properties, particularly the masses of PSR J1614–2230 and PSR J0348+0432 (∼2 M⊙).
