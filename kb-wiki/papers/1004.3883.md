# Maxwell-Chern-Simons Hydrodynamics for the Chiral Magnetic Effect

> The rate of vacuum changing topological solutions of the gluon field, sphalerons, is estimated to be large at the typical temperatures of heavy-ion collisions, particularly at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. Such windings in the gluon field are expected to produce parity-odd bubbles, which cause separation of positively and negatively charged quarks along the axis of the external magnetic field. This chiral magnetic effect can be mimicked by Chern-Simons modified electromagnetism. Here we present a model of relativistic hydrodynamics including the effects of axial anomalies via the Chern-Simons term.

# Maxwell-Chern-Simons Hydrodynamics for the Chiral Magnetic Effect

## Key claim
The paper presents a relativistic hydrodynamic model that incorporates axial anomalies via a Chern-Simons term, where the entropy of the fluid subsystem can locally decrease due to the Chern-Simons term, but total entropy including the vacuum degrees of freedom is conserved when a modified Gibbs relation is used.

## Method
The author starts from the effective Maxwell-Chern-Simons Lagrangian where a spacetime-dependent θ field mimics parity-odd bubbles from gluon field topology. The equations of motion for the electromagnetic field are derived with Chern-Simons modifications, then coupled to ideal relativistic hydrodynamics through the conservation of total energy-momentum (fluid plus electromagnetic). The entropy production is computed by contracting the hydrodynamic equation with the fluid four-velocity, and a modified Gibbs relation including a generalized force Rθ is introduced to restore entropy conservation for the full system.

## Key numbers
- The sphaleron rate is approximately Γ ≈ 386 α_s⁵ T⁴, where α_s = g²/4π and T is the temperature.
- For typical RHIC parameters, the magnetic field generated normal to the collision plane is of order eB ∼ (10–100 MeV)² during the first moments (τ ∼ 1 fm/c) of the collision.
- For comparison, typical magnetic fields in magnetized neutron stars and magnetars are about eB ≈ (2 MeV)², or 10¹⁰ T.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) originally proposed by Kharzeev (2004) and developed by Kharzeev, McLerran, and Warringa (2007–2008), as well as Fukushima, Kharzeev, and Warringa (2008). The model uses Chern-Simons modified electromagnetism following earlier work by Sikivie (1984), Wilczek (1987), and Carroll, Field, and Jackiw (1989). The author notes that Son and Surowka (2009) added an extra term to the current to cure negative entropy change, while this paper takes a different approach using a modified Gibbs relation. The equations derived are intended for numerical solution with realistic heavy-ion collision parameters, which the author states is work in progress.
