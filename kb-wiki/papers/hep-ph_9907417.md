# Omega Meson Cloud and the Proton's Light Anti-Quark Distribution

## Key claim

Including the omega meson cloud with a coupling constant \(g_\omega^2/4\pi \approx 8\) in the meson cloud model of the nucleon allows a reasonably good description of both the \((\bar{d} - \bar{u})\) distribution and the \(\bar{d}/\bar{u}\) ratio, which previous meson cloud models had failed to reproduce simultaneously.

## Method

The authors use the meson cloud model (generalized Sullivan process) to compute the quark distribution functions of the proton, writing the proton wave function as a superposition of a bare nucleon and Fock states containing a baryon and a meson (pion, omega, and eta mesons). The splitting functions for the meson-baryon fluctuations are computed with exponential form factors using cut-off parameters \(\Lambda_M\), and the quark distributions of the mesons are taken from parametrizations of pion structure functions, assuming the omega meson structure function is the same as that of the pion. The bare nucleon sea is parametrized with a simple functional form, and the omega meson coupling constant and cut-off are varied to fit the experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The integral \(D \equiv \int_0^1 dx\; (\bar{d}(x)-\bar{u}(x)) = 0.100 \pm 0.018\) is used to constrain the pion cut-off parameter \(\Lambda_\pi = (0.83 \pm 0.05)\) GeV.
- The omega meson cut-off parameter is varied in the range 1.3 GeV \(< \Lambda_\omega <\) 1.8 GeV, with a central value of 1.5 GeV used for the main result.
- The omega-proton coupling constant is taken as \(g_\omega^2/4\pi = 8.1\), with a range explored from 7 to 20.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant is taken as \(g_\pi^2/4\pi = 13.6\).
- Eta meson contributions change \(\bar{d}/\bar{u}\) by only 1 to 2 percent.

## Context

This work extends the meson cloud model approach to the proton's light anti-quark asymmetry, which was motivated by the NMC measurement of the Gottfried sum rule violation and subsequent Drell-Yan measurements by NA51 and E866 (1998). Previous meson cloud models could describe the \(x\)-dependence of \((\bar{d} - \bar{u})\) but failed to reproduce the \(\bar{d}/\bar{u}\) ratio, which either increased monotonically with \(x\) or turned back towards unity too slowly (Alberg and Henley, 1998). The inclusion of the omega meson, which provides repulsive short-range nucleon-nucleon forces and has been important in deep inelastic scattering from nuclei (GamLF), enables a simultaneous description of both observables. This work enables further tests with upcoming higher-precision data and suggests that including sigma meson effects could further improve the description.
