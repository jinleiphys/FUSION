# Similarity Renormalization Group for Few-Body Systems

> 3 pages, 6 figures. To appear in the proceedings of the 20th European Conference on Few-Body Problems in Physics (EFB20), Pisa, September 10-14, 2007

## Key claim

The Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) evolves internucleon potentials to softer forms that decouple low- from high-momentum matrix elements, but the evolution is only approximately unitary in few-body calculations because the commutator structure generates many-body interactions that must be truncated.

## Method

The SRG implements a series of unitary transformations on the Hamiltonian parameterized by a flow parameter \(s\) (or \(\lambda \equiv s^{-1/4}\)) through a flow equation \(dH_s/ds = [[G_s, H_s], H_s]\), with \(G_s = T_{\rm rel}\) in the partial-wave momentum basis. The flow equation drives off-diagonal matrix elements to zero, partially diagonalizing the momentum-space potential. The evolution is applied to the NN part only, with the induced three-nucleon (3N) interactions truncated; the 3N evolution is being implemented separately in three-particle space.

## Key numbers

- Argonne \(v_{18}\) \(^1\)S\(_0\) phase shifts are shown up to 800 MeV lab energy; SRG-evolved potentials at any \(\lambda\) produce indistinguishable phase shifts from the original due to exact unitarity at the two-body level.
- SRG evolution to \(\lambda = 2\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) with a momentum cutoff \(k_{\rm max} = 2.2\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) leaves phase shifts unchanged up to the corresponding \(E_{\rm lab}\), demonstrating decoupling.
- Triton ground-state energy with a chiral EFT N\(^3\)LO potential converges to the full answer at lower \(k_{\rm max}\) for smaller \(\lambda\), as shown in the right panel of Fig. 2.
- Ground-state energy in \(^6\)Li is plotted as a function of \(\lambda\) in the left panel of Fig. 4, showing improved convergence (decreasing error bars) for smaller \(\lambda\).

## Context

This work extends the SRG approach from nuclear matter studies by Bogner et al. (2006, 2007) to few-body systems, demonstrating that evolved soft potentials yield rapid variational convergence in no-core shell model calculations. The decoupling property contradicts the misconception that high-energy phase shifts are necessary for nuclear structure, as shown by the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential. The approach enables systematic inclusion of induced three-nucleon forces, with a first approximation of re-fitting short-distance coefficients of the chiral EFT 3N force at each \(\lambda\), while full 3N evolution via the flow equation in three-particle space is being implemented in parallel.
