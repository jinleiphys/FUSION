# Another walk through the world of chiral dynamics

> Chiral dynamics is a pretty mature field. Nonetheless, there are many exciting new developments. In this opening talk, I consider S-wave, isospin-zero pion-pion scattering and the calculation of the width of the lightest baryon resonances at two loops. New insights into the chiral dynamics of charm-strange mesons are discussed as well as recent results on the flavor decomposition of the pion-nucleon $σ$-term. I end with a short wish-list of lattice QCD tests pertinent to chiral dynamics.

## Key claim

Chiral dynamics remains a mature but active field, with new progress in two-loop calculations of baryon resonance widths and in heavy-light meson spectroscopy; however, the author notes that lattice QCD still lacks precise determinations of several key scattering lengths, and the three-flavor sector remains ambiguous because the strange quark mass is comparable to the QCD scale.

## Method

The talk reviews four topics: (1) S-wave isospin-zero pion-pion scattering length $a_0$, analyzed via partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQCHPT) to separate connected and disconnected Wick contractions; (2) the widths of the $\Delta(1232)$ and Roper $N^*(1440)$ resonances calculated at two-loop order using a complex-mass renormalization scheme and cutting rules; (3) heavy-light meson spectroscopy for charm-strange and bottom-strange systems, combining unitarized chiral perturbation theory with lattice QCD results and experimental data; (4) the flavor decomposition of the pion-nucleon $\sigma$-term using baryon chiral perturbation theory to fourth order in both heavy-baryon and covariant (EOMS) frameworks.

## Key numbers

* Pion-pion scattering length $a_0$ from lattice QCD: Fu (2013) gives $0.214(4)(7)$ using asqtad staggered fermions with pion mass range 240–430 MeV; Liu et al. (2016) give $0.198(9)(6)$ using twisted mass fermions with pion mass range 250–320 MeV. Both are consistent with the Roy-equation result $a_0 = 0.220 \pm 0.005$.
* Neglecting the doubly disconnected vacuum (V-type) contraction in $\pi\pi$ scattering reduces $a_0$ by about 12%.
* $\Delta(1232)$ decay width formula: $\Gamma(\Delta \to N\pi) = (53.91\,h_A^2 + 0.87\,g_1^2 h_A^2 - 3.31\,g_1 h_A^2 - 0.99\,h_A^4)$ MeV.
* Roper $N^*(1440)$ one-pion decay width: $\Gamma(R \to N\pi) = 550(58)\,g_{\pi NR}^2$ MeV; with $g_{\pi NR} = \pm (0.47 \pm 0.04)$ fixed from the PDG value $\Gamma_{R\to\pi N} = (123.5 \pm 19.0)$ MeV.
* Roper two-pion decay width prediction under maximal mixing ($g_R = g_A$, $h_R = h_A$): $\Gamma(R \to N\pi\pi) = (41 \pm 22_{\text{LECs}} \pm 17_{\text{h.o.}})$ MeV, consistent with the PDG value $(67 \pm 10)$ MeV.
* $DK$ scattering length from lattice QCD: $a_0 = -1.33(20)$ fm, consistent with the molecular-scenario prediction $a_0^{\text{mol}} \simeq -1$ fm.
* Wavefunction renormalization constant for $D_{s0}^*(2317)$: $1-Z = 1.04(0.08)(0.30)$, supporting the molecular interpretation.
* Predicted bottom analogues: $M_{B_{s0}^\star} \simeq 5.730$ GeV and $M_{B_{s1}} \simeq 5.776$ GeV, consistent with lattice QCD values $M_{B_{s0}^\star}^{\text{lat}} = (5.711 \pm 0.013 \pm 0.019)$ GeV and $M_{B_{s1}}^{\text{lat}} = (5.750 \pm 0.017 \pm 0.019)$ GeV.
* Two-pole structure for $I=1/2$ $D_0^\star$: lower pole at $(2105^{+6}_{-8}, 102^{+10}_{-11})$ MeV (mass, half-width), higher pole at $(2451^{+36}_{-26}, 134^{+7}_{-8})$ MeV.
* Flavor-singlet $\sigma$-term $\sigma_0$ at $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$: heavy-baryon result $64.1(31.7)(9.3)$ MeV; heavy-baryon with decuplet $64.0(31.7)(18.7)$ MeV; EOMS result $51.8(31.4)(5.7)$ MeV; EOMS with decuplet $61.8(31.4)(9.3)$ MeV.

## Context

This opening talk at the 9th International Workshop on Chiral Dynamics (2018) extends the author's 2012 review of chiral dynamics, updating progress in $\pi\pi$ scattering, baryon resonances, and heavy-light mesons. The two-loop calculation of the $\Delta$ width builds on the complex-mass scheme introduced for precision electroweak physics and later applied to chiral EFT by Djukanovic et al. (2009). The heavy-light meson analysis extends the molecular interpretation of $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ from earlier work by Kolomeitsev and Lutz (2003) and Guo et al. (2006), and is now supported by lattice QCD from Mohler et al. (2013) and Bali et al. (2017). The flavor decomposition of the $\sigma$-term addresses the tension between Roy-Steiner analyses and lattice determinations, using both heavy-baryon and covariant frameworks as reviewed by Bernard (2007). The talk identifies open challenges for lattice QCD: precise determinations of $\pi\pi$, $\pi K$, and $\pi N$ scattering lengths, and calculations of three-body decays such as $\omega \to 3\pi$ and $R \to N\pi\pi$.
