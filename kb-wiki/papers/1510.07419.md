# The $Ξ^* \bar{K}$ and $Ωη$ interaction within a chiral unitary approach

> In this work we study the interaction of the coupled channels $Ωη$ and $Ξ^* \bar{K}$ within the chiral unitary approach. The systems under consideration have total isospins $0$, strangeness $S = -3$, and spin $3/2$. We studied the $s$ wave interaction which implies that the possible resonances generated in the system can have spin-parity $J^P = 3/2^-$. The unitary amplitudes in coupled channels develop poles that can be associated with some known baryonic resonances. We find there is a dynamically generated $3/2^-$ $Ω$ state with mass around $1800$ MeV, which is in agreement with the predictions of the five-quark model.

## Key claim
By adjusting the subtraction constant in the chiral unitary approach, the authors find a dynamically generated \(3/2^-\) \(\Omega\) state with mass around 1800 MeV, which is a bound state of the \(\Xi^* \bar{K}\) and \(\Omega \eta\) channels; this mass is lower than that of the lowest \(1/2^-\) \(\Omega\) state and agrees with the five-quark model predictions of An (2014).

## Method
The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the \(s\)-wave coupled channels \(\Xi^* \bar{K}\) and \(\Omega \eta\) (total isospin \(I=0\), strangeness \(S=-3\), spin \(J=3/2\)) using the chiral unitary approach. The transition potential is derived from chiral perturbation theory with the pion decay constant \(f = 93\) MeV, and the loop function \(G\) is regularized via dimensional regularization with scale \(\mu = 700\) MeV. The subtraction constant \(a(\mu)\) is tuned to reproduce the mass of the \(3/2^-\) \(\Omega\) state predicted by An (2014), and poles of the scattering amplitude are searched for on the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers
- Pole position of the dynamically generated \(\Omega\) state: \(z_R = (1785.7, -i0)\) MeV, obtained with subtraction constant \(a(\mu) = -3.4\).
- Couplings of the state to the channels: \(g_{11} = 2.04\) (to \(\Xi^* \bar{K}\)) and \(g_{22} = 2.31\) (to \(\Omega \eta\)).
- Mass thresholds: \(\Xi^* \bar{K}\) at 2029 MeV, \(\Omega \eta\) at 2220 MeV (computed from \(M_{\Xi^*} = 1533.4\) MeV, \(m_{\bar{K}} = 495.6\) MeV, \(M_{\Omega} = 1672.5\) MeV, \(m_{\eta} = 547.9\) MeV).
- Varying \(a(\mu)\) from \(-4.0\) to \(-1.5\) shifts the pole mass from 1699 MeV to 2217 MeV; the value \(a(\mu) = -3.4 \pm 0.1\) reproduces the mass \(M_{\Omega^*} = 1785 \pm 25\) MeV from An (2014).

## Context
This work extends the chiral unitary study of baryon decuplet and meson octet interactions by Sarkar (2004), who found a pole at \((2141, -i38)\) MeV for the same quantum numbers. By adjusting the subtraction constant, the authors obtain a lower-mass \(\Omega\) state that supports the five-quark model predictions of Yuan (2012), An (2013), and An (2014), where mixing with five-quark components lowers the \(3/2^-\) \(\Omega\) mass to around 1785 MeV. The result suggests that meson-baryon (five-quark) components are more important in the \(3/2^-\) \(\Omega\) excited states than in the \(1/2^-\) states, and it provides a target for future experimental searches at BESIII.
