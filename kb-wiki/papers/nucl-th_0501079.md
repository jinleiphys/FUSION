# RDWIA analysis of 12C(e,e'p) for Q^2 < 2 (GeV/c)^2

> We analyze data for 12C(e,e'p) with Q^2 < 2 (GeV/c)^2 using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) based upon Dirac-Hartree wave functions. The 1p normalization extracted from data for Q^2 > 0.6 (GeV/c)^2 is approximately 0.87, independent of Q^2, which is consistent with the predicted depletion by short-range correlations. The total 1p and 1s strength for E_m < 80 MeV approaches 100% of IPSM, consistent with a continuum contribution for 30 < E_m < 80 MeV of about 12% of IPSM. Similarly, a scale factor of 1.12 brings RDWIA calculations into good agreement with 12C(e,e'p) data for transparency. We also analyzed low Q^2 data from which a recent NDWIA analysis suggested that spectroscopic factors might depend strongly upon the resolution of the probe. We find that momentum distributions for their empirical Woods-Saxon wave functions fit to low Q^2 data for parallel kinematics are too narrow to reproduce data for quasiperpendicular kinematics, especially for larger Q^2, and are partly responsible for reducing fitted normalization factors.

## Key claim

The RDWIA analysis finds that the 1p normalization factor for 12C(e,e'p) at Q2 > 0.6 (GeV/c)2 is approximately 0.87 and independent of Q2, contradicting claims from a prior NRDWIA analysis that spectroscopic factors depend strongly on the resolution of the probe; the paper argues that the apparent Q2 dependence arises from using Woods-Saxon wave functions that are too narrow to describe data for quasiperpendicular kinematics.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculations using Dirac-Hartree wave functions (Horowitz-Serot and NLSH models) and the CC2 current operator with MMD form factors in Coulomb gauge, treating electron distortion in the q_eff approximation. They analyzed data from Tokyo, Saclay, NIKHEF, SLAC, and JLab experiments covering Q2 from 0.02 to 1.84 (GeV/c)2, fitting normalization factors for the 1p and 1s shells to reduced cross sections in bins of missing energy, using data with |p_m| ≤ 200 MeV/c. They also compared with Woods-Saxon wave functions from the NIKHEF analysis and with effective momentum approximation (EMA) calculations.

## Key numbers

- 1p normalization factor for Q2 > 0.6 (GeV/c)2: approximately 0.87, independent of Q2
- Total 1p and 1s strength for Em < 80 MeV approaches 100% of IPSM
- Continuum contribution for 30 < Em < 80 MeV: about 12% of IPSM
- Scale factor of 1.12 brings RDWIA calculations into agreement with 12C(e,e'p) data for transparency
- NIKHEF NRDWIA normalization factors from Lapikas et al. (2000): N1p = 0.56 ± 0.02 and N1s = 0.59 ± 0.04 for Em < 80 MeV
- RDWIA 1p normalization for JLab data at Q2 = 0.6 (GeV/c)2: 0.885 (NLSH), 0.852 (HS)
- RDWIA 1p normalization for JLab data at Q2 = 1.2 (GeV/c)2: 0.868 (NLSH), 0.836 (HS)
- RDWIA 1p normalization for JLab data at Q2 = 1.8 (GeV/c)2: 0.847 (NLSH), 0.830 (HS)
- IPSM normalization for JLab inclusive bin (10-80 MeV): 0.985 (NLSH average over Q2)

## Context

This work extends the RDWIA analyses previously applied to 16O(e,e'p) by Fissum et al. (2004) and addresses the claim by Lapikas et al. (2000) that spectroscopic factors for 12C vary strongly with Q2, based on NRDWIA calculations using Woods-Saxon wave functions fit to NIKHEF data at Tp = 70 MeV. The paper argues that the narrower momentum distributions from those Woods-Saxon wave functions, rather than genuine Q2 dependence of spectroscopic factors, are responsible for the lower normalization factors at low Q2. The results are consistent with self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) calculations by Muther et al. (1994, 1995) and Frick et al. (2004) predicting approximately 85% occupancy of IPSM orbitals due to short-range correlations, and with correlated basis function (CBF) theory by Benhar et al. (1990). The findings have implications for nuclear transparency measurements by Dutta et al. (2003) and Garrow et al. (2002) that depend on accurate depletion factors.
