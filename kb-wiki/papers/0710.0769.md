# Four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations using a transformed harmonic oscillator basis

> The scattering of a weakly bound three-body system by a target is discussed. A transformed harmonic oscillator basis is used to provide an appropriate discrete and finite basis for treating the continuum part of the spectrum of the projectile. The continuum-discretized coupled channels framework is used for the scattering calculations. The formalism is applied to different reactions, 6He+12C at 229.8 MeV, 6He+64Zn at 10 and 13.6 MeV, and 6He+208Pb at 22 MeV, induced by the Borromean nucleus 6He. Both the Coulomb and nuclear interactions with a target are taken into account.

## Key claim

The transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis provides a discrete and finite representation of the three-body continuum that converges quickly for light-target reactions but shows slower convergence for heavy-target reactions, particularly for the 6He+208Pb system at 22 MeV where the oscillatory pattern in the rainbow region changes between different numbers of hyperradial excitations (nb).

## Method

The authors diagonalize the three-body Hamiltonian of the projectile in a finite THO basis expanded in hyperspherical harmonics, generating pseudo-states that discretize the continuum. These pseudo-states are then used within the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) framework to solve the four-body scattering problem, with coupling potentials calculated via a multipole expansion of the projectile-target interaction folded with phenomenological optical potentials. The method is applied to reactions of the Borromean nucleus 6He with 12C, 64Zn, and 208Pb targets, including both Coulomb and nuclear interactions and continuum-continuum couplings.

## Key numbers

- 6He ground state binding energy: 0.95372 MeV (calculated with Kmax=8, nb=4)
- 6He root-mean-square point nucleon matter radius: 2.46 fm (assuming alpha-particle rms matter radius of 1.47 fm)
- 6He+12C at Elab=229.8 MeV: full CDCC calculation reproduces data up to 10 degrees but underestimates data at larger angles
- 6He+64Zn at Elab=13.6 MeV: one-channel calculation shows pronounced rainbow peak at around 30 degrees which is suppressed by continuum couplings
- 6He+64Zn at Elab=10 MeV: full CDCC calculation improves agreement at backward angles, though some underestimation remains
- 6He+208Pb at Elab=22 MeV: convergence with respect to hypermomentum achieved for Kmax=6 or 8; convergence with respect to nb is slow with differences less than 5%

## Context

This work extends the THO pseudo-state method, previously applied to two-body continuum problems by Rodriguez-Gallardo (2004) and Moro (2002, 2006), to four-body CDCC calculations for three-body projectiles. It builds on prior CDCC calculations for 6He scattering using Gaussian pseudo-state bases by Matsumoto (2004, 2006), and reproduces the same underestimation of large-angle data for 6He+12C at 229.8 MeV found in the phenomenological analysis of Lapoux (2002). The method enables systematic four-body reaction calculations for Borromean nuclei across different target masses and energies, though the slow convergence for heavy targets suggests further development is needed for such systems.
