# Exclusive semileptonic decays of ground-state $cb$ baryons driven by a $c\to s,d$ quark transition

> We evaluate semileptonic decays of spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 doubly heavy $cb$ baryons. The decays are driven by a $c\to s,d$ transition at the quark level. We check our results for the form factors against heavy quark spin symmetry constraints obtained in the limit of very large heavy quark masses and near zero recoil.

## Key claim

The authors evaluate semileptonic decay widths of ground-state doubly heavy $cb$ baryons driven by a $c\to s,d$ transition, and find that configuration mixing (hyperfine mixing between $S=0$ and $S=1$ components in spin-1/2 states) has an important effect on the decay widths, particularly when the two light quarks in the final state couple to total spin 0.

## Method

The authors use a constituent quark model to compute hadronic matrix elements for $1/2\to1/2$, $1/2\to3/2$, $3/2\to1/2$, and $3/2\to3/2$ transitions, parameterizing them in terms of vector and axial form factors. They then calculate total semileptonic decay widths by integrating over the product of leptonic and hadronic tensors, using CKM matrix elements $|V_{cs}|=0.97345$ and $|V_{cd}|=0.2252$ and the Fermi constant $G_F=1.16637(1)\times10^{-11}$ MeV$^{-2}$. The results are checked against Heavy Quark Spin Symmetry (HQSS) constraints derived from the Trace Formalism, which are strictly valid in the limit of very large heavy quark masses and near zero recoil.

## Key numbers

- For $c\to s$ decays: $\Xi^{(1)+}_{cbu}\to\Xi^0_b\, e^+\nu_e$ width = $3.74\times10^{-14}$ GeV (with mixing) vs $3.45\times10^{-14}$ GeV (without mixing)
- For $c\to s$ decays: $\Xi^{(1)+}_{cbu}\to\Xi'^0_b\, e^+\nu_e$ width = $3.88\times10^{-14}$ GeV (with mixing) vs $1.66\times10^{-14}$ GeV (without mixing), showing a factor ~2.3 enhancement from configuration mixing
- For $c\to s$ decays: $\Omega^{*0}_{cbs}\to\Omega^{*-}_b\, e^+\nu_e$ width = $10.2\times10^{-14}$ GeV, the largest single decay width reported
- For $c\to d$ decays: $\Xi^{(1)+}_{cbu}\to\Lambda^0_b\, e^+\nu_e$ width = $0.219\times10^{-14}$ GeV (with mixing) vs $0.196\times10^{-14}$ GeV (without mixing)
- For $c\to d$ decays: $\Omega^{*0}_{cbs}\to\Xi^{*-}_b\, e^+\nu_e$ width = $0.223\times10^{-14}$ GeV
- HQSS constraints are satisfied at the 10% level near zero recoil, with deviations attributed to corrections in the inverse of the heavy quark masses

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of doubly heavy baryon decays, including their calculation of $b\to c$ semileptonic decays (Albertus 2009) and electromagnetic transitions (Albertus 2010), as well as their study of $c\to s,d$ decays of double charmed baryons (Albertus 2011). The results confirm the earlier finding by Roberts and Pervin (2008, 2009) that hyperfine mixing in $cb$ states has a tremendous impact on decay widths. The authors also compare with prior calculations by Sanchis (1995), Faessler (2001), and Kiselev (2001), finding reasonable agreement where comparisons are possible. The work enables future experimental searches for these decays at facilities such as LHCb, and provides model-independent HQSS-based predictions for ratios of decay widths that should hold at the 10% level in other theoretical approaches.
