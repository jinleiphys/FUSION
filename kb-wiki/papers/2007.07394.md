# Nonperturbative quark matter equations of state with vector interactions

> Nonperturbative equations of state (EoSs) for two and three quark flavors are constructed with the functional renormalization group (FRG) within a quark-meson model truncation augmented by vector mesons for low temperature and high density. Based on previous FRG studies without repulsive vector meson interactions the influence of isoscalar vector $ω$- and $ϕ$-mesons on the dynamical fluctuations of quarks and (pseudo)scalar mesons is investigated. The grand potential as well as vector meson condensates are evaluated as a function of quark chemical potential and the quark matter EoS in $β$-equilibrium is applied to neutron star (NS) physics. The tidal deformability and mass-radius relations for hybrid stars from combined hadronic and quark matter EoSs are compared for different vector couplings. We observe a significant impact of the vector mesons on the quark matter EoS such that the resulting EoS is sufficiently stiff to support two-solar-mass neutron stars.

## Key claim

The inclusion of isoscalar vector mesons (omega and phi) on a constant background level within a functional renormalization group (FRG) treatment of a quark-meson model stiffens the quark matter equation of state sufficiently to support two-solar-mass neutron stars, in contrast to previous FRG studies without vector interactions which produced too soft an EoS for hybrid stars with three-flavor quark cores.

## Method

The authors constructed nonperturbative equations of state for two and three quark flavors using the functional renormalization group within a quark-meson model truncation, where (pseudo)scalar mesons and quarks were treated dynamically via the Wetterich flow equation, while vector mesons (omega for two flavors; omega and phi for three flavors) were included as static background fields with a mean-field potential. The grand potential was evaluated as a function of quark chemical potential, and the resulting quark matter EoS in beta-equilibrium was combined with a hadronic EoS to compute hybrid star properties including mass-radius relations and tidal deformability for different vector couplings.

## Key numbers

- The vector meson masses were fixed to measured values: m_omega = 782 MeV and m_phi = 1020 MeV, leaving the vector coupling g_v as the only free parameter.
- The saturation density of nuclear matter is given as rho_0 ~ 0.16 fm^{-3}, with neutron star core densities expected up to approximately 8-10 rho_0.
- At extremely high densities above rho > 60 rho_0, perturbative QCD becomes applicable.
- The quark single-particle energies are given by E_l = sqrt(k^2 + g_s^2 sigma_l^2/4) for light quarks and E_s = sqrt(k^2 + g_s^2 sigma_s^2/2) for strange quarks.

## Context

This work extends previous FRG studies by Otto et al. (2019) that investigated the impact of (pseudo)scalar meson fluctuations on the compact star EoS but found the resulting soft EoS could not support two-solar-mass neutron stars with three-flavor quark cores. The inclusion of repulsive vector interactions, which are known to stiffen the EoS from phenomenological quark matter studies (e.g., Buballa 1996, Alvarez-Castillo 2018) and hyperonic models (e.g., Weissenborn 2011, Oertel 2014), enables the construction of hybrid stars compatible with current neutron star mass measurements from Demorest (2010), Antoniadis (2013), and Cromartie (2019). This work enables future comparisons with multimessenger observations from LIGO/Virgo and NICER to further constrain the dense matter EoS.
