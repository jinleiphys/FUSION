# Pion Photo- and Electroproduction and the Chiral MAID Interface

> We discuss the extended on-mass-shell scheme for manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. We present a calculation of pion photo- and electroproduction up to and including order $q^4$. The low-energy constants have been fixed by fitting experimental data in all available reaction channels. Our results can be accessed via a web interface, the so-called chiral MAID (http://www.kph.uni-mainz.de/MAID/chiralmaid/).

## Key claim

The authors present a manifestly Lorentz-invariant (relativistic) baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation of pion photo- and electroproduction up to order \(q^4\), using the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme to restore power counting. The low-energy constants (LECs) are fixed by fitting experimental data across all available reaction channels, but the authors note that the approach has a limited energy range of applicability and that new experiments will lead to different LEC estimates.

## Method

The authors applied the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme to baryon chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) to compute the invariant amplitudes for pion photo- and electroproduction up to and including order \(q^4\). At \({\cal O}(q^3)\), the calculation involved 15 tree-level and 50 one-loop diagrams; at \({\cal O}(q^4)\), 20 tree-level and 85 one-loop diagrams were included. The loop contributions were evaluated numerically using MATHEMATICA with the FeynCalc and LoopTools packages, and the LECs were determined by fitting experimental data in all physical reaction channels. The numerical results are made available through a web interface called chiral MAID (\(\chi\)MAID), where users can modify the LECs of the contact interactions.

## Key numbers

- The calculation includes 19 LECs at orders \(q^3\) and \(q^4\): four independent LECs at \({\cal O}(q^3)\) and 15 additional LECs at \({\cal O}(q^4)\).
- For neutral pion photoproduction \(\gamma + p \rightarrow p + \pi^0\), the theory starts to deviate from experimental data for laboratory photon energies above \(E_\gamma^{\text{lab}} \approx 170\) MeV.
- For the charged channels, the estimated range of applicability is for total center-of-mass energies \(W > 1160\) MeV, beyond which the truncation of the multipole expansion becomes visible.
- Selected LEC values from the fit include: \(d_9 = -1.22 \pm 0.12\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) (isospin 0 channel), \(d_8 = -1.09 \pm 0.12\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) (isospin + channel), and \(d_{20} = 4.34 \pm 0.08\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) (isospin \(-\) channel).
- For electroproduction LECs, the combinations \(\tilde{e}_{52} = e_{52} + e_{72} = 6.4 \pm 0.7\ \text{GeV}^{-3}\) and \(\tilde{e}_{53} = e_{53} + e_{73} = -0.5 \pm 0.2\ \text{GeV}^{-3}\) are reported.

## Context

This work extends earlier heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) calculations by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1992) to a manifestly Lorentz-invariant framework using the EOMS scheme, which solves the power-counting problem that had previously plagued relativistic ChPT. The calculation provides a consistent description of all pion photo- and electroproduction channels in the threshold region, and the results are made publicly available via the chiral MAID web interface. This enables future studies of the range of validity of ChPT and allows for re-fitting of LECs as new experimental data become available, such as from the experiments by Chirapatpimol (2015) and Friscic (2015).
