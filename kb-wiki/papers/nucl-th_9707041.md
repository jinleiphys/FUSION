# Sum rules for isospin centroids in pick-up reactions on general multishell target states

> Sum Rules equations for pick-up reactions are presented for the first time for the energy centroids of states both for the isospin T_< (\equiv T_0 - 1 \over 2) and T_> (\equiv T_0 + {1 \over 2}) of the final nucleus when a nucleon is picked up from a general multishell target state with isospin T_0. These equations contain two-body correlation terms, <H^{01}>, which, at the present moment, are difficult to handle analytically. These terms are managed by combining these equations with the known stripping reactions equations. Sample applications of these equations to experimental data are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents, for the first time, explicit algebraic sum-rule equations for the isospin energy centroids of residual nucleus states produced by single-nucleon pick-up from a general multishell target state; these equations contain an isovector two-body correlation term that cannot be evaluated analytically, but the authors show it can be eliminated by combining the pick-up equations with previously known stripping equations for the same target.

## Method

The authors derived linear energy-weighted sum rules for single-nucleon pick-up reactions using the formalism of product space (angular momentum and isospin), assuming a pure multishell configuration for the target state with isospin T₀ = (N−Z)/2. They obtained separate expressions for the energy centroids of states with isospin T_> (T₀+½) and T_< (T₀−½), each containing the problematic isovector two-body correlation term ⟨H⁰¹⟩. To eliminate this term, they combined one pick-up equation with one stripping equation from their earlier work (Bansal and Kumar, 1989), then applied the combined equations to experimental data for reactions involving the 1f₇/₂ orbit with targets having ⁴⁰Ca as an inert core, extracting average effective two-body interaction parameters via least-squares fits.

## Key numbers

* For the 1f₇/₂ shell, the extracted average two-body interaction parameters are: $\overline{W}^{T=1}_{f_{7/2}f_{7/2}} = -0.212$ MeV and $\overline{W}^{T=0}_{f_{7/2}f_{7/2}} = -1.662$ MeV, with an rms deviation of 0.542 MeV.
* The previous calculation (Bansal and Kumar, 1989) gave $\overline{W}^{T=1} = -0.215$ MeV and $\overline{W}^{T=0} = -1.714$ MeV with rms deviation 0.191 MeV.
* The Kuo-Brown interaction (1968) gives $\overline{W}^{T=1} = -0.128$ MeV and $\overline{W}^{T=0} = -1.154$ MeV.
* Lips and McEllistren (1970) give $\overline{W}^{T=1} = -0.240$ MeV.
* Federman and Pittel (1970) give $\overline{W}^{T=1} = -0.228$ MeV.
* Schiffer and True (1976) give $\overline{W}^{T=0} = -1.739$ MeV (from a potential fit) and $-1.594$ MeV (from a direct fit to data).
* Example centroid values from Table 1 include: for ⁴¹Ca, the stripping (d,p) T_> centroid is 2.819 MeV and the pick-up (d,t) T_< centroid is 0.083 MeV; for ⁴⁶Ti, the stripping (d,p) T_> centroid is 0.555 MeV and the pick-up (p,d) T_> centroid is 4.760 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the sum-rule approach for isospin centroids from stripping reactions (Bansal and Kumar, 1989) to pick-up reactions on general multishell target states, completing the set of sum rules for direct single-nucleon transfer reactions. Previous calculations (Bansal and Kumar, 1989) were restricted to targets with only active neutrons, where the isovector correlation term equals the isoscalar correlation due to isospin constraints; the new equations remove this restriction. The method enables extraction of average effective two-body interaction parameters from combined stripping and pick-up data on the same target, and the authors state it can now be extended to much larger experimental datasets.
