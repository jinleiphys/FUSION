# The LPM effect in sequential bremsstrahlung: from large-N QCD to N=3 via the SU(N) analog of Wigner 6-j symbols

> Consider a high-energy parton showering as it traverses a QCD medium such as a quark-gluon plasma. Interference effects between successive splittings in the shower are potentially very important but have so far been calculated (even in idealized theoretical situations) only in soft emission or large-$N_{\rm c}$ limits, where $N_{\rm c}$ is the number of quark colors. In this paper, we show how one may remove the assumption of large $N_{\rm c}$ and so begin investigation of $N_{\rm c}{=}3$ without soft-emission approximations. Treating finite $N_{\rm c}$ requires (i) classifying different ways that four gluons can form a color singlet and (ii) calculating medium-induced transitions between those singlets, for which we find application of results for the generalization of Wigner 6-$j$ symbols from angular momentum to SU($N_{\rm c}$). Throughout, we make use of the multiple scattering ($\hat q$) approximation for high-energy partons crossing quark-gluon plasmas, and we find that this approximation is self-consistent only if the transverse-momentum diffusion parameter $\hat q$ for different color representations satisfies Casimir scaling (even for strongly-coupled, and not just weakly-coupled, quark-gluon plasmas). We also find that results for $N_{\rm c}{=}3$ depend, mathematically, on being able to calculate the propagator for a coupled non-relativistic quantum harmonic oscillator problem in which the spring constants are operators acting on a 5-dimensional Hilbert space of internal color states. Those spring constants are represented by constant $5{\times}5$ matrices, which we explicitly construct. We are unaware of any closed form solution for this type of harmonic oscillator problem, and we discuss prospects for using numerical evaluation.

## Key claim

The paper shows how to remove the large-\(N_{\rm c}\) assumption (where \(N_{\rm c}\) is the number of quark colors) from calculations of interference effects between successive splittings in a high-energy parton shower traversing a QCD medium, thereby enabling investigation of \(N_{\rm c}=3\) without soft-emission approximations, but the resulting problem involves a coupled non-relativistic quantum harmonic oscillator with \(5\times5\) matrix spring constants for which no closed-form solution is known.

## Method

The author classifies the eight different ways that four gluons can form a color singlet, then uses SU(\(N\)) generalizations of Wigner 6-\(j\) symbols to compute the unitary transformations between different basis choices (\(s\)-channel, \(t\)-channel, and \(u\)-channel) for these singlet states. These transformations are used to construct the \(5\times5\) matrix representation of the 4-body potential \(\underline{V}(\mathbf{b}_1,\mathbf{b}_2,\mathbf{b}_3,\mathbf{b}_4)\) in the multiple scattering (\(\hat q\)) approximation, which is then reduced via permutation symmetries to an effective 2-body harmonic oscillator Hamiltonian with matrix-valued spring constants.

## Key numbers

* The tensor product \(\mathbf{8}\otimes\mathbf{8} = \mathbf{1}_{\rm s} \oplus \mathbf{8}_{\rm a} \oplus \mathbf{8}_{\rm s} \oplus \mathbf{10}_{\rm a} \oplus \overline{\mathbf{10}}_{\rm a} \oplus \mathbf{27}_{\rm s}\) for SU(3) gluons, giving five distinct irreducible representations (with two distinct \(\mathbf{8}\) representations) for combining two gluons.
* There are eight independent color singlet states from four gluons, which reduce to a 5-dimensional subspace relevant for \(g \to ggg\) after applying permutation symmetries.
* The spring constants in the effective Hamiltonian are represented by constant \(5\times5\) symmetric matrices \(\underline{a}\), \(\underline{b}\), and \(\underline{c}\) that do not all commute.
* The overlap matrix \(\langle s_{\mathcal{R}} | t_{\mathcal{R}'} \rangle\) for SU(3) is given explicitly in an \(8\times8\) table, with entries such as \(\langle s_{\mathbf{1}} | t_{\mathbf{1}} \rangle = \frac18\) and \(\langle s_{\mathbf{27}} | t_{\mathbf{27}} \rangle = \frac{7}{40}\).

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier et al., 1990s; Zakharov, 1990s) for the LPM effect in QCD to the case of overlapping formation times for sequential splittings, which previously had only been treated in the soft bremsstrahlung limit (Blaizot, Iancu, Wu) or the large-\(N_{\rm c}\) limit (Arnold, 2010s). The paper shows that consistency of the \(\hat q\) approximation requires Casimir scaling (\(\hat q_R \propto C_R\)) even for strongly-coupled plasmas, generalizing a result previously known only for weakly-coupled plasmas (Simon, next-to-leading order). The explicit \(5\times5\) matrix potential enables future numerical evaluation of overlap effects for \(g \to ggg\) in \(N_{\rm c}=3\) QCD, though the author does not attempt such numerics in this paper.
