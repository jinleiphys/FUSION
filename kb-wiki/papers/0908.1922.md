# Super-soft symmetry energy encountering non-Newtonian gravity in neutron stars

> Considering the non-Newtonian gravity proposed in the grand unification theories, we show that the stability and observed global properties of neutron stars can not rule out the super-soft nuclear symmetry energies at supra-saturation densities. The degree of possible violation of the Inverse-Square-Law of gravity in neutron stars is estimated using an Equation of State (EOS) of neutron-rich nuclear matter consistent with the available terrestrial laboratory data.

## Key claim

The authors show that the stability and observed global properties of neutron stars cannot rule out super-soft nuclear symmetry energies at supra-saturation densities, provided that a non-Newtonian Yukawa term (violating the Inverse-Square-Law of gravity) is included in the equation of state; the super-soft symmetry energy alone would otherwise make neutron stars unstable.

## Method

The authors use the modified Gogny-Hartree-Fock (GHF) approximation to generate two symmetry energy parametrizations (MDIx1 and MDIx0) and add a Yukawa term to the gravitational potential, characterized by a coupling strength \(g^2/\mu^2\). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation for static neutron stars, using the MDIx1 super-soft symmetry energy (which becomes negative above \(3\rho_0\)) together with the Yukawa contribution, and compare the resulting mass-radius relations to the rotational constraint from pulsar J1748-2446ad (716 Hz) and the causality limit.

## Key numbers

- The MDIx1 symmetry energy becomes negative above \(3\rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0\) is the saturation density).
- With \(g^2/\mu^2 = 50\)–\(150\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (equivalently \(|\alpha|\lambda^2 = (2.6\)–\(7.8)\times 10^7\) m\(^2\)), neutron stars using the MDIx1 symmetry energy have a maximum mass between \(1.4\) and \(2.5\) M\(_\odot\) and a corresponding radius between \(12\) and \(18\) km.
- The rotational limit from pulsar J1748-2446ad (716 Hz) restricts \(g^2/\mu^2\) to less than \(150\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
- To produce a neutron star with a maximum mass above \(1.4\) M\(_\odot\), \(g^2/\mu^2\) must be higher than about \(50\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
- For a canonical \(1.4\) M\(_\odot\) neutron star, the MDIx0 symmetry energy without the Yukawa term gives a moment of inertia no more than \(1.8\times 10^{38}\) kg\(\cdot\)m\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the debate on the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy, where many models (e.g., UV14+TNI in Wiringa (1988) and group II in Stone (2003)) are soft but stable, while super-soft predictions (e.g., the original GHF prediction by Das (2003) and group III in Stone (2003)) were previously considered unstable or unphysical. The authors show that including a non-Newtonian Yukawa term, motivated by grand unification theories and the possible violation of the Inverse-Square-Law, can reconcile the super-soft symmetry energy preferred by FOPI/GSI pion data (Xiao (2009)) with neutron star stability. This enables future joint constraints on the symmetry energy and the strength of non-Newtonian gravity from neutron star mass, radius, and moment of inertia measurements.
