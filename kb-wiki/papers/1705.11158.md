# Universality in fermionic dimer-dimer scattering

> Collisions of two fermionic dimers near the unitary limit are studied using exact four-particle equations for transition operators in momentum space. Universal properties of dimer-dimer phase shifts and effective range expansion (ERE) parameters are determined. The inclusion of the fourth-order momentum term in the ERE significantly extends its validity to higher collision energies. The dimer-dimer scattering length and effective range are determined in the unitary limit as well as their corrections arising due to the finite range of the two-fermion interaction. These results are of considerably higher accuracy as compared to previous works, but confirm most of the previous results except for the lattice effective field theory calculations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the universal dimer-dimer scattering length and effective range in the unitary limit are \(a_{dd}/a = 0.5986 \pm 0.0005\) and \(r_{dd}/a = 0.133 \pm 0.002\), respectively, but these values are conditional on the inclusion of a fourth-order momentum term in the effective range expansion (ERE) to extend validity to higher collision energies, and the results confirm most prior work except for lattice effective field theory calculations.

## Method

The author solved exact four-particle Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for transition operators in momentum-space partial-wave representation, using three types of interaction models (separable Gaussian, modified CD Bonn, and modified LM2M2 potentials) to ensure universality. The equations were discretized with Gaussian quadratures and solved iteratively using double Pade summation, with the dimer-dimer pole treated by a subtraction method. Phase shifts were extracted from the on-shell scattering amplitude, and the effective range expansion parameters were determined by fitting the calculated \(ap_{dd}\cot\delta_0\) up to the fourth-order momentum term.

## Key numbers

- Dimer-dimer scattering length in the unitary limit: \(a_{dd}/a = 0.5986 \pm 0.0005\)
- Dimer-dimer effective range in the unitary limit: \(r_{dd}/a = 0.133 \pm 0.002\)
- Fourth-order ERE coefficient in the unitary limit: \(c_{dd} = 0.026 \pm 0.004\)
- Linear finite-range corrections: \(a_{dd}/a = 0.5986 + 0.105\,(r_e/a) \pm 0.0005\); \(r_{dd}/a = 0.133 + 0.51\,(r_e/a) \pm 0.002\); \(c_{dd} = 0.026 - 0.1\,(r_e/a) \pm 0.004\)
- Phase shift \(\delta_0\) changes by at most 0.005 deg when reducing partial-wave truncation from \(l_y,l_z \leq 4\) to \(l_y,l_z \leq 3\), and by 0.001 deg or less when increasing to \(l_y,l_z \leq 5\)
- Dimer breakup threshold corresponds to \(ap_{dd} \approx \sqrt{2}\)

## Context

This work extends the exact AGS methodology previously applied to bosonic four-body Efimov physics (Deltuva 2010, 2011) to fermionic dimer-dimer scattering, providing benchmark results that confirm prior correlated Gaussian, fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo, and hyperspherical adiabatic calculations (PhysRevA.77.043619, PhysRevA.79.030501) for the effective range. The results contradict the recent lattice effective field theory calculation (Elhatisari 2017) which claimed \(r_{dd}/a = -0.431(48)\), and the author notes this discrepancy is important for evaluating the reliability of lattice methods for scattering calculations in nuclear physics.
