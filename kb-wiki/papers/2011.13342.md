# Nucleon axial and pseudoscalar form factors from lattice QCD at the physical point

> We compute the nucleon axial and induced pseudoscalar form factors using three ensembles of gauge configurations, generated with dynamical light quarks with mass tuned to approximately their physical value. One of the ensembles also includes the strange and charm quarks with their mass close to physical. The latter ensemble has large statistics and finer lattice spacing and it is used to obtain final results, while the other two are used for assessing volume effects. The pseudoscalar form factor is also computed using these ensembles. We examine the momentum dependence of these form factors as well as relations based on pion pole dominance and the partially conserved axial-vector current hypothesis.

## Key claim

The authors compute the nucleon axial and induced pseudoscalar form factors from lattice QCD at the physical pion mass, using one ensemble with dynamical strange and charm quarks (Nf=2+1+1) as the primary source for final results, while two Nf=2 ensembles are used only to assess finite-volume effects. The abstract's claim of "physical point" is qualified by the fact that the Nf=2 ensembles have a slight extrapolation of the pion mass to match the physical value, whereas the Nf=2+1+1 ensemble has negligible deviation.

## Method

The authors extract the axial form factor G_A(Q^2) and induced pseudoscalar form factor G_P(Q^2) from three-point correlation functions of the isovector axial-vector current, computed on three ensembles of twisted-mass clover-improved fermion gauge configurations. They apply Gaussian smearing to the quark fields, use a fixed-sink method with sequential propagators, and employ three techniques to control excited-state contamination: the plateau method, the summation method, and two-state fits (allowing the first excited state to differ between two- and three-point functions in the M2 variant). The form factors are extracted via a singular value decomposition (SVD) that minimizes a chi-squared over multiple kinematic combinations, and the Q^2 dependence is fitted using both a dipole Ansatz and a z-expansion.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice QCD studies of nucleon axial form factors by the same collaboration (Alexandrou (2017)) and others (Jang (2019), Bali (2018), Bali (2019)), moving to simulations at the physical pion mass with both Nf=2 and Nf=2+1+1 ensembles. The authors examine the partially conserved axial-vector current (PCAC) relation and pion pole dominance (PPD) relations, which have shown discrepancies in prior work (Bali (2018) found violations of PPD; Jang (2019) found both PCAC and PPD satisfied when allowing different excited states in three-point functions). The results enable more reliable extraction of the axial charge g_A, the induced pseudoscalar coupling g_P*, and the pion-nucleon coupling g_piNN from lattice QCD, and provide a benchmark for experimental determinations from neutrino scattering and muon capture experiments.
