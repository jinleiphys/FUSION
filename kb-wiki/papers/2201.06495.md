# Single-pion contribution to the Gerasimov--Drell--Hearn sum rule and related integrals

> Phenomenological amplitudes obtained in partial-wave analyses (PWA) of single-pion photoproduction are used to evaluate the contribution of this process to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH), Baldin and Gell-Mann-Goldberger-Thirring (GGT) sum rules, by integrating up to 2 GeV in photon energy. Our study confirms that the single-pion contribution to all these sum rules converges even before the highest considered photon energy, but the levels of saturation are very different in the three cases. Single-pion production almost saturates the GDH sum rule for the proton, while a large fraction is missing in the neutron case. The Baldin integrals for the proton and the neutron are both saturated to about four fifths of the predicted total strength. For the GGT sum rule, the wide variability in predictions precludes any definitive statement.

## Key claim

Single-pion photoproduction almost saturates the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule for the proton but leaves a large fraction missing for the neutron, while the Baldin integrals for both nucleons are saturated to about four-fifths of the predicted total strength; for the Gell-Mann-Goldberger-Thirring (GGT) sum rule, wide variability in predictions precludes any definitive statement.

## Method

Phenomenological amplitudes from the SAID MA19 and MAID2007 partial-wave analyses (PWA) of single-pion photoproduction were used to evaluate the contributions of this process to the GDH, Baldin, and GGT sum rules by integrating up to 2 GeV in photon energy (SAID) or 1.67 GeV (MAID). The helicity-dependent cross-section difference Δσ and total cross-section σtot were constructed from the PWA amplitudes, and the running integrals were compared to the sum rule predictions based on known nucleon static properties and to experimental data from photoproduction and electroproduction experiments.

## Key numbers

- Proton GDH integral from SAID single-pion PWA: 183.4 μb, missing about 21 μb (approximately 10% of the predicted 204.784482(35) μb from the anomalous magnetic moment)
- Neutron GDH integral from SAID single-pion PWA: 129.5 μb, missing about 104 μb (approximately 44% of the predicted 232.25159(13) μb)
- Proton Baldin integral from SAID single-pion PWA: 11.47 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, missing about 2.7 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (approximately 19% of the PDG value 14.2 ± 0.5 × 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Neutron Baldin integral from SAID single-pion PWA: 13.01 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, missing about 2.5 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (approximately 16% of the PDG value 15.5 ± 1.6 × 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Proton GGT integral (γ₀) from SAID single-pion PWA: -1.00 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Neutron GGT integral (γ₀) from SAID single-pion PWA: -0.04 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Baldin integral evaluated from total photoabsorption data on the proton (188 MeV to 23.3 TeV): (13.1 ± 1.4) × 10⁻⁴ fm³

## Context

This work extends earlier single-pion evaluations by Arndt et al. (2005) and compares SAID and MAID PWA results to experimental data from the GDH Collaboration, A2 Collaboration at MAMI, CBELSA, and CLAS at JLab. The study enables quantification of the missing strength from multi-pion and eta-meson production channels, particularly highlighting the large neutron GDH deficit that calls for further measurements of Δσ and phenomenological studies beyond the resonance region, where Regge approaches by Bass et al. (2018) become applicable.
