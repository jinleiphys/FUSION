# Stopping power of hot QCD plasma

> The partonic energy loss has been calculated taking both the hard and soft contributions for all the $2 \to 2$ processes, revealing the importance of the individual channels. Cancellation of the intermediate separation scale has been exhibited. Subtleties related to the identical final state partons have properly been taken into account. The estimated collisional loss is compared with its radiative counter part. We show that there exists a critical energy ($E_c$) below which the collisional loss is more than its radiative counterpart. In addition, we present closed form formulas for both the collision probabilities and the stopping power ($dE/dx$)

# Stopping power of hot QCD plasma

## Key claim

The authors calculate the collisional energy loss of light partons in a hot QCD plasma by including all 2→2 processes (not just t-channel diagrams as in earlier work) and show that there exists a critical energy below which collisional loss exceeds radiative loss, approximately 85 GeV for quarks and 60 GeV for gluons under the specific conditions considered (T=400 MeV, N(E)=10, μ=1 GeV, α_s=0.3, L/λ=4).

## Method

The authors compute the differential collision probability Θ(E,E′)dE′ for each 2→2 parton scattering channel (qq→qq, qq′→qq′, qq̄→qq̄, qg→qg, qq̄→gg, gg→gg, gq→gq) using matrix elements from perturbative QCD, then integrate over energy transfer E′ to obtain dE/dx. For the infrared region, they employ hard thermal loop (HTL) resummed gluon propagators to handle screening, and explicitly demonstrate cancellation of the intermediate separation scale q* when soft and hard contributions are added. Subtleties from identical final-state partons are treated by restricting the integration range appropriately.

## Key numbers

- For a 20 GeV parton at T=250 MeV, dE/dx ≈ 0.8 GeV/fm (total collisional loss), compared to 0.2 GeV/fm from Bjorken (1982) and Braaten and Yuan (1991)
- Critical energy Ec ≈ 85 GeV for quarks and ≈ 60 GeV for gluons (at T=400 MeV, N(E)=10, μ=1 GeV, α_s=0.3, L/λ=4)
- For qg→qg scattering: (dE/dx) = (2πα_s²/3) T² [(22/9) ln(E/ω₀) − 0.176]
- For gg→gg scattering: (dE/dx) = 3πα_s² T² [ln(E/ω₀) − 0.038]
- For qq→qq scattering: (dE/dx) = (πα_s²/9) T² [ln(E/ω₀) + Δ ln 2 + 1.125] with Δ = −10/3
- Gluon rapidity density dN_g/dy ≈ 1000 used for RHIC energy estimates, giving T ≈ 400 MeV with formation time τ₀ = 0.5 fm/c
- Fractional energy loss ΔE/E ≈ 1/5 for L=2 fm at T=400 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier treatment of partonic energy loss by Bjorken (1982), who considered only t-channel processes and introduced an ad hoc infrared cutoff, and by Mrowczynski (1991) and Braaten and Yuan (1991), who developed the separation of soft and hard momentum transfer regimes. The authors include all 2→2 diagrams (including u-channel divergences and exchange terms) and properly treat identical final-state particles, which were overlooked in prior calculations. The results enable more accurate modeling of jet quenching in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, and provide closed-form formulas for collision probabilities and stopping power that can be used in transport simulations of parton propagation through quark-gluon plasma.
