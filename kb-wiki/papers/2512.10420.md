# Stationary Couette-type flows in relativistic fluids

> We investigate a class of stationary, planar-symmetric solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics, in which a dissipative fluid is confined between two parallel plates that move relative to each other and/or are maintained at different temperatures. We find that neglecting the heat flux leads to qualitatively incorrect flow profiles, even in systems with temperature-independent viscosity. This arises from the fact that, in special relativity, the heat flux itself contributes to the momentum density (the so-called "inertia of heat"). This effect is most evident in the Landau frame, where the fluid removes the excess energy generated by viscous heating by streaming across the boundaries. The analysis is further extended to the limit of vanishing chemical potential.

## Key claim

Neglecting heat flux in relativistic Couette flow leads to qualitatively incorrect velocity profiles, even when viscosity is temperature-independent, because the heat flux itself contributes to momentum density (the "inertia of heat"); this correction is universal in the sense that the resulting velocity profile does not depend on the exact value of thermal conductivity, provided viscosity is independent of temperature.

## Method

The authors solve the conservation laws for energy-momentum and particle number for a stationary, planar-symmetric relativistic fluid confined between two parallel plates moving at opposite velocities and held at fixed temperatures. They work in the Eckart frame (where the four-velocity is aligned with the particle current) and use Israel-Stewart-type relaxation equations as a regularization scheme, though the relaxation terms vanish under the assumed symmetries, effectively recovering Eckart's first-order theory. The resulting ordinary differential equations for the velocity and temperature profiles are solved analytically for the symmetric case (equal plate temperatures) and parametrically for the asymmetric case (unequal plate temperatures), assuming constant shear viscosity and thermal conductivity.

## Key numbers

- The exact velocity profile for equal plate temperatures is \( u(x) = \tan\left[\dfrac{2x}{L} \arctan\left(\dfrac{v}{\sqrt{1{-}v^2}} \right) \right] \), where \(v\) is the plate speed and \(L\) the plate separation.
- In the limit \(v \to 1\), the velocity profile approaches \( u(x) \to \tan\left( \frac{\pi x}{L} \right) \), remaining finite except at the boundaries.
- The ratio of perpendicular to parallel Landau-frame velocity components is \( \dfrac{u_L^x}{u_L^y} = \dfrac{2\eta}{L(\varepsilon{+}P)} \arctan\left(\dfrac{v}{\sqrt{1{-}v^2}}\right) \), which for \(v \to 1\) becomes \( \dfrac{\pi\eta}{L(\varepsilon{+}P)} \), equal to the Knudsen number \(\tau_R/L\) up to a numerical factor of order unity.
- For non-relativistic speeds (\(|v| \ll 1\)), this ratio scales as \( \dfrac{2\eta v}{L(\varepsilon{+}P)} \sim (\tau_R/L) \times c_s^2 v \).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Couette flow solution of Rogava (1996), who set heat conductivity to zero and obtained a linear velocity profile that violates energy conservation because viscous heating is not balanced by heat removal. It also generalizes the analysis of Granik (1996), who studied similar profiles within Eckart's first-order framework at finite chemical potential. The results enable systematic study of how heat flux modifies relativistic shear flows and clarify the differences between Eckart-frame and Landau-frame descriptions of dissipative fluids, with implications for modeling relativistic fluids in astrophysical and heavy-ion collision contexts.
