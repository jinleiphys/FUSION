# Measures of pion and kaon structure from generalised parton distributions

> Pion and kaon structural properties provide insights into the emergence of mass within the Standard Model and attendant modulations by the Higgs boson. Novel expressions of these effects, in impact parameter space and in mass and pressure profiles, are exposed via $π$ and $K$ generalised parton distributions, built using the overlap representation from light-front wave functions constrained by one-dimensional valence distribution functions that describe available data. Notably, e.g. $K$ pressure profiles are spatially more compact than $π$ profiles and both achieve near-core pressures of similar magnitude to that found in neutron stars.

## Key claim

Pion and kaon generalised parton distributions (GPDs) built from light-front wave functions constrained by one-dimensional valence distribution functions that describe available data expose novel expressions of emergent hadron mass and Higgs-boson modulation in impact parameter space and in mass and pressure profiles; notably, kaon pressure profiles are spatially more compact than pion profiles and both achieve near-core pressures of similar magnitude to that found in neutron stars.

## Method

The authors construct pion and kaon light-front wave functions (LFWFs) using two approaches: a perturbation theory integral representation (PTIR) with a spectral weight function, and a factorised (separable) Ansatz where the x-dependence is fixed by valence quark distribution functions from Cui et al. (2020) and the transverse momentum dependence is constrained by the meson charge radius. These LFWFs are then used in the overlap representation to compute the DGLAP-domain GPDs for both mesons, from which form factors, charge radii, and mass/pressure profiles are derived.

## Key numbers

- Pion charge radius from PTIR LFWF: r_pi ≈ 0.69 fm (experiment: 0.659(4) fm)
- Kaon charge radius from PTIR LFWF: r_K^2 = (0.56 fm)^2 (experiment: (0.560(31) fm)^2)
- Pion leptonic decay constant: f_pi = 0.092 GeV
- Kaon leptonic decay constant: f_K = 0.11 GeV
- Pion mass: m_pi = 0.14 GeV
- Kaon mass: m_K = 0.49 GeV
- Dressed u-quark mass scale: M_u = 0.31 GeV
- Dressed s-quark mass scale: M_s = 1.2 × M_u = 0.372 GeV
- Factorised kaon LFWF skewing parameter: d_K = 0.07

## Context

This work extends continuum Schwinger function method analyses of pion and kaon structure, building on prior calculations of distribution amplitudes and distribution functions by Cui et al. (2020) and Xu et al. (2018). It provides the first systematic comparison of PTIR and factorised LFWF approaches for meson GPDs, enabling three-dimensional imaging of pion and kaon structure that can be tested at new experimental facilities. The results enable future calculations of meson mass and pressure distributions and provide benchmarks for lattice QCD studies of meson GPDs.
