# Pinning down QCD-matter shear viscosity in A+A collisions via EbyE fluctuations using pQCD + saturation + hydrodynamics

> We compute the initial energy densities produced in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions from NLO perturbative QCD using a saturation conjecture to control soft particle production, and describe the subsequent space-time evolution of the system with hydrodynamics, event by event. The resulting centrality dependence of the low-$p_T$ observables from this pQCD + saturation + hydro ("EKRT") framework are then compared simultaneously to the LHC and RHIC measurements. With such an analysis we can test the initial state calculation, and constrain the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio $η/s$ of QCD matter. Using these constraints from the current RHIC and LHC measurements we then predict the charged hadron multiplicities and flow coefficients for the 5.023 TeV Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the EKRT framework (pQCD + saturation + hydrodynamics) can simultaneously describe low-transverse-momentum observables at both RHIC and LHC energies, and that this combined analysis constrains the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio \(\eta/s\) of QCD matter, but only under the condition that the model parameters are fixed at one collision energy and then tested at others.

## Method

The initial energy densities in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions are computed from NLO perturbative QCD using collinear factorization, with a saturation condition (the EKRT saturation conjecture) that determines a semi-hard saturation scale \(p_{\rm sat}\) locally in the transverse plane. Event-by-event fluctuations are introduced by sampling nucleon positions from Woods-Saxon density profiles and assigning Gaussian transverse densities to each nucleon. The subsequent space-time evolution is solved with 2+1 D dissipative fluid dynamics using the \(s95p\)-PCE-v1 equation of state, with the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio \(\eta/s(T)\) as the key free parameter, while heat conductivity and bulk viscosity are neglected.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale \(p_{\rm sat}\) is determined by the condition \(\frac{\mathrm{d}E_T}{\mathrm{d}^2\mathbf{r}} = \frac{K_{\rm sat}}{\pi}p_0^3\Delta y\), where \(K_{\rm sat}\) is a free parameter fixed separately for each \(\eta/s(T)\) parametrization using the charged hadron multiplicity in \(0-5\%\) most central 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions.
- The width of the Gaussian transverse density around each nucleon is \(\sigma = 0.43\) fm, obtained from \(J/\psi\) electroproduction measurements.
- The hydrodynamical evolution starts at a common time \(\tau_0 = 1/p_{\rm sat}^{\rm min} = 0.2\) fm, with the minimum saturation scale set to \(p_{\rm sat}^{\rm min} = 1\) GeV at the edges of the system.
- Kinetic freeze-out occurs at \(T_{\rm dec} = 100\) MeV, and chemical decoupling at \(T_{\rm chem} = 175\) MeV.
- The parameter \(\beta\) controlling the minimum \(E_T\) in the measurement functions is set to \(\beta = 0.8\).

## Context

This work extends the EKRT framework (Eskola 1999, Paatelainen 2012) by including event-by-event fluctuations and a systematic comparison of multiple \(\eta/s(T)\) parametrizations against both RHIC and LHC data. It enables predictions for 5.023 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, including charged hadron multiplicities and flow coefficients. The analysis builds on prior work by Denicol (2012) and Molnar (2013) for second-order dissipative hydrodynamics, and by Huovinen (2009) for the equation of state.
