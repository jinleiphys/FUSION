# Enhancement of the electron electric dipole moment in gadolinium garnets

> Effects caused by the electron electric dipole moment (EDM) in gadolinium garnets are considered. Experimental studies of these effects could improve current upper limit on the electron EDM by several orders of magnitude. We suggest a consistent theoretical model and perform calculations of observable effects in gadolinium gallium garnet and gadolinium iron garnet. Our calculation accounts for both direct and exchange diagrams.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the electron electric dipole moment (EDM) can be probed in gadolinium garnets with sensitivity several orders of magnitude better than current limits, but the final observable effects are larger than a prior calculation (Simon 2003) by a factor of 3, mainly due to a different lattice elastic constant rather than the electronic part, which accidentally gave similar results despite including previously neglected exchange diagrams.

## Method

The authors developed a "jelly model" for the electronic structure of GdO8 clusters in gadolinium gallium garnet (GGG) and gadolinium iron garnet (GdIG), replacing the eight surrounding oxygen ions with a spherically symmetric attractive potential. They solved the Dirac equation with this combined potential and calculated the T,P-odd energy correction in third-order perturbation theory, accounting for both direct and exchange diagrams (15 diagrams total) involving the electron EDM operator, lattice deformation operator, and residual Coulomb interaction. The lattice elastic constant was estimated from infrared spectroscopy data on garnet vibrational modes rather than from the dielectric constant used in prior work.

## Key numbers

- Current upper limit on electron EDM: \(d_e < 1.6 \times 10^{-27} e\cdot cm\) (from thallium beam experiment, Commins 1995)
- Induced voltage across a 10 cm magnetically polarized GdIG sample at current EDM limit: \(\Delta V = 1.1 \times 10^{-9} V\)
- Spin-dependent energy shift per Gd ion in GGG under external electric field \(E = 10 kV/cm\) at current EDM limit: \(\Delta \epsilon = 5.7 \times 10^{-22} eV\)
- Gd number density in GdIG: \(n_{Gd} = 1.235 \times 10^{22} cm^{-3}\)
- Effective elastic constant per Gd ion: \(K = 0.095 E_0 / a_B^2\) (where \(E_0 = 27.2 eV\))
- Effective vibrational frequency for Gd motion: \(\omega = 180 cm^{-1}\) (average of N mode at \(213 cm^{-1}\) and O mode at \(147 cm^{-1}\))
- Reduced mass for Gd motion in GdIG: \(\mu = 77\) (atomic units)
- Gd-O distance: \(r_o = 4.53 a_B\)
- 5d-4f splitting in garnet environment: \(\Delta E_{fd} \approx 66 \times 10^3 cm^{-1}\)

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Simon (2003) on T,P-odd effects in GGG and GdIG, which treated the Gd ion as having an effective Schiff moment and neglected exchange diagrams. The present calculation finds that exchange diagrams are important but accidentally cancel to give a similar electronic effective Hamiltonian. The main improvement comes from using infrared spectroscopy data (from references ir1 and ir2) to determine the lattice elastic constant, rather than the dielectric constant estimate used by Simon (2003). The results enable experimental searches for the electron EDM in solid-state systems as proposed by Lamoreaux (2002) and Hunter (2002), with predicted signals three times larger than previously estimated.
