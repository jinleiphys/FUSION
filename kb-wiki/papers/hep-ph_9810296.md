# Effect of Color Screening on Heavy Quarkonia Regge Trajectories

> Using an unquenched lattice potential to calculate the spectrum of the bottomonium system, we demonstrate numerically that the effect of pair creation is to produce termination of hadronic Regge trajectories, in contrast to the Veneziano model and the vast majority of phenomenological generalizations. Termination of Regge trajectories may have significant experimental consequences.

## Key claim
Using an unquenched lattice potential to calculate the bottomonium spectrum, the authors demonstrate numerically that the effect of pair creation (color screening) produces termination of hadronic Regge trajectories, in contrast to the Veneziano model and most phenomenological generalizations. The termination occurs at a finite maximum angular momentum, rather than the trajectories continuing indefinitely.

## Method
The authors computed the spectrum of the bottomonium system using an unquenched lattice potential that incorporates color screening effects from dynamical quark pair creation. They then constructed Regge trajectories (plots of angular momentum versus squared mass) for various values of the screening parameter mu, and determined the maximum angular momentum at which bound states exist for each trajectory.

## Key numbers
- For the screening parameter mu = 0.18 GeV, the Regge trajectory terminates at approximately M^2 = 130 GeV^2 (from Fig. 1 axis labels).
- For mu = 0.22 GeV, the trajectory terminates at approximately M^2 = 128 GeV^2.
- For mu = 0.28 GeV, the trajectory terminates at approximately M^2 = 121 GeV^2.
- The maximum angular momentum L_max decreases as the screening parameter mu increases, with L_max approximately 10 for mu = 0.18 GeV, approximately 8 for mu = 0.22 GeV, and approximately 5 for mu = 0.28 GeV (from Fig. 2).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of Regge trajectories in heavy quarkonia by incorporating unquenched lattice potentials, which include the effects of dynamical quark pair creation (color screening). The results contradict the Veneziano model and its phenomenological generalizations, which predict linear Regge trajectories continuing indefinitely. The finding that Regge trajectories terminate at finite angular momentum has potential experimental consequences for the search for high-spin heavy quarkonium states, and provides a numerical benchmark for models of color screening in nonperturbative QCD.
