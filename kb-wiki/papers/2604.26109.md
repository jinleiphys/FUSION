# Formation of gaseous, doubly charged cerium monofluoride CeF$^{2+}$ and its sensitivity to new physics

> Tricationic protactinium monofluoride ($^{229}$PaF$^{3+}$) has been proposed as a candidate for probing physics beyond the Standard Model of particle physics. Since studies with $^{229}$PaF$^{3+}$ require significant experimental advances, we exploit the stable, valence-isoelectronic dicationic cerium monofluoride (CeF$^{2+}$) as a surrogate. Gas-phase fluorinated-cerium molecular ions are formed and identified using the Off-Line Ion Source and TITAN mass measurement facilities at TRIUMF. Quantum chemical calculations are performed on the electronic structure of CeF$^{2+}$, revealing a parallel to that of $^{229}$PaF$^{3+}$. Moreover, these calculations provide estimates on the sensitivity of CeF$^{2+}$ itself to various $\mathcal{P,T}$-odd properties. A brief discourse on the specifics of the quantum control of CeF$^{2+}$ is presented which anticipates future searches for symmetry violations.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that gaseous CeF$^{2+}$ can be formed at a rare isotope facility using stable cerium, serving as a valence-isoelectronic surrogate for the radioactive $^{229}$PaF$^{3+}$ candidate; the paper explicitly hedges that while CeF$^{2+}$ has its own sensitivity to parity- and time-reversal-violating physics, studies with $^{229}$PaF$^{3+}$ require significant experimental advances that have not yet been achieved.

## Method

The authors produced beams of $^{140}$Ce$^{+}$ and $^{140}$Ce$^{3+}$ via electron cyclotron resonance ionization at the Off-Line Ion Source at TRIUMF, then injected these ions into a radio-frequency quadrupole cooler-buncher where sulfur hexafluoride was added to the helium buffer gas to form cerium fluoride molecules. The resulting molecular ions were identified by mass-over-charge ratio using a multiple-reflection time-of-flight mass spectrometer with resolving powers exceeding $10^5$, supported by quantum chemical calculations of the electronic structure of CeF$^{2+}$.

## Key numbers

- $^{140}$Ce$^{+}$ beams of over $5 \times 10^8$ particles per second and $^{140}$Ce$^{3+}$ beams of $1.5 \times 10^8$ particles per second were delivered from the ion source for molecule formation experiments.
- The MR-TOF system achieved mass resolving powers up to $R = 5 \times 10^5$ at 250-500 turns, and a resolving power of 296,000 was used to identify $^{140}$CeF$^{2+}$.
- The typical rate of MR-TOF mass separated CeF$^{2+}$ was approximately 350 ions per second, corresponding to an efficiency of at least $3 \times 10^{-6}$ relative to the number of Ce$^{3+}$ ions injected into the RFQcb.
- The RFQcb was held at a pressure of approximately $10^{-1}$ mbar with a partial pressure of SF$_6$ of approximately $10^{-3}$ mbar.
- The formation period in the RFQcb was typically 5-25 ms, with loading times of approximately 500 $\mu$s.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Zulch et al. (2022) that $^{229}$PaF$^{3+}$ is a candidate for probing nuclear Schiff moments, by demonstrating that the valence-isoelectronic stable molecule CeF$^{2+}$ can be formed using instrumentation available at radioactive ion beam facilities. The formation method builds on molecular synthesis in gas-filled RFQ coolers previously applied by Au et al. (2023) and others, and the results enable future precision spectroscopy of CeF$^{2+}$ to constrain $\mathcal{P,T}$-odd parameters in global analyses such as that of Gaul (2024), as well as providing a pathway toward forming $^{229}$PaF$^{3+}$ once protactinium beams become available.
