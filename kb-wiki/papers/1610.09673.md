# Longitudinal correlations in the initial stages of ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions

> In this talk we review some of our results for the longitudinal correlations in connection with recent experimental data, in particular for the {\em torque} effect and for the two-particle correlations in pseudorapidity, $C(η_1,η_2)$. The model framework involves event-by-event fluctuations of the initial conditions, followed with 3D viscous hydrodynamic evolution and statistical hadronization.

## Key claim

The paper argues that longitudinal decorrelations (the "torque effect") in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions arise from event-by-event fluctuations in the number of forward- and backward-going participants combined with asymmetric emission profiles in rapidity, and that for p-Pb collisions an additional mechanism of fluctuating string endpoints is necessary to reproduce CMS data.

## Method

The authors use event-by-event simulations with GLISSANDO for initial conditions, followed by 3D viscous hydrodynamic evolution and statistical hadronization with THERMINATOR. They compute the 3-bin correlator r_n(η^a, η^b) proposed by the CMS Collaboration and the two-particle correlation function C(η_1, η_2) to compare with experimental data from Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, the torque effect is significantly larger for triangular flow (n=3) than for elliptic flow (n=2), as shown in the r_n(η^a, η^b) plots.
- From matching a_11^exp = a_11^mod, the authors obtain N_ch = 4.7 N_+, where N_+ is the total number of sources.
- In the CMS acceptance range Δη = 4.8, this gives dN_ch/dη ≈ 1 × N_+.
- For comparison, from multiplicity data dN_ch/dη ≈ (3-4) × N_W (number of wounded nucleons) and dN_ch/dη ≈ 1.3 × Q_W (number of wounded quarks).

## Context

This work extends the wounded-nucleon model of Bialas (1976) and the torque effect originally proposed by Bozek (2010) to include 3D viscous hydrodynamics and comparisons with recent CMS data (Khachatryan 2015) and ATLAS measurements (2015). The results enable quantitative extraction of the number of constituent hot-spots in the nucleon from longitudinal correlation data, suggesting a wounded-quark picture with approximately 3 constituents per nucleon.
