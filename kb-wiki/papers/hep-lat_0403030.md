# In Search of the Chiral Regime

> A critical appraisal is given of a recent analysis of the quark-mass and finite-size dependence of unquenched lattice QCD data for the nucleon mass. We use this forum to estimate the boundary of the chiral regime for nucleon properties.

## Key claim

The chiral expansion for the nucleon mass, when fitted to unquenched lattice QCD data at pion masses above 500 MeV, carries a 50–80% error from neglected higher-order terms at the lowest simulated pion mass, meaning the data cannot yet be reliably extrapolated to predict nucleon properties with controlled uncertainties.

## Method

The author fits the nucleon mass from baryon chiral perturbation theory (χPT) at O(m_q²) to unquenched lattice QCD data from several collaborations (Khan 2003, UKQCD, CP-PACS, JLQCD) over a wide range of pion masses, using both infrared regularization and dimensional regularization with MS-bar. The error from omitted higher-order terms is estimated by varying the chiral-limit values of the axial coupling g_A and the pion decay constant f over their experimentally allowed ranges, as determined from πN→ππN analyses and Kℓ3 decays.

## Key numbers

- The spread of fit curves suggests a **50% error** (Fit 1) or **80% error** (Fit 2) associated with neglected higher orders at the lattice point with the lowest pion mass.
- If one tolerates a **20% error** from neglected higher-order terms, the chiral regime boundary is at **m_π ≲ 300 MeV** (conservative estimate, dashed curves) or **m_π ≲ 400 MeV** (less conservative, solid curves).
- The physical pion mass is indicated by a star in the error plot; the chiral expansion converges nicely near that point.
- Fit parameters for infrared regularization (Fit 1): M₀ = 0.880 GeV, c₁ = -0.95 GeV⁻¹, e₁ = 2.44 GeV⁻³ (at μ=1 GeV), with f=131 MeV and g_A=1.267 fixed.
- Fit parameters for infrared regularization (Fit 2): M₀ = 0.872 GeV, c₁ = -1.22 GeV⁻¹, e₁ = 4.90 GeV⁻³, with f=124 MeV and g_A=1.520 fixed.
- The experimental/theoretical uncertainty in the chiral-limit axial coupling gives g_A⁽⁰⁾ = 1.10–1.52, based on the range -2.61 GeV⁻² < d̅₁₆ < 2.43 GeV⁻².

## Context

This work critically appraises the analysis of Procura (2003) and Khan (2003), who fitted the nucleon mass with baryon χPT at O(m_q²) and claimed successful prediction of finite-size dependence. Beane argues those fits lack a proper error estimate and shows that varying the chiral-limit values of g_A and f (as constrained by πN→ππN analyses from Fettes (1999) and a Δ-inclusive analysis from Thomas and Wolf) yields large uncertainties. The result extends the discussion of Bernard (2003), who found a chiral regime boundary at m_π ≲ 500 MeV by comparing different orders in the expansion. The paper concludes that definitive determination of the chiral regime boundary must await smaller lattice quark masses and better determination of the low-energy constants d̅₁₆ and l̅₄.
