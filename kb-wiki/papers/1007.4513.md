# Puzzles of J/Psi production off nuclei

> Nuclear effects for J/Psi production in pA collisions are controlled by the coherence and color transparency effects. Color transparency onsets when the time of formation of the charmonium wave function becomes longer than the inter-nucleon spacing. In this energy regime the effective break-up cross section for a c-cbar dipole depends on energy and nuclear path length, and agrees well with data from fixed target experiments, both in magnitude and energy dependence. At higher energies of RHIC and LHC coherence in c-cbar pair production leads to charm quark shadowing which is a complement to the high twist break up cross section. These two effects explain well with no adjusted parameters the magnitude and rapidity dependence of nuclear suppression of J/Psi observed at RHIC in dAu collisions, while the contribution of leading twist gluon shadowing is found to be vanishingly small. A novel mechanism of double color filtering for c-cbar dipoles makes nuclei significantly more transparent in AA compared to pA collisions. This is one of the mechanisms which make impossible a model independent "data driven" extrapolation from pA to AA. This effect also explains the enhancement of nuclear suppression observed at forward rapidities in AA collisions at RHIC, what hardly can be related to the produced dense medium. J/Psi is found to be a clean and sensitive tool measuring the transport coefficient characterizing the dense matter created in AA collisions. RHIC data for pT dependence of J/Psi production in nuclear collisions are well explained with the low value of the transport coefficient q_0-hat<0.5 GeV^2/fm.

# Puzzles of J/Psi production off nuclei

## Key claim

Nuclear effects for J/Psi production in pA collisions are controlled by coherence and color transparency, with the effective break-up cross section for a c-cbar dipole depending on energy and nuclear path length; the paper claims that these effects explain RHIC and LHC data with no adjusted parameters, while leading twist gluon shadowing contributes vanishingly little, and a novel double color filtering mechanism makes nuclei significantly more transparent in AA compared to pA collisions.

## Method

The author develops a model based on the uncertainty principle for the transverse expansion of a c-cbar dipole moving through nuclear matter, calculating the mean break-up cross section as a function of path length L and dipole energy E_ccbar. For high energies where the charm production time becomes long, the production amplitude is averaged over dipole size using a Gaussian size distribution, leading to a nuclear ratio expression that accounts for coherence effects. The survival probability in AA collisions is computed by integrating over impact parameter with a double color filtering formalism, and the transport coefficient q_hat is extracted by fitting the final-state interaction contribution to RHIC data.

## Key numbers

- Initial c-cbar dipole separation: r_ccbar ~ 1/m_c ≈ 0.1 fm; final J/Psi mean size: r_J ≈ 0.4 fm
- Break-up cross section factor C(E_ccbar) at x_F=0: C = 2.89 for NA60 (CERN SPS) energies, C = 3.14 for E866 (Fermilab) energies
- Coherence time for charm production: t_c ~ 2E/M_J^2; coherence becomes significant when E ≳ 25 GeV × L(fm)
- Mean squared dipole size used in Gaussian approximation: ⟨r_T^2⟩ = 6/m_c^2 = 0.1 fm^2
- At sqrt(s)=200 GeV, x_2 values range from 0.024 to 0.0033 within rapidity interval 0 < y < 2
- At LHC energy sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV and y=0, gluon shadowing is only 3% (x_2 = 5.5×10^{-3})
- Transport coefficient extracted from RHIC data: q_hat_0 ≈ 0.3–0.5 GeV^2/fm
- J/Psi formation time in medium: t_f < 0.5 fm
- Boosted saturation scale increases break-up cross section by factor 1.5 at RHIC and factor 3 at LHC

## Context

This work extends the color transparency and coherence framework developed by Kopeliovich and collaborators (KZ91, KST2) for vector meson photoproduction on nuclei, and applies it to J/Psi production in hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions. The author directly contradicts the interpretation of PHENIX data (Phenix-dA, 2007) which attributed nuclear suppression entirely to gluon shadowing using the nDSg parameterization; the paper argues that nDSg and EKS shadowing are unreliable because they enforce large shadowing at small x by hand. The analysis also challenges the "data driven" extrapolation from pA to AA proposed by Cassagnac (2008), showing that double color filtering and boosted saturation scale make such extrapolation model-dependent. The extracted transport coefficient q_hat_0 ≈ 0.3–0.5 GeV^2/fm is more than an order of magnitude smaller than values from jet quenching data (Phenix-theory), suggesting J/Psi production may provide a cleaner probe of medium density.
