# Probing the Weinberg Operator at Colliders

> Motivated by searches for $0νββ$ decay in nuclear experiments and collider probes of lepton number violation at dimension $d\geq7$, we investigate the sensitivity to the $d=5$ Weinberg operator using the non-resonant signature $pp\to \ell^\pm \ell'^{\pm} j j$ at the LHC. We develop a prescription for the operator that is applicable in collisions and decays, and focus on the $\ell\ell'=μμ$ channel, which is beyond the reach of nuclear decays. For a Wilson coefficient $C^{μμ}_5=1$, scales as heavy as $Λ\sim 8.3~(11)$~TeV can be probed with $\mathcal{L}=300~{\rm fb}^{-1}~(3~{\rm ab}^{-1})$. This translates to an effective $μμ$ Majorana mass of $\vert m_{μμ}\vert\sim7.3~(5.4)$~GeV, and establishes a road map for testing the Weinberg operator at accelerators.

## Key claim

The LHC can probe the dimension-5 Weinberg operator via same-sign $W^\pm W^\pm$ scattering into muon pairs, reaching sensitivity to effective Majorana masses of order GeV, but only under the condition that the expansion parameter $v^2/\Lambda$ remains small compared to the virtuality of the internal neutrino current, requiring $\Lambda \gg 200$ TeV for physically meaningful cross sections.

## Method

The authors model the Weinberg operator as an unphysical Majorana neutrino $N$ with mass $m_{\ell\ell'} = C_5^{\ell\ell'} v^2/\Lambda$ that couples to $W$ bosons and charged leptons via the Lagrangian of Eq. (4), implementing this in FeynRules to produce the SMWeinberg UFO library. They simulate the $pp \to \ell^\pm \ell'^{\pm} jj$ process at NLO in QCD using MadGraph5_aMC@NLO with Pythia8 for parton showering and Delphes for ATLAS-like detector simulation, focusing on the $\mu\mu$ channel with a signal region defined by same-sign dimuons, high dijet invariant mass, low missing transverse momentum, and a cut on $H_T/p_T^{\mu_1}$.

## Key numbers

- For a Wilson coefficient $C_5^{\mu\mu}=1$, scales as heavy as $\Lambda \sim 8.3$ TeV can be probed at 95% CL with $\mathcal{L}=300$ fb$^{-1}$ at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV.
- For $\mathcal{L}=3$ ab$^{-1}$ at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV, the sensitivity reaches $\Lambda \sim 11$ TeV at 95% CL.
- These correspond to effective $\mu\mu$ Majorana masses of $|m_{\mu\mu}| \sim 7.3$ GeV (300 fb$^{-1}$) and $5.4$ GeV (3 ab$^{-1}$).
- At $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV and $\Lambda=10$ TeV (corresponding to $m_{\mu\mu}\sim6$ GeV), the NLO cross section is $\sigma\sim0.14$ fb.
- At $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV, the rate reaches $\sigma\sim1$ ab at $\Lambda\sim120$ TeV ($m_{\mu\mu}\sim500$ MeV).
- For a $\sqrt{s}=100$ TeV proton collider with $\mathcal{L}=30$ ab$^{-1}$, sensitivity reaches $\Lambda/|C_5^{\mu\mu}| \lesssim 48$ TeV at 95% CL.
- The signal efficiency after all cuts is $\varepsilon\sim12\%$ for $\Lambda=200$ TeV, with signal rate $\sigma\sim42$ zb and total background $\sigma\sim25$ ab.

## Context

This work extends collider probes of lepton number violation from dimension $d\geq7$ operators (studied by Dicus (1991), Datta (1993), Aoki (2020), and Fuks (2020)) down to the dimension-5 Weinberg operator, which is the minimal EFT description of Majorana neutrino masses. The approach overcomes the flavor limitations of nuclear $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay experiments, which are only sensitive to the electron-flavor effective mass $|m_{ee}|$, by enabling sensitivity to muon-flavored Majorana masses that are kinematically forbidden in nuclear decays. The results establish a road map for testing the Weinberg operator at accelerators, with sensitivity that exceeds representative searches at $B$- and $K$-meson factories such as LHCb and NA-62.
