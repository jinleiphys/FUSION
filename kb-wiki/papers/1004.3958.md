# An analytic parametrization of the hyperonic matter equation of state

> An analytic parametrization of the hyperonic matter equation of state based on microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations has been constructed using the realistic Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential plus a three-body force of Urbana type, and three models of the hyperon-nucleon interaction: the Nijmegen soft-core models NSC89 and NSC97e, and the most recent meson-exchange potential of the Juelich group. The construction of this parametrization is based on a simple phase-space analysis and reproduces with good accuracy the results of the microscopic calculations with a small number of parameters. This parametrization allows for rapid calculations that accurately mimic the microscopic results, being therefore, very useful from a practical point of view.

# An analytic parametrization of the hyperonic matter equation of state

## Key claim

The authors construct an analytic parametrization of the hyperonic matter equation of state that reproduces microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results with deviations of at most 2% to 10-11% depending on the hyperon-nucleon interaction model used, but only for densities up to 0.5 fm^-3 and for compositions where the hyperon fraction, isospin asymmetry, and asymmetry between Lambda and Sigma- hyperons remain small enough that linear approximations hold.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations of hyperonic matter using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential plus a three-nucleon force of Urbana type, combined with three different hyperon-nucleon interaction models: the Nijmegen soft-core models NSC89 and NSC97e, and the Julich meson-exchange potential. They derived a functional form for the correlation energy per particle based on a phase-space analysis of the single-particle potentials, expressing it as a sum of five density-dependent coefficients V_i(rho) fitted to reproduce BHF results for five specific sets of hyperon fraction Y, isospin asymmetry beta, and Lambda/Sigma- asymmetry alpha. Each coefficient V_i(rho) was parametrized as a rho^gamma + b rho^delta.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density of the model: rho_0 = 0.175 fm^-3.
* Density range of the parametrization: 0.01 fm^-3 < rho < 0.5 fm^-3.
* Maximum deviation from microscopic BHF results: 2% for the NSC97 model, 5-6% for the Julich model, and 10-11% for the NSC89 model at the highest densities explored.
* The parametrization uses five coefficients V_1 through V_5, each fitted with two power-law terms (a rho^gamma + b rho^delta), with parameters given in Tables 1-3 of the paper.

## Context

This work extends microscopic BHF calculations of hyperonic matter by Schulze (1995), Baldo (2000), and Vidana (2000) into a practical analytic form, contrasting with phenomenological approaches such as the Skyrme-type effective EoS of Balberg and Gal (1997) and relativistic mean field theory treatments by Glendenning (1985) and others. The parametrization enables rapid calculations of the hyperonic matter equation of state for applications to neutron star structure, and the authors state it will be extended in future work to include finite temperature effects needed for newborn neutron stars and relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
