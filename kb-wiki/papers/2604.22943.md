# Amplitude-Based Analysis of QED Radiative Corrections to Electroproduction of $η$-Mesons on Protons

> A formalism for radiative correction calculations in exclusive $η$ electroproduction on the proton is presented, extending the treatment developed for the pion channel. The EXCLURAD code is used in the radiative correction procedure with EtaMAID-2023 multipole amplitudes. The cross-section correction factor $δ$ varies by up to ${\sim}\,30\%$ across the resonance region $W = 1.49$-$2.0$ GeV at $E_{\rm beam} = 6.535$ GeV, with a local maximum near $W \simeq 1.66$ GeV driven by the $S_{11}(1535)$ and $S_{11}(1650)$ resonances. The beam-spin asymmetry is suppressed by 15-25% at the same kinematics. Numerical results covering $Q^2 = 0.3$-$4.0$ GeV$^2$ and the full angular range are provided for kinematics relevant to CLAS12 experiments at Jefferson Lab.

# Amplitude-Based Analysis of QED Radiative Corrections to Electroproduction of η-Mesons on Protons

## Key claim
The cross-section correction factor δ varies by up to ~30% across the resonance region W = 1.49–2.0 GeV at Ebeam = 6.535 GeV, with a local maximum near W ≃ 1.66 GeV driven by the S11(1535) and S11(1650) resonances; the beam-spin asymmetry is suppressed by 15–25% at the same kinematics, but these results depend on the specific hadronic model (EtaMAID-2023) and the chosen exclusivity cut vcut = 0.166 GeV².

## Method
The EXCLURAD code, originally developed for pion electroproduction, was extended to the η channel by setting the outgoing meson mass to mη = 0.54786 GeV and replacing the hadronic model input with EtaMAID-2023 multipole amplitude tables covering W from threshold to 2.0 GeV and Q² up to 5 GeV². The code computes O(α) QED radiative corrections using the Bardin-Shumeiko covariant IR-cancellation scheme, including soft-photon exponentiation, vertex and vacuum-polarization corrections, and a three-dimensional hard-bremsstrahlung integral over the inelasticity variable v, the photon polar angle cosθγ, and the azimuthal angle φγ.

## Key numbers
- Cross-section correction factor δ varies by up to ~30% across the resonance region W = 1.49–2.0 GeV at Ebeam = 6.535 GeV
- Local maximum in δ near W ≃ 1.66 GeV, driven by the S11(1535) and S11(1650) resonances
- Beam-spin asymmetry suppression factor RA = 0.75–0.85 (15–25% suppression) at the same kinematics
- Numerical results cover Q² = 0.3–4.0 GeV² and the full angular range for CLAS12 kinematics at Ebeam = 6.535 GeV
- The leading-logarithm approximation deviates from the exact result by 2.7–8.8% while running approximately 600 times faster
- The exclusivity cut vcut = 0.166 GeV² corresponds to the π⁰ production threshold in the missing mass
- The η production threshold is Wth = mp + mη ≈ 1.486 GeV, lying only ~49 MeV below the S11(1535) peak
- The EtaMAID-2023 multipole tables cover W = 1.4856–2.000 GeV in 5 MeV steps and Q² = 0.00–5.01 GeV² in 0.01 GeV² steps

## Context
This work extends the radiative correction formalism developed for exclusive pion electroproduction by Afanasev et al. (2002) to the η channel, using the same EXCLURAD code framework. The η channel provides a cleaner probe of N* resonances than pion channels because the η meson carries isospin I=0, so only I=1/2 nucleon resonances contribute, excluding all Δ states (I=3/2) that appear in pion electroproduction. The results enable interpretation of CLAS12 η-electroproduction measurements at Jefferson Lab, with the publicly available dataset of ~265,000 kinematic points supporting future analyses of nucleon resonance structure.
