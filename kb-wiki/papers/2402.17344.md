# Impact of strong magnetic field, baryon chemical potential, and medium anisotropy on polarization and spin alignment of hadrons

> The recent observation of global spin polarization of $Λ$ ($\barΛ$) hyperons and the spin alignment of $ϕ$ and $K^{*0}$ vector mesons create remarkable interest in investigating the particle polarization in the relativistic fluid produced in heavy-ion collisions at GeV/TeV energies. Among other sources of spin polarization phenomena, the Debye mass of a medium plays a crucial role in particle polarization. Any modification brought to the effective mass due to the temperature, strong magnetic field ($eB$), baryonic chemical potential ($μ_{B}$), medium anisotropy ($ξ$), and vorticity, etc., certainly affects the particle spin polarization. In this work, we explore the global hyperon spin polarization and the spin alignment of vector mesons corresponding to the strong magnetic field, baryonic chemical potential, and medium anisotropy. We find that the degree of spin polarization is flavor-dependent for hyperons. Meanwhile, vector meson spin alignment depends on the hadronization mechanisms of initially polarized quarks and anti-quarks. Medium anisotropy significantly changes the degree of spin polarization compared to the magnetic field and baryon chemical potential.

## Key claim

The degree of spin polarization is flavor-dependent for hyperons, and vector meson spin alignment depends on the hadronization mechanisms of initially polarized quarks and anti-quarks; medium anisotropy significantly changes the degree of spin polarization compared to the magnetic field and baryon chemical potential.

## Method

The authors computed global quark polarization from parton elastic scattering within an effective static potential model using the Debye screening mass, then derived hyperon spin polarization and vector meson spin alignment for both recombination and fragmentation hadronization scenarios. The Debye mass was calculated separately under a strong magnetic field (using lowest Landau level approximation), finite baryon chemical potential, and momentum-space anisotropy, then combined into a net Debye mass. The resulting quark polarization was used to predict spin observables for hyperons (Λ, Σ±, Ξ−, Ω−, Δ++) and vector mesons (ρ, φ, K*) as functions of transverse momentum at fixed temperature.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass increases with magnetic field; at T = 200 MeV, the increase is stronger than at T = 300 MeV (left panel of Fig. 1)
- Debye mass shows a modest decrease with increasing baryon chemical potential, more prominent at lower temperatures (middle panel of Fig. 1)
- Debye mass decreases significantly as the anisotropy parameter ξ increases (right panel of Fig. 1)
- Λ hyperon spin polarization decreases as a function of transverse momentum pT for magnetic field values eB = 0.1 and 0.3 GeV² at T = 200 MeV (left panel of Fig. 2)
- Baryon chemical potential values μB = 0.1 and 0.3 GeV show very marginal effect on Λ spin polarization at T = 200 MeV (middle panel of Fig. 2)
- Anisotropy parameter values ξ = 0.5, 1.0, and 5.0 cause Λ hyperon spin polarization to decrease compared to the isotropic case ξ = 0 at T = 200 MeV (right panel of Fig. 2)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Liang and Wang (2004) on quark polarization from parton scattering and the spin alignment formalism for vector mesons, incorporating medium modifications through Debye mass. It builds on studies of magnetic field effects by Skokov (2009) and Yang (2017), baryon chemical potential effects by Zhao (2022), and anisotropic Debye mass parameterization by Dong (2021) and Romatschke (2003). The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from STAR and ALICE collaborations on Λ hyperon polarization and φ, K*0 meson spin alignment, particularly to disentangle the contributions of magnetic fields, baryon chemical potential, and medium anisotropy to observed spin phenomena.
