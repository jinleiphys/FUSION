# Sensitivity of exclusive proton knockout spin observables to different Lorentz invariant representations of the NN interaction

> Within the framework of the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation, we study the observable consequences of employing a complete Lorentz invariant representation of the NN scattering matrix in terms of 44 independent amplitudes, as opposed to the previously-employed, but ambiguous, five-term Lorentz invariant parametrization of the NN scattering matrix, for the prediction of complete sets of exclusive ($\vec{p},2 \vec{p} $) polarization transfer observables. Two kinematic conditions are considered, namely proton knockout from the $3s_{1/2}$ state of $^{208}$Pb at an incident energy of 202 MeV for coplanar scattering angles ($28.0^{\circ}, -54.6^{\circ}$), as well as an incident energy of 392 MeV for the angle pair ($32.5^{\circ}, -80.0^{\circ}$). The results indicate that certain spin observables are ideal for discriminating between the two representations.

## Key claim

Within the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation, certain exclusive (p,2p) polarization transfer observables can discriminate between the complete 44-amplitude IA2 representation of the NN scattering matrix and the ambiguous five-term IA1 representation, but the sensitivity depends on the specific observable and the incident energy; the authors emphasize that these are qualitative plane wave results not intended for direct comparison to data.

## Method

The authors computed complete sets of polarization transfer observables (analyzing power, induced polarization, and five spin transfer coefficients) for exclusive proton knockout from the 3s1/2 state of 208Pb using both the five-term IA1 representation and the complete 44-amplitude IA2 representation of the NN scattering matrix within a relativistic plane wave impulse approximation. They considered two kinematic conditions: incident energy of 202 MeV with coplanar scattering angles (28.0°, -54.6°) and incident energy of 392 MeV with angles (32.5°, -80.0°). The bound state wave functions were obtained from selfconsistent solutions of the Dirac-Hartree field equations of quantum hadrodynamics, and the transition matrix elements were evaluated in momentum space using plane wave Dirac spinors for the projectile and ejectile nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Incident energies: 202 MeV and 392 MeV for proton knockout from the 3s1/2 state of 208Pb
- Coplanar scattering angles: (28.0°, -54.6°) at 202 MeV; (32.5°, -80.0°) at 392 MeV
- The IA2 representation employs 44 independent invariant amplitudes, of which 5 are determined from free NN scattering data and the remaining 39 from a one-boson exchange Bethe-Salpeter model
- The threshold for sensitivity to model ingredients is stated as approximately ±0.1 in the spin observable value, based on expected maximum experimental error

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the IA1 representation's limitations in inclusive quasielastic proton-nucleus scattering (Hillhouse et al., 1994, 1995, 1998; van der Ventel and Hillhouse, 1999, 2000), where different five-term representations were required for different reaction channels. The IA2 representation was developed by Tjon and Wallace (1985, 1987) and had been applied to elastic and inelastic proton-nucleus scattering but not previously to exclusive knockout reactions. The present plane wave study provides a baseline for future distorted wave calculations that would include optical potentials, which the authors previously showed are essential for describing analyzing power data (Neveling et al., 2002; Hillhouse et al., 2003).
