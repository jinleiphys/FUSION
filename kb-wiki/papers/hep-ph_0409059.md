# Relativity and constituent quark structure in model calculations of parton distributions

> According to recent studies, Parton Distribution Functions (PDFs) and Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) can be evaluated in a Constituent Quark Model (CQM) scenario, considering the constituent quarks as composite objects. In here, a fully covariant model for a system of two particles, together with its non relativistic limit, are used to calculate PDFs and GPDs. The analysis permits to realize that by no means the effects of Relativity can be simulated taking into account the structure of the constituent particles, the two effects being independent and necessary for a proper description of available high energy data in terms of CQM.

## Key claim

Relativity and constituent quark structure are independent effects that cannot substitute for one another in model calculations of parton distributions; both are necessary for a proper description of high-energy data, and the structure of constituent quarks cannot simulate the high-momentum components lost in a non-relativistic limit.

## Method

The authors use a fully covariant Bethe-Salpeter model of two scalar particles bound by a zero-range interaction, which allows exact analytic evaluation of PDFs and GPDs. They also compute the non-relativistic (NR) limit of the same model by approximating energies to order O(p²/m²). The structure of the constituent quark is then incorporated via a convolution formula using the Altarelli-Cabibbo-Maiani-Petronzio (ACMP) scheme, with the constituent quark GPD modelled through Radyushkin’s alpha-Double Distribution ansatz and a monopole form factor corresponding to a constituent quark size of 0.3 fm.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the relativistic system: 48 MeV, i.e. 20% of the constituent mass (constituent mass m = 0.240 MeV, hadron mass M = 0.432 MeV).
- Second moment of the exact PDF in the relativistic system: 0.5 (first moment fixed to 1); second moment of the NR distribution: 0.444, a violation of about 10% due to poor support.
- For the weakly bound system (M = 0.475 MeV, binding energy 2% of constituent mass): second moment of the NR distribution is 0.488, a support violation of about 1%.
- The hadronic scale from the ACMP structure is μ₀² = 0.34 GeV².
- For the pion-like test (M = 0.140 MeV, m = 0.240 MeV), the model result after including structure and evolution is compared with data at Q² = 4 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the program of evaluating PDFs and GPDs in Constituent Quark Models (CQM), following earlier studies by the same group (Scopetta et al., 2004) and others (Traini et al., 1997) that used the ACMP scheme to incorporate constituent quark structure. It directly addresses whether the relativistic light-front approach (used by Tiburzi and Miller, 2003; Boffi et al., 2003) and the ACMP structure approach describe the same physics. The authors conclude they are independent, enabling future CQM calculations to include both relativity and constituent substructure simultaneously when comparing with high-energy data.
