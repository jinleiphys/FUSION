# Phase Transitions in Dense and Hot Matter

> In this conference proceeding, I discuss in detail the deconfinement to quark matter that takes place at large densities and/or temperatures. The first-order phase transition that is assumed to appear beyond a critical point gives rise to mixtures of phases when more than one globally conserved quantity (such as charge fraction) is imposed. The modifications caused by these mixtures of phases in the QCD phase diagram can have consequences on signals of the existence of quark matter expected to be created in heavy-ion collisions, as well as supernova explosions and neutron-star mergers.

# Phase Transitions in Dense and Hot Matter

## Key claim
The deconfinement phase transition to quark matter at large densities and/or temperatures is assumed to be first-order beyond a critical point, but this critical point has not been confirmed; the existence and location of mixtures of phases depend on the number of globally conserved quantities and on whether local or global charge neutrality is imposed, and these mixtures can modify observable signals in heavy-ion collisions, supernova explosions, and neutron-star mergers.

## Method
The author employs the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, an extended non-linear realization of the SU(3) sigma model that includes baryon octet, leptons, and quarks as degrees of freedom, with a Polyakov-loop-like field Phi as an order parameter for deconfinement. The model is fitted to reproduce nuclear, lattice QCD, heavy-ion collision, and astrophysical constraints, and is used to compute phase diagrams for isospin-symmetric matter, isospin-asymmetric matter with fixed charge fractions, and neutron-star matter under both local and global charge neutrality conditions.

## Key numbers
- For isospin-symmetric matter with charge fraction Y_q = 0.5, the deconfinement and confinement curves coincide (azeotropic behavior), with no extended phase coexistence region.
- For isospin-asymmetric matter with charge fraction Y_q = 0.3, the phase coexistence occupies an extended region between the confinement and deconfinement curves, but this area becomes vanishingly small for large temperatures.
- The charge fraction in Au-Au or Pb-Pb collisions is approximately 0.4, though for very energetic collisions the matter created in the fireball has no net baryon number density.
- The CMF model reproduces the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition at low chemical potentials and temperatures for isospin-symmetric matter.

## Context
This work extends the study of non-congruent phase transitions in QCD matter, building on prior work by Iosilevskiy (2010), Hempel (2013, 2015), and Glendenning (1992) on Gibbs and Maxwell constructions for systems with multiple conserved charges. The CMF model results are compared to lattice QCD results from Aoki (2006) and to heavy-ion collision data from the STAR collaboration at RHIC (Adamczyk 2014, Luo 2017). The findings enable more realistic modeling of deconfinement signals in heavy-ion collisions, supernova explosions, and neutron-star mergers by accounting for the possibility of mixed phases under different conservation laws.
