# Quantum interference effects in ($\vec e,e'p$) reactions

> The response to longitudinally polarized electrons in coincident out-of-plane ($\vec{\mathrm e},{\mathrm e}'{\mathrm p}$) reaction is discussed to study the role of final state interactions and quantum interference between different reaction channels, i.e. between direct (quasi) elastic proton emission and baryon resonance production. The high-energy suppression of this effect due to colour transparency is also discussed. Results are given for the $^{12}$C($\vec{\mathrm e},{\mathrm e}'{\mathrm p}$)$^{11}$B$_{\mathrm {g.s.}}$

## Key claim

The helicity asymmetry $A$ in out-of-plane ($\vec e,e'p$) reactions is sensitive to quantum interference between direct elastic proton emission and baryon resonance production channels, but the predicted absolute values of this asymmetry are likely too low to be observed experimentally.

## Method

The authors extended a time-independent coupled-channel formalism to three channels: the nucleon, the S$_{11}$(1535) resonance, and the F$_{15}$(1680) resonance. They calculated the helicity asymmetry $A$ for the $^{12}$C($\vec{\mathrm e},{\mathrm e}'{\mathrm p}$)$^{11}$B$_{\mathrm {g.s.}}$ reaction using photocoupling amplitudes from phenomenological helicity amplitudes, and compared results from the full quantum interference calculation with plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and conventional distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) without interference.

## Key numbers

- The calculation was performed for $^{12}$C($\vec{\mathrm e},{\mathrm e}'{\mathrm p}$)$^{11}$B$_{\mathrm {g.s.}}$ with the proton knocked out from the p-shell with $j=3/2$, at momentum transfers $\vert{\vec q}\vert =$ 2--6 GeV.
- The transverse component of missing momentum was taken as $p_{m\perp} = 200$ MeV, comparable with the Fermi momentum $p_{\mathrm F} = 221$ MeV.
- The asymmetry $A$ is roughly proportional to $1/Q^2$ in the considered range, becoming approximately constant when multiplied by $Q^2$.
- At large and positive ${\vec p}_m \cdot \hat{\vec q}$ but low $Q^2$, the asymmetry can be enhanced by quantum interference with an excited channel.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel formalism developed in a series of papers (Bianconi and collaborators, 1990s) that studied interference between elastic and resonance channels in quasielastic scattering, with applications to colour transparency and anomalous resonance production in nuclei. The kinematic range ($\vert{\vec q}\vert =$ 2--6 GeV) is relevant for planned experiments at CEBAF and partially explored in the NE18 experiment at SLAC (1990s). The results show that while the ratio $R$ is sensitive to channel remixing without interference, the asymmetry $A$ is more affected by quantum interference effects, and its sign change correlates with the dominance of elastic versus inelastic channels in final-state interactions.
