# The dissociation of the J/psi by light mesons and chiral symmetry

> The implication of chiral symmetry for the pion-induced dissociation of the J/psi is examined in detail. It is shown how the low-energy dynamics of pions, constrained by chiral symmetry, affect the dissociation cross-section. The derived soft-pion theorem is then integrated into a Lagrangian model which includes also abnormal parity content and chiral-symmetric form factors. Dissociation by the rho meson is also considered.

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry implies that the normal-parity pion-induced dissociation of the J/psi should vanish in the soft-pion limit, but this decoupling theorem is circumvented by abnormal-parity interactions, which dominate the cross-section near threshold.

## Method

The authors constructed a chirally invariant Lagrangian model using a linear sigma model framework that includes the chiral partners of the D and D* mesons (D0* and D1), as well as abnormal parity interactions and chiral-symmetric form factors. They computed inelastic scattering amplitudes for J/psi dissociation by pions and rho mesons into open charm final states (D + Dbar, D* + Dbar, D* + Dbar*), and verified the soft-pion theorem analytically before evaluating cross-sections numerically with and without form factors.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s) = 3.9 GeV, excluding the abnormal parity sub-amplitude reduces the pion-induced cross-section into (Dbar + D*) + (Dbar* + D) by 65%
- At sqrt(s) = 3.9 GeV, removing the two sub-amplitudes necessary to maintain chiral symmetry increases the same cross-section by 27%
- For a cutoff parameter Lambda = 2 GeV, the inclusive pion-dissociation cross-section is of order a few millibarns near threshold
- For Lambda = 1 GeV, the inclusive pion-dissociation cross-section is a fraction of a millibarn near threshold
- At sqrt(s) = 3.9 GeV with Lambda = 1 GeV, neglecting chiral symmetry sub-amplitudes increases the cross-section by 51%, while omitting abnormal parity content decreases it by 72%
- The four-point form factor mass scale M0 is set to 1.94 GeV (average of D and D* masses)
- The cutoff parameter Lambda is set to either 1 GeV or 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Bourque and Gale (2005) that first examined chiral symmetry effects in a simple Lagrangian model without form factors, showing a reduction at threshold for J/psi + pi -> (D* + Dbar) + (D + Dbar*). The present paper improves upon that model by incorporating abnormal parity processes (previously studied by Oh et al. (2001)), chiral-symmetric form factors, and rho-meson dissociation channels. The results enable more realistic modeling of charmonium suppression in heavy ion collisions by providing microscopic cross-sections constrained by chiral symmetry, though the authors note that relative strengths of pion versus rho dissociation remain model-dependent.
