# Influence of dynamical screening of four-quarks interaction on the chiral phase diagram

> We investigate the effect of screening of the four-quarks contact interactions by the ring diagram at finite temperature and density in an effective chiral model inspired by QCD in the Coulomb gauge. As a consequence, a medium-dependent coupling naturally emerges which, in a class of chiral models, brings the chiral crossover temperature down to the value calculated in LQCD at low net-baryon density. Furthermore, it implies a stronger divergence of the chiral susceptibility at the critical point compared to the mean-field dynamics. At vanishing temperature, however, the transition sets in at unphysically small chemical potential, indicating a need for additional effects to compensate for the screening strength. We discuss the properties of an effective potential for a class of models described by momentum-independent gap equations. In particular, we introduce the method to construct an approximate effective potential from the gap equations to determine the location of the first-order phase transition.

## Key claim

The inclusion of dynamical screening of the four-quark interaction via the ring diagram reduces the chiral crossover temperature to values consistent with lattice QCD at low net-baryon density, but at vanishing temperature the transition occurs at an unphysically small chemical potential, indicating that additional effects are needed to compensate for the screening strength.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral Lagrangian motivated by Coulomb-gauge QCD with a contact four-quark interaction and introduce screening by dressing the effective coupling through the ring-diagram polarization in the static limit. The model is coupled to the Polyakov loop to incorporate statistical confinement, and the coupled gap equations for the dressed quark mass, Polyakov loop, and its conjugate are solved self-consistently using the proper-time regularization scheme. An approximate effective potential is constructed from the gap equations to locate the first-order phase transition, since the exact potential does not exist due to the truncation.

## Key numbers

* The pseudo-critical temperature at vanishing chemical potential is reduced from approximately 230 MeV in the PNJL model to T_pc ≈ 165 MeV due to screening.
* The critical point is located at μ_B,CP = 314.88 MeV and T_CP = 106.95 MeV.
* The model parameters are GΛ² = 3.668, Λ = 1.101 GeV, and m₀ = 5 MeV, yielding vacuum values m_π = 137.8 MeV, f_π = 92.9 MeV, and ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩ = -(250 MeV)³.
* The deconfinement temperature of the pure SU(3) gauge sector is T_d = 270 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of screening-induced inverse magnetic catalysis at finite magnetic field (Lo 2021) to finite temperature and baryon chemical potential, the conditions relevant for relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The screening mechanism naturally connects the pure-gauge deconfinement temperature T_d ≈ 270 MeV with the chiral crossover temperature for light quarks without artificial parameter adjustments. The results indicate that while screening improves the description at low densities, it becomes too strong at larger densities, motivating future inclusion of additional compensating effects such as the dressing of the chemical potential or back-reaction on the Polyakov loop sector.
