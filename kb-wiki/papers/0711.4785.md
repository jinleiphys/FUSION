# Deuteron form factors in chiral effective theory: regulator-independent results and the role of two-pion exchange

> We evaluate the deuteron charge, quadrupole, and magnetic form factors using wave functions obtained from chiral effective theory ($χ$ET) when the potential includes one-pion exchange, chiral two-pion exchange, and genuine contact interactions. We study the manner in which the results for form factors behave as the regulator is removed from the $χ$ET calculation, and compare co-ordinate- and momentum-space approaches. We show that, for both the LO and NNLO chiral potential, results obtained by imposing boundary conditions in co-ordinate space at $r=0$ are equivalent to the $Λ\to \infty$ limit of momentum-space calculations. The regulator-independent predictions for deuteron form factors that result from taking the $Λ\to \infty$ limit using the LO $χ$ET potential are in reasonable agreement with data up to momentum transfers of order 600 MeV, provided that phenomenological information for nucleon structure is employed. In this range the use of the NNLO $χ$ET potential results in only small changes to the LO predictions, and it improves the description of the zero of the charge form factor.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that regulator-independent predictions for deuteron form factors can be obtained from chiral effective theory by taking the momentum-space cutoff to infinity, provided the potential is attractive at short distances (as at LO and NNLO, but not at NLO). The LO predictions agree with data up to momentum transfers of order 600 MeV when phenomenological nucleon structure information is used, while NNLO contributions produce only small changes and improve the description of the charge form factor zero.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for the deuteron bound state using both momentum-space (Lippmann-Schwinger equation with exponential cutoff regularization) and coordinate-space (boundary conditions at r=0) approaches for the LO and NNLO chiral potentials. They computed the deuteron charge, quadrupole, and magnetic form factors from the resulting wave functions using the impulse approximation, with nucleon isoscalar form factors taken from phenomenology. The equivalence of the two regularization schemes was verified by comparing wave functions and form factors as the momentum-space cutoff was increased toward infinity.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy fixed to B = 2.225 MeV throughout
- Asymptotic D/S ratio from LO OPE potential: η_OPE(γ) ≈ 0.026333
- Experimental D/S ratio used: η = 0.0256 (Rodning 1990)
- LO predictions agree with data up to momentum transfers of order 600 MeV
- For NNLO potential with Set IV LECs: R_+ = 2.11 fm, R_- = 1.16 fm
- Pion mass m_π = 138 MeV, axial coupling g_A = 1.29 (LO) or 1.26 (NNLO TPE), pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV
- NNLO momentum-space cutoffs that reproduce η = 0.0256: approximately 3.9 fm⁻¹, 8.45 fm⁻¹, 14.1 fm⁻¹, and 20.9 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral effective theory calculations of deuteron form factors (Phillips 1999, Walzl 2001, Phillips 2003, Phillips 2006) by achieving regulator-independent results through the Λ → ∞ limit, resolving controversies about cutoff dependence in Weinberg's approach (Pavon Valderrama 2005, Nogga 2005, Epelbaum 2006, Birse 2007). The results enable systematic tests of two-pion-exchange contributions to the NN force using deuteron electromagnetic observables, though interpretation is complicated by two-nucleon current contributions at the same chiral order as the TPE potential.
