# VISHNU hybrid model for viscous QCD matter at RHIC and LHC energies

> In this proceeding, we briefly describe the viscous hydrodynamics + hadron cascade hybrid model VISHNU for relativistic heavy ion collisions and report the current status on extracting the QGP viscosity from elliptic flow data.

# VISHNU hybrid model for viscous QCD matter at RHIC and LHC energies

## Key claim
The specific QGP shear viscosity can be extracted from elliptic flow data using the VISHNU hybrid model, but the extracted value changes by a factor of two depending on whether MC-Glauber or MC-KLN initial conditions are used, and the question of whether the QGP fluid is more viscous or more perfect at LHC energies remains open.

## Method
The VISHNU hybrid model couples VISH2+1, which solves the Viscous Israel-Stewart hydrodynamic equations in 2+1 dimensions with longitudinal boost invariance, to the hadron cascade model UrQMD via the H2O interface that converts hydrodynamic output into particle profiles using a modified Cooper-Frye formula. The switching temperature between the hydrodynamic and cascade stages is set to T_sw = 165 MeV, close to the QGP phase transition temperature. The integrated elliptic flow v_2^ch for all charged hadrons, scaled by the initial spatial eccentricity, is compared as a function of multiplicity density per overlap area to extract the QGP shear viscosity.

## Key numbers
- The extracted specific shear viscosity from RHIC 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions yields 1 < 4π(η/s)_QGP < 2.5 for the temperature region T_c < T < 2T_c.
- For MC-Glauber initial conditions, (η/s)_QGP ≃ 1/(4π); for MC-KLN initial conditions, (η/s)_QGP ≃ 2/(4π).
- At LHC energies, (η/s)_QGP must be increased from 2/(4π) to 2.5/(4π) (for MC-KLN) to better describe the ALICE elliptic flow data.
- The VISHNU model successfully describes p_T-spectra and differential elliptic flow v_2(p_T) for p_T < 2 GeV for all charged and identified hadrons at different centrality bins.

## Context
This work extends earlier purely hydrodynamic simulations by Song (2007) and Romatschke (2007) that showed elliptic flow suppression from even the minimal KSS bound η/s = 1/(4π), and by Schenke (2010) and Qiu (2011) on triangular flow sensitivity to viscosity. The hybrid model improves upon purely hydrodynamic approaches by eliminating additional adjustable parameters for the hadronic freeze-out stage. The results enable future combined analysis of v_2 and v_3 from VISHNU to yield more precise values of (η/s)_QGP and reduce ambiguities from initial conditions, though the temperature dependence of the QGP shear viscosity cannot be uniquely constrained from spectra and v_2 alone.
