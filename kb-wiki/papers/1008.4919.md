# Azimuthal correlations from transverse momentum conservation and possible local parity violation

> We analytically calculate the contribution of transverse momentum conservation to the azimuthal correlations that have been proposed as signals for possible local strong parity violation and recently measured in heavy ion collisions. These corrections are of the order of the inverse of the total final state particle multiplicity and thus are of the same order as the observed signal. The corrections contribute with the same sign to both like-sign and opposite-sign pair correlations. Their dependence on the momentum is in qualitative agreement with the measurements by the STAR collaboration, while the pseudorapidity dependence differs from the data.

## Key claim

Transverse momentum conservation contributes to the azimuthal correlation observables $\gamma$ and $\delta$ at the same order of magnitude as the observed signal and as the Chiral Magnetic Effect prediction, but it cannot alone explain the data because it contributes identically to like-sign and opposite-sign pairs and produces no pseudorapidity dependence, unlike the STAR measurements.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the two-particle azimuthal correlators $\gamma = \langle \cos(\phi_1+\phi_2) \rangle$ and $\delta = \langle \cos(\phi_1-\phi_2) \rangle$ under the sole constraint of transverse momentum conservation, using a Gaussian approximation from the central limit theorem for the sum of many uncorrelated transverse momenta. They assumed a thermal single-particle distribution with elliptic flow and a double-Gaussian pseudorapidity distribution, then compared their results to STAR data for AuAu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, including differential studies in $p_+ = (p_{1,t}+p_{2,t})/2$, $p_- = |p_{1,t}-p_{2,t}|$, and pseudorapidity separation.

## Key numbers

- For the STAR acceptance, the contribution from transverse momentum conservation to $\gamma$ is approximately $\gamma \approx -1.7 v_2 / N$, where $v_2$ is the elliptic flow coefficient at midrapidity and $N$ is the total number of produced particles (charged and neutral).
- Using $v_2 = 0.06$ and $N = 21 N_{part}$ (where $N_{part}$ is the number of participants), the calculation yields $\gamma \cdot N_{part} \approx -0.005$, which is a factor of 3-5 (very peripheral to mid-central) smaller than the experimental data.
- For $\delta$, the contribution from transverse momentum conservation in the STAR acceptance is $\delta \approx -1/N$, giving $\delta \cdot N_{part} \approx -0.05$, which is a factor of 1.5-4 (mid-central to very peripheral) larger than the STAR data.
- The differential correlation $\gamma$ grows with $p_+$ as $\gamma \propto (p_+ / \langle p_t \rangle)^{\alpha_+}$ with $\alpha_+ = 2-3$, and with $p_-$ as $\gamma \propto (p_- / \langle p_t \rangle)^{\alpha_-}$ with $\alpha_- \approx 1$.
- The total number of charged particles at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV is approximately $N_{ch} \approx 14 N_{part}$, giving total particle number $N \approx (3/2) N_{ch} = 21 N_{part}$.

## Context

This work provides an analytic calculation of the background from transverse momentum conservation to the azimuthal correlation observables proposed by Voloshin (2004) as signals for the Chiral Magnetic Effect, which were measured by the STAR collaboration (2009). The results extend earlier qualitative discussions by Schlichting and Pratt (2010) and complement studies of other background sources such as local charge conservation by Schlichting and Pratt (2010) and flow-induced correlations by Bzdak, Koch, and Liao (2010). The finding that transverse momentum conservation contributes at the same order as the data but with no charge dependence and no pseudorapidity dependence enables future work to subtract this background and isolate possible CME signals, and motivates the construction of a transverse momentum balance function to determine the rapidity interval over which momentum is actually balanced in heavy ion collisions.
