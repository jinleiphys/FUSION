# The piNN system - recent progress

> Recent progress towards an understanding of the piNN system within chiral perturbation theory is reported. The focus lies on an effective field theory calculation and its comparison to phenomenological calculations for the reaction NN->d pi. In addition, the resulting absorptive and dispersive corrections to the pi-d scattering length are discussed briefly.

# The piNN system - recent progress

## Key claim
Chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) calculations for the reaction NN→dπ, when properly accounting for energy-dependent πN→πN vertices and the resulting cancellation of NLO loop contributions, yield results that differ fundamentally from phenomenological calculations, the ChPT result is dominated by long-range pion exchange, while phenomenological models rely on short-range operators that conflict with field-theoretic consistency and chiral symmetry.

## Method
The author applies effective field theory with a modified power counting that accounts for the threshold momentum scale p_thr = sqrt(M_N m_π) and treats the Delta-isobar as an explicit dynamical degree of freedom. Tree-level diagrams for pp→dπ⁺ are evaluated with the πN→πN vertex taken at its on-shell value (2m_π), and the cancellation of NLO loop contributions against induced irreducible topologies from off-shell vertex terms is demonstrated. For the πd scattering length, dispersive corrections are computed using the CD-Bonn potential for NN distortions, with transition operators from the NN→NNπ calculation.

## Key numbers
- Threshold momentum: p_thr = sqrt(M_N m_π), where M_N is the nucleon mass and m_π the pion mass
- Experimental πd scattering length: a_πd^exp = (-26.1 ± 0.5 + i(6.3 ± 0.7)) × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹
- Dispersive correction to πd scattering length from hadronic transitions: a_πd^disp = -1.6 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹ (approximately 6% of the real part)
- Individual contributions to dispersive correction (in 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹): -6.3 (direct), +2 (diagram b), +3.1 (diagram c), -0.4 (diagram d)
- Electromagnetic contribution to dispersive correction: -0.1 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹
- Estimated uncertainty Δa_πd^disp ≈ 1.4 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹ (about 6% of Re(a_πd^exp))
- Ratio R = lim(q→0) σ(πd→NN)/σ(πd→γNN) = 2.83 ± 0.04

## Context
This work extends the Weinberg (1992) approach of convoluting ChPT transition operators with nuclear wave functions, addressing the convergence problems observed in NN→NNπ calculations by Park and others. The author resolves a puzzle identified by Gasparyan and Epelbaum regarding linear momentum growth of loop amplitudes for pp→dπ⁺. The calculation of dispersive corrections to the πd scattering length within ChPT is performed for the first time, building on earlier Faddeev calculations by Afnan and Thomas and the estimate by Brückner. This enables future high-precision extraction of the isoscalar scattering length from upcoming PSI measurements, though isospin-violating corrections and Delta-isobar contributions remain to be evaluated.
