# Production of eta mesons in nucleon-nucleon collisions

> A microscopic calculation of near-threshold eta-meson production in the reaction NN -> NNeta is presented. It is assumed that the eta meson is produced via direct emission and via elementary rescattering processes MN -> etaN of various mesons M = pi, rho, etc. As a novel feature the amplitudes for the latter production mechanism are taken from a multi-channel meson-exchange model of the pi N system developed by the Juelich group which contains explicitly the channels pi N, rho N, eta N, sigma N, and pi Delta. Furthermore, effects of the NN interaction in the final as well as in the initial state are taken into account microscopically. Our results are compared with recent data from the COSY and CELSIUS accelerator facilities. Reasonable agreement with available near-threshold cross section data for the reactions pp -> ppeta, pn -> pneta, and pn -> deta is found.

## Key claim

The paper presents a microscopic calculation of near-threshold eta-meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, claiming reasonable agreement with experimental data for the reactions pp → ppη, pn → pnη, and pn → dη, but with the hedge that the original meson-nucleon model overestimates the pp → ppη cross section by about 30% and underestimates the pn → pnη cross section by about 50%, requiring modifications to the ρN → ηN amplitude to improve agreement.

## Method

The authors employed a distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) to calculate the transition amplitude for NN → NNη, incorporating elementary production operators from a coupled-channels meson-exchange model of the πN system (including channels πN, ρN, ηN, σN, and πΔ) for the rescattering processes MN → ηN. The NN interactions in both the initial and final states were treated microscopically using the CCF model (a folded-diagram version of the full Bonn model) for the initial state and both the CCF and Bonn B models for the final state, with the calculation restricted to S-wave contributions for excess energies Q up to about 50 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The pp → ppη cross section is overestimated by approximately 30% in the calculation using the original meson-nucleon model.
- The pn → pnη cross section is underestimated by about 50% in the calculation using the original meson-nucleon model.
- The η production threshold in the laboratory frame is at T_lab = 1250 MeV.
- The ηN threshold in the center-of-mass frame is at E_cm ≈ 1486 MeV.
- The πN → ηN cross section from the meson-nucleon model overestimates the experimental data by roughly 15%.
- A 15% decrease in σ(π⁻p → ηn) leads to a reduction in σ(NN → NNη) by roughly the same amount (about 15%).
- The coupling constant used for the ηNN vertex is g_ηNN²/(4π) = 1.8, taken from SU(6) symmetry.
- The cutoff masses for the monopole form factors are: Λ_πNN = 1300 MeV, Λ_ρNN = 1400 MeV, Λ_ηNN = 1500 MeV, Λ_σNN = 1700 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of η-meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, such as those by Gedalin (1997), Santra (1998), and Nakayama (2001), by using a fully microscopic coupled-channels model for the meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes (developed by the Jülich group, Schutz 1998, Oliphant 2000) rather than simple tree-level parametrizations with the N*(1535) resonance. The approach enables a consistent treatment of relative phases between different meson exchange contributions and includes full NN final-state and initial-state interactions, addressing shortcomings identified in earlier works by Hanhart (2000) and Baru (2001). The results enable future investigations of ηN final-state interactions and three-body effects, which the authors note may be important for excess energies below 10-15 MeV.
