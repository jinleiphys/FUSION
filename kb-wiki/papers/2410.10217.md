# Correlations and fluctuations in a magnetized PNJL model with and without inverse magnetic catalysis effect

> The correlation $χ^{BQ}_{11}$ and quadratic fluctuations $χ^B_2,\ χ^Q_2,\ χ^T_2$ of baryon number $B$, electric charge $Q$ and temperature $T$ are investigated in a two-flavor Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model at finite temperature and magnetic field. The inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect is introduced through the magnetic field dependent parameters $G(eB)$ or $T_0(eB)$, and we make comparison of the results in the cases with and without IMC effect. With nonvanishing magnetic field, the correlation $χ^{BQ}_{11}$ and fluctuations $χ^B_2,\ χ^Q_2,\ χ^T_2$ increase with temperature, and then show the peak around the pseudocritical temperatures of chiral restoration and deconfinement phase transitions in the cases with and without the IMC effect. The correlation and fluctuations along the phase transition line under external magnetic field are characterized by the scaled correlation ${\hat χ}_{11}^{BQ}=\frac{χ_{11}^{BQ}(eB,T_{pc}^c(eB))}{χ_{11}^{BQ}(eB=0,T_{pc}^c(eB=0))}$ and scaled fluctuations ${\hat χ}_2^{B(Q,T)}=\frac{χ_2^{B(Q,T)}(eB,T_{pc}^c(eB))}{χ_2^{B(Q,T)}(eB=0,T_{pc}^c(eB=0))}$ at the pseudocritical temperature $T_{pc}^c$ of chiral restoration phase transition. ${\hat χ}_{11}^{BQ},\ {\hat χ}_2^{B}$, and ${\hat χ}_2^{Q}$ increase with magnetic fields, and the inclusion of IMC effect leads to some enhancement in their values. However, ${\hat χ}_2^{T}$ is altered by the IMC effect. Without IMC effect, ${\hat χ}^T_2$ slightly increases and then decreases with magnetic fields. Taking into account of the IMC effect by $G(eB)$, ${\hat χ}^T_2$ monotonically increases with magnetic fields, and by $T_0(eB)$, it is a nonmonotonic function of magnetic field.

## Key claim

The correlation and quadratic fluctuations of baryon number, electric charge, and temperature in the PNJL model show peaks around the pseudocritical temperatures of chiral restoration and deconfinement phase transitions under magnetic fields, but the specific behavior of the scaled temperature fluctuation depends qualitatively on whether and how the inverse magnetic catalysis effect is introduced (via magnetic-field-dependent coupling \(G(eB)\) or Polyakov-loop scale \(T_0(eB)\)).

## Method

The two-flavor Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model is solved at finite temperature and magnetic field in mean-field approximation, using Pauli-Villars regularization. The inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect is incorporated by fitting Lattice QCD results for the decreasing pseudocritical temperature of chiral restoration to either a magnetic-field-dependent quark coupling \(G(eB)\) or a magnetic-field-dependent Polyakov potential scale \(T_0(eB)\). The correlation \(\chi^{BQ}_{11}\) and quadratic fluctuations \(\chi^B_2,\ \chi^Q_2,\ \chi^T_2\) are computed from derivatives of the thermodynamic potential at zero chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* Scaled correlation \(\hat{\chi}^{BQ}_{11}\) at the chiral pseudocritical temperature reaches 1.75 at \(eB = 10 m_\pi^2\) in the case without IMC effect; Lattice QCD reports \(\hat{\chi}^{BQ}_{11} \simeq 2.4\) at \(eB = 8 m_\pi^2\).
* Scaled baryon fluctuation \(\hat{\chi}^{B}_{2}\) at the chiral pseudocritical temperature reaches 1.36 at \(eB = 10 m_\pi^2\) without IMC effect.
* Scaled charge fluctuation \(\hat{\chi}^{Q}_{2}\) at the chiral pseudocritical temperature reaches 1.26 at \(eB = 10 m_\pi^2\) without IMC effect.
* The NJL model parameters are fixed to vacuum values: current quark mass \(m_0 = 5\) MeV, coupling \(G = 7.79\) GeV\(^{-2}\), Pauli-Villars mass \(\Lambda = 1127\) MeV, yielding chiral condensate \((-250\ \text{MeV})^3\), pion mass \(m_\pi = 134\) MeV, and pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.
* Polyakov potential parameters: \(a_0 = 6.75\), \(a_1 = -1.95\), \(a_2 = 2.625\), \(a_3 = -7.44\), \(b_3 = 0.75\), \(b_4 = 7.5\), and \(T_0 = 210\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous analytical studies of conserved-charge fluctuations in magnetized QCD matter (Ding et al., 2022; Ding et al., 2024) by incorporating the inverse magnetic catalysis effect into the PNJL model, which earlier model calculations (Ding et al., 2022) had not fully considered. The results enable comparison with Lattice QCD data on the magnetometer response of QCD and show that the temperature fluctuation \(\chi^T_2\) is a sensitive probe of the IMC mechanism, unlike the baryon and charge fluctuations. The study also notes that the peak structure of quadratic fluctuations seen in Lattice QCD (Ding et al., 2022) is reproduced here, in contrast to earlier PNJL calculations using a soft-cutoff regularization (Ding et al., 2022).
