# New nuclear three-body clusters ϕ{NN}

> Binding energies of three-body systems of the type ϕ+2N are estimated. Due to the strong attraction between ϕ-meson and nucleon, suggested in different approaches, bound states can appear in systems like ϕ+np (singlet and triplet) and ϕ+pp. This indicates the principal possibility of the formation of new nuclear clusters.

## Key claim
Binding energies of three-body systems of the type φ+2N are estimated, showing that bound states can appear in φ+np (singlet and triplet) and φ+pp, but the binding is defined by a delicate balance of a narrow φ-n attraction and the repulsive part of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, making the system very sensitive to the short-range behavior of NN potentials.

## Method
The authors used a variational method with a trial function to obtain the φ-n binding energy, then applied a folding approximation to solve the Schrödinger equation in Jacobi coordinates for the three-body systems φ+np and φ+pp. The nucleon-nucleon interaction was modeled using Malfliet-Tjon singlet (I) and triplet (III) potentials, and for φ+pp the Coulomb potential was added to the singlet np interaction.

## Key numbers
- φ-n binding energy: about 9.3 MeV, obtained from the Yukawa-type potential with α = 1.25 and μ = 600 MeV
- φ+np (singlet, ¹S₀ for np) binding energy: about 10.1 MeV
- φ+np (triplet, ³S₁ for np) binding energy: 17.7 MeV
- φ+pp binding energy: near 9.6 MeV (including Coulomb potential)
- Malfliet-Tjon singlet potential parameters: λ_A = 2.64, μ_A = 1.55 fm⁻¹, λ_R = 7.39, μ_R = 3.11 fm⁻¹
- Malfliet-Tjon triplet potential parameters: λ_A = 3.22, μ_A = 1.55 fm⁻¹, λ_R = 7.39, μ_R = 3.11 fm⁻¹

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of charmonium meson interactions with nucleons (references [1-3] in the paper) and builds on predictions of strong φ-N attraction from phenomenological models (Gao et al., following ideas of Brodsky) and quark-model calculations using Resonating Group techniques (Huang et al.). The results indicate the principal possibility of forming new nuclear clusters like φ+2N, which would represent a new kind of few-body system whose properties are highly sensitive to the short-range behavior of NN potentials.
