# Why N*'s are Important

> The study of N*'s can provide us with critical insights into the nature of QCD in the confinement domain. The keys to progress in this domain are the identification of its important degrees of freedom and the effective forces between them. I report on the growing evidence in support of the flux tube model, and comment on the connection between this model and spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, on the spin-dependence of the long-range confining potential, on the evidence for short-range one gluon exchange, on instantons, and on the one pion exchange model.

## Key claim

The study of N* (nucleon resonance) spectra provides critical insights into the nature of QCD in the confinement domain, but the key challenge is identifying the appropriate degrees of freedom and effective forces between them, since plane wave quarks and gluons are not a useful starting point for low-energy, confinement-dominated physics.

## Method

The paper reviews converging evidence from four theoretical directions that support the flux tube model as the appropriate description of low-energy QCD: spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, the large N_c limit of QCD, quenched lattice QCD, and the heavy quark limit. The author analyzes the spin-dependence of the confining potential by examining meson and baryon spectra, particularly the spin-orbit matrix elements extracted from P-wave mesons and the hyperfine splittings observed across different quark masses.

## Key numbers

- The string tension b of QCD depends on the strong coupling constant g according to b ∝ exp[-16π²/(11g²)]
- The spin-orbit matrix element extracted from isovector mesons is -3 ± 20 MeV, much smaller than either the OGE or Thomas precession contributions of about ±200 MeV each
- The D*-D splitting and the 1/m_Q heavy quark scaling law predicts a K*-K splitting of 460 MeV, close to the actual splitting of 400 MeV
- The Σ*_SU(3) - Λ_SU(3) splitting equals Δ - N by SU(3) symmetry
- Standard constituent quark masses used: m_d = m_u = 0.33 GeV, m_s = 0.55 GeV, m_c = 1.82 GeV, m_b = 5.20 GeV
- The center-of-gravity of Λ_c(2594)1/2⁻ and Λ_c(2627)3/2⁻ is 330 MeV above Λ_c(2285); the center-of-gravity of Λ(1520) and Λ(1405) lies 365 MeV above Λ(1115)
- The Λ sector splitting is about 110 MeV predicted versus 115 MeV observed
- The first excited adiabatic surfaces of the flux tube have an energy gap δV(r) = π/r above the quarkonium potential

## Context

This invited talk at the N*2000 workshop extends the flux tube model developed by Isgur and Paton (1980s) and the Isgur-Karl model for baryons, building on Nambu's early 1970s string theory picture of QCD. The paper connects these models to heavy quark symmetry developed by Isgur and Wise, the large N_c expansion, and quenched lattice QCD results. The work enables predictions for hybrid N* electroproduction cross sections relevant to the Hall D program at Jefferson Lab's 12 GeV Upgrade, and calls for more data to confirm whether the flux tube model is on the right path for understanding the confinement domain of QCD.
