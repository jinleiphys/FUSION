# A theoretical approach to the (pion^{-}, gamma gamma) reaction in nuclei

> We have studied the pion^{-} capture in nuclei leading to two photons, using improved many body methods which have been tested with success in one photon pion^{-} radiative capture, muon^{-} capture and neutrino scattering. The qualitative features of the experimental data are reproduced but there are still some disagreement at small relative photon angles. The reaction is a potential source of information on details of the gamma-gamma-pion-pion vertex where chiral corrections can be relevant. New and more precise experiments are planned which make calculations like the present one relevant and opportune. ---------------------------------------------------------

# A theoretical approach to the (π⁻, γγ) reaction in nuclei

## Key claim

The authors reproduce the qualitative features of experimental data for pion⁻ capture leading to two photons in nuclei, but there remains disagreement at small relative photon angles. The reaction is presented as a potential source of information on the γγππ vertex where chiral corrections can be relevant, and the calculations are made timely by planned new experiments.

## Method

The authors evaluate the π⁻ capture width into two photons using infinite nuclear matter methods with Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, and explicit summation over occupied nucleon states, avoiding the closure sum approximation. They compute the π⁻p → γγn cross section using a model where the dominant amplitude involves a pion exchange diagram, then map the results to finite nuclei via the local density approximation using pionic wave functions from the Klein-Gordon equation. A weighted average over pionic orbits is performed, and medium effects in the pion propagator are included through renormalization of the longitudinal response using the Lindhard function with a realistic excitation gap energy.

## Key numbers

- For ⁹Be, the results approximately agree with experiment for angles above 90°, lying on the upper side of the data.
- For ¹²C, the results approximately agree with experiment for angles above 90°, lying on the lower side of the data.
- At small relative photon angles, the theoretical results are consistently below the experimental data.
- A lower threshold of 25 MeV is applied for photon energies k₁ and k₂ to compare with experimental data from the experiment of ref. [9] (1990).
- The renormalization of the longitudinal response reduces the results, particularly at large angles, leading to better agreement with data.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical approaches to the (π⁻, γγ) reaction by Baym and Grinstein (1980s) and others, which showed only rough agreement with experiment (discrepancies of factor two to four) and poor reproduction of angular dependence. The method improves upon the closure sum approach used by Christillin et al. (1980s) by avoiding dependence on average nuclear excitation energy, and has been previously tested successfully in one-photon radiative pion capture, μ⁻ capture, and neutrino scattering by the same group (Oset et al., 1990s). The results enable more precise comparisons with planned experiments and could provide tests of chiral perturbation theory corrections to the γγππ vertex, relevant for understanding pion condensation precritical phenomena and nuclear renormalization of weak currents in μ⁻ capture and neutrino scattering.
