# Many-body effects on dense matter with hyperons at finite temperature

> In this work, we present the first extension of the Many-Body Forces (MBF) Model to finite temperature. The MBF Model describes nuclear matter in a relativistic quantum hadrodynamics formalism that takes many-body forces into account through a field dependence of the nuclear interaction coupling constants. Assuming nuclear matter to be charge neutral, beta-equilibrated, and populated by the baryon octet, electrons, and muons, we explore the parameters of the model, three different hyperon coupling schemes (also introduced here for the first time in MBF), and temperature effects to describe basic properties of nuclear matter, including the speed of sound, compressibility, and adiabatic index. We also investigate the mass-radius relation of compact stars by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations at zero and finite temperature, including scenarios with fixed entropy per baryon. Our original results at finite temperature open the path to a new description of proto-neutron stars.

# Many-body effects on dense matter with hyperons at finite temperature

## Key claim

The Many-Body Forces (MBF) Model is extended to finite temperature for the first time, but only the scalar (S) version of the model is explored, with the adjustable parameter ζ controlling the many-body contributions while the vector parameters ξ, κ, and η are set to zero. The results are conditional on the specific choice of hyperon coupling schemes and model parameterizations, meaning the finite-temperature behavior depends on which of the three newly introduced hyperon coupling schemes is adopted.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic quantum hadrodynamics formalism in which the MBF Model's meson-baryon coupling constants depend on scalar meson fields through a parametric term Π_{λb} = (1 + (g_{σb}σ + g_{σ*b}σ* + ½ g_{δb}τ·δ)/(λ m_b))^{-λ}, introducing many-body forces beyond the Walecka and Zimanyi-Moszkowski models. They assume charge-neutral, beta-equilibrated matter composed of the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ⁰, Σ⁻, Σ⁰, Σ⁺, Ξ⁻, Ξ⁰), electrons, and muons, and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations at zero and finite temperature, including scenarios with fixed entropy per baryon. Three different hyperon coupling schemes are introduced for the first time within the MBF framework.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: n₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, corresponding to an average density of 7 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³ in neutron stars
- Typical neutron star masses range from 1 to 2 M_Sun, with maximum mass estimates reaching M_max = 2.3 M_Sun and radii of order 10–13 km
- Proto-neutron stars reach temperatures of tens of MeV (~10¹¹ K), while evolved neutron stars have temperatures on the order of 1 MeV (~10¹⁰ K)
- The σ meson mass is taken as 550 MeV, ω meson mass as 783 MeV, ρ meson mass as 775 MeV, δ meson mass as 980 MeV, σ* meson mass as 990 MeV, and φ meson mass as 1019 MeV
- Baryon masses from the Particle Data Group: proton 939.6 MeV, neutron 938.3 MeV, Λ⁰ 1116 MeV, Σ⁺ 1189 MeV, Σ⁰ 1193 MeV, Σ⁻ 1197 MeV, Ξ⁰ 1315 MeV, Ξ⁻ 1322 MeV
- Lepton masses: electron 0.5110 MeV, muon 105.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends the MBF Model, originally developed by Taurines et al. (2000) and further applied by Gomes et al. (2014) to hyperonic matter at high densities, to finite temperature for the first time. It builds on the Walecka Model (1974) and the Zimanyi-Moszkowski Model (1992), with the MBF Model providing a parametric interpolation between these approaches through the adjustable parameter ζ. The finite-temperature results enable new descriptions of proto-neutron stars, connecting to observations from NICER (Riley 2019, Miller 2019) and LIGO/Virgo (2017) that have constrained neutron star radii and tidal deformabilities, and opening the path to studies of supernova explosions and neutron star mergers where temperature effects are essential.
