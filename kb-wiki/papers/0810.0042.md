# Critical Points in the Linear Sigma Model with Quarks

> We employ a simple effective model to study the chiral dynamics of two flavors of quarks at finite temperature and density. In particular, we determine the phase diagram in the plane of temperature and baryon chemical potential as a function of the pion mass. An interesting phase structure occurs which results in zero, one or two critical points depending on the value of the vacuum pion mass.

# Critical Points in the Linear Sigma Model with Quarks

## Key claim

The linear sigma model with two flavors of quarks, when treated beyond mean-field approximation with thermal fluctuations of meson and fermion fields, can produce zero, one, or two critical points in the temperature-baryon chemical potential plane depending on the value of the vacuum pion mass. The existence of two critical points for vacuum pion masses between 0 and approximately 35 MeV is described as "unconventional or exotic" and is presented as the first model calculation to yield such a phase diagram.

## Method

The authors employ the linear sigma model coupled to two flavors of identical-mass quarks, with parameters fixed by the pion decay constant (92.4 MeV), sigma mass (700 MeV), and quark mass (313 MeV). They go beyond the mean-field approximation by including thermal fluctuations of the meson and fermion fields using a self-consistent Gaussian averaging technique developed in prior work (Carter 1997, Carter 2000, Mocsy 2004). The vacuum pion mass is scanned from 0 to 350 MeV, and the phase diagram is determined by solving the self-consistent equations for the condensate, effective meson masses, and thermodynamic potential at each temperature and chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- For vacuum pion mass of 138 MeV (physical value), the critical point is located at approximately T_c = 75 MeV and mu_c = 850 MeV.
- For vacuum pion mass of 35 MeV, two critical points appear: one at mu_{c1} approximately 725 MeV and T_{c1} approximately 92 MeV, and another at mu_{c2} approximately 240 MeV and T_{c2} approximately 137 MeV.
- For vacuum pion mass of 321 MeV, the remaining critical point sits on the T = 0 axis; for vacuum pion mass greater than 321 MeV, no phase transition exists.
- At T = 0 and large chemical potential, latent heats are on the order of 270 MeV/fm^3.
- The energy density divided by T^4 at zero chemical potential reaches approximately 7.5 around T = 200 MeV for vacuum pion masses of 0 and 200 MeV.
- For comparison, the mean-field calculation of Scavenius (2001) found T_c = 99 MeV and mu_c = 621 MeV using the physical pion mass and a sigma mass of 600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the linear sigma model with quarks studied by Scavenius (2001) in two ways: it goes beyond the mean-field approximation by including thermal fluctuations of meson and fermion fields, and it scans the vacuum pion mass from zero to over 300 MeV rather than fixing it at the physical value. The techniques are based on the self-consistent method developed by Fraser (1994), Carter (1997), and Carter (2000), and extend the work of Mocsy (2004) to nonzero chemical potential. The results for the critical point at large T and small mu are consistent with lattice calculations by de Forcrand and Philipsen (2003), while the critical point at small T and large mu represents the conventional picture. The model enables future studies of transport coefficients (shear and bulk viscosities, thermal conductivity) for fluid dynamic modeling of heavy ion collisions at FAIR and low-energy RHIC runs.
