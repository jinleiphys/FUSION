# Light-Front Realization of Chiral Symmetry Breaking

> We discuss a description of chiral symmetry breaking in the light-front (LF) formalism. Based on careful analyses of several models, we give clear answers to the following three fundamental questions: (i) What is the difference between the LF chiral transformation and the ordinary chiral transformation? (ii) How does a gap equation for the chiral condensate emerge? (iii) What is the consequence of the coexistence of a nonzero chiral condensate and the trivial Fock vacuum? The answer to Question (i) is given through a classical analysis of each model. Question (ii) is answered based on our recognition of the importance of characteristic constraints, such as the zero-mode and fermionic constraints. Question (iii) is intimately related to another important problem, reconciliation of the nonzero chiral condensate and the invariance of the vacuum under the LF chiral transformation. This and Question (iii) are understood in terms of the modified chiral transformation laws of the dependent variables. The characteristic ways in which the chiral symmetry breaking is realized are that the chiral charge is no longer conserved and that the transformation of the scalar and pseudoscalar fields is modified. We also discuss other outcomes, such as the light-cone wave function of the pseudoscalar meson in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that chiral symmetry breaking can be realized in the light-front formalism despite the trivial Fock vacuum, but only if the chiral transformation laws of dependent variables (zero modes and the "bad" fermion component) are modified in the broken phase, and the light-front chiral charge is no longer conserved.

## Method

The authors analyze three models in the light-front formalism: the sigma model (scalar fields), the chiral Yukawa model (scalar and fermionic fields), and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (purely fermionic fields). For scalar fields they use the discretized light-cone quantization (DLCQ) method with periodic boundary conditions to treat zero modes explicitly; for fermionic fields they solve the fermionic constraint equation that relates the dependent "bad" component to the independent "good" component, carefully handling infrared singularities from the longitudinal zero mode of fermion bilinears.

## Key numbers

- The light-front dispersion relation is \(p^- = (p_\perp^2 + m^2)/(2p^+)\), which enforces \(p^+ \geq 0\) for semi-positive energy.
- The light-front chiral charge is defined as \(Q_5^{\rm LF} = \int dx^- d^2x_\perp \, \psi^\dagger \sigma^3 \psi\), and it always annihilates the vacuum: \(:Q_5^{\rm LF}:|0\rangle = 0\).
- For the massive free fermion, the light-front Lagrangian is invariant under the light-front chiral transformation even with \(m \neq 0\), due to the relation \(\sigma^i \partial_i \chi^{(m)} + m \chi^{(0)} = 0\).
- The ordinary chiral current divergence gives \(\partial_\mu J_5^\mu = 2m \bar\Psi i\gamma_5 \Psi\) for a massive fermion.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of zero-mode physics in light-front field theory, particularly the DLCQ method developed by Maskawa and Yamawaki (1976) and Pauli and Brodsky (1985), and earlier work on spontaneous symmetry breaking in scalar models. The paper directly addresses the apparent paradox between the trivial light-front Fock vacuum and nonzero chiral condensates, which is essential for connecting the constituent quark model to QCD at a field-theoretic level as envisioned by Wilson (1994). The results enable a consistent description of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in the light-front formalism, including the light-cone wave function of the pseudoscalar meson in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model.
