# A Brief History and Outlook of Hadronic Physics in Indonesia

> Hadronic physics has gradually emerged as one of the growing research frontiers in Indonesia, driven by efforts to better understand the properties of the strong interaction and the internal structure of hadrons from the fundamental principles of Quantum Chromodynamics. In the last few decades, Indonesian researchers have made significant contributions to developing various theoretical and phenomenological aspects of hadrons. In addition, on the experimental side, Indonesian scientists have participated in hadron experiment facilities overseas, such as the ALICE collaboration at CERN, which has strengthened the scientific activities and networks and has supported the training of young Indonesian researchers. In the present paper, we review Indonesian scientists' contributions to hadronic physics, highlight ongoing research directions in both experimental and theoretical, and outline strategies for future development toward integration into the international hadronic physics community.

## Key claim

This review paper claims that hadronic physics in Indonesia has gradually emerged as a growing research frontier, but the authors explicitly note that the number of active researchers remains relatively limited and that the article is not intended to create an exclusive community but rather to identify a relatively small segment of the broader high-energy physics community in Indonesia.

## Method

The authors conducted a historical review of Indonesian hadronic physics research spanning from the 1970s to the present, categorizing development into three stages: Pioneering Stage (1970s–2000s), Early Millennium (2000s–2010s), and Recent Developments (2010s–present). They surveyed the educational backgrounds, doctoral advisors, research foci, and institutional affiliations of Indonesian researchers who have contributed to theoretical and experimental hadronic physics, both domestically and abroad.

## Key numbers

- The Ph.D. program in theoretical physics at Universitas Indonesia was formally established around 2017, with its first graduate completing the program in 2019.
- Terry Mart's research group at Universitas Indonesia has mentored more than 50 students, most of whom were bachelor's or master's students who later pursued graduate studies abroad.
- The International School of Strangeness Nuclear Physics (SNP) has been held since 2012, with many of Terry Mart's students actively participating, including the most recent school held in 2024.
- Indonesia joined the Asia Pacific Center for Theoretical Physics (APCTP) in 2023.
- The Kaon-Maid phenomenological model for electromagnetic kaon production on the nucleon was publicly released in 2000 and has been used for over 25 years.

## Context

This review extends the documentation of hadronic physics development in Indonesia, building upon earlier contributions from pioneering figures such as M. Barmawi (University of Chicago, 1964), Tjia May On (Northwestern University, 1969), and Darmadi Kusno (University of Oregon, 1979). The paper enables international colleagues to understand the current physics landscape and identifies active domestic research groups at Universitas Indonesia, Universitas Gadjah Mada, and the National Research and Innovation Agency (BRIN), while noting that Indonesia recently joined the APCTP in 2023 to strengthen international collaboration.
