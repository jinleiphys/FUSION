# Signature of strange dibaryons in kaon- and photon-induced reactions

> We examine how the signature of the strange dibaryon resonances shows up in scattering amplitudes and observables of the three-body $\bar{K}NN$-$πY N (Y=Σ, Λ)$ system on the physical real energy axis. The so-called point method is applied to handle logarithmic singularities that appear in solving the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations for the real scattering energies. By taking two different kinds of models for the two-body $\bar{K}N$-$πΣ$ subsystem, both of which reproduce the available data equally well but give quite a different resonance-pole structure for $Λ(1405)$, we also investigate whether the strange-dibaryon production reactions can be used for disentangling the nature of $Λ(1405)$.

## Key claim

The signature of strange-dibaryon resonances with isospin 1/2 and spin-parity 0- can be observed as bumps in the quasi-two-body amplitudes of the three-body $\bar{K}NN$-$\pi Y N$ system at real scattering energies, but the number and positions of these bumps depend strongly on whether an energy-independent or energy-dependent model is used for the two-body $\bar{K}N$-$\pi\Sigma$ subsystem, both of which reproduce available data equally well.

## Method

The authors solved coupled-channel Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for the three-body $\bar{K}NN$-$\pi Y N$ system using separable two-body interactions, applying the point method to handle logarithmic singularities that appear at real scattering energies. Two models for the $s$-wave meson-baryon interactions were employed: an energy-independent (E-indep) model and an energy-dependent (E-dep) model, both derived from the leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa term of the effective chiral Lagrangian but with different off-shell behavior. The transition probability for the quasi-two-body to three-body reaction $(Y_K)_{I=0} + N \to \pi + \Sigma + N$ was computed, along with an estimation of the probability for the kaon absorption process $(Y_K)_{I=0} + N \to \Lambda + N$.

## Key numbers

* The $\bar{K}NN$ and $\pi\Sigma N$ threshold energies are 2370 MeV and 2267 MeV, respectively.
* For the E-indep model, the strange-dibaryon resonance pole mass has real part 2312-2326 MeV and imaginary part -17 to -20 MeV.
* For the E-dep model, two resonance poles are found: one with real part 2354-2361 MeV and imaginary part -17 to -23 MeV, and another with real part 2281-2303 MeV and imaginary part -122 to -160 MeV.
* The bump in the quasi-two-body amplitude $|X_{(Y_K)_{I=0}N,(Y_K)_{I=0}N}|^2$ appears at $W\sim 2305$ MeV for the E-indep model and at $W\sim 2340$ MeV for the E-dep model.
* Cutoff parameters for the $\bar{K}N$-$\pi Y$ interaction range from 640 MeV to 1000 MeV depending on the channel and model.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Ikeda 2007, Ikeda 2010) where resonance poles of strange dibaryons were found in the complex energy plane by solving coupled-channel AGS equations, but only pole positions were presented rather than observable cross sections. The study aims to connect theoretical predictions to experiments planned at J-PARC (E15 and E27 experiments) and SPring-8 (LEPS Collaboration) by computing transition probabilities that can be compared with measured invariant-mass and missing-mass distributions. The two models for $\Lambda(1405)$ (one-pole vs. two-pole structure) are used to investigate whether strange-dibaryon production reactions can help disentangle the nature of $\Lambda(1405)$, a question previously addressed in chiral unitary approaches such as Jido (2003).
