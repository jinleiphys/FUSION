# Electric dipole strength and dipole polarizability in $^{48}$Ca within a fully self-consistent second random-phase approximation

> The second random-phase-approximation model corrected by a subtraction procedure designed to cure double counting, instabilities, and ultraviolet divergences, is employed for the first time to analyze the dipole strength and polarizability in $^{48}$Ca. All the terms of the residual interaction are included, leading to a fully self-consistent scheme. Results are illustrated with two Skyrme parametrizations, SGII and SLy4. Those obtained with the SGII interaction are particularly satisfactory. In this case, the low-lying strength below the neutron threshold is extremely well reproduced and the giant dipole resonance is described in a very satisfactory way especially in its spreading and fragmentation. Spreading and fragmentation are produced in a natural way within such a theoretical model by the coupling of 1 particle-1 hole and 2 particle-2 hole configurations. Owing to this feature, we may provide for the electric polarizability as a function of the excitation energy a curve with a similar slope around the centroid energy of the giant resonance compared to the corresponding experimental results. This represents a considerable improvement with respect to previous theoretical predictions obtained with the random-phase approximation or with several ab-initio models. In such cases, the spreading width of the excitation cannot be reproduced and the polarizability as a function of the excitation energy displays a stiff increase around the predicted centroid energy of the giant resonance.

## Key claim

The subtracted second random-phase-approximation (SSRPA) model, applied for the first time in a fully self-consistent scheme including all terms of the residual interaction, reproduces the low-lying dipole strength below the neutron threshold in $^{48}$Ca and provides a dipole polarizability curve with a slope around the giant dipole resonance centroid that matches experimental results, representing a considerable improvement over random-phase approximation (RPA) and several ab-initio models. The improvement is conditional on using the SGII Skyrme parametrization, which yields particularly satisfactory results, whereas SLy4 results are less satisfactory.

## Method

The authors employed the second random-phase-approximation (SRPA) model corrected by a subtraction procedure designed to cure double counting, instabilities, and ultraviolet divergences, using two Skyrme parametrizations (SGII and SLy4) in a fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock scheme that includes all terms of the residual interaction including spin-orbit and Coulomb contributions. A cutoff of 100 MeV was used for 1 particle-1 hole (1p1h) configurations, with cutoffs of 60 MeV (SGII) and 70 MeV (SLy4) for 2 particle-2 hole (2p2h) configurations, and a diagonal approximation was adopted in the 2p2h sector of the subtractive term. The dipole strength distributions and electric dipole polarizability were computed and compared with experimental data from ($\gamma,\gamma'$) reactions for the low-lying region and ($p,p'$) reactions for the giant dipole resonance region.

## Key numbers

- Experimental summed $B(E1)$ between 5 and 10 MeV: 0.068 $\pm$ 0.008 e$^2$ fm$^2$; SSRPA with SGII gives 0.078 e$^2$ fm$^2$, while standard SRPA gives 0.563 e$^2$ fm$^2$ (eight times larger)
- Experimental summed $\sum_i E_i B_i(E1)$ between 5 and 10 MeV: 0.570 $\pm$ 0.062 MeV e$^2$ fm$^2$; SSRPA with SGII gives 0.621 MeV e$^2$ fm$^2$, while standard SRPA gives 4.618 MeV e$^2$ fm$^2$
- Experimental centroid energy $E_C$ of the giant dipole resonance: 18.9 $\pm$ 0.2 MeV; SSRPA with SGII gives 17.4 MeV (underestimation of approximately 1.5 MeV)
- Experimental width $\Gamma$ of the giant dipole resonance: 3.9 $\pm$ 0.4 MeV; SSRPA with SGII gives 2.5 MeV
- Experimental contribution to electric dipole polarizability between 10 and 25 MeV: 1.73 $\pm$ 0.18 fm$^3$
- RPA centroid energy with SGII: 18.6 MeV; SRPA centroid with SGII: 13.5 MeV (too much shifted downward)
- With SLy4: SSRPA centroid energy 16.2 MeV, width 2.1 MeV (more than 2.5 MeV lower than experimental centroid)

## Context

This work extends previous SRPA studies of $^{48}$Ca by Gambacurta et al. (2015) that suffered from a too strong downward shift of the spectrum and overestimation of low-lying transition probabilities, and by applying the subtraction procedure developed by Tselyaev (2013) it resolves those issues. The results contradict the inability of standard RPA and ab-initio coupled-cluster calculations (Hagen et al., 2015) to reproduce the spreading width of the giant dipole resonance and the smooth increase of the dipole polarizability around the centroid energy. This work enables more reliable predictions of the dipole polarizability in neutron-rich nuclei, which has implications for constraining the symmetry energy, neutron-skin thickness, and neutron star properties.
