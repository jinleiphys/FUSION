# Microscopic modeling of mass and charge distributions in the spontaneous fission of 240Pu

> In this letter, we outline a methodology to calculate microscopically mass and charge distributions of spontaneous fission yields. We combine the multi-dimensional minimization of collective action for fission with stochastic Langevin dynamics to track the relevant fission paths from the ground-state configuration up to scission. The nuclear potential energy and collective inertia governing the tunneling motion are obtained with nuclear density functional theory in the collective space of shape deformations and pairing. We obtain a quantitative agreement with experimental data and find that both the charge and mass distributions in the spontaneous fission of 240Pu are sensitive both to the dissipation in collective motion and to adiabatic characteristics.

# Microscopic modeling of mass and charge distributions in the spontaneous fission of 240Pu

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that mass and charge distributions in spontaneous fission of 240Pu can be predicted microscopically by combining density functional theory with Langevin dynamics, but the quantitative agreement with experiment depends critically on including both dissipation in collective motion and the full non-perturbative cranking inertia tensor; the distributions are sensitive to both dissipative and adiabatic characteristics, not solely to the potential energy surface.

## Method

The authors compute the nuclear potential energy surface and collective inertia tensor using the Skyrme SkM* energy density functional with a density-dependent mixed-type pairing interaction, solved via symmetry-unrestricted Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations in a 3D+3D collective space of quadrupole moments (Q20, Q22), octupole moment (Q30), and dynamic pairing fluctuations (λ2). They minimize the WKB action integral along multidimensional fission paths using dynamic programming to obtain penetration probabilities and a hypersurface of outer turning points, then propagate the system from each outer turning point to scission by solving dissipative Langevin equations with a constant dissipation tensor, sampling 900 outer turning points with 100 trajectories each to build yield distributions.

## Key numbers

- The experimental spontaneous fission half-life of 240Pu is reproduced with a collective ground-state energy E0 = 0.97 MeV, close to the 1 MeV value assumed in prior work.
- The standard assault frequency adopted is n = 10^20.38 s^-1.
- The level density parameter is a = A/10 MeV^-1, where A = 240.
- The Gaussian convolution width for particle number fluctuations at scission is σ = 3 for mass number A and σ = 2 for charge number Z.
- The dissipation tensor values used for the best agreement are η11 = 50 ħ, η22 = 40 ħ, and η12 = 0.
- The uncertainty band for dissipation tensor variations spans η11, η22 in the range [30 ħ, 400 ħ] with η11/η22 between 1 and 1.25, and η12 in [0, 30 ħ].
- The uncertainty band for E0 spans 0.7 MeV to 1.2 MeV, which changes the half-life by over two orders of magnitude but minimally affects yield distributions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DFT-based studies of spontaneous fission pathways (Sadhukhan 2013) and pairing dynamics (Sadhukhan 2014) to the first microscopic prediction of fragment yield distributions. It contrasts with empirical scission-point models (Wilkins 1976) and the Brownian shape-motion approach applied to induced fission (Randrup 2011, 2013; Moller 2015), by incorporating self-consistent effective interactions, explicit action minimization rather than static paths, and full inertial effects in both tunneling and post-tunneling dynamics. The results enable future benchmarking of theoretical spontaneous fission models against yield distributions as observables (Bertsch 2015) and confirm that the topology of the potential energy surface in the pre-scission region determines the most probable splits, consistent with prior DFT studies (Staszczak 2009; Warda 2012; McDonnell 2013, 2014; Scamps 2015).
