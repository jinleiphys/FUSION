# eta-^4He Bound States in the Skyrme Model

> The rational map ansatz for light nuclei in the Skyrme model is shown to imply the existence of an η-^4He bound state, with a binding energy of ~ 30 KeV.

## Key claim

Using the rational map ansatz for light nuclei in the Skyrme model, the authors predict an η-⁴He bound state with a binding energy of approximately 30 keV, but note that the binding energy varies from 30 keV to 1.3 MeV depending on the parameter set used, and that the 30 keV estimate is considered more realistic.

## Method

The authors employed Pari's version of the Skyrme model with an SU(3) extension of the skyrmion field, using the rational map ansatz for the α-particle (baryon number n=4) with the specific rational map R₄(z) = (z⁴ + 2√3 i z² + 1)/(z⁴ - 2√3 i z² + 1). They expanded the Lagrangian density to second order in the η field to obtain a wave equation for the η-meson in the skyrmion field, then solved this equation numerically for baryon numbers A=3-5 using three different parameter sets for the pion decay constant fπ and the inverse strength parameter e.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of η-⁴He: 30 keV (parameter set 1, fπ=64.5 MeV, e=5.45, mπ=0) and 30 keV (parameter set 2, fπ=54 MeV, e=4.84, mπ=138 MeV)
- Binding energy of η-⁴He with parameter set 3 (fπ=93 MeV, e=4.25, mπ=138 MeV): 1.3 MeV
- Binding energy of η-⁵He: 1.1 MeV (set 1), 0.5 MeV (set 2), 3.2 MeV (set 3)
- Binding energy of η-³He: 0.02 MeV (set 3 only; no bound state found with sets 1 and 2)
- η-nucleon scattering length used to determine coupling: aηN = 0.717 ± 0.030
- Ratio fη/fπ = 1.28 used in calculations
- Kaon mass: mK = 495 MeV

## Context

This work extends the rational map method for multiskyrmions, previously applied to strange multiskyrmions by Schvellinger and Scoccola (1998), to the study of η-meson binding with light nuclei. The prediction supports the long-standing expectation of η-⁴He bound states raised by Bhalerao and Liu (1985) and Haider and Liu (1986), and is consistent with recent experimental results on η-nuclear interactions from Calén et al. (1998). The authors note that precision experiments at cooler ring facilities are expected to settle the issue definitively.
