# Systematic Study of High p_T Hadron Spectra in pp, pA and AA Collisions from SPS to RHIC Energies

> High-$p_T$ particle spectra in $p+p$ ($\bar p + p$), $p+A$ and $A+B$ collisions are calculated within a QCD parton model in which intrinsic transverse momentum, its broadening due to initial multiple parton scattering, and jet quenching due to parton energy loss inside a dense medium are included phenomenologically. The intrinsic $k_T$ and its broadening in $p+A$ and $A+B$ collisions due to initial multiple parton scattering are found to be very important at low energies ($\sqrt{s}<50$ GeV). Comparisons with $S+S$, $S+Au$ and $Pb+Pb$ data with different centrality cuts show that the differential cross sections of large transverse momentum pion production ($p_T>1$ GeV/$c$) in $A+B$ collisions scale very well with the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions (modulo effects of multiple initial scattering). This indicates that semi-hard parton scattering is the dominant particle production mechanism underlying the hadron spectra at moderate $p_T \stackrel{>}{\sim} 1$ GeV/$c$. However, there is no evidence of jet quenching or parton energy loss. Within the parton model, one can exclude an effective parton energy loss $dE_q/dx>0.01$ GeV/fm and a mean free path $λ_q< 7$ fm from the experimental data of $A+B$ collisions at the SPS energies. Predictions for high $p_T$ particle spectra in $p+A$ and $A+A$ collisions with and without jet quenching at the RHIC energy are also given. Uncertainties due to initial multiple scattering and nuclear shadowing of parton distributions are also discussed.

## Key claim

High-pT hadron spectra in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS energies scale with the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions, indicating semi-hard parton scattering as the dominant production mechanism, but there is no evidence of jet quenching or parton energy loss; the data can only exclude an effective parton energy loss above 0.01 GeV/fm and a mean free path below 7 fm.

## Method

The author calculated single-inclusive hadron spectra within a lowest-order QCD parton model, incorporating intrinsic transverse momentum with a Gaussian distribution, its broadening due to multiple initial-state parton scattering in pA and AA collisions, and phenomenological jet quenching via modified fragmentation functions. The model was compared to experimental data for pp, pA, S+S, S+Au, and Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, using a Glauber-based correlation between impact parameter and transverse energy to implement centrality cuts.

## Key numbers

- Intrinsic transverse momentum variance: ⟨k²_T⟩_N(Q²) = 1.2 GeV² + 0.2 α_s(Q²) Q², chosen to reproduce pp data from Elab = 200 to 800 GeV
- Scale-dependent kT broadening per nucleon-nucleon collision: δ²(Q²) = 0.225 ln²(Q/GeV) / [1 + ln(Q/GeV)] GeV²/c², chosen to fit pA data
- For Q = 2-3 GeV, δ² = 0.064 to 0.129 GeV²/c², consistent with J/Ψ production analysis
- Regulator μ = 0.8 GeV used in parton-parton cross section denominators
- K factor of approximately 2 used for pp and pA calculations; K = 1.5 used for pp̅ at collider energies
- Upper limit on effective parton energy loss: dE_q/dx < 0.01 GeV/fm from SPS heavy-ion data
- Lower limit on mean free path: λ_q > 7 fm from SPS heavy-ion data

## Context

This work extends the QCD parton model approach of Owens (1987) and earlier phenomenological studies of intrinsic kT in Drell-Yan and heavy quark production to systematically describe high-pT hadron spectra across pp, pA, and AA collisions from SPS to RHIC energies. The analysis provides a baseline for future studies of jet quenching at RHIC, where the author predicts spectra both with and without parton energy loss, and discusses uncertainties from initial multiple scattering and nuclear shadowing of parton distributions as parameterized in the HIJING model.
