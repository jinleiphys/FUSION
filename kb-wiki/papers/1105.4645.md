# Second order hydrodynamic coefficients from 3-point stress tensor correlators via AdS/CFT

> We study second order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics in 4-dimensional conformal field theories. We derive Kubo-type relations for second order hydrodynamic coefficients in terms of 3-point stress tensor retarded correlators. For N=4 super Yang-Mills theory at strong coupling and at finite temperature we compute these stress tensor 3-point correlators, using AdS/CFT, by evaluating real-time cubic Witten diagrams in the AdS-Schwarzschild background. The small momentum expansion of the 3-point correlators in terms of first and second order hydrodynamic coefficients is matched with the AdS result. We arrive at the same expressions for the hydrodynamic coefficients which multiply terms quadratic in the shear and vorticity tensors in the hydrodynamic expansion of the stress tensor as did Bhattacharyya, Hubeny, Minwalla and Rangamani \cite{Bhattacharyya:2008jc}. Our method extends the results of Baier et al \cite{Baier:2007ix}, and allows for a unified treatment of hydrodynamic coefficients, which are extracted from 2-, and now, 3-point retarded stress tensor correlators in the AdS-Schwarzschild background.

# Second order hydrodynamic coefficients from 3-point stress tensor correlators via AdS/CFT

## Key claim

The authors derive Kubo-type relations that express the second-order hydrodynamic coefficients λ₁, λ₂, and λ₃ of a conformal fluid in terms of three-point retarded stress tensor correlators, and compute these coefficients for N=4 super Yang-Mills theory at strong coupling and finite temperature using AdS/CFT. The method reproduces the same expressions previously obtained by Bhattacharyya, Hubeny, Minwalla and Rangamani (2008), but does so without deforming the gravitational background, instead computing higher-order correlators in the fixed AdS-Schwarzschild background.

## Method

The authors first derive Kubo relations for λ₁, λ₂, and λ₃ by expanding the stress tensor response to metric perturbations in two ways: (i) solving the stress tensor conservation law order-by-order in gradients and metric fluctuations, and (ii) expanding in retarded correlators via the response formula. They then compute the relevant three-point retarded stress tensor correlators in the AdS-Schwarzschild background dual to finite-temperature strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills theory, using real-time cubic Witten diagrams with two advanced and one retarded graviton bulk-to-boundary propagators joined at a bulk vertex integrated up to the black hole horizon. The small momentum expansion of the three-point correlators is matched to the hydrodynamic expansion to extract the coefficients.

## Key numbers

- λ₁ = N_c² T² / 16 (for N=4 super Yang-Mills at strong coupling)
- λ₂ = − N_c² T² ln 2 / 8
- λ₃ = 0
- Background energy density: ⟨T⁰⁰⟩₀ = 3 N_c² / (2⁷ π²) in units where 2πT = 1 (restoring units: ε̄ = 3π² N_c² T⁴ / 8)
- Background pressure: ⟨Tⁱʲ⟩₀ = δⁱʲ N_c² / (2⁷ π²) (restoring units: P̄ = π² N_c² T⁴ / 8)
- Shear viscosity η appears in the diffusion constant D_η = η / (ε̄ + P̄)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Baier et al (2007), who used Kubo-like formulae from two-point stress tensor correlators to access a subset of second-order hydrodynamic coefficients. The authors' method allows a unified treatment of hydrodynamic coefficients extracted from both two-point and three-point retarded stress tensor correlators in the AdS-Schwarzschild background. The results agree with those of Bhattacharyya, Hubeny, Minwalla and Rangamani (2008), who used a generalized black brane metric ansatz and solved Einstein equations perturbatively. The technique enables computation of second-order coefficients in other settings, such as at finite chemical potential or with fundamental matter, and provides a framework for investigating the breakdown of second-order hydrodynamics.
