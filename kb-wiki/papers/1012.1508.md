# The Chiral Magnetic Effect: Beam-energy and system-size dependence

> We consider the energy dependence of the local ${\cal P}$ and ${\cal CP}$ violation in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions over a large energy range within a simple phenomenological model. It is expected that at LHC the chiral magnetic effect will be about 20 times weaker than at RHIC. At lower energy range this effect should vanish sharply at energy somewhere above the top SPS one. To elucidate CME background effects a transport model including magnetic field evolution is put forward.

# The Chiral Magnetic Effect: Beam-energy and system-size dependence

## Key claim
The chiral magnetic effect (CME) in heavy-ion collisions is predicted to be about 20 times weaker at LHC than at RHIC, and to vanish sharply at an energy somewhere above the top SPS energy; however, these predictions depend sensitively on the assumed critical magnetic field threshold and on the definition of the lifetime during which both strong magnetic fields and deconfined matter coexist.

## Method
The authors use a phenomenological model based on the saturation momentum Q_s and sphaleron transition rates to relate the topological charge (winding number) to the measured charge-separation correlator a. They extract the lifetime of the magnetic field τ_B from UrQMD simulations of the time evolution of the magnetic field B_y and energy density ε in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions, then compute the correlator a^2 as a function of beam energy and centrality. A kinetic transport approach based on the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) model with retarded Lienard-Wiechert potentials is introduced for future background studies.

## Key numbers
- At LHC energy (√s_NN = 4.5 × 10^3 GeV), the predicted correlator a^2 = 0.016 × 10^(-4) for Au+Au at b = 10 fm with eB_crit = 0.2 m_π^2, which is a factor of about 20 weaker than at RHIC (a^2 = 0.31 × 10^(-3) at √s_NN = 200 GeV).
- The measured same-charge correlator ⟨cos(ψ_α+ψ_β-2Ψ_RP)⟩ for Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV, b = 10 fm (40-50% centrality) is -(0.312 ± 0.027) × 10^(-3).
- The magnetic field strength eB_y in Au+Au collisions at b = 10 fm decreases by a factor of about 10 from √s_NN = 200 GeV to E_lab = 11 GeV, while the energy density ε decreases by a factor of 250.
- For Au+Au at b = 10 fm with eB_crit = 0.2 m_π^2, the magnetic field lifetime τ_B ranges from 0.018 fm/c at LHC to 1.66 fm/c at √s_NN = 11 GeV, while the deconfined matter lifetime τ_ε remains approximately 2 fm/c.
- The correlator ratio a^2(200)/a^2(62) = 0.387 from the model, compared to the experimental ratio of approximately 0.72, requiring adjustment of the effective lifetime definition.
- The almond-shaped overlap area ratio S_Cu(b=4.2)/S_Au(b=10) ≈ 1.65 is consistent with the experimental CME enhancement for Cu+Cu collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological CME framework of Kharzeev (2004) and the STAR Collaboration measurements (2009) by providing quantitative beam-energy and system-size predictions across a wide range from LHC to FAIR/NICA energies. The authors use the UrQMD model (Bass, 1998) for magnetic field evolution following the method of Skokov, Illarionov, and Toneev (2009). The results enable experimental tests through the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program and LHC measurements, and the proposed HSD-based kinetic approach offers a path toward dynamical background subtraction for CME signals.
