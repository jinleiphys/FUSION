# Relativistic spin dynamics for vector mesons

> We propose a relativistic theory for spin density matrices of vector mesons based on Kadanoff-Baym equations in the closed-time-path formalism. The theory puts the calculation of spin observables such as the spin density matrix element $ρ_{00}$ for vector mesons on a solid ground. Within the theory we formulate $ρ_{00}$ for $ϕ$ mesons into a factorization form in separation of momentum and space-time variables. We argue that the main contribution to $ρ_{00}$ at lower energies should be from the $ϕ$ fields that can polarize the strange quark and antiquark in the same way as electromagnetic fields. The key observation is that there is correlation inside the $ϕ$ meson wave function between the $ϕ$ field that polarizes the strange quark and that polarizes the strange antiquark. This is reflected by the fact that the contributions to $ρ_{00}$ are all in squares of fields which are nonvanishing even if the fields may strongly fluctuate in space-time. The fluctuation of strong force fields can be extracted from $ρ_{00}$ of quarkonium vector mesons as links to fundamental properties of quantum chromodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a relativistic theory for spin density matrices of vector mesons based on Kadanoff-Baym equations, which puts the calculation of spin observables such as $\rho_{00}$ on a solid ground; the main contribution to $\rho_{00}$ at lower energies is argued to come from $\phi$ fields that polarize strange quarks and antiquarks, with the key observation that contributions to $\rho_{00}$ are all in squares of fields which remain nonvanishing even if the fields strongly fluctuate in space-time.

## Method

The authors derive Kadanoff-Baym equations for vector mesons in the closed-time-path formalism, starting from two-point Green functions for massive spin-1 particles described by the Proca equation. They then obtain a spin Boltzmann equation for the matrix-valued spin dependent distributions of vector mesons, expressed in terms of quark and antiquark distributions with polarization four-vectors. The spin density matrix element $\rho_{00}$ for $\phi$ mesons is formulated through a factorization form separating momentum and space-time variables, evaluated using a quark coalescence model with a Bethe-Salpeter wave function parametrized by a width parameter $\sigma\approx0.522$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- The width parameter of the Bethe-Salpeter wave function for the $\phi$ meson is $\sigma\approx0.522$ GeV.
- The spin density matrix element $\rho_{00}=1/3$ corresponds to equally populated spin states (no polarization), while $\rho_{00}\neq1/3$ indicates spin alignment.
- The STAR collaboration measured $\rho_{00}$ for $\phi(1020)$ mesons in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, with results consistent with $1/3$ within errors; recent STAR measurements at lower energies show a significant deviation from $1/3$.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic quark coalescence model for vector meson spin density matrices proposed by Sheng et al. (2019) to a fully relativistic framework based on Kadanoff-Baym equations. The theory provides a foundation for interpreting recent STAR measurements of $\phi$ meson $\rho_{00}$ at lower energies (STAR 2022), which cannot be explained by conventional mechanisms such as those in Yang et al. (2017), Xia et al. (2020), Gao et al. (2021), or Muller and others (2021). The formalism enables extraction of strong force field fluctuations from $\rho_{00}$ of quarkonium vector mesons, linking to fundamental properties of quantum chromodynamics.
