# Formal Theory of Heavy Ion Double Charge Exchange Reactions

> The theory of heavy ion double charge exchange (DCE) reactions $A(Z,N)\to A(Z\pm 2,N\mp 2)$ is recapitulated emphasizing the role of Double Single Charge Exchange (DSCE) and pion-nucleon Majorana DCE (MDCE) reactions. DSCE reactions are of second--order distorted wave character, mediated by isovector nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions. The DSCE response functions resemble the nuclear matrix elements (NME) of $2ν2β$ decay. The MDCE process proceeds by a dynamically generated effective rank-2 isotensor interaction, defined by off--shell pion--nucleon DCE scattering. In closure approximation pion potentials and two--nucleon correlations are obtained, similar to the neutrino potentials and the intranuclear exchange of Majorana neutrinos in $0ν2 β$ Majorana double beta decay (MDBD).

# Formal Theory of Heavy Ion Double Charge Exchange Reactions

## Key claim

Heavy ion double charge exchange (DCE) reactions are governed by two competing and interfering collisional mechanisms, Double Single Charge Exchange (DSCE) and pion-nucleon Majorana DCE (MDCE), rather than by sequential nucleon pair transfer, but the relative magnitude and coherence of these two mechanisms remain to be fully determined because the MDCE cross section shown is preliminary, neglecting non-diagonal pion potentials and not yet taking into account the coherence of the MDCE and DSCE amplitudes.

## Method

The authors formulate DSCE as a second-order distorted wave reaction mediated by isovector nucleon-nucleon T-matrices, evaluated in pole approximation with intermediate SCE states described by Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA). The MDCE mechanism is treated as a dynamically generated effective rank-2 isotensor interaction arising from off-shell pion-nucleon DCE scattering, with the pion potentials evaluated in closure approximation using dipole form factors with a cutoff of 2000 MeV/c and including resonances up to 2000 MeV. Theoretical angular distributions for the reaction 40Ca(18O, 18Ne)40Ar at 275 MeV are compared to NUMEN data.

## Key numbers

- The reaction 40Ca(18O, 18Ne)40Ar was measured at T_lab = 275 MeV; theoretical DSCE and MDCE cross sections are compared to NUMEN data from Cappuzzello (2015).
- The pion rest mass m_pi ~ 139 MeV defines the natural separation scale for closure approximation, valid when intermediate state energies omega_CA << m_pi.
- Pion potentials are regularized by dipole form factors with cutoff Lambda = 2000 MeV/c; resonances up to the mass region of 2000 MeV must be included for converged below-threshold results.
- MDCE pion potentials are shown for collinear momenta p1 = p2 = 50 MeV/c and p1 = p2 = 500 MeV/c; the angular region covered in the data corresponds to linear momentum transfers up to 500 MeV/c.
- The theoretical angular distribution is folded with the experimental angular resolution of ±0.6 degrees.

## Context

This work extends the collisional SCE reaction framework of Lenske (2018, 2019) to DCE reactions, contradicting the earlier hypothesis that sequential proton and neutron pair transfers dominate, which was questioned by Cappuzzello (2022) showing pair transfer suppression especially in mass-asymmetric systems. The DSCE response functions are shown to resemble the nuclear matrix elements of 2nu2beta decay, while the MDCE pion potentials and two-nucleon correlations are similar to the neutrino potentials and intranuclear exchange of Majorana neutrinos in 0nu2beta Majorana double beta decay. The formalism enables spectroscopic studies of rank-2 polarization tensors, which also occur in double-gamma decay as discussed by Soderstrom (2020).
