# Physical Significance of q Deformation and Many-Body Interactions in Nuclei

> The quantum deformation concept is applied to a study of pairing correlations in nuclei with mass 40<A<100. While the nondeformed limit of the theory provides a reasonable overall description of certain nuclear properties and fine structure effects, the results show that the q deformation plays a significant role in understanding higher-order effects in the many-body interaction.

## Key claim

The paper shows that q-deformation of the Sp(4) algebraic model introduces higher-order many-body interactions beyond the standard two-body pairing force, and that the deformation parameter varies smoothly with proton and neutron numbers, suggesting the deformation has physical significance related to the nature of the nuclear interaction itself rather than being merely a fitting parameter.

## Method

The authors extend the Sp(4) dynamical symmetry model for isovector pairing correlations by applying a quantum (q-) deformation to the sp(4) algebra, realized through q-deformed fermion operators. They fix the global two-body strength parameters to values previously determined for the nondeformed model and allow only the deformation parameter (varkappa, where q = e^varkappa) to vary per nucleus, solving for the minimum eigenvalue of the q-deformed Hamiltonian to match experimental energies of 0+ isobaric analog states in even-even nuclei with mass 40 ≤ A ≤ 100. A parametrized functional form for varkappa(N, T_0) is then fitted to the experimental data using only four free parameters.

## Key numbers

- In the fpg shell (1f5/2 2p1/2 2p3/2 1g9/2), the q-deformed model yields SOS_q = 130.21 MeV^2 (chi_q = 1.28 MeV), compared to the nondeformed theory SOS = 271.63 MeV^2 (chi = 1.79 MeV), showing a clear improvement.
- The four fitted parameters for the varkappa(N, T_0) function in the fpg shell are: xi_1 = -2.13, xi_2 = 0.37, xi_3 = 3.07, xi_4 = 0.15.
- The energy contribution of the four-body interaction in the expansion of the q-deformed Hamiltonian can reach several MeV in nuclei in the fpg shell.
- The global parameters used for the fpg shell (in MeV) are: epsilon = 13.851, G/Omega = 0.296, F/Omega = 0.056, E/(2Omega) = -0.489, D = -0.307, C = 0.190.
- For the 1f7/2 shell, the global parameters (in MeV) are: epsilon = 13.149, G/Omega = 0.453, F/Omega = 0.072, E/(2Omega) = -1.120, D = 0.149, C = 0.473.

## Context

This work extends the nondeformed Sp(4) dynamical symmetry model for isovector pairing correlations previously developed by Sviratcheva et al. (2002, 2003), which provided a reasonable description of 0+ isobaric analog states in 136 nuclei. The q-deformed approach addresses the need for including higher-order many-body interactions, as noted by Navratil et al. (2003), while retaining the exact solvability of the algebraic model. The results enable future studies of many-body effects in nuclear pairing phenomena and suggest the approach is model-independent, potentially applicable to other nuclear structure problems.
