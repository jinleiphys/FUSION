# Criteria for nonlinear parameters of relativistic mean field models

> Based on the properties of the critical and the actual effective masses of sigma and omega mesons, criteria to estimate the values of the isoscalar nonlinear terms of the standard relativistic mean field model that reproduce stable equations of state in respect to particle hole excitation at high densities are derived. The relation between nuclear matter stability and the symmetric nuclear matter properties are shown. The criteria are used to analyze in a more systematic way the high-density longitudinal and transverse instabilities of some parameter sets of relativistic mean field models. The critical role of the vector and vector-scalar nonlinear terms is also discussed quantitatively.

# Criteria for nonlinear parameters of relativistic mean field models

## Key claim

The paper derives criteria for the isoscalar nonlinear parameters of standard relativistic mean field (RMF) models that produce equations of state stable against particle-hole excitation at high densities, but the criteria are based on using "standard" average values of the coupling constants g_sigma and g_omega; for parameter sets where these couplings deviate appreciably from the standard values, the critical nonlinear parameter values can be smaller or larger.

## Method

The authors use the ratio of critical effective meson masses (m*^2_c) to actual effective meson masses (m*^2) for sigma and omega mesons as a stability criterion, where m*^2_c is obtained from the condition that the longitudinal and transverse dielectric functions vanish at zero energy transfer. They generate test parameter sets with nearly identical g_sigma and g_omega but varying b_3 (the quartic scalar nonlinear parameter), adjusting b_2 and c_3 to maintain standard symmetric nuclear matter properties at saturation, and then apply the criteria to analyze existing S-RMF, V-RMF, and E-RMF parameter sets.

## Key numbers

- For the standard RMF model to be free of transverse instability at high densities, the quartic vector nonlinear parameter must satisfy c_3 > 71, which corresponds to b_3 close to or larger than zero.
- To push longitudinal instability to unphysical densities, b_3 >= 20 is required, which in the test parameter sets corresponds to c_3 >= 160.
- With b_3 >= 20, the critical density for longitudinal instability can be pushed to rho_c >= 23 rho_0, where rho_0 is the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter.
- For typical high-density applications (neutron stars, supernova matter), the maximum density is 10 rho_0, so b_3 slightly lower than 20 may be sufficient.
- For the test parameter sets, b_3 <= -28 generates an additional onset of longitudinal instability due to the effective sigma meson mass becoming imaginary.
- The mixing nonlinear terms in E-RMF models contribute at most about 2.5% (lambda_1) and 15% (lambda_2) of the total contributions to the critical effective sigma meson mass.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous letter (Sulaksono et al., 2007) which studied high-density instabilities of RMF parameter sets, by providing quantitative criteria for the nonlinear parameters. The criteria are used to systematically analyze parameter sets including NLZ, NL3, NLSH, NL2 (S-RMF models), NLSV1, NLSV2, TM1, PK1, Z271, FSUGold (V-RMF models), and G1, G2, FSUZ003, FSUZ006 (E-RMF models with mixing terms). The analysis shows that parameter sets with stable high-density equations of state are consistent with experimental flow data from Danielewicz et al. (2002) and microscopic calculations from Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998). The criteria provide practical guidance for future RMF parameterizations aimed at high-density applications such as neutron star and supernova matter.
