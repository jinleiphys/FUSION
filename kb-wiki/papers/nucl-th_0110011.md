# Nuclear Effects on Bremsstrahlung Neutrino Rates of Astrophysical Interest

> We calculate in this work the rates for the neutrino pair production by nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung taking into account the full contribution from a nuclear one-pion-exchange potential. It is shown that if the temperatures are low enough ($T \leq 20 MeV$), the integration over the nuclear part can be done for the general case, ranging from the completely degenerate (D) to the non-degenerate (ND) regime. We find that the inclusion of the full nuclear contribution enhances the neutrino pair production by $nn$ and $pp$ bremsstrahlung by a factor of about two in both the D and ND limits when compared with previous calculations. This result may be relevant for the physical conditions of interest in the semitransparent regions near the neutrinosphere in type II supernovae, cooling of neutron stars and other astrophysical situations.

# Nuclear Effects on Bremsstrahlung Neutrino Rates of Astrophysical Interest

## Key claim

The inclusion of the full momentum-dependent one-pion-exchange (OPE) nuclear potential enhances neutrino pair production from nn and pp bremsstrahlung by a factor of about two compared to previous calculations that used a constant matrix element, but only under the condition that the temperature is below approximately 20 MeV, where the integral over the nuclear part collapses to an angle-independent form.

## Method

The authors computed the total volumetric neutrino emissivity from nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung using the full spin-summed matrix element for the OPE potential, expressed in terms of direct and exchange momentum transfers. They introduced a center-of-mass coordinate transformation and separated the matrix element into a constant term (the high-momentum limit) and a correction term, then performed the angular integration analytically after showing that a term proportional to (2mT)^4 can be neglected when T < 20 MeV. The remaining integrals over nucleon and neutrino energies were evaluated numerically for both the completely degenerate (D) and non-degenerate (ND) limits.

## Key numbers

- The enhancement factor for nn (or pp) neutrino emissivity relative to the constant-matrix-element calculation of Thompson, Burrows and Horvath (2000) is 1.065 in the ND limit (equation 11).
- The enhancement factor for nn (or pp) neutrino emissivity relative to the same reference is 1.0578 in the D limit (equation 12).
- The temperature condition for the approximation to hold is T < m_pi^2 / m, which is approximately 20 MeV.
- The correction term B_3 in the matrix element can be neglected because its inclusion changes the integrated result by less than 2%.
- The "true" bremsstrahlung emissivity is about 2/3 of its high-momentum limit, corresponding to a fudge factor xi of about 2.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of neutrino bremsstrahlung rates that used either a constant matrix element (Thompson, Burrows and Horvath 2000) or a Fermi liquid parametrization (Flowers, Sutherland and Bond 1975), and it improves upon OPE-based treatments that approximated the full momentum transfer dependence (Friman and Maxwell 1979; Hannestad and Raffelt 1998). The method enables more accurate computation of neutrino emissivities for conditions relevant to the semitransparent regions near the neutrinosphere in type II supernovae and cooling of neutron stars. The authors note that a detailed comparison with the model-independent analysis of Hanhart, Phillips and Reddy remains to be performed, and that medium correlations (Hannestad and Raffelt 1995; Burrows and Sawyer 1998) may further affect the rates.
