# The octupole collective Hamiltonian. Does it follow the example of the quadrupole case?

> A general form of the octupole collective Hamiltonian is introduced and analyzed based on fundamental tensors in the seven-dimensional tensor space. Possible definitions of intrinsic frames of reference possessing cubic symmetry for the octupole tensor are considered. Cubic intrinsic octupole coordinates or deformations are introduced. Shapes of the octupoloid are investigated. The octupole collective Hamiltonian is expressed in intrinsic coordinates. An intrinsic angular momentum carried by the octupole vibrations is discovered. Small oscillations about an axially-symmetric pear shape are analyzed. Formulation of a unified quadrupole-octupole collective model is discussed.

# The octupole collective Hamiltonian. Does it follow the example of the quadrupole case?

## Key claim

The octupole collective Hamiltonian is fundamentally more complicated than the quadrupole case, with the key difference being that octupole vibrations carry their own intrinsic angular momentum, a feature absent in quadrupole beta and gamma vibrations. The paper demonstrates that while the quadrupole inverse inertial bitensor has at most six independent components out of fifteen, the octupole case allows all twenty-eight components of the inverse inertial bitensor to be independent tensor fields, with no additional relations between them.

## Method

The authors construct the general form of the octupole collective Hamiltonian as a second-order differential operator in the seven-dimensional tensor space of octupole coordinates, following the same quantization procedure used for the Bohr quadrupole Hamiltonian. They decompose the octupole tensor representation of O(3) into cubic group irreps (A₂⁻, F₁⁻, F₂⁻) to define intrinsic frames with cubic symmetry, introduce cubic intrinsic coordinates (b, f_x, f_y, f_z), and express the Hamiltonian in these intrinsic coordinates by converting derivatives from laboratory to intrinsic variables. The analysis of small oscillations about an axially-symmetric pear shape (b = f_x = f_y = 0, f_z = ±f_eq) is performed using the simplest inverse inertial bitensor (the original Bohr form with constant mass parameter B₃).

## Key numbers

- The octupole symmetric bitensor B⁻¹_{3μ3ν} has 28 components, all of which can be independent tensor fields in the octupole collective space
- The octupole collective space is 7-dimensional (2λ+1 = 7 for λ=3), compared to 5-dimensional for quadrupole (λ=2)
- The octupole Hamiltonian in intrinsic coordinates contains 10 kinetic energy terms (4 separate vibrations and 6 rotational terms) for the simplest case, with up to 6 additional mixed vibrational terms and 12 vibration-rotation terms in the general case
- The Jacobian of the transformation from laboratory to intrinsic coordinates is D_f(ϑ, b, f_x, f_y, f_z) = 8 sinϑ [b(b² - (15/16)(f_x²+f_y²+f_z²)) + (15/8)√(15/16) f_x f_y f_z]
- The moment of inertia tensor for rotations around axes perpendicular to the symmetry axis is (15/4)B₃ f_eq² for the axially-symmetric pear shape
- The octupole deformation space has 4 deformation parameters (b, f_x, f_y, f_z) compared to 2 for quadrupole (β, γ)

## Context

This work extends the collective model formalism originally developed by Bohr (1952) for quadrupole excitations to the octupole (λ=3) case, which has been studied experimentally since Asaro (1953) and reviewed by Rohozinski (1988) and Butler (1996). The paper builds on earlier attempts to define intrinsic frames for the octupole tensor that had failed (Davies 1965), and on the authors' own prior work (Rohozinski 1990, 2017) proposing cubic intrinsic coordinates. The formalism enables the formulation of a unified quadrupole-octupole collective model, which is particularly relevant given recent measurements of static octupole deformation in ²²⁴Ra (Gaffney 2013) and ¹⁴⁴Ba (Bucher 2016).
