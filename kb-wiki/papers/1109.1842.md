# Tidal Waves -- a non-adiabatic microscopic description of the yrast states in near-spherical nuclei

> The yrast states of nuclei that are spherical or weakly deformed in their ground states are described as quadrupole waves running over the nuclear surface, which we call "tidal waves". The energies and E2 transition probabilities of the yrast states in nuclides with $Z$= 44, 46, 48 and $N=56, ~58,..., 66$ are calculated by means of the cranking model in a microscopic way. The nonlinear response of the nucleonic orbitals results in a strong coupling between shape and single particle degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The yrast states of near-spherical nuclei can be described as "tidal waves", quadrupole surface waves that run with a static deformation in the co-rotating frame, but the authors qualify that this simple picture does not account for the full complexity of real nuclei because single-particle degrees of freedom also provide angular momentum, and the separation of collective and single-particle motion becomes problematic for increasing spin and decreasing valence nucleon number.

## Method

The authors used the Shell Correction version of the Tilted Axis Cranking model (SCTAC) to calculate yrast-state energies and E2 transition probabilities for nuclides with Z=44, 46, 48 and N=56, 58, 60, 62, 64, 66. They solved the cranking condition ⟨J_x⟩=J=I numerically on a grid in the ε₂-γ deformation plane using a diabatic tracing technique, then minimized the interpolated energy E(ε₂,γ,J) with respect to deformation at fixed angular momentum. Pairing parameters were fixed to Δₙ=Δₚ=1.1 MeV, and a small correction term was added to the angular momentum to account for neglected couplings.

## Key numbers

- Pairing parameters fixed to Δₙ=Δₚ=1.1 MeV throughout the calculations.
- A correction term of 100 MeV⁻¹ ε₂² sin²(γ-π/3) ω was added to J(ω), contributing less than 0.5 ħ to the angular momentum.
- Ground-state energy calculations used a small adjustment k ε₂² with k values ranging from 7.9 MeV (N=58) to 14.5 MeV (N=66).
- Equilibrium deformations for I≥2 were γₑ=0° and 0.1<βₑ<0.2, except for ¹¹⁰,¹¹²Cd where γ=10°-15° for 2≤I≤8.
- In ¹⁰²Pd, the g-sequence (tidal wave) was observed up to I=20, with the I=16 state corresponding to eight stacked phonons.
- The avoided crossing between normal parity neutron orbitals occurred around ħω≈0.53 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier demonstration by Marshalek that RPA equations for harmonic quadrupole vibrations are a solution to the selfconsistent cranking model under small-deformation approximation, by solving the cranking problem numerically without that approximation. The authors also build on their own prior report (arXiv) which used a less accurate interpolation. The approach enables microscopic calculation of yrast states across the full range from harmonic vibrators to rigid rotors, and the results reproduce experimental energies and B(E2) values in detail for the mass-110 region, including the back-bending caused by rotational alignment of h₁₁/₂ neutron pairs as discussed by Hamamoto.
