# Dynamical Pion Production via Parametric Resonance from Disoriented Chiral Condensate

> We discuss a dynamical mechanism of pion production from disoriented chiral condensates (DCC). It leads to an explosive production of pions via the parametric amplification mechanism, which is similar to the reheating mechanism in inflationary cosmology. Classically, it is related with the instability in the solutions of the Mathieu equation and we explore the quantum aspects of the mechanism. We show that nonlinearlities and back reactions can be ignorable for sufficiently long time under the small amplitude approximations of background $σ$ oscillations, which may be appropriate for the late stage of nonequilibrium phase transition. It allows us to obtain an explicit quantum state of the produced pions and $σ$, the squeezed state of BCS type. Single particle distributions and two-pion correlation functions are computed within these approximations. The results obtained illuminate the characteristic features of multi-pion states produced through the parametric amplification mechanism. In particular, two-pion correlations of various charge combinations contain back-to-back correlations which cannot be masked by the identical particle interference effect.

# Dynamical Pion Production via Parametric Resonance from Disoriented Chiral Condensate

## Key claim

The authors propose that parametric resonance from oscillating background sigma fields, rather than the conventional rolling-down instability, may be the dominant mechanism for the long-lasting amplification of low-momentum pion modes in disoriented chiral condensate formation, but they explicitly state that this mechanism is not the whole story and that the initial instability from Nambu-Goldstone modes is still required to trigger the growth.

## Method

The authors work within the linear sigma model, expanding fields around the minimum of the Mexican hat potential along the sigma direction and decomposing them into classical time-dependent background fields and quantum fluctuations. Under the small-amplitude approximation for background sigma oscillations (|χ₀/v| ≪ 1), they neglect nonlinearities and back reactions, reducing the fluctuation equations to Mathieu equations. They then quantize the system using Bogoliubov transformations to obtain the squeezed state of BCS type and compute single-particle distributions and two-pion correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Model parameters: λ = 20, v₀ = 90 MeV, m_π = 140 MeV, m_σ = √(λ(3v² − v₀²))
- Background oscillation amplitude used in numerical analysis: |χ₀/v| = 0.05 (with factors of 1/2, 1, 2 for variation)
- q parameters: q_π = 2.0(χ₀/v), q_σ = 6.0(χ₀/v); numerically q_π ≲ 0.1 and q_σ ≲ 0.3 under the approximation (χ₀/v) < (m_π/m_σ)²
- First resonance for pions occurs at A_π = 1, corresponding to k = 276 MeV; second resonance at A_π = 4 gives k = 603 MeV
- First resonance for sigma occurs at A_σ = 4 at zero momentum; second resonance at A_σ = 9 gives k = 692 MeV
- Nonlinearities in pion fluctuations are negligible until dimensionless time z ∼ 67 for |χ₀/v| = 0.05
- Quantum back reactions are negligible until z ∼ 32 for |χ₀/v| = 0.05

## Context

This work extends the parametric resonance mechanism previously explored by Mrowczynski and Muller (1995) and the authors' own earlier work (1998) to the context of disoriented chiral condensate formation. It contrasts with the conventional DCC picture of Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993), who attributed low-momentum pion amplification to Nambu-Goldstone mode instability during rolling down from the top of the Mexican hat potential. The authors note that the enhancement in Rajagopal-Wilczek simulations occurs on a much longer timescale than the rolling-down motion (~1/m_σ), suggesting parametric resonance as the responsible mechanism. The work enables experimental hunting of DCC through characteristic two-pion back-to-back correlations that cannot be masked by identical particle interference effects.
