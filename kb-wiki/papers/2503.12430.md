# Axion-Like Particle Mediated Dark Matter and Neutron Star Properties in the QHD Model

> We investigate the effects of the ALP-mediated dark matter (DM) model on neutron star properties using the Quantum Hadrodynamics model (QHD). Using the relativistic mean-field approximation with the QHD-ALP-DM framework, we compute the equation of state (EoS) of neutron stars. Based on our previous study, we find that typical ALP parameter values have no significant effect on the EoS. We then explore various parametrizations of this model by varying the DM Fermi momentum, $q_f$, and DM mass, $m_χ$. Our results show that increasing $q_f$ or $m_χ$ shifts the energy density to higher values while reducing the maximum mass, radius, and tidal deformability of neutron stars. Finally, comparison with observational constraints from gravitational wave events and pulsar measurements indicates that the allowed parameter space for this model is constrained to $q_f < 0.05$ GeV and $m_χ < 1000$ GeV. As a result, our study highlights the importance of next-generation gamma-ray observatories, such as the Cherenkov Telescope Array (CTA), in probing the ALP-mediated DM model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that typical axion-like particle (ALP) parameter values have no significant effect on the neutron star equation of state, and that the allowed parameter space for the ALP-mediated dark matter model is constrained to DM Fermi momentum \(q_f < 0.05\) GeV and DM mass \(m_\chi < 1000\) GeV when compared with observational constraints from gravitational wave events and pulsar measurements.

## Method

The authors used the Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) model with the relativistic mean-field approximation to compute the equation of state of neutron stars containing ALP-mediated dark matter. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain the mass-radius relation and computed the tidal deformability by solving the perturbation equations for a static, spherically symmetric star. The analysis varied the DM Fermi momentum \(q_f\) from 0.02 GeV to 0.05 GeV and the DM mass \(m_\chi\) from 50 GeV to 1000 GeV, using the NL3 parameter set for the hadronic part with ALP mass \(m_a = 100\) GeV and couplings \(g_{aff} = g_{a\chi\chi} = 10^{-3}\).

## Key numbers

- The allowed parameter space is constrained to \(q_f < 0.05\) GeV and \(m_\chi < 1000\) GeV from comparison with observational constraints.
- For fixed DM mass \(m_\chi = 200\) GeV, results for \(q_f = 0.02\) GeV and 0.03 GeV satisfy all observational constraints, while \(q_f = 0.04\) GeV fails GW170817 constraints and \(q_f = 0.05\) GeV fails both GW170817 and PSR J0030+0451 constraints.
- For fixed DM mass \(m_\chi = 500\) GeV, only \(q_f = 0.02\) GeV is consistent with all observational constraints; \(q_f = 0.03\) GeV fails GW190814 constraints.
- For fixed \(q_f = 0.02\) GeV, all results for \(m_\chi = 50-1000\) GeV remain consistent with observational constraints.
- For fixed \(q_f = 0.04\) GeV, results for \(m_\chi = 50\) GeV and 100 GeV satisfy all observational constraints, while \(m_\chi = 500\) GeV fails GW190814 and PSR J0030+0451 constraints.
- The tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 gives \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 190^{+390}_{-120}\) and from GW190814 gives \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 616^{+273}_{-158}\); none of the results satisfy the GW190814 tidal deformability constraint.
- The ALP mean field was found to be between \(a_0 = 10^{-10}\) and \(10^{-8}\) for ALP mass \(m_a = 10^2 - 10^3\) GeV and couplings \(g_{aff} = g_{a\chi\chi} = 10^{-3} - 1\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Klangburam 2023) on ALP-mediated dark matter by applying the model to neutron star properties within the QHD framework. The results are compared with the Higgs-mediated dark matter model studied by Das (2018), finding that the ALP-mediated model yields slightly lower maximum mass and maximum radius than the Higgs-mediated case. The study enables future constraints from next-generation gamma-ray observatories such as the Cherenkov Telescope Array (CTA) to probe the ALP-mediated dark matter model.
