# Nuclear transparency of the charged hadron produced in the inclusive electronuclear reaction

> The nuclear transparency of the charged hadron produced in the inclusive $(e,e^\prime)$ reaction on the nucleus has been calculated using Glauber model for the nuclear reaction. The color transparency (CT) of the produced hadron and the short-range correlation (SRC) of the nucleons in the nucleus have been incorporated in the Glauber model to investigate their effects on the nuclear transparency of the hadron. The calculated nuclear transparencies for the proton and pion are compared with the data.

## Key claim

The nuclear transparency of the proton shows no color transparency effect for photon virtualities \(0.64 \le Q^2 \le 14.2\) GeV\(^2\), ruling out color transparency for the three-quark system; for the pion, the calculated transparency including color transparency agrees with the data, but the data available up to \(Q^2 = 4.69\) GeV\(^2\) cannot distinguish color transparency from short-range correlation effects, so higher-\(Q^2\) data are needed to confirm pionic color transparency.

## Method

The nuclear transparency \(T_A\) for the inclusive \((e,e^\prime)\) reaction was calculated using the Glauber model, with the hadron-nucleon total cross section \(\sigma_t^{hN}\) and the measured nuclear density distribution \(\varrho(r)\). Color transparency was incorporated via the quantum diffusion model, replacing the free-space \(\sigma_t^{hN}\) with a \(Q^2\)- and path-length-dependent cross section \(\sigma^{hN}_{t,CT}\); short-range correlation was incorporated by multiplying the nuclear density by a correlation function \(C(|z_j - z_i|)\) derived from nuclear matter estimates. The calculated transparencies for proton and \(\pi^+\) were compared with experimental data from SLAC and JLab.

## Key numbers

- Proton transparency: data cover \(0.64 \le Q^2 \le 14.2\) GeV\(^2\) for \(^{12}\)C, \(0.64 \le Q^2 \le 8.1\) GeV\(^2\) for \(^{56}\)Fe, and \(0.64 \le Q^2 \le 6.77\) GeV\(^2\) for \(^{197}\)Au; color transparency is ruled out in all cases.
- Pion transparency: data cover \(1.1 \le Q^2 \le 4.69\) GeV\(^2\) for \(^{12}\)C, \(^{27}\)Al, \(^{63}\)Cu, and \(^{197}\)Au; calculations are extended to \(Q^2 = 9.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- Hadron formation length: \(l_h = 2k_h / \Delta M^2\), with \(\Delta M^2\) taken as 0.7 and 1.4 GeV\(^2\) for the pion.
- Quark transverse momentum: \(\langle k_t^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 0.35\) GeV/\(c\).
- Fermi momentum: \(k_F = 1.36\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- Correlation function parameters: \(\alpha = 1.1\) fm\(^{-2}\), \(\beta = 0.68\) fm\(^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-model analysis of nuclear transparency by incorporating both color transparency and short-range correlation, following the formalism of Howell and others. It contradicts the expectation of color transparency for the proton, consistent with earlier A\((e,e^\prime p)\) experiments at SLAC (Neill) and JLab (Dutta, Bhetuwal), and supports the pionic color transparency interpretation of the JLab data (Clasie) as modeled by Kaskulov and Larson. The results motivate future JLab measurements of \(T_A(\pi^+)\) for \(5 \le Q^2 \le 9.5\) GeV\(^2\) to definitively confirm pionic color transparency.
