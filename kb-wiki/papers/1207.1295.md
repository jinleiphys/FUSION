# Effective theory for low-energy nuclear energy density functionals

> We introduce a new class of effective interactions to be used within the energy-density-functional approaches. They are based on regularized zero-range interactions and constitute a consistent application of the effective-theory methodology to low-energy phenomena in nuclei. They allow for defining the order of expansion in terms of the order of derivatives acting on the finite-range potential. Numerical calculations show a rapid convergence of the expansion and independence of results of the regularization scale.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new class of regularized zero-range pseudopotentials for nuclear energy-density-functional (EDF) approaches that provide a consistent effective-theory expansion with a controlled regularization scale, but the numerical demonstration is performed by fitting to the existing Gogny interaction rather than by direct optimization to experimental data.

## Method

The authors construct regularized pseudopotentials using Gaussian functions of range \(a\) to replace the delta interactions, with derivative operators acting on the regularized delta to define orders of expansion (NLO, N\(^2\)LO, N\(^3\)LO). They derive coupling constants for the regularized potential by matching its lowest moments to those of the finite-range Gogny D1S interaction, then implement the resulting EDF in the code HFODD to compute binding energies and radii for eight doubly magic nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy deviations relative to the Gogny interaction decrease by about a factor of four with each successive order; at N\(^3\)LO they are well below 1% for all eight doubly magic nuclei tested.
- The regularization scale \(a\) is varied; at \(a = 0.85\) fm, the reduced deviations \(\Delta b = \Delta B/A\) and \(\Delta r = \Delta R/A^{1/3}\) are plotted for nuclei from \(^{16}\)O to \(^{208}\)Pb.
- The characteristic scale \(\Lambda\) extracted from the coupling constants is approximately 700 MeV/\(\hbar c\) (3.5 fm\(^{-1}\)).
- The normalization factor \(f\) is about 35 MeV/(\(\hbar c)^{3/2}\), significantly smaller than the pion decay constant \(f_\pi \approx 93\) MeV/(\(\hbar c)^{3/2}\).
- The Gogny interaction uses two Gaussian ranges: \(a_1 = 0.7\) fm and \(a_2 = 1.2\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the quasilocal EDF approach based on higher-order derivatives of one-body densities (Car98, Car10e) and the zero-range pseudopotential framework (Rai11a) by introducing a regularization scale that enables a consistent power-counting scheme, analogous to the cutoff regularization proposed by Mog10 and Mog12. It enables future order-by-order optimization of EDFs directly to experimental data, with the potential for controlled and correctible expansions similar to those in chiral effective field theory for nuclear forces (Epe09, Epe10).
