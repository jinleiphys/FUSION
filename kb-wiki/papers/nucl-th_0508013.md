# The physical interest in kaonic- and antiprotonic-deuterium atoms

> Exotic deuterium and helium are discussed. The S, P and D levels of antiprotonic and kaonic atoms are calculated. Absorptive, subthreshold antiproton-nucleon amplitudes are extracted from experimental data and compared to model calculations. The existence of a quasi-bound state in the antiproton-nucleon system is indicated. In the kaonic atoms some effects of the Sigma(1385) resonance are evaluated.

# The physical interest in kaonic- and antiprotonic-deuterium atoms

## Key claim

The paper argues that subthreshold antiproton-nucleon amplitudes extracted from antiprotonic deuterium and helium atomic data indicate a quasi-bound state in the antiproton-nucleon system, specifically in a P-wave, and that the Sigma(1385) resonance may produce detectable effects in the upper-level widths of kaonic deuterium and helium atoms, though the chances of observing the 2P to 1S transition in deuterium are low.

## Method

The authors calculate S, P, and D atomic levels for antiprotonic and kaonic deuterium using a multiple scattering series summed into a quasi-geometric approximation, relating level shifts to hadron-nucleus scattering parameters via Trueman-type formulas. For antiprotonic atoms, they extract subthreshold antiproton-nucleon scattering lengths and volumes from experimental data on antiprotonic deuterium, helium-3, and helium-4, and compare these to an updated Paris potential model. For kaonic atoms, they evaluate the contribution of the Sigma(1385) resonance to upper-level widths using SU(3) coupling and S-wave amplitudes from the literature.

## Key numbers

- Antiprotonic deuterium 1S level shift: delta E = 1.05(25) keV, Gamma/2 = 0.55(37) keV (from Augsburger et al., 1999)
- Antiprotonic deuterium 2P level shift: delta E = 243(26) eV, Gamma/2 = 245(15) eV (from Augsburger et al., 1999)
- Extracted antiproton-deuteron S-wave scattering parameter: A_0 = 0.71(16) - i0.40(27) fm
- Extracted antiproton-deuteron P-wave scattering parameter: A_1 = 3.15(33) - i3.17(19) fm^(2L+1)
- Antiproton capture ratio on deuterium: R_n/p = 0.81(3) (from Bizzarri et al., 1974)
- Antiproton capture ratio on helium-3: R_n/p = 0.47(4) (from Balestra et al., 1989)
- Antiproton capture ratio on helium-4: R_n/p = 0.48(3) (from Balestra et al., 1989)
- Kaonic deuterium 1S level shift predictions vary by about 15% in width depending on parametrization: e.g., with constant scattering lengths, width = 1.02 keV; with K-matrix, width = 0.89 keV (from Deloff, 2000)
- Kaonic deuterium 2P absorptive width (S-wave only): 26 meV; with S+P wave including Sigma(1385): 32 meV
- Kaonic deuterium 3D absorptive width (S-wave only): 0.10 micro-eV; with S+P wave: 0.13 micro-eV
- Kaonic helium-4 3D absorptive width (S-wave only): 0.02 meV; with S+P wave: 0.038 meV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of antiprotonic atoms beyond the standard optical model description used for heavier nuclei, following experiments on antiprotonic hydrogen, deuterium, and helium by Augsburger (1999), Gotta (1999), and others. The authors compare their extracted subthreshold antiproton-nucleon amplitudes to an updated Paris potential model (2005), finding evidence for a quasi-bound P-wave state. For kaonic atoms, the paper addresses the long-standing question of the nature of the Lambda(1405) resonance, discussed by Dalitz and by Akaishi and Yamazaki, and evaluates the role of the Sigma(1385) resonance in light of the recent discovery of a nuclear KNNN state by Suzuki (2004). The results enable future extraction of subthreshold KN amplitudes when both kaonic deuterium and kaonic helium data become available.
