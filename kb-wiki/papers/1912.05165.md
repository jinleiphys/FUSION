# Nucleon-to-meson transition distribution amplitudes in impact parameter space

> Recent analyses of backward meson electroproduction support the validity of a collinear QCD factorization framework for these hard exclusive reactions. This opens a way to the extraction of nucleon-to-meson transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs) from the experimental data. Similarly to the generalized parton distributions, TDAs - after the Fourier transform in the transverse plane - carry valuable information on the transverse location of hadron constituents. We address the properties of integrated nucleon-to-meson TDAs in the impact parameter representation. We argue that the emerging picture provides an intuitive interpretation for the hadron structural information contained in nucleon-to-meson TDAs and allows to study diquark-quark contents of fast moving hadrons in the transverse plane.

## Key claim

The authors argue that nucleon-to-meson transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs), after integration over one momentum fraction variable and Fourier transform to impact parameter space, provide an intuitive picture of the diquark-quark content of fast-moving hadrons in the transverse plane, analogous to the Burkardt interpretation for generalized parton distributions (GPDs). However, unlike GPDs, the v-integrated TDAs do not possess a forward limit that allows a probabilistic interpretation, so the picture is in terms of probability amplitudes rather than probability densities.

## Method

The authors consider leading twist-3 proton-to-pion0 uud TDAs and integrate over the momentum fraction variable v3 = (x1 - x2)/2, which collapses two of the three quark field operators to the same light-cone point, yielding a bilocal diquark-quark operator. They then perform a Fourier transform of the resulting v3-integrated TDA combination with respect to the transverse momentum transfer D, obtaining a matrix element of the diquark-quark operator between localized hadron states with specified transverse positions b. The analysis is carried out for three kinematic regions: DGLAP-like I (w3 ≤ -ξ), DGLAP-like II (w3 ≥ ξ), and ERBL-like (-ξ ≤ w3 ≤ ξ).

## Key numbers

- The support domain for nucleon-to-meson TDAs in momentum fraction variables xi is given by the intersection of 3 stripes: -1+ξ ≤ xi ≤ 1+ξ, with the longitudinal momentum constraint ∑i xi = 2ξ.
- In quark-diquark coordinates (wi, vi), the support is parameterized as: -1 ≤ wi ≤ 1 and -1+|ξ-ξ'i| ≤ vi ≤ 1-|ξ-ξ'i| with ξ'i ≡ (ξ-wi)/2.
- The v3-integrated TDA combination HπN(w3, ξ, D) involves a factor (4MN2 - Δ2) and the combination (3MN2 + mπ2 - Δ2)V1πN + (-2Δ2 - 2MN2 + 2mπ2)V2πN, where MN is the nucleon mass and mπ is the pion mass.
- The impact parameter representation yields a normalization factor N-1(1+ξ2)/(1-ξ2)2 in the final expression.

## Context

This work extends the impact parameter space interpretation pioneered by Burkardt (2000, 2002) for GPDs in the zero-skewness limit, and by Ralston (2001) and Diehl (2002) for GPDs with non-zero skewness, to the case of nucleon-to-meson TDAs. The analysis is motivated by recent experimental studies at JLab (Park 2017, Li 2019) that support the validity of collinear QCD factorization for backward meson electroproduction. The results enable future extraction of nucleon-to-meson TDAs from experimental data and provide a framework for studying the transverse structure of hadrons in terms of diquark-quark configurations, complementing the low Fock component picture proposed by Pasquini (2009).
