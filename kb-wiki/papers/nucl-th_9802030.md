# Strange Phases in Neutron Stars

> The equation of state and the properties of neutron stars are studied for a phase transition to a charged kaon condensate. We study the mixed phase by using Gibbs condition with comparison to the hitherto applied Maxwell construction. Implications for kaon condensation and for the mass-radius relation of condensed neutron stars are examined.

## Key claim
The mixed phase of kaon-condensed neutron-star matter, treated via the Gibbs condition (equality of both baryochemical and electrochemical potentials), produces a smooth equation of state without the constant-pressure plateau of the Maxwell construction, but the maximum mass of the star changes only modestly (1.6–1.4 solar masses) for the plausible range of the kaon optical potential.

## Method
The authors use a relativistic mean-field model with minimal coupling of the kaon to scalar and vector meson fields. Vector coupling constants are fixed by quark counting rules (g_ωK = g_ωN/3, g_ρK = g_ρN), and the scalar coupling is set to reproduce a chosen K⁻ optical potential at normal nuclear density. The phase transition is treated by imposing Gibbs conditions (equal pressure, baryochemical potential, and electrochemical potential in both phases) rather than the simpler Maxwell construction, allowing a mixed phase with spatially separated regions of different local charge that globally neutralize.

## Key numbers
- The mixed phase begins at about 3 times normal nuclear density (3ρ₀) and ends at about 6.5ρ₀ for an optical potential of U_K = −140 MeV.
- In the mixed phase, the effective nucleon mass in the kaon-condensed phase is about 300 MeV lower than in the nucleon phase.
- At the end of the mixed phase (6.5ρ₀), the nucleon effective mass in the kaon-condensed phase remains around 200 MeV.
- The maximum mass of a kaon-condensed neutron star lies between 1.4 and 1.6 solar masses for optical potentials between U_K = −120 and −140 MeV.
- For the stronger potential (U_K = −140 MeV), the minimum radius is 8 km; for the weaker potential (U_K = −120 MeV), the minimum radius is above 12 km.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of kaon condensation in neutron stars by Glendenning (1985) and Thorsson et al. (1994), which used the Maxwell construction. It corrects that approach by applying the Gibbs condition, previously argued by Glendenning (1992) to be necessary for systems with two conserved chemical potentials (baryon number and charge). The results enable more realistic mass-radius predictions for neutron stars with kaon condensates, though the authors note that finite-size effects (surface tension, Coulomb energy) and the inclusion of hyperons (Knorren et al., 1995; Schaffner & Mishustin, 1996) remain to be incorporated.
