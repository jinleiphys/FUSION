# On a possible $^{3}_ϕ$H hypernucleus with HAL QCD interaction

> Within the framework of the Faddeev formalism in configuration space, we investigate bound states in the $ϕNN$ system with total isospin $T=0$ and $T=1$. The recently proposed lattice HAL QCD $ϕN$ potential in the $^{4}S_{3/2}$ channel does not support either $ϕN$ or $ϕNN$ bound states. The HAL QCD $ϕN$ potential in the $^{2}S_{1/2}$ channel suggests the bound states for $ϕN$ and $ϕNN (S=0)$ systems. However, the binding energies are highly sensitive to variations of the enhancement factor $β$, and the $ϕNN$ system is extremely strongly bound in the state $S=0$. Considering a spin-averaged potential %$(\frac{1}{3}V_{ϕN}^{1/2}+\frac{2}{3}V_{ϕN}^{3/2})$ for the state $S=1$ yields a bound state for $^3_ϕ$H $(S=1)$ hypernucleus with the binding energy (BE) 14.9 MeV when $β= 6.9$. The evaluation of the BE for the $S=1$, $T=1$ three-body state results in 5.47 MeV. %Also, We evaluated the BE for the $S=1$, $T=1$ three-body state as 5.47 MeV. Additionally, calculations using our approach confirm the bound states for the $ϕNN$ ($S=2,T=0$ and $S=1, T=1$) system previously predicted with the Yukawa-type potential motivated by the QCD van der Waals attractive force, mediated by multi-gluon exchanges.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a bound state for the hypothetical $^{3}_{\phi}$H hypernucleus (a $\phi NN$ system with spin $S=1$) exists with a binding energy of 14.9 MeV, but only when using a spin-averaged HAL QCD potential with a specific enhancement factor $\beta = 6.9$; the binding energy is highly sensitive to variations of $\beta$, and the system becomes extremely strongly bound in the $S=0$ state.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in configuration space for the $\phi NN$ three-body system, using the HAL QCD $\phi N$ potentials in the $^{4}S_{3/2}$ and $^{2}S_{1/2}$ channels, with the $NN$ MT I-III potential for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. For the $S=1$ state, they employed a spin-averaged potential $\bar V_{\phi N} = \frac{1}{3}V_{\phi N}^{1/2} + \frac{2}{3}V_{\phi N}^{3/2}$ to combine the two spin channels, and varied the enhancement factor $\beta$ to study sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- The $^{3}_{\phi}$H hypernucleus ($S=1$, $T=0$) has a binding energy of 14.9 MeV when using the spin-averaged potential with $\beta = 6.9$.
- The $S=1$, $T=1$ three-body state has a binding energy of 5.47 MeV with $\beta = 6.9$.
- The $\phi N$ system in the $^{2}S_{1/2}$ channel has a two-body binding energy of 27.7 MeV with $\beta = 6.9$.
- The $\phi N$ scattering length in the $^{4}S_{3/2}$ channel is $a_{\phi N}^{3/2} = -1.37$ fm, and no bound state is found in this channel.
- The $\phi N$ scattering length in the $^{2}S_{1/2}$ channel is $a_{\phi N}^{1/2} = 1.5$ fm with $\beta = 6.9$.
- For $\beta = 6.0$, the $S=1$, $T=0$ binding energy becomes 13.09 MeV; for $\beta = 8.0$, it becomes 17.52 MeV.
- The Yukawa-type potential from Gao (2001) gives a $\phi N$ binding energy of 9.40 MeV and a $\phi NN$ ($S=2$) binding energy of 38.04 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of $\phi N$ and $\phi NN$ systems using the HAL QCD potentials from Lyu (2022) and Chizzali (2024), which were derived from lattice QCD simulations. The results contradict the earlier prediction by EA24 of a bound $^{3}_{\phi}$H with binding energy 6.7-7.3 MeV using the same HAL QCD potential in the $^{4}S_{3/2}$ channel, as the present work finds no bound state in that channel. The calculations confirm the bound states for the $\phi NN$ system previously predicted by BSS and Sofi using the Yukawa-type potential from Gao (2001), and enable future experimental searches for the $^{3}_{\phi}$H hypernucleus, particularly in light of the ALICE (2021) measurement of the $\phi N$ correlation function.
