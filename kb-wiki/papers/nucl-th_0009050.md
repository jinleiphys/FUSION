# Quasi-SU(3) truncation scheme for even-even sd-shell nuclei

> The Quasi-SU(3) symmetry was uncovered in full pf and sdg shell-model calculations for both even-even and odd-even nuclei. It manifests itself through a dominance of single-particle and quadrupole-quadrupole terms in the Hamiltonian used to describe well-deformed nuclei. A practical consequence of the quasi-SU(3) symmetry is an efficient basis truncation scheme. In a recent work was shown that when this type of Hamiltonian is diagonalized in an SU(3) basis, only a few irreducible represntations (irreps) of SU(3) are needed to describe the Yrast band, the leading S = 0 irrep augmented with the leading S = 1 irreps in the proton and neutron subspaces. In the present article the quasi-SU(3) truncation scheme is used, in conjunction with a "realistic but schematic" Hamiltonian that includes the most important multipole terms, to describe the energy spectra and B(E2) transition strengths of 20-Ne, 22-Ne, 24-Mg and 28-Si. The effect of the size of the Hilbert space on both sets of observables is discussed, as well as the structure of the Yrast band and the importance of the various terms in the Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The quasi-SU(3) truncation scheme, which includes only the leading S=0 SU(3) irrep augmented with the leading S=1 irreps in the proton and neutron subspaces, provides an efficient basis for describing yrast bands and B(E2) transition strengths in even-even sd-shell nuclei, but the truncated space fails to reproduce highly excited states and some inter-band transitions.

## Method

The authors diagonalized a Hamiltonian containing single-particle terms, quadrupole-quadrupole and pairing interactions, plus three rotor-like terms (K_J^2, J^2, and a symmetry term) in an SU(3) basis for 20-Ne, 22-Ne, 24-Mg, and 28-Si. For 20-Ne and 22-Ne, calculations were performed in both the full Hilbert space and a truncated space containing only the SU(3) irreps with the largest Casimir operator expectation values and spin 0 and 1; for 24-Mg and 28-Si, only truncated spaces were used. The Hamiltonian parameters were taken from systematics, with the rotor-like parameters adjusted to fit the spectra in each truncated subspace.

## Key numbers

- For 20-Ne, the leading irrep (8,0) contributes only 44% to the J=6 state and 23% to the J=8 state of the ground state band, demonstrating strong mixing with S=1 irreps
- For 20-Ne, B(E2; 0_1 -> 2_1) = 2.4051 x 10^-2 e^2b^2 in the 12-irrep truncated space with optimized parameters, compared to the experimental value of 3.2731 +/- 0.1612 x 10^-2 e^2b^2
- For 22-Ne, B(E2; 0_1 -> 2_1) = 2.3192 x 10^-2 e^2b^2 in the truncated basis and 2.0925 x 10^-2 e^2b^2 in the complete basis, compared to the experimental value of 2.2886 +/- 0.0915 x 10^-2 e^2b^2
- For 24-Mg, B(E2; 0_1 -> 2_1) = 4.7742 x 10^-2 e^2b^2 in the truncated basis, compared to the experimental value of 4.3588 +/- 0.1028 x 10^-2 e^2b^2
- The quadrupole-quadrupole strength chi = 17/A^(5/3) and pairing strengths G_pi = G_nu = 9.5/A were used for all nuclei

## Context

This work extends the quasi-SU(3) truncation scheme, previously uncovered in full pf and sdg shell-model calculations by Zuker (1995) and applied to odd-even nuclei by Martinez-Pinedo (1997), to even-even sd-shell nuclei. The approach differs from earlier SU(3)-based truncation schemes by Trotenier (1995) through the inclusion of spin-1 states, which the authors show are essential for reproducing yrast band wavefunctions. The results provide justification for applying the quasi-SU(3) truncation scheme to nucleons occupying unique parity orbitals in heavy deformed nuclei, with a follow-on paper by Vargas (2000) extending the work to odd-mass and odd-odd light nuclei.
