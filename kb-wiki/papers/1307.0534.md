# Folding model study of the charge-exchange scattering to the isobaric analog state and implication for the nuclear symmetry energy

> The Fermi transition (ΔL=ΔS=0 and ΔT=1) between the nuclear isobaric analog states (IAS), induced by the charge-exchange (p,n) or (3He,t) reaction, can be considered as "elastic" scattering of proton or 3He by the isovector term of the optical potential (OP) that flips the projectile isospin. The accurately measured (p,n) or (3He,t) scattering cross-section to the IAS can be used, therefore, to probe the isospin dependence of the proton or 3He optical potential. Within the folding model, the isovector part of the OP is determined exclusively by the neutron-proton difference in the nuclear densities and the isospin dependence of the effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction. Because the isovector coupling explicitly links the isovector part of the proton or 3He optical potential to the cross section of the charge-exchange (p,n) or (3He,t) scattering to the IAS, the isospin dependence of the effective (in-medium) NN interaction can be well tested in the folding model analysis of these charge-exchange reactions. On the other hand, the same isospin- and density dependent NN interaction can also be used in a Hartree-Fock calculation of asymmetric nuclear matter, to estimate the nuclear matter energy and its asymmetry part (the nuclear symmetry energy). As a result, the fine-tuning of the isospin dependence of the effective NN interaction against the measured (p,n) or (3He,t) cross sections should allow us to make some realistic prediction of the nuclear symmetry energy and its density dependence.

## Key claim

The isospin dependence of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, fine-tuned against charge-exchange (p,n) or (3He,t) scattering cross sections to the isobaric analog state (IAS), can provide realistic predictions of the nuclear symmetry energy and its density dependence, with the (3He,t) data potentially sensitive to higher nuclear densities (rho >= rho_0) than the (p,n) data (rho < rho_0).

## Method

The authors performed a consistent folding model analysis of both (p,n) and (3He,t) charge-exchange scattering to the isobaric analog state (IAS) using the complex density-dependent CDM3Y6 interaction. The isovector part of the optical potential was determined from the neutron-proton density difference of the target and the isospin dependence of the effective NN interaction, with the complex folded optical potential used in coupled-channel Lane equations solved with the ECIS97 code. The isovector density dependence of the CDM3Y6 interaction was adjusted at each energy to reproduce the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results of Jeukenne, Lejeune and Mahaux (JLM) for the nucleon optical potential in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- For the (p,n) reaction on 208Pb at 35 MeV, the best-fit renormalization factor for the real folded proton optical potential was N_V = 0.858 and for the imaginary part N_W = 0.494, giving a total reaction cross section of 1934 mb compared to the experimental 1974 +/- 38 mb.
- For the (p,n) reaction on 48Ca at 35 MeV, the best-fit renormalization factor for the real folded charge-exchange form factor was N_V1 = 1.352 (complex folded OP) or 1.370 (hybrid OP), with N_W1 = 1.00 in both cases.
- For the (p,n) reaction on 90Zr at 45 MeV, the best-fit renormalization factor for the real folded charge-exchange form factor was N_V1 = 1.557 (complex folded OP) or 1.578 (hybrid OP).
- The Coulomb correction Delta_E_C for the p+208Pb system at 45 MeV was found to be about 14.6 MeV, lower than the empirical CH89 value of about 19 MeV.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density E_sym = S(rho = rho_0 ≈ 0.17 fm^-3) is predicted to be around 30 MeV by numerous nuclear many-body calculations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous folding model studies of (p,n) scattering to the IAS (Khoa 2005, 2007) by including (3He,t) data, which are sensitive to higher nuclear densities (rho >= rho_0) due to the larger projectile size. The approach contrasts with empirical global optical potential systematics such as Becchetti and Greenlees (1969), the CH89 global OP by Varner et al. (1991), and Koning and Delaroche (2003), which derive the isovector term mainly from elastic proton and neutron scattering rather than charge-exchange data. The results enable more realistic predictions of the nuclear symmetry energy and its density dependence, relevant for understanding supernova dynamics and neutron star formation.
