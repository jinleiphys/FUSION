# Hanbury-Brown-Twiss Interferometry with Identical Bosons in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions: Comparisons with Hadronic Scattering Models

> Identical boson Hanbury-Brown-Twiss interferometry as applied to relativistic heavy-ion collisions is reviewed. Emphasis is placed on the use of hadronic scattering models to interpret the physical significance of experimental results. Interferometric studies with center-of-mass energies from < 1 GeV/nucleon up to 5500 GeV/nucleon are considered.

## Key claim

This review argues that hadronic scattering models (specifically the Intranuclear Cascade Model for low energies and the Hadronic Rescattering Model for high energies) can successfully reproduce experimental Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) pion source parameters, but only when the model parameters are adjusted to match freezeout observables; the models serve as interpretive tools rather than first-principles predictions of the initial collision state.

## Method

The author reviews two hadronic scattering approaches: the Intranuclear Cascade Model (CASCADE code by Cugnon) for center-of-mass energies below 1 GeV/nucleon, which treats heavy-ion collisions as superpositions of nucleon-nucleon interactions with Delta isobars mediating pion production and rescattering; and the Hadronic Rescattering Model (HRM) for energies above 1 GeV/nucleon, which uses a semi-classical Monte Carlo calculation with a simple hadronization parameterization (thermal transverse momentum distribution, gaussian rapidity distribution, and hadronization proper time) followed by binary hadronic rescattering to freezeout. Two-pion HBT correlation functions are generated from the freezeout information by weighting pion pairs with Bose symmetrization using a four-vector version of the two-pion probability, then fitting gaussian source models to extract radius, lifetime, and lambda parameters.

## Key numbers

- For 1.5 GeV/nucleon Ar40+Ar40 collisions at impact parameter b=2 fm with minimum pion momentum k_min=50 MeV/c, the CASCADE model yields source parameters R=3.6±0.1 fm, τ=3.2±0.5 fm/c, and λ=0.94±0.05
- For central RHIC Au+Au collisions at √s=130 GeV/nucleon from the STAR experiment, gaussian fits to two-pion correlation functions give r∼8 fm and λ∼0.5-0.6
- The review covers interferometric studies with center-of-mass energies from less than 1 GeV/nucleon up to 5500 GeV/nucleon (LHC Pb+Pb predictions)
- For the Bevalac experiments, model predictions mostly agree with experimental source parameters within 1σ, with the largest disagreement seen for spectrometer λ parameters (model predictions near unity versus experimental values in the 0.5-0.7 range)

## Context

This review extends the application of HBT interferometry from the original astronomical measurements by Hanbury-Brown and Twiss (1956) to relativistic heavy-ion collisions, following the first two-pion interferometry measurement by Goldhaber, Goldhaber, Lee, and Pais (1960) in proton+antiproton collisions. The work compares the CASCADE intranuclear cascade model (Cugnon) with Bevalac experiments at 1.2-1.8 GeV/nucleon and the Hadronic Rescattering Model (Herrmann and Bertsch, 1995) with AGS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC energy collisions, enabling future interpretation of HBT results as probes of the space-time dynamics of the collision before rescattering masks the initial state.
