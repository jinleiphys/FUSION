# $η^{\prime}$ production in proton-proton scattering close to threshold}

> The $pp \to pp η^{\prime}$ (958) reaction has been measured at COSY using the internal beam and the COSY-11 facility. The total cross sections at the four different excess energies \mbox{$ Q = ~1.5 ~MeV, ~1.7 ~MeV, ~2.9 ~MeV,$ and $ ~4.1 MeV$} have been evaluated to be \mbox{$ σ= 2.5 \pm 0.5~nb$, $~~~ 2.9 \pm 1.1~nb$, $~~~ 12.7 \pm 3.2~nb$, ~ and $~~~ 25.2 \pm 3.6 ~nb $}, respectively. In this region of excess energy the $η^{\prime}$ (958) cross sections are much lower compared to those of the $π^0$ and $η$ production.

# η′ production in proton-proton scattering close to threshold

## Key claim
The total cross sections for the pp → ppη′ (958) reaction at four excess energies between 1.5 and 4.1 MeV are much lower than those for π0 and η production, and the data suggest that no S-wave Nη′ resonance dominates the reaction mechanism, unlike the η case where the S11(1535) resonance is important.

## Method
The experiment was performed at COSY using the internal beam and the COSY-11 facility, with an internal cluster target in front of a C-shaped dipole magnet acting as a magnetic spectrometer. The η′ mesons were not directly identified; their four-momentum vectors were determined via the missing mass method by registering the two outgoing protons in drift chamber stacks and scintillator detectors, with particle identification from time-of-flight measurements. Total cross sections were extracted after correcting for detector acceptance and reconstruction efficiency via Monte Carlo simulations that included the pp final state interaction and Coulomb repulsion, with luminosity determined from simultaneously recorded elastic scattering normalized to EDDA data.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section at Q = 1.5 MeV: σ = 2.5 ± 0.5 nb
- Total cross section at Q = 1.7 MeV: σ = 2.9 ± 1.1 nb
- Total cross section at Q = 2.9 MeV: σ = 12.7 ± 3.2 nb
- Total cross section at Q = 4.1 MeV: σ = 25.2 ± 3.6 nb
- Overall systematic error: 15% (10% from detection efficiency, 5% from luminosity)
- Beam momentum spread: Δp = 1.1 MeV/c
- Upper limit on pseudoscalar coupling constant gη′pp < 2.5, compared to predictions ranging from 1.9 to 7.5

## Context
This work provides the first threshold measurements of η′ production in elementary proton-proton collisions, extending prior studies of π0 and η production in the same channel. The results contrast with the η case, where the S11(1535) resonance dominates as shown by CoWi and Vett, and instead suggest a production mechanism similar to π0 production, with cross sections scaling approximately as the square of the mass ratio (135/958)². The data enable constraints on the η′NN coupling constant and test quark model predictions by Zha, Dum, and Efr, while also motivating further theoretical work on heavy meson exchange contributions.
