# Pion properties in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter using in-medium chiral perturbation theory

> We compute the density dependence of in-medium pion properties, such as mass, wave function renormalization, and decay constant in the correlation function approach, and how they change under the influence of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. To this end, we use in-medium chiral perturbation theory to compute the relevant Feynman diagrams up to two-loop diagrams. Our results show that the isospin asymmetry of the nuclear matter splits these quantities into three separate values, corresponding to the three pions. Consequently, the tendency of each in-medium pion mass, wave function renormalization, and decay constant is dependent on the density and the neutron-to-proton ratio $ρ_n/ρ_p$ of nuclear matter. We also derive an in-medium Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner relation which is valid for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter and investigate to what extent it holds within our calculations.

## Key claim

The isospin asymmetry of nuclear matter splits the in-medium mass, wave function renormalization, and decay constant into three separate values corresponding to the three pions, with each quantity's density dependence governed by both the total density and the neutron-to-proton ratio; the authors also note that more diagrams than previously considered are necessary to fully describe pion properties in asymmetric matter.

## Method

The authors used in-medium chiral perturbation theory to compute Feynman diagrams up to two-loop order for the pion self-energy, decay constant, and quark condensate in the correlation function approach. They employed a chiral Lagrangian and evaluated the diagrams in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, where proton and neutron densities can be varied independently, using the physical basis of pions to separate results by isospin.

## Key numbers

- The in-medium pion mass, wave function renormalization, and decay constant each split into three distinct values corresponding to pi+, pi-, and pi0 in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter.
- The behavior of each in-medium pion property depends on the density and the neutron-to-proton ratio rho_n/rho_p of nuclear matter.
- An in-medium Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation was derived that is valid for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends an earlier study by Goda et al. (2014) that calculated pion properties in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, now generalizing to the more realistic case of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. It builds on the in-medium chiral perturbation theory framework introduced by Oller (2002) and Meissner (2002), and relates to prior calculations of in-medium pion masses by Kaiser (2001) using two-loop chiral perturbation theory, in-medium decay constants by Meissner (2002) and Lacour (2010), and the pion-nuclear s-wave optical potential in asymmetric matter by Kolomeitsev (2003). The results enable improved predictions for pion properties in neutron-rich environments such as neutron stars.
