# Muon capture on deuteron and the neutron-neutron scattering length

> We study the capture rate in the doublet hyperfine initial state for the muon capture reaction $μ^- + \,^2{\rm H} \rightarrow ν_μ+ n + n$ ($Γ^D$) and the total capture rate for the reaction $ μ^- + \,^3{\rm He} \rightarrow ν_μ+ \,^3{\rm H}$ ($Γ_0$). We investigate whether $Γ^D$ and $Γ_0$ could be sensitive to the $nn$ $S$-wave scattering length ($a_{nn}$). To this aim, we consider nuclear potentials and weak currents derived within $χ$EFT. We employ the N3LO chiral potential with cutoff $Λ$=500 MeV, but the low-energy constant (LEC) determining $a_{nn}$ is varied so as to obtain $a_{nn}$=-18.95 (the present empirical value), -16.0, -22.0, and +18.22 fm. The last value leads to a $nn$ bound state with a binding energy of 139 keV. The LECs $c_D$ and $c_E$, present in the three-nucleon potential and axial-vector current, are fitted to reproduce the $A=3$ binding energies and the triton Gamow-Teller matrix element. The capture rate $Γ^D$ is found to be 399(3) s$^{-1}$ for $a_{nn}$=-18.95 and -16.0 fm; and 400(3) s$^{-1}$ for $a_{nn}$=-22.0 fm. For $a_{nn}$=+18.22 fm, we obtain 275(3) s$^{-1}$ (135(3) s$^{-1}$), when the final $nn$ system is unbound (bound). The rate $Γ_0$ is found to be 1494(15), 1491(16), 1488(18), and 1475(16) s$^{-1}$ for $a_{nn}$=-18.95, -16.0, -22.0, and +18.22 fm, respectively. The theoretical uncertainties are due to the fitting procedure and radiative corrections. Our results seem to exclude the possibility of constraining a negative $a_{nn}$ with an uncertainty of less than $\sim \pm$ 3 fm through an accurate determination of the muon capture rates, but the uncertainty on the present empirical value will not complicate the interpretation of the (forth-coming) experimental results for $Γ^D$. Finally, a comparison with the already available experimental data discourages the possibility of a bound $nn$ state.

## Key claim

The doublet muon capture rate on deuteron (Γ^D) is not sensitive to variations of the negative neutron-neutron scattering length (a_nn) within approximately ±3 fm of the empirical value, meaning the upcoming MuSun experiment cannot extract a more precise a_nn from this reaction; however, the uncertainty in the empirical a_nn will not complicate interpretation of the MuSun results.

## Method

The authors used chiral effective field theory (χEFT) with the N3LO nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential (cutoff Λ=500 MeV) and N2LO three-nucleon (NNN) interactions, together with χEFT charge-changing weak currents up to N3LO. The low-energy constant (LEC) determining a_nn in the NN potential was varied to produce four values: -18.95 fm (empirical), -16.0 fm, -22.0 fm, and +18.22 fm (the last producing a bound di-neutron state with binding energy 139 keV). The LECs c_D and c_E in the NNN potential and axial-vector current were fitted to reproduce A=3 binding energies and the triton Gamow-Teller matrix element. Wave functions were calculated using the hyperspherical harmonics method.

## Key numbers

- Γ^D = 399(3) s⁻¹ for a_nn = -18.95 fm and -16.0 fm; 400(3) s⁻¹ for a_nn = -22.0 fm
- Γ^D = 275(3) s⁻¹ for a_nn = +18.22 fm (unbound final nn system); 135(3) s⁻¹ for bound di-neutron final state
- Γ_0 (muon capture on ³He) = 1494(15) s⁻¹, 1491(16) s⁻¹, 1488(18) s⁻¹, and 1475(16) s⁻¹ for a_nn = -18.95, -16.0, -22.0, and +18.22 fm respectively
- Experimental Γ_0 = 1496(4) s⁻¹ from Ackermann (1998)
- Binding energy of hypothetical bound nn state: B_nn = 0.139 MeV (for a_nn = +18.22 fm)

## Context

This work extends the consistent χEFT approach of Marcucci et al. (2012) for muon capture on deuteron and ³He, which previously achieved excellent agreement with experimental data. The study investigates whether the "clean" two-neutron final state in μ⁻ + ²H → ν_μ + n + n could constrain a_nn, motivated by the upcoming MuSun experiment at PSI (Andreev et al., 2007; Kammel, 2010). The results show that the reaction cannot improve the empirical a_nn from pion capture on deuteron (Chen et al., 2007) and neutron-deuteron breakup (Gonzalez Trotter et al., 2006), but the MuSun experiment can still extract the LEC d_R (or c_D) and potentially exclude or confirm a bound di-neutron state, which would require major revision of nuclear theory if confirmed.
