# Parity violation in nuclear systems

> Parity violation in nuclear systems is reviewed. A few ingredients relevant to the description of the parity-violating nucleon-nucleon force in terms of meson exchanges are reminded. Effects in nuclear systems are then considered. They involve pp scattering, some complex nuclei and the deuteron system.

# Parity violation in nuclear systems

## Key claim
Parity-violating effects in nuclear systems are qualitatively understood, but little quantitative information has been extracted on the parity-non-conserving component of nucleon-nucleon forces; the expected size of parity-non-conserving amplitudes at low energy is of order 10^{-7}, though enhancements up to 10^{-1} occur in neutron-nucleus scattering near p-wave resonances.

## Method
The parity-violating nucleon-nucleon force is described in terms of meson exchanges (π, ρ, ω) with one strong vertex and one weak parity-violating vertex, characterized by coupling constants h^1_π (ΔT=1), h^{0,1,2}_ρ (ΔT=0,1,2), and h^{0,1}_ω (ΔT=0,1). At low energy, the interaction is parametrized by five S↔P nucleon-nucleon transition amplitudes (¹S₀↔³P₀ with ΔT=0,1,2; ³S₁↔¹P₁ with ΔT=0; ³S₁↔³P₁ with ΔT=1). The analysis combines measurements of longitudinal asymmetry in pp scattering at multiple energies, circular polarization in ¹⁸F, the ¹³³Cs anapole moment, and several observables in the np system including the deuteron.

## Key numbers
- Expected size of parity-non-conserving amplitudes at low energy: order 10^{-7} (for the amplitude)
- Enhancement in neutron-nucleus scattering near p-wave resonances: effects of order 10^{-1}
- pp scattering longitudinal asymmetry measurements at 13.6 MeV and 45 MeV are in complete agreement with each other
- At 221 MeV, the ¹S₀↔³P₀ transition amplitude vanishes, providing a window to determine the ³P₂↔¹D₂ transition amplitude
- Fitted coupling constants from pp data: h^{pp}_ρ = -22.3 × 10^{-7}, h^{pp}_ω = +5.2 × 10^{-7}
- DDH "best guess" coupling constants: h^{pp}_ρ = -15.5 × 10^{-7}, h^{pp}_ω = -3.0 × 10^{-7}
- Upper limit on h^1_π from ¹⁸F: |h^1_π| ≤ 1.3 × 10^{-7}
- DDH range for h^1_π: 0 to 11 × 10^{-7}; DDH "best" value: 4.6 × 10^{-7}
- Kaiser-Meissner (KM) value for h^1_π: 0.2 × 10^{-7} (without s̄s) or 0.8–1.3 × 10^{-7} (with s̄s)
- DDH range for h^0_ρ: -31 to 6 × 10^{-7}; DDH "best": -11 × 10^{-7}; KM: -4 × 10^{-7}
- DDH range for h^0_ω: -10 to 6 × 10^{-7}; DDH "best": -2 × 10^{-7}; KM: -6 × 10^{-7}
- Asymmetry in thermal neutron radiative capture on protons: A_γ = -0.11 h^1_π
- Effective-field theory overestimates A_γ by 60% at leading order
- Circular polarization of photons in deuteron photodisintegration near threshold: as large as 1 × 10^{-7} is not excluded for some models
- Nuclear parity-violating effect in electron scattering on deuteron: a few percent, negligible

## Context
This review extends the framework of Desplanques (1998) and the DDH work (Desplanques 1980) on parity-violating meson-nucleon couplings, and contrasts with effective-field theory approaches such as Holstein (2004) and Kaplan (1999). The paper discusses how the pp scattering data from Carlson (2002) provide the only calibration of parity-violating nucleon-nucleon forces, while the ¹⁸F result constrains h^1_π. The analysis of the ¹³³Cs anapole moment by Haxton (2001) is noted to rely on an approximation that omits some orbital contributions with the wrong sign. The review identifies that among the five low-energy parity-violating nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes, only the pp amplitude is determined with good accuracy; the other four (involving polarized neutrons) have only upper limits. Future experiments on neutron-spin rotation and the Lobashov experiment (circular polarization in thermal neutron-proton capture) are needed to determine the remaining amplitudes and the debated h^1_π coupling.
