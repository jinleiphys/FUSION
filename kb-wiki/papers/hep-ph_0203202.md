# Electric dipole moments of Hg, Xe, Rn, Ra, Pu, and TlF induced by the nuclear Schiff moment and limits on time-reversal violating interactions

> We have calculated the atomic electric dipole moments (EDMs) induced in ^{199}Hg, ^{129}Xe, ^{223}Rn, ^{225}Ra, and ^{239}Pu by their respective nuclear Schiff moments S. The results are (in units 10^{-17}S(e {fm}^{3})^{-1}e cm): d(^{199}Hg)=-2.8, d(^{129}Xe)=0.38, d(^{223}Rn)=3.3, d(^{225}Ra)=-8.5, d(^{239}Pu)=-11. We have also calculated corrections to the parity- and time-invariance-violating (P,T-odd) spin-axis interaction constant in TlF. These results are important for the interpretation of atomic and molecular experiments on EDMs in terms of fundamental P,T-odd parameters.

## Key claim

The paper presents calculated atomic electric dipole moments (EDMs) induced by nuclear Schiff moments for five diamagnetic atoms, finding that the new result for mercury is about 40% smaller than the previous indirect estimate, which places a more conservative constraint on the Schiff moment and the underlying time-reversal violating mechanisms.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations for all atoms, with core polarization included via the random-phase approximation (RPA). For mercury and radium, which have two valence electrons above closed shells, they additionally used the configuration interaction plus many-body perturbation theory (CI+MBPT) method to account for valence-valence and valence-core correlations. For plutonium, a simple V^N approximation was used with the open f-shell weighted by occupancy. The atomic wave functions were tested by comparing calculated ionization potentials and scalar polarizabilities with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- d(199Hg) = -2.8 × 10^(-17) S (e fm^3)^(-1) e cm (final value, 40% smaller than previous estimate of -4 × 10^(-17))
- d(129Xe) = 0.38 × 10^(-17) S (e fm^3)^(-1) e cm (40% larger than previous HF value of 0.27 × 10^(-17))
- d(223Rn) = 3.3 × 10^(-17) S (e fm^3)^(-1) e cm
- d(225Ra) = -8.5 × 10^(-17) S (e fm^3)^(-1) e cm
- d(239Pu) = -11 × 10^(-17) S (e fm^3)^(-1) e cm
- Schiff moment limit from Hg: S(199Hg) = (3.8 ± 1.8 ± 1.4) × 10^(-12) e fm^3
- Schiff moment limit from TlF molecule: S(205Tl) = (69 ± 111) × 10^(-12) e fm^3
- Schiff moment limit from Xe: S(129Xe) = (184 ± 868 ± 26) × 10^(-12) e fm^3
- Correction coefficients for TlF molecular calculation: k1 = 0.89, k2 = 1.10

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates of Schiff-moment-induced EDMs, notably the indirect calculation for Hg by Flambaum (1985) and the extrapolation-based estimates for Rn, Ra, and Pu by Spevak (1997) and Flambaum (2002). The new results enable more reliable interpretation of the best experimental EDM limits, particularly the Hg measurement by Romalis (2001) and the Xe measurement by Rosenberry (2001), in terms of fundamental P,T-odd parameters such as the neutron EDM, the P,T-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction constant, the QCD theta parameter, and quark chromoelectric dipole moments. The limits extracted from Hg are found to be stronger than those from direct neutron EDM experiments.
