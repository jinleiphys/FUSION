# Dynamical deformation effects in subbarrier fusion of $^{64}$Ni+$^{132}$Sn

> We show that dynamical deformation effects play an important role in fusion reactions involving the $^{64}$Ni nucleus, in particular the $^{64}$Ni+$^{132}$Sn system. We calculate fully microscopic interaction potentials and the corresponding subbarrier fusion cross sections.

## Key claim
Dynamical deformation effects, captured via a parameter-free microscopic TDHF+DCHF approach, produce a significantly lower fusion barrier for perpendicular orientation of the oblate ⁶⁴Ni nucleus (150.1 MeV) compared to the parallel orientation (155.8 MeV), which can explain the enhanced subbarrier fusion cross sections for the ⁶⁴Ni+¹³²Sn system. However, the calculated cross section at the lowest subbarrier energy (0.0035 mb) remains a factor of 200 below the experimental value, indicating that additional structure information is needed.

## Method
The authors performed fully microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations for head-on collisions of ⁶⁴Ni+¹³²Sn at E_c.m. = 158 MeV, using the Skyrme SLy5 effective interaction with all time-odd terms. At each time step during the TDHF evolution, density-constrained Hartree-Fock (DCHF) calculations were used to extract the internuclear potential V(R) = E_DC(R) - E_A1 - E_A2, which contains no parameters and no normalization. Fusion cross sections were then computed using a simple WKB approach for the lowest energy point and the parabolic Wong formula for higher energies.

## Key numbers
- The calculated barrier height for parallel orientation (β=0°) is 155.8 MeV, essentially identical to the empirical Woods-Saxon barrier used in Li03.
- The calculated barrier height for perpendicular orientation (β=90°) is 150.1 MeV, which is 5.7 MeV lower and has a narrower width.
- The experimental fusion cross sections were measured in the energy range 142 MeV ≤ E_c.m. ≤ 176 MeV, with fission negligible for E_c.m. ≤ 160 MeV.
- At the lowest subbarrier energy point, the calculated fusion cross section is 0.0035 mb, which is a factor of 200 lower than the experimental value.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previously developed TDHF+DCHF method (Umar and Oberacker, 2006) to the neutron-rich ⁶⁴Ni+¹³²Sn system, where enhanced subbarrier fusion was observed by Li et al. (2003). The results contradict earlier coupled-channel calculations (Balantekin and Takigawa, 1998; Esbensen, 2004) that significantly underestimated the subbarrier fusion cross sections for this system. The findings underscore the need for detailed structure information on neutron-rich nuclei like ⁶⁴Ni to resolve remaining discrepancies, particularly at the lowest energies.
