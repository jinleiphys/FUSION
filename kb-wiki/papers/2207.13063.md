# Mirror Neutron Stars: How QCD can be used to study dark matter through gravitational waves

> Given the lack of empirical evidence of weakly interacting dark matter, it is reasonable to look to other candidates such as a confining dark sector with a similar number of particles as the standard model. Twin Higgs mirror matter is one such model that is a twin of the standard model with particles masses 3--6 times heavier than the standard model that solves the hierarchy problem. This generically predicts mirror neutron stars, degenerate objects made entirely of mirror nuclear matter. We find their structure using a realistic equation of state from crust (nuclei) to core (relativistic mean-field model) and scale the particle masses using lattice QCD results. We find that mirror neutron stars have unique signatures that are detectable via gravitational waves and binary pulsars, that provides an intriguing possibility for probing dark matter.

## Key claim

Mirror neutron stars, predicted by the minimal Mirror Twin Higgs model with particle masses 3–6 times heavier than the standard model, occupy a unique portion of the mass-radius diagram and are heavy enough to be discovered via binary merger events by advanced LIGO, but the actual predictions depend on the ratio of Higgs vacuum expectation values f/v lying in the phenomenologically relevant range 3–7.

## Method

The authors built a complete description of standard-model neutron stars using the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland model for the stellar crust (a lattice of nuclei in an electron gas) and a relativistic mean-field model for the core (nucleons interacting via scalar σ, vector ωμ, and vector-isovector ρμ mesons), with smooth interpolation between crust and core. They then extrapolated this model to the mirror sector by rescaling parameters according to the mirror QCD scale and mirror pion mass, using lattice QCD calculations at heavier pion masses and chiral perturbation theory data to extract the scaling of each parameter with (mπ'/ΛQCD')².

## Key numbers

- The ratio of mirror Higgs to SM Higgs vacuum expectation values must lie in the range f/v ∼ 3–10 to evade collider constraints and solve the Hierarchy Problem; the phenomenologically relevant range for mirror neutron stars is f/v = 3–7.
- Mirror pion masses are 2–4 times larger than in the standard model.
- The scaling of the mirror QCD scale with f/v is given by ΛQCD'/ΛQCD ≈ 0.68 + 0.41 log(1.32 + f/v).
- Stellar masses and radii scale with mirror baryon mass as mB'^1.9.

## Context

This work extends the Twin Higgs mirror matter model proposed by Chacko (2005) as a solution to the Hierarchy Problem, and builds on previous lattice QCD calculations at heavier pion masses from Hanhart (2008), McNeile (2009), Walker-Loud (2008), and others. The results enable gravitational-wave searches for dark matter via binary merger events, as mirror neutron stars would be distinguishable from neutron stars, strange quark stars, and black holes by their unique mass-radius relation and modest tidal deformabilities, combined with the absence of electromagnetic signatures.
