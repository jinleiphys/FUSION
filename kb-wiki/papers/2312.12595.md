# The role of longitudinal decorrelations for measurements of anisotropic flow in small collision systems

> Within a (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model we compute anisotropic flow in small system collisions as performed at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and measured by the STAR and PHENIX Collaborations. We emphasize the importance of the rapidity dependence of the geometry for interpreting the differences encountered in measurements by the two collaborations.

## Key claim

The differences between STAR and PHENIX measurements of anisotropic flow in small collision systems can be largely explained by the rapidity dependence of the collision geometry, specifically the longitudinal decorrelations that affect two-particle correlation measurements when different pseudorapidity ranges are used.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic calculations using the 3d-glauber+music+urqmd hybrid model within the iebe-music framework for p+Au, d+Au, and 3He+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. The initial state dynamically deposits energy, momentum, and baryon number with full three-dimensional fluctuating structure, and the fluid evolution uses the NEOS-BQS equation of state with specific shear and bulk viscosities tuned to reproduce central 3He+Au data. The authors compared elliptic flow v2 and triangular flow v3 obtained using different detector combinations (backward-backward versus backward-forward) to replicate the PHENIX and STAR measurement conditions.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV for p+Au, d+Au, and 3He+Au systems at RHIC
- Particlization energy density: e_sw=0.50 GeV/fm^3 for the Cooper-Frye freeze-out surface
- Good agreement with PHENIX backward-backward triangular flow data for p_T less than 2 GeV
- The ratio of v2(p_T) from backward-backward to backward-forward detector combinations is well described by the model for p+Au and 3He+Au collisions

## Context

This work extends the findings of Zhao (2022) by providing additional evidence that longitudinal decorrelations, previously studied by CMS (2015) and Bozek (2015), are essential for reconciling the apparent contradictions between STAR and PHENIX anisotropic flow measurements in small systems. The results demonstrate that comparisons between hydrodynamic calculations and experimental data must account for the full (3+1)D structure of the produced matter, and that the triangular flow measurements in the two smallest systems (p+Au and d+Au) are non-flow dominated when forward-going detectors are included. This enables more precise interpretation of future small-system flow measurements at RHIC and the LHC.
