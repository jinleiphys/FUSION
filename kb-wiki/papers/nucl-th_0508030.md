# A simple analytical calculation of mean-field potential in heavy nuclei

> In many-body theory of particles with long range interaction potential, such as the potential of Newtonian gravity and its relativistic generalization, Newton-Birkhoff theorem plays a very important role. This theorem is violated in the case of short range interaction potential, such as the Yukawa potential in nuclear physics. We discuss the relevance of this in the many-body treatment of heavy nuclei. In particular, using techniques similar to those in Newtonian gravity, we calculate analytically the mean-field potential in heavy nuclei. The mean-field potential thus calculated depends on well known quantities such as the pion-nucleon coupling constant, inverse pion mass, nuclear radius and density. The qualitative features of the mean-field potential thus obtained is similar to the well known phenomenological Wood-Saxon potential.

## Key claim

Using techniques analogous to Newtonian gravity, the authors derive an analytical expression for the mean-field potential in heavy nuclei from the Yukawa interaction between nucleons, but the result is only qualitatively (not quantitatively) similar to the phenomenological Wood-Saxon potential; the central depth of the potential is not shown to agree numerically.

## Method

The authors treat a heavy spherical nucleus as a constant-density distribution of nucleons interacting via the two-body Yukawa potential. They calculate the mean-field potential at any point inside the nucleus by summing the contributions from the interior sphere and the exterior spherical shell, exploiting the fact that the Yukawa potential violates the Newton-Birkhoff theorem. The resulting analytical expression depends on the pion-nucleon coupling constant, the inverse pion mass, the nuclear radius, and the constant nucleon density.

## Key numbers

* Pion-nucleon coupling constant: \(g^2/c\hbar = 0.081\) (dimensionless).
* Range of the two-body Yukawa potential: \(1/\mu = 1.4094\) fm.
* Nuclear radius: \(R = 1.07 A^{1/3}\) fm, where \(A\) is the atomic mass number.
* Constant nucleon density: \(n_{\rho} = 0.0165\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* Conversion constant: \(c\hbar = 197.328\) (units not specified in text, but context implies MeV·fm).

## Context

This work extends the many-body treatment of nuclei with short-range interactions by applying a gravitational analogy (Newton-Birkhoff theorem) to the Yukawa potential, a method not previously used in nuclear physics. The derived mean-field potential qualitatively reproduces the Wood-Saxon potential (Wood and Saxon, 1954), and the authors argue that the violation of the Newton-Birkhoff theorem by the Yukawa interaction also provides a mechanism for surface tension in nuclei. The paper does not solve the Schroedinger equation with this potential, leaving the demonstration of shell model structure as a future step.
