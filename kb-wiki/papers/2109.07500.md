# Universality of a truncated sigma-model

> Bosonic quantum field theories, even when regularized using a finite lattice, possess an infinite dimensional Hilbert space and, therefore, cannot be simulated in quantum computers with a finite number of qubits. A truncation of the Hilbert space is then needed and the physical results are obtained after a double limit: one to remove the truncation and another to remove the regulator (the continuum limit). A simpler alternative is to find a model with a finite dimensional Hilbert space belonging to the same universality class as the continuum model (a "qubitization"), so only the space continuum limit is required. A qubitization of the $1+1$ dimensional asymptotically free $O(3)$ nonlinear $σ$-model based on ideas of non-commutative geometry was previously proposed arXiv:1903.06577 and, in this paper, we provide evidence that it reproduces the physics of the $σ$-model both in the infrared and the ultraviolet regimes.

## Key claim

The paper provides evidence that the anti-ferromagnetic fuzzy sphere model reproduces the physics of the 1+1 dimensional asymptotically free O(3) nonlinear sigma-model at all energy scales, from the infrared (E much less than m) to the ultraviolet (E much greater than m), while the ferromagnetic fuzzy model agrees only in the deep infrared and deviates at higher energies.

## Method

The authors use matrix product state (MPS) variational algorithms to compute low-lying eigenstates and eigenvalues of the fuzzy model Hamiltonian on finite lattices with periodic boundary conditions. They extract the mass gap and correlation length from finite-volume energy gaps using Luscher's formula, then compare the continuum-limit step-scaling curves (the ratio of energy gaps at system sizes L and 2L as a function of the correlation length over L) against exact predictions from the thermodynamic Bethe ansatz and perturbation theory for the O(3) sigma-model.

## Key numbers

- The MPS bond dimension D is varied between 500 and 1500 depending on system size, yielding relative errors for the energy gaps estimated at about 1 part in 10^4.
- Open-boundary MPS results agree with periodic MPS results to a relative error of order 10^-4 to 10^-6 for low-lying energies.
- At fixed mL = 1, the continuum extrapolation of the anti-ferromagnetic fuzzy model mass gap is consistent with the thermodynamic Bethe ansatz prediction, while the ferromagnetic model shows tension.
- At mL = 4 and 6 (infrared regime), both ferromagnetic and anti-ferromagnetic models agree with Luscher formula predictions within error bars.
- The step-scaling ratio Delta(L)/Delta(2L) approaches 1 in the infrared limit (mL to infinity) and approaches 2 in the ultraviolet limit (mL to 0), consistent with the O(3) sigma-model behavior.

## Context

This work extends the qubitization approach proposed by Alexandru et al. (2019) for the O(3) nonlinear sigma-model using a fuzzy sphere construction from non-commutative geometry. It addresses concerns raised by Singh (2019) and Bhattacharya (2020) that symmetries alone might not guarantee reproduction of both infrared and ultraviolet physics. The anti-ferromagnetic fuzzy model goes beyond the spin-1 anti-ferromagnetic Heisenberg chain (Haldane, 1982) which only reproduces the infrared physics, by also matching the ultraviolet regime where asymptotic freedom becomes important. This provides a qubit regularization suitable for quantum computation of this bosonic field theory.
