# Lattice Boltzmann simulations of a two-dimensional Fermi gas at unitarity

> We present fully nonlinear dissipative fluid dynamics simulations of a trapped two-dimensional Fermi gas at unitarity using a Lattice Boltzmann algorithm. We are able to simulate non-harmonic trapping potentials, temperature-dependent viscosities as well as a discretized version of the ballistic (non-interacting) behavior. Our approach lends itself to direct comparison with experimental data, opening up the possibility of a precision determination of transport coefficients in the unitary Fermi gas. Furthermore, we predict the presence of a non-hydrodynamic component in the quadrupole mode, which should be observable experimentally.

## Key claim

The Lattice Boltzmann method can simulate fully nonlinear dissipative fluid dynamics of a trapped two-dimensional Fermi gas at unitarity, including non-harmonic trapping potentials and temperature-dependent viscosities, but the approach is only quantitatively accurate for the hydrodynamic regime (small relaxation times) and provides only a qualitative description of the non-interacting (ballistic) regime and of non-hydrodynamic modes.

## Method

The authors discretize the Boltzmann equation with a BGK collision term onto a D2Q25 velocity lattice (25 discrete velocities in two spatial dimensions), using Hermite polynomial expansions to represent the equilibrium distribution and force terms. They simulate small-amplitude collective oscillations (sloshing, breathing, and quadrupole modes) of a trapped 2D Fermi gas, extracting frequencies and damping rates as functions of the dimensionless relaxation time ω⊥τR, and compare numerical results to analytic solutions of the linearized Boltzmann equation. For the interacting case, they implement a tabulated equation of state from PhysRevLett.112.135302 and simulate a Gaussian trapping potential.

## Key numbers

- Breathing mode frequency wB = 2 (in units of the base frequency ω⊥), undamped (ΓB = 0) for an ideal gas in a harmonic trap.
- Quadrupole mode frequency wQ = √2 in the hydrodynamic limit ω⊥τR → 0, increasing to wQ = 2 in the ballistic limit ω⊥τR → ∞.
- Hydrodynamic quadrupole damping rate ΓQ,0 ≃ ω⊥τR for ω⊥τR ≪ 1, and ΓQ,0 ≃ 1/(4ω⊥τR) for ω⊥τR ≫ 1.
- Non-hydrodynamic quadrupole damping rate ΓQ,1 ≃ (1 − 2ω⊥²τR²)/(ω⊥τR) for ω⊥τR ≪ 1, and ΓQ,1 ≃ 1/(2ω⊥τR) for ω⊥τR ≫ 1.
- Turnover from hydrodynamic to ballistic regime occurs at ω⊥τR ∼ 0.5.
- Numerical LB results agree quantitatively with analytic results for wB, ΓB, and ΓQ,0 even outside the hydrodynamic regime, but wQ disagrees for ω⊥τR ≳ 0.5.
- The D2Q25 lattice has lattice speed cL² = 1 − √(2/5).

## Context

This work extends analytic hydrodynamic and kinetic treatments of cold Fermi gases (Schafer 2007, Schaefer 2009, 2013PhRvA..87d3612B) by providing a fully numerical method capable of handling non-ideal equations of state and non-harmonic trapping potentials. It is closely related to numerical solutions of the Boltzmann equation using the test particle method (Pantel 2014), the relaxation time approximation (0295-5075-97-1-16003, PhysRevA.85.045601), and moment approximations (2013PhRvA..88e3616C, 2014JPhCS.497a2028U), but offers lower computational cost and flexibility for non-ideal equations of state at the cost of poorer accuracy in the non-interacting regime. The prediction of a non-hydrodynamic component in the quadrupole mode, analogous to quasinormal modes in relativistic fluid dynamics (Kovtun 2005, Heller 2013), opens the possibility of experimentally determining the radius of convergence of the hydrodynamic approximation in strongly coupled quantum fluids.
