# Description of single-Lambda hypernuclei with relativistic point coupling model

> We extend the relativistic point coupling model to single-$Λ$ hypernuclei. For this purpose, we add $N$-$Λ$ effective contact couplings to the model Lagrangian, and determine the parameters by fitting to the experimental data for $Λ$ binding energies. Our model well reproduces the data over a wide range of mass region although some of our interactions yield the reverse ordering of the spin-orbit partners from that of nucleons for heavy hypernuclei. The consistency of the interaction with the quark model predictions is also discussed.

## Key claim

The relativistic point coupling model can be extended to single-Lambda hypernuclei by adding effective N-Lambda contact couplings, and the model reproduces experimental Lambda binding energies over a wide mass range; however, some of the fitted interactions yield a reverse ordering of the spin-orbit partners compared to nucleons for heavy hypernuclei, a result that is not excluded by current experimental data.

## Method

The authors extended the relativistic point coupling (RPC) model for normal nuclei by adding N-Lambda four-fermion contact couplings, derivative couplings, and a tensor coupling term to the Lagrangian, corresponding to scalar, vector, and tensor couplings between nucleons and the Lambda particle. They assumed spherical symmetry and used the PC-F1 parameter set for the N-N part, then fitted five N-Lambda coupling constants to experimental Lambda binding energies for s, p, d, f, and g orbitals across six hypernuclei (O-16, Ca-40, V-51, Y-89, La-139, Pb-208) plus the spin-orbit splitting of the p orbital in O-16, minimizing chi-squared per degree of freedom.

## Key numbers

* The best-fit parameter set PCY-S1 yields a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.54, with scalar coupling alpha_S^{(N Lambda)} = -2.0305 x 10^{-4} MeV^{-2}, vector coupling alpha_V^{(N Lambda)} = 1.6548 x 10^{-4} MeV^{-2}, and tensor coupling alpha_T^{(N Lambda)} = -1.0603 x 10^{-7} MeV^{-3}.
* The ratios of the resulting N-Lambda coupling constants to the naive quark model expectations are R = 0.79 for the scalar coupling, R = 0.96 for the vector coupling, and R = 1.37 for the tensor coupling.
* The spin-orbit splitting for the p orbital of Lambda in O-16 is constrained to be between 300 keV and 600 keV, based on the model-dependent value from the literature used as a fitting criterion.
* Without the tensor coupling (PCY-S2), the scalar and vector couplings are suppressed to R = 0.16 and R = 0.08 respectively, with chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.85.
* The parameter set PCY-S4, which omits the vector derivative term, yields chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.92 and does not show the inversion of spin-orbit partner ordering.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic point coupling model, previously applied to normal nuclei by Burvenich et al. (2002) and Nikolaus et al. (1992), to the hypernuclear sector, providing a zero-range alternative to meson-exchange relativistic mean field models for hypernuclei such as those by Sugahara and Toki (1994) and Song et al. (2010). The model is similar in spirit to the chiral SU(3) approach of Finelli et al. (2009) but determines all parameters phenomenologically rather than from chiral perturbation theory. The results enable future relativistic calculations for hypernuclei on three-dimensional meshes, overcoming the variational collapse problem, and provide a foundation for extending the point coupling model to multi-Lambda and Xi hypernuclei.
