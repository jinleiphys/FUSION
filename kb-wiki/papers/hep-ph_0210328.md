# Bose-Einstein Correlations as correlations of fluctuations

> The limitations of the recently proposed new method of numerical modelling of Bose-Einstein correlations (BEC) are explicitly demonstrated. It is then argued that BEC should still be considered as emerging from the correlations of fluctuations, however they have to be modelled first in any Monte Carlo event generator (MCEG) and not added {\it a posteriori} to the existing output of some MCEG.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that their previously proposed method for numerically modeling Bose-Einstein correlations (BEC) by reallocating charges in existing Monte Carlo event generator (MCEG) output produces unwanted anticorrelations between unlike-sign particle pairs, and they argue that BEC must instead be built into the MCEG from the very beginning rather than added a posteriori.

## Method

The authors first test their earlier algorithm, which takes the output of a MCEG (with fixed particle positions and multiplicities) and reassigns charges to create "elementary emitting cells" (EEC) with geometrically distributed particle multiplicities, fitting the resulting correlation function C₂(Q) to DELPHI e⁺e⁻ data at W=91.2 GeV. They then construct a toy model where particles are allocated to EECs from the start, with the number of cells following a Poisson distribution and the number of like-sign particles per cell following a geometrical distribution, using a Gaussian energy uncertainty (width σ) to produce the observed C₂(Q) structure.

## Key numbers

- The toy model fits DELPHI e⁺e⁻ annihilation data at center-of-mass energy W = 91.2 GeV.
- The mean multiplicity of particles in an EEC is ⟨n⟩ ≈ 1.1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Utyuzh, Wilk, Wlodarczyk, 2002) for modeling BEC via charge reallocation in MCEG output, but explicitly demonstrates a flaw (unlike-sign anticorrelations) that was pointed out by W. Kittel. The paper then returns to an older approach (OMT, likely from the 1990s) where BEC effects are incorporated from the start of event generation, and shows that a toy model with EECs playing the role of "clans" (as in the Negative Binomial multiplicity distribution) can reproduce the data. This enables future development of MCEGs that conserve charge and energy-momentum while intrinsically including BEC.
