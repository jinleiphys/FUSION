# Radiative parton energy loss and baryon stopping in $AA$ collisions

> We study the radiative energy loss contribution to proton stopping in $AA$ collisions. The analyses is performed within the light-cone path integral approach to induced gluon emission. We have found that the radiative correction can fill in partly the midrapidity dip in the net proton rapidity distribution in $AA$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}\sim 10$ GeV. We argue that at $\sqrt{s}\sim 10$ GeV the net proton fluctuations at midrapidity may be dominated by the initial fluctuations of the proton flow, which, to a good accuracy, should be binomial.

## Key claim

The radiative energy loss from diquarks can partly fill in the midrapidity dip in the net proton rapidity distribution in AA collisions at sqrt(s) ~ 10 GeV, but the magnitude of this effect depends strongly on the assumed gluon mass (400–750 MeV). At these energies, the net proton fluctuations at midrapidity may be dominated by initial-state fluctuations of the proton flow, which should be binomial to a good accuracy.

## Method

The analysis uses the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach to induced gluon emission to compute the radiative energy loss of scalar diquarks and quarks traversing nuclear matter in AA collisions. The gluon spectrum is calculated beyond the frozen-size approximation using an oscillator model for the dipole cross section, with the transport coefficient q̂ = 0.01 GeV³. The radiative correction is then incorporated into the diquark distribution function of the quark-gluon string model (QGSM) to obtain the net proton rapidity distribution, which is compared to NA49 data for central Pb+Pb collisions at E = 40 GeV.

## Key numbers

- For a Pb nucleus, the fractional radiative energy loss ΔE/E for diquarks at E ~ 1000 GeV is approximately 0.18 for m_g = 400 MeV and 0.062 for m_g = 750 MeV.
- The radiative correction increases the net proton spectrum at y = 0 by a factor of about 1.35 for m_g = 400 MeV and 1.12 for m_g = 750 MeV in central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 8.76 GeV.
- The estimated diffusion width for baryon rapidity fluctuations is ⟨Δy_d(τ_f)²⟩^{1/2} ≈ 0.3, based on an initial temperature T₀ ≈ 170–175 MeV and freeze-out time τ_f ≈ 13 fm.
- The scalar ud diquark formfactor has Q²₀ ≈ 10 GeV², which is much larger than the typical virtuality scale for induced gluon emission (~ m_g² ≲ 0.5 GeV²).

## Context

This work extends the quark-gluon string model (QGSM) of Capella et al. (1994, 1996, 1999) by adding radiative energy loss as a new mechanism for baryon stopping, which was previously absent in the QGSM framework. The study is motivated by the beam energy scan program at RHIC and future NICA experiments searching for the QCD critical point, and it provides a baseline for understanding net proton fluctuations that must be accounted for before critical point signals can be identified. The finding that net proton fluctuations at sqrt(s) ~ 10 GeV are close to binomial supports the diquark mechanism of baryon number transfer over the alternative quark-based picture of Kovchegov (2002), and suggests that the absence of a clear critical point signal in STAR data (2014) at these energies is not surprising.
