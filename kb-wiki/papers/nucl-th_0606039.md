# J/psi production from charm coalescence in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> J/psi production and collective flow is studied with a coalescence model based on phase space distribution of charm quarks from a multi-phase transport model simulation of relativistic heavy ion collisions. Both the yield and the flow of J/psi particles are sensitive to charm quark final state interactions. As the charm quark rescattering cross section increases from 3 mb to 10 mb, J/psi elliptic flow increases faster than corresponding light hadron elliptic flows. The v_2(p_t) of J/psi crosses that of D mesons to reach a value that is about the peak value of the D meson flow but at a higher p_t. As J/psi elliptic flow has only contributions from charm quarks, it complements D meson elliptic flow in reflecting charm properties in the Quark-Gluon Plasma.

# J/psi production from charm coalescence in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
J/psi production from charm coalescence in relativistic heavy ion collisions is sensitive to charm quark final state interactions, with the yield and elliptic flow depending on the charm quark rescattering cross section; however, the study only considers coalescence production and does not account for initial production from nucleon-nucleon collisions, binding energy effects, or feeddown from higher charmonium resonances.

## Method
The study uses a phase-space coalescence model applied to charm quark freeze-out distributions from a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model simulation of Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. Charm quarks are evolved using screened Coulomb cross sections of 3 mb and 10 mb, and J/psi momentum distributions are computed via a Wigner function formalism with a Gaussian spatial wave function and an rms radius of 0.5 fm.

## Key numbers
- J/psi yield in central collisions with 3 mb cross section is larger than the 10 mb case by a factor of about 2.4
- For a charm production cross section of 0.6 mb in nucleon-nucleon collisions, the J/psi yield decreases to about 20% of the 1.4 mb result
- Average p_t^2 is about 3 GeV^2 in the 10 mb case and about 1.5 GeV^2 in the 3 mb case
- J/psi elliptic flow v_2(p_t) crosses that of D mesons and reaches about the peak value of D meson elliptic flow but at a higher p_t
- Charm quark rescattering cross sections studied are 3 mb and 10 mb

## Context
This work extends coalescence and recombination models for J/psi production at RHIC energies, building on prior studies by Thews (2005), Grandchamp and Rapp (2003), and others who proposed charm recombination as a significant contribution to J/psi yield. The results complement D meson elliptic flow studies from Zhang (2005) using the same AMPT model, and the centrality dependence of the coalescence yield shows similar shape to recombination calculations by Grandchamp and Rapp (2003). The study enables combined open and hidden charm measurements to provide a more complete picture of charm quark evolution in the Quark-Gluon Plasma.
