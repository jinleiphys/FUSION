# Higher harmonics of azimuthal anisotropy in relativistic heavy ion collisions in HYDJET++ model

> The LHC data on azimuthal anisotropy harmonics from PbPb collisions at center-of-mass energy 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair are analyzed and interpreted in the framework of the HYDJET++ model. The cross-talk of elliptic $v_2$ and triangular $v_3$ flow in the model generates both even and odd harmonics of higher order. Comparison with the experimental data shows that this mechanism is able to reproduce the $p_{\rm T}$ and centrality dependencies of quadrangular flow $v_4$, and also the basic trends for pentagonal $v_5$ and hexagonal $v_6$ flows.

## Key claim

The HYDJET++ model can reproduce LHC data on the transverse momentum and centrality dependencies of quadrangular flow \(v_4\) and the basic trends for pentagonal \(v_5\) and hexagonal \(v_6\) flows in PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair, but only if the higher harmonics arise solely from the interference of the lower elliptic (\(v_2\)) and triangular (\(v_3\)) harmonics, without introducing intrinsic higher-order eccentricities.

## Method

The HYDJET++ model combines a soft "thermal" hadronic state from a parametrized hydrodynamic freeze-out hypersurface with a hard component from the PYQUEN partonic energy loss model. To generate higher harmonics, the freeze-out hypersurface was modulated with a triangular coefficient \(\epsilon_3(b)\) and the maximal transverse flow rapidity was modulated with a quadrangular coefficient \(\rho_{4u}(b)\), both expressed as simple functions of the initial ellipticity \(\epsilon_0(b)\). The model was tuned by fitting the proportionality factors to the \(v_3(p_{\rm T})\) and \(v_4(p_{\rm T})\) data, and the resulting \(v_n\) were compared to ATLAS and CMS event-plane, cumulant, and Lee-Yang zero measurements.

## Key numbers

- PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV per nucleon pair.
- HYDJET++ reproduces \(v_2\), \(v_3\), and \(v_4\) up to \(p_{\rm T} \sim 5\) GeV/\(c\) and up to 40% centrality.
- The model underestimates \(v_5\{{\rm EP}\}\) for central and semi-central collisions up to \(\sigma/\sigma_{\rm geo} \approx 20\%\), but agrees for more peripheral collisions.
- For hexagonal flow, \(v_6(\Psi_2^{\rm RP})\) in the model underestimates ATLAS \(v_6\{{\rm EP}\}\) for most central events, but starting from 20-30% centralities the model and data approximately agree.
- In the model, \(v_4 = 0\) if elliptic flow is absent; \(v_5 = 0\) if either \(v_2 = 0\) or \(v_3 = 0\); \(v_6 = 0\) if both \(v_2 = 0\) and \(v_3 = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the HYDJET++ analysis of LHC data on multiplicity, spectra, elliptic flow, and femtoscopic correlations (Lokhtin 2012) to higher harmonics \(v_3\) through \(v_6\). It contrasts with the AMPT model (Xu 2011), which describes \(v_2\) through \(v_4\) for semi-central collisions, and the IP-Glasma+MUSIC model (Gale 2012), which reproduces \(v_2\) through \(v_5\) via event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics. The results suggest that higher harmonics are not independent probes of the early phase, as they receive large contributions from \(v_2\) and \(v_3\) interference, and that future data on \(v_5\) may require introducing an intrinsic pentagonal eccentricity \(\epsilon_5(b)\).
