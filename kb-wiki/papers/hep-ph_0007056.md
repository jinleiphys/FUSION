# Low energy analysis of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors

> We analyze the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon to fourth order in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory. We employ the recently proposed infrared regularization scheme and show that the convergence of the chiral expansion is improved as compared to the heavy fermion approach. We also discuss the inclusion of vector mesons and obtain an accurate description of all four nucleon form factors for momentum transfer squared up to Q^2 \simeq 0.4 GeV^2.

## Key claim

The authors show that using infrared regularization in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory improves the convergence of the chiral expansion for nucleon electromagnetic form factors compared to the heavy baryon approach, but the pure chiral expansion to fourth order can only accurately describe the neutron charge form factor; the other three form factors require the inclusion of vector mesons to achieve a good description up to momentum transfer squared of about 0.4 GeV².

## Method

The authors performed a complete one-loop analysis of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors to fourth order in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory using the infrared regularization scheme of Becher and Leutwyler. They computed tree graphs with insertions from the effective Lagrangian up to fourth order and one-loop diagrams with lowest-order couplings and at most one insertion from the second-order Lagrangian, then incorporated vector mesons in a chirally symmetric manner using parameters taken from dispersion-theoretical analyses.

## Key numbers

- The chiral description in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory already fails for momentum transfer squared of about Q² ≃ 0.2 GeV²
- The infrared regularized relativistic approach achieves an accurate description of all four nucleon form factors for momentum transfer squared up to Q² ≃ 0.4 GeV²
- The isovector spectral function has its threshold at t₀ = 4M_π² and a singularity on the second Riemann sheet at t_c = 4M_π² - M_π⁴/m_N² = 3.98M_π²
- The ratio μ = M_π/m_N ≃ 1/7 appears throughout the loop calculations

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nucleon electromagnetic form factors in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory by BKKM and BFHM, which found the chiral description fails above Q² ≃ 0.2 GeV², and addresses the problem that the heavy mass expansion distorts the analytic structure of the isovector spectral function as pointed out in spectral function studies. The infrared regularization scheme developed by Becher and Leutwyler (2000) is applied here to preserve correct relativistic analyticity properties, and the results are compared to dispersion-theoretical analyses by Hoehler (1976) and Mergell, Meissner, and Drechsel (1996). The approach enables a model-independent interpretation of low-energy electron-nucleon scattering data, particularly for the neutron electric form factor, and can be extended to include vector meson contributions without introducing additional free parameters.
