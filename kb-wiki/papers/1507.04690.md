# Elliptic flow difference of charged pions in heavy-ion collisions

> Recently, the STAR collaboration at RHIC has presented experimental evidence for the correlation between the elliptic flow difference of charged pions and charge asymmetry as a possible signal of the chiral magnetic wave. We demonstrate that the STAR results can be understood within the standard viscous hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The STAR collaboration's observed linear correlation between the elliptic flow difference of charged pions and charge asymmetry, previously interpreted as a possible signal of the chiral magnetic wave, can be reproduced by standard viscous hydrodynamics without exotic mechanisms, provided one accounts for the nontrivial relationship between charge asymmetry and isospin chemical potential at high freezeout temperature.

## Method

The authors analytically compute the elliptic flow difference \(\Delta v_2^{\pi}\) using the anisotropic Gubser flow coupled with conserved currents, finding it proportional to shear viscosity \(\eta\) and isospin chemical potential \(\mu_I\). They then relate the experimentally measured charge asymmetry \(A_{ch}\) to the chemical potentials using a statistical hadron model, with parameterizations of \(\mu_I\) and \(\mu_S\) as functions of \(\mu_B\) extracted from fits to SPS-to-RHIC data and lattice QCD results. The centrality and energy dependence of the slope parameter \(r\) is derived from the scaling relation \(r \sim (\epsilon/S^2)(dN/dy)\) for centrality and \(r \sim (\mu_I/T)/A_{ch} \times dN/dy\) for energy dependence.

## Key numbers

* Slope parameter \(r = 0.032\) measured by STAR in the 30-40% centrality range at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, used as input to fix the normalization \(v_2^{\pi,ideal}K = 0.32\) (for the parameterization with intercepts) or \(v_2^{\pi,ideal}K = 0.28\) (for the lattice-motivated parameterization).
* At freezeout temperature \(T = 159\) MeV, the linear coefficients relating \(A_{ch}\) to chemical potentials are \(c = 0.383\), \(c' = 5.45\), and \(c'' = 1.45\) in units of GeV\(^{-1}\).
* The extracted Knudsen number \(K \approx 2.78\) for 30-40% centrality at 200 GeV, which when converted to the normalization of Bhalerao (2005) gives \(K_{\text{Ref.[13]}} \approx 0.26\), consistent with the range \(1/7 < K_{\text{Ref.[13]}} < 0.5\).
* The parameterizations for chemical potentials (in MeV) are: \(\mu_I = -0.0308\mu_B + 2.77\times10^{-8}\mu_B^3\) and \(\mu_S = 0.249\mu_B - 1.09\times10^{-7}\mu_B^3\) (lattice-motivated); or \(\mu_I = -0.293 - 0.0264\mu_B\) and \(\mu_S = 1.032 + 0.232\mu_B\) (with intercepts).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytic calculation of \(\Delta v_2^{\pi}\) for anisotropic Gubser flow (Hatta, 2015) and directly addresses the STAR collaboration's experimental results (Adamczyk, 2015) which had been interpreted as evidence for the chiral magnetic wave (Burnier, 2011). The paper contradicts alternative non-exotic explanations proposed by Bzdak (2013) and Campbell (2013) that were already disfavored by data, and provides a standard hydrodynamics baseline against which future CMW searches must be compared. The results enable predictions for triangular flow differences \(\Delta v_3^{\pi}\) and for kaon and proton elliptic flow differences, which can be tested with wider \(p_T\) coverage than the STAR measurement of \(0.15 < p_T < 0.5\) GeV.
