# Radial flow induced by inhomogeneous magnetic field in heavy ion collisions

> In this paper, we study the effects of an in-homogeneous magnetic field on the quark gluon plasma dynamics, within the Magneto-Hydrodynamic framework. We have investigated the effect of an inhomogeneous external magnetic field on the transverse expansion of in-viscid fluid created in high energy nuclear collisions. Transverse velocity and energy density are modified by the presence of the magnetic field. This effects can also influence the transverse momentum spectrum for particles at the freeze-out surface. In this context we obtained an interesting comparison with data extracted from heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an inhomogeneous external magnetic field, with a specific space-time profile, induces non-zero radial flow in an inviscid quark-gluon plasma, modifying the transverse velocity and energy density; however, the analysis is a perturbation solution valid only for a weak magnetic field, and the resulting proton transverse momentum spectra systematically underestimate experimental data except at low transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic ideal magneto-hydrodynamic (RMHD) equations for a boost-invariant, inviscid fluid with infinite electrical conductivity, treating the magnetic field as a weak perturbation. They assume a specific azimuthal magnetic field profile \(b_\phi^2(\tau, x_\perp)=B_c^2 \tau^{-1}\sqrt{\alpha} x_\perp e^{-\alpha x_\perp^2}\) and derive partial differential equations for the transverse velocity \(u_\perp\) and the first-order correction to the energy density \(\epsilon_1\). The transverse momentum spectra for pions and protons are then computed using the Cooper-Frye freeze-out formula and compared to PHENIX data from central Au-Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- The unperturbed energy density is taken as \(\epsilon_0(\tau)=\epsilon_c / \tau^{4/3}\), where \(\epsilon_c\) is a constant.
- The magnetic field profile is \(b_\phi^2(\tau, x_\perp)=B_c^2 \tau^{-1}\sqrt{\alpha} x_\perp e^{-\alpha x_\perp^2}\), with \(B_c\) and \(\alpha\) as parameters.
- Freeze-out temperatures of 140, 150, and 160 MeV are used for the transverse momentum spectrum calculations.
- The degeneracy factor is \(g_i=2\) for both pions and protons in the Cooper-Frye formula.

## Context

This work extends the Bjorken model by incorporating an inhomogeneous external magnetic field into the relativistic magneto-hydrodynamic description of heavy-ion collisions, building on prior studies by Tuchin (2013) and Gursoy (2014) on magnetic field effects in QGP. The results are compared to experimental data from PHENIX and to the Gubser flow model, which the authors note overestimates the data. The paper suggests that light pions are more sensitive to the weak magnetic field than heavy protons, and points to the need for including additional effects beyond the present model to better reproduce proton spectra.
