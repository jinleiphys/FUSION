# Hyperonic uncertainties in neutron stars, mergers and supernovae

> In this work we delve into the temperature-dependent Equation of State (EoS) of baryonic matter within the framework of the FSU2H$^*$ hadronic model, which comprehensively incorporates hyperons and is suitable for relativistic simulations of neutron star mergers and supernovae. To assess the impact of the uncertainties in the hyperonic sector on astrophysical observables, we introduce two additional models, namely FSU2H$^*$L and FSU2H$^*$U. These models cover the entire spectrum of variability of hyperonic potentials, as derived from experimental data. Our investigations reveal that these uncertainties extend their influence not only to the relative abundances of various particle species but also to the EoS itself and, consequently, have an impact on the global properties of both cold and hot neutron stars. Notably, their effects become more pronounced at large temperatures, owing to the increased presence of hyperons. These findings have direct implications for the outcomes of relativistic simulations of neutron star mergers and supernovae, emphasizing the need of accounting for hyperonic uncertainties to ensure the accuracy and reliability of such simulations in astrophysical contexts.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that uncertainties in hyperonic potentials, derived from scarce experimental data, propagate significantly into the equation of state (EoS) of baryonic matter and the global properties of neutron stars, with effects becoming more pronounced at high temperatures due to increased hyperon abundance; however, the entropy per particle shows only mild dependence on these uncertainties once hyperons are present.

## Method

The authors employ the relativistic mean-field (RMF) FSU2H$^*$ hadronic model, which includes the full baryon octet (n, p, $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, $\Xi$) interacting via $\sigma$, $\omega$, $\rho$, $\sigma^*$, and $\phi$ mesons. They construct two additional parametrizations, FSU2H$^*$L and FSU2H$^*$U, by varying the hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon coupling constants to cover the lower and upper limits of experimental uncertainties in hyperonic potentials, then compute the composition, EoS, and stellar properties for temperatures from 1 to 80 MeV and charge fractions from 0.01 to 0.5.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda$ hyperon potential at saturation density: $U_{\Lambda} = -28$ MeV (central value), with uncertainty band $(-30, -25)$ MeV
- $\Xi$ hyperon potential uncertainty band: $U_{\Xi} = (-24, -10)$ MeV
- $\Sigma$ hyperon potential uncertainty band: $U_{\Sigma} = (10, 50)$ MeV
- $\Lambda\Lambda$ bond energy uncertainty: $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = (-6, 0)$ MeV at density $\rho_0/5$
- Temperature range explored: $T = 0 - 100$ MeV
- Baryon density range explored: $\rho_B = \rho_0/2 - 6\rho_0$, where $\rho_0$ is saturation density
- Charge fraction range explored: $Y_Q = 0.01 - 0.6$
- For $Y_Q = 0.01$ at low temperature, $\Sigma^-$ hyperons appear before $\Lambda$ in the FSU2H$^*$L model, while in FSU2H$^*$U they appear above $0.6$ fm$^{-3}$
- At $Y_Q = 0.5$ and $T=80$ MeV, the $\Sigma^+$ abundance in FSU2H$^*$L reaches that of neutrons and $\Lambda$ at high densities

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous FSU2H$^*$ model (Kochankovski 2022) by systematically exploring hyperonic uncertainties and their propagation to astrophysical observables, going beyond the standard $\Gamma$-law thermal treatment shown to be insufficient for hyperonic matter in mergers (Bauswein 2010, Raithel 2021, Blacker 2023). The results provide EoS tables spanning the temperature, charge fraction, and density ranges needed for relativistic simulations of neutron star mergers and supernova evolution, enabling more realistic modeling that accounts for hyperonic uncertainties.
