# Particle spectra in the integrated hydrokinetic model at RHIC Beam-Energy-Scan energies

> We study light-hadron production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7-39$ GeV using an extended Integrated HydroKinetic Model (iHKMe). Focusing on transverse momentum spectra, we investigate the sensitivity to key model parameters, particularly the thermalization timescale. We consider two distinct equations of state: one featuring a crossover and the other a first-order phase transition. In both cases, thermalization begins shortly before full nuclear overlap and lasts approximately 1~fm/$c$ across all energies. Both equations of state provide a similarly good description of the soft particle momentum spectra once the other parameters are slightly adjusted. The most pronounced differences arise at the lower RHIC BES energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$~GeV, particularly in proton and kaon yields, reflecting their sensitivity to the freeze-out parameters.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that at RHIC Beam-Energy-Scan energies (7.7–39 GeV), both a crossover equation of state and a first-order phase transition equation of state provide similarly good descriptions of soft particle momentum spectra once model parameters are slightly adjusted, with the most pronounced differences appearing only at the lowest energy of 7.7 GeV in proton and kaon yields.

## Method
The authors used an extended Integrated HydroKinetic Model (iHKMe) that combines UrQMD cascade simulations for pre-equilibrium dynamics with relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (vHLLE code) for the equilibrated phase, then returns to UrQMD for the hadronic afterburner stage. They performed 750 parameter sets using Latin hypercube sampling at 14.5 GeV to study correlations between five free parameters (thermalization start time, relaxation time, shear viscosity to entropy ratio, switching energy density, and Gaussian smearing parameter) and transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and (anti)protons. The model was then calibrated against STAR data for Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies from 7.7 to 39 GeV in the 20–30% centrality class.

## Key numbers
- Thermalization begins at proper times close to 0.75 times the nuclear overlap time, with deviations not exceeding ±0.2 fm/c across all energies studied
- The thermalization duration (relaxation time) is approximately 1 fm/c across all collision energies from 7.7 to 39 GeV
- At 7.7 GeV, the optimal thermalization start time differs by about 0.2 fm/c between 0–5% and 30–40% centrality classes; at 39 GeV the difference is no more than 0.1 fm/c
- The maximum proper time on the switching hypersurface increases from 9.2 fm/c at 39 GeV to 9.8 fm/c at 7.7 GeV for most central collisions; for 20–30% centrality the corresponding times are 8.8 fm/c and 11.5 fm/c
- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio was fixed at η/s = 0.08
- The Gaussian smearing parameter R was set to 0.5 fm
- The switching energy density ε_sw was constrained using antiproton data and kept constant across collision energies

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous introduction of the extended Integrated HydroKinetic Model (Adzhymambetov 2024) to the RHIC Beam Energy Scan energy range, building on hybrid approaches that combine hydrodynamics with transport models such as those by Steinheimer (2012), Shen (2017), and Akamatsu (2018). Unlike many hybrid models that segment evolution into distinct phases in space-time, iHKMe integrates microscopic and macroscopic descriptions concurrently through a continuous thermalization function. The results enable systematic comparison of crossover versus first-order phase transition scenarios at BES energies and provide calibrated parameters for future studies of fluctuations and critical point signatures.
