# Probing short range correlations in Heavy-Ion Double Charge Exchange reactions

> The Majorana Double Charge Exchange (MDCE) provides a suitable environment for studying the dynamics of the neutrinoless double beta ($0νββ$) decay, particularly short-range correlations among nucleons. The study of the pion potential is essential in this respect, as it represents the strong interaction counterpart of the neutrino potential, driving nucleon correlations in $0νββ$ decay. Numerical studies on pion potential have revealed an effective range of about 1 fm with slight dispersion around this value, confirming the short-range character of the MDCE process.

# Probing short range correlations in Heavy-Ion Double Charge Exchange reactions

## Key claim
The pion potential in Majorana Double Charge Exchange (MDCE) reactions has an effective range of about 1 fm with slight dispersion, confirming the short-range character of the MDCE process; however, this result is obtained under the approximation of replacing the sum over intermediate states with the identity operator, as commonly performed in the neutrinoless double beta decay framework.

## Method
The authors evaluated the pion potential analytically in closed form by expanding the pion-nucleon isovector T-matrix in partial waves, separating S-wave and P-wave contributions. They performed numerical calculations for the reaction of an 18O beam colliding on a 48Ti target at laboratory energy 270 MeV, using a collinear exchange charged pion momentum of 400 MeV/c. The monopole moments of the pion potential components were computed to characterize strength, range, and spatial extension.

## Key numbers
- The pion potential effective range is approximately 1 fm, with variances ranging from 0.03 fm² for the P-wave component up to 0.10 fm² for the S-wave component
- For the P-wave component (U11): mean radius ⟨r⟩ = 1.04 fm, root-mean-square radius = 1.29 fm, variance = 0.03 fm²
- For the S-wave component (U00): mean radius ⟨r⟩ = 1.89 fm, root-mean-square radius = 2.53 fm, variance = 0.10 fm²
- The P-wave component (U22): mean radius ⟨r⟩ = 1.49 fm, root-mean-square radius = 1.70 fm, variance = 0.06 fm²
- The mixed component (U12): mean radius ⟨r⟩ = 1.06 fm, root-mean-square radius = 1.28 fm, variance = 0.03 fm²
- The pion rest mass used as separation scale: mπ ≈ 139 MeV
- The laboratory energy of the 18O beam: Tlab = 270 MeV
- The exchanged pion momentum: p = 400 MeV/c

## Context
This work extends the NUMEN project (Cappuzzello 2018, 2023) which studies heavy-ion Double Charge Exchange reactions as a surrogate for neutrinoless double beta decay. The MDCE mechanism (Lenske 2024) is distinguished from competing processes like Transfer Double Charge Exchange (Ferreira 2022, 2025) and Double Single Charge Exchange (Bellone 2020, 2025). The pion potential is presented as the strong interaction counterpart of the neutrino potential in 0νββ decay (Tomoda 1991, Simkovic 2009), and the results enable future extraction of pion potentials from experimental cross sections, providing a direct probe of short-range two-body correlations in nuclei.
