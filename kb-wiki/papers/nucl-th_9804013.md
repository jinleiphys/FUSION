# $K^-/K^+$ ratios in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study $K^-/K^+$ ratios as a function of centrality (participant nucleon number), transverse mass ($m_t$), and rapidity, in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies between 1A and 2A GeV. We use the relativistic transport model that includes expicitly the strangeness degrees of freedom and consider two scenarios for kaon properties in dense matter, one with and one without medium modifications of their properties. In both scenarios, The $K^-/K^+$ ratio does not change very much with the centrality, while the $K/π$ and ${\bar K}/π$ ratios increase with increasing centrality. Significant differences are predicted, both in magnitudes and shapes, for the $m_t$ spectra and rapidity distributions of $K^-/K^+$ ratio. Experimental measurement of these ratios, currently under investigation by the FOPI, KaoS, E866, and E895 collaborations, will be useful in revealing the kaon in-medium properties.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the $K^-/K^+$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions at 1–2A GeV is nearly independent of centrality (participant nucleon number) in both scenarios with and without kaon medium modifications, but that the transverse mass spectra and rapidity distributions of the ratio show significant differences between the two scenarios, which can be used to reveal kaon in-medium properties.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic transport model based on an effective chiral Lagrangian (Furnstahl, Tang, and Serot) to simulate Ni+Ni, Ru+Ru, and Au+Au collisions at beam energies between 1A and 2A GeV. They included explicit strangeness degrees of freedom and compared two scenarios: one with kaon and antikaon in-medium potentials (scalar attraction and vector repulsion) and one without any medium modifications. Kaon production cross sections from pion-baryon and baryon-baryon collisions were taken from resonance and one-boson-exchange models, while antikaon production included strangeness-exchange processes with cross sections from detailed balance.

## Key numbers

- Without kaon medium effects, the $K^-/K^+$ ratio ranges from 0.006 in Ru+Ru at 1.69A GeV, to 0.01 in Ni+Ni at 1.8A GeV, to 0.0075 in Au+Au at 2A GeV.
- With kaon medium effects included, the $K^-/K^+$ ratio increases by about a factor of 4 for all systems considered, resulting from a factor of 3 increase in antikaon yield and about 35% reduction in kaon yield.
- For Ru+Ru collisions at 1.69A GeV with medium effects, the $K^-/K^+$ ratio increases from about 0.02 at $m_t-m_K=0.3$ GeV to about 0.08 when $m_t-m_K$ approaches zero.
- The system compresses to a density of around 3$\rho_0$ for a considerable duration in Au+Au collisions at 1.5A GeV and b=3 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of kaon production in heavy-ion collisions (Li and Ko, 1995; Li et al., 1995, 1996) and the chiral perturbation theory predictions for kaon in-medium properties (Kaplan and Nelson, 1986; Brown et al., 1987; Lee et al., 1995). It directly addresses the need for additional observables to confirm medium effects, as pointed out by Ogilvie et al. (1997, 1998). The predictions are compared with experimental data from the KaoS collaboration (1997) for Ni+Ni and preliminary data from the FOPI collaboration (Leifels, 1997; Hong, 1998) for Ru+Ru and Ni+Ni, and the paper calls for independent data from the E866 and E895 collaborations for Au+Au at 2A GeV to further constrain the critical density for kaon condensation.
