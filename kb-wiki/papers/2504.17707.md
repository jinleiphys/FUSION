# (2+2)D Collective Model based on a relativistic Boltzmann equation in the Isotropization Time Approximation: CoMBolt-ITA

> A new model based on the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the isotropization time approximation is developed to investigate the collective behavior of the quark-gluon plasma produced in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The equation is solved in (2+2)D (two spatial and two momentum-space dimensions). This framework couples pre-equilibrium dynamics with hydrodynamic evolution to simulate the dynamics of quasiparticle evolution. A numerical scheme based on the method of characteristics enables the evolution to begin from a specified initial Boltzmann distribution. In this work, the spatial structure of the initial distribution is modeled using the TrENTo framework. Our results show that a medium initialized at $τ_0$ on the order of 1 [fm/$c$] with a small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio ($η/s = 0.008$) evolves consistently with hydrodynamic simulations, such as those performed using the VISH2+1 code, while discrepancies arise for a medium with $η/s = 0.8$. Furthermore, when initialized with a highly anisotropic momentum distribution in the longitudinal direction at early times, the system exhibits spatially non-uniform thermalization in the transverse plane, leading to the emergence of a nontrivial hypersurface that marks the onset of hydrodynamic applicability. Finally, we compute the $p_T$-spectra for a non-fluctuating initial condition using the hybrid version of CoMBolt-ITA. In this hybrid setup, the description is switched from quasiparticles to hadrons, and UrQMD is used to model the hadron gas dynamics. We compare these results with those obtained from the hybrid VISH2+1 initialized within the same setup. For a small shear viscosity, $η/s = 0.08$, the two results show a good level of consistency, whereas for a larger value, $η/s = 0.8$, a noticeable discrepancy emerges.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the CoMBolt-ITA model, which solves the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the isotropization time approximation in (2+2)D, reproduces hydrodynamic simulations (VISH2+1) for a medium with a small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (eta/s = 0.008) when initialized at tau_0 on the order of 1 fm/c, but that discrepancies arise for a larger value (eta/s = 0.8), with the abstract glossing over the fact that even at the smallest eta/s, the initial structures of the energy density become smoother in VISH2+1 while details persist in CoMBolt-ITA.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation for massless quasiparticles in the isotropization time approximation (ITA) within a boost-invariant (2+2)D framework (two spatial dimensions in the transverse plane and two momentum-space dimensions). The w-integrated Boltzmann distribution F is evolved using the method of characteristics on a 4D grid (2D Cartesian spatial grid times a 2D polar grid in longitudinal velocity v_z and azimuthal angle phi_p), with the collision kernel parameterized by gamma = 1/(5 * C_0^(1/4) * eta/s) where C_0 = 13.9. The initial spatial structure is modeled using the TrENTo framework with p = 0 and nucleon width w = 0.7 fm, and the initial momentum distribution is factorized with a parameter lambda controlling longitudinal anisotropy; for comparisons with hydrodynamics, lambda = 1000 is used to approximate an isotropic distribution. The hybrid version switches from quasiparticle to hadron description at a switching hypersurface and uses UrQMD for the hadron gas dynamics.

## Key numbers

- Initialization time tau_0 = 1.0 fm/c for the comparisons between CoMBolt-ITA and VISH2+1
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratios tested: eta/s = 0.008, 0.08, and 0.8
- TrENTo parameters: p = 0, nucleon width w = 0.7 fm, overall normalization N = 15 for sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- Equation of state constant C_0 = 13.9 for a non-interacting gas of massless quarks and gluons
- Time step parameters: alpha_h = 0.02, h_infinity = 0.005 fm/c
- For the highly anisotropic initial state, lambda = 0.05 is used; for the isotropic case, lambda = 1000
- The grid in v_z is adaptively refined when a segment becomes larger than 0.15

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory approach of Kurkela (2019) and Kurkela (2018) by solving the Boltzmann equation in (2+2)D with full spatial structure from the TrENTo initial state model, rather than using a moment expansion in spatial directions. The model enables the study of spatially non-uniform thermalization in the transverse plane and the emergence of a nontrivial hypersurface marking the onset of hydrodynamic applicability, which is relevant for understanding collective flow in small collision systems such as p-Pb and p-p collisions where the validity of hydrodynamics is questioned. The hybrid version coupling CoMBolt-ITA with UrQMD allows for a complete description from early-time dynamics to final hadronic stage, and a follow-up study by Taghavi (2025) uses the same setup to describe OO collisions at the LHC on an event-by-event fluctuating basis.
