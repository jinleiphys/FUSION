# Proton radioactivity described by covariant density functional theory with Similarity Renormalization Group method

> Half-life of proton radioactivity of spherical proton emitters is studied within the scheme of covariant density functional (CDF) theory, and for the first time the potential barrier that prevents the emitted proton is extracted with the similarity renormalization group (SRG) method, in which the spin-orbit potential along with the others that turn out to be non-negligible can be derived automatically. The spectroscopic factor that is significant is also extracted from the CDF calculations. The estimated half-lives are found in good agreement with the experimental values, which not only confirms the validity of the CDF theory in describing the proton-rich nuclei, but also indicates the prediction power of present approach to calculate the half-lives and in turn to extract the structural information of proton emitters.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for the first time, the potential barrier for proton radioactivity can be extracted from covariant density functional (CDF) theory using the similarity renormalization group (SRG) method, which automatically derives the spin-orbit potential and other relativistic corrections. The half-lives calculated with this approach agree with experimental values within a factor of about 3 for most of the 29 spherical proton emitters studied, but the agreement is conditional on including the spectroscopic factor from CDF+BCS calculations and on using a fitted assault frequency.

## Method

The Dirac equation from CDF theory is reduced to a Schrodinger-type equation using the SRG method, which decouples the upper and lower components of the Dirac spinor and yields a potential barrier consisting of a main term, a spin-orbit term, and a residual correction term. The half-life is then calculated using the WKB approximation for barrier penetrability, combined with the spectroscopic factor extracted from CDF+BCS calculations and a constant assault frequency fitted to the experimental half-lives of 29 spherical proton emitters.

## Key numbers

- Assault frequency fitted as 2.68 x 10^21 s^-1 from the linear relationship between log10(T) and log10(PSp) for 29 emitters
- Statistical correlation coefficient R = 0.992 for the linear fit of log10(Texp) versus log10(PSp)
- For 18 of 29 emitters, calculated half-lives reproduce within experimental and theoretical error bars
- Ratio of calculated half-life to experimental half-life lies within the range from 1/3 to 3 for all emitters
- For most emitters, relative deviations (RD) of Tcal from Texp are less than 50%
- Spin-orbit contribution Cso can be as large as 21.0% for l=5 transitions, but less than 4% for l=0 and 2
- Residual correction contribution Crc can be as large as 13%
- RMSD of log10(T) with full potential V = Vm + Vso + Vrc: 0.211
- RMSD without spectroscopic factor: 0.349; with spectroscopic factor: 0.211
- RMSD with only Vm: 0.242; with Vm+Vso: 0.215; with Vm+Vrc: 0.236
- For 151Lu: Tcal = 129.8 ms (experimental: 127.1 ms), RD = 2.1%
- For 147Tm: Tcal = 4.24 s (experimental: 3.78 s), RD = 12.3%
- For 155Ta: Tcal = 4.1 ms (experimental: 2.9 ms), RD = 41.4%

## Context

This work extends previous applications of CDF theory to proton radioactivity by introducing the SRG method to extract the potential barrier, whereas earlier approaches used the R3Y interaction (Sahu 2011) or non-linear CDF schemes (Ferreira 2011) to calculate half-lives. The method enables the automatic derivation of spin-orbit and relativistic correction terms without additional free parameters, and the good agreement with experimental half-lives for 29 spherical emitters suggests the approach can be used to predict half-lives and extract structural information for proton-rich nuclei near the proton drip line.
