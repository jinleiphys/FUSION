# Realistic Calculations of One- and Two-Hadron Emission Processes off Few- and Many-Body Nuclei

> The exclusive electro-disintegration processes 2H(e,e'p)n, 3He(e,e'p)2H, 3He(e,e'p)pn, 3He(e,e'pp)n and 4He(e,e'p)3H have been calculated using realistic few-body wave functions and treating final state interaction (FSI) effects within a generalized eikonal approach. The semi-inclusive scattering A(e,e'p)X off complex nuclei has been analyzed using many-body wave functions for 16O and 40Ca, obtained within the framework of a linked cluster expansion and taking FSI into account by a Glauber-type approach. The effect of color transparency has also been included by considering the Finite Formation Time (FFT) that the hit hadron needs to reach its asymptotic physical state.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that realistic calculations of one- and two-hadron emission processes, using few-body wave functions from the AV18 interaction and many-body wave functions from a linked-cluster expansion, can reproduce experimental data only when final state interaction (FSI) effects are included via a generalized eikonal or Glauber-type approach; however, the agreement depends on kinematics, and in some cases FSI almost entirely exhausts the cross section rather than revealing the underlying nuclear wave function directly.

## Method

The authors calculated exclusive electro-disintegration processes for 2H, 3He, and 4He using realistic few-body wave functions from the AV18 interaction, treating FSI within a generalized eikonal approximation that includes elastic nucleon-nucleon rescattering. For complex nuclei (16O and 40Ca), they employed many-body wave functions obtained from a linked-cluster expansion of the ground state energy using the Argonne V8' potential, with FSI treated by a Glauber-type approach and color transparency effects included via a finite formation time (FFT) model.

## Key numbers

- For 16O, the ground state energy per particle calculated at first order of the eta-expansion with Harmonic Oscillator single-particle wave functions gave E/A = -4.2 MeV (kinetic energy 22.4 MeV, potential energy -26.6 MeV); with Saxon-Woods wave functions, E/A = -3.1 MeV (kinetic energy 28.4 MeV, potential energy -31.5 MeV)
- The integrated nuclear transparency for 16O was calculated to be 0.5
- For the 4He(e,e'p)3H process, FSI effects were predicted to vanish at Q2 approximately 20 (GeV/c)2 when FFT effects are included, with a dip in the cross section predicted at missing momentum pm approximately 2.2 fm-1
- The average excitation mass m*Av used in the FFT model was taken as 1.8 GeV/c
- For the 3He(e,e'p)(np) process, at missing momentum pm greater than or equal to 440 MeV/c and missing energy Em greater than or equal to 10 MeV, FSI between the struck proton and the (np) pair almost entirely exhausts the cross section

## Context

This work extends the Perugia group's program of calculating electro-nuclear reactions with realistic wave functions, building on prior few-body calculations by Gloeckle and collaborators and the Pisa group, and on variational Monte Carlo results by Pieper and collaborators (2001). The cluster expansion approach for complex nuclei follows the FHNC/SOC method of Fabrocini and collaborators, and the color transparency analysis extends earlier work by Benhar and collaborators (2000) on 4He(e,e'p)3H, with the present model predicting vanishing FSI at high Q2 in contrast to Benhar's model. The results enable future experimental tests at JLab and SLAC to discriminate between different models of hadronic final state rescattering and color transparency.
