# Phenomenological study of light (anti)nuclei, (anti)hypertriton and di-Lambda production at RHIC

> We present the production of light (anti)nuclei, (anti)hypertriton and di-Lambda based on coalescence model in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200GeV$. The invariant yields of \He(\Hebar), \hypert(\hypertbar), \Hee(\Heebar) obtained is found to be consistent with the STAR measurements. The $p_{T}$ integrated yields for di-Lambda $dN_{ΛΛ}/dy \sim 2.23\times10^{-5}$, and is not strongly dependent on the parameter employed for coalescence process. Relative particle ratios of light anti(nuclei) and (anti)hypertriton are explored, and agree with experimental data and thermal model predictions quite well. An exponential reducion behavior is presented for the differential invariant yields with increased baryon number. The production rate reduces by a factor of 1692 (1285) for each additional antinucleon (nucleon) added to antinuclei (nuclei), and the production rate of \Libar is predicted to be $10^{-16}$ which is consistent with STAR result.

# Phenomenological study of light (anti)nuclei, (anti)hypertriton and di-Lambda production at RHIC

## Key claim

The coalescence model reproduces the measured invariant yields of light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, and predicts a di-Lambda yield that is not strongly dependent on the coalescence parameters. The results are consistent with STAR data and do not support the hypothesis of excitation production from a highly correlated vacuum.

## Method

The authors applied a coalescence model to central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, calculating the differential invariant yields versus transverse momentum for protons, antiprotons, Lambda, anti-Lambda, light (anti)nuclei, (anti)hypertriton, and di-Lambda. The model results were compared with STAR experimental data and thermal model predictions, and the exponential scaling of yields with baryon number was fitted to extract reduction factors per additional nucleon or antinucleon.

## Key numbers

- The pT-integrated yield for di-Lambda is dN(Lambda-Lambda)/dy ~ 2.23 x 10^-5.
- The pT-integrated yield for anti-hypertriton is about 4.9 x 10^-5.
- The pT-integrated yield for anti-helium-4 is about 1.10 x 10^-7.
- The production rate reduces by a factor of 1692 for each additional antinucleon added to antinuclei.
- The production rate reduces by a factor of 1285 for each additional nucleon added to nuclei.
- The predicted production rate of anti-lithium-6 is about 10^-16.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model approach previously applied to lighter antinuclei at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2011) to include anti-hypertriton and di-Lambda states. The results are compared with thermal model predictions (PBM, year not specified) and experimental data from STAR and PHENIX. The exponential scaling with baryon number enables predictions for heavier antimatter nuclei such as anti-lithium-6, and the consistency with STAR data constrains alternative production mechanisms such as excitation from the vacuum (newphysics, year not specified).
