# Evolution of magnetic fields in a transversely expanding highly conductive fluid

> Due to the absence of a transverse expansion with respect to the beam direction, the Bjorken flow is unable to describe certain observables in heavy ion collisions. This caveat has motivated the introduction of analytical relativistic hydrodynamics (RH) solutions with transverse expansion, in particular, the 3+1 self-similar (SSF) and Gubser flows. Inspired by recent generalizations of the Bjorken flow to the relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (RMHD), we present a procedure for a generalization of RH solutions to RMHD. Our method is mainly based on symmetry arguments. Using this method, we find the relation between RH degrees of freedom and the magnetic field evolution in the ideal limit for an infinitely conductive fluid, and determine the proper time dependence of the magnetic field in aforementioned flows. In the case of SSF, a family of solutions are found that are related through a certain differential equation. To find the magnetic field evolution in the Gubser flow, we solve RMHD equations for a stationary fluid in a conformally flat $dS^3\times E^1$ spacetime. The result is then Weyl transformed back into the Minkowski spacetime. In this case, the temporal evolution of the magnetic field exhibits a transmission between $1/t$ to $1/t^3$ near the center of the collision. The longitudinal component of the magnetic field is found to be sensitive to the transverse size of the fluid. We also find the radial evolution of the magnetic field for both flows. The radial domain of validity in the case of SSF is highly restricted, in contrast to the Gubser flow. A comparison of the results suggests that the Gubser RMHD may give a more appropriate qualitative picture of the magnetic field decay in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP).

## Key claim

The paper claims that by using symmetry arguments and a similarity variable, one can generalize existing relativistic hydrodynamic solutions with transverse expansion (self-similar flow and Gubser flow) to relativistic magnetohydrodynamics; the resulting magnetic field evolution depends on the transverse size of the fluid and, in the Gubser flow, exhibits a transition from a 1/t to a 1/t^3 decay near the collision center, but the authors note that the self-similar flow solution is only valid in a highly restricted radial domain.

## Method

The authors start from the ideal magnetohydrodynamic limit (infinite conductivity, zero electric field in the fluid rest frame) and impose two symmetry constraints (boost invariance along the beam axis and rotational invariance around it) plus a proper similarity variable to fix the velocity profile. For the self-similar flow (SSF), they solve the Euler equation in Minkowski spacetime to obtain a family of solutions linked by a differential equation; for the Gubser flow, they first solve the RMHD equations for a stationary fluid in a conformally flat dS^3 x E^1 spacetime and then Weyl-transform the result back to Minkowski spacetime.

## Key numbers

- At top RHIC energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV), the magnetic field is estimated to be on the order of 10^18 Gauss.
- In the Bjorken flow generalization, the magnetic field magnitude evolves as B = B_0 (tau_0 / tau), with no transverse expansion.
- In the SSF solution, the magnetic field is given by B = (B_0 / sqrt(1 + alpha_0^2)) (rho_0 / rho)^2 sqrt((r_0 / r)^2 + alpha_0^2 (tau_0 / tau)^2), where alpha_0 is the ratio B_z / B_y at initial time and radius tau_0 and r_0.
- In the Gubser flow solution, the magnetic field magnitude contains a term with the inverse transverse size q, given by B = A_1 sqrt(1/(r^2 tau^2) + beta_0^2 (4q^2 / (1 + q^4 (tau^2 - r^2)^2 + 2q^2 (tau^2 + r^2)))^2).
- The Gaussian ansatz from prior work uses parameters a_1 = 78.2658, b_1 = 79.5457 fm^{-1}, and sigma_r = 3.5 fm for central collisions.
- The pion mass used for normalization is m_pi = 137 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the generalization of Bjorken flow to relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (first presented by Rischke and collaborators) to flows with transverse expansion, specifically the 3+1 self-similar flow introduced by Csorgo and the Gubser flow introduced by Gubser. The authors compare their results to the early-time dynamics model of Huang and the Gaussian ansatz of Roy, based on numerical work by Zakharov. The paper enables future studies of magnetic field decay in the quark-gluon plasma with finite conductivity (beyond the ideal MHD limit) and extensions to noncentral collisions, following the analytical hydrodynamic solution of Bantilan (2018).
