# Improved limits for Higgs-portal dark matter from LHC searches

> Searches for invisible Higgs decays at the Large Hadron Collider constrain dark matter Higgs-portal models, where dark matter interacts with the Standard Model fields via the Higgs boson. While these searches complement dark matter direct-detection experiments, a comparison of the two limits depends on the coupling of the Higgs boson to the nucleons forming the direct-detection nuclear target, typically parameterized in a single quantity $f_N$. We evaluate $f_N$ using recent phenomenological and lattice-QCD calculations, and include for the first time the coupling of the Higgs boson to two nucleons via pion-exchange currents. We observe a partial cancellation for Higgs-portal models that makes the two-nucleon contribution anomalously small. Our results, summarized as $f_N=0.308(18)$, show that the uncertainty of the Higgs-nucleon coupling has been vastly overestimated in the past. The improved limits highlight that state-of-the-art nuclear physics input is key to fully exploiting experimental searches.

## Key claim

The Higgs-nucleon coupling parameter \(f_N\) is determined to be \(0.308(18)\), which dramatically reduces the previously assumed uncertainty range of \(0.260\ldots 0.629\) and tightens the exclusion limits for Higgs-portal dark matter from LHC invisible Higgs decay searches, provided that two-nucleon contributions from pion-exchange currents are included for consistency with direct-detection conventions.

## Method

The authors evaluated the one-body scalar couplings of light quarks \(u,d,s\) by averaging four recent lattice-QCD calculations and combining them with the phenomenological \(\pi N\) sigma-term from Roy-Steiner analyses, then added perturbative corrections for heavy-quark loops. They computed two-body contributions from chiral effective field theory, where the Higgs boson couples to a pion exchanged between two nucleons, using large-scale shell-model diagonalizations with state-of-the-art nuclear interactions for a range of target nuclei including xenon, argon, germanium, and silicon isotopes.

## Key numbers

- \(f_N = 0.308(18)\): final combined value for the Higgs-nucleon coupling, including one-body and two-body contributions.
- One-body contribution: \(f_N^\text{1b} = 0.307(18)\), derived from \(f_u^N+f_d^N = 0.052(12)\) and \(f_s^N = 0.043(20)\) with perturbative error \(\Delta f_N^\text{pert} = 0.005\).
- Two-body contribution: \(f_N^\text{2b} = 1.8(2.1) \times 10^{-3}\), resulting from a partial cancellation between a term proportional to the pion-nucleus structure factor and a term proportional to the nuclear binding energy.
- The \(\pi N\) sigma-term from phenomenology: \(\sigma_{\pi N} = 59.1(3.5)\ \text{MeV}\) (Hoferichter 2015).
- Higgs mass: \(m_h = 125.09(24)\ \text{GeV}\) (Aad 2015).
- Adopted CMS limit on invisible Higgs branching fraction: \(\text{BR}_\text{inv} < 0.20\) at 90% confidence level.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of two-body currents for xenon (Hoferichter 2016) to a broad range of target nuclei, and updates the one-body scalar couplings using recent lattice-QCD results from Durr (2015), Yang (2015), Abdel-Rehim (2016), and Bali (2016), as well as the \(\pi N\) sigma-term from Hoferichter (2015). The result contradicts the large uncertainty range \(f_N = 0.260\ldots 0.629\) used in the most recent ATLAS (Aad 2015) and CMS (Khachatryan 2016) analyses, which relied on older determinations from Young (2009) and Toussaint (2009). The improved limits enable consistent comparison between collider and direct-detection experiments (SuperCDMS, PandaX-II, LUX, XENON1T) for Higgs-portal dark matter models.
