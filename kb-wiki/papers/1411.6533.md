# Recent advances in neutrino astrophysics

> Neutrinos are produced by a variety of sources that comprise our Sun, explosive environments such as core-collapse supernovae, the Earth and the Early Universe. The precise origin of the recently discovered ultra-high energy neutrinos is to be determined yet. These weakly interacting particles give us information on their sources, although the neutrino fluxes can be modified when neutrinos traverse an astrophysical environment. Here we highlight recent advances in neutrino astrophysics and emphasise the important progress in our understanding of neutrino flavour conversion in media.

# Recent advances in neutrino astrophysics

## Key claim
Neutrino flavor conversion in media is far more complex than previously assumed, with new collective phenomena (synchronization, bipolar oscillations, spectral splits) emerging from neutrino-neutrino interactions in supernovae, though the final impact on observable signals and the validity of the mean-field approximation used in most calculations remain open questions.

## Method
The paper reviews the current state of neutrino astrophysics by synthesizing results from solar neutrino experiments (Borexino, SNO, KamLAND), core-collapse supernova simulations (multi-dimensional models including turbulence and hydrodynamical instabilities), and high-energy neutrino telescopes (IceCube, ANTARES). It also surveys theoretical developments in neutrino flavor evolution, including the derivation of extended mean-field descriptions using coherent-state path integrals, the Born-Bogoliubov-Green-Kirkwood-Yvon hierarchy, and the two-particle-irreducible effective action formalism.

## Key numbers
- Solar mass-squared difference: Δm₁₂² = 2.4 × 10⁻⁵ eV²
- Atmospheric mass-squared difference: Δm₂₃² = 7.6 × 10⁻³ eV²
- Solar electron neutrino survival probability for low energy (< 2 MeV): P(νₑ → νₑ) ≈ 1 − ½ sin² 2θ₁₂ ≈ 0.57 (with θ₁₂ = 34°)
- Solar electron neutrino survival probability for high energy (²⁰B spectrum, matter-dominated): P(νₑ → νₑ) → sin²θ₁₂ ≈ 0.31
- Solar energy production via pp chain: 3.84 × 10³³ ergs/s
- Supernova gravitational energy emitted as neutrinos: about 3 × 10⁵³ ergs over 10 seconds
- IceCube collected 37 events with deposited energies from 30 TeV to 2 PeV, with the 2 PeV event being the highest-energy neutrino ever observed
- IceCube detection significance for high-energy astrophysical neutrinos: 5.7 σ

## Context
This review extends the understanding of neutrino flavor conversion beyond the Mikheev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein (MSW) effect first described by Wolfenstein (1977) and Mikheev and Smirnov (1986), incorporating collective effects from neutrino-neutrino interactions first pointed out by Pantaleone (1992). It synthesizes results from the solar neutrino puzzle solved by SNO (Ahmad, 2002) and KamLAND (Eguchi, 2002), and the recent Borexino measurement of pp neutrinos (Bellini, 2014). The paper enables future work on determining the neutrino mass hierarchy from supernova signals (using IceCube or Cherenkov/scintillator detectors) and on assessing the impact of beyond-mean-field corrections (spin coherence and neutrino-antineutrino pairing correlations) on flavor evolution in dense astrophysical environments.
