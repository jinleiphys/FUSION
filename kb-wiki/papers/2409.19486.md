# State-of-the-art of Strangeness in Quark Matter Theory 2024

> I discuss the theoretical developments in related to Strangeness in Quark Matter (SQM) leading up to the SQM2024 conference. These advances include mapping out the Quantum Chromodynamics phase diagram; puzzles that exist in hadron physics from light to heavy particles; and advanced in relativistic hydrodynamics with the inclusion of spin and magnetic fields.

# State-of-the-art of Strangeness in Quark Matter Theory 2024

## Key claim
The paper surveys theoretical developments in high-energy nuclear theory leading up to the SQM2024 conference, but explicitly notes that the QCD critical point cannot yet be determined from first-principle lattice calculations, with its existence and location remaining an open question. The author presents a broad overview of puzzles and open questions rather than a single definitive result.

## Method
The author reviews recent theoretical advances across three main areas: mapping the QCD phase diagram using constraints from heavy-ion collisions, neutron stars, binary neutron star mergers, and low-energy nuclear physics; developments in relativistic viscous hydrodynamics including spin and magnetic field effects; and hadron physics puzzles from light to heavy flavor. The review synthesizes results from lattice QCD, perturbative QCD, effective field theories, hydrodynamic simulations, and hadron resonance gas models.

## Key numbers
- Critical point location estimated at $\mu_B/T \sim 4-8$ from multiple theoretical frameworks (Fu 2019, Gunkel 2021, Basar 2021, Dimopoulos 2021, Hippert 2023, Sorensen 2024)
- Taylor series equation of state from lattice QCD valid only up to $\mu_B/T \sim [2-3]$ (Bollweg 2022)
- Speed of sound squared surpasses the conformal limit $c_s^2 > 1/3$ to support heavy neutron stars (Bedaque 2014)
- Perturbative QCD calculations relevant at $n_B \gtrsim 40\,n_{sat}$
- Bump in speed of sound squared around $[2,3]n_{sat}$ from heavy-ion collision flow data (Oliinychenko 2022)
- Heavy flavor nuclear modification factor $R_{AA} \sim 1$ in small systems, while pPb D meson $v_2$ is large (CMS 2018)
- Charmonium $v_2 \sim 0$ and bottomonium $v_2 \sim 0$ in small systems (CMS 2018, CMS 2023)
- In pp collisions, D mesons $v_2 > 0$ and B mesons $v_2 < 0$ (ATLAS 2023)

## Context
This review extends the theoretical framework established by Stephanov (1998) on the QCD critical point and builds on Bayesian extractions of QGP properties at zero baryon density by Bernhard (2019), JETSCAPE (2020), and Nijs (2020). The work connects heavy-ion collision physics to neutron star constraints through the symmetry energy expansion (Yao 2023) and temperature expansions (Mroczek 2024), enabling future cross-disciplinary constraints on the QCD equation of state. The MUSES collaboration (2023) provides open-source equation of state tools that enable systematic comparisons across different theoretical approaches.
