# Nuclear Reactions from Lattice QCD

> One of the overarching goals of nuclear physics is to rigorously compute properties of hadronic systems directly from the fundamental theory of strong interactions, Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). In particular, the hope is to perform reliable calculations of nuclear reactions which will impact our understanding of environments that occur during big bang nucleosynthesis, the evolution of stars and supernovae, and within nuclear reactors and high energy/density facilities. Such calculations, being truly ab initio, would include all two-nucleon and three- nucleon (and higher) interactions in a consistent manner. Currently, lattice QCD provides the only reliable option for performing calculations of some of the low- energy hadronic observables. With the aim of bridging the gap between lattice QCD and nuclear many-body physics, the Institute for Nuclear Theory held a workshop on Nuclear Reactions from Lattice QCD on March 2013. In this review article, we report on the topics discussed in this workshop and the path planned to move forward in the upcoming years.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD, combined with effective field theories and finite-volume formalisms, can provide first-principle calculations of nuclear reaction cross sections, but this requires extrapolating quantities to the continuum and infinite-volume limits and tuning quark masses to their physical values, a path that is still under construction for multi-hadron observables.

## Method

The paper reviews the workshop discussions on bridging lattice QCD and nuclear many-body physics, focusing on finite-volume formalisms that connect lattice energy eigenvalues to scattering amplitudes. These include the Luscher method and its generalizations to two-nucleon systems with arbitrary spin, isospin, and center-of-mass motion, as well as to coupled-channel systems and three-particle systems. The potential method developed by the HAL QCD Collaboration is also discussed as a complementary approach.

## Key numbers

* Binding energies of light nuclei and hypernuclei with A < 5 were computed at the SU(3) symmetric point with m_pi approximately 800 MeV, using three lattice volumes with spatial extents L approximately 3.4 fm, 4.5 fm, and 6.7 fm, and a single lattice spacing b approximately 0.145 fm (Beane et al., 2012).
* Binding energies of light nuclei were determined at a slightly lighter pion mass, m_pi approximately 500 MeV (Yamazaki et al., 2012).
* The I=1, P-wave pi pi elastic scattering phase shift was extracted for m_pi = 391 MeV below the K Kbar threshold, featuring the rho resonance (Dudek et al., 2012).
* The ratio of the two-nucleon scattering length a to the effective range r in the 3S1 and 1S0 channels was studied at the physical point and at the SU(3) symmetric point with m_pi approximately 800 MeV (Beane et al., 2013).

## Context

This review extends the work of Luscher (1986, 1990) on finite-volume formalisms for two-body scattering, and reports on generalizations to nucleon-nucleon systems by Briceno et al. (2013) and to three-particle systems by Hansen and Sharpe (2014). It also discusses the potential method of the HAL QCD Collaboration (Aoki et al., 2013) as an alternative. The paper outlines a path for lattice QCD to provide input to many-body nuclear physics calculations such as no-core shell model, coupled-cluster methods, and quantum Monte Carlo, aiming to reduce systematic uncertainties from poor knowledge of nuclear few-body forces.
