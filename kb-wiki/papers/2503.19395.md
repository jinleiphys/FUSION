# Quantum molecular dynamics model based on relativistic mean field theory for light nucleus fragmentation in hadron therapy

> This study evaluates the accuracy of nuclear fragmentation simulations using a quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model based on relativistic mean field (RMF) theory for an energy range of 50-400 MeV/u, relevant to hadron therapy. A total of 16 parameter sets within the RMF framework are assessed based on their ability to reproduce ground-state properties such as the mean squared radius and binding energy, as obtained in QMD simulations. Among these, the NS2 parameter set is identified as the most suitable for describing stable nuclei over a wide mass range, with the use of an adaptive Gaussian wave packet width. Fragmentation cross sections of carbon ion projectiles on light nuclei targets (H, C, O, Al, Ti, and Cu) are simulated at incident energies of 50, 95, 290, and 400 MeV/u and compared with experimental data. The results indicate that the RQMD.RMF model provides superior reproductions for fragmentation at lower energies (50 and 95 MeV/u) compared to the Light Ion QMD (LIQMD) model implemented in Geant4 version 11.2. At higher energies (290 and 400 MeV/u), the RQMD.RMF model performs comparably to the LIQMD. This study demonstrates that the RQMD.RMF model provides a reliable framework for analyzing nuclear fragmentation and holds potential for applications in the planning and quality assurance of hadron therapy.

## Key claim

The RQMD.RMF model provides superior reproductions for nuclear fragmentation at lower energies (50 and 95 MeV/u) compared to the LIQMD model in Geant4 version 11.2, while at higher energies (290 and 400 MeV/u) it performs comparably; however, this conclusion is conditional on the selection of the NS2 parameter set from among 10 RMF parameter sets evaluated for ground-state properties, and on the use of an adaptive Gaussian wave packet width rather than a fixed width.

## Method

The authors implemented a quantum molecular dynamics model based on relativistic mean field theory (RQMD.RMF) using the Walecka-type RMF Lagrangian with sigma, omega, rho mesons and photon fields, including nonlinear sigma self-interaction terms. They evaluated 10 RMF parameter sets by simulating ground-state properties (charge radius, stability, and binding energy versus nuclear size) for 10 stable nuclides from carbon-12 to lead-208, then selected the NS2 parameter set for fragmentation simulations. Fragmentation cross sections of carbon-12 projectiles on hydrogen, carbon, oxygen, aluminum, titanium, and copper targets were simulated at 50, 95, 290, and 400 MeV/u and compared with experimental data from GANIL and QST facilities.

## Key numbers

- 10 RMF parameter sets were assessed for ground-state properties across 10 stable nuclides (carbon-12 through lead-208)
- Fragmentation cross sections were simulated at four incident energies: 50, 95, 290, and 400 MeV/u
- The adaptive Gaussian wave packet width was set to L = 0.035A^(2/3) + 0.9, with alpha cluster parameters (a,b) = (0.7, 3.0)
- The clustering distance R = 4 fm was used for fragment formation
- The time step in QMD dynamics was dt = 1 fm/c
- For ground-state evaluation, the binding energy criterion was less than 0.5 MeV/A difference from experimental values
- 16 fragments were identified for targets at 50 and 95 MeV/u: hydrogen-1, hydrogen-2, hydrogen-3, helium-3, helium-4, helium-6, lithium-6, lithium-7, beryllium-7, beryllium-9, beryllium-10, boron-8, boron-10, boron-11, carbon-10, and carbon-11
- At 95 MeV/u, carbon-12 fragment was also included
- For QST data at 290 and 400 MeV/u, production cross sections were evaluated for effective charge values Z_eff in (5, 4.4, 4, 3.5, 3, 2, 1) at three acceptance angles (2.5, 3.9, and 7.3 degrees)

## Context

This work extends the RQMD.RMF model developed by Nara et al. (2019, 2020), which had previously been applied only to heavy nucleus collisions such as Au+Au at high energies (center-of-mass energies 2.5 to 20 GeV), into the lower energy range of 50-400 MeV/u relevant to hadron therapy. The study bridges a gap in understanding light nucleus collisions (such as C+C) at these energies, where prior work by Dudouet et al. (2013, 2014), Divay et al. (2017), and Zeitlin et al. (2007) provided experimental fragmentation data but no RMF-based QMD simulations existed. The results enable improved modeling of nuclear fragmentation for treatment planning and quality assurance in carbon-ion therapy, as well as for imaging applications such as positron emission tomography and prompt gamma-ray measurement for range monitoring.
