# Initial Data for First-order Causal Viscous Conformal Fluids in General Relativity

> We solve the Einstein constraint equations for a first-order causal viscous relativistic hydrodynamic theory in the case of a conformal fluid. For such a theory, a direct application of the conformal method does not lead to a decoupling of the equations, even for constant-mean curvature initial data. We combine the conformal method applied to a background perfect fluid theory with a perturbative argument in order to obtain the result.

## Key claim
The authors solve the Einstein constraint equations for a first-order causal viscous conformal fluid by combining the conformal method for a background perfect fluid with a perturbative argument, but only for sufficiently small viscosity parameters and constant-mean curvature initial data; a direct application of the conformal method fails because the shear tensor term prevents decoupling of the momentum constraint.

## Method
The authors first apply the conformal method to construct initial data for a perfect conformal fluid, using scaling laws derived from the diffeomorphism-invariant Lagrangian framework of Isenberg, Maxwell, and collaborators. They then treat the viscosity parameters as small perturbations and apply the Implicit Function Theorem to the coupled Einstein constraint equations, obtaining nearby solutions for the viscous fluid model. The construction is carried out for both compact and asymptotically Euclidean three-manifolds, and relies on the Weyl covariant derivative to handle the conformal properties of the viscous stress-energy tensor.

## Key numbers
- The equation of state is that of pure radiation: \(p = \epsilon/3\), where \(\epsilon\) is the equilibrium energy density and \(p\) is the pressure.
- The rest energy density scales as \(\epsilon(n) = 3 k_\epsilon n^{4/3}\) for some constant \(k_\epsilon\), and the temperature scales as \(T = k_\epsilon n^{1/3}\).
- The rest energy density relates to temperature as \(\epsilon = c_\epsilon T^4\) with \(c_\epsilon = 3/k_\epsilon^3\).

## Context
This work extends the existence theory for initial data for the BDNK theory of first-order causal viscous fluids (Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha 2018), for which local well-posedness and causality were previously established assuming initial data exist. It builds on the conformal method of Lichnerowicz (1944) and York (1973) and the recent Lagrangian-based scaling rules for perfect fluids developed by Isenberg and Maxwell (2023). The results enable numerical simulations of general-relativistic viscous fluid dynamics, relevant for neutron star mergers (Alford 2017, Most 2022) and quark-gluon plasma studies, and provide a proof-of-concept for extending the construction to non-conformal fluids with general equations of state.
