# Thermal Static Potential and Pseudo-Scalar Quarkonium Spectral Functions from 2+1 Flavor Lattice QCD

> Quarkonia, which are bound states of a heavy quark and antiquark, play a key role in probing the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The dynamics of quarkonia in the QGP are encoded in their finite-temperature spectral functions. In this work, we estimate the quarkonium spectral functions in the pseudo-scalar channel using 2+1 flavor lattice QCD with a pion mass of $320\,\text{MeV}$, at temperatures of $220\,\text{MeV}\,(1.2\,T_{pc}),\,251\,\text{MeV}\,(1.4\,T_{pc})\,\text{and}\,293\,\text{MeV}\,(1.6\,T_{pc})$. Reconstructing the spectral function from the Euclidean lattice correlator is a well-known ill-posed problem, requiring additional physics-motivated input. We address this by smoothly matching contributions from different frequency regions of the spectral function, using appropriate physics valid for each region. The spectral function around $ω\sim 2\,M_q$ is obtained using a non-perturbative complex potential, while for $ω\gg 2\,M_q$ it is modeled using results from vacuum perturbation theory. Since the pseudoscalar channel does not receive a transport contribution near $ω\sim 0$, we find that the combination of these two regions already provides a good description of the relativistic lattice pseudoscalar correlator. We observe a substantial thermal width in the $η_c(1S)$ state, indicating that pseudoscalar charmonium ($η_c$) is nearing dissolution at the studied temperatures. In comparison, the $η_b$ ground state exhibits little change and remains well-defined.

# Thermal Static Potential and Pseudo-Scalar Quarkonium Spectral Functions from 2+1 Flavor Lattice QCD

## Key claim
The authors reconstruct pseudo-scalar quarkonium spectral functions by smoothly matching a non-perturbatively determined complex thermal potential near the threshold region with vacuum perturbation theory at high frequencies, finding that the pseudoscalar charmonium (η_c) exhibits substantial thermal width and is nearing dissolution at temperatures of 220-293 MeV, while the η_b ground state remains well-defined with little change.

## Method
The authors compute Euclidean pseudoscalar correlators using 2+1 flavor lattice QCD with clover-improved Wilson fermions on HISQ gauge configurations at a fixed lattice spacing of 0.028 fm, varying temperature by adjusting the temporal extent N_τ. They extract the non-perturbative thermal static potential from Coulomb-gauge fixed Wilson line correlators using a multi-parameter fit, then solve the Schrödinger equation with this complex potential to obtain the spectral function near the threshold region ω ∼ 2M_q. The spectral function at high frequencies ω ≫ 2M_q is modeled using vacuum perturbation theory, and the two regions are smoothly matched to construct the full spectral function.

## Key numbers
- Temperatures studied: 220 MeV (1.2 T_pc), 251 MeV (1.4 T_pc), and 293 MeV (1.6 T_pc), with T_pc ∼ 180 MeV
- Pion mass: m_π = 320 MeV
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.028 fm
- Zero-temperature η_c mass: 2.96 ± 0.011 GeV; η_b mass: 9.40 ± 0.02 GeV
- Charm quark pole mass: 1.35 ± 0.01 GeV; bottom quark pole mass: 4.78 GeV (fixed from PDG)
- Charm MS-bar mass: 1.04 ± 0.01 GeV at μ_ref = 2 GeV; bottom MS-bar mass: 5.3 GeV at μ_ref = 2 GeV
- String tension from zero-temperature Cornell fit: √σ = 0.45 GeV; Coulomb coefficient α = 0.42
- Matching distance for potential: r_m = 0.11 fm
- χ²/ndf range for η_c (τ_min = 0.22 fm) and η_b (τ_min = 0.28 fm): 0.1-0.9
- Overall normalization factor A: ∼11 for η_c correlator, ∼7 for η_b correlator

## Context
This work extends the approach of Burnier et al. (2017) for spectral function reconstruction by replacing the perturbative thermal potential with a non-perturbatively determined complex potential from lattice QCD, which is particularly relevant at temperatures just above T_pc where non-perturbative effects are significant. The authors find that color screening in the non-perturbative potential is weaker than in the perturbative potential from Laine et al. (2006), while the non-perturbative imaginary part is significantly larger than the hard-thermal-loop perturbative result, leading to higher dissociation rates. This work enables more reliable input for real-time quarkonium evolution models using open quantum system approaches and provides a benchmark for comparing with Bayesian reconstruction methods such as the Maximum Entropy Method used by Asakawa et al. (2003) and Datta et al. (2003).
