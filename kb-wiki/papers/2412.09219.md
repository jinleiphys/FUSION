# Exploring nuclear force with pulsar glitch observation

> We connect nuclear forces to one of the most notable irregular behaviors observed in pulsars, already detected in approximately 6\% known pulsars, with increasingly accurate data expected from upcoming high-precision timing instruments on both ground and space. Built on Shang & Li (2021), we conduct a case study on the 2001 glitch of the Vela pulsar. For our purpose, we adopt the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model as the theoretical many-body framework to describe nuclear systems. We refit three representative RMF parameter sets (DD-ME2, PKDD, NL3), considering the uncertainties in nuclear matter saturation properties. Utilizing the resulting star structure, composition and nucleon properties in the medium obtained in a consistent manner, we calculate the pinning energy of superfluid vortex in the nuclear lattice in the inner crust. This leads to the evolution of associated pinning force that acts on the vortex, which can be confronted with observed glitch amplitude and short-time relaxation in the 2000 Vela glitch event, following the snowplow model of pulsar glitch. We discuss how the vortex configuration and pinning properties depend on the nuclear parameters, and find an interesting and dominant role of the nuclear symmetry energy slope on pinning strength.

## Key claim

The paper connects nuclear force parameters, specifically the symmetry energy slope \(L_0\), to the pinning strength of superfluid vortices in the inner crust of neutron stars, and shows that this pinning strength can be confronted with observed glitch amplitude and short-time relaxation in the 2000 Vela glitch event, but only under the specific snowplow model framework and for the chosen RMF parameter sets.

## Method

The authors adopt the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model to construct unified equations of state for neutron stars, refitting three representative RMF parameter sets (DD-ME2, PKDD, NL3) with adjusted symmetry energy slopes \(L_0\) = 30, 40, 60, and 80 MeV. Using the resulting star structure and composition, they calculate the pinning energy of superfluid vortices in the nuclear lattice of the inner crust via a semi-classical method based on the local density approximation, incorporating the neutron \(^1S_0\) pairing gap from BCS theory. The pinning force derived from this energy is then input into the snowplow model to compare with the observed 2000 Vela glitch.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 6% of known pulsars exhibit glitches; 25 glitches of the Vela pulsar have been detected over 56 years.
- The three original RMF parameter sets have symmetry energy slopes: DD-ME2 \(L_0 = 51.26\) MeV, PKDD \(L_0 = 90.21\) MeV, NL3 \(L_0 = 118.5\) MeV.
- The outer-inner crust transition density \(\rho_{\rm oi}\) ranges from \(1.416 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\) (PKDD, \(L_0=30\) MeV) to \(2.129 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\) (DD-ME2, \(L_0=80\) MeV).
- The crust-core transition density \(\rho_{\rm cc}\) ranges from 0.097 fm\(^{-3}\) (PKDD, \(L_0=30\) MeV) down to 0.055 fm\(^{-3}\) (DD-ME2, \(L_0=80\) MeV).
- Maximum neutron star masses \(M_{\rm TOV}\) range from 2.325 \(M_\odot\) (PKDD, \(L_0=80\) MeV) to 2.759 \(M_\odot\) (NL3, \(L_0=40\) MeV).
- The neutron pairing strength used is \(G_N = 738\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) with a finite range \(\alpha = 0.636\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the snowplow model of pulsar glitches from Shang & Li (2021) by replacing the analytic pinning force with a microscopically calculated pinning energy derived from RMF-based unified equations of state and self-consistent inner crust compositions. It builds on earlier semi-classical pinning calculations by Negele (1973), Epstein (1988), Pizzochero (1997), and Donati (2004), and on more microscopic HFB calculations by Avogadro (2008) and Klausner (2023). The results enable future constraints on the nuclear symmetry energy slope \(L_0\) from high-precision pulsar timing observations, complementing constraints from neutron star cooling (Newton 2013) and asteroseismology (Neill 2024).
