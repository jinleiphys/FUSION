# Photon production from Pb+Pb collisions at {$\sqrt{s_{\rm {NN}}}$} = 5.02 TeV at LHC and at {$\sqrt{s_{\rm {NN}}}$} = 39 TeV at FCC

> We calculate the production of prompt and thermal photons from Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02A TeV at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and at 39A TeV at the proposed Future Circular Collider (FCC) facility. The photon spectra and anisotropic flow at these energies are compared with the results obtained from 2.76A TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC for three different centrality bins. The prompt photons originating from initial hard scatterings are found to increase by a factor of 1.5 to 2 at 5.02A TeV in the $p_T$ region 2 to 15 GeV and the enhancement is found to be about 5 to 15 times at FCC energy compared to 2.76A TeV in the same $p_T$ region. The evolution of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) formed in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC and FCC energies are studied using a hydrodynamical model and the $p_T$ spectra and elliptic flow of thermal photons are calculated using state-of-the-art photon rates. The relative enhancement in the production of thermal photons is found to be more compared to prompt photons at FCC than at the LHC energies. Although the production of direct (prompt+thermal) photons is found to enhance significantly with increase in beam energy, the photon elliptic flow increases only marginally and does not show strong sensitivity to the collision energy.

## Key claim

The direct photon yield increases significantly with collision energy (by factors of 1.5–2 at 5.02A TeV and 5–15 at 39A TeV relative to 2.76A TeV in the pT range 2–15 GeV), but the photon elliptic flow (v2) increases only marginally and does not show strong sensitivity to the collision energy, even at the much higher FCC energy.

## Method

The authors calculate prompt photon production using the NLO pQCD Monte Carlo code JETPHOX with CTEQ6.6 parton distribution functions, BFG-II fragmentation functions, and EPS09 nuclear shadowing. Thermal photon production is computed by integrating state-of-the-art photon emission rates over the space-time evolution of the fireball, which is modeled using a (2+1)-dimensional longitudinally boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamic framework with smooth entropy-initialized transverse profiles averaged over many Monte Carlo Glauber events.

## Key numbers

- Prompt photon enhancement factor at 5.02A TeV relative to 2.76A TeV: 1.5 to 2 in the pT region 2 to 15 GeV (0–20% centrality)
- Prompt photon enhancement factor at 39A TeV relative to 2.76A TeV: about 5 (at pT ~ 2 GeV) to 15 (at pT ~ 15 GeV) in the same pT region
- Thermal photon spectra at pT ~ 1 GeV: about 1.5 times larger at 5.02A TeV than at 2.76A TeV; about 2.8 times larger at 39A TeV than at 2.76A TeV
- Central temperature at formation time (τ0 = 0.14 fm/c) for most central collisions: about 650 MeV at 2.76A TeV, about 690 MeV at 5.02A TeV, and about 850 MeV at 39A TeV
- Average temperature at FCC is about 850 MeV at τ0, compared to about 690 MeV and 650 MeV at 5.02A TeV and 2.76A TeV respectively
- Final charged particle multiplicity (dNch/dη) for 0–5% centrality: 1600 at 2.76A TeV, 2000 at 5.02A TeV, and 3600 at 39A TeV (extrapolated)
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross-sections used: 42 mb at 2.76A TeV, 64 mb at 5.02A TeV, and 80 mb at 39A TeV
- Prompt to thermal photon ratio at pT = 6 GeV for 40–60% centrality at FCC: about 10; for 0–20% centrality: about 4

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of direct photon production at 2.76A TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC (Chatterjee et al., 2013; 2014) to the higher LHC energy of 5.02A TeV and to the proposed FCC energy of 39A TeV. The study is motivated by the "photon v2 puzzle", the discrepancy between experimental data and theory calculations for photon elliptic flow at RHIC (PHENIX Collaboration) and LHC (ALICE Collaboration). The results provide predictions for future FCC heavy-ion runs and suggest that simultaneous measurement of direct photon spectra and elliptic flow at these higher energies could help constrain the initial conditions and theoretical description of the QGP.
