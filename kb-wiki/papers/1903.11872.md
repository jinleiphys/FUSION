# Study of $P_c(4457)$, $P_c(4440)$, and $P_c(4312)$ in a quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation approach

> Very recently, the LHCb Collaboration reported their new results about the pentaquarks at charm energy region. Based on the new experimental results, we recalculate the molecular states composed of a $Σ_c^{(*)}$ baryon and a $\bar{D}^{(*)}$ meson in a quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation approach. The two-peak structure around 4450 MeV can be interpreted as two $Σ_c \bar{D}^*$ bound states with spin parities $1/2^-$ and $3/2^-$. The newly observed pentaquark $P_c(4312)$ can be assigned as a $Σ_c\bar{D}$ bound state with spin parity $1/2^-$. The experimental determination of spin parities will be very helpful to understand the internal structure of these pentaquarks.

## Key claim

The two-peak structure around 4450 MeV observed by LHCb can be interpreted as two S-wave Σ_c D̅* bound states with spin parities 1/2⁻ and 3/2⁻, and the newly observed P_c(4312) can be assigned as a Σ_c D̅ bound state with spin parity 1/2⁻, but the experimental determination of spin parities is essential to confirm this molecular interpretation.

## Method

The author performed a coupled-channel quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation calculation for the Σ_c^{(*)} D̅^{(*)} system, including Σ_c D̅, Σ_c* D̅, Σ_c D̅*, and Σ_c* D̅* channels. The interaction kernel was constructed from effective Lagrangians describing light meson exchanges (pseudoscalar, vector, and sigma mesons) between charmed baryons and anti-charmed mesons, with coupling constants taken from the literature. Bound states were identified as poles of the scattering amplitude by varying the cutoff parameter Λ in an exponential regularization from 0.8 to 1.8 GeV.

## Key numbers

- P_c(4440) candidate: bound state with J^P = 1/2⁻ at 4439.4 MeV (Λ = 1.0 GeV) from coupled-channel calculation, mainly from Σ_c D̅* interaction
- P_c(4457) candidate: bound state with J^P = 3/2⁻ at 4461.7 MeV (Λ = 1.0 GeV) from coupled-channel calculation, mainly from Σ_c D̅* interaction
- P_c(4312) candidate: bound state with J^P = 1/2⁻ at 4320.0 MeV (Λ = 1.0 GeV) from coupled-channel calculation, mainly from Σ_c D̅ interaction
- The 1/2⁻ Σ_c D̅ state mass is stable around 4320 MeV across the full cutoff range (0.8 to 1.8 GeV), varying only from 4320.4 to 4320.5 MeV
- The 3/2⁻ state mass varies from 4461.7 to 4460.9 MeV as Λ increases from 1.0 to 1.8 GeV
- The 1/2⁻ state from Σ_c D̅* varies from 4450.8 to 4426.7 MeV as Λ increases from 0.8 to 1.8 GeV in single-channel calculation

## Context

This work extends the author's previous molecular state calculations (He 2015, He 2016) which assigned the original P_c(4450) as a 5/2⁺ Σ_c D̅* state, now revised to interpret the two-peak structure as lower-spin states consistent with early predictions by Yang (2011) and Wang (2011). The results are consistent with recent one-boson-exchange calculations by Chen (2019) and complement QCD sum rule studies by Chen (2019). The work enables future experimental partial-wave analysis to distinguish between this molecular interpretation and alternative assignments such as 5/2⁺ and 5/2⁻ states or deeply bound Σ_c* D̅ configurations.
