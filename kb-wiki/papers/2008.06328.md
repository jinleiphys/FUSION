# Vortex lattice in rotating neutron spin-triplet superfluid

> In the Ginzburg-Landau limit, a possible texture without singular cores in a rotating neutron spin-triplet superfluid is studied. It is constructed the lattice of non-singular vortices with a vorticity diffusely distributed over the entire unit cell. The upper limit of the free energy associated with this structure is estimated, and the result shows that non-singular vortices are more preferable in the core of rotating neutron stars than ordinary linear singular vortices. The order parameter of the studied neutron system belongs to the unitary class. This implies that the superfluid under consideration does not have spin polarization and the core-less $^{3}$P$_{2}$ vortices do not have magnetization, which makes electron scattering by vortices ineffective. For the same reason, pinning with flux tubes is also suppressed. This can affect the hydrodynamics of superfluid neutron stars.

# Vortex lattice in rotating neutron spin-triplet superfluid

## Key claim

Non-singular vortices with vorticity diffusely distributed over the entire unit cell may be energetically more favorable in the inner superfluid core of rotating neutron stars than ordinary linear singular vortices, contradicting the established opinion that neutron ³P₂ superfluid motion is irrotational. The result is an upper bound on free energy obtained using trial functions, so the exact relationship between the energies of the two structures is expected to remain the same but is not proven definitively.

## Method

The author constructs a trial order parameter for a rotating ³P₂ neutron superfluid in the Ginzburg-Landau limit, using Euler angles to describe coordinate-dependent orientations of the triad of basis vectors (u₁, u₂, l). The free energy in the rotating frame is derived in the London limit, keeping the condensation energy constant, and a trial function for the angle β is chosen to have lattice symmetry. Numerical integration over a unit cell yields the free energy per area carrying unit vorticity, which is compared to the known Tkachenko (1965) result for singular vortex lattices in superfluid ⁴He.

## Key numbers

- Free energy per unit cell for the non-singular vortex lattice: **47.7 ℏ²ρ_s / (2M)²** (dimensionless, from numerical integration)
- Free energy per unit cell for singular square lattice: **-4.117 + π ln(d/ξ)** in same units; for triangular lattice: **-4.150 + π ln(d/ξ)** (Tkachenko 1965)
- Estimated number of vortices in a rotating neutron star: **N_v ≈ 1.9 × 10¹⁹ (1 ms / P) (M* / 900 MeV) (R / 10 km)**, where P is the period, M* the effective neutron mass, R the radius of the ³P₂ superfluid
- Distance between vortices: **d ≈ 2.3 × 10⁻⁴ cm × (900 MeV / M*)¹/² (P / 1 ms)¹/² (R / 10 km)¹/²**
- Coherence length of ³P₂ superfluid: **ξ ≈ 1.1 × 10⁻¹¹ cm × (M / M*) ρ₁₄¹/³ (10⁹ K / T_c)**, where ρ₁₄ = ρ_n / 10¹⁴ g/cm³ and T_c is the superfluid transition temperature
- The logarithm π ln(d/ξ) is approximately **60** for typical neutron star parameters
- Each unit cell contains one quantum of circulation: **∫ (∇ × v_s)_z dx dy = 2π** (vorticity quantized to ϰ = 1 per cell)

## Context

This work extends the theory of non-singular (core-less) vortices known from ³He-A (Mermin 1976, Chechetkin 1976, Volovik 1977, Fujita 1978) to the ³P₂ neutron superfluid in neutron star cores, where previous work by Richardson (1972), Sauls (1982), and Masuda (2015) assumed singular vortices with irrotational flow. The result contradicts the established view that neutron ³P₂ superfluid motion is irrotational, as assumed in pulsar glitch models (Ho 2015, Montoli 2020). If non-singular vortices are indeed more favorable, the absence of magnetization in these unitary-class vortices would suppress electron scattering and flux-tube pinning, potentially affecting the hydrodynamics of superfluid neutron stars and glitch theory (Alpar 2017). The author notes that entrainment effects from the superfluid proton component, not considered here, could lead to magnetization of non-singular vortices and merit further study.
