# Photodisintegration of Ultra-High-Energy Cosmic Rays revisited

> Recent microscopic and phenomenological calculations of giant dipole resonances for A <= 56 nuclei are presented. The derived photodisintegration cross sections are exhaustively compared to the photonuclear data available to date. An accurate description of the data is found. Our new calculations are also compared with the previous and widely-used estimates of Puget, Stecker and Bredekamp. The present calculations also include all the possible paths down the nuclear chart. The impact on the photodisintegration of ultra-high-energy cosmic rays (UHECR) is illustrated for a Fe source with typical energies of 10^{20-21} eV. At energies around 10^20 eV, the new cross sections are found to modify the UHECR photodisintegration rates. At energies around 10^21 eV, it is recommended to solve a full reaction network to estimate the photodisintegration rate of the UHECR.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the widely-used Puget, Stecker, and Bredekamp (PSB) parameterization of giant dipole resonance (GDR) cross sections often overestimates the GDR width by about 2 MeV, and that using a full reaction network rather than the simplified PSB path significantly modifies the predicted photodisintegration of ultra-high-energy cosmic rays (UHECRs), especially for nuclei with A >= 45.

## Method
The authors calculated photodisintegration cross sections for nuclei with A <= 56 using the Talys reaction code with four different models for the E1-strength function: a simple Lorentzian, a generalized Lorentzian (Kopecky-Uhl), and two microscopic models (HFBCS+QRPA and HFB+QRPA). They compared these predictions against available experimental photonuclear data from the IAEA atlas, then used the generalized Lorentzian model to compute photodisintegration rates for a full reaction network of all stable and neutron-deficient unstable nuclei with A <= 56, simulating the propagation of a pure Fe-56 source at various Lorentz factors.

## Key numbers
- The PSB parameterization overestimates the GDR width by typically 2 MeV compared to the Lorentzian description.
- At maximum spread in the mass distribution, the standard deviation from the average mass number reaches values as large as 7 mass units.
- For a Lorentz factor of gamma = 7 x 10^9 (E = 3.6 x 10^20 eV), Fe-56 nuclei propagate up to distances 10 times larger than at higher energies before being fully disintegrated.
- For heavy nuclei (A >= 45), about 70% of the nuclei are shortcut by the simplified PSB path; for light nuclei (A <= 45), less than 42% are bypassed.
- The photon energies in the nucleus rest frame overlap with the nuclear GDR for Lorentz factors gamma ranging from 5 x 10^9 to 10^12.

## Context
This work extends the original calculations of Puget, Stecker, and Bredekamp (1976) by replacing their Gaussian parameterization of the GDR with more accurate Lorentzian and microscopic models, and by replacing their reduced reaction network (one nucleus per mass number) with a full network including all open photoemission channels. The new cross sections are made available via the nuclear astrophysics library at ULB, enabling future studies of UHECR propagation with astrophysically motivated source compositions and intergalactic magnetic fields.
