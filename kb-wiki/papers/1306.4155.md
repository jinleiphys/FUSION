# Symbolic-numerical Algorithm for Generating Cluster Eigenfunctions: Tunneling of Clusters Through Repulsive Barriers

> A model for quantum tunnelling of a cluster comprising A identical particles, coupled by oscillator-type potential, through short-range repulsive potential barriers is introduced for the first time in the new symmetrized-coordinate representation and studied within the s-wave approximation. The symbolic-numerical algorithms for calculating the effective potentials of the close-coupling equations in terms of the cluster wave functions and the energy of the barrier quasistationary states are formulated and implemented using the Maple computer algebra system. The effect of quantum transparency, manifesting itself in nonmonotonic resonance-type dependence of the transmission coefficient upon the energy of the particles, the number of the particles A=2,3,4, and their symmetry type, is analyzed. It is shown that the resonance behavior of the total transmission coefficient is due to the existence of barrier quasistationary states imbedded in the continuum.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a model for quantum tunnelling of a cluster of A identical particles through short-range repulsive potential barriers using a new symmetrized-coordinate representation, showing that the resonance behavior of the total transmission coefficient is due to barrier quasistationary states embedded in the continuum, but only within the s-wave approximation (one-dimensional Euclidean space, d=1) and for clusters coupled by oscillator-type potentials.

## Method

The authors reduce the A-particle Schrodinger equation to a boundary-value problem for a system of close-coupling ordinary differential equations in the center-of-mass variable by expanding the wave function in a Galerkin series over cluster eigenfunctions in a symmetrized coordinate representation. The effective potentials and transmission coefficients are calculated using symbolic-numerical algorithms implemented in the Maple computer algebra system and FORTRAN environment, with the KANTBP 3.0 program solving the resulting boundary-value problem on a finite-element grid. For comparison, the authors also implement a Dirichlet condition (DC) procedure that approximates narrow barriers as impenetrable walls to compute approximate eigenenergies of quasistationary states.

## Context

This work extends the two-particle tunneling model of P00 to systems of A identical particles, building on prior approaches for near-surface quantum diffusion (Fasolino 2007, Ivanov 2010, Shegelski 2012) and heavy ion collisions (Hofmann, Krappe, CwiokDudek, Hagino, Zagrebaev, Volya, Shotter 2011, Shilov). The benchmark model with long-range potentials from yaf09, casc11, and stankin11 is generalized to short-range oscillator-type interactions. The results enable future calculations of cluster tunneling in molecular and heavy-ion collisions, with the symbolic-numerical algorithms providing a framework for treating larger clusters and more complex barrier geometries beyond the s-wave approximation.
