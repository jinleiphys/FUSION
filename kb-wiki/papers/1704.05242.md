# Initial conditions for hydrodynamics from kinetic theory equilibration

> We use effective kinetic theory to study the pre-equilibrium dynamics in heavy-ion collisions. We describe the evolution of linearized energy perturbations on top of out-of-equilibrium background to the energy-momentum tensor at a time when hydrodynamics becomes applicable. We apply this description to IP-Glasma initial conditions and find an overall smooth transition to hydrodynamics. In a phenomenologically favorable range of $η/s$ values, early time dynamics can be accurately described in terms of a few functions of a scaled time variable $τT/(η/s)$. Our framework can be readily applied to other initial state models to provide the pre-equilibrium dynamics of the energy momentum tensor.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that pre-equilibrium dynamics in heavy-ion collisions can be described by linearized perturbations on an out-of-equilibrium background using effective kinetic theory, and that for a phenomenologically favorable range of shear viscosity over entropy ratio \(\eta/s\), the early time evolution can be accurately captured by a few functions of the scaled time variable \(\tau T/(\eta/s)\), enabling a smooth transition to hydrodynamics that is largely independent of the hydrodynamic initialization time.

## Method

The authors used effective kinetic theory with gluonic elastic scatterings and inelastic particle number changing processes to simulate the pre-equilibrium evolution of the energy-momentum tensor. They decomposed the energy density into a uniform boost-invariant background and linearized perturbations, computing the background evolution via direct simulation and the perturbation response via coordinate Green functions derived from the linearized Boltzmann equation. The framework was applied to IP-Glasma initial conditions at \(\tau_0 = 0.2\,\text{fm}\), propagating the energy perturbations and background to hydrodynamic initialization times \(\tau_\text{init}\) of 0.4, 0.8, and 1.2 fm.

## Key numbers

- Hydrodynamic initialization time \(\tau_\text{init}\) was varied among 0.4, 0.8, and 1.2 fm for matching kinetic theory pre-equilibrium evolution to hydrodynamics.
- Initial state from IP-Glasma model was taken at \(\tau_0 = 0.2\,\text{fm}\).
- The background energy density evolution in kinetic theory was compared to a second-order hydrodynamic gradient expansion: \(\tau^{4/3}\bar{e} = (\tau^{4/3}\bar{e})_\infty \left(1 - \frac{8}{3}\frac{\eta/s}{\tau T} + C_2\left(\frac{\eta/s}{\tau T}\right)^2 + \ldots\right)\), where \(C_2\) depends on second-order transport coefficients.
- The scaled time variable used for universal equilibration plots was \(\tau T/(4\pi\eta/s)\) for the background energy density.

## Context

This work extends the bottom-up thermalization scenario studied in classical lattice simulations by Berges et al. (2013) and the kinetic theory realization of uniform background evolution by Kurkela and collaborators (2015). It provides a practical framework for pre-equilibrium dynamics that can be applied to other initial state models, such as those from the Color-Glass Condensate saturation framework, and eliminates the dependence on hydrodynamic initialization time \(\tau_\text{init}\) that was problematic in earlier approaches by van der Schee (2013) and Romatschke (2015).
