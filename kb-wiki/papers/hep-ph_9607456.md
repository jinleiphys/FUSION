# Cold Strangelets Formation with Finite Size Effects in High Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We have studied the phase diagram and evolution of a strangelet in equilibrium with a finite hadronic gas. Significant finite size modifications of the phase diagram are found and their parameter dependences are studied. With the inclusion of finite size effects we have also been able to obtain the detailed properties of the cold strangelet emerging in the final stage of the isentropic expansion of a finite strange fireball in high energy heavy-ion collisions.

# Cold Strangelets Formation with Finite Size Effects in High Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Finite size effects significantly modify the phase diagram and evolution of strangelets in equilibrium with a hadronic gas, making the dynamical formation of cold strangelets much more restrictive than previously predicted by bulk-phase calculations. The authors find that including finite size effects tends to make strangeness separation less effective and imposes much narrower windows for the bag constant, total entropy per baryon, and strangeness fraction under which cold strangelet formation is possible.

## Method

The authors model the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase as an MIT bag containing free massless up and down quarks, massive strange quarks (m_s = 150 MeV), their antiparticles, and gluons, with finite size effects introduced via the multiple reflection expansion (liquid drop model) including volume, surface, and curvature terms. The hadronic phase is modeled as a mixture of Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac gases of mesons (pi, K, eta), baryons (N, Lambda, Sigma, Xi, Omega, Delta) and their antiparticles, with repulsive interactions accounted for by a Hagedorn eigenvolume factor. Gibbs phase equilibrium (mechanical, thermal, and chemical) is assumed between a finite QGP droplet and a finite hadronic gas, with conservation of total net baryon number and strangeness, and the isentropic expansion of the system is studied to determine whether a cold strangelet survives in the final stage.

## Key numbers

- Strange quark mass: m_s = 150 MeV (fixed throughout)
- Bag constant values studied: B^(1/4) = 145, 180, and 235 MeV
- For a finite system with N_b^(tot) = 100, N_s^(tot) = 50, and B^(1/4) = 180 MeV, the phase diagram shows significant growth of the hadronic phase compared to the bulk case
- For B^(1/4) = 145 MeV and strangeness fraction f_s = 3.0, an upper limit S^(tot)/N_b^(tot) < 52 is required for cold strangelet formation
- Surviving cold strangelet properties for B^(1/4) = 180 MeV, N_b^(tot) = 100, S^(tot)/N_b^(tot) = 5: with f_s^(tot) = 1.0, R^Q = 3.07 fm, N_b^Q = 62.7, rho_b^Q = 0.52 fm^(-3), N_s^Q = 100, f_s^Q = 1.59, E^Q/N_b^Q = 1184 MeV; with f_s^(tot) = 2.0, R^Q = 3.43 fm, N_b^Q = 84.2, rho_b^Q = 0.50 fm^(-3), N_s^Q = 170, f_s^Q = 2.02, E^Q/N_b^Q = 1250 MeV

## Context

This work extends the bulk-phase study of Lee and Heinz (1993) on the phase structure of strange quark matter in equilibrium with hadronic gas, incorporating finite size effects that were neglected in that earlier calculation. It also builds on the liquid drop model approach of Madsen (1993) for strangelets at zero temperature and the finite-temperature strangelet work of He, Gao, Li, and Chao (1996). The results show that previous optimistic predictions for cold strangelet formation in heavy-ion collisions (Greiner, Koch, and Stoecker, 1987; Barz, Friman, Knoll, and Schulz, 1990) may need revision, and that future realistic calculations of strangelet searches must include finite size effects to provide reliable guidance for experiments such as those by Barrette et al. (1990).
