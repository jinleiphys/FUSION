# Recovering the conformal limit of color superconducting quark matter within a confining density functional approach

> We generalize a recently proposed confining relativistic density-functional approach to the case of density dependent vector and diquark couplings. The particular behavior of these couplings is motivated by the non-perturbative gluon exchange in dense quark matter and provides the conformal limit at asymptotically high densities. We demonstrate that this feature of the quark matter EoS is consistent with a significant stiffness in the density range typical for the interiors of neutron stars. In order to model these astrophysical objects we construct a family of hybrid quark-hadron EoSs of cold stellar matter. We also confront our approach with the observational constraints on the mass-radius relation of neutron stars and their tidal deformabilities and argue in favor of a quark matter onset at masses below $1.0~{\rm M}_\odot$

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a confining relativistic density-functional approach for quark matter can recover the conformal limit (squared sound speed \(c_s^2 = 1/3\)) at asymptotically high densities by introducing density-dependent vector and diquark couplings motivated by non-perturbative gluon exchange, while simultaneously maintaining sufficient stiffness at densities typical for neutron star interiors to satisfy observational constraints.

## Method

The authors generalize a previously developed confining relativistic density-functional (RDF) approach by making the vector coupling \(G_V\) and diquark coupling \(G_D\) density-dependent, with functional forms inspired by non-perturbative gluon exchange in the Landau gauge: \(G_V \propto (9M_g^2 + 8k_F^2)^{-1}\) and an analogous form for \(G_D\). They introduce rearrangement terms \(\Theta_V\) and \(\Theta_D\) to maintain thermodynamic consistency, solve the mean-field equations for the chiral condensate, vector field, and diquark pairing gap at zero temperature, and construct hybrid quark-hadron equations of state for cold, electrically neutral matter in \(\beta\)-equilibrium using the DD2 hadronic model with hyperons.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass \(M_\pi = 140\) MeV and decay constant \(F_\pi = 90\) MeV from the vacuum parameter set (current quark mass \(m = 4.2\) MeV, cutoff \(\Lambda = 573\) MeV, \(\alpha = 1.43\), \(D_0\Lambda^{-2} = 1.39\))
- Constituent quark mass in vacuum \(m^* = 718\) MeV, providing phenomenological confinement
- Pseudocritical temperature \(T_{PC} = 163\) MeV defined by chiral susceptibility peak
- Maximum diquark coupling ratio \(\eta_D^{max} = 0.78\) beyond which the vacuum becomes color superconducting; calculations use \(\eta_D < 0.77\)
- Non-perturbative gluon mass range considered: \(M_g = 300-942\) MeV, with specific values 500, 600, 700, and 800 MeV shown in figures
- Observational constraints used: radius \(R_{1.4} = 11.7^{+0.86}_{-0.81}\) km at \(1.4 M_\odot\) (Dietrich 2020) and \(R_{2.0} = 13.7^{+2.6}_{-1.5}\) km at \(2.0 M_\odot\) (Miller 2021); tidal deformability \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 70-580\) from GW170817

## Context

This work extends the confining RDF approach of Ivanytskyi (2022) and Kaltenborn (2017) by introducing density-dependent couplings that recover the conformal limit at high densities, addressing a limitation of previous constant-coupling versions where the squared sound speed exceeded \(1/3\) asymptotically. The approach is motivated by the recent demonstration by Komoltsev (2021) that perturbative QCD can constrain neutron star equations of state through thermodynamic stability and causality. The resulting hybrid equations of state, combined with the DD2 hadronic model including hyperons, enable modeling of neutron stars with quark cores and are confronted with observational constraints from NICER (Miller 2021, Riley 2021) and GW170817 (LIGO Scientific 2018), supporting a quark matter onset at masses below \(1.0 M_\odot\).
