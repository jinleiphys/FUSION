# Fragmentation, Factorization and Infrared Poles in Heavy Quarkonium Production

> We explore the role of soft gluon exchange in heavy quarkonium production at large transverse momentum. We find uncanceled infrared poles at NNLO that are not matched by conventional NRQCD matrix elements. We show, however, that gauge invariance and factorization require that conventional NRQCD production operators be modified to include nonabelian phases or Wilson lines. With appropriately modified operators, factorization is restored at NNLO. We also argue that, in the absence of special cancellations, infrared poles at yet higher orders may require the inclusion of additional nonlocal operators, not present in the NRQCD expansion in relative veloctiy.

# Fragmentation, Factorization and Infrared Poles in Heavy Quarkonium Production

## Key claim

The authors show that at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in perturbative QCD, infrared divergences appear in heavy quarkonium production that cannot be absorbed into conventional NRQCD matrix elements as defined in Bodwin, Braaten and Lepage (1995); however, gauge invariance and factorization can be restored by modifying the NRQCD production operators to include nonabelian phase operators (Wilson lines), at least to order v^2 in the relative velocity expansion.

## Method

The authors study the gluon fragmentation function for g → c\bar{c} + X in the eikonal approximation, where soft gluon energies are taken to be much below the charm quark mass m_c. They compute the infrared pole structure of fragmentation diagrams at NNLO by expanding in the relative momentum q of the heavy quark pair, isolating terms of order v^2 = q^2/m_c^2, and evaluating the resulting integrals in dimensional regularization with D = 4 - 2ε dimensions. The gauge transformation properties of color octet NRQCD matrix elements are analyzed, and a gauge-completed definition incorporating Wilson lines along a lightlike direction l is proposed.

## Key numbers

- The NLO infrared pole from single soft gluon emission (Fig. 1b) is proportional to (16/3)(α_s/π)(\vec{q}^2/P^2)(1/(-ε)), where P is the total momentum of the quark pair and q is the relative momentum.
- The uncanceled NNLO infrared pole from diagrams with a three-gluon vertex (Fig. 2c) is given by Σ^(2) = α_s^2 × (4/(3ε)) × [(P·q)^2/P^4 - q^2/P^2].
- In the quarkonium rest frame, this NNLO pole simplifies to α_s^2 × (1/(3ε)) × (\vec{v}^2/4), where \vec{v} is the relative velocity of the heavy quark pair.

## Context

This work extends the NRQCD factorization formalism of Bodwin, Braaten and Lepage (1995) for heavy quarkonium production at large transverse momentum, which had previously lacked fully convincing proofs for production processes as noted in the review by Brambilla et al. (2004) and by Bodwin (2003). The authors demonstrate that the topological factorization argument of Braaten, Fleming and Yuan (1996) and Braaten (1997) fails at NNLO due to noncancelling infrared poles, but can be restored through gauge completion of octet operators. The results are consistent with factorization of evolution logarithms in the ratio of momentum transfer to quark mass, but the authors anticipate that higher orders may require additional nonlocal operators beyond the standard NRQCD expansion in relative velocity.
