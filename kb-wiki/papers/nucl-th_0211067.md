# Probing the strange quark condensate by di-electrons from phi meson decays in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies

> QCD sum rules predict that the change of the strange quark condensate $<\bar s s>$ in hadron matter at finite baryon density causes a shift of the peak position of the di-electron spectra from $ϕ$ meson decays. Due to the expansion of hadron matter in heavy-ion collisions, the $ϕ$ peak suffers a smearing governed by the interval of density in the expanding fireball, which appears as effective broadening of the di-electron spectrum in the $ϕ$ region. The emerging broadening is sensitive to the in-medium change of $<\bar s s>$. This allows to probe directly in-medium modifications of $<\bar s s>$ via di-electron spectra in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies with HADES.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the effective broadening of the di-electron spectrum from phi meson decays in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies is directly sensitive to the in-medium change of the strange quark condensate ⟨s̄s⟩, and this broadening is almost insensitive to collision broadening, offering a way to probe the strange quark condensate via HADES measurements. The key hedge is that the effect depends on the poorly known strangeness content of the nucleon (parameter y), which is varied between 0 and 0.2, and that the results rely on a schematic fireball model with two extreme scenarios for phi meson chemical equilibrium.

## Method

The authors employ QCD sum rules to relate the phi meson mass shift to the density-dependent strange quark condensate, parameterized by the nucleon strangeness content y. They model the space-time evolution of heavy-ion collisions using a blast-wave fireball model with constant radial expansion velocity (v_r = 0.3), initial baryon density 3n_0, freeze-out density 0.3n_0, and initial temperature 90 MeV. The di-electron spectrum from phi decays is calculated using a Breit-Wigner spectral function, with the phi meson multiplicity treated either in chemical equilibrium (case i) or with fixed number (chemical off-equilibrium, case ii).

## Key numbers

- The phi meson mass shift in nuclear matter is given by m_phi = m_phi^vac (1 - 0.14 y n_N / n_0), where n_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3} is nuclear matter saturation density.
- The initial baryon density is taken as n_N(t=0) = 3 n_0, and freeze-out density as n_N(t_f.o.) = 0.3 n_0.
- Initial temperature T(t=0) = 90 MeV, freeze-out temperature T_f.o. = 50 MeV.
- The specific entropy is 5 per baryon.
- The in-medium total width of the phi meson is taken as Gamma_phi^tot = 20 to 30 MeV due to collision broadening.
- The parameter y (strangeness content of the nucleon) is varied in the interval 0 to 0.2.

## Context

This work extends QCD sum rule predictions by Hatsuda (1992) and the authors' own prior work (Kampfer et al.) on the phi meson mass shift in nuclear matter, applying them to the experimentally accessible di-electron channel in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies with HADES. It contrasts with effective hadronic Lagrangian approaches (Weise, Cabrera) which predict only tiny phi mass shifts from phi-N scattering, and with the rho and omega mesons whose in-medium modifications are complicated by four-quark condensates. The results enable a direct experimental probe of the strange quark condensate and the strangeness content of the nucleon, which has implications for deep-inelastic scattering, strange particle production, and dark matter studies.
