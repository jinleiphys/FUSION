# Entropy Production, Hydrodynamics, and Resurgence in the Primordial Quark-Gluon Plasma from Holography

> Microseconds after the Big Bang quarks and gluons formed a strongly-coupled non-conformal liquid driven out-of-equilibrium by the expansion of the Universe. We use holography to determine the non-equilibrium behavior of this liquid in a Friedmann-Lemaitre-Robertson-Walker Universe and develop an expansion for the corresponding entropy production in terms of the derivatives of the cosmological scale factor. We show that the resulting series has zero radius of convergence and we discuss its resurgent properties. Finally, we compute the resummed entropy production rate in de Sitter Universe at late times and show that the leading order approximation given by bulk viscosity effects can strongly overestimate/underestimate the rate depending on the microscopic parameters.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the hydrodynamic gradient expansion for entropy production in a strongly coupled non-conformal quark-gluon plasma in an expanding FLRW Universe has zero radius of convergence, and that the leading-order bulk viscosity approximation can strongly overestimate or underestimate the actual entropy production rate depending on the microscopic parameters (the dimension of the operator breaking conformal symmetry).

## Method

The authors use holography (the AdS/CFT correspondence) to model the strongly coupled \({\cal N}=2^{*}\) gauge theory plasma in a Friedmann-Lemaitre-Robertson-Walker (FLRW) background, linearizing the bulk scalar equations on the AdS-FLRW-Schwarzschild solution. They solve the resulting nested system of differential equations for the scalar field profiles to compute the entropy production rate via the apparent horizon entropy, both in the hydrodynamic regime (small Knudsen number) and in the non-hydrodynamic regime (large expansion rate).

## Key numbers

* The bulk viscosity of the \({\cal N}=2^{*}\) plasma in the small-mass expansion is \(\frac{\zeta}{s} = \frac{[\Gamma(3/4)]^4}{18\pi^4} \left(\frac{m_f}{T}\right)^2 + \frac{1}{216\pi^3} \left(\frac{m_b}{T}\right)^4\).
* For de Sitter cosmology, the coefficients in the hydrodynamic expansion grow factorially: \(\mathcal{T}_{\Delta,n}[a] = \Gamma(n+4-\Delta) a^n H^n\).
* The resummed entropy production rate in de Sitter at late times scales as \(\frac{d(a^3s)}{dt}\big|_{\text{resummed}} \propto \left(\frac{aH}{\mu}\right)^{2\Delta-5} \frac{d(a^3s)}{dt}\big|_{\text{hydro}}\) with \(aH/\mu \gg 1\).
* The positions of the first 10 singularities in the Borel plane for the \(\Delta=3\) operator match the 10 lowest non-hydrodynamic quasinormal mode frequencies from Nunez (2003) at the level of a fraction of a percent or better.

## Context

This work extends the study of divergent hydrodynamic gradient expansions beyond the Bjorken flow case (Heller 2013, Heller 2015, Basar 2015, Aniceto 2015) to a cosmological setting with broken conformal invariance, providing the first example of such behavior in a non-conformal relativistic flow. It enables the use of resurgence techniques to resum the hydrodynamic series for primordial QGP dynamics and suggests that non-perturbative non-hydrodynamic degrees of freedom must be included to correctly describe entropy production in the early Universe, with potential implications for primordial gravitational wave signatures.
