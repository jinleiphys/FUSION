# Exploratory Calculations of the Effects of Higher Shell Admixtures on Static Electric Quadrupole and Magnetic Dipole Moments of Excited States

> Using the interaction $Q \cdot Q ~+~ xV_{S.O.}$ where $V_{S.O.}$ is a two-body spin-orbit interaction, we study the effects of varying x on the static electric quadrupole and magnetic dipole moments of the $2^+_1$ and $2^+_2$ states of $^{10}Be$. This is done both in the valence space $s^4p^6$ and in a larger space in which 2 $\hbar ω$ excitations are allowed. In the former case, for x=0, we have the Wigner Supermultiplet limit in which the $2^+_1$ and $2^+_2$ states are degenerate and correspond to K=0 and K=2 rotational states, with equal and opposite static quadrupole moments. Turning on the spin-orbit interaction with sufficient strength in the valence space gives an energy splitting to the two $2^+$ states in accord with experiment. When higher shell admixtures are allowed, we get quite a different behaviour as a function of x than in the valence space. Of particular interest is a value of x for which $Q(2^+_1)$ and $Q(2^+_2)$ both nearly vanish, and so does (somewhat coincidentally) $μ(2^+_1)$.

## Key claim

Using the interaction \(Q \cdot Q + xV_{S.O.}\) in a larger shell-model space that includes 2 \(\hbar\omega\) excitations, the static electric quadrupole moments \(Q(2^+_1)\) and \(Q(2^+_2)\) and the magnetic dipole moment \(\mu(2^+_1)\) of \({}^{10}\)Be all nearly vanish at a specific spin-orbit strength \(x \approx 0.63\), a behavior absent in the valence-space-only calculation where these moments remain large and opposite in sign.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed for \({}^{10}\)Be using the interaction \(Q \cdot Q + xV_{S.O.}\), where \(V_{S.O.}\) is a two-body spin-orbit interaction. The strength \(\chi\) of the \(Q \cdot Q\) term was fixed to reproduce the experimental \(2^+_1\) excitation energy (3.368 MeV) at \(x=1\), yielding \(\chi = 0.5241\) MeV/fm\(^4\) in the valence space (\(s^4p^6\)) and \(\chi = 0.4157\) MeV/fm\(^4\) in the larger space allowing 2 \(\hbar\omega\) excitations. The parameter \(x\) was then varied from 0 to 4 to study its effect on static electric quadrupole moments, magnetic dipole moments, and \(B(E2)\) transition strengths for the \(2^+_1\) and \(2^+_2\) states.

## Key numbers

- At \(x=0.63\) in the large space: \(Q(2^+_1) = -0.034\) \(e\) fm\(^2\), \(Q(2^+_2) = 0.278\) \(e\) fm\(^2\), and \(\mu(2^+_1) = 0.013\) \(\mu_N\) (nearly vanishing).
- At \(x=1.5\) (realistic value) in the large space: \(Q(2^+_1) = -5.039\) \(e\) fm\(^2\), \(Q(2^+_2) = 5.384\) \(e\) fm\(^2\), \(B(E2: 0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1) = 42.63\) \(e^2\) fm\(^4\), and \(B(E2: 0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_2) = 0.989\) \(e^2\) fm\(^4\).
- At \(x=0\) in the large space: \(E(2^+_1) = 2.485\) MeV, \(Q(2^+_1) = 5.453\) \(e\) fm\(^2\), \(B(E2: 0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1) = 5.502\) \(e^2\) fm\(^4\); \(E(2^+_2) = 3.972\) MeV, \(Q(2^+_2) = -5.559\) \(e\) fm\(^2\), \(B(E2: 0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_2) = 50.48\) \(e^2\) fm\(^4\).
- Experimental excitation energies: \(E(2^+_1) = 3.368\) MeV, \(E(2^+_2) = 5.960\) MeV.
- In the large space at \(x=1.5\): \(E(2^+_1) = 3.513\) MeV, \(E(2^+_2) = 6.307\) MeV.
- In the large space at \(x=0.63\): \(B(E2: 0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1) = 24.39\) \(e^2\) fm\(^4\) and \(B(E2: 0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_2) = 23.07\) \(e^2\) fm\(^4\) (nearly equal, supporting strong \(K\)-mixing).

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model studies using the \(Q \cdot Q\) interaction (Fayache, Sharma, and Zamick, 1996; Fayache, Zamick, and Sharon, 1997) and the two-body spin-orbit interaction introduced by Zheng and Zamick (1991). It contrasts valence-space results with those including 2 \(\hbar\omega\) excitations, revealing that higher shell admixtures can reverse the ordering of \(K=0\) and \(K=2\) bands at small spin-orbit strength and produce a critical region where quadrupole and magnetic moments nearly vanish. The findings suggest that in other nuclei with nearly degenerate \(K=0\) and \(K=2\) states, higher shell admixtures could lead to similarly unexpected cancellations, motivating further experimental measurements of excited-state moments in \({}^{10}\)Be and beyond.
