# Hexadecapole deformation of $^{238}$U from relativistic heavy-ion collisions using a nonlinear response coefficient

> The hexadecapole deformation ($β_4$) of the $^{238}$U nucleus has not been determined because its effect is overwhelmed by those from the nucleus' large quadrupole deformation ($β_2$) in nuclear electric transition measurements. In this Letter, we identify the nonlinear response of the hexadecapole anisotropy to ellipticity in relativistic $^{238}$U+ $^{238}$U collisions that is solely sensitive to $β_4$ and insensitive to $β_2$. We demonstrate this by state-of-the-art hydrodynamic calculations and discuss the prospects of discovering the $β_4$ of $^{238}$U in heavy-ion data at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

# Hexadecapole deformation of $^{238}$U from relativistic heavy-ion collisions using a nonlinear response coefficient

## Key claim

The nonlinear response coefficient $\chi_{4,22}$ in relativistic $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U collisions is solely sensitive to the hexadecapole deformation $\beta_4$ of $^{238}$U and is insensitive to the quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$, system size differences, and ambiguities from the reference nucleus $^{197}$Au, provided the observable is constructed as a relative difference between ultra-central U+U and Au+Au collisions.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the iEBE-VISHNU model with TRENTo initial conditions, followed by the UrQMD hadron cascade, for U+U and Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy. They constructed the nonlinear response coefficient $\chi_{4,22} = v_4\{\Phi_2\} / \langle v_2^4\rangle^{1/2}$ from the three-particle asymmetry cumulant and four-particle cumulant, and computed the relative difference $R(X) = 2(X_{\rm UU} - X_{\rm AuAu})/(X_{\rm UU} + X_{\rm AuAu})$ for various observables across centrality. Five Woods-Saxon density distributions for uranium with different $\beta_2$ and $\beta_4$ values were tested, along with two gold configurations.

## Key numbers

- The commonly accepted quadrupole deformation of $^{238}$U is $\beta_{2,\rm U}^* = 0.286 \pm 0.002$, derived from $B(E2) = 12.09 \pm 0.20$ e$^2$b$^2$ (Raman, 2001).
- For the test case U$^{\rm (new)}$, $\beta_{4,\rm U} = 0.100$ with $\beta_{2,\rm U} = 0.259$; for U$^{\rm (test3)}$, $\beta_{4,\rm U} = 0.200$ with $\beta_{2,\rm U} = 0.286$.
- The Woods-Saxon radius parameter for uranium is $R_0 = 6.87$ fm and diffuseness $a = 0.556$ fm; for gold, $R_0 = 6.38$ fm and $a = 0.535$ fm with $\beta_{2,\rm Au} = -0.131$ and $\beta_{4,\rm Au} = -0.031$.
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section used is $\sigma_{\rm NN} = 42$ mb, with Gaussian smearing parameter $w = 0.5$ fm.
- The minimum nucleon-nucleon distance used in nuclear sampling is $d_{\rm min} = 0.4$ fm.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of nuclear deformation in heavy-ion collisions that focused on quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$ (Heinz, 2004; Masui, 2009; Schenke, 2020; Giacalone, 2021; STAR, 2015; STAR, 2024; ATLAS, 2022) and addresses the long-standing difficulty that hexadecapole effects are overwhelmed by quadrupole effects in electric transition measurements. The authors build on the finding by Ryssens (2023) that a finite $\beta_4$ could resolve the $v_2$ ratio puzzle between U+U and Au+Au collisions, but note that approach was indirect because $v_2$ is insensitive to $\beta_4$. The nonlinear response coefficient $\chi_{4,22}$ was previously studied in isobar collisions (Zhao, 2022) where it was found insensitive to $\beta_2$ and $\beta_3$; the present work demonstrates its exclusive sensitivity to $\beta_4$ in uranium collisions. This observable can be applied to existing RHIC data and to isobar collisions for precise $\beta_4$ extraction.
