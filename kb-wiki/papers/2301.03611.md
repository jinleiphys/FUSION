# 1+1D Hadrons Minimize their Biparton Renyi Free Energy

> We use a variational method to calculate the spectrum and the parton distribution function of ground state hadrons of various gauge theories in 1+1 dimensions. The template functions in our method minimize a free energy functional defined as a combination of free valence partons' kinetic energy on the lightcone and the Renyi entanglement entropy of biparton subsystems. Our results show that hadrons in these theories minimize the proposed free energy. The success of this technique motivates applying it to confining gauge theories in higher dimensions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that hadron wavefunctions in 1+1D gauge theories can be accurately approximated by minimizing a biparton Renyi free energy functional, where the order parameter α is determined variationally by minimizing the Hamiltonian expectation value; the method works for all quark masses, including the deep non-perturbative regime where the earlier von Neumann entropy approach of Asadi (2022) failed.

## Method

The authors construct a free energy functional F_α = E − T S_α, where E is the kinetic energy of free valence partons on the lightcone and S_α is the Renyi entropy of biparton subsystems. They derive analytic template wavefunctions for mesons and baryons by minimizing this functional, then treat the Renyi order α as a variational parameter whose value is fixed by minimizing the expectation value of the full Hamiltonian for each specific theory (Schwinger model and 't Hooft model with N=3). The resulting wavefunctions are used to compute hadron masses and parton distribution functions (PDFs).

## Key numbers

- For the Schwinger model ground state meson mass, the method reproduces existing numerical results from Sriganesh (1999) across all quark masses, with perfect agreement shown in Fig. 3.
- In the 't Hooft model with N=3, the ground state meson mass matches the exact results of 't Hooft (1973) and baryon mass matches Hornbostel (1988) for all quark masses shown in Fig. 5.
- The Renyi order α asymptotes to α → 1 in the limit m_q/g ≫ 1 (large quark mass), and approaches α → −∞ as m_q/g → 0.
- For the Schwinger model, the order α becomes negative when m_q/g ≪ 1, marking the transition into the non-perturbative regime.

## Context

This work extends the thermal ensemble approach of Asadi (2022), which used von Neumann entropy and worked only in the large quark mass limit, by replacing it with Renyi entropy to handle the full range of quark masses including the deep non-perturbative regime. The method successfully reproduces existing numerical results for the Schwinger model from Sriganesh (1999) and for the 't Hooft model from 't Hooft (1973) and Hornbostel (1988). The authors propose this variational principle as a candidate first principle for bound states of confining gauge theories, with the ultimate goal of extending to higher dimensions where complications like renormalization group evolution, spin, and gluon degrees of freedom must be incorporated.
