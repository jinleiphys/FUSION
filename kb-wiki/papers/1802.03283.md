# Chiral magnetic effect search in p(d)+Au, Au+Au collisions at RHIC

> The chiral magnetic effect (CME) refers to charge separation along a strong magnetic field of single-handed quarks, caused by interactions with topological gluon fields from QCD vacuum fluctuations. A major background of CME measurements in heavy-ion collisions comes from resonance decays coupled with elliptical flow anisotropy. These proceedings present two new studies from STAR to shed further light on the background issue: (1) small system p+Au and d+Au collisions where the CME signal is not expected, and (2) pair invariant mass dependence where resonance peaks can be identified.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the charge-dependent azimuthal correlator difference (Δγ) measured in small-system p+Au and d+Au collisions at RHIC is comparable to that in peripheral Au+Au collisions at similar multiplicities, suggesting that the measured Δγ in heavy-ion collisions is dominated by background from resonance decays coupled with elliptic flow, though a small finite CME signal is not disallowed given present uncertainties.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured the three-particle azimuthal correlator γ = ⟨cos(φα + φβ − 2φc)⟩/v2 for opposite-sign (OS) and same-sign (SS) charged particle pairs in p+Au, d+Au, and Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, using particles in the TPC acceptance (|η| < 1, 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c). The charge-dependent difference Δγ = γOS − γSS was studied as a function of particle multiplicity and as a function of pair invariant mass (minv) to identify resonance backgrounds, with a two-component model fit used to extract possible CME signal at low minv.

## Key numbers

- In 50-80% centrality Au+Au collisions, the inclusive Δγ over all mass is (7.45 ± 0.21) × 10⁻⁴; after applying a cut of minv > 1.5 GeV/c², Δγ becomes (1.3 ± 5.7) × 10⁻⁵, which is (1.8 ± 7.6)% of the inclusive value.
- In 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions, the inclusive Δγ is (1.82 ± 0.03) × 10⁻⁴; with the minv > 1.5 GeV/c² cut, Δγ becomes (7.7 ± 9.0) × 10⁻⁶, or (4.3 ± 4.9)% of the inclusive value.
- In 0-20% centrality Au+Au collisions, the inclusive Δγ is (3.70 ± 0.67) × 10⁻⁵; with the minv cut, Δγ becomes (−0.1 ± 1.8) × 10⁻⁵, or (−3.8 ± 49)% of the inclusive value.
- The scaled correlator Δγscaled = Δγ × multiplicity / v2 in p+Au and d+Au collisions is compatible with or larger than that in Au+Au collisions, with AMPT simulations accounting for about 2/3 of the STAR data and being approximately constant over multiplicity.

## Context

This work extends the CME search program at RHIC by providing control measurements in small collision systems (p+Au, d+Au) where the CME signal is expected to average to zero, following similar studies at the LHC by CMS (Khachatryan 2016, Sirunyan 2017) that found comparable Δγ in p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions. The invariant mass method is a new approach to isolate resonance decay backgrounds from possible CME signals, building on the understanding that resonance decays coupled with elliptic flow (v2) constitute the major background, as established by Wang (2009), Bzdak (2009), and Schlichting (2010). The results enable future higher-statistics measurements to potentially extract a CME signal at low invariant mass after subtracting resonance contributions.
