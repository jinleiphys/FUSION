# Non-hydrodynamic transport in trapped unitary Fermi gases

> Many strongly coupled fluids are known to share similar hydrodynamic transport properties. In this work we argue that this similarity could extend beyond hydrodynamics to transient dynamics through the presence of non-hydrodynamic modes. We review non-hydrodynamic modes in kinetic theory and gauge/gravity duality and discuss their signatures in trapped Fermi gases close to unitarity. Reanalyzing previously published experimental data, we find hints of non-hydrodynamic modes in cold Fermi gases in two and three dimensions.

## Key claim

The authors argue that strongly interacting quantum fluids (SIQFs) may share not only similar hydrodynamic transport properties but also transient non-hydrodynamic modes; reanalyzing previously published experimental data, they find hints of such non-hydrodynamic modes in trapped unitary Fermi gases in two and three dimensions, but note that the currently available data is not sufficient to extract information about non-hydrodynamic frequencies with statistical significance.

## Method

The authors review non-hydrodynamic modes in kinetic theory (Boltzmann equation with relaxation time approximation) and gauge/gravity duality (black hole quasinormal modes), then derive collective mode dispersion relations for a trapped Fermi gas in a harmonic potential using Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics, second-order hydrodynamics, and kinetic theory. They reanalyze raw time-evolution data from published experiments on collective oscillations of trapped Fermi gases: quadrupole and breathing modes in a two-dimensional gas of potassium-40 atoms (Vogt et al., 2011) and radial breathing mode oscillations in a three-dimensional gas of lithium-6 atoms close to unitarity (Kinast et al., 2005), fitting the data with a function containing both a hydrodynamic and a non-hydrodynamic mode and using the Akaike information criterion to avoid overfitting.

## Key numbers

- For the two-dimensional quadrupole mode data (240 data points), the Akaike information criterion decreases by 80 units when allowing for a non-hydrodynamic mode with amplitude a_nh not equal to zero.
- For the two-dimensional breathing mode, the extracted non-hydrodynamic mode amplitude is consistent with zero.
- For the three-dimensional breathing mode data (1100 data points), the AIC decreases by 80 units when allowing for a non-hydrodynamic mode.
- The two-dimensional experiment used a trap frequency omega_perp approximately 2 pi times 125 Hz with N approximately 2000 potassium-40 atoms at T/T_F = 0.45, for four values of the interaction strength parameter ln(k_F a).
- The three-dimensional experiment used a magnetic field of B = 840 G (close to unitarity), trap frequency omega_perp approximately 2 pi times 1700 Hz, with N approximately 2 times 10^5 lithium-6 atoms and trap anisotropy lambda approximately 0.045, for several temperatures T/T_F.

## Context

This work extends the idea that strongly coupled fluids (such as hot quark-gluon plasmas, high-temperature superconductors, and black hole duals via the AdS/CFT conjecture) share similar transport properties, specifically the low shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, to the possibility that they also share non-hydrodynamic quasinormal modes. The authors build on prior theoretical work on black hole quasinormal modes (Kovtun, 2005) and kinetic theory analyses of trapped Fermi gases (Brewer, 2015; PhysRevA.76.033610; 2008PhRvA..78e3609R), and reanalyze experimental data from Vogt et al. (2011) and Kinast et al. (2005). The results suggest that further experimental work could open a new window into understanding transport properties of strongly interacting quantum matter across multiple subfields of physics.
