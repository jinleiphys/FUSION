# A Crossover phase transition based on the phenomenology of hadronic matter and quark matter

> The phase transition calculations are utilized for an in-depth understanding of the thermodynamics of the deconfinement transition to perform the best analysis of the QCD phase diagram. The phenomenological justifications for a mathematical approach to constructing the phase transition are found based on the properties of hadronic matter at low densities as well as quark matter at high densities. We modify both the quark matter equation of state by confining effects at low densities and the hadronic matter equation of state by excluded volume effects at higher densities. Over the intermediate densities, the transition from the hadronic phase to the quark phase is modified by the surface tension between to phases. The results are in agreement with the observational constraints of neutron stars.

# A Crossover phase transition based on the phenomenology of hadronic matter and quark matter

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a crossover phase transition between hadronic matter and quark matter can be constructed phenomenologically by modifying both equations of state with excluded volume effects in hadronic matter and a chemical-potential-dependent bag pressure in quark matter, such that the resulting hybrid star equations of state satisfy observational constraints from neutron stars. The key hedge is that the mixed phase construction relies on a free parameter Delta_P related to surface tension, and the physically reasonable range is limited to 1% to 7% based on prior work linking Delta_P to the transition between Maxwell and Glendenning constructions.

## Method

The hadronic equation of state is described using the relativistic density-functional model with DD2F parameterization, modified by excluded volume effects at high densities. The quark matter equation of state uses the constant speed of sound (CSS) parameterization fitted to the nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (nlNJL) model, with an additional chemical-potential-dependent bag pressure to incorporate confining effects at low densities. The mixed phase between hadronic and quark matter is constructed using two approaches: the replaced interpolation method (RIM) with a polynomial ansatz for pressure as a function of chemical potential, and the two-parabola interpolation method where pressure is given by parabolic functions in two chemical potential intervals. Both methods enforce continuity of pressure and baryon density at the phase boundaries and satisfy thermodynamic stability and causality conditions.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic matter EoS uses the DD2F parameterization with flow constraint parameters k=0.04, r=0.07, and s=2.25
- The nlNJL model parameters are varied in the ranges 0.7 < eta_D < 0.8 and 0.11 < eta_V < 0.18
- The mixed phase boundaries are set at hadronic density n_H = n_0 (saturation density) and quark density n_Q = 4 n_0
- For the model with excluded volume (EV=0.05) and eta_V=0.14, the mu-dependent bag pressure parameters are B_0=10 MeV fm^{-3}, mu_s=1000 MeV, and Gamma_s=112
- The critical chemical potential mu_C for the model with EV and bag pressure is 1038 MeV, with hadronic boundary density n_H=1.33 n_0 at mu_H=990 MeV and quark boundary density n_Q=2.4 n_0 at mu_Q=1069 MeV for Delta_P=2.5%
- The Delta_P parameter is constrained to the range 1% to 7% based on the relation to surface tension from Maslov (2018)
- For the model without EV and without bag pressure, the critical chemical potential is 1135.9 MeV with n_H=2.3 n_0 at mu_H=1072.6 MeV and n_Q=3.3 n_0 at mu_Q=1313.5 MeV for Delta_P=-3%

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach to the QCD phase transition by combining the DD2F hadronic model with excluded volume effects (Typel, 2009; Typel, 2016) and the CSS parameterization of the nlNJL model developed in the author's prior work (Antic, 2021). The crossover construction using the replaced interpolation method follows the formalism of Ayriyan (2017) and Abgaryan (2018), while the two-parabola method is from Ayriyan (2021). The results enable the construction of hybrid neutron star equations of state that satisfy observational constraints from NICER and the Hebeler (2013) constraints, and allow investigation of the twin phenomenon in neutron star mass-radius curves.
