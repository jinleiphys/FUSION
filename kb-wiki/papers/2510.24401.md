# Comprehensive Inclusion of Higher-order Ca$^+$ Isotope Shifts in the King's Plot Yields an Order Improvement on the $e^-$-$n$ Coupling Limit

> By critically evaluating higher-order nonlinear effects to the isotope shifts (ISs) in the low-lying transition frequencies of the singly charged calcium ion, stringent constraint on the electron-neutron coupling due to a hypothetical boson describing physics beyond the Standard Model is inferred. It shows an order magnitude difference compared to the previously reported limit demonstrating importance of higher-order effects in the analysis of nonlinearity in the King's plot. The first-order IS parameters and enhancement factor ($D$) were evaluated using two complementary approaches in the relativistic coupled-cluster theory framework: namely finite-field (FF) and analytical response (AR) approaches. Extraction of the second-order IS parameters in the FF approach show numerical instabilities, so they are determined in the AR approach. Comparison of these factors with previous calculation shows substantial differences in the magnitudes. However, $D$ values from both the FF and AR approaches display excellent agreement. We also show explicitly roles of electron correlation effects in the evaluation of $D$ values accurately.

# Comprehensive Inclusion of Higher-order Ca$^+$ Isotope Shifts in the King's Plot Yields an Order Improvement on the $e^-$-$n$ Coupling Limit

## Key claim
When higher-order Standard Model nonlinear effects (second-order mass-shift and field-shift constants) are rigorously included in the King's plot analysis of Ca$^+$ isotope shifts, the inferred upper bound on the electron-neutron coupling constant $\alpha_{NP}$ worsens by approximately an order of magnitude compared to analyses that neglect these Standard Model nonlinearities, demonstrating that previous limits were overly optimistic.

## Method
The authors employed relativistic coupled-cluster theory at the singles, doubles, and triples excitation approximation (RCCSDT method) to compute first-order isotope shift parameters (normal mass shift $K_{NMS}$, specific mass shift $K_{SMS}$, and field shift $F$) using both finite-field (FF) and analytical response (AR) approaches. Second-order isotope shift constants ($G^{(2)}$, $G^{(4)}$, $F^{(2)}$, $K_{NMS}^{(2)}$, $K_{SMS}^{(2)}$) and the enhancement factor $D$ were evaluated primarily via the AR approach, as the FF approach showed numerical instabilities for these higher-order quantities. The calculated constants were then combined with experimental isotope shift data from Solaro (2020) and Chang (2024) to extract constraints on $\alpha_{NP}$ under two scenarios: Case I (neglecting Standard Model nonlinearities) and Case II (including all Standard Model nonlinear contributions).

## Key numbers
- For $m_{\phi} = 0$ eV, Case I analysis yields $|\alpha_{NP}| < 0.80 \times 10^{-10}$ (FF approach) at $2\sigma$ level; Case II analysis yields $|\alpha_{NP}| < 5.9 \times 10^{-10}$ (FF approach), approximately an order of magnitude weaker bound.
- The second-order mass shift constant $K^{(2)}$ contributes about 1.5 MHz to the isotope shifts, while the second-order field shift $F^{(2)}$ contributes about 0.13 MHz.
- For the $4S - 3D_{3/2}$ transition, the $G^{(2)}$ constant is $3.55(20)$ MHz/fm$^4$ (RCC method), compared to $0.07$ MHz/fm$^4$ from Viatkina (2023), approximately a factor of 50 difference.
- The $D$ values for the $4S \rightarrow 3D_{3/2}$ and $4S \rightarrow 3D_{5/2}$ transitions show substantial differences between Dirac-Hartree-Fock and RCC methods, with non-RPA correlation contributions being small but non-negligible.
- For the $A=40$ and $A'=46$ isotope pair, the proportionality factor $f_{ho}$ relating $G^{(2)}$ to the FS constant is $1.17$ in this work, versus $0.0238$ in Viatkina (2023).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of King plot nonlinearities in Ca$^+$ previously performed by Solaro (2020) and Chang (2024), who considered only new physics contributions to nonlinearity, and by Viatkina (2023), who calculated second-order isotope shift constants but obtained values approximately an order of magnitude smaller than the present results. The authors attribute these discrepancies to numerical instabilities in the finite-field approach used by Viatkina (2023) for extracting second-order effects. The improved calculation of higher-order Standard Model contributions enables more reliable extraction of bounds on the electron-neutron coupling from existing Ca$^+$ isotope shift measurements, and provides a framework for future analyses as experimental precision approaches the 1 Hz regime where these nonlinearities become detectable.
