# Supernova Neutrinos as a Precise Probe of Nuclear Neutron Skin

> A precise and model-independent determination of the neutron distribution radius $R_{\rm n}$ and thus the neutron skin thickness $R_{\rm skin}$ of atomic nuclei is of fundamental importance in nuclear physics, particle physics and astrophysics but remains a big challenge in terrestrial labs. We argue that the nearby core-collapse supernova (CCSN) in our Galaxy may render a neutrino flux with unprecedentedly high luminosity, offering perfect opportunity to determine the $R_{\rm n}$ and $R_{\rm skin}$ through the coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE$ν$NS). We evaluate the potential of determining the $R_{\rm n}$ of lead (Pb) via CE$ν$NS with the nearby CCSN neutrinos in the RES-NOVA project which is designed to hunt CCSN neutrinos using an array of archaeological Pb based cryogenic detectors. We find that an ultimate precision of $\sim 0.1 \%$ for the $R_{\rm n}$ ($\sim 0.006$ fm for the $R_{\rm skin}$) of Pb can be achieved via RES-NOVA in the most optimistic case that the CCSN explosion were to occur at a distance of $\sim 1$ kpc from the Earth.

## Key claim

The authors argue that neutrinos from a nearby galactic core-collapse supernova (CCSN) can determine the neutron radius \(R_n\) and neutron skin thickness \(R_{\rm skin}\) of lead via coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE\(\nu\)NS) in the RES-NOVA experiment, but only if the supernova occurs within about 1 kpc of Earth and systematic uncertainties are controlled below about 13%.

## Method

The authors model the time-integrated CCSN neutrino flux using a pinched thermal spectrum with parameters from a typical axisymmetric simulation, then compute the expected CE\(\nu\)NS event rates in the RES-NOVA detector (465 t archaeological Pb, 1 keV threshold) as a function of nuclear recoil energy. They use a Helm parametrization of the weak form factor to relate the neutron radius to the differential cross section, and perform a chi-squared analysis over recoil energy bins (4.5–29.5 keV for 1 kpc, 4.5–20.5 keV for 5 kpc) to estimate the statistical precision on \(R_n\) and \(R_{\rm skin}\), including a systematic error parameter \(p\).

## Key numbers

- Ultimate precision of \(\sim 0.1\%\) for the neutron radius \(R_n\) of Pb, corresponding to \(\sim 0.006\) fm for the neutron skin thickness \(R_{\rm skin}\), achievable for a CCSN at 1 kpc with 1% systematic error.
- For a CCSN at 5 kpc with 1% (5%) systematic error, the \(1\sigma\) uncertainty on \(R_{\rm skin}\) is \(\pm 0.030\) fm (\(\pm 0.044\) fm).
- For a CCSN at 1 kpc with 1% (5%) systematic error, the \(1\sigma\) uncertainty on \(R_{\rm skin}\) is \(\pm 0.008\) fm (\(\pm 0.016\) fm).
- The RES-NOVA detector mass is 465 t with an energy threshold of 1 keV; the energy resolution is expected to be 0.2 keV.
- The PREX-2 result for \(^{208}\)Pb is \(R_{\rm skin} = 0.283 \pm 0.071\) fm; the CREX result for \(^{48}\)Ca is \(R_{\rm skin} = 0.121 \pm 0.026\) (exp) \(\pm 0.024\) (model) fm.

## Context

This work extends the use of CE\(\nu\)NS for neutron skin determination, previously attempted with COHERENT data on CsI (Cadeddu 2017, Huang 2019) but with insufficient precision, to the much higher neutrino flux from a galactic CCSN. It directly addresses the tension between the PREX-2 result (Adhikari 2021) favoring a stiff symmetry energy and the CREX result (Adhikari 2022) favoring a soft one, as well as the tension with ab initio predictions (Hu 2022) that give \(R_{\rm skin}^{208} = 0.139\)–0.200 fm. The method enables a model-independent cross-check of these results and can in principle be applied to other detector media such as xenon in next-generation dark matter experiments.
