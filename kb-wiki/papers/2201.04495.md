# Diabatic Hamiltonian matrix elements made simple

> With a view to applying the Generator Coordinate Method to large configuration spaces, we propose a simple approximate formula to compute diabatic many-body matrix elements without having to evaluate two-body interaction matrix elements. The method is illustrated with two analytically solvable Hamiltonians based on the harmonic oscillator.

# Diabatic Hamiltonian matrix elements made simple

## Key claim

The authors propose a simple approximate formula for diabatic many-body Hamiltonian matrix elements in the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) that requires only orbital overlaps and single-particle energies, avoiding explicit evaluation of two-body interaction matrix elements. The formula is exact for the leading dependence on the coordinate difference in two analytically solvable harmonic oscillator models, but is approximate in general and relies on a separable residual interaction determined by a diabatic treatment of the generator coordinate.

## Method

The authors derive an orbital energy formula (Eq. 8) by placing a reference configuration midway between two GCM configurations and applying the Löwdin formula for matrix elements in the limit of small particle-hole amplitudes. They assume a separable form for the residual two-body interaction (Eq. 5) with strength determined by a self-consistency condition (Eq. 7), and further simplify by considering axially symmetric diabatic transformations where the particle-hole amplitudes satisfy c⁻ = -c⁺. The formula is tested against exact analytic solutions for two models: translational motion of N distinguishable particles in a harmonic oscillator potential, and a simplified Nilsson model for ¹⁶O with a deformation coordinate δ.

## Key numbers

- Translational model: exact Hamiltonian matrix element yields h₂ = N α² ℏ²/(8m) (Eq. 15), where α = (N-1)^(1/2) ω_v m/ℏ; the orbital energy formula reproduces this exactly with ∑ c⁺₁₀ c⁻₁₀ (ε₁ - ε₀) = N (α/2)(α/m)ℏ² · (1/4)(z-z')².
- Translational model: the collective mass extracted from GCM/GOA is N m, the exact total mass of N particles.
- Nilsson model for ¹⁶O: exact Hamiltonian matrix element expanded to quadratic order gives h₂ = ℏω₀ (Eq. 24), with the ground-state energy E₀ = 18ℏω₀.
- Nilsson model: the orbital energy formula yields -∑|C_ph|²(ε_p - ε_h) ∼ -4ℏω₀ δ² for δ' = -δ, matching the exact second-order term in Eq. 25.
- Nilsson model: the separable interaction strength is κ = -m²ω₀³/18 (Eq. 20).

## Context

This work extends the GCM formalism described in Ring and Schuck (1980) and earlier treatments by Brink (1965) and Löwdin (1955), providing a simplified alternative to the generalized Wick's theorem approach of Balian and Brézin (1969) and Bonche et al. (1990). The formula enables GCM calculations using only mean-field potentials (e.g., Woods-Saxon) without full knowledge of the many-body Hamiltonian, and avoids difficulties of treating energy functionals as Hamiltonians. The authors plan to report numerical calculations for more realistic models in a separate publication.
