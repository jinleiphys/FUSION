# Signature of the eta-NN configurations in coherent pi0 photoproduction on the deuteron

> The photoproduction of a neutral pion on the deuteron is considered in the energy region around the eta threshold, where a bump-like structure was observed at very backward pion angles. Different dynamical aspects which may be responsible for this phenomenon are analysed within a theoretical frame which includes intermediate eta-NN configurations. The results show in particular, that a three-body treatment of the eta-NN interaction is of special importance.

# Signature of the eta-NN configurations in coherent pi0 photoproduction on the deuteron

## Key claim

The bump-like structure observed in the differential cross section for coherent π⁰ photoproduction on the deuteron near the η threshold at very backward angles is primarily caused by two-nucleon mechanisms involving intermediate ηNN configurations, but a three-body treatment of the ηNN interaction is essential to reproduce the prominence and position of the peak, and the theory still underestimates the measured cross section by about one order of magnitude.

## Method

The author calculates the γd → π⁰d differential cross section using three successively improved approximations: impulse approximation (IA) based on the MAID elementary amplitude and Bonn-potential deuteron wave function; first-order rescattering approximation adding π and η exchange via the S₁₁(1535) resonance; and a full three-body calculation for the ηNN system in the s-wave (J^π,T = 0⁻,1) using a separable representation of the driving two-body interactions. The elementary amplitudes for γN → ηN and ηN → π⁰N are modeled exclusively through the S₁₁(1535) resonance with parameters fitted to reproduce γN → αN and π⁻p → ηn data.

## Key numbers

- The bump in the differential cross section appears around a lab photon energy E_γ = 650 MeV at very backward pion angles (θ_cm ≈ 180°).
- The four-momentum transfer squared in the backward region ranges between 15 and 35 fm⁻².
- The ηN scattering length used in the model is a_ηN = (0.5 + i0.3) fm.
- The bare resonance mass M₀ for the S₁₁(1535) is 1598 MeV.
- The ratio of η photoproduction cross sections on neutron versus proton is taken as σ(γn → ηn)/σ(γp → ηp) ≈ 0.67.
- The theory underestimates the CLAS preliminary data at backward angles by about one order of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kudryavtsev et al. (2004), who attributed the backward-angle bump in γd → π⁰d near the η threshold solely to the S₁₁(1535) resonance in the η rescattering mechanism. Fix shows that the impulse approximation already produces a shoulder near E_γ = 700 MeV from non-resonant terms in the MAID amplitude, and that including full three-body dynamics for the ηNN system shifts the peak to lower energy and makes it significantly more pronounced. The results contradict the simpler interpretation of Kudryavtsev et al. (2004) and suggest that extracting model-independent information on the fundamental ηN interaction from (γ,π⁰) reactions is not feasible. The work enables future investigations of ηNN correlations and possible virtual states, but the large discrepancy with CLAS data (Yordanov et al., preliminary) indicates that additional two-nucleon mechanisms such as meson exchange currents may be needed.
