# Isospin splitting of nucleon effective mass and symmetry energy in isotopic nuclear reactions

> Within an isospin and momentum dependent transport model, the dynamics of isospin particles (nucleons and light clusters) in Fermi-energy heavy-ion collisions are investigated for constraining the isospin splitting of nucleon effective mass and the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities. The mass splitting of $m^{*}_{n}>m^{*}_{p}$ and $m^{*}_{n}<m^{*}_{p}$ in nuclear matter and the different stiffness of symmetry energy are used in the model. The single and double neutron to proton ratios of free nucleons and light particles are thoroughly investigated in the isotopic nuclear reactions of $^{112}$Sn+$^{112}$Sn and $^{124}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn at the incident energies of 50 and 120 MeV/nucleon, respectively. It is found that the both effective mass splitting and symmetry energy impact the kinetic energy spectra of the single ratios, in particular at the high energy tail (larger than 20 MeV). Specific constraints are obtained from the double ratio spectra, which are evaluated from the ratios of isospin observables produced in $^{124}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn over $^{112}$Sn+$^{112}$Sn collisions. A mass splitting of $m^{*}_{n}<m^{*}_{p}$ is constrained from the available data at the energy of 120 MeV/nucleon. A soft symmetry energy with the stiffness of $γ_{s}=$0.5 is close to the experimental double ratio spectra at both energies.

# Isospin splitting of nucleon effective mass and symmetry energy in isotopic nuclear reactions

## Key claim
The double neutron-to-proton ratio spectra from isotopic Sn+Sn reactions at 120 MeV/nucleon constrain a neutron-proton effective mass splitting of \(m^{*}_{n}<m^{*}_{p}\), while the same observable at both 50 and 120 MeV/nucleon favors a soft symmetry energy with stiffness parameter \(\gamma_{s}=0.5\). The constraint on mass splitting is only clearly visible at the higher beam energy; at 50 MeV/nucleon the difference between the two mass-splitting scenarios is very small.

## Method
The authors used the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model, which incorporates isospin-, density-, and momentum-dependent mean-field potentials derived from Skyrme interactions. They simulated collisions of \(^{112}\)Sn+\(^{112}\)Sn and \(^{124}\)Sn+\(^{124}\)Sn at incident energies of 50 and 120 MeV/nucleon, with impact parameters 0-3 fm and a polar angle cut of \(70^{\circ}<\theta<110^{\circ}\). Single and double neutron-to-proton ratios of free nucleons and gas-phase particles (nucleons plus hydrogen and helium isotopes) were computed for two mass-splitting scenarios (\(m^{*}_{n}>m^{*}_{p}\) and \(m^{*}_{n}<m^{*}_{p}\)) and three symmetry-energy stiffnesses (\(\gamma_{s}=0.5, 1, 2\)), then compared to experimental data from the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory (NSCL).

## Key numbers
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: 31.5 MeV, obtained with \(C_{sym}=52.5\) MeV for \(m^{*}_{n}>m^{*}_{p}\) and \(C_{sym}=23.5\) MeV for \(m^{*}_{n}<m^{*}_{p}\).
- Effective mass in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density: \(m^{*}/m=0.75\), from parameters \(C_{mom}=1.76\) MeV and \(\epsilon=500\) c\(^{2}\)/GeV\(^{2}\).
- Compression modulus of isospin symmetric nuclear matter: K=230 MeV, from parameters \(\alpha=-215.7\) MeV, \(\beta=142.4\) MeV, \(\gamma=1.322\).
- Saturation density: \(\rho_{0}=0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The mass-splitting parameter \(x\) takes values \(-0.65\) for \(m^{*}_{n}>m^{*}_{p}\) and \(+0.65\) for \(m^{*}_{n}<m^{*}_{p}\).
- The isospin splitting of effective mass impacts the n/p ratio at kinetic energies above 20 MeV/nucleon; the double ratio data at 120 MeV/nucleon favor \(m^{*}_{n}<m^{*}_{p}\).
- A soft symmetry energy with \(\gamma_{s}=0.5\) is consistent with the experimental double ratio spectra at both 50 and 120 MeV/nucleon.

## Context
This work extends prior efforts to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy and the isospin splitting of the nucleon effective mass using heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies. The authors compare their LQMD model results to updated NSCL data (Coupland et al., 2016) for Sn+Sn reactions. Previous theoretical predictions for the mass splitting are contradictory: Landau-Fermi-liquid theory (Sjoberg, 2003) and nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory (Zuo, 2004) give \(m^{*}_{n}>m^{*}_{p}\), while relativistic mean-field and Dirac-Brueckner approaches (Li, 2005; Hofmann, 2006) give \(m^{*}_{n}<m^{*}_{p}\); the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach can produce either sign depending on parameters (Bao, 2007). The double ratio technique used here reduces sensitivity to Coulomb effects and detector efficiencies, enabling cleaner constraints. The results enable more precise modeling of neutron-rich matter for nuclear structure and astrophysical applications.
