# Mapping GENERIC hydrodynamics into Carter's multifluid theory

> We show that the GENERIC model for relativistic heat conduction is a multifluid of Carter. This allows one to compute the multifluid constitutive relations directly from the GENERIC formalism. As a quick application, we prove that, in the limit of infinite heat conductivity, GENERIC heat conduction reduces to the relativistic two-fluid model for superfluidity. This surprising ``crossover'' is a consequence of relativistic causality: If diffusion happens too fast, all the diffusing charge cumulates on the surface of the lightcone, and it eventually travels at the speed of light like a wave. Our analysis is non-perturbative, and it is carried out in the fully non-linear regime.

## Key claim

The paper proves an exact, non-perturbative mathematical equivalence between the GENERIC model for relativistic heat conduction and Carter's multifluid theory, showing that the constitutive relations of the latter can be derived directly from the GENERIC formalism without any approximation.

## Method

The author demonstrates that by adopting the Eckart frame (where the four-velocity is aligned with the particle current) and decomposing the multifluid tensors in terms of the vectors \(u^\mu\) and \(w^\mu\), the Carter multifluid constitutive relations map exactly onto the GENERIC equations for heat conduction. The field equations of the multifluid theory, including the relaxation equation for heat flux and the entropy production formula, are shown to coincide with the GENERIC equations of motion derived by Ottinger (1998).

## Key numbers

* The shear stress of ketchup at 30.5\(^\circ\)C follows \(\Pi_{12} \propto |\sigma_{12}|^{0.136}\) for shear rates \(|\sigma_{12}| \in [0.1, 100] \text{ s}^{-1}\), illustrating non-Newtonian behavior that motivates non-perturbative approaches.
* The entrainment matrix in Carter's theory is positive definite, yielding three inequalities: \(C>0\), \(B>0\), and \(CB-A^2>0\), which guarantee stability.
* The transport coefficient \(\Xi\) in the relaxation equation is set to \(\Xi = n/\tau\), where \(\tau\) is the heat relaxation time, connecting the multifluid and GENERIC formulations.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ottinger (1998) on GENERIC heat conduction and Carter's multifluid theory (Carter 1991, 1992) by establishing an exact dictionary between the two formalisms. It enables the derivation of non-perturbative constitutive relations for relativistic heat conduction from microphysics using the GENERIC framework, which was originally designed for complex fluids. The result also generalizes a previous linearized analysis by Gavassino (2023) showing that infinite heat conductivity in Israel-Stewart theory reduces to the Landau two-fluid model for superfluidity, now extended to the fully non-linear regime.
