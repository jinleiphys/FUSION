# Improved sd shell effective interactions from Daejeon16

> We present new microscopic effective shell-model interactions in the valence sd shell, obtained from the modern Daejeon16 nucleon-nucleon potential using no-core shell-model (NCSM) wave functions of 18F at Nmax=6 (total oscillator quanta of excitation) model space and the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki transformation. First, we explore the convergence properties of our calculations and show that the excitation energies of states in 18F, characterized by the largest valence-like configurations, are reasonably converged and the lowest states are in sensible agreement with experiment. Then, we investigate the monopole properties of that interaction in comparison with the phenomenological universal sd-shell interaction, USDB, and with the previously derived interaction at Nmax=4. Theoretical binding energies and low-energy spectra of the O isotopes, as well as low-energy spectra of a selection of $sd$-shell nuclei, are presented. We conclude that the use of larger-space NCSM wave functions leads to a noticeable improvement in the quality of the derived effective interaction. We propose monopole modifications of the Daejeon16 centroids which further improve the agreement with experiment throughout the sd shell, as demonstrated by a compilation of spectra contained in Supplemental Material.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using larger NCSM model spaces (Nmax=6) to derive effective sd-shell interactions from the Daejeon16 nucleon-nucleon potential yields noticeable improvement over previous Nmax=4 results, but the resulting single-particle energies remain significantly different from empirical values, requiring adoption of phenomenological USDB single-particle energies and monopole modifications to achieve agreement with experimental data across the sd shell.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell-model (NCSM) calculations for 16O, 17O, 17F, and 18F using the bare Daejeon16 NN potential at Nmax=6 with oscillator parameter hbar Omega=14 MeV, then applied the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki transformation to the 28 selected eigenstates of 18F to derive a valence-space effective Hamiltonian. The core energy from 16O and one-body terms from 17O and 17F were subtracted to obtain 63 two-body matrix elements (TBMEs) for the sd-shell valence space, which were then used with USDB single-particle energies and an (A/A0)^(-0.3) mass dependence for shell-model calculations across the sd shell.

## Key numbers

- NCSM ground state energy of 16O at Nmax=6: -124.485 MeV (core energy)
- Neutron single-particle energies at Nmax=6: 1s1/2 = -3.697 MeV, 0d5/2 = -3.299 MeV, 0d3/2 = 5.823 MeV
- Proton single-particle energies at Nmax=6: 1s1/2 = -0.253 MeV, 0d5/2 = 0.290 MeV, 0d3/2 = 9.063 MeV
- The spin-orbit splitting between d3/2 and d5/2 at Nmax=6 is about 9 MeV, compared to the empirical value of about 6.0 MeV
- USDB single-particle energies used for comparison: 0d5/2 = -3.9257 MeV, 1s1/2 = -3.2079 MeV, 0d3/2 = 2.1117 MeV
- The lowest 1+ T=0 state in 18F at Nmax=6 has energy -133.311 MeV with N=0 component probability 0.583

## Context

This work extends the approach of Smirnova and Barrett (2019), who derived sd-shell effective interactions from NCSM calculations at Nmax=4 using various NN potentials including Daejeon16, and found that Daejeon16 gave the best agreement with experiment after monopole corrections. The present study improves upon those results by using larger Nmax=6 model spaces, which leads to better convergence and improved description of binding energies and spectra for O isotopes and selected sd-shell nuclei. The results are compared with the phenomenological universal sd-shell interaction USDB of Brown and Richter, and the monopole properties are analyzed through effective single-particle energies (ESPEs) to identify remaining deficiencies in the microscopic interactions.
