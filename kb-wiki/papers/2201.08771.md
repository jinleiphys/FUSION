# Tsallis statistics and thermofractals: applications to high energy and hadron physics

> We study the applications of non-extensive Tsallis statistics to high energy and hadron physics. These applications include studies of $pp$ collisions, equation of state of QCD, as well as Bose-Einstein condensation. We also analyze the connections of Tsallis statistics with thermofractals, and address some of the conceptual aspects of the fractal approach, which are expressed in terms of the renormalization group equation and the self-energy corrections to the parton mass. We associate these well-known concepts with the origins of the fractal structure in the quantum field theory.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the fractal structure observed in Yang-Mills field theories, arising from renormalization group invariance and self-energy corrections, provides a physical origin for the power-law distributions described by Tsallis statistics in high-energy physics, with the entropic index \(q\) quantitatively linked to the number of colors and flavors in QCD.

## Method

The authors review the formalism of Tsallis non-extensive statistics and thermofractals, then derive an effective coupling for Yang-Mills fields using the thermofractal energy distribution. They compare the resulting 1-loop beta function with the standard QCD beta function to relate the entropic index \(q\) to the gauge group parameters \(N_c\) and \(N_f\). Applications are surveyed: transverse momentum distributions in \(pp\) collisions are fitted using the non-extensive self-consistent thermodynamics (NESCT) distribution; QCD thermodynamics is studied via a non-extensive hadron resonance gas (HRG) model; and Bose-Einstein condensation is analyzed using the non-extensive grand-canonical partition function.

## Key numbers

*   The entropic index extracted from the relation \(q = 1 + 3/(11 N_c - 2 N_f)\) with \(N_c = 3\) and \(N_f = 6\) yields \(q \simeq 1.14\).
*   Fits to transverse momentum distributions in \(pp\) collisions using the NESCT distribution give \(q = 1.14(1)\) and \(T = 62(5) \, \textrm{MeV}\).
*   The bag constant derived from the condition \(\varepsilon \cdot V_{\textrm{\scriptsize proton}} = m_{\textrm{\scriptsize proton}}\) and \(P \simeq \varepsilon/3\) is \(P = (0.135 \, \textrm{GeV})^4\).
*   The critical temperature for Bose-Einstein condensation in the non-extensive case scales as \(T_c = (N_q/V)^{1/3} \, \pi / [\pi \zeta_q(0)]^{1/3}\), with the maximum allowed entropic index for condensation being \(q_c = 3/2\).
*   The peak in the condensate fraction at \(T = T_c\) occurs at \(q_{\textrm{\scriptsize max}} = 1.14\) for a system with \(N_q = 409\) particles.

## Context

This work extends the non-extensive self-consistent thermodynamics (NESCT) approach, previously applied to \(pp\) collisions by Deppman (2012) and Marques (2012, 2015), to a broader set of QCD phenomena including the equation of state and Bose-Einstein condensation. It also builds on the thermofractal formalism of Deppman (2016, 2019, 2020) to provide a field-theoretic justification for Tsallis statistics, linking it to the renormalization group invariance of Yang-Mills theories. The results enable further studies of non-extensive effects in neutron star structure, as initiated by Menezes (2014), and open the question of how the fractal structure emerges from quantum field theory.
