# Criticality in a Hadron Resonance Gas model with the van der Waals interaction

> The van der Waals interaction is implemented in a Hadron Resonance Gas model. It is shown that this model can describe Lattice QCD data of different thermodynamical quantities satisfactorily with the van der Waals parameters $a = 1250 \pm 150$ MeV fm$^3$ and $r = 0.7 \pm 0.05$ fm. Further, a phase transition is observed in this model with the critical point at temperature, $T = 62.1$ MeV and baryon chemical potential, $μ_B = 708$ MeV.

## Key claim

The van der Waals Hadron Resonance Gas (VDWHRG) model, with parameters fitted to lattice QCD data at zero baryon chemical potential, predicts a liquid-gas phase transition and a critical point at finite chemical potential; the authors explicitly note that the nature of the interaction is assumed to be similar in both the zero and non-zero chemical potential regions.

## Method

The authors implement a van der Waals interaction (attractive parameter *a* and repulsive hard-core radius *r*) only for baryons and antibaryons in a Hadron Resonance Gas model, treating mesons as non-interacting. They perform a chi-square minimization to fit the model to continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD data (from the Wuppertal-Budapest and HotQCD collaborations) for pressure, energy density, entropy density, specific heat, and baryon number susceptibility at zero chemical potential over the temperature range 130–180 MeV. Using the best-fit parameters, they then extend the model to finite baryon chemical potential and locate the critical point of the liquid-gas phase transition by finding where the derivative of pressure with respect to number density vanishes.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit van der Waals parameters: attractive parameter *a* = 1250 ± 150 MeV fm³ and hard-core radius *r* = 0.7 ± 0.05 fm.
- Critical point of the liquid-gas phase transition: temperature *T* = 62.1 MeV and baryon chemical potential μ_B = 708 MeV.
- Uncertainties on the critical point: *T* = 62.1^{+25.4}_{-19.1} MeV, μ_B = 708^{+90}_{-146} MeV, derived from the uncertainties on the van der Waals parameters.
- The fit used lattice QCD data in the temperature range 130–180 MeV; a refit using data only up to 165 MeV gave *a* = 1210 MeV fm³, within the uncertainty of the main fit.

## Context

This work extends the VDWHRG model approach of Vovchenko et al. (2015), who fixed van der Waals parameters from nuclear matter ground-state properties and obtained only a qualitative description of lattice QCD data. By instead fitting the parameters directly to lattice QCD data at zero chemical potential, the authors obtain a quantitative description of those data and predict a liquid-gas critical point at (T = 62.1 MeV, μ_B = 708 MeV), which is comparable to a critical point found using holographic gauge/gravity correspondence by Critelli et al. (2017). The result enables future improvements such as incorporating mesonic interactions and additional missing resonances.
