# Lambda transverse polarization in NA61\SHINE at the CERN SPS: feasibility studies

> The spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons produced in inclusive reactions with unpolarized protons on unpolarized targets has been studied for almost 40 years. The NA61\SHINE experiment at the CERN SPS has a great potential to study transverse polarization in p-p and p-A collisions. In this work, we discuss the impact of magnetic field and limited detector acceptance on the possible polarization measurement by this experiment. Our analysis shows that the magnetic field impact on the $Λ$ polarization due to precession is one order of magnitude smaller than the detector acceptance-based polarization bias, and the identification of an experimental signal similar to that observed before by other experiments is possible.

# Lambda transverse polarization in NA61/SHINE at the CERN SPS: feasibility studies

## Key claim

The NA61/SHINE experiment can identify a Lambda transverse polarization signal similar to that observed by previous experiments, provided that the detector acceptance bias (which is one order of magnitude larger than the magnetic field precession effect) is corrected via Monte Carlo simulations.

## Method

The authors generated 1.2 x 10^8 p+p inelastic collision events at 158 GeV/c beam momentum using the Epos1.99 model, passed through a Geant3 detector simulation of the NA61/SHINE spectrometer. They applied geometric acceptance cuts analogous to those used in the NA61/SHINE Lambda production analysis (Aduszkiewicz 2016) and numerically integrated the Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi equation for Lambda spin precession in the spectrometer's magnetic field, using the measured field map with 1 cm step size along the beamline.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum: 158 GeV/c, corresponding to center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 17.3 GeV
- Magnetic field: 1.5 T in the first magnet, 1.1 T in the second magnet, with maximum total bending power 9 Tm
- Lambda mass: 1.115 GeV/c^2; asymmetry parameter alpha = 0.732 +/- 0.014
- 8.7 x 10^6 Lambda hyperons in the p pi- decay channel were analyzed from the simulated events
- Detector acceptance bias yields fitted polarization values P_x = -0.01, P_y = -0.04, P_z = -0.31 from initially uniform angular distributions
- Magnetic field precession bias components are smaller than 10^-2, with fitted values P_x = (1.4 +/- 1.8) x 10^-3, P_y = (-1.8 +/- 1.8) x 10^-3, P_z = (5.2 +/- 1.8) x 10^-3 after propagation
- Maximum precession angle reaches 0.35 radians for the most energetic Lambdas, but remains below 0.1 for the majority of particles
- Lambda transverse momentum coverage: p_T in (0, 1.6) GeV/c; Feynman variable |x_F| less than 0.4

## Context

This work extends the long-standing study of Lambda transverse polarization in inclusive reactions with unpolarized protons, which has been measured by experiments such as those reported by Erhan (1979), the EMC collaboration, and Fantietal (1999). The NA61/SHINE experiment offers the opportunity to measure this polarization at the previously unstudied center-of-mass energy of 17.3 GeV. The analysis demonstrates that the dominant systematic effect is the detector acceptance bias, not the magnetic field precession, enabling future measurements that can test theoretical models such as those by Andersson (1979) and Szwed (1981).
