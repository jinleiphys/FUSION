# Causality in the relativistic bound-state problem

> Although the exact Bethe-Salpeter equation is certainly the appropriate field-theoretic framework to describe the non-perturbative problem of scattering and bound states, the inevitable truncations introduce inconsistencies such as loss of symmetries or incorrect one-body limit. I conjecture that these problem can be overcome if the truncation preserves the field-redefinition invariance of the exact equation. A sum rule for light-by-light scattering can provide a testing ground of this conjecture.

## Key claim

The author conjectures that the inconsistencies introduced by truncating the Bethe-Salpeter equation (such as loss of symmetries or incorrect one-body limit) can be overcome if the truncation preserves field-redefinition invariance, and that a sum rule for light-by-light scattering can provide a testing ground for this conjecture.

## Method

The paper examines the Gross equation (a 3D reduction of the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation obtained by putting the heavier particle on mass shell) and the infrared regularization of baryon chiral perturbation theory, showing that both procedures remove the negative-energy pole of nucleon propagators. The author argues that this removal violates charge-conjugation symmetry and therefore causality, and proposes that preserving field-redefinition invariance in truncations of the Bethe-Salpeter equation could resolve these issues.

## Key numbers

- The Gross equation recovers the correct one-body limit for one-neutral-boson-exchange potentials only, as shown in Pascalutsa (1999)
- The infrared regularization procedure replaces each nucleon propagator such that the modified propagator has only one pole, the positive-energy one
- The sum rule for light-by-light scattering is given as: ∫₀^∞ (ds/s)[σ₂(s) - σ₀(s)] = 0, where σ_λ are polarized total cross sections for γγ fusion

## Context

This work extends the author's previous collaboration with John Tjon on charge-conjugation symmetry violations in the Gross equation (Pascalutsa and Tjon, 1997), and connects to the infrared regularization scheme of Becher and Leutwyler (1999). The paper suggests that preserving field-redefinition invariance in truncations of the Bethe-Salpeter equation could resolve problems with fundamental symmetries, gauge invariance, and causality that plague current approaches including chiral effective field theory calculations of the nuclear force (Weinberg, 1991; Epelbaum, 2010).
