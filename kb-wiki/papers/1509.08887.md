# Charm Degrees of Freedom in Quark Gluon Plasma

> Lattice QCD studies on fluctuations and correlations of charm quantum number have established that deconfinement of charm degrees of freedom sets in around the chiral crossover temperature, $T_c$, i.e. charm degrees of freedom carrying fractional baryonic charge start to appear. By reexamining those same lattice QCD data we show that, in addition to the contributions from quark-like excitations, the partial pressure of charm degrees of freedom may still contain significant contributions from open-charm meson and baryon-like excitations associated with integral baryonic charges for temperatures up to $1.2~ T_c$. Charm quark-quasiparticles become the dominant degrees of freedom for temperatures $T>1.2~ T_c$.

## Key claim
By reexamining lattice QCD data on charm fluctuations and correlations, the authors show that weakly interacting charm quasi-quarks become the dominant charm degrees of freedom only above approximately 200 MeV (about 1.2 Tc), while for temperatures between Tc and 200 MeV, significant contributions from open-charm meson and baryon-like excitations carrying integral baryonic charge persist alongside the quark-like excitations.

## Method
The authors reexpress lattice QCD results for up to fourth order generalized charm susceptibilities from Bazavov (2014) in terms of partial pressures of charm quark-like, meson-like, and baryon-like excitations, assuming a non-interacting gas of these excitations within the Boltzmann approximation. They isolate the individual partial pressures using specific combinations of baryon-charm and strange-charm susceptibilities, and validate their model by checking four independent constraints (c1 through c4) that must hold if the model is correct. The analysis covers the temperature range from Tc (154 ± 9 MeV) up to approximately 200 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The chiral crossover temperature is Tc = 154 ± 9 MeV (Bazavov 2011, Borsanyi 2010, Bhattacharya 2014).
- Weakly coupled charm quasi-quarks become the dominant charm degrees of freedom only above T ≈ 200 MeV (approximately 1.3 Tc).
- For T ≲ 180 MeV, the combined contributions of meson-like and baryon-like excitations exceed the contribution from quark-like excitations.
- The partial pressure of S=2 charm baryon-like excitations is negligible throughout the temperature range studied.
- All four constraints c1, c2, c3, and c4 are consistent with zero within the precision of the lattice data, supporting the proposed model.

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD studies of charm fluctuations and correlations by Bazavov (2014), which established that charm degrees of freedom carrying fractional baryonic charge appear at Tc and that below Tc charm is well described by a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model. The present analysis shows that weakly interacting quasi-quarks, which describe light quark degrees only for T ≳ 2 Tc (Bazavov 2013, 2014), also fail to describe charm degrees for T ≲ 200 MeV. The results support earlier suggestions of heavy-light bound states in QGP (He 2012, 2014; Adil 2006; Sharma 2009) and resonant scattering of heavy quarks (Mannarelli 2005), with implications for understanding elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor of heavy flavors in heavy-ion collisions.
