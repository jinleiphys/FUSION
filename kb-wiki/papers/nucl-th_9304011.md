# Determination of Dipole Polarization Effects in $^7$Li and $^{11}$Li

> The structure of $^6$Li, $^7$Li and $^{11}$Li nuclei is investigated in a model space which includes all configurations with oscillator energy up to $3\hbarω$ above the ground state configuration. The energy spectra and electromagnetic properties of the low-lying states are determined with various two-body interactions, which are derived from the Bonn potential. The results of these shell-model calculations depend on the strength of the tensor component contained in the NN interaction and also on the treatment of the Dirac spinors for the nucleons in the nuclear medium. In addition the calculation determines the {\it dipole polarizability} of $^7$Li and $^{11}$Li caused from virtual $E1$ excitations to the positive parity states of these nuclei. It is demonstrated that the BAGEL scheme provides a very powerful tool to consider contributions from virtual excitations up to large energies.

## Key claim

The dipole polarizability of \(^{7}\)Li and \(^{11}\)Li, arising from virtual \(E1\) excitations to positive-parity states, is calculated using a shell-model space that includes all configurations up to \(3\hbar\omega\) above the ground state; the results depend on the tensor strength of the NN interaction and on the treatment of Dirac spinors in the nuclear medium, and the authors note that for \(^{11}\)Li the predictions should be considered theoretical because experimental data are scarce.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for \(^{6}\)Li, \(^{7}\)Li, and \(^{11}\)Li in a model space including all configurations with oscillator energy up to \(3\hbar\omega\) above the ground state (i.e., \((0+2)\hbar\omega\) for natural-parity states and \((1+3)\hbar\omega\) for unnatural-parity states). Two-body interactions were derived from the Bonn potential (versions A and C, differing in tensor strength) via the Bethe-Goldstone equation, and the Dirac spinors for nucleons in the medium were either taken as free (\(m^* = 938\) MeV) or modified (\(m^* = 630\) MeV). The BAGEL scheme was used to selectively diagonalize the large \((1+3)\hbar\omega\) space and extract the unnatural-parity states that carry \(E1\) strength to the ground and first excited states.

## Key numbers

- The experimental binding energies are \(-31.99\) MeV for \(^{6}\)Li, \(-39.25\) MeV for \(^{7}\)Li, and \(-45.54\) MeV for \(^{11}\)Li; all interactions considered in the calculation greatly underestimate these values.
- For \(^{7}\)Li, the \(G^C_m\) interaction (Bonn C potential with \(m^* = 630\) MeV) gave the largest magnitude for the polarizability tensors \(\tau_{11}\) and \(\tau_{12}\) and the monopole term \(P\); the oscillator parameter adopted for this interaction was \(b = 2.0\) fm for \(^{7}\)Li and \(b = 2.1\) fm for \(^{11}\)Li.
- For \(^{7}\)Li, the \(1/2^+\) unnatural-parity states concentrate about 40% of the \(E1\) strength in the lowest state at about 9.5 MeV, while for \(3/2^+\) states one must extend to about 25 MeV to exhaust 50% of the total strength.
- The \(r_{ms}\) radius of \(^{11}\)Li is \(3.16 \pm 0.11\) fm from experiment; the authors note that their parameter-free calculation does not expect to account for all properties of this loosely bound nucleus.
- For \(^{7}\)Li, the polarizability quantities \(\tau_{11}\), \(\tau_{12}\), and \(P\) converge rapidly with BAGEL iterations; after the fifth iteration the change is insignificant (25 iterations were used in the final calculation).

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of dipole polarization in \(^{7}\)Li by Gomez-Comacho and Nagarajan (1992) and Barker and Woods (1990), which used only \(0p\)-shell configurations for natural-parity states and \(1\hbar\omega\) spaces for unnatural-parity states, by employing a larger model space (\(3\hbar\omega\)) and a realistic Hamiltonian with no adjustable parameters. For \(^{11}\)Li, it contrasts with earlier large-scale shell-model calculations by Hoshino et al. (1991) and Hayes and Strottman (1991), which used mixed effective Hamiltonians, by using a consistent \(G\)-matrix derived from the Bonn potential. The results enable future comparisons with cluster-model approaches and suggest that including even higher configurations (e.g., \(4\hbar\omega\) for natural-parity and \(5\hbar\omega\) for unnatural-parity states) could bring shell-model predictions closer to those of cluster models.
