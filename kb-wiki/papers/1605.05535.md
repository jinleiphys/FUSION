## Key claim

The paper uses in-medium QCD sum rules with interpolating currents containing an arbitrary mixing parameter (not just the Ioffe current) to calculate the mass shifts, residue shifts, and scalar and vector self-energies of heavy baryons in nuclear matter, finding that the maximum mass shift occurs for the Sigma_c baryon at -936 MeV, but the results depend on the choice of the mixing parameter beta and the working windows for Borel mass and continuum threshold.

## Method

The authors construct in-medium two-point correlation functions for the heavy Lambda_Q, Sigma_Q, and Xi_Q baryons (with Q = b or c) using interpolating currents that include an arbitrary mixing parameter beta. They match the hadronic and operator product expansion (OPE) sides of the correlation function after Borel transformation, including contributions from perturbative, two-quark, two-gluon, mixed, and four-quark condensates in nuclear matter. The sum rules are solved simultaneously for the modified mass, modified residue, and vector self-energy, with the scalar self-energy obtained from the difference between in-medium and vacuum masses.

## Key numbers

* Maximum mass shift in nuclear medium: Delta m_{Sigma_c} = -936 MeV (Sigma_c baryon)
* Maximum residue shift in nuclear medium: Delta lambda_{Sigma_b} = -0.014 GeV^3 (Sigma_b baryon)
* Scalar self-energies: Sigma^S_{Lambda_b} = 653 MeV, Sigma^S_{Sigma_b} = -614 MeV, Sigma^S_{Xi_b} = -17 MeV, Sigma^S_{Lambda_c} = 272 MeV, Sigma^S_{Sigma_c} = -936 MeV, Sigma^S_{Xi_c} = -5 MeV
* Vector self-energies: Sigma^nu_{Lambda_b} = 436 +/- 148 MeV, Sigma^nu_{Sigma_b} = 382 +/- 129 MeV, Sigma^nu_{Xi_b} = 15 +/- 5 MeV, Sigma^nu_{Lambda_c} = 151 +/- 45 MeV, Sigma^nu_{Sigma_c} = 486 +/- 144 MeV, Sigma^nu_{Xi_c} = 1.391 +/- 0.529 MeV
* Vacuum mass of Lambda_b: 5.614 +/- 0.345 GeV; in-medium mass: 6.267 +/- 0.175 GeV
* Vacuum mass of Sigma_c: 2.451 +/- 0.208 GeV; in-medium mass: 1.515 +/- 0.321 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy baryons in nuclear matter by Wang (2011) and Wang (2012), which used Ioffe currents (beta = -1) to study spectroscopic parameters. The present authors use a more general interpolating current with an arbitrary mixing parameter, finding significantly larger shifts in masses and self-energies compared to the Ioffe current results. The results enable predictions for experiments at FAIR (PANDA and CBM collaborations) studying in-medium properties of hadrons, and provide input for understanding neutron star structure and heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
