# Critical properties of calibrated relativistic mean-field models for the transition to warm, non-homogeneous nuclear matter

> The critical properties for the transition to warm, asymmetric, non-homogeneous nuclear matter are analysed within a thermodynamical spinodal approach for a set of well calibrated equations of state. It is shown that even though different equations of state are constrained by the same experimental, theoretical and observational data, and the properties of symmetric nuclear matter are similar within the models, the properties of very asymmetric nuclear matter, such as the one found inside of neutron stars, differ a lot for various models. Some models predict larger transition densities to homogeneous matter for beta-equilibrated matter than for symmetric nuclear matter. Since one expects that such properties have a noticeable impact on the the evolution of either a supernova or neutron star merger, this different behavior should be understood in more detail.

## Key claim

Even though different relativistic mean-field (RMF) equations of state are calibrated to the same experimental, theoretical, and observational data and produce similar symmetric nuclear matter properties, the properties of very asymmetric nuclear matter relevant to neutron stars differ substantially between models; some models predict that the transition density to homogeneous matter for beta-equilibrated matter is larger than for symmetric nuclear matter, contrary to the expected behavior from the repulsive symmetry term.

## Method

The authors calculated the thermodynamical spinodal surfaces in the (proton density, neutron density, temperature) space for ten RMF models (SFHo, SFHx, FSU2R, FSU2H, TM1, TM1e, DD2, DDME2, D1, D2) by identifying the region where the curvature matrix of the free energy density has a negative eigenvalue. They determined the critical points (where spinodal and binodal coincide) at several temperatures and extracted the transition densities for beta-equilibrated matter and symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- SFHo critical end point (CEP) occurs at T = 16.14 MeV and density = 0.051 fm^-3
- For SFHo at T = 0 MeV, the beta-equilibrium transition density is rho_t = 0.105 fm^-3, while symmetric matter transition density is rho_sym = 0.101 fm^-3
- For SFHx at T = 0 MeV, the beta-equilibrium transition density is rho_t = 0.122 fm^-3 with proton fraction y_p = 0.041, and symmetric matter transition density is rho_sym = 0.103 fm^-3
- For TM1 at T = 0 MeV, the beta-equilibrium transition density is rho_t = 0.047 fm^-3 with proton fraction y_p = 0.025, and symmetric matter transition density is rho_sym = 0.070 fm^-3
- For DD2 at T = 0 MeV, the beta-equilibrium transition density is rho_t = 0.081 fm^-3 with proton fraction y_p = 0.034, and symmetric matter transition density is rho_sym = 0.095 fm^-3
- At T = 0 MeV, SFHx has a critical point with proton fraction y_pc = 0.0 and critical density rho_c = 0.1010 fm^-3; SFHo has y_pc = 0.0283 and rho_c = 0.1015 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamical spinodal analysis of Avancini et al. (2006) and Ducoin et al. (2008) to a set of recently calibrated RMF models that satisfy modern constraints from nuclear experiments, neutron star observations, and chiral effective field theory calculations. The study enables better understanding of how different equations of state will affect simulations of core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers, particularly regarding the formation and dissolution of nuclear clusters in warm, asymmetric matter. The authors note that the thermodynamical spinodal gives transition densities approximately 10% larger than more realistic Thomas-Fermi calculations, as shown by Avancini et al. (2010) and Ducoin et al. (2011).
