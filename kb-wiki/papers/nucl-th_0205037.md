# Microscopic Study of Slablike and Rodlike Nuclei: Quantum Molecular Dynamics Approach

> Structure of cold dense matter at subnuclear densities is investigated by quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) simulations. We succeeded in showing that the phases with slab-like and rod-like nuclei etc. can be formed dynamically from hot uniform nuclear matter without any assumptions on nuclear shape. We also observe intermediate phases, which has complicated nuclear shapes. Geometrical structures of matter are analyzed with Minkowski functionals, and it is found out that intermediate phases can be characterized as ones with negative Euler characteristic. Our result suggests the existence of these kinds of phases in addition to the simple ``pasta'' phases in neutron star crusts.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that slablike and rodlike nuclear phases (nuclear "pasta") can be formed dynamically from hot uniform nuclear matter using quantum molecular dynamics simulations without any prior assumptions on nuclear shape, but only for proton fractions of x=0.3 and 0.5; the authors hedge that this conclusion must be confirmed for more neutron-rich cases (x ~ 0.1) relevant to neutron star crusts.

## Method
The authors performed quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) simulations of infinite (n,p,e) systems with fixed proton fractions x=0.3 and 0.5 at subnuclear densities (0.05-1.0 ρ₀, where ρ₀=0.165 fm⁻³). They placed 2048 nucleons (1372 in some cases) in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, treated relativistic degenerate electrons as a uniform background, and calculated Coulomb interactions using the Ewald method. The system was initialized as a hot uniform nucleon gas at k_BT ~ 20 MeV, then cooled slowly over 10³-10⁴ fm/c to temperatures of ~0.1 MeV or less using frictional relaxation, with no artificial fluctuations introduced during the simulation.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear density: ρ₀ = 0.165 fm⁻³
- Proton fractions studied: x = 0.3 and 0.5
- Density range investigated: 0.05 - 1.0 ρ₀
- System sizes: 2048 nucleons (1372 in some cases)
- Initial temperature: k_BT ~ 20 MeV
- Final temperature: ~0.1 MeV or less
- Relaxation time scales: ~10³ fm/c for bubble phases, ~O(10⁴) fm/c for nucleus phases
- Typical inter-structure length scale: l ~ 10 fm
- Threshold nucleon density for defining nuclear surface: ρ_th* in range 0.7-0.9 ρ₀
- Density where area-averaged mean curvature ⟨H⟩ ≈ 0: ~0.4 ρ₀ for x=0.5, ~0.35 ρ₀ for x=0.3

## Context
This work extends earlier liquid-drop model calculations by Ravenhall et al. and Hashimoto et al. that predicted nuclear "pasta" phases (sphere → cylinder → slab → cylindrical hole → spherical hole → uniform) based on energy minimization with assumed nuclear shapes. It improves upon a pioneering QMD attempt by Maruyama et al. that could not reproduce nonspherical nuclei due to inconsistent Coulomb treatment and insufficient annealing. The results contradict Thomas-Fermi calculations by Williams and Lassaut that did not find intermediate phases, likely because those one-body approximations could not incorporate density fluctuations and contained only one structure per simulation box. The work enables future investigation of how these intermediate "sponge-like" phases (characterized by negative Euler characteristic) affect neutron star crust dynamics, pulsar glitches, neutrino cooling, and supernova neutrino transport.
