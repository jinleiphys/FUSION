# Dependence of related parameters on centrality and mass in a new treatment for transverse momentum spectra in high energy collisions

> We collected the experimental data of transverse momentum spectra of identified particles produced in proton-proton ($p$-$p$), deuteron-gold ($d$-Au or $d$-$A$), gold-gold (Au-Au or $A$-$A$), proton-lead ($p$-Pb or $p$-$A$), and lead-lead (Pb-Pb or $A$-$A$) collisions measured by the ALICE, CMS, LHCb, NA49, NA61/SHINE, PHENIX, and STAR collaborations at different center-of mass energies. The multisource thermal model at the quark level or the participant quark model is used to describe the experimental data. The free parameters, the effective temperature $T$, entropy index-related $n$, and revised index $a_{0}$, in the revised Tsallis--Pareto-type function are extracted at the quark level. In most cases, $T$ and $n$ in central collisions are larger than those in peripheral collisions, and $a_0$ does not change in different centrality classes. With the increase in the mass of produced particle or participant quark, $T$ and $a_0$ increase, and $n$ does not change significantly. The behaviors of related parameters from $p$-$p$, $p(d)$-$A$, and $A$-$A$ collisions are similar.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the effective temperature $T$ and entropy index-related $n$ extracted from a revised Tsallis-Pareto-type function at the quark level are larger in central collisions than in peripheral collisions for most cases, while the revised index $a_0$ does not change with centrality; however, these trends are qualified as holding "in most cases" and the parameter $n$ shows no significant change with particle or quark mass.

## Method

The authors collected experimental transverse momentum spectra of identified particles from $p$-$p$, $d$-Au, Au-Au, $p$-Pb, and Pb-Pb collisions measured by ALICE, CMS, LHCb, NA49, NA61/SHINE, PHENIX, and STAR collaborations at various center-of-mass energies. They applied the multisource thermal model at the quark level (participant quark model), using the convolution of two (for mesons) or three (for baryons) revised Tsallis-Pareto-type (TP-like) functions to fit the spectra, extracting the free parameters effective temperature $T$, entropy index-related $n$, and revised index $a_0$ at the quark level.

## Key numbers

- For $\pi^+$+$\pi^-$ in $p$-$p$ collisions at 2.76 TeV: $T = 0.216 \pm 0.001$ GeV, $n = 5.047 \pm 0.023$, $a_0 = -0.500 \pm 0.004$
- For $p$+$\bar p$ in $p$-$p$ collisions at 2.76 TeV: $T = 0.205 \pm 0.001$ GeV, $n = 6.128 \pm 0.020$, $a_0 = -0.130 \pm 0.002$
- For $\Upsilon(1S)$ in $p$-$p$ collisions at 5.02 TeV: $T = 0.668 \pm 0.002$ GeV, $n = 2.918 \pm 0.021$, $a_0 = 0.297 \pm 0.003$
- For prompt $\psi(2S)$ in $p$-$p$ collisions at 7 TeV: $T = 0.538 \pm 0.002$ GeV, $n = 4.962 \pm 0.023$, $a_0 = 0.300 \pm 0.003$
- The critical temperature of chemical freeze-out is approximately 0.16 GeV, which is smaller than the $T$ values extracted at the quark level

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the multisource thermal model at the quark level (Yang et al., 2020) by systematically studying the dependence of the three free parameters on centrality, hadron rest mass, and constituent quark mass across multiple collision systems. It differs from earlier Tsallis-inspired model analyses (various authors, 2000-2020) by performing the analysis at the quark level rather than the hadron level and using a more flexible treatment for the low transverse momentum region. The results enable systematic comparison of parameter behaviors across $p$-$p$, $p(d)$-$A$, and $A$-$A$ collisions, and the extracted effective temperature $T$ at the quark level may be interpreted as approximately the physical temperature since collective flow has not yet formed at the participant quark stage.
