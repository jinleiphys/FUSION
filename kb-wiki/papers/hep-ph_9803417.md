# Effective Field Theory for Low-Energy np Systems

> The properties of low-energy neutron-proton systems are studied in an effective field theory where only nucleons figure as relevant degrees of freedom. With a finite momentum cut-off regularization scheme, we show that the large scattering lengths of the np systems do not spoil the convergence of the effective field theory, which turns out to be extremely successful in reproducing, with little cut-off dependence, the deuteron properties, the np 1S0 scattering amplitude and most significantly, the M1 transition amplitude entering into the radiative np capture process.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a finite momentum cut-off regularization scheme, rather than dimensional regularization, resolves the convergence problems of effective field theory (EFT) for low-energy neutron-proton systems, even when the large scattering lengths are present. The claim is conditional on choosing an optimal cut-off around Λ ∼ 200 MeV, which yields results with little cut-off dependence.

## Method

The authors construct a non-relativistic EFT with only nucleon degrees of freedom, integrating out pions and all other massive fields. The nucleon-nucleon potential consists of zero-range contact interactions up to next-to-leading order (NLO), regularized with a Gaussian momentum cut-off S_Λ(p²) = exp(-p²/2Λ²). The Lippmann-Schwinger equation is solved for the ¹S₀ and ³S₁ channels, with five low-energy constants (C₀, C₂ in each channel plus D₂ in the triplet channel) fixed by fitting to the effective-range parameters from the Argonne v₁₈ potential.

## Key numbers

- Scattering lengths used: a = -23.732 fm for ¹S₀ channel, a = 5.419 fm for ³S₁ channel (from Argonne v₁₈ potential)
- Effective ranges used: r_e = 2.697 fm for ¹S₀, r_e = 1.753 fm for ³S₁ (from Argonne v₁₈ potential)
- Maximum cut-off for Hermiticity: Λ_Max ≈ 348.0 MeV for ¹S₀, Λ_Max = 304.0 MeV for ³S₁
- Special cut-off Λ_{Z=1} = 172.2 MeV for ¹S₀ (where NLO contribution vanishes); Λ_{Z=1} = 198.8 MeV for ³S₁
- Deuteron binding energy B_d = 2.211 MeV at Λ = 216.1 MeV (experiment: 2.225 MeV)
- Deuteron quadrupole moment Q_d = 0.288 fm² at Λ = 216.1 MeV (experiment: 0.286 fm²)
- M1 transition amplitude M_{1B} = 3.99 fm at Λ = 216.1 MeV (Argonne v₁₈: 3.98 fm)
- Dimensionless naturalness of coefficients at Λ = 216.1 MeV: m_π C₀ = -1.4, m_π³ C₂ = 0.1, m_π³ D₂ = 0.4

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculation of the np → dγ process by Park, Min, and Rho (1997), which had used phenomenological wavefunctions from the Argonne v₁₈ potential, to a complete first-principles EFT calculation. It directly addresses the breakdown of dimensional regularization found by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996) and Luke and Manohar (1996), who showed that EFT with large scattering lengths breaks down at a critical momentum p_crit ∼ √(2/a r_e). The finite cut-off approach, following the observations of Beane et al. (1997) and Lepage (1997), converts the large scattering length problem into a weakly interacting system by replacing a with an effective a_Λ of order Λ⁻¹. This enables future extensions to the proton fusion process pp → de⁺ν and inclusion of pion degrees of freedom for higher chiral orders.
