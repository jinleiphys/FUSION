# Relativistic Covariance and Quark-Diquark Wave Functions

> We derive covariant wave functions for hadrons composed of two constituents for arbitrary Lorentz boosts. Focussing explicitly on baryons as quark-diquark systems, we reduce their manifestly covariant Bethe-Salpeter equation to covariant 3-dimensional forms by projecting on the relative quark-diquark energy. Guided by a phenomenological multi gluon exchange representation of covariant confining kernels, we derive explicit solutions for harmonic confinement and for the MIT Bag Model. We briefly sketch implications of breaking the spherical symmetry of the ground state and the transition from the instant form to the light cone via the infinite momentum frame.

# Relativistic Covariance and Quark-Diquark Wave Functions for Baryons

## Key claim

The paper derives covariant wave functions for baryons modeled as quark-diquark systems by projecting the Bethe-Salpeter equation onto a three-dimensional form, but the results depend on a phenomenological parametrization of the confining kernel whose full momentum dependence is not known from first principles; the model is therefore limited to analytical solutions for harmonic confinement and the MIT Bag Model, and the quantitative importance of Lorentz covariance for practical calculations at momentum transfers of 1 GeV remains an open question.

## Method

The author starts from the manifestly covariant four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation for a quark-diquark system and projects out the relative energy dependence using a specific limit (nu -> infinity) that yields a single-particle Dirac equation for the quark. The interaction kernel is parametrized as a superposition of weighted gluon exchange contributions with variable power n, which after Fourier transformation to coordinate space gives confining potentials proportional to r^(2n-1). The resulting Dirac equation is reduced to a Schroedinger-type equation for the large component, and explicit solutions are obtained for harmonic confinement (n=2) and for the MIT Bag Model (n -> infinity).

## Key numbers

- The quenching parameter lambda(P) = M / sqrt(M^2 + P^2) = M / E(P) governs the Lorentz contraction of the wave function along the boost direction.
- For harmonic confinement, the effective P-dependent size parameter is a^2(P) = (M^2 / (P^2 + M^2)) * a^2, where a^2 = 2 / sqrt(V_c) and V_c is proportional to 1/R^4.
- Simple rescaling approximations for the wave function break down completely for boost momenta P/M >= 1.
- The transition to the infinite momentum frame yields identical equations in the instant form and on the light cone in the limit M -> infinity.

## Context

This work extends earlier approaches to Lorentz-boosted hadronic wave functions that used simple kinematical rescaling prescriptions (references 31, 32 in the paper) and addresses the problem of center-of-mass corrections in the instant form (references 3-6). The model builds on the quark-diquark picture of baryons (reference 33) and the Bethe-Salpeter formalism (reference 34) with a projection method following Blankenbecler and Sugar (reference 35). The results enable systematic studies of form factors at momentum transfers around 1 GeV, but the author notes that a deeper analytical understanding of confinement beyond lattice calculations is needed, pointing to recent work on confinement in Coulomb gauge (reference 44) and meron solutions (reference 45) as promising directions.
