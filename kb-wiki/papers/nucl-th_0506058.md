# Hadron-string cascade versus hydrodynamics in Cu+Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV

> Single particle spectra as well as elliptic flow in Cu+Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV are investigated within a hadronic cascade model and an ideal hydrodynamic model. Pseudorapidity distribution and transverse momentum spectra for charged hadrons are surprisingly comparable between these two models. However, a large deviation is predicted for the elliptic flow. The forthcoming experimental data will clarify the transport and thermalization aspects of matter produced in Cu+Cu collisions.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that single-particle spectra (pseudorapidity and transverse momentum distributions) for charged hadrons in Cu+Cu collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV are surprisingly similar between a hadron-string cascade model (JAM) and an ideal hydrodynamic model, but a large deviation is predicted for elliptic flow \(v_2\), which will clarify whether the produced matter is thermalized or not.

## Method

The authors compared two models for Cu+Cu collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV: the hadron-string cascade model JAM (which simulates individual hadron-hadron collisions with resonance and string excitations, without a quark-gluon plasma phase) and a three-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model with initial conditions from the color glass condensate (CGC) picture and an ideal gas equation of state with a bag constant. They computed pseudorapidity distributions, transverse momentum spectra, and elliptic flow \(v_2\) as functions of impact parameter, pseudorapidity, and transverse momentum for charged hadrons, using kinetic freezeout temperatures of 100 and 160 MeV in the hydrodynamic model.

## Key numbers

- Pseudorapidity distributions and transverse momentum spectra for charged hadrons from JAM and hydrodynamics agree well for transverse momentum \(p_T < 2\) GeV/\(c\) at impact parameters \(b = 1, 2, 5\) fm.
- Elliptic flow \(v_2\) at mid-rapidity in JAM shows a peak at around \(b = 6\) fm, with magnitude only about 20% of the hydrodynamic prediction with \(T^{\mathrm{th}} = 100\) MeV.
- In the hydrodynamic model, \(v_2\) grows almost linearly with impact parameter \(b\), similar to Au+Au collisions.
- For the hydrodynamic model, chemical freezeout temperature \(T^{\mathrm{ch}} = 170\) MeV and kinetic freezeout temperatures \(T^{\mathrm{th}} = 100\) and 160 MeV were used; the bag constant was \(B^{1/4} = 247\) MeV with critical temperature \(T_c = 170\) MeV.
- In JAM, \(v_2\) starts to saturate at around \(p_T = 0.8\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, where ideal hydrodynamics successfully reproduced data near mid-rapidity (Kolb 2000, Hirano 2001), while hadronic cascade models underestimated elliptic flow at RHIC (Bleicher 2000, Cassing 2003, 2004). The authors note that in Cu+Cu collisions, the smaller system size and particle density may spoil the agreement seen in Au+Au, making non-equilibrium hadronic descriptions relatively important. The predictions enable experimental data from RHIC to distinguish between a thermalized (hydrodynamic) picture and a transport (cascade) picture, and the authors emphasize that measurements of \(v_2(b)\), \(v_2(\eta)\), and \(v_2(p_T)\) will provide important information on transport aspects of QCD matter.
