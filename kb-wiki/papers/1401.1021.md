# Mean-field approximation for the chiral soliton in a chiral phase transition

> In the mean-field approximation we study the chiral soliton within the linear sigma model in a thermal vacuum. The chiral soliton equations with different boundary conditions are solved at finite temperatures and densities. The solitons are discussed before and after the chiral restoration. We find that the system has soliton solutions even after the chiral restoration and they are very different from those before the chiral restoration, which indicates that the quarks are still bounded after the chiral restoration.

# Mean-field approximation for the chiral soliton in a chiral phase transition

## Key claim
The authors claim that chiral soliton solutions exist even after chiral symmetry restoration, contrary to prior work by Mao and colleagues (2010) which asserted that solitons melt away after the phase transition. This result is conditional on using the chiral-restored vacuum value as the boundary condition for the sigma field at infinity, rather than the chirally broken vacuum value.

## Method
The authors embed a single chiral soliton in a homogeneous hot and dense quark medium at finite temperature T and chemical potential μ, using the linear sigma model (LSM) in the mean-field approximation with the hedgehog ansatz. They replace the zero-temperature potential U(σ,π) with the thermal effective potential Ω(σ,π;T,μ) derived from finite-temperature field theory, then numerically solve the coupled nonlinear differential equations for the quark fields u(r), v(r) and meson fields σ(r), π(r) with boundary conditions that use the thermal vacuum value σ_v at infinity. The soliton solutions are obtained for temperatures below, at, and above the chiral phase transition temperature T_c, for two cases: μ=0 and μ=300 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV
- Pion mass: m_π = 138 MeV
- Constituent quark mass in vacuum: M_q = 500 MeV (set as input parameter)
- Sigma meson mass: m_σ = 1200 MeV (set as input parameter)
- Coupling constants: g ≈ 5.28, λ ≈ 82.1
- Lowest quark eigen-energy at zero temperature and density: ε = 30.5 MeV
- Phase transition temperature at μ=0: T_c = 187 MeV
- Phase transition temperature at μ=300 MeV: T_c = 114 MeV
- Degeneracy factor for quarks: ν_q = 12 (2 spin × 2 flavor × 3 color)

## Context
This work extends the chiral soliton model to finite temperature and density, building on the zero-temperature formalism of Birse and colleagues (1988). It directly contradicts the findings of Mao and colleagues (2010), who studied chiral solitons in the phase transition but only solved for solutions before chiral restoration and concluded solitons do not exist afterward. The results are consistent with more recent research by Shuryak and colleagues (2004) and lattice studies suggesting that nucleon mass does not decrease to zero after chiral restoration, and with Shu and colleagues (2011) who found a similar bound state of quarks after deconfinement in the Friedberg-Lee soliton model. The work enables further study of the role of chirally-even mass terms in understanding quark binding after chiral restoration.
