# Directed flow in an extended multiphase transport model

> We have studied the rapidity-odd directed flow in $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions in the beam energy range from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7 to 39 GeV within the framework of an extended multiphase transport model with both partonic and hadronic mean-field potentials incorporated. Effects of the partonic scatterings, mean-field potentials, hadronization, and hadronic evolution on the directed flow are investigated, and it is found that the final directed flow is mostly sensitive to the partonic scatterings and the hadronization mechanism. Our study shows that a negative slope of the proton directed flow does not necessarily need the equation of state with a first-order phase transition.

## Key claim
The negative slope of proton directed flow at midrapidity in high-energy heavy-ion collisions can arise from strong partonic scatterings and a momentum-space coalescence hadronization mechanism, without requiring a first-order phase transition or a soft equation of state.

## Method
The authors used an extended multiphase transport (AMPT) model that incorporates partonic mean-field potentials from the 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and hadronic mean-field potentials from relativistic mean-field theory and chiral effective field theory. They simulated midcentral (10-40% centrality) Au+Au collisions at beam energies from sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 39 GeV, tracking the directed flow v_1 through partonic scatterings (with cross sections of 1, 3, and 10 mb), partonic mean-field evolution, hadronization via spatial or dynamical coalescence, and hadronic evolution with the ART model.

## Key numbers
- Beam energies studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV for Au+Au collisions.
- Parton elastic scattering cross sections tested: 1, 3, and 10 mb (constant and isotropic for all parton types).
- Hadronization threshold: central energy density below about 0.8 GeV/fm^3 triggers the coalescence process.
- Hadron formation time: additional 0.7 fm/c used for all hadrons after coalescence.
- Longitudinal coordinate sampling range for initial partons: uniform within (-l m_N / sqrt(s_NN), l m_N / sqrt(s_NN)) with l = 14 fm and m_N = 0.938 GeV.
- Total inelastic cross section for Au+Au: about 705 fm^2, corresponding to impact parameters b = 4.7 - 9.5 fm for 10-40% centrality.
- NJL model parameters: current quark masses m_u = m_d = 3.6 MeV, m_s = 87 MeV; coupling constants G_S Lambda^2 = 3.6, K Lambda^5 = 8.9 with cutoff Lambda = 750 MeV; vector coupling ratio R_V = G_V/G_S tested at 0 and 1.1.

## Context
This work extends the AMPT model framework (Lin et al., 2005) by incorporating mean-field potentials, building on prior studies of elliptic flow splitting (Xu et al., 2014; AMPT_MF). It directly addresses the interpretation of directed flow data from the RHIC beam energy scan program (STAR Collaboration, 2014), where the proton v_1 slope changes sign between 7.7 and 11.5 GeV. The results contradict earlier hydrodynamic and transport model claims (e.g., "antiflow," "third flow," "wiggle" predictions) that a negative v_1 slope necessarily indicates a first-order phase transition, and instead support the findings of Steinheimer et al. (2014) that hadronization mechanisms significantly affect directed flow. The study enables future work to disentangle partonic scattering effects from equation-of-state effects in heavy-ion collisions.
