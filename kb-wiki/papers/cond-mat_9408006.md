# Classical Analysis of Phenomenological Potentials for Metallic Clusters

> The classical trajectories of single particle motion in a Wodds-Saxon and a modified Nilsson potential are studied for axial quadrupole deformation. Both cases give rise to chaotic behaviour when the deformation in the Woods-Saxon and the l**2 term in the modified Nilsson potential are turned on. Important similarities, in particular with regard to the shortest periodic orbits, have been found.

## Key claim
The classical analogues of the Woods-Saxon and modified Nilsson potentials, both used in quantum mechanical models for metallic clusters, show important similarities in their shortest periodic orbits despite appearing dissimilar at first glance; however, both models become chaotic when deformation or the \(\vec l\,^2\)-term is turned on, which may interfere with shell structure for larger clusters.

## Method
The authors numerically solved the canonical equations of motion for single-particle classical trajectories in a deformed Woods-Saxon potential (with quadrupole deformation of the boundary and a deformed diffuseness) and in a modified Nilsson Hamiltonian (with a \(\vec l\,^2\)-term and axial quadrupole deformation). They restricted the analysis to zero z-component of angular momentum and used surfaces of section in the \(z-p_z\) plane to study the onset of chaos and identify short periodic orbits.

## Key numbers
- Woods-Saxon parameters: \(V_0 = -6\) eV, \(d_0 = 0.74\) Å, \(R_0 = 15\) Å, energy \(E = -3\) eV (corresponding to the Fermi level of a cluster with 300 particles).
- For the Woods-Saxon potential, increasing the deformation parameter \(\alpha\) from 0.1 to 0.16 caused stability islands to shrink; at \(\alpha = 0.16\) the islands at the fringes (\(z = \pm 18.7\)) had virtually shrunk to zero.
- For the Nilsson Hamiltonian, the parameter \(b = \omega_\perp / \omega_z\) was investigated in the interval \(1.5 \le b \le 2\); surfaces of section were shown for \(\lambda = -0.01\), energy \(E = 50\), and \(\omega_z = \pi/2\).
- For the Nilsson Hamiltonian with \(\lambda < 0\), the variation of the angular momentum \(p_\theta\) ranged typically between \(-50\) and \(+50\) for generic orbits inside the ellipse for the chosen parameters.

## Context
This work extends the use of nuclear shell model potentials (Woods-Saxon and modified Nilsson) to the classical analysis of metallic clusters, following prior quantum mechanical studies by Clemenger (1985), Nishioka (1990), Frauendorf (1993), and others. It builds on the connection between periodic orbits and quantum shell structure established by Balian and Bloch (1975) and Strutinsky (1976). The results enable a comparison of the two phenomenological potentials in the classical regime and suggest caution when applying them to large deformed clusters, where chaos may obscure shell effects; further investigations are needed to clarify the role of shell structure for prolate deformations, as previously discussed by Heiss (1994).
