# Atiyah-Manton Approach to Skyrmion Matter

> We propose how to approach, and report on the first results in our effort for, describing nuclear matter starting from the solitonic picture of baryons which is supposed to represent QCD for large number of colors. For this purpose, the instanton-skyrmion connection of Atiyah and Manton is exploited to describe skyrmion matter. We first modify 't Hooft's multi-instanton solution so as to suitably incorporate proper dynamical variables into the skyrmion matter and then by taking these variables as variational parameters, we show that they cover a configuration space sufficient to adequately describe the ground state properties of nuclear matter starting from the skyrmion picture. Our results turn out to be comparable to those so far found in different numerical calculations, with our solution reaching stability at high density for a crystal structure and obtaining a comparable value for the energy per baryon at the minimum, thus setting the stage for the next step.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a modified 't Hooft multi-instanton solution, when used within the Atiyah-Manton ansatz, provides a variational framework that can adequately describe the ground state properties of skyrmion matter, with the caveat that the instanton configuration is no longer an exact solution of the self-duality equation after the modifications.

## Method

The authors adopt the Atiyah-Manton ansatz, which constructs skyrmion fields from the holonomy of Yang-Mills instantons, and modify the 't Hooft multi-instanton solution by introducing relative orientation parameters \(C_n\) for each instanton and a spatial truncation function to make the instanton profiles finite-ranged. They then construct periodic instanton crystals with FCC symmetry, treat the instanton parameters (size \(\lambda\), range \(R\), temporal separation \(T\)) as variational parameters, and minimize the energy per baryon as a function of the lattice constant \(L_F\) by numerically solving the differential equations for the Sugawara variables.

## Key numbers

* Minimum energy per baryon: \((E/B)_{min} = 1.044\) (in units of \(6\pi^2 f_\pi/e\)), obtained at lattice constant \(L_{\min}= 4.82\) (in units of \(\hbar c/(e f_\pi)\)).
* For the FCC crystal, the best fit to the energy curve near the minimum is \(E/B = 0.1056L_F + 0.0255 + 2.4581/L_F\).
* Phase transition from single-skyrmion FCC to half-skyrmion CC occurs at around \(L_F\sim 7.5\).
* In the high density phase (\(L_F \le 7.5\)), the variational parameters settle around \(\lambda/L_F\sim 1.1\) and \(R/L_F \sim 1.4\).

## Context

This work extends the Atiyah-Manton approach from few-nucleon systems (Atiyah and Manton 1989, Leese and Manton 1994) to infinite skyrmion matter, providing a variational method that introduces dynamical variables (position, orientation, size) for each skyrmion through the instanton parameters. The results are comparable to the variational crystal calculations of Kugler and Shtrikman (1989), who found \((E/B)_{min} = 1.038\) at \(L_{\min}= 4.72\) for a half-skyrmion CC, and to the instanton-on-a-four-torus calculation of Manton and Schramm (1995) with \((E/B)_{min} = 1.058\) at \(L_{\min}= 4.95\). This framework sets the stage for introducing quantization and thermalization to move from classical skyrmion matter toward a description of nuclear matter.
