# Event-by-event fluctuations in heavy ion collisions

> We discuss the physics underlying event-by-event fluctuations in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We will argue that the fluctuations of the ratio of positively over negatively charged particles may serve as a unique signature for the Quark Gluon Plasma.

## Key claim

Event-by-event fluctuations of the ratio of positively over negatively charged particles may serve as a unique signature for the Quark Gluon Plasma, but the observable \(\tilde{D}\) must be corrected for global charge conservation, and a measured value of \(\tilde{D} \simeq 3\) does not rule out QGP creation due to possible signal-destroying effects such as large rapidity shifts during hadronization.

## Method

The authors derive the relation between charge fluctuations and the observable \(D = \langle N_{\rm ch} \rangle \langle \delta R^2 \rangle\) for the ratio \(R = N_+/N_-\), showing it measures charge fluctuations per unit entropy. They compare analytic calculations for a free pion gas (\(D \approx 4\)), a pion gas with resonances (reduced by about 30%), a free quark-gluon gas (\(D \simeq 3/4\)), and lattice QCD results (\(D \simeq 1-1.5\)). They also compute the electromagnetic current-current correlator \(\Pi^{00}\) to first order in the interaction for pions interacting via \(\rho\)-meson exchange, using a 2-loop diagram calculation.

## Key numbers

- For a free pion gas, \(D_{\rm \pi-gas} \approx 4\).
- For a thermal system of free quarks and gluons, \(D_{\rm QGP} \simeq 3/4\).
- From lattice QCD results, \(D_{\rm Lattice-QCD} \simeq 1 - 1.5\).
- In the URQMD simulation, after charge-conservation corrections, \(\tilde{D} \simeq 3\) for rapidity windows \(\Delta y > 1.5\), and \(\tilde{D} \simeq 4\) for small \(\Delta y\).
- Preliminary experimental results from STAR, CERES, and NA49 find \(\tilde{D} \simeq 4\).
- None of the event generators studied by Gale (2002) gave a value larger than \(\tilde{D} \simeq 3\) for rapidity windows \(\Delta y > 2\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for event-by-event fluctuations introduced by Jeon and Koch (2000) and addresses the charge conservation corrections derived by Bleicher (2000). It builds on earlier studies of resonance effects by Shuryak (1998), Jeon (2000), and Heiselberg and Jackson (2000), and on lattice QCD calculations of charge susceptibilities by Gupta (2001) and the MILC collaboration (2000). The paper enables experimental searches for QGP signatures via charge ratio fluctuations, as pursued by the NA49, STAR, and CERES collaborations, and highlights the need for further investigation into why resonance-induced correlations appear absent in preliminary data.
