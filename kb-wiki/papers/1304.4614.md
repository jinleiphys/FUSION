# Determination of the $Δ(1232)$ axial and pseudoscalar form factors from lattice QCD

> We present a lattice QCD calculation of the $Δ(1232)$ matrix elements of the axial-vector and pseudoscalar currents. The decomposition of these matrix elements into the appropriate Lorentz invariant form factors is carried out and the techniques to calculate the form factors are developed and tested using quenched configurations. Results are obtained for 2+1 domain wall fermions and within a hybrid scheme with domain wall valence and staggered sea quarks. Two Goldberger-Treiman type relations connecting the axial to the pseudoscalar effective couplings are derived. These and further relations based on the pion-pole dominance hypothesis are examined using the lattice QCD results, finding support for their validity. Utilizing lattice QCD results on the axial charges of the nucleon and the $Δ$, as well as the nucleon-to-$Δ$ transition coupling constant, we perform a combined chiral fit to all three quantities and study their pion mass dependence as the chiral limit is approached.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the axial-vector and pseudoscalar form factors of the Δ(1232) resonance, deriving two Goldberger-Treiman type relations that connect the axial to the pseudoscalar effective couplings, and finding support for their validity as well as for pion-pole dominance predictions, though the second GT relation cannot be extrapolated to physical pion mass because the assumption that h₁ and H_{πΔΔ} are slowly varying functions of q² no longer holds.

## Method

The authors computed two-point and three-point Euclidean correlation functions on the lattice using standard techniques, with sequential inversion through the sink to obtain full momentum dependence economically. They formed optimal linear combinations (Type-I and Type-II) of the Dirac and Lorentz indices to extract the four axial form factors (g₁, g₃, h₁, h₃) and two pseudoscalar form factors (g̃, h̃) from overconstrained systems of plateau averages. The calculations were performed on three sets of ensembles: quenched Wilson fermions at β=6.0, a mixed-action scheme with Asqtad staggered sea and domain wall valence fermions, and 2+1 domain wall fermions.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses used: 563(4) MeV, 490(4) MeV, and 411(4) MeV for quenched ensembles; 498(3) MeV and 353(2) MeV for mixed-action ensembles; 297(5) MeV for the domain wall fermion ensemble
- Lattice spacings: a⁻¹ = 2.14(6) GeV for quenched (a=0.092 fm), a⁻¹ = 1.58(3) GeV for mixed-action (a=0.124 fm), a⁻¹ = 2.34(3) GeV for domain wall fermions
- Δ masses: 1.470(15) GeV, 1.425(16) GeV, 1.382(19) GeV for quenched; 1.589(35) GeV and 1.533(27) GeV for mixed-action; 1.455(17) GeV for domain wall fermions
- Momentum transfers studied up to approximately 3 GeV² per ensemble
- Source-sink separation set at ΔT = 12a = 1.1 fm for quenched ensembles
- Statistics: 200 configurations per quenched ensemble, 264 and 550 for mixed-action, 1428 for domain wall fermions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice QCD studies of nucleon axial form factors (Alexandrou 2007) and nucleon-to-Δ axial transition form factors (Alexandrou 2010) to the Δ(1232) itself, completing the calculation of the nucleon/Δ sector on the same ensembles. The results provide first-principles input for chiral perturbation theory calculations that include the Δ as an explicit degree of freedom (Bernard 2005, Hemmert 1997, Jenkins 1991), where the Δ axial charge and πΔΔ coupling constants have previously been treated as free parameters or estimated using large-Nc limits (Dashen 1993) or SU(4) symmetry (Brown 1975). The combined chiral fit to the nucleon axial charge, Δ axial charge, and nucleon-to-Δ transition coupling enables study of their pion mass dependence as the chiral limit is approached.
