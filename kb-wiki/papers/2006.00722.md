# Two-proton momentum correlation from photondisintegration of $α$-clustering light nuclei in the quasi-deuteron region

> The proton-proton momentum correlation function is constructed in three-body photo-disintegration channels from $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O targets in the quasi-deuteron regime within the framework of an extended quantum molecular dynamics model. Using the formula of Lednicky and Lyuboshitz (LL) for the momentum correlation function, we obtain a proton-proton momentum correlation function for the specific three-body photon-disintegration channels of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O targets, which are assumed to have different initial geometric structures, and extract their respective emission source sizes for the proton-proton pair. The results demonstrate that constructing a proton-proton momentum correlation is feasible in photo-nuclear reactions, and it is sensitive to the initial nuclear structure. For future experimental studies investigating the $α$-clustering structures of light nuclei, the present work can be used to shed light on the performance and correlation function analysis of ($γ$,pp) or (e,$e'$pp) reactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that constructing a proton-proton momentum correlation function is feasible in photo-nuclear reactions in the quasi-deuteron regime, and that this correlation is sensitive to the initial α-clustering geometric structure of light nuclei such as 12C and 16O, but only for the specific three-body photo-disintegration channels (γ,pp) with very low branching ratios (0.40%–5.13%).

## Method

The authors used the extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) model to simulate photo-disintegration of 12C and 16O targets with different initial α-clustering configurations (chain, triangle, tetrahedron, square, kite, and spherical Woods-Saxon distributions) at incident photon energies around 100 MeV. They applied the quasi-deuteron absorption mechanism, where a single proton-neutron pair inside an α cluster absorbs the photon, and then used the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz (LL) analytical formula to construct the two-proton momentum correlation function from the phase-space information of emitted protons in the three-body decay channel.

## Key numbers

- For 12C(γ,pp)10Be at Eγ = 100 MeV, the extracted emission source sizes (Rsource) are: chain configuration 1.85 fm, triangle 1.55 fm, sphere 1.25 fm.
- For 16O(γ,pp)14C at Eγ = 100 MeV, the extracted emission source sizes (Rsource) are: chain 2.40 fm, kite 1.75 fm, square 1.60 fm, tetrahedron 1.50 fm, sphere 1.40 fm.
- The two-proton emission branching ratios for 16O configurations at 0.5 million simulation events are: chain 0.40%, kite 0.70%, square 0.85%, tetrahedron 1.30%, sphere 5.13%.
- The two-proton emission branching ratios for 12C configurations at 0.25 million simulation events are: chain 0.45%, triangle 0.75%, sphere 5.05%.
- The RMS radii for 12C configurations are: chain 2.71 fm, triangle 2.35 fm, sphere 2.23 fm (experimental ground state value: 2.4702(22) fm).
- The RMS radii for 16O configurations are: chain 3.782 fm, kite 3.254 fm, square 2.908 fm, tetrahedron 2.761 fm, sphere 2.6 fm (experimental ground state value: 2.6991(52) fm).

## Context

This work extends the application of the Hanbury-Brown and Twiss (HBT) intensity interferometry technique, previously used in heavy-ion collisions (Ma 2015, Fang 2016) and for exotic nuclei (Marques, Wei), to photo-nuclear reactions on α-clustering light nuclei. It builds on the authors' prior studies of 12C and 16O photo-disintegration (Huang 2019a, Huang 2019b) and the quasi-deuteron model of Levinger (1951). The results suggest that the (γ,pp) or (e,e'pp) reaction channels at future photon-beam facilities could serve as a probe for distinguishing different α-clustering configurations in light nuclei.
