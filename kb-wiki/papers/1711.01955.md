# Supernova β⁻ decay of nuclides ⁵³Fe, ⁵⁴Fe, ⁵⁵Fe, and ⁵⁶Fe in strongly screened plasma

## Key claim

The strong electron screening (SES) beta decay rates for iron isotopes ⁵³Fe, ⁵⁴Fe, ⁵⁵Fe, and ⁵⁶Fe in supernova conditions increase by about two orders of magnitude compared to rates without screening, but only when the plasma is sufficiently dense and degenerate; the enhancement is larger for the SES model that includes Q-value correction (model II) than for the model without it (model I), with the difference diminishing at higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors used the linear response theory model (LRTM) combined with the shell model Fermi theory to compute β⁻ decay rates for four iron isotopes in strongly screened plasma. They calculated the screening potential using Jancovici's static longitudinal dielectric function for relativistic degenerate electrons, and incorporated the screening effect into the phase space integral and Q-value of the decay. The Gamow-Teller strength distributions were computed using the Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method, and the authors compared two screening models: model I (without Q-value correction) and model II (with Q-value correction from the screening potential).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of beta decay in supernova environments by Fuller et al. (1982), Aufderheide et al. (1990, 1994), and Langanke et al. (1998, 2003), who neglected the strong electron screening effect. The authors compare their Gamow-Teller strength distributions with those of Nabi (2010), who used the proton-neutron quasi particle RPA model, finding their SMMC-based results are lower. The computed screening-enhanced beta decay rates are relevant for numerical simulations of supernova explosions, stellar thermal evolution, and antineutrino energy loss calculations.
