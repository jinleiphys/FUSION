# Schwinger-Dyson truncations in the all-soft limit: a case study

> We study a special Schwinger-Dyson equation in the context of a pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, formulated in the background field method. Specifically, we consider the corresponding equation for the vertex that governs the interaction of two background gluons with a ghost-antighost pair. By virtue of the background gauge invariance, this vertex satisfies a naive Slavnov-Taylor identity, which is not deformed by the ghost sector of the theory. In the all-soft limit, where all momenta vanish, the form of this vertex may be obtained exactly from the corresponding Ward identity. This special result is subsequently reproduced at the level of the Schwinger-Dyson equation, by making extensive use of Taylor's theorem and exploiting a plethora of key relations, particular to the background field method. This information permits the determination of the error associated with two distinct truncation schemes, where the potential advantage from employing lattice data for the ghost dressing function is quantitatively assessed.

## Key claim

The exact all-soft limit of the four-particle vertex consisting of two background gluons and a ghost-antighost pair (BBcc) can be derived from the Schwinger-Dyson equation that governs it, provided that key gauge cancellations are correctly implemented and the Landau gauge limit is taken after canceling terms proportional to 1/ξ; this exact result then allows quantitative error estimation for truncation schemes where the full ghost-gluon vertex is replaced by its tree-level counterpart.

## Method

The authors studied the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the BBcc vertex in pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory formulated in the Background Field Method (BFM). They first derived the exact all-soft limit of this vertex from the Abelian Slavnov-Taylor identity it satisfies, which becomes a Ward identity when all momenta vanish, expressing the vertex in terms of the ghost-dressing function at the origin. They then reproduced this result from the full Schwinger-Dyson equation by carefully implementing the all-soft limit, exploiting Taylor's theorem and properties of vertices and kernels particular to the BFM, including the cancellation of ξ-dependent terms before taking the Landau gauge limit ξ→0. Finally, they evaluated the error induced by truncating the ghost-gluon vertex to its tree-level value under two different treatments of the ghost dressing function.

## Key numbers

- When the ghost-gluon vertex is approximated by its tree-level value simultaneously at the level of the Schwinger-Dyson equation governing the ghost propagator (coupled equations), the error in the all-soft limit of the BBcc vertex is 47%.
- When the ghost dressing function is instead taken as an external input from lattice data, the error is reduced by a factor of two relative to the coupled-equation case.
- The exact all-soft limit result is T(0) = T_{11}(0,0,0,0) = T_{12}(0,0,0,0) = F^{-1}(0), where F(0) is the ghost dressing function at zero momentum.

## Context

This work extends the program of studying Schwinger-Dyson equations within the Background Field Method, building on prior analyses of BFM vertices and their Slavnov-Taylor identities by Aguilar (2017) and others. The paper provides a controlled toy scenario where the exact result is known from general field-theoretic principles, enabling quantitative error assessment of truncation schemes that are otherwise difficult to evaluate in Schwinger-Dyson studies due to the absence of a definite expansion parameter. This enables future work to benchmark truncation strategies for more complex vertices and to assess the advantage of incorporating lattice data for the ghost dressing function as external input.
