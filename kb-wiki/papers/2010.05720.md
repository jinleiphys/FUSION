# Assessing the impact of valence sd neutrons and protons on fusion

> Experimental above-barrier fusion cross-sections for $^{17}$F + $^{12}$C are compared to the fusion excitation functions for $^{16,18}$O, $^{19}$F, and $^{20}$Ne ions on a carbon target. In comparing the different systems both the differing static size of the incident ions and changes in fusion barrier are accounted for by examining the reduced fusion cross-section. Remaining trends of the fusion cross-section above the barrier which reflect the sensitive interplay of the sd protons and neutrons are observed. The experimental data are also compared to both a widely-used analytical model of near-barrier fusion, as well as a time-dependent Hartree-Fock model. Both models fail to describe the trends observed.

## Key claim

After accounting for differences in static nuclear size and fusion barrier via the reduced cross-section (σ_F/σ_I), the remaining trends in above-barrier fusion for sd-shell nuclei on a carbon target reveal a sensitive interplay: valence sd neutrons enhance fusion beyond the static size increase, while valence sd protons suppress this enhancement, an effect not captured by either a widely-used analytic model or time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations.

## Method

The authors measured fusion cross-sections for a radioactive beam of ¹⁷F on a ¹²C target at near-barrier energies (E_cm ≈ 10–18 MeV) using the RESOLUT spectrometer at Florida State University, simultaneously measuring ¹⁶O + ¹²C as an internal reference. Evaporation residues were identified by energy and time-of-flight in annular silicon detectors, with detection efficiency (~81–85%) determined via statistical model simulations. These data were compared to existing literature data for ¹⁶,¹⁸O, ¹⁹F, and ²⁰Ne on carbon, and to predictions from an analytic fusion model (Sao Paulo potential), TDHF calculations (Sky3D with SV-bas interaction), and coupled-channels calculations (CCFULL).

## Key numbers

- Interaction cross-sections σ_I (at ~900 A MeV) for nuclei on carbon: ¹⁶O = 982±6 mb, ¹⁸O = 1032±26 mb, ¹⁷F = 982±32 mb, ¹⁹F = 1043±24 mb, ¹⁸Ne = 1076±25 mb, ²⁰Ne = 1144±10 mb.
- For ¹⁷F + ¹²C, the beam intensity was 3–7 × 10³ ions/s with purity 37–54%; target thickness was 105 ± 0.5 μg/cm².
- For (E_cm – V_B) ≥ 9 MeV, the average reduced cross-section ⟨σ_F/σ_I⟩ was 1.11±0.04 for ¹⁸O and 1.06±0.07 for ¹⁹F.
- CCFULL barrier parameters: for ¹⁷F, R_B = 7.89 fm, V_B = 9.17 MeV, ħω = 3.36 MeV; for ¹⁹F, R_B = 8.42 fm, V_B = 8.58 MeV, ħω = 2.94 MeV.

## Context

This work extends systematic fusion studies of light sd-shell nuclei (O, F, Ne) on carbon, building on prior measurements by Cujec (1976), Eyal (1976), Kovar (1979), and others. It directly compares the radioactive ¹⁷F beam data to stable-beam data for ¹⁶,¹⁸O, ¹⁹F, and ²⁰Ne, and contrasts with the recent ¹⁷F measurement by Asher (2020), which reported larger cross-sections. The observed suppression of fusion by sd protons relative to sd neutrons motivates further high-quality measurements at radioactive beam facilities like FRIB and GANIL, and calls for more sophisticated theoretical treatments that include pairing and nuclear structure beyond the qualitative interpretation given here.
