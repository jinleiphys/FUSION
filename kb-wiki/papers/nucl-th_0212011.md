# Quasiparticle random phase approximation based on the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model

> The relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) is formulated in the canonical single-nucleon basis of the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model. For the interaction in the particle-hole channel effective Lagrangians with nonlinear meson self-interactions are used, and pairing correlations are described by the pairing part of the finite range Gogny interaction. The RQRPA configuration space includes the Dirac sea of negative energy states. Both in the particle-hole and particle-particle channels, the same interactions are used in the RHB calculation of the ground state and in the matrix equations of the RQRPA. The RHB+RQRPA approach is tested in the example of multipole excitations of neutron rich oxygen isotopes. The RQRPA is applied in the analysis of the evolution of the low-lying isovector dipole strength in Sn isotopes and N=82 isotones.

## Key claim

The relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) is formulated in the canonical single-nucleon basis of the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model, using the same interactions in both the particle-hole and particle-particle channels for the ground state and the RQRPA matrix equations, with the configuration space including the Dirac sea of negative energy states.

## Method

The RQRPA matrix equations were derived from the time-dependent RHB model in the limit of small amplitude oscillations, with the generalized density matrix expanded around the stationary ground state. The equations were solved in the canonical basis, where the RHB wave functions take a BCS-like form, requiring only the matrix elements of the residual particle-hole interaction (from effective Lagrangians with nonlinear meson self-interactions) and the particle-particle pairing interaction (from the finite range Gogny interaction). The approach was tested on multipole excitations of neutron rich oxygen isotopes and applied to analyze low-lying isovector dipole strength in Sn isotopes and N=82 isotones.

## Key numbers

- For the test case of $^{22}$O, the isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole, and isoscalar quadrupole response were calculated and compared with non-relativistic QRPA calculations.
- The RQRPA configuration space includes the Dirac sea of negative energy states, with the Dirac equation solved using a spherical harmonic oscillator basis expansion.
- The pairing interaction uses the finite range Gogny interaction, with the pairing field kernel given by $\Delta_{ab}(\mathbf{r},\mathbf{r}^{\prime}) = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{c,d} V_{abcd}^{pp}(\mathbf{r},\mathbf{r}^{\prime}) \kappa_{cd}(\mathbf{r},\mathbf{r}^{\prime})$.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) framework, previously applied to nuclear compressional modes, giant resonances, and low-lying collective states (Vretenar 2000, Piekarewicz 2001, Ma and Wandelt 2001), by incorporating pairing correlations through the RHB model. It builds on non-relativistic HFB-based QRPA formulations, such as the fully self-consistent QRPA in the HFB canonical basis by Engel (1999) and the continuum QRPA response function in coordinate space by Khan (2002). The approach enables the description of collective excitations in weakly bound neutron-rich nuclei where the Fermi surface is close to the particle continuum, including states with both nucleons in the continuum.
