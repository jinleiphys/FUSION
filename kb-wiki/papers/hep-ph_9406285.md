# Classical Transport Theory and Hard Thermal Loops in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Classical transport theory for colored particles is investigated and employed to derive the hard thermal loops of QCD. A formal construction of phase-space for color degrees of freedom is presented. The gauge invariance of the non-Abelian Vlasov equations is verified and used as a guiding principle in our approximation scheme.We then derive the generating functional of hard thermal loops from a constraint satisfied at leading-order by the color current. This derivation is more direct than alternative ones based on perturbative quantum field theory, and shows that hard thermal effects in hot QCD are essentially {\it classical}. As an illustration, we analyze color polarization in the QCD plasma.

## Key claim

Hard thermal loops (HTLs) of QCD can be derived from classical transport theory for colored particles, showing that hard thermal effects in hot QCD are essentially classical, provided one works at leading order in the coupling constant and preserves the non-Abelian gauge symmetry of the Boltzmann equation by not discarding the non-Abelian contributions as was done in earlier work by Elze and Heinz (1990).

## Method

The authors start from the Wong equations for classical colored particles and the collisionless Boltzmann equation for the one-particle distribution function in extended phase-space (x, p, Q), together with the Yang-Mills equation. They expand the distribution function in powers of the coupling constant g around the equilibrium distribution, keeping only leading-order terms while preserving the non-Abelian gauge invariance of the Vlasov equations. From the resulting constraint on the color current, they integrate over the momentum measure using the massless on-shell condition to obtain an equation for the integrated current density, which is then shown to be equivalent to the gauge-invariance condition for the generating functional of HTLs found by Taylor and Wong (1992).

## Key numbers

- Debye screening mass: \( m_D = gT \sqrt{(N + N_F/2)/3} \), where \( N \) is the number of colors and \( N_F \) the number of quark flavors.
- For \( SU(3) \) with \( N=3 \), the quadratic Casimir constant \( q_2 \) and cubic Casimir constant \( q_3 \) fix the color charge measure; the Jacobian for the transformation to Darboux variables is \( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{48} J_1 J_2 (J_1+J_2) \).
- The imaginary part of the polarization tensor describing Landau damping contains a factor \( m_D^2 \pi \omega/(2|\mathbf{k}|) \) for the 00-component, with a step function \( \theta(|\mathbf{k}|^2 - \omega^2) \) indicating Landau damping occurs only for space-like wave vectors.

## Context

This work extends the classical transport theory for non-Abelian plasmas developed by Elze and Heinz (1990), who had linearized the transport equations and thereby neglected non-Abelian contributions. It provides a more direct derivation of the HTL generating functional than the perturbative quantum field theory approaches of Braaten and Pisarski (1990) and Frenkel and Taylor (1990), or the Schwinger-Dyson truncation method of Blaizot and Iancu (1993). The result enables a classical understanding of hard thermal effects in hot QCD and connects to the Chern-Simons theory description of Efraty and Nair (1992) and the non-Abelian Kubo formula of Jackiw and Nair (1993).
