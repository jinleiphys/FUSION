# Practical method for decomposing discretized breakup cross sections into components of each channel

> Background: In the continuum-discretized coupled-channel method, a breakup cross section (BUX) is obtained as an admixture of several components of different channels in multi-channel scattering. Purpose: Our goal is to propose an approximate way of decomposing the discretized BUX into components of each channel. This approximation is referred to as the "probability separation (P-separation)". Method: As an example, we consider $^{11}$Be scattering by using the three-body model with core excitation ($^{10}\mathrm{Be}+n+\mathrm{T}$, where T is a target). The structural part is constructed by the particle-rotor model and the reaction part is described by the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA). Results: The validity of the P-separation is tested by comparing with the exact calculation. The approximate way reproduces the exact BUXs well regardless of the configurations and/or the resonance positions of $^{11}$Be. Conclusion: The method proposed here can be an alternative approach for decomposing discretized BUXs into components in four- or five-body scattering where the strict decomposition is hard to perform.

## Key claim

The "probability separation" (P-separation) method can approximately decompose discretized breakup cross sections into components of each channel, reproducing exact results well regardless of the configurations and/or resonance positions of the projectile, provided that a threshold-energy condition is imposed to prevent unphysical contributions from closed channels.

## Method

The authors first construct projectile wave functions for 11Be using the particle-rotor model, then calculate breakup cross sections via the extended distorted-wave Born approximation (xDWBA). They discretize the continuum states by diagonalization, obtaining pseudostates that mix core-ground and core-excited components. The P-separation assigns each discretized breakup cross section to a channel component by multiplying it by the probability that the corresponding pseudostate overlaps with that channel's basis states, with the additional condition that below the core-excited breakup threshold all cross section is assigned to the core-ground channel.

## Key numbers

- For 11Be+p scattering at 63.7 MeV/nucleon (set 2), the exact total breakup cross section is 54.8 mb; the P-separation yields core-ground BUX of 44.3 mb (exact: 47.8 mb) and core-excited BUX of 10.5 mb (exact: 7.0 mb)
- For set 6 (S_n=5.0 MeV, epsilon_2=0.5 MeV), the exact core-ground BUX is 4.0 mb and the P-separation gives 3.4 mb; exact core-excited BUX is 3.6 mb and P-separation gives 4.2 mb
- For set 7 (with resonances above the 10Be(2_1+)+n threshold at 0.5 MeV), exact total BUX is 24.2 mb; P-separation gives core-ground 12.4 mb (exact: 13.7 mb) and core-excited 11.8 mb (exact: 10.5 mb)
- For 6Li+208Pb scattering at 39 MeV, the predicted d-alpha BUX is 45.3 mb and the np-alpha BUX is 23.4 mb
- For 6Li+208Pb scattering at 210 MeV, the predicted d-alpha BUX is 89.9 mb and the np-alpha BUX is 47.1 mb

## Context

This work extends the continuum-discretized coupled-channel method (CDCC) framework, specifically addressing the problem of decomposing breakup cross sections in multi-channel scattering where pseudostates mix different channels. The P-separation was previously applied by Watanabe et al. (2015) to separate channel-coupling effects on elastic cross sections in 6Li scattering, but here it is applied after reaction calculations to decompose breakup cross sections themselves. The method provides an alternative to the complex-scaled Lippmann-Schwinger equation approach of Kikuchi et al. (2011), which is numerically demanding and has been limited to Borromean systems. This enables decomposition of breakup cross sections in four- or five-body scattering where exact decomposition is impractical.
