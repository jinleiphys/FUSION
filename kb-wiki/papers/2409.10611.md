# Neutron Tagging Can Greatly Reduce Spallation Backgrounds in Super-Kamiokande

> Super-Kamiokande's spallation backgrounds - the delayed beta decays of nuclides following cosmic-ray muons - are nearly all produced by the small fraction of muons with hadronic showers. We show that these hadronic showers also produce neutrons; their captures can be detected with high efficiency due to the recent addition of dissolved gadolinium to Super-Kamiokande. We show that new cuts based on the neutron tagging of showers could reduce spallation backgrounds by a factor of at least four beyond present cuts. With further work, this could lead to a near-elimination of detector backgrounds above about 6 MeV, which would significantly improve the sensitivity of Super-Kamiokande. These findings heighten the importance of adding gadolinium to Hyper-Kamiokande, which is at a shallower depth. Further, a similar approach could be used in other detectors, for example, the JUNO liquid-scintillator detector, which is also at a shallower depth.

## Key claim

The paper claims that neutron tagging of hadronic showers in Super-Kamiokande with dissolved gadolinium can reduce spallation backgrounds by a factor of at least four beyond present cuts, with the potential for near-elimination of detector backgrounds above about 6 MeV. This result depends on the condition that the majority of isotope-producing showers also produce neutrons that can be detected with high efficiency using the gadolinium-enhanced neutron capture signal.

## Method

The authors used the FLUKA Monte Carlo simulation package (version 4-4.0) to model muon propagation through Super-Kamiokande's fiducial volume, calculating both isotope yields and neutron yields for vertical throughgoing muons. They simulated neutron production in hadronic and electromagnetic showers induced by muons, assuming Super-K's present gadolinium concentration of 0.033% by mass, and compared the neutron yields to the production rates of spallation isotopes that cause detector backgrounds in the 5-20 MeV energy range.

## Key numbers

- The cosmic-ray muon rate at Super-K is approximately 2 s$^{-1}$, with an average muon energy of 271 GeV
- The spallation decay rate is approximately 3000 day$^{-1}$ above 3.5 MeV, while the solar neutrino-electron scattering signal rate is approximately 20 day$^{-1}$ above 3.5 MeV
- Present spallation cuts remove approximately 90% of spallation backgrounds at a cost of approximately 10% detector deadtime (signal loss)
- At the present gadolinium concentration of 0.033% by mass, 75% of neutrons capture on gadolinium with a capture time of approximately 60 $\mu$s
- The accidental coincidence rate for neutron captures with muons is approximately 1 day$^{-1}$ for a separation of approximately 100 $\mu$s
- The average muon track length inside the fiducial volume is approximately 24 m, with the longest diagonal path being approximately 44 m

## Context

This work extends the theoretical studies of spallation in Super-K by Li and Beacom (2014-2015), who showed that nearly all relevant isotopes are produced by muon secondaries in hadronic showers and that neutrons are often produced in association with spallation isotopes. The paper builds on Super-K's demonstrated improved spallation cut based on localizing showers through neutron clouds in pure water (Super-Kamiokande Collaboration, 2021, 2023), and generalizes approaches used by Borexino (2021) and KamLAND-Zen (2023) that incorporate neutron multiplicity to tag spallation events. The findings enable new cuts for Super-K with gadolinium and heighten the importance of adding gadolinium to Hyper-Kamiokande, which will be at a shallower depth with approximately five times higher muon flux.
