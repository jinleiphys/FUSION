# $f$-mode oscillations in hot Neutron Stars: Effect of hyperons and neutrino trapping

> In this work, we present an equation of state formalism for hot Neutron Stars (NSs) which consistently includes the effects of finite temperature, hyperons as well as neutrino trapping, relevant for the study of proto-neutron stars, binary neutron star mergers and supernova explosions. Within a non-linear relativistic mean field description, the framework allows for a systematic variation of nuclear parameters within the range allowed by uncertainties in nuclear experimental data, ensuring compatibility with nuclear theory, astrophysical and heavy-ion data. We then investigate the role of nuclear and hypernuclear parameters as well as thermal effects on NS macroscopic properties and $f$-mode oscillations in hot neutron stars within Cowling approximation. Our results reveal that in hyperonic neutron stars with trapped neutrinos, the saturation nuclear density shows moderate to strong correlation with NS astrophysical observables. We also investigate whether thermal effects break universal relations and provide fit relations for hot NS configurations in the neutrino-trapped regime.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in hyperonic neutron stars with trapped neutrinos, the saturation nuclear density shows moderate to strong correlation with astrophysical observables, and that thermal effects do not break universal relations for hot neutron star configurations in the neutrino-trapped regime, for which the authors provide new fit relations.

## Method

The authors employ a non-linear relativistic mean field (NL-RMF) model to describe baryon-baryon interactions via exchange of $\sigma$, $\omega$, $\rho$, and $\phi$ mesons, including the full baryon octet (n, p, $\Lambda$, $\Sigma^-$, $\Sigma^0$, $\Sigma^+$, $\Xi^-$, $\Xi^0$). They compute finite-temperature equations of state for both neutrino-free and neutrino-trapped matter, then solve for $f$-mode oscillations within the relativistic Cowling approximation for spherically symmetric hot neutron star configurations. A Bayesian filtering scheme is applied to vary nuclear and hypernuclear parameters within uncertainty ranges constrained by chiral effective field theory at low densities, heavy-ion collision data at intermediate densities, and astrophysical observations (including PSR J0740-6620 and GW170817) at high densities.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass constraint: PSR J0740-6620 has a gravitational mass of $2.08^{+0.07}_{-0.07} M_{\odot}$, used as a lower bound on the TOV limit
- Temperature range considered: up to 100 MeV, relevant for proto-neutron stars and binary neutron star mergers
- Density range considered: 0.5 to 6 times nuclear saturation density ($n_{sat}$)
- Neutrino trapping becomes relevant for temperatures above approximately 5 MeV, based on neutrino mean free path considerations
- Two thermal configurations studied in the neutrino-trapped regime: NY(1, 0.4) (entropy per baryon S/A=1, total lepton fraction Y_L=0.4) and NY(2, 0.2) (S/A=2, Y_L=0.2)
- The $\Lambda$-hyperon potential is fixed to -30 MeV based on experimental constraints

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Bayesian analysis of hot nucleonic matter (Barman, Pradhan, and Chatterjee, 2025) to include hyperonic degrees of freedom and neutrino trapping, which were neglected in that earlier study. It builds on a series of publications (Ghosh, Chatterjee, and collaborators, 2022-2023) that developed the NL-RMF formalism for cold neutron star equations of state with multi-physics constraints. The paper addresses the need for systematically variable finite-temperature equations of state with non-nucleonic degrees of freedom, going beyond the limited number of fixed-parameter general purpose EOSs available in the CompOSE database (such as LS220 by Lattimer and Swesty, 1991; STOS by Shen, Toki, and collaborators, 1998). The results enable more realistic modeling of proto-neutron star evolution and binary neutron star merger simulations, where the simple $\Gamma$-law approximation for thermal contributions has been shown inadequate for matter containing hyperons (Raduta, 2022; Blacker, Kochankovski, and collaborators, 2024).
