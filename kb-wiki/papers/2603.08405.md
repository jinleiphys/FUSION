# Connecting baryon light-front wave functions to quasi-transverse-momentum-dependent correlators in lattice QCD

> Within light-front quantization, hadrons can be represented on a Fock-space basis of configurations of elementary partons. The coefficients of the expansion are called light-front wave functions (LFWFs), and encode all the dynamical degrees of freedom. We show how to extract the LFWFs of baryons, such as the proton, from equal-time correlators suitable for Lattice QCD simulations. Using an operator product expansion, we prove the factorization of the relevant correlator in the three-quark color-singlet LFWF, a residual lattice factor, and a soft factor that systematically subtracts the additional divergences arising from the factorization. We verify up to next-to-leading order the independent renormalizability of the LFWF, and we derive the evolution equations that govern its scale dependence.

## Key claim
The authors show that the leading three-quark color-singlet light-front wave function (LFWF) of a baryon such as the proton can be extracted from equal-time quasi-transverse-momentum-dependent (QTMD) correlators suitable for lattice QCD simulations, provided that additional ultraviolet and rapidity divergences arising from the factorization are systematically canceled by an appropriate soft factor, and that the resulting LFWF is independently renormalizable quark leg by quark leg.

## Method
The authors construct a QTMD correlator defined on a Euclidean lattice, using semicompact quark operators connected to the lattice boundary via Wilson lines along the v-direction. They apply a TMD operator expansion based on the background field method with two background fields (nbar-collinear and v-collinear), factorizing the correlator at leading power into the three-quark LFWF, a residual lattice factor, and a soft factor. They verify the factorization and cancellation of divergences up to next-to-leading order (NLO) using dimensional regularization for UV/IR divergences and delta-regularization for rapidity divergences, and derive the independent renormalization-group evolution equations for the LFWF.

## Key numbers
- The smallness parameter lambda = M / P^+ << 1, where M is the baryon mass and P^+ is its large light-cone momentum component.
- The transverse field separations b are of order 1/M = (lambda P^+)^(-1), while longitudinal separations l are of order 1/P^+.
- The NLO coefficient function C_{1,NLO}(x) contains a factor Gamma(-epsilon) Gamma(2epsilon) with epsilon = (4-D)/2 in dimensional regularization.
- The color factor for the interaction between quark 1 and the Wilson line of quark 2 is -C_F/(N-1), where C_F is the Casimir operator for the fundamental representation.

## Context
This work extends established factorization frameworks for transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) parton distributions and fragmentation functions (Vladimirov, 2020; Rodini, 2022) and for meson LFWFs (Ji, 2021; Deng, 2022; LatticeParton, 2023) to the richer color structure of baryons, most notably the proton. The approach enables first-principle lattice QCD calculations of baryon LFWFs, which would allow direct comparisons with phenomenological models and provide a unified description of the proton's internal dynamics. Future work includes studying higher Fock components, verifying the factorization beyond NLO, and performing explicit lattice simulations with careful treatment of systematic effects such as finite-volume corrections and discretization errors.
