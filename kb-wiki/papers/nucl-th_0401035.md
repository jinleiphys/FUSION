# Quark-mass dependence of baryon resonances

> We study the quark-mass dependence of J^P = \frac12^- s-wave and J^P = \frac32^- d-wave baryon resonances. Parameter-free results are obtained in terms of the leading order chiral Lagrangian. In the 'heavy' SU(3) limit with m_π=m_K \simeq 500 MeV the s-wave resonances turn into bound states forming two octets plus a singlet representations of the SU(3) group. Similarly the d-wave resonances turn into bound states forming an octet and a decuplet in this limit. A contrasted result is obtained in the 'light' SU(3) limit with m_π=m_K \simeq 140 MeV for which no resonances exist.

## Key claim

The quark-mass dependence of s-wave and d-wave baryon resonances is studied using only the leading-order chiral Lagrangian, yielding parameter-free predictions; however, the existence of resonances depends critically on the meson masses, with bound states forming only in the 'heavy' SU(3) limit (m_π=m_K ≈ 500 MeV) and no resonances appearing in the 'light' SU(3) limit (m_π=m_K ≈ 140 MeV).

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation using the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction from the leading-order chiral SU(3) Lagrangian, with a renormalization condition that fixes subtraction points at natural baryon masses to preserve chiral power counting. The scattering amplitudes are decomposed into projectors with definite total angular momentum J and parity P, and poles are searched for on the second Riemann sheet to determine resonance masses and widths. The quark-mass dependence is explored by varying the pion mass via a parameter x that interpolates between the physical pion mass and the kaon mass, while keeping the kaon mass fixed.

## Key numbers

- In the 'heavy' SU(3) limit with m_π=m_K ≈ 500 MeV, s-wave resonances become bound states forming two octets plus a singlet representation of SU(3); d-wave resonances become bound states forming an octet and a decuplet.
- In the 'light' SU(3) limit with m_π=m_K ≈ 140 MeV, no resonances exist.
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is Λ_χ ≈ 4πf ≈ 1.13 GeV, with f ≈ 90 MeV.
- For the s-wave sector, the leading-order calculation predicts the N(1535), Λ(1405), and Λ(1670) resonances with masses and widths close to empirical values.
- A pole in the (I,S)=(1,-1) sector appears with M_R=1466 MeV and Γ_R=574 MeV in the 11100 sheet, identified with the Σ(1750) resonance after chiral corrections.
- In the d-wave sector, the approach predicts the four-star hyperon resonances Ξ(1820), Λ(1520), and Σ(1670) with masses close to empirical values, and the nucleon and isobar resonances N(1520) and Δ(1700) with less accuracy.
- A bound state in the (0,-3) sector appears at mass 1950 MeV, associated with a decuplet resonance not yet observed.
- Decuplet states appear in the (1/2,-2) and (1,-1) sectors at masses 2100 MeV and 1920 MeV, the latter identified with the three-star Ξ(1940) resonance.

## Context

This work extends the χ-BS(3) approach developed by Lutz and Kolomeitsev (2001, 2002) and Lutz, Wolf, and Friman (2002), which demonstrated that chiral coupled-channel dynamics can dynamically generate baryon resonances without adjustable parameters. The results contrast with phenomenological approaches such as those of Oset and collaborators (2001, 2002) and Jido et al. (2003), which use subtraction scales as free parameters that the authors argue may violate chiral power counting. The predicted quark-mass dependence provides a clear test for unquenched QCD lattice simulations, and the framework enables systematic inclusion of chiral correction terms to improve quantitative agreement with experimental resonance spectra.
