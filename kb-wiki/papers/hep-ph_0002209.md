# Meson Masses in High Density QCD

> The low-energy effective theories for the two- and three-flavor color-superconductors arising in the high density limit of QCD are discussed. Using an effective field theory to describe quarks near the fermi surface, we compute the masses of the pseudo-Goldstone bosons that dominate the low-momentum dynamics of these systems.

## Key claim

The masses of the pseudo-Goldstone bosons in high-density QCD vanish in the large-chemical-potential limit, proportional to \(\sim \Delta^2/\mu^2 \log(\Delta/\mu)\), contradicting earlier claims that they become independent of \(\mu\); the authors compute the leading contribution to these masses and decay constants using a hierarchy of effective field theories.

## Method

The authors construct a 1+1 dimensional effective field theory for quarks near the Fermi surface by projecting onto positive-energy states and integrating out anti-quarks at the scale \(\mu\). They then compute the Debye and Meissner masses of fictitious gauge bosons in both perturbative QCD and the low-energy effective theory to determine the decay constants \(f_8\) and \(f_1\), and match the shift in ground-state energy due to quark masses between the two theories to extract the coefficients \(A_i\) that determine the meson masses.

## Key numbers

- Decay constant for the octet pseudo-Goldstone bosons: \(f_8^2 = \frac{\mu^2}{\pi^2} \frac{21 - 8\log[2]}{9}\)
- Decay constant for the singlet pseudo-Goldstone boson: \(f_1 = \frac{9}{4} \frac{\mu^2}{\pi^2}\)
- Velocity of all Goldstone modes at leading order: \(|\mathbf{v}_1|^2 = |\mathbf{v}_8|^2 = \frac{1}{3}\)
- Coefficient \(A_1 = -A_2 = -\frac{\overline{\Delta}\Delta}{4\pi^2} \log(\frac{\Delta}{\mu})\) and \(A_3 = 0\) for the three-flavor case
- For the two-flavor case: \(f_{\eta'}^2 = \frac{\mu^2}{\pi^2}\), \(|\mathbf{v}|^2 = \frac{1}{3}\), and \(B = -\frac{\overline{\Delta}\Delta}{2\pi^2} \log(\frac{\Delta}{\mu})\)
- Two-flavor \(\eta'\) mass: \(m_{\eta'}^2 = -\frac{4\overline{\Delta}\Delta}{\mu^2} \log(\frac{\Delta}{\mu}) m_u m_d\)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Son and Stephanov (1999) and Casalbuoni and Gatto (1999), who computed pseudo-Goldstone masses and decay constants in the \(N_f=3\) color-flavor locked scenario but found masses independent of \(\mu\). The authors agree with later claims by Hong (1999), Pisarski and Rischke (1999), and Manuel and Tytgat (1999) that the masses actually vanish in the large-\(\mu\) limit, and provide a systematic effective field theory derivation. The results enable computation of the far-infrared dynamics of color-superconducting quark matter, relevant for neutron star physics, and provide a framework for including subleading corrections from \(1/\mu\) and \(\alpha_s\) expansions.
