# Nonlinear susceptibilities under the framework of Dyson-Schwinger equations

> Since the baryon-number susceptibilities are correlated with the cumulant of baryon-number fluctuations in experiments, we do calculations of the susceptibilities and compare them with the experimental fluctuation data under the framework of the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) approach. We compare our results with lattice QCD and experimental data at RHIC. The fitness of the results indicates that under the framework of DSEs, we can deal with the problems of heavy ion collisions properly.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) approach, using a rank-1 separable model for the gluon propagator, can reproduce the experimental baryon-number fluctuation ratios (skewness times sigma, kurtosis times sigma-squared, and kurtosis times sigma over skewness) from RHIC, but only if the freeze-out temperature is shifted downward by about 20 MeV relative to the standard parametrization used for lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors compute the second, third, and fourth order baryon-number susceptibilities from the dressed quark propagator obtained by solving the rainbow DSEs with a rank-1 separable gluon model at finite temperature and chemical potential. They then form the experimental observables Sσ, κσ², and κσ/S from these susceptibilities and compare them to lattice QCD results and STAR data for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 19.6, 62.4, and 200 GeV. To explore the temperature dependence, they fix the quark chemical potential at the values corresponding to those collision energies and vary the temperature from 100 to 160 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The rank-1 separable model parameters: Λ = 0.678 GeV, D₀Λ² = 128.0, light quark mass m = 6.6 MeV.
- Freeze-out parameters from the standard parametrization (Eq. 24): at √s_NN = 19.6 GeV, T = 159 MeV, μ_B = 229 MeV, μ = 77 MeV; at 62.4 GeV, T = 165 MeV, μ_B = 82 MeV, μ = 28 MeV; at 200 GeV, T = 166 MeV, μ_B = 27 MeV, μ = 9 MeV.
- The DSEs results fit the experimental data completely when the temperature is in the range from 138 to 145 MeV, approximately 20 MeV lower than the standard freeze-out temperatures.
- The critical end point (CEP) from the same rank-1 separable model has μ_CEP = 164 MeV and T_CEP = 117 MeV, compared to experimental estimates of μ_CEP ~ 150–180 MeV and T_CEP ~ 165–170 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the DSEs approach to nonlinear (third and fourth order) baryon-number susceptibilities, building on the earlier result that the quark-number density is determined solely by the dressed quark propagator (Zhao et al., 2014). The authors compare their results to lattice QCD calculations with two flavors of quark and a cutoff of 1/a ≈ 960–1000 MeV, and to STAR experimental data. The finding that the freeze-out temperature must be reduced by about 20 MeV to achieve agreement is consistent with the earlier observation from the same model (Zhao et al., 2014) that the pseudo-critical temperature T_c = 150 MeV is smaller than the lattice-based value of 175 MeV. The authors note that the rank-1 separable model is a strong approximation and that ongoing work using more sophisticated gluon models (such as those in Refs. [c3,c2]) may yield more reliable conclusions.
