# Phase transition from nuclear matter to color superconducting quark matter

> We construct the nuclear and quark matter equations of state at zero temperature in an effective quark theory (the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model), and discuss the phase transition between them. The nuclear matter equation of state is based on the quark-diquark description of the single nucleon, while the quark matter equation of state includes the effects of scalar diquark condensation (color superconductivity). The effect of diquark condensation on the phase transition is discussed in detail.

## Key claim
The paper constructs a unified description of nuclear matter and color superconducting quark matter within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, finding that diquark condensation softens the quark matter equation of state and lowers the transition density, but the authors note that the parameters determined from single nucleon and nuclear matter properties (infrared cut-off, vector interaction strength, diquark coupling strength) may not directly carry over to the quark matter phase.

## Method
The authors use the NJL model with proper time regularization to construct equations of state at zero temperature for both nuclear matter and quark matter. Nuclear matter is described via a quark-diquark description of the nucleon in the static approximation to the Faddeev equation, while quark matter includes scalar diquark condensation (color superconductivity) in the Hartree approximation. The effective potential is derived using path integral methods in the Nambu-Gorkov formalism, and the phase transition between the two phases is studied by comparing their equations of state.

## Key numbers
- The nucleon mass at zero density is determined from the pole of the quark-diquark t-matrix, with the positive energy spectrum given by epsilon_N(p) = E_N(p) + 3*omega^0, where E_N(p) = sqrt(p^2 + M_N^2)
- The quark propagator in the superconducting phase has 4 poles at p_0 = +/- sqrt((E_q(p) +/- mu_q*)^2 + Delta^2) with degeneracy 8, and 4 poles at p_0 = +/- |E_q(p) +/- mu_q*| with degeneracy 4, where Delta = sqrt(3/2)*hat_Delta
- The effective potential for nuclear matter includes a nucleon Fermi motion contribution: V_N = -4 * integral d^3p/(2pi)^3 * Theta(mu* - E_N(p)) * (mu* - E_N(p))
- The effective potential for quark matter includes a quark Fermi motion contribution: V_Q = -12 * integral d^3p/(2pi)^3 * Theta(mu_q* - E_q(p)) * (mu_q* - E_q(p))

## Context
This work extends the approach of Bentz and Thomas (2001) who showed that the quark-diquark description of the nucleon could be extended to describe nuclear matter, and combines it with the color superconducting quark matter description from the NJL model developed by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and others. The paper enables future studies of the phase transition in neutron stars and heavy ion collisions, and raises the open question of whether parameters like the infrared cut-off and coupling strengths determined from nuclear properties should be applied to the quark matter phase.
