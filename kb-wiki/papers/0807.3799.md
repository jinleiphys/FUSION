# A New Glauber Theory based on Multiple Scattering Theory

> Glauber theory for nucleus-nucleus scattering at high incident energies is reformulated so as to become applicable also for the scattering at intermediate energies. We test validity of the eikonal and adiabatic approximations used in the formulation, and discuss the relation between the present theory and the conventional Glauber calculations with either the empirical nucleon-nucleon profile function or the modified one including the in-medium effect.

## Key claim

The authors reformulate Glauber theory for nucleus-nucleus scattering by replacing the bare nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential with an effective NN interaction \(\tau\) derived from multiple scattering theory, thereby extending applicability to intermediate energies (below 500 MeV/nucleon) where the standard eikonal approximation for NN scattering fails.

## Method

The authors extend the Kerman-McManus-Thaler (KMT) multiple scattering formalism to nucleus-nucleus scattering, expressing the transition matrix in terms of an effective NN interaction \(\tau\) that satisfies the eikonal condition better than the bare NN potential. They then apply the Glauber approximation (eikonal and adiabatic approximations) to the resulting Schrödinger equation with \(\tau\) replacing the bare potential, obtaining a profile function \(\Gamma_{\rm NN}^{({\rm eff})}\) based on the \(z\)-integrated trivially equivalent local (TEL) potential derived from \(\tau\). The validity of the eikonal approximation is tested by comparing Glauber and exact adiabatic calculations for a single NN collision in \(^4\)He+\(^{208}\)Pb scattering.

## Key numbers

- At \(E_{\rm NN}=300\) MeV, the \(z\)-integrated bare potential \(\tilde{v}=1587\ {\rm MeV}\cdot{\rm fm}\) at \(b_{ij}=0\), while the TEL potential \(\tilde{\tau}^{\rm loc}=278-86i\ {\rm MeV}\cdot{\rm fm}\) at the same point.
- At \(E_{\rm NN}=800\) MeV, the TEL potential \(\tilde{\tau}^{\rm loc}=267-212i\ {\rm MeV}\cdot{\rm fm}\) at \(b_{ij}=0\).
- The eikonal approximation test for a single NN collision in \(^4\)He+\(^{208}\)Pb scattering at \(E_{\rm PA}=300 N_{\rm P}\) MeV shows excellent agreement between Glauber and exact adiabatic amplitudes.
- The adiabatic approximation is tested for \(^{11}\)Be+\(p\) and \(^{40}\)Ca+\(p\) at 300 MeV per nucleon, using a Woods-Saxon potential with parameters listed (e.g., \(V_0=51.60\) MeV, \(R_0=2.996\) fm, \(a_0=0.52\) fm for \(^{10}\)Be-\(n\) with separation energy \(S_n=0.503\) MeV).

## Context

This work extends the Glauber theory (Glauber, 1959) beyond its original high-energy domain by addressing failures reported at energies below 500 MeV/nucleon (Xiang; Horiuchi, 2006). It builds on the multiple scattering theory of Watson (1953) and the KMT formalism (Kerman, McManus, and Thaler, 1959), and relates to prior attempts using empirical NN profile functions (Glauber and Matthiae, 1970) or Brueckner \(g\)-matrix modifications (Xiang). The reformulation enables accurate treatment of intermediate-energy nucleus-nucleus scattering, including reactions with unstable nuclei, and provides a foundation for systematic non-eikonal corrections via the effective interaction \(\tau\).
