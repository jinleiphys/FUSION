# Chiral Symmetry and Charmonium Decays to Two Pseudoscalars

> We apply hard pion Chiral Perturbation Theory to charmonium decays to $ππ$, $KK$ and $ηη$. We first discuss why we expect to be able to provide results for the chiral logarithms in $χ_{c0}$ and $χ_{c2}$ decays to two pseudoscalars while for the decays from $J/ψ$, $ψ(nS)$ and $χ_{c1}$ no simple prediction is possible. The leading chiral logarithm turns out to be absent for $χ_{c0},χ_{c2}\to PP$. This result is true for all fully chiral singlet states of spin zero and two.

## Key claim

The leading chiral logarithm (of order \(m^2\log m^2\)) is absent for \(\chi_{c0},\chi_{c2}\to PP\) decays, and this result holds for all fully chiral singlet states of spin zero and two; however, no simple prediction is possible for decays from \(J/\psi\), \(\psi(nS)\), or \(\chi_{c1}\).

## Method

The authors applied hard pion Chiral Perturbation Theory (HPChPT) to charmonium decays into two light pseudoscalar mesons (\(\pi\pi\), \(KK\), \(\eta\eta\)). They constructed the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian for the \(\chi_{c0}\) (scalar) and \(\chi_{c2}\) (tensor) fields as chiral singlets, then computed loop diagrams and wavefunction renormalization contributions to isolate terms of order \(m^2\log m^2\), showing that the loop and wavefunction renormalization contributions exactly cancel.

## Key numbers

* \(\chi_{c0}\) mass: \(3414.75\pm0.31\) MeV; width: \(10.4\pm0.6\) MeV
* \(\chi_{c2}\) mass: \(3556.20\pm0.09\) MeV; width: \(1.97\pm0.11\) MeV
* Branching fraction for \(\chi_{c0}\to\pi\pi\): \((8.5\pm0.4)\times10^{-3}\)
* Branching fraction for \(\chi_{c2}\to\pi\pi\): \((2.42\pm0.13)\times10^{-3}\)
* Branching fraction for \(\chi_{c0}\to K^+K^-\): \((6.06\pm0.35)\times10^{-3}\)
* Branching fraction for \(\chi_{c2}\to K^+K^-\): \((1.09\pm0.08)\times10^{-3}\)
* Branching fraction for \(\chi_{c0}\to\eta\eta\): \((3.03\pm0.21)\times10^{-3}\)
* Branching fraction for \(\chi_{c2}\to\eta\eta\): \((0.59\pm0.05)\times10^{-3}\)
* Normalized amplitude \(G_0\) for \(\chi_{c0}\to\pi\pi\): \((3.15\pm0.07)\times10^{10}\) MeV\(^{-5/2}\)
* Normalized amplitude \(G_2\) for \(\chi_{c2}\to\pi\pi\): \((3.04\pm0.08)\times10^{10}\) MeV\(^{-5/2}\)

## Context

This work extends the hard pion ChPT method previously applied to \(K_{\ell3}\) decays by Fearing and Scherer (1996), \(K\to\pi\pi\) by Bijnens and Celis (2009), and \(B\to D,\pi,K,\eta\) vector formfactors by Bijnens and Jemos (2010, 2011). The vanishing of chiral logarithms for \(\chi_{c0},\chi_{c2}\to PP\) implies that \(SU(3)_V\) breaking effects in these decays are expected to be smaller than the typical 20% seen in quantities like \(F_K/F_\pi\) or \(m_\Lambda/m_p\), and the experimental data show corrections of about 10% for both \(\chi_{c0}\) and \(\chi_{c2}\) decays to pions and kaons. The result also predicts that the energy-momentum tensor matrix elements at large \(q^2\) should have no \(m^2\log m^2\) terms, consistent with the one-loop ChPT calculation by Donoghue and Leutwyler (1991).
