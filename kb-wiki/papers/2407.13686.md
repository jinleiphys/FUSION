# Josephson currents in neutron stars

> We demonstrate that the interface between $S$-wave and $P$-wave paired superfluids in neutron stars induces a neutron supercurrent, a charge-neutral analog of the Josephson junction effect in electronic superconductors. The proton supercurrent entrainment by the neutron superfluid generates, in addition to the neutral supercurrent, a charged current across the interface. Beyond this stationary effect, the motion of the neutron vortex lines responding to secular changes in the neutron star's rotation rate induces a time-dependent oscillating Josephson current across this interface when proton flux tubes are dragged along with them. We show that such motion produces radiation from the interface once clusters of proton flux tubes intersect the interface. The power of radiation exceeds by orders of magnitude the Ohmic dissipation of currents in neutron stars. This effect appears to be phenomenologically significant enough to heat the star and alter its cooling rate during the photon cooling era.

## Key claim

The interface between S-wave and P-wave paired superfluids in neutron stars acts as a neutral analog of a Josephson junction, generating both stationary and time-dependent supercurrents; the time-dependent oscillating Josephson current from proton flux tubes crossing this interface produces electromagnetic radiation that exceeds Ohmic dissipation by orders of magnitude and may alter neutron star cooling during the photon cooling era, but this mechanism operates only on secular timescales and assumes that proton flux tubes on average move together with neutron vortex lines.

## Method

The authors developed a Ginzburg-Landau model for coupled neutron superfluid and proton superconductor condensates, including entrainment coupling between the two fluids, and derived boundary conditions at the S-wave/P-wave interface that account for tunneling of Cooper pairs. They computed stationary Josephson currents from phase differences across the interface, then extended the analysis to time-dependent currents arising from the secular motion of neutron vortex lines and proton flux tubes responding to the star's spin-down, estimating the electromagnetic dipole radiation power from these oscillating currents.

## Key numbers

- The number density ratio of proton flux tubes to neutron vortices is approximately n_Phi/n_V ~ 10^13, where n_Phi = B_bar/Phi_0 and n_V = 2Omega/kappa with quantum circulation kappa = pi hbar/m_n*
- The total radiated power from neutron vortex lines crossing the S-P interface is estimated as 3 x 10^7 erg/s times tau_5^{-2} R_6^5 Omega_{100}^{3/2}, where tau_5 is spin-down lifetime in units of 10^5 yr, R_6 is stellar radius in units of 10^6 cm, and Omega_{100} is rotation frequency in units of 100 s^{-1}
- The total radiated power from proton flux tubes crossing the S-P interface is estimated as 2 x 10^28 erg/s times tau_5^{-2} R_6^5 B_{13}^{3/2}, where B_{13} is magnetic field in units of 10^13 G
- The Ohmic dissipation heating power in the crust is approximately 10^27 erg/s times (B_cr/10^13 G)^2 (10^23 s^{-1}/sigma) (delta R/0.5 km), where sigma is crustal electric conductivity

## Context

This work extends the concept of Josephson junctions from electronic superconductors (Landau 1980, Ketterson 2016) to neutral superfluid systems in neutron stars, building on prior studies of S-wave and P-wave neutron pairing (Sedrakian 2019, Chamel 2024) and the entrainment effect between neutron and proton condensates (Sedrakian 1980, Alpar 1984, Allard 2021). The proposed heating mechanism could impact late-time neutron star cooling simulations that incorporate secular spin-down dynamics and magnetic field evolution, and may have analogs in ultracold atomic gas mixtures where Josephson junctions have been recently realized (Pezze 2024).
