# Quasi Free 238U (e,e'f)-Cross Section in Macroscopic-Microscopic Approach

> We present the result of a theoretical study of inclusive quasi free electrofission of $^{238}$U. The off-shell $ $cross sections for the quasi free reaction stage have been calculated within the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA), using a Macroscopic -Microscopic description of the proton and neutron single particle momentum distributions. Electron wave function distortion corrections were included using the effective momentum approximation, and the Final State Interaction (FSI) effects were calculated using an optical potential. The fissility for the proton single hole excited states of the residual nucleus $^{237}$Pa was calculated both without and with contributions of the pre-equilibrium emission of the particles. The fissility for $^{237,238}U$ residual nuclei was calculated within the compound nucleus model. The $(e,e^{\prime}f)-$cross sections thus obtained were compared with available experimental data.

## Key claim

The macroscopic-microscopic approach, combined with the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) and mean-field distortion corrections, provides an adequate description of inclusive quasi-free electrofission of 238U at GeV energies, but only when final-state interaction (FSI) corrections to fissility are included; calculations without and with preequilibrium emission gave close results, showing that inclusive cross sections are not sensitive to the difference in reaction mechanisms.

## Method

The authors calculated the off-shell electron-nucleon cross section within PWIA using a macroscopic-microscopic description of proton and neutron single-particle momentum distributions for 238U, with electron wave function distortion corrections via the effective momentum approximation and FSI effects via an optical potential. Fissility for proton single-hole excited states of 237Pa was calculated both without and with preequilibrium particle emission using the exciton model, while fissility for 237U and 238U residual nuclei was calculated within the compound nucleus model using a Monte Carlo evaporation-fission algorithm. The total (e,e'f) cross section was obtained by summing contributions from all proton and neutron orbitals, weighted by their respective fissilities, and compared with experimental data from Hansen (1990).

## Key numbers

- Ground state deformation parameters for 238U: elongation epsilon = 0.227, hexadecapole moment alpha_4 = 0.059.
- Fission barriers used in the first step of the evaporation chain: 6.13 MeV for 237Pa, 5.2 MeV for 237U, and 5.62 MeV for 238U.
- Neutron separation energies in the first step: 5.78 MeV for 237Pa, 5.12 MeV for 237U, and 5.04 MeV for 238U.
- Incident electron energy epsilon_1 = 750 MeV and electron scattering angle theta_e = 37.5 degrees for the kinematics used in comparison with experimental data.
- The spin-orbit potential strength parameter kappa = 0.63 for the central potential depth parametrization.

## Context

This work extends earlier inclusive electrofission studies by Likhachev and by Hansen (1990), which dealt only with the total QF contribution, by providing a detailed theoretical calculation of the (e,e'f) differential cross section for 238U. The approach enables future exclusive (e,e'pf) experiments at facilities like TJNAF to extract single-hole contributions and study the dissociation of well-defined single-hole configurations into complex nuclear configurations, potentially revealing state-dependent fission probabilities that are averaged out in conventional reactions.
