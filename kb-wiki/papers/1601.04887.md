# Dilepton production rate in a hot and magnetized quark-gluon plasma

> The differential multiplicity of dileptons in a hot and magnetized quark-gluon plasma, $Δ_{B}\equiv dN_{B}/d^{4}xd^{4}q$, is derived from first principles. The constant magnetic field $B$ is assumed to be aligned in a fixed spatial direction. It is shown that the anisotropy induced by the $B$ field is mainly reflected in the general structure of photon spectral density function. This is related to the imaginary part of the vacuum polarization tensor, $\mbox{Im}[Π^{μν}]$, which is derived in a first order perturbative approximation. As expected, the final analytical expression for $Δ_{B}$ includes a trace over the product of a photonic part, $\mbox{Im}[Π^{μν}]$, and a leptonic part, ${\cal{L}}_{μν}$. It is shown that $Δ_{B}$ consists of two parts, $Δ_{B}^{\|}$ and $Δ_{B}^{\perp}$, arising from the components $(μ,ν)=(\|,\|)$ and $(μ,ν)=(\perp,\perp)$ of $\mbox{Im}[Π^{μν}]$ and ${\cal{L}}_{μν}$. Here, the transverse and longitudinal directions are defined with respect to the direction of the $B$ field. Combining $Δ_{B}^{\|}$ and $Δ_{B}^{\perp}$, a novel anisotropy factor $ν_{B}$ is introduced. Using the final analytical expression of $Δ_{B}$, the possible interplay between the temperature $T$ and the magnetic field strength $eB$ on the ratio $Δ_{B}/Δ_{0}$ and $ν_{B}$ is numerically studied. Here, $Δ_{0}$ is the Born approximated dilepton multiplicity in the absence of external magnetic fields. It is, in particular, shown that for each fixed $T$ and $B$, in the vicinity of certain threshold energies, $Δ_{B}\gg Δ_{0}$ and $Δ_{B}^{\perp}\gg Δ_{B}^{\|}$. The latter anisotropy may be interpreted as one of the microscopic sources of the macroscopic anisotropies, reflecting themselves, e.g., in the elliptic asymmetry factor $v_{2}$ of dileptons.

## Key claim

The paper derives the differential multiplicity of dileptons in a hot quark-gluon plasma under a constant magnetic field using a first-order perturbative approximation, showing that near certain threshold energies of virtual photons the magnetized rate exceeds the unmagnetized Born rate and that the perpendicular component dominates over the parallel component, which may serve as a microscopic source for macroscopic anisotropies such as the elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\).

## Method

The authors follow the finite-temperature field theory method of Weldon (1990) to relate the dilepton production rate to the imaginary part of the vacuum polarization tensor. They compute the one-loop photon self-energy in a constant magnetic field using the Ritus eigenfunction method for charged fermions, then separate the resulting differential multiplicity into components parallel and perpendicular to the magnetic field direction. The analytical expressions are evaluated numerically for dielectrons and dimuons, and the ratio \(\Delta_B/\Delta_0\) and the anisotropy factor \(\nu_B\) are studied as functions of the scaled photon energy \(q_0/T\).

## Key numbers

* For dielectrons at \(eB = 0.3\) GeV\(^2\) (LHC scale) and \(T = 0.2\) GeV, near threshold energies \(\Delta_B \gg \Delta_0\) and \(\Delta_B^\perp \gg \Delta_B^\parallel\); the anisotropy factor \(\nu_B\) approaches values near unity in this regime.
* The magnetic field strengths considered are \(eB \simeq 0.03\) GeV\(^2\) at RHIC and \(eB \simeq 0.3\) GeV\(^2\) at LHC, corresponding to \(B \simeq 1.7 \times 10^{20}\) Gauss for \(eB = 1\) GeV\(^2\).
* In the limit of large photon energies \(q_0 \gg T\), \(\Delta_B\) becomes smaller than \(\Delta_0\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Weldon (1990) for dilepton production in a hot plasma to include a constant magnetic field, motivated by the strong fields generated in noncentral heavy-ion collisions estimated by Skokov et al. (2009) and Kharzeev et al. (2007). It builds on the authors' previous study of hot magnetized plasminos (Taghinavaz and Sadooghi, 2015) and connects to recent work on magnetic-field-induced anisotropies in heavy quark diffusion by Fukushima et al. (2015). The results enable future computation of elliptic flow coefficients \(v_n\) from the magnetized dilepton rate, though the authors note that a rigorous link requires solving relativistic magnetohydrodynamics and determining the equation of state of the magnetized plasma.
