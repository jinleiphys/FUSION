# Statistical approach for supernova matter

> We formulate a statistical model for description of nuclear composition and equation of state of stellar matter at subnuclear densities and temperature up to 20 MeV, which are expected during the collapse and explosion of massive stars. The model includes nuclear, electromagnetic and weak interactions between all kinds of particles, under condition of statistical equilibrium. We emphasize importance of realistic description of the nuclear composition for understanding stellar dynamics and nucleosynthesis. It is demonstrated that the experience accumulated in studies of nuclear multifragmentation reactions can be used for better modelling properties of stellar medium.

# Statistical approach for supernova matter

## Key claim
The authors formulate a statistical model (SMSM) for the nuclear composition and equation of state of stellar matter at subnuclear densities and temperatures up to 20 MeV, arguing that the experience from nuclear multifragmentation reactions can be used to improve modeling of supernova matter. The key hedge is that the model assumes statistical equilibrium for strong and electromagnetic interactions, while weak interactions may often be out of equilibrium, requiring specification of which equilibrium regime applies.

## Method
The authors construct a grand-canonical statistical model (SMSM) that includes nuclear species (A up to 1000), nucleons, electrons, positrons, photons, and neutrinos, with chemical potentials determined by baryon number, charge, and lepton number conservation. Nuclear species are described using a liquid-drop parameterization with bulk, surface, symmetry, and Coulomb terms, where the Coulomb term includes electron screening via the Wigner-Seitz approximation and the surface energy is temperature-dependent. The model calculates densities of all particles self-consistently and is compared to the Lattimer-Swesty (LS) equation of state, with calculations performed for baryon number B=1000 in a fixed volume.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear density: $\rho_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$
- Temperature range considered: $T \approx (0.5 \div 10)$ MeV, with model applicable up to 20 MeV
- Density range considered: $\rho \approx (10^{-6} \div 2) \rho_0$
- Liquid-drop parameters: $w_0 = 16$ MeV (bulk), $\varepsilon_0 = 16$ MeV, $\beta_0 = 18$ MeV (surface), $T_c = 18$ MeV, $\gamma = 25$ MeV (symmetry)
- Nuclear radius parameter: $r_0 = 1.17$ fm
- Reaction times for neutron capture, photodisintegration, and nucleon emission vary from 10 to $10^6$ fm/c
- At $T=1$ MeV, neutron capture dominates for $\rho_n \gtrsim 10^{-5}\rho_0$; at $T=3$ MeV for $\rho_n \gtrsim 10^{-3}\rho_0$; at $T=5$ MeV for $\rho_n \gtrsim 10^{-2}\rho_0$
- Weak interaction characteristic times: 10 ms to 10 s
- At $\rho_B > 10^{-3}\rho_0$, more than half of neutrons are free
- Lepton fractions considered for trapped neutrinos: $Y_L = 0.1, 0.2, 0.3$

## Context
This work extends the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) approach, previously successful in describing nuclear reactions, to supernova matter. It directly contrasts with the Lattimer-Swesty (LS) model (Lamb and Lattimer, 1981; Lattimer and Swesty, 1991) which replaces the ensemble of heavy nuclei with a single "average" nucleus, and with other statistical calculations (e.g., Japan group, Langanke et al., Janka et al.) that include only nuclei in long-lived states known from terrestrial experiments. The model enables more realistic calculations of nuclear composition, equation of state, and weak reaction rates for supernova simulations, particularly by incorporating in-medium modifications of nuclear properties suggested by multifragmentation experiments.
