# Production of heavy meson pairs in $p\bar{p}$ collisions within a double handbag approach

> We study the pair-production of heavy mesons in proton-antiproton annihilations within a perturbative QCD-motivated framework. In particular we investigate $p \bar{p} \rightarrow \overline{D^0} D^0$ within a double handbag approach, where a hard subprocess factorizes from soft hadronic matrix elements. The soft matrix elements can be parametrized by transition distribution amplitudes, which are off-diagonal in flavor space. The transition distribution amplitudes are modelled as overlaps of light-cone wave functions. We obtain rather robust model results for $p \bar{p} \rightarrow \overline{D^0} D^0$ cross sections, which are expected to be measured at the future $\bar{\text{P}}\text{ANDA}$ detector at GSI-FAIR.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the cross section for \(p \bar{p} \rightarrow \overline{D^0} D^0\) can be predicted within a double handbag approach using perturbative QCD, but the result is explicitly model-dependent because the soft hadronic transition matrix elements are approximated by modeled light-cone wave functions with free parameters fixed by physical constraints.

## Method
The authors factorize the scattering amplitude into a hard partonic subprocess (scalar diquark–antidiquark annihilation into a charm–anticharm pair, treated perturbatively) and soft hadronic transition matrix elements for \(p \to \overline{D^0}\) and \(\bar{p} \to D^0\). These soft matrix elements are parametrized as overlaps of light-cone wave functions for the proton (in a quark–scalar-diquark picture) and the D meson, with the proton wave function taken from Kroll (1988) and the D meson wave function peaked at a momentum fraction \(x_0 \approx m_c/M\). The hard subprocess amplitude is evaluated at the peak position and taken out of the convolution integral.

## Key numbers
- The differential cross section \(d\sigma_{p\bar{p}\rightarrow\overline{D^0}D^0}/d\Omega\) at Mandelstam \(s = 15\ \text{GeV}^2\) is in the region of nb.
- The integrated cross section as a function of \(s\) is of the order of nb.
- The transverse size parameters for both the proton and D meson wave functions are \(a_p = 1.1\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(a_D = 1.1\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\).
- The D meson decay constant used to fix the D meson wave function is \(f_D = 206\ \text{MeV}\).
- The root-mean-square intrinsic transverse momentum of the active constituent in the proton is set to \(\langle \mathbf{k}_\perp^2 \rangle_p^{1/2} = 280\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context
This work extends the double handbag approach previously applied to \(p\bar{p} \rightarrow \Lambda_c^+ \overline{\Lambda}_c^-\) by Goritschnig, Pire, and Schweiger (2009) to the production of heavy meson pairs. The cross section predictions are in accordance with the quark-diquark model results of Kroll (1988) but are one order of magnitude smaller than the hadronic interaction-model calculations of Khodjamirian (2011) and Haidenbauer (2010). The results are intended for comparison with future measurements at the \(\bar{\text{P}}\text{ANDA}\) detector at GSI-FAIR.
