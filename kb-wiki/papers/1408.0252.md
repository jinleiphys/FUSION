# Infrared extrapolations for atomic nuclei

> Harmonic oscillator model-space truncations introduce systematic errors to the calculation of binding energies and other observables. We identify the relevant infrared scaling variable and give values for this nucleus-dependent quantity. We consider isotopes of oxygen computed with the coupled-cluster method from chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order and show that the infrared component of the error is sufficiently understood to permit controlled extrapolations. By employing oscillator spaces with relatively large frequencies, well above the energy minimum, the ultraviolet corrections can be suppressed while infrared extrapolations over tens of MeVs are accurate for ground-state energies. However, robust uncertainty quantification for extrapolated quantities that fully accounts for systematic errors is not yet developed.

## Key claim

The infrared (IR) component of the systematic error from harmonic oscillator model-space truncations can be sufficiently understood to permit controlled extrapolations of ground-state energies over tens of MeV, provided that ultraviolet (UV) corrections are suppressed by using oscillator frequencies well above the energy minimum; however, robust uncertainty quantification that fully accounts for systematic errors is not yet developed.

## Method

The authors computed ground-state energies of the oxygen isotopes 16O, 22O, and 24O using the coupled-cluster method with singles and doubles (CCSD) and a chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) with a regulator cutoff of 500 MeV. They identified a nucleus-dependent effective box size L_eff for the harmonic oscillator basis by matching the lowest eigenvalue of the total squared momentum operator in the oscillator basis to that in a spherical box, and then applied an exponential extrapolation formula E(L) = E_inf + A_inf exp(-2 k_inf L) to data from model spaces with N=8, 10, and 12 oscillator quanta and oscillator frequencies in the range 16 to 70 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For 16O, the extrapolated ground-state energy E_inf from N_max=12 data is -122.73 ± 0.35 MeV (statistical error only), compared to the minimum energy at fixed N_max=12 of -122.56 MeV.
- For 22O, the extrapolated ground-state energy E_inf from N_max=12 data is -148.41 ± 0.60 MeV (statistical error only), compared to the minimum energy at N_max=12 of -148.85 MeV.
- For 24O, the extrapolated ground-state energy E_inf from N_max=12 data is -155.38 ± 0.83 MeV (statistical error only), compared to the minimum energy at N_max=12 of -156.35 MeV.
- The exponential extrapolation for 16O works over an energy range of about 60 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for infrared extrapolations in harmonic oscillator bases developed by Furnstahl et al. (2012), More et al. (2013), and Furnstahl et al. (2014), which established that a finite oscillator basis is equivalent to a spherical box of size L at low momenta. The paper generalizes this single-particle result to many-body systems by introducing a nucleus-dependent effective box size L_eff, and demonstrates its practical application to coupled-cluster calculations of oxygen isotopes. This enables more reliable extrapolations from smaller model spaces (e.g., N=8) to the infinite-basis limit, though the authors note that a Bayesian framework may be needed for proper systematic error quantification.
