# On the use of the Operator Product Expansion to constrain the hadron spectrum

> We call into question a recently proposed idea on how the short-distance behavior of QCD correlation functions constrains hadronic states to become degenerate parity eigenstates as one goes up in the spectrum. In particular, we point out that the sum rules which have been proposed in this context are in general regulator dependent, and thus ill-defined.

## Key claim

The authors argue that sum rules previously proposed to constrain the hadron spectrum, which claim that vector and axial-vector mesons must become degenerate parity eigenstates at high excitation, are regulator-dependent and therefore ill-defined, making the claimed constraint unjustified.

## Method

The authors analyze the large-\(N_c\) QCD representation of vector and axial-vector two-point functions as sums over infinitely narrow resonances, and examine the proposed expansion of the difference \(\Pi_V - \Pi_A\) in inverse powers of \(Q^2\). They demonstrate that the sums in the proposed sum rules depend on the arbitrary finite parts of the ultraviolet cutoff, using the Euler-Maclaurin summation formula and a specific model ansatz for the meson spectrum to illustrate the regulator dependence. They also present a counterexample from two-dimensional large-\(N_c\) QCD, where the spectrum alternates in parity and does not become degenerate.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is given as \(F_\pi = 93\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The parton-model coefficient relating the asymptotic constants is \(\frac{F_V^2}{\Lambda_V^2} = \frac{F_A^2}{\Lambda_A^2} = \frac{N_c}{24 \pi^2}\).
- In the model ansatz, the relation between cutoffs is \(\Lambda^2 \equiv N_A \Lambda_A^2 = N_V \Lambda_V^2 + \mu^2\), where \(\mu\) is a finite but undetermined parameter.
- The condition for the first sum rule in the model ansatz becomes \(\frac{N_c}{24 \pi^2} \mu^2 = -F_\pi^2 + F_A^2 + F_\rho^2 - F_V^2\), showing regulator dependence.
- For large \(k\), the OPE coefficients behave as \(c_k \sim \frac{(2k)!}{2^{2k-1} k \pi^{2k}} \left( F_V^2 \Lambda_V^{2(k-1)} - F_A^2 \Lambda_A^{2(k-1)} \right)\).

## Context

This paper directly challenges the claims of Beane (2001) that the Operator Product Expansion forces parity doubling in the high-energy hadron spectrum, a result that had been used to infer hybrid meson spectra and to argue for chiral symmetry restoration in highly excited states by Cohen (2001) and Glozman (2001). The authors show that two-dimensional large-\(N_c\) QCD, solved by 't Hooft (1974) and further analyzed by Zhitnitsky (1984), provides a counterexample where the spectrum alternates in parity without degeneracy. The work clarifies that the OPE constrains only the asymptotic behavior of spectral function coefficients, not the detailed parity structure of the spectrum, leaving the question of chiral restoration for highly excited hadrons open for further investigation.
