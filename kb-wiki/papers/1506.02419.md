# Slope-dependent nuclear-symmetry energy within the effective surface approximation

> The effective-surface approximation is extended taking into account derivatives of the symmetry-energy density per particle with respect to the mean particle density. The isoscalar and isovector particle densities in this extended effective-surface approximation are derived. The improved expressions of the surface symmetry energy, in particular, its surface tension coefficients in the sharp-edged proton-neutron asymmetric nuclei take into account important gradient terms of the energy density functional. For most Skyrme forces the surface symmetry-energy constants and the corresponding neutron skins and isovector stiffnesses are calculated as functions of the Swiatecki derivative of the nongradient term of the symmetry-energy density per particle with respect to the isoscalar density. Using the analytical isovector surface-energy constants in the framework of the Fermi-liquid droplet model we find energies and sum rules of the isovector giant-dipole resonance structure in a reasonable agreement with the experimental data, and they are compared with other theoretical approaches.

## Key claim

The effective-surface approximation is extended by including derivatives of the symmetry-energy density per particle with respect to the mean particle density (the Swiatecki derivative L and the second derivative K_-), which were neglected in prior work; the resulting surface symmetry-energy constants, neutron skins, and isovector stiffnesses depend significantly on the slope parameter L but are independent of K_- up to second order in the small expansion parameter gamma.

## Method

The authors minimize the nuclear energy functional under constraints of fixed particle number and neutron excess using Lagrange multipliers, solving the resulting variational equations analytically within the leptodermous (effective-surface) approximation for the isoscalar and isovector particle densities. They then compute the surface symmetry-energy constants, neutron skin thickness, and isovector stiffness as functions of the Skyrme force parameters and the Swiatecki derivative L, and use these quantities as inputs to the Fermi-liquid droplet model to calculate the energies and sum rules of the isovector giant-dipole resonance (IVDR) structure.

## Key numbers

- Volume symmetry-energy constant J ≈ 30 MeV (infinite nuclear matter)
- Isoscalar in-compressibility modulus K_+ ≈ 215–245 MeV (symmetric nuclear matter)
- Slope parameter L ≈ 20–120 MeV (range of values considered)
- Diffuseness parameter a ≈ 0.5–0.6 fm
- Mean particle density of infinite nuclear matter rho_bar ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Separation energy b_V ≈ 16 MeV
- Spin-orbit parameter W_0 ≈ 100–130 MeV·fm^5
- Neutron skin thickness tau is proportional to the asymmetry parameter I (tau ∝ I), with explicit expression given in Eq. (skin) involving the diffuseness a, the constant c_sym, and the function g(w_r)

## Context

This work extends the effective-surface approximation of Strutinsky, Magner, and others (Strutinsky 1984, Magner 1999, Magner 2002) by incorporating the Swiatecki derivative L of the symmetry energy, which was neglected in the prior calculations of Blocki, Magner, and Ring (2013). The results are compared with the liquid droplet model of Myers and Swiatecki (1969, 1974, 1977) and with more recent works by Vretenar (2003), Danielewicz (2004), Pearson (2005), and Vinas (2007, 2009). The improved surface symmetry-energy constants enable more accurate predictions of the IVDR splitting into main and satellite modes within the Fermi-liquid droplet model, and provide a foundation for calculating pygmy dipole resonances and neutron skin thicknesses across the nuclear chart.
