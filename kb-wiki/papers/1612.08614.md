# Collective Excitations in Hot QCD Plasma

> We study the long wavelength excitations in rotating QCD fluid in presence of an external magnetic field at finite vector and axial charge densities. We consider the fluctuations of vector and axial charge currents coupled to energy and momentum fluctuations and compute the $SO(3)$ covariant dispersion relations of the six corresponding hydrodynamic modes. Among them, there are always two scalar Chiral Magnetic-Vortical-Heat (CMVH) waves; In the absence of magnetic field (vorticity) these waves reduce to CVH (CMH) waves. While CMVH waves are the mixed of CMH and CVH waves, they have generally different velocities compared to the sum of velocities of the latter waves. The other four modes, which are made out of scalar-vector fluctuations, are mixed Sound-Alfvén waves. We show that in the direction parallel to both magnetic field and vorticity, these four modes are the two ordinary sound modes together with two Chiral Alfvén waves (CAW).

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a rotating QCD fluid with an external magnetic field at finite vector and axial charge densities, the six hydrodynamic modes include two scalar Chiral Magnetic-Vortical-Heat (CMVH) waves and four mixed Sound-Alfvén waves; however, the velocity of the mixed CMVH waves is generally not equal to the sum of the velocities of the individual Chiral Magnetic-Heat (CMH) and Chiral Vortical-Heat (CVH) waves when momentum perturbations are included, even when vorticity is aligned with the magnetic field.

## Method

The authors linearize the conservation equations for energy-momentum, vector current, and axial current around a thermodynamic equilibrium state with constant temperature, chemical potentials, magnetic field, and rotation. They compute the SO(3) covariant dispersion relations for the six hydrodynamic modes by solving the characteristic equation of the linearized system, using the Landau-Lifshitz frame and a conformal equation of state for high-temperature QCD plasma. The analysis is performed separately for cases with only magnetic field, only vorticity, and both fields present, with dissipation neglected except in a final subsection.

## Key numbers

- The chiral anomaly coefficient is C = 1/(2 pi^2) and the gravitational anomaly coefficient is D = 1/6.
- The conformal equation of state used is epsilon = 3p = (7 pi^2/60) T^4 + (1/2)(mu^2 + mu_5^2) T^2 + (1/(4 pi^2))(mu^4 + 6 mu^2 mu_5^2 + mu_5^4).
- The sound speed for the conformal fluid is c_s = 1/sqrt(3).
- The dispersion relation for the two scalar Chiral Magnetic-Heat waves is given by omega^B_{1,2}(k) = -(A_1 +/- sqrt(A_1^2 - A_2 E))/E * (B dot k), where the coefficients A_1, A_2, and E depend on thermodynamic derivatives and anomaly coefficients.

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral transport phenomena, specifically the Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW) found by Kharzeev et al. (2010) and the Chiral Vortical Wave (CVW) by Jiang et al. (2015), by coupling charge fluctuations to energy-momentum fluctuations. It generalizes previous results that assumed decoupled charge fluctuations, such as those in Stephanov et al. (2014), and shows that the full spectrum includes four additional mixed Sound-Alfvén modes. The results enable a more complete hydrodynamic description of the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy ion collisions, where both magnetic fields and vorticity are present, and can be applied to phenomena in neutron stars, supernovae, and Weyl semimetals.
