# Neutrino processes in partially degenerate neutron matter

> We investigate neutrino processes for conditions reached in simulations of core-collapse supernovae. Where neutrino-matter interactions play an important role, matter is partially degenerate, and we extend earlier work that addressed the degenerate regime. We derive expressions for the spin structure factor in neutron matter, which is a key quantity required for evaluating rates of neutrino processes. We show that, for essentially all conditions encountered in the post-bounce phase of core-collapse supernovae, it is a very good approximation to calculate the spin relaxation rates in the nondegenerate limit. We calculate spin relaxation rates based on chiral effective field theory interactions and find that they are typically a factor of two smaller than those obtained using the standard one-pion-exchange interaction alone.

## Key claim

The authors show that for essentially all conditions encountered in the post-bounce phase of core-collapse supernovae, it is a very good approximation to calculate spin relaxation rates in the nondegenerate limit, even though the matter is partially degenerate. This extends earlier work that addressed only the fully degenerate regime.

## Method

The authors derive expressions for the spin structure factor in neutron matter using Landau's theory of normal Fermi liquids generalized to higher temperatures, with quasiparticle interactions described by a spin-dependent coupling parameter. They calculate spin relaxation rates based on chiral effective field theory (EFT) interactions and compare these to results obtained using the standard one-pion-exchange interaction alone. The relevant thermodynamic conditions are identified from spherically symmetric supernova simulations of 15 and 40 solar mass progenitor stars using two different equations of state.

## Key numbers

- Spin relaxation rates from chiral EFT interactions are typically a factor of two smaller than those obtained using the standard one-pion-exchange interaction alone.
- The hot mantle of the protoneutron star spans densities from approximately 10^11 g cm^-3 at temperatures of about 3 MeV down to 10^14 g cm^-3 at temperatures of about 30 MeV.
- The cold core of the protoneutron star has densities around 3 × 10^14 g cm^-3 and temperatures around 10-20 MeV.
- The Landau parameter G0 is approximately 0.45 at a density of ρ0/100 and 0.8 at nuclear saturation density ρ0 = 2.8 × 10^14 g cm^-3.
- More than 70% of the ν_e, ν̄_e, and ν_μ,τ luminosities are emitted from the hot mantle during the first second of the post-bounce phase.

## Context

This work extends the degenerate neutron matter calculations of Bacca et al. (2009) and the unified approach to structure factors described by Lopez et al. (2005) to partially degenerate and nondegenerate conditions relevant for core-collapse supernova simulations. The results enable more accurate treatment of neutrino-matter interactions in the hot mantle of the protoneutron star, which is critical for modeling neutrino transport, energy deposition behind the accretion shock, and the development of fluid instabilities that may revive the supernova shock. The paper also generalizes the spin fluctuation rate formalism of Hannestad and Raffelt (1998) to include spin correlations and mean-field effects.
