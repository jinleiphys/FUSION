# Quark stars under strong magnetic fields

> Within the confined isospin- and density-dependent mass model, we study the properties of strange quark matter (SQM) and quark stars (QSs) under strong magnetic fields. The equation of state of SQM under a constant magnetic field is obtained self-consistently and the pressure perpendicular to the magnetic field is shown to be larger than that parallel to the magnetic field, implying that the properties of magnetized QSs generally depend on both the strength and the orientation of the magnetic fields distributed inside the stars. Using a density-dependent magnetic field profile which is introduced to mimic the magnetic field strength distribution in a star, we study the properties of static spherical QSs by assuming two extreme cases for the magnetic field orientation in the stars, i.e., the radial orientation in which the local magnetic fields are along the radial direction and the transverse orientation in which the local magnetic fields are randomly oriented but perpendicular to the radial direction. Our results indicate that including the magnetic fields with radial (transverse) orientation can significantly decrease (increase) the maximum mass of QSs, demonstrating the importance of the magnetic field orientation inside the magnetized compact stars.

# Quark stars under strong magnetic fields

## Key claim

The maximum mass of quark stars can be either significantly decreased or increased depending on whether the magnetic field orientation inside the star is radial or transverse, demonstrating that the magnetic field orientation is as important as its strength for determining quark star structure. This conclusion holds within the confined isospin- and density-dependent mass (CIDDM) model, which was previously shown to reproduce the mass of PSR J0348+0432 (2.01 solar masses) when magnetic fields are neglected.

## Method

The authors used the CIDDM model, where quark confinement is modeled by density- and isospin-dependent quark masses, to compute the equation of state of strange quark matter under constant magnetic fields at zero temperature. They then introduced a density-dependent magnetic field profile (parametrized as B = B_surf + B_0[1 - exp(-beta_0 (n_B/n_0)^gamma)]) to mimic the spatial distribution of magnetic field strength inside stars, and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static spherical quark stars under two extreme magnetic field orientations: radial (local fields along the radial direction) and transverse (local fields randomly oriented but perpendicular to the radial direction).

## Key numbers

- The density at the minimum of energy per baryon under constant magnetic fields increases from 0.49 fm^-3 to 0.61 fm^-3 to 0.86 fm^-3 when B changes from 1x10^18 G to 2x10^18 G to 3x10^18 G.
- For baryon density n_B = 5n_0 (where n_0 = 0.16 fm^-3), the critical magnetic field strength B_c (where longitudinal pressure becomes zero) is approximately 2.80x10^18 G; for n_B = 7n_0, B_c is approximately 3.74x10^18 G.
- For the fast B-profile with B_0 = 1.28x10^19 G, the maximum mass of static quark stars with transverse orientation reaches about 2.13 solar masses, while with radial orientation it reaches about 1.57 solar masses, giving a normalized mass asymmetry delta_m = 30%.
- For the slow B-profile with B_0 = 1.20x10^19 G, the maximum mass with transverse orientation reaches about 2.18 solar masses, while with radial orientation it reaches about 1.29 solar masses, giving delta_m = 51%.
- The pressure splitting factor delta_p at 1.2 fm^-3 is 0.25 (P_parallel/P_perp = 0.78) for B_0 = 4x10^18 G, and 1.6 (P_parallel/P_perp = 1/9) for B_0 = 1x10^19 G.

## Context

This work extends the CIDDM model previously developed by Chu et al. (2014) to include strong magnetic fields, which were neglected in that earlier study. It addresses the controversial question of whether magnetic fields enhance or reduce the maximum mass of compact stars, a debate involving prior work by Ban et al. (1997, 1998), Menezes et al. (2009), Ryu et al. (2010, 2012), and others. The results show qualitative agreement with the magnetic-color-flavor-locked phase calculations of Paulucci et al. (2011), who found similar magnetic field effects on pressure anisotropy and quark star mass using an effective bag model. The work enables future studies of more general magnetic field orientations and distributions where spherical symmetry is broken, requiring full solutions of Einstein field equations.
