# Mass-radius constraints for compact stars and a critical endpoint

> We present two types of models for hybrid compact stars composed of a quark core and a hadronic mantle with an abrupt first order phase transition at the interface which are in accordance with the latest astrophysical measurements of two 2 M_sun pulsars. While the first is a schematic one, the second one is based on a QCD motivated nonlocal PNJL model with density-dependent vector coupling strength. Both models support the possibility of so called twin compact stars which have the same mass but different radius and internal structure at high mass (~2 M_sun), provided they exhibit a large jump Δεin the energy density of the first order phase transition fulfilling Δε/ε_crit > 0.6. We conclude that the measurement of high-mass twin stars would support the existence of a first order phase transition in symmetric matter at zero temperature entailing the existence of a critical end point in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

A strong first-order phase transition in cold, dense matter inside compact stars, with a large energy-density jump satisfying Δε/ε_crit > 0.6, would produce "twin" compact stars of the same mass (~2 M_sun) but different radii; if such high-mass twins are observed, this would prove the existence of a first-order phase transition at zero temperature and thus imply a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid compact star equations of state (EoS) using a two-phase construction: a hadronic EoS (DD2 or APR) for the outer mantle and a quark-core EoS, either via a schematic three-parameter model (ZHAHP scheme) with a squared speed of sound c_QM^2 and a density jump Δε, or via a nonlocal PNJL model with density-dependent vector coupling strength η_v(μ). The Gibbs condition for phase equilibrium determines the critical chemical potential μ_c, and the resulting EoS is used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius sequences.

## Key numbers

- The condition for high-mass twin stars requires Δε/ε_crit > 0.6, where Δε is the energy-density jump at the first-order phase transition.
- For the ZHAHP scheme example: c_QM^2 = 0.94, Δε = 0.67 ε_crit, ε_crit = 485 MeV/fm^3, corresponding to P(ε_crit) = 100 MeV/fm^3 and baryon density n_crit = 2.9 n_0 (with n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}).
- For the nonlocal PNJL model with DD2 hadronic EoS: ε_crit = 225.3 MeV/fm^3, μ_c = 1420 MeV, Γ = 360 MeV, η_< = 0.10, η_> = 0.20.
- For the nonlocal PNJL model with APR hadronic EoS: ε_crit = 373.0 MeV/fm^3, μ_c = 1400 MeV, Γ = 400 MeV, η_< = 0.05, η_> = 0.25.
- The two known 2 M_sun pulsars are PSR J1614-2230 with 1.97 ± 0.04 M_sun and PSR J0348+0432 with 2.01 ± 0.04 M_sun.
- High-mass twins would have a radius difference of about 2 km, requiring radius measurements to an accuracy of less than 1 km (about 5% or δR ~ 600 m) for detection.

## Context

This work extends the earlier ZHAHP scheme of Zdunik and Haensel (2012) and Alford, Han and Prakash (2013) for hybrid star EoS, and applies a nonlocal PNJL model developed by Contrera (2012) and Benic (2013) with a density-dependent vector coupling suggested by Blaschke (2013). The paper addresses the open question of whether a critical endpoint exists in the QCD phase diagram, given that lattice QCD simulations by the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi, 2010) and HotQCD Collaboration (Bazavov, 2011) show a crossover at zero chemical potential. The authors propose that measuring radii of the known 2 M_sun pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432 with missions like LOFT or NICER could detect high-mass twin stars, which would prove a first-order phase transition at T=0 and thus imply a critical endpoint at finite temperature.
