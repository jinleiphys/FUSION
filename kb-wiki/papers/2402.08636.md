# Finite density QCD equation of state: critical point and lattice-based $T'$-expansion

> We present a novel construction of the QCD equation of state (EoS) at finite baryon density. Our work combines a recently proposed resummation scheme for lattice QCD results with the universal critical behavior at the QCD critical point. This allows us to obtain a family of equations of state in the range $0 \leq μ_B \leq 700$ MeV and 25 MeV $\leq T \leq 800$ MeV, which match lattice QCD results near $μ_B=0$ while featuring a critical point in the 3D Ising model universality class. The position of the critical point can be chosen within the range accessible to beam-energy scan heavy-ion collision experiments. The strength of the singularity and the shape of the critical region are parameterized using a standard parameter set. We impose stability and causality constraints and discuss the available ranges of critical point parameter choices, finding that they extend beyond earlier parametric QCD EoS proposals. We present thermodynamic observables, including baryon density, pressure, entropy density, energy density, baryon susceptibility and speed of sound, that cover a wide range in the QCD phase diagram relevant for experimental exploration.

## Key claim
The paper presents a new construction of the QCD equation of state at finite baryon density that combines a resummation scheme for lattice QCD results (the $T'$-expansion) with universal critical behavior from the 3D Ising model, enabling coverage up to $\mu_B = 700$ MeV without the unphysical oscillations that limited the earlier BEST collaboration approach to $\mu_B \leq 450$ MeV.

## Method
The authors parametrize lattice QCD results for the baryon susceptibility $\chi_2^B(T,0)$ and the $T'$-expansion coefficient $\kappa_2^{BB}(T)$ using analytic fitting functions over the range 25 MeV $\leq T \leq 800$ MeV, merging with hadron resonance gas results at low temperature. They then introduce a 3D Ising model critical point by mapping the Ising coordinates $(r,h)$ first to the $T'$-expansion coordinates $(T',\mu_B^2)$ via a quadratic mapping, and then to QCD coordinates $(T,\mu_B)$ using the relation between $T$ and $T'$. The singular contribution to the baryon density is carried by the $T'$ function, while the regular part comes from the lattice-based $\chi_2^B(T',0)$.

## Key numbers
- The equation of state covers the range $0 \leq \mu_B \leq 700$ MeV and 25 MeV $\leq T \leq 800$ MeV
- The pseudo-critical temperature at $\mu_B=0$ is $T_0 = 158.0\pm0.6$ MeV from Borsanyi (2020)
- Two example critical point positions are illustrated: $\mu_{BC}=350$ MeV with $T_C=140$ MeV, and $\mu_{BC}=500$ MeV with $T_C=116$ MeV
- The 3D Ising model critical exponents used are $\beta = 0.326$, $\delta = 4.80$, and $\alpha = 0.11$
- The parametrization of $\chi_2^B(T)$ uses five fit parameters $d_1=0.73$, $d_2=11.19$, $d_3=0.32$, $d_4=0.20$, $d_5=0.69$
- The parametrization of $\kappa_2^{BB}(T)$ uses four fit parameters $a_2=0.652$, $a_3=-2.60$, $b_0=21.4$, $b_1=-9.81$

## Context
This work extends the BEST collaboration equation of state (Parotto, 2018; Mroczek, 2020; An, 2021; Karthein, 2021) which used Taylor expansion and was limited to $\mu_B\leq450$ MeV due to unphysical oscillations at large $\mu_B$. The new approach replaces Taylor expansion with the $T'$-expansion scheme developed by the Wuppertal-Budapest lattice collaboration (Borsanyi, 2021; Borsanyi, 2022), which shows faster convergence and avoids wiggly behavior. The resulting family of equations of state enables hydrodynamic simulations for the Beam Energy Scan II at RHIC covering $\mu_B\lesssim700$ MeV, and provides a framework for testing critical point scenarios against experimental data.
