# A theoretical view on bound antikaon-nuclear states

> We present an overview of the latest theoretical studies on the antikaon properties in the nuclear medium, in connection with the recent experimental claims of very deeply bound antikaon-nuclear states. We argue that proper many-body formulations using modern realistic antikaon-nucleon interactions are not able to generate such systems. Instead, a simple two-nucleon antikaon absorption mechanism where the remaining nucleus acts as spectator explains the peak in the semi-inclusive proton momentum spectrum, observed on a 4He target at KEK (but later not confirmed in an inclusive experiment) and on a 6Li target at FINUDA. This signal is clearly seen in another FINUDA experiment measuring the invariant mass of Lambda-proton pairs after two-nucleon kaon absorption. We show that another peak of this experiment, seen at lower invariant masses and interpreted as a bound K-pp state, is simply generated by the same two-nucleon absorption mechanism followed by final-state interactions of the produced particles with the residual nucleus. Our conclusion is that all the experimental claims for the formation of very deeply bound antikaonic nuclear systems receive an alternative explanation in terms of conventional nuclear processes.

# A theoretical view on bound antikaon-nuclear states

## Key claim

The authors argue that all experimental claims for very deeply bound antikaon-nuclear states (binding energies of 100-200 MeV) can be explained by conventional nuclear processes, specifically two-nucleon antikaon absorption followed by final-state interactions, without requiring exotic bound-state formation. The hedge is that this conclusion depends on using proper many-body formulations with modern realistic antikaon-nucleon interactions, which the authors claim are not able to generate such deeply bound systems.

## Method

The authors perform a Monte Carlo simulation of stopped kaon absorption on nuclei (6Li, 7Li, 12C) using a local density approximation, where the kaon is absorbed by two nucleons (pp or pn pairs) from the local Fermi sea, producing a Lambda and a nucleon. The simulation tracks the propagation of the produced particles through the residual nucleus, allowing for secondary collisions according to experimental NN and Lambda-N cross sections, and applies the same kinematic cuts as the FINUDA experiment (p_Lambda > 300 MeV/c and cos(theta) < -0.8 for back-to-back events). The resulting Lambda-proton invariant mass distributions are compared directly to the experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The self-consistent antikaon optical potential at normal nuclear density (rho = 0.16 fm^-3) is attractive and around -50 MeV at zero momentum, with depths between -40 and -70 MeV.
- The two-nucleon absorption mechanism produces a proton peak at p_p ~ 480 MeV/c, with broadening of ~70 MeV for K- absorption in 4He and ~20 MeV in 6Li.
- The production rate for the candidate reaction K- 4He -> Sigma- p d is 1.6%, compatible with the ~1% branching ratio required for the observed peak.
- The FINUDA Lambda-p invariant mass spectrum shows a narrow peak at 2340 MeV and a wider peak at around 2255 MeV; the latter was interpreted as a K-pp bound state with B = 115 MeV and width Gamma = 67 MeV.
- The authors' simulation reproduces the main bump at 2260-2270 MeV in the Lambda-p invariant mass distribution for light nuclei.
- Recent few-body calculations predict K-pp binding energies of 50-70 MeV with widths of 95-110 MeV (Faddeev) or ~50 MeV with widths >100 MeV (variational).

## Context

This work extends the criticisms of Oset and Toki (2006) against the deeply bound kaonic states predicted by Akaishi, Dote, and collaborators (2002-2005), who used variational calculations with simplified K-N interactions and predicted binding energies around 100-200 MeV with nuclear densities up to nine times normal. The paper directly contradicts the interpretation of the FINUDA collaboration (2005) that a peak at 2255 MeV in the Lambda-p invariant mass spectrum represents a bound K-pp state. Instead, the authors show this peak arises from final-state interactions of particles produced in conventional two-nucleon kaon absorption, consistent with the self-consistent chiral unitary approach to the K-N interaction developed by Ramos, Oset, and collaborators (2000-2006). The work enables a reinterpretation of all existing experimental claims for deeply bound antikaonic nuclear systems and supports the view that such states, if they exist, would have binding energies of only 50-70 MeV with widths too large to be clearly observable.
