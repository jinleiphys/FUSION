# Refining the deep sub-barrier 12C+12C excitation function with STELLA

> Purpose: We have investigated the cross section around the lowest direct coincident gamma-particle measurements, where previously only limits could be established with the aim of obtaining a detailed description of the excitation function. We have furthermore analysed the ratio of extreme decay branching into the first excited state of daughter nuclei with alpha or proton emission at relative kinetic energies where previous measurements are in disagreement with each other. Conclusions: Our findings in the astrophysics RoI support reaction-rate models with a lower average S-factor trend, that deviates significantly from standard extrapolations between 2.2 MeV and 2.6 MeV, for stellar carbon burning simulations of up to 25 Msol stars. Based on our data, an overall increase of the S-factor at deep subbarrier energy cannot be confirmed. The extremely low ratio of the branching into the first excited state with proton over alpha emission of ~ 2% at 3.23 MeV might indicate the presence of alpha cluster compound states in 24Mg. This highly favours α emission with fundamental consequences in possible stellar carbon burning sites.

## Key claim

The paper's data support reaction-rate models with a lower average S-factor trend that deviates significantly from standard extrapolations between 2.2 MeV and 2.6 MeV, but the authors cannot confirm an overall increase of the S-factor at deep sub-barrier energy, contrary to some previous claims.

## Method

The experiment used the STELLA apparatus with UK-FATIMA at the Andromède accelerator (IJCLab Orsay), delivering carbon beams up to 3 pμA on thin rotating carbon targets (20–80 μg/cm²). Light charged particles were detected with silicon strip detectors covering 30% of 4π, and gamma decays were detected with 36 LaBr₃(Ce) scintillators in coincidence with nanosecond timing precision. The authors measured the α₁ and p₁ exit channels to the first excited states of ²⁰Ne and ²³Na, converting these to total cross sections using branching corrections from Becker (1981).

## Key numbers

- Proton cross section at E_rel = 3.23 MeV: (3.0 ± 0.9) × 10⁻⁵ mb
- Alpha cross section at E_rel = 3.23 MeV: (8.6 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ mb
- Ratio σ_p1/(σ_α1 + σ_p1) at E_rel = 3.23 MeV: 1.68 ± 0.01%
- Ratio σ_p1/(σ_α1 + σ_p1) at E_rel = 2.32 MeV: 24.5 ± 4.0%
- Alpha cross section at E_rel = 2.32 MeV: (2.5 ± 1.8) × 10⁻⁷ mb
- Proton cross section at E_rel = 2.32 MeV: (1.7 ± 1.4) × 10⁻⁷ mb
- Gamma detection efficiency at E(p₁) = 440 keV: 8.6%
- Gamma detection efficiency at E(α₁) = 1634 keV: 2.6%
- Coincidence timing resolution for alphas: 10.3(5) ns (sigma)
- Coincidence timing resolution for protons: 8(1) ns (sigma)
- Systematic uncertainty: 16.5%

## Context

This work extends direct coincidence measurements of the ¹²C+¹²C fusion excitation function into the deep sub-barrier regime, addressing discrepancies between the earlier particle-detection data of Becker (1981) and the gamma-detection data of Spillane (2007). The results support the fusion hindrance model of Jiang (2007) over the CF88 extrapolation of Fowler (1975), and are consistent with the coincidence measurements of Fruet (2020) and Tan (2024). The extremely low proton branching at 3.23 MeV may indicate alpha cluster compound states in ²⁴Mg, with implications for stellar carbon burning simulations in stars up to 25 solar masses, following the nucleosynthesis studies of Pignatari (2013) and Monpribat (2022).
