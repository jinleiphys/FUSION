# Valence quark contributions for the gamma N -> P11(1440) transition

> A covariant spectator quark model is applied to estimate the valence quark contributions to the$ F1*(Q2) and F2*(Q2) transition form factors for the gamma N -> P11(1440) reaction. The Roper resonance, P11(1440), is assumed to be the first radial excitation of the nucleon. The model requires no extra parameters except for those already fixed by the previous studies for the nucleon. The results are consistent with the experimental data in the high Q2 region, and those from the lattice QCD. Finally the model is also applied to estimate the meson cloud contributions from the CLAS and MAID analysis.

## Key claim

The covariant spectator quark model, with no additional free parameters beyond those fixed by nucleon data, predicts the valence quark contributions to the $\gamma N \to P_{11}(1440)$ transition form factors; the model agrees with experimental data only in the high $Q^2$ region ($Q^2 > 2$ GeV$^2$), while the low $Q^2$ disagreement is attributed to missing meson cloud effects.

## Method

The authors model the nucleon and the Roper resonance $P_{11}(1440)$ as three-valence-quark systems using a covariant spectator quark model, where one quark interacts with the electromagnetic field while the other two form an on-shell diquark. The Roper is treated as the first radial excitation of the nucleon, with its scalar wave function differing from the nucleon's by a factor linear in the momentum variable $\chi_R$, characterized by a new parameter $\beta_3$ fixed by orthogonality with the nucleon wave function. The transition form factors $F_1^*(Q^2)$ and $F_2^*(Q^2)$ are computed via a relativistic impulse approximation, and the meson cloud contributions are estimated by subtracting the valence quark (bare) results from experimental data (CLAS and MAID analysis).

## Key numbers

* The Roper resonance mass is taken as $M_R = 1.440$ GeV (physical mass) and also as $M_R = 1.750$ GeV (bare mass from dynamical coupled channel models).
* The model agrees with experimental data for $Q^2 > 2$ GeV$^2$, particularly for $F_1^*$.
* The heavy effective meson mass in the quark form factor parametrization is $M_h = 2M$, where $M$ is the nucleon mass.
* Significant meson cloud effects are found for $Q^2 < 1$ GeV$^2$, with a fast falloff as $Q^2$ increases.

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model, previously applied to the nucleon (Ramalho, 2007) and $\Delta$ systems (Ramalho, 2008), to the Roper resonance $P_{11}(1440)$. The results contradict the expectation that constituent quark models alone can describe the low-$Q^2$ region, supporting the view from dynamical coupled channel models (Suzuki, 2010) that the Roper is a heavy bare core dressed by meson clouds. The model enables estimation of meson cloud contributions from CLAS and MAID data, and its success in describing lattice QCD data at heavy pion masses (Lin, 2009) suggests it reliably isolates valence quark effects.
