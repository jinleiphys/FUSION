# Neutrino nucleus cross sections for low energy neutrinos at SNS facilities

> We calculate the neutrino nucleus cross sections for charged lepton production relevant for the experiments proposed with the stopped muon neutrinos using neutron spallation source facility. The calculations are done in local density approximation taking into account Pauli blocking, Fermi motion effects and renormalization of weak transition strengths in the nuclear medium. The effect of Coulomb distortion of the lepton produced in charge current reactions is taken into account by using the Fermi function as well as in a model where an effective momentum has been used for the lepton moving in the local Coulomb field of the final nucleus. The numerical results for the neutrino nucleus total cross sections averaged over Michel spectrum are presented for various nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors calculate charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections for low-energy neutrinos (Michel spectrum, up to 52.8 MeV) from stopped muon decays at spallation neutron source facilities, using a local density approximation that includes Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, RPA correlations, and Coulomb distortion; the abstract does not emphasize that the RPA correlations cause a further reduction of 50-70% at 50 MeV depending on the nucleus, and that the Coulomb correction method (Fermi function vs. modified effective momentum approximation) yields different results for intermediate and heavy nuclei.

## Method

The cross section for the reaction ν_e + n → e^- + p in nuclei is calculated in the local density approximation, where the nuclear response is obtained from the imaginary part of the Lindhard function for particle-hole excitations, including Pauli blocking and Fermi motion via local Fermi momenta derived from nuclear density distributions. The renormalization of weak transition strengths in the nuclear medium is treated by summing RPA correlations in the longitudinal and transverse channels using π and ρ exchange potentials with a Landau-Migdal parameter g' = 0.7, including Δ-hole excitations. Coulomb distortion of the outgoing electron is accounted for either by a Fermi function F(Z,E_e) or by a modified effective momentum approximation (MEMA) where the electron energy and momentum are shifted by the local Coulomb potential of the final nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Michel spectrum maximum energy: E_0 = 52.8 MeV
- For ¹²C at E_ν = 50 MeV, suppression from Q-value, Pauli blocking, and Fermi motion is ≈ 93%; for ⁵⁶Fe, ¹²⁷I, and ²⁰⁸Pb it is ≈ 75-77%
- At E_ν = 50 MeV, RPA correlations give a further reduction of 50% for ¹²C, 60% for ⁵⁶Fe, and ≈ 70% for ¹²⁷I and ²⁰⁸Pb
- At E_ν = 200 MeV, the suppression from Pauli blocking and Fermi motion reduces to 40-45% for all these nuclei; RPA reduction becomes 35% for ¹²C, 40% for ⁵⁶Fe, and ≈ 50% for ¹²⁷I and ²⁰⁸Pb
- Flux-averaged cross section <σ> for ¹²C: 11.80 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (with RPA, no Coulomb); 13.60 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (with RPA and Coulomb)
- Flux-averaged cross section <σ> for ⁵⁶Fe: 161.40 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (with RPA, no Coulomb); 277.00 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (with RPA and Coulomb)
- Flux-averaged cross section <σ> for ²⁰⁸Pb: 161.40 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (with RPA, no Coulomb); 277.00 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (with RPA and Coulomb), note: the table shows ²⁰⁸Pb value as 161.40 and 277.00, same as ⁵⁶Fe in the provided text
- Coulomb distortion increases cross section by 15% for ¹²C at E_ν = 50 MeV, decreasing to 10% at 200 MeV; for ⁵⁶Fe the increase is 83% at 50 MeV and 75% at 200 MeV (using Fermi function)

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of low-energy neutrino-nucleus cross sections reviewed by Donnelly and Peccei, Kubodera et al., and Kolbe et al., and targets the specific list of nuclei proposed for SNS experiments by Avignone and Efremenko. The local density approach with RPA correlations had been previously applied to electromagnetic and weak processes in nuclei by Singh et al. and Athar et al. The results for ¹²C, ⁵⁶Fe, and ²⁰⁸Pb are compared with shell model calculations (e.g., Kolbe et al., Suzuki et al.) and RPA with pairing correlations (Kolbe et al., Volpe et al.); the authors note that for ²⁰⁸Pb their flux-averaged cross section is smaller than those of Kolbe et al., Suzuki et al., and Volpe et al., indicating that more work is needed for that nucleus. The numerical results provide benchmarks for planned neutrino-nucleus cross section measurements at SNS facilities.
