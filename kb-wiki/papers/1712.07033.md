# Effects of chiral three-nucleon forces on $^{4}$He-nucleus scattering in a wide range of incident energies

> It is a current important subject to clarify properties of chiral three-nucleon forces (3NFs) not only in nuclear matter but also in scattering between finite-size nuclei. Particularly for the elastic scattering, this study has just started and the properties are not understood in a wide range of incident energies ($E_{\rm in}$). We investigate basic properties of chiral 3NFs in nuclear matter with positive energies by using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method with chiral two-nucleon forces of N$^{3}$LO and 3NFs of NNLO, and analyze effects of chiral 3NFs on $^{4}$He elastic scattering from targets $^{208}$Pb, $^{58}$Ni and $^{40}$Ca over a wide range of $30 \lesssim E_{\rm in}/A_{\rm P} \lesssim 200$ MeV by using the $g$-matrix folding model, where $A_{\rm P}$ is the mass number of projectile. In symmetric nuclear matter with positive energies, chiral 3NFs make the single-particle potential less attractive and more absorptive. The effects mainly come from the Fujita-Miyazawa 2$π$-exchange 3NF and slightly become larger as $E_{\rm in}$ increases. These effects persist in the optical potentials of $^{4}$He scattering. As for the differential cross sections of $^{4}$He scattering, chiral-3NF effects are large in $E_{\rm in}/A_{\rm P} \gtrsim 60$ MeV and improve the agreement of the theoretical results with the measured ones. Particularly in $E_{\rm in}/A_{\rm P} \gtrsim 100$ MeV, the folding model reproduces measured differential cross sections pretty well. Cutoff ($Λ$) dependence is investigated for both nuclear matter and $^{4}$He scattering by considering two cases of $Λ=450$ and $550$ MeV. The uncertainty coming from the dependence is smaller than chiral-3NF effects even at $E_{\rm in}/A_{\rm P}=175$ MeV.

## Key claim

Chiral three-nucleon forces (3NFs) make the single-particle potential in symmetric nuclear matter less attractive and more absorptive at positive energies, with effects that increase slightly with incident energy; these corrections persist in the optical potentials for 4He-nucleus elastic scattering and improve agreement with measured differential cross sections, particularly at incident energies per nucleon above 100 MeV, but the quantitative results depend on tuning the low-energy constants cD and cE to restore reasonable nuclear matter saturation properties rather than using values fixed solely from few-body systems.

## Method

The authors used the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) method with chiral two-nucleon forces at N3LO and chiral three-nucleon forces at NNLO to calculate the g matrix in symmetric nuclear matter. They then constructed a local version of the chiral g matrix (the "Kyushu chiral g matrix") parameterized in a 3-range Gaussian form and folded it with projectile and target densities using the double-folding model with target-density approximation to obtain optical potentials for 4He elastic scattering from 208Pb, 58Ni, and 40Ca targets.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy range studied: 30 less than or approximately equal to Ein/AP less than or approximately equal to 200 MeV, with experimental data available from 26 to 175 MeV per nucleon
- Cutoff values investigated: Lambda = 450 and 550 MeV
- Fermi momentum for peripheral density: kF = 1.2 fm^-1, corresponding to density realized in peripheral region of target nuclei
- Normal nuclear matter density: rho0 = 0.17 fm^-3 at kF = 1.35 fm^-1
- Low-energy constants for 3NFs: (c1, c3, c4) = (-0.81, -3.4, 3.4) in units of GeV^-1
- Tuned parameters: cD = -2.5 with cE = 0 for Lambda = 450 MeV; cD = -2.5 with cE = 0.25 for Lambda = 550 MeV
- Range parameters for central part of local g matrix: (0.4, 0.9, 2.5) in units of fm
- Chiral-3NF effects are large for Ein/AP greater than or approximately equal to 60 MeV and improve agreement with data; uncertainty from cutoff dependence is smaller than chiral-3NF effects even at Ein/AP = 175 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Toyokawa et al. (2015) and Minomo et al. (2014) that qualitatively discussed chiral-3NF effects on elastic scattering using a hybrid method, and corrects numerical errors found in earlier nuclear-matter calculations by Kohno (2013). The results are consistent with second-order perturbation calculations by Holt et al. (2013) and show that the Fujita-Miyazawa 2pi-exchange 3NF dominates the 3NF effects. The local version of the chiral g matrix (Kyushu chiral g matrix) enables systematic application to various nuclear reactions, building on the Melbourne g-matrix approach by Amos et al.
