# Large transverse momentum dilepton production in heavy ion collisions with two-photon processes

> The cold component of large transverse momentum dilepton production via semi-coherent two-photon interaction is calculated. The cold contribution is essential to the dilepton spectra in the soft region for different mass bins. The results are compared with the PHENIX experimental data at RHIC, and we find that the modification of semi-coherent two-photon processes is more evident with the rising dilepton mass bins.

# Large transverse momentum dilepton production in heavy ion collisions with two-photon processes

## Key claim

The semi-coherent two-photon process (gamma gamma -> e+ e-), where one photon carries large transverse momentum and the other is coherent with small transverse momentum, provides a significant "cold" contribution to dilepton spectra in the soft transverse momentum region (P_T < 1 GeV) for mass bins above 300 MeV, contrary to an earlier claim by Shuryak et al. that such processes do not contribute significantly to PHENIX data.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section for dilepton production via semi-coherent two-photon interactions in Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV/A, using the equivalent photon approximation with the MacDonald approximation for the photon spectrum. They impose the restriction that the impact parameter b is much smaller than 2R_perp (where quark-gluon plasma may be created) and apply a single-track acceptance condition requiring the minimum transverse momentum of one photon to be 0.2 GeV, which allows large transverse momentum dileptons to be produced. The yield is obtained by integrating the differential cross section over mass bins and normalizing by the total cross section.

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au 200 GeV/A collisions at RHIC: gamma = 106, R_min ~ 7 fm, and total cross section sigma_tot ~ 1.4 x 10^4 GeV^{-2}
- The minimum transverse momentum of photon q2 is q2Tmin ~ 0.2 GeV due to single track acceptance
- The two-photon contribution is valid only for P_T < 3 GeV due to the radiation limit condition omega < gamma/R_min
- In mass bins 300 MeV < m_ee < 500 MeV, 500 MeV < m_ee < 750 MeV, and 810 MeV < m_ee < 990 MeV, the data are higher by almost one order of magnitude than the expectation from cocktail and charm decays
- For mass bins 100 MeV < m_ee < 200 MeV and 200 MeV < m_ee < 300 MeV, the yield enhancement from two-photon processes is not evident

## Context

This work extends the discussion of cold dilepton production mechanisms first suggested by Shuryak et al. (2010) for the soft P_T region where pQCD is inapplicable, and addresses the puzzle from PHENIX collaboration data showing excess dilepton yield above cocktail and charm decays at P_T < 1 GeV with an effective slope T_eff ~ 100 MeV. The authors find that the semi-coherent two-photon process, which Shuryak et al. had concluded did not contribute significantly, becomes essential when the restriction b << 2R_perp is applied and the Woods-Saxon form factor approximation is avoided. This mechanism provides a complement to standard cold dilepton production and may help explain the low-mass dilepton enhancement observed at RHIC that cannot be fully accounted for by vector meson modification scenarios.
