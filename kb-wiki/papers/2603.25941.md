# Kaon Boer-Mulders function using a contact interaction

> Using a symmetry preserving treatment of a vector*vector contact interaction (SCI), results are delivered for the four kaon transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs), viz. helicity-independent (HI) and Boer-Mulders (BM) TMDs for the kaon's $u$, $s$ valence degrees of freedom. In completing this analysis, we are able to deliver insights into, amongst other things, the role played by emergent hadron mass (EHM) phenomena in producing these TMDs; the EHM modulating effect of the Higgs-boson coupling that produces the strange quark current mass; the impact of gauge link models on whether predictions satisfy the positivity constraint that bounds the BM function relative to the HI TMD; and the size of the BM shift and effects thereupon of off-diagonal terms in the associated scale-evolution kernel.

## Key claim

Using a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector×vector contact interaction (SCI), the authors deliver results for the four kaon transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs), but the predictions for the Boer-Mulders function depend on the choice of gauge link model; only a momentum-dependent gluon propagator model (rather than the pure SCI) yields results that satisfy the positivity constraint bounding the BM function relative to the helicity-independent TMD.

## Method

The authors compute the kaon helicity-independent and Boer-Mulders TMDs using a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector×vector contact interaction (SCI) within the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter equation framework. For the BM function, they implement two gauge link models: a pure SCI momentum-independent gluon propagator and a more realistic momentum-dependent gluon propagator with an infrared coupling constant α_L = 0.97π, and they derive the TMDs from both the correlation function formalism and the light-front wave function overlap representation.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of strange to light quark current masses is approximately 27:1, but the infrared ratio of running quark masses drops to 5:4
- The hadron-scale momentum fractions for the K+ are ⟨x u⟩ = 0.47 and ⟨x s̄⟩ = 0.53, summing to 1, with ratio ⟨x s̄⟩/⟨x u⟩ = 1.13
- The u-in-K TMD maximum at k⊥²=0 lies at x ≈ 0.3, shifted by roughly (4/5)² from x=1/2
- The gauge link coupling is set to α_L = 0.97π, known with 4% precision
- The ratio f_π/f_K = 0.84 reflects Higgs boson modulation of emergent hadron mass

## Context

This work extends the pion TMD study of Cheng (2024) to the kaon using the same SCI framework, addressing the question of how the large strange quark current mass (≈27 times the light quark mass) affects kaon TMD properties. It complements the few existing kaon TMD studies: Zhang (2024) computed helicity-independent kaon TMDs but omitted the BM function; Kaur (2020) used Gaussian Ansätze for light-front wave functions to compute both pion and kaon TMDs including the BM function but could not link Higgs couplings to kaon properties; and Lu (2026) used basis light-front quantisation for helicity-independent kaon TMDs only. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from next-generation high-luminosity accelerators and provide tests of the emergent hadron mass paradigm through empirical comparisons between kaon and pion properties.
