# Neutron star calculations with the phenomenological three-nucleon force

> In this work, we have studied the effect of three-nucleon interaction on the neutron stars structure. In our calculations, we have considered the neutron star matter as a beta-stable nuclear matter. We have put the results concerning the TBF effect in perspective against two-body results and other calculations of three-nucleon interactions, using the Urbana v14 potential and the parabolic approximation of the nuclear-matter energy for approximating the problem of asymmetric nuclear matter. As such, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation, we have estimated bulk properties of neutron stars and investigated how the present calculations would agree with the expected dynamical-stability condition.

# Neutron star calculations with the phenomenological three-nucleon force

## Key claim

The phenomenological three-nucleon force (TBF) constructed using the UVII model and Urbana v14 potential increases the maximum mass and radius of neutron stars compared to two-body-only calculations, but the specific form of the repulsive three-body term may not capture the full spin-isospin dependence needed to predict pure neutron matter at high densities, and the model cannot reproduce radii for observed neutron stars above approximately 1.59 solar masses without TBF inclusion.

## Method

The authors used the lowest order constrained variational method (LOCV) with the Urbana v14 two-nucleon potential and a phenomenological three-nucleon force based on the UVII model, which combines a two-pion-exchange attractive term and a repulsive term. They applied the parabolic approximation for asymmetric nuclear matter energy to derive the equation of state for beta-stable matter including electrons and muons, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation to obtain neutron star bulk properties such as mass, radius, central density, and dynamical stability conditions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: 0.178 fm$^{-3}$ for Uv14+TBF (compared to 0.364 fm$^{-3}$ for Uv14 alone); empirical value is 0.17 fm$^{-3}$
- Incompressibility: 193 MeV for Uv14+TBF (compared to 302 MeV for Uv14 alone); empirical value is 230$\pm$40 MeV
- Symmetry energy: 29.2 MeV for Uv14+TBF (compared to 44.8 MeV for Uv14 alone); empirical value is 32$\pm$1 MeV
- Maximum mass for beta-stable matter: 1.89 $M_{\odot}$ with TBF (radius 9.36 km, central density 3.26$\times$10$^{15}$ g/cm$^3$) versus 1.59 $M_{\odot}$ without TBF (radius 6.96 km, central density 5.37$\times$10$^{15}$ g/cm$^3$)
- Maximum mass for pure neutron matter: 1.91 $M_{\odot}$ with TBF (radius 9.59 km, central density 3.19$\times$10$^{15}$ g/cm$^3$) versus 1.50 $M_{\odot}$ without TBF (radius 8.13 km, central density 4.38$\times$10$^{15}$ g/cm$^3$)
- In the electrons-only case, the binding energy increases relative to the electrons-plus-muons case by a maximum of 7.8% (Uv14 at $\rho=0.67$ fm$^{-3}$) and 2.6% (Uv14+TBF at $\rho=0.59$ fm$^{-3}$)
- The proton density in the electrons-only case increases relative to the electrons-plus-muons case by a maximum of 33% (Uv14) and 34% (Uv14+TBF)

## Context

This work extends previous LOCV calculations by Moeini and Bordbar (2022) on symmetric nuclear matter with the UVII three-nucleon potential to the case of beta-stable and pure neutron matter relevant for neutron star structure. The results are compared with earlier two-body calculations by Bordbar and Riazi (2002) and Bordbar and Hayati (2006), and with other neutron star studies by Eslam Panah and Bordbar (2017), Bordbar and Rezaei (2013), and Bordbar and Karami (2022). The paper enables future comparisons with more realistic nucleon-nucleon models and with observations of high-mass neutron stars such as PSRJ0740+6620 (2.10 $M_{\odot}$), PSRJ0348+0432 (2.01 $M_{\odot}$), and PSRJ1614-2230 (1.97 $M_{\odot}$), though the present model cannot reproduce radii for masses above about 1.59 $M_{\odot}$ without TBF, suggesting possible quark-hadron phase transitions in the core.
