# Correlation search for coherent pion emission in heavy ion collisions

> The methods allowing to extract the coherent component of pion emission conditioned by the formation of a quasi-classical pion source in heavy ion collisions are suggested. They exploit a nontrivial modification of the quantum statistical and final state interaction effects on the correlation functions of like and unlike pions in the presence of the coherent radiation. The extraction of the coherent pion spectrum from pi+pi-, pi+pi+ and pi-pi- correlation functions and single--pion spectra is discussed in detail for large expanding systems produced in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions.

# Correlation search for coherent pion emission in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the presence of a coherent (quasi-classical) component of pion radiation in heavy ion collisions can be extracted from combined analysis of like-pion and unlike-pion correlation functions, provided one accounts for the super-selection rule requiring averaging over isospin orientations of the classical source. The method works specifically for large expanding systems produced in ultra-relativistic collisions, and the coherence suppression of identical-pion correlations determines the coherence enhancement of non-identical-pion correlations.

## Method

The authors construct a density matrix formalism that incorporates both chaotic (thermal) and coherent pion components, where the coherent component arises from a quasi-classical pion source such as a disoriented chiral condensate (DCC). They average over all orientations of the isovector of the classical source in isospin space to satisfy the super-selection rule for charged pions, and derive expressions for single-pion spectra and two-pion correlation functions including quantum statistical effects. The formalism is then extended to account for final state interactions and resonance decays, and the authors show how the coherent fraction can be extracted from the intercepts of correlation functions of like and unlike pions.

## Key numbers

- The coherent fraction of the single-pion spectrum is given by \( G(p) = D(p)/[1+D(p)] \), where \( D(p) \) is the ratio of coherent to chaotic yields; the factor 1/3 appears from averaging over isospin orientations: \( (4\pi)^{-1} \int d\Omega(\mathbf{e}) |e_i|^2 = 1/3 \).
- For purely chaotic emission, the quantum statistical correlation function intercept is \( C_{QS}(p,0) = 2 \); in the presence of coherent radiation, \( C_{QS}(p,0) < 2 \).
- The intercept of the pure QS correlation function for identical pions is suppressed by coherence, while the intercept for non-identical pions is enhanced, with the relation determined by the isospin averaging factors \( 9\langle |e_i e_j|^2 \rangle \).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of coherent pion production in the Gyulassy-Kauffmann-Wilson (GKW) model (Gyulassy, Kauffmann, Wilson, 1979) and the quantum-mechanical model of phase-correlated wave packets (Sinyukov, 1994). It also builds on the treatment of coherent processes in hadron-nucleus collisions via Glauber theory (Glauber, 1959) and the concept of coherent states introduced by Glauber (1963). The method enables experimental extraction of the coherent pion component from two-particle correlation data at RHIC and LHC energies, offering a way to distinguish coherence from other suppression mechanisms such as long-lived resonance decays, which was previously addressed by Heinz (1996) using three-particle correlations.
