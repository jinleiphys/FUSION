# Probing nuclear compressibility via fragmentation in Au+Au reactions at 35 AMeV

> The molecular dynamics study of fragmentation in peripheral $^{197}$Au +$^{197}$Au collisions at 35 MeV/nucleon is presented to probe the nuclear matter compressibility in low density regime. The yields of different fragment species, rapidity spectra, and multiplicities of charged particles with charge $3\leq Z \leq 80$ are analyzed at different peripheral geometries employing a soft and a hard equations of state. Fragment productions is found to be quite insensitive towards the choice of nucleon-nucleon cross sections allowing us to constrain nuclear matter compressibility. Comparison of calculated charged particle multiplicities with the experimental data indicates preference for the \emph{soft} nature of nuclear matter.

## Key claim
The study finds that charged-particle multiplicities from peripheral Au+Au collisions at 35 AMeV favor a *soft* equation of state (incompressibility κ=200 MeV) over a hard one (κ=380 MeV), but this conclusion is conditional on the use of the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model with simulated annealing clusterization and applies only to the low-density regime probed by spectator fragmentation.

## Method
The authors performed QMD model simulations of peripheral ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au collisions at 35 MeV/nucleon for six impact parameters (reduced impact parameters b/b_max from 0.55 to 0.85). They employed both a soft (κ=200 MeV) and a hard (κ=380 MeV) equation of state, and tested three different nucleon-nucleon cross sections (energy-dependent, constant 40 mb, and constant 55 mb) to verify insensitivity. Final fragments were identified using the simulated annealing clusterization algorithm (SACA) with a binding-energy criterion, and calculated charged-particle multiplicities (charge 3 ≤ Z ≤ 80) were compared to experimental data from the Multics-Miniball array.

## Key numbers
- Soft EoS incompressibility: κ = 200 MeV; hard EoS incompressibility: κ = 380 MeV (Table 1).
- Beam energy: Elab = 35 AMeV for ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au collisions.
- Reduced impact parameters studied: b/b_max = 0.55, 0.85 (and four others in Fig. 3).
- Binding energy threshold for fragment identification: Ebind = 4.0 MeV for fragments with Nf ≥ 3 (Eq. 8).
- Gaussian width parameter: √L = 1.8 fm (fixed for all nucleons).
- Saturation binding energy of infinite nuclear matter: E/A = -16 MeV at ρ₀.

## Context
This work extends earlier attempts to constrain the nuclear equation of state via giant monopole resonance studies (Young 1999, Itoh 2003) and collective flow measurements (Pan 1993, Part 1995), which had left large uncertainties. By focusing on low-energy peripheral collisions where nucleon-nucleon cross sections have minimal influence, the authors demonstrate that spectator fragmentation can serve as a clean probe of nuclear compressibility. The results support the softer incompressibility (κ≈200 MeV) previously favored by GMR studies and compound-nucleus formation data (Cib 1995), and enable future molecular-dynamics studies to use spectator decay observables as a constraint on the equation of state in the Fermi-energy domain.
