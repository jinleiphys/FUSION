# The N(1440) Roper resonance in the nuclear model with explicit mesons

> We show that the N(1440) Roper resonance naturally appears in the nuclear model with explicit mesons as a structure in the continuum spectrum of the physical proton which in this calculation is made of a bare nucleon dressed with a pion cloud.

## Key claim

The N(1440) Roper resonance appears as a structure in the continuum spectrum of the physical proton within the one-pion nuclear model with explicit mesons (MEM), where the physical proton is treated as a bare nucleon dressed with a pion cloud. The claim is conditional on the specific choice of the strength and range parameters of the Gaussian nucleon-pion coupling form-factor.

## Method

The author constructs a semi-relativistic Schrodinger equation for a two-component wave-function representing a bare nucleon and a bare nucleon plus one pion, with a coupling operator that conserves isospin, angular momentum, and parity. The system is discretized on a radial grid and solved as a real symmetric matrix eigenproblem, yielding a bound ground state (the physical proton) and discretized continuum states. A strength-function for a fictional transition from the ground state to continuum states is computed, and the resonance parameters (mass and width) are extracted by fitting the strength-function with an energy-dependent Breit-Wigner distribution.

## Key numbers

- The Roper resonance mass is reproduced at 1439-1440 MeV for three parameter sets, with widths of 260 MeV, 357 MeV, and 454 MeV, covering the tabulated range of 250-450 MeV.
- The binding energy of the virtual pion (B) ranges from 30 MeV to 72 MeV across the three parameter sets.
- The contribution of the one-pion state to the total norm of the physical proton wave-function (N_pi) ranges from 8% to 16%.
- The Gaussian form-factor range parameter (b_w) varies from 1.35 fm to 1.50 fm, and the strength parameter (S_w) varies from 63 MeV to 99 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear model with explicit mesons (MEM), previously applied to the sigma meson and gamma-pi processes by Fedorov and collaborators, to the description of the N(1440) Roper resonance. It contrasts with quark-based approaches and other models cited in the HADRON, solution, jose, and dynamic references. The results enable future studies of the Roper resonance within a framework that treats mesons explicitly on the same footing as nucleons, and the author notes that the extracted pion component (8-16%) is much smaller than estimates from pion photo-production cross-section calculations.
