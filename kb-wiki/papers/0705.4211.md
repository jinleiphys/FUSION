# Multiple scattering and energy loss in semi-inclusive deeply inelastic eA scattering

> We calculate the multiple scattering effect on single hadron production in semi-inclusive lepton-nucleus deeply inelastic scattering. We show that the quantum interference of multiple scattering amplitudes leads to suppression in hadron productions. At the leading power in medium length, the suppression can be approximately expressed in terms of a shift in $z$ of the fragmentation function $D(z)$, and could be therefore interpreted as the collisional energy loss. We compare our calculation with existing experimental data. We also discuss the effect of quark mass on the suppression. Our approach can be extended to other observables in hadronic collisions.

## Key claim

The quantum interference of multiple scattering amplitudes in semi-inclusive deeply inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering leads to suppression in hadron production, which at leading power in medium length can be approximately expressed as a shift in the momentum fraction \(z\) of the fragmentation function \(D(z)\) and interpreted as collisional energy loss, but this result is derived under the approximation of summing scatterings to all order and is not applicable at large \(z_h\) where hadron formation time effects become important.

## Method

The authors calculate the leading pole contribution from double scattering Feynman diagrams in semi-inclusive deeply inelastic scattering (SIDIS) on a quark state, then generalize to \(n\)th-order multiple scattering by summing over all possible insertions of gluon pairs to both sides of the final state cut line. Using a model of constant nuclear density and approximating the multi-field matrix element as a product of expectation values in a nucleon state, they sum the \(A^{1/3}\)-enhanced contributions to all orders, obtaining a resummed expression for the semi-inclusive hadronic tensor. The result is compared with HERMES experimental data for pion and kaon production on a krypton target, and predictions are made for D meson production.

## Key numbers

- The shift in \(z\) for the fragmentation function is given by \(\Delta z(z_h) = z_h \, \kappa^2 (A^{1/3} - 1) / Q^2\), where \(\kappa^2\) is a single parameter characterizing the quark-medium interaction scale.
- For heavy quarks with mass \(m\), the shift becomes \(\Delta z_m(z_h) = z_h \, [2/(1+\sqrt{1+4m^2/Q^2})]^2 \, \kappa^2 (A^{1/3} - 1) / Q^2\).
- The parameter \(\kappa^2\) is related to the \(\lambda^2\) in the LQS model by \(\kappa^2 = (4\pi^2\alpha_s/3)\lambda^2\), where \(\lambda^2\) has been estimated in the range \(0.01-0.1\) GeV\(^2\) from Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening and DIS momentum imbalance.
- The HERMES data used for comparison have \(x_B\) in the range \(0.084-0.1\) and \(Q^2 \sim 2.2-2.6\) GeV\(^2\).
- For the modified model at large \(z_h\), the shift is \(\Delta z(z_h) = z_h \, \kappa^2 (A^{1/3} - 1) / Q^2 \cdot (1-z_h)/(1-z_c)\) when \(z > z_c\), with \(z_c = 0.6\).

## Context

This work extends the study of parton energy loss in nuclear media, complementing the radiative energy loss mechanism described by Guo (2000) and Wang (2001) by showing that quantum interference of multiple scattering amplitudes without induced radiation also produces suppression that can be interpreted as collisional energy loss. The result is compared with HERMES experimental data and can be related to the twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function \(T_{qg}\) in the LQS model (Luo, Qiu, and Sterman). The approach can be extended to other observables in hadronic collisions, and the predictions for D meson production are relevant for future Electron-Ion Collider experiments.
