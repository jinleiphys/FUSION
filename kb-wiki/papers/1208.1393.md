# On holographic thermalization and gravitational collapse of massless scalar fields

> In this paper we study thermalization in a strongly coupled system via AdS/CFT. Initially, the energy is injected into the system by turning on a spatially homogenous scalar source coupled to a marginal composite operator. The thermalization process is studied by numerically solving Einstein's equations coupled to a massless scalar field in the Poincare patch of AdS_5. We define a thermalization time t_T on the AdS side, which has an interpretation in terms of a spacelike Wilson loop <W(l =1/T)> in CFT. Here T is the thermal equilibrium temperature. We study both cases with the source turned on in short(Delta t <= 1/T) and long(Delta t >= 1/T) durations. In the former case, the thermalization time t_T = g_t/T <= 1/T and the coefficient g_t = 0.73 in the limit Delta t <= 0.02/T. In the latter case, we find double- and multiple-collapse solutions, which may be interpreted as the gravity duals of two- or multi-stage thermalization in CFT. In all the cases our results indicate that such a strongly coupled system thermalizes in a typical time scale t_T=O(1)/T.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a strongly coupled CFT with a marginal scalar source, the thermalization time is \(t_T = O(1)/T\) regardless of whether the source is turned on for a short or long duration; for short pulses (\(\Delta t \lesssim 0.02/T\)) the coefficient is \(g_t \simeq 0.73\), and for longer pulses the system can exhibit two-stage or multi-stage thermalization.

## Method

The author numerically solves the Einstein-Klein-Gordon equations for a massless scalar field coupled to gravity in the Poincare patch of \(AdS_5\), using a third-order Adams-Bashforth time-marching scheme with either Chebyshev pseudo-spectral or finite difference methods. Energy is injected into the CFT vacuum by turning on a spatially homogeneous Gaussian scalar source \(\phi_0(t) = (\epsilon/a) e^{-a t^2}\) on the boundary, and the bulk metric is evolved from either vacuum AdS or AdS black hole initial conditions. The thermalization time \(t_T\) is defined as the first time when the metric function \(f(t,u)\) matches the AdS black hole form for \(u < u_{min}\) with \(f_{min}=0.01\) and \(u_{min}=0.997 u_0\), corresponding to a spacelike Wilson loop scale \(l \sim 1/T\).

## Key numbers

- For narrow waves with \(\Delta t \lesssim 0.02/T\), the thermalization time coefficient \(g_t = 0.73\) in the relation \(t_T = g_t/T\).
- For narrow waves satisfying \(\epsilon/a \lesssim 0.5\), the thermalization time satisfies \(g_t \lesssim 1.0\).
- The equilibrium temperature scales as \(T \simeq 0.455 \epsilon^{1/2}\) for \(\epsilon/a \lesssim 0.1\) (i.e., \(\Delta t \lesssim 0.14/T\)).
- For the narrow wave example with \(a=400\) and \(\epsilon=0.5\), the numerical thermalization time is \(t_T \simeq 2.29\) in units where \(u_0=1.00\).
- The estimated lower bound for thermalization time from causality is \(\Delta t_T \simeq 2.02 u_0 \simeq 0.64/T\).
- For broad waves, double-collapse solutions occur when \(\Delta t \gtrsim 1/T_L\) and \(\epsilon/a \gtrsim 3.0\), with the first collapse at \(t=0\) showing AdS black hole metric for \(u \leq 0.98 u_0\) at temperature \(T_L\).

## Context

This work extends perturbative calculations of holographic thermalization by Minwalla and collaborators (2009) to the fully nonlinear regime, using numerical methods similar to those applied in the global patch of \(AdS_{d+1}\) by Bizon (2011), Bizon (2012), Garfinkle (2012), and Garfinkle (2012). The results confirm the top-down thermalization picture proposed by Lin and Shuryak (2008) and by holographic thermalization studies, in contrast to bottom-up thermalization in perturbative QCD. The findings enable future quantitative studies of thermalization times for non-local operators such as spacelike Wilson loops, as discussed in holographic thermalization literature.
