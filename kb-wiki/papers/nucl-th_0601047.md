# Medium modifications of the nucleon-nucleon elastic cross section in neutron-rich intermediate energy HICs

> Several observables of unbound nucleons which are to some extent sensitive to the medium modifications of nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections in neutron-rich intermediate energy heavy ion collisions are investigated. The splitting effect of neutron and proton effective masses on cross sections is discussed. It is found that the transverse flow as a function of rapidity, the $Q_{zz}$ as a function of momentum, and the ratio of halfwidths of the transverse to that of longitudinal rapidity distribution $R_{t/l}$ are very sensitive to the medium modifications of the cross sections. The transverse momentum distribution of correlation functions of two-nucleons does not yield information on the in-medium cross section.

## Key claim

The medium modifications of nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections in neutron-rich intermediate energy heavy ion collisions can be probed by several observables, but the difference between the "Dirac" and "NR" types of neutron-proton effective mass splitting on these observables is very small, suggesting that more sensitive observables are needed to explore these splitting effects.

## Method

The authors used the updated UrQMD transport model with a soft equation of state and a symmetry potential energy depending linearly on nuclear density. They simulated neutron-rich reactions ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr and ⁷⁸Ni+⁹⁶Zr at beam energy 100A MeV for reduced impact parameters b/b₀=0 and 0.5, with 2·10⁵ events per reaction. The in-medium cross sections were factorized as a product of a medium correction factor F(u,α,p) and the free NN elastic scattering cross sections, with three cases considered: "NoMed" (free cross sections), "PartMed" (density and isospin splitting effects), and "FullMed" (adding momentum constraints). A conventional phase-space coalescence model with P₀=0.3 GeV/c and R₀=3.5 fm was used to construct clusters, with freeze-out time 150 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: E_b = 100A MeV for both ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr and ⁷⁸Ni+⁹⁶Zr reactions
- Reduced impact parameters: b/b₀ = 0 and 0.5, where b₀ = R_proj + R_targ
- Initial isospin asymmetry: α ≃ 0.167 for ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr; α ≃ 0.218 for ⁷⁸Ni+⁹⁶Zr
- Medium correction factor at u=2: F_{u=2} = 0.35 from the density-dependent scaling factor
- Momentum cutoff for medium effects: p_NN > 1 GeV/c for disappearance of density and splitting effects
- Coalescence parameters: P₀ = 0.3 GeV/c, R₀ = 3.5 fm
- Freeze-out time: 150 fm/c
- For the ratio R_{t/l}, medium modification effect is more than 20% (from "NoMed" to "FullMed"), while the difference between "FullMed-NR" and "FullMed-Dirac" is only about 2%
- For two-nucleon correlation functions, 10⁹ neutron-neutron and proton-neutron pairs and 10⁸ proton-proton pairs were analyzed for P_T < 500 MeV/c and q < 50 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium NN elastic cross sections based on the extended QHD model including ρ and δ mesons (Li 2003) and DBHF calculations (Sammarruca 2005), which give opposite predictions for the splitting of σ_nn* and σ_pp* in neutron-rich matter. The paper builds on earlier work by Li (2005) on sensitive probes of medium modifications and by Chen (2003) on two-nucleon correlation functions. The results enable future experimental searches at facilities like FAIR and RIA to distinguish between different theoretical predictions of the isospin dependence of in-medium cross sections, though the authors note that the splitting effects from Dirac versus NR mass definitions are too small to be distinguished with the observables studied.
