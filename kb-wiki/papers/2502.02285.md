# Absorption of $ψ(2S)$ mesons in nuclei

> In the present work we explore the inclusive $ψ(2S)$ meson photoproduction from nuclei near the kinematic threshold within the collision model, based on the nuclear spectral function, for incoherent direct photon--nucleon charmonium creation processes. The model takes into account the final $ψ(2S)$ absorption, target nucleon binding and Fermi motion. We calculate the absolute and relative excitation functions for production of $ψ(2S)$ mesons on $^{12}$C and $^{184}$W target nuclei at near-threshold photon beam energies of 8.0--16.4 GeV, the absolute momentum differential cross sections for their production off these target nuclei at laboratory polar angles of 0$^{\circ}$--10$^{\circ}$, the momentum dependence of the ratio of these cross sections as well as the A-dependences of the ratios (transparency ratios) of the total cross cross sections for $ψ(2S)$ production at photon energy of 13 GeV within the different scenarios for the $ψ(2S)N$ absorption cross section. We also calculate the A-dependence of the ratio of $ψ(2S)$ and $J/ψ$ photoproduction transparency ratios at photon energies around of 11.5 GeV within the adopted scenarios for this cross section. We demonstrate that both the absolute and relative observables considered reveal a definite sensitivity to these scenarios. Therefore, the measurement of such observables in future experiments at the upgraded up to 22 GeV CEBAF facility in the near-threshold energy region might shed light both on the $ψ(2S)N$ absorption cross section and on its part associated with the nondiagonal process $ψ(2S)+N \to J/ψ+N$ at finite momenta, which are of crucial importance in understanding of charmonium production and suppression in high-energy nucleus--nucleus collisions in a search for the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that absolute and relative observables (excitation functions, momentum distributions, transparency ratios) for near-threshold \(\psi(2S)\) photoproduction on nuclei are sensitive to the \(\psi(2S)N\) absorption cross section, but this sensitivity is conditional on the assumption that the genuine inelastic cross section dominates over the nondiagonal \(\psi(2S)+N \to J/\psi+N\) process at finite momenta, which is estimated to be only 0.2–0.3 mb.

## Method

The author uses a nuclear spectral function approach within a first-collision model for incoherent direct photon–nucleon charmonium creation, accounting for target nucleon binding, Fermi motion, and final-state \(\psi(2S)\) absorption. The free \(\gamma p \to \psi(2S)p\) cross section near threshold is estimated by scaling the \(J/\psi\) photoproduction cross section (parametrized from two-gluon and three-gluon exchange fits to GlueX data) by the ratio 0.166, assuming equal excess energies above each respective threshold. Calculations are performed for \(\psi(2S)N\) absorption cross sections of 0, 7, 14, 21, and 28 mb, using local proton and neutron densities for target nuclei from carbon to uranium.

## Key numbers

- The free \(\gamma p \to \psi(2S)p\) total cross section is predicted to be of order 0.1–1 nb for center-of-mass energies \(W \sim 5.0\)–6.5 GeV (corresponding to photon energies \(\sim 10.93\)–16.4 GeV).
- The \(\psi(2S)N\) absorption cross section scenarios adopted are 7, 14, 21, and 28 mb, with an additional zero-absorption case; these are motivated by QCD expectations that the \(\psi(2S)N\) cross section is a factor of 2–4 larger than the \(J/\psi N\) cross section of 3.5 mb from SLAC.
- The nondiagonal \(\psi(2S)+N \to J/\psi+N\) inelastic cross section is estimated at 0.2–0.3 mb, one to two orders of magnitude smaller than the genuine \(\psi(2S)N\) inelastic cross section of 7.5–8.5 mb from the generalized vector meson dominance model.
- The \(\psi(2S)\) meson laboratory momenta in the direct process on a free proton at rest at \(E_\gamma = 13\) GeV range from 7.478 to 12.058 GeV/c.
- The maximum laboratory angle for \(\psi(2S)\) production on a free proton at rest at \(E_\gamma = 13\) GeV is about 5.0°.
- The exponential \(t\)-slope parameter for \(\psi(2S)\) production is taken as \(b_{\psi(2S)} \approx 2.0\) GeV\(^{-2}\) at \(E_\gamma = 13\) GeV, consistent with a LHCb fit value of 1.97 GeV\(^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends the spectral-function-based collision model previously applied to \(X(3872)\) photoproduction (Paryev, 2023) to the \(\psi(2S)\) case, providing predictions for future measurements at the upgraded CEBAF facility with a 22 GeV photon beam (proposed in 2020–2022). It addresses the poorly known \(\psi(2S)N\) interaction cross section, which is crucial for disentangling cold nuclear matter effects from quark-gluon plasma signatures in heavy-ion collisions, where \(\psi(2S)\) suppression relative to \(J/\psi\) has been observed at SPS, RHIC, and LHC. The results enable extraction of the genuine \(\psi(2S)N\) absorption cross section from photoproduction data, complementing indirect estimates from hadronic collisions and theoretical models such as the generalized vector meson dominance model (Huefner and Kopeliovich, 2003) and effective Lagrangian approaches (Lin and Ko, 2000).
