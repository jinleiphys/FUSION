# Ab initio nonperturbative calculation of physical observables in light-front dynamics. Application to the Yukawa model

> We present a coherent and operational strategy to calculate, in a nonperturbative way, physical observables in light-front dynamics. This strategy is based on the decomposition of the state vector of any compound system in Fock components, and on the covariant formulation of light-front dynamics, together with the so-called Fock sector dependent renormalization scheme. We apply our approach to the calculation of the electromagnetic form factors of a fermion in the Yukawa model, in the nontrivial three-body Fock space truncation, for rather large values of the coupling constant. We find that, once the renormalization conditions are properly taken into account, the form factors do not depend on the regularization scale, when the latter is much larger than the physical masses. We then extend the Fock space by including antifermion degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a truncated Fock space, the bare coupling constant and counterterms must become functions of the kinematical variable \(x\) (the longitudinal momentum fraction) to satisfy renormalization conditions, and that this \(x\)-dependence restores rotational invariance and regularization-scale independence of observables, a refinement over the authors' prior work (Karmanov et al., 2010) where these parameters were treated as constants.

## Method

The authors applied covariant light-front dynamics (CLFD) with the Fock sector dependent renormalization (FSDR) scheme to the Yukawa model, truncating the Fock space to include the one-fermion (\(f\)), one-fermion-plus-one-boson (\(fb\)), and one-fermion-plus-two-bosons (\(fbb\)) sectors. They solved the coupled integral equations for the two-body vertex function \(\Gamma_2\) with Pauli-Villars regularization, enforcing renormalization conditions that forced the bare parameters \(g_{03}\) and \(Z_\omega^{(3)}\) to become \(x\)-dependent. They then extended the Fock space by including the \(ff\bar{f}\) sector (antifermion degrees of freedom) and examined the resulting reduction of the \(x\)-dependence.

## Key numbers

* The anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of the fermion was calculated for coupling constant \(\alpha \equiv g^2/4\pi\) of order 0.2 and 0.5; at \(\alpha \sim 0.5\) the earlier constant-parameter calculation (Karmanov et al., 2010) showed weak residual dependence on the Pauli-Villars boson mass \(\mu_1\), whereas the present \(x\)-dependent parameter method removes this dependence.
* The on-shell condition for the two-body vertex uses \(R_\perp^* = i\sqrt{x^2 m^2 + (1-x)\mu^2}\) (Eq. R*), where \(m\) is the fermion mass and \(\mu\) the boson mass.
* The renormalized two-body vertex condition requires \(b_2^{(N)}(s_2=M^2) \equiv 0\) and \(b_1^{(N)}(s_2=M^2) \equiv g \sqrt{I_1^{(N-1)}}\) (Eqs. b2oms, b1oms), with \(I_1^{(N-1)}\) being the norm of the one-body sector in the \(N-1\) truncation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous nonperturbative CLFD calculations (Karmanov et al., 2004, 2007, 2008, 2010) by fully incorporating the \(x\)-dependence of bare parameters that arises from Fock space truncation, which was only partially accounted for in the 2010 calculation of the anomalous magnetic moment. The approach enables reliable calculation of electromagnetic form factors for coupling constants up to \(\alpha \sim 0.5\) without residual regularization-scale dependence, and the inclusion of antifermion degrees of freedom (the \(ff\bar{f}\) sector) is shown to substantially reduce the \(x\)-dependence of the bare parameters, indicating improved convergence toward the exact theory. This provides a systematic path for nonperturbative calculations in light-front dynamics applicable to more realistic field theories.
