# Structure of odd Ge isotopes with $40 < N < 50$

> We have interpreted recently measured experimental data of $^{77}$Ge, and also for $^{73,75,79,81}$Ge isotopes in terms of state-of-the-art shell model calculations. Excitation energies, B(2) values, quadrupole moments and magnetic moments are compared with experimental data when available. The calculations have been performed with the recently derived interactions, namely with JUN45 and jj44b for ${f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}}$ space. We have also performed calculation for ${fpg_{9/2}}$ valence space using an ${fpg}$ effective interaction with $^{48}$Ca core and imposing a truncation to study the importance of the proton excitations across the Z=28 shell in this region. The predicted results of jj44b interaction are in good agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that shell-model calculations using the jj44b interaction provide the best overall agreement with experimental data for odd Ge isotopes with 40 < N < 50, particularly for nuclei further from the closed shell, but this agreement is qualified by the fact that all three interactions tested (JUN45, jj44b, and fpg) fail to predict the correct ground state for 73Ge and show varying degrees of success across different isotopes and observables.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations for 73,75,77,79,81Ge in two model spaces: the full f5/2pg9/2 space using the JUN45 and jj44b effective interactions with a 56Ni core, and the fpg9/2 space using an fpg effective interaction with a 48Ca core, allowing up to four particle excitations from the f7/2 orbital. Calculations were carried out using the shell-model code ANTOINE at the KanBalam computational facility, computing excitation energies, B(E2) transition probabilities, quadrupole moments, and magnetic moments for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For 73Ge, the 5/2+1 level is predicted too high relative to 7/2+1 and 9/2+1 by all three interactions, with the triplet of levels experimentally very close but calculated with significant splitting
- For 75Ge, the jj44b interaction predicts the experimental 1/2-2 level at 885 keV to be at 1381 keV, 3/2-3 at 1137 keV to be at 1569 keV, and (5/2-3) at 1241 keV to be at 1210 keV
- For 77Ge, the 5/2+1 level is predicted by JUN45 only 6 keV higher than experiment, by jj44b 181 keV higher, and by fpg 161 keV lower than experiment
- For 77Ge, the experimental 7/2+2 level at 761 keV is predicted with only 3 keV difference by JUN45, while jj44b predicts 971 keV and fpg predicts 1079 keV
- For 79Ge, the 9/2+1 state has a ν(g9/2^7) configuration with probability 34% (JUN45) and 35% (jj44b)
- For 81Ge, the 9/2+1 state has a ν(g9/2^9) configuration with probability 42% (JUN45), 36% (jj44b), and 80% (fpg)
- Effective charges of e_p=1.5 and e_n=0.5 were used for B(E2) calculations

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of Ge isotopes by Honma et al. (2009) using JUN45 and by Yoshinaga using pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole interactions, and addresses the importance of proton excitations across the Z=28 shell as suggested by Cheal et al. (2010) for Ga isotopes. The calculations enable comparison with recent experimental data from the ATLAS facility for 77Ge reported by Kay et al. (2009), and the results suggest that the recently developed jj44b interaction performs best for nuclei far from closed shells, while the inclusion of the f7/2 orbital in the fpg space improves electromagnetic properties. The authors note that a modern effective interaction in the fpg9/2d5/2 space developed by the Strasbourg-Madrid group (Lenzi et al., 2009) remains to be tested for Z>28.
