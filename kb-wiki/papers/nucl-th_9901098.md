# Status and Future Prospects in Searches for New Interactions in Neutron and Nuclear Beta-decay, Muon- and Pion-decay

> The interest, the status and the perspectives of various experiments in neutron and nuclear beta-decay, muon-decay and pion-decays are discussed. The talk is segmented into a discussion of the decay-rates and of the energy-spectra and correlations. The impact on various scenarios of "new physics" is briefly mentioned; left-right symmetric models are discussed in more detail and the informations gained from the considered experiments is compared to those from other sources.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the status and future prospects of precision experiments in neutron, nuclear beta-, muon-, and pion-decays, arguing that these low-energy measurements provide complementary and sometimes more stringent constraints on new physics (such as left-right symmetric models) than direct collider searches, particularly when the gauge-coupling ratio or the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix elements in the right-handed sector differ from those in the left-handed sector.

## Method

The talk surveys experimental results and ongoing projects across several decay channels: muon-decay (lifetime and Michel parameters), pion beta-decay (branching ratio), the ratio of electron to muon decay of the pion, neutron lifetime, electron-neutrino directional correlations in nuclear beta-decay, and polarization observables (neutron beta-decay asymmetries, beta-decay asymmetry/polarization correlations in nuclear and muon decays). For each, the author summarizes the current precision, the experimental techniques (e.g., fiber scintillation targets, 4π spectrometers, ultracold neutron traps, electromagnetic traps for radioactive nuclei), and the projected improvements. The constraints from these experiments are then compared to those from collider searches and other sources (e.g., SN1987A, K_L-K_S mass difference) within the framework of manifest and generalized left-right symmetric models.

## Key numbers

- Muon decay constant (G_F) relative precision: 17 ppm (9 ppm experimental, 15 ppm from 2-loop corrections); after Ritbergen (1998) the experimental error dominates.
- Muon lifetime measurement planned at PSI: precision of 2 ps (order-of-magnitude improvement).
- Pion beta-decay branching ratio: 10^{-8}; first-phase goal of 0.5% precision.
- Ratio of electron/muon decay rate of the pion: (1.2312 ± 0.0037)×10^{-4} (TRIUMF+PSI combination); Standard Model prediction: (1.2352 ± 0.0005)×10^{-4}; yields g_e/g_μ = 0.9989 ± 0.0016.
- Neutron lifetime (Bondarenko et al., communicated by Geltenbort): τ_n = 885.4 ± 0.9 (stat) ± 0.4 (syst) s; world average: τ_n = 885.8 ± 0.9 s.
- Electron-neutrino correlation coefficient in ^6He decay (corrected): a = 0.3310 ± 0.0030; pure axial expectation: a = 0.3333; tensor coupling strength limit: ≤ 0.8% of axial (68% CL).
- Neutron asymmetry A_n: four latest results: -0.1160 ± 0.0014, -0.1189 ± 0.0012, -0.1135 ± 0.0014, -0.1187 ± 0.0008; weighted average: -0.11721 ± 0.00053 (χ²/n = 3.3).
- Neutrino asymmetry B_n (Kuznetsov et al.): B_n = 0.9796 ± 0.0044; world average: B_n = 0.9816 ± 0.0040; deviation from V-A prediction: Δ = 0.0064 ± 0.0041.
- Muon-decay asymmetry parameter P_μ ξ δ/ρ = 0.99790 ± 0.00088 (Jodidio et al., 1986); 2.4σ from Standard Model value of 1.
- Lower limit on right-handed gauge boson mass from combined nuclear beta-decay tests: 320 GeV (90% CL); ISOLDE experiment aims for ~450 GeV.
- Muon-decay asymmetry measurement (Jodidio et al.) mass limit: 430 GeV (for ξ=0); TRIUMF E614 aims for 800 GeV.
- PSI R-97.06 muon-decay experiment: first phase expected upper limit of 500 GeV.
- K_L-K_S mass difference constraint in manifest left-right models: 1.6 TeV (Beall, 1982) or >3 TeV (Bigi, 1986).
- Direct collider limits on second gauge boson: CDF 650 GeV (95% CL); DO 720 GeV (95% CL).

## Context

This review extends the discussions of Towner (this conference) on superallowed Fermi decays and the unitarity of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix, and of Herczeg (1995) on exotic fermions, charged Higgs, s-lepton, and leptoquark exchange. It compares the constraints from low-energy beta-decay and muon-decay experiments with those from collider searches (CDF, DO) and astrophysical limits (SN1987A, Mohapatra 1989). The paper enables future work by identifying the most promising experimental directions (e.g., traps at TRIUMF, LANL, ANL, ISOLDE; the LANSCE neutron asymmetry experiment; the PSI muon-decay experiments) and by showing how relative measurements (polarization/asymmetry correlations) can complement absolute ones in constraining generalized left-right symmetric models, especially when gauge couplings or Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix elements differ between helicity sectors.
