# Thermal Bottomonium Suppression at RHIC and LHC

> In this paper we consider the suppression of bottomonium states in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. We compute the suppression as a function of centrality, rapidity, and transverse momentum for the states Upsilon(1s), Upsilon(2s), Upsilon(3s), chi_b1, and chi_b2. Using this information, we then compute the inclusive Upsilon(1s) suppression as a function of centrality, rapidity, and transverse momentum including feed down effects. Calculations are performed for both RHIC sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV Au-Au collisions and LHC sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions. From the comparison of our theoretical results with data available from the STAR and CMS Collaborations we are able to constrain the shear viscosity to entropy ratio to be in the range 0.08 < eta/S < 0.24. Our results are consistent with the creation of a high temperature quark-gluon plasma at both RHIC and LHC collision energies.

## Key claim

The authors constrain the shear viscosity to entropy ratio of the quark-gluon plasma to the range 0.08 < eta/S < 0.24 by comparing their theoretical calculations of bottomonium suppression with data from the STAR and CMS Collaborations, but this constraint depends on the specific potential model and dynamical framework (anisotropic hydrodynamics) used.

## Method

The authors compute the suppression of bottomonium states (Upsilon(1s), Upsilon(2s), Upsilon(3s), chi_b1, chi_b2) by solving the Schrodinger equation with a complex heavy-quark potential that includes both real and imaginary parts, where the real part is modeled as a Debye-screened Coulomb plus linear potential with an anisotropic screening mass parametrized from numerical integration. The temporal evolution of the plasma temperature and momentum-space anisotropy is provided by a (1+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) framework, and the nuclear modification factor R_AA is computed from the spatial and proper-time dependence of the binding energies, including feed-down contributions from excited states to the inclusive Upsilon(1s) suppression.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity to entropy ratio constrained to 0.08 < eta/S < 0.24 from comparison of theoretical results with STAR and CMS data.
* Initial maximum central temperatures: T0 ~ 450 MeV for RHIC sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV Au-Au collisions and T0 ~ 550 MeV for LHC sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions.
* For the upcoming full energy LHC heavy ion runs with sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV, expected T0 ~ 700-800 MeV.
* String tension used in the potential: sigma = 0.223 GeV^2, obtained from fits to lattice data.
* Short-range Coulomb parameter a = 0.385, consistent with the short range part of the heavy quark potential measured on the lattice.
* Isotropic Debye mass uses a factor of (1.4)^2 to account for higher-order corrections measured in lattice simulations.
* Three-loop running coupling alpha_s with Lambda_MSbar = 344 MeV gives alpha_s(5 GeV) = 0.2034.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Strickland (2011) which computed thermal suppression of Upsilon(1s) and chi_b1 at LHC energies, now including Upsilon(2s), Upsilon(3s), and chi_b2 states at both RHIC and LHC energies. The calculations use an improved potential model compared to Margotta et al. (2011) and Strickland (2011), incorporating running coupling and an improved parametrization of the short-range anisotropic potential, and the results are compared with experimental data from the STAR and CMS Collaborations to extract the shear viscosity to entropy ratio.
