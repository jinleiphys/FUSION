# Particle Number Fluctuations and Correlations in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> Particle number fluctuations and correlations in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS and RHIC energies are studied within the statistical hadron-resonance gas model in different statistical ensembles and in the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport approach. Event-by-event fluctuations of the proton to pion and kaon to proton number ratios are calculated in the HSD model for the samples of most central collision events and compared with the available experimental data. The role of the experimental acceptance and centrality selection is discussed.

## Key claim

The Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport model reproduces the sign and magnitude of dynamical fluctuations for the proton-to-pion ratio at SPS energies, but strongly overestimates the preliminary STAR data for that ratio at RHIC energies, while showing qualitative agreement for the kaon-to-proton ratio at RHIC.

## Method

The authors calculated event-by-event scaled variances and correlation coefficients for pions, kaons, and protons in central Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions using the statistical hadron-resonance gas model in the grand canonical, canonical, and micro-canonical ensembles, and in the HSD transport approach. They then computed the dynamical fluctuation measure σ^{dyn} for the p/π and K/p ratios from HSD, applying the same experimental acceptance cuts and centrality selections (3.5% most central at SPS, 5% most central at RHIC) as the NA49 and STAR experiments, and compared these results to the published data.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 17.3 GeV (SPS), HSD gives σ^{dyn}_{pπ} negative, in agreement with NA49 data; the HSD scaled variance ω_π at b=0 is 1.619, and ω_p is 0.641.
- For Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV (RHIC), HSD gives large positive σ^{dyn}_{pπ} that strongly overestimates preliminary STAR data; the HSD scaled variance ω_π at b=0 is 5.838, and ω_p is 1.122.
- For the K/p ratio, HSD results show a weak energy dependence within SPS and RHIC regions, but a "jump" of about 3% in σ^{dyn}_{Kp} when switching from NA49 to STAR acceptance at 160 A GeV.
- The statistical model in the micro-canonical ensemble gives ω_p values ranging from 0.446 at √s_NN = 6.27 GeV to 0.894 at √s_NN = 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of pion and kaon fluctuations (Konchakovski et al., 2008) to include protons and proton correlations, comparing the statistical hadron-resonance gas model with the HSD transport approach. The results are compared to NA49 data for Pb+Pb at SPS energies and preliminary STAR data for Au+Au at RHIC energies. The paper demonstrates that experimental acceptance and centrality selection strongly affect fluctuation observables, especially at RHIC energies, and that the simple acceptance scaling formula underestimates the scaled variance at those energies. The findings enable future comparisons with upcoming data from NA49 for K/p fluctuations and with new STAR data to clarify the discrepancy for p/π fluctuations at RHIC.
