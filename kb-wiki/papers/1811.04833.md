# Does chiral perturbation theory rule out QCD-based solutions to the strong CP problem?

> The conventional view is that a solution of the strong CP problem lies beyond QCD. A strong argument supporting this view is that the chiral expansion shows that observables depend on theta (unless a quark mass is zero); this eliminates the possibility that theta is physically irrelevant and appears to necessitate an explanation beyond the standard model. However, scenarios that solve the strong CP problem exist that are consistent with known chiral behavior; in these, QCD becomes nonviable as a theory for nonzero theta. Such scenarios appear to be compatible with lattice studies of the topological susceptibility.

### Key claim
Chiral perturbation theory (χPT) does not definitively rule out QCD-based solutions to the strong CP problem, because a loophole exists: if the vacuum energy density ε(θ̅) is ill-defined for real θ̅ away from zero (mod 2π) but well-defined for imaginary θ̅, and is nonanalytic at θ̅=0 in a way subleading to all orders in χPT, then nonzero topological cumulants from χPT can be reconciled with a scenario where QCD is nonviable for nonzero θ̅.

### Method
The paper analyzes the topological susceptibility and higher-order cumulants of topological charge in two-flavor QCD, using the standard chiral Lagrangian to leading order in the quark masses and the chiral expansion. It constructs an explicit illustrative functional form for the vacuum energy density ε(θ̅) that satisfies nine enumerated conditions, including that ε(θ̅) is finite for imaginary θ̅ but divergent for real θ̅ away from θ̅=0 (mod 2π), and that its chiral expansion is asymptotic with a zero radius of convergence. The argument then examines whether lattice calculations of topological cumulants could provide indirect evidence for such behavior.

### Key numbers
- The current bound on the neutron electric dipole moment is −3.2×10⁻²⁶ e·cm < dₙ < 2.8×10⁻²⁶ e·cm (90% C.L.), leading to an estimate |θ̅| ≲ 10⁻¹¹.
- The up quark mass differs from zero by more than 20 standard deviations based on lattice studies, ruling out mᵤ=0 scenarios.
- In the illustrative model, the ratio fₙ ≡ |c⁽²ⁿ⁺²⁾| / [(n+1)(n+2)|c⁽²ⁿ⁾|] grows linearly with n for large n, with onset of this behavior near n=30 for Λ=300 MeV and near n=110 for Λ=400 MeV.
- The illustrative model requires accurate topological cumulants up to at least c⁽⁶²⁾ (for Λ=300 MeV) or c⁽²²²⁾ (for Λ=400 MeV) before the divergence becomes noticeable.

### Context
This work extends the analysis of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979), who used χPT to argue that θ̅ dependence of observables rules out QCD-based solutions to the strong CP problem. It also relates to the 1/N_c expansion of Witten (1979) and Veneziano (1979), which can mislead for small quark masses. The paper proposes that QCD could solve the strong CP problem if the theory is nonviable for nonzero θ̅, analogous to triviality in φ⁴ theory (Luscher, 1987). It suggests that future lattice studies of topological cumulants or resurgence methods (Aniceto, 2018; Dunne, 2016) might test this scenario, though direct lattice calculations of ε(θ̅) are precluded by a sign problem.
