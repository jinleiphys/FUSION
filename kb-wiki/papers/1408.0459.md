# Mirror symmetry breaking in He isotopes and their mirror nuclei

> We study the mirror symmetry breaking of $^6$He-$^6$Be and $^8$He-$^8$C using the $^4$He + $X$N ($X$=2, 4) cluster model. The many-body resonances are treated for the correct boundary condition using the complex scaling method. We find that the ground state radius of $^8$C is larger than that of $^8$He due to the Coulomb repulsion in $^8$C. On the other hand, the $0^+_2$ resonances of the two nuclei exhibit the inverse relation; the $^8$C radius is smaller than the $^8$He radius. This is due to the Coulomb barrier of the valence protons around the $^4$He cluster core in $^8$C, which breaks the mirror symmetry of the radius in the two nuclei. A similar variation in the radius is obtained in the mirror nuclei, $^6$He and $^6$Be. A very large spatial extension of valence nucleons is observed in the $0^+_2$ states of $^8$He and $^8$C. This property is related to the dominance of the $(p_{3/2})^2(p_{1/2})^2$ configuration for four valence nucleons, which is understood from the reduction in the strength of the couplings to other configurations by involving the spatially extended components of valence nucleons.

## Key claim

The mirror symmetry between $^8$He and $^8$C is broken in the $0^+_2$ resonances, where the $^8$C radius becomes smaller than the $^8$He radius, contrary to the ground-state trend; this inversion is attributed to the Coulomb barrier of valence protons around the $^4$He core in $^8$C, which prevents spatial extension of the proton wave function.

## Method

The authors employed a $^4$He + $XN$ ($X=2,4$) cluster-orbital shell model (COSM) to describe $^6$He-$^6$Be and $^8$He-$^8$C systems, using Gaussian basis functions for single-particle states with length parameters up to about 40 fm. Many-body resonances were treated with the complex scaling method (CSM) to impose correct boundary conditions for unbound states, and the Hamiltonian included the KKNN potential for $^4$He-$N$ interactions and the Minnesota potential for $N$-$N$ interactions.

## Key numbers

- $^8$He ground state: energy $-3.22$ MeV relative to $^4$He + $4n$ threshold (experiment: $-3.11$ MeV); matter radius $R_{\rm m} = 2.52$ fm
- $^8$C ground state: energy $3.32$ MeV relative to $^4$He + $4p$ threshold (experiment: $3.449(30)$ MeV); matter radius $R_{\rm m} = 2.81-0.08i$ fm (real part 12% larger than $^8$He)
- $^8$He $0^+_2$ resonance: energy $3.07$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 3.19$ MeV; matter radius $R_{\rm m} = 7.56 + 2.04i$ fm
- $^8$C $0^+_2$ resonance: energy $8.88$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 6.64$ MeV; matter radius $R_{\rm m} = 4.87 + 0.13i$ fm (smaller than $^8$He $0^+_2$)
- $^8$He $0^+_2$: neutron radius $R_n = 8.53 + 2.32i$ fm; $^8$C $0^+_2$: proton radius $R_p = 5.46 + 0.15i$ fm
- $^8$He ground state charge radius: $R_{\rm ch} = 1.92$ fm (experiment: $1.959(16)$ fm)
- $^6$He ground state: energy $-0.975$ MeV relative to $^4$He + $2n$ threshold; $^6$Be ground state: energy $1.383$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 0.041$ MeV (experiment: $1.370$ MeV, $0.092$ MeV)
- $^6$He $0^+_2$: energy $3.88$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 8.76$ MeV; $^6$Be $0^+_2$: energy $5.95$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 11.21$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous COSM+CSM analyses of He isotopes and their mirror nuclei (Myo 2010, Myo 2013) and their study of mirror symmetry breaking in $A=7$ systems (Myo 2011). The results contradict the dineutron-cluster model of Kobayashi (2013), which predicted dineutron clustering in $^8$He($0^+_2$) under bound-state approximation; the present CSM treatment finds instead a nearly pure $(p_{3/2})^2(p_{1/2})^2$ single-particle configuration for the $0^+_2$ states. The findings enable future experimental searches for the predicted $0^+_2$ resonances in $^8$C and $^6$Be, and provide a framework for understanding Coulomb-induced mirror symmetry breaking in unbound proton-rich nuclei.
