# Isospin Symmetry Breaking Effects on the Properties of Asymmetrical Nuclear Matter and $β$-Stable Matter

> We have studied the influences of isospin symmetry breaking of nucleon-nucleon interaction on the various properties of asymmetrical nuclear matter and $β$-stable matter. For asymmetrical nuclear matter, it is found that by including this isospin symmetry breaking, the changes of bulk properties increase by increasing both density and asymmetry parameter. However, these effects on the total energy and equation of state of $β$-stable matter are ignorable. For asymmetrical nuclear matter, the validity of the empirical parabolic law in the isospin symmetry breaking case is shown. It is observed that the isospin symmetry breaking of nucleon-nucleon interaction affects the $β$-equilibrium conditions in $β$-stable matter.

# Isospin Symmetry Breaking Effects on the Properties of Asymmetrical Nuclear Matter and β-Stable Matter

## Key claim

The inclusion of isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) in the nucleon-nucleon interaction produces measurable changes in the bulk properties of asymmetrical nuclear matter that increase with both density and asymmetry parameter, but these effects on the total energy and equation of state of β-stable matter are negligible (described as "ignorable"). The validity of the empirical parabolic law for asymmetrical nuclear matter holds even when ISB is included.

## Method

The author used a variational approach based on cluster expansion of the energy functional with state-dependent correlation functions, employing the Reid-93 potential which distinguishes between neutral pion and charged pion exchange. For the ISB case, the one-pion exchange and ¹S₀ potentials for nn, np, and pp interactions were treated as different; for the isospin symmetric (IS) case, the nn and pp interactions were replaced by the corresponding np interaction. Calculations were performed for asymmetrical nuclear matter at various asymmetry parameters β and for β-stable matter under β-equilibrium conditions.

## Key numbers

- The mass difference between neutral pion and charged pion responsible for ISB is Δm ≃ 4.6 MeV.
- At density ρ = 1.0 fm⁻³, the energy correction to β-stable matter due to ISB effects is about 1.6%.
- For asymmetrical nuclear matter, the saturation point shifts to lower densities as the asymmetry parameter increases, and for large β values the energy curve shows no minimum.
- The differences between ISB and IS energies for asymmetrical nuclear matter at β = 0.0 were compared with Muther et al. (1999) results for the Nijm-II potential and found to be in good agreement.
- The potential energy shift due to ISB is positive for each value of density and asymmetry parameter, reflecting that in the T=1 partial wave, the M_T=0 potential is more attractive than the M_T=+1 and M_T=-1 potentials.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous variational calculations (Bordbar and Modarres, 1997, 1998; Modarres and Bordbar, 1998; Bordbar and Riazi, 2001, 2002; Bordbar, 2002a,b) by incorporating ISB effects from modern nucleon-nucleon potentials. It builds on the Reid-93 potential (Stoks et al., 1994) and compares with results from Muther et al. (1999) using the Nijm-II potential. The findings are relevant for understanding supernova collapse, heavy-ion reactions (Lattimer and Prakash, 2000; Li et al., 1997), and neutron star properties including stability (Akmal et al., 1998; Heiselberg and Pandharipande, 2000) and cooling (Prakash, 1994). The work enables more precise modeling of neutron star interiors by showing that while ISB effects on the total energy and equation of state of β-stable matter are negligible, they do affect the β-equilibrium conditions and proton fraction.
