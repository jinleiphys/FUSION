# The effective ω-meson mass with a vector field self-interaction

> We calculate the effective mass m_ω^* of the ω-meson in nuclear medium by the σ-ωmodel with a vector field self-interaction (VFSI). We found that the direct and the indirect effects of the VFSI almost cancel each other and the result is changed only slightly at the normal density. The VFSI makes m^*_ωsmaller at lower density and makes m_ω^* larger at higher density. However, both effects are not large.

## Key claim

The vector field self-interaction (VFSI) has only a small net effect on the effective ω-meson mass in nuclear matter because the direct effect (which increases the mass) and the indirect effect (via changes in the ω-nucleon coupling and effective nucleon mass, which decrease the mass) almost cancel each other at normal nuclear density.

## Method

The authors used the σ-ω model (quantum hadrodynamics) including a vector field self-interaction term in the Lagrangian. They computed the effective ω-meson mass by solving Dyson's equation in the random phase approximation (RPA), with the nucleon propagator treated in the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA). The VFSI strength parameter y was varied (0, 0.2, 0.5), and the coupling constants were refitted to reproduce saturation conditions at baryon density 0.15 fm⁻³ and binding energy −15.75 MeV for each case.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear density (0.15 fm⁻³), the effective ω-meson mass is changed only slightly by the VFSI; the direct and indirect effects nearly cancel.
- For the RHA model without VFSI (y=0): effective nucleon mass ratio M₀*/M = 0.730, incompressibility K = 454.1 MeV, C_ω² = 146.86, C_σ² = 226.94.
- For VS1 (y=0.2): M₀*/M = 0.721, K = 422.5 MeV, C_ω² = 159.13, C_σ² = 239.84.
- For VS2 (y=0.5): M₀*/M = 0.692, K = 363.4 MeV, C_ω² = 203.59, C_σ² = 284.73.

## Context

This work extends the σ-ω model studies of effective meson masses in nuclear matter, which had been previously investigated using quantum hadrodynamics (e.g., by Jean and collaborators), QCD sum rules (Hatsuda, Asakawa), and the quark meson coupling model (Saito). The VFSI had been introduced by Bodmer for better fitting of the equation of state at high density, and the present paper examines its specific effect on the ω-meson mass, finding that the net modification is small despite the individual direct and indirect contributions being sizable.
