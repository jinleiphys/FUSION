# N-Body Theory Revisited and its Extension to the pinnn-NNN Problem^*

> In order to approach the pion--multinucleon problem, we have found it convenient to reformulate the general N--body theory starting from the fully unclusterized (i.e., N <- N) amplitude. If we rewrite such an amplitude in terms of new unknowns which can be later identified as the amplitudes for all the (N-1) <- (N-1) cluster processes, and repeat recursively the procedure up to the treatment of the 2 <- 2 cluster processes, we obtain very naturally the hierarchy of equations which ranges from the N--body fully--disconnected Lippmann--Schwinger equation to the N--body connected--kernel Yakubovskii--Grassberger--Sandhas one. This revisitation turns out to be very useful when considering the modifications required in case one of the bodies is a pion and the remaining are nucleons, with the pion being allowed to disappear and reappear through the action of a pion--nucleon vertex. In fact, we obtain a new set of coupled pion-- multinucleon equations which allow a consistent and simultaneous treatment of pion scattering and absorption. For the piNNN system, the kernel of these coupled equations is shown to be connected after three iterations.

## Key claim

The paper reformulates general N-body scattering theory by starting from the fully unclusterized N←N amplitude and recursively introducing chain-labelled operators, then extends this formalism to the pion–multinucleon problem where pion absorption and production are allowed. For the πNNN system specifically, the kernel of the resulting coupled equations is shown to be connected after three iterations, though the authors note this connectedness is achieved at different stages for different sectors of the kernel.

## Method

The authors begin with the N-body Lippmann–Schwinger equation for the fully unclusterized N←N amplitude and introduce Alt–Grassberger–Sandhas (AGS) transition operators via an ansatz relating the amplitude to sums over pair t-matrices. They then recursively apply the same procedure, rewriting the super Lippmann–Schwinger equation at each cluster level in terms of new unknowns labelled by chains of partitions, until reaching the connected-kernel Yakubovskii–Grassberger–Sandhas equations. For the pion–multinucleon case, they start from the Afnan–Blankleider equations coupling amplitudes with and without a pion, extend the AGS matrix method to include vertex operators for πNN absorption/production, and derive a new set of coupled equations in a super-matrix form that mixes the N-body and (N-1)-nucleon sectors.

## Key numbers

- For the πNNN system, the kernel of the coupled equations is connected after three iterations.
- The standard four-body Yakubovskii equations are connected after two iterations, whereas the πNNN equations require three iterations for the NNNπ←NNNπ and NNNπ←NNN sectors.
- The NNN←NNN sector of the πNNN kernel is connected after one iteration, the NNN←NNNπ sector after two iterations.
- In the πNNN case, the formalism yields 24 coupled equations, compared to 18 equations in the standard Yakubovskii-type formalism for four bodies.

## Context

This work extends the Alt–Grassberger–Sandhas (AGS) and Yakubovskii–Grassberger–Sandhas formulations of N-body scattering theory to systems where particle number is not conserved due to pion absorption and production. It addresses the π–multinucleon problem previously tackled by Avishai and Mizutani (1983), who used a quasi-particle approximation and two-potential formulation that did not yield a closed set of coupled equations. The present approach provides a closed set of coupled equations for the πNNN system and establishes a hierarchical scheme that could in principle be extended to general pion–multinucleon systems, though the authors note that achieving fully connected kernels for N>4 remains an open problem.
