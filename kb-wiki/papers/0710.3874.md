# Equilibrium sequences of non rotating and rapidly rotating crystalline color superconducting hybrid stars

> The three-flavor crystalline color-superconducting (CCS) phase of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) is a candidate phase for the ground state of cold matter at moderate densities above the density of the deconfinement phase transition. Apart from being a superfluid, the CCS phase has properties of a solid, such as a lattice structure and a shear modulus, and hence the ability to sustain multipolar deformations in gravitational equilibrium. We construct equilibrium configurations of hybrid stars composed of nuclear matter at low, and CCS quark matter at high, densities. Phase equilibrium between these phases is possible only for rather stiff equations of state of nuclear matter and large couplings in the effective Nambu--Jona-Lasinio Lagrangian describing the CCS state. We identify a new branch of stable CCS hybrid stars within a broad range of central densities which, depending on the details of the equations of state, either bifurcate from the nuclear sequence of stars when the central density exceeds that of the deconfinement phase transition or form a new family of configurations separated from the purely nuclear sequence by an instability region. The maximum masses of our non-rotating hybrid configurations are consistent with the presently available astronomical bounds. The sequences of hybrid configurations that rotate near the mass-shedding limit are found to be more compact and thus support substantially larger spins than their same mass nuclear counterparts.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that stable hybrid stars containing a crystalline color-superconducting (CCS) quark core can exist, but only when the nuclear equation of state is very stiff and the diquark coupling in the NJL model is large (η = 1 rather than the conventional η = 0.75), and that a new stable branch of such stars appears over a broad range of central densities.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid star equations of state by matching a low-density nuclear matter phase (described by stiff Dirac-Bruckner-Hartree-Fock equations of state) to a high-density CCS quark matter phase (described by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with three-flavor LOFF pairing) using the Maxwell construction at the phase interface. They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for non-rotating configurations and the general relativistic equations for rapidly rotating stars at the Keplerian mass-shedding limit, computing mass, radius, and moment of inertia sequences for three model variants (A, A1, and B) that differ in the nuclear equation of state and the zero-density pressure normalization of the quark phase.

## Key numbers

- The LOFF phase exists within the chemical potential range 442 MeV ≤ μ ≤ 515 MeV (from self-consistent strange quark mass calculations)
- The deconfinement phase transition occurs at chemical potentials and pressures of (μ = 1234.2 MeV; p = 96 MeV/fm³) for model A1, (1230.0; 95) for model A, and (1234.8; 108) for model B
- Stable hybrid configurations exist in the central density range 1.3 ≤ ρc ≤ 2.5 × 10¹⁵ g cm⁻³
- Maximum masses for non-rotating hybrid configurations: approximately 2.05 M⊙ (model A), 2.02 M⊙ (model A1), and 1.98 M⊙ (model B)
- For rapidly rotating configurations at the Keplerian limit, maximum masses increase to: 2.052 → 2.462 M⊙ (model A), 2.017 → 2.428 M⊙ (model A1), and 1.981 → 2.35 M⊙ (model B)
- The radius difference between hybrid and purely nuclear stars of the same mass is ≤ 1 km
- The Keplerian frequency of massive hybrid configurations is a factor of two larger than 716 Hz (the frequency of the fastest known millisecond pulsar PSR J1748-2446ad)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars with color-superconducting quark matter, which had found either complete instability (Buballa 2003) or only narrow stable branches (Grigorian 2003, Alford 2004), by identifying a new broad stable branch of CCS hybrid stars analogous to the "twin" configurations found for non-superconducting quark matter by Glendenning (1998). The results enable quantitative predictions for gravitational wave emission from CCS hybrid stars and show that simultaneous mass and moment-of-inertia measurements (as possible for the double-pulsar system PSR J0737-3039) could distinguish hybrid from purely nuclear configurations.
