# Renormalization of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Equations in the Case of a Zero Range Pairing Interaction

> We introduce a natural and simple to implement renormalization scheme of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations for the case of zero range pairing interaction. This renormalization scheme proves to be equivalent to a simple energy cut-off with a position dependent running coupling constant.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a renormalization scheme for the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations with zero-range pairing interactions that is equivalent to a simple energy cutoff with a position-dependent running coupling constant, but only works when the cutoff energy is chosen sufficiently far from the Fermi level to ensure convergence.

## Method

The authors implement a regularization scheme that first introduces an explicit energy cutoff \(E_c\) in evaluating the anomalous density, then uses a Thomas-Fermi approximation for the regulator quantities (single-particle wave functions, energies, and propagator) rather than the exact Hartree-Fock quantities. This yields a position-dependent effective coupling constant \(g_{\text{eff}}(\mathbf{r})\) that renormalizes the pairing interaction, with the final result independent of the cutoff energy when chosen appropriately.

## Key numbers

- The bare coupling constant used in calculations for a Woods-Saxon potential corresponding to \(^{110}\text{Sn}\) is \(g = -200\ \text{MeV}\cdot\text{fm}^3\).
- The chemical potential is set to \(\mu = -0.1\ \text{MeV}\) (essentially at the neutron drip line) for the illustrative calculations.
- Convergence of the neutron pairing field \(\Delta(\mathbf{r})\) is achieved for cutoff energies \(E_c = 40,\ 45,\ \text{and}\ 50\ \text{MeV}\), with the last two curves being indistinguishable on the scale of the figure.
- The pairing field is shown for cutoff energies \(E_c = 20,\ 30,\ 35,\ 40,\ 45,\ \text{and}\ 50\ \text{MeV}\) in the convergence study.

## Context

This work extends the regularization approach of Bruun et al. (1999) for finite systems in harmonic traps to self-sustaining systems like atomic nuclei, where the one-to-one correspondence between Hartree-Fock and HFB spectra breaks down. The scheme enables practical HFB calculations with zero-range pairing interactions in nuclei near the drip lines, where previous methods requiring exact single-particle Green's functions were not computationally feasible. The approach contrasts with finite-range interactions like the Gogny force (Ring, 1980) and with the vacuum renormalization scheme used in phenomenological energy-cutoff approaches (Henning, 1999).
