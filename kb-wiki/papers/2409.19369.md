# Transport properties of the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma

> We investigate the transport properties of the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) by comparing the role of elastic and inelastic (radiative) processes in the sQGP medium within the effective dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM), constructed for the description of non-perturbative quantum chromodynamic (QCD) phenomena of the sQGP in line with the lattice QCD (lQCD) equation of state. First, we present the results for the energy and temperature dependencies of the total radiative cross sections and compare them to the corresponding elastic cross sections. Second, we perform a calculation of the interaction rate and relaxation time of radiative versus elastic scatterings. Finally, we obtain the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ and investigate its dependence on the choice of the strong coupling in thermal, jet parton and radiative vertices.

## Key claim

The paper claims that inelastic (radiative) processes are generally suppressed compared to elastic processes in the thermal quark-gluon plasma within the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM), but this suppression is conditional on the temperature and energy regime: inelastic cross sections become significant at low temperatures and high energies, while the interaction rates and relaxation times remain dominated by elastic scattering for thermal partons.

## Method

The authors compute partonic cross sections for elastic (2-to-2) and inelastic (2-to-3) processes using DQPM dressed propagators and couplings, with parton masses taken as the pole masses of spectral functions at a given temperature. They then calculate interaction rates and relaxation times by integrating these cross sections over thermal distribution functions. Finally, they compute the jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) using kinetic theory within the Boltzmann transport equation, testing five different scenarios for the strong coupling at thermal, jet parton, and radiative vertices.

## Key numbers

- The DQPM strong coupling \(\alpha_s\) grows above 1 in the vicinity of the critical temperature \(T_c = 0.158\) GeV.
- The jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}/T^3\) is calculated for a quark jet with mass \(M_j = 0.01\) GeV and energy \(E = 10\) GeV across five coupling scenarios.
- Scenario I uses a fixed \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) for all vertices.
- The critical temperature is given as \(T_c = 0.158\) GeV.
- The JET Collaboration phenomenological extraction is presented for \(p = 10\) GeV/\(c\).
- The JETSCAPE Collaboration results are shown for \(p = 100\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the DQPM framework previously developed by Peshier (2005) and Grishmanovskii (2023) to systematically compare elastic and inelastic contributions to transport properties. The results contradict the expectation that radiative processes dominate jet energy loss at high energies, finding instead that inelastic processes with massive gluon emission are suppressed in the thermalized sQGP medium. The study enables future work to constrain the choice of strong coupling in jet-medium interactions, as the large spread between scenarios (including those from Zakharov (2020), Liu (2023), and Karmakar (2023)) shows that \(\hat{q}\) is highly sensitive to this choice, particularly at low temperatures.
