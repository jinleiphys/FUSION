# Forward-backward eccentricity and participant-plane angle fluctuations and their influences on longitudinal dynamics of collective flow

> We argue that the transverse shape of the fireball created in heavy ion collision is controlled by event-by-event fluctuations of the eccentricity vectors for the forward-going and backward-going wounded nucleons: $\vecε_n^{\mathrm{F}}\equiv ε_n^{\mathrm{F}} e^{i nΦ_n^{\mathrm{*F}}}$ and $\vecε_n^{\mathrm{B}}\equiv ε_n^{\mathrm{B}} e^{i nΦ_n^{\mathrm{*B}}}$. Due to the asymmetric energy deposition of each wounded nucleon along its direction of motion, the eccentricity vector of the produced fireball is expected to interpolate between $\vecε_n^{\mathrm{F}}$ and $\vecε_n^{\mathrm{B}}$ along the pseudorapidity, and hence exhibits sizable forward-backward(FB) asymmetry ($ε_n^{\rm B}\neqε_n^{\rm F}$) and/or FB-twist ($Φ_n^{\mathrm{*F}}\neqΦ_n^{\mathrm{*B}}$). A transport model calculation shows that these initial state longitudinal fluctuations for $n=2$ and 3 survive the collective expansion, and result in similar FB asymmetry and/or a twist in the final state event-plane angles. These novel EbyE longitudinal flow fluctuations should be accessible at RHIC and the LHC using the event-shape selection technique proposed in earlier papers. If these effects are observed experimentally, it could improve our understanding of the initial state fluctuations, particle production and collective expansion dynamics of the heavy ion collision.

## Key claim

The paper argues that event-by-event fluctuations in the eccentricity vectors of forward-going and backward-going wounded nucleons cause the transverse shape of the fireball to vary along pseudorapidity, producing forward-backward asymmetry and twist in both the initial eccentricity and the final-state collective flow; these effects are expected to be observable at RHIC and the LHC using event-shape selection techniques.

## Method

The authors used a simple wounded-nucleon model with independent emission to relate the pseudorapidity-dependent transverse density profile to the eccentricity vectors of the two colliding nuclei. They then performed simulations with the AMPT transport model (string-melting mode, partonic cross-section 1.5 mb, strong coupling constant α_s=0.33) for Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN=2.76 TeV with impact parameter b=8 fm. Events were classified into four types based on the forward-backward eccentricity difference and participant-plane twist angle, and the pseudorapidity dependence of harmonic flow coefficients v_n and event-plane angles was studied for n=2 and n=3.

## Key numbers

- The RMS width of the twist angle 2(Φ_2*F - Φ_2*B) is about 0.21 radian for the top 10% of events with largest ε_2, and increases to 0.70 radian for the bottom 10% of events with smallest ε_2.
- For type2 elliptic flow events, the average eccentricity values are ⟨ε_2^F⟩=0.456 and ⟨ε_2^B⟩=0.282.
- For type3 elliptic flow events, the average eccentricity values are ⟨ε_2^F⟩=⟨ε_2^B⟩=0.314.
- For type4 elliptic flow events, the average eccentricity values are ⟨ε_2^F⟩=0.386 and ⟨ε_2^B⟩=0.197.
- For type2 triangular flow events, the average eccentricity values are ⟨ε_3^F⟩=0.293 and ⟨ε_3^B⟩=0.126.

- For type4 triangular flow events, the average eccentricity values are ⟨ε_3^F⟩=0.246 and ⟨ε_3^B⟩=0.0686.

## Context

This work extends the concept of event-by-event forward-backward multiplicity fluctuations (Bialas 1976, Bialas 2007, Bzdak 2009) to the eccentricity and participant-plane angles of harmonic flow. It builds on earlier studies of twist effects in hydrodynamic evolution (Bozek 2010, Jia 2014) and on the event-shape selection technique (Schukraft 2012, Huo 2013, Jia 2014). The results enable experimental searches for longitudinal flow fluctuations and event-plane decorrelations at RHIC and the LHC, which could improve understanding of initial-state fluctuations, particle production, and collective expansion dynamics in heavy-ion collisions.
