# Isotropization of a longitudinally expanding system of scalar fields in the 2PI formalism

> Motivated by isotropization of QCD matter in the initial stages of heavy-ion collisions, we consider a system of scalar fields that undergoes a boost invariant longitudinal expansion. We use the framework of the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action, which is close to the underlying quantum field theory, and resum self-energy corrections up to three loops. The resulting 2PI equations of motion are expressed in terms of the Milne coordinates to account for longitudinal expansion. By solving numerically these equations of motion, we can extract the occupation density and the effective mass generated by in-medium interactions. At the largest values of the coupling considered in this study, we observe the onset of isotropization both in the occupation number and in the momentum dependence of the effective mass.

## Key claim
For a longitudinally expanding scalar field system, the onset of isotropization is observed in both the occupation number and the momentum dependence of the effective mass, but only at the largest values of the coupling considered in this study.

## Method
The authors use the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action truncated at three-loop order (sunset approximation) to derive Kadanoff-Baym equations for a real scalar field with quartic interaction. They express these equations in Milne coordinates to enforce boost invariance along the longitudinal direction and assume translation and rotation invariance in the transverse plane. The equations are solved numerically after performing a spatial Fourier transform in the transverse momentum and rapidity-conjugate momentum variables.

## Key numbers
- The coupling constant of the quartic interaction is denoted by \(g^2\), with thermal masses expected to be of order \(gQ\) where \(Q\) is the typical particle momentum.
- The truncation of the 2PI effective action is performed at order \(g^4\), corresponding to the sunset approximation.
- The effective mass \(M_0^2(\tau)\) is defined as \(\frac{g^2}{2} \int \frac{dp_\perp p_\perp\,d\nu}{(2\pi)^2}\,F(\tau,\tau,p_\perp,\nu)\).
- The longitudinal momentum variable \(\nu\) is approximately related to the physical longitudinal momentum by \(\nu \approx \tau\, p_z\).
- For an isotropic system, typical momenta decrease as \(p_\perp,p_z\sim \tau^{-1/3}\), implying \(\nu\sim \tau^{2/3}\).

## Context
This work extends the pioneering 2PI calculation for longitudinally expanding systems by Hatta (2012) with improved numerical resolution, and addresses shortcomings of the classical statistical approximation used in CGC-based studies by Berges (2013, 2015) and Epelbaum (2013, 2015). The 2PI framework provides a unified description that interpolates between classical field theory at high occupancy and kinetic theory, while including off-shell excitations and dynamical soft modes that kinetic theory cannot capture. This enables future benchmarking of kinetic theory and classical-statistical calculations for heavy-ion collisions, and may eventually inform calculations of the transport coefficient \(\widehat{q}\) for jet energy loss.
