# Amplitude extraction in pseudoscalar-meson photoproduction: towards a situation of complete information

> A complete set for pseudoscalar-meson photoproduction is a minimum set of observables from which one can determine the underlying reaction amplitudes unambiguously. The complete sets considered in this work involve single- and double-polarization observables. It is argued that for extracting amplitudes from data, the transversity representation of the reaction amplitudes offers advantages over alternate representations. It is shown that with the available single-polarization data for the p(γ,K^+)Λreaction, the energy and angular dependence of the moduli of the normalized transversity amplitudes in the resonance region can be determined to a fair accuracy. Determining the relative phases of the amplitudes from double-polarization observables is far less evident.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while the moduli of normalized transversity amplitudes for pseudoscalar-meson photoproduction can be extracted to fair accuracy from existing single-polarization data, determining the relative phases from double-polarization observables is far less evident and, for currently achievable experimental accuracies, the actual phases cannot be extracted in the majority of situations.

## Method

The authors use the transversity representation of reaction amplitudes for the \(p(\gamma,K^+)\Lambda\) reaction, where single-polarization observables \(\{\Sigma, T, P\}\) are linearly related to the squared moduli of normalized transversity amplitudes (NTA). They extract the NTA moduli from published GRAAL data at 66 \((W, \cos\theta_{\text{c.m.}})\) combinations. For phase extraction, they generate ensembles of 200 pseudo data sets from the RPR-2011 model, drawing observables from Gaussians with a given experimental accuracy \(\sigma_{\text{exp}}\), and analyze the fraction of cases where the NTA phases can be correctly retrieved using complete sets of observables.

## Key numbers

- The \(p(\gamma,K^+)\Lambda\) data base contains 4231 measured differential cross sections, 2260 single-polarization observables (178 \(\Sigma\), 2013 \(P\), 69 \(T\)), and 452 double-polarization data points (320 \(C_{(x,z)}\) and 132 \(O_{(x',z')}\)).
- The GRAAL collaboration provides data at 66 \((W, \cos\theta_{\text{c.m.}})\) combinations in the ranges \(1.65 \lesssim W \lesssim 1.91\) GeV (bin width \(\Delta W \approx 50\) MeV) and \(-0.81 \lesssim \cos\theta_{\text{c.m.}} \lesssim 0.86\) (bin width \(\Delta \cos\theta_{\text{c.m.}} \approx 0.3\)).
- For 7 out of 131 extracted points, the \(r_i\) could not be retrieved due to negative terms in Eq. (1) from finite experimental error bars.
- For realistic experimental accuracies of \(\sigma_{\text{exp}} = 0.1\) (10% of an asymmetry's maximum possible value), the total insolvability \(\eta_{\text{total}}\) for the complete set \(\{\Sigma, P, T, C_x, O_x, E, F\}\) is of order 0.6–0.7.
- For \(\sigma_{\text{exp}} = 0.1\) and \(W \gtrsim 1.8\) GeV, the imaginary-solution insolvability \(\eta_{\text{imaginary}}\) clusters around 0.6–0.7 for all 36 complete sets of the first kind.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of complete experiments in pseudoscalar-meson photoproduction, following the seminal paper by Barker, Donnachie, and Storrow (1975) who argued nine observables are needed, and later work by Keaton and Workman (1996) and Chiang and Tabakin (1996) who asserted eight suffice. The authors previously showed (TomComp) that for realistic experimental accuracies, a complete set does not guarantee unambiguous amplitude extraction. The paper uses the RPR-2011 model (developed by the same group) as a benchmark and demonstrates that while single-polarization data can yield NTA moduli, phase extraction from double-polarization data remains problematic, motivating future work with overcomplete sets.
