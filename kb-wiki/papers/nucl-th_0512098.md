# Neutron star cooling constraints for color superconductivity in hybrid stars

> We apply the recently developed Log N - Log S test of compact star cooling theories for the first time to hybrid stars with a color superconducting quark matter core. While there is not yet a microscopically founded superconducting quark matter phase which would fulfill constraints from cooling phenomenology, we explore the hypothetical 2SC+X phase and show that the magnitude and density-dependence of the X-gap can be chosen to satisfy a set of tests: temperature - age (T - t), the brightness constraint, Log N - Log S, and the mass spectrum constraint. The latter test appears as a new conjecture from the present investigation.

## Key claim

While no microscopically founded superconducting quark matter phase yet fulfills constraints from cooling phenomenology, the hypothetical "2SC+X" phase with a density-dependent X-gap can be chosen to satisfy a set of tests (temperature-age, brightness constraint, Log N - Log S, and a new mass spectrum constraint) for hybrid stars with a color superconducting quark matter core.

## Method

The authors calculated cooling curves for hybrid stars using a nonlocal chiral quark model with a hadronic equation of state based on the Argonne V18+δv+UIX* model. They applied a population synthesis scenario to generate artificial neutron star populations in the solar neighborhood, computing Log N - Log S distributions, temperature-age diagrams, and brightness constraints for four models characterized by different parameters of the density-dependent X-gap ansatz Δ_X = Δ_0 exp[-α (μ - μ_c)/μ_c] with μ_c = 330 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The threshold density for the direct Urca process is n_c^DU ≈ 5.19 n_0, occurring in stars with masses exceeding M_c^DU ≈ 1.839 M_⊙
- The critical mass for quark core formation is close to 1.22 M_⊙
- The opacity temperature for neutrino untrapping is T_opac ∼ 1 MeV
- The nuclear saturation density is n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- The weak pairing channels for the X-gap are characterized by gaps typically in the interval 10 keV to 1 MeV
- The mass spectrum uses eight bins with borders at 1.05, 1.13, 1.22, 1.28, 1.35, 1.45, 1.55, 1.65, and 1.75 M_⊙
- The Gould Belt is modeled with radii R_belt = 300 and 500 pc
- The birth rate in the disc is 250 NS per Myr, and in the Gould Belt is 20 per Myr
- The velocity distribution has two maxima at ∼127 and ∼707 km s^{-1}
- The binary pulsar J1906+0746 has total mass 2.61 ± 0.02 M_⊙

## Context

This work extends the Log N - Log S test developed by Popov et al. (2004) (Paper I) from hadron stars to hybrid stars with color superconducting quark matter cores, using cooling curves calculated by Grigorian et al. (2004) (Paper II). The study introduces a new mass spectrum constraint based on the observation that neutron stars in double neutron star binaries have masses in the narrow range 1.18-1.39 M_⊙, suggesting that most neutron stars have masses below ∼1.4-1.5 M_⊙. The results enable future searches for the microscopic origin of the hypothetical X-gap and its steep density dependence.
