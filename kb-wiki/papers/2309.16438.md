# Sound velocity, equation of state and strangeness in neutron star matter

> This presentation in two parts starts with a brief review on the speed of sound in neutron star matter as inferred from observational data. It is pointed out that, in view the strong constraints imposed by observed properties of the heaviest neutron stars, the equation of state must be very stiff. Stringent limits can be set on the possible appearance of phase transitions in neutron star cores. The second part discusses issues concerning the occurance of strangeness degrees of freedom, in particular of hyperons, in neutron stars. Here a focus is on the role of repulsive hyperon-nuclear three-body forces, potentially resolving the so-called hyperon puzzle.

# Sound velocity, equation of state and strangeness in neutron star matter

## Key claim
The speed of sound in neutron star matter rises rapidly beyond the conformal bound of 1/3 at energy densities relevant for neutron stars with masses 1.4–2.3 solar masses, and repulsive hyperon-nuclear three-body forces derived from chiral SU(3) effective field theory may prevent the appearance of hyperons in neutron star cores, potentially resolving the hyperon puzzle. However, the final solution of the hyperon puzzle continues to be under discussion, and the results so far point only into an at least qualitatively promising direction.

## Method
Bayesian statistical inference was used to deduce the squared sound velocity and equation of state from observational data including Shapiro delay mass measurements of pulsars, NICER mass-radius data, and the GW170817 tidal deformability, with constraints from chiral effective field theory at low densities and perturbative QCD at high densities. For the strangeness sector, Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations were performed using NLO19 hyperon-nucleon two-body interactions from chiral SU(3) effective field theory, supplemented by three-body forces approximated via decuplet dominance with parameters constrained by empirical hypernuclear binding energies.

## Key numbers
- PSR J0952-0607 mass: 2.35 ± 0.17 solar masses (heaviest neutron star observed so far)
- PSR J0740+6620 mass: 2.08 ± 0.07 solar masses; radius: 12.39 +1.30/-0.98 km (from NICER)
- PSR J0030+0451 mass: 1.34 +0.15/-0.16 solar masses; radius: 12.71 +1.14/-1.19 km (from NICER)
- Tidal deformability for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star from GW170817: Lambda_1.4 = 190 +390/-120
- Central baryon density for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: rho_c = (2.6 ± 0.4) rho_0 at 68% confidence
- Central baryon density for a 2.3 solar mass neutron star: rho_c = (3.8 ± 0.8) rho_0 at 68% confidence
- Equilibrium density of normal nuclear matter: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Empirical Lambda-nuclear single-particle potential depth: U_Lambda(r=0) approximately -30 MeV
- Best-fit total Lambda potential at nuclear saturation density: U_Lambda(rho_0) = -(27.3 ± 1.6) MeV, with two-body contribution U_0^(2) = -(38.6 ± 0.8) MeV and three-body contribution U_0^(3) = (11.3 ± 1.4) MeV
- ChEFT-based three-body potential coefficient: U_0^(3) approximately 10 MeV

## Context
This work extends the Bayesian inference approach of Brandes (2023) and Brandes (2023a) for the neutron star equation of state, incorporating the newly discovered heavy pulsar PSR J0952-0607 from Romani (2022). The strangeness analysis builds on hyperon-nucleon interactions from chiral SU(3) effective field theory developed by Haidenbauer (2013, 2020) and Petschauer (2020), and addresses the hyperon puzzle first identified when adding strangeness degrees of freedom made the equation of state too soft to support two-solar-mass neutron stars. The results enable further investigation of hadron-quark continuity crossover scenarios as discussed by Baym (2019), McLerran (2019), and Fukushima (2020), while placing stringent limits against strong first-order phase transitions in neutron star cores.
