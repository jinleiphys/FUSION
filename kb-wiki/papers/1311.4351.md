# Production of exotic isotopes in complete fusion reactions with radioactive beams

> The isotopic dependence of the complete fusion (capture) cross section is analyzed in the reactions $^{130,132,134,136,138,140,142,144,146,148,150}$Xe+$^{48}$Ca with stable and radioactive beams. It is shown for the first time that the very neutron-rich nuclei $^{186-191}$W can be reached with relatively large cross sections by complete fusion reactions with radioactive ion beams at incident energies near the Coulomb barrier. A comparison between the complete fusion and fragmentation reactions for the production of neutron-rich W and neutron-deficient Rn isotopes is performed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates for the first time that very neutron-rich tungsten isotopes \(^{186-191}\)W can be produced with relatively large cross sections in complete fusion reactions with radioactive ion beams at energies near the Coulomb barrier, but the authors ultimately conclude that fragmentation reactions are more preferable than complete fusion for producing these neutron-rich W isotopes when realistic beam intensities are considered.

## Method

The authors employed a quantum diffusion approach to calculate capture cross sections for reactions of \(^{130,132,134,136,138,140,142,144,146,148,150}\)Xe projectiles with a \(^{48}\)Ca target, treating the relative distance between colliding nuclei as the single relevant collective variable. The model incorporates fluctuation and dissipation effects, nuclear deformation through quadrupole deformation parameters, and the influence of neutron transfer with positive Q-values on the capture probability. For the neutron-deficient Rn isotopes, the survival probabilities of excited compound nuclei in neutron evaporation channels were calculated using the modified statistical code GROGIF combined with the dinuclear system fusion model.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction \(^{146}\)Xe+\(^{48}\)Ca producing \(^{189}\)W, cross sections up to about 2 mb are predicted at E\(_{cm}\) = 110 MeV.
- The production cross sections of neutron-rich \(^{190,191}\)W isotopes in complete fusion reactions are between the 10 \(\mu\)b and 100 mb levels.
- For fragmentation reactions, cross sections smaller than 0.4 \(\mu\)b were measured for \(^{190-192}\)W in cold fragmentation of 950 MeV/nucleon \(^{197}\)Au beams on Be targets.
- For W isotopes with mass numbers A \(\geq\) 190 produced in projectile fragmentation of \(^{238}\)U at 1 GeV/nucleon, cross sections smaller than 5 nb were measured.
- At sub-barrier energies, the fusion cross section decreases by about one order of magnitude with increasing projectile mass number from A=130 up to A=136.
- For producing \(^{189}\)W via fusion, \(^{146}\)Xe beams with intensities of at least \(10^{13}\) ions/s are required to match fragmentation yields of \(10^4\) ions/s; at SPIRAL-II, \(10^5\) \(^{146}\)Xe projectiles per second are predicted, eight orders of magnitude less than needed.
- For neutron-deficient Rn isotopes with \(188 \leq A \leq 190\), fusion reactions with \(^{123}\)Cs beams at \(10^{10}\) ions/s yield at least 2-7 times lower yields than fragmentation.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of complete fusion reactions with radioactive beams to the production of neutron-rich W isotopes, comparing with fragmentation results from Benlliure (2002) and Kurcewicz (2012) at GSI's Fragment Separator. The authors find that despite the large predicted cross sections for fusion, the low intensities of available radioactive beams make fragmentation reactions more practical for producing neutron-rich W isotopes, and similarly for neutron-deficient Rn isotopes when compared with yields from the Super-FRS facility. The paper enables future experimental planning by quantifying the beam intensity requirements for fusion-based production of exotic nuclei near the drip lines.
