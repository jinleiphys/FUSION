# Hybrid states with heavy quarks

> Model-independent features of hybrid states with heavy quarks and adiabatic approximation for such systems are discussed.These general arguments are applied to the QCD-based constituent gluon model.The leading- order Hamiltonian is derived from the general formalism. The masses of heavy quarks hybrids, including charmonium hybrid states,are calculated. We predict $c\bar c g$ hybrids at 4.0$\pm$0.1 GeV and $b \bar b g$ ones at 10.5$\pm$0.1 GeV.Comparison with some other models are made.

# Hybrid states with heavy quarks

## Key claim

The paper predicts masses for heavy quark hybrid states (c-cbar-g and b-bbar-g) using a constituent gluon model within the adiabatic (Born-Oppenheimer) approximation, but notes that the numerical smallness condition for the adiabatic expansion parameter is not satisfied for charm and bottom quarks, and that the absolute mass scale depends on additive constants fitted from heavy-light meson data.

## Method

The author starts from a constituent gluon model using the Feynman-Schwinger representation for the Green function to write an effective Lagrangian for the q-qbar-g system in Euclidean space-time, neglecting spin degrees of freedom. The Lagrangian is treated with auxiliary field formalism to handle square-root string terms, and center-of-mass motion is eliminated. The Hamiltonian is expanded in the limit of small interquark separation, yielding a leading-order gluonic subsystem Hamiltonian and quark corrections that produce an oscillator potential. Variational calculations are performed with three different ansatzes for the effective string energy, and additive constants are determined by fitting known D-meson and B-meson S-state masses.

## Key numbers

- Predicted c-cbar-g hybrid mass: 4.0 ± 0.1 GeV
- Predicted b-bbar-g hybrid mass: 10.5 ± 0.1 GeV
- Gluon subsystem energy (dimensionless): E_gl / sqrt(sigma) = 4.30 ± 0.05
- Quark correction (dimensionless): E_qqbar / (sqrt(sigma) * sqrt(sqrt(sigma)/m)) = 1.4 ± 0.2
- String tension: sigma = 0.18 GeV^2
- Strong coupling constant: alpha_s = 0.3
- Charm quark mass used: 1.2 GeV (with variations to 1.3, 1.5, 1.7 GeV)
- Bottom quark mass used: 4.2 GeV (with variations to 4.5, 4.9, 5.2 GeV)
- Calculated hybrid masses for m_c = 1.2 GeV range from 3.873 GeV (Ansatz III) to 4.089 GeV (potential model)
- Calculated hybrid masses for m_b = 4.2 GeV range from 10.647 GeV (Ansatz III) to 10.828 GeV (Ansatz I)

## Context

This work extends the constituent gluon model approach described in an earlier paper (referred to as [1]) to heavy quark hybrids, using the adiabatic approximation that separates slow quark motion from fast gluonic degrees of freedom. The results are compared with other models (referred to as [3] and [4]) and show agreement, though the author cautions this may be accidental due to different prescriptions for additive constants in the interquark potential. The paper enables future studies of spin-dependent forces, particularly spin-orbit couplings, which are noted as the next step in forthcoming work.
