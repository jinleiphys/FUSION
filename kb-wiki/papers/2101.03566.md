# Hybrid nature of the abnormal solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the Wick-Cutkosky model

> In the Wick-Cutkosky model, where two scalar massive constituents interact by means of the exchange of a scalar massless particle, the Bethe-Salpeter equation has solutions of two types, called "normal" and "abnormal". In the non-relativistic limit, the normal solutions correspond to the usual Coulomb spectrum, whereas the abnormal ones do not have non-relativistic counterparts -- they are absent in the Schrödinger equation framework. We have studied, in the formalism of the light-front dynamics, the Fock-space content of the abnormal solutions. It turns out that, in contrast to the normal ones, the abnormal states are dominated by the massless exchange particles (by 90 \% or more), what provides a natural explanation of their decoupling from the two-body Schrödinger equation. Assuming that one of the massive constituents is charged, we have calculated the electromagnetic elastic form factors of the normal and abnormal states, as well as the transition form factors. The results on form factors confirm the many-body nature of the abnormal states, as found from the Fock-space analysis. The abnormal solutions have thus properties similar to those of hybrid states, made here essentially of two massive constituents and several or many massless exchange particles. They could also be interpreted as the Abelian scalar analogs of the QCD hybrid states. The question of the validity of the ladder approximation of the model is also examined.

## Key claim

The abnormal solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the Wick-Cutkosky model are dominated by Fock-space sectors containing two massive constituents and many massless exchange particles (by 90% or more), which explains their absence from the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation framework; they are thus analogous to QCD hybrid states, but only for coupling constants above a critical value of α ≥ π/4.

## Method

The authors analyzed the Fock-space content of normal and abnormal Bethe-Salpeter solutions using light-front dynamics, extracting the two-body sector norm N₂ from the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. They solved the coupled integral equations for the functions g^ν_nκ(z) that parametrize the S-wave BS amplitude, computing binding energies and two-body norms for n=1,2 states with κ=0,2,4 at coupling constant α=5. They also calculated electromagnetic elastic and transition form factors assuming one massive constituent is charged, using the BS amplitude to determine their asymptotic behavior.

## Key numbers

- For the normal ground state (n=1, κ=0) at α=5: binding energy B = 0.999259 (in units where m=1), two-body norm N₂ = 0.65
- For the normal n=2, κ=0 state at α=5: B = 0.208410, N₂ = 0.61
- For the abnormal n=1, κ=2 state at α=5: B = 3.51169 × 10⁻³, N₂ = 0.094
- For the abnormal n=2, κ=2 state at α=5: B = 1.12118 × 10⁻³, N₂ = 0.077
- The two-body norm of normal states approaches N₂ ≈ 1 as B → 0, while abnormal states have N₂ → 0 in the same limit
- The critical coupling constant for existence of abnormal solutions is α ≥ π/4 (λ ≥ 1/4)
- The maximal coupling constant for a well-defined ground state is α = 2π (λ = 2)
- The asymptotic form factors of abnormal solutions decrease faster than those of normal solutions by factors of order 10³
- Transition form factors between normal and abnormal states are suppressed by factors of 5 to 10 relative to normal-normal or abnormal-abnormal transitions

## Context

This work extends the original analysis of Wick (1954) and Cutkosky (1954), who first discovered the abnormal solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the scalar massless-exchange model. The authors build on the Fock-space decomposition method of Carbonell, Desplanques, Karmanov, and Mathiot (1998) and the two-body norm calculations for normal states by Dorkin, Semikh, and Karmanov (2014). The results enable future experimental searches for analogous hybrid states in QCD, where quarks interact via massless gluon exchange, and clarify why abnormal states decouple from non-relativistic descriptions. The question of the validity of the ladder approximation for the large coupling constants required to produce abnormal states remains an open issue.
