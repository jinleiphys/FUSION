# Gap equation with pairing correlations beyond mean field and its equivalence to a Hugenholtz-Pines condition for fermion pairs

> The equation for the gap parameter represents the main equation of the pairing theory of superconductivity. Although it is formally defined through a single-particle property, physically it reflects the pairing correlations between opposite-spin fermions. Here, we exploit this physical connection and cast the gap equation in an alternative form which explicitly highlights these two-particle correlations, by showing that it is equivalent to a Hugenholtz-Pines condition for fermion pairs. At a formal level, a direct connection is established in this way between the treatment of the condensate fraction in condensate systems of fermions and bosons. At a practical level, the use of this alternative form of the gap equation is expected to make easier the inclusion of pairing fluctuations beyond mean field. As a proof-of-concept of the new method, we apply the modified form of the gap equation to the long-pending problem about the inclusion of the Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov correction across the whole BCS-BEC crossover, from the BCS limit of strongly overlapping Cooper pairs to the BEC limit of dilute composite bosons, and for all temperatures in the superfluid phase. Our numerical calculations yield excellent agreement with the recently determined experimental values of the gap parameter for an ultra-cold Fermi gas in the intermediate regime between BCS and BEC, as well as with the available quantum Monte Carlo data in the same regime.

## Key claim

The authors prove that the gap equation of pairing theory can be cast into a form equivalent to a Hugenholtz-Pines condition for fermion pairs, which holds for any self-consistent (conserving) approximation and across the entire BCS-BEC crossover, provided the inter-particle interaction is of the contact type. This equivalence is claimed to make the inclusion of pairing fluctuations beyond mean field more straightforward than in traditional approaches based on the anomalous single-particle self-energy.

## Method

The authors first derive a modified form of the gap equation by combining the Nambu representation of the Gor'kov equations with an identity relating the anomalous propagator to the normal propagators and self-energies. They then prove diagrammatically that this modified gap equation reduces to a Hugenholtz-Pines condition for fermion pairs, where the bare pair propagator (built from ladder diagrams) plus bosonic-like self-energy corrections (from Maki-Thompson and Aslamazov-Larkin processes) must satisfy a gapless condition at zero momentum. As a proof-of-concept, they implement this formalism to include the Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov (GMB) correction and an additional Popov correction across the whole BCS-BEC crossover and for all temperatures below the critical temperature, solving the resulting equations numerically.

## Key numbers

* The dimensionless coupling parameter spanning the BCS-BEC crossover is \((k_F a_F)^{-1}\), ranging from \((k_F a_F)^{-1} \lesssim -1\) in the BCS regime to \((k_F a_F)^{-1} \gtrsim +1\) in the BEC regime, across the unitary limit where \(|a_F|\) diverges.
* In the extreme BCS limit at zero temperature, the original GMB result gives a reduction of the gap parameter by a factor of 2.2 relative to its mean-field value.
* The numerical calculations yield excellent agreement with experimental values of the gap parameter for an ultra-cold Fermi gas in the intermediate (unitary) regime and with available quantum Monte Carlo data in the same regime.

## Context

This work extends the original Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov analysis (GMB, 1961), which was limited to the extreme BCS limit at zero temperature and determined the gap through an instability condition rather than a gap equation, to the entire BCS-BEC crossover and all temperatures below \(T_c\). It contrasts with approaches that use the functional-renormalization-group formalism (Floerchinger, 2010) or that neglect the wave-vector and frequency dependence of the pair propagator (Chen, 2016). The method enables systematic inclusion of pairing fluctuations beyond mean field and establishes a formal connection between the treatment of condensate fractions in fermionic and bosonic systems.
