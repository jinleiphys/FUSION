# QCD Equation of State and Hadron Resonance Gas

> We compare the trace anomaly, strangeness and baryon number fluctuations calculated in lattice QCD with expectations based on hadron resonance gas model. We find that there is a significant discrepancy between the hadron resonance gas and the lattice data. This discrepancy is largely reduced if the hadron spectrum is modified to take into account the larger values of the quark mass used in lattice calculations as well as the finite lattice spacing errors. We also give a simple parametrization of QCD equation of state, which combines hadron resonance gas at low temperatures with lattice QCD at high temperatures. We compare this parametrization with other parametrizations of the equation of state used in hydrodynamical models and discuss differences in hydrodynamic flow for different equations of state.

## Key claim

The hadron resonance gas (HRG) model with physical hadron masses significantly disagrees with lattice QCD data for the trace anomaly, strangeness fluctuations, and baryon number fluctuations; however, this discrepancy is largely reduced if the hadron spectrum is modified to account for the larger quark masses and finite lattice spacing errors used in the lattice calculations, allowing the HRG to describe lattice data reasonably well up to temperatures near the crossover transition.

## Method

The authors compared lattice QCD results for the trace anomaly (energy density minus three times pressure), baryon number fluctuations, and strangeness fluctuations with predictions from the HRG model. They modified the hadron masses in the HRG to match the cutoff-dependent masses observed in asqtad lattice simulations, using parametrizations of the pseudo-scalar meson splittings, vector meson masses, and baryon masses as functions of lattice spacing and pion mass. They then constructed a parametrization of the QCD equation of state that combines the HRG model at low temperatures (below about 180 MeV) with a fit to lattice data at high temperatures (above 250 MeV), imposing constraints on the entropy density at 800 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Baryon number fluctuations from asqtad action on N_tau=6 lattices fall considerably below the HRG prediction with physical baryon masses; using modified baryon masses with cutoff m_cut^B=1.8 GeV or 2.5 GeV greatly improves agreement.
- Strangeness fluctuations on N_tau=8 lattices for asqtad action are significantly below the HRG prediction with physical hadron masses; the HRG with modified masses (m_cut^B=1.8 or 2.5 GeV) gives a good description of the lattice data.
- The trace anomaly from lattice QCD on N_tau=8 lattices is well described by the HRG with modified masses up to temperatures of about 180 MeV.
- Three parametrizations of the trace anomaly are provided: s95p-v1, s95n-v1, and s90f-v1, with matching temperatures T_0 of 183.8 MeV, 171.8 MeV, and 170.0 MeV respectively, and with entropy density at T=800 MeV set to 95% or 90% of the ideal gas limit.
- The HRG trace anomaly is parametrized as (epsilon-3P)/T^4 = 4.654 T - 879 T^3 + 8081 T^4 - 7039000 T^10 (with T in GeV), valid for low temperatures.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons of lattice QCD thermodynamics with the HRG model, which had shown good agreement for charge susceptibilities at low temperatures (Redlich et al., 2004; Redlich et al., 2006; fluctuations paper). It directly confronts recent lattice calculations with light quark masses (asqtad and p4 actions from the HotQCD collaboration) against HRG predictions, finding that the naive HRG with physical masses fails but a modified HRG accounting for lattice artifacts succeeds. The resulting parametrized equation of state is intended for use in hydrodynamic models of heavy ion collisions, where it can be compared to earlier EoS with first-order phase transitions (Kolb et al.) and to the lattice-inspired crossover EoS of Pasi (2005) that overpredicts proton elliptic flow.
