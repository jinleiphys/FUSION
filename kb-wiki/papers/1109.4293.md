# $^6$He-triton cluster states in $^9$Li

> Cluster states in $^9$Li are investigated with calculations of a $^6$He-$t$ cluster model. Results suggest $^6$He-$t$ cluster states near the $^6$He-$t$ threshold energy. These states construct a $K^π=1/2^-$ band and their neutron configuration is similar to that of the $K^π=0^+_2$ band in $^{10}$Be.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that $^6$He-triton cluster states exist in $^9$Li near the $^6$He+$t$ threshold energy, forming a $K^\pi=1/2^-$ band, but these states are resonances above threshold whose widths vary significantly; the $1/2^-$ and $3/2^-$ members may have widths of order 1 MeV while the $5/2^-$ and $7/2^-$ members are expected to have larger widths.

## Method

The authors performed generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations using Bloch-Brink $^6$He+$t$ cluster wave functions with six different valence neutron configurations for the $^6$He cluster, superposing wave functions with inter-cluster distances $d=1$ to $8$ fm for a bound-state approximation and $d=1$ to $15$ fm for resonance analysis. To evaluate resonance features, they introduced a pseudo potential and analyzed the fragmentation of pseudo-bound-state amplitudes in the energy spectra at zero pseudo-potential strength, validating the method against the known $2^+$ resonance in $^8$Be from an $\alpha+\alpha$ cluster model.

## Key numbers

- Calculated $^9$Li ground state energy: $-34.3$ MeV (overestimates experimental $-45.3$ MeV); $^6$He+$t$ threshold energy: $-26.0$ MeV (overestimates experimental $-37.8$ MeV)
- Calculated $^{10}$Be ground state energy: $-56.9$ MeV (overestimates experimental $-65.0$ MeV); $^6$He+$\alpha$ threshold energy: $-46.8$ MeV (overestimates experimental $-57.6$ MeV)
- $B(E2)$ strength for $3/2^-_3 \rightarrow 1/2^-_2$ transition: $31$ e$^2$fm$^4$
- $B(E2)$ strength for $7/2^-_2 \rightarrow 3/2^-_3$ transition: $26$ e$^2$fm$^4$
- $B(E2)$ strength for $5/2^-_2 \rightarrow 1/2^-_2$ transition: $15$ e$^2$fm$^4$
- Overlap of $3/2^-_3$ state with $P^{J=3/2,-}_{M,K=1/2}\Phi_{(d)}(d=5 \text{ fm})$: 65%; with $P^{J=3/2,-}_{M,K=1/2}\Phi_{(a)}(d=5 \text{ fm})$: 45%
- Width parameter $\nu=0.235$ fm$^{-2}$ used for all clusters
- Generator coordinate $d$ values: $1,2,\cdots,8$ fm (bound-state approximation) and $1,2,\cdots,15$ fm (resonance analysis)

## Context

This work extends previous AMD calculations by Suhara and Kanada-En'yo (2009) that indicated a largely deformed $^6$He+$t$ structure in excited $^9$Li states, and is motivated by Ikeda's threshold rule (Ikeda 1968) which predicts cluster states near corresponding threshold energies. The $K^\pi=1/2^-$ band in $^9$Li is analogous to the $K^\pi=0^+_2$ band in $^{10}$Be, which previous studies (e.g., Dote 1997, Itagaki 2001, Ito 2003) described as molecular orbital states with two valence neutrons in $\sigma$ orbitals around a $2\alpha$ core. The results enable experimental searches for these predicted resonances in $^9$Li and provide a framework for understanding strong-to-weak coupling transitions in neutron-rich cluster states.
