# Hybrid chiral condensate in the external magnetic field

> We study the phase diagram of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in the external magnetic field within the mean-field approximation, taking into account the inhomogeneous chiral condensate. It is shown that there appears a new type of the chiral condensate, endowed with two features of real kink crystal and dual chiral density wave, in the magnetic field. We also show that there are first order phase transitions between different inhomogeneous phases in the presence of magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new type of inhomogeneous chiral condensate, called the hybrid chiral condensate (HCC), appears in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model under an external magnetic field, but this result is conditional on the mean-field approximation and the specific choice of the magnetic field being parallel to the modulation direction; the authors also note that the RKC phase itself does not appear in the phase diagram when the magnetic field is present, because the phase degree of freedom is always favored.

## Method

The authors studied the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation with an external magnetic field, considering one-dimensional spatial modulation of the chiral condensate along the magnetic field direction. They introduced a new condensate ansatz called the hybrid chiral condensate (HCC), which is the product of the real kink crystal (RKC) and the dual chiral density wave (DCDW) forms, and derived the quark energy spectrum by combining Nickel's method with Frolov's method, using the Landau gauge and the proper-time regularization for the vacuum contribution. The thermodynamic potential was then minimized numerically with respect to the order parameters (amplitude m, wavevector q, and modulus parameter nu) to obtain the phase diagram at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff Lambda = 660 MeV and coupling G*Lambda^2 = 6.35 were used, which reproduce f_pi = 93 MeV and the constituent quark mass approximately 330 MeV in the vacuum.
- Triple points appear at (mu, sqrt(eB)) approximately (320 MeV, 110 MeV) and (mu, sqrt(eB)) approximately (350 MeV, 30 MeV).
- The approximation Omega(B) approximately Omega(B=0) + eB*Omega^(1) was found valid for sqrt(eB) < 0.2*Lambda approximately 120 MeV.
- At sqrt(eB) = 60 MeV, the order parameters behave like continuous functions of the chemical potential; at sqrt(eB) = 120 MeV, the order parameters exhibit discontinuous jumps.

## Context

This work extends the study of inhomogeneous chiral phases in QCD, which was previously explored using the NJL model and Schwinger-Dyson approach by Nickel (2009) and others, where the real kink crystal (RKC) was found more favorable than the dual chiral density wave (DCDW) in the absence of a magnetic field. The paper builds on the work of Frolov et al. (2010), who first studied the DCDW phase in a magnetic field but did not consider the RKC possibility, and on Tatsumi (2014) who discussed spectral asymmetry and its topological origin. The results enable future studies of the QCD phase diagram under strong magnetic fields relevant for compact stars and heavy-ion collisions, and suggest that the de Haas-van Alphen effect should appear when full contributions of higher Landau levels are included.
