# Newly observed $B(5970)$ and the predictions of its spin and strange partners

> Using the effective Lagrangian approach, we study the decay behavior of the newly observed $B(5970)$ meson, which is assigned as the $2^3S_1$ state in the $B$ meson family. What is more important is that we predict the information of the partial and total decay decay widths of $B(2^1S_0)$, $B_s(2^1S_0)$ and $B_s(2^3S_1)$ as the spin and strange partners of $B(5970)$, which is valuable to future experimental search for these missing states.

# Newly observed $B(5970)$ and the predictions of its spin and strange partners

## Key claim
The newly observed $B(5970)$ meson can be assigned as the $2^3S_1$ state (first radial excitation of $B^*(5325)$) in the $B$ meson family, and its spin partner $B(2^1S_0)$ and strange partners $B_s(2^1S_0)$ and $B_s(2^3S_1)$ are predicted to have narrow widths that should be experimentally accessible, though the authors note that chiral corrections (from higher-order operators and chiral loops) could be very large and are not included in the error estimates.

## Method
The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach combining heavy quark effective theory (HQET) and chiral symmetry to compute OZI-allowed two-body strong decays of $B(5970)$ and its partners. They calculated ratios of partial decay widths that are independent of the unknown coupling constant $g$, then extracted $g$ by combining the computed branching fractions with the experimental total width of $B(5970)$. This extracted $g$ value was then used to predict the partial and total decay widths of $B(2^1S_0)$, $B_s(2^1S_0)$, and $B_s(2^3S_1)$.

## Key numbers
- $B(5970)^0$ mass: $5978 \pm 5 \pm 12$ MeV; width: $70 \pm 18 \pm 31$ MeV
- $B(5970)^+$ mass: $5961 \pm 5 \pm 12$ MeV; width: $60 \pm 20 \pm 40$ MeV
- Extracted coupling constant $g = 0.148 \pm 0.009$
- $B(2^1S_0)^+$ total width: $40 \pm 11$ MeV (using input mass $5890 \pm 30$ MeV)
- $B_s(2^1S_0)$ total width: $25.70 \pm 10.95$ MeV (using input mass $5980 \pm 30$ MeV)
- $B_s(2^3S_1)$ total width: $36.66 \pm 14.06$ MeV (using input mass $6000 \pm 30$ MeV)
- Ratio $R_1 = \Gamma(B(5970)^+ \to B^* \pi) / \Gamma(B(5970)^+ \to B \pi) = 1.64 \pm 0.01$
- Branching fraction for $B(5970)^+ \to B^{*0} \pi^+$: $(38.5 \pm 0.6)\%$
- Branching fraction for $B(2^1S_0)^+ \to B^{*0} \pi^+$: $(66.3 \pm 0.2)\%$

## Context
This work extends the study of radially excited bottom mesons following the CDF observation of $B(5970)^{+/0}$ (Aaltonen 2013). It uses the same effective Lagrangian framework previously applied to charmed mesons by Colangelo (2007) and others, and compares its results to those from the quark pair creation (QPC) model used by Sun (2014). The predictions for $B(2^1S_0)$, $B_s(2^1S_0)$, and $B_s(2^3S_1)$ provide specific decay channels and widths that can guide experimental searches at LHCb, CDF, and D0. The authors note that after completing this work, a related theoretical paper by Wang (2014) appeared using heavy meson effective theory to study $B_1(5721)$, $B_2(5747)$, $B_{s1}(5830)$, $B_{s2}(5840)$, and $B(5970)$.
