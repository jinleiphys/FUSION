# A three-parameter characterization of neutron stars' mass-radius relation and equation of state

> Numerous models of neutron star (NS) equation of state (EoS) exist based on different superdense-matter physics approaches. Nevertheless, some NS properties show universal (EoS-independent) relations. Here, we propose a novel class of such universalities. Despite different physics inputs, a wide class of realistic nucleonic, hyperonic, and hybrid EoS models can be accurately described using only three parameters. For a given EoS, these are the mass and radius of the maximum-mass NS (or pressure and density in its center) and the radius of a half-maximum-mass star. With such a parametrization, we build universal analytic expressions for mass-radius and pressure-density relations. They form a semi-analytic mapping from the mass-radius relation to the EoS in NS cores (the so-called inverse Oppenheimer-Volkoff mapping). This mapping simplifies the process of inferring the EoS from observations of NS masses and radii. Applying it to current NS observations we set new limits on the high-density end of the EoS.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a wide class of realistic neutron star (NS) equation of state (EoS) models (nucleonic, hyperonic, and hybrid) can be accurately described using only three parameters, enabling a semi-analytic inverse Oppenheimer-Volkoff mapping (IOVM). The hedge is that this approximation is valid only for masses \(M\gtrsim 1\,M_\odot\) and that the universal relations are fitted to a finite set of 169 theoretical EoS models, so the method may fail if the true EoS differs strongly from those in the collection.

## Method

The authors collected 169 theoretical EoS models (100 nucleonic, 36 with hyperons or \(\Delta\)-isobars, 33 hybrid) and verified that the mass-radius (\(M-R\)) curves can be described by a universal analytic expression depending on three parameters: the maximum-mass NS mass \(\Mmax\), its radius \(\Rmax\), and the ratio \(\ratio = \Rhalf/\Rmax\) where \(\Rhalf\) is the radius of a half-maximum-mass star. They also derived correlations linking \(\Mmax\), \(\Rmax\), and the central sound speed \(\csmax\) to the central pressure \(\Pmax\) and density \(\rhomax\). Using these fits, they constructed a semi-analytic IOVM that maps observed \(M-R\) data to the EoS (\(P-\rho\) relation) in NS cores, and applied Bayesian inference to a set of 9 sources with joint mass-radius measurements and 8 sources with mass-only measurements to constrain the EoS.

## Context

This work extends the known concept of universal (EoS-independent) relations for neutron stars, such as the I-Love-Q relation by Yagi and Yunes (2013), by proposing a new class that directly parametrizes the \(M-R\) and \(P-\rho\) relations. It provides an explicit semi-analytic inverse Oppenheimer-Volkoff mapping, simplifying the inference of the EoS from observations compared to computationally expensive methods that repeatedly solve the TOV equations. The results are consistent with but narrower than recent constraints from Annala et al. (2023) and Brandes et al. (2023), and the method enables rapid testing of different data combinations, such as including the recent NICER observation of PSR J0437-4715 by Choudhury et al. (2024).
