# Significant in-medium reduction of the mass of eta' mesons in sqrt(s(NN)) = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions

> PHENIX and STAR data on the intercept parameter of the two-pion Bose-Einstein correlation functions in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 200$ GeV Au+Au collisions were analysed in terms of various models of hadronic abundances. To describe these data, an in-medium $η^\prime$ mass decrease of at least 200 MeV was needed in each case.

## Key claim

To describe PHENIX and STAR data on the two-pion Bose-Einstein correlation intercept parameter in sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions, an in-medium mass decrease of the eta' meson of at least 200 MeV was required at the 99.9% confidence level, within the considered class of hadronic abundance models.

## Method

The authors performed extensive Monte Carlo simulations using several hadronic abundance models (ALCOR, FRITIOF, RQMD, and thermal models by Kaneta, Rafelski, Stachel and collaborators) and the JETSET 7.4 resonance decay package. They calculated the number of in-medium eta' mesons using an improved Hagedorn formula that included a prefactor with an expansion-dynamics-dependent exponent alpha, and introduced a slope parameter B^{-1} to describe the transverse mass spectra of eta' mesons produced when the condensed in-medium eta' mesons come on-shell. Systematic studies were carried out for various values of alpha and other model parameters like the eta' freezeout temperature T_cond.

## Key numbers

* In-medium eta' mass decrease of at least 200 MeV was required at the 99.9% confidence level to describe both PHENIX and STAR data on lambda*(m_T)/lambda*_max in sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions.
* The best values for the in-medium mass of eta' mesons were in the theoretically predicted range (from Kapusta) or slightly below it.
* For the simulations shown in the confidence level distribution, parameters included alpha=0, T_FO = T_cond = 177 MeV, and mean transverse flow u_T = 0.48 (from Vance).

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Vance and by PHENIX on the sensitivity of the two-pion correlation intercept parameter to enhanced eta' abundance. The authors improved upon those simulations by using an improved Hagedorn formula with an expansion-dynamics-dependent exponent. The results support theoretical predictions by Kapusta and others that the U_A(1) or chiral symmetry may be temporarily restored in high-energy heavy ion collisions, leading to a reduced eta' mass. The analysis enables future experimental searches for the predicted low-transverse-momentum enhancement in the eta' spectrum, which could be more than a factor of 10 above the scenario without in-medium mass reduction.
