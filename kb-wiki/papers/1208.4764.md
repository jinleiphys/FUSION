# 2D Gauge Field Theory

> We show from the action integral that under the assumption of longitudinal dominance and transverse confinement, QCD4 in (3+1) dimensional space-time can be approximately compactified into QCD2 in (1+1) dimensional space-time. In such a process, we find the relation between the coupling constant g(2D) in QCD2 and the coupling constant $g(4D)$ in QCD4. We also show that quarks and gluons in QCD2 acquire masses as a result of the compactification.

# 2D Gauge Field Theory

## Key claim
Under the assumptions of longitudinal dominance and transverse confinement, QCD4 in (3+1) dimensions can be approximately compactified into QCD2 in (1+1) dimensions via the action integral, yielding relations between the coupling constants and showing that quarks and gluons acquire masses from the compactification process itself, but the result depends on the specific transverse wave functions and the validity of neglecting transverse gauge field components.

## Method
The authors start from the SU(N) gauge-invariant QCD4 action integral and impose longitudinal dominance (neglecting transverse gauge field components A₁ and A₂) and transverse confinement (described by a scalar confining interaction S(r⊥) added to the quark rest mass). They decompose the 4D Dirac fermion field into products of transverse wave functions G₁(r⊥), G₂(r⊥) and 2D longitudinal fields f±(x⁰,x³), then integrate over the transverse coordinates x¹,x² to obtain a compactified 2D action. The gauge field part is similarly reduced, introducing a gluon mass term from the transverse gradient of the wave function normalization.

## Key numbers
- The 2D coupling constant g(2D) is given by g(2D) = ∫ dx¹ dx² g(4D) [|G₁(r⊥)|² + |G₂(r⊥)|²]^(3/2), where the transverse wave functions are normalized to ∫ dx¹ dx² (|G₁|² + |G₂|²) = 1.
- The transverse quark mass m_qT is defined as m_qT = ∫ dx¹ dx² { m(r⊥) (|G₁|² − |G₂|²) + [G₁*(p₁ − ip₂)G₂ − h.c.] }, and is shown to equal the Lagrange multiplier λ, which is the eigenvalue of the transverse motion equations.
- The gluon compactification mass m_gT is given by m_gT² = (1/2) ∫ dx¹ dx² [ {∂₁(Σ|G_i|²)^(1/2)}² + {∂₂(Σ|G_i|²)^(1/2)}² ].
- The 2D gauge field action acquires a mass term (1/8π) m_gT² [A_a^μ(2D) A_μ^a(2D)].

## Context
This work extends the large-N_c planar diagram approach of t'Hooft (1974) and the flux tube/string fragmentation picture used in the Lund model (Andersson 1983, Wong 1991, 1994, 2009) by providing a formal compactification from QCD4 to QCD2 starting from the action integral. It enables quantitative connections between 4D and 2D parameters (coupling constant, quark mass, gluon mass) for applications in high-energy hadron collisions and e⁺e⁻ annihilations, where longitudinal dynamics dominate and transverse momenta are limited. The results depend on the unknown transverse wave functions G₁, G₂, leaving open the problem of determining these from the confining potential.
