# Total J/psi production cross section at the LHC

> We evaluate the production cross section for direct J/psi integrated in P_T for various collision energies of the LHC in the QCD-based Colour-Singlet Model. We consider the LO contribution from gluon fusion as well as the one from a fusion of a gluon and a charm quark from the colliding protons. The rapidity distribution of the yield is evaluated in the central region relevant for the ATLAS and CMS detectors, as well as in the more forward region relevant for the ALICE and LHC-b detectors. The results obtained here are compatible with those of other approaches within the range of the theoretical uncertainties which are admittedly very large. This suggests that the "mere" measurements of the yield at the LHC will not help disentangle between the different possible quarkonium production mechanisms.

## Key claim

The Colour-Singlet Model (CSM) predictions for the total J/ψ production cross section at LHC energies are compatible with other approaches (Colour Evaporation Model, gluon tower model) only within very large theoretical uncertainties, suggesting that simply measuring the integrated yield will not distinguish between different quarkonium production mechanisms.

## Method

The author evaluates the leading-order (LO) contribution from gluon fusion (gg → J/ψ g) and from gluon-charm fusion (cg → J/ψ c) in the Colour-Singlet Model, using the same parameter choices for charm quark mass (m_c), renormalisation scale (μ_R), and factorisation scale (μ_F) as a prior study (Brodsky 2009). The rapidity distributions are computed for central (ATLAS, CMS) and forward (ALICE, LHC-b) detector regions at collision energies of 2.75, 7, and 14 TeV.

## Key numbers

- At √s = 7 TeV, the gg contribution to dσ/dy at y=0 × Br ranges from 100 to 800 nb.
- At √s = 14 TeV, the gg contribution to dσ/dy at y=0 × Br ranges from 200 to 1400 nb.
- The Colour Evaporation Model gives central (upper) values of 140 nb (400 nb) at 7 TeV and 200 nb (550 nb) at 14 TeV.
- The gluon tower model gives 300 nb at 7 TeV and 480 nb at 14 TeV, with an expected accuracy of about a factor of 2-3.
- The gluon-charm fusion contribution remains a visible fraction of the direct yield at LHC energies; the ratio (dσ_cg/dy)/(dσ_cg+gg/dy) is computed for m_c = 1.4 GeV using three charm distribution choices.

## Context

This work extends the Colour-Singlet Model predictions from RHIC energies (where LO and NLO results agreed with PHENIX data, Brodsky 2009) to LHC energies, and compares with the Colour Evaporation Model (Bedjidian 2004) and the gluon tower model (Khoze 2004). The large theoretical uncertainties, reminiscent of total charm production (Vogt 2000), motivate studying other observables such as J/ψ production in association with a charm quark, a photon, or a c-cbar pair to discriminate between mechanisms.
