# Effective chiral model with Polyakov loops and its application to hot/dense medium

> We study the thermodynamics of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with Polyakov loops, where spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and confinement are taken into account. We focus on the phase structure of the model and explore the susceptibilities associated with corresponding order parameters under the mean-field approximation.

## Key claim

The PNJL model, under mean-field approximation, shows that deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration do not generally coincide along the same phase boundary; their coincidence is accidental in this model, and the polynomial form of the effective gluon potential yields unphysical negative diagonal Polyakov loop susceptibilities in a broad temperature range above the chiral critical temperature.

## Method

The authors study the thermodynamics of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model extended with Polyakov loops (PNJL model) under the mean-field approximation. They compute the chiral susceptibility \(\chi_{mm}\) and the Polyakov loop susceptibility \(\chi_{l\bar{l}} = \langle \bar{\Phi}\Phi \rangle - \langle \bar{\Phi} \rangle \langle \Phi \rangle\) at zero chemical potential in the chiral limit, and use the peak positions of these susceptibilities to map the phase boundaries in the \((T,\mu)\)-plane. They also examine the diagonal Polyakov loop susceptibilities \(\chi_{ll}\) and \(\chi_{\bar{l}\bar{l}}\) and compare their behavior to lattice results.

## Key numbers

- The tricritical point (TCP) is located at \((T_c = 157, \mu_c = 266)\) MeV.
- At \(\mu = 0\) in the chiral limit, the chiral susceptibility \(\chi_{mm}\) exhibits a very narrow divergent peak at the chiral critical temperature \(T_{\rm ch}\), while the Polyakov loop susceptibility \(\chi_{l\bar{l}}\) remains finite at all temperatures.
- The effective Polyakov loop potential with \(\mu\)-independent coefficients is considered a good approximation only for \(\mu/T < 1\).

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model approach of Fukushima (2004) and Ratti, Thaler, and Weise (2006) by focusing on susceptibilities of order parameters rather than just thermodynamic observables. The results contradict lattice QCD findings for two flavors (Lattice collaborations, 2006) which show deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration along the same critical line. The paper identifies a deficiency in the polynomial form of the Polyakov loop potential used in earlier PNJL studies, and points to the improved logarithmic potential of Roessner, Ratti, and Weise (2007) that accounts for the Haar measure and yields positive diagonal Polyakov loop susceptibilities in agreement with lattice results. This enables future work to refine the effective gluon potential to include \(\mu\)- and \(N_f\)-dependence, as explored by Schaefer, Pawlowski, and Wambach (2007).
