# Baryonic Bound States in the Non-Local NJL Model

> Baryons, as three-quark bound states, require a covariant treatment in the intermediate-energy regime where perturbative QCD is no longer applicable and where nonperturbative correlations dominate. This article reformulates the content of the CERN Baltic Conference 2025 presentation on baryonic bound states in the non-local Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. We review how the relativistic Faddeev approach reduces the three-body quark problem to an effective quark--diquark bound-state problem, describe the scalar and axial-vector diquark channels, and show how the resulting quark--diquark Bethe--Salpeter equation can be written as an eigenvalue problem for the baryon mass. The non-local NJL framework, motivated by QCD-based nonlocal interactions and Dyson--Schwinger considerations, provides a compact description in which baryon masses and form factors are extracted from the numerical solution of coupled integral equations.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how the relativistic Faddeev approach reduces the three-quark baryon problem to an effective quark–diquark bound-state problem within the non-local Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, but the actual numerical results for baryon masses and form factors are presented only as a future goal rather than as completed calculations.

## Method

The authors start from the six-point quark correlator and identify baryonic bound states as poles in this correlator. They decompose the three-quark scattering kernel into pairwise interactions (the Faddeev approximation), neglect irreducible three-quark forces, and express each Faddeev component in terms of diquark correlations in scalar and axial-vector channels. The resulting quark–diquark Bethe–Salpeter equation is written as an eigenvalue problem for the baryon mass, with the mass determined by the condition λ(M²)=1. The numerical strategy involves expanding the vertex and wave functions in a finite covariant basis, expanding the angular dependence in Chebyshev polynomials, and solving coupled one-dimensional integral equations for the Chebyshev moments.

## Key numbers

- The scalar diquark propagator is taken as d₅(P) = −i/(P² − m_S² + iε), where m_S is the scalar diquark mass.
- The axial-vector diquark propagator is d_{μν}(P) = [−i/(P² − m_A² + iε)] [g_{μν} − (1−ξ)P_μ P_ν/(P² − ξ m_A²)], where m_A is the axial-vector diquark mass.
- The baryon mass M is determined by the eigenvalue condition λ(M²) = 1.
- A momentum partitioning parameter η ∈ [0,1] is introduced; physical observables must be independent of this unphysical parameter, used as a stability criterion.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Faddeev approach as developed by Oettel (2002), Ahlig (2001), and Rezaeian (2004, 2005) to the non-local NJL model, which is motivated by QCD-based nonlocal interactions and Dyson–Schwinger considerations. The non-local NJL framework is expected to lower baryon ground-state masses relative to local NJL models, indicating more compact baryonic states, as noted by Rezaeian (2004). The paper enables future numerical implementation of the non-local interaction directly in the quark–diquark kernel, with comparison to both local NJL calculations and phenomenological expectations.
