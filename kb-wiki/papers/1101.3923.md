# Testing Partonic Charge Symmetry at a High-Energy Electron Collider

> We examine the possibility that one could measure partonic charge symmetry violation (CSV) by comparing neutrino or antineutrino production through charged-current reactions induced by electrons or positrons at a possible electron collider at the LHC. We calculate the magnitude of CSV that might be expected at such a facility. We show that this is likely to be a several percent effect, substantially larger than the typical CSV effects expected for partonic reactions.

## Key claim

Partonic charge symmetry violation (CSV) could be measured at a proposed electron-proton/deuteron collider at the LHC (LHeC) with effects of several percent in the ratio R-(x), substantially larger than the typical 1% level CSV effects expected for most partonic reactions, because the valence CSV distributions experience a smaller shift to low x with increasing Q^2 than the valence PDFs.

## Method

The authors calculate the charged-current structure functions F2 for electron and positron deep inelastic scattering on protons and deuterons at LHeC energies (50 GeV electrons on 7 TeV hadrons, Q^2 = 10^5 GeV^2). They define the observable R-(x) as the difference between electron-deuteron and positron-deuteron CC structure functions divided by the average proton CC structure function, which is proportional to valence quark CSV distributions plus the strange quark asymmetry. The valence CSV contributions are computed from two sources: QED splitting effects (photon radiation by quarks, evolved via DGLAP equations) and quark mass difference effects (from u-d quark mass difference and n-p mass difference, calculated using the Sather prescription and Rodionov et al. bag model results), with the strange quark asymmetry taken from NuTeV dimuon data fits.

## Key numbers

- The predicted ratio R-(x) at x = 0.6 is greater than 6% when all contributions (QED splitting, quark mass CSV, and strange quark asymmetry) are included at Q^2 = 10^5 GeV^2.
- The lattice calculation of the second moment of valence CSV distributions gives δU+ = -0.0023(6) and δD+ = +0.0020(3) at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2.
- The quark model valence CSV second moments at a similar scale are δU- = -0.0014 and δD- = +0.0015.
- The n-p mass difference used is δM = 1.3 MeV, and the diquark mass difference estimate is δm̃ ≈ 4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of partonic charge symmetry violation by Londergan, Thomas, and collaborators (2009) and the MRST group (2003, 2004), who found current upper limits on CSV at the 5-10% level. The proposed LHeC observable R-(x) could provide a much larger signal than typical CSV effects (1% or smaller) and would test both the QED splitting mechanism for CSV (previously tested by ZEUS in ep → eγX) and quark mass difference effects, with the latter recently confirmed by lattice calculations from Horsley et al. (2010). The measurement would also help constrain the strange quark asymmetry, which has been extracted from CCFR and NuTeV dimuon data by multiple groups including CTEQ, MSTW, and NNPDF.
