# Threshold Two-Pion Photo- and Electroproduction: More neutrals than expected

> We present an exploratory study of two pion photo-- and electroproduction off the nucleon in the threshold region. To calculate the pertinent amplitudes, we make use of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. We show that due to finite chiral loops the production cross section for final states with two neutral pions is considerably enhanced. The experimental implications are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, contrary to the pattern in single-pion production where charged-pion channels dominate, two-pion photo- and electroproduction near threshold shows a "considerable enhancement" of cross sections for final states with two neutral pions, driven by finite chiral one-loop corrections. This result is presented as an exploratory study within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) up to and including order \(q^3\), with the caveat that the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance contributions are included but found to be under control because the small denominator \(m_\Delta - m - 2M_\pi\) cancels in the numerator.

## Method

The authors used heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) to compute the threshold multipole amplitudes \(M_{1,2,3}\) (transverse) and \(N_{1,2,3}\) (longitudinal) for two-pion photo- and electroproduction off the nucleon, working to chiral order \(q^3\) (one-loop level). They included tree diagrams from the leading and next-to-leading order chiral Lagrangians, one-loop diagrams with leading-order vertices, and \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance contributions estimated via resonance saturation, with the off-shell parameter \(Z\) varied over its allowed range (\(-0.8 < Z < 0.3\)) to check sensitivity. Total cross sections were approximated using the threshold amplitude multiplied by the three-body phase space, with a handy analytic formula given for the energy dependence near threshold.

## Key numbers

* The threshold photon lab energy for \(\gamma p \to \pi^+ \pi^- p\) is \(E_\gamma^{\rm thr} = 320.66\) MeV; for \(\gamma p \to \pi^0 \pi^0 p\) it is \(E_\gamma^{\rm thr} = 308.77\) MeV, reflecting the 4.6 MeV pion mass difference and an 11.9 MeV gap between thresholds.
* The \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance lies only 14.6 MeV above the two-pion production threshold (\(m_\Delta - m - 2 M_{\pi^+} = 14.6\) MeV).
* The leading-order transverse multipole \(M_1\) is zero at order \(q^0\) and \(q\); at order \(q^2\) it receives only a relativistic nucleon-pole contribution: \(M_1 = e g_A^2 M_\pi / (4 m^2 F_\pi^2) + \mathcal{O}(q^2)\).
* The loop contributions to the photoproduction transverse multipoles are complex constants: \(M_2^{\rm loop} = (e g_A^2 M_\pi)/(64 \pi F_\pi^4) \{ 3\pi/2 + i [2\sqrt{3} - \ln(2+\sqrt{3})] \}\) and \(M_3^{\rm loop} = (e g_A^2 M_\pi)/(256 \pi F_\pi^4) \{ 6 - 3\pi/2 + i [2\sqrt{3} - \ln(2+\sqrt{3})] \}\).
* The \(\Delta\) contribution parameter \(B_\Delta\) varies between 9.9 GeV and 15.1 GeV over the allowed range of the off-shell parameter \(Z\), showing weak dependence.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study of two-pion photoproduction by Dahm and Drechsel (1993), who used Weinberg's chiral pion-nucleon Lagrangian but did not include the anomalous magnetic moment coupling or loop effects. The paper provides the first complete one-loop HBCHPT calculation of threshold two-pion photo- and electroproduction, demonstrating that chiral loops dramatically enhance neutral-pion final states, a counter-intuitive result relative to single-pion production. The predictions are intended to motivate new low-energy measurements at facilities like MAMI, and the formalism enables future calculations at higher chiral orders (e.g., \(q^4\)) for improved precision.
