# Parton distributions of light quarks and antiquarks in the proton

> An algebraic Ansatz for the proton's Poincaré-covariant wave function, which includes both scalar and pseudovector diquark correlations, is used to calculate proton valence, sea, and glue distribution functions (DFs). Regarding contemporary data, a material pseudovector diquark component in the proton is necessary for an explanation of the neutron-proton structure function ratio; and a modest Pauli blocking effect in the gluon splitting function is sufficient to explain the proton's light-quark antimatter asymmetry. In comparison with pion DFs, the light-front momentum fractions carried by all identifiable parton classes are the same; on the other hand, the higher moments are different. Understanding these features may provide insights that explain distinctions between Nambu-Goldstone bosons and seemingly less complex hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a material pseudovector diquark component in the proton is necessary to explain the neutron-proton structure function ratio, and that a modest Pauli blocking effect in the gluon splitting function is sufficient to explain the proton's light-quark antimatter asymmetry. The authors hedge that while the light-front momentum fractions carried by all identifiable parton classes are the same between proton and pion, the higher moments differ, which may provide insights into distinctions between Nambu-Goldstone bosons and other hadrons.

## Method

The authors use an algebraic Ansatz for the proton's Poincaré-covariant wave function, representing the proton as a quark+diquark system with both scalar (isoscalar-scalar, 0+) and pseudovector (isovector-pseudovector, 1+) diquark correlations. They calculate valence-quark distribution functions (DFs) at a hadron scale ζ_H = 0.331 GeV, then evolve these DFs to higher scales using an all-orders DGLAP evolution scheme based on a process-independent effective charge. Pauli blocking is implemented via a modification of the gluon splitting function that favors d+dbar production over u+ubar.

## Key numbers

* Hadron scale: ζ_H² = (0.331(2) GeV)², fixed by QCD's renormalisation group invariant gluon mass.
* Model parameters: M = 0.4 GeV (dressed light quark mass), M₀ = 0.78 GeV (scalar diquark mass), M₁ = 0.92 GeV (pseudovector diquark mass).
* Large-x limit of valence-quark ratio: lim_{x→1} d_V^p(x;ζ_H)/u_V^p(x;ζ_H) = 0.230(55) for r₁₀ = 0.47(6).
* Large-x limit of neutron-proton structure function ratio: lim_{x→1} F₂ⁿ(x)/F₂ᵖ(x) = 0.453(46).
* Momentum fractions at ζ_M² = 7.2 GeV²: ⟨x⟩_u = 0.31, ⟨x⟩_d = 0.14, total valence = 0.45, sea = 0.12, glue = 0.43.
* Momentum fractions at ζ_SQ² = 30 GeV²: ⟨x⟩_u = 0.28, ⟨x⟩_d = 0.13, total valence = 0.41, sea = 0.14, glue = 0.45.
* Gottfried sum rule discrepancy on domain [0.004, 0.8]: I_{d-u}^{0.004,0.8} = 0.091(23) for Pauli blocking parameter g = 0.4(1).

## Context

This work extends continuum Schwinger function methods previously applied to meson DFs (Chang 2014, Chen 2016, Ding 2019, Cui 2020) and proton structure (Roberts 2013, Segovia 2014, Xu 2015). It directly addresses the MARATHON experiment data (Abrams 2021) on the neutron-proton structure function ratio and the SeaQuest/E906 data (SeaQuest 2021) on the light-quark sea asymmetry. The results enable comparisons with global fits such as CT18 (Hou 2019) and provide a framework for understanding distinctions between Nambu-Goldstone bosons and other hadrons through the different higher moments of their DFs.
