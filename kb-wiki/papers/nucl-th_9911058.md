# Isoscalar off-shell effects in threshold pion production from pd collisions

> We test the presence of pion-nucleon isoscalar off-shell effects in the $pd\to π^+ t$ reaction around the threshold region. We find that these effects significantly modify the production cross section and that they may provide the missing strength needed to reproduce the data at threshold.

## Key claim

The paper tests whether pion-nucleon isoscalar off-shell effects, previously invoked to explain the missing strength in the $pp\rightarrow pp\pi^o$ reaction, also provide the missing strength needed to reproduce the $pd\rightarrow \pi^+ t$ cross section data near threshold. The authors find that these effects significantly modify the production cross section and may provide the missing strength, but they stress that large uncertainties remain in the off-shell extrapolation and that other production mechanisms omitted here should be included in future calculations.

## Method

The authors calculate the $pd\rightarrow \pi^+ t$ cross section using a model that includes one-body $p$-wave production, $p$-wave $\Delta$ rescattering, isovector $\rho$-exchange rescattering, and isoscalar $s$-wave rescattering with off-shell effects parameterized following the Hamilton prescription (a short-range repulsive term plus a $\sigma$-exchange attractive term). The three-nucleon bound-state wave function for the triton is taken from a separable representation of the Paris interaction, and initial-state interactions are treated by solving Faddeev-type AGS equations for the $n$-$d$ system.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar off-shell form uses $m_\sigma = 550$ MeV, $a_\sigma = 0.22\ m_\pi^{-1}$, $a_{sr} = -0.233\ m_\pi^{-1}$, and $\epsilon = m_\pi/M$, leading to an on-shell value of $\lambda_O \approx 0.007$.
- The isovector coupling is set to $\lambda_I = 0.045$ via the $\rho$-dominance relation.
- The pion decay constant is taken as $f_\pi \simeq 93.2$ MeV, and the axial nucleonic normalization as $g_A \simeq 1.255$.
- The $\pi NN$ coupling constant $f_{\pi NN}^2/(4\pi)$ is cited in the range 0.0735 to 0.081 from various sources.
- The isoscalar coupling $\lambda_O$ is cited with indetermination ranging from 0.007 to $-0.0013$.
- The isovector coupling $\lambda_I$ is cited within the range 0.055 to 0.045.

## Context

This work extends to the three-nucleon system the off-shell isoscalar enhancement mechanism previously applied by Hernandez and Oset (1995) to explain the $pp\rightarrow pp\pi^o$ cross section at threshold, where standard theory underestimated the data by a factor of five. The calculation uses the same Faddeev technique applied by Canton et al. (1997) to pion production at the $\Delta$ resonance, and compares with experimental data from Nikulin et al. (1996), Pickar et al. (1992), and Kerboul et al. (1986). The results enable future tests of other off-shell extrapolations (such as the Juelich model of Schutz et al. (1994) or chiral perturbation theory approaches) in the three-nucleon environment, and motivate inclusion of additional production mechanisms that proved relevant in $NN$ collisions.
