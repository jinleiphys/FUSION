# Dirac-Fock Current and Magnetic-Moment in the Relativistic Approach

> We define the Dirac-Fock current to hold the current conservation in the momentum-dependent Dirac fields, and discuss its effect on the magnetic-moment in the nuclear matter. It is shown that the Dirac-Fock current can largely reduce the magnetic-moment even in the isovector case, whose value has been too big due to the small effective mass in the usual relativistic Hartree approximation.

# Dirac-Fock Current and Magnetic-Moment in the Relativistic Approach

## Key claim
The Dirac-Fock current, defined to restore current conservation in momentum-dependent Dirac fields, can reduce the isovector magnetic-moment in nuclear matter by about 20% even with only a 4% momentum-dependence of the vector field, resolving the overestimation that occurs in the usual relativistic Hartree approximation due to the small effective mass.

## Method
The author defines a new current vertex correction \(\Lambda^\mu\) via the Ward-Takahashi identity to maintain current conservation when the nucleon self-energy \(\Sigma(p)\) depends on momentum. The resulting spatial current in infinite nuclear matter is shown to be determined by the Landau mass \(M^*_L\) rather than the relativistic effective mass \(M^*\). The analysis uses the single-particle energy on the Fermi surface and derives the relation between the Landau mass and the momentum-dependence of the scalar and vector fields.

## Key numbers
- Landau mass ratio \(M^*_L/M \approx 0.85\) from analysis of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR) by T. Suzuki, giving about 15% enhancement of the magnetic-moment from the Schmidt value.
- Typical relativistic effective mass ratio \(M^*/M \approx 0.55\)–\(0.6\) from Dirac phenomenology and DBHF calculations, which would give about 50% enhancement in the Hartree approximation.
- With \(M^*_L/M = 0.85\) and \(M^*/M = 0.6\), the required momentum-dependence of the vector field is only \([U_0(p_F) - U_0(0)]/U_0(p_F) \approx 0.04\) (4%), which reduces the magnetic-moment by about 20%.
- The actual enhancement of the isovector magnetic-moment is estimated to be less than 10% when including the anomalous magnetic moment and surface effects.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic treatment of nuclear matter beyond the Hartree approximation, where the small effective mass (from Dirac Brueckner Hartree-Fock calculations by DBHF authors and Dirac phenomenology by Hama (1990)) leads to overestimated magnetic moments. It builds on the finding by Bentz (1985) that gauge invariance cancels the enhancement for the isoscalar case via Ring-Diagram contributions, but addresses the isovector case where that cancellation is weak. The paper enables future studies of non-local Dirac field effects in finite nuclei and high-momentum-transfer phenomena such as quasielastic electron scattering.
