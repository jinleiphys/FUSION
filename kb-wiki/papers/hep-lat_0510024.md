# Ginsparg-Wilson Pions Scattering in a Sea of Staggered Quarks

> We calculate isospin 2 pion-pion scattering in chiral perturbation theory for a partially quenched, mixed action theory with Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks and staggered sea quarks. We point out that for some scattering channels, the power-law volume dependence of two pion states in nonunitary theories such as partially quenched or mixed action QCD is identical to that of QCD. Thus one can extract infinite volume scattering parameters from mixed action simulations. We then determine the scattering length for both 2 and 2+1 sea quarks in the isospin limit. The scattering length, when expressed in terms of the pion mass and the decay constant measured on the lattice, has no contributions from mixed valence-sea mesons, thus it does not depend upon the parameter, C_Mix, that appears in the chiral Lagrangian of the mixed theory. In addition, the contributions which nominally arise from operators appearing in the mixed action O(a^2 m_q) Lagrangian exactly cancel when the scattering length is written in this form. This is in contrast to the scattering length expressed in terms of the bare parameters of the chiral Lagrangian, which explicitly exhibits all the sicknesses and lattice spacing dependence allowed by a partially quenched mixed action theory. These results hold for both 2 and 2+1 flavors of sea quarks.

## Key claim

The authors show that for isospin 2 pion-pion scattering in a mixed action theory with Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks and staggered sea quarks, the power-law volume dependence is identical to that of QCD, enabling extraction of infinite-volume scattering parameters via Lüscher's formula despite the nonunitary nature of the theory. The scattering length, when expressed in terms of the lattice-measured pion mass and decay constant, has no dependence on the mixed-action parameter C_Mix and no contributions from mixed valence-sea mesons.

## Method

The authors calculate the isospin 2 pion-pion scattering amplitude at next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory for a partially quenched, mixed action theory with Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks and staggered sea quarks, working in the isospin limit for both valence and sea sectors. They use dimensional regularization with a modified minimal subtraction scheme and express the scattering length in terms of the lattice-physical pion mass and decay constant rather than the bare parameters of the chiral Lagrangian. The calculation is performed for both N_f = 2 and N_f = 2+1 flavors of sea quarks.

## Key numbers

- The tree-level isospin 2 pion scattering amplitude at threshold is -4i m_pi^2 / f_pi^2
- The NLO scattering amplitude for continuum SU(2) is given by: iA = -(4i m_pi^2/f_pi^2) {1 + [m_pi^2/(4pi f_pi)^2] [3 ln(m_pi^2/mu^2) - 1 + l_pipi(mu)]}
- The mixed meson mass shift is Delta_Mix = 16 C_Mix / f^2, where C_Mix is the parameter appearing in the mixed action chiral Lagrangian
- The LO meson mass for valence-valence mesons is m_ab^2 = B(m_a + m_b), identical to continuum
- Sea-sea meson masses receive lattice spacing shifts: mtilde_{s1 s2,t}^2 = B(m_{s1} + m_{s2}) + a^2 Delta(xi_t)

## Context

This work extends the mixed action chiral perturbation theory (MAχPT) formalism introduced by Bar et al. (2005) and Tiburzi (2005) to the calculation of two-pion scattering, building upon continuum ππ scattering calculations by Weinberg (1966), Gasser and Leutwyler (1983, 1985), and later two-loop results by Knecht et al. (1995) and Bijnens et al. (1995, 1997, 2004). The results enable continuum and chiral extrapolation of mixed action lattice data from ongoing simulations using domain-wall or overlap valence quarks on MILC staggered configurations, including a determination of the I=2 ππ scattering length by Beane et al. (2005). The authors also provide the corresponding partially quenched χPT scattering amplitudes, which did not previously appear in the literature.
