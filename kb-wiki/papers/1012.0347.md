# Gradient corrections to the kinetic energy density functional of a two-dimensional Fermi gas at finite temperature

> We examine the leading order semiclassical gradient corrections to the non-interacting kinetic energy density functional of a two dimensional Fermi gas by applying the extended Thomas-Fermi theory at finite temperature. We find a non-zero von Weizsäcker-like gradient correction, which in the high-temperature limit, goes over to the familiar functional form $(\hbar^2/24m) (\nablaρ)^2/ρ$. Our work provides a theoretical justification for the inclusion of gradient corrections in applications of density-functional theory to inhomogeneous two-dimensional Fermi systems at any {\em finite} temperature.

# Gradient corrections to the kinetic energy density functional of a two-dimensional Fermi gas at finite temperature

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a non-zero von Weizsäcker-like gradient correction to the kinetic energy density functional of a two-dimensional Fermi gas exists at finite temperatures, even though such corrections are absent at zero temperature. The gradient correction takes the form $(\hbar^2/24m)(\nabla\rho)^2/\rho$ in the high-temperature (Boltzmann) limit, but the functional is only valid within the semiclassical approximation and retains non-analytic behaviour at the classical turning point when taking the $T\to 0$ limit.

## Method

The authors apply extended Thomas-Fermi theory at finite temperature (ETFT) to a two-dimensional non-interacting Fermi gas, using the Wigner-Kirkwood semiclassical expansion of the Bloch density matrix up to second order in $\hbar^2$. They perform inverse Laplace transforms to obtain the finite-temperature spatial density and kinetic energy density, then eliminate the chemical potential and external potential in favour of the density to construct an explicit orbital-free kinetic energy density functional. The functional is tested against exact analytical results for the two-dimensional harmonic oscillator potential at various temperatures and particle numbers.

## Key numbers

- The high-temperature limit of the gradient correction is $(\hbar^2/24m)(\nabla\rho)^2/\rho$, with the full functional approaching ${\cal T}_{\rm ETFT}[\rho] \to k_BT\rho - (\hbar^2/6m)\nabla^2\rho + (\hbar^2/24m)(\nabla\rho)^2/\rho$ as $T\to\infty$.
- For $N=42$ particles in a 2D harmonic oscillator, the largest relative percentage error of the ETFT functional (including gradient corrections) is $\Delta K_{\rm ETFT} \sim 0.2\%$, compared to $\Delta K_{\rm TFT} \sim 0.5\%$ for the Thomas-Fermi functional without gradients.
- For $N=420$ particles, the largest relative percentage errors are $\Delta K_{\rm TFT} \sim 0.06\%$ and $\Delta K_{\rm ETFT} \sim 0.03\%$.
- The von Weizsäcker coefficient $f_2(\bfr;T)$ approaches its Boltzmann value of 1 by $T \gtrsim 18$ for $N=420$ particles, and at lower temperatures for smaller particle numbers.
- At $T=2.0$ (scaled units), the exact and ETFT kinetic energy densities become indistinguishable for $N=42$ particles, including the tail region.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature extended Thomas-Fermi theory previously developed by Brack (1984) for nuclear matter to two-dimensional Fermi systems. It resolves a long-standing puzzle identified by March (1975), Salasnich (2000), and Koivisto (2004) that zero-temperature ETF theory predicts no gradient corrections for 2D systems, contradicting physical intuition. The results provide a theoretical justification for the phenomenological inclusion of von Weizsäcker-like gradient corrections in density-functional theory applications to inhomogeneous 2D electron gases, as previously done by van Zyl (2001). The work enables future finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi-Dirac-von Weizsäcker calculations for low-dimensional systems and may be relevant to experiments on ultra-cold trapped Fermi gases where inter-atomic interactions can be tuned via Feshbach resonance, as studied by Hulet (2004).
