# Charm mesons at FAIR

> The in-medium properties of charm mesons ($D$ and $\bar D$) in a hot and dense matter are studied. A self-consistent coupled-channel approach is driven by a broken SU(4) s-wave Tomozawa-Weinberg interaction supplemented by an attractive isoscalar-scalar term. As medium effects, we include Pauli blocking, baryon mean-field bindings, and $π$ and open-charm meson self-energies. The dynamically generated $\tildeΛ_c$ and $\tildeΣ_c$ resonances in the $DN$ sector remain close to their free space position but acquire large widths. The resultant $D$ meson spectral function, which shows a single pronounced quasiparticle peak close to the free mass that broadens with increasing density, also has a long low energy tail associated with smeared $\tildeΛ_c N^{-1}$, $\tildeΣ_c N^{-1}$ configurations. The low-density approximation for the $\bar D N$ is questionable already at subsaturation densities. We touch upon the implication of our study for $J/Ψ$ suppression at FAIR.

## Key claim

The in-medium spectral properties of charm mesons are studied using a self-consistent coupled-channel approach, but the low-density approximation for the $\bar D N$ interaction is already questionable at subsaturation densities, and the $J/\Psi$ suppression via $J/\Psi \to D \bar D$ dissociation is very unlikely because the low-energy tail of the $D$ spectral function does not extend far enough in mass with sufficient strength.

## Method

A self-consistent coupled-channel calculation was performed using a broken SU(4) s-wave Tomozawa-Weinberg interaction supplemented by an attractive isoscalar-scalar term ($\Sigma_{DN}$). The multi-channel transition matrix was solved with cutoff regularization, fixed by reproducing the position and width of the $I=0$ $\Lambda_c(2593)$ resonance. Medium effects incorporated Pauli blocking, baryon mean-field bindings via a temperature-dependent $\sigma$-$\omega$ model, and $\pi$ and open-charm meson self-energies in the intermediate propagators.

## Key numbers

- The $I=0$ $\Lambda_c(2593)$ resonance and $I=1$ $\Sigma_c(2770)$ resonance were studied at saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.17\ \mathrm{fm^{-3}}$ for temperatures $T=0$ and $T=100$ MeV.
- At $T=100$ MeV for model A ($\Sigma_{DN} \neq 0$), $\tilde\Lambda_c$ is at 2579 MeV and $\tilde\Sigma_c$ at 2767 MeV; for model B ($\Sigma_{DN}=0$), $\tilde\Lambda_c$ is at 2602 MeV and $\tilde\Sigma_c$ at 2807 MeV.
- For the $\bar D N$ sector, scattering lengths for model A (B) are $a^{I=0}=0.61\ (0)$ fm and $a^{I=1}=-0.26\ (-0.29)$ fm.
- At $T=0$ and $\rho_0$, the $D$ meson feels an attractive potential of $-12$ MeV (model A) or $-18$ MeV (model B), while the $\bar D$ feels a repulsion of $11$ MeV (model A) or $20$ MeV (model B).

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent coupled-channel approach of Tolos (2004, 2006), Lutz (2006), and Mizutani (2006) to finite temperature, examining the spectral properties of $D$ and $\bar D$ mesons in nuclear matter relevant for the CBM experiment at FAIR. The results contradict the repulsive $I=0$ scattering length found in Lutz (2006) for the $\bar D N$ sector, while agreeing with the meson- and one-gluon exchange calculation of Haidenbauer (2007). The study enables a more realistic assessment of $J/\Psi$ suppression mechanisms at FAIR, suggesting that multi-nucleon absorption of excited charmonia ($\chi_{c\ell}(1P)$ or $\Psi'$) is the only viable path rather than direct $J/\Psi \to D \bar D$ dissociation.
