# Effect of Spin-Dependent Short-Range Correlations on Nuclear Matrix Elements for Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay of $^{48}$Ca

> The neutrinoless double beta decay is a pivotal weak nuclear process that holds the potential to unveil the Majorana nature of neutrinos and predict their absolute masses. In this study, we delve into examining the impact of spin-dependent short-range correlations (SRC) on the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for the light neutrino-exchange mechanism in neutrinoless double beta ($0νββ$) decay of $^{48}$Ca, employing an extensive interacting nuclear shell model. All computations are performed employing the effective shell model Hamiltonian GXPF1A, encompassing the entire $fp$ model space through the closure approximation. Our investigation examines the NMEs' dependencies on factors such as the number of intermediate states, coupled spin-parity attributes of neutrons and protons, neutrino momentum, inter-nucleon separation, and closure energy. This scrutiny is performed with respect to both the conventional Jastrow-type approach of SRC, employing various parameterizations, and the spin-dependent SRC paradigm. Our findings illuminate a discernible distinction in NMEs induced by spin-dependent SRC, differing by approximately 10-20\% from those computed through the conventional Jastrow-type SRC, incorporating distinct parameterizations.

# Effect of Spin-Dependent Short-Range Correlations on Nuclear Matrix Elements for Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay of $^{48}$Ca

## Key claim

The authors find that spin-dependent short-range correlations (SRC) reduce nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for the light neutrino-exchange mechanism of neutrinoless double beta decay in $^{48}$Ca by approximately 10-20% compared to conventional Jastrow-type SRC with different parameterizations, but this result is obtained within the closure approximation using a specific shell model Hamiltonian (GXPF1A) and does not include tensor-type NMEs due to computational limitations.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear shell model calculations using the KSHELL code with the GXPF1A effective Hamiltonian across the full $fp$ model space (orbitals $0f_{7/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{3/2}$, and $1p_{1/2}$) for $^{48}$Ca. They computed two-body transition densities via the $(n-2)$ channel through $^{46}$Ca intermediate states, and evaluated two-body matrix elements using a proprietary code, incorporating both conventional Jastrow-type SRC with three parameterizations (Miller-Spencer, CD-Bonn, AV18) and a spin-dependent SRC approach, all within the closure approximation with $\langle E\rangle = 0.5$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- Total NME ($M^{0\nu}$) with spin-dependent SRC: 0.792 (no tensor contribution included)
- Total NME ($M^{0\nu}$) with CD-Bonn Jastrow SRC: 0.950
- Total NME ($M^{0\nu}$) with AV18 Jastrow SRC: 0.872
- Total NME ($M^{0\nu}$) with Miller-Spencer Jastrow SRC: 0.629
- Total NME ($M^{0\nu}$) with no SRC: 0.873
- Fermi NME ($M_F$) with spin-dependent SRC: -0.190
- Gamow-Teller NME ($M_{GT}$) with spin-dependent SRC: 0.674
- Spin-dependent SRC NMEs are approximately 10% smaller than AV18-type NMEs and approximately 20% smaller than CD-Bonn-type NMEs
- Spin-dependent SRC NMEs are approximately 20% larger than Miller-Spencer type NMEs
- For closure energy varying from 0 to 15 MeV, total NMEs decrease by approximately 10-15%
- Most NME contribution comes from neutrino momentum $q$ below 500 MeV, with peak contributions around 10 MeV and 180 MeV
- Most NME contribution comes from inter-nucleon separation $r$ less than 4 fm, peaking around 1 fm

## Context

This work extends the spin-dependent SRC approach introduced by PhysRevC.90.065504 (2014), which previously operated within a confined valence space focusing only on the $f_{7/2}$ orbital, to the full $fp$ model space for $^{48}$Ca. The study contrasts with prior NME calculations for $^{48}$Ca using Jastrow-type SRC (PhysRevC.81.024321, PhysRevC.88.064312, PhysRevLett.113.262501, and others) and enables more refined predictions for the half-life and effective Majorana neutrino mass from $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay of $^{48}$Ca, with the authors indicating plans to extend this approach to other decay candidates.
