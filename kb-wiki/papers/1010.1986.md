# Spin-one color superconductors: collective modes and effective Lagrangian

> We investigate the collective excitations in spin-one color superconductors. We classify the Nambu--Goldstone modes by the pattern of spontaneous symmetry breaking, and then use the Ginzburg--Landau theory to derive their dispersion relations. These soft modes play an important role for the low-energy dynamics of the system such as the transport phenomena and hence are relevant for late-stage evolution of neutron stars. In the case of the color-spin-locking phase, we use a functional technique to obtain the low-energy effective action for the physical Nambu--Goldstone bosons that survive after gauging the color symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper classifies the Nambu-Goldstone (NG) modes in spin-one color superconductors using Ginzburg-Landau theory, but the authors explicitly restrict to length scales much larger than the average interquark distance yet much smaller than the wavelength of the helical ordering that arises from weak interactions, thereby neglecting the inhomogeneous ground state structure shown in Brauner (2008).

## Method

The authors construct time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau Lagrangians for complex vector order parameters (single-color case) and 3×3 complex matrix order parameters (single-flavor case), respecting rotational, U(1) phase, and parity symmetries. They parameterize the fields in terms of NG modes and massive fluctuations, expand to bilinear order, and derive dispersion relations for each phase by analyzing longitudinal and transverse momentum directions separately. For the color-spin-locking (CSL) phase in three-flavor quark matter, they use a functional technique generalized from Son (2002) to construct the low-energy effective action for the physical NG bosons that survive after gauging color and electromagnetic symmetries.

## Key numbers

- In the polar phase of the single-color superconductor, the NG mode dispersion along the symmetry axis is \(E^2 = \frac{4v^2(d_1+d_2)(a_1+a_2)}{4v^2(d_1+d_2)c_2+c_1^2} k_3^2\) (type-I, linear).
- In the A-phase of the single-color superconductor, the \((\pi_1,\pi_2)\) sector produces one type-II NG mode with dispersion \(E = \frac{a_1+a_2}{|c_1|} k_3^2\) (quadratic).
- In the CSL phase of the single-flavor superconductor, the NG singlet from broken U(1)_B has dispersion \(E^2 \sim (a_1+a_2)k^2\) (type-I, linear).
- In the polar phase of the single-flavor superconductor, the type-II NG doublet from broken \(\lambda_{4,5,6,7}\) generators has dispersion \(E^2 \sim a_1^2 k_\perp^4 + (a_1+a_2)^2 k_3^4\) (quadratic).
- In the A-phase of the single-flavor superconductor, the type-II NG singlet from broken \(\openone \otimes \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(J_1 \pm iJ_2)\) has dispersion \(E^2 \sim a_1^2 k_\perp^4 + (a_1+a_2)^2 k_3^4\) (quadratic).
- In the \(\varepsilon\)-phase, the type-II NG singlet from broken \(\openone \otimes \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(J_1 \pm iJ_2)\) has dispersion \(E^2 \sim a_1^2 k_\perp^4 + (a_1+a_2)^2 k_3^4\) (quadratic).

## Context

This work extends the classification of spin-one color superconductor phases from Schmitt (2004) and Brauner (2008) by computing the full NG boson spectrum and dispersion relations using Ginzburg-Landau theory, including both type-I (linear) and type-II (quadratic) NG modes. It enables future studies of transport phenomena in neutron star cores where spin-one pairing may occur, and provides the low-energy effective field theory for the CSL phase needed to compute properties such as the Meissner effect and photon mass. The analysis of NG mode counting in the A-phase and polar phase explicitly demonstrates the Nielsen-Chadha counting rule from Nielsen (1975) in a nonrelativistic many-body context.
