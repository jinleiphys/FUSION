# The universal freeze-out criterion at SPS and RHIC

> We formulate a freeze-out criterion for ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions in terms of the pion escape probability from the collision region. We find that the increase in pion phase-space density from SPS to RHIC reported at this conference has a small influence on particle freeze-out because of the small pi-pi cross-section. Our treatment takes into account dynamical expansion, chemical composition and momentum of the escaping particle for particle freeze-out. It supports a freeze-out at rather low temperature - below 100 MeV - and earlier decoupling of high p_t particles.

# The universal freeze-out criterion at SPS and RHIC

## Key claim
The paper proposes a freeze-out criterion based on the escape probability of individual pions from the collision region, rather than a universal value of pion phase-space density or total particle density. The authors find that the increase in pion phase-space density from SPS to RHIC has only a small influence on freeze-out because the ππ cross-section is much smaller than the πN cross-section, and that freeze-out likely occurs at temperatures below 100 MeV.

## Method
The authors formulate freeze-out as the condition when a pion's escape probability reaches a universal value of order one, calculated by integrating the scattering rate along the pion's trajectory. They compute the scattering rate for pions using a resonance-based parametrization of cross-sections, summing over scattering partners including pions, nucleons, antinucleons, kaons, rho mesons, Delta baryons, and anti-Deltas, with thermal particle densities at temperatures of 90, 100, and 120 MeV. The opacity integral is evaluated for a longitudinally boost-invariant and transversely expanding fireball with expansion gradient ξ = 0.08 fm⁻¹.

## Key numbers
- The transverse expansion gradient used in the model is ξ = 0.08 fm⁻¹.
- The density decrease rate is given by ∂_μ u^μ = 1/τ_em + 2ξ, where τ_em is the emission time.
- The opacity integral for a zero-momentum pion yields ∫_τ_em^∞ dτ R(τ) = R_em / (2ξ), where R_em is the scattering rate at emission time.
- The scattering rate is calculated for collisions at √s = 17 A GeV (SPS) and √s = 130 A GeV (RHIC).
- The relative contribution shifted from baryons to mesons when going from SPS to RHIC is about 10%.
- A pion has a reasonable chance to escape (of order 50%) only when the temperature is 100 MeV at most.

## Context
This work extends the freeze-out discussion beyond the suggestion of a universal pion phase-space density criterion (Ferenc, 1999) and the total particle density criterion, which showed non-monotonous energy dependence (CERES collaboration). The authors build on the argument that chemical composition matters (Nagamiya, 1980s; CERES) and incorporate the effect of collective expansion (Bondorf, 1970s). The approach goes beyond the Cooper-Frye prescription (Cooper and Frye, 1974) used in hydrodynamic models by allowing freeze-out to depend on particle type, position, and momentum. The results suggest that high p⊥ pions may decouple earlier from hotter and smaller systems, potentially providing a new contribution to the observed M⊥ dependence of HBT radii.
