# Hydrodynamic Flow and Jet Induced Mach Shocks at RHIC and LHC

> We discuss the present collective flow signals for the phase transition to quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and the collective flow as a barometer for the equation of state (EoS). A study of Mach shocks induced by fast partonic jets propagating through the QGP is given. We predict a significant deformation of Mach shocks in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC and LHC energies as compared to the case of jet propagation in a static medium. Results of a hydrodynamical study of jet energy loss are presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the collapse of both directed flow ($v_1$) and elliptic flow ($v_2$) of protons observed by NA49 in Pb+Pb collisions at 40 A GeV provides evidence for a first-order phase transition to quark-gluon plasma at high baryon densities, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the transition line in the QCD phase diagram is crossed at that energy, which lattice QCD predicts occurs only for baryochemical potentials above approximately 400 MeV.

## Method

The authors review collective flow observables from AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies, comparing ideal (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic calculations using the SHASTA algorithm with microscopic transport models (UrQMD, HSD) and experimental data from NA49, STAR, and PHENIX. They also perform a linearized fluid-dynamical analysis of Mach shocks induced by fast partonic jets propagating through an expanding QGP, studying how radial flow deforms the conical shock structure by Lorentz-boosting between the fluid rest frame and the center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

- Critical baryochemical potential from lattice QCD: $\mu_B^c \approx 400 \pm 50$ MeV, critical temperature $T_c \approx 150-160$ MeV
- Energy density at the critical point: $\approx 1$ GeV/fm$^3$
- Sound velocity assumed for QGP: $c_s = 1/\sqrt{3} \approx 0.57c$; for strongly interacting plasma: $c_s = c$
- Mach angle in static medium for ultrarelativistic jet: $\Delta\theta \simeq 0.96$ radians (approximately 55 degrees)
- Estimated angular spread of emitted hadrons due to radial flow with $\langle u \rangle \sim 0.4$: $30^\circ - 50^\circ$
- Initial energy density in hydrodynamic simulation: $e_0 = 1.68$ GeV/fm$^3$
- Jet energy density increase: $\Delta e = 5$ GeV/fm$^3$
- Jet initial velocity: $v_x = 0.96c$
- Initial fireball radius: 5 fm
- Angular spread from di-jet momentum imbalance: $|\pi - \theta_*| \sim \Delta p_*/p_0 \lesssim 0.1$ radians

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions by Hofmann (1974, 1976) and Stoecker (1979-1986) that hydrodynamic flow and shock formation serve as barometers for the nuclear equation of state, and that flow collapse signals a first-order phase transition. It directly addresses the NA49 collaboration's 40 A GeV data showing proton antiflow, which contradicts hadronic transport models like UrQMD (Bass, 1998) that produce no such effect without a phase transition. The Mach shock analysis builds on linearized fluid-dynamical studies by Casalderrey-Solana (2004) and Satarov (2005), and the predicted sideward peaks in azimuthal distributions are compared to two-particle correlation data from STAR and PHENIX at RHIC. The results enable future measurements at the FAIR facility at GSI and the planned RHIC low-energy run to probe the first-order phase transition region at high baryochemical potential.
