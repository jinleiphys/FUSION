# Strange Nuclear Physics from QCD on Lattice

> We study single-particle potential of Lambda, Sigma, and Xi hyperons in nucleonic matter starting from the fundamental theory of the strong interaction, QCD. First we carry out a lattice QCD numerical simulation, and extract baryon-baryon interactions from QCD by means of the HAL QCD method. We employ a full QCD gauge configuration ensemble at almost physical point so that we can study the physical world, hence mass of hadrons are nearly physical, for example, pion mass is 146 MeV, kaon mass is 525 MeV, and nucleon mass is 958 MeV. Then, we apply the obtained hyperon interactions to the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-nucleon theory, and calculate single-particle potential of hyperons in nucleonic matter U_{Y}(k). We obtain for hyperons stopping in the symmetric nuclear matter at the normal nuclear matter density, U_{Lambda}(0)=-28 MeV, U_{Sigma}(0)=+15 MeV, and U_{Xi}(0)=-4 MeV with a statistical error about +/- 2 MeV associated with our Monte Carlo simulation. These results are qualitatively compatible with values suggested from hypernuclear experiments. This success is remarkable and very encouraging since this proves that our approach to strange nuclear physics starting from QCD is essentially correct.

# Strange Nuclear Physics from QCD on Lattice

## Key claim

The authors claim that single-particle potentials of Lambda, Sigma, and Xi hyperons in nuclear matter, calculated from lattice QCD via the HAL QCD method combined with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory, agree qualitatively with experimental indications from hypernuclear physics. The hedge is that the results are based on S-wave hyperon interactions only, with partial wave truncation at $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$, and the lattice QCD simulation uses quark masses slightly different from the physical world (pion mass 146 MeV rather than the physical 135 MeV).

## Method

The authors first perform lattice QCD numerical simulations using a full QCD gauge configuration ensemble at nearly physical quark masses (the K-configuration set on the K computer), and extract baryon-baryon interaction potentials via the HAL QCD method, which inverts coupled-channel Schrodinger equations from four-point correlation functions. They then parameterize the resulting potentials with analytic functions and apply them as input to the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) many-body theory, solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation with continuous choice to obtain self-consistent single-particle potentials for hyperons in symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter at normal nuclear matter density.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass = 146 MeV, kaon mass = 525 MeV, nucleon mass = 958(3) MeV, Lambda mass = 1140(2) MeV, Sigma mass = 1223(2) MeV, Xi mass = 1354(1) MeV (from lattice QCD simulation with K-configuration set)
- Normal nuclear matter density used: $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$
- For hyperons at rest in symmetric nuclear matter at $\rho_0$: $U_{\Lambda}(0) = -28$ MeV, $U_{\Sigma}(0) = +15$ MeV, $U_{\Xi}(0) = -4$ MeV, each with statistical error about $\pm 2$ MeV from Monte Carlo simulation
- Lattice spacing $a \simeq 0.0846$ fm; spatial volume $V \simeq (8.1$ fm$)^3$
- Sink-source separation $t-t_0 = 12$ in lattice units (approximately 1.0 fm)
- 414 gauge configurations used, with $4 \times 96$ measurements per configuration

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method, previously applied to nucleon-nucleon interactions (Ishii 2006) and hyperon-nucleon systems (Nemura 2008), to predict hyperon single-particle potentials in nuclear matter from first principles QCD. It addresses the hyperon puzzle in neutron stars, where the softening of the equation of state by hyperons appears incompatible with observations of heavy neutron stars (Demorest 2010). The results are compared with empirical values from hypernuclear experiments: $U_{\Lambda}(0) \simeq -30$ MeV (well established from Lambda hypernuclei), $U_{\Sigma}(0) \geq 20$ MeV, and $U_{\Xi}(0) \simeq -10$ MeV (less certain, from limited Sigma and Xi data). The work enables future quantitative studies using the full original lattice potentials including explicit flavor SU(3) breaking, and extension to higher partial waves for application to neutron star cores at supra-normal densities.
