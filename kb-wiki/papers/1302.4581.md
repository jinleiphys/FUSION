# Exploring the breakup and transfer coupling effects in 9Be elastic scattering

> Two cluster structures of 9Be namely, 5He+4He and 8Be+n have been considered for its breakup to describe the available data on elastic scattering for 9Be projectile with 28Si, 64Zn and 144Sm targets. The results of these calculations suggest that the breakup coupling effects are significant for 5He+4He cluster model at above the barrier energies, while they are dominant at relatively lower energies for 8Be+n model. The addition of one neutron stripping channel in the 8Be+n model gives an overall good description of the elastic data for all the systems considered. The couplings generated by breakup in 8Be+n model have different behaviour than the coupling effects obtained within the 5He+4He model, the former being more prominent at lower energies and for the heavier target systems. The behaviour of extracted dynamic polarisation potentials (DPP) generated due to breakup and one neutron transfer couplings have been investigated.

# Exploring the breakup and transfer coupling effects in 9Be elastic scattering

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the relative importance of breakup coupling effects in 9Be elastic scattering depends on which cluster model is used: the 5He+4He model shows significant breakup coupling at above-barrier energies, while the 8Be+n model shows dominant breakup coupling at relatively lower energies. The addition of one-neutron stripping in the 8Be+n model provides an overall good description of elastic data across all three target systems, but the transfer couplings affect the elastic cross-section less than the breakup couplings, contrary to the behavior previously observed for the 9Be+208Pb system by Pandit (2011).

## Method
Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculations were performed for 9Be elastic scattering on 28Si, 64Zn, and 144Sm targets, treating 9Be as either a 5He+4He cluster (breakup threshold 2.46 MeV) or an 8Be+n cluster (breakup threshold 1.67 MeV). Coupled Reaction Channels (CRC) calculations were additionally performed for the one-neutron stripping channel in the 8Be+n model, using the code FRESCO. Dynamic Polarization Potentials (DPP) were extracted to quantify the effects of breakup and transfer couplings on the elastic scattering.

## Key numbers
- Breakup thresholds: 2.46 MeV for 5He+4He cluster, 1.67 MeV for 8Be+n cluster
- One-neutron stripping Q-values: 6.81 MeV for 28Si(9Be,8Be)29Si, 6.31 MeV for 64Zn(9Be,8Be)65Zn, 5.09 MeV for 144Sm(9Be,8Be)145Sm
- Spectroscopic amplitudes for 9Be ground state: 0.81 (L=0 component) and 0.5358 (L=2 component) from shell model calculations
- 5He+4He continuum model space limited to momentum 0 ≤ k ≤ 0.8 fm^-1
- Strong absorption radii: 11.3 fm for 9Be+144Sm, 9.4 fm for 9Be+64Zn, 8.2 fm for 9Be+28Si
- Real part of n+144Sm optical potential normalized by factor 0.8 at 9Be incident energies 37 MeV and below; same factor applied for n+28Si at 12 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Pandit (2011) on 9Be+208Pb at sub-Coulomb energies, where only the 8Be+n model explained the data, and by Keeley (2001), who used the 5He+4He model for 9Be+208Pb at and above the Coulomb barrier. The paper investigates three additional target systems (28Si, 64Zn, 144Sm) across different mass regions to validate both cluster models. The results enable future comparisons with full four-body CDCC calculations treating 9Be as a three-body α+α+n system, which could provide a unified framework for understanding both cluster configurations simultaneously.
