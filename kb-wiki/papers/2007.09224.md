# Fluctuating Relativistic hydrodynamics from Crooks theorem

> We use the Crooks fluctuation theorem together with Zubarev hydrodynamics to develop a bottom-up theory of hydrodynamic fluctuations. We also use thermodynamic uncertainity relations to estimate bottom-up limits to dissipative transport coefficients.

# Fluctuating Relativistic hydrodynamics from Crooks theorem

## Key claim

The paper proposes a bottom-up theory of hydrodynamic fluctuations by combining Crooks fluctuation theorem with Zubarev hydrodynamics, but the approach is presented as a formal framework rather than a fully derived or numerically tested theory. The author explicitly states that Crooks theorem is used "as a postulate" and acknowledges that its applicability to quantum field theory is "problematic" because "in QFT every point is correlated with every other," with justification relying on scale separation assumptions that may not hold in small systems.

## Method

The author constructs a density matrix for the energy-momentum tensor that separates into an equilibrium part (defined via Zubarev's partition function with a field of Lagrange multipliers βμ) and a non-equilibrium part Πμν. Crooks fluctuation theorem is then generalized from 0+1 dimensional quantum mechanics to relativistic field theory by treating neighboring fluid cells as analogous to system-bath pairs, using the foliation of spacetime to define forward and backward paths. The resulting ratio of conditional probabilities (Equation hydrocrook) relates the probability of forward versus reverse evolution of the energy-momentum tensor to the entropy production, and is connected to the propagator of Tμν via Gaussian approximation and gravitational Ward identities.

## Key numbers

- The hierarchy of scales for hydrodynamics is given by two ratios: α = s^{-1/3} / (η/sT) and K = (η/sT) / (1/∂μuν), where the Knudsen number K is the expansion parameter for standard hydrodynamics and α acts as an effective "Planck constant" for fluctuations.
- Systems with approximately 50 particles in the final state are noted as exhibiting collective behavior comparable to systems of 1000 particles, based on experimental data from CMS.
- For water, a cube of side η/(sT) in natural units would contain approximately 10^9 molecules, making significant deviations from the mean exponentially suppressed as P(N ≠ ⟨N⟩) ~ exp[-(N-⟨N⟩)²/⟨N⟩].

## Context

This work extends the program of deriving hydrodynamics from statistical mechanics (Kodama, 2010s) by addressing the inclusion of stochastic fluctuations, which previous approaches via transport theory and functional Lagrangian hydrodynamics (Glorioso, Grozdanov, 2010s) did not fully incorporate. The author contrasts the approach with Landau's (1950s) Gaussian fluctuation-dissipation relations and with Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics, arguing that these are limited to linearized or average treatments. The framework aims to enable a first-principles treatment of fluctuations in small systems where the hierarchy α ≪ 1 fails, and could potentially be implemented on a lattice via Metropolis-type weighting analogous to Wilson loop techniques in QCD (Peskin, 1990s).
