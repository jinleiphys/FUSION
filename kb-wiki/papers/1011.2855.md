# Constraints on the threshold K- nuclear potential from FINUDA (stopped K-, pi-) hypernuclear spectra

> 1s(Lambda) hypernuclear formation rates in stopped K- reactions on several p-shell targets are derived from hypernuclear formation spectra measured recently by the FINUDA Collaboration and are compared with calculated 1s(Lambda) formation rates based on a KbarN coupled channels chiral model. The calculated rates are about 15% of the derived rates, depending weakly on the depth of the threshold K- nuclear potential. The A dependence of the calculated rates is in fair agreement with that of the derived rates, showing a slight preference for a deep density dependent K- nuclear potential of depth (150-200) MeV at nuclear matter density over a shallow potential of depth about 50 MeV. These new features originate from a substantial energy and density dependence found for the in-medium subthreshold (K- n --> pi- Lambda) branching ratio that serves as input to the K- capture at rest calculations.

## Key claim

The A-dependence of 1s(Lambda) hypernuclear formation rates derived from FINUDA data slightly favors a deep density-dependent K- nuclear potential (depth 150-200 MeV at nuclear matter density) over a shallow potential (depth about 50 MeV), but this conclusion depends on using the best-fit pion optical potential and the specific chiral model version; with other pion potentials no firm conclusion can be drawn.

## Method

The authors derived 1s(Lambda) formation rates per stopped K- from FINUDA hypernuclear excitation spectra on five p-shell targets (7Li, 9Be, 12C, 13C, 16O) by dividing measured peak formation rates by spectroscopic structure fractions. They then calculated 1s(Lambda) formation rates using distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with K- atomic wavefunctions generated by shallow and deep K- nuclear potentials, incorporating energy- and density-dependent K- n -> pi- Lambda branching ratios from a chirally motivated coupled-channel model (CS30 version). The calculated A-dependence was compared to the experimentally derived A-dependence after normalizing to the 7Li rate.

## Key numbers

- Derived 1s(Lambda) formation rate for 7Li: (1.48 ± 0.16 ± 0.19) × 10^(-3) per stopped K- (procedure a, using the 3+ peak at 2.19 MeV core excitation)
- Derived 1s(Lambda) formation rate for 16O: (0.42 ± 0.06 ± 0.06) × 10^(-3) per stopped K- (procedure a, using the (3/2)- peak at 6.18 MeV core excitation)
- The derived rates decrease by a factor of 3.5 from 7Li to 16O
- Deep K- potential depth: Re V_K-(rho_0) ~ -190 MeV (denoted V_K-^DD)
- Shallow K- potential depth: Re V_K-(rho_0) ~ -50 MeV (denoted V_K-^SH)
- Calculated rates for 7Li are 15-18% of the experimentally derived rate using the best-fit pion optical potential pi_e, and 23-26% using pion potential pi_b
- The free-space K- n -> pi- Lambda branching ratio is about 10% at threshold, decreasing to roughly half near the Lambda(1405) resonance, then increasing to approximately 40% about 100 MeV below threshold

## Context

This work extends previous DWIA calculations of stopped K- hypernuclear formation rates by Cieply, Friedman, Gal, and Mares (2010) that used a fixed threshold branching ratio, and now incorporates the strong energy and density dependence of the in-medium K- n -> pi- Lambda branching ratio from the chirally motivated model of Cieply and Smejkal (2010). The shallow potential scenario follows self-consistent calculations by Ramos and Oset (2000) and Cieply, Friedman, Gal, and Mares (2001), while the deep potential scenario is motivated by global fits to K- atom data by Friedman and Gal (2007). The results enable future tests with other chiral model versions and extension to heavier nuclear targets.
