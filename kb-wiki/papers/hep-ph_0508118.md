# Extraction of the KK-bar isovector scattering length from pp -> d K^+ K-bar^0 data near threshold

> The results of a recent experiment measuring the reaction pp -> dK^+ \bar K^0 near threshold are interpreted in terms of a spectator model that encapsulates the main features of the observed K^+ \bar K^0 invariant mass distribution. A chi^2 fit to this data leads to an imaginary part of the isovector scattering length in the K \bar K channel of Im(a_1) = -(0.63 \pm 0.24) fm. We then use the Flatte representation of the scattering amplitude to infer a value Re(a_1) = -(0.02 \pm 0.02) fm for the real part under the assumption that scaling is approximately satisfied. We show further that it is not possible to exclude the effects of π^+ηto K^+ \bar K^0 channel coupling within the context of our model.

# Extraction of the $K\bar K$ isovector scattering length from $pp\to dK^+\bar K^0$ data near threshold

## Key claim
The imaginary part of the isovector $K\bar K$ scattering length is extracted directly from a spectator model fit to the $K^+\bar K^0$ invariant mass distribution, while the real part is inferred only under the additional assumption that scaling of Flatté parameters is approximately satisfied. The analysis cannot exclude effects of $\pi^+\eta$ to $K^+\bar K^0$ channel coupling within the spectator model.

## Method
A spectator model is constructed in which the deuteron acts as a spectator and the $pp\to dK^+\bar K^0$ reaction is dominated by final-state interactions in the meson-meson channels, treating $\pi^+\eta$ and $K^+\bar K^0$ production in a coupled-channel description. A $\chi^2$ fit is performed to the $K^+\bar K^0$ invariant mass distribution data from the ANKE experiment at COSY-Jülich, using the $s$-wave cross section expressed in terms of the imaginary part of the isovector scattering length $\beta_s = -\text{Im}(a_1)$, with a separate $p$-wave contribution fixed to the experimental determination. The real part is then inferred via the Flatté representation of the $a_0(980)$ resonance, using the linear relation $\text{Re}(-a_1) = \text{Im}(-a_1)\,\alpha$ and a weighted mean of the scaling parameter $\alpha$ from literature values.

## Key numbers
- Imaginary part of the isovector $K\bar K$ scattering length: $\text{Im}(a_1) = -(0.63 \pm 0.24)$ fm, from the $\chi^2$ fit to the $K^+\bar K^0$ invariant mass distribution with $\chi^2_{min}=0.65$ and no channel mixing ($\rho=0$).
- Real part of the isovector $K\bar K$ scattering length: $\text{Re}(a_1) = -(0.02 \pm 0.02)$ fm, inferred via the Flatté representation assuming scaling is approximately satisfied, using the weighted mean $\alpha_c = 0.031 \pm 0.054$.
- Total cross section from the spectator model: $\sigma(pp\to dK^+\bar K^0) = 36$ nb, compared to the experimental value of $38 \pm 2(\text{stat}) \pm 14(\text{syst})$ nb reported in the experiment.
- The $K\bar K$ invariant mass range at the COSY beam energy $T_p = 2.65$ GeV ($p_p = 3.46$ GeV/c) is from $(P_0)_{min} = 0.9914$ GeV to $(P_0)_{max} = 1.038$ GeV, corresponding to $K\bar K$ relative momenta $0 < k < 0.15$ GeV and $\pi\eta$ momenta $0.33 < p < 0.36$ GeV.
- The $p$-wave contribution is reproduced with $C_p = 150.6\ \mu\text{b}\ \text{GeV}^{-6}$.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the $pp\to dK^+\bar K^0$ reaction measured by the ANKE collaboration at COSY-Jülich (V. Kleber et al., 2003), which had achieved a resolution of 1-8 MeV for the $K\bar K$ invariant mass spectrum. The spectator model approach contrasts with the detailed Faddeev calculations of Grishina et al. (2004) and the chiral approach of Oset, Oller, and Meissner (2001), which suggested large sensitivity to the $\bar K d$ scattering length. The extracted scattering length is consistent with the Flatté parameter compilations from $\pi\eta$ production studies in proton-antiproton annihilation, pion-proton reactions, and $\Phi$ decay (e.g., N. N. Achasov et al., 2003; A. Aloisio et al., 2002), and enables future determination of both $R$ and $\alpha$ independently once direct $K\bar K$ elastic scattering cross section measurements near threshold become available.
