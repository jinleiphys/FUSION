# Electromagnetic Properties of the Delta(1232)

> We calculate the electromagnetic moments and radii of the Delta(1232) in the nonrelativistic quark model, including two-body exchange currents. We show that two-body exchange currents lead to nonvanishing Delta and N-->Delta transition quadrupole moments even if the wave functions have no D-state admixture. The usual explanation based on the single-quark transition model involves D-state admixtures but no exchange currents. We derive a parameter- free relation between the N-->Delta transition quadrupole moment and the neutron charge radius: Q(N-->Delta) = r^2(neutron)/sqrt(2). Furthermore, we calculate the M1 and E2 amplitudes for the process photon + N -->Delta. We find that the E2 amplitude receives sizeable contributions from exchange currents. These are more important than the ones which result from D-state admixtures due to tensor forces between quarks if a reasonable quark core radius of about 0.6 fm is used. We obtain a ratio of E2/M1=-3.5%.

## Key claim

The paper shows that two-body exchange currents (gluon and pion) produce nonvanishing Delta and N-to-Delta transition quadrupole moments even when the wave functions contain no D-state admixture, and that these exchange-current contributions to the E2 amplitude are more important than those from D-state admixtures due to tensor forces between quarks, provided a quark core radius of about 0.6 fm is used.

## Method

The authors calculate electromagnetic moments, radii, and transition amplitudes for the Delta(1232) within the nonrelativistic constituent quark model, using pure L=0 harmonic oscillator ground-state wave functions for both nucleon and Delta. They include one-body (impulse approximation) currents and two-body exchange currents derived from one-gluon exchange, one-pion exchange, and one-sigma exchange potentials between constituent quarks, with the sigma-meson parameters fixed by chiral symmetry constraints. The model parameters (oscillator parameter b, confinement strength a_c, strong coupling alpha_s, and chiral cutoff Lambda) are determined by fitting the nucleon mass, the Delta-N mass splitting of 293 MeV, and the stability condition for the nucleon mass.

## Key numbers

*   Delta-N mass splitting: 293 MeV, used as a constraint to determine model parameters.
*   Ratio of E2/M1 for the process gamma + N -> Delta: -3.5%.
*   Quark core radius of about 0.6 fm is used as the condition under which exchange-current contributions to the E2 amplitude dominate over D-state admixture contributions.
*   Constituent quark mass: m_q = M_N/3 = 313 MeV.
*   Hadronic size of the constituent quark: r_q = 0.4 fm, corresponding to a chiral cutoff Lambda = 4.2 fm^{-1}.
*   The impulse approximation prediction for the proton-to-Delta+ magnetic transition moment is mu_{p -> Delta+} = 2.63 mu_N, which is about 30% lower than the empirical value of 3.5(2) mu_N (Dalitz, 1966).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of two-body exchange currents in nucleon electromagnetic properties (Buchmann, 1991; Buchmann, 1994) to the Delta(1232) system, where they had shown that gluon and pion exchange currents are essential for simultaneously describing proton and neutron charge radii and the positive parity excitation spectrum. It contradicts the additive quark model prediction of a zero N-to-Delta quadrupole transition moment (Morpurgo, 1965) and the single-quark transition model that relies solely on D-state admixtures (Koniuk, 1980; Gershtein, 1982; Drechsel, 1984). The derived parameter-free relation Q(N->Delta) = r^2(neutron)/sqrt(2) connects the transition quadrupole moment to the neutron charge radius, and the results enable future high-precision pion-production experiments with real and virtual photons in the Delta-resonance region (Erickson, 1994) to be interpreted in terms of quark dynamics and tensor forces.
