# On the temperature dependence of the chiral vortical effects

> We discuss the origins of temperature dependence of the axial vortical effect, i.e. generation of an axial current in a rotating chiral medium along the rotation axis. We show that the corresponding transport coefficient depends, in general, on the number of light weakly interacting degrees of freedom, rather than on the gravitational anomaly. We also comment on the role of low-dimensional defects in the rotating medium, and appearance of the chiral vortical effect due to them.

## Key claim

The temperature-dependent coefficient \(c_T\) in the axial vortical effect is not universally determined by the mixed gauge-gravity anomaly, but is instead model-dependent and reflects the statistical properties (Bose-Einstein vs. Fermi-Dirac) of the chiral degrees of freedom; even the sign of the \(T^2\) correction can change within the same model (QCD) depending on the phase.

## Method

The author computes the axial vortical transport coefficient \(\xi_\omega\) in two regimes of QCD with two massless flavors under uniform rotation. At low temperatures (below deconfinement), the axial current is carried by condensed \(\pi^0\) mesons, and thermal corrections are obtained via leading tadpole resummation of the chiral Lagrangian with a Wess-Zumino-Witten term. At high temperatures (above deconfinement), the chirality is carried by free chiral fermions, and the transport coefficients are derived from derivatives of the grand thermodynamic potential with respect to chemical potentials, using kinetic theory and Wigner function results.

## Key numbers

* Low-temperature phase: the baryon axial current coefficient is \(\xi_{\omega B} = \frac{N_c}{36\pi^2} \left(\mu_5^2 - \frac{\mu_5^2}{9 f_\pi^2} T^2 \right)\), with the \(T^2\) correction negative and proportional to \(\mu_5^2 / f_\pi^2\).
* High-temperature phase: the axial vortical effect coefficient for the usual axial current is \(\xi_{\omega} = \frac{N_c N_f}{6}T^2 +\frac{N_c N_f}{2\pi^2}(\mu^2+\mu_5^2)\), with a positive \(T^2\) term independent of chemical potentials.
* High-temperature phase: the baryon axial vortical coefficient is \(\xi_{\omega B} = \frac{N_c}{18}T^2 + \frac{N_c}{6\pi^2}(\mu^2+\mu_5^2)\).
* Low-temperature phase: the chiral magnetic effect coefficient acquires a thermal correction \(\langle j^\alpha (T) \rangle = -\frac{N_c}{12\pi^2} \mu_5 \tilde F^{\alpha\beta}u^S_\beta \left(1-\frac{T^2}{6 f_\pi^2} \right)\).
* The persistent current along a vortex line in the low-temperature phase is \(\langle J_{\mathrm{tot}}^z \rangle = \frac{N_c \mu_5^2}{72 \pi^2} \left(1 -\frac{T^2}{9 f_\pi^2} \right)\langle\Omega^z\rangle\).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of temperature-dependent corrections to anomalous transport coefficients, which were noted as integration constants in hydrodynamic derivations by Oz (2010) and conjectured to originate from the mixed gauge-gravity anomaly by Landsteiner (2011). The author shows that the coefficient \(c_T\) is not fixed by anomaly arguments but depends on the microscopic statistics, contradicting the universality implied by the gravitational anomaly conjecture. The results enable future studies of the rich phenomenology of low-dimensional defects (vortex lines) in rotating chiral superfluids, and are in qualitative agreement with quenched lattice data from Braguta (2014).
