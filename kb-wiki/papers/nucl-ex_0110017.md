# New Technique for a Simultaneous Estimation of the Level Density and Radiative Strength Functions of Dipole Transitions at Eex < Bn-0.5 MeV

> The new, model-independent method to estimate simultaneously the level densities excited in the (n,gamma) reaction and the radiative strength functions of dipole transitions is developed. The method can be applied for any nucleus and reaction followed by cascade gamma-emission. It is just necessary to measure the intensities of two-step gamma-cascades depopulating one or several high-excited states and determine the quanta ordering in the main portion of the observed cascades. The method provides a sufficiently narrow interval of most probable densities of levels with given J,pi and radiative strength functions of dipole transitions populating them.

# New Technique for a Simultaneous Estimation of the Level Density and Radiative Strength Functions of Dipole Transitions at Eex < Bn-0.5 MeV

## Key claim
The authors present a model-independent method for simultaneously estimating level densities and radiative strength functions of dipole transitions from two-step gamma-cascade data, but the method cannot yield unique values, it only provides intervals of probable values whose widths depend on the number of unknown parameters and cannot be reduced below approximately 20% even with zero experimental error.

## Method
The method analyzes the intensities of two-step gamma-cascades depopulating high-excited states in the (n,gamma) reaction, using the measured total radiative width of the compound nucleus and the cascade intensity distribution as a function of intermediate level energy. A Monte Carlo iterative algorithm generates random ensembles of level densities and partial widths for dipole transitions, selecting those that simultaneously reproduce the experimental cascade intensities and total radiative widths within acceptable uncertainty. The analysis assumes that strength functions depend only on transition energy and not on the structure of the excited states, and that the ratio of primary to secondary transition widths is constant for equal multipolarity and energy.

## Key numbers
- The ratio R = I_exp/I_cal averaged over 30 nuclei ranges from 1.5(1) to 2.7(2) depending on the combination of level density and strength function models used (Dilg73/Axel62, Dilg73/Kadm83, Ign75/Axel62, Ign75/Kadm83).
- The total uncertainty in level density and strength functions is about 40-50% in the worst case at current experimental precision; the asymptotic uncertainty at zero experimental error cannot be less than approximately 20%.
- The systematic error in cascade intensity decomposition does not usually exceed approximately 20% for primary transition energies below 3-4 MeV.
- The correlation coefficient between strength functions and level densities ranges from -0.6 to -1.0 in the majority of nuclei studied.
- The threshold energy Eb for change in level density behavior is approximately 3 MeV for N-odd nuclei and approximately 4 MeV for N-even nuclei.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of two-step gamma-cascades previously applied to approximately 30 nuclei in the mass region 114 ≤ A ≤ 200 (Bon99, Khit98, Sukh99) by developing a simultaneous estimation method for both level density and radiative strength functions, rather than estimating one parameter using model assumptions for the other. The method addresses systematic discrepancies found when using standard models such as the Fermi-gas back-shift model (Dilg73), the generalized superfluid nucleus model (Ign75), and the Axel (1962) or Kadmensky (1983) strength function models, which give R values deviating from unity by factors of 1.5 to 2.7. The results enable model-independent testing of nuclear structure theories and suggest that vibrational excitations dominate below Eb while quasiparticle excitations dominate above it, consistent with earlier observations (Sukh97) of harmonicity in cascade intermediate levels.
