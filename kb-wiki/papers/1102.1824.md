# Generalized thermodynamic uncertainty relations

> We analyze ensemble in which energy (E), temperature (T) and multiplicity (N) can all fluctuate and with the help of nonextensive statistics we propose a relation connecting all fluctuating variables. It generalizes Lindhard's thermodynamic uncertainty relations known in literature.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalized thermodynamic uncertainty relation that connects fluctuations of energy, temperature, and multiplicity in an ensemble where all three can vary simultaneously, using nonextensive Tsallis statistics. The relation generalizes Lindhard's earlier thermodynamic uncertainty relation, but the authors note that the nonextensive approach remains subject to debate about its consistency with equilibrium thermodynamics.

## Method

The authors analyze an ensemble of N independently produced particles with energies distributed according to a Boltzmann distribution, then extend the analysis using Tsallis nonextensive statistics where temperature fluctuations follow a gamma distribution. They derive the joint probability distributions for the cases where two of the three variables (energy U, temperature T, multiplicity N) are fixed, and then generalize to the case where all three fluctuate simultaneously. The resulting relation connects the relative variances of U, T, and N through a correlation coefficient ρ(U,T).

## Key numbers

- For the Boltzmann-Gibbs case with fixed T and N: Var(U)/⟨U⟩² = 1/N, where ⟨U⟩ = T N and Var(U) = T² N
- For fixed T and U: Var(N)/⟨N⟩² = 1/⟨N⟩ (Poisson distribution)
- For fixed N and U: Var(T)/⟨T⟩² ≅ 1/N
- The nonextensivity parameter q is related to temperature fluctuations by: ω²_T = q - 1
- The Negative Binomial parameter k is given by: k = 1/(q - 1)
- For uncorrelated fluctuations (ρ = 0): q - 1 = ω²_U + ω²_T
- The relation between q from multiplicity distributions and q_T from transverse momentum distributions: q_T = (1 + 2q)/(2 + q)

## Context

This work extends Lindhard's thermodynamic uncertainty relation (which connected only energy and temperature fluctuations) to include multiplicity fluctuations, using the framework of nonextensive Tsallis statistics. The authors build on prior work by Wilk and Wlodarczyk (2000) and Beck and Cohen (2003) showing that temperature fluctuations in a heat bath result in Tsallis distributions. The derived relation enables connecting fluctuations observed in different parts of phase space in high-energy multiparticle production processes, as demonstrated by comparing q values from multiplicity distributions with q_T values from transverse momentum distributions from experiments including CMS, NA49, and Wibig (2009).
