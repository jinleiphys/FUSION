# Neutron diffusion in magnetars as a source of astrophysical bursts

> Neutron tunneling in neutron star crusts can release enormous amounts of energy on a short timescale. We have clarified aspects of this process occurring in the outer crust regions of neutron stars when oscillations or cataclysmic events changes the crustal ambient density. We report a time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model to determine the rate of neutron diffusion and conclude that a large amount of energy, in the range of 10^40 - 10^44 erg, can be released rapidly. We suggest that this mechanism may be the source of hitherto unknown phenomena such as the Fast Radio Bursts (FRBS).

## Key claim

Neutron tunneling in the outer crust of neutron stars, triggered by oscillations or cataclysmic events that change the local density, can release energies in the range of 10^40 to 10^44 erg on short timescales, and this mechanism may be the source of Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs) and other magnetar bursts. The authors hedge that these are crude estimates relying on the WKB approximation, and that microscopic many-body effects can change the figures by an order of magnitude.

## Method

The authors solved the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations in one dimension for clumps of N=20, 40, 80, and 160 neutrons initially confined by a harmonic oscillator potential plus a Woods-Saxon potential. At t>0, the confining potential was removed and replaced by a chain of equally spaced Woods-Saxon potentials (separation D=20-50 fm), and the time evolution of the neutron density was computed using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta algorithm to extract neutron transfer rates. The initial many-body wavefunction was obtained by diagonalizing the static HFB equations with a Gaussian particle-particle interaction (v0=-14 MeV, sigma0=2.5 fm) and enforcing particle-number conservation via Lagrange multipliers.

## Key numbers

- Energy release range: 10^40 to 10^44 erg, estimated for the burst mechanism.
- Neutron separation energies for the four clump sizes: S_n = 1.55 MeV (N=20), 0.96 MeV (N=40), 0.40 MeV (N=80), and 0.25 MeV (N=160).
- Average TDHFB neutron transfer rate for N=160 at D=20 fm: <Lambda_n> = 5.86 x 10^-4 c/fm; at D=35 fm: 1.13 x 10^-5 c/fm; at D=50 fm: 0.334 x 10^-5 c/fm.
- Gamow (WKB) transfer rates for N=160 at D=20 fm: Lambda_G = 5.70 x 10^-3 c/fm; at D=35 fm: 8.27 x 10^-4 c/fm; at D=50 fm: 1.95 x 10^-4 c/fm.
- TDHFB rates are a factor of 6-12 smaller than Gamow model predictions.
- Turning off the particle-particle interaction increases tunneling probabilities by a factor of about 4-5.
- Pairing correlations change transfer rates by less than 3% for N=160 and less than 5% for N=20.
- Typical FRB duration range: 30 microseconds to 20 milliseconds.
- Magnetar magnetic field range: B ~ 10^12 to 10^15 G.
- Observed short burst energies from magnetars: ~10^39 to 10^41 erg/s; observed FRB burst energies: ~10^38 to 10^46 erg/s.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior study (Bertulani and Lobato, 2021) on isospin diffusion in inhomogeneous neutron media due to tunneling, and contrasts with perturbative WKB calculations (Gamow, 1928) which yield rates orders of magnitude larger than the microscopic TDHFB results. The proposed mechanism augments existing ideas that FRBs originate from magnetars (Margalit and Metzger, 2018) and that neutron transfer modifies cooling rates in accreting neutron stars (Chugunov, 2018). The results enable future three-dimensional lattice calculations of neutron diffusion in neutron star crusts and mergers, and suggest that dynamical density homogenization in stellar environments deserves more extensive theoretical study.
