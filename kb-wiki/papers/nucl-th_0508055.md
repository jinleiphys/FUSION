# Heavy-Quark Probes of the Quark-Gluon Plasma at RHIC

> Thermalization and collective flow of charm (c) and bottom (b) quarks in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions are evaluated based on elastic parton rescattering in an expanding quark-gluon plasma (QGP). We show that resonant interactions in a strongly interacting QGP (sQGP), as well as the effects of parton coalescence, can play an essential role in the interpretation of recent data from the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC), and thus illuminate the nature of the sQGP and its hadronization. Our main assumption, motivated by recent findings in lattice computations of Quantum Chromodynamics, is the existence of D- and B-meson states in the sQGP, providing resonant cross sections for heavy quarks up to temperatures of sim 2 T_c. Pertinent drag and diffusion coefficients are implemented into a relativistic Langevin simulation to compute transverse-momentum spectra and azimuthal asymmetries (v_2) of b- and c-quarks in Au-Au collisions at RHIC. Hadronization into D- and B-mesons is calculated from a combination of coalescence with light quarks and fragmentation, and associated electron-decay spectra and v_2 are compared to recent RHIC data. We also comment on the relative importance of radiative and elastic energy loss of heavy quarks in the QGP.

## Key claim

Resonant interactions in a strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP), modeled via D- and B-meson states up to temperatures of approximately 2 T_c, combined with parton coalescence during hadronization, can explain the observed suppression and elliptic flow of single electrons from heavy-quark decays at RHIC without introducing extra scale factors beyond the mass and width of the meson states.

## Method

The authors computed drag and diffusion coefficients for charm and bottom quarks from elastic scattering in the QGP, using both perturbative QCD (pQCD) Born diagrams and resonant scattering mediated by D- and B-meson states with widths of 0.4-0.75 GeV. These coefficients were implemented into a relativistic Langevin simulation of an isentropically expanding QGP fireball for semicentral Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV with impact parameter b=7 fm. Hadronization was performed using a combination of quark coalescence with light quarks and delta-function fragmentation, followed by 3-body decays to single-electron spectra.

## Key numbers

- Charm-quark thermalization times are reduced by a factor of approximately 3 when resonances are included compared to pQCD scattering alone
- For charm quarks with resonance rescattering, R_AA is lowered down to 0.2 and v_2 raised up to 10%
- The resonance width range used is Gamma = 0.4-0.75 GeV
- D-meson mass fixed at 2 GeV (0.5 GeV above c-quark threshold of 1.5 GeV); B-meson mass fixed at 5 GeV (0.5 GeV above b-quark threshold of 4.5 GeV)
- Initial temperature T_0 = 340 MeV at formation time tau_0 = 0.33 fm/c; critical temperature T_c = 180 MeV
- Surface flow velocity reaches v_perp = 0.5c and momentum anisotropy v_2 = 5.5% at end of QGP phase after about 5 fm/c
- Cross section ratio sigma_bbar/sigma_ccbar = 5 x 10^(-3)
- Single-electron R_AA approximately 0.5 and v_2 up to 10% with combined resonance and coalescence effects
- Bottom contributions dominate single-electron spectra above p_T approximately 3.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier Langevin simulations by Moore and Teaney (2004) that used only pQCD elastic cross sections and found those had to be upscaled substantially to match RHIC data. It also builds on the resonant interaction framework of van Hees and Rapp (2005) which showed that D- and B-meson states in the sQGP reduce heavy-quark thermalization times by a factor of about 3 compared to pQCD. The coalescence approach follows Greco, Ko, and Rapp (2004) who argued that light-quark v_2 must be imparted to D-mesons to achieve electron v_2 above 10%. The results suggest that elastic rescattering dominates over radiative energy loss below p_T approximately 6 GeV, and that a complete picture requires both elastic and inelastic mechanisms.
