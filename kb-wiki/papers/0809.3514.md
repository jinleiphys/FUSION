# Aspects of quantum phase transitions

> A unified description of i) classical phase transitions and their remnants in finite systems and ii) quantum phase transitions is presented. The ensuing discussion relies on the interplay between, on the one hand, the thermodynamic concepts of temperature and specific heat and on the other, the quantal ones of coupling strengths in the Hamiltonian. Our considerations are illustrated in an exactly solvable model of Plastino and Moszkowski [Il Nuovo Cimento {\bf 47}, 470 (1978)].

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified description of both classical phase transitions (and their remnants in finite systems) and quantum phase transitions, showing that information about quantum phase transitions can be extracted from the specific heat in the zero-temperature limit, or equivalently from the derivative of the ground state energy with respect to the coupling constant, even though the specific heat itself vanishes as temperature approaches zero.

## Method

The authors use the Maximum Entropy Principle to derive the canonical statistical operator and the specific heat for a Hamiltonian of the form H = H0 + λH1 with commuting H0 and H1. They then analyze the conditions under which the specific heat vanishes at T=0, showing that the critical coupling constant λc for a quantum phase transition can be determined either from the zero-temperature limit of the specific heat or from the derivative ∂Egs/∂λ of the ground state energy. These methods are illustrated using the exactly solvable Plastino-Moszkowski SU(2) two-level model, also known as a special case of the Lipkin model, for systems of N=2, N=4, and N=8 particles.

## Key numbers

- For the N=2 particle case in the Plastino-Moszkowski model, the quantum phase transition occurs at ξ = 1 (the critical coupling constant), obtained analytically from both the specific heat condition and the derivative G(ξ) = ∂⟨E⟩/∂ξ in the T→0 limit.
- For the N=4 particle case, level crossings occur at λc,1 = 1/3 and λc,2 = 1, as given by the formula λc,n = 1/[N-(2n-1)].
- For the N=8 particle case, the critical coupling constants are λc,n = 1/7, 1/5, 1/3, and 1, with the first three peaks in C*β shown at β=110.
- For the N=4 case with 0 ≤ λ < 1/3, C*λ → 0 as β becomes large (slope of ground state eigenvalue ε = -2); for 1/3 < λ < 1, C*λ → -3 (slope of ground state eigenvalue ε = -1-3λ).

## Context

This work extends the understanding of quantum phase transitions by connecting them to classical thermodynamic quantities, specifically the specific heat, in a unified framework. The authors build on the exactly solvable model of Plastino and Moszkowski (1978), which is a special case of the Lipkin model (1965), and reference prior work on finite-temperature remnants of phase transitions by Dukelsky and Molina (1987) and on thermal fluctuations in nuclei by Egido, Ring, and Ibarra (1985) and Goodman (1984). The approach enables the identification of quantum phase transitions in finite systems without requiring infinite-system thermodynamic limits, and could be applied to other models where the Hamiltonian has a commuting set of observables.
