# Complete one-loop analysis of the nucleon's spin polarizabilities

> We present a complete one-loop analysis of the four nucleon spin polarizabilities in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The first non-vanishing contributions to the isovector and first corrections to the isoscalar spin polarizabilities are calculated. No unknown parameters enter these predictions. We compare our results to various dispersive analyses. We also discuss the convergence of the chiral expansion and the role of the delta isobar.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete one-loop (O(p^4)) calculation of all four nucleon spin polarizabilities in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT), finding that the isoscalar spin polarizabilities receive 25-45% corrections at this order, but the authors caution that the results for gamma_2 and gamma_4 are not meaningful due to the absence of explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom, which would only enter at O(p^5) in HBChPT.

## Method

The authors calculate the four spin-dependent polarizabilities gamma_1 through gamma_4 from the excitation parts of the spin-structure amplitudes for Compton scattering, after removing all one-particle-reducible (1PR) contributions. They perform a complete O(p^4) calculation in SU(2) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory using 25 one-loop pi-N continuum diagrams for the isoscalar components and 16 one-loop diagrams for the isovector components, with vertices from the O(p) and O(p^2) Lagrangians. No unknown chiral counterterms enter the predictions; the only parameters are g_A = 1.26, F_pi = 92.4 MeV, m_pi = 138 MeV, M_N = 938 MeV, and the anomalous magnetic moments kappa^(s) = -0.12 and kappa^(v) = 3.7.

## Key numbers

* Isoscalar spin polarizabilities at O(p^4) in HBChPT (units of 10^(-4) fm^4): gamma_1^(s) = +4.0, gamma_2^(s) = +2.0, gamma_3^(s) = +1.0, gamma_4^(s) = -1.0 (from Table 1, with gamma_4^(s) changing sign relative to leading order)
* The O(p^4) corrections to gamma_1^(s), gamma_2^(s), gamma_3^(s) amount to 25-45% of the leading order result, with gamma_4^(s) showing a sign change
* Isovector spin polarizabilities at O(p^4) (units of 10^(-4) fm^4): gamma_1^(v) = -0.9, gamma_2^(v) = -0.1, gamma_3^(v) = +0.1, gamma_4^(v) = 0 (from Table 1)
* The LEGS group experimental result for the linear combination gamma_pi^(p) = gamma_1^(p) + gamma_2^(p) + 2 gamma_4^(p) = (17.3 +/- 3.4) x 10^(-4) fm^4, which is about a factor of 2 larger than most theoretical predictions
* The parameter mu = m_pi / M_N is approximately 1/7

## Context

This work extends previous O(p^3) HBChPT calculations by BKM (1997) and O(epsilon^3) small-scale expansion (SSE) calculations by Hemmert et al. (1997) that included the Delta(1232) resonance explicitly. The authors show that the poor convergence for gamma_0 found in recent O(p^4) calculations by Ji (1999) and Birse (1999) is expected because gamma_2 and gamma_4 receive large Delta(1232)-related corrections that cannot be captured in HBChPT without explicit delta degrees of freedom until O(p^5). The results enable comparison with dispersion analyses from Mainz (Krein, 1999; Gorchtein, 1999) and provide the first chiral effective field theory predictions for the isovector spin polarizabilities, which are an order of magnitude smaller than their isoscalar counterparts.
