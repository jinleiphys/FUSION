# Moat Regimes in QCD and their Signatures in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Dense QCD matter can exhibit spatially modulated regimes. They can be characterized by particles with a moat spectrum, where the minimum of the energy is over a sphere at nonzero momentum. Such a moat regime can either be a precursor for the formation inhomogeneous condensates, or signal a quantum pion liquid. We introduce the quantum pion liquid and discuss the underlying physics of the moat regime based on studies in low-energy models and preliminary results in QCD. Heavy-ion collisions at small beam energies have the potential to reveal the rich phase structure of QCD at low temperature and nonzero density. We show how moat regimes can be discovered through such collisions. Particle production is enhanced at the bottom of the moat, resulting in a peak at nonzero momentum, instead of zero, in the particle spectrum. Particle number correlations can even increase by several orders of magnitude at nonzero momentum in the moat regime.

## Key claim

Moat regimes, where the minimum of the particle energy lies at nonzero momentum, can exist in dense QCD matter and may represent either a precursor to inhomogeneous condensates or a stable quantum pion liquid (QπL); the authors argue that fluctuation analyses strongly favor the QπL over any inhomogeneous phase with massless transverse modes, but further studies are required to confirm this.

## Method

The authors analyze the QCD phase diagram using low-energy models and preliminary functional renormalization group (FRG) results from Fu (2019). They compute particle spectra and correlations on the chemical freeze-out surface using a generalized Cooper-Frye formula based on the Wigner function, with a quasi-particle spectral function for bosons in a moat regime. The fluid velocity and freeze-out surface are modeled with a blast-wave fit to data at a beam energy of √s = 5 GeV, and thermodynamic properties are described by a hadron resonance gas.

## Key numbers

- The chemical potential at the blast-wave fit is μ_B = 536 MeV, consistent with where the moat regime is expected in the phase diagram.
- Connected two-particle correlations in the moat regime show an enhancement of about two orders of magnitude at the peak compared to the normal phase.
- The moat regime is identified by the condition Z < 0 in the dispersion relation E_φ(p²) = √(Z p² + W (p²)² + m_eff²), with W > 0.

## Context

This work extends studies of spatially modulated regimes in QCD, including inhomogeneous chiral condensates and crystalline phases discussed by Fukushima (2010) and Buballa (2014). It introduces the quantum pion liquid (QπL) concept from Pisarski (2020, 2021) and Tsvelik (2021), where quantum fluctuations prevent true long-range order. The results enable experimental searches for moat regimes in heavy-ion collisions at low beam energies, particularly through peaks at nonzero transverse momentum in particle spectra and correlations, which would be clear signatures of spatial modulation in QCD.
