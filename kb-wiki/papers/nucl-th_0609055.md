# NN scattering in higher derivative formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We consider a new approach to the nucleon-nucleon scattering problem in the framework of the higher-derivative formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory. Starting with a Lorentz-invariant form of the effective Lagrangian we work out a new symmetry-preserving framework where the leading-order amplitude is calculated by solving renormalizable equations and corrections are taken into account perturbatively. Analogously to the KSW approach, the (leading) renormalization scale dependence to any finite order is absorbed in the redefinition of a finite number of parameters of the effective potential at given order. On the other hand, analogously to Weinberg's power counting, the one-pion-exchange potential is of leading order and is treated non-perturbatively.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new framework for nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering within baryon chiral perturbation theory that combines the renormalizability of the KSW approach (where the leading-order equation is renormalizable and corrections are perturbative) with the non-perturbative treatment of the one-pion-exchange (OPE) potential at leading order as in Weinberg's scheme, thereby avoiding the convergence problems of KSW and the consistency problems of Weinberg's original approach.

## Method

The authors start from a manifestly Lorentz-invariant effective Lagrangian and apply field redefinitions to obtain a higher-derivative formulation that improves the ultraviolet behavior of the nucleon propagator. They expand the effective potential, the two-nucleon propagator, and the scattering amplitude in small parameters, solve the leading-order Bethe-Salpeter-type equation non-perturbatively for the projected amplitude, and treat higher-order corrections perturbatively. The leading-order potential consists of contact interactions and the OPE potential, with the OPE potential simplified by separating the pion denominator into a static part (treated at leading order) and a retarded part (treated perturbatively).

## Key numbers

* The pion-decay constant in the chiral limit is given as \(F_\pi = F[1 + \mathcal{O}(\hat{m})] = 92.4\) MeV.
* The parameter \(L\) in the modified propagator is varied from 750 MeV to infinity to investigate the consistency problem; iterations of the leading-order equation remain finite in the limit \(L \to \infty\).
* The leading-order two-nucleon propagator is assigned order \(q^{-4}\), while the leading-order OPE potential uses the static approximation \(\frac{1}{k^2-M^2} \to -\frac{1}{\vec k^2+M^2}\).
* The leading-order equation is reduced to a three-dimensional integral equation of the form \(\mathcal{T}_0 = \mathcal{V}_0 - m \int \frac{d^3 k}{(2\pi)^3} \mathcal{V}_0 \frac{1}{\vec p^2 - \vec k^2 + i\epsilon} \mathcal{T}_0\).

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing tension between Weinberg's approach (Weinberg, 1990-1991), where the OPE potential is treated non-perturbatively but leads to an infinite number of required counter-terms, and the KSW approach (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, 1996-1998), which is renormalizable but suffers from convergence problems, especially in the triplet channel as shown by Fleming, Mehen, and Stewart (1999). The paper also builds on the higher-derivative formulation of Djukanovic, Gegelia, and Scherer (2004) and extends it to the two-nucleon sector. The framework enables systematic, order-by-order calculations of NN scattering amplitudes that are both renormalizable and preserve the non-perturbative treatment of pion exchange at leading order.
