# Event Anisotropy in High Energy Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> The predictions of event anisotropy parameters from transport model RQMD are compared with the recent experimental measurements for 158$A$ GeV Pb+Pb collisions. Using the same model, we study the time evolution of event anisotropy at 2$A$ GeV and 158$A$ GeV for several colliding systems. For the first time, both momentum and configuration space information are studied using the Fourier analysis of the azimuthal angular distribution. We find that, in the model, the initial geometry of the collision plays a dominant role in determining the anisotropy parameters.

## Key claim

The transport model RQMD(v2.3 cascade mode) provides a reasonable description of the experimentally observed event anisotropy in 158\(A\) GeV Pb+Pb collisions, but the initial collision geometry and nuclear passing time, rather than purely pressure-driven hydroexpansion, play a dominant role in determining the anisotropy parameters \(v_1\) and \(v_2\).

## Method

The authors used the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD) model in cascade mode (version 2.3) to simulate nucleus-nucleus collisions at 2\(A\) GeV and 158\(A\) GeV for several systems (S+S, Ru+Ru, Au+Au, Pb+Pb). They performed a Fourier analysis of the azimuthal angular distribution of particles in both momentum space and configuration space, extracting the first two Fourier coefficients \(v_1\) (directed flow) and \(v_2\) (elliptic flow) as functions of rapidity, collision time, and system size.

## Key numbers

- For 158\(A\) GeV Pb+Pb collisions, the model calculations of \(v_1\) and \(v_2\) for protons and pions as functions of rapidity are in reasonable agreement with NA49 experimental data (filled circles in Figure 1).
- The maximum values of \(v_1\) for nucleons are independent of beam energy for a given colliding system; for 2\(A\) GeV S+S (impact parameter \(3 \leq b \leq 5\) fm), 2\(A\) GeV Ru+Ru (\(4 \leq b \leq 6\) fm), and 2\(A\) GeV Pb+Pb (\(5 \leq b \leq 8\) fm), the maximum \(v_1\) increases with nuclear size.
- Nuclear passing time \(t_p\) in the nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass frame is 12 fm/c for 2\(A\) GeV Au+Au and 2 fm/c for 158\(A\) GeV Pb+Pb collisions.
- The nucleon mean freeze-out time \(t_m\) from RQMD is such that the ratio \(t_m/t_p\) is much smaller for low-energy collisions than for high-energy collisions.
- At the time scale comparable to nuclear passing time, nucleons and pions show out-of-plane emission (negative \(v_2\)) for 2\(A\) GeV collisions, while this "squeeze-out" effect is absent for 158\(A\) GeV collisions.
- At about the corresponding passing time, the average number of collisions suffered by mid-rapidity nucleons is 3 for 158\(A\) GeV and 5 for 2\(A\) GeV collisions.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of event anisotropy (flow) in heavy-ion collisions at various laboratory energies, including experimental measurements by the NA49 collaboration (Poskanzer 1997, NA49flow) and theoretical predictions by Ollitrault (1992) that \(v_2\) should be positive at high beam energies. The paper contradicts the purely hydrodynamic interpretation that flow is driven primarily by pressure, instead arguing that initial geometry and nuclear shadowing dominate, especially at early times. The results enable future experimental studies of source anisotropy via two-particle correlation functions, as suggested by Voloshin (1996, 1997) and Panitkin (1997).
