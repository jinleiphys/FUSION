# SPIN-DEPENDENT NUCLEAR STRUCTURE FUNCTIONS: GENERAL APPROACH WITH APPLICATION TO THE DEUTERON

> We study deep-inelastic scattering from polarized nuclei within a covariant framework. A clear connection is established between relativistic and non-relativistic limits, which enables a rigorous derivation of convolution formulae for the spin-dependent nuclear structure functions g_1^A and g_2^A in terms of off-mass-shell extrapolations of polarized nucleon structure functions, g_1^N and g_2^N. Approximate expressions for g_{1,2}^A are obtained by expanding the off-shell g_{1,2}^N about their on-shell limits. As an application of the formalism we consider nuclear effects in the deuteron, knowledge of which is necessary to obtain accurate information on the spin-dependent structure functions of the neutron.

## Key claim

The paper derives rigorous convolution formulae for spin-dependent nuclear structure functions \(g_1^A\) and \(g_2^A\) from a covariant framework, showing that in the non-relativistic limit (to order \({\bf p}^2/M^2\)) one recovers two-dimensional convolution formulae with different flux factors than those found in the literature, and that the simple one-dimensional convolution model breaks down when relativistic off-mass-shell effects are fully incorporated.

## Method

The authors start from a fully relativistic impulse approximation for deep-inelastic scattering from polarized nuclei, expressing the nuclear hadronic tensor in terms of the off-mass-shell nucleon propagator and the virtual nucleon hadronic tensor. They perform a non-relativistic reduction of the relativistic expressions by expanding the nucleon propagator to order \({\bf p}^2/M^2\), deriving two-dimensional convolution formulae for \(g_1^A\) and \(g_T^A\) in terms of off-shell nucleon structure functions and nucleon momentum distribution functions. They then obtain approximate expansion formulae by Taylor-expanding the off-shell nucleon structure functions about their on-shell limits, keeping terms up to order \(\varepsilon/M\) and \({\bf p}^2/M^2\).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear effects in the deuteron from convolution-breaking are typically \(\sim 0.5\%\) (from Ref. [MPT] cited in the paper)
- For heavy nuclei, the Fermi momentum is \(p_F \approx 300\) MeV
- For the deuteron, the average kinetic energy gives a characteristic momentum \(\sqrt{M\,T} \approx 140\) MeV
- The non-relativistic expansion is valid for bound nucleon momenta \(|{\bf p}| \ll M\), where \(M\) is the nucleon mass

## Context

This work extends the covariant treatment of unpolarized nuclear structure functions by Kulagin, Piller, and Weise (1994) to the spin-dependent case, establishing a rigorous connection between relativistic and non-relativistic approaches. It addresses inconsistencies in earlier convolution models for polarized scattering by Kapusta (1992), Ciofi degli Atti (1993), and others, where off-shell effects were not seriously addressed. The formalism enables accurate extraction of neutron spin structure functions from deuteron data, which is essential for upcoming experiments measuring \(g_2\) on deuterium targets, and for testing the fundamental Bjorken sum rule.
