# Study of nuclear giant resonances using a Fermi-liquid method

> The nuclear giant resonances are studied by using a Fermi-liquid method, and the nuclear collective excitation energies of different values of $l$ are obtained, which are fitted with the centroid energies of the giant resonances of spherical nuclei, respectively. In addition, the relation between the isovector giant resonance and the corresponding isoscalar giant resonance is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the centroid energies of nuclear giant resonances for spherical nuclei can be reproduced by a Fermi-liquid model extended to three dimensions with spin, using a Walecka-model Lagrangian as input, but only if the effective nucleon mass is adjusted separately for heavy nuclei (0.742–0.755 \(M_N\)) versus light nuclei (0.69–0.717 \(M_N\)).

## Method

The author generalizes a one-dimensional Fermi-liquid model to three dimensions including nucleon spin, derives the Landau Fermi-liquid function from the linear \(\sigma-\omega\) (Walecka) model, and solves the liquid equation of motion in momentum space for the collective excitation energies. The eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian yield the frequencies of collective modes; the positive isolated eigenvalues correspond to isoscalar giant resonances, while isovector giant resonances (except \(l=1\)) are interpreted as a proton creation mode paired with a neutron annihilation mode of the same multipolarity, giving \(E_V(l) = 2E_S(l)\). The calculation uses fixed coupling constants and nucleon momentum \(|\vec{q}| = k_F = 1.36\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\), with effective nucleon mass varied per nucleus to fit experimental centroid energies.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) isoscalar monopole (\(l=0\)): calculated proton excitation energy \(E_0(p) = 16.28\ \mathrm{MeV}\), neutron excitation energy \(E_0(n) = 7.05\ \mathrm{MeV}\), sum \(23.33\ \mathrm{MeV}\); experimental isoscalar centroid \(E^S_{\mathrm{exp}} = 14.17 \pm 0.28\ \mathrm{MeV}\), isovector centroid \(E^V_{\mathrm{exp}} = 26.0 \pm 3.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) isoscalar dipole (\(l=1\)): calculated \(E_1(p) = 15.53\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(E_1(n) = 6.57\ \mathrm{MeV}\), sum \(22.1\ \mathrm{MeV}\); experimental isoscalar centroid \(E^S_{\mathrm{exp}} = 22.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), isovector centroid \(E^V_{\mathrm{exp}} = 13.5 \pm 0.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) isoscalar quadrupole (\(l=2\)): calculated \(E_2(p) = 15.02\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(E_2(n) = 5.84\ \mathrm{MeV}\), sum \(20.86\ \mathrm{MeV}\); experimental isoscalar centroid \(E^S_{\mathrm{exp}} = 10.9 \pm 0.1\ \mathrm{MeV}\), isovector centroid \(E^V_{\mathrm{exp}} = 22\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(^{40}\mathrm{Ca}\) isovector monopole: calculated sum \(31.16\ \mathrm{MeV}\); experimental \(E^V_{\mathrm{exp}} = 31.1 \pm 2.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(^{40}\mathrm{Ca}\) isovector dipole: experimental \(E^V_{\mathrm{exp}} = 19.8 \pm 0.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(^{40}\mathrm{Ca}\) isoscalar quadrupole: experimental \(E^S_{\mathrm{exp}} = 17.8 \pm 0.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\); isovector \(E^V_{\mathrm{exp}} = 32.5 \pm 1.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the Fermi-liquid approach of Wen (referenced as Wen) to three-dimensional nuclear systems with spin, building on earlier Landau Fermi-liquid studies of nuclear matter properties by Matsui (1981), Friman (1996), and Song (2001). It contrasts with random-phase-approximation (RPA) methods using Skyrme interactions (Zhang) and relativistic RPA (Cao, Ma, Ring 2009) by offering a simpler analytic relation between isoscalar and isovector energies. The results enable future checks of whether the Dirac sea of nucleons contributes to collective excitations (the paper finds it does not), and suggest that low-lying quadrupole states in heavy nuclei may be described by neutron collective excitations.
