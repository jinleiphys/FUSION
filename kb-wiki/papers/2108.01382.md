# Impact of $^7$Be breakup on $^7$Li(p,n) Neutron Spectrum

> The formation of continuum neutron distribution in $^7$Li(p,n) has been identified as due to the coupling of the $^7$Be breakup levels to the final state of the reaction. The continuum neutron spectra produced by $^7$Li(p,n) reaction has been estimated by measuring the double differential cross sections for continuum and resonant breakup of $^7$Be, through $^7$Li(p,n)$^7$Be$^*$ reaction at 21 MeV of proton energy. The breakup contributions from continuum and $5/2^-$, $7/2^-$ states of $^7$Be have been identified. The measured double differential cross sections have been reproduced through CDCC-CRC calculations. The cross sections were projected to neutron spectrum using Monte-Carlo approach and validated using experimentally measured $^3$He gated neutron spectra. $^7$Li(p,n) neutron spectrum at 20 MeV incident proton energy measured by McNaughton. et al. has been reproduced by adapting estimated model parameters for the reaction.

## Key claim

The continuum neutron distribution in the $^7$Li(p,n) reaction at proton energies above the three-body breakup threshold is formed specifically by the coupling of $\alpha$-$^3$He continuum levels of $^7$Be to the outgoing neutron wave function, with additional resonant contributions from the $5/2^-$ and $7/2^-$ states of $^7$Be. The abstract's claim that this coupling "has been identified" is qualified by the paper's reliance on matching measured double differential cross sections to CDCC-CRC calculations with optimized spectroscopic amplitudes, rather than direct observation of the coupling mechanism.

## Method

The experiment used a 21 MeV proton beam on a thin 20 $\mu$g/cm$^2$ $^{nat}$Li target (sandwiched between 5 $\mu$g/cm$^2$ Al backing and 5 $\mu$g/cm$^2$ carbon capping) at the BARC-TIFR Pelletron-Linac facility. Two silicon detector telescopes at $\pm 55^\circ$ recorded $^3$He-$\alpha$ coincidences from $^7$Li(p,n)$^7$Be$^* \rightarrow ^3$He + $\alpha$, with events identified via fixed-radius nearest-neighborhood kinematic gates. The measured double differential cross sections for the laboratory folding angle of $110^\circ$ were reproduced through Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) plus Coupled Reaction Channels (CRC) calculations using the FRESCO code, with continuum states of $^7$Be discretized in $\alpha$-$^3$He relative momentum bins up to $\hbar k = 5$ fm$^{-1}$. The full neutron spectrum was generated via Monte-Carlo approach using the FRESCO-calculated cross sections and validated against experimentally measured $^3$He-gated neutron spectra from three EJ301 liquid scintillators at $30^\circ$ and $60^\circ$.

## Key numbers

- Proton beam energy: 21 MeV for the primary measurement; 20 MeV for validation against McNaughton et al. (1975) data.
- Target thickness: 20 $\mu$g/cm$^2$ $^{nat}$Li, with 5 $\mu$g/cm$^2$ Al backing and 5 $\mu$g/cm$^2$ carbon capping.
- $^7$Be breakup threshold: 1.58 MeV (or 1.59 MeV as used in calculations); $^7$Li(p,n) threshold: 1.64 MeV; three-body breakup Q-value: -3.23 MeV.
- Resonant state energies of $^7$Be: $7/2^-$ at 4.57 MeV; $5/2^-$ at 6.73 and 7.21 MeV.
- Spectroscopic amplitudes used in CDCC-CRC calculations: 0.26 for continuum, 1 for $7/2^-$, 0.57 for $5/2^-$ contributions.
- Continuum discretization: relative momentum $\hbar k$ up to 5 fm$^{-1}$, bin interval $\Delta k = 0.125$ fm$^{-1}$.
- Neutron detectors: three EJ301 liquid scintillators, 12.7 cm diameter, 5 cm thick, at distances of 1 m ($30^\circ$ and $60^\circ$) and 1.5 m ($30^\circ$).
- Silicon telescope $\Delta$E-E pairs: 25 $\mu$m and 1500 $\mu$m thicknesses, at 7.5 cm from target.

## Context

This work extends the single existing measurement of the $^7$Li(p,n) neutron spectrum above 5 MeV by McNaughton et al. (1975), which used a thick $^{nat}$Li target and time-of-flight mode. Prior theoretical modeling attempts by Mashnik et al., Meadows and Smith, and Drosg (2000) could not reproduce the neutron spectrum due to lack of experimental data on $^7$Li(p,n)$^7$Be$^* \rightarrow ^3$He + $\alpha$ breakup. The study also builds on breakup measurements of $^7$Be by Chattopadhyay et al. and Summers and Nunes, as well as n_TOF collaboration data on $^7$Be(n,p) and $^7$Be(n,$\alpha$), though those works had different entrance/exit conditions. The results enable improved modeling of the $^7$Li(p,n) neutron source for fusion reactor and accelerator-driven system applications, particularly for activation analysis using quasi-monoenergetic neutrons above 15 MeV.
