# Nuclear modification of the flavor asymmetry ubar - dbar

> In order to test the NMC finding of flavor asymmetry $\overline u-\overline d$ in the nucleon, existing Drell-Yan data for the tungsten target are often used. However, we have to be careful in comparing nuclear data with the nucleon ones. We investigate whether there exists significant nuclear modification of the $\overline u - \overline d$ distribution in a parton-recombination model. It should be noted that a finite $\overline u - \overline d$ distribution is theoretically possible in nuclei even if the sea is symmetric in the nucleon. In neutron-excess nuclei such as the tungsten, there exist more $d$-valence quarks than $u$-valence quarks, so that more $\overline d$-quarks are lost than $\overline u$-quarks due to parton recombinations. Our results suggest that the nuclear modification in the tungsten is a 2--10 \% effect on the NMC $\overline u - \overline d$ distribution. The nuclear modification of the flavor asymmetry should be an interesting topic in connection with ongoing Drell-Yan experiments.

## Key claim

The nuclear modification of the flavor asymmetry \(\overline u - \overline d\) in a tungsten target is a 2–10% effect on the NMC distribution, meaning that existing tungsten Drell-Yan data cannot be directly compared with the nucleon asymmetry at the several-percent level. A finite \(\overline u - \overline d\) distribution is theoretically possible in nuclei even if the sea is symmetric in the nucleon, due to parton recombination in neutron-excess nuclei.

## Method

The author applies a parton-recombination model, originally used to explain nuclear shadowing in the structure function \(F_2\), to the flavor asymmetry \(\overline u - \overline d\) in the tungsten nucleus (\(^{184}_{74}W_{110}\), neutron-excess parameter \(\varepsilon = 0.196\)). The calculation evaluates recombination contributions at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) using MRS-D0 (1993) parton distributions, considering both the case where the nucleon sea is symmetric (\(\overline u - \overline d = 0\)) and asymmetric. The dominant recombination processes are \(\bar q q \rightarrow G\) at small \(x\) and \(\bar q G \rightarrow \bar q(x)\) in the medium \(x\) region.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-excess parameter for tungsten: \(\varepsilon = 0.196\) for \(^{184}_{74}W_{110}\).
- Nuclear modification of \(\overline u - \overline d\) in tungsten is a 2–10% effect relative to the NMC asymmetry.
- At \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\), the recombination contributions are calculated; the overall \(Q^2\) dependence yields only factor-of-two differences between \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) and \(Q^2 \approx 1\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the parton-recombination model previously applied to nuclear shadowing (e.g., by the recombination model references cited in the paper) to the flavor asymmetry \(\overline u - \overline d\). It directly addresses the NMC finding (1991) of a violation of the Gottfried sum rule, which indicated a \(\bar d\) excess over \(\bar u\) in the nucleon. The results caution against using existing tungsten Drell-Yan data to test the NMC asymmetry without accounting for nuclear effects, and they motivate future Drell-Yan experiments to measure the nuclear modification of flavor asymmetry.
