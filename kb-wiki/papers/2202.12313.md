# Gauge dependence of the quark gap equation: an exploratory study

> We study the gauge dependence of the quark propagator in quantum chromodynamics by solving the gap equation with a nonperturbative quark-gluon vertex which is constrained by longitudinal and transverse Slavnov-Taylor identities, the discrete charge conjugation and parity symmetries and which is free of kinematic singularities in the limit of equal incoming and outgoing quark momenta. We employ gluon propagators in renormalizable $R_ξ$ gauges obtained in lattice QCD studies. We report the dependence of the nonperturbative quark propagator on the gauge parameter, in particular we observe an increase, proportional to the gauge-fixing parameter, of the mass function in the infrared domain, whereas the wave renormalization decreases within the range $0 \leq ξ\leq 1$ considered here. The chiral quark condensate reveals a mild gauge dependence in the region of $ξ$ investigated. We comment on how to build and improve upon this exploratory study in future in conjunction with generalized gauge covariance relations for QCD.

## Key claim

The quark mass function increases proportionally to the gauge-fixing parameter ξ in the infrared domain, while the wave renormalization decreases, within the range 0 ≤ ξ ≤ 1; however, the chiral quark condensate exhibits only a mild gauge dependence, with a maximum deviation of 7% from the Landau-gauge value, suggesting that full gauge invariance of the condensate would require formal incorporation of local gauge transformations of every Green function in the gap equation.

## Method

The authors solved the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) in Euclidean space for renormalizable Rξ gauges, using quenched gluon propagators from lattice QCD fitted with a refined Gribov-Zwanziger parametrization and a ghost propagator independent of ξ. They employed a nonperturbative quark-gluon vertex decomposed into four longitudinal form factors constrained by Slavnov-Taylor identities and eight transverse form factors constrained by transverse Slavnov-Taylor identities, with the quark-ghost scattering form factor X0ξ(q²) computed from a one-loop dressed integral equation.

## Key numbers

- The renormalization scale was set to μ = 4.3 GeV with current-quark mass m(μ²) = 25 MeV
- The strong coupling parametrization used was αsξ = 0.29 + 0.098ξ − 0.064ξ²
- The gluon dressing parametrization parameters for ξ = 0.0: Z = 8.407 ± 0.031, M₀² = 0.071 ± 0.023 GeV², M₁² = 2.783 ± 0.128 GeV², M₂² = 0.532 ± 0.055 GeV², M₃⁴ = 0.380 ± 0.012 GeV⁴
- The gluon dressing parametrization parameters for ξ = 1.0 (extrapolated): Z = 7.585, M₀² = 0.080 GeV², M₁² = 2.378 GeV², M₂² = 0.473 GeV², M₃⁴ = 0.344 GeV⁴
- The chiral quark condensate showed a maximum deviation of 7% from the Landau-gauge value for ξ ≳ 0.3
- Setting X0ξ(q²) = 1 for all ξ led to a 10% reduction of Mξ=1(0) in Feynman gauge and almost no variation of Mξ=0(p²) in Landau gauge

## Context

This work extends prior Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of the quark propagator that were largely restricted to Landau gauge (ξ = 0), such as those by Rojas (2013) and Serna (2018), by incorporating lattice gluon propagators in Rξ gauges from Bicudo (2015) and a full 12-component quark-gluon vertex constrained by both longitudinal and transverse Slavnov-Taylor identities developed by Albino (2018, 2021). The results provide a necessary first step toward verifying the formal gauge invariance of the quark condensate predicted by the generalized Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transformations in QCD derived by Aslam (2015) and De Meerleer (2018), and enable future studies of how gauge dependence is canceled in bound-state observables computed from Bethe-Salpeter and Faddeev equations.
