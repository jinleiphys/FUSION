# Regge behavior in effective field theory

> We derive the Regge behavior for the forward scattering amplitude in scalar field theory using the method of regions. We find that the leading Regge behavior to all orders can be obtained. Regge physics emerges from a kinematic region that involves the overlap of several modes, so that a careful treatment of the overlap regions is important. The most consistent and efficient approach utilizes graphs containing collinear, anti-collinear and Glauber modes, or modes of SCET_G

## Key claim

The authors derive the leading Regge behavior of forward scattering amplitudes in scalar $\phi^3$ theory to all orders using the method of regions, but the abstract's claim that this behavior can be obtained straightforwardly glosses over the fact that Regge physics emerges from overlap regions between multiple modes (collinear, anti-collinear, and Glauber), requiring careful subtraction of double-counted overlaps to reproduce the full theory result.

## Method

The authors analyze the forward scattering amplitude in massive scalar $\phi^3$ theory in the Regge limit ($s \to \infty$, $t$ fixed) by decomposing loop integrals into momentum regions corresponding to collinear, anti-collinear, and Glauber modes using the method of regions and Soft Collinear Effective Theory with Glauber modes (SCET_G). They compute the one-loop box diagram and two-loop ladder diagram in both the full theory and the effective theory, systematically subtracting overlap contributions between different momentum regions to match the full QCD result.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop box plus crossed box amplitude in the full theory yields $\mathcal{M}_{\text{QCD}}^{(1)} = i \pi g^2 \beta(t) / s$, where $\beta(t) = (g^2 / (8 \pi^2 (-t) \chi(t))) \ln((\chi(t)+1)/(\chi(t)-1))$ with $\chi(t) = \sqrt{1 - 4m^2/t} > 1$
- The two-loop ladder diagram's imaginary part in the leading logarithmic approximation gives $\text{Im} \, \mathcal{M}^{(2)}_{\text{QCD}} \approx (\pi g^2 \beta^2(t) / s) \ln s$
- The two-loop overlap contribution between $nG$ and $G\bar{n}$ modes yields $\text{Im} \, \mathcal{M}^{(2)}_{nG/G\bar{n}} = (\pi g^2 \beta^2(t) / s) \ln s$, matching the full theory result
- The Glauber mode alone at one loop reproduces the full QCD amplitude: $\mathcal{M}_G^{(1)} = \mathcal{M}_{\text{QCD}}^{(1)}$

## Context

This work extends the original Regge behavior analysis of Polkinghorne (1960s) for scalar $\phi^3$ theory by reformulating it within the modern effective field theory framework of SCET, including Glauber modes as introduced by Idilbi et al. (2010) and Jantzen and Smirnov. The paper resolves a paradox where collinear modes alone produce an imaginary part from off-shell propagators, showing that the Glauber region is responsible for the on-shell intermediate states required by unitarity. This enables a systematic all-order treatment of Regge physics in effective field theory and provides a foundation for extending such analyses to QCD and the BFKL framework of Balitsky, Fadin, Kuraev, and Lipatov (1970s).
