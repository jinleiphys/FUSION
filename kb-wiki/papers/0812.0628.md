# Eta-eta' mixing from V->Pgamma and J/psi->VP decays

> The $풩$-$풩^\prime$ pseudoscalar mixing angle and the gluonium content of the $풩^\prime$ meson are deduced from an updated phenomenological analysis of $V\to P풥$ and $J/픧\to VP$ decays. In absence of gluonium, the value of the mixing angle in the quark-flavour basis is found to be $픳_P=(41.5\pm 1.2)^\circ$ from $V\to P풥$ and $픳_P=(40.5\pm 2.4)^\circ$ from $J/픧\to VP$. In presence of gluonium, the values for the mixing angle and the gluonic content of the $풩^\prime$ wave function are $픳_P=(41.4\pm 1.3)^\circ$ and $Z^2_{풩^\prime}=0.04\pm 0.09$ from $V\to P풥$ and $픳_P=(44.5\pm 4.3)^\circ$ and $Z^2_{풩^\prime}=0.28\pm 0.21$ from $J/픧\to VP$, respectively.

## Key claim

The gluonium content of the eta-prime meson is found to be negligible from V to P gamma decays (Z^2_eta-prime = 0.04 췀 0.09) but substantial from J/psi to VP decays (Z^2_eta-prime = 0.28 췀 0.21), though both results are compatible with zero at the 2-sigma level due to large uncertainties.

## Method

The author performs a phenomenological analysis of radiative V to P gamma decays (with V = rho, K*, omega, phi and P = pi, K, eta, eta-prime) and J/psi to VP decays, using a quark-flavor basis with SU(3)-breaking corrections and allowing for a gluonium component in the eta-prime wave function. The V to P gamma analysis uses a magnetic dipole transition model with spin-flip amplitudes and wave-function overlaps, while the J/psi to VP analysis uses a parametrization including SU(3)-symmetric coupling, electromagnetic coupling, doubly disconnected diagrams, and a gluonic production amplitude.

## Key numbers

- From V to P gamma decays without gluonium: mixing angle phi_P = (41.5 췀 1.2) degrees
- From J/psi to VP decays without gluonium: mixing angle phi_P = (40.5 췀 2.4) degrees
- From V to P gamma decays with gluonium: phi_P = (41.4 췀 1.3) degrees and Z^2_eta-prime = 0.04 췀 0.09
- From J/psi to VP decays with gluonium: phi_P = (44.5 췀 4.3) degrees and Z^2_eta-prime = 0.28 췀 0.21
- From V to P gamma decays: vector mixing angle phi_V = (3.2 췀 0.1) degrees and mass ratio m_s/m_bar = 1.24 췀 0.07
- From J/psi to VP decays with phi_V = 0 and x = 0.81: phi_P = (44.8 췀 4.3) degrees and Z^2_eta-prime = 0.29 췀 0.21

## Context

This work extends the seminal analysis by Rosner (1982) and the later study by Kou (1999), who suggested a gluonic component as large as 26%, and contrasts with the KLOE Collaboration result of Z^2_eta-prime = 0.14 췀 0.04 from phi decay ratios. The results are consistent with the study by Thomas (2007), who found that data hint at a small gluonic component but depend sensitively on unknown form factors. The analysis enables future clarification of the eta-prime gluonium content through more precise experimental data, particularly for the phi to eta-prime gamma channel.
