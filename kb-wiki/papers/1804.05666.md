# Possible generation of $π$-condensation in a free space by collisions between photons and protons

> A sharply peaked structure is found in the angular distribution of emitted $π^+$ mesons from the photon-proton collisions. It offers a possible way for generating a $π^+$-condensation in free space. To make the stimulated emission of $π^+$-mesons efficient, a ring resonator is designed.

# Possible generation of π-condensation in a free space by collisions between photons and protons

## Key claim

A sharply peaked angular distribution of π⁺ mesons from photon-proton collisions, combined with a ring resonator design, offers a possible way to generate π⁺-condensation in free space; however, the author notes that hadron electrodynamics is only an effective theory and quantitative results should not be relied upon, though the qualitative kinetic features are claimed to be reliable.

## Method

The reaction γ + p → π⁺ + n is analyzed using hadron quantum electrodynamics, with a Lagrangian including proton, neutron, charged pion, and photon fields plus a strong interaction term with coupling constant α_s = G²/4π = 14.6. The lowest-order transition matrix element is computed, and the differential cross-section for pion photo-production on a proton is derived after summing over final spins and averaging over initial spins. A ring resonator is designed with two gaps where photons and protons collide head-on, and a constant magnetic field of 1 T steers the emitted pions along a circular channel to enable stimulated emission.

## Key numbers

- The differential cross-section for pion emission is sharply peaked at an angle of 0.006278π relative to the incident proton line, for a head-on collision of a 1.4 MeV photon with a 434 GeV proton.
- The energy of the most probable emitted π⁺ meson in this example is 6.9 GeV.
- In a magnetic field of 1 T, the 6.9 GeV pions would move in a storage ring of radius 23 m.
- The central angle between the two gaps on the ring is 0.012556π, giving an arc length of 0.9 m between gaps on the 23 m radius ring.
- The half-life of a 6.9 GeV π⁺ meson is 8.95 × 10⁻⁷ s, allowing a half of the pions to travel 268.33 m before decay, about twice the ring circumference.
- The strong interaction coupling constant is α_s = 14.6, and the electromagnetic fine-structure constant is α = 1/137.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior proposal for generating a γ-ray laser using photon-electron collisions (Zhang, 2018), where a similar sharply peaked angular distribution was found. It also builds on earlier theoretical considerations of π-condensation in nuclear matter from the 1970s (Sawyer and Scalapino, 1970s) and the author's own relativistic mean field theory incorporating a mean π-field (Zhang, 1990s). The paper enables experimental attempts to generate π-condensation in free space, which has not been observed in either nuclear matter or free space, and proposes a specific resonator design to achieve stimulated pion emission.
