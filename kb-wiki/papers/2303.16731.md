# Heavy quarkonium with finite three momentum near $T_c$

> We investigate the non-trivial 3-momentum effects on the masses of heavy quarkonium states that are moving in a hot medium using QCD sum rules. For all charmonium states, we observe a negative mass shift near $T_c$ that is less than 3$\%$ at a momentum of 1$\rm{GeV}$. Specifically, we first investigate the difference between the longitudinal and transverse modes of both $J/ψ$ and $χ_{c1}$. We find that the transverse mode of the $J/ψ$ experiences larger modification than the longitudinal mode, while the $χ_{c1}$ has the opposite behavior. By comparing the $η_c$ and $χ_{c0}$, and also the unpolarized $J/ψ$ and $χ_{c1}$, we recognize that the P-wave particles have stronger momentum dependencies on their masses than the S-wave ones. We also find $Υ$(1S) has negligible 3-momentum dependence compared to the charmonium states, e.g. less than 0.01$\%$ even at 1.4$T_c$ and at a momentum of 4$\rm{GeV}$.

## Key claim

Using QCD sum rules, the authors find that all charmonium states moving in a hot medium experience a negative mass shift near $T_c$ that is less than 3% at a momentum of 1 GeV, but this result depends on the assumption that broadening effects are not considered, meaning the computed mass changes could be the maximum values expected from the sum rule.

## Method

The authors apply QCD sum rules to two-point correlators for pseudoscalar, scalar, vector, and axialvector channels, decomposing the vector and axialvector channels into longitudinal and transverse modes. They perform an operator product expansion up to dimension-4 gluon operators (scalar and twist-2 gluon condensates), derive new Wilson coefficients responsible for non-trivial 3-momentum dependence, and extract ground state masses by modeling the spectral function with a single pole plus perturbative continuum within a Borel window. The deviation parameter $\alpha(T)$ characterizing the non-trivial 3-momentum dependence is extracted by fitting the energy ratio as a function of velocity squared.

## Key numbers

- For all charmonium states, the negative mass shift near $T_c$ is less than 3% at a momentum of 1 GeV.
- For $\Upsilon$(1S), the mass shift caused by 3-momentum is less than 0.01% even at 1.4$T_c$ and at a momentum of 4 GeV.
- The maximum temperature studied for $J/\psi$ is 1.05$T_c$; for other charmonium states the maximum is 1.03$T_c$.
- For $\Upsilon$(1S), the temperature is considered up to 1.4$T_c$ and the maximum 3-momentum is 4 GeV, corresponding to $v^2 \approx 0.18$.
- Input charm quark mass is $m_c(p^2=-m_c^2)=1.262$ GeV with $\alpha_s(8m_c^2)=0.21$; bottom quark mass is $m_b(p^2=-m_b^2)=4.12$ GeV with $\alpha_s(8m_b^2)=0.158$.

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of light vector mesons moving in a dense medium (Lee 1997, Leupold 1998, Kim 2019) to heavy quarkonium at finite temperature. It addresses a gap left by lattice simulations (Oktay 2010, Ding 2012, Aarts 2012, Ikeda 2016, Ding 2017) that studied momentum effects on $\eta_c$, $J/\psi$, $\Upsilon$(1S), and $\eta_b$ but could not make definite conclusions due to large uncertainties. The results provide a guideline for the precision needed in future lattice simulations to detect genuine 3-momentum effects on heavy quarkonium dispersion relations at finite temperature.
