# Implicit vs Explicit renormalization of the $NN$ force

> We use an S-wave toy model for the two-nucleon system to show that the implicit renormalization of a contact theory matches the explicit renormalization through a flow equation which integrates out the high momentum components. By fitting the low-momentum interaction with a new contact theory, we show that the running of the contact strengths in both original and fitted contact theories match over a wide cutoff range.

## Key claim

Using an S-wave toy model of the two-nucleon system, the implicit renormalization of a contact theory (where low-energy constants are fixed by scattering length and effective range) matches the explicit renormalization via a similarity renormalization group (SRG) flow equation that integrates out high-momentum components, but only when the low-momentum part of the evolved interaction is refitted with a contact theory at very low momenta.

## Method

The authors constructed a pionless effective field theory at next-to-leading order (NLO) regulated with a smooth Gaussian cutoff, adjusting the low-energy constants C0^(2) and C2^(2) to reproduce the scattering length and effective range in the 1S0 and 3S1 channels for several cutoff scales (implicit renormalization). Separately, they evolved an S-wave toy model potential using the SRG with the Block-Diagonal generator, which unitarily separates low- and high-momentum components; they then extracted the low-momentum part at a similarity cutoff of λ = 0.1 fm⁻¹ and fitted its diagonal low-momentum behavior with a contact theory at NLO to obtain new low-energy constants (explicit renormalization). The running of the contact strengths with cutoff was compared between the two approaches.

## Key numbers

- The low-momentum components of the evolved interaction were extracted at a similarity cutoff λ = 0.1 fm⁻¹.
- The running of contact strengths in the original and fitted contact theories matched over a "wide cutoff range" (the specific numerical range is not given in the text; the figures show the matching range indicated by blue arrows but no explicit numerical boundaries are provided in the text).
- The toy model was used to overcome numerical difficulties when the SRG evolution is pushed towards the infrared region of the similarity cutoff.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Epelbaum, Glöckle and Meißner (early 2000s) on low-momentum interactions from unitary transformations, and the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) method applied to NN interactions by the Ohio group (Bogner, Furnstahl, Perry, 2007). It also builds on the authors' own previous toy model study (Arriola, Szpigel, Timóteo, 2013). The result enables a direct comparison between two renormalization philosophies, implicit (fitting low-energy constants to scattering data) and explicit (integrating out high-momentum modes via SRG), showing they produce equivalent running of contact strengths in a simplified model, which can guide future work on realistic NN interactions.
