# Anomalous Strangeness Transport

> Nondissipative transport of strangeness is studied in a chiral hadronic plasma with three flavors. In the phase in which chiral symmetry is preserved, strangeness transport is found to be driven by both an external magnetic field and fluid vorticity. As for the constitutive relations of the baryon and electromagnetic currents, they exhibit vortical terms proportional to the strangeness chemical potential. In the superfluid phase, transverse nondissipative diffusion of the baryon, electromagnetic, and strangeness charges is found, which survives in the limit of vanishing chiral imbalance and mixes in a fashion similar to standard dissipative diffusion in quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

Nondissipative transport of strangeness exists in a chiral hadronic plasma with three flavors, driven by both an external magnetic field and fluid vorticity in the symmetric phase; in the superfluid phase, transverse nondissipative diffusion of strangeness (and other charges) survives even in the limit of vanishing chiral imbalance, mixing in a fashion similar to standard dissipative diffusion in quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors model nuclear matter as a chiral hadronic fluid with three flavors (up, down, strange) in equilibrium, coupled to external vector and axial-vector gauge fields with a 't Hooft anomaly to implement chiral imbalance. They compute the equilibrium partition function from the Abelian Bardeen Chern-Simons form using the Manes-Stora-Zumino transgression formula, then derive the covariant vector currents (baryon, electromagnetic, and strangeness) by varying the partition function with respect to the field strengths. For the broken phase, they transform the Bardeen-Zumino currents using the Nambu-Goldstone boson matrix (pions, kaons, eta_8) and evaluate the resulting constitutive relations.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is given as f_pi ≈ 92 MeV.
- The strange quark mass relative to the effective plasma temperature is m_s/T ~ 1/3, with finite mass effects scaling as (m_s/T)^2.
- In the symmetric phase, the strangeness current spatial components are ⟨J_S^i⟩_cov = (N_c / 6π^2) μ_5 (2 μ_B ω^i + e 𝔹^i - 6 μ_S ω^i), where N_c is the number of colors.
- In the symmetric phase, the electromagnetic current includes a chiral vortical effect: ⟨J_em^i⟩_cov = (e N_c / 3π^2) μ_5 (e 𝔹^i - μ_S ω^i).
- In the symmetric phase, the baryon current is ⟨J_bar^i⟩_cov = -(N_c / 3π^2) μ_5 (μ_B - μ_S) ω^i.

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor (N_f=2) analysis of Manes (2019) to three flavors, addressing the physical issue that the strange quark mass (m_s/T ~ 1/3) could suppress chiral transport; the authors rely on the argument of Guo (2016) and Shi (2017) that finite mass effects scale as (m_s/T)^2, making the three-flavor approximation reliable. The nondissipative diffusion terms found in the broken phase (surviving at μ_5=0) are structurally analogous to the dissipative diffusion matrix studied by Greif (2017), but with transport coefficients determined by gradients of neutral meson fields (π^0 and η_8). The results suggest a possible new experimental signature for nondissipative phenomena in heavy-ion collisions by focusing on strangeness transport.
