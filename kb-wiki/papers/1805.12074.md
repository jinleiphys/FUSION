# Systematic analysis of the peripherality of the $^{10}$Be$(d,p)$$^{11}$Be transfer reaction and extraction of the asymptotic normalization coefficient of $^{11}$Be bound states

> We reanalyze the experiment of Schmitt $\textit{et al.}$ on the $^{10}$Be$(d,p)^{11}$Be transfer reaction [Phys. Rev. Lett. $\textbf{108}$, 192701 (2012)] by exploring the beam-energy and angular ranges at which the reaction is strictly peripheral. We consider the adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) to model the reaction and use a Halo-EFT description of $^{11}$Be to systematically explore the sensitivity of our calculations to the short-range physics of the $^{10}$Be-$n$ wave function. We find that by selecting the data at low beam energy and forward scattering angle the calculated cross sections scale nearly perfectly with the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the $^{11}$Be bound states. Following these results, a comparison of our calculations with the experimental data gives a value of $C_{1s1/2}=0.785\pm0.03$ fm$^{-1/2}$ for the $\frac{1}{2}^+$ ground-state ANC and $C_{0p1/2}=0.135\pm0.005$ fm$^{-1/2}$ for the $\frac{1}{2}^-$ excited-state, which are in perfect agreement with the $\textit{ab initio}$ calculations of Calci $\textit{et al.}$, who obtain $C^{\textit{ab initio}}_{1/2^+}=0.786$ fm$^{-1/2}$ and $C^{\textit{ab initio}}_{1/2^-}=0.129$ fm$^{-1/2}$ [Phys. Rev. Lett. $\textbf{117}$, 242501 (2016)].

## Key claim

By selecting data at low beam energy and forward scattering angles, the calculated cross sections for the $^{10}$Be$(d,p)^{11}$Be transfer reaction scale nearly perfectly with the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the $^{11}$Be bound states, enabling a model-independent extraction of the ANC that agrees with ab initio calculations.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the experiment of Schmitt et al. (2012) on the $^{10}$Be$(d,p)^{11}$Be transfer reaction using the adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) to model the reaction. They employed a Halo-EFT description of $^{11}$Be at leading order, generating nine different Gaussian $^{10}$Be-$n$ potentials with widths ranging from 0.4 fm to 2.0 fm to systematically explore the sensitivity of the calculated cross sections to the short-range physics of the $^{10}$Be-$n$ wave function. The depth of each Gaussian potential was adjusted to reproduce the neutron binding energies of the $^{11}$Be ground state (0.502 MeV) and excited state (0.182 MeV), and the resulting cross sections were analyzed to identify angular and energy ranges where the reaction is strictly peripheral.

## Key numbers

- Ground state ANC extracted: $C_{1s1/2} = 0.785 \pm 0.03$ fm$^{-1/2}$, in agreement with the ab initio value of Calci et al. (2016): $C^{\textit{ab initio}}_{1/2^+} = 0.786$ fm$^{-1/2}$
- Excited state ANC extracted: $C_{0p1/2} = 0.135 \pm 0.005$ fm$^{-1/2}$, compared to the ab initio value: $C^{\textit{ab initio}}_{1/2^-} = 0.129$ fm$^{-1/2}$
- The peripheral angular range at $E_d = 12$ MeV extends to $\theta < 20^\circ$ for the ground state transfer
- At $E_d = 21.4$ MeV, the peripheral angular range is limited to $\theta < 7^\circ$ for the ground state
- The nine Gaussian potentials used had widths $r_0$ from 0.4 fm to 2.0 fm, producing SPANCs ranging from 0.601 to 0.888 fm$^{-1/2}$ for the ground state and 0.068 to 0.177 fm$^{-1/2}$ for the excited state

## Context

This work reanalyzes the transfer experiment of Schmitt et al. (2012) on $^{10}$Be$(d,p)^{11}$Be, extending previous analyses by Belyaeva et al. and others that used coupled-reaction channel models. The authors systematically determine the exact conditions of peripherality using a Halo-EFT approach, which provides a more controlled way to assess sensitivity to short-range physics than earlier studies using Woods-Saxon potentials (e.g., Mukhamedzhanov et al., 2005; Pang et al., 2006). The extracted ANCs are compared to the ab initio no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) calculations of Calci et al. (2016), showing excellent agreement. This work enables future transfer experiments to be designed at lower beam energies (below 10 MeV) to extract more precise ANCs for the excited state of $^{11}$Be.
