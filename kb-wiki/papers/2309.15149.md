# Bounding the QCD Equation of State with the Lattice

> The equation of state of QCD matter at high densities is relevant for neutron star structure and for neutron star mergers and has been a focus of recent work. We show how lattice QCD simulations, free of sign problems, can provide an upper bound on the pressure as a function of quark chemical potentials. We show that at large chemical potentials this bound should become quite sharp; the difference between the upper bound on the pressure P-phase-quenched and the true pressure P is of order alpha^3 P. The corrections arise from a single Feynman diagram; its calculation would render remaining corrections of order alpha^4 P.

# Bounding the QCD Equation of State with the Lattice

## Key claim

Phase-quenched lattice QCD simulations, which are free of sign problems, provide a strict upper bound on the true QCD pressure at finite quark chemical potentials; the relative difference between the phase-quenched pressure and the true pressure is of order αₛ³ P, and at this order the difference arises from a single Feynman diagram whose calculation would render remaining corrections of order αₛ⁴ P.

## Method

The authors prove that replacing the complex fermion determinants in the QCD partition function with their absolute values (phase quenching) yields a real, positive integrand and a pressure PPQ that is a strict upper bound on the true pressure P. They then analyze the perturbative expansion of the phase-quenched theory, showing that each fermion loop receives a factor of 1/2 and that the fermion species are averaged over positive and negative chemical potentials. Using Furry's theorem and group theory for SU(Nc), they identify that the first difference between PPQ and P appears at order αₛ³ and arises from a diagram with two fermion loops each attached to three gluons, involving the symmetric dABC group factor. The authors also estimate lattice discretization effects for staggered fermions by computing the free-theory pressure on the lattice versus the continuum.

## Key numbers

- The difference between the phase-quenched pressure PPQ and the true pressure P is of order PPQ − P = O(αₛ³ P)
- For nearest-neighbor staggered fermions, to keep lattice-continuum pressure differences below 10%, the condition is aμ < 0.42
- For Naik-improved staggered fermions, aμ = 1.0 still yields less than 10% lattice artifacts, but the improvement breaks down beyond aμ = 1.147
- For thermal effects to remain at the 10% level, the condition is μ > 14 T (from the free fermion pressure formula P = 4(7π²/720 T⁴ + 1/24 μ²T² + 1/(48π²) μ⁴))
- The dABC contraction relevant for the distinguishing diagram gives dABC dABC = (Nc²−1)(Nc²−4)/Nc for SU(Nc)

## Context

This work extends the inequality established by Cohen (2003) that phase-quenched lattice QCD provides an upper bound on the true pressure, which Cohen applied to isospin chemical potential. The authors argue that performing new phase-quenched lattice simulations with the physical combination of up, down, and strange quark chemical potentials (rather than isospin chemical potential) will provide the tightest bounds on the neutron star equation of state. This approach complements existing constraints from perturbative QCD calculations at high densities (Kurkela 2009, Gorda 2021, Gorda 2023), astrophysical observations of neutron stars, and low-density nuclear effective field theory calculations (Tews 2012, Drischler 2020). The work enables a new lattice-based constraint on the dense QCD equation of state that can be compared with perturbative expansions and used to bound proposed equations of state for neutron star matter.
