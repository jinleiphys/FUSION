# Structure of QC$_2$D ground state fields at nonzero matter densities

> A quantitative investigation into the modification of ground-state field structures in two-color QCD (QC$_2$D) is presented at finite chemical potential. Using lattice simulations with Wilson gauge and fermion actions, we explore the chromo-electromagnetic field strengths under varying matter densities. To ensure accurate measurements, we develop and calibrate two highly improved topological charge operators and evaluate four gradient flow actions. Our results reveal a finite-volume crossover in the regime of the anticipated phase boundary at $μ= m_π/2$, with both chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic field strengths suppressed before recovering and exceeding vacuum values at higher chemical potentials. We find the difference between the squared chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic field strengths, $E^2-B^2$, to increase in magnitude monotonically with increasing chemical potential. At $aμ=0.7$, we find an $11\%$ suppression of $E^2$, a relatively small effect. A systematic analysis using sigmoid fits of lattice simulations in the crossover regime is performed to confirm the critical chemical potential obtained from the field structure is in agreement with the phase boundary at $m_π/ 2$. These findings provide new insight into non-Abelian ground-state vacuum field structures and offer a foundation for future studies in real QCD.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the ground-state chromo-electromagnetic field structure in two-color QCD undergoes a finite-volume crossover near the anticipated phase boundary at \(\mu = m_\pi/2\), with both chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic field strengths first suppressed and then recovering to exceed vacuum values at higher chemical potentials; however, the qualitative visualizations show that the modification is subtle, and at \(a\mu = 0.7\) the suppression of \(E^2\) is only 11%, indicating a relatively small effect.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of two-color QCD using the Wilson plaquette gauge action and two flavors of Wilson fermions with a diquark source term, on \(16^3 \times 24\) lattices at \(\beta = 0.190\) and \(\kappa = 1.680\). They applied gradient flow smoothing using four different improved gauge actions (Moran, Intermediate, Iwasaki, and DBW2) and developed two five-loop \(\mathcal{O}(a^4)\)-improved topological charge operators (Improved \(F_{\mu\nu}\) and Direct schemes) to calibrate the optimal flow time. The chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic field strengths were measured using the five-loop improved lattice field-strength tensor, and sigmoid fits were performed in the crossover regime to extract the critical chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass on the primary lattice: \(a m_\pi = 0.645(8)\), corresponding to \(m_\pi = 717(25)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) with lattice spacing \(a = 0.178(6)\ \mathrm{fm}\) set by string tension \(\sqrt{\sigma} = 440\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Anticipated phase boundary at \(\mu = m_\pi/2 = 0.323(4)\) in lattice units
- At \(a\mu = 0.7\), an 11% suppression of \(E^2\) relative to vacuum is observed
- Lattice spacing for the finer \(16^3 \times 32\) lattices: \(a = 0.130(1)\ \mathrm{fm}\) at \(\beta = 2.1\) and \(\kappa = 0.1577\)

## Context

This work extends the early exploratory study of topological charge density at nonzero chemical potential in two-color QCD by Hands (2011) and complements prior investigations of the gluon propagator and string tension in QC\(_2\)D by Hands (2006), Boz (2013, 2018), and Begun (2022). The findings provide a foundation for future studies of ground-state vacuum field structures in real QCD, where the sign problem prevents direct lattice simulations at finite density.
