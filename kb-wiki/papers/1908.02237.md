# Dynamical Nucleon-Pion System via Basis Light-Front Quantization

> We present the first application of the Basis Light-Front Quantization method to study a simple chiral model of the nucleon-pion system via an ab initio, non-perturbative, Hamiltonian approach. As a test problem, we consider the physical proton as the relativistic bound state of the nucleon-pion system. Based on the chiral model of the nucleon-pion system, we construct the mass-squared matrix of the system within our light-front basis representation. We obtain the proton's mass and the corresponding light-front wave function by solving the eigenvalue problem of the mass-squared matrix. With the resulting boost-invariant light-front wave function, we also compute the proton's parton distribution function.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first non-perturbative, *ab initio* treatment of a chiral nucleon-pion model using the Basis Light-Front Quantization method, but only as a test problem where the physical proton is treated as a relativistic bound state of the nucleon-pion system truncated to the lowest two Fock sectors (nucleon and nucleon-plus-one-pion).

## Method

The authors start from a chiral Lagrangian of the Gursey-type linear representation, perform a Legendre transformation to obtain the light-front Hamiltonian density, and keep only terms corresponding to single-pion emission and absorption. They construct a basis using discretized plane waves in the longitudinal direction and two-dimensional harmonic oscillator states in the transverse direction, with truncation parameters \(K_{\rm max}\) and \(N_{\rm max}\). The mass-squared matrix is built and diagonalized numerically, with a Fock-sector-dependent renormalization scheme used to tune the bare nucleon mass so that the ground state eigenvalue matches the physical proton mass of 938 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant \(f\) is set to 93 MeV.
- The physical proton mass used for renormalization is 938 MeV; the pion mass is fixed at 137 MeV.
- The proton's root-mean-square charge radius used for fitting the basis strength is 0.844 fm.
- For the model space with \(N_{\rm max}=6\), the basis strength \(b\) is 176.95 MeV; for \(N_{\rm max}=8\), \(b=245.54\) MeV; for \(N_{\rm max}=10\), \(b=279.55\) MeV.
- The probability to find a bare nucleon in the physical proton is 0.83 for \(N_{\rm max}=6\), 0.69 for \(N_{\rm max}=8\), and 0.62 for \(N_{\rm max}=10\).
- The computed parton distribution function peaks at about \(x_\pi=0.45\) (or \(x_N=0.55\)) for the model spaces with \(N_{\rm max}=8\) and \(N_{\rm max}=10\).
- The continuum threshold for the nucleon-pion system is 1075 MeV (sum of physical proton and pion masses).

## Context

This work extends the non-perturbative Basis Light-Front Quantization method, previously applied to QED systems and mesons (Vary 2009, Wiecki 2014, Li 2015, Jia 2018), to a chiral nucleon-pion model originally studied perturbatively by Miller (1997, 2000). The results enable future calculations of additional proton observables such as elastic form factors and spin decomposition, and provide a foundation for including higher Fock sectors and internal quark degrees of freedom to study phenomena like the proton's flavor asymmetry (Alberg 2017).
