# Quantum Monte Carlo study of strongly interacting Fermi gases

> In recent years Quantum Monte Carlo techniques provided to be a valuable tool to study strongly interacting Fermi gases at zero temperature. We have used QMC methods to investigate several properties of the two-components Fermi gas at unitarity and in the BCS-BEC crossover, both with equal and unequal masses corresponding to the $Li-K$ Fermi mixture. In this paper we present several recent QMC results, including the energy at zero and finite effective range, the contact parameter and the static structure factor, which, at low momentum, depends strongly on the phonons in the unitary Fermi gas.

# Quantum Monte Carlo study of strongly interacting Fermi gases

## Key claim
Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) methods provide accurate zero-temperature results for strongly interacting Fermi gases at unitarity and across the BCS-BEC crossover, but the fixed-node approximation used in Diffusion Monte Carlo yields an upper bound to the exact energy whose accuracy depends on the quality of the trial wave function. The Auxiliary Field Monte Carlo (AFMC) method on a lattice avoids the fermion sign problem for unpolarized systems and gives exact energies, but requires extrapolation to zero effective range.

## Method
The authors model the two-component Fermi gas with a Hamiltonian using a Poschl-Teller two-body potential, tuning parameters to achieve desired scattering length and effective range. They use Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) with a Jastrow-BCS wave function including long-range phonon correlations, then project the ground state using Diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) with the fixed-node approximation. For unpolarized equal-mass systems, they also employ Auxiliary Field Monte Carlo (AFMC) on a lattice, which is exact because it avoids the sign problem.

## Key numbers
- Bertsch parameter at unitarity: xi = 0.372(5) from AFMC extrapolated to zero effective range (Carlson 2012), in agreement with MIT experiment (Ku 2012)
- Best fixed-node DMC upper bound: xi = 0.389(1) (Forbes 2012)
- Effective range slope parameter at unitarity: s = 0.11(3) and s = 0.12(2) from DMC and AFMC respectively, independent of mass ratio
- Fit to BCS-BEC crossover equation of state (extrapolated to r_e=0): xi = 0.3726(6), zeta = 0.900(2), nu = 0.46(1)
- Static structure factor S(q) agrees with experimental Bragg spectroscopy data within 1% (Hoinka 2013)

## Context
This work extends QMC calculations of the unitary Fermi gas by Carlson (2003, 2005, 2008, 2011) and Astrakharchik (2004) to include unequal-mass mixtures (Li-K) and finite effective range effects. The results enable quantitative comparison between cold atom systems and low-density neutron matter, where the neutron-neutron interaction has a small but finite effective range (Gezerlis 2008, 2010). The contact parameter calculations agree with Tan's relations (Tan 2008) and with experimental measurements from Bragg spectroscopy (Hoinka 2013, Kuhnle 2011). Future directions include dynamic response, inhomogeneous systems, and multi-component Fermi gases.
