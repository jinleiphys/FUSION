# Evolution of the phenomenologically determined collective potential along the chain of Zr isotopes

> The properties of the collective low-lying states of Zr isotopes which include excitation energies and $E2$ reduced transition probabilities indicate that some of these states are mainly spherical and the other are mainly deformed ones. We investigate the properties of the low-lying collective states of $^{92-102}$Zr and their evolution with increase of the number of neutrons based on the five-dimensional Geometrical Quadrupole Collective Model. The quadrupole-collective Bohr Hamiltonian with a potential having spherical and deformed minima, is applied. The relative depth of two minima, height and width of the barrier, rigidity of the potential near both minima are determined so as to achieve the best possible description of the observed properties of the low-lying collective quadrupole states of $^{92-102}$Zr. Satisfactory agreement with the experimental data on the excitation energies and the $E2$ reduced transition probabilities is obtained. The evolution of the collective potential with increase of $A$ is described and the distributions of the wave functions of the collective states in $ﾎｲ-ﾎｳ$ plane are found. The resulting potential evolves with $A$ increase from having only one spherical minimum in $^{92}$Zr, through the potentials having both spherical and deformed minima, to the potential with one deformed minimum in $^{102}$Zr.

## Key claim
The collective potential for the chain of even-even Zr isotopes from A=92 to A=102 evolves from a single spherical minimum in $^{92}$Zr, through potentials with both spherical and deformed minima separated by a barrier, to a single deformed minimum in $^{102}$Zr, as determined by fitting the Bohr Hamiltonian to reproduce experimental excitation energies and E2 transition probabilities.

## Method
The authors solved the five-dimensional Bohr Hamiltonian with a simplified inertia tensor (assuming $B_{¥beta¥beta}=B_{¥gamma¥gamma}=B_0$, $B_{¥beta¥gamma}=0$, and a step-function rotational inertia coefficient $b_{rot}$ that takes value 1 for $¥beta$ below the barrier and $b_{def}<1$ above it). The potential energy was taken as $V(¥beta,¥gamma)=U(¥beta)+C_¥gamma¥beta^3(1-¥cos 3¥gamma)$, where $U(¥beta)$ was approximated by three parabolas (two for the spherical and deformed minima, one inverted parabola for the barrier). The eigenfunctions were expanded in a basis of SO(5) spherical harmonics and harmonic oscillator functions in $¥beta$, with the oscillator length $¥beta_0$ set to the barrier position and basis truncation at $v_{max}=50$ and $(n_¥beta)_{max}=30$.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of $^{96}$Zr (Mardyban et al., 2020) by applying the same five-dimensional Bohr Hamiltonian approach to the full chain $^{92-102}$Zr, using a simplified three-parabola parametrization of the collective potential rather than the 16-point interpolation used earlier. The results are consistent with the known shape transition in Zr isotopes previously studied by various theoretical approaches including shell model, Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov, and energy density functional methods (Federman and Pittel, 1977; Bonche et al., 1985; Skalski et al., 1997; Delaroche et al., 2010). The work enables systematic comparison of phenomenological collective potentials across isotopic chains and provides wave function distributions in the $¥beta-¥gamma$ plane that can guide microscopic calculations.
