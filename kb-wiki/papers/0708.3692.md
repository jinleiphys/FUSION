# Influence of the N*(1440) and N*(1535) Resonances in Intermediate Energy pp and np Scattering

> Motivated by a recent measurement of proton-proton elastic scattering observables up to 3.0 GeV, we investigate the description of those data within models of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction valid above the pion production threshold. In addition to including the well known Delta resonance we incorporate two low-lying N* resonances, the N*(1440) and the N*(1535), and study their influence on pp and np observables for projectile laboratory kinetic energies up to 1.5 GeV.

# Influence of the N*(1440) and N*(1535) Resonances in Intermediate Energy pp and np Scattering

## Key claim

The inclusion of the N*(1440) (Roper) and N*(1535) resonances in a meson-exchange model of nucleon-nucleon scattering improves the description of observables up to 1.5 GeV projectile kinetic energy, but the effect of the Roper resonance is small overall and the model still systematically overpredicts the analyzing power and elastic cross section above 800 MeV, particularly for pp scattering at larger angles.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic meson-exchange model using the Thompson reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, including exchange of pi, sigma, rho, omega, eta, a0, f0, and eta' mesons. They incorporate the Delta(1232), N*(1440), and N*(1535) resonances via iterative loop diagrams with resonance intermediate states, parameterizing the resonance widths using penetrability factors fitted to experimental partial widths from the Particle Data Group. The model parameters are fitted to the SAID SP07 partial wave analysis phase shifts rather than directly to scattering data, using the Minuit minimization program.

## Key numbers

- The N*(1440) resonance partial width for Npi decay is determined from a total width of 391 MeV (from Manley), with the total PDG range being 200-450 MeV
- The N*(1535) resonance decays into Npi and Neta, with the eta production threshold corresponding to NN laboratory energy 1253 MeV
- The NNpi coupling constant is fixed to g^2_NNpi/4pi = 13.8, based on IUCF measurements and Nijmegen extractions
- The f^2_NDelta pi/4pi coupling is fixed to 0.224 after fitting, compared to values of 0.35 used in prior work (D52, Eyser) and 0.36 from Schutz (1998)
- The cutoff parameter Lambda_NN*pi for the Roper resonance is slightly reduced compared to the model without it
- The width cutoff parameter kappa is set to 200 MeV for the Delta resonance and 400 MeV for the two N* resonances
- The resonance on-shell energies for intermediate states are: NDelta at T_lab = 632 MeV, NP11 at 1136 MeV, and NS11 at 1381 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier meson-exchange models of NN scattering above the pion production threshold, particularly the models of D52 and Eyser which included only the Delta resonance. The authors note that Eyser found polarization observables in pp scattering above 1 GeV were badly represented by their model. The study is motivated by new EDDA Collaboration measurements of pp elastic scattering observables up to 3.0 GeV and the SAID SP07 phase shift analysis (Arndt, 2007). The inclusion of the N*(1440) is found to be vital for T=0 inelasticities close to threshold, consistent with earlier findings by Lee (1984). The work enables future studies of the second resonance region in NN scattering and complements efforts at the Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) using photoproduction data from ELSA, JLab, MAMI, GRAAL, and Spring-8.
