# High Density Quark Matter and the Renormalization Group in QCD with two and three flavors

> We consider the most general four fermion operators in QCD for two and three massless flavors and study their renormalization in the vicinity of the Fermi surface. We show that, asymptotically, the largest coupling corresponds to scalar diquark condensation. Asymptotically the direct and iterated (molecular) instanton interactions become equal. We provide simple arguments for the form of the operators that diagonalize the evolution equations. Some solutions of the flow equations exhibit instabilities arising out of purely repulsive interactions.

## Key claim

The renormalization group analysis shows that, asymptotically near the Fermi surface, the largest coupling corresponds to scalar diquark condensation, and the direct and iterated (molecular) instanton interactions become equal; however, the renormalization group only determines the running of couplings down to some infrared scale, and if a subdominant coupling is unusually large at the matching scale, it might still dominate the pairing.

## Method

The authors consider the most general four-fermion operators in QCD for two and three massless flavors, restricted to a spherical Fermi surface and local operators invariant under the appropriate chiral symmetry. They study the renormalization group evolution of these operators as modes are successively integrated out closer to the Fermi surface, using the loop integral for quark-quark scattering in the vicinity of the Fermi surface. The evolution equations are diagonalized by transforming to a basis of diquark operators, and the flow equations are solved analytically.

## Key numbers

- The density of states on the Fermi surface is given by \(N = \mu^2/(2\pi^2)\).
- The loop integral factor is \(I = \frac{i}{8\pi^2}\mu^2 \log(\Lambda_{IR}/\Lambda_{UV})\), where \([\Lambda_{IR},\Lambda_{UV}]\) is the range of momenta integrated out.
- For the dominant operator in the \(N_f=3\) case, the coupling evolves as \(G_1(t) = 1/(1+(N/3)G_1(0)t)\), reaching a Landau pole at \(t_c = 3/(N G_1(0))\).
- For the \(N_f=2\) case with instanton operators, the solutions for the couplings are \(G_2(t) = \frac{1}{2}\{1/(a+Nt) + 1/(b+Nt)\}\) and \(K_2(t) = \frac{1}{2}\{1/(a+Nt) - 1/(b+Nt)\}\), where \(a,b = (G_2(0)\pm K_2(0))^{-1}\), with the Landau pole located at \(t_c = -a/N\) or \(t_c = -b/N\).
- The ratio of the two instanton-like couplings is fixed as \(K_T/K_S = 1/(2N_c-1)\).

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group approach to cold Fermi systems, first applied to QCD by Evans, Hsu, and Schwetz (1998) who concentrated on one-gluon exchange interactions. The analysis broadly supports the findings of Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and Rapp, Schafer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky (1998) regarding scalar diquark condensation and color superconductivity. The results enable a systematic framework for analyzing the most general quark-quark interactions near the Fermi surface, though the authors note that determining the actual gap and pairing structure still requires a variational calculation.
