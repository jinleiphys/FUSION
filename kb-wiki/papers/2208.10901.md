# Multiplicity, transverse momentum and pseudorapidity dependence of open-heavy flavored hadron production in proton+proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}$= 13 TeV using PYTHIA8

> Recently, with the upgradation of LHC, it is realized that study of heavy-flavored hadrons, namely $Λ_{c}^{+}$ and $\rm{D}^{0}$ in hadronic collisions, could reveal the possibility of thermalization of charm quarks. With this motivation, we study the production dynamics of these hadrons using a pQCD-inspired Monte Carlo event generator called PYTHIA8 in proton+proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV. The production dynamics of these hadrons are analyzed through charged-particle multiplicity, transverse momentum, and pseudorapidity. With the help of the established PYTHIA8 tunes to mimic the behavior of flow-like features, we investigated the variation of effective temperature and degree of non-extensivity using thermodynamically consistent non-extensive Tsallis statistics. We further attempted to establish a connection between the initial state and final state by estimating the correlation between the number of multi-partonic interactions ($\rm{n}_{MPI}$) with the Knudsen number.

# Multiplicity, transverse momentum and pseudorapidity dependence of open-heavy flavored hadron production in proton+proton collisions at √s = 13 TeV using PYTHIA8

## Key claim
Using PYTHIA8 simulations with color reconnection and multi-partonic interactions, the authors find that the effective temperature and non-extensive parameter extracted from Tsallis fits to Λc+ and D0 pT-spectra increase with charged-particle multiplicity, suggesting that PYTHIA8's color reconnection mechanism can mimic thermalization-like features for heavy-flavored hadrons in pp collisions, though the system remains far from equilibrium as indicated by q > 1.

## Method
The authors generated 350 million inelastic, non-diffractive pp collision events at √s = 13 TeV using PYTHIA8 with the 4C tune, color reconnection mode 2, and multi-partonic interactions enabled. They fitted the simulated pT-spectra of Λc+ (decaying via p + K− + π+) and D0 (decaying via K− + π+) with a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis non-extensive distribution function to extract effective temperature (T) and non-extensive parameter (q) as functions of charged-particle multiplicity, transverse momentum range, and pseudorapidity. They further computed thermodynamic quantities including squared speed of sound, isothermal compressibility, and Knudsen number from the Tsallis fit parameters.

## Key numbers
- Simulation: 350 million pp collision events at √s = 13 TeV generated with PYTHIA8
- Transverse momentum cut: pT ≥ 0.5 GeV/c (PhaseSpace:pTHatMinDiverge = 0.5) with pT minimum phase space cut-off of 2.0 GeV/c (PhaseSpace:pTHatMin = 2.0)
- Multiplicity classes: Nch ranges of 5-10, 10-15, 15-20, 20-30, 30-40, and 40-150 (|η| < 0.8)
- pT range studied: from (0.2-2.0) GeV/c up to (0.2-9.0) GeV/c in steps of 1.0 GeV/c
- Pseudorapidity range: η from 0 to 2.4 in steps of 0.2
- Hard-core radii used for Knudsen number calculation: rh = 0.62 fm for Λc+ and rh = 0.2 fm for D0
- Effective temperature and q increase with charged-particle multiplicity for both Λc+ and D0 in both color reconnection on and off modes
- Effective temperature is lower for color reconnection off compared to color reconnection on for all multiplicity classes
- A step-like behavior in T is observed around pT ≈ 6 GeV/c for Λc+ with color reconnection on
- Effective temperature decreases with increasing pseudorapidity for both hadron species

## Context
This work extends phenomenological studies of heavy-flavor production in small systems using PYTHIA8, building on prior investigations of flow-like features from color reconnection and multi-partonic interactions by Ortiz Velasquez (2013). The authors compare their simulated results with ALICE experimental data on multiplicity-dependent prompt Λc+ and D0 production in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV (ALICE, 2021). The study enables further investigation of thermalization possibilities for charm quarks in hadronic collisions, particularly relevant for LHC Run 3 measurements of baryon-to-meson ratios in the charm sector, and extends previous Tsallis statistics analyses of light-flavor hadrons by Khuntia (2018) and Rath (2019) to the heavy-flavor sector.
