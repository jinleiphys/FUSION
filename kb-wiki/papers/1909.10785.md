# Suppression of quarkonia in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV

> We study different processes responsible for the modification of quarkonia yields in the medium produced in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV. The quarkonia and heavy flavour cross sections are estimated using the measurements in pp collisions at LHC energies and shadowing corrections are obtained using the EPS09 parametrizations. A kinetic model is used which incorporates quarkonia suppression inside the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP), suppression due to hadronic comovers, and regeneration from recombination of heavy quark pairs. The quarkonia dissociation cross section due to gluon collisions, including both color-electric dipole and color-magnetic dipole transitions, has been employed. The regeneration rate is obtained using the principle of detailed balance. The effect of these processes on the nuclear modification factors for both $\Jpsi$ and $Υ$ in different ranges of transverse momentum $\pT$ and rapidity has been studied for PbPb collisions at $\sNN =$ 5.02 TeV. The calculations are compared with the available results from LHC experiments. Both the suppression and regeneration due to a QGP are effective in the low and intermediate $\pT$ range. The large observed suppression of $\Jpsi$ at $\pT~>$ 10 GeV/$c$ is larger than the suppression expected due to gluon dissociation.

## Key claim

The large observed suppression of J/ψ at pT > 10 GeV/c in PbPb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV is larger than the suppression expected due to gluon dissociation alone, indicating that additional mechanisms such as partonic energy loss may be required to explain high-pT data.

## Method

The authors employ a kinetic model that evolves quarkonia populations using a rate equation incorporating gluon dissociation (including both color-electric dipole E1 and color-magnetic dipole M1 transitions), suppression from hadronic comovers, and regeneration via recombination of heavy quark pairs using detailed balance. The initial heavy quark and quarkonia cross sections are estimated from pp measurements at LHC energies with EPS09 NLO shadowing corrections, and the system evolution is governed by an isentropic cylindrical expansion using lattice QCD equation of state with initial temperatures extracted from measured charged particle multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T0 = 0.516 GeV for 0-5% most central collisions at midrapidity, and T0 = 0.487 GeV at forward rapidity, extracted from (dNch/dη)0-5% = 1943 with initial time τ0 = 0.3 fm/c
- Binding energies used: ε0 = 0.64 GeV for J/ψ and ε0 = 1.10 GeV for Υ(1S)
- Total ccbar production cross section in pp at 5.02 TeV: σpp = 6.754+1.195-2.015 mb; total bbbar production cross section: σpp = 210.30+70.769-77.640 μb
- Number of ccbar pairs per PbPb event: NPbPb = 26.23+4.639-7.825; number of J/ψ per PbPb event: NPbPb = 0.1381+0.024-0.041
- Number of bbbar pairs per PbPb event: NPbPb = 1.007+0.339-0.372; number of Υ per PbPb event: NPbPb = 0.0020+0.0007-0.0007
- Total PbPb cross section at 5.02 TeV: 7.7 b
- Freeze-out temperature: Tf = 0.140 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous kinetic model study (Kumar 2014) by incorporating the updated gluon dissociation cross section from Chen and He (2017) that includes both color-electric and color-magnetic dipole transitions, and applies it to the newer LHC data at √sNN = 5.02 TeV from CMS, ATLAS, and ALICE. The calculations show that regeneration from charm quark recombination is dominant at low pT and compensates strong gluon dissociation, while at high pT the observed suppression exceeds what gluon dissociation predicts, suggesting that partonic energy loss mechanisms (as studied for open heavy flavor) may be needed. The model reproduces the flat centrality dependence of J/ψ RAA at low pT observed by ALICE and the centrality dependence at higher pT seen by CMS and ATLAS, while for Υ states the regeneration contribution is negligible even at LHC energies.
