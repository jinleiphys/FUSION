# Interactions of quarkonium at low energies

> We present two examples of the short-distance QCD calculations for the quarkonium (Φ) interactions; 1) πΦelastic and πΦ-> πΦ' cross sections, and 2) the potential between two Φ's. The former is relevant for the Φsuppression in heavy ion collisions; we find that the corresponding cross sections are very small. For the latter we derive the sum rule which relates the strength of the potential to the energy density of the QCD vacuum. The key starting point in both cases is that the leading operator in the operator product expansion (OPE) for the low--energy scattering amplitude is the trace of the QCD stress tensor whose matrix elements are unambiguously fixed by the low energy theorem. The resonances in the two-pion scalar channel are taken into account as a formfactor.

# Interactions of quarkonium at low energies

## Key claim
The cross sections for pion-quarkonium elastic scattering and inelastic excitation are very small due to a double-dipole suppression factor and chiral symmetry constraints, but the calculations are probably valid only up to a factor of 2 based on comparison with the measured decay width of ψ' → J/ψ ππ.

## Method
The authors use the operator product expansion (OPE) for low-energy quarkonium interactions, where the leading operator is the trace of the QCD stress tensor θμμ, whose matrix elements are fixed by the low-energy theorem and scale anomaly. For πΦ scattering, the amplitude is expressed in terms of the pion formfactor in the scalar-isoscalar channel, while for onium-onium scattering, the potential is derived from the θμμ correlator and related to the QCD vacuum energy density through a sum rule.

## Key numbers
- The π J/ψ elastic cross section is shown in Fig. 1 to be very small, with the complete result (including the ππ formfactor F) yielding values below approximately 0.1 mb for center-of-mass momenta up to 1 GeV/c.
- The π J/ψ → π ψ' transition cross section is shown in Fig. 2, with the complete result (including F) yielding values below approximately 0.01 mb for center-of-mass momenta up to 1 GeV/c.
- The calculated decay width for ψ' → J/ψ ππ is 260 keV with the formfactor F(s) and 70 keV without it, compared to the experimentally measured value of 135 ± 20 keV.
- The sum rule for the onium-onium potential relates the volume integral of the potential (minus its perturbative part) to the QCD vacuum energy density |εvac|, with the prefactor (d̄2 a0²/ε0)² (4π²/b)² where b = 9 for Nc = 3, Nf = 3.
- The long-distance limit of the onium-onium potential scales as R^{-5/2} exp(-2 μπ R) where μπ is the pion mass, with a dependence on Nc and Nf proportional to (Nf² - 1)² / (11 Nc - 2 Nf)².

## Context
This work extends the multipole expansion approach to quarkonium interactions developed by Peskin (1979), Bhanot and Peskin (1979), and Kaidalov (1982), and applies it to exclusive πΦ cross sections relevant for J/ψ suppression in heavy ion collisions. The authors previously evaluated inclusive quarkonium dissociation cross sections in Kharzeev and Satz (1994). The results support the idea of McLerran and Svetitsky (1994) and Kharzeev and Satz (1994) that heavy quarkonium interactions in hadron gas and quark-gluon plasma are very different. The onium-onium potential sum rule provides a first step toward understanding nuclear force from QCD, building on earlier work by Appelquist (1975), Bhanot and Peskin (1979), and Brambilla and Montagna (1993).
