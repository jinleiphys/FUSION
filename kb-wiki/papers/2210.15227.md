# Ab initio no-core shell model study of neutron-rich $^{18,19,20}$C isotopes

> We implement the ab initio no-core shell model approach to study neutron-rich $^{18}$C, $^{19}$C and $^{20}$C isotopes. For this purpose, we employ charge-dependent Bonn 2000 (CDB2K), inside non-local outside Yukawa (INOY) and chiral next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^{3}$LO) nucleon-nucleon interactions. Low-lying energy spectra, electromagnetic properties and point-proton radii for these nuclei up to basis space $N_{\rm max}$ = 4 are calculated. Binding energies obtained with INOY interaction are in better agreement with the experimental values as compared to other \textit{ab initio} interactions. We also show the behavior of ground state energy and point-proton radii with the NCSM parameters, $\hbar$$Ω$ and $N_{\rm max}$. We report a strong sensitivity of the B(E2) values from the first excited $2^+$ to the ground state of $^{18}$C and $^{20}$C to the nuclear interaction. Shell model calculations with YSOX interaction are also performed, and corresponding results are compared with ab initio one.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the INOY nucleon-nucleon interaction yields binding energies for neutron-rich carbon isotopes that are in better agreement with experimental values than other *ab initio* interactions, but only within the limited basis space of Nmax = 4, where all interactions still underbind the ground states by approximately 11% for INOY.

## Method

The authors performed *ab initio* no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for 18C, 19C, and 20C using three realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions: charge-dependent Bonn 2000 (CDB2K), inside non-local outside Yukawa (INOY), and chiral N3LO. Calculations were carried out in harmonic oscillator basis spaces up to Nmax = 4, with the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki renormalization scheme used to derive effective interactions. Additionally, shell model calculations using the YSOX interaction in the psd-model space were performed for comparison using the KSHELL code.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of 18C: -102.593 MeV (INOY) compared to experimental -115.670(31) MeV, with CDB2K giving -94.275 MeV and N3LO giving -97.657 MeV
- Ground state binding energy of 19C: -101.687 MeV (INOY) compared to experimental -116.242(95) MeV
- Ground state binding energy of 20C: -106.447 MeV (INOY) compared to experimental -119.22(24) MeV
- B(E2; 2+1 to 0+1) for 18C: 3.134 e2fm4 (CDB2K), 1.507 e2fm4 (INOY), 3.364 e2fm4 (N3LO), compared to experimental 3.64(+0.15/-0.14)(stat)(+0.40/-0.47)(syst) e2fm4
- B(E2; 2+1 to 0+1) for 20C: 3.865 e2fm4 (CDB2K), 2.099 e2fm4 (INOY), 4.080 e2fm4 (N3LO), compared to experimental upper limit of less than 3.68 e2fm4
- Point-proton radius for 18C: 2.29 fm (CDB2K), 2.06 fm (INOY), 2.30 fm (N3LO), compared to experimental 2.39(4) fm
- Point-proton radius for 19C: 2.30 fm (CDB2K), 2.07 fm (INOY), 2.31 fm (N3LO), compared to experimental 2.40(3) fm
- Excitation energy of 2+1 state in 18C: 1.855 MeV (CDB2K), 1.550 MeV (INOY), 1.849 MeV (N3LO), compared to experimental 1.588(8) MeV
- Excitation energy of 2+1 state in 20C: 1.757 MeV (CDB2K), 1.848 MeV (INOY), 1.730 MeV (N3LO), compared to experimental 1.618(11) MeV
- B(M1; 3/2+1 to 1/2+1) for 19C: less than 0.001 muN2 (CDB2K and N3LO), 0.003 muN2 (INOY), compared to experimental 0.00321(25) muN2

## Context

This work extends previous NCSM studies of carbon isotopes by Forssen et al. (2009), who examined even-even carbon isotopes from A=10 to A=20 using the NCSM approach with CDB2K and chiral interactions. The present study is the first NCSM investigation of 19C, a known one-neutron halo nucleus. The results enable comparison with experimental B(E2) measurements for 18C and 20C, where conflicting experimental values exist from Ong et al. and Petri et al., and with point-proton radii measurements from Kanungo et al. using charge-changing cross-section techniques. The work also tests whether the INOY interaction, which incorporates non-locality to account for some three-nucleon force effects, can improve description of near-drip-line carbon isotopes without explicit three-body forces.
