# Scalar mesons in a finite volume

> Using effective field theory methods, we discuss the extraction of the mass and width of the scalar mesons f0(980) and a0(980) from the finite-volume spectrum in lattice QCD. In particular, it is argued that the nature of these states can be studied by invoking twisted boundary conditions, as well as investigating the quark mass dependence of the spectrum.

## Key claim

Using effective field theory methods, the authors argue that the mass and width of the scalar mesons f0(980) and a0(980) can be extracted from finite-volume lattice QCD spectra by determining the K-matrix elements near the K̅K threshold, but only if the inelastic channels effectively decouple and the Bethe-Salpeter kernels are nearly volume-independent.

## Method

The authors generalize the Lüscher finite-volume formalism to coupled-channel ππ–K̅K scattering, deriving a secular equation that relates the finite-volume energy spectrum to the K-matrix elements K11, K22, and K12. They propose using twisted boundary conditions on the strange quark field to vary the K̅K threshold energy, enabling independent measurements at the same center-of-mass energy with different box sizes L and twist angles θ. The K-matrix elements extracted from the spectrum are then analytically continued into the complex plane via the secular equation to locate the S-matrix poles, giving the resonance mass and width.

## Key numbers

- The inelasticity parameter η ≥ 0.98 below the K̅K threshold, indicating that coupling to inelastic channels is extremely weak.
- The correction from mixing of S-waves with partial waves of orbital momentum l ≥ 4 does not exceed a few percent below 1 GeV.
- For a K̅K molecular state, the mass can be written as M_mol = M_K + M_̅K − ε, with binding energy ε ≪ M_K, leading to a kaon mass dependence that is linear with a slope of two.
- For a tetraquark state, the leading kaon mass dependence is expected to be M_K² or M_K³, in contrast to the linear dependence for a molecule.

## Context

This work extends the Lüscher finite-volume method (Lüscher 1990) to the coupled-channel case relevant for scalar mesons near the K̅K threshold, building on earlier work by Lage, Bernard, and collaborators (2009) that used only periodic boundary conditions. The approach provides a model-independent framework to distinguish molecular states from compact quark states using the "pole counting" method of Weinberg (1963) and Morgan (1992), reformulated for lattice QCD. The paper also proposes a strangeness-content criterion based on the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to distinguish tetraquarks from q̅q mesons, complementing the hybrid boundary condition method of Suganuma (2005).
