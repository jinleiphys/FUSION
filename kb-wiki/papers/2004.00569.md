# Chirality and Magnetic Field

> We present a brief overview on recent developments of theory and phenomenology for novel many-body phenomena related to the chirality and magnetic field, with an emphasis on their experimental implications and possible detection in relativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

This paper presents a brief overview of recent developments in theory and phenomenology for many-body phenomena related to chirality and magnetic fields in relativistic nuclear collisions, with the caveat that the interpretation of experimental data on the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) remains inconclusive due to significant background contamination, and the CME-contributed portion is likely only at the order of (5-10)% level.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical foundations of chiral anomaly, axial charge generation via gluon field topology, and anomalous transport processes including the Chiral Magnetic Effect and Chiral Vortical Effect. They summarize experimental searches for CME using charge asymmetry in two-particle azimuthal correlations at RHIC and LHC, and describe the development of the EBE-AVFD (Event-By-Event Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics) framework for simulating CME signals and backgrounds in heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- The fireball in non-central heavy ion collisions inherits angular momentum of order ~10^{4-5} hbar, inducing strong vorticity in the fluid.
- The fireball experiences an extremely strong magnetic field of order ~10^{17-18} Gauss.
- The initial magnetic field estimate gives |eB| ~ m_pi^2 at the center point between two colliding nuclei upon initial impact.
- The magnetic field lifetime is estimated as tau_B ~ (115 +/- 16) GeV*fm/c / sqrt(s_NN), giving a duration of (0.5-0.6) fm/c at 200 GeV collisions.
- The initial magnetic field decays over a short period of order (R_A/gamma) ~ 0.1 fm/c due to spectator departure.
- The isobaric collision experiment contrasts RuRu (Z=44) and ZrZr (Z=40) systems, both with A=96.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on chiral anomaly and anomalous transport in quark-gluon plasma, building on earlier theoretical developments by Kharzeev (2004) on CME and Voloshin (2004) on charge asymmetry correlations. It summarizes recent experimental results from STAR at RHIC and ALICE and CMS at LHC, and describes the EBE-AVFD framework developed through the BEST Collaboration CME Working Group effort. The paper enables future interpretation of the isobaric collision experiment data from the 2018 RHIC run, which aims to provide a decisive test for CME detection.
