# A critical analysis on deeply bound kaonic states in nuclei

> We make a critical analysis on the theoretical calculations that lead to predictions of deeply bound kaonic states in nuclei. The model set-up, after dropping several important processes and channels, leads unavoidably to an unrealistic deep potential with a very small imaginary part. We review also the experimental results taken as reference for the claim of deeply bound kaons. We suggest that the peaks of the proton spectra come from $K^-$ absorption on a pair of nucleons, leaving the rest of the nucleons as spectators. Based on this conjecture we predict what would happen in other nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that predictions of deeply bound kaonic states in nuclei rely on a simplified theoretical model that omits important coupled-channel dynamics and self-consistency effects, leading to an unrealistically deep kaon-nucleus potential with a very small imaginary part; the authors suggest that experimental peaks attributed to deeply bound kaons instead come from K- absorption on a pair of nucleons with the remaining nucleons acting as spectators.

## Method

The authors perform a critical analysis of the theoretical framework used by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002), comparing it with the chiral unitary model that includes coupled channels (K- N, pi Sigma, pi Lambda) and self-consistent medium corrections. They examine the treatment of the Lambda(1405) resonance, the Pauli blocking effects, and the self-consistency procedure for the kaon self-energy in nuclear matter. They also review the experimental results from the KEK experiment on stopped K- on 4He and propose an alternative interpretation based on two-nucleon kaon absorption.

## Key numbers

- The chiral unitary model gives a kaon optical potential strength of 40-50 MeV at nuclear matter density, compared to 618 MeV with an imaginary part of 11 MeV in the Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) approach after modifications
- The Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) model predicts kaon binding energies of 108 MeV in 3H and 86 MeV in 4H, with widths of 20 and 24 MeV respectively
- The experimental peak in the proton spectrum from stopped K- on 4He appears at 475 MeV/c (before energy loss corrections), matching the 488 MeV/c proton momentum from K- NN to Sigma N events
- The chiral unitary model predicts two Lambda(1405) states: one around 1396 MeV with width about 140 MeV coupling mostly to pi Sigma, and another around 1420 MeV with width about 30 MeV coupling mostly to K- N
- The K- p scattering amplitude below threshold is about 2.4 fm in the chiral approach versus about 5 fm in the Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) model

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach to kaon-nucleon interactions developed by Weise (2001), Angels (2001), and Oset (2001), and directly contradicts the predictions of deeply bound kaonic states by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002). The paper argues that self-consistent calculations including medium corrections (Lutz 1998, Schaffner 2000, Angels 2001) produce a much weaker attractive potential (40-50 MeV) than claimed by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002), and that the experimental peaks from Suzuki (2004) can be explained by conventional two-nucleon kaon absorption rather than exotic deeply bound states. The analysis enables future experiments to test the two-nucleon absorption mechanism by measuring pion spectra in coincidence with the proton peaks.
