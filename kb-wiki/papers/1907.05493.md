# Variational approximations to exact solutions in shell-model valence spaces: calcium isotopes in the pf-shell

> We study the performance of self-consistent mean-field and beyond-mean-field approximations in shell-model valence spaces. In particular, Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov, particle-number variation after projection and projected generator coordinate methods are applied to obtain ground-state and excitation energies for even-even and odd-even Calcium isotopes in the pf-shell. The standard (and non-trivial) KB3G nuclear effective interaction has been used. The comparison with the exact solutions -- provided by the full diagonalization of the Hamiltonian -- shows an outstanding agreement when particle-number and angular-momentum restorations are performed and both quadrupole and neutron-neutron pairing degrees of freedom are explicitly explored as collective coordinates.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) calculations using particle-number variation after projection (PNVAP) intrinsic states and exploring both quadrupole and neutron-neutron pairing degrees of freedom can reproduce exact shell-model diagonalization results for calcium isotopes in the pf-shell to within approximately 119 keV for the ground state, though the agreement degrades for higher-spin excited states.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) and particle-number variation after projection (PNVAP) calculations to generate sets of intrinsic Bogolyubov quasi-particle states constrained on quadrupole deformation (β2, γ) and neutron-neutron pairing (δnn) degrees of freedom. These intrinsic states were then used in projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) calculations that restore particle-number and angular-momentum symmetry and mix configurations along the collective coordinates. The results were compared to exact interacting shell model (ISM) solutions obtained by full diagonalization of the KB3G effective interaction in the pf-shell valence space using the code ANTOINE.

## Key numbers

- The difference between the exact and PGCM3 ground-state energy for 48Ca is 119 keV
- The PNVAP spherical minimum for 48Ca lies at -7.195 MeV, compared to the exact ISM ground state at -7.569 MeV
- The HFB+PNAMP minimum for 48Ca lies 300 keV above the PNVAP+PNAMP minimum
- PNVAP provides approximately 750 keV extra binding energy compared to HFB at the spherical point in 48Ca
- The B(E2:2+1→0+1) transition probability is 12.7 e2fm4 in all PGCM schemes versus 11.5 e2fm4 in the exact ISM calculation
- The spectroscopic quadrupole moment Q(2+1) is +4.4 e fm2 in PGCM3 versus +4.1 e fm2 in ISM
- The spectroscopic quadrupole moment Q(4+1) is +8.3 e fm2 in PGCM3 versus +7.5 e fm2 in ISM

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of HFB and PNVAP potential energy surfaces in sd- and pf-shell nuclei by Maqbool (2011) by adding angular momentum restoration and configuration mixing. It also connects to the VAMPIR approach of Schmid (1987, 2004) but omits the simultaneous angular-momentum variation after projection due to computational cost. The results enable benchmarking of variational methods against exact solutions in a controlled valence space, and the authors note that future improvements could include cranking calculations or multi-quasi-particle excitations to better reproduce high-spin states.
