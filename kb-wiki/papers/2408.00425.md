# Improving relativistic energy density functionals with tensor couplings

> Energy density functionals (EDFs) have been used extensively with great success to calculate properties of nuclei and to predict the equation of state (EOS) of dense nuclear matter. Besides non-relativistic EDFs, mostly of the Skyrme or Gogny type, relativistic EDFs of different types are in widespread use. In these latter approaches, the effective in-medium interaction is described by an exchange of mesons between nucleons. In most cases, only minimal meson-nucleon couplings are considered. The effects of additional tensor couplings were rarely investigated. In this work, a new relativistic EDF with tensor couplings and density dependent minimal meson-nucleon couplings will be presented. The parameters of the model are determined using a carefully selected set of experimental data with realistic uncertainties that are determined self-consistently. Predictions for various nuclear observables, the nuclear matter equation of state, and properties of neutron stars are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new relativistic energy density functional that includes tensor couplings between nucleons and mesons (specifically omega and rho mesons) alongside density-dependent minimal meson-nucleon couplings, with model parameters determined using a carefully selected set of experimental data where the uncertainties are determined self-consistently rather than preset.

## Method

The authors construct a covariant Lagrangian density with scalar (sigma, delta) and vector (omega, rho) mesons plus the photon, adding tensor coupling terms for the omega and rho mesons to the nucleons. The minimal meson-nucleon couplings are taken as functions of the vector density following the functional form introduced in Typel (1999), while the tensor couplings are constants. The Dirac equation for nucleons and the meson field equations are solved self-consistently in the Hartree approximation (no-sea approximation) using the Lagrange-mesh technique for spherical nuclei, with center-of-mass corrections applied in non-relativistic form.

## Key numbers

* The conversion value hbar = 197.3269804 MeV fm/c is used throughout the calculations.
* The Lagrange-mesh calculation uses N_zero = 25 zeroes of Laguerre polynomials with alpha = 2|kappa| for the radial grid.
* The scale parameter h is chosen so the largest zero of L_N^(0) lies at radius r = 0.95R, with R = r_0 A^(1/3) + 10 fm and r_0 = 1.2 fm, depending on mass number A.
* The tensor couplings Gamma_Tomega and Gamma_Trho are dimensionless, defined with a division by the proton mass M_pi in the Lagrangian.

## Context

This work extends the rare investigations of tensor couplings in relativistic energy density functionals, which were explored in early work by Rufa (1988) and later by Furnstahl (1997) for omega-tensor effects on spin-orbit splittings, and by Mao (2002) for a model with both omega and rho tensor couplings fitted to eight nuclei from O-16 to Pb-208. The present paper improves upon the more limited parameter fit of Typel (2020) and the recent parameterisation by Mercier (2022) by using a larger set of nuclear observables with self-consistently determined uncertainties, enabling more reliable predictions for nuclear properties, the nuclear matter equation of state, and neutron star observables.
