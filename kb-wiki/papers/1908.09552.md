# Meson Screening Masses in (2+1)-Flavor QCD

> We present lattice QCD results for mesonic screening masses in the temperature range 140 MeV $\lesssim T \lesssim$ 2500 MeV. Our calculations were carried out using (2+1)-flavors of the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action, with a physical value for the strange quark mass and two values of the light quark mass corresponding to pion masses of 160 MeV and 140 MeV. Continuum-extrapolated results were obtained using calculations with a variety of lattice spacings corresponding to temporal lattice extents $N_τ= 6 - 16$. We discuss the implications of these results for the effective restoration of various symmetries in the high temperature phase of QCD, as well as the approach toward the perturbative limit.

## Key claim
The paper provides continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results for mesonic screening masses in (2+1)-flavor QCD, showing that chiral SU_L(2) x SU_R(2) partners become degenerate at or very close to the pseudo-critical temperature T_pc, while axial U_A(1) partners become degenerate only at about 1.3 T_pc, with this pattern persisting in light-strange and strange-strange sectors but at higher temperatures.

## Method
The authors used the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action with physical strange quark mass and light quark masses corresponding to pion masses of 140 MeV and 160 MeV. They performed lattice calculations on ensembles with temporal extents N_tau = 6, 8, 10, 12, and 16, enabling continuum extrapolations in the temperature range 140 MeV to 974 MeV, with additional calculations up to 2.5 GeV. Screening masses were extracted from the exponential decay of spatial meson correlation functions for pseudoscalar (PS), vector (V), scalar (S), and axial vector (AV) channels in light-light, light-strange, and strange-strange flavor combinations.

## Key numbers
- Pseudo-critical temperature T_pc = (156.5 +/- 1.5) MeV, taken from Bazavov (2018)
- Goldstone pion masses: 140 MeV for m_l/m_s = 1/27 and 160 MeV for m_l/m_s = 1/20
- Strange quark mass tuned to M_eta_ss = 686 MeV via leading-order chiral perturbation theory
- Continuum-extrapolated screening masses provided for temperatures from 0.132 GeV to 1.000 GeV for all four channels and three flavor combinations
- At T = 0.140 GeV (light-light): m_PS = 0.150(2) GeV, m_V = 0.70(7) GeV, m_S = 0.24(1) GeV, m_AV = 0.94(7) GeV
- At T = 0.200 GeV (light-light): m_PS = 0.64(2) GeV, m_V = 1.09(4) GeV, m_S = 0.68(3) GeV, m_AV = 1.11(3) GeV
- At T = 1.000 GeV (light-light): m_PS = 6.3(2) GeV, m_V = 6.63(9) GeV, m_S = 6.30(5) GeV, m_AV = 6.62(9) GeV
- At T = 0.140 GeV (light-strange): m_PS = 0.519(5) GeV, m_V = 0.90(2) GeV, m_S = 0.67(2) GeV, m_AV = 1.14(5) GeV
- At T = 0.140 GeV (strange-strange): m_PS = 0.714(4) GeV, m_V = 1.040(5) GeV, m_S = 1.00(2) GeV, m_AV = 1.33(4) GeV
- Lattice spacings ranged from 0.017 fm to 0.234 fm

## Context
This work substantially improves over earlier screening mass calculations by Cheng (2010) and Ohno (2012) that used coarser p4 staggered fermions, by employing the HISQ action with reduced taste-splitting and performing controlled continuum extrapolations. The results confirm findings from overlap and domain wall fermion studies by Buchoff (2013), Bazavov (2012), Suzuki (2018), and others that U_A(1) effective restoration occurs at about 1.2-1.3 T_pc, while chiral SU(2) restoration occurs at T_pc. The high-temperature results show screening masses overshooting free theory expectations in qualitative agreement with O(g^2_E) weak-coupling calculations, but with J=0 and J=1 channels remaining non-degenerate up to 2.5 GeV, consistent with observations by Rohrhofer (2019) and indicating the need for calculations beyond O(g^2_E) in the EQCD framework.
