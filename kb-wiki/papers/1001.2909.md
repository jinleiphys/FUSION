# The status of the Excited Baryon Analysis Center

> The Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC), which is associated with the Theory Group at Jefferson Laboratory, was initiated in 2006. Its main goal is to extract and interpret properties of nucleon resonances (N*) from the world data of meson production reactions induced by pions, photons and electrons. We review the main accomplishments of the center since then and sketch its near future perspectives.

## Key claim

The Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) has demonstrated that a dynamical coupled-channels model can serve as a common framework to correlate hadronic and electromagnetic meson production reactions, but the model's predictions for double pion photoproduction were not satisfactory in the resonance region without further parameter adjustments, and the analysis of $\pi N \to \pi \pi N$ reactions could not be used to constrain the model due to a lack of sufficient experimental data.

## Method

The EBAC group constructed a dynamical coupled-channels model (JLMS) incorporating $\gamma N$, $\pi N$, $\pi \pi N$, and $\eta N$ channels with a multi-resonance framework, deriving an effective Hamiltonian from Lagrangians via a unitary transformation method. The model was built by performing extensive $\chi^2$ minimizations to experimental data from the GWU-SAID database and to partial wave amplitudes, then analytically continued to the complex energy plane to extract resonance pole positions. For electromagnetic reactions, the only free parameters in the $\chi^2$-fit to photoproduction data were the bare $\gamma N \rightarrow N^*$ helicity amplitudes, while the hadronic parameters were fixed from the $\pi N$ scattering analysis.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic model (JLMS) was fit to $\pi N \to \pi N$ and $\pi N \to \eta N$ data in the energy range 1 GeV $< W_{\rm c.m.} < 2$ GeV.
- For the $\pi N \to \eta N$ analysis, 294 measured differential cross-sections from five collaborations were fitted, yielding a reduced $\chi^2$ = 1.94 for the best model (model B).
- The $P_{11}$ partial wave contains two poles near the PDG Roper resonance values (1350-1380, 80-110) MeV, specifically at (1357, 76) MeV and (1364, 105) MeV, plus a higher mass pole at (1820, 248) MeV.
- The $S_{11}$ partial wave has poles at (1540, 191) MeV and (1642, 41) MeV; the $D_{13}$ at (1521, 58) MeV; the $D_{15}$ at (1654, 77) MeV; the $F_{15}$ at (1674, 53) MeV; the $S_{31}$ at (1563, 95) MeV; the $P_{33}$ at (1211, 50) MeV; the $D_{33}$ at (1604, 106) MeV; the $F_{35}$ at (1738, 110) MeV and (1928, 165) MeV; and the $F_{37}$ at (1858, 100) MeV.
- No resonance poles were found for $P_{13}$, $P_{31}$, or $P_{33}(1600)$ in the considered complex energy region (Re$(E)\leq 2000$ MeV and $-$Im$(E)\leq 250$ MeV).
- Coupled-channels effects in the $\Delta$(1232) resonance region for photoproduction were found to be about 30-40%.
- The electroproduction analysis considered data up to $W=1.6$ GeV and $Q^2=1.45$ (GeV/c)$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels approach previously developed by the EBAC group (Matsuyama, Sato, Lee 2007) and applies it to both hadronic and electromagnetic meson production reactions. The pole positions extracted from the JLMS model agree within errors with those reported by the Particle Data Group (PDG) for most resonances, but the model does not find poles corresponding to several $N^*$ states rated with three or fewer stars by the PDG, consistent with recent findings from the GWU-SAID group (Arndt et al. 2006). The analysis of the $P_{11}$ partial wave reveals two poles near the Roper resonance, consistent with results from Cutkosky and Wang (1990), GWU/VPI (Arndt et al. 2006), and the Julich group, and finds that these two poles plus a higher mass pole originate from a single bare state. The framework enables future extension to describe electromagnetic reactions up to $W=2$ GeV and $Q^2\sim 6$ GeV$^2$, and incorporation of $KY$ channels.
