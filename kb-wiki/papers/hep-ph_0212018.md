# Baryon inhomogeneity generation via cosmic strings at QCD scale and its effects on nucleosynthesis

> We have earlier shown that cosmic strings moving through the plasma at the time of a first order quark-hadron transition in the early universe can generate large scale baryon inhomogeneities. In this paper, we calculate detailed structure of these inhomogeneities at the quark-hadron transition. Our calculations show that the inhomogeneities generated by cosmic string wakes can strongly affect nucleosynthesis calculations. A comparison with observational data suggests that such baryon inhomogeneities should not have existed at the nucleosynthesis epoch. If this disagreement holds with more accurate observations, then it will lead to the conclusions that cosmic string formation scales above $10^{14} - 10^{15}$ GeV may not be consistent with nucleosynthesis and CMBR observations. Alternatively, some other input in our calculation should be constrained, for example, if the average string velocity remains sufficiently small so that significant density perturbations are never produced at the QCD scale, or if strings move ultra-relativistically so that string wakes are very thin, trapping negligible amount of baryons. Finally, if quark-hadron transition is not of first order then our calculations do not apply.

## Key claim

The paper argues that baryon inhomogeneities generated by cosmic string wakes during a first-order quark-hadron transition would have been large enough to affect nucleosynthesis calculations, and that comparison with observational data suggests such inhomogeneities should not have existed at the nucleosynthesis epoch. This conclusion is conditional: it applies only if the quark-hadron transition is first order, and if string velocities are neither too small (so that significant density perturbations are produced) nor ultra-relativistic (so that wakes trap negligible baryons).

## Method

The authors model the density fluctuations produced by a straight cosmic string moving through a relativistic fluid at the QCD scale, using the weak-shock formalism of previous work to obtain the wake half-angle and overdensity. They then simulate the dynamics of a first-order quark-hadron transition in the presence of such a wake, treating the wake as a planar overdense region where hadronic bubble nucleation is suppressed relative to the outside. Using transport rate equations adapted from Fuller et al. (1995) for baryon transfer across planar interfaces, they solve coupled evolution equations for baryon densities in the quark-gluon plasma and hadron phases as the interfaces collapse, obtaining the final baryon inhomogeneity profile.

## Key numbers

- Wake half-angle: \(\theta_w \simeq 20^\circ\) for a sample string velocity of 0.9
- Density fluctuation in the wake: \(\delta \rho/\rho \simeq 3 \times 10^{-5}\)
- Temperature fluctuation in the wake: \(\Delta T_{wake} \equiv \delta T/T \simeq 10^{-5}\)
- Supercooling before nucleation: \(\Delta T_{sc} \equiv 1 - T_{sc}/T_c \simeq 10^{-4}\) (with \(T_c = 150\) MeV)
- Nucleation temperature range: \(\Delta T_n \simeq 10^{-6}\), for time duration \(\Delta t_n \simeq 10^{-5} t_H\)
- Horizon size at transition: \(r_H \simeq 10\) km; universe age \(t_H \simeq 30\ \mu\)s
- Typical wake thickness: \(d_{shk} \simeq 3\) km
- Time lag for nucleation in wake: \(\Delta t_{lag} \simeq 10^{-5} t_H\) (with \(v_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\))
- Number of long strings per horizon: about 15
- Degrees of freedom: \(g_q \simeq 51\) (QGP), \(g_h \simeq 17\) (hadron phase)
- Surface tension: \(\sigma \simeq 0.015 T_c^3\); latent heat: \(L \simeq 3 T_c^4\)
- Baryon recombination rate prefactor: \(\Lambda \approx 3.3 \times 10^{42}\ [T/(100\ \mathrm{MeV})]^{12} \Sigma_q\ (\mathrm{cm}^{-2}\mathrm{s}^{-1})\)
- Filter factor: \(F = (2.3 \times 10^{-6})\ [T/(100\ \mathrm{MeV})]^{9} \Sigma_q v_z^{-1}\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Layek et al., 2002) that cosmic string wakes can generate large-scale baryon inhomogeneities during a first-order quark-hadron transition, by calculating the detailed structure of those inhomogeneities. It builds on prior studies of baryon fluctuation generation during the QCD phase transition (Fuller et al., 1995; Ignatius and Schwarz, 2001) and on the theory of cosmic string wakes in relativistic fluids (Silk and Vilenkin, 1984; Sornborger, 1997). The results enable constraints on cosmic string formation scales above \(10^{14} - 10^{15}\) GeV from nucleosynthesis and CMBR observations, provided the quark-hadron transition is first order.
