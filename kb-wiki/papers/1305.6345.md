# Determination of the magnetic dipole moment of the rho meson using 4 pion electroproduction data

> We determine the magnetic dipole moment of the rho meson using preliminary data from the BaBar Collaboration for the $e^+ e^- \to π^+ π^- 2 π^0$ process, in the center of mass energy range from 0.9 to 2.2 GeV. We describe the $γ^* \to 4π$ vertex using a vector meson dominance model, including the intermediate resonance contributions relevant at these energies. We find that $μ_ρ= 2.1 \pm 0.5$ in $e/2 m_ρ$ units.

## Key claim

The magnetic dipole moment of the rho meson is determined to be 2.1 ± 0.5 in e/2m_rho units, based on a fit to preliminary BaBar data for e+ e- → π+ π- 2π0 in the center-of-mass energy range 0.9 to 2.2 GeV, using a vector meson dominance model where the channel containing the electromagnetic vector meson vertex becomes relevant only between 1.5 and 2.2 GeV.

## Method

The authors model the γ* → 4π vertex using a vector meson dominance approach that includes intermediate π, ω, a1, σ, f(980), ρ, and ρ'(1450) meson exchanges. The magnetic dipole moment is extracted by fitting the total cross section of e+ e- → π+ π- 2π0 from BaBar preliminary data, with all other coupling constants fixed from independent observables such as ρ → ππ and ρ → e+ e- decays, ω → 3π decay, and a1 → ρπ decay.

## Key numbers

- μ_ρ = 2.1 ± 0.5 in e/2 m_ρ units, determined from fitting BaBar data in the energy range 0.9 to 2.2 GeV
- g_{ωρπ} = 14.7 ± 0.1 GeV^{-1} from analysis of vector meson radiative decays and ω → 3π decay width
- g_{ωρ'π} = 10.8 ± 0.6 GeV^{-1} from fitting SND and BaBar data for the ω channel (E < 2 GeV)
- g_{ρππ} = 5.96 ± 0.02 from ρ → ππ decay
- g_ρ = 4.96 ± 0.02 from ρ → e+ e- decay
- BR(ρ^0 → π+ π- 2π0) = 1.7 ± 0.6 × 10^{-5}, consistent with the experimental value of 1.6 ± 0.8 × 10^{-5}
- g_{a1ρπ} = 3.25 ± 0.3 GeV^{-1} from a1 → ρπ decay
- g_{ρσγ} = 0.63 ± 0.15 GeV^{-1} from ρ → σγ decay
- g_{σππ} = 3.7 ± 1.6 GeV from σ → ππ decay

## Context

This work provides the first determination of the rho meson magnetic dipole moment from experimental data, addressing a long-standing problem where the extremely short lifetime of vector mesons (≈10^{-23} s) prevents standard spin precession techniques. Previous theoretical predictions from effective QCD approaches placed the MDM in the range 1.9 to 3 in e/2M_ρ units, and earlier studies of radiative decays ρ → ππγ and τ → νρ γ (García-López Castro and Toledo Sánchez, 2010) had shown that kinematical regions exist where the MDM effect becomes leading, but no experimental data were available. The analysis enables future refinements with definitive data from BaBar and SND (SND preliminary data appear consistent) and improved knowledge of ρ' meson properties.
