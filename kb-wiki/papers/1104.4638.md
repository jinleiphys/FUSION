# Quadrupole deformation $(β,γ)$ of light $Λ$ hypernuclei in constrained relativistic mean field model: shape evolution and shape polarization effect of $Λ$ hyperon

> The shapes of light normal nuclei and $Λ$ hypernuclei are investigated in the $(β, γ)$ deformation plane by using a newly developed constrained relativistic mean field (RMF) model. As examples, the results of some C, Mg, and Si nuclei are presented and discussed in details. We found that for normal nuclei the present RMF calculations and previous Skyrme-Hartree-Fock models predict similar trends of the shape evolution with the neutron number increasing. But some quantitative aspects from these two approaches, such as the depth of the minimum and the softness in the $γ$ direction, differ a lot for several nuclei. For $Λ$ hypernuclei, in most cases, the addition of a $Λ$ hyperon alters slightly the location of the ground state minimum towards the direction of smaller $β$ and softer $γ$ in the potential energy surface $E \sim (β, γ)$. There are three exceptions, namely, $^{13}_Λ$C, $^{23}_Λ$C, and $^{31}_Λ$Si in which the polarization effect of the additional $Λ$ is so strong that the shapes of these three hypernuclei are drastically different from their corresponding core nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper claims that for most light Λ hypernuclei, adding a Λ hyperon only slightly shifts the ground-state minimum toward smaller β and softer γ in the potential energy surface, but in three specific cases (¹³ΛC, ²³ΛC, and ³¹ΛSi) the shape polarization effect is so strong that the hypernucleus becomes drastically different from its core nucleus, a condition the abstract does not quantify.

## Method
The authors performed constrained relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations in the (β, γ) deformation plane, using an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis modified to allow triaxial deformation and to include a Λ hyperon. The RMF equations were solved self-consistently with a BCS pairing scheme using constant gaps, and two parameter sets (PK1-Y1 and NLSH-A) were employed for the nucleon-meson and Λ-meson couplings. Potential energy surfaces were mapped for even-even C, Mg, and Si isotopes and their corresponding single-Λ hypernuclei.

## Key numbers
- For ¹⁰C, the prolate-oblate energy difference E_po is 0.74 MeV with PK1-Y1 and 0.53 MeV with NLSH-A, indicating a stiffer γ-softness compared to Skyrme-Hartree-Fock results (less than 40 keV).
- For ¹²C, the deformation energy E_def (spherical to ground state) is 0.26 MeV with PK1-Y1 and 0.11 MeV with NLSH-A, while Skyrme-Hartree-Fock gives about 1 MeV.
- The single-Λ separation energy B_Λ for ¹¹ΛC is 9.250 MeV, for ¹³ΛC is 11.990 MeV, for ¹⁵ΛC is 12.209 MeV, for ¹⁷ΛC is 13.024 MeV, for ¹⁹ΛC is 13.560 MeV, for ²¹ΛC is 13.990 MeV, and for ²³ΛC is 14.605 MeV (all from PK1-Y1).
- For ¹⁸C, the oblate saddle point is lower than the prolate one by 0.26 MeV with PK1-Y1, whereas Skyrme-Hartree-Fock predicts the prolate one lower.
- The truncation error in binding energy for ²⁶Si at (β=0.4, γ=30°) is less than 30 keV for N_F ≥ 14 fermion shells.

## Context
This work extends earlier axially deformed RMF studies of hypernuclei (Win 2008) and triaxially deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock studies (Win 2011) by incorporating triaxial deformation into the RMF framework for Λ hypernuclei. It contradicts the earlier Skyrme-Hartree-Fock finding (Zhou 2007) that core and hypernuclei have similar deformations with the same sign, by identifying three exceptions where the Λ hyperon drastically changes the shape. The results enable future beyond-mean-field studies (e.g., generator coordinate method) for nuclei with extremely soft potential energy surfaces, such as ¹⁶C and ¹⁸C.
