# Thermal effects on stellar neutron capture reactions: a quantum dynamical approach

> The neutron capture process plays a vital role in creating the heavy elements in the universe. Astrophysical environments involved in these processes are characterized by two distinct reaction mechanisms: the slow and rapid neutron capture processes. In this work, the slow neutron capture process is described with the time-dependent coupled channels wave-packet (TDCCWP) method that uses both a many-body nuclear potential and an initial temperature-dependent state to account for the thermal environment. To evaluate the role of a mixed and entangled initial state in the temperature-dependent neutron capture cross section, TDCCWP calculations are compared with those from the coupled-channels density matrix (CCDM) method based on the Lindblad equation. The importance of including temperature in the initial wave-function of the TDCCWP approach is compared to a thermalisation of the reaction rate using a Hauser-Feshbach style approach. TDCCWP calculations indicate a decrease of the n+$^{188}$Os capture cross section with increasing temperature, along with a decrease in reaction rates for the highest thermal energies studied, which are contrary to Hauser-Feshbach calculations and important in the rapid neutron capture process. The physical reason for this discrepancy is the key role of the dynamical nuclear coupling between the thermally populated states of the target nucleus, which is neglected in the Hauser-Feshbach approach, but creates a dominant neutron capture pathway with increased neutron speed and thus reduces the neutron capture cross section.

## Key claim
The paper claims that including temperature in the initial wave-function of the time-dependent coupled channels wave-packet (TDCCWP) method leads to a decrease in the n+$^{188}$Os neutron capture cross section with increasing temperature, contrary to Hauser-Feshbach calculations. This discrepancy arises because dynamical nuclear coupling between thermally populated states of the target nucleus, neglected in Hauser-Feshbach approaches, creates a dominant neutron capture pathway with increased neutron speed that reduces the capture cross section.

## Method
The authors used the TDCCWP method with a many-body nuclear potential derived from Hartree-Fock calculations using the Sky3D code, fitted to a Woods-Saxon parameterization. The initial wave-packet was thermalized using Boltzmann factors to populate the ground and first excited states of $^{188}$Os, and the system was propagated using a modified Chebyshev scheme with a phenomenological absorption potential. Transmission coefficients were extracted from the reflected wave-packet and used to calculate capture cross sections and reaction rates, which were compared to results from the coupled-channels density matrix (CCDM) method based on the Lindblad equation and to a Hauser-Feshbach style calculation.

## Key numbers
- The first excited state of $^{188}$Os is at 155 keV, defining the threshold between kinematically closed and open channels.
- The Woods-Saxon potential fitted to the Hartree-Fock potential has parameters: $V_0 = -42.9311$ MeV, $a_0 = 0.6129$ fm, $R_0 = 7.3033$ fm.
- The wave-packet width of 50 fm gives an energy resolution error of 0.004 keV; a larger width of 80 fm yields a percentage difference of less than 1% in transmission coefficients.
- The quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_2 = 0.179$ and hexadecapole deformation $\beta_4 = -0.059$ were used for $^{188}$Os.
- The absorption potential parameters are: strength $V_{W0} = -7$ MeV, diffuseness $a_{W0} = 0.75$ fm, and radius $R_{W0} = 5$ fm.
- The percentage difference in $l=0$ transmission coefficients between Koning-Delaroche and Hartree-Fock nuclear potentials is at most 0.02% at the highest incident energies.
- The percentage difference in $l=0$ transmission coefficients between different deformation parameter sets is at most approximately 0.2%.
- The reaction rate calculation used an incident energy range from $E_i = 10$ keV to $E_f = 500$ keV.

## Context
This work extends the TDCCWP method, previously applied to low-energy heavy-ion fusion by Diaz-Torres and collaborators (2018, 2021, 2022, 2024), to neutron capture reactions for the first time. It contradicts the standard Hauser-Feshbach approach used in codes such as TALYS by Koning (2005) and NON-SMOKER by Rauscher (2000), which neglect dynamical coupling between thermally populated states. The results are relevant for improving the Re-Os cosmic chronometer, which relies on accurate neutron capture cross sections on osmium isotopes to estimate the age of the universe, as discussed by Clayton (1964) and Segawa (2007).
