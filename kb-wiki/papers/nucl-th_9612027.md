# Relativistic Description of Exclusive Deuteron Breakup Reactions

> The exclusive deuteron break-up reaction is analyzed within a covariant approach based on the Bethe-Salpeter equation with realistic meson-exchange interaction. Relativistic effects in the cross section, tensor analyzing power and polarization transfer are investigated in explicit form. Results of numerical calculations are presented for kinematical conditions in forthcoming p + D reactions at COSY.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for the exclusive deuteron break-up reaction p + D → p₁(0°) + p₂(180°) + n₃(0°) under the kinematical conditions of the planned COSY experiments, relativistic corrections to the cross section, tensor analyzing power, and polarization transfer are negligible, being much smaller than the expected experimental errors. This conclusion is hedged by the condition that the momentum of the backward-emitted proton |p₂| is at most about 0.51 GeV/c at the planned beam energy of E_p ∼ 3 GeV, and that relativistic corrections only become significant at |p₂| > 0.6 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors performed a covariant analysis of the exclusive deuteron break-up reaction within the Bethe-Salpeter (BS) formalism, using a realistic meson-exchange interaction kernel (π, ω, ρ, σ, η, δ exchange). They derived fully covariant expressions for the unpolarized cross section, tensor analyzing power T₂₀, and polarization transfer κ, separating the contributions from positive-energy S and D waves from the purely relativistic corrections arising from negative-energy P waves. Numerical calculations were carried out using previously obtained numerical solutions of the BS equation, and the results were presented for the kinematical conditions of the planned p + D experiments at COSY.

## Key numbers

- The maximum momentum of the backward-emitted proton in the planned COSY experiments is |p₂|_max ≈ 0.51 GeV/c at a beam energy E_p ∼ 3 GeV.
- Relativistic corrections become significant only at |p₂| > 0.6 GeV/c, which is above the maximum momentum accessible in the planned experiments.
- The maximum value of |p₂| within the spectator mechanism is restricted to |p₂|_max ∼ 0.8 GeV/c.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant used in the one-iteration approximation for the P-wave vertices is g_π² ≈ 14.5.

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic investigations of one-nucleon exchange diagrams for inclusive and elastic D + p scattering, which were based on numerical solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation (Keister, Gross). It also builds on non-relativistic calculations of the spectator mechanism for deuteron break-up (Lykasov). The paper shows that the planned exclusive experiments at COSY, which can kinematically exclude Δ excitation processes, will be able to investigate the role of meson-exchange currents and non-nucleonic degrees of freedom without contamination from relativistic effects, which are found to be negligible under the specific kinematical conditions.
