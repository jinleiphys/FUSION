# Isospin asymmetry in holographic baryonic matter

> We study baryonic matter with isospin asymmetry, including fully dynamically its interplay with pion condensation. To this end, we employ the holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model and the so-called homogeneous ansatz for the gauge fields in the bulk to describe baryonic matter. Within the confined geometry and restricting ourselves to the chiral limit, we map out the phase structure in the presence of baryon and isospin chemical potentials, showing that for sufficiently large chemical potentials condensed pions and isospin-asymmetric baryonic matter coexist. We also present first results of the same approach in the deconfined geometry and demonstrate that this case, albeit technically more involved, is better suited for comparisons with and predictions for real-world QCD. Our study lays the ground for future improved holographic studies aiming towards a realistic description of charge neutral, beta-equilibrated matter in compact stars, and also for more refined comparisons with lattice studies at nonzero isospin chemical potential.

## Key claim

The authors show that within the holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model in the chiral limit, baryonic matter and pion condensation coexist for sufficiently large chemical potentials, and that baryons appear even at infinitesimally small baryon chemical potential if the isospin chemical potential is large enough, a result that contrasts with the conjecture that baryonic degrees of freedom do not appear at ultra-high isospin density.

## Method

The authors employ the holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model with two flavors in the probe brane limit, using a homogeneous ansatz for the non-abelian gauge fields in the bulk to describe baryonic matter. They work within the Yang-Mills approximation of the Dirac-Born-Infeld action, restrict to the chiral limit (zero current quark masses), and map out the phase structure in the confined geometry with antipodal brane separation, then present first results in the deconfined geometry.

## Key numbers

* The dimensionless location of the tip of the cigar-shaped subspace is \(u_{\rm KK} = 4/9\).
* The critical temperature separating confined and deconfined geometries is \(T_c = M_{\rm KK}/(2\pi)\).
* For a single baryon, the symmetry energy is \(S/M_{\rm KK} = (\sqrt{15}/8\sqrt{2}) u_{\rm KK}^{1/2} N_c \simeq 0.2282 N_c\).
* The baryon density is related to the IR boundary condition \(h_c\) by \(n_B = -(3/4)\lambda_0 h_c^3\).

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of isospin-symmetric baryonic matter (Li 2015, Preis 2016, Bitaghsir Fadafan 2018, Kovensky 2020) and purely mesonic studies at nonzero isospin chemical potential (Aharony 2007, Rebhan 2008) by including both baryons and pion condensation dynamically. The results lay the ground for future holographic studies aiming toward a realistic description of charge-neutral, beta-equilibrated matter in compact stars and for comparisons with lattice studies at nonzero isospin chemical potential (Kogut 2002, Brandt 2017).
