# Particle-number fluctuations near the critical point of nuclear matter

> The equation of state with quantum statistics corrections is used for particle number fluctuations $ω$ of isotopically symmetric nuclear matter with interparticle van der Waals and Skyrme local density interactions. The fluctuations, $ω\propto 1/\mathcal{K}$, are analytically derived through the isothermal incompressibility $\mathcal{K}$ at first order over a small quantum-statistics parameter. Our approximate analytical results appear to be in good agreement with the results of accurate numerical calculations. These results are also close to those obtained by using more accurate Tolman and Rowlinson expansions of the incompressibility $\mathcal{K}$ near the critical point. A more general formula for fluctuations $ω$, improved at the critical point, was obtained for a finite particle-number average $\langle N \rangle$ by neglecting, for simplicity, small quantum statistics effects. It is shown that for a large dimensionless parameter, $α\propto \mathcal{K}^2\langle N \rangle/\mathcal{K}^{\prime\prime} $, where $\mathcal{K}^{\prime\prime}$ is the second derivative of the incompressibility $\mathcal{K}$ as function of the average particle density $n$, far from the critical point ($α\gg 1$), one finds the traditional asymptote, $ω\propto 1/\mathcal{K}$, for the fluctuations $ω$. For a small parameter, $α\ll 1$, near the critical point, where $\mathcal{K}=0$ and $α=0$, one obtains another asymptote of $ω$. These fluctuations, having a maximum near the critical point as function of the average density $n$, for finite values of $\langle N \rangle$ are finite and relatively small, in contrast to the results of the traditional calculations.

## Key claim

The traditional expression for particle-number fluctuations, ω ∝ 1/𝒦, diverges at the critical point where the isothermal incompressibility 𝒦 vanishes; the authors show that by including fourth-order terms in the free-energy expansion and accounting for a finite average particle number ⟨N⟩, the fluctuations remain finite and relatively small near the critical point, in contrast to the divergent traditional result.

## Method

The authors start from the Smoluchowski–Einstein fluctuation theory, expanding the free energy F(ρ) to fourth order in the density difference (ρ−n) around the average density n. They derive an improved analytical formula for the particle-number fluctuation ω that depends on a dimensionless parameter α ∝ 𝒦²⟨N⟩/𝒦′′, where 𝒦′′ is the second derivative of the incompressibility with respect to density. They apply this formalism to symmetric nuclear matter using the quantum van der Waals (QvdW) and quantum Skyrme local density (QSLD) equations of state, including first-order quantum-statistics corrections, and compare their analytical results with numerical calculations and with the earlier Tolman (1938) and Rowlinson (1958) expansions.

## Key numbers

* For the QvdW model, the first-order analytical fluctuation is ω₁ = [(1+2δ)/(1−nb)² − 2na/T]⁻¹, where δ = ε/(1−nb) and ε = ħ³π³/² n / [2g (mT)³/²].
* The critical point conditions are (∂P/∂n)_T = 0 and (∂²P/∂n²)_T = 0 at the critical density n_c and critical temperature T_c.
* The parameter α is defined as α ∝ 𝒦²⟨N⟩/𝒦′′; far from the critical point (α ≫ 1) one recovers ω ∝ 1/𝒦, while near the critical point (α ≪ 1, where 𝒦=0) a different, finite asymptote for ω is obtained.
* The temperature range considered is T ≲ 30 MeV, with the restriction that T is sufficiently large for the quantum-statistics parameter to be small.

## Context

This work extends the analytical treatment of quantum-statistics corrections to the nuclear-matter equation of state developed by Magner et al. (2019, 2022) for the QvdW and QSLD models, now applied to particle-number fluctuations. It addresses the divergence problem in the traditional Landau fluctuation theory (Landau and Lifshitz, 1980) near the critical point, which was previously noted by Tolman (1938), Rowlinson (1958), and others. The improved fluctuation formula enables finite predictions for fluctuations in finite nuclear systems, with planned applications to statistically averaged level-density calculations by Magner et al. (2021, 2022).
