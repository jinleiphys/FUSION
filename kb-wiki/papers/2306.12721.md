# Exclusive $J/Ψ$ plus jet associated production in ultraperipheral $PbPb$ collisions

> The study of exclusive processes in ultraperipheral collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) has allowed us to test several aspects of the Standard Model and to search for New Physics. In this letter, we investigate the possibility of using these processes to improve our understanding of the quarkonium production mechanism through the study of the exclusive $J/Ψ$ plus jet associate production in ultraperipheral $PbPb$ collisions. We estimate the transverse - momentum and rapidity distributions considering that the $γγ\rightarrow J/Ψ+ X$ ($X = γ, \, g$) subprocess is described by the Non - Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) formalism and present predictions for the rapidity ranges covered by central and forward detectors. The experimental separation of these events is discussed and the results indicate that a future experimental analysis is, in principle, feasible in future runs of the LHC and the Future Circular Collider (FCC).

# Exclusive J/ψ plus jet associated production in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions

## Key claim

The exclusive J/ψ plus jet (photon or gluon) production in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions, described by the Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) formalism, could be experimentally observable at the LHC and FCC, provided that a minimum transverse momentum cut (e.g., p_t^min = 2.0 GeV) is imposed and the associated system X is measured in central or forward detectors to suppress the dominant γ-Pomeron background.

## Method

The authors compute differential cross sections for the process PbPb → Pb ⊗ J/ψ X ⊗ Pb (X = γ, g) using the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) with a realistic Wood-Saxon form factor for the nuclear charge distribution and a Glauber absorptive factor to enforce ultraperipheral collisions (impact parameter ≥ 2 R_Pb). The γγ → c\bar{c}(n) a subprocess is treated within the NRQCD formalism, using long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) from Butenschoen (2011): ⟨O_1^{J/ψ}(³S_1)⟩ = 1.32 GeV³ and ⟨O_8^{J/ψ}(³S_1)⟩ = 0.0022 GeV³. Predictions are presented for LHC (√s_NN = 5.5 TeV) and FCC (√s_NN = 39.0 TeV) energies, with the J/ψ in central (|y| < 2.5) or forward (2.0 < y < 4.5) rapidity ranges and the associated system X either integrated over full rapidity or fixed at specific values (y_1 = 0 or y_1 = 3).

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for exclusive J/ψ + X production at LHC (√s_NN = 5.5 TeV) with full rapidity coverage: 77.87 nb
- Total cross section at FCC (√s_NN = 39.0 TeV) with full rapidity coverage: 384.77 nb
- Cross section at LHC with central J/ψ (|y| < 2.5) and X at y_1 = 0: 17.97 nb
- Cross section at LHC with forward J/ψ (2.0 < y < 4.5) and X at y_1 = 3: 2.07 nb
- Cross section at FCC with central J/ψ and X at y_1 = 0: 57.33 nb
- Cross section at FCC with forward J/ψ and X at y_1 = 3: 23.85 nb
- The J/ψ + γ contribution dominates over J/ψ + g by a factor of approximately 8 at y = 0 for p_t^min = 2.0 GeV
- The rapidity distribution at y = 0 decreases by one order of magnitude when p_t^min is increased from 0.1 GeV to 2.0 GeV
- The transverse momentum distribution peaks at p_t ≈ 1.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the NRQCD-based analysis of J/ψ + X production in two-photon collisions, previously studied for e⁺e⁻ colliders by Klasen (2001, 2004), to ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions at the LHC and FCC. It directly addresses the challenge of establishing the NRQCD formalism for quarkonium production, which remains an open problem in particle physics as reviewed by Lansberg (2019). The process offers an alternative to future e⁺e⁻ colliders by exploiting the Z⁴ enhancement of the photon flux in PbPb collisions. The results motivate detailed experimental simulations with realistic LHC detector cuts, and the approach could be extended to other quarkonium states or to constrain the color-octet LDMEs through global fits.
