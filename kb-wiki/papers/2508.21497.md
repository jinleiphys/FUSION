# A Benchmark Study of the Relativistic Plane Wave Impulse Approximation for Polarized ($\vec{p},2\vec{p}\,$) Reactions on the 3s$_{1/2}$ state in $^{208}$Pb at 392 MeV

> The Relativistic Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (RPWIA) is benchmarked against the established Relativistic Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (RDWIA) for exclusive proton-induced proton knockout reactions ($\vec{p},2\vec{p}\,$) from the 3s$_{1/2}$ state in $^{208}$Pb at 392 MeV. While the RDWIA provides the proper description of the reaction, the RPWIA-which neglects nuclear distortion and absorption effects-fails dramatically for absolute cross-sections, requiring a scaling factor of $\frac{1}{26}$ to match the data. However, within $\pm10$ MeV of the recoilless condition, the RPWIA quantitatively describes the analyzing power ($A_y$) data and yields similar results as the RDWIA predictions for all polarization transfer observables (D$_{i'j}$). These polarization transfer observables are insensitive to the choice of the Relativistic Mean Field model used. The study establishes clear boundaries for the RPWIA's utility: computationally simple predictions for polarization observables near zero recoil, but essential reliance on the RDWIA for spectroscopic factor extraction.

# A Benchmark Study of the Relativistic Plane Wave Impulse Approximation for Polarized ($\vec{p},2\vec{p}\,$) Reactions on the 3s$_{1/2}$ state in $^{208}$Pb at 392 MeV

## Key claim

The Relativistic Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (RPWIA), which neglects nuclear distortion and absorption effects, fails dramatically for absolute cross-sections in ($\vec{p},2\vec{p}\,$) reactions on $^{208}$Pb at 392 MeV, requiring an unphysical scaling factor of 1/26 to match experimental data; however, within ±10 MeV of the recoilless condition, the RPWIA quantitatively describes the analyzing power ($A_y$) data and yields results similar to the full Relativistic Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (RDWIA) predictions for all polarization transfer observables (D$_{i'j}$).

## Method

The authors benchmarked the RPWIA against the established RDWIA for exclusive proton-induced proton knockout from the 3s$_{1/2}$ state in $^{208}$Pb at an incident laboratory kinetic energy of 392 MeV, with fixed coplanar scattering angles of 32.5° and -50.0°. In the RPWIA framework, all proton wave functions were treated as relativistic Dirac plane waves, while the RDWIA included nuclear distortion and absorption effects through optical potentials. Bound state wave functions were obtained from four different Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) models (QHDII, NL3, FSUGold, and FSUGold2), and the IA1 parameterization of the nucleon-nucleon interaction was used consistently in both frameworks.

## Key numbers

- The RPWIA cross-section required a scaling factor of 1/26 to match experimental data, compared to the RDWIA spectroscopic factor of S = 1/1.7 (approximately 0.59) for the same data.
- The RDWIA-extracted spectroscopic factor for the 3s$_{1/2}$ state was S = 0.61(14).
- At the recoilless energy (T$_{a'}$ = 253 MeV), scaled RPWIA cross-sections ranged from 185.9 $\mu$b (FSUGold) to 240.6 $\mu$b (QHDII), with FSUGold2 at 192.4 $\mu$b and NL3 at 186.8 $\mu$b, giving a spread of approximately 29% between highest and lowest values.
- The experimental peak cross-section was 188.0 ± 1.3 $\mu$b.
- The RPWIA quantitatively described analyzing power and polarization transfer observables within ±10 MeV of the recoilless condition.
- NRDWIA analyses from prior work yielded spectroscopic factors of approximately 1.6–1.8 for the same reaction on $^{208}$Pb.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Van der Ventel and Hillhouse (2004) who compared RPWIA calculations of ($\vec{p},2\vec{p}\,$) reactions on $^{208}$Pb at 392 MeV using different NN interaction parameterizations. The study directly benchmarks against the RDWIA framework and experimental data from Noro and collaborators (2020), which had previously shown that NRDWIA overestimates spectroscopic factors for heavy nuclei like $^{208}$Pb compared to ($e,e'p$) measurements. The results establish clear boundaries for RPWIA utility: computationally simple predictions for polarization observables near zero recoil, which may be valuable for exploratory studies with radioactive beams where optical potentials are unknown, while confirming that RDWIA or NRDWIA remain essential for spectroscopic factor extraction and full kinematic analyses.
