# Searching for single-particle resonances with the Green's function method

> Single-particle resonances in the continuum are crucial for studies of exotic nuclei. In this study, the Green's function approach is employed to search for single-particle resonances based on the relativistic-mean-field model. Taking $^{120}$Sn as an example, we identify single-particle resonances and determine the energies and widths directly by probing the extrema of the Green's functions. In contrast to the results found by exploring for the extremum of the density of states proposed in our recent study [Chin. Phys. C, 44:084105 (2020)], which has proven to be very successful, the same resonances as well as very close energies and widths are obtained. By comparing the Green's functions plotted in different coordinate space sizes, we also found that the results very slightly depend on the space size. These findings demonstrate that the approach by exploring for the extremum of the Green's function is also very reliable and effective for identifying resonant states, regardless of whether they are wide or narrow.

## Key claim

The Green's function method can reliably identify single-particle resonances (both narrow and wide) by directly probing the extrema of the Green's functions themselves, yielding results nearly identical to those from the previously established density-of-states extremum method, with differences in widths typically within 0.2 keV.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation within the relativistic-mean-field (RMF) model using the PK1 density functional for the nucleus ¹²⁰Sn. They construct the radial Green's function for each partial wave κ by integrating two independent Dirac spinors from the origin and from infinity, then scan the complex energy plane (ε = ε_r + iε_i) in steps of 1×10⁻⁴ MeV to locate the poles or extrema of the Green's function, which directly give the resonance energy E and width Γ. The coordinate space size is R_box = 20 fm with step dr = 0.1 fm, and calculations are repeated with R_box = 25 and 30 fm to check space-size dependence.

## Key numbers

- For the neutron resonant state 2f₅/₂ in ¹²⁰Sn: resonance energy E = 0.8705 MeV, width Γ/2 = 0.0326 MeV (i.e., ε = 0.8705 − i0.0326 MeV).
- For the wide resonant state 2g₉/₂ in ¹²⁰Sn: ε = 5.4428 − i1.6948 MeV.
- For the narrow resonant state 1h₉/₂ in ¹²⁰Sn: width refined to Γ/2 = 4×10⁻⁸ MeV (ε = 0.2507 − i4×10⁻⁸ MeV).
- For the wide resonant state 2g₇/₂: ε = 6.3585 − i3.1053 MeV.
- For the wide resonant state 2h₁₁/₂: ε = 10.5130 − i6.7683 MeV.
- For the wide resonant state 1j₁₃/₂: ε = 18.1846 − i3.1532 MeV.
- The scanning energy step dε = 1×10⁻⁴ MeV gives accuracy up to 0.1 keV for resonance energies and widths.
- Differences between Green's function extremum results and density-of-states extremum results are within 0.2 keV for all states.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Green's function approach for single-particle resonances (Sun, 2014; Chen, 2020) by replacing the density-of-states (DOS) extremum method with a direct Green's function extremum search, which is easier, more direct, and less time-consuming. The results are consistent with earlier RMF Green's function calculations (Sun, 2014) and identify four wide resonant states (2g₇/₂, 2g₉/₂, 2h₁₁/₂, 1j₁₃/₂) that were not resolved by the earlier DOS comparison method. This technique enables future studies of pairing effects on resonant states using the relativistic continuum Hartree–Bogoliubov model, and can be applied to dynamic reactions and resonance structures in nuclear fusion.
