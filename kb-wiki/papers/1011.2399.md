# Dynamically generated $N^{*}$ and $Λ^*$ resonances in the hidden charm sector around 4.3 GeV

> The interactions of $\bar{D}Σ_{c}$-$\bar DΛ_c$, $\bar{D}^{*}Σ_{c}$-$\bar D^*Λ_c$, and related strangeness channels, are studied within the framework of the coupled channel unitary approach with the local hidden gauge formalism. A series of meson-baryon dynamically generated relatively narrow $N^*$ and $Λ^*$ resonances are predicted around 4.3 GeV in the hidden charm sector. We make estimates of production cross sections of these predicted resonances in $\bar{p} p $ collisions for PANDA at the forthcoming FAIR facility.

## Key claim
The paper predicts a series of dynamically generated, relatively narrow N* and Lambda* resonances in the hidden charm sector around 4.3 GeV, but these predictions are contingent on the use of SU(4) flavor symmetry extended from the local hidden gauge formalism, which the authors acknowledge is not fully justified in the charmed sector due to the large charm quark mass.

## Method
The interactions of anticharmed mesons (D-bar, D*-bar) with charmed baryons (Sigma_c, Lambda_c, Xi_c, Xi'_c) were studied using a coupled-channel unitary approach based on the Bethe-Salpeter equation, with transition potentials derived from SU(4)-extended local hidden gauge Lagrangians. The loop function G was regularized using both dimensional regularization (with subtraction constant alpha) and a three-momentum cutoff method. Decay widths to light meson-light baryon channels were estimated via box diagrams involving heavy vector meson exchange.

## Key numbers
- N* resonance from PB interaction: pole at 4269 MeV (alpha=-2.3) with total width 34.3 MeV; dominant decay to K Sigma (17.0 MeV)
- Lambda* resonance from PB interaction: pole at 4213 MeV (alpha=-2.3) with total width 26.4 MeV; dominant decay to anti-K N (15.8 MeV)
- Lambda* resonance from PB interaction: pole at 4403 MeV (alpha=-2.3) with total width 28.2 MeV; dominant decay to pi Sigma (10.6 MeV)
- N* resonance from VB interaction: pole at 4418 MeV (alpha=-2.3) with total width 28.4 MeV; dominant decay to K* Sigma (13.7 MeV)
- Lambda* resonance from VB interaction: pole at 4370 MeV (alpha=-2.3) with total width 23.3 MeV; dominant decay to anti-K* N (13.9 MeV)
- Lambda* resonance from VB interaction: pole at 4550 MeV (alpha=-2.3) with total width 23.7 MeV; dominant decay to omega Lambda (9.1 MeV)
- Coupling constants: N*(4269) couples to D-bar Sigma_c with g=2.85; Lambda*(4213) couples to D_s-bar Lambda_c (g=1.37) and D-bar Xi_c (g=3.25); Lambda*(4403) couples to D-bar Xi'_c (g=2.64)

## Context
This work extends the approach of Mizutani (2006) and the vector-meson studies of the Valencia group, which successfully generated resonances like the Lambda_c(2593) in the open charm sector, to the hidden charm sector where no such resonances were previously predicted. The predicted N* and Lambda* states around 4.3 GeV could be searched for at the PANDA experiment at FAIR through anti-p p collisions, where the authors estimate production cross sections that could enhance J/psi and eta_c production near the resonance energies.
