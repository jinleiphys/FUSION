# Possibility of observation of supernarrow dibaryons with symmetric wave function in $γd --> π^{\pm}+γNN$ reactions

> The possibility of obsevation of supernarrow dibaryons with symmetric wave function in reactions $γ+d to π^+ +γ+nn$ and $γ+d to π^- +γ+pp$ is studied. These dibaryons satisfy the condition $(-1)^{T+S}P=+1$ (where T is the isospin, S is the internal spin and P is the dibaryon parity) and their decay into two nucleons is suppressed by the Pauly principle. In the present paper the method of a dibaryon masses reconstruction over the dibaryon decay products (γnn) and (γpp) is analysed. The Monte-Carlo simulation is used to choose the optimal location of the detectors, to estimate the contribution of the main background processes and to calculate the expected yields of the dibaryon production. It is shown that 100 hours of the exposition time at the microtron MAMI (Mainz) is quite enough to determine whether or not the supernarrow dibaryons with symmetric wave function exist.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that supernarrow dibaryons with symmetric wave function (satisfying the condition (-1)^(T+S)P = +1) can be observed in the reactions gamma d -> pi+ + gamma nn and gamma d -> pi- + gamma pp, and that 100 hours of exposure at the MAMI microtron in Mainz would be sufficient to determine their existence, provided the probability of a six-quark state in the deuteron (eta) is at least 0.01.

## Method

The authors calculated the cross sections for photoproduction of the dibaryons D(1,1^+,1) and D(1,1^-,0) in the reactions gamma d -> pi+ D and gamma d -> pi- D using gauge-invariant amplitudes derived from four diagrams. They performed Monte-Carlo simulations to generate events for dibaryon production and the main background processes (gamma d -> pi+ pi0 nn and gamma d -> pi+ gamma nn), using these to determine optimal detector locations and estimate expected yields for a setup at the MAMI microtron with tagged photons in the energy range 500-800 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Decay widths for D(1,1^+,1): range from 0.2 eV at M=1.90 GeV to 35 eV at M=2.013 GeV
- Decay widths for D(1,1^-,0): range from 0.05 eV at M=1.90 GeV to 8.75 eV at M=2.013 GeV
- About 70% of the decay width contribution lies within 1 MeV of the maximum photon energy omega_m
- Assumed probability of six-quark state in the deuteron: eta = eta_0 = 0.01
- Incident photon intensity: 1.3 x 10^6 sec^-1 at nu_1 = 500 MeV in a 2 MeV interval
- Geometrical efficiency of the setup: 0.05% at M=1900 MeV, 0.09% at M=1950 MeV, 0.16% at M=2000 MeV
- Expected yields of dibaryon production in gamma d -> pi+ + gamma nn: 202 events/hour at M=1.9 GeV, 307 events/hour at M=1.95 GeV, 444 events/hour at M=2.00 GeV
- Expected yields of dibaryon production in gamma d -> pi- + gamma pp: approximately 150 events/hour for each dibaryon mass
- Background events in the mass range M=1870-2040 MeV: 636 events/hour
- Neutron kinetic energies in the dibaryon center-of-mass system: 0.07-1.9 MeV for M=1900-2000 MeV
- Proton energies in the lab system do not exceed 150 MeV
- Angle between two neutrons from dibaryon decay: theta_nn <= 15 degrees

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for supernarrow dibaryons with symmetric wave function developed by Fil'kov (1986, 1988) and Akhmedov and Fil'kov (1992), who predicted that such dibaryons would have suppressed decay into two nucleons due to the Pauli principle and would decay primarily via photon emission. The paper builds on mass predictions from the MIT bag model by Mulders et al. (1979) and the stretched rotating bag model by Kondratuk et al. (1987), as well as the chiral soliton model by Kopeliovich (1995). The proposed experiment at MAMI would enable a definitive test of these dibaryon states, with the additional possibility that a null result would constrain the probability of six-quark states in the deuteron to below 0.001.
