# Effective Theory for Neutron-Deuteron Scattering and the Triton

> We apply the effective field theory approach to the three-nucleon system. In particular, we consider neutron-deuteron scattering and the triton. Precise predictions for S=3/2 scattering are obtained in a straightforward way. In the S=1/2 channel, however, a unique nonperturbative renormalization takes place which requires the introduction of a three-body force at leading order. We also show that invariance under the renormalization group explains some universal features of the three-nucleon system.

# Effective Theory for Neutron-Deuteron Scattering and the Triton

## Key claim

The effective field theory (EFT) approach to the three-nucleon system yields precise predictions for neutron-deuteron scattering in the S=3/2 channel straightforwardly, but in the S=1/2 channel a nonperturbative renormalization occurs that requires a leading-order three-body force counterterm, with the caveat that one three-body datum (the S=1/2 scattering length) must be used as input to fix the new dimensionful parameter Lambda_*.

## Method

The pionless effective field theory was applied to neutron-deuteron scattering below the deuteron breakup threshold, using dibaryon fields to represent two-nucleon interactions in the ^3S_1 and ^1S_0 channels. Coupled integral equations for the S-wave scattering amplitudes were derived and solved numerically with a momentum cutoff Lambda, and a three-body force counterterm H(Lambda) was introduced to control the cutoff dependence in the S=1/2 channel. The running of the three-body force with cutoff was determined from renormalization group invariance, and the single free parameter Lambda_* was fixed by the experimental S=1/2 nd-scattering length.

## Key numbers

- S=3/2 nd-scattering length at O((r_2/a_2)^2): a_3^(3/2) = (6.33 ± 0.10) fm, compared to experimental value (6.35 ± 0.02) fm (Dilg et al.)
- Contributions to a_3^(3/2) by order: (5.09 + 0.91 + 0.33) fm from O((r_2/a_2)^0) through O((r_2/a_2)^2)
- S=1/2 nd-scattering length used as input: a_3^(1/2) = (0.65 ± 0.04) fm (Dilg et al.)
- Three-body force parameter determined: Lambda_* = 0.9 fm^{-1}
- Triton binding energy predicted at leading order: B_3 = 8.0 MeV, compared to experimental B_3^exp = 8.48 MeV
- s_0 ≈ 1.0064, the constant governing the logarithmic periodicity of the three-body force running
- Poles of H(Lambda) occur at Lambda_n = f(Lambda_* a_2) exp(n pi / s_0) a_2^{-1}, with exp(pi/s_0) ≈ 22.7

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach from the two-nucleon system (Weinberg 1990; Kaplan, Savage, and Wise 1998) to the three-nucleon system, building on earlier studies of three spinless bosons (Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck 1999). The paper shows that the Phillips line (Phillips 1968), the correlation between triton binding energy and S=1/2 nd-scattering length observed in potential model calculations, arises naturally from variations in the three-body force parameter Lambda_*. The EFT also recovers the Efimov effect (Efimov 1970) and the Thomas effect (Thomas 1935) in appropriate limits. This work enables systematic calculations of three-nucleon observables including polarization observables in nd-scattering and the triton charge form factor, and opens the possibility of applying EFT methods to systems with three or more nucleons.
