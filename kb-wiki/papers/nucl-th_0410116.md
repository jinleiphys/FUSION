# Isoscaling of the Fission Fragments with Langevin Equation

> Langevin equation is used to simulate the fission process of $^{112}$Sn + $^{112}$Sn and $^{116}$Sn + $^{116}$Sn. The mass distribution of the fission fragments are given by assuming the process of symmetric fission or asymmetric fission with the Gaussian probability sampling. Isoscaling behavior has been observed from the analysis of fission fragments of both reactions and the isoscaling parameter $α$ seems to be sensitive to the width of fission probability and the beam energy.

## Key claim

Langevin simulations of fission for two Sn+Sn systems with different isospin show isoscaling behavior, but the isoscaling parameter α is strongly sensitive to the width of the Gaussian probability distribution used for the mass asymmetry parameter and to the beam energy, meaning the observed isoscaling reflects the fission dynamics rather than being a universal thermodynamic signature.

## Method

The authors used an overdamped Langevin equation coupled with a Monte Carlo procedure for light-particle emission (Combine Dynamical and Statistical Model, CDSM) to simulate fission of compound nuclei formed in complete fusion of 112Sn + 112Sn and 116Sn + 116Sn. The mass asymmetry parameter at scission was sampled from a Gaussian distribution with variable width σα0 (0.04, 0.06, 0.08, 0.20) to produce symmetric or asymmetric fission fragments, assuming fragments have the same N/Z as the initial system. 200,000 fission events from the dynamical channel were analyzed for each condition.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy per nucleon E/A = 8.4 MeV used for the isoscaling ratio plots (Figure 1).
- Gaussian widths of the mass asymmetry parameter σα0 = 0.04, 0.06, 0.08, and 0.20 were tested to study the effect on the isoscaling parameter α.
- Isoscaling parameter α was extracted for fragments with Z = 44-52 as a function of beam energy, showing α decreases with increasing Elab.
- Fission fragments analyzed ranged from Z = 37 to Z = 59 in the isoscaling ratio plots.

## Context

This work extends the study of isoscaling from statistical models and experimental evaporation/fission data (Tsang et al., 2001; Friedman, 2003; Veselsky et al., 2004) into a dynamical Langevin framework for fission. The observed backbending of α with Z at intermediate σα0 reproduces the behavior seen in 238,233U fission data induced by 14 MeV neutrons (Veselsky et al., 2004), while the increase of α with Z at small σα0 agrees with a simple liquid-drop model prediction (Friedman, 2003). The results enable using isoscaling as a probe of fission dynamics, particularly the width of the fission probability distribution and the temperature dependence of the isospin effect.
