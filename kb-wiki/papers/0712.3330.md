# Role of Higher Multipole Excitations in the Electromagnetic Dissociation of One Neutron Halo Nuclei

> We investigate the role of higher multipole excitations in the electromagnetic dissociation of one-neutron halo nuclei within two different theoretical models -- a finite range distorted wave Born approximation and another in a more analytical method with a finite range potential. We also show, within a simple picture, how the presence of a weakly bound state affects the breakup cross section.

# Role of Higher Multipole Excitations in the Electromagnetic Dissociation of One Neutron Halo Nuclei

## Key claim

Higher multipole excitations beyond dipole do not play a significant role in the electromagnetic breakup of one-neutron halo nuclei, even as one moves from stable isotopes toward the neutron drip line. This conclusion is qualified by the caveat that the results were obtained for weakly-bound neutron halos and may differ for heavier or charged clusters.

## Method

The authors compare two theoretical approaches: a post-form finite range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA, Method 1) that includes the electromagnetic interaction between core and target to all orders and accounts for all multipoles and orbital angular momenta, and a first-order Alder-Winther Coulomb dissociation theory based on multipole expansion (Method 2). For the structure of the halo nuclei ¹¹Be and ¹⁹C, single-particle Woods-Saxon potentials are used with artificially varied depths to simulate different one-neutron separation energies, and for ¹¹Be a separate shallow potential generates the 1p₁/₂ excited state bound by 0.18 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For ¹¹Be on ²⁰⁸Pb at 72 MeV/u, the experimental one-neutron separation energy is 504 keV; the peak of the relative energy spectra scales with separation energy with a slope of approximately 0.4, close to the simple analytical estimate of 3/5 from the dipole strength distribution.
- For ¹⁹C on ²⁰⁸Pb at 67 MeV/u, the one-neutron separation energy is debated: direct mass measurements give 0.16 ± 0.11 MeV, while indirect measurements yield 0.53 ± 0.13 MeV (Nakamura 1999) and 0.58 ± 0.09 MeV (Audi 2003).
- Grazing angles for ¹¹Be on ²⁰⁸Pb are 8.12 degrees at 30 MeV/u, 3.62 degrees at 72 MeV/u, and 0.995 degrees at 400 MeV/u.
- The total one-neutron removal cross section for both ¹¹Be and ¹⁹C follows a 1/Sₙ dependence at higher binding energies, with deviations at low binding energy; the FRDWBA results and pure E1 contributions are almost indistinguishable, indicating negligible higher multipole contributions.

## Context

This work extends the study of Coulomb dissociation as a probe of halo nuclei structure, building on the analytical models of Nakada (1994) and Bertulani (1996) for dipole strength distributions, and on the FRDWBA formalism developed by Chatterjee (2004). The comparison of two theoretical methods enables assessment of whether higher multipoles become important for weakly bound systems, which is relevant for extracting astrophysical S-factors via the Coulomb dissociation method (Baur, Bertulani, and Rebel 1986). The results confirm the dominance of dipole transitions for one-neutron halos and suggest that the relative importance of E1/E2 transitions remains fairly constant across binding energies, enabling future work on determining separation energies from experimental relative energy spectra.
