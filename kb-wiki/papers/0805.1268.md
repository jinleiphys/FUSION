# Structure of upper g9/2-shell nuclei and shape effect in the 94Ag isomeric states

> Using a shell model which is capable of describing the spectra of upper $g_{9/2}$-shell nuclei close to the $N=Z$ line, we study the structure of two isomeric states $7^{+}$ and $21^{+}$ in the odd-odd $N=Z$ nucleus $^{94}$Ag. It is found that both isomeric states exhibit a large collectivity. The $7^{+}$ state is oblately deformed, and is suggested to be a shape isomer in nature. The $21^{+}$ state becomes isomeric because of level inversion of the $19^{+}$ and $21^{+}$ states due to core excitations across the $N=Z=50$ shell gap. Calculation of spectroscopic quadrupole moment indicates clearly an enhancement in these states due to the core excitations. However, the present shell model calculation that produces the $19^{+}$-$21^{+}$ level inversion cannot accept the large-deformation picture of Mukha {\it et al.} in Nature {\bf 439}, 298 (2006).

## Key claim

The 7+ state in 94Ag is suggested to be a shape isomer with oblate deformation, while the 21+ state becomes isomeric due to a 19+-21+ level inversion caused by core excitations across the N=Z=50 shell gap; however, the shell model calculation that produces this level inversion cannot accept the large-deformation (prolate) picture proposed by Mukha et al. (2006).

## Method

Large-scale spherical shell model calculations were performed in two model spaces: the fpg model space (1p3/2, 0f5/2, 1p1/2, 0g9/2) for systematic study of upper g9/2-shell nuclei, and the gds model space (0g9/2, 1d5/2, 0g7/2, 2s1/2) allowing up to 3p-3h excitations across the N=Z=50 shell gap to study the 21+ isomer in 94Ag. The Hamiltonian included pairing, quadrupole-quadrupole, and octupole-octupole forces with monopole corrections, using the extended P+QQ model.

## Key numbers

- The 21+ isomer in 94Ag has an excitation energy of 6.7(5) MeV with a half-life of 0.39(4) s
- The 7+ isomer in 94Ag has a half-life of 0.59(2) s, with excitation energy calculated at 0.66 MeV
- In the gds-shell calculation, the 19+ level lies 25.7 keV above the 21+ level, producing the level inversion
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment for the 7+ state: 109.6 e fm2 (gds-shell) vs 61.2 e fm2 (fpg-shell)
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment for the 21+ state: 84.1 e fm2 (gds-shell) vs 57.7 e fm2 (fpg-shell)
- B(E2;14+ -> 12+) in 94Pd calculated as 39.6 e2fm4, close to experimental estimates of 44(6) e2fm4 or 39(9) e2fm4
- B(E2;7+ -> 5+) in 94Ag is 0.0 e2fm4 (fpg-shell) and 0.26 e2fm4 (gds-shell), indicating hindered decay

## Context

This work extends previous shell model studies of upper g9/2-shell N=Z nuclei, particularly the restricted (1p1/2, 0g9/2) model space calculations by Gross and Serduke that failed to predict the 21+ isomerism in 94Ag, and the large-scale calculations by Plettner et al. that first showed the 21+-19+ level inversion. The paper contradicts the strong prolate deformation interpretation of Mukha et al. (2006) for the 21+ isomer, instead finding oblate deformation for the 7+ state and attributing the 21+ isomerism to core excitations rather than large deformation. The results enable improved reaction rate calculations for the rp-process nucleosynthesis path, particularly for waiting point nuclei like 92,93Pd and 96Cd.
