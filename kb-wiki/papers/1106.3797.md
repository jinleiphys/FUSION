# Momentum dependence of the symmetry potential and its influence on nuclear reactions

> A Skyrme-type momentum-dependent nucleon-nucleon force distinguishing isospin effect is parameterized and further implemented in the Lanzhou Quantum Molecular Dynamics (LQMD) model for the first time, which leads to a splitting of nucleon effective mass in nuclear matter. Based on the isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model, we investigate the influence of momentum-dependent symmetry potential on several isospin-sensitive observables in heavy-ion collisions. It is found that symmetry potentials with and without the momentum dependence but corresponding to the same density dependence of the symmetry energy result in different distributions of the observables. The mid-rapidity neutron/proton ratios at high transverse momenta and the excitation functions of the total $π^{-}/π^{+}$ and $K^{0}/K^{+}$ yields are particularly sensitive to the momentum dependence of the symmetry potential.

# Momentum dependence of the symmetry potential and its influence on nuclear reactions

## Key claim
Symmetry potentials with and without momentum dependence but corresponding to the same density dependence of the symmetry energy produce different distributions of isospin-sensitive observables in heavy-ion collisions, meaning that the momentum dependence of the symmetry potential must be separately constrained before the density dependence of the symmetry energy can be reliably extracted.

## Method
A Skyrme-type momentum-dependent nucleon-nucleon force distinguishing isospin effects was parameterized and implemented in the Lanzhou Quantum Molecular Dynamics (LQMD) model for the first time. The model includes resonances (Delta(1232), N*(1440), N*(1535)), hyperons (Lambda, Sigma), and mesons (pi, K, eta) with hadron-hadron collisions and resonance decays, treating the time evolution of baryons and mesons under self-consistently generated mean fields via Hamilton's equations of motion. The momentum-dependent interaction leads to a splitting of nucleon effective mass in nuclear matter, with the parameter x determining whether m*_n < m*_p or m*_n > m*_p.

## Key numbers
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: 31.5 MeV (all stiffness cases cross at this value)
- Effective mass m*/m = 0.75 in nuclear medium at saturation density for symmetric nuclear matter (using C_mom = 1.76 MeV, epsilon = 500 c^2/GeV^2, x = -0.65)
- Local symmetry energy parameters: C_sym = 52.5 MeV for cases with momentum-dependent interactions; a_sym = 43 MeV, b_sym = -16.75 MeV for cases without momentum-dependent interactions
- Gamma_s values of 0.5, 1.0, and 2.0 correspond to soft, linear, and hard symmetry energy, respectively
- The symmetry energy extracted from prior data: E_sym(rho) approximately 31.6(rho/rho_0)^gamma MeV with gamma = 0.69-1.05 for densities between 0.1 rho_0 and 1.2 rho_0 (Li 2008)

## Context
This work extends prior transport model studies by implementing momentum-dependent symmetry potentials in a full N-body approach (LQMD) for the first time, whereas previous versions of LQMD only considered scalar momentum-dependent interactions and density-dependent symmetry potentials. The results are consistent with calculations based on the IBUU04 transport model (Li 2004) and the stochastic mean-field model (Tolokonnikov 1999, Baran 2005). The updated LQMD model enables future extraction of the high-density symmetry energy from heavy-ion collision data, particularly at supra-normal densities, and can help analyze experimental data for constraining the symmetry energy in neutron-rich matter relevant to neutron star structure and supernova nucleosynthesis.
