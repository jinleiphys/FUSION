# Connected and Disconnected Contractions in Pion-Pion Scattering

> We show that the interplay of chiral effective field theory and lattice QCD can be used in the evaluation of so-called disconnected diagrams, which appear in the study of the isoscalar and isovector channels of pion-pion scattering and have long been a major challenge for the lattice community. By means of partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory, we distinguish and analyze the effects from different types of contraction diagrams to the pion-pion scattering amplitude, including its scattering lengths and the energy-dependence of its imaginary part. Our results may be used to test the current degree of accuracy of lattice calculation in the handling of disconnected diagrams, as well as to set criteria for the future improvement of relevant lattice computational techniques that may play a critical role in the study of other interesting QCD matrix elements.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQChPT) can systematically separate and quantify the contributions of different types of quark contraction diagrams (connected, singly-disconnected, and doubly-disconnected) to pion-pion scattering amplitudes up to next-to-leading order (NLO, order p^4), providing a theoretical benchmark for lattice QCD calculations that struggle with disconnected diagrams.

## Method

The authors construct an SU(4|2) partially-quenched QCD framework with four valence quarks and two ghost quarks, allowing them to isolate specific Wick contraction diagrams by choosing appropriate external quark flavors. They compute the two independent contraction amplitudes T1 and T2 up to NLO in PQChPT, then decompose the isospin amplitudes (I=0,1,2) into contributions from crossed (C), direct (D), rectangular (R), and vacuum (V) diagrams. The results are expressed in terms of both physical and unphysical low-energy constants, with the unphysical dependence canceling when physical scattering amplitudes are reconstructed.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant used: F_pi = 92.2 MeV
* The T1 amplitude (singly-disconnected) begins at order p^2, while the T2 amplitude (doubly-disconnected) begins at order p^4, as shown by the leading terms: T1 contains the term (2M_pi^2 - u)/(2F_pi^2) while T2 has no order p^2 term
* The doubly-disconnected (V) contribution to the I=0 amplitude is an NLO effect, while the singly-disconnected (R) diagrams contribute at LO
* In the I=1 channel, the singly-disconnected piece dominates over the connected piece because the former is of order p^2 while the latter is of order p^4 in chiral power counting
* The beta-function coefficients Gamma_i for the SU(4|2) PQChPT low-energy constants are tabulated, with values ranging from 0 (for L3, L7, L8) to 1/4 (for L5)

## Context

This work extends the leading-order analysis of contraction diagrams in pion-pion scattering by Guo (2013) to complete next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory. The partially-quenched approach builds on the foundational PQChPT framework developed by Bernard (1993), Sharpe (1999-2001), and others, and follows the strategy previously applied to hadronic vacuum polarization by Della Morte (2010) and the pion scalar form factor by Juttner (2011). The results provide a theoretical standard for testing the accuracy of lattice QCD calculations in the I=0 and I=1 channels, where disconnected diagrams have been a major challenge, and can guide future improvements in lattice computational techniques for other QCD matrix elements involving disconnected diagrams.
