# The first large-scale shell-model calculation of the two-neutrino double beta decay of $^{76}$Ge to the excited states in $^{76}$Se

> Large-scale shell-model calculations were carried out for the half-lives and branching ratios of the $2νββ$ decay of $^{76}$Ge to the ground state and the lowest three excited states $2_1^+$, $0_2^+$ and $2_2^+$ in $^{76}$Se. In total, the wave functions of more than 10,000 intermediate $1^+$ states in $^{76}$As were calculated in a three-step procedure allowing an efficient use of the available computer resources. In the first step, 250 lowest states, below some 5 MeV of excitation energy, were calculated without truncations within a full major shell $0f_{5/2}-1p-0g_{9/2}$ for both protons and neutrons. The wave functions of the rest of the states, up to some 30 MeV, were computed in two more steps by introducing two consecutive stages of truncation. The computed magnitudes of the $2νββ$ nuclear matrix elements (including the value of the axial-vector coupling $g_{\rm A}$), $\vert M_{2ν}\vert g_{\rm A}^2$, converged to the values 0.168$g_{\rm A}^2$, $1.2\times10^{-3}$$g_{\rm A}^2$, 0.121$g_{\rm A}^2$, and $3.1\times10^{-3}$$g_{\rm A}^2$ for the $0^+_{\rm g.s.}$, $2^+_1$, $0^+_2$, and $2^+_2$ states, respectively. Using up-to-date phase-space integrals, the corresponding branching ratios were derived to be 99.926\%, 4.4$\times10^{-5}$\%, 0.074\% and 2.5$\times10^{-7}$\%. The experimental half-life $(1.926\pm0.094)\times10^{21}$ yr of the ground-state transition was used to derive the value $g_{\rm A}=0.80\pm0.01$ for the axial-vector coupling, which is consistent with other shell-model calculations suggesting a quenched value of $g_{\rm A}$. Using this value of $g_{\rm A}$, predictions for the transition half-lives were derived.

## Key claim

The first large-scale shell-model calculation of the two-neutrino double beta decay of 76Ge to excited states in 76Se was performed, using a three-step truncation procedure to handle more than 10,000 intermediate 1+ states; the branching ratios are independent of the adopted value of the axial-vector coupling gA as long as the same effective value applies to all transitions.

## Method

The authors used the shell-model software NuShellX@MSU with the effective Hamiltonian JUN45 in the 0f5/2-1p-0g9/2 major shell for both protons and neutrons. They calculated the lowest 250 intermediate 1+ states in 76As without truncation, then introduced two consecutive truncations (first keeping the neutron 0f5/2 orbital filled, then additionally keeping the proton 0g9/2 orbital empty) to reach up to 30.6 MeV in excitation energy, combining results from all three steps to obtain 10,266 intermediate states. The computed excitation energies of the lowest three 1+ states were shifted to match experimental values, and the experimental half-life of the ground-state transition was used to derive the quenched value gA = 0.80 ± 0.01.

- Nuclear matrix element magnitude |M2ν|gA² for the 2+2 state: 3.1×10⁻³ gA²

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model calculations for the ground-state transition by Caurier (2012) and Brown (2015), which gave NME values of 0.170 gA² and similar results, to include excited-state transitions for the first time. The results agree closely with the Multiple Commutator Model calculations of Civitarese (1994) for the excited-state NMEs, and show reasonable agreement with various QRPA-based approaches including the renormalized QRPA of Toivanen (1997) and the higher QRPA of Stoica (1996). The step-wise shell-model procedure introduced here could be applied to other computationally intense double-beta decay calculations, and the predicted branching ratios can guide future experiments using a 76Ge source.
