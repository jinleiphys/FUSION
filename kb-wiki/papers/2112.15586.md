# Meson structure on the light-front III : The Hamiltonian, heavy quarkonia, spin and orbit mixing

> This is the third paper on hadronic light front wave functions (LFWFs). We derive a light front Hamiltonian from first principles using the key features of the QCD vacuum at low resolution. In the first approximation, it gives transverse oscillator and longitudinal harmonic modes and yields the correct Regge trajectories. For heavy quarkonia, we compare its spectrum to that obtained from the usual Schroedinger equation in the rest frame. We use the same approach for light quarks, and investigate the role of confinement and chiral symmetry breaking in the quark-antiquark sector. We then study spin-spin and spin-orbit mixing, resulting in e.g. quadrupole moments of vector mesons. For the light mesons, we show how to extend the famed t$^\prime$Hooft interaction to the light front, which solves the U(1) problem and helps produce a light pion. We use the ensuing light front wavefunctions, to derive the pertinent parton distribution functions, parton amplitudes and low energy constants.

## Key claim

The paper derives a light-front Hamiltonian for mesons from first principles using QCD vacuum properties, claiming that in the first approximation it yields transverse oscillator and longitudinal harmonic modes with correct Regge trajectories, but the derivation relies on analytical continuation of Euclidean Wilson loops and the random instanton vacuum model, with the Hamiltonian's validity contingent on the specific non-perturbative gluonic contributions and the instanton ensemble parameters.

## Method

The authors construct the light-front Hamiltonian by analytically continuing sloped Wilson loops from Euclidean to Minkowski signature, dressing them with spin factors to include perturbative one-gluon exchange contributions (Coulomb, spin-spin, and spin-orbit interactions) and non-perturbative instanton-induced central and spin-dependent potentials. They then diagonalize the Hamiltonian in a truncated basis of 2-dimensional transverse oscillator and longitudinal sine functions, applying the einbein trick to eliminate square roots, and compare the resulting spectra for heavy quarkonia (bottomonium) to solutions of the Schroedinger equation with the Cornell potential in the rest frame.

## Key numbers

* The gluon mass in the random instanton vacuum is estimated as \( m_G \rho \approx 0.55 \), using \( \kappa = \pi^2 \rho^4 n_{I+\bar I} \).
* The central instanton potential is given by \( \mathbb V_C(\xi_x) = (4\kappa / (N_c \rho)) \mathbf{H}(\tilde\xi_x) \), with \( \tilde\xi_x = \xi_x / \rho \).
* The squared masses \( M_{n+1}^2 \) for bottomonium states with \( n = 0 \) to \( 5 \) and orbital momenta \( L = 0, 1, 2 \) are calculated and compared to experimental Upsilon masses; the linear Regge trajectory slope for heavy bottomonia differs from the \( 1/\alpha' \) slope for light mesons.
* The variational parameter \( a \) is set to \( a = 25 \) as a common value for all states after minimization.

## Context

This is the third paper in a series on hadronic light-front wave functions, extending the authors' prior work on non-perturbative spin contributions (Shuryak and Zahed, 2021) and the rest-frame analysis of spin-dependent forces (Shuryak and Zahed, 2021). The approach contrasts with lattice QCD calculations of quasi-PDFs (Ji, 2020) and the Bethe-Salpeter approach for the pion (Shi, 2015; Shi, 2018), aiming to bridge the gap between light-front observables (PDFs, DAs) and hadronic spectroscopy by providing a Hamiltonian that generates orthogonal wave functions for any number of matrix elements. The work enables calculation of parton distribution functions, parton amplitudes, and low-energy constants from the derived light-front wavefunctions.
