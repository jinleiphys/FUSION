# Neutron EDM from Electric and Chromoelectric Dipole Moments of Quarks

> Using QCD sum rules, we calculate the electric dipole moment of the neutron d_n induced by all CP violating operators up to dimension five. We find that the chromoelectric dipole moments of quarks \tilde d_i, including that of the strange quark, provide significant contributions comparable in magnitude to those induced by the quark electric dipole moments d_i. When the theta term is removed via the Peccei-Quinn symmetry, the strange quark contribution is also suppressed and d_n =(1\pm 0.5)[1.1e(\tilde d_d + 0.5\tilde d_u)+1.4(d_d-0.25d_u)].

# Neutron EDM from Electric and Chromoelectric Dipole Moments of Quarks

## Key claim

The chromoelectric dipole moments (CEDMs) of quarks, including the strange quark, contribute to the neutron electric dipole moment (EDM) at a magnitude comparable to that of the quark electric dipole moments (EDMs), contradicting earlier QCD sum rule analyses that predicted a suppression of CEDM contributions. When the Peccei-Quinn mechanism removes the theta term, the strange quark CEDM contribution is also suppressed, yielding a simplified expression for the neutron EDM.

## Method

The authors used QCD sum rules to compute the neutron EDM induced by all CP-violating operators up to dimension five (the theta term, quark EDMs, and quark CEDMs). They constructed a correlator of neutron interpolating currents in a background with CP-odd sources and an electromagnetic field, performed an operator product expansion (OPE) to next-to-leading order, and extracted the EDM from the tensor structure {Fσγ₅, p̸} which avoids contamination from unphysical phases. The vacuum matrix elements for the CEDM sources were evaluated using chiral perturbation theory, saturating them by π₀ and η₈ exchange.

## Key numbers

- Neutron EDM experimental limit: dₙ < 6 × 10⁻²⁶ e cm
- Quark condensate: ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(0.225 GeV)³
- Condensate susceptibilities: m₀² = 0.8 GeV², χ = -5.7 ± 0.6 GeV⁻², ξ = -0.74 ± 0.2, κ = -0.34 ± 0.1
- Neutron coupling extracted from CP-even sum rules: (2π)⁴λ ∼ 1.05 ± 0.1
- Borel parameter at which the sum rule is evaluated: M² ∼ 0.55 GeV²
- Final result with Peccei-Quinn mechanism: dₙ^(PQ) = (1 ± 0.5) × [1.1e(Ẽ_d + 0.5Ẽ_u) + 1.4(d_d - 0.25d_u)], where the factor |⟨q̄q⟩|/(225 MeV)³ is set to unity
- Overall coefficient from sum rule analysis: dₙ = (0.4 ± 0.2) times the combination of operators (before PQ mechanism)

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule calculation of the neutron EDM from the theta term performed by Pospelov and Ritz (1999) and Henley (1992) to include all dimension-five CP-violating operators. It contradicts earlier QCD sum rule calculations of CEDM contributions that predicted a suppression, while agreeing with chiral loop estimates by Khatsymovsky and Khriplovich that indicated sizable CEDM effects. The results enable improved phenomenological analyses of CP violation in supersymmetric and other new physics models by providing the necessary hadronic matrix elements connecting quark-level CP-odd operators to the neutron EDM.
