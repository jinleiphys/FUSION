# Pairing Reentrance Phenomenon in Heated Rotating Nuclei in the Shell Model Monte Carlo Approach

> Rotational motion of heated 72-Ge is studied within the microscopic Shell Model Monte Carlo approach. We investigate the the angular momentum alignment and nuclear pairing correlations associated with J-pi Cooper pairs as a function of the rotational frequency and temperature. The reentrance of pairing correlations with temperature is predicted at high rotational frequencies. It manifests itself through the anomalous behavior of specific heat and level density.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that pairing correlations in the rotating nucleus ⁷²Ge can locally increase with temperature at high rotational frequencies, a phenomenon called "pairing reentrance" or "thermally assisted pairing," which manifests as anomalous behavior in the specific heat and level density. This effect is conditional on the nucleus being both rapidly rotating and at intermediate temperatures, where less-aligned excited configurations with lower seniorities enter the canonical ensemble and reintroduce pair correlations.

## Method

The authors used the Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method to study ⁷²Ge at finite temperature and rotation, employing a pairing-plus-quadrupole-quadrupole Hamiltonian with strength parameters G=0.106 MeV and χ=0.0104 MeV·fm². They considered a single-particle space consisting of the complete (0f1p-0g1d2s) shells for protons and neutrons, with 12 valence protons and 20 valence neutrons outside a ⁴⁰Ca core, and introduced rotation through the cranking term ωĴz in the routhian Ĥ^ω = Ĥ - ωĴz. Calculations were performed with up to 15,840 statistical samples.

## Context

This work extends earlier SMMC studies of pairing in ⁷²Ge by Langanke (2005) and provides the first microscopic demonstration of the pairing reentrance phenomenon predicted by Kammuri (1964) and explained by Moretto (1971). The results contradict the simple expectation of a monotonic pairing collapse with increasing temperature and rotation (the Coriolis-antipairing effect), instead showing that nuclei behave as type II superconductors where pairing can reappear at intermediate temperatures. The calculations enable future studies of pairing reentrance signatures in level densities and pair transfer amplitudes, and connect to broader work on partial order phenomena in finite Fermi systems by authors such as Frauendorf (2003) and Hunsucker (2008).
