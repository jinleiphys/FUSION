# Electromagnetic probes for critical fluctuations of phase transitions in dense QCD

> We study how the dilepton production rates and electric conductivity are affected by the phase transition to color superconductivity and the QCD critical point. Effects of the soft modes associated with these phase transitions are incorporated through the photon self-energy called the Aslamazov-Larkin, Maki-Thompson, and density-of-states terms, which are responsible for the paraconductivity in metallic superconductors. We show that anomalous enhancements of the production rate in the low energy/momentum region and the conductivity occur around the respective critical points.

## Key claim

The paper shows that soft modes associated with the second-order color-superconducting phase transition and the QCD critical point cause anomalous enhancements of the dilepton production rate at low energy and momentum and of the electric conductivity near each critical point, but these enhancements are calculated using a 2-flavor NJL model with a free diquark coupling parameter, not from full QCD.

## Method

The authors employ a 2-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with scalar and diquark couplings to study the medium near the 2-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) phase transition and the QCD critical point. They compute the photon self-energy by including the Aslamazov-Larkin, Maki-Thompson, and density-of-states diagrams, which incorporate the effects of soft collective modes (diquark fluctuations for 2SC, chiral fluctuations for the QCD critical point) via the random-phase approximation. The dilepton production rate and electric conductivity are then obtained from the imaginary part and zero-frequency slope of the retarded photon self-energy, respectively.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the 2SC phase transition at quark chemical potential μ = 350 MeV and diquark coupling G_D = 0.7 G_S: T_c = 42.94 MeV.
- Location of the QCD critical point with G_D = 0.7 G_S: (T_CP, μ_CP) = (46.712, 329.34) MeV.
- NJL model parameters: scalar coupling G_S = 5.50 GeV^{-2}, three-momentum cutoff Λ = 631 MeV, current quark mass m = 5.5 MeV, yielding pion mass m_π = 138 MeV and pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV.
- The dilepton production rate from soft modes is shown for temperatures T/T_c = 1.01, 1.1, and 1.5 near the 2SC-PT, and for T/T_CP = 1.01, 1.1, and 1.2 near the QCD-CP.

## Context

This work extends the theory of paraconductivity in metallic superconductors, originally developed by Larkin and others, to the dense QCD phase diagram. It builds on prior studies of soft modes near the 2SC phase transition by Kitazawa (2005) and near the QCD critical point by Fujii (2004), and on the authors' own recent work (Nishimura 2022, 2023). The results suggest that two distinct enhancements of dilepton production as a function of heavy-ion collision energy could serve as collateral evidence for both the QCD critical point and the color-superconducting phase transition in experiments such as the RHIC beam-energy scan, HADES, NA61/SHINE, and future FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC-HI programs.
