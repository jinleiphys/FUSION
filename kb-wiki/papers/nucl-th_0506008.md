# Time-dependent analysis of the nuclear and Coulomb dissociation of 11Be

> The breakup of 11Be on carbon and lead targets around 70 MeV/nucleon is investigated within a semiclassical framework. The role of the 5/2+ resonance is analyzed in both cases. It induces a narrow peak in the nuclear-induced breakup cross section, while its effect on Coulomb breakup is small. The nuclear interactions between the projectile and the target is responsible for the transition toward this resonant state. The influence of the parametrization of the 10Be-n potential that simulates 11Be is also addressed. The breakup calculation is found to be dependent on the potential choice. This leads us to question the reliability of this technique to extract spectroscopic factors.

## Key claim

The breakup cross section of 11Be on a lead target at 69 MeV/nucleon varies by up to 30% in amplitude depending on the choice of the 10Be-n potential used to describe the projectile, even though all potentials reproduce the same bound and resonant states; this sensitivity leads the authors to question the reliability of using Coulomb breakup to extract spectroscopic factors.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for a two-body projectile (10Be core plus neutron) moving along a classical trajectory relative to a structureless target. The breakup probability is obtained by projecting the final wave function onto continuum eigenstates of the projectile internal Hamiltonian, and the cross section is integrated over impact parameters. For the 12C target at 67 MeV/nucleon, both nuclear and Coulomb interactions are included via optical potentials; for the 208Pb target at 69 MeV/nucleon, the reaction is Coulomb-dominated.

## Key numbers

- For 11Be breakup on 12C at 67 MeV/nucleon, the nuclear-induced cross section shows a narrow peak at the 5/2+ resonance energy; the peak shape (height and width) is similar across four different combinations of optical potentials, but the overall cross section amplitude varies by almost a factor of 2 when the 10Be-12C potential is changed from ATB to RPP.
- For 11Be breakup on 208Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon, calculations with six different 10Be-n potentials (V1 through V6) produce cross sections that differ by up to 30% in amplitude; potential V6 (Fukuda et al.) gives a cross section approximately 30% above the V1 curve.
- For the Coulomb breakup on 208Pb, a 20% variation in the amplitude of the optical potentials leads to only a 2% variation in the breakup cross section.
- Removing the Pauli-forbidden 0s1/2 and 0p3/2 states via supersymmetric transformation changes the breakup cross section by only 1% in the peak region.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent semiclassical approach previously applied to 11Be breakup on 12C by Capel et al. (2004) to the Coulomb-dominated case on 208Pb, and compares with experimental data from Fukuda et al. (2004) measured at RIKEN. The analysis shows that the simple two-body description of 11Be has reached its limit: the strong sensitivity of the cross section to the 10Be-n potential choice (up to 30% variation) makes spectroscopic factor extraction unreliable, and the model cannot reproduce the higher-lying resonances at 2.2 MeV and 2.9 MeV seen in the experimental 11Be spectrum. The authors call for improved projectile descriptions constrained by other experimental data or precise structure models.
