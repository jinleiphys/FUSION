# Viscous hydrodynamics relaxation time from AdS/CFT

> We consider an expanding boost-invariant plasma at strong coupling using the AdS/CFT correspondence for N=4 SYM. We determine the relaxation time in second order viscous hydrodynamics and find that it is around thirty times shorter than weak coupling expectations. We find that the nonsingularity of the dual geometry in the string frame necessitates turning on the dilaton which leads to a nonvanishing expectation value for tr F^2 behaving like tau^(-10/3).

# Viscous hydrodynamics relaxation time from AdS/CFT

## Key claim

The relaxation time in second order viscous hydrodynamics for strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma is approximately thirty times shorter than the value expected from Boltzmann kinetic theory estimates, making second order hydrodynamics much closer to the first order formalism than previously assumed. This result depends on the nonsingularity criterion applied to the dual geometry in the string frame, which necessitates turning on the dilaton field to cancel a remaining logarithmic divergence.

## Method

The authors used the AdS/CFT correspondence to construct dual five-dimensional geometries for an expanding boost-invariant plasma in N=4 SYM at strong coupling, expanding the metric coefficients in a series in inverse powers of proper time tau. They solved Einstein equations order by order in the scaling variable v = z/tau^(1/3), imposing nonsingularity of the Riemann tensor squared as the criterion to fix integration constants, and extracted the relaxation time by matching the energy density expansion to the equations of second order viscous hydrodynamics of Israel and Stewart.

## Key numbers

- The relaxation time ratio r = tau_Pi / tau_Pi^Boltzmann = (1 - log 2)/9 ≈ 0.034 ≈ 1/30, where tau_Pi^Boltzmann = 3 eta / (2p) is the weak coupling expectation
- The relaxation time expressed in terms of temperature: tau_Pi = (1 - log 2)/(6 pi T)
- The shear viscosity coefficient eta_0 = 1/(2^(1/2) * 3^(3/4)), reproducing the known N=4 SYM value
- The integration constant C = (6 log 2 - 17)/sqrt(3), determined from nonsingularity at order tau^{-2}
- The expectation value of tr F^2 scales as tau^{-10/3} with coefficient -sqrt(2)/(7 * 3^(1/4)) * N^2/(2 pi^2)
- The leading order temperature: T(tau) = sqrt(2)/(3^(1/4) pi tau^(1/3))

## Context

This work extends the quantitative framework of Janik and Peschinski (2006) for studying time-dependent expansion of boost-invariant plasma via AdS/CFT, which had previously determined the shear viscosity coefficient from nonsingularity of the dual geometry. It goes one order higher in the subasymptotic expansion to determine the relaxation time, a parameter in the second order viscous hydrodynamics of Israel and Stewart (1976) that is crucial for causal relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions. The result enables more accurate modeling of strongly coupled plasma dynamics and raises questions about the microscopic origin of the very short relaxation time compared to Boltzmann kinetic theory estimates.
