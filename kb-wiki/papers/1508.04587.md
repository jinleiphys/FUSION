# Symmetry Remnants in the Face of Competing Interactions in Nuclei

> Detailed description of nuclei necessitates model Hamiltonians which break most dynamical symmetries. Nevertheless, generalized notions of partial and quasi dynamical symmetries may still be applicable to selected subsets of states, amidst a complicated environment of other states. We examine such scenarios in the context of nuclear shape-phase transitions.

# Symmetry Remnants in the Face of Competing Interactions in Nuclei

## Key claim
Even at the critical point of a first-order quantum phase transition between spherical and deformed nuclear shapes, where the Hamiltonian is non-integrable and the dynamics is partly chaotic, generalized symmetries (partial and quasi dynamical symmetries) can still be assigned to selected regular multiplets of states that survive amidst a complicated environment of other states.

## Method
The authors study the intrinsic dynamics at the critical point of a first-order quantum phase transition between U(5) (spherical) and SU(3) (deformed) shapes in the interacting boson model (IBM), using a Hamiltonian that mixes terms from different dynamical symmetry chains. They analyze classical dynamics via Poincaré surfaces of section and quantum dynamics via Peres lattices for eigenstates with angular momenta L=0,2,3,4 and boson number N=50, then quantify the wavefunction purity using Shannon entropies in the U(5) and SU(3) bases and Pearson correlation coefficients to measure SU(3) coherence across rotational band members.

## Key numbers
- The zero-energy L=0⁺₂ state is a pure n_d=0 state with U(5) Shannon entropy S_U5=0, exemplifying exact U(5) partial dynamical symmetry.
- The 2⁺₂ state has a 96% n_d=1 component.
- States with n_d≤2 have U(5) Shannon entropy S_U5 < 0.15.
- The L=3⁺₂ state is a solvable U(5)-PDS eigenstate with n_d=3.
- The ground g(K=0) and γ(K=2) bands are pure with SU(3) Shannon entropy S_SU3=0, having (λ,μ) = (2N,0) and (2N-4,2) SU(3) character respectively.
- The Pearson correlator C_SU3(0_i-6) ≈ 1 for the regular non-solvable K-bands, indicating SU(3) quasi dynamical symmetry.

## Context
This work extends the concepts of partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) and quasi dynamical symmetry (QDS) introduced by Leviatan (1996, 2011) and Rowe (2004) respectively, applying them to the critical-point dynamics of a first-order shape-phase transition between the U(5) spherical and SU(3) deformed limits of the IBM. The analysis builds on previous studies of quantum phase transitions in nuclei by Cejnar (2010) and on the use of Peres lattices for distinguishing regular from chaotic dynamics. The results demonstrate that PDS and QDS can characterize symmetry remnants even in the presence of competing interactions that drive the system toward chaos, enabling future studies of order amidst chaos in nuclear collective motion.
