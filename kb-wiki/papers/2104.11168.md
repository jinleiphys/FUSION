# Color Transparency and the Proton Form Factor- Feynman Wins

> A recent experiment [{\bf Phys. Rev. Lett. 126,082301 (2021)}] used the $(e,e'p)$ reaction on $^{12}$C to search for the effects of color transparency (the absence of final state interactions). Color transparency was said to be ruled out. The ability to observe the effects of color transparency depends on the ability of a putative point-like-configuration (PLC), formed in a high-momentum transfer coherent reaction, to escape the nucleus without expanding its size. We study the expansion aspect of color transparency using superconformal baryon-meson symmetry and light-front holographic QCD. A new formalism is obtained and used to analyze the recent experiment. The resulting conclusion is that effects of expansion would not be sufficiently significant in causing final state interactions to occur. Therefore we conclude that a PLC was not formed. This means that the Feynman mechanism involving virtual photon absorption on a single high momentum quark is responsible for high momentum electromagnetic form factor of the proton.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the recent experiment claiming to rule out color transparency in the $(e,e'p)$ reaction on $^{12}$C up to momentum transfer of 14.2 GeV$^2$ is inconclusive because a point-like configuration (PLC) was never formed; instead, the Feynman mechanism (virtual photon absorption on a single high-momentum quark) dominates the proton form factor at these kinematics, meaning color transparency effects would not be expected regardless of expansion dynamics.

## Method

The authors use light-front holographic QCD with superconformal baryon-meson symmetry to model the time evolution of a putative PLC formed in a high-momentum transfer reaction. They derive a time-development operator using the Feynman path integral formalism applied to the light-front Schrodinger equation, with the effective potential from the soft-wall model (harmonic confinement with strength $\kappa = 0.53$ GeV). The expansion of a PLC is quantified by computing the ratio $b^2(t)$ of the transverse separation squared as a function of time, using Gaussian wave packets as initial PLC states and the ground-state wave functions obtained from the holographic model.

## Key numbers

- The confinement strength is taken as $\kappa = 0.53$ GeV, a universal value obtained from fitting the baryon and meson spectrum.
- The constant terms in the effective potential are $C_\pi = -2\kappa^2$, $C_\rho = 0$, and $C_N = 2\kappa^2$ for pion, rho meson, and nucleon respectively.
- The average transverse separation squared in the ground state is $\bar{b}^2 = 1/(\kappa^2 x(1-x))$, where $x$ is the plus-momentum fraction of one quark.
- The experiment analyzed had momentum transfer up to 14.2 GeV$^2$ (from the cited experiment Bhetuwal:2020jes).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate between two competing mechanisms for high-momentum transfer elastic reactions: the PLC/pQCD mechanism (Farrar, Efremov, Lepage, Brodsky, 1979-1980) and the Feynman mechanism (Drell and Yan, 1969; Feynman, 1973). The authors build on the light-front holographic QCD framework reviewed by Brodsky (2014) and the superconformal baryon-meson symmetry relations developed by de Teramond (2018). The analysis directly addresses the recent JLab experiment by Bhetuwal (2021) that claimed to rule out color transparency, and concludes that the Feynman mechanism is responsible for the proton form factor at these momentum transfers, consistent with earlier work by Miller (1998) and the GPD-based analysis of de Teramond (2018).
