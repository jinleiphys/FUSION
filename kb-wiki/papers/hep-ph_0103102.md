# On the Manifestation of Chiral Symmetry in Nuclei and Dense Nuclear Matter

> This article reviews our view on how chiral symmetry, its pattern of breaking and restoration under extreme conditions manifest themselves in the nucleon, nuclei, nuclear matter and dense hadronic matter. Topics treated are nucleon structure in terms of chiral symmetry, "first-principle" (QCD) calculations of the properties of finite nuclei effectuated by embedding the ``standard nuclear physics approach" into the framework of effective field theories of nuclei with predictions for certain astrophysical processes, a reinterpretation of the Brown-Rho (BR) scaling that implements chiral symmetry property of baryon-rich medium à la "vector manifestation" of hidden local symmetry, evidences for BR scaling in nuclear processes at normal nuclear matter density and at higher density, the notion of "broadband equilibration" in heavy-ion processes, and the role of strangeness in the formation of compact stars and their collapse into black-holes. We revisit the "Cheshire-Cat phenomenon" recently revived in the form of "quark-hadron continuity" in mapping low-density structure of hadrons to high-density structure of quarks and gluons and suggest how color-flavor locking in terms of QCD variables and hidden local symmetry in terms of hadronic variables may be connected and how BR scaling could fit into this "continuity" scheme.

## Key claim

This review argues that Brown-Rho (BR) scaling, which describes how hadron masses drop with increasing density due to changes in the QCD vacuum, is supported by both direct and indirect evidence in finite nuclei and dense matter; the authors acknowledge that their views are not necessarily shared by others in all details and that some points may not be fully correct.

## Method

The authors review the chiral bag model of the nucleon, effective field theories (EFTs) based on chiral symmetry, and the vector manifestation of hidden local symmetry as formulated by Harada and Yamawaki. They reinterpret BR scaling in terms of this vector manifestation and examine evidence from nuclear response functions, heavy-ion collisions, and compact-star observables, connecting the hadronic description to quark-gluon variables via the Cheshire Cat principle and color-flavor locking.

## Key numbers

- A quenching of approximately 20% in the longitudinal response function in medium-weight and heavy nuclei is reported from the analysis of Morgenstern and Meziani, attributed to BR scaling.
- The "little bag" model proposed a nucleon bag size of approximately 0.3 fm, reduced from the MIT bag model's typical 1 fm scale.
- The flavor-singlet axial charge a^0 for the proton comes out small as observed in the EMC/SMC experiments, with the total value falling within the experimental range shown in the paper's Figure 1.

## Context

This review extends the authors' earlier work on BR scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991) and the Cheshire Cat phenomenon, placing it within the framework of Harada and Yamawaki's vector manifestation of hidden local symmetry. It connects to the "standard nuclear physics approach" (SNPA) and potential models, and discusses how the chiral quark-soliton model of Diakonov and the cloudy bag model of Thomas and co-workers relate to the chiral bag picture. The work enables a unified description of nuclear matter from low densities up to compact-star densities, linking hadronic and quark-gluon descriptions via quark-hadron continuity.
