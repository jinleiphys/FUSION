# Isospin breaking of the narrow charmonium state of Belle at 3872 MeV as a deuson

> The narrow charmonium state near 3872 MeV reported by the Belle collaboration and confirmedby CDF lies almost exactly at the neutral D anti-D* threshold. As was predicted many years ago it can be a deuteronlike meson-meson state called a deuson. If so, it should be an axial, or possibily a pseudoscalar state with C=0, and isospin predominantly 0. Large isospin breaking is expected because of the isospin mass splitting between the neutral and charged D (D*) mesons. Because of this large isospin breaking the decay X(3872) to J/psi rho would be allowed, while J/psi sigma would be forbidden by C-parity, as indicated by the present data.

# Isospin breaking of the narrow charmonium state of Belle at 3872 MeV as a deuson

## Key claim

The X(3872) state observed by Belle and CDF can be interpreted as a deuteronlike $D\bar D^*$ bound state (a "deuson") with quantum numbers $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ or possibly $0^{-+}$, but only if one accepts that the state exhibits large isospin breaking because the neutral $D^0\bar D^{*0}$ threshold lies 8.1 MeV below the charged $D^+ D^{*-}$ threshold, forcing a dominant $D^0\bar D^{*0}$ component and a substantial I=1 admixture that allows the observed decay to $J/\psi \rho$.

## Method

The author applies a nonrelativistic potential model based on one-pion exchange, analogous to the deuteron, to the $D\bar D^*$ system. The central and tensor potentials from pion exchange are regularized at short distances with a cutoff parameter $\Lambda$, and the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation is solved for the $^3S_1$ and $^3D_1$ partial waves (for $1^{++}$) or the P-wave (for $0^{-+}$), including the mass splittings between neutral and charged $D$, $D^*$, and pion states to compute isospin-breaking wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The X(3872) mass measured by Belle is $3872 \pm 0.6 \text{(stat)} \pm 0.5 \text{(syst)}$ MeV with width $< 2.3$ MeV at 95% C.L.
- The CDF measurement gives a mass of $3871.3 \pm 0.7 \pm 0.4$ MeV.
- The $D^0\bar D^{*0}$ threshold is at 3871.2 MeV; the $D^+ D^{*-}$ threshold is 8.1 MeV higher.
- The predicted width for $X \to D^0\bar D^0\pi^0$ is estimated at 30-40 keV, with a $D^0\bar D^0\gamma / D^0\bar D^0\pi^0$ ratio of 62%.
- The $D^{*+} \to D^0\pi^+$ width used in the model is 63.3 keV, in agreement with the measured value of $65 \pm 3$ keV (Anastassov 2002).
- The overall potential normalization is $V_0 = 1.3$ MeV, fixed by the pion-nucleon coupling constant and pion decay constant.
- The regularization parameter $\Lambda$ is 1.355 GeV for the exact-isospin $1^{++}$ calculation, 1.40 GeV for the isospin-breaking $1^{++}$ calculation, 1.46 GeV for the exact-isospin $0^{-+}$ calculation, and 1.53 GeV for the isospin-breaking $0^{-+}$ calculation.

## Context

This work extends the deuson model originally proposed by Tornqvist (1994), which predicted $D\bar D^*$ bound states near 3870 MeV with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ and $0^{-+}$ using one-pion exchange, and is consistent with the earlier suggestion by Manohar and Wise and by Ericson and Karl that such states could exist. The paper directly addresses the Belle (2003) and CDF observations, showing that the large isospin breaking from the 8.1 MeV threshold splitting explains why the X(3872) decays to $J/\psi \rho$ (allowed by the I=1 admixture) while $J/\psi \sigma$ is forbidden by C-parity. The calculation also notes that Swanson (2003) finds a significant $\omega J/\psi$ component from short-range quark exchange, which could contribute additional binding. If confirmed, this interpretation would open a new spectroscopy of heavy meson deusons, including predicted $B\bar B^*$ and $B^*\bar B^*$ states with binding energies around 50 MeV.
