# The BCS theory of q-deformed nucleon pairs - qBCS

> We construct a coherent state of q-deformed zero coupled nucleon pairs distributed in several single-particle orbits. Using a variational approach, the set of equations of qBCS theory, to be solved self consistently for occupation probabilities, gap parameter Delta, and the chemical potential lambda, is obtained. Results for valence nucleons in nuclear degenerate sdg major shell show that the strongly coupled zero angular momentum nucleon pairs can be substituted by weakly coupled q-deformed zero angular momentum nucleon pairs. A study of Sn isotopes reveals a well defined universe of (G, q) values, for which qBCS converges. While the qBCS and BCS show similar results for Gap parameter Delta in Sn isotopes, the ground state energies are lower in qBCS. The pairing correlations in N nucleon system, increase with increasing q (for q real).

# qBCS - the BCS theory of q-deformed nucleon pairs

## Key claim

The paper proposes that strongly coupled zero angular momentum nucleon pairs of conventional BCS theory can be replaced by weakly coupled q-deformed zero angular momentum nucleon pairs, with the deformation parameter q serving as a measure of pairing correlations left out in standard BCS. The claim is conditional: the equivalence holds for a well-defined universe of (G, q) values for which qBCS converges, and the ground state energies are lower in qBCS than in BCS while the gap parameter Delta remains similar.

## Method

The authors construct a coherent trial wave function for N nucleons distributed over several single-particle orbits, using q-deformed pair creation operators that satisfy the commutation relations of the quantum group SU_q(2). They minimize the expectation value of a single-particle plus pairing Hamiltonian under a number constraint, deriving self-consistent equations for occupation probabilities, the gap parameter Delta(q), and the chemical potential lambda(q). The formalism is applied to a degenerate sdg major shell with Omega = 16 and to the realistic case of Sn isotopes with N = 14, 16, 18, 20, 22, and 24 neutrons outside the 100Sn core, using five single-particle orbits with specified excitation energies.

## Key numbers

- For the degenerate sdg major shell (Omega = 16), the pairing strength G' = 0.187 MeV is used to compare exact energies with qBCS energies for 4, 14, 20, and 30 valence nucleons.
- For 120Sn with N = 20, the critical pairing strength G_c for conventional BCS (q = 1.0) is approximately 0.065 MeV; for q = 1.3, G_c drops to approximately 0.04 MeV.
- For 120Sn, the pairing strength G can be chosen up to G = 0.217 MeV with a matching q value to reproduce the experimental pairing gap; for G = 0.07 MeV, a deformation of q = 1.7 is needed.
- The model space for Sn isotopes includes single-particle orbits with excitation energies 0.0, 0.22, 1.90, 2.20, and 2.80 MeV for the 1d5/2, 0g7/2, 2s1/2, 1d3/2, and 0h11/2 orbits respectively.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies of q-deformed nucleon pairs in the seniority scheme (Sharma 1992) and q-deformed random phase approximation for pairing vibrations in Pb isotopes (Sharma and Sharma 1994). It contrasts with the approach of Bonatsos et al. (1992, 1994), who treated nucleon pairing in a single j shell by associating two Q-oscillators with J=0 and J not equal to 0 pairs, introducing deformation in a different way. The qBCS formalism reduces to conventional BCS in the limit q approaches 1, and the results open the possibility of obtaining exact correlation energies by choosing appropriate combinations of G and q values, with potential applications to other systems such as metal grains where Cooper pairing plays an important role.
