# Multipole Modes for Triaxially Deformed Superfluid Nuclei

> To study shape fluctuations of nuclei in transitional regions, the collective Hamiltonian method has often been employed. We intend to construct the quadrupole collective Hamiltonian with the collective inertial functions given by the local quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) based on the Skyrme energy density functional. For this purpose, we first construct a practical framework of Skyrme QRPA for triaxial nuclear shapes with the finite amplitude method (FAM). We show quadrupole strength functions for a triaxial superfluid nucleus $^{188}$Os and the Thouless-Valatin rotational moment of inertia by the local FAM-QRPA for $^{106}$Pd.

## Key claim

The authors present a practical framework for Skyrme QRPA calculations for triaxial nuclear shapes using the finite amplitude method (FAM), which avoids constructing and diagonalizing the huge QRPA matrix (dimension about 10^6 for deformed nuclei) that would otherwise be computationally prohibitive at each point of the (β,γ) deformation plane.

## Method

The authors constructed a three-dimensional FAM-QRPA code on a Cartesian coordinate mesh with reflection symmetry, solving the linear-response equation iteratively using the modified Broyden method. They performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations at each (β,γ) deformation point, then applied the local FAM-QRPA on top of these states to compute strength functions and the Thouless-Valatin rotational moment of inertia via the relation between the FAM strength function at zero frequency and the Nambu-Goldstone mode inertia.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁸⁸Os: ground state deformation at β=0.216, γ=20.7° using SkM* functional with 19³ mesh and 1360 basis states; pairing strength reproduces neutron pairing gap of 1.25 MeV in ¹²⁰Sn.
- Strength functions calculated at 200 ω points between ω=0 and 50 MeV with Δω=0.25 MeV and smearing width of 0.5 MeV.
- Energy-weighted sum rule for K=0 and K=2 modes in ¹⁸⁸Os satisfied at 98.6% for both modes when summed up to ω=50 MeV.
- For ¹⁰⁶Pd: constrained HFB + local FAM-QRPA calculations used 17³ mesh and 1120 basis states with SkM* functional.
- Computational cost: strength function at one ω point in about 8 minutes average; one moment of inertia value at one (β,γ) point in about 2 minutes, with parallelization of 16 threads.

## Context

This work extends the local QRPA approach previously developed with pairing-plus-quadrupole forces (Hinohara 2010, 2011, 2012) to the Skyrme energy density functional framework, combining it with the finite amplitude method (Nakatsukasa 2007, Avogadro 2011, Stoitsov 2011, Kortelainen 2015). The authors aim to construct a five-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian with microscopically derived inertial functions, going beyond the Inglis-Belyaev cranking formula used in earlier collective Hamiltonian approaches (Prochniak 2004, Niksic 2009, Delaroche 2010). The next step is to estimate vibrational inertial functions and construct the full collective Hamiltonian based on Skyrme EDF.
