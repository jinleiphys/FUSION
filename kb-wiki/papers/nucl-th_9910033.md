# The Quark-Photon Vertex and the Pion Charge Radius

> The rainbow truncation of the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation is combined with the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation for the dressed quark-photon vertex to study the low-momentum behavior of the pion electromagnetic form factor. With model gluon parameters previously fixed by the pion mass and decay constant, the pion charge radius $r_π$ is found to be in excellent agreement with the data. When the often-used Ball-Chiu Ansatz is used to construct the quark-photon vertex directly from the quark propagator, less than half of $r_π^2$ is generated. The remainder of $r^2_π$ is seen to be attributable to the presence of the $ρ$-pole in the solution of the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation.

## Key claim

The pion charge radius calculated from the rainbow-ladder Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach, with model parameters fixed solely by the pion mass and decay constant, yields excellent agreement with data (r_π² = 0.46 fm² vs. experimental 0.44 fm²). The Ball-Chiu Ansatz alone generates less than half of r_π², with the remainder attributable to the ρ-meson pole present in the full Bethe-Salpeter solution for the quark-photon vertex.

## Method

The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation in rainbow truncation and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion, combined with the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the dressed quark-photon vertex in ladder truncation, using a common effective gluon propagator model. The quark-photon vertex is decomposed into twelve Lorentz covariants (four longitudinal fixed by the Ward-Takahashi identity, eight transverse solved numerically), and the pion electromagnetic form factor is computed in impulse approximation via the triangle diagram. The model parameters ω = 0.4 GeV and D = 0.93 GeV² are fixed by m_π and f_π from prior work (Maris and Roberts, 1997; Maris and Tandy, 1999).

## Key numbers

- Pion charge radius from full BSE vertex: r_π² = 0.46 fm² (using all eight transverse covariants), compared to experimental value 0.44 fm²
- Ball-Chiu Ansatz alone produces less than half of r_π² (implied < 0.22 fm²)
- Vector meson pole from ladder BSE appears at Q² ≈ -0.55 GeV² (ρ mass about 4% below physical m_ρ = 0.593 GeV²)
- The resonant-improved BC Ansatz (BC plus ρ-pole term) is applicable in the region -0.4 GeV² < Q² < 0.2 GeV²
- Four of eight transverse amplitudes (F₃, F₆, F₇, F₈) contribute less than 1% to F_π(Q²) over the range studied

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach of Maris and Roberts (1997) and Maris and Tandy (1999) by solving the full inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the quark-photon vertex rather than using the Ball-Chiu Ansatz (Ball and Chiu, 1980) or the Curtis-Pennington Ansatz (Curtis and Pennington, 1990). It reconciles the vector meson dominance picture with QCD by showing that the ρ-pole contribution naturally emerges from the vertex BSE solution, accounting for the missing portion of the pion charge radius. The results enable parameter-free calculations of electromagnetic form factors for other mesons and baryons within the same framework, and motivate inclusion of ππ decay channels to generate a finite ρ width.
