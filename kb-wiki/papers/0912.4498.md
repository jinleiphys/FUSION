# Centrality, Rapidity and Transverse-Momentum Dependence of Cold Nuclear Matter Effects on J/Psi Production in d+Au, Cu+Cu and Au+Au Collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV

> We have carried out a wide study of Cold Nuclear Matter (CNM) effects on J/Psi production in d+Au, Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. We have studied the effects of three different gluon-shadowing parametrisations, using the usual simplified kinematics for which the momentum of the gluon recoiling against the J/Psi is neglected as well as an exact kinematics for a 2 -> 2 process, namely g+g -> J/psi+g as expected from LO pQCD. We have shown that the rapidity distribution of the nuclear modification factor R_dAu, and particularly its anti-shadowing peak, is systematically shifted toward larger rapidities in the 2 -> 2 kinematics, irrespective of which shadowing parametrisation is used. In turn, we have noted differences in the effective final-state nuclear absorption necessary to fit the PHENIX d+Au data. Taking advantage of our implementation of a 2 -> 2 kinematics, we have also computed the transverse momentum dependence of the latter nuclear modification factor, which cannot be predicted with the usual simplified kinematics. All the corresponding observables have been computed for Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions and compared to the PHENIX and STAR data. Finally, we have extracted the effective nuclear absorption from the recent measurements of R_CP in d+Au by the PHENIX collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using a 2→2 partonic process (g+g→J/ψ+g) instead of the simplified 2→1 kinematics systematically shifts the antishadowing peak of the nuclear modification factor R_dAu toward larger rapidities, regardless of which gluon-shadowing parametrization is used, and that this kinematic choice affects the effective nuclear absorption cross section needed to fit PHENIX d+Au data.

## Method

The authors implemented a Monte-Carlo Glauber framework with Woods-Saxon nuclear density profiles and a Hulthen wavefunction for the deuteron, comparing three gluon-shadowing parametrizations (EKS98, EPS08, nDSg) under both intrinsic (2→1) and extrinsic (2→2) kinematics for J/ψ production. For the extrinsic scheme, they used the partonic differential cross section from Haberzettl et al. (2007) incorporating s-channel cut contributions, and computed nuclear modification factors R_dAu, R_CP, R_AuAu, and R_CuCu as functions of rapidity, centrality, and transverse momentum, then compared these to PHENIX and STAR data.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section at √s_NN = 200 GeV: σ_NN = 42 mb
- Maximum nucleon density: ρ_0 = 0.17 nucleons/fm³
- The difference between forward and mid-rapidity R_AA is approximately 20% for rather central Au+Au collisions when σ_abs = 0
- EPS08 and EKS98 gluon shadowing differ by a factor of 3 at Q² = 1.69 GeV², but the difference is less than 20% at Q² ≈ M_J/ψ²
- STAR preliminary measurement of R_dAu in |y| < 0.5 for 0-20% central collisions: R_dAu = 1.45 ± 0.60
- The effective absorption cross section that best describes the trend of R_CP data lies in the range of 2-4 mb

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Ferreiro et al., 2008, 2009) on how gluon shadowing depends on the partonic process producing the c-cbar pair, and contradicts the simplified 2→1 kinematics used in earlier shadowing studies (Eskola et al., 1998, 2008; de Florian et al., 2003). The results enable more accurate subtraction of cold nuclear matter effects from J/ψ suppression measurements in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions, which is essential for interpreting quark-gluon plasma signatures, and provide predictions for the transverse-momentum dependence of nuclear modification factors that cannot be obtained with the usual simplified kinematics.
