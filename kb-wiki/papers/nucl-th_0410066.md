# Isospin Asymmetry in Nuclei and Neutron Stars

> The roles of isospin asymmetry in nuclei and neutron stars are investigated using a range of potential and field-theoretical models of nucleonic matter. The parameters of these models are fixed by fitting the properties of homogeneous bulk matter and closed-shell nuclei. We discuss and unravel the causes of correlations among the neutron skin thickness in heavy nuclei, the pressure of beta-equilibrated matter at a density of 0.1 fm$^{-3}$, the derivative of the nuclear symmetry energy at the same density and the radii of moderate mass neutron stars. Constraints on the symmetry properties of nuclear matter from the binding energies of nuclei are examined. The extent to which forthcoming neutron skin measurements will further delimit the symmetry properties is investigated. The impact of symmetry energy constraints for the mass and moment of inertia contained within neutron star crusts and the threshold density for the nucleon direct Urca process, all of which are potentially measurable, is explored. We also comment on the minimum neutron star radius, assuming that only nucleonic matter exists within the star.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that correlations among the neutron skin thickness in heavy nuclei, the pressure of beta-equilibrated matter at 0.1 fm$^{-3}$, the derivative of the nuclear symmetry energy at the same density, and the radii of moderate mass neutron stars arise from the density dependence of the isospin asymmetric part of the nuclear interaction, but these correlations are model-dependent and constrained by the assumption that only nucleonic matter exists within the star.

## Method

The authors employed both Skyrme-type potential models and Walecka-type field-theoretical models of nucleonic matter, with parameters fixed by fitting properties of homogeneous bulk matter and closed-shell nuclei. They performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations for potential models and Hartree calculations for field-theoretical models, including a fit to the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) equation of state. Semi-infinite nucleonic matter was analyzed variationally to determine surface properties and neutron skin thickness, and these results were connected to neutron star properties through the symmetry energy density dependence.

## Key numbers

- The APR equation of state has saturation density $n_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, energy per nucleon $E/A = -16$ MeV, compressibility $K = 266$ MeV, and symmetry energy $E_{sym}(n_0) = 32.6$ MeV
- The neutron skin thickness $\delta R$ for $^{208}$Pb from medium-energy proton scattering is constrained to $0.07 < \delta R < 0.16$ fm
- Antiprotonic atom data give $\delta R = 0.15 \pm 0.02$ fm for $^{208}$Pb
- The volume symmetry energy $S_v$ lies in the range 25-35 MeV
- The net asymmetry $I = (N-Z)/(N+Z)$ in stable nuclei ranges up to about 0.24, while in neutron star interiors $I$ could be as large as 0.95

## Context

This work extends the correlations discovered by Lattimer and Prakash (2000, 2001) between neutron star radius and pressure, by Typel and Brown (2000, 2001) between neutron skin thickness and pure neutron matter pressure, and by Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001) between neutron skin thickness and neutron star radius. The paper enables improved predictions for neutron star radii, crust properties, and the direct Urca threshold density, and highlights how forthcoming parity-violating electron scattering measurements of the neutron skin in $^{208}$Pb at Jefferson Laboratory will further constrain the symmetry energy.
