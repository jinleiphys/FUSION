# Pion dispersion relation at finite temperature in the linear sigma model from chiral Ward identities

> We develop one-loop effective vertices and propagators in the linear sigma model at finite temperature satisfying the chiral Ward identities. We use these in turn to compute the pion dispersion relation in a pion medium for small momentum and temperatures on the order of the pion mass at next to leading order in the parameter m_pi^2/4pi^2f_pi^2 and to zeroth order in the parameter (m_pi/m_sigma)^2. We show that this expansion reproduces the result obtained from chiral perturbation theory at leading order. The main effect is a perturbative, temperature-dependent increase of the pion mass. We find no qualitative changes in the pion dispersion curve with respect to the leading order behavior in this kinematical regime.

## Key claim
The linear sigma model, when expanded to zeroth order in \((m_\pi/m_\sigma)^2\) and next-to-leading order in \(m_\pi^2/4\pi^2 f_\pi^2\), reproduces the leading-order chiral perturbation theory result for the pion dispersion relation at finite temperature; the main effect is a perturbative, temperature-dependent increase of the pion mass with no qualitative change in the dispersion curve shape for small momentum and temperatures on the order of the pion mass.

## Method
The authors constructed one-loop effective vertices and propagators in the linear sigma model at finite temperature that satisfy chiral Ward identities. They then computed the one-loop modification to the pion self-energy in a pion medium, keeping only zeroth-order terms in \((m_\pi/m_\sigma)^2\) and next-to-leading order terms in \(m_\pi^2/4\pi^2 f_\pi^2\). The pion dispersion relation was obtained by solving \(P^2 + m_\pi^2 + \text{Re}\,\Pi(P)=0\) after analytical continuation to Minkowski space, using the imaginary-time formalism of thermal field theory.

## Key numbers
- The expansion parameter is \(\xi = m_\pi^2 / (4\pi^2 f_\pi^2) \ll 1\).
- At leading order, the dispersion relation is \(p_0^2 = p^2 + m_\pi^2 [1 + \xi\, g(T/m_\pi)]\), where \(g(T/m_\pi) \sim 1\) for \(T \sim m_\pi\).
- The next-to-leading order correction to the pion mass squared includes a term \(\frac{9}{2} \xi^2\, g(T/m_\pi)\, h(T/m_\pi)\, m_\pi^2\), where \(h(T/m_\pi)\) is a dimensionless function defined by an integral over the Bose-Einstein distribution.
- The full dispersion relation up to next-to-leading order for \(T \sim m_\pi\) is \(p_0^2 = p^2 + \{1 + \xi g(T/m_\pi) + \frac{\xi^2}{2} g(T/m_\pi)[9h(T/m_\pi) - 4g(T/m_\pi)]\} m_\pi^2 + \xi^2 \tilde{S}(p_0,p)\), with \(\tilde{S}\) a numerically evaluated function.
- The sigma meson mass is taken on the order of 600 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the connection between the linear sigma model and chiral perturbation theory (Gasser, 1980s) at finite temperature, showing that the linear sigma model yields the same leading-order result for the pion mass shift. It builds on prior calculations of the pion dispersion relation at second order (Goity, 1990s) and on the renormalization of the linear sigma model at finite temperature (Mohan, 1990s). The formalism enables future exploration of the pion dispersion curve at large momentum, where lowest-order chiral perturbation theory cannot be applied, and could be extended to include effects of high nucleonic density.
