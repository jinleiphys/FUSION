# Anomalous fluctuations of s-wave reduced neutron widths of $^{192,194}$Pt resonances

> We obtained an unprecedentedly large number of s-wave neutron widths through R-matrix analysis of neutron cross-section measurements on enriched Pt samples. Careful analysis of these data rejects the validity of the Porter-Thomas distribution with a statistical significance of at least 99.997%.

## Key claim

The s-wave reduced neutron widths of \(^{192,194}\)Pt resonances deviate from the Porter-Thomas distribution with a combined statistical significance of at least 99.997%, contradicting the long-standing consensus that such widths follow this distribution. The authors conclude that the assumption that the widths are statistical is violated, as the observed departure points toward a smaller degrees-of-freedom parameter \(\nu \approx 0.5\) rather than the expected \(\nu = 1\).

## Method

Neutron total and capture cross sections were measured on enriched Pt samples at the Oak Ridge Electron Linear Accelerator (ORELA) facility. Resonance parameters were extracted via R-matrix analysis using the code SAMMY, and an energy-dependent threshold of the form \(T_0 = a_0 E_n^{3/2}\) was applied to minimize p-wave contamination. Maximum-likelihood estimation and additional statistical tests (Z and Z² statistics) were used to compare the observed distribution of reduced neutron widths to the Porter-Thomas distribution.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{192}\)Pt: maximum-likelihood estimate of the degrees-of-freedom parameter \(\widehat{\nu}_{\mathrm{exp}} = 0.57^{+0.16}_{-0.15}\) (from 153 resonances up to \(E_{\mathrm{max}} = 4.98\) keV).
- For \(^{194}\)Pt: \(\widehat{\nu}_{\mathrm{exp}} = 0.47^{+0.19}_{-0.18}\) (from 161 resonances up to \(E_{\mathrm{max}} = 15.93\) keV).
- For \(^{196}\)Pt: \(\widehat{\nu}_{\mathrm{exp}} = 0.60^{+0.28}_{-0.26}\) (from 68 resonances up to \(E_{\mathrm{max}} = 15.99\) keV).
- Statistical significance for rejecting the Porter-Thomas distribution: \(S = 0.9970\) for \(^{192}\)Pt and \(S = 0.9975\) for \(^{194}\)Pt.
- Combined probability that the Porter-Thomas distribution is valid for both isotopes: less than \(1.2 \times 10^{-5}\).
- Probability of p-wave contamination among the s-wave observables: 0.069% for \(^{192}\)Pt and 0.0047% for \(^{194}\)Pt.

## Context

This work extends tests of the Porter-Thomas distribution (Porter and Thomas, 1956) and random matrix theory (Guhr, 1998) to a new, high-statistics data set, challenging the results of the previously best test (Bohr and Mottelson, 1983) which the authors argue had data and analysis problems (Koehler, 2009). The observed deviation toward \(\nu \approx 0.5\) suggests that the \(^{193,195}\)Pt nuclei may display regular rather than chaotic behavior near the neutron threshold, and it enables further theoretical work on the form-invariance assumption (Krieger, 1963) and the role of collectivity (Alhassid and Whelan, 1992).
