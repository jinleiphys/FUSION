# Pauli rearrangement potential for a scattering state with the interaction in chiral effective field theory

> The Pauli rearrangement potential given by the second-order diagram is evaluated for a nucleon optical model potential (OMP) with $G$ matrices of the nucleon-nucleon interaction in chiral effective field theory. The results obtained in nuclear matter are applied for $^{40}$Ca in a local-density approximation. The repulsive effect is of the order of 5MeV at the normal density. The density dependence indicates that the real part of the microscopic OMP becomes shallower in a central region, but is barely affected in a surface area. This improves the overall resemblance of the microscopic OMP to the empirical one.

# Pauli rearrangement potential for a scattering state with the interaction in chiral effective field theory

## Key claim
The second-order Pauli rearrangement potential for a nucleon optical model potential (OMP) is evaluated using chiral effective field theory interactions, and its inclusion improves the overall resemblance of the microscopic OMP to the empirical one, though the effect is mainly in the central region and barely affects the surface area where elastic scattering is determined.

## Method
The Pauli rearrangement potential given by the second-order diagram (Fig. 1a) was evaluated in symmetric nuclear matter using G-matrix calculations with chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions at N3LO including N2LO three-nucleon forces (3NFs) via a normal-ordering prescription. The results were applied to finite nuclei (40Ca) using a local-density approximation, where the Fermi momentum was replaced by a local quantity derived from the density distribution obtained from Hartree-Fock wave functions with the Gogny D1S effective force.

## Key numbers
- The Pauli rearrangement potential is of the order of 5 MeV at normal density (rho_0 = 0.166 fm^-3, corresponding to k_F = 1.35 fm^-1).
- The rearrangement potential in nuclear matter is 5-10 MeV at the normal density.
- Calculations were performed for five Fermi momenta: 0.8, 1.07, 1.2, 1.35, and 1.5 fm^-1, corresponding to densities 0.21 rho_0, 0.5 rho_0, 0.70 rho_0, rho_0, and 1.37 rho_0 respectively.
- The 3NF effect makes the potential shallower by about 10 MeV, and the Pauli rearrangement effect provides an additional repulsive potential of comparable magnitude.
- The Pauli rearrangement effect makes the OMP shallower in the central region by 5-10 MeV for 40Ca at incident energies of 65 MeV and 100 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the microscopic derivation of the OMP by Toyokawa et al. (2018) and Toyokawa et al. (2015) using ChEFT NN and 3NF forces, and follows the nuclear matter approach developed by Jeukenne, Lejeune, and Mahaux (1974, 1976, 1977). The Pauli rearrangement contribution had been left out in recent microscopic OMPs by Amos et al. (2000), Furumoto et al. (2008), and Toyokawa et al. (2018), and this paper demonstrates that its inclusion improves correspondence with the empirical OMP of Koning and Delaroche (2003). The results also suggest that the additional repulsive effect may help improve descriptions of nucleus-nucleus scattering, where higher-density regions participate.
