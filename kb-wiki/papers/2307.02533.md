# Correlations of $C$ and $CP$ violation in $η\to π^0\ell^+\ell^-$ and $η'\to η\ell^+\ell^-$

> Based on recent progress in the systematic analysis of $C$ and $CP$ violation in the light-meson sector, we calculate the $C$-odd transition amplitudes $η\toπ^0\ell^+\ell^-$ and $η'\toη\ell^+\ell^-$. Focusing on long-distance contributions driven by the lowest-lying hadronic intermediate states, we work out the correlations between these beyond-the-Standard-Model signals and the Dalitz-plot asymmetries in $η\rightarrow π^0 π^+ π^-$ and $η' \rightarrow ηπ^+ π^- $, using dispersion theory.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the C-odd transition amplitudes for η→π0ℓ+ℓ− and η′→ηℓ+ℓ−, focusing on long-distance contributions from hadronic intermediate states, and works out correlations between these beyond-the-Standard-Model signals and Dalitz-plot asymmetries in η→π0π+π− and η′→ηπ+π− using dispersion theory, under the assumption that any observable signal would be dominated by a C-odd single-photon mechanism rather than the Standard Model two-photon contribution.

## Method

The authors decompose the C- and CP-violating transition form factors for X→Yγ* into isovector and isoscalar parts. For the isovector part, they use dispersion relations with a unitarity cut through the two-pion intermediate state, combining the P-wave projection of the C-odd X→Yπ+π− amplitudes (obtained from Khuri-Treiman framework) with the pion vector form factor. For the isoscalar part, they employ a vector-meson-dominance model with ρ, ω, and φ mesons, extracting couplings by analytic continuation of the three-body amplitudes to the second Riemann sheet and relating them via SU(3) symmetry and naive dimensional analysis.

## Key numbers

- Standard Model predictions for branching ratios: B(η→π0e+e−) = 1.36(15)×10−9, B(η→π0μ+μ−) = 0.67(7)×10−9, B(η′→ηe+e−) = 0.50(4)×10−9, B(η′→ημ+μ−) = 0.240(21)×10−9 (from Schafer (2023))
- Experimental upper limits: B(η→π0e+e−) < 7.5×10−6 (WASA-at-COSY, 2018), B(η→π0μ+μ−) < 5×10−6 (Dzhelyadin, 1980), B(η′→ηe+e−) < 2.4×10−3 (CLEO, 1999), B(η′→ημ+μ−) < 1.5×10−5 (Dzhelyadin, 1980)
- The ratio of direct photonic to semi-leptonic form factors is estimated as F2(s)/F1(s) ∼ αv/(4πF0) ≈ 1.5
- Vector meson masses used: Mω = 782.66 MeV, Mρ = 763.7 MeV (pole value from Garcia-Martin (2011))

## Context

This work extends the systematic analysis of C and CP violation in the light-meson sector initiated by Gardner (2019) for η→π0π+π− and further developed by Akdag (2021, 2023) for η′→π0π+π− and η′→ηπ+π− using dispersion-theoretical Khuri-Treiman framework. It builds on the derivation of C- and CP-violating quark-level operators in low-energy effective field theory and their matching onto T-odd and P-even chiral perturbation theory by Akdag (2022). The results enable future experimental searches at facilities like REDTOP to constrain or discover C and CP violation in these radiative decays, with the correlations between different decay channels providing a way to distinguish underlying operator structures.
