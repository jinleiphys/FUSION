# Systematic study of $\mathcalα$ decay half-lives based on Gamow--like model with a screened electrostatic barrier

> In the present work we systematically study $\mathcalα$ decay half-lives of $Z>51$ nuclei using the modified Gamow-like model which includes the effects of the centrifugal potential and electrostatic shielding. For the case of even-even nuclei, this model contains two adjustable parameters: the parameter $a$ related to the screened electrostatic barrier and the radius constant $r_0$, while for the case of odd-odd and odd-A nuclei, it is added a new parameter i.e. hindrance factor $h$ which is used to describe the effect of an odd-proton and/or an odd-neutron. Our calculations can well reproduce the experimental data. In addition, we use this modified Gamow-like model to predict the $\mathcalα$-decay half-lives of seven even-even nuclei with $Z=120$ and some un-synthesized nuclei on their $\mathcalα$ decay chains.

## Key claim

The modified Gamow-like model, which adds a screened electrostatic barrier (Hulthen potential) and centrifugal potential to the original Gamow-like model of Pomorski (2013), reproduces experimental alpha decay half-lives for Z>51 nuclei with standard deviations reduced by 24-39% compared to the original model, contingent on fitting three adjustable parameters (r0, a, and hindrance factor h) to the NUBASE2016 database.

## Method

The authors modified the Gamow-like model by replacing the pure Coulomb potential with a Hulthen-type screened electrostatic potential V_h(r) = a Z_d Z_alpha e^2 / (e^{ar} - 1) and adding a Langer-modified centrifugal potential V_l(r) = hbar^2 (l+1/2)^2 / (2 mu r^2). For even-even nuclei, two parameters (radius constant r0 and screening parameter a) were fitted to 169 experimental half-lives; for odd-A and odd-odd nuclei, a hindrance factor h was additionally fitted to 292 nuclei. The model was then applied to predict half-lives for seven Z=120 superheavy nuclei and their decay chains, comparing results against six other models (CPPM-Bass73, VSS, UNIV, Royer, UDL, NRDX).

## Key numbers

* Fitted parameters: r0 = 1.14 fm, a = 7.8 x 10^{-4}, h = 0.3455 (for odd-proton or odd-neutron nuclei; h = 0.691 for odd-odd nuclei)
* Standard deviation sigma_1 for even-even nuclei: 0.348 (compared to sigma_2 = 0.487 for the original Gamow-like model), representing a 28.5% improvement
* Standard deviation sigma_1 for odd-proton (even-Z, odd-N) nuclei: 0.681 (sigma_2 = 0.967), a 29.6% improvement
* Standard deviation sigma_1 for odd-neutron (odd-Z, even-N) nuclei: 0.598 (sigma_2 = 0.789), a 24.2% improvement
* Standard deviation sigma_1 for odd-odd nuclei: 0.748 (sigma_2 = 1.235), a 39.4% improvement
* Predicted half-life for 296120: log10(T_1/2) = -5.662 s (Q_alpha = 13.187 MeV)
* Predicted half-life for 308120: log10(T_1/2) = -5.559 s (Q_alpha = 13.036 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the Gamow-like model of Pomorski (2013) by incorporating electrostatic shielding effects previously studied by Wan (2016) and the Hulthen potential approach used by Budaca and Budaca (2017) for proton emission. The model enables predictions for un-synthesized superheavy nuclei with Z=120, complementing other approaches such as the Viola-Seaborg-Sobiczewski formula, the Universal decay law of Qi (2009), and the Royer formula. The predictions for seven Z=120 decay chains provide guidance for future experimental searches at superheavy element facilities.
