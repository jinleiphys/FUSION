# Chiral Soliton Model vs Pentaquark Structure for Θ(1540)

> The exotic baryon $Θ^+$ (1540 MeV)$ is visualised as an expected (iso) rotational excitation in the Chiral Soliton Model. It is also argued as a Pentaquark baryon state in a constituent quark model with strong diquark correlations. I contrast the two points of view; the similarities and differences between the two pictures. Collective excitation, characteristic of Chiral Soliton Model points toward small mixing of representations in the wake of SU(3) breaking. In contrast, Constituent quark Models prefer near ``ideal'' mixing, similar to $ω- ϕ$ mixing.

# Chiral Soliton Model vs Pentaquark Structure for Θ(1540)

## Key claim

The Θ⁺(1540) can be understood either as a rotational excitation in the Chiral Soliton Model (CSM) or as a pentaquark state in a constituent quark model with strong diquark correlations; however, the two pictures make sharply different predictions for the mixing between SU(3) representations when SU(3) flavor symmetry is broken, with CSM favoring small mixing angles and constituent quark models favoring near-ideal mixing analogous to ω-φ mixing.

## Method

The paper contrasts the two theoretical frameworks by deriving the mass spectrum and decay widths of the Θ⁺ and its SU(3) multiplet partners. For the Chiral Soliton Model, the author uses the rigid-rotor quantization of the Skyrmion, computing the Hamiltonian with moments of inertia ℐ₁ and ℐ₂, and extracts mixing angles from both the mass spectrum (using Gell-Mann–Okubo mass formulas) and from decay widths (using SU(3)-symmetric couplings with next-to-leading-order 1/N_c corrections). For the constituent quark model, the author analyzes the Jaffe-Wilczek diquark-diquark-antiquark picture, where ideal mixing is predicted based on the number of strange quarks in each state.

## Key numbers

- The average masses of the SU(3) multiplets used to determine moments of inertia: 1115 MeV for {8}_{1/2}, 1382 MeV for {10}_{3/2}, and 1755 MeV for {\(\overline{10}\)}_{1/2}
- From the Θ⁺(1540) and Ξ₃/₂(1862) masses, the equal-spacing parameter a' = 107 MeV for the antidecouplet
- The mixing angle for nucleon-like states from the mass spectrum gives tan² θ_N = 0.087 (cos 2θ_N = 0.84), corresponding to δ = 220 MeV for the representation mixing matrix element
- The mixing angle for Σ-like states from the mass spectrum gives cos 2θ_Σ = 0.63, corresponding to δ = 264 MeV
- From decay data, comparing Γ(N(1710)→Δπ) ≈ 5 MeV with Γ(Δ(1232)→Nπ) = 120 MeV yields tan² θ_N = 0.0035, much smaller than the value from the mass spectrum
- For ideal mixing in the constituent quark model, tan θ = √2, which is incompatible with the decay data

## Context

This paper contrasts the Chiral Soliton Model prediction by Diakanov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) with the diquark-correlated pentaquark model of Jaffe and Wilczek (2003) for the exotic Θ⁺(1540) baryon. The work extends earlier Skyrmion analyses by Sriram, Mani, and Ramachandran (1984) and Guadagnini (1984), and contrasts with the analysis of Pakvasa and Suzuki (2004), who used the Roper resonance N(1440) as the octet nucleon and found larger mixing angles. The paper identifies that future experiments should distinguish the two pictures by searching for the additional J^P = 3/2⁺ states predicted by the pentaquark model (with mass splitting comparable to M_Δ − M_N ≈ 293 MeV) versus the radial excitations predicted by the chiral soliton model.
