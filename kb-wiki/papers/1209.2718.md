# Pure Neutron Matter Constraints and Nuclear Symmetry Energy

> In this review, we will discuss the results of our recent work to study the general optimization of the pure isovector parameters of the popular relativistic mean-field (RMF) and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) nuclear energy-density functionals (EDFs), using constraints on the pure neutron matter (PNM) equation of state (EoS) from recent {\sl ab initio} calculations. By using RMF and SHF parameterizations that give equivalent predictions for ground-state properties of doubly magic nuclei and properties of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and PNM, we found that such optimization leads to broadly consistent symmetry energy $J$ and its slope parameter $L$ at saturation density within a tight range of $σ(J) < 2$ MeV and $σ(L) < 6$ MeV. We demonstrate that a clear model dependence shows up (a) in the curvature parameter of the symmetry energy $K_{\rm sym}$, (b) the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, and (c) the radius of neutron stars.

## Key claim

After optimizing the two pure isovector parameters of RMF and SHF models to match *ab initio* pure neutron matter (PNM) calculations, the symmetry energy \(J\) and its slope \(L\) at saturation density become broadly consistent across models within \(\sigma(J) < 2\) MeV and \(\sigma(L) < 6\) MeV, but a clear model dependence remains in the curvature parameter \(K_{\rm sym}\), the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, and neutron star radii.

## Method

The authors used linear regression and covariance analysis to optimize the two pure isovector parameters of RMF models (\(g_\rho\) and \(\Lambda_{\rm v}\)) and SHF models (\(x_0\) and \(x_3\)) against microscopic PNM equation-of-state calculations from auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) and chiral effective field theory, over the density range \(0.04 \leq \rho \leq 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\). They kept all other parameters fixed to preserve predictions for ground-state properties of doubly magic nuclei and symmetric nuclear matter (SNM). They then propagated the 1\(\sigma\) uncertainties on the isovector parameters to symmetry energy parameters, neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb, and neutron star radii.

## Key numbers

- After PNM optimization, the RMF model gives \(J = 30.2\)–\(31.4\) MeV and \(L = 36.1\)–\(59.3\) MeV; the SHF model gives \(J = 30.1\)–\(33.2\) MeV and \(L = 28.5\)–\(64.4\) MeV (Table 1).
- For the IU-FSU parametrization after optimization: \(J = 31.4 \pm 0.7\) MeV, \(L = 52.9 \pm 2.0\) MeV, \(K_{\rm sym} = -6.8 \pm 12.9\) MeV (Table 3).
- For the SkIU-FSU parametrization after optimization: \(J = 31.4 \pm 0.9\) MeV, \(L = 48.0 \pm 6.2\) MeV, \(K_{\rm sym} = -130.2 \pm 13.3\) MeV (Table 3).
- Neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb after optimization: RMF predicts \(0.18 \pm 0.01\) fm; SHF predicts \(0.16 \pm 0.01\) fm (Table 3).
- The original NL3* parametrization predicted a neutron skin of 0.29 fm; after PNM optimization it became \(0.18 \pm 0.01\) fm.
- The original IU-FSU and SkIU-FSU both predicted \(R_{\rm skin} = 0.16\) fm before optimization.
- The experimental PREX result for the neutron skin of lead is \(0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18}\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the program of constraining nuclear energy-density functionals using *ab initio* PNM calculations from Gandolfi (2009, 2011), Hebeler (2009), and Akmal (1998). It contrasts with earlier RMF and SHF parameter sets (e.g., NL3*, IU-FSU) that gave widely varying symmetry energy predictions before PNM optimization. The results show that low-density PNM constraints alone cannot resolve model-dependent predictions for neutron star radii, motivating further constraints from heavy-ion collision experiments (Danielewicz 2002, Li 2008) and neutron star observations (Lattimer 2006, Steiner 2004).
