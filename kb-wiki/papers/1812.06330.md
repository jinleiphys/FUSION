# Resonance production in high energy collisions from small to big systems

> The aim of this paper is to understand resonance production (and more generally particle production) for different collision systems, namely proton-proton (pp), proton-nucleus (pA), and nucleus-nucleus (AA) scattering at the LHC. We will investigate in particular particle yields and ratios versus multiplicity, using the same multiplicity definition for the three different systems, in order to analyse in a compact way the evolution of particle production with the system size and the origin of a very different system size dependence of the different particles.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the multiplicity dependence of particle ratios across pp, pA, and AA collisions can be understood within a single framework (EPOS3) using a core-corona separation, where the relative core fraction increases continuously from zero at low multiplicity to unity at high multiplicity, producing universal curves independent of system size.

## Method
The authors employ the EPOS3 model, which uses a Gribov-Regge multiple scattering approach with parton ladders (Pomerons) and includes non-linear effects via a dynamical saturation scale. The core-corona procedure separates high-density regions treated with viscous hydrodynamics (eta/s=0.08) from low-density string segments that hadronize directly, followed by a hadronic cascade via UrQMD. Particle yields and ratios are studied as a function of average charged-particle multiplicity per pseudorapidity interval at central pseudorapidity, using the same multiplicity definition for pp, pA, and AA systems at LHC energies.

## Key numbers
- Hadronic phase lifetime increases continuously from less than 1 fm/c to 10 fm/c for big systems (AA), as shown in Figure 6(b).
- Phi meson lifetime: 46.2 fm/c, resulting in few decays in the hadronic phase.
- Xi* resonance lifetime: 21.7 fm/c.
- K0* resonance lifetime: 4.2 fm/c, causing particles to decay within the hadronic stage.
- Lambda* resonance lifetime: 12.6 fm/c.
- Sigma* resonance lifetime: 5 fm/c, with competing decay and resonance creation effects.
- Delta++ resonance lifetime: 1.7 fm/c.
- Core and corona contributions for Omega baryons are separated by about a factor of 10 in the Omega/pion ratio.
- Core and corona contributions for kaons are separated by roughly a factor of 2 in the kaon/pion ratio.

## Context
This work extends the core-corona approach, first described in earlier EPOS3 publications (Werner et al., 2013), to small collision systems (pp and pA) in addition to AA, using the same multiplicity definition across systems. It enables systematic comparison of resonance production mechanisms across system sizes, showing that the multiplicity dependence of particle ratios is driven by the changing core fraction rather than system size itself. The results are compared to ALICE data from multiple publications (2010-2016) and provide a framework for interpreting future measurements of short-lived resonances as probes of the hadronic phase lifetime.
