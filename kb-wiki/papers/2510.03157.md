# Application of the holographic equations of state for modeling experiments on heavy ion collisions

> In this paper, we propose a method for numerical modeling of the nuclear matter properties within the framework of relativistic heavy-ion collisions using a holographic equation of state. Machine learning methods were applied to address the regression and optimization issues during the calibration of the relevant parameters using the LQCD results for quark masses that approximate the physical values. Numerical simulations are performed using the iEBE-MUSIC and vHLLE-SMASH frameworks, which incorporate certain relativistic hydrodynamics solvers. We modify the code by implementing a tabulated holographic equation of state, enabling simulations of quark-gluon plasma evolution with dynamically generated initial conditions via the 3D Monte Carlo Glauber Model and SMASH. Finally, the spectra of produced hadrons are computed using a hybrid iSS+UrQMD and Hadron Sampler+SMASH approaches at the freeze-out stage.12 p

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that holographic equations of state (EoS) calibrated using machine learning and lattice QCD results can be integrated into relativistic hydrodynamics frameworks to produce transverse mass spectra comparable to those from lattice-based EoS, but the overall scale in hydrodynamic simulations is highly sensitive to initial conditions, preventing direct quantitative comparison with experimental data.

## Method

The authors calibrate isotropic and anisotropic holographic soft-wall AdS/QCD models by fitting the entropy density ratio \(s/T^3\) and quark susceptibility to lattice QCD results from the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov et al., 2014) using a two-stage machine learning approach: first a neural network regression on lattice data, then gradient descent optimization of the holographic model parameters. The calibrated tabulated holographic EoS are implemented in the iEBE-MUSIC and vHLLE-SMASH hybrid frameworks, which use 3D Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions or SMASH transport for initial conditions, relativistic hydrodynamics, particle sampling via iSS or Hadron Sampler, and final-state hadron transport with UrQMD or SMASH.

## Context

This work extends the holographic EoS approach of Arefieva's group (2018-2021) and the calibration method of Italian authors (2020) by applying machine learning for parameter optimization and implementing the EoS in full hybrid hydrodynamic+transport frameworks (iEBE-MUSIC and vHLLE-SMASH). The results are compared with the NEOS EoS (Monnai et al., 2019) for MUSIC and the AZ hydro EoS for vHLLE, using NA49 experimental data (Anticic et al., 2011) as reference. The paper enables future studies of QGP dynamics with holographic EoS that can describe the full QCD phase diagram including nonzero baryon chemical potential, though the authors note that more advanced observables and better hydrodynamic parameter tuning are needed for quantitative experimental comparison.
