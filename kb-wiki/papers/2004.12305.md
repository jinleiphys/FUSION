# Sequential hadronization in heavy ion collisions

> Heavy flavor supplies a chance to constrain and improve the hadronization mechanism. We have established a sequential coalescence model with charm conservation and applied it to the charmed hadron production in heavy ion collisions. The charm conservation enhances the earlier hadron production and suppresses the later production. This relative enhancement (suppression) changes significantly the ratios between charmed hadrons in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a sequential coalescence model with charm conservation, where charmed hadrons form at different temperatures according to their dissociation temperatures, significantly alters the ratios between charmed hadrons in heavy ion collisions compared to simultaneous production, but the model's agreement with experimental data is mixed (good for STAR data on Lambda_c+/D0, but preliminary ALICE data shows a large discrepancy).

## Method

The authors solve two- and three-body Dirac equations with a temperature-dependent potential (using free energy from lattice QCD) to obtain the binding energies and radii of charmed hadrons as functions of temperature, from which they determine each hadron's dissociation temperature. They then use a coalescence formula integrated over a hypersurface defined by when the local temperature drops below each hadron's dissociation temperature, with the Wigner function constructed from the hadron wavefunctions, and include a time-dependent charm quark number fraction to enforce charm conservation across the sequential hadronization.

## Key numbers

- The dissociation temperature sequence is: T_d^{J/psi} > T_d^{D_s} > T_d^{Omega_ccc} > T_d^{D0} > T_d^{Omega_cc, Xi_cc} > T_d^{Omega_c, Xi_c, Lambda_c} > T_d^{pi, K, N} approx T_c, where T_c is the confinement/deconfinement phase transition temperature
- The charm quark number fraction r_h = 1 for D_s, approximately 0.9 for other charmed mesons, and approximately 0.6 for charmed baryons
- The charm quark mass m_Q is much larger than the typical temperature of the hot medium at RHIC and LHC energies, with m_Q >> T ~ 500 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous sequential coalescence model (Zhao 2018) by applying it to charmed hadron production at both RHIC and LHC energies. It addresses open questions in coalescence models regarding energy conservation, entropy, coalescence probability, heavy quark number conservation, and hadronization sequence that were noted in prior work by Molnar (2003), Greco (2003), Fries (2003), and others. The model enables future inclusion of fragmentation contributions and space-momentum correlation, and the discrepancy with preliminary ALICE data on Lambda_c+/D0 ratio points to needed refinements in the treatment of charm quark thermalization or hadronization dynamics.
