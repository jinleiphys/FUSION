# Role of density profile of sub-GeV dark matter in the properties of dark matter admixed quark stars with Bayesian analysis of dark-NJL model

> We investigate the structural and oscillation properties of dark matter (DM) admixed strange quark stars (DMSQSs). The strange quark matter (SQM) is described with the well-known Nambu-Jona-Lasino (NJL) model and the self-interacting fermionic DM is included in a systematic manner. The self-interaction of DM is of four-Fermi type and the overall DM density is considered as a function of the baryon density of SQM with two free parameters ($α$, $ρ_{sc}$). This work is the first to consider four-Fermi interactions between fermionic DM and SQM in DMSQSs. Certain experiments like LZ, XENON, DarkSide, CRESST, and LHC have almost ruled out the possibility of contact interaction between SQM and massive DM (in GeV order). Recently, the quest for sub-GeV DM has garnered significant attention. We show that recent astrophysical constraints on the structural properties of compact stars also do not support the presence of massive DM in DMSQSs. On the other hand, we find sub-GeV DM to successfully concur with such observational constraints. We also calculate the fundamental $f$-mode frequency ($f_f$) of the DMSQSs, which shows universality with compactness, mean density, and tidal deformability. Further, we investigate the prospect of detection of $f_f$ with respect to the projected sensitivity of upcoming gravitational wave detectors like aLIGO, A+, Cosmic Explorer, and Einstein Telescope. In our DMSQS model, the three free parameters are $α$, $ρ_{sc}$, and the ratio of repulsive to attractive interaction in SQM ($G_V/G_S$), which are optimized by Bayesian analysis in light of various recent astrophysical data.

## Key claim

Sub-GeV dark matter (DM) with masses below 1 GeV can successfully satisfy current astrophysical constraints on the structural properties of compact stars when admixed with strange quark matter, whereas massive DM (GeV order) is ruled out by both direct detection experiments and astrophysical observations; this conclusion depends on modeling the DM density as a function of baryon density with two free parameters (α, ρ_sc) rather than assuming a constant DM density.

## Method

The authors describe strange quark matter (SQM) using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with the Hatsuda-Kunihiro parameter set and introduce fermionic DM with four-Fermi self-interactions and DM-SQM contact interactions, which they call the dark-NJL (DNJL) model. The DM number density is taken as a function of baryon density via ρ_χ = ρ_sc α (e^(ρ/ρ_sc) - 1), making the DM density profile variable with stellar radius. The DM model parameters (momentum cut-offs Λ_SD, Λ_VD, Λ_SqD, Λ_VqD) are fixed using the DM self-interaction bound σ/m_χ = 1 cm²/gm and relic density constraints from MicrOMEGAs, and the three free parameters (G_V/G_S, α, ρ_sc) are optimized by Bayesian analysis against astrophysical data including PSR J0740+6620, GW170817, NICER data, and HESS J1731-347.

## Key numbers

- DM masses considered: 5 × 10³ MeV, 100 MeV, 125 MeV, and 150 MeV (sub-GeV range)
- DM self-interaction cross section fixed to σ/m_χ = 1 cm²/gm
- Momentum cut-offs for DM self-interaction: Λ_SD = 0.108 GeV and Λ_VD = 0.134 GeV for m_χ = 5 × 10³ MeV; Λ_SD = 0.041 GeV and Λ_VD = 0.050 GeV for m_χ = 100 MeV
- DM-quark interaction cut-offs: Λ_SqD^max = 295.0 GeV and Λ_VqD^max = 345.0 GeV for m_χ = 5 × 10³ MeV; Λ_SqD^max = 3.06 GeV and Λ_VqD^max = 4.0 GeV for m_χ = 100 MeV
- NJL model parameters: m_u,d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 135.7 MeV, Λ = 631.4 MeV, G_S Λ² = 3.67, K Λ⁵ = 9.29
- Two values of G_V/G_S considered: 0.3 (consistent with lattice QCD) and 0.5
- Reference number density ρ_sc initially taken as 1 fm⁻³ for α constraint studies

## Context

This work extends previous studies of DM admixed compact stars that assumed constant DM density (e.g., Panotopoulos (2017), Guha (2021), Sen (2022)) by introducing a density-dependent DM profile that avoids thermodynamic inconsistency as noted by Hajkarim (2025) and follows the suggestion of Kumar (2025) that gravitational effects lead to variable DM density. It is the first to consider four-Fermi interactions between fermionic DM and SQM in DMSQSs, and it enables future gravitational wave detection prospects by calculating f-mode frequencies and comparing with sensitivities of aLIGO, A+, Cosmic Explorer, and Einstein Telescope.
