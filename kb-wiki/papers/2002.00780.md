# Beryllium-9 in Cluster Effective Field Theory

> We study the 9 Be ground-state energy with non-local $α-$n and $α-α$ potentials derived from Cluster Effective Field Theory. The short-distance dependence of the interaction is regulated with a momentum cutoff. The potential parameters are fitted to reproduce the scattering length and effective range. We implement such potential models in a Non-Symmetrized Hyperspherical Harmonics (NSHH) code in momentum space. In addition we calculate ground state energies of various alpha nuclei. Work is in progress on a calculation of the photodisintegration of 9Be with the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that non-local alpha-neutron and alpha-alpha potentials derived from Cluster Effective Field Theory, when implemented in a Non-Symmetrized Hyperspherical Harmonics code in momentum space, can reproduce the experimental ground-state energy of Beryllium-9 for selected cutoff values, but the results show a strong cutoff dependence that indicates the need for three-body forces.

## Method

The authors constructed non-local momentum-space potentials for the alpha-neutron and alpha-alpha interactions using a Cluster Effective Field Theory expansion up to effective-range order, with parameters fitted to reproduce experimental scattering lengths and effective ranges. The short-distance behavior was regulated with a Gaussian momentum cutoff. These potentials were implemented in a Non-Symmetrized Hyperspherical Harmonics (NSHH) basis in momentum space, where the Hamiltonian was diagonalized to obtain ground-state energies of Beryllium-9 and various alpha-cluster nuclei (Carbon-12, Oxygen-16, Neon-20, Magnesium-24).

## Key numbers

- Beryllium-9 three-body cluster binding energy (alpha-alpha-neutron separation energy): 1.572 MeV
- Alpha particle proton separation energy S_p(He-4): approximately 19.813 MeV
- Alpha-neutron system resonance energy (He-5 decay): 0.798 MeV with width 0.648 MeV
- Low-energy scales derived from power counting: M_lo approximately 50 MeV for alpha-neutron, approximately 20 MeV for alpha-alpha; M_hi approximately 170 MeV for both
- Precision estimates: O(M_lo/M_hi) approximately 0.3 for alpha-neutron, approximately 0.1 for alpha-alpha
- Wigner bound limits on cutoff: Lambda_MAX = 340 MeV for alpha-neutron, 230 MeV for alpha-alpha
- Beryllium-9 ground-state energy variation with cutoff: between 0 and -5 MeV
- HH quantum number K=11 sufficient for good convergence of Beryllium-9 ground state

## Context

This work extends the cluster-model approach to Beryllium-9 previously employed by Efros et al. (1998) using phenomenological local potentials and by Casal et al. using a three-body force, by instead using non-local potentials from Cluster Effective Field Theory with a more solid theoretical foundation. The approach enables future calculation of Beryllium-9 photodisintegration via the Lorentz Integral Transform method, which is relevant to understanding carbon nucleosynthesis through the alpha-alpha-neutron chain in supernovae as an alternative to the triple-alpha process.
