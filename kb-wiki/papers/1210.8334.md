# Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock Approach to Nuclear Pasta at Finite Temperature

> We present simulations of neutron-rich matter at subnuclear densities, like supernova matter, with the time-dependent Hartree-Fock approximation at temperatures of several MeV. The initial state consists of $α$ particles randomly distributed in space that have a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution in momentum space. Adding a neutron background initialized with Fermi distributed plane waves the calculations reflect a reasonable approximation of astrophysical matter. This matter evolves into spherical, rod-like, and slab-like shapes and mixtures thereof. The simulations employ a full Skyrme interaction in a periodic three-dimensional grid. By an improved morphological analysis based on Minkowski functionals, all eight pasta shapes can be uniquely identified by the sign of only two valuations, namely the Euler characteristic and the integral mean curvature. In addition, we propose the variance in the cell density distribution as a measure to distinguish pasta matter from uniform matter.

## Key claim

Time-dependent Hartree-Fock simulations of neutron-rich matter at subnuclear densities and temperatures of several MeV evolve into spherical, rod-like, slab-like, and bubble shapes, but the final shape can depend on the random initial conditions in some density-temperature regions where two configurations compete.

## Method

The authors performed fully three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations on a 16×16×16 grid with 1 fm spacing and periodic boundary conditions, using the Skyrme SLy6 interaction. Initial states consisted of randomly distributed alpha particles with a Maxwell-Boltzmann momentum distribution plus a neutron background with Fermi-distributed plane waves, at a proton fraction of 1/3. Each configuration was evolved for 1500 fm/c, and the resulting pasta shapes were classified using Minkowski functionals (volume, surface area, integral mean curvature, and Euler characteristic) computed as functions of a threshold density.

## Key numbers

- Simulation box size: 16 fm in each direction, with grid spacing of 1 fm
- Proton fraction: X_p = 1/3
- Time evolution: 1500 fm/c
- Temperature calibration relation: T ≈ 7 MeV + sqrt(T_init (T_init + 100 MeV))/6
- Threshold density range for stable Minkowski functional values at lowest temperatures: ρ_th ∈ [0.03 fm^{-3}, 0.08 fm^{-3}]
- Variance limit for distinguishing pasta from uniform matter: 2×10^{-4} fm^{-6}
- Transition density to uniform matter at T = 7 MeV: 9.5×10^{-2} fm^{-3}
- Transition density to uniform matter at T = 10 MeV: about 9.0×10^{-2} fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends previous static Hartree-Fock calculations of nuclear pasta (Newton, Stone; Magierski; Goegelein) and quantum molecular dynamics simulations (Sonoda 2008) by using a time-dependent mean-field approach that allows nucleons to explore multiple low-lying configurations. The TDHF results agree qualitatively with the phase diagram from QMD calculations (Sonoda 2008) and show similar rod(3) structures found earlier by Newton and Stone and by Sebille. The improved morphological analysis using Minkowski functionals, previously applied to pasta by Watanabe (2003, 2004) and Sonoda (2008), enables unique identification of all eight pasta shapes from the signs of just two quantities (Euler characteristic and integral mean curvature). The results enable future studies of neutrino cross-sections for the identified pasta shapes, which may affect supernova explosion mechanisms and protoneutron star cooling.
