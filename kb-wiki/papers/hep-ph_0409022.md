# Mass spectrum of pentaquarks

> We discuss the properties of the pentaquark in a collective stringlike model with a nonplanar configuration of the four quarks and the antiquark. In an application to the mass spectrum of exotic Theta baryons, we find that the ground state pentaquark has angular momentum and parity J(p)=1/2(-) and a small magnetic moment of 0.382 nuclear magnetons. The decay width is suppressed by the spatial overlap with the decay products.

## Key claim

The ground state pentaquark has angular momentum and parity J^p = 1/2^- and a small magnetic moment of 0.382 nuclear magnetons, but this result depends on the specific collective stringlike model with a nonplanar tetrahedral configuration of the four quarks and the antiquark, and the decay width is suppressed by the spatial overlap with the decay products.

## Method

The authors constructed a collective stringlike model for q^4\bar{q} pentaquarks in which the four quarks are placed at the corners of an equilateral tetrahedron and the antiquark at its center, using a mass-squared operator with vibrational, rotational, and spin-flavor terms. The spin-flavor part was expressed in a Gürsey-Radicati form using Casimir invariants of SU_sf(6) and its subgroups, with coefficients taken from a previous study of nonstrange and strange baryon resonances, and the constant M_0^2 was determined by identifying the ground state exotic pentaquark with the observed Θ(1540) resonance.

## Key numbers

- Ground state pentaquark mass: 1540 MeV (fitted to the observed Θ(1540) resonance)
- Ground state angular momentum and parity: J^p = 1/2^-
- Ground state magnetic moment of Θ^+: 0.382 μ_N (nuclear magnetons)
- First excited state (Θ_1, isospin triplet of the 27-plet): 1599 MeV with J^p = 1/2^-
- Lowest positive parity pentaquark state: 1668 MeV, belonging to the [51111]_{A_1} spin-flavor multiplet with J^p = 1/2^+, 3/2^+
- Additional predicted Θ pentaquark masses: 1661 MeV (^4\overline{10}), 1711 MeV (^2 35), 1717 MeV (^4 27), and 1723 MeV (^2 27)
- Magnetic moments of ground state antidecuplet members: N^0 = 0.108 μ_N, N^+ = 0.422 μ_N, Σ^- = -0.166 μ_N, Σ^0 = 0.148 μ_N, Σ^+ = 0.462 μ_N, Ξ^{--}_{3/2} = -0.440 μ_N, Ξ^{-}_{3/2} = -0.126 μ_N, Ξ^{0}_{3/2} = 0.188 μ_N, Ξ^{+}_{3/2} = 0.502 μ_N

## Context

This work extends the collective stringlike model previously developed for q^3 baryons by Bijker, Iachello, and Leviatan to the pentaquark system, and it agrees with QCD sum rules and lattice QCD calculations that predict a negative parity ground state, contrary to chiral soliton models, various cluster models, and a lattice calculation by Chiu that predict positive parity. The results enable experimental tests through measurement of the angular momentum and parity of the Θ(1540) and its excited states, which could distinguish between different theoretical approaches and clarify the relevant degrees of freedom for exotic baryons.
