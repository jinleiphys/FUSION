# Quantum fluctuations and stability of tetrahedral deformations in atomic nuclei

> The possible existence of stable axial octupole and tetrahedral deformations is investigated in $^{80}$Zr and $^{98}$Zr. HFBCS calculations with parity projection have been performed for various parametrizations of the Skyrme energy functional. The correlation and excitation energies of negative parity states associated with shape fluctuations have been obtained using the generator coordinate method (GCM). The results indicate that in these nuclei both the axial octupole and tetrahedral deformations are of dynamic character and possess similar characteristics. Various Skyrme forces give consistent results as a function of these two octupole degrees of freedom both at the mean-field level as well as for configuration mixing calculations.

## Key claim
The paper concludes that stable tetrahedral deformation is unlikely in $^{80}$Zr and $^{98}$Zr at the mean-field level; instead, both axial octupole and tetrahedral deformations are of dynamic character, with shape fluctuations playing a significant role and the tetrahedral configuration being only slightly more favored than the axial octupole one.

## Method
HFBCS (Hartree-Fock plus BCS) calculations with parity projection were performed on a 3-dimensional coordinate-space mesh using three parametrizations of the Skyrme energy functional (SLy4, SIII, SkM$^{*}$) and a zero-range density-dependent pairing interaction. The generator coordinate method (GCM) was then applied to mix parity- and particle-number-projected mean-field states along the axial octupole coordinate $Q_{30}$ and the tetrahedral coordinate $Q_{32}$, solving the discretized Hill-Wheeler equation separately for each collective coordinate.

## Key numbers
- Correlation energies for $^{80}$Zr: $E_{corr}(Q_{30})$ = 1.507 MeV (SLy4), 1.459 MeV (SIII), 1.290 MeV (SkM$^{*}$); $E_{corr}(Q_{32})$ = 1.567 MeV (SLy4), 1.499 MeV (SIII), 1.338 MeV (SkM$^{*}$).
- Excitation energies of the first negative-parity state in $^{80}$Zr: $E_{exc}(Q_{30})$ = 2.802 MeV (SLy4), 2.520 MeV (SIII), 3.111 MeV (SkM$^{*}$); $E_{exc}(Q_{32})$ = 2.425 MeV (SLy4), 2.220 MeV (SIII), 2.881 MeV (SkM$^{*}$).
- Correlation energies for $^{98}$Zr: $E_{corr}(Q_{30})$ = 1.389 MeV (SLy4), 1.387 MeV (SIII), 1.554 MeV (SkM$^{*}$); $E_{corr}(Q_{32})$ = 1.400 MeV (SLy4), 1.485 MeV (SIII), 1.564 MeV (SkM$^{*}$).
- Excitation energies of the first negative-parity state in $^{98}$Zr: $E_{exc}(Q_{30})$ = 2.644 MeV (SLy4), 1.090 MeV (SIII), 2.116 MeV (SkM$^{*}$); $E_{exc}(Q_{32})$ = 2.498 MeV (SLy4), 0.784 MeV (SIII), 1.776 MeV (SkM$^{*}$).
- The energy difference between spherical and tetrahedral configurations at the mean-field level does not exceed 1 MeV for both nuclei.
- Doubling the pairing gap changes correlation energies by 10-20% and approximately doubles the excitation energy for negative parity states.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Zberecki et al. (2005) and others (e.g., Yamagami et al., Takami et al., and the microscopic-macroscopic model calculations by Dudek et al.) that identified candidate tetrahedral magic numbers and suggested stable tetrahedral deformation in $^{80}$Zr and $^{98}$Zr. The present results contradict the possibility of static tetrahedral deformation in these nuclei, showing instead that quantum fluctuations beyond mean-field (via GCM) make the deformation dynamic. The findings enable future studies of octupole correlations in other candidate nuclei and highlight the need to include configuration mixing when assessing tetrahedral stability.
