# Mass and radius of the most massive neutron star: The probe of the equation of state and perturbative QCD

> Recently, an association of GW190425 and FRB 20190425A had been claimed and a highly magnetized neutron star (NS) remnant was speculated. Given the $\sim 2.5$-h delay of the occurrence of FRB 20190425A, a uniformly rotating supramassive magnetar is favored since the differential rotation would have been promptly terminated by the magnetic braking. The required maximum gravitational mass ($M_{\rm TOV}$) of the nonrotating NS is $\approx 2.77M_\odot$, which is strongly in tension with the relatively low $M_{\rm TOV}\approx 2.25M_\odot$ obtained in current equation of state (EOS) constraints incorporating perturbative quantum chromodynamics (pQCD) information. However, the current mass-radius and mass-tidal deformability measurements of NSs alone do not convincingly exclude the high $M_{\rm TOV}$ possibility. By performing EOS constraints with mock measurements, we find that with a $2\%$ determination for the radius of PSR J0740+6620-like NS it is possible to distinguish between the low and high $M_{\rm TOV}$ scenarios. We further explore the prospect to resolve the issue of the appropriate density to impose the pQCD constraints with future massive NS observations or determinations of $M_{\rm TOV}$ and/or $R_{\rm TOV}$. It turns out that measuring the radius of a PSR J0740+6620-like NS is insufficient to probe the EOSs around 5 nuclear saturation density, where the information from pQCD becomes relevant. The additional precise $M_{\rm TOV}$ measurements anyhow could provide insights into the EOS at such a density. Indeed, supposing the central engine of GRB 170817A is a black hole formed via the collapse of a supramassive NS, the resulting $M_{\rm TOV}\approx 2.2M_\odot$ considerably softens the EOS at the center of the most massive NS, which is in favor of imposing the pQCD constraint at density beyond the one achievable in the NSs.

## Key claim
The paper argues that if the claimed association between GW190425 and FRB 20190425A is real and requires a supramassive neutron star remnant, the resulting maximum nonrotating neutron star mass of approximately 2.77 solar masses is in strong tension with equation of state constraints that incorporate perturbative QCD information, but current measurements alone cannot definitively exclude this high-mass scenario.

## Method
The authors used Gaussian process regression to generate an ensemble of neutron star equations of state, conditioned on chiral effective field theory results at low densities and on observational data including NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, tidal deformability measurements from GW170817, and perturbative QCD constraints imposed at 10 times nuclear saturation density. They evaluated the likelihood of forming a supramassive neutron star from GW190425 using universal relations for rotating neutron stars, and performed Bayesian model comparison with mock measurements to assess future observational prospects.

## Context
This work extends the debate over the maximum neutron star mass following the claimed association of GW190425 and FRB 20190425A by Moroianu et al. (2023). It builds on the equation of state constraints incorporating perturbative QCD from Gorda et al. (2023) and Fan et al. (2023), and uses the universal relations for rotating neutron stars developed by Breu and Rezzolla (2016) and refined by Most et al. (2020). The paper enables future observational strategies by showing that a 2% radius measurement of PSR J0740+6620-like neutron stars can distinguish between low and high M_TOV scenarios, and that precise M_TOV measurements are essential for validating perturbative QCD constraints at high densities.
