# Nambu-Covariant Many-Body Theory II: Self-Consistent Approximations

> The theory of Self-Consistent Green's Function (SCGF) is reformulated in an explicit Nambu-covariant fashion for applications to many-body systems at non-zero temperature in symmetry-broken phases. This is achieved by extending the Nambu-covariant formulation of perturbation theory, presented in the first part of this work, to non-perturbative schemes based on self-consistently dressed propagators and vertices. We work out in detail the self-consistent ladder approximation, motivated by a trade-off between numerical complexity and many-body phenomenology. Taking a complex general Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) propagator as a starting point, we also formulate and prove a sufficient condition on the stability of the HFB self-energy to ensure the convergence of the initial series of ladders at any energy. The self-consistent ladder approximation is written purely in terms of spectral functions and the resulting set of equations, when expressed in terms of Nambu tensors, are remarkably similar to those in the symmetry-conserving case. This puts the application of the self-consistent ladder approximation to symmetry-broken phases of infinite nuclear matter within reach.

## Key claim

The paper reformulates Self-Consistent Green's Function (SCGF) theory in a Nambu-covariant framework for many-body systems at non-zero temperature in symmetry-broken phases, showing that the resulting self-consistent ladder approximation, when expressed in terms of Nambu tensors, yields equations "remarkably similar to those in the symmetry-conserving case", but this equivalence holds only under the condition that the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) self-energy satisfies a proven stability condition ensuring convergence of the ladder series at any energy.

## Method

The authors extend the Nambu-covariant formulation of perturbation theory (Part I) to non-perturbative, self-consistent schemes by dressing propagators and vertices. They work out the self-consistent ladder approximation in detail, starting from a complex general Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) propagator, and formulate the resulting equations purely in terms of spectral functions using Nambu tensors. A sufficient condition on the stability of the HFB self-energy is proved to guarantee convergence of the initial ladder series at any energy.

## Key numbers

- The 0th moment sum rule for the spectral function: ∫ dω/(2π) S(ω) = g (the metric tensor), from the anticommutation relation of Nambu fields.
- The asymptotic behavior of diagonal propagator components: G^μ_μ(z) = 1/z + O(1/z²) as |z| → ∞.
- The asymptotic behavior of off-diagonal propagator components (μ ≠ ν): G^μ_ν(z) = (m₁)^μ_ν / z² + O(1/z³) as |z| → ∞.
- The Galitskii-Migdal-Koltun sum rule connects the one-body spectral function to the Hamiltonian expectation value, but is valid only for the specific field basis where Nambu fields are pure creation or annihilation operators and H conserves particle number.

## Context

This work extends the Nambu-covariant perturbation theory developed in Part I (Drissi, Rios, Barbieri, 2021) to self-consistent, non-perturbative approximations, specifically targeting symmetry-broken phases of infinite nuclear matter at non-zero temperature. It builds on prior hybrid approaches that combine particle-number symmetry-breaking with many-body methods, such as Bogoliubov MBPT (Tichai, 2018), Gorkov SCGF (Soma, 2011; Soma, 2013; Soma, 2014; Barbieri, 2022), and Bogoliubov IMSRG (Tichai, 2021). The formalism enables future application of the self-consistent ladder approximation to superfluid nuclear matter, addressing the need for thermodynamically consistent calculations of neutron-star matter with anisotropic pairing gaps and strong interactions.
