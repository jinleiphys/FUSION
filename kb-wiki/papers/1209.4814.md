# Ferromagnetic neutron stars: axial anomaly, dense neutron matter, and pionic wall

> We show that a chiral nonlinear sigma model coupled to degenerate neutrons exhibits a ferromagnetic phase at high density. The magnetization is due to the axial anomaly acting on the parallel layers of neutral pion domain walls spontaneously formed at high density. The emergent magnetic field would reach the QCD scale ~ 10^19 [G], which suggests that the quantum anomaly can be a microscopic origin of the magnetars (highly magnetized neutron stars).

## Key claim

A chiral nonlinear sigma model coupled to degenerate neutrons exhibits a ferromagnetic phase at high density, where the magnetization arises from the axial anomaly acting on parallel layers of neutral pion domain walls spontaneously formed at high density. The mechanism requires a small seed external magnetic field and a threshold density where the in-medium pion domain wall tension is sufficiently reduced.

## Method

The authors constructed an in-medium chiral Lagrangian for pions and nucleons up to O(p^2), including one-loop corrections from background neutrons parameterized by the ratio x = μ_n/m_N. They studied classical π^0 domain wall solutions in dense neutron matter and evaluated the baryon number induced on the walls via the Wess-Zumino-Witten term under an external magnetic field. The magnetization from neutron spin alignment on the walls was computed, and a self-consistency condition was imposed to find the equilibrium magnetic field where the induced magnetization equals the background field.

## Key numbers

- β vanishes at μ_n ∼ 1.08 m_N, corresponding to ρ_n ∼ 0.23 fm^{-3} (using m_N = 940 MeV, f_π = 93 MeV, g_A = 1)
- The critical seed magnetic field for domain wall formation is B_3 > √(β γ_0) × 10^{19} G
- The equilibrium induced magnetic field at the core is B_3 ∼ 3 × 10^{19} G, assuming ρ ∼ ρ_dw ∼ ρ_n
- For a 1.41 M_⊙ neutron star with the APR equation of state, R_core/R_NS = 0.1 corresponds to a critical density of 3.2 ρ_0 for pionic wall formation
- The surface magnetic field at the North pole scales as B_surface = B_core (R_core/R_NS)^3, giving B_surface ∼ O(10^{16}) G for R_core/R_NS ∼ 1/10

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Son and Stephanov (2007) on baryon number induced on pion domain walls by magnetic fields, and the earlier study of π^0 domain walls in dense matter by Hatsuda (1986). It provides a microscopic mechanism for magnetar magnetic fields that differs from earlier proposals of nuclear ferromagnetism (Brownell and Callaway, 1969) and quark liquid ferromagnetism (Tatsumi, 1999), which faced difficulties from modern many-body calculations (Bordbar, 2008). The mechanism predicts two classes of neutron stars depending on whether the core density exceeds the threshold for domain wall formation, potentially explaining the observed bimodal distribution of neutron star magnetic fields.
