# Related Power-law Growth of Particle Multiplicities near Midrapidity in Central Au+Au Collisions and in $\ol{p}(p)-p$ Collisions

> A simple power-law growth of charged-particle multiplicities near midrapidity in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=56$ and 130 GeV, recently measured at RHIC, is derived. We give predictions for the central particle densities up to $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=1800$ GeV. A strong growth of the Au+Au densities above those for $\ol{p}(p)-p$ collisions is predicted.

## Key claim
The paper derives a simple power-law growth for charged-particle multiplicities near midrapidity in central Au+Au collisions, using the same slowly-increasing powers that were previously calculated for antiproton-proton (proton-proton) collisions. The key condition is that the power-law representation works only when the densities are normalized to the measured number of participating nucleon pairs, and the powers are taken from a prior theoretical calculation (Barshay 1984) that involves a single phenomenological parameter controlling the growth of the power toward a limiting value of order 0.1.

## Method
The authors first represent the normalized particle density near midrapidity for antiproton-proton (proton-proton) collisions as ρ(√s) = A (√s)^(2p(s)), with A ≈ 1.2 and p(s) taken from a prior calculation at 12 values of √s from 14 to 40,000 GeV (Barshay 1984). For central Au+Au collisions, they then multiply this base density by an additional factor A (√s_NN / ⟨n(s_NN)⟩)^(2p̃), where ⟨n(s_NN)⟩ is the average charged-particle multiplicity from the same prior calculation, and p̃ is the power appropriate to the reduced collision energy √s̃_NN = √s_NN / ⟨n(s_NN)⟩. This second factor accounts for additional collisions among the initially produced particles, with p̃ taken as approximately 0.055 for all relevant sub-energies below 50 GeV.

## Key numbers
- For antiproton-proton collisions: ρ(22) ≈ 1.6–1.7 at √s = 22 GeV, ρ(53) ≈ 1.9 at √s = 53 GeV, ρ(200) ≈ 2.5 at √s = 200 GeV, ρ(546) ≈ 3.1 at √s = 546 GeV, ρ(630) ≈ 3.2 at √s = 630 GeV, ρ(900) ≈ 3.45 at √s = 900 GeV, ρ(1800) ≈ 4 at √s = 1800 GeV.
- For central Au+Au collisions: ρ_Au(56) ≈ 2.6 at √s_NN = 56 GeV, ρ_Au(130) ≈ 3.4 at √s_NN = 130 GeV, ρ_Au(200) ≈ 4 at √s_NN = 200 GeV, ρ_Au(1800) ≈ 7.1 at √s_NN = 1800 GeV.
- Measured dN/dη for |η| < 1 at √s_NN = 56 GeV: 408 ± 12 (stat) ± 30 (syst); at √s_NN = 130 GeV: 555 ± 12 (stat) ± 35 (syst).
- Predicted dN/dη at √s_NN = 200 GeV: 680 (for ⟨N_part⟩ = 343); at √s_NN = 1800 GeV: 1220 (for ⟨N_part⟩ = 343) or 1350 (for ⟨N_part⟩ = 380).
- The additional multiplicity factor from secondary collisions is about 20% at 56 GeV, increasing to about 30% at 130 GeV, and about 50% at 1800 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the power-law description of particle multiplicities previously applied to antiproton-proton and proton-proton collisions (Barshay 1984) to the new RHIC data for central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 56 and 130 GeV (PHENIX collaboration, 2001). The authors contrast their physically motivated power-law approach with purely phenomenological parameterizations using three-parameter fits of the form A + B(ln s) + C(ln s)^2 (refs. 5 and 16 in the paper). The predictions for higher energies up to √s_NN = 1800 GeV enable estimates of maximum energy density reached in heavy-ion collisions, and the approach can be generalized to describe the centrality dependence of the particle density as a function of the number of participating nucleons.
