# Pion mass effects on axion emission from neutron stars through NN bremsstrahlung processes

> The rates of axion emission by nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung are calculated with the inclusion of the full momentum contribution from a nuclear one pion exchange (OPE) potential. The contributions of the neutron-neutron (nn), proton-proton (pp) and neutron-proton (np) processes in both the nondegenerate and degenerate limits are explicitly given. We find that the finite momentum corrections to the emissivities are quantitatively significant for the non-degenerate regime and temperature-dependent, and should affect the existing axion mass bounds. The trend of these nuclear effects is to diminish the emissivities.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the full momentum dependence of the one-pion exchange (OPE) nuclear matrix elements produces temperature-dependent reductions to axion emissivities from nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung that are quantitatively significant for the non-degenerate regime, diminishing the emissivities and thus affecting existing axion mass bounds.

## Method

The authors calculated axion emission rates for nn, pp, and np bremsstrahlung processes using Fermi's Golden Rule with a one-pion exchange potential, including the full momentum dependence of the nuclear matrix elements rather than the constant high-momentum limit used in prior work. They derived analytic expressions for the emissivities in both the non-degenerate (ND) and degenerate (D) limits, separating the constant term (corresponding to previous calculations) from a correction term that accounts for finite pion mass effects. The phase-space integrals were evaluated numerically for temperatures relevant to hot neutron star cores.

## Key numbers

- For the ND regime at T = 25 MeV, the relative correction to the emissivity (Delta-epsilon/epsilon_0) is 0.8503 for nn/pp processes and 0.8490 for np processes.
- For the ND regime at T = 75 MeV, the relative correction is 0.3010 for nn/pp processes and 0.3008 for np processes.
- For the D regime at T = 1 MeV, the relative correction is 0.1030 for nn/pp processes and 0.1070 for np processes.
- For the D regime at T = 20 MeV, the relative correction is 0.0440 for nn/pp processes and 0.0435 for np processes.
- The ND regime corrections reduce emissivities by 30% to 85% depending on temperature, while D regime corrections reduce emissivities by at most 11%.

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Brinkmann and Turner (1988) for the non-degenerate limit and Iwamoto (1984, 2001) for the degenerate limit by including the full momentum dependence of the OPE nuclear matrix elements, which those earlier works had approximated with constant matrix elements or high-momentum limits. The results show larger reductions than those reported by Raffelt and Seckel (1995), who found that pion mass effects do not reduce emissivities by more than 50% even in the ND regime. The findings imply that axion mass bounds derived from supernova cooling arguments (such as SN1987A) should be revised, and the method can be extended to include two-pion exchange effects (through rho meson exchange) in future work.
