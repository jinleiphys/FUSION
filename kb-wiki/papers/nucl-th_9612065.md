# The nonmesonic weak decay of the hypertriton

> The nonmesonic decay of the hypertriton is calculated based on a hypertriton wavefunction and 3N scattering states, which are rigorous solutions of 3-body Faddeev equations using realistic NN and hyperon-nucleon interactions. The pion-exchange together with heavier meson exchanges for the $ΛN \to N N $ transition is considered. The total nonmesonic decay rate is found to be 0.5% of the free $Λ$ decay rate. Integrated as well as differential decay rates are given. The p- and n- induced decays are discussed thoroughly and it is shown that the corresponding total rates cannot be measured individually.

## Key claim

The total nonmesonic decay rate of the hypertriton is found to be 0.5% of the free Lambda decay rate, but this result depends on using rigorous Faddeev solutions for both the hypertriton wavefunction and the final three-nucleon scattering states, incorporating full two-body correlations and final-state interactions that were neglected in prior simplified treatments.

## Method

The authors solved three-body Faddeev equations using realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) and hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions to obtain the hypertriton bound state wavefunction and the final three-nucleon scattering states. They constructed a weak transition operator for the Lambda N to N N process including exchanges of pi, eta, K, rho, omega, and K* mesons, with monopole form factors at each vertex. The decay matrix elements were evaluated by projecting the Faddeev equations into a partial wave basis and solving the resulting coupled integral equations for the amplitudes U, which incorporate final-state interactions among the three nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Total nonmesonic decay rate of the hypertriton is 0.5% of the free Lambda decay rate (free Lambda lifetime tau_Lambda = 2.63 x 10^-10 seconds)
- Hypertriton binding energy against Lambda-deuteron decay is 0.13 +/- 0.05 MeV, reproduced by the Faddeev calculations using realistic NN forces and the Nijmegen YN interaction
- Weak coupling constant G_F m_pi^2 = 2.21 x 10^-7, with parity-violating amplitude A_pi = 1.05 and parity-conserving amplitude B_pi = -7.15 for pion exchange, adjusted to reproduce free Lambda decay observables

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the nonmesonic decay of the hypertriton, specifically improving upon the simplified uncorrelated deuteron-Lambda wavefunction used in an earlier study (Ref. 4 in the paper) by incorporating full two-body correlations from realistic interactions and including final-state interactions among the three nucleons. It also builds on a major study of nonmesonic decay of p-shell hypernuclei (Ref. 7) that found short-range correlations to be important, but where bound state wavefunctions, spectroscopic factors, and final-state interactions did not all originate from the same underlying dynamics. The results enable future extraction of information on hadronic weak vertices from the Lambda N to N N process in few-body hypernuclei, where all nuclear structure ingredients are derived from the same baryon-baryon interaction.
