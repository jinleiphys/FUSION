# Proton emission induced by polarized photons

> The proton emission induced by polarized photons is studied in the energy range above the giant resonance region and below the pion emission threshold. Results for the 12C, 16O and 40Ca nuclei are presented. The sensitivity of various observables to final state interaction, meson exchange currents and short range correlations is analyzed. We found relevant effects due to the virtual excitation of the $Δ$ resonance.

## Key claim

The asymmetry observable in polarized-photon-induced proton emission is extremely sensitive to meson exchange currents (MEC), particularly the Δ-current contributions, which produce both quantitative and qualitative modifications of the angular distributions; this sensitivity could allow clean identification of MEC effects in medium-heavy nuclei, where such effects have not been clearly identified previously.

## Method

The authors used a continuum shell model with an optical potential to describe final state interactions (FSI), and included short range correlations (SRC) via a cluster expansion of transition matrix elements retaining terms linear in the correlation function. Meson exchange currents were incorporated through one-pion exchange diagrams including seagull, pionic, and Δ-current terms, with the Δ-current coupling constants taken from three different parameter sets (AMA, GIU, RYC). Calculations were performed for the doubly closed shell nuclei ¹²C, ¹⁶O, and ⁴⁰Ca at photon energies of 80 MeV and 150 MeV, studying the asymmetry Σ = (σ∥ − σ⊥)/(σ∥ + σ⊥) as a function of proton emission angle.

## Key numbers

- Photon energies studied: 80 MeV and 150 MeV, chosen above the giant resonance region and below the pion emission threshold.
- For ¹⁶O at ω = 80 MeV, the asymmetry for the 1p₃/₂ hole state shows a peak near 90° when using the Schwandt (Sc) or Comfort-Karp (CK) optical potentials; the 1p₁/₂ state asymmetry is flat between 60° and 150°.
- At ω = 80 MeV and large emission angles, the Δ-current contributions strongly modify the asymmetry; the three parameter sets (AMA: fγNΔ = 0.299, fπNΔ = 1.69; GIU: fγNΔ = 0.373, fπNΔ = 2.15; RYC: fγNΔ = 0.120, fπNΔ = 2.15) produce markedly different results.
- At θ = 120°, the cross sections show large enhancement from MEC at all excitation energies studied; the unpolarized cross section, σ∥, and σ⊥ all increase substantially when MEC and SRC are included compared to one-body currents only.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of (γ,p) reactions (Anguiano et al., 2002) by studying polarization observables to disentangle MEC and SRC effects, which were found to be difficult to separate in unpolarized cross sections. The calculations use the same nuclear model previously applied to inclusive electron scattering (Co' et al., 1998; Amaro et al., 1998) and single-coincidence experiments (Mokeev et al., 2001; Anguiano et al., 2002). The results contradict those of Bright and Cotanch (1993), who found smaller MEC effects in ¹⁶O unpolarized (γ,p) cross sections; the authors attribute this difference to the use of a real mean-field potential by Bright and Cotanch versus the complex optical potential used here. The work enables future experiments at facilities such as MAMI (Mainz), MAX-lab (Lund), and HIGS (Duke University) to use polarized photon beams to study MEC in medium-heavy nuclei.
