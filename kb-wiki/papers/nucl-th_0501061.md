# Delta Effects in Pion-Nucleon Scattering and the Strength of the Two-Pion-Exchange Three-Nucleon Interaction

> We consider the relationship between P-wave pi-N scattering and the strength of the P-wave two-pion-exchange three-nucleon interaction (TPE3NI). We explain why effective theories that do not contain the delta resonance as an explicit degree of freedom tend to overestimate the strength of the TPE3NI. The overestimation can be remedied by higher-order terms in these ``delta-less'' theories, but such terms are not yet included in state-of-the-art chiral EFT calculations of the nuclear force. This suggests that these calculations can only predict the strength of the TPE3NI to an accuracy of +/-25%.

## Key claim

Effective field theories (EFTs) of nuclear forces that do not include the delta resonance as an explicit degree of freedom overestimate the strength of the P-wave two-pion-exchange three-nucleon interaction (TPE3NI) by a factor of approximately 4/3; this overestimation can only be remedied by including higher-order terms that are not yet present in state-of-the-art chiral EFT calculations, meaning such calculations can only predict the TPE3NI strength to an accuracy of +/-25%.

## Method

The authors compare the leading-order TPE3NI derived from a theory with explicit delta degrees of freedom (the Fujita-Miyazawa potential) to the TPE3NI obtained from a "delta-less" EFT where the delta is integrated out and the P-wave pi-N scattering amplitude is fitted to threshold data. They compute the tree-level delta contributions to both pi-N scattering and the three-nucleon amplitude in the static limit, then show that the energy denominator in the pi-N scattering amplitude (evaluated at the physical pion mass) differs from the energy denominator in the three-nucleon potential (evaluated at virtual pion energies near zero), leading to a systematic discrepancy. They demonstrate that including higher-order terms with time derivatives (suppressed by (omega/Delta M)^2) can partially correct this discrepancy.

## Key numbers

- The delta-nucleon mass difference Delta M is approximately 300 MeV, which is about 2 m_pi (where m_pi is the pion mass).
- The leading-order delta-less theory overestimates the TPE3NI strength by a factor of 4/3 compared to the theory with explicit deltas.
- The factor 4/3 arises because the pi-N scattering amplitude at threshold has an energy denominator 1/(Delta M - m_pi) while the TPE3NI has denominator 1/Delta M; with Delta M approximately 2 m_pi, these give factors of approximately 4/(3 m_pi) and 1/m_pi respectively.
- The minimum permissible theoretical error assigned to the leading-order extraction of the TPE3NI strength in the delta-less theory is +/-25%.
- The axial-vector constant g_A is approximately 1.29, the pion decay constant f_pi is approximately 93 MeV, and the pi-N-Delta coupling h_A is approximately 2.8.

## Context

This work extends the Fujita-Miyazawa (FM) three-nucleon force (Fujita and Miyazawa, 1957) and the Sugawara-von Hippel two-pion-exchange two-nucleon potential (Sugawara and von Hippel, 1960s), which include the delta as an explicit degree of freedom. It contradicts the expectation that delta-less EFTs should work well in nuclear-structure physics because the relevant pion energies (of order m_pi^2/M) are much smaller than Delta M. The paper shows that the poor convergence of the derivative expansion in delta-less theories contaminates parameters extracted from pi-N scattering data at threshold (where omega ~ m_pi) when they are used in the TPE3NI (where omega ~ 0). This problem affects state-of-the-art N^3LO chiral EFT calculations of nuclear forces (Epelbaum et al., 2000s), which would need to go to N^4LO to include the necessary corrections. The work suggests that an EFT with explicit deltas may be a more efficient tool, as argued in earlier studies (Ordóñez, Ray, and van Kolck, 1994; van Kolck, 1994).
