# Surprises in threshold antikaon-nucleon physics

> Low energy \bar{K}N interactions are studied within Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory at next-to-leading order with ten coupled channels. We pay special attention to the recent precise determination of the strong shift and width of the kaonic hydrogen 1s state by the DEAR Collaboration that has challenged our theoretical understanding of this sector of strong interactions. We typically find two classes of solutions, both of them reproducing previous data, that either can or cannot accommodate the DEAR measurements. The former class has not been previously discussed.

## Key claim

The authors find two classes of solutions within Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory that both reproduce previous scattering data, but only one class (class A) can accommodate the precise DEAR measurement of kaonic hydrogen, which points to a less repulsive K⁻p interaction at threshold than previously thought.

## Method

The authors perform a next-to-leading order Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory (UCHPT) analysis of S-wave antikaon-nucleon scattering, using a coupled-channel dispersion relation for the inverse partial wave amplitude with ten coupled channels of strangeness S=-1. They fit the model to K⁻p scattering cross sections, threshold ratios, and πΣ event distributions, while also constraining the fit with πN observables calculated at next-to-leading order in SU(3) baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory. The kaonic hydrogen shift and width are calculated using isospin breaking corrections from Akaki (2004) rather than the simpler Deser formula.

## Key numbers

- DEAR measurement of kaonic hydrogen 1s state: ΔE = 193 ± 37 (stat) ± 6 (syst) eV, Γ = 249 ± 111 (stat) ± 39 (syst) eV
- KEK measurement (for comparison): ΔE = 323 ± 63 ± 11 eV, Γ = 407 ± 208 ± 100 eV
- K⁻p scattering length from class A fit (A₄⁺): a_{K⁻p} = (-0.51 + i 0.42) fm
- K⁻p scattering length from class B fit (B₄⁺): a_{K⁻p} = (-1.01 + i 0.80) fm
- Isospin 0 scattering length from fit A₄⁺: a₀ = (-1.23 + i 0.45) fm
- Isospin 1 scattering length from fit A₄⁺: a₁ = (0.98 + i 0.35) fm
- Λ(1405) pole positions from fit A₄⁺: (1321 - i 43.5) MeV and (1402 - i 39.6) MeV
- Λ(1405) pole positions from fit B₄⁺: (1361 - i 29.9) MeV and (1433 - i 31.7) MeV
- Threshold ratio γ = σ(K⁻p → π⁺Σ⁻)/σ(K⁻p → π⁻Σ⁺) = 2.36 ± 0.04
- Threshold ratio R_c = 0.664 ± 0.011
- Threshold ratio R_n = 0.189 ± 0.015

## Context

This work extends the Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory approach pioneered by Oller (2000) and applied to K⁻p scattering by Bura (2004), who were unable to simultaneously reproduce scattering data and the new DEAR measurement. The authors resolve this apparent inconsistency by identifying a new class of fits (class A) that accommodate both datasets. The results confirm the two-pole structure of the Λ(1405) resonance previously found by Jido (2003) and Oset (2001), with the class A poles lying 30-40 MeV lower than class B, which is crucial for calculations of the K⁻-nucleus potential and its implications for dense nuclear matter and kaon condensation in neutron stars.
