# Testing AdS/CFT Drag and pQCD Heavy Quark Energy Loss

> We present charm and bottom nuclear modification factors for RHIC and LHC using Standard Model perturbative QCD and recent AdS/CFT string drag energy loss models. We find that extreme extrapolations to LHC mask potential experimentally determinable differences in the individual $R_{AA}$s but that their ratio, $R_{AA}^c/R_{AA}^b$ as a function of transverse momentum is a remarkably robust observable for finding deviations from either theoretical framework.

# Testing AdS/CFT Drag and pQCD Heavy Quark Energy Loss

## Key claim
The ratio of charm to bottom nuclear modification factors, $R_{AA}^c/R_{AA}^b$ as a function of transverse momentum, provides a robust observable for distinguishing between pQCD-based and AdS/CFT string drag energy loss models, even though individual $R_{AA}$ predictions from extreme extrapolations to LHC energies may be experimentally indistinguishable.

## Method
The authors computed nuclear modification factors $R_{AA}^Q(p_T)$ for charm and bottom quarks at both RHIC and LHC energies using two classes of energy loss models: the WHDG pQCD model (convolving inelastic and elastic energy loss with fixed $\alpha_s = 0.3$) and the AdS/CFT string drag model (with $dp_T/dt = -\pi\sqrt{\lambda}T^2 p_T / 2M_Q$). For the AdS/CFT calculations, two parameter mapping prescriptions between QCD and supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) were employed: an "obvious" mapping with $\alpha_s = \alpha_{SYM}$ and $T_{QCD} = T_{SYM}$, and an "alternative" mapping with $\lambda \sim 5.5$ and $T_{SYM} \approx T_{QCD}/3^{1/4}$ derived from matching the $q\bar{q}$ force on the lattice and equating energy densities. Medium densities at LHC were modeled using PHOBOS extrapolation ($dN_g/dy = 1750$) and KLN CGC model ($dN_g/dy = 2900$).

## Key numbers
- For LHC, pQCD predictions yield $R_{AA}^c(\hat{q}=40) \sim 0.15$ and $R_{AA}^c(\hat{q}=100) \sim 0.02$ using diffuse Woods-Saxon nuclear geometry and Bjorken expansion
- The AdS/CFT string drag "speed limit" is given by $\gamma_c = (1 + 2M_q/\sqrt{\lambda}T)^2 \sim 4M_q^2/\lambda T^2$, parametrically identical to the Bethe-Heitler to LPM transition scale with one less power of $T/M_q$
- For pQCD, the asymptotic ratio $R^{cb}(p_T) \sim 1 - \alpha_s n(p_T) L^2 \log(M_b/M_c)(\hat{q}/p_T)$, approaching 1 from below at high $p_T$
- For AdS string drag, $R^{cb}(p_T) \sim M_c/M_b$, significantly below 1 and independent of momentum

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior calculations (Horowitz 2007) of heavy quark energy loss and addresses the falsification of perturbative QCD radiative energy loss models for nonphotonic electrons at RHIC (Akiba 2005, Bielcik 2005). The paper builds on the AdS/CFT string drag formalism (Gubser 2006, Herzog 2006) and the WHDG pQCD model. The key advance is demonstrating that the ratio $R_{AA}^c/R_{AA}^b$ cancels much of the input parameter dependence that makes individual $R_{AA}$ predictions ambiguous, enabling experimental discrimination between the two theoretical frameworks at both RHIC and LHC. Future RHIC detector upgrades capable of individual charm and bottom measurements are identified as crucial for testing these predictions.
