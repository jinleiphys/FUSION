# Drag force on heavy diquarks and gauge/string duality

> We use gauge/string duality to model a doubly heavy diquark in a color antitriplet moving in a thermal plasma at temperatures near the critical. With the assumption that there is no relative motion between the constituents, we calculate the drag force on the diquark. At high enough speed we find that diquark string configurations develop a cusp. In addition, we estimate the spatial string tension at non-zero baryon chemical potential, and also briefly discuss an extension to a triply heavy triquark in a color triplet.

## Key claim

Using gauge/string duality, the drag force on a doubly heavy diquark in a color antitriplet moving through a thermal plasma near the critical temperature is shown to be equal to that on a single heavy quark, under the explicit assumption that there is no relative motion between the two constituent quarks.

## Method

The author models the diquark as a Nambu-Goto string configuration in a five-dimensional curved black hole background, with a baryon vertex (string junction) where three strings meet. The drag force is computed from the trailing string attached to the vertex, using the first integrals of the equations of motion derived from the total action, which includes the Nambu-Goto actions for three strings, an effective action for the baryon vertex, and boundary terms. The analysis is performed in the lab frame of the thermal medium, with the quarks placed on the boundary and moving uniformly in the x-direction.

## Key numbers

* The drag force on the diquark is given by \(F_{\text{drag}} = -\mathfrak{g} w(r_v) v\), where \(r_v = r_h \sqrt[4]{1-v^2}\) is the position of the induced horizon, and \(\mathfrak{g} = R^2/(2\pi\alpha')\).
* For the concrete model with warp factor \(w(r) = e^{\mathfrak{s} r^2}/r^2\) and blackening factor \(f(r) = 1-(r/r_h)^4\), the critical temperature is \(T_c = \sqrt{\mathfrak{s}}/\pi\).
* The dissociation temperature for charm diquarks is estimated as \(T_{\text{diss}} \sim 2.1\,T_c\) and for bottom diquarks as \(T_{\text{diss}} \sim 2.9\,T_c\), using radii of \(0.28\,\text{fm}\) (cc) and \(0.19\,\text{fm}\) (bb) from an effective quark-quark potential.
* At a temperature slice with \(h = \mathfrak{s} r_h^2 = 4/9\) (corresponding to \(T \approx 1.5 T_c\)), the limiting speed above which the interquark distance vanishes is \(v_{\text{lim}} \approx 0.52\).

## Context

This work extends the gauge/string duality approach to drag forces, previously applied to single heavy quarks (drag-ads, a-D), to the case of doubly heavy diquarks in the color antitriplet channel. It builds on the author's prior static diquark analysis (a-screen) and the three-quark potential model (3q). The results enable future studies of diquark motion in hot media, including the possibility of cusp formation on string configurations at high speeds, and provide a framework for comparing with lattice QCD estimates of heavy diquark diffusion and dissociation.
