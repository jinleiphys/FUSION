# Transitional Lu and Spherical Ta Ground-State Proton Emitters in the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model

> Properties of transitional Lu and spherical Ta ground-state proton emitters are calculated with the Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) model. The NL3 effective interaction is used in the mean-field Lagrangian, and pairing correlations are described by the pairing part of the finite range Gogny interaction D1S. Proton separation energies, ground-state quadrupole deformations, single-particle orbitals occupied by the odd valence proton, and the corresponding spectroscopic factors are compared with recent experimental data, and with results of the macroscopic-microscopic mass model.

# Transitional Lu and Spherical Ta Ground-State Proton Emitters in the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model

## Key claim

The Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the NL3 effective interaction and Gogny D1S pairing reproduces experimental transition energies and orbital assignments for ground-state proton emission in Lu and Ta isotopes, but only for nuclei where the model parameters were not adjusted to the specific properties of these emitters; the model predicts oblate shapes for Lu isotopes and spherical shapes for Ta isotopes, with the predicted proton emitter 149Lu having a one-proton separation energy of -1.77 MeV corresponding to a half-life of a few microseconds, just above the lower limit of current experimental observation.

## Method

The RHB equations were solved self-consistently in the quasiparticle basis using the NL3 parameter set for the mean-field Lagrangian and the pairing part of the finite-range Gogny interaction D1S for pairing correlations. A simple blocking prescription was used for odd-proton systems without breaking time-reversal symmetry, and the resulting eigenspectrum was transformed into the canonical basis to determine energies and occupation probabilities of single-particle states. Constrained calculations of the quadrupole deformation were performed to map potential energy curves for the Lu isotopes.

## Key numbers

- 149Lu: predicted one-proton separation energy -1.77 MeV, ground-state quadrupole deformation β2 = -0.158, odd proton occupies the 7/2-[523] Nilsson orbital with spectroscopic factor 0.60
- 150Lu: calculated one-proton separation energy -1.31 MeV, β2 = -0.153, spectroscopic factor 0.61, compared to experimental transition energy 1.261(4) MeV
- 151Lu: calculated one-proton separation energy -1.24 MeV, β2 = -0.151, spectroscopic factor 0.58, compared to experimental transition energy 1.233(3) MeV
- 155Ta: calculated one-proton separation energy -1.677 MeV, h11/2 orbital with spectroscopic factor 0.60, compared to experimental transition energy -1.765(10) MeV and experimental spectroscopic factor 0.58(20)
- 156Ta: calculated one-proton separation energy -1.129 MeV, d3/2 orbital with spectroscopic factor 0.51, compared to experimental transition energy -1.007(5) MeV
- 157Ta: calculated one-proton separation energy -1.040 MeV, h11/2 orbital with spectroscopic factor 0.42, compared to experimental transition energy -0.927(7) MeV and experimental spectroscopic factor 0.56(24)
- 156Ta isomer: calculated one-proton separation energy -1.250 MeV, h11/2 orbital with spectroscopic factor 0.79, compared to experimental transition energy 1.103(12) MeV and experimental spectroscopic factor 0.92(4)
- The separation energy window for observable ground-state proton decay in rare-earth nuclei is about 0.8 to 1.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous RHB studies of proton-rich spherical even-even nuclei with Z=14-28 (Vretenar, Lalazissis, Ring 1998) and deformed odd-Z ground-state proton emitters in the region Z=53-69 (Lalazissis, Vretenar, Ring 1999; Vretenar, Lalazissis, Ring 1999) to complete the analysis of proton emitters up to Z=73. The results are compared with the macroscopic-microscopic finite-range droplet model (FRDM) of Moller, Nix, and Kratz (1997) and with experimental data from Sellin (1993), Uusitalo (1999), Page (1992), and Irvine (1997). The RHB model provides complementary information to the Woods-Saxon potential approaches of Aberg, Semmes, and Nazarewicz (1997) and the deformed tunneling models of Rykaczewski (1999) and Maglione, Ferreira, and Liotta (1998), as it predicts proton separation energies and spectroscopic factors self-consistently rather than requiring parameter adjustment for each orbital. The predicted proton emitter 149Lu has been proposed for experimental search by Batchelder (1999).
