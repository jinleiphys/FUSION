# Towards the solution of the Gn/Gp Puzzle in the Non-Mesonic Weak Decay of Lambda-Hypernuclei

> One of the main open problems in the physics of Lambda-hypernuclei is the lack of a sound theoretical interpretation of the large experimental values for the ratio Gn/Gp=G(Lambda n -> nn)/G(Lambda p -> np). To approach the problem, we have incorporated a one-meson-exchange model for the Lambda N -> nN transition in finite nuclei in an intranuclear cascade code for the calculation of single and double-coincidence nucleon distributions corresponding to the non-mesonic weak decay of 5_Lambda-He and 12_Lambda-C. Due to the elimination of interferences, two-nucleon coincidences are expected to give a cleaner determination of Gn/Gp than single-nucleon observables. Single-nucleon distributions are found to be less sensitive to variations of Gn/Gp than double-coincidence spectra. The comparison of our results with preliminary KEK coincidence data allows us to extract a Gn/Gp ratio for 5_Lambda-He of 0.39+-0.11 when multinucleon induced channels are omitted.

## Key claim

The authors argue that two-nucleon coincidence measurements provide a cleaner determination of the ratio \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p = \Gamma(\Lambda n \to nn)/\Gamma(\Lambda p \to np)\) than single-nucleon observables, because they eliminate quantum mechanical interference effects between neutron-induced and proton-induced decay channels that cannot be accounted for by classical intranuclear cascade simulations. By comparing their calculations with preliminary KEK coincidence data, they extract a value of \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p = 0.39 \pm 0.11\) for \(^5_\Lambda\)He, but only when multinucleon induced channels are omitted.

## Method

The authors combined a one-meson-exchange (OME) model for the \(\Lambda N \to nN\) weak transition in finite nuclei with an intranuclear cascade Monte Carlo code that simulates nucleon propagation and final-state interactions inside the residual nucleus. The OME potential included exchange of \(\pi\), \(\rho\), \(K\), \(K^*\), \(\omega\), and \(\eta\) mesons, with strong final-state interactions described by NSC97 potentials. For the two-nucleon induced decay channel (\(\Lambda np \to nnp\)), they used a polarization propagator method in local density approximation (LDA) with OPE plus short-range correlations, scaling the ratio \(\Gamma_2/\Gamma_1\) to 0.20 for \(^5_\Lambda\)He and 0.25 for \(^{12}_\Lambda\)C.

## Key numbers

- Extracted \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p = 0.39 \pm 0.11\) for \(^5_\Lambda\)He from comparison with preliminary KEK coincidence data, when multinucleon induced channels are omitted.
- The OMEf model predicts \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p = 0.46\) for \(^5_\Lambda\)He and \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p = 0.34\) for \(^{12}_\Lambda\)C.
- The ratio \(\Gamma_2/\Gamma_1\) from LDA is 0.20 for \(^5_\Lambda\)He and 0.25 for \(^{12}_\Lambda\)C.
- For \(^{12}_\Lambda\)C, a previous corrected analysis gave \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p = 0.87 \pm 0.23\) from KEK-E307 proton spectra, incompatible with pure theoretical estimates ranging from 0.1 to 0.5.
- The fraction of protons from neutron-induced decays in the 30-40 MeV kinetic energy bin is \(N^{\Lambda n\to nn}_p / N^{\Lambda p\to np}_p \simeq 0.15\) for both hypernuclei, corresponding to an interference term \(I_p \cos \phi_p = 0.67 \cos \phi_p\).

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing "\(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\) puzzle" in non-mesonic weak decay of \(\Lambda\)-hypernuclei, where theoretical calculations (especially OPE models giving ratios of 0.05-0.20) systematically underestimate experimental values. It extends previous finite-nucleus calculations by Parreno, Ramos, and collaborators (2001) and corrects an error in the intranuclear cascade code of Ramos et al. (1997) that underestimated nucleon-nucleon collision probabilities by a factor of ten. The results enable a more reliable extraction of \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\) from upcoming experiments at KEK, FINUDA, and BNL, and support recent theoretical studies (Parreno et al. 2001, Itonaga et al. 1998, Oka et al. 1999) that include heavy-meson exchange and direct quark contributions to obtain ratios more consistent with data.
