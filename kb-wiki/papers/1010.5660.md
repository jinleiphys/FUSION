# The chiral and angular momentum content of the $ρ$-mesons in lattice QCD

> The variational method allows one to study the mixing of interpolators with different chiral transformation properties in the nonperturbatively determined physical state. It is then possible to define and calculate in a gauge-invariant manner the chiral as well as the partial wave content of the quark-antiquark component of a meson in the infrared, where mass is generated. Using a unitary transformation from the chiral basis to the $^{2S+1}L_J$ basis one may extract the partial wave content of a meson. We present results for the $ρ$- and $ρ'$-mesons using a simulation with $N_f=2$ dynamical quarks, all for lattice spacings close to 0.15 fm. Our results indicate a strong chiral symmetry breaking in the $ρ$ state and its simple $^3S_1$-wave composition in the infrared. For the $ρ'$-meson we find a small chiral symmetry breaking in the infrared as well as a leading contribution of the $^3D_1$ partial wave, which is contradictory to the quark model.

## Key claim
Using lattice QCD with dynamical quarks, the authors find that the ground-state ρ-meson is predominantly a ³S₁ state with strong chiral symmetry breaking, while the first excited ρ′-meson shows a leading ³D₁ partial wave contribution and small chiral symmetry breaking, contradicting the quark model prediction that ρ′ is a radial ³S₁ excitation.

## Method
The authors used the variational method with a cross-correlation matrix of four interpolators (local and smeared vector and pseudotensor operators) on 16³×32 lattices with N_f=2 dynamical quarks and the Chirally Improved Dirac action. They extracted the ratio of vector to pseudotensor couplings a_V/a_T for the ρ and ρ′ states at three lattice spacings near 0.15 fm and three pion masses, then applied a unitary transformation between the chiral basis and the ²S⁺¹L_J basis to obtain the partial wave decomposition at infrared scales set by gauge-invariant smearing widths up to 0.67 fm.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: 0.1507(17) fm (Set A), 0.1500(12) fm (Set B), 0.1440(12) fm (Set C)
- Pion masses: 526(7) MeV (Set A), 469(5) MeV (Set B), 323(5) MeV (Set C)
- ρ-meson masses: 911(11) MeV (Set A), 870(10) MeV (Set B), 795(15) MeV (Set C)
- For the ground state ρ at largest smearing width R ≈ 0.67 fm, the ratio a_V/a_T ≈ 1.2, yielding a partial wave decomposition of 0.997|³S₁⟩ − 0.073|³D₁⟩
- For the ρ′ state, assuming a vanishing ratio a_V/a_T at R ∼ 0.8–1 fm gives the decomposition √(1/3)|³S₁⟩ − √(2/3)|³D₁⟩

## Context
This work extends the chiral classification of quark-antiquark states by Glozman (2007) and applies the variational method of Michael (1985) to extract the wave function content of mesons in lattice QCD. The results contradict the quark model prediction (PDG, Isgur and others) that ρ(1450) is a radial ³S₁ excitation, and instead support the hypothesis of effective chiral restoration in excited hadrons proposed by Glozman (2007). The method enables direct lattice determination of chiral and angular momentum content for any meson, with implications for understanding mass generation mechanisms and the role of the quark condensate.
