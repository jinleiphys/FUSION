# Updated QCD global analysis of single transverse-spin asymmetries I: Extracting $\tilde{H}$, and the role of the Soffer bound and lattice QCD

> We present an update to the QCD global analysis of single transverse-spin asymmetries presented in Cammarota, et al., PRD 102, 054002 (2020) (JAM3D-20). JAM3D-20 simultaneously included transverse momentum dependent and collinear twist-3 observables, both of which are sensitive to quark-gluon-quark correlations in hadrons. In this study we extract for the first time the twist-3 chiral odd fragmentation function $\tilde{H}$ by incorporating the $\sinϕ_s$ modulation data from SIDIS along with its contribution to the single transverse-spin asymmetry in pion production from proton-proton collisions. We also explore the impact of lattice QCD tensor charge calculations and the Soffer bound on our global analysis. We find that both constraints can be accommodated within our results, with $\tilde{H}$ playing a key role in maintaining agreement with the data from proton-proton collisions.

## Key claim

This work presents the first extraction of the twist-3 chiral-odd fragmentation function $\tilde{H}$ within a global QCD analysis of single transverse-spin asymmetries, finding that both lattice QCD tensor charge calculations and the Soffer bound on transversity can be accommodated simultaneously, with $\tilde{H}$ playing a key role in maintaining agreement with proton-proton collision data.

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous QCD global analysis of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) and collinear twist-3 observables, extending the JAM3D-20 framework by including HERMES $A_{UT}^{\sin\phi_S}$ SIDIS data to constrain $\tilde{H}(z)$ for the first time. They employed a Gaussian ansatz in transverse momentum space with decoupled $x$ and $k_T$ (or $z$ and $p_T$) dependence, and incorporated lattice QCD tensor charge data from Alexandrou (2019) as a constraint alongside the Soffer bound on transversity. The analysis included Sivers and Collins asymmetries in SIDIS from HERMES and COMPASS, Collins effect in SIA from Belle, BaBar, and BESIII, Sivers effect in DY from STAR and COMPASS, and $A_N^\pi$ from BRAHMS and STAR.

## Key numbers

- The up and down quark tensor charges $\delta u$ and $\delta d$, and their isovector combination $g_T$, were computed from transversity $h_1(x)$ via $\delta u = \int_0^1 dx\,(h_1^{u}(x)-h_1^{\bar{u}}(x))$, $\delta d = \int_0^1 dx\,(h_1^{d}(x)-h_1^{\bar{d}}(x))$, $g_T \equiv \delta_u - \delta_d$; lattice QCD computations at the physical point from Gupta (2018), Yamanaka (2018), Hasan (2019), and Alexandrou (2019) provide constraints on these charges.
- The Soffer bound $|h_1^q(x)| \le \frac{1}{2}(f_1^q(x)+g_1^q(x))$ was imposed on transversity; JAM3D-20 had shown clear violation for the down quark.
- The $\vec{P}_{hT}$-integrated $F_{UT}^{\sin\phi_S}$ structure function is given by $\int d^2\vec{P}_{hT}\, F_{UT}^{\sin\phi_S} = -\frac{x}{z}\sum_q e_q^2\,\frac{2M_h}{Q} h_1^{q/N}(x)\,\tilde{H}^{h/q}(z)$, where $M$ ($M_h$) is the nucleon (hadron) mass.
- The $A_N$ asymmetry in proton-proton collisions involves $\tilde{H}(z)$ through the term $\mathcal{H}^{h/c,i}(x,x',z) = [H_1^{\perp(1),h/c}(z)-z\frac{dH_1^{\perp(1),h/c}(z)}{dz}]\tilde{S}_{H_1^\perp}^i + [-2H_1^{\perp(1),h/c}(z) + \frac{1}{z}\tilde{H}^{h/c}(z)]\tilde{S}_H^i$.

## Context

This work extends the JAM3D-20 global analysis by Cammarota (2020), which first demonstrated simultaneous extraction of universal non-perturbative functions from SIDIS, SIA, DY, and proton-proton collision SSAs, and found agreement with lattice QCD tensor charges without including lattice data. Prior extractions of tensor charges from phenomenology (e.g., Anselmino 2013, Radici 2015, Kang 2015, Radici 2018, Benel 2019, D'Alesio 2020) found tension with lattice QCD calculations, while Lin (2017) showed that including lattice $g_T$ data along with Collins effect SIDIS data alleviated that discrepancy. The inclusion of $\tilde{H}(z)$ and the Soffer bound enables more complete tests of the common-origin hypothesis for all SSAs and provides a foundation for incorporating additional observables like Collins effect hadron-in-jet and $A_N$ for jets in future work.
