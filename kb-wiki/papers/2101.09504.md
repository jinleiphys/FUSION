# Chirality imbalance and chiral magnetic effect under a parallel electromagnetic field

> We study the time evolution of the chirality imbalance $n_5$ and the chiral magnetic effect (CME) under the external parallel electromagnetic fields without assuming the artificial chiral asymmetric source. We adopt the time-dependent Sauter-type electric and constant magnetic field, and obtain analytical solutions of the Dirac equation for a massive fermion. We use the point-split regularization to calculate the vacuum contribution in the gauge invariant way. As a result, we find that $n_5$ and CME current increase substantially as the electric field increases, and stay finite after the electric field is switched off. The chirality imbalance and CME current are shown to consist of a dominant contribution, which is essentially proportional to relativistic velocity, and a small oscillating part. We find a simple analytical relation between $n_5$ and the fermion pair-production rate from the vacuum. We also discuss dynamical origin of the chirality imbalance in detail.

## Key claim

The chirality imbalance $n_5$ and chiral magnetic effect (CME) current are generated dynamically from the vacuum under parallel electromagnetic fields without assuming an artificial chiral asymmetric source, and they persist after the electric field is switched off; the dominant contribution is proportional to relativistic velocity, with a small oscillating part.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation analytically for a massive fermion under a time-dependent Sauter-type electric field (half-pulse shape) and a constant magnetic field parallel to it, using the chiral representation of gamma matrices. They performed canonical quantization with the vacuum state defined at $t \to -\infty$, and employed point-split regularization with a Wilson line to compute gauge-invariant vacuum expectation values of bilinear fermion operators, including the chirality imbalance $n_5$ and the CME current. The solutions were expressed in terms of Gauss hypergeometric functions for the time-dependent part and Hermite polynomials for the spatial (Landau level) part.

## Key numbers

- Only the lowest Landau level (LLL, $n=0$) contributes to the chirality imbalance $n_5$ and the pseudoscalar density, while all Landau levels ($n=0,1,2,\dots$) contribute to the vector current (CME current).
- The eigenvalue for the $x$-dependent part is $\kappa = 2eBn$, where $n$ is the Landau level index.
- The magnetic helicity density is $h(t) = -BE\tau(\tanh(t/\tau)+1)$, which is zero at $t\to -\infty$ and finite at $t\to\infty$.
- The regularization introduces a cutoff parameter $\Lambda^2 = 1/\varepsilon$ with the regularization factor $\exp(-[p_z+eA_z(t)]^2/\Lambda^2)$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the chiral magnetic effect and chirality imbalance by using a time-dependent Sauter electric field rather than a constant electric field, avoiding the divergence of the vector potential at asymptotic times. It provides analytical solutions of the Dirac equation for massive fermions, complementing earlier work using the Schwinger mechanism with constant electromagnetic fields (CM-Schwinger1), the Wigner function method with collinear fields (Wigner1), and cylindrical Dirac equation with chiral chemical potential (Fukushima2). The results enable a systematic study of the relation between chirality imbalance and fermion pair-production rate via the Bogoliubov transformation, and clarify the dynamical origin of chirality imbalance without assuming an initial chiral chemical potential.
