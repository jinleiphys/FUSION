# Exploring effective interactions through transition charge density study of $^{70,72,74,76}\rm Ge$ nuclei

> Transition charge density (TCD) for $ 0^+\to 2_1^+ $ excitation have been calculated for $\rm ^{70,72,74,76}Ge$ nuclei within microscopic variational framework employing $2p_{3/2}, 1f_{5/2}, 2p_{1/2}$ and $1g_{9/2}$ valence space. The calculated TCDs for different monopole variants of Kuo interaction are compared with available experimental results. Other systematics like reduced transition probabilities B(E2) and static quadrupole moments Q(2) are also presented. It is observed that the transition density study acts as a sensitive probe for discriminating the response of different parts of effective interactions.

## Key claim

Transition charge density (TCD) for the \(0^+ \to 2_1^+\) excitation in \(^{70,72,74,76}\rm Ge\) nuclei acts as a more sensitive probe than B(E2) or static quadrupole moments for discriminating the response of different parts of effective interactions, specifically the monopole strengths of the Kuo interaction in the f-p-g valence space.

## Method

The authors performed angular-momentum-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for the even-even Ge isotopes, using a valence space consisting of the \(2p_{3/2}, 1f_{5/2}, 2p_{1/2}\) and \(1g_{9/2}\) orbits above a \(^{56}\rm Ni\) inert core. They employed the Kuo effective interaction and created five monopole variants (Kuo00, Kuo10, Kuo15, Kuo20, Kuo25) by making the \(\langle (f_{5/2})^2 JT| V |(f_{5/2})^2 JT\rangle\) matrix elements more attractive and the \(\langle (p_{3/2})^2 JT| V |(p_{3/2})^2 JT\rangle\) matrix elements more repulsive by 0, 100, 150, 200, and 250 keV, respectively. Transition charge densities, reduced transition probabilities B(E2), and static quadrupole moments Q(2) were computed for each variant and compared with available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The interior peak of the TCD is located around r = 1.25 fm and the surface peak around r = 4.25 fm for all four Ge isotopes.
- For \(^{70,72}\rm Ge\), effective charges of \((e_\pi, e_\nu) = (1.15, 0.15)\) were used; for \(^{74,76}\rm Ge\), effective charges of \((e_\pi, e_\nu) = (1.45, 0.45)\) were used to match the surface peak height.
- The monopole strength was varied in steps of 100, 150, 200, and 250 keV, corresponding to the Kuo10, Kuo15, Kuo20, and Kuo25 variants.
- The single-particle energies (in MeV) used were: \(\epsilon(2p_{3/2}) = 0.00\), \(\epsilon(1f_{5/2}) = 0.78\), \(\epsilon(2p_{1/2}) = 1.08\), and \(\epsilon(1g_{9/2}) = 3.50\).
- The B(E2) values for \(^{74,76}\rm Ge\) required an increase in effective charge by approximately 0.3 units compared to \(^{70,72}\rm Ge\) to match experiment.

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic variational studies of Ge and Se isotopes (Tripathi 1986, Rath 1988) and shell-model studies in the f-p-g region (Dhiman 1994, Caurier 1996) by applying monopole modifications to the Kuo interaction, similar to the successful KB3 interaction in the f-p shell (Poves 1991). The study is motivated by experimental TCD data for Ge nuclei (Bazant 1990) that had previously been described in the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (Duval 1983). The results show that TCD is a more discriminating observable than B(E2) or Q(2) for fixing monopole strengths, and the authors suggest that the Kuo20 or Kuo25 variant is the appropriate monopole modification for this mass region.
