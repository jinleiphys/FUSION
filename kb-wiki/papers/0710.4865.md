# Exact mesoscopic correlation functions of the pairing model

> We study the static correlation functions of the Richardson pairing model (also known as the reduced or discrete-state BCS model) in the canonical ensemble. Making use of the Algebraic Bethe Ansatz formalism, we obtain exact expressions which are easily evaluated numerically for any value of the pairing strength up to large numbers of particles. We provide explicit results at half-filling and extensively discuss their finite-size scaling behavior.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact expressions for static correlation functions of the Richardson pairing model in the canonical ensemble using the Algebraic Bethe Ansatz, reducing the computational complexity from factorial to a sum of only \(N_r\) determinants (where \(N_r\) is the number of rapidities), enabling calculations for systems with many more particles than previously possible.

## Method

The authors use the Algebraic Bethe Ansatz (ABA) formalism to express static correlation functions as scalar products of Bethe eigenstates, then apply Slavnov's formula for scalar products to obtain determinant representations. They reduce the original expressions from sums over \(N_r^2\) determinants to sums over only \(N_r\) determinants by performing column operations on the determinant matrices. The ground-state rapidities are obtained by numerically solving the Richardson equations using Newton's method with a change of variables to handle complex conjugate pairs at critical coupling values.

## Key numbers

- The computational complexity is reduced to order \(N_r^5\) (from factorial) for the determinant representation, and further reduced by a factor of \(N_r\) to sums over only \(N_r\) determinants
- Previous methods were limited to system sizes of up to 16 particles; the new method allows calculations for systems with many more particles
- At half-filling with equally spaced levels \(\epsilon_\alpha = \alpha\), the ground state rapidities form an arc in the complex plane for sufficiently large coupling \(g\)
- For even \(N\) (number of levels), all rapidities form complex conjugate pairs at large \(g\); for odd \(N_r\), the largest rapidity remains real

## Context

This work extends the determinant representation approach of Zhou et al. (2002) and Amico and Osterloh (2002) for static correlation functions in the Richardson pairing model, reducing the computational complexity significantly. It enables systematic study of the crossover from mesoscopic to macroscopic physics in superconducting systems, going beyond previous results limited to fewer particles by MFF (1998) and Amico and Osterloh (2002). The method allows calculation of correlation functions for systems with fixed particle number, relevant for nuclei and ultra-small metallic grains where the canonical ensemble is required.
