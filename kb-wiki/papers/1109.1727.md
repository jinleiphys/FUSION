# Calculation of the even-odd energy difference in superfluid Fermi systems using the pseudopotential theory

> The pseudopotential theory is extended to the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations to determine the excess energy when one atom is added to the trapped superfluid Fermi system with even number of atoms. Particular attention is paid to systems being at the Feshbach resonance point. The results for relatively small particle numbers are in harmony with the Monte-Carlo calculations, but are also relevant for systems with larger particle numbers. Concerning the additional one quasiparticle state we define and determine two new universal numbers to characterize its widths.

## Key claim

The authors extend pseudopotential theory to the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations to calculate the excess energy when one atom is added to a trapped superfluid Fermi system with an even number of atoms, focusing on the Feshbach resonance (unitarity) where the s-wave scattering length diverges. The method yields results consistent with Monte Carlo calculations for small particle numbers and predicts an N^(1/9) scaling for larger systems, but the approach relies on a local density approximation and a model pseudopotential that neglects nonlocality near the Thomas-Fermi boundary.

## Method

The authors generalize the pseudopotential method to the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations for a two-component Fermi gas at zero temperature in a spherical harmonic trap, working at unitarity where the interaction range can be neglected. They construct a nonlocal pseudopotential operator that cancels the external and mean-field potentials within the Thomas-Fermi region, reducing the BdG equations to a simplified form with a diagonal pseudopotential that becomes a delta-function in the local density approximation. The smallest positive eigenvalue of the modified BdG equations is obtained by expanding the pseudo wave functions in a 3D harmonic oscillator basis (up to n_max=100) and numerically diagonalizing the matrix, with the pair potential Δ(r) taken as proportional to (μ - U_ext(r)) using a universal constant δ.

## Key numbers

- The energy difference E^(N+1) - E^(N) - μ follows an N^(1/9) scaling law, as predicted by Son (2007).
- For N=20, the result lies between Monte Carlo findings (Blume 2007, Chang 2007) and the superfluid density functional calculation (Bulgac 2007).
- Two new universal numbers characterizing the widths of the pseudo wave function components are determined: A_u = 1.2774 and A_v = 1.14616, from fits to w_u = A_u (ħω/μ)^(2/3) and w_v = A_v (ħω/μ)^(2/3).
- The universal constant δ (the ratio Δ(r)/(μ - U_ext(r)) at unitarity) is taken as δ = 1.16 in the calculations.
- For l=0, with μ = 100 ħω and δ = 1.16, the smallest positive eigenvalue ε_min = 4.437 ħω.
- The universal constant ξ (the ratio (U(r)-μ)/(U_ext(r)-μ) at resonance) has recent values of 0.372(0.005) (Carlson 2011) and 0.376(5) (Mark 2011).

## Context

This work extends the pseudopotential method, originally developed for electronic structure in solids and quantum chemistry (Hellmann 1935, Gombas 1935, Austin 1962), to superfluid Fermi gases described by Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations, specifically at the Feshbach resonance where universal properties emerge. The results confirm the N^(1/9) scaling predicted by Son (2007) for the even-odd energy difference and provide two new universal numbers (A_u, A_v) characterizing the quasiparticle state width. The approach enables calculations for larger particle numbers than feasible with Monte Carlo methods (Blume 2007, Chang 2007) and could be improved by including Weizsacker-type gradient corrections (Csordas 2010) and restoring the nonlocality of the pseudopotential.
