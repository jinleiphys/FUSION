# Extended-soft-core baryon-baryon model III. $S=-2$ hyperon-hyperon/nucleon interaction

> This paper presents the Extended-Soft-Core (ESC) potentials ESC04a-d for baryon-baryon channels with total strangeness $S=-2$. For these channels no experimental scattering data exist, and alsothe information from hypernuclei is very limited.The potential models for $S=-2$ are based on SU(3) extensions of potential models for the $S=0,-1$ sectors, which are fitted to experimental data. Flavor su(3)-symmetry is broken 'kinematically'by the masses of the baryons and the mesons.Moreover, in ESC04a,b aldo the coupling constants are broken, albeit in a well defined way using the $^3P_0$ quark-antiquark pair creation model as a guidance. But the fit to the S=0 and $S=-1$ sectors provides the necessary constraints to fix all free parameters.Therefore, the potentials for the $S=-2$ sectors do not contain additional free parameters, which situation is similar to the soft-core one-boson-exchange NSC97-models. Various properties of the potentials are illustrated by giving results for scattering lengths, bound states, phase-parameters, and total cross sections. The features of $Ξ$-hypernuclei predicted by ESC04d are studied on the basis of the G-matrix approach.

## Key claim
The ESC04 potentials for the $S=-2$ baryon-baryon channels contain no additional free parameters beyond those fixed by fitting to the $S=0$ and $S=-1$ sectors, but the predictions for $\Xi N$ interactions differ qualitatively among the four model versions (ESC04a-d), with ESC04a,b giving repulsive and ESC04c,d giving attractive $\Xi$ potentials in nuclear matter.

## Method
The authors construct Extended-Soft-Core (ESC) potentials ESC04a-d for all baryon-baryon channels with total strangeness $S=-2$ by SU(3) extension of models fitted to $NN$ and $YN$ scattering data. The potentials include one-boson-exchange (OBE), two-pseudoscalar-exchange (TME), and meson-pair-exchange (MPE) contributions with gaussian form factors, and are solved via the multi-channel Schrödinger equation in configuration space. For the $\Xi N$ sector of ESC04d, G-matrix interactions are derived and used to compute $\Xi$-nucleus folding potentials for hypernuclear structure calculations.

## Key numbers
- The Nagara event indicates the $\Lambda\Lambda$-interaction is rather weak, in contrast to older experimental estimates (Danysz 1963, Prowse 1966).
- For the $I=0$ channel, the coupled system includes $\Lambda\Lambda$, $\Xi N$, and $\Sigma\Sigma$ states; for $I=1$ the system includes $\Xi N$, $\Sigma\Lambda$, and $\Sigma\Sigma$; for $I=2$ only $\Sigma\Sigma$ is present.
- The $K(495)$, $K^*(892)$, $\kappa(900)$, and $K_1(1270)$ mesons are identified as the strange mesons that give transition potentials between $\Lambda\Lambda$ and $\Xi N$ channels.
- The $\Xi N$ interaction of ESC04d is attractive enough to produce various $\Xi$ hypernuclei, as studied via the G-matrix approach.

## Context
This work extends the ESC04 potential series (Rijken 2005, Rijken and Yamamoto 2005) from the $S=0$ and $S=-1$ sectors to the $S=-2$ sector, where no experimental scattering data exist. It follows the approach of the Nijmegen soft-core NSC97 models (Stoks and Rijken 1999) which also used SU(3) extensions without additional free parameters for multi-strange channels. The results enable predictions for $\Xi$ hypernuclear structure and provide scattering lengths, bound states, phase parameters, and total cross sections for use in constructing $\Lambda$-, $\Sigma$-, and $\Xi$-nucleus potentials.
