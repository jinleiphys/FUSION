# How big is the source that produces quark gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions?

> We study, for the first time, the spatial extension of the "source" that produces quark gluon plasma (QGP) in ultra relativistic heavy ion collisions. The longitudinal dimension is studied as a function of time as the system evolves. The source size is found to exhibit a novel non-classical feature.

## Key claim

The spatial extension of the source that produces quark gluon plasma (QGP) in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions is found to be twice as large as the space between the two receding nuclei, a non-classical feature that arises because particles are produced in regions where the chromoelectric field identically vanishes, with the longitudinal boundary of the source located at |z| = 2t rather than at the classical field boundary |z| = t.

## Method

The authors compute the probability density for parton production in configuration space by taking the incomplete Fourier transform of a time-dependent chromoelectric field (CEF) that models the boost-invariant field between receding nuclei, then performing a multi-dimensional fast Fourier transform on the momentum-space production amplitude for gluons and quarks. The probability density P(r, t) is obtained by summing over spin, colour, and one of the two position coordinates of the produced pair, and is evaluated as a function of longitudinal coordinate z at several time instants. The CEF has the form E^a_i = δ_{i,z} E_0 (δ_{a,3} + δ_{a,8}) exp{(|z| - t)/t_0} θ(t) θ(t^2 - z^2), with support only in the interval |z| < t.

## Key numbers

* The longitudinal probability density for gluons extends as a broad plateau from |z| = t to |z| = 2t, where it falls abruptly and almost discontinuously, for all times studied (t = 1, 2, 5, 10, 20 in units of t_0).
* The ratio of probability for finding gluons in the region |z| > t (the classically forbidden region) relative to the region |z| < t varies from a maximum of about 38% at t = 1.0 (in units of t_0) to 15% at t = 20.
* For quarks, the corresponding ratio varies from 19% at t = 1.0 to 7% at t = 20.
* The quark results show the plateau structure clearly delineated at t = 10 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously proposed mechanism for QGP production via decay of a mean chromoelectric field (Jain and Ravishankar, 2003, preprint Nucl-Th/0212001), which differs from conventional approaches that employ the Schwinger mechanism valid only for uniform, constant fields. The study provides an independent theoretical investigation of space-time dynamics that could supplement HBT correlation measurements, which are beset with uncertainties including the assumption of Gaussian source profiles, ill-defined emission instants, and the RHIC puzzle (STAR Collaboration, 2001; PHENIX Collaboration, 2002). The results enable future self-consistent solutions of the transport equation to determine the full spatial dimensions of QGP as it evolves.
