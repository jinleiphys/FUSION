# Resonance width distribution for open quantum systems

> Recent measurements of resonance widths for low-energy neutron scattering off heavy nuclei show large deviations from the standard Porter-Thomas distribution. We propose a new resonance width distribution based on the random matrix theory for an open quantum system. Two methods of derivation lead to a single analytical expression; in the limit of vanishing continuum coupling, we recover the Porter-Thomas distribution. The result depends on the ratio of typical widths $Γ$ to the energy level spacing $D$ via the dimensionless parameter $κ=(πΓ/2D)$. The new distribution suppresses small widths and increases the probabilities of larger widths.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new analytical resonance width distribution for open quantum systems with a single decay channel, derived from random matrix theory, which deviates from the standard Porter-Thomas distribution by suppressing small widths and enhancing larger widths; the deviation is controlled by the dimensionless parameter κ = (πΓ/2D), and the Porter-Thomas distribution is recovered only in the limit of vanishing continuum coupling (κ ≪ 1).

## Method

The authors derive the width distribution using two complementary approaches: (1) starting from the joint distribution of complex energy poles of the effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian for N intrinsic states with GOE statistics and Gaussian random decay amplitudes, then integrating over all but one width variable via steepest descent; (2) using a doorway-state formalism where a single broad doorway state couples to N stable intrinsic states through Gaussian-distributed matrix elements, solving the secular equation via steepest descent. Both methods yield the same analytical expression, and the derivation is extended to include gamma-channels by shifting the distribution by the mean radiation width γ.

## Key numbers

- The control parameter is κ = πΓ/(2D), where Γ is the typical width and D is the mean level spacing.
- In the practical region far from super-radiance (κ < 1), the distribution reduces to P(Γ) = C χ²₁(Γ) (sinh κ / κ)^(1/2).
- The Porter-Thomas distribution is recovered in the limit κ ≪ 1.
- The possible values of widths are restricted to 0 < Γ < η, where η is the total sum of all N widths (the trace of the imaginary part of the effective Hamiltonian).
- With gamma-channels included, the distribution is shifted by the mean radiation width γ: P(Γ, γ) = χ²₁[Γ - γ] (sinh[π(Γ - γ)/(2D)] / [π(Γ - γ)/(2D)])^(1/2).

## Context

This work extends the random matrix theory of open quantum systems, building on the effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian formalism of Sokolov and Zelevinsky (1989) and the super-radiance mechanism reviewed by Auerbach and Zelevinsky (2011). It directly addresses recent measurements by Koehler et al. (2010, 2011) showing deviations from the Porter-Thomas distribution (Porter and Thomas, 1956) in low-energy neutron resonances on heavy nuclei. The proposed distribution provides a new baseline for analyzing experimental data, enabling separation of generic statistical effects from specific nuclear structure features such as doorway resonances or shape transformations.
