# Quantal diffusion approach for multi-nucleon transfers in Xe + Pb collisions

> Employing a quantal diffusion description based on the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach, we analyze the mass distribution of the primary fragments in the collisions of ${}^{136} \text{Xe}+{}^{208} \text{Pb}$ system at the bombarding energy $E_\text{c.m.} =526$~MeV. This quantal approach provides a good description of the primary fragment distribution without any adjustable parameter, including the effects of shell structure.

## Key claim

The stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach, combined with quantal diffusion coefficients computed from time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) single-particle wave functions, reproduces the measured primary fragment mass distribution in \({}^{136}\)Xe+\({}^{208}\)Pb collisions at \(E_{\text{c.m.}} = 526\) MeV without adjustable parameters, but only when the potential energy surface is treated as asymmetric (different curvatures toward symmetry and asymmetry) due to shell effects and negative \(Q_{gg}\)-values.

## Method

The authors performed TDHF calculations for \({}^{136}\)Xe+\({}^{208}\)Pb at \(E_{\text{c.m.}} = 526\) MeV for initial orbital angular momenta \(l = 100\hbar\) to \(300\hbar\) to obtain mean drift paths, single-particle wave functions, and nucleon currents. From these TDHF solutions, they computed quantal neutron and proton diffusion coefficients \(D_{NN}(t)\) and \(D_{ZZ}(t)\) using the SMF formalism, including Pauli blocking terms. To determine the curvature parameters of the potential energy surface in the (N,Z) plane, they performed additional TDHF calculations for the neighboring systems \({}^{130}\)Te+\({}^{214}\)Po and \({}^{138}\)Ce+\({}^{206}\)Pt at the same bombarding energy and extracted the iso-scalar curvature \(\beta_{<} = 0.127\) (asymmetric side) and the iso-vector curvature \(\alpha\) from the drift and diffusion coefficients via Einstein relations. The mass distribution of primary fragments was then obtained by solving the coupled Langevin equations for the variances \(\sigma_{NN}^2\), \(\sigma_{ZZ}^2\), and \(\sigma_{NZ}^2\), yielding a Gaussian distribution for the mass number \(A\).

## Key numbers

- The bombarding energy is \(E_{\text{c.m.}} = 526\) MeV for the \({}^{136}\)Xe+\({}^{208}\)Pb system.
- The initial orbital angular momenta considered range from \(l = 100\hbar\) to \(300\hbar\).
- The contact time between the colliding nuclei is about 600 fm/c, while the memory time for nucleon diffusion is estimated as \(\tau_T = \tau_P \approx 25\) fm/c.
- The average iso-scalar curvature parameter in the asymmetric direction is \(\beta_{<} = 0.127\), extracted from the \({}^{130}\)Te+\({}^{214}\)Po system over the time interval \(t_1 = 480\) fm/c to \(t_2 = 540\) fm/c.
- The TDHF calculations show very small mass drift: for example, at \(l = 100\hbar\) the final projectile-like fragment has \(A_1^f = 135\) and \(Z_1^f = 53.6\), while the target-like fragment has \(A_2^f = 209\) and \(Z_2^f = 82.4\), with total kinetic energy TKE = 346 MeV and total excitation energy \(E^* = 185\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic mean-field approach of Ayik (2008) and Lacroix (2014) to multi-nucleon transfer in heavy-ion collisions, providing a fully microscopic alternative to the semi-classical Langevin models of Zagrebaev (2008, 2011) and Karpov (2017) that require adjustable parameters. The method overcomes the limitations of standard TDHF, which gives nearly zero mass drift for this system, and the technical difficulties of the time-dependent random phase approximation (TDRPA) approach of Balian and Veneroni (1984, 1985) for asymmetric systems. The results enable parameter-free predictions of primary fragment distributions for heavy projectile-target combinations near the Coulomb barrier, relevant for synthesizing neutron-rich nuclei.
