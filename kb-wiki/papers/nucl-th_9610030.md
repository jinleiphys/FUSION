# Probability of a Solution to the Solar Neutrino Problem Within the Minimal Standard Model

> Tests, independent of any solar model, can be made of whether solar neutrino experiments are consistent with the minimal Standard Model (stable, massless neutrinos). If the experimental uncertainties are correctly estimated and the sun is generating energy by light-element fusion in quasi-static equilibrium, the probability of a standard-physics solution is less than 2%. Even when the luminosity constraint is abandoned, the probability is not more than 4%. The sensitivity of the conclusions to input parameters is explored.

## Key claim

If experimental uncertainties are correctly estimated and the sun generates energy by light-element fusion in quasi-static equilibrium, the probability that solar neutrino data can be described within the minimal Standard Model (stable, massless neutrinos) is less than 2%; even when the luminosity constraint is abandoned, the probability is not more than 4%.

## Method

The authors performed a model-independent analysis by solving the system of equations linking the measured capture rates from the Homestake Cl-Ar, Kamiokande, SAGE, and Gallex experiments to the fluxes of neutrino sources (pp+pep, 7Be+CNO, and 8B), using only laboratory-determined cross sections and electroweak theory. They tested consistency both with and without the additional constraint that the total neutrino flux must match the measured solar luminosity, and computed the probability that the derived fluxes are physically realizable (non-negative). The sensitivity of the conclusions to input parameters was explored by computing differential coefficients for the derived 7Be+CNO flux.

## Key numbers

- Probability of a standard-physics solution with the luminosity constraint: less than 2%
- Probability of a standard-physics solution without the luminosity constraint: not more than 4%
- Homestake Cl-Ar capture rate: 2.55 ± 0.17 ± 0.18 SNU
- Kamiokande 8B neutrino flux: (2.95 +0.22/-0.21 ± 0.36) × 10^6 8B ν_e cm^-2 s^-1
- Weighted average of SAGE and Gallex capture rates: 73.8 ± 7.8 SNU
- Derived 7Be+CNO flux (luminosity unconstrained): -0.43 ± 0.24 × 10^10 cm^-2 s^-1
- Derived 7Be+CNO flux (luminosity constrained): -0.25 ± 0.11 × 10^10 cm^-2 s^-1
- Measured solar irradiance I: 85.31 ± 0.34 × 10^10 MeV cm^-2 s^-1
- With unpublished new data, confidence levels reach 99.5% (with luminosity) and 94% (without luminosity)

## Context

This work extends the model-independent analyses of Hata et al. (1994), who showed the data to be inconsistent with hydrogen burning and the luminosity constraint without new physics. The authors add a test free of the luminosity constraint, a test for inconsistency with total solar luminosity, and explicit probability calculations. The results quantify the minimum extent of the solar neutrino problem and demonstrate that the solution is unlikely to be found in astrophysics, instead pointing toward new neutrino physics such as oscillations. The analysis enables future work by highlighting the most critical experimental inputs (Kamiokande data, gallium cross sections, and the 8B spectrum shape) and by providing a framework for testing new data from SuperKamiokande, SNO, and Borexino.
