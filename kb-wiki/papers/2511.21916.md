# Energy-momentum tensor form factor D(t) of proton and neutron

> The energy-momentum tensor (EMT) form factor $D(t)$ is finite and negative in hadronic models and lattice QCD when only strong forces are included. However, when electromagnetic forces are considered, the $D(t)$ of charged hadrons undergoes a dramatic change: at small $t$, it changes sign and diverges like $1/\sqrt{-t}$ as shown for the proton in the classical model by Białynicki-Birula based on residual nuclear forces which can be understood as a mean field approach. We construct an analogous neutron model and show that this framework accurately explains the electromagnetic proton-neutron mass difference. We demonstrate that, after appropriately rescaling the residual nuclear forces, the model can reproduce lattice data on the nucleon $D(t)$ up to $(-t)\lesssim 1\,$GeV$^2$ as well as QED effects. Based on this realistic model description, we show that the proton and neutron $D(t)$ form factors are practically indistinguishable down to $(-t) \approx 10^{-4}\rm GeV^2$ far below what can currently be accessed experimentally. We conclude that in the foreseeable future the $D(t)$ form factors of proton and neutron will practically look the same in experiments and phenomenology.

## Key claim

The proton and neutron energy-momentum tensor form factor \(D(t)\) are practically indistinguishable down to \((-t) \approx 10^{-4}\,\text{GeV}^2\), far below current experimental access, so that in the foreseeable future they will look the same in experiments and phenomenology, despite the fact that the proton \(D(t)\) diverges like \(1/\sqrt{-t}\) at small \(t\) due to QED effects while the neutron \(D(t)\) does not.

## Method

The authors construct a classical model of the neutron by taking the limit \(e \to 0\) in the Białynicki-Birula proton model, which describes the nucleon as a dust distribution bound by scalar (\(\sigma\)-meson) and vector (\(\omega\)-meson) fields plus (for the proton) the Coulomb field. The static field equations are solved analytically, and the EMT form factors \(A(t)\) and \(D(t)\) are computed from the resulting energy density, pressure, and shear distributions via Fourier transforms. The model parameters are taken from the QHD-I mean field theory of nuclear matter, with the dust mass adjusted so that the proton mass reproduces the physical value after including binding energy.

## Key numbers

* Proton-neutron electromagnetic mass difference from the model: \((M_{\text{prot}} - M_{\text{neut}})_{\text{e.m.}} = 0.95\,\text{MeV}\), compared to lattice QCD+QED result \(1.00 \pm 0.07_{\text{stat}} \pm 0.14_{\text{syst}}\,\text{MeV}\) from BMW (2014).
* Neutron binding energy: \(E_B^n = -16.6614\,\text{MeV}\); proton binding energy: \(E_B^p = -15.7089\,\text{MeV}\).
* Neutron radius: \(R_n = 1.03958\,\text{fm}\); proton radius: \(R_p = 1.04906\,\text{fm}\).
* Proton and neutron \(D(t)\) form factors are indistinguishable down to \((-t) \approx 10^{-4}\,\text{GeV}^2\); QED effects in the proton \(D(t)\) become noticeable only at \((-t) \lesssim 10^{-5}\,\text{GeV}^2\).
* The model reproduces lattice QCD data on the nucleon \(D(t)\) up to \((-t) \lesssim 1\,\text{GeV}^2\) after rescaling residual nuclear forces.

## Context

This work extends the classical proton model of Białynicki-Birula (1993) and the subsequent study of the proton \(D(t)\) by Varma and Schweitzer (2020) to the neutron case. It demonstrates that the simple mean-field framework accurately captures the electromagnetic proton-neutron mass difference and can be scaled to match lattice QCD results on the nucleon \(D(t)\) from Hackett et al. (2023). The results enable phenomenological studies of hard exclusive reactions to treat proton and neutron \(D(t)\) as identical for the foreseeable future, and they clarify that the QED-induced divergence in the proton \(D(t)\) is confined to momentum transfers far below experimental reach.
