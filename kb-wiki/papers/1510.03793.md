# Theoretical corrections and world data for the superallowed ft values in the beta decays of 42Ti, 46Cr, 50Fe and 54Ni

> Prompted by recent measurements, we have surveyed world data and calculated radiative and isospin-symmetry breaking corrections for the superallowed 0+ -to- 0+ Fermi transitions from 42Ti, 46Cr, 50Fe and 54Ni. This increases the number of such transition with a complete set of calculated corrections from 20 to 23. The results are compared with their equivalents for the mirror superallowed transitions from 42Sc, 46V, 50Mn and 54Co. The predicted ft-value asymmetries of these mirror pairs are sensitive to the correction terms and provide motivation for improving measurement precision so as to be able to test the corrections. To aid in that endeavor, we present a parameterization for calculating the f values for the new transitions to plus/minus 0.01%.

## Key claim

The paper extends the set of superallowed Fermi transitions with complete theoretical corrections from 20 to 23 by calculating radiative and isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections for four \(T_z=-1\) nuclei, but the authors note that the \(Q_{EC}\) values for three of these transitions are still poorly known and the new measurements have not yet reached the precision required to contribute meaningfully to standard-model tests.

## Method

The authors surveyed world data for the superallowed \(0^+ \rightarrow 0^+\) Fermi \(\beta\) decays of \(^{42}\)Ti, \(^{46}\)Cr, \(^{50}\)Fe, and \(^{54}\)Ni, compiling \(Q_{EC}\) values, half-lives, and branching ratios from published measurements. They then calculated the nucleus-dependent outer radiative correction \(\delta_R'\) using standard QED methods with four component contributions, the nuclear-structure-dependent radiative correction \(\delta_{NS}\) from shell-model calculations with multiple effective interactions, and the isospin-symmetry-breaking correction \(\delta_C\) separated into a shell-model component \(\delta_{C1}\) and a radial-overlap component \(\delta_{C2}\) using Woods-Saxon potentials constrained by measured separation energies.

## Key numbers

* \(^{42}\)Ti: \(Q_{EC} = 7016.83 \pm 0.25\) keV; half-life \(208.29 \pm 0.79\) ms; superallowed branching ratio \(48.1 \pm 1.4\%\); \(ft = 3090 \pm 88\) s; corrected \(\mathcal{F}t = 3096 \pm 88\) s
* \(^{46}\)Cr: \(Q_{EC} = 7600 \pm 20\) keV; half-life \(224.3 \pm 1.3\) ms; superallowed branching ratio \(76.7 \pm 2.3\%\); \(ft = 3120 \pm 110\) s; corrected \(\mathcal{F}t = 3130 \pm 110\) s
* \(^{50}\)Fe: \(Q_{EC} = 8139 \pm 60\) keV; half-life \(152.02 \pm 0.59\) ms; superallowed branching ratio \(74.3 \pm 1.6\%\); \(ft = 3060 \pm 140\) s; corrected \(\mathcal{F}t = 3080 \pm 140\) s
* \(^{54}\)Ni: \(Q_{EC} = 8787 \pm 50\) keV; half-life \(114.20 \pm 0.30\) ms; superallowed branching ratio \(78.9 \pm 1.2\%\); \(ft = 3170 \pm 110\) s; corrected \(\mathcal{F}t = 3180 \pm 110\) s
* The mirror asymmetry in \(\delta_{NS}\) between \(T_z=-1\) and \(T_z=0\) transitions ranges from \(0.115\%\) to \(0.270\%\), at the edge of detectability for experiments aiming at \(0.1\%\) precision
* A parameterization for calculating \(f\) values for the new transitions is provided to \(\pm 0.01\%\) precision

## Context

This work extends the 2015 survey by Hardy and Towner (2015), which covered 20 superallowed transitions, by adding three new cases (\(^{46}\)Cr, \(^{50}\)Fe, \(^{54}\)Ni) and updating \(^{42}\)Ti with data from Molina et al. (2015). The calculated \(ft\)-value asymmetries between mirror pairs (\(T_z=-1\) vs. \(T_z=0\)) provide a stringent test of the correction terms once experimental precision improves, and the parameterization for \(f\) values enables future high-precision measurements to contribute to tests of the standard model.
