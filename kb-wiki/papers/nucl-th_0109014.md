# Nuclear Anapole Moments

> Nuclear anapole moments are parity-odd, time-reversal-even E1 moments of the electromagnetic current operator. Although the existence of this moment was recognized theoretically soon after the discovery of parity nonconservation (PNC), its experimental isolation was achieved only recently, when a new level of precision was reached in a measurement of the hyperfine dependence of atomic PNC in 133Cs. An important anapole moment bound in 205Tl also exists. In this paper, we present the details of the first calculation of these anapole moments in the framework commonly used in other studies of hadronic PNC, a meson exchange potential that includes long-range pion exchange and enough degrees of freedom to describe the five independent $S-P$ amplitudes induced by short-range interactions. The resulting contributions of pi-, rho-, and omega-exchange to the single-nucleon anapole moment, to parity admixtures in the nuclear ground state, and to PNC exchange currents are evaluated, using configuration-mixed shell-model wave functions. The experimental anapole moment constraints on the PNC meson-nucleon coupling constants are derived and compared with those from other tests of the hadronic weak interaction. While the bounds obtained from the anapole moment results are consistent with the broad ``reasonable ranges'' defined by theory, they are not in good agreement with the constraints from the other experiments. We explore possible explanations for the discrepancy and comment on the potential importance of new experiments.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of nuclear anapole moments for 133Cs and 205Tl using the full Desplanques-Donoghue-Holstein (DDH) meson-exchange potential including π-, ρ-, and ω-exchange, rather than only long-range pion exchange. While the anapole moment constraints on PNC meson-nucleon coupling constants are consistent with broad theoretical "reasonable ranges," they are not in good agreement with constraints from other hadronic PNC experiments.

## Method

The authors evaluate the contributions of π-, ρ-, and ω-exchange to the single-nucleon anapole moment, parity admixtures in the nuclear ground state, and PNC exchange currents using configuration-mixed shell-model wave functions. They employ a form of the anapole operator derived from the extended Siegert's theorem that avoids one-body reductions, instead evaluating exchange currents and polarizabilities from shell-model two-body density matrices modified by short-range correlation functions. The summation over intermediate nuclear states in the polarizability is performed by closure, calibrated through more complete shell-model calculations in lighter nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The Colorado group's measurement of atomic PNC in 133Cs achieved 0.35% precision, yielding a definitive (7σ) nuclear-spin-dependent effect from hyperfine differences
- The anapole moment of a heavy nucleus grows as A^(2/3), so that for A ≳ 20 the anapole contribution dominates over tree-level Z^0 exchange in spin-dependent atomic PNC
- In the single-particle model for 133Cs, the leading-order (O(α^0)) contribution to the anapole moment is 61.4 (in units of C ≡ 4π√(2/7)(g_S+g_V) M b^2), reduced by ~20% to 50.5 when next-to-leading-order (O(α^1)) contributions are included with α = 0.1
- The spin-orbit strength parameter α ≡ f/ω is approximately 0.1 for nuclei near A ~ 100

## Context

This work extends the treatment of Haxton, Henley, and Musolf (1998) by including heavy-meson PNC contributions beyond long-range π exchange, using the full DDH potential framework of Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980). The approach differs from earlier calculations by Flambaum, Khriplovich, and collaborators (1984), Bouchiat and Piketty (1991), and others by avoiding one-body reductions of currents and potentials. The results enable comparison of anapole moment constraints with other tests of the low-energy hadronic weak interaction, and the authors identify discrepancies that motivate future experimental and theoretical work.
