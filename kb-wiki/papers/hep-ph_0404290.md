# New phases in CFL quark matter

> We consider order $α_s$ corrections to the squared masses of the pseudo-Goldstone excitations about the ground state of dense quark matter. We show that these contributions tend to destabilize the vacuum, leading to a surprisingly complex phase structure for quark matter as a function of quark mass, even for small $α_s$. In particular we find two new phases of CFL quark matter possibly relevant for the real world, for which the QCD angle theta-bar equals pi/2.

## Key claim

The paper shows that at next-to-leading order in the strong coupling constant α_s, the squared masses of pseudo-Goldstone bosons in color-flavor-locked (CFL) quark matter receive negative corrections that can destabilize the vacuum, leading to two new phases (η condensation and combined η+K⁰ condensation) even for small α_s, with the strong CP angle θ̄_QCD = π/2.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral effective Lagrangian for the CFL phase of dense three-flavor quark matter, expanding to O(δ) in (m_s²/μΔ)² and O(ε) in α_s m_s²/μ², where m_s is the strange quark mass, m is the light quark mass (isospin limit m_u=m_d=m), μ is the baryon chemical potential, and Δ is the pairing gap. They compute the free energy density including both the leading-order a₃ term (from color-triplet pairing) and the new O(α_s) a₆ term (from color-sextet pairing), then minimize the free energy to determine the vacuum alignment of the U(3) order parameter U = V Σ as a function of quark masses.

## Key numbers

- The tetracritical point coordinates at μ = 10¹⁰ GeV (perturbative regime): m* = 320 keV, m_s* = 89 MeV
- The tetracritical point coordinates at μ = 500 MeV (neutron star regime, assuming α_s/π ≈ 1, Δ ≈ 100 MeV, f_π ≈ 90 MeV): m* ≈ 4.6 MeV, m_s* ≈ 120 MeV
- The ratio of squared gaps: Δ₆² = α_s (ln 2)²/(162π) Δ₃²
- The leading-order kaon condensation condition: cos θ = min[1, c₀] with c₀ = 16 m a₃ μ²/(m_s³ f_π²)
- The η condensation condition: cos φ = Sign[a₃ m² - a₆ m_s² cos θ]

## Context

This work extends the analysis of CFL quark matter beyond leading order in α_s, where previous studies (Schäfer 2000, Bedaque 2001, Kaplan 2001) found only kaon condensation. The new η-condensed phases break an exact Z₂ parity symmetry and have θ̄_QCD = π/2, analogous to the Dashen phase transition (Dashen 1971) in vacuum QCD but now in dense matter. The results provide a rigorous starting point for exploring the phase structure of CFL matter at larger quark masses and guide numerical simulations of QCD at high baryon density and low temperature.
