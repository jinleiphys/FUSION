# The study of the nucleus-nucleus interaction potential for $^{16}$O+$^{27}$Al and $^{16}$O+$^{28}$Si fusion reactions

> Using the Monte Carlo simulation method accompanied by the modifying effects of the density distributions overlapping, we have examined the nuclear matter incompressibility effects for asymmetric systems with light nuclei, namely $^{16}$O+$^{27}$Al and $^{16}$O+$^{28}$Si fusion reactions. The obtained results show that the nuclear equation of state has considerable influence on the calculation of fusion probabilities for these asymmetric systems.

## Key claim

The nuclear equation of state has considerable influence on fusion probability calculations for asymmetric light-light systems such as ¹⁶O+²⁷Al and ¹⁶O+²⁸Si, even though the radii of the interacting nuclei are small; this effect is demonstrated at energies above the fusion barrier, where the authors explicitly ignore coupled-channel effects.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo simulation method with M3Y-Paris nucleon-nucleon interaction to calculate the nuclear potential for ¹⁶O+²⁷Al and ¹⁶O+²⁸Si. They modified the double-folding potential by adding a zero-range repulsive interaction to simulate Pauli Exclusion Principle effects and nuclear matter incompressibility, with the repulsive strength parameter V_rep and density diffuseness a_rep adjusted to match the predicted nuclear potential at r=0 and experimental fusion barrier heights. Fusion cross sections were then computed using the one-dimensional barrier penetration model (ODBPM) with WKB transmission coefficients.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fusion hindrance effects that used repulsive core modifications to the M3Y interaction for symmetric systems such as ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni and ⁶⁴Ni+⁶⁴Ni (Misicu and Esbensen, 2006) and for light-heavy systems such as ¹⁶O+²⁰⁸Pb (Esbensen and Misicu, 2007), as well as symmetric light-light systems like ¹⁶O+¹⁶O. The present paper applies the same methodology to asymmetric light-light systems with positive Q-values, demonstrating that nuclear matter incompressibility effects remain important even for small nuclei. The results enable future studies of fusion reactions at deep sub-barrier energies for these asymmetric light systems, where coupled-channel effects could be incorporated.
