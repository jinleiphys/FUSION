# Preliminary EoS for core-collapse supernova simulations with the QMC model

> In this work we present the preliminary results of a complete equation of state (EoS) for core-collapse supernova simulations. We treat uniform matter made of nucleons using the the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. We show a table with a variety of thermodynamic quantities, which covers the proton fraction range $Y_{p}=0-0.65$ with the linear grid spacing $ ΔY_{p}=0.01$ ($66$ points) and the density range $ρ_{B}=10^{14}-10^{16}$g.cm$^{-3}$ with the logarithmic grid spacing $Δlog_{10}(ρ_{B}/[$g.cm$^{-3}])=0.1 $ ($21$ points). This preliminary study is performed at zero temperature and our results are compared with the widely used EoS already available in the literature.

## Key claim

The authors present a preliminary equation of state (EoS) table for core-collapse supernova simulations using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, but only at zero temperature, which is a significant limitation since supernova simulations require finite-temperature EoS.

## Method

The authors model uniform nuclear matter as a system of non-overlapping MIT bags containing quarks that interact through scalar and vector meson fields in the mean field approximation. They solve the Dirac equation for quarks inside the bag, determine the effective nucleon mass by minimizing the bag energy with respect to the bag radius, and compute the total energy density and pressure from the resulting mean fields. The EoS table covers a grid of 66 proton fraction values from Y_p=0 to 0.65 with linear spacing ΔY_p=0.01, and 21 baryon density points from ρ_B=10^14 to 10^16 g.cm^-3 with logarithmic spacing Δlog10(ρ_B/[g.cm^-3])=0.1.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: n_0 = 0.15 fm^-3
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: B/A = -15.7 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation relative to free mass: M*/M = 0.77
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym = 34.5 MeV
- Compression modulus at saturation: K = 295 MeV
- Bag constant: B_N^(1/4) = 210.85 MeV
- Bag radius: R_N = 0.6 fm
- Bare nucleon mass: M = 939.0 MeV
- Quark-meson coupling constant: g_σ^q = 5.9810
- Vector coupling constants: g_ω = 8.9817, g_ρ = 8.6510
- Grid covers proton fraction Y_p = 0-0.65 with 66 points and density ρ_B = 10^14-10^16 g.cm^-3 with 21 points

## Context

This work extends the approach of Shen (1998, 2011) and Hempel (2012), who used Walecka model parameterizations (TM1, TMA, FSUgold) for supernova EoS, by instead using the quark-meson coupling model which incorporates quark degrees of freedom. The QMC model produces a softer EoS than the NL3 and GM1 parameterizations and gives a higher effective mass (0.77M) that agrees with neutron-lead scattering analysis by Johnson (1990), which may be advantageous when hyperons are included. The next steps are to compute the finite-temperature EoS grid and to study the non-uniform pasta phase at low densities, which the authors note is essential for realistic supernova simulations.
