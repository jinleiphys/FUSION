# Singlet pairing gaps of neutrons and protons in hyperonic neutron stars

> The $^{1}S_{0}$ nucleonic superfluids are investigated within the relativistic mean-field model and Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer theory in hyperonic neutron stars. The $^{1}S_{0}$ pairing gaps of neutrons and protons are calculated based on the Reid soft-core interaction as the nucleon-nucleon interaction. We have studied particularly the influence of hyperons degrees of freedom on the $^{1}S_{0}$ nucleonic pairing gap in neutron star matter. It is found that the appearance of hyperons has little impact on baryonic density range and size for the $^{1}S_{0}$ neutronic pairing gap, the $^{1}S_{0}$ protonic pairing gap also decreases slightly in this region $ρ_B=0.0-0.393$ fm$^{-3}$. However, if baryonic density becomes greater than 0.393 fm${^{-3}}$, the $^{1}S_{0}$ protonic pairing gap obviously increases. In addition, the protonic superfluid range is obviously enlarged due to the presence of hyperons. In our results, the hyperons change the $^{1}S_{0}$ protonic pairing gap which must change the cooling properties of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The presence of hyperons in neutron star matter has little impact on the $^{1}S_{0}$ neutronic pairing gap, but significantly increases the $^{1}S_{0}$ protonic pairing gap at baryonic densities above 0.393 fm$^{-3}$, which must change the cooling properties of neutron stars.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with TM1 parameter set, including $\sigma$, $\omega$, $\rho$, $\sigma^{*}$, and $\phi$ mesons, to describe baryonic interactions in neutron star matter containing n, p, $\Lambda$, and $\Xi$ baryons. The $^{1}S_{0}$ pairing gaps of neutrons and protons were calculated using the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) theory with the Reid soft-core (RSC) interaction as the nucleon-nucleon potential. The calculations were performed under conditions of electrical neutrality and $\beta$ equilibrium for two cases: matter without hyperons (npe$\mu$) and matter with hyperons (npHe$\mu$).

## Key numbers

- The threshold densities for hyperons are 0.320 fm$^{-3}$ for $\Lambda$, 0.389 fm$^{-3}$ for $\Xi^{-}$, and 0.734 fm$^{-3}$ for $\Xi^{0}$.
- The $^{1}S_{0}$ neutronic pairing gap exists in the baryonic density range $0.0 \leq \rho_B \leq 0.188$ fm$^{-3}$ in npe$\mu$ matter and $0.0 \leq \rho_B \leq 0.191$ fm$^{-3}$ in npHe$\mu$ matter.
- The $^{1}S_{0}$ protonic pairing gap exists in the baryonic density range $0.0 \leq \rho_B \leq 0.509$ fm$^{-3}$ in npe$\mu$ matter and $0.0 \leq \rho_B \leq 0.588$ fm$^{-3}$ in npHe$\mu$ matter.
- The $^{1}S_{0}$ protonic pairing gap decreases slightly in the region $\rho_B = 0.0 - 0.393$ fm$^{-3}$ and increases obviously in the region $\rho_B = 0.393 - 0.588$ fm$^{-3}$ when hyperons are present.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $^{1}S_{0}$ nucleonic superfluidity in neutron stars (Zuo et al. 2004, Chen et al. 2006) by specifically investigating how the inclusion of hyperon degrees of freedom modifies the pairing gaps. The results enable improved modeling of neutron star cooling, as the enhanced protonic pairing gap and enlarged superfluid range in the presence of hyperons would further suppress neutrino emission from direct Urca processes on nucleons (Yakovlev et al. 1999, Haensel and Gnedin 1994).
