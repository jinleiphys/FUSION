# Improved estimates of the nuclear structure corrections in $μ$D

> We calculate the nuclear structure corrections to the Lamb shift in muonic deuterium by using state-of-the-art nucleon-nucleon potentials derived from chiral effective field theory. Our calculations complement previous theoretical work obtained from phenomenological potentials and the zero range approximation. The study of the chiral convergence order-by-order and the dependence on cutoff variations allows us to improve the estimates on the nuclear structure corrections and the theoretical uncertainty coming from nuclear potentials. This will enter the determination of the nuclear radius from ongoing muonic deuterium experiments at PSI.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the nuclear structure corrections to the Lamb shift in muonic deuterium using chiral effective field theory potentials, finding that the total two-photon exchange contribution is -1.690 meV with an estimated 1.16% overall uncertainty, which improves upon previous theoretical estimates that did not systematically account for nuclear potential uncertainties.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the nuclear Hamiltonian in a harmonic oscillator basis to obtain deuteron ground and excited states, then compute the nuclear polarization contributions using a multipole expansion formalism that includes electric dipole, Coulomb distortion, relativistic, magnetic dipole, and finite-nucleon-size corrections. They perform calculations with the AV18 phenomenological potential and with chiral effective field theory potentials at NLO, N2LO, and N3LO orders, varying the regularization cutoff between 400 and 600 MeV to assess theoretical uncertainty from the nuclear force.

## Key numbers

- Total two-photon exchange contribution: delta_TPE = -1.690 +/- 0.020 meV
- Nuclear polarization contribution: delta^A_pol = -1.239 +/- 0.005 meV (1 sigma)
- Zemach term contribution: delta_Zem = -0.424 +/- 0.003 meV (1 sigma)
- Overall accuracy of delta_Zem + delta^A_pol estimated at 1.16% from quadrature sum of uncertainties
- From N2LO to N3LO in chiral expansion, delta_Zem + delta^A_pol changes by 0.3%, considered the systematic truncation error
- Proton two-photon exchange contribution: -0.043(3) meV
- Neutron-inclusive nucleon polarizability contribution: -0.027(2) meV
- Deuteron binding energy from AV18 and N3LO-EM potentials: 2.2246 MeV, matching experimental value 2.224573(2) MeV within 0.4%

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Pachucki (2011) using the AV18 phenomenological potential and by Friar (2013) using the zero-range approximation, providing a systematic uncertainty estimate from nuclear forces that was missing in those earlier studies. The results complement the dispersion-relation analysis by Carlson et al. (2014), which suffered from 35% uncertainty due to lack of low-energy quasielastic data. The improved theoretical precision will support the CREMA collaboration's muonic deuterium experiment at PSI for extracting the deuteron charge radius and testing the proton radius puzzle.
