# Studying the doublet bands at the borders of the $A \approx 130$ island of chiral candidates: the $^{120}$I case

> Based on the reported positive-parity doublet bands in $^{120}$I, the corresponding experimental characteristics including rotational alignment have been discussed and corresponding configuration assignment is reexamined. The self-consistent tilted axis cranking relativistic mean-field calculations indicates that this doublet bands is built on configuration $πh_{11/2}\otimes νh ^{-1}_{11/2}$. In addition, adopting the two quasiparticles coupled with a triaxial rotor model, the excitation energies, energy staggering parameter $S(I)$, $B(M1)/B(E2)$, effective angles, and $K$ $plots$ have been discussed and also compared with the available data. All of these results support the interpretation of chiral doublet bands for the positive-parity doublet bands in $^{120}$I, and hence identify this nucleus as the border of the $A \approx 130$ island of chiral candidates.

## Key claim

The positive-parity doublet bands in \(^{120}\)I are interpreted as chiral doublet bands, and this nucleus is identified as lying at the border of the \(A \approx 130\) island of chiral candidates, based on the condition that the high-\(j\) proton-particle and neutron-hole configuration (\(\pi h_{11/2} \otimes \nu h^{-1}_{11/2}\)) is still present but the neutron hole character is not ideal because seven neutrons occupy the \(h_{11/2}\) orbital.

## Method

The experimental rotational alignment of the doublet bands in \(^{120}\)I was extracted and compared with neighboring chiral candidates \(^{122}\)Cs and \(^{124}\)Cs. Self-consistent tilted axis cranking relativistic mean-field (TAC-RMF) calculations were performed using the point-coupling interaction PC-PK1 to determine the configuration. Additionally, a two-quasiparticle plus triaxial rotor model (particle rotor model, PRM) was used to calculate excitation energies, energy staggering parameters \(S(I)\), \(B(M1)/B(E2)\) ratios, effective angles, and \(K\) plots, which were compared with available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The yrast band in \(^{120}\)I is built on a (\(10^+\)) level at 1008 keV and extended up to the (\(23^+\)) state; the side band is also built on the (\(10^+\)) state and extended up to (\(17^+\)).
- The large initial alignments of about \(6\hbar\) for both yrast and side bands indicate the involvement of the \(h_{11/2}[550]1/2^{-}\) proton, which can donate about \(5.5\hbar\).
- The alignment of the yrast band suddenly increases by about \(2\hbar\) at a rotational frequency around 0.34 MeV.
- The energy differences \(\Delta E = E(I)_{\text{side}} - E(I)_{\text{yrast}}\) gradually decrease from 360 to 160 keV as spin increases from 14 to \(17\hbar\).
- The quadrupole deformation parameter \(\beta = 0.36\) and triaxial deformation parameter \(\gamma = 20^\circ\) were adopted for the PRM calculations.
- The moment of inertia \(\mathcal{J} = 25\hbar^2/\text{MeV}\) and pairing gap \(\Delta = 1.1\) MeV were used in the PRM.
- The effective angles \(\theta_{pn}\), \(\theta_{Rp}\), and \(\theta_{Rn}\) are greater than \(60^\circ\) around \(I = 10\hbar\) and remain larger than \(45^\circ\) in the spin region \(10-15\hbar\).

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral doublet bands to the lower edge of the \(A \approx 130\) island of chiral candidates, following earlier suggestions by Moon et al. (2003) and Li et al. (2013) for \(^{120}\)I, and recent shell-model and total Routhian surface calculations by Moon et al. (2018). The results support the interpretation of chirality in \(^{120}\)I and identify it as a border nucleus, enabling future searches for chirality in neighboring nuclei with neutron number \(N < 77\) or proton number \(Z < 53\).
