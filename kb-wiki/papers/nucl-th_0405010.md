# Superfluidity of $Σ^-$ hyperons in $β$-stable neutron star matter

> In this work we evaluate the $^1S_0$ energy gap of $Σ^-$ hyperons in $β$-stable neutron star matter. We solve the BCS gap equation for an effective $Σ^-Σ^-$ pairing interaction derived from the most recent parametrization of the hyperon-hyperon interaction constructed by the Nijmegen group. We find that the $Σ^-$ hyperons are in a $^1S_0$ superfluid state in the density region $\sim 0.27-0.7$ fm$^{-3}$, with a maximum energy gap of order 8 MeV at a total baryon number density of $\sim 0.37$ fm$^{-3}$ and a $Σ^-$ fraction of about 8%. We examine the implications on neutron star cooling.

## Key claim

The authors find that Σ⁻ hyperons in β-stable neutron star matter form a ¹S₀ superfluid state in a specific density range, but they caution that this result depends on the strongly attractive Σ⁻Σ⁻ interaction in the Nijmegen NSC97e model, which may be questionable given that experimental data on Σ⁻ atoms and hypernuclei suggest a much weaker or even repulsive Σ nuclear potential.

## Method

The authors solved the BCS gap equation at T=0 and at finite temperature for an effective Σ⁻Σ⁻ pairing interaction derived from the bare Σ⁻Σ⁻ interaction of the Nijmegen NSC97e baryon-baryon potential, using the scheme of Baldo et al. (1990) to sum two-particle excitations above a cutoff momentum of 2 fm⁻¹. The Σ⁻ fraction, single-particle energy, and chemical potential were taken from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of β-stable neutron star matter using the same NSC97e interaction, making this the first work to use a consistent baryon-baryon interaction model for all these properties.

## Key numbers

- Maximum energy gap of order 8 MeV at a total baryon number density of approximately 0.37 fm⁻³ and a Σ⁻ fraction of about 8%.
- The ¹S₀ Σ⁻ superfluid state exists for total baryon number densities in the range approximately 0.27–0.7 fm⁻³.
- The Σ⁻ superfluid exists for Σ⁻ number densities from 2.3 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³ up to approximately 0.15 fm⁻³.
- The critical temperature T_c is approximately 10¹⁰ K, well above the typical internal temperature of evolved normal neutron stars (approximately 10⁸ K).
- The threshold density for the rapid cooling reaction Σ⁻ → n + e⁻ + ν̄_e is around 1.6n₀, where n₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hyperon superfluidity by Balberg and Barnea (1998) and Takatsuka et al. (1999, 2000, 2002), who investigated Λ and Σ⁻ pairing using different interaction models. The authors find that Λ hyperons are unlikely to form a superfluid within their model, contradicting Balberg and Barnea (1998) but agreeing with Takatsuka et al. (2000, 2002) that Σ⁻ hyperons become superfluid as soon as they appear in matter. The results enable more realistic calculations of neutron star cooling rates, since Σ⁻ superfluidity can suppress the neutrino emissivity of the reaction Σ⁻ → n + e⁻ + ν̄_e by a factor of exp(-Δ_F/k_BT), potentially making surface temperatures more compatible with observations.
