# On multiple scatterings of mesons in hot and cold QCD matter

> We study the propagation of a color singlet q\bar{q} pair undergoing multiple scatterings in hot and cold QCD matter. The interaction of the dipole with the nucleus or plasma is described with the McLerran-Venugopalan and Gyulassy-Wang models respectively. We find identical results when expressed in terms of the saturation momentum of either the nucleus or the plasma. We compare two kinds of multiple scatterings, elastic and inelastic with respect to the target. When allowing the target to scatter inelastically, the difference with the elastic case is suppressed by a 1/N^2_c factor. We also discuss some implications of our results in the following situations: the survival probability of quarkonia in a hot medium, the production of high-p_T heavy mesons in nucleus-nucleus collisions, and the production of vector mesons in deep inelastic scattering off nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the four-point dipole correlator (which accounts for inelastic target scattering) can be computed analytically in both the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model for cold nuclear matter and the Gyulassy-Wang (GW) model for hot QCD matter, yielding identical results when expressed in terms of the saturation momentum. The difference between elastic and inelastic target scattering is suppressed by a factor of \(1/N_c^2\) in the large-\(N_c\) limit.

## Method

The authors compute the medium average of a two-dipole correlator \(\langle S_{q\bar q}(x,y) S_{q\bar q}(u,v) \rangle\) using the MV model for cold matter and the GW model for hot matter, both in the eikonal approximation. The calculation involves expanding Wilson lines in powers of the color field, applying Wick's theorem, and resumming all orders using a \(2\times2\) matrix eigenvalue method. For the GW model, a diagrammatic analysis in the large-\(N_c\) limit is performed separately for single-scattering and double-scattering diagrams.

## Key numbers

* The saturation momentum in the MV model is defined as \(Q_s^2(r) = \frac{g_S^4 C_F}{4\pi} \left( \int dz^+ \mu^2(z^+) \right) \log\left( \frac{1}{r^2 \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2} \right)\), with the infrared cutoff \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}\).
* In the large-\(N_c\) limit for the quarkonium case, the dipole-dipole correlator is \(\langle S_{q\bar q}(r) S_{q\bar q}(r') \rangle = e^{-\frac{Q_s^2}{4}(r^2 + r'^2)} \left[ 1 - \frac{16}{N_c^2} \frac{(r \cdot r')^2}{(r+r')^4} \left( 1 + \frac{Q_s^2 (r+r')^2}{8} \right) \right] + \frac{16}{N_c^2} \frac{(r \cdot r')^2}{(r+r')^4} e^{-\frac{Q_s^2}{8}(r-r')^2}\).
* For the heavy meson case in the large-\(N_c\) limit, the correlator is \(\langle S_{q\bar q}(r) S_{q\bar q}(r') \rangle = e^{-\frac{Q_s^2}{4}(r^2 + r'^2)} - \frac{1}{N_c^2} e^{-\frac{Q_s^2}{4}(r^2 + r'^2)} \left( 1 + \frac{Q_s^2}{2} r \cdot r' \right) + \frac{1}{N_c^2} e^{-\frac{Q_s^2}{4}(r-r')^2}\).
* The Debye mass in the GW model is \(\mu_D^2 = \frac{1}{6} g^2 T^2 (N_c + \frac{N_f}{2})\).

## Context

This work extends the study of multiple scatterings of color singlet dipoles in QCD matter, previously addressed for elastic target scattering, by deriving the full four-point function that includes inelastic target breakup. The results are relevant for understanding quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions (where Debye screening was proposed by Matsui and Satz), heavy-meson suppression in nucleus-nucleus collisions (where standard energy loss calculations by Adil and others underestimate suppression), and vector meson production in deep inelastic scattering off nuclei (relevant for future electron-ion collider programs). The paper enables quantitative comparison between collisional dissociation and Debye screening as mechanisms for meson dissociation in hot matter, and provides a framework for separating cold nuclear matter effects from hot matter effects in experimental observations.
