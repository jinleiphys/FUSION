# Searching for QGP droplets with high-$p_T$ hadrons and heavy flavor

> The search for the smallest quark-gluon plasma (QGP) droplets in nature has motivated recent small collisions system programs at RHIC and LHC. Unambiguous identification of jet quenching due to final-state interactions is key to confirming QGP formation in these reactions. We compute the nuclear modification factors $R_{AA}$ and $R_{p(d)A}$ of charged hadrons and heavy flavor mesons in large (Au-Au, Xe-Xe, Pb-Pb) and small ($d$-Au, $p$-Pb, O-O) colliding systems, respectively. Our results include the Cronin effect and initial-state parton energy loss in cold nuclear matter. In the final state, hard partons undergo collisional energy loss and branching that was recently derived using Soft-Collinear-Effective-Theory with Glauber Gluon (SCET$_{\rm G}$). In large colliding systems, medium-modified QCD evolution of the fragmentation functions dominates the nuclear correction. As the system size decreases, we find that cold nuclear matter effects, collisional energy loss, and QGP-induced radiations can become equally important. A systematic scan over the medium size and mass/flavor dependence of $R_{AA}$ provides the opportunity to separate these individual contributions and identify QGP signatures in small systems. Predictions for $R_{AA}^{h}$, $R_{AA}^{D}$, $R_{AA}^{B}$ in O-O collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV are presented with and without the formation of a QGP and contrasted with the corresponding $R_{p(d)A}$ calculations. Upcoming single-hadron measurements at the LHC will not only test the O-O predictions for both light and heavy flavor production, but will shed light on the possibly very different dynamics of $p$-A and A-A reactions at similar soft particle production multiplicities.

## Key claim

The authors compute nuclear modification factors for light and heavy flavor hadrons across both large (Au-Au, Xe-Xe, Pb-Pb) and small (d-Au, p-Pb, O-O) colliding systems, finding that cold nuclear matter effects, collisional energy loss, and QGP-induced radiation can become equally important as system size decreases. The key hedge is that unambiguous identification of jet quenching in small systems requires separating these individual contributions through a systematic scan over medium size and mass/flavor dependence of R_AA.

## Method

The authors use Soft-Collinear-Effective-Theory with Glauber Gluon (SCET_G) to compute medium-modified QCD splitting functions for both light and heavy flavor partons, incorporating collisional energy loss from hard-thermal-loop calculations. They implement medium-modified DGLAP evolution of fragmentation functions, including the Cronin effect and initial-state parton energy loss in cold nuclear matter via a dynamical approach with screened Coulomb potentials. The QGP medium is modeled using hydrodynamic simulations with temperature profiles, and the jet-medium coupling parameter g_s is fixed in large colliding systems to make predictions for small systems.

## Key numbers

* In central Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, the medium correction to the q→q+g splitting function at its peak can be much larger than the vacuum contribution
* In central O-O collisions at 7 TeV, the medium correction to the splitting function is estimated to be about a factor of three smaller than in central Pb-Pb at 5.02 TeV
* The screened Coulomb potential for cold nuclear matter uses typical transverse momentum transfer squared μ² = 0.12 GeV² and mean free paths λ_g = (C_F/C_A)λ_q = 1.5 fm
* The fluctuation factor for CNM energy loss is ε_fl = 0.7 for the moderate energy dependence of the initial parton flux
* The collisional energy loss of a quark per unit length in a weakly-coupled thermal plasma is given by dE_el/dΔz = (C_F/4)(1+N_f/6) α_s(ET) g_s² T² ln(ET/m_D²)(1/v - (1-v²)/(2v²) ln((1+v)/(1-v)))
* The radiative energy loss fraction scales linearly with medium size L, while the elastic energy loss fraction scales as L^{1/3}

## Context

This work extends the SCET_G framework previously developed by Idilbi (2008), Ovanesyan (2011), and Kang (2016) for parton interactions in nuclear matter, applying it to both large and small collision systems. It addresses the puzzle from recent observations at RHIC and LHC where small systems like d-Au and p-Pb show collective behavior but no clear jet quenching evidence, as reported by STAR (2003), PHENIX (2006), ATLAS (2016), CMS (2016), and ALICE (2017). The predictions for O-O collisions at √s=7 TeV provide testable benchmarks for the upcoming LHC oxygen run program proposed by Citron (2018) and Brewer (2021), enabling separation of cold nuclear matter effects from QGP signatures through comparison of light and heavy flavor suppression patterns.
