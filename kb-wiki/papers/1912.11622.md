# The $σ$ and $ρ$ coupling constants for the charmed and beauty mesons

> We investigate the $σ$ and $ρ$ coupling constants for the $DD$ and $D^*D^*$ interactions, based on correlated $2π$ exchange in the $DD$ and $D^*D^*$ interactions. Starting from the $D\bar{D}\to ππ$ and $D^*\bar{D}^*\to ππ$ amplitudes derived in the pseudophysical region ($4m_π^2\le t \le 52m_π^2$) with the $S$- and $P$-wave $2π$ correlations considered, we obtain the spectral functions for the $DD\to DD$ and $D^*D^*\to D^*D^*$ amplitude with correlated $S$- and $P$-wave $2π$ exchanges. Using the pole approximation, we estimate the $DDσ$, $DDρ$, $D^*D^*σ$, and $D^*D^*ρ$ coupling constants. We extended phenomenologically the present results to the region in $t\le 0$ and compare them with those from lattice QCD. The results are also compared with those of other models. We also present the results of the $BBσ$, $BBρ$, $B^*B^*σ$, and $B^*B^*ρ$ coupling constants. We observe that it is unlikely that the $σ$ and $ρ$ coupling constants for the $B$ and $B^*$ mesons are the same as those for the $D$ and $D^*$ mesons. On the contrary, they are quite larger than those for the charmed mesons.

## Key claim

The σ and ρ coupling constants for beauty mesons (B and B*) are significantly larger than those for charmed mesons (D and D*), contradicting the common assumption of heavy-quark spin symmetry that they would be equal; however, the results depend on the choice of cutoff masses and the omission of the K̅K channel introduces additional uncertainties.

## Method

The authors computed off-shell D̅D → ππ and D*̅D* → ππ Born amplitudes using effective Lagrangians from heavy-quark effective theory, then combined these with off-shell ππ amplitudes from the Jülich meson-exchange model via the Blankenbecler-Sugar equation to incorporate ππ rescattering. From the two-body unitarity relation, they extracted spectral functions for the DD → DD and D*D* → D*D* amplitudes with correlated S-wave (scalar-isoscalar) and P-wave (vector-isovector) 2π exchange. Using a pole approximation and dispersion relations, they extracted the on-mass-shell coupling constants g_DDσ, g_DDρ, g_D*D*σ, g_D*D*ρ, and f_D*D*ρ, and extended the results phenomenologically to the B and B* meson systems.

## Key numbers

- g_DD*π = 17.9 (from CLEO Collaboration data), with the strong coupling g = 0.59 and pion decay constant f_π = 132 MeV
- Cutoff masses used: Λ_D = 2.5 GeV and Λ_D* = 2.8 GeV, with an estimated uncertainty of about 20% from cutoff mass variation
- The spectral functions were computed in the pseudophysical region 4m_π² ≤ t ≤ 52m_π², where m_π is the pion mass
- The σ-meson mass was taken as 550 MeV and the ρ-meson mass as 770 MeV for the pole approximation

## Context

This work extends the method used for the NN interaction by Durso (1980) and Kim (1994), where correlated 2π exchange was used to determine σ and ρ coupling constants in the full Bonn potential, to the heavy meson sector. It provides coupling constants needed for molecular interpretations of exotic states such as X(3872) discovered by Belle (2003), where σ exchange provides medium-range attraction for DD and D*D* bound states. The results enable more quantitative studies of heavy meson molecules and can be compared with lattice QCD results from Can (2012) and Bracco (2001), as well as with quark model estimates by Liu (2008, 2010, 2019) and Lee (2009).
