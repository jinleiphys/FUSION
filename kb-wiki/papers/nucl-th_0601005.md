# Beyond the relativistic mean-field approximation: configuration mixing of angular momentum projected wave functions

> We report the first study of restoration of rotational symmetry and fluctuations of the quadrupole deformation in the framework of relativistic mean-field models. A model is developed which uses the generator coordinate method to perform configuration mixing calculations of angular momentum projected wave functions, calculated in a relativistic point-coupling model. The geometry is restricted to axially symmetric shapes, and the intrinsic wave functions are generated from the solutions of the constrained relativistic mean-field + BCS equations in an axially deformed oscillator basis. A number of illustrative calculations are performed for the nuclei 194Hg and 32Mg, in comparison with results obtained in non-relativistic models based on Skyrme and Gogny effective interactions.

## Key claim

This paper reports the first implementation of angular momentum projection and generator coordinate method (GCM) configuration mixing within a relativistic mean-field framework, specifically using a relativistic point-coupling model. The authors hedge that the geometry is restricted to axially symmetric shapes and that the calculations are illustrative rather than comprehensive, performed only for two nuclei (194Hg and 32Mg) as proof-of-principle.

## Method

The authors solve constrained relativistic mean-field + BCS equations in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis to generate intrinsic wave functions for different quadrupole deformations. They then perform angular momentum projection on these intrinsic states and use the generator coordinate method to mix configurations with different quadrupole moments, with the mass quadrupole moment serving as the generating coordinate. The norm overlap and Hamiltonian kernels are evaluated using the generalized Wick theorem, with the rotation operator matrix elements computed by transforming from the axially deformed oscillator basis to a spherical oscillator basis.

## Key numbers

- For 194Hg: the potential energy surface as a function of quadrupole deformation is calculated, showing a prolate minimum at quadrupole moment q ≈ 4 b and an oblate minimum at q ≈ -2 b, with the spherical configuration (q=0) lying higher in energy
- For 32Mg: the ground state becomes deformed as a result of the balance between rotational symmetry restoration energy correction and correlations from quadrupole fluctuations
- The oscillator frequency used for the basis is ℏω₀ = 41A^(-1/3) MeV
- The integration interval for the Euler angle β is reduced from [0,π] to [0,π/2] using symmetry properties

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field framework beyond the mean-field approximation for the first time, following similar non-relativistic approaches that used the generator coordinate method with angular momentum projection. The authors compare their results with calculations using Skyrme effective interactions (as in the work of Bender and collaborators, 2003) and Gogny effective interactions (as in the series of papers by Rodriguez, Egido, and collaborators, 2002-2004). This enables future studies of shape coexistence phenomena in relativistic models, particularly for neutron-rich nuclei where shell closures erode and deformed ground states appear.
