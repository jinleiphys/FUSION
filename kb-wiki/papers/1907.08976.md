# Harmonic potential theorem: \\extension to spin-, velocity- and density-dependent interactions

> One of the few exact results for the description of the time-evolution of an inhomogeneous, interacting many-particle system is given by the Harmonic Potential Theorem (HPT). The relevance of this theorem is that it sets a tight constraint on time-dependent many-body approximations. In this contribution, we show that the original formulation of the HPT is valid also for the case of spin-, velocity- and density-dependent interactions. This result is completely general and relevant, among the rest, for nuclear structure theory both in the case of ab initio and of more phenomenological approaches. As an example, we report on a numerical implementation by testing the small-amplitude limit of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock -- also known as Random Phase Approximation (RPA) -- for the translational frequencies of a neutron system trapped in a harmonic potential.

## Key claim

The authors show that the Harmonic Potential Theorem (HPT), originally proven for two-body forces depending only on relative coordinates, remains valid for interactions that depend on spin, velocity, and density (including three-body forces modeled as density-dependent two-body forces), provided the interaction preserves Galilean invariance. The proof relies on showing that the commutators central to the original HPT proof are unchanged by these generalized interactions.

## Method

The authors analytically extend the operator-method proof of the HPT (Appendix B.1 of Sahni (2016)) to Hamiltonians containing spin-dependent, velocity-dependent, and density-dependent interactions, verifying that the key commutators in Eqs. (B16-B19) remain unchanged. They then numerically test the extended theorem by performing Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations for neutron drops (2 to 50 neutrons) in a harmonic trap with \(\hbar\omega_{\rm trap}=10\) MeV, using the SAMi parameterization of the Skyrme interaction (a zero-range, spin-, velocity-, and density-dependent force). The translational mode frequency and transition density from RPA are compared to the HPT predictions.

## Key numbers

- For neutron drops with N=2 to 50 in a harmonic trap with \(\hbar\omega_{\rm trap}=10\) MeV, the RPA translational mode frequency \(\omega_{\rm RPA}/\omega_{\rm trap}\) ranges from 1.000 (N=2) to 1.007 (N=50), agreeing with the HPT prediction of 1.000 within permil accuracy.
- The fraction of the model-independent energy-weighted sum rule (\(m_1/m_1^{\rm D.C.}\)) exhausted by the translational mode ranges from 99.99% (N=2) to 99.89% (N=50), confirming the mode is essentially the only one excited.
- The root mean square deviation between the RPA transition density and the HPT prediction (Eq. 20) is around or smaller than \(6\times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\), corresponding to a numerical error at the 1% level or below.
- Total binding energies relative to the Thomas-Fermi solution (\(E/E_{\rm TF}\)) range from 0.844 (N=2) to 0.685 (N=50), and root mean square radii range from 2.22 fm (N=2) to 3.65 fm (N=50).

## Context

This work extends the original HPT of Dobson (1994) and Kohn's theorem (Kohn, 1961) to interactions relevant for nuclear structure theory, including the spin-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction and the density-dependent forces used in Skyrme-type energy density functionals (Vautherin and Brink, 1972). The generalization enables testing time-dependent many-body approximations such as time-dependent Hartree-Fock and RPA (Onida et al., 2002) for systems governed by these more realistic interactions, and is relevant for both phenomenological and ab initio approaches to nuclear structure (Gandolfi et al., 2011; Maris et al., 2013).
