# Bifurcations in Boltzmann-Langevin One Body dynamics for fermionic systems

> We investigate the occurrence of bifurcations in the dynamical trajectories depicting central nuclear collisions at Fermi energies. The quantitative description of the reaction dynamics is obtained within a new transport model, based on the solution of the Boltzmann-Langevin equation in three dimensions, with a broad applicability for dissipative fermionic dynamics. Dilute systems formed in central collisions are shown to fluctuate between two energetically favourable mechanisms: reverting to a compact shape or rather disintegrating into several fragments. The latter result can be connected to the recent observation of bimodal distributions for quantities characterising fragmentation processes and may suggest new investigations.

## Key claim

Central nuclear collisions at Fermi energies can exhibit bifurcations in their dynamical trajectories, where dilute systems fluctuate between reverting to a compact shape or disintegrating into several fragments; this behavior is connected to bimodal distributions observed in fragmentation experiments but does not necessarily require the system to reach thermodynamical equilibrium.

## Method

The authors developed a new three-dimensional transport model called Boltzmann-Langevin-One-Body (BLOB), which solves the Boltzmann-Langevin equation for the semiclassical one-body distribution function. The model implements nucleon-nucleon correlations by organizing test particles into phase-space agglomerates of Ntest elements of identical isospin to simulate nucleon wave packets, with accurate treatment of Pauli-blocking factors for the full phase-space extension of the wave packets. The scattering probability is computed by confronting the transition rate (weighted by relative velocity and differential N-N cross section) with a random number, and modulation functions are applied to adapt final-state density profiles to available vacancy profiles while conserving energy.

## Key numbers

- The free elastic N-N cross section at Fermi energies is taken equal to 50 mb, used to define the scattering distance for selecting test particles that form nucleon wave packets.
- The model employs Ntest test particles per nucleon to sample the dynamics via the test-particle method.
- The soft equation of state uses a compressibility of k_inf = 200 MeV (mentioned in the truncated text as part of the model description).

## Context

This work extends previous Boltzmann-Langevin implementations, particularly the approach of Rizzo (2008) which treated nuclear matter in a periodic box, by constructing a full model for heavy-ion collisions. It also builds on the prescription of Bauer and Bertsch (1987) for organizing test particles into wave packets, while correcting the incomplete Pauli-blocking treatment identified by Burgio (1991) and Chapelle (1992). The results connect to experimental observations of bimodal distributions in fragmentation by Bonnet (2009) and Pichon (2006), and suggest that bimodality can have a dynamical origin related to fragment-formation mechanisms as proposed by LeFevre (2009), without requiring thermodynamical equilibrium.
