# A Study of Fluctuations of Voids in Relativistic Ion-Ion Collisions

> Event-by-event fluctuations (ebe) of hadronic patterns are investigated in terms of voids by analyzing the experimental data on 4.5, 14.5 and 60A GeV/c 16O-AgBr collisions. The findings are compared with the predictions of a multi-phase transport AMPT model. Dependence of voids on phase space bin width is examined in terms of two lowest moments of event-by-event fluctuations of voids, <Gq> and Sq. The findings reveal that scaling exponent estimated from the observed power-law behavior of the voids may be used to characterize the various properties of hadronic phase transition. The results also rule out occurrence of 2nd order quark-hadron phase transition at the projectile energies considered.

## Key claim

The scaling exponents "c" and "s" derived from the power-law behavior of void fluctuations are much smaller than the ranges predicted for a second-order quark-hadron phase transition (0.75–0.96 for "c" and 0.7–0.9 for "s"), ruling out the occurrence of such a phase transition at the projectile energies of 4.5, 14.5, and 60A GeV/c.

## Method

Event-by-event fluctuations of hadronic spatial patterns were studied by analyzing the distribution of voids (contiguous empty bins) in pseudorapidity space for 16O-AgBr collisions at three beam momenta. The pseudorapidity range was transformed into a cumulative variable X(eta) to obtain a flat distribution, then divided into M bins (M = 16 to 96). For each event, normalized G-moments of order q = 2–5 were computed from the void fractions, and their event-averaged values <G_q> and entropy-like moments S_q were examined as functions of M. The results were compared with events generated by the AMPT model (versions v1.21–v2.21) with matched mean multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- For experimental data at 4.5A GeV/c: scaling exponent "c" = 0.26; "s" = 0.23.
- For experimental data at 14.5A GeV/c: scaling exponent "c" = 0.34; "s" = 0.31.
- For experimental data at 60A GeV/c: scaling exponent "c" = 0.46; "s" = 0.38.
- For comparison, in 200A GeV/c 32S-AgBr collisions, previously reported values were "c" = 0.56 ± 0.01 and "s" = 0.43 ± 0.02.
- The predicted ranges for a second-order quark-hadron phase transition are "c" = 0.75–0.96 and "s" = 0.7–0.9.
- Event samples: 1200 events at 4.5A GeV/c, 370 events at 14.5A GeV/c, and 422 events at 60A GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the void fluctuation method proposed by Hwa and Zhang (2001) to lower beam energies (AGS and SPS range), following earlier applications to 200A GeV/c 32S-AgBr and 350 GeV/c pi- AgBr collisions by other authors. The results establish a baseline for event-by-event fluctuations from non-critical-point physics at these energies, using emulsion data with full phase-space coverage. The findings enable future comparisons with higher-energy RHIC and LHC data where critical point searches using higher moments of conserved quantities (e.g., net proton distributions studied by the STAR collaboration) are ongoing.
