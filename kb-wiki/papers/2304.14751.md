# Building models of quarks and gluons with an arbitrary number of colors using Cartan-Polyakov loops

> In this work we introduce the concept of Cartan-Polyakov loops, a special subset of Polyakov loops in the fundamental representation of the $\mathrm{SU}(N_c)$ group, with charges $k=1,\ldots,(N_c-1)/2$. It constitutes a sufficient set of independent degrees of freedom and it is used to parametrize the thermal Wilson line. Polyakov loops not contained in this set are classified as non-Cartan-Polyakov loops. Using properties of the characteristic polynomial of the thermal Wilson line, we write a non-Cartan-Polyakov loop charge decomposition formula. This formalism allows one to readily build effective models of quarks and gluons with an arbitrary number of colors. We apply it to the Polyakov$-$Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio model and to an effective glue model, in the mean field approximation, showing how to directly extend these models to higher values of $N_c$.

## Key claim

The paper introduces Cartan-Polyakov loops as a sufficient set of independent degrees of freedom (Polyakov loops in the fundamental and antifundamental representations with charges \(k=1,\ldots,(N_c-1)/2\)) that parametrize the thermal Wilson line, enabling systematic construction of effective models of quarks and gluons for any number of colors \(N_c\), but the formalism is primarily demonstrated for odd \(N_c\) and in the mean field approximation.

## Method

The authors define Cartan-Polyakov loops as the subset of Polyakov loops in the fundamental and antifundamental representations of \(\mathrm{SU}(N_c)\) with charges \(k=1,\ldots,(N_c-1)/2\), and classify all other Polyakov loops as non-Cartan-Polyakov loops. Using properties of the characteristic polynomial of the thermal Wilson line and the unitary matrix identity \(c_j(M) = (-1)^N \det[M] c_{N-j}(M^\dagger)\), they derive a charge decomposition formula (Eq. 13) that expresses any non-Cartan-Polyakov loop of charge \(k\) in terms of loops with smaller charges, together with a Cayley-Hamilton-based formula (Eq. 17) for charges \(k \geq N_c\). They apply this formalism to extend the Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and an effective glue model to arbitrary \(N_c\) in the mean field approximation.

## Key numbers

- For \(N_c=3\), the Cartan-Polyakov loop set is \(\mathcal{C}_3 = \{ \ell_{\mathrm{F},1}, \bar{\ell}_{\mathrm{F},1} \}\), and the charge-2 loop decomposes as \(\ell_{\mathrm{F},2} = 3 \ell_{\mathrm{F},1}^2 - 2 \bar{\ell}_{\mathrm{F},1}\).
- For \(N_c=3\), the charge-3 loop decomposes as \(\ell_{\mathrm{F},3} = 1 - 9 \ell_{\mathrm{F},1} \bar{\ell}_{\mathrm{F},1} + 9 \ell_{\mathrm{F},1}^3\).
- For \(N_c=3\), the charge-4 loop decomposes as \(\ell_{\mathrm{F},4} = 4 \ell_{\mathrm{F},1} - 36 \ell_{\mathrm{F},1}^2 \bar{\ell}_{\mathrm{F},1} + 6 \bar{\ell}_{\mathrm{F},1}^2 + 27 \ell_{\mathrm{F},1}^4\).
- For \(N_c=3\), the charge-5 loop decomposes as \(\ell_{\mathrm{F},5} = 15 \ell_{\mathrm{F},1}^2 + 81 \ell_{\mathrm{F},1}^5 - 135 \ell_{\mathrm{F},1}^3 \bar{\ell}_{\mathrm{F},1} + 45 \ell_{\mathrm{F},1} \bar{\ell}_{\mathrm{F},1}^2 - 5 \bar{\ell}_{\mathrm{F},1}\).
- For \(N_c=5\), the Cartan-Polyakov loop set is \(\mathcal{C}_5 = \{ \ell_{\mathrm{F},1}, \ell_{\mathrm{F},2}, \bar{\ell}_{\mathrm{F},1}, \bar{\mathrm{F},2} \}\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Dumitru et al. (2003) on classifying Polyakov loop representations in the large \(N_c\) limit and of Hidaka et al. (2009) on parametrizing the thermal Wilson line, providing a systematic method for building effective models at arbitrary \(N_c\) rather than only for \(N_c=3\) or in the large \(N_c\) limit. The formalism enables studies of the conjectured quarkyonic phase (McLerran et al., 2007) and the behavior of the critical endpoint for increasing \(N_c\) (Kovacs, 2022), as well as systematic exploration of the deconfinement phase transition in models like the PNJL model and effective glue models.
