# Quadrupole dynamics of carbon isotopes and 10Be

> Electric quadrupole (E2) moments and transitions provide measures of nuclear deformation and related collective structure. However, matrix elements of the E2 operator are sensitive to the nuclear wave function at large distances and are poorly convergent within the ab initio no-core shell model approach. We demonstrate for the first time that the ratio of neutron to proton quadrupole transition matrix elements, Mn/Mp, is well-converged in the ab initio no-core shell model and provides a new and robust tool for comparing with experimental results. We find that our parameter-free results for Mn/Mp for the carbon isotopes and 10Be compare well with experiment, where available, and offer new insight into the quadrupole dynamics of nuclear response.

## Key claim

The ratio of neutron to proton quadrupole transition matrix elements, \(M_n/M_p\), is well-converged in the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) even though individual electric quadrupole matrix elements converge poorly, and this ratio provides a robust tool for comparing with experimental results in carbon isotopes and \(^{10}\)Be.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio NCSM calculations using the Daejeon16 \(NN\) interaction, which is based on a softened chiral effective field theory potential, for \(^{10}\)Be, \(^{10}\)C, \(^{11}\)C, \(^{12}\)C (up to \(N_{\max}=10\)), \(^{15}\)C, and \(^{16}\)C (up to \(N_{\max}=8\)). They diagonalized the Hamiltonian in a many-body harmonic oscillator basis characterized by the energy scale \(\hbar\Omega\) and the truncation parameter \(N_{\max}\), then extracted the ratios \(M_n/M_p\), \(B(E2)/Q_p^2\), and \(M_nQ_p/M_pQ_n\) from the resulting wave functions.

## Key numbers

- \(^{10}\)Be (2\(_1^+\) → 0\(_1^+\)): \(M_n/M_p = 0.96(4)\) (converged), compared to experimental 1.1(1) from Iwasaki (2000)
- \(^{10}\)C (2\(_1^+\) → 0\(_1^+\)): \(M_n/M_p = 0.98(5)\) (converged), compared to experimental 0.70(8) from Jouanne (2005) and 1.05(20) from Furuno (2019)
- \(^{12}\)C (2\(_1^+\) → 0\(_1^+\)): \(M_n/M_p = 0.982(2)\) (converged); \(M_nQ_p/M_pQ_n = 0.9992(3)\) (converged)
- \(^{15}\)C (5/2\(_1^+\) → 1/2\(_1^+\)): \(M_n/M_p = 3.6(2)\) (converged), compared to experimental 3.6(4) from Chen (2022)
- \(^{16}\)C (2\(_1^+\) → 0\(_1^+\)): \(M_n/M_p = 3.6(2)\) (converged), compared to experimental 3.17(67) from Ong (2008)
- \(^{10}\)Be: \(B(E2)/Q_p^2 = 0.29(1)\) (converged)
- \(^{12}\)C: \(B(E2)/Q_p^2 = 0.24(1)\) (converged)

## Context

This work extends the class of ratio observables previously shown to be robustly convergent in NCSM, such as \(B(E2)/Q_p^2\) studied by Caprio (2022) for \(^8\)Li and neighbors. The results provide the first systematic ab initio study of \(M_n/M_p\) across multiple carbon isotopes and \(^{10}\)Be, going beyond earlier limited theoretical work using the \(0\hbar\Omega\) shell model (Sagawa 2004, Iwasaki 2000), antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (Kanada-Enyo 2011, 2020), and in-medium similarity renormalization group calculations for \(^{16}\)C (Jiang 2020). The converged ratios enable predictions of neutron quadrupole moments, which are experimentally inaccessible, and support the emerging picture that ab initio results align with collective enhancements in light nuclei.
