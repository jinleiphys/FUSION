# The production of $b\bar{b}$ dijet in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC

> We report our recent theoretical calculations for $b\bar{b}$ dijet production in high-energy nuclear collisions. The NLO+parton shower (PS) event generator SHERPA has been employed to provide the pp baseline of $b\bar{b}$ dijet production. A framework which combines Langevin transport equation to describe the evolution of heavy quarks and the higher-twist scheme to consider the inelastic energy loss of both light and heavy partons has been implemented. Within this framework, we present the theoretical result of transverse momentum imbalance $x_J=p_{T1}/p_{T2}$ both for inclusive dijet and $b\bar{b}$ dijet in Pb+Pb collision at 5.02 TeV. The energy loss of b jet would shift $x_J$ to smaller value relative to p+p reference which is consistent with the CMS data. In addition, we show the medium modification for angular correlation of $b\bar{b}$ dijet in A+A collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$~TeV. We observe a stronger suppression in small $Δϕ=|ϕ_{b1}-ϕ_{b2}|$ region where the gluon splitting processes dominate relative to large $Δϕ$ region. The difference leads to a modest suppression on near side ($Δϕ\sim 0$) and enhancement on away side ($Δϕ\simπ$).

## Key claim
The paper reports the first theoretical calculations of transverse momentum imbalance and angular correlation for \(b\bar{b}\) dijets in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, using a combined framework of NLO+parton shower event generation and in-medium energy loss. The key hedge is that the observed shift in the momentum imbalance \(x_J\) to smaller values relative to p+p is consistent with CMS data, but the predictions for angular correlation are presented as first results without direct experimental confirmation.

## Method
The authors used the NLO+parton shower event generator SHERPA to produce the p+p baseline for \(b\bar{b}\) dijet production. They then implemented a framework combining a Langevin transport equation to describe heavy quark evolution in the quark-gluon plasma and a higher-twist scheme to model inelastic energy loss for both light and heavy partons. Jets were reconstructed using the anti-\(k_{\rm T}\) algorithm in FASTJET, and the medium modification was applied to Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV.

## Key numbers
- Transverse momentum imbalance \(x_J = p_{T1}/p_{T2}\) for \(b\bar{b}\) dijets in central (0-10%) and peripheral (30-100%) Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV, compared with CMS data.
- The spacial diffusion coefficient was fixed at \(2\pi TD_s = 4\) from Lattice QCD calculations.
- The drag coefficient \(\Gamma\) and diffusion coefficient \(\kappa\) satisfy \(\kappa = 2ET\Gamma = \frac{2T^2}{D_s}\).
- The averaged \(x_J\) as a function of number of participants shows a reduction in central collisions for both inclusive dijet and \(b\bar{b}\) dijet, with a smaller reduction for \(b\bar{b}\) dijets in peripheral collisions relative to their p+p reference.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions by focusing on the flavour dependence of energy loss for \(b\) quarks, building on the framework of Wang (1991) for jet quenching and Norrbin (2000) for heavy flavour production mechanisms. It provides the first theoretical predictions for \(b\bar{b}\) dijet angular correlations in Pb+Pb collisions, enabling future comparisons with LHC data from CMS (Sirunyan 2018) and ATLAS (Aaboud 2016) to test the flavour dependence of parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma.
