# Two approximate formulae for the binding energies in Lambda hypernuclei and light nuclei

> Two approximate formulae are given for the binding energies in Lambda-hypernuclei and light nuclei by means of the (reduced) Poeschl-Teller and the Gaussian central potentials. Those easily programmable formulae combine the eigenvalues of the transformed Jacobi eigenequation and an application of the hypervirial theorems.

# Two approximate formulae for the binding energies in Lambda hypernuclei and light nuclei

## Key claim
Two approximate, easily programmable formulae for the binding energies of Λ-hypernuclei and light nuclei are derived from the reduced Poeschl-Teller (RPT) and Gaussian central potentials, but the Gaussian formula is only satisfactory for ground-state s-states, while the RPT formula works well for lower excited states of relatively heavy nuclei.

## Method
The author applies hypervirial theorems (HVT) to obtain s-series expansions for the eigenvalues of the RPT and Gaussian potentials, then uses the Jacobi eigenequation (JEQ) as an approximation to truncate these series into simple closed-form expressions. The resulting formulae are compared against numerical integration, a perturbation method from Lalazissis (1994), and experimental data for several Λ-hypernuclei.

## Key numbers
- For the RPT potential, the analytical formula gives E = -6.86 MeV for ²⁸ΛSi (core A=27, n=0, l=1) compared to the experimental value of -7.0 ± 1 MeV.
- For the RPT potential, the analytical formula gives E = -10.77 MeV for ⁵¹ΛV (core A=50, n=0, l=1) compared to the experimental value of -12 ± 1 MeV.
- For the Gaussian potential, the analytical formula gives E = -12.97 MeV for ¹⁶ΛO (core A=15, n=0, l=0) compared to the experimental value of -12.5 ± 0.35 MeV.
- For the Gaussian potential, the analytical formula gives E = -16.18 MeV for ²⁸ΛSi (core A=27, n=0, l=0) compared to the experimental value of -16.0 ± 0.28 MeV.
- For the Gaussian potential, the analytical formula gives E = -21.26 MeV for ⁸⁹ΛY (core A=88, n=0, l=0) compared to the experimental value of -22.1 ± 1.6 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the use of the Jacobi eigenequation as an approximation to the Schrödinger equation for Gaussian potentials, following Besbes (1982) and earlier studies on hypernuclear binding energies. The method provides a simpler alternative to the perturbation approach of Lalazissis (1994) and can handle states (such as 2p and 2d) that the perturbation method could not cover. The s-dependence of the formulae enables straightforward study of how potential depth, width, and nuclear mass number affect binding energies, and the kinetic energy can be derived via the Hellman-Feynmann theorem.
