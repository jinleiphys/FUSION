# Exploring QCD phase diagram at vanishing baryon density on the lattice

> I report on the current status of QCD phase diagram at vanishing baryon density. I focus on the QCD phase diagram with three degenerate quark flavor using Highly Improved Staggered Quarks on $N_τ=6$ lattices. No evidence of a first order phase transition in the pion mass window of $80\lesssim m_π \lesssim 230 $MeV is found. The pion mass at the critical point where the chiral first order phase transition ends is estimated to be $m^c_π \lesssim 45$ MeV.

# Exploring QCD phase diagram at vanishing baryon density on the lattice

## Key claim
Using Highly Improved Staggered Quarks on Nτ=6 lattices, no evidence of a first order phase transition is found in the pion mass window of 80 ≲ mπ ≲ 230 MeV, and the pion mass at the critical point where the chiral first order phase transition ends is estimated to be mπc ≲ 45 MeV, an upper bound, not a precise determination, and subject to finite volume and lattice cutoff effects.

## Method
The author performed lattice QCD simulations with three degenerate quark flavors using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action on Nτ=6 lattices, with quark masses corresponding to pion masses from 80 to 230 MeV. Volume dependence of the chiral condensate and chiral susceptibility was studied on lattices ranging from 10³×6 to 24³×6, and a Z(2) universal scaling fit to the inverse peak heights of the chiral susceptibility was used to estimate the critical quark mass.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass window studied: 80 ≲ mπ ≲ 230 MeV; no first order phase transition observed in this range.
- Upper bound on the critical pion mass: mπc ≲ 45 MeV, obtained from Z(2) scaling analysis of chiral susceptibility peak heights.
- Quark masses simulated: amq = 0.0075, 0.00375, 0.0025, 0.001875, 0.00125, and 0.0009375, corresponding to pion masses of 230, 160, 130, 110, 90, and 80 MeV respectively.
- Maximum number of trajectories per parameter set ranged from 7670 to 12900.
- The critical point in the quark mass plane is estimated at approximately (msphys/270, msphys/270), far from the physical point at (msphys/27, msphys).

## Context
This work extends lattice studies of the 3-flavor QCD phase diagram using improved staggered fermions, following earlier simulations with unimproved and p4-improved actions that found first order transitions at coarser lattice spacings with critical pion masses varying from ~300 MeV to ~50 MeV (Karsch 2004, 1stOrder, stout, HISQmc). The results suggest the first order region is very small, implying that the chiral critical surface at finite chemical potential must bend with a very large curvature to affect the transition at physical quark masses, consistent with findings from the imaginary chemical potential approach (deForcrand 2008) but with a more improved action. This enables future determination of the curvature of the chiral critical surface and assessment of the existence of a QCD chiral critical point.
