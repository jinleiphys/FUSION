# Skyrme mean-field study of rotational bands in transfermium isotopes

> Self-consistent mean field calculations with the SLy4 interaction and a density-dependent pairing force are presented for nuclei in the Nobelium mass region. Predicted quasi-particle spectra are compared with experiment for the heaviest known odd N and odd Z nuclei. Spectra and rotational bands are presented for nuclei around No252,4 for which experiments are either planned or already running.

## Key claim

Self-consistent mean-field calculations with the SLy4 Skyrme interaction and a density-dependent pairing force can reproduce the qualitative features of rotational bands and quasi-particle spectra in transfermium isotopes, but systematic discrepancies (e.g., the energy of the 9/2⁻ state in ²⁵¹Cf being too low by almost 300 keV) indicate that the spin-orbit strength and the positions of high-j sub-shells (1j15/2⁻ neutrons, 1i13/2⁺ protons) may need modification, though such changes are constrained by data from the A=190 super-deformed region.

## Method

Cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations were performed using the SLy4 parameterization of the Skyrme interaction in the particle-hole channel and a zero-range density-dependent pairing force with strength V = -1250 MeV·fm³ and a 5 MeV cutoff. The Lipkin-Nogami prescription prevented pairing collapse at high angular momentum, and quasi-particle excitations were calculated fully self-consistently, including core polarization and blocking effects.

## Key numbers

- Pairing force strength: V = -1250 MeV·fm³ with a 5 MeV cutoff.
- Ground-state deformation of ²⁵⁴No: β₂ = 0.28.
- Deformation of ²⁵¹Cf states: β₂ around 0.26, except the 1/2⁻ state at 410 keV which has β₂ = 0.273.
- Deformation of ²⁴⁹Bk states: β₂ around 0.255.
- Largest discrepancy in ²⁵¹Cf: the 9/2⁻ state is too low by almost 300 keV.
- In ²⁵⁴No, the only 2qp excitation below 2.0 MeV is a 7⁻ state around 1.5 MeV, with a magnetic moment around 1.5 W.U.
- In ²⁵³No, the ground state is predicted to be 9/2⁻, with a 1/2⁺ state at 300 keV; magnetic moments: 1 W.U. for 9/2⁻, 0.25 W.U. for 1/2⁺, 0.1 W.U. for 7/2⁺.
- In ²⁴⁰Pu, no alignment of a proton i13/2⁺ orbital occurs up to J=34 ħ, consistent with experiment.

## Context

This work extends previous cranked HFB studies of ²⁵²No and ²⁵⁴No by Duguet et al. (2001) to a broader set of even-even and odd-mass nuclei in the Nobelium mass region, including ²⁵¹Cf, ²⁴⁹Bk, ²⁵¹Md, ²⁵⁵Lr, ²⁵³No, and ²⁵⁵No. The results are compared with experimental data from Reiter et al. (1999), Leino et al. (1999), Herzberg et al. (2002), and Butler et al. (2002), as well as with prior calculations using the Gogny force by Egido et al. (2000), the SkM* interaction by Lafchiev et al. (2001), the relativistic mean-field model by Afanasjev et al. (2002), and the macroscopic-microscopic study by Muntian et al. (1999). The findings enable future experiments on odd-mass transfermium nuclei and highlight the need for refined spin-orbit strength in effective forces, while cautioning against direct extrapolation of single-particle spectra to super-heavy magic numbers.
