# Pairing-induced localization of the particle continuum in weakly bound nuclei

> The Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) problem for the cutoff local energy-density functional is solved numerically by using the Gor'kov formalism with an exact treatment of the particle continuum. The contributions from the resonant and "gas" continuum to the spectral density of the HFB eigenstates as well as the shifting and broadening of the discrete HF hole orbitals are clearly demonstrated with the illustrative example of the drip-line nucleus ^{70}Ca. The structure of the neutron density distribution in the localized ground state is analyzed, and the formation of its extended tail ("halo") is shown to be a collective pairing effect.

## Key claim

The formation of an extended neutron halo in the drip-line nucleus ⁷⁰Ca is shown to be a collective pairing effect, but the authors note that the density dependence of the effective pairing force is not well known and the extrapolation to the drip line with their parametrization may be questionable.

## Method

The Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) problem for a cutoff local energy-density functional was solved numerically using the Gor'kov formalism with an exact treatment of the particle continuum in coordinate space, employing physical boundary conditions for both bound and scattering states. The normal and anomalous densities were evaluated as contour integrals of the generalized Green's function, and the spectral densities were analyzed by integrating the imaginary parts of the Green's functions up to a finite radius. The calculations used the DF3 functional with a contact "gradient" pairing force whose parameters were deduced from neutron separation energies and charge radii in the Pb isotope chain.

## Key numbers

- Chemical potential for ⁷⁰Ca: μ = -149 keV
- For the "HF" potential in ⁷⁰Ca: scattering length a = -14.8 fm, effective range r_eff = 14.4 fm, giving an enhancement factor of about 7 for the s-wave level density compared to free gas (with R = 10 fm)
- Virtual s-state at ε₀ = -51 keV with width ≈ 23 keV
- Energy gap 2Δ̄ ≈ 10 MeV between the two components of the 1g₉/₂ resonance in ⁷⁰Ca
- For ⁷⁰Ca: R_F = 4.60 fm, d = 0.73 fm, giving R₁ = 6.2 fm (where ρ_n = 0.01 fm⁻³); for ⁴⁸Ca: R_F = 3.85 fm, d = 0.52 fm, R₁ = 5.0 fm
- Number of neutrons in the halo region of ⁷⁰Ca: N_halo varies from 1.41 to 5.30 (for tail density cutoff 0.01 fm⁻³), from 0.47 to 2.35 (0.003 fm⁻³), and from 0.19 to 1.04 (0.001 fm⁻³); relative increase for the latter choice reaches a factor of 5.5
- For the "constant" pairing force in ⁷⁰Ca: S₂ₙ = -236 keV (negative, indicating unbound)
- Energy cutoff: 40 MeV above the Fermi level

## Context

This work extends earlier HFB studies that discretized the continuum in a box (Dobaczewski et al. 1996, Mizutori et al. 1999) and recent continuum HFB calculations using a fixed mean field with discretized continuum (Bennaceur et al. 1999). The exact treatment of the continuum reveals the genetic relation between HF single-particle states and HFB resonances, showing how discrete hole orbitals acquire widths and shift in energy. The results enable future studies to test improved parametrizations of the effective pairing force and improve the predictive power of self-consistent mean-field models for drip-line nuclei.
