# Neutrinos and rare isotopes

> The close connection between neutrino physics and the physics explored at rare isotope facilities is explored. The duality between the Hamiltonian describing the self-interacting neutrino gas near the proto-neutron star in a core-collapse supernova and the BCS theory of pairing is elucidated. This many neutrino system is unique as it is the only many-body system driven by weak interactions. Its symmetries are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the many-neutrino system in a core-collapse supernova is mathematically dual to the BCS pairing problem in nuclear physics, but with the crucial qualifier that the sign of the two-body term is different and antineutrino terms are omitted in the single-angle approximation used to demonstrate the analogy.

## Method

The author derives the Hamiltonian for a self-interacting neutrino gas near a proto-neutron star, including both one-body (mass and matter) terms and two-body neutrino-neutrino interaction terms. For the case of two neutrino flavors in the single-angle approximation with negligible background electron density, this Hamiltonian is shown to be mathematically similar to the BCS Hamiltonian in the quasi-spin basis. The author then discusses the integrability of this Hamiltonian via constants of motion analogous to those found by Richardson and Gaudin for the pairing problem, and describes an RPA-like linearization procedure used to make the problem numerically tractable.

## Key numbers

- The number of neutrinos emitted from the cooling proto-neutron star is approximately \(10^{58}\), necessitating inclusion of neutrino-neutrino interactions.
- The neutrino-neutrino interaction strength is given by \(\mu = \sqrt{2} G_F / V\), where \(G_F\) is the Fermi constant and \(V\) is the volume.
- The Hamiltonian includes a term \((1 - \cos\vartheta_{\mathbf{p}\mathbf{q}})\) in the neutrino-neutrino interaction, where \(\vartheta_{\mathbf{p}\mathbf{q}}\) is the angle between neutrino momenta \(\mathbf{p}\) and \(\mathbf{q}\).
- The \(\nu\)-process production of \(^{19}\mathrm{F}\) by neutrino capture on \(^{20}\mathrm{Ne}\) accounts for the entire observed abundance of \(^{19}\mathrm{F}\).
- Matter-enhanced neutrino oscillations governed by mixing between the first and third neutrino generations boost \(\nu\)-process yields of \(^{11}\mathrm{B}\) and \(^{7}\mathrm{Li}\) for the inverted neutrino mass hierarchy, but not for the normal one.

## Context

This work extends the analogy between supernova neutrino many-body systems and the BCS pairing problem, building on the exact solution of the BCS Hamiltonian by Richardson and later generalized by Gaudin. The paper connects to prior work by Balantekin (2006), Pehlivan (2011), and Volpe (2013) on many-body aspects of supernova neutrinos, and to Otsuka (2005, 2009) on the tensor force in shell model Hamiltonians. The results enable better modeling of collective neutrino oscillations and their impact on r-process nucleosynthesis yields, which is a key driver for experiments at rare isotope facilities.
