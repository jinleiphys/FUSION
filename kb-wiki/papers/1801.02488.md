# Structure of $^{14}$N nucleus within a five-cluster model

> The spatial structure of $^{14}$N nucleus is studied within a five-particle model (three $α$-particles plus two nucleons). Using the variational approach with Gaussian bases, the ground-state energy and wave function are calculated for this five-particle system. Two spatial configurations in the ground-state wave function are revealed. The density distributions, pair correlation functions, and the momentum distributions of particles are analyzed and compared with those of the mirror nuclei $^{14}$C and $^{14}$O.

## Key claim

The ground-state wave function of \(^{14}\)N, modeled as three \(\alpha\)-particles plus a proton and a neutron, contains two distinct spatial configurations: one where the dinucleon center of mass coincides with that of the \(^{12}\)C cluster, and another where one nucleon lies outside the cluster. The abstract does not specify that this second configuration is less probable and is more pronounced in the mirror nuclei \(^{14}\)C and \(^{14}\)O than in \(^{14}\)N itself.

## Method

The authors solved the five-body Schrödinger equation using a variational method with a basis of up to 600 Gaussian functions, including explicit symmetrization over the three identical \(\alpha\)-particles. The Hamiltonian contained pairwise nuclear potentials (a local triplet \(np\) potential, generalized local-plus-separable \(p\alpha\), \(n\alpha\), and \(\alpha\alpha\) potentials) plus Coulomb terms. The \(\alpha\alpha\) potential parameters were slightly adjusted from earlier work on \(^{14}\)C and \(^{14}\)O to reproduce the experimental binding energy and charge radius of \(^{14}\)N. After obtaining the wave function, the authors computed density distributions, pair correlation functions, and momentum distributions, and compared them with results for \(^{14}\)C and \(^{14}\)O from the same model.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energy of \(^{14}\)N (subtracting the \(\alpha\)-particle internal binding energy): 19.772 MeV (used as a fit target).
- Charge radius of \(^{14}\)N: 2.558 fm (used as a fit target, from reference R15).
- Deuteron binding energy of the free \(np\) potential: 2.224576 MeV; charge radius: 2.140 fm; triplet scattering length: 5.424 fm.
- Local attraction intensity in the \(\alpha\alpha\) potential: \(-43.5\) MeV; local repulsion intensity: 240.0 MeV; attraction radius: 2.746 fm; repulsion radius: 1.530 fm.
- Root-mean-square relative distances in \(^{14}\)N: \(r_{pn}=2.237\) fm, \(r_{p\alpha}=2.692\) fm, \(r_{n\alpha}=2.683\) fm, \(r_{\alpha\alpha}=3.559\) fm.
- Root-mean-square radii in \(^{14}\)N: extra proton \(R_p=1.598\) fm, extra neutron \(R_n=1.585\) fm, \(\alpha\)-particle \(R_\alpha=2.064\) fm, matter radius \(R_m=2.556\) fm, charge radius \(R_{\mathrm{ch}}=2.558\) fm.
- Average kinetic energies in \(^{14}\)N: extra proton 33.52 MeV, extra neutron 33.53 MeV, each \(\alpha\)-particle 5.79 MeV.
- Average kinetic energies in \(^{14}\)C: each extra neutron 32.66 MeV, each \(\alpha\)-particle 6.83 MeV.
- Average kinetic energies in \(^{14}\)O: each extra proton 31.77 MeV, each \(\alpha\)-particle 6.62 MeV.
- Ratio of nucleon to \(\alpha\)-particle average velocity: about 4.8 for \(^{14}\)N, about 4.4 for \(^{14}\)C and \(^{14}\)O.

## Context

This work extends the five-cluster model (three \(\alpha\)-particles plus two nucleons) previously applied to \(^{14}\)C and \(^{14}\)O by the same group (Grinyuk and Piatnytskyi, 2016, cited as R6) to the mirror nucleus \(^{14}\)N. The authors adjusted the \(\alpha\alpha\) interaction to fit the \(^{14}\)N ground state, obtaining a larger \(\alpha\alpha\) distance (3.559 fm) than in \(^{14}\)C and \(^{14}\)O (about 3.2 fm). The two-configuration structure found here is similar to that reported earlier for \(^{6}\)He, \(^{6}\)Li, \(^{10}\)Be, and \(^{10}\)C (references R1–R5). The results enable future work to include antisymmetrization over all 14 nucleons for a more accurate charge density, which the authors note is beyond the present study.
