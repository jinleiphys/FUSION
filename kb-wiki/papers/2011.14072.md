# $Î·$ mesons in hot and dense asymmetric nuclear matter

> We study the $Î·N$ interactions in the hot and dense isospin asymmetric nuclear matter using two different approaches. In the first approach, the in-medium mass and optical potential of $Î·$-meson have been calculated in the chiral SU(3) model, considering the effect of explicit symmetry breaking term and range terms in the $Î·N$ interaction Lagrangian density. In the second scenario, the conjunction of chiral perturbation theory and chiral SU(3) model is employed. In this case, the next-to-leading order $Î·N$ interactions are evaluated from the chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), and the in-medium contribution of scalar densities are taken as input from chiral SU(3) model. We observe a larger negative mass-shift in the ChPT+chiral model approach compared to the chiral SU(3) model alone as a function of nuclear density. Moreover, the increase in the asymmetry and temperature cause a decrease in the magnitude of mass-shift. We have also studied the impact of $Î·N$ scattering length $a^{Î·N}$ on the $Î·$ meson mass $m^*_Î·$ and observed that the $m^*_Î·$ decrease more for increasing the value of scattering length.

## Key claim

The in-medium mass of the Î· meson decreases more strongly in the combined chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) plus chiral SU(3) model approach than in the chiral SU(3) model alone, but this negative mass-shift is reduced when the nuclear asymmetry or temperature increases, and the magnitude of the shift depends on the assumed value of the Î·N scattering length.

## Method

The authors used two theoretical approaches to compute the Î· meson effective mass and optical potential in hot, isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. In the first approach, the in-medium Î· mass was derived from the Î·N interaction Lagrangian within the chiral SU(3) model, including explicit symmetry breaking and range terms, with scalar and vector fields obtained from mean-field equations. In the second approach, the next-to-leading order Î·N interactions from chiral perturbation theory were combined with scalar densities computed from the chiral SU(3) model, and the resulting dispersion relation was solved for the effective mass.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Î· meson properties in nuclear matter, which have shown a wide range of predicted optical potentials from -20 MeV (Waas and Weise, 1997) to -85 MeV (Zhong et al., 2006), indicating strong model dependence. The authors build on the chiral SU(3) model previously applied to kaons and antikaons (Mishra et al., 2004; Kumar et al., 2011) and on the chiral perturbation theory approach for Î·N interactions (Zhong et al., 2006; Jenkins and Manohar, 1991). The results enable further studies of Î·-mesic nuclei formation and the momentum dependence of Î· meson properties in heavy-ion collision environments such as those at CBM and PANDA.
