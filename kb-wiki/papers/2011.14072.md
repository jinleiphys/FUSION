# $η$ mesons in hot and dense asymmetric nuclear matter

> We study the $ηN$ interactions in the hot and dense isospin asymmetric nuclear matter using two different approaches. In the first approach, the in-medium mass and optical potential of $η$-meson have been calculated in the chiral SU(3) model, considering the effect of explicit symmetry breaking term and range terms in the $ηN$ interaction Lagrangian density. In the second scenario, the conjunction of chiral perturbation theory and chiral SU(3) model is employed. In this case, the next-to-leading order $ηN$ interactions are evaluated from the chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), and the in-medium contribution of scalar densities are taken as input from chiral SU(3) model. We observe a larger negative mass-shift in the ChPT+chiral model approach compared to the chiral SU(3) model alone as a function of nuclear density. Moreover, the increase in the asymmetry and temperature cause a decrease in the magnitude of mass-shift. We have also studied the impact of $ηN$ scattering length $a^{ηN}$ on the $η$ meson mass $m^*_η$ and observed that the $m^*_η$ decrease more for increasing the value of scattering length.

## Key claim

The in-medium mass of the η meson decreases more strongly in the combined chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) plus chiral SU(3) model approach than in the chiral SU(3) model alone, but this negative mass-shift is reduced when the nuclear asymmetry or temperature increases, and the magnitude of the shift depends on the assumed value of the ηN scattering length.

## Method

The authors used two theoretical approaches to compute the η meson effective mass and optical potential in hot, isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. In the first approach, the in-medium η mass was derived from the ηN interaction Lagrangian within the chiral SU(3) model, including explicit symmetry breaking and range terms, with scalar and vector fields obtained from mean-field equations. In the second approach, the next-to-leading order ηN interactions from chiral perturbation theory were combined with scalar densities computed from the chiral SU(3) model, and the resulting dispersion relation was solved for the effective mass.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum mass of the η meson in the chiral SU(3) model is calculated as 574.374 MeV, which is within 4.9% of the physical mass of 547.862 MeV.
- At normal nuclear density (ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³), the optical potential of the η meson is observed to be more negative in the ChPT+chiral model approach than in the chiral SU(3) model alone, with the magnitude decreasing as asymmetry and temperature increase.
- The ηN scattering length a^{ηN} is used as an input parameter; increasing its value leads to a larger decrease in the η meson effective mass m*_η.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of η meson properties in nuclear matter, which have shown a wide range of predicted optical potentials from -20 MeV (Waas and Weise, 1997) to -85 MeV (Zhong et al., 2006), indicating strong model dependence. The authors build on the chiral SU(3) model previously applied to kaons and antikaons (Mishra et al., 2004; Kumar et al., 2011) and on the chiral perturbation theory approach for ηN interactions (Zhong et al., 2006; Jenkins and Manohar, 1991). The results enable further studies of η-mesic nuclei formation and the momentum dependence of η meson properties in heavy-ion collision environments such as those at CBM and PANDA.
