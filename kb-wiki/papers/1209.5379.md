# Particle Production at RHIC and LHC Energies

> The production of different particle species is recently measured in $Pb-Pb$ collisions by the ALICE experiment at $\sqrt{s}=7 $TeV. This motivates the use of various bosons and baryons measured at lower center-of-mass energies in comparing their ratios to the hadron resonance (HRG) gas model and PYTHIA event generator. It is found that the particle-to-antiparticle ratios are perfectly reproduce by means of HRG and PYTHIA at RHIC and LHC energies. The kaon-to-pion and proton-to-pion ratios are entirely overestimated by the HRG model. The PYTHIA event generator obviously underestimates the kaon-to-pion ratio and simultaneously reproduces the proton-to-pion ratio, almost perfectly, especially at LHC energy. While matter-to-antimatter and non-strange abundances are partly in line with predictions from the HRG model, it is found in the ALICE experiment that the measured baryon ratios are suppressed by a factor of $\sim1.5$. The strange abundances are overestimated in the HRG model.

## Key claim

The hadron resonance gas (HRG) model and PYTHIA event generator both perfectly reproduce particle-to-antiparticle ratios at RHIC and LHC energies, but the HRG model entirely overestimates kaon-to-pion and proton-to-pion ratios, while PYTHIA underestimates kaon-to-pion ratios and simultaneously reproduces proton-to-pion ratios almost perfectly, especially at LHC energy.

## Method

The authors calculated particle number densities using the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, treating hadron resonances as a non-interacting gas with masses below 2.8 GeV, assuming conserved strange degrees of freedom and characterizing chemical freeze-out by constant s/T^3 over the entire energy range. They compared these HRG results with PYTHIA event generator simulations performed for pp collisions at 2.76 TeV and with experimental measurements from RHIC at 200 GeV and ALICE at 2.76 TeV for Pb-Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

* Particle-to-antiparticle ratios from HRG at LHC energy: K-/K+ = 0.994, pi+/pi- = 0.9997, anti-p/p = 0.978, anti-Lambda/Lambda = 0.983
* Mixed particle ratios from HRG at LHC energy: K+/pi+ = 0.182, K-/pi- = 0.181, p/pi+ = 0.056, Lambda/p = 0.437, Lambda/pi- = 0.024
* The HRG model gives approximately 30% higher values for kaon-to-pion ratios than the measured ratios across the entire energy range
* ALICE measurements at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV show baryon ratios suppressed by a factor of approximately 1.5 compared to HRG predictions

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Tawfik (2010) on particle ratios as functions of center-of-mass energy, motivated by new ALICE measurements at 2.76 TeV. The results contradict earlier expectations from thermal models that successfully described particle abundances at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies. The findings enable further investigation into strangeness production mechanisms, particularly the discrepancy between HRG overestimation and PYTHIA underestimation of kaon-to-pion ratios, suggesting that strange quark production thresholds in QGP differ from those in hadronic matter.
