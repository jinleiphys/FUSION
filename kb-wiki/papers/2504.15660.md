# Electroweak form factors of baryons in dense nuclear matter

> There is evidence that the properties of hadrons are modified in a nuclear medium. Information about the medium modifications of the internal structure of hadrons is fundamental for the study of dense nuclear matter and high-energy processes, including heavy-ion and nucleus--nucleus collisions. At the moment, however, empirical information about medium modifications of hadrons is limited; therefore, theoretical studies are essential for progress in the field. In the present work, we review theoretical studies of the electromagnetic and axial form factors of octet baryons in symmetric nuclear matter. The calculations are based on a model that takes into account the degrees of freedom revealed in experimental studies of low and intermediate square transfer momentum $q^2=-Q^2$: valence quarks and meson cloud excitations of baryon cores. The formalism combines a covariant constituent quark model, developed for a free space (vacuum) with the quark--meson coupling model for extension to the nuclear medium. We conclude that the nuclear medium modifies the baryon properties differently according to the flavor content of the baryons and the medium density. The effects of the medium increase with density and are stronger (quenched or enhanced) for light baryons than for heavy baryons. In particular, the in-medium neutrino--nucleon and antineutrino--nucleon cross-sections are reduced compared to the values in free space. The proposed formalism can be extended to densities above the normal nuclear density and applied to neutrino--hyperon and antineutrino--hyperon scattering in dense nuclear matter.

## Key claim
The electromagnetic and axial form factors of octet baryons are modified in symmetric nuclear matter, with the modifications depending on both the baryon’s flavor content and the medium density. The abstract’s claim of “quenched or enhanced” effects is qualified by the finding that the effects are stronger for light baryons than for heavy baryons, and that in-medium neutrino-nucleon and antineutrino-nucleon cross-sections are reduced compared to free-space values.

## Method
The authors combine a covariant constituent quark model (developed for free space) with the quark-meson coupling model to extend calculations to symmetric nuclear matter. They then compute the electromagnetic and axial form factors of all octet baryons as functions of the squared four-momentum transfer, \(q^2 = -Q^2\), for low and intermediate momentum transfers. The model explicitly includes valence quark degrees of freedom and meson cloud excitations of the baryon cores.

## Key numbers

*   The effects of the medium increase with density and are stronger for light baryons than for heavy baryons.
*   The formalism is applicable for low and intermediate square transfer momentum \(q^2 = -Q^2\).

## Context
This work extends the covariant constituent quark model, previously applied to baryons in vacuum, to the nuclear medium by incorporating the quark-meson coupling model. It provides a theoretical framework for understanding medium modifications of hadron structure, which is essential for interpreting data from heavy-ion and nucleus-nucleus collisions. The formalism can be extended to densities above normal nuclear density and applied to neutrino-hyperon and antineutrino-hyperon scattering in dense matter, enabling future studies of neutrino interactions in astrophysical environments.
