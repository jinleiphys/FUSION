# The non-Abelian feature of parton energy loss in energy dependence of jet quenching in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> One of the non-Abelian features of parton energy loss is the ratio $ΔE_g/ΔE_q=9/4$ between gluon and quark jets. Since jet production rate is dominated by quark jets at high $x_T=2p_T/\sqrt{s}$ and by gluon jets at low $x_T$, high $p_T$ hadron suppression in high-energy heavy-ion collisions should reflect such a non-Abelian feature. Within a leading order perturbative QCD parton model that incorporates transverse expansion and Woods-Saxon nuclear distribution, the energy dependence of large $p_T\sim 5-20$ GeV/$c$ hadron suppression is found to be sensitive to the non-Abelian feasture of parton energy loss and could be tested by data from low energy runs at RHIC or data from LHC.

# The non-Abelian feature of parton energy loss in energy dependence of jet quenching in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The energy dependence of high-$p_T$ hadron suppression in heavy-ion collisions is sensitive to the non-Abelian ratio $\Delta E_g/\Delta E_q = 9/4$ between gluon and quark energy loss, and this sensitivity can be tested by comparing data from low-energy RHIC runs (e.g., $\sqrt{s}=62.4$ AGeV) with data from LHC, provided that the dense medium lifetime is sufficiently long to allow full parton energy loss.

## Method

The authors employ a leading-order perturbative QCD parton model with Woods-Saxon nuclear distribution and include both longitudinal and transverse expansion of the dense medium. Parton energy loss is encoded in an effective modified fragmentation function, with parameters tuned to reproduce RHIC data at $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV. The nuclear modification factor $R_{AB}$ is calculated for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=62.4$, 200, and 5500 AGeV, comparing a QCD case (where $\Delta E_g/\Delta E_q = 9/4$) against a non-QCD case (where gluon and quark energy loss are set equal).

## Key numbers

- The non-Abelian ratio of parton energy loss is $\Delta E_g/\Delta E_q = 9/4$ between gluon and quark jets.
- At $E=5$ GeV, the average 1-dimensional energy loss $\langle dE/dL\rangle_{1d} = 0.19$ GeV/fm (with parameters $\epsilon_0=1.2$ GeV, $\mu_0=1.6$ GeV); at $E=20$ GeV, $\langle dE/dL\rangle_{1d} = 1.05$ GeV/fm.
- The nuclear modification factor for neutral pions at 62.4 AGeV and 200 AGeV intersect at about $p_T = 11$ GeV/$c$ in the QCD case; for charged hadrons the intersection is at about $p_T = 10$ GeV/$c$.
- The energy loss in a corresponding static medium is 14 GeV/fm, about 30 times higher than in cold nuclei (Wang 2003).
- The suppression factor $R_{AA}$ for neutral pions at fixed $p_T = 6$ GeV/$c$ is calculated as a function of $\sqrt{s}$ from 20 AGeV to 5500 AGeV, with a change of slope around $\sqrt{s}=1300$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous LO pQCD parton model calculations (Wang 1998, Wang 2000, Gyulassy 2000, Vitev 2002) that used hard-sphere nuclear distributions and assumed only longitudinal expansion, by incorporating a more realistic Woods-Saxon nuclear distribution and including transverse expansion of the dense medium. The study builds on earlier investigations of flavor dependence of high-$p_T$ hadron suppression (Wang 1998) and on extractions of initial gluon density from RHIC data (Wang 2003, Vitev 2004). The results enable experimental tests of the non-Abelian nature of QCD energy loss by comparing hadron suppression across collision energies from RHIC to LHC, and suggest that above $\sqrt{s}=1000$ GeV the suppression becomes dominated by surface emission with finite depth, leading to weaker energy dependence.
