# Solutions of Conformal Israel-Stewart Relativistic Viscous Fluid Dynamics

> We use symmetry arguments developed by Gubser to construct the first radially-expanding explicit solutions of the Israel-Stewart formulation of hydrodynamics. Along with a general semi-analytical solution, an exact analytical solution is given which is valid in the cold plasma limit where viscous effects from shear viscosity and the relaxation time coefficient are important. The radially expanding solutions presented in this paper can be used as nontrivial checks of numerical algorithms employed in hydrodynamic simulations of the quark-gluon plasma formed in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. We show this explicitly by comparing such analytic and semi-analytic solutions with the corresponding numerical solutions obtained using the MUSIC viscous hydrodynamics simulation code.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the first radially-expanding explicit solutions of the Israel-Stewart formulation of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, but these solutions are restricted to conformal fluids and the exact analytical solution is only valid in the "cold plasma limit" where viscous effects dominate and the shear-stress tensor term in the relaxation equation can be neglected.

## Method

The authors used symmetry arguments developed by Gubser (2010) to impose SO(3) ⊗ SO(1,1) ⊗ Z₂ invariance on the fluid dynamical variables, which reduces the partial differential equations of Israel-Stewart theory to ordinary differential equations in a de Sitter coordinate system. They performed a Weyl rescaling and coordinate transformation to map the expanding fluid in hyperbolic coordinates to a static fluid in dS₃ ⊗ R space, then solved the resulting coupled ordinary differential equations for temperature and shear-stress tensor components. The semi-analytical solutions were obtained by numerical integration of the reduced equations, while the analytical solution was derived by taking the limit where η/(sT) ≫ 1 (the cold plasma limit).

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.2 was used for numerical comparisons, a value in the ballpark of that used in hydrodynamic simulations of the quark-gluon plasma
- Relaxation time parameter c = 5 was used, corresponding to τ_R = 5η/(ε+P), which is the typical value from Boltzmann equation approximations
- Initial temperature at ρ=0 was set to T̂(0) = 1.2, corresponding to T(τ₀=1 fm, r=0) = 1.2 fm⁻¹ with q = 1 fm⁻¹
- Numerical simulation used time step δτ = 0.005 fm and grid spacing δx = δy = 0.05 fm
- The analytical cold plasma limit solution requires 4c > 1 for the temperature to vanish at infinite ρ, and c > 1 for the dissipative correction to remain smaller than the ideal fluid contribution
- The asymptotic value of the dimensionless shear variable π̄_ξ^ξ approaches √(1/c) as ρ → ±∞ in Israel-Stewart theory

## Context

This work extends the symmetry-based approach of Gubser (2010) and Gubser and Yarom (2011), who derived SO(3) ⊗ SO(1,1) ⊗ Z₂ invariant solutions for ideal relativistic conformal fluid dynamics and relativistic Navier-Stokes theory. The authors provide the first nontrivial test solutions for Israel-Stewart theory beyond the Bjorken flow (Bjorken, 1983), enabling systematic validation of numerical algorithms used in heavy ion collision simulations. The solutions were used to test the MUSIC viscous hydrodynamics code (Schenke et al., 2010) and to determine the optimal value of the van Leer minmod flux limiter parameter θ, finding that θ = 1.8 (less diffusive) gives better agreement than the previously used θ = 1.1.
