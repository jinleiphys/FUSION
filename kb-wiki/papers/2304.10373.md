# Self-consistent calculations for atomic electron capture

> We present a comprehensive investigation of electron capture (EC) ratios spanning a broad range of atomic numbers. The study employs a self-consistent computational method that incorporates electron screening, electron correlations, overlap and exchange corrections, as well as shake-up and shake-off atomic effects. The electronic wave functions are computed with the Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Slater (DHFS) method, chosen following a systematic comparison of binding energies, atomic relaxation energies and Coulomb amplitudes against other existing methods and experimental data. A novel feature in the calculations is the use of an energy balance employing atomic masses, which avoids approximating the electron total binding energy and allows a more precise determination of the neutrino energy. This leads to a better agreement of our predictions for capture ratios in comparison with the experimental ones, especially for low-energy transitions. We expand the assessment of EC observables uncertainties by incorporating atomic relaxation energy uncertainties, in contrast to previous studies focusing only on Q-value and nuclear level energies. Detailed results are presented for nuclei of practical interest in both nuclear medicine and exotic physics searches involving liquid Xenon detectors ($^{67}\mathrm{Ga}$, $^{111}\mathrm{In}$, $^{123}\mathrm{I}$, $^{125}\mathrm{I}$ and $^{125}\mathrm{Xe}$). Our study can be relevant for astrophysical, nuclear, and medical applications.

## Key claim

The authors present a self-consistent computational method for electron capture ratios that, by using atomic masses in the energy balance rather than approximating the total electron binding energy, achieves better agreement with experimental capture ratios especially for low-energy transitions, while also incorporating atomic relaxation energy uncertainties that previous studies omitted.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation using the Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Slater (DHFS) self-consistent method to obtain bound electron wave functions for both the initial neutral atom and the final excited atom with a hole in the captured-electron shell. They compute electron capture ratios by including overlap and exchange corrections following the generalized Vatai approach, shake-up and shake-off effects, and a refined energy balance that uses atomic masses from the AME2020 database to determine the neutrino energy without approximating the total electron binding energy. The calculations are performed for allowed and first unique forbidden transitions across a broad range of atomic numbers, with detailed results for five isotopes of practical interest.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of electron capture calculations originally developed by Bahcall (1962-1965) and Vatai (1970), and builds upon recent calculations by Mougeot (2018, 2019, 2021) using the BetaShape program. The key advance over Mougeot's approach is the use of exact final-state atomic configurations for overlap and exchange corrections rather than first-order perturbation theory, and the use of atomic masses in the energy balance rather than approximating the total binding energy. The results are relevant for nuclear medicine applications using Auger-electron-emitting isotopes, for background characterization in liquid xenon dark matter experiments (XENON, LUX, PandaX), and for two-neutrino double-electron capture measurements in ¹²⁴Xe.
