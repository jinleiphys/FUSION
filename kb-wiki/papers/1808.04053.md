# Extracting strange quark freeze-out information in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV from $ϕ$ and $Ω$ production

> Using a covariant quark coalescence model combined with a blast-wave-like analytical parametrization for (anti-)strange quark phase-space freeze-out configuration, we extract information on strange quark freeze-out dynamics in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV by fitting the measured transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flows ($v_2$) of $ϕ$ mesons and $Ω$ baryons. We find that although both the measured and calculated $v_2$ of $ϕ$ and $Ω$ satisfy the number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling, the NCQ-scaled $v_2$ is significantly smaller than the $v_2$ of strange quarks, implying that the NCQ-scaled $v_2$ of $ϕ$ and $Ω$ cannot be simply identified as the $v_2$ of strange quarks at hadronization. Meanwhile, our results indicate that the covariant quark coalescence model can nicely describe the spectra and elliptic flows of $ϕ$ and $Ω$ simultaneously, suggesting the coalescence mechanism is still valid for $ϕ$ and $Ω$ production in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The NCQ-scaled elliptic flow of phi mesons and Omega baryons cannot be simply identified as the elliptic flow of strange quarks at hadronization; the strange quark elliptic flow is about 1.35 times larger than the NCQ-scaled hadron values in centrality 10-20% Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, even though both measured and calculated hadron v2 satisfy the NCQ scaling relation.

## Method

The authors used a covariant quark coalescence model with a blast-wave-like analytical parametrization for the (anti-)strange quark phase-space freeze-out configuration. They fitted the measured transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flows of phi mesons and Omega baryons from ALICE data in centrality 10-20% Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, extracting nine freeze-out parameters (T, rho0, R0, tau0, Delta tau, xi, c1, c2, s2) for the strange quarks. The cluster Wigner function was computed using harmonic oscillator wave functions with root-mean-square radii of 0.87 fm for phi and 1.0 fm for Omega.

## Key numbers

- Local freeze-out temperature fixed at T = 154 MeV, following the QCD transition temperature from lattice QCD studies
- Transverse flow parameter rho0 = 1.06, transverse radius R0 = 14.8 fm, longitudinal proper time tau0 = 13.0 fm/c, time dispersion Delta tau = 1.3 fm/c
- Strange quark fugacity xi_s = 0.78, geometric anisotropy s2 = -0.05, anisotropy parameters c1 = 0.38 and c2 = 8.7 fm
- Strange quark mass taken as 500 MeV; root-mean-square radii of phi = 0.87 fm and Omega = 1.0 fm
- The Omega/phi yield ratio increases from about 0.02 at pT = 0.5 GeV/c to about 0.1 at pT = 3 GeV/c, an enhancement factor of about 5
- The elliptic flow of (anti-)strange quarks is about 1.35 times the NCQ-scaled elliptic flows of phi and Omega in centrality 10-20% Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV

## Context

This work extends the covariant coalescence model previously applied to (anti-)light-(hyper)nuclei production (Sun 2015, Sun 2016) to multistrange hadrons at LHC energies. It addresses the question raised by Cho (2017) about whether quark coalescence remains valid at LHC energies, given the observed violation of NCQ scaling between phi mesons and protons in Pb+Pb collisions (ALICE 2015). The results contradict the naive momentum-space coalescence model (Mol03, Che04, Kol04) which predicts NCQ-scaled hadron v2 equals quark v2, and improve upon earlier dynamical coalescence work (Che06) that failed to reproduce the transverse momentum spectra of phi and Omega at RHIC. The extracted freeze-out parameters enable future studies of strangeness dynamics at beam energy scan energies at STAR/RHIC.
