# Enhancement of low-mass dileptons in heavy-ion collisions

> Using a relativistic transport model for the expansion stage of S+Au collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon, we show that the recently observed enhancement of low-mass dileptons by the CERES collaboration can be explained by the decrease of vector meson masses in hot and dense hadronic matter.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of low-mass dileptons in central S+Au collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon by the CERES collaboration can be explained by the decrease of vector meson masses in hot and dense hadronic matter, but only when the medium modification is included via a self-consistent coupling of vector mesons to the scalar field in the relativistic transport model.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic transport model based on the nonlinear σ-ω model to describe the expansion stage of a fire-cylinder formed in S+Au collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon. Initial conditions (baryon number 72, initial baryon density 3.5ρ₀, initial temperature 185 MeV) were determined by fitting observed pion and proton rapidity distributions and transverse mass spectra from the NA44 collaboration. Dileptons were produced from decays of rho and omega mesons, including pion-pion annihilation through explicit rho meson formation, propagation, and decay, with medium effects incorporated by coupling vector mesons to the scalar field using the quark-meson coupling model.

## Key numbers

- Initial baryon density: 3.5ρ₀ (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³)
- Initial temperature: 185 MeV
- Baryon number in initial fire-cylinder: 72
- Initial fire-cylinder cross section: 40 fm²
- Longitudinal length of fire-cylinder: 3.2 fm
- At 3.5ρ₀ and 185 MeV, the rho meson mass reduces to about 380 MeV
- Pion chemical potential: 135 MeV without medium effects; 50 MeV with medium effects
- Initial particle numbers without medium effects: 95 pions, 57 rhos, 39 omegas
- Initial particle numbers with medium effects: 44 pions, 88 rhos, 39 omegas
- Enhancement factor of about 5 in dilepton yield for masses from 250 MeV to about 500 MeV
- For HELIOS-3 forward rapidity (3.7 < η < 5.5), enhancement is only about a factor of two
- Experimental resolution folding used Gaussian smearing with σ ≈ 15 MeV
- Dilepton acceptance: transverse momentum p_t > 0.2 GeV, pseudo-rapidity 2.1 < η < 2.65

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study at SIS/GSI energies (Li 1995) to SPS/CERN energies, showing that the same mechanism of dropping vector meson masses can explain the CERES data. It builds on the Brown-Rho scaling prediction (Brown 1991, Brown 1993) that non-strange vector meson masses should be reduced in dense matter, supported by QCD sum rules (Hatsuda 1992). A similar calculation using the RQMD model (Hoffmann 1994) had shown a shift in the rho meson peak at AGS/BNL energies. This work enables quantitative predictions for future Pb+Pb collisions at SPS/CERN, where larger initial baryon density is expected to produce more prominent medium effects.
