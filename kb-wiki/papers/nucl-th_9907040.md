# Baryon Fluctuations in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> We propose that dramatic changes in the variances and covariance of protons and antiprotons can result if baryons approach chemical equilibrium in nuclear collisions at RHIC. To explore how equilibration alters these fluctuations, we formulate both equilibrium and nonequilibrium hadrochemical descriptions of baryon evolution. Contributions to fluctuations from impact parameter averaging and finite acceptance in nuclear collisions are numerically simulated.

# Baryon Fluctuations in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim
Chemical equilibration of baryons in RHIC collisions can reduce the variance of proton yields to half the level expected from thermal or participant-nucleon models, and produce a negative proton-antiproton covariance, provided that baryon and antibaryon rapidity densities are comparable at midrapidity. These signatures survive competition from volume fluctuations, thermal fluctuations, impact-parameter averaging, and finite-acceptance effects.

## Method
The authors formulate both equilibrium and nonequilibrium hadrochemical descriptions of baryon evolution using a Bjorken hydrodynamic framework with local thermal and chemical equilibrium. They compute variances and covariances of proton and antiproton yields from thermodynamic fluctuations of temperature, volume, and net baryon number, then incorporate these into a Monte Carlo event generator that simulates impact-parameter averaging and finite acceptance for Au+Au collisions at RHIC. A kinetic model with annihilation and production reactions is used to study the approach to chemical equilibrium, and elastic rescattering after chemical freezeout is estimated using a survival probability formalism.

## Key numbers
- In chemical equilibrium with comparable baryon and antibaryon numbers, the variance can fall to **half** the thermal and participant-nucleon model level.
- For central Au+Au at RHIC, the correction to the variance from volume fluctuations is of order **4%**, since N/N_tot ~ 0.04.
- At chemical freezeout temperature T = 160 MeV and m = 938 MeV, the sum of volume and thermal terms contributes roughly **1 + epsilon^2/12 ~ 5.5** to the relative variance.
- For N_tot ~ 10^3 in Au+Au, the contribution from volume and thermal terms to either V_ch/N^2 or C_ch/(N*Nbar) is ~ **0.5%**.
- For Nbar ~ N ~ 40, baryon density fluctuations contribute ~ **+1.3%** to the variance and **-1.3%** to the covariance, yielding totals V_ch/N^2 ~ **1.8%** and C_ch/(N*Nbar) ~ **-0.8%**.
- Chemical nonequilibrium results give values ~ **2.5%** and **+0.1%** for the relative variance and covariance.
- The annihilation cross section used is ~ **4.4 fm^2**.
- The survival probability for elastic scattering after chemical freezeout is estimated as **0.8 < P < 0.9** for central collisions, assuming n_tot(tau_F) ~ 0.1 fm^{-3}.
- Rapidity densities at zero impact parameter are taken as n ~ nbar ~ **40** for both baryons and antibaryons.
- The average charged particle multiplicity at zero impact parameter is N_tot(0) = **2100**.
- Hadron formation time is taken as tau_0 = **8 fm**; chemical freezeout time is varied up to tau_F ~ **14 fm** for central Au+Au.

## Context
This work extends the thermodynamic fluctuation framework of Gavin and Pruneau (1999) and the kinetic model of Gavin, Gyulassy, Plumer, and Venugopalan (1990) to study baryon fluctuations as a signal of chemical equilibration. It contrasts with participant-nucleon models such as HIJING and with thermal equilibrium expectations, which yield Poissonian fluctuations and positive or vanishing covariance. The results enable experimental searches for chemical equilibrium signatures using event-by-event proton and antiproton measurements at RHIC, particularly in the STAR acceptance, and suggest that such measurements can distinguish equilibrium from nonequilibrium scenarios even if equilibration is incomplete or if rescattering occurs after chemical freezeout.
