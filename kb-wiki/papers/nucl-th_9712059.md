# Testing the Core-Halo Model on Bose-Einstein Correlation Functions

> Having first performed a Monte Carlo simulation to justify the analysis technique to be used, we then analyze the Bose-Einstein correlation functions from CERN experiment NA44 in the context of the core-halo model. Although experimental resolution and error bar distribution prevents a direct observation of the halo structure, the values for the core radius and the core fraction of pions can be obtained in a straight-forward manner. These are found to be independent of the structure of the correlation function at small relative momenta of Q < 50 MeV. Hence, the omega meson decay products do not distort the Gaussian shape of the Bose-Einstein correlation function in S + Pb -> pi + pi + X reaction at CERN SPS. As we find that the "model-independent" HBT radii yield results that are quantitatively as well as qualitatively unreliable for systems with long-lived resonances, we present their corrected form that applies for correlation functions with lambda(K) < 1.

## Key claim

The core-halo model can be applied to the NA44 S+Pb data, and the extracted core radius and core fraction of pions are independent of the structure of the correlation function at small relative momenta Q < 50 MeV, meaning the omega meson decay products do not distort the Gaussian shape of the Bose-Einstein correlation function in this specific reaction at CERN SPS, but the authors emphasize that this conclusion must be re-investigated for other reactions or experimental resolutions.

## Method

The authors first performed a Monte Carlo simulation using an acceptance-rejection method to generate correlation functions with known input parameters, fitting them with a standard Gaussian form to establish the range of Q_min (10 to 50 MeV/c) over which reliable core parameters could be extracted. They then applied this "data chopping" analysis to published one-dimensional slices of the three-dimensional NA44 S+Pb correlation functions for high-p_t pions, low-p_t pions, and kaons, gradually removing data points at the lowest relative momentum Q and fitting the remaining data with a constant to extract core radius R_c and core fraction f_c.

## Key numbers

* Core radius for high p_t pions: R_c_out = 2.92 ± 0.13 fm, R_c_side = 2.90 ± 0.18 fm, R_c_long = 3.31 ± 0.16 fm
* Core radius for low p_t pions: R_c_out = 4.29 ± 0.13 fm, R_c_side = 4.24 ± 0.26 fm, R_c_long = 5.43 ± 0.30 fm
* Core radius for kaons: R_c_out = 2.54 ± 0.18 fm, R_c_side = 2.22 ± 0.19 fm, R_c_long = 2.67 ± 0.22 fm
* Core fraction for high p_t pions: f_c_out = 0.704 ± 0.012, f_c_side = 0.735 ± 0.021, f_c_long = 0.738 ± 0.014
* Core fraction for low p_t pions: f_c_out = 0.725 ± 0.011, f_c_side = 0.647 ± 0.021, f_c_long = 0.724 ± 0.015
* Core fraction for kaons: f_c_out = 0.802 ± 0.027, f_c_side = 0.736 ± 0.033, f_c_long = 0.789 ± 0.029
* The omega meson has a full width of 8.4 MeV and a lifetime of 23.4 fm/c; the eta and eta-prime have lifetimes of 164417 fm/c and 986.5 fm/c respectively; the K_S^0 has a lifetime of 8.9 × 10^{-9} s (2.7 cm decay length)

## Context

This work extends the core-halo model formalism developed in Csorgo (1995) and applies it to published NA44 data to resolve a scientific debate about whether omega meson decay products contribute to the core or the halo of the source. The authors explicitly contradict earlier conclusions from the SPACER simulation (1995) and the HYLANDER simulation (1996) which claimed omega decays are clearly visible in the correlation function, and instead support the view of Csorgo (1995) and Simon (1996) that at current experimental resolution (Q_min ≈ 5-10 MeV) the omega decay products cannot be resolved and belong to the halo. The analysis enables future three-dimensional studies of correlation functions at fixed mean momentum and provides corrected "model-independent" HBT radii for systems with lambda(K) < 1.
