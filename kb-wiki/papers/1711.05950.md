# Comparison of heavy-ion transport simulations: Collision integral in a box

> Simulations by transport codes are indispensable to extract valuable physics information from heavy ion collisions. In order to understand the origins of discrepancies between different widely used transport codes, we compare 15 such codes under controlled conditions of a system confined to a box with periodic boundary, initialized with Fermi-Dirac distributions at saturation density and temperatures of either 0 or 5 MeV. In such calculations, one is able to check separately the different ingredients of a transport code. In this second publication of the code evaluation project, we only consider the two-body collision term, i.e. we perform cascade calculations. When the Pauli blocking is artificially suppressed, the collision rates are found to be consistent for most codes (to within $1\%$ or better) with analytical results, or completely controlled results of a basic cascade code after eliminating the correlations within the same pair of colliding particles. In calculations with active Pauli blocking, the blocking probability was found to deviate from the expected reference values. The reason is found in substantial phase-space fluctuations and smearing tied to numerical algorithms and model assumptions in the representation of phase space. This results in the reduction of the blocking probability in most transport codes, so that the simulated system gradually evolves away from the Fermi-Dirac towards a Boltzmann distribution. As a result of this investigation, we are able to make judgements about the most effective strategies in transport simulations for determining the collision probabilities and the Pauli blocking. Investigation in a similar vein of other ingredients in transport calculations, like the mean field propagation or the production of nucleon resonances and mesons, will be discussed in the future publications.

## Key claim

Under controlled box conditions with periodic boundaries and Fermi-Dirac initializations at saturation density, the two-body collision rates in 15 transport codes agree with analytical results to within 1% or better when Pauli blocking is artificially suppressed, but with active Pauli blocking the blocking probability deviates from expected reference values due to phase-space fluctuations and smearing from numerical algorithms, causing most codes to gradually evolve from a Fermi-Dirac toward a Boltzmann distribution.

## Method

The authors performed cascade calculations (two-body collision term only, no mean field) for 15 transport codes (7 Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck type and 8 quantum molecular dynamics type) in a cubic box of side 20 fm with periodic boundary conditions, initialized with uniform density 0.16 fm^-3 and Fermi-Dirac momentum distributions at temperatures of 0 MeV and 5 MeV. They compared collision rates and Pauli blocking probabilities against analytical limits and a basic reference cascade code, using an isotropic constant elastic cross section of 40 mb with no inelastic processes.

## Key numbers

- Box dimensions: 20 fm per side, containing A = 1280 nucleons (640 neutrons, 640 protons) at density 0.16 fm^-3
- Without Pauli blocking, collision rates for most codes agreed with analytical results to within 1% or better
- Fermi momentum: p_F = 263.04 MeV/c
- Chemical potential at T = 0 MeV: 36.882 MeV (non-relativistic), 36.184 MeV (relativistic)
- Average energy per particle at T = 0 MeV: 22.129 MeV (non-relativistic), 21.827 MeV (relativistic)
- Chemical potential at T = 5 MeV: 36.306 MeV (non-relativistic), 35.544 MeV (relativistic)
- Average energy per particle at T = 5 MeV: 23.740 MeV (non-relativistic), 23.510 MeV (relativistic)
- Boltzmann equilibration temperature T_B at T = 0 MeV: 14.753 MeV (non-relativistic), 14.284 MeV (relativistic)
- Boltzmann equilibration temperature T_B at T = 5 MeV: 15.833 MeV (non-relativistic), 15.364 MeV (relativistic)
- Recommended time step: 0.5 or 1.0 fm/c
- BUU codes used 100 test particles per nucleon; QMD codes used 200 runs

## Context

This work extends the code comparison project initiated at workshops at ECT* in Trento and Shanghai Jiao Tong University, following the earlier comparison of 19 codes in Au+Au collisions at 100 and 400 AMeV by Xu (2016), which found model uncertainties in transverse flow predictions of about 30% at 100 AMeV and 13% at 400 AMeV. By isolating the two-body collision term in box calculations, the authors identify that phase-space fluctuations in QMD-type codes cause faster degradation of Fermi-Dirac statistics compared to BUU-type codes, enabling future systematic studies of mean field propagation and resonance/meson production in subsequent publications.
