# Density and expansion effects on pion spectra in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We compute the pion inclusive momentum distribution in heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies, assuming thermal equilibrium and accounting for density and expansion effects at the time of decoupling. We compare to data on mid rapidity charged pions produced in central Au + Au collisions and find a very good agreement. The shape of the distribution at low $m_t-m$ is explained in part as an effect arising from the high mean pion density achieved in these reactions. The difference between the positive and negative pion distributions in the same region is attributed in part to the different average yields of each kind of charged pions.

# Density and expansion effects on pion spectra in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The concave shape of the pion transverse mass distribution at low $m_t-m$ can be explained as a density effect arising from the high mean pion density achieved in central Au+Au collisions, while the difference between positive and negative pion distributions in the same region is attributed in part to their different average yields. However, the authors note that a more conclusive statement requires proper inclusion of Coulomb corrections, which are deferred to future work.

## Method
The authors model the pion system as a confined, thermally equilibrated fluid within a spherical fireball of radius R, solving the Klein-Gordon equation with a rigid boundary condition (wave functions vanish at the surface). They incorporate collective expansion by substituting the momentum operator $p^\mu$ with $p^\mu - m u^\mu$, where $u^\mu$ is the four-velocity field of the fluid, using a parametrization where the three-velocity scales linearly with distance from the fireball center. The momentum distribution is obtained by summing over discrete energy states weighted by Bose-Einstein factors, with the chemical potential determined from the total pion multiplicity.

## Key numbers
- Pion strong interaction range $d_s \sim 1.4$ fm, used as threshold for liquid-like behavior
- Average pion separation $d \sim 0.6$ fm, computed from total pion yield $dN_\pi/dy \sim 300$ at mid rapidity, transverse area $A \sim 64$ fm$^2$, and formation time $t_0 \sim 1$ fm
- Temperature fixed at $T = 120$ MeV for comparison to data
- Surface expansion velocity $\beta = 0.5$, corresponding to average collective expansion velocity $\langle v \rangle \simeq 0.4c$
- Fireball radius $R = 8$ fm
- Negative pion multiplicity $N_{\pi^-} = 160$; positive pion multiplicity $N_{\pi^+} = 115$
- Normalization constant $\mathcal{N} = 0.56$ for negative pions and $\mathcal{N} = 0.59$ for positive pions, minimizing $\chi^2$ above $m_t - m = 0.4$ GeV
- Data compared are from central Au+Au collisions at $11.6~A$ GeV/c

## Context
This work extends the boundary-effect approach of Wong and Mostafa (1995) and Ayala et al. (1997), which successfully reproduced the concave shape at high transverse momentum but failed to describe the overall fall-off because they omitted collective expansion. The authors incorporate hydrodynamical flow following the fireball model of Siemens and Rasmussen (1979), using a spherical geometry motivated by the significant stopping reported by the E-802/866 collaboration at AGS energies. The calculation enables a unified description of both low- and high-transverse-momentum regions of pion spectra, with the explicit prediction that Coulomb corrections will be needed to fully describe the data below $m_t-m = 0.4$ GeV.
