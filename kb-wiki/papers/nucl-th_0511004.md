# The structure of the nucleon

> Experimental data on electromagnetic and weak form factors of the nucleon are analyzed in a two-component model with a quark-like intrinsic structure surrounded by a meson cloud. The contribution from strange quarks is discussed and compared with recent data from the G0 Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a two-component model combining an intrinsic three-quark structure with a meson cloud (via vector-meson dominance) can simultaneously describe electromagnetic form factors of the proton and neutron and the strange quark form factors, with the strange magnetic moment emerging as positive (0.315 μ_N) without introducing new free parameters beyond those fixed by the electromagnetic data.

## Method

The author parametrizes the Dirac and Pauli form factors of the nucleon as a sum of an intrinsic three-quark contribution (with a dipole form factor g(Q²) = (1+γQ²)⁻²) and a meson-cloud contribution via vector-meson dominance (ρ, ω, φ mesons). The five free parameters (γ from the intrinsic form factor, β_φ and α_φ from isoscalar couplings, β_ρ and α_ρ from isovector couplings) are determined by a least-square fit to the same electromagnetic form factor data set used in Bijker and Iachello (2004). The strange form factors then follow from the same couplings via mixing-angle relations without additional parameters.

## Key numbers

- The strange magnetic moment is calculated as μ_s = G_M^s(0) = 0.315 μ_N, a positive value.
- The SAMPLE Collaboration determined the strange magnetic form factor at Q² = 0.1 (GeV/c)² to be G_M^s = 0.37 ± 0.20 ± 0.26 ± 0.07.
- An analysis of world data gives G_M^s(0.1) = 0.55 ± 0.28.
- The size of the intrinsic structure is found to be approximately 0.49 fm.
- The ω-φ mixing angle ε is taken as 0.053 rad from the decay properties of the ω and φ mesons.

## Context

This work extends the vector-meson dominance model of Iachello, Jackson, and Lande (1973) by adding an intrinsic contribution to the isovector Pauli form factor, as suggested by relativistic constituent quark models in the light-front approach (Frank and Salme, 1995). The model reproduces the linear drop in the proton form factor ratio predicted by Iachello, Jackson, and Lande (1973) and later by chiral soliton models, and improves agreement with neutron form factor data from Madey (2003). The positive strange magnetic moment contradicts most theoretical predictions but agrees with recent parity-violating electron scattering results from the SAMPLE, HAPPEX, G0, and PVA4 collaborations, enabling future experiments to disentangle quark flavor contributions to nucleon structure.
