# The spatial distributions of chiral magnetic field in the RHIC and LHC energy regions

> Relativistic heavy-ion collisions can produce extremely strong magnetic field in the collision regions. The spatial variation features of the magnetic fields are analyzed in detail for non-central Pb - Pb collisions at LHC $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 900, 2760 and 7000 GeV and Au-Au collisions at RHIC $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 62.4, 130 and 200 GeV. The dependencies of magnetic field on proper time, collision energies and impact parameters are investigated in this paper. It is shown that a enormous with highly inhomogeneous spatial distribution magnetic field can indeed be created in off-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions in RHIC and LHC energy regions. The enormous magnetic field is quite large, especially just after the collision, and then decreases rapidly with time. We are surprised to find that the magnetic effect decreases with increasing energy from RHIC to LHC energy region. It is found that the magnitude of magnetic field in the LHC energy region is far less than that of magnetic field in the RHIC energy region.

## Key claim

The paper claims that at very small proper times (τ < 8 × 10⁻³ fm), the magnetic field magnitude in the LHC energy region is larger than that in the RHIC energy region, but that the magnetic field in the LHC region decreases more quickly with proper time, so that beyond τ ≈ 8 × 10⁻³ fm the RHIC field becomes larger, a reversal of the naive expectation that higher collision energies always produce stronger magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors model the magnetic field produced in non-central heavy-ion collisions using a Wood-Saxon nucleon distribution (rather than a uniform distribution) to compute the contributions from both spectator and participant nucleons separately. They calculate the spatial distributions of the eB_x and eB_y components of the chiral magnetic field as functions of proper time τ, impact parameter b, and center-of-mass energy √s_NN for Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies (62.4, 130, 200 GeV) and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies (900, 2760, 7000 GeV). The magnetic field is evaluated at the central point (x,y) = (0,0) and at off-center positions (5,5) and (10,10) fm.

## Key numbers

- Maximum eB_y in the RHIC energy region reaches 2.2 × 10⁵ MeV² at τ = 0.0001 fm and b = 8 fm.
- Maximum eB_y in the LHC energy region reaches 2.0 × 10⁶ MeV² at τ = 0.0001 fm and b = 8 fm.
- The crossover proper time where the RHIC magnetic field becomes larger than the LHC field is τ = 8.0 × 10⁻³ fm.
- At τ = 3 fm and √s_NN > 200 GeV, the magnetic field at the central point approaches zero.
- The maximum magnetic field in the calculation is eB ≈ 2 × 10⁷ MeV² at τ = 0.0001 fm, b ≈ 8 fm, and √s_NN ≈ 7000 GeV.
- The ratio eB_y/(eB) along the x = 0 line ranges between 0.5 and 1.0, while along y it stays between 0.9 and 1.0.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Zhong, Yang, Cai, Feng, 2014) that used a Wood-Saxon nucleon distribution instead of a uniform distribution to improve the magnetic field calculation at the central point. It systematically maps the spatial distribution of the chiral magnetic field across RHIC and LHC energies, revealing that the field is highly inhomogeneous and that its energy dependence reverses with proper time. The results provide input for interpreting the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) measurements at RHIC (experiments by the STAR collaboration) and LHC (ALICE collaboration), and for understanding how the magnetic field environment differs between the two facilities.
