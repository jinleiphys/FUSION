# Electron Propagation in the Field of Colliding Nuclei at Ultrarelativistic Energies

> We calculate the asymptotic high-energy amplitude for electrons scattering at one ion as well as at two colliding ions, respectively, by means of perturbation theory. We show that the interaction with one ion eikonalizes and that the interaction with two ions causally decouples. We are able to put previous results on perturbative grounds and propose further applications for the obtained rules for interactions on the light cone. The formalism will be of use for the calculation of Coulomb corrections to electron-positron pair creation in heavy ion collisions. Finally we discuss the results and inherent dangers of the employed approximations.

## Key claim

The authors show that the asymptotic high-energy amplitude for electron scattering off one ultrarelativistic ion eikonalizes and that scattering off two colliding ions causally decouples, but they caution that the limit \(\gamma \to \infty\) is mathematically pathological and that the approximations must be treated with care, as the required transformation is not an element of the Lorentz group and the potential becomes an unbounded operator.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for an electron in the field of a fast-moving Coulomb potential by applying an operator identity to eliminate the scalar part of the vector potential, achieving a temporal gauge with a purely transverse vector potential. They then compute the scattering amplitude via perturbation theory, summing ladder diagrams in the high-energy limit where longitudinal photon components are neglected, and extend the formalism to two colliding ions using the causal decoupling property implied by the projection operators \((1\pm\hat{\alpha}_z)\).

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal components of the photon momentum are neglected, with corrections suppressed by \(1/\gamma^2\).
- The transverse vector potential in the limit \(\gamma \to \infty\) exhibits a Heaviside step function dependence \(\sim \theta(t-z)\vec{x}_\perp/x_\perp^2\).
- The time integral of the transverse electric field is \(\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\vec{E}_\perp = -2Z\alpha\vec{x}_\perp/(\beta x_\perp^2)\).
- The asymptotic potential limit requires \(\gamma \gg x_\perp/|z-\beta t|\), which in momentum space translates to \(k_\perp \gg \omega/\gamma\).
- The Fourier transform of the potential in the high-energy limit involves the Bessel function \(K_0(\omega x_\perp/\gamma)\), which for \(\gamma\to\infty\) becomes \(-2e^{i\omega t}\ln(\omega x_\perp e^C/(2\gamma))\) where \(C\) is Euler's constant.

## Context

This work extends the eikonal approximation methods of Abarbanel and Itzykson, Chang and Ma (1970), and Chang and Fishbane to the case of ultrarelativistic moving sources, putting previous results by Segev and Wells on perturbative grounds. The formalism enables calculation of Coulomb corrections to electron-positron pair production in heavy-ion collisions, where the scattering amplitude maintains the structure of a two-photon process with a modified potential, allowing cross sections to be obtained from the Klein-Nishina formula for Compton scattering.
