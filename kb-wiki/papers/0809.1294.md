# On the TFD treatment of collective vibrations in hot nuclei

> The approach in a theory of collective excitations in hot nuclei exploring the formalism of thermo field dynamics and the model Hamiltonian consisting of a mean field, the BCS paring interaction and long-range particle-hole effective forces is reexamined. In contrast with earlier studies it is found that a wave function of a thermal phonon is depended not only on the Fermi-Dirac thermal occupation numbers of Bogoliubov quasiparticles consisting the phonon but on the Bose thermal occupation numbers of the phonon as well. This strongly affects a thermal phonon couplings due to renormalizing of a phonon-phonon interaction and enlarging the number of thermal two-phonon configurations coupled with one-phonon ones. Moreover, it is shown that the formulation of the double tilde conjugation rule for fermions proposed by I.Ojima is more appropriate in the context of the present study than the original one by H.Umezawa and coworkers.

## Key claim

The authors reexamine the thermo field dynamics (TFD) treatment of collective vibrations in hot nuclei and find that, contrary to earlier studies (Kos1994, Kos1995, Storo2004), the wave function of a thermal phonon depends not only on the Fermi-Dirac thermal occupation numbers of Bogoliubov quasiparticles but also on the Bose thermal occupation numbers of the phonon itself, which renormalizes the phonon-phonon interaction and enlarges the number of thermal two-phonon configurations coupled to one-phonon states.

## Method

The authors apply the TFD formalism to the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) Hamiltonian, which includes a mean field, BCS pairing interaction, and separable multipole-multipole particle-hole forces. They perform a thermal Bogoliubov transformation to define thermal quasiparticles, then derive the thermal random phase approximation (TRPA) equations for collective phonon excitations, explicitly including the Bose-Einstein occupation factors in the phonon amplitudes. The authors also compare two formulations of the double tilde conjugation rule (DTCR): the original one by Umezawa and coworkers and the alternative by Ojima, adopting the latter as more appropriate.

## Key numbers

- The thermal occupation factors for quasiparticles are given by \( y_j = [1 + \exp(\varepsilon_j/T)]^{-1/2} \), the Fermi-Dirac distribution, with \( x_j = (1 - y_j^2)^{1/2} \).
- The Bose-Einstein distribution function appears as \( Y(\omega) = [\exp(\omega/T) - 1]^{-1/2} \) and \( X(\omega) = [1 + Y^2(\omega)]^{1/2} \) in the transition probabilities between the BCS thermal vacuum and thermal two-quasiparticle states.
- The squared reduced matrix elements for Eλ transitions satisfy the relation \( \Phi^2_{\lambda}(\omega) = \exp(\omega/T) \, \Phi^2_{\lambda}(-\omega) \), linking transition probabilities at positive and negative energies.

## Context

This work extends the TFD-based QPM approach developed by Kos1994, Kos1995, and Storo2004 for treating collective excitations in hot nuclei, correcting an omission of Bose thermal occupation factors in the phonon wave functions. The corrected formalism enables a more complete microscopic treatment of the spreading width of giant resonances at finite temperature and the coupling of different excitation modes in hot nuclei, building on earlier studies using the Matsubara Green function technique (Bort1986) and Nuclear Field Theory (NFT1977). The adoption of Ojima's double tilde conjugation rule (Ojima1981) resolves inconsistencies found in the earlier TFD-QPM treatment.
