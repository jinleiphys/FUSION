# Universality in the triton charge form factor

> We consider the three-nucleon system within an effective theory with contact interactions at leading order in the large scattering length. We calculate the charge form factor of the triton at low momentum transfer and extract the triton charge radius. At this order, no two-body currents contribute and the calculation can be performed in the impulse approximation. We also comment on the power counting for higher orders. The requirement of a three-body force for renormalization of the three-nucleon system explains the previously observed correlation between the triton binding energy and charge radius for different model potentials.

## Key claim

The triton charge radius and binding energy are correlated at leading order in the pionless effective field theory, and this correlation is parametrized by the same three-body parameter \(L_3\) that governs the Phillips and Tjon lines; the calculation is performed in the impulse approximation with no two-body currents, and the result is consistent with the experimental charge radius within the estimated 30% leading-order uncertainty.

## Method

The authors construct a leading-order effective potential with contact interactions in the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) channels, regularized with a Gaussian cutoff, and solve the Faddeev equations for the triton wave function. The charge form factor is computed in the impulse approximation by applying the electric charge operator to the wave function, and the charge radius is extracted from the slope of the form factor at low momentum transfer. The three-body parameter \(L_3\) is fixed by reproducing the experimental triton binding energy \(B_t = 8.48\) MeV.

## Key numbers

* Triton charge radius extracted using \(a_t\) and \(a_s\) as two-body input: \(r_C = 2.05\) fm.
* Triton charge radius extracted using \(B_d\) and \(a_s\) as two-body input: \(r_C = 2.18\) fm.
* Leading-order averaged result: \(r_C^{LO} = (2.1 \pm 0.6)\) fm, compared to the experimental value \(r_C^{exp} = (1.755 \pm 0.086)\) fm (Amroun 1994).
* Three-body parameter values: \(L_3 = 21.2\) fm\(^{-1}\) (using \(a_t\), \(a_s\)) and \(L_3 = 19.8\) fm\(^{-1}\) (using \(B_d\), \(a_s\)).
* The correlation curve from the effective theory yields a prefactor of 7.6 (solid) and 9.5 (dashed) with exponents \(-0.61\) and \(-0.69\), respectively, when fitted to \(r_C \propto B_t^{\text{exponent}}\).

## Context

This work extends the universal correlations previously observed in the three-nucleon system, the Phillips line (Phillips 1968) for the triton binding energy and neutron-deuteron scattering length, and the Tjon line (Tjon 1975) for the triton and alpha-particle binding energies, to the electromagnetic sector. Friar, Gibson, Chen, and Payne (1985) had noted a correlation between the triton charge radius and binding energy using potential models; the present calculation explains that correlation as a consequence of the three-body parameter \(L_3\) required for renormalization in the pionless effective theory. The results enable future higher-order calculations including effective range corrections and two-body currents, and they provide a framework for studying universality in other few-nucleon electromagnetic observables.
