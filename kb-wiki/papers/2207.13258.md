# Exploring Light-Cone Distribution Amplitudes from Quantum Computing

> Light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs) are essential nonperturbative quantities for theoretical predictions of exclusive high-energy processes in quantum chromodynamics (QCD). We demonstrate the prospect of calculating LCDAs on a quantum computer by applying a recently proposed quantum algorithm, with staggered fermions, to the simulation of the LCDA in the (1+1)-dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model on classical hardware. The agreement between the result from the classical simulation of the quantum algorithm and that from exact diagonalization justifies the proposed quantum algorithm. We find that the resulting LCDA in the NJL model exhibits features shared with the LCDAs obtained from QCD.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a quantum algorithm can compute light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs) in a simplified (1+1)-dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, with results matching exact diagonalization; however, the work is limited to a classical simulation of the quantum algorithm on a desktop workstation, not on actual quantum hardware, and uses a non-gauge model as a proxy for QCD.

## Method
The authors discretize the (1+1)D NJL model on a lattice with N=14 qubits using staggered fermions and a Jordan-Wigner transformation. They prepare the vacuum and hadronic states using a quantum-number-resolving variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) with a quantum alternating operator ansatz (QAOA), and evaluate the real-time light-cone correlator via a Hadamard test on an ancillary qubit. The entire quantum circuit is simulated classically using the QuSpin and ProjectQ packages.

## Key numbers
- Lattice size: N=14 qubits (7 spatial sites after staggering).
- Hadron mass values studied: m_h = 1.3 a^{-1}, 1.5 a^{-1}, and 1.7 a^{-1}, where a is the lattice spacing.
- Bare coupling constant values: g = 0.1 and g = 0.5 (with m_h fixed at 1.5 a^{-1} for the coupling dependence study).
- The condition for lattice parameters: 2π/(Na) < m_h < π/a ensures the lattice size exceeds the hadron size and the spacing is smaller than the hadron size.
- The asymptotic QCD LCDA used for comparison is φ_asymp(x) = 6x(1-x) (Lepage 1980).

## Context
This work extends the quantum algorithm previously proposed by Li et al. (2021) for parton distribution functions to the calculation of LCDAs, using the same (1+1)D NJL model as a testbed. The results show that the LCDA converges to the asymptotic QCD form φ(x)=6x(1-x) as the coupling g approaches zero, and that the peak narrows with increasing hadron mass, consistent with lattice QCD results from Zhang et al. (2020). The study enables future application of quantum computing to real-time light-cone correlators in QCD, though the inclusion of gauge fields and Wilson lines remains a challenge for near-term quantum devices.
