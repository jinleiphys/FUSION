# Hadron structure on the back of an envelope

> In order to remove a little of the mysticism surrounding the issue of strangeness in the nucleon, we present simple, physically transparent estimates of both the strange magnetic moment and charge radius of the proton. Although simple, the estimates are in quite good agreement with sophisticated calculations using the latest input from lattice QCD. We further explore the possible size of systematic uncertainties associated with charge symmetry violation (CSV) in the recent precise determination of the strange magnetic moment of the proton. We find that CSV acts to increase the error estimate by 0.003 μ_N such that G_M^s = -0.046 +/- 0.022 μ_N.

## Key claim

The paper presents simple, physically transparent estimates of the strange magnetic moment and charge radius of the proton, arguing that these estimates agree with sophisticated lattice QCD calculations. The authors further find that including charge symmetry violation (CSV) increases the error estimate on the strange magnetic moment by 0.003 μ_N, yielding a final value of G_M^s = -0.046 ± 0.022 μ_N.

## Method

The authors estimate the strangeness form factors by assuming the dominant strange-quark contribution comes from a virtual N → K Λ transition, with a probability of about 5% derived by comparison to the known 20% N π component. For the strangeness radius, they combine the mean-square radius of the Λ bag (0.36–0.5 fm²) with the kaon cloud radius (1.0–1.4 fm²), weighted by the strange quark charges and the K Λ probability. For the magnetic moment, they use Clebsch-Gordon coefficients for the spin and orbital angular momentum of the Λ and kaon, combined with the Λ magnetic moment and the orbital contribution of the charged antistrange quark. To assess CSV, they introduce Δ_B parameters representing 1% uncertainties in baryon magnetic moments and propagate these through the exact relations linking octet baryon moments to the strange magnetic moment.

## Key numbers

- The estimated K Λ probability in the proton is about 5%, based on the known N π probability of about 20% and the roughly double energy cost of the K Λ configuration.
- The estimated strange magnetic moment from the simple model is G_M^s = -0.063 μ_N.
- The lattice QCD-based determination including CSV is G_M^s = -0.046 ± 0.022 μ_N, where the CSV contribution adds 0.003 μ_N to the error.
- The estimated strangeness electric form factor at Q² = 0.1 GeV² is G_E^s ∈ (+0.01, +0.02).
- The ratio of valence u-quark contributions is u^p/u^Σ = 1.092 ± 0.030 and u^n/u^Ξ = 1.254 ± 0.124.
- The ratio of s- to d-quark sea-quark loop contributions is ^ℓR_d^s = 0.139 ± 0.042.

## Context

This work extends the chiral extrapolation program for lattice QCD data, particularly the determination of the strange magnetic moment by Leinweber et al. (2004), by providing simple physical estimates using the cloudy bag model (CBM) of Thomas (1982) and Miller (1984). The paper also quantifies the systematic uncertainty from charge symmetry violation in that determination, finding it to be small. The results enable comparison with experimental measurements from parity-violating electron scattering (e.g., the HAPPEX and G0 collaborations) and provide a benchmark for future lattice QCD calculations of strangeness form factors.
