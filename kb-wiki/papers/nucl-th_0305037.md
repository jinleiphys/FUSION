# Nuclear Supersymmetry: New Tests and Extensions

> Extensions of nuclear supersymmetry are discussed, together with a proposal for new, more stringent and precise tests that probe the susy classification and specific two-particle correlations among supersymmetric partners. The combination of these theoretical and experimental studies may play a unifying role in nuclear phenomena.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nuclear supersymmetry (n-susy) may be a more general phenomenon than previously realized, and that it does not require the existence of a particular dynamical symmetry; however, the authors acknowledge that the general statement of supersymmetry as a dynamical superalgebra does not provide a general recipe for constructing the Hamiltonian and operators, unlike the more restrictive dynamical-symmetry approach.

## Method

The authors propose new experimental tests of nuclear supersymmetry using one-nucleon and two-nucleon transfer reactions within the Pt-Au supermultiplet quartet (194Pt, 195Pt, 195Au, 196Au), specifically the 194Pt(α,d)196Au reaction to probe neutron-proton correlations. They also develop a generalized theoretical framework for nuclear supersymmetry that does not require a dynamical symmetry, applying it to Ru-Rh isotopes and to W and Hf nuclei using combinations of U(5), SO(6), and SU(3) symmetries within the U(6/12) and U(6/4) superalgebras.

## Key numbers

* The best example of n-susy in nature is the quartet of nuclei 194Pt, 195Au, 195Pt, and 196Au, classified by the Uν(6/12) × Uπ(6/4) dynamical supersymmetry.
* For 182W, the calculated B(E2) value for the 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺ transition is 0.8422 e²b², compared to the experimental value of 0.839(18) e²b².
* For 182W, the calculated quadrupole moment of the 2₁⁺ state is -1.86 eb, compared to the experimental value of -2.00_{-0.08}^{+0.04} eb.
* For 183W, the calculated B(E2) value for the 3/2₁⁻ → 1/2₁⁻ transition is 0.603 e²b², compared to the experimental value of 0.938(62) e²b².
* For 183W, the calculated B(E2) value for the 5/2₁⁻ → 1/2₁⁻ transition is 0.603 e²b², compared to the experimental value of 0.68(4) e²b².

## Context

This work extends the original nuclear supersymmetry framework proposed by Iachello (1980) and the subsequent extension to nuclear quartets, which had been tested primarily through one-nucleon transfer reactions from outside the supermultiplet (e.g., 197Au(d,t)196Au). The paper proposes new two-nucleon transfer reactions (α,d) that directly test supersymmetric wave functions and two-particle correlations, building on recent experimental facilities and detection techniques. It also generalizes the concept by showing that supersymmetry can be tested in other nuclear regions (Ru-Rh, W-Hf) without requiring a dynamical symmetry, which had previously limited the study of nuclear supersymmetry to very few cases.
