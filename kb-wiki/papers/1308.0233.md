# Metropolis Monte Carlo on the Lefschetz thimble: application to a one-plaquette model

> We propose a new algorithm based on the Metropolis sampling method to perform Monte Carlo integration for path integrals in the recently proposed formulation of quantum field theories on the Lefschetz thimble. The algorithm is based on a mapping between the curved manifold defined by the Lefschetz thimble of the full action and the flat manifold associated with the corresponding quadratic action. We discuss an explicit method to calculate the residual phase due to the curvature of the Lefschetz thimble. Finally, we apply this new algorithm to a simple one-plaquette model where our results are in perfect agreement with the analytic integration. We also show that for this system the residual phase does not represent a sign problem.

# Metropolis Monte Carlo on the Lefschetz thimble: application to a one-plaquette model

## Key claim
The proposed Metropolis-based algorithm, which maps the curved Lefschetz thimble to a flat Gaussian thimble via integration of steepest-descent equations, produces results in perfect agreement with analytic integration for the U(1) one-plaquette model and shows no sign problem from the residual phase for this system; however, the authors caution that this absence of a sign problem cannot be extrapolated to systems with many degrees of freedom.

## Method
The algorithm maps the curved Lefschetz thimble of the full action to the flat manifold of the corresponding quadratic (Gaussian) action by solving the generalized eigenvalue problem for the Hessian matrix at each critical point. Field configurations on the Lefschetz thimble are obtained by integrating the steepest-descent equations from a small radius epsilon to r=1, using the Gaussian thimble parametrization as boundary condition. The Jacobian of this mapping is computed by evolving a differential equation along the steepest-descent flow, and the residual phase from the curved thimble is calculated from the determinant of this Jacobian. Metropolis sampling is performed by proposing independent Gaussian-distributed eta variables on the flat thimble, then accepting or rejecting based on the ratio of the full action to the Gaussian action.

## Key numbers
- For the U(1) one-plaquette model, the exact plaquette average is given by ⟨e^{iφ}⟩ = i J₁(β)/J₀(β), where Jₙ are Bessel functions of the first kind.
- With N_τ = 200 (where N_τ = 1/ε), results from the method are identical within statistical errors to the analytical results for the range of β considered.
- The action S = -iβ cos φ has two critical points at φ = 0 and φ = π, both non-degenerate, with intersection numbers m_σ = 1 for both thimbles.
- The residual phase keeps the same sign throughout the full domain of integration at β = 1, indicating no sign problem for this model.

## Context
This work extends the Lefschetz thimble formulation of quantum field theory proposed by Witten (2010) and developed by Cristoforetti et al. (2012, 2013) for alleviating the sign problem in Monte Carlo simulations. The algorithm provides an alternative to the Langevin-based approach previously proposed by the same authors, offering inherent stability since field configurations are computed from the Gaussian thimble parametrization rather than evolved stochastically. The one-plaquette model serves as a benchmark where complex Langevin without ad-hoc optimizations gives wrong results (Berges 2007), making it a nontrivial test case. The method enables future application to systems with many degrees of freedom where the O(N³) cost of Jacobian computation is still far cheaper than the exponential scaling of reweighting approaches.
