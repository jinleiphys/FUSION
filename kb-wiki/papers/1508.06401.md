# Isospin splittings of meson and baryon masses from three-flavor lattice QCD + QED

> Lattice QCD simulations are now reaching a precision where isospin breaking effects become important. Previously, we have developed a program to systematically investigate the pattern of flavor symmetry beaking within QCD and successfully applied it to meson and baryon masses involving up, down and strange quarks. In this Letter we extend the calculations to QCD + QED and present our first results on isospin splittings in the pseudoscalar meson and baryon octets. In particular, we obtain the nucleon mass difference of $M_n-M_p=1.35(18)(8)\,\mbox{MeV}$ and the electromagnetic contribution to the pion splitting $M_{π^+}-M_{π^0}=4.60(20)\,\mbox{MeV}$. Further we report first determination of the separation between strong and electromagnetic contributions in the $\bar{MS}$ scheme.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD+QED calculation of isospin splittings in pseudoscalar meson and octet baryon masses, including a separation of strong and electromagnetic contributions in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme, but the QED contribution to the nucleon mass difference depends sensitively on how electromagnetic and strong contributions are separated, and the authors note that their QED result for $M_n-M_p$ is larger than some other estimates.

## Method

The authors performed three-flavor lattice QCD+QED simulations using a tree-level Symanzik improved SU(3) gauge action and a noncompact U(1) gauge action for the photon, with nonperturbatively $O(a)$ improved SLiNC fermions. They simulated at a single lattice spacing ($\beta=5.50$, $a=0.068(2)$ fm) on three volumes ($24^3\times48$, $32^3\times64$, $48^3\times96$) at the SU(3) symmetric point where all three quark masses are equal, using an artificially large electromagnetic coupling $e^2=1.25$ and then interpolating to the physical fine structure constant $\alpha_{\rm EM}=1/137$. They employed partial quenching to vary valence and sea quark masses independently, computed about 40 pseudoscalar masses and 70 baryon masses per ensemble, and corrected for finite size effects using effective field theory.

## Key numbers

- $M_n-M_p = 1.35(18)(8)$ MeV (total QCD+QED, infinite volume)
- QED contribution to $M_n-M_p = -2.20(28)(10)$ MeV
- $M_{\pi^+}-M_{\pi^0}$ (QED contribution only) = $4.60(20)$ MeV
- $M_{K^0}-M_{K^+} = 4.09(10)$ MeV (total QCD+QED)
- $M_{\Sigma^-}-M_{\Sigma^+} = 7.60(73)(8)$ MeV (total QCD+QED)
- $M_{\Xi^-}-M_{\Xi^0} = 6.10(55)(45)$ MeV (total QCD+QED)
- Quark mass ratios in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme at $\mu^2=4$ GeV$^2$: $m_u/m_d = 0.52(2)$, $m_s/m_d = 19.7(9)$
- $\epsilon = 0.50(6)$ in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme, where $\epsilon$ is defined by $(M_{K^+}^2-M_{K^0}^2)_{\rm QED}-M_{\pi^+}^2+M_{\pi^0}^2=\epsilon\,\Delta_\pi$
- Lattice spacing $a=0.068(2)$ fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous program of flavor symmetry breaking in three-flavor lattice QCD (Bietenholz et al., 2010, 2011) by adding dynamical QED, and is one of only two published works (alongside Borsanyi et al., 2014) reporting fully dynamical QCD+QED simulations. The results enable first-principles determination of the separation between strong and electromagnetic contributions to hadron mass splittings in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme, and allow the authors to map the allowed region of the fundamental parameters $m_u/m_d$ and $\alpha_{\rm EM}$ for which the Universe would permit nucleosynthesis. The QED contribution to the nucleon mass difference is found to be larger than estimates from the Cottingham formula (Walker-Loud, 2012) and from Borsanyi et al. (2014), which the authors attribute to different schemes for separating QCD and QED contributions.
