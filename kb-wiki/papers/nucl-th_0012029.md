# Equilibration and freeze-out in an exploding system

> We use a simple gas model to study non-equilibrium aspects of the multiparticle dynamics relevant to heavy ion collisions. By performing numerical simulations for various initial conditions we identify several characteristic features of the fast dynamics occurring in implosion-explosion like processes.

# Equilibration and freeze-out in an exploding system

## Key claim

Using a classical hard-sphere gas model, the authors show that small exploding nuclear systems do not reach full thermodynamic equilibrium even at the moment of maximum compression, and that the space-time distribution of last scatterings (freeze-out) is broad rather than sharp, contradicting the Cooper-Frye picture of a sharp freeze-out hypersurface.

## Method

The authors simulate classical non-relativistic hard-sphere elastic scatterings of identical balls (mass 940 MeV, hard-core radius 0.4–0.5 fm) initially placed in a sphere of radius R = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm, with A = 50 or 100 particles. Initial velocities are a superposition of thermal motion and a spherically symmetric linear collective velocity field v(r) = v_0f r/R, with the flow energy fraction eta varied from 0 (thermal explosion) to 1 (implosion). The scattering rate, freeze-out field (space-time distribution of last scatterings), freeze-out flow field, and an equilibrium measure Sigma based on phase-space entropy are computed from ensembles of 10–20 events.

## Key numbers

- Particle mass is 940 MeV; hard-core radius r_c = 0.4–0.5 fm; initial sphere radius R = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm.
- Simulations performed with A = 50 and A = 100 particles; flow velocity parameter v_0f varied from -0.5 to 0.5.
- Flow energy fraction eta varied from 0 (thermal explosion) to 1 (implosion).
- For the thermal explosion (eta = 0) with 100 particles and v_0f = -0.25, 362 last scatterings occurred in 10 events; for the implosion (eta = 1), all 500 particles scattered at least once.
- The equilibrium measure Sigma reaches an asymptotic value of approximately 0.55 for both thermal explosion and implosion-explosion cases; for 100% outgoing flow, Sigma is approximately constant at 0.4.
- In the implosion-explosion case, particles obtain a nearly Maxwellian velocity distribution from t = 6 fm/c, which is also the time of maximum compression.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Nuclear Molecular Dynamics (NMD) studies (Bondorf et al., 1995) and their earlier use of pseudo-entropy (Neergaard et al., 2000) to investigate equilibration in exploding systems. The finding of a broad freeze-out distribution contradicts the Cooper-Frye picture (Cooper and Frye, 1974) of a sharp freeze-out hypersurface, consistent with microscopic simulations of relativistic heavy ion collisions by Bravina et al. (1999). The results challenge equilibrium-based models ranging from statistical multifragmentation to fluid dynamical quark-gluon plasma models, and the authors plan to extend the study to other types of interactions.
