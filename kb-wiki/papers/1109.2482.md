# Fluid dynamical equations and transport coefficients of relativistic gases with non-extensive statistics

> We derive equations for fluid dynamics from a non-extensive Boltzmann transport equation consistent with Tsallis' non-extensive entropy formula. We evaluate transport coefficients employing the relaxation time approximation and investigate non-extensive effects in leading order dissipative phenomena at relativistic energies, like heat conductivity, shear and bulk viscosity.

## Key claim

The paper derives relativistic fluid-dynamical equations and transport coefficients from a non-extensive Boltzmann transport equation consistent with Tsallis entropy, showing that the formal structure of the dissipative fluid equations remains identical to the classical Boltzmann-Gibbs case (q=1) while the transport coefficients acquire q-dependent modifications through generalized thermodynamic integrals.

## Method

The authors start from Lavagno's non-extensive Boltzmann equation (NEBE) with a q-modified collision integral and the Tsallis entropy four-current. They apply the Chapman-Enskog expansion near local q-equilibrium and use the Anderson-Witting relaxation time approximation to linearize the collision term. From the resulting non-equilibrium distribution function, they compute the shear stress tensor, heat flow, and bulk viscous pressure as momentum integrals, identifying the shear viscosity, thermal conductivity, and bulk viscosity coefficients in terms of generalized thermodynamic integrals K_q(i,j).

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity coefficient is given by η = τ_C β_0 K_q(3,2), where τ_C is the relaxation time and β_0 = 1/T
- The thermal conductivity coefficient is κ = τ_C h_0 β_0^2 (K_q(2,1) - h_0 K_q(1,1)), where h_0 = (e_0 + p_0)/n_0 is the enthalpy per particle
- For q>1, convergence of the thermodynamic integrals requires (i+2) < 1/(q-1) for I_q(i,j) integrals to be finite
- For q<1, the integrals run over a finite energy range E_k ≤ T/(1-q)
- In the massless limit, the speed of sound is c_s = √(1/3) irrespective of the q-parameter
- Fits to RHIC and LHC spectra give hadronic matter values q_H ≈ 1.08..1.2 and quark matter values q_Q ≈ 1.22

## Context

This work extends the relativistic fluid dynamics framework to incorporate non-extensive Tsallis statistics, building on the non-extensive Boltzmann equation proposed by Lavagno (2002) and correcting the entropy four-current definition following Osada and Wilk (2008-2009). The authors note that previous transport coefficient calculations were done in the non-relativistic limit by Lima et al. (2001, 2005) using a different non-extensive Boltzmann equation. The results enable first-order relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics (Navier-Stokes-Fourier equations) for systems with power-law tailed equilibrium distributions, relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision data where q≠1 fits are observed.
