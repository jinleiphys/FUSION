# Hidden-charm tetraquarks with strangeness in the chiral quark model

> The hidden-charm tetraquarks with strangeness, $c\bar{c}s\bar{q}$ $(q=u,\,d)$, in $J^P=0^+$, $1^+$ and $2^+$ are systematically investigated in the framework of real- and complex-scaling range of a chiral quark model, whose parameters have been fixed in advance describing hadron, hadron-hadron and multiquark phenomenology. Each tetraquark configuration, compatible with the quantum numbers studied, is taken into account; this includes meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark and K-type arrangements of quarks with all possible color wave functions in four-body sector. Among the different numerical techniques to solve the Schrödinger-like 4-body bound state equation, we use a variational method in which the trial wave function is expanded in complex-range Gaussian basis functions, which is characterized by its simplicity and flexibility. This theoretical framework has already been used to study different kinds of multiquark systems, such as the hidden-charm pentaquarks, $P^+_c$, and doubly-charmed tetraquarks, $T^+_{cc}$. The recently reported $Z_{cs}$ states by the BESIII and LHCb collaborations are generally compatible with either compact tetraquark or hadronic molecular resonance configurations in our investigation. Moreover, several additional exotic resonances are found in the mass range between 3.8 GeV and 4.6 GeV.

## Key claim

The recently reported $Z_{cs}$ states by the BESIII and LHCb collaborations are generally compatible with either compact tetraquark or hadronic molecular resonance configurations in the chiral quark model, but the paper does not claim a unique identification for any single state; instead it finds that several additional exotic resonances exist in the mass range between 3.8 GeV and 4.6 GeV.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrodinger-like 4-body bound state equation for the $c\bar{c}s\bar{q}$ ($q=u,d$) system using a variational method with complex-range Gaussian basis functions, within a chiral quark model whose parameters were fixed from hadron, hadron-hadron, and multiquark phenomenology. They considered all tetraquark configurations compatible with $J^P=0^+$, $1^+$, and $2^+$ quantum numbers, including meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark, and K-type arrangements with all possible color wave functions, and applied the complex-scaling method to distinguish bound, resonance, and scattering states.

## Key numbers

* The $Z_{cs}(3985)$ state has an experimentally measured mass of $3982.5^{+1.8}_{-2.6}\pm 2.1$ MeV and width of $12.8^{+5.3}_{-4.4}\pm 3.0$ MeV, reported by BESIII with a significance of $5.3\sigma$.
* The $Z_{cs}(4000)$ state has mass $4003\pm 6^{+4}_{-14}$ MeV and width $131\pm 15\pm 26$ MeV, with preferred spin-parity $J^P=1^+$, reported by LHCb.
* Additional exotic resonances are found in the mass range between 3.8 GeV and 4.6 GeV.
* Model parameters include quark masses: $m_q=313$ MeV, $m_s=555$ MeV, $m_c=1752$ MeV; confinement parameters: $a_c=430$ MeV, $\mu_c=0.70$ fm$^{-1}$, $\Delta=181.10$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral quark model studies of hidden-charm pentaquarks (Yang 2015) and doubly-charmed tetraquarks (Yang 2020) to the hidden-charm tetraquark sector with strangeness. It directly addresses the $Z_{cs}(3985)$ state reported by BESIII (2020) and the $Z_{cs}(4000)$, $X(4685)$, $Z_{cs}(4220)$, and $X(4630)$ states reported by LHCb (2021), providing a unified theoretical framework that can accommodate both compact tetraquark and hadronic molecular interpretations. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted additional resonances in the 3.8-4.6 GeV mass range.
