# Excitation of Pygmy Dipole Resonance in neutron-rich nuclei via Coulomb and nuclear fields

> We study the nature of the low-lying dipole strength in neutron-rich nuclei, often associated to the Pygmy Dipole Resonance. The states are described within the Hartree-Fock plus RPA formalism, using different parametrizations of the Skyrme interaction. We show how the information from combined reactions processes involving the Coulomb and different mixtures of isoscalar and isovector nuclear interactions can provide a clue to reveal the characteristic features of these states.

# Excitation of Pygmy Dipole Resonance in neutron-rich nuclei via Coulomb and nuclear fields

## Key claim
The low-lying dipole strength in neutron-rich nuclei, often interpreted as a collective Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR), may not be fully collective; the authors show that combined Coulomb and nuclear excitation processes with different isoscalar/isovector mixtures can reveal the characteristic features of these states, but caution that a full interpretation of the state in terms of PDR is not obvious and the collective nature requirement was not found to be fulfilled in their calculations.

## Method
The authors describe dipole states in neutron-rich tin isotopes using Hartree-Fock plus discrete Random Phase Approximation (RPA) with Skyrme interactions (SGII and SLY4 parametrizations). They construct nuclear formfactors by double-folding the M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction with RPA transition densities and the density of the reaction partner, then calculate excitation cross sections for the system $^{132}$Sn with different projectiles ($\alpha$, $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca) at various bombarding energies using a semiclassical approach along classical ion-ion trajectories.

## Key numbers
- Low-lying dipole strength in $^{132}$Sn carries a fraction of the Energy Weighted Sum Rule (EWSR) of the order of few per cent, appearing below 10 MeV.
- For the $^{132}$Sn + $^{208}$Pb reaction, at very low bombarding energy the probability of exciting the PDR region becomes even larger than exciting the Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR).
- Differential cross sections are calculated for the systems $^{132}$Sn + $\alpha$, $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca at 10 MeV per nucleon.
- The RPA dipole strength distributions for $^{100,120,132}$Sn are calculated with a smoothing procedure using a Lorentzian with 1 MeV width.

## Context
This work extends theoretical studies of low-lying dipole strength in neutron-rich nuclei, which have been predicted by almost all microscopic models including Hartree-Fock plus RPA with Skyrme interactions and relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov plus relativistic quasiparticle RPA (Colò et al.). The authors note that previous experimental evidence for these states came from high-energy Coulomb excitation processes (GSI collaboration), which only provide B(E$\lambda$) transition rates. The present work builds on earlier calculations by Lanza et al. and aims to show how combined Coulomb and nuclear excitation with different projectiles can probe transition densities beyond simple B(E1) values, enabling future experiments to distinguish between PDR and GDR states and test the collective nature of the low-lying dipole strength.
