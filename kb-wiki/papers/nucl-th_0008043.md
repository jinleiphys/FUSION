# Density Effect on Hadronization of a Quark Plasma

> The hadronization cross section in a quark plasma at finite temperature and density is calculated in the framework of Nambu--Jona-lasinio model with explicit chiral symmetry breaking. In apposition to the familiar temperature effect, the quark plasma at high density begins to hadronize suddenly. It leads to a sudden and strong increase of final state pions in relativistic heavy ion collisions which may be considered as a clear signature of chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, unlike the smooth hadronization driven by temperature, a quark plasma at high density begins to hadronize suddenly, producing a sharp jump in the hadronization cross section; this effect persists even when explicit chiral symmetry breaking (nonzero current quark mass) is included, whereas the temperature-driven singularity at the critical point is completely washed out.

## Method

The authors calculate the total hadronization cross section for the process \(q\bar q \rightarrow 2\pi\) in the flavor \(SU(2)\) Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, working to first order in a \(1/N_c\) expansion. They include explicit chiral symmetry breaking via a nonzero current quark mass \(m_0 = 0.005\) GeV and evaluate the cross section as a function of center-of-mass energy \(s\), temperature \(T\), and baryon density \(n_b\). The quark and pion masses are determined self-consistently from the gap equation and the meson dispersion relation, and the hadronization line is defined by the condition \(m_\pi = 2 m_q\).

## Key numbers

* Tricritical point \(P\) in the chiral limit is located at \(T = 0.079\) GeV and \(\mu = 0.28\) GeV, corresponding to a baryon density \(n_b/n_0 = 1.95\) with \(n_0 = 0.17/\text{fm}^3\).
* Vacuum parameters: constituent quark mass \(m_q = 0.32\) GeV, pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 0.093\) GeV, pion mass \(m_\pi = 0.134\) GeV, and current quark mass \(m_0 = 0.005\) GeV.
* At low density (\(n_b/n_0 = 1.1\)) and zero temperature, the cross section shows a singularity at threshold energy \(s_{th} = 1.33\) GeV\(^2\) and a second singularity at \(s = m_\sigma^2\).
* At zero temperature, the hadronization cross section jumps precipitously from zero to a maximum at the hadronization point for any \(s\), due to the first-order chiral transition.
* The temperature-driven hadronization occurs smoothly over a range \(\Delta T \sim 0.1\) GeV, in contrast to the density-driven sudden jump.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hadronization in the NJL model, specifically the calculation of the \(q\bar q \rightarrow 2\pi\) cross section at zero baryon density and in the chiral limit by Huefner et al. (1994), which found a divergence at the critical temperature. The present paper introduces finite baryon density and explicit chiral symmetry breaking, showing that the first-order chiral transition at high density (predicted by models such as the instanton model of Berges and Rajagopal (1999)) survives in the hadronization rate, while the temperature-driven singularity is washed out. This enables a possible experimental signature of chiral symmetry restoration in relativistic heavy ion collisions: a sudden strong increase in final-state pions.
