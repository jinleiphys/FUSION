# Theoretical study of the N N --> N N pi pi reaction

> We have developed a model for the N N --> N N pi pi reaction and evaluated cross sections for the different charged channels. The low energy part of those channels where the pions can be in an isospin zero state is dominated by N* excitation, driven by an isoscalar source recently found experimentally, followed by the decay N* --> N (pi pi, T=0, s-wave). At higher energies, and in channels where the pions are not in T=0, Delta excitation mechanisms become relevant. A rough agreement with the experimental data is obtained in most channels. Repercussions of the present findings for the ABC effect and the p p --> p p pi0 reaction close to threshold are also suggested.

## Key claim

The model shows that the low-energy part of the \(NN \rightarrow NN\pi\pi\) reaction in channels where the two pions can be in an isospin-zero state is dominated by \(N^*\) (1440) excitation driven by an isoscalar source, followed by the decay \(N^* \rightarrow N (\pi\pi)^{T=0}_{S-wave}\); however, the agreement with experimental data is only "rough" in most channels, and the theoretical uncertainties from the \(N^*\) decay couplings can change the predicted cross sections by about a factor of two at low energies.

## Method

The authors constructed a model for the \(NN \rightarrow NN\pi\pi\) reaction using tree-level diagrams that include non-resonant chiral terms (from lowest-order SU(2) chiral Lagrangians), \(\Delta\) resonance excitation terms, and \(N^*\) (1440) resonance excitation terms. The \(NN \rightarrow NN^*\) transition was modeled using an effective isoscalar "\(\sigma\)" exchange, with the coupling strength \(g_{\sigma NN^*}\) extracted from a fit to \((\alpha,\alpha')\) data on a proton target. The \(N^* \rightarrow N (\pi\pi)^{T=0}_{S-wave}\) decay was described by an \(O(p^2)\) Lagrangian with two parameters \(c^*_1\) and \(c^*_2\), constrained by fitting the \(\pi^- p \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^- n\) reaction. Short-range correlations were included for diagrams with two p-wave couplings using a correlated \(\pi+\rho\) exchange model.

## Key numbers

* The isoscalar \(\pi N \rightarrow \pi N\) amplitude parameter \(\lambda_1 = 0.0075\) (dimensionless, with \(\mu \equiv m_\pi\)).
* The effective \(\sigma NN\) coupling squared: \(g^2_{\sigma NN}/4\pi = 5.69\); the fitted \(\sigma NN^*\) coupling squared: \(g^2_{\sigma NN^*}/4\pi = 1.33\), with both couplings of the same sign.
* The \(N^* \rightarrow N (\pi\pi)^{T=0}_{S-wave}\) coupling parameters: set I gives \(c^*_1 = -7.27\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\), \(c^*_2 = 0\); set II gives \(c^*_1 = -12.7\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\), \(c^*_2 = 1.98\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\).
* The \(\pi N\Delta\) coupling squared: \(f^{*2}/4\pi = 0.36\); the \(\pi\Delta\Delta\) coupling ratio: \(f_\Delta/f_{\pi NN} = 4/5\).
* The \(N^* N\pi\) coupling constant \(\tilde{f} = 0.477\); the \(N^* \Delta\pi\) coupling constant \(g_{\pi N^*\Delta} = 2.07\).
* For the \(pp \rightarrow pp\pi^+\pi^-\) channel, the \(N^*\) terms dominate at low energies (below \(T_p = 900\ \text{MeV}\)), and omitting them gives disagreement larger than two orders of magnitude; the \(\Delta\Delta\) mechanism becomes dominant above \(T_p = 1\ \text{GeV}\) in the laboratory frame.
* The short-range correlations reduce the \(\Delta\Delta\) term contribution by about a factor of three relative to pure pion exchange.

## Context

This work extends the study of two-pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, building on earlier models for \(\pi N \rightarrow \pi\pi N\) (Oset and Vicente-Vacas, 1991) and \(\gamma N \rightarrow \pi\pi N\) reactions. It incorporates the recent experimental finding of a strong isoscalar \(NN \rightarrow NN^*\) transition from \((\alpha,\alpha')\) experiments (Morsch et al., 1992), analyzed by Oset et al. (1995). The model improves upon the earlier one-pion-exchange approach of Risser and Shuster (1973) by including the dominant isoscalar excitation mechanism and short-range correlations. The results suggest that the \(pp \rightarrow pp\pi^+\pi^-\) reaction can provide stronger constraints on the \(N^* \rightarrow N(\pi\pi)^{T=0}_{S-wave}\) couplings than the \(\pi N \rightarrow \pi\pi N\) reaction, and may help resolve longstanding issues with the ABC effect in the \(np \rightarrow d+X\) reaction.
