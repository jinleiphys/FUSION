# QED radiative corrections in inverse beta decay from virtual pions

> Inverse beta decay (IBD), $\overlineν_e p \to e^+ n \left( γ\right)$, is the main detection channel for reactor and supernova antineutrinos. To provide precise IBD cross sections at antineutrino energies $E_{\overlineν_e} \gtrsim 10~\mathrm{MeV}$, we evaluate radiative corrections from virtual pions within the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. At leading order, only the pion isospin-splitting contributions are not suppressed by the electron mass. At next-to-leading order, besides recoil effects, only the Wilson coefficient $c_4$ contributes to the kinematic dependence. However, its precise value is not relevant for IBD at relatively low energies since all next-to-leading order radiative corrections are relatively small. We find the kinematic dependence of the pion-induced QED radiative corrections at the level and below the uncertainty from the momentum dependence of the nucleon form factors. Our results enable sub-permille theoretical precision of charged-current elastic (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering at antineutrino energies $E_{\overlineν_e} \gtrsim 10~\mathrm{MeV}$.

## Key claim

The kinematic-dependent QED radiative corrections from virtual pions in inverse beta decay are at the level of or below the uncertainty from the momentum dependence of the nucleon form factors, meaning that for antineutrino energies above approximately 10 MeV, these pion-induced corrections do not currently limit theoretical precision.

## Method

The author evaluates QED radiative corrections from virtual pion fields within the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT), considering both leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) contributions. The calculation includes pion isospin-splitting contributions, recoil effects, and the Wilson coefficient \(c_4\), while neglecting terms suppressed by the electron mass. The kinematic-dependent corrections are expressed as shifts in the vector coupling constant and additional non-factorizable cross-section terms.

## Key numbers

- The threshold antineutrino energy for inverse beta decay is \(E_{\overline{\nu}_e}^\mathrm{thr} \approx 1.806066~\mathrm{MeV}\).
- The leading-order differential cross section uses the Fermi constant \(G_F = 1.1663787(6)\times 10^{-5}~\mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\) and the CKM matrix element \(V_{ud} = 0.97348(31)\).
- The contribution from the Wilson coefficient \(c_4\) to the kinematic-dependent QED correction is below \(0.1\%\) at antineutrino energies up to \(E_{\overline{\nu}_e} \sim 150~\mathrm{MeV}\), reaching \(0.16\%\) at \(E_{\overline{\nu}_e} \sim 200~\mathrm{MeV}\).
- The dominant kinematic-dependent pion-induced QED contribution scales as \(\alpha \frac{E_{\overline{\nu}_e}}{m_\pi}\), while subleading corrections enter as \(\frac{\alpha}{\pi} \frac{E^2_{\overline{\nu}_e}}{m^2_\pi}\).

## Context

This work extends the pionless HBChPT calculations of QED radiative corrections by Tomalak (2025) to higher antineutrino energies by including pion degrees of freedom, building on the matching framework of Cirigliano (2022). The results enable sub-permille theoretical precision for charged-current elastic (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering at energies above 10 MeV, but the pion-induced corrections are currently smaller than the uncertainty from the nucleon axial-vector form factor, as measured by MINERvA (2023). Future improvements in form-factor measurements, as suggested by Petti (2023), would make these corrections relevant for reactor and supernova antineutrino experiments.
