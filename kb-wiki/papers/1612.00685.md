# Deeply quasi-bound state in single- and double-$\bar{K}$ nuclear clusters

> New calculations of the quasi-bound state positions in $K^{-}K^{-}pp$ kaonic nuclear cluster are performed using non-relativistic four-body Faddeev-type equations in AGS form. The corresponding separable approximation for the integral kernels in the three- and four-body kaonic clusters is obtained by using the Hilbert-Schmidt expansion procedure. Different phenomenological models of $\bar{K}N-πΣ$ potentials with one- and two-pole structure of $Λ$(1405) resonance and separable potential models for $\bar{K}$-$\bar{K}$ and nucleon-nucleon interactions, are used. The dependence of the resulting four-body binding energy on models of $\bar{K}N-πΣ$ interaction is investigated. We obtained the binding energy of the $K^{-}K^{-}pp$ quasi-bound state $\sim$ 80-94 MeV with the phenomenological $\bar{K}N$ potentials. The width is about $\sim$ 5-8 MeV for the two-pole models of the interaction, while the one-pole potentials give $\sim$ 24-31 MeV width.

## Key claim

The $K^{-}K^{-}pp$ four-body system is deeply bound with binding energy of 80-94 MeV, but the exact value and width depend strongly on whether the $\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$ interaction model has a one-pole or two-pole structure of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance, with the two-pole models yielding much narrower widths (5-8 MeV) than one-pole models (24-31 MeV).

## Method

The authors solved non-relativistic four-body Faddeev-type Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations in momentum space, using separable representations of two-body potentials and applying the Hilbert-Schmidt expansion procedure to obtain separable approximations for the three- and four-body integral kernels. They used four different phenomenological models of the coupled-channel $\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$ interaction (one-pole and two-pole versions fitted to SIDDHARTA and KEK experimental data), a separable PEST potential for the $NN$ interaction, and a Yamaguchi-form repulsive potential for the $\bar{K}\bar{K}$ interaction with parameters reproducing lattice QCD $K^{+}K^{+}$ scattering length.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $K^{-}K^{-}pp$ quasi-bound state: 80-94 MeV (range across all potential models used)
- Width of $K^{-}K^{-}pp$: 5-8 MeV for two-pole models of $\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$ interaction; 24-31 MeV for one-pole models
- For the SIDDHARTA-fitted one-pole potential, $K^{-}K^{-}pp$ pole position: $-92.7-i15.5$ MeV (binding energy 92.7 MeV, half-width 15.5 MeV)
- For the SIDDHARTA-fitted two-pole potential, $K^{-}K^{-}pp$ pole position: $-83.8-i4.0$ MeV (binding energy 83.8 MeV, half-width 4.0 MeV)
- For the KEK-fitted one-pole potential, $K^{-}K^{-}pp$ pole position: $-80.0-i12.0$ MeV (from table continuation, binding energy 80.0 MeV, half-width 12.0 MeV)
- For the KEK-fitted two-pole potential, $K^{-}K^{-}pp$ pole position: $-94.0-i3.0$ MeV (from table continuation, binding energy 94.0 MeV, half-width 3.0 MeV)
- Addition of one nucleon to $K^{-}K^{-}p$ gains approximately 55-75 MeV to ground-state energy
- Addition of one $\bar{K}$ to $K^{-}pp$ gains approximately 35-40 MeV to ground-state energy
- $K^{-}pp$ binding energy: $-52.8-i31.5$ MeV (SIDDHARTA one-pole) and $-48.5-i24.1$ MeV (SIDDHARTA two-pole)
- $K^{-}K^{-}p$ binding energy: $-17.8-i56.7$ MeV (SIDDHARTA one-pole) and $-27.6-i41.2$ MeV (SIDDHARTA two-pole)

## Context

This work extends previous Faddeev calculations of the $K^{-}pp$ and $K^{-}K^{-}p$ three-body systems by Shevchenko and Haidenbauer (using phenomenological and chirally motivated potentials) and by Kanada-En'yo and Jido (using Gaussian expansion method) to the four-body $K^{-}K^{-}pp$ system. The results contradict the earlier prediction by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2004) of 117 MeV binding energy for $K^{-}K^{-}pp$ using a G-matrix method, finding instead 80-94 MeV. The study enables systematic investigation of multi-$\bar{K}$ nuclear clusters as possible doorways to kaon condensation in dense matter and neutron star studies.
