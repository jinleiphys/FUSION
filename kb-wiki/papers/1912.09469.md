# A schematic reaction-theory model for nuclear fission

> The K-matrix formalism is applied to a schematic model for nuclear fission. The purpose is to explore the dependence of observables on the assumptions made about the configuration space and nucleon interaction in the Hamiltonian of the fissile nucleus. As expected, branching ratios in induced fission are found to depend sensitively on the character of the residual interaction, whether it is pairing in form or taken from a random ensemble. On the other hand, the branching ratio is not much affected by the presence of additional configurations that do not introduce new fission paths.

# A schematic reaction-theory model for nuclear fission

## Key claim
The fission-to-capture branching ratio in induced fission depends sensitively on whether the residual interaction is pairing-like or random, but is hardly affected by adding "spectator" configurations that do not introduce new fission paths. The abstract's claim that branching ratios "depend sensitively" on the character of the residual interaction is qualified by the model's restriction to seniority-zero configurations with only six orbitals and six nucleons.

## Method
The K-matrix formalism is applied to a schematic configuration-interaction (CI) Hamiltonian with six orbitals (N_orb = 6) half-filled by six nucleons in seniority-zero pairs, giving 20 internal configurations. The Hamiltonian includes a quadrupole-like operator Q̂ to measure elongation, a pairing or random two-body interaction, and decay widths assigned to entrance (neutron), capture, and fission channels. Branching ratios are computed as energy-averaged ratios of S-matrix transmission strengths over a window from E = -4e₀ to E = +4e₀.

## Key numbers
- Model space dimension: N_conf = 20 configurations (6 orbitals, 6 nucleons, seniority zero)
- Pairing strength: G = 1 e₀ (in units of the single-particle spacing e₀)
- Partial widths assigned: Γ_n/e₀ = 0.063 (entrance), Γ_c/e₀ = 1.0 (capture), Γ_f/e₀ = 1.0 (fission)
- Branching ratio α⁻¹ for pairing interaction with 20 configurations: 0.82 ± 0.06
- Branching ratio α⁻¹ for random interaction with 20 configurations: 0.28 ± 0.13
- Branching ratio α⁻¹ for pairing interaction with 20+18 spectator configurations: 0.73 ± 0.05
- Branching ratio α⁻¹ for random interaction with 20+18 spectator configurations: 0.28 ± 0.12
- Empirical pairing gap in actinides: Δ ≈ 0.75 MeV (from Δ ≈ 12/A¹/²)
- Empirical neutron orbital spacing: e₀ ≈ 0.33 MeV (from e₀ ≈ 4ε_f/3N)

## Context
This work extends the K-matrix reaction-theory approach to fission, contrasting with time-dependent mean-field methods (Simenel 2018, Bulgac 2015) and R-matrix theory (Wigner 1947, Bouland 2013). The schematic model is designed to test approximations needed for realistic Hamiltonian-based fission theory, where full configuration spaces are intractable. The finding that spectator configurations barely affect branching ratios challenges expectations from time-dependent models coupling collective variables to internal degrees of freedom (Caldeira 1983, Scamps 2015), and raises open questions about whether this insensitivity persists in larger spaces including quasiparticle excitations.
