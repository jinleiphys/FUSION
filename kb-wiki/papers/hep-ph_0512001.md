# Nonlocality effects on spin-one pairing patterns in two-flavor color superconducting quark matter and compact stars applications

> We study the influence of nonlocality in the interaction on two spin one pairing patterns of two-flavor quark matter: the anisotropic blue color paring besides the usual two color superconducting matter (2SCb), in which red and green colors are paired, and the color spin locking phase (CSL). The effect of nonlocality on the gaps is rather large and the pairings exhibit a strong dependence on the form factor of the interaction, especially in the low density region. The application of these small spin-one condensates for compact stars is analyzed: the early onset of quark matter in the nonlocal models may help to stabilize hybrid star configurations. While the anisotropic blue quark pairing does not survive a big asymmetry in flavor space as imposed by the charge neutrality condition, the CSL phase as a flavor independent pairing can be realized as neutral matter in compact star cores. However, smooth form factors and the missmatch between the flavor chemical potential in neutral matter make the effective gaps of the order of magnitude $\simeq 10$ keV, and a more systematic analysis is needed to decide whether such small gaps could be consistent with the cooling phenomenology.

## Key claim
Nonlocality in the quark interaction dramatically reduces spin-one pairing gaps relative to the local NJL model, and for the color-spin locking (CSL) phase under charge neutrality and beta equilibrium the effective gaps become of order 10 keV, which may suppress the direct Urca process in hybrid stars, but a more systematic analysis is needed to decide whether such small gaps are consistent with cooling phenomenology.

## Method
The authors extend a nonlocal, instantaneous chiral quark model with separable form factors (Gaussian, Lorentzian, and sharp cutoff) to two spin-one pairing patterns: the anisotropic blue color pairing (2SCb) and the color-spin locking (CSL) phase. They solve the gap equations for the chiral condensate and diquark gaps at zero temperature as functions of quark chemical potential, and then impose beta equilibrium and electric charge neutrality to compute the gaps for matter in compact star cores.

## Key numbers
- For the 2SCb phase with the Gaussian form factor, the blue quark pairing gaps are about two orders of magnitude lower than the corresponding NJL model gaps (both with fixed constituent quark mass M=380 MeV).
- For the CSL phase in symmetric matter, the effective gaps for the Gaussian form factor increase from approximately 15 keV to 35 keV as a function of chemical potential; for the Lorentzian form factor they are nearly constant at 80-90 keV; for the NJL model they are in the range 300-200 keV.
- For beta-equilibrated neutral quark matter with the Gaussian form factor, the smallest CSL gap (for up quarks) runs from approximately 100 keV near the onset to approximately 500 keV at mu=500 MeV, while the down quark gap increases from approximately 380 keV to 1.4 MeV in the same range.
- The effective CSL gaps for neutral matter with the Gaussian form factor are of order 10 keV.

## Context
This work extends the study of spin-one pairing patterns in two-flavor color superconducting quark matter, previously investigated within the local NJL model by Buballa (2002) for the 2SCb phase and by Aguilera (2005) for the CSL phase, by introducing nonlocality via form factors. The nonlocal models produce an earlier onset of quark matter than the NJL model, which may help stabilize hybrid star configurations as shown by Grigorian (2003). The results are a preparatory step for consistent calculations of cooling phenomenology in hybrid stars, where the small CSL gaps (approximately 10 keV) might suppress the direct Urca process, but the strong parameter dependence calls for more systematic studies.
