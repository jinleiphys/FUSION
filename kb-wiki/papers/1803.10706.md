# Complementary Polynomials From Rodrigues' Representations For Confluent And Hypergeometric Functions And More

> Complementary polynomials of Legendre polynomials are briefly presented, as well as those for the confluent and hypergeometric functions, relativistic Hermite polynomials and corresponding new pre-Laguerre polynomials. The generating functions are all given in closed form and are much simpler than the standard ones. Some are simply polynomials in two variables. New recursions and addition formulas are derived.

# Complementary Polynomials From Rodrigues' Representations For Confluent And Hypergeometric Functions And More

## Key claim

Complementary polynomials derived from Rodrigues' formulas for Legendre, confluent hypergeometric, hypergeometric, relativistic Hermite, and pre-Laguerre polynomials all admit generating functions in closed form that are "much simpler than the standard ones", a claim qualified by the fact that these generating functions apply specifically to the complementary polynomial sets, not necessarily to the original polynomials themselves.

## Method

The author defines complementary polynomials recursively from the Rodrigues' formula of a given polynomial family, then derives their generating functions using a theorem from prior work (Weber, earlier publication) that expresses the sum over complementary polynomials as a Taylor expansion of a closed-form expression. For each polynomial family (Legendre, confluent hypergeometric, hypergeometric, relativistic Hermite, pre-Laguerre), the author computes the generating function, then extracts recursion relations, composition laws, addition formulas, and normalization constants by expanding the generating function and taking partial derivatives.

## Key numbers

- For complementary Legendre polynomials: normalization factor \( N_l^m = (-1)^m \binom{l}{(l-m)/2} \frac{(l-m)!!(l-m)!}{(l+m-1)!!} \) for even \( l-m \), with recursion \( N_l^{m+1} = N_l^m / [(l-m)(l+m+1)] \) for \( -l < m < l \)
- For confluent hypergeometric complementary polynomials: \( {\cal P}_\nu(0,l) = \nu! \binom{c-1+l}{\nu} \)
- For hypergeometric complementary polynomials: \( {\cal P}_\nu(0,l) = \nu! \binom{l+C-1}{\nu} \) where \( C = l - \nu + c \)
- For pre-Laguerre polynomials at \( x=0 \): \( {\cal P}_\nu^N(0,l) = \nu! \binom{l}{\nu} \) for \( \nu \le l \), and 0 for \( \nu > l \)
- For pre-Laguerre polynomials at \( x=-N \): \( {\cal P}_\nu^N(-N,l) = \nu! (-1)^\nu \binom{-N}{\nu} \)

## Context

This work extends the author's prior method (Weber, earlier publication) for constructing complementary polynomials from Rodrigues' formulas, and completes the theory of complementary Legendre polynomials by providing a simple recursion for their normalizations, rendering unnecessary the lengthy developments in a prior trio of papers (Weber, earlier work). The generating functions for confluent and hypergeometric complementary polynomials are so simple that the author notes the H-atom wave functions become "truly accessible to undergraduates" using the associated Laguerre polynomial generating function, as implemented in a textbook (Arfken, Weber, and Harris, 2013). The results enable new composition laws and addition formulas for these polynomial families, with potential applications in quantum mechanical problems involving radial Schrodinger equations.
