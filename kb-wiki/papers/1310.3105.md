# Fluid dynamics with saturated minijet initial conditions in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Using next-to-leading order perturbative QCD and a conjecture of saturation to suppress the production of low-energy partons, we calculate the initial energy densities and formation times for the dissipative fluid dynamical evolution of the quark-gluon plasma produced in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We identify the framework uncertainties and demonstrate the predictive power of the approach by a good global agreement with the measured centrality dependence of charged particle multiplicities, transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow simultaneously for the Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC and Au+Au at RHIC. In particular, the shear viscosity in the different phases of QCD matter is constrained in this new framework simultaneously by all these data.

## Key claim

Using next-to-leading order perturbative QCD combined with a saturation conjecture to suppress low-energy parton production, the authors compute initial energy densities and formation times for dissipative fluid dynamics and achieve good global agreement with measured centrality dependence of charged particle multiplicities, transverse momentum spectra, and elliptic flow simultaneously for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC and Au+Au at RHIC, thereby constraining the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio in different phases of QCD matter.

## Method

The authors compute minijet transverse energy production in A+A collisions using NLO pQCD with collinear factorization and CTEQ6M parton distribution functions with EPS09s nuclear PDFs, imposing a local saturation criterion that equates the computed dE_T/d^2s to K_sat p_0^3/pi to determine the saturation momentum p_sat at each transverse point. The resulting energy densities at formation time tau_s = 1/p_sat are evolved to a common initial time tau_0 = 1 GeV^-1 using either Bjorken free streaming or Bjorken hydrodynamic scaling to bracket pre-thermalization uncertainty, then used as initial conditions for 2+1D dissipative fluid dynamics with shear-stress evolution from transient relativistic fluid dynamics, with hadronization via Cooper-Frye freeze-out at T_dec = 100 MeV using the s95p-PCE-v1 equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Minimum saturation scale set to p_sat^min = 1 GeV, corresponding to maximum formation time tau_0 = 1 GeV^-1
- Freeze-out temperature T_dec = 100 MeV used for all calculations
- Chemical freeze-out temperature T_chem = 175 MeV used in the equation of state
- K_sat parameter tuned to reproduce multiplicity in 0-5% most central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- Hardness parameter beta scanned in range [0,1], with data favoring beta ~ 1
- Shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio eta/s(T) assumed to have a minimum at matching temperature 180 MeV between HRG and QGP phases
- Charged hadron multiplicity centrality dependence computed for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV and Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Elliptic flow coefficients v_2(p_T) compared with ALICE 4-particle cumulant data at LHC and STAR data at RHIC

## Context

This work extends the EKRT saturation model (Eskola et al., 1999) by using an NLO-improved pQCD plus local saturation framework from Paatelainen et al. (2012) to compute initial conditions for viscous fluid dynamics, rather than relying on Glauber model initial states (eBC and eWN) used in earlier work by Kolb et al. (2001). The approach is complementary to Color-Glass Condensate models (Gelis et al., 2010) and enables simultaneous multi-observable constraints on the temperature dependence of eta/s(T) across both LHC and RHIC collision energies, with future extension planned to include event-by-event fluctuations.
