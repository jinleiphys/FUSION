# Electromagnetic and weak decays of baryons in the unquenched quark model

> In this contribution, we discuss the electromagnetic and weak decays of baryons in the unquenched quark model and show that the observed discrepancies between the experimental data and the predictions of the constituent quark model can be accounted for in large part by the effects of sea quarks. Finally, the obtained results are discussed in terms of flavor-symmetry breaking.

## Key claim

The observed discrepancies between experimental data and constituent quark model predictions for baryon electromagnetic and weak decays can be accounted for in large part by including sea quark effects via the unquenched quark model, though for some hyperon decays the calculated widths remain significantly below experimental values.

## Method

The authors unquench the constituent quark model by incorporating a $^{3}P_{0}$ quark-antiquark pair-creation mechanism, summing over a complete set of intermediate baryon-meson states (limited to octet and decuplet baryons with pseudoscalar octet and singlet mesons, excluding radially excited states). The resulting baryon wave function is a superposition of a valence contribution and higher-Fock components consisting of intermediate baryon-meson configurations. Electromagnetic decay widths are computed from transition magnetic moments, and weak axial couplings are calculated for semileptonic beta decays.

## Key numbers

- Electromagnetic decay width $\Gamma(\Delta \rightarrow N \gamma)$: CQM 399 keV, UQM 606 keV, experiment $704 \pm 63$ keV
- Electromagnetic decay width $\Gamma(\Sigma^{\ast 0} \rightarrow \Lambda \gamma)$: CQM 260 keV, UQM 318 keV, experiment $451 \pm 77$ keV
- Electromagnetic decay width $\Gamma(\Sigma^{\ast +} \rightarrow \Sigma^+ \gamma)$: CQM 110 keV, UQM 131 keV, experiment $254 \pm 59$ keV
- Neutron axial coupling $g_A(n \rightarrow p)$: CQM 1.67, UQM 1.35, experiment $1.2701 \pm 0.0025$
- Axial coupling $g_A(\Sigma^- \rightarrow n)$: CQM -0.33, UQM -0.31, experiment $-0.340 \pm 0.017$
- Transition magnetic moment $\mu_{\Delta N}$: UQM 3.29 $\mu_N$, experiment $3.53 \pm 0.16$ $\mu_N$
- Transition magnetic moment $\mu_{\Sigma^{*+} \Sigma^+}$: UQM -2.52 $\mu_N$, experiment $-3.50 \pm 0.43$ $\mu_N$
- Flavor-symmetry breaking in transition magnetic moments: $\sim 15-25\%$; in axial couplings: less than $5\%$

## Context

This work extends the unquenched quark model approach previously applied to baryon masses, magnetic moments, and flavor asymmetries (Bijker and collaborators, 2009-2012) to electromagnetic and weak decay observables. The results show that the meson-cloud effects, particularly pions as noted in earlier work from Salamanca, can largely account for the effective $F$ and $D$ couplings used in the Cabibbo approach, consistent with findings from the meson-cloud model of Speth (1998). The calculations are limited to ground-state baryons and mesons, leaving the inclusion of radially excited states for future work.
