# Lefschetz thimbles in fermionic effective models with repulsive vector-field

> We discuss two problems in complexified auxiliary fields in fermionic effective models, the auxiliary sign problem associated with the repulsive vector-field and the choice of the cut for the scalar field appearing from the logarithmic function. In the fermionic effective models with attractive scalar and repulsive vector-type interaction, the auxiliary scalar and vector fields appear in the path integral after the bosonization of fermion ilinears. When we make the path integral well-defined by the Wick rotation of the vector field, the oscillating Boltzmann weight appears in the partition function. This "auxiliary" sign problem can be solved by using the Lefschetz-thimble path-integral method, where the integration path is constructed in the complex plane. Another serious obstacle in the numerical construction of Lefschetz thimbles is caused by singular points and cuts induced by multivalued functions of the complexified scalar field in the momentum integration. We propose a new prescription which fixes gradient flow trajectories on the same Riemann sheet in the flow evolution by performing the momentum integration in the complex domain.

## Key claim

The Lefschetz-thimble path-integral method can resolve the "auxiliary sign problem" that arises when the repulsive vector field in fermionic effective models is Wick rotated to make the path integral well-defined, but only if a new prescription is used to fix gradient flow trajectories on the same Riemann sheet by performing momentum integration in the complex domain to handle singular points and cuts from multivalued functions.

## Method

The authors applied the Lefschetz-thimble path-integral method to the two-flavor three-color Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with repulsive vector-current interaction, using the homogeneous auxiliary-field ansatz for the scalar field σ and the temporal component of the vector field ω₄. They analytically summed over Matsubara frequencies and then constructed gradient flow trajectories in the complex plane of the auxiliary fields, proposing a prescription where the momentum integration path in the complex plane is adjusted at each gradient-flow time step to keep the integration on the same Riemann sheet, avoiding singular points and cuts from the square root and logarithmic functions.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Lefschetz-thimble method, previously used in lattice QCD and the Thirring model (Fujii 2015, Alexandru 2016), to the "auxiliary sign problem" in fermionic effective models with repulsive vector interactions. The problem appears in the NJL model with vector-current interaction and also in relativistic mean-field models with ω meson fields in nuclear physics and in shell model Monte-Carlo methods (Koonin 1997). The proposed prescription for handling singularities from multivalued functions in the momentum integration is relevant for both the Lefschetz-thimble method and the complex Langevin method when applied to four-dimensional and higher-dimensional fermionic models.
