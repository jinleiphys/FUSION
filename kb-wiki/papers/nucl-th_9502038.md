# Correlations and Fluctuations in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

> Nucleon correlations in the target and projectile nuclei are shown to reduce significantly the fluctuations in multiple nucleon-nucleon collisions, total multiplicity and transverse energy in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, in particular for heavy projectile and target. The interplay between cross-section fluctuations, from color transparency and opacity, and nuclear correlations is calculated and found to be able to account for large fluctuations in transverse energy spectra. Numerical implementation of correlations and cross-section fluctuations in Monte-Carlo codes is discussed.

# Correlations and Fluctuations in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim
Nucleon correlations in the target and projectile nuclei significantly reduce fluctuations in multiple nucleon-nucleon collisions, total multiplicity, and transverse energy in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the interplay between these correlations and cross-section fluctuations from color transparency and opacity can account for the large fluctuations observed in transverse energy spectra. The reduction in fluctuations depends critically on the detailed structure of the correlation function and the interaction range, with the correlation length being comparable to both the internucleon distance and the cross-sectional distance.

## Method
The authors developed an analytical framework within the Glauber approximation and AGK cutting rules to calculate the variance in the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions, incorporating nuclear two-body density distributions with correlation functions and a diffuse interaction profile for the nucleon-nucleon scattering probability. They derived expressions for the fluctuation parameter ω_N (variance over mean) for both proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions, using Gaussian parametrizations for both the correlation function and the scattering probability, and compared results with numerical studies using correlated many-body wave functions and excluded-volume Monte-Carlo approaches.

## Key numbers
- The mean number of nucleon-nucleon collisions for a proton traversing a heavy nucleus like 208Pb is ⟨N⟩ = 2σρ₀R ≈ 7 target nucleons.
- The nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross-section at CERN energies is σ = 32 mb.
- The nuclear matter density is ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³, giving an internucleon distance r₀ = (3/4πρ₀)^(1/3) ≈ 1.16 fm.
- The correlation length ξ derived from the Gaussian sum rule is ξ = r₀(2/9π)^(1/6) ≈ 0.75 fm.
- The slope parameter for the scattering amplitude at CERN energies (Elab ≈ 200 GeV) is r_σ ≈ 1.2 fm.
- For proton-nucleus collisions with Gaussian parametrizations (ξ=0.75 fm, r_σ=1.2 fm, σ=32 mb), the correlation parameter α = 0.25, giving ω_N = 1 - α = 0.75.
- For nucleus-nucleus collisions with the same parameters, α = β = 0.25, γ = 0.15, and ω_N = 0.65.
- Pieper's numerical calculations with correlated wave functions and a step-function interaction give α ≈ 0.31-0.33 for central collisions in both A=40 and A=224 systems.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier brief accounts in Baym et al. (1991) and Baym et al. (1991, QM91) by providing a detailed derivation of how nuclear correlations and cross-section fluctuations affect observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It addresses the discrepancy noted by Baym et al. (BFS) between the large fluctuations in transverse energy spectra observed by the NA34 collaboration and those expected from microscopic models with known nucleon-nucleon fluctuations. The results enable improved Monte-Carlo simulations of nucleus-nucleus collisions by showing that simple excluded-volume treatments of nucleon positions fail to capture the correlations characteristic of a self-bound nuclear liquid, and by providing the framework to implement both correlations and cross-section fluctuations correctly.
