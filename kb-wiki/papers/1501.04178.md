# Competition between Coulomb and Symmetry Potential in Semi-peripheral Heavy-ion Collisions

> The anisotropy of angular distributions of emitted nucleons and light charged particles for the asymmetric reaction system, $^{40}$Ar+$^{197}$Au, at b=6fm and $E_{beam}$=35, 50 and 100MeV/u, are investigated by using the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics model. The competition between the symmetry potential and Coulomb potential shows large impacts on the nucleons and light charged particles emission in projectile and target region. As a result of this competition, the angular distribution anisotropy of coalescence invariant Y(n)/Y(p) ratio at forward regions shows sensitivity to the stiffness of symmetry energy as well as the value of Y(n)/Y(p). This observable can be further checked against experimental data to understand the reaction mechanism and to extract information about the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities.

## Key claim

The anisotropy of the angular distribution of the coalescence invariant neutron-to-proton ratio (CI n/p ratio) in semi-peripheral collisions of the asymmetric system $^{40}$Ar+$^{197}$Au is sensitive to the stiffness of the symmetry energy, but this sensitivity is conditional on the competition between the Coulomb potential and the symmetry potential, which manifests differently depending on the beam energy and the angular region (projectile versus target).

## Method

The authors used the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (ImQMD05 code) to simulate semi-peripheral collisions (impact parameter b=6 fm) of $^{40}$Ar+$^{197}$Au at beam energies of 35, 50, and 100 MeV/u. They analyzed the time evolution of the symmetry potential and single-particle potentials in the overlapping (neck) region, and constructed the angular distributions of coalescence invariant (CI) neutron and proton yields by summing neutrons and protons from free nucleons and light clusters (n, p, d, t, $^{3}$He, $^{4}$He, $^{6}$He). Two forms of the symmetry energy were compared, corresponding to a soft (stiffness parameter $\gamma_i$=0.5) and a stiff ($\gamma_i$=2.0) density dependence.

## Key numbers

* For $^{40}$Ar+$^{197}$Au at b=6 fm and $E_{beam}$=35 MeV/u, the anisotropy ratio $R_{iso1}=R_{CIn/p}(20^\circ)/R_{CIn/p}(90^\circ)$ is $0.94\pm0.01$ for $\gamma_i$=0.5 and $1.14\pm0.01$ for $\gamma_i$=2.0.
* $R_{iso1}$ increases by approximately 20% when $\gamma_i$ varies from 0.5 (corresponding to a slope parameter $L$=51 MeV) to 1.5 ($L$=104 MeV) at $E_{beam}$=35 MeV/u.
* For $^{40}$Ar+$^{197}$Au at b=6 fm and $E_{beam}$=100 MeV/u, $R_{iso1}$ is $0.92\pm0.01$ for $\gamma_i$=0.5 and $1.13\pm0.01$ for $\gamma_i$=2.0.
* The CI n/p ratios at forward regions for $\gamma_i$=0.5 decrease with increasing beam energy, tending toward the initial N/Z value of the projectile (N/Z$_{proj}$=1.22) at 100 MeV/u.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of isospin transport and symmetry energy probes in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the analysis of isotopic composition of intermediate mass fragments by Filippo et al. and the study of light charged particle energy spectra in coincidence with fission fragments in $^{40}$Ar+$^{197}$Au at 35 MeV/u by Wang et al. (2014). The proposed observable, the anisotropy of the CI n/p ratio ($R_{iso1}$), is suggested as a new probe that is sensitive to the stiffness of the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities and is relatively insensitive to impact parameter variations from b=4 to 8 fm, making it suitable for experimental verification to further discriminate between different forms of the symmetry energy.
