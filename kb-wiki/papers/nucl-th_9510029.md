# Studies of valence-quark shadowing at HERA

> We propose to measure valence-quark shadowing by observing charged pion productions in electron-nucleus scattering at HERA. Studies of shadowing phenomena in the valence-quark distribution could be useful in discriminating among various models, which produce similar results in the $F_2$ shadowing.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring valence-quark shadowing via charged pion production in electron-nucleus scattering at HERA could discriminate between different shadowing models, because the models produce similar predictions for the structure function \(F_2\) shadowing but predict completely different behavior for valence-quark distributions at small \(x\): antishadowing in a parton-recombination model versus shadowing in an aligned-jet model.

## Method

The author compares two theoretical models for nuclear valence-quark distributions: a hybrid parton model combining \(Q^2\) rescaling with parton recombination, and an aligned-jet model based on vector-meson-dominance ideas. The valence-quark shadowing ratio \(R_V \equiv V_A(x)/V_D(x)\) is calculated for each model and contrasted with the \(F_2\) shadowing ratio. An experimental restriction on valence shadowing is estimated by extrapolating SLAC \(F_2^A/F_2^D\) data at medium \(x\) to small \(x\) using baryon-number conservation.

## Key numbers

- The parton model (model 1) predicts \(V_A(x)/V_N(x) \ne F_2^A(x)/F_2^N(x)\) at small \(x\), with antishadowing (ratio > 1) in the valence distribution.
- The aligned-jet model (model 2) predicts \(V_A(x)/V_N(x) \approx F_2^A(x)/F_2^N(x)\) at small \(x\), with shadowing (ratio < 1) in the valence distribution.
- The experimental restriction uses SLAC \(R_2^{Ca}\) data for \(x \ge 0.3\), assuming \(R_V = R_2\) in that region, and extrapolates to small \(x\) via baryon-number conservation \(\int dx V(x) = 3\).

## Context

This work extends studies of nuclear shadowing in the structure function \(F_2\) (Kumano, 1994) and previous suggestions that valence-quark distributions could test shadowing models (Kumano and Miyama, 1994). The paper contrasts two model classes: vector-meson-dominance-type models and parton-recombination-type models, which give similar \(F_2\) shadowing but opposite valence-quark behavior at small \(x\). The proposed measurement would enable discrimination between these models, which is not possible with existing \(F_2\) data alone.
