# Equation of state of spin-polarized nuclear matter in the relativistic Hartree-Fock method

> We calculate the equation of state (EOS) of spin-polarized nuclear matter in the relativistic Hartree-Fock method. To this end, we employ the relativistic point-coupling model, with which the Fock terms are considerably simplified, reducing them to the same form as the Hartree terms. In analogy to the slope parameter $L$ of the isospin-symmetry energy for spin-unpolarized matter, we evaluate the spin slope parameter $L_s$ of the corresponding spin-symmetry energy for spin-polarized matter. We find that the slope parameter $L$ and the spin slope parameter $L_s$ have a negative correlation in the case of isoscalar polarization, where neutrons and protons are spin-polarized in the same direction. On the other hand, the spin slope parameter is nearly independent of the slope parameter in the case of isovector polarization, where neutrons are spin-polarized along the opposite direction to protons. We show that these correlations are a natural consequence of the relativistic point coupling model which we employ.

## Key claim

The relativistic point-coupling model predicts a negative linear correlation between the slope parameter \(L\) and the spin slope parameter \(L_s\) for isoscalar spin polarization, while for isovector spin polarization \(L_s\) is nearly independent of \(L\); these correlations are a natural consequence of the Fierz transformation relations among the coupling constants in the relativistic framework.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic point-coupling model (PCF-PK1 parameter set) with the Hartree-Fock method, using the Fierz transformation to simplify the Fock exchange terms into the same form as the Hartree terms. They solved the Dirac equation numerically for spin-polarized symmetric nuclear matter by iteratively diagonalizing the 4x4 single-particle Hamiltonian, considering two polarization modes: isoscalar (neutrons and protons spin-polarized in the same direction) and isovector (neutrons and protons spin-polarized in opposite directions). To study correlations, they varied the slope parameter \(L\) by modifying the density dependence of the isovector-vector coupling constant \(\alpha_{tV}(\rho_B)\) while keeping its value at saturation density fixed.

## Key numbers

- The slope parameter for spin-unpolarized matter is \(L = 79.9\) MeV, consistent with the empirical value \(L = 58.7 \pm 28.1\) MeV (Oertel).
- The spin slope parameter for isoscalar polarization is \(L_s = 151.6\) MeV; for isovector polarization it is \(L_s = 73.1\) MeV.
- From the PREX measurement of the neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) (\(\Delta r_{np} = 0.283 \pm 0.071\) fm), the extracted spin slope parameter for isoscalar polarization is \(86\ \text{MeV} \lesssim L_s \lesssim 135\) MeV.
- From proton elastic scattering data (\(\Delta r_{np} = 0.211^{+0.054}_{-0.063}\) fm), the extracted spin slope parameter for isoscalar polarization is \(113\ \text{MeV} \lesssim L_s \lesssim 173\) MeV.
- The non-relativistic calculation by Khoa et al. (2022) gave \(L_s \simeq 96\) MeV for isovector polarization, which is larger than the relativistic result.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin-polarized nuclear matter, which were primarily performed in non-relativistic frameworks using Skyrme interactions (Margueron 2009, Chamel 2010) or the CDM3Yn interaction (Khoa 2020, 2022). The relativistic approach more naturally treats the spin degree of freedom and reveals that the Fock terms are essential for spin-polarized matter. The finding that the isoscalar spin slope parameter can be constrained through the neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) provides a new indirect probe of the equation of state for spin-polarized matter, which is relevant for understanding neutron star mergers with strong magnetic fields and the possibility of ferromagnetic phase transitions in nuclear matter.
