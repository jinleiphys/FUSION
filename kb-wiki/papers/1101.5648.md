# Light Cone Dynamics and EMC Effects in the Extraction of F_{2n} at Large Bjorken x

> We discuss theoretical issues related to the extraction of deep inelastic~(DIS) structure function of neutron from inclusive DIS scattering off the deuteron at large Bjorken x. Theoretical justification is given to the consideration of only $pn$ component of the deuteron wave function and consistency with both the baryonic number and light-cone momentum conservation sum rules. Next we discuss the EMC type effects and argue that in all cases relevant to the nuclear DIS reactions at large x the main issue is the medium modification of the properties of bound nucleon rather than the non-nucleonic components like pions. We give brief description of the color screening model of EMC and within this model we estimate uncertainties in the extraction of the neutron DIS structure function at large x. We emphasize also that these uncertainties are rather "model independent" since any theoretical framework accounting for the medium modification is proportional to the magnitude of the virtuality of bound nucleon which increases with an increase of x.

# Light Cone Dynamics and EMC Effects in the Extraction of F_{2n} at Large Bjorken x

## Key claim

The uncertainties in extracting the neutron deep inelastic scattering (DIS) structure function from inclusive deuteron data at large Bjorken x are "model independent" because any theoretical framework that accounts for medium modification of the bound nucleon is proportional to the magnitude of the nucleon's virtuality, which increases with x. The paper argues that the dominant issue in nuclear DIS at large x is medium modification of bound nucleon properties rather than non-nucleonic components such as pions.

## Method

The author develops a light-cone (LC) description of inclusive DIS scattering off the deuteron, using only the proton-neutron component of the deuteron wave function and verifying consistency with both baryon number and light-cone momentum conservation sum rules. The color screening model of the EMC effect is then applied, where the suppression of point-like configurations (PLC) in bound nucleons is calculated as a function of nucleon virtuality, and this suppression factor is incorporated into the LC convolution formula for the deuteron structure function. The neutron structure function F_{2n} is extracted by first modifying the proton structure function, smearing it by Fermi motion, subtracting from deuteron data, and then correcting back to reconstruct the "free" neutron structure function.

## Key numbers

- For Q^2 >= 5 GeV^2 and Bjorken x <= 1, non-nucleonic components can be safely neglected in the ground state wave function of the deuteron, based on the observation that the nucleonic component in isosinglet pn short-range correlation dominates until approximately 650 MeV/c relative momenta.
- The typical energy for nucleon excitations within the nucleus, Delta E_A, is taken as M* - M ~ 0.6 to 1 GeV.
- The suppression factor for PLC in a bound nucleon is given by delta_A(p^2) = 1 / [1 + (p^2 / M + 2 epsilon_A) / Delta E_A]^2.
- The PLC contribution in the nucleon wave function is assumed negligible at x <= 0.3 and dominant at x >= 0.5, with a simple linear fit used between these values.

## Context

This work extends the light-cone description of nuclear DIS developed by Frankfurt and Strikman (1981, 1988) and the color screening model of the EMC effect from Frankfurt and Strikman (1985, 1988). It addresses the need for proper treatment of nuclear effects in extracting neutron PDFs from deuteron data, a program that began with the SLAC experiments of Bodek (late 1970s) and continues with new semi-inclusive experiments such as BONUS at Jefferson Lab. The paper enables more reliable extraction of the neutron structure function F_{2n} at large x by quantifying the uncertainties introduced by medium modification effects, and it emphasizes the urgency of understanding the origin and extent of EMC effects relevant to large-x kinematics.
