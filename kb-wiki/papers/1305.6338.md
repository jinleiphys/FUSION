# Triangle anomaly in Weyl semi-metals

> Weyl semimetals possess massless chiral quasi-particles, and are thus affected by the triangle anomalies. We discuss the features of the chiral magnetic and chiral vortical effects specific to Weyl semimetals, and then propose three novel phenomena caused by the triangle anomalies in this material: 1) anomaly cooling; 2) charge transport by soliton waves as described by the Burgers' equation, and 3) the shift of the BKT phase transition of superfluid vortices coupled to Weyl fermions. In addition, we establish the conditions under which the chiral magnetic current exists in real materials.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in Weyl semimetals vanishes in global equilibrium when all Weyl points are filled to the same Fermi energy, contrary to some earlier claims; a net CME current requires that left- and right-handed sectors are filled to different energies, i.e., a nonzero chiral chemical potential that can evolve in time.

## Method

The authors use a kinetic theory approach incorporating Berry phase effects, treating both positive and negative energy branches of the quasiparticle dispersion as physical electrons (without subtracting a "Dirac sea" vacuum contribution). They derive equations of motion for quasiparticles near each Weyl point, compute the anomaly-induced current contributions from both branches, and sum them from the bottom of the filled band to the high-energy cutoff. The same formalism is extended to the chiral vortical effect by including rotation as a Coriolis force.

## Key numbers

* The chiral magnetic current from a single Weyl point at zero temperature is given by **j_i = B (k_i / 4π²) (ε_F - E_0)**, where ε_F is the Fermi energy and E_0 is the bottom of the filled sea.
* The net chiral magnetic current in global equilibrium vanishes because **Σ_i k_i = 0**.
* The chiral vortical current at zero temperature for a single Weyl point is **j_i = ω (k_i / 4π²) (ε_F - E_0)(ε_F + E_0 - 2E_i)**.
* The net chiral vortical current after summing over all Weyl points is **j_total = -ω (Σ_i k_i E_i / 2π²) (ε_F - E_0)**, which may be nonzero even in equilibrium.
* The anomaly cooling power is given by **dE/dt = (e ω E / 4π²) Σ_i k_i (μ_i² + π²T²/3)**, where the T² term drops out because Σ_i k_i = 0.
* The Burgers' equation for charge transport has coefficient **C = (k e ω) / (2π² χ²)**, where χ is the charge susceptibility.

## Context

This work extends the study of triangle anomaly-induced transport phenomena, previously developed for quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions by Kharzeev (2007), Fukushima (2008), and Son and Surowka (2009), to the condensed matter setting of Weyl semimetals. The paper clarifies conditions for the existence of the chiral magnetic current, resolving a tension with earlier claims by Vilenkin (1979) and by Elia and Leonid regarding conductors with mirror isomer symmetry. The proposed phenomena of anomaly cooling, soliton charge transport via the Burgers' equation, and shifts in the Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless (BKT) phase transition open avenues for experimental tests of anomaly-induced transport in controlled laboratory settings, potentially enabling "chiral electronics" as discussed by Kharzeev (2012).
