# Low-lying odd-parity nucleon resonances as quark-model like states

> Recent lattice QCD results for the low-lying odd-parity excitations of the nucleon near the $N^{*}(1535)$ and $N^{*}(1650)$ resonance positions have revealed that the lattice QCD states have magnetic moments consistent with predictions from a constituent-quark-model. Using Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory (HEFT) to describe pion-nucleon scattering in the $I(J^{P}) = \frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{2}^{-})$ channel, we represent these two quark-model like states as two single-particle bare basis states, dressed and mixed by meson-baryon scattering channels. By constraining the free parameters of the Hamiltonian with $S_{11}$ pion-nucleon scattering data, we perform the first calculation of the finite-volume spectrum using two bare-baryon basis states. By comparing this spectrum to contemporary lattice QCD results at three lattice volumes, we analyse the eigenvectors of the Hamiltonian to gain insight into the structure and composition of these two low-lying resonances. We find that an interpretation of the two low-lying nucleon resonances as quark-model like states dressed by meson-baryon interactions is consistent with both the $S_{11}$ scattering data and lattice QCD. We introduce a novel HEFT formalism for estimating scattering-state contaminations in lattice QCD correlation functions constructed with standard three-quark operators. Not only are historical lattice QCD results described with excellent accuracy, but correlation functions with large scattering-state contaminations are identified.

## Key claim

The two low-lying odd-parity nucleon resonances, the N*(1535) and N*(1650), can be interpreted as quark-model-like three-quark states (bare basis states) dressed and mixed by meson-baryon interactions, provided the Hamiltonian includes two bare-baryon basis states rather than one; this interpretation is consistent with both S11 scattering data and lattice QCD results at multiple volumes.

## Method

The authors constructed a Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory (HEFT) model with two single-particle bare basis states representing the three-quark cores, coupled to πN, ηN, and KΛ scattering channels via vertex interactions and direct channel-channel couplings. They constrained the 21 free parameters of the Hamiltonian by fitting the calculated S11 πN scattering phase shifts and inelasticities to the SAID WI08 solution up to 1.75 GeV, then solved the finite-volume eigenvalue equation to predict energy spectra at lattice volumes of approximately 2, 3, and 4 fm for comparison with lattice QCD results. The pion mass dependence of the bare-state masses was determined by fitting to lattice QCD data at three heavy pion masses at L ~ 3 fm, and the eigenvectors of the Hamiltonian were analyzed to reveal the composition of each finite-volume eigenstate.

## Key numbers

- Pole positions from HEFT: E1 = 1500 - 50i MeV and E2 = 1658 - 56i MeV, compared to PDG values of 1510 ± 10 - (65 ± 10)i MeV and 1655 ± 15 - (67 ± 18)i MeV respectively.
- Bare state masses at the physical point: m_N1^(0) = 1.6301 GeV and m_N2^(0) = 1.8612 GeV.
- Bare mass slopes from the 3 fm fitting: α_N1 = 0.944 GeV^{-1} and α_N2 = 0.611 GeV^{-1}.
- χ²/d.o.f. for the scattering fit: 10.6 (χ² = 604 with 57 degrees of freedom).
- Regulator parameters for the two bare states: Λ_πN^(N1) = 1.2335 GeV, Λ_πN^(N2) = 1.4000 GeV; Λ_ηN^(N1) = 1.2642 GeV, Λ_ηN^(N2) = 0.9521 GeV; Λ_KΛ^(N2) = 0.7283 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous HEFT studies that were limited to a single bare basis state (Hall 2013, Wu 2014, Liu 2015, Liu 2016) and provides the first quantitative analysis of a baryon system with two nearby single-particle basis states. It builds on lattice QCD results from Stokes (2019) showing that the magnetic moments of odd-parity nucleon states resemble constituent-quark-model predictions, and on earlier debates about whether these resonances are dynamically generated (Bruns 2010, Bruns 2019) or primarily three-quark states dressed by meson-baryon interactions (Liu 2015). The novel HEFT formalism for estimating scattering-state contaminations in lattice QCD correlation functions enables identification of correlation functions with large contaminations, and the method can be applied to future lattice QCD studies of baryon resonances.
