# $η(1295)\to3π$ decays

> The radially excited pseudoscalar state $η(1295)$ is still poorly understood [as well as its $SU(3)$ partners $π(1300)$ and $K(1460 )$] and we want to attract attention experimenters to its comprehensive study. As for the main three-body decay $η(1295)\to ηππ$, here the main interest is the measurements of the shapes of the $ηπ$ and $ππ$ mass spectra in which the contributions from the $a_0(980)$ resonance and the large $ππ$ $S$ wave ($σ$) should manifest themselves. To describe these mass spectra, we propose to use a simple isobar model with a single fitting parameter characterizing the relative intensity of the $a_0 (980)$ and $σ$ state production. Our main goal is to discuss the dynamics of the isospin-breaking decays $η(1295)\toπ^+ π^-π^0$ and $η(1295)\to3π^0$, whose experimental studies could continue the impressive story of isospin violations in the decays of light isoscalar mesons $ω\to2π$, $η\to3 π$, $η'\to3π$, $η(1405)\to3π$, and $f_1(1285)\to3π$. We estimate the widths of the isospin-breaking decays $η(1295)\to 3π$ produced via the mixing of the $π^0-η$ and $a^0_0(980) -f_0(980)$ states and also due to the $π^0(1300)-η(1295)$ mixing. Processes that have the potential for detecting $η(1295) \to3π$ decays are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors estimate that the isospin-breaking decay width of η(1295)→3π is of the order of 0.1 MeV, corresponding to a branching fraction of about 0.2%, but caution that these are rough estimates based on a simple isobar model with minimal free parameters, and that the applicability of chiral perturbation theory to this decay is not obvious.

## Method

The authors construct a simple isobar model for the main decay η(1295)→ηππ, describing the amplitude as a coherent sum of contributions from intermediate a0(980)π and ησ states, with a single fitting parameter for their relative intensity. They then estimate the widths of the direct isospin-breaking decays η(1295)→π⁺π⁻π⁰ and η(1295)→3π⁰ by incorporating π⁰-η mixing and a⁰₀(980)-f₀(980) mixing mechanisms into the same model. Additionally, they estimate the contribution from π⁰(1300)-η(1295) mixing by assuming a mixing strength comparable to ρ⁰-ω mixing and using the known decay width of π(1300)→3π.

## Key numbers

* The mass and total width of η(1295) are 1294±4 MeV and 55±5 MeV, respectively (Particle Data Group).
* The estimated width for the direct decay η(1295)→π⁰π⁺π⁻ is approximately 0.027 MeV.
* The estimated width for the direct decay η(1295)→3π⁰ is approximately 0.031 MeV.
* The estimated width for η(1295)→3π via π⁰(1300)-η(1295) mixing is approximately 0.023 MeV, using ReΠ_{π̂⁰η̂} ≈ 0.0034 GeV² and Γ_{π̂→3π} ≈ 300 MeV.
* The total η(1295)→3π decay width is expected to be of the order of 0.1 MeV, giving a branching fraction B(η(1295)→3π) ≈ 0.2%.
* The π⁰-η mixing parameter Π_{π⁰η} is taken as -0.004 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the study of isospin violations in light isoscalar meson decays, following the impressive history of such effects in ω→2π, η→3π, η′→3π, η(1405)→3π, and f₁(1285)→3π decays. The authors note that a hint of η(1295)→3π was obtained by the BESIII Collaboration (2012) in studies of J/ψ→γ3π, but it remained unclear whether η(1295) or f₁(1285) caused the excess. The paper provides theoretical estimates to guide future high-statistics experiments, particularly in J/ψ radiative decays and D⁺ semileptonic decays, where interference between η(1295) and π(1300) resonances could be observed.
