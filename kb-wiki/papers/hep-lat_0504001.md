# Nucleon and Delta masses in twisted mass chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate the masses of the nucleons and deltas in twisted mass heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. We work to quadratic order in a power counting scheme in which we treat the lattice spacing and the quark masses to be of the same order. We give expressions for the mass and the mass splitting of the nucleons and deltas both in and away from the isospin limit. We give an argument using the chiral Lagrangian treatment that, in the strong isospin limit, the nucleons remain degenerate and the delta multiplet breaks into two degenerate pairs to all orders in chiral perturbation theory. We show that the mass splitting between the degenerate pairs of the deltas first appears at quadratic order in in the lattice spacing. We discuss the subtleties in the effective chiral theory that arise from the inclusion of isospin breaking.

## Key claim

In twisted mass lattice QCD with mass-degenerate quarks, the nucleons remain degenerate and the delta multiplet breaks into two degenerate pairs to all orders in chiral perturbation theory, with the mass splitting between the degenerate pairs first appearing at quadratic order in the lattice spacing.

## Method

The authors construct a twisted mass heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (tmHBχPT) for SU(2) flavor by extending the Symanzik effective continuum Lagrangian to include discretization effects from the twisted mass lattice action. They work to quadratic order in a power counting scheme where the lattice spacing a, quark masses m_q, and mass splitting ε_q are all treated as order ε^2, and compute the nucleon and delta masses including O(a^2) operators and one-loop contributions.

## Key numbers

- The power counting scheme treats aΛ_QCD ∼ m_q/Λ_QCD ∼ ε_q/Λ_QCD ∼ ε^2, with ε^2 denoting small dimensionless expansion parameters
- The pion decay constant is normalized so that f_π = 132 MeV
- The leading order pion mass-squared is M' = √((m̂+â)^2 + μ̂^2), where m̂, â, and μ̂ are the shifted mass, lattice spacing, and twisted mass spurions respectively
- The nucleon-delta mass splitting Δ is treated as Δ ∼ m_π ∼ ε^2 in the power counting
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ_χ is defined as Λ_χ ≡ 4πf

## Context

This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory treatments of baryons on the lattice, which had been done for untwisted Wilson fermions to O(a) by Beane and Savage (2003) and to O(a^2) by Tiburzi (2005). It also builds on the formulation of twisted mass chiral perturbation theory for mesons by Munster (2004), Scorzato (2004), and Sharpe and Wu (2004). The results enable analytic study of baryonic quantities that do not involve quark-disconnected diagrams in twisted mass lattice QCD simulations, complementing the mesonic sector where many quantities are difficult to compute numerically.
