# Homogeneous isotropization and equilibration of a strongly coupled plasma with a critical point

> We use holography to investigate the process of homogeneous isotropization and thermalization in a strongly coupled $\mathcal{N} = 4$ Super Yang-Mills plasma charged under a $U(1)$ subgroup of the global $SU(4)$ R-symmetry which features a critical point in its phase diagram. Isotropization dynamics at late times is affected by the critical point in agreement with the behavior of the characteristic relaxation time extracted from the analysis of the lowest non-hydrodynamic quasinormal mode in the $SO(3)$ quintuplet (external scalar) channel of the theory. In particular, the isotropization time may decrease or increase as the chemical potential increases depending on whether one is far or close enough to the critical point, respectively. On the other hand, the thermalization time associated with the equilibration of the scalar condensate, which happens only after the system has relaxed to a (nearly) isotropic state, is found to always increase with chemical potential in agreement with the characteristic relaxation time associated to the lowest non-hydrodynamic quasinormal mode in the $SO(3)$ singlet (dilaton) channel. These conclusions about the late dynamics of the system are robust in the sense that they hold for different initial conditions seeding the time evolution of the far-from-equilibrium plasma.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a strongly coupled plasma with a critical point, the isotropization time can either decrease or increase with chemical potential depending on whether the system is far from or close to the critical point, while the thermalization time (equilibration of the scalar condensate) always increases with chemical potential; these conclusions are robust for different initial conditions.

## Method

The authors used the characteristic formulation of general relativity in asymptotically AdS spacetimes to solve the coupled nonlinear partial differential equations of the 1RCBH model, a top-down holographic construction describing a strongly coupled N=4 Super Yang-Mills plasma charged under a U(1) subgroup of the SU(4) R-symmetry. They evolved time-dependent, spatially homogeneous anisotropic metric and matter field configurations from far-from-equilibrium initial states until thermodynamic equilibrium, extracting the pressure anisotropy and scalar condensate as functions of time. The late-time dynamics was matched to the lowest non-hydrodynamic quasinormal modes in the SO(3) quintuplet (external scalar) and SO(3) singlet (dilaton) channels.

## Key numbers

- The isotropization time, defined by the decay of the pressure anisotropy, was found to decrease with increasing chemical potential far from the critical point and increase with chemical potential close to the critical point, in agreement with the behavior of the lowest non-hydrodynamic quasinormal mode in the external scalar channel.
- The thermalization time, associated with the equilibration of the scalar condensate, always increased with chemical potential, consistent with the lowest non-hydrodynamic quasinormal mode in the dilaton channel.
- The model features a critical point in its phase diagram, with the chemical potential and temperature at the critical point determined by the thermodynamics of the 1RCBH solution.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio for the model is 1/(4 pi), a universal value for a broad class of strongly coupled systems.

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of homogeneous equilibration in charged plasmas without a critical point, such as Fuini (2015), by introducing a dilaton field that generates a critical point in the phase diagram. It also builds on the quasinormal mode analysis of the 1RCBH model by Finazzo (2016), which found that QNM oscillations in the external scalar channel are damped by increasing chemical potential away from criticality but enhanced near the critical point. The results enable future comparisons with phenomenological bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton models that quantitatively describe QCD thermodynamics, such as those of Rougemont (2015) and Critelli (2017), to test whether the critical point effects on far-from-equilibrium dynamics are universal across strongly coupled systems.
