# Pseudoscalar bosonic excitations in the color-flavor locked phase at moderate densities

> The properties of pseudoscalar bosonic excitations in the color-flavor locked phase at moderate densities are studied within a model of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio type. Our previous analysis is extended to Goldstone bosons with hidden flavor and to higher-lying modes which stay massive in the chiral limit. The bosons are constructed explicitly by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation for quark-quark scattering in random phase approximation. The masses and weak decay constants of the Goldstone bosons are found in good agreement with predictions from the low-energy effective theory. In the non-Goldstone sector we find an SU(3) octet which is weakly bound, while the singlet appears to be unbound.

## Key claim

The paper extends the authors' previous NJL-model analysis of pseudoscalar bosonic excitations in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase to hidden-flavor Goldstone bosons and higher-lying modes, finding that the low-energy effective theory (LEET) predictions hold at moderate densities only when the NJL-model values for decay constants and the coefficient A are used, with quantitative deviations from the weak-coupling limit of QCD.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for quark-quark scattering in the random phase approximation (RPA) within a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type model at zero temperature and fixed quark number chemical potential μ=500 MeV, using a three-momentum cutoff Λ=600 MeV and diquark coupling H=1.4 Λ⁻². The 36×36 T-matrix in Nambu-Gorkov space is block-diagonalized into 2×2 blocks for open-flavor mesons and a 6×6 block for hidden-flavor mesons (π⁰, η₈, η₀), from which masses and weak decay constants are extracted.

## Key numbers

- Gap parameter Δ = 79.1 MeV in the chiral limit for the standard parameters (μ=500 MeV, Λ=600 MeV, H=1.4 Λ⁻²)
- Weak decay constants in the chiral limit: f_π and f_η' approach the weak-coupling limits f_π² = (21-8 ln(2))/18 × (μ²/(2π²)) and f_η'² = 3/4 × (μ²/(2π²)) as Δ→0
- For equal quark masses m_q, octet Goldstone boson masses grow linearly as m_π = √(8A/f_π²) × m_q and singlet as m_η' = √(8A/f_η'²) × m_q
- Higher-lying octet mode masses in the chiral limit vary between 189 and 223 MeV for μ between 360 and 500 MeV (with parameters Λ=602.3 MeV, H=1.73925 Λ⁻²)
- Higher-lying octet modes become unbound at m_q ≈ 190 MeV for equal quark masses
- For m_u=m_d=30 MeV, higher-lying pion and η poles stay near 155 MeV, while kaon poles range from 136 to 177 MeV as m_s varies from 30 to 140 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Kleinhaus 2007) from open-flavor Goldstone bosons to the hidden-flavor sector (π⁰, η, η') and to higher-lying pseudoscalar modes, which were studied only in the chiral limit by Ebert (2007). The results are consistent with LEET predictions by Son (1999), Bedaque (2001), and Schafer (2000, 2001), but show quantitative differences from the weak-coupling limit of QCD at moderate densities. The findings enable further studies of meson condensation (particularly kaon condensation) in the CFL phase and its implications for compact star physics.
