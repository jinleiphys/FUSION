# P and T odd effects in deuteron in the Reid potential

> The $P$ and $T$ odd deuteron multipoles are calculated in the Reid nucleon-nucleon potential in the chiral limit $m_π\to 0$. The contact current generated by the $π$-meson exchange does not contribute to the anapole moment. The contact current generated by the vector meson exchange is negligible in comparison with other contributions of vector mesons. The result for the deuteron electric dipole moment is of great interest because of the experiment on its measurement discussed in Brookhaven. The deuteron photodisintegration cross section asymmetry at the threshold is also calculated. It is shown that its value strongly depends on the tensor forces and d-wave contribution to the deuteron wave function.

# P and T odd effects in deuteron in the Reid potential

## Key claim
The deuteron anapole moment, electric dipole moment, magnetic quadrupole moment, and photodisintegration cross section asymmetry are calculated using the Reid nucleon-nucleon potential in the chiral limit where the pion mass goes to zero. The results show that the d-wave contribution to the deuteron wave function strongly influences the calculated values, and that vector meson exchange contributions are small compared to pion exchange contributions but depend sensitively on the short-distance behavior of the wave function.

## Method
The deuteron wave functions for the 3S1-3D1 state were obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with the soft-core Reid potential. Perturbed wave functions in the 3P1 and 1P1 channels were computed by solving coupled differential equations with weak parity-nonconserving (PNC) nucleon-nucleon potentials from pion exchange and rho/omega meson exchange, using the DDH parameter set. The anapole moment, electric dipole moment, magnetic quadrupole moment, and photodisintegration asymmetry were then evaluated from integrals of these wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: epsilon = 2.23 MeV; d-wave probability: P_d = 6.5% (from Reid potential)
- Deuteron anapole moment (total): a_d = -(e/(6 m m_pi)) (20.14 g_bar + 7.5 h^1_rho - 0.132 h^1_omega - 0.074 h^0_rho + 1.78 h^0_omega) I
- Deuteron electric dipole moment: d = -(e g_1)/(12 pi m_pi) * 5.3 I
- Deuteron magnetic quadrupole moment: M_zz = -(e/(12 pi m m_pi)) (11.5 g_0 + 19.8 g_1)
- Photodisintegration asymmetry at threshold (with d-wave): A = 0.16 x 10^{-7}; without d-wave: A = 0.74 x 10^{-7}
- P-odd electron-deuteron interaction constant: C_2d = 0.0215 +/- 0.0035
- Experimental asymmetry from Leningrad group: A = (1.8 +/- 1.8) x 10^{-7}

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of deuteron anapole moments by Khriplovich and Korkin (zero-range approximation) and by Dmitriev et al. (using Argonne v18 potential) to the Reid potential, and provides independent calculations of the electric dipole moment and magnetic quadrupole moment relevant to the Brookhaven experiment. The photodisintegration asymmetry results are compared with calculations using Argonne v18 (A = 0.253 x 10^{-7}) and Paris potential (A = 0.335 x 10^{-7}), showing that the strong dependence on d-wave content (6.5% in Reid vs 5.7% in Argonne v18) explains much of the discrepancy between models. The work enables improved interpretation of planned deuteron EDM experiments and highlights that vector meson exchange contributions cannot be reliably calculated due to their sensitivity to wave function behavior at distances below 0.3 fm.
