# The role of five-quark components in gamma decay of the $Δ(1232)$

> An admixture of 10-20 % of qqqq\bar q components in the Delta(1232) resonance is shown to reduce the well known underprediction for the decay width for Delta(1232)->N gamma decay by about half and that of the corresponding helicity amplitudes from a factor ~ 1.7 to ~ 1.5. The main effect is due to the quark-antiquark annihilation transitions qqqq\bar q -> qqq gamma, the consideration of which brings the ratio A_{3/2}/A_{1/2} and consequently the E2/M1 ratio R_{EM} into agreement with the empirical value. Transitions between qqqq\bar q components in the resonance and the nucleon qqqq\bar q->qqqq\bar q gamma are shown to enhance the calculated width by only a few percent, as long as the probability of the qqqq\bar q component of the Delta(1232) and the proton is at most ~ 20 %. The transitions qqqq\bar q->qqqq\bar q gamma between the qqqq\bar q components in the Delta(1232) and the proton do not lead to a nonzero value for R_{EM}.

# The role of five-quark components in gamma decay of the Δ(1232)

## Key claim

An admixture of 10–20% five-quark (qqqqq̄) components in the Δ(1232) resonance reduces the underprediction of the Δ(1232) → Nγ decay width by about half and the helicity amplitude discrepancy from a factor ~1.7 to ~1.5, but does not fully remove the underprediction; the main improvement comes from quark-antiquark annihilation transitions (qqqqq̄ → qqqγ), while transitions between five-quark components in the resonance and nucleon enhance the width by only a few percent.

## Method

The authors extend the conventional three-quark (qqq) constituent quark model by including P-wave qqqqq̄ configurations in both the proton and Δ(1232) wave functions, using harmonic oscillator spatial wave functions with oscillator parameters set by the proton radius (ω₃ ≈ 225 MeV). They calculate helicity amplitudes A₃/₂ and A₁/₂ for Δ⁺ → pγ decay from three types of transitions: direct transitions between qqqqq̄ components in the Δ and proton, direct qq̄ annihilation transitions (qqqqq̄ → qqqγ), and confinement-triggered annihilation transitions modeled with both linear and harmonic confining potentials.

## Key numbers

- The empirical helicity amplitudes are A₁/₂ = −0.135 ± 0.006/√GeV and A₃/₂ = −0.255 ± 0.008/√GeV (Particle Data Group values)
- The pure three-quark model (constituent quark mass 340 MeV) gives A₁/₂⁽³q⁾ = −0.083/√GeV and A₃/₂⁽³q⁾ = −0.143/√GeV, smaller by factors 1.6 and 1.8 respectively
- With 10% qqqqq̄ probability in both proton and Δ(1232) (equal J=1 and J=2 proportions), direct annihilation transitions enhance A₁/₂ by factor 1.01 and A₃/₂ by factor 1.08; combined with qqqqq̄ → qqqqq̄ transitions, the net enhancement factors are 1.12 and 1.18, yielding A₁/₂ = −0.093/√GeV and A₃/₂ = −0.171/√GeV
- With 10% qqqqq̄ in the proton and 20% in the Δ(1232), the calculated helicity amplitudes become A₁/₂ = −0.096/√GeV and A₃/₂ = −0.180/√GeV, giving a ratio A₃/₂/A₁/₂ = 1.88 (empirical value 1.89) and an enhancement of the calculated decay width by factor 1.5
- The photon momentum in the Δ(1232) center-of-mass frame is kγ = 259 MeV
- The string tension for the linear confining potential is taken as c = 280 MeV/fm

## Context

This work extends the valence three-quark quark model, which typically underpredicts Δ(1232) electromagnetic decay widths by more than a factor of two (Bruno et al., Melde et al.), by incorporating explicit sea-quark configurations motivated by experimental evidence for sea quarks in the proton (Towell et al., NA51, NMC, HERMES). The approach parallels a previous study of five-quark components in pion decay of the Δ(1232) (Li and Riska, 2004) and is consistent with coupled-channel N-Δ-π treatments (Sato et al.) that attribute ~30% of the N-Δ transition magnetic moment to pion cloud effects. The results show that five-quark admixtures bring the helicity amplitude ratio into agreement with the large-Nc QCD expectation A₃/₂/A₁/₂ = √3 + O(1/Nc²), but additional mechanisms beyond direct annihilation and confinement-triggered transitions are needed to fully resolve the underprediction of the absolute helicity amplitudes.
