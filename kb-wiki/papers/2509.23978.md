# Decoupling hydrodynamization from thermalization via nonlinear Boltzmann equation

> The early thermalization puzzle arises from the unexpectedly early applicability of hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions. While hydrodynamics has traditionally been associated with the onset of local thermal equilibrium, its derivations -- whether microscopic or macroscopic -- rely instead on linearization around equilibrium. However, the linearization timescale -- the time at which a system's evolution begins to follow a linearized equation -- has not been systematically investigated. In this work, we employ the spectral nonlinear Boltzmann equation -- the lowest-order truncation of the spectral Bogoliubov--Born--Green--Kirkwood--Yvon (BBGKY) hierarchy -- to analyze the timescales of linearization and thermalization under three distinct truncation schemes. The first two truncations allow for analytic treatment via recursive spectral equations, while the third requires numerical methods for generic initial conditions. The analysis is performed for a homogeneous, massless system with a constant differential cross section. For this simplified setup, we find a robust separation: the linearization time is consistently about half the thermalization time ($τ_{\mathrm{lin}}/τ_{\mathrm{therm}} \approx 1/2$). This separation of timescales suggests an explanation for the early applicability of hydrodynamics and points toward a possible quantitative resolution of the early thermalization puzzle.

## Key claim

For a homogeneous, massless system with constant differential cross section, the linearization time (when evolution begins to follow a linearized equation) is consistently about half the thermalization time, with a ratio tau_lin/tau_therm approximately 1/2, suggesting a quantitative resolution of the early thermalization puzzle in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors employ the spectral nonlinear Boltzmann equation, the lowest-order truncation of the spectral Bogoliubov-Born-Green-Kirkwood-Yvon (BBGKY) hierarchy, to evolve distribution functions with full nonlinear collision kernels. They analyze three distinct truncation schemes: isotropic (ell_max=0), axisymmetric angular-only (n_max=0 and m=0), and a fully coupled numerical treatment with sufficiently large truncation parameters. The first two truncations allow analytic treatment via recursive spectral equations, while the third requires numerical methods for generic initial conditions, all applied to a spatially homogeneous, massless system with a momentum-independent isotropic cross section.

## Key numbers

- Linearization time to thermalization time ratio: tau_lin/tau_therm approximately 1/2, found robustly across different truncation schemes
- For isotropic distributions, the relaxation timescale tau is defined as pi^3/(T^3 * sigma_0), where T is the equilibrium temperature and sigma_0 is the constant cross section
- For the isotropic truncation, the longest relaxation time for nonconserved modes is 3*tau (for the n=2 mode), with relaxation times decreasing as (n+1)/(n-1)*tau for higher n
- The lowest nontrivial mode f_2(t) obeys exponential decay with characteristic time 3*tau: f_2(t) = c_2 * exp(-t/(3*tau))
- The matching time parameter t_match is chosen such that t_match is greater than t_lin

## Context

This work extends the spectral BBGKY framework recently developed by the authors in Lu (2025) to analyze the conditions for hydrodynamic behavior emergence. It addresses the early thermalization puzzle identified by Heinz (2002) and Shuryak (2003), where hydrodynamic simulations require validity beginning around 0.6-1 fm/c after collision, significantly shorter than conventional mean-free-path estimates. The separation of linearization and thermalization timescales provides a microscopic foundation for anisotropic hydrodynamics approaches developed by Martinez (2010, 2012) and others, and suggests that the applicability of hydrodynamics requires only linearization around equilibrium rather than full thermalization.
