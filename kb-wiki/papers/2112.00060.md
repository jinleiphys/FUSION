# Physics with CEBAF at 12 GeV and Future Opportunities

> We summarize the ongoing scientific program of the 12 GeV Continuous Electron Beam Accelerator Facility (CEBAF) and give an outlook into future scientific opportunities. The program addresses important topics in nuclear, hadronic, and electroweak physics including nuclear femtography, meson and baryon spectroscopy, quarks and gluons in nuclei, precision tests of the standard model, and dark sector searches. Potential upgrades of CEBAF are considered, such as higher luminosity, polarized and unpolarized positron beams, and doubling the beam energy.

## Key claim

The paper summarizes the ongoing scientific program of the 12 GeV CEBAF facility and outlines future opportunities, but the specific upgrades discussed (higher luminosity, positron beams, and energy doubling to 20-24 GeV) are presented as potential developments that require significant additional engineering, design, and research before realization, with no firm timeline for completion.

## Method

The paper reviews the experimental equipment and programs across all four CEBAF experimental halls (A, B, C, D), describing the spectrometers, detectors, and targets available for nuclear, hadronic, and electroweak physics. It then presents conceptual designs for two major upgrades: a positron beam capability using the PEPPo technique of polarization transfer through electromagnetic showers in a high-Z target, and an energy upgrade to 20-24 GeV by replacing the highest-energy arcs with Fixed Field Alternating Gradient (FFA) arcs that can simultaneously transport multiple beam passes.

## Key numbers

- Hall B's CLAS12 detector achieves momentum resolutions of less than 1% in the forward direction and less than 3% in the central direction, operating at a luminosity of 10^35 cm^-2 s^-1
- Hall C spectrometers (HMS and SHMS) reach momenta of 11 GeV/c and 6+ GeV/c respectively, with momentum reconstruction resolution and accuracy at the 0.1% level, operating at luminosities of 10^38 to 10^39 cm^-2 s^-1
- The PEPPo technique demonstrated polarization transfer from an 8.2 MeV/c electron beam to positrons with polarization approaching approximately 85%
- A 1 mA polarized electron beam would produce a 66 nA positron beam with polarization larger than 66% for acceleration in CEBAF to energies as high as 12 GeV
- The GlueX experiment in Hall D runs at a post-collimator photon flux of approximately 50 MHz, with a DAQ event rate of 80 kHz and data recording at about 1 GB/s
- The proposed energy upgrade would increase CEBAF energy from 12 GeV to 20-24 GeV by adding FFA arcs supporting 6-7 additional passes, with the FFA arc cell designed for 10-22 GeV beams confined to a region of -5 mm to +4 mm

## Context

This paper extends the planning documents for the 12 GeV CEBAF program that began operations in 2018, summarizing the current capabilities described in earlier Jefferson Lab design reports. It enables future experimental programs including the JEF experiment requiring an upgraded forward calorimeter, the GDH experiment requiring a polarized target, and the KLF experiment requiring beamline modifications in Hall D, as well as the conditionally approved DVCS experiments using positron beams. The FFA technology discussion builds directly on the successful operation of the EMMA accelerator at Daresbury Laboratory and the Cornell-BNL CBETA accelerator, which demonstrated eight-pass beam recirculation with energy recovery.
