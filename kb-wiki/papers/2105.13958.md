# Effects of Nuclear Equation of State on Type-I X-ray Bursts: Interpretation of the X-ray Bursts from GS 1826-24

> Type I X-ray bursts are thermonuclear explosions on the neutron star (NS) surface caused by mass accretion from a companion star. Observations of X-ray bursts provide valuable information on X-ray binary systems, e.g., binary parameters, the chemical composition of accreted matter, and the nuclear equation of state (EOS) of NSs. There have been several theoretical studies to constrain the physics of X-ray bursters. However, they have mainly focused on the burning layers above the solid crust of the NS, which brings up issues of the treatment of NS gravitation and internal energy. In this study, focusing on the microphysics inside NSs, we calculate a series of X-ray bursts using a general-relativistic stellar-evolution code with several NS EOSs. We compare the X-ray-burst models with the burst parameters of a clocked burster associated with GS 1826-24. We find a monotonic correlation between the NS radius and the light-curve profile. A larger radius shows a higher recurrence time and a large peak luminosity. In contrast, the dependence of light curves on the NS mass becomes more complicated, where the neutrino cooling suppresses the efficiency of nuclear ignition. We also constrain the EOS and mass of GS 1826-24, i.e., stiffer EOSs, corresponding to larger NS radii, are not preferred due to too-high peak luminosity. The EOS and the cooling and heating of NSs are important to discuss the theoretical and observational properties of X-ray bursts.

## Key claim

The authors find a monotonic correlation between neutron star radius and X-ray burst light-curve profile, but caution that the dependence on neutron star mass is more complicated because neutrino cooling suppresses nuclear ignition efficiency, meaning that the simple one-zone shell-flash model based solely on surface gravity is insufficient to explain multizone burst simulations.

## Method

The authors performed one-dimensional general-relativistic stellar-evolution calculations covering the entire neutron star from core to atmosphere, using four finite-temperature nuclear equations of state (Togashi, LS220, TM1, and TM1e). They evolved accreting neutron star models without nuclear burning to a steady thermal state over 10^4-10^5 years, then computed X-ray burst sequences with an approximate 88-nuclei reaction network, comparing the resulting light-curve parameters to observations of the clocked burster GS 1826-24.

## Key numbers

- For GS 1826-24, the mass accretion rate is constrained to 0.084-0.170 M_Edd, where M_Edd = 1.75 x 10^-8 M_sun yr^-1 (from prior work by Johnston et al. 2020)
- The net base heat including neutrino losses Q_{b+nu} ranges from 0.54 MeV u^-1 (for 2.0 M_sun with Togashi EOS) to 0.73 MeV u^-1 (for 1.1 M_sun with TM1 EOS)
- The maximum neutron star mass constraint used is M_NS = 2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} M_sun from PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie et al. 2020)
- The radius constraint from GW170817 is R_{1.4} < 13.6 km, where R_{1.4} is the radius of a 1.4 M_sun neutron star

## Context

This work extends prior X-ray burst modeling by Johnston et al. (2020) and Meisel et al. (2018) that used codes like KEPLER and MESA covering only the accreted layer above the crust, by instead solving the full general-relativistic stellar structure including the neutron star interior. The approach enables self-consistent treatment of crustal heating (following Haensel & Zdunik 1990) and neutrino cooling (modified Urca and bremsstrahlung processes) without an adjustable boundary parameter Q_b. The results enable new constraints on the nuclear equation of state from clocked burster observations, complementing constraints from NICER (Miller et al. 2019) and gravitational-wave events like GW170817.
