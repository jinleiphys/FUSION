# Drag force in a strongly coupled anisotropic plasma

> We calculate the drag force experienced by an infinitely massive quark propagating at constant velocity through an anisotropic, strongly coupled N=4 plasma by means of its gravity dual. We find that the gluon cloud trailing behind the quark is generally misaligned with the quark velocity, and that the latter is also misaligned with the force. The drag coefficient $μ$ can be larger or smaller than the corresponding isotropic value depending on the velocity and the direction of motion. In the ultra-relativistic limit we find that generically $μ\propto p$. We discuss the conditions under which this behaviour may extend to more general situations.

## Key claim
The drag force on an infinitely massive quark moving through a strongly coupled anisotropic N=4 plasma is generally misaligned with the quark's velocity, and the drag coefficient can be either larger or smaller than the isotropic value depending on the velocity magnitude and direction of motion, with the condition that the quark mass is sent to infinity first and then the velocity approaches the speed of light.

## Method
The authors use the gauge/gravity duality (AdS/CFT correspondence) to model an infinitely massive quark as a string propagating in a type IIB supergravity solution dual to an anisotropic N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma. The string action is evaluated in the background metric with an axion field linear in the z-coordinate, and the drag force is extracted from the momentum flow along the string toward the horizon, with the metric functions determined numerically for general anisotropy parameter a/T.

## Key numbers
- The drag force for motion along the longitudinal z-direction is given by F_z = (sqrt(lambda)/2pi) * (v / u_c^2) * ch_c * cos(phi), where u_c is determined by the condition cb_c * cf_c - v^2(ch_c cos^2(phi) + sin^2(phi)) = 0
- For motion along the transverse x-direction (phi=pi/2), the anisotropic drag is smaller than isotropic for 0 <= v < v_c and larger for v_c < v <= 1, with v_c approximately 0.9 for small anisotropies
- In the ultra-relativistic limit v -> 1, for motion not perfectly aligned with the x-direction, the ratio F_aniso/F_iso diverges as 1/sqrt(1-v^2)
- The entropy density of the anisotropic plasma at large a/T scales as s = c_ent * N_c^2 * a^(1/3) * T^(8/3), where c_ent is a numerically determined constant
- The isotropic drag force is F_iso(T) = (pi/2) * sqrt(lambda) * T^2 * v / sqrt(1-v^2)

## Context
This work extends the seminal holographic drag force calculations of Herzog et al. (2006) and Gubser (2006) from isotropic N=4 plasma to an anisotropic plasma held in equilibrium by an external position-dependent theta parameter. The gravity solution was previously developed by Mateos and Trancanelli (2011). The results enable studies of heavy quark energy loss in anisotropic phases of quark-gluon plasma relevant to heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the system at intermediate times after collision has unequal longitudinal and transverse pressures.
