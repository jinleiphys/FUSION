# An Effective Lagrangian with Broken Scale and Chiral Symmetry IV: Nucleons and Mesons at Finite Temperature

> We study the finite temperature properties of an effective chiral Lagrangian which describes nuclear matter. Thermal fluctuations in both the nucleon and the meson fields are considered. The logarithmic and square root terms in the effective potential are evaluated by expansion and resummation with the result written in terms of the exponential integral and the error function, respectively. In the absence of explicit chiral symmetry breaking a phase transition restores the symmetry, but when the pion has a mass the transition is smooth. The nucleon and meson masses as a functions of density and temperature are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the absence of explicit chiral symmetry breaking, a phase transition restores chiral symmetry at finite temperature, but when the pion has a physical mass the transition becomes smooth (a crossover) rather than a sharp phase transition.

## Method

The authors extend their previously developed effective chiral Lagrangian with broken scale and chiral symmetry to finite temperature by including thermal fluctuations in both nucleon and meson fields. They evaluate logarithmic and square root terms in the effective potential using expansion and resummation techniques, expressing results in terms of the exponential integral and error function respectively. The equations of motion, effective masses, and thermodynamic quantities are solved self-consistently at finite temperature and density.

## Key numbers

- Chiral restoration temperature at zero baryon density (with explicit symmetry breaking absent): 162 MeV
- Chiral restoration temperature at saturation density (with explicit symmetry breaking absent): 125 MeV
- Chiral restoration temperature at very high density (with explicit symmetry breaking absent): 45 MeV
- For the physical case with explicit symmetry breaking, pion and sigma masses become degenerate at approximately 250 MeV
- At T=250 MeV, the nucleon effective mass ratio M*/M is approximately 0.43
- Critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition: 16 MeV
- Vacuum glueball mass: approximately 1.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature studies (Carter and Ellis, Papers I and II) of an effective Lagrangian incorporating broken scale and chiral symmetry that successfully described nuclear matter and finite nuclei. It also builds on their Paper III which treated the meson sector alone at finite temperature. The results contrast with earlier finite-temperature studies by Kalogera and Papazoglou that included only nucleon thermal effects, and with the Gell-Mann-Levy model which yields Tc=132 MeV. The work enables improved modeling of hadronic matter for astrophysical applications and relativistic heavy ion collisions, though the authors note that at temperatures above 250 MeV, glueball fluctuations and additional meson fields (rho, a1) would need to be included for a complete treatment.
