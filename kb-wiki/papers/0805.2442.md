# Is the Z^+(4430) a radially excited state of D_s?

> We present the interpretation that the recently discovered Z^+(4430) by the Belle Collaboration can be a radial excitation of the cs-bar state. We give an explicit cs-bar candidate for this state by calculating the mass value in our semirelativistic quark potential model and also give a natural interpretation on the reason why the decay mode Z-> J/ψπ^+ has not yet been seen while Z-> ψ' πcan be seen.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the \(Z^{+}(4430)\) discovered by Belle is not a tetraquark but a radially excited \(c\bar s\) (i.e., excited \(D_s\)) meson, with the caveat that the observed total decay width of 45 MeV does not rule out this interpretation because the dominant strong decays of such a state would be to \(DK\) final states, while the observed \(\psi' \pi\) mode proceeds via a weak interaction.

## Method

The authors used a semirelativistic quark potential model, previously successful in reproducing known heavy meson masses, to calculate the mass spectrum of radially excited \(D_s\) states up to principal quantum number \(n=4\). They then compared the calculated masses to the measured mass of the \(Z^{+}(4430)\) and estimated the relative decay rates of a candidate \(D_s(4430)\) state to \(J/\psi \pi\) versus \(\psi' \pi\) using a simple wavefunction overlap model based on Hermite polynomials, where the node number of the wavefunction determines the suppression.

## Key numbers

*   The measured mass of the \(Z^{+}(4430)\) is \(m_Z = (4433 \pm 4 \pm 2)\) MeV and its total width is \(\Gamma_Z = 45^{+18+30}_{-13-13}\) MeV (Belle Collaboration).
*   The product of branching ratios is \(\mathcal{B}(B^0 \to K^\mp Z^\pm(4430)) \times \mathcal{B}(Z^\pm(4430) \to \pi^\pm \psi') = (4.1 \pm 1.0(\text{stat}) \pm 1.4(\text{syst})) \times 10^{-5}\).
*   Two calculated \(n=4\) \(D_s\) states are candidates for the \(Z^{+}(4430)\): the \(n=4\; ^3P_1(1^+)\) state at 4440 MeV and the \(n=4\; ^3P_2(2^+)\) state at 4411 MeV.
*   The calculated ratio of decay widths for a candidate with \(n=4\) is \(\Gamma(D_s(4430) \to J/\psi \pi) / \Gamma(D_s(4430) \to \psi' \pi) = 6.9 \times 10^{-7}\).

## Context

This work directly challenges the prevailing interpretation at the time that the \(Z^{+}(4430)\) was a tetraquark or molecular state, as proposed in several papers (e.g., Rosner, Meng). The authors argue that their \(D_s\) interpretation naturally explains the non-observation of the \(J/\psi \pi\) decay mode via a node-suppression mechanism, which tetraquark models struggled to do. The paper enables experimental discrimination by predicting that if the state is an excited \(D_s\), it should be seen prominently in \(DK\) invariant mass distributions (e.g., in \(\bar{B}^0 \to K^- K^0 D^+\) decays), whereas a tetraquark would not. It also notes that the CDF observation of \(B_c^\pm \to J/\psi \pi^\pm\) provides a test of the node-suppression idea.
