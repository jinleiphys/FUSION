# Nucleon spin structure measurements at Jefferson Lab

> We summarize the measurements investigating the nucleon spin structure done at Jefferson Lab, a multi-GeV continuous electron beam facility located in Newport News, Virgina, USA. After motivating spin structure studies, we explain how Jefferson Lab uniquely contributes to them and describe the experimental program that was achieved with energies up to 6 GeV. We then discuss the continuation of this program at the higher energies now available thanks to the recent 12 GeV upgrade of Jefferson Lab's accelerator.

## Key claim

Jefferson Lab’s nucleon spin structure program has provided a precise mapping of the low and moderate $Q^2$ regions, triggering improvements in chiral perturbation theory predictions at low $Q^2$ and in perturbative techniques at high $Q^2$, but the agreement between data and chiral perturbation theory predictions is ambivalent, depending on the observable and the $Q^2$ range.

## Method

The paper summarizes the inclusive polarized electron scattering program at Jefferson Lab using continuous electron beams at 6 GeV, measuring spin structure functions $g_1$ and $g_2$, spin asymmetries, and their moments (such as $\Gamma_1$) across the resonance and deep inelastic scattering regions. Experiments covered the transition from the hadronic to partonic degrees of freedom, including the low-$Q^2$ chiral perturbation theory region, the moderate-$Q^2$ region, and the high-$x$ deep inelastic scattering region. The program used polarized targets (proton, deuteron, neutron) and measured helicity-dependent cross sections to extract quark polarizations and test sum rules.

## Key numbers

* The twist-4 coefficient $f_2^{p-n}$ was found to be relatively large, about half the leading twist contribution at $Q^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$.
* The Bjorken sum rule relates $\Gamma_1^{p-n}(Q^2) \equiv \int_0^{1^-} (g_1^p - g_1^n) dx = \frac{g_a}{6}[1 - \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} - 3.58(\frac{\alpha_s}{\pi})^2 - O(\alpha_s)] + O(1/Q^2)$.
* The Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule at $Q^2=0$ gives $\int_{\nu_0}^{\infty} \frac{\sigma_P(\nu) - \sigma_A(\nu)}{\nu} d\nu = \frac{4\pi^2 S \alpha \kappa^2}{M^2}$.
* The generalized GDH sum rule for $Q^2>0$ gives $\Gamma_1(Q^2) = \int_0^{x_0} g_1 dx = \frac{Q^2}{2M^2} I_1(Q^2)$.
* Precise polarized PDF measurements will be extended to $x \approx 0.8$ with data taken in 2019.

## Context

This work extends the nucleon spin structure measurements previously done at SLAC, CERN, DESY, BNL, and MAMI, which provided data at $Q^2=0$ and large $Q^2$. The Jefferson Lab 6 GeV program mapped the moderate $Q^2$ range, while new experiments (E97-110, E08-027, EG4) measured $g_1$, $g_2$, and their moments well into the chiral perturbation theory region ($Q^2 \lesssim 0.1$ GeV$^2$). The data enabled tests of chiral perturbation theory predictions from Ji (1999), Bernard (2002, 2012), Kao (2002), and Lensky (2014), and motivated an AdS/QCD calculation of $\alpha_s$ by Brodsky (2010) that led to an analytical calculation of the hadron mass spectrum with $\Lambda_s$ as the single input parameter. The 12 GeV upgrade enables precision measurements of $g_1$ and $g_2$ at higher $x$ and $Q^2$, as well as generalized parton distributions and transverse momentum dependent parton distribution extractions, with the SoLID detector and the Electron-Ion Collider planned for longer-term exploration.
