# On the near-threshold enhancement in $J/ψ\to γX$ with $X\toωϕ$

> We investigate the possibility of producing the enhancement observed in $J/ψ\to γX$ with $X\to ωϕ$ at BES by intermediate meson rescatterings through $f_0(1710)\to PP\to ωϕ$, $f_0(1710)\to VV\to ωϕ$, and $f_0(1710)\to SS\to ωϕ$. We find that intermediate meson rescatterings can produce some enhancement near the $ωϕ$ threshold. Implications about the property of this enhancement are discussed.

# On the near-threshold enhancement in \(J/\psi\to \gamma X\) with \(X\to\omega\phi\)

## Key claim

Intermediate meson rescatterings through the established \(f_0(1710)\) resonance, particularly via \(K^*\bar{K^*}\) rescattering with scalar meson exchange, can produce an enhancement near the \(\omega\phi\) threshold in \(J/\psi\to\gamma X\) with \(X\to\omega\phi\); however, the derived partial decay width for \(f_0\to\omega\phi\) is at least one order-of-magnitude smaller than the observed width of the \(X(1810)\) enhancement, making it unlikely that the observed signal originates solely from such rescattering in \(f_0(1710)\) decays.

## Method

The authors calculated the contributions of three classes of intermediate meson rescattering processes, pseudoscalar meson rescattering (\(PP\to\omega\phi\)), vector meson rescattering (\(VV\to\omega\phi\)), and scalar meson rescattering (\(SS\to\omega\phi\)), assuming the initial state is the \(f_0(1710)\) scalar meson produced in \(J/\psi\to\gamma f_0\). They applied the Cutkosky rule to evaluate the loop integrals for each rescattering channel, using dipole form factors with cut-off energies \(\Lambda=1.2\sim 1.8\) GeV, and determined coupling constants from experimental branching ratios and SU(3) flavor symmetry relations. The ratio of the rescattering amplitude squared to the corresponding tree-level decay amplitude was computed as a function of the \(\omega\phi\) invariant mass \(W\), and the resulting partial width for \(f_0\to\omega\phi\) was estimated by integrating over the Breit-Wigner propagator of the \(f_0\).

## Key numbers

- Mass and width of the observed enhancement from BESII: \(M=1812^{+19}_{-26}(\text{stat})\pm 18(\text{syst})\) MeV, \(\Gamma=105\pm 20(\text{stat})\pm 28(\text{syst})\) MeV.
- Production branching ratio from BESII: \(B(J/\psi\to\gamma X)\cdot B(X\to\omega\phi)=(2.61\pm 0.27(\text{stat})\pm 0.65(\text{syst}))\times 10^{-4}\).
- For \(K^*\bar{K^*}\) rescattering via scalar (\(\kappa\)) exchange with \(M_\kappa=0.7\) GeV and \(B.R.(f_0\to K^*\bar{K^*})=0.1\), the derived partial width \(\Gamma_{\omega\phi}=1.36\sim 3.01\) MeV for \(f_0\) mass from 1.74 to 1.81 GeV.
- With \(M_\kappa=0.7\) GeV, \(f_0\) mass fixed at 1.74 GeV, and \(B.R.(f_0\to K^*\bar{K^*})=0.1\sim 0.3\), the resulting \(B.R.(f_0\to\omega\phi)=(0.97\sim 2.92)\%\), corresponding to \(B.R.(J/\psi\to\gamma f_0\to\gamma\omega\phi)\simeq (1.36\sim 4.09)\times 10^{-5}\).
- With \(M_\kappa\) varied from 0.8 to 0.6 GeV, \(f_0\) mass at 1.74 GeV, and \(B.R.(f_0\to K^*\bar{K^*})=0.1\), the branching ratio \(B.R.(J/\psi\to\gamma f_0\to\gamma\omega\phi)\simeq (0.45\sim 3.61)\times 10^{-5}\).
- For the highest bound (\(B.R.(f_0\to K^*\bar{K^*})=0.3\), \(M_\kappa=0.6\) GeV), \(\Gamma(f_0\to\omega\phi)=10.8\) MeV, giving \(B.R.(J/\psi\to\gamma f_0\to\gamma\omega\phi)\simeq 1.08\times 10^{-4}\), comparable to the experimental value but with a much narrower width than the observed \(\Gamma=105\) MeV.
- PDG average for \(f_0(1710)\): \(M=1714\pm 5\) MeV, \(\Gamma_{tot}=140\pm 10\) MeV; BES data indicate a larger mass pole (\(\sim 1740\) MeV) and total width (\(\sim 166\) MeV).
- \(B.R.(f_0(1710)\to K\bar{K})\simeq 0.6\) from BES data (PDG average 0.38).
- \(B.R.(J/\psi\to\gamma f_0(1710))=1.4\times 10^{-3}\) derived from \(B.R.(J/\psi\to\gamma f_0\to\gamma K\bar{K})=8.5\times 10^{-4}\) and \(B.R.(f_0\to K\bar{K})=0.6\).

## Context

This work extends the study of intermediate meson rescattering (final state interactions) as a mechanism for OZI-rule violations in charmonium decays, following earlier work by Lipkin (1980s), Geiger and Isgur (1990s), and applications to \(J/\psi\) decays by Locher, Lu, and Zou (1990s) and Zhao, Zou, and collaborators (2000s). The specific enhancement in \(J/\psi\to\gamma X\) with \(X\to\omega\phi\) was observed by the BES Collaboration (2006), and the paper tests whether the established \(f_0(1710)\) resonance (PDG 2004) or the newly reported \(f_0(1790)\) (BES 2005) could produce the signal via rescattering, rather than interpreting \(X(1810)\) as a genuine exotic state such as a glueball (Bicudo 2006, Bugg 2006), hybrid (Chao 2006, HLLZ 2006), or four-quark state (Li and Bugg 2006). The results suggest that rescattering alone cannot reproduce the observed width, motivating further experimental searches for \(X(1810)\) in other decay channels (\(PP\), \(VV\)) and direct analyses of \(J/\psi\to\phi X\to\phi\omega\phi\) and \(J/\psi\to\omega X\to\omega\omega\phi\) to clarify its nature.
