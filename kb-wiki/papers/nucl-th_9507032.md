# Magnetic Moments of the Octet Baryons in a Chiral Quark Potential Model

> In quark potential models, two--body current contributions to baryon magnetic moments arise necessarily to satisfy the continuity equation for the electromagnetic current. On the other hand, the naive additive quark model predicts the experimental octet magnetic moments to within 5$\%$. We demonstrate that consistently derived two--body current contributions to the octet baryon magnetic moments are individually large, but tend to cancel each other globally.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two-body current contributions to octet baryon magnetic moments, which are required by gauge invariance in quark potential models, are individually large but tend to cancel each other globally, thereby preserving the approximate 5% agreement of the naive additive quark model with experimental data.

## Method

The authors use a chiral quark potential model (χQPM) with a Hamiltonian containing confinement, one-pion exchange, one-sigma exchange, and one-gluon exchange potentials between three constituent quarks. They derive the two-body exchange currents (pion-pair, pionic, gluon-pair, confinement-pair, and sigma-pair currents) that satisfy the continuity equation with these potentials, and compute their matrix elements using simple (0s)³ harmonic oscillator wave functions for the full baryon octet. The parameters are fitted to the nucleon and Δ-resonance masses, not to the magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- The naive additive quark model predicts experimental octet magnetic moments to within 5%.
- For the proton, the isovector pion-pair current contributes μ = -0.29 n.m. and the pionic (γππ) current contributes μ = 0.41 n.m., showing a near cancellation.
- The nucleon harmonic oscillator length b_N ≈ 0.6 fm is obtained from the variational principle and from the relation ⟨r²⟩_n = -b_N² (M_Δ - M_N)/M_N, which gives b_N = 0.612 fm.
- Taking b_N = 0.5 fm would give a proton magnetic moment of μ_p ≈ 5.6 n.m., spoiling the cancellations.
- The electromagnetic constituent quark size is r²_γq = 0.36 fm², taken from nucleon charge radius calculations.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-body exchange currents in non-strange baryons (Buchmann 1991) and the deuteron (Buchmann 1989) to the full baryon octet, including strange quarks with non-equal masses. It offers an explanation for the surprising success of the naive additive quark model predictions (Karl 1992, Close 1979) by showing that the large individual two-body current contributions cancel. The results enable further tests of exchange current effects in magnetic radii, where the authors note that current experimental errors for nucleon magnetic radii make clear evidence difficult to obtain.
