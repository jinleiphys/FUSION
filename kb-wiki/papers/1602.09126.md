# Phi meson spectral moments and QCD condensates in nuclear matter

> A detailed analysis of the lowest two moments of the $ϕ$ meson spectral function in vacuum and nuclear matter is performed. The consistency is examined between the constraints derived from finite energy QCD sum rules and the spectra computed within an improved vector dominance model, incorporating the coupling of kanonic degrees of freedom with the bare $ϕ$ meson. In the vacuum, recent accurate measurements of the $e^+ e^- \to K^+ K^-$ cross section allow us to determine the spectral function with high precision. In nuclear matter, the modification of the spectral function can be described by the interactions of the kaons from $ϕ\rightarrow K\bar{K}$ with the surrounding nuclear medium. This leads primarily to a strong broadening and an asymmetric deformation of the $ϕ$ meson peak structure. We confirm that, both in vacuum and nuclear matter, the zeroth and first moments of the corresponding spectral functions satisfy the requirements of the finite energy sum rules to a remarkable degree of accuracy. Limits on the strangeness sigma term of the nucleon are examined in this context. Applying our results to the second moment of the spectrum, we furthermore discuss constraints on four-quark condensates and the validity of the commonly used ground state saturation approximation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the lowest two moments of the phi meson spectral function in both vacuum and nuclear matter satisfy the constraints of finite energy QCD sum rules to a remarkable degree of accuracy, but this consistency depends on the assumption that the in-medium modifications are treated at leading (linear) order in baryon density and that the continuum onset is modeled with a step function or a ramp function with slope above about 1 GeV^-2.

## Method

The authors constructed the vacuum phi meson spectral function using an improved vector dominance model that couples the bare phi meson to kaon pairs, fitting the parameters to BABAR measurements of the e+e- -> K+K- cross section. For nuclear matter, they computed the in-medium spectral function by incorporating kaon and antikaon self-energies derived from chiral SU(3) effective field theory, including the non-perturbative generation of the Lambda(1405) resonance in the antikaon-nucleon channel. They then tested the consistency of the zeroth and first spectral moments with finite energy sum rules by solving for the delineation scale s0 where the perturbative QCD continuum sets in.

## Key numbers

- Bare phi meson mass fitted from data: 797 MeV
- Coupling constant g_phi = 0.74 x (-3g/sqrt(2)) = -10.2
- Delineation scale sqrt(s0) = 1.55 +/- 0.01 GeV from both zeroth and first moment equations in vacuum
- Average phi mass defined by ratio of first to zeroth moment up to sqrt(s-bar) = 1.2 GeV: 1038 MeV (compared to physical phi mass of 1019 MeV)
- In-medium kaon mass at normal nuclear matter density rho_0: m*_K(rho_0) = 535 MeV (from vacuum average m_K = 496 MeV with shift Delta m_K = 39 MeV)
- Ramp function parameters: sqrt(s0) = 1.66 +/- 0.03 GeV, slope W' = 0.8 +/- 0.2 GeV^-2

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Gubler and Weise, 2015) by providing a more detailed analysis of the lowest three spectral moments and their relation to QCD condensates. It builds on prior QCD sum rule studies of the phi meson in matter (Hatsuda, Klingl, Koike, Zschocke) that established a linear relation between the in-medium mass shift and the strangeness content of the nucleon, but which did not realistically consider the phi meson width. The results enable future interpretation of dedicated experiments measuring the in-medium phi meson width and mass shift at J-PARC (Kawama, Aoki) and provide constraints on four-quark condensates and the validity of the ground state saturation approximation.
