# Simulating plasma instabilities in SU(3) gauge theory

> We compute nonequilibrium dynamics of plasma instabilities in classical-statistical lattice gauge theory in 3+1 dimensions. The simulations are done for the first time for the SU(3) gauge group relevant for quantum chromodynamics. We find a qualitatively similar behavior as compared to earlier investigations in SU(2) gauge theory. The characteristic growth rates are about 25 % lower for given energy density, such that the isotropization process is slower. Measured in units of the characteristic screening mass, the primary growth rate is independent of the number of colors.

# Simulating plasma instabilities in SU(3) gauge theory

## Key claim

The first classical-statistical lattice gauge theory simulations of plasma instabilities for the SU(3) gauge group relevant to QCD show qualitatively similar behavior to earlier SU(2) results, but with characteristic growth rates about 25% lower for a given energy density, making the isotropization process slower. When measured in units of the characteristic screening mass, the primary growth rate becomes independent of the number of colors.

## Method

The authors performed classical-statistical lattice gauge theory simulations in 3+1 dimensions using a Wilsonian lattice action with leapfrog-type equations of motion for the spatial link variables. For SU(3), the temporal plaquette was solved numerically using a multi-dimensional Newton method in 18 real variables, unlike the simpler analytic procedure available for SU(2). Initial conditions were sampled from a Gaussian probability functional with an anisotropic momentum distribution (Delta_z much smaller than Delta) to mimic the locally anisotropic medium in heavy-ion collisions, and expectation values were averaged over multiple runs (approximately 20 for SU(3) and 100 for SU(2)).

## Key numbers

- Primary growth rates for SU(3) are approximately 25% lower than for SU(2) at the same energy density (lattice energy density epsilon_hat = 0.05)
- The squared gauge field amplitude ratio between SU(3) and SU(2) at fixed energy density is: A_tilde^2_SU(3) = (3/8) A_tilde^2_SU(2)
- The screening mass ratio derived from tadpole diagram analysis: m_T,SU(3) = (3/4) m_T,SU(2)
- Inverse maximum primary growth rate for RHIC energies (5-25 GeV/fm^3): gamma_max,pr^(-1) approximately 1.6-2.4 fm/c
- Inverse maximum primary growth rate for LHC energies (approximately factor of two higher energy density): gamma_max,pr^(-1) approximately 1.3-2.0 fm/c
- Effective isotropization occurs up to characteristic momentum |p| less than or approximately 1 GeV
- Lattice size used: 64^3 and 96^3; initial transverse width Delta = 1.06 epsilon^(1/4)

## Context

This work extends earlier classical-statistical simulations of plasma instabilities in SU(2) gauge theory by Berges (2007) to the SU(3) group relevant for QCD, and complements hard-loop approximation studies for SU(3) in 1+1 dimensions by previous authors. The results provide quantitative estimates of isotropization time scales for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, showing that isotropization is slower than previously suggested by SU(2) results, and that the primary growth rates scale with the screening mass in a color-independent way. The secondary growth rates, arising from fluctuation effects beyond the hard-loop approximation as analyzed via 2PI resummed loop diagrams, show similar values between SU(2) and SU(3) within errors.
