# Relationship Between the Azimuthal Dependencies of Nuclear Modification Factor and Ridge Yield

> The azimuthal angular dependence of the nuclear modification factor R_{AA}(p_T, phi,N_{part}) recently obtained by PHENIX is related at low p_T to the trigger phi dependence of the ridge yield as measured by STAR in a framework in which the azimuthal anisotropy is driven by semihard scattering near the surface. Careful consideration of the initial geometry leads to the determination of a surface segment in which the production of semihard partons are responsible for the phi dependence of the inclusive distribution on the one hand, and for the angular correlation in ridge phenomenology on the other. With v_2 also being well reproduced along with R_{AA} and ridge yield, all relevant phi dependencies in heavy-ion collisions can now be understood in a unified description that emphasizes the ridge production whether or not a trigger is used.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the azimuthal angular dependence of the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}(p_T,\phi,N_{\rm part})\) at low \(p_T\) (below 2 GeV/c) and the trigger-angle dependence of the ridge yield are two sides of the same physical phenomenon, both driven by semihard parton scattering near the surface of the nuclear overlap region. The hedge is that this relationship holds specifically for \(p_T < 2\) GeV/c, where a modified nuclear modification factor \(R'_{AA}\) (scaled by number of participants rather than binary collisions) is used, and that the ridge component of the single-particle distribution is responsible for the azimuthal anisotropy.

## Method

The authors decompose the single-particle distribution \(\rho_1(p_T,\phi,N_p)\) into a \(\phi\)-independent bulk component \(B(p_T,N_p)\) and a \(\phi\)-dependent ridge component \(R(p_T,\phi,N_p)\). The ridge component is modeled as proportional to a surface segment \(S(\phi,b)\) of the elliptical nuclear overlap, calculated from the geometry of the almond-shaped initial configuration using an elliptic integral of the second kind. The correlation between semihard parton direction \(\phi_1\) and ridge particle direction \(\phi_2\) is taken from the correlated emission model (CEM) with a Gaussian width \(\sigma = 0.33\). The elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) is then computed from the second harmonic of \(\rho_1\), and the ridge yield \(Y^R(\phi_s,N_p)\) is obtained by projecting the two-particle ridge distribution \(\rho_2^R(\phi_1,\phi_2)\) onto the trigger angle \(\phi_1\).

## Key numbers

- The analysis is focused on \(p_T < 2\) GeV/c, with effective \(p_T\) values of 1.21 and 1.71 GeV/c taken from PHENIX tables.
- The Gaussian width of the correlation between semihard parton direction and flow direction is \(\sigma = 0.33\).
- The inverse slope for the ridge component is \(T' = 0.317\) GeV, with \(\Delta T = T' - T = 0.045\) GeV, giving \(T'' = 1.916\) GeV from the relation \(1/T'' = 1/T - 1/T'\).
- The free parameter \(a\) in the ridge amplitude is set to \(a = 0.47\) to fit the \(v_2\) data.
- The surface segment \(S(\phi,b)\) is calculated for \(\phi = 0\) and \(\phi = \pi/2\) as a function of impact parameter \(b\) (normalized by nuclear radius \(R_A = 7\) fm), with \(\sigma = 0.33\).
- The local density \(D(x',y',b)\) uses a Woods-Saxon nuclear density with parameters \(r_0 = 0.92\), \(\xi = 0.08\), and \(\rho_0 = 0.285\) (all in units of \(R_A = 7\) fm), and \(\omega = 4.6\) for Au+Au collisions.
- The representative point for semihard scattering is at \(\delta r = 0.17\) from the surface on the short axis.

## Context

This work extends the semihard scattering and ridge phenomenology developed by Hwa and collaborators (2007, 2008) and the correlated emission model (CEM) of Chiu and Hwa (2005), which previously used a simpler \(\Theta(\phi)\) function for the azimuthal dependence. It directly relates the PHENIX measurements of \(R_{AA}(p_T,\phi,N_{\rm part})\) from Safarik (2007) to the STAR measurements of ridge yield as a function of trigger angle \(\phi_s\) from Abelev (2009). The approach contrasts with hydrodynamic models such as the Buda-Lund model (Csorgo 2004, 2005) by attributing azimuthal anisotropy to semihard scattering near the surface rather than pressure gradients from rapid thermalization. The unified description enables simultaneous reproduction of \(R_{AA}\), \(v_2\), and ridge yield for \(p_T < 2\) GeV/c, and suggests that ridge production is pervasive whether or not a trigger is used.
