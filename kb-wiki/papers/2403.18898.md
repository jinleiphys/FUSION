# Exact evaluation of large-charge correlation functions in non-relativistic conformal field theory

> The large-charge master field which generates all n-point correlation functions with an insertion of large charge Q in non-relativistic conformal field theory is obtained. This field is used to compute Schrödinger-invariant n-point correlation functions of large-charge operators via a direct evaluation of the path integral. Conformal dimensions are found to agree with calculations based on the state-operator correspondence. The master field solution exhibits an emergent harmonic trap whose frequency is a function of the Euclidean time. The large-charge effective action with operator insertions describes a droplet of superfluid matter whose spatial size scales with the time separation of sources. The solution is used to compute Schrödinger symmetry breaking corrections in the large-charge effective field theory (EFT) due to a finite scattering length in the fundamental theory of fermions near unitarity. The scaling of these effects in the large-charge power counting scheme is established, and the size of the effects is quantified using input from quantum Monte Carlo simulations of the near-unitary gas, as well as from the large-N expansion at large charge.

## Key claim
The paper presents an exact evaluation of large-charge correlation functions in non-relativistic conformal field theory via a direct path integral method, finding that the large-charge "master field" solution of the Goldstone field generates all n-point functions with large-charge Q operators. The results agree with prior calculations based on the state-operator correspondence, but the authors note that the method works only in the semiclassical approximation where the Euclidean time ordering emerges naturally from the diffusion equation structure.

## Method
The authors formulate the superfluid effective field theory (EFT) in Euclidean space, where the Goldstone boson field θ describes the phase of the condensate. They exponentiate operator insertions of charge Q into the action, then solve the resulting Euler-Lagrange equations using an ansatz that reduces the highly nonlinear equation of motion to a diffusion equation. The Green's function solution yields explicit expressions for the Goldstone field and density, from which two-point, three-point, and general n-point correlation functions are computed by evaluating the path integral at the saddle point. Schrödinger symmetry breaking corrections due to finite scattering length are incorporated via a spurion analysis matched to operators in the superfluid EFT.

## Key numbers
- The conformal dimension of a free boson operator of charge Q is Δ_Q = 3Q/2, derived from the free theory calculation with Δ_φ = 3/2.
- The Bertsch parameter ξ appears in the energy per particle at unitarity: E/N = (3/5)(k_F²/2M)ξ, where k_F is the Fermi momentum.
- The low-energy constant c_0 in the superfluid EFT is fixed as c_0 = 2^(5/2)/(15π² ξ^(3/2)) by matching to the unitary Fermi gas equation of state.
- The two-point function takes the Schrödinger-invariant form G(x₁,x₂) = θ(τ₁₂) τ₁₂^(-3Q/2) exp(-M Q x₁₂²/(2τ₁₂)) Ψ₂ e^(μQτ₁₂), where Ψ₂ = N² [(M/(2π))^(3/2) e^(1-iπ) 2Q]^Q.

## Context
This work extends the large-charge expansion method previously developed for relativistic conformal field theories (Hellerman et al., 2015; Gaumé et al., 2020) to non-relativistic systems with Schrödinger symmetry. It provides a direct path integral verification of conformal dimensions computed via the state-operator correspondence (Nishida, 2007; Favrod et al., 2018; Kravec et al., 2019). The results enable computation of Schrödinger symmetry breaking corrections due to finite scattering length in the fundamental fermion theory, with applications to neutron matter and the "unnuclear" physics framework of Hammer and Son (2021) for nuclear reactions with many neutrons in final states.
