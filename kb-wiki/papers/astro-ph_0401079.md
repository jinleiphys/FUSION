# Neutrino-pasta scattering: the opacity of nonuniform neutron-rich matter

> Neutron-rich matter at subnuclear densities may involve complex structures displaying a variety of shapes, such as spherical, slablike, and/or rodlike shapes. These phases of the {\it nuclear pasta} are expected to exist in the crust of neutron stars and in core-collapse supernovae. The dynamics of core-collapse supernovae is very sensitive to the interactions between neutrinos and nucleons/nuclei. Indeed, neutrino excitation of the low-energy modes of the pasta may allow for a significant energy transfer to the nuclear medium, thereby reviving the stalled supernovae shock. The linear response of the nuclear pasta to neutrinos is modeled via a simple semi-classical simulation. The transport mean-free path for $μ$ and $τ$ neutrinos (and antineutrinos) is expressed in terms of the static structure factor of the pasta, which is evaluated using Metropolis Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key claim

Neutrino excitation of low-energy collective modes in nuclear pasta may allow significant energy transfer to the nuclear medium, potentially reviving the stalled supernova shock, but the authors caution that their semi-classical simulation may not have converged to the true thermal-equilibrium state and that the static structure factor could change with additional Monte Carlo or molecular dynamics evolution.

## Method

The authors model neutrino-pasta scattering using a classical two-body potential with short-range nuclear attraction and long-range screened Coulomb repulsion, with parameters adjusted to reproduce bulk nuclear matter properties. Metropolis Monte Carlo simulations in a canonical ensemble at T=1 MeV are performed for 4000 nucleons (3200 neutrons, 800 protons) at subnuclear densities, and the transport mean-free path for muon and tau neutrinos is expressed in terms of the static structure factor S(q) computed from the neutron-neutron correlation function.

## Key numbers

- Baryon density of ρ = 0.01 fm⁻³ (about 1/16 of normal nuclear density) and ρ = 0.025 fm⁻³ (about 1/6 of saturation density) are simulated
- Temperature fixed at T = 1 MeV for all calculations
- Electron fraction Y_e = 0.2 used as a typical neutron-rich condition
- Simulation uses A = 4000 particles (N = 3200 neutrons, Z = 800 protons) in a cube of length L = 73.7 fm
- Electron screening length arbitrarily reduced to λ = 10 fm to control finite-size effects
- Potential energy of -5.385 ± 0.003 MeV per nucleon obtained from statistical average of final 50,000 Metropolis sweeps
- Molecular dynamics evolution for 46,500 fm/c increased the peak of S(q) by only about 10 percent

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear pasta phases, previously calculated for ground-state structure by Ravenhall (1983), Hashimoto (1984), and others, into the domain of low-energy dynamics and neutrino response. The authors note that while quantum molecular dynamics has been used to study pasta structure by Maruyama (1998) and Watanabe (2003), no calculations of the linear response to weakly-interacting probes had been reported. The work enables future comparisons with conventional nuclear models and could provide physics missing from core-collapse supernova simulations, which currently use liquid-drop models such as that of Lattimer and Swesty (1992) that neglect interactions between nuclei.
