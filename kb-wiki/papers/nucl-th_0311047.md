# Hadronic Effective Field Theory Applied to $Λ$-Hypernuclei

> In the present work, the approach of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (FST) is extended to the region of nonzero strangeness in application to single-particle states in single $Λ$-hypernuclei. To include $Λ$'s, an additional contribution to their effective lagrangian is systematically constructed within the framework of FST. The relativistic Hartree (Kohn-Sham) equations are solved numerically, and least-square fits to a series of experimental levels are preformed at various levels of truncation in the extended lagrangian. The ground-state properties of any $Λ$-hypernuclei are then predicted. In addition, ground-state $Lambda$-particle-nucleon-hole splittings are calculated where appropriate, and the apporach is calibrated against a calculation of the $s_{1/2}$-doublet splitting in the nucleus $^{32}_{15}P_{17}$.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (FST) effective field theory approach, previously applied only to ordinary nuclei, to single $\Lambda$-hypernuclei by systematically constructing an additional contribution to the effective lagrangian; the extension is calibrated against experimental data including ground-state binding energies, s-p shell excitations, and the spin-orbit splitting of p-states in $^{13}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{C}$, with the hedge that the $\Sigma$ hyperon is not explicitly included and $\Lambda-\Sigma$ mixing effects are only implicitly considered through the scalar meson as a two-pion resonance.

## Method

The author constructs a $\Lambda$-lagrangian as an additional contribution to the FST effective lagrangian, including terms up to order $\nu=3$: a minimal coupling term (order $\nu=2$), tensor couplings to the vector and photon fields (order $\nu=3$), and three nonlinear terms involving scalar and vector meson fields (order $\nu=3$). The relativistic Hartree (Kohn-Sham) equations are solved numerically for single $\Lambda$-hypernuclei, with the nucleon and meson parameters fixed from the FST G2 parameter set and the remaining $\Lambda$ parameters determined via least-squares fits to 10 pieces of experimental data at four different levels of truncation. Ground-state $\Lambda$-particle-nucleon-hole splittings are calculated using an effective particle-hole interaction derived from scalar and vector meson exchange, with the radial Slater integrals evaluated using the Hartree wave functions.

- The G2 parameter set from FST includes: $\mathrm{m_{S}/M}=0.55410$, $\mathrm{g_{S}/4\pi}=0.83522$, $\mathrm{g_{V}/4\pi}=1.01560$, $\mathrm{g_{\rho}/4\pi}=0.75467$, $\eta_{1}=0.64992$, $\kappa_{3}=3.2467$, $\eta_{\rho}=0.3901$, $f_{\mathrm{V}}/4=0.1734$, $f_{\rho}/4=0.9619$, $\eta_{2}=0.10975$, $\kappa_{4}=0.63152$, $\zeta_{0}=2.6416$, $\beta_{S}=-0.09328$, $\beta_{V}=-0.45964$, $\alpha_{1}=1.7234$, $\alpha_{2}=-1.5798$
- The $\Lambda$ anomalous magnetic moment is $\lambda_{\Lambda} = -0.613$
- The fit uses 10 pieces of experimental data: six ground-state binding energies, three s-p shell excitations for the $\Lambda$, and the spin-orbit splitting of the p-states in $^{13}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{C}$
- The scaled meson mean fields satisfy $\Phi/M \sim 1/3$, $W/M \sim 1/3$, and $k_{\mathrm{F}}/M \sim 1/4$

## Context

This work extends the FST effective field theory approach (Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang, 1997) from ordinary nuclei to the strangeness sector, specifically single $\Lambda$-hypernuclei with $S=-1$ and $T=0$. It builds on earlier relativistic mean field models of hypernuclei that required weaker meson couplings to the $\Lambda$ (Brockmann and Weise, 1977; Bouyssy, 1981) and later models that included tensor couplings to reproduce the weak spin-orbit interaction (Noble, 1980; Mares and Zofka, 1994; Cohen, 1992; Jennings, 1990). The approach enables predictions of ground-state properties and $\mathrm{s}_{1/2}$-doublet splittings for any single $\Lambda$-hypernucleus, with upcoming high-resolution experiments at Jefferson Lab expected to measure these splittings in $^{12}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{B}$ and $^{16}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{N}$ (Garrett, 1994; Urciuoli, 2001).
