# Novel Phases of a Baryon-Dense QCD-like Theory

> We investigate the phases of a strongly coupled QCD-like theory at finite baryon chemical potential using s-confining supersymmetric QCD deformed by anomaly-mediated supersymmetry breaking. Focusing on the case of three colors and four flavors, we identify novel phases including spontaneous breaking of baryon number and/or parity. Both first-order and second-order phase transitions are observed as the baryon chemical potential is varied. These findings may offer insights into possible phases of real QCD at intermediate baryon densities.

## Key claim

Using s-confining supersymmetric QCD with three colors and four flavors deformed by anomaly-mediated supersymmetry breaking, the authors identify novel phases including spontaneous breaking of baryon number and/or parity at finite baryon chemical potential, but only under the assumption that higher-order Kähler coefficients are positive and that the chemical potential remains below a UV-critical value where the theory becomes unstable.

## Method

The authors couple s-confining SQCD (N_c=3, N_f=4) to anomaly-mediated SUSY breaking (AMSB) and introduce a finite baryon chemical potential via a background U(1)_B super-gauge field spurion. They stabilize the resulting runaway directions by including dimension-six Kähler potential operators generated by strong dynamics, then numerically minimize the scalar potential in a reduced four-dimensional field space (x, v, b, \bar{b}) to identify vacuum structures as functions of μ_B and the SUSY-breaking scale m_{3/2}.

## Key numbers

- The UV-critical chemical potential for instability is estimated as (μ_B^{UV-crit})^2 ∼ N_c^2 m_{3/2}^2, above which the squark effective mass becomes negative and the theory runs away.
- The IR-critical chemical potential for developing a baryon-number-breaking vacuum is (μ_B^{IR-crit})^2 = m_{3/2}^2 (N_c+1)(2N_c+3)|κ|^4 / (256 π^4), which is much smaller than μ_B^{UV-crit} for perturbative κ.
- The sbaryon soft mass squared from AMSB at leading order is m_{B, \widetilde{B}}^2 = (N_c+1)(2N_c+3)|κ|^4 |m_{3/2}|^2 / (256 π^4), with N_c=3 giving m_{B, \widetilde{B}}^2 = (4)(9)|κ|^4 |m_{3/2}|^2 / (256 π^4).
- The squark mass squared in the UV from AMSB is m_{q, \bar{q}}^2 = (N_c^2-1)(2N_c-1)/(N_c) g(μ_RG)^4/(64 π^4) m_{3/2}^2 - (1/N_c^2) μ_B^2.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of anomaly-mediated SQCD from Murayama (2021), Csaki (2021, 2022), and de Lima (2023) by adding a finite baryon chemical potential, which was previously shown by Harnik (2003) to cause instabilities in the supersymmetric theory. The stabilization mechanism using higher-order Kähler operators enables a controlled exploration of the QCD-like phase diagram at intermediate baryon densities, potentially offering insights into real QCD phases and providing a calculable framework for strongly coupled theories at finite density that are difficult to study with lattice techniques.
