# Indications for Cluster Melting from Forward-Backward Charge Fluctuations at RHIC Energies

> We study forward-backward charge fluctuations to probe the correlations among produced particles in ultra relativistic heavy ion collisions. We develop a model that describes the forward-backward dynamical fluctuations and apply it to interpret the recent PHOBOS data. Within the present model, the dynamical fluctuations are related to the particle production mechanism via cluster decay and to long range correlations between the forward and backward rapidity hemispheres. We argue that with a tight centrality cut, PHOBOS may see a strong decrease of the dynamical fluctuations. Within the present model, this deterioration of the correlation among the produced hadrons can be interpreted as a sign for the production of a hot, dense and interacting medium.

## Key claim

The observed decrease in forward-backward charge fluctuations and effective cluster multiplicity in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, compared to peripheral collisions, can be interpreted as a sign of "cluster melting" in a hot, dense, interacting medium, but this interpretation depends on the model's assumption that the dynamical fluctuations are dominated by cluster decay and long-range correlations.

## Method

The authors developed a model decomposing the variance of the forward-backward correlation variable C = (N_F - N_B)/sqrt(N_F + N_B) into short-range correlations from cluster decay and long-range correlations between hemispheres. They fitted this model to PHOBOS data for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, extracting parameters including the effective cluster multiplicity k, the fraction f of particles from cluster decay, and the short-range rapidity correlation length lambda_short for both central (0%-20%) and peripheral (40%-60%) centrality bins.

## Key numbers

* Effective cluster multiplicity k = 2.7 for 40%-60% peripheral Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, measured at eta = 2 with Delta eta = 2.
* Effective cluster multiplicity k = 2.2 for 0%-20% central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
* Short-range correlation length lambda_short = 0.6 for peripheral (40%-60%) Au+Au collisions, decreasing to lambda_short = 0.4 for central (0%-20%) Au+Au collisions.
* Fraction of correlated particles f = 0.99 for peripheral collisions, decreasing to f = 0.88 for central collisions.
* Extrapolated cluster multiplicity k approx 1.9 and lambda_short approx 0.3 for 0%-3% most central Au+Au collisions.
* For comparison, the UA5 experiment found k approx 2 in pp and p-pbar interactions, and a hadron resonance gas model predicts k_HG = 1.5.

## Context

This work extends the UA5 (1983) analysis of cluster multiplicities in pp and p-pbar collisions to nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC, using PHOBOS data. The observed decrease of k from 2.7 in peripheral to 2.2 in central Au+Au, along with the decreasing short-range correlation length, contradicts expectations from simple string fragmentation models like HIJING and suggests a transition to deconfined matter. The authors propose that tighter centrality cuts could reveal complete cluster melting (k approaching 1), and recommend future measurements at different energies, ion sizes, and with transverse momentum cuts below 2 GeV to distinguish hadronic from QGP effects.
