# Nuclear Shadowing and the Optics of Hadronic Fluctuations

> A coordinate space description of shadowing in deep-inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering is presented. The picture in the laboratory frame is that of quark-gluon fluctuations of the high-energy virtual photon, propagating coherently over large light-cone distances in the nuclear medium. We discuss the detailed dependence of the coherence effects on the invariant mass of the fluctuation. We comment on the issue of possible saturation in the shadowing effects at very small Bjorken-$x$.

## Key claim
The paper argues that nuclear shadowing in deep-inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering can be understood as the coherent interaction of hadronic (quark-gluon) fluctuations of the virtual photon with the nuclear medium, and that the asymptotic high-energy behavior of shadowing sets in when coherence lengths of low-mass fluctuations exceed nuclear dimensions, but the nuclear diameter itself is not apparent in the observed x-dependence because the sum over fluctuations of different masses masks its role.

## Method
The authors present a coordinate-space description of nuclear structure functions, using a Fourier transform relation between momentum-space structure functions F2 and coordinate-space functions F2(l). They analyze the shadowing correction using the Gribov relation that connects diffractive production cross sections on nucleons to coherent multiple scattering in nuclei, and illustrate the role of hadronic fluctuation masses with a schematic vector-meson-dominance model and a Regge-based parametrization of diffractive production.

## Key numbers
- Shadowing appears at Bjorken-x < 0.1, where the structure function ratio F2^A/F2^N shows significant reduction.
- The coherence length of a hadronic fluctuation is lambda_X = 2 nu / (Q^2 + M_X^2) = (1/(M x)) * (Q^2/(Q^2 + M_X^2)).
- Shadowing onset requires lambda_X > d ~ 2 fm, the average nucleon-nucleon separation.
- At x = 3 x 10^{-3} and Q^2 ~ 0.7 GeV^2, the rho meson coherence length becomes lambda_rho ~ 36 fm.
- The asymptotic exponent epsilon in R_A - 1 = -c * (1/x)^epsilon is epsilon ~ 0.1 from Regge phenomenology.
- For a hadronic state with mass m_V, saturation of shadowing occurs when l > (2 Delta/3) * (1 + m_V^2/Q^2), with Delta = 2 R_A.
- For carbon-12, the nuclear diameter 2R ~ 5 fm.

## Context
This work extends the Gribov (1970) connection between shadowing and diffraction, and builds on coordinate-space analyses by Hoyer (1996) and Vanttinen (1998). It provides a framework to interpret NMC and E665 data on nuclear structure functions, and enables future studies of possible saturation of shadowing at very small Bjorken-x by clarifying how the nuclear diameter is masked by the sum over hadronic fluctuation masses.
