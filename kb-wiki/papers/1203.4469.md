# High-precision scale setting in lattice QCD

> Scale setting is of central importance in lattice QCD. It is required to predict dimensional quantities in physical units. Moreover, it determines the relative lattice spacings of computations performed at different values of the bare coupling, and this is needed for extrapolating results into the continuum. Thus, we calculate a new quantity, $w_0$, for setting the scale in lattice QCD, which is based on the Wilson flow like the scale $t_0$ (M. Luscher, JHEP 1008 (2010) 071). It is cheap and straightforward to implement and compute. In particular, it does not involve the delicate fitting of correlation functions at asymptotic times. It typically can be determined on the few per-mil level. We compute its continuum extrapolated value in 2+1-flavor QCD for physical and non-physical pion and kaon masses, to allow for mass-independent scale setting even away from the physical mass point. We demonstrate its robustness by computing it with two very different actions (one of them with staggered, the other with Wilson fermions) and by showing that the results agree for physical quark masses in the continuum limit.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new lattice QCD scale-setting quantity, \(w_0\), based on the Wilson flow, which is cheaper and easier to compute than the established Sommer scale \(r_0\) and which has smaller systematic uncertainties than the related \(t_0\) scale, while agreeing between two very different fermion actions (staggered and Wilson) in the continuum limit at the physical point.

## Method

The authors define \(w_0\) via the condition \(W(t) \equiv t \frac{d}{dt}\{t^2 \langle E(t)\rangle\} = 0.3\) at flow time \(t = w_0^2\), where \(\langle E(t)\rangle\) is the action density from the Wilson (or Symanzik) flow. They compute \(w_0/a\) on ensembles with 2+1 flavors of either 2HEX smeared Wilson fermions or 2-stout smeared staggered fermions, at multiple lattice spacings and pion masses down to the physical value, then interpolate in quark mass and extrapolate to the continuum limit using the Omega baryon mass to set the absolute scale.

## Key numbers

*   \(w_0 = 0.1755(18)(04)~\mathrm{fm}\) (Wilson fermions, continuum limit, physical quark masses); the first error is statistical, the second systematic; overall uncertainty is 1%.
*   The statistical error on \(w_0/a\) itself is typically at the few per mil level or less.
*   The relative systematic error of \((t_0)^{1/2}\) is about four times larger than that of \(w_0\).
*   Finite-volume effects on \(w_0/a\) become relevant only for box sizes \(\lesssim 2~\mathrm{fm}\) (where \(M_\pi L = 1.35\)).
*   The integrated autocorrelation time of \(E(t=w_0^2)\) is around or below 50 unit-length trajectories for lattice spacings down to about \(a = 0.054~\mathrm{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the Wilson-flow-based scale-setting approach of Luscher (2010), who introduced the \(t_0\) scale, by defining a new quantity \(w_0\) that is less sensitive to cutoff effects. It provides an alternative to the Sommer scale \(r_0\) (Sommer, 1994) and the related \(r_1\) scale (MILC Collaboration, 2000), which require delicate fitting of Wilson loops. The paper enables future lattice QCD calculations to set the scale reliably using a cheap, precise, and mass-independent observable, and provides a parameterization of \(w_0\) for non-physical pion and kaon masses.
