# Theoretical evaluation of solar proton-proton fusion reaction rate and its uncertainties

> The weak proton-proton fusion into a deuteron ($^2$H) is the driving reaction in the energy production in the Sun, as well as similar main sequence stars. Its reaction rate in the solar interior is determined only theoretically. Here, we provide a new determination of the rate of this reaction in solar conditions $S^{11}(0)$, and analyze theoretical and experimental sources for uncertainties, using effective field theory of quantum chromo-dynamics without explicit pions at next-to-leading order. We find an enhancement of $S^{11}$ by $1-4\%$ over the previously recommended value. This change reduces the calculated fluxes of neutrinos originating in $^8$B and $^7$Be nuclear reactions in the Sun, thus favoring higher abundances for metallic photospheric elements, in the tension between different composition determination, known as the ``Solar Composition Problem''.

## Key claim

The authors find an enhancement of the astrophysical S-factor for proton-proton fusion, \(S^{11}(0)\), by \(1-4\%\) over the previously recommended value from the SFII review (Adelberger et al., 2011), but this result depends on the choice of the axial charge of the nucleon \(g_A\), which is currently under tension between different neutron half-life measurements.

## Method

The authors use pionless effective field theory (EFT) of quantum chromodynamics at next-to-leading order (NLO), calibrating the two-body weak low-energy constant \(L_{1,A}\) using a consistent calculation of tritium beta decay (\(^3\text{H} \rightarrow ^3\text{He} + e^- + \bar{\nu}_e\)). They validate the approach by calculating the analogous electromagnetic reaction, thermal neutron capture on a proton (\(n+p\rightarrow d+\gamma\)), and comparing it to the experimental value, and they estimate theoretical uncertainty using a Bayesian analysis of the EFT expansion truncation error.

## Key numbers

*   \(S^{11}(0) = (4.14 \pm 0.01 \pm 0.005 \pm 0.06) \times 10^{-23} \text{ MeV fm}^2\), where the uncertainties are due to \(g_A\), experimental uncertainties in tritium decay, and theoretical uncertainty, respectively.
*   The nuclear matrix element squared is \(\Lambda_{pp}^2(0) = 7.21 \pm 0.03_{g_A} \pm 0.005_{exp} \pm 0.11\).
*   The calculated matrix element for thermal neutron capture is \(Y'_{np} = 1.253 \pm 0.006\), compared to the experimental value \(Y'^{exp}_{np} = 1.2532 \pm 0.0019\).
*   The increase in \(S^{11}(0)\) reduces the expected neutrino fluxes from \(^8\)B and \(^7\)Be reactions in the Sun by \(3-11\%\) and \(1-4\%\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends the line of theoretical calculations of the proton-proton fusion rate, which have shown a general upward trend from the recommended value of the SFII review (Adelberger et al., 2011). It provides a new determination using pionless EFT, which the authors argue allows for a more robust and reliable assessment of systematic theoretical uncertainty compared to previous chiral EFT calculations (Acharya et al., 2016, 2019) and lattice QCD calculations (NPLQCD collaboration, 2017). The result favors higher abundances for metallic photospheric elements, as in the analysis by Magg et al. (2022), over the analysis by Asplund et al. (2021), contributing to the "Solar Composition Problem."
