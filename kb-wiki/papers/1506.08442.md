# Electromagnetic currents induced by color fields

> The quark production in classical color fields is investigated with a focus on the induction of an electromagnetic current by produced quarks. We show that the color SU(2) and the SU(3) theories lead significantly different results for the electromagnetic current. In uniform SU(2) color fields, the net electromagnetic current is not generated, while in SU(3) color fields the net current is induced depending on the color direction of background fields. Also the numerical study of the quark production in inhomogeneous color fields is done. Motivated by gauge field configurations provided by the color glass condensate framework, we introduce an ensemble of randomly distributed color electric fluxtubes. The spectrum of photons emitted from the quarks by a classical process is shown.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the color SU(2) and SU(3) theories lead to significantly different results for the electromagnetic current induced by produced quarks in classical color fields, with the net current being exactly zero in uniform SU(2) fields but nonzero in SU(3) fields depending on the color direction of the background field. This difference persists even in inhomogeneous fields, where the SU(2) current remains several orders of magnitude smaller than the SU(3) current.

## Method

The author solves the Dirac equation numerically on a real-time lattice for quarks propagating in classical color gauge fields, computing the one-point expectation value of the electromagnetic current operator. For uniform fields, analytic solutions via Abelianization (diagonalizing the color matrix) are used to interpret the numerical results, while for inhomogeneous fields an ensemble of randomly distributed color electric fluxtubes with Gaussian profiles is introduced to mimic the Glasma configuration from the color glass condensate framework. The photon spectrum from the induced current is then computed by solving the classical Maxwell equation coupled to the electromagnetic current.

## Key numbers

- For uniform SU(3) color fields with parameters \(m/Q_s=0.1\), the electromagnetic current shows a linear increase in time with a slope that depends on the color angle \(\theta\), varying by orders of magnitude between \(\theta=0\) (zero current) and other values
- In the Glasma-like inhomogeneous fields with \(Q_s L_x = Q_s L_y = 10\), the space-averaged squared electromagnetic current for SU(2) is several orders smaller than for SU(3)
- The photon number spectrum from SU(3) random fields at \(Q_s t=5\) shows an exponential behavior \(e^{-2.73 p_T/Q_s}\) fitted in the region \(1 < p_T/Q_s < 4\)
- The effective temperature extracted from the exponential photon spectrum is \(T_\text{eff} \simeq Q_s/3\)

## Context

This work extends the study of photon production in the Glasma (the initial state of heavy-ion collisions described by the color glass condensate framework) by providing a first-principles calculation of quark production and subsequent electromagnetic current induction, going beyond simplified models used in earlier work by Chiu (2012) and McLerran (2014). The finding that SU(2) and SU(3) theories give qualitatively different results for electromagnetic observables contradicts the expectation from Berges (2008) that classical gauge field dynamics show no qualitative difference between the two gauge groups. This enables more realistic calculations of early-time photon emission in heavy-ion collisions and suggests that electromagnetic observables may be more sensitive to color field configurations than hadronic observables.
