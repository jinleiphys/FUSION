# Mass Effect on Axial Charge Dynamics

> We studied effect of finite quark mass on the dynamics of axial charge using the D3/D7 model in holography. The mass term in axial anomaly equation affects both the fluctuation (generation) and dissipation of axial charge. We studied the dependence of the effect on quark mass and external magnetic field. For axial charge generation, we calculated the mass diffusion rate, which characterizes the helicity flipping rate. The rate is a non-monotonous function of mass and can be significantly enhanced by the magnetic field. The diffusive behavior is also related to a divergent susceptibility of axial charge. For axial charge dissipation, we found that in the long time limit, the mass term dissipates all the charge effectively generated by parallel electric and magnetic fields. The result is consistent with a relaxation time approximation. The rate of dissipation through mass term is a monotonous increasing function of both quark mass and magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper shows that finite quark mass affects axial charge dynamics in two ways, generation via a diffusive mass operator and dissipation, but the dissipation result is conditional on the existence of an adjoint reservoir (the D3 brane sector) that acts as a perfect sink, so that in the hydrodynamic limit all axial charge generated by parallel electric and magnetic fields is dissipated through the mass term, leaving no net axial charge.

## Method

The authors use the D3/D7 holographic model in the probe limit (quenched approximation) with a finite-temperature black hole background, turning on a constant magnetic field and studying fluctuations of the D7 brane embedding angle φ and the worldvolume gauge field A_z. They compute retarded correlators of the mass operator O_η and the vector current J^z in the low-frequency (hydrodynamic) limit, extracting the mass diffusion rate Γ_m from the imaginary part of the O_η correlator and the mass dissipation rate r from the ratio O_η/(𝒩 E·B) under parallel electric and magnetic fields.

## Key numbers

* Mass diffusion rate at B=0: Γ_m = (𝒩/π)(r_0²/2)² 8 cos³θ_h sin²θ_h, where θ_h is the embedding angle at the horizon; this rate is bounded and non-monotonous in quark mass.
* At small mass and B=0, the mass diffusion rate scales as Γ_m ∼ m² (linear behavior in the Γ_m vs. m² plot).
* The mass dissipation rate r = 1 − cos⁴θ_h; it is a monotonically increasing function of both quark mass m and magnetic field B, with r → 0 as m → 0.
* The dynamical susceptibility χ defined via CME diverges as χ ∼ O(ω⁻¹) in the static limit ω → 0.

## Context

This work extends holographic studies of axial anomaly effects beyond the massless quark approximation, which is relevant because the strange quark mass is comparable to QGP temperatures at RHIC and LHC. It builds on the D3/D7 framework of Mateos (2006) and Hoyos (2011) for realizing axial symmetry breaking, and complements studies of QCD anomaly effects by Fukushima (2010), Jimenez-Alba (2014), and Iatrakis (2014, 2015). The mass dissipation result is consistent with the relaxation time approximation used by Jimenez-Alba (2015) and Sun (2016). The findings enable quantitative modeling of axial charge dynamics with realistic quark masses and magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions.
