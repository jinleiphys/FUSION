# The contribution of hard processes to elliptic flow

> I study a possible effect of momentum deposition from many hard partons traversing the hot and dense region produced early in nuclear collisions at the LHC. The expected number of such hard partons is large. It is argued that the induced diffusion wakes which carry the momentum deposited to the medium may interact. Due to azimuthally asymmetric geometry in non-central collisions, this may lead to small preference of the collective flow in the direction of the impact parameter. As a result, a small contribution to the azimuthal asymmetry of hadronic spectra is obtained. It may be important to take even such a small contribution into account if quantitative conclusions about early thermalisation and low viscosity are to be made, based on the measurements of the elliptic flow parameter v2.

# The contribution of hard processes to elliptic flow

## Key claim

The paper argues that momentum deposition from many hard partons traversing the hot dense medium in non-central LHC collisions may produce a small positive contribution to the elliptic flow parameter \(v_2\), but the author explicitly states the numerical results were obtained with a "very simple toy model" and that the jet multiplicities are "most probably overestimated" due to extrapolation of cross-section parametrisations to lower transverse energies.

## Method

The author constructs a toy model in which diffusion wakes from stopped hard partons are represented as "blobs" of matter moving at 0.999\(c\) in isotropically distributed directions, with initial positions weighted by the nuclear overlap function. Blobs merge upon contact conserving energy and momentum, and when no further mergers occur, they evaporate pions according to a thermal distribution at 170 MeV. The number of blobs is calculated from parametrised nucleon-nucleon cross sections for jet production, integrated over pseudorapidity \([-2.5, 2.5]\) and extrapolated down to a minimum transverse energy \(E_m = 4\) GeV, with blob radius set to 2.5 fm.

## Key numbers

- The parametrised differential cross section for hard parton production in transverse energy is \(d\sigma_{NN}/dE_T = 8.3385 \times 10^8 \, (E_T / 1\,\text{GeV})^{-4.29717}\) \(\mu\)b/GeV, integrated over pseudorapidity \([-2.5, 2.5]\).
- The pseudorapidity distribution is proportional to \(1 - 0.067017 \eta^2\) over the interval \([-2.5, 2.5]\).
- The formation time of the stream is chosen as \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\).
- The blob velocity is fixed at 0.999\(c\).
- The pion evaporation temperature is 170 MeV.
- The minimum transverse energy for jets is \(E_m = 4\) GeV.
- The blob radius is 2.5 fm.
- For centrality 0–10% (impact parameter \(b = 2.8\) fm), the average number of minijets \(N_j = 14174\).
- For centrality 10–30% (\(b = 5.9\) fm), \(N_j = 7820\).
- For centrality 30–50% (\(b = 8.4\) fm), \(N_j = 3330\).
- For centrality 50–75% (\(b = 10.5\) fm), \(N_j = 933\).
- For centrality 75–100% (\(b = 12.4\) fm), \(N_j = 56\).
- The simulated \(v_2\) from this mechanism is "just slightly less than 1%".

## Context

This work extends the discussion of elliptic flow beyond the standard hydrodynamic interpretation based on pressure gradients in an almond-shaped fireball, which at RHIC requires fast thermalisation (Heinz, 2001; Pratt, 2008; Broniowski, 2008) and very low shear viscosity (Teaney, 2003). The paper proposes that at LHC energies, the large number of hard partons and their diffusion wakes (studied by Stoecker, 2004; Satarov, 2005; Casalderrey-Solana, 2004; Ruppert, 2005; Betz, 2008) may produce an additional geometric contribution to \(v_2\) that cannot be tagged as non-flow. The author calls for proper hydrodynamic simulations with source terms to replace the toy model, and notes that improved parametrisations of hard parton production are being developed.
