# Relativistic spin sum rules and the role of the pivot

> Spin sum rules depend on the choice of a pivot, i.e. the point about which the angular momentun is defined, usually identified with the center of the nucleon. The latter is however not unique in a relativistic theory and has led to apparently contradictory results in the literature. Using the recently developed phase-space approach, we compute for the first time the contribution associated with the motion of the center of the nucleon, and we derive a general spin sum rule which reduces to established results after appropriate choices for the pivot and the spin component.

## Key claim

The old results (Bakker 2004, Leader 2011) and the new results (Ji 2020) for transverse spin sum rules are both correct; they simply refer to different definitions for the "center" of the nucleon, and hence for what is meant by "spin" or "internal angular momentum," with the contribution from the center-of-mass motion computed explicitly for the first time using the phase-space approach.

## Method

The author adopts the phase-space (Wigner distribution) approach developed in earlier work (Lorce 2017, 2018) to compute expectation values of operators of the form ∫ d³r rⁱ O(r) for a nucleon described by a wave packet. This method separates the contribution from the center of the wave packet from the internal structure contribution, allowing explicit identification of the center-of-mass motion term. The formalism is applied to the Belinfante energy-momentum tensor and the Poincaré generators to derive a general spin sum rule that reduces to established results after appropriate choices for the pivot (center of energy, center of mass, or center of spin) and the spin component.

## Key numbers

- The expectation value of the canonical spin component along the polarization vector for a spin-1/2 momentum eigenstate is exactly 1/2: s·S_c |p,s⟩ = 1/2 |p,s⟩.
- The shift between the relativistic centers of energy and mass is given by R_E − R_M = (P × W)/(P⁰ M²), where W is the Pauli-Lubanski pseudovector.
- Four-momentum conservation imposes the constraints ∑_a A_a(0) = 1 and ∑_a C̄_a(0) = 0 on the energy-momentum tensor form factors.

## Context

This work resolves an apparent contradiction between earlier transverse spin sum rules (Bakker 2004, Leader 2011) and more recent results (Ji 2020) by clarifying that different definitions of the nucleon's "center" lead to different spin operators. The paper extends the phase-space formalism developed by Lorce (2017, 2018) and the discussion of relativistic centers (Pryce 1948, Moller 1949, Fleming 1965, Lorce 2018) to the problem of spin sum rules. It enables a unified treatment of longitudinal and transverse spin decompositions and clarifies the role of the pivot choice in relativistic angular momentum discussions.
