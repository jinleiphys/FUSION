# Bethe-Salpeter-Motivated Modelling of Pseudo-Goldstone Pseudoscalar Mesons

> We apply a description of bound states of fermion and antifermion by means of our approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter formalism that retains part of the information on relativistic effects provided by the full fermion propagator to the lightest pseudoscalar mesons. Therein, the pseudo-Goldstone nature of the latter quark-antiquark bound states is taken into account by appropriately formulated effective interactions. Scrutinizing the predictions of this bound-state approach for meson masses, decay constants and in-meson condensates by relying on a generalized Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation shows that the light-quark-mass values required for agreement are all in the right ballpark.

# Bethe-Salpeter-Motivated Modelling of Pseudo-Goldstone Pseudoscalar Mesons

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, retaining relativistic effects from the full fermion propagator and incorporating Fierz-symmetric effective interactions, can reproduce the masses, decay constants, and in-meson condensates of the lightest pseudoscalar mesons with light-quark-mass values that are "all in the right ballpark", but only when the pseudo-Goldstone nature of these mesons is explicitly accounted for via the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation.

## Method

The author applies an instantaneous (Salpeter-equation-generalizing) reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation that keeps only terms linear in the time-component of momentum in the fermion propagators. The Dirac structure of the interaction kernel is fixed by requiring Fierz invariance, yielding a single Lorentz-scalar potential function V(r). For the chiral limit (vanishing meson mass), the bound-state equations decouple, allowing inversion to extract the interquark potential from the known chiral-limit quark propagator obtained from Dyson-Schwinger equation solutions. This potential is then used to solve the eigenvalue problem for nonzero meson masses, with results checked against a generalized Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation linking meson mass, decay constant, in-meson condensate, and quark mass.

## Key numbers

- Chiral-limit pseudoscalar meson mass: 6.8 MeV, with decay constant 151 MeV and in-meson condensate 0.585 GeV², requiring quark mass 0.0059 MeV
- u/d quark pseudoscalar meson mass: 148.6 MeV, with decay constant 155 MeV and in-meson condensate 0.598 GeV², requiring quark mass 2.85 MeV (compared to current-quark mass 3.5^{+0.5}_{-0.2} MeV at 2 GeV)
- s quark pseudoscalar meson mass: 620.7 MeV, with decay constant 211 MeV and in-meson condensate 0.799 GeV², requiring quark mass 51.0 MeV (compared to current-quark mass 95^{+9}_{-3} MeV at 2 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation (Lucha, several prior papers) by applying it specifically to pseudo-Goldstone pseudoscalar mesons, where chiral symmetry breaking is essential. The approach uses quark propagator solutions from Maris and Tandy (1999) based on QCD-inspired Dyson-Schwinger equation ansatze. The results enable a simplified but relativistic treatment of light meson properties that connects Bethe-Salpeter dynamics to the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation (Gell-Mann, Oakes, and Renner, 1968), and can be compared to other recent Bethe-Salpeter calculations (e.g., Gomez-Rocha, Higuera, and Kohlfürst; Chen; all circa 2013-2018).
