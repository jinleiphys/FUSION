# Stability of the pion string in a thermal and dense medium

> We investigate the stability of the pion string in a thermal bath and a dense medium. We find that stability is dependent on the order of the chiral transition. String core stability within the experimentally allowed regime is found only if the chiral transition is second order, and even there the stable region is small, i.e., the temperature below which the core is unstable is close to the critical temperature of the phase transition. We also find that the presence of a dense medium, in addition to the thermal bath, enhances the experimentally accessible region with stable strings. We also argue that once the string core decays, the "effective winding" of the string persists at large distances from the string core. Our analysis is done both in the chiral limit, which is mainly what has been explored in the literature up to now, and for the physical $h \ne 0$ case, where a conceptual framework is set up for addressing this regime and some simple estimates are done.

## Key claim

Pion string core stability in a thermal and dense medium is possible only if the chiral phase transition is second order, and even then the stable region is small, with the temperature below which the core is unstable being close to the critical temperature of the phase transition. The presence of a dense medium (nonzero chemical potential) enhances the experimentally accessible region with stable strings compared to a purely thermal bath.

## Method

The authors study the linear sigma model with quarks (LSMq) at finite temperature and chemical potential, using a one-loop effective potential for the fermions while neglecting meson quantum and thermal fluctuations in a mean-field (Hartree) approximation. They derive the modified equations of motion for the pion string solution including thermal photon interactions (giving a thermal mass to charged pions) and fermion interactions (giving scalar and pseudoscalar densities), then analyze the sign of the energy variation under infinitesimal charged pion perturbations to determine core stability. The analysis is performed both in the chiral limit (h=0) and for the physical case (h ≠ 0), where the latter is treated with a conceptual framework and simple estimates.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant in vacuum: fπ = 93 MeV.
- Pion mass in vacuum (physical case): mπ,0 = 139 MeV.
- Sigma mass in vacuum: mσ = 600 MeV.
- Constituent quark mass in vacuum: mq = 300 MeV.
- Critical point in the chiral limit: T = 50 MeV and μq = 306 MeV, separating first-order from second-order phase transitions.
- Second-order critical temperature at μq = 0 in the chiral limit: Tc = 176.0 MeV.
- First-order critical temperature at μq = 322 MeV in the chiral limit: Tc = 11.5 MeV.
- Pseudocritical temperature at μq = 0 in the physical case: Tpc = 172 MeV.
- Pseudocritical chemical potential at T = 0 in the physical case: μq,pc = 329 MeV.
- Stability condition derived: e²T²/4 > λ v²(T, μq), where v(T, μq) is the temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent sigma field expectation value.

## Context

This work extends the stabilization mechanism for the pion string proposed by Nagasawa and Brandenberger (1999), who showed that a thermal bath of photons can stabilize the string via a thermal mass for charged pions, and later refined by Karouby and Brandenberger (2012, 2013). The present paper adds the effect of a dense medium (nonzero quark chemical potential) and includes fermion interactions, making the model more realistic than earlier studies by Mao (2004) and Lu (2015). The results enable future searches for stable pion strings in heavy-ion collision experiments and in the early Universe, particularly by identifying the region in the (μq, T) plane where stable strings could exist.
