# Exclusive Electro-Disintegration of 3He at high Q2: II. Decay Function Formalism

> Based on the theoretical framework of generalized eikonal approximation we study the two-nucleon emission reactions in high $Q^2$ electro-disintegration of $^3He$. Main aim is to investigate those features of the reaction which can be unambiguously identified with the short range properties of the ground state nuclear wave function. To evaluate the differential cross section we work in the formalism of the decay function which characterizes the property of the ground state wave function as well as the decay properties of final two nucleon spectator system. Our main motivation here is to explore the accessibility of two-- and three--nucleon short range correlations in $^3$He as well as to isolate unambiguously single and double rescattering processes in the reaction dynamics. Our analysis allowed us also to identify new approaches for investigating the role of the practically unknown three-nucleon forces in the ground state wave function of $^3He$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that exclusive two-nucleon emission in high-\(Q^2\) electro-disintegration of \(^3\)He can be used to isolate short-range two- and three-nucleon correlations, but only if one carefully controls for final-state interactions (FSI) and pair-distortion effects, which can mimic or obscure the correlation signals. The authors hedge that the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) and virtual nucleon framework are reliably applied only for recoil momenta up to 400–500 MeV/c, though they extend exploratory studies to 700–800 MeV/c and 1 GeV/c for double-rescattering kinematics.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section for the \(^3\)He\((e,e'NN)N\) reaction within the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA), expressing the scattering amplitude as a sum of impulse approximation (IA), single-rescattering (FSI1), and double-rescattering (FSI2) terms. They introduce a nuclear decay function \(D^N(\mathbf{p}_m, E_m, \mathbf{p}_{r2})\) that generalizes the spectral function to two-nucleon emission, and they compute it in both plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) using the Bochum group's Faddeev-based \(^3\)He wave functions, SAID parameterizations for low-energy NN scattering, and a high-energy NN amplitude parameterization fitted to published data.

## Key numbers

- Momentum range studied: recoil momenta \(|\mathbf{p}_{r2}|, |\mathbf{p}_{r3}|, |\mathbf{p}_m|\) up to 700–800 MeV/c, and up to 1 GeV/c for double-rescattering-dominated kinematics.
- \(Q^2\) range: \(4 \ge Q^2 \ge 1\) GeV\(^2\).
- Fermi momentum of nuclear system: \(k_F \approx 250\) MeV/c.
- Pair distortion suppresses the spectral function by a factor of five at small \(p_m\) and \(E_m\) (e.g., at \(p_m \approx 0\), \(E_m \approx 0\)).
- The node in the \(S\)-state of the \(pp\) relative momentum distribution occurs at approximately 420 MeV/c.
- Characteristic average momenta transferred during NN rescattering: \(\langle k^2\rangle, \langle k_2^2\rangle, \langle k_3^2\rangle \le 2/B \sim 250\) (MeV/c)\(^2\).
- For \(Q^2 \ge 2\) GeV\(^2\), the charge-exchange part of the NN amplitude is estimated to contribute only a few percent to the rescattering amplitude.

## Context

This work extends the GEA formalism developed in Part I (Sargsian et al., 2005) for \(^3\)He electro-disintegration, building on earlier spectral function analyses by Ciofi degli Atti (1990s) and the decay function concept introduced by Frankfurt and Strikman (1988). The numerical results use the Bochum group's Faddeev wave functions (based on Urbana-IX and other potentials) and SAID NN scattering amplitudes. The paper identifies kinematic conditions (e.g., the \(p_Z\) condition of Eq. (pZ)) to suppress FSI and enable clean access to short-range correlations, and it suggests that exclusive \((e,e'NN)\) reactions can provide a new probe of three-nucleon forces in \(^3\)He, which are otherwise masked by pair-distortion effects in inclusive spectral functions.
