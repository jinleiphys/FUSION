# Ground-state properties of closed-shell nuclei with low-momentum realistic interactions

> Ground-state properties of 16O and 40Ca are calculated with a low-momentum nucleon-nucleon potential, V-low-k, derived from the chiral N3LO interaction recently constructed by Entem and Machleidt. The smooth V-low-k is used directly in a Hartree-Fock approach, avoiding the difficulties of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock procedure. Corrections up to third order in the Goldstone expansion are evaluated, leading to results that are in very good agreement with experiment. Convergence properties of the expansion are examined.

# Ground-state properties of closed-shell nuclei with low-momentum realistic interactions

## Key claim
Using the low-momentum potential V_low-k derived from the chiral N3LO interaction directly in a Hartree-Fock plus Goldstone expansion up to third order yields binding energies and charge radii for 16O and 40Ca that are in very good agreement with experiment, provided the cutoff momentum is chosen around 2.1 fm^-1 and the harmonic-oscillator basis includes up to N=5 major shells.

## Method
The authors first construct the V_low-k potential from the chiral N3LO interaction of Entem and Machleidt by integrating out high-momentum components above a cutoff Lambda = 2.1 fm^-1 using the Andreozzi-Lee-Suzuki iterative method, preserving the deuteron binding energy and low-energy phase shifts. They then solve the Hartree-Fock equations for 16O and 40Ca in a harmonic-oscillator basis expanded up to N=5 major shells, using the resulting self-consistent field as the auxiliary potential. Finally, they evaluate the Goldstone expansion including first-order (HF), second-order, and third-order diagrams to obtain the ground-state energy and charge radius.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy per nucleon for 16O: HF = 3.23 MeV, HF+2nd = 7.22 MeV, HF+2nd+3rd = 7.52 MeV, experiment = 7.98 MeV
- Charge radius for 16O: HF = 2.30 fm, HF+2nd = 2.52 fm, HF+2nd+3rd = 2.65 fm, experiment = 2.73 +/- 0.02 fm
- Binding energy per nucleon for 40Ca: HF = 6.19 MeV, HF+2nd = 9.10 MeV, HF+2nd+3rd = 9.19 MeV, experiment = 8.55 MeV
- Charge radius for 40Ca: HF = 2.610 fm, HF+2nd = 3.302 fm, HF+2nd+3rd = 3.444 fm, experiment = 3.485 +/- 0.003 fm
- Cutoff momentum Lambda = 2.1 fm^-1; varying from 2.0 to 2.2 fm^-1 changes the second-order binding energy per nucleon for 16O from 7.29 to 7.12 MeV
- Ratio of second-order to first-order potential energy: 0.17 for 16O, 0.09 for 40Ca; ratio of third-order to second-order: 0.08 for 16O, 0.03 for 40Ca

## Context
This work extends the V_low-k approach previously applied to shell-model calculations for few-valence-particle nuclei (Coraggio et al., 2002) to the ground-state properties of doubly closed-shell nuclei 16O and 40Ca. It contrasts with the traditional Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach, which requires calculating the G-matrix and typically yields insufficient binding and too small charge radii (Schmid, Muther, and Machleidt, 1991). The method is similar in spirit to early work using the Tabakin separable potential in HF calculations (Kerman, Svenne, and Villars, 1966; Bassichis, Kerman, and Svenne, 1967), but achieves significantly better agreement with experiment. The results enable direct use of low-momentum potentials in many-body calculations without the complications of the G-matrix approach, and the demonstrated rapid convergence suggests that higher-order contributions beyond third order are negligible.
