# A quantum kinetic equation for Fermi-systems including three-body correlations

> A single-time quantum transport equation, which includes effects beyond the quasiparticle approximation, is derived for Fermi-systems in the framework of non-equilibrium real-time Green's functions theory. Ternary correlations are incorporated in the kinetic description via a cluster expansion for the self-energies (e.g., the transport vertex and the width) truncated at the level of three-body scattering amplitudes. A finite temperature/density formulation of the three-body problem is given. Corresponding three-body equations reduce to the well-known Faddeev equations in the vacuum limit. In equilibrium the equation of state contains virial corrections proportional to the third quantum virial coefficient.

## Key claim

The paper derives a single-time quantum transport equation for Fermi-systems that includes three-body correlations beyond the quasiparticle approximation, but the derivation is restricted to next-to-leading order in an expansion of the spectral function with respect to its width, and the three-body collision integrals are constructed to be free of singularities by using Faddeev-type amplitudes rather than Born approximation.

## Method

The authors start from the Kadanoff-Baym real-time Green's functions formalism and expand the single-particle spectral function to next-to-leading order in the spectral width, decomposing all diagrams into quasiparticle (on-mass-shell) and correlated (off-mass-shell) parts via a cluster expansion for the self-energies. They then derive coupled single-time kinetic equations for the on-shell quasiparticle distribution function and the off-shell correlated distribution function, and formulate the three-body problem at finite temperature and density by writing the three-body scattering amplitude in terms of Faddeev-type equations that reduce to the standard Faddeev equations in the vacuum limit.

## Key numbers

- The kinetic equation is derived for Fermi-systems with two-body forces, with the free single-particle spectrum given by ε_p = p²/2m.
- The spectral function expansion is carried out to first order in the width γ(p,x), with the spectral sum rule ∫(dω/2π) a(p,x) = 1 fulfilled to any order in the expansion.
- The two-particle propagator in the particle-particle channel is expanded to order O(γ²), with the leading term containing the factor [1 - f(ε₊,x) - f(ε₋,x)] for the on-mass-shell propagation.
- The equilibrium equation of state contains virial corrections proportional to the third quantum virial coefficient, though no explicit numerical value for this coefficient is given in the text.

## Context

This work extends the Kadanoff-Baym kinetic theory (Kadanoff and Baym, 1962) and the Keldysh formalism (Keldysh, 1965) to include three-body correlations, building on earlier studies of virial corrections by Cohen (1963), Snyder (1971), and the density-matrix approach of Röpke and Schulz (1986). It also generalizes the finite-temperature three-body problem treated by Bezzerides and DuBois (1972) in Born approximation, replacing their singular collision integrals with well-behaved Faddeev-type amplitudes (Faddeev, 1960; AGS, 1964). The work enables kinetic descriptions of nuclear matter in heavy-ion collisions at ~100 MeV/nucleon and astrophysical contexts such as supernova collapse and proto-neutron stars, where bound-state formation (deuterons, tritons, alpha particles) and off-mass-shell propagation effects become important.
