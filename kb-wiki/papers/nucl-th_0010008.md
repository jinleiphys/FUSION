# Strangeness Equilibration at GSI Energies

> We develop the notion of "broad-band equilibration" in heavy-ion processes involving dense medium. Given density-dependent \Km-masses we show that the equilibration at GSI energies claimed to hold in previous treatments down to $\sim ρ_0/4$, can be replaced by a broad-band equilibration in which the \Km-meson and hyperons are produced in an essentially constant ratio independent of density. There are experimental indications that this also holds for AGS energies. We then proceed to argue that {\it both} $K^+$ and $K^-$ must get lighter in dense medium at some density $ρ>ρ_0$ due to the decoupling of the vector mesons. As a consequence, kaon condensation in compact stars could take place {\it before} chiral restoration since the sum of bare quark masses in the kaon should lie below $μ_e$. Another consequence of the decoupling vector interactions is that the quasi-particle picture involving (quasi)quarks, presumably ineffective at low densities, becomes more appropriate at higher densities as chiral restoration is approached.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the apparent strangeness equilibration observed at GSI energies, previously attributed to a low-density freeze-out at approximately rho_0/4, can instead be explained by a "broad-band equilibration" in which the ratio of K- mesons to hyperons remains nearly constant over a wide range of densities, due to the near cancellation between the dropping K- effective mass and the increasing baryon chemical potential.

## Method

The authors develop a theoretical framework combining density-dependent kaon masses from chiral Lagrangian approaches with a "top-down" description using constituent quark degrees of freedom, where vector meson couplings to kaons are taken as one-third of the nucleon-omega coupling. They show that the sum mu_B + omega_K- (the K- energy at rest) remains approximately constant between 1230 MeV and 1227 MeV as density increases from 1.2 rho_0 to 2.1 rho_0, which keeps the K+/K- ratio insensitive to density. The analysis uses a schematic model with the effective kaon mass given by m_K*^2 approximately equal to m_K^2 times (1 - rho (Sigma_KN)_eff / (f^2 m_K^2)), with Sigma_KN approximately 400 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The K+/K- ratio measured in Ni+Ni experiments is approximately 30.
- At rho = 1.2 rho_0, mu_B = 860 MeV and omega_K- = 370 MeV, giving mu_B + omega_K- = 1230 MeV; at rho = 2.1 rho_0, mu_B = 905 MeV and omega_K- = 322 MeV, giving mu_B + omega_K- = 1227 MeV.
- The ratio n_K- / n_(Lambda+Sigma+Xi) is approximately 1/32, yielding n_K+/n_K- approximately 33.
- At rho approximately 3 rho_0, the schematic model gives m_K-* approximately 230 MeV, less than half the bare mass.
- The vector form factor cutoff is at R_cutoff approximately 0.5 fm, corresponding to Lambda_V = 0.9 GeV.
- The electron chemical potential in neutron-star matter at rho approximately 3 rho_0 is mu_e approximately 214 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the thermal model analysis of Cleymans, Oeschler and Redlich (1999), who found that particle ratios at GSI energies could be described with a common freeze-out temperature and chemical potential but required a diffuse system at density approximately rho_0/4. The authors contradict the equilibration time estimate of approximately 40 fm/c by Bratkovskaya et al. (1999) by showing that medium effects on kaon masses dramatically change the equilibration dynamics. The paper enables a revised understanding of kaon condensation in neutron stars, suggesting it could occur before chiral restoration if the sum of bare quark masses in the kaon lies below the electron chemical potential, and motivates further transport calculations incorporating density-dependent kaon masses and vector decoupling.
