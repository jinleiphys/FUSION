# Note on fast spinning neutron stars with unified equations of states

> For the propose of confronting updated pulsar observations with developed neutron star equation of states (EoSs), we employ four unified EoSs for both the core and the crust, namely BCPM, BSk20, BSk21, Shen-TM1, as well as two non-unified EoSs widely used in the literature, i.e. APR and GM1 EoS, which are commonly matched with the Negele-Vautherin and the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland crust EoS. All the core EoSs satisfy the recent observational constraints of the two massive pulsars whose masses are precisely measured. We show that the NS mass-equatorial radius relations are slightly affected by the smoothness at the core-crust matching interface. Moreover, the uncertainties in the crust EoS and the matching interface bring insignificant changes, even at maximally rotating (Keplerian) configurations. We also find that for all four unified EoSs, rotation can increase the star's gravitational mass up to $18\%-19\%$ and the equatorial radius by $29\%-36\%$, which are consistent with the previous calculations using non-unified EoSs. For stars as heavy as 1.4 M$_{\odot}$, the radius increase is more pronounced, reaching $41\%-43\%$, i.e. $5-6$ km. Moreover, by confronting the results using unified EoSs, which give the correct empirical values at saturation density, with two controversial determinations of the radius for the fast rotator 4U 1608-52, we address that a small radius may be better justified for this source.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for fast-spinning neutron stars, the choice of unified versus non-unified equations of state (EoSs) and the smoothness of the core-crust matching interface have only slight effects on the mass-radius relation, even at maximally rotating (Keplerian) configurations; however, for the fast rotator 4U 1608-52, the authors argue that a small radius (below 13 km) may be better justified when using unified EoSs that give correct empirical values at saturation density.

## Method

The authors employed four unified EoSs (BCPM, BSk20, BSk21, Shen-TM1) covering both core and crust, plus two non-unified EoSs (APR and GM1) matched with Negele-Vautherin and Baym-Pethick-Sutherland crust EoSs. They computed stationary and equilibrium sequences of rapidly rotating relativistic stars in general relativity using the rns code, assuming the star matter to be a perfect fluid, for both static and Keplerian rotating configurations.

## Key numbers

- Rotation increases gravitational mass by 18%–19% for all four unified EoSs
- Rotation increases equatorial radius by 29%–36% for all four unified EoSs
- For stars of 1.4 M☉, radius increase reaches 41%–43%, corresponding to 5–6 km
- Maximum gravitational masses in Keplerian rotation: BCPM 2.34 M☉, BSk20 2.58 M☉, BSk21 2.73 M☉, Shen-TM1 2.60 M☉, APR* 2.61 M☉
- Keplerian frequencies range from 1333 Hz (Shen-TM1) to 1940 Hz (APR*)
- For BSk21 EoS, a spin frequency of 620 Hz increases radius from 11.03 km to 11.29 km (less than 3%)
- Saturation density properties: BCPM has n0=0.16 fm⁻³, E/A=-16.00 MeV, K=213.75 MeV, Esym=31.92 MeV, L=52.96 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies on unified EoSs (e.g., Sharma et al. 2015, Li et al. 2016) by examining their effects on rapidly rotating neutron stars up to the Keplerian limit, and addresses the controversy between two radius determinations for the fast rotator 4U 1608-52: one analysis (Ozel et al. 2016) giving 6.8–12.6 km and another (Güver et al. 2016) favoring radii above 13 km. The results enable better interpretation of observations from NICER, eXTP, and other upcoming missions, and the authors note that new EoSs consistent with both two-solar-mass constraints and the GW170817 tidal deformability are in preparation.
