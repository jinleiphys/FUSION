# Comparison of Gamow-Teller strengths in the random phase approximation

> The Gamow-Teller response is astrophysically important for a number of nuclides, particularly around iron. The random phase approximation (RPA) is an efficient way to generate strength distributions. In order to better understand both theoretical systematics and uncertainties, we compare the Gamow-Teller strength distributions for a suite of nuclides and for a suite of interactions, including semi-realistic interactions in the $1p$-$0f$ space with the RPA and a separable multi-shell interaction in the quasi-particle RPA. We also compare with experimental results for GT$_-$ on ${54}$Fe.

## Key claim

The paper systematically compares Gamow-Teller strength distributions from several random phase approximation (RPA) models to quantify theoretical uncertainties, finding that the quasi-particle RPA (QRPA) with a separable interaction places the strength centroid at lower energies than shell-model RPA interactions, while the GXPF1 interaction places it highest; the authors note that all calculations are limited to ground-state parents and use a quenching factor of 0.6, which differs from the 0.55 used in some shell-model comparisons.

## Method

The authors performed proton-neutron RPA (pn-RPA) calculations in the 1p-0f harmonic oscillator shell using three semi-realistic interactions (KB3G, FPD6G, GXPF1) and pn-QRPA calculations in a multi-shell space (up to 7 hbar-omega shells) with a separable particle-hole and particle-particle Gamow-Teller force, for the nuclides 54,55,56Fe and 56,58Ni. The pn-RPA calculations started from a Hartree-Fock mean field, while the pn-QRPA used a Nilsson deformed basis with BCS pairing, and all results were multiplied by a quenching factor of 0.6 before comparison with experimental data and shell-model results.

## Key numbers

* For 54Fe GT- strength, the pn-QRPA model gave a centroid at lower energy than other RPA models; the measured total strength from Adachi et al. (2012) was 4.00 +/- 0.37 up to 12 MeV in 54Co, while Rapaport et al. (1983) reported 7.8 +/- 1.9.
* For 56Ni GT- strength, the pn-QRPA model produced two distinct peaks at 5.7 MeV and 10.6 MeV in the daughter nucleus, while the FPD6, GXPF1, and KB3G interactions placed all strength at single energies of 10.6, 11.5, and 9.8 MeV, respectively.
* For 58Ni GT- centroid, the pn-QRPA model calculated approximately 5 MeV in 58Cu, compared to the experimental value of 6.9 MeV; the GXPF1 centroid was about three times the pn-QRPA value.
* For 58Ni GT+ centroid, the pn-QRPA model calculated 3.6 MeV compared to the measured centroid of 4.4 MeV; the total measured GT+ strength was 4.1 +/- 0.3 (Cole et al., 2006) and 4.0 (El-Kateb et al., 1994).
* For 56Fe GT- total strength, Rapaport et al. (1983) reported 9.9 +/- 2.4 up to 15 MeV in the daughter.

## Context

This work extends previous comparisons of RPA and shell-model Gamow-Teller strengths by Stetcu and Johnson (2004) to a suite of iron-region nuclides and multiple interactions, and it contrasts the 0-hbar-omega shell-model RPA calculations with multi-shell QRPA calculations used extensively for astrophysical rates by Nabi and collaborators (2005, 2008, 2009, 2011, 2012). The results enable better quantification of theoretical uncertainties in weak interaction rates for core-collapse supernova simulations and explosive nucleosynthesis, particularly by showing that the QRPA model's lower centroid placement would favor higher electron capture rates in stellar environments compared to the GXPF1 interaction.
