# Symmetry-restored Skyrme-Random-Phase-Approximation calculations of the monopole strength in deformed nuclei

> Within the Energy Density Functional (EDF) approach, the use of mean-field wave-functions deliberately breaking (some) symmetries of the underlying Hamiltonian is an efficient and largely utilized way to incorporate static correlations. However, the restoration of broken symmetries is eventually mandatory to recover the corresponding quantum numbers and to achieve a more precise description of nuclear properties. While symmetry-restored calculations are routinely performed to study ground-state properties and low-lying excitations, similar applications to the nuclear response are essentially limited to either formal studies or to schematic models. In the present paper, the effect of angular momentum restoration on the monopole and quadrupole responses of doubly open-shell nuclei is investigated. Based on deformed Skyrme-Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations, the exact Angular Momentum Projection (AMP) is implemented in the calculation of the multipole strength functions, thus defining a projection after variation (PAV-RPA) scheme. The method is employed for the first time in a realistic study to investigate the effect of AMP on the coupling of monopole and quadrupole modes in $^{24}$Mg resulting from its intrinsic deformation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that angular momentum projection after variation (PAV-RPA) applied to deformed Skyrme-RPA calculations produces an anomalous enhancement of low-energy monopole strength, which is traced to a contamination of RPA phonons by non-infinitesimal rotational motion rather than to genuine vibrational excitations; this spurious strength can be subtracted, but the work motivates future implementation of the full variation-after-projection (VAP-RPA) scheme.

## Method

The authors start from a deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (HF) solution for \(^{24}\)Mg obtained with the SkM* functional, then solve the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) in matrix form for \(K^\pi=0^+\) states. They implement exact Angular Momentum Projection (AMP) a posteriori in the computation of multipole strength functions, defining a projection-after-variation (PAV-RPA) scheme, and compute the reduced transition matrix elements for isoscalar monopole and quadrupole operators using the quasi-boson approximation to retain the backward (Y) amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The HF ground state of \(^{24}\)Mg has a quadrupole deformation \(\beta = 0.383\) (for \(N_{\text{sh}}=11\) shells) and a HF energy of \(-196.93\) MeV.
* The Isoscalar Giant Quadrupole Resonance (ISGQR) appears at 17.5 MeV in the quadrupole RPA strength.
* The Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance (ISGMR) displays a low-energy component near 17.5 MeV (coupled to the ISGQR) and a higher-energy component at 24 MeV.
* The overlap between the low-energy RPA state at 10.8 MeV and the rotated RPA ground state is maximal for a rotation angle \(\beta \approx \pm 24^\circ\).

## Context

This work extends the deformed Skyrme-RPA approach previously applied to Molybdenum isotopes (Colo 2020) by adding exact angular momentum projection, going beyond the "needle" approximation used in earlier studies (PhysRevC.77.034317, Peru 2014). The only prior exact AMP PAV-RPA implementation (Erler) gave unexplained results that the present paper now analyzes. The study is motivated by the longstanding puzzle of extracting the nuclear matter incompressibility \(K_\infty\) from the Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance in deformed nuclei, where monopole-quadrupole coupling complicates the interpretation. The spurious low-energy strength observed here was also seen in Projected Generator Coordinate Method (PGCM) calculations using a similar PAV approach but disappears in the full VAP counterpart, motivating future development of VAP-RPA.
