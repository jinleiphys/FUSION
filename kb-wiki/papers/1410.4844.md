# Azimuthal asymmetries and the emergence of "collectivity" from multi-particle correlations in high-energy pA collisions

> We show how angular asymmetries $\sim\cos 2ϕ$ can arise in dipole scattering at high energies. We illustrate the effects due to anisotropic fluctuations of the saturation momentum of the target with a finite correlation length in the transverse impact parameter plane, i.e.\ from a domain-like structure. We compute the two-particle azimuthal cumulant in this model including both one-particle factorizable as well as genuine two-particle non-factorizable contributions to the two-particle cross section. We also compute the full BBGKY hierarchy for the four-particle azimuthal cumulant and find that only the fully factorizable contribution to $c_2\{4\}$ is negative while all contributions from genuine two, three and four-particle correlations are positive. Our results may provide some qualitative insight into the origin of azimuthal asymmetries in p+Pb collisions at the LHC which reveal a change of sign of $c_2\{4\}$ in high-multiplicity events.

## Key claim

Angular asymmetries ∼cos 2φ in high-energy pA collisions can arise from anisotropic fluctuations of the target's saturation momentum with a finite correlation length in the transverse plane, without requiring hydrodynamic flow; the four-particle cumulant c₂{4} changes sign when the single-particle anisotropy amplitude 𝒜 exceeds ∼1/N_c^{3/2}, which is a parametric condition that may explain the sign change observed in high-multiplicity p+Pb events at the LHC.

## Method

The authors compute two- and four-particle azimuthal cumulants in a dipole model of high-energy scattering, where the S-matrix for a small dipole is expanded to leading order in the dipole size r ∼ 1/p⊥ as tr(ig r·E)². The target's color-electric field E is assumed to have a domain-like structure with Gaussian correlation length ξ in the impact-parameter plane, and the ensemble is divided into classes with a preferred anisotropy direction â and amplitude 𝒜. The cumulants are evaluated by averaging over E-field configurations within each class, then summing over â, using a Gaussian action for the field correlators and keeping both factorizable (disconnected) and genuine multi-particle (connected) contributions.

## Key numbers

- The two-particle cumulant c₂{2} = (v₂{2})² = (1/N_D)[𝒜² + 1/(4(N_c²−1))], where N_D = S⊥/(πξ²) is the effective number of domains; for N_c=3 and 𝒜≈0.2 the two terms are comparable.
- The four-particle cumulant c₂{4} = −(1/N_D³)[𝒜⁴ − 1/(4(N_c²−1)³)] when 𝒜 = O(N_c^{−3/2}); the fully connected contribution to c₂{4} is positive and equals (1/(4N_D³))·(1/(N_c²−1)³)·(1+8𝒜²).
- The transition from c₂{4}>0 to c₂{4}<0 occurs parametrically when 𝒜 ∼ 1/N_c^{3/2}, which is before the one-particle factorizable contribution dominates c₂{2} (the latter requires 𝒜 ∼ 1/N_c).
- The connected contribution to the two-particle cumulant is 1/(4(N_c²−1)) per domain, independent of 𝒜.

## Context

This work extends the idea that anisotropic fluctuations of the saturation momentum (Kovner and Lublinsky, 2006; Dumitru, McLerran, and Skokov, 2014) can generate azimuthal asymmetries in pA collisions, providing a QCD-based alternative to hydrodynamic explanations. It computes the full BBGKY-like hierarchy for the four-particle cumulant, showing that only the fully factorizable (disconnected) term gives a negative contribution to c₂{4}, which is the signature of "collectivity" observed by ALICE and CMS in high-multiplicity p+Pb collisions at √s=5 TeV. The results enable future studies of how multiplicity bias affects the anisotropy amplitude 𝒜 and how the transition to negative c₂{4} emerges from small-x QCD dynamics.
