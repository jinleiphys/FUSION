# Chiral Kinetic Theory

> We derive the non-equilibrium kinetic equation describing the motion of chiral massless particles in the regime where it can be considered classically. We show that the Berry monopole which appears at the origin of the momentum space due to level crossing is responsible for the chiral magnetic and vortical effects.

# Chiral Kinetic Theory

## Key claim

The paper derives a non-equilibrium kinetic equation for chiral massless particles in the regime where motion between collisions can be treated classically, and shows that the Berry curvature monopole at the origin of momentum space is responsible for both the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral vortical effect (CVE). The derivation assumes weak coupling and classical motion between collisions, with the classical description breaking down at momenta near zero where the quantum anomaly operates.

## Method

The authors start from the Weyl Hamiltonian H = σ·p and use a path integral formulation to obtain the classical action, diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in the helicity basis via a unitary transformation. They identify the Berry connection as a U(1) gauge field arising from abelian projection of the U(2) gauge freedom, compute the Berry curvature b = p̂/(2|p|²) which is the field of a monopole at p=0, and derive classical equations of motion including the Berry flux term. The kinetic equation is then obtained by substituting these equations into the Boltzmann equation with the collision integral set to zero, using the invariant phase-space measure √G d³x d³p/(2π)³ where G = (1 + b·B)².

## Key numbers

- Berry curvature: **b = p̂/(2|p|²)** (monopole field at p=0)
- Phase-space determinant: **G = (1 + b·B)²**
- Classical validity condition for magnetic field: **B ≪ |p|²** (forces must be weak compared to energy gap 2|p|)
- CME current (general non-equilibrium): **j_CME = (B/(4π²)) ∫₀^∞ f̄(E, p̂) dE**, where overbar denotes angular average
- CME current (Fermi-Dirac, zero temperature): **j_CME = μ B/(2π)²**
- CVE current (general non-equilibrium): **j_CVE = (ω/(4π²)) ∫₀^∞ f̄(E, p̂) 2E dE**
- CVE current (Fermi-Dirac, zero temperature): **j_CVE = μ² ω/(2π)²**
- Anomaly equation: **∂n/∂t + ∇·j = (1/(4π²)) E·B f₀**, where f₀ is the distribution at p=0

## Context

This work extends the understanding of chiral transport effects, previously derived mostly under thermal and chemical equilibrium assumptions in hydrodynamics (Son 2009) and gauge-gravity duality (Erdmenger 2008, Banerjee 2008), to non-equilibrium conditions relevant for heavy-ion collisions (Fukushima 2008, 2010). The kinetic approach builds on Berry phase concepts (Berry 1984), classical equations of motion with Berry curvature (Sundaram and Niu 1999, Xiao et al. 2010), and recent kinetic theory developments (Wong 2011, Son and Yamamoto 2012, Loganayagam 2012). The results enable investigation of chiral transport effects in the highly non-equilibrium early stages of heavy-ion collisions where magnetic fields are still strong.
