# Fully self-consistent relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory for finite nuclei

> Starting from the relativistic form of the Bonn potential as a bare nucleon-nucleon interaction, the full Relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) equations are solved for finite nuclei in a fully self-consistent basis. This provides a relativistic ab initio calculation of the ground state properties of finite nuclei without any free parameters and without three-body forces. The convergence properties for the solutions of these coupled equations are discussed in detail at the example of the nucleus $^{16}$O. The binding energies, radii, and spin-orbit splittings of the doubly magic nuclei $^{4}$He, $^{16}$O, and $^{40}$Ca are calculated and compared with the earlier RBHF calculated results in a fixed Dirac Woods-Saxon basis and other non-relativistic ab initio calculated results based on pure two-body forces.

## Key claim

Starting from the bare Bonn potential without any free parameters or three-body forces, the authors solve the full Relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equations for finite nuclei in a fully self-consistent basis, going beyond their earlier work where the Bethe-Goldstone equation was solved in a fixed Dirac Woods-Saxon basis.

## Method

The coupled system of the Bethe-Goldstone equation and the relativistic Hartree-Fock equation is solved iteratively in a spherical Dirac Woods-Saxon basis, with the single-particle wave functions and energies updated at each iteration until self-consistency is achieved. The G-matrix is obtained by solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation in the particle-particle coupled channels with quantum numbers J, parity, and isospin, using the Bonn potential as the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. Center-of-mass corrections are treated with both projection after variation and projection before variation methods.

## Key numbers

- For $^{16}$O, convergence is achieved with an energy cut-off close to 1.1 GeV
- Binding energies, radii, and spin-orbit splittings are calculated for $^{4}$He, $^{16}$O, and $^{40}$Ca
- Near saturation density, the continuous choice produces several MeV per nucleon more binding than the gap choice at the BHF level

## Context

This work extends the earlier RBHF calculation for finite nuclei by Shen (2016), where the Bethe-Goldstone equation was solved in a fixed Dirac Woods-Saxon basis, by making the basis fully self-consistent. It provides a relativistic ab initio alternative to non-relativistic approaches such as the coupled-cluster method (Hagen 2014), the no core shell model (Barrett 2013), and the self-consistent Green's function method (Dickhoff 2004), all of which typically require three-body forces. The fully self-consistent RBHF framework enables future applications to heavier nuclei and systematic comparisons with other ab initio methods based on pure two-body forces.
