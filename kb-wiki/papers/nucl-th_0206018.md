# Anisotropic multi-gap superfluid states in nuclear matter

> It is shown that under changing density or temperature a nucleon Fermi superfluid can undergo a phase transition to an anisotropic superfluid state, characterized by nonvanishing gaps in pairing channels with singlet-singlet (SS) and triplet-singlet (TS) pairing of nucleons (in spin and isospin spaces). In the SS pairing channel nucleons are paired with nonzero orbital angular momentum. Such two-gap states can arise as a result of branching from the one-gap solution of the self-consistent equations, describing SS or TS pairing of nucleons, that depends on the relationship between SS and TS coupling constants at the branching point. The density/temperature dependence of the order parameters and the critical temperature for transition to the anisotropic two-gap state are determined in a model with the SkP effective interaction. It is shown that the anisotropic SS-TS superfluid phase corresponds to a metastable state in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

Under changing density or temperature, a nucleon Fermi superfluid can undergo a phase transition to an anisotropic two-gap superfluid state with nonvanishing gaps in both the singlet-singlet (SS) and triplet-singlet (TS) pairing channels; however, this anisotropic SS-TS superfluid phase corresponds to a metastable state in nuclear matter, not the thermodynamically most favorable one.

## Method

The authors used a Fermi-liquid approach with normal and anomalous interaction amplitudes treated on an equal footing, employing the density-dependent Skyrme effective interaction (SkP potential) as the nucleon-nucleon interaction. They solved self-consistent equations for the order parameters \(\Delta_{00}\) and \(\Delta_{30}\) describing SS and TS pairing, respectively, at zero and finite temperature in symmetric nuclear matter. The analysis was performed in the logarithmic approximation assuming the anomalous amplitudes are nonzero only in a narrow layer near the Fermi surface, and the free energies of the one-gap and two-gap solutions were compared to determine thermodynamic stability.

## Key numbers

- The maximum critical temperature for the SS-TS state is approximately \(T_c \approx 0.38\) MeV at density \(\varrho_m \approx 0.139\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- The SS-TS mixed state exists in the finite density interval \(\varrho_1 < \varrho < \varrho_2\), with \(\varrho_1 \approx 0.134\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) and \(\varrho_2 \approx 0.15\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) at zero temperature for the SkP force.
- At the left branching point (\(\varrho = \varrho_1\)), the one-gap SS and TS order parameters are equal: \(\Delta^{ss}(\varrho_1) = \Delta^{ts}(\varrho_1)\).
- The maximum ratio of the energy gaps to the cutoff energy \(\theta = 0.1\varepsilon_F\) does not exceed 0.26 in the density domain where two-gap solutions exist.
- The TS-ST mixed states studied earlier exist only at low densities \(\varrho \lesssim 0.05\ \text{to}\ 0.06\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), whereas the SS-TS states exist near nuclear matter saturation density.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of multi-gap superfluid states in nuclear matter by Isayev (2002) and Isayev and Röpke (2002), which considered isotropic two-gap condensates with \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) pairing (zero orbital angular momentum). The present paper introduces anisotropic pairing by allowing nonzero orbital angular momentum in the SS channel (\(L=1\)), leading to an order parameter that depends on momentum direction. The results contrast with the TS-ST mixed states found at low densities by Isayev (2002), showing that SS-TS states occupy a different density regime near saturation density. The work enables future studies of metastable superfluid phases in neutron star interiors and finite nuclei with \(N \approx Z\).
