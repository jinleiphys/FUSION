# Properties of neutron star described by a relativistic $ab~ initio$ model

> Properties of neutron star are investigated by an available relativistic $ab~ initio$ method, i.e., the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) model, with the latest high-precision relativistic charge-dependent potentials, pvCD-Bonn A, B, C. The neutron star matter is solved within the beta equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions in the framework of RBHF model. Comparing to the conventional treatment, where the chemical potential of lepton was approximately represented by the symmetry energy of nuclear matter, the equation of state (EOS) of neutron star matter in the present self-consistent calculation with pvCD-Bonn B has striking difference above the baryon number density $n_b=0.55$ fm$^{-3}$. However, these differences influence the global properties of neutron star only about $1\%\sim2\%$. Then, three two-body potentials pvCD-Bonn A, B, C, with different tensor components, are systematically applied in RBHF model to calculate the properties of neutron star. It is found that the maximum masses of neutron star are around $2.21\sim2.30M_\odot$ and the corresponding radii are $R =11.18\sim11.72$ km. The radii of $1.4M_\odot$ neutron star are predicated as $R_{1.4} = 12.34\sim12.91$ km and their dimensionless tidal deformabilities are $Λ_{1.4} = 485\sim 626$. Furthermore, the direct URCA process in neutron star cooling will happen from $n_b=0.414\sim0.530$ fm$^{-3}$ with the proton fractions, $Y_p=0.136\sim0.138$. All of the results obtained from RBHF model only with two-body pvCD-Bonn potentials completely satisfy various constraints from recent astronomical observations of massive neutron stars, gravitational wave detection (GW 170817), and mass-radius simultaneous measurement (NICER).

## Key claim

The relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) model with high-precision pvCD-Bonn potentials produces neutron star properties that satisfy all current observational constraints, but the self-consistent treatment of beta equilibrium and charge neutrality yields equations of state that differ significantly from the symmetry energy approximation above baryon density 0.55 fm$^{-3}$, yet these differences affect global neutron star properties by only 1-2%.

## Method

The authors solved the in-medium Blankenbecler-Sugar equation for the G-matrix in the RBHF framework using three pvCD-Bonn potentials (A, B, C) with different tensor components, treating the Pauli operator exactly rather than with angle-average approximations. Neutron star matter was computed under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions both through a self-consistent method and through the conventional symmetry energy approximation, with the outer crust described by the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland EOS and the inner crust by a relativistic mean-field Thomas-Fermi approach. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation was then solved to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star masses: 2.21-2.30 $M_\odot$ across pvCD-Bonn A, B, C potentials
- Radii at maximum mass: 11.18-11.72 km
- Radii of 1.4 $M_\odot$ neutron stars: $R_{1.4} = 12.34-12.91$ km
- Dimensionless tidal deformabilities at 1.4 $M_\odot$: $\Lambda_{1.4} = 485-626$
- Direct URCA process threshold densities: $n_b = 0.414-0.530$ fm$^{-3}$ with proton fractions $Y_p = 0.136-0.138$
- For pvCD-Bonn B specifically: self-consistent method gives maximum mass 2.28 $M_\odot$ with radius 11.52 km; symmetry energy approximation gives 2.25 $M_\odot$ with radius 11.27 km
- For pvCD-Bonn B at 1.4 $M_\odot$: self-consistent method gives $R_{1.4} = 12.74$ km, $\Lambda_{1.4} = 580$; symmetry energy approximation gives $R_{1.4} = 12.69$ km, $\Lambda_{1.4} = 578$
- Saturation densities for symmetric nuclear matter: 0.192 fm$^{-3}$ (pvCD-Bonn A), 0.158 fm$^{-3}$ (pvCD-Bonn B), 0.139 fm$^{-3}$ (pvCD-Bonn C)
- Binding energies at saturation: -16.82 MeV (pvCD-Bonn A), -12.91 MeV (pvCD-Bonn B), -10.72 MeV (pvCD-Bonn C)
- D-state probabilities of deuteron: 4.2% (pvCD-Bonn A), 5.5% (pvCD-Bonn B), 6.1% (pvCD-Bonn C)
- Symmetry energy slopes: $L = 80$ MeV (pvCD-Bonn A), 57 MeV (pvCD-Bonn B), 45 MeV (pvCD-Bonn C)

## Context

This work extends the RBHF treatment of neutron star matter by Katayama and Saito (2013) by using the newly developed high-precision pvCD-Bonn potentials (Wang et al., 2019) and by systematically comparing the self-consistent method with the symmetry energy approximation previously used by Krastev and Sammarruca (2006) and others. The results satisfy constraints from massive neutron star observations (PSR J0740+6620 at 2.14 $M_\odot$), gravitational wave detection (GW 170817), and NICER mass-radius measurements, enabling future studies of neutron star cooling and internal composition where accurate particle fractions are essential.
