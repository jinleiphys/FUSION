# Investigation of the onset of deconfinement with the NA61/SHINE experiment

> High-energy heavy-ion collisions provide a unique framework for studying the phase transition of strongly interacting matter. The NA61/SHINE experiment, located in the North Area of CERN's SPS, is a fixed-target facility designed to perform a systematic exploration of the QCD phase diagram. This is achieved through a two-dimensional scan that varies both the beam momentum (from 13A to 150/158A GeV/c) and the size of the colliding systems (p+p, p+Pb, Be+Be, Ar+Sc, Xe+La, Pb+Pb, O+O). Such a wide scan enables detailed studies of how collision dynamics evolve with system size and energy. A central objective of the NA61/SHINE research program is to investigate the onset of deconfinement - the transition from hadronic matter to a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) - by analyzing observables such as the strangeness-to-entropy ratio, where entropy is proportional to pion yields. According to the Statistical Model of the Early Stage (SMES), this ratio is expected to exhibit a horn-like structure within the SPS energy range. This article discusses the theoretical framework of the SMES, its assumptions, and compares recent NA61/SHINE results with other experimental data worldwide, contributing to a deeper understanding of the QCD phase transition.

## Key claim

The NA61/SHINE experiment's preliminary measurement of the $\langle K^{+}\rangle/\langle\pi^{+}\rangle$ ratio in central Pb+Pb collisions at 30A GeV/c ($\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} \approx 7.6$ GeV) is consistent with the NA49 measurement and supports the interpretation of a non-monotonic "horn" structure as a signature of the onset of deconfinement, though the paper notes that the order of the phase transition (rapid crossover versus first-order) cannot be resolved experimentally from global observables.

## Method

The analysis used the $h^{-}$ method to extract $\pi^{-}$ production spectra from central Pb+Pb collisions at 30A GeV/c, applying Monte Carlo-based background subtraction to correct for contamination from $K^{-}$, antiprotons, and weak-decay pions. The double-differential spectra $\mathrm{d}^{2}n/\mathrm{d}y\,\mathrm{d}p_{\mathrm{T}}$ were projected onto $p_{\mathrm{T}}$ and fitted with an exponential function for extrapolation, then integrated and fitted with a sum of two symmetrically displaced Gaussian functions to obtain rapidity distributions and mean multiplicities. For $K^{+}$ and $K^{-}$ mesons, particle identification was performed using the specific energy loss ($\mathrm{d}E/\mathrm{d}x$) technique in the Time Projection Chambers, with corrections from EPOS1.99 coupled with GEANT4 simulations.

## Key numbers

* Beam momentum scan range: 13A to 150/158A GeV/c, covering systems from p+p to Pb+Pb
* Central Pb+Pb collisions at 30A GeV/c ($\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} \approx 7.6$ GeV) were analyzed for $\pi^{-}$, $K^{+}$, and $K^{-}$ production
* Two centrality selections were used: 0--7.2% and 0--10% most central Pb+Pb collisions
* The $p_{\mathrm{T}}$ spectra were summed up to $p_{\mathrm{T}}^{\text{max}} = 1.5$ GeV/c for rapidity distribution integration
* The $\langle K^{+}\rangle/\langle\pi^{+}\rangle$ ratio at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} \approx 7.6$ GeV is consistent with the NA49 measurement (no explicit numerical value given for the ratio itself)
* The $h^{-}$ method has systematic uncertainties typically around 10%

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of the onset of deconfinement first observed by the NA49 experiment (Afanasiev 2002) in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, where the "kink," "horn," and "step" structures were identified. The NA61/SHINE experiment now performs a two-dimensional scan varying both beam momentum and system size (p+p, p+Pb, Be+Be, Ar+Sc, Xe+La, Pb+Pb, O+O) to determine whether deconfinement can also occur in smaller collision systems, as noted in Abgrall (2014) and Aduszkiewicz (2020). The results enable comparison with theoretical models including the Statistical Model of the Early Stage (SMES) from Gazdzicki (1998), the Hadron Resonance Gas model (Andronic 2009), and the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics model (Bratkovskaya 2011, Cassing 2016), all of which suggest deconfined phase formation may occur at SPS energies.
