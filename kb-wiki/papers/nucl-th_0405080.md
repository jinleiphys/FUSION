# Modified Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula with isotonic shift and new driplines

> Nuclear masses are calculated using the modified Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula in which the isotonic shifts have been incorporated. The results are compared with the improved liquid drop model with isotonic shift. Mass excesses predicted by this method compares well with the microscopic-macroscopic model while being much more simple. The neutron and proton drip lines have been predicted using this modified Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula with isotonic shifts.

# Modified Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula with isotonic shift and new driplines

## Key claim

The modified Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula with isotonic shifts (BWMIS) predicts neutron and proton drip lines that are almost identical to those from the more complex Myers-Swiatecki (MS) mass formula, while providing better mass predictions for some nuclei with higher N and Z values, despite being a much simpler phenomenological model. The improvement is conditional on applying isotonic shifts only beyond A=30, since the base BWM formula was designed for the low-mass region.

## Method

The authors took the existing modified Bethe-Weizsacker (BWM) mass formula of Samanta and Adhikari (2002) and incorporated isotonic mass shifts, defined as the average deviation between theoretical and experimental binding energies for the three nearest isotones on either side of the nucleus of interest. They used the Audi-Wapstra 2003 mass table as the experimental reference, computed one-proton and one-neutron separation energies from the corrected binding energies, and identified drip line nuclei as the last bound isotopes where these separation energies remain positive.

## Key numbers

- Root mean square deviation sigma for BWMIS: 1.645 MeV (for 3175 nuclei), compared to 0.865 MeV for MS and 3.061 MeV for ILDM
- Chi-squared per point for BWMIS: 7.758 (including all nuclei), reducing to 0.907 when excluding F-20 and F-21 whose experimental mass excesses are of order 10^-2 MeV
- Chi-squared per point for MS: 1.398 (for 3152 nuclei)
- Base BWM parameters from chi-squared minimization: a_v = 15.777(53) MeV, a_s = 18.34(17) MeV, a_c = 0.710(3) MeV, a_sym = 23.21(10) MeV, a_p = 12(2) MeV
- Parameters c = 30 and k = 17 in the modified pairing term
- Neutron drip line predicted for He-8 (Z=2, N=6) with S_n = -0.07(14) MeV; one neutron beyond gives S_n = -3.18(15) MeV
- Proton drip line predicted for F-15 (Z=9, N=6) with S_p = 0.09(16) MeV; one proton beyond gives S_p = -2.33(16) MeV
- Formula for neutron number on beta-stability line: N_s = 0.968051 Z + 0.00658803 Z^2

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological Bethe-Weizsacker approach by incorporating isotonic shifts, a technique previously applied in the improved liquid drop model (ILDM) by Souza et al. (2003). It directly compares its predictions to the microscopic-macroscopic model of Myers and Swiatecki (1994, 1996), finding that the simpler BWMIS formula yields comparable or better results for heavy nuclei. The predicted drip lines and separation energies provide guidance for experimental searches of exotic nuclei, particularly near the neutron and proton drip lines where many masses remain unknown.
