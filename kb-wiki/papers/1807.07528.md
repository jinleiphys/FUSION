# Quasi-parton distribution functions: two-dimensional scalar and spinor QCD

> We construct the quasi-parton distributions of mesons for two-dimensional QCD with either scalar or spinor quarks using the $1/N_c$ expansion. We show that in the infinite momentum limit, the parton distribution function is recovered in both leading and sub-leading order in $1/N_c$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in two-dimensional scalar and spinor QCD, the quasi-parton distribution function (quasi-PDF) of a meson matches the light-cone parton distribution function (PDF) in the infinite momentum limit, both at leading and subleading order in the 1/N_c expansion, without requiring additional renormalization; however, the limit is subtle at the parton fraction endpoints x=0 and x=1.

## Method

The authors construct quasi-PDFs for mesons in two-dimensional QCD with either scalar or spinor quarks using a bosonized form of the Hamiltonian in the 1/N_c expansion. They solve the gap equation for the self-consistent single-particle energy E(k) in the planar limit, derive the light-cone wavefunction equation in the infinite momentum limit, and then compute the quasi-PDF from equal-time Euclidean correlators. The subleading 1/N_c corrections to both the PDF and quasi-PDF are computed using perturbation theory in the bosonized operator algebra, with the sign s=+1 for scalar QCD and s=-1 for spinor QCD.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order light-cone wavefunction equation for scalar QCD in the infinite momentum limit is given by (m^2/x + m^2/(1-x) - M_n^2) phi_n(x) = (lambda/(4 pi)) PV integral dy/(x-y)^2 * (2-x-y)(x+y)/sqrt(x(1-x)y(1-y)) phi_n(y), where lambda = g^2 N_c is the t'Hooft coupling.
* The leading 1/P correction to the backward-moving wavefunction is phi^-_1 = P^2 phi_n^-(x) = (lambda/(24 pi sqrt(x(1-x)))) integral_0^1 dy phi_n(y)/sqrt(y(1-y)).
* The 1/N_c correction to the spinor QCD PDF is delta q_i(x) = (lambda^2/(pi^2 N_c)) integral_0^{1-x} dy sum_{kk'l} (F_{kli}(x,y) F_{k'li}(x,y))/(x+y), with F_{kli}(x,y) defined in terms of the wavefunctions and energy denominators.
* The renormalized gap equation for scalar QCD includes a subtraction term: (lambda/(4 pi)) integral dk_1 ( (E_{k1}/E_k - E_k/E_{k1}) PV/|k+k1|^2 - (E_{k1}/E_k)(1/k1^2) ) = 0.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Ji (2013) for extracting light-cone parton distributions from Euclidean correlators using quasi-PDFs, providing a non-perturbative check in two-dimensional QCD models. It builds on the large N_c analysis of two-dimensional scalar QCD by Tam (1975) and Grinstein (1975), and the spinor QCD analysis by t'Hooft (1974). The leading-order result for spinor QCD agrees with a recent study by the same authors (2018). The paper enables further lattice QCD studies of quasi-PDFs by demonstrating that the matching holds without additional renormalization in these solvable models, though the endpoint behavior requires careful treatment.
