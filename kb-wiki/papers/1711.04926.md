# The dominance of the $ν(0d_{5/2})^2$ configuration in the $N=8$ shell in $^{12}$Be from the breakup reaction on a proton target at intermediate energy

> The momentum distribution of $^{11}$Be fragments produced by the breakup of $^{12}$Be interacting with a proton target at 700.5 MeV/$u$ energy has been measured at GSI Darmstadt. To obtain the structure information on the anomaly of the $N=8$ neutron shell, the momentum distribution of $^{11}$Be fragments from the one-neutron knockout $^{12}$Be(p,pn) reaction, measured in inverse kinematics, has been analysed in the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) based on a quasi-free scattering scenario. The DWIA analysis shows a surprisingly strong contribution of the neutron $0d_{5/2}$ orbital in $^{12}$Be to the transverse momentum distribution of the $^{11}$Be fragments. The single-neutron $0d_{5/2}$ spectroscopic factor deduced from the present knock-out data is 1.39(10), which is significantly larger than that deduced recently from data of $^{12}$Be breakup on a carbon target. This result provides a strong experimental evidence for the dominance of the neutron $ν(0d_{5/2})^2$ configuration in the ground state of $^{12}$Be.

## Key claim
The ground state of \(^{12}\)Be is dominated by the \(\nu(0d_{5/2})^2\) intruder configuration, with a spectroscopic factor of 1.39(10) for the neutron \(0d_{5/2}\) orbital deduced from the DWIA analysis of the one-neutron knockout reaction, which is significantly larger than the value of about 0.48 reported earlier from breakup on a carbon target.

## Method
The transverse momentum distribution of \(^{11}\)Be fragments from the one-neutron knockout reaction \(^{12}\)Be(p,pn)\(^{11}\)Be was measured in inverse kinematics at GSI Darmstadt using a 700.5 MeV/u \(^{12}\)Be beam incident on a hydrogen gas target (IKAR ionization chamber). The data were analyzed within the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) based on a quasi-free scattering scenario, where the spectroscopic factors for the \(\nu 1s_{1/2}\), \(\nu 0d_{5/2}\), and \(\nu 1p_{3/2}\) single-particle configurations were treated as free parameters in a fit to the measured momentum distribution.

## Key numbers
- The total width (FWHM) of the transverse momentum distribution of \(^{11}\)Be fragments, after deconvolution from momentum resolution, is 248.7(153) MeV/c.
- The momentum resolution of \(p_x\) is 12.0(12) MeV/c, determined from a scattering angle uncertainty of \(\sigma_s \approx 0.61\) mrad.
- Spectroscopic factors from the best DWIA fit: \(S(\nu 1s_{1/2}) = 0.17 \pm 0.06\), \(S(\nu 0d_{5/2}) = 1.39 \pm 0.10\), and \(S(\nu 1p_{3/2}) = 0.10 \pm 0.04\).
- The secondary \(^{12}\)Be beam energy was 700.5 MeV/u with 1.1% FWHM energy spread and an intensity of about 6000 ions/s.

## Context
This work extends studies of the \(N=8\) shell anomaly in neutron-rich Be isotopes, following earlier measurements of \(^{12}\)Be breakup on a \(^{9}\)Be target by Navin et al. (2000) and on a \(^{12}\)C target by Pain et al. (2006), which showed mixing of \(p\) and \(sd\) shells. The present result provides stronger evidence for the dominance of the \(\nu(0d_{5/2})^2\) intruder configuration, consistent with the microscopic particle-vibration coupling study by Gori et al. (2004). The large \(d\)-wave spectroscopic factor challenges future experimental and theoretical studies of neutron-rich Be isotopes and clarifies the mechanism breaking the \(N=8\) magicity.
