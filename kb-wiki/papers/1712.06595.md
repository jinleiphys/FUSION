# A no-go theorem for non-standard explanations of the $τ\to K_Sπν_τ$ CP asymmetry

> The CP asymmetry in $τ\to K_Sπν_τ$, as measured by the BaBar collaboration, differs from the Standard Model prediction by $2.8σ$. Most non-standard interactions do not allow for the required strong phase needed to produce a non-vanishing CP asymmetry, leaving only new tensor interactions as a possible mechanism. We demonstrate that, contrary to previous assumptions in the literature, the crucial interference between vector and tensor phases is suppressed by at least two orders of magnitude due to Watson's final-state-interaction theorem. Furthermore, we find that the strength of the relevant CP-violating tensor interaction is strongly constrained by bounds from the neutron electric dipole moment and $D$-$\bar{D}$ mixing. These observations together imply that it is extremely difficult to explain the current $τ\to K_Sπν_τ$ measurement in terms of physics beyond the Standard Model originating in the ultraviolet.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, contrary to earlier claims in the literature, the strong phase needed for a non-standard tensor interaction to produce the observed CP asymmetry in τ → K_S π ν_τ is suppressed by at least two orders of magnitude due to Watson's final-state-interaction theorem, and that the required tensor Wilson coefficient is simultaneously ruled out by bounds from the neutron electric dipole moment and D-anti-D mixing.

## Method

The authors derived the unitarity relations for the vector and tensor πK form factors, showing that in the elastic region dominated by the K*(892) resonance the phases of f_+(s) and B_T(s) are identical, causing the CP-violating interference to vanish. They estimated the residual inelastic effects from the K*(1410) resonance and used lattice QCD input for the tensor form factor normalization to compute the resulting CP asymmetry. They then used SU(2) gauge invariance to relate the tensor operator in τ decay to a neutral-current operator, and applied renormalization group evolution to derive constraints from the neutron electric dipole moment and D-anti-D mixing.

## Key numbers

- The BaBar collaboration measured A_CP^τ,exp = -3.6(2.3)(1.1) × 10^{-3}, which differs from the Standard Model prediction A_CP^τ,SM = 3.6(1) × 10^{-3} by 2.8σ.
- The indirect CP violation parameter A_L = 3.32(6) × 10^{-3} from semileptonic kaon decays sets the Standard Model expectation for the τ asymmetry.
- The estimated BSM contribution to the CP asymmetry is bounded by |A_CP^τ,BSM| ≲ 0.03 |Im c_T|, about two orders of magnitude smaller than previously assumed.
- The tensor form factor ratio from lattice QCD is B_T(0)/f_+(0) = 0.676(27) (Baum 2011).
- The neutron EDM bound gives |Im c_T(μ_τ)| ≲ 10^{-5} for Λ ≳ 100 GeV, while explaining the τ asymmetry would require Im c_T(μ_τ) ∼ 0.1.
- The branching ratio for τ → K_S π ν_τ is BR = 4.04(13) × 10^{-3} (Epifanov 2007).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Grossman (2011) on the Standard Model prediction for the τ CP asymmetry and directly contradicts the earlier claims of Devi (2013), Dhargyal (2016), and Dhargyal (2016) that tensor interactions could explain the BaBar measurement. The paper uses the Omnès formalism for form factors, following the dispersion relation approach of Moussallam (2007) and Boito (2008, 2010), and applies the neutron EDM bound from Baker (2006) and Afach (2015) together with lattice QCD results from Bhattacharya (2015). The result rules out ultraviolet-origin beyond-the-Standard-Model explanations, but leaves open the possibility of light new physics below the electroweak scale, which would motivate further measurements by Belle and Belle II.
