# Triaxial dynamics in the quadrupole-deformed rotor

> The triaxial dynamics of the quadrupole-deformed rotor model of both the rigid and the irrotational type have been investigated in detail. The results indicate that level patterns and E2 transitional characters of the two types of the model can be matched with each other to the leading order of the deformation parameter $β$. Especially, it is found that the dynamical structure of the irrotational type with most triaxial deformation ($γ=30^\circ$) is equivalent to that of the rigid type with oblate deformation ($γ=60^\circ$), and the associated spectrum can be classified into the standard rotational bands obeying the rotational $L(L+1)$-law or regrouped into a new ground- and $γ$-band with odd-even staggering in the new $γ$-band commonly recognized as a signature of the triaxiality. The differences between the two types of the model in this case are emphasized especially on the E2 transitional characters.

## Key claim

The level patterns and E2 transitional characters of the rigid and irrotational quadrupole-deformed rotor models can be matched to leading order in the deformation parameter β, with the irrotational type at the most triaxial deformation (γ=30°) being dynamically equivalent to the rigid type at oblate deformation (γ=60°), though the E2 transitional characters differ between the two types in this case.

## Method

The authors solved the quantum rotor Hamiltonian for both rigid and irrotational ellipsoids with quadrupole deformation, using moments of inertia expressed as functions of the deformation parameters β and γ. Energy levels and B(E2) transition rates were computed via numerical diagonalization of the Hamiltonian in a basis of Wigner D-functions, with the D₂ symmetry group used to classify states. The analysis compared level patterns and E2 transition strengths at several characteristic γ values (prolate, triaxial, and oblate cases) for both rotor types.

## Key numbers

- At γ≈0 (prolate case), the ground-band energy ratio E₄_g/E₂_g = 3.33 for both rigid and irrotational types, confirming the L(L+1) rotational law.
- For the γ-band in the prolate case, the ratio R_γ = (E₄_γ − E₂_γ)/(E₃_γ − E₂_γ) = 2.33 for both types, consistent with the L(L+1) law.
- In the triaxial case (rigid at γ=30°, irrotational at γ=15°), R_γ = 2.52 for the rigid type and R_γ = 2.42 for the irrotational type.
- B(E2; 4_g→2_g) = 109 (rigid) vs. 145 (irrotational) in the triaxial case, normalized to B(E2; 2_g→0_g)=100.
- B(E2; 6_g→4_g) = 99 (rigid) vs. 165 (irrotational) in the triaxial case, normalized to B(E2; 2_g→0_g)=100.
- In the oblate/most-triaxial case (rigid at γ≈60°, irrotational at γ≈30°), B(E2; 4_g→2_g) = 140 (rigid) vs. 1 (irrotational), normalized to B(E2; 2_g→0_g)=100.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of rigid and irrotational rotor dynamics beyond the well-known axial-deformed case (Bohr and Mottelson textbook; Ui 1970) into the triaxial regime. It builds on the triaxial rotor model with independent inertia and E2 tensors suggested by Wood (2004) and Allmond (2008-2010), and relates to the SU(3) shell model realization of the rotor by Leschber (1987) and the interacting boson model treatments by Smirnov (2000) and Zhang (2014). The results enable systematic identification of whether experimental nuclear spectra correspond to rigid or irrotational dynamics based on E2 transition patterns, particularly for triaxially deformed nuclei.
