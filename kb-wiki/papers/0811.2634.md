# Fast Generators of Direct Photons

> Three fast generators of direct photons in the central rapidity region of high-energy heavy-ion collisions have been presented The generator of prompt photons is based on a tabulation of $p+p(\bar p)$ data and binary scaling. Two generators of thermal direct photons, for hot hadron gas (HHG) and quark-gluon plasma (QGP) scenarios, assume the 1+1 Bjorken hydrodynamics. SPS and RHIC data can be fitted better by scenario with QGP. Predictions for the LHC energy have been made. The generators have been realized as macros for the ROOT analysis package.

## Key claim
The paper presents three fast Monte Carlo generators for direct photons in heavy-ion collisions, but the QGP thermal photon rates are acknowledged as an "educated guess" because thermal photon production in QGP is a non-perturbative mechanism that cannot be reliably accessed in perturbative hard thermal loop resummed thermal field theory.

## Method
The prompt photon generator uses a tabulation of existing p+p(pbar) data on the dimensionless function F(x_T) = (sqrt(s))^5 E d^3 sigma^{pp}/d^3 p, then applies binary scaling to obtain the spectrum in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The two thermal photon generators assume 1+1 Bjorken hydrodynamics with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, using different photon emission rates for hot hadron gas (HHG) and quark-gluon plasma (QGP) scenarios. The generators are implemented as ROOT macros.

## Key numbers
- SPS data (sqrt(s)=17 GeV) fitted with QGP scenario: T0=340 MeV, tau0=0.20 fm/c, dN_gamma/dy=14, CPU time 110 s
- RHIC data (sqrt(s)=200 GeV) fitted with QGP scenario: T0=430 MeV, tau0=0.15 fm/c, dN_gamma/dy=31, CPU time 160 s
- LHC predictions (sqrt(s)=5500 GeV) with QGP scenario: T0=650 MeV, tau0=0.10 fm/c, dN_gamma/dy=173, CPU time 390 s
- HHG scenario requires unreasonable parameters for RHIC: tau0=7 fm/c, T0=200 MeV
- QGP outshines HHG at p_t > 2 GeV/c at both RHIC and LHC energies
- QGP contribution to dN_gamma/dy is approximately 10% despite dominating at high p_t
- Freeze-out temperature T_f = 100 MeV used for all thermal generators

## Context
This work extends the approach of PHENIX (2006) for prompt photon scaling and uses the Bjorken hydrodynamics framework (Bjorken, 1983) for thermal photon production. The generators enable fast Monte Carlo simulation of direct photons for comparison with experimental data from SPS (WA98), RHIC (PHENIX), and predictions for LHC. The results are compared with 2+1 hydrodynamics calculations (2+1RHIC, 2+1LHC), which show a steeper HHG spectrum due to radial flow effects. The generators have been implemented into the FASTMC code of the UHKM package.
