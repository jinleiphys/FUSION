# In-medium Pion and Partial Restoration of Chiral Symmetry

> The partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear medium is investigated in a model independent way by exploiting operator relations in QCD. An exact sum rule is derived for the quark condensate valid for all density. This sum rule is simplified at low density to a new relation with the in-medium quark condensate <bar{q}q>*, in-medium pion decay constant F_{pi}^t and in-medium pion wave-function renormalization Z_{pi}*. Calculating Z_{pi}*at low density from the iso-scalar pion-nucleon scattering data and relating F_{pi}^t to the isovector pion-nucleus scattering length b_1^*, it is concluded that the enhanced repulsion of the s-wave isovector pion-nucleus interaction observed in the deeply bound pionic atoms directly implies the reduction of the in-medium quark condensate. The knowledge of the in-medium pion mass m_{pi}* is not necessary to reach this conclusion.

# In-medium Pion and Partial Restoration of Chiral Symmetry

## Key claim

The enhanced repulsion of the s-wave isovector pion-nucleus interaction observed in deeply bound pionic atoms directly implies the reduction of the in-medium quark condensate, without requiring knowledge of the in-medium pion mass. This conclusion holds at low density under the linear density approximation, using only current commutation relations, spectral decomposition, and experimental scattering data.

## Method

The authors derive an exact sum rule for the in-medium quark condensate valid at all density in the chiral limit by equating the operator relation for the axial-current-pseudoscalar correlator with its hadronic spectral representation. At low density, they simplify this to an in-medium Glashow-Weinberg relation connecting the temporal pion decay constant, the pion wave-function renormalization, and the quark condensate. They then evaluate the wave-function renormalization from isoscalar pion-nucleon scattering data and relate the temporal decay constant to the isovector pion-nucleus scattering length via the in-medium Tomozawa-Weinberg relation.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar pion-nucleon scattering amplitude coefficient beta = 2.17 ± 0.04 fm^3, extracted from the pion-nucleon sigma term sigma_piN ≈ 45 MeV and the scattering length a_piN = (0.0016 ± 0.0013) m_pi^{-1}.
- The in-medium wave-function renormalization parameter gamma = 0.184, giving Z_pi^{*1/2} = 1 - gamma * rho/rho_0.
- The ratio b_1/b_1^* = 0.79 ± 0.05 at effective density rho ≈ 0.6 rho_0 from deeply bound pionic atoms, leading to <qbar q>^* / <qbar q> ≈ 1 - 0.37 rho/rho_0.
- Using b_1/b_1^* = 0.75 from elastic pion-nucleus scatterings at rho ≈ rho_0 gives <qbar q>^* / <qbar q> ≈ 1 - 0.43 rho/rho_0.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent approach of Glashow and Weinberg (1967) and Gell-Mann, Oakes, and Renner (1968) to finite density, providing a direct link between experimental pion-nucleus observables and the chiral order parameter. It builds on earlier suggestions by Kolomeitsev and Weise (2002) and Weise (2005) connecting the temporal pion decay constant to pionic atom data, and by Jido, Hatsuda, and Kunihiro (2000) relating the decay constant to pion-pion interactions. The derived sum rules enable extraction of the in-medium quark condensate from existing pionic atom and scattering data without model-dependent assumptions about the in-medium pion mass, and provide consistency checks for in-medium chiral perturbation theory calculations such as those by Thorsson, Wirzba, and Meissner (1995).
