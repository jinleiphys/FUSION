# Pseudoscalar current and covariance with the light-front approach

> Quantum Field Theory (QFT) is used to describe the physics of particles in terms of their fundamental constituents. The Light-Front Field Theory~(LFFT), introduced by Paul Dirac in 1949, is an alternative approach to solve some of the problems that arise in quantum field theory. The LFFT is similar to the Equal Time Quantum Field Theory~(EQT), however, some particularities are not, such as the loss of covariance in the light-front. Pion electromagnetic form factor is studied in this work at lower and higher momentum transfer regions to explore the constituent quark models and the differences among these and other models. The electromagnetic current is calculated with both the ``plus'' and ``minus'' components in the light-front approach. The results are compared with other models, as well as with experimental data.

## Key claim
The paper shows that, for the pion electromagnetic form factor calculated in the light-front approach, the "plus" component of the electromagnetic current with a non-symmetric vertex is free from non-valence (pair term) contributions, while the "minus" component requires explicit inclusion of those pair terms to restore covariance and obtain the same form factor as the plus component.

## Method
The authors calculate the pion electromagnetic form factor using light-front constituent quark models with two types of pion-quark-antiquark vertex functions: a non-symmetric vertex and a symmetric vertex. The electromagnetic current is evaluated in the Breit frame with the Drell-Yan condition (q^+=0) using both the plus (J^+) and minus (J^-) components, and the pair term (non-valence) contributions are computed via the dislocation pole method for the minus component. The results are compared with the vector meson dominance model and with experimental data for momentum transfer up to Q^2 = 20 (GeV/c)^2.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass used as input: m_pi = 0.140 GeV (experimental value)
- Quark mass for both vertex models: m_q = 0.220 GeV
- Regulator mass for non-symmetric vertex: m_R = 1.0 GeV
- Regulator mass for symmetric vertex: m_R = 0.600 GeV
- Pion decay constant obtained with non-symmetric vertex: f_pi = 92.13 MeV, compared to experimental value f_pi = 92.28(7) MeV
- Experimental pion charge radius: r_exp = 0.672 +/- 0.02 fm
- Rho meson mass used in VMD: m_rho = 0.767 GeV

## Context
This work extends previous light-front calculations of the pion electromagnetic form factor by deMelo (1999, 2002) to higher momentum transfer regions up to Q^2 = 20 (GeV/c)^2, and compares with the vector meson dominance model of Kroll, Lee and Zumino (1967) and with perturbative QCD predictions from Brodsky and Lepage (1980). The study demonstrates that proper treatment of pair terms restores covariance in the light-front approach, enabling consistent extraction of form factors regardless of which current component is used. The results enable further tests of constituent quark models against experimental data from Amendolia (1984, 1986), Baldini (1999), Volmer (2001), Blok (2002), Horn (2006), and Tadevosyan (2007).
