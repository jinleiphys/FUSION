# Superdeformation and clustering in $^{40}$Ca studied with Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics

> Deformed states in $^{40}$Ca are investigated with a method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. Above the spherical ground state, rotational bands arise from a normal deformation and a superdeformation as well as an oblate deformation. The calculated energy spectra and $E2$ transition strengths in the superdeformed band reasonably agree to the experimental data of the superdeformed band starting from the $0^+_3$ state at 5.213 MeV. By the analysis of single-particle orbits, it is found that the superdeformed state has particle-hole nature of an $8p$-$8h$ configuration. One of new findings is parity asymmetric structure with $^{12}$C+$^{28}$Si-like clustering in the superdeformed band. We predict that $^{12}$C+$^{28}$Si molecular bands may be built above the superdeformed band due to the excitation of inter-cluster motion. They are considered to be higher nodal states of the superdeformed state. We also suggest negative-parity bands caused by the parity asymmetric deformation.

## Key claim

The superdeformed band in $^{40}$Ca, built on the $0^+_3$ state at 5.213 MeV, is found to have an intrinsic $^{12}$C+$^{28}$Si-like clustering structure with parity asymmetry, rather than being a pure mean-field $8p$-$8h$ configuration; the authors predict that $^{12}$C+$^{28}$Si molecular bands may exist above this superdeformed band as higher nodal states of the inter-cluster motion.

## Method

The authors applied the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) method with parity projection before energy variation and total-angular-momentum projection after variation. They imposed a constraint on the total number of harmonic oscillator quanta $\langle \hat N_{\rm os}\rangle$ to obtain energy curves as a function of deformation, and then superposed spin-parity projected wave functions from multiple intrinsic states to calculate level schemes and $E2$ transition strengths. The effective nuclear force used was the F3B force with finite-range two-body and three-body terms, with two choices of spin-orbit strength ($u_{ls}=2500$ MeV and $u_{ls}=1800$ MeV).

## Key numbers

- The superdeformed band head $0^+_3$ state is experimentally at 5.213 MeV; the calculated excitation energy with the stronger spin-orbit force (interaction (i)) is slightly higher than this value.
- The superdeformed intrinsic state has a quadrupole deformation $\beta_2 \sim 0.52$ at $N_{\rm os}\sim 70$.
- The lowest $3^-$ state (a $1p$-$1h$ state) is calculated at 4.7 MeV excitation energy with interaction (i), compared to the experimental value of 3.74 MeV.
- The oblate state and superdeformed state appear at 7-8 MeV excitation energy above the spherical ground state in the $J^\pi=0^+$ energy curve with interaction (i).
- The $\alpha$+$^{36}$Ar-like cluster state appears at $N_{\rm os}\ge 69$ with interaction (i), but is much higher than the superdeformed state.
- With the weaker spin-orbit force (interaction (ii)), the superdeformed state excitation energy is about 20 MeV, largely overestimating the experimental 5.21 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape coexistence in $^{40}$Ca, where the $0^+_2$ state at 3.35 MeV was identified as a $4p$-$4h$ band (Grace and Green, 1967, 1969) and the $0^+_3$ state at 5.21 MeV as an $8p$-$8h$ superdeformed band (Middleton et al., 1972). The authors address the instability of the $8p$-$8h$ configuration found in 3D Hartree-Fock calculations (Inakura et al., 2002) and the configuration mixing seen in GCM calculations (Bender et al., 2003), proposing that clustering stabilizes the superdeformation. The results enable future searches for $^{12}$C+$^{28}$Si molecular resonances in $^{40}$Ca, which have been suggested by fusion cross section data (Racca et al., 1983) and elastic scattering (Ost et al., 1979) at $E_{\rm cm}=20$-$30$ MeV above the $^{28}$Si+$^{12}$C threshold.
