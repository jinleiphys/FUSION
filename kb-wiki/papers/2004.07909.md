# Broken SU(6) symmetry and massive hybrid stars

> In this work we revisit the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) formalism to investigate hyperonic and hybrid stars with hyperon-meson couplings fixed via broken SU(6) group, in favor of a more general flavor group SU(3). We also employ an additional channel, the strangeness-hidden $ϕ$ meson, which couples only to the hyperons. In hybrid stars, the quark phase is built with the SU(3) NJL model also with an additional vector channel in the Lagrangian. We found that within the models chosen the hyperon puzzle cannot be avoided by the quark-hadron phase transition, once the hyperon threshold always happens at lower density. Also, the contribution of the quark core in a hybrid star is more relavant to the radius than to the mass, and strongly depends on the quark equation of state. We are able to reproduce 2.21 $M_\odot$ hyperonic star and 2.10 $M_\odot$ hybrid star. Both results are in agreement with the recently detected hyper massive pulsar MSP J0740+6620.

## Key claim

Within the specific QHD and NJL models chosen, the hyperon puzzle (the softening of the equation of state by hyperons that prevents reaching observed pulsar masses) cannot be avoided by a quark-hadron phase transition, because the hyperon threshold always occurs at a lower density than the quark deconfinement threshold; however, by breaking the SU(6) symmetry group in favor of SU(3) and tuning the free parameter α_v, the authors can still reproduce a 2.21 M⊙ hyperonic star and a 2.10 M⊙ hybrid star, both consistent with the massive pulsar MSP J0740+6620.

## Method

The hadronic phase was described using the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) formalism with a modified GM1 parametrization, including σ, ω, ρ mesons and an additional strangeness-hidden φ meson coupling only to hyperons. Hyperon-meson vector coupling constants were fixed via broken SU(6) symmetry in favor of SU(3) flavor symmetry, making all vector couplings dependent on a single free parameter α_v, while scalar couplings were fixed to reproduce hyperon potential depths (U_Λ = -28 MeV, U_Σ = +30 MeV, U_Ξ = -18 MeV). The quark phase was described using the SU(3) Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with an additional vector channel G_V, and hybrid star matter was obtained via Maxwell construction.

## Key numbers

- Maximum hyperonic star mass: 2.21 M⊙ for α_v = 0.25 (with central density n_c = 0.93 fm⁻³ and strangeness fraction f_sc = 0.129)
- Maximum hyperonic star mass for SU(6) case (α_v = 1.00): 2.00 M⊙ (R = 11.95 km, n_c = 0.92 fm⁻³, f_sc = 0.200)
- Maximum hybrid star mass: 2.10 M⊙ (with G_V = 1.0G_s, R = 10.60 km, n_c = 0.97 fm⁻³, f_sc = 0.155)
- Maximum quark star mass with LQCD-consistent G_V = 0.33G_s: 1.73 M⊙ (R = 9.59 km, n_c = 1.10 fm⁻³)
- Canonical 1.4 M⊙ star radius for all parametrizations: 13.68 km
- Hyperon potential depths used: U_Λ = -28 MeV, U_Σ = +30 MeV, U_Ξ = -18 MeV
- s-quark onset density in NJL model: approximately 0.66 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Lopes (2013) and Lopes (2020) on using symmetry groups to fix hyperon-meson couplings, now incorporating quark matter to study hybrid stars. It contradicts the possibility that a quark core could resolve the hyperon puzzle (the conflict between hyperon softening and observed 2 M⊙ pulsars like PSR J0348+0432 and MSP J0740+6620), finding instead that hyperons always appear before quarks. The results enable further investigation of the role of the φ meson and broken SU(3) symmetry in producing massive neutron stars, and suggest that pure quark stars require vector couplings far above lattice QCD constraints to reach observed masses, favoring the hybrid star scenario for massive pulsars.
