# Scalar and vector form factors of the in-medium nucleon

> Using the quark-meson coupling model, we calculate the form factors at sigma- and omega-nucleon strong-interaction vertices in nuclear matter. The Peierls-Yoccoz projection technique is used to take account of center of mass and recoil corrections. We also apply the Lorentz contraction to the internal quark wave function. The form factors are reduced by the nuclear medium relative to those in vacuum. At normal nuclear matter density and Q^2 = 1 GeV^2, the reduction rate in the scalar form factor is about 15%, which is almost identical to that in the vector one. We parameterize the ratios of the form factors in symmetric nuclear matter to those in vacuum as a function of nuclear density and momentum transfer.

## Key claim

Using the quark-meson coupling model, the scalar and vector form factors at sigma- and omega-nucleon vertices are reduced in nuclear matter relative to vacuum, with the reduction rate for the scalar form factor being about 15% at normal nuclear matter density and Q² = 1 GeV², which is almost identical to that for the vector form factor; however, the reduction depends on the confining potential parameter β and becomes more pronounced at higher densities, with the scalar form factor reduced by 35% for β = 0 versus 24% for β = 1.0 at twice normal density.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic constituent quark model with harmonic oscillator or linearly rising confining potentials, applying the Peierls-Yoccoz projection technique to account for center-of-mass and recoil corrections, and included Lorentz contraction of the internal quark wave function. The quark-meson coupling model was employed with mean-field sigma and omega mesons coupling to confined quarks, and the minimax principle was used to obtain approximate solutions to the Dirac equation for the quark wave functions. The form factors were calculated analytically in the Breit frame, and the ratios of in-medium to vacuum form factors were parameterized as quadratic functions of nuclear density.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ and Q² = 1.0 GeV², the scalar form factor is reduced by about 15% relative to vacuum, and the vector form factor by about 14% (for the harmonic oscillator potential)
- At ρ_B/ρ₀ = 2 and Q² = 1.0 GeV², the scalar form factor decreases by 35% (β = 0), 29% (β = 0.5), or 24% (β = 1.0); the vector form factor diminishes by 28% (β = 0), 26% (β = 0.5), or 22% (β = 1.0)
- The free quark mass m_q is taken as 300 MeV; the sigma meson mass m_σ = 550 MeV and omega meson mass m_ω = 783 MeV
- The effective nucleon mass ratio M_N*/M_N at ρ₀ is 0.71–0.72 for the parameterization with β = 0.5 (HO) or β = 1.0 (LR)
- The full vector form factor with Lorentz contraction gives a root-mean-square radius of 0.53 fm (HO case with β = 0.5); without Lorentz contraction it is 0.46 fm
- At ρ_B/ρ₀ = 2 and Q² = 1 GeV², the vector form factor with Lorentz contraction is about 7% larger than without it

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium nucleon form factors using the quark-meson coupling model, which had already been applied to electromagnetic form factors of bound nucleons (using an improved cloudy bag model) and axial form factors in nuclear matter. The calculated reduction of scalar and vector form factors is expected to help explain polarization transfer observables measured in exclusive (p, 2p) proton knockout reactions at RCNP and iThemba labs, which conventional relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation calculations have failed to reproduce. The parameterization provided enables re-analysis of those experimental data including both form factor modifications and meson mass changes in matter.
