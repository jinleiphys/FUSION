# Relativistic Friedrichs-Lee model and quark-pair creation model

> In this paper, we present how the Friedrichs-Lee model could be extended to the relativistic scenario and be combined with the relativistic quark pair creation model in a consistent way. This scheme could be applied to study the "unquenched" effect of the meson spectra. As an example, if the lowest $J^{PC}=0^{++}$ $(u\bar u+d\bar d)/\sqrt{2}$ bound state in the potential model is coupled to the $ππ$ continuum, two resonance poles could be found from the scattering amplitude for the continuum states. One of them could correspond to the $f_0(500)/σ$ and the other probably $f_0(1370)$. This scheme might shed more light on why extra states could appear in the hadron spectrum other than the prediction of the quark potential model.

## Key claim

The authors present a relativistic extension of the Friedrichs-Lee model combined with a relativistic quark-pair creation (QPC) model, and show that coupling the lowest \(J^{PC}=0^{++}\) \((u\bar u+d\bar d)/\sqrt{2}\) bound state from the potential model to the \(\pi\pi\) continuum can generate two resonance poles in the scattering amplitude, one corresponding to \(f_0(500)/\sigma\) and the other probably to \(f_0(1370)\), with the hedge that this scheme "might shed more light on why extra states could appear in the hadron spectrum other than the prediction of the quark potential model."

## Method

The authors first define relativistic canonical single-particle and two-particle states with Lorentz transformation properties, then construct a relativistic Friedrichs-Lee model in the angular momentum representation using creation and annihilation operators for a bare meson state and a bilocal field representing a two-particle continuum. The coupling form factor between the bare meson and the meson-pair continuum is derived from a relativistic QPC model following Fuda's approach, which accounts for Lorentz transformations when quarks and antiquarks regroup to form a new meson pair. The resolvent function \(\eta(s)\) is expressed in terms of the invariant mass squared \(s\) rather than energy \(E\), and the poles of the scattering amplitude are found by analytically continuing \(\eta(s)\) to the complex \(s\)-plane.

## Key numbers

* The lowest scalar meson predicted in potential models is located at above 1.0 GeV (Godfrey-Isgur model).
* The \(I=0\) \(\pi\pi\) scattering phase shift rises smoothly and passes \(90^\circ\) at about 850 MeV, as measured in \(\pi p\) experiments (Protopopescu 1973, Grayer 1974, Becker 1978).
* The two resonance poles from the coupled system contribute a mild total phase shift of about \(180^\circ\), consistent with experimental measurements.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic Friedrichs-Lee model (Friedrichs 1948, Lee 1954) and the non-relativistic QPC model (Micu 1968) to a relativistic formalism, building on earlier unitarized quark models by Tornqvist (1995) and van Beveren (1982, 1986) that used non-relativistic QPC interactions. The approach also follows the relativistic QPC treatment by Fuda (2012) and the method of Antoniou (1998) for incorporating relativistic effects into the Friedrichs model. This scheme enables the study of "unquenched" effects in meson spectra, particularly for light scalar mesons where the quark potential model predictions deviate from experiment, and could be applied to understand dynamically generated states such as \(X(3872)\) in the heavy quarkonium sector.
