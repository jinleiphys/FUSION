# The $Ω(2012)$ as a hadronic molecule

> Recently, a new excited baryon state, $Ω(2012)$, was observed in the invariant mass spectra of $K^-Ξ^0$ and $K^0_S Ξ^-$ by the Belle Collaboration. This state has a narrow width ($\sim 6$ MeV) compared to other baryon states with a similar mass. In this paper, we provide a mini-review on the $Ω(2012)$ state from the molecular perspective, where it appears to be a dynamically generated state with spin-parity $3/2^-$ from the coupled-channels interactions of the $\bar{K} Ξ(1530)$ and $ηΩ$ in $s$-wave and $\bar{K} Ξ$ in $d$-wave. Additionally, alternative explanations for the $Ω(2012)$ resonance are also discussed.

# The Ω(2012) as a hadronic molecule

## Key claim
The Ω(2012) resonance, observed by Belle with a narrow width of about 6 MeV, can be interpreted as a dynamically generated hadronic molecule with spin-parity 3/2⁻ arising from coupled-channel interactions of \(\bar{K} \Xi(1530)\) and \(\eta \Omega\) in s-wave and \(\bar{K} \Xi\) in d-wave, though alternative explanations such as a three-quark 1P-wave excitation remain viable and cannot be clearly distinguished.

## Method
The authors review the molecular interpretation of Ω(2012) using the chiral unitary approach, where the resonance is dynamically generated from coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations including \(\bar{K} \Xi(1530)\) and \(\eta \Omega\) in s-wave and \(\bar{K} \Xi\) in d-wave. They analyze the two-body decay \(\Omega(2012) \to \bar{K} \Xi\) and the three-body decay \(\Omega(2012) \to \bar{K} \Xi(1530) \to \bar{K} \pi \Xi\), extracting the effective coupling constant \(g_{\Omega(2012)\bar{K}\Xi}\) from the unitarized scattering amplitude. Alternative quark-model and pentaquark interpretations are also discussed.

## Key numbers
- Mass of Ω(2012): \(M = 2012.4 \pm 0.7 (\text{stat}) \pm 0.6 (\text{syst})\) MeV, measured by Belle in \(K^- \Xi^0\) and \(K^0_S \Xi^-\) invariant mass distributions.
- Width of Ω(2012): \(\Gamma = 6.4^{+2.5}_{-2.0} (\text{stat}) \pm 1.6 (\text{syst})\) MeV.
- The Ω(2012) mass is 16.6 MeV below the \(\bar{K} \Xi(1530)\) mass threshold (2029 MeV) and 198.5 MeV above the \(\bar{K} \Xi\) mass threshold (1813.9 MeV).
- Ratio of branching fractions: \(\mathcal{R}^{\bar{K}\pi\Xi}_{\bar{K}\Xi} = \text{Br}[\Omega(2012) \to \bar{K}\Xi(1530) \to \bar{K}\pi\Xi] / \text{Br}[\Omega(2012) \to \bar{K}\Xi] = 0.97 \pm 0.24 \pm 0.07\) from Belle (2022).
- Effective coupling constant: \(|g_{\Omega(2012)\bar{K}\Xi}| = 0.28 \pm 0.14\), derived from the measured ratio and total width.
- A dynamically generated state with mass around 2012.7 MeV and \(J^P = 3/2^-\) was predicted in the chiral unitary approach by Si-Qi et al. (2016) using subtraction constant \(a_\mu = -2.5\) and \(\mu = 700\) MeV.
- The three-body decay width for \(\Omega(2012) \to \bar{K}\pi\Xi\) is comparable to the two-body decay \(\Omega(2012) \to \bar{K} \Xi\) in the molecular picture.

## Context
This mini-review extends the molecular interpretation of Ω(2012) first proposed by Guo et al. (2017) and developed in chiral unitary approaches by Lin et al. (2018), Huang et al. (2018), Pavao et al. (2018), and Lu et al. (2020). It contrasts with classical quark-model predictions by Capstick (1986), Loring et al. (2001), and others, where Ω(2012) is interpreted as a 1P-wave orbital excitation of the ground-state Ω baryon. The paper highlights that the recent Belle measurement of the three-body decay ratio (2022) strongly supports the molecular picture, while the predicted spin-orbit partner with \(J^P = 1/2^-\) around 1950 MeV (Liu et al., 2019; Arifi et al., 2022) would be a crucial discriminator between the molecular and three-quark interpretations. Future measurements of radiative decay \(\Omega(2012) \to \gamma \Omega\) and femtoscopic studies of \(\eta \Omega\) and \(\bar{K} \Xi(1530)\) interactions are proposed to further clarify the nature of this state.
