# Saturation properties and liquid-gas phase transition of nucleus

> Saturation properties and liquid-gas phase transition of nucleus are analysed in the framework of Hatree-Fock theory. We modify Hill-Wheller formula with a finite-size-effect parameter by fitting the zero-temperature properties of nucleus. Employing Gogny effective interaction and phenomenological expression of Coulomb energy, we give the critical temperature of liquid-gas phase transition of nucleus being about 12 MeV, which agrees with the result extracted from heavy-ion collision experiments. It is pointed out that a phenomenological formula of surface energy of hot nucleus is not avaliable in the region where nucleon density is far away from the normal density.

## Key claim

The critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition of a nucleus is about 12 MeV, but this result depends on a modified Hill-Wheeler formula that introduces a finite-size-effect parameter (a_F = 0.35) to correctly reproduce zero-temperature saturation properties, which the unmodified Hill-Wheeler formula fails to do.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock calculations for finite nuclear matter using the Gogny D1 effective interaction, modifying the Hill-Wheeler formula for the density of states by introducing an adjustable finite-size-effect parameter a_F fitted to experimental binding energies of six typical nuclei (from ⁴⁰Ca to ²³⁸U). Coulomb effects were included via a phenomenological expression for Coulomb energy per proton, and the critical point of the liquid-gas phase transition was determined from the inflection point condition of the chemical potential versus density isotherms.

## Key numbers

- The finite-size-effect parameter a_F = 0.35 was obtained by fitting experimental binding energies of nuclei from ⁴⁰Ca to ²³⁸U.
- Saturation densities ρ₀ of the six typical nuclei are about 0.13 fm⁻³, yielding an effective radius parameter r₀ = 1.21–1.22 fm.
- Limit temperatures T_l for the six typical nuclei are about 9.0–9.5 MeV, with limit densities ρ_l about 0.075 fm⁻³.
- The critical temperature T_c of the liquid-gas phase transition for realistic nuclei is about 12 MeV, with mild dependence on nucleon number A.
- Finite-size effect, Coulomb interaction, and asymmetry together reduce the critical temperature by about 3–4 MeV compared to symmetric infinite nuclear matter.
- The surface energy expression E'_S = C₁ ρ^(2/3) A^(-1/3) + C₂ ρ^(5/3) A^(-1/3) was obtained with C₁ = 53.0 MeV·fm² and C₂ = 108 MeV·fm⁵.

## Context

This work extends earlier Hartree-Fock calculations of infinite nuclear matter (Jaqaman, Mekjian, and Zamick; Song) that used the unmodified Hill-Wheeler formula, which the authors show fails to reproduce correct zero-temperature binding energies for finite nuclei. The results are consistent with critical temperatures extracted from heavy-ion collision experiments by Panagiotou et al. (about 12 MeV) and with Monte Carlo calculations by Bondorf et al. (about 11 MeV for A=100). The paper also demonstrates that a widely used phenomenological surface energy expression with constant tension coefficient is not valid at densities far from normal nuclear density, and provides an alternative density-dependent surface energy formula that can be used in studies of the equation of state of hot nuclei.
