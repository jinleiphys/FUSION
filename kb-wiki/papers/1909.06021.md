# Status on $\mathbf{{}^{12}{\rm C}+{}^{12}{\rm C}}$ fusion at deep subbarrier energies: impact of resonances on astrophysical $S^{*}$ factors

> Since the discovery of molecular resonances in $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C in the early sixties a great deal of research work has been undertaken to study $α$-clustering and resonant effects of the fusion process at sub-Coulomb barrier energies. The modified astrophysical $S^{*}$ factors of $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion have been extracted from direct fusion measurements at deep sub-barrier energies near the Gamow window. They were also obtained by the indirect Trojan horse method (THM). A comparison of direct measurements and the THM, which elucidates problems in the analysis of the THM, is discussed in this Letter to the Editor.

# Status on \(^{12}\text{C}+^{12}\text{C}\) fusion at deep subbarrier energies: impact of resonances on astrophysical \(S^{*}\) factors

## Key claim

The indirect Trojan Horse Method (THM) data for the \(^{12}\text{C}+^{12}\text{C}\) fusion \(S^{*}\)-factor disagree with direct measurements in the energy range 2.16–2.7 MeV, and the steep rise of the THM \(S^{*}\)-factor at lower energies is an artifact of using a plane-wave approximation that neglects Coulomb and nuclear interactions.

## Method

The authors compare direct fusion measurements of the \(^{12}\text{C}+^{12}\text{C}\) modified astrophysical \(S^{*}\)-factor (defined as \(S^{*}(E) = \sigma(E)\,E\,\exp(87.21/\sqrt{E} + 0.46E)\)) with indirect THM data from the \(^{12}\text{C}(^{14}\text{N},d)(\alpha+^{20}\text{Ne})\) and \(^{12}\text{C}(^{14}\text{N},d)(p+^{24}\text{Na})\) reactions. They perform a \(\chi^{2}\) test between the THM data and direct data from Spillane (2010) and Jiang (2018) over 19 points in the interval 2.16–2.7 MeV, and they analyze the off-shell factors and penetrability ratios for different partial waves to assess the validity of the THM analysis.

## Key numbers

- The reduced \(\chi^{2}\) between THM data and direct data in the energy interval 2.16–2.7 MeV is 4.76; for \(\chi^{2} > 2.226\) the probability of coincidence is only 0.1%.
- After correcting a normalization mistake (THM used the total direct \(S^{*}\)-factor instead of the \(\alpha_1\) channel), the reduced \(\chi^{2}\) is still 2.76.
- Normalizing the THM data by a factor of 0.4 reduces the reduced \(\chi^{2}\) to 0.72 in the 2.16–2.7 MeV range, but increases it to 1.67 in the normalization interval 2.50–2.63 MeV.
- The THM \(S^{*}\)-factor uncertainty is \(\pm 21\%\).
- A strong resonance at 2.14 MeV was reported in direct measurements (Spillane, 2010), which is 1.87\(\sigma\) higher than the proposed upper limit.
- The deuteron energy for the \(^{24}\text{Mg}\) resonance at \(E=2.1\) MeV is \(E_{d+^{24}\text{Mg}} = 1.47\) MeV; at \(E=2.6\) MeV it is 0.97 MeV, both well below the Coulomb barrier of 3 MeV in the \(d+^{24}\text{Mg}\) system.
- Resonances in \(^{12}\text{C}+^{12}\text{C}\) have a characteristic width of about 100 keV.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing discussion on \(^{12}\text{C}+^{12}\text{C}\) fusion that began with the discovery of molecular resonances in the 1960s. It directly critiques the THM analysis of Tumino (2018), which reported a sharp rise in the \(S^{*}\)-factor at low energies, and compares it with direct measurements from Spillane (2010), Jiang (2018), and earlier work by Becker, Kettner, Aguilera, and Patterson. The paper identifies three main problems with the THM analysis: disagreement with direct data, contamination by non-THM mechanisms at energies above 2.2 MeV, and the use of plane-wave approximation which neglects Coulomb+nuclear interactions. The discovery of two strong resonances at 0.88 and 1.5 MeV in the THM data is acknowledged as an important achievement, and the paper calls for extending direct measurements below 2.1 MeV, performing new indirect measurements (one underway at Texas A&M University), and improving theoretical models to predict low-energy resonances.
