# Scalar-isoscalar states, gravitational form factors, and dimension-2 condensates in a large-Nc Regge approach

> Scalar-isoscalar states are analyzed within the large-Nc Regge approach. We find that the lightest f0(600) scalar-isoscalar state fits very well into the pattern of the radial Regge trajectory. We confirm the obtained mass values from an analysis of the pion and nucleon spin-0 gravitational form factors, recently measured on the lattice. We find that a simple two-state model suggests a meson nature of f0(600), and a glueball nature of f0(980), which naturally explains the ratios of various coupling constants. Finally, matching to the OPE requires a fine-tuned mass condition of the vanishing dimension-2 condensate in the Regge approach with infinitely many scalar-isoscalar states.

# Scalar-isoscalar states, gravitational form factors, and dimension-2 condensates in a large-Nc Regge approach

## Key claim

The lightest scalar-isoscalar state f0(600) fits into a single radial Regge trajectory with all other 0++ states listed in the PDG tables, provided the resonance half-width is used as the mass uncertainty in the fit; the meson nature of f0(600) and glueball nature of f0(980) are suggested by a two-state model, but the vanishing dimension-2 condensate required by OPE matching only emerges after regularization of the infinite Regge spectrum.

## Method

The authors fit all 0++ scalar-isoscalar states from the PDG tables to a single radial Regge trajectory of the form M_S(n)^2 = (a/2)n + m_sigma^2, using the half-width of each resonance as the mass uncertainty in a chi-squared minimization. They then analyze the pion and nucleon spin-0 gravitational form factors measured on the lattice to extract the sigma meson mass, and use a two-state model with Nc-scaling arguments to distinguish meson versus glueball nature. Finally, they apply zeta-function regularization to the infinite Regge spectrum to enforce the vanishing of the dimension-2 condensate C2 required by the operator product expansion.

## Key numbers

- Radial Regge trajectory slope: a = 1.31(12) GeV^2, with chi^2/DOF = 0.12
- Sigma meson mass from Regge fit: m_sigma = 556(127) MeV (lowest state excluded from fit)
- String tension from slope: sqrt(sigma) = 456(21) MeV, consistent with lattice value of 420 MeV
- Sigma meson mass from pion gravitational form factor fit: m_sigma = 445(32) MeV, with valence quark momentum fraction <x>_{u+d}^pi = 0.52(2)
- Sigma meson mass from nucleon gravitational form factor fit: m_sigma = 550^{+180}_{-200} MeV, with <x>_{u+d}^N = 0.447(14)
- Condition for vanishing dimension-2 condensate at leading Nc: m_{f0} = sqrt(a/2) = 810(40) MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of radial Regge trajectories for scalar states by Anisovich (2000, 2005) by including all 0++ states from the PDG tables and using half-widths as mass uncertainties. The gravitational form factor analysis confirms the mass estimates from lattice data by the LHPC collaboration (Hagler, 2007) and QCDSF collaboration (Gockeler, 2003) for the pion (Brommel, 2007) and nucleon. The two-state model distinguishing f0(600) as a meson and f0(980) as a glueball builds on Nc-scaling arguments from Pich (2002) and the pole versus Breit-Wigner definition analysis by Nieves (2009). The regularization of the dimension-2 condensate enables matching to the OPE, which otherwise would be violated by a finite number of states.
