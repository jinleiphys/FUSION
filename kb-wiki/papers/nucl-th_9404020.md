# Vibrations versus collisions and the iterative structure of two-body dynamics

> We adopt a truncated version of two-body dynamics by neglecting three-body correlations, as is supported by microscopic numerical calculations. Introducing orthogonal channel correlations for the pp- and the ph-channel and integrating the latter in terms of vibrational RPA-states we derive a retarded two-body equation. Its solution is nonperturbative with respect to loops, ladders and mixed contributions. In the stationary limit we obtain an equation for a generalised effective interaction which iterates both the G-matrix and the polarisation matrix. An in-medium scattering approach transparently demonstrates the collisional damping of the vibrations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a consistent treatment of both short-range (pp/hh) and long-range (ph) correlations, including their mutual mixing, is essential for approaching the true ground state energy of nuclear systems, and that three-body correlations can be safely neglected for configurations close to the ground state.

## Method

The authors adopt a truncated version of two-body dynamics by neglecting three-body correlations, supported by numerical calculations for a 16O model system. They introduce orthogonal channel correlations for the pp-channel and the ph-channel, integrate the ph-channel in terms of vibrational RPA-states, and derive a retarded two-body equation whose solution is nonperturbative with respect to loops, ladders, and mixed contributions. In the stationary limit they obtain an equation for a generalized effective interaction that iterates both the G-matrix and the polarization matrix, and they develop an in-medium scattering approach to demonstrate collisional damping of vibrations.

## Key numbers

* The time-averaged traces of correlation functions for the 16O model system show that the effect of higher-order correlations decreases by about one order of magnitude from tr C2 to tr C3 and again from tr C3 to tr C4, supporting convergence of the cluster expansion.
* The adiabatic switching time required to approach the correlated ground state is ts >= 10^-21 s, which guarantees the closest approach to the ground state energy in each limit (BORN, TDGMT, RPA, NQCD, SCD).
* The NQCD and SCD limits give practically the same result for the ground state energy of 16O, confirming that three-body terms in equation (2.2d) can be neglected for the investigations presented.

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions by Abrikosov et al. for infinite nuclear matter and Migdal for finite nuclei, which showed that ladder diagrams dominate at large momentum transfer while loop diagrams dominate at small momentum transfer. The paper addresses the mutual influence of short- and long-range correlations that is not included in standard G-matrix calculations or RPA calculations, which specialize on one channel only. The approach enables a nonperturbative treatment of channel mixing relevant for understanding occupation number distributions and damping widths of single-particle and collective excitations, and relates to the parquet formalism as discussed by earlier authors.
