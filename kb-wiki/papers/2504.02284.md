# Nonperturbative heavy quark diffusion coefficients in a weakly magnetized thermal QCD medium

> In this work, the perturbative and non-perturbative contributions to the heavy quark (HQ) momentum ($κ$) as well as spatial ($D_s$) diffusion coefficients are computed in a weak background magnetic field. The formalism adopted here involves calculation of the in-medium potential of the HQ in a weak magnetic field, which then serves as a proxy for the resummed gluon propagator in the calculation of HQ self-energy ($Σ$). The self-energy determines the scattering rate of HQs with light thermal partons, which is subsequently used to evaluate $κ$ and $D_s$. It is observed that non-perturbative effects play a dominant role at low temperature. The spatial diffusion coefficient $2πT D_s$, exhibits good agreement with recent LQCD results. These findings can be applied to calculate the heavy quark directed flow at RHIC and LHC energies. An extension of this formalism to the case of finite HQ momentum has also been attempted.

## Key claim

The authors compute heavy quark momentum and spatial diffusion coefficients in a weakly magnetized thermal QCD medium by using the in-medium heavy quark potential as a proxy for the resummed gluon propagator, finding that non-perturbative contributions dominate at low temperature and that the resulting spatial diffusion coefficient \(2\pi T D_s\) agrees with recent lattice QCD results, though the calculation is restricted to the regime \(qB/M_Q \ll 1\) where Landau quantization of heavy quark energy levels can be ignored.

## Method

The authors evaluate the heavy quark self-energy using the in-medium heavy quark potential, which incorporates both perturbative (Yukawa) and non-perturbative (string) contributions in a weak magnetic field, with the potential derived from the temporal component of the HTL-resummed gluon propagator modified by a weak magnetic field. The imaginary part of the potential is used to compute the scattering rate of heavy quarks with light thermal partons via the Weldon formula, from which the momentum diffusion coefficients \(\kappa_{\parallel}\) and \(\kappa_{\perp}\) (and subsequently the spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s\)) are obtained through integration over momentum transfer. The non-perturbative string contribution is included via a phenomenological ansatz for the gluon propagator that reproduces the confining term in vacuum, with the string tension \(\sigma\) related to a dimensional constant \(m_G^2\) as \(\sigma = \frac{2}{3}\alpha_s m_G^2\).

## Key numbers

* The Debye screening mass in the absence of magnetic field is \(m_D^2 = g^2 T^2 (N_c^2/3 + N_f/6)\), with the magnetic field contribution \(\delta m_D^2 = \frac{g^2}{12\pi^2 T^2}(q_f B)^2 \sum_{l=1}^\infty (-1)^{l+1} l^2 K_0(m_f l / T)\) where \(K_0\) is the modified Bessel function of the second kind.
* The perturbative (Yukawa) part of the potential in momentum space is \(V_Y(q) = -\frac{4}{3}g^2 \left[ \frac{1}{q^2 + m_D^2 + \delta m_D^2} + i \frac{\pi T [m_D^2 - \sum_f \frac{g^2 (q_f B)^2}{2\pi^2} \{F_1(1+\cos^2\theta) + F_2(7/3+\cos^2\theta)\}]}{q (q^2 + m_D^2 + \delta m_D^2)^2} \right]\).
* The non-perturbative (string) part of the potential is \(V_S(q) = -\frac{4}{3}g^2 \left[ \frac{m_G^2}{(q^2 + m_D^2 + \delta m_D^2)^2} + i \frac{\pi T m_G^2 [m_D^2 - \sum_f \frac{g^2 (q_f B)^2}{\pi^2} \{F_1(1+\cos^2\theta) + F_2(7/3+\cos^2\theta)\}]}{q (q^2 + m_D^2 + \delta m_D^2)^3} \right]\).
* The spatial diffusion coefficient \(2\pi T D_s\) shows good agreement with recent lattice QCD results.

## Context

This work extends previous perturbative QCD studies of heavy quark diffusion in magnetic fields (Singh 2020, Bandyopadhyay 2022, Dey 2024) by incorporating non-perturbative effects via the heavy quark potential approach, motivated by the fact that leading-order pQCD predictions for the spatial diffusion coefficient were an order of magnitude larger than lattice QCD results (He 2023). The formalism builds on the potential-based approach used by Hees (2008), Riek (2010), and Xing (2023) for the zero-field case, and extends the heavy quark potential calculations in magnetic fields by Lata (2018), Hasan (2020), and Indrani (2023) to compute transport coefficients. The results enable calculation of heavy quark directed flow \(v_1\) at RHIC and LHC energies, where experimental measurements by STAR (2019) and ALICE (2019) have shown non-zero D-meson directed flow.
