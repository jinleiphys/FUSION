# Geometry Induced Chiral Transport and Entanglement in $AdS_2$ Background

> We study the real-time chiral dynamics of Dirac fermions in AdS$_2$ and AdS$_2$ black hole backgrounds. The spacetime curvature generates a spin connection term, acting as an effective magnetic field and a position-dependent chiral chemical potential. This leads to strongly asymmetric wave propagation, confined within an inhomogeneous Lieb-Robinson cone. The front velocities decrease with increasing fermion mass and horizon radius. The entanglement entropy grows inside the causal cone, and it saturates due to screening/dephasing in the finite inhomogeneous chain. In dipole-dipole collision, the central bipartite entropy rises when the inward Lieb-Robinson fronts intersect, forming a bright ridge in the local entanglement profile. Charge and current correlators peak at the front arrival, providing a real-time diagnostic of chiral transport. These results establish a causality-respecting framework, linking curvature and horizons to transport and entanglement in (1+1)-dimensional fermionic matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that spacetime curvature in AdS₂ and AdS₂ black hole backgrounds generates chiral transport and entanglement dynamics through a spin connection term that acts as an effective position-dependent chiral chemical potential, even in the absence of gauge fields or external drives; the authors emphasize that this geometric chirality is a property of the curved background itself and persists irrespective of fermion mass.

## Method

The authors map continuum Dirac fermions in AdS₂ and AdS₂ black hole backgrounds to a one-dimensional spin chain using staggered fermions and a Jordan-Wigner transformation, producing a qubit Hamiltonian with redshift-weighted kinetic couplings and Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya type interactions from the spin connection. Real-time dynamics following a localized dipole quench are simulated using matrix product states with the time-dependent variational principle, with system size N=40, time step Δt=10⁻⁴, and maximum bond dimension χ_max=400.

## Key numbers

- System size N=40 sites used for all MPS simulations of charge and entanglement dynamics
- Time step Δt=10⁻⁴ used for real-time evolution up to t_max=5×10⁻²
- Maximum bond dimension χ_max=400 for time evolution; ground state bond dimension up to χ_gs≤1200
- Horizon radii r_h=0 (pure AdS) and r_h=5 (AdS black hole) compared
- Fermion masses m=0, 1, 4 studied in both pure AdS and black hole backgrounds
- Truncation cutoff 10⁻⁹ used in both DMRG and TDVP simulations

## Context

This work extends real-time tensor-network studies of (1+1)-dimensional fermions from flat space, where homogeneous couplings produce straight parity-symmetric causal cones, to curved AdS₂ backgrounds where the spin connection generates chirality without external fields. The approach connects gravitational physics with quantum information theory and is directly compatible with programmable quantum simulators capable of engineering spatially varying couplings, building on prior staggered fermion formulations by Kogut (1974) and Susskind (1976) and the Jordan-Wigner transformation by Jordan and Wigner (1928).
