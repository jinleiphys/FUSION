# Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen Correlations in Deuteron Photodisintegration

> Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen correlations have so far been measured only between pairs of photons and between pairs of protons. It is proposed to measure these correlations between the proton and the neutron emerging from breakup of the deuteron induced by gamma rays near threshold. The feasibility of the experiment is discussed. Polarimeters with substantially higher overall efficiency than the presently reported value of $10^{-4}$ are needed in order to get enough events.

# Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen Correlations in Deuteron Photodisintegration

## Key claim

The paper proposes measuring Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen (EPR) correlations between the proton and neutron from deuteron photodisintegration near threshold, but notes that polarimeters with substantially higher overall efficiency than the presently reported value of about 10^{-4} are needed to obtain enough events. The author also notes a personal motivation: he has proposed an alternative to the Copenhagen interpretation in which he predicts that no EPR correlations will show up between elementary particles that are not identical and not particle-antiparticle pairs.

## Method

The proposed experiment would follow the procedure of the proton-proton scattering experiment by Lamehi-Rachti and Mittig (1976): protons and neutrons from deuteron photodisintegration would each enter a spin-component analyzer (polarimeter) where they scatter from carbon foils and are registered by one of two detectors at fixed angles. The coincidences between detectors of the two analyzers are counted to compute the experimental spin correlation function E_exp, which is compared with the maximum value E_max compatible with Bell's inequality. The author analyzes the relative contribution of the spin triplet state (from E1 transitions) versus the desired singlet state (from M1 transitions) as a function of photon energy and angular acceptance, and estimates the achievable coincidence event rate.

## Key numbers

- Photodisintegration threshold: 2.226 MeV photon energy in the laboratory system
- Total cross section formula: sigma = 675 * sqrt(E_gamma/MeV - 2.226) / [(E_gamma/MeV) * (E_gamma/MeV - 2.149)] mb
- Maximum cross section: 2.5 mb at about 4.4 MeV photon energy
- At 22 MeV photon energy, cross section has decreased to 0.5 mb
- Relative triplet contribution in forward direction (Theta = 0 degrees) at E_gamma = 5 MeV: 11%
- Relative triplet contribution with opening half-angle alpha = 2 degrees at E_gamma = 5 MeV: 13%
- Maximum acceptable triplet contribution for demonstrating Bell inequality violation: 18% (r = 0.18, at Theta = 90 degrees)
- For r = 0.1 (10% triplet), the difference Delta between quantum and Bell-limited correlation is 0.07 at Theta = 50 degrees
- Coincidence rates in the deuteron experiment estimated to be smaller by a factor of 10^{10} than those in the pp experiment
- Overall polarimeter efficiency for nucleons of a few MeV: order of 10^{-4}
- Nucleon-carbon cross section for a few MeV nucleons: order of a barn (10^{-28} m^2)

## Context

This proposal extends the experimental study of EPR correlations beyond the only prior measurements with massive particles, which were on pairs of protons by Lamehi-Rachti and Mittig (1976), and the many experiments with photon pairs reviewed by Home and Selleri (1991). It differs from proposals involving atom pairs from molecular dissociation (Livi, 1978; Lo and Shimony, 1981) and particle-antiparticle pairs (reviewed in Home and Selleri, 1991; Tornqvist, 1988) by concerning elementary particles that are neither identical nor particle-antiparticle pairs. The paper identifies the need for improved polarimeter efficiency as the main technical barrier, and suggests exploring lower-energy nucleons (down to 100 keV) where nucleon-carbon cross sections increase considerably, potentially enabling novel polarimeter designs.
