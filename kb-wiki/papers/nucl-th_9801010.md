# Fermionic Molecular Dynamics and short range correlations

> Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) models a system of fermions by means of many-body states which are composed of antisymmetrized products of single-particle states. These consist of one or several Gaussians localized in coordinate and momentum space. The parameters specifying them are the dynamical variables of the model. As the repulsive core of the nucleon-nucleon interaction induces short range correlations which cannot be accommodated by a Slater determinant, a novel approach, the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM), is applied. The unitary correlator moves two particles away from each other whenever their relative distance is within the repulsive core. The time-dependent variational principle yields the equations of motion for the variables. Energies of the stationary ground states are calculated and compared to exact many-body results for nuclei up to Ca 48. Time-dependent solutions are shown for collisions between nuclei.

## Key claim

Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) combined with the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) can describe ground states of nuclei up to ⁴⁸Ca and time-dependent collisions, but the method relies on neglecting irreducible three-body and higher terms in the correlated Hamiltonian, which is justified only at typical nuclear densities where three-body corrections are estimated to be less than 5% of the binding energy for the α-particle.

## Method

The authors model fermionic systems using antisymmetrized many-body states composed of single-particle Gaussian wave packets localized in coordinate and momentum space, with the Gaussian parameters serving as dynamical variables. To handle short-range correlations from the repulsive core of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, they apply a unitary correlation operator C = exp(-iG) that moves pairs of nucleons apart when their relative distance lies within the repulsive core, and they derive equations of motion from the time-dependent variational principle. For ground states, they minimize the expectation value of the correlated Hamiltonian (keeping only one- and two-body terms) with respect to the uncorrelated Slater determinant of Gaussians; for time-dependent collisions, they use a simplified version of the correlated Hamiltonian with one Gaussian per particle and one Slater determinant.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for ⁴He, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, and ⁴⁸Ca calculated with the correlated Hamiltonian (keeping up to two-body terms) deviate by within 8% from exact Yakubovski results for ⁴He and CBF calculations for ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, and ⁴⁸Ca.
- Three-body contributions to the correlated Hamiltonian are estimated to give corrections less than 5% of the binding energy for the α-particle.
- For ⁶He, using two co-centered Gaussians per particle instead of one increases the binding energy by about 3.5 MeV.
- Time-dependent collisions of ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca are shown at impact parameter b = 2.75 fm and laboratory energy E_lab = 35 AMeV.

## Context

This work extends the Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) approach by introducing the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) to handle short-range correlations that cannot be captured by a single Slater determinant, in contrast to the Jastrow approach or Brueckner's G-matrix method. The UCOM method differs from the work of Suzuki (1990s) by defining the unitary generator based on the behavior of the relative wave function at small distances rather than on a preferred shell model basis. The results enable future calculations of nuclear ground states and collisions using a correlated Hamiltonian that is not density dependent, and the Gaussian basis is shown to be suitable for describing halo nuclei such as ⁶He.
