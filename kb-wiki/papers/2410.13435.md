# Microscopic description of spontaneous fission based on a Gogny energy density functional including tensor contributions

> This paper extends previous studies on the impact of tensor forces in fission dynamics of neutron-deficient Thorium isotopes to other isotopic chains of heavy actinides and low-mass super-heavy nuclei. Calculations are carried out within a mean-field framework based on the Gogny-D1S parametrization supplemented with the D1ST2a perturbative tensor term as driving force. Fission barrier heights and spontaneous fission half-lives are used as benchmarks to analyze the impact of the tensor term. A significant reduction of fission barrier heights and half-lives is associated to the tensor component of the force.

# Microscopic description of spontaneous fission based on a Gogny energy density functional including tensor contributions

## Key claim

The tensor component of the Gogny-D1ST energy density functional significantly reduces fission barrier heights and spontaneous fission half-lives across heavy actinide and low-mass super-heavy nuclei, but the effect is modulated by the mass-dependent behavior of the outer fission path, with a gentler decline at large deformation for some isotopes partially counteracting the barrier reduction.

## Method

Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations were performed using the Gogny-D1S parametrization supplemented with the D1ST2a perturbative tensor term, with axial and simplex symmetries maintained and the tensor contribution included only in the Hartree-Fock field (not the pairing field). Spontaneous fission half-lives were computed via the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) approximation using collective potentials that include vibrational and rotational zero-point corrections, with collective masses obtained from both the Gaussian Overlap Approximation (GCM) and the Adiabatic Time-Dependent HFB (ATDHFB) perturbative cranking schemes.

## Key numbers

- For $^{248}$Cf, the inner barrier height with D1S is $B_{I,GCM} = 12.55$ MeV and $B_{I,ATDHFB} = 11.74$ MeV, while with D1ST it is $B_{I,GCM} = 9.79$ MeV and $B_{I,ATDHFB} = 9.20$ MeV
- For $^{248}$Cf, the outer barrier height with D1S is $B_{II,GCM} = 7.01$ MeV and $B_{II,ATDHFB} = 6.04$ MeV, while with D1ST it is $B_{II,GCM} = 6.43$ MeV and $B_{II,ATDHFB} = 5.70$ MeV
- For $^{248}$Cf, the spontaneous fission half-life values are $\log_{10} t_{SF,GCM}^{D1S} = 15.66$ and $\log_{10} t_{SF,GCM}^{D1ST} = 12.71$, compared to the experimental value $\log_{10} t_{SF} = 12$
- For $^{248}$Cf, the ATDHFB half-life values are $\log_{10} t_{SF,ATDHFB}^{D1S} = 20.06$ and $\log_{10} t_{SF,ATDHFB}^{D1ST} = 17.29$
- At $Q_{20} = 16$ b in $^{248}$Cf, the underbinding of D1ST relative to D1S is $\delta V_{GCM} = 9.64$ MeV and $\delta V_{ATDHFB} = 9.39$ MeV
- The D1ST spherical configuration in $^{244}$Cm ($^{244}$Cf) is predicted 6.91 (6.56) MeV below the D1S one within the GCM scheme
- A constant zero-point energy $E_0 = 0.5$ MeV was employed for all calculations
- The tensor range parameter is $\mu_{TS} = 1.2$ fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies on tensor force effects in fission dynamics from neutron-deficient Thorium isotopes (Rodriguez-Guzman et al., 2020) to Curium, Californium, and Fermium isotopic chains. It builds on the Gogny-D1S parametrization (Berger et al., 1984) and the D1ST2a tensor extension (Marta et al., several works), which was previously shown to be crucial for opening a new valley associated with symmetric bimodal fission in neutron-deficient Th isotopes. The results enable systematic benchmarking of tensor contributions against experimental spontaneous fission half-lives (from the compilation of tsf-exp) and highlight the need for future inclusion of triaxiality and a full refit of the Gogny-EDF parameters including the tensor term.
