# Do lattice data constrain the vector interaction strength of QCD?

> We show how repulsive interactions of deconfined quarks as well as confined hadrons have an influence on the baryon number susceptibilities and the curvature of the chiral pseudo critical line in effective models of QCD. We discuss implications and constraints for the vector interaction strength from comparisons to lattice QCD and comment on earlier constraints, extracted from the curvature of the transition line of QCD and compact star observables. Our results clearly point to a strong vector repulsion in the hadronic phase and near-zero repulsion in the deconfined phase.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD data on baryon number susceptibilities constrain the vector repulsion strength of deconfined quarks to be near zero, while the hadronic phase requires a strong vector repulsion; earlier claims that the curvature of the pseudo-critical line alone can fix the quark vector coupling are not upheld because the curvature is sensitive to repulsive interactions in the confined phase, not necessarily the deconfined phase.

## Method

The authors construct a combined equation of state (EoS) that includes a hadronic parity-doublet model (with all hadronic resonances up to 2.2 GeV) and a Polyakov-loop extended quark model for deconfined quarks, connected by a smooth crossover. They compute the second- and fourth-order baryon number susceptibilities at zero baryochemical potential from this EoS and compare them to continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results, varying the vector coupling strengths for hadrons (\(g_V^B\)) and quarks (\(g_V^Q\)) separately. They also calculate the curvature \(\kappa\) of the chiral pseudo-critical line for different combinations of vector couplings.

## Key numbers

* Second-order baryon number susceptibility \(\chi_2^B\): the combined EoS with zero quark vector coupling reproduces lattice data (grey band from Borsanyi (2011)); a finite quark vector coupling \(g_V^Q\) causes a strong decrease above \(T_{PC}\).
* Fourth-to-second order susceptibility ratio \(\chi_4^B/\chi_2^B\): decreases by a factor of \(1/2\) above \(T_{PC}\) when a finite quark vector coupling is introduced, deviating from lattice results (grey band from Bellwied (2013)).
* Curvature of the chiral pseudo-critical line \(\kappa\): for the combined EoS with \(g_V^Q=0\) and \(g_V^N=5.563\), \(\kappa = 0.143\); with \(g_V^Q=3 g_S^Q\) and \(g_V^N=5.563\), \(\kappa = 0.1\).
* The hadronic excluded volume parameter corresponds to a hadronic radius of \(r = 0.84\) fm; the temperature-dependent sigmoid function uses \(\tau = 156\) MeV and \(\delta \tau = 8\) MeV.
* The quark mass suppression term uses \(m^0 = 400\) MeV, \(\tau = 130\) MeV, and \(\delta \tau = 3\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kunihiro (1991), who used an NJL model without a hadronic phase and could not fully disentangle phase contributions to susceptibilities. It contradicts claims by Bratovic (2012), Contrera (2012), and Lourenco (2012) that the quark vector coupling can be fixed from the curvature of the transition line and compact star masses; the authors show those constraints are not strict because a realistic hadronic phase and a strong change of vector coupling at deconfinement can accommodate both observables. The results provide strict constraints for effective models of QCD and enable a clearer separation of hadronic and quark repulsive interactions.
