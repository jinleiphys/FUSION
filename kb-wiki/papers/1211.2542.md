# High cumulants from the 3-dimensional $O(1, 2, 4)$ spin models

> Considering different universality classes of the QCD phase transitions, we perform the Monte Carlo simulations of the 3-dimensional $O(1, 2, 4)$ models at vanishing and non-vanishing external field, respectively. Interesting high cumulants of the order parameter and energy from O(1) (Ising) spin model, and the cumulants of the energy from O(2) and O(4) spin models are presented. The critical features of the cumulants are discussed. They are instructive to the high cumulants of the net baryon number in the QCD phase transitions.

## Key claim

The paper presents Monte Carlo simulations of 3-dimensional O(1), O(2), and O(4) spin models to obtain high cumulants of the order parameter and energy, arguing that these cumulants are instructive for understanding net baryon number fluctuations in QCD phase transitions, but only under the condition that the universality class mapping (Ising for the QCD critical point, O(4) or O(2) for chiral transitions) holds and that the finite-size system results are applicable to heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations using the Wolff algorithm with helical boundary conditions for the 3-dimensional Ising (O(1)), O(2), and O(4) spin models on cubic lattices of varying sizes (L=8 to L=24 depending on the observable and external field). They computed cumulants of the order parameter (up to sixth order) for the Ising model and cumulants of the energy (up to fourth or sixth order) for all three models, at three external field values (H=0, 0.05, 0.1), and analyzed the temperature dependence near the respective critical temperatures (T_c^Ising=4.51, T_c^O(2)=2.202, T_c^O(4)=1.068).

## Key numbers

- Critical temperatures used: T_c^Ising = 4.51, T_c^O(2) = 2.202, T_c^O(4) = 1.068 (reduced units, J=beta)
- System sizes for cumulant measurements: L=24 for Ising order parameter at H=0; L=20 for Ising energy at H=0 and for O(2) and O(4) energy at H=0; L=12 for Ising order parameter at H=0.05; L=10 for Ising energy at H=0.05 and O(2) energy at H=0.05; L=8 for O(4) energy at H=0.05 and for all models at H=0.1
- External field values used: H = 0, 0.05, 0.1
- For the Ising model energy cumulants: kappa_2^E shows a maximum peak; kappa_3^E changes sign from positive to negative; kappa_4^E has two positive maxima with a negative minimum between them; kappa_6^E has two negative minima and one positive maximum near T_c
- For the Ising model order parameter at non-zero field: kappa_2^S shows a peak; kappa_3^S has a negative valley with no sign change; kappa_4^S shows a positive maximum and a very small negative minimum; kappa_6^S oscillates from positive to negative
- For the Ising model order parameter at H=0: kappa_2^S shows a narrow sharp peak; kappa_3^S changes from negative to positive; kappa_4^S changes from positive to negative; kappa_6^S has two positive maxima and a negative minimum

## Context

This work extends the study of critical fluctuations from spin models to higher-order cumulants (up to sixth order), building on the known universality class mapping between the 3-dimensional Ising model and the QCD critical point (class 1, class 2, class 3, class 4) and between O(4) spin models and 2-flavor chiral transitions (class 4). The results are compared with effective model calculations such as the PNJL model (Fu weijie-PNJL) and PQM model (PQM model), as well as lattice QCD results (M. Cheng), showing qualitative consistency. The paper enables future comparisons between spin model predictions and experimental measurements of net baryon number cumulants from heavy-ion collision experiments such as those at RHIC (Star-BES).
