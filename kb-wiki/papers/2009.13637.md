# Resolving the Bethe-Salpeter kernel

> A novel method for constructing a Bethe-Salpeter kernel for the meson bound-state problem is described. It produces a closed-form kernel that is symmetry-consistent (discrete and continuous) with the gap equation defined by any admissible gluon-quark vertex. Applicable even when the diagrammatic content of that vertex is unknown, the scheme can foster new synergies between continuum and lattice approaches to strong interactions. The framework is illustrated by demonstrating that the presence of a dressed-quark anomalous magnetic moment in the gluon-quark vertex, an emergent feature of strong interactions, can remedy many defects of widely used meson bound-state kernels, including the level ordering of pseudoscalar and vector meson radial excitations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a novel method for constructing a Bethe-Salpeter kernel that is symmetry-consistent with any admissible gluon-quark vertex, even when the diagrammatic content of that vertex is unknown. The method is illustrated by showing that including a dressed-quark anomalous magnetic moment in the vertex can remedy many defects of widely used meson bound-state kernels, but the authors note that the construction yields a *minimal* solution, not the complete result.

## Method

The authors begin with the gap equation for the dressed-quark propagator and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states. They decompose the two-particle irreducible quark-antiquark scattering kernel into tensor products of Dirac matrices and impose constraints from parity, charge conjugation, and the vector and axial-vector Ward-Green-Takahashi identities. By expressing the self-energy in terms of its chirality-even and chirality-odd components and using a corresponding decomposition of the kernel, they derive a set of coupled integral equations that the kernel must satisfy. They then construct a minimal solution for the kernel by choosing the simplest allowable basis for the unconstrained part, using a gluon-quark vertex that includes a dressed-quark anomalous chromomagnetic moment with a Gaussian form factor.

## Key numbers

- With rainbow-ladder truncation (η=0), D=(1.105 GeV)^2, and m=3 MeV, the pion mass m_π = 0.14 GeV and pion decay constant f_π = 0.095 GeV.
- With the ACM-corrected vertex, the pairing D=(0.92 GeV)^2, η=2/5 yields f_π=0.095 GeV and m_π=0.14 GeV at m=3 MeV.
- The empirical a_1-ρ mass splitting of approximately 0.45 GeV is reproduced at η=1.6, compared to the rainbow-ladder result which is only 1/3 of the measured value.
- At η=1.6, the mass difference m_ρ′ - m_π′ ≈ 50 MeV, commensurate with the empirical value 170(100) MeV.
- The computed f_0 mass reaches approximately 1.3 GeV at η=1.6, matching an estimate of the q\bar q-core component from unitarised chiral perturbation theory.
- The radial excitation of the b_1-meson is predicted to have mass m_{b_1′} ≈ 1.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the program of symmetry-preserving truncations of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations developed by Chang (2009), Fischer (2009), Chang (2011), Williams (2015), and Binosi (2016). It directly addresses known failures of the rainbow-ladder truncation, which produces masses that are too light for most mesons except ground-state π and ρ mesons, as documented by Roberts (1996), Krassnigg (2009, 2010), Bashir (2012), and Xu (2019). The method enables new synergies between continuum and lattice approaches to strong interactions, as it can be applied even when the gluon-quark vertex is obtained from lattice QCD. The scheme can be extended to three-flavor QCD and to baryon Faddeev equations, which are currently only tractable using rainbow-ladder truncation.
