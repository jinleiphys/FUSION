# Properties and Stability of Hybrid Stars

> We discuss the properties of neutron stars and their modifications due to the occurrence of hyperons and quarks in the core of the star. More specifically, we consider the general problem of exotic particles inside compact stars in light of the observed two-solar mass pulsar. In addition, we investigate neutron star cooling and a possible explanation of the recently measured cooling curve of the neutron star in the supernova remnant Cas A.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hybrid stars containing both hyperons and a quark core can still reach masses consistent with the observed two-solar mass pulsar PSR J1614-2230, provided the quark phase is described with a unified model that includes vector interactions and a Polyakov-loop-like field, rather than a simple bag model.

## Method

The authors use a flavor-SU(3) hadronic model that includes the lowest baryon and meson multiplets, coupled to a quark phase described via a PNJL-like approach with an effective Polyakov loop field \(\Phi\) that controls deconfinement. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static and rotating stars, and compute cooling curves by modeling neutrino emission via direct Urca processes, with the spin-down evolution of rotating stars affecting the timing of the cooling drop.

## Key numbers

- Pulsar PSR J1614-2230 mass: \(M = 1.97 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\) (the observational benchmark).
- Purely nucleonic model (no hyperons): maximum star mass of 2.12 \(M_\odot\).
- Including hyperons: maximum mass drops slightly to 2.06 \(M_\odot\).
- Including hyperons and the spin-3/2 baryon decuplet (mainly \(\Delta\)): maximum mass of 1.93 \(M_\odot\).
- Reducing the hyperon vector coupling by 50 percent increases the strangeness fraction \(f_s\) from 0.1 to 1 (one strange quark per baryon) and reduces the maximum mass by half a solar mass.
- Onset of the quark phase reduces the maximum mass by about 10 percent relative to the purely baryonic value.
- The maximum mass in the baryonic model is reached at roughly 6 \(\rho_0\) (nuclear saturation density); quarks populate at densities beyond \(\rho \approx 4 \rho_0\).
- Rotating stars at the Kepler frequency show roughly a 20 percent increase in maximum mass compared to static solutions.
- The core-crust coupling time scale is about 100 years; the observed cooling drop in Cas A occurs at more than 300 years after the supernova explosion.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of exotic matter in neutron stars following the measurement of the two-solar mass pulsar PSR J1614-2230, which ruled out many models with hyperons that predicted lower masses (e.g., studies cited as arXiv:1006.5660 and arXiv:1107.2497). It contrasts with simple bag-model approaches (e.g., Alford et al., 2004) by using a unified hadron-quark model that can produce masses consistent with observation. The cooling analysis provides a possible explanation for the observed temperature drop in the Cas A neutron star, complementing alternative explanations based on neutron superfluidity (e.g., works cited as arXiv:1012.0045 and arXiv:1011.6142).
