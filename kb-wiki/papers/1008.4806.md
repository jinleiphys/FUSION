# Numerical Simulation of the Hydrodynamical Combustion to Strange Quark Matter

> We present results from a numerical solution to the burning of neutron matter inside a cold neutron star into stable (u,d,s) quark matter. Our method solves hydrodynamical flow equations in 1D with neutrino emission from weak equilibrating reactions, and strange quark diffusion across the burning front. We also include entropy change due to heat released in forming the stable quark phase. Our numerical results suggest burning front laminar speeds of 0.002-0.04 times the speed of light, much faster than previous estimates derived using only a reactive-diffusive description. Analytic solutions to hydrodynamical jump conditions with a temperature dependent equation of state agree very well with our numerical findings for fluid velocities. The most important effect of neutrino cooling is that the conversion front stalls at lower density (below approximately 2 times saturation density). In a 2-dimensional setting, such rapid speeds and neutrino cooling may allow for a flame wrinkle instability to develop, possibly leading to detonation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including hydrodynamics, self-consistent temperature evolution, and neutrino cooling in a 1D simulation of neutron matter burning to strange quark matter yields laminar front speeds of 0.002c to 0.04c, which are 5-6 orders of magnitude faster than earlier analytic estimates, but that neutrino cooling can cause the conversion front to stall at densities below approximately 2 times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors solved the 1D hydrodynamical flow equations for a system of u, d, and s quarks with a finite-temperature bag model equation of state, including strange quark diffusion, weak reaction rates for beta-equilibration, and neutrino emission with an exponential cutoff based on the neutrino mean free path. They used a fourth-order Runge-Kutta time integration with a third-order upwinded finite-volume spatial scheme, and evolved entropy density rather than energy density to self-consistently account for binding energy release during conversion to strange quark matter.

## Key numbers

- Burning front laminar speeds: 0.002c to 0.04c for initial baryon densities ranging from 1.7 n0 to 5.3 n0, where n0 is nuclear saturation density
- Interface width: approximately 1 cm when hydrodynamics is included, compared to approximately 10 cm for a purely reaction-diffusion system
- Diffusion coefficient: D approximately 10^-1 (mu_f / 300 MeV)^(2/3) (T / 10 MeV)^(-5/3) cm^2/s
- Neutrino mean free path: approximately 100 cm at temperatures of tens of MeV and densities of approximately 10^15 g/cc
- Grid spacing: Delta x = 0.05, with timestep constraints Delta t / Delta x < 0.3 from advection and Delta t / (Delta x)^2 < 1/D from diffusion

## Context

This work extends earlier reactive-diffusive treatments of strange quark matter combustion by Olinto (1987), Horvath (1988), and others, which assumed constant-temperature zero-thickness interfaces and yielded much slower burning speeds. The authors find that including hydrodynamics and self-consistent temperature evolution increases front velocities by 5-6 orders of magnitude over those earlier analytic estimates. The results enable future investigation of flame wrinkle instabilities in 2D, which could lead to detonation, and have implications for models of neutron star to strange quark star conversion as a mechanism for gamma-ray bursts, as studied by Lugones (2002), Ouyed (2002), and others.
