# Observables in Unpolarized and Polarized Virtual Compton Scattering

> Below pion threshold virtual Compton scattering off the nucleon gives access to the generalized electromagnetic polarizabilities. Different theoretical results for the generalized polarizabilities have been compared. In particular, the influence of the generalized polarizabilities on the unpolarized cross section and on double polarization observables has been investigated. Predictions for these observables have been obtained in the linear sigma model and chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

Below pion threshold, virtual Compton scattering (VCS) off the nucleon gives access to generalized electromagnetic polarizabilities (GPs), but the dipole approximation (keeping only leading order in the outgoing photon energy ω′) is valid only when ω′ ≤ 60 MeV and when the three-momentum transfer q̅ is much larger than ω′; the paper predicts that double polarization observables are needed to fully disentangle the six independent GPs, as unpolarized cross sections alone only constrain three of them.

## Method

The authors computed the VCS amplitude as the sum of the Bethe-Heitler (BH) amplitude, the VCS Born amplitude (determined by nucleon mass, charge, and elastic form factors G_E and G_M), and the residual amplitude T_R^{VCS} parametrized by ten generalized polarizabilities (GPs) in the dipole approximation (L′=1 for the real photon). They then performed a low-energy expansion of the spin-averaged matrix element to isolate the coefficient C_0^{Pol} containing the GP information, and expressed this coefficient in terms of structure functions for both the unpolarized cross section and for double-polarization target asymmetries T^{(i)} (i = x, y, z). Numerical predictions for the GP contributions were obtained using the linear sigma model (LSM) and heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to O(p³), for two kinematic settings: Bates kinematics (q̅ = 240 MeV, ε = 0.9, ω′ = 100 MeV) and MAMI kinematics (q̅ = 600 MeV, ε = 0.62, ω′ = 111 MeV).

## Key numbers

- For the proton electric polarizability at the real photon point, the LSM one-loop expansion gives α_p(0) = [13.6 − 8.8 + 4.2 + O(μ)] × 10⁻⁴ fm³, where μ = m_π/m_N.
- For the proton magnetic polarizability at the real photon point, the LSM one-loop expansion gives β_p(0) = [1.4 − 8.8 + 8.1 + O(μ)] × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- Empirical values for the proton at Q₀² = 0 are α_p^{exp}(0) = 12.1 ± 1.0 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and β_p^{exp}(0) = 2.1 ∓ 1.0 × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- HBChPT to O(p⁴) gives α_p = 10.5 ± 2.0 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and β_p = 3.5 ± 3.6 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ for the proton.
- For Bates kinematics (q̅ = 240 MeV, ω′ = 100 MeV), the GPs change the unpolarized cross section by about 20%, with the effect almost completely due to α(q̅).
- For MAMI kinematics (q̅ = 600 MeV, ω′ = 111 MeV), the GPs change the unpolarized cross section by about 15%, with only 60% of the signal due to the electric polarizability in the LSM.
- The maximum signature in the target asymmetry T^(x) is about 10% for MAMI kinematics but only about 3% for Bates kinematics.
- At ω′ = 70 MeV, the GP effects shown in the figures reduce by roughly 50% compared to the nominal kinematics.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for VCS developed by Guichon (1995) and Scherer (1996), who introduced the decomposition into Born and residual amplitudes and the parametrization of GPs. It compares predictions from the LSM (Metz, 1996, 1997), HBChPT (Hemmert, 1997; Knoechlein, 1997), and the nonrelativistic constituent quark model (Guichon, 1995; Liu, 1996), finding that the CQM violates model-independent constraints at q̅ = 0. The paper provides the first predictions for beam-target polarization asymmetries (relevant for the BLAST detector at MIT-Bates), complementing earlier work by Vanderhaeghen (1996, 1997) on beam-recoil polarization. The results enable experimental extraction of all six independent GPs through combined unpolarized and double-polarization measurements at facilities like MAMI, MIT-Bates, and Jefferson Lab, which will test chiral dynamics and resonance contributions to nucleon structure.
