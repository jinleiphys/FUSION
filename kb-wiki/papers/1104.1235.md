# Three-Body Model Analysis of Subbarrier alpha Transfer Reaction

> Subbarrier alpha transfer reaction 13C(6Li,d)17O(6.356 MeV, 1/2+) at 3.6 MeV is analyzed with a alpha + d + 13C three-body model, and the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for alpha + 13C --> 17O(6.356 MeV, 1/2+), which essentially determines the reaction rate of 13C(alpha,n)16O, is extracted. Breakup effects of 6Li in the initial channel and those of 17O in the final channel are investigated with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC). The former is found to have a large back-coupling to the elastic channel, while the latter turns out significantly small. The transfer cross section calculated with Born approximation to the transition operator, including breakup states of 6Li, gives (C_{alpha 13C}{17O*})^2 =1.03 \pm 0.29 fm^{-1}. This result is consistent with the value obtained by the previous DWBA calculation.

## Key claim

The asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for the alpha + 13C -> 17O(6.356 MeV, 1/2+) system is extracted from a three-body model analysis of the subbarrier transfer reaction, yielding a value consistent with the previous DWBA result; however, the breakup of 6Li is found to have a large back-coupling effect on the elastic channel, while the breakup of 17O is negligible, and the breakup transfer process itself is much smaller than the elastic transfer process.

## Method

The 13C(6Li,d)17O(6.356 MeV, 1/2+) reaction at 3.6 MeV was analyzed using an alpha + d + 13C three-body model. Breakup effects of 6Li in the initial channel and 17O in the final channel were investigated with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC), and the transfer cross section was calculated with Born approximation to the transition operator, including breakup states of 6Li.

## Key numbers

*   (C_alpha13C^17O*)^2 = 1.03 ± 0.25 (theor) ± 0.15 (expt) fm^{-1}, extracted from the 13C(6Li,d)17O(6.356 MeV, 1/2+) reaction at 3.6 MeV.
*   The previous DWBA analysis gave (C_alpha13C^17O*)^2 = 0.89 ± 0.23 fm^{-1}, consistent with the present result within uncertainties.
*   The breakup effects of 17O on the d-17O elastic cross section are at most 5% for angles greater than about 60 degrees.
*   The absolute value of the d-17O wave function for J=0 at R_dO = 10 fm changes by about 3% when breakup states of 17O are included (0.982 with breakup, 0.956 without).

## Context

This work extends the DWBA analysis of the same reaction by Johnson and collaborators (Eric1), who extracted an ANC of 0.89 ± 0.23 fm^{-1} without considering breakup effects. The present three-body approach using CDCC, developed by the Kyushu group, explicitly accounts for the breakup of 6Li and 17O, finding that the back-coupling effect of 6Li breakup is important but that the DWBA result is still consistent. This enables more reliable extraction of ANCs for astrophysical alpha-induced reactions, such as 13C(alpha,n)16O, which is a neutron source for the s-process in AGB stars.
