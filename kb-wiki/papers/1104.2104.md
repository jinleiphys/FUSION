# Analytic derivation of an approximate SU(3) symmetry inside the symmetry triangle of the Interacting Boson Approximation model

> Using the contraction of the SU(3) algebra to the algebra of the rigid rotator in the large boson number limit of the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) model, a line is found inside the symmetry triangle of the IBA, along which the SU(3) symmetry is preserved. The line extends from the SU(3) vertex to near the critical line of the first order shape/phase transition separating the spherical and prolate deformed phases, and lies within the Alhassid--Whelan arc of regularity, the unique valley of regularity connecting the SU(3) and U(5) vertices amidst chaotic regions. In addition to providing an explanation for the existence of the arc of regularity, the present line represents the first example of an analytically determined approximate symmetry in the interior of the symmetry triangle of the IBA. The method is applicable to algebraic models possessing subalgebras amenable to contraction. This condition is equivalent to algebras in which the equilibrium ground state (and its rotational band) become energetically isolated from intrinsic excitations, as typified by deformed solutions to the IBA for large numbers of valence nucleons.

## Key claim

Using the contraction of the SU(3) algebra to the rigid rotator algebra in the large boson number limit of the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) model, the authors derive an analytic line inside the IBA symmetry triangle along which the Hamiltonian approximately commutes with the SU(3) generators, with the caveat that this holds only for the lower part of the spectrum and in the limit where the ground state band becomes energetically isolated.

## Method

The authors start from the Alhassid-Whelan IBA Hamiltonian with parameters η and χ, and compute the commutators of the Hamiltonian with the SU(3) quadrupole generators. In the large boson number limit, they exploit the contraction of SU(3) to [R⁵]SO(3), where the quadrupole operators become mutually commuting and can be replaced by the intrinsic quadrupole moment. By requiring the commutators to vanish, they derive an analytic condition relating η and χ. They also perform a complementary calculation of matrix elements within the ground state band to justify the approximations used.

## Key numbers

- The derived line in the large N limit is given by χ(η) = (1/(2√2)) × η/(1-η) - √7/2, where η ranges from 1 to 0.789 and χ ranges from -√7/2 to 0.
- The alternative matrix-element derivation gives χ(η) = (√7/8) × η/(1-η) - √7/2, with √7/8 = 0.3307 compared to 1/(2√2) = 0.3536 in the first derivation.
- For N=250, the ratio R₂/R₁ deviates by less than 1% from the approximate value 16/3 for angular momenta between 8 and 88, and by less than 5% for angular momenta between 4 and 176.
- The critical line in the large N limit corresponds to η_crit = 0.8 for χ=0 and η_crit = 9/11 = 0.818 for χ = -√7/2.
- The line cannot reach the critical line, being confined between the critical line and the SU(3) vertex.

## Context

This work provides the first analytical identification of an approximate symmetry inside the IBA symmetry triangle, extending prior numerical observations of the Alhassid-Whelan arc of regularity (Alhassid and Whelan, 1990s) and the E(2⁺_β)=E(2⁺_γ) degeneracy line (Bonatsos et al., 2010). The result explains the origin of the arc of regularity as arising from an underlying approximate SU(3) symmetry, and the method is applicable to other algebraic models whose subalgebras admit contraction, such as IBA-2, sdg-IBA, and spdf-IBA models.
