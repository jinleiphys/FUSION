# Signatures of the chiral two-pion exchange electromagnetic currents in the 2H and 3He photodisintegration reactions

> The recently derived long-range two-pion exchange (TPE) contributions to the nuclear current operator which appear at next-to-leading order (NLO) of the chiral expansion are used to describe electromagnetic processes. We study their role in the photodisintegration of 2H and 3He and compare our predictions with experimental data. The bound and scattering states are calculated using five different parametrizations of the chiral next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential which allows us to estimate the theoretical uncertainty at a given order in the chiral expansion. For some observables the results are very close to the predictions based on the AV18 NN potential and the current operator (partly) consistent with this force. In the most cases, the addition of long-range TPE currents improved the description of the experimental data.

## Key claim

The long-range two-pion exchange (TPE) contributions to the nuclear current operator, derived at next-to-leading order (NLO) in chiral effective field theory, improve the description of experimental photodisintegration data for both deuteron and helium-3, but the calculations are not yet complete because the corresponding one-pion exchange at NLO and short-range contributions to the current operator are not yet available.

## Method

The authors calculated photodisintegration observables for deuteron and helium-3 using five different parametrizations of the chiral N2LO nucleon-nucleon potential, varying the cutoff parameters Lambda between 450 and 600 MeV and tilde-Lambda between 500 and 700 MeV. They included single-nucleon, one-pion exchange (OPE), and the newly derived long-range two-pion exchange (TPE) contributions to the nuclear current operator, solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the two-nucleon system and Faddeev-type equations for the three-nucleon system in momentum space with partial wave decomposition.

## Key numbers

- Photon laboratory energies studied: E_gamma = 10, 30, and 60 MeV for deuteron photodisintegration
- Photon laboratory energies studied: E_gamma = 12, 20.5, and 50 MeV for helium-3 two-body photodisintegration
- Cutoff parameter Lambda varied between 450 and 600 MeV; tilde-Lambda varied between 500 and 700 MeV
- Total cross section calculated for E_gamma approximately 2 to 80 MeV for deuteron photodisintegration
- Energy bins for deuteron tensor analyzing powers: E_gamma = 25-45 MeV and E_gamma = 45-70 MeV
- Partial wave truncation: total angular momentum J less than or equal to 15/2 and subsystem total angular momentum j less than or equal to 3 for three-nucleon calculations

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to electromagnetic currents by applying the leading two-pion exchange current operator derived by the same group (TPE:2009) to photodisintegration reactions for the first time. It builds on prior work using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential with associated current operators (Riska 1989) and earlier chiral EFT studies of deuteron electromagnetic properties (Walzl 2001, Phillips 2009) and helium-3 (Shukla 2009). The results enable future inclusion of subleading OPE and short-range current contributions to achieve a complete NLO description of electromagnetic reactions within chiral EFT.
