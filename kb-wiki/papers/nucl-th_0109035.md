# Mesons as Bound States of Confined Quarks: Zero and Finite Temperature

> We survey recent work on the properties and decays of mesons as bound states of confined quarks at both zero and finite temperature. The framework for these investigations is the set of QCD Dyson--Schwinger equations truncated to ladder-rainbow level. The infrared structure of the ladder-rainbow kernel is described by two parameters; the ultraviolet behavior is fixed by the one-loop renormalizaton group behavior of QCD. The work is restricted to the $u$, $d$ and $s$ quark sector and allows a Poincaré-covariant study of the masses and electroweak decay constants of the ground state pseudoscalars and vectors: $π$, $K$, $ρ/ω$, $K^\star$ and $ϕ$. Within the impulse approximation, we summarize results for the $π$ and $K$ charge form factors. Their timelike behavior exhibits the vector meson production resonances and from this the associated vector meson strong decay constants are extracted. The confining property of the quark self-energy dynamically produced in this work prevents the appearance of spurious $\bar{q}q$ production effects. The finite temperature behavior of this model, and closely related ones, is summarized for: the chiral restoration transition, the deconfinement transition, the $T$-dependence of the spatial meson mode masses, and the $T$-dependence of selected electroweak and strong decay widths. The $T$-dependence obtained for meson masses is compared to results from lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The ladder-rainbow truncation of the QCD Dyson-Schwinger equations, with an effective interaction constrained by perturbative QCD in the ultraviolet and a phenomenological infrared form, can simultaneously describe the masses, decay constants, and electromagnetic form factors of light pseudoscalar and vector mesons at zero temperature, and the chiral restoration and deconfinement transitions at finite temperature, provided that the truncation preserves the relevant Ward-Takahashi identities.

## Method

The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation in rainbow truncation coupled to the meson Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder truncation, using a model effective coupling that combines an infrared Gaussian term (parameters ω = 0.4 GeV and D = 0.93 GeV²) with the one-loop perturbative QCD running coupling in the ultraviolet. The dressed quark propagators, meson bound-state amplitudes, and dressed quark-photon vertex (from the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation) are then used in the impulse approximation to compute electromagnetic form factors. At finite temperature, the Matsubara formalism is employed to study the chiral phase transition and the temperature dependence of spatial meson masses and decay widths.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for chiral restoration: 120–150 MeV (second-order, mean-field transition, depending on ladder kernel details)
- Pion mass: calculated 0.138 GeV (fitted to experiment)
- Pion decay constant: calculated 0.131 GeV (fitted to experiment)
- Kaon mass: calculated 0.497 GeV (fitted to experiment)
- Kaon decay constant: calculated 0.155 GeV (experiment: 0.160 GeV)
- Rho meson mass: calculated 0.742 GeV (experiment: 0.770 GeV)
- Rho meson decay constant: calculated 0.207 GeV (experiment: 0.216 GeV)
- Phi meson mass: calculated 1.072 GeV (experiment: 1.020 GeV)
- Pion charge radius squared: calculated 0.45 fm² (experiment: 0.44 ± 0.01 fm²)
- Charged kaon charge radius squared: calculated 0.38 fm² (experiment: 0.34 ± 0.05 fm²)
- Neutral kaon charge radius squared: calculated -0.086 fm² (experiment: -0.054 ± 0.026 fm²)
- Chiral condensate at renormalization scale: calculated (0.241 GeV)³ (experimental estimate: (0.236 GeV)³)

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach developed by Maris and Roberts (1997) and Maris and Tandy (1999) for light meson properties, providing a unified framework for both zero-temperature observables and finite-temperature transitions. The results for the pion form factor improve upon earlier semi-phenomenological studies by Bicudo, Ribeiro, and Tandy (1996) by using numerical solutions of truncated DSEs for all elements. The finite-temperature results for spatial meson masses are compared to lattice QCD calculations, and the approach enables future studies of meson properties beyond the ladder-rainbow truncation and inclusion of meson loop corrections.
