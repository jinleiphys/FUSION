# Hydrodynamic modeling of pseudorapidity flow correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and the torque effect

> We analyze correlations between the event-plane angles in different intervals of pseudorapidity within the 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics with the Glauber-model initial conditions. As predicted earlier, the fluctuations in the particle production mechanism in the earliest stage, together with asymmetry of the emission profiles in pseudorapidity from the forward- and backward going wounded nucleons, lead to the torque effect, namely, decorrelation of the event-plane angles in distant pseudorapidity bins. We use two- or three-bin measures of correlation functions to quantify the effect, with the latter compared to the recent data from the CMS collaboration. We find a sizable torque effect, with magnitude larger at RHIC than at the LHC.

## Key claim

The torque effect, decorrelation of event-plane angles across pseudorapidity, is confirmed in 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations with Glauber-model initial conditions, but the magnitude of the effect depends on the specific asymmetric longitudinal emission profile assumed for wounded nucleons, and the model's quantitative agreement with CMS data is limited by finite statistics.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations (η/s=0.08, ζ/s=0.04) with Glauber Monte Carlo initial conditions from GLISSANDO, using an asymmetric longitudinal entropy deposition profile for forward- and backward-going wounded nucleons. The fluid evolved from τ₀=0.6 fm/c to freeze-out at T_F=150 MeV, followed by statistical hadronization and resonance decays via THERMINATOR, generating 200–300 hydro events per centrality with 200–2000 THERMINATOR events per freeze-out hypersurface. Two- and three-bin correlation measures of event-plane angles were computed for charged hadrons and compared to CMS data.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.08 (temperature-independent); bulk viscosity ζ/s = 0.04 (for T < 170 MeV)
- Initial time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c; freeze-out temperature T_F = 150 MeV
- Gaussian width of transverse nucleon profile σ = 0.4 fm; mixed Glauber parameter α = 0.12 at RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV) and 0.15 at LHC (√s_NN = 2.76 TeV)
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section: 42 mb at RHIC, 63 mb at LHC
- Longitudinal profile parameters: σ_η = 1.4; η_p = 0.75 at RHIC, 1.15 at LHC
- 200–300 hydro events per centrality; 200–2000 THERMINATOR events per freeze-out hypersurface
- Pseudorapidity bins: -2.4 < η^a < 2.4, 4 < η^b < 5; transverse momentum cuts: 0.3 < p_T^a < 3 GeV, 0 < p_T^b < 3 GeV (LHC); 0.15 < p_T < 3 GeV (RHIC) for 2-bin measures

## Context

This work extends the torque effect prediction of Bozek (2010) by performing the first full event-by-event hydrodynamic analysis, rather than using the earlier approach of implementing a fixed standard deviation of the torque angle. The three-bin correlation measure follows the method introduced by the CMS Collaboration (2015) to minimize non-flow effects. The results enable future application of Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to hydrodynamic models, as proposed by Bhalerao (2014), and provide a framework for testing alternative initial-state models such as those from Petersen (2011), Xiao (2012), Jia (2014), and Pang (2014).
