# The $ΛΛ\to YN$ Weak Transition in Double-$Λ$ Hypernuclei

> We calculate the $ΛΛ\to YN$ transition rate of ${^{\phantomΛ6}_{ΛΛ}}$He by the hybrid picture, the $π$ and $K$ exchanges plus the direct quark processes. It is found that the hyperon-induced decay is weaker than the nucleon-induced decay, but the former may reveal the short-range mechanism of the weak transition and also give a clear signal of the strong $ΔI=3/2$ transition. The $ΛΛ\to Y N$ transition in double-$Λ$ hypernucleus is complement to the $ΛN \to NN$ transition as it occurs only in the J=0 channel, while the J=1 transition is dominant in the $ΛN \to NN$ case.

## Key claim

The hyperon-induced decay (\(\Lambda\Lambda\to YN\)) in double-\(\Lambda\) hypernuclei is weaker than the nucleon-induced decay (\(\Lambda N\to NN\)), but it may reveal the short-range mechanism of the weak transition and provide a clear signal of the strong \(\Delta I=3/2\) transition, because it occurs only in the \(J=0\) channel, whereas the \(J=1\) transition dominates in the \(\Lambda N\to NN\) case.

## Method

The authors calculated the \(\Lambda\Lambda\to YN\) transition rate for \({^{\phantom{\Lambda}6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}}\)He using a quark-meson hybrid model: at long and medium distances, one-pion exchange (OPE) and one-kaon exchange (OKE) potentials were employed, while at short distances a direct quark (DQ) transition potential based on the constituent quark picture and the effective four-quark weak Hamiltonian was used. The initial \(\Lambda\Lambda\) pair was assumed to be in a \(^1S_0\) state, described by a Gaussian wave function with a size parameter \(b=1.9\) fm, and phenomenological correlation functions were applied to both the initial and final states. The final \(YN\) state was treated as a plane wave.

## Key numbers

- The total \(\Lambda\Lambda\to\Lambda n\) decay rate in the full \(\pi+K+\)DQ model is \(0.0024\ \Gamma_\Lambda\) (where \(\Gamma_\Lambda\) is the free \(\Lambda\) decay rate).
- The total \(\Lambda\Lambda\to\Sigma^0 n\) decay rate in the full model is \(0.0065\ \Gamma_\Lambda\).
- The total \(\Lambda\Lambda\to\Sigma^- p\) decay rate in the full model is \(0.0085\ \Gamma_\Lambda\).
- The hyperon binding energy used is \(\epsilon_{\Lambda\Lambda}\simeq 7.25\) MeV.
- The cutoff parameters for the meson exchange form factors are \(\Lambda_{\pi}=800\) MeV and \(\Lambda_{K}=1300\) MeV.
- The effective meson mass energy transfer \(q^0\) is 88.5 MeV for \(\Lambda\Lambda\to\Lambda n\) and 50.2 MeV for \(\Lambda\Lambda\to\Sigma N\).
- The strong coupling constants used include \(g_{\Lambda\Sigma\pi}=12.04\) and \(g_{\Lambda NK}=-17.65\).
- The weak coupling constants (in units of \(g_w\equiv G_F m_\pi^2 = 2.21\times10^{-7}\)) include \(A_{\Lambda n\pi^0}=-1.05\) and \(B_{\Lambda n\pi^0}=7.15\) for the pion, and \(A_{\Lambda\Lambda K^0}=0.67\) and \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda K^0}=12.72\) for the kaon.

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson hybrid model previously applied to the \(\Lambda N\to NN\) transition (Sasaki et al., 2001; 2002) to the double-\(\Lambda\) system, specifically the \(\Lambda\Lambda\to YN\) transition in \({^{\phantom{\Lambda}6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}}\)He. It contrasts with earlier calculations by Itonaga et al. (2002), which included two-pion exchange in scalar and vector channels, and by Parreno et al. (2002), which used all pseudoscalar and vector mesons with different treatments of final state interactions. The results enable direct experimental tests of the \(\Delta I=1/2\) rule in the \(J=0\) channel, which is masked in \(\Lambda N\to NN\) decays, and may help clarify the role of short-range weak baryonic interactions.
