# Behaviour of the $ΛN$ and $ΛNN$ potential strengths in the $_Λ^5$He hypernucleus

> Variational study of $_Λ^5$He hypernucleus is presented using a realistic Hamiltonian and a fully correlated wave function including $ΛN$ space-exchange correlation. Behaviour of the $Λ$-separation energy ($B_Λ$) with two- and three- baryon potential strengths is thoroughly investigated. Solutions of the potential strengths giving experimental $B_Λ$ are presented.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the Λ-separation energy (B_Λ) of the _Λ^5He hypernucleus can be reproduced by a range of combinations of the ΛN potential strengths (spin-average strength v̄ and space-exchange parameter ε) and the ΛNN potential strengths (C^P and W^D), but these strengths are not uniquely determined from this single hypernucleus alone; the solutions form continuous curves in parameter space.

## Method
A variational Monte Carlo calculation was performed for the _Λ^5He hypernucleus using a realistic Hamiltonian with the Argonne v_18 NN potential, an Urbana-type NNN potential, a charge-symmetric ΛN potential including space-exchange correlation, and a ΛNN potential with dispersive and two-pion exchange terms. The wave function was fully correlated, including ΛN space-exchange correlations, and the ground-state energy was computed as a function of the ΛN and ΛNN potential strengths. For each combination of strengths, the repulsive ΛNN strength W^D was adjusted to reproduce the experimental B_Λ.

## Key numbers
- The spin-average ΛN potential strength v̄ is approximately 6.15(5) MeV, consistent with low-energy Λp scattering data.
- Three sets of ΛN singlet and triplet strengths were used: v̄1 = 6.15 MeV (v_s=6.33, v_t=6.09), v̄2 = 6.10 MeV (v_s=6.28, v_t=6.04), v̄3 = 6.05 MeV (v_s=6.23, v_t=5.99), all with v_σ = 0.24 MeV.
- The space-exchange parameter ε was varied over 0.1, 0.2, and 0.3.
- The ΛNN p-wave strength C^P was varied from 0.5 MeV up to 2.0 MeV.
- The s-wave ΛNN strength C^S was taken as 1.5 MeV, based on comparison with the Tucson-Melbourne NNN model where |A^S| ≈ 0.8 MeV.
- For a 4-fold increase in C^P (e.g., from 0.5 to 2.0 MeV), the attraction ⟨V_Λij^{2π}⟩ increases more than 12 times, and the nuclear core energy E_NC increases by about 4 MeV.

## Context
This work extends variational studies of s-shell hypernuclei by Usmani et al. (2006) and addresses the outstanding A=5 anomaly (Dabrowski, 1972; Gal, 1975; Hungerford, 1984). The results provide constraints on ΛN and ΛNN potential strengths that can be applied to other single- and double-Λ hypernuclei, including the conjectured bound _ΛΛ^4H state (Ahn, 2001). The study enables future investigations of heavy hypernuclei such as _Λ^209Pb and charmed or bottom hypernuclei, as well as neutron star structure.
