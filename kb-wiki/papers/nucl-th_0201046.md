# Scaling in the Lattice Gas Model

> A good quality scaling of the cluster size distributions is obtained for the Lattice Gas Model using the Fisher's ansatz for the scaling function. This scaling identifies a pseudo-critical line in the phase diagram of the model that spans the whole (subcritical to supercritical) density range. The independent cluster hypothesis of the Fisher approach is shown to describe correctly the thermodynamics of the lattice only far away from the critical point.

## Key claim

Fisher scaling of cluster size distributions in the Lattice Gas Model produces a good-quality collapse of data across a wide density range, but the extracted critical temperature is about 20% higher than the thermodynamic critical point, and the independent-cluster hypothesis of the Fisher model correctly describes the lattice thermodynamics only at low densities and high temperatures, far from the critical point.

## Method

The authors performed canonical-ensemble Metropolis Monte Carlo simulations of the Lattice Gas Model on a cubic lattice with periodic boundary conditions, defining fragments via the Coniglio-Klein bond-breaking probability. They applied both renormalization-group (RG) scaling and Fisher droplet-model scaling to the cluster size distributions at various densities and temperatures, fitting the Fisher ansatz with polynomial parametrizations of the chemical potential difference and surface energy coefficient, and compared the reconstructed coexistence line and ideal-gas pressure to the exact thermodynamic quantities from the canonical partition sum.

## Key numbers

- Critical exponents from RG scaling at critical density ρ_c = 1/2: τ = 2.2, σ = 0.61, close to the liquid-gas universality class values τ = 2.2, σ = 0.64.
- Critical temperature from RG scaling: T_c = 1.16c, in good agreement with the thermodynamic critical point T_c^th = 1.22c and the infinite-system limit T_c^∞ = 1.12c.
- Critical temperature from Fisher scaling comes out about 20% higher than the RG value.
- The coexistence line reconstructed from Fisher analysis yields an exponent β = 0.84, strongly deviating from the expected β = 0.31 (liquid-gas universality class β = 0.33).
- Scaling analysis performed over temperatures 0.36 < T/c < 3.6 and cluster sizes 4 < A < 30 on an 8×8×8 lattice.

## Context

This work extends the Fisher scaling analysis previously applied to nuclear multifragmentation data by Moretto (2002) and tests it on the Lattice Gas Model, which is isomorphous to the Ising model at fixed magnetization (Lee, 1993). The results contradict the interpretation that Fisher scaling uniquely identifies the thermodynamic critical point; instead, the observed pseudo-critical line spans both subcritical and supercritical densities, consistent with finite-size effects previously seen in RG analyses by Campi (2001) and by the authors themselves (2001). The paper enables a critical reassessment of thermodynamic quantities (coexistence curve, critical temperature, saturated pressure) extracted from fragment yields in nuclear experiments, showing that the independent-cluster hypothesis must be validated before such extraction is reliable.
