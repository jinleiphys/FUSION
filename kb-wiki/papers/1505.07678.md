# Microscopic evaluation of the hypernuclear chart with $Λ$-hyperons

> A large number of hypernuclei, where a considerable fraction of nucleons is replaced by strange baryons, and even pure hyperonic species are expected to be bound. Though, the hypernuclear landscape remains largely unknown because of scarce constraints on the $NY$ and $YY$ interactions. We want to estimate the number of potentially bound hypernuclei. In order to evaluate realistic error bars within the theoretical uncertainties associated to the spherical mean-field approach, and the present information from already synthetized hypernuclei on the $N-Y$ and $Y-Y$ channels, we limit ourselves to purely $Λ$-hypernuclei, to magic numbers of $Λ$'s (for Z $\leq$ 120 and $Λ\leq$70), and to even-even-even systems. We consider a density functional approach adjusted to microscopic Bruckner-Hartree-Fock calculations, where the $ΛΛ$ term is corrected in a phenomenological way, to reproduce present experimental constraints. The number of bound even-even-even $Λ$-hypernuclei is estimated to 491680 $\pm$ 34400. This relatively low uncertainty is due to the fact that the well constrained low density and highly unconstrained high density behavior of the energy functional turn out to be largely decoupled. Results in $Λ$-hypernuclei appear to be almost independent of the choice for the high-density part of the $ΛΛ$ interaction. The location of the $Λ$-hyperdriplines is also evaluated. Significant deviations from Iron-Nickel elements can be found for $Λ$-hypernuclei with the largest binding energy per baryon. Proton, neutron and $Λ$-hyperon magicity evolution and triple magic $Λ$-hypernuclei are studied. Possible bubbles and haloes effect in $Λ$-hypernuclei are also discussed.

## Key claim
The number of bound even-even-even Λ-hypernuclei is estimated to be 491680 ± 34400, with the relatively low uncertainty arising because the well-constrained low-density and highly unconstrained high-density behaviors of the energy functional are largely decoupled, making results almost independent of the choice for the high-density part of the ΛΛ interaction.

## Method
A density functional approach was used, adjusted to microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations, with the ΛΛ term corrected phenomenologically to reproduce experimental constraints from double-Λ hypernuclei. The study was limited to purely Λ-hypernuclei with magic numbers of Λ's (for Z ≤ 120 and Λ ≤ 70) and even-even-even systems, using three different functionals (DF-NSC89, DF-NSC97a, DF-NSC97f) based on Nijmegen potentials, with the ΛΛ interaction modified by introducing a parameter x controlling the density where the Λ potential becomes repulsive.

## Key numbers
- Number of bound even-even-even Λ-hypernuclei: 491680 ± 34400
- The Λ potential at saturation density is about -30 MeV, satisfied by all three functionals (DF-NSC89, DF-NSC97a, DF-NSC97f)
- For DF-NSC89, the ΛΛ term becomes repulsive at ρΛ > 0.27 fm⁻³; for DF-NSC97a at ρΛ > 0.09 fm⁻³; for DF-NSC97f at ρΛ > 0.15 fm⁻³
- The threshold Λ fraction for Ξ appearance is estimated to be ρΛ/ρN = 0.17 for DF-NSC89, 0.15 for DF-NSC97a, and 0.08 fm⁻³ for DF-NSC97f
- The bond energy ΔBΛΛ for ⁶He and related hypernuclei is approximately 1 MeV (from Aoki09, Ahn13)
- The Ξ potential in symmetric nuclear matter VΞ = -14 MeV (from Khaustov2000) and -15 MeV for DF-NSC89

## Context
This work extends microscopic studies of the nuclear landscape (Erler 2012) to the hypernuclear chart, using density functional theory with BHF-based functionals to evaluate uncertainties from scarce experimental data on hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions. The results provide a first microscopic evaluation of the Λ-hypernuclear landscape, enabling future studies of triple magic Λ-hypernuclei, bubble and halo effects, and will benefit from more accurate Λ-based functionals as well as measurements of Λ and multi-Λ hypernuclei and additional constraints on ΞΛ and ΞΞ interactions.
