# Triply Heavy $Ω$ Baryons with JETHAD: A High-Energy Viewpoint

> We investigate the leading-power fragmentation of triply heavy $Ω$ baryons in high-energy hadronic collisions. Extending our previous work on the $Ω_{3c}$ sector, we release the full OMG3Q1.0 family of collinear fragmentation functions by completing the description of the charm channel and delivering the novel $Ω_{3b}$ functions. These hadron-structure-oriented functions are constructed from improved proxy-model calculations for heavy-quark and gluon fragmentation, matched to a flavor-aware DGLAP evolution based on the HF-NRevo scheme. For phenomenological applications, we employ the (sym)JETHAD multimodular interface to compute and analyze NLL/NLO$^+$ semi-inclusive $Ω_{3Q}$ plus jet distributions at the HL-LHC and FCC. This work consolidates the link between hadron structure, rare baryon production, and resummed QCD at the energy frontier.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the OMG3Q1.0 family of collinear fragmentation functions for triply heavy Omega baryons, constructed from diquark-inspired proxy models and evolved via the HF-NRevo scheme, enables NLL/NLO+ predictions for semi-inclusive Omega plus jet distributions at HL-LHC and FCC energies, but only within the leading-power fragmentation approximation and the specific hybrid-factorization framework employed.

## Method

The author constructed collinear fragmentation functions for Omega_{3c} and Omega_{3b} baryons using a scalar-diquark proxy model for heavy-quark and gluon fragmentation channels at the initial perturbative scale, then evolved these inputs to higher energy scales using DGLAP evolution equations in a variable-flavor number scheme implemented within the HF-NRevo scheme. The evolved functions were interfaced with the (sym)JETHAD multimodular interface to compute NLL/NLO+ semi-inclusive distributions within a hybrid-factorization framework that merges high-energy logarithmic resummation with fixed-order collinear elements.

## Key numbers

* The Omega_{3c} baryon is predicted to have a mass around 4.8 GeV (from prior work by Shah (2017) cited in the text).
* Fragmentation probabilities for triply heavy baryons at current collider energies are estimated in the range 10^{-5} to 10^{-7}, with total cross sections at the nanobarn level (from prior work by Chen (2011) and Gomshi Nobary (2005) cited in the text).
* The LHC has collected datasets at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV and 13.6 TeV, with the HL-LHC and FCC (projected 100 TeV energy) as target facilities for future searches.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous release of OMG3Q1.0 fragmentation functions for the Omega_{3c} sector (Celiberto (2025)) by completing the charm channel and introducing the novel Omega_{3b} functions. It builds upon prior diquark-model calculations for triply heavy baryon fragmentation by Moosavi Nejad (2017) and Delpasand (2019), and complements recent efforts on fragmentation functions for fully heavy tetraquarks (Celiberto (2024, 2025)) and pentaquarks (Celiberto (2025)). The results enable phenomenological studies of rare baryon production at HL-LHC and FCC, and can be interfaced with resummation-based tools and Monte Carlo generators for collider simulations.
