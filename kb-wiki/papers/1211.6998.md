# Coupled-channel dynamics in the reactions piN --> piN, etaN, KLambda, KSigma

> Elastic piN scattering and the world data of the family of reactions pi^- p --> eta n, K^0 Lambda$, K^0 Sigma^0, K^+ Sigma^-, and pi^+ p --> K^+ Sigma^+ are described simultaneously in an analytic, unitary, coupled-channel approach. SU(3) flavor symmetry is used to relate both the t- and the u- channel exchanges that drive the meson-baryon interaction in the different channels. Angular distributions, polarizations, and spin-rotation parameters are compared with available experimental data. Partial-wave amplitudes are determined and the resonance content is extracted from the analytic continuation, including resonance positions and branching ratios, and possible sources of uncertainties are discussed. The results provide the final-state interactions for the ongoing analysis of photo- and electroproduction data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simultaneous, coupled-channel description of elastic πN scattering and the world data for the reactions π⁻p → ηn, K⁰Λ, K⁰Σ⁰, K⁺Σ⁻, and π⁺p → K⁺Σ⁺, using SU(3) flavor symmetry to relate t- and u-channel exchanges, but the extracted resonance properties depend on the fit scenario (A or B) which differ predominantly in the strength of the coupling of the πN system to the effective ππN channels, notably to σN.

## Method

The scattering amplitude is obtained as the solution of a Lippmann-Schwinger-type scattering equation defined by time-ordered perturbation theory, which automatically implements unitarity constraints. The non-resonant interactions are given by t- and u-channel exchanges of known mesons and baryons, while s-channel processes are treated as bare resonances; the approach includes three effective ππN channels (πΔ, σN, ρN) whose properties are matched to the corresponding phase shifts. The interaction potentials are derived from effective Lagrangians with SU(3) flavor symmetry relating coupling constants, and the amplitude is decomposed into a non-pole part (from t- and u-channel exchanges) and a pole part (from s-channel resonances) to speed up numerical evaluation.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon pole is fixed at the physical nucleon mass m_N = 939 MeV with the physical coupling f_{πNN} = 0.989.
- The considered energy range extends beyond 2 GeV and resonances up to J = 9/2 are included.
- For the KΣ final states, all available data lie considerably above the KΣ thresholds, so effects from differences in those thresholds are negligible.
- The renormalization procedure ensures a nucleon pole at z₀ = 939 MeV in the P₁₁ partial wave.

## Context

This work extends the Jülich model (Schutz 1994, Krehl 1999, Döring 2009, 2010) from the isospin I=3/2 sector (previously analyzed with π⁺p → K⁺Σ⁺ data) to the isospin I=1/2 sector, including ηN, KΛ, and KΣ final states. The results provide the final-state interactions for ongoing analyses of photo- and electroproduction data (Huang 2011), and the approach can be adapted to finite volume for comparison with lattice QCD calculations (Döring 2011).
