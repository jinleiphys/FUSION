# Signatures of the correlation hole in total and partial cross sections

> In a complex scattering system with few open channels, say a quantum dot with leads, the correlation properties of the poles of the scattering matrix are most directly related to the internal dynamics of the system. We may ask how to extract these properties from an analysis of cross sections. In general this is very difficult, if we leave the domain of isolated resonances. We propose to consider the cross correlation function of two different elastic or total cross sections. For these we can show numerically and to some extent also analytically a significant dependence on the correlations between the scattering poles. The difference between uncorrelated and strongly correlated poles is clearly visible, even for strongly overlapping resonances.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the cross-correlation function between two different elastic or total cross sections can reveal the correlation properties of scattering-matrix poles, and thus the chaoticity or integrability of the internal dynamics, even when resonances are strongly overlapping, a regime where conventional spectral analysis fails.

## Method

The authors construct scattering ensembles using random matrix theory: the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) for chaotic internal dynamics and the Poisson Orthogonal Ensemble (POE) for integrable dynamics, both with orthogonal invariance and the same semicircle level density. They compute Fourier transforms of auto- and cross-correlation functions of total and partial cross sections, using a "rescaled Breit-Wigner" approximation that replaces coupling constants with transmission coefficients to extend validity into the strong-absorption regime. Numerical simulations with N=300 internal states and M channels test the approximation against exact results from the Verbaarschot-Weidenmüller-Zirnbauer (VWZ) integral for the GOE case.

## Key numbers

- The correlation hole size for the autocorrelation function of a total cross section (a=b) is 1/3 of the maximal POE value.
- The correlation hole size for the cross-correlation function between two different total cross sections (a≠b) is 1 (i.e., the full POE value).
- The rescaled Breit-Wigner approximation yields elastic enhancements of 2 (GOE case) and 3 (POE case) for partial fluctuating cross sections.
- The correlation length for Ericson fluctuations is given by Γ_C = d Σ_a T_a / (2π), where d is the mean level spacing and T_a are transmission coefficients.

## Context

This work extends the use of Fourier transform methods and the "correlation hole" concept, previously successful for level spectra and intensity spectra (Leviandier 1986, Guhr 1990, Lombardi 1993, Alhassid 1997), to scattering cross sections in the overlapping-resonance regime. It contrasts with the exact VWZ integral (Verbaarschot, Weidenmüller, Zirnbauer 1985), which is only available for GOE two-point S-matrix functions, and with the pure Breit-Wigner approximation (Breit and Wigner 1936) which fails for stronger coupling. The rescaled approximation enables analytical treatment of POE and partial-cross-section correlations where no exact theory exists, and the cross-correlation signatures could be tested in quantum-dot experiments with leads (Chan 1995).
