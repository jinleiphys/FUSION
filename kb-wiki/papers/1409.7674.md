# Gradient Correction to Photon Emission Rate at Strong Coupling

> We compute the correction to the photon emission rate in first order of shear components of fluid velocity gradients, $σ_{ij}$, in near-equilibrium hydrodynamic plasma at strong coupling regime, using the real-time Schwinger-Keldysh formalism in AdS/CFT correspondence. Our result is an integral of an analytic expression. We observe that the gradient correction to the photon emission rate at strong coupling is about 0.3 - 0.4 times of the equilibrium rate in units of $σ_{ij}/T$.

## Key claim

The gradient correction to the photon emission rate at strong coupling is about 0.3 to 0.4 times the equilibrium rate in units of the shear component of the fluid velocity gradient divided by temperature, but this result is obtained only for the specific holographic model of N=4 Super Yang-Mills theory, not for QCD directly.

## Method

The authors used the real-time Schwinger-Keldysh formalism within the AdS/CFT correspondence to compute the first-order correction to the photon emission rate in a strongly coupled hydrodynamic plasma. They considered a 5-dimensional holographic action describing U(1) R-symmetry dynamics of N=4 Super Yang-Mills theory, and computed the correction to the Wightman function of the U(1) current at linear order in the shear component of the fluid velocity gradient, assuming the gradient is space-time homogeneous. The result is expressed as an integral of an analytic expression involving hypergeometric functions that must be evaluated numerically.

## Key numbers

- The gradient correction to the photon emission rate at strong coupling is about 0.3 - 0.4 times of the equilibrium rate in units of sigma_ij / T.
- The result involves the Bose-Einstein distribution n_B(omega) = 1/(e^(omega/T) - 1).
- The 5-dimensional Newton constant is G_5 = pi/(2 N_c^2), where N_c is the number of colors in SU(N_c) gauge group.
- The analytic expression for Gamma^(1)(omega) includes the factor N_c^2 T^2 / (16 pi) multiplied by an integral over the holographic coordinate u from 0 to 1.

## Context

This work extends the computation of photon emission rates from static equilibrium plasma in AdS/CFT, previously done by Caron-Huot (2006), to the case of near-equilibrium hydrodynamic plasma with non-vanishing shear velocity gradients. It complements the weak-coupling kinetic theory calculation of the same gradient correction by Dusling (2009), which has been implemented in numerical simulations of heavy-ion collisions by Chaudhuri (2011), Mitra (2011), Dion (2011), and Shen (2013). The result enables comparison between strong-coupling and weak-coupling predictions for photon emission in hydrodynamic descriptions of quark-gluon plasma.
