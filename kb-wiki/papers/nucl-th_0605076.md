# The role of $q\bar q$ components in the N(1440) resonance

> The role of 5-quark components in the pion and electromagnetic decays and transition form factors of the N(1440) is explored. The $qqqq\bar q$ components, where the 4-quark subsystem has the flavor-spin symmetries $[4]_{FS}[22]_F[22]_S$ and $[4]_{FS}[31]_F[31]_S$, which are expected to have the lowest energy of all $qqqq\bar q$ configurations, are considered in detail with a nonrelativistic quark model. The matrix elements between the 5-quark components of the N(1440) and the nucleon, $qqqq\bar q\to qqqq\bar q$, play a minor role in these decays, while the transition matrix elements $qqqq\bar q\to qqq$ and $qqq\to qqqq\bar q$ that involve quark antiquark annihilation are very significant. Both for the electromagnetic and strong decay the change from the valence quark model value is dominated by the confinement triggered $q\bar q$ annihilation transitions. In the case of pion decay the calculated decay width is enhanced substantially both by the direct $q\bar q \to π$ and also by the confinement triggered $q\bar q\to π$ transitions. Agreement with the empirical value for the pion decay width may be reached with a $\sim$ 30% $qqqq\bar q$ component in the N(1440).

## Key claim

The N(1440) resonance likely contains a significant (~30%) 5-quark (qqqq\bar q) component, and agreement with the empirical pion decay width can be reached only when both direct and confinement-triggered q\bar q annihilation transitions between the 5-quark and 3-quark components are included, not merely from the diagonal 5-quark to 5-quark transitions.

## Method

The authors employed a nonrelativistic quark model with harmonic oscillator wave functions to calculate the helicity amplitude for N(1440)+ → pγ decay and the width for N(1440)+ → Nπ decay. They constructed explicit wave functions for the qqqq\bar q components with the lowest-energy flavor-spin symmetries [4]FS[22]F[22]S and [4]FS[31]F[31]S, and computed matrix elements for three types of transitions: diagonal qqq → qqq and qqqq\bar q → qqqq\bar q, and off-diagonal qqqq\bar q → qqq and qqq → qqqq\bar q involving quark-antiquark annihilation, including those triggered by the confining interaction.

## Key numbers

- The helicity amplitude for N(1440)+ → pγ in the pure 3-quark model at Q²=0 is A_{1/2}^{(3q)} = -0.037/√GeV, compared to the experimental value of -0.065 ± 0.004/√GeV (a factor of 1.8 smaller).
- With a 10% 5-quark component in the proton and 30% in the N(1440), and assuming phase +1 for both 5-quark components, the combined helicity amplitude at Q²=0 becomes A_{1/2} = -0.022/√GeV, smaller than the 3-quark model value by a factor of 0.6.
- With the same 10% and 30% proportions but assuming phase -1 for the 5-quark components (A_{p5} = -0.32, A_{r5} = -0.55) and b=0, the calculated helicity amplitude at Q²=0 is A_{1/2} = -0.056/√GeV, an enhancement of the decay width by a factor of 2.3 over the 3-quark model.
- With b=200 MeV, the helicity amplitude at Q²=0 becomes A_{1/2} = -0.049/√GeV.
- The diagonal 5-quark contribution to the helicity amplitude at Q²=0 is A_{1/2}^{(5q)} = -0.0047/√GeV (for ω₅ = 200 MeV).
- The direct qqqq\bar q → qqqγ annihilation contribution at Q²=0 is A_{1/2}^{(53)} = -0.035/√GeV.
- The direct qqq → qqqq\bar qγ annihilation contribution at Q²=0 is A_{1/2}^{(35)} = +0.0390/√GeV.
- The confinement-triggered qqqq\bar q → qqqγ contribution at Q²=0 (b=0) is A_{1/2}^{(53c)} = +0.111/√GeV.
- The confinement-triggered qqq → qqqq\bar qγ contribution at Q²=0 (b=0) is A_{1/2}^{(35c)} = -0.092/√GeV.
- The real-photon three-momentum in the N(1440) center-of-mass frame is K_γ = 414 MeV.
- The constituent quark mass is taken as m = 340 MeV.
- The oscillator frequency for the 3-quark component is ω₃ = 110 MeV (chosen to match the empirical shape of the helicity amplitude).
- The string tension used is c = 280 MeV/fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of 5-quark components in baryons, previously applied to the Δ(1232) decay by the same authors (Li and Riska, 2005), to the N(1440) resonance. The conventional 3-quark model fails to reproduce the N(1440) decay widths by an order of magnitude, as noted by earlier calculations (Bruno, 2001; Melde, 2005). The Skyrme model (Biedenharn, 1985) had suggested the N(1440) might be a collective vibrational state, consistent with the need for additional components. The results enable future calculations of transition form factors for the N(1440) and suggest that the empirical Q²-dependence of the helicity amplitude requires a spatially extended wave function model.
