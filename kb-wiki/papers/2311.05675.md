# First Detailed Calculation of Atmospheric Neutrino Foregrounds to the Diffuse Supernova Neutrino Background in Super-Kamiokande

> The Diffuse Supernova Neutrino Background (DSNB) -- a probe of the core-collapse mechanism and the cosmic star-formation history -- has not been detected, but its discovery may be imminent. A significant obstacle for DSNB detection in Super-Kamiokande (Super-K) is detector backgrounds, especially due to atmospheric neutrinos (more precisely, these are foregrounds), which are not sufficiently understood. We perform the first detailed theoretical calculations of these foregrounds in the range 16--90 MeV in detected electron energy, taking into account several physical and detector effects, quantifying uncertainties, and comparing our predictions to the 15.9 livetime years of pre-gadolinium data from Super-K stages I--IV. We show that our modeling reasonably reproduces this low-energy data as well as the usual high-energy atmospheric-neutrino data. To accelerate progress on detecting the DSNB, we outline key actions to be taken in future theoretical and experimental work. In a forthcoming paper, we use our modeling to detail how low-energy atmospheric-neutrino events register in Super-K and suggest new cuts to reduce their impact.

## Key claim
The paper provides the first detailed theoretical calculation of atmospheric-neutrino foregrounds to the Diffuse Supernova Neutrino Background in Super-Kamiokande, showing that their modeling reasonably reproduces both low-energy (16--90 MeV) and high-energy atmospheric-neutrino data, but the authors hedge that the GENIE neutrino-nucleus cross section models are "surely not perfect" and that the predictions depend on uncertain inputs including flux uncertainties of 15--25% and cross section uncertainties of about 20%.

## Method
The authors simulated atmospheric-neutrino interactions in water using GENIE (v3.02.02) with two model sets (LFG-NAV and RFG-LS), incorporating atmospheric-neutrino fluxes from Honda (2015) and Battistoni (2005), full three-flavor neutrino mixing via nuCraft, and detector effects including energy resolution and detection efficiency for Super-K stages I--IV. They calculated the angle-averaged momentum spectra of charged leptons from neutrino-oxygen charged-current interactions and compared these predictions to 15.9 livetime years of pre-gadolinium Super-K data in the 16--90 MeV electron energy range, as well as to high-energy Super-K atmospheric-neutrino data from stage I.

## Key numbers
- The atmospheric-neutrino background rate in Super-K is approximately 50 events per year in the 16--90 MeV energy range, compared to a DSNB signal rate of approximately 2--4 events per year
- The analysis covers 15.9 livetime years of Super-K data from stages I--IV, with a fiducial volume of 22.5 kton
- The energy resolution at relevant energies is less than 12% (less than 18% for Super-K stage II), with detection efficiency of approximately 90% (except for stage IV at some energies)
- The Cherenkov threshold for electrons is 0.26 MeV kinetic energy, for muons 54 MeV, for charged pions 72 MeV, and for protons 481 MeV
- The neutrino flux uncertainties adopted are approximately 25% for 10--100 MeV, approximately 20% for 0.1--1 GeV, and approximately 15% for 1--10 GeV
- The neutrino-nucleus cross section uncertainties are assumed to be approximately 20% in the relevant energy range

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical efforts by Beacom (2003) and Bell (2020) and goes beyond the empirical treatment used by Super-K in Malek (2002), Super-Kamiokande (2011), and Super-Kamiokande (2013), where background normalizations were simply fit to data. The results will enable improved background rejection for DSNB searches in SK-Gd (which began gadolinium loading in 2020) and Hyper-Kamiokande, and the authors plan a forthcoming paper detailing how low-energy atmospheric events register in Super-K to suggest new data cuts.
