# Extended QCD

> I consider a reformulation of QCD which incorporates additional bosonic fields, and analyze it in the large-N limit. This construction makes a more direct connection with the nonrelativistic quark and the chiral quark models, as well as the bag model for baryons. This extension of QCD is neither a model nor an effective low energy theory, but is exactly equivalent to QCD.

## Key claim

The paper introduces Extended QCD (XQCD), a reformulation of QCD that includes additional bosonic fields (a scalar field \(\Phi\) and vector/axial-vector fields \(\bfv_\mu, \bfa_\mu\)) such that the theory is exactly equivalent to QCD at all energies, not a model or low-energy effective theory. The construction makes the quark model picture of weakly interacting massive quarks and the Goldstone boson nature of pions simultaneously evident, particularly in the large-\(N_c\) limit, but the equivalence holds only after the auxiliary fields are integrated out via a Gaussian path integral identity.

## Method

The author inserts into the QCD path integral a Gaussian integral over a scalar field \(\Phi\) (transforming as a bifundamental under chiral symmetry) and over colored vector and axial-vector fields \(\bfv_\mu, \bfa_\mu\) (hermitian \(N_c\times N_c\) matrices). A Fierz identity is used to rewrite a four-quark contact interaction as exchanges of these auxiliary fields, yielding an action where quarks couple to \(\Phi\), \(\bfv_\mu\), and \(\bfa_\mu\) in addition to gluons. The theory is then analyzed in the large-\(N_c\) limit using double-line diagrams, showing that \(\Phi\) contributes only through tree diagrams and satisfies a classical gap equation, while the auxiliary fields provide repulsive interactions that partially cancel gluon attraction.

## Key numbers

- The constituent quark mass \(M\) is given by \(M = 2\Sigma/\lambda^2\), where \(\Sigma = -\langle \bar\psi\psi \rangle_{\rm QCD}\) is the quark condensate for a single flavor (normalized independent of \(N_c\)).
- The scale \(\lambda\) is chosen such that \(M \gtrsim m_\rho/2\) (i.e., a little more than half the \(\rho\) meson mass), making the \(\rho\) meson correlator near its pole well approximated by free quarks with mass \(M\).
- In the large-\(N_c\) limit, every connected planar diagram involving colored boson fields with a fermion loop boundary is order \(N_c\); diagrams with a \(\Phi\) propagator inserted within a planar diagram are suppressed by \(1/N_c\).
- Baryon correlators receive combinatoric enhancements: a \(\Phi\) tadpole attached to a single valence quark line is \(O(1)\) but gets a factor of \(N_c\) from combinatorics; a three-point \(\Phi\) diagram is \(O(1/N_c^2)\) but gets a factor of \(N_c^3\).

## Context

This work extends the line of thought connecting QCD to the quark model, following the chiral quark model of Manohar and Georgi (1984) which introduced quarks, gluons, and pions at low energy but assumed weak gluon coupling below the chiral symmetry breaking scale. It also relates to the large-\(N_c\) analysis of Weinberg (2010) that extended the energy range of the chiral quark model. The XQCD construction makes contact with the bag model of Chodos et al. (1974) for baryons and with Skyrme-type solutions for nuclear matter (Klebanov, 1985). The framework enables future lattice studies of QCD at nonzero baryon density, though the complex determinant of the XQCD operator presents an obstacle for Monte Carlo simulation.
