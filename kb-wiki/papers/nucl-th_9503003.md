# HOT GLUON PROPAGATOR

> A general expression of the hard thermal loop gluon propagator is given for a variety of abelian gauge fixing conditions (Coulomb gauge, axial gauge, covariant gauge, temporal gauge ...) The current --- current interaction energy, in particular the Coulomb energy, is shown to be independent of the gauge fixing condition (not only of the parameter).

## Key claim

The paper derives a general form of the hard thermal loop (HTL) gluon propagator that works for any abelian gauge fixing condition linear in the derivative, and shows that the Coulomb energy (the interaction energy between static color charges) is independent of the chosen gauge fixing condition, not just of the gauge parameter.

## Method

The author constructs the inverse propagator from the second variation of the Yang-Mills action, adding a general gauge-fixing term of the form \(F_\mu A^\mu = 0\) where \(F_\mu\) is a linear combination of timelike and longitudinal contributions. The HTL polarization tensor is decomposed into longitudinal and transverse components, and the full resummed propagator is obtained by inverting the equation of motion operator including the self-energy insertion. The general expression is then specialized to recover known results for covariant, Coulomb, axial, and temporal gauges.

## Key numbers

- The HTL expansion parameter is \(gT\), where \(T\) is the temperature and \(g\) the coupling constant, with infrared divergences appearing on the supersoft momentum scale \(\mathcal{O}(g^2 T)\).
- The gluon momentum is parameterized as \(k_\mu = \omega n_\mu + k e_\mu\), with \(\omega\) the plasmon energy and \(k\) the absolute value of the three-momentum.
- The longitudinal dielectric function is \(\epsilon^L = 1 - \frac{1}{(n\cdot k)^2}\Pi^L - \frac{1}{k\cdot k}\Pi^{\text{vac}}\).
- The transverse dielectric function is \(\epsilon^T = 1 + \frac{1}{k^2}\Pi^T\) (in Coulomb gauge) or appears in the denominator \(\omega^2 - k^2\epsilon^T\).
- The Coulomb energy expression is \(\int \frac{d^4k}{(2\pi)^4} \frac{\rho(k)\rho(-k)}{k^2\epsilon^L} + \frac{j^T_1(k)j^T_1(-k) + j^T_2(k)j^T_2(-k)}{\omega^2 - k^2\epsilon^T}\).

## Context

This work extends the hard thermal loop resummation method developed by Braaten and Pisarski (1990) to a unified treatment of gauge fixing conditions, generalizing earlier explicit calculations that were limited to Coulomb gauge and axial gauge. The result confirms that the static (zero-momentum) behavior of the gluon propagator is gauge invariant, which was previously shown only for specific gauges. The paper enables systematic calculations of gauge-invariant observables in the quark-gluon plasma, particularly the screening of color charges, and resolves the nontrivial issue of gauge invariance in temporal gauge (\(A_0=0\)).
