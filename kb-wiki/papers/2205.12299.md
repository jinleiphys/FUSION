# Electromagnetic measurement of the temperature of quark-gluon plasma produced in central ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

> Ultrarelativistic collisions of large nuclei produce a short-lived plasma of deconfined quarks and gluons. Of all the GeV-energy photons detected in these nuclear collisions, only a small number are emitted directly by the quark-gluon plasma. We use the characteristic near-exponential energy spectrum of these photons to estimate the maximum temperature of the plasma, assuming it undergoes hydrodynamic expansion. We quantify non-exponential corrections to the photon spectrum resulting from the characteristic equation of state and photon emission rate of nuclear matter.

## Key claim
The paper derives an analytic expression relating the photon energy spectrum to the maximum temperature of quark-gluon plasma, but the authors explicitly hedge that this relation is valid only for high-energy photons (E ≳ 2–3 GeV) and assumes ideal hydrodynamics, a Gaussian initial temperature profile, and neglects transverse flow effects.

## Method
The authors start from the thermal photon production integral in a boost-invariant Bjorken expansion, approximate the initial temperature profile as a Gaussian with width σ₀ ≈ 5–10 fm, and derive an analytic formula for the photon energy spectrum at midrapidity. They use the mean value theorem to incorporate a temperature-dependent speed of sound, and validate their expression by comparing with full numerical hydrodynamic simulations from Gale (2021). They then apply the formula to experimental data from PHENIX (Au-Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV) and ALICE (Pb-Pb at √s_NN = 2760 GeV), subtracting prompt photon contributions using calculations from Gale (2021), and perform Bayesian inference to constrain the maximum temperature T₀,max and the invariant τ₀^(1/3) T₀,max.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature profile width σ₀ ≈ 5–10 fm for heavy ions such as gold or lead
- Lower bound on temperature for the photon integration T_f ≈ 120–140 MeV, based on Schafer (2021)
- Speed of sound varies from ≈ 1/7 at T = 170 MeV to 1/3 in the high-temperature limit, from Borsanyi (2013) and HotQCD (2014)
- Validation uses τ₀ = 0.4 fm and T₀,max ≈ 0.53 GeV from Gale (2021) numerical calculations for Au-Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV, 0–20% centrality
- The invariant τ₀^(1/3) T₀,max = 0.39 fm^(1/3) GeV for the validation case
- Experimental data limited to photon energies higher than 2.5 GeV
- PHENIX and ALICE measurements have 20–35% combined statistical and systematic relative uncertainties before prompt photon subtraction
- A conservative 50% uncertainty is assumed on prompt photon calculations, yielding ≥ 40% total uncertainty on non-prompt non-decay photons
- The analysis constrains T₀,max to be larger than 400 MeV at RHIC and 500 MeV at the LHC

## Context
This work extends analytic approaches to photon emission from quark-gluon plasma that began with Shuryak (1978) and McLerran (1984), who recognized that the effective temperature T_eff from the exponential slope represents a convoluted average over the plasma's temperature profile. The authors derive explicit non-exponential corrections to the photon spectrum that are independent of transverse Doppler shifts, complementing previous numerical studies by Shen (2013), van Hees (2011), and Paquet (2015, 2016). The results enable direct extraction of the maximum plasma temperature from high-energy photon measurements, and the constraints obtained are consistent with multistage numerical simulations from Gale (2021) that were calibrated using hadronic data.
