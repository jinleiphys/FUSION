# Stiffer EoS for Compact Star with New Scaling Law

> We present in this paper a simple and unequivocal prediction on the deformation of the compact star, that will be measured in gravity waves, with an EoS obtained in renormailzation-group implemented effective field theory anchored on scale and hidden-local symmetric Lagrangian endowed with topology change . The objective is not to offer a superior or improved EoS for compact stars but to confront with a forthcoming astrophysical observable the given model formulated in what is considered to be consistent with the premise of QCD. The model so obtained is found to satisfactorily describe the observation of a 2-solar mass neutron star with a minimum number of parameters. Specifically the observable we are considering in this paper is the tidal deformability parameter $λ$ (equivalently the Love number, $k_2$) in gravity waves. The merit of our prediction is that the prediction can be readily confirmed or falsified by forth-coming aLIGO and aVirgo gravity-wave observations and provide a valuable guidance for arriving at a better understanding of highly compressed baryonic matter.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a specific tidal deformability parameter for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star using an equation of state derived from a renormalization-group-implemented effective field theory that incorporates a topology change (skyrmion to half-skyrmion transition) at high density; the prediction is intended to be confirmed or falsified by forthcoming aLIGO and aVirgo gravitational-wave observations, not as a superior EoS but as a test of the underlying model assumptions.

## Method

The authors construct an equation of state for neutron star matter containing neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons in weak equilibrium and charge neutrality, using the ground-state energy and symmetry energy factor from a previous work (Dong et al.) that implements a new density-scaling law (new-BR/BLPR) based on topology change in dense skyrmion crystals. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius relations, and then solve the linearized Einstein equations for static, even-parity perturbations to compute the tidal deformability parameter λ (equivalently the Love number k₂) as a function of stellar mass. For densities below 0.5 n₀, they adopt the equation of state from Hebeler et al. (2013).

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for npeμ matter: M ∼ 2.1 M⊙ with radius R ∼ 11 km, at central density n_c ∼ 5.7 n₀
- For pure neutron matter (from Dong et al.): maximum mass M ∼ 2.4 M⊙ with radius R ∼ 12 km at central density n ∼ 4.7 n₀
- Compactness C = M/R in mass range 1.0–2.0 M⊙: 0.12–0.26; for 1.4 M⊙: C = 0.14
- Tidal deformability parameter λ for 1.4 M⊙: 2.86 (in units of 10³⁶ g cm² s²)
- For comparison, λ values for other EoSs at 1.4 M⊙: SLy = 1.70, AP3 = 2.22, MPA1 = 2.79
- Maximum λ value: 4.2 at 0.84 M⊙
- Radius range for 1 M⊙ – 2 M⊙: 11.2 – 12.2 km
- Deformability parameter range over full mass range: 4.0 – 0.68
- Causality limit (sound speed ≤ c) constrains highest density to n_c ∼ 5.7 n₀

## Context

This work extends the pure neutron matter calculation of Dong et al. to a realistic npeμ composition, and applies the resulting stiffer equation of state to predict tidal deformability, a quantity measurable in gravitational-wave signals from binary neutron star mergers. The approach contrasts with standard nuclear physics EoSs such as SLy (Douchin and Haensel, 2001), AP3 (Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall, 1998), and MPA1 (Müther, Prakash, and Ainsworth, 1987), and is compared to hybrid hadron-quark models (Hatsuda, 2014; Baym, 2014) that also produce stiffening at high density. The authors suggest the topology-change mechanism may be related to baryon-quark continuity via the Cheshire cat phenomenon.
