# Exploring Baryon Resonances with Transition Generalized Parton Distributions: Status and Perspectives

> QCD gives rise to a rich spectrum of excited baryon states. Understanding their internal structure is important for many areas of nuclear physics, such as nuclear forces, dense matter, and neutrino-nucleus interactions. Generalized parton distributions (GPDs) are an established tool for characterizing the QCD structure of the ground-state nucleon. They are used to create 3D tomographic images of the quark/gluon structure and quantify the mechanical properties such as the distribution of mass, angular momentum and forces in the system. Transition GPDs extend these concepts to $N \rightarrow N^\ast$ transitions and can be used to characterize the 3D structure and mechanical properties of baryon resonances. They can be probed in high-momentum-transfer exclusive electroproduction processes with resonance transitions $e + N \rightarrow e' + M + N^\ast$, such as deeply-virtual Compton scattering ($M = γ$) or meson production ($M = π, K$, $etc.$), and in related photon/hadron-induced processes. This White Paper describes a research program aiming to explore baryon resonance structure with transition GPDs. This includes the properties and interpretation of the transition GPDs, theoretical methods for structures and processes, first experimental results from JLab 12 GeV, future measurements with existing and planned facilities (JLab detector and energy upgrades, COMPASS/AMBER, EIC, EicC, J-PARC, LHC ultraperihperal collisions), and the theoretical and experimental developments needed to realize this program.

## Key claim

Transition generalized parton distributions (GPDs) can extend the established framework of nucleon GPDs to characterize the three-dimensional quark/gluon structure and mechanical properties of baryon resonances, but realizing this program requires substantial theoretical and experimental developments, including new measurements at JLab 12 GeV, the future Electron-Ion Collider, and hadron facilities.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical formulation of transition GPDs for N → Δ and N → N* transitions, including the parametrization of matrix elements of chiral-even and chiral-odd QCD operators and the QCD energy-momentum tensor. It surveys experimental measurements of exclusive electroproduction processes such as e + p → e' + π⁻ + Δ⁺⁺ at JLab CLAS12 and e + p → e' + π⁺ + Δ⁰ at JLab Hall C, and discusses the application of nonperturbative methods including the 1/N_c expansion of QCD, chiral perturbation theory, holographic QCD models, and lattice QCD to predict transition GPDs.

## Key numbers

- The first experimental results from JLab 12 GeV on deeply virtual N → N* meson production and N → N* Compton scattering have been obtained, with measurements of e + p → e' + π⁻ + Δ⁺⁺ performed at CLAS12 and interpreted in terms of transition GPDs
- The accessible Q² range for factorization of processes requires energy and momentum transfers ≫ 1 GeV, with the asymptotic regime defined as Q², W² ≫ 1 GeV² and |t| ≲ 1 GeV²
- The future EIC will enable GPD measurements at high energies with x_B in the range 10⁻³ ≲ x_B ≲ 0.1
- Ultraperipheral collisions at LHC enable measurements of photon-proton scattering at energies W ∼ 10³ GeV, reaching momentum fractions x ∼ 10⁻⁶ for gluon GPDs

## Context

This white paper extends the established GPD framework for ground-state nucleon structure (Muller 1994, Radyushkin 1997, Ji 1996) to baryon resonance transitions, building on prior work on N → Δ transition GPDs (Goeke 2001, Belitsky 2005) and recent theoretical developments on chiral-odd transition GPDs (Kroll 2022) and QCD angular momentum in transitions (Kim 2023). It enables future experimental programs at JLab with detector and energy upgrades, COMPASS/AMBER, EIC, EicC, J-PARC, and LHC ultraperipheral collisions, and motivates further lattice QCD calculations of transition GPDs combining techniques for excited states (Edwards 2011) with partonic structure computations.
