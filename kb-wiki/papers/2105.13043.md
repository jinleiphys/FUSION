# Correlations between nuclear landscape boundaries and neutron-rich r-process abundances

> Motivated by the newly observed $^{39}$Na in experiments, systematic calculations of global nuclear binding energies with seven Skyrme forces are performed. We demonstrate the strong correlation between the two-neutron separation energies ($S_{2n}$) of $^{39}$Na and the total number of bound nuclei of the whole nuclear landscape. Furthermore, with calculated nuclear masses, we perform astrophysical rapid-neutron capture process ($r$-process) simulations by using nuclear reaction code TALYS and nuclear reaction network code SkyNet. $r$-process abundances from ejecta of neutron star mergers and core-collapse supernova are compared. Prominent covariance correlations between nuclear landscape boundaries and neutron-rich $r$-process abundances before the third peak are shown. This study highlights the needs for further experimental studies of drip-line nuclei around $^{39}$Na for better constraints on nuclear landscape boundaries and $r$-process.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates a strong linear correlation between the two-neutron separation energy ($S_{2n}$) of the newly observed drip-line nucleus $^{39}$Na and the total number of bound nuclei in the nuclear landscape, and shows that this correlation propagates to uncertainties in $r$-process abundances before the third peak, but only in the neutron star merger scenario and not in the core-collapse supernova scenario.

## Method
The authors performed global Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations of nuclear binding energies using seven different Skyrme-type effective forces (SkM*, SLy4, SLy4', UNEDF0, and three extended versions with an additional high-order density-dependent term). Using the calculated masses, they computed neutron capture reaction rates with the TALYS code for targets from Z=10 to Z=112, updated the REACLIB database, and performed $r$-process nucleosynthesis simulations with the SkyNet reaction network code for both neutron star merger and core-collapse supernova ejecta conditions.

## Key numbers
- The total number of bound nuclei from Z=8 to Z=120 ranges from 7105 to 8761 across the seven Skyrme forces.
- The correlation coefficient between $S_{2n}$ of $^{39}$Na and total bound nuclei is $r=0.947$, with linear regression parameters $a=638.5$, $b=7789.7$, and $\sigma=199.9$.
- The SkM*$_{\rm ext1}$ force predicts 7671 bound nuclei, close to the Bayesian mixing inference of 7708$\pm$534 from prior work.
- The symmetry energies at subsaturation density (0.11 fm$^{-3}$) range from 24.31 MeV (SkM*) to 26.90 MeV (SLy4') and correlate with total bound nuclei with $r=-0.989$.
- For the neutron star merger scenario, the initial conditions were temperature 7.1 GK, electron fraction $Y_e=0.03$, density $2.2\times10^{11}$ g cm$^{-3}$, and expansion timescale 1 ms.
- For the core-collapse supernova scenario, the initial conditions were temperature 10 GK, $Y_e=0.2$, density $2.0\times10^{8}$ g cm$^{-3}$, and expansion timescale 20 ms.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of nuclear mass model uncertainties and their impact on $r$-process nucleosynthesis, particularly following the experimental observation of $^{39}$Na at RIKEN by Ahn et al. (2019). The authors build on earlier work by Erler et al. (2012) and Chen et al. (2015) on nuclear landscape predictions, and on Mumpower et al. (2016) regarding the sensitivity of $r$-process abundances to mass variations. The study enables future experimental measurements of $S_{2n}$ of $^{39}$Na to directly constrain both the total number of bound nuclei and $r$-process abundance predictions in neutron-rich environments, while showing that core-collapse supernova $r$-process is insensitive to neutron drip-line uncertainties.
