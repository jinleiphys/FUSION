# Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation of charge radii of Sn, Ba, Yb, and Pb isotopes

> Charge radii of Sn, Ba, Yb, and Pb isotopes are calculated within Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with a Skyrme force and a density-dependent delta-force pairing. We investigate mean field effects of the pairing upon odd-even staggering of isotope shifts. HFB equations are solved in the canonical basis. Odd nuclei are treated in the blocking approximation.

# Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation of charge radii of Sn, Ba, Yb, and Pb isotopes

## Key claim

The odd-even staggering of isotope shifts in charge radii can be reproduced by including both the pair mean-field and the gap potential arising from a density-dependent delta-force pairing within Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory, but only if the density parameter of the pairing force is taken lower than the saturation density (0.140 fm⁻³ rather than 0.160 fm⁻³). The pair mean-field and gap potential are both responsible for the staggering, with the pair mean-field having a larger effect, yet these mean fields have only marginal effects on the kink around the neutron shell closure at N=126 in Pb isotopes.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations in the canonical basis using the Skyrme SLy4 parameterization for the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent delta-force pairing for the particle-particle channel. Odd-neutron nuclei were treated in the blocking approximation, and single-particle states were expanded in an axially-symmetric deformed oscillator basis with eleven major shells. The pairing force parameters (V₀ = -1250 MeV·fm³, ρc = 0.140 fm⁻³) were determined by fitting to one-neutron separation energies and odd-even staggering of charge radii across all isotopic chains.

## Key numbers

- Pairing force parameters: V₀ = -1250 MeV·fm³ and ρc = 0.140 fm⁻³, obtained by fitting to one-neutron separation energies and odd-even staggering of charge radii
- Saturation density comparison: ρc = 0.140 fm⁻³ (used) versus ρc = 0.160 fm⁻³ (saturation density); the lower value gave better agreement with experiment
- Pair mean-fields contribute to HF+BCS results by an order of ten keV; gap potentials contribute by an order of hundred keV
- Convergence criteria: |ΔE| < 10⁻⁴ MeV for energy and |Δr| < 10⁻⁴ fm for charge radius between successive iterations
- Active pairing space cutoff: all bound levels with energies lower than eF + 1ℏω, where 1ℏω = 41A⁻¹/³ MeV
- For Ba isotopes with ρc = 0.160 fm⁻³, a strength of V₀ = -1000 MeV·fm³ was necessary to fit neutron separation energies, but odd-even staggering did not agree well with experiment

## Context

This work extends the mechanism proposed by Zawischa et al. (1988, 1994, 1995, 1996) and by Terasaki and Brink (1993), who demonstrated the importance of density-dependent pairing and its mean field effects on odd-even staggering of isotope shifts. The authors show that ordinary HF+BCS theory with density-dependent pairing cannot reproduce odd-even staggering without including the mean fields of the pairing. The calculation reproduces the kink in Ba isotopes through changes in nuclear deformation, but fails to reproduce the kink in Pb isotopes around ²⁰⁸Pb, which is better described by relativistic mean field theory (Sharma and Lalazissis, 1993) or by nonrelativistic Skyrme calculations with modified spin-orbit interactions (Sharma and Lalazissis, 1995; Reinhard and Flocard, 1995). The results enable systematic HFB studies of isotope shifts across spherical, transitional, and deformed regions.
