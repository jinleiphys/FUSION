# Hard Thermal Photon Production in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The recent status of hard thermal photon production in relativistic heavy ion collisions is reviewed and the current rates are presented with emphasis on corrected bremsstrahlung processes in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and quark-hadron duality. Employing Bjorken hydrodynamics with an EOS supporting the phase transition from QGP to hot hadron gas (HHG), thermal photon spectra are computed. For SPS 158 GeV Pb+Pb collisions, comparison with other theoretical results and the WA98 direct photon data indicates significant contributions due to prompt photons. Extrapolating the presented approach to RHIC and LHC experiments, predictions of the thermal photon spectrum show a QGP outshining the HHG in the high-pT-region.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the status of hard thermal photon production in relativistic heavy ion collisions, presenting corrected 2-loop QGP bremsstrahlung rates that enhance the total QGP spectrum by about a factor of 3 (not the factor of 4 erroneously used in earlier work), but emphasizes that the sum of the 1-loop and 2-loop rates should be considered only an "educated guess" due to the non-perturbative nature of thermal photon production in the QGP.

## Method

The authors compute thermal photon spectra by convoluting QGP rates (1-loop Compton scattering and qqbar-annihilation, plus 2-loop bremsstrahlung and qqbar-annihilation with scattering) and HHG rates (from effective chiral Lagrangians including pi, rho, a1, and omega mesons) with Bjorken 1+1 hydrodynamics using an equation of state with a first-order phase transition from a two-flavor massless parton gas to a massless pion gas. For SPS 158 GeV Pb+Pb collisions, the theoretical spectra are compared with WA98 direct photon data and with results from other models (Gallmeister et al. 2000 and Srivastava et al. 2000) to extract upper limits on the initial temperature and assess the need for prompt photon contributions.

## Key numbers

- The 1-loop QGP rate: E dN/d^4x d^3p = 0.0281 alpha alpha_s ln(0.23 E / (alpha_s T)) T^2 exp(-E/T) for N_f=2
- The bremsstrahlung QGP rate: E dN/d^4x d^3p = 0.0219 alpha alpha_s T^2 exp(-E/T) for N_f=2
- The qqbar-annihilation with scattering QGP rate: E dN/d^4x d^3p = 0.0105 alpha alpha_s E T exp(-E/T) for N_f=2
- For SPS 158 GeV Pb+Pb collisions with tau_0=1 fm, T_c=170 MeV, T_f=150 MeV, g_h=3: even T_0=T_c=170 MeV gives a spectrum exceeding experimental limits at low p_T
- With g_h=8 and T_c=170 MeV: T_0_max=220 MeV; with g_h=3 and T_c=160 MeV: T_0_max=175 MeV; with g_h=8 and T_c=160 MeV: T_0_max=235 MeV
- For RHIC (tau_0=0.5 fm, T_0=310 MeV): QGP outshines HHG for p_T > 3.2 GeV (g_h=3) or p_T > 2.2 GeV (g_h=8)
- For LHC (tau_0=0.5 fm, T_0=450 MeV): QGP outshines HHG for p_T > 2.5 GeV (g_h=3) or p_T > 1.7 GeV (g_h=8)
- At SPS, HHG dominates the spectrum for both g_h=3 and g_h=8 at all p_T values

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of Kapusta (1991), Baier (1991), and Aurenche (1998) on thermal photon production in the QGP, correcting a factor-of-4 error in the 2-loop bremsstrahlung rates that had been used by Srivastava (1999) and Steffen (1999). The paper also builds on the HHG rate calculations of Song (1993, 1998) and Kapusta (1991) for the omega decay. The comparison with WA98 data (2000) and the work of Gallmeister (2000) and Srivastava (2000) shows that a significant prompt photon contribution is needed to explain the high-p_T data, and that the simple Bjorken model is as competent as 2+1 hydrodynamical models at SPS energies. The predictions for RHIC and LHC provide p_T thresholds where QGP photons may dominate, enabling future experimental searches for a QGP signature.
