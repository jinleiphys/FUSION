# Initial State Energy Loss Dependence of J/Psi and Drell-Yan in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We present a Glauber-based study of J/Psi and Drell-Yan yields in nucleus-nucleus collisions. Using this approach, we have investigated the impact of energy loss by the colliding nuclei on observed yields and transverse momentum spectra of J/Psi and Drell-Yan. These studies permit an assessment of the importance of initial state energy loss in relation to "anomalous" J/Psi suppression.

## Key claim

Initial state energy loss cannot simultaneously explain the observed J/Psi yields, Drell-Yan yields, and J/Psi transverse momentum data in Pb-Pb collisions at SPS energies, contrary to earlier claims based only on minimum bias J/Psi yields.

## Method

The authors constructed a Glauber model of nuclear collisions with Woods-Saxon nucleon distributions and a 30 mb nucleon-nucleon cross section, where nucleons lose a constant fraction of their momentum per interaction (5%, 10%, or 15% per collision). The resulting center-of-mass energy for each N-N interaction was used to calculate J/Psi production probabilities via the Schuler parameterization and Drell-Yan production via tau scaling, with produced J/Psi subject to a 7.1 mb breakup cross section. The Cronin effect was implemented by assigning transverse momentum distributions based on the number of prior collisions, using a measured Delta p_t^2 of 0.125 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

- J/Psi breakup cross section used: 7.1 mb, approximately 15% higher than the NA50 reported value of 6.2 +/- 0.7 mb
- Momentum loss per N-N collision tested: 5%, 10%, and 15%, compared to the 40% loss realized as time between collisions approaches infinity
- J/Psi <p_t^2>_pp value at 160 AGeV/c: 1.13 GeV^2, scaled from the NA3 measurement of 1.23 +/- 0.05 GeV^2 at 200 AGeV/c
- Delta p_t^2 per prior collision for Cronin effect: 0.125 GeV^2, from a fit combining p-A and A-A data at 200 AGeV/c
- NA50 E_t resolution used in simulations: 94%/sqrt(E_t)

## Context

This work extends the initial state energy loss proposal of Frankel (1998) beyond minimum bias J/Psi yields to include centrality dependence and transverse momentum spectra, directly challenging the claim that energy loss explains the "anomalous" J/Psi suppression observed by NA50 in Pb-Pb collisions at 158 AGeV/c. The study contradicts the NA50 collaboration's assertion that hadronic scenarios produce only smooth monotonic behavior, and it enables future systematic comparisons of all available data before invoking deconfinement. The authors note that precision studies of energy loss effects on Drell-Yan in p-A collisions are also being done by Leitch and Kapusta.
