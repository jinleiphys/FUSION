# On the Nucleon Stability and Sigma_N Term Puzzles

> A stable soliton configuration for a nucleon emerges when the nucleon stability and $Σ_N$ term discrepancy problems are studied semi-quantitatively within a local theory developed recently. The approach developed here goes beyond the mean field or Hartree-Fock approximation by taking into account of the non-perturbative wave function renormalization in a way that is consistent with the chiral Ward-Takahashi identities of QCD. The stability condition for a nucleon and the recently extracted nucleon $Σ_N$ term are used to estimate the radius of the soliton. It is found that 0.67 fm $\le R \le$ 0.78 fm. A new mechanism for the stability of a nucleon is proposed. The discrepancy between the nucleon $Σ_N$ term extracted from pion--nucleon scattering data and the one from baryonic spectra is resolved by assuming the existence of a metastable virtual color superconducting phase for the strong interaction vacuum. Under such a scenario, the difference between the energy density of the chiral symmetry breaking phase and the metastable color superconducting phase is found to be 0.41 GeV/fm$^3$ $\le Δε\le $ 0.85 GeV/fm$^3$.

## Key claim

A stable soliton configuration for a nucleon emerges when the nucleon stability and Sigma_N term discrepancy problems are studied semi-quantitatively within a local theory that goes beyond mean field or Hartree-Fock approximation; the stability condition and the recently extracted nucleon Sigma_N term are used to estimate the soliton radius, and the discrepancy between Sigma_N values from different sources is resolved by assuming a metastable virtual color superconducting phase for the strong interaction vacuum.

## Method

The author constructs a chiral quark model with 4-fermion interactions, incorporating non-perturbative wave function renormalization (Z_psi = 1 - J_n) consistent with chiral Ward-Takahashi identities of QCD. The nucleon is treated as a non-topological soliton (bag) in which the scalar field sigma is distorted, and the stability condition from the local theory (requiring the lowest energy level E = epsilon_0 - mu = 0) is combined with the Sigma_N term extracted from pion-nucleon scattering to determine the bag radius. The Sigma_N discrepancy is addressed by introducing a metastable color superconducting vacuum phase with vanishing chiral order parameter.

## Key numbers

- Soliton radius: 0.67 fm ≤ R ≤ 0.78 fm
- Energy density difference between chiral symmetry breaking phase and metastable color superconducting phase: 0.41 GeV/fm^3 ≤ Δε ≤ 0.85 GeV/fm^3
- Wave function renormalization factor: J_n = 0.21 (corresponding to Z_psi = 0.79), consistent with lattice result Z_psi = 0.7-0.8 from Wlms
- Vacuum expectation value from GOR relation: <0|anti-psi psi|0> = -0.024 GeV^3 (assuming m_0 = 7 MeV)
- Vacuum sigma field: sigma_vac ≈ 350 MeV
- Cutoff: Lambda = 0.9 GeV
- Coupling parameter: alpha_0 ≈ 0.38
- Vacuum polarization contribution to Sigma_N: Δv δ<0|anti-psi psi|0> = 5.5 to 8.4
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV

## Context

This work extends the local theory developed by the author (Ying, earlier publications) which uses an 8-component real representation for fermions and incorporates statistical blocking effects. The approach addresses two puzzles: the stability of nucleons inside nuclei (where bag/soliton models typically allow significant changes to the nucleon structure) and the Sigma_N term discrepancy between values extracted from pion-nucleon scattering data (Olsson, GWash) versus baryonic spectra (Gasser). The resolution via a metastable color superconducting phase builds on earlier work on PCAC extensions (pcac1, pcac2) and enables a consistent picture where the nucleon retains its identity in nuclear matter while explaining the large Sigma_N values from recent data and lattice QCD evaluations (latt1, latt2).
