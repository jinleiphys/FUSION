# Systematics of the first 2+ excitation in spherical nuclei with Skryme-QRPA

> We use the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) and the Skyrme interactions SLy4 and SkM* to systematically calculate energies and transition strengths for the lowest 2+ state in spherical even-even nuclei.The SkM* functional, applied to 178 spherical nuclei between Z=10 and 90, produces excitation energies that are on average 11% higher than experimental values, with residuals that fluctuate about the average by -35%+55%. The predictions of SkM* and SLy4 have significant differences, in part because of differences in the calculated ground state deformations; SkM* performs better in both the average and dispersion of energies. Comparing the QRPA results with those of generator-coordinate-method (GCM) calculations, we find that the QRPA reproduces trends near closed shells better than the GCM, and overpredicts the energies less severely in general. We attribute part of the difference to a deficiency in the way the GCM is implemented.

# Systematics of the first 2+ excitation in spherical nuclei with Skyrme-QRPA

## Key claim

The QRPA with the SkM* functional, applied to 178 spherical even-even nuclei between Z=10 and 90, produces excitation energies that are on average 11% higher than experimental values, but with residuals that fluctuate about the average by -35% to +55%, indicating that the systematic offset is smaller than the dispersion. The QRPA reproduces trends near closed shells better than Generator-Coordinate Method (GCM) calculations and overpredicts energies less severely, though part of the difference is attributed to a deficiency in the way the GCM is implemented.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations assuming spherical symmetry with a radial mesh of 0.05 fm and box radius of 16 fm, using two Skyrme functionals (SLy4 and SkM*) with volume pairing interactions. They constructed the QRPA in a two-quasiparticle basis with truncation limits on occupation numbers and single-particle energies, then computed energies and B(E2) transition strengths for the lowest 2+ state in spherical even-even nuclei selected from the 507 nuclei with measured 2+ states tabulated in Raman (2001). Spherical nuclei were identified by constrained HFB calculations on a grid of deformations, dropping cases where the lowest-energy configuration had nonzero deformation.

## Key numbers

- SkM* functional applied to 178 spherical nuclei: excitation energies on average 11% higher than experiment, with residuals fluctuating by -35% to +55%
- SLy4 functional applied to 155 spherical nuclei: average R_E = 0.33 (calculated energies about 40% higher than experiment), dispersion sigma_E = 0.51 (error bars of +66/-40%)
- SkM* functional on 178 spherical nuclei: average R_E = 0.11, dispersion sigma_E = 0.44
- For the 129 nuclei common to both functionals: SLy4 average R_E = 0.26, SkM* average R_E = 0.14
- For B(E2) transition strengths: SLy4 average R_Q = -0.32, sigma_Q = 0.42; SkM* average R_Q = -0.29, sigma_Q = 0.53
- The nucleus 210Po shows B(E2) strength of 200 e^2fm^4, compared to a pure shell-model transition of 454 e^2fm^4
- Largest QRPA matrix dimension encountered: 6086 for 220Th
- Pairing strengths for SkM*: V_0 = 170 MeV fm^3 for neutrons, V_0 = 200 MeV fm^3 for protons
- Pairing strengths for SLy4: V_0 = 200 MeV fm^3 for neutrons, V_0 = 240 MeV fm^3 for protons

## Context

This work extends systematic QRPA calculations previously applied to Ca, Ni, and Sn isotopes (Terasaki 2006) to cover essentially all known lowest-lying 2+ states in spherical nuclei. It provides direct comparison with two other systematic approaches: the GCM-Hill-Wheeler calculations of Sabbey (2007) using the same SLy4 functional, and the GCM-5Dimensional Collective Hamiltonian calculations of Goutte (2007) using the Gogny interaction. The results enable benchmarking of different theoretical approaches and functionals for collective excitations, and the authors plan to extend the study to deformed nuclei for more comprehensive comparison.
