# Phenomenology of light baryon resonances

> Protons and neutrons are the building blocks of matter, glued together in nuclei by strong interactions. They can be excited by pions, real and virtual photons, neutrinos and other probes. These excitations are referred to as light baryon resonances. A short, pedagogical overview of the field is presented including experimental progress, interpretation of light baryon resonances, and a focus on analysis methods to extract the resonance spectrum from data.

## Key claim
This paper provides a pedagogical overview of light baryon resonance phenomenology, focusing on analysis methods to extract the resonance spectrum from data, while acknowledging that the separation of overlapping resonances from background is inherently model-dependent and that the only universal resonance parameters are complex pole positions and residues.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental progress in light baryon spectroscopy using pion-induced reactions, photo- and electroproduction, and discusses analysis methods including partial-wave decomposition, coupled-channel approaches, and the use of S-matrix theory to extract resonance parameters. It presents a quantum mechanical example using the finite spherical well solved via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to illustrate how bound states and scattering states are connected through analyticity, and compares this to actual pion-nucleon scattering data for the P33 and S11 partial waves. The paper also covers lattice QCD methods using the Luscher formalism to extract phase shifts from finite-volume energy eigenvalues, and chiral unitary approaches that generate resonances through non-linear resummation of interactions.

## Key numbers
- The elastic window between the pi N and pi pi N threshold is larger when using unphysical pion masses about twice as heavy as the physical one (e.g., in lattice QCD studies such as Silvi (2021))
- The second resonance region is located at W approximately 1.5-1.6 GeV, containing resonances with J^P = 1/2^- and states like the N(1520)3/2^-
- A sharp threshold cusp exists at W = 1486 MeV next to the N(1535)1/2^- resonance
- The Delta(1232)3/2^+ is the only light baryon resonance clearly and directly visible in pion- and photon-induced reactions

## Context
This pedagogical review extends the recent comprehensive review by Doring (2025) and complements the interpretation-focused review by Burkert (2025) and the experimental review by Thiel (2022). It provides a literature collection of almost all reviews and key publications on light baryon spectroscopy, and enables future work by summarizing the current state of analysis methods including lattice QCD approaches from Bulava (2022) and Silvi (2021), chiral unitary approaches, and the challenges of complete experiments in photo- and electroproduction reactions.
