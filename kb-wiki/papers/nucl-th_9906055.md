# Transverse Energy Production at RHIC

> We study the mechanism of transverse energy (E_T) production in Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The time evolution starting from the initial energy loss to the final E_T production is closely examined in transport models. The relationship between the experimentally measured E_T distribution and the maximum energy density achieved is discussed.

## Key claim

The maximum energy density achieved in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC cannot be determined solely from the measured transverse energy distribution, because the time evolution of energy density and transverse energy are not exactly correlated in transport model simulations.

## Method

The authors constructed the energy-momentum tensor and particle number current from 144 central (b=0) Au+Au events generated with the transport model RQMD v2.4 at RHIC energy (100 GeV/A + 100 GeV/A). They computed local energy density, pressure, and flow velocity by boosting and diagonalizing the energy-momentum tensor in small volume elements (spheres of 1 fm radius), then studied the time evolution of these quantities and the transverse energy at mid-rapidity.

## Key numbers

- 144 central (b=0) Au+Au events were generated using RQMD v2.4 at RHIC energy (100 GeV/A + 100 GeV/A)
- The small volume V used for computing the energy-momentum tensor was a sphere of 1 fm radius
- After about 7 fm/c, the system becomes approximately isotropic in pressure, though not in local thermal equilibrium

## Context

This work extends the study of energy density extraction from transverse energy measurements at RHIC, using the transport model RQMD (Sorge et al.) as a first step. The authors note that RQMD's initial state is not partonic, so it may not be fully applicable for RHIC, but it has a well-tested hadronic after-burner. They plan to extend the analysis to other models including VNI, HIJING, VENUS, and UrQMD, and conclude that a reliable estimate of maximum energy density will require combining transverse energy measurements with collective observables such as radial flow, elliptic flow, and HBT system size.
