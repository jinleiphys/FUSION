# Three-body forces in Bethe-Salpeter and light-front equations

> In relativistic frameworks, given by the Bethe-Salpeter and light-front bound state equations, the binding energies of system of three scalar particles interacting by scalar exchange particles are calculated. In contrast to two-body systems, the three-body binding energies obtained in these two approaches differ significantly from each other: the ladder kernel in light-front dynamics underbinds by approximately a factor of two compared to the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation. By taking into account three-body forces in the light-front approach, generated by two exchange particles in flight, we find that most of this difference disappears; for small exchange masses, the obtained binding energies coincide with each other.

# Three-body forces in Bethe-Salpeter and light-front equations

## Key claim
For three scalar particles interacting by scalar exchange, the ladder kernel in light-front dynamics underbinds by approximately a factor of two compared to the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation; however, when three-body forces generated by two exchange particles in flight are included perturbatively in the light-front approach, most of this difference disappears and the binding energies coincide for small exchange masses.

## Method
The authors solved the three-body Bethe-Salpeter (BS) bound state equation and the three-body light-front dynamics (LFD) bound state equation numerically for a system of three scalar particles interacting via one-boson exchange, using a scalar field theory with interaction \(-g\phi^2\varphi\). The BS equation was transformed to Euclidean space and solved as a four-dimensional integral equation, while the LFD equation was solved as a three-dimensional integral equation; both amplitudes depend on five scalar variables and were discretized directly without partial-wave expansion. The correction from three-body forces in LFD was computed perturbatively by evaluating the set of LFD graphs corresponding to two exchange particles in flight and a pair-creation diagram, then adding the resulting mass-squared shift \(\Delta M_3^2\) to the LFD bound state mass squared.

## Key numbers
- For exchange mass \(\mu = 0.01\) and \(\mu = 0.5\) (with constituent mass \(m = 1\)), the three-body binding energies from BS, LFD, and non-relativistic SchrÃ¶dinger equation all differ significantly; the LFD ladder kernel underbinds by approximately a factor of two compared to the BS ladder kernel.
- For exchange mass \(\mu = 1.5\) (three times the constituent mass), the perturbative correction from three-body forces explains about 70% of the difference in \(M_3^2\) between the BS approach and LFD.
- For small exchange masses (\(\mu = 0.01\)), the perturbative correction brings the LFD mass squared \(M_3^2\) into good agreement with the BS result, even when the correction is large compared to \(M^2\) itself.

## Context
This work extends the comparison of BS and LFD approaches from two-body systems, where Karmanov and Maris (2000) found that the binding energies nearly coincide, to three-body systems where a large discrepancy appears. The three-body forces from two exchange particles in flight were first identified by Brueckner (1953) and their contribution to the triton was calculated by Yang (1974), though GlÃ¶ckle (1978) showed that in the non-relativistic SchrÃ¶dinger equation these corrections cancel with relativistic corrections to the one-boson exchange iteration. The present work demonstrates that in a fully relativistic framework these three-body forces are essential and explains the difference between BS and LFD results. This enables future studies of three-nucleon bound states (triton, \({}^3\)He) and baryons as bound states of three dressed quarks, where such three-body forces from exchange particles in flight will contribute significantly beyond experimental precision.
