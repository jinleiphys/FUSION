# Chiral effective field theory predictions for muon capture on deuteron and 3He

> The muon-capture reactions 2H(μ^-,ν_μ)nn and 3He(μ^-,ν_μ)3H are studied with nuclear strong-interaction potentials and charge-changing weak currents, derived in chiral effective field theory. The low-energy constants (LEC's) c_D and c_E, present in the three-nucleon potential and (c_D) axial-vector current, are constrained to reproduce the A=3 binding energies and the triton Gamow-Teller matrix element. The vector weak current is related to the isovector component of the electromagnetic current via the conserved-vector-current constraint, and the two LEC's entering the contact terms in the latter are constrained to reproduce the A=3 magnetic moments. The muon capture rates on deuteron and 3He are predicted to be 399(3) sec^{-1} and 1494 (21) sec^{-1}, respectively, where the spread accounts for the cutoff sensitivity as well as uncertainties in the LEC's and electroweak radiative corrections. By comparing the calculated and precisely measured rates on 3He, a value for the induced pseudoscalar form factor is obtained in good agreement with the chiral perturbation theory prediction.

# Chiral effective field theory predictions for muon capture on deuteron and 3He

## Key claim
The muon capture rates on deuteron and 3He are predicted to be 399 ± 3 sec⁻¹ and 1494 ± 21 sec⁻¹, respectively, where the spread accounts for cutoff sensitivity, uncertainties in low-energy constants, and electroweak radiative corrections. By comparing the calculated and precisely measured rate on 3He, a value for the induced pseudoscalar form factor is obtained in good agreement with the chiral perturbation theory prediction.

## Method
The authors used nuclear strong-interaction potentials and charge-changing weak currents both derived in chiral effective field theory (χEFT). The low-energy constants c_D and c_E in the three-nucleon potential and axial-vector current were constrained by fitting the A=3 binding energies and the triton Gamow-Teller matrix element; the vector weak current was related to the isovector electromagnetic current via the conserved-vector-current constraint, with its contact-term LEC's constrained by A=3 magnetic moments. Nuclear wave functions for ³H and ³He were calculated with the hyperspherical-harmonics method using chiral NN+NNN potentials for momentum cutoffs Λ = 500 MeV and 600 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Muon capture rate on deuteron: 399 ± 3 sec⁻¹ (includes cutoff sensitivity, LEC uncertainties, and electroweak radiative corrections)
- Muon capture rate on ³He: 1494 ± 21 sec⁻¹ (includes same sources of uncertainty)
- Experimentally measured muon capture rate on ³He: 1496 ± 4 sec⁻¹ (Ackerbauer et al., 1998)
- Induced pseudoscalar form factor G_PS at q₀² = -0.954 m_μ²: 8.2 ± 0.7, compared to χPT prediction of 7.99 ± 0.20
- Triton Gamow-Teller matrix element (experimental): GT^EXP = 0.955 ± 0.004
- Range of c_D values for Λ = 500 MeV: [-0.20, -0.04]; for Λ = 600 MeV: [-0.32, -0.19]
- Range of c_E values for Λ = 500 MeV: [-0.208, -0.184]; for Λ = 600 MeV: [-0.857, -0.833]
- Doublet nd scattering length: 0.666 ± 0.001 fm (Λ = 500 MeV) and 0.696 ± 0.001 fm (Λ = 600 MeV), compared to experimental value 0.645 ± 0.010 fm (Schoen et al., 2003)

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of muon capture on deuteron and ³He performed within the "standard nuclear physics approach" (SNPA) and the "hybrid" χEFT approach, where weak operators from χEFT were evaluated with wave functions from conventional potentials. Prior SNPA calculations (Marcucci et al., 2011) gave 391 sec⁻¹ for deuteron and 1486 sec⁻¹ for ³He, while hybrid χEFT studies (Ando et al., 2002; Gazit et al., 2008; Marcucci et al., 2011) reported rates ranging from 386 to 393 sec⁻¹ for deuteron and 1484 to 1499 sec⁻¹ for ³He. The present fully consistent χEFT calculation enables tighter constraints on the induced pseudoscalar form factor and provides a framework for testing chiral perturbation theory predictions, with direct relevance to the forthcoming MuSun collaboration measurement of the deuteron capture rate at PSI with projected 1% precision.
