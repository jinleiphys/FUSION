# Rapidity decorrelation of anisotropic flow caused by hydrodynamic fluctuations

> We investigate the effect of hydrodynamic fluctuations on the rapidity decorrelations of anisotropic flow in high-energy nuclear collisions using a (3+1)-dimensional integrated dynamical model. The integrated dynamical model consists of twisted initial conditions, fluctuating hydrodynamics, and hadronic cascades on an event-by-event basis. To understand the rapidity decorrelation, we analyze the factorization ratio in the longitudinal direction. Comparing the factorization ratios between fluctuating hydrodynamics and ordinary viscous hydrodynamics, we find a sizable effect of hydrodynamic fluctuations on rapidity decorrelations. We also propose to calculate the Legendre coefficients of the flow magnitude and the event-plane angle to understand the decorrelation of anisotropic flow in the longitudinal direction.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic fluctuations, arising from thermal noise in the shear stress tensor, cause a measurable decorrelation of anisotropic flow in the longitudinal (rapidity) direction, an effect not captured by ordinary viscous hydrodynamics alone; the magnitude of this decorrelation depends on the cutoff parameters that regulate the ultraviolet singularity in the fluctuation-dissipation relation.

## Method

The authors employed a (3+1)-dimensional integrated dynamical model combining Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions with a modified Brodsky-Gunion-Kuhn longitudinal profile, the relativistic fluctuating hydrodynamics code rfh (solving second-order causal equations with stochastic shear-stress noise), and the JAM hadronic cascade for the final stage. They compared fluctuating hydrodynamics (with four choices of the Gaussian smearing cutoff parameters λ⊥ and λη) against ideal hydrodynamics and ordinary viscous hydrodynamics (η/s = 1/4π) for Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, analyzing the factorization ratio rn(ηpa, ηpb) and Legendre coefficients of flow magnitude and event-plane angle.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity fixed at η/s = 1/4π and relaxation time τπ = 3/4πT throughout all viscous and fluctuating hydrodynamic runs.
- Initial proper time τ0 = 0.6 fm and switching temperature Tsw = 155 MeV.
- Cutoff parameters for fluctuating hydrodynamics: λ⊥ = λη = 2.5, 2.0, 1.5, and 1.0 (λ⊥ in fm, λη dimensionless).
- Normalization factor C/τ0 ranges from 62 fm⁻¹ (ideal) down to 31 fm⁻¹ (fluctuating hydro with λ=1.0); hard fraction α ranges from 0.08 (ideal) to 0.20 (fluctuating hydro with λ=1.0).
- 4000 hydrodynamic events generated per parameter set, with 100 independent particlization/hadronic-cascade samples per hydrodynamic event, yielding 400,000 total events.
- Elliptic flow v2{2}(pT) from viscous and fluctuating hydrodynamics reproduces ALICE data for pT below 1.5 GeV in 40–50% centrality; fluctuating hydrodynamics gives nearly identical v2{2}(pT) to ordinary viscous hydrodynamics.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of rapidity decorrelation that considered initial twists (Bozek 2017), longitudinal fluctuations (Wu 2018, Jia 2017, Bozek 2015), glasma (Schenke 2016), and dynamical initial states (Shen 2017), none of which included hydrodynamic fluctuations. By incorporating thermal noise via the fluctuation-dissipation relation, the authors introduce a new physical mechanism for longitudinal decorrelation that is independent of initial-state longitudinal structure. The results enable future systematic studies to constrain transport coefficients and cutoff scales by comparing factorization ratios across centrality, harmonic order, and collision energy within a single model.
