# The pseudo scalar form factor of the nucleon, the sigma-like term, and the $L_0^+$ amplitude for charged pion electro-production near threshold

> The pseudo scalar form factor, which represents the pseudo scalar quark density distribution due to finite quark masses on the nucleon, is shown to manifest itself with the induced pseudo scalar form factor in the $L_0^+$ amplitude for the charged pion electro-production. Both form factors show their own peculiar momentum dependence. Under the approximation on which the Goldberg-Treimann relation holds, a sum of both form factors' contributions accounts for the t-channel contribution in the charged pion electro-production near threshold.

## Key claim

The pseudo scalar (PS) form factor of the nucleon, representing the pseudo scalar quark density distribution due to finite quark masses, contributes alongside the induced pseudo scalar form factor to the \(L_0^+\) amplitude for charged pion electro-production near threshold; under the approximation where the Goldberger-Treiman relation holds, the sum of both form factors accounts for the t-channel contribution, but beyond that approximation they must be distinguished.

## Method

The authors start from the QCD Lagrangian with external electromagnetic fields and use chiral Ward identities to derive the transition amplitude for charged pion electro-production near threshold. They express the amplitude in terms of the induced pseudo scalar form factor \(G_P(t)\) (from the axial current) and the pseudo scalar form factor \(\Pi(t)\) (from the sigma-like term \(\Sigma_a^\nu\), an explicit chiral symmetry breaking effect). The \(L_0^+\) amplitude is then evaluated at the pion threshold using a tree-diagram approach with form factors taken from third-order chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- The time component of the sigma-like term at the pion threshold, \(i \bar{\Sigma}_\alpha^0 |_{\text{thre.}}\), is 38.1 MeV at the \(\gamma\) point (\(k^2 = 0\)).
- The Goldberger-Treiman deviation constant \(\Delta_{\pi N} = 0.026\).
- The axial mass \(M_A = 1069\) MeV.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant \(g_{\pi N} = 13.21\).
- The axial coupling constant \(g_A = 1.267\).
- The pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV.
- The ratio \(R = L_0^+_{(\Sigma_a^0)} / L_0^+\) for \(\gamma^* \pi^+\) at the pseudo-threshold limit is about \(1/2\), meaning half of the total \(L_0^+\) amplitude comes from the sigma-like term.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of explicit chiral symmetry breaking effects in nucleon structure, analogous to how the \(\sigma_{\pi N}\) term in \(\pi-N\) scattering reveals scalar quark distributions. It builds on prior calculations of the pseudo scalar form factor and the induced pseudo scalar form factor from chiral perturbation theory (Fuc03, Bern02) and on the Ward-Takahasi identity approach (Schae91). The results enable future extraction of the pseudo scalar form factor \(\Pi(t)\) from charged pion electro-production data, complementing efforts to measure the induced pseudo scalar form factor \(G_P(t)\) and the pion form factor \(F_\pi(k^2)\) from the \(L_0^+\) amplitude, as attempted at Saclay (Choi93) and Mainz.
