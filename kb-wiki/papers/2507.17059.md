# Quantifying alpha clustering in the ground states of 16-O and 20-Ne

> Understanding the role of multi-nucleon correlations in the structure of light nuclei is at the forefront of modern nuclear science. In this letter, we present a quantitative benchmark study of alpha-cluster correlations in the ground states of 16-O and 20-Ne. Experimental data provide direct evidence that the wave functions of the ground states of 16-O and 20-Ne are dominated by alpha-cluster correlations, in agreement with the predictions of sophisticated nuclear structure models. We also provide a new model-independent constraint for the alpha asymptotic normalization coefficient of the 16-O ground state and discuss the implications of these findings on the 12-C(alpha,gamma)16-O reaction, which is of critical importance for nuclear astrophysics.

# Quantifying alpha clustering in the ground states of 16-O and 20-Ne

## Key claim
The ground-state wave functions of 16-O and 20-Ne are dominated by alpha-cluster correlations, with the experimental product of squared asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) reaching only 30% below the maximum predicted by an extreme core-plus-alpha potential model. The result provides a model-independent benchmark for microscopic theories, but the 16-O ground-state alpha ANC extracted using theoretical 20-Ne ANC values shows significant variation across different theoretical approaches.

## Method
The 12-C(20-Ne,16-O)16-O alpha-transfer reaction was measured at a sub-Coulomb beam energy of 1.0 MeV/u (20 MeV total) using the K150 cyclotron at Texas A&M University, with the MDM magnetic spectrometer and TexPPAC focal-plane detector. Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) calculations using FRESCO were performed to extract the product of squared alpha ANCs for the ground states of 16-O and 20-Ne, with the experimental analysis and three independent theoretical calculations (Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory, Symmetry-Adapted No-Core Shell Model, and Cluster Nucleon Configuration Interaction) conducted in a double-blind protocol to prevent confirmation bias.

## Key numbers
- Experimental product of squared ANCs: (C^{16-O}_{alpha,12-C})^2 (C^{20-Ne}_{alpha,16-O})^2 = 9.4(1.4)(2) x 10^12 fm^{-2}, where uncertainties are statistical and theoretical systematic, respectively
- Extreme cluster model prediction for the same product: 13 x 10^12 fm^{-2}, only 30% above the experimental value
- NLEFT predictions: 16-O ANC = 380(80) fm^{-1/2}, 20-Ne ANC = 3800(950) fm^{-1/2}
- SA-NCSM predictions: 16-O ANC = 210(30) fm^{-1/2}, 20-Ne ANC = 3760(470) fm^{-1/2}
- CN-CI predictions: 16-O ANC = 520(30) fm^{-1/2}, 20-Ne ANC = 4500(500) fm^{-1/2}
- Alpha separation energies: 7.16 MeV for 16-O, 4.73 MeV for 20-Ne
- Beam energy: 1.0 MeV/u (20 MeV total), corresponding to c.m. energy of 7.5 MeV for the 20-Ne+12-C system, below the Coulomb barrier of approximately 11 MeV
- Target thickness: 22 microgram/cm^2 natural carbon
- Overall theoretical uncertainty of the DWBA analysis: 20%

## Context
This work extends the sub-Coulomb alpha-transfer technique pioneered by Brune (1999) and applied by Johnson (2006, 2009) to quantify alpha clustering in ground states, providing a benchmark for three state-of-the-art microscopic approaches: NLEFT (Epelbaum 2012), SA-NCSM (Launey 2021), and CN-CI (Kravvaris 2019). The results enable new model-independent constraints on the 16-O ground-state alpha ANC, which directly impacts the 12-C(alpha,gamma)16-O reaction rate critical for nuclear astrophysics; the new constraints are significantly above the most recent transfer-reaction ANC value from Shen (2023) but consistent with upper limits from Adhikari (2017) and Mondal (2021). The R-matrix analysis using these constraints shows a 5% to 10% decrease in the total S-factor at the Gamow energy of 300 keV, remaining within the 20% uncertainty quoted by deBoer (2017).
