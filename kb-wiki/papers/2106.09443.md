# Global spin polarization of multistrange hyperons and feed-down effect in heavy-ion collisions

> Global spin polarization of hyperons is an important observable to probe the vorticity of the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions. We calculate the global polarizations of $Λ$, $Ξ^-$, and $Ω^-$ in Au+Au collisions at energies $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}=$ 7.7--200 GeV based on a multiphase transport model. Our calculations suggest that their primary global polarizations fulfill $P_{Ω^-}\simeq 5/3P_{Ξ^-}\simeq 5/3P_Λ$. We also estimate the feed-down effect of particle decay on the global polarization. With the feed-down effect taken into account, the global polarizations of $Λ$ and $Ξ^-$ are clearly separated, and the final global polarizations are in the ordering: $P_{Ω^-}>P_{Ξ^-}>P_Λ$. Such a relation can be tested in experiments, which will provide us more information about the global polarization mechanism.

## Key claim

The primary global polarizations of Λ, Ξ⁻, and Ω⁻ hyperons satisfy the approximate relation P_Ω⁻ ≃ 5/3 P_Ξ⁻ ≃ 5/3 P_Λ, and after including the feed-down effect from particle decays, the final global polarizations follow the ordering P_Ω⁻ > P_Ξ⁻ > P_Λ. This ordering is consistent with experimental data at 200 GeV but requires further measurements at other beam energies to be definitive.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of a multiphase transport (AMPT) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 7.7–200 GeV. They extracted the thermal vorticity field via a coarse-grained method, dividing space-time into grid cells and computing the energy-momentum tensor from event-averaged particle momenta, then solving for fluid velocity and temperature using the lattice equation of state. The primary global polarization of each hyperon was calculated by applying the thermal vorticity formula (Eq. PH) at each hyperon's formation location and averaging over the kinematic region |y| < 1 and p_T > 0.5 GeV/c. The feed-down effect on Λ and Ξ⁻ polarizations was estimated using a thermal statistical model (THERMUS) to compute relative yields of parent particles and applying polarization transfer factors for two-body decays.

## Key numbers

- Primary global polarizations satisfy P_Ω⁻ ≃ 5/3 P_Ξ⁻ ≃ 5/3 P_Λ, based on the spin factor (S+1) where S_Ω⁻ = 3/2 and S_Λ = S_Ξ⁻ = 1/2.
- Feed-down effect reduces the global Λ polarization by 8.3% at √s_NN = 200 GeV and by 13.2% at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV (20–50% centrality).
- Feed-down effect increases the global Ξ⁻ polarization by 27.1% at √s_NN = 200 GeV and by 24.2% at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV (20–50% centrality).
- The ratio of primary Ξ⁻ to Ξ(1530)→Ξ⁻ yields is about 59:41 in 20–50% central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV.
- Polarization transfer factor C for Ξ⁻ → Λπ⁻ is approximately 0.944; for Ξ⁰ → Λπ⁰ it is approximately 0.915.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of global Λ polarization using the AMPT model (Li 2017, Shi 2017, Xia 2018) and hydrodynamic calculations (Karpenko 2016, Fu 2020) by computing the global polarizations of multistrange hyperons Ξ⁻ and Ω⁻ for the first time in this framework. It also improves upon earlier feed-down estimates for Λ (Becattini 2016, Karpenko 2016, Li 2017) by including the two-step decay chain Ξ(1530)→Ξ→Λ, which was previously neglected. The predicted ordering P_Ω⁻ > P_Ξ⁻ > P_Λ can be tested by the STAR experiment's beam energy scan program at RHIC, providing a test of the thermal vorticity interpretation of global polarization.
