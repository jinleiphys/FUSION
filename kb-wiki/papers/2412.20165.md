# Spectral sum rules and phase transition in strongly coupled QCD

> By incorporating contributions from both the (chromo)electric scale $gT$ and (chromo)magnetic scale $g^2T$, we establish spectral sum rules of quarks for strongly coupled QCD that respect Fermi-Dirac statistics as required by quantum mechanics. In sharp contrast to QED and weakly coupled QCD whose spectral functions consist of discontinuous zero-dimensional (poles) and one-dimensional (branch cuts) non-analytic contributions from real energy $p_0 \in \mathbb{R}$, the derived spectral function for strongly coupled quarks features continuous but non-analytic contributions from complex energy $p_0 \in \mathbb{C}$ that are two-dimensional in nature. In light of the novel sum rules, we uncover an intrinsic QCD transition between a three-mode phase at small coupling and a one-mode phase at large coupling. The transition is induced by the magnetic scale that generates a massless hydro-like mode with the genuine non-Abelian feature of positivity violation and serving as the Goldstone mode of the Lorentz symmetry breaking. The thermal mass serves as an order parameter of the transition and vanishes at large coupling in line with phenomenological predictions from Dyson-Schwinger equations and gauge/gravity duality. This result provides novel insights into the mechanism of the QCD deconfinement transition.

## Key claim

By incorporating both the (chromo)electric scale \(gT\) and (chromo)magnetic scale \(g^2T\) via the Gribov-Zwanziger quantization, the authors derive spectral sum rules for quarks in strongly coupled QCD that reveal an intrinsic transition between a three-mode phase at small coupling and a one-mode phase at large coupling, with the thermal mass serving as an order parameter that vanishes at large coupling.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop quark propagator in the Gribov-Zwanziger (GZ) quantization of finite-temperature Yang-Mills theory, using hard-thermal-loop (HTL) systematics to incorporate both electric and magnetic scales. They define a novel spectral density sensitive to local non-analytic features in the complex energy plane via Green's theorem, and evaluate it numerically for varying coupling strengths at vanishing and finite spatial momentum. The spectral sum rules are derived by expanding the propagator in inverse powers of energy and comparing contour integrals around the non-analytic regions.

## Key numbers

- The Gribov parameter is \(\gamma_\smallG = \frac{D-1}{D} \frac{N_c}{4\sqrt{2}\pi} g^2 T\) at asymptotically high temperatures, providing a non-perturbative infrared cutoff at the magnetic scale.
- The thermal quark mass in QED (or weakly coupled QCD) is \(m_q^2 = g^2 T^2 C_F/8\).
- The \(n=2\) sum rule involves \(\tilde m_q^2(\gamma_\smallG) = \frac{g^2 C_F}{4\pi} \sum_\pm \int_0^\infty dk \,k^2 \tilde n_\pm(k,\gamma_\smallG)/E_\pm\), which reduces to \(m_q\) when \(\gamma_\smallG=0\).
- The transition from three-mode to one-mode phase occurs in the coupling range \(1.5 < g < 6.5\), with poles collapsing around \(g \gtrsim 4.5\) at vanishing spatial momentum.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Zwanziger quantization approach to thermal quarks, building on prior studies of the gluon propagator and collective excitations in the GZ formalism by Zwanziger (2004, 2006), Fukushima (2013), and Su (2014). The results contrast with thermal QED and weakly coupled QCD, where spectral functions have poles and branch cuts only on the real energy axis, as described by Bellac (2011). The finding of a massless hydro-like mode with positivity violation connects quark-gluon plasma phenomenology (Hidaka 2011, Satow 2013, Blaizot 2014) with color confinement studies (Cucchieri 2004, Sternbeck 2006, Fischer 2008). The vanishing thermal mass at large coupling agrees with predictions from Dyson-Schwinger equations and gauge/gravity duality, providing new insights into the QCD deconfinement transition.
