# Various Models for Pion Probability Distributions from Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Various models for pion multiplicity distributions produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions are discussed. The models include a relativistic hydrodynamic model, a thermodynamic description, an emitting source pion laser model, and a description which generates a negative binomial description. The approach developed can be used to discuss other cases which will be mentioned. The pion probability distributions for these various cases are compared. Comparison of the pion laser model and Bose-Einstein condensation in a laser trap and with the thermal model are made. The thermal model and hydrodynamic model are also used to illustrate why the number of pions never diverges and why the Bose-Einstein correction effects are relatively small. The pion emission strength $η$ of a Poisson emitter and a critical density $η_c$ are connected in a thermal model by $η/n_c = e^{-m/T} < 1$, and this fact reduces any Bose-Einstein correction effects in the number and number fluctuation of pions. Fluctuations can be much larger than Poisson in the pion laser model and for a negative binomial description. The clan representation of the negative binomial distribution due to Van Hove and Giovannini is discussed using the present description. Applications to CERN/NA44 and CERN/NA49 data are discussed in terms of the relativistic hydrodynamic model.

# Various Models for Pion Probability Distributions from Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that four distinct models of pion production (thermal, relativistic hydrodynamic, emitting source/pion laser, and negative binomial) can be unified through a single formalism based on cycle class decompositions, where each model corresponds to a different choice of the quantity $x_k$. The thermal model and hydrodynamic model show that Bose-Einstein correction effects remain relatively small because the ratio $\eta/\eta_c = e^{-m/T} < 1$ ensures the total pion number never diverges, unlike the pion laser model where $\eta$ and $\eta_c$ are independent parameters.

## Method

The authors express each model's pion multiplicity distribution through a weight function $W_A(\vec n, \vec x) = (1/Z_A(\vec x)) \prod_k x_k^{n_k}/n_k!$, where $x_k$ encodes the model-specific physics. For the thermal model, $x_k = (VT^3/2\pi^2)(m/T)^2 (1/k^2) K_2(k m/T)$; for the relativistic hydrodynamic model (HYLANDER-C), $x_k$ values are obtained by expanding the Bose-Einstein occupation factor in the Cooper-Frye freeze-out integral; for the emitting source model, $x_k$ follows from a thermal Gaussian source strength; and for the negative binomial distribution, $x_k = a y^k/k$. The canonical partition function $Z_N$ is computed via the recurrence relation $Z_A = (1/A) \sum_k k x_k Z_{A-k}$ with $Z_0=1$, and the probability of $N$ pions is $P_N = Z_N/z$ where $z = \exp(\sum_k x_k)$.

## Key numbers

- For the relativistic hydrodynamic model (HYLANDER-C) applied to 158 A GeV Pb+Pb collisions at CERN, the number of thermal pions (excluding resonance decays) is 853, with a freeze-out temperature $T_f = 139$ MeV and critical temperature $T_C = 200$ MeV for the quark-gluon plasma phase transition.
- The fluctuation in thermal pions from the hydrodynamic model is $\langle N^2 \rangle - \langle N \rangle^2 = 312.97$, a 20% enhancement over the Poisson result of $x_1 = 260$, with mean $\langle N \rangle = 283.9$.
- For the thermal model at $T = 163$ MeV, $V = 22.5$, and $m/T = 0.85$, the probability distribution $P_N$ is tabulated using the recurrence relation.
- In the ultrarelativistic (massless) limit of the thermal model, $x_k = (V/\pi^2) T^3 (1/k^4)$ with Fisher exponent $\tau = 4$; in the nonrelativistic limit, $x_k = (V/\lambda_T^3) e^{-k m/T} (1/k^{5/2})$ with $\tau = 5/2$.
- For the negative binomial distribution with $x_k = a y^k/k$, the variance is $\langle N^2 \rangle - \langle N \rangle^2 = \langle N \rangle (1 + \langle N \rangle / a)$.

## Context

This work extends the unified cluster-model formalism developed by Mekjian (1990) and collaborators for nuclear fragmentation and Bose-Einstein condensation, applying it to pion multiplicity distributions in heavy-ion collisions. The thermal model results connect to the statistical approach of Becattini, Giovannini, and Lupia (1996); the emitting source model follows Pratt (1993) with further developments by Csörgő and Zimányi (1997) and Chao, Gao, and Zhang (1995); and the negative binomial distribution is linked to the clan representation of Van Hove and Giovannini (1986-1989). The relativistic hydrodynamic model HYLANDER-C, previously applied to S+S reactions at 200 AGeV and validated against NA35 data, is here extended to Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV measured by CERN/NA44 and CERN/NA49. The formalism enables systematic comparison of different production mechanisms and can be applied to other bosonic systems, including Bose-Einstein condensation of atoms in boxes and laser traps.
