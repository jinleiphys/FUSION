# Relativistic Quark Spin Coupling Effects in the Correlations Between Nucleon Electroweak Properties

> We investigate the effect of different relativistic spin couplings of constituent quarks on nucleon electroweak properties. Within each quark spin coupling scheme the correlations between static electroweak observables are found to be independent of the particular shape of the momentum part of the nucleon light-front wave function. The neutron charge form factor is very sensitive to different choices of spin coupling schemes once the magnetic moment is fitted to the experimental value. However, it is found rather insensitive to the details of the momentum part of the three-quark wave function model.

## Key claim

The correlations between static nucleon electroweak observables are independent of the particular shape of the momentum part of the nucleon light-front wave function within each quark spin coupling scheme, but the neutron charge form factor is very sensitive to different choices of spin coupling schemes once the magnetic moment is fitted to the experimental value.

## Method

The authors constructed nucleon electromagnetic and weak isovector axial vector current matrix elements from an effective Lagrangian describing quark spin coupling to the nucleon, using a three-dimensional reduction to the null-plane (light-front dynamics). They evaluated the two-loop triangle diagrams in impulse approximation with complete antisymmetrization of quark states, introducing either a harmonic oscillator (Gaussian) or power-law form for the momentum part of the three-quark light-front wave function. The parameter alpha in the effective Lagrangian was varied to represent scalar pair coupling (alpha=1), gradient coupling (alpha=0), and a mixed case (alpha=1/2) that resembles but is not equivalent to the Bakamjian-Thomas spin coupling scheme.

## Key numbers

- Neutron charge radius experimental value corresponds to xi(0) = 0.21 +/- 0.08, where xi is a dimensionless function defined from the neutron Dirac form factor
- The Foldy approach gives neutron charge radius <r^2_En>^Foldy = -0.126 fm^2 (corresponding to xi(0)=0)
- Constituent quark mass of 220 MeV was used for the main calculations, with additional calculations at 330, 360, and 380 MeV for comparison with Konen and Weber
- The power-law wave function used exponent p=3, chosen because p>2 is required for model independence of static observable correlations
- In the pointlike nucleon limit (beta to infinity), the axial vector coupling constant from the effective Lagrangian tends to approximately -5/3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Suisso et al., 1999) of nucleon electromagnetic form factors using different relativistic spin couplings, where they found that scalar pair coupling was preferred by neutron charge form factor data below 1 GeV/c momentum transfer using only a Gaussian wave function. The present study introduces a power-law wave function to test whether the neutron charge form factor is still reproduced with scalar quark pair coupling when the momentum part of the wave function is relaxed, and extends the investigation to axial vector form factors and correlations between static electroweak observables. The results are compared with the Bakamjian-Thomas (BT) spin coupling scheme (Bakamjian and Thomas, 1953) and with calculations by Konen and Weber (1997), and the model-independent correlations found within the BT scheme by Beyer et al. and Brodsky et al. are shown to persist in the different spin coupling schemes from the effective Lagrangian.
