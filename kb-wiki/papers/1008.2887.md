# Search for $η$-bound Nuclei

> The $η$ meson can be bound to atomic nuclei. Experimental search is discussed in the form of final state interaction for the reactions $dp\to{^3\text{He}}η$ and $dd\to{^4\text{He}}η$. For the latter case tensor polarized deuterons were used in order to extract the s-wave strength. For both reactions complex scattering lengths are deduced: $a_{{}\rm{^3He}η} = \left[ { \pm \left( {10.7 \pm 0.8_{ - 0.5}^{ + 0.1} } \right) + i \cdot \left( {1.5 \pm 2.6_{ - 0.9}^{ + 1.0} } \right)} \right]$ fm and $a_{^4\text{He}η} = \left[ { \pm \left( {3.1 \pm 0.5} \right) + i \cdot \left( {0 \pm 0.5} \right)} \right]$ fm. In a two-nucleon transfer reaction under quasi-free conditions, $p^{27}\text{Al}\to {^3\text{He}}X$, was investigated. The system $X$ can be the bound $^{25}\text{Mg}\otimesη$ at rest. When a possible decay of an intermediate $N^*/1535)$ is required, a highly significant bump shows up in the missing mass spectrum. The data give for a bound state a binding energy of 13.3$\pm$1.6 MeV and a width of $σ$=4.4$\pm$1.3 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper presents evidence that the η meson can form bound states with atomic nuclei, specifically claiming a strong hint of a nuclear η bound state in the system ²⁵Mg ⊗ η, with the caveat that the sign of the real part of the scattering lengths for the ³Heη and ⁴Heη systems remains ambiguous, preventing a definitive conclusion on whether those lighter systems are bound or unbound.

## Method

The study employed two approaches: final-state interaction (FSI) analysis of the two-body reactions dp → ³Heη and dd → ⁴Heη, and a missing-mass technique for the two-nucleon transfer reaction p + ²⁷Al → ³He + X under quasi-free conditions. For the dd → ⁴Heη reaction, a tensor-polarized deuteron beam was used to extract the s-wave contribution to the cross section. In the transfer reaction, the unobserved system X could be a bound ²⁵Mg ⊗ η state, and detection of back-to-back proton-pion pairs from the decay of an intermediate N*(1535) resonance was required to identify a signal in the missing-mass spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length for ³Heη: a = [±(10.7 ± 0.8⁺⁰·¹₋₀·₅) + i·(1.5 ± 2.6⁺¹·⁰₋₀·₉)] fm
- Scattering length for ⁴Heη: a = [±(3.1 ± 0.5) + i·(0 ± 0.5)] fm
- Pole position for ³Heη: |Q| ≈ 0.30 MeV
- Pole position for ⁴Heη: |Q| ≈ 4 MeV
- Total cross section for dd → αη at beam momentum 2385.5 MeV/c (excess energy Q = 16.6 MeV): σ = 16.0 ± 0.4 nb (statistical) ± 1.6 nb (systematic)
- For the bound state candidate in the p + ²⁷Al → ³He + p + π⁻ + X reaction: binding energy BE = 13.13 ± 1.64 MeV, width σ = 4.35 ± 1.27 MeV, with a statistical significance of 6.20σ
- For a two-Gaussian fit to the same data: 1p state centroid at −12.3 ± 1.2 MeV with width σ = 5.1 ± 1.6 MeV; 1s state centroid at −45.4 ± 19.0 MeV with width σ = 37.7 ± 26.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions by Haider and Liu (1986) that η mesons could be bound in nuclei with A ≥ 10, and addresses inconclusive experimental results from BNL (Chrien, 1988) and LAMPF (Lieb, 1988) using the (π⁺, p) reaction. The study also responds to the disputed claim of an η-mesic ³He state observed at MAMI (Pfeiffer, 2004), which was questioned by Hanhart (2005). The results enable future experiments with improved statistics to confirm the nature of the observed missing-mass peak and to search for η-bound states in heavier nuclear systems.
