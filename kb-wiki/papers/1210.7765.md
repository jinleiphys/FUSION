# New picture of jet quenching dictated by color coherence

> We propose a new description of the jet quenching phenomenon observed in nuclear collisions at high energies in which coherent parton branching plays a central role. This picture is based on the appearance of a dynamically generated scale, the jet resolution scale, which controls the transverse resolution power of the medium to simultaneously propagating color probes. Since from the point of view of the medium all partonic jet fragments within this transverse distance act coherently as a single emitter, this scale allows us to rearrange the jet shower into effective emitters. We observe that in the kinematic regime of the LHC, the corresponding characteristic angle is comparable to the typical opening angle of high energy jets such that most of the jet energy is contained within a non-resolvable color coherent inner core. Thus, a sizable fraction of the jets are unresolved, losing energy as a single parton without modifications of their intra-jet structure. This new picture provides a consistent understanding of the present data on reconstructed jet observables and constitute the basis for future developments.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that color coherence between partons in a jet shower, governed by a dynamically generated medium resolution scale, causes many jets at LHC energies to act as a single coherent emitter, losing energy collectively without modification of their internal vacuum-like fragmentation pattern.

## Method

The authors analyze vacuum jet showers from PYTHIA 8.150 at partonic level for p+p collisions at 2.76 TeV, reconstructed with the anti-k\(_t\) algorithm. They embed these events into a 3D hydrodynamical model of Pb+Pb collisions, assigning each jet a production point and trajectory, and compute a local quenching parameter \(\hat q(\xi) = 2K\epsilon(\xi)^{3/4}\) along the path. They then re-cluster the jet constituents using the Cambridge/Aachen algorithm with a resolution scale \(R_{\rm med}\) derived from the medium properties, and count the number of resolved sub-jets above a fractional transverse momentum cut-off \(f_{cut} = 0.1\).

## Key numbers

* For jets with \(R = 0.2\) in the 100-150 GeV \(p_T\) bin, the probability that the jet is unresolved (contains only one sub-jet at scale \(R_{\rm med}\)) is large and decreases with increasing medium resolution power \(K\).
* For jets with \(R = 0.2\) in the 200-300 GeV \(p_T\) bin, the probability of being unresolved is also large and shows similar dependence on \(K\).
* The decoherence parameter for a single antenna in a static medium is \(\Delta_{\text{med}}\simeq 1- {\rm e}^{-\frac{1}{12}\hat qLr_\perp^2}\), where \(\hat q\) is the quenching parameter, \(L\) the medium length, and \(r_\perp\) the transverse separation of the pair.
* The medium resolution scale is defined as \(R_{\rm med}\equiv 2 \left(\int d\xi \,\xi^2\hat q(\xi)\right)^{-1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of jet quenching beyond the single-parton BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier 1997, Zakharov 1997, Wiedemann 2000) by incorporating color coherence effects from antenna radiation studies (Mehtar-Tani 2010-2012, Casalderrey-Solana 2011, Armesto 2011). It provides a consistent explanation for the observed suppression of high-\(p_T\) particles at RHIC (Adcox 2005, Back 2005, Arsene 2005, Adams 2005) and LHC (Aamodt 2011, CMS 2012), and for reconstructed jet observables (Chatrchyan 2012), while enabling future development of a full in-medium parton shower formalism.
