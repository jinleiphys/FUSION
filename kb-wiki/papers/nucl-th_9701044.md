# Shell Model for Warm Rotating Nuclei

> In order to provide a microscopic description of levels and E2 transitions in rapidly rotating nuclei with internal excitation energy up to a few MeV, use is made of a shell model which combines the cranked Nilsson mean-field and the residual surface delta two-body force. The damping of collective rotational motion is investigated in the case of a typical rare-earth nucleus, namely \Yb. It is found that rotational damping sets in at around 0.8 MeV above the yrast line, and the levels which form rotational band structures are thus limited. We predict at a given rotational frequency existence of about 30 rotational bands of various lengths, in overall agreement with the experimental findings. The onset of the rotational damping proceeds quite gradually as a function of the internal excitation energy. The transition region extends up to around 2 MeV above yrast and it is characterized by the presence of scars of discrete rotational bands which extend over few spin values and stand out among the damped transitions, and by a two-component profile in the $E_γ-E_γ$ correlation. The important role played by the high-multipole components of the two-body residual interaction is emphasized.

## Key claim

Rotational damping in rapidly rotating nuclei sets in at around 0.8 MeV above the yrast line, but the transition proceeds quite gradually and extends up to around 2 MeV above yrast, characterized by scars of discrete rotational bands and a two-component profile in the E_gamma-E_gamma correlation.

## Method

The authors constructed a shell model combining the cranked Nilsson mean-field with a residual surface delta two-body interaction, using a diabatic single-particle basis to ensure smooth evolution of rotational bands as a function of rotational frequency. They generated many-particle many-hole (np-nh) configurations for 168Yb at spins I=20-61, diagonalized the shell model Hamiltonian for each spin and parity, and calculated stretched E2 transition strengths between the resulting energy eigenstates to study the fragmentation of collective rotational strength.

## Key numbers

- Rotational damping sets in at around 0.8 MeV above the yrast line
- The transition region extends up to around 2 MeV above yrast
- About 30 rotational bands exist at a given rotational frequency, in agreement with experimental findings
- Level density at 1-2 MeV above yrast is 10^2 to 10^3 MeV^-1 in normally deformed rare-earth nuclei
- Equivalent level spacing of 10 to 1 keV is small compared to typical residual interaction matrix elements of about 10 keV
- The width of the E2 strength distribution is about 100 keV for I ~ 30 and 250 keV for I ~ 50
- The level density parameter from the calculation is about a ~ A/10 MeV^-1
- Deformation parameters used for 168Yb were (eps, eps_4) = (0.255, 0.014)
- Rigid-body moment of inertia for 168Yb is J_rigid = 80.3 MeV^-1
- The branching number n_branch > 2 characterizes the onset of damping

## Context

This work extends earlier statistical approaches to rotational damping by Leander and Lauritzen (1980s) and the schematic residual interaction calculations by Aberg (1990) by using a more realistic surface delta interaction with high-multipole components. The model provides a microscopic description of the transition from discrete rotational bands to damped rotational motion in warm rotating nuclei, enabling detailed comparison with experimental fluctuation analysis methods developed by the FAM collaboration and Leoni (1990s) for 168Yb.
