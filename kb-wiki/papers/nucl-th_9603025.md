# Nuclear Matter Properties in Derivative Coupling Models Beyond Mean - Field Approximation

> The structure of infinite nuclear matter is studied with two of the Zimanyi - Moszkowski (ZM) models in the framework of a relativistic approximation which takes into account Hartree terms and beyond and is compared with the results which come out of the relativistic Hartree - Fock approach in the linear Walecka model. The simple treatment applied to these models can be used in substitution to the more complicated Dirac - Brueckner - Hartree - Fock method to perform future calculations in finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) and ZM3 derivative coupling models, when treated with an approximation that includes both Hartree and exchange (Fock-like) terms, yield improved nuclear matter properties (effective mass, incompressibility) compared to the linear Walecka model, and can serve as a simpler alternative to the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method for future finite-nuclei calculations.

## Method

The authors applied a relativistic approximation to infinite nuclear matter using two Zimanyi-Moszkowski models (ZM and ZM3), incorporating Hartree terms exactly and Fock-like terms approximately by treating the effective mass \(m^*_w(k) = M^*(k)/M\) as momentum-dependent only. They solved the coupled nonlinear integral equations for the self-energy components \(\Sigma^s\), \(\Sigma^0\), and \(\Sigma^v\) via direct iteration, and compared results with the relativistic Hartree-Fock approach in the linear Walecka model.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon saturated at \(-16.15\) MeV at Fermi momentum \(1.14 \, \text{fm}^{-1}\) for the ZM model (with \(g_s^2 = 100\), \(g_v^2 = 60\)).
- Binding energy per nucleon saturated at \(-15.18\) MeV at Fermi momentum \(1.38 \, \text{fm}^{-1}\) for the ZM3 model (with \(g_s^2 = 102\), \(g_v^2 = 105\)).
- Binding energy per nucleon saturated at \(-15.75\) MeV at Fermi momentum \(1.31 \, \text{fm}^{-1}\) for the Walecka model (with \(g_s^2 = 108.8\), \(g_v^2 = 149.4\)).
- The expected incompressibility value from the literature is \(K = 210 \pm 30\) MeV; the ZM and ZM3 models showed improvement over the Walecka model in this quantity.

## Context

This work extends the Zimanyi-Moszkowski derivative coupling models (Zimanyi and Moszkowski, 1990) beyond the mean-field approximation by including exchange terms, and compares results with the relativistic Hartree-Fock treatment of the linear Walecka model (Walecka, 1974; Serot and Walecka, 1986). The approach is intended as a simpler substitute for the more computationally demanding Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method (used by Brockmann and Machleidt, 1990) for future calculations in finite nuclei.
