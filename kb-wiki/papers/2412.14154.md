# Small-$x$ Asymptotics of the Leading-Twist Flavor Singlet Quark TMDs

> In this paper, we investigate the small-$x$ behavior of the flavor-singlet, leading-twist quark Transverse Momentum Dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) using the Light-Cone Operator Treatment. This formalism allows us to express TMD operators at small $x$ in terms of polarized dipole amplitudes, enabling a systematic approach to their small-$x$ evolution. We derive the evolution equations for these TMDs and solve them within the large-$N_c$ approximation under the linearized, Double-Logarithmic Approximation (DLA), where $N_c$ represents the number of quark colors. Expanding on previous work on unpolarized and helicity TMDs, we present the small-$x$ asymptotics for a comprehensive set of TMDs, including the Sivers function, helicity worm-gear, transversity, pretzelosity, Boer-Mulders, and transversity worm-gear distributions. Our results provide a complete picture of the small-$x$ asymptotic behavior for all leading-twist flavor-singlet quark TMDs. We also discuss the implications of our findings for phenomenological applications and outline potential avenues for further research, particularly in understanding non-linear effects and extending beyond the DLA and large-$N_c$ approximations.

## Key claim

The paper derives the small‑\(x\) asymptotic behavior of all six remaining leading‑twist flavor‑singlet quark transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs), the Sivers function, helicity worm‑gear, transversity, pretzelosity, Boer‑Mulders, and transversity worm‑gear, within the large‑\(N_c\) approximation and the linearized Double‑Logarithmic Approximation (DLA), thereby completing the set of asymptotic predictions for all eight leading‑twist flavor‑singlet quark TMDs.

## Method

The authors apply the Light‑Cone Operator Treatment (LCOT) to express the TMD operators at small \(x\) in terms of polarized dipole amplitudes that contain sub‑eikonal and sub‑sub‑eikonal corrections to the usual eikonal dipole amplitudes. They derive the evolution equations for these polarized dipole amplitudes and solve them in the large‑\(N_c\) limit under the linearized DLA, which resums logarithms of \(1/x\) while neglecting non‑linear saturation effects. The analysis is performed for a proton target moving along the \(x^+\) direction in \(A^- = 0\) gauge, using future‑pointing SIDIS staple gauge links and Brodsky‑Lepage spinors.

## Key numbers

* Sivers function and helicity worm‑gear TMD (flavor singlet): \(f_{1T}^{\perp \, \mathrm{S}}(x \ll 1, k_T^2) \sim g_{1T}^{\mathrm{S}}(x \ll 1, k_T^2) \sim (1/x)^{2.9 \sqrt{\alpha_s N_c / (2\pi)}}\)
* Transversity and pretzelosity TMDs (flavor singlet): \(h_{1}^{\mathrm{S}}(x \ll 1, k_T^2) \sim h_{1T}^{\perp \, \mathrm{S}}(x \ll 1, k_T^2) \sim (1/x)^{-1 + 2 \sqrt{\alpha_s N_c / (2\pi)}}\)
* Boer‑Mulders and helicity worm‑gear TMDs (flavor singlet): \(h_1^{\perp \, \mathrm{S}}(x \ll 1, k_T^2) \sim h_{1L}^{\mathrm{S}}(x \ll 1, k_T^2) \sim x\)
* Unpolarized quark TMD (flavor singlet): \(f_1^{\mathrm{S}} \sim x^{- (4 \alpha_s N_c / \pi) \ln 2}\) (from eikonal BFKL evolution, cited from Balitsky (1978))
* Helicity TMD (flavor singlet): \(g_1^{\mathrm{S}} \sim x^{- 3.66 \sqrt{\alpha_s N_c / (2\pi)}}\) (calculated previously by Cougoulic et al. (2022))

## Context

This work extends the small‑\(x\) asymptotic analysis of leading‑twist quark TMDs from the flavor non‑singlet sector, previously completed by Santiago et al. (2023), to the flavor singlet sector, where only the unpolarized and helicity TMDs were known. The results provide a complete set of asymptotic scaling predictions for all eight leading‑twist flavor‑singlet quark TMDs in the large‑\(N_c\), linearized DLA limit, enabling their inclusion in phenomenological studies and global analyses at small \(x\) where saturation effects are not yet dominant. The authors note that extending beyond the DLA and large‑\(N_c\) approximations, as well as including non‑linear saturation effects, remain open avenues for future research.
