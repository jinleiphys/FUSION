# Broken-symmetry $ν=0$ quantum Hall states in bilayer graphene: Landau level mixing and dynamical screening

> For bilayer graphene in a magnetic field at the neutral point, we derive and solve a full set of gap equations including all Landau levels and taking into account the dynamically screened Coulomb interaction. There are two types of the solutions for the filling factor $ν=0$: (i) a spin-polarized type solution, which is the ground state at small values of perpendicular electric field $E_{\perp}$, and (ii) a layer-polarized solution, which is the ground state at large values of $E_{\perp}$. The critical value of $E_{\perp}$ that determines the transition point is a linear function of the magnetic field, i.e., $E_{\perp,{\rm cr}}=E_{\perp}^{\rm off}+a B$, where $E_{\perp}^{\rm off}$ is the offset electric field and $a$ is the slope. The offset electric field and energy gaps substantially increase with the inclusion of dynamical screening compared to the case of static screening. The obtained values for the offset and the energy gaps are comparable with experimental ones. The interaction with dynamical screening can be strong enough for reordering the levels in the quasiparticle spectrum (the $n=2$ Landau level sinks below the $n=0$ and $n=1$ ones).

## Key claim

For bilayer graphene at the neutral point in a magnetic field, the inclusion of dynamical screening and Landau level mixing in the gap equations produces energy gaps and an offset electric field for the spin-polarized to layer-polarized phase transition that are substantially larger than those obtained with static screening, and the resulting gaps are comparable with experimental values from high-mobility samples.

## Method

The authors derived and solved a full set of Schwinger-Dyson gap equations in the Hartree-Fock approximation for bilayer graphene at filling factor ν=0, including all Landau levels up to a cutoff and using a dynamically screened Coulomb interaction within the random phase approximation. The frequency-dependent polarization function was calculated numerically and fitted to an analytical form, then used to replace the static screening approximation employed in prior work. The gap equations were solved self-consistently for the order parameters (chemical potentials and Dirac/Haldane masses) as functions of Landau level index, magnetic field, and perpendicular electric field.

## Key numbers

- The cyclotron energy is ℏωc = ℏ|eB|/mc ≃ 2.15 B [T] meV, with quasiparticle mass m ≃ 0.054 me.
- The Zeeman energy is Z = μBB = 0.027 ℏωc = 0.059 B [T] meV.
- The dimensionless interlayer coupling constant is αIL = e²γ1d/(2ℏ²vF²κ) ≈ 0.354/κ.
- The dimensionless parameter x ≡ 2ℏ⁴/(e⁴m²ℓ²) ≈ 0.003 B [T] determines the momentum scale below which screening effects are negligible.
- The fit parameters for the dynamical polarization function are b1 = 0.608, b2 = 1.572, b3 = 0.357, and b4 = 0.868, valid for 0 ≤ (pℓ)²/2 ≤ 10 and 0 ≤ ω/ωc ≤ 10.
- The energy separation between the lowest Landau levels (n=0,1) and the n=2 level in the free model is √2 ℏωc.
- Experimental gaps in high-mobility suspended bilayer graphene are about 6.5 meV at B ≃ 1 T, with mobility 80,000 to 100,000 cm²/Vs.
- Dynamical screening leads to gaps about three times larger than those obtained with static screening.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of broken-symmetry quantum Hall states in bilayer graphene by Gorbar, Gusynin, Miransky, and Shovkovy (2010, 2011) that used static screening and a lowest-Landau-level approximation. The inclusion of Landau level mixing is motivated by experiments in high-mobility suspended bilayer graphene by Freitag and Velasco (2011) that found gaps exceeding the energy of the third Landau level, invalidating the LLL approximation. The results enable quantitative comparison with experiments on the spin-polarized to layer-polarized phase transition in the B-E⊥ plane and reveal a possible Landau level reordering where the n=2 level sinks below the n=0 and n=1 levels.
