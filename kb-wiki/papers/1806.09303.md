# Deuteron and Antideuteron Production Simulation in Cosmic-ray Interactions

> The study of the cosmic-ray deuteron and antideuteron flux receives an increasing interest in current astrophysics investigations. For both cases an important contribution is expected from the nuclear interactions of primary cosmic rays with intergalactic matter. In this work, deuteron and antideuteron production from 20 to 2.6$\times$10$^{7}$ GeV beam energy in p+p and p+A collisions were simulated using EPOS-LHC and Geant4's FTFP-BERT Monte Carlo models by adding an event-by-event coalescence model afterburner. These estimates depend on a single parameter ($p_0$) obtained from a fit to the data. The $p_0$ for deuterons in this wide energy range was evaluated for the first time. It was found that $p_0$ for antideuterons is not a constant at all energies as previous works suggested and as a consequence the antideuteron production cross section can be at least 20 times smaller in the low collision energy region, than earlier estimations.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the coalescence momentum parameter \(p_0\) for antideuterons is not constant across all collision energies, as earlier works assumed; instead, it grows from the production threshold and saturates at high energies, leading to an antideuteron production cross section that can be at least 20 times smaller in the low collision energy region (20–100 GeV) than previous estimations by Duperray et al.

## Method

The authors simulated deuteron and antideuteron production in p+p and p+A collisions over a beam energy range of 20 to 2.6×10⁷ GeV using the EPOS-LHC and Geant4's FTFP-BERT Monte Carlo event generators. They added an event-by-event coalescence afterburner that identifies proton-neutron or antiproton-antineutron pairs with a momentum difference below a free parameter \(p_0\) and replaces them with a (anti)deuteron. The value of \(p_0\) was determined by fitting simulated (anti)deuteron spectra to experimental data from multiple accelerator experiments, varying \(p_0\) in steps of 5 MeV/\(c\) and selecting the value that minimized \(\chi^2\).

## Key numbers

* The coalescence momentum \(p_0\) for antideuterons at 70 GeV/\(c\) in p+p collisions was found to be 25 MeV/\(c\) for EPOS-LHC and 50 MeV/\(c\) for FTFP-BERT, compared to a constant 79 MeV/\(c\) assumed by Duperray et al.
* The antideuteron production cross section in p+p collisions is at least 20 times smaller than the Duperray et al. parametrization in the low kinetic energy region (20–100 GeV), while at high energies (~1000 GeV) it is 2.4 times larger.
* In p+He collisions, the antideuteron cross section is at least 6 times smaller than the Duperray et al. parametrization in the low-T region.
* The deuteron coalescence momentum \(p_0\) saturates for collision kinetic energies T > 100 GeV, with a fitted saturation value of \(A = 80.6 \pm 2.39\) MeV/\(c\) for EPOS-LHC and \(118.1 \pm 2.42\) MeV/\(c\) for FTFP-BERT.
* The antideuteron coalescence momentum \(p_0\) saturates at high energies with fitted parameters \(A = 89.6 \pm 3.0\) MeV/\(c\) for EPOS-LHC and \(A = 170.2 \pm 10.5\) MeV/\(c\) for FTFP-BERT.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model approach used by Duperray et al. (2003, 2005) and Korsmeier et al. (2017) by incorporating event-by-event correlations from modern Monte Carlo generators, which earlier analytical models neglected. The finding that \(p_0\) is energy-dependent contradicts the constant-\(p_0\) assumption of Duperray et al. and refines the antideuteron background predictions for cosmic-ray experiments such as AMS-02 and GAPS. The results enable more accurate calculations of the secondary cosmic-ray antideuteron flux, which is critical for dark matter searches that rely on a low-energy antideuteron signal window.
