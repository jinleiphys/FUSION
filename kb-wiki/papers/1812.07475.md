# Anyon optics with time-of-flight two-particle interference of double-well-trapped interacting ultracold atoms

> The subject of bianyon interference with ultracold atoms is introduced through theoretical investigations pertaining to the second-order momentum correlation maps of two anyons (built upon spinless and spin-1/2 bosonic, as well as spin-1/2 fermionic ultracold atoms) trapped in a double-well optical trap. The two-particle system is modeled according to the recently proposed protocols for emulating an anyonic Hubbard Hamiltonian in ultracold-atom one-dimensional lattices. Because the second-order momentum correlations are mirrored in the time-of-flight second-order interference patterns in space, our findings provide impetus for time-of-flight experimental protocols for detecting anyonic statistics via interferometry measurements of massive particles that broaden the scope of the biphoton interferometry of quantum optics.

# Anyon optics with time-of-flight two-particle interference of double-well-trapped interacting ultracold atoms

## Key claim

The second-order momentum correlation maps of two anyons (built from spinless and spin-1/2 bosonic, as well as spin-1/2 fermionic ultracold atoms) in a double-well trap exhibit signatures of anyonic statistics that depend on the statistical angle θ, but only when the two-particle eigenstate has a momentum part symmetric under exchange of the two momenta; the eigenstate with antisymmetric momentum part (energy E₄=0) is devoid of anyonic statistics regardless of θ.

## Method

The authors formulate anyonic Hubbard Hamiltonians for three cases, spinless bosonic-based anyons, spin-1/2 bosonic-based anyons, and spin-1/2 fermionic-based anyons, using density-dependent hopping terms derived via fractional Jordan-Wigner transformations. They solve the two-site, two-particle eigenvalue problems exactly, obtaining analytic eigenvectors that depend on the statistical angle θ, and compute the second-order momentum correlation functions G(k₁,k₂,θ) from the momentum-space wavefunctions using displaced Gaussian single-particle orbitals representing the left and right wells.

## Key numbers

- Interwell distance: 2d = 2 μm; width of single-particle orbital: s = 0.2 μm (values similar to experimental investigations of 1D trapped ultracold atoms, Ref. Preiss (2018))
- Eigenenergies for all three cases: E₁ = (J/2)(U − √(U²+16)), E₂ = U, E₃ = (J/2)(U + √(U²+16)), and E₄ = 0 (for spin-1/2 cases only); these are independent of θ
- Strong attractive interaction: U = −20 (in units of J); strong repulsive interaction: U = +20; vanishing interaction: U = 0
- Statistical angles displayed: θ = 0 (pure bosons or fermions), θ = π/2 (intermediate anyons), θ = π (hard bosons or pseudofermions)

## Context

This work extends the anyonic Hubbard model protocols of Keilmann (2011), Greschner (2015), and Eckardt (2016) from extended 1D lattices to an isolated double-well system, and generalizes the spinless bosonic formalism to include spin-1/2 bosonic and fermionic anyons. The results establish analogies with biphoton interferometry in quantum optics (Mandel, 1999; Shih, 2003; Ou, 2007) and with photonic anyon simulations in waveguide lattices (Bromberg, 2010; Longhi, 2012; Lebugle, 2015). The predicted correlation maps provide a blueprint for time-of-flight experimental protocols using deterministically prepared few-atom states in optical tweezers, as demonstrated with fermionic ⁶Li atoms by Jochim (2015, 2018) and Preiss (2018).
