# Analysis of the Low-Energy Theorem for Оіp \to p ПҖ^0

> The derivation of the `classical' low-energy theorem (LET) for Оіp \rightarrow pПҖ^0 is re-examined and compared to chiral perturbation theory. Both results are correct and are not contradictory; they differ because different expansions of the same quantity are involved. Possible modifications of the extended partially conserved axial-vector current relation, one of the starting points in the derivation of the LET, are discussed. An alternate, more transparent form of the LET is presented.

# Analysis of the Low-Energy Theorem for Оіp вҶ’ pПҖвҒ°

## Key claim
The classical low-energy theorem (LET) for neutral pion photoproduction and the chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) result are both correct and not contradictory; they differ because the LET expands in the pion energy Пү, while CHPT expands in the pion mass Ој, and the coefficients of the Пү expansion may be nonanalytic in Ој due to the Li-Pagels mechanism.

## Method
The authors re-derive the LET for Оіp вҶ’ pПҖвҒ° starting from Lorentz invariance, gauge invariance, crossing symmetry, and the extended partially conserved axial-vector current (PCAC) hypothesis. They separate the amplitude into class A diagrams (containing dressed nucleon propagators and half off-shell vertices) and class B diagrams (general non-pole contributions), introduce a fictitious nucleon mass difference Оҙ as a technical tool to isolate pole terms, and expand the amplitude in the pion energy Пү and Оҙ. They then compare this result to the CHPT expansion in the pion mass Ој and examine the role of non-minimal electromagnetic couplings that could modify the extended PCAC relation.

## Context
This paper resolves an apparent contradiction between the classical LET for Оіp вҶ’ pПҖвҒ°, originally derived by Kroll and Ruderman (1954) and later by De Baenst (1970), and the CHPT calculations of Bernard et al. (1991, 1992, 1993) and Ecker (1994). The authors show that both results are valid within their respective expansion schemes and that the LET remains a theorem based on general principles. The work enables future tests of models that satisfy extended PCAC, and it identifies that non-minimal contact couplings in effective theories (such as CHPT at order qвҒҙ) may modify the extended PCAC relation and consequently the low-energy expansion. The authors also note that isospin violation corrections are expected to be large in CHPT but only trivially modify the LET, as shown in Naus (1994).
