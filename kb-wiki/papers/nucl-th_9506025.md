# Analysis of the Low-Energy Theorem for γp \to p π^0

> The derivation of the `classical' low-energy theorem (LET) for γp \rightarrow pπ^0 is re-examined and compared to chiral perturbation theory. Both results are correct and are not contradictory; they differ because different expansions of the same quantity are involved. Possible modifications of the extended partially conserved axial-vector current relation, one of the starting points in the derivation of the LET, are discussed. An alternate, more transparent form of the LET is presented.

# Analysis of the Low-Energy Theorem for γp → pπ⁰

## Key claim
The classical low-energy theorem (LET) for neutral pion photoproduction and the chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) result are both correct and not contradictory; they differ because the LET expands in the pion energy ω, while CHPT expands in the pion mass μ, and the coefficients of the ω expansion may be nonanalytic in μ due to the Li-Pagels mechanism.

## Method
The authors re-derive the LET for γp → pπ⁰ starting from Lorentz invariance, gauge invariance, crossing symmetry, and the extended partially conserved axial-vector current (PCAC) hypothesis. They separate the amplitude into class A diagrams (containing dressed nucleon propagators and half off-shell vertices) and class B diagrams (general non-pole contributions), introduce a fictitious nucleon mass difference δ as a technical tool to isolate pole terms, and expand the amplitude in the pion energy ω and δ. They then compare this result to the CHPT expansion in the pion mass μ and examine the role of non-minimal electromagnetic couplings that could modify the extended PCAC relation.

## Key numbers
- The LET result for the s-wave multipole E₀₊ at ω = μ: −2.3 × 10⁻³ Mπ⁺⁻¹
- The CHPT result at order q³ at threshold: +0.9 × 10⁻³ Mπ⁺⁻¹ (same units)
- The current experimental result: −2.0 ± 0.2 × 10⁻³ Mπ⁺⁻¹
- The LET expression: E₀₊ = −(eg)/(8πM) [ω − (ω²/2)(3 + κp)] + O(ω³), where M is the nucleon mass, e is the proton charge, κp is the proton anomalous magnetic moment, and g is the pion-nucleon coupling constant
- The CHPT expression at order q³: E₀₊ = −(eg)/(8πM) [μ − (μ²/2)(3 + κp + M²/(8fπ²))] + O(μ³)
- The alternative LET form using the exact Goldberger-Treiman relation: E₀₊ = −(egA)/(8πfπ cos θc) [ω − (ω²/2)(3 + κp)] + O(ω³), where gA is the nucleon axial-vector coupling constant, fπ is the pion decay constant, and θc is the Cabibbo angle

## Context
This paper resolves an apparent contradiction between the classical LET for γp → pπ⁰, originally derived by Kroll and Ruderman (1954) and later by De Baenst (1970), and the CHPT calculations of Bernard et al. (1991, 1992, 1993) and Ecker (1994). The authors show that both results are valid within their respective expansion schemes and that the LET remains a theorem based on general principles. The work enables future tests of models that satisfy extended PCAC, and it identifies that non-minimal contact couplings in effective theories (such as CHPT at order q⁴) may modify the extended PCAC relation and consequently the low-energy expansion. The authors also note that isospin violation corrections are expected to be large in CHPT but only trivially modify the LET, as shown in Naus (1994).
