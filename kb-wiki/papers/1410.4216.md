# The Evolution of Soft Collinear Effective Theory

> Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) is an effective field theory of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) for processes where there are energetic, nearly lightlike degrees of freedom interacting with one another via soft radiation. SCET has found many applications in high-energy and nuclear physics, especially in recent years the physics of hadronic jets in $e^+e^-$, lepton-hadron, hadron-hadron, and heavy-ion collisions. SCET can be used to factorize multi-scale cross sections in these processes into single-scale hard, collinear, and soft functions, and to evolve these through the renormalization group to resum large logarithms of ratios of the scales that appear in the QCD perturbative expansion, as well as to study properties of nonperturbative effects. We overview the elementary concepts of SCET and describe how they can be applied in high-energy and nuclear physics.

## Key claim

Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) provides a systematic framework for factorizing multi-scale cross sections in QCD into single-scale hard, collinear, and soft functions, enabling resummation of large logarithms and analysis of nonperturbative effects; the paper reviews these concepts but notes that the talk and proceedings are "by nature limited in time and length" and acknowledges "inevitable omissions of important work."

## Method

The paper constructs the leading-order collinear quark Lagrangian of SCET by starting from the QCD Dirac Lagrangian, factoring out large label momenta from quark fields, projecting out large and small spinor components, integrating out the large-component spinor via its equation of motion, and introducing Wilson lines for collinear gauge invariance. It then demonstrates factorization for the 1-jettiness cross section in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) by matching the QCD electromagnetic current onto SCET operators, computing the matching coefficient to O(αs), and showing that the hadronic tensor factorizes into a hard function, jet function, beam function, and soft function. The paper also describes renormalization group evolution to resum logarithms and discusses nonperturbative effects via an operator product expansion of the soft shape function.

## Key numbers

- The collinear momentum scaling in SCET_I is given by p^- ~ Q, p^+ ~ Qλ², p_⊥ ~ Qλ, where λ ≪ 1 and λ is determined by the typical invariant mass p_c² ~ Qλ².
- The matching coefficient for the electromagnetic current to O(αs) is C^μ(ṗ₁,ṗ₂) = γ_⊥^μ {1 + (αs(μ)C_F)/(4π)[ -ln²(μ²/(-ṗ₁·ṗ₂)) - 3ln(μ²/(-ṗ₁·ṗ₂)) - 8 + π²/6 ]}.
- The jet function J_q(t,μ) to O(αs) is J_q(t,μ) = δ(t) + (αs(μ))/(4π) {(7-π²)δ(t) - (3/μ²)[θ(t)/(t/μ²)]_+ + (4/μ²)[θ(t) ln(t/μ²)/(t/μ²)]_+}.
- The leading nonperturbative effect in the tail region τ ≫ Λ_QCD/Q but τ ≪ 1 is a shift of the distribution by Ω₁^e/Q, where Ω₁^e = c_e Ω₁ with c_e = ∫₀^∞ dη f_e(η) and Ω₁ universal across multiple event shapes in a given process.

## Context

This review extends the line of work developing SCET, originally formulated by Bauer, Fleming, Pirjol, Rothstein, and Stewart (2000-2002), and its applications to factorization and resummation in QCD. It builds on prior results including N³LL resummation for e⁺e⁻ event shapes by Becher and Schwartz (2008), universality of nonperturbative corrections by Lee and Sterman (2006-2007), and the development of SCET_II for transverse-momentum-dependent observables by Bauer, Fleming, and others (2002-2003). The paper enables future applications of SCET to jet physics at the LHC, RHIC, and a future Electron-Ion Collider, as well as extensions to SCET_G for jets in heavy-ion collisions and to rapidity renormalization group methods by Chiu, Jain, Neill, and Rothstein (2011-2012).
