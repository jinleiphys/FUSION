# Four-Body Bound State Formulation in Three-Dimensional Approach (Without Angular Momentum Decomposition)

> The four-body bound state with two-body forces is formulated by the Three-Dimensional approach, which greatly simplifies the numerical calculations of few-body systems without performing the Partial Wave components. We have obtained the Yakubovsky equations directly as three dimensional integral equations.

## Key claim
The paper formulates the four-body bound state problem using a three-dimensional approach that avoids partial wave decomposition entirely, but this initial formulation is restricted to spinless identical bosons (neglecting spin and isospin degrees of freedom) and only presents the integral equations as a starting point for future numerical calculations, not actual numerical results.

## Method
The authors start from the coupled Yakubovsky equations for four identical bosons interacting via pairwise forces, then introduce basis states in terms of vector Jacobi momenta (three momentum vectors per component). They evaluate all matrix elements analytically, including the permutation operators \(P\) and \(\tilde{P}\) and the symmetrized two-body \(t\)-matrix, to reduce the coupled equations to explicit three-dimensional integral equations in the magnitudes of the Jacobi momenta and the angles between them.

## Key numbers
- The four-body bound state energy denominator for the \(|\psi_1\rangle\) component is \(E - \frac{u_1^2}{m} - \frac{3u_2^2}{4m} - \frac{2u_3^2}{3m}\), where \(u_1, u_2, u_3\) are Jacobi momenta and \(m\) is the boson mass.
- The four-body bound state energy denominator for the \(|\psi_2\rangle\) component is \(E - \frac{v_1^2}{m} - \frac{v_2^2}{2m} - \frac{v_3^2}{m}\), where \(v_1, v_2, v_3\) are Jacobi momenta.
- The paper states that modern partial-wave four-body calculations require "1572 or more different spin, isospin and angular momentum combinations" (citing reference [3]).

## Context
This work extends the three-dimensional (3D) approach previously developed by Gloeckle and collaborators for two- and three-body systems (Gloeckle et al., 2004-2005) to the four-body bound state. It contrasts with traditional partial-wave Yakubovsky calculations performed in configuration space (reference [1]) and momentum space (references [2,3]), which require thousands of coupled channels. The formulation enables future numerical calculations of the four-boson ground state without angular momentum decomposition, and can later be extended to include spin and isospin degrees of freedom for nuclear systems.
