# Near-threshold heavy quarkonium photoproduction in a light-front spectator model

> The near-threshold photo- and electroproduction of heavy vector quarkonia off the proton provides direct access to its gluonic structure. In particular, the cross section for $J/Ψ$ photoproduction near threshold is governed by the proton's gluon gravitational form factors (GFFs). In this work, we employ the generalized parton distribution framework together with gluon GFFs calculated in a light-front gluon-spectator model inspired by soft-wall AdS/QCD to predict both the differential and total cross sections for near-threshold $J/Ψ$ and $Υ$ photoproductions. Our results for $J/Ψ$ photoproduction show good agreement with recent experimental data from the $J/Ψ$-007 and GlueX Collaborations at Jefferson Lab, as well as with earlier measurements from SLAC and Cornell.

## Key claim

The cross sections for near-threshold J/psi and Upsilon photoproduction can be predicted using gluon gravitational form factors (GFFs) calculated from a light-front gluon-spectator model inspired by soft-wall AdS/QCD, with the J/psi predictions showing good agreement with experimental data from JLab, SLAC, and Cornell, though the relation between cross sections and gluonic GFFs is strictly justified only in the heavy-quark limit where the skewness parameter xi approaches 1.

## Method

The authors model the proton as a composite system of an active gluon and a spin-1/2 spectator (representing the three valence quarks) using light-front wave functions based on effective solutions from soft-wall AdS/QCD, with parameters fitted to the unpolarized gluon PDF from NNPDF3.0 data at mu_0 = 2 GeV. They compute the gluon GFFs A_g, B_g, D_g, and Cbar_g from the second Mellin moments of gluon GPDs using the hadronic matrix elements of the gluonic energy-momentum tensor, then employ the leading-moment approximation to relate these GFFs to the Compton form factors that enter the differential cross section formula for near-threshold heavy vector meson photoproduction.

## Key numbers

* J/psi photoproduction threshold energy: W_th(J/psi) = M_J/psi + M_N = 4.04 GeV, using M_J/psi = 3.10 GeV and M_N = 0.94 GeV
* Upsilon photoproduction threshold energy: W_th(Upsilon) = M_Upsilon + M_N = 10.4 GeV, using M_Upsilon = 9.46 GeV
* Momentum transfer at threshold for J/psi: -t_th(J/psi) = M_N * M_J/psi^2 / (M_N + M_J/psi) approximately -(1.5 GeV)^2
* Momentum transfer at threshold for Upsilon: -t_th(Upsilon) = M_N * M_Upsilon^2 / (M_N + M_Upsilon) approximately -(2.84 GeV)^2
* Gluon angular momentum: J_g = 0.206 +/- 0.013, compared with lattice QCD values of 0.231(11)(22) (Wang 2021), 0.187(46) (Alexandrou 2020), and 0.255(13) (Hackett 2023)
* Druck term D_g(0) = -8.61^{+0.71}_{-0.75}, compared with lattice QCD values of -10(3) (Shanahan 2018) and -2.57(84) (Hackett 2023)
* Nonrelativistic wave function at origin for J/psi: |psi_NR(0)|^2 = 1.0952/(4pi) (GeV)^3
* Strong coupling constant used: alpha_S = 0.3
* Model parameters at mu_0 = 2 GeV: kappa = 2.62 GeV, a = 3.880 +/- 0.223, b = -0.530 +/- 0.007
* Spectator mass: M_s = 0.985^{+0.044}_{-0.045} GeV

## Context

This work extends the light-front spectator model approach previously applied to gluon GFFs by Sain (2025) and Chakrabarti (2023, 2024, 2025) to predict photoproduction cross sections, complementing earlier extractions of gluon GFFs from J/psi-007 data using holographic models (Mamo 2021) and GPD-based models (Guo 2021). The results enable future comparisons with upcoming measurements at the Electron-Ion Colliders and provide QCD-based constraints on gluon GFFs that have primarily been studied through lattice QCD simulations (Shanahan 2018, Hackett 2023, Pefkou 2021, Alexandrou 2020) and holographic QCD models (Mamo 2019, 2022).
