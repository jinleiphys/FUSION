# Shapes of the Nucleon

> Previously defined spin-dependent quark densities that are matrix elements of specific density operators in proton states of definite spin-polarization generally have an infinite variety of non-spherical shapes. The present application is concerned with both charge and matter densities. We show that the Gross & Agbakpe model nucleon harbors an interesting variety of non-spherical shapes.

## Key claim

The Gross & Agbakpe (GA) model nucleon, which was claimed to be spherical because its wave function depends only on the magnitude of momentum, actually harbors non-spherical shapes when evaluated using spin-dependent density operators; the spherical claim arises from averaging over the spin direction, which buries the effects of orbital angular momentum.

## Method

The authors compute matrix elements of spin-dependent charge and matter density operators in the rest frame of the proton, using the GA covariant nucleon wave function that includes scalar and vector diquark components. They evaluate the expectation value of the density operator as three times the integrated current of a single off-shell quark with the spectator diquark on mass-shell, and compare the resulting shapes to those from the earlier relativistic three-quark constituent model of Miller (2003). The non-spherical nature is quantified by the coefficient of the term proportional to \(\bfn\cdot\bfK\;\bfs\cdot\bfK\) in the general shape expression.

## Key numbers

- For the GA model with quark spin anti-parallel to nucleon spin, the non-sphericity is more pronounced than in the Miller (2003) model, with both showing a toroid shape.
- For the GA model with quark spin parallel to nucleon spin, the ratio of quark probability at the top versus the side is 1.24 for quarks of momentum 1 GeV/c; in the limit \(K\to\infty\) the ratio becomes 2 in both models.
- The most likely value of quark momentum in the Miller (2003) model is \(K \approx 0.25\) GeV/c, corresponding to \(\gamma(K)=0.16\).
- For \(K=1\) GeV/c, the parameter \(\gamma=0.6\) in the Miller (2003) model, and in the GA model this corresponds to \(K^2/m_s^2=3.75\), \(\chi=2.36\), and \(\mathcal{R}=0.53\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Miller (2003), which used a relativistic three-quark constituent model to show that spin-dependent density operators reveal non-spherical nucleon shapes, and directly contradicts the claim of Gross and Agbakpe (2005) that their manifestly covariant nucleon wave function with only \(l=0\) yields a spherical nucleon. The results enable future work on the general nature of the coefficient \(C_{\cal O}(\bfK^2)\) that controls non-sphericity, and on the role of pion cloud effects and color gauge invariance in QCD.
