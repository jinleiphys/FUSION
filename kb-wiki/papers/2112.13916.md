# Pion and Kaon box contribution to $a_μ^{\text{HLbL}}$

> We present an evaluation of the $π^\pm$ and $K^\pm$ box contributions to the hadronic light-by-light piece of the muon's anomalous magnetic moment, $a_μ$. The calculation of the corresponding electromagnetic form factors (EFFs) is performed within a Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) approach to quantum chromodynamics. These form factors are calculated in the so-called rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation, following two different evaluation methods and, subsequently, in a further improved approximation scheme which incorporates meson cloud effects. The results are mutually consistent, indicating that in the domain of relevance for $a_μ$ the obtained EFFs are practically equivalent. Our analysis yields the combined estimates of $a_μ^{π^\pm-box}=-(15.6\pm 0.2)\times 10^{-11}$ and $a_μ^{K^\pm-\text{box}}=-(0.48\pm 0.02)\times 10^{-11}$, in full agreement with results previously obtained within the DSE formalism and other contemporary estimates.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) calculation of the pion and kaon box contributions to the hadronic light-by-light piece of the muon anomalous magnetic moment yields results that are mutually consistent across three different evaluation methods (rainbow-ladder direct, rainbow-ladder perturbation theory integral representation, and beyond rainbow-ladder), indicating that in the domain of relevance for a_mu the obtained electromagnetic form factors are practically equivalent, with the combined estimates agreeing with previous DSE results and other contemporary calculations.

## Method

The electromagnetic form factors for charged pions and kaons were computed within the Dyson-Schwinger equations approach to quantum chromodynamics using the rainbow-ladder truncation with the Qin-Chang effective interaction, employing both a direct computation of the quark-photon vertex via the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation and a perturbation theory integral representation method. A further improved approximation scheme (beyond rainbow-ladder) incorporated meson cloud effects through pionic degrees of freedom in the quark propagator and Bethe-Salpeter kernel. The resulting form factors were then used as input to the master formula derived by Colangelo et al. (2017) for the box contributions to a_mu.

## Key numbers

* Pion box contribution: a_mu^(pi±-box) = -(15.6 ± 0.2) × 10^(-11), combined estimate from three methods
* Kaon box contribution: a_mu^(K±-box) = -(0.48 ± 0.02) × 10^(-11), combined estimate from three methods
* Pion charge radius: r_pi = 0.677(4) fm (RL direct), 0.676(2) fm (BRL), 0.676(5) fm (RL-PTIR)
* Kaon charge radius: r_K = 0.597(3) fm (RL direct), 0.593(2) fm (BRL), 0.596(5) fm (RL-PTIR)
* Pion mass: 0.139(3) GeV (RL), 0.139(4) GeV (BRL); pion decay constant: 0.131(2) GeV (both)
* Kaon mass: 0.493(4) GeV (RL), 0.493(5) GeV (BRL); kaon decay constant: 0.157(2) GeV (RL), 0.159(2) GeV (BRL)

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based calculation of hadronic light-by-light contributions to the muon g-2, following the earlier DSE evaluation by Eichmann et al. (2019) and the dispersive evaluation by Colangelo et al. (2017) which achieved uncertainties of 0.2 × 10^(-11) for the pion box. The results confirm the robustness of the DSE formalism for these observables and enable further calculations of related contributions, such as those from axial mesons discussed by Roig (2019) and potentially from excited states.
