# Effects of cluster correlations on fragment emission in 12C + 12C at 50 MeV/nucleon

> The effects of cluster correlations have been studied in the $^{12}$C + $^{12}$C reaction at 50 MeV/nucleon, using three AMD models, the AMD (AMD/D) without any additional cluster correlations, AMD/D-COALS with nucleon correlations based on a coalescence prescription for light cluster formations with $A\leq4$ and AMD-Cluster with an extended cluster correlation in two-nucleon collision processes and a special treatment for intermediate fragment formation with A $\le 9$. The angular distributions and energy spectra of fragments have been simulated and compared with the available experimental data. It is found that the cluster correlations take a crucial role to describe the productions of light charged particles (LCPs) and intermediate mass fragments (IMFs), and the AMD-cluster studied here provides a consistent overall reproduction of the experimental data. It is also shown that the significant effects of the secondary decay processes are involved for the fragment production besides the dynamical productions in the AMD stage. Detailed LCP and IMF production mechanisms involved in the intermediate energy heavy ion collisions are discussed.

# Effects of cluster correlations on fragment emission in 12C + 12C at 50 MeV/nucleon

## Key claim
The AMD-Cluster model, which incorporates extended cluster correlations in two-nucleon collision processes and a special treatment for intermediate fragment formation with A ≤ 9, provides a consistent overall reproduction of experimental angular distributions and energy spectra for light charged particles (LCPs) and intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) in the 12C + 12C reaction at 50 MeV/nucleon, whereas AMD/D without additional cluster correlations and AMD/D-COALS with coalescence only for A ≤ 4 fail to reproduce the IMF yields at larger angles. However, all three models significantly overpredict 4He and 12C yields at forward angles, indicating that the excitation energy of projectile-like fragments may be too high in the primary AMD stage.

## Method
Three versions of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model were used to simulate the 12C + 12C reaction at 50 MeV/nucleon: AMD/D without additional cluster correlations, AMD/D-COALS with a stochastic coalescence prescription for light cluster formation with A ≤ 4 (p+n→d, p+n+n→t, p+p+n→3He, p+p+n+n→α), and AMD-Cluster with extended cluster correlations in two-nucleon collision processes allowing cluster formation up to A=4 for each scattered nucleon and a binding procedure for intermediate fragments with A between 6 and 9. The Gogny interaction was used for AMD/D and AMD/D-COALS, while the SLy4 interaction was used for AMD-Cluster. Secondary decay processes were treated using the GEMINI afterburner, and results were compared to experimental data from Divay (2017) for impact parameters b=0-8 fm.

## Key numbers
- Reaction energy: 50 MeV/nucleon for 12C + 12C
- Impact parameter range: b = 0-8 fm
- Width parameter for Gaussian wave packets: ν = 0.16 fm−2
- Cluster binding condition for AMD-Cluster: relative kinetic energy threshold of 12 MeV (½ μV²rel < 12 MeV) and relative distance range 1 < Rrel < 5 fm
- Mass range for linked system binding in AMD-Cluster: 6 ≤ A ≤ 9
- Phase-space density threshold for coalescence in AMD/D-COALS: f < 1.5
- AMD/D overpredicts proton yields at all angles by a significant factor compared to experimental data
- AMD/D-COALS improves proton yields but still slightly overpredicts at all angles
- AMD-Cluster reproduces angular distributions of all LCPs very well, with noticeable underprediction for protons at θ < 20° and significant overprediction for 3He at θ > 25° and 4He at θ < 10°
- All three models overpredict experimental 4He yields by a factor of more than 2 at forward angles
- For 12C, all three calculations significantly overpredict yields at forward angles
- AMD/D and AMD/D-COALS show PLF components overpredicted by a factor of 1.5 to 3 for light IMFs

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Tian (2017) and Tian (2018) on cluster correlation effects in heavy-ion collisions, comparing three different implementations of cluster correlations within the AMD framework originally developed by Ono (1996, 1999). The experimental data for the 12C + 12C reaction at 50 MeV/nucleon were taken from Divay (2017), motivated by carbon therapy beam applications. The study demonstrates that AMD-Cluster with extended cluster correlations significantly improves the description of IMF production compared to AMD/D-COALS, which only treats coalescence for A ≤ 4, and enables better understanding of the role of secondary decay processes in fragment production. The results also reveal that the excitation energy of projectile-like fragments, particularly 12C, is systematically overestimated in all models, pointing to needed improvements in the treatment of peripheral collisions.
