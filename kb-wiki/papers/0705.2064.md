# EMC effect in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering process

> By considering the $x$-dependence of $π^+$, $π^-$, $K^+$, $K^-$, $Λ$, $\barΛ$, $p$, $\bar{p}$ hadron productions in charged lepton semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off nuclear target (using Fe as an example) and deuteron D target, % at $Q^2=5$ GeV$^2$, we find that $(\barΛ^A/Λ^A)/(\barΛ^D/Λ^D)$ and $({\bar{p}}^A/{p}^A)/({\bar{p}}^A/p^A)$ are ideal to figure out the nuclear sea content, which is predicted to be different by different models accounting for the nuclear EMC effect.

## Key claim

The ratios \((\bar{\Lambda}^A/\Lambda^A)/(\bar{\Lambda}^D/\Lambda^D)\) and \(({\bar{p}}^A/{p}^A)/({\bar{p}}^A/p^A)\) in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering off iron (Fe) versus deuterium (D) can distinguish between different models of the nuclear EMC effect, but only as qualitative predictions because the fixed-order calculation used is not appropriate at high \(z\) where large logarithms need resummation.

## Method

The authors calculated the \(x\)-dependence of hadron production ratios for \(\pi^+\), \(\pi^-\), \(K^+\), \(K^-\), \(\Lambda\), \(\bar{\Lambda}\), \(p\), and \(\bar{p}\) in charged lepton semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering off an iron target (Fe) and a deuteron target (D) at \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\). They used three models of the nuclear EMC effect, the quark cluster model, the pion excess model, and the rescaling model, to generate nuclear parton distributions, and adopted an effective parton energy loss model to modify fragmentation functions in the nuclear medium. The analysis focused on the large-\(z\) region (e.g., \(0.7 < z < 0.9\)) to suppress unfavored fragmentation contributions from valence quarks.

## Key numbers

- The quark cluster model uses a six-quark cluster probability \(f = 0.30\) for Fe, with coefficients \(N_v = 1.3875\) and \(N_{sea} = 0.2521\) for valence and sea quark distributions in the six-quark cluster.
- The rescaling model uses \(\xi = 1.83\) at \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\) for Fe (\(A=56\)).
- The pion excess model uses \(\langle n_{\pi}\rangle = 0.22\), with parameters \(a=1\) and \(b=3\) for the pion probability distribution.
- The parton energy loss model uses transport coefficient \(\hat{q} = 0.72\) GeV/fm\(^2\) and nuclear radius \(L = 3R/4\), with log-normal parameters \(\sigma = 0.73\) and \(\mu = -1.5\).
- The ratio \((\bar{\Lambda}^A/\Lambda^A)/(\bar{\Lambda}^D/\Lambda^D)\) is shown to be nearly independent of \(\hat{q}\) when comparing \(\hat{q} = 0.72\) GeV/fm\(^2\) and \(\hat{q} = 0.25\) GeV/fm\(^2\).
- The ratio \(({\bar{p}}^A/{p}^A)/({\bar{p}}^A/p^A)\) also shows similar \(\hat{q}\)-independence.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the nuclear EMC effect, where inclusive structure function ratios \(F_2^A/F_2^D\) from the European Muon Collaboration (1982) and SLAC E139 collaboration could not distinguish between models with different sea quark predictions. The Fermilab E772 Drell-Yan experiment (DMA) favored no sea enhancement but suffered from uncertainties in quark energy loss. The authors propose semi-inclusive hadron production as an alternative, noting that HERMES data on multiplicity ratios are not ideal for probing nuclear sea quarks. The results enable future experiments (e.g., at higher energy) to test whether nuclear sea quarks are enhanced (cluster model), reduced (rescaling model), or selectively enhanced for \(\bar{u},\bar{d}\) only (pion excess model).
