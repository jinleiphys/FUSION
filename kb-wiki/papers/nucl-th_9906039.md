# SU(3) quasi-dynamical symmetry as an organizational mechanism for generating nuclear rotational motions

> The phenomenological symplectic model with a Davidson potential is used to construct rotational states for a rare-earth nucleus with microscopic wave functions. The energy levels and E2 transitions obatined are in remarkably close agreement (to within a few percent) with those of the rotor model with vibrational shape fluctuations that are adiabatically decoupled from the rotational degrees of freedom. An analysis of the states in terms of their SU(3) content shows that SU(3) is a very poor dynamical symmetry but an excellent quasi-dynamical symmetry for the model. It is argued that such quasi-dynamical symmetry can be expected for any Hamiltonian that reproduces the observed low-energy properties of a well-deformed nucleus, whenever the latter are well-described by the nuclear rotor model.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that SU(3) is a very poor dynamical symmetry but an excellent quasi-dynamical symmetry for a symplectic model Hamiltonian with a Davidson potential, and argues that such quasi-dynamical symmetry can be expected for any Hamiltonian that reproduces the observed low-energy properties of a well-deformed nucleus described by the nuclear rotor model.

## Method
The authors constructed rotational states for a rare-earth nucleus (166Er) using the phenomenological symplectic model with a Davidson potential, diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a basis that reduces the dynamical subalgebra chain Sp(3,R) ⊃ SU(3) ⊃ SO(3). The calculations were carried out in a space restricted to states belonging to shells below 12ħω, using vector coherent state methods to calculate matrix elements, with the deformation parameter chosen to give a minimum at β = 0.35 and the strength χ of the potential varied across four values.

## Key numbers
- The deformation parameter ε = 1.01 × 10¹¹ gives the minimum of the collective potential at β_in = 0.35, close to the value inferred from experimental B(E2: 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) transition rate
- The Sp(3,R) irrep used has lowest weight (327½, 249½, 249½), equivalent to 826½(78,0) in U(1) × SU(3) notation
- For χ = 3.33 × 10⁻⁴ MeV, the B(E2: 2 → 0) transition strength is 207 W.u. (compared to experimental 214 ± 10 W.u.)
- The B(E2: 4 → 2) transition strength is 295 W.u. (experimental: 311 ± 20 W.u.)
- Moments of inertia for L=2 range from 207.62 MeV⁻¹ (χ = 1.67 × 10⁻⁵ MeV) to 15.72 MeV⁻¹ (χ = 8.33 × 10⁻⁴ MeV)
- The kinetic energy contribution to excitation energy ranges from -31.95% (χ = 1.67 × 10⁻⁵ MeV, L=2) to 9.46% (χ = 8.33 × 10⁻⁴ MeV, L=2)
- The average deformation β₀ of the L=0 ground state ranges from 0.247 (χ = 1.67 × 10⁻⁵ MeV) to 0.351 (χ = 8.33 × 10⁻⁴ MeV)

## Context
This work extends the symplectic model framework developed by Rosensteel and Rowe (1977, 1980) and builds on earlier studies showing coherent mixing of SU(3) irreps by spin-orbit interactions (Rochford 1988) and pairing forces (Bahri, Rowe, and Wijesundera 1998). The results demonstrate that both SU(3) and the rigid-rotor algebra [R⁵]SO(3) are excellent quasi-dynamical symmetries for the Hamiltonian, which enables future inclusion of symmetry-breaking interactions like spin-orbit and pairing while preserving rotational predictions. The concept of quasi-dynamical symmetry provides a group-theoretical expression of the Born-Oppenheimer approximation and standard adiabatic decoupling methods in nuclear rotor models.
