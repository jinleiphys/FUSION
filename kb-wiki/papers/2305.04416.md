# Threshold resummation for computing large-$x$ parton distribution through large-momentum effective theory

> Parton distribution functions (PDFs) at large $x$ are poorly constrained by high-energy experimental data, but extremely important for probing physics beyond standard model at colliders. We study the calculation of PDFs at large-$x$ through large-momentum $P^z$ expansion of the lattice quasi PDFs. Similar to deep-inelastic scattering, there are two distinct perturbative scales in the threshold limit where the matching coefficient can be factorized into a space-like jet function at scale $P^z|1-y|$ and a pair of heavy-light Sudakov form factors at scale $P^z$. The matching formula allows us to derive a full renormalization group resummation of large threshold logarithms, and the result is consistent with the known calculation to the next-to-next to leading order (NNLO). This paves the way for direct large-$x$ PDFs calculations in lattice QCD. As by-products, we find that the space-like jet function is related to a time-like version calculated previously through analytic continuation, and the heavy-light Sudakov form factor, calculated here to NNLO, is a universal object appearing as well in the large momentum expansion of quasi transverse-momentum-dependent PDFs and quasi wave-function amplitudes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the matching coefficient between quasi-PDFs and PDFs in large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) can be factorized in the threshold limit into a space-like jet function and a pair of heavy-light Sudakov form factors, enabling full renormalization group resummation of large threshold logarithms; the result is consistent with known next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) calculations, but the formalism itself is derived to all orders in perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors factorize the perturbative matching kernel for quark non-singlet PDFs in the threshold limit \(\xi \rightarrow 1\) into a real hard kernel \(H\) (the absolute value of a pair of heavy-light Sudakov form factors at scale \(4x^2 P_z^2\)) and a space-like jet function \(J_f\) (resumming soft gluon exchanges at the semi-hard scale \(|1-\xi|xP^z\)). They compute the jet function in coordinate space using dimensional regularization, relate it to a time-like version through analytic continuation, and derive renormalization group equations for both components to resum large logarithms. The formalism is validated by comparing the predicted threshold limit at NNLO against the exact two-loop matching kernel from prior work.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop space-like jet function in coordinate space (Eq. (jetoneloop)): \(\tilde J(\mu z) = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi} \left( \frac{1}{2} l_z^2 + l_z + \frac{\pi^2}{12} + 2 \right)\), where \(l_z = \ln(e^{2\gamma_E} \mu^2 z^2 / 4)\).
* The one-loop hard kernel (Eq. (hardoneloop)): \(H(L_z) = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi} \left( -2 + \frac{\pi^2}{12} - \frac{L_z^2}{2} + L_z \right)\), with \(L_z = \ln(4p_z^2 / \mu^2)\).
* The two-loop anomalous dimension \(\tilde \gamma_J\) for the jet function (Eq. (eq:resulttildegammaJ)): \(\tilde \gamma_J(\alpha_s) = -\frac{\alpha_s C_F}{\pi} + \left( \frac{\alpha_s}{4\pi} \right)^2 \left[ C_F C_A \left( -\frac{1396}{27} + \frac{23\pi^2}{9} + 20\zeta_3 \right) + C_F n_f \left( \frac{232}{27} - \frac{2\pi^2}{9} \right) \right]\).
* The one-loop imaginary part of the jet function (Eq. (eq:JetoneloopIM)): \(\text{Im} J_f(z\mu) = \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi} (L_z - 1) i\pi \,\text{sign}(z)\).

## Context

This work extends the threshold resummation techniques developed for deep-inelastic scattering (Sterman 1986, Catani 1989, Becher 2006) to the LaMET framework for lattice QCD calculations of PDFs. It builds on prior LaMET developments (Ji 2013, Ji 2014) and the known NNLO matching kernel for quasi-PDFs (Li 2020, Chen 2020). The factorization formula introduces a universal heavy-light Sudakov form factor that also appears in quasi-TMDPDF factorization (Ebert 2019, Ji 2019, Vladimirov 2020) and quasi-LFWF amplitudes (Ji 2021), enabling direct large-\(x\) PDF calculations from lattice QCD without fitting functional forms.
