# Quasi-distribution amplitudes for pion and kaon via the nonlocal chiral-quark model

> We investigate the pseudoscalar (PS) meson ($π$ and $K$) quasi-distribution amplitude (QDA), which is supposed to be an asymptotic analog to the meson distribution amplitude (DA) $ϕ_{π,K}(x)$ in the limit of the large longitudinal PS-meson momentum, i.e. $p_3\to\infty$, in the nonperturbative region. For this purpose, we employ the nonlocal chiral-quark model (NLChQM) in the light-front formalism with a minimal Fock-state for the mesons $\sim q\bar{q}$ at the low-energy scale parameter of the model $Λ\sim1$ GeV. As a trial, we extract the transverse-momentum distribution amplitude (TMDA) from the light-front wave function within the model, and convert it to QDA with help of the virtuality-distribution amplitude. By doing that, we derive an analytical expression for the nonperturbative QDA with the current-quark mass correction up to $\mathcal{O}(Δm_q)$. Numerically, we confirm that the obtained TMDA reproduces the experimental data for the photon-pion transition form factor $F_{γγ^*π^0}(Q^2)$ at the low-$Q^2$ qualitatively well. We also observe that the obtained QDA approaches to DA as $p_3$ increases, showing the symmetric and asymmetric curves with respect to $x$ for the pion and kaon, respectively, due to the current-quark mass difference $m_{u,d}\ll m_s$. Assigning $ξ\equiv2x-1$, the moments $\langleξ^n\rangle_{π,K}$ are computed, using the pion and kaon QDAs, and there appear only a few percent deviations in the moments for $p_3\gtrsim30Λ$ in comparison to the values calculated directly from DAs. It turns out that the higher moments are more sensitive to the change of $p_3$, whereas the lower ones depend less on it.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nonperturbative quasi-distribution amplitude (QDA) for pion and kaon, derived from the nonlocal chiral-quark model (NLChQM), approaches the standard distribution amplitude (DA) as the longitudinal momentum \(p_3\) increases, with only a few percent deviation in moments for \(p_3 \gtrsim 30\Lambda\) (approximately 30 GeV), but this holds specifically at the low-energy model scale \(\Lambda \sim 1\) GeV and under the assumption that the transverse-momentum distribution amplitude (TMDA) equals the light-front wave function (LFWF) in the nonperturbative region.

## Method

The author employed the nonlocal chiral-quark model (NLChQM) in the light-front formalism with a minimal \(q\bar{q}\) Fock state for the pseudoscalar mesons. The transverse-momentum distribution amplitude (TMDA) was extracted from the light-front wave function (LFWF) computed within the model, and then converted to the quasi-distribution amplitude (QDA) using the virtuality-distribution amplitude (VDA) formalism of Radyushkin (2017). An analytical expression for the nonperturbative QDA was derived including current-quark mass corrections up to \(\mathcal{O}(\Delta m_q)\), and the moments \(\langle \xi^n \rangle_{\pi,K}\) were computed for various \(p_3\) values.

## Key numbers

- Model scale parameter \(\Lambda\) determined as 1.02 GeV for the pion in the chiral limit, 1.01 GeV for the pion with finite current-quark masses, and 1.05 GeV for the kaon.
- Constituent-quark mass \(M_0 = 350\) MeV; current-quark masses \(m_{u,d} = 5\) MeV and \(m_s = 135\) MeV.
- Weak-decay constants: \(F_\pi = 93\) MeV and \(F_K = 113\) MeV (empirical inputs).
- For \(p_3 \gtrsim 30\Lambda\) (approximately 30 GeV), the moments \(\langle \xi^n \rangle_{\pi,K}\) from QDA show only a few percent deviation compared to those from DA.
- The pion-photon transition form factor at zero virtuality: \(\lim_{Q^2 \to 0} F^{\text{NLChQM}}_{\gamma\gamma^*\pi^0}(Q^2) = 0.191\) GeV\(^{-1}\), giving a ratio of 0.702 relative to the Adler-Bell-Jackiw anomaly value of \(0.272\) GeV\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the quasi-distribution amplitude approach proposed by Ji (2013) and the TMDA/VDA formalism of Radyushkin (2017) to a nonperturbative model, the nonlocal chiral-quark model based on the instanton vacuum of Diakonov (2002). It provides a model calculation of QDA for pion and kaon at a low renormalization scale \(\Lambda \sim 1\) GeV, complementing lattice QCD studies (e.g., Zhang 2017, Carlson 2017) and earlier model parameterizations using Gaussian and Bessel functions by Radyushkin (2017). The results enable future extraction of meson distribution amplitudes from lattice QCD simulations of QDA by matching at finite \(p_3\), and suggest that non-flat DAs are favored in the nonperturbative region based on comparison with the photon-pion transition form factor data from CELLO (1990), CLEO (1997), BaBar (2009), and Belle (2012).
