# Lorentz Integral Transform for Inclusive and Exclusive Cross Sections with the Lanczos Method

> The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method is reformulated via the Lanczos algorithm both for inclusive and exclusive reactions. The new technique is tested for the total photoabsorption cross section of 3H and 4He. Due to the rapid convergence of the algorithm one has a decrease in cpu time by two orders of magnitude, but at the same time an excellent agreement with the results of a conventional LIT calculation. The present work opens up the possibility of ab initio calculations for inclusive and exclusive processes for A greater equal 6 with inclusion of complete final state interactions.

# Lorentz Integral Transform for Inclusive and Exclusive Cross Sections with the Lanczos Method

## Key claim

The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method can be reformulated using the Lanczos algorithm to avoid explicit inversion of the Hamiltonian matrix, reducing CPU time by two orders of magnitude while maintaining agreement with conventional LIT calculations to within 0.1% relative difference, thereby enabling ab initio calculations for inclusive and exclusive reactions in systems with A ≥ 6.

## Method

The authors reformulate the LIT method for both inclusive and exclusive reactions by expressing the Lorentz transform as a continued fraction of Lanczos coefficients obtained from a tridiagonalization of the Hamiltonian matrix. For inclusive processes, the LIT is computed directly from the continued fraction without solving the differential equation for the Lorentz states; for exclusive processes, the transition matrix elements are obtained via a recursive calculation of continued fractions. The method is tested on total photoabsorption cross sections of ³H and ⁴He using the dipole approximation, with ground and Lorentz states expanded on a hyperspherical harmonics basis using the EIHH approach and the semirealistic MTI-III potential.

## Key numbers

- CPU time reduction of two orders of magnitude compared to conventional LIT calculation
- Relative difference between Lanczos-based and conventional LIT results for ³H photodisintegration (T=1/2 isospin channel) is far below 0.1%
- For ⁴He photodisintegration with 1372 basis states, relative error in the LIT is smaller than 0.5% with 150 Lanczos coefficients, and 200-300 steps are completely sufficient
- Overlap between the 0-th and 300-th Lanczos vector is about 10⁻⁴, indicating good orthogonality

## Context

This work extends the LIT method developed by Efros, Leidemann, and Orlandini (1994) and applied to nuclei up to A=4, which required explicit inversion of large Hamiltonian matrices that became prohibitive for larger systems. The Lanczos approach was inspired by condensed matter calculations by Dagotto, Hallberg, and Kuehner. The method has already enabled the first microscopic calculation of the total photoabsorption cross section of A=6 nuclei with complete final state interactions, as reported by Bacca, Marchisio, Barnea, Leidemann, and Orlandini. This opens the possibility for ab initio calculations of reactions in systems with six or more nucleons using realistic interactions.
