# Particle-Set Identification method to study multiplicity fluctuations

> In this paper a new method of experimental data analysis, the Particle-Set Identification method, is presented. The method allows to reconstruct moments of multiplicity distribution of identified particles. The difficulty the method copes with is due to incomplete particle identification -- a particle mass is frequently determined with a resolution which does not allow for a unique determination of the particle type. Within this method the moments of order $k$ are calculated from mean multiplicities of $k$-particle sets of a given type. The Particle-Set Identification method remains valid even in the case of correlations between mass measurements for different particles. This distinguishes it from the Identity method introduced by us previously to solve the problem of incomplete particle identification in studies of particle fluctuations.

# Particle-Set Identification method to study multiplicity fluctuations

## Key claim

The Particle-Set (PSET) Identification method allows reconstruction of moments of multiplicity distributions of identified particles even when mass measurements for different particles are correlated, which distinguishes it from the earlier Identity method that assumed independent mass measurements. The method remains valid under correlations between mass measurements for different particles, a condition the abstract notes but does not quantify.

## Method

The method calculates moments of order k from mean multiplicities of k-particle sets of a given type, extracted by fitting multi-dimensional distributions of a particle "mass" variable (e.g., specific energy loss, time-of-flight). For second moments, the two-dimensional mass distribution of particle pairs is fitted using the maximum likelihood method to determine the fractions of pion-pion, kaon-kaon, and pion-kaon pairs, from which mean multiplicities of identified pairs and subsequently second moments are derived. The procedure extends to k-particle sets and moments of order k for an arbitrary number of particle types.

## Key numbers

- Mean multiplicities used in simulation: λ_π = 6 and λ_K = 4 for pions and kaons respectively, generated from independent Poisson distributions
- Correlation coefficients tested: R = 0.1 and R = 0.5 between x₁ and x₂ for same-particle mass measurements
- For R = 0.1, the PSET method recovers second moments of pions consistent with the theoretical baseline, while the Identity method shows deviation
- For R = 0.5, the PSET method remains consistent with the theoretical baseline, while the Identity method deviates further from the theoretical baseline

## Context

This work extends the Identity method previously introduced by Gazdzicki, Gorenstein, and collaborators (2011) for solving incomplete particle identification in studies of multiplicity fluctuations. The PSET method overcomes the limitation of the Identity method, which assumes independent mass measurements for different particles, a condition that can be violated due to detector properties (e.g., finite two-track resolution, time-dependent response functions) and analysis procedures (e.g., neglecting momentum dependence within finite bins). The method enables experimental searches for the critical point of strongly interacting matter and tests of statistical models beyond mean multiplicities, and is currently usable by experimental collaborations such as NA61/SHINE and ALICE.
