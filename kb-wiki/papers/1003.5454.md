# Direct photon emission in Heavy Ion Collisions from Microscopic Transport Theory and Fluid Dynamics

> Direct photon emission in heavy-ion collisions is calculated within a relativistic micro+macro hybrid model and compared to the microscopic transport model UrQMD. In the hybrid approach, the high-density part of the collision is calculated by an ideal 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamic calculation, while the early (pre-equilibrium-) and late (rescattering-) phase are calculated with the transport model. Different scenarios of the transition from the macroscopic description to the transport model description and their effects are studied. The calculations are compared to measurements by the WA98-collaboration and predictions for the future CBM-experiment are made.

# Direct photon emission in Heavy Ion Collisions from Microscopic Transport Theory and Fluid Dynamics

## Key claim
Direct photon spectra from heavy-ion collisions calculated with a hybrid model (transport + hydrodynamics) are largely insensitive to the specific transition scenario (isochronous vs. gradual) between the hydrodynamic and cascade phases, but the relative contributions of the intermediate and late stages to the total photon yield differ significantly between the two scenarios.

## Method
The authors used the UrQMD v2.3 microscopic transport model for the pre-equilibrium and final-state rescattering phases, coupled to an ideal 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamic calculation for the high-density intermediate stage. Photon emission was calculated perturbatively in both phases: using cross-sections from Kapusta et al. (1991) for the transport part and parametrizations by Turbide, Rapp and Gale (2003) and Arnold et al. for the hydrodynamic part. Three different equations of state were employed: a Hadron Gas EoS, a MIT-Bag Model EoS with a first-order phase transition, and a chiral EoS with a critical end point.

## Key numbers
- The transition from hydrodynamics to cascade occurs when the local rest frame energy density drops below a threshold of approximately 5 times the nuclear ground state energy density (ε_crit ≈ 5 ε_0).
- Calculations for minimum bias U+U collisions were performed at E_lab = 45 AGeV, corresponding to planned FAIR-CBM experiment energies.
- The hadron gas EoS and hybrid calculations yield consistent results at E_lab = 158 AGeV, as previously found by Baeuchle et al. (2009).
- The Bag Model EoS calculations show a greatly enhanced photon yield relative to the hadronic baseline, while the chiral EoS shows significant enhancement at large transverse momentum (p⊥).

## Context
This work extends the hybrid model approach of Petersen et al. (2008) by applying it to direct photon emission, complementing earlier theoretical work on elementary photon production cross-sections by Kapusta et al. (1991) and Xiong et al. (1992). The calculations are compared to measurements by the WA98 collaboration at CERN-SPS and provide predictions for the future CBM experiment at FAIR. The results suggest that experiments at FAIR will be well-suited to distinguish between different equations of state, particularly at low beam energies where pQCD contributions are negligible.
