# Enhancement of deltaful two-pion exchange nuclear forces

> The role of the delta isobar degrees of freedom in nucleon-nucleon scattering is revisited. We attempt to understand why the dimensionally regularized two-pion exchanges with the explicit delta isobar is much stronger than the ones with spectral function regularization. When the cutoff value of spectral function regularization is varied, the isoscalar central component exhibits a rather large cutoff variation. This reveals a surprisingly large numerical factor of the deltaful two-pion exchange potentials. The power counting is adjusted accordingly and we discuss the results and how to improve upon this finding.

## Key claim

The isoscalar central component of deltaful two-pion-exchange (TPE) potentials is numerically enhanced relative to naive dimensional analysis (NDA) expectations, being suppressed by only one order relative to one-pion-exchange (OPE) rather than two orders, which explains why dimensional regularization (DR) produces overly attractive potentials compared to spectral function regularization (SFR) with a finite cutoff.

## Method

The authors analytically expand the SFR expressions for deltaful TPE potentials in powers of 1/\(\tilde{\Lambda}\) to identify the residual cutoff dependence, which corresponds to contact interactions. They isolate the \(q^4\) counterterm in the isoscalar central force and determine its numerical coefficient. They then adjust the power counting for peripheral partial waves (D and F waves) by promoting the central part of deltaful TPE to N\(^2\)LO and compute NN phase shifts perturbatively up to N\(^3\)LO, comparing results with empirical partial-wave analyses and deltaless TPE calculations.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon-delta mass splitting \(\Delta = 293.7\) MeV
- Pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, nucleon axial-vector coupling \(g_A = 1.29\), pion mass \(M_\pi = 138\) MeV, \(N\Delta\) axial-coupling \(h_A = 1.40\), nucleon mass \(M_N = 939\) MeV
- The dominant \(q^4\) counterterm coefficient: \(D_{C-\Delta} = \frac{g^2_A h^2_A}{ 24\pi f^4_{\pi} \Delta \tilde{\Lambda}} \left(1 + \frac{4}{9} \frac{h_A^2}{g_A^2} \right)\), evaluated at \(\tilde{\Lambda} = 700\) and \(800\) MeV
- Cutoff for NN intermediate states fixed at \(\Lambda = 800\) MeV throughout
- The isoscalar central force \(V_{C-\Delta}^{\text{TPE}}\) is promoted to N\(^2\)LO (one order higher than NDA), while the \(q^4\) counterterm is assigned to N\(^3\)LO

## Context

This work extends the analysis of deltaful chiral nuclear forces originally derived by Kaiser (1998) using DR and by Krebs, Epelbaum, and Meissner (2007) using SFR. It addresses the known discrepancy where DR deltaful TPE potentials are overly attractive compared to empirical phase shifts, while SFR with \(\tilde{\Lambda} = 700\) MeV yields better agreement. The authors identify a numerical enhancement mechanism similar to that found by Liebig, Baru, and collaborators (2010-2012) for seagull diagrams in deltaless TPE. The adjusted power counting enables systematic improvement of peripheral NN scattering predictions and provides a foundation for consistent inclusion of delta isobar degrees of freedom in chiral effective field theory.
