# Faddeev calculations for light $Ξ$-hypernuclei

> The hypernuclear systems $NNΞ$ and $ΞΞN$ are considered as an analogue of $nnp$ ($^3$H) nuclear system (with the notation as $AAB$ system). We use the recently proposed modification for the $s$-wave Malfliet-Tjon potential. The modification simulates the Extended-Soft-Core model (ESC08c) for baryon-baryon interactions. The $ΞN$ spin/isospin triplet $(S, I)=(1, 1)$ potential generates a bound state with the energy $B_2(AB)$=1.56~MeV. Three-body binding energy $B_3$ for the states with maximal total isospin is calculated employing the configuration-space Faddeev equations. Comparison with the results obtained within the integral representation for the equations is presented. The different types of the relation between $B_2$ and $B_3(V_{AA}=0)$ are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the three-body hypernuclear systems \(NN\Xi\) and \(\Xi\Xi N\) have bound states when the \(\Xi N\) pair is in the spin/isospin triplet \((S, I) = (1, 1)\) state, but this stability depends on the condition that all pairs are in triplet isospin states, which forbids the strong decay \(N\Xi \to \Lambda\Lambda\). The authors note that their differential Faddeev calculations generally agree with prior integral Faddeev results from Refs. [GV16, GVV16], but a small correction to those earlier results is needed.

## Method

The authors solved the configuration-space differential Faddeev equations for the \(AAB\) systems \(NN\Xi\) and \(\Xi\Xi N\), restricting to \(s\)-wave states with total angular momentum \(L=0\). They used a modified \(s\)-wave Malfliet-Tjon potential for nucleon-nucleon interactions and simulated the Extended-Soft-Core model (ESC08c) for \(\Xi N\) and \(\Xi\Xi\) interactions via Yukawa-type potentials. The calculations were performed for states with maximal total isospin, and the results were compared with those from the integral representation of the Faddeev equations.

## Key numbers

- The \(\Xi N\) spin/isospin triplet \((S, I) = (1, 1)\) potential generates a two-body bound state with binding energy \(B_2(AB) = 1.56\) MeV.
- For the \(NN\Xi\) system in the \((S, I) = (3/2, 1/2)\) state, the three-body binding energy is \(B_3 = 17.205\) MeV (differential Faddeev) compared to 17.203 MeV (integral Faddeev).
- For the \(NN\Xi\) system in the \((S, I) = (1/2, 3/2)\) state, the three-body binding energy is \(B_3 = 2.886\) MeV (differential Faddeev) compared to 2.8855 MeV (integral Faddeev).
- For the \(\Xi\Xi N\) system in the \((S, I) = (1/2, 3/2)\) state, the three-body binding energy is \(B_3 = 4.512\) MeV (differential Faddeev) compared to 4.5119 MeV (integral Faddeev).
- For the \(\Xi\Xi\alpha\) system in the \((S, I) = (0, 1)\) state, the three-body binding energy is \(B_3 = 7.635\) MeV (differential Faddeev only).
- The mass polarization contribution to the \(\Xi\Xi\alpha\) binding energy is 3.6%, defined as \((B_3(V_{AA}=0) - 2B_2(AB))/B_3(V_{AA}=0)\).

## Context

This work extends the predictions of bound states in \(\Xi NN\) and \(\Xi\Xi N\) systems made by Gal and collaborators (2015, 2016) using integral Faddeev equations, and provides a cross-check using differential Faddeev equations in configuration space. The authors also calculate the previously unconsidered \(\Xi\Xi\alpha\) system. The results enable further study of the \(\Xi\Xi\) interaction strength in three-body systems and the role of mass polarization effects, analogous to those studied in \(\Lambda\Lambda\alpha\) systems by Filikhin and collaborators (2002) and Hiyama (2002).
