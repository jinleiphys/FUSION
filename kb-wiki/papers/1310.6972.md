# Coarse grained NN potential with Chiral Two Pion Exchange

> We determine the chiral constants of the Nucleon-Nucleon Two Pion Exchange potential deduced from Chiral Perturbation Theory. By using a coarse grained representation of the short distance interactions with 30 parameters, the Partial Wave Analysis fit gives chi^2/dof = 1.1 to a mutually consistent set of 6713 data previously built from all published proton-proton and neutron proton scattering data from 1950 till 2013 with LAB energy below 350 MeV. We obtain (c1, c3, c4)=(-0.41+- 1.08,-4.66+- 0.60, 4.31+- 0.17)/GeV with an almost 100% anti-correlation between c1 and c3. We also provide the errors in the short distance parameters and propagate them to the deuteron properties and low partial waves phase shifts.

## Key claim

The chiral constants \(c_1, c_3, c_4\) of the nucleon-nucleon two-pion exchange potential are extracted from a partial wave analysis of 6713 mutually consistent scattering data, yielding \(\chi^2/\nu = 1.08\), but the fit is performed using a fixed database selected by an improved \(3\sigma\) criterion based on a previous one-pion-exchange analysis, not by a self-consistent rejection driven by the chiral two-pion-exchange potential itself.

## Method

The authors used a coarse-grained representation of the short-range nucleon-nucleon interaction as a sum of Dirac delta-shells at radii 0.6, 1.2, and 1.8 fm, with a cut-off radius \(r_c = 1.8\) fm, and added the chiral two-pion-exchange potential at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) to the long-range part. They performed a partial wave analysis fitting 30 short-distance parameters plus the three chiral constants \(c_1, c_3, c_4\) to a fixed consistent database of 6713 proton-proton and neutron-proton scattering data with laboratory energy below 350 MeV, using the covariance matrix to propagate uncertainties to deuteron properties and phase shifts.

## Key numbers

* Chiral constants obtained: \(c_1 = -0.41 \pm 1.08\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\), \(c_3 = -4.66 \pm 0.60\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\), \(c_4 = 4.31 \pm 0.17\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\).
* Fit quality: \(\chi^2/\nu = 1.08\) for 6713 data points with 33 parameters (30 short-range + 3 chiral constants).
* Deuteron asymptotic D/S ratio: \(\eta = 0.02473 \pm 0.00004\).
* Deuteron asymptotic S-wave amplitude: \(A_S = 0.8854 \pm 0.0002\ \mathrm{fm}^{1/2}\).
* Deuteron matter radius: \(r_m = 1.9689 \pm 0.0004\ \mathrm{fm}\).
* Deuteron quadrupole moment: \(Q_D = 0.2658 \pm 0.0005\ \mathrm{fm}^2\).
* Deuteron D-wave probability: \(P_D = 5.30 \pm 0.03\ \%\).
* Deuteron inverse matter radius: \(\langle r^{-1} \rangle = 0.4542 \pm 0.0002\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the coarse-grained delta-shell partial wave analysis of Perez, Amaro, and Ruiz Arriola (2013) by adding the chiral two-pion-exchange potential of Kaiser (1997) to the long-range interaction, reducing the cut-off radius from 3 fm to 1.8 fm. It updates the earlier chiral TPE analyses of the Nijmegen group (Rentmeester 1999, 2003) with a larger and more consistent database (6713 data vs. 1951 or 9895 data) and provides statistical error propagation to deuteron properties and phase shifts, enabling uncertainty quantification in nuclear structure calculations using chiral interactions.
