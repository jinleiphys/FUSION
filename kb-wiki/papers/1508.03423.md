# Hadron interferences in the proton induced coherent $η$ meson production reaction on a scalar-isovector nucleus

> The coherent $η$ meson energy $E_η$ distribution spectra in the proton nucleus reaction have been calculated to investigate the $π^0 - η$ mesons' interference, in addition to the study of resonance $N^*$ dynamics in the nucleus. The elementary reaction occurring in the nucleus is assumed to proceed as $ pN \to pN^* $; $ N^* \to Nη$. Born terms in the intermediate state is also considered. In a scalar-isovector nucleus, this reaction occurs because of $π^0$ and $η$ meson exchange interactions for the forward going proton and $η$ meson; other meson exchange potentials do not contribute in this process. The sensitivity of the cross section to the hadron nucleus interactions, and the beam energy dependence of the cross section are studied for this reaction.

## Key claim

The coherent η meson production cross section on a scalar-isovector nucleus (¹⁴C) is calculated to show that both π⁰ and η meson exchange potentials contribute, and their interference is distinctly visible in the η meson energy distribution spectrum, but only because the target is isovector (unlike the isoscalar case studied previously where π⁰ exchange vanishes).

## Method

The elementary reaction is modeled as pN → pN* followed by N* → Nη, with Born terms also included in the intermediate state. The T-matrix is constructed from π⁰ and η meson exchange potentials between the projectile proton and a nucleon in the target, using coupling constants extracted from measured decay widths of resonances (N(1520), N(1535), N(1650), N(1710), N(1720)). Distorted wave functions for protons and the η meson are expressed in eikonal form, and optical potentials are evaluated using the "tρ(r)" approximation; the η meson optical potential is evaluated from its self-energy following the approach of Alvaredo and Oset.

## Key numbers

- The cross section due to π⁰ meson exchange potential is approximately 1/5 that due to η meson exchange potential at 2.5 GeV beam energy (for N(1520) resonance only).
- The peak cross section due to V_π⁰ appears at four-momentum transfer q² ≈ -0.18 GeV²; the peak due to V_η appears at q² ≈ -0.25 GeV².
- The form factor values at the respective peaks: F_π(q² ≈ -0.18 GeV²) ≈ 0.89; F_η(q² ≈ -0.25 GeV²) ≈ 0.78.
- The pseudoscalar meson propagator values: G_π(q² ≈ -0.18 GeV²) ≈ -5.06 GeV⁻²; G_η(q² ≈ -0.25 GeV²) ≈ -1.83 GeV⁻².
- The ratio |V_π⁰(q)/V_η(q)|² ≈ 1/3.06 from coupling constants, isospin, form factors, and propagators; the calculated peak cross section ratio shows V_η is ~5 times larger than V_π⁰.
- The plane wave cross section is reduced by a factor of 3.74 due to initial state interaction (ISI) alone, by a further factor of 2.86 due to final state interaction (FSI), and by a total factor of 11.81 when all hadron nucleus interactions are included.
- The peak position shifts by about 40 MeV toward higher E_η due to hadron nucleus interactions.
- Increasing beam energy from 2.25 GeV to 3 GeV increases the peak cross section by a factor of 5.37 and shifts the peak from E_η ≈ 1.39 GeV to E_η ≈ 1.75 GeV.
- The coupling constants used: g_π (πNN(940)) ≈ 13.4; g_η (ηNN(940)) ≈ 7.93; g*_η ≈ 1.86 for N(1535); f*_η ≈ 9.98 for N(1520); Λ_π = 1.3 GeV; Λ_η = 1.5 GeV.
- The ¹⁴C nuclear density parameters: w = 1.38, c = 1.73 fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Alvaredo and Oset (2003) on coherent η production in (p,p') reactions on spin-isospin saturated (isoscalar) nuclei, where only η meson exchange contributed. By choosing a scalar-isovector target (¹⁴C), the author enables the study of π⁰-η interference, which was not possible in the isoscalar case. The work also builds on Das (2014) which demonstrated the importance of N(1520) resonance (with updated coupling constant f_ηNN(1520) = 9.98) over N(1535) at higher energies. The results enable future experimental tests of hadron-nucleus optical potentials and resonance-nucleus interactions using coherent η production on isovector targets.
