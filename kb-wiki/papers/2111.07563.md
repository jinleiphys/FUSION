# Dragging heavy quark in an anisotropic QCD medium beyond the static limit

> Heavy quark dynamics in an anisotropic QCD medium have been analyzed within the Fokker-Planck approach. Heavy quark drag force and momentum diffusion tensor have been decomposed by employing a general tensor basis for an anisotropic medium. Depending upon the relative orientation of the direction of the momentum anisotropy of the medium and heavy quark motion, two drag and four diffusion coefficients have been estimated in the anisotropic QCD medium. The relative significance of different components of drag and momentum diffusion coefficients has been explored. The dependence of the angle between the anisotropic vector and heavy quark motion to the drag and diffusion coefficients has also been studied. Further, the energy loss of heavy quarks due to the elastic collisional process in an anisotropic medium has been studied. It is seen that the anisotropic contributions to heavy quark transport coefficients and its collisional energy loss have a strong dependence on the direction and strength of momentum anisotropy in the QCD medium.

## Key claim

The paper claims that heavy quark transport coefficients (drag and momentum diffusion) and collisional energy loss in an anisotropic QCD medium have a strong dependence on both the strength of momentum anisotropy and the relative orientation between the anisotropy direction and the heavy quark motion, but this conclusion is explicitly conditioned on a weakly anisotropic medium (anisotropy parameter ξ ≪ 1) and on elastic collisional processes only.

## Method

The authors employ the Fokker-Planck approach to describe heavy quark Brownian motion in a hot QCD medium, computing drag force and momentum diffusion tensor from two-body elastic collisions (HQ + quark/gluon → HQ + quark/gluon). They decompose the drag force into two components and the diffusion tensor into four components using a general tensor basis constructed from the heavy quark momentum vector and the anisotropy direction vector, with anisotropic effects entering through a non-equilibrium correction to the medium particle distribution function obtained by solving the transport equation in the weak anisotropy limit (ξ ≪ 1). The matrix elements for elastic collisions are taken from Combridge (1978) and Svetitsky (1987), and the anisotropic distribution is modeled by rescaling one momentum direction following Romatschke and Strickland (2003).

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to m_HQ = 1.3 GeV for numerical estimates
- Temperature values used: T = 360 MeV (RHIC energy) and T = 480 MeV (LHC energy)
- The anisotropic correction δA₀ considerably reduces the drag coefficient A₀, especially at low momentum regimes
- The additional drag coefficient A^(aniso)_1 is negligible at high momentum regimes but may have an important role at higher temperature regimes (T = 480 MeV)
- In the static limit p → 0, the additional diffusion coefficients B^(aniso)_2 and B^(aniso)_3 are non-negligible, especially for strongly anisotropic media
- In the static limit, B^(aniso)_0 = B^(aniso)_1, similar to the isotropic case
- The ratio of collisional energy loss in anisotropic medium to isotropic medium is plotted for both RHIC (T = 360 MeV) and LHC (T = 480 MeV) energies, showing suppression with increasing anisotropy strength

## Context

This work extends the isotropic heavy quark transport framework of Svetitsky (1987) and Golam Mustafa et al. (1997) to include momentum anisotropy, and generalizes the anisotropic decomposition approach of Romatschke and Strickland (2003) from collective modes to heavy quark transport. It builds on prior studies of heavy quark dynamics in anisotropic media by Srivastava et al. (2016) and Kurian et al. (2020), and is motivated by recent experimental observations of enhanced directed flow of D-meson at ALICE (2019) and STAR (2019) that indicate magnetic field-induced anisotropy. The results provide anisotropic drag and diffusion coefficients that can serve as input parameters for Langevin dynamics simulations of heavy quark observables such as the nuclear suppression factor R_AA and flow coefficients in heavy-ion collisions.
