# Signs of Phase Transitions in Two-Nucleon Systems

> The properties of dense nuclear matter under extreme conditions are a subject of the large experimental activities worldwide. Heavy-ion collision experiments are the agenda item at RHIC, LHC, FAIR, and NICA facilities. Meanwhile, a complementary approach to the heavy-ion collision researches devoted to investigation of phase transitions in few-nucleon systems has not been discussed. In this paper, we try to fill up the gap. It is shown that signals of the phase transition of deuteron into 6-q bag as well as signs of formation of the pion Bose condensate in compressed two-nucleon systems might be already observed in deuteron-deuteron collisions.

# Signs of Phase Transitions in Two-Nucleon Systems

## Key claim

Signals of the phase transition of deuteron into a 6-quark bag, as well as signs of formation of a pion Bose-Einstein condensate in compressed two-nucleon systems, might already be observable in deuteron-deuteron collisions at 8.9 GeV/c momentum, based on reanalysis of existing experimental data from the JINR synchrophasotron.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed missing-mass spectra from the reaction D+D→M_X+D at t=−0.495 GeV², originally measured by Baldin et al. at 8.9 GeV/c primary deuteron momentum. They compared the observed peak structures with kinematic models for elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering, dibaryon production, and constituent quark scattering, and then cross-referenced the kinematically allowed masses with dibaryon spectra reported by Troyan et al. (2013) from 38,915 events in pp collisions. An equidistant mass spectrum formula was assumed for np-dibaryons, and the dynamical Casimir effect was invoked to model bound pion pair production via a time-dependent potential well.

## Key numbers

- Primary deuteron momentum: 8.9 GeV/c
- Momentum transfer squared: t = −0.495 GeV²
- Dibaryon candidate at 2.37 GeV with Γ ≈ 70 MeV, reported by WASA-at-COSY Collaboration in reaction pp → D π⁰ π⁰
- pp-dibaryon masses observed by Troyan et al.: 1886±1, 1898±1, 1916±2, 1937±2, 1965±2, 1980±2, 1999±2, 2017±3, 2087±3, 2106±2 MeV/c²
- Equidistant spectrum formula: M_n = M_NN + 10.08·n MeV, with n = 0, 1, 2, ..., 40
- Constituent quark mass from kinematic fit: 0.351 GeV (compared to 0.318 GeV from Jovanovic (2000))
- Spatial dimension of interaction region: a ≈ 0.68 fm
- Pion energy per pair: E_π = 5.04 MeV
- Momentum inside potential well: p ≈ 0.53 GeV
- Square root of potential depth: √U₀ ≈ 0.55 GeV
- Number of pion pairs observed in Troyan data: n = 80

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Kostenko (2010, 2012) that few-nucleon short-range correlations (SRC) can serve as targets for studying chiral phase transitions, complementing heavy-ion collision programs at RHIC, LHC, FAIR, and NICA. The analysis reinterprets older experimental data from Baldin et al. (JINR synchrophasotron) and Troyan et al. (2013) to argue that dibaryon signals and pion condensate formation are already present in existing measurements. The authors contrast their findings with lattice QCD results from HAL QCD Collaboration (2011), NPLQCD Collaboration (2011), and PACS Collaboration (2013), which found stable dibaryons only at unphysical quark masses, and with chiral perturbation theory calculations by Soto and Tarrús (2010) that introduced dibaryon contact terms. The work suggests that future experiments could search for bound pion pairs via the predicted SU(1,1) coherent state distributions.
