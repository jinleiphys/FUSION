# Chemical equilibration of quarks and gluons at RHIC and LHC energies

> We study chemical equilibration of quarks and gluons in central nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The initial quark and gluon densities are taken from earlier studies as well as from recent perturbative QCD estimates and are then evolved via rate equations coupled to longitudinally boost-invariant fluid dynamics. We find that, for RHIC initial conditions, the lifetime of quark-gluon matter is too short in order for the quark and gluon number densities to chemically equilibrate prior to hadronization. In contrast, at LHC energies chemical equilibration is complete before the system hadronizes. Entropy production due to chemical equilibration can be as large as 30%.

## Key claim
Chemical equilibration of quarks and gluons in central nuclear collisions depends critically on collision energy: at RHIC energies the quark-gluon matter lifetime is too short for chemical equilibration before hadronization, whereas at LHC energies equilibration is complete prior to hadronization. The authors qualify that this conclusion holds under the assumption that kinetic equilibrium is established by a proper time τ₀ ~ 0.1–0.25 fm/c, and that entropy production from chemical equilibration can reach 30%.

## Method
The authors evolved initial quark and gluon densities (taken from the self-screened parton cascade model and from perturbative QCD estimates of Eskola and Kajantie (1997)) using rate equations for the reactions gg ↔ qq̅ and gg ↔ ggg, coupled to longitudinally boost-invariant ideal fluid dynamics. They used the full phase-space distribution function (rather than the factorized approximation of Biró et al. (1993)) and accounted for non-zero net-baryon number. The evolution was terminated when the plasma reached the hadronization energy density ε_h = 1.45 GeV/fm³.

## Key numbers
- For RHIC initial conditions from the SSPC model: τ₀ = 1.25 fm/c, ε₀ = 22.9 GeV/fm³, T₀ = 0.57 GeV, n_g⁰/ñ_g⁰ = 0.39, n_q⁰/ñ_q⁰ = 0.14, n_q̅⁰/ñ_q̅⁰ = 0.14.
- For RHIC initial conditions from pQCD: τ₀ = 0.10 fm/c, ε₀ = 4.7 GeV/fm³, T₀ = 0.44 GeV, n_g⁰/ñ_g⁰ = 0.29, n_q⁰/ñ_q⁰ = 0.08, n_q̅⁰/ñ_q̅⁰ = 0.08.
- For LHC initial conditions from the SSPC model: τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c, ε₀ = 137 GeV/fm³, T₀ = 0.89 GeV, n_g⁰/ñ_g⁰ = 0.28, n_q⁰/ñ_q⁰ = 0.08, n_q̅⁰/ñ_q̅⁰ = 0.08.
- For LHC initial conditions from pQCD: τ₀ = 0.10 fm/c, ε₀ = 340 GeV/fm³, T₀ = 1.12 GeV, n_g⁰/ñ_g⁰ = 0.74, n_q⁰/ñ_q⁰ = 0.37, n_q̅⁰/ñ_q̅⁰ = 0.37.
- Entropy production due to chemical equilibration can be as large as 30%.
- The strong coupling constant was taken as α_s = 0.3 throughout the analysis.
- The number of massless flavors used was N_f = 2.5 to mimic the effect of the nonzero strange quark mass.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Biró et al. (1993) and Srivastava et al. (1997) on chemical equilibration in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, using updated pQCD estimates from Eskola and Kajantie (1997) and comparing with the self-screened parton cascade model. The authors assess the validity of the factorized phase-space distribution approximation used in prior work and include non-zero net-baryon number, which was neglected in earlier SSPC-based studies. The results are directly relevant for interpreting experiments at RHIC (commencing in 1999) and for planning at the LHC, and the authors note that extension to nonzero strange quark mass is planned in subsequent work (Elliott and Rischke, in progress).
