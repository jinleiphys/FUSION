# Transport Coefficients of Gluon Plasma

> Transport coefficients of gluon plasma are calculated for a SU(3) pure gauge model by lattice QCD simulations on $16^3 \times 8$ and $24^3 \times 8$ lattices. Simulations are carried out at a slightly above the deconfinement transition temperature $T_c$, where a new state of matter is currently being pursued in RHIC experiments. Our results show that the ratio of the shear viscosity to the entropy is less than one and the bulk viscosity is consistent with zero in the region, $1.4 \leq T/T_c \leq 1.8 $.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy ratio of gluon plasma is less than one in the temperature range \(1.4 \leq T/T_c \leq 1.8\), but most probably larger than the conjectured lower bound of \(1/4\pi\), while the bulk viscosity is consistent with zero within the present statistics.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of SU(3) pure gauge theory using Iwasaki's renormalization-group improved action on \(16^3 \times 8\) and \(24^3 \times 8\) lattices. Transport coefficients were extracted from Matsubara Green's functions of the energy-momentum tensor via linear response theory, using a three-parameter ansatz for the spectral function (a relativistic Breit-Wigner form) fitted to the lattice data at discrete imaginary-time points.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity in lattice units on the \(24^3 \times 8\) lattice: \(\eta a^3 = 0.0036(36)\) at \(\beta = 3.05\) and \(0.0072(30)\) at \(\beta = 3.3\).
- Shear viscosity in physical units on the \(24^3 \times 8\) lattice: \(\eta = 0.106(108)\ \mathrm{GeV}^3\) at \(\beta = 3.05\) and \(0.471(194)\ \mathrm{GeV}^3\) at \(\beta = 3.3\).
- Bulk viscosity in lattice units on the \(24^3 \times 8\) lattice: \(\zeta a^3 = -0.00095(288)\) at \(\beta = 3.05\) and \(-0.0031(26)\) at \(\beta = 3.3\), consistent with zero within errors.
- Lattice scales used: \(a^{-1} = 3.09\ \mathrm{GeV}\) at \(\beta = 3.05\), \(3.62\ \mathrm{GeV}\) at \(\beta = 3.20\), and \(4.03\ \mathrm{GeV}\) at \(\beta = 3.30\).
- The ratio \(\eta/s\) is less than one and larger than \(1/4\pi\) (the KSS bound) across the temperature range studied.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering lattice calculation of transport coefficients by Karsch and Wyld (1987), who used an \(8^3 \times 4\) lattice that was too small in the temporal direction to determine the spectral function parameters. The results support the phenomenological finding by Teaney (2003) that \(\eta/s \sim 0.04\) from elliptic flow data, and are consistent with the strongly coupled QGP picture proposed by Shuryak and Zahed. The next step is to reduce statistical and systematic errors using anisotropic lattices and the maximum entropy method to test the conjectured lower bound \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\) from the AdS/CFT correspondence (Policastro, Son, and Starinets, 2001; Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, 2004).
