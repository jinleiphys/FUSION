# Equation of state and neutrino transfer in supernovae and neutron stars

> We overview the progress of the tables of the equation of state for astrophysical simulations and the numerical methods of neutrino transfer. Hot and dense matter play essential roles in core-collapse supernovae and neutron stars. Equation of state determines the structure of compact objects and their dynamics through its behavior of thermodynamic quantities. In addition, neutrinos are trapped in supernova cores and neutron star mergers and frequently interact with matter to crucially affect dynamics in determining the explosion mechanism and the final form of compact objects. Therefore, it is essential to implement detailed processes of nuclear and neutrino physics in numerical simulations by having reliable data set of the equation of state and reaction rates. We show examples of developments of the equation of state and the neutrino transfer and discuss research directions toward understanding the explosive phenomena by the first principle calculation.

## Key claim

The paper overviews progress in constructing equation of state (EOS) tables and neutrino transfer methods for astrophysical simulations, but the abstract's claim of "first principle calculation" is hedged: the author notes that while full Boltzmann neutrino transfer is now possible in spherical symmetry, no explosion is obtained for ordinary massive star models under those conditions, and the neutrino heating mechanism still has unresolved uncertainties related to microphysics and numerical methods.

## Method

The author reviews the history and current status of supernova EOS tables, focusing on two main approaches: microscopic many-body theories (cluster variational method and Dirac Brückner Hartree-Fock) and systematic variations of relativistic mean-field models (TM1 and TM1e parameter sets). Numerical simulations of core-collapse supernovae are performed using general relativistic neutrino-radiation hydrodynamics under spherical symmetry, solving the Boltzmann equation for neutrino transfer without approximations. The EOS tables are constructed to cover wide ranges of density, temperature, and proton fraction, with data made publicly available through the CompOSE repository.

## Key numbers

- TM1 interaction: incompressibility K = 281 MeV, symmetry energy E_sym = 36.89 MeV, slope parameter L = 110.8 MeV
- TM1e interaction: incompressibility K = 281 MeV, symmetry energy E_sym = 31.38 MeV, slope parameter L = 40 MeV
- Maximum neutron star masses for both TM1 and TM1e are beyond 2.0 solar masses
- Simulations of a 15 solar mass star show central density and temperature profiles at 200 ms after bounce for VM-EOS, DBHF-EOS, TM1, and TM1e
- VM-EOS leads to higher density at core bounce compared to Shen-EOS; does not provide explosion of a 15 solar mass star under spherical symmetry
- Black hole forming collapse of a 40 solar mass star with VM-EOS shows shorter neutrino signal duration than Shen-EOS but longer than LS-EOS
- Proto-neutron star cooling with VM-EOS is slower than with Shen-EOS

## Context

This review extends the line of work on supernova EOS tables that began with Wolff and Hillebrandt (1984), followed by the first publicly available EOS code by Lattimer and Swesty (1991, LS-EOS) and the Shen et al. (1998a, 1998b) data table (Shen-EOS). The paper discusses recent microscopic approaches by Togashi et al. (2017) using the cluster variational method and by Fuchs et al. (2020) using Dirac Brückner Hartree-Fock theory, as well as systematic variations of the symmetry energy in the Shen-EOS framework (Sumiyoshi et al. 2019, Hu et al. 2020). The work enables future studies of how nuclear physics uncertainties, particularly the symmetry energy slope parameter L, affect supernova dynamics and neutrino emission, and calls for extension of data entries for neutrino processes on the CompOSE platform.
