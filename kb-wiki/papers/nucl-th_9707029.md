# The reactions $pp\to ppπ^0$ and $pp\to dπ^+$ at threshold: The role of the isoscalar $πN$ scattering amplitude

> We examine the role of the elementary isoscalar pion--nucleon scattering amplitude in the description of the processes $pp \to ppπ^0$ and $pp\to dπ^+$ at threshold. We argue that the presently used tree level dimension two approximation used in chiral perturbation theory is insufficient as input by direct comparison with the $π$N scattering data. We also show that a successful semi--phenomenological boson--exchange model does better in the description of these data. The influence of the violation of crossing symmetry in the meson--exchange model has to be studied in more detail. We stress that further investigations of the process $pp\to dπ^+$ can pave the way to a deeper understanding of the pion dominated part of the transition operator.

## Key claim

The tree-level dimension-two chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) approximation used in previous calculations of \(pp \to pp\pi^0\) is insufficient for the momentum transfers involved, and the resulting sign of the rescattering contribution (destructive interference with the direct term) is likely an artifact of this truncation; a one-loop treatment is required before decisive conclusions can be drawn.

## Method

The authors compare two approaches for the isoscalar \(\pi N\) scattering amplitude that enters the rescattering contribution in \(pp \to pp\pi^0\) and \(pp \to d\pi^+\) at threshold: (1) tree-level CHPT with dimension-two operators, using two different sets of low-energy constants \(c_i\) (from a tree-level fit and from a one-loop fit), and (2) a semi-phenomenological meson-exchange model fitted to \(\pi N\) phase shifts up to 500 MeV. They compute the half-off-shell isoscalar \(T\)-matrix and the resulting transition operator for pion production, then evaluate the cross sections in distorted-wave Born approximation including final-state nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

* The four-momentum of the exchanged pion at threshold is \(k^2 = -m M_\pi \approx -7 M_\pi^2 \approx -0.1\ \text{GeV}^2\), which is deeply space-like.
* Tree-level fit values for the low-energy constants (in GeV\(^{-1}\)): \(c_1 = -0.64 \pm 0.14\), \(c_2 = 1.78 \pm 0.10\), \(c_3 = -3.90 \pm 0.09\), giving \(c^+ = c_2 + c_3 - 2c_1 = -0.84\).
* One-loop fit values (in GeV\(^{-1}\)): \(c_1 = -0.93 \pm 0.10\), \(c_2 = 3.34 \pm 0.20\), \(c_3 = -5.29 \pm 0.25\), giving \(c^+ = -0.09\) and \(a^+ = -4.7 \times 10^{-3}/M_\pi\).
* The meson-exchange model yields \(a^+ = -1.7 \times 10^{-3}/M_\pi\).
* The isoscalar scattering length \(a^+\) is expressed as \(4\pi (1 + M_\pi/m) a^+ = (2 M_\pi^2 / F_\pi^2)(c_2 + c_3 - 2c_1 - g_A^2/(8m)) + 3 g_A^2 M_\pi^3/(64\pi F_\pi^4) + \mathcal{O}(M_\pi^4)\), with \(F_\pi = 92.4\ \text{MeV}\) and \(g_A = 1.26\).

## Context

This work addresses the sign discrepancy between the meson-exchange model of Schutz et al. (1996) and tree-level CHPT calculations by Park et al. (1996), Biraud et al. (1996), and Lee (1996) for the rescattering contribution in \(pp \to pp\pi^0\). The authors argue that the CHPT approach must be extended to one-loop order to correctly capture the momentum dependence of the isoscalar \(\pi N\) amplitude at the large space-like momenta involved. They propose that further study of \(pp \to d\pi^+\) can help disentangle the isoscalar and isovector components of the transition operator, since heavy-meson exchanges are negligible in that channel.
