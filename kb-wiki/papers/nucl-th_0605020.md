# Removal of forbidden states in a three-$α$ system

> The ground and excited 0$^+$ states of $^{12}$C are investigated in a 3$α$ macroscopic model using the deep potential of Buck, Friedrich and Wheatley. The elimination of forbidden states is performed either by constructing the allowed state space explicitly or by using the orthogonalizing pseudopotential. The well-known enigmatic behavior of the latter approach is resolved. It is safe to define the forbidden states referring to the underlying microscopic model.

## Key claim

The enigmatic behavior of the orthogonalizing pseudopotential method for removing forbidden states in the 3α macroscopic model is resolved by showing that two particular 3α states, which have very small but non-zero eigenvalues of the projection operator Γ, are incorrectly excluded when the projection constant λ is made sufficiently large, yet these states should be included as allowed states from the microscopic Pauli principle.

## Method

The authors solve the 3α Schrödinger equation with the deep Buck-Friedrich-Wheatley (BFW) α-α potential, removing the three forbidden bound states (0s, 1s, 0d) either by explicitly constructing the allowed state space via diagonalization of the projection operator Γ in a translationally invariant harmonic-oscillator basis, or by adding the orthogonalizing pseudopotential λΓ to the Hamiltonian. They compare results from three basis sets: Group I (eigenfunctions of Γ with γ=0), Groups I+II (including two states with very small γ), and the Pauli-allowed states defined by harmonic-oscillator forbidden states corresponding to the microscopic (0s)⁴ α-particle configuration.

## Key numbers

- The BFW potential parameters are v₀ = -122.6225 MeV, ρ₀ = 0.22 fm⁻², and β = 0.75 fm⁻¹, with ħ²/m_α = 10.4465 MeV·fm² and e² = 1.44 MeV·fm.
- The two Group II eigenvalues are γ₁ = 1.35152×10⁻⁵ and γ₂ = 1.07152×10⁻³ (for Q_max=30), stable to at least five digits for Q_max=50.
- Using only Group I (the standard projection technique result), the ground state energy at Q_max=60 is -0.22 MeV relative to the 3α threshold, with rms radius 2.31 fm.
- Using Groups I+II, the ground state energy at Q_max=60 is -19.90 MeV with rms radius 1.31 fm; using Pauli-allowed states (set iii), the ground state energy is -19.25 MeV with rms radius 1.31 fm.
- The experimental ground state energy of ¹²C is -7.27 MeV; the microscopic 3α calculation gives -7.72 MeV and rms radius 1.92 fm (Matsumura, earlier work).
- The probability of finding the γ₂ state in the ground state wave function is 0.64 for λ=10⁴ MeV, decreasing rapidly for λ ≥ 10⁵ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic 3α model studies using the BFW potential (Buck, Friedrich, and Wheatley, 1977) and addresses the long-standing puzzle reported by Tursunov (2001), Descouvemont (2001), and others that the orthogonalizing pseudopotential method gives unstable, threshold-bound ground states far from the experimental -7.27 MeV. The authors show that the correct treatment requires including the two Group II states, which correspond to Pauli-allowed states from the microscopic 3α model (Kamimura, 1981; Uegaki, 1979). This enables a consistent macroscopic treatment that respects the Pauli principle as defined by the underlying harmonic-oscillator (0s)⁴ α-particle configuration, rather than by the bound states of the phenomenological potential.
