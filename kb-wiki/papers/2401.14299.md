# Estimates of Lee-Yang zeros and a possible critical point on the pion condensate boundary in the QCD isospin phase diagram using an unbiased exponential resummation on the lattice

> Without invoking any cumulant determination at the input level, we present here the first calculations of direct estimates of the Lee-Yang zeros of QCD partition function in (2+1)-flavor QCD. These zeros are obtained in complex isospin chemical potential $μ_I$ plane using the unbiased exponential resummation formalism on $N_τ=8$ lattices and with physical quark masses. For different temperatures, we illustrate the stability of the zeros closest to the origin from which, we subsequently procure the radius of convergence estimates. From the temperature-dependence study of the real and imaginary parts of these zeros, we try estimating one of the possible critical points forming the second order pion condensate critical line in the isospin phase diagram. Further, we compare these resummed estimates with the corresponding Mercer-Roberts estimates of the subsequent Taylor series expansions of the first three partition function cumulants. We also outline comparisons between resummed and Taylor series results of these cumulants for real and imaginary values of $μ_I$ and highlight the behavior of different expansion orders within and beyond the obtained resummed estimates of radius of convergence. We also re-establish that this resummed radius of convergence can efficiently capture the onset of overlap problem for finite real $μ_I$ simulations.

## Key claim
Using the unbiased exponential resummation formalism on Nτ=8 lattices with physical quark masses, the paper presents the first direct estimates of Lee-Yang zeros of the QCD partition function in the complex isospin chemical potential plane without relying on cumulant determination at the input level, and from the temperature dependence of these zeros attempts to estimate a possible critical point on the second-order pion condensate critical line.

## Method
The partition function was approximated into polynomial form using unbiased exponential resummation, and Lee-Yang zeros were computed in the complex μI plane using the Newton-Raphson method with error tolerance 0.002 and up to 10^8 iterations. The analysis used 20K gauge configurations from HotQCD collaboration on 32^3 × 8 lattices with (2+1)-flavor HISQ fermions and physical quark masses, with 500 Gaussian random volume sources per configuration for the first eight derivatives. The closest zero to the origin was identified for each temperature, and its distance provided the resummed radius of convergence, while the temperature dependence of the real and imaginary parts was used to extrapolate toward a critical point.

## Key numbers
- Temperature range studied: T ∈ [125:176] MeV
- Lattice size: 32^3 × 8
- The resummed radius of convergence ρR = |μI^0| remains close to approximately 1.5 within errorbars across the temperature range
- The Newton-Raphson tolerance was set to ε = 0.002 with NU ∼ O(10^8) maximum iterations
- 500 Gaussian random volume sources were used per configuration for derivative estimation
- 20K configurations were analyzed per temperature, with NB = 100 bootstrap samples

## Context
This work extends the unbiased exponential resummation formalism developed by Mitra (2023) to the isospin sector of QCD, providing a new approach to studying the pion condensate phase boundary without introducing pionic regulators. The results are compared with Mercer-Roberts estimates from Taylor series expansions (Mercer 1990, Pasztor 2019) and with eighth-order Taylor expansions around μI=0 for the first three cumulants (Gavai 2003, Ejiri 2003). The critical point estimate lies on the second-order pion phase boundary critical line, consistent with the convention where pion condensation onset occurs at μI = mπ (Son 2000, Andersen 2023), as opposed to the mπ/2 convention used by Brandt (2017). This enables future application of the same methodology to the more experimentally relevant finite baryon density regime.
