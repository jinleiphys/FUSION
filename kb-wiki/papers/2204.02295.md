# Three-body molecules $\bar{D}\bar{D}^{\ast}Σ_{c}$- understanding the nature of $T_{cc}$, $P_{c}(4312)$, $P_{c}(4440)$ and $P_{c}(4457)$

> The nature of the three pentaquark states, $P_{c}(4312)$, $P_{c}(4440)$ and $P_{c}(4457)$, discovered by the LHCb Collaboration in 2019, is still under debate, although the $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}Σ_{c}$ molecular interpretation seems to be the most popular. In this work, by adding a $\bar{D}$ meson into the $\bar{D}^{\ast}Σ_{c}$ pair, we investigate the mass and decay width of the three-body molecules $\bar{D}\bar{D}^{\ast}Σ_{c}$ and explore the correlation between the existence of the $\bar{D}\bar{D}^{\ast}Σ_{c}$ molecules with the existence of $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}Σ_{c}$ and $\bar{D}^{\ast}\bar{D}$ two-body molecules. The latter can be identified with the doubly charmed tetraquark state $T_{cc}$ recently discovered by the LHCb Collaboration. Based on the molecular nature of $P_{c}(4312)$, $P_{c}(4440)$, $P_{c}(4457)$, and $T_{cc}$, our results indicate that there exist two three-body bound states of $\bar{D}\bar{D}^{\ast}Σ_{c}$ with $I(J^{P})=1(1/2^{+})$ and $I(J^{P})=1(3/2^{+})$, and binding energies $37.24$ MeV and $29.63$ MeV below the $\bar{D}\bar{D}^{\ast}Σ_{c}$ mass threshold. In addition, we find that the mass splitting of these two three-body molecules are correlated to the mass splitting of $P_{c}(4440)$ and $P_{c}(4457)$, which offers a non-trivial way to reveal the molecular nature of these states. The partial widths of two $\bar{D}\bar{D}^{\ast}Σ_{c}$ molecules decaying into $J/ψp \bar{D}$ and $J/ψp \bar{D}^{\ast}$ are found to be several MeV. We recommend the experimental searches for the $\bar{D}\bar{D}^{\ast}Σ_{c}$ molecules in the $J/ψp \bar{D}$ and $J/ψp \bar{D}^{\ast}$ invariant mass distributions.

# Three-body molecules $\bar{D}\bar{D}^{\ast}\Sigma_{c}$ – understanding the nature of $T_{cc}$, $P_{c}(4312)$, $P_{c}(4440)$ and $P_{c}(4457)$

## Key claim

Assuming the molecular nature of $T_{cc}$ (as a $\bar{D}\bar{D}^{\ast}$ bound state) and of $P_{c}(4312)$, $P_{c}(4440)$, $P_{c}(4457)$ (as $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}\Sigma_{c}$ bound states), the three-body system $\bar{D}\bar{D}^{\ast}\Sigma_{c}$ forms two bound states with $I(J^{P})=1(1/2^{+})$ and $I(J^{P})=1(3/2^{+})$; the existence of these three-body molecules is correlated with the existence of the two-body subsystems, and their mass splitting is oppositely correlated to the mass splitting of the $\bar{D}^{\ast}\Sigma_{c}$ doublet.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Schrodinger equation using the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) with one-boson-exchange (OBE) potentials for the $\bar{D}\Sigma_{c}$, $\bar{D}^{\ast}\Sigma_{c}$, and $\bar{D}^{\ast}\bar{D}$ subsystems. The cutoff parameters of the OBE potentials were fixed by reproducing the masses of the molecular candidates: $\Lambda_{P}=1.16$ GeV for the $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}\Sigma_{c}$ system (reproducing the three pentaquark states) and $\Lambda_{T}=0.998$ GeV for the $\bar{D}\bar{D}^{\ast}$ system (reproducing $T_{cc}$). Partial decay widths of the three-body molecules into $J/\psi p \bar{D}$, $J/\psi p \bar{D}^{\ast}$, and $\bar{T}_{cc}\Lambda_{c}\pi$ were calculated using an effective Lagrangian approach with coupling constants determined via the compositeness condition.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies of the $\bar{D}\bar{D}^{\ast}\Sigma_{c}$ molecules in Case II ($\Lambda_{T}=0.998$ GeV, $\Lambda_{P}=1.16$ GeV): $37.24$ MeV for $I(J^{P})=1(1/2^{+})$ and $29.63$ MeV for $I(J^{P})=1(3/2^{+})$ below the $\bar{D}\bar{D}^{\ast}\Sigma_{c}$ mass threshold.
- Binding energies in Case I ($\Lambda_{P}=\Lambda_{T}=0.998$ GeV): $10.86$ MeV for $1(1/2^{+})$ and $7.06$ MeV for $1(3/2^{+})$.
- Binding energies in Case III ($\Lambda_{P}=\Lambda_{T}=1.16$ GeV): $63.07$ MeV for $1(1/2^{+})$ and $46.94$ MeV for $1(3/2^{+})$.
- Partial decay width of $Hq_{1}$ ($J=1/2$) into $J/\psi p \bar{D}$: $12.3$ MeV.
- Partial decay width of $Hq_{2}$ ($J=3/2$) into $J/\psi p \bar{D}$: $0.9$ MeV.
- Partial decay widths into $J/\psi p \bar{D}^{\ast}$: $3.7$ MeV ($Hq_{1}$) and $3.9$ MeV ($Hq_{2}$).
- Partial decay widths into $\bar{T}_{cc}\Lambda_{c}\pi$: $0.2$ keV ($Hq_{1}$) and $3.3$ keV ($Hq_{2}$).
- Cutoff for $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}\Sigma_{c}$ potential: $\Lambda_{P}=1.16$ GeV; cutoff for $\bar{D}\bar{D}^{\ast}$ potential: $\Lambda_{T}=0.998$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecular interpretation of the LHCb pentaquark states $P_{c}(4312)$, $P_{c}(4440)$, $P_{c}(4457)$ (discovered in 2019) and the doubly charmed tetraquark $T_{cc}$ (discovered in 2021) by studying the three-body $\bar{D}\bar{D}^{\ast}\Sigma_{c}$ system. The authors had previously employed a contact-range effective field theory (Liu et al., 2019) to assign the pentaquarks as $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}\Sigma_{c}$ molecules dictated by heavy quark spin symmetry, and the approach follows earlier work on $DDK$ and $DDDK$ systems (Wu et al., 2019) that tested the molecular nature of $D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)$. The predicted three-body molecules, if observed in $J/\psi p \bar{D}$ or $J/\psi p \bar{D}^{\ast}$ invariant mass distributions, would provide a nontrivial consistency check on the molecular nature of both the pentaquark states and $T_{cc}$.
