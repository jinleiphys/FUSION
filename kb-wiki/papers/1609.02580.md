# The thermalization of soft modes in non-expanding isotropic quark gluon plasmas

> We discuss the role of elastic and inelastic collisions and their interplay in the thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma. We consider a simplified situation of a static plasma, spatially uniform and isotropic in momentum space. We focus on the small momentum region, which equilibrates first, and on a short time scale. We obtain a simple kinetic equation that allows for an analytic description of the most important regimes. The present analysis suggests that the formation of a Bose condensate, expected when only elastic collisions are present, is strongly hindered by the inelastic, radiative, processes.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that inelastic (radiative) processes strongly hinder the formation of a transient Bose-Einstein condensate that would otherwise occur in an overpopulated gluon system when only elastic collisions are present, but this conclusion is drawn from a simplified kinetic analysis of a static, uniform, and isotropic plasma focusing on the small momentum region and short time scales.

## Method

The authors reduced the Boltzmann kinetic equation for gluons to a Fokker-Planck equation in the small scattering angle approximation, splitting the collision integral into elastic (number-conserving) and inelastic (number-changing) contributions. They solved this simplified kinetic equation analytically and numerically for a uniform, non-expanding, isotropic quark-gluon plasma with initial conditions inspired by the color glass condensate picture, focusing specifically on the evolution of soft momentum modes.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium temperature for the initial step-function distribution is \( T_{\rm eq} = \left(\frac{15 f_0}{4\pi^4}\right)^{1/4} Q_s \approx 0.44 \, f_0^{1/4} Q_s \), where \( f_0 \) is the initial occupation factor and \( Q_s \) the saturation momentum.
- The critical occupation factor separating overpopulated from underpopulated systems is \( f_c \approx 0.154 \) for the step-function initial distribution and \( f_c = 0.167 \) for the smooth distribution.
- For the overpopulated case with \( f_0 = 1 \), the initial energy density is \( \epsilon_{\rm in} \simeq 0.037 \) and the equilibrium temperature is \( T_{\rm eq} \simeq 0.58 \) (in units of \( Q_s \)).
- The onset time for Bose-Einstein condensation with only elastic collisions, for \( f_0 \gtrsim 1 \), is \( \tau_c \simeq 2/{f_0(1+f_0)} \), where \( \tau \) is the rescaled time variable.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Blaizot, Liao, Mehtar-Tani, 2013) that considered only elastic scatterings and predicted Bose-Einstein condensation in overpopulated gluon systems. It builds on the parametric analysis of thermalization by Baier et al. (2000) and the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario, while also drawing on analogies with gluon cascades in jet physics (Blaizot, Mehtar-Tani, 2015). The simplified kinetic equation enables analytic understanding of the competition between elastic and inelastic processes, and the results suggest that future work should examine the large momentum region and effects of longitudinal expansion.
