# The anomalous quadrupole collectivity in Te isotopes

> We present systematic calculations on the spectroscopy and transition properties of even-even Te isotopes by using the large-scale configuration interaction shell model approach with a realistic interaction. These nuclei are of particular interest since their yrast spectra show a vibrational-like equally-spaced pattern but the few known E2 transitions show anomalous rotational-like behavior, which cannot be reproduced by collective models. Our calculations reproduce well the equally-spaced spectra of those isotopes as well as the constant behavior of the $B(E2)$ values in $^{114}$Te. The calculated $B(E2)$ values for neutron-deficient and heavier Te isotopes show contrasting different behaviors along the yrast line. The $B(E2)$ of light isotopes can exhibit a nearly constant bevavior upto high spins. We show that this is related to the enhanced neutron-proton correlation when approaching $N=50$.

## Key claim
The paper claims that large-scale shell-model calculations can reproduce the anomalous "rotational-like" constant B(E2) values along the yrast line in Te isotopes, despite their vibrational-like equally-spaced spectra, and that this behavior arises from competition between seniority coupling and enhanced neutron-proton correlations, particularly strong when approaching N=50.

## Method
The author performed full configuration-interaction shell-model calculations for even-even Te isotopes from 104Te to 132Te, using a model space comprising the 0g7/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, and 0h11/2 orbitals with 100Sn as the inert core. The effective interaction was derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential renormalized via a perturbative G-matrix approach, with the T=1 monopole part optimized by fitting to low-lying states in Sn isotopes. Diagonalizations were carried out in the M-scheme using parallel shell-model codes on supercomputers, with effective charges ep=1.5e and en=0.8e for B(E2) calculations.

## Key numbers
- The ratio B(E2,4+1 -> 2+1)/B(E2,2+1 -> 0+1) for 114Te is calculated to be 1.38, which agrees with the prediction for a rotor rather than a vibrator (where the ratio would be 2).
- For 120Te, the calculated ratio B(E2,4+1 -> 2+1)/B(E2,2+1 -> 0+1) is 1.322, compared to the experimental value of 1.640; for 122Te the calculated ratio is 1.299 versus experimental 1.500; for 124Te the calculated ratio is 1.301 versus experimental 1.162.
- The ground state of 104Te shows 47% with Jpi=Jv=0, 30% with Jpi=Jv=2, 12.5% with Jpi=Jv=4, and 6.7% with Jpi=Jv=6 in the proton-neutron coupled wave function decomposition.
- For 128Te, 130Te, and 132Te, the Jpi=Jv=0 components in the ground state are 65.2%, 74.4%, and 85.5% respectively.
- The excitation energy of the 1d5/2 orbital is taken as epsilon(1d5/2)=0.172 MeV.

## Context
This work extends systematic shell-model studies of Sn and Sb isotopes (Back 2013, Honma 2014) to the Te isotopic chain, addressing the puzzle that Te isotopes show vibrational-like equally-spaced yrast spectra but rotational-like constant B(E2) values, which could not be reproduced by collective models or the interacting boson model (PhysRevC.71.064324, PhysRevC.90.024316). The calculations enable future studies of alpha formation amplitudes in neutron-deficient Te isotopes and provide a baseline for interpreting ongoing experimental measurements in the 100Sn region.
