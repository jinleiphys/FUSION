# Photoproduction of $Λ(1405)$ with the two-pole structure

> We investigate the $Λ(1405,1/2^-)\equivΛ^*$ photoproduction off the proton target, i.e. $γp\to K^+Λ^*$, considering explicitly its two-pole structure, the higher ($Λ^*_H:\,1430\,\mathrm{MeV}$) and lower ($Λ^*_L:\,1390\,\mathrm{MeV}$) mass-pole contributions, suggested by the chiral-unitary model (ChUM) approaches. For this purpose, we construct a two-body process model, which mimics the Dalitz process, $γp\to K^+πΣ$, assuming that the mass of $Λ^*$ as the invariant mass of $π$ and $Σ$, i.e. $M_{Λ^*}\sim M_{πΣ}$. We employ the effective Lagrangian method with the tree-level Born approximation, using the gauge-invariant prescription for the phenomenological form factors. We provide the numerical results for the energy and angular dependences, $π$-$Σ$ invariant-mass distribution, and so on. It turns out that the model parameters determined from ChUM reproduce the experimental data qualitatively well, supporting the two-pole structure. Moreover, the nucleon resonance contribution near the threshold plays an important role to describe the data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple two-body effective Lagrangian model, which explicitly incorporates the two-pole structure of Λ(1405) (higher pole at ~1430 MeV and lower pole at ~1390 MeV) as suggested by chiral-unitary model (ChUM) calculations, can reproduce experimental CLAS data qualitatively well, but the authors acknowledge that a single-pole scenario could also produce similar results, making it difficult to definitively confirm the two-pole structure from this effective approach alone.

## Method

The authors constructed a two-body process model mimicking the Dalitz process γp → K⁺πΣ, treating the Λ(1405) mass as the π-Σ invariant mass. They employed the effective Lagrangian method with tree-level Born approximation and gauge-invariant phenomenological form factors, considering s-channel (nucleon and N(2080) resonance), t-channel (K and K* exchange), and u-channel (Λ*_H and Λ*_L) contributions. The coupling strengths for the hypothetical Λ*_H and Λ*_L states were taken from ChUM calculations, with the coupling between hypothetical and physical Λ* states computed via a meson-baryon loop diagram using on-shell factorization and dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

- Higher mass pole Λ*_H: 1430 MeV; lower mass pole Λ*_L: 1390 MeV (from ChUM, with few percent deviations from ChUM values of 1429 and 1398 MeV)
- Full decay widths for Λ*_H and Λ*_L: Γ_H = 30 MeV, Γ_L = 70 MeV (deviating from ChUM estimations of Γ_H ≈ 14 MeV and Γ_L ≈ 74 MeV)
- Phase angle between invariant amplitudes: φ = 2.83 (about 90% of the ChUM estimation of approximately π)
- K*-exchange coupling strength: g_{K*NΛ*} ≈ -2.5
- N(2080) resonance parameters used: (M, Γ) ≈ (2000, 230) MeV
- Coupling constant g_{πΣΛ*} = 0.91, determined from experimental decay width Γ_{Λ*→πΣ} ≈ 50 MeV
- Coupling strengths from loop calculation: g_{Λ*Λ*_H} = -(41.59~39.25) MeV, g_{Λ*Λ*_L} = -(26.24~24.76) MeV for M_{πΣ} = (1.355~1.455) GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Λ(1405) photoproduction, particularly addressing the failure of a simple Born approximation model (Nam 2008) to reproduce CLAS data (Moriya 2013). It builds upon the chiral-unitary model (ChUM) calculations by Magas (2005), Jido (2003), and Hyodo (2011) that predicted the two-pole structure, and uses the theoretical framework of Nacher (1998) who studied the Dalitz process by gauging the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction. The results enable future investigations of the Λ(1405) internal structure, though the authors note that distinguishing between one-pole and two-pole scenarios remains challenging with current effective approaches.
