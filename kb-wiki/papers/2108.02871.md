# Threshold Upsilon-meson Photoproduction at EIC and EicC

> High-accuracy $Υ$-meson photoproduction data from EIC and EicC experiments will allow the measurement of the near-threshold total cross section of the reaction $γp\toΥp$, from which the absolute value of the $Υp$ scattering length, $|α_{Υp}|$, can be extracted using a Vector-Meson Dominance model. For this evaluation, we used $Υ$-meson photoproduction quasi-data from the QCD approach (the production amplitude can be factorized in terms of gluonic generalized parton distributions and the quarkonium distribution amplitude). A comparative analysis of $|α_{Υp}|$ with the recently determined scattering lengths for $ωp$, $ϕp$, and $J/ψp$ using the A2, CLAS, and GlueX experimental data are performed. The role of the "young" vector-meson effect is evaluated.

## Key claim

Using quasi-data generated from a QCD approach (factorized in terms of gluonic generalized parton distributions and the quarkonium distribution amplitude), the absolute value of the Upsilon-proton scattering length is predicted to be \(|\alpha_{\Upsilon p}| = (0.51 \pm 0.03) \times 10^{-3}\) fm, which is smaller than all previously determined vector-meson-proton scattering lengths and extends the hierarchy \(|\alpha_{\Upsilon p}| \ll |\alpha_{J/\psi p}| \ll |\alpha_{\phi p}| \ll |\alpha_{\omega p}|\). The extraction relies on the Vector-Meson Dominance (VMD) model and the assumption that the near-threshold total cross section can be described by a series of odd powers in the center-of-mass momentum \(q\).

## Method

The authors generated quasi-data for the total cross section of \(\gamma p \to \Upsilon p\) from the QCD model of Guo (2021) and fit it with a polynomial \(\sigma_t(q) = b_1 q + b_3 q^3 + b_5 q^5\) to extrapolate to threshold. Using the VMD model, they related the linear coefficient \(b_1\) to the absolute scattering length via \(|\alpha_{Vp}| = B_V \sqrt{b_1}\), where \(B_V\) is a kinematic factor derived from the vector-meson mass and its leptonic decay width. The resulting value for \(\Upsilon\) was compared with previously extracted scattering lengths for \(\omega\), \(\phi\), and \(J/\psi\) from A2 (MAMI), CLAS (JLab), and GlueX (JLab) data.

## Key numbers

- The absolute value of the Upsilon-proton scattering length: \(|\alpha_{\Upsilon p}| = (0.51 \pm 0.03) \times 10^{-3}\) fm, extracted from quasi-data with a minimum center-of-mass momentum \(q_{min} = 521\) MeV/\(c\).
- The linear coefficient for the Upsilon total cross section fit: \(b_1 = (0.37 \pm 0.04) \times 10^{-9}\) \(\mu\)b/(MeV/\(c\)).
- The Upsilon leptonic decay width: \(\Gamma(\Upsilon(1S) \to e^+e^-) = 1.340 \pm 0.018\) keV, yielding the VMD coupling constant \(g_V = 19.85 \pm 1.21\) and the kinematic factor \(B_V = 2654.96 \pm 162.15\) MeV\(^{1/2}\).
- For comparison, the previously determined scattering lengths are: \(|\alpha_{\omega p}| = 0.82 \pm 0.03\) fm (from A2 data), \(|\alpha_{\phi p}| = 0.063 \pm 0.010\) fm (from CLAS data), and \(|\alpha_{J/\psi p}| = (3.08 \pm 0.55) \times 10^{-3}\) fm (from GlueX data).
- The threshold photon energy for \(\Upsilon\) photoproduction off the proton: \(E_{thr} = 57152.9\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of vector-meson-proton scattering lengths previously performed for \(\omega\), \(\phi\), and \(J/\psi\) by Strakovsky (2014, 2019, 2020) using data from A2, CLAS, and GlueX. The predicted \(\Upsilon p\) scattering length is significantly smaller than the charmonium value, consistent with the trend of increasing proton transparency for heavier mesons. The result enables future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and the Electron-ion Collider in China (EicC) to test the VMD model's validity for bottomonium and to probe the gluonic structure of the nucleon via generalized parton distributions. The authors note that the VMD model may overestimate the cross section by up to a factor of 50 for heavy mesons, as shown in the Dyson-Schwinger equation study by Xu (2021), which would translate to a factor of 7 uncertainty in the scattering length.
