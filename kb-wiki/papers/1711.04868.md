# Polarization Transfer in Weak Pion Production off the Nucleon

> Polarization transfer (PT) observables in the single pion production induced by the charged current interaction of the neutrino with the nucleon are examined. The polarization components of the final nucleon and the charged lepton are calculated within two models for the pion production. The predictions are made for neutrino energy of the order of 1~GeV as well as for the T2K energy distribution. It is demonstrated that the PT observables, the degree of polarization and the polarization components of outgoing fermions, are sensitive on assumptions about the nonresonant background model. In particular it is shown that the normal components of the polarization of the outgoing nucleon and the lepton are determined by the interference between the resonant (RES) and nonresonant (NB) amplitudes. Moreover the sign of the normal component of the polarization of the charged lepton is fixed by the relative sign between the RES and the NB amplitudes.

## Key claim
Polarization transfer observables in single pion production are sensitive to assumptions about the nonresonant background model, with the normal components of the polarization of the outgoing nucleon and charged lepton being determined by the interference between resonant and nonresonant amplitudes, and the sign of the normal component of the charged lepton polarization being fixed by the relative sign between the resonant and nonresonant amplitudes.

## Method
The authors calculated the polarization components of the final nucleon and charged lepton in charged-current single pion production off the nucleon using two phenomenological models: the Hernandez-Nieves-Valverde (HNV) model and the Fogli-Nardulli (FN) model. The HNV model includes seven Feynman diagrams (five nonresonant background diagrams and two resonant diagrams for the Delta(1232) transition), while the FN model includes four diagrams (three nonresonant and one resonant). Predictions were made for neutrino energies of order 1 GeV and for the T2K energy distribution.

## Key numbers
- Neutrino energy of order 1 GeV is the kinematic domain considered, typical for long and short baseline accelerator neutrino experiments
- The Fermi constant used is G_F ≈ 1.16639 × 10^(-5) GeV^(-2)
- The Cabibbo angle gives cosθ_C ≈ 0.97427
- The Delta(1232) resonance mass is M_Δ = 1232 MeV
- The axial nucleon coupling is g_A = 1.26
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant in the FN model is g_NNπ = √(14.8 · 4π)
- The f* coupling for the Delta transition is f* = 2.14

## Context
This work extends previous studies of polarization transfer in quasielastic neutrino-nucleon scattering (Llewellyn-Smith 1971, Bilenky 2013, Akbar 2017) and in tau lepton production from single pion production (Hagiwara 2003, Kuzmin 2003), which did not include nonresonant background contributions. The authors show that polarization transfer observables can distinguish between different treatments of the nonresonant background, which is not possible from unpolarized cross section data alone. This enables future experimental studies of the interference between resonant and nonresonant amplitudes in neutrino-nucleon scattering.
