# Photodisintegration of aligned deuterons at astrophysical energies using linearly polarized photons

> Following the model independent approach to deuteron photodisintegration with linearly polarized $Îł-$rays, we show that the measurements of the tensor analyzing powers on aligned deuterons along with the differential cross section involve five different linear combinations of the isovector $E1^j_v; j=0,1,2$ amplitudes interfering with the isoscalar $M1_s$ and $E2_s$ amplitudes. This is of current interest in view of the recent experimental finding \cite{blackston1} that the three $E1^j_v$ amplitudes are distinct and also the reported experimental observation \cite{sawatzky} on the front-back (polar angle) asymmetry in the differential cross section.

## Key claim

The paper shows that measurements of tensor analyzing powers on aligned deuterons, combined with the differential cross section for photodisintegration with linearly polarized photons, involve five distinct linear combinations of the three isovector electric dipole amplitudes \(E1_v^j\) (j=0,1,2) interfering with the isoscalar magnetic dipole \(M1_s\) and electric quadrupole \(E2_s\) amplitudes, enabling empirical determination of these amplitudes at astrophysically relevant energies, provided the three \(E1_v^j\) amplitudes are indeed unequal as recently observed at higher energies.

## Method

The authors extend a model-independent irreducible-tensor formalism for deuteron photodisintegration with linearly polarized photons to include aligned deuteron targets (spin-1 nuclei with zero vector polarization but non-zero tensor polarization). They derive explicit expressions for the differential cross section and the tensor analyzing powers \(A^2_0\) and \(A^2_2\) in terms of the isovector \(E1_v^j\) (j=0,1,2), isovector \(M1_v\), isoscalar \(M1_s\), and isoscalar \(E2_s\) multipole amplitudes, showing that the coefficients of the \(\cos\theta\) terms in these observables contain five different linear combinations of the three \(E1_v^j\) amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The differential cross section for \(d(\vec\gamma,n)p\) with linearly polarized photons is given by \(d\sigma/d\Omega = (2\pi^2/6)[a + b\sin^2\theta(1+\cos2\phi) - c\cos\theta]\), where the coefficient \(c = 4\sqrt{6}\,[\text{Re}(E1_v(1)M1_s^*) + \text{Re}(E1_v(2)E2_s^*)]\).
- The front-back asymmetry in the differential cross section is \(d\sigma/d\Omega(\theta) - d\sigma/d\Omega(180-\theta) = -(2\pi^2/3)c\cos\theta\).
- The tensor analyzing power \(A^2_0\) has the form \(A^2_0 = \sqrt{2}\pi^2[a_0 + b_0\sin^2\theta + 2c_0\sin^2\theta\cos2\phi + d_0\cos\theta]\), with \(d_0 = (4/\sqrt{6})[(3/2)\text{Re}(E1_v(1)+3E1_v(2))M1_s^* + (3/2)\text{Re}(3E1_v(1)+E1_v(2))E2_s^*]\).
- The tensor analyzing power \(A^2_2\) has the form \(A^2_2 = 2\sqrt{3}\pi^2[a_2 + b_2\sin^2\theta + c_2\sin^2\theta e^{2i\phi} + d_2\cos\theta]\), with \(d_2 = \sqrt{3/2}\,\text{Re}[(E1_v(2)-E1_v(1))(M1_s^*-E2_s^*)]\).

## Context

This work extends the model-independent formalism of Ramachandran et al. (2004) for deuteron photodisintegration with linearly polarized photons, motivated by the recent experimental finding of Blackston et al. (2010) that the three \(E1_v^j\) amplitudes are distinct at 14 and 16 MeV, and by the observation of Sawatzky (2005) of a front-back asymmetry in the photoneutron yield at photon energies 3.5â€“10 MeV that is not reproduced by prevailing low-energy nucleon-nucleon models. The proposed measurements of tensor analyzing powers on aligned deuterons could clarify the roles of the isoscalar \(M1_s\) and \(E2_s\) amplitudes at astrophysically relevant energies (center-of-mass neutron energies 25â€“200 keV), where only two experimental measurements exist (Suzuki et al., 1995 at 20, 40, 60 keV; Nagai et al., 2000 at 550 keV), and enable empirical determination of the individual \(E1_v^j\) amplitudes without relying on specific deuteron models.
