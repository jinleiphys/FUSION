## Key claim

The authors compute the $J/\psi\pi\to \text{charmed mesons}$ cross section using QCD sum rules with a three-point function and vacuum-pion correlation functions, working up to twist-4 in the soft-pion limit. The result is a thermal average $\langle\sigma^{\pi J/\psi} v\rangle\sim 0.2-0.4$ mb at $T=150\ \text{MeV}$, which is smaller than values used in phenomenological studies of $J/\psi$ absorption by comoving hadrons.

## Method

The authors construct QCD sum rules for the hadronic matrix elements of $J/\psi\pi\to \bar{D}D^*$, $D\bar{D}^*$, $\bar{D}^*D^*$, and $\bar{D}D$ using vacuum-pion correlation functions. They perform an operator product expansion (OPE) up to twist-4, apply a single Borel transformation to all external momenta taken equal, and extract the hadronic amplitudes by extrapolating the sum rule results to $M^2=0$. The cross sections are then computed from these amplitudes and thermally averaged over a Bose-Einstein distribution of pion momenta at temperature $T$.

## Key numbers

- Thermal average of the $J/\psi$-pion dissociation cross section: $\langle\sigma^{\pi J/\psi} v\rangle \sim 0.2-0.4$ mb at $T=150\ \text{MeV}$
- Amplitude for $J/\psi\pi\to \bar{D}D$: $\Lambda \simeq 11.4\ \text{GeV}^{-3}$ (using experimental meson decay constants) or $\Lambda \simeq 14.9\ \text{GeV}^{-3}$ (using sum rules for decay constants)
- Input parameters: $m_c=1.37\ \text{GeV}$, $m_\pi=140\ \text{MeV}$, $m_D=1.87\ \text{GeV}$, $m_{D^*}=2.01\ \text{GeV}$, $m_\psi=3.097\ \text{GeV}$, $f_\pi=131.5\ \text{MeV}$, $f_\psi=270\ \text{MeV}$, $f_D=170\ \text{MeV}$, $f_{D^*}=240\ \text{MeV}$, $\langle\overline{q}q\rangle=-(0.23)^3\ \text{GeV}^3$, $m_0^2=0.8\ \text{GeV}^2$, $\delta^2=0.2\ \text{GeV}^2$, $f_{3\pi}=0.0035\ \text{GeV}^2$
- Borel window used: $6\leq M^2\leq 16\ \text{GeV}^2$

## Context

This work extends the earlier QCD sum rule calculation by N. N. et al. (2001) by using a three-point function with a pion rather than a four-point function, allowing inclusion of twist-3 and twist-4 contributions to study OPE convergence. The results are smaller than those from meson-exchange models (without form factors) but larger than quark-exchange model calculations, and the authors note that the dominant twist-2 contribution comes from the quark condensate, suggesting the $J/\psi$ interacts with the pion surface. The approach could be applied to compute $\Upsilon\pi$, $\chi_c\pi$, and $\eta_c\pi$ cross sections in future work.
