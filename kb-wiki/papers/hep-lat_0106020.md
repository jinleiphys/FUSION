# Radial Correlations between two quarks

> In nuclear many-body problems the short-range correlation between two nucleons is well described by the corresponding correlation in the {two}-body problem. Therefore, as a first step in any attempt at an analogous description of many-quark systems, it is necessary to know the two-quark correlation. With this in mind, we study the light quark distribution in a heavy-light meson with a static heavy quark. The charge and matter radial distributions of these heavy-light mesons are measured on a lattice with a light quark mass about that of the strange quark. Both distributions can be well fitted upto r approx 0.7 fm with the exponential form w_i^2(r), where w_i(r)=A exp(-r/r_i). For the charge(c) and matter(m) distributions r_c approx 0.32(2) fm and r_m \approx 0.24(2) fm. We also discuss the normalisation of the total charge (defined to be unity in the continuum limit) and matter integrated over all space, finding 1.30(5) and 0.4(1) respectively for a lattice spacing approx 0.17 fm.

# Radial Correlations between two quarks

## Key claim
The charge and matter radial distributions of a heavy-light meson (with a static heavy quark and a light quark mass near the strange quark mass) can be well fitted by an exponential form \(w_i^2(r)\) out to about 0.7 fm, but the integrated charge sum-rule gives 1.30(5) rather than unity, which the authors interpret as a non-perturbative estimate of the vector vertex renormalisation factor (0.77(3)) at the finite lattice spacing used.

## Method
The UKQCD Collaboration computed 2-point and 3-point correlation functions on a \(16^3 \times 24\) quenched lattice at \(\beta = 5.7\) (lattice spacing \(\approx 0.17\) fm) with a light quark mass approximately equal to the strange quark mass. They extracted the ground-state \(S_{1/2}\)-wave charge and matter densities of a heavy-light meson by diagonalising a \(3\times3\) matrix of correlation functions built from three levels of fuzzing (basic, F1, F2). The densities were fitted to the exponential form \(w_i(r) = A_i \exp(-r/r_i)\) and also integrated over all lattice sites to obtain sum-rule normalisations.

## Key numbers
- Charge density fall-off radius: \(r_c = 0.32(2)\) fm.
- Matter density fall-off radius: \(r_m = 0.24(2)\) fm.
- RMS radius for charge density: 0.55(3) fm.
- RMS radius for matter density: 0.42(3) fm.
- Integrated charge sum-rule: \(I_c = 1.30(5)\) (lattice spacing \(\approx 0.17\) fm).
- Integrated matter sum-rule: \(I_m = 0.4(1)\) (lattice spacing \(\approx 0.17\) fm).
- Vector meson mass from Fourier transform of charge density: \(M_v = 0.9(1)\) GeV.
- Scalar meson mass from Fourier transform of matter density: \(M_s = 1.3(1)\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier studies of four-quark systems (Green, Pennanen, Michael 1999; Green, Koponen, Pennanen 2000), which found that four-quark energies could not be understood from two-quark potentials alone and required a four-quark form-factor. By extracting the two-quark radial correlation in a heavy-light meson, the authors aim to provide input for modelling many-quark systems analogous to how two-nucleon correlations are used in nuclear many-body problems (e.g., the Moszkowski-Scott separation method, 1960; variational approaches in Carlson 1987). The results enable future comparisons with Dirac-equation solutions and with correlations in the \(Q^2\bar{q}^2\) system, and they motivate unquenched lattice calculations at smaller lattice spacing.
