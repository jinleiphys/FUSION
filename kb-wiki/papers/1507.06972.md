# Suppression of baryon diffusion and transport in a baryon rich strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma

> Five dimensional black hole solutions that describe the QCD crossover transition seen in $(2+1)$-flavor lattice QCD calculations at zero and nonzero baryon densities are used to obtain predictions for the baryon susceptibility, baryon conductivity, baryon diffusion constant, and thermal conductivity of the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma in the range of temperatures $130\,\textrm{MeV}\le T\le 300\,\textrm{MeV}$ and baryon chemical potentials $0\le μ_B \le 400\,\textrm{MeV}$. Diffusive transport is predicted to be suppressed in this region of the QCD phase diagram, which is consistent with the existence of a critical end point at larger baryon densities. We also calculate the fourth-order baryon susceptibility at zero baryon chemical potential and find quantitative agreement with recent lattice results. The baryon transport coefficients computed in this paper can be readily implemented in state-of-the-art hydrodynamic codes used to investigate the dense QGP currently produced at RHIC's low energy beam scan.

## Key claim
Baryon diffusive transport in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma is predicted to be suppressed at finite baryon chemical potentials up to 400 MeV, even before reaching a critical end point that would appear only at much larger densities in this holographic model.

## Method
The authors used a five-dimensional Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton (EMD) holographic model with parameters fixed by (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD data for the equation of state at zero baryon chemical potential and by lattice data for the baryon susceptibility at zero chemical potential. They numerically generated approximately 10^5 black hole solutions to model a hot and dense plasma, then applied linear response theory and Kubo formulas to compute transport coefficients from the retarded two-point functions of gauge-invariant perturbations.

## Key numbers
- Temperature range studied: 130 MeV ≤ T ≤ 300 MeV; baryon chemical potential range: 0 ≤ μ_B ≤ 400 MeV
- Energy scale conversion factor: Λ ≈ 831 MeV, obtained by matching the dip of the speed of sound in the model to lattice results
- Gravitational constant: κ² = 12.5
- Ratio T² σ_B / η < 0.05 across the entire studied region, roughly an order of magnitude lower than recent kinetic theory results from Jaiswal (2015)
- Baryon diffusion constant at μ_B=0 varies significantly across the crossover transition, approaching its conformal limit only at large temperatures
- Fourth-order baryon susceptibility at μ_B=0 shows quantitative agreement with lattice data from Bellwied (2015) for T ≥ 150 MeV

## Context
This work extends the holographic EMD model previously developed by Gubser (2008) and refined by Rougemont (2015) to describe the QGP equation of state at nonzero baryon density, now providing the first realistic predictions for baryon transport coefficients. The results can be directly implemented in hydrodynamic codes used to analyze data from RHIC's low energy beam scan and the future CBM experiment at FAIR, enabling more accurate modeling of baryon-rich QGP dynamics. The predicted suppression of baryon diffusion is consistent with the existence of a critical end point at larger densities, though the model belongs to the type B dynamic universality class while QCD is argued to belong to type H.
