# Reaction Mechanisms of the Proton - Deuteron Breakup Process at GeV Energies

> The deuteron fragmentation by fast protons has been studied both near the kinematics of quasi-free proton - proton scattering and far away from it. We have concentrated on the interplay between different reaction mechanisms associated with the antisymmetrization of the initial and final states and rescattering contributions. A multiple-scattering-expansion technique has been applied to evaluate the reaction amplitude. An essential element of this approach in the momentum representation is the use of the effective nucleon- nucleon interaction constructed by Love and Franey as a two-body t-matrix for the incident proton scattering on a bound nucleon in the deuteron. Along with the five-fold cross sections, the proton analyzing power and the deuteron analyzing powers have been calculated as function of the momentum of the outgoing fast proton. The results are compared with the data obtained by the Gatchina-Saclay collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the multiple-scattering expansion approach, incorporating antisymmetrization, rescattering contributions, and the Love-Franey effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, can describe the proton-deuteron breakup reaction at 1 GeV, but the agreement with experimental data is limited because only the differential cross section has been measured under the kinematic conditions studied, while the polarization observables (which are very sensitive to reaction mechanisms) remain untested.

## Method

The authors applied a multiple-scattering-expansion technique based on the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) formalism of three-body collision theory, retaining first- and second-order contributions to the reaction amplitude. They used the Love-Franey effective nucleon-nucleon interaction as a two-body t-matrix, incorporated the matrix inversion method (MIM) to calculate half-off-energy-shell t-matrices for final-state interactions in the p-n pair, and included full spin structure and Lorentz transformation properties (including Wigner rotations) of the high-energy vertices. Calculations were performed at 1 GeV projectile energy in the deuteron rest frame using the Paris potential for the deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers

- Projectile energy: 1 GeV in the deuteron rest frame for all calculations
- Kinematics I: momentum transfer q ranges from 500 to 650 MeV/c; neutron momentum p3 ranges from 300 to 570 MeV/c
- Kinematics II: maximum momentum transfer q_max = 217 MeV/c; maximum neutron momentum p3^max = 180 MeV/c; fast proton scattering angle θ1 = 6.77°; slow proton scattering angle θ2 = 83°; cut at p1 = 1.60 GeV/c
- For kinematics II, the differential cross section in the quasi-free scattering region is approximately 2 times smaller relative to PWIA predictions due to final-state interaction effects
- For the tensor analyzing power C_yy,0,0,0,0 in kinematics I, the full result varies from approximately 0.5 to 0.8, while the PWIA curve changes rapidly from negative values to nearly the upper limit of 1
- Final-state interaction calculations used partial waves up to J_max = 3 for convergence in kinematics I, and J_max = 1 and 2 for kinematics II

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the pd → ppn reaction that used the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) or spectator mechanism, which were shown by Pun (year not specified) to fail in describing inclusive ^1H(d,p)X data at deuteron kinetic energies of 1.25 and 2.1 GeV. The authors improve upon the approach of Zb (year not specified) by including the dependence of elementary nucleon-nucleon amplitudes on the internal motion of bound nucleons in the deuteron and avoiding artificial truncation in angular momentum decomposition. The results enable future experimental tests of polarization observables (proton analyzing power and deuteron vector and tensor analyzing powers) which the calculations show are highly sensitive to reaction mechanisms beyond PWIA.
