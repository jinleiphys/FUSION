# Hadron Structure in the Non-Perturbative Regime of QCD: Isospin Symmetry and its Violation

> I discuss recent progress made in calculating electromagnetic corrections in the framework of the effective field theory of QCD. In the case of elastic pion-pion scattering, strong interaction predictions have been worked out to two loop accuracy. I present first results for the electromagnetic corrections in the case of neutral pions. Here, the only sizeable effect comes from the charged to neutral pion mass difference. In the presence of nucleons, isospin violation can be measured in threshold pion photoproduction. I review the present status of the theoretical predictions and the experimental data. I argue that a deeper understanding of isospin violation based on a more precise study of such reactions can be achieved.

## Key claim

Isospin violation in low-energy QCD can be systematically calculated using chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) with virtual photons included, but the only sizeable electromagnetic effect in neutral-pion elastic scattering comes from the charged-to-neutral pion mass difference; for reactions involving nucleons, threshold pion photoproduction is sensitive to isospin violation from the light quark mass difference \(m_d - m_u\) and virtual photon effects at the level of a few percent up to 15%, though this estimate is indicative and not based on a fully self-consistent calculation.

## Method

The author constructs the next-to-leading-order chiral pion Lagrangian including virtual photons, counting the electric charge \(e\) as a small momentum of order \(p\). For elastic \(\pi^0\pi^0 \to \pi^0\pi^0\) scattering, the electromagnetic corrections are computed to one-loop order by inserting the dimension-two operator (whose low-energy constant \(C\) is fixed from the pion mass difference) and the dimension-four operators with scale-dependent low-energy constants \(k_i(\lambda)\). For threshold pion photoproduction, the electric dipole amplitudes \(E_{0+}\) for the four channels (\(\gamma p \to \pi^+ n\), \(\gamma n \to \pi^- p\), \(\gamma p \to \pi^0 p\), \(\gamma n \to \pi^0 n\)) are computed in CHPT to order \(p^4\), and the neutron amplitude is extracted via a calculation of the deuteron electric dipole amplitude using Weinberg's approach with phenomenological deuteron wave functions.

## Key numbers

*   \(a_0^{0} = 0.156 \cdot (1 + 0.28 + 0.11 + \mathcal{O}(M_\pi^6))\): the S-wave scattering length for elastic pion-pion scattering, with the current algebra result (0.156), one-loop correction (0.28), and two-loop correction (0.11) shown as multiplicative factors.
*   The electromagnetic correction to the S-wave scattering length \(a_0\) for \(\pi^0\pi^0 \to \pi^0\pi^0\) is 5%, while the effective range \(b_0\) increases by 36% due to the cusp effect from the charged pion threshold opening at \(W_c = 2M_{\pi^+}\).
*   \(E_{0+}^{\rm thr}(\pi^+ n) = 28.2 \pm 0.6\) (CHPT) vs. \(27.9 \pm 0.5\) (experiment, Burg) in units of \(10^{-3}/M_{\pi}\): the electric dipole amplitude for charged pion photoproduction off protons at threshold.
*   \(E_{0+}^{\rm thr}(\pi^- p) = -32.7 \pm 0.6\) (CHPT) vs. \(-31.5 \pm 0.8\) (experiment, TRIUMF) in units of \(10^{-3}/M_{\pi}\): the electric dipole amplitude for charged pion photoproduction off neutrons at threshold.
*   \(E_{0+}^{\rm thr}(\pi^0 p) = -1.16\) (CHPT) vs. \(-1.31 \pm 0.08\) (experiment, Fuchs) in units of \(10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\): the electric dipole amplitude for neutral pion photoproduction off protons at threshold.
*   \(E_{0+}^{\rm thr}(\pi^0 n) = 2.13\) (CHPT) vs. \(1.9 \pm 0.3\) (experiment, Argan) in units of \(10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\): the electric dipole amplitude for neutral pion photoproduction off neutrons at threshold.
*   \(E_d = (-1.8 \pm 0.2) \cdot 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\): the deuteron electric dipole amplitude for \(\pi^0\) photoproduction, calculated in CHPT, compared to the empirical value \((-1.7 \pm 0.2) \cdot 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\) (Argan).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to QCD (chiral perturbation theory) by including virtual photons systematically, building on the two-loop strong-interaction predictions for pion-pion scattering by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) and by Bijnens et al. The electromagnetic corrections for \(\pi^0\pi^0\) scattering were first computed by Meissner, Muller, and Steininger (1997). For the nucleon sector, the author reviews threshold pion photoproduction, comparing CHPT calculations (Bernard, Kaiser, Meissner) with dispersion-theoretical analyses by the Mainz group (Drechsel, Hanstein, Tiator) and with experimental data from TRIUMF, MAMI, and SAL. The deuteron calculation follows Weinberg's approach (1990) and extends the work of Beane, Bedaque, van Kolck, and others. The results enable a quantitative test of isospin violation from the light quark mass difference \(m_d - m_u\) and from virtual photons, with future applications to \(K_{\ell 4}\) decays at DA\(\Phi\)NE and pionium experiments at CERN.
