# Medium Modification of The Pion-Pion Interaction at Finite Density

> We discuss medium modifications of the unitarized pion-pion interaction in the nuclear medium. We incorporate both the effects of chiral symmetry restoration and the influence of collective nuclear pionic modes originating from the p-wave coupling of the pion to delta-hole configurations. We show in particular that the dropping of the sigma meson mass significantly enhances the low energy structure created by the in-medium collective pionic modes.

# Medium Modification of The Pion-Pion Interaction at Finite Density

## Key claim

The authors show that the dropping of the sigma meson mass due to chiral symmetry restoration significantly enhances the low-energy structure in the pion-pion strength function that is created by in-medium collective pionic modes, but this enhancement is less pronounced at densities typical of ordinary nuclei than in prior work by Hatsuda et al. (1999) because the authors use a larger vacuum sigma mass of 1 GeV and a density-dependent sigma-pion-pion coupling constant.

## Method

The authors construct an in-medium unitarized pion-pion T-matrix in the scalar-isoscalar channel by combining two separate mechanisms: (1) the medium modification of the basic pion-pion potential from chiral symmetry restoration, calculated using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at finite baryonic density to obtain density-dependent values of the pion mass, sigma mass, and pion decay constant; and (2) the modification of the two-pion propagator from collective pionic modes originating from the p-wave coupling of pions to delta-hole configurations, described through standard nuclear phenomenology with a two-level model including screening effects from short-range correlations. The unitarized T-matrix is obtained by solving a Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a separable potential derived from the linear sigma model, using the in-medium parameters from the NJL model and the in-medium two-pion propagator from the nuclear matter description.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum sigma mass is taken as 1 GeV (parameter set II), giving a chiral symmetry restoration density of about 2.6 times normal nuclear matter density (2.6 rho_0)
- The sigma mass drops by 110 MeV at half nuclear matter density (0.5 rho_0) and by 225 MeV at normal nuclear matter density (rho_0)
- The in-medium pion mass changes only slightly: from 139 MeV in vacuum to 140.3 MeV at 0.5 rho_0 and 143 MeV at rho_0
- The effective s-wave scattering length from the pion mass variation is approximately -0.01 m_pi^{-1}
- The density at which the inverse sigma propagator vanishes at the two-pion threshold (precursor effect) occurs at about 2.4 rho_0 in the NJL calculation, decreasing to about 2 rho_0 after unitarization
- The vacuum scattering length is a_0 = 0.23 m_pi^{-1}, reproducing experimental data
- The sigma-pion-pion coupling constant decreases by about 20% at normal nuclear matter density

## Context

This work extends prior studies of in-medium pion-pion interactions by incorporating both chiral symmetry restoration effects and collective pionic modes simultaneously, whereas earlier work by Noro (1988), Chanfray (1991), and Aouissat et al. (1994) had considered only the pionic collective modes from delta-hole excitations. The authors build on the finite-temperature NJL model calculations of Quack et al. (1995) by generalizing to finite baryonic density, and they compare their results to the work of Hatsuda et al. (1999) on precursor effects of chiral symmetry restoration. The results are relevant for interpreting the CHAOS collaboration data on pion-induced two-pion production on nuclei (Bonatsos et al., 1996) and for understanding the role of correlated two-pion exchange in nuclear saturation, as studied by Durso et al. (1993), Rapp et al. (1997), and Machleidt (1997).
