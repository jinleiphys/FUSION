# Determination of the $螛^+$ parity from $纬n \to K^- K^+ n$

> It is demonstrated that measurements of photon asymmetry in the $纬n \to K^- K^+ n$ reaction, can most likely determine the parity of the newly discovered $螛^+$ pentaquark. We predict that if the parity of $螛^+$ is positive, the photon asymmetry is significantly positive; if the parity is negative, the photon asymmetry is significantly negative. If the background contribution is large, the photon asymmetry may become very small in magnitude, thereby making it difficult to distinguish between the positive and negative parity results. However, even in this case, a combined analysis of the $(K^+ n)$ invariant mass distribution and photon asymmetry should allow a determination of the parity of $螛^+$.

## Key claim

Measurements of photon asymmetry in the $\gamma n \to K^- K^+ n$ reaction can most likely determine the parity of the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark, provided the background contribution is not too large; if the background is large, a combined analysis of the $(K^+ n)$ invariant mass distribution and the photon asymmetry should still allow parity determination.

## Method

The authors performed a hadronic-level effective Lagrangian calculation of the $\gamma n \to K^- K^+ n$ reaction, including $K$ and $K^*$ exchange diagrams for $\Theta^+$ excitation and background contributions from $\rho$, $\omega$, $\phi$ meson exchanges and $\Sigma(1197)^-$ and $\Sigma(1660)^-$ intermediate states. They computed the photon asymmetry $\Sigma$ and the $(K^+ n)$ invariant mass distribution for both positive and negative parity assignments of a spin-1/2, isoscalar $\Theta^+$, treating the pseudoscalar-pseudovector mixing parameter $\lambda$ and the $\Theta^+$ anomalous magnetic moment $\kappa_\Theta$ as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $\Theta^+$: $m_{\Theta^+} = 1.54 \pm 0.01$ GeV, width $\Gamma < 25$ MeV (LEPS Collaboration).
- For positive parity $\Theta^+$, the peak-to-background ratio in the $(K^+ n)$ invariant mass distribution is large ($\sim 10$); for negative parity, it is relatively small ($\sim 1.4$).
- Calculations performed at incident photon laboratory energy $T_\gamma = 2$ GeV and fixed $(K^+ n)$ invariant mass $m_{(K^+ n)} = 1.54$ GeV.
- For the $K$ contribution alone, the photon asymmetry $\Sigma$ is always positive for positive parity $\Theta^+$ and always negative for negative parity $\Theta^+$, across the range of $\lambda$ and $\kappa_\Theta$ considered.
- Including $K^*$ contributions with $g_{NK^*\Theta} = \pm 2.45$ (positive parity) or $\pm 0.34$ (negative parity) preserves the sign separation: $\Sigma > 0$ for positive parity, $\Sigma < 0$ for negative parity.

## Context

This work extends theoretical efforts to determine the quantum numbers of the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark, discovered by the LEPS Collaboration (2003) and confirmed by DIANA, CLAS, SAPHIR, and neutrino experiments. Prior theoretical predictions were controversial: chiral soliton models (Diakonov, Petrov, Polyakov 1997) and correlated quark models (Jaffe, Karliner) predicted positive parity, while QCD sum rules and quenched lattice QCD (lattice) predicted negative parity. The present study is the first to consider the three-body final state $\gamma n \to K^- K^+ n$ as actually measured experimentally, and it demonstrates that photon asymmetry measurements can resolve the parity ambiguity. The results are consistent with a concurrent study by Zhao and Al-Khalili (2003) on the $\gamma n \to K^- \Theta^+$ reaction.
