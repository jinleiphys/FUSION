# Hadronic resonance gas and charged particle's $p_T$ spectra and elliptic flow in $\sqrt{s}$=200 GeV Au+Au collisions

> Charged particles $p_T$ spectra and elliptic flow in 0-60% Au+Au collisions at RHIC are analyzed in a hydrodynamic model with hot, hadronic resonance gas in the initial state. Physically conceivable hadronic resonance gas, thermalized in the time scale $τ_i$=1 fm, at a (central) temperature $T_i$=220 MeV, with viscosity to entropy ratio $η/s$=0.24, reasonably well explains $p_T$ spectra in all the collision centralities. Centrality dependence of elliptic flow however demands continual increase of viscosity to entropy ratio with centrality.

## Key claim

A viscous hadronic resonance gas (HRG) initialized at a physically motivated limiting temperature of 220 MeV can reproduce charged particle $p_T$ spectra across all centralities, but cannot simultaneously explain the centrality dependence of elliptic flow, which requires a continually increasing viscosity-to-entropy ratio as collisions become more peripheral.

## Method

The authors solve causal dissipative (Israel-Stewart) relativistic hydrodynamics using the code AZHYDRO-KOLKATA, assuming longitudinal boost-invariance and a baryon-free hadronic resonance gas containing all resonances with mass ≤ 2.5 GeV. The initial energy density at τ_i = 1 fm is fixed to the limiting value ε_0 = 5.1 GeV/fm³ (corresponding to T_i = 220 MeV) with participant scaling (hard scattering fraction x = 0), and the fluid freezes out at T_F = 110 MeV. Simulations are performed for four constant values of η/s (0, 0.08, 0.16, 0.24) and compared to PHENIX charged particle p_T spectra and elliptic flow v_2 data in six centrality bins from 0-10% to 50-60%.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature T_i = 220 MeV, corresponding to limiting energy density ε_0 = 5.1 GeV/fm³, chosen so hadron density ρ_had ≤ 2 fm⁻³ (hadron size ≈ 0.5 fm)
- Thermalization time τ_i = 1 fm; freeze-out temperature T_F = 110 MeV
- With η/s = 0.24, simulated p_T spectra agree with PHENIX data within ~10% across all centralities; at p_T ≈ 1.75 GeV, ideal HRG (η/s = 0) under-predicts data by a factor of ~6, while η/s = 0.24 reduces this to a factor of ~2
- Central temperature decreases from T_i ≈ 219 MeV in 0-10% collisions to T_i ≈ 210 MeV in 30-40% collisions
- Elliptic flow in 10-20% collisions requires η/s in the range 0-0.08; 20-30% collisions prefer η/s ≈ 0.16; 30-40% collisions are approximately explained with η/s ≈ 0.24; 40-50% and 50-60% collisions demand η/s > 0.24

## Context

This work extends earlier ideal hydrodynamic analyses of RHIC data with hadronic resonance gas (Kolb 2000) that found poor agreement, by incorporating shear viscosity. The η/s = 0.24 value that fits p_T spectra agrees with theoretical estimates for hot hadronic resonance gas of η/s = 0.24-0.30 (Gorenstein 2007, Pal 2010). The failure to simultaneously describe p_T spectra and elliptic flow mirrors a similar difficulty found in QGP-initialized viscous hydrodynamics (Chaudhuri 2009, Roy 2010), suggesting that the centrality-dependent viscosity requirement is not unique to the HRG assumption. The results leave open the question of whether QGP formation is necessary to explain RHIC data, as a hadronic explanation cannot be ruled out by p_T spectra alone.
