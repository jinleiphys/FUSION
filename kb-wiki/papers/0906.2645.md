# Relativistic descriptions of inclusive quasielastic electron scattering: application to scaling and superscaling ideas

> An analysis of inclusive quasielastic electron scattering is presented using different descriptions of the final state interactions within the framework of the relativistic impulse approximation. The relativistic Green's function approach is compared with calculations based on the use of relativistic purely real mean field potentials in the final state. Both approaches lead to a redistribution of the strength but conserving the total flux. Results for the differential cross section at different energies are presented. Scaling properties are also analyzed and discussed.

## Key claim

The relativistic Green's function (GF) approach and the relativistic mean field (RMF) model both conserve total flux in inclusive quasielastic electron scattering, but they produce different scaling functions; the GF approach yields a less asymmetric scaling function than the RMF model, and its predictions depend significantly on the specific parameterization of the complex optical potential used.

## Method

The authors calculated inclusive quasielastic electron scattering cross sections for 12C within the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA), comparing four treatments of final state interactions: relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA, no FSI), real relativistic optical potential (rROP), relativistic mean field (RMF, using the same real energy-independent potential for bound and scattering states), and the relativistic Green's function (GF) approach (using complex energy-dependent optical potentials EDAD1 and EDAD2). The bound nucleon states were Dirac-Hartree solutions from RMF theory with the NLSH parameterization, and the single-nucleon current used the CC2 form with Dirac and Pauli form factors.

## Key numbers

- Cross sections were calculated for 12C at incident electron energy ε = 1 GeV and three momentum transfers: q = 500, 800, and 1000 MeV/c.
- At q = 1000 MeV/c, the GF1 result at the quasielastic peak overshoots the RMF cross section by up to 40%.
- The imaginary part of the optical potential in the GF approach presents its maximum strength around 500 MeV.
- The rROP and RPWIA results approach each other for large values of the transferred energy ω, as the real part of the optical potential decreases with increasing energy.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of scaling and superscaling in quasielastic electron scattering by Donnelly (1999) and others, which showed that the longitudinal response exhibits superscaling and allowed extraction of a phenomenological scaling function. The RMF model had been shown by Caballero (2005) to produce an asymmetric scaling function consistent with data, while RPWIA and rROP produced symmetric shapes. The present study introduces the GF approach as an alternative that treats FSI consistently in inclusive and exclusive channels, using complex optical potentials from proton-nucleus scattering phenomenology. The comparison helps assess model uncertainties for applications to neutrino-nucleus cross sections relevant to oscillation experiments such as MiniBooNE and T2K.
