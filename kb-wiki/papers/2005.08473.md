# Charmonium transition in electromagnetic and rotational fields

> We study charmonia in electromagnetic and rotational fields in the frame of a potential model. Different from the temperature field which is isotropic and leads to the well-known charmonium dissociation, the electromagnetic and rotational fields break down the radial symmetry, and the competition between strong interaction and electromagnetic and rotational interaction in the direction of Lorentz force makes the charmonia transit from an isotropic bound state of strong interaction with positive binding energy to an anisotropic bound state of electromagnetic and rotational interaction with negative binding energy. The transition seems possible to be realized in high energy nuclear collisions.

# Charmonium transition in electromagnetic and rotational fields

## Key claim

The paper claims that charmonia can undergo a transition from an isotropic bound state of strong interaction with positive binding energy to an anisotropic bound state of electromagnetic and rotational interaction with negative binding energy, but only under the condition that the electromagnetic and rotational fields are strong enough and aligned approximately parallel, as is the case in high-energy nuclear collisions. The transition is anisotropic and occurs only in the direction of the Lorentz force, unlike thermal dissociation which is isotropic.

## Method

The authors use a non-relativistic potential model based on the Schrodinger equation for the c-cbar system, with the Cornell potential (Coulomb plus linear confinement) and spin-spin interaction as the strong interaction part. They introduce electromagnetic fields via minimal coupling and rotational fields via the equivalence to a rotating frame, treating the rotational coupling as a perturbation on the electromagnetic interaction. The relative wave function is expanded in spherical harmonics, and the radial equations are solved numerically using the inverse power method to obtain binding energies and wave functions for J/psi and eta_c states.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass: m = 1.29 GeV
- Cornell potential parameters: alpha = 0.312, sigma = 0.174 (GeV)^2
- Spin-spin interaction parameters: beta = 1.982 GeV, gamma = 2.06 GeV (fixed by fitting vacuum charmonium masses)
- Transverse pseudomomentum: P_ps^perp = 2.5 GeV
- Transverse coordinate: <R_perp> = 2/3 fm (for Fig. 2) and 4/3 fm (for Fig. 1)
- At eE = 12 m_pi^2 and eB = 28 m_pi^2, the binding energy epsilon_0 is reduced to 4.5% of its vacuum value at omega = 0
- Transition point for J/psi_0 with maximum potential V_+: m omega = 1.15 m_pi^2 (at eE = 12 m_pi^2, eB = 28 m_pi^2)
- Maximum perturbative correction from rotation: <H'>/E_E = 9.4% at epsilon^(0) = 0
- Critical magnetic field for transition without rotation: eB = 29.5 m_pi^2
- Maximum rotation for transition: m omega = 8 m_pi^2
- Causality constraint: R_max = 15 fm for omega = 0.1 m_pi

## Context

This work extends the study of quarkonia in medium beyond the well-known thermal dissociation (Matsui and Satz, 1986) to include electromagnetic and rotational fields, which are generated in high-energy nuclear collisions. It builds on prior potential model studies of charmonia in electromagnetic fields by Alford and Strickland (2015) and in rotational fields by Jiang et al. (2016), Chen (2016), and Liu et al. (2018). The paper enables future investigation of whether such transitions can be observed experimentally in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and contrasts the anisotropic transition mechanism with the isotropic thermal dissociation studied by Petreczky (2004), Burnier et al. (2015), and Lafferty (2015).
