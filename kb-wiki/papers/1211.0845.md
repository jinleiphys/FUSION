# Correlations from hydrodynamic flow in p-Pb collisions

> Two-particle correlations in relative rapidity and azimuth are studied for the p-Pb collisions at the LHC energy of 5.02 TeV in the framework of event-by-event 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics. It is found that for the highest-multiplicity events the observed ridge structures appear in a natural way, suggesting that collective flow may be an important element in the evolution of the system. We also discuss the role of the charge balancing and the transverse-momentum conservation.

# Correlations from hydrodynamic flow in p-Pb collisions

## Key claim
Collective hydrodynamic flow, driven by event-by-event fluctuations in the initial fireball geometry, can reproduce the near-side ridge structures observed in two-particle correlations for high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions at LHC energies, suggesting that collectivity may be an important element even in small colliding systems. The authors note that this hydrodynamic contribution does not exclude other sources of correlations such as jets, string decays, or correlated gluon emission.

## Method
The authors performed event-by-event 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations with shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s=0.08, using GLISSANDO to generate initial conditions from a Glauber Monte Carlo model with Gaussian wounding profile and a nucleon-nucleon cross section of 67.7 mb. The hydrodynamic evolution started at proper time τ=0.6 fm, and particles were generated from freeze-out hypersurfaces at T_f=150 MeV using THERMINATOR, including local charge conservation at hadronization and a global transverse-momentum conservation constraint limiting total transverse momentum to P_T=5 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: √s_NN = 5.02 TeV for p-Pb collisions at the LHC
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 0.08
- Initial hydrodynamic starting time: τ = 0.6 fm
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 150 MeV
- Gaussian smoothing width for participant positions: 0.4 fm
- Initial entropy profile parameters: η_0 = 2.5, σ_η = 1.4
- Nucleon-nucleon cross section: 67.7 mb
- Most central event class: N_part ≥ 18, corresponding to 3.4% of most central events
- Second most central class: 16 ≤ N_part ≤ 17, summing to 4.4% of cross section
- Transverse momentum conservation cutoff: P_T = 5 GeV, retaining about 8% of least-P_T events
- 300 initial configurations per centrality class, with 1000 THERMINATOR events per freeze-out configuration

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic explanation of ridge structures from relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions (Takahashi 2009, Luzum 2010, Bozek 2012) to the smaller p-Pb system, building on earlier hydrodynamic studies of elliptic and triangular flow in p-Pb and deuteron-Pb collisions by Bozek (2011). The results directly address the CMS measurement of two-particle correlations in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV (CMS 2012), and contribute to the ongoing debate about whether collectivity can arise in small systems including pp collisions (Luzum 2009, d'Enterria 2010, Bozek 2010, Werner 2010). The findings enable future direct measurements of harmonic flow coefficients v_2 and v_3 in p-Pb collisions, and suggest that separating contributions from the thermalized core and corona could be achieved through analysis of the Δη≈0 ridge at low transverse momenta.
