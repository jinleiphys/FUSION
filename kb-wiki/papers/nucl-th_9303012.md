# Left-Right Asymmetry in $\bar{p}p \rightarrow π^- π^+$

> Results for d$σ$/d$Ω$ and $A_N$ in the reaction $\bar{p}p \rightarrow π^- π^+$ are predicted by a simple quark model. They are compared to recent experimental data from LEAR, as well as to previous predictions from nucleon-exchange models. At low energy the quark model does better than the nucleon-exchange models, but the overall comparison to experiment remains poor. In particular, the double-dip structure of the experimental $A_N$ data is only partly represented. This shortcoming of the simple quark model is traced back to a too small J=2 amplitude. This has interesting implications for the range of this specific annihilation process.

## Key claim

The simple quark model predicts an overall negative analyzing power \(A_N\) at low antiproton momenta (e.g., 497 MeV/c) where nucleon-exchange models fail, but the predicted double-dip structure of \(A_N\) is only partly reproduced because the model's J=2 amplitude is too small.

## Method

The authors compute differential cross section \(d\sigma/d\Omega\) and analyzing power \(A_N\) for \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \pi^- \pi^+\) using a transition potential derived from a constituent quark model with \(^3P_0\) and \(^3S_1\) annihilation mechanisms. The initial \(\bar{p}p\) wavefunction is taken from the 1982 Paris model including Coulomb interaction, and the final pions are treated as non-interacting plane waves. The angular momentum amplitudes are obtained by two-dimensional integrals over the radial coordinates of the initial and final wavefunctions weighted by the non-local transition potential.

## Key numbers

- At \(p_{\rm LAB} = 497\) MeV/c (\(T_{\rm LAB} = 123\) MeV), the quark model with \(\lambda = -0.5\) gives an overall negative \(A_N\), though the forward minimum is not deep enough.
- At \(p_{\rm LAB} = 679\) MeV/c (\(T_{\rm LAB} = 210\) MeV), the same parameters predict a positive \(A_N\) near \(\cos\theta = 0\) with negative forward and backward values, matching the experimental double-dip trend.
- The nucleon size parameter is \(\alpha = 2.8\) fm\(^{-2}\) (nucleon radius 0.60 fm) and the pion size parameter is \(\beta = 3.23\) fm\(^{-2}\) (pion radius 0.48 fm) for the standard calculation.
- Increasing the range by lowering \(\beta\) to 1.73 fm\(^{-2}\) (pion radius 0.66 fm) or \(\alpha\) to 1.24 fm\(^{-2}\) (proton radius 0.90 fm) enhances the J=2 amplitude and improves the double-dip structure of \(A_N\).
- Partial waves with J<5 are sufficient at both 497 and 679 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the study of \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \pi^- \pi^+\) beyond nucleon-exchange models (Moussallam 1983; Mull et al. 1991; Birsa et al. 1991) which fail to predict the negative \(A_N\) at all angles at low momenta (467-585 MeV/c). The quark model improves agreement at low energy but still cannot fully reproduce the experimental double-dip structure, which the authors trace to insufficient J=2 amplitude. This suggests that the range of the annihilation transition potential, determined by nucleon and pion size parameters, is a critical factor; future work could explore initial and final state interaction effects or measure \(A_N\) in \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \pi^0 \pi^0\) to isolate even-J contributions.
