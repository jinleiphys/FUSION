# Power Corrections in Charmless Nonleptonic B-Decays: Annihilation is Factorizable and Real

> We classify LambdaQCD/mb power corrections to nonleptonic B-> M1 M2 decays, where M1 and M2 are charmless non-isosinglet mesons. Using recent developments in soft-collinear effective theory, we prove that the leading contributions to annihilation amplitudes of O[alphas(mb) LambdaQCD/mb] are real. The leading annihilation amplitudes depend on twist-2 and twist-3 three parton distributions. A complex nonperturbative parameter from annihilation first appears at O[alphas^2(sqrt{Lambda mb}) LambdaQCD/mb]. ``Chirally enhanced'' contributions are also factorizable and real at lowest order. Thus, incalculable strong phases are suppressed in annihilation amplitudes, unless the alphas(sqrt{Lambda mb}) expansion breaks down. Modeling the distribution functions, we find that (11 +- 9)% and (15 +- 11)% of the absolute value of the measured B-> K- pi+ and B-> K- K0 penguin amplitudes come from annihilation. This is consistent with the expected size of power corrections.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the leading annihilation amplitudes in charmless nonleptonic B decays, which are of order α_s(m_b) Λ_QCD/m_b, are real and factorizable, contrary to previous assumptions that they involve complex nonperturbative parameters. A complex nonperturbative parameter from annihilation first appears only at higher order, O[α_s^2(√(Λ m_b)) Λ_QCD/m_b], meaning incalculable strong phases are suppressed unless the α_s(√(Λ m_b)) expansion breaks down.

## Method

The authors classify Λ_QCD/m_b power corrections to B → M1 M2 decays (where M1,2 are charmless non-isosinglet mesons) using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET). They construct a complete basis of local six-quark operators in SCET at order Λ/m_b and derive factorization theorems for their contributions, separating physics at the hard scale m_b, the intermediate scale √(m_b Λ), and the nonperturbative scale Λ. The analysis uses minimal subtraction with the zero-bin method to handle endpoint divergences in convolution integrals, and classifies all time-ordered products in SCET that contribute to annihilation amplitudes at leading power-suppressed order.

## Key numbers

- The leading annihilation amplitudes are of order α_s(m_b) Λ_QCD/m_b, with Λ_QCD ~ 500 MeV and m_b = 4.8 GeV
- Modeling distribution functions yields that (11 ± 9)% of the measured B̅^0 → K⁻π⁺ penguin amplitude comes from annihilation
- Modeling distribution functions yields that (15 ± 11)% of the measured B⁻ → K⁻K̅^0 penguin amplitude comes from annihilation
- The chirally enhanced parameter μ_P ~ 2 GeV for kaons and pions, making μ_P/m_b corrections sizable
- Wilson coefficients at m_b = 4.8 GeV in the NDR scheme: C₁ = 1.080, C₂ = -0.177, C₃ = 0.011, C₄ = -0.033, C₅ = 0.010, C₆ = -0.040

## Context

This work extends the factorization theorems for B → M M' decays proven to all orders in α_s at leading order in Λ/m_b by Bauer et al. (2001-2004) and Manohar and Stewart (2006). It contradicts the approach of Keum, Li, and Sanda (pQCD/KLS), who found large imaginary parts from the jet scale √(m_b Λ), and the BBNS/QCDF approach of Beneke, Buchalla, Neubert, and Sachrajda, which parameterized divergent convolutions with complex parameters X_A = (1 + ρ_A e^{iφ_A}) ln(m_B/Λ). The results enable absolute predictions for annihilation amplitudes in B → PP, PV, VV channels given meson distribution functions as inputs, and show that the parameterization of annihilation by complex amplitudes in SU(3) flavor analyses is not theoretically justified at leading power-suppressed order.
