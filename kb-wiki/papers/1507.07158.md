# Measurement of interaction between antiprotons

> One of the primary goals of nuclear physics is to understand the force between nucleons, which is a necessary step for understanding the structure of nuclei and how nuclei interact with each other. Rutherford discovered the atomic nucleus in 1911, and the large body of knowledge about the nuclear force since acquired was derived from studies made on nucleons or nuclei. Although antinuclei up to antihelium-4 have been discovered and their masses measured, we have no direct knowledge of the nuclear force between antinucleons. Here, we study antiproton pair correlations among data taken by the STAR experiment at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and show that the force between two antiprotons is attractive. In addition, we report two key parameters that characterize the corresponding strong interaction: namely, the scattering length (f0) and effective range (d0). As direct information on the interaction between two antiprotons, one of the simplest systems of antinucleons, our result provides a fundamental ingredient for understanding the structure of more complex antinuclei and their properties.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first direct measurement of the strong nuclear force between two antiprotons, finding it to be attractive and consistent with the proton-proton interaction within experimental uncertainties, thereby providing a quantitative test of matter-antimatter symmetry (CPT) in the nuclear force.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration analyzed antiproton pair correlations from 500 million minimum-bias Au + Au collisions at a centre-of-mass energy of 200 GeV per nucleon pair, selecting events in the 30%-80% centrality class. The correlation function was constructed as the ratio of antiproton pairs from the same event to pairs from mixed events, and fitted with the Lednicky and Lyuboshitz analytical model that accounts for final-state interactions including Coulomb effects, strong interactions, and residual correlations from weak decays of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons. Particle identification was achieved using the Time Projection Chamber for energy loss measurements and the Time of Flight detector for mass-squared determination, achieving greater than 99% purity for antiprotons with transverse momentum below 2 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Singlet s-wave scattering length for antiproton-antiproton interaction: \(f_0 = 7.41 \pm 0.19 (\mathrm{stat}) \pm 0.36(\mathrm{sys})\) fm
- Effective range for antiproton-antiproton interaction: \(d_0 = 2.14 \pm 0.27 (\mathrm{stat}) \pm 1.34 (\mathrm{sys})\) fm
- Extracted source radius for antiprotons: \(R_{\bar{p}\bar{p}} = 2.80 \pm 0.02(\mathrm{stat}) \pm 0.03(\mathrm{sys})\) fm
- Extracted source radius for protons: \(R_{pp} = 2.75 \pm 0.01(\mathrm{stat}) \pm 0.04(\mathrm{sys})\) fm
- Fixed proton-proton scattering parameters used for comparison: \(f_0 = 7.82\) fm and \(d_0 = 2.78\) fm from Mathelitsch (1984)

## Context

This work extends the technique of momentum correlation femtoscopy, previously applied to nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon systems by the STAR Collaboration (STARpLambda, STARLL), to the antinucleon-antinucleon sector for the first time. The results provide a direct experimental parameterization of the strong interaction between antiprotons, which is a necessary ingredient for understanding the structure of more complex antinuclei such as antideuterons, antitritons, and antihelium-4, and for describing elastic collisions in ultra-cold trapped antimatter systems. The consistency with proton-proton scattering parameters from Mathelitsch (1984) and Slaus (1989) constitutes a test of CPT symmetry in the nuclear force, complementing prior CPT tests from the Particle Data Group (2014) that were many orders of magnitude more precise but did not directly probe the strong interaction between antinucleons.
