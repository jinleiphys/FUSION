# Short-Range Correlations in Nuclear Matter at Finite Temperatures and High Densities

> The density and temperature dependence of nucleonic single particle spectral function in symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures and densities beyond normal nuclear matter density is investigated in a model emphasizing short-range correlations and phase space aspects. A simple but self-consistent approach based on quantum transport theory is used. In particular we consider the density and temperature regime occuring e.g. during the core collapse in a supernova explosion and the subsequent formation of a neutron star. Mean-field effects are incorporated by a Skyrme type potential.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations in symmetric nuclear matter saturate at densities above twice normal nuclear matter density, an effect explained by Pauli-blocking that dominates over the increased number of collision partners at high density. The authors claim this is the first consistent investigation of the nucleon spectral function covering temperatures from 10 to 70 MeV and densities from ρ₀ to 3ρ₀ within the same model.

## Method

The authors extend the self-consistent quantum transport approach of Lehr et al. (2000, 2002) from zero temperature and saturation density to finite temperatures and high densities, using a constant matrix element for the NN interaction combined with a t-channel form factor. Mean-field effects are incorporated via a Skyrme-type energy density functional (SIII' parameter set) to ensure thermodynamical consistency, and the spectral function is obtained iteratively from the collision integrals on the 2p1h and 1p2h level. The dispersive real part of the collisional self-energy is neglected based on earlier findings that it does not significantly affect the results.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Skyrme SIII' parameters give binding energy −16.1 MeV, effective mass m*/m = 0.76, and mean-field potential U_eff = −61.6 MeV at Fermi momentum k_F = 1.29 fm⁻¹ (ρ = ρ₀, T = 0)
- Averaged scattering amplitude (|M²|)^(1/2) = 309 MeV fm³, adjusted to reproduce Benhar et al. (T = 0) results
- Cutoff parameter Λ = 1.2 GeV, form factor mass m_μ = 600 MeV
- On-shell width ⟨Γ_on⟩ ranges from 10 MeV at low temperatures up to 40 MeV at high temperatures over the full density and temperature range
- Temperatures studied: 10 to 70 MeV; densities studied: ρ₀ to 3ρ₀

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature, saturation-density calculations of Lehr et al. (2000, 2002) to the finite-temperature, high-density regime relevant for supernova core collapse and neutron star formation, as well as heavy-ion collisions at CBM. The results are compared to the semi-phenomenological approach of Alvarez-Ruso et al. (2002) for densities up to 2ρ₀ and temperatures up to 20 MeV, and to the self-consistent T-matrix calculations of Bozek (2002) and the variational results of Benhar et al. and Baldo et al. The model enables future extension to isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter and provides input for neutrino opacity calculations in dense astrophysical environments.
