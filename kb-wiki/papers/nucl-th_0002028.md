# Inclusive meson production in peripheral collisions of ultrarelativistic heavy ions

> There exist several proposals to use Weizsäcker-Williams photons generated by ultrarelativistic heavy ions to produce exotic particles in $γγ$ fusion reactions. To estimate the background conditions for such reactions we analyze various mechanisms of meson production in very peripheral collisions of ultrarelativistic heavy ions at RHIC and LHC energies. Besides $γγ$ fusion they include also electromagnetic $γA$ interactions and strong nucleon-nucleon interactions in grazing $AA$ collisions. All these processes are characterised by low multiplicities of produced particles. $γA$ and $AA$ events are simulated by corresponding Monte Carlo codes, RELDIS and FRITIOF. In each of these processes a certain fraction of pions is produced close to the mid-rapidity region that gives a background for the $γγ$ events. The possibility of selecting mesons produced in $γγ$ fusion events via different $p_t$ cut procedures is demonstrated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while photon-photon (γγ) fusion is a promising mechanism for producing exotic particles in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, the inclusive production of pions via γA interactions and grazing nuclear collisions creates a significant background that must be rejected using transverse momentum (pt) cuts, because pions from γγ fusion have pt below 30 MeV/c in PbPb collisions.

## Method

The authors used the Weizsäcker-Williams equivalent photon approximation to calculate γγ fusion cross sections for meson production, including a Dominant Resonance Production (DRP) model that sums contributions from intermediate meson decays to obtain inclusive π0 yields. They simulated γA interactions using the RELDIS Monte Carlo code based on an intranuclear cascade model of photonuclear reactions, and simulated grazing nuclear collisions using the FRITIOF 7.1 event generator for nucleon-nucleon interactions in peripheral PbPb and AuAu collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive cross section for π0 production in PbPb collisions at LHC energies via γγ fusion (DRP model): σ_incl(π0) = 106 mb, compared to the exclusive direct production cross section of 36.25 mb
- Transverse momentum of mesons produced in γγ fusion in PbPb collisions: pt < 30 MeV/c
- Average number of pions per charge in γA processes at RHIC and LHC energies: approximately 1 per charge
- Contribution from double photon absorption in γA collisions: less than 10% for heavy ions with γ ≫ 100
- Photon energy threshold for pion production in γA collisions: ω_min ≈ 140 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of exclusive meson production in γγ fusion by Klein and Nystrand (1999) for the STAR detector at RHIC, and by Krauss (1997) and Baur (1998) for equivalent photon methods, by providing inclusive cross sections that include contributions from intermediate resonance decays. The results enable experimentalists at RHIC and LHC to design pt cut procedures for selecting γγ fusion events and rejecting background from γA interactions and grazing nuclear collisions, complementing the exclusive channel studies of Klein (1999).
