# Can hadronic rescattering explain the "jet quenching" at RHIC?

> Recent RHIC data have shown novel nuclear modifications of moderate to high pt particle production in central Au+Au collisions, including a suppression of hadron production and a disappearance of back-to-back hadron pairs. In this paper, we investigate whether final-state hadronic interactions of the jet fragments can reproduce the RHIC data. We find that hadronic rescattering can account for the disappearance of back-to-back hadron pairs, but cannot reproduce other features of the RHIC data.

# Can hadronic rescattering explain the "jet quenching" at RHIC?

## Key claim

Hadronic rescattering of jet fragments can account for the disappearance of back-to-back hadron pairs in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but cannot simultaneously reproduce the observed suppression of single inclusive hadron production and the narrow near-angle azimuthal correlations, because the model produces copious resonance decays that broaden the near-angle correlation peak beyond what is seen in data.

## Method

The authors embedded PYTHIA-generated jet events into a semi-classical Monte Carlo hadronic rescattering model that simulates Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy using isospin-averaged binary hadronic collisions with relativistic kinematics. They tracked particles tagged as originating from PYTHIA or from resonances induced by PYTHIA-particle interactions, and compared the resulting nuclear modification factor R_AA and two-particle azimuthal correlations to PHENIX and STAR data.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear modification factor R_AA for pions in central Au+Au collisions is approximately 1/5 (factor of 5 suppression) above p_T = 5-6 GeV/c.
- The pure rescattering model produces a near-angle azimuthal correlation width of sigma = 0.39 ± 0.04, compared to the STAR-measured width of sigma ≈ 0.2 for p+p, d+Au, and central Au+Au collisions.
- The rescattering model with embedded PYTHIA events reproduces the absolute value of R_AA at p_T ≈ 4 GeV/c but shows R_AA trending upward with increasing p_T, contrary to the experimental data.
- The hadronization model parameters that reproduce RHIC low-p_T data are T = 300 MeV, sigma_y = 2.4, and tau_had = 1 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the debate between partonic energy loss models (Gyulassy, Wang, Baier) and hadronic rescattering models (Gallmeister, Cassing) for explaining jet quenching at RHIC. The authors show that single-particle observables alone cannot distinguish between a dense medium with small cross-sections and a dilute medium with large cross-sections, as noted in Humanic (2004). The failure of the hadronic rescattering model to reproduce two-particle correlations supports the interpretation that jet fragmentation occurs outside the dense medium, consistent with parton energy loss in a quark-gluon plasma. This enables future work to focus on fluctuation observables and correlation measurements as discriminators between hadronic and partonic descriptions of the medium.
