# Description of bound and unbound many-body systems at the drip-lines

> We construct a 1D toy model to describe the structural properties of Borromean nuclei at the continuum threshold. The system is composed by two valence neutrons that are bound thanks to a residual interaction. Different discretization procedures are used (harmonic oscillator, transformed harmonic oscillator, rigid wallbox). The resulting energies and wave functions, as well as transition intensities and resonances are compared within the different discretization techniques.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a one-dimensional toy model to study Borromean nuclei at the continuum threshold, but explicitly states that the simple 1D nature of the model prevents any direct comparison with experimental data, and the chosen parameters are not intended to describe any particular nucleus quantitatively.

## Method

The authors solve a 1D time-independent Schrödinger equation with a Woods-Saxon potential using three different pseudostate discretization methods: harmonic oscillator (HO) basis, transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis, and an infinite square well (ISQW) basis for the "potential in a box" approach. They compare the resulting bound-state energies, wave functions, continuum pseudostates, electric dipole (E1) and quadrupole (E2) transition intensities, and resonance behavior across these methods, also validating against a Numerov numerical integration and the binning (average) method for continuum discretization.

## Key numbers

- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters are: V₀ = -50.00 MeV, R = 2.00 fm, a = 0.40 fm⁻¹, μ = 0.975 amu.
- The system has three bound states; the weakly-bound state Ψ₂ has energy E₂ = -0.51 MeV.
- Energy convergence criterion is ΔE = 5 keV for a dimension increment ΔN = 10.
- For the HO basis, convergence of bound states is obtained at N = 50.
- For the THO basis with γ/b = 1.2 fm⁻¹/², an N = 20 basis achieves the 5 keV convergence goal, while γ/b = 2.4 fm⁻¹/² requires N = 50.
- For the BOX method with x_b = 35 fm, bound state convergence (ΔE = 5 keV) is achieved at N = 85.
- Total E1 strength from the ground state converges to 0.978 fm² with N = 15 for HO and THO methods and x_b = 35 fm for BOX.
- Total E2 strength from the ground state converges to approximately 2.95-2.96 fm⁴ across methods.
- The total strength S_T^(gs)(x, N) = 0.978 fm², S_T^(1)(x, N) = 3.19 fm², S_T^(2)(x, N) = 34.4 fm² for the three bound states.
- Energy-weighted sum rule E_W^(gs)(x, N) converges to 0.500 in units of ħ²/μ for the HO case at N = 5.

## Context

This work extends the pseudostate methodology previously applied to three-dimensional nuclear systems (e.g., by Thompson (2004) and others) into a simplified 1D toy model to study Borromean nuclei and weakly-bound systems near the continuum threshold. The paper compares three discretization techniques (HO, THO, BOX) and validates them against the binning method used in continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) calculations. The results enable future application of these pseudostate methods to more realistic many-body calculations of halo nuclei and systems at the drip-lines, and the authors note that other potentials (Morse, Hazi-Taylor, Ginocchio, Poeschl-Teller) have also been tested with their computational tools.
