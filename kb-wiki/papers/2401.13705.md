# The earliest phase of relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> According to the Color Glass Condensate approach to relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the earliest phase of the collision is a glasma which is made of highly populated gluon fields that can be treated classically. Using a proper time expansion we study analytically various properties of the glasma. In particular, we compute the glasma energy-momentum tensor which allows us to obtain the energy density, longitudinal and transverse pressure, collective flow, and angular momentum. We also study the role of the glasma in jet quenching by computing collisional energy loss and transverse momentum broadening.

## Key claim

Using a proper time expansion (near field expansion) to analytically study the glasma phase of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the authors compute the energy-momentum tensor and jet quenching observables, finding that the glasma has a sizable impact on jet quenching comparable to that of the long-lasting equilibrium phase, but the results are valid only for small proper times and depend on the adopted regularization procedure.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in Milne coordinates using an expansion in powers of the proper time \(\tau\) (treated as a small parameter), with the gauge condition \(A^\tau=0\) and a boost-invariant ansatz for the gauge potential. They express the expansion coefficients in terms of initial potentials at \(\tau=0\), which are connected via boundary conditions to pre-collision potentials of the two incoming nuclei described by the Color Glass Condensate effective theory. Physical observables are obtained by averaging over a Gaussian distribution of colour charges within each nucleus, using the Glasma Graph Approximation to express higher-order correlators in terms of two-point correlators of pre-collision potentials, which are regularized in both the infrared and ultraviolet domains.

## Key numbers

- The energy density of the glasma phase is significantly higher than that of the equilibrium phase, with the equilibrium phase having a lifetime of approximately 10 fm while the pre-equilibrium phase lasts about 1 fm.
- The longitudinal magnetic field at \(\tau=0\) is given by \(B = B^z(0,\vec x_\perp) = \partial^y \alpha_\perp^x(0,\vec x_\perp) - \partial^x \alpha_\perp^y(0,\vec x_\perp) - ig[\alpha_\perp^y(0,\vec x_\perp),\alpha_\perp^x(0,\vec x_\perp)]\).
- The two-point correlator of pre-collision potentials for uniform surface colour charge density \(\bar\mu\) is \(B^{ij}(\vec x_\perp,\vec y_\perp) = g^2\,\left(\frac{e^{g^4 N_c \left(\bar\gamma(r)-\bar\gamma(0)\right)}-1}{g^4 N_c \left(\bar\gamma(r)-\bar\gamma(0)\right)} \right)\partial_x^i \partial_y^j \bar\gamma(r)\) with \(\bar \gamma(r) = \frac{\bar\mu}{4 \pi m} r K_1(m r)\), where \(m\) is an infrared regulator and \(K_1\) is a modified Bessel function.
- The expansion coefficients for the gauge potential to fourth order in \(\tau\) are given explicitly, e.g., \(\alpha^{(2)} = \frac{1}{8} \big[{\cal D}^j,[{\cal D}^j,\alpha^{(0)}]\big]\) and \(\alpha^{i(2)}_{\perp} = \frac{1}{4} \epsilon^{ij} [{\cal D}^j, B]\).

## Context

This work extends the analytic near field expansion approach originally proposed by Fries (2005) and further developed by Fukushima (2007), Fujii (2008), Chen (2015), and others, to compute glasma properties from the energy-momentum tensor and jet quenching observables. It builds on the Color Glass Condensate effective theory of McLerran and Venugopalan (1993-1994) and the McLerran-Venugopalan model, using the Glasma Graph Approximation of Lappi (2017). The results enable analytic comparison with numerical simulations of glasma dynamics (e.g., Sun 2019, Boguslavski 2021, Ipp 2021) and provide a framework to assess the role of the pre-equilibrium glasma phase in jet quenching, which is typically attributed to the longer-lived quark-gluon plasma phase.
