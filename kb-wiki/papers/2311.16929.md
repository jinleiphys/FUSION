# Universality in Ground State Masses of Nuclei

> The beautiful and profound result that the first eigenvalue of Schroedinger operator can be interpreted as a large deviation of certain kind of Brownian motion leads to possible existence of universality in the distribution of ground state energies of quantal systems. Existence of such universality is explored in the distribution of the ground state energies of nuclei with Z $\ge$ 8 and N $\ge$ 8. Specifically, it has been demonstrated that the nuclear masses follow extreme-value statistics, implying that the nuclear ground state energies indeed can be treated as extreme values in the sense of the large deviation theory of Donsker and Varadhan.

## Key claim

The ground state energies of nuclei (for Z >= 8 and N >= 8) follow extreme-value statistics, specifically a Gumbel distribution, implying that nuclear masses can be treated as extreme values in the sense of the large deviation theory of Donsker and Varadhan. The authors note that the fitted Frechet distribution (with shape parameter xi = 0.13552) is statistically indistinguishable from the Gumbel distribution for this dataset.

## Method

The authors computed the difference (delta E) between experimental binding energies from the 2012 Atomic Mass Evaluation and liquid-drop model (LDM) binding energies for 2353 nuclei with Z >= 8 and N >= 8. They used the Shimazaki-Shinomoto procedure to determine an optimal bin width of about 1 MeV for the histogram of delta E values. The resulting probability distribution was fitted to both Gumbel and general extreme-value (GEV) distributions using Powell's conjugate direction method for chi-squared minimization, with statistical comparison via the Kolmogorov-Smirnov test.

## Key numbers

- 2353 nuclei with Z >= 8 and N >= 8 were analyzed using experimental masses from the 2012 Atomic Mass Evaluation
- Optimal bin width for the histogram of delta E was h ~ 1 MeV, determined by the Shimazaki-Shinomoto cost function minimization
- Gumbel distribution fit parameters: mu = -1.12445, beta = 1.78709, with rms deviation of 1.4 x 10^{-4}
- GEV (Frechet) distribution fit parameters: mu = -1.03173, beta = 1.79009, xi = 0.13552, with rms deviation of 1.2 x 10^{-4}
- Kolmogorov-Smirnov statistic D = 1.5 x 10^{-2} (P ~ 1) for comparing Gumbel and Frechet cumulative distributions, and D ~ 0.1 (P ~ 0.99) for comparing binned data to Gumbel distribution
- Mean of delta E from binned data: 0.003; variance: 6.319; skewness: 1.269; excess kurtosis: 2.144; Shannon entropy: 2.247

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Donsker and Varadhan (1975) on large deviations and principal eigenvalues to nuclear ground state masses, proposing a universality class for nuclear masses. It contrasts with the well-established Random Matrix Theory (RMT) description of nuclear energy level fluctuations (as developed by Guhr, Weidenmuller, and others), instead focusing on ground state energies as extreme values of the nuclear spectrum. The finding enables a new statistical approach to nuclear mass predictions and connects nuclear structure to extreme-value statistics, potentially allowing extrapolation to unknown nuclei based on the Gumbel distribution.
