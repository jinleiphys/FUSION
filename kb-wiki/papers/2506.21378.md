# Proto-neutron Stars with Dark Matter Admixture: A Single-Fluid Approach

> This work investigates the impact of dark matter (DM) on the microscopic and macroscopic properties of proto-neutron stars (PNSs). We employ a single-fluid framework in which DM interacts with ordinary matter (OM) via the Higgs portal and remains in thermal equilibrium through non-gravitational interactions. Using a quasi-static approximation, we analyze the evolution of PNSs during the Kelvin-Helmholtz phase by varying the DM mass while keeping the entropy per baryon and lepton fraction fixed. Our results show that DM absorbs thermal energy from the stellar medium without efficient re-emission, thereby altering neutrino emission and affecting the star's thermal evolution history. Furthermore, neutrinos contribute significantly to pressure support in the PNS phase, inhibiting DM mass accretion during neutrino-trapped stages. Based on the requirement to satisfy the observed $2,\rm M_\odot$ neutron star mass constraint and to maintain consistency with supernova remnant data, we suggest an upper limit of $m_χ\leq 0.62,\rm GeV$ for the DM mass that can accrete in evolving PNSs, within the model framework. In contrast, we established that cold neutron stars (NSs) can support higher DM masses without compromising equilibrium stability, owing to increased central density, enhanced gravitational binding energy, and reduced thermal pressure.

## Key claim

The paper claims that dark matter (DM) with masses up to 0.62 GeV can be admixed in proto-neutron stars (PNSs) while still satisfying the observed 2 solar mass neutron star constraint and remaining consistent with supernova remnant data, but this upper limit is specific to the DDME2 equation of state used and may vary with EoS stiffness and DM interaction strength.

## Method

The authors employ a single-fluid formalism where fermionic dark matter interacts with ordinary matter via the Higgs portal mechanism, using a relativistic mean-field model with density-dependent couplings (DDME2 parameterization) for the hadronic component. They apply a quasi-static approximation to study PNS evolution during the Kelvin-Helmholtz phase by fixing entropy per baryon and lepton fraction, integrating over the full DM momentum space while varying DM mass between 0.4 and 0.62 GeV for PNSs and between 1 and 2 GeV for cold stars. The equation of state is determined under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions for both neutrino-trapped and neutrino-transparent regimes.

## Key numbers

- Maximum DM mass upper limit for accreting in evolving PNSs: m_chi ≤ 0.62 GeV, based on satisfying the 2 solar mass neutron star constraint
- For PNS at s_B=1, Y_L,e=0.4 with m_chi=0.62 GeV: maximum mass = 2.27 solar masses, radius = 11.42 km, core temperature = 6.69 MeV
- For PNS at s_B=2, Y_L,e=0.2 with m_chi=0.62 GeV: maximum mass = 2.01 solar masses, radius = 10.47 km, core temperature = 14.27 MeV
- For cold stars (s_B=0.2, Y_nu_e=0) with m_chi=2 GeV: maximum mass = 2.27 solar masses, radius = 11.13 km, core temperature = 4.40 MeV
- For cold stars with no DM content at T=0: maximum mass = 2.49 solar masses, radius = 12.05 km
- Nuclear saturation density under DDME2: n_0 = 0.152 fm^-3
- Higgs-nucleon form factor fixed at f = 0.35; DM-Higgs coupling strength g_h = 0.07
- Spin-independent scattering cross-section: 9.70 × 10^-46 cm^2, consistent with XENON-1T, PandaX-II, PandaX-4T, and LUX constraints

## Context

This work extends the two-fluid framework of Issifu (2024) which studied PNSs admixed with DM through purely gravitational interactions, by instead incorporating non-gravitational Higgs portal interactions between DM and ordinary matter within a single-fluid formalism. The study enables comparison between different DM-OM interaction types and their influence on stellar properties, and provides constraints on DM mass that can be tested against future neutron star observations and supernova remnant data. The authors note that no prior work has investigated PNS evolution considering thermal equilibrium between DM and ordinary matter that consistently derives the EoS from the neutrino-rich phase to the neutrino-poor regime in the single-fluid formalism.
