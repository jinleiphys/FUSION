# Color Transfer in Associated Heavy-Quarkonium Production

> We study the production of heavy quarkonium in association with an additional heavy pair. We argue that important contributions may come from phase space regions where three heavy fermions are separated by relative velocities much lower than the speed of light, and to which standard effective field theories do not apply. In this region, infrared sensitive color exchange is specific to the presence of the unpaired (anti)quark. This effect vanishes as the motion of the additional particle becomes relativistic with respect to the pair, and is completely absent for massless quarks and gluons in the final state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in associated heavy-quarkonium production (e.g., J/psi with an additional heavy quark pair), a new "color transfer" mechanism exists that is infrared-divergent and cannot be absorbed into standard NRQCD coefficient functions or matrix elements, but this effect vanishes when the additional heavy particle moves relativistically with respect to the active pair and is completely absent for massless quarks and gluons in the final state.

## Method

The authors compute NLO virtual infrared corrections to e+e- annihilation into two heavy quark pairs, analyzing the pole structure of soft gluon exchange diagrams in dimensional regularization. They examine the imaginary parts of amplitudes involving gluon exchange between the active quark pair and a spectator heavy quark, expanding the resulting expressions in the relative velocity of the active pair and the spectator velocity beta_S to identify non-factorizing contributions.

## Key numbers

- The infrared pole contribution from gluon exchange between particles i and j scales as (alpha_s/2pi) * [-1/(2epsilon)] * [(1/beta_ij + beta_ij) * (2beta_ij - i*pi)] in dimensional regularization with epsilon = 2 - D/2
- The color transfer amplitude expansion gives a leading term proportional to (4/beta_S^2) * v * cos(phi_S), where v is the active pair relative velocity and beta_S is the spectator velocity
- At beta_S ~ 0.3, the estimated color transfer contribution becomes competitive with the color singlet cross section at the same beta_S
- The color transfer contribution is subleading in the number of colors N_c

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous factorization analysis for heavy quarkonium production (Nayak, Qiu, Sterman 2005, 2006) to the case of associated production with an additional heavy pair. It addresses data anomalies observed by the BELLE and Babar collaborations showing unexpectedly large rates for J/psi associated production with c-cbar pairs compared to J/psi with light hadrons, and complements leading-order NRQCD studies by Artoisenet, Lansberg, and Maltoni (2007) for hadroproduction and NLO calculations by Zhang and Chao (2007) for e+e- annihilation. The work enables development of new effective field theory operators incorporating three-field products to describe color transfer in associated production.
