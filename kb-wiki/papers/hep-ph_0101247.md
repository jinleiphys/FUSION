# The pionic width of the $ω(782)$ meson within a well-defined, unitary quantum field theory of (anti-)particles and (anti-)holes

> We investigate the indirect generation of the partial width of the $ω(782)$ meson with respect to the reaction $ω(782) \to ρ(770) π\toπ^+π^-π^0$ within the context of a ``Unitary Effective Resonance Model'' (UERM), which has previously been applied to resonant fermionic field operators, here extended to scalar, pseudoscalar and vector bosonic fields. The $ω(782)$ meson is described by a (quasi-)real field, while the intermediate $ρ(770)$ meson is considered to be a resonant degree of freedom, which can be treated consistently within the UERM. In the limit of an infinitesimal width (imaginary part of self-energy), the UERM yields a consistent treatment of relativistic quantum field theory (QFT). Some aspects of the UERM of (anti-)particles and (anti-)holes are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the indirect generation of the ω(782) meson's partial width for the cascade decay ω(782) → ρ(770)π → π⁺π⁻π⁰ can be consistently calculated within a unitary effective resonance model (UERM) that allows complex masses for resonant particles, but only under the condition that direct interactions between (anti-)particles and (anti-)holes are forbidden to avoid the "radiation catastrophe."

## Method

The authors extend the Unitary Effective Resonance Model (UERM), previously developed for fermionic resonances, to scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector bosonic fields by introducing "quasi-real" fields and their hermitian conjugates, along with corresponding hole and anti-hole degrees of freedom. They perform canonical quantization of these fields with complex mass M = m − iΓ/2, derive the Feynman propagators, and then apply the formalism to the ωρπ system using a lowest-order chiral Lagrangian based on the hidden-symmetry approach, calculating the ω → 3π decay width at tree level by treating the ρ meson as a resonant degree of freedom with complex mass.

## Key numbers

* The ρ meson mass is taken as m_ρ ≈ 760 MeV and the pion mass as m_π ≈ 140 MeV, with the ρ(770)π channel being closed for the ω(782) meson when using real masses.
* The pion decay constant is F_π ≈ 93 MeV, giving f = √2 F_π ≈ 132 MeV.
* The vector-meson mass relation from the hidden-symmetry Lagrangian gives m²_ρ ≈ m²_ω ≈ m²_V = 2 g² f² ≈ 0.60 GeV².
* The ρ meson is described with a complex mass M_ρ = m_ρ − i Γ_ρ/2, where Γ_ρ is the observed ρ decay width.

## Context

This work extends the Unitary Effective Resonance Model (UERM) developed by Kleefeld (1999) for fermionic baryonic resonances to bosonic systems, providing a field-theoretic framework that allows complex masses without violating unitarity. The approach builds on the unitarised meson model (NUMM) of van Beveren (1983), which found that the ρ(770)π channel is responsible for the correct central mass position of the ω(782) meson, and on the early work of Gell-Mann, Sharp, and Wagner (1962) who suggested the cascade mechanism. The formalism enables consistent treatment of resonant degrees of freedom in effective field theories and provides a new interpretation of anti-particles and holes that eliminates the need for a Dirac sea in the bosonic case.
