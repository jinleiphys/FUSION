# Coulomb Energy Density Functionals for Nuclear Systems: Recent Studies of Coulomb Exchange and Correlation Functionals

> The Coulomb exchange and correlation energy density functionals for electron systems are applied to nuclear systems. It is found that the exchange functionals in the generalized gradient approximation provide agreements with the exact-Fock energy with one adjustable parameter within a few dozen $ \mathrm{keV} $ accuracy, whereas the correlation functionals are not directly applicable to nuclear systems due to the existence of the nuclear force.

## Key claim

The Perdew-Burke-Ernzerhof generalized gradient approximation (PBE-GGA) Coulomb exchange functional, with an adjustable parameter λ multiplying the standard μ = 0.21951, can reproduce exact-Fock Coulomb exchange energies for atomic nuclei within a few dozen keV accuracy, but only if λ is tuned to the mass region (λ = 1.50 or more for light nuclei, λ = 1.25 for medium-heavy and heavy nuclei). The Coulomb correlation functionals from electron systems are not directly applicable to nuclear systems because the nuclear force dominates the total correlation energy, causing the Coulomb correlation contribution to have the opposite sign from what electron-system functionals predict.

## Method

Self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations were performed using the SAMi functional for the nuclear part, with the PBE-GGA Coulomb exchange functional replacing the exact-Fock term. The Coulomb correlation energy was evaluated in the local density approximation (LDA) using experimentally measured charge-density distributions from DeVries (1987) for selected nuclei. The free parameter μ in the PBE-GGA enhancement factor was multiplied by a factor λ, and the resulting Coulomb exchange energies were compared to exact-Fock calculations from Roca-Maza (2016).

## Key numbers

- Coulomb correlation energies in LDA for selected nuclei range from -0.05218 MeV (¹⁶O) to -0.5524 MeV (²⁰⁸Pb), all around 2% of the corresponding LDA exchange energies (e.g., 1.823% for ²⁰⁸Pb, 1.978% for ¹⁶O)
- The PBE-GGA exchange functional with λ = 1.25 reproduces exact-Fock results in medium-heavy and heavy mass regions, while λ = 1.50 or more is required for light nuclei
- The PBE-GGA exchange functional with λ = 1.00 reproduces exact-Fock results for the super-heavy nucleus ³¹⁰126
- Self-consistent calculations with the PBE-GGA exchange functional scale as O(N³), compared to O(N⁴) for exact-Fock calculations

## Context

This work extends the application of Coulomb energy density functionals from electron systems to nuclear systems, building on earlier studies by Naito (2018, 2019). The finding that correlation functionals from electron systems are not directly applicable to nuclei contradicts the earlier estimates by Bulgac and Shaginyan (1996, 1999), who predicted Coulomb correlation contributions of -40% to -20% of the exchange energy rather than the ~2% found here. The tuned PBE-GGA exchange functional enables more efficient self-consistent calculations for nuclear structure studies, particularly for heavy and super-heavy nuclei where exact-Fock calculations become computationally expensive.
