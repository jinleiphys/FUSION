# Baryon masses and pion-nucleon $σ$-term to second order in the quark masses

> We analyze the octet baryon masses and the pion--nucleon $σ$--term in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. In contrast to previous investigations, we include {\it all} terms up-to-and-including quadratic order in the light quark masses. The pertinent low--energy constants are fixed from resonance exchange. This leaves as the only free parameter the baryon mass in the chiral limit, $\krig{m}$. We find $\krig{m} = 749 \pm 125$~MeV together with $σ_{πN} (0) = 48 \pm 10$ ~MeV. We discuss various implications of these results.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including all terms up to quadratic order in the light quark masses in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, with low-energy constants fixed by resonance exchange, yields a baryon mass in the chiral limit of 749 ± 125 MeV and a pion-nucleon sigma-term of 48 ± 10 MeV; however, the fit cannot simultaneously reproduce all four octet baryon masses exactly, so the chiral-limit mass is obtained by averaging the individual fits.

## Method

The authors performed a complete one-loop calculation of the octet baryon masses (N, Λ, Σ, Ξ) and the pion-nucleon σ-term in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, including all terms up to order m_q^2 (quadratic in the light quark masses). The 20 unknown low-energy constants appearing in the dimension-two and dimension-four meson-baryon Lagrangians were fixed using the resonance saturation principle, primarily from spin-3/2 decuplet excitation and scalar meson exchange, leaving the baryon mass in the chiral limit as the only free parameter.

## Key numbers

- Baryon mass in the chiral limit: 749 ± 125 MeV (average from individual fits: m_N = 711 MeV, m_Λ = 679 MeV, m_Σ = 877 MeV, m_Ξ = 728 MeV)
- Pion-nucleon σ-term at zero momentum transfer: σ_πN(0) = 48 ± 10 MeV
- Nucleon mass decomposition: m_N = 711 MeV (chiral limit) + 202 MeV (order m_q) - 272 MeV (order m_q^3/2) + 298 MeV (order m_q^2) = 939 MeV
- σ_πN(0) decomposition: 54 MeV (order q^2) - 33 MeV (order q^3) + 27 MeV (order q^4) = 48 MeV
- Gell-Mann-Okubo deviation: Δ_GMO = 31 MeV (empirical value is about 6.5 MeV)
- Decuplet mass used: m_Δ = 1.455 GeV
- Pseudoscalar decay constant: F_P = 100 MeV
- Axial coupling constraint: F + D = g_A = 1.25

## Context

This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory calculations of baryon masses, such as those by Lang and Pagels (1970s) and Borasoy, Meißner, Kaiser, and Weise (1990s), which only included the leading non-analytic corrections (order m_q^3/2) or partial m_q^2 terms. By including all terms to quadratic order in quark masses and fixing constants via resonance saturation rather than fitting, the authors resolve the ambiguity between the chiral-limit mass and the low-energy constant b_0 that plagued earlier analyses. The results enable future determination of the quark mass ratio R = (m_d - m_u)/(m_s - m̂) from baryon masses once electromagnetic corrections are included, though the current accuracy is insufficient for that purpose.
