# Short-range nuclear forces in singlet channels

> Continuing our effort to build a consistent power counting for chiral nuclear effective field theory (EFT), we discuss the subleading contact interactions, or counterterms, in the singlet channels of nucleon-nucleon scattering, with renormalization group invariance as the constraint. We argue that the rather large cutoff error of the leading amplitude requires O(Q) of the EFT expansion to be nonvanishing, contrary to Weinberg's original power counting. This, together with the ultraviolet divergences of two-pion exchanges in the distorted-wave expansion, leads to enhancement of the 1S0 counterterms and results in a pionless-theory-like power counting for the singlet channels.

## Key claim

The authors argue that, contrary to Weinberg's original power counting, the leading-order amplitude in the \(^1S_0\) channel has a cutoff error of order \(Q\) rather than \(Q^2\), which forces the next-to-leading order (NLO) contact interactions to be nonvanishing and leads to a pionless-theory-like power counting for singlet channels.

## Method

The authors analyze the renormalization group (RG) invariance of nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes in singlet channels within chiral effective field theory (EFT). They classify subleading contact interactions into three categories (residual, primordial, and distorted-wave counterterms) and study the ultraviolet divergences of two-pion exchanges in the distorted-wave expansion. The LO amplitude is constructed by resumming one-pion exchange and a constant counterterm, and subleading orders are treated as perturbations on top of the LO T-matrix.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon coupling constant is \(g_A = 1.26\) and the pion decay constant is \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV.
- The parameter \(\alpha_\pi \equiv g_A^2 m_N / 16\pi f_\pi^2 \sim (290 \text{ MeV})^{-1}\).
- The LO cutoff error in \(^1S_0\) scales as \(\mathcal{O}(k^2 / m_{\text{lo}} \Lambda)\), where \(m_{\text{lo}} \sim \alpha_\pi^{-1}\).
- The renormalized two-derivative counterterm \(D_R\) scales as \(D_R \sim (4\pi/m_N)(1/m_{\text{lo}}^2 m_{\text{hi}})\).
- The distorted-wave counterterm for two-pion exchanges in \(^1S_0\) requires a four-derivative operator \(E^{(0)} p'^2 p^2\) to cancel divergences proportional to \(\rho_2 k^4 \ln \Lambda\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of triplet channels (Long 2011, Long 2011) and addresses inconsistencies in Weinberg's original power counting (Weinberg 1990-1991) for singlet channels. The analysis builds on earlier RG studies by Kaplan (1996), Beane (2000, 2001), Nogga (2005), and Birse (2005), and specifically reproduces the distorted-wave enhancement of \(^1S_0\) subleading counterterms first shown by Valderrama (2009). The results enable a consistent power counting for chiral nuclear EFT that respects RG invariance across both singlet and triplet channels.
