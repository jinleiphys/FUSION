# Detecting a First-Order Transition in the QCD Phase Diagram with Baryon-Baryon Correlations

> We suggest baryon-baryon correlations as an experimentally accessible signature for a first-order phase transition between a baryon-rich phase, like quarkyonic, and a baryon-suppressed hadronic phase in the QCD phase diagram. We examine the consequences of baryon-rich bubble formation in an expanding medium and show how the two-particle correlations vary in the transverse and longitudinal direction depending on the strength of the radial flow, the bubble temperature, and the time when the baryons are emitted.

## Key claim

Baryon-baryon correlations in azimuth and rapidity can serve as an experimentally accessible signature for a first-order phase transition between a baryon-rich phase (such as quarkyonic matter) and a baryon-suppressed hadronic phase, provided that bubble nucleation occurs and that the bubbles decay late enough in the system evolution for radial flow to convert spatial inhomogeneities into momentum-space correlations.

## Method

The authors constructed a Bubble Monte-Carlo Blast-Wave Model with two components: a spatial Monte-Carlo that distributes baryons into bubbles (with a Gaussian distribution of baryons per bubble) using either a center-weighted or surface-weighted transverse source function, and a blast-wave Monte-Carlo that assigns radial boosts and thermal momenta to the emitted baryons. They then computed the two-particle correlation measure Δρ/√ρ_ref as a function of azimuthal angle difference Δφ and rapidity difference Δy, varying the bubble temperature T, average radial flow velocity ⟨β⟩, number of baryons per bubble n_B, decay time τ_decay, and source geometry.

## Key numbers

- For n_B = 2, the Gaussian amplitude of the azimuthal correlation is 1.3; for n_B = 8, the amplitude is 8.9 (Surface Source, T = 100 MeV, ⟨β⟩ = 0.75).
- The r.m.s. width σ of the azimuthal correlation varies with temperature as σ = 0.694 − 0.00639T + 0.0000435T² for T from 120 MeV to 180 MeV (⟨β⟩ = 0.75, n_B = 8).
- For the Center Weighted source, σ = 1.317 − 1.103β; for the Surface Weighted source, σ = 1.357 − 1.367β, valid for 0.29 < β < 0.75.
- The width in rapidity as a function of decay time is σ_y = 0.37 + 0.47τ^(−1.58) (τ in fm).
- For T = 100 MeV and ⟨β⟩ = 0.55, N_B(v₂{2}² − v₂{4}²) ≈ 5 for n_B = 8 and ≈ 2.7 for n_B = 4; increasing T to 180 MeV with n_B = 4 reduces this to ≈ 0.37.
- The HBT correlation C(k*) from bubbles with n_B = 8 has a Gaussian RMS width of 750 MeV and an amplitude of 8%.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Voloshin (2004) that coordinate-space correlations can be transferred to momentum-space through radial flow, and the discussion of baryon number fluctuations by Gavin (2004) which did not consider radial expansion or azimuthal correlations. It is motivated by the large-N_c arguments of McLerran and Pisarski (2007) and the subsequent work by Hidaka, McLerran, and Pisarski (2008) identifying baryon number as an order parameter for the transition to quarkyonic matter. The bubble nucleation scenario is analogous to that proposed by Witten (1984) for strangelet condensation in the early universe, and dynamical calculations by Paech and Dumitru (2005) showed that baryon inhomogeneities can form. The results enable experimental searches for a first-order phase transition in the QCD phase diagram using baryon-baryon correlation measurements at RHIC and future facilities, and suggest that the absence of such correlations would either rule out a first-order transition near freeze-out or constrain its location relative to the freeze-out curve.
