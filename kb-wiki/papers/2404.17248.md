# Bayesian and Principal Component Analyses of Neutron Star Properties

> A Bayesian method is used in this extensive work to generate a large set of minimally constrained equations of state (EOSs) for matters in neutron stars (NS). These EOSs are analyzed for their correlations with key NS properties, such as the tidal deformability, radius, and maximum mass, within the mass range of $1.2-2M_\odot$. The observed connections between the pressure of $β$-equilibrated matter and the properties of neutron stars at different densities offer significant insights into the behavior of NS matter in a nearly model-independent manner. The study also examines the influence of various factors on the correlation of symmetry energy parameters, such as slope and curvature parameters at saturation density ($ρ_0=0.16 ~\text{fm}^{-3}$) with the tidal deformability and radius of neutron stars. This study investigates the robustness of the observed correlations by considering the distributions and interdependence of symmetry energy parameters. Furthermore, the utilization of Principal Component Analysis (PCA) is employed to unveil the complicated relationship between various nuclear matter parameters and properties of neutron stars. This analysis highlights the importance of employing multivariate analysis techniques in order to comprehend the variety in tidal deformability and radius observed across distinct masses of NS. This comprehensive study aims to establish a connection between the parameters of nuclear matter and the properties of neutron stars, providing significant insights into the behavior of NS matter across different circumstances.

## Key claim

Using a Bayesian approach to generate minimally constrained equations of state, the paper finds that correlations between neutron star properties (tidal deformability, radius) and nuclear matter parameters are highly sensitive to the choice of distributions of symmetry energy parameters and their interdependence, rather than being robust model-independent relations.

## Method

The author generates a large set of equations of state for neutron star matter using a Bayesian method with minimal constraints, then analyzes correlations between the pressure of β-equilibrated matter and neutron star properties (tidal deformability, radius, maximum mass) across the mass range 1.2–2 M⊙. Principal Component Analysis (PCA) is employed as a multivariate statistical tool to uncover relationships between multiple nuclear matter parameters (including symmetry energy slope L0 and curvature Ksym,0 at saturation density ρ0 = 0.16 fm−3) and the tidal deformability and radius of neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: ρ0 = 0.16 fm−3
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: e0 = −15.88 ± 0.24 MeV
- Nuclear incompressibility: K0 ≈ 240 ± 20 MeV
- Symmetry energy coefficient at saturation: J0 = 32.10 ± 0.31 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope parameter: L0 = 53 ± 15 MeV (from combined astronomical and PREX-II analysis)
- Symmetry energy curvature parameter: Ksym,0 = −111.8 ± 71.8 MeV
- Tidal deformability for 1.4 M⊙ neutron star from GW170817: Λ1.4 = 190+390−120 at 90% confidence
- Radius of PSR J0030+0451: R = 13.02+1.24−1.06 km for mass 1.44+0.15−0.14 M⊙ (Miller 2019) and R = 12.71+1.14−1.19 km for mass 1.34+0.15−0.16 M⊙ (Riley 2019)
- Radius of PSR J0740+6620: R = 13.7+2.6−1.5 km with mass 2.08 ± 0.07 M⊙ (Miller 2021) and R = 12.39+1.30−0.98 km with mass 2.072+0.067−0.066 M⊙ (Riley 2021)
- Lower bound on maximum NS mass from PSR J0952-0607: Mmax = 2.35 ± 0.17 M⊙
- Contributions from iso-vector nuclear matter parameters to tidal deformability and radius decrease by approximately 25% as neutron star mass increases from 1.2 M⊙ to 1.8 M⊙

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Lattimer (2000) showing radius correlations with pressure at 1–2ρ0, and by Tsang (2019, 2020) on tidal deformability correlations with pressure at 2ρ0. It addresses the unresolved question from Carson (2018), Malik (2018), and Kunjipurayil (2022) regarding which nuclear matter parameters most strongly influence neutron star properties at canonical mass 1.4 M⊙. The PCA approach enables disentangling the complex multivariate relationships between nuclear matter parameters and neutron star observables, potentially guiding future Bayesian inference analyses of neutron star structure.
