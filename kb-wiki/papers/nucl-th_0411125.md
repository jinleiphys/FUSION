# Canonical Strangeness and Distillation Effects in Hadron Production

> Strangeness canonical ensemble for Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics is reconsidered for excited nuclear systems with non-vanishing net strangeness. A new recurrence relation method is applied to find the partition function. The method is first generalized to the case of quantum strangeness canonical ensemble. Uncertainties in calculation of the K+/pi+ excitation function are discussed. A new scenario based on the strangeness distillation effect is put forward for a possible explanation of anomalous strangeness production observed at the bombarding energy near 30 AGeV. The peaked maximum in the K+/pi+ ratio is considered as a sign of the critical end-point reached in evolution of the system rather than a latent heat jump emerging from the onset of the first order deconfinement phase transition.

# Canonical Strangeness and Distillation Effects in Hadron Production

## Key claim

The peaked maximum in the K+/π+ excitation function near 30 AGeV bombarding energy is considered as a sign of the critical end-point reached in evolution of the system rather than a latent heat jump emerging from the onset of the first order deconfinement phase transition, based on a strangeness distillation scenario where the hadronic subsystem carries positive net strangeness while the quark subsystem carries negative net strangeness.

## Method

The authors reconsider the strangeness canonical ensemble for Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics with non-vanishing net strangeness S, applying both a direct integral method and a new recurrence relation method to evaluate the partition function. They generalize the recurrence relation method to quantum statistics (Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein) for the strangeness canonical ensemble. The derived formulae are used to calculate the K+/π+ excitation function along an empirical freeze-out curve defined by temperature T and baryon chemical potential μB, with hadron yields including contributions from strong decays of heavier resonances.

## Key numbers

- The freeze-out curve parametrization: μB [MeV] = 1270/(1 + √s/4.3) and T [MeV] = 170/(1 + e^{-0.48(√s - 3.8)}), where √s is in GeV
- For s_max=1 assumption (only single-strange particles), the K+/π+ ratio decreases by about 5% at colliding energy √s ~ 8 GeV compared to the full calculation
- The strangeness correlation volume is taken as a sphere with radius R ~ 7 fm for the constant-volume calculations
- For the thermodynamically consistent calculation with baryon charge B=364 and electric charge Q=142 (5% centrality selection), the system volume exhibits a minimum near √s ~ 10 GeV
- The critical end-point from lattice QCD is cited at μB = 360 ± 40 MeV and T = 162 ± 2 MeV, corresponding to bombarding energy ~ 30 GeV
- Weak decays of hadrons with lifetime τ < 10^{-10} sec decrease the K+/π+ ratio by 15% without changing the shape of the excitation function

## Context

This work extends the canonical strangeness treatment of Cleymans and Redlich (1999) and Hamieh, Redlich, and Tounsi (2000) from the global strangeness neutrality condition S=0 to systems with non-vanishing net strangeness. The recurrence relation method was previously applied to nuclear multifragmentation by Parvan and Toneev (2000) and Parvan (2004). The paper proposes a new scenario for the anomalous strangeness production observed near 30 AGeV, contrasting with the interpretation of Gazdzicki and Gorenstein (1999) as a first-order deconfinement phase transition. The strangeness distillation effect was first noted by Stocker, Greiner, and Sahm (1986) in the context of Gibbs phase coexistence in baryon-rich quark-gluon plasma. The results enable further dynamical calculations to test whether the critical end-point signature can be distinguished from a first-order phase transition signal.
