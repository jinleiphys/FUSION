# Proton-proton scattering on a quantum computer

> Scattering of charged particles is ubiquitous in nuclear physics. We calculate the proton-proton $s$-wave phase shift at low energy relevant to solar physics. The phase shift is calculated from the ratio of the regular and irregular solutions to the radial Schrödinger equation on a hard spherical wall boundary for the ground state. The ground state energy is calculated using a hybrid quantum-classical variational algorithm. A theory with short-ranged nuclear interaction in the presence of the long-ranged Coulomb force is used to describe the scattering. The theory is discretized on a spatial lattice for adaptation to the quantum computer in the second quantized language. The phase shifts at low momenta are accurately reproduced.

# Proton-proton scattering on a quantum computer

## Key claim

The authors calculate the proton-proton s-wave phase shift at low momentum using a hybrid quantum-classical algorithm, but the calculation is limited to leading-order effective field theory with a short-ranged momentum-independent interaction and the long-range Coulomb force, and the phase shifts are accurately reproduced only at low momenta where lattice discretization artifacts are small.

## Method

The authors discretize the one-dimensional radial Schrödinger equation for two protons on a spatial lattice and impose a hard spherical wall boundary condition at a large radial distance. The ground state energy of the system is computed using a hybrid quantum-classical variational algorithm (the Gauss-Siedel relaxation method) on the IBMQ-Brisbane quantum processor, where only the initial energy expectation value is obtained from quantum measurements and subsequent iterations are performed classically. The s-wave phase shift is then extracted from the ratio of regular and irregular Coulomb wave functions evaluated at the wall radius using the ground state momentum. Schmidt decomposition reduces all quantum measurements to at most two-qubit circuits.

## Key numbers

- Reduced mass of two protons: μ = 469.15 MeV (in natural units ħ = c = 1)
- Fine-structure constant: α_EM = 1/137
- Proton-proton scattering length: a_0 = -7.81 fm
- Lattice spacings used: b = 2 fm, 1 fm, and 0.75 fm
- Number of quantum measurements (shots) per Pauli string: 5000 (and 20000 for one set of ideal simulations)
- Phase shift data shown near p ∼ 60 MeV for b = 1 fm shows slight deviation above the analytical result
- About 30 iterations of the relaxation method provide sufficient accuracy (200 iterations performed for results shown)

## Context

This work extends prior classical lattice effective field theory calculations of proton-proton scattering (Rupak, 2014) to quantum computation, and follows similar approaches for neutral particle scattering on quantum computers (Sharma, 2023; Wang, 2024; Turro, 2024). The spherical wall method for phase shift calculation was introduced by Carlson (1984) and Borasoy (2007) and has been successfully applied in classical lattice EFT calculations for proton fusion (Rupak, 2014), proton-deuteron scattering (Elhatisari, 2016), and alpha-alpha scattering (Elhatisari, 2015). The relaxation method used here was previously applied to two-particle systems in a harmonic trap (Yusf, 2024). This work enables future quantum computation of inelastic reactions such as proton-proton fusion, where the Rabi oscillation method proposed by Bedaque (2022) could be applied.
