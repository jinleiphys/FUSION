# Coherent QCD multiple scattering in proton-nucleus collisions

> We argue that high energy proton-nucleus (p+A) collisions provide an excellent laboratory for studying nuclear size enhanced parton multiple scattering where power corrections to the leading twist perturbative QCD factorization approach can be systematically computed. We identify and resum these corrections and calculate the centrality and rapidity dependent nuclear suppression of single and double inclusive hadron production at moderate transverse momenta. We demonstrate that both spectra and dihadron correlations in p+A reactions are sensitive measures of such dynamical nuclear attenuation effects

## Key claim

Coherent QCD multiple scattering, where a parton interacts with several nucleons simultaneously due to an extended longitudinal interaction length, produces nuclear-size-enhanced power corrections to the leading-twist perturbative QCD factorization approach that can be systematically computed and resummed, leading to a dynamical suppression of hadron production in proton-nucleus collisions at moderate transverse momenta that grows with rapidity and centrality.

## Method

The authors identify and resum all contributions from coherent multiple scattering of the struck parton with N nucleons in proton-nucleus collisions, expressed as two-gluon exchange diagrams. They derive the nuclear-modified cross sections for single and double inclusive hadron production by replacing the function \(F_{ab\rightarrow cd}(x_b)\) in the leading-twist formulas with \(F_{ab\rightarrow cd}\left(x_b \left[ 1+ C_d \frac{\xi^2}{-t} (A^{1/3}-1) \right]\right)\), where \(\xi^2\) is the scale of power corrections, \(C_d\) is a color factor, and \(t\) is the hard scale. The centrality dependence is obtained through the nuclear thickness function \(T_A(b)\).

## Key numbers

* The scale of power corrections is \(\xi^2 = 0.09 - 0.12\) GeV\(^2\), derived from the matrix element \(\langle p|\hat{F}^2|p\rangle = \frac{1}{2} \lim_{x\rightarrow 0}xG(x,Q^2)\).
* The color factor for quarks and antiquarks is \(C_{q(\bar{q})}=1\) and for gluons is \(C_g=C_A/C_F=9/4\).
* For d+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{S}=200\) GeV, forward rapidity \(y_1=4\) hadron production at \(p_{T_1}=1\) GeV corresponds to nuclear \(x_b \geq 1.3 \times 10^{-4}\).
* The elastic scattering power of cold nuclear matter in minimum bias d+Au collisions is characterized by \(\mu^2 L_{\rm eff} /\lambda = 0.72\) GeV\(^2\).
* At small transverse momenta \(p_{T_1}=1.5\) GeV, \(p_{T_2}=1\) GeV, going from midrapidity to forward rapidity (\(y_1=4, y_2=0\)) yields a reduction by a factor of 3-4 in the strength of dihadron correlations from nuclear enhanced power corrections.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic calculation of coherent multiple scattering in deeply inelastic scattering (Qiu 2003) to hadronic collisions, specifically proton-nucleus reactions. The results complement the effects of leading twist nuclear shadowing from modified nuclear parton distribution functions (Frankfurt 2003) and the Cronin effect from elastic multiple scattering (Vitev 2003). The approach enables perturbative QCD calculations at relatively small transverse momenta and bridges the gap between the parton model and possible gluon saturation (Gyulassy 2004, McLerran 1993), with predictions testable against RHIC data from BRAHMS, PHENIX, and STAR.
