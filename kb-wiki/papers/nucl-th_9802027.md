# Cascade-Exciton Model Analysis of Nucleon-Induced Fission Cross Sections of Lead and Bismuth at Energies from 45 to 500 MeV

> An extended version of the Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM) of nuclear reactions is applied to analyze nucleon-induced fission cross sections for Bi-209 and Pb-208 nuclei in the 45-500 MeV energy range. The available data on linear momentum transfer are analyzed as well. The results are compared with analytical approximations resulting from a comparative critical analysis of all available experimental data. Systematic discrepancies between calculations and experimental data are revealed. A modification of the CEM is proposed, which significantly improves the model predictions for projectile energies above 100 MeV.

## Key claim

The Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM) as implemented in the code CEM95 systematically underestimates nucleon-induced fission cross sections for lead and bismuth in the 70-100 MeV range and overestimates them above 100-150 MeV and below 50-60 MeV, with discrepancies growing larger as projectile energy increases above 100 MeV; a modification of the model is proposed to improve predictions above 100 MeV.

## Method

The authors applied the extended Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM95) to calculate proton- and neutron-induced fission cross sections for 209Bi and 208Pb targets at incident energies from 45 to 500 MeV, using a statistical weight method with 3000 inelastic events per calculation to achieve statistical uncertainties of 5-10%. They compared calculated fission cross sections and linear momentum transfer (LMT) distributions against experimental data and analytical approximations derived from a critical ranking of all available experimental datasets, assigning reliability grades based on experimental technique quality. A sensitivity study was performed by varying input parameters including the level density parameter ratio a_f/a_n, fission barrier models, level density systematics, and shell correction systematics.

## Key numbers

- The ratio a_f/a_n for 209Bi(p,f) was set to 1.21, for 209Bi(n,f) to 1.20, for 208Pb(p,f) to 1.19, and for 208Pb(n,f) to 1.20 in the 45-160 MeV region
- A variation of a_f/a_n by +/- 0.03 (about 2.5%) from the optimal value of 1.21 changes the 209Bi(p,f) cross section by a factor of 1.5-2
- The dispersion of the LMT distribution for 209Bi(p,f) at 96 MeV was calculated as 0.38 (corrected for prompt neutron emission) compared to the experimental value of 0.36 +/- 0.02 from Stephan (1965)
- The dispersion of the LMT distribution for 209Bi(p,f) at 160 MeV was calculated as 0.38 (corrected) compared to the experimental value of 0.38 +/- 0.02 from Kowalski (1963) at 156 MeV
- The natPb/208Pb (n,f) cross section ratio was determined to be 1.43 +/- 0.13 in the 45-160 MeV energy region
- The discrepancy between Fukahori's (n,f) approximations and the present work reaches up to a factor of 2 in the 50-500 MeV region
- The discrepancy for (p,f) cross section approximations is no more than 35%

## Context

This work extends the Cascade-Exciton Model, previously applied mainly to actinide fission by Konshin (1997), to pre-actinide nuclei (lead and bismuth) for the first time, revealing systematic discrepancies that were not apparent in earlier applications. The study provides new analytical approximations for 208Pb(n,f) cross sections (previously unpublished) and improved approximations for 209Bi(p,f), 209Bi(n,f), and 208Pb(p,f) based on a critical ranking of experimental data, superseding the earlier ENDF/B-VI evaluations by Fukahori and Pearlstein (1991) which show discrepancies up to a factor of 2. The results enable more reliable nuclear data evaluations for accelerator-driven systems (transmutation, energy production, tritium production) that may use massive lead or lead-bismuth targets, and identify the need for model improvements at energies above 100 MeV where the current CEM95 predictions become progressively worse.
