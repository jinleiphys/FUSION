# Microscopic analysis of two-body correlations in light nuclei

> Within a nonperturbative dynamical two-body approach - based on coupled equations of motion for the one-body density matrix and the two-body correlation function - we study the distribution of occupation numbers in a correlated system close to the groundstate, the relaxation of single-particle excitations and the damping of collective modes. For this purpose the nonlinear equations of motion are solved numerically within a finite oscillator basis for the first time adopting short-range repulsive and long-range attractive two-body forces. We find in all cases that the formation of long- and short-range correlations and their mixing is related to the long- and short-range part of the nucleon-nucleon interaction which dominate the resummation of loop or ladder diagrams, respectively. However, the proper description of relaxation or damping phenomena is found to require both types of diagrams as well as the mixed terms simultaneously.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that short-range correlations are governed by the particle-particle (ladder) channel and long-range correlations by the particle-hole (loop) channel, but that a proper description of relaxation and damping phenomena requires both channels and their mixed terms simultaneously, not just one type.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled nonperturbative equations of motion for the one-body density matrix and the two-body correlation function numerically within a finite harmonic oscillator basis for light nuclei. They adopted both a short-range contact interaction (delta-force) and a long-range Yukawa potential consisting of repulsive omega-exchange and attractive pi-exchange terms, and compared results from approximations that included only the pp-channel (TDGMT), only the ph-channel (RPA), both channels (NQCD), and the full trace-conserving scheme (SCD).

## Key numbers

- For the contact interaction with V0 = -220 MeV fm^3, the damping width gamma for a single-particle excitation about 13 MeV above the Fermi surface in a 20Ne-like nucleus was largest in the NQCD calculation, while restriction to only pp or ph channels gave longer equilibration times.
- With the Yukawa potential, the RPA approach gave a damping width gamma about a factor of 4 larger than in the TDGMT and BORN approximations, and still considerably larger than the NQCD value.
- The incompressibility K for 16O ranged from 91.30 MeV (for density-dependence parameter alpha_DD = 0.00) to 310.25 MeV (for alpha_DD = 0.30), with corresponding coupling constants V0pi from -5.1580 to -5.7434 and V0omega from 49.9002 to 60.7640.
- Initialization with T = 0 MeV corresponded to a 40Ca nucleus with a realistic temperature of 0.7 MeV, i.e. a thermal excitation energy E* less than 2.5 MeV above the true groundstate.
- The trace conservation in the SCD limit was improved numerically by about two orders of magnitude compared to previous work, and was tested on a much longer time scale (up to about 120 x 10^-23 s).

## Context

This work extends earlier time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM) calculations by Gong (1990) and others that used only contact interactions, by implementing finite-range nucleon-nucleon potentials for the first time in this framework. The approach builds on the BBGKY hierarchy and the coupled equations for the one-body density matrix and two-body correlation function developed by Cassing and Pfitzner (1990) and others. The results enable systematic studies of how the range of the nuclear force determines which correlation channel dominates, and provide a foundation for future investigations of groundstate correlations, single-particle damping, and collective mode decay in light nuclei using realistic interactions.
