# Parton rescattering and screening in Au+Au collisions at RHIC

> We study the microscopic dynamics of quarks and gluons in relativistic heavy ion collisions in the framework of the Parton Cascade Model. We use lowest order perturbative QCD cross sections with fixed lower momentum cutoff p_0. We calculate the time-evolution of the Debye-screening mass for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV per nucleon pair. The screening mass is used to determine a lower limit for the allowed range of p_0. We also determine the energy density reached through hard and semi-hard processes at RHIC, obtain a lower bound for the rapidity density of charged hadrons produced by semihard interactions, and analyze the extent of perturbative rescattering among partons.

## Key claim

The paper uses the Parton Cascade Model (PCM) to show that the low-momentum cutoff \(p_T^{\rm min}\) in perturbative QCD calculations for Au+Au collisions at RHIC must satisfy \(p_T^{\rm min} \ge \mu_D\) (the Debye screening mass), yielding a lower bound of approximately 0.8 GeV; this condition is necessary for internal consistency of the model, but the authors note that higher-order radiative corrections and parton fusion processes will likely alter this value.

## Method

The authors implemented a parton cascade model (VNI/BMS) using leading-order perturbative QCD two-body scattering cross sections with a fixed low-momentum cutoff \(p_T^{\rm min}\) to regularize infrared divergences. They calculated the time evolution of the Debye screening mass \(\mu_D\) from the parton phase-space distributions using the one-loop perturbative QCD formula, and determined the allowed range of \(p_T^{\rm min}\) by requiring \(p_T^{\rm min} \ge \mu_D\) at \(\tau = 1/p_T^{\rm min}\). They also computed the energy density, rapidity density of produced partons, and collision rates for central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV per nucleon pair.

## Key numbers

- The smallest allowed value for \(p_T^{\rm min}\) is approximately 0.8 GeV, determined from the condition \(p_T^{\rm min} \ge \mu_D\) at \(\tau = 1/p_T^{\rm min}\) for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV per nucleon pair.
- The maximum energy density obtained in Au+Au collisions in the PCM approach is on the order of 100 GeV/fm\(^3\), with a maximum below approximately 70 GeV/fm\(^3\) for the allowed \(p_T^{\rm min}\) range.
- For \(p_T^{\rm min}=0.7\) GeV, the rapidity density of produced partons at mid-rapidity is approximately 650, which would translate to approximately 430 charged hadrons under parton-hadron duality and entropy conservation.
- The transverse screening mass \(\mu_D^\perp\) differs from the longitudinal screening mass \(\mu_D^{||}\) by at most 10%.

## Context

This work extends the Parton Cascade Model originally formulated by Geiger (VNI) and addresses the infrared divergence problem by using the Debye screening mass to set a self-consistency condition on \(p_T^{\rm min}\), building on prior work by Biro, Muller, and Wang (1993) on screening in partonic matter. The authors note that many previous PCM implementations (Zhang, Molnar, AMPT, Gromit) assume a time-independent screening mass, whereas this work finds a strong time dependence that affects equilibration dynamics. The results provide a lower bound on charged hadron multiplicity from hard and semi-hard processes that must be compatible with experimental data from RHIC, and the analysis of multiple parton rescattering sets the stage for identifying observables sensitive to perturbative rescattering in future work.
