# Coordinate-space Faddeev-Hahn-type approach to three-body charge transfer reactions involving exotic particles

> Low-energy muon-transfer cross sections and rates in collisions of muonic atoms with hydrogen isotopes are calculated using a six-state close-coupling approximation to coordinate-space Faddeev-Hahn-type equations. In the muonic case satisfactory results are obtained for all hydrogen isotopes and the experimentaly observed strong isotopic dependence of transfer rates is also reproduced. A comparison with results of other theoretical and available experimental works is presented. The present model also leads to good transfer cross sections in the well-understood problem of antihydrogen formation in antiproton-positronium collision.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a six-state close-coupling approximation to coordinate-space Faddeev-Hahn-type equations yields satisfactory muon-transfer cross sections and rates for all hydrogen isotopes and reproduces the experimentally observed strong isotopic dependence, but only for energies below 0.1 eV where the transfer rates become essentially constant; the method also gives reasonable antihydrogen formation cross sections below 1 eV, but agreement worsens at 2 eV.

## Method

The authors solve a set of two coupled Faddeev-Hahn integro-differential equations in coordinate space for the three-body wave function components, each carrying the asymptotic boundary condition for a specific two-cluster configuration. They expand the wave function components in terms of bound-state eigenfunctions (1s, 2s, 2p) of the subsystem Hamiltonians in the initial and final channels, project the equations onto these states, perform a partial-wave projection retaining only the lowest partial wave (L=0), and solve the resulting one-dimensional integro-differential equations numerically by discretization and Gauss elimination.

## Key numbers

- Muon transfer rate for D–Hμ system: 133×10⁸ s⁻¹ (six-state approximation, low-energy limit below 0.1 eV)
- Muon transfer rate for T–Hμ system: 61×10⁸ s⁻¹ (six-state approximation, low-energy limit)
- Muon transfer rate for T–Dμ system: 2.3×10⁸ s⁻¹ (six-state approximation, low-energy limit)
- Mass values used (in electron mass units): m_H = 1836.152, m_D = 3670.481, m_T = 5496.918, m_μ = 206.769
- Liquid hydrogen density N₀ = 4.25×10²² cm⁻³ used in transfer rate formula λ_tr = σ_tr v N₀
- Numerical grid: distances up to 50 a_μ (muonic atomic units) with 400–600 discretization points

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev-Hahn formalism (Hahn 1968, 1972) to muon-transfer reactions in the muon-catalyzed fusion cycle, where prior theoretical studies by Cohen (1991), Adam (1992), Fukuda (1990), Kamimura (1993), Igarashi (1994), and Lec (1997) showed discrepancies with experimental data from Dzhelepov (1962), Bleser (1963), Bertin (1972), Mulhauser (1996), and others. The method also tests antihydrogen formation in antiproton-positronium collisions, comparing with hyperspherical coupled-channel calculations by Toshima (1994) and close-coupling approximations by Mitroy (1997). The approach enables future study of muon-transfer reactions to elements with Z ≥ 2, where Coulomb repulsion in the final state introduces additional theoretical challenges.
