# Effects of weak magnetic field and finite chemical potential on the transport of charge and heat in hot QCD matter

> We have studied the effects of weak magnetic field and finite chemical potential on the transport of charge and heat in hot QCD matter by estimating their respective response functions, such as the electrical conductivity ($σ_{\rm el}$), the Hall conductivity ($σ_{\rm H}$), the thermal conductivity ($κ_0$) and the Hall-type thermal conductivity ($κ_1$). The expressions of charge and heat transport coefficients are obtained by solving the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation at weak magnetic field and finite chemical potential. The interactions among partons are incorporated through their thermal masses. We have observed that $σ_{\rm el}$ and $κ_0$ decrease and $σ_{\rm H}$ and $κ_1$ increase with the magnetic field in the weak magnetic field regime. On the other hand, the presence of a finite chemical potential increases these transport coefficients. The effects of weak magnetic field and finite chemical potential on aforesaid transport coefficients are found to be more conspicuous at low temperatures, whereas at high temperatures, they have only a mild dependence on magnetic field and chemical potential. We have found that the presence of finite chemical potential further extends the lifetime of the magnetic field. Furthermore, we have explored the effects of weak magnetic field and finite chemical potential on the Knudsen number, the elliptic flow coefficient and the Wiedemann-Franz law.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in hot QCD matter, weak magnetic fields (where \(T^2 \gg eB\)) and finite chemical potential modify charge and heat transport coefficients, with the effects being more conspicuous at low temperatures; the abstract's statement that "the presence of a finite chemical potential further extends the lifetime of the magnetic field" is conditional on the medium having finite electrical conductivity and is derived from the calculated conductivities in the weak-field regime.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation, using an ansatz method valid in the weak magnetic field limit (where Landau quantization is neglected). They incorporated parton interactions through thermal masses and a running coupling constant that depends on both temperature and magnetic field. The transport coefficients, electrical conductivity (\(\sigma_{\rm el}\)), Hall conductivity (\(\sigma_{\rm H}\)), thermal conductivity (\(\kappa_0\)), and Hall-type thermal conductivity (\(\kappa_1\)), were derived for a hot QCD medium with three quark flavors (u, d, s) at finite chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The initial magnetic field strength at RHIC is given as \(eB = m_\pi^2\) (\(\simeq 10^{18}\) Gauss) and at LHC as \(eB = 15\, m_\pi^2\).
- The relaxation time for quarks/antiquarks is \(\tau_{f(\bar{f})} = \frac{1}{5.1\,T\,\alpha_s^2 \log(1/\alpha_s) [1 + 0.12(2N_f+1)]}\).
- The running coupling uses \(\Lambda_{\overline{\rm MS}} = 0.176\) GeV and \(\Lambda = 2\pi\sqrt{T^2 + \mu_f^2/\pi^2}\).
- The weak magnetic field condition is \(T^2 \gg eB\) (no specific numerical threshold given beyond this inequality).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of transport in QCD matter under magnetic fields, such as those by Feng et al. (2017) using Kubo formalism, Das et al. (2019, 2020) for hadronic matter, and Rath et al. (2019, 2020) for strong magnetic fields. It specifically addresses the combined effects of weak magnetic field and finite chemical potential on both charge and heat transport, which had not been simultaneously treated in earlier kinetic theory approaches. The results enable improved modeling of magnetohydrodynamic evolution and observables like elliptic flow and the Wiedemann-Franz law in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
