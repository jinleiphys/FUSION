# Heavy $K^*$ mesons with open charm from $KD^{(*)}D^*$ interactions

> In view of the recent discovery of $T_{cc}$, which can be described as a $DD^*$ molecular state, we perform a study of the $KDD^*$ system, and its extension to studying $KD^*D^*$, where $D^*D^*$ is bound as a spin 1 $T_{cc}$-like state, to search for the possible existence of exotic mesons with the open flavors $ccs$ being part of their quark configuration. Considering the additional attractive interactions present in the $KD$ and $KD^*$ subsystems, where the states $D^*_{s0}(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ are generated, we solve the Faddeev equations considering the fixed-center approximation for the mentioned three-body systems and find the existence of two doubly charmed $K^*$-like mesons, $K_{cc}^*(4309)$ and $K_{cc}^*(4449)$, with quantum numbers $I(J^P)=1/2\,(1^-)$. Considering the respective three-body thresholds of the two systems, both $K_{cc}$-states are bound by around $60$ MeV. An experimental confirmation will bring evidence for the existence of a degree of exoticity beyond charm +2.

## Key claim
The paper predicts the existence of two doubly charmed strange mesons, \(K_{cc}^*(4309)\) and \(K_{cc}^*(4449)\), as three-body bound states of the \(KDD^*\) and \(KD^*D^*\) systems, respectively, with the hedge that these states arise specifically within the fixed-center approximation to the Faddeev equations and depend on the assumption that the \(DD^*\) and \(D^*D^*\) subsystems form \(T_{cc}\)-like clusters.

## Method
The authors solved the Faddeev equations using the fixed-center approximation, treating the \(DD^*\) or \(D^*D^*\) pair as a heavy cluster (\(T_{cc}\) or a \(T_{cc}\)-like state) and the kaon as a light particle scattering off that cluster. The two-body input amplitudes for the \(KD\) and \(KD^*\) subsystems were obtained from coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations using chiral and heavy-quark symmetry Lagrangians, which generate the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) and \(D_{s1}(2460)\) resonances. The three-body \(T\)-matrix was then constructed from the single and multiple scattering series of the kaon with the two constituents of the cluster.

## Key numbers
- \(K_{cc}^*(4309)\): mass of 4309 MeV with a binding energy of 62 MeV relative to the \(KDD^*\) three-body threshold.
- \(K_{cc}^*(4449)\): mass of 4449 MeV with a binding energy of 64 MeV relative to the \(KD^*D^*\) three-body threshold.
- The \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) and \(D_{s1}(2460)\) poles from the two-body \(KD\) and \(KD^*\) interactions are found at 2312 MeV and 2452 MeV, respectively.
- The cluster masses used are 3874.82 MeV for \(DD^*\) (forming \(T_{cc}\)) and 4015.54 MeV for \(D^*D^*\) (forming a \(T_{cc}\)-like state), with corresponding cutoffs of 415 MeV and 420 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the \(KD\bar{D}^*\) system by Ren (2018) and the \(KDD\) system by Sanchez Sanchez (2017) and Martinez Torres (2018) into the doubly charmed sector with open strangeness, motivated by the recent LHCb discovery of \(T_{cc}^+\). The results are consistent with earlier calculations for the \(KDD^*\) system by Ma (2017), who found a binding energy of 54(4) MeV using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, and by Zhang (2024), who found 79(4) MeV using lattice effective field theory. The predicted states enable experimental searches via \(D^{(*)}D_s^{(*)}\) invariant mass distributions in \(\Upsilon\) decays or \(e^+e^-\) collisions, and provide a target for future lattice QCD studies of doubly heavy-flavored hadrons.
