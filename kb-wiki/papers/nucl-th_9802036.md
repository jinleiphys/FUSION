# Baryon Junction Stopping at the SPS and RHIC via HIJING/B

> Baryon stopping at the SPS and RHIC energies is calculated by introducing a new baryon junction mechanism into HIJING. The exchange of a baryon junction, according to Regge phenomenology, leads to a cosh(y/2) rapidity dependence and an s^(-1/4) energy dependence of the inclusive baryon cross section. This baryon junction dynamics also leads naturally to enhanced p_T broadening in pA and AA together with enhanced mid-rapidity hyperon production.

# Baryon Junction Stopping at the SPS and RHIC via HIJING/B

## Key claim

The baryon junction exchange mechanism, implemented in the HIJING/B Monte Carlo event generator, can reproduce the observed baryon stopping in pA and AA collisions at SPS energies, but the model still underestimates the preliminary valence hyperon yield in PbPb collisions without including multiple final state interactions or "rope" effects.

## Method

The authors implemented a baryon junction exchange mechanism into the HIJING event generator (creating HIJING/B) using a "Y" string configuration for excited baryons, where the baryon junction and produced baryon are distributed according to Regge phenomenology with a cosh(y/2) rapidity dependence and an s^(-1/4) energy dependence. For each nucleon-nucleon interaction, a ~60% probability is assigned that only one of the two nucleons will have its baryon junction stopped in the central rapidity region, with junction exchange allowed only when the invariant mass exceeds 4.5 GeV. The junction exchange probability was fitted to reproduce pp valence proton data, and for multiple collisions it was assumed that once the baryon junction is exchanged, it remains stopped in subsequent soft interactions.

## Key numbers

- The baryon junction exchange trajectory has intercept alpha_J\bar{J}(0) approximately 1/2, leading to a cosh(y/2) rapidity dependence and an s^(-1/4) energy dependence for the inclusive baryon cross section.
- For each nucleon-nucleon interaction, a ~60% probability is assigned that only one of the two nucleons will have its baryon junction stopped in the central rapidity region.
- Junction exchange is allowed only if the invariant mass exceeds m >= 4.5 GeV.
- HIJING/B predicts nearly twice the initial baryon density, rho_B(1 fm) approximately 2 rho_0, at mid-rapidity for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies (sqrt(s) = 200 GeV) compared to HIJING.
- The baryon junction mechanism leads to a factor of 3 enhancement in hyperon production from random combinatorics of s versus u,d pair production, and the transverse momentum of the final baryon is enhanced by a factor of sqrt(3) from the random addition of three sea quarks.

## Context

This work extends the HIJING event generator (Wang and Gyulassy, 1991) by implementing a baryon junction exchange model proposed by Kharzeev (1996), based on the Rossi-Veneziano baryon junction Regge exchange model (Rossi and Veneziano, 1977). The mechanism addresses the failure of the original HIJING, which used diquark-quark string excitations from the LUND Fritiof and dual parton model (DPM), to reproduce the mid-rapidity valence baryon yield in heavy nuclear collisions at SPS energies. The model competes with the VENUS double string mechanism and the Kopeliovich-Zakharov diquark breakup mechanism (implemented in a new version of DPM), and it enables predictions for baryon stopping and hyperon production at RHIC energies that can be tested against future data.
