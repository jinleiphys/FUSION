# Neutrino and antineutrino CCQE scattering in the SuperScaling Approximation from MiniBooNE to NOMAD energies

> We compare the predictions of the SuperScaling model for charged current quasielastic muonic neutrino and antineutrino scattering from $^{12}$C with experimental data spanning an energy range up to 100 GeV. We discuss the sensitivity of the results to different parametrizations of the nucleon vector and axial-vector form factors. Finally, we show the differences between electron and muon (anti-)neutrino cross sections relevant for the $ν$STORM facility.

## Key claim

The SuperScaling (SuSA) model, using the standard axial-vector dipole mass of 1.032 GeV/c^2, underestimates MiniBooNE neutrino and antineutrino cross sections on carbon but is compatible with NOMAD data at higher energies; the model does not include two-body meson-exchange current contributions that could increase cross sections by 10-15%, and the low-excitation-energy region (below 50 MeV) contributes about 10% of the total cross section even at high energies.

## Method

The authors applied the SuSA model, which uses a phenomenological superscaling function extracted from quasielastic electron scattering data on 12C, multiplied by the appropriate charge-changing weak interaction cross sections to obtain the longitudinal (L), transverse (T), and transverse-axial (T') response functions for charged-current quasielastic muon neutrino and antineutrino scattering. They compared the model predictions with MiniBooNE data (flux peaking at 0.79 GeV for neutrinos and 0.66 GeV for antineutrinos) and NOMAD data (3-100 GeV), and tested sensitivity to different parametrizations of nucleon vector form factors (GKex and others) and axial-vector form factors (dipole with M_A=1.032 and 1.35 GeV/c^2; monopole with M_A_tilde=0.5 and 1.0 GeV/c^2).

## Key numbers

- MiniBooNE neutrino flux peaks at 0.79 GeV; antineutrino flux peaks at 0.66 GeV; both fluxes extend from 0 to about 7 GeV with most contributions below 3 GeV.
- Standard axial-vector dipole mass M_A = 1.032 GeV/c^2; MiniBooNE collaboration proposed M_A = 1.35 GeV/c^2.
- Low-excitation-energy region (below 50 MeV) contributes about 10% of the total cross section even at very high neutrino energies.
- For a monopole axial-vector form factor, fitting NOMAD data yields M_A_tilde = 0.70 +/- 0.06 GeV/c^2 for neutrinos and 0.72 +/- 0.14 GeV/c^2 for antineutrinos.
- Old deuterium bubble chamber experiments gave monopole axial masses of M_A_tilde = 0.57 +/- 0.05 (Baker 1981) and M_A_tilde = 0.54 +/- 0.05 (Miller 1982).
- The ratio of electron to muon neutrino cross sections reaches unity for energies above 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the SuSA model, previously developed by Amaro, Barbaro, Caballero, Donnelly, and collaborators (2004-2011), from intermediate to high energies (0-100 GeV) to address the discrepancy between MiniBooNE data (which favor a large axial mass M_A=1.35 GeV/c^2) and NOMAD data (which do not). The results contradict the simple RFG model and support the need for two-body meson-exchange current contributions as proposed by Martini (2010) and Nieves (2011), while also providing predictions for the proposed nuSTORM facility to study electron versus muon neutrino cross section differences.
