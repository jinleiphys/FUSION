# Relativistic analysis of the 208Pb(e,e'p)207Tl reaction at high momentum

> The recent 208Pb(e,e'p)207Tl data from NIKHEF-K at high missing momentum (p_m>300 MeV/c) are compared to theoretical results obtained with a fully relativistic formalism previously applied to analyze data on the low missing momentum (p_m < 300 MeV/c) region. The same relativistic optical potential and mean field wave functions are used in the two p_m-regions. The spectroscopic factors of the various shells are extracted from the analysis of the low-p_m data and then used in the high-p_m region. In contrast to previous analyses using a nonrelativistic mean field formalism, we do not find a substantial deviation from the mean field predictions other than that of the spectroscopic factors, which appear to be consistent with both low- and high-p_m data. We find that the difference between results of relativistic and nonrelativistic formalisms is enhanced in the p_m<0 region that will be interesting to explore experimentally.

## Key claim

The authors find that a fully relativistic formalism, using the same mean field wave functions and spectroscopic factors that describe low missing momentum data, can also account for high missing momentum data without requiring additional correlation effects, in contrast to previous nonrelativistic analyses that reported substantial deviations from mean field predictions.

## Method

The authors analyzed the 208Pb(e,e'p)207Tl reaction at NIKHEF-K using a fully relativistic formalism based on Dirac equation solutions with scalar-vector potentials for both bound and outgoing nucleons. Spectroscopic factors for five shells (3s1/2, 2d3/2, 1h11/2, 2d5/2, 1g7/2) were extracted from low missing momentum data (pm < 300 MeV/c) and then used without adjustment to calculate reduced cross sections in the high missing momentum region (340 < pm < 500 MeV/c) at fixed q = 221 MeV/c and omega = 110 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Spectroscopic factors from relativistic analysis: 3s1/2 = 0.70(5), 2d3/2 = 0.73(5), 1h11/2 = 0.64(4), 2d5/2 = 0.60(5), 1g7/2 = 0.30(4)
* Nonrelativistic spectroscopic factors from Quint (1988) were substantially smaller: 3s1/2 = 0.50, 2d3/2 = 0.53, 1h11/2 = 0.42, 2d5/2 = 0.44, 1g7/2 = 0.19
* High missing momentum region covered 340 < pm < 500 MeV/c at fixed q = 221 MeV/c and omega = 110 MeV, with incoming electron energy 487 MeV
* Cross sections obtained with CC1 current operator were typically 10% larger than those with CC2 in the low-pm region
* Relativistic calculations with CC1 and CC2 could differ by as much as one order of magnitude in the high-pm region

## Context

This work extends the relativistic formalism previously applied to the 3s1/2 and 2d3/2 shells in 208Pb (Udias et al., 1993, 1995) to additional shells and to the high missing momentum region. It contradicts the conclusion of Bobeldijk et al. (1994) that high-pm data require substantial correlation effects beyond mean field predictions when analyzed with nonrelativistic formalisms. The results support the suggestion by Kleinmann et al. (1994) that relativistic nuclear models may emulate the role of correlations, and the authors identify the negative missing momentum region (pm < 0) as particularly interesting for future experimental exploration due to enhanced differences between relativistic and nonrelativistic predictions.
