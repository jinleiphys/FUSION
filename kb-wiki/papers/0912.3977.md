# Three-particle coincidence of the long range pseudorapidity correlation in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We report the first three-particle coincidence measurement in pseudorapidity ($Δη$) between a high transverse momentum ($p_{\perp}$) trigger particle and two lower $p_{\perp}$ associated particles within azimuth $\mid$$Δϕ$$\mid$$<$0.7 in $\sqrt{{\it s}_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV $d$+Au and Au+Au collisions. Charge ordering properties are exploited to separate the jet-like component and the ridge (long-range $Δη$ correlation). The results indicate that the particles from the ridge are uncorrelated in $Δη$ not only with the trigger particle but also between themselves event-by-event. In addition, the production of the ridge appears to be uncorrelated to the presence of the narrow jet-like component.

## Key claim
The first three-particle coincidence measurement in pseudorapidity shows that particles from the ridge (long-range Delta-eta correlation) are uncorrelated in Delta-eta not only with the trigger particle but also between themselves event-by-event, and the ridge production appears uncorrelated with the presence of the narrow jet-like component. This conclusion is based on charge ordering properties used to separate the jet-like component from the ridge.

## Method
The STAR Collaboration measured three-particle coincidences in pseudorapidity (Delta-eta) between a high transverse momentum (p_T) trigger particle and two lower p_T associated particles, all within azimuth |Delta-phi| < 0.7. The analysis was performed for sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV d+Au and Au+Au collisions at RHIC. Charge ordering properties were exploited to separate the jet-like component from the ridge (long-range Delta-eta correlation).

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for both d+Au and Au+Au systems
- Azimuthal acceptance for associated particles: |Delta-phi| < 0.7
- This is the first three-particle coincidence measurement of its kind (no specific numerical correlation strength values are provided in the available text)

## Context
This work extends previous two-particle correlation measurements that identified the "ridge" phenomenon in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC. The three-particle technique allows the STAR Collaboration to distinguish between jet-like correlations and the ridge component, which was not possible with two-particle measurements alone. The finding that ridge particles are uncorrelated with each other event-by-event constrains theoretical models of ridge production, such as those based on collective flow or glasma flux tubes, and enables future studies of the underlying mechanism of long-range correlations in high-energy nuclear collisions.
