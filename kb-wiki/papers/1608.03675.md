# Energy dependence of resonance production in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The production of hadronic resonances $K^{*}(892)$, $ϕ(1020)$, $Σ^{*}(1385)$, and $Ξ^{*}(1530)$ in central AA collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 17.3, 200, and 2760 GeV are systematically studied. The direct production of these resonances at system hadronization are described by the quark combination model and the effects of hadron multiple-scattering stage are dealt with by a ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics model (UrQMD). We study the contribution of these two production sources to final observation and compare the final spectra with the available experimental data. The $p_T$ spectra of $K^{*}(892)$ calculated directly by quark combination model are explicitly higher than the data at low $p_T \lesssim 1.5$ GeV and taking into account the modification of rescattering effects the resulting final spectra well agree with the data at all three collision energies. The rescattering effect on $ϕ(1020)$ production is weak and including it can slightly improve our description at low $p_T$ on the basis of overall agreement with the data. We also predict the $p_T$ spectra of $Σ^{*}(1385)$ and $Ξ^{*}(1530)$ to be tested by the future experimental data.

## Key claim

The quark combination model (SDQCM) plus hadronic rescattering via UrQMD can explain the measured $p_T$ spectra of $K^{*0}(892)$ and $\phi(1020)$ at all three collision energies, but only after the rescattering stage is included; the direct quark combination results alone overestimate $K^{*0}(892)$ production at low $p_T \lesssim 1.5$ GeV, and the rescattering correction is essential for agreement.

## Method

The authors used the Shandong quark combination model (SDQCM) to compute the initial $p_T$ spectra of resonances at hadronization from the quark momentum distributions previously tuned to reproduce stable hadron data. They then fed these spectra into the UrQMD (version 3.4) hybrid mode as input at chemical freeze-out, running the hadronic transport to simulate rescattering, decays, and regeneration. Final spectra were compared with experimental data from NA49 (SPS), STAR (RHIC), and ALICE (LHC) for central AA collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3$, 200, and 2760 GeV.

## Key numbers

- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3$ GeV, the rescattering suppression of $K^{*0}(892)$ exceeds 50% at low $p_T$; the suppression weakens with increasing $p_T$ due to regeneration around $p_T \sim 2$ GeV.
- For $\phi(1020)$, the rescattering effect deviates from unity by about 20% at 17.3 and 200 GeV, and reaches about 30% suppression at low $p_T$ at 2760 GeV.
- The vector-to-pseudoscalar ratio $R_{V/P}$ for $K^{*0}(892)$ relative to kaon is fixed at 0.45, and this single value simultaneously describes data at all three energies.
- At LHC (2760 GeV), the final $K^{*0}(892)$ spectrum including rescattering agrees with ALICE data; an alternative rescattering calculation from Ref. knospe2016 shows weaker suppression, exceeding data by about 20% at $p_T \lesssim 2$ GeV.
- For $\Sigma^{*}(1385)$ at 200 GeV, the final results are "basically agreement" with available STAR data; no numerical discrepancy is quoted.

## Context

This work extends the quark combination approach previously applied to stable hadrons (pions, kaons, protons, $\Lambda$, $\Xi$, $\Omega$) by the same group (Shao et al., 2012; Wang et al., 2015) to short-lived resonances, testing the universality of the hadronization mechanism. It contrasts with hydrodynamical models, which overestimate $\phi(1020)$ production by about 50% at $p_T \lesssim 2$ GeV at LHC and fail to reproduce the $\Omega/\phi(1020)$ ratio above 2 GeV (as noted in ALICE data). The results enable future tests of the predicted $p_T$ spectra for $\Sigma^{*}(1385)$ and $\Xi^{*}(1530)$ at SPS and LHC energies, and constrain spin-dependent dynamics in quark combination models.
