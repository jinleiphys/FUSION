# What did we learn about GPDs from hard exclusive electroproduction of mesons?

> It is reported on an analysis of electroproduction of light mesons at small Bjorken-x within the handbag approach. The partonic subprocesses, meson electroproduction off quarks or gluons, are calculated within the modified perturbative approach in which quark transverse momenta are retained. The soft hadronic matrix elements, generalized parton distributions, are constructed by means of double distributions. The constraints from parton distributions and sum rules are taken into account. Various moments of these generalized parton distributions are compared to recent results from lattice gauge theories.

## Key claim

The handbag approach, using generalized parton distributions (GPDs) constructed from double distributions and power corrections from quark transverse momenta, describes meson electroproduction data well over a wide kinematic range, but only for small skewness (ξ ≤ 0.1) and low momentum transfer (−t ≤ 0.6 GeV²); the approach fails to reproduce the sharp increase of the longitudinal cross section for ρ⁰ production at low W (below about 5 GeV) when the low-skewness GPDs are simply extrapolated to larger ξ.

## Method

The analysis uses the handbag factorization scheme for exclusive meson electroproduction at small Bjorken-x, where partonic subprocesses (γ*g → Vg and γ*q → Vq) are calculated in the modified perturbative approach that retains quark transverse momenta and includes Sudakov suppressions. The soft hadronic matrix elements (GPDs) are constructed via double distributions, with zero-skewness GPDs parameterized using Regge-inspired forms that incorporate the forward limits from known parton distribution functions and exponential t-dependencies. The GPDs are constrained by parton distributions, sum rules, and positivity bounds, and their moments are compared to lattice gauge theory results.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of longitudinal over transverse cross sections for ρ⁰ or φ mesons is about 2 for Q² ≈ 4 GeV²
- Kinematic regime: W ≥ 4 GeV, ξ ≤ 0.1, −t ≤ 0.6 GeV²
- Gluon trajectory intercept: α_g = 1.10 + 0.06 ln(Q²/4 GeV²) + 0.15 GeV⁻² t
- Valence quark trajectory: α_val = 0.48 + 0.90 GeV⁻² t
- Kinematic range for analysis: W ≈ 5–180 GeV, Q² ≈ 3–100 GeV²
- For HERMES kinematics (W ≈ 5 GeV), gluon plus sea contribution amounts to about 50% of the cross section
- The parameter μ_π takes a value of about 2 GeV at a scale of 2 GeV
- Lowest moment of H_T for u quarks is about 25% smaller than the lattice result
- Lattice QCD results at m_π = 352 MeV are compared for form factors

## Context

This work extends the handbag factorization approach developed by Rad96 and others, using GPDs constructed from double distributions (Muller) and the modified perturbative approach (Botts89, Ellis, Collins). The analysis of Refs. GK1, GK5, GK4 provides GPDs valid for ξ ≤ 0.1 and x ≤ 0.6, which are compared to lattice QCD studies (Haegler, Goeckeler) showing good agreement for relative strengths and t-dependences of moments, though lattice results typically give flatter t-dependences than form factor data. The extracted GPDs enable evaluation of Ji's sum rule, yielding large total angular momenta for u quarks and gluons while d and strange quarks give very small values, in agreement with lattice gauge theory (Haegler). Future improvement requires more polarization data and data on π⁰ electroproduction.
