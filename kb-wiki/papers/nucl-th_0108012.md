# Bridging Two Ways of Describing Final-State Interactions in A(e,e'p) Reactions

> We outline a relativistic and unfactorized framework to treat the final-state interactions in quasi-elastic A(e,e'p) reactions for four-momentum transfers Q$^{2} \gtrsim 0.3$ (GeV/c)$^{2}$. The model, which relies on the eikonal approximation, can be used in combination with optical potentials, as well as with the Glauber multiple-scattering method. We argue that such a model can bridge the gap between a typical ``low'' and ``high-energy'' description of final-state interactions, in a reasonably smooth fashion. This argument is made on the basis of calculated structure functions, polarization observables and nuclear transparencies for the target nuclei $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a relativistic, unfactorized eikonal framework can produce consistent descriptions of final-state interactions in A(e,e'p) reactions across both the "low-energy" regime (where optical potentials are used) and the "high-energy" regime (where the Glauber multiple-scattering method is used), provided the calculations are performed at intermediate four-momentum transfers where both methods are applicable.

## Method
The authors developed a relativistic eikonal approximation that can be used with either relativistic optical potentials (the OMEA method) or a relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (the RMSGA method) without changing other model ingredients. Bound-state wavefunctions were computed from a relativistic mean-field sigma-omega model. The model was applied to calculate structure functions, polarization observables, and nuclear transparencies for the target nuclei 12C and 16O, using the same bound-state wavefunctions and electron-proton coupling for both FSI treatments.

## Key numbers
- The model is applicable for four-momentum transfers Q^2 >= 0.3 (GeV/c)^2, with intermediate values defined as Q^2 >= 0.5 (GeV/c)^2
- At Q^2 = 0.8 (GeV/c)^2, optical-potential and Glauber calculations produced almost identical spectroscopic factors for 16O(e,e'p) data from JLAB
- For the 12C(e,e'p) experiment of Woo et al. (1998), the energy and momentum transfer were (omega, q) = (294 MeV, 756 MeV/c)
- Nuclear transparency calculations were performed for 12C(e,e'p) over a Q^2 range of 0.3 to 20 (GeV/c)^2
- Short-range correlations increased the calculated transparencies by about 10%
- At Q^2 >= 3-4 (GeV/c)^2, differences between CC1 and CC2 current operator predictions for transparencies became negligible
- The optical potential results are limited to kinetic energies below T_p = 1 GeV

## Context
This work extends the relativistic eikonal approach to bridge the gap between the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) using optical potentials (Picklesimer, 1985) and the Glauber multiple-scattering method (Glauber, 1970) for describing final-state interactions in A(e,e'p) reactions. The framework enables consistent analysis of the apparent Q^2 evolution of spectroscopic factors noted by Lapikas (2000), where low-Q^2 optical-potential analyses gave 50-70% of sum-rule strength while higher-Q^2 Glauber analyses required substantially higher values. The model was tested against high-quality 16O(e,e'p) data at Q^2 = 0.8 (GeV/c)^2 from Gao et al. (2000) and 12C(e,e'p) polarization data from Woo et al. (1998).
