# Imaging of Sources in Heavy-Ion Reactions

> Imaging of sources from data within the intensity interferometry is discussed. In the two-pion case, the relative pion source function may be determined through the Fourier transformation of the correlation function. In the proton-proton case, the discretized source function may be fitted to the correlation data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that source functions in heavy-ion reactions can be directly imaged from two-particle correlation data, rather than only inferred through Gaussian fitting or comparison to simulations. The authors hedge that this imaging is straightforward for like-charged pions via Fourier transformation, but for proton-proton correlations the procedure requires discretized fitting and is limited by the kernel (null-space) of the inversion operator, particularly for large particle separations.

## Method

The authors invert the relation between the two-particle correlation function C(q) and the relative source function S(r) using two approaches. For like-charged pions, they apply direct Fourier transformation of the correlation function to obtain the source, after correcting for Coulomb interactions between pions and with the source. For proton-proton correlations, they discretize the source function at points separated by 1.8 fm and fit it to the angle-averaged correlation data by minimizing chi-squared, using the Reid soft-core potential to compute the wavefunctions and kernel K_0(q,r) for the inversion.

## Key numbers

- For the pion analysis: integration cutoff at q_max approximately 50 MeV/c, giving a resolution in relative distance of Delta r approximately 2.0 fm; momentum resolution of the data Delta q = 5 MeV/c
- For the proton analysis: analysis region restricted to q between q_min = 10 MeV/c and q_max approximately 80 MeV/c, giving a lower limit on source resolution of 1.4 fm; source values determined at points separated by Delta r = 1.8 fm; source determined up to r_max = 16.2 fm
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for the proton source fits: 1.1 for the highest momentum gate, 1.8 for the lowest, and 4.9 for the intermediate gate
- From the intermediate-momentum gate for the 14N + 27Al reaction: average nuclear density in the vicinity of any emitted proton estimated as 17 x 0.0015 fm^{-3} = 0.025 fm^{-3} = 0.16 n_0
- Using a thermal distribution for central events: average phase-space occupancy at freeze-out approximately 0.23 and entropy per nucleon S/A approximately 2.7

## Context

This work extends the intensity interferometry technique beyond the standard Gaussian fitting approach reviewed by Boal, Gelbke, and Jennings (1990) and the integral relation noted by Bertsch (1994) and Mrowczynski (1992). The authors apply their imaging method to pion correlation data from the E877 Collaboration (Miskowiec, 1996) for 10.8 GeV/c Au+Au and to proton-proton correlation data from Gong et al. (1991, 1993) for 75 MeV/nucleon 14N + 27Al, comparing extracted sources to Boltzmann-equation transport model calculations by Danielewicz (1995). The method enables direct extraction of freeze-out densities and phase-space occupancies from correlation data, and the authors suggest singular value decomposition as a tool for determining what source parametrizations can be constrained by the data.
