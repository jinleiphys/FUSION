# Neutron Stars and Quantum Billiards

> Homogeneous neutron matter at subnuclear densities becomes unstable towards the formation of inhomogeneities. Depending on the average value of the neutron density one can observe the appearance of either bubbles, rods, tubes or plates embeded in a neutron gas. We estimate the quantum corrections to the ground state energy (which could be termed either shell correction or Casimir energy) of such phases of neutron matter. The calculations are performed by evaluating the contribution of the shortest periodic orbits in the Gutzwiller trace formula for the density of states. The magnitude of the quantum corrections to the ground state energy of neutron matter are of the same order as the energy differences between various phases.

# Neutron Stars and Quantum Billiards

## Key claim

The quantum corrections (shell correction or Casimir energy) to the ground state energy of inhomogeneous neutron matter at subnuclear densities are of the same order of magnitude as the energy differences between the various "pasta phases" (bubbles, rods, tubes, plates), meaning the previously assumed sequence of phase transitions may not occur and strong disorder could dominate over regular lattices.

## Method

The authors compute quantum corrections to the ground state energy of neutron matter in the "pasta phase" by evaluating the contribution of the shortest periodic orbits using the Gutzwiller trace formula for the density of states. For plate-like nuclei or voids, analytic expressions are derived for the shell correction energy. For spherical voids (bubbles) in a simple cubic lattice, the shell correction is computed from 26 periodic orbits between nearest neighbors of three different lengths, with the fermions treated as moving in a hard-wall potential.

## Key numbers

- The bubble-bubble interaction energy at large separations scales as $E_{\circ \circ} \approx (\hbar^2 k_F^2 / 2m) (R/a)^2 [2 \sin(2k_F a) / \pi^2]$, where $k_F$ is the Fermi wave vector, $R$ is the bubble radius, and $a$ is the separation distance
- For plate-like structures, the shell correction energy per unit volume is $E_{shell}/L^3 = (\hbar^2 k_F^3 / (48 a L m)) G(k_F a / \pi)$, where $G(x)$ is an approximately periodic function with $G(x=n) \approx -1$ and $-1 \le G(x) \le 0.5$
- The shell correction energy amplitude grows approximately as $\propto N^{1/6}$ for a saturating $N$-fermion system
- The condition for the semiclassical approach is $k_F a \gg 1$, $k_F R \gg 1$, and $k_F s = \mathcal{O}(1)$ where $s$ is the skin thickness

## Context

This work extends the liquid drop and Thomas-Fermi approximations used in earlier studies of neutron star crust phases (Baym and collaborators) by including quantum corrections that had been neglected. Previous Hartree-Fock calculations (Negele and collaborators) and shell effect studies (Oyamatsu and collaborators) concluded that quantum corrections would not qualitatively change the sequence of nuclear shape transitions. The authors show instead that quantum corrections from the unbounded motion of fermions outside inhomogeneities are more important than previously recognized, and that the interaction between bubbles has a surprisingly long range (decaying as $1/a^2$ rather than $1/a^3$ as in the Ruderman-Kittel interaction for point-like impurities). This work enables future studies of the static and dynamic properties of inhomogeneous fermion systems, including the possibility of bubble-bubble molecules and multi-body interactions beyond pairwise forces.
