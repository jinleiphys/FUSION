# Compression modes in nuclei: microscopic models with Skyrme interactions

> The isoscalar giant monopole resonances (ISGMR) and giant dipole resonances (ISGDR) in medium-heavy nuclei are investigated in the framework of HF+RPA and HF-BCS+QRPA with Skyrme effective interactions. It is found that pairing has little effect on these modes. It is also found that the coupling of the RPA states to 2p-2h configurations results in about (or less than) 1 MeV shifts of the resonance energies and at the same time gives the correct total widths. For the ISGMR, comparison with recent data leads to a value of nuclear matter compression modulus close to 215 MeV. However, a discrepancy between calculated and measured energies of the ISGDR in $^{208}$Pb is found and remains an open problem.

# Compression modes in nuclei: microscopic models with Skyrme interactions

## Key claim

The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) data in medium-heavy nuclei can be reproduced by Skyrme-RPA calculations with a nuclear matter compression modulus close to 215 MeV, but the isoscalar giant dipole resonance (ISGDR) in 208Pb shows a persistent discrepancy of about 4 MeV between calculated and measured centroid energies that remains unexplained by any existing theoretical model.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus Random-Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations using Skyrme effective interactions for the ISGMR and ISGDR in 90Zr, 116Sn, 144Sm, and 208Pb. For open-shell nuclei, they extended the approach to HF-BCS plus quasiparticle RPA (QRPA) with a constant pairing gap. In 208Pb, they also included coupling of RPA states to the continuum and to 2p-2h configurations via a projection operator formalism to account for escape and spreading widths.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter compression modulus K = 215 MeV for the SGII Skyrme force, which best reproduces ISGMR centroid energies
- ISGMR centroid energy in 208Pb: experimental 14.2 MeV, theoretical (SGII RPA) 14.1 MeV
- ISGMR centroid energy in 116Sn: experimental 16.0 MeV, theoretical (SGII RPA) 17.2 MeV
- ISGMR centroid energy in 144Sm: experimental 15.3 MeV, theoretical (SGII RPA) 16.2 MeV
- ISGMR centroid energy in 90Zr: experimental 17.9 MeV, theoretical (SGII RPA) 19.1 MeV
- ISGDR centroid energy in 208Pb: experimental 20.3 MeV, theoretical (SGII RPA) 24.1 MeV (discrepancy of about 4 MeV)
- ISGDR centroid energy in 116Sn: experimental 23.0 MeV, theoretical (SGII RPA) 26.3 MeV
- ISGDR centroid energy in 144Sm: experimental 24.2 MeV, theoretical (SGII RPA) 25.4 MeV
- ISGDR centroid energy in 90Zr: experimental 26.2 MeV, theoretical (SGII RPA) 27.1 MeV
- Pairing correlations shift ISGMR centroid in 116Sn by only 0.01 MeV (from 17.18 to 17.19 MeV with SGII)
- Coupling to 2p-2h configurations shifts ISGMR centroid downward by about 0.5 MeV and ISGDR centroid by about 1 MeV
- Total width of ISGMR in 208Pb: about 3 MeV (reproduced by theory including 2p-2h coupling)
- Total width of ISGDR in 208Pb: theoretical about 6 MeV, experimental about 7 MeV

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent HF-RPA approach of Blaizot et al. (1995) using Gogny interactions (which gave K = 231 MeV) to Skyrme interactions, finding a lower incompressibility near 215 MeV consistent with Hamamoto et al. (1997) using SKM*. The study systematically addresses pairing effects (finding negligible impact, unlike the larger shifts reported by Civitarese et al. (1991) in a non-self-consistent model) and demonstrates that 2p-2h coupling reproduces the ISGMR linewidth in 208Pb as previously shown by Colo et al. (1992). The persistent ISGDR discrepancy in 208Pb, also found in Gogny calculations by Decharge and Sips (1983) and in relativistic models, remains an open problem that challenges the extraction of nuclear incompressibility from the dipole compression mode.
