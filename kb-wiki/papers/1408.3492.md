# Electron and Positron Capture Rates on $\bf{^{55}}$Co in Stellar Matter

> Cobalt-55 is not only present in abundance in presupernova phase but is also advocated to play a decisive role in the core collapse of massive stars. The spectroscopy of electron capture and emitted neutrinos yields useful information on the physical conditions and stellar core composition. B(GT) values to low-lying states are calculated microscopically using the pn-QRPA theory. Our rates are enhanced compared to the shell model rates. The enhancement is attributed partly to the liberty of selecting a huge model space, allowing consideration of many more parent excited states in our rate calculation. Unlike previous calculations, the so-called Brink's hypothesis is not assumed leading to a more realistic estimate of the rates. The electron and positron capture rates are calculated over a wide temperature and density grid.

## Key claim

The authors calculate electron and positron capture rates on $^{55}$Co using the pn-QRPA theory and find their rates are enhanced compared to shell model rates, particularly at low temperatures, because they do not assume Brink's hypothesis and instead perform a state-by-state evaluation of transitions from parent excited states.

## Method

The pn-QRPA (proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation) theory was applied to calculate Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions and weak interaction rates for $^{55}$Co in stellar matter. A large model space of $7\hbar\omega$ was used, considering 30 parent excited states and approximately 200 daughter states for each parent state, with GT transitions calculated microscopically for each pair. The formalism includes pairing in the BCS approximation, separable particle-hole and particle-particle Gamow-Teller forces, and phase space integrals with Fermi-Dirac distributions for electrons and positrons.

## Key numbers

- Total GT strength for electron capture on $^{55}$Co: 7.4
- Total GT strength for positron capture on $^{55}$Co: 17.9
- Calculated half-life of $^{55}$Co: 1108 seconds (18.74 hours), compared to experimental value of 17.53 hours
- GT centroid for electron capture ground state transitions resides in energy range 7.1 to 7.4 MeV in daughter $^{55}$Fe
- GT centroid for positron capture ground state transitions resides at 9.1 MeV in daughter $^{55}$Ni
- Electron capture rates calculated over temperature range $0.01\times10^{9}$ to $30\times10^{9}$ K and density range $10$ to $10^{11}$ g cm$^{-3}$
- At low temperature (log T = 7.0), electron capture rates increase by as much as 7 orders of magnitude when density shifts from $10^{7}$ to $10^{11}$ g cm$^{-3}$
- At low densities ($10-10^{4}$ g cm$^{-3}$) and low temperatures, electron capture rates are enhanced by one order of magnitude compared to FFN rates (Fuller et al. 1982)
- At presupernova temperatures, positron capture rates are very slow compared to electron capture rates; e.g., at log T = 9.2 and density $10$ g cm$^{-3}$, positron capture rate is $3.2\times10^{-33}$ s$^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends the pn-QRPA calculations of stellar weak interaction rates previously applied to sd-shell and fp/fpg-shell nuclei by Nabi and Klapdor (1999, 2000). It directly compares with shell model calculations by Langanke and collaborators (2003) and with the systematic FFN rates (Fuller et al. 1982) for 226 nuclei with masses between A=21 and 60. The enhanced rates for $^{55}$Co, identified by Heger et al. (2001) as the most important nuclide for electron capture in massive stars ($25M_\odot$), may have significant astrophysical impact on core collapse simulations.
