# Higher flow harmonics from (3+1)D event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics

> We present event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic calculations of the anisotropic flow coefficients v_2 to v_5 for heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC). We study the dependence of different flow harmonics on shear viscosity and the morphology of the initial state. v_3 and higher flow harmonics exhibit a particularly strong dependence on both the initial granularity and shear viscosity. We argue that a combined analysis of all available flow harmonics will allow to determine eta/s of the quark gluon plasma precisely. Presented results strongly hint at a value (eta/s)_QGP < 2/4pi at RHIC. Furthermore, we demonstrate the effect of shear viscosity on pseudo-rapidity spectra and the mean transverse momentum as a function of rapidity.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a combined analysis of all available flow harmonics (v2 through v5) will allow precise determination of eta/s of the quark-gluon plasma, and that the presented results strongly hint at a value (eta/s)_QGP less than 2/(4pi) at RHIC, but the authors do not claim an exact extraction and note that a detailed systematic analysis of different initial state models is needed for more precise statements.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)D event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code with a variant of the Israel-Stewart formalism, employing Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions with Gaussian smearing of wounded nucleon and binary collision distributions. They computed anisotropic flow coefficients v2 through v5 for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200A GeV, averaging over 100-200 single events per parameter set, and compared results to experimental data from PHENIX and PHOBOS.

## Key numbers

- For eta/s=0.16, v2 is suppressed by approximately 20% relative to ideal hydrodynamics, while v5 is suppressed by approximately 80%
- The initial Gaussian width sigma_0 was varied from 0.2 fm to 0.8 fm to study granularity effects
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross-section used was sigma_NN=42 mb at sqrt(s)=200A GeV
- The initial time tau_0 was set to 0.4 fm/c for all parameter sets
- The freeze-out temperature T_FO was varied from 145 MeV to 155 MeV depending on the parameter set
- The maximum average energy density for most central (0-5%) collisions ranged from 50 GeV/fm^3 (eta/s=0.16) to 65.7 GeV/fm^3 (ideal case)
- The lattice spacing used was a=0.115 fm (standard) and a=0.2 fm for verification

## Context

This work extends earlier (2+1)D viscous hydrodynamic calculations by including full (3+1)D dynamics, and builds on event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations by Holopainen (2010) and Schenke (2010) that showed modifications to spectra and flow from fluctuating initial conditions. The results enable a more precise determination of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of the quark-gluon plasma by demonstrating that higher flow harmonics (v3, v4, v5) are substantially more sensitive to both eta/s and initial state granularity than elliptic flow v2 alone, as previously suggested by Alver (2010).
