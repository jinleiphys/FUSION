# Diffractive S and D-wave vector mesons in deep inelastic scattering

> We derive helicity amplitudes for diffractive leptoproduction of the S and D wave states of vector mesons. We predict a dramatically different spin dependence for production of the S and D wave vector mesons. We find very small $R=σ_{L}/σ_{T}$ and abnormally large higher twist effects in production of longitudinally polarized D-wave vector mesons.

## Key claim

The authors derive helicity amplitudes for diffractive leptoproduction of pure S-wave and pure D-wave vector mesons, predicting that the ratio \(R = \sigma_L/\sigma_T\) is dramatically smaller for D-wave states than for S-wave states, and that higher-twist effects in longitudinally polarized D-wave production are abnormally large, but these results are conditional on the assumption that the D-wave admixture in ground-state vector mesons (such as \(\rho^0\) and \(\omega^0\)) arises from nonperturbative pion-exchange between light quarks.

## Method

The authors compute diffractive helicity amplitudes for \(\gamma^* + p \to V + p'\) using a leading-log(1/x) perturbative QCD framework with Sudakov expansion and unintegrated gluon density. They construct pure S-wave and D-wave spinor vertices from the simplest \(\gamma_\mu\) vertex via projection operators, then substitute these into the existing amplitude formalism of KNNZ (1998) to obtain helicity amplitudes for each wave state. A twist expansion in inverse powers of the hard scale \(\overline{Q}^2 = m^2 + z(1-z)Q^2\) is performed to isolate leading and higher-twist contributions.

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(R^D = \sigma_L/\sigma_T\) for D-wave vector mesons satisfies \(R^D \lesssim 1\) in a broad range of \(Q^2 \lesssim 225 m_V^2\).
- The amplitude \(A_{0L}\) for D-wave production changes sign at \(Q^2 \sim 7 m_V^2\).
- For charmonium, the suppression parameter for spin-flip amplitudes is \((m_V a_S)^2 \approx 27\), where \(a_S\) is the radius of the 1S state.
- The diffraction cone slope is \(B_{3\Pom} \sim 6\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) for small momentum transfer \(\mathbf{\Delta}\).

## Context

This work extends the helicity amplitude formalism of KNNZ (1998) for diffractive vector meson production to explicitly treat pure S-wave and D-wave states, which previous calculations had mixed via the simple \(\gamma_\mu\) vertex. The results address persistent discrepancies between theory and experiment for the ratio \(R = \sigma_L/\sigma_T\) in ground-state vector mesons, and offer a way to distinguish D-wave from 2S-wave assignments for excited mesons such as \(\rho'(1480)\), \(\rho'(1700)\), \(\omega'(1420)\), and \(\omega'(1600)\), states that are indistinguishable in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation. The technique can be generalized to higher excited states (e.g., \(3^-\)) that cannot be formed in \(e^+e^-\) collisions.
