# Establishing the vector-meson-exchange dominance for the short range interactions of light quarks

> I give a brief comment on a recent study revealing the vector meson exchange (VME) dominant for the short range interactions between u/d quarks in the $NN$, $D_{03}$, and $D_{30}$ systems. The finding echoes nicely with an earlier study of hadron spectroscopy using a quark model with hidden local symmetry which also favors the VME dominant for the short range interactions of light quarks. The VME dominance for the short range interactions of light quarks gives a natural explanation of the empiric VME dominance for the interactions between hadrons containing light quarks.

## Key claim

The paper argues that vector meson exchange (VME) dominates the short-range interactions between light u/d quarks, based on a recent study of dibaryons that found a large VME contribution is necessary to simultaneously explain data in the NN, D03, and D30 systems, while only a small residual one gluon exchange interaction is allowed.

## Method

The author comments on a systematic study by Lü et al. (2024) that jointly investigated the NN, D03, and D30 systems using the extended chiral SU(3) quark model, which includes vector meson fields in addition to one gluon exchange, confinement, and scalar/pseudo-scalar chiral fields. By comparing these three distinct dibaryon systems, the study determined the relative strengths of one gluon exchange versus vector meson exchange interactions needed to reproduce experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies of D03 as a ΔΔ bound state: 58–111 MeV, corresponding to the experimentally observed deeply bound state d*(2380)
- Binding energies of D30 as a ΔΔ bound state: 5–12 MeV, corresponding to a possible weakly bound state

## Context

This work extends the earlier hadron spectroscopy study by He (2023) using a quark model with hidden local symmetry, which also favored VME dominance for reproducing meson and baryon spectra. The VME dominance for light quark interactions provides a natural explanation for the empirical VME dominance in hadron-hadron interactions, as noted by Dong (2021), and enables predictions of hadronic molecules including doubly charmed dibaryons. Future theoretical and experimental work on other dibaryons and hadronic molecules will help confirm this dynamic mechanism.
