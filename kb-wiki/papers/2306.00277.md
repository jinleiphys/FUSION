# Neutrino-induced reactions on 18O and implications of 18O mixture in water Cherenkov detectors on supernova neutrino events

> Neutrino-nucleus reaction cross sections on 18O are evaluated by shell-model calculations and compared with those on 16O. Important contributions from Gamow-Teller transitions are noticed for 18O (ν_e, e^{-})18F in contrary to the case for 16O, where spin-dipole transitions are dominant contributions. Calculated cross sections for 18O(ν_e, e^{-})18F are shown to be larger than for 16O at low neutrino energies below 20 MeV in natural water with the 0.205% admixture of 18O due to the lower threshold energy (1.66 MeV) for 18O than that for 16O (15.42 MeV). The resulting electron spectra, that is, the cross sections as functions of emitted electron energy Te, are also shown to be quite different, reflecting the different threshold energies. The electron spectra from (ν_e, e^{-}) reactions on 18O and 16O in water Cherenkov detectors for supernova neutrino detection are investigated for both the cases with and without the neutrino oscillation and compared with those of the neutrino-electron scattering. It has been shown that the contribution from 18O (0.205% mixture) enhances the rates from 16O by 60% for the case without the oscillation and by 20-30% for the case with the oscillation below Te =20 MeV. For the case with the neutrino oscillation, the event rates for 18O and 16O become comparable to those of the neutrino-electron scattering. However, their rates at low energy (Te < 20 MeV) are much smaller than those of the neutrino-electron scattering, which is important for the pointing accuracy to the supernova direction.

## Key claim

The contribution from the 0.205% admixture of 18O in natural water enhances the event rates from 16O by 60% for the case without neutrino oscillation and by 20-30% for the case with oscillation below emitted electron energy Te = 20 MeV, but these rates remain much smaller than those of neutrino-electron scattering at low energy, which is important for pointing accuracy to the supernova direction.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations using the SFO-tls Hamiltonian in the p-sd shell, including configurations up to 2p-2h (3p-3h) excitations for positive (negative) parity states, to evaluate neutrino-nucleus reaction cross sections on 18O. They determined the quenching factor for the axial-vector coupling constant gA by fitting to experimental Gamow-Teller strength data from (3He, t) reactions on 18O, obtaining q = 0.88. The cross sections were computed using multipole expansion of the weak hadronic currents up to multipolarity lambda = 4, and the resulting electron spectra were folded over supernova neutrino spectra from the NK1 model, modified Maxwell-Boltzmann distributions, and Fermi-Dirac distributions to estimate event rates in water Cherenkov detectors.

## Key numbers

- Threshold energy for 18O(nu_e, e-)18F reaction: 1.66 MeV; for 16O(nu_e, e-)16F reaction: 15.42 MeV
- Isotope abundance of 18O in natural water: 0.205%
- Quenching factor for gA determined from experimental GT strength in 18O: q = 0.88
- Enhancement of event rates from 18O admixture relative to 16O alone for NK1 spectra without oscillation: 60% below Te = 20 MeV
- Enhancement of event rates from 18O admixture relative to 16O alone for NK1 spectra with oscillation: 20-30% below Te = 25 MeV
- Expected event numbers for NK1 spectra without oscillation: 36 (16O only) vs 42 (16O + 18O); with normal hierarchy: 156 vs 165; with inverted hierarchy: 118 vs 126
- For mMB spectra from Tambora (2016) with (<E_nu_e>, <E_nu_x>) = (10.14, 12.89) MeV and alpha = (2.90, 2.39): without oscillation 4 (16O) vs 11 (16O+18O); with normal hierarchy 63 vs 76; with inverted hierarchy 44 vs 55
- Percentage increase from 18O admixture for Tambora mMB spectra without oscillation: 175%

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model calculations of neutrino-induced reactions on 16O by Suzuki and Chiba (2018) and the CRPA calculations by Kolbe and Langanke, which found spin-dipole transitions dominate for 16O. The authors build on earlier estimates by Haxton (1988) that 18O could account for about 10% of electron events from solar 8B neutrinos, updating those calculations with modern supernova neutrino spectra and including neutrino oscillation effects. The results enable more accurate predictions for supernova neutrino detection in water Cherenkov detectors such as Superkamiokande and the planned Hyperkamiokande, and suggest that experiments with decay-at-rest neutrino sources, such as COHERENT, could separately measure the 18O and 16O cross sections to test the model calculations.
