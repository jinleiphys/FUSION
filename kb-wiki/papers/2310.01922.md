# The hyperon superfluidity and the hyperon couplings in neutron stars within the relativistic mean field model

> A systematic study of the effects of hyperon couplings on hyperon superfluidity is conducted by using the relativistic mean field model. Combining the slope of symmetry energy, the hyperon couplings are determined in two ways -- by the hypernuclear potentials or under the SU(3) symmetry. In either way, the hyperon coupling constants cannot be fixed uniquely but vary within a certain range due to the uncertainties in hypernuclear potentials or the breaking of SU(6) to SU(3) symmetry. When the coupling constants are constrained by the hypernuclear potentials, the pairings of $Λ$ and $Ξ^{0,-}$ are strong and they each show little variations. The pairing of $Σ^-$ is more sensitive to hyperon potentials and the slope of symmetry energy. Under the SU(3) symmetry, the superfluidity of various hyperons differ significantly. The dependence of the pairings of $Λ$ and $Ξ^{0,-}$ on the additional parameters of SU(3) symmetry are the opposite to that of the maximum mass of neutron stars on the additional parameters of SU(3) symmetry, while the pairing of $Σ^-$ shows a similar trend in general. These results suggest that the hyperon superfluidity associated with astrophysical processes is an essential window to probe the physics of neutron star cores, the hyperon-hyperon interactions and the SU(3) symmetry. Compared with other hyperons, $Σ^-$ could serve as a cleaner glass for this purpose.

## Key claim

The hyperon coupling constants cannot be fixed uniquely but vary within a certain range due to uncertainties in hypernuclear potentials or the breaking of SU(6) to SU(3) symmetry; the pairing of Sigma- is more sensitive to hyperon potentials and the slope of symmetry energy than the pairings of Lambda and Xi0,-, which are strong and show little variation when couplings are constrained by hypernuclear potentials.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with density-dependent parameterization (DD-ME2) to calculate the equation of state and single-particle energies of baryonic matter in neutron stars. Hyperon pairing gaps in the 1S0 channel were computed at zero temperature using the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) approximation with a separable pairing force of finite range. Hyperon couplings were determined in two ways: by fitting empirical hypernuclear potentials U_Sigma and U_Xi, or by applying SU(3) symmetry with two free parameters z and alpha_V, while varying the slope of symmetry energy L_sym across four values (30, 51.26, 80, and 100 MeV).

## Key numbers

- For Lambda hyperons, the variation of the pairing gap in the (U_Sigma, U_Xi) parameter space is smaller than 0.08 MeV.
- For Sigma- hyperons, the pairing gap can reach about 1 MeV below a critical U_Sigma of 20 MeV for L_sym = 30 MeV.
- The critical U_Sigma for Sigma- pairing reduces to approximately 15 MeV for L_sym = 51.26 MeV, 10 MeV for L_sym = 80 MeV, and 0 MeV for L_sym = 100 MeV.
- Under SU(3) symmetry, the mass-radius relations of neutron stars differ by more than 0.85 solar masses between alpha_V = 0.0 and alpha_V = 1.0.
- The variation of maximum neutron star mass induced by varying hyperon potentials is less than 0.03 solar masses.
- The maximum masses of neutron stars are nearly independent of L_sym, with variation Delta M_max less than 0.1 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hyperon superfluidity in neutron stars (Vidana 2004, Takatsuka 2006, Wang 2010, Raduta 2017, Sedrakian 2019, Tu 2022) by systematically connecting hyperon couplings to pairing gaps for the first time. It builds on prior RMF treatments of hyperonic matter (Weissenborn 2012, Providencia 2019) and SU(3) symmetry approaches (Weissenborn 2012, Lim 2018, Li 2018, Fu 2022). The results enable future work linking hyperon superfluidity to neutron star cooling observations, potentially constraining the slope of symmetry energy and hyperon-hyperon interactions through astrophysical measurements of Sigma- pairing.
