# Omega- and Phi-meson production in pn -> dV reactions and OZI-rule violation

> We investigate the reactions p n -> d omega and p n -> d phi close to threshold and at higher energies. Near threshold we calculate the S-wave amplitudes within the framework of the two-step model which is described by a triangle graph with pi-mesons in the intermediate state and find a ratio of the S-wave amplitudes squared of R =|A(phi)|^2/|A(omega)|^2 = (4-8) x 10^{-3}. Any significant enhancement of the experimental value of R(phi/omega) over this prediction can be interpreted as a possible contribution of the intrinsic s - anti s component in the nucleon-wave function. We present arguments that there is a strong resonance effect in the omega N channel close to threshold. At higher energies we calculate the differential cross sections of the reactions p n -> d omega, p n -> d phi and the ratio of the phi/omega yields within the framework of the quark-gluon string model. An irregular behavior of the phi/omega-ratio is found at s <= 12 GeV^2 due to the interference of the t- and u-channel contributions.

## Key claim

The ratio of the S-wave amplitudes squared for phi to omega production in pn -> dV reactions near threshold is predicted to be R = (4-8) x 10^{-3} within the two-step model with pion intermediate states; any significant experimental enhancement over this prediction can be interpreted as evidence for an intrinsic s\bar{s} component in the nucleon wave function, but the authors note that a strong resonance effect in the omega N channel close to threshold may also affect the results.

## Method

The authors calculated the S-wave amplitudes for pn -> d omega and pn -> d phi near threshold using a two-step model described by triangle graphs with pi-mesons in the intermediate state, where the vector meson creates a pion on one nucleon via VN -> N pi and the pion is absorbed by the other nucleon. At higher energies, they applied the quark-gluon string model (QGSM) based on three valence-quark exchange in the t- and u-channels, corresponding to nucleon Regge-pole exchange, with the overall normalization fixed by the two-step model predictions at lower energies.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of S-wave amplitudes squared: R = |A_phi|^2 / |A_omega|^2 = (4-8) x 10^{-3} near threshold
- Cross section of pn -> d omega at T_p = 2.1 GeV: 34-60 microbarns (depending on cutoff parameter Lambda = 1.0-1.2 GeV)
- Cross section of pn -> d phi at T_lab = 2.8 GeV: 0.16-0.33 microbarns (depending on cutoff parameter Lambda = 1.0-1.2 GeV)
- Ratio R_0(s, theta_cm = 0 degrees) converges to 10 x 10^{-3} above s = 12 GeV^2
- Line-reverse invariance prediction for pn -> d omega at T_p = 2.0 GeV: sigma_LRI = 4.3 microbarns, which is 7-12 times smaller than the two-step model prediction
- Line-reverse invariance prediction for pn -> d phi at T_p = 2.7 GeV: sigma_LRI = 0.22 microbarns, in agreement with the two-step model

## Context

This work extends the two-step model previously applied to Pontecorvo reactions (Kondratyuk, 1990s) to the pn -> dV reactions, where the model successfully described branching ratios for vector meson production in antiproton-deuteron annihilation. The predictions are relevant for experiments at COSY-Juelich, and the authors note that the SPES3 and DISTO collaborations at SATURNE measured omega and phi production in pp -> ppV near threshold (SPES3, DISTO). The results also relate to measurements of the phi/omega ratio in pd -> He-3 X at SATURNE II (Wurzinger, 1990s) which gave R/f = (63 +/- 5 +27/-8) x 10^{-3}, and to OBELIX and Crystal-Barrel data on antiproton-deuteron annihilation giving phi/omega ratios of about (230 +/- 60) x 10^{-3} (Sapozhnikov). The work enables experimental tests of OZI rule violation and searches for resonances in the omega N channel near threshold.
