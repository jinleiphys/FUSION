# Revisiting gauge invariance and Reggeization of pion exchange

> The Reggeized pion is expected to provide the main contribution to the forward cross section in light meson photoproduction reactions with charge exchange at high energies. We discuss the Reggeization of pion exchange in charged pion photoproduction with an emphasis on consistency with current conservation. We show that the gauge-invariant amplitude for the exchange of a particle with generic even spin $J\geq 2$ in the $t$-channel is analytic at $J=0$, and that it can be interpreted in terms of the nucleon electric current. This enables us to reconcile the dynamics in the $s$- and $u$-channel, which involves also nucleon exchanges, with the amplitude expressed in terms of $t$-channel partial waves, as required by Regge theory.

## Key claim

The authors show that a gauge-invariant amplitude for the exchange of a particle with generic even spin J >= 2 in the t-channel is analytic at J = 0, and that this analytic continuation can be interpreted in terms of the nucleon electric current, reconciling s- and u-channel nucleon exchange dynamics with t-channel partial wave amplitudes required by Regge theory.

## Method

The authors construct t-channel partial waves for exchanges of particles with definite spin J on the pion trajectory, imposing gauge invariance at the vertex level using Lorentz-covariant tensor structures. They analytically continue the resulting amplitude from J >= 2 to J = 0, where a kinematic pole is canceled by a zero in the Wigner d-function, yielding a finite contribution that contains the pion pole. The full Reggeized amplitude is obtained by summing the tower of higher-spin exchanges using the generating function for Jacobi polynomials, with the coupling constants parameterized in terms of an effective interaction radius R and parameters j_p and j_z describing singularities in the left-half J-plane.

## Key numbers

* Pion trajectory slope: alpha' = 0.7 GeV^{-2}
* Characteristic energy scale s_0 = 1 GeV^2, corresponding to effective interaction radius R ~ 0.2 fm
* The reduced amplitude at t = m_pi^2 in the large-s limit: A^{J>=2}(s, m_pi^2) = -alpha' (2 - i*pi/2 + ln(s*R^2/4)) + O(m_pi^2 - t)
* The J=0 contribution recovers the pion pole form: A^{J->0} ~ -i * 2 * e_pi * g_{pi NN} * t / (m_pi^2 - t) * (2*lambda_i*lambda_gamma*delta_{lambda_i lambda_f})

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological Reggeization prescription of Vanderhaeghen, Guidal and Laget (1997), which multiplied a gauge-invariant Born amplitude by an overall Regge factor without rigorous theoretical justification. The authors provide a fundamental derivation of Reggeized pion exchange that is consistent with current conservation, enabling systematic investigation of subleading terms at finite s and the influence of left-half J-plane features. This formalism is relevant for analyzing high-precision photoproduction data from JLab, particularly for light exotic hybrid mesons and XYZ states in the charmonium spectrum as studied by Albaladejo (2020) and Winney (2022).
