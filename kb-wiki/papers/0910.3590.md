# Soft Contribution to the Hard Ridge in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> Nuclear collisions exhibit long-range rapidity correlations not present in proton-proton collisions. Because the correlation structure is wide in relative pseudorapidity and narrow in relative azimuthal angle, it is known as the ridge. Similar ridge structures are observed in correlations of particles associated with a jet trigger (the hard ridge) as well as correlations without a trigger (the soft ridge). Earlier we argued that the soft ridge arises when particles formed in an early Glasma stage later manifest transverse flow. We extend this study to address new soft ridge measurements. We then determine the contribution of flow to the hard ridge.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the soft ridge and the hard ridge in relativistic nuclear collisions can both be explained by a common mechanism: particles produced in an early Glasma stage that later manifest transverse flow, with the hard ridge receiving an additional contribution from jet-bulk correlations that the authors model following Shuryak (2007).

## Method

The authors extend their earlier flow-based model (Gavin 2008) by incorporating jet production and computing the contribution of thermal bulk-bulk correlations and jet-bulk correlations to the hard ridge. They use a blast-wave model to describe the effect of transverse flow on initially correlated particles from Glasma flux tubes, and model jet survival probability using an exponential attenuation with path length through the medium following Shuryak (2007). The correlation strength is derived from the Color Glass Condensate saturation scale, and the model is applied to compute the observable Delta rho / sqrt(rho_ref) for Au+Au and Cu+Cu systems at 62 and 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

* The saturation scale Q_s is approximately 1-2 GeV, setting the transverse scale for flux tube correlations.
* The jet attenuation length l_abs is taken as 0.25 fm for modeling jet survival probability.
* The hard ridge measurement uses trigger particles with 3 < p_t,trigg < 4 GeV and associated particles with 2 < p_t,assoc < 3 GeV in Au+Au at 200 GeV.
* The soft ridge analysis considers particles with p_t < 1 GeV as the thermal bulk dominated range.
* The jet peak has a Gaussian width of approximately 0.2 units in pseudorapidity.
* Bulk-bulk correlations make up approximately 75 percent of the total amplitude of the hard ridge calculation shown in Figure 4.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model (Gavin 2008) that explained the soft ridge using Glasma correlations plus transverse flow, and now addresses the hard ridge by adding a jet contribution following the approach of Shuryak (2007). The model is challenged by PHOBOS data (Wenger 2008) suggesting ridges extend over the broad range -4 < eta < 2, which can only originate from the earliest stages of the collision. The results enable future experimental discrimination between thermal bulk and jet contributions to ridge measurements by comparing the p_t dependence of the azimuthal width and amplitude.
