# Approximate treatment of electron Coulomb distortion in quasielastic (e,e') reactions

> In this paper we address the adequacy of various approximate methods of including Coulomb distortion effects in (e,e') reactions by comparing to an exact treatment using Dirac-Coulomb distorted waves. In particular, we examine approximate methods and analyses of (e,e') reactions developed by Traini et al. using a high energy approximation of the distorted waves and phase shifts due to Lenz and Rosenfelder. This approximation has been used in the separation of longitudinal and transverse structure functions in a number of (e,e') experiments including the newly published 208Pb(e,e') data from Saclay. We find that the assumptions used by Traini and others are not valid for typical (e,e') experiments on medium and heavy nuclei, and hence the extracted structure functions based on this formalism are not reliable. We describe an improved approximation which is also based on the high energy approximation of Lenz and Rosenfelder and the analyses of Knoll and compare our results to the Saclay data. At each step of our analyses we compare our approximate results to the exact distorted wave results and can therefore quantify the errors made by our approximations. We find that for light nuclei, we can get an excellent treatment of Coulomb distortion effects on (e,e') reactions just by using a good approximation to the distorted waves, but for medium and heavy nuclei simple additional ad hoc factors need to be included. We describe an explicit procedure for using our approximate analyses to extract so-called longitudinal and transverse structure functions from (e,e') reactions in the quasielastic region.

## Key claim

The approximate treatment of Coulomb distortion used in prior analyses of quasielastic (e,e') reactions on medium and heavy nuclei, particularly the Effective Momentum Approximation (EMA) employed by Traini et al., is not valid for typical experiments, and the extracted structure functions based on this formalism are not reliable; the authors propose an improved approximation (LEMA') that reproduces exact Dirac-Coulomb distorted wave results to within 2% near the quasielastic peak.

## Method

The authors compared exact Dirac-Coulomb distorted wave calculations for (e,e') reactions to several approximate treatments: the Effective Momentum Approximation (EMA), the Local Effective Momentum Approximation (LEMA), and a modified version (LEMA') that includes an ad-hoc correction factor λ = (ω/ω₀)² in the longitudinal component and a magnitude factor (p_i'(0)/p_i)². They tested these approximations against exact partial-wave solutions for ²⁰⁸Pb at various electron energies and scattering angles, using relativistic single-particle wavefunctions with free nucleon current operators and standard electromagnetic form factors.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁰⁸Pb at E_i = 400 MeV and E_f = 300 MeV (ω = 100 MeV), the second correction term in the potential series contributed less than 2% for momentum transfer q ≥ 350 MeV/c
- The LEMA' approximation agreed with exact DWBA results to within 2% around the quasielastic peak and about 5% in the side regions
- Prior analyses of ²⁰⁸Pb(e,e') data from Saclay claimed up to 50% suppression of the longitudinal structure function, which the authors attribute to inadequate Coulomb distortion treatment
- In a previous calculation for ¹⁶O(e,e'p), Coulomb effects on extracted spectroscopic factors were approximately 3%, while effects on the fourth structure function were approximately 15%

## Context

This work extends the high-energy approximation of Lenz and Rosenfelder and the analyses of Knoll to develop a practical approximate treatment of Coulomb distortion for quasielastic (e,e') reactions. The authors directly challenge the structure function extraction methodology used by Traini et al. and the Saclay collaboration for ²⁰⁸Pb, which claimed large longitudinal suppression. The improved approximation enables reliable extraction of longitudinal and transverse structure functions from medium and heavy nuclei, supporting the conclusions of Jourdan (who found no evidence of suppression at q = 570 MeV/c using the authors' Coulomb corrections) and contradicting the earlier Saclay results.
