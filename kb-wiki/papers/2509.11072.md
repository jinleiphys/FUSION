# Data-driven trap theory for nuclear scattering

> We present a novel data-driven trap theory (abbreviated as DDTT) for nuclear scattering, which aims to overcome the limitations of the traditional trap method in dealing with narrow potential wells, while also providing a more efficient framework for handling long-range Coulomb interactions. As proof-of-concept examples, we employ this unified theory to analyze the elastic scattering of nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-α systems. DDTT can successfully produce results consistent with those from traditional approaches, highlighting its significance for ab initio light nuclei scattering studies and potential for applications in the heavier mass region.

## Key claim

The data-driven trap theory (DDTT) provides a unified framework for extracting scattering phase shifts from confined systems that works for narrow potential wells and long-range Coulomb interactions, where the traditional trap method's assumption that traps only modify boundary conditions without affecting short-range interactions breaks down.

## Method

DDTT combines spectral expansions via generalized zeta functions with renormalization through momentum projection operators, then trains on stochastic ensembles of near-zero-range potentials to determine a correction term \(U_{corr}\) in the quantization condition. The quantization condition relates the Coulomb-modified effective range expansion on the left-hand side to a pole-summation term \(U_\omega\), a data-driven correction \(U_{corr}\), and a contamination term accounting for finite-size effects on the right-hand side. For charged-particle systems, the poles and modified residues are computed numerically from the Hamiltonian containing kinetic energy, harmonic oscillator trap, and Coulomb potential, with a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) implemented through a projection operator on regular Coulomb wave functions.

## Key numbers

* Neutron-neutron \(s\)-wave scattering phase shifts were computed with Av18 interaction using harmonic oscillator frequencies \(\omega = 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11\) MeV; the chosen interaction range \(b_0\) satisfied \(b_0 / b_\omega \approx 0.002 \ll 1\)
* Proton-proton \(s\)-wave scattering phase shifts were computed with Av18 interaction using harmonic oscillator frequencies \(\omega = 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 16\) MeV
* \(\alpha\)-proton \(p\)-wave scattering phase shifts for \(\frac{3}{2}^{-}\) and \(\frac{1}{2}^{-}\) states were computed using harmonic oscillator frequencies \(\omega = 3, 4, 6, 9, 12\) MeV
* The momentum cutoff used in the neutral \(s\)-wave demonstration was \(\Lambda = 4.65\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) with harmonic oscillator frequency \(\omega = 3\) MeV and reduced mass equal to half the nucleon mass

## Context

This work extends the trap method formalism beyond the Busch-Englert-Rzazewski-Wilkens (BERW) formula for harmonic oscillator traps and Luscher's formula for periodic cubic boxes, which assume traps only modify boundary conditions. Previous efforts to handle Coulomb corrections by Beane (2014), Davoudi (2018), and others using perturbation expansions or mesh-based Green's function solvers exhibited numerical instabilities near singularities. DDTT enables future ab initio studies of nuclear scattering for light nuclei via the trap method and is expected to be applicable to complex interactions and quantum computing frameworks for non-Hermitian systems.
