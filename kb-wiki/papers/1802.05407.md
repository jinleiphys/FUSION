# New high-spin structure and possible chirality in $^{109}$In

> High-spin structure of $^{109}$In has been investigated with the $^{100}$Mo($^{14}$N, 5$n$)$^{109}$In reaction at a beam energy of 78 MeV using the in-beam $γ$ spectroscopic method. The level scheme of $^{109}$In has been modified considerably and extended by 46 new $γ$-rays to the highest excited state at 8.979 MeV and $J^π$=(45/2$^{+}$). The new level scheme consists of eight bands, six of which are identified as dipole bands. The configurations have been tentatively assigned with the help of the systematics of neighboring odd-$A$ indium isotopes and the experimental aligned angular momenta. The dipole bands are then compared with the titled axis cranking calculation in the framework of covariant density function theory (TAC-CDFT). The results of theoretical calculation based on the configurations, which involve one proton hole at the $g_{9/2}$ orbital and two or four unpaired neutrons at $g_{7/2}$, $d_{5/2}$ and $h_{11/2}$ orbitals, show that the shape of $^{109}$In undergoes an evolution on both $β$ and $γ$ deformations and possible chirality is suggested in $^{109}$In.

## Key claim

The paper suggests possible chirality in $^{109}$In based on tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (TAC-CDFT) calculations, but this is presented as a tentative suggestion rather than a confirmed observation, with the authors stating only that "possible chirality is suggested" rather than claiming its definitive identification.

## Method

High-spin states in $^{109}$In were populated using the $^{100}$Mo($^{14}$N, 5$n$)$^{109}$In fusion-evaporation reaction at a beam energy of 78 MeV delivered by the HI-13 tandem accelerator at the China Institute of Atomic Energy. Gamma-ray coincidence events (84$\times$10$^{6}$ total) were detected using an array of BGO-Compton-suppressed HPGe detectors, low-energy photon HPGe detectors, and one clover detector, with data sorted into symmetric and asymmetric matrices for coincidence analysis and directional correlation of oriented states (DCO) ratio measurements. Configurations were assigned using systematics of neighboring odd-A indium isotopes and experimental aligned angular momenta, and compared with TAC-CDFT calculations.

## Key numbers

- Highest excited state observed at 8.979 MeV with spin-parity $J^{\pi}$ = (45/2$^{+}$)
- 46 new $\gamma$-rays added to the level scheme
- Level scheme extended to 8 bands, 6 of which are identified as dipole bands
- Beam energy of 78 MeV used for the $^{100}$Mo($^{14}$N, 5$n$)$^{109}$In reaction
- Total of 84$\times$10$^{6}$ $\gamma$-$\gamma$ coincidence events recorded
- Isomeric state with half-life $T_{1/2}$ = 210 ms at the 19/2$^{+}$ level (2101.8 keV)
- Doublet 673-keV transitions: 673.5 keV (19/2$^{+}$ $\rightarrow$ 13/2$^{+}$, $M3$) and 673.7 keV (17/2$^{+}$ $\rightarrow$ 13/2$^{+}$, $E2$)
- Band 2 shows a sharp backbending at rotational frequency 0.42 MeV with a gain in aligned spin of about 3$\hbar$
- Alignment spin of band 1 is around 6$\hbar$ relative to the yrast band of $^{108}$Cd

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $^{109}$In by Poelgeest (1979), Kownacki (1997), and Negi (2012), who established level schemes up to $J$=35/2 at 5.89 MeV and discussed the shears mechanism using tilted axis cranking calculations. The new results significantly revise and extend the level scheme with 46 new $\gamma$-rays and 5 new band structures. The possible chirality suggested in $^{109}$In would represent the first report of chirality in indium isotopes, following similar chiral band observations in neighboring Rh and Ag isotopes such as those reported in $^{103}$Rh, $^{104}$Rh, $^{105}$Rh, $^{106}$Rh, and $^{106}$Ag. The TAC-CDFT calculations show shape evolution in both $\beta$ and $\gamma$ deformations, enabling further theoretical and experimental investigation of chiral rotation in the $A\sim110$ mass region.
