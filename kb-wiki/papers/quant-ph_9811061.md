# Algebraic Nature of Shape-Invariant and Self-Similar Potentials

> Self-similar potentials generalize the concept of shape-invariance which was originally introduced to explore exactly-solvable potentials in quantum mechanics. In this article it is shown that previously introduced algebraic approach to the latter can be generalized to the former. The infinite Lie algebras introduced in this context are shown to be closely related to the q-algebras. The associated coherent states are investigated.

# Algebraic Nature of Shape-Invariant and Self-Similar Potentials

## Key claim
The paper shows that the algebraic approach previously developed for shape-invariant potentials (where parameters are related by translation) can be generalized to self-similar potentials (where parameters are related by scaling), and that the resulting infinite Lie algebras are closely related to q-algebras. The authors establish that the algebra for self-similar potentials is identical to a previously introduced algebra (Chaturvedi et al., 1998) and is a deformation of the standard SO(2,1) algebra.

## Method
The authors start from the shape-invariance condition for supersymmetric quantum mechanics, where parameters a₁ and a₂ are related by either translation or scaling. For self-similar potentials with scaling relation aₙ = qⁿ⁻¹a₁, they introduce scaled operators and derive commutation relations that take the form of q-deformed oscillator relations. They then construct coherent states using a generalized exponential function and show that these states are eigenstates of the operator B₋, coinciding with Barut-Girardello type coherent states for SU(1,1) cases.

## Key numbers
- Energy eigenvalues for self-similar potentials: Eₙ = (1-qⁿ)/(1-q) × c a₁, where c is a constant and q is the scaling parameter
- The scaling relation between parameters: aₙ = qⁿ⁻¹a₁
- The remainder in the shape-invariance condition for the simplest case: R(a₁) = c a₁
- The q-deformed oscillator relation satisfied by the operators: Ŝ₋Ŝ₊ - q Ŝ₊Ŝ₋ = 1

## Context
This work extends the algebraic formulation of shape-invariance introduced by Balantekin (1998) and Gendenshtein (1983) to self-similar potentials previously studied by Shabat (1992), Spiridonov (1992), and Barclay et al. (1993). The paper establishes that the infinite Lie algebra for self-similar potentials is identical to the algebra introduced by Chaturvedi et al. (1998) and represents a deformation of the standard SO(2,1) algebra. The coherent states constructed here belong to the one-parameter family of q-exponential functions considered by Floreanini et al. (1995), and the results enable further investigation of path integrals for q-algebras as explored by Ellinas (1993).
