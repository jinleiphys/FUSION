# Multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean-field study of spontaneous fission: coupling between shape and pairing degrees of freedom

> Studies of fission dynamics, based on nuclear energy density functionals, have shown that the coupling between shape and pairing degrees of freedom has a pronounced effect on the nonperturbative collective inertia and, therefore, on dynamic (least-action) spontaneous fission paths and half-lives. Collective potentials and nonperturbative cranking collective inertia tensors are calculated using the multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean-field (MDC-RMF) model. Pairing correlations are treated in the BCS approximation using a separable pairing force of finite range. Pairing fluctuations are included as a collective variable using a constraint on particle-number dispersion. Fission paths are determined with the dynamic programming method by minimizing the action in multidimensional collective spaces. The dynamics of spontaneous fission of $^{264}$Fm and $^{250}$Fm are explored. Fission paths, action integrals and corresponding half-lives computed in the three-dimensional collective space of shape and pairing coordinates, using the relativistic functional DD-PC1 and a separable pairing force of finite range, are compared with results obtained without pairing fluctuations. Results for $^{264}$Fm are also discussed in relation with those recently obtained using the HFB model. The inclusion of pairing correlations in the space of collective coordinates favors axially symmetric shapes along the dynamic path of the fissioning system, amplifies pairing as the path traverses the fission barriers, significantly reduces the action integral and shortens the corresponding SF half-life.

## Key claim

The inclusion of pairing fluctuations as a dynamical collective variable in the three-dimensional space of shape and pairing coordinates significantly reduces the action integral and shortens spontaneous fission half-lives for both symmetric fission of 264Fm and asymmetric fission of 250Fm, but the effect is conditional on the specific energy functional and the height of the axially symmetric fission barrier relative to the triaxial energy gain.

## Method

Collective potentials and nonperturbative cranking collective inertia tensors were calculated using the multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean-field (MDC-RMF) model with the DD-PC1 relativistic functional. Pairing correlations were treated in the BCS approximation using a separable pairing force of finite range, with pairing fluctuations included as a collective variable via a constraint on particle-number dispersion. Fission paths were determined by minimizing the action integral in three-dimensional collective spaces using the dynamic programming method, and spontaneous fission half-lives were computed in the WKB approximation.

## Key numbers

* For 264Fm, the fission action integral along the 3D dynamic path including pairing fluctuations is reduced by about 5 units compared to the 2D path without dynamic pairing, and the predicted spontaneous fission half-life is shortened by almost five orders of magnitude.
* For 250Fm, the action integral between the inner turning point and the isomeric state decreases from 23.06 along the 2D dynamic path to 14.90 along the 3D fission path that includes pairing fluctuations.
* The pairing strength parameters used were Gn/G0 = 1.21 and Gp/G0 = 1.14, relative to the original pairing strength G0 = -738 MeV fm-3 adjusted in nuclear matter.
* The number of assaults on the fission barrier per unit time was taken as 10^20.38 s-1, corresponding to a vibrational frequency hbar-omega0 = 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous MDC-RHB study of spontaneous fission (Zhao 2015) by adding pairing fluctuations as a dynamical collective coordinate, and it directly parallels nonrelativistic HFB studies using the Skyrme SkM* functional (Sadhukhan 2014, Sadhukhan 2016) that also included the pairing gap as a dynamical variable. The results enable future calculations in higher-dimensional collective spaces and provide a basis for comparing relativistic and nonrelativistic energy density functional predictions for fission dynamics.
