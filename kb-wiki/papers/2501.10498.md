# Excited s-wave $1^{--}$ vector mesons, their leptonic decays and (in-)complete absence of abnormal states as seen from the constituent quark BSE

> Within a lattice inspired interaction between quark and antiquark, we obtain a hierarchy of solutions to the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for vector quarkonia excited states in the constituent quark mass approximation. As a toy model, we apply the similar to calculate ground and excited states of $ϕ$ and $ω/ρ$ meson. Through detailed numerical searches, our study provides evidence that the single-valued constant mass quark propagator does not yield known meson spectra without the simultaneous presence of abnormal (unphysical) solutions. We classify normal and abnormal solutions and discuss necessary changes in the calculation scheme to avoid the spectrum of inconsistent solutions. While all experimental narrow vector mesons are identified with a normal state of the BSE, the occurrence of mutually cancelled normal-abnormal states are reported. The results are slightly different for the heavy and light mesons, but in both cases they seem to result from the inconsistent use of the hmogeneous BSE to describe broad resonances.

# Excited s-wave $1^{--}$ vector mesons, their leptonic decays and (in-)complete absence of abnormal states as seen from the constituent quark BSE

## Key claim

Within the constituent quark approximation (CQA) to the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) using a lattice-inspired interaction kernel, the single-valued constant mass quark propagator does not yield known meson spectra without the simultaneous presence of abnormal (unphysical) solutions; however, when normal and abnormal states appear in degenerate pairs, their poles cancel in the S-matrix, suggesting that the homogeneous BSE is inconsistently applied to describe broad resonances.

## Method

The author solves the homogeneous BSE in the Feynman gauge for vector quarkonia ($1^{--}$ s-wave states) using a two-pole approximation to the gluonic spectral function, with a separable effective coupling that behaves as $\simeq 1/\ln^2 q^2$ at large loop momenta, allowing the renormalization constant $Z_2=1$. Constituent quark masses are tuned separately for ground and excited states (Table 1: $m_u=0.29$ GeV, $m_s=0.522$ GeV, $m_c=1.49$ GeV, $m_b=4.709$ GeV for excited states), and the BSE is solved numerically without Tchebychev expansion using a two-component approximation ($F_1, F_5$) excluding D-wave components. Leptonic decay constants $F_V$ are computed from the BSE vertex functions using the formula of Maris and Tandy (1999).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous lattice-inspired BSE kernel (Sauli, 2020; Sauli, bot) from heavy bottomonia to charmonium, strangeonium, and light vector mesons within the constituent quark approximation. It contrasts with the Maris-Tandy model (Maris and Tandy, 1999) which uses dressed quark propagators from gap equations and respects the Goldstone character of pions, but which produces complex conjugated branch poles in quark propagators (Windisch, 2017; Dorkin et al., 2015) that complicate calculations for heavy states. The study also compares with Salpeter equation results (Wang and Wang, 2020) and other BSE studies (Fischer et al., 2015; Hilger et al., 2015; Krassnigg et al., 2016). The finding that normal-abnormal pairs cancel in the S-matrix suggests that future work should incorporate dressed propagators via simultaneous Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter treatment to eliminate single ghost states, and that the homogeneous BSE is inappropriate for describing broad resonances, pointing toward coupled-channel analyses (Hilger et al., 2022; Li et al., 2020) as alternatives.
