# Triaxial nuclear shapes from simple ratios of electric-quadrupole matrix elements

> Theoretical models often invoke triaxial nuclear shapes to explain elusive collective phenomena, but such assumptions are usually difficult to confirm experimentally. The only direct measurements of the nuclear axial asymmetry $γ$ is based on rotational invariants of zero-coupled products of the electric-quadrupole (E2) operator, the Kumar-Cline sum rule analysis, which generally require knowledge of a large number of E2 matrix elements connecting the state of interest. We propose an alternative assumptions-free method to determine $γ$ of even-even rotating nuclei using only two E2 matrix elements, which are among the easiest to measure. This approach is based on a simple description of nuclear rotation, where the underlying assumptions of the Davydov-Filippov model are either empirically proven or unnecessary. The $γ$ values extracted here are found in agreement with the values deduced from Kumar-Cline sum rules measurements (where available), providing further evidence that the proposed approach represents a reliable, model-independent deduction of $γ$. The technique was applied to more than 60 deformed even-even rotating nuclei and the results indicate that rotating nuclei generally exhibit well-defined stable axially-asymmetric shapes.

# Triaxial nuclear shapes from simple ratios of electric-quadrupole matrix elements

## Key claim

The authors propose that the triaxial deformation parameter γ of even-even rotating nuclei can be determined using only two experimentally accessible E2 matrix elements (the diagonal ⟨2⁺₁∥Ê2∥2⁺₁⟩ and the transitional ⟨0⁺₁∥Ê2∥2⁺₁⟩), provided the empirically established irrotational-flow relation between the moment-of-inertia asymmetry parameter Γ and the shape asymmetry γ is adopted rather than assumed. The extracted γ values agree with those from the more demanding Kumar-Cline sum-rule analysis where available, supporting the method as a reliable, model-independent deduction of triaxiality.

## Method

The authors define the ratio R₂₂/₀₂ = ⟨2⁺₁∥Ê2∥2⁺₁⟩ / ⟨0⁺₁∥Ê2∥2⁺₁⟩, which depends on γ and Γ through the Davydov-Filippov model equations. By substituting the empirically validated irrotational-flow relation Γ(γ) (Eq. 8 in the paper), R₂₂/₀₂ becomes a function of γ alone, allowing γ to be read off from the experimental ratio. A second ratio R₂₂γ/₂₂ = ⟨2⁺₁∥Ê2∥2⁺_γ⟩ / ⟨2⁺₁∥Ê2∥2⁺₁⟩ provides an independent determination using the 2⁺_γ band-head state. The method is applied to 63 deformed even-even nuclei with R₄/₂ > 2.4, using matrix elements derived from evaluated spectroscopic quadrupole moments and B(E2) values.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R₂₂/₀₂ varies continuously from −1.195 at γ = 0° (prolate) to +1.195 at γ = 60° (oblate), vanishing at γ = 30°
- For ⁵⁶Fe: γ_R₂₂/₀₂ = 22.4° (+1.8°, −3.2°)
- For ⁷⁸Kr: γ_R₂₂/₀₂ = 21.7° (+1.0°, −1.3°)
- For ¹⁵²Sm: γ_R₂₂/₀₂ = 12.6° (+1.8°, −4.3°)
- For ¹⁷⁰Er: γ_R₂₂/₀₂ = 20.9° (+2.1°, −4.4°)
- For ¹⁹²Pt: γ_R₂₂/₀₂ = 33.4° (+1.6°, −1.3°)
- For ¹⁹⁸Pt: γ_R₂₂/₀₂ = 33.2° (+1.2°, −1.0°)
- For ¹⁹⁸Hg: γ_R₂₂/₀₂ = 36.8° (+3.4°, −1.9°)
- The method was applied to 63 deformed even-even nuclei with R₄/₂ > 2.4

## Context

This work extends the generalized triaxial-rotor model approach of Wood et al. (2004) and Allmond et al. (2017), which required four E2 matrix elements, by reducing the requirement to only two matrix elements through adoption of the empirically established irrotational-flow relation between Γ and γ. The method provides γ values for over 60 nuclei, far exceeding the 19 nuclei for which Kumar-Cline sum-rule analyses (Kumar and Cline, 1972; Cline, 1986) were previously available. The results enable triaxiality assignments for nuclei where the γ band is not established (e.g., ⁵⁶Fe) or where the 2⁺_γ state is mixed by shape coexistence (e.g., ⁷⁸Kr, ¹⁵²Sm, ¹⁷⁰Er), and challenge the assumption of axially-symmetric oblate shapes for heavy Hg isotopes.
