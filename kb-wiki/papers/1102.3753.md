# Possibility of QCD critical point sweep during black hole formation

> We discuss the possibility to probe the QCD critical point during the dynamical black hole formation from a gravitational collapse of a massive star, where the temperature and the baryon chemical potential become as high as T ~ 90 MeV and $μ_B$ ~ 1300 MeV. Comparison with the phase boundary in chiral effective models suggests that quark matter is likely to be formed before the horizon is formed. Furthermore, the QCD critical point may be probed during the black hole formation. The critical point is found to move in the lower temperature direction in asymmetric nuclear matter, and in some of the chiral models it is found to be in the reachable region during the black hole formation processes.

## Key claim

The QCD critical point may be probed during black hole formation from gravitational collapse of a massive star, but only in certain chiral effective models and only if the isospin chemical potential shifts the critical point to lower temperatures such that the thermodynamic trajectory of the collapsing matter passes through it.

## Method

The authors computed the location of the QCD critical point and first-order phase boundary in the temperature-baryon chemical potential plane using four chiral effective models (NJL, P-NJL, P-NJL with eight-quark interaction, and PQM) at finite isospin chemical potential. They then compared these model predictions with the thermodynamic profiles (temperature, baryon chemical potential, and isospin chemical potential as functions of radius) obtained from neutrino-radiation hydrodynamical simulations of black hole formation from a 40 solar mass star using the Shen equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Temperature reaches T ~ 90 MeV and baryon chemical potential reaches μB ~ 1300 MeV during black hole formation
- Baryon density reaches ρB ~ 4 ρ0 (where ρ0 is nuclear saturation density) just before horizon formation at t = 1.344 sec after bounce
- In the PQM model with no vector interaction (r=0), the critical point in symmetric matter is at (TCP, μCP) = (105 MeV, 964 MeV); at δμ = 70 MeV it shifts to (62 MeV, 989 MeV)
- In the NJL model with no vector interaction, the critical point moves from (50 MeV, 993 MeV) at δμ=0 to (37 MeV, 1005 MeV) at δμ=65 MeV
- The transition chemical potential at T=0 (μc) ranges from 1000 MeV to 1110 MeV across models
- Isospin chemical potential during black hole formation is 50-130 MeV in the inner region
- The proto-neutron star mass reaches 1.6 solar masses at t=1.344 sec

## Context

This work extends the study of QCD phase transitions in astrophysical settings beyond supernova explosions, where earlier work by Sagert (2008) suggested quark matter formation could trigger second shocks in core-collapse supernovae with a small bag constant (B^{1/4} ~ 162 MeV). The authors build on black hole formation simulations by Sumiyoshi (2006, 2007, 2008) which used neutrino-radiation hydrodynamics in general relativity. The paper enables future searches for signatures of the QCD critical point in neutrino signals from black hole formation, complementing heavy-ion collision searches at RHIC and FAIR.
