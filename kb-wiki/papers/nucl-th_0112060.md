# The Hypernuclei $^4_Λ$He and $^4_Λ$H: Challenges for Modern Hyperon-Nucleon Forces

> The hypernuclei $^4_Λ$He and $^4_Λ$H provide important information on the hyperon-nucleon interaction. We present accurate Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for the $Λ$ separation energies of the $0^+$ ground and the $1^+$ excited states based on the Nijmegen SC YN interactions. We explicitly take the $Σ$ admixture into account. Mass differences of the baryons and the charge-dependence of the interaction are considered. The results show that the Nijmegen models cannot predict all separation energies simultaneously hinting to failures of the current interaction models.

## Key claim

The Nijmegen soft-core hyperon-nucleon interaction models (SC89 and SC97 series) cannot simultaneously reproduce the Λ separation energies of the 0⁺ ground state and the 1⁺ excited state of ⁴ΛHe and ⁴ΛH, nor the charge-symmetry-breaking splitting between the two mirror hypernuclei, indicating failures in the current interaction models.

## Method

The authors solved the five coupled Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for the four-body system of three nucleons and one hyperon, using the full Nijmegen SC89 and SC97a-f YN interactions with explicit Λ-Σ conversion included. They accounted for baryon mass differences, charge dependence of the interaction, and Coulomb forces in pp, Σ⁺p, and Σ⁻p pairs, while restricting the total four-body isospin to T=1/2. The calculations used the Nijm93 or Bonn B NN interactions, with the Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force tested for dependence studies.

## Key numbers

- ⁴ΛHe 0⁺ state Λ separation energy: SC89 gives 2.14 MeV, SC97f gives 1.72 MeV, SC97e gives 1.54 MeV, SC97d gives 1.29 MeV; experimental value is 2.39(3) MeV
- ⁴ΛHe 1⁺ state Λ separation energy: SC89 gives 0.02 MeV, SC97f gives 0.53 MeV, SC97e gives 0.72 MeV, SC97d gives 0.80 MeV; experimental value is 1.24(5) MeV
- 0⁺-1⁺ splitting Δ for ⁴ΛHe: SC89 gives 2.06 MeV, SC97f gives 1.16 MeV, SC97e gives 0.79 MeV, SC97d gives 0.47 MeV; experimental value is 1.15 MeV
- Charge-symmetry-breaking splitting Δ_CSB for 0⁺ state: SC89 gives 0.34 MeV, SC97e gives 0.07 MeV; experimental value is 0.35(5) MeV
- For ⁴ΛHe 0⁺ state with SC89, the Σ probability P_Σ = 4.08%; for SC97d-f models, P_Σ = 1.49% to 1.76%
- r.m.s. distance of Λ from NN center of mass in ⁴ΛHe 0⁺ state with SC89: 3.5 fm; for 1⁺ state with SC89: 20.1 fm
- r.m.s. distance of Σ from nucleons in ⁴ΛHe: 1.6 fm to 2.0 fm for all considered states

## Context

This work extends earlier Faddeev calculations for ³ΛH by Miyagawa (1993, 1995) to the four-body hypernuclei ⁴ΛHe and ⁴ΛH, providing the first complete results based on full meson-theoretical YN interactions. The results contradict the ability of the Nijmegen models to describe hypernuclear binding, as earlier studies by Gibson (1972, 1994) had suggested the importance of Λ-Σ conversion and charge-symmetry breaking. The findings enable future work to focus on the charge-symmetry-breaking splitting as a cleaner observable to probe YN interactions, since it is less affected by unknown YNN three-body forces than the spin splitting.
