# Hyperon single-particle potentials from QCD on lattice

> We study single-particle potential of hyperons in nuclear medium starting from QCD. First we carry out lattice QCD numerical simulation to extract baryon-baryon interactions from QCD by means of the HAL QCD method. We employ a full QCD gauge configuration ensemble at almost physical point so that hadron masses are nearly physical, e.g. pion mass is 146 MeV, kaon mass is 525 MeV, and nucleon mass is 956 MeV. Then, with some simplifications, we apply the obtained hyperon interactions to the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory and calculate single-particle potential of hyperons in nuclear medium $U_{Y}(ρ,k)$. For the symmetric nuclear matter at the normal nuclear matter density, we obtain $U_Λ(ρ_0,0)=-33$ MeV, $U_Σ(ρ_0,0)=+11$ MeV, and $U_Ξ(ρ_0,0)=-6$ MeV. These results are qualitatively compatible with values suggested from experiments. This success is remarkable and encouraging because we are trying to reveal nature of baryon-baryon interactions starting from QCD, and this agreement proves that our approach is essentially correct.

# Hyperon single-particle potentials from QCD on lattice

## Key claim
Using lattice QCD at nearly physical quark masses (pion mass 146 MeV) combined with the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation, the authors obtain hyperon single-particle potentials in nuclear matter that are qualitatively compatible with experimental values, but the results are preliminary, based on only 414 gauge configurations with 4×28 measurements per configuration, and the authors note they have not yet estimated the influence of statistical error.

## Method
The HAL QCD method is used to extract baryon-baryon interaction potentials from 4-point correlation functions computed on a lattice with 2+1 flavor dynamical quarks at almost physical mass (K-configuration ensemble, spatial volume ~(8 fm)^3). The resulting hyperon-nucleon potentials, truncated to leading order in a derivative expansion and parameterized by fitting to smooth functions, are then input into the Bethe-Goldstone equation within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation to compute single-particle potentials U_Y(ρ,k) for Λ, Σ, and Ξ hyperons in symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass = 146 MeV, kaon mass = 525 MeV, nucleon mass = 956 ± 12 MeV (from lattice QCD simulation)
- Λ mass = 1121 ± 4 MeV, Σ mass = 1201 ± 3 MeV, Ξ mass = 1328 ± 3 MeV
- For symmetric nuclear matter at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³: U_Λ(ρ₀,0) = -33 MeV, U_Σ(ρ₀,0) = +11 MeV, U_Ξ(ρ₀,0) = -6 MeV
- Lattice spatial volume ~ (8 fm)^3
- 414 gauge configurations used; 4 × 28 measurements per configuration (with 4 × 96 planned)
- Sink-source time separation t-t₀ = 11 in lattice units

## Context
This work extends the HAL QCD method (Ishii 2006, Inoue 2010) from nucleon-nucleon to hyperon-nucleon interactions, providing the first QCD-based calculation of hyperon single-particle potentials that were previously only accessible via phenomenological models (Baldo 1999, Kohno 2009) with large uncertainties due to scarce hyperon-nucleon scattering data. The results enable future studies of hyperon appearance in neutron star cores, where the puzzle of heavy neutron stars (2 solar masses) conflicting with soft hyperonic equations of state remains a major challenge. The authors note that experimental data indicate U_Λ(ρ₀,0) ≈ -30 MeV (well established), U_Σ(ρ₀,0) ≥ 20 MeV, and U_Ξ(ρ₀,0) ≈ -14 MeV (poorly constrained), and their QCD results qualitatively support these values.
