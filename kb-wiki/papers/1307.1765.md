# Fluctuations of the initial color fields in high energy heavy ion collisions

> In the Color Glass Condensate approach to the description of high energy heavy ion collisions, one needs to superimpose small random Gaussian distributed fluctuations to the classical background field, in order to resum the leading secular terms that result from the Weibel instability, that would otherwise lead to pathological results beyond leading order. In practical numerical simulations, one needs to know this spectrum of fluctuations at a proper time $τ\ll Q_s^{-1}$ shortly after the collision, in the Fock-Schwinger gauge $A^τ=0$. In this paper, we derive these fluctuations from first principles, by solving the Yang-Mills equations linearized around the classical background, with plane wave initial conditions in the remote past. We perform the intermediate steps in light-cone gauge, and we convert the results to the Fock-Schwinger gauge at the end. We obtain simple and explicit formulas for the fluctuation modes.

## Key claim

The paper derives the spectrum of small Gaussian-distributed fluctuations that must be superimposed on the classical Color Glass Condensate background field in high-energy heavy ion collisions, by solving the linearized Yang-Mills equations with plane-wave initial conditions from the remote past, rather than relying on local orthonormal mode constructions that may produce incorrect fluctuation ensembles due to non-unitary transformations preserving the inner product.

## Method

The authors solve the Yang-Mills equations linearized around the classical CGC background field, using plane-wave initial conditions at \(x^0 = -\infty\) in the light-cone gauge \(\mathcal{A}^- = 0\). The evolution of each fluctuation is tracked through four steps: free propagation in the backward light cone, crossing the first nucleus (where the fluctuation's \(a^+\) component induces a change in the nuclear current), propagation between the two nuclei, and crossing the second nucleus. The results are then transformed from the light-cone gauge to the Fock-Schwinger gauge \(A^\tau = 0\) at a small proper time \(\tau \ll Q_s^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

* The saturation momentum at LHC energies is \(Q_s \approx 1-2\) GeV, with the strong coupling \(\alpha_s \approx 0.3\) at that scale.
* The proper time at which the fluctuation spectrum is evaluated satisfies \(\tau \ll Q_s^{-1}\), i.e., shortly after the collision.
* The viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio \(\eta/s\) is close to the conjectured lower bound \(1/4\pi\) from the strong coupling limit of QCD-like theories.

## Context

This work extends the CGC framework beyond leading order by providing the first-principles derivation of the fluctuation spectrum needed to resum the secular terms arising from the Weibel instability, which was identified in earlier work by Romatschke and collaborators (2006) and by Mrowczynski (1993). The authors correct a caveat in the alternative approach proposed by Dusling, Gelis, and Venugopalan (2011), which used local orthonormal modes at \(\tau > 0\) but could produce incorrect Gaussian ensembles due to non-unitary transformations that preserve the inner product. The results enable numerical simulations of the early-time isotropization and thermalization of gluonic matter in heavy ion collisions, building on the classical Yang-Mills solutions of Kovner, McLerran, and Weigert (1995).
