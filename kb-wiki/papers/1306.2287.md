# Isospin splittings in the light baryon octet from lattice QCD and QED

> While electromagnetic and up-down quark mass difference effects on octet baryon masses are very small, they have important consequences. The stability of the hydrogen atom against beta decay is a prominent example. Here we include these effects by adding them to valence quarks in a lattice QCD calculation based on $N_f=2+1$ simulations with 5 lattice spacings down to 0.054 fm, lattice sizes up to 6 fm and average up-down quark masses all the way down to their physical value. This allows us to gain control over all systematic errors, except for the one associated with neglecting electromagnetism in the sea. We compute the octet baryon isomultiplet mass splittings, as well as the individual contributions from electromagnetism and the up-down quark mass difference. Our results for the total splittings are in good agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The paper computes the isospin mass splittings of the light-baryon octet (nucleon, Sigma, Xi) from lattice QCD with valence QED, achieving results compatible with experiment, but with the caveat that electromagnetic contributions from sea quarks are neglected, introducing an estimated O(10%) quenching uncertainty.

## Method

The authors start from 47 isospin-symmetric Nf=2+1 QCD ensembles with five lattice spacings down to 0.054 fm, lattice sizes up to 6 fm, and pion masses down to 120 MeV. They add QED to valence quarks using a noncompact U(1) action in Coulomb gauge, generating three valence data sets that vary the up-down quark mass difference and the fine-structure constant. The octet baryon mass splittings are extracted from simultaneous correlated fits to two-point functions, then extrapolated to the physical point, continuum, and infinite-volume limits using a nine-parameter LO isospin expansion in α and ΔM², with systematic errors estimated from 128 different fit variations.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon splitting ΔM_N = M_p - M_n = -0.68(39)(36) MeV (statistical and systematic errors)
- Sigma splitting ΔM_Σ = M_Σ⁺ - M_Σ⁻ = -7.84(87)(72) MeV
- Xi splitting ΔM_Ξ = M_Ξ⁰ - M_Ξ⁻ = -7.16(76)(47) MeV
- Electromagnetic contribution to nucleon splitting Δ_QED M_N = 1.59(30)(35) MeV
- Electromagnetic contribution to Xi splitting Δ_QED M_Ξ = -1.29(15)(8) MeV
- Finite-volume correction to Δ_QED M_Ξ ranges from 123% at L=1.6 fm down to 36% at L=6 fm

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD program of the Budapest-Marseille-Wuppertal Collaboration (Durr 2008, Durr 2010) by adding valence QED to compute isospin splittings from first principles. It is the first fully computed baryon octet isosplitting calculation beyond the quenched pioneering work of Duncan (1996), and improves on the nucleon-only calculation of Blum (2010) by using larger volumes, multiple lattice spacings, and systematic error estimation. The results enable future calculations that include QED in the sea, and provide lattice determinations of the individual electromagnetic and up-down quark mass difference contributions that can be compared with phenomenological estimates from Gasser (1982) and Cottingham-formula evaluations by Walker-Loud (2012).
