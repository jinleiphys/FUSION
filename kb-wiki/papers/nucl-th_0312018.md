# Supersymmetric Patterns in the Pseudospin Spin and Coulomb Limits of the Dirac Equation with Scalar and Vector Potentials

> We show that the Dirac equation in 3+1 dimensions gives rise to supersymmetric patterns when the scalar and vector potentials are (i) Coulombic with arbitrary strengths or (ii) when their sum or difference is a constant, leading to relativistic pseudospin and spin symmetries. The conserved quantities and the common intertwining relation responsible for such patterns are discussed.

# Supersymmetric Patterns in the Pseudospin, Spin, and Coulomb Limits of the Dirac Equation with Scalar and Vector Potentials

## Key claim

The Dirac equation in 3+1 dimensions exhibits supersymmetric patterns when the scalar and vector potentials are either (i) Coulombic with arbitrary strengths or (ii) when their sum or difference is a constant, leading to relativistic pseudospin and spin symmetries; the paper demonstrates that these patterns arise from a common intertwining relation, but the resulting supersymmetric algebra is a quadratic deformation of conventional supersymmetric quantum mechanics rather than the standard linear algebra.

## Method

The author constructs a matricial Darboux transformation operator L of first order in the radial derivative, with 2×2 matrix coefficients A(r) and B(r), and imposes an intertwining relation LH_{κ1} = H_{κ2}L between radial Dirac Hamiltonians H_κ for different angular momentum quantum numbers κ. By requiring that both partner Hamiltonians have the same scalar and vector potentials, the system of equations yields six solutions characterized by ω_+ = κ_1+κ_2 and ω_- = κ_2-κ_1 taking values 0, ±1, with the physically interesting cases being ω_+ = 0 (Coulomb limit), ω_+ = 1 (pseudospin limit), and ω_+ = -1 (spin limit). For each limit, the explicit form of the transformation operator L is derived and shown to connect degenerate partner states.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb limit: quantization condition γ + η_1 = -n_r (n_r = 0,1,2,...), where γ = √(κ² + α_S² - α_V²), η_1 = (α_S M + α_V E)/λ, λ = √(M² - E²); eigenvalues given by E^{(±)}_{n_r,κ}/M = [ -α_S α_V ± ξ√(ξ² - α_S² + α_V²) ]/(α_V² + ξ²) with ξ = (n_r + γ)
- Coulomb limit: for n_r ≥ 1, eigenvalues in each branch are two-fold degenerate with respect to the sign of κ, i.e., E^{(+)}_{n_r,κ}=E^{(+)}_{n_r,-κ} and E^{(-)}_{n_r,κ}=E^{(-)}_{n_r,-κ}
- Coulomb limit: for n_r = 0, only one acceptable state per κ exists, κ < 0 for the (+) branch and κ > 0 for the (-) branch
- Pseudospin limit: transformation operator L connects doublet states with constant C = b(M + Δ_0 - E), where Δ_0 = V_S + V_V is constant
- Spin limit: transformation operator L connects doublet states with constant C = -b(E + M + Σ_0), where Σ_0 = V_S - V_V is constant
- Pseudospin and spin limits: when the constant condition is broken, the intertwining relation acquires a residual term: LH_{κ1} - H_{κ2}L = i b Δ' σ_2 (pseudospin) or = -i b Σ' σ_2 (spin)

## Context

This work extends prior studies of relativistic symmetries in nuclear and hadronic physics by showing that the degeneracy patterns of pseudospin doublets in nuclei (Arima 1969, Ginocchio 1997) and spin doublets in heavy-light quark mesons (Page 2001) can be understood within a unified supersymmetric framework. The paper builds on the observation that Dirac Hamiltonians with selected external fields are known to be supersymmetric (Thaller 1992, Sukumar 1985), and on the work of de Castro et al. (2002) on intertwining relations for Dirac Hamiltonians. The results enable a systematic classification of degeneracy patterns in relativistic mean-field descriptions of nuclei (Walecka 1986) and hadrons (Gromes 1991), and suggest that the grouping of multiple doublets, rather than individual doublets, reveals the fingerprints of supersymmetry in these dynamical systems.
