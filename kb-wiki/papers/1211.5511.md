# Partonic mean-field effects on matter and antimatter elliptic flows

> Using a partonic transport model based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, we study the effect of scalar and vector mean fields on the elliptic flows of quarks and antiquarks in relativistic heavy ion collisions in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=7.7~$GeV and impact parameter $b=8~{\rm fm}$ that leads to the production of a baryon-rich matter. Although the scalar mean field, which is attractive for both quarks and antiquarks, reduces both their elliptic flows, the vector mean field, which is repulsive for quarks and attractive for antiquarks, leads to a splitting of their elliptic flows, and this effect increases with the strength of the vector coupling in the baryon-rich quark matter. Converting quarks and antiquarks at hadronization to hadrons via the quark coalescence model, we further study the dependence of the transverse momentum integrated relative elliptic flow differences between protons and antiprotons, lambda and anti-lambdas, and positively and negatively charged kaons on the strength of the quark vector coupling. These results are then compared with the experimental data measured by the STAR Collaboration in the Beam Energy Scan program at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

# Partonic mean-field effects on matter and antimatter elliptic flows

## Key claim

The scalar mean field reduces both quark and antiquark elliptic flows similarly, while the vector mean field (repulsive for quarks, attractive for antiquarks in baryon-rich matter) causes a splitting of their elliptic flows that increases with vector coupling strength; however, the model can describe the measured relative elliptic flow differences for protons/antiprotons and lambdas/antilambdas but fails to reproduce the measured difference for kaons, indicating that other effects (chemical reactions, hadronic mean fields) must also be included.

## Method

The authors used a partonic transport model based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with scalar and vector mean fields to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV and impact parameter b=8 fm. Initial parton distributions were taken from the AMPT model with string melting, and the time evolution was solved via the test particle method with a constant isotropic parton elastic scattering cross section of 2 mb. At hadronization, quarks and antiquarks were converted to hadrons using the quark coalescence model with Wigner functions, and the resulting elliptic flows of protons, antiprotons, lambdas, antilambdas, K+, and K- were computed.

## Key numbers

- Collision system: Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV, impact parameter b=8 fm
- Parton elastic scattering cross section: 2 mb (constant isotropic)
- Hadronization occurs when energy density in the center decreases to about 0.8 GeV/fm^3, approximately 2.5 fm/c after the start of partonic evolution
- Without vector mean field, the relative integrated v2 difference between light quarks and antiquarks is about 15%
- For g_V=G/6, the relative integrated v2 differences between particles and antiparticles are: p/antip 49%, Lambda/anti-Lambda 48%, K+/K- 9%
- Experimental values for comparison: p/antip 63±14%, Lambda/anti-Lambda 54±27%, K+/K- 13±2%
- NJL model parameters: m_u=m_d=3.6 MeV, m_s=87 MeV, G*Lambda^2=3.6, K*Lambda^5=8.9, Lambda=750 MeV
- Vector coupling values studied: g_V/G = 0, 1/6, and 0.73 (Fierz transformation value)
- Hadron root-mean-square radii used: 0.6 fm for K±, 0.877 fm for p, Lambda, antip, anti-Lambda

## Context

This work extends the study of Plumari et al. (2010) on scalar mean-field effects in partonic matter at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV to include vector mean fields at lower collision energies where baryon-rich matter is produced. It addresses the failure of the hadronic mean-field study by Xu et al. (2012) using the AMPT model, which found relative integrated v2 differences about a factor of two smaller than measured for protons/antiprotons and about a factor of two larger for kaons. The results demonstrate that partonic vector interactions can help explain the large particle-antiparticle elliptic flow differences observed by the STAR Collaboration in the Beam Energy Scan program, and suggest that studying elliptic flow at BES energies could allow determination of the partonic vector interaction strength in baryon-rich QGP, which is sensitive to the existence and location of the QCD critical point.
