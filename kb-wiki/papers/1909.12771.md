# Baryon modes in string breaking from gauge/string duality

> We consider the string breaking phenomenon within effective string models which purport to mimic QCD with two light flavors, with a special attention to baryon modes. We make some estimates of the string breaking distances at zero and non-zero baryon chemical potentials. Our estimates point towards the enhancement of baryon production in strong decays of heavy mesons in dense baryonic matter. We also suggest that the enhanced production of $Λ_c^+$ baryons in PbPb collisions is mainly due to larger values of chemical potential.

# Baryon modes in string breaking from gauge/string duality

## Key claim

The paper argues that baryon production in strong decays of heavy mesons is enhanced in dense baryonic matter, and that the enhanced production of $\Lambda_c^+$ baryons observed in PbPb collisions is mainly due to larger values of baryon chemical potential. However, the estimates rely on effective string models with several free parameters, and the conclusion about $\Lambda_c^+$ enhancement is presented as a general argument using the minimal free energy principle rather than a direct calculation of production rates.

## Method

The author uses effective string models based on the gauge/string duality (AdS/QCD) with a deformed five-dimensional metric and an open string tachyon background to mimic QCD with two light flavors. String breaking distances are computed by equating the energy (or free energy) of a heavy quark-antiquark pair with the sum of energies (or free energies) of the decay products, using a mixing matrix formalism analogous to lattice QCD. The analysis is performed at zero temperature and chemical potential, and then extended to cold baryonic matter by introducing a Reissner-Nordström charged black hole background with a baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- String breaking distance for the baryon mode at zero temperature and chemical potential using lattice-calibrated parameters (set L): $\ell_c^{(b)} = 2.35$ fm, with a range from 2.23 fm to 2.35 fm depending on the vertex parameter $\kappa$, giving an error not exceeding 5%.
- String breaking distance for the meson mode using the same lattice-calibrated parameters: $\ell_c^{(m)} = 1.22$ fm (from Bulava et al.).
- String breaking distances using phenomenology-calibrated parameters (set P): $\ell_c^{(m)} = 1.07$ fm and $\ell_c^{(b)} = 1.99$ fm.
- The mass difference used to calibrate the phenomenological parameters: $M_{\Lambda_c^+} - M_{D^0} \approx 420$ MeV.
- The string tension parameter $\sigma = e^{\gamma} \gamma s$ with $s = 0.45$ GeV$^2$ for the lattice set and $s = 0.15$ GeV$^2$ for the phenomenological set.
- The maximal baryon chemical potential in the confined phase is given by $\mu_{pc} = r \sqrt{s / h_c}$ with $h_c \approx 3.50$.
- At $\mu \sim 0.7 \mu_{pc}$, a transition occurs to a regime where baryon formation is energetically favorable over meson formation.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD mixing analysis of string breaking by Bulava et al., which was limited to meson modes at zero temperature and zero chemical potential, to include baryon modes and finite baryon chemical potential. The effective string model builds on the soft-wall AdS/QCD geometry of Andreev (2006) and the heavy quark potential of Andreev and Zakharov (2007), and uses the baryon vertex construction from Andreev (2018) for the three-quark potential. The results enable estimates of baryon production in heavy-ion collisions and suggest that the ALICE measurement of enhanced $\Lambda_c^+$ production in PbPb collisions compared to pp and pPb collisions can be explained by the larger chemical potential in the medium, though temperature effects are not included in the current analysis.
