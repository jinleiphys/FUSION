# Real-time Dynamics of Plasma Balls from Holography

> Plasma balls are droplets of deconfined plasma surrounded by a confining vacuum. We present the first holographic simulation of their real-time evolution via the dynamics of localized, finite-energy black holes in the five-dimensional anti-de Sitter (AdS) soliton background. The dual gauge theory is four-dimensional, N=4 super Yang-Mills compactified on a circle with supersymmetry-breaking boundary conditions. We consider horizonless initial data sourced by a massless scalar field. Prompt scalar field collapse then produces an excited black hole at the bottom of the geometry together with gravitational and scalar radiation. The radiation disperses to infinity in the noncompact directions and corresponds to particle production in the dual gauge theory. The black hole evolves toward the dual of an equilibrium plasma ball on a time scale longer than naively expected. This feature is a direct consequence of confinement and is caused by long-lived, periodic disturbances bouncing between the bottom of the AdS soliton and the AdS boundary.

## Key claim

The first fully nonlinear holographic simulation of a finite-energy black hole in a confining geometry shows that the dual plasma ball relaxes to equilibrium on a time scale longer than naively expected, due to long-lived, periodic disturbances bouncing between the infrared bottom and the AdS boundary.

## Method

The authors solve the generalized harmonic form of the Einstein field equations in five-dimensional anti-de Sitter (AdS) soliton background, coupled to a massless real scalar field. They construct time-symmetric initial data by solving the Hamiltonian constraint with a superposition of Gaussian scalar field lumps, which undergo prompt gravitational collapse to form an excited black hole at the infrared bottom of the geometry.

## Key numbers

* Total mass of the formed black hole: M ≈ 0.226 Λ, where Λ is the confinement scale set by the inverse period of the compact circle.
* Deconfinement temperature: T_c = Λ.
* Bounce time for light rays traveling from the infrared bottom to the AdS boundary and back: t_b ≈ 0.8 Λ⁻¹.
* Equilibrium temperature of the late-time plasma ball: T_equil ≈ 2.1 T_c.
* Naive equilibration time estimate based on final temperature: Λ t_equil ≈ Λ / T_equil ≈ 0.5.
* Frequencies ω_n(k)/Λ of low-lying modes with k₁ = k₂ = k, extracted from sinusoidal fits, approach the known normal frequencies of the AdS soliton (e.g., for n=1, k/Λ=0: fitted values 9.6, 10.4, 10.5 versus normal frequency 10.7; for n=2, k/Λ=0: fitted values 16.8, 17.6, 18.4 versus normal frequency 18.5).

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of far-from-equilibrium dynamics from conformal field theories (Chesler 2008, Heller 2011, Bantilan 2012) and nonconformal theories (Attems 2016, Attems 2017) to confining gauge theories, which previous studies failed to incorporate. It builds on the static plasma ball solutions constructed by Figueras (2014) and the linearized gravity analysis of collisions by Cardoso (2013). The results enable future fully nonlinear studies of finite-energy black hole collisions in confining geometries, which will provide holographic insights on confinement and finite-size effects in equilibration of large-N_c gauge theories.
