# Asymmetric Colliding Nuclear Matter Approach in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The early stage of a heavy ion collision is governed by local non-equilibrium momentum distributions which have been approximated by colliding nuclear matter configurations, i.e. by two Lorentz elongated Fermi ellipsoids. This approach has been extended from the previous assumption of symmetric systems to asymmetric 2-Fermi sphere configurations, i.e. to different densities. This provides a smoother transition from the limiting situation of two interpenetrating currents to an equilibrated system. The model is applied to the dynamical situations of heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies within the framework of relativistic transport (RBUU) calculations. We find that the extended colliding nuclear matter approach is more appropriate to describe collective reaction dynamics in terms of flow observables, in particular, for the elliptic flow at low energies.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Colliding Nuclear Matter (CNM) model from symmetric two-Fermi-sphere configurations to asymmetric configurations with different densities (ACNM), finding that this extension provides a smoother transition from two interpenetrating currents to an equilibrated system and is more appropriate for describing collective flow observables, particularly elliptic flow at low energies, than either the symmetric CNM or the Local Density Approximation (LDA).

## Method

The authors implement the Asymmetric Colliding Nuclear Matter (ACNM) model within a relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport framework, where the local momentum distribution is parametrized by two boosted Fermi ellipsoids with different Fermi momenta and densities. The relativistic self-energies are constructed by extrapolating Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DB) ground state calculations to the ACNM configuration, using effective coupling functions averaged over the ACNM momentum distribution and parametrized as an interpolation between the LDA and symmetric CNM limits. The model is applied to Au+Au collisions at intermediate energies, and the resulting in-plane flow (v1) and elliptic flow (v2) are compared to experimental data from the FOPI collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Saturation properties of the DB model with Bonn A potential: saturation density ρ_sat = 0.185 fm^{-3}, binding energy E = -16.1 MeV, compression modulus K = 230 MeV (soft EOS)
- Au+Au reactions analyzed at beam energies of 0.25 AGeV and 0.4 AGeV for semi-central and peripheral collisions
- Central Au+Au reaction at E_beam = 600 AMeV used to demonstrate local momentum distributions at time t = 10 fm/c
- Local temperatures extracted from fits to RBUU momentum distributions: T_1,2 = 29 MeV (central cell), T_1 = 40 MeV and T_2 = 8 MeV (off-center), T_1 = 25 MeV and T_2 = 1 MeV (further off-center)
- Effective coupling functions shown at fixed total densities of ρ_tot = 1 [ρ_sat] and ρ_tot = 2 [ρ_sat] with relative velocity v_rel = 0.5 [c]
- Elliptic flow excitation function studied over energy range from 0.1 to 4 AGeV

## Context

This work extends the Colliding Nuclear Matter (CNM) model previously developed by Sehn et al. (1997) and applied by Gaitanos et al. (1999, 2001, 2003) to include asymmetric density configurations, addressing a limitation noted in those earlier studies where symmetric two-stream configurations were assumed. The ACNM approach provides a more realistic description of peripheral collisions and asymmetric mass systems, and the results show that non-equilibrium effects at the mean-field level are as important as the choice between a soft or hard equation of state (EOS) in determining collective flow observables. The next step identified by the authors is to include finite temperature effects and explicit momentum dependence of the fields, moving toward the fully consistent treatment of transport and microscopic structure equations within Dirac-Brueckner theory called for by Horowitz and collaborators.
