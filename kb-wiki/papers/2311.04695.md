# Analysis of $DD^*$ and $\bar{D}^{(*)}Ξ_{cc}^{(*)}$ molecule by one boson exchange model based on Heavy quark symmetry

> Numerous exotic hadrons with heavy quarks have been reported in the experiments. In such states, symmetries of heavy quarks are considered to play a significant role. In particular, the superflavor symmetry, or also called the heavy quark anti-diquark symmetry is one of the interesting symmetries, which links a quark $Q$ to an anti-diquark $\bar{Q}\bar{Q}$ having the same color representation as $Q$. In this paper, we study a $\bar{D}Ξ_{cc}$ molecular state as a superflavor partner of the doubly charm tetraquark $T_{cc}$ reported by LHCb recently. $T_{cc}$ locating slightly below the $DD^*$ threshold is a candidate of the hadronic molecule. Thus by replacing the singly charm meson $D^{(*)}$ with the doubly charm baryon $Ξ_{cc}^{(*)}$, superflavor symmetry predicts the existence of the $\bar{D}^{(*)}Ξ_{cc}^{(*)}$ bound state. We employ the one boson exchange model respecting with the heavy quark spin symmetry where the parameter is obtained to reproduce the $T_{cc}$ binding energy. We apply this model with superflavor symmetry to the $\bar{D}^{(*)}Ξ_{cc}^{(*)}$ molecule and predict a bound state with $I(J^P) = 0(\frac{1}{2}^-)$. If the pentaquark state corresponding to $\bar{D}^{(*)} Ξ_{cc}^{(*)}$ molecular state is observed in future experiments as predicted in this work, it is more likely that $T_{cc}$ is a $DD^*$ molecular state.

## Key claim

The authors predict that a $\bar{D}^{(*)}\Xi_{cc}^{(*)}$ molecular pentaquark state with quantum numbers $I(J^P) = 0(\frac{1}{2}^-)$ should exist as a superflavor partner of the $T_{cc}$ tetraquark, provided that $T_{cc}$ is indeed a $DD^*$ molecular state; the prediction depends on the assumption that superflavor symmetry holds between $\bar{D}$ mesons and $\Xi_{cc}$ baryons, with an estimated symmetry breaking error of 30-40%.

## Method

The authors employ a one boson exchange model (OBEM) with heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) to first describe the $T_{cc}$ state as a coupled-channel $DD^*$ and $D^*D^*$ molecule, fitting the cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ to reproduce the experimental binding energy of $T_{cc}$. They then apply superflavor symmetry to relate the coupling constants of $\bar{D}^{(*)}$ mesons to those of $\Xi_{cc}^{(*)}$ baryons, using the same cutoff parameter to solve coupled-channel Schrödinger equations for the $\bar{D}^{(*)}\Xi_{cc}^{(*)}$ system with channels including $\bar{D}\Xi_{cc}$, $\bar{D}\Xi_{cc}^*$, $\bar{D}^*\Xi_{cc}$, and $\bar{D}^*\Xi_{cc}^*$ in $S$-wave and $D$-wave configurations.

## Key numbers

- The $T_{cc}$ binding energy used as input is $\delta m_{BW} = -273 \pm 61 \pm 5^{+11}_{-14}$ keV (Breit-Wigner mass measured from the $D^{*+}D^0$ threshold), with the pole position at $\delta m_{\rm pole} = -360 \pm 40^{+4}_{-0}$ keV and $\Gamma_{\rm pole} = 48 \pm 2^{+0}_{-14}$ keV
- With $g_\sigma^{(1)} = 3.4$ and cutoff $\Lambda = 1182$ MeV, the predicted $\bar{D}^{(*)}\Xi_{cc}^{(*)}$ binding energy is $7.46$ MeV measured from the $\bar{D}\Xi_{cc}$ threshold, with root mean square radius $1.38$ fm
- With $g_\sigma^{(2)} = 0.76$ and cutoff $\Lambda = 1631$ MeV, the predicted $\bar{D}^{(*)}\Xi_{cc}^{(*)}$ binding energy is $19.0$ MeV, with root mean square radius $0.91$ fm
- The mass difference between $D^*(2008)$ and $D(1867)$ is about $140$ MeV; the mass difference between $\Xi_{cc}^*$ and $\Xi_{cc}$ is estimated as about $105$ MeV using the relation $\frac{3}{4}(m_{\Xi_{cc}^*} - m_{\Xi_{cc}}) = m_{D^*} - m_D$
- The $\Xi_{cc}$ mass is taken from LHCb as approximately $3727$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecule interpretation of the $T_{cc}$ tetraquark reported by LHCb (2021-2022) by applying superflavor symmetry (Georgi 1990, Savage 1990) to predict a partner pentaquark state $\bar{D}^{(*)}\Xi_{cc}^{(*)}$. The approach builds on previous one boson exchange model studies of doubly heavy tetraquarks (Manohar 1992, Ohkoda 2012, Li 2013, Cheng 2022) and uses coupling constants from form factor analyses of $B$ meson decay (Liu 2019) and lattice QCD (Isola 2003). If observed experimentally, the predicted $\bar{D}^{(*)}\Xi_{cc}^{(*)}$ bound state would provide strong evidence that $T_{cc}$ is a $DD^*$ molecular state rather than a compact tetraquark.
