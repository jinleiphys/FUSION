# Shell-model-Like APproach (SLAP) for the nuclear properties in relativistic mean field theory

> A Shell-model-Like APproach (SLAP) suggested to treat the pairing correlations in relativistic mean field theory is introduced, in which the occupancies thus obtained having been iterated back into the densities. The formalism and numerical techniques are given in detail. As examples, the ground state properties and low-lying excited states for Ne isotopes are studied. The results thus obtained are compared with the data available. The binding energies, the odd-even staggering, as well as the tendency for the change of the shapes in Ne isotopes are correctly reproduced.

## Key claim

The Shell-model-Like APproach (SLAP) can treat pairing correlations in relativistic mean field (RMF) theory by iterating the occupation probabilities obtained from direct diagonalization of the pairing Hamiltonian back into the densities, thereby correctly reproducing binding energies, odd-even staggering, and shape changes in Ne isotopes without the particle-number violation or improper Pauli blocking inherent in BCS or Bogoliubov methods.

## Method

The authors first solve the RMF Dirac equation for axially deformed nuclei using a harmonic oscillator basis expansion to obtain single-particle levels and wavefunctions. They then construct multi-particle configurations (MPC) with seniority s=0, s=2, etc., truncating the configuration space by an energy cutoff E_c=40 MeV, and diagonalize the pairing Hamiltonian H = H_s.p. + H_pair (with pairing strength G_tau = C_tau/A, C_tau=10) in each (K, parity, seniority) subspace. The resulting ground-state occupation probabilities n_i are fed back into the density source terms of the Klein-Gordon equations, and the Dirac equation is solved again iteratively until convergence. The method is applied to Ne isotopes from A=18 to A=32 using the PK1 effective interaction.

## Key numbers

- Truncation energy E_c = 40 MeV used for all Ne isotopes; with this cutoff, all configurations with weight above 3.7×10^{-6} are included for ^24Ne, and configurations with weight above 0.032% are included for ^18Ne.
- Pairing strength parameter C_tau = 10 (for both neutrons and protons) fixed by reproducing the experimental odd-even mass difference of ^24-26Ne.
- For ^24Ne, the calculated 0^+ excitation energy is E_n^{0_1^+} = 5.622 MeV for neutrons and E_p^{0_1^+} = 4.912 MeV for protons, compared to the experimental value E_exp^{0_1^+} = 4.764 MeV.
- The harmonic oscillator basis uses 14 oscillator shells with oscillator frequency hbar omega_0 = 41 A^{-1/3} MeV.

## Context

This work extends the SLAP (originally called the Particle Number Conserving method) developed by Zeng and collaborators in the 1980s (Zeng 1983) from non-relativistic Woods-Saxon potentials to the relativistic mean field framework. It directly addresses the particle-number violation and improper Pauli blocking in the BCS approximation and Bogoliubov transformation commonly used in RMF calculations (Gambhir 1990, Geng 2003). The RMF+SLAP approach provides a unified description of both ground and excited states, including high-K energy traps, and gives better results for ^26Ne and ^28Ne than the RMF+BCS calculations of Geng (2004). The method enables self-consistent treatment of pairing in open-shell and exotic nuclei, with the potential to incorporate resonant states via the ACCC or S-matrix method for nuclei near the drip line.
