# Quarkonium as a tool: cold nuclear matter effects

> We discuss the quarkonium production as a tool for the study of the Quark Gluon Plasma. In particular, we concentrate on the Cold Nuclear Matter effetcs. We show that quarkonium production is also useful for the study of Quantum Chromodynamics first principles and the nuclear Parton Distribution Functions.

## Key claim

Quarkonium production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions is subject to significant Cold Nuclear Matter (CNM) effects, specifically nuclear shadowing and nuclear absorption, that must be properly subtracted before any Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) studies at the LHC become meaningful; the theoretical uncertainty on shadowing is not limited to the modification of parton distributions but also arises from not knowing the exact partonic production kinematics, which prevents precise determination of the momentum fractions of the gluon in the nucleus.

## Method

The authors compute the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AB}\) for \(J/\psi\) production in \(p\)Pb and PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5\) TeV using two partonic production schemes: an intrinsic \(2\to 1\) process (where the \(J/\psi\) transverse momentum comes entirely from intrinsic gluon \(k_T\)) and an extrinsic \(2\to 2\) process (where the \(J/\psi\) is produced by gluon fusion with emission of a hard final-state gluon). The shadowing correction is applied using the EKS98 gluon nuclear Parton Distribution Function (nPDF) parametrisation, and nuclear absorption is parametrised via an effective absorption cross section \(\sigma_{\mathrm{abs}}\) taking three values (0, 1.5, 2.8 mb). The partonic differential cross section is computed using the Colour-Singlet Model (CSM) at leading order.

## Key numbers

* In \(p\)Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5\) TeV, shadowing alone is responsible for up to 36% suppression of \(J/\psi\) yield at rapidity \(y = 6\).
* The nuclear modification factor \(R_{p\mathrm{Pb}}\) versus rapidity is shown for three values of \(\sigma_{\mathrm{abs}}\): 0, 1.5, and 2.8 mb, using the EKS98 gluon shadowing parametrisation.
* For PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5\) TeV, the \(y\)-dependence of \(R_{\mathrm{PbPb}}\) is shown for various centrality selections, with \(R_{\mathrm{PbPb}}\) systematically smaller at mid-rapidity than at forward rapidity.
* The \(\Upsilon\) nuclear modification factor in \(d\)Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV is shown versus rapidity for three shadowing parametrisations (EKS98, EPS08, nDSg) and three values of \(\sigma_{\mathrm{abs}}\), compared to available RHIC data.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Ferreiro et al. (2008, 2009, 2010) on the impact of partonic production kinematics on nuclear shadowing calculations for quarkonium. It addresses the fact that most previous studies used only the intrinsic \(2\to 1\) scheme, which is insufficient to describe the \(p_T\) spectrum of quarkonia in hadron collisions (Lansberg, 2006). The results enable more accurate subtraction of CNM effects at the LHC, where the gluon nPDF is probed down to \(x \sim 10^{-5}\), and highlight that the rapidity dependence of CNM effects may partially or completely compensate the opposite effect expected from \(c\bar{c}\) recombination in PbPb collisions.
