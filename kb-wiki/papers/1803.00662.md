# Beta equilibrium in neutron star mergers

> We show that the commonly used criterion for beta equilibrium in neutrino-transparent dense nuclear matter becomes invalid as temperatures rise above 1 MeV. Such temperatures are attained in neutron star mergers. By numerically computing the relevant weak interaction rates we find that the correct criterion for beta equilibrium requires an isospin chemical potential that can be as large as 10-20 MeV, depending on the temperature at which neutrinos become trapped.

## Key claim

The standard low-temperature beta equilibrium condition \(\mu_n = \mu_p + \mu_e\) becomes invalid in neutrino-transparent nuclear matter at temperatures above about 1 MeV, which are reached in neutron star mergers; the correct criterion requires an additional isospin chemical potential that can reach 10–20 MeV depending on the temperature at which neutrinos become trapped.

## Method

The authors numerically evaluated the full phase-space integrals for direct Urca neutron decay and electron capture rates in nuclear matter described by the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) equation of state, without assuming particles lie on their Fermi surfaces. They compared these exact rates to the standard Fermi-surface-approximation rates for both direct and modified Urca processes, and then varied the proton fraction to find the isospin chemical potential \(\mu_\delta = \mu_n - \mu_p - \mu_e\) that balances the net neutron production and destruction rates at each density and temperature.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi surface approximation for beta equilibrium breaks down at temperatures \(T \gtrsim 1\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- At \(T = 5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the required isospin chemical potential \(\mu_\delta\) is about \(15\ \mathrm{MeV}\) below the direct Urca threshold.
- At \(T = 10\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\mu_\delta\) reaches about \(23\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- At \(T = 500\ \mathrm{keV}\), the correction \(\mu_\delta\) is at most about \(1.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), occurring just below the direct Urca threshold.
- The direct Urca threshold density for the APR equation of state is around \(5\) times nuclear saturation density \(n_\mathrm{sat}\).
- The typical single-particle free energy for below-threshold direct Urca electron capture is \(10\)–\(15\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and for neutron decay is \(20\)–\(25\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (APR equation of state).

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of beta equilibrium in cold neutron stars (Yakovlev et al., various years) to the hot, neutrino-transparent conditions found in neutron star mergers (Baiotti 2016, Shibata et al., various years). It contradicts the common assumption that the zero-temperature condition \(\mu_n = \mu_p + \mu_e\) remains valid at merger temperatures, and provides the leading finite-temperature correction. The results enable more accurate modeling of the composition and evolution of merger ejecta, and can be incorporated into simulations that currently use a cold beta-equilibrated equation of state up to a neutrino trapping temperature (e.g., Shibata, Bauswein 2010, Hotokezaka 2011).
