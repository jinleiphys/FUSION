# Multiplicity Dependence of Charged Particle, $ϕ$ Meson and Multi-strange Particle Productions in p+p Collisions at $\sqrt{\rm s}$ = 200 GeV with PYTHIA Simulation

> We report the multiplicity dependence of charged particle productions for $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p$, $\overline{p}$ and $ϕ$ meson at $|y| < 1.0$ in p+p collisions at $\sqrt{\rm s}$ = 200 GeV with $\rm PYTHIA$ simulation. The impact of parton multiple interactions and gluon contributions is studied and found to be possible sources of the particle yields splitting as a function of $p_T$ with respect to multiplicity. No obvious particle species dependence for the splitting is observed. The multiplicity dependence on ratios of $K^-/π^-$, $K^+/π^+$, $\overline{p}/π^-$, $p/π^+$ and $Λ/K^{0}_{s}$ in mid-rapidity in p+p collisions is found following the similar tendency as that in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV from RHIC, which heralds the similar underlying initial production mechanisms despite the differences in the initial colliding systems.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the multiplicity dependence of particle yield ratios (K/π, p/π, Λ/K₀ˢ) in PYTHIA-simulated p+p collisions at √s = 200 GeV shows a similar tendency to that observed in Au+Au collisions at the same energy, suggesting similar underlying initial production mechanisms despite the differences in colliding systems; however, this claim is explicitly qualified as being based on a PYTHIA 6.416 simulation, not on experimental data, and the Λ/K₀ˢ ratio enhancement seen in central Au+Au collisions is not reproduced in the simulation.

## Method

The authors generated p+p collision events at √s = 200 GeV using PYTHIA 6.416 with CTEQ5L parton distribution functions, including initial- and final-state QCD radiation and a common K factor of 1.5. Events were categorized into six multiplicity bins based on charged particle multiplicity within |η| < 0.5, and the multiplicity dependence of transverse momentum spectra, average transverse momenta, and particle yield ratios for π±, K±, p, p̅, φ meson, and Λ were studied. The effects of parton multiple interactions and gluon contributions were isolated by switching these processes on and off in the simulation.

## Key numbers

- The mean charged particle multiplicity ⟨Nch⟩ for minibias events with multiple interactions (MPI) on is 3.14, and with MPI off is 2.55.
- The six multiplicity bins have mean charged particle multiplicities ⟨Nch1⟩ through ⟨Nch6⟩ of 1.50, 3.41, 5.38, 7.65, 10.80, and 14.99 respectively (with MPI on).
- The integrated Rpp distributions are studied for pT > 1.3 GeV/c.
- Particle ratios (K⁻/π⁻, K⁺/π⁺, p̅/π⁻, p/π⁺) are presented as a function of dNch/dy within |y| < 0.1.
- The Λ/K₀ˢ ratio at high multiplicity in p+p collisions is consistent with data in peripheral Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of multiplicity-dependent particle production from heavy-ion collisions (STAR (2005-2010) for Au+Au at √sNN = 200 GeV) to p+p collisions at the same energy using PYTHIA simulation, motivated by recent ALICE (2017) observations of strangeness enhancement in high-multiplicity p+p collisions at LHC energies. The paper enables comparison between elementary and heavy-ion systems at RHIC energies, finding that the similar tendency of particle ratios across systems suggests baseline contributions from jet fragmentation and parton multiple interactions hold in different collision systems, while the Λ/K₀ˢ enhancement in central Au+Au collisions cannot be reproduced by PYTHIA, supporting the coalescence hadronization picture.
