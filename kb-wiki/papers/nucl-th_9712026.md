# Estimates of dilepton spectra from open charm and bottom decays

> The spectra of lepton pairs from correlated open charm and bottom decays in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions are calculated. Our approach includes energy loss effects of the fast heavy quarks in deconfined matter which are determined by temperature and density of the expanding parton medium. We find a noticeable suppression of the initial transverse momentum spectrum of heavy quarks due to the energy loss within the central rapidity covered by the PHENIX detector system. As a result the dilepton yields from correlated decays of heavy quarks are in the same order of magnitude as the Drell-Yan signal.

## Key claim
The dilepton yields from correlated open charm and bottom decays, after including energy loss effects of heavy quarks in an expanding deconfined parton medium, are in the same order of magnitude as the Drell-Yan signal at RHIC, rather than being clearly suppressed below it as earlier constant-energy-loss approximations had suggested.

## Method
The authors calculated initial heavy quark pair production using lowest-order perturbative QCD with a K-factor of 2 and the MRS D'- structure functions, then modeled the propagation of charm and bottom quarks through a longitudinally expanding parton medium with time-dependent temperature and fugacity. The energy loss rate was taken from the Baier et al. (1997) formalism for gluon radiation, with the stopping power depending on the evolving medium properties; a Monte Carlo simulation with random initial positions and orientations in the transverse plane was used to integrate the equations of motion. The resulting heavy quark spectra were fragmented into D and B mesons using a delta-function fragmentation, and dilepton spectra from correlated semileptonic decays were generated with JETSET 7.4, applying the PHENIX detector acceptance cuts of electron pseudorapidity |η_e| < 0.35 and transverse momentum p_T^e > 1 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Heavy quark masses used: m_c = 1.5 GeV, m_b = 4.5 GeV.
- Initial temperature of the mini-jet plasma at RHIC: T_i = 550 MeV at initial time τ_i = 0.2 fm/c.
- Initial temperature at LHC: T_i = 1000 MeV.
- Deconfinement temperature: T_c = 170 MeV.
- For a heavy quark with initial momentum of 5 GeV at RHIC, the stopping power -dE/dx drops from 2.8 GeV/fm down to 0.5 GeV/fm during the expansion of deconfined matter.
- Average branching ratio for D → l + X: 12%; for b → l + X: 10%.
- Electron transverse momentum cut: p_T^e > 1 GeV.
- Electron pseudorapidity acceptance for PHENIX: |η_e| < 0.35; for ALICE at LHC: |η_e| ≤ 0.9.

## Context
This work extends the earlier studies by Shuryak (1997) and Lin et al. (1997), which used a constant energy loss rate of 1 or 2 GeV/fm, by implementing a time-dependent energy loss determined by the evolving temperature and density of the deconfined medium following the Baier et al. (1997) gluon radiation mechanism. The results show that at RHIC, the charm and bottom decay dilepton signals compete with the Drell-Yan yield, whereas at LHC the bottom signal remains above Drell-Yan even with strong energy loss; this enables future measurements at PHENIX and ALICE to distinguish between the background from heavy quark decays and the thermal dilepton signal from a quark-gluon plasma.
