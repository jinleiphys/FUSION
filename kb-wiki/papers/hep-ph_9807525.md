# Do hep neutrinos affect the solar neutrino energy spectrum?

> If the low energy cross section for 3He + p goes to 4He + e + nu_e, the `hep' reaction, is > 20 times larger than the best (but uncertain) theoretical estimates, then this reaction could significantly influence the electron energy spectrum produced by solar neutrino interactions and measured in the SuperKamiokande, SNO, and ICARUS experiments. We compare predicted energy spectra for different assumed hep fluxes and different neutrino oscillation scenarios with the observed SuperKamiokande spectrum. The spectra with enhanced hep contributions provide better fits to the SuperKamiokande data.

## Key claim

If the low-energy cross section for the `hep' reaction (³He + p → ⁴He + e⁺ + νₑ) is more than about 20 times larger than the best theoretical estimate of S₀(hep) = 2.3 × 10⁻²⁰ keV b, then hep neutrinos could significantly distort the measured solar neutrino energy spectrum; the authors find that enhanced hep fluxes provide better fits to the SuperKamiokande data but cannot rule out such large enhancements from first-principle physics.

## Method

The authors performed global fits to all available solar neutrino data (total event rates from chlorine, GALLEX, SAGE, and SuperKamiokande; the SuperKamiokande energy spectrum in 16 bins from 6.5 to 20 MeV; and the SuperKamiokande zenith-angle dependence) allowing the hep neutrino flux to vary as a free parameter α times the standard S₀(hep) value. They compared the fits for three scenarios: no oscillations, MSW oscillations, and vacuum oscillations, using the same methods as their earlier BKS98 analysis.

## Key numbers

- Best-estimate hep flux: φ(hep) = 2.1 × 10³ cm⁻² s⁻¹, with only 3% uncertainty from solar model parameters if S₀(hep) is known exactly (Eq. 4)
- Best-estimate S₀(hep) = (2.3 ± 0.9) × 10⁻²⁰ keV b (Schiavilla et al., 1992)
- Best-fit enhancement factor α for no-oscillation scenario: α = 26, giving χ²_min = 25.3 (25 d.o.f., P = 0.954)
- Best-fit α for MSW scenario: α = 25, giving χ²_min = 30.7 (25 d.o.f., P = 0.80)
- Best-fit α for vacuum oscillation scenario: α = 30, giving χ²_min = 23.0 (17 d.o.f., P = 0.85)
- For α = 1 (standard flux), no-oscillation fit gives χ²_min = 36.6 (P = 0.998)
- Predicted events in 16-20 MeV bin for 504 days: ~12 ± 2 for best global fits (large α), versus ~6-7 for α = 1
- Ratio r of events in 14-16 MeV bin to 16-20 MeV bin: r ≥ 4 for large α, r ≥ 10 for α = 1
- Best-fit MSW parameters with arbitrary hep flux: Δm² = 5.4 × 10⁻⁶ eV², sin²2θ = 5.0 × 10⁻³
- Best-fit vacuum oscillation parameters: Δm² = 7.8 × 10⁻¹¹ eV², sin²2θ = 0.71

## Context

This work extends the global solar neutrino analysis of Bahcall, Krastev, and Smirnov (BKS98) by explicitly allowing the hep neutrino flux to vary, motivated by an excess of high-energy events in the SuperKamiokande energy spectrum reported at Neutrino 98. The paper contradicts the implicit assumption in previous analyses that hep neutrinos are negligible, and shows that the hep flux could be large enough to affect inferences about neutrino oscillations. The results enable future experiments (SuperKamiokande, SNO, ICARUS) to test for hep neutrinos by measuring the energy spectrum above the ⁸B endpoint (~14 MeV), where SNO is predicted to have 2-3 times the event rate of SuperKamiokande in the 14-16 MeV bin.
