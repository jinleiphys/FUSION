# Nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering to 3 GeV

> A partial-wave analysis (PWA) of NN elastic scattering data has been completed. This analysis covers an expanded energy range, from threshold to a laboratory kinetic energy of 3 GeV, in order to include recent elastic pp polarized scattering measurements performed at SATURNE II. Results of the energy-dependent fit are compared with single-energy solutions and Saclay amplitudes obtained via the direct-reconstruction approach. We also comment on the status of Eps1 in the low-energy region.

## Key claim

The partial-wave analysis of nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering has been extended to a laboratory kinetic energy of 3 GeV, but the resulting energy-dependent solution (SP00) shows surprisingly little change from the previous solution (SM97) despite the addition of many new polarization data, particularly from SATURNE II. The authors find no significant evidence for a narrow dibaryon resonance near 2.7 GeV center-of-mass energy, and they argue that the low-energy behavior of the mixing parameter epsilon_1 does not support an anomalously large tensor interaction.

## Method

An energy-dependent partial-wave analysis (PWA) was performed on combined pp and np elastic scattering data up to 1.3 GeV, and on pp data alone from 1.3 to 3 GeV, using the same formalism as previous analyses (Arndt 1994, Arndt 1997). Single-energy solutions were also produced up to 2825 MeV for pp scattering, with initial values and fixed energy derivatives taken from the energy-dependent solution, and results were compared to Saclay amplitudes obtained via direct reconstruction of scattering amplitudes at fixed energies and angles.

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers laboratory kinetic energies from threshold to 3 GeV for pp scattering, and to 1.3 GeV for np scattering.
- The full database increased by 30% since the 1997 analysis (Arndt 1997) and is about 70% larger than the set available for the 1994 analysis (Arndt 1994).
- 4802 new pp polarization data were added, two-thirds of which came from SATURNE II measurements of 9 spin-dependent quantities.
- The energy-dependent analysis (SP00) used 147 variable parameters total.
- The low-energy fit (SP40, 0-400 MeV) used 30 I=1 and 27 I=0 variable parameters, giving chi-squared per datum of 4398/3454 (pp) and 5415/3831 (np); the global fit SP00 gave 4593/3454 (pp) and 5371/3831 (np) over the same range.
- 247 new np spin measurements from the Geneva group at PSI were added, covering 260 to 538 MeV.
- 182 SATURNE II np polarized measurements fall within the energy range, extending from about 1.1 to 2.4 GeV.
- Single-energy solutions were produced up to 2825 MeV for pp scattering.

## Context

This work extends the previous partial-wave analyses of Arndt (1994) to 1.6 GeV and Arndt (1997) to 2.5 GeV, incorporating new pp polarized measurements from SATURNE II and differential cross sections from the EDDA collaboration at COSY. The results are compared with Saclay single-energy analyses and direct-reconstruction amplitudes (Saclay group, 1999), revealing non-uniqueness in the isovector partial waves above 1 GeV. The analysis enables future comparisons with polarization measurements at COSY and JINR, and the extended np analysis would benefit studies of deuteron photodisintegration at Jefferson Lab.
