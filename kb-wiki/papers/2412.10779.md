# Dynamics of Hot QCD Matter 2024 -- Bulk Properties

> The second Hot QCD Matter 2024 conference at IIT Mandi focused on various ongoing topics in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, encompassing theoretical and experimental perspectives. This proceedings volume includes 19 contributions that collectively explore diverse aspects of the bulk properties of hot QCD matter. The topics encompass the dynamics of electromagnetic fields, transport properties, hadronic matter, spin hydrodynamics, and the role of conserved charges in high-energy environments. These studies significantly enhance our understanding of the complex dynamics of hot QCD matter, the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed in high-energy nuclear collisions. Advances in theoretical frameworks, including hydrodynamics, spin dynamics, and fluctuation studies, aim to improve theoretical calculations and refine our knowledge of the thermodynamic properties of strongly interacting matter. Experimental efforts, such as those conducted by the ALICE and STAR collaborations, play a vital role in validating these theoretical predictions and deepening our insight into the QCD phase diagram, collectivity in small systems, and the early-stage behavior of strongly interacting matter. Combining theoretical models with experimental observations offers a comprehensive understanding of the extreme conditions encountered in relativistic heavy-ion and proton-proton collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the pseudogauge freedom in relativistic spin hydrodynamics leads to an unresolved ambiguity in counting transport coefficients for spin evolution, and that the longitudinal polarization sign problem for Lambda hyperons may arise because the global equilibrium condition (thermal vorticity) used at freeze-out is not achievable due to the short fireball lifetime in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The author reviews two theoretical puzzles in relativistic spin hydrodynamics: (1) the pseudogauge transformation of energy-momentum and spin tensors, comparing the Belinfante, Canonical, and de Groot-van Leeuwen-van Weert (GLW) pseudogauge choices to show how spin evolution information is redistributed between tensors; and (2) the longitudinal polarization sign problem, where experimental data from STAR for Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV are compared with theoretical predictions using thermal vorticity, and alternative approaches such as incorporating thermal shear or projected thermal vorticity are discussed.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal spin polarization of Lambda hyperons in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV shows a quadrupole structure in the transverse momentum plane that disagrees in sign with most theoretical predictions (the "sign problem")
- The paper references a mean-field repulsion strength of K = 33 GeV^{-2} for baryons in a hadron resonance gas model, constrained by lattice QCD results for baryon number susceptibilities up to eighth order
- The chiral pseudo-critical temperature at zero baryon density is given as T_pc = 156.5(1.5) MeV from HotQCD (2018) and T_pc = 161.2(1.7) MeV from an ideal HRG model (Biswas 2022)
- The pseudo-critical line is calculated up to mu_B = 750 MeV using the repulsive mean-field QMHRG model
- For p-O and p-C collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 9.9 TeV, elliptic flow v2 is largest in the (5-10)% centrality class for both SOG and alpha-cluster density profiles
- Energy density of charged particles in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV shows a trend around p_T = 3-7 GeV/c, then a plateau region

## Context

This proceedings volume from the Hot QCD Matter 2024 conference collects 19 contributions on bulk properties of hot QCD matter, including electromagnetic fields, transport properties, spin hydrodynamics, and conserved charges. The spin hydrodynamics discussion extends work by Florkowski (2018), Becattini (2017), and others on spin polarization, while addressing the unresolved pseudogauge freedom problem raised by Dey (2023). The hadron resonance gas model work extends studies by Huovinen (2017) and Vovchenko (2016) on repulsive baryon interactions, and provides predictions for the chiral pseudo-critical line that can be tested in future heavy-ion collision experiments at facilities like the LHC and RHIC. The small-system collectivity study using AMPT provides transport model predictions for the upcoming O-O and p-O collisions planned for LHC Run 3, building on earlier work by Behera (2023) on alpha-clustered nuclear structure effects.
