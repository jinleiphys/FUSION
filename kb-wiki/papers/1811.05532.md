# Magneto-Transport in a Chiral Fluid from Kinetic Theory

> We argue that in order to study the magneto-transport in a relativistic Weyl fluid, it is needed to take into account the associated quantum corrections, namely the side-jump effect, at least to second order. To this end, we impose Lorentz invariance to a system of free Weyl fermions in the presence of the magnetic field and find the second order correction to the energy dispersion. By developing a scheme to compute the integrals in the phase space, we show that the mentioned correction has non-trivial effects on the thermodynamics of the system. Specifically, we predict the form of the negative magnetoresistance in the system from the enthalpy density in equilibrium. Then in analogy with Weyl semimetal, in the framework of the chiral kinetic theory and under the relaxation time approximation, we explicitly compute the magneto-conductivities, at low temperature limit ($T\ll μ$). We show that the conductivities obey the set of Ward identities which follow from the generating functional including the Chern-Simons part.

## Key claim

The authors argue that to compute magneto-transport in a relativistic Weyl fluid, one must include the second-order quantum correction to the energy dispersion (the side-jump effect), because this correction itself depends quadratically on the magnetic field and therefore unavoidably contributes to the magnetoresistance; without it, the Ward identities between conductivities are no longer satisfied.

## Method

The authors impose Lorentz invariance on a system of free Weyl fermions in a magnetic field to derive the second-order correction to the energy dispersion. They then use chiral kinetic theory with the relaxation time approximation to compute the magneto-conductivities (electric, thermoelectric, and thermal) in the low-temperature limit (\(T \ll \mu\)), developing a regularization scheme with an infrared cutoff \(\Delta_B\) to handle divergences from the \(1/p^5\) term in the dispersion. Finally, they derive Ward identities from the covariant generating functional (including Chern-Simons terms) and show that their computed conductivities satisfy these constraints.

## Key numbers

- The second-order correction to the energy dispersion is \(\gamma_2(p) = -e^2/(8 p^5)\), derived from solving the Lorentz invariance condition.
- The longitudinal anomalous current coefficient is \(\sigma_B = J_\parallel/(eB) = e\mu/(4\pi^2)\).
- The energy current coefficient is \(\sigma_B^\epsilon = T^{0\parallel}/(eB) = (1/3 + 2/3)(\mu^2/(8\pi^2) + T^2/24)\), where the \(2/3\) contribution comes from the first-order correction.
- The enthalpy density in equilibrium is \(w = \epsilon + p = T^4(\mu^4/(6\pi^2 T^4) + \mu^2/(3T^2) + 7\pi^2/90) + e^2 B^2/(16\pi^2)(1 + O(T^5/\mu^5))\).
- The electric conductivity including the second-order correction is 6.25% less than its value without the quantum correction to the energy dispersion.
- The transverse diagonal stress tensor components satisfy \(T^{11} = T^{22} = p - e^2 B^2/(24\pi^2)\).
- The charge density is \(n = T^3(\mu^3/(6\pi^2 T^3) + \mu/(6T)) + e^2 B^2/(16\pi^2 \mu)(1 + \pi^2 T^2/(3\mu^2) + O(T^4/\mu^4))\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral kinetic theory approach of Son and Spivak (2013) for non-relativistic Weyl semimetals to the relativistic regime, incorporating the side-jump effect previously studied by Son and Yamamoto (2012) and Chen et al. (2014). The authors show that their computed conductivities satisfy the same constraint equations derived from Ward identities as those found by Hartnoll (2009) and Herzog (2009) for 2+1 dimensional systems, and also match the constraints from the covariant hydrodynamic model of Lucas et al. (2016). This enables a method to derive the electrical resistivity from a first-order differential equation once the equilibrium charge density is known.
