# Coherent Photoproduction of Dileptons on Light Nuclei - a New Means to Learn about Vector Mesons

> In the last years much work has been done to learn about the properties of the $ρ$-meson in nuclear matter. In a calculation of the in-medium spectralfunction of the $ρ$ we find that it is strongly modified by baryonic resonances, especially the $D_{13}(1520)$, through its coupling to resonance-hole states. In order to test the predictions of a model for the in-medium properties of the $ρ$-meson so far mostly heavy-ion collision have been used. We argue that the coherent photoproduction of $ρ$-mesons yields additional information about the nature of the medium modification. It can be shown that the production amplitude is sensitive on the momentum dependence of the $ρ$-selfenergy. This can be used to distinguish experimentally between various models for the $ρ$ in matter.

## Key claim

Coherent photoproduction of rho-mesons off light nuclei can distinguish between different models of the rho-meson's in-medium modification, specifically by probing the momentum dependence of the rho selfenergy, which heavy-ion collision dilepton spectra cannot resolve.

## Method

The authors calculate the in-medium rho-meson selfenergy using a resonance-hole model that includes baryonic resonances, particularly the D13(1520), coupling to rho-N states via the low-density theorem. They then compute the cross-section for coherent photoproduction of rho-mesons on carbon-12, where the nucleus remains in its ground state, using a meson-exchange potential from Friman et al. (1997) and accounting for the rho decay into dileptons. The production amplitude is evaluated for different selfenergy models: the resonance-hole model (broadening) and a tadpole model (mass shift), and the results are compared for transverse and longitudinal polarizations.

## Key numbers

*   The vacuum rho-meson mass is given as m_rho = 0.768 GeV.
*   At an incident photon energy of 0.85 GeV, the minimal momentum transfer q_min for producing a particle of mass 0.5 GeV and 0.768 GeV is indicated on the nuclear formfactor of carbon-12.
*   The dilepton mass spectrum dsigma/dm for the reaction gamma + carbon-12 -> V + carbon-12 -> e+e- + carbon-12 shows two peaks at m ~ 0.77 GeV and m ~ 0.55 GeV for the rho-only contribution.
*   The ratio R = dsigma_tr/dm / dsigma_long/dm for the resonance-hole model shows a strong enhancement in the mass region around 0.6 GeV compared to the vacuum case.

## Context

This work extends calculations of the rho-meson selfenergy in nuclear matter by Peters et al. (1998) and addresses the problem that different selfenergy models (e.g., Brown and Rho, Herrmann, Rapp, Klingl and Weise, Friman, Friman and Lutz, Peters et al.) yield very similar dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions, as noted by Cassing and Bratkovskaya (1997). The proposed coherent photoproduction reaction provides a new experimental test of these models, particularly the momentum dependence of the selfenergy, and enables future work including calculation of the Bethe-Heitler background and a more refined production potential consistent with the selfenergy.
