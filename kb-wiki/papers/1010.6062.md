# Exotic dynamically generated baryons with C$=-$1

> We follow a model based on the SU(8) symmetry for the interaction of mesons with baryons. The model treats on an equal footing the pseudo-scalars and the vector mesons, as required by heavy quark symmetry. The T-matrix calculated within an unitary scheme in coupled channels has poles which are interpreted as baryonic resonances.

## Key claim

The SU(8) spin-flavor model predicts a rich spectrum of dynamically generated exotic baryon resonances with charm C=-1, but results for states bound by 200-300 MeV are less reliable because the Weinberg-Tomozawa Lagrangian is only a first-order low-momentum expansion, while states bound by 150 MeV or less are expected to be more precise.

## Method

The authors construct a meson-baryon interaction kernel using the Weinberg-Tomozawa term with SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry, which treats pseudoscalar and vector mesons on equal footing as required by heavy quark symmetry. They compute the T-matrix in a unitary coupled-channel scheme using physical hadron masses and meson decay constants, then identify poles in the T-matrix on appropriate Riemann sheets as resonances. The couplings of each resonance to different channels are obtained from the residues of the T-matrix at the pole positions.

## Key numbers

- The decay constants used are f_Ds = 193.7 MeV and f_D = f_D* = f_Ds* = 157.4 MeV.
- One J=3/2 pole has a mass close to 3100 MeV, corresponding to an experimental claim for an anti-charmed pentaquark.
- A S=0, I=0 state generated by N Dbar and N D*bar coupled-channel dynamics appears bound by only 1 MeV.
- Resonances bound by energies of the order of 200-300 MeV relative to their main channel thresholds are considered less reliable, while states bound by 150 MeV or less are expected to be more precise.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Hofmann and collaborators that used chiral and SU(4) flavor symmetry with the KSFR relation to generate dynamically exotic baryons with C=2, 1, 0, and -1, but those models did not treat pseudoscalar and vector mesons on equal footing. The present SU(8) approach follows the framework developed by Garcia-Recio, Nieves, and Salcedo (2009) and reproduces the SU(3) Weinberg-Tomozawa Lagrangian for light mesons and baryons. The predicted state near 3100 MeV could correspond to the anti-charmed pentaquark claimed experimentally, and the authors suggest that additional decay channels involving the Delta(1232) resonance may have been missed in the experimental search.
