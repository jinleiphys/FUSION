# Neutron beta decay in effective field theory

> Radiative corrections to the lifetime and angular correlation coefficients of neutron beta-decay are evaluated in effecitive field theory. We also evaluate the lowest order nucleon recoil corrections, including weak-magnetism. Our results agree with those of the long-range and model-independent part of previous calculations. In an effective theory the model-dependent radiative corrections are replaced by well-defined low-energy constants. The effective field theory allows a systematic evaluation of higher order corrections to our results to the extent that the relevant low-energy constants are known.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of radiative corrections to neutron beta-decay using a low-energy effective field theory (EFT), which reproduces the model-independent universal function of Sirlin (1959) while replacing model-dependent hadronic radiative corrections with two well-defined low-energy constants (LEC's) that must be determined empirically.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for neutron beta-decay using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT), retaining only interactions between the heavy nucleon field, lepton current, and photons while suppressing pion fields due to the extremely small momentum transfer (Q ~ 0.782 MeV) compared to the pion mass. They evaluate Feynman diagrams up to order α (fine structure constant) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in the 1/m_N expansion, treating α/(2π) and Q/m_N corrections as the same order (~10^{-3}), and renormalize the ultraviolet divergences from photon-loop diagrams using dimensional regularization with four low-energy constants (e_1, e_2, e_V, e_A).

## Key numbers

- Maximum energy release in neutron beta-decay: ΔM = m_n - m_p - m_e = 0.782 MeV
- Nucleon recoil scale: Q/m_N ≈ 0.8 × 10^{-3}
- Radiative correction scale: α/(2π) ~ 10^{-3}
- Pion-pole diagram suppression factor: (Q/m_π)^2 ≈ 3 × 10^{-5} relative to leading order terms
- Pion-loop suppression factor: (m_π/Λ_χ)^2 ≈ 2 × 10^{-2} relative to leading radiative corrections
- Chiral scale: Λ_χ ≈ 4π f_π ≈ 1 GeV, with f_π = 92.4 MeV
- Fermi constant from muon decay: G_F = 1.166 × 10^{-5} GeV^{-2}
- Isovector magnetic moment: μ_V = 4.706
- Rough estimate of LEC e_V^R ≈ 20, giving (α/2π) e_V^R ~ 4 × 10^{-2}
- Expected precision of calculated neutron-decay rate and angular correlation coefficients: better than 10^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the classic calculations of Sirlin (1959), Marciano and Sirlin (1986), and others by providing an effective field theory framework that systematically separates model-independent radiative corrections from short-distance hadronic effects parameterized by low-energy constants. The results enable future empirical determination of these LEC's from neutron beta-decay data, which is important for extracting the CKM matrix element V_ud and the axial-vector coupling constant g_A, complementing the most precise V_ud values obtained from super-allowed 0+ → 0+ nuclear beta-decays.
