# Holographic renormalization and anisotropic black branes in higher curvature gravity

> We consider five-dimensional AdS-axion-dilaton gravity with a Gauss-Bonnet term and find a solution of the equations of motion which corresponds to a black brane exhibiting a spatial anisotropy, with the source of the anisotropy being an axion field linear in one of the horizon coordinates. Our solution is static, regular everywhere on and outside the horizon, and asymptotically AdS. It is analytic and valid in a small anisotropy expansion, but fully non-perturbative in the Gauss-Bonnet coupling. We discuss various features of this solution and use it as a gravity dual to a strongly coupled anisotropic plasma with two independent central charges, $a\neq c$. In the limit of small Gauss-Bonnet coupling, we carry out holographic renormalization of the system using (a recursive variant of) the Hamilton-Jacobi method and derive a generic expression for the boundary stress tensor, which we later specialize to our solution. Finally, we compute the shear viscosity to entropy ratios and conductivities of this anisotropic plasma.

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytic solution for a five-dimensional AdS-axion-dilaton black brane with a Gauss-Bonnet term that exhibits spatial anisotropy, valid in a small anisotropy expansion but fully non-perturbative in the Gauss-Bonnet coupling, and uses it as a gravity dual to a strongly coupled anisotropic plasma with unequal central charges ($a \neq c$).

## Method

The authors solve the equations of motion for five-dimensional AdS-axion-dilaton gravity with a Gauss-Bonnet term by expanding all fields around the isotropic Gauss-Bonnet black brane solution to second order in the anisotropy parameter $a$, where the anisotropy is sourced by an axion field linear in one horizon coordinate ($\chi = a z$). They perform holographic renormalization using a recursive Hamilton-Jacobi method to first order in the Gauss-Bonnet coupling, deriving general expressions for the boundary stress tensor, and then specialize these to their anisotropic solution. Transport coefficients including shear viscosity to entropy ratios and conductivities are computed using the membrane paradigm.

## Key numbers

- Gauss-Bonnet coupling must satisfy $\lambda_{GB} < 1/4$ for the isotropic background solution
- Temperature expression: $T = \sqrt{B_0}\left(\frac{1}{\pi u_H} - \frac{2B_0 - 6\lambda_{GB} + \sqrt{\lambda_{GB}}\log\left(\frac{1+2\sqrt{\lambda_{GB}}}{1-2\sqrt{\lambda_{GB}}}\right) - \log\left(\frac{4B_0}{\sqrt{1-4\lambda_{GB}}}\right)}{48\pi(1-4\lambda_{GB})} u_H a^2 + O(a^4)\right)$
- Entropy density: $s = \frac{\pi}{4G B_0^{3/2}}\left(\pi^2 T^3 + \frac{1}{8}T B_0 a^2 + O(a^4)\right)$
- Boundary speed of light normalization constant: $B_0 = \frac{1}{2}\left(1 + \sqrt{1-4\lambda_{GB}}\right)$

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic black brane solutions of Mateos and Trancanelli (2011) and Mateos, Trancanelli, and Talavera (2011) by adding a Gauss-Bonnet higher curvature correction, which corresponds to allowing different central charges $a \neq c$ in the dual gauge theory, following the framework of Aharony, Buchel, and collaborators. The holographic renormalization method builds on the Hamilton-Jacobi approach developed by Papadimitriou (2011) for axion-dilaton gravity and extends it to include Gauss-Bonnet corrections following Liu and Sabra (2005). The solution enables the study of how higher curvature corrections affect transport coefficients such as shear viscosity and conductivities in anisotropic strongly coupled plasmas, relevant for modeling the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
