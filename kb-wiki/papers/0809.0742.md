# Long-lived $2s$ state of muonic hydrogen: population and lifetime

> Ab initio study of the density-dependent population and lifetime of the long-lived $(μp)_{2s}$ and the yield of $(μp)_{1s}$ atoms with kinetic energy 0.9 keV have been performed for the first time. The direct Coulomb $2s\to 1s$ deexcitation is proved to be the dominant quenching mechanism of the $2s$ state at kinetic energy below $2p$ threshold and explain the lifetime of the metastable $2s$ state and high-energy 0.9 keV component of $(μp)_{1S}$ observed at low densities. The cross sections of the elastic, Stark and Coulomb deexcitation processes have been calculated in the close-coupling approach taking into account for the first time both the closed channels and the threshold effects due to vacuum polarization shifts of the $ns$ states. The cross sections are used as the input data in the detailed study of the atomic cascade kinetics. The theoretical predictions are compared with the known experimental data at low densities. The 40% yield of the 0.9 keV$(μp)_{1s}$ atoms is predicted for liquid hydrogen density.

## Key claim
The direct Coulomb $2s\to 1s$ deexcitation is the dominant quenching mechanism of the metastable $2s$ state in muonic hydrogen at kinetic energies below the $2p$ threshold, contrary to earlier assumptions that resonant muonic molecule formation was responsible. The authors assert this mechanism explains both the observed lifetime of the metastable $2s$ state and the high-energy 0.9 keV component of $(\mu p)_{1s}$ atoms at low densities.

## Method
The authors performed ab initio quantum-mechanical calculations of cross sections for elastic scattering, Stark transitions, and Coulomb deexcitation in $(\mu p)+H$ collisions using a close-coupling approach that included closed channels and threshold effects from vacuum polarization shifts of the $ns$ states. These cross sections were used as input for detailed atomic cascade kinetics calculations covering a density range from $10^{-9}$ to 1 times liquid hydrogen density (LHD), with initial distributions in quantum numbers and kinetic energy fitted to reproduce experimental $K_\alpha$ X-ray yields and kinetic energy distributions.

## Key numbers
- The $2s-2p$ Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen is $E_L = 202.0$ meV.
- The muon decay rate is $\lambda_{\mu} = 4.55 \times 10^5$ s$^{-1}$.
- Liquid hydrogen density (LHD) is $4.25 \times 10^{22}$ atoms/cm$^3$.
- The experimentally extracted quenching rate from the side path model was $4.4^{+2.1}_{-1.8} \times 10^{11}$ s$^{-1}$, while the side path model itself gave $\sim 5 \times 10^{10}$ s$^{-1}$ at LHD.
- The metastable $2s$ fraction is about 1% below $10^{-7}$ LHD, slowly increases to $\sim 4\%$ in the range $10^{-7}-10^{-2}$ LHD, and reaches $\sim 40\%$ at LHD.
- The yield of hot (0.9 keV) $(\mu p)_{1s}$ atoms is predicted to be $\sim 40\%$ at LHD.
- At densities below $10^{-7}$ LHD, the lifetime of the metastable $2s$ state is mainly determined by the muon lifetime; above $10^{-5}-10^{-4}$ LHD, quenching is entirely determined by the Coulomb deexcitation process.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous predictions (Popov and Pomerantsev, 2007) that direct Coulomb deexcitation, rather than resonant muonic molecule formation as assumed in the side path model of WJKF, explains the quenching of the metastable $2s$ state. The calculations improve upon earlier close-coupling approaches by including closed channels and vacuum polarization shifts, and the results disagree with the sharper increase of metastable population up to 65% at LHD predicted by Jensen and Markushin (2002). The predicted density-dependent population and lifetime of the metastable $2s$ state are directly relevant for optimizing conditions in the $\mu p$ Lamb shift experiment, which aims to determine the proton charge radius with $10^{-3}$ relative accuracy.
