# Kaonic modes in hyperonic matter and p-wave kaon condensation

> Kaon excitations (kaonic modes) are investigated in hyperonic matter, where hyperons ($Λ$, $Σ^-$, $Ξ^-$) are mixed in the ground state of neutron-star matter. $P$-wave kaon-baryon interactions as well as the s-wave interactions are taken into account within chiral effective Lagrangian, and the nonrelativistic effective baryon-baryon interactions are incorporated. When the hyperon $Λ$ is more abundant than the proton at high baryon density, a proton-particle-$Λ$-hole mode, which has the $K^+$ quantum number, appears in addition to other particle-hole modes with the $K^-$ quantum number. It is shown that the system becomes unstable with respect to a spontaneous creation of a pair of the particle-hole modes with $K^+$ and $K^-$ quantum numbers, stemming from the p-wave kaon-baryon interaction. The onset density of this p-wave kaon condensation may be lower than that of the s-wave $K^-$ condensation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in hyperonic matter (where hyperons are mixed in the ground state of neutron-star matter), a new mechanism for p-wave kaon condensation can occur at densities potentially lower than the critical density for s-wave K⁻ condensation, driven by the spontaneous creation of a pair of particle-hole modes with K⁺ and K⁻ quantum numbers.

## Method

The author investigates kaon excitations in hyperonic matter using a chiral effective SU(3)ₗ × SU(3)ᵣ Lagrangian that includes both s-wave and p-wave kaon-baryon interactions, supplemented by nonrelativistic effective baryon-baryon interactions from Balberg and Gal (1997). The kaon inverse propagator is derived from an expansion of the effective energy density with respect to the classical kaon field, and the kaon self-energy includes contributions from s-wave scalar and vector interactions as well as p-wave Yukawa couplings to particle-hole excitations involving Λ, Σ⁻, and Ξ⁻ hyperons. The baryon-baryon potential energy density incorporates hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions, with parameters fitted to nuclear saturation properties and hypernuclear experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- The critical density for s-wave K⁻ condensation in neutron-star matter without hyperons is estimated as ρ_B^C = 3–4 ρ₀
- Hyperons appear at baryon number density ρ_B = 2–3 ρ₀ based on relativistic mean-field models
- The p-wave axial vector coupling strengths are: g_Λp = 0.87, g_Σ⁻n = 0.37, g_Ξ⁻Λ = 0.21
- The Λ potential depth in nuclear matter is −27 MeV
- The Ξ⁻ potential depth in nuclear matter is taken as −16 MeV
- For the repulsive Σ⁻ potential (Case I), V_Σ⁻ at nuclear matter density is 23.5 MeV with an isospin-dependent term of 40.2 MeV
- Free kaon mass m_K = 493.7 MeV
- The kaon-hyperon sigma terms are: Σ_KΛ = 374 MeV (for a₃ = −0.9), Σ_KΣ⁻ = 305 MeV, Σ_KΞ⁻ = 374 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon condensation in neutron-star matter by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and others, which considered only s-wave kaon-nucleon interactions, and earlier work by Muto (1993) that included p-wave interactions but without hyperons in the ground state. The paper addresses the competition between kaon condensation and hyperon mixing in neutron stars, which had been studied by Ellis, Kapusta, and Olive (1995) and Schaffner and Mishustin (1996) using only s-wave interactions. The new mechanism involving a proton-particle-Λ-hole mode with K⁺ quantum number enables investigation of p-wave kaon condensation in hyperonic matter, with implications for neutron-star equation of state and cooling.
