# Centrifugal-corrected harmonic oscillator model for spherical proton emitters

> In the present work, we propose an improved harmonic oscillator model to systematically evaluate the proton radioactivity half-lives in spherical nuclei, incorporating centrifugal potential effects. By fitting the experimental data, the centrifugal parameter $d = 0.143$ for the correction term $dl(l+1)$ and nuclear potential depth $V_0 = 62.4$ MeV are obtained. The model integrates the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the BCS method based on the DD-ME2 force to determine spectroscopic factors $S_p$. Moreover, by verifying the linear relationship between the logarithm of the normalized width $\log_{10}{γ^2}$ and fragmentation potential $V_{frag}$, the connection between nuclear structure and tunneling dynamics is confirmed, and an analytical expression for the adjustable parameter $d$ corresponding to the centrifugal potential is derived as $d^{\rm{Ae}}$ $\approx$ 0.167. Compared with $d^{\rm{Ae}}$, the modified model based on $d$ yields results in better agreement with experimental half-lives, and is able to control the error of the experimental data within a factor of 2.4. Furthermore, the extended improved model is used to predict the half-lives of some possible proton radioactivity candidates in NUBASE2020 that are energetically allowed or have been observed but not yet quantified. This work improves the accuracy of proton radioactivity studies and provides a robust theoretical framework for future nuclear structure research.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an improved harmonic oscillator model for proton radioactivity half-lives that incorporates centrifugal potential effects via a correction term \(dl(l+1)\), but the model's accuracy is conditional on fitting the centrifugal parameter \(d = 0.143\) to experimental data, while an analytically derived value \(d^{\rm{Ae}} \approx 0.167\) yields less accurate results.

## Method

The authors extended the harmonic oscillator model (HOPM) previously used for alpha decay to spherical proton emitters by adding a centrifugal correction term \(dl(l+1)\) to the logarithmic half-life expression. They calculated spectroscopic factors \(S_p\) using relativistic mean field (RMF) theory combined with the BCS method with the DD-ME2 force, and determined the nuclear potential depth \(V_0 = 62.4\) MeV and centrifugal parameter \(d = 0.143\) by fitting experimental half-lives of 32 spherical proton emitters. They also derived an analytic expression for the centrifugal parameter \(d^{\rm{Ae}} \approx 0.167\) from the linear relationship between the logarithm of the normalized width \(\log_{10}{\gamma^2}\) and fragmentation potential \(V_{frag}\).

## Context

This work extends the harmonic oscillator model (HOPM) previously applied to alpha decay by Bayrak (2020) to the study of spherical proton emitters, motivated by the shared quantum tunneling mechanism among alpha decay, cluster radioactivity, and proton radioactivity. The model improves upon existing approaches such as the universal decay law (UDL) by Qi (2012), the new Geiger-Nuttall law (N-GNL) by Chen (2019), and the unified fission model (UFM) by Jian (2010), achieving better agreement with experimental half-lives particularly for high-angular-momentum decays. The work also builds on the analytic framework of Delion (2009) connecting reduced widths to fragmentation potentials, and enables predictions of half-lives for proton radioactivity candidates in the NUBASE2020 database that are energetically allowed or observed but not yet quantified.
