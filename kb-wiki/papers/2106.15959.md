# Comparison of fission and quasi-fission modes

> Quantum shell effects are known to affect the formation of fragments in nuclear fission. Shell effects also affect quasi-fission reactions occurring in heavy-ion collisions. Systematic time-dependent Hartree-Fock simulations of 50Ca+176Yb collisions show that the mass equilibration between the fragments in quasi-fission is stopped when they reach similar properties to those in the asymmetric fission mode of the 226Th compound nucleus. Similar shell effects are then expected to determine the final repartition of nucleons between the nascent fragments in both mechanisms. Future experimental studies that could test these observations are discussed.

## Key claim
Systematic TDHF simulations of 50Ca+176Yb collisions show that mass equilibration in quasi-fission stops when the fragments reach proton and neutron numbers similar to those in the asymmetric fission mode of the 226Th compound nucleus, suggesting that the same shell effects determine the final nucleon repartition in both mechanisms. However, the symmetric fission mode of 226Th, which has comparable yields in fission, is not observed in the quasi-fission simulations.

## Method
The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock calculations with BCS pairing correlations to construct a potential energy surface for 226Th, identifying symmetric and asymmetric fission valleys. They then carried out 40 time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations of 50Ca+176Yb collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 172 MeV (13% above the Coulomb barrier), varying the initial orientation of the prolately deformed 176Yb target and scanning orbital angular momenta in steps of 2 hbar. The Skyrme SLy4d energy density functional was used consistently for both static and dynamic calculations.

## Key numbers
- Center-of-mass energy: 172 MeV, approximately 13% above the Coulomb barrier (V_B ~ 151.8 MeV from Swiatecki systematics)
- Contact time for an example quasi-fission trajectory with L=82 hbar: tau ~ 22.9 zs (1 zs = 10^-21 s)
- Critical angular momenta for fusion: L_c ~ 60 hbar for side collisions with 176Yb, L_c ~ 80 hbar for tip collisions
- Quasi-elastic collisions occur at L_q ~ 68 hbar (side) and L_q ~ 96 hbar (tip)
- Energy difference between saddle points in the 226Th PES: 1.2 MeV
- Mesh size for TDHF calculations: 0.8 fm, on grids of 72 x 72 x (28/2) points
- 176Yb ground-state deformation: beta_2 ~ 0.33 (prolate)

## Context
This work extends studies of shell effects in fission (Schmidt 2000, Bockstiegel 2008, Chatillon 2019) and quasi-fission (Itkis 2004, Nishio 2008, Kozulin 2010, Wakhle 2014, Morjean 2017, Hinde 2018) by directly comparing the fragment properties from both mechanisms in the same compound system (226Th). The octupole deformed shell effects at Z=52-56 proposed by Scamps (2018) to explain asymmetric fission are invoked as the likely origin of the stopped mass equilibration in quasi-fission. The results suggest that quasi-fission reactions could be used to probe fission modes in nuclei that are difficult to produce experimentally, such as superheavy nuclei, though the authors caution that not all fission modes may appear in quasi-fission and relative abundances may differ.
