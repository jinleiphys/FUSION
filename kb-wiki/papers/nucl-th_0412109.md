# Applying the Bloch-Horowitz equation to s- and p-shell nuclei

> The Bloch-Horowitz (BH) equation has been successfully applied to calculating the binding energies of the deuteron and 3H/3He systems. For the three-body systems, BH was found to be perturbative for certain choices of the harmonic oscillator (HO) parameter b. We extend upon this work by applying this formalism to the alpha particle and certain five-, six-, and seven-body nuclei in the p-shell. Furthermore, we use only the leading order BH term and work in the smallest allowed included-spaces for each few-body system (0hw and 2hw). We show how to calculate A-body matrix elements within this formalism. Stationary solutions are found for all nuclei investigated within this work. The calculated binding energy of the alpha particle differs by ~1 MeV from Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations. However, calculated energies of p-shell nuclei are underbound, leaving p-shell nuclei that are susceptible to cluster breakup. Furthermore, convergence is suspect when the size of the included-space is increased. We attribute this undesirable behavior to lack of a sufficiently binding mean-field.

## Key claim
The Bloch-Horowitz (BH) equation, applied at leading order in the smallest allowed included-spaces (0ħω and 2ħω), yields stationary solutions for alpha particle and p-shell nuclei up to seven-body systems, but the calculated binding energies are underbound and convergence is suspect when the included-space is increased, which the authors attribute to the lack of a sufficiently binding mean-field.

## Method
The authors applied the leading-order Bloch-Horowitz equation to the alpha particle and to five-, six-, and seven-body p-shell nuclei, working exclusively in the 0ħω and 2ħω harmonic oscillator spaces. They calculated A-body matrix elements within this formalism and solved for stationary solutions. The resulting binding energies were compared to Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for the alpha particle and examined for convergence behavior when the model space was enlarged.

## Key numbers
- The calculated binding energy of the alpha particle differs by approximately 1 MeV from Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations.
- The study examined nuclei in the p-shell up to seven-body systems, using only the 0ħω and 2ħω included-spaces.
- The harmonic oscillator parameter b was varied, and for the three-body systems (³H/³He) BH was found to be perturbative for certain choices of b.

## Context
This work extends the application of the Bloch-Horowitz equation from the deuteron and ³H/³He systems (where it was previously shown to be perturbative for certain HO parameter choices) to the alpha particle and heavier p-shell nuclei. The results contradict the expectation that the BH approach would remain perturbative for these larger systems; instead, the underbinding and convergence problems point to the need for a more binding mean-field. This enables future work to incorporate a mean-field term into the BH framework to improve convergence and binding for medium-mass nuclei.
