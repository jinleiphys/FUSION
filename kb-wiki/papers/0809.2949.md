# Collective phenomena in non-central nuclear collisions

> Recent developments in the field of anisotropic flow in nuclear collision are reviewed. The results from the top AGS energy to the top RHIC energy are discussed with emphasis on techniques, interpretation, and uncertainties in the measurements.

## Key claim

This review argues that anisotropic flow measurements, particularly elliptic flow, provide strong evidence for the formation of a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) at RHIC, but the authors emphasize that the interpretation depends critically on understanding uncertainties from initial conditions, viscous effects, and fluctuations, with the data potentially being up to 30% below the ideal hydrodynamic limit even for the most central collisions.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results and theoretical models for anisotropic flow (directed flow v1, elliptic flow v2, and higher harmonics) in nuclear collisions from AGS to RHIC energies. It examines the interplay of radial and anisotropic flow, coalescence scaling for light nuclei, the excitation functions of flow harmonics, and the use of the v2/ε plot scaled by particle density to compare data across systems and energies. The review also discusses viscous hydrodynamic calculations, hybrid models combining hydrodynamics with hadronic cascades, and the role of initial geometry fluctuations from participant nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow v2 at RHIC approaches predictions of ideal hydrodynamic models, with the first RHIC results shown in Fig. 2 of Adler (2002) plotting v2 vs charged hadron multiplicity divided by maximum observed multiplicity.
- In the Knudsen number analysis, the parameter K0 ≈ 0.7 is estimated from fits to data, and the extracted shear viscosity to entropy ratio is η/s = 0.11–0.19 depending on CGC or Glauber initial conditions.
- Even minimal viscosity with η/s = 1/(4π) leads to approximately 25–30% reduction in flow values in Au+Au collisions and probably more than 50% in Cu+Cu collisions.
- At SPS energies, v2 values from hybrid hydro+RQMD calculations are about a factor of 2 lower compared to ideal hydrodynamic predictions.
- The directed flow of charged hadrons at RHIC for Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at the same fraction of cross section shows the same magnitude over the whole measured pseudorapidity range, despite Au+Au being three times more massive.
- The directed flow as a function of η − y_beam for different species and collision energies collapses into a unified curve, a scaling not predicted by any model calculation.

## Context

This review extends the work on anisotropic flow from the AGS (E877, E895 Collaborations) through SPS (NA49) to RHIC (STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS), building on the foundational hydrodynamic prediction by Ollitrault (1992) that in-plane elliptic flow signals collective expansion. The paper discusses the v2/ε plot proposed by Voloshin and Poskanzer (1999) as a tool to compare results across energies and systems, and it addresses recent developments in viscous hydrodynamics by Romatschke (2007), Song (2007), and Dusling (2007) that show significant viscous effects even at minimal viscosity. The review also covers the clarification of eccentricity fluctuations by Voloshin (2007), Bhalerao (2006), and Alver (2008), showing that higher-order cumulant flow values provide elliptic flow relative to the reaction plane rather than the participant plane. Future beam energy scans at RHIC are identified as necessary to clarify whether the v2/ε data exhibit non-smooth behavior at the color percolation phase transition predicted by Satz (2002).
