# Study of possible $DND^*$ bound states

> We start from a recently favored picture in which the $Λ_c(2940)$ and $Λ_c(2910)$ correspond mostly to $ND^*$ bound states with $J^P = 1/2^-,\, 3/2^-$ and then add a $D$ as a third particle, looking for the possible binding of the $DND^*$ three body system within the framework of the Fixed Center Approximation. We find that the system is bound with respect to the corresponding $Λ_c^* D$ threshold with a binding of about $60$ MeV and a width of about $90$ MeV. Alternatively we assume a cluster of $ND$ and a $D^*$ meson interacting with the cluster and we find similar results. The observation of these states of $J^P = 1/2^+,\, 3/2^+$ would provide new and valuable information concerning the $DN$ and $D^* N$ interaction, an issue of current debate.

# Study of possible $DND^*$ bound states

## Key claim
The $DND^*$ three-body system is predicted to form bound states with $J^P = 1/2^+$ and $3/2^+$, with binding energies of about 60 MeV relative to the $\Lambda_c^* D$ threshold, but this result depends on the assumption that the $\Lambda_c(2940)$ and $\Lambda_c(2910)$ are predominantly $ND^*$ molecular states and that the $\Lambda_c(2765)$ is the corresponding $DN$ bound state partner.

## Method
The authors apply the Fixed Center Approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations, treating either a $ND^*$ cluster (the $\Lambda_c(2940)$ or $\Lambda_c(2910)$) with an external $D$ meson, or alternatively a $ND$ cluster (the $\Lambda_c(2765)$) with an external $D^*$ meson. The $DN$ and $DD^*$ scattering amplitudes are constructed using empirical masses and widths of known resonances, with couplings determined from the Weinberg compositeness condition. The $D$ propagator is modulated by the form factor of the bound two-body cluster, regulated by a cutoff $q_\mathrm{max} = 600$ MeV.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of the $DND^*$ system relative to the $\Lambda_c^* D$ threshold: about 60 MeV (stated as "about 60 MeV" in abstract; specific values from Table 1: 4738.6 MeV for $1/2^+$ and 4726.5 MeV for $3/2^+$ in the $D(ND^*)$ scenario with $q_\mathrm{max}=600$ MeV)
- Width of the three-body states: about 90 MeV
- In the alternative $D^*(ND)$ scenario, binding energies relative to the $\Lambda_c(2765) D^*$ threshold: 15 MeV for $1/2^+$ state and 70 MeV for $3/2^+$ state (masses: 4760.6 MeV and 4706.8 MeV respectively, with $q_\mathrm{max}=600$ MeV)
- $DN$ coupling constant $g_{DN} = 3.70$ for the $\Lambda_c(2765)$ state
- $D^*N$ coupling constants: $g_{D^*N}^{S=1/2} = 2.63$, $g_{D^*N}^{S=3/2} = 3.71$
- $DD^*$ coupling constant $g_{DD^*}^{I=0} = 5359$ MeV
- $T_{cc}$ mass: $M_{D^0 D^{*+}} - 360$ keV; $T_{cc}$ width: 48 keV

## Context
This work extends the study of three-body hadronic molecules containing one nucleon and two mesons, following earlier investigations of the $\pi\pi N$, $K\bar{K}N$, and $ND\bar{D}$ systems (Martinez-Torres et al., 2010; Xiao et al., 2011). It directly builds on the suggestion by Luo et al. (2022) that the $DND^*$ system could be bound, using the Gaussian expansion method, but here the FCA approach additionally provides widths. The paper adopts the interpretation that the $\Lambda_c(2940)$ and $\Lambda_c(2910)$ are $ND^*$ molecular states, as proposed by Yue et al. (2024), and uses heavy quark symmetry to identify the $\Lambda_c(2765)$ as the $DN$ spin partner. The predicted $DND^*$ states, if observed, would help resolve the ongoing debate about the nature of the $DN$ and $D^*N$ interactions, complementing information from the $P_c$ pentaquarks (LHCb, 2019) and the $T_{cc}$ tetraquark (LHCb, 2021).
