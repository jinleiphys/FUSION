# Non-relativistic QCD Study of Excited Bottomonia at Finite Temperatures on a Fine Lattice

> The temperature dependence of bottomonium correlators up to the 3S and 3P excited states are presented in the range $T \simeq 133-250$ MeV. These lattice calculations employ the non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) approach for bottom quarks on (2+1)-flavor gauge backgrounds, using the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action near the physical point. The study utilizes a fine lattice spacing of 0.0493 fm at all temperatures. Extended bottomonium operators are implemented to achieve optimized overlaps with the targeted excited states, enhancing sensitivity to thermal effects. To probe in-medium modifications of excited bottomonia, we extract thermal widths and in-medium masses from bottomonium correlators, parameterizing the spectral function with a Gaussian ansatz. Our results confirm nonzero thermal widths for various bottomonium states as the temperature increases, while no significant mass shifts are observed. Additionally, we check that the in-medium properties of bottomonia are almost not affected by variations in the choice of extended operators.

## Key claim

The paper reports nonzero thermal widths for various bottomonium states up to 3S and 3P excitations as temperature increases from 133 to 250 MeV, but finds no significant mass shifts. The authors caution that the Gaussian parametrization used to extract these widths is not physically motivated, so the Gaussian widths should be interpreted only as qualitative results or possible upper bounds for the in-medium width.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using the non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) approach for bottom quarks on (2+1)-flavor gauge backgrounds generated with the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action near the physical point. They used a fine lattice spacing of 0.0493 fm at all temperatures and implemented two types of extended meson operators (Gaussian-smeared and wave-function-optimized sources) to achieve optimized overlap with targeted excited states up to 3S and 3P. In-medium masses and thermal widths were extracted by fitting continuum-subtracted correlators with a Gaussian ansatz for the spectral function.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: T = 133 to 250 MeV, corresponding to temporal extents N_tau = 30 to 16 at fixed lattice spacing a = 0.0493 fm
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0493 fm
- Spatial extent: N_sigma = 64
- Bottom-quark mass parameter: aM_b = 0.957 (fixed by matching the kinetic mass of eta_b to its PDG value)
- Light-quark masses set to m_s/20, corresponding to Goldstone pion mass of approximately 160 MeV
- For Upsilon(1S) and chi_b0(1P), plateaus in effective masses exist up to T = 182 MeV and T = 167 MeV respectively, indicating these states remain bound with roughly vacuum masses
- In-medium widths show hierarchical pattern: Gamma_med,1S(P) < Gamma_med,2S(P) < Gamma_med,3S(P) across the temperature range

## Context

This work extends previous lattice NRQCD studies of bottomonia using extended operators by Larsen (2019) to include higher excited states up to 3S and 3P, and to systematically study their temperature dependence. The results contradict the original color-screening picture of Matsui (1986) by finding no significant mass shifts, consistent with more recent work showing that the quark-antiquark potential acquires a complex imaginary component without strong screening of the real part (Laine 2006, Brambilla 2008, Bala 2021, Bazavov 2023). The findings enable future comparisons with experimental sequential suppression patterns observed at CMS (2011, 2012) and STAR (2019), and motivate further work using Breit-Wigner parametrizations of the spectral function as noted by the authors.
