# Effects of tensor interaction on pseudospin energy splitting and shell correction

> In the framework of a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach combined with BCS method, the role of the tensor force on the pseudospin energy splitting for tin isotope chain is investigated. The tensor force turns out to obviously affect the pseudospin energy splitting of the spin-unsaturated nuclei. Since the tensor force shifts the single-particle levels, it modifies the single-particle level density and the shell correction energy thereof. The influence of the tensor interaction on shell correction energy is considerable according to our analysis taking a magic nucleus $^{132}$Sn as well as a superheavy nucleus $^{298}114$ as examples. This modification of the shell correction energy due to the tensor component affects the stability of the superheavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The tensor force significantly affects pseudospin energy splitting and shell correction energy in nuclei, but the magnitude and direction of this effect depend on whether the nucleus is spin-unsaturated and on the specific pseudospin doublet considered; the tensor force can either restore or break pseudospin symmetry depending on the doublet.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations with BCS pairing for a chain of tin isotopes, using two parameter sets (SLy5 and T31) both with and without a tensor component added perturbatively (SLy5+TF and T31+TF). The pseudospin energy splitting was computed for proton and neutron pseudospin doublets, and shell correction energies were extracted using the Strutinsky method with a Gaussian folding function and associated Laguerre polynomial of order M=3.

## Key numbers

- For $^{132}$Sn without tensor force: neutron shell correction energy ≈ -12.0 MeV (SLy5) and -10.3 MeV (T31); proton shell correction energy ≈ -7.0 MeV (SLy5) and -5.5 MeV (T31)
- For $^{132}$Sn with tensor force: neutron shell correction lowered by about 2.3 MeV (SLy5+TF) and 3.0 MeV (T31+TF); proton shell correction lowered by about 1.1 MeV (SLy5+TF) and 1.8 MeV (T31+TF)
- For $^{298}$114: proton shell correction energy altered by 4-5 MeV due to tensor force; neutron shell correction altered by 1.0-1.6 MeV
- The plateau condition for $^{132}$Sn shell correction was satisfied for smoothing range γ between 1.5 and 2.7 for neutrons and 1.2 to 2.0 for protons (with box size 10 fm)
- The total binding energy convergence was achieved with accuracy 0.03% using box size 10 fm

## Context

This work extends studies of tensor force effects on shell evolution by Otsuka and collaborators (2005) and on spin-orbit splitting by several groups (2008-2010), applying the tensor component to pseudospin symmetry for the first time. The pseudospin concept was experimentally observed about 40 years ago and has been used to explain deformation, superdeformation, magnetic moments, and identical bands. The results enable improved predictions for superheavy nucleus stability and fission barriers, as the tensor force modifies shell correction energies that are crucial for these phenomena.
