# Scale for the Phase Diagram of Quantum Chromodynamics

> Matter described by Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), the theory of strong interactions, may undergo phase transitions when its temperature and the chemical potentials are varied. QCD at finite temperature is studied in the laboratory by colliding heavy-ions at varying beam energies. We present a test of QCD in the non-perturbative domain through a comparison of thermodynamic fluctuations predicted in lattice computations with the experimental data of baryon number distributions in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. This study provides evidence for thermalization in these collisions, and allows us to find the crossover temperature between normal nuclear matter and a deconfined phase called the quark gluon plasma. This value allows us to set a scale for the phase diagram of QCD.

## Key claim

The paper claims that lattice QCD predictions for baryon number fluctuations agree with experimental data from heavy-ion collisions, allowing a direct extraction of the crossover temperature \(T_c\) between hadronic matter and the quark-gluon plasma. The key hedge is that this extraction relies on the assumption that net-proton distributions measured experimentally reflect net-baryon distributions up to distortions smaller than the estimated errors, and that the lattice computations used a pion mass of about 230 MeV rather than the physical value.

## Method

The authors compared ratios of cumulants of net-proton distributions (\(S\sigma\), \(\kappa\sigma^2\), and \(\kappa\sigma/S\)) measured by the STAR experiment at RHIC for Au+Au collisions at three beam energies (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 39\), 62.4, and 200 GeV) with lattice QCD predictions for the corresponding ratios of baryon number susceptibilities. The lattice computations used two flavors of staggered quarks with a cutoff of \(1/a \simeq 960-1000\) MeV and a pion mass of about 230 MeV, with Taylor series coefficients resummed via Pade approximants to extrapolate to freeze-out conditions. The scale \(T_c\) was then determined by minimizing a \(\chi^2\) function comparing the lattice predictions (computed for different assumed \(T_c\) values) to the experimental \(m_3 = \kappa\sigma/S\) data.

## Key numbers

- The crossover temperature extracted from the fit is \(T_c = 175^{+1}_{-7}\) MeV, with the minimum \(\chi^2\) occurring at 175 MeV and errors bounded by +5 and -10 MeV (refined to +1 and -7 MeV by piecewise linear interpolation).
- The lattice cutoff used was \(1/a \simeq 960-1000\) MeV, with the bare quark mass tuned to give a pion mass of about 230 MeV.
- For Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV with impact parameter less than 3 fm, the chemical potentials were \(\mu_B = 22 \pm 4.5\) MeV and \(\mu_S = 3.9 \pm 2.6\) MeV, with \(\mu_Q\) even smaller.
- The experimental data for \(m_1 = S\sigma\) and \(m_2 = \kappa\sigma^2\) were measured at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 39\), 62.4, and 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions with impact parameter less than 3 fm, using protons and anti-protons in the momentum range 400 MeV/\(c\) to 800 MeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD computations of baryon number susceptibilities (from the ILGTI collaboration) and the hadron resonance gas model parameterizations of freeze-out conditions (Peter and Cleymans, 2003) by directly comparing them to experimental data from the STAR collaboration. It provides the first direct test of non-perturbative QCD thermodynamics against experimental data from bulk hadronic matter, and enables a new method for determining the QCD crossover temperature that closes the "circle of reasoning" connecting lattice QCD, hadron phenomenology, and heavy-ion collision observables. The approach can be extended to search for the QCD critical point by measuring \(\kappa\sigma^2\) as a function of beam energy, where a non-monotonic variation would indicate proximity to the critical point.
