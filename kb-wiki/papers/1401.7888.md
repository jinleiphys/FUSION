# Baryon stopping in the Color Glass Condensate formalism: A phenomenological study

> The net-baryon production at forward rapidities is investigated considering the Color Glass Condensate formalism. We assume that at large energies the coherence of the projectile quarks is lost and that the leading baryon production mechanism changes from recombination to independent fragmentation. The phenomenological implications for net-baryon production in $pp/pA/AA$ collisions are analysed and predictions for LHC energies are presented.

# Baryon stopping in the Color Glass Condensate formalism: A phenomenological study

## Key claim

The authors assume that at sufficiently high collision energies, the coherence of projectile valence quarks is lost and the leading baryon production mechanism changes from quark recombination to independent fragmentation; this assumption is tested by comparing predictions to data at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, with the caveat that the independent fragmentation approach underpredicts data at lower energies (SPS and RHIC at 62.4 GeV) but describes data well at higher energies (RHIC at 200 GeV).

## Method

The authors compute net-baryon production in pp, pA, and AA collisions using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism, where the differential cross section is expressed as a convolution of the projectile valence quark distribution, a net-baryon fragmentation function (difference between baryon and antibaryon fragmentation), and the unintegrated gluon distribution of the target. The unintegrated gluon distribution is derived from the forward dipole scattering amplitude using the BUW model, which incorporates geometric scaling and the correct large-pT behavior, and results are compared to predictions using the older GBW model. The authors integrate over transverse momentum to obtain rapidity distributions and xF spectra, and compute the nuclear modification ratio RpA for pPb collisions.

## Key numbers

- Net-baryon rapidity distributions are computed for PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 17.3 GeV (SPS) and AuAu collisions at sqrt(s) = 62.4 and 200 GeV (RHIC), with the BUW model improving the description at 200 GeV forward rapidities compared to the MT and GBW models.
- For AuAu collisions at RHIC, the BUW model reproduces the pT spectra up to pT ~ 4 GeV, while the GBW model fails for pT > 1 GeV due to its lack of DGLAP evolution.
- Predictions for PbPb collisions at LHC energies (sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV) show that GBW and BUW predictions are distinct at large pT for y <= 5, but become similar at larger rapidities due to phase space limitations.
- The nuclear modification ratio RpA for net-proton production in pPb collisions shows suppression at small pT, increasing with energy and rapidity.
- The proton-to-pion ratio in PbPb collisions is small, decreases with pT, and depends weakly on rapidity and collision energy, in contrast to experimental data showing a ratio close to 1 at intermediate pT.

## Context

This work extends previous CGC-based studies of net-baryon production by Tani (2012) and Tani (2013), which used the GBW model and did not compute pT distributions; the authors improve upon those calculations by using the BUW dipole amplitude (Buccella et al., 2010), which correctly reproduces the large-pT behavior and describes RHIC and LHC hadron production data. The study is motivated by the LHCf experimental results for neutral pion production (LHCf Collaboration, 2013), which were well described by the CGC formalism. The authors also compare their results to the baryon anomaly observed by ALICE (Chinelato, 2013) and find that the CGC formalism does not produce this anomaly, suggesting it originates from central-region dynamics of gluons and sea quarks rather than forward valence quark fragmentation.
