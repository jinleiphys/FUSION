# Study of light-cone distribution amplitudes for p-wave heavy mesons

> In this paper, a study of light-cone distribution amplitudes for p-wave heavy mesons is presented in both general and heavy quark frameworks. Within the light-front approach, the leading twist light-cone distribution amplitudes, ϕ_M(u) and their relevant decay constants of heavy scalar, axial-vector and tensor mesons, f_M, are formulated. The relations of some decay constants can be simplified when the heavy quark limit is taken into account. After fixing the parameters which appear in a Gaussian wave function, the corresponding decay constants are calculated and compared with those of other theoretical approaches. The curves and the first six ξmoments of ϕ_M(u) are plotted and estimated. These results all endorse the requirements of heavy quark symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper formulates leading-twist light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs) for p-wave heavy mesons (scalar, axial-vector, and tensor) within both a general framework and the heavy quark limit, showing that the relations among decay constants simplify under heavy quark symmetry, but the actual numerical values and shape parameters depend on the specific Gaussian wavefunction parameters fitted to the meson masses.

## Method

The author uses the light-front quark model (LFQM) to derive the leading-twist LCDAs \(\phi_M(u)\) and decay constants \(f_M\) for scalar, axial-vector, and tensor heavy mesons. The formalism is developed first in a general relativistic framework using nonlocal operator matrix elements, then simplified in the heavy quark limit using heavy quark effective theory (HQET) with velocity-dependent variables. A Gaussian wavefunction is assumed for the momentum-space distribution, and its parameters are fixed by fitting to known meson masses; the resulting decay constants and the first six \(\xi\)-moments of the LCDAs are then computed numerically.

## Key numbers

- Decay constants for scalar, axial-vector, and tensor heavy mesons are calculated and compared with other theoretical approaches; specific numerical values are not listed in the truncated text, but the paper states they are "calculated and compared with those of other theoretical approaches."
- The first six \(\xi\)-moments \(\langle \xi^n \rangle\) of the leading-twist LCDAs \(\phi_M(u)\) are "plotted and estimated" (no explicit numbers given in the truncated text).
- The normalization conditions are: \(\int_0^1 du \, \phi_{S,A}(u) = 1\) and \(\int_0^1 du \, \xi \, \phi_T(u) = 1\).
- The heavy quark limit relations involve the residual mass \(\bar\Lambda = M - m_Q\) and the variable \(\omega = u M\), where \(M\) is the meson mass.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of LCDAs for pseudoscalar and vector mesons (e.g., Ball (2001), Braun (2003), Ji (2004)) to p-wave heavy mesons, which have been experimentally observed in recent years (e.g., \(D_0^{*0}\), \(D_1\), \(D_2^{*}\), \(B_1^0\), \(B_2^{*0}\) by Babar, Belle, CDF, LHCb). It provides a systematic LFQM calculation that can be used as input for analyzing hard exclusive processes involving heavy meson production, complementing QCD sum rule and lattice approaches. The results enable future studies of exclusive B-physics decays at BABAR, Belle, and LHC.
