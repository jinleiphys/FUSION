# Observation of Entanglement Enabled Spin-Interference in the Drell-Söding Process in Au+Au Ultraperipheral Collisions at RHIC

> We report a measurement of the Drell-Söding $π^+ π^-$ production in Au + Au ultraperipheral collisions at a center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200~\mathrm{GeV}$ using the STAR detector. For the first time, the Entanglement Enabled Spin-Interference (EESI) effect is observed in the Drell-Söding process through the amplitude $A_{2Δϕ}$ of the $\cos(2Δϕ)$ modulation. The measured $A_{2Δϕ}$ exhibits no significant dependence on the invariant mass $M_{π^+ π^-}$. An enhancement of three standard deviations is observed for the Drell-Söding process compared to $ρ^0$ photoproduction. Furthermore, we measure the $|t|$-dependence of the Drell-Söding process and $ρ^0$ photoproduction. The Drell-Söding spectrum falls more steeply with $|t|$ and exhibits diffractive structures shifted to lower $|t|$ compared to $ρ^0$ photoproduction, features that are not captured by theoretical calculations. These results provide new insights into the interplay between quantum interference and photon-nuclear interactions in ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The STAR Collaboration reports the first observation of the Entanglement Enabled Spin-Interference (EESI) effect in the Drell-Soding process, measured through the amplitude A2Δφ of the cos(2Δφ) modulation. The observed A2Δφ shows no significant dependence on the invariant mass Mπ+π- and exhibits an enhancement of three standard deviations compared to ρ0 photoproduction, though the statistical significance is modest and the result is conditional on the specific kinematic range studied.

## Method

The STAR detector measured π+π- pairs from Au+Au ultraperipheral collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, selecting events with exactly two oppositely charged tracks in the kinematic range 0 < pT < 0.1 GeV/c, |y| < 0.9, and 0.6 < Mπ+π- < 0.9 GeV/c2. The Drell-Soding process was separated from ρ0 photoproduction through a combined fit to the invariant mass and azimuthal angle distributions, extracting the cos(2Δφ) modulation amplitude A2Δφ for each process. The |t|-dependence was measured in the range 0 < |t| < 0.1 GeV2/c2 by converting the pT2 distributions.

## Key numbers

- The A2Δφ amplitude for the Drell-Soding process shows an enhancement of 3 standard deviations compared to ρ0 photoproduction in the kinematic range 0 < pT < 0.1 GeV/c, |y| < 0.9, and 0.6 < Mπ+π- < 0.9 GeV/c2
- The |t| spectrum of the Drell-Soding process falls more steeply with |t| and exhibits diffractive structures shifted to lower |t| compared to ρ0 photoproduction in the range 0 < |t| < 0.1 GeV2/c2
- The center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair is √sNN = 200 GeV for the Au+Au collisions

## Context

This measurement extends the study of quantum interference effects in photon-nuclear interactions at RHIC, building on previous ρ0 photoproduction measurements by STAR (Phys. Rev. C 96, 054904, 2017). The observation of EESI in the Drell-Soding process provides new insights into the interplay between quantum interference and photonuclear interactions in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions, and enables future comparisons with theoretical calculations that currently do not capture the observed |t|-dependent features.
