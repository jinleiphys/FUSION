# Kaon Interferometry as Signal for the QCD Phase Transition at RHIC

> Pion and kaon correlations in relativistic nuclear collisions are studied in the framework of boost-invariant, cylindrically symmetric hydrodynamics. It is investigated how the inverse widths of the two-particle correlation functions in out- and side-direction depend on the average transverse momentum of the particle pair, the initial energy density, and the equation of state of the system. The QCD transition leads to a time delay in the expansion of the system and consequently to an enhancement of the ratio of inverse widths in out- and side-direction. This time-delay signal is found to be particularly strong for large average transverse momenta of order 1 GeV and initial energy densities accessible at RHIC. Neutral kaon pair correlation functions, which are not influenced by final state Coulomb effects and less contaminated by resonance decays than pion correlation functions, seem to be the ideal tool to detect this collective time-delay signature of the QCD transition.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the ratio of inverse widths \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) of two-particle correlation functions for neutral kaons can serve as a signal for the QCD phase transition at RHIC, provided the initial energy density is in the range \(\epsilon_0 \sim 10-20\,{\rm GeV\,fm}^{-3}\) and the average transverse pair momentum is large (\(K_\bot \sim 1\) GeV). The hedge is that the signal is strongest for a first-order phase transition (\(\Delta T=0\)) and weaker for a smooth transition (\(\Delta T=0.1\,T_c\)), and that the enhancement depends on freeze-out temperature and pair momentum.

## Method

The authors used boost-invariant, cylindrically symmetric (Bjorken cylinder) relativistic hydrodynamics to model the expansion of matter created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. They solved the hydrodynamic equations with three different equations of state: an ultrarelativistic ideal gas, a first-order QCD phase transition (\(\Delta T=0\)), and a smooth transition (\(\Delta T=0.1\,T_c\)). Particle spectra and two-particle correlation functions for pions and kaons were computed along a freeze-out hypersurface using the Cooper-Frye formalism, and the inverse widths \(R_{\rm out}\) and \(R_{\rm side}\) were extracted from the correlation functions at midrapidity.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the QCD transition is \(T_c \sim 160\) MeV.
- The initial energy density range studied is \(\epsilon_0 \sim 10-20\,{\rm GeV\,fm}^{-3}\), corresponding to RHIC conditions.
- For kaons with \(K_\bot = 300\) MeV, the ratio \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) is about a factor of 2 smaller than for pions at the same \(K_\bot\).
- For kaons with \(K_\bot = 700\) MeV and a first-order transition, the enhancement of \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) over the ideal gas case is about a factor of 2 for all three freeze-out temperatures studied (\(T_f = 0.7\,T_c\), \(0.9\,T_c\), and \(T_c\)).
- The maximum time-delay signal occurs at initial energy density \(\epsilon_0 \sim 20\,T_c s_c \simeq 15\,{\rm GeV\,fm}^{-3}\) for \(\tau_0 = 0.1\,R_0 \simeq 0.5\) fm.
- The freeze-out temperature range considered is \(T_f = (0.7-1)\,T_c \simeq (110-160)\) MeV.
- The kaon mass is \(m_K = 497\) MeV; the pion mass is \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV.
- The number of degrees of freedom in the QGP phase is \(d_{\rm Q}=37\) and in the hadronic phase \(d_{\rm H}=3\).
- The critical enthalpy density is \(T_c s_c \simeq 0.75\,{\rm GeV\,fm}^{-3}\).
- The initial thermalization time is \(\tau_0 = 0.1\,R_0\) with \(R_0 = 5\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier hydrodynamic study of Rischke et al. (1996) (RisGySig) by systematically investigating the dependence of \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) on both initial energy density \(\epsilon_0\) and average transverse pair momentum \(K_\bot\), and by including kaon interferometry in addition to pion interferometry. The authors build on the conjecture of Pratt (1984) and Bertsch (1989) that the lifetime of the system can be inferred from the ratio of out- to side-direction correlation widths. The paper enables experimental searches for the QCD phase transition at RHIC using neutral kaon correlations, which are argued to be cleaner than pion correlations because they are free from final-state Coulomb effects and less contaminated by resonance decays.
