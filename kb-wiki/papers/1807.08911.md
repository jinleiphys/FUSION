# Charged current quasi elastic scattering of muon anti-neutrino off ^{12}C

> In this work, we study charged current quasi elastic scattering of muon anti-neutrino off nucleon and nucleus using a formalism based on Llewellyn Smith (LS) model. Parameterizations by Galster et al. are used for electric and magnetic Sach's form factors of nucleons. We use Fermi gas model along with Pauli suppression condition to take into account the nuclear effects in anti-neutrino - nucleus QES. We calculate muon anti-neutrino-p and muon anti-neutrino-^{12}C charged current quasi elastic scattering differential and total cross sections for different values of axial mass M_{A} and compare the results with data from GGM, SKAT, BNL, NOMAD, MINERvA and MiniBooNE experiments. The present theoretical approach gives an excellent description of differential cross section data. The calculations with axial mass M_{A} = 0.979 and 1.05 GeV are compatible with data from most of the experiments.

## Key claim

The Llewellyn Smith model with Galster et al. form factors and a Fermi gas model with Pauli suppression provides an excellent description of muon anti-neutrino charged current quasi elastic scattering differential cross section data on carbon, but the agreement depends on the chosen axial mass value; calculations with axial mass M_A = 0.979 and 1.05 GeV are compatible with data from most experiments, while M_A = 1.23 GeV is needed to match MiniBooNE data.

## Method

The authors used the Llewellyn Smith (LS) model to compute the charged current quasi elastic scattering differential cross section for muon anti-neutrinos on free protons and on carbon-12 nuclei. For the nucleon form factors, they employed the Galster et al. parameterizations for the electric and magnetic Sachs form factors, with the axial form factor taken in dipole form. Nuclear effects for the carbon target were incorporated using a Fermi gas model with a Fermi momentum of 0.221 GeV and a Pauli suppression condition, including a binding energy of 10 MeV and a diffuseness parameter of 0.020 GeV. The differential and total cross sections were calculated for several axial mass values (0.979, 1.05, 1.12, and 1.23 GeV) and compared with experimental data from GGM, SKAT, BNL, NOMAD, MINERvA, and MiniBooNE.

## Key numbers

- Axial mass values used: M_A = 0.979, 1.05, 1.12, and 1.23 GeV.
- Fermi momentum for carbon: k_F = 0.221 GeV.
- Binding energy for carbon: E_B = 10 MeV.
- Diffuseness parameter: a = 0.020 GeV.
- Fermi coupling constant: G_F = 1.16 x 10^{-5} GeV^{-2}.
- Cabibbo angle: cos(theta_c) = 0.97425.
- Axial vector constant: g_A = -1.267.
- Dipole form factor mass squared: M_v^2 = 0.71 GeV^2.
- For MINERvA data at average anti-neutrino energy <E_anti-nu> = 3.5 GeV, the calculation with M_A = 0.979 GeV is compatible with data.
- For GGM data at <E_anti-nu> = 2 GeV, calculations with M_A = 0.979 and 1.05 GeV are compatible with data.
- For SKAT data at <E_anti-nu> = 3 GeV, calculations with M_A = 0.979 and 1.05 GeV are compatible with data.
- For MiniBooNE data at <E_anti-nu> = 665 MeV, calculations with M_A = 0.979 and 1.05 GeV are compatible with data.
- For BNL and NOMAD total cross section data on free proton, the calculation with M_A = 1.05 GeV is compatible with data.
- For total cross section on carbon, M_A = 1.05 GeV is compatible with NOMAD data, and M_A = 1.23 GeV is compatible with MiniBooNE data.

## Context

This work extends the Llewellyn Smith (1971) formalism for anti-neutrino-nucleon quasi elastic scattering to nuclear targets using the Fermi gas model with Pauli suppression, following the approach of Smith (1972) and Kuzmin (2007). The calculations are compared with experimental data from GGM (Bonetti 1977, Armenise 1979), SKAT (Brunner 1989), BNL (Fanourakis 1980), NOMAD (Lyubushkin 2008), MINERvA (Fields 2013), and MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arevalo 2013). The authors note that their model does not include two-nucleon two-hole (2N2h) effects, and they suggest that parameterizing the axial mass as a function of anti-neutrino energy, as in Kolupaeva (2016), could improve agreement with data at low energies.
