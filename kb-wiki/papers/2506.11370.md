# Asymmetric muon-antimuon emission from $Z^0$ decays: a clear magnetometer in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We show that a very clear signal of the presence of a strong magnetic field during the early stage of a high-energy heavy-ion collision is provided by the decay of the $Z^0$ into dimuon pairs. We find that the process is highly anisotropic, producing pairs mainly out of plane, as signaled by a negative value of $v_2$, and leads to an antimuon transverse momentum distribution which peaks at a higher value of the transverse momentum compared to the peak of the muon transverse momentum distribution. We also show that the process does not produce a significant distortion of the $Z^0$ spectral function. The signal can be identified by comparing the dimuon-invariant mass and the individual muon and antimuon spectra produced in semicentral heavy-ion collisions with the corresponding scaled spectra produced in p+p collisions at the $Z^0$ peak.

## Key claim

The decay of $Z^0$ bosons into dimuon pairs during the pre-equilibrium stage of heavy-ion collisions provides a clear signal of a strong magnetic field, but the signal is not a distortion of the $Z^0$ spectral function; rather, it manifests as an anisotropic emission pattern and a harder transverse momentum distribution for antimuons compared to muons, with the effect depending on the magnetic field being strong enough that the spin relaxation time (inverse Larmor frequency) is shorter than the pre-equilibrium stage duration.

## Method

The authors computed the current-current correlation function describing $Z^0$ production from quark-antiquark annihilation followed by decay into a dimuon pair in a constant magnetic field, using the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation for quark propagators and Ritus functions for lepton wave functions. The hadron tensor was obtained via the optical theorem from the imaginary part of the magnetic-field-modified $Z^0$ polarization tensor at one-loop order, while the lepton tensor incorporated spin projection operators for LLL fermions. The invariant mass distribution of dimuons was derived by contracting the hadron and lepton tensors, with transverse momentum phase-space integration replaced by a sum over Landau levels weighted by a density-of-states factor.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic-field-induced modifications to the $Z^0$ mass and width are suppressed by a factor $(m_q/M_Z)^2$, where $m_q$ is the quark mass and $M_Z$ is the $Z^0$ mass, making spectral distortions negligible even for field strengths of order $(10 m_\pi)^2$ (with $m_\pi \sim 0.14$ GeV) possibly achieved at the LHC.
* The dimuon emission rate is suppressed by a factor $(m_l/M_Z)^2$, where $m_l$ is the lepton mass, motivating the focus on dimuon rather than dielectron pairs.
* The second harmonic coefficient $v_2$ of the azimuthal dilepton distribution is given by $v_2 \sim -e^{-a/2} I_1(a/2) < 0$, where $a = (1 + 2|e_q/e|)$ and $I_1$ is a Bessel function, indicating preferred out-of-plane emission.
* The spin relaxation time for muons in the magnetic field is $\tau \sim m_{\text{muon}}/|eB|$, where $m_{\text{muon}}$ is the muon mass.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dilepton production from virtual photons in a magnetized QGP (Wang 2021, Sadooghi 2016) and from $\rho$ decays in a magnetized hadronic medium (Mondal 2023) by instead considering $Z^0$ decays during the pre-equilibrium stage. The $Z^0$ polarization tensor in a magnetic field was computed by Ghosh (2025). The proposed signal complements existing magnetic field probes such as the linear Breit-Wheeler process in ultraperipheral collisions (STAR 2019, Brandenburg 2021, 2022) and correlations between directed flow coefficients and magnetic field strength in semi-central collisions (STAR 2023, Sun 2021, 2023). The anisotropic emission and asymmetric muon-antimuon $p_T$ distributions provide experimentally searchable signatures that can be identified by comparing semicentral heavy-ion collision data with scaled p+p collision data at the $Z^0$ peak, and the CMS collaboration (2021) has already measured the $v_2$ coefficient for $Z^0$ and $\gamma^*$ dileptons in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, finding it compatible with zero but with large uncertainties.
