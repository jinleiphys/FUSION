# Integrated Dynamical Approach to Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We review integrated dynamical approaches to describe heavy ion reaction as a whole at ultrarelativistic energies. Since final observables result from all the history of the reaction, it is important to describe all the stages of the reaction to obtain the properties of the quark gluon plasma from experimental data. As an example of these approaches, we develop an integrated dynamical model, which is composed of a fully (3+1) dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model with the state-of-the-art equation of state based on lattice QCD, and subsequent hadronic cascade in the late stage. Initial conditions are obtained employing Monte Carlo versions of the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi model (MC-KLN) or the Glauber model (MC-Glauber). Using this integrated model, we first simulate relativistic heavy ion collisions at the RHIC and LHC energies starting from conventional smooth initial conditions. We next utilise each Monte Carlo samples of initial conditions on an event-by-event basis and perform event-by-event dynamical simulations to accumulate a large number of minimum bias events. A special attention is paid to performing the flow analysis as in experiments toward consistent comparison of theoretical results with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a fully integrated dynamical model combining (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with a lattice-QCD-based equation of state and a subsequent hadronic cascade can describe heavy ion collisions from initial state to final observables, but the authors emphasize that consistent comparison with experimental data requires applying the same event-by-event flow analysis methods used in experiments, rather than relying on theoretically known reaction or participant planes.

## Method

The authors constructed an integrated dynamical model with three stages: initial conditions from Monte Carlo versions of the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi model (MC-KLN) or the Glauber model (MC-Glauber), followed by (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic evolution using the Piecewise Parabolic Method with the s95p-v1.1 equation of state based on lattice QCD results from the hotQCD collaboration, and finally a hadronic cascade using JAM at a switching temperature T_sw. They performed both conventional smooth initial condition simulations and event-by-event simulations with Monte Carlo sampled initial conditions, then analyzed the output using experimental flow analysis methods to enable consistent comparison with data.

## Key numbers

- RHIC collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV; LHC collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- Switching temperature from hydro to cascade: T_sw = 183 MeV for the s95p parametrization
- Freeze-out temperature used for EoS comparison: T_fo = 125 MeV for both Budapest-Wuppertal and s95p EoSs
- Impact parameter for elliptic flow comparison: b = 7 fm in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Typical fluid element volume in sampling: approximately 0.1 fm^3
- In-coming particle correction at particlisation: approximately 5% of total multiplicity and energy (with some reports of 10% for different longitudinal structures)

## Context

This work extends the integrated dynamical approach previously developed by Hirano et al. (2010) and builds on the discovery that participant eccentricity rather than standard eccentricity explains elliptic flow scaling in Cu+Cu collisions (PHOBOS collaboration, 2007). The paper addresses the need for consistent flow analysis methods between theory and experiment, similar to how analysis tools in observational cosmology extract cosmological parameters from cosmic microwave background data (Komatsu, 2008). The approach enables extraction of QGP properties from experimental data by properly accounting for event-by-event fluctuations in initial conditions and using the same flow analysis techniques as experiments.
