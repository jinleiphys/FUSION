# Initial State Parton Broadening and Energy Loss Probed in d+Au at RHIC

> The impact parameter and rapidity dependence of the Cronin effect for massless pions in $d+Au$ reactions at $\sqrt{s}_{NN}=200$ GeV at RHIC is computed in the framework of pQCD multiple elastic scattering on a nuclear target. We introduce a formalism to incorporate initial state energy loss in perturbative calculations and take into account the elastic energy loss in addition to the transverse momentum broadening of partons.We argue that the centrality dependence of the Cronin effect can distinguish between different hadron production scenarios at RHIC. Its magnitude and rapidity dependence are shown to carry important experimental information about the properties of cold nuclear matter up to the moderate- and large-$x$ antishadowing/EMC regions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the centrality and rapidity dependence of the Cronin effect in d+Au collisions at RHIC can distinguish between different hadron production scenarios, but the magnitude of the effect depends on whether strong gluon antishadowing/EMC modifications are included; without such modifications the Cronin enhancement reaches only about 1.2 (a 20% enhancement) rather than the larger values predicted with strong antishadowing.

## Method

The author computes the impact parameter and rapidity dependence of the Cronin effect for massless pions in d+Au reactions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV using perturbative QCD with multiple elastic scattering on a nuclear target. A formalism is introduced to incorporate initial state elastic energy loss in addition to transverse momentum broadening of partons, implemented via a Poisson distribution of multiple elastic scatterings with mean opacity chi = <L>/lambda. The calculation uses lowest-order pQCD cross sections with k_T-broadening functions, EKS'98 shadowing functions, and Glauber model geometry for nuclear thickness functions.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s)=200 GeV, the vacuum radiation induced parton broadening <k_T^2>_pp = 1.920 GeV^2 for hadrons at y=0, p_T ~ 2.5 GeV
- The transport coefficients of cold nuclear matter are fixed to mu^2/lambda_q ~ 0.06 GeV^2/fm and mu^2/lambda_g ~ 0.14 GeV^2/fm (a ~20% increase from previous estimates of 0.05 GeV^2/fm)
- At midrapidity (y=0) without strong antishadowing, the Cronin enhancement reaches 1.2 with peak position in the p_T ~ 3-4 GeV range
- In going from central (b=0 fm) to peripheral (b=6 fm) reactions, the magnitude of the Cronin effect is reduced by a factor ~2.5
- At backward rapidity (y=-3), switching off initial state energy loss changes R_dAu(p_T) from moderate ~25% suppression to almost perfect binary collision scaling (R_dAu ~ 1) for p_T >= 3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the discussion of Vitev and Gyulassy (2002) on the systematics of nuclear modification factors in p+A and A+A reactions, specifically in light of upcoming d+Au data at RHIC. The calculations contrast with alternative mechanisms proposed by Gallmeister, Greiner, and Xu (2002) involving hadronic rescattering/absorption and gluon saturation models that predict suppression rather than enhancement. The results enable experimental tests to differentiate between competing interpretations of Au+Au data, particularly through centrality-dependent measurements at midrapidity and rapidity-dependent measurements at forward/backward rapidities accessible to the BRAHMS detector.
