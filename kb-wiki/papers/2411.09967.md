# Properties of a kaon-condensed phase in hyperon-mixed matter with three-baryon forces

> Coexistent phase of kaon condensates and hyperons [($Y$+$K$) phase] in beta equilibrium with electrons and muons is investigated as a possible form of dense hadronic phase with multi-strangeness. The effective chiral Lagrangian for kaon-baryon and kaon-kaon interactions is utilized within chiral symmetry approach in combination with the interaction model between baryons. For the baryon-baryon interactions, we adopt the minimal relativistic mean-field theory with exchange of scalar mesons and vector mesons between baryons without including the nonlinear self-interacting meson field terms. In addition, the universal three-baryon repulsion and the phenomenological three-nucleon attraction are introduced as density-dependent effective two-body potentials. The repulsive effects leading to stiff equation of state at high densities consist of both the two-baryon repulsion via the vector-meson exchange and the universal three-baryon repulsion. Interplay of kaon condensates with hyperons through chiral dynamics in dense matter is clarified, and resulting onset mechanisms of kaon condensation in hyperon-mixed matter and the equation of state with the ($Y$+$K$) phase and characteristic features of the system are presented. It is shown that the slope $L$ of the symmetry energy controls the two-baryon repulsion beyond the saturation density and resulting stiffness of the equation of state. The stiffness of the equation of state in turn controls admixture of hyperons and the onset and development of kaon condensates as a result of competing effect between kaon condensates and hyperons. The equation of state with the ($Y$+$K$) phase becomes stiff enough to be consistent with recent observations of massive neutron stars. Static properties of neutron stars with the ($Y$+$K$) phase are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a coexistent phase of kaon condensates and hyperons [($Y$+$K$) phase] can produce an equation of state stiff enough to be consistent with recent observations of massive neutron stars, but only when the universal three-baryon repulsion (UTBR) is included alongside the minimal relativistic mean-field model without nonlinear self-interacting meson terms; the slope $L$ of the symmetry energy controls the two-baryon repulsion beyond saturation density and thereby governs the admixture of hyperons and the onset of kaon condensation through a competing effect between the two strangeness-bearing components.

## Method
The author uses an effective chiral Lagrangian for kaon-baryon and kaon-kaon interactions combined with a minimal relativistic mean-field (MRMF) model for baryon-baryon interactions mediated by scalar mesons ($\sigma$, $\sigma^\ast$) and vector mesons ($\omega$, $\rho$, $\phi$), excluding nonlinear self-interacting meson terms. Universal three-baryon repulsion (UTBR) from the string-junction model (SJM2) and phenomenological three-nucleon attraction (TNA) are introduced as density-dependent effective two-body potentials. The ground state of the ($Y$+$K$) phase in beta equilibrium with electrons and muons is solved self-consistently for baryon species including protons, neutrons, $\Lambda$, $\Sigma^-$, and $\Xi^-$ hyperons.

## Key numbers
- The nuclear saturation density $\rho_0$ is taken as 0.16 fm$^{-3}$; $\Lambda$ hyperons appear at baryon densities $\rho_{\rm B}$ = (2.5$-$3.0) $\rho_0$.
- Two typical values of the $K$-neutron sigma term are adopted: $\Sigma_{Kn}$ = 300 MeV (with $y_N$ = 0) and 400 MeV (with $y_N$ = 0.2), where $y_N$ is the strangeness condensate ratio in the nucleon.
- The $\pi N$ sigma term $\Sigma_{\pi N}$ is set to 49.0 MeV for the $\Sigma_{Kn}$ = 300 MeV case and 53.0 MeV for the $\Sigma_{Kn}$ = 400 MeV case, using current quark masses ($m_u, m_d, m_s$) = (6, 12, 240) MeV.
- The $\Lambda^\ast$ (1405) pole contribution uses coupling constant $g_{\Lambda^\ast}$ = 0.583 and width $\gamma_{\Lambda^\ast}$ = 12.4 MeV.
- The $s$-wave on-shell $K$-$N$ scattering lengths are reproduced with $d_p$ = $0.351-\Sigma_{Kp}/m_K$ and $d_n$ = $0.130-\Sigma_{Kn}/m_K$.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous studies of the ($Y$+$K$) phase (Muto 2008; Muto, Takatsuka, Tamagaki 2019; Muto, Muroya, Takatsuka 2021) by replacing the earlier relativistic mean-field model with nonlinear self-interacting meson terms (NLSI) with a minimal RMF plus explicit three-baryon forces (UTBR+TNA). It addresses the "hyperon puzzle" identified by Nishizaki, Takatsuka, and Yamamoto (2002), where hyperon mixing softens the equation of state too much to support 2 $M_\odot$ neutron stars. The approach contrasts with density-dependent RMF models (Choi, Bhowmick 2014; Tsubakihara, Sumiyoshi 2020) and multi-pomeron exchange potentials (Yamamoto 2014, 2017), and it enables consistent modeling of massive neutron stars with multi-strangeness cores that can be tested against NICER observations (Miller 2021; Riley 2021) and gravitational-wave constraints from GW170817 (Abbott 2018).
