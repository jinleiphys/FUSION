# Insight into Multiple Partonic Interactions and Production of Charmonia in p+p Collisions at the LHC energies

> At the LHC energies, the underlying observables are of major topic of interest in high multiplicity $p+p$ collisions. Multiple Parton Interactions (MPI) is one of them, in which several interactions occur in a single $p+p$ collision. It is believed that MPI is the main reason behind the high multiplicity in $p+p$ collisions at the LHC. It was believed that MPI has only effect to the soft particle production, but recent ALICE result reveals that it can also affect the hard-particle production. In such case, the self normalized yield of heavy particle like $\rm J/ψ$ shows an increasing trend with event multiplicity. In the present contribution, we discuss the energy and multiplicity dependence of charmonium production to understand the effects of MPI on charmonium production.

## Key claim

The authors argue that Multiple Parton Interactions (MPI) can affect hard-particle production, specifically charmonia, in high-multiplicity p+p collisions at LHC energies, contrary to the earlier belief that MPI only affects soft-particle production. The claim is hedged by the fact that the study uses PYTHIA8 simulations rather than experimental data for most energies, and the effect is inferred from the behavior of self-normalized J/ψ yields as a function of charged-particle multiplicity.

## Method

The authors used PYTHIA8.2 with the 4C tune, including varying impact parameter and MPI-based color reconnection (CR), to simulate inelastic non-diffractive p+p collisions at √s = 0.9, 2.76, 5.02, 7, and 13 TeV. Charged-particle multiplicity was measured at mid-rapidity (|y| < 1.0) while J/ψ was measured at forward rapidity (2.5 < y < 4.0), and the self-normalized yields were calculated by dividing the yield in each multiplicity bin by the minimum-bias yield. The study compared simulations with and without color reconnection to isolate final-state effects on J/ψ production.

## Key numbers

- The maximum deviation between PYTHIA8 and experimental data for rapidity spectra is approximately 1% across all studied energies (0.9, 2.76, 5.02, 7, and 13 TeV).
- For transverse momentum spectra, the maximum deviation between PYTHIA8 and experimental data is 50-60% for certain pT bins, while most pT bins show deviations of 10-20%.
- The α parameter from the phenomenological fit y = Ax^α, representing the rate of increase of relative J/ψ yield with center-of-mass energy, is negative for multiplicity bins up to Nch = 10-20 and becomes positive for higher-multiplicity bins, indicating MPI dominance for J/ψ production when Nch > 20.
- The difference in relative J/ψ yield between simulations with and without color reconnection increases with both charged-particle multiplicity and center-of-mass energy.

## Context

This work extends the observation from the ALICE experiment (Abelev 2012, Adam 2015) that J/ψ production shows an increasing trend with event multiplicity at √s = 7 TeV and 13 TeV, which could not be explained by PYTHIA6 where MPI only affects soft processes. The study uses PYTHIA8 (Sjostrand 2008) which incorporates MPI for heavy quark production, and the results enable future experimental measurements of quarkonia production as a function of multiplicity at various LHC energies to further explore MPI effects.
