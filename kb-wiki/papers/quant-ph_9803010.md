# Weakly bound states in 2+εdimensions

> We study the critical behaviour near the threshold where a first bound state appears at some value of coupling constant in an attractive short-range potential in $2+ε$ dimensions. We obtain general expression for the binding energy near the threshold and also demonstrate that the critical region is correctly described by an effective separable potential. The critical exponent of the radius of weakly bound state is shown to coincide with the correlation length exponent for the spin model in the large-N limit. In two dimensions, where the binding energy is exponentially small in coupling constant, we obtain a general analytic expression for the prefactor.

# Weakly bound states in 2+ε dimensions

## Key claim

The critical exponent for the binding energy near threshold in 2+ε dimensions is β = 2/ε for ε ≤ 2 and β = 1 for ε ≥ 2, with the result that the critical region is exactly described by an effective separable potential. In two dimensions (ε → 0), the binding energy is exponentially small in the coupling constant, and the paper provides a general analytic expression for the prefactor that was previously unknown.

## Method

The author starts from the integral representation of the Schrödinger equation and derives exact expressions for the binding energy near the critical coupling λ_c by expanding the Green's function around zero energy. The critical coupling λ_c is expressed in terms of the zero-energy wavefunction ψ_0 and the potential V(r). The author then shows that the separable approximation, where the true potential is replaced by V_sep = -V|ψ_0⟩⟨ψ_0|V, reproduces the exact critical behavior. For the two-dimensional case, the author takes the ε → 0 limit of the general expression to obtain the binding energy and prefactor, and applies the result to two-center potentials.

## Key numbers

- Critical exponent β = 2/ε for 0 < ε < 2, and β = 1 for ε ≥ 2
- Critical exponent ν = 1/ε for 2 < d < 4, and ν = 1/2 for d ≥ 4
- For the square well potential in three dimensions: λ_c = π²/4a² and A = π²/8a
- For the Yukawa potential in three dimensions, the ε-expansion extrapolation gives λ_c a² ≈ 1.693, compared to the exact result λ_c a² = 1.6798
- For the square well potential in three dimensions, the extrapolated prefactor Aa ≈ 1.442, compared to the exact value Aa = π²/8 = 1.234
- For two identical centers in two dimensions at separation R, the binding energy is E₂ = -2 e^{-γ} κ₀/R, where κ₀ is the square root of the binding energy on one center

## Context

This work extends the study of critical behavior near the threshold for bound state formation in short-range potentials, previously investigated by Lassaut et al. (1996) for the three-dimensional case with non-zero orbital momentum. The author notes that the critical exponents obtained coincide with the correlation length exponent for the spherical model (N-component spin model at N → ∞) from Ma (1976) and with the localization length exponent in the self-consistent theory of Anderson localization from Vollhardt and Wölfle (1982). The same exponents were also obtained from scaling arguments by Hwa and Nattermann (1995) and Kolomeisky and Straley (1995) for the problem of unbinding a directed polymer from a columnar defect. The results enable the calculation of binding energies for weakly bound states in arbitrary short-range potentials in two dimensions, including multi-center systems.
