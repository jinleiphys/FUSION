# Evidence of magic numbers in nuclei by statistical indicators

> The calculation of a statistical measure of complexity and the Fisher-Shannon information in nuclei is carried out in this work. We use the nuclear shell model in order to obtain the fractional occupation probabilities of nuclear orbitals. The increasing of both magnitudes, the statistical complexity and the Fisher-Shannon information, with the number of nucleons is observed. The shell structure is reflected by the behavior of the statistical complexity. The magic numbers are revealed by the Fisher-Shannon information.

## Key claim

The Fisher-Shannon information \(P\) reveals the sequence of magic numbers in nuclei (2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126, 184) through peaks in its derivative, except that peaks at 6 and 14 disagree with the magic-number sequence, which the authors suggest could be justified by statistical indicators working better for large numbers.

## Method

The authors used the nuclear shell model with an intermediate three-dimensional potential between an infinite well and the harmonic oscillator, including spin-orbit interaction, to obtain fractional occupation probabilities of nucleons (protons or neutrons) in nuclear orbitals. From these probabilities, they computed the LMC statistical complexity \(C = H \cdot D\) (where \(H\) is the exponential Shannon entropy and \(D\) is the disequilibrium) and the Fisher-Shannon information \(P = J \cdot I\) (where \(J\) is a version of the power Shannon entropy and \(I\) is the discrete Fisher information measure) as functions of the number of nucleons \(Z\) or \(N\).

## Key numbers

- The Fisher-Shannon information \(P\) shows peaks (changes in the sign of the derivative) at the numbers 2, 6, 14, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126, 184.
- The sequence of magic numbers is {2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126, 184}; only the peaks at 6 and 14 disagree with this sequence.
- The carbon nucleus \(C_{Z=6}^{N=6}\) has the highest abundance in its isotopic series at 98.9%, and the silicon nucleus \(Si_{Z=14}^{N=14}\) has the highest abundance in its isotopic series at 92.2%.

## Context

This work extends the application of information-theoretic indicators (statistical complexity and Fisher-Shannon information) from atomic electronic shell structure, as studied by Panos (2005, 2007, 2009), Borgoo (2008), Romera (2008), and Sanudo (2008, 2009), to nuclear shell structure. The method using fractional occupation probabilities was previously applied to atoms by Panos (2009) and Sanudo (2009). The results enable the use of statistical indicators to probe structural aspects of quantum many-body systems, suggesting that such indicators can identify magic numbers in nuclei and potentially other shell-like structures.
