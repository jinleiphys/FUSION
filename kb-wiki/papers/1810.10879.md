# Matching the Quasi Meson Distribution Amplitude in RI/MOM scheme

> The $x$-dependence of light-cone distribution amplitude (LCDA) can be directly calculated from a quasi distribution amplitude (DA) in lattice QCD within the framework of large-momentum effective theory (LaMET). In this paper, we study the one-loop renormalization of the quasi-DA in the regularization-independent momentum subtraction (RI/MOM) scheme. The renormalization factor for the quasi parton distribution function can be used to renormalize the quasi-DA provided that they are implemented on lattice and in perturbation theory in the same manner. We derive the one-loop matching coefficient that matches quasi-DA in the RI/MOM scheme onto LCDA in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme. Our result provides the crucial step to extract the LCDAs from lattice matrix elements of quasi-DAs.

## Key claim

The one-loop matching coefficient that converts quasi-DA in the RI/MOM scheme onto LCDA in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme is derived, enabling the extraction of full $x$-dependence of LCDAs from lattice QCD; the renormalization factor for quasi-PDFs can be reused for quasi-DAs provided they are implemented identically on the lattice and in perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors calculate the one-loop matching coefficient by computing the off-shell quark matrix elements of both the quasi-DA and the LCDA in perturbation theory, replacing the meson state with the lowest Fock state of a quark and anti-quark. The bare matching coefficient is obtained by taking the on-shell and large-momentum limits of the quasi-DA, and the RI/MOM counterterm is derived from the quasi-PDF renormalization factor in Landau gauge using the minimal projection. The matching coefficient for the quasi-DA in the RI/MOM scheme to the LCDA in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme is then given by the sum of the bare matching coefficient and the counterterm.

## Key numbers

* The matching coefficient $C_\Gamma$ is expressed as $\delta(x-y) + C_B^{(1)} - C_{CT}^{(1)} + \mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)$, where $C_B^{(1)}$ and $C_{CT}^{(1)}$ are the one-loop bare and counterterm contributions respectively.
* The bare matching coefficient $C_B^{(1)}$ contains functions $H_1$ and $H_2$ with logarithmic terms such as $\ln\frac{4x(y-x)(P^z)^2}{\mu^2}$ for the $\gamma^z\gamma_5$, $\gamma^t$, and $\gamma^z\gamma_\perp$ spin structures.
* The RI/MOM counterterm $C_{CT}^{(1)}$ depends on the quasi-PDF real contribution $\widetilde{q}^{(1)}(\Gamma,x,r)$, which is given explicitly for $\Gamma=\gamma^z\gamma_5$, $\gamma^t$, and $\gamma^z\gamma_\perp$ as functions of $r=\mu_R^2/(p_R^z)^2$ and the momentum fraction $x$.

## Context

This work extends the LaMET framework for LCDAs, for which matching coefficients were previously calculated only in dimensional regularization and transverse-momentum cutoff schemes (Zhang 2017, Bali 2017, Chen 2017, Xu 2018). The RI/MOM scheme has already been applied to the lattice renormalization of quasi-PDFs (Alexandrou 2017, Chen 2017, Lin 2017, Stewart 2017, Liu 2018), and this paper provides the missing perturbative matching coefficient needed to convert RI/MOM quasi-DA lattice results into $\overline{\rm MS}$ LCDAs. The result enables first-principles lattice calculations of the full $x$-dependence of meson LCDAs for pseudoscalar, longitudinally polarized vector, and transversely polarized vector mesons.
