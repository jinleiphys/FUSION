# Matching universal behavior with potential models

> Two-, three-, and four-boson systems are studied close to the unitary limit using potential models constructed to reproduce the minimal information given by the two-body scattering length $a$ and the two-body binding energy or virtual state energy $E_2$. The particular path used to reach the unitary limit is given by varying the potential strength. In this way the energy spectrum in the three- and four-boson systems is computed. The lowest energy states show finite-range effects absorbed in the construction of level functions that can be used to study real systems. Higher energy levels are free from finite-range effects, therefore the corresponding level functions tend to the zero-range universal function. Using this property a zero-range equation for the four-boson system is proposed and the four-boson universal function is computed.

# Matching universal behavior with potential models

## Key claim

The paper shows that two-parameter gaussian potentials (local or nonlocal) can capture the essential few-boson dynamics near the unitary limit, and that for sufficiently high excited states (n>1) the finite-range effects become negligible, allowing the construction of universal zero-range functions for the four-boson system. The claim is hedged by the fact that the lowest energy states (n=0,1) still show finite-range effects that must be absorbed into level-specific functions, and the path to the unitary limit is defined by varying the potential strength in a particular way.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation (using hyperspherical harmonic expansion) and the Faddeev-Yakubovsky or Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations (using momentum-space methods) for two-, three-, and four-boson systems interacting via local and nonlocal gaussian potentials. The potential strength is varied as a control parameter to reach the unitary limit, and the resulting energy spectra are used to construct level functions that absorb finite-range effects for low-lying states while converging to universal zero-range functions for higher excited states.

## Context

This work extends the Efimov theory for three identical bosons (Efimov 1970) to the four-boson system by proposing a zero-range equation analogous to the Efimov radial law. It builds on prior studies of four-boson universal behavior by Platter (2004), Hammer (2007), and Deltuva (2010, 2012), who established the two-level tree structure of tetramers and computed universal ratios at the unitary limit. The approach using potential models with variable strength follows the methodology of Kievsky (2015) and provides a practical way to compute level functions that can be applied to real systems such as $^4$He atoms, with accuracy better than 1% when compared to realistic LM2M2 calculations by Aziz (1991) and Hiyama (2012, 2014). The results enable the determination of universal four-body functions that can be used to study ultracold atomic gases near Feshbach resonances and may be extended to systems with more bosons or with spin-isospin degrees of freedom.
