# Solar Fusion and The Coulomb Dissociation of 8B; What Have We Learned and Where Do We Go From Here?

> The much needed nuclear input to the Standard Solar Model, $S_{17}(0)$, has now been measured with high precision ($\pm$5% or better) by different groups and good agreement is found, even when very different methods are employed. We review the decade long research program to measure the cross section of the $^7Be(p,γ)^8B$ reaction using the Coulomb dissociation method, including the pioneering RIKEN1 experiment carried out during March 1992, followed by RIKEN2, GSI1, GSI2 and an MSU experiment. Our RIKEN and GSI data allow us to rule out the much tooted large E2 contribution to the Coulomb dissociation of $^8B$. Specifically recent results of the MSU experiment are not confirmed. The GSI1 and GSI2 high precision measurements are in good (to perfect) agreement with the newly published high precision measurements of direct capture with $^7Be$ targets. From these GSI-Seattle-Weizmann high precision data we conclude that the astrophysical cross section factor, $S_{17}(0)$, is most likely in the range of 20 - 22 eV-b. We point out to an additional large uncertainty (-10% +3%) that still exists due to uncertainty in the measured slope of the S-factor and the theoretical extrapolation procedure which may still lower $S_{17}(0)$ down to approximately 18.5 eV-b. For quoting $S_{17}(0)$ with an uncertainty of $\pm$5% or better, yet another measurement needs to be performed at very low energies, as recently discussed by the UConn-Weizmann-LLN collaboration for the CERN/ISOLDE facility.

## Key claim

The astrophysical cross section factor \(S_{17}(0)\) for the \(^7Be(p,\gamma)^8B\) reaction is most likely in the range of 20–22 eV-b, but an additional uncertainty of \(-10\%\) to \(+3\%\) remains due to differences in the measured slope of the S-factor and the theoretical extrapolation procedure, which could lower the value to approximately 18.5 eV-b.

## Method

The paper reviews a decade-long program measuring the \(^7Be(p,\gamma)^8B\) cross section via Coulomb dissociation of \(^8B\) at medium-energy heavy-ion facilities (RIKEN1, RIKEN2, GSI1, GSI2, and MSU). These experiments used different kinematical regions and experimental techniques to extract the astrophysical S-factor, and the results are compared with direct capture measurements using \(^7Be\) targets from the Seattle, Weizmann, Orsay, and Bochum groups. The analysis focuses on the E2 contribution to the Coulomb dissociation and on the slope of the S-factor for extrapolation to zero energy.

## Key numbers

- RIKEN1 (1994): \(S_{17}(0) = 16.9 \pm 3.2\) eV-b, with E2/E1 ratio \(< 7 \times 10^{-4}\) at 0.6 MeV.
- RIKEN2 (1998): \(S_{17}(0) = 18.9 \pm 1.8\) eV-b, with E2/E1 ratio \(< 4 \times 10^{-5}\).
- GSI1 (1999): \(S_{17}(0) = 20.6^{+1.2}_{-1.0}\) eV-b, with E2/E1 ratio \(< 3 \times 10^{-5}\).
- GSI2 (2003): \(S_{17}(0) = 20.8 \pm 0.5 \pm 0.5\) eV-b, with E2/E1 ratio \(< 3 \times 10^{-5}\) (yield \(< 1\%\)).
- MSU (2001): \(S_{17}(0) = 17.8^{+1.4}_{-1.2}\) eV-b, with E2/E1 ratio \((4.7^{+2.0}_{-1.3}) \times 10^{-4}\).
- Seattle (2002): \(S_{17}(0) = 22.3 \pm 0.7\) eV-b.
- Weizmann (2003): \(S_{17}(0) = 21.2 \pm 0.7\) eV-b.
- Average of high-precision results (GSI1, Seattle, Weizmann, GSI2): \(21.2 \pm 0.8\) eV-b (\(\chi^2 = 1.2\)).
- Using Typel’s potential model extrapolation: \(S_{17}(0) = 18.6 \pm 0.5 \pm 1.0\) eV-b.

## Context

This work extends the direct capture measurements of the \(^7Be(p,\gamma)^8B\) reaction by the Seattle (2002), Weizmann (2003), Orsay (2001), and Bochum (2001) groups, and the Coulomb dissociation experiments by RIKEN (1994, 1998), GSI (1999, 2003), and MSU (2001). The paper contradicts the MSU (2001) claim of a large E2 contribution to the Coulomb dissociation, finding instead that the GSI and RIKEN data rule out such a large effect. The unresolved discrepancy in the slope of the S-factor between different high-precision data sets (e.g., GSI-Weizmann vs. Seattle) and between theoretical extrapolations (Descouvemont and Baye vs. Typel) prevents a \(\pm 5\%\) determination of \(S_{17}(0)\). A future low-energy measurement at CERN/ISOLDE, using an intense \(^7Be\) beam of up to 100 nA, is proposed to resolve the extrapolation issue.
