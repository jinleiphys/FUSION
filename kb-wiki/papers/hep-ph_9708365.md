# Chiral symmetry and mixing of axial and vector correlators in matter

> The effect of mixing of the vector and axial vector correlation functions in the nuclear medium arising from the interaction of nuclear pions with corresponding interpolating currents is considered. It is shown that the mass difference between $ρ$ and $a_1$ meson gets smaller with the increase of the nuclear density reflecting the phenomena of partial restoration of chiral symmetry whereas the absolute values of meson masses may both decrease and increase in nuclear medium depending on the model used for the phenomenological spectral density.

# Chiral symmetry and mixing of axial and vector correlators in matter

## Key claim
The mass difference between the rho and a1 meson decreases with increasing nuclear density, indicating partial restoration of chiral symmetry, but the absolute values of the meson masses may either decrease or increase depending on the model used for the phenomenological spectral density. The mixing effect is inevitable as chiral symmetry gets restored, but quantitative determination of in-medium masses is impossible due to large uncertainties in the unmixed correlators.

## Method
The author calculated the two-point correlator of the vector current in nuclear matter including explicit interactions of nuclear pions with the vector currents, which induces mixing between vector and axial vector correlation functions. Using soft-pion theorems and current algebra commutation relations, expressions for the mixed vector and axial correlators were derived in terms of the unmixed correlators and a parameter xi proportional to the nuclear density and the pion-nucleon sigma term. The resulting QCD sum rules were then solved using Borel transforms with pole-plus-continuum spectral densities to extract the density-dependent masses.

## Key numbers
- The mixing parameter xi is defined as xi = (rho * sigma_bar_piN) / (f_pi^2 * m_pi^2), where sigma_bar_piN is approximately -25 MeV (the leading nonanalytic term in the pion-nucleon sigma term)
- Complete mixing of vector and axial correlators arises at approximately rho = 3 rho_0 (three times normal nuclear density), though this estimate should not be taken too seriously
- The effective thresholds used in the sum rules are S_A1 = 2.2 GeV and S_V = 1.5 GeV
- At zero density, the unmixed A1 parameters are F^2_A1 = 0.021 GeV^2 and M_A1 = 1.26 GeV
- The rho meson mass decrease at normal nuclear density is about 20% in calculations without pion corrections (citing Hatsuda and Lee)

## Context
This work extends the finite-temperature mixing of vector and axial correlators found by Eletsky and Ioffe to the case of finite baryon density. It addresses the inconsistency noted by previous authors: QCD sum rule calculations of the rho meson mass in matter (such as Hatsuda and Lee, and Asakawa and Ko) are not fully consistent with chiral symmetry unless the in-medium modifications of the A1 meson are included. The paper shows that the mixing effect reduces the rho mass drop compared to unmixed calculations, while the A1 mass drops faster, leading to convergence of the masses. The approach is similar to that used by Krippa to remove chiral-symmetry-forbidden pieces in QCD sum rules for the nucleon mass in matter.
