# Anomalies in Particle Physics

> I provide a (personal) review of the current hints for physics beyond the Standard Model, called ``anomalies'', obtained both at the intensity frontier (flavour and electroweak precision observables) and in direct LHC searches. This includes the deviations from the Standard Model predictions in semi-leptonic $B$ decays, the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly, the $W$ mass as well as non-resonant di-lepton searches, the hints for new scalar particles around $\approx\! 95\,$GeV, $\approx\! 151\,$GeV, $\approx\! 670\,$GeV and the (di-)di-jet excess at $\approx \!1\,$TeV ($\approx 3.6\,$TeV). Possible explanations in terms of new particles are briefly summarized and discussed.

# Anomalies in Particle Physics

## Key claim

This review argues that the collection of currently observed "anomalies", deviations from Standard Model predictions spanning five orders of magnitude in energy, are unlikely to all be statistical flukes, though probability theory dictates that not all will be confirmed; the paper assesses which extensions of the Standard Model these anomalies collectively point toward, potentially also addressing neutrino masses and dark matter.

## Method

The author compiles and reviews experimental hints for physics beyond the Standard Model from intensity-frontier measurements (flavor observables and electroweak precision data) and direct LHC searches, ordering them by increasing energy scale. For each anomaly, the paper summarizes the current experimental status, statistical significance, and then surveys which classes of new particles, leptoquarks, W' bosons, Z' bosons, vector-like fermions, and new scalars, can provide renormalizable explanations, noting where full explanations are possible versus where conflicts with other observables arise.

## Key numbers

- Muon anomalous magnetic moment: 4.2 sigma tension between combined Brookhaven E821 and Fermilab g-2 result and the Standard Model prediction from the g-2 theory initiative
- Cabibbo Angle Anomaly: 2.8 sigma deviation from CKM unitarity in the V_ud–V_us plane (68% C.L. ellipse)
- R(D^(*)) ratios: approximately 20% larger than Standard Model predictions, with greater than 3 sigma significance
- b to s l+ l- global fit: scenario with a lepton-flavor-universal contribution to C_9 is preferred over the Standard Model hypothesis by more than 6 sigma
- W boson mass: combined value m_W = (80.413 ± 0.015) GeV, corresponding to 3.7 sigma tension with the Standard Model prediction
- Neutral scalar hints: new scalar particles around 95 GeV, 151 GeV, and approximately 670 GeV in di-photon channels
- Di-di-jet excess: CMS found hints for pair production of di-jet resonances with mass approximately 950 GeV, local (global) significance 3.6 sigma (2.5 sigma); resonant scenario at approximately 3.6 TeV yields 4 sigma significance
- Non-resonant di-electron search: CMS observed 44 electron events with invariant mass above 1.8 TeV versus 29.2 ± 3.6 expected; model-independent fit finds new physics at 10 TeV scale improves over Standard Model by approximately 3 sigma

## Context

This review extends and updates the ongoing discussion of Standard Model anomalies, building on the global fits by Alguero (2019), Buras (2022), and Ciuchini (2022) for b to s transitions, and the HFLAV (2022) combination for b to c transitions. The paper connects anomalies across energy scales, from the muon g-2 and Cabibbo Angle Anomaly at low energies to LHC di-jet and di-lepton excesses at TeV scales, and evaluates which new particle representations (leptoquarks, vector-like fermions, Z' bosons, W' bosons, new scalars) can simultaneously address multiple anomalies, enabling searches for unified beyond-Standard-Model frameworks that could also accommodate neutrino masses and dark matter.
