# Electromagnetic mass differences of SU(3) baryons within a chiral soliton model

> We investigate the electromagnetic mass differences of SU(3) baryons, using an "model-independent approach" within a chiral soliton model. The electromagnetic self-energy corrections to the masses of the baryon are expressed as the baryonic two-point correlation function of the electromagnetic currents. Using the fact that the electromagnetic current can be treated as an octet operator, and considering possible irreducible representations of the correlation function, we are able to construct a general collective operator for the electromagnetic self-energies, which consists of three unknown parameters. These parameters are fixed, the empirical data for the electromagnetic mass differences of the baryon octet being employed. We predict those of the baryon decuplet and antidecuplet. In addition, we obtain various mass relations between baryon masses within the corresponding representation with isospin symmetry breaking considered. We also predict the physical mass differences of the baryon decuplet. The results are in good agreement with the exisiting data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that electromagnetic mass differences of SU(3) baryons can be described by a "model-independent" collective operator with only two free parameters (c^(8) and c^(27)) within a chiral soliton framework, but the approach is not truly model-independent because these parameters are fixed by fitting to empirical data from Gasser and Leutwyler (1982) rather than computed from first principles.

## Method

The authors express electromagnetic self-energy corrections to baryon masses as baryonic two-point correlation functions of electromagnetic currents in the static limit. They construct a general collective operator for the electromagnetic self-energies by treating the electromagnetic current as an octet operator and considering irreducible representations of the correlation function, which yields three unknown parameters (reduced to two for mass differences). These parameters are determined by fitting to the empirical electromagnetic mass differences of the baryon octet extracted by Gasser and Leutwyler (1982), and then used to predict electromagnetic mass differences for the baryon decuplet and antidecuplet.

## Key numbers

- c^(8) = -0.15 ± 0.23 MeV and c^(27) = 8.62 ± 2.39 MeV (the two fitted parameters for the electromagnetic operator)
- (M_p - M_n)_EM = 0.74 ± 0.22 MeV (reproduced from input value 0.76 ± 0.30 MeV from Gasser and Leutwyler (1982))
- (M_Σ+ - M_Σ-)_EM = -0.15 ± 0.23 MeV (reproduced from input value -0.17 ± 0.30 MeV)
- (M_Ξ0 - M_Ξ-)_EM = -0.88 ± 0.28 MeV (reproduced from input value -0.86 ± 0.30 MeV)
- (M_Δ++ - M_Δ-)_EM = 0.71 ± 0.29 MeV (predicted electromagnetic mass difference)
- (M_Δ++ - M_Δ-)_physical = -5.86 ± 0.38 MeV (predicted physical mass difference, compared to experimental -5.9 ± 3.1 MeV from Dakhno (1980))
- (M_Ξ*0 - M_Ξ*-)_physical = -3.32 ± 0.32 MeV (compared to experimental -2.9 ± 0.9 MeV from Nakamura (2010))
- (M_Σ*0 - M_Σ*-)_physical = -3.32 ± 0.32 MeV (compared to experimental -3.1 ± 0.6 MeV from Thomas (1973))

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton model approach to electromagnetic mass splittings, improving upon earlier work by Praszalowicz et al. (1992) which used the Dashen Ansatz (originally derived for mesons and valid only in the chiral limit). The authors build on the foundational analysis of Gasser and Leutwyler (1982) who separated electromagnetic from hadronic contributions to baryon masses. The results enable unambiguous determination of hadronic isospin symmetry breaking parameters in chiral soliton models, and provide predictions for the baryon decuplet and antidecuplet electromagnetic mass differences that can be tested against future experimental data.
