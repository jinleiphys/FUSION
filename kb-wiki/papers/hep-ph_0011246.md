# Hydrodynamics of relativisic systems with broken continuous symmetries

> We show how hydrodynamics of relativistic system with broken continuous symmetry can be constructed using the Poisson bracket technique. We illustrate the method on the example of relativistic superfluids.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Poisson bracket technique can be used to construct the hydrodynamic equations for relativistic systems with broken continuous symmetries, specifically showing that the resulting equations for relativistic superfluids are equivalent to earlier formulations by Carter, Khalatnikov, and Lebedev, but with clearer physical interpretation and field-theoretical origin.

## Method

The author first writes down the classical Poisson brackets between hydrodynamic variables (energy-momentum density, charge density, entropy density, and condensate phase) for a complex phi^4 field theory as the relativistic generalization of superfluids. The most general Hamiltonian is then expressed as a function of these variables, and the equations of motion are derived from the Poisson bracket relations. Relativistic invariance imposes constraints on the Hamiltonian that lead to a covariant formulation of hydrodynamics, yielding a set of equations including energy-momentum conservation, charge conservation, entropy conservation, and a Josephson-type equation.

## Key numbers

- The hydrodynamic modes in the complex phi^4 theory include five conserved densities: energy density T^00, three components of momentum density T^0i, and U(1) charge density n, plus one non-conserved phase phi.
- For nuclear matter near the chiral limit with SU(N_f) x SU(N_f) -> SU(N_f) symmetry breaking, the hydrodynamics involves 5 + 2(N_f^2 - 1) conserved charges (energy, momentum, entropy, left and right-handed isospin charges) and N_f^2 - 1 condensate phases (Goldstone bosons).

## Context

This work extends the Poisson bracket approach to hydrodynamics of relativistic systems with broken continuous symmetries, building on the foundational hydrodynamic models of heavy-ion collisions by Landau and Bjorken. The resulting equations are shown to be equivalent to the earlier formulations for relativistic superfluids by Carter and Khalatnikov (1998) and by Khalatnikov and Lebedev (1982), but with the advantage of making the physical meaning and field-theoretical origin of all quantities clear. The technique can be generalized to nuclear matter near the chiral limit, enabling the study of coupled dynamics of Goldstone modes and fluid dynamical modes in systems with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry.
