# Van der Waals equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter

> The application of van der Waals equation of state to the asymmetric nuclear matter is considered in a critical state region. The corrections to the van der Waals pressure and free energy due to the Fermi statistics are obtained starting from the Thomas--Fermi entropy expression which ensures the fulfilment of Nernst theorem. The derived corrections account for the effective nucleon mass and neutron-proton isotopic asymmetry. The parameters of van der Waals equation of state are deduced taking the experimental value of critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter and testing the model of van der Waals with statistics corrections included against the theory of Skyrme energy density functional. Critical line in pressure-temperature-composition space is considered. Incompressibility coefficient is determined along the critical line as a function of nuclear matter composition. Jump in the value of specific heat upon crossing critical line is discussed.

## Key claim

The van der Waals equation of state, corrected for Fermi statistics via the Thomas-Fermi entropy expression, can be applied to asymmetric nuclear matter in the critical state region, but the model is oversimplified for describing cold nuclear matter at the saturation point and should be avoided there.

## Method

The author derived Fermi-statistics corrections to the van der Waals pressure and free energy starting from the Thomas-Fermi entropy expression, which ensures the Nernst theorem is satisfied. The corrections account for the effective nucleon mass and neutron-proton isotopic asymmetry. Model parameters were determined using the experimental critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter and by testing against Skyrme energy density functional theory, specifically using the ratio of critical to saturation density and the curvature of the critical compression factor.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter: \( T_{\rm cr} = 16.6\ \mathrm{MeV} \) (from Naha02)
- Saturation density: \( \rho_{\rm sat} = 0.165\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3} \)
- Ratio of critical to saturation density: \( \rho_{\rm cr}/\rho_{\rm sat} \approx 1/3 \)
- Van der Waals parameters obtained: \( a_0 = 365.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\mathrm{fm}^3 \), \( a_1 = -191.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\mathrm{fm}^3 \), \( b = 4.418\ \mathrm{fm}^3 \)
- Incompressibility coefficient from giant resonance experiments: \( K = 230\ \mathrm{MeV} \)
- Isoscalar effective mass at saturation from recent analysis: \( m_0^{\ast}/m = 0.68 \pm 0.04 \)
- Isovector effective mass splitting at saturation from recent analysis: \( m_1^{\ast}/m = (-0.20 \pm 0.09)X \)
- Curvature of critical compression factor for different Skyrme EDFs ranges from \( \alpha_z = 0.73 \) (SKRA) to \( \alpha_z = 1.09 \) (KDE0v1)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of van der Waals equations for symmetric nuclear matter (Voan15) and asymmetric nuclear matter (Povo19) by using a canonical ensemble approach with Thomas-Fermi entropy, which highlights the effect of in-medium interaction (effective mass) ignored in those earlier works. The model is tested against Skyrme energy density functional theory (using parametrizations KDE0v1, LNS, NRAPR, SKRA, SQMC700 from Agsh05, Calo06, Stpr05, Rash00, Guma06) and enables calculation of the critical line in pressure-temperature-composition space for asymmetric nuclear matter, as well as the incompressibility coefficient and specific heat jump along that line.
