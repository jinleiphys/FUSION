# Backflow and dissipation during the quantum decay of a metastable Fermi liquid

> The particle current in a metastable Fermi liquid against a first-order phase transition is calculated at zero temperature. During fluctuations of a droplet of the stable phase, in accordance with the conservation law, not only does an unperturbed current arise from the continuity at the boundary, but a backflow is induced by the density response. Quasiparticles carrying these currents are scattered by the boundary, yielding a dissipative backflow around the droplet. An energy of the hydrodynamic mass flow of the liquid and a friction force exerted on the droplet by the quasiparticles have been obtained in terms of a potential of their interaction with the droplet.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the particle current and dissipation during quantum nucleation of a stable-phase droplet in a metastable Fermi liquid at zero temperature, finding that the backflow current and friction force can be expressed in terms of the interaction potential between quasiparticles and the droplet, but only under the assumption that the liquid is nearly incompressible and the droplet radius is much larger than the interaction range.

## Method

The author treats the droplet radius as a macroscopic variable and calculates the particle current from the continuity equation with a boundary condition at the droplet surface, then adds a linear-response backflow induced by the density perturbation of the droplet. The dissipative backflow and friction force are obtained by computing the scattering of quasiparticles on the Fermi surface off the droplet, using the asymptotic form of scattering wavefunctions and the transport cross section. The results are extended to interacting Fermi liquids via Landau theory and to charged systems via Thomas-Fermi screening.

## Key numbers

- The unperturbed current density at distance r from the droplet center is j(r,t)|_0 = (n1 - n2) dR/dt (R/r)^2 r-hat for r >= R, where n1 and n2 are the number densities of the metastable and stable phases respectively
- The radial backflow from linear response is j(r,t)|_res = -(∂n1/∂μ1) [n2/(n2-n1)] v(q=0) (a/R) j(r,t)|_0, where a is the interaction range and v(q=0) is the zero-momentum Fourier transform of the interaction potential
- The dissipative backflow from scattering is δj|_curr = -[3σ_tr(k_F)/(4πr^2)] j_mac, where σ_tr(k_F) is the transport cross section at the Fermi momentum
- The friction force on the droplet is F = [1 - (∂n1/∂μ1) n2/(n2-n1) v(q=0) a/R] n1 ħ k_F σ_tr(k_F) (1 - n2/n1) dR/dt r-hat
- The rate of energy dissipation is dE/dt = -4π^2 α [1 - 2(∂n1/∂μ1) n2/(n2-n1) v(q=0) a/R] n1 ħ k_F (1 - n2/n1)^2 R^2 (dR/dt)^2, with α = σ_tr(k_F)/(πR^2)
- For charged systems, the kinetic energy includes a correction term -(3/2)κR where κ = 1/λ_TF is the inverse Thomas-Fermi screening length

## Context

This work extends calculations by Zwerger (1997) on backflow around a slowly moving impurity in a Fermi liquid to the case of a spherically symmetric droplet during quantum nucleation. It also generalizes the dimensional analysis of Burmistrov and Dubovskii (1987-1988) by expressing the previously unknown coefficient of the dissipation rate in terms of the interaction potential. The results enable calculation of quantum nucleation rates in metastable Fermi liquids such as supersaturated ³He-⁴He mixtures (Satoh, 1992) and deconfining nuclear matter in neutron stars (Iida, 1997).
