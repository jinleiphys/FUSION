# Second look to the Polyakov Loop Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at finite baryonic density

> We revisit the Polyakov Loop coupled Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model that maintains the Polyakov loop dynamics in the limit of zero temperature. This is of interest for astrophysical applications in the interior of neutron stars. For this purpose we re-examine the form of the potential for the deconfinement order parameter at finite baryonic densities. Since the modification of this potential at any temperature is formally equivalent to assigning a baryonic charge to gluons, we develop a more general formulation of the present model that cures this spurious effect and is normalized to match the asymptotic behaviour of the QCD equation of state given by $\mathcal{O}(α_s^2)$ and partial $\mathcal{O}(α_s^3\ln^2α_s)$ perturbative results.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model loses Polyakov loop dynamics at zero temperature because the coupling between quarks and the Polyakov loop is exponentially suppressed and the gluonic potential vanishes, so the authors propose a new density-dependent parametrization of the Polyakov loop potential that cures the spurious assignment of baryonic charge to gluons and matches perturbative QCD asymptotics.

## Method

The authors modify the PNJL model by making the coefficients \(b_2\) and \(b_4\) of the Polyakov loop potential \(\mathcal{U}(\Phi,\Phi^*)\) functions of baryonic density \(n_B\) rather than chemical potential, with power-law exponents \(\kappa_2 = 4/3\) and \(\kappa_4 = 0\) chosen to ensure correct high-density asymptotic behavior. They also introduce a companion potential \(\mathcal{V}(\Phi,\Phi^*)\) that shifts the effective quark chemical potential and enforce a consistency condition \(n_B \partial\mathcal{V}/\partial n_B - \partial\mathcal{U}/\partial n_B = 0\) to guarantee that only quarks contribute to baryonic density. The model is fitted to vacuum condensates from lattice QCD and normalized so that the zero-temperature pressure reaches 80% of the Stefan-Boltzmann limit at high density, matching \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\) perturbative results.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum light quark condensate: \(|\langle\overline{l}l\rangle_0|^{1/3} = 281\) MeV, consistent with lattice result \(283(2)\) MeV.
- Vacuum strange quark condensate: \(|\langle\overline{s}s\rangle_0|^{1/3} = 315\) MeV, exceeding the lattice value \(290(15)\) MeV by about 9% (3% within errors).
- Pion decay constant from Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation: \(f_\pi = 126.96\) MeV, close to the Particle Data Group value \(130.2(1.7)\) MeV.
- Effective quark masses in vacuum: \(m^*_u = 182.6\) MeV, \(m^*_d = 185.1\) MeV, \(m^*_s = 275.4\) MeV.
- Parameter \(\widetilde{a}_2 = 0.358 \cdot N_f^{-1/3}\), giving \(\widetilde{a}_2 \simeq 0.25\) for \(N_f = 3\).
- Critical baryonic density for nonzero Polyakov loop: \(n_c = (3\widetilde{a}_4/\widetilde{a}_2)^{3/4} T_0^3\), with \(T_0 = 270\) MeV.
- Applicability range: baryonic chemical potential \(\mu_B \lesssim 2\) GeV for neutron star interiors, with cutoff \(\Lambda = 925.06\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model of Fukushima (2004) and Ratti et al. (2006) to zero temperature by introducing a density-dependent Polyakov loop potential, correcting the spurious baryonic charge of gluons that arises when the potential depends on chemical potential as in previous approaches (e.g., Schaefer et al., 2007). The model is normalized to match perturbative QCD results at high density from Kurkela et al. (2010) and lattice QCD data for the equation of state. It enables construction of hybrid quark-hadron equations of state for neutron star interiors, where the Polyakov loop modulus serves as a deconfinement order parameter that switches from zero to nonzero at a critical density, providing a Polyakov-Gibbs phase transition criterion.
