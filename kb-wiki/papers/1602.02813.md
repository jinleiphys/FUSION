# Mode-coupling effects in anisotropic flow in heavy-ion collisions

> Higher-order anisotropic flows in heavy-ion collisions are affected by nonlinear mode coupling effects. It has been suggested that the associated nonlinear hydrodynamic response coefficients probe the transport properties and are largely insensitive to the spectrum of initial density fluctuations of the medium created in these collisions. To test this suggestion, we explore nonlinear mode coupling effects in event-by-event viscous fluid dynamics, using two different models for the fluctuating initial density profiles, and compare the nonlinear coupling coefficients between the initial eccentricity vectors before hydrodynamic expansion and the final flow vectors after the expansion. For several mode coupling coefficients we find significant sensitivity to the initial fluctuation spectrum. They all exhibit strong sensitivity to the specific shear viscosity at freeze-out, but only weak dependence on the shear viscosity during hydrodynamic evolution.

## Key claim

The paper tests the claim by Yan and Ollitrault (2015) that nonlinear hydrodynamic mode coupling coefficients are largely insensitive to the initial density fluctuation spectrum and thus provide a clean probe of transport properties; it finds that while some coefficients are indeed weakly sensitive to initial conditions, others (especially trilinear coupling terms) show significant sensitivity to the initial fluctuation spectrum, contradicting the claim of universal initial-state independence.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the iEBE-VISHNU code package for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 A TeV, starting at tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c without pre-equilibrium flow and ending on an isothermal freeze-out surface at T_dec = 120 MeV. They used two different models for fluctuating initial density profiles (Monte Carlo Glauber and Monte Carlo Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi) and compared the nonlinear coupling coefficients between initial eccentricity vectors and final flow vectors, computing Pearson correlation coefficients and mode coupling coefficients from the hydrodynamic output using the Cooper-Frye algorithm including resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- Hydrodynamic simulations started at tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c and ended at T_dec = 120 MeV
- Default specific shear viscosity values: eta/s = 0.08 for MC-Glauber initial conditions and eta/s = 0.2 for MC-KLN initial conditions
- 2000 hydrodynamic runs with fluctuating initial profiles were performed for each initial condition model and each centrality bin
- The bilinear coupling coefficient chi_422 showed complete insensitivity to shear viscosity, with differences between eta/s values much smaller than differences between MC-Glauber and MC-KLN initial conditions
- For 0-5% centrality, results for trilinear coupling coefficients fluctuated significantly and were statistically uncertain

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nonlinear mode coupling in anisotropic flow introduced by Yan and Ollitrault (2015) and Bhalerao et al. (2014), who suggested that nonlinear hydrodynamic response coefficients should be independent of initial density fluctuations. The paper contradicts this suggestion by demonstrating that several mode coupling coefficients (chi_6222 and chi_7223) differ significantly between MC-Glauber and MC-KLN initial conditions. The results also disagree with Yan and Ollitrault (2015) on the magnitudes of some coefficients (finding chi_633 less than or equal to chi_6222, whereas Yan and Ollitrault found the opposite), which the authors attribute to the use of genuinely fluctuating initial conditions versus smooth Gaussian profiles. The work enables future studies to identify which observables can separate sensitivity to transport properties from sensitivity to initial conditions.
