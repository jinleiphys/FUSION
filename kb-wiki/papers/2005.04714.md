# Non-resonant Density of States Enhancement at Low Energies for Three or Four Neutrons

> The low energy systems of three or four neutrons are treated within the adiabatic hyperspherical framework, yielding an understanding of the low energy quantum states in terms of an adiabatic potential energy curve. The dominant low energy potential curve for each system, computed here using widely accepted nucleon-nucleon interactions with and without the inclusion of a three-nucleon force, shows no sign of a low energy resonance. However, both systems exhibit a low energy enhancement of the density of states, or of the Wigner-Smith time-delay, which derives from long-range universal physics analogous to the Efimov effect. That enhancement could be relevant to understanding the low energy excess of correlated 4-neutron ejection events observed experimentally in a nuclear reaction by Kisamori et al.

## Key claim
The three-neutron and four-neutron systems show no low-energy resonance or bound state, but both exhibit a low-energy enhancement of the density of states (or Wigner-Smith time delay) that derives from long-range universal physics analogous to the Efimov effect, not from a resonant state.

## Method
The authors solved the Schrodinger equation for three and four neutrons using the adiabatic hyperspherical representation, diagonalizing the fixed-hyperradius Hamiltonian at each hyperradius to obtain adiabatic potential energy curves. They employed two variational basis sets: hyperspherical harmonics (accurate at small and intermediate hyperradii) and correlated Gaussian functions (accurate at large hyperradii), using realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (AV18, AV8', chiral NV2-Ia) with and without three-nucleon forces, plus a simple Gaussian potential tuned to the neutron-neutron scattering length and effective range.

## Key numbers
- The neutron-neutron singlet scattering length is approximately a = -18 fm, consistently reproduced by all NN interactions considered.
- For the 3n system (J^pi = 3/2^- symmetry), the effective angular momentum l_eff = 5/2 and the long-range coefficient C = 15.22.
- For the 4n system (J^pi = 0^+ symmetry), the effective angular momentum l_eff = 5 and the long-range coefficient C = 86.68.
- The HH basis used K_max = 140 for the 4n system and K_max = 801 for the 3n system.
- The CGHS expansion used 208 L=0 and 92 L=2 basis functions for the 4n system, and 57 L=1 basis functions for the 3n system.
- The density of states diverges as E^{-1/2} as energy approaches zero, with the scattering phase shift behaving as delta -> -C a k/(2 l_eff + 2 l_eff^2) as wavenumber k -> 0.

## Context
This work extends the adiabatic hyperspherical approach previously successful in atomic physics (Lin 1975, Botero 1986) to few-neutron systems, contradicting claims of a low-energy tetraneutron resonance from some prior studies (Shirokov and Vary 2016, Gandolfi 2017, Li, Michel and Hu 2019) while supporting alternative analyses that found no resonance (Hiyama and Lazauskas 2016, Deltuva 2018, Deltuva and Lazauskas 2019). The results enable interpretation of the enhanced 4-neutron coincidence events observed by Kisamori et al. as arising from a non-resonant density of states enhancement rather than a tetraneutron resonance, and connect the behavior to universal physics of few-fermion systems near the unitarity limit.
