# Electromagnetic Contribution to the Proton-Neutron Mass Splitting

> We study the electromagnetic contribution to the proton-neutron mass splitting by combining lattice simulations and the modified Cottingham sum rule of Walker-Loud, Carlson and Miller. This analysis yields an estimate of the isovector nucleon magnetic polarizability as a function of pion mass. The physical value, obtained by chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass, is $β_{p-n}=(-1.12 \pm 0.40)\times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{fm}^3$, which is in agreement with the empirical result, albeit with a somewhat smaller error. As a result, we find $δM^γ_{p-n}=1.04 \pm 0.11\ \mathrm{MeV}$, which represents a significant improvement in precision.

# Electromagnetic Contribution to the Proton-Neutron Mass Splitting

## Key claim
By combining lattice QCD simulations from the RBC Collaboration with the modified Cottingham sum rule of Walker-Loud, Carlson and Miller (WLCM), the authors extract the isovector nucleon magnetic polarizability as a function of pion mass and obtain an improved estimate of the electromagnetic contribution to the proton-neutron mass splitting, with the caveat that the result depends on the choice of form factor (cubic or quartic) used to model the inelastic subtraction term.

## Method
The authors take lattice QCD data from the RBC Collaboration for the electromagnetic proton-neutron mass splitting at two lattice volumes (16^3 and 24^3) and several pion masses, then compute the finite-volume elastic contributions using parametrized nucleon form factors fitted to lattice data from the QCDSF Collaboration. The discrepancy between the calculated elastic contributions and the full lattice results is attributed to the finite-volume inelastic subtraction term, which is proportional to the isovector magnetic polarizability β_{p-n}; fitting this discrepancy at each pion mass yields β_{p-n}(m_π), which is then chirally extrapolated to the physical pion mass using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers
- Physical proton-neutron mass splitting: M_n - M_p = 1.2933322(4) MeV (from Mohr (2012) and PDG (2012))
- Isovector nucleon magnetic polarizability at physical pion mass: β_{p-n} = (-1.12 ± 0.40) × 10^{-4} fm^3 (average of cubic and quartic form factor results)
- Total electromagnetic contribution to the proton-neutron mass splitting: δM^γ_{p-n} = 1.04 ± 0.11 MeV
- Inelastic subtraction term (averaged): δM^{inel}_{sub}|_{p-n} = 0.21 ± 0.11 MeV
- Resulting up-down quark mass contribution: δM^{d-u} = 2.33 ± 0.11 MeV

## Context
This work extends the modified Cottingham sum-rule approach of Walker-Loud, Carlson and Miller (2012), who obtained δM^γ = 1.30(03)(47) MeV with large uncertainty, and improves upon the earlier standard Cottingham sum-rule result of Gasser and Leutwyler (1982) of 0.76(30) MeV. The analysis also provides an alternative to the direct lattice QCD calculation by the BMW Collaboration (2013), who reported 1.59(30)(35) MeV. The improved precision enables a more accurate determination of the up-down quark mass difference, which is critical for understanding explicit chiral symmetry breaking in QCD and has implications for grand unification and CP violation studies.
