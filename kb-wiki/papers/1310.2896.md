# Flow probe of symmetry energy in relativistic heavy-ion reactions

> Flow observables in heavy-ion reactions at incident energies up to about 1 GeV per nucleon have been shown to be very useful for investigating the reaction dynamics and for determining the parameters of reaction models based on transport theory. In particular, the elliptic flow in collisions of neutron-rich heavy-ion systems emerges as an observable sensitive to the strength of the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities. The comparison of ratios or differences of neutron and proton flows or neutron and hydrogen flows with predictions of transport models favors an approximately linear density dependence, consistent with ab-initio nuclear-matter theories. Extensive parameter searches have shown that the model dependence is comparable to the uncertainties of existing experimental data. Comprehensive new flow data of high accuracy, partly also through providing stronger constraints on model parameters, can thus be expected to improve our knowledge of the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The comparison of ratios or differences of neutron and proton flows (or neutron and hydrogen flows) with transport model predictions favors an approximately linear density dependence of the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, consistent with ab-initio nuclear-matter theories, but the model dependence is comparable to the uncertainties of existing experimental data.

## Method

The authors review experimental data on collective flow observables (directed and elliptic flow) from heavy-ion reactions at incident energies up to about 1 GeV per nucleon, primarily using data from the FOPI/LAND Collaboration for 197Au + 197Au collisions at 400 MeV per nucleon. They compare measured neutron and proton flow ratios and differences with predictions from isospin-dependent transport models, including quantum-molecular dynamics (QMD) and Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) approaches, using various parameterizations of the symmetry energy characterized by power-law coefficients γ or the parameter x in the MDI interaction.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 nucleons/fm³
- The Fermi energy ε_F ≈ 28 MeV, giving a kinetic contribution to the symmetry energy of ε_F/3
- The slope parameter L = 3ρ₀ · dE_sym/dρ|ρ₀ falls in the interval 20 MeV ≤ L ≤ 100 MeV, with most probable value L ≈ 60 MeV, corresponding to power-law coefficient γ ≈ 0.6
- The result γ = 0.69 (L = 65 MeV) was deduced from MSU isospin-diffusion data and neutron-skin thickness in 208Pb
- The compressibility K ≈ 230 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter
- The balance energy for directed flow is roughly 50 to 100 MeV per nucleon
- Squeeze-out (v₂ < 0) is observed at incident energies between about 150 MeV per nucleon and 4 GeV per nucleon, with a maximum near 400 MeV per nucleon

## Context

This review extends earlier work on collective flows as probes of the nuclear equation of state (EoS), building on studies by Bao-An Li (2002) who proposed differential directed flow for studying the asy-EoS, and by Russotto (2011) and Cozma (2011) who showed elliptic flow sensitivity to symmetry energy variations. The paper addresses conflicting interpretations from meson production data (π⁻/π⁺ ratios analyzed by Xiao (2009), Feng and Jin (2010), and Xie (2013)) which yielded contradictory conclusions from soft to super-soft symmetry energy behavior. The work enables future high-precision measurements of collective flows using the LAND and CHIMERA detector systems at GSI, which can provide stronger constraints on the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities.
