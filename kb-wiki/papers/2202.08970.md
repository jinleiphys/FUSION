# Status and initial physics performance studies of the MPD experiment at NICA

> The Nuclotron-base Ion Collider fAcility (NICA) is under construction at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR), with commissioning of the facility expected in late 2022. The Multi-Purpose Detector (MPD) has been designed to operate at NICA and its components are currently in production. The detector is expected to be ready for data taking with the first beams from NICA. This document provides an overview of the landscape of the investigation of the QCD phase diagram in the region of maximum baryonic density, where NICA and MPD will be able to provide significant and unique input. It also provides a detailed description of the MPD set-up, including its various subsystems as well as its support and computing infrastructures. Selected performance studies for particular physics measurements at MPD are presented and discussed in the context of existing data and theoretical expectations.

## Key claim

The MPD experiment at NICA is designed to explore the QCD phase diagram in the region of maximum baryonic density, and the paper presents performance studies indicating that the detector will provide "significant and unique input" to this investigation, contingent on the facility's commissioning expected in late 2022 and the staged installation of detector subsystems.

## Method

The paper describes the MPD detector subsystems (Time Projection Chamber, Time-of-Flight system, Electromagnetic Calorimeter, Fast Forward Detector, Forward Hadronic Calorimeter, and superconducting solenoid magnet) and reports selected performance studies based on Monte Carlo simulations and test-beam measurements of individual detector components. The performance evaluations use standard heavy-ion physics observables including particle identification via energy loss and time-of-flight, momentum resolution, and event-plane determination for collective flow measurements.

## Key numbers

- Solenoid magnetic field: up to 0.57 T, with default operational setting of 0.5 T, uniform along the beam direction in an aperture of 4596 mm diameter
- TPC transverse momentum threshold: pT > 50 MeV/c for charged particle tracking
- TPC drift gas mixture: 90% Ar + 10% CH4 at atmospheric pressure plus 2 mbar overpressure
- TPC drift velocity: 5.45 cm/microsecond, with maximum drift time less than 30 microseconds
- TPC maximum event rate: 7 kHz at luminosity L = 10^27 cm^-2 s^-1
- TPC two-track resolution: approximately 1 cm
- TPC total number of readout pads: 95232, arranged in 53 pad rows
- TPC signal-to-noise ratio: 30:1 with 10-bit dynamical range
- TPC electric field strength: approximately 140 V/cm
- TPC gas amplification factor: approximately 10^4
- TPC temperature stability requirement: less than 0.5 degrees Celsius
- Magnet yoke mechanical precision: 200 micrometers or better
- Momentum range for reconstructed particles: 0.1 to 3 GeV/c

## Context

This paper presents the status of the MPD experiment at the NICA facility under construction at JINR, extending the heavy-ion collision program previously conducted at RHIC (STAR Beam Energy Scan) and SPS (NA61/SHINE) into the region of maximum baryonic density. The MPD will enable measurements of collective flow, particle yields, and fluctuations that can be compared with predictions from models such as UrQMD, AMPT, PHSD, and SMASH, and will complement the future FAIR program at GSI. The paper provides the technical basis for the MPD physics program and establishes performance benchmarks for the first stage of detector operation beginning in late 2022.
