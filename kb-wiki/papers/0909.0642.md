# Neutrino interactions with nuclei

> We present a model for neutrino-nucleus scattering in the energy region relevant for present and forthcoming neutrino-oscillation experiments. The model is based on the RPA treatment of the nuclear responses in the quasi-elastic and Delta-resonance region. It includes also in a phenomenological way nucleon knock-out. It aims at the description, within a single framework, of several final state channels i.e. quasi-elastic, incoherent and coherent one-pion production and two- or several-nucleon knock-out.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified model for neutrino-nucleus scattering around 1 GeV that treats quasi-elastic, coherent and incoherent pion production, and multi-nucleon knockout within a single RPA framework; the model's predictions for ratios of cross-sections are compatible with experimental limits from K2K, SciBooNE, and MiniBooNE, but the multi-nucleon knockout contribution is not well constrained by phenomenology.

## Method

The authors calculate nuclear response functions in the random phase approximation (RPA) ring approximation, starting from bare polarization propagators and solving integral equations with an effective particle-hole interaction. The bare response includes contributions from NN quasi-elastic (Lindhard function), NN 2p-2h, NΔ and ΔN 2p-2h, ΔΔ πN, ΔΔ 2p-2h, and ΔΔ 3p-3h components, evaluated using a local density approximation with experimental nuclear densities. The RPA response separates into a term from cuts on the pion exchange potential (coherent pion production) and a term proportional to the bare polarization propagator modified by collective effects.

## Key numbers

- For charged current coherent pion production off 12C, the K2K collaboration established a 90% confidence-level upper bound on the ratio to total cross-section of 0.60 × 10⁻² averaged over a neutrino flux with mean energy 1.3 GeV
- The SciBooNE collaboration found a ratio of 0.67 × 10⁻² for the same quantity at neutrino energy 1.1 GeV
- The incoherent π⁺ channel results to be 5/6 of the total incoherent pion production for charged current on 12C
- The model's predictions for the ratio of π⁺ production to quasi-elastic cross-section are compatible with MiniBooNE data up to approximately 1.2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the RPA treatment of nuclear responses developed by Marteau (1999) to simultaneously describe multiple final-state channels in neutrino-nucleus scattering. The model addresses discrepancies between experimental results from K2K (Hasegawa, 2005), SciBooNE (Hiraide, 2008), MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arevalo, 2009), and ANL (Radecky, 1981) by providing a unified theoretical framework. The approach enables comparison with measured ratios of pion production to quasi-elastic cross-sections and suggests that 2p-2h and 3p-3h channels contribute significantly to the total cross-section, though the multi-nucleon knockout component remains poorly constrained by existing phenomenology.
