# Triton/$^{3}$He ratio as an observable for neutron skin thickness

> Based on the framework of the Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model in which the initial neutron and proton densities are sampled according to the droplet model, the correlation between triton-to-$^{3}$He yield ratio (R(t/$^{3}$He)$=$Yield(t)/Yield($^{3}$He)) and neutron skin thickness ($δ_{np}$) in neutron-rich projectile induced reactions is investigated. By changing the diffuseness parameter of neutron density distribution in the droplet model for the projectile to obtain different $δ_{np}$, the relationship between $δ_{np}$ and the corresponding R(t/$^{3}$He) in semi-peripheral collisions is obtained. The calculated results show that R(t/$^{3}$He) has a strong linear correlation with $δ_{np}$ for neutron-rich $^{50}$Ca and $^{68}$Ni nuclei. It is suggested that R(t/$^{3}$He) could be regarded as a good experimental observable to extract $δ_{np}$ for neutron-rich nuclei because the yields of charged particles triton and $^{3}$He can be measured quite precisely.

## Key claim

The triton-to-\(^{3}\)He yield ratio R(t/\(^{3}\)He) shows a strong linear correlation with neutron skin thickness \(\delta_{np}\) for neutron-rich projectiles in IQMD simulations, and is proposed as a practical experimental observable because charged particles triton and \(^{3}\)He can be measured more precisely than neutrons. The hedge is that this correlation is demonstrated only for the specific systems \(^{50}\)Ca and \(^{68}\)Ni at 50 MeV/nucleon in semi-peripheral collisions, and the linear dependence holds under the particular IQMD parameter set used (soft EOS, \(L = 2.16\) fm\(^2\)).

## Method

The authors used the Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate semi-peripheral collisions of \(^{50}\)Ca and \(^{68}\)Ni projectiles on a \(^{12}\)C target at 50 MeV/nucleon. Initial neutron and proton density distributions for the projectile were sampled from the droplet model, and the neutron diffuseness parameter was varied (by a factor \(f_n\) from 1.0 to 1.5) to produce different values of neutron skin thickness \(\delta_{np}\). Fragments were constructed using a coalescence model with relative momentum cutoff \(P_0 = 300\) MeV/c and relative distance cutoff \(R_0 = 3.5\) fm, and yields were accumulated for emitted particles with reduced impact parameter \(0.6 < b/b_{\text{max}} < 1.0\) and rapidity \(y > 0\) over the time interval 150–200 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{50}\)Ca+\(^{12}\)C at 50 MeV/nucleon, a strong linear correlation between R(t/\(^{3}\)He) and \(\delta_{np}\) was obtained under conditions \(0.6 < b/b_{\text{max}} < 1.0\) and \(y > 0\).
- For \(^{68}\)Ni+\(^{12}\)C at 50 MeV/nucleon, a similar strong linear correlation between R(t/\(^{3}\)He) and \(\delta_{np}\) was obtained under the same conditions.
- The double ratio R(t/\(^{3}\)He)/R(n/p) was found to be almost constant as a function of \(\delta_{np}\) for both systems, indicating proportionality between R(t/\(^{3}\)He) and the n/p ratio.
- For \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{12}\)C compared with \(^{50}\)Ca+\(^{12}\)C, the value of R(t/\(^{3}\)He) decreased by about 7%, consistent with the change in N/Z from 1.5 (\(^{50}\)Ca) to 1.4 (\(^{48}\)Ca), while the slope of the linear dependence on \(\delta_{np}\) remained the same.
- The Gaussian wave packet width used in the IQMD model was \(L = 2.16\) fm\(^2\).
- The coalescence parameters were \(P_0 = 300\) MeV/c and \(R_0 = 3.5\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Sun et al. (2010) which proposed the neutron-to-proton ratio R(n/p) as an observable for neutron skin thickness using the IQMD model, but noted the experimental difficulty of precise neutron detection. The present paper replaces R(n/p) with the charged-particle ratio R(t/\(^{3}\)He), which is easier to measure experimentally, and demonstrates that it retains the same linear sensitivity to \(\delta_{np}\). The approach enables future experimental extraction of neutron skin thickness for neutron-rich nuclei from measured triton and \(^{3}\)He yields, which could then constrain the nuclear symmetry energy and the equation of state of neutron-rich matter.
