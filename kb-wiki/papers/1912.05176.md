# Effect of color reconnection on forward-backward multiplicity and mean transverse momentum correlation

> Color reconnection (CR) mechanism in PYTHIA model has been reported to be essential to describe the flow-like collective effect observed in high multiplicity $p$+$p$ and $p$+Pb collisions. In this work, we test this mechanism towards explaining the Forward-Backward multiplicity correlation (b$_{\mathrm {cor}}$) measurements in $p$+$p$ collisions at the LHC energies. Out of the three different CR schemes implemented in PYTHIA, (a) MPI based CR (default mechanism), (b) QCD based CR and (c) Gluon moved CR, we found that the QCD based CR scheme describes relatively better the ALICE measurements of b$_{\mathrm {cor}}$ in $p$+$p$ collisions at $\sqrt{\mathrm s}$ = 0.9 and 7 TeV. In addition, we have tuned the parameters of the default CR mechanism in PYTHIA to describe simultaneously the measured charged particle multiplicity pseudo-rapidity ($η$) distribution and b$_{\mathrm {cor}}$. We found that an average number of multipartonic interactions ($\langle N_{\mathrm {MPI}} \rangle$) between 2.5 to 3 and CR range between 0.9 to 2.5 best describes the experimental data. Finally, we have presented a study using PYTHIA events for $p$+$p$ collisions at $\sqrt{\mathrm s}$ = 7 TeV which shows that the strength of Forward-Backward mean transverse momentum correlation (b$^{\langle p_{\mathrm{T}}\rangle \langle p_{\mathrm{T}} \rangle}_{\rm {cor}}$) is found to increase with CR in contrast to decrease of b$_{\rm {cor}}$ values with CR effect. Hence, simultaneously studying the b$^{\langle p_{\mathrm{T}}\rangle \langle p_{\mathrm{T}} \rangle}_{\rm {cor}}$ and b$_{\mathrm {\rm {cor}}}$ in the experiments will help in establishing the arguments either in favour or in disfavour of CR effect in the measurements.

## Key claim

Color reconnection (CR) is required to describe ALICE forward-backward multiplicity correlation measurements in p+p collisions, but all three CR schemes implemented in PYTHIA overestimate the experimental values; the QCD-based CR scheme provides the best relative description, and a tuned default CR model with average multipartonic interactions between 2.5 and 3 and CR range between 0.9 and 2.5 best describes the data simultaneously for both the pseudorapidity distribution and the correlation.

## Method

The authors generated 15 million inelastic p+p events at sqrt(s) = 0.9 and 7 TeV using PYTHIA 8.235 with four configurations: no CR, MPI-based default CR, QCD-based CR (new CR), and gluon-move CR. They compared the forward-backward multiplicity correlation b_cor as a function of eta_gap for different delta_eta against ALICE measurements, then tuned the default CR model parameters pT0Ref and CR range via chi-squared minimization to simultaneously describe the charged-particle pseudorapidity distribution and b_cor. They also computed the forward-backward mean transverse momentum correlation b_cor^<pT><pT> for CR-on and CR-off cases.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the ALICE Collaboration (2015) and ATLAS Collaboration (2016) on forward-backward multiplicity correlations in p+p collisions at LHC energies, and builds on the finding by the PYTHIA authors (2016) that color reconnection is essential for describing flow-like collective effects in small systems. The authors refine the approach of a prior PYTHIA study (2018) that used sharp cuts on number of MPI, instead performing a systematic tuning of both MPI and CR parameters simultaneously. The results enable experimental tests of the CR mechanism by comparing the opposite effects of CR on b_cor (decreases) versus b_cor^<pT><pT> (increases), and provide tuned PYTHIA parameters for future small-system studies.
