# Hidden-charm and bottom meson-baryon molecules coupled with five-quark states

> In this paper, we investigate the hidden-charm pentaquarks as $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}Λ_{\rm c}$ and $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}Σ^{(\ast)}_{\rm c}$ molecules coupled to the five-quark states. Furthermore, we extend our calculations to the hidden-bottom sector. The coupling to the five-quark states is treated as the short range potential, where the relative strength for the meson-baryon channels is determined by the structure of the five-quark states. We found that resonant and/or bound states appear in both the charm and bottom sectors. The five-quark state potential turned out to be attractive and, for this reason, it plays an important role to produce these states. In the charm sector, we need the five-quark potential in addition to the pion exchange potential in producing bound and resonant states, whereas, in the bottom sector, the pion exchange interaction is strong enough to produce states. Thus, from this investigation, it emerges that the hidden-bottom pentaquarks are more likely to form than their hidden-charm counterparts; for this reason, we suggest that the experimentalists should look for states in the bottom sector.

## Key claim

Hidden-bottom pentaquarks are more likely to form than their hidden-charm counterparts because in the bottom sector the one-pion exchange interaction alone is strong enough to produce bound and resonant states, whereas in the charm sector an additional attractive five-quark short-range potential is required alongside the pion exchange.

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled-channel Schrödinger equation for meson-baryon systems ($\bar{D}^{(\ast)}\Lambda_{\rm c}$, $\bar{D}^{(\ast)}\Sigma^{(\ast)}_{\rm c}$ for charm; $B^{(\ast)}\Lambda_{\rm b}$, $B^{(\ast)}\Sigma^{(\ast)}_{\rm b}$ for bottom) coupled to compact five-quark states. The long-range interaction was described by the one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) with dipole form factors, while the coupling to five-quark states was treated as a short-range effective potential whose relative strengths between channels were determined by spectroscopic factors from the quark cluster model. The model was solved for total spin-parities $J^P = 1/2^-$, $3/2^-$, and $5/2^-$ with isospin $I=1/2$.

## Key numbers

- The mass difference between $\bar{D}^\ast$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons is approximately 140 MeV, while between $B^\ast$ and $B$ mesons it is approximately 45 MeV.
- The mass difference between $\Sigma^\ast_{\rm c}$ and $\Sigma_{\rm c}$ baryons is approximately 65 MeV, while between $\Sigma^\ast_{\rm b}$ and $\Sigma_{\rm b}$ baryons it is approximately 20 MeV.
- The cutoff for the nucleon form factor is $\Lambda_N = 837$ MeV, with heavy meson cutoffs $\Lambda_{\bar{D}} = 1.35 \Lambda_N$ and $\Lambda_{B} = 1.29 \Lambda_N$, and heavy baryon cutoffs approximately equal to $\Lambda_N$.
- The pion decay constant used is $f_\pi = 92.3$ MeV, and the heavy meson-pion coupling constant is $g_\pi = 0.59$.
- The threshold difference between open-bottom meson-baryon channels and the $\Upsilon(1S)N$ channel is on the order of 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the molecular approach to pentaquarks by incorporating compact five-quark states as short-range components, inspired by the quark cluster model calculations of Takeuchi et al. (2016). It builds upon studies of hidden-charm pentaquarks $P^+_c(4380)$ and $P^+_c(4450)$ observed by LHCb (Aaij et al., 2015) and prior molecular interpretations by Wu et al. (2010), Garcia-Recio et al. (2013), and Karliner and Rosner (2015). The paper provides predictions for hidden-bottom pentaquark masses and widths that could guide experimental searches at facilities such as LHCb, Jefferson Lab, and J-PARC.
