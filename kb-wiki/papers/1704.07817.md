# $A$-dependence of quasielastic charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections

> Background: 12C has been and is still widely used in neutrino-nucleus scattering and oscillation experiments. More recently, 40Ar has emerged as an important nuclear target for current and future experiments. Liquid argon time projection chambers (LArTPCs) possess various advantages in measuring electroweak neutrino-nucleus cross sections. Concurrent theoretical research is an evident necessity. Purpose: 40Ar is larger than 12C, and one expects nuclear effects to play a bigger role in reactions. We present inclusive differential and total cross section results for charged-current neutrino scattering on 40Ar and perform a comparison with 12C, 16O and 56Fe targets, to find out about the A-dependent behavior of model predictions. Method: Our model starts off with a Hartree-Fock description of the nucleus, with the nucleons interacting through a mean field generated by an effective Skyrme force. Long-range correlations are introduced by means of a continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) approach. Further methods to improve the accuracy of model predictions are also incorporated in the calculations. Results: We present calculations for 12C, 16O, 40Ar and 56Fe, showcasing differential cross sections over a broad range of kinematic values in the quasielastic regime. We furthermore show flux-folded results for 40Ar and we discuss the differences between nuclear responses. Conclusions: At low incoming energies and forward scattering we identify an enhancement in the 40Ar cross section compared to 12C, as well as in the high $ω$ (low $T_μ$ ) region across the entire studied $E_ν$ range. The contribution to the folded cross section of the reaction strength at values of $ω$ lower than 50 MeV for forward scattering is sizeable.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for charged-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, the cross section per neutron for 40Ar is enhanced relative to 12C at low incoming energies and forward scattering angles, and also in the high energy-transfer (low muon kinetic energy) region across the entire studied neutrino energy range, but this enhancement is partially due to the lower threshold energy of heavier nuclei.

## Method

The authors use a Hartree-Fock description of the nucleus with an effective Skyrme interaction (SkE2 parametrization) to generate single-particle wave functions, then incorporate long-range correlations through a continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) approach in coordinate space. The model includes several improvements: a dipole cutoff at the strong vertex (Lambda = 455 MeV), Coulomb corrections via the Modified Effective Momentum Approach, effective relativistic corrections, and a phenomenological folding with a Lorentzian of width 3 MeV to account for resonance widths. Calculations are performed for inclusive charged-current quasielastic scattering on 12C, 16O, 40Ar, and 56Fe targets.

## Key numbers

- The dipole cutoff parameter Lambda = 455 MeV was optimized in a chi-squared test comparing CRPA cross sections with worldwide A(e,e') experimental data.
- The Lorentzian folding width Gamma = 3 MeV is used to correct the overestimated height and underestimated width of giant resonances in the basic RPA formalism.
- For forward scattering angles (0.97 < cos(theta_mu) < 1.0) in flux-folded results, the contribution from excitations at energy transfer omega < 50 MeV contributes more than 50% of the strength when the outgoing muon kinetic energy T_mu is lower than approximately 700 MeV for 40Ar.
- The ratio of flux-folded single differential cross sections sigma_Ar/sigma_C starts at around 92% and increases slowly, with 40Ar getting more strength for forward bins with cos(theta) > 0.7.
- The Coulomb correction uses R = 1.24 A^(1/3) fm for the nuclear radius.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CRPA studies of inclusive quasielastic electron scattering on 12C, 16O, and 40Ca (Pandey 2014) and charged-current quasielastic neutrino scattering on 12C with comparisons to MiniBooNE and T2K data (Pandey 2013, 2014, 2016). It provides the first systematic comparison of A-dependence across four nuclei (12C, 16O, 40Ar, 56Fe) within the CRPA framework, motivated by the emergence of liquid argon time projection chambers (LArTPCs) in experiments such as MicroBooNE and DUNE. The results highlight that relativistic Fermi gas models, commonly used in Monte Carlo generators, are less suitable for modeling low momentum transfer events where nuclear effects like low-lying excitations and giant resonances are important, and that these effects are even more significant for 40Ar than for 12C.
