# Valence and sea quarks in the nucleon

> In this contribution, we discuss the spin and flavor content of the proton in the framework of the unquenched quark model, and address the role of valence and sea quarks in the nucleon.

# Valence and sea quarks in the nucleon

## Key claim
In the unquenched quark model (UQM), the pion cloud of the nucleon provides a qualitative understanding of the proton's spin and flavor content, but only when the $^{3}P_{0}$ coupling strength is normalized to the observed value of the proton flavor asymmetry; the model does not reproduce the experimental strange quark spin contribution $\Delta s$, which remains zero in the calculation.

## Method
The authors employ a simplified version of the UQM in which only the contribution of the pion cloud is taken into account, using a $^{3}P_{0}$ quark-antiquark pair creation mechanism. The baryon wave function includes the valence quark core plus contributions from $N\pi$ and $\Delta\pi$ components, with the $^{3}P_{0}$ coupling strength determined from the flavor asymmetry of the proton. The spin and flavor content of the proton is then computed from the resulting wave function and compared to experimental data from HERMES, COMPASS, E866/NuSea, and NMC.

## Key numbers
- Flavor asymmetry ${\cal A}(p)$: UQM1 value fixed to $0.118$ (E866/NuSea value) and UQM2 value fixed to $0.158$ (NMC value)
- $\Delta u$: UQM1 = $1.132$, UQM2 = $1.064$, compared to experimental $0.842 \pm 0.013$ (HERMES)
- $\Delta d$: UQM1 = $-0.368$, UQM2 = $-0.380$, compared to experimental $-0.427 \pm 0.013$ (HERMES)
- $\Delta \Sigma = \Delta u + \Delta d + \Delta s$: UQM1 = $0.764$, UQM2 = $0.684$, compared to experimental $0.330 \pm 0.039$ (HERMES)
- $g_A = \Delta u - \Delta d$: UQM1 = $1.500$, UQM2 = $1.444$, compared to experimental $1.2701 \pm 0.0025$ (PDG)
- Probability of $p \rightarrow n\pi^+$ fluctuation: UQM1 = $0.180$, in agreement with experimental $0.17 \pm 0.01$ (H1 and ZEUS)
- Total probability for pion fluctuation of the proton: UQM1 = $0.455$, in agreement with $0.470$ from Drell-Yan and semi-inclusive DIS analyses (Chang)

## Context
This work extends the constituent quark model (CQM) by incorporating sea quark effects through the unquenched quark model, addressing the proton flavor asymmetry and spin crisis that the CQM cannot explain. The authors show that their UQM results also hold for the meson-cloud model, and they contrast their approach with the chiral quark model of Eichten (1995) and Cheng and Li (1998), which predicts $\Delta L = 3{\cal A}(p)/2$ rather than the relation ${\cal A}(p) = \Delta L$ derived by Garvey (2001). The results enable further study of the role of orbital angular momentum in the proton spin, which the UQM finds contributes 25-30% of the proton spin.
