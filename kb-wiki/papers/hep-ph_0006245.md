# The chiral symmetry restoration phase transition in baryon spectrum

## Key claim
In the large $N_c$ limit, the light baryon spectrum exhibits a chiral restoration phase transition at sufficiently high excitation energy, evidenced by systematic parity doublets in the upper part of $N$ and $\Delta$ spectra. The argument is robust and general, not relying on the NJL model used for illustration, but the author notes that predictions of the large $N_c$ limit are expected to be correct only at the 20-30% confidence level when extrapolated to the real world with $N_c=3$.

## Method
The author uses the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the local approximation to illustrate the chiral symmetry breaking mechanism via the gap equation for the quark condensate. The analysis extends the finite-temperature gap equation to include a valence quark distribution function $n_v(p)$, representing baryons as systems of valence quarks on top of the vacuum. In the large $N_c$ limit, where the Hartree-Fock picture becomes exact and baryon strong decays vanish, the valence quarks are argued to be in thermal equilibrium with the vacuum, allowing a temperature to be ascribed to the system; at some critical temperature (excitation energy), the self-consistent gap solution vanishes, indicating chiral restoration.

## Key numbers
- Quark condensate value: $<\bar \psi \psi> \simeq -(240 - 250 \text{ MeV})^3$, representing the order parameter for chiral symmetry breaking.
- Parity doublet splittings in the upper baryon spectrum are typically within 5% of the baryon mass, with experimental uncertainties for baryon masses of order 100 MeV.
- Specific parity doublets listed include: $N(2220), \frac{9}{2}^+ - N(2250), \frac{9}{2}^-$; $N(1990), \frac{7}{2}^+ - N(2190), \frac{7}{2}^-$; $N(2000), \frac{5}{2}^+ - N(2200), \frac{5}{2}^-$; $N(1900), \frac{3}{2}^+ - N(2080), \frac{3}{2}^-$; $N(2100), \frac{1}{2}^+ - N(2090), \frac{1}{2}^-$; $\Delta(2300), \frac{9}{2}^+ - \Delta(2400), \frac{9}{2}^-$; $\Delta(1950), \frac{7}{2}^+ - \Delta(2200), \frac{7}{2}^-$; $\Delta(1905), \frac{5}{2}^+ - \Delta(1930), \frac{5}{2}^-$; $\Delta(1920), \frac{3}{2}^+ - \Delta(1940), \frac{3}{2}^-$; $\Delta(1910), \frac{1}{2}^+ - \Delta(1900), \frac{1}{2}^-$.
- The $N$-$\Delta$ splitting is of order 25-30% of the baryon mass, used to estimate the confidence level of large $N_c$ predictions.

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier speculation (Glozman, 2000) that the upper part of $N$ and $\Delta$ spectra exhibits chiral restoration, providing a general proof using large $N_c$ arguments. It builds on the Witten (1979) large $N_c$ baryon picture and the NJL model framework (Vogl and Weise, 1991; Klevansky, 1992; Hatsuda and Kunihiro, 1994) for chiral symmetry breaking. The paper enables future experimental searches for missing parity partners, particularly noting that states $N(2600), \frac{11}{2}^-$ and $\Delta(2420), \frac{11}{2}^-$ lack observed parity partners, and raises the question of whether chiral and deconfinement phase transitions coincide, which would imply a maximum baryon mass around 2.5 GeV.
