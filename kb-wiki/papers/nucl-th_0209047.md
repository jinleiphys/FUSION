# Scalar meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions near threshold

> We establish the model-independent spin structure of the matrix elements for the near-threshold scalar meson production in $pp-$ and $np$-collisions, when the final particles are emitted in S-state. Polarization phenomena are derived in a general form. The properties of the $t-$channel dynamics, which is based on different meson exchanges, are studied in terms of the $s-$channel parametrization of the matrix element. The prediction of a 'realistic' model, based on $π+σ$-exchanges are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a model-independent spin structure for scalar meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions near threshold, but only under the condition that all final particles are emitted in a relative S-state. The authors hedge that the "realistic" model predictions based on π+σ exchanges are presented as an example, not as a definitive calculation.

## Method

The authors derived the spin structure of the matrix elements for pp→pp+S⁰ and np→np+S⁰ (S⁰ = σ, f₀, or a₀) using two-component Pauli spinor parametrization, applying selection rules from P-parity, total angular momentum, and the Pauli principle. They then transformed t-channel meson-exchange contributions (σ, η, π, a₀) into the s-channel parametrization using a two-component Fierz transformation, and computed the resulting polarization observables and cross-section ratios in terms of partial threshold amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- For pp→pp+f₀ at energy excess Q=17 MeV over threshold: σ = (1.84 ± 0.29 +0.25/−0.35) nb
- For σ-exchange in np→np+S⁰: the ratio of cross sections ℛ_σ = σ(np)/σ(pp) = 2
- For π-exchange in np→np+S⁰: ℛ_π = 14
- For a₀-exchange in np→np+S⁰: ℛ_a = 14
- For π-exchange with FSI corrections using scattering lengths a_s(np)=−23.768 fm, a_t(np)=5.424 fm, a_s(pp)=−7.8098 fm: ℛ^(π) ≈ 11 (reduced from 14)
- For π-exchange with FSI: spin correlation coefficient C_nn^(π) ≈ −0.22 (changed sign from 0.29)
- For π-exchange with FSI: D_nn^(π) ≈ −0.96 (changed from −0.43)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model-independent formalism for vector meson production (Rekalo, 2001), pseudoscalar meson production (Rekalo, 2002), and strange particle production (Rekalo, 2002) in nucleon-nucleon collisions near threshold. The results enable experimental determination of the relative strength of σ- and π-exchange contributions through measurement of polarization observables in np collisions, and provide a framework for interpreting upcoming experiments at DISTO and COSY facilities. The paper notes that the structure of f₀ and a₀ mesons (whether qq̄ states, four-quark states, or KK̄ molecules) does not affect the spin-structure predictions.
