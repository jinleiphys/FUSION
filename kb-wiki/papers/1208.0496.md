# Multiplicities in Au-Au and Cu-Cu collisions at \sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4 and 200 GeV

> Likelihood ratio tests are performed for the hypothesis that charged-particle multiplicities measured in Au-Au and Cu-Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4$ and 200 GeV are distributed according to the negative binomial form. Results suggest that the hypothesis should be rejected in the all classes of collision systems and centralities of PHENIX-RHIC measurements. However, the application of the least-squares test statistic with systematic errors included shows that for the collision system Au-Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4$ GeV the hypothesis could not be rejected in general.

## Key claim

The hypothesis that charged-particle multiplicities measured in Au-Au and Cu-Cu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 and 200 GeV follow a negative binomial distribution should be rejected for all centrality classes when using the likelihood ratio test, but when the least-squares test statistic with systematic errors is applied, the hypothesis could not be rejected for the Au-Au system at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV.

## Method

The author applied the maximum likelihood method and likelihood ratio test (using the "likelihood chi-squared" statistic) to test the negative binomial distribution hypothesis for charged-particle multiplicity data from PHENIX-RHIC measurements. The analysis was performed on binned histograms with two different tail-cutting criteria (bins with n_i less than or equal to 5 excluded, and bins with n_i less than or equal to 40-80 excluded), and the test statistic distribution was approximated by a chi-squared distribution via Wilks theorem. The author also recalculated the least-squares test statistic using the PHENIX Collaboration's systematic error treatment, where systematic errors were estimated as 3.0 times the statistical errors, and derived the correct asymptotic distribution for this PHENIX test statistic.

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, centrality 0-5%: N = 653145 events, k-hat = 270.0, n-bar-hat = 61.85, chi-squared_lambda/n_d = 23.73 with P-value 0%
- For Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, centrality 50-55%: N = 662114 events, k-hat = 21.3, n-bar-hat = 8.82, chi-squared_lambda/n_d = 38.8 with P-value 0%
- The PHENIX systematic error factor was 3.0 times the statistical errors, making err_n_i^2 = 10 * n_i (since 1^2 + 3^2 = 10)
- The expectation value of the PHENIX test statistic divided by degrees of freedom is 0.1, not 1
- For Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV, the number of events was substantially greater (about 14%) than for other systems
- Parameter k-hat_dyn values differed from PHENIX results by about 20-30% for least central classes to about 5-10% for most central classes

## Context

This work extends the analysis of charged-particle multiplicity distributions originally performed by the PHENIX Collaboration (Adare 2008), who claimed the distributions followed the negative binomial form using least-squares fitting. The author applies the maximum likelihood method, which was previously used at lower energies by the E-802 Collaboration (Abbott 1995), and demonstrates that the PHENIX test statistic is equivalent to Neyman's chi-squared test statistic multiplied by 0.1, enabling proper goodness-of-fit calculations. The results enable future analyses to correctly assess whether multiplicity distributions in heavy-ion collisions follow the negative binomial distribution, particularly for the Au-Au system at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV where the hypothesis could not be rejected.
