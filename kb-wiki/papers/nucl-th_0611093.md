# Rapidity Dependence of HBT radii based on a hydrodynamical model

> We calculate two-pion correlation functions at finite rapidities based on a hydrodynamical model which does not assume explicit boost invariance along the collision axis. Extracting the HBT radii through $χ^2$ fits in both Cartesian and Yano-Koonin-Podgoretski\uı parametrizations, we compare them with the experimental results obtained by the PHOBOS. Based on the results, we discuss longitudinal expansion dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper shows that two different initial energy density distributions for a hydrodynamical model, both tuned to reproduce the same pseudorapidity distribution of charged hadrons, produce measurably different HBT radii at nonzero rapidities, particularly the out-long cross term in the Cartesian parametrization and the longitudinal radius in the Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskiı parametrization, but the Yano-Koonin rapidity shows no difference between the two initial conditions and agrees with experimental data.

## Method

The author solves (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics for a perfect fluid with azimuthal symmetry, using a Lagrangian method, with an equation of state featuring a first-order phase transition from a massless partonic gas to a hadron resonance gas. Two initial conditions (IC.A and IC.B) are constructed with different longitudinal profiles of energy density and baryon number density, both normalized to reproduce the PHOBOS pseudorapidity distribution and PHENIX transverse momentum spectra for 200A GeV Au+Au central collisions. Two-pion correlation functions are computed via the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription at T_f = 140 MeV, and HBT radii are extracted by chi-squared fits in both Cartesian and Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskiı parametrizations, integrating over the PHOBOS acceptance windows.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density maximum: 23.0 GeV/fm^3 for IC.A, 20.5 GeV/fm^3 for IC.B
- Initial proper time: tau_0 = 1 fm/c
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 140 MeV
- Critical temperature at vanishing baryonic chemical potential: T_c = 160 MeV
- R_long of IC.A is about 1 fm smaller than that of IC.B at midrapidity
- Out-long cross term R_ol^2 difference between IC.A and IC.B at the lowest K_T bin: about 4 fm^2
- R_tau values from the calculation show emission duration larger than the 0-2 fm/c range from parametric models

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic HBT analyses at RHIC that were limited to midrapidity (Heinz 2001, Morita 2002, Hirano 2002) by using the wider acceptance of the PHOBOS detector to study rapidity dependence. It follows the approach of Hirano (2005) in testing two different initial longitudinal profiles that both reproduce the pseudorapidity distribution. The results enable future measurements at forward rapidity to discriminate between initial conditions and improve understanding of longitudinal expansion dynamics beyond the boost-invariant approximation.
