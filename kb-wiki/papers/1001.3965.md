# Spin-flip M1 giant resonance as a challenge for Skyrme forces

> Despite a great success of the Skyrme mean-field approach in exploration of nuclear dynamics, it seems to fail in description of the spin-flip M1 giant resonance. The results for different Skyrme parameterizations are contradictory and poorly agree with experiment. In particular, there is no parameterization which simultaneously describes the one-peak gross structure of M1 strength in doubly magic nuclei and two-peak structure in heavy deformed nuclei. The reason of this mismatch could lie in an unsatisfactory treatment of spin correlations and spin-orbit interaction. We discuss the present status of the problem and possible ways of its solution. In particular, we inspect i) the interplay of the collective shift and spin-orbit splitting, ii) the isovector M1 response versus isospin-mixed responses, and iii) the role of tensor and isovector spin-orbit interaction.

## Key claim

Despite the success of the Skyrme mean-field approach for nuclear dynamics, no existing Skyrme parameterization can simultaneously describe the one-peak gross structure of the spin-flip M1 giant resonance in doubly magic nuclei and the two-peak structure in heavy deformed nuclei; the failure likely stems from an unsatisfactory treatment of spin correlations and spin-orbit interaction.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent separable Random-Phase-Approximation (SRPA) calculations based on the Skyrme energy-density functional, using eight different Skyrme parameterizations (SkT6, SkO, SkO', SG2, SkM*, SLy6, SkI4, SV-bas). The spin-flip M1 strength was computed for the spherical nucleus 208Pb and the deformed nuclei 158Gd and 238U, with both the static mean field and residual interaction derived from the same Skyrme functional. The analysis compared the unperturbed (two-quasiparticle) strength with the full SRPA strength to isolate the collective shift caused by the residual interaction, and examined the separate isovector M1(T=1) response as well as the effects of tensor and isovector spin-orbit terms.

## Key numbers

* For the eight Skyrme forces studied, the relative spin-orbit splitting E_so = E_so^n - E_so^p and the collective shift E_coll were compared; for forces SkM*, SLy6, SkT6, SG2, and SV-bas, E_coll < E_so, yielding a double-peak M1GR, while for SkI4, SkO, and SkO', E_coll > E_so, yielding a one-peak M1GR.
* In 208Pb, the isovector M1(T=1) response gave a dominant peak at 6.8 MeV (SG2), 7.2 MeV (SkO), and 8 MeV (SV-bas), compared to the experimental average resonance energy of 7.3 MeV.
* The Lorentz averaging parameter used for the strength function was Delta = 1 MeV for comparison with experiment, and Delta = 0.2 MeV for detailed spectral analysis in 208Pb.
* The spin g-factors were quenched by factors of 0.68 for protons and 0.64 for neutrons, giving g_s^p = 5.58 * 0.68 and g_s^n = -3.82 * 0.64.
* In the heaviest nucleus 238U, approximately 17000 two-quasiparticle K^pi = 1^+ pairs were included with excitation energies up to 50-70 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the first fully self-consistent systematic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock investigation of the spin-flip M1GR by Vesely et al. (2009), which used the same SRPA model and eight Skyrme forces to show that none could simultaneously describe spherical and deformed nuclei. The paper also relates to the study by Sagawa et al. (2009) which found a considerable influence of tensor forces on the M1GR in spherical nuclei. The results are an alarming message for Skyrme-Hartree-Fock descriptions of the Gamow-Teller resonance, which is a counterpart of the M1GR determined by the same spin and spin-orbit factors. The work enables future development of Skyrme functionals with improved treatment of tensor forces and isovector spin-orbit interaction, which could resolve the long-standing failure to describe this resonance.
