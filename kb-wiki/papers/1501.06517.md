# Instantons and Monopoles

> This study is part of a research program aimed to investigate the relations between instantons, monopoles, and chiral symmetry breaking. Monopoles are important 3-dimensional topological configurations existing in QCD, which are believed to produce colour confinement. Instantons are 4-dimensional topological configurations and are known to be related to chiral symmetry breaking. To study the relation between monopoles and instantons we generate configurations adding to the vacuum state static monopole-antimonopole pairs of opposite charges by use of a monopole creation operator. We observe that the monopole creation operator only adds long monopole loops to the configurations. We then count the number of fermion zero modes using Overlap fermions as a tool. As a result we find that each monopole-antimonopole pair of magnetic charge one adds one zero mode of chirality $\pm1$, i.e. one instanton of topological charge $\pm1$.

# Instantons and Monopoles

## Key claim

Each static monopole-antimonopole pair of magnetic charge one added to the QCD vacuum produces exactly one fermion zero mode of chirality ±1, equivalent to one instanton of topological charge ±1; however, the authors observe only the net topological charge in their simulations because pairs of zero modes of opposite chirality appear to escape detection at the modest lattice volumes studied.

## Method

The authors generate SU(3) pure gauge configurations on the lattice using the Wilson gauge action, then add static monopole-antimonopole pairs of opposite magnetic charges (mc = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4) using a monopole creation operator. They count fermion zero modes using the Overlap Dirac operator, compute the topological charge Q = n+ − n−, and determine the topological susceptibility from both direct zero-mode counting and Gaussian fits to the topological charge distribution. They verify that the monopole creation operator adds long monopole loops by measuring monopole densities and loop-length histograms in the Maximally Abelian gauge.

## Key numbers

- Topological susceptibility: χ = (1.86(6) × 10² MeV)⁴, consistent with the theoretical expectation of approximately (1.80 × 10² MeV)⁴ from the Witten-Veneziano formula.
- Instanton density: ρi = 8.3(3) × 10⁻⁴ GeV⁴, consistent with the instanton liquid model of Shuryak.
- The average squared topological charge ⟨Q²⟩r₀⁴/V = 6.8(2) × 10⁻², determined from the slope of ⟨Q²⟩ versus physical volume V/r₀⁴ over a range from 24 to 330 in units of r₀⁴.
- Monopole density for normal configurations at β = 6.00, V = 14⁴: ρm r₀³ = 0.893(12); with added monopole charge mc = 4 at distance 8: ρm r₀³ = 3.024(15).
- Lattice spacing at β = 6.00 from analytic interpolation: a = 9.315 × 10⁻² fm.

## Context

This work extends the program of Di Giacomo and collaborators (2013, 2014) on monopole creation operators in QCD, and tests the conjecture of Rubakov (1982) that monopoles catalyse instantons. The result that each monopole-antimonopole pair produces one instanton provides a quantitative link between the 3-dimensional topological configurations believed to produce colour confinement (monopoles) and the 4-dimensional configurations responsible for chiral symmetry breaking (instantons). The finding that only net topological charge is observed at small volumes raises questions about the detection of instanton-anti-instanton pairs that will need to be resolved at larger volumes, and the topological susceptibility results are consistent with those of Giusti et al. (2003) and Del Debbio et al. (2002).
