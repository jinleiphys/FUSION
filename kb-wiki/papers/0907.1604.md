# Gluon saturation effects on J/Psi production in heavy ion collisions

> We discuss a novel mechanism for J/Psi$ production in nuclear collisions arising due to the high density of gluons. We demonstrate that gluon saturation in the colliding nuclei is a dominant source of J/Psi suppression and can explain its experimentally observed rapidity and centrality dependence.

# Gluon saturation effects on J/Psi production in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
Gluon saturation in the colliding nuclei is a dominant source of J/Psi suppression in heavy ion collisions, and cold nuclear matter effects alone can account for a significant part of the observed "anomalous" suppression even without invoking hot nuclear medium effects such as quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method
The cross section for J/Psi production in nucleus-nucleus collisions is calculated using the quasi-classical approximation that includes multiple scattering of the c-cbar pair in cold nuclear matter, with the result expressed in terms of the saturation scale Q_s,A^2. The impact parameter dependence of the saturation scales for each nucleus is treated explicitly using the Glauber approximation, and quantum evolution is encoded phenomenologically through the energy/rapidity dependence of the saturation scale following the approach of KLN (2005) and Kharzeev (2004). The overall normalization constant C is fixed by fitting to experimental data, with no other free parameters.

## Key numbers
- Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV: the rapidity distribution of J/Psi becomes narrower as centrality increases, with the most central bin significantly more narrow than the peripheral bin
- The nuclear modification factor shows J/Psi suppression even at y=0, unlike open charm production
- Cold nuclear matter effects account for a significant part of the anomalous J/Psi suppression both at y=0 and y=2

## Context
This work extends the approach previously applied to dAu collisions by Tuchin (2007) (Ref. KT), which used an oversimplified nuclear geometry, to heavy ion collisions with proper treatment of impact parameter dependence. The mechanism relies on the observation that in high-energy nuclear collisions, the three-gluon fusion process is enhanced by 1/alpha_s compared to two-gluon fusion due to the coherent interaction of all A nucleons as a quasi-classical field, following the work of McLerran-Venugopalan (1994), Jalilian-Marian et al. (JIMWLK), and Kovchegov (1996, 1999). The saturation scale values are taken from fits to nuclear reaction multiplicities at RHIC by Kharzeev and Nardi (2001) and Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi (KLN), and from fits to the F2 structure function in DIS by Bartels, Golec-Biernat, and Kowalski (MOD). This work enables a quantitative separation of cold nuclear matter effects from hot medium effects in J/Psi suppression, and suggests that a fair amount (perhaps most) of the observed suppression arises from gluon saturation rather than quark-gluon plasma formation.
