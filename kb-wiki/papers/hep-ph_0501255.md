# Large Angle Hadron Correlations from Medium-Induced Gluon Radiation

> Final state medium-induced gluon radiation in ultradense nuclear matter is examined and shown to favor large angle emission when compared to vacuum bremsstrahlung due to the suppression of collinear gluons. Perturbative expression for the contribution of its hadronic fragments to the back-to-back particle correlations is derived. It is found that in the limit of large jet energy loss gluon radiation determines the yield and angular distribution of | Delta phi | > Pi/2 di-hadrons to transverse momenta pT2 of the associated particles. Clear transition from enhancement to suppression of the away-side hadron correlations is established at moderate pT2 and its experimentally accessible features are predicted versus the trigger particle momentum pT1.

# Large Angle Hadron Correlations from Medium-Induced Gluon Radiation

## Key claim

Medium-induced gluon radiation in ultradense nuclear matter favors large angle emission compared to vacuum bremsstrahlung due to destructive quantum interference that suppresses collinear gluons, and for large jet energy loss this radiation determines the yield and angular distribution of away-side di-hadrons to transverse momenta of associated particles much higher than naively anticipated (up to pT2 ~ 10 GeV), not just below 500 MeV as previously thought.

## Method

The author derives the medium-induced gluon bremsstrahlung spectrum using the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev formalism to first order in the mean number of soft interactions in the quark-gluon plasma, incorporating a (1+1)D Bjorken expanding medium. This spectrum is then embedded into a perturbative QCD factorization approach for double inclusive hadron production, where the away-side jet undergoes fractional energy loss and the radiative gluons fragment into hadrons that contribute to the di-hadron correlation function. The nuclear modification factor R^(2)_AA is computed for central and peripheral Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies.

## Key numbers

- Mean emission angle of medium-induced gluons: ⟨θ*⟩ ~ 0.5 rad for semihard few-GeV gluons from a 6 GeV gluon jet
- Medium parameters: μ_D(z0) = 1.5 GeV, λ_g(z0) = 0.75 fm at initial formation time z0 = 0.25 fm
- Strong coupling constant: α_s = 0.25
- Path lengths: L = 9 fm for central collisions, L = 3 fm for peripheral collisions
- Suppression of gluon emission within a cone of opening angle θ* ≃ 0.25 rad
- Transition from enhancement to suppression of away-side hadron correlations occurs at pT2 ≤ 2 GeV
- Medium-induced gluon radiation dominates di-hadron correlations up to pT2 ~ 10 GeV for large energy loss
- Vacuum di-jet transverse momentum squared: ⟨k_T_vac^2⟩_φ = 5 GeV^2 for scattered quark (2.25 times larger for scattered gluon)
- Trigger hadron momentum range: pT1 = 4-6 GeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of jet quenching and parton energy loss in quark-gluon plasma developed by Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev (2000) and Baier et al. (1999), and directly addresses experimental observations from the STAR collaboration at RHIC on di-hadron correlations (Adler 2002, Rak 2004). Prior transport models by Pal and Pratt (2003) predicted that medium-induced radiation would only affect associated particles below pT2 ≤ 500 MeV, and Salgado and Wiedemann (2003) found jet cone broadening of less than 10% even at LHC energies. This work shows that the medium-induced gluon spectrum produces much larger effects at higher transverse momenta than previously expected, and provides experimentally testable predictions for the shape and magnitude of R^(2)_AA and the enhancement-to-suppression transition versus trigger momentum. The results enable future experimental determination of the differential spectrum of medium-induced non-Abelian bremsstrahlung at RHIC and predict measurable jet broadening at the LHC.
