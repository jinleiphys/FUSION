# Nonrelativistic Effective Field Theory with a Resonance Field

> We discuss shallow resonances in the nonrelativistic scattering of two particles using an effective field theory (EFT) that includes an auxiliary field with the quantum numbers of the resonance. We construct the manifestly renormalized scattering amplitude up to next-to-leading order in a systematic expansion. For a narrow resonance, the amplitude is perturbative except in the immediate vicinity of the resonance poles. It naturally has a zero in the low-energy region, analogous to the Ramsauer-Townsend effect. For a broad resonance, the leading-order amplitude is nonperturbative almost everywhere in the regime of validity of the EFT. We regain the results of an EFT without the auxiliary field, which is equivalent to the effective-range expansion with large scattering length and effective range. We also consider an additional fine tuning leading to a low-energy amplitude zero even for a broad resonance. We show that in all cases the requirement of renormalizability when the auxiliary field is not a ghost ensures the resonance poles are in the lower half of the complex momentum plane, as expected by other arguments. The systematic character of the EFT expansion is exemplified with a toy model serving as underlying theory.

## Key claim

The paper extends effective field theory (EFT) for shallow $s$-wave resonances to cover both broad and narrow resonances using an auxiliary "dimer" field, showing that renormalizability with a non-ghost dimer field forces resonance poles into the lower half of the complex momentum plane, and that a narrow resonance naturally produces a low-energy amplitude zero analogous to the Ramsauer-Townsend effect.

## Method

The authors construct a nonrelativistic Lagrangian with a particle field $\psi$ and an auxiliary dimer field $d$ carrying the resonance quantum numbers, including contact interactions and dimer-particle couplings up to next-to-leading order. They regulate the theory with a momentum cutoff $\Lambda$, renormalize by absorbing cutoff-dependent loop divergences into the bare low-energy constants, and classify interactions according to a power counting that distinguishes broad resonances (where the dimer self-energy must be resummed at leading order) from narrow resonances (where the dimer propagator is perturbative except near the pole). The resulting scattering amplitudes are matched to the effective-range expansion and to a toy model serving as the underlying theory.

## Key numbers

- For a broad resonance at leading order: $k_I^{(0)} = -1/r_0 > 0$ and $k_R^{(0)} = -(1/r_0)\sqrt{2r_0/a_0 - 1} > 0$, valid for $2r_0 < a_0 < 0$, with $|a_0| \sim |r_0| = \mathcal{O}(1/M_{lo})$.
- For a narrow resonance at leading order: the real pole momentum $k_R^{(0)} = k_r > 0$ with $k_r = \mathcal{O}(M_{lo})$, and the amplitude zero occurs at $k = \pm k_0$ with $|k_0| = \mathcal{O}(M_{lo})$.
- For a narrow resonance, the effective range is $r_0 = -(2/a_0 k_r^2)(k_r^2/k_0^2 - 1) < 0$, with $|r_0| = \mathcal{O}(M_{hi}/M_{lo}^2)$ and $|a_0/r_0| = \mathcal{O}(M_{lo}^2/M_{hi}^2)$.
- At next-to-leading order for a broad resonance, the shape parameter $|P_0| = \mathcal{O}(M_{lo}/M_{hi})$ appears in the $k^4$ term of the inverse amplitude.

## Context

This work extends earlier EFT formulations for shallow resonances (Bertulani, 2002; Bedaque, 2003) by systematically including both broad and narrow $s$-wave resonances with a dimer field, and by incorporating the non-resonant background emphasized by Gelman (2009). It recovers the momentum-dependent EFT results of Habashi (2020) for broad resonances, which are equivalent to the effective-range expansion with large scattering length and effective range. The formalism enables model-independent descriptions of shallow $s$-wave resonances in atomic, nuclear, and particle physics systems, including neutron-nucleus scattering and exotic hadronic states near threshold, and can be extended to include Coulomb interactions for proton-nucleus or nucleus-nucleus scattering.
