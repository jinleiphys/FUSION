# The High-Density Symmetry Energy in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The nuclear symmetry energy as a function of density is rather poorly constrained theoretically and experimentally both below saturation density, but particularly at high density, where very few relevant experimental data exist. We discuss observables which could yield information on this question, in particular, proton-neutron flow differences, and the production of pions and kaons in relativistic heavy ion collisions. For the meson production we investigate particularly ratios of the corresponding isospin partners $π^-/π^+$ and $K^0/K^+$, where we find that the kaons are an interesting probe to the symmetry energy. In this case we also discuss the influnece of various choices for the kaon potentials or in-medium effective masses.

## Key claim

The paper argues that kaon yield ratios (K^0/K^+) in relativistic heavy ion collisions are a more promising probe of the high-density symmetry energy than pion ratios (π^-/π^+), because kaons are produced near threshold and thus sample the dense phase where symmetry energy differences are largest, though the effect is reduced in finite nuclei compared to infinite nuclear matter calculations.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic transport simulations with finite-size test particles, propagating protons, neutrons, Δ resonances, pions, and kaons separately. The nucleon self-energies were specified in relativistic mean-field (RMF) models with either ρ mesons only (NLρ) or ρ and δ mesons (NLρδ) to represent different stiffnesses of the symmetry energy. Kaon in-medium properties were modeled using either chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) or one-boson exchange (OBE) models, and the authors compared calculated rapidity distributions and yield ratios of π^-/π^+ and K^0/K^+ for various collision systems and energies.

## Key numbers

- For the collision 132Sn+132Sn at 1.5 AGeV incident energy, the proton-neutron differential directed flow and elliptic flow show appreciable signals that distinguish between the NLρδ (stiffer symmetry energy) and NLρ (softer) models.
- In semicentral Au+Au collisions at 800 AMeV, the calculated π^- and π^+ rapidity distributions are compared to FOPI data, with the yield generally underpredicted but reasonable at mid-rapidity.
- For semicentral Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 AGeV, K^+ rapidity distributions are compared to FOPI and KaoS data, showing that the kaon potential (ChPT vs. OBE) substantially influences the yields.
- The π^-/π^+ ratio in Au+Au collisions as a function of incident energy shows a small effect from different symmetry energy choices (NL, NLρ, NLρδ).
- The K^0/K^+ ratio in Au+Au collisions shows a substantial effect from different symmetry energy choices, with the largest sensitivity at around 1 AGeV incident energy.
- For central Au+Au collisions at 1 AGeV, the K^0/K^+ ratio is not sensitive to an isospin-independent kaon potential, but the isospin-dependent part (particularly in the ChPT model) does influence the ratio.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of the symmetry energy from low-density studies (relevant for exotic nuclei and neutron star crusts) to high densities relevant for neutron star interiors, building on prior reviews by Baran et al. and B.A. Li et al. The paper discusses how the same observables used to constrain the symmetric equation of state (flow, meson production) can be applied to isospin differences, and notes that the FOPI collaboration's double ratio data for Ru+Ru/Zr+Zr showed a smaller effect than predicted in infinite nuclear matter calculations by Ferini et al. The results point toward the need for absolute measurements of kaon ratios in asymmetric systems at future facilities such as FAIR, RIKEN, and FRIB.
