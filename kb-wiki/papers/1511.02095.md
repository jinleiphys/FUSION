# Leading three-baryon forces from SU(3) chiral effective field theory

> Leading three-baryon forces are derived within SU(3) chiral effective field theory. Three classes of irreducible diagrams contribute: three-baryon contact terms, one-meson exchange and two-meson exchange diagrams. We provide the minimal non-relativistic terms of the chiral Lagrangian, that contribute to these diagrams. SU(3) relations are given for the strangeness S=0 and -1 sectors. In the strangeness-zero sector we recover the well-known three-nucleon forces from chiral effective field theory. Explicit expressions for the lambda-nucleon-nucleon chiral potential in isospin space are presented.

## Key claim

The paper derives the leading three-baryon forces (3BF) within SU(3) chiral effective field theory, providing the minimal non-relativistic Lagrangian terms that contribute to these forces. The authors note that while these forces formally appear at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), low-energy constants for three-nucleon forces are unnaturally large and cause effects comparable to NLO, which can be understood via resonance saturation involving the Delta(1232) isobar; in strangeness S=-1 systems, resonances such as the Sigma*(1385) could play a similar role.

## Method

The authors constructed the minimal non-relativistic chiral Lagrangian for six-baryon contact terms, one-meson exchange, and two-meson exchange diagrams, respecting SU(3) flavor symmetry, charge conjugation, parity, and Hermitian conjugation. They derived antisymmetrized potentials by performing all 36 Wick contractions for three-baryon scattering processes and decomposed the results into spin operators and partial waves. SU(3) group-theoretical classification was used to identify which irreducible representations contribute to each three-baryon state, and the potentials were expressed in the isospin basis using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.

## Key numbers

- 18 low-energy constants (c_1 through c_18) appear in the minimal six-baryon contact Lagrangian.
- The chiral dimension nu = 3 for the leading three-baryon diagrams at NNLO.
- Three classes of irreducible diagrams contribute: three-baryon contact terms, one-meson exchange, and two-meson exchange diagrams.
- The SU(3) decomposition of three octet baryons yields representations: 64, 35, 35-bar, 27, 10, 10-bar, 8, and 1, with multiplicities as given in the text.

## Context

This work extends chiral effective field theory three-nucleon forces (Weinberg 1990, 1991, 1992; Van Kolck 1994; Epelbaum 2002) to the strangeness sector, providing a systematic framework for three-baryon forces involving hyperons. It enables consistent few- and many-body calculations of hypernuclei and hypernuclear matter, addressing the need for repulsive Lambda NN forces suggested by phenomenological studies (Bhaduri 1967; Gal 1971, 1972; Bodmer 1988; Usmani 1995; Lonardoni 2013) and by the stiffness constraints from two-solar-mass neutron star observations (Demorest 2010; Antoniadis 2013). The derived SU(3) relations connect the strangeness S=0 and S=-1 sectors, recovering the known three-nucleon forces in the zero-strangeness limit.
