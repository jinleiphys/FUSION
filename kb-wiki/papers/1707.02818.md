# Delocalized SYZ Mirrors and Confronting Top-Down $SU(3)$-Structure Holographic Meson Masses at Finite $g$ and $N_c$ with P(article) D(ata) G(roup) Values

> Meson spectroscopy at finite gauge coupling - whereat any perturbative QCD computation would break down - and finite number of colors, from a top-down holographic string model, has thus far been entirely missing in the literature. This paper fills in this gap. Using the delocalized type IIA SYZ mirror (with $SU(3)$ structure) of the holographic type IIB dual of large-$N$ thermal QCD of arXiv:hep-th/0902.1540 as constructed in arXiv:hep-th/1306.4339 at finite coupling and number of colors ($N_c$ = Number of $D5(\overline{D5}$)-branes wrapping a vanishing two-cycle in the top-down holographic construct of arXiv:hep-th/0902.1540 = ${\cal O}(1)$ in the IR in the MQGP limit of arXiv:hep-th/1306.4339 at the end of a Seiberg duality cascade), we obtain analytical (not just numerical) expressions for the vector and scalar meson spectra and compare our results with previous calculations of hep-th/0412141, arXiv:1409.0559 [hep-th], and obtain a closer match with the Particle Data Group (PDG) results (Chin. Phys. C, 38, 090001 (2014) and 2015 update). Through explicit computations, we verify that the vector and scalar meson spectra obtained by the gravity dual with a black hole for all temperatures (small and large) are nearly isospectral with the spectra obtained by a thermal gravity dual valid for only low temperatures; the isospectrality is much closer for vector mesons than scalar mesons. The black hole gravity dual (with a horizon radius smaller than the deconfinement scale) also provides the expected large-$N$ suppressed decrease in vector meson mass with an increase of temperature.

## Key claim
Using a delocalized type IIA SYZ mirror (with SU(3) structure) of a top-down type IIB holographic dual of large-N thermal QCD at finite gauge coupling and finite number of colors (N_c = O(1) in the IR), the authors obtain analytical expressions for vector and scalar meson spectra that yield a closer match with Particle Data Group (PDG) values than previous calculations in Sakai-Sugimoto (2005) and Dasgupta et al (2010).

## Method
The authors construct the delocalized Strominger-Yau-Zaslow (SYZ) type IIA mirror of the type IIB background of Dasgupta et al (2010) in the MQGP limit, then embed D6-branes as the mirror of D7-branes. They solve the equations of motion for gauge field fluctuations (vector mesons) and transverse fluctuations (scalar mesons) on the D6-brane worldvolume in a black hole gravity dual background, converting the equations to Schrodinger-like forms and applying WKB quantization to obtain analytical meson spectra.

## Key numbers
- Vector meson masses from the black hole gravity dual are nearly isospectral with those from a thermal gravity dual; the isospectrality is much closer for vector mesons than for scalar mesons.
- The black hole gravity dual (with horizon radius smaller than the deconfinement scale) yields a large-N suppressed decrease in vector meson mass with increasing temperature.
- The number of colors N_c in the IR is identified with M, which in the MQGP limit can be tuned to equal 3.
- The length scale in the IR is L_OKS-BH ~ sqrt(M) N_f^(3/4) sqrt( sum_{m,n} N_f^m M^n f_mn(Lambda) ) ( sum_{l,p} N_f^l M^p g_lp(Lambda) )^(1/4) g_s^(1/4) sqrt(alpha'), implying that even for N_c^IR = M = 3 and N_f = 2, L_OKS-BH >> L_KS in the MQGP limit.

## Context
This work extends the top-down holographic approach to meson spectroscopy initiated in Dasgupta et al (2010) and the MQGP limit construction of Misra et al (2013). It improves upon the Sakai-Sugimoto model (2005) and the single T-dual calculation of Dasgupta et al (2010) by working at finite gauge coupling and finite N_c, obtaining analytical spectra that better match PDG values. The results enable further top-down holographic phenomenology of thermal QCD at finite coupling, including potential extensions to baryons and higher-spin mesons.
