# Neutron-3H and Proton-3He Zero Energy Scattering

> The Kohn variational principle and the (correlated) Hyperspherical Harmonics technique are applied to study the n-3H and p-3He scattering at zero energy. Predictions for the singlet and triplet scattering lengths are obtained for non-relativistic nuclear Hamiltonians including two- and three-body potentials. The calculated n-3H total cross section agrees well with the measured value, while some small discrepancy is found for the coherent scattering length. For the p-3He channel, the calculated scattering lengths are in reasonable agreement with the values extrapolated from the measurements made above 1 MeV.

# Neutron-3H and Proton-3He Zero Energy Scattering

## Key claim
The Kohn variational principle combined with the correlated Hyperspherical Harmonics technique yields accurate predictions for n-3H and p-3He zero-energy scattering lengths using realistic nuclear Hamiltonians, but the calculated coherent scattering length for n-3H shows a small discrepancy (about 3%) with experimental values, while the total cross section agrees well with measurement.

## Method
The wave functions of the four-nucleon scattering states were expanded in a correlated Hyperspherical Harmonics (CHH) basis, with the internal part describing the core region and an asymptotic part describing the separated n-3H or p-3He configuration. The Kohn and Hulthén variational principles were applied to determine the reactance matrix elements and scattering lengths, using both two-body potentials (AV14, AV8, AV18) and combinations with three-body potentials (Urbana VIII, Urbana IX, Brazil models). Convergence was studied by systematically increasing the number of channels included in the wave function expansion.

## Key numbers
- n-3H total cross section at zero energy: σ_T = 1.70 ± 0.03 b (experimental value from Phillips (1980))
- n-3H coherent scattering length: a_c = 3.82 ± 0.07 fm (Rauch 1981), a_c = 3.59 ± 0.02 fm (Rauch 1985), a_c = 3.607 ± 0.017 fm (Hale 1990 from p-3He data)
- Calculated coherent scattering lengths for n-3H differ from experimental values by about 3%
- 3H binding energy B_3 = 8.48 MeV (experimental value used to parameterize three-body potentials)
- Number of channels required for convergence in S-wave, T=1 states: N_c ≈ 6 to 8

## Context
This work extends the application of the CHH technique, previously successful for N-d scattering (Kievsky, Rosati, Viviani 1994), to the four-nucleon scattering problem. It provides a direct comparison with Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations by Carbonell (1997) using the AV14 potential, showing excellent agreement. The results enable testing of NN and 3N interaction models in the A=4 system, where previous accurate calculations of the alpha particle binding energy (Pieper, Pandharipande, Carlson, Wiringa 1997; Varga 1997; Kievsky, Rosati, Viviani 1995) had shown that no four-nucleon potential appears necessary when three-body forces are included. The small discrepancy in the coherent scattering length is surprising given that the corresponding quantity in N-d scattering is well reproduced, and motivates further experimental and theoretical work on low-energy p-3He and n-3H scattering.
