# Elastic scatering cross-sections obtained on the basis of the potential of the modified Thomas-Fermi method and taking the core into account

> Nucleon density distributions and nucleus-nucleus interaction potentials for the reactions $^{16}$O+$^{40}$Ca, $^{16}$O+$^{56}$Fe and $^{16}$O+$^{90}$Zr have been calculated in the framework of the modified Thomas-Fermi method and considering all terms up to the second order in $\hbar$ in the quasi-classical expansion of the kinetic energy. Skyrme forces dependent on the nucleon density are used as the nucleon-nucleon interaction. A parametrization of the nucleus-nucleus interaction potential, which well describes the potential value calculated within the modified Thomas-Fermi approach with density-dependent Skyrme forces, is found. On the basis of the obtained potentials, the cross-sections of elastic scattering are calculated in a good agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nucleus-nucleus interaction potentials calculated using the modified Thomas-Fermi method with Skyrme forces (SkM* parametrization), which include a repulsive core at short distances, can successfully describe elastic scattering cross-sections for several oxygen-induced reactions, provided the imaginary part of the optical potential is fitted to experimental data.

## Method

Nucleon density distributions for 16O, 40Ca, 56Fe, and 90Zr were first calculated using the modified Thomas-Fermi method with Skyrme SkM* forces, including all terms up to second order in hbar in the semiclassical expansion of kinetic energy. The nucleus-nucleus interaction potentials for the reactions 16O+40Ca, 16O+56Fe, and 16O+90Zr were then obtained as the difference between the energy of the two-nucleus system at finite separation and at infinite separation, using the frozen-density approximation. A six-parameter analytic form combining a Woods-Saxon term and a kinetic-energy-like term (proportional to density to the 5/3 power) was fitted to the calculated nuclear potentials. Elastic scattering cross-sections were computed in the optical model using this analytic form as the real potential and a standard Woods-Saxon plus derivative Woods-Saxon form for the imaginary part, with the imaginary parameters fitted to experimental data.

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the modified Thomas-Fermi approach with Skyrme forces (Denisov and collaborators, various years) by applying the resulting potentials with repulsive cores to describe elastic scattering, which had rarely been done for heavy-ion systems. The repulsive core at short distances, associated with nuclear matter incompressibility, has been observed in proximity potentials (Blocki et al., 1977) and microscopic approaches, and has been used to describe deep subbarrier fusion hindrance (various authors, 2000s). The present results enable systematic optical-model analyses of elastic scattering using microscopically-motivated real potentials that include the repulsive core, rather than purely phenomenological Woods-Saxon forms.
