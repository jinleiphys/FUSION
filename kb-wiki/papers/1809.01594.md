# Strangeness neutrality and baryon-strangeness correlations

> We derive a simple relation between strangeness neutrality and baryon-strangeness correlations. In heavy-ion collisions, the former is a consequence of quark number conservation of the strong interactions while the latter are sensitive probes of the character of QCD matter. This relation allows us to directly extract baryon-strangeness correlations from the strangeness chemical potential at strangeness neutrality. The explicit calculations are performed within a low energy theory of QCD with 2+1 dynamical quark flavors at finite temperature and density. Non-perturbative quark and hadron fluctuations are taken into account within the functional renormalization group. The results show the pronounced sensitivity of baryon-strangeness correlations on the QCD phase transition and the crucial role that strangeness neutrality plays for this observable.

## Key claim

The authors derive an exact relation showing that the slope of the strangeness chemical potential at strangeness neutrality with respect to baryon chemical potential equals one-third of the baryon-strangeness correlation, enabling direct extraction of this correlation from the strangeness chemical potential; this relation holds for any temperature and baryon chemical potential, not just in an expansion about zero baryon chemical potential.

## Method

The authors use a Polyakov loop enhanced quark-meson model (PQM) with 2+1 dynamical quark flavors as a low-energy effective theory of QCD, incorporating non-perturbative quantum, thermal, and density fluctuations via the functional renormalization group (FRG). They solve the FRG flow equation for the effective action, obtain the pressure from the full quantum effective action, compute the strangeness number as a function of temperature, baryon chemical potential, and strangeness chemical potential, and determine the strangeness chemical potential that enforces strangeness neutrality. The baryon-strangeness correlation is then extracted from the slope of this strangeness chemical potential using the derived relation.

## Key numbers

* At vanishing baryon chemical potential, the baryon-strangeness correlation from the FRG calculation agrees with lattice QCD results from Bazavov (2013, 2014, 2017, 2018).
* The strangeness chemical potential at strangeness neutrality is computed for baryon chemical potentials up to 675 MeV, with temperatures ranging from 100 MeV to 250 MeV.
* The pseudocritical deconfinement temperature in the model is approximately 155 MeV.
* At baryon chemical potentials above approximately 420 MeV, the baryon-strangeness correlation develops a non-monotonic temperature dependence with a pronounced maximum in the crossover region.
* The initial cutoff scale for the FRG flow is Lambda = 900 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of strangeness neutrality effects on QCD phase structure and thermodynamics (Fu 2018) by establishing a direct connection to baryon-strangeness correlations introduced by Koch (2005) as a probe of QCD matter composition. The relation generalizes lattice QCD freeze-out conditions used by Bazavov (2012), Borsanyi (2013), and Bazavov (2017) from expansions about zero baryon chemical potential to arbitrary temperature and baryon chemical potential. The results enable experimental extraction of the strangeness chemical potential at freeze-out by integrating measured baryon-strangeness correlations over beam energy, and predict enhanced baryon-strangeness correlations at small beam energies that would indicate a sharp crossover transition.
