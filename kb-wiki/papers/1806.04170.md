# Hadron-quark phase transition: the QCD phase diagram and stellar conversion

> Different extensions of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, known to satisfy expected QCD chiral symmetry aspects, are used to investigate a possible hadron-quark phase transition at zero temperature and to build the corresponding binodal sections. We have shown that the transition point is very sensitive to the model parameters and that both pressure and chemical potential increase drastically with the increase of the vector interaction strength in the quark sector. Within the same framework, the possibility of quark and hybrid star formation is analyzed. The same conclusions drawn before with respect to the coexistence pressure and chemical potentials are reinforced. We conclude that even if a transition from a metastable hadronic star to a quark star is thermodinamically possible, it is either energetically forbidden or gives rise to a blackhole. Nevertheless, conversions from metastable to hybrid stars are possible, but the mass difference between both compact objects is very small, never larger than 0.2 M$_\odot$.

## Key claim
The paper shows that a hadron-quark phase transition at zero temperature is possible only for specific combinations of NJL-model parameter sets, and that even when thermodynamically allowed, the conversion of a metastable hadronic star to a pure quark star is either energetically forbidden or produces a black hole, while conversions to hybrid stars yield mass differences never exceeding 0.2 solar masses.

## Method
The authors used the PPM NJL model for hadronic matter and the SU(2) and SU(3) versions of the NJL model for quark matter, all incorporating chiral symmetry. They constructed binodal sections at zero temperature by applying Gibbs conditions (equal chemical potentials, temperature, and pressure) to find coexistence points for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter. For stellar applications, they imposed beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality to obtain equations of state for hadronic, hybrid, and quark stars, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to determine stellar structure and stability.

## Key numbers
- For symmetric matter, the coexistence chemical potential ranges from 1312 MeV (PCP-0.0 with eNJL2mσρ1) to 1787 MeV (PCP-0.2 with eNJL3σρ1)
- Coexistence pressure ranges from 158 MeV/fm³ (PCP-0.0 with eNJL2mσρ1) to 586 MeV/fm³ (BuballaR-2 with eNJL2mσρ1)
- Coexistence baryonic density in the hadronic phase ranges from 0.506 fm⁻³ (PCP-0.0 with eNJL2mσρ1) to 1.497 fm⁻³ (BuballaR-2 with eNJL2mσρ1)
- Chiral symmetry restoration in the hadronic phase occurs at ρ_B = 1.0 fm⁻³ for both eNJL3σρ1 (μ_B = 1674 MeV) and eNJL2mσρ1 (μ_B = 1568 MeV)
- The mass difference between metastable hadronic stars and hybrid stars is never larger than 0.2 M_⊙

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the hadron-quark phase transition by Cavagnoli (2011) and Marquez (2017), who used the non-linear Walecka model for hadrons and the MIT bag model for quarks, and by Tsue (2010) and Lee (2013), who used NJL-type models for both phases. The novelty is the use of the PPM NJL model for hadronic matter, which includes additional interaction channels to better describe nuclear bulk properties as developed by Pais (2016). The results enable future inclusion of finite temperature and magnetic field effects in the QCD phase diagram, and provide constraints for interpreting observations from NICER, Athena, and gravitational wave detections of compact star mergers.
