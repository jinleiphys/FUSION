# $I-$Love$-C$ relation for an anisotropic neutron star

> One of the most common assumptions has been made that the pressure inside the star is isotropic in nature. However, the pressure is locally anisotropic in nature which is a more realistic case. In this study, we investigate certain properties of anisotropic neutron stars with the scalar pressure anisotropy model. Different perfect fluid conditions are tested within the star with the relativistic mean-field model equation of states (EOSs). The anisotropic neutron star properties such as mass ($M$), radius ($R$), compactness ($C$), Love number ($k_2$), dimensionless tidal deformability ($Λ$), and the moment of inertia ($I$) are calculated. The magnitude of the quantities as mentioned above increases (decreases) with the positive (negative) value of anisotropy except $k_2$ and $Λ$. The Universal relation $I-$Love$-C$ is calculated with almost 58 EOSs spans from relativistic to non-relativistic cases. We observed that the relations between them get weaker when we include anisotropicity. With the help of the GW170817 tidal deformability limit and radii constraints from different approaches, we find that the anisotropic parameter is less than 1.0 if one uses the BL model. Using the universal relation and the tidal deformability bound given by the GW170817, we put a theoretical limit for the canonical radius, $R_{1.4}=10.74_{-1.36}^{+1.84}$ km, and the moment of inertia, $I_{1.4} = 1.77_{-0.09}^{+0.17}\times10^{45}$ g cm$^2$ with 90\% confidence limit for isotropic stars. Similarly, for anisotropic stars with $λ_{\rm BL}=1.0$, the values are $R_{1.4}=11.74_{-1.54}^{+2.11}$ km, $I_{1.4} = 2.40_{-0.08}^{+0.17} \times10^{45}$ g cm$^2$ respectively.

## Key claim

The universal I-Love-C relations, which are nearly equation-of-state independent for isotropic neutron stars, become weaker (larger scatter) when pressure anisotropy is included via the Bowers-Liang model; the anisotropic parameter λ_BL is constrained to be less than 1.0 by the GW170817 tidal deformability limit and radii constraints, but the paper does not claim that anisotropy is required by the data.

## Method

The author solves the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static, spherically symmetric neutron stars with the Bowers-Liang (BL) scalar pressure anisotropy model, using 58 equations of state from relativistic mean-field, Skyrme-Hartree-Fock, and density-dependent models. The moment of inertia is computed via the Hartle-Thorne slow-rotation approximation, and the tidal Love number k2 and dimensionless tidal deformability Λ are obtained from linear metric perturbations matched at the stellar surface. The I-Love-C relations are fitted with polynomial functions for λ_BL = 0, 1, and 2, and the residuals and reduced chi-squared values quantify the weakening of universality.

## Key numbers

- Canonical radius for isotropic stars: R_1.4 = 10.74_{-1.36}^{+1.84} km (90% confidence limit, from GW170817 tidal deformability bound)
- Canonical moment of inertia for isotropic stars: I_1.4 = 1.77_{-0.09}^{+0.17} × 10^45 g cm^2 (90% confidence limit)
- Canonical radius for anisotropic stars with λ_BL = 1.0: R_1.4 = 11.74_{-1.54}^{+2.11} km (90% confidence limit)
- Canonical moment of inertia for anisotropic stars with λ_BL = 1.0: I_1.4 = 2.40_{-0.08}^{+0.17} × 10^45 g cm^2 (90% confidence limit)
- Fitting coefficients for I-Lambda relation at λ_BL = 0: a_0 = 7.5026 × 10^{-1}, a_1 = 4.1857 × 10^{-2}, a_2 = 8.0495 × 10^{-2}, a_3 = -8.9478 × 10^{-3}, a_4 = 5.4767 × 10^{-4}; reduced chi-squared χ_r^2 = 0.4133 × 10^{-5}
- Fitting coefficients for I-Lambda relation at λ_BL = 2.0: a_0 = 8.3145 × 10^{-1}, a_1 = 5.6152 × 10^{-2}, a_2 = 1.11359 × 10^{-1}, a_3 = -1.81670 × 10^{-2}, a_4 = 1.44628 × 10^{-3}; reduced chi-squared χ_r^2 = 4.5281 × 10^{-5}
- Canonical compactness for isotropic stars from GW170817: C_1.4 = 0.192_{-0.03}^{+0.03}
- Canonical compactness for anisotropic stars with λ_BL = 1.0 from GW170817: C_1.4 = 0.177_{-0.03}^{+0.03}

## Context

This work extends the universal I-Love-C relations originally discovered by Yagi and Yunes (2013) for isotropic neutron stars to the case of anisotropic pressure using the Bowers-Liang model, following earlier studies of anisotropic stars by Silva et al. (2015) and Biswas and Bose (2019). The results enable constraints on the degree of pressure anisotropy from multimessenger observations (GW170817, NICER) and provide theoretical limits on the radius and moment of inertia for both isotropic and anisotropic canonical neutron stars, which can be tested by future gravitational-wave and x-ray observations.
