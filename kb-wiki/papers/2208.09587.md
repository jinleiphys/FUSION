# Determination of asymptotic normalization coefficients for the channel $^{16}$O$\to α+^{12}$C. Excited state $^{16}$O($0^+; 6.05$ MeV)

> Asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANC) determine the overall normalization of cross sections of peripheral radiative capture reactions. In the present paper, we treat the ANC $C$ for the virtual decay $^{16}$O$(0^+; 6.05$ MeV)$\to α+^{12}$C(g.s.), the known values of which are characterized by a large spread $(0.29-1.65)\times 10^3$ fm$^{-1/2}$. The ANC $C$ is found by analytic continuation in the energy of the $α^{12}$C $s$-wave scattering amplitude, known from the phase-shift analysis of experimental data, to the pole corresponding to the $^{16}$O bound state and lying in the unphysical region of negative energies. To determine $C$, two different methods of analytic continuation are used. In the first method, the scattering data are approximated by the sum of polynomials in energy in the physical region and then extrapolated to the pole. The best way of extrapolation is chosen on the basis of the exactly solvable model. Within the second approach, the ANC $C$ is found by solving the Schrödinger equation for the two-body $α^{12}$C potential, the parameters of which are selected from the requirement of the best description of the phase-shift analysis data at a fixed experimental binding energy of $^{16}$O$(0^+; 6.05$ MeV) in the $α+^{12}$C channel. The values of the ANC $C$ obtained within these two methods lie in the interval (886--1139) fm$^{-1/2}$.

# Determination of asymptotic normalization coefficients for the channel $^{16}$O$\to \alpha+^{12}$C. Excited state $^{16}$O($0^+; 6.05$ MeV)

## Key claim

The asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for the virtual decay $^{16}$O$(0^+; 6.05$ MeV)$\to \alpha+^{12}$C(g.s.) is determined to lie in the interval (886--1139) fm$^{-1/2}$, significantly narrowing the previously reported spread of $(0.29-1.65)\times 10^3$ fm$^{-1/2}$, but the result depends on the choice of energy range used for fitting and the method of analytic continuation.

## Method

The ANC $C$ is extracted by analytic continuation in energy of the $\alpha^{12}$C $s$-wave scattering amplitude, using phase-shift analysis data from Tischhauser (2009) in the laboratory energy range 2.607--6.620 MeV. Two independent methods are employed: (1) polynomial approximation of the $\Delta_0(E)$ function (the real part of the denominator of the Coulomb-modified amplitude) in the physical region followed by extrapolation to the bound-state pole at $E=-\varepsilon$, with the best extrapolation form chosen via an exactly solvable square-well model; (2) solving the Schr\"odinger equation for a square-well nuclear potential plus Coulomb interaction, with potential parameters fitted to reproduce the experimental binding energy $\varepsilon=1.113$ MeV and the phase-shift data.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the virtual decay: $\varepsilon = 1.113$ MeV.
- Previously reported ANC values span $(0.29-1.65)\times 10^3$ fm$^{-1/2}$.
- ANC from the polynomial continuation method (Version 3, narrow energy range): $C = 1139$ fm$^{-1/2}$.
- ANC from the square-well potential method (narrow energy range, two bound states): $C = 938$ fm$^{-1/2}$ (best $\chi^2 = 0.7756$).
- ANC from the square-well potential method (narrow energy range, three bound states): $C = 886$ fm$^{-1/2}$.
- Combined interval from all methods using narrow energy range: (886--1139) fm$^{-1/2}$.
- Square-well potential parameters for the best fit (narrow range, two bound states): $V_0 = 22.7495$ MeV, $R = 4.16411$ fm.
- For the wide energy range, the square-well method gives a lower limit of $C = 734$ fm$^{-1/2}$ ($\chi^2 = 175.6$).

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing discrepancy in the ANC for the $^{16}$O$(0^+; 6.05$ MeV)$\to \alpha+^{12}$C channel, where prior values from Avila (2015), Ando (2001), Orlov (2015), and Orlov (2016) ranged from $0.293\times 10^3$ to $1.560\times 10^3$ fm$^{-1/2}$. The authors demonstrate that the $\Delta$-method, previously criticized for having an essential singularity at $E=0$, is actually valid because the singularity cancels in the full denominator of the Coulomb-modified amplitude. The obtained ANC directly normalizes the astrophysical $S$-factor for the $^{12}$C$(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}$O$(0^+; 6.05$ MeV) radiative capture reaction, which contributes to the $^{12}$C/$^{16}$O abundance ratio during stellar helium burning. The authors note that the method cannot be applied to the $^{16}$O ground state because multiple bound states with the same quantum numbers prevent reliable extrapolation.
