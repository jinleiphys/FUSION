# $ϕ$ meson production and partonic collectivity at RHIC

> New results on $ϕ$-meson production and elliptic flow $v_{2}$ measurements from RHIC 2004 run (Run-IV) have been reviewed. In addition, the di-hadron correlation function between the trigged $ϕ$ and $Ω$ and the associated soft particles was simulated. Knowledge about these results are discussed.

# Wiki Page: φ Meson Production and Partonic Collectivity at RHIC

## Key claim

The paper reviews evidence that φ-meson elliptic flow (v₂) at RHIC is comparable to that of lighter mesons like kaons, which, combined with the ruling out of kaon coalescence as the dominant production mechanism, indicates that strange quarks themselves flow and that partonic collectivity is established before hadronization. The claim is hedged by noting that the φ-meson v₂ results are preliminary and that the di-hadron correlation simulations are from the AMPT model, not yet confirmed experimentally.

## Method

The paper reviews STAR and PHENIX measurements of φ-meson transverse momentum spectra, nuclear modification factor (R_CP), and elliptic flow (v₂) from Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV from the RHIC 2004 run (Run-IV). Additionally, the author presents AMPT model simulations of di-hadron azimuthal correlations triggered by high-p_T φ or Ω mesons and associated soft hadrons, using the mixed-event technique to extract correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- φ-meson lifetime: ~41 fm/c (stated as relatively long)
- φ-meson p_T spectra measured for Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV across multiple centrality bins; central data fitted equally well by exponential and Levy functions, while peripheral spectra are better described by Levy function
- R_CP comparison shows baryon (Λ) R_CP larger than meson (K_s⁰ and φ) R_CP at intermediate p_T, confirming baryon-meson grouping rather than mass dependence
- φ-meson v₂ is non-zero in the hydrodynamic region (p_T < 2 GeV/c) and significant at p_T > 2 GeV/c, comparable to K⁺+K⁻ v₂
- Di-hadron correlation simulations use trigger particles with 3 < p_T < 10 GeV/c for φ/Ω and 3 < p_T < 6 GeV/c for any hadrons, with associated particles in 0.15 < p_T < 3 GeV/c
- For 10-20% centrality, a clean split of away-side peaks (Mach-like cone) is observed in AMPT simulations; for peripheral collisions (40-90% centrality), the Mach-like cone vanishes and only a mono peak remains

## Context

This work extends the STAR measurements of φ-meson production from the 2002 run (STAR-phi, 2002) which ruled out kaon coalescence as the dominant source, and builds on the observation of baryon-meson grouping in R_CP and v₂ at intermediate p_T (STAR-K0L, 2004). The results support the partonic recombination/coalescence models (REC1, REC2, REC3, 2003-2004) and the AMPT model with string melting (AMPT, 2005). The paper enables future experimental searches for differences in di-hadron correlations between φ/Ω-triggered and normal hadron-triggered events, and motivates the planned STAR Time-of-Flight detector upgrade for improved φ-meson identification via the di-electron decay channel.
