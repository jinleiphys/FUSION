# Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering Parameters in the Limit of SU(3) Flavor Symmetry

> The scattering lengths and effective ranges that describe low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering are calculated in the limit of SU(3)-flavor symmetry at the physical strange-quark mass with Lattice Quantum Chromodynamics. The calculations are performed with an isotropic clover discretization of the quark action in three volumes with spatial extents of L \sim 3.4 fm, 4.5fm and 6.7 fm, and with a lattice spacing of b \sim 0.145 fm. With determinations of the energies of the two-nucleon systems (both of which contain bound states at these up and down quark masses) at rest and moving in the lattice volume, Luscher's method is used to determine the low-energy phase shifts in each channel, from which the scattering length and effective range are obtained. The scattering parameters, in the 1S0 channel are found to be m_pi a^(1S0) = 9.50^{+0.78}_{-0.69}^{+1.10}_{-0.80} and m_pi r^(1S0) = {4.61^{+0.29}_{-0.31}^{+0.24}_{-0.26}, and in the 3S1 channel are m_pi a^(3S1) = 7.45^{+0.57}_{-0.53}^{+0.71}_{-0.49} and m_pi r^(3S1) = 3.71^{+0.28}_{-0.31}^{+0.28}_{-0.35}. These values are consistent with the two-nucleon system exhibiting Wigner's supermultiplet symmetry, which becomes exact in the limit of large-N_c. In both spin channels, the phase shifts change sign at higher momentum, near the start of the t-channel cut, indicating that the nuclear interactions have a repulsive core even at the SU(3)-symmetric point.

## Key claim
The scattering lengths and effective ranges in both the 1S0 and 3S1 channels at the SU(3)-flavor symmetric point (with pion mass ~806 MeV) are consistent with the two-nucleon system exhibiting Wigner's supermultiplet symmetry, which becomes exact in the limit of large N_c, though the calculations are performed at a single lattice spacing and at heavier-than-physical quark masses.

## Method
The authors used Lattice Quantum Chromodynamics with an isotropic clover discretization of the quark action on three ensembles of gauge-field configurations with spatial extents of 3.4 fm, 4.5 fm, and 6.7 fm, and a lattice spacing of approximately 0.145 fm. Energies of two-nucleon systems at rest and with nonzero total momentum were determined from correlation functions, and Luscher's method was used to extract low-energy phase shifts, from which the scattering length and effective range were obtained via the effective range expansion.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass at the SU(3)-symmetric point: 805.9(0.6)(0.4)(8.9) MeV
- Di-neutron binding energy in the 1S0 channel: 15.9(2.7)(2.7)(0.2) MeV
- Deuteron binding energy in the 3S1 channel: 19.5(3.6)(3.1)(0.2) MeV
- 1S0 channel scattering length: m_pi a^(1S0) = 9.50^{+0.78}_{-0.69}^{+1.10}_{-0.80} (dimensionless)
- 1S0 channel effective range: m_pi r^(1S0) = 4.61^{+0.29}_{-0.31}^{+0.24}_{-0.26} (dimensionless)
- 3S1 channel scattering length: m_pi a^(3S1) = 7.45^{+0.57}_{-0.53}^{+0.71}_{-0.49} (dimensionless)
- 3S1 channel effective range: m_pi r^(3S1) = 3.71^{+0.28}_{-0.31}^{+0.28}_{-0.35} (dimensionless)
- 1S0 channel scattering length in physical units: 2.33^{+0.19}_{-0.17}^{+0.27}_{-0.20} fm
- 3S1 channel scattering length in physical units: 1.82^{+0.14}_{-0.13}^{+0.17}_{-0.12} fm

## Context
This work extends the NPLQCD Collaboration's previous Lattice QCD calculations of s-shell nuclei and hypernuclei at the SU(3)-flavor symmetric point (Beane 2012) by extracting the full scattering parameters in the nucleon-nucleon channels. The results are consistent with Wigner's supermultiplet symmetry and provide a benchmark for understanding how nuclear fine-tunings depend on the light-quark masses. The calculations disagree with the HALQCD collaboration's results (Inoue 2011), which did not find bound states at this pion mass using a different method that the authors argue is theoretically unsound. These results enable future studies of the light-quark mass dependence of nuclear forces and constraints on fundamental parameters of the standard model.
