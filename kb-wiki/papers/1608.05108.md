# Examination of strangeness instabilities and effects of strange meson couplings in dense strange hadronic matter and compact stars

> Background : The emergence of hyperon degrees of freedom in neutron star matter has been associated to first order phase transitions in some phenomenological models, but conclusions on the possible physical existence of an instability in the strangeness sector are strongly model dependent. Purpose : The purpose of the present study is to assess whether strangeness instabilities are related to specific values of the largely unconstrained hyperon interactions, and to study the effect of the strange meson couplings on phenomenological properties of neutron stars and supernova matter, once these latter are fixed to fulfill the constraints imposed by hypernuclear data. Method : We consider a phenomenological RMF model sufficiently simple to allow a complete exploration of the parameter space. Results : We show that no instability at supersaturation density exists for the RMF model, as long as the parameter space is constrained by basic physical requirements. This is at variance with a non-relativistic functional, with a functional behavior fitted through ab-initio calculations. Once the study is extended to include the full octet, we show that the parameter space allows reasonable radii for canonical neutron stars as well as massive stars above two-solar mass, together with an important strangeness content of the order of 30\%, slightly decreasing with increasing entropy, even in the absence of a strangeness driven phase transition. Conclusions : We conclude that the hyperon content of neutron stars and supernova matter cannot be established with present constraints, and is essentially governed by the unconstrained coupling to the strange isoscalar meson.

## Key claim

The authors show that, within a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model constrained by hypernuclear data, no strangeness-driven instability (first-order phase transition) exists at supersaturation densities, contrary to what is observed with a non-relativistic Skyrme functional fitted to ab-initio calculations; the hyperon content of neutron stars and supernova matter remains essentially governed by the unconstrained coupling to the strange isoscalar meson.

## Method

The authors use a phenomenological RMF model with nonlinear scalar terms (GM1 parametrization) and include the full baryon octet plus leptons, allowing the couplings \(\chi_{\sigma\Lambda}\) and \(\chi_{\sigma^{\ast}\Lambda}\) to vary freely while fixing \(\chi_{\omega\Lambda}\) and \(\chi_{\phi\Lambda}\) from the empirical constraints \(U^N_\Lambda(n_0) = -28\) MeV and \(U^\Lambda_\Lambda(n_0/5) = -0.67\) MeV. They diagonalize the curvature matrix of the energy density in the \((n_B, n_S, n_L)\) space to search for instabilities, and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star structure. For comparison, they also analyze the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) model fitted to ab-initio calculations.

## Key numbers

* The \(\Lambda\) potential in nuclear matter is fixed to \(U^N_\Lambda(n_0) = -28\) MeV at nuclear saturation density \(n_0\).
* The \(\Lambda\)-\(\Lambda\) potential is fixed to \(U^\Lambda_\Lambda(n_0/5) = -0.67\) MeV at one fifth of nuclear saturation density.
* For \(\chi_{\sigma\Lambda} = 0.70\), the maximum neutron star mass reaches \(2.08\,M_\odot\) with a central \(\Lambda\) fraction \(Y_\Lambda \approx 0.52\)–\(0.55\).
* For \(\chi_{\sigma\Lambda} = 1.00\), the maximum mass is \(2.35\)–\(2.36\,M_\odot\) with a negligible \(\Lambda\) fraction (\(Y_\Lambda \approx 0.03\)).
* For \(\chi_{\sigma\Lambda} = 0.50\), the maximum mass is \(1.68\)–\(1.69\,M_\odot\) with \(Y_\Lambda \approx 0.63\)–\(0.64\).
* The strangeness content in neutron star matter can be of the order of 30%, slightly decreasing with increasing entropy.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Oertel et al. (2015) by systematically exploring the RMF parameter space for strangeness instabilities, and contrasts with the non-relativistic Skyrme functionals of Fran02, Fran04, and Fran05 that predicted first-order phase transitions at supersaturation densities. The results show that, within RMF, no such instability appears when physical constraints are applied, but the AFDMC ab-initio model (Lonardoni et al., 2014) yields a different behavior, indicating that the hyperon content of compact stars cannot be settled with current constraints. The study enables future work to focus on constraining the strange meson couplings from hypernuclear data and to explore quark-level phase transitions.
