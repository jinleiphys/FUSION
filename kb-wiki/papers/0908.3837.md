# Dynamical Model of Coherent Electroweak Pion Production Reactions on Nuclei

> We have developed a dynamical model for a unified description of the pion-nucleus scattering and photo- and neutrino-induced coherent pion production on nuclei. Our approach is based on a combined use of the Sato-Lee model for the electroweak pion production on a single nucleon and the Delta-hole model of pion-nucleus scattering. Numerical calculations are carried out for the case of the C12 target. After testing our model with the use of the pion photo-production data, we confront our predictions of the neutrino-induced coherent pion production reactions with the recent data from K2K and MiniBooNE.

## Key claim
The authors present a dynamical model that unifies pion-nucleus scattering with photo- and neutrino-induced coherent pion production on nuclei, but the model's predictions for neutrino-induced reactions are tested only against existing K2K and MiniBooNE data after first validating against pion photo-production data, meaning the model's reliability for neutrino processes depends on the consistency of this multi-step validation.

## Method
The approach combines the Sato-Lee (SL) model for elementary electroweak pion production on a single nucleon with the Delta-hole model for pion-nucleus scattering. The on- and off-shell pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes from the SL model are used to construct a pion-nucleus optical potential within the Delta-hole model, with four free parameters (two complex couplings for s- and p-wave absorption, and two parameters for the spreading potential) determined by fitting to pion-nucleus scattering data. The coherent pion production amplitudes are then calculated by convoluting the nuclear amplitude, which includes medium modifications to the Delta propagator (including non-local effects), with pion scattering wave functions obtained from the optical potential.

## Key numbers
- Total cross sections for pi- - C12 scattering are calculated and shown in a figure (solid curve), though the specific numerical values from the figure are not provided in the truncated text.
- The model uses four free parameters: two complex couplings (c_s and c_p) for the phenomenological density-squared term describing s- and p-wave pion absorption by nucleon pairs, and two parameters specifying the strength of the spreading potential (Sigma_spr).
- Numerical calculations are carried out for the C12 target.

## Context
This work extends the Sato-Lee model (Sato and Lee, no year given) for single-nucleon electroweak pion production to nuclear targets by incorporating the Delta-hole model of pion-nucleus scattering (Karaoglu, no year given). It addresses the discrepancy between K2K and SciBooNE experiments (which reported no evidence of charged-current coherent pion production) and MiniBooNE (which found a signal for neutral-current coherent pion production), where isospin considerations predict sigma_CC approximately 2 sigma_NC. The model enables parameter-free predictions for coherent electroweak pion production after fixing all parameters from pion-nucleus scattering data, and it is the first calculation to include non-local effects on in-medium Delta propagation for neutrino-induced coherent pion production.
