# Variational Monte-carlo study of ${_{ΛΛ}^4}H$

> Variational Monte-Carlo calculations are carried out for ${_{ΛΛ}^4}H$ to explore on the possibility of its existence, using realistic NN, NNN, and phenomenological ΛN and ΛNN interactions. We also perform calculations using FGNN and NSC97 ΛN potentials.We have demonstrated the effect of the exchange part of the two-body ΛN interaction and three-body ΛNN interaction on the binding energy of . Still the stability of ${_{ΛΛ}^ 4} H$ remains an open question and this hypernuclear system has to be explored more, theoretically.

# Variational Monte-Carlo Study of \( {_{\Lambda\Lambda}^4 H} \)

## Key claim
The stability of \( {_{\Lambda\Lambda}^4 H} \) depends sensitively on the space-exchange part of the two-body \( \Lambda N \) interaction and on the three-body \( \Lambda NN \) interaction; with certain interaction models the system appears bound, but when the space-exchange parameter is set to 0.2 (consistent with low-energy \( \Lambda p \) scattering data) and realistic \( \Lambda NN \) forces are included, the system becomes unbound relative to \( {_{\Lambda}^3 H} \).

## Method
Variational Monte-Carlo calculations were performed for the four-body \( \Lambda\Lambda p n \) system using Argonne \( V_{18} \) NN and Urbana IX NNN potentials, together with phenomenological \( \Lambda N \) potentials (including central, Majorana space-exchange, and spin-spin components) and \( \Lambda NN \) potentials (two-pion exchange and dispersive parts). The \( \Lambda\Lambda \) interaction was a soft-core potential fitted to Nijmegen models, with strength parameter \( \gamma \) varied to represent NSC97e, ND, and NEC00 interactions. The variational wave function included two-body, three-body, and spin-orbit correlations, with cosine corrections to the Jastrow functions.

## Key numbers
- For the \( \Lambda N1 \) potential (space-exchange parameter \( \epsilon = 0.2 \)), \( B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 0.38(03) \) MeV for \( {_{\Lambda\Lambda}^4 H} \), but the system is unstable with respect to \( {_{\Lambda}^3 H} \) (energy -2.56(01) MeV).
- For the \( \Lambda N2 \) potential (\( \epsilon = 0 \)), \( B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 0.31(00) \) MeV and the system is bound relative to \( {_{\Lambda}^3 H} \) (energy -2.35(00) MeV).
- With the \( \Lambda N3 \) potential (no \( \Lambda NN \) interaction), \( {_{\Lambda\Lambda}^4 H} \) was found to be unbound.
- Using FGNN potential and NSC97e \( \Lambda N \) potential with \( \epsilon = 0 \), \( B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 0.35(02) \) MeV; with NSC97f, \( B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 0.36(03) \) MeV.
- Using Argonne \( V_{18} \) NN and NSC97e with \( \epsilon = 0.2 \), \( B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 0.08(02) \) MeV; with NSC97f and \( \epsilon = 0.2 \), \( B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 0.13(02) \) MeV.
- For the ND interaction (\( \gamma = 1.0 \)) with \( V_\sigma = 0.2 \) and \( \bar{V} = 6.17 \), \( B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 0.67(01) \) MeV; with \( V_\sigma = 0.3 \), \( B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 0.08(00) \) MeV.
- For the NEC00 interaction (\( \gamma = 1.2044 \)) with \( V_\sigma = 0.2 \), \( B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 1.12(01) \) MeV; with \( V_\sigma = 0.3 \), \( B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 0.13(00) \) MeV.
- The experimental \( B_{\Lambda} \) for \( {_{\Lambda}^3 H} \) is 0.13 \( \pm \) 0.05 MeV (Ref. [19]).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of double-hypernuclear systems following the identification of \( {_{\Lambda\Lambda}^6 He} \) at KEK E373 (with \( \Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 1.01 \pm 0.20^{+0.18}_{-0.11} \) MeV, later updated to \( 0.67 \pm 0.17 \) MeV). Prior calculations by Filikhin and Gal (Faddeev \( \Lambda\Lambda d \) model) predicted \( B_{\Lambda\Lambda} \approx 0.3 \) MeV, but their four-body \( \Lambda\Lambda p n \) Faddeev-Yakubovsky solution failed to bind the system. Nemura, Akaishi and Myint (stochastic variational model) reported \( B_{\Lambda\Lambda} \approx 0.4 \) MeV, and Shoeb (variational Monte-Carlo) found bound states for NSC97e and NSC97f potentials. The present results show that when the space-exchange parameter \( \epsilon = 0.2 \) (consistent with low-energy \( \Lambda p \) scattering data) is used with realistic Argonne \( V_{18} \) NN potentials, \( {_{\Lambda\Lambda}^4 H} \) becomes unbound for \( V_\sigma \) values that reproduce experimental \( {_{\Lambda}^4 H} \) and \( {_{\Lambda}^4 H}^* \) energies, leaving the existence of this hypernucleus an open question requiring further theoretical exploration.
