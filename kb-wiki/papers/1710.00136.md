# Hot Spaghetti: Viscous Gravitational Collapse

> We explore the fate of matter falling into a macroscopic Schwarzschild black hole for the simplified case of a radially collapsing thin spherical shell for which the back reaction of the geometry can be neglected. We treat the internal dynamics of the infalling matter in the framework of viscous relativistic hydrodynamics and calculate how the internal temperature of the collapsing matter evolves as it falls toward the Schwarzschild singularity. We find that viscous hydrodynamics fails when either, the dissipative radial pressure exceeds the thermal pressure and the total radial pressure becomes negative, or the time scale of variation of the tidal forces acting on the collapsing matter becomes shorter than the characteristic hydrodynamic response time.

# Hot Spaghetti: Viscous Gravitational Collapse

## Key claim

Viscous relativistic hydrodynamics fails to describe matter collapsing into a Schwarzschild black hole when either the dissipative radial pressure exceeds the thermal pressure (making total radial pressure negative) or when the tidal force variation timescale becomes shorter than the hydrodynamic response time; for a thin spherical shell with negligible back-reaction, the second-order causal theory only marginally extends validity beyond the Navier-Stokes regime, with the failure radius $r_\mathrm{g}$ approximately equal to the matching radius $r_\mathrm{m}$.

## Method

The authors model a thin spherical shell of matter falling radially into a pre-existing Schwarzschild black hole along a geodesic trajectory, using Painlevé-Gullstrand coordinates and a comoving coordinate system. They solve the coupled equations of second-order (Israel-Stewart) relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for the shear stress and entropy density, assuming a constant shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio $\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)$, and match the Navier-Stokes solution at large radii to the second-order solution at small radii.

## Key numbers

- For a 10 solar mass black hole ($R = 30$ km), hadrons are disrupted by tidal forces at $r_h \sim 3$ nm, well above the Planck curvature scale $r_c \approx 10^{-22}$ m.
- The matching radius where Navier-Stokes fails is $r_\mathrm{m} \approx - (3/\bar\omega) (\eta/s) \sqrt{R/r_0} (1/T_0)$; for $T_0 = 0.25$ meV (CMB temperature) this gives $r_\mathrm{m} = 0.5$ mm and $T_\mathrm{m} = 1.1$ eV.
- For $T_0 = 100$ MeV (pre-heated matter), $r_\mathrm{m} = 1.3$ fm and $T_\mathrm{m} = 2.8 \times 10^8$ GeV.
- The dimensionless shear stress in the second-order regime is $\bar\omega \approx -0.454$ for the conformal Boltzmann gas ($\beta = 5$) and $\bar\omega \approx -0.698$ for strongly coupled super-Yang-Mills theory ($\beta = 2(2-\ln 2) \approx 2.61$).
- The temperature in the second-order regime scales as $T(r) \propto r^{-b}$ with $b = 1/2 - \bar\omega/3$.

## Context

This work extends the analytical collapse models of Adler et al. (2005) and the viscous collapse formalism of Herrera et al. (2008) by applying second-order causal relativistic hydrodynamics (Israel-Stewart theory with complete 14-moment approximation terms from Denicol et al., 2012) to the specific case of a thin shell in Schwarzschild geometry. The results show that hydrodynamics fails before reaching Planck-scale curvatures, motivating future work using anisotropic hydrodynamics (Florkowski, 2010; Bazow, 2013), kinetic theory (Florkowski, 2017), or holographic descriptions (Kovtun, 2003) to treat the post-hydrodynamic regime where the matter may cavitate or develop coherent color fields.
