# Lattice QCD constraints on pion electroproduction off a nucleon

> Very recently, a lattice QCD collaboration has explored threshold pion electroproduction near the physical pion mass and has simulated the relevant multipole amplitudes. Different multipole amplitudes are usually entangled in experimental data, and thus extracting each of them independently from first principles provides additional essential constraints on phenomenological theories. We use nonperturbative Hamiltonian theory to investigate the electroproduction process, providing an advanced approach with additional two-particle coupled channels to acquire the physical electric dipole amplitudes from the original lattice QCD data. We note that future lattice QCD simulations of the electric dipole amplitudes at higher energies will be much closer to their physical counterparts than the current ones near threshold. In addition, we obtain a new expression which, like that of Lellouch-Lüscher, depends only on the final-state interactions but provides both the real and imaginary parts of the transition amplitudes.

# Lattice QCD constraints on pion electroproduction off a nucleon

## Key claim

The authors use nonperturbative Hamiltonian theory (NPHT) to extract both the real and imaginary parts of the physical electric dipole amplitudes for pion electroproduction from original lattice QCD data, whereas the standard Lellouch-Luscher method only provides the absolute value. The finite-volume effects on these amplitudes are shown to be much smaller for the first excited eigenstates than for the ground states, suggesting that future lattice simulations at higher energies will provide more powerful constraints.

## Method

The authors employ nonperturbative Hamiltonian theory (NPHT) to relate finite-volume lattice QCD spectra to infinite-volume scattering observables for the process gamma* N -> pi N. They include the eta N and K Lambda coupled channels in the final-state interactions and use the tree-level transition potential from effective Lagrangians with nucleon and pion electromagnetic form factors parameterized from experiment. The coupling constant f_pi NN is fitted to the original lattice QCD data for the finite-volume electric dipole amplitude E_{0+}^L, yielding a best-fit value of 0.96 plus or minus 0.05 with chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.43.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit value of the pion-nucleon coupling constant f_pi NN is 0.96 plus or minus 0.05, consistent with the empirical value of 0.984 plus or minus 0.007 from Reinert (2020).
- The ratio of infinite-volume to finite-volume amplitudes near the pi N threshold is approximately 1.11 to 1.13 in the NPHT approach, while the Lellouch-Luscher factor from Gao (2025) is around 1.15.
- The extracted beta parameter for Im E_{0+}^{gamma p -> pi^0 p} is 2.97 plus or minus 0.16 in units of 10^{-3}/m_{pi^+}, compared to 2.83 (HBChPT) and 3.16 (RChPT) from Hilt (2013).
- The extracted gamma parameter for the same amplitude is -1.71 plus or minus 0.40 in the same units, compared to -1.97 (HBChPT) and -1.08 (RChPT).
- Removing the eta N and K Lambda contributions reduces the physical E_{0+} of the proton target by 3.7 percent to 4.5 percent over the range of q^2 values considered.
- For a 3 fm box, the binding energy U_0^{bind} is 15 MeV.
- The finite-volume correction factor F_NPHT for the ground state at L = 3 fm is 1.164; the separable-potential approximation gives 1.145 and the Lellouch-Luscher formula gives 1.206.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NPHT-based analysis of pion photoproduction (Zhuge 2024) to the case of pion electroproduction with a virtual photon, incorporating the very recent lattice QCD data from Gao (2025) that simulated multipole amplitudes near the physical pion mass. The approach provides an alternative to the Lellouch-Luscher method (Lellouch 2000) for extracting infinite-volume amplitudes, with the advantage of yielding both real and imaginary parts. The results can be compared with chiral perturbation theory analyses (Bernard 1995, Hilt 2013) and partial-wave analyses from SAID (Briscoe 2023), EBAC, and MAID (Drechsel 2007). The framework enables future lattice QCD simulations at higher energies to better constrain the electromagnetic properties of nucleon excited states.
