# Dilepton production in pion-nucleon collisions in an effective field theory approach

> We present a model of electron-positron pair production in pion-nucleon collisions in the exclusive reaction πN -> Ne^+e^-. The model is based on an effective field theory approach, incorporating 16 baryon resonances below 2 GeV. Parameters of the model are fitted to pion photoproduction data. We present the resulting dilepton invariant mass spectra for π^- p collisions up to sqrt(s)=1.9 GeV center-of-mass collision energy. These results are meant to give predictions for the planned experiments at the HADES spectrometer in GSI, Darmstadt.

## Key claim
The model provides predictions for dilepton invariant mass spectra in π⁻p collisions up to √s = 1.9 GeV center-of-mass energy, but these predictions depend on resonance parameters fitted to pion photoproduction data rather than directly to dilepton data, and the model incorporates 16 baryon resonances below 2 GeV with coupling constants whose signs are largely unknown from partial decay widths alone.

## Method
The authors constructed an effective field theory model for the exclusive reaction πN → Ne⁺e⁻, including Born diagrams (s-channel, u-channel, t-channel, and contact terms), vector meson exchange diagrams, and s-channel and u-channel baryon resonance contributions for 16 resonances below 2 GeV. They employed a vector meson dominance (VMD) model for the electromagnetic interaction of hadrons, with the ρ⁰ meson mediating the hadron-photon coupling, and used pseudovector couplings for pion-nucleon interactions. The model parameters were fitted to pion photoproduction data, and the resulting matrix elements were used to compute differential cross sections as functions of dilepton invariant mass.

## Key numbers
- The model includes 16 baryon resonances below 2 GeV center-of-mass energy.
- The pseudovector NNπ coupling constant used is f_NNπ = 0.97.
- The ρ meson coupling constant g_ρ = 4.96 is obtained from the ρ → e⁺e⁻ decay width.
- The ρππ coupling constant g_ρππ = 5.96 is obtained from the ρ → ππ decay width.
- The g_NNρ coupling constant is set to 2.98 (half of g_ρππ).
- The ρπγ coupling constant g_ρπγ = 0.103 is obtained from the ρ → πγ decay width.
- The a₁πγ coupling constant g_a₁πγ = 0.106 is obtained from the a₁ → πγ decay width.
- The NNa₁ coupling constant g_NNa₁ = 8.65 is derived from the relation g_NNa₁/m_a₁ = f_NNπ/m_π.
- The cutoff parameter Λ_u = 0.3 GeV is used for u-channel resonance diagrams.
- The t-channel ρ exchange contribution is at least three orders of magnitude smaller than the Born contribution in the √s ≤ 1 GeV energy range.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach previously applied to NN → NNe⁺e⁻ by Shyam (2003, 2009, 2010), Kaptari (2006, 2007, 2009), and others to the πN → Ne⁺e⁻ channel, which had not been studied with such methods before. The model is designed to provide input for transport models used to interpret heavy ion collision data from the DLS at LBL and HADES at GSI, where planned experiments with pion beams will study both πA and πN collisions. The approach builds on pion photoproduction models by Garcilazo, Feuster and Mosel (1998), and Fernandez (2002), and the results are meant to give predictions for upcoming HADES experiments with pion beams.
