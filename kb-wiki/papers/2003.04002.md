# Nuclear matrix elements for $λ$ mechanism of $0νββ$ of $^{48}$Ca in nuclear shell-model: Closure versus nonclosure approach

> The $λ$ and $m_{ββ}$ mechanisms of neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$) occur with light neutrino exchange via $W_L-W_R$, and $W_L-W_L$ mediation, respectively. In the present study, we calculate the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for the $m_{ββ}$ and $λ$ mechanisms of $0νββ$, which has origin in the left-right symmetric model with right-handed gauge boson at TeV scale. The NMEs are calculated for one of the $0νββ$ decaying isotope $^{48}$Ca in the interacting nuclear shell-model using the GXPF1A effective interaction of $pf$-shell. The NMEs are calculated in both closure and nonclosure approaches using four different methods: closure, running closure, running nonclosure, and mixed methods. All the NMEs are calculated incorporating the effects of the finite size of nucleons and the revisited higher order terms such as isoscalar and weak magnetism terms of the nucleon currents. Inclusion of the short-range nature of nucleon-nucleon interaction in Miller-Spencer, CD-Bonn, and AV18 parametrizations is also taken care of. The comparative dependence of the running closure and running nonclosure NMEs with the spin-parity of the allowed states of intermediate nucleus $^{48}$Sc, the coupled spin-parity of the two initial decaying neutrons and the final two protons, the cutoff excitation energy of $^{48}$Sc, the cutoff number of states of $^{48}$Sc are also examined. Results show that there are about 2-20\% enhancements in different types of total NMEs, calculated in the nonclosure approach as compared to the closure approach. The significant enhancements are found in the $M_{qGT}$ and $M_{qT}$ type NMEs for the inclusion of the higher-order terms of the nucleon currents.

# Nuclear matrix elements for λ mechanism of 0νββ of ⁴⁸Ca in nuclear shell-model: Closure versus nonclosure approach

## Key claim

The nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for both the mββ and λ mechanisms of neutrinoless double beta decay in ⁴⁸Ca, calculated using the interacting shell-model with the GXPF1A effective interaction, show approximately 2-20% enhancements when computed in the nonclosure approach compared to the closure approach, with the most significant enhancements appearing in the MqGT and MqT type NMEs due to inclusion of higher-order nucleon current terms.

## Method

The authors calculated NMEs for ⁴⁸Ca using the interacting shell-model with the GXPF1A effective interaction of the pf-shell, employing four different methods: closure, running closure, running nonclosure, and mixed methods. The calculations incorporated finite nucleon size effects, revisited higher-order terms (isoscalar and weak magnetism) of nucleon currents, and short-range correlations using Miller-Spencer, CD-Bonn, and AV18 parametrizations. The NMEs were computed for both the standard mββ mechanism (W_L-W_L exchange) and the λ mechanism (W_L-W_R exchange) originating from the left-right symmetric model.

## Key numbers

- For Mν type NMEs with no SRC: closure method gives 0.765, running nonclosure gives 0.836 (about 9% larger)
- For Mν type NMEs with CD-Bonn SRC: closure method gives 0.799, running nonclosure gives 0.874 (about 9% larger)
- For Mνω type NMEs with AV18 SRC: closure method gives 0.670, running nonclosure gives 0.781 (about 17% larger)
- For MqGT type NMEs with CD-Bonn SRC: closure method gives 3.554, running nonclosure gives 3.709 (about 4% larger)
- For M1+ type NMEs with CD-Bonn SRC: closure method gives 4.221, running nonclosure gives 4.356 (about 3% larger)
- For M2- type NMEs with AV18 SRC: closure method gives 0.214, running nonclosure gives 0.319 (about 49% larger)
- MGT type NMEs in running nonclosure are about 10-13% larger than corresponding NMEs in running closure method
- MωGT type NMEs in running nonclosure are about 16-21% larger than corresponding NMEs in running closure method
- Standard closure energy ⟨E⟩ = 7.72 MeV was used for closure and running closure methods
- Bare axial-vector constant gA = 1.27 was used

## Context

This work extends the revisited formalism for light neutrino-exchange mechanisms of 0νββ developed by Simkovic et al. (2015) and the QRPA calculations for λ and mββ mechanisms by Simkovic et al. (2017), which used closure approximation. It also builds upon earlier shell-model calculations for ⁴⁸Ca by Menendez et al. (2018) that used closure approximation without including higher-order nucleon current terms, and by Neacsu and Horoi (2018) who applied nonclosure approach only for the mββ mechanism. The results enable more precise comparisons between closure and nonclosure approaches for both mechanisms, and provide NMEs needed to extract neutrino physics parameters from future 0νββ experiments on ⁴⁸Ca.
