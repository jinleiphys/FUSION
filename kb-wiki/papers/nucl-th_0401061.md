# Correlated two-pion exchange and large-N(C) behavior of nuclear forces

> The effect of correlated scalar-isoscalar two-pion exchange (CrTPE) modes is considered in connection with central and spin-orbit parts of the NN force. The two-pion correlation function is coupled directly to the scalar form factor of the nucleon which we calculate in the large-N(C) limit where the nucleon can be described as a soliton of an effective chiral theory. The results for the central NN force show a strong repulsive core at short internucleon distances supplemented by a moderate attraction beyond 1 fm. The long-range tail of the central NN potential is driven by the the pion-nucleon sigma term and consistent with the effective $σ$ meson exchange. The spin-orbit part of the NN potential is repulsive. The large-N(C) scaling behavior of the scalar-isoscalar NN interaction is addressed. We show that the spin-orbit part is O(1/N^2(C)) in strength relative to the central force resulting in the ratio $\simeq 1/9$ suggested by the 1/N(C) expansion for N(C)=3. The latter is in agreement with our numerical analysis and with the Kaplan-Manohar large-N(C) power counting. Unitarization of the $ππ$ scattering amplitude plays here an important role and improves the tree level results. Analytical representations of the CrTPE NN potential in terms of elementary functions are derived and their chiral content is discussed.

## Key claim

The correlated scalar-isoscalar two-pion exchange (CrTPE) produces a strong repulsive core in the central nucleon-nucleon force at short distances (below 1 fm) and a moderate attraction beyond 1 fm, even at tree level, with the repulsive feature depending primarily on the functional form of the nucleon scalar form factor rather than on unitarization of the pion-pion amplitude.

## Method

The authors couple the scalar-isoscalar two-pion correlation function to the scalar form factor of the nucleon, calculated in the large-N_c limit using the Chiral Quark-Soliton Model (χQSM) where the nucleon is a soliton of an effective chiral theory. The tree-level and unitarized ππ scattering amplitudes are used to construct the central and spin-orbit NN potentials via a Partovi-Lomon quasi-potential reduction, with analytical representations derived in terms of elementary functions.

## Key numbers

- Central potential at the origin: V_C(r=0) ≈ 600 MeV (repulsive core)
- Central potential minimum: V_C ≈ -20 MeV at internucleon distances r > 1 fm (moderate attraction)
- Pion-nucleon sigma term from the model: σ_πN = σ(0) ≈ 68 MeV
- Ratio of spin-orbit to central force strength: V_LS/V_C ≈ 1/9, consistent with the 1/N_c expansion for N_c=3
- Dynamical σ meson pole position from unitarized ππ amplitude: M - iΓ/2 ≈ 450 - i221 MeV
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV
- Dynamical quark mass: M_q = 350 MeV (at zero momentum transfer)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Oset (2000) on unitarized chiral perturbation theory for the NN interaction and the Skyrme model results of Walhout (1991), confirming that correlated two-pion exchange produces a repulsive core in the scalar-isoscalar channel rather than the purely attractive Yukawa-like σ exchange of one-boson-exchange models such as CD-Bonn and Nijmegen. The large-N_c scaling analysis shows consistency with the Kaplan-Manohar (1996) power counting and with the scalar-isoscalar components of phenomenological potentials studied by Riska (2002). The results enable a connection between the effective σNN coupling constant and the pion-nucleon sigma term, and provide analytical forms of the CrTPE potential suitable for use in nuclear structure calculations.
