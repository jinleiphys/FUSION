# Mixing interactions and effects in the NJL-model

> The flavor-dependent quark-antiquark contact interactions, induced by vacuum polarization and recently derived for flavor U(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, are articulated with the resulting mixing effects emerging from flavor symmetry breaking in view. The formal effects of the explicit mixing interactions, $G_{i\neq j}$, are detailed firstly for the meson mixing problem without the inclusion of 't Hooft interactions induced by instantons. Secondly, it is shown that these mixings, in the scalar channel of quark-antiquark interactions, might give rise to quark mixing in the gap equations. Sixth order quark-antiquark interactions from vacuum polarization, that break $U_A(1)$ symmetry, also contribute.

# Mixing interactions and effects in the NJL-model

## Key claim

Flavor-dependent quark-antiquark contact interactions derived from vacuum polarization in the U(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model produce explicit mixing interactions that, when diagonalized in the meson sector, generate quark mixing in the gap equations even without the 't Hooft instanton-induced interactions; sixth-order quark-antiquark interactions from vacuum polarization also contribute to these mixing effects.

## Method

The author derives flavor-dependent coupling constants from one-loop vacuum polarization corrections to the NJL model, expressed in terms of quark propagators with constituent masses. The mixing interactions are analyzed in both the adjoint representation (for meson Bethe-Salpeter equations) and the fundamental representation (for quark gap equations), with a rotation among neutral pseudoscalar meson fields (P3, P0, P8) used to diagonalize the meson nonet masses. Sixth-order quark-antiquark interactions from vacuum polarization are reduced to effective fourth-order interactions via mean-field approximation of scalar currents.

## Key numbers

- Mixing interactions $G_{i \neq j}$ are proportional to $(M_{f_1} - M_{f_2})^n$ with $n=1,2$ for the neutral flavor states $i,j=0,3,8$
- The diagonalized coupling constants in the fundamental representation yield $2 G_{uu} = 2 G_{dd} = (14 I_{uu} + 14 I_{dd} + 8 I_{ss})/18$
- The strange quark self-interaction becomes $2 G_{ss} = (8 I_{uu} + 8 I_{dd} + 20 I_{ss})/18$
- The off-diagonal mixing $2 G_{ud} = (-8 I_{uu} - 8 I_{dd} + 16 I_{ss})/18$
- The off-diagonal mixings $2 G_{us} = 2 G_{ds} = (4 I_{uu} + 4 I_{dd} - 8 I_{ss})/18$

## Context

This work extends the author's previous derivations of flavor-dependent coupling constants from vacuum polarization (Braghin, PRD 2021; JPG 2022) and their applications to pion and kaon structure (JPG 2022; EPJA 2023). It contrasts with the standard NJL approach where the 't Hooft determinantal interaction (Creutz 1980; Osipov 2000) is the sole source of UA(1) symmetry breaking and meson mixing. The results enable future numerical studies of charmed pseudoscalar meson spectra and pseudoscalar meson decay constants, with the sixth-order interactions from vacuum polarization (EPJA 2024; PRD 2014) providing contributions that can be added to those from 't Hooft interactions.
