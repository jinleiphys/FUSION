# Proton Polarizability Contribution: Muonic Hydrogen Lamb Shift and Elastic Scattering

> The uncertainty in the contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen, $ΔE^{subt}$ arising from proton polarizability effects in the two-photon exchange diagram at large virtual photon momenta is shown large enough to account for the proton radius puzzle. This is because $ΔE^{subt}$ is determined by an integrand that falls very slowly with very large virtual photon momenta. We evaluate the necessary integral using a set of chosen form factors and also a dimensional regularization procedure which makes explicit the need for a low energy constant. The consequences of our two-photon exchange interaction for low-energy elastic lepton-proton scattering are evaluated and could be observable in a planned low energy lepton-proton scattering experiment planned to run at PSI.

# Proton Polarizability Contribution: Muonic Hydrogen Lamb Shift and Elastic Scattering

## Key claim
The uncertainty in the subtraction-term contribution to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift from proton polarizability effects in the two-photon exchange diagram is large enough to account for the proton radius puzzle, but only if the high-momentum behavior of the subtraction function is assumed to produce a sufficiently large effect. The result depends heavily on the arbitrary choice of form factor or renormalization scheme used to handle the logarithmic divergence in the integral.

## Method
The author evaluates the subtraction-term contribution ΔE^subt to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift using two approaches: (1) a parameterized form factor F_loop(Q^2) with variable power-law behavior at low and high Q^2, constrained by chiral perturbation theory results from Birse and McGovern (2012); and (2) dimensional regularization of the two-photon exchange loop integral, which introduces a low-energy contact interaction whose finite part is fit to the required 0.31 meV shift. The consequences for low-energy elastic muon-proton scattering are then computed as an interference term between the one-photon and two-photon exchange amplitudes.

## Key numbers
- The Lamb shift difference needed to resolve the proton radius puzzle is 0.31 meV = 3.1 × 10^{-10} MeV.
- The magnetic polarizability β_M = (3.1 ± 0.5) × 10^{-4} fm^3 from Griesshammer et al. (2012).
- The Birse-McGovern form factor uses M_β = 460 ± 50 MeV.
- Using the form factor approach with N=5, n=2, β = 10^{-3} fm^3, and λ = 30.9 reproduces ΔE = 0.31 meV.
- Using dimensional regularization, the parameter λ = 769 is required to set ΔE^DR = 0.31 meV, which corresponds to a natural-sized coefficient of 3.95 when expressed in units of 4π/Λ_χ^3.
- The predicted effect on muon-proton scattering at 100-200 MeV/c laboratory momentum is a few percent, detectable in the planned PSI experiment with sub-1% accuracy.

## Context
This work addresses the proton radius puzzle, where the muonic hydrogen measurement by Pohl et al. (2010) extracted a proton radius 4% smaller than the CODATA value from electronic hydrogen, a 5.0 standard deviation discrepancy. The paper extends prior work by Pachucki (1999), Carlson and Vanderhaeghen (2011), and Birse and McGovern (2012) on the two-photon exchange subtraction term, showing that the uncertainty from high-momentum behavior is larger than previously appreciated. The author's earlier attempt (Miller, 2011) to explain the puzzle via two-photon exchange in quasi-elastic electron-nucleus scattering was ruled out by experiment (Miller, 2012). The present work enables experimental tests via the planned low-energy muon-proton scattering experiment at PSI (Arrington, 2012), which could distinguish between the form-factor and effective-field-theory approaches.
