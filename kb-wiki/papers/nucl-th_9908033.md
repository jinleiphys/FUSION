# Implications of the unusual structure in the pp correlation from Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV

> The recent NA49 measurement of two-proton correlation function shows an interesting and unexpected structure at large relative momentum. Applying source imaging techniques to the measurement, we find an unusually steep drop-off in the two-proton source function. We show that the steep drop-off is due to the structure in the correlation and the drop-off cannot be explained using conventional correlation analysis. We suggest possible physics reasons for the unusual source function.

## Key claim

The unusual structure observed in the NA49 two-proton correlation function at large relative momentum (around 70 MeV/c) leads to a source function that violates the positivity condition required by the Koonin-Pratt formalism, indicating that either the imaging procedure, the assumptions of the formalism, or both are invalid for this dataset.

## Method

The authors applied source imaging techniques to invert the NA49 two-proton correlation function from Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV, extracting the two-proton source function in a model-independent manner without assuming a Gaussian source. They solved the Schrödinger equation with the REID93 and Coulomb potentials for partial waves with ℓ≤2 and used pure Coulomb waves for ℓ≥3 to calculate the two-proton relative wavefunctions, then minimized χ² to obtain the source from the integral equation C(q)-1 = 4π ∫ dr r² K(q,r) S_q(r). They tested the resulting source against the Fourier transform positivity condition derived from the Koonin-Pratt formalism, and compared the imaged source to NA49's best-fit Gaussian source and to sources obtained after smoothing out the correlation structure.

## Key numbers

- The correlation function shows an unexpected structure at q_inv (magnitude of proton momentum in pair center-of-mass frame) of approximately 70 MeV/c.
- The imaged source is consistent with a step-function with radius approximately 10 fm.
- The integral of the source over the imaged region is 4π ∫₀¹²ᶠᵐ dr r² S(r) = 0.78 ± 0.55.
- NA49's best-fit Gaussian single-particle source had a radius of 3.85 fm, fitted to correlation points with q < 48 MeV/c.
- The Fourier transform of the imaged source, Ṡ(k), goes negative in the range 100–150 MeV/c.
- The kernel cuts off contributions from pairs with relative momentum q ≳ q_cut ≈ 100 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the application of source imaging techniques (Brown 1997, Brown 1998) to the NA49 two-proton correlation data, which shows a structure not seen in other experiments for different collision systems or energies. Similar structures had been predicted in two-pion correlation tails by Makhlin (1999) and Pratt (1997), attributed to hidden correlations or breakdown of the smoothness approximation, but applying those predictions to two-proton correlations is difficult due to differences in final-state interactions. The results challenge the standard Koonin-Pratt formalism (Koonin 1977, Pratt 1990) and suggest the need for experimental confirmation, while pointing toward possible physics explanations including strong low-q dependence of the source, non-convolution of independent single-particle sources, or breakdown of the smoothness assumption.
