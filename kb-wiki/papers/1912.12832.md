# Collisional dynamics of polaronic clouds immersed in a Fermi sea

> We propose a new protocol to examine many-polaron properties in a cold atom experiment. Initially, polaronic clouds are prepared around the opposite edges of a majority gas cloud. After time evolution, the collision of two clouds exhibits various polaronic effects. To see how {\it collective} properties of many polarons with mediated interactions appear in the case in which the impurity and majority gases are composed of mass-balanced fermions with different spin components, we perform a nonlinear hydrodynamic simulation for collisional dynamics of two Fermi polaronic clouds. We found that the dynamics is governed by the impurity Fermi pressure, polaron energy, and multi-polaron correlations. In particular, shock waves occur in such a way as to reflect the many-body properties of polarons through the first sound of minority clouds. Our idea is applicable to other systems such as Bose polarons as well as mass-imbalanced mixtures.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that collisional dynamics of two polaronic clouds in a cold atom experiment can reveal collective many-polaron properties, but the authors hedge that their hydrodynamic simulation is limited to the regime where the relaxation time is shorter than the inverse of the mode frequency, and that the results depend on the specific choice of parameters such as the initial separation distance and impurity concentration.

## Method

The authors performed nonlinear hydrodynamic simulations of two Fermi polaronic clouds (minority spin-down atoms) immersed in a majority Fermi gas (spin-up atoms) in an elongated harmonic trap, solving Euler's equation and the continuity equation for the minority component. The energy density was constructed from the Landau-Pomeranchuk Hamiltonian including the impurity Fermi pressure term, the polaron energy term, the polaron-polaron interaction term, and an induced three-body force term, with parameters taken from previous variational theory for mass-balanced unitary Fermi polarons. The majority gas was treated as an ideal Fermi gas in the Thomas-Fermi approximation with zero velocity, and the one-dimensional axial dynamics was solved using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method with smearing.

## Key numbers

- Impurity concentration Y = 0.01 (1% of majority density) was used to keep the majority cloud stable.
- Initial separation of polaronic clouds was set to z_I = 0.8R_z (where R_z is the axial Thomas-Fermi radius of the majority cloud) for the main results, with a comparison case of z_I = 0.1R_z.
- The collision time t_c was estimated as t_c * E_F,0 = 666, where E_F,0 is the Fermi energy of majority atoms.
- The effective mass of polarons was taken as m_down*/m_down = 1.17, with polaron energy coefficient chi = 0.6 and polaron-polaron interaction coefficient kappa = 0.2.
- The trap aspect ratio was omega_z/omega_perp = 20/233, with total majority atom number N_up = 1.5 x 10^5.
- The width of initial impurity clouds was eta = 0.05R_z.
- For z_I = 0.1R_z, the colliding velocity was estimated as v_col ~ 0.11 omega_z R_z, compared to the first sound velocity c_1,down ~ 0.14 omega_z R_z.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of Fermi polarons (Chevy 2006, Pilati 2014) and induced polaron-polaron interactions (Nishida 2015, Naidon 2014) by proposing a dynamical protocol to probe collective multi-polaron effects, in contrast to existing experiments that measure single-polaron properties via RF spectroscopy (MIT 2009, LENS 2012) or spin-dipole oscillations (ENS 2012, MIT 2014). The approach is analogous to earlier collisional studies of two-spin mixtures (MIT 2007, Goulko 2011) but focuses on indirect majority-mediated interactions between polarons rather than direct interspecies attraction. The results enable future experiments to extract information on multi-polaron correlations and induced interactions by tuning the majority density gradient and initial cloud separation.
