# Structure of the vacuum states in the presence of isovector and isoscalar pairing correlations

> The long standing problem of proton-neutron pairing and, in particular, the limitations imposed on the solutions by the available symmetries, is revisited. We look for solutions with non-vanishing expectation values of the proton, the neutron and the isoscalar gaps. For an equal number of protons and neutrons we find two solutions where the absolute values of proton and neutrons gaps are equal but have the same or opposite sign. The behavior and structure of these solutions differ for spin saturated (single l-shell) and spin unsaturared systems (single j-shell). In the former case the BCS results are checked against an exact calculation.

## Key claim

The paper revisits the long-standing problem of proton-neutron pairing and shows that, contrary to earlier work by Goodman (1972), the mean-field solutions may take more general forms than previously allowed, specifically with non-vanishing expectation values for the proton gap, neutron gap, and isoscalar gap simultaneously. For equal numbers of protons and neutrons, two non-trivial solutions exist with \(\Delta_p = \pm \Delta_n\), but their existence and physical meaning depend critically on whether the system is spin-saturated (single \(l\)-shell) or spin-unsaturated (single \(j\)-shell).

## Method

The authors construct a generalized BCS transformation from particles to quasi-particles that allows for non-zero proton, neutron, and isoscalar pairing gaps, starting from a number- and isospin-conserving separable pairing Hamiltonian. They solve the self-consistency and number equations for a single \(l\)-shell and a single \(j\)-shell model space, with equal proton and neutron numbers and identical single-particle spectra. For the \(l\)-shell case, the BCS results are compared against exact solutions obtained from the \(SO(8)\)-invariant Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- For the \(l\)-shell solution with \(\Delta_p = -\Delta_n\), the self-consistency condition forces \(x = 0\) (where \(x\) parametrizes the relative strength of isovector and isoscalar interactions via \(g_1 = g(1-x)\) and \(g_0 = g(1+x)\)), and the allowed interval around this value has zero width.
- For the \(l\)-shell solution with \(\Delta_p = \Delta_n\), the domain of existence in the \((x, \eta)\) plane is bounded by the curves \(\eta_1 = \sqrt{(1+x_1)/(1-x_1)}\) for \(-1 \leq x_1 \leq 0\) and \(\eta_1 = \sqrt{(1-x_1)/(1+x_1)}\) for \(0 \leq x_1 \leq 1\), and by \(\eta_2 = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{1-x_2^2}\) which does not exist for \(\eta \geq 1/2\).
- For the \(j\)-shell with \(j = 21/2\) and occupation parameter \(\eta = -4/11\), the solution with \(\Delta_p = -\Delta_n\) exists within a finite interval starting at \(x \geq -1/7\), where it becomes the lowest energy state.
- For the \(j\)-shell at half-filling (\(\eta = 0\)), the solution with \(\Delta_p = \Delta_n\) exists in \(j - 1/2\) closed intervals, with the first interval degenerate with the trivial \(\Delta_0 \neq 0\) solution extending within \(-\frac{1}{2} \leq x \leq 0\) (to leading order in \(1/j\)).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Goodman (1972) on symmetry-imposed limitations in proton-neutron pairing, showing that more general solutions exist than those previously considered. It also connects to the results of Engel et al. (1996, 1997) for nuclei with equal proton and neutron numbers, which found two degenerate BCS-like solutions with \(T=1\) pairs. The paper enables future experimental searches for non-trivial pairing solutions in nuclei where valence nucleons occupy a single \(j\)-level integrated into the lower harmonic oscillator shell, and suggests that projection techniques (as in Dobaczewski and Płoszajczak, 1998) may be needed to relate intrinsic and laboratory frames.
