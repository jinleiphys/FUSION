# Algebraic Model for Quantum Scattering. Reformulation, Analysis and Numerical Strategies

> The convergence problem for scattering states is studied in detail within the framework of the Algebraic Model, a representation of the Schrodinger equation in an L^2 basis. The dynamical equations of this model are reformulated featuring new "Dynamical Coefficients", which explicitly reveal the potential effects. A general analysis of the Dynamical Coefficients leads to an optimal basis yielding well converging, precise and stable results. A set of strategies for solving the equations for non-optimal bases is formulated based on the asymptotic behaviour of the Dynamical Coefficients. These strategies are shown to provide a dramatically improved convergence of the solutions.

## Key claim

The convergence of scattering solutions in the Algebraic Model depends critically on the ratio of the oscillator radius to the potential width, and an optimal basis exists that yields well-converging, precise, and stable results; when this optimal basis cannot be used, algorithmic strategies based on the asymptotic behaviour of the newly introduced "Dynamical Coefficients" can dramatically improve convergence.

## Method

The authors reformulated the Algebraic Model equations by separating the expansion coefficients into an asymptotic part (satisfying the free Schrodinger equation) and a deviation part, introducing Dynamical Coefficients V_n^(+) and V_n^(-) that represent the global effect of the potential on the regular and irregular asymptotic solutions. They analyzed these Dynamical Coefficients analytically for a Gaussian potential, deriving a closed-form expression for V_n^(+) and studying its dependence on the ratio gamma = (b/a)^2 where b is the oscillator radius and a is the potential width. The analysis was performed for a nuclear two-cluster problem with a Gaussian potential of depth V_0 = -8 MeV and width a = 1 fm, varying the oscillator radius b to explore different gamma values.

## Key numbers

- The optimal oscillator radius for a Gaussian potential occurs at gamma = 1/2, i.e., b = a/sqrt(2), where a = 1 fm gives b = 0.707 fm
- For a precision of about 10^{-8}, less than 12 basis functions are needed for all b values between 0.5 fm and 1.0 fm at an energy of 1 MeV
- The potential parameters used in the analysis were V_0 = -8 MeV and a = 1 fm
- The Dynamical Coefficient V_n^(+) at the optimal gamma = 1/2 behaves as V_n^(+) approximately proportional to 1/(n! R_n^{L+1/2})

## Context

This work extends the Algebraic Model formulation previously developed by Heller and Yamani (using "Kato correction"), Revai et al. (introducing the "Lanczos factor"), and Fillipov et al. (using an intuitive approximation). The analysis provides a systematic method for determining the optimal oscillator basis and the minimal number of basis states needed for converging scattering solutions, which enables controlled and stable calculations for nuclear cluster problems and can be generalized to other L^2 basis representations. The authors indicate that the different strategies will be applied explicitly to problems from nuclear, atomic, and molecular physics in a forthcoming paper.
