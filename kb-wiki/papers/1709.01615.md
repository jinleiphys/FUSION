# Electric fields at finite temperature

> Partial differential equations for the electric potential at finite temperature, taking into account the thermal Euler-Heisenberg contribution to the electromagnetic Lagrangian are derived. This complete temperature dependence introduces quantum corrections to several well known equations such as the Thomas-Fermi and the Poisson-Boltzmann equation. Our unified approach allows at the same time to derive other similar equations which take into account the effect of the surrounding heat bath on electric fields. We vary our approach by considering a neutral plasma as well as the screening caused by electrons only. The effects of changing the statistics from Fermi-Dirac to the Tsallis statistics and including the presence of a magnetic field are also investigated. Some useful applications of the above formalism are presented.

## Key claim

The paper derives a unified set of nonlinear Poisson-like equations for the electric potential at finite temperature that incorporate quantum corrections from the thermal Euler-Heisenberg effective Lagrangian, but the results are restricted to weak fields (compared to the critical field \(B_c = e^2/m_e\)) and slowly varying fields, and only the low-temperature (\(T \ll m_e\)) and high-temperature (\(T \gg m_e\)) limits are treated explicitly.

## Method

The authors start from the finite-temperature Euler-Heisenberg effective Lagrangian for QED, which modifies Maxwell's equations via the Gauss law \(\nabla \cdot \mathbf{D} = 4\pi\rho\). They then combine this modified Gauss law with temperature-dependent charge densities derived from Fermi-Dirac statistics (or Tsallis statistics in one variant) to obtain nonlinear Poisson equations. The approach is applied to several physical scenarios: a temperature-independent charge density (giving analytic solutions via Cardano's formula), degenerate and dilute matter at low temperature (generalizing the Thomas-Fermi and Poisson-Boltzmann equations), and dilute matter at high temperature (yielding renormalized Debye screening). Variants using Tsallis statistics and including a magnetic field via Landau level quantization are also developed.

## Key numbers

- The zero-temperature Euler-Heisenberg coefficient is \(a = e^4/(360\pi^2 m_e^4)\).
- For \(T \ll m_e\), the two-loop thermal Lagrangian coefficients are: \(b = (44\alpha^2\pi^2/2025)(T^4/m_e^4)\), \(c = (2^6 \times 37 \alpha^3 \pi^3)/(3^4 \times 5^2 \times 7) (T^4/m_e^8)\), and \(k = (2^{13} \alpha^3 \pi^5)/(3^6 \times 5 \times 7^2) (T^6/m_e^{10})\).
- For \(T \gg m_e\), the high-temperature thermal correction is \(\mathcal{L}_{EH}^T = -(2\alpha/3\pi)\mathcal{F}\ln(T/m_e) + (\alpha/6\pi)\mathcal{E} - \mathcal{L}_{EH}^0\).
- The Debye parameter in the high-temperature dilute case is renormalized to \(\kappa_{EH}^2 = \kappa^2 / [ (8\alpha/3)\ln(T/m_e) + 4\alpha/3 + 1 ]\).
- The non-degenerate matter density threshold is \(\rho \le 8.49 \times 10^{-17} (T^3/1\,\text{K})\,\text{g/cm}^3\) and the degenerate threshold is \(\rho \ge 4.2 \times 10^{-10} (T^3/1\,\text{K})\,\text{g/cm}^3\).

## Context

This work extends the Feynman-Metropolis-Teller (FMT) approach to include quantum electrodynamic corrections from the Euler-Heisenberg Lagrangian at finite temperature, building on the zero-temperature Euler-Heisenberg theory (Euler and Heisenberg 1936; Weisskopf 1936; Schwinger 1951) and the finite-temperature effective Lagrangian developed by Dittrich (1979) and others. The derived equations generalize the Thomas-Fermi equation (used in atomic physics, astrophysics, and solid-state physics) and the Poisson-Boltzmann equation (used in plasma physics and solutions) by adding thermal QED corrections. The formalism enables future studies of electric fields in hot, dense plasmas such as those in stars, supernovae, and the early universe, as well as applications to tunneling in a heat bath and electron-positron plasmas.
