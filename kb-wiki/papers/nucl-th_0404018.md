# Analysis of the GSI A+p and A+A Spallation, Fission, and Fragmentation Measurements with the LANL CEM2k and LAQGSM codes

> The CEM2k and LAQGSM codes have been recently developed at Los Alamos National Laboratory to simulate nuclear reactions induced by particles and nuclei for a number of applications. We have benchmarked our codes against most available measured data at projectile energies from 10 MeV/A to 800 GeV/A and have compared our results with predictions of other current models used by the nuclear community. Here, we present a brief description of our codes and show illustrative results obtained with CEM2k and LAQGSM for A+p and A+A spallation, fission, and fragmentation reactions measured recently at GSI compared with predictions by other models. Further necessary work is outlined.

## Key claim

The CEM2k and LAQGSM codes, merged with the GEM2 evaporation/fission model, describe a large variety of spallation, fission, and fragmentation reactions measured at GSI with an accuracy of a factor of two or better, without any refitted parameters; however, LAQGSM+GEM2 does not reproduce well the mass distributions for some fission-fragment elements from the reaction 1 GeV/A 238U + 208Pb, likely because it does not account for electromagnetic-induced fission or angular momentum.

## Method

The authors benchmarked their CEM2k and LAQGSM Monte Carlo codes against GSI measurements of A+p and A+A spallation, fission, and fragmentation reactions at projectile energies from 10 MeV/A to 800 GeV/A. They merged both codes with the Generalized Evaporation Model GEM2, which considers evaporation of up to 66 types of particles and light fragments, and compared their results with predictions from other models including LAHET3 using INCL+ABLA, ISABEL+Dresner/Atchison, and Bertini+Dresner/Atchison. All calculations were performed with fixed parameters, changing only the mass and charge numbers of projectile and target and the incident energy.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction 800 MeV/A 197Au+p, calculations using CEM2k+GEM2 with evaporation of up to 66 particle types gave results almost identical to those considering only 6 types (n, p, d, t, 3He, 4He) for all products measured at GSI; the "66" option requires about 7-8 times more computing time than the "6" option.
- LAQGSM+GEM2 describes the spallation and fission product cross sections for the reaction 1 GeV/A 208Pb+d with an accuracy of a factor of two or better, similar to the INCL+ABLA and ISABEL+Dresner/Atchison models.
- For the reaction 950 MeV/nucleon 238U on copper, LAQGSM+GEM2 describes most spallation product data with an accuracy of a factor of two or better.
- The codes were benchmarked against measured data at projectile energies from 10 MeV/A to 800 GeV/A.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of CEM2k and LAQGSM (Mashnik et al., 2002-2004) by merging them with the GEM2 code of Furihata (2000) to describe fission and fragmentation reactions in addition to spallation. The results are compared with predictions from LAHET3 using the INCL model of Cugnon et al. (1996) with the ABLA evaporation/fission model of Schmidt et al., as well as the ISABEL and Bertini intranuclear cascade models coupled with the Dresner evaporation code and the RAL fission model of Atchison. The codes have been incorporated into the MARS and LAHET transport codes and are being incorporated into MCNPX, enabling their use as event generators for simulations with arbitrary target geometries and nuclide compositions.
