# The Nuclear Scissors Mode in a Solvable Model

> The coupled dynamics of the scissors mode and the isovector giant quadrupole resonance is studied in a model with separable quadrupole-quadrupole residual interactions. The method of Wigner function moments is applied to derive the dynamical equations for angular momentum and quadrupole moment. Analytical expressions for energies, B(M1)- and B(E2)-values, sum rules and flow-patterns of both modes are found for arbitrary values of the deformation parameter. Some predictions for the case of superdeformation are given. The subtle nature of the phenomenon and its peculiarities are clarified.

## Key claim

The scissors mode is a pure quantum mechanical phenomenon that would be a zero-energy mode without the additional restoring force from nuclear elasticity (Fermi surface deformation), and its dynamics are inextricably coupled to the isovector giant quadrupole resonance (IVGQR), meaning the two modes cannot be excited independently.

## Method

The authors apply the Wigner Function Moments (WFM) method to the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equations for a harmonic oscillator mean field with separable quadrupole-quadrupole residual interactions, deriving closed dynamical equations for irreducible tensors of rank 0, 1, and 2. They linearize these equations around equilibrium for axially symmetric, non-rotating nuclei with equal neutron and proton deformations, then solve the resulting characteristic equations to obtain analytical expressions for eigenfrequencies of both isoscalar and isovector modes.

## Key numbers

- Scissors mode frequency: \(\Omega_-^2 = \bar{\omega}^2(2-\alpha)(1+\delta/3) - \sqrt{\bar{\omega}^4(2-\alpha)^2(1+\delta/3)^2 - 4\omega^4(1-\alpha)\delta^2}\), where \(\delta\) is the deformation parameter and \(\alpha = \kappa_1/\kappa_0\) is the ratio of isovector to isoscalar coupling constants
- IVGQR \(\mu=1\) branch frequency: \(\Omega_+^2 = \bar{\omega}^2(2-\alpha)(1+\delta/3) + \sqrt{\bar{\omega}^4(2-\alpha)^2(1+\delta/3)^2 - 4\omega^4(1-\alpha)\delta^2}\)
- Isoscalar GQR \(\mu=1\) branch frequency: \(\Omega_{is}^2 = 2\bar{\omega}^2(1+\delta/3)\)
- For the isoscalar system, the characteristic equation yields \(\Omega^2[\Omega^2 - 2\bar{\omega}^2(1+\delta/3)] = 0\), with the trivial solution \(\Omega = 0\) corresponding to the conserved isoscalar angular momentum

## Context

This work extends the RPA results of Hamamoto and Nazarewicz (1994) by providing analytical expressions for the coupled scissors mode and IVGQR dynamics, and reproduces the small-deformation limit of Suzuki (1973). The WFM approach avoids the spurious rotational components that plague intrinsic-coordinate calculations (as noted by Zawischa, 1998) and clarifies the debate about whether neutron and proton fluids separate during scissors motion. The results enable predictions for superdeformed and hyperdeformed nuclei, a regime where only the phenomenological model of Lo Iudice (2000) and the microscopic calculation of Hamamoto (1994) had previously made predictions.
