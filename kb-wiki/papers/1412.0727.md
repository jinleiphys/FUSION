# Parameterization of the statistical rate function for select superallowed transitions

> We present a parameterization of the statistical rate function, f, for 20 superallowed 0+-to-0+ nuclear beta transitions between T=1 analog states, and for 18 superallowed "mirror" transitions between analog T=1/2 states. All these transitions are of interest in the determination of V_{ud}. Although most of the transition Q_{EC} values have been measured, their precision will undoubtedly be improved in future. Our parameterization allows a user to easily calculate the corresponding new f value to high precision (+/-0.01%) without complicated computing.

## Key claim

The authors provide a simple parameterization of the statistical rate function \(f\) for 20 superallowed \(0^+ \to 0^+\) transitions and 18 mirror \(T=1/2\) transitions, which allows users to compute updated \(f\) values to \(\pm 0.01\%\) precision without running a full code, but only for a limited energy range (\(\pm 60\) keV around the current \(Q_{EC}\) value, except \(\pm 600\) keV for \(^{70}\text{Br}\)) and only for the specific transitions listed.

## Method

The authors computed the exact statistical rate function \(f\) by solving the Dirac equation for the electron in the Coulomb field of the nuclear charge distribution, including screening (Rose 1936), atomic overlap corrections, and kinematic recoil corrections. They then factored \(f = f_0 (1 + \delta_S)\), where \(f_0\) was fitted to a four-parameter analytic form (with two parameters fixed to their \(Z=0\) values) and the small correction \(\delta_S\) was fitted to a polynomial in \(W_0\) with coefficients derived from the shape-correction function of Behrens and Bühring (1982). For each transition, 100 values of \(f\) were computed over the energy range and fitted to obtain the tabulated coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The parameterization reproduces the exact \(f\) value to an accuracy of \(\pm 0.01\%\).
- For each transition, 100 values of \(f\) were computed over a range of \(\pm 60\) keV around the known \(Q_{EC}\) value; for \(^{70}\text{Br}\) the range was extended to \(\pm 600\) keV.
- The kinematic recoil correction is \(R(W_0) \simeq 1 - 3W_0/(2M_A)\), where \(M_A\) is the average nuclear mass in electron-mass units.
- The fixed coefficients in the \(f_0\) fit are \(a_2 = -2/15\) and \(a_3 = 1/4\).
- Example parameters for \(^{10}\text{C}\): \(a_0 = 0.0297225\), \(a_1 = -0.1431540\), \(b_0 = 0.01178\%\), \(b_1 = 0.02006\%\), \(b_2 = 0.05203\%\), \(b_3 = -0.00096\%\).
- Example parameters for \(^{74}\text{Rb}\): \(a_0 = 0.0162385\), \(a_1 = 0.0446304\), \(b_0 = 0.44643\%\), \(b_1 = 0.32180\%\), \(b_2 = 0.46490\%\), \(b_3 = -0.00269\%\).

## Context

This work extends the survey of superallowed \(0^+ \to 0^+\) transitions from Hardy and Towner (2009, 2015) and the survey of mirror \(T=1/2\) transitions from Severijns et al. (2008) by providing a practical parameterization for experimentalists. It enables future measurements of improved \(Q_{EC}\) values to be directly converted into high-precision \(f\) values for the determination of \(V_{ud}\) and tests of CKM unitarity, without requiring the user to run the full Dirac-equation code described in Hardy and Towner (2005).
