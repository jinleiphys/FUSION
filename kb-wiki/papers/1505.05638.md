# Nucleon-Antinucleon Annhiliation at Large Nc

> Nucleon-antinucleon annihilation in the large $N_c$ limit of QCD in the Witten regime of fixed velocity is considered with a focus on the spin and isospin dependence of the annihilation cross-section. In general, time-reversal and isospin invariance restricts the annihilation cross-section to depend on 6 independent energy-dependent terms. At large $N_c$, a spin-flavor symmetry emerges in the theory that acts to further restrict the dependence of the annihilation cross-section to three of these terms; the other terms amount to $1/N_c$ corrections. Assuming dominance of the leading order terms, several identities are derived that relate annihilation in different spin-isospin channels. A key prediction is that for unpolarized nucleons in Witten kinematics, the proton-antiproton annihilation cross-section should be equal to the proton-antineutron annihilation cross-section up to corrections of relative order $1/N_c$. Unpolarized nucleon-antinucleon annihilation data appears to be consistent with this expectation.

## Key claim
The paper argues that at large \(N_c\) in the Witten regime of fixed velocity, the spin-flavor dependence of the nucleon-antinucleon annihilation cross-section is restricted to only three of the six terms allowed by time-reversal and isospin invariance, with the other three terms being suppressed as \(1/N_c\) corrections. The key prediction is that for unpolarized nucleons, the proton-antiproton annihilation cross-section equals the proton-antineutron annihilation cross-section up to corrections of relative order \(1/N_c\).

## Method
The authors extend the large \(N_c\) analysis previously applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering to nucleon-antinucleon annihilation, using the emergent contracted SU(4) spin-flavor symmetry of baryons at large \(N_c\). They employ topological soliton models (Skyrme model) as a surrogate for QCD, where classical scattering solutions of hedgehog baryons are requantized via collective coordinates (Wigner \(D\)-matrices) to extract the leading-order spin-isospin dependence of cross-sections associated with bulk properties like energy flow or baryon number. The analysis is restricted to Witten kinematics, where incident momentum scales as \(N_c^1\) and velocity is fixed.

## Key numbers
- The general annihilation cross-section depends on 6 independent energy-dependent terms (\(X_0, X_1, Y_0, Y_1, Z_0, Z_1\)); at large \(N_c\), only 3 terms (\(X_0, Y_1, Z_1\)) contribute at leading order, with the other 3 suppressed as \(1/N_c\) corrections.
- For unpolarized nucleons, the proton-antiproton annihilation cross-section equals the proton-antineutron annihilation cross-section up to corrections of relative order \(1/N_c\).

## Context
This work extends the large \(N_c\) analysis of nucleon-nucleon scattering (Cohen, 2004) to nucleon-antinucleon annihilation, building on Witten's (1979) foundational treatment of baryons and scattering in the large \(N_c\) limit. The emergent spin-flavor symmetry at large \(N_c\) (Dashen, Jenkins, Manohar, 1994-1995) is used to derive model-independent relations. The paper enables future empirical tests of large \(N_c\) predictions using existing unpolarized annihilation data, which the authors note appears consistent with the expectation that proton-antiproton and proton-antineutron cross-sections are equal.
