# The nuclear equation of state probed by $K^+$ production in heavy ion collisions

> The dependence of $K^+$ production on the nuclear equation of state is investigated in heavy ion collisions. An increase of the excitation function of $K^+$ multiplicities obtained in heavy ($Au+Au$) over light ($C+C$) systems when going far below threshold which has been observed by the KaoS Collaboration strongly favours a soft equation of state. This observation holds despite of the influence of an in-medium kaon potential predicted by effective chiral models which is necessary to reproduce the experimental $K^+$ yields. Phase space effects are discussed with respect to the $K^+$ excitation function.

## Key claim

The ratio of $K^+$ multiplicities in heavy ($Au+Au$) over light ($C+C$) systems, when going far below the free production threshold, strongly favours a soft nuclear equation of state, even when the repulsive in-medium kaon potential predicted by chiral models is taken into account.

## Method

The authors used the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) transport model with soft and hard Skyrme forces (compression moduli K=200 MeV and 380 MeV, respectively) and momentum dependence adjusted to the empirical optical nucleon-nucleus potential. They included $\Delta$ and $N^*(1440)$ resonances, an in-medium kaon potential based on effective chiral models, and elementary kaon production cross sections from Sibirtsev (1995) and Tsushima (1999). The kaon production was treated perturbatively, and the ratio of $K^+$ multiplicities from $Au+Au$ over $C+C$ reactions was computed for central and inclusive collisions.

## Key numbers

- The compression modulus for the soft EOS is K=200 MeV; for the hard EOS it is K=380 MeV.
- The saturation point of nuclear matter is fixed at $E_B = -16$ MeV and $\rho_{\rm sat}= 0.17~{\rm fm}^{-3}$.
- The free $K^+$ production threshold in free space is $E_{thr}= 1.58$ GeV; calculations start at 0.8 A$\cdot$GeV.
- In central $Au+Au$ reactions at 0.8 A$\cdot$GeV, the average density at kaon production is 1.47 $\rho_{\rm sat}$ for the hard EOS and 1.57 $\rho_{\rm sat}$ for the soft EOS.
- The average shift of the in-medium kaon mass compared to the vacuum value is 55 MeV for the hard EOS and 61 MeV for the soft EOS (a relative shift of 6 MeV).
- The enhancement of the available centre-of-mass energy from multiple scattering is $<\sqrt{s}>=90$ MeV for the soft EOS in $Au+Au$ at 0.8 A$\cdot$GeV; for $C+C$ this effect is reverse ($<\sqrt{s}>=-45$ MeV).
- In $C+C$, the mean densities at kaon creation are $<\rho /\rho_{\rm sat} >=1.46$ (hard) and 1.40 (soft); in $Au+Au$ they are 1.47 (hard) and 1.57 (soft).
- In $Au+Au$, kaons are created at densities up to three times saturation density; in $C+C$, densities above $2\rho_{\rm sat}$ are rarely reached.

## Context

This work extends earlier transport-model studies by Aichelin and Ko (1985), Li and collaborators (1995, 1997), and Cassing and collaborators (1999), which showed that $K^+$ yields are sensitive to the nuclear equation of state but were burdened by uncertainties in elementary cross sections and the role of the pionic channel. The paper uses new data from the KaoS Collaboration (Sturm, 2000) on $K^+$ production far below threshold in $Au+Au$ and $C+C$ systems, and argues that taking the ratio of heavy to light systems cancels many theoretical uncertainties. The results enable a clearer extraction of the nuclear equation of state at high densities (up to $3\rho_{\rm sat}$) and contradict a purely thermal or phase-space-limited explanation for the rise of the ratio, as also supported by the constant ratio of high-energy pions measured by KaoS.
