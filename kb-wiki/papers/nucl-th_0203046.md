# Superfluid Phases of Triplet Pairing

> The phase transitions in dense neutron matter with triplet pairing have been investigated. The spectrum of phases of the $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ model, which adequately describes pairing in this system, is identified and classified with the aid of a separation method for solving BCS gap equations associated with pairing states of arbitrary angular-momentum content. In addition to usual solutions involving a single value of the magnetic quantum number (and its negative), there exist ten real multicomponent solutions. Five of the corresponding angle-dependent order parameters have nodes, and five do not. In contrast to the case of superfluid $^3$He, transitions occur between phases with nodeless order parameters. Results for the temperature dependence of the competition between the various phases have been obtained.

# Superfluid Phases of Triplet Pairing

## Key claim

The paper identifies and classifies the spectrum of superfluid phases of the $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ pairing model in dense neutron matter, finding that in addition to the three single-component solutions there exist ten real multicomponent solutions, and that, unlike in superfluid $^3$He, transitions occur between phases with nodeless order parameters. The results are universal in that the angular properties of the solutions depend only on two energy scales and are independent of the detailed nature of the pairing interaction.

## Method

The authors implement a separation method for solving the BCS gap equations associated with pairing states of arbitrary angular-momentum content, which decomposes the gap components into a shape factor (determined by a nonsingular integral equation) and numerical amplitudes that control the angle-dependence. They then apply a perturbative treatment of the coupling between the $^3P_2$ and $^3F_2$ channels, retaining only the leading nondiagonal contributions, and solve the resulting system of equations for the amplitude ratios $\lambda_1$ and $\lambda_2$ that define the angular structure of the order parameter.

## Key numbers

- The coupling parameter $\eta$ between $^3P_2$ and $^3F_2$ channels varies around 0.3 in the density interval $\rho_0 < \rho < 2\rho_0$ for the in-vacuum neutron-neutron interaction.
- The splitting between upper and lower groups of states is of order 2% in the neutron-matter problem, relative to the absolute pairing energy.
- The gap parameter $\Delta_F(T=0)$ reaches a maximum value near 0.44 MeV at $k_F \simeq 2.1$ fm$^{-1}$ when using the Argonne $v_{18}$ potential with free normal-state single-particle energies.
- At $T \simeq 0.7\,T_c$, the roles of the two lowest-energy groups are interchanged and transitions occur.
- The leading-order approximation in $d_F = \Delta_F/\epsilon_F$ is good to about one part in $10^5$ or $10^6$ for $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ pairing in neutron matter.

## Context

This work extends the pure $^3P_2$ pairing problem solved analytically in earlier work by Clark, Khodel, and Zverev (Refs. [4,6]) to the coupled $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ channel, which is the adequate description of triplet pairing in dense neutron matter. The separation method was introduced in a series of papers by the same group (Refs. [3-8]) to overcome the limitations of standard iterative procedures and the Ginzburg-Landau approach (restricted to temperatures near $T_c$). The results provide a foundation for the more ambitious project of characterizing the superfluid phases of liquid $^3$He, and the predicted phase transitions may ultimately be detectable in the thermal history or rotational dynamics of cooling neutron stars.
