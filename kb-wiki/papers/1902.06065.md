# An in-medium chiral power counting for nuclear matter and some applications

> We review on a chiral power counting for in-medium chiral perturbation theory with nucleons and pions as explicit degrees of freedom coupled to external sources. It allows for a systematic expansion including both local and pion-mediated inter-nucleon interactions. One can identify from this power counting classes of non-perturbative diagrams that require resummation. A non-perturbative method based on Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory was also developed for performing those resummations. This power counting and non-perturbative techniques were firstly applied to calculate the pion self-energy, the pion-decay constants and the quark condensate in nuclear matter up-to-and-including next-to-leading order (NLO) contributions. The cancellation of the contributions at NLO to the pion self-energy and decay constants from in-medium nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions was derived. Some NLO NN contributions survive for the quark condensate due to the quark-mass dependence of the pion mass. Next, we discuss the calculation of the energy density in the nuclear medium by employing the in-medium NN scattering amplitudes. For symmetric and neutron matter it reproduces in good agreement, and without fine tuning, calculations from realistic NN potentials with a model for the three-nucleon interaction. These results are applied to derive the equation of state (EOS) for neutron stars and obtain an upper limit for a neutron mass slightly above 2 solar masses. Our results also fulfill other constraints from the detection of the gravitational waves in the event GW170817, like the upper bound on the maximal mass of a neutron star and the allowed interval for the radius of a 1.4-solar-mass neutron star. The knowledge of the neutron-matter EOS is also employed to give an upper bound of the gravitational constant within the strong gravitational field of a 2 solar-mass neutron star.

## Key claim

The paper presents a chiral power counting for in-medium chiral perturbation theory that systematically accounts for the infrared enhancements of nucleon propagators, which are often counted as \( \mathcal{O}(p^{-2}) \) rather than the standard \( \mathcal{O}(p^{-1}) \), and demonstrates that this counting reproduces nuclear matter and neutron star properties without fine tuning, though the approach is one of several within the field and relies on approximations for nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Method

The author derives an in-medium chiral power counting by considering Feynman diagrams with in-medium generalized vertices (IGVs), where nucleon propagators are always counted as \( \mathcal{O}(p^{-2}) \) to account for non-standard infrared enhancements. A non-perturbative method based on Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory (UChPT) is used to resum classes of non-perturbative diagrams, and this framework is applied to calculate the pion self-energy, pion-decay constants, quark condensate, and the energy density of nuclear matter up to next-to-leading order (NLO). The resulting equation of state for neutron matter is then used to derive neutron star properties.

## Key numbers

*   The upper limit for a neutron star mass is slightly above 2 solar masses, in agreement with the detection of a two-solar mass neutron star by Demorest (2010).
*   The radius of a 1.4-solar-mass neutron star is around 12.6 km, which lies within the interval allowed by the analysis of the GW170817 event.
*   For symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density with Fermi momentum \( \xi \simeq 2 m_\pi \), corrections to the results are expected to be of \( \mathcal{O}(2 m_\pi / \Lambda_\xi) \simeq 0.3 \), where \( \Lambda_\xi = \upsilon \pi f \) is the in-medium expansion scale.
*   The in-medium low-energy scale \( \Lambda_{\rm low} \) is given by \( \Lambda_{\rm low} \simeq (\upsilon \pi f)^2 / (2m) \), which is typically reduced by a factor of 2 to 4 compared to \( \upsilon \pi f \).

## Context

This work extends the in-medium chiral perturbation theory framework of Lacour (2010) by incorporating a power counting that systematically handles infrared enhancements of nucleon propagators, a step forward from earlier approaches that applied vacuum chiral power counting to in-medium calculations, such as those by Fiorilla (2012) and Holt (2013). The results enable the calculation of neutron star equations of state that satisfy constraints from gravitational wave event GW170817 and the existence of two-solar-mass neutron stars, and also provide an upper bound on the gravitational constant in strong gravitational fields.
