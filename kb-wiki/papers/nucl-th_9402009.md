# Systematics of Gamow-Teller strengths in mid-fp-shell nuclei

> We show that the presently available data on the Gamow-Teller (GT) strength in mid-fp-shell nuclei are proportional to the product of the numbers of valence protons and neutron holes in the full fp-shell. This observation leads to important insights into the mechanism for GT quenching and to a simple parametrization of the Gamow-Teller strengths important for electron capture by fp-shell nuclei in the early stage of supernovae.

## Key claim

The total Gamow-Teller (GT+) strength in mid-fp-shell nuclei is proportional to the product of the number of valence protons and the number of neutron holes in the full fp-shell, but this parametrization is derived solely from experiments with even-N targets and its validity for odd-N nuclei remains to be verified; the apparent odd-even dependence in the data is attributed to an experimental excitation energy cutoff rather than a genuine nuclear structure effect.

## Method

The authors analyzed existing (n,p) charge-exchange reaction data from the CHARGEX collaboration at TRIUMF for several fp-shell nuclei, extracting total Gamow-Teller strengths B(GT+) summed to excitation energies of approximately 8 MeV (and in some cases up to 12 MeV). They plotted the experimental B(GT+) values divided by the number of valence protons (Z-20) as a function of the number of valence neutrons (N-20), then fitted the data to a linear parametrization B(GT+) = a * Z_val * (b - N_val) using chi-squared minimization.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit parameters for the parametrization B(GT+) = a * Z_val * (b - N_val): a = (4.55 +/- 0.25) x 10^-2 and b = 19.54 +/- 0.32 with chi^2 = 1.0 per degree of freedom
- With b constrained to 20: a = (4.29 +/- 0.15) x 10^-2 with chi^2 = 1.1 per degree of freedom
- For 48Ti (Z=22, N=26): experimental B(GT+) = 1.31 +/- 0.2, single particle estimate = 4.07, quenching factor q = 3.1 +/- 0.5
- For 56Fe (Z=26, N=30): experimental B(GT+) = 2.85 +/- 0.3, single particle estimate = 10.29, quenching factor q = 3.6 +/- 0.4
- For 70Ge (Z=32, N=38): experimental B(GT+) = 0.84 +/- 0.13, single particle estimate = 2.67, quenching factor q = 3.2 +/- 0.5
- Fitting the same data to a constant quenching factor gives q approximately 3.7 with chi^2 = 2.4 per degree of freedom, a noticeably worse fit than Eq. (2)
- For 45Sc (Z_val=1): experimentally observed quenching q = 1.1, much less than the q = 3.4 predicted by Eq. (2)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Fuller et al. (1982) who recognized the importance of Gamow-Teller resonances for electron capture in supernovae, and addresses criticism by Aufderheide et al. (1993) that using constant quenching factors (q=2) in pre-supernova studies is questionable. The parametrization enables improved estimates of electron capture and beta-decay rates for nuclei between 48Ti and 70Ge (Z=22-32, N approximately 26-38), which govern the early stages of supernova collapse, and suggests that shell model calculations must include the full fp-shell model space to reproduce GT strengths, consistent with Monte Carlo calculations by Koonin et al. (1993) using the Brown-Richter interaction.
