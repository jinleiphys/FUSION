# Thermodynamics of the BPS Skyrme model

> One problem in the application of the Skyrme model to nuclear physics is that it predicts too large a value for the compression modulus of nuclear matter. Here we investigate the thermodynamics of the BPS Skyrme model at zero temperature and calculate its equation of state. Among other results, we find that classically (i.e. without taking into account quantum corrections) the compressibility of BPS skyrmions is, in fact, infinite, corresponding to a zero compression modulus. This suggests that the inclusion of the BPS submodel into the Skyrme model lagrangian may significantly reduce this too large value, providing further evidence for the claim that the BPS Skyrme model may play an important role in the description of nuclei and nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The authors find that, classically (without quantum corrections), the BPS Skyrme model has infinite compressibility and zero compression modulus, which is the opposite of the standard Skyrme model's too-large compression modulus; this suggests that including the BPS submodel in the Skyrme model Lagrangian may significantly reduce the compression modulus to more physical values.

## Method

The authors compute the zero-temperature equation of state for the BPS Skyrme model by introducing external pressure analytically and solving the static field equations for nonzero pressure, using the fact that the static field equations reduce to a constant-pressure condition. They derive the volume and energy as functions of pressure for spherically symmetric compacton solutions, then calculate the compressibility and compression modulus from these thermodynamic relations.

## Key numbers

- For the standard Skyrme model, the compression modulus predicted is much higher than the nuclear matter value of about 230 MeV (the nucleon mass is about 940 MeV, and the simple rescaling argument gives compression modulus equal to nucleon mass divided by baryon number).
- For the BPS Skyrme model with a potential behaving as U ~ xi^alpha near the vacuum, the compression modulus is zero for alpha >= 2 (the compressibility is infinite).
- For the specific potential U = eta (beta=1, corresponding to cubic near-vacuum behavior), the volume as a function of pressure is V(P) proportional to Z = 2( sqrt(pi/2 + P_tilde) - sqrt(P_tilde) ), where P_tilde = P/mu^2.
- For the specific potential U = eta^(2/3) (beta=2/3, corresponding to quadratic near-vacuum behavior), the volume as a function of pressure includes a term proportional to P_tilde ln P_tilde, leading to infinite compressibility at zero pressure.

## Context

This work extends the BPS Skyrme model introduced by Adam et al. (2010) and addresses a known problem of the standard Skyrme model: its prediction of too large a compression modulus for nuclear matter, as discussed by Manton (2012) and others. The BPS Skyrme model was previously shown to give zero classical binding energies and good agreement with nuclear binding energies after semiclassical quantization (Adam et al., 2013; Marleau, 2012), and the present results suggest it can also resolve the compression modulus problem. The work enables further study of generalized Skyrme models that include all four terms (L_2, L_4, L_0, L_6) to simultaneously describe Roper resonances and nuclear matter compressibility.
