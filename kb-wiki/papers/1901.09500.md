# Forward and backward comparative study of jet properties in pp collisions at \sqrt{s}=7 TeV

> We propose a forward method, based on the PYTHIA6.4, to study theoretically the jet properties in the ultra-relativistic pp collisions. In the forward method, the partonic initial states are first generated with PYTHIA6.4 and then hadronized in the Lund srting fragmentation regime, and finally hadronic jets are constructed from the created hadrons. Jet properties calculated in the forward method for pp collisions at \sqrt{s}=7 TeV are comparable with the corresponding ones calculated with usual anti-k_t algorithm (backward method) in the PYTHIA6.4. The comparison between the results in the backward and forward methods may bring benefit to the understanding of the partonic origin of jets in the backward method.

## Key claim

The authors propose a "forward method" for jet analysis that follows the natural time evolution of a collision (partonic initial state → hadronization → jet construction) and show that the intra-jet particle distributions it produces are comparable to those from the usual anti-\(k_t\) "backward method" in PYTHIA6.4 for pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV, with the caveat that the forward method may help identify the partonic origin of jets and eliminate combinatorial or fake jets.

## Method

The forward method first generates the partonic initial state using PYTHIA6.4 with hadronization switched off, then splits all gluons into quark pairs to form simple strings, and hadronizes each simple string individually via the Lund string fragmentation model (PYSTRF subroutine). Hadrons from each string are grouped by proximity to the two quark ends in transverse momentum space, and a jet is formed from each quark end if the hadrons satisfy the anti-\(k_t\) distance criterion with \(R=0.6\) in rapidity-azimuth phase space. The backward method uses the standard anti-\(k_t\) algorithm on the final hadronic state from PYTHIA6.4, with charged particles of \(p_T > 300\) MeV/c and \(|\eta|<2.5\), distance parameter \(R=0.6\), and jets accepted with \(p_T > 4\) GeV/c and \(|y|<1.9\).

## Key numbers

- The K factor in PYTHIA6.4 was tuned to 4 (from the default 1.5) to reproduce CMS data on charged particle transverse momentum and pseudorapidity distributions.
- Charged particles with transverse momentum \(p_T > 300\) MeV/c and pseudorapidity \(|\eta| < 2.5\) were used in the backward method jet reconstruction.
- Jets were accepted with \(p_T > 4\) GeV/c and \(|y| < 1.9\), and divided into five bins: 4-6, 6-10, 10-15, 15-24, and 24-40 GeV/c.
- The distance parameter \(R = 0.6\) was used in both methods.

## Context

This work extends the use of PYTHIA6.4 (Sjostrand et al., 2006) for jet studies by introducing a forward method that directly links final hadronic jets to their initial partonic strings, contrasting with the standard anti-\(k_t\) algorithm (Cacciari et al., 2008) used in experimental analyses by ATLAS (2011), CMS, and ALICE. The comparison between the two methods aims to address the key problem of identifying jets from hard-scattered partons and eliminating combinatorial or fake jets, as discussed in Connors et al. (2017). The forward method could serve as a theoretical reference for backward-method results and may enable cleaner jet studies in heavy-ion collisions.
