# Helical ordering in the ground state of spin-one color superconductors as a consequence of parity violation

> We investigate spin-one color superconductivity of a single quark flavor using the Ginzburg-Landau theory. First we examine the classic analysis of Bailin and Love and show that by restricting to the so-called inert states, it misses the true ground state in a part of the phase diagram. This suggests the use of the more general, noninert states in particular within three-flavor quark matter where the color neutrality constraint imposes stress on the spin-one pairing and may disfavor the symmetric color-spin-locked state. In the second part of the paper we show that, in analogy to some ferromagnetic materials, lack of space-inversion symmetry leads to a new term in the Ginzburg-Landau functional, which favors a spatially nonuniform long-range ordering with a spiral structure. In color superconductors, this new parity-violating term is a tiny effect of weak-interaction physics. The modified phase diagram is determined and the corresponding ground states for all the phases constructed. At the end, we estimate the coefficient of the new term in the free energy functional, and discuss its relevance for the phenomenology of dense quark matter.

## Key claim

The true ground state of a single-flavor spin-one color superconductor is not always one of the "inert" states considered in the classic analysis of Bailin and Love (1984); in a region of the phase diagram where the quartic coupling \(d_3 > d_2 > 0\), the absolute minimum of the free energy is instead a noninert state of the \(\varepsilon\) type. Additionally, parity violation from weak interactions introduces a new term in the Ginzburg-Landau functional that favors a spatially nonuniform helical (spiral) ordering, analogous to the Dzyaloshinsky-Moriya effect in ferromagnets.

## Method

The author classifies all possible forms of the order parameter (a complex \(3\times3\) matrix) for single-flavor spin-one color superconductivity using the symmetry group \(U(3)_L \times SO(3)_R\). A set of inequalities between the three independent quartic invariants \(\mathcal A, \mathcal B, \mathcal C\) is derived and used to find the absolute minimum of the Ginzburg-Landau free energy density (without the parity-violating term) as a function of the quartic couplings \(d_2, d_3\). The parity-violating Dzyaloshinsky-Moriya (DM) term is then added to the functional; its effect is analyzed by expanding the order parameter in Fourier modes and estimating the free energy, following the method of Binz (2006) for complex ferromagnets. The coefficient of the DM term is estimated from weak-interaction physics in the weak-coupling limit.

## Key numbers

* The inequalities for the quartic invariants are: \(\frac13\mathcal A \leq \mathcal B \leq \mathcal A\), \(0 \leq \mathcal C \leq \mathcal B\), \(\frac23\mathcal A \leq \mathcal B + \mathcal C\), and for \(\mathcal C \leq \frac19\mathcal A\), \(\sqrt{\mathcal A} \leq \sqrt{\mathcal C} + \sqrt{\mathcal B - \mathcal C}\).
* The effective quartic coupling for the \(\varepsilon\) phase ground state is \(\bar d_\varepsilon = d_1 + \frac{d_2(d_2+d_3)}{2d_2+d_3}\).
* The minimum free energy density for a uniform ground state is \(\mathcal F_{\text{min}} = -\frac{b^2}{4\bar d}\).
* For the helical state in the toy model (complex ferromagnet), the minimum free energy density is \(\mathcal F_{\text{min}} = -\frac{b^2}{4d} - \frac{c^2}{4a_1}\mathcal M\), where \(\mathcal M\) is the squared norm of the order parameter.
* The wavevector magnitude that minimizes the free energy in the toy model is \(|\vek k|_{\text{min}} = c / 2a_1\).

## Context

This work extends the classic Ginzburg-Landau analysis of spin-one color superconductivity by Bailin and Love (1984), who restricted their study to "inert" states and missed the \(\varepsilon\) phase as the true ground state in a part of the phase diagram. The classification of order parameters builds on the work of Schmitt (2004) for weak-coupling QCD, but here the order parameter is a local quantity rather than a momentum-dependent gap function. The introduction of a parity-violating DM term, inspired by the theory of helical magnetism in materials like MnSi (Dzyaloshinsky, 1958; Moriya, 1960), provides a mechanism for spatially nonuniform (helical) ordering in dense quark matter. This enables future studies of the phenomenology of compact stars, where spin-one pairing may occur when the Fermi surface mismatch is too large for two-flavor (2SC) pairing.
