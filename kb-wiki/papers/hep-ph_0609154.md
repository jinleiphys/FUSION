# Form factors in B->f0(980) and D->f0(980) transitions from dispersion relations

> Within the dispersion relation approach we give the double spectral representation for space-like and time-like B-> f_0(980) and D-> f_0(980) transition form factors in the full q^2 range. The spectral densities, being the input of the dispersion relations, are obtained from a triangle diagram in the relativistic quark model.

# Form factors in B→f0(980) and D→f0(980) transitions from dispersion relations

## Key claim

The authors provide a double spectral representation for B→f0(980) and D→f0(980) transition form factors valid over the full q² range, but only under the assumption that the f0(980) is treated exclusively as a conventional ¯qq state, neglecting higher Fock state contributions such as ¯q²q² configurations.

## Method

The transition form factors F₊(q²) and F₋(q²) are derived from a triangle diagram in a relativistic constituent quark model using dispersion relations. The double spectral representation is obtained by applying Landau-Cutkosky rules to put internal quarks on their mass shells, and the spectral densities Δ±(s₁,s₂,q²;m₁,m₂,m₃) are computed from the double discontinuities of the triangle diagram. The bound-state wavefunctions are taken as Gaussian forms with a size parameter α determined phenomenologically from meson decay constants or from fitting D(Dₛ)→f₀(980)X branching fractions.

## Key numbers

- f0(980) mass: m = 980 ± 10 MeV
- f0(980) width: Γ = 70 ± 30 MeV
- The form factors are computed for the full momentum range including q² = m_K², the kaon mass squared, needed for B→f0(980)K decays
- The wavefunction normalization includes factors of the form ξ±(s) = √2 (8πα/(s μ²))^(3/4) √((s² - (m_q² - m_¯q²)²)/(s - (m_q ± m_¯q)²))

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach previously applied to pseudoscalar-to-pseudoscalar transitions (e.g., B→π) by Melikhov (year not specified in text) to the case of pseudoscalar-to-scalar transitions involving the f0(980). The authors note that the f0(980) structure remains debated, with Jaffe (year not specified) advocating a ¯q²q² state while Cheng, Chua and Yang (year not specified) suggest the ¯qq component dominates in energetic B decays. The results aim to reduce uncertainties in hadronic matrix elements for factorization approaches to B and D decays into scalar mesons, where non-perturbative QCD effects are the dominant source of error.
