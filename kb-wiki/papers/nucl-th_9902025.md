# Pre-critical Chiral Fluctuations in Nuclear Medium -- precursors of chiral restoration at $ρ_{B}\not=0 --

> It is shown that an enhancement in the spectral function near 2$m_π$ threshold in the I=J=0 channel is a good signal of the partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear medium. Several experiments are discussed to detect the enhancement, which uses hadron-nucleus and photo-nucleus reactions.

# Pre-critical Chiral Fluctuations in Nuclear Medium, precursors of chiral restoration at ρ_B ≠ 0

## Key claim
An enhancement in the spectral function near the 2m_π threshold in the I=J=0 channel is a good signal of partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear medium, but this enhancement can appear even before the σ-meson mass and pion mass become degenerate (i.e., before full chiral restoration), and the description in terms of meson mass and width may become inadequate due to strong interactions with environmental particles.

## Method
The authors calculate the spectral function ρ_σ(ω) of the σ-meson in nuclear matter using the SU(2) linear σ-model with one-loop corrections, treating the meson-loop effects and baryon density as perturbations to vacuum quantities. They parametrize the chiral condensate in nuclear matter as ⟨σ⟩ = σ_0 Φ(ρ), where Φ(ρ) = 1 − Cρ/ρ_0 in the linear density approximation, and examine the spectral function for various values of Φ corresponding to different densities up to ρ_0 = 0.17 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ_0 = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Vacuum pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV
- Vacuum pion mass: m_π = 140 MeV
- Plausible value of Φ(ρ = ρ_0) = ⟨σ⟩/σ_0 at normal nuclear density: 0.7 to 0.9
- Two values of the σ-meson peak mass used in calculations: m_σ^(peak) = 550 MeV and m_σ^(peak) = 750 MeV
- Estimated incident kinetic energy of deuteron in (d, ³He) reactions to cover spectral function range 2m_π < ω < 750 MeV: 1.1 GeV < E < 10 GeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Hatsuda and Kunihiro, 1994) that partial chiral restoration in nuclei leads to softening of the scalar-isoscalar excitation and partial degeneracy of the σ-meson with the pion. It builds on a finite-temperature calculation by Chiku and Hatsuda (1998) showing spectral enhancement at the two-pion threshold, now applied to finite baryon density. The results suggest that the CHAOS collaboration's observation of increasing π⁺π⁻ yield near the 2m_π threshold with nuclear mass number A may already provide experimental evidence for partial chiral restoration, and the work enables future experimental searches using hadron-nucleus and photo-nucleus reactions, including 2π⁰ → 4γ measurements and (d, ³He) reactions.
