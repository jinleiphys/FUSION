# Three Body Bound State Calculations without Angular Momentum Decomposition

> The Faddeev equations for the three body bound state are solved directly as three dimensional integral equation without employing partial wave decomposition. The numerical stability of the algorithm is demonstrated. The three body binding energy is calculated for Malfliet-Tjon type potentials and compared with results obtained from calculations based on partial wave decomposition. The full three body wave function is calculated as function of the vector Jacobi momenta. It is shown that it satisfies the Schrödinger equation with high accuracy. The properties of the full wave function are displayed and compared to the ones of the corresponding wave functions obtained as finite sum of partial wave components. The agreement between the two approaches is essentially perfect in all respects.

## Key claim

The Faddeev equations for the three-body bound state can be solved directly as a three-dimensional integral equation without partial wave decomposition, yielding binding energies and wave functions that agree "essentially perfectly" with traditional partial-wave-based calculations, but with a simpler numerical implementation.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equation for three identical bosons interacting via Malfliet-Tjon type potentials directly in momentum space using vector Jacobi momenta, without projecting onto partial waves. The resulting three-dimensional integral equation was discretized on Gaussian grids in the magnitudes p and q and the angle between them, with the two-body t-matrix also computed directly as a function of vector momenta. The eigenvalue equation was solved by iteration, and the full three-body wave function was constructed from the Faddeev component using permutation operators.

## Key numbers

- For the MT-V potential, the three-body binding energy converged to E_t = -7.7365 MeV in the 3D approach, compared to E_t = -7.7366 MeV from partial wave decomposition (l=12), in agreement with the value E_t = -7.7366 MeV from Scheller (1992).
- For the MT-IV potential, the 3D calculation converged to E_t = -25.050 MeV, compared to E_t = -25.057 MeV from partial waves (l=12), in agreement with the value E_t = -25.05 MeV from Kalusche (1995).
- Typical grid sizes of 97 x 97 x 42 points in (p,q,x) were sufficient for 4 significant figures in the binding energy.
- The q-integration cutoff was q_max = 20 fm^{-1}, with p_max = 60 fm^{-1}.
- The expectation value <H> deviated from the eigenvalue by less than 0.1% for both potentials.

## Context

This work extends the direct vector-variable approach previously applied to two-body t-matrices by Elster (1997) and to the Schrodinger equation for three identical particles by Kalusche (1995). It contrasts with traditional Faddeev calculations that require 34 or more partial wave combinations (as in the 1992 benchmark by Picklesimer), and with variational and Green's function Monte Carlo methods that work in configuration space. The approach enables simpler numerical implementation for three-body bound states and provides a full wave function that satisfies the Schrodinger equation pointwise with high accuracy, which the authors note had not been demonstrated before.
