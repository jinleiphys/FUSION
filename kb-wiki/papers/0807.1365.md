# Neutron Electric Dipole Moment at Fixed Topology

> We describe the finite volume effects of CP-odd quantities, such as the neutron electric dipole moment and the anapole moment in the $θ$-vacuum, under different topological sectors. We evaluate the three-point Green's functions for the electromagnetic current in a fixed non-trivial topological sector in order to extract these CP-odd observables. We discuss the role of zero modes in the CP-odd Green's function and show that, in the quenched approximation, there is a power divergence in the quark mass for CP-odd quantities at finite volume.

# Neutron Electric Dipole Moment at Fixed Topology

## Key claim

The neutron electric dipole moment (NEDM) and other CP-odd observables can be extracted from lattice QCD simulations performed within a single fixed topological sector, provided finite-volume corrections are properly accounted for; however, in the quenched approximation, zero-mode contributions cause a power divergence in the quark mass at the chiral limit, making quenched calculations of NEDM unreliable except for algorithm testing.

## Method

The author derives the finite-volume behavior of CP-odd three-point Green's functions for the electromagnetic current in the nucleon, evaluated in a fixed topological sector ν of the QCD θ-vacuum. Using a saddle-point expansion of the partition function and Green's functions around θ=0, expressions are obtained for the two-point nucleon correlator and the three-point correlator with the electromagnetic current, from which the electric dipole form factor F₃(q²) and anapole form factor F_A(q²) can be extracted by combining results from several volumes or topological sectors. The role of zero modes in the quark propagator is analyzed for both quenched and dynamical fermion cases.

## Key numbers

- The experimental bound on the QCD θ parameter is θ < 10⁻⁹, based on the non-observation of the neutron electric dipole moment.
- The leading finite-volume correction to the CP-odd Green's function in a fixed topological sector is proportional to 1/V, where V is the four-volume.
- In the quenched approximation, zero-mode contributions produce a power divergence in the quark mass m_q: the divergence scales as 1/m_q³ for topological charge |ν|=1 and as 1/m_q⁴ for |ν| ≥ 2.
- The topological susceptibility χ_t = ⟨Q²⟩/V appears as the key parameter controlling the saddle-point expansion, with the expansion parameter ν/(Vχ_t).

## Context

This work extends the fixed-topology formalism previously developed for CP-even quantities such as hadron masses by Brower et al. (2003) to CP-odd observables including the neutron electric dipole moment and anapole moment. It addresses the practical problem that lattice QCD simulations using overlap fermions can become trapped in a single topological sector, as observed by JLQCD and by Streuer (2007) in HMC simulations. The paper shows that, unlike the dynamical fermion case where the fermion determinant cancels zero-mode singularities, quenched calculations of CP-odd quantities suffer from an uncontrolled power divergence in the quark mass, rendering such calculations unreliable. The work also discusses a proposed relation between the electric dipole form factor F₃(q²) and the anomalous magnetic form factor F₂(q²) from Brodsky, Gardner, and Hwang (2006), noting that the present formalism does not require proportionality between these form factors.
