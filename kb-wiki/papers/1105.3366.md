# When hadrons become unstable: a novel type of non-analyticity in chiral extrapolations

> Hadron masses show a specific dependence on the quark masses. Therefore, the variation of these masses can cause a resonance in a hadronic scattering amplitude to become a bound state. Consequently, the amplitude exhibits a non-analytic behavior at this transition. Crossed amplitudes, where the resonance can be exchanged in the t-channel, can be shown to exhibit the same phenomenon by s-->t analytic continuation. This entails possible kinks in lattice quark mass extrapolations needed to compute hadronic observables.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that when a hadronic resonance becomes a bound state as the quark (pion) mass is varied, the scattering amplitude and related observables (such as form factors) develop a non-analyticity (kink) in the quark-mass dependence, which is model-independent and persists after analytic continuation from the time-like to the space-like region.

## Method

The authors study the ρ-meson contribution to the ππ scattering amplitude and the pion electromagnetic form factor at one-loop order in a simple model with derivative coupling, using on-shell unitarization and dimensional regularization. They then generalize the result using an Omnès representation for the form factor, which expresses it as a dispersion integral over the ππ scattering phase shift, and show that the non-analyticity appears when the ρ mass equals the two-pion threshold.

## Key numbers

- The ρ meson becomes bound at a pion mass of approximately 430 MeV (from the one-loop model, Fig. 3 caption).
- The K* resonance should become bound at mπ ≈ 350 MeV (from the text).
- The low-energy constant c1 = (mK* - mρ)/(mK² - mπ²) = 0.51 GeV⁻¹ (from Appendix 2).
- The bare mass parameter m̄0ρ = 0.707 GeV and m̄0ρ′ = 1.13 from fitting lattice data (Appendix 1).
- The crossing condition c1 ≤ 1/mρ0 requires mρ0 ≤ 1960 MeV (Appendix 2), which is satisfied since mρ0 ≈ 700 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the quark-mass dependence of the ρ-meson mass itself (Hanhart 2008, Nebreda 2010) to show that the non-analyticity affects all hadronic observables, including form factors and charge radii. It complements finite-volume effects discussed by Bernard (2009) and provides a model-independent framework using the Omnès representation (Gasser 1990, Oller 2007). The results are relevant for lattice QCD extrapolations to physical quark masses, particularly for the scalar pion form factor where the effect should appear directly in the function rather than its derivative.
