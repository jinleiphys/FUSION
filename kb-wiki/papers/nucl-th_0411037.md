# Microscopic analysis of T=1 and T=0 proton-neutron correlations in $N=Z$ nuclei

> The competition between the isovector (T=1) and isoscalar (T=0) proton-neutron ($p-n$) correlations in $N=Z$ nuclei is investigated by calculating their correlation energies with a realistic effective interaction which reproduces observed nuclear properties very well, in a strict shell model treatment. It is shown in the realistic shell model that the double-differences of binding energies $(B(A+pn:T) + B(A) - B(A+p) - B(A+n))$ ($B(A)$ being the binding energy) are good indicators of the T=1 and T=0 $p-n$ correlations. Each of them, however, originates in plural kinds of correlations with T=1 or T=0.

## Key claim

The double-differences of binding energies \(-\delta^2V_{pn}(T=0)\) and \(-\delta^2V_{pn}(T=1)\) are good indicators of T=0 and T=1 proton-neutron correlations in N=Z nuclei, but each indicator originates in plural kinds of correlations with T=0 or T=1 rather than a single type of pairing correlation.

## Method

The authors performed strict spherical shell-model calculations using the realistic effective USD interaction for sd-shell N=Z nuclei from 20Ne to 32S. They separated the T=0 proton-neutron interactions into an average monopole field \(V^{T=0}_{mp}\) and residual interactions \(V^{T=0}_{res}\), then calculated the expectation values (interaction energies) of \(V^{T=0}_{mp}\), \(V^{T=0}_{res}\), and \(V^{T=1}\) in the lowest T=0 and T=1 states. They evaluated the double-differences of binding energies \(-\delta^2V_{pn}(T=0)\) and \(-\delta^2V_{pn}(T=1)\) and decomposed the contributions from each interaction component.

## Key numbers

- The force strength \(k^0\) extracted from the USD interaction is 2.8 MeV for 22Na, giving a bonus of 2.8 MeV to T=0 states against T=1 states in that nucleus.
- The empirical symmetry energy coefficient \(a_T \approx 134.4 (1 - 1.52 A^{-1/3})\) from the Duflo mass formula explains the A-dependent value of \(\delta^2V_{pn}(T=0)\).
- The odd-even mass difference expressed as \(5.18A^{-1/3}\) in the mass formula corresponds well with \(\delta^2V_{pn}(T=1)/2\).
- For the sd-shell nuclei 24Mg and 28Si, the quantity \(-\delta^2V_{2p2n}/4\) evaluates the T=0 p-n correlation energy, with values smaller than those of \(-\delta^2V_{pn}(T=0)\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of T=1 and T=0 pairing competition in N=Z nuclei by Poves (1998), Martinez-Pinedo (1996), and Satula (1997), which used large-scale shell-model calculations but did not compare total correlation energies of T=0 and T=1 interactions. The authors previously showed (Kaneko, 2004) that the competition can be approximately explained with a T=1, J=0 pairing force and a T=0 p-n force. The present analysis using realistic effective interactions reveals that the monopole field \(V^{T=0}_{mp}\) makes a large contribution to binding energy and that the double-difference indicators contain contributions from multiple correlation types, not just pairing. This enables future studies to properly account for the symmetry energy when evaluating T=0 correlations, avoiding the omission of T=0 p-n interaction contributions that occurs in approaches like that of Macchiavelli (2000).
