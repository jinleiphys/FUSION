# Correlation Effects on the Charge Radii of Exotic Nuclei

> The structures and distributions of light nuclei are investigated within a microscopic correlation model. Two particle correlations are responsible for the scattering of model particles either to low momentum- or to high momentum-states. The low momentum states form the model space while the high momentum states are used to calculate the G-matrix. The three and higher order particle correlations do not play a role in the latter calculation especially if the correlations induced by the scattering operator are of sufficient short range. They modify however, via the long tail of the nuclear potential, the Slater determinant of the (A) particles by generating excited Slater's determinants.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including three-body (S₃) long-range correlations in the Boson Dynamic Correlation Model (BDCM) increases the calculated charge radii of exotic nuclei like ⁶He and lithium isotopes compared to models that treat only two-body correlations, but this effect is conditional on the long-range component of the nuclear potential generating excited Slater determinants.

## Method

The authors apply the Boson Dynamic Correlation Model (BDCM) to light nuclei, starting from a Hamiltonian with a singular nucleon-nucleon potential. They separate the two-body potential into short-range and long-range components, using the short-range part to define an effective Hamiltonian via the e^(iS) unitary-model operator truncated at two-body correlations (S₂). The long-range component is treated by solving non-linear equations of motion (EoM) for configuration-mixing wave functions that include 2p and 3p1h states, with higher-order (4p2h) terms linearized via a generalized linearization approximation (GLA). The eigenvalue equations are solved self-consistently using cluster factorization theory (CFT), and spurious center-of-mass components are removed.

## Key numbers

*   Charge radius of ⁶He calculated with BDCM: 2.25 fm (this work, including folded diagrams).
*   Charge radius of ⁶He calculated with BDCM without folded diagrams: 2.39 fm (this work).
*   Charge radius of ⁶He from two correlated 1s₁/₂ protons only: 2.06 fm (this work).
*   Charge radius of ⁶Li calculated with BDCM: 2.55 fm, reproducing the experimental value of 2.55 fm from electron scattering (Ref. [23]).
*   Charge radius of ⁷Li calculated with BDCM: 2.41 fm, compared to the experimental value of 2.39 fm (GSI) and 2.43 fm (Exp.+Theo.).
*   Charge radius of ⁸Li calculated with BDCM: 2.40 fm, compared to the experimental value of 2.30 fm (GSI).
*   Charge radius of ⁹Li calculated with BDCM: 2.42 fm, compared to the experimental value of 2.22 fm (GSI).
*   Charge radius of ¹¹Li calculated with BDCM: 2.67 fm, compared to the experimental value of 2.47 fm (GSI).
*   Experimental charge radius of ⁶He from isotopic shift (IS) theory: 2.054 ± 0.014 fm (Wang 2004).

## Context

This work extends the unitary-model operator approach of Villars (1963) and Shakin (1966, 1968) by including long-range three-body correlations (S₃) that are neglected in standard no-core shell model calculations (Navratil 1998) and quantum Monte Carlo methods (Pieper 2001). The BDCM results for lithium isotopes are consistently larger than those from cluster models (Wurzer 1997, Funada 1994, Varga 1994) and the isotopic-shift theory (Wang 2004), which the authors attribute to the inclusion of S₃ correlations. The method enables future reevaluation of isotopic-shift theory for extracting charge radii from two-photon experiments, and the authors suggest applying the dynamic correlation model to atomic systems (Helium and Lithium) as a next step.
