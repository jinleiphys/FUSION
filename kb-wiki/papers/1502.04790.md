# Energy-dependence of skin-mode fraction in $E1$ excitations of neutron-rich nuclei

> We have extensively investigated characters of the low-energy $E1$ strengths in $N>Z$ nuclei, by analyzing the transition densities obtained by the HF+RPA calculations with several effective interactions. Crossover behavior has been confirmed, from the skin mode at low energy to the $pn$ mode at higher energy. Decomposing the $E1$ strengths into the skin-mode, $pn$-mode and interference fractions, we show that the ratio of the skin-mode strength to the full strength may be regarded as a generic function of the excitation energy, insensitive to nuclides and effective interactions, particularly beyond Ni.

## Key claim

The ratio of skin-mode strength to full E1 strength may be regarded as a generic function of excitation energy, insensitive to nuclides and effective interactions, particularly beyond Ni. This insensitivity is further clarified by fitting parameters of a model function for the ratio, though certain fluctuation exists in O and Ca isotopes.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock plus random-phase-approximation (HF+RPA) calculations with several effective interactions (SkI2, D1S, D1M, M3Y-P6, M3Y-P7) for spherical N>Z nuclei. They decomposed the E1 transition matrix elements into skin-mode, pn-mode, and interference fractions via the transition densities, using a criterion with λ_s=0.05 to classify the transition density at each radial point. The ratio R_skin(ω) was fitted to a Fermi-function model with parameters ω_cr and D_cr for each nuclide and interaction.

## Key numbers

- For the decomposition criterion, the threshold value λ_s = 0.05 was adopted.
- The fitted parameters ω_cr and D_cr were extracted for 10 nuclei: 22,24O, 52,60,70Ca, 68,84,86Ni, 132Sn, and 208Pb.
- The extracted ω_cr values are quite stable from 68Ni to 208Pb, surprisingly insensitive to the effective interactions.
- Fluctuation of the extracted D_cr values is not significantly large.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of transition densities in doubly-magic nuclei (Nakada, Inakura, Sawai 2013) to a broader set of nuclei and interactions. The low-energy E1 strengths studied are relevant to the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) observed experimentally in many N>Z nuclei (Savage, Aumann, Zegers 2013) and predicted systematically (Inakura, Nakatsukasa, Yabana 2009). The generic energy-dependence of the skin-mode fraction may help extract skin-mode strengths from measurements and could be correlated to the slope parameter of the nuclear symmetry energy L, which is relevant to neutron star structure.
