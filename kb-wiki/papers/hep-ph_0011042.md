# New Anomalous Exchange in Regge Phenomenology and Hard Diffraction

> A new mechanism for hard diffraction based on the anomalous $f_1$ trajectory exchange, which we identify as the odd signature partner of the Pomeron, is suggested. We calculate the contribution of the $f_1$ exchange to elastic and dissociative electromagnetic production of vector mesons and show that it gives a dominant contribution to the differential cross sections at large momentum transfers.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the anomalous $f_1$ trajectory, identified as the odd-signature partner of the Pomeron, gives a dominant contribution to the differential cross sections for elastic and dissociative electromagnetic production of vector mesons at large momentum transfers, where the conventional soft Pomeron exchange fails to describe the data.

## Method

The authors calculate the contribution of the $f_1$ exchange to vector meson photoproduction using a Regge phenomenology approach, where the effective $f_1$-quark vertex is treated as point-like and the coupling constant $g_{f_1NN} = 2.5$ is fixed from analyses of the $f_1$ contribution to the proton spin. The differential cross sections for elastic production are computed using a flavor-singlet axial vector form factor of the nucleon, while for dissociative production the proton form factor is replaced by parton distribution functions from LO GRV98.

## Key numbers

- The $f_1$ trajectory has intercept $\alpha_{f_1}(0) \approx 1$ and slope $\alpha_{f_1}^\prime \approx 0$ (with a variant using $\alpha_{f_1}^\prime = 0.025$ GeV$^{-2}$ shown for comparison).
- The soft Pomeron trajectory used for comparison is $\alpha_P^{\rm soft}(t) = 1.08 + 0.25t$.
- The hard Pomeron trajectory proposed by others is $\alpha_P^{\rm hard} = 1.4 + 0.1t$.
- The coupling constant used is $g_{f_1NN} = 2.5$, obtained from proton spin analyses.
- The parameters for the Pomeron-quark coupling are $\beta_u = \beta_d = 2$ GeV$^{-1}$, $\beta_s = 1.5$ GeV$^{-1}$, and $s_0 = 4$ GeV$^2$.
- The differential cross section for $\rho$ meson photoproduction in proton dissociation at $W = 100$ GeV shows a $1/t^3$ dependence, contrasting with the $1/t^4$ dependence expected from pQCD.
- Experimental data cover energies $W \approx 100$ GeV and momentum transfers up to $-t \approx 2$ GeV$^2$ in elastic production and up to $-t \approx 11$ GeV$^2$ in low-mass dissociation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Kochelev et al., 2000) that the anomalous $f_1$ exchange can describe both elastic hadron-hadron and $\rho/\phi$ photoproduction cross sections consistently. It contradicts the conventional soft Pomeron exchange model (Landshoff and Nachtmann, 1987) which fails to describe HERA data at large $|t|$, and offers an alternative to the hard Pomeron approach (Donnachie and Landshoff, 2000). The results enable further tests of the $f_1$ exchange hypothesis through spin-dependent cross section measurements and provide a framework for understanding the universal behavior of elastic $pp$ and $p\bar p$ scattering at large momentum transfers.
