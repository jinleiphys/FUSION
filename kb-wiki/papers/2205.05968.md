# Analysis of 2-Body Central Events for $^{129}Xe+^{nat}Sn$ from $8A$ $MeV$ up to $18A$ $MeV$ and for $^{129}Xe+^{197}Au$ at $15A$ $MeV$ and $18A$ $MeV$

> A study of medium-mass heavy-ion reactions leading to two fragments in the exit channel from barrier to 18A MeV is proposed. A special focus is made on fission and quasi-fission for events with two fragments ($Z\geq10$) in the exit channel selected. Reactions induced by $^{129}Xe$ projectiles on $^{nat}Sn$ and on $^{197}Au$ at energies ranging from $8A$ MeV to $18A$ MeV were analyzed. Using the fragment ($Z\geq10$) multiplicity equal to 2, fission and quasi-fission events were studied for the lowest beam energies using the fission fragment charge distributions, the total kinetic energy distribution (TKE) and its standard deviation $σ_{TKE}$. For the lighter system it is still possible to observe fission events from incomplete fusion. At variance, for the heavier system, Xenon on gold target only quasi-fission is evidenced. The study of the events characterized by two fragments in the final channel shows that fission, related to fusion in the entrance channel, disappears around 20A MeV for both systems. At lower energies (8A, 12A and 15A MeV) for the Sn target, an evolution with increasing energies towards an asymmetric fission mode is displayed in the fragment charge distributions. This trend can be attributed to the increasing angular momentum as indicated by the out-of-plane angular distribution for light charged particles. The same effect is also observed in the case of the gold target at 15A MeV. However, for this heavier system, a strong memory of the entrance channel leading to quasi-fission is evidenced. A comparison with the Viola systematics, moreover, shows a deviation, greater for the heavier system than for $^{129}Xe+^{nat}Sn$ system. A complete understanding of the above results would certainly require precise model calculations at these energies.

## Key claim

Fission (related to fusion in the entrance channel) disappears around 20A MeV for both the Xe+Sn and Xe+Au systems, but for the heavier Xe+Au system only quasi-fission is evidenced, with a strong memory of the entrance channel, whereas for the lighter Xe+Sn system fission events from incomplete fusion are still observable at the lowest beam energies.

## Method

Reactions induced by 129Xe projectiles on natSn targets at 8A, 12A, 15A, and 18A MeV and on 197Au targets at 15A and 18A MeV were analyzed using data from the INDRA multidetector at GANIL. Events with exactly two fragments (Z ≥ 10) in the exit channel were selected by requiring the total detected charge to be at least 90% of the initial charge and the total pseudo-momentum parallel to the beam to exceed 80%, combined with the condition EIsoMax ≤ 0 to select fusion or incomplete fusion events. Fission and quasi-fission were studied using fission fragment charge distributions, total kinetic energy (TKE) distributions and their standard deviation σTKE, relative velocity distributions, and folding angle distributions.

## Key numbers

- For the 129Xe+natSn system, the product of projectile and target charges is ZPZT = 2700; for 129Xe+197Au, ZPZT = 4266 (both above the quasi-fission threshold of 1600).
- The Bass effective fissility parameter xeff is 0.898 for the Sn target and 1.116 for the Au target; the average fissility parameter xm is 0.676 for Sn and 0.840 for Au (QF threshold at xm > 0.68, QF dominance at xm > 0.765).
- Mean excitation energies per nucleon for the Xe+Sn system range from 1.24 MeV/nucleon at 8A MeV to 3.43 MeV/nucleon at 18A MeV; for Xe+Au, 2.16 MeV/nucleon at 15A MeV and 2.63 MeV/nucleon at 18A MeV.
- The Viola systematics relative velocity is approximately 2.7 cm/ns; for Xe+Sn at 8A MeV the mean relative velocity is very close to this value, while for Xe+Au at both 15A and 18A MeV the departure from Viola is more evident.
- For Xe+Au, the TKE values are roughly 100 MeV higher than for Xe+Sn at the same incident energies.

## Context

This work extends the study of reaction mechanisms for the nearly symmetric 129Xe+natSn system using the EIsoMax observable introduced by Manduci et al. (2020) to select fusion events. It compares the lighter system with the heavier 129Xe+197Au system to investigate the role of system mass on the competition between fusion-fission and quasi-fission, building on the extra-push model of Bass (1974) and the average fissility parameter criterion of du Rietz et al. (2013). The results show a smooth transition from symmetric to asymmetric fission with increasing beam energy for the Sn target, attributed to increasing angular momentum, while for the Au target quasi-fission dominates with a strong entrance-channel memory. The authors note that precise model calculations at these energies are needed for a complete understanding.
