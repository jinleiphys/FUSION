# Quenched Static force from generalized Wilson loops with gradient flow

> We compute the static force on the lattice in the quenched case directly through generalized Wilson loops. We modify the Wilson loop by inserting an $E$-field component on one of the temporal Wilson lines. However, chromo-field components prevent us from performing the continuum limit properly, hence, we use gradient flow to renormalize the field insertion. As a result, we obtain continuum results and compare them to perturbative expression to extract $Λ_0$, and we predict the value $\sqrt{8t_0} Λ_{\overline{\textrm{MS}}}^{n_f=0} =0.629^{+22}_{-26}$. This work serves as preparation for similar operators with field insertions required in nonrelativistic effective field theories.

## Key claim

The paper claims that gradient flow successfully renormalizes the chromoelectric field insertion in generalized Wilson loops, enabling a direct lattice computation of the static force that avoids the systematic uncertainties of numerical differentiation, and yields a value for the QCD scale parameter \(\sqrt{8t_0} \Lambda_{\overline{\textrm{MS}}}^{n_f=0} = 0.629^{+22}_{-26}\) in the quenched approximation, but only after imposing a minimum flow radius \(\sqrt{8\tau_F} > a\) to ensure the renormalization factor \(Z_E \approx 1\) within 1%.

## Method

The static force was computed directly on the lattice by inserting a clover-discretized chromoelectric field component into one temporal Wilson line of a Wilson loop, rather than taking a numerical derivative of the static energy. Gradient flow was applied to renormalize the field insertion and improve the signal-to-noise ratio, and continuum limits were performed at finite flow time using four lattice ensembles with lattice spacings from 0.030 fm to 0.060 fm. The QCD scale \(\Lambda_0\) was extracted by fitting perturbative expressions (at 1-loop and at 3-loop with leading ultrasoft resummation) to the lattice data, either by first extrapolating the force to zero flow time or by fitting directly at finite flow time with a scale choice \(\mu = 1/\sqrt{r^2 + 8b\tau_F}\).

## Key numbers

*   \(\sqrt{8t_0} \Lambda_{\overline{\textrm{MS}}}^{n_f=0} = 0.629^{+22}_{-26}\) from the 3-loop with leading ultrasoft resummation (F3lLus) analysis, reported as the final result.
*   \(\sqrt{8t_0} \Lambda_0^\mathrm{F1l} = 0.814^{+122}_{-73}\) from the 1-loop (F1l) analysis.
*   Cornell fit parameters for the zero-flow-time force at large \(r\): \(A = 0.268(33)\) and \(\sigma t_0 = 0.154(6)\), where \(\sigma\) is the string tension.
*   Lattice spacings: 0.060 fm (\(\beta=6.284\)), 0.046 fm (\(\beta=6.481\)), 0.040 fm (\(\beta=6.594\)), 0.030 fm (\(\beta=6.816\)), with corresponding \(t_0/a^2\) values of 7.868(8), 13.62(3), 18.10(5), and 32.45(7), respectively.

## Context

This work extends the direct-force method proposed by Vairo (2015) and Brambilla (2000) by applying gradient flow to renormalize the field insertion, solving the slow continuum convergence identified by Lepage (1992). The result for \(\Lambda_0\) agrees with previous gradient-flow determinations by Hasenfratz (2023) and Dalla Brida (2019), who found \(\sqrt{8t_0}\Lambda = 0.622(10)\) and \(0.6227(98)\), respectively. The method enables future applications to nonrelativistic effective field theory correlators that require similar field insertions.
