# $(h_{11/2})^2$ alignments in neutron-rich $^{132}$Ba with negative-parity pairs

> The shell-model collective-pair truncation with negative-parity pairs is adopted to study the $(h_{11/2})^2$ alignment in $^{132}$Ba. The proton $(h_{11/2})^2$-alignment state is predicted as an $E\sim4.6$ MeV and $τ\sim 0.5~μ$s isomer with relatively strong E3 decay channels. The oblately deformed neutron $(h_{11/2})^{-2}$ alignment in the yrast band and four negative-parity bands are confirmed, even although two of these negative-parity bands favor the prolate deformation, which directly manifests the $γ$ unstability of $^{132}$Ba.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a proton $(h_{11/2})^2$-alignment state in $^{132}$Ba as an isomer at approximately 4.6 MeV excitation energy with a lifetime of about 0.5 μs, but this prediction requires a ~0.6 MeV downward correction to the raw calculated value because the shell-model framework cannot intrinsically account for the prolate deformation effect that depresses the $h_{11/2}$ proton single-particle energy.

## Method

The authors adopt a shell-model collective-pair truncation approach that includes negative-parity pairs to describe the $(h_{11/2})^2$ alignment in $^{132}$Ba. The model space couples three proton pairs and three neutron-hole pairs in the 50-82 major shell, using four types of pairs: collective $0^+$ and $2^+$ pairs, neutron $5^-$ and $6^-$ pairs, a non-collective $(\pi h_{11/2} \times \pi g_{7/2})^{9^-}$ pair, and a $(\pi h_{11/2} \times \pi h_{11/2})^{10^+}$ pair. The Hamiltonian parameters and electromagnetic operators are taken from Yoshinaga (2012), and the $(\nu h_{11/2})^{-2}$ alignment is represented by the coupling of two $5^-$ and/or $6^-$ pairs rather than an explicit $(\nu h_{11/2} \times \nu h_{11/2})^{10^+}$ pair.

## Key numbers

- The proton $(h_{11/2})^2$-alignment state is predicted at $E \sim 4.6$ MeV (after a 0.6 MeV correction from the raw calculated 5.2 MeV) with $\tau \sim 0.5$ μs isomer lifetime.
- The $I^\pi = 10^+$ isomer in the yrast band has an experimental lifetime of $\tau = 8.94(14)$ ns, assigned as the neutron $(h_{11/2})^{-2}$ alignment with oblate shape.
- Negative-parity bandhead energies: $5^-$ band at 2.12 MeV, $6^-$ band at 2.358 MeV, $7^-$ band at 2.902 MeV, $8^-$ band at 3.105 MeV.
- The main decay branch of the predicted isomer is $E_\gamma \sim 1.3$ MeV to the $I^\pi = 9^-$ level in the $7^-$ band, with B(E3) = 0.109 W.u. for that transition.
- The $(\nu h_{11/2})^{-2}$ alignment occurs at $I = 10\hbar$ in the yrast band, at $I = 14\hbar$ in the $5^-$ and $6^-$ bands, and at $I = 16\hbar$ in the $7^-$ and $8^-$ bands.

## Context

This work extends the shell-model pair-truncation method of Yoshinaga (2012) for Ba isotopes by incorporating negative-parity pairs, following the approach of Lei (2014) for neutron-rich nuclei. The predicted proton $(h_{11/2})^2$ alignment in $^{132}$Ba fills a gap in the systematic competition between proton and neutron $(h_{11/2})^2$ alignments observed in this nuclear region by previous studies (e.g., Paul 1995, Smith 1999). The confirmation of oblate neutron $(h_{11/2})^{-2}$ alignment in prolate-favoring $7^-$ and $8^-$ bands directly manifests the $\gamma$ instability of $^{132}$Ba, enabling future experimental searches for the predicted isomer via its E3 decay channels.
