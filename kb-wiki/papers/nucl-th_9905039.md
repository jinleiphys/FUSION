# Coherence Effects and Neutrino Pair Bremsstrahlung in Neutron Stars

> We calculate the rate of energy radiation by bremsstrahlung of neutrino pairs by baryons in neutron stars employing a transport model in which neutrinos couple to baryons with spectral width. The coherence effects, which are included by computing the self energies with fully dressed propagators, lead to the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression of the neutrino emission rates in the soft neutrino and high temperature limit. A microscopic evaluation of the bremsstrahlung rate by neutrons is carried out using the Brueckner theory of nuclear matter at finite temperatures.

## Key claim

The Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) suppression of neutrino pair bremsstrahlung rates in neutron stars becomes significant when the ratio of the baryon collisional width to temperature (γ/T) exceeds unity, which occurs at temperatures above approximately 5 MeV; at lower temperatures the rates are enhanced relative to the standard Friman and Maxwell (1979) result, contrary to the abstract's implication that suppression applies generally in the soft neutrino and high temperature limit.

## Method

The authors derived the neutrino emissivity from the Kadanoff-Baym transport equation, computing the neutrino self-energies with fully dressed baryon propagators that include a finite spectral width. The baryon self-energies and spectral widths were evaluated microscopically using the self-consistent lowest-order Brueckner theory at finite temperatures with the Paris NN interaction, keeping partial waves with L ≤ 2. The resulting one-loop polarization function was then used to compute the neutrino pair bremsstrahlung rate, with the LPM effect naturally included through the finite width of the baryon spectral functions.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino emissivity formula: ε_{νν̄} = (7.56 × 10¹⁸ erg cm⁻³ s⁻¹) × T₉⁷ × ℐ(T), where T₉ is temperature in units of 10⁹ K
- At saturation density n_s (0.16 fm⁻³) and T = 0.5 MeV: ℐ = 34.0, ratio to Friman and Maxwell result = 2.46 (enhancement)
- At saturation density n_s and T = 5 MeV: ℐ = 18.3, ratio to Friman and Maxwell result = 0.13 (suppression)
- At saturation density n_s and T = 30 MeV: ℐ = 0.4, ratio to Friman and Maxwell result = 5 × 10⁻⁴ (strong suppression)
- At twice saturation density (2n_s) and T = 0.5 MeV: ℐ = 33.0, ratio = 2.38
- At twice saturation density (2n_s) and T = 5 MeV: ℐ = 17.64, ratio = 0.127
- At twice saturation density (2n_s) and T = 30 MeV: ℐ = 2.12, ratio = 4.3 × 10⁻⁴
- Typical onset temperature for LPM suppression: T ∼ 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Knoll and Voskresensky (1995) for the LPM effect in particle production to the specific case of neutrino pair bremsstrahlung in neutron star matter. It provides a microscopic evaluation using Brueckner theory, going beyond the Fermi-liquid interaction approach of Friman and Maxwell (1979) and Voskresensky and Senatorov (1986). The results enable more accurate cooling calculations for neutron stars during their first several thousand years, particularly at temperatures where the LPM suppression becomes important, and highlight the need for future inclusion of quantum interference diagrams to satisfy Ward-Takahashi identities at finite temperature.
