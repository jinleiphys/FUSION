# Coherent photon-photon interactions in very peripheral relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Heavy ions at high velocities provide very strong electromagnetic fields for a very short time. The main characteristics of ultraperipheral relativistic heavy ion collisions are reviewed, characteristic parameters are identified. The main interest in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions at relativistic ion colliders like the LHC is the interactions of very high energy (equivalent) photons with the countermoving (equivalent) photons and hadrons (protons/ions). The physics of these interactions is quite different from and complementary to the physics of the strong fields achieved with current and future lasers.

## Key claim

Ultraperipheral relativistic heavy ion collisions produce electromagnetic fields far exceeding the Schwinger critical field, but these fields act for such an extremely short time (zeptoseconds to yoctoseconds) that perturbation theory remains the appropriate tool, in contrast to the nonperturbative strong-field regime accessible with lasers.

## Method

The paper reviews the characteristics of the electromagnetic pulse generated in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions, using the equivalent photon (Weizsacker-Williams) approximation for one-photon exchange processes and the Glauber approximation for multi-photon exchange processes. The transition from perturbative to nonperturbative regimes is analyzed via a Keldysh-like parameter adapted to heavy ion collisions, and specific examples of photon-nucleus, photon-proton, and photon-photon interactions at RHIC and LHC are discussed.

## Key numbers

- For Pb ions (Z=82), the product Zα ≈ 0.6, where α ≈ 1/137 is the fine structure constant.
- Maximum electric field at impact parameter b = electron Compton wavelength: E_max = E_critical × Zαγ, where E_critical = 1.3×10^16 V/cm (Schwinger critical field).
- Peak power density: ~10^29 (Zαγ)^2 W/cm^2.
- Collision time τ ~ 10^-21 s / γ ≡ 1 zs/γ; for RHIC Au-Au (γ=100) this is a small fraction of a zeptosecond, for LHC Pb-Pb (γ=3000) it is ~10^-24 s ≡ 1 yoctosecond.
- Maximum equivalent photon energy at RHIC (γ=100): ~3 GeV; at LHC Pb-Pb (γ=3400): ~100 GeV (in the lab frame).
- Coulomb parameter η = 49 for LHC Pb-Pb collisions.
- Electron-positron pair production cross section at LHC Pb-Pb: ~100 kb.
- Bound-free pair production cross section scales approximately as Z^7 ln γ / n^3.
- Multiple pair production cross section is of order kilobarns.

## Context

This review extends the comprehensive treatments of ultraperipheral collisions by Baltz et al. (2008) and Baur, Hencken, and Trautmann (2007), providing a qualitative and context-setting overview of key physics aspects. The work contrasts the extremely short, intense electromagnetic pulses in relativistic heavy ion collisions with the longer-duration strong fields achievable with current and future lasers, and identifies the main physics opportunities at the LHC: photoproduction of heavy vector mesons up to the Upsilon, electroweak and beyond-standard-model studies in pp collisions, and the strong-field effect of multiple electron-positron pair production.
