# Symplectic No-core Shell-model Approach to Intermediate-mass Nuclei

> We present a microscopic description of nuclei in an intermediate-mass region, including the proximity to the proton drip line, based on a no-core shell model with a schematic many-nucleon long-range interaction with no parameter adjustments. The outcome confirms the essential role played by the symplectic symmetry to inform the interaction and the winnowing of shell-model spaces. We show that it is imperative that model spaces be expanded well beyond the current limits up through fifteen major shells to accommodate particle excitations that appear critical to highly-deformed spatial structures and the convergence of associated observables.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a no-core shell model with a schematic many-nucleon long-range interaction, using a single adjustable parameter fixed from carbon-12, can describe ground-state rotational bands of intermediate-mass nuclei without further parameter adjustments, but only when model spaces are expanded through fifteen major shells to achieve convergence of collective observables.

## Method

The authors employ the no-core symplectic shell model (NCSpM) with a Hamiltonian that includes a harmonic oscillator term and an exponential quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, H_gamma = H_0 + (chi/2)(exp[-gamma(Q.Q - <Q.Q>_Nn)] - 1)/gamma. Model spaces are down-selected to a single symplectic irreducible representation built over the most deformed 0-hbar-omega bandhead for each nucleus, with calculations performed up through N_max = 12 for A=20, 22, and 24 isotopes. The oscillator strength is set to hbar-omega = 15 MeV for A=20 and A=22 nuclei, and 21 MeV for silicon-24, while the gamma parameter is fixed at 0.74 x 10^-4 from prior carbon-12 analysis.

## Key numbers

* For neon-20, the ground-state rotational band energies and B(E2) transition strengths converge at N_max = 12 using the (8,0) symplectic irrep with 1070 basis states; B(E2; 2+_1 -> 0+_gst.) and B(E2; 4+_1 -> 2+_1) require N_max >= 10 to avoid effective charges.
* For magnesium-20 and oxygen-20, the (4,2) symplectic irrep with 1299 basis states yields matter rms radii of 2.73 fm and 2.73 fm respectively, compared to experimental values of 2.88(4) fm and 2.69(3) fm.
* For neon-20, the NCSpM electric quadrupole moment for the 2+_1 state is -15.69 e fm^2, compared to the experimental value of -23(3) e fm^2.
* For magnesium-22 and neon-22, the (8,2) symplectic irrep with 2900 basis states yields matter rms radii of 2.82 fm for both nuclei; the neon-22 quadrupole moment is -14.90 e fm^2 versus the experimental -17(3) e fm^2.
* For silicon-24, the (10,0) symplectic irrep with 1171 basis states yields a matter rms radius of 2.40 fm and a 2+_1 quadrupole moment of -14.38 e fm^2.

## Context

This work extends the no-core symplectic shell model previously applied to carbon-12 and beryllium-8 by Dreyfuss et al. (2013) and Launey et al. (2012), demonstrating applicability to sd-shell nuclei including those near the proton drip line. The approach builds on the Elliott model (1958) and earlier symplectic model studies by Rosensteel and Rowe (1977, 1980) and Draayer et al. (1984), while providing guidance for the ab initio symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) of Dytrych et al. (2013). The results enable predictions for unstable isotopes such as magnesium-20 and silicon-24 that are relevant for modeling X-ray bursts and novae, where experimental data from radioactive-beam measurements remain sparse.
