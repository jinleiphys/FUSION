# Qu8its for Quantum Simulations of Lattice Quantum Chromodynamics

> We explore the utility of $d=8$ qudits, qu8its, for quantum simulations of the dynamics of 1+1D SU(3) lattice quantum chromodynamics, including a mapping for arbitrary numbers of flavors and lattice size and a re-organization of the Hamiltonian for efficient time-evolution. Recent advances in parallel gate applications, along with the shorter application times of single-qudit operations compared with two-qudit operations, lead to significant projected advantages in quantum simulation fidelities and circuit depths using qu8its rather than qubits. The number of two-qudit entangling gates required for time evolution using qu8its is found to be more than a factor of five fewer than for qubits. We anticipate that the developments presented in this work will enable improved quantum simulations to be performed using emerging quantum hardware.

## Key claim

Using qu8its (d=8 qudits) rather than qubits for quantum simulations of 1+1D SU(3) lattice QCD with Kogut-Susskind fermions reduces the number of two-qudit entangling gates required for time evolution by more than a factor of five, but this advantage depends on recent advances in parallel gate applications and the shorter application times of single-qudit versus two-qudit operations on emerging quantum hardware.

## Method

The authors map the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian for 1+1D SU(3) lattice QCD in axial gauge onto qu8its, where the eight states of a single quark flavor (vacuum, three single-quark states, three di-quark states, and one tri-quark state) are encoded into one qu8it, and anti-quarks are mapped to a separate anti-qu8it. They decompose the resulting Hamiltonian into generators of Givens rotations (X_ij, Y_ij, and Hadamard-Walsh matrices) for SU(8) transformations on each qu8it, and analyze the gate counts for Trotterized time evolution compared to the qubit-based Jordan-Wigner mapping from their prior work Farrell (2022).

## Key numbers

* The number of two-qudit entangling gates required for time evolution using qu8its is more than a factor of five fewer than for qubits.
* The number of units in the quantum register is reduced by a factor of 3 when mapping to qu8its compared to qubits.
* For N_f=2 SU(3) QCD, one spatial site requires 12 qubits in the qubit mapping, while the qu8it mapping requires 2 qu8its per quark flavor.
* For the L=1, N_f=1 system, the Hamiltonian matrix is 64x64, and the B=0 sector contains 20 basis states, with only 4 color-singlet states remaining at low energies in the large-h limit.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior demonstration of qu5its for nuclear many-body systems with pairing interactions Illa (2023) and their qubit-based mapping of 1+1D SU(3) lattice QCD Farrell (2022). It builds on recent developments in qudit trapped-ion systems (Low 2023), superconducting circuits (Nguyen 2023), and photonic systems (Chi 2022), and enables improved quantum simulations of non-Abelian lattice gauge theories on emerging quantum hardware with d>10 capabilities.
