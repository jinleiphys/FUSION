# Renormalization of chiral two-pion exchange NN interactions with Delta-excitations: Central Phases and the Deuteron

> The renormalization of the chiral np interaction with Delta-excitations in intermediate states is considered at next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) for central waves. The inclusion of the Delta-excitation as an explicit degree of freedom improves the convergence properties of the effective field theory results for np scattering with respect to Delta-less theory, and allows the existence of a deuteron bound state in the infinite cut-off limit. The 1S_0 singlet and 3S_1 triplet phase shifts reproduce data for p ~ m_pi. The role of spectral function regularization is also discussed.

## Key claim
The inclusion of explicit Delta-excitation degrees of freedom in chiral two-pion exchange NN potentials improves the convergence of the effective field theory expansion and, unlike the Delta-less theory, allows the deuteron to remain bound in the infinite cut-off limit at both NLO and N2LO, provided the short-distance potential is attractive and renormalization conditions are properly imposed.

## Method
The authors solve the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation in coordinate space for the np system using energy-independent chiral potentials that include Delta-isobar intermediate states at NLO and N2LO, as derived by Kaiser et al. (1997, 1998) and Krebs et al. (2007). Renormalization is implemented via short-distance boundary conditions on the reduced wave functions at a cut-off radius \(r_c\), which is taken to zero; the number of counter-terms is determined by the number of attractive eigen-channels of the van der Waals matrix at short distances. Phase shifts are computed for the \(^1S_0\) singlet and \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) triplet channels, and deuteron properties are extracted from the bound-state solution.

## Key numbers
- The \(N\Delta\) splitting is taken as \(\Delta = 293\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The van der Waals coefficient for the \(^1S_0\) channel at NLO-\(\Delta\) (Fit 1) is \(M C_{6,^1S_0}^{\mathrm{NLO-}\Delta} = -11.138\ \mathrm{fm}^4\); at N2LO-\(\Delta\) (Fit 1) it is \(M C_{6,^1S_0}^{\mathrm{N2LO-}\Delta} = -3.519\ \mathrm{fm}^4\).
- The van der Waals coefficient for the \(^3S_1\) channel at NLO-\(\Delta\) (Fit 1) is \(M C_{6,^3S_1}^{\mathrm{NLO-}\Delta} = -3.932\ \mathrm{fm}^4\); at N2LO-\(\Delta\) (Fit 1) it is \(M C_{6,^3S_1}^{\mathrm{N2LO-}\Delta} = -0.585\ \mathrm{fm}^4\).
- The negative eigenvalues of the van der Waals matrix in the deuteron channel for NLO-\(\Delta\) (Fit 1) are \(-R_+^4 = -5.141\ \mathrm{fm}^4\) and \(-R_-^4 = -3.327\ \mathrm{fm}^4\); for N2LO-\(\Delta\) (Fit 1) they are \(-R_+^4 = -3.823\ \mathrm{fm}^4\) and \(-R_-^4 = 1.035\ \mathrm{fm}^4\).
- The pion mass used is \(m_\pi = 138.03\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and the axial coupling \(g_A = 1.29\) for the OPE piece and \(g_A = 1.26\) for the TPE piece.

## Context
This work extends the coordinate-space renormalization program for chiral NN potentials previously applied to Delta-less theories by Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola (2005, 2005, 2005), where it was found that the NLO potential without Delta made the deuteron unbound due to a repulsive \(1/r^5\) singularity. By including the Delta as an explicit degree of freedom, the leading singularity becomes an attractive \(1/r^6\) van der Waals interaction, restoring the existence of a bound deuteron and improving convergence relative to Delta-less calculations. The results enable a systematic, regulator-independent description of NN scattering and deuteron properties within chiral EFT, and they motivate further study of Delta-based power counting for three-nucleon forces and higher partial waves.
