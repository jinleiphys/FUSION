# Updating the nuclear reaction rate library REACLIB. (I. Experimental reaction rates of the proton-proton chain)

> REACLIB is one of the most comprehensive and popular astrophysical reaction rate libraries. However, its experimentally obtained rates for light isotopes still rely mainly on the Caughlan & Fowler (1988) compilation and have never been updated despite the progress in many relevant nuclear astrophysics experiments. Moreover, due to fitting errors REACLIB is not reliable at temperatures lower than 10^{7}K. In this work we establish the formalism for updating the obsolete Caughlan-Fowler experimental rates of REACLIB. Then we use the NACRE compilation and results from the LUNA experiments to update some important charged-particle induced rates of REACLIB focusing on the proton-proton chain. The updated rates (available also in digital form) can now be used in the low temperature regime (below 10^{7}K) which was forbidden to the old version of REACLIB.

# Updating the nuclear reaction rate library REACLIB. (I. Experimental reaction rates of the proton-proton chain)

## Key claim

The experimentally obtained charged-particle reaction rates for light isotopes in the REACLIB library, which still rely on the Caughlan and Fowler (1988) compilation, can be updated using the NACRE compilation and LUNA experimental data to produce rates that are reliable at temperatures below 10^7 K, a regime where the old REACLIB was unreliable due to fitting errors. The update focuses on the proton-proton chain and provides rates that can now be used in the critical low-temperature region (T_9 < 0.01) that was previously forbidden.

## Method

The author fitted the seven-parameter REACLIB fitting formula to the NACRE analytical approximations for each rate component (non-resonant, narrow resonant, and multi-resonant) over the temperature range where each component plays a non-negligible role, using the Levenberg-Marquardt algorithm for non-linear regression. For the 3He(3He,2p)4He reaction, the author performed numerical integration of the thermonuclear reaction rate integral using the most recent LUNA experimental data, then fitted the REACLIB formula to the resulting tabular data. The quality of the updated rates was assessed by plotting the relative difference between the new REACLIB rates and the NACRE or LUNA reference rates as a function of temperature.

## Key numbers

- The old REACLIB rate for 2H(p,gamma)3He shows a maximum relative difference of 37% compared to the NACRE rate, with roughly 25% relative difference in the solar temperature regime.
- For 2H(d,gamma)4He, the old REACLIB rate in the solar regime can be up to 15% larger than the NACRE rate.
- For 2H(d,n)3He, the old REACLIB rates are up to 12% smaller than the new (NACRE) rates in the solar regime, while at higher temperatures the old rates become larger by up to 90%.
- For 2H(d,p)3H, the old REACLIB rates are up to 10% smaller than the new (NACRE) rates in the solar regime, while at higher temperatures the old rates become larger by up to 90%.
- For 3He(3He,2p)4He, the old REACLIB rate deviates from the LUNA rate by up to 7% in the solar regime, while the new REACLIB rate shows a relative difference of less than 1% compared to the LUNA data.
- For 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be, the new REACLIB rate approximates the NACRE rate with an accuracy of 1% or better in the solar region, while the old REACLIB rate can be up to 2.5% larger than the NACRE one.
- For 6Li(p,gamma)7Be, the old REACLIB rate can differ from the NACRE rate by up to 80% in the solar region, while the new REACLIB discrepancy is always less than 2%.
- For 7Be(p,gamma)8B, the NACRE recommended S-factor at zero energy is S_17(0) = 21 +/- 2 eV b, while a more recent value from Schumann (2003) is 18.6 +/- 1.2 eV b, which would lead to an 11.4% decrease in the relevant non-resonant rate.

## Context

This work extends the REACLIB library, originally based on the Caughlan and Fowler (1988) compilation, by incorporating the NACRE compilation (Angulo et al., 1999) and results from the LUNA experiments (Junker et al., 1998). The update addresses the known limitation that REACLIB could not be safely used at temperatures below T_9 = 0.01 due to fitting errors, which affected pre-main sequence and main-sequence stellar evolution simulations including solar evolution and neutrino studies. The updated rates enable more accurate simulations in the low-temperature regime (0.001 < T_9 < 0.01) relevant to deuterium burning and pre-main sequence stellar evolution, and the formalism established here provides a framework for future more extended updates of REACLIB.
