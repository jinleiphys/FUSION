# Transverse-momentum-dependent gluon distributions of proton within basis light-front quantization

> Gluon transverse-momentum dependent distributions (TMDs) are very important for revealing the internal structure of the proton. They correspond to a variety of experiments and are among the major tasks of the future electron-ion colliders. In this paper, we calculate the T-even gluon TMDs at the leading twist within the Basis Light-front Quantization framework. We employ the light-front wave functions of the proton obtained from a light-front quantized Hamiltonian with Quantum Chromodynamics input determined for its valence Fock sector with three constituent quarks and an additional Fock sector, encompassing three quarks and a dynamical gluon, with a three-dimensional confinement. After obtaining the numerical results we investigate the properties of gluon TMDs by checking whether they satisfy the Mulders-Rodrigues inequalities and investigate the correlations between transverse and longitudinal degrees of freedom. With their connection to unintegrated gluon distributions in mind, we further investigate the limiting behaviours of gluon TMDs in the small-$x$ and large-$x$ regions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first numerical calculation of the four T-even leading-twist gluon transverse-momentum-dependent distributions (TMDs) of the proton within the Basis Light-front Quantization (BLFQ) framework, using a Fock-space truncation that includes both a three-quark sector and a sector with three quarks plus one dynamical gluon. The results satisfy the Mulders-Rodrigues positivity inequalities and show promising trends toward the theoretically required small-x and large-x limiting behaviors, though the small-x region is affected by numerical artifacts from the finite basis.

## Method

The authors solve the light-front eigenvalue equation for the proton using an effective Hamiltonian that includes QCD interactions and a three-dimensional confinement potential, with the Fock-space expansion truncated to the |qqq⟩ and |qqqg⟩ sectors. The light-front wave functions are expanded in a basis of two-dimensional harmonic oscillator states in the transverse direction and plane waves in the longitudinal direction, with cutoffs Nmax=9 and K=16.5. The four T-even gluon TMDs (unpolarized f1^g, helicity g1L^g, worm-gear g1T^g, and Boer-Mulders h1^⟂g) are then computed from overlaps of the |qqqg⟩ light-front wave functions, with the gauge link approximated to zeroth order (so T-odd TMDs vanish).

## Key numbers

* Model parameters used: quark masses mu=0.31 GeV, md=0.25 GeV; gluon mass mg=0.5 GeV; confinement strength κ=0.54 GeV; vertex quark mass mf=1.80 GeV; basis scale b=0.7 GeV; coupling gs=2.2 (dimensionless).
* Cutoffs: Nmax=9, K=16.5 (longitudinal momentum fraction resolution Δx=1/16.5).
* The unpolarized TMD f1^g, helicity TMD g1L^g, and worm-gear TMD g1T^g are positive and peak in the small-x, small-k⊥² region; the Boer-Mulders TMD h1^⟂g shows a zero-crossing behavior.
* At fixed x=9/16.5, all four TMDs decrease monotonically with increasing k⊥², and their products with k⊥² approach zero as k⊥²→0, indicating they grow slower than 1/k⊥².

* The helicity asymmetry ratio ∫g1L^g/∫f1^g approaches 0 as x→0 and approaches 1 as x→1, tested with both K=16.5 and K=32.5.

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach previously applied to quark TMDs (Hu 2022) to gluon TMDs, providing the first such calculation within this framework. It contrasts with other theoretical approaches: the light-cone spectator model (Chakrabarti 2023) and AdS/QCD calculations (Sumrule) find all four T-even gluon TMDs positive, while another spectator model (Bacchetta 2020) finds g1T^g negative. The results are consistent with recent extractions of gluon PDFs f1^g and g1L^g (Ball 2017, Nocera 2014) and lattice calculations of their moments (Alexandrou 2020). The work enables future studies of T-odd gluon TMDs via gauge link inclusion, and provides predictions for experiments at future electron-ion colliders.
