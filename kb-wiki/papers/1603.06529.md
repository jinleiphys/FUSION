# Universal Strangeness Production in Hadronic and Nuclear Collisions

> We show that strangeness suppression in hadronic and nuclear collisions is fully determined by the initial energy density of the collision. The suppression factor $γ_s(s)$, with $\sqrt s$ denoting the collision energy, can be expressed as a universal function of the initial energy density $ε_0(s)$, and the resulting pattern is in excellent agreement with data from $p-p,~p-Pb,~Cu-Cu,~Au-Au$ and $Pb-Pb$ data over a wide range of energies and for different centralities.

## Key claim

Strangeness suppression in hadronic and nuclear collisions is fully determined by the initial energy density of the collision, not by the overall collision energy; the suppression factor γ_s(s) can be expressed as a universal function of the initial energy density ε_0(s), with the caveat that the specific relation between energy density and multiplicity (e.g., whether flow contributions are included) modifies the detailed form of the scaling variable but not the universal behavior.

## Method

The authors relate the size of causally correlated space-time regions in a boost-invariant fireball to the initial energy density ε_0, using the Bjorken expression for energy density and parametrizations of charged multiplicity as a function of collision energy for both pp and AA collisions. They then take existing fits for γ_s(s) as a function of collision energy from Becattini (2013) and Plumari (2015) and plot γ_s against ε_0τ_0, showing that the pp and AA curves coincide into a single universal function. The same scaling is tested for non-central collisions by replacing the nuclear radius with a participant-number-dependent radius.

## Key numbers

- Hadronisation energy density ε_h ≃ 0.4–0.6 GeV/fm³ from lattice QCD
- Thermalization time τ_0 conventionally assumed to be about 1 fm
- For central AA collisions: (dN/dy)_{y=0}^{AA} = a(√s)^{0.3} + b with a=0.7613, b=0.0534
- For pp collisions: (dN/dy)_{y=0}^{pp} = a(√s)^{0.22} + b with a=0.797, b=0.04123, R_p = 0.8 fm
- Fit parameters for γ_s^A(s) = 1 − a_A exp(−b_A √(A√s)): a_A=0.606, b_A=0.0209
- Fit parameters for γ_s^p(s) = 1 − a_p exp(−b_p s^{1/4}): a_p=0.5595, b_p=0.0242
- For p-Pb collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV, average number of participants N̄_part ≃ 8
- Average transverse energy per produced hadron m_T ≃ 0.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the causality-based approach to strangeness correlation volumes developed by Castorina and Satz (2014, 2015), which showed that the fireball is partitioned into causally disconnected regions similar to the horizon problem in cosmology. It contradicts the earlier assumption that strangeness suppression differs fundamentally between elementary (pp) and nuclear (AA) collisions, showing instead that the apparent difference arises because γ_s depends on energy density rather than collision energy. The result enables a unified description of strangeness production across all collision systems and centralities, and the authors note that a specific example (the K⁻/π ratio) was already observed by Wang (2000). Work extending these considerations to e⁺e⁻ annihilation is stated to be in progress.
