# Taylor approximation to treat nonlocality in scattering processes

> Study of scattering process in the nonlocal interaction framework leads to an integro-differential equation. The purpose of the present work is to develop an efficient approach to solve this integro-differential equation with high degree of precision. The method developed here employs Taylor approximation for the radial wave function which converts the integro-differential equation in to a readily solvable second-order homogeneous differential equation. This scheme is found to be computationally efficient by a factor of 10 when compared to the iterative scheme developed in J.~Phys.~G~Nucl.~Part.~Phys.~{\bf 45},~015106~(2018). The calculated observables for neutron scattering off $^{24}$Mg, $^{40}$Ca, $^{100}$Mo and $^{208}$Pb with energies up to 10 MeV are found to be within at most 8$\%$ of those obtained with the iterative scheme. Further, we propose an improvement over the Taylor scheme that brings the observables so close to the results obtained by iterative scheme that they are visually indistinguishable. This is achieved without any appreciable change in the run time.

## Key claim

The Taylor approximation method converts the integro-differential equation for nonlocal scattering into a readily solvable second-order homogeneous differential equation, yielding observables within at most 8% of those from the full iterative mean value theorem (IMVT) scheme of Upadhyay and Bhagwat (2018) while being computationally faster by a factor of 10; an iterative perturbation improvement brings the observables to within 2% of the IMVT results with no appreciable increase in run time.

## Method

The authors expand the radial wave function in the nonlocal kernel integral using a first-order Taylor series about r = r', retaining terms up to the second derivative. This transforms the integro-differential equation into a homogeneous second-order differential equation with an explicit first-derivative term, which is solved using the fourth-order Runge-Kutta method with initial conditions derived from a Frobenius series near the origin. An iterative perturbation approach (IPA) then applies successive corrections to the Taylor-solution wave function, with convergence achieved after five iterations.

## Key numbers

- Computational speed-up factor of 10 for the Taylor scheme compared to the IMVT scheme (run time of about 5 minutes versus about 1 hour on a single Intel i7-6700 processor for a given energy).
- Taylor-scheme total cross sections for neutron scattering off 24Mg, 40Ca, 100Mo, and 208Pb at energies up to 10 MeV are within at most 8% of the IMVT results.
- IPA with five iterations (IPA5) yields cross sections within 2% of the IMVT results for all targets and energies studied.
- Radial step size of 0.02 fm and matching radius of 20 fm used in all calculations.
- Nonlocal range parameter beta = 0.9 fm for the Gaussian form factor in the TPM15 potential.

## Context

This work extends the iterative mean value theorem (IMVT) approach developed by Upadhyay and Bhagwat (2018) for solving the integro-differential equation arising from nonlocal nucleon-nucleus interactions. The Taylor method provides a computationally efficient alternative to the IMVT scheme, which was robust but time-consuming due to slow convergence. The technique enables large-scale scattering calculations that were previously impractical, and the authors demonstrate its robustness against different forms of nonlocality (Gaussian versus exponential) and different nonlocal ranges (beta = 0.6, 0.9, and 1.2 fm).
