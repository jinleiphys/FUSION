# Low energy $n-\nuc{3}{H}$ scattering : a novel testground for nuclear interaction

> The low energy $n-\nuc{3}{H}$ elastic cross sections near the resonance peak are calculated by solving the 4-nucleon problem with realistic NN interactions. Three different methods -- Alt, Grassberger and Shandas (AGS), Hyperspherical Harmonics and Faddeev-Yakubovsky -- have been used and their respective results are compared. We conclude on a failure of the existing NN forces to reproduce the $n-\nuc{3}{H}$ total cross section.

## Key claim

Existing realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) forces fail to reproduce the experimental total cross section for low-energy \(n-\nuc{3}{H}\) elastic scattering near the resonance peak, even when three-nucleon forces (3NF) like Urbana IX (UIX) are included; the disagreement is approximately 10% near the resonance and is attributed specifically to deficiencies in the NN P-wave components rather than to missing 3NF effects.

## Method

The four-nucleon scattering problem was solved using three independent methods: Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations in momentum space with a rank-one separable expansion of the NN t-matrix, Faddeev-Yakubovsky (FY) equations in configuration space, and the Hyperspherical Harmonic (HH) variational method in configuration space. Calculations were performed with the AV18 and Nijm-II realistic NN interaction models, including partial waves up to \(j \leq 2\) for AGS and \(l_x \le 3\) for FY, with convergence studied as a function of the number of partial waves and HH expansion order. The Kohn variational principle was used in the HH method to determine S-matrix elements, while FY calculations imposed Dirichlet-type boundary conditions at large intercluster separation.

## Key numbers

- Singlet scattering length \(a_1 = 4.09\) fm (AGS), 4.25 fm (FY), 4.28 fm (HH) for AV18; triplet scattering length \(a_3 = 3.70\) fm (AGS), 3.74 fm (FY), 3.73 fm (HH) for AV18
- Zero-energy total cross section \(\sigma(0) = 1.82\) b (AGS), 1.88 b (FY), 1.89 b (HH) for AV18; experimental value is \(\sigma(0) = 1.70 \pm 0.03\) b
- Total cross section at \(E_{\mathrm{cm}} = 3.0\) MeV: 2.48 b (AGS), 2.21 b (FY), 2.21 b (HH) for AV18; AGS overestimates FY/HH by approximately 12% at this energy
- Phase shifts at \(E_{\mathrm{cm}} = 3.0\) MeV for AV18: \(0^+\) state \(\delta = -70.0^\circ\) (FY), \(-69.8^\circ\) (HH); \(0^-\) state \(\delta = 23.9^\circ\) (FY), \(23.4^\circ\) (HH); \(2^-\) state \(\delta = 45.4^\circ\) (FY), \(44.5^\circ\) (HH)
- AV18+UIX model gives \(\sigma(0) = 1.73\) b, consistent with the experimental value

## Context

This work extends earlier four-nucleon scattering calculations by the same groups (Lazauskas et al., Carbonell et al., Viviani et al.) that had suggested but not conclusively demonstrated the failure of realistic NN forces due to convergence uncertainties. The close agreement between FY and HH methods (to better than 1% for positive parity states and a few percent for negative parity states) establishes that the discrepancy with experiment is not a numerical artifact. The failure to reproduce the resonance peak with standard 3NF (UIX) points to the same underlying deficiency in NN P-waves that has been invoked to explain the \(A_y\) puzzle in \(N-d\) scattering (Kievsky et al., 1996; Witala et al., 1988). The results enable systematic testing of improved NN potential models and motivate new experimental measurements of \(n-\nuc{3}{H}\) scattering, as only one accurate data set (Phillips et al., 1980) exists in the resonance region.
