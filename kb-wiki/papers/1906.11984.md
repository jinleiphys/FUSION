# Elastic Antiproton-Nucleus Scattering from Chiral Forces

> Elastic scattering of antiprotons off $^4$He, $^{12}$C, and $^{16,18}$O is described for the first time with a totally microscopic approach based on the calculation of an optical potential (OP) describing the antiproton-target interaction. The OP is derived using the recent antiproton-nucleon ($\bar{p}N$) chiral interaction to calculate the $\bar{p}N$ $t$ matrix, while the target densities are computed with the ab initio no-core shell model using chiral interactions as well. Our results are in a good agreement with the existing experimental data and the results computed at different chiral orders of the $\bar{p}N$ interaction display the convergence pattern expected from the theory.

## Key claim

For the first time, elastic antiproton-nucleus scattering is described using a fully microscopic optical potential derived from chiral effective field theory, with no phenomenological input. The agreement with experimental data is good, but the authors note that the description requires the antiproton-nucleon interaction to be computed at least up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) in the chiral expansion, as lower orders fail to reproduce the data.

## Method

The optical potential for antiproton-nucleus scattering was constructed in momentum space using the first-order term of the Watson multiple scattering theory within the impulse approximation. The antiproton-nucleon t-matrix was computed from a chiral N3LO antiproton-nucleon interaction (Dai, Haidenbauer, and Meissner, 2017) with a coordinate-space cutoff of 0.9 fm, while the one-body translationally invariant nuclear densities for 4He, 12C, and 16,18O were obtained from ab initio no-core shell model calculations using chiral two-nucleon (N4LO) and three-nucleon (N2LO) interactions with a similarity renormalization group scale of 2.0 fm^-1.

## Key numbers

- Elastic antiproton scattering off 4He and 12C was computed at an antiproton laboratory energy of 180 MeV; off 16,18O at 178 MeV.
- The NCSM densities for 4He used harmonic oscillator frequency hbar-Omega = 20 MeV and Nmax = 14; for 12C and 16,18O, hbar-Omega = 16 MeV and Nmax = 8.
- At leading order (LO), the calculated differential cross section for 12C has a diffraction minimum at about 33 degrees, more than 2 orders of magnitude lower than the experimental minimum at about 23 degrees; at N3LO the minimum is well reproduced.
- The analyzing power predictions for 12C, 16O, and 18O are consistent with the small measured asymmetries from LEAR, which are statistically compatible with zero.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic optical potential approach previously applied to proton-nucleus scattering by Gennari et al. (2017) to the antiproton-nucleus system, using the same ab initio NCSM densities and chiral interactions. It provides the first fully consistent microscopic description of antiproton elastic scattering, enabling future studies of antiproton-nucleus reactions at facilities like FAIR and PUMA, and raises open questions about the role of antiproton-nucleon-nucleon three-body forces.
