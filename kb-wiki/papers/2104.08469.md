# Implications of the $Z_{cs}(3985)$ and $Z_{cs}(4000)$ as two different states

> Recently, the hidden charm tetraquark states $Z_{cs}(3985)$ and $Z_{cs}(4000)$ with strangeness were observed by the BESIII and LHCb collaborations, respectively, which are great breakthroughs for exploring exotic QCD structures. The first and foremost question is whether they are the same state. In this work, we explore the implications of the narrower state $Z_{cs}(3985)$ in BESIII and the wider one $Z_{cs}(4000)$ in LHCb as two different states. Within a solvable nonrelativistic effective field theory, we include the possible violations of heavy quark spin symmetry and SU(3) flavor symmetry in a comprehensive approach. If $Z_{cs}(3985)$ and $Z_{cs}(4000)$ are two different states, our results show that $Z_{cs}(4000)/Z_{cs}(3985)$ is the pure $(|\bar{D}_s^*D\rangle+/- |\bar{D}_sD^*\rangle)/\sqrt{2}$ state, and the SU(3) flavor partner of $Z_{c}(3900)$ is $Z_{cs}(4000)$ rather than the $Z_{cs}(3985)$. Another two important consequences are the existence of a tensor $\bar{D}_s^*D^*$ resonance with mass about 4126 MeV and width 13 MeV, and the suppression of the decay mode $Z_{cs}(3985) \to J/ψK$. The two consequences can be tested in experiments and distinguish the two-state interpretation from the one-state scheme.

## Key claim
If the $Z_{cs}(3985)$ (observed by BESIII) and $Z_{cs}(4000)$ (observed by LHCb) are two different states rather than the same state, then $Z_{cs}(4000)$ is the pure $(|\bar{D}_s^*D\rangle+|\bar{D}_sD^*\rangle)/\sqrt{2}$ state and the SU(3) flavor partner of $Z_{c}(3900)$, while $Z_{cs}(3985)$ is the pure $(|\bar{D}_s^*D\rangle-|\bar{D}_sD^*\rangle)/\sqrt{2}$ state; this assignment implies large SU(3) symmetry violation but good heavy quark spin symmetry.

## Method
The authors used a solvable nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT) with contact S-wave interactions up to next-to-leading order, fitting the low-energy constants to the masses and widths of the $Z_c(3900)$, $Z_c(4020)$, $Z_{cs}(3985)$, and $Z_{cs}(4000)$ states as single-channel resonances. They then performed a coupled-channel analysis of the $|\bar{D}_sD^*/\bar{D}_s^*D,+\rangle$ and $|\bar{D}_sD^*/\bar{D}_s^*D,-\rangle$ systems, introducing a parameter $R_{\text{HQSSB}}$ to quantify heavy quark spin symmetry breaking and evaluating the mixing angle between the two basis states.

## Key numbers
- $Z_{cs}(3985)$ width is about ten times smaller than that of $Z_{cs}(4000)$ (the latter is over 100 MeV).
- Mass splitting $m_{D_{s}^{(*)}}-m_{D^{(*)}} \simeq 100$ MeV breaks SU(3) flavor symmetry.
- Mass splitting $m_{D_{(s)}^{*}}-m_{D_{(s)}} \simeq 140$ MeV breaks heavy quark spin symmetry.
- Predicted tensor $\bar{D}_s^*D^*$ resonance: mass $M = 4126 \pm 3$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 13 \pm 6$ MeV.
- Scattering lengths and effective ranges extracted: $a_s = -0.76 \pm 0.26$ fm and $r_0 = -6.70 \pm 5.54$ fm for $Z_{cs}(3985)$; $a_s = -0.32 \pm 0.07$ fm and $r_0 = -2.08 \pm 0.80$ fm for $Z_{cs}(4000)$.
- Branching ratio $\mathcal{R}(Z_{cs}\to \bar{D}_s^*D / Z_{cs}\to \bar{D}_sD^*) \approx 0.5$ for both $Z_{cs}(3985)$ and $Z_{cs}(4000)$.

## Context
This work extends the molecular interpretation of the $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4020)$ states (observed by BESIII) to the strange sector, where BESIII found $Z_{cs}(3985)$ and LHCb found $Z_{cs}(4000)$ and $Z_{cs}(4220)$. Prior works such as Meng (2020) and Wang (2020) treated $Z_{cs}(3985)$ as the SU(3) partner of $Z_c(3900)$ with good heavy quark spin symmetry; the present paper instead treats the two $Z_{cs}$ states as distinct, predicting a tensor $\bar{D}_s^*D^*$ resonance and a suppressed $Z_{cs}(3985) \to J/\psi K$ decay mode that can be tested experimentally to distinguish the two-state interpretation from the one-state scheme.
