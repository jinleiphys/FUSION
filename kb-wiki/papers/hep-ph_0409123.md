# Renormalized thermodynamics from the 2PI effective action

> High-temperature resummed perturbation theory is plagued by poor convergence properties. The problem appears for theories with bosonic field content such as QCD, QED or scalar theories. We calculate the pressure as well as other thermodynamic quantities at high temperature for a scalar one-component field theory, solving a three-loop 2PI effective action numerically without further approximations. We present a detailed comparison with the two-loop approximation. One observes a strongly improved convergence behavior as compared to perturbative approaches. The renormalization employed in this work extends previous prescriptions, and is sufficient to determine all counterterms required for the theory in the symmetric as well as the spontaneously broken phase.

## Key claim

The three-loop 2PI effective action yields a strongly improved convergence behavior for high-temperature thermodynamics compared to standard perturbative approaches, but this improvement is conditional on a proper renormalization scheme that extends previous prescriptions to determine all counterterms required in both the symmetric and spontaneously broken phases.

## Method

The authors solved the three-loop 2PI (two-particle irreducible) effective action numerically for a real scalar \(g^2 \phi^4\) field theory in thermal equilibrium, without further approximations beyond the loop truncation. They imposed renormalization conditions on the two-point and four-point functions at zero momentum, using a lattice regularization, and determined all counterterms (mass, wavefunction, and coupling) self-consistently from the stationarity conditions for the propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the four-point vertex.

## Key numbers

* Pressure normalized to the ideal gas (one-loop) value, shown for renormalized coupling \(g_R\) at temperature \(T = 2 T_0\) where \(T_0 = m_R(T_0)\); the three-loop corrections to the pressure are "rather small" at this temperature.
* The four-point field \(V_R(T)\) is shown as a function of the logarithm of the lattice cutoff for \(T = 2 T_0\) and \(T = 3 T_0\), with \(T_0 = m_R(T_0)\) and lattice volume \(L m_R(T_0) = 2\); the upper curves demonstrate cutoff independence when renormalization is done properly.
* The renormalized four-vertex at zero momentum is set to \(-4!\, g_R^2\) by the renormalization condition (Eq. r3).

## Context

This work extends the renormalization scheme for 2PI effective actions developed by Hees and Knoll (2002) and by Blaizot, Iancu, and Reinosa (2003), providing a formulation that also renormalizes all functional field-derivatives of the effective action (i.e., proper vertices) in the symmetric phase. The results address the well-documented poor convergence of high-temperature resummed perturbation theory, which has been studied in scalar theories as a simpler testbed for problems also present in QCD and QED. The approach enables rigorous non-perturbative calculations of thermodynamic quantities beyond two-loop order without additional approximations, and the authors note that the good convergence properties may be attributed to the \(n\)PI loop expansion with \(n > 2\) rather than to the 2PI loop expansion alone.
