# Effects of the Dirac Sea on the Giant Monopole States

> Effects of the Dirac sea on the excitation energy of the giant monopole states are investigated in an analytic way within the $σ-ω$ model. The excitation energy is determined by the relativistic Landau-Migdal parameters, $F_0$ and $F_1$. Their analytic expressions are derived in the relativistic random phase approximation(RRPA) without the Dirac sea, with the Pauli blocking terms and with the full Dirac sea. It is shown that in the RRPA based on the mean field approximation the Pauli blocking terms should be included in the configuration space, according to the relativistic Landau theory. In the renormalized RRPA, the incompressibility coefficient becomes negative, if N$\nbar$ excitations are neglected.

## Key claim

The excitation energy of giant monopole states in the relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) depends critically on whether the Pauli blocking terms (transitions between the Dirac sea and the occupied Fermi sea) are included; without them, the Landau-Migdal parameter \(F_0\) changes sign and the incompressibility coefficient \(K\) is substantially reduced, and in the fully renormalized RRPA the incompressibility becomes negative if nucleon-antinucleon (N\(\bar{\mathrm{N}}\)) excitations are neglected.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the relativistic Landau-Migdal parameters \(F_0\) and \(F_1\) within the \(\sigma-\omega\) model by calculating the longitudinal RRPA correlation functions at the limit \(k \rightarrow 0\) and comparing them with the correlation function of Landau theory. They performed this derivation for three cases: without the Dirac sea (neglecting Pauli blocking terms), with Pauli blocking terms included, and with the full Dirac sea (renormalized RRPA). The incompressibility coefficient \(K\) was then obtained from the Landau-Migdal parameters via \(K = (3p_F^2/\epsilon_F)(1+F_0)/(1+F_1/3)\).

## Key numbers

- With Pauli blocking terms included (RMFA parameters: \(M=939\) MeV, \(m_\sigma=520\) MeV, \(m_\omega=783\) MeV, \(g_\sigma^2=109.626\), \(g_\omega^2=190.431\)): \(F_0 = 0.569\), \(F_1 = -1.151\), \(K = 544\) MeV.
- Neglecting Pauli blocking terms (same RMFA parameters): \(F_0 = -0.368\), \(F_1 = -1.866\), \(K = 357\) MeV.
- In the renormalized RRPA (RHA parameters: \(g_\sigma^2 = 66.117\), \(g_\omega^2 = 79.927\), \(M^* = 0.7306M\), \(m_0 = 691.171\) MeV): \(F_0 = 0.676\), \(F_1 = -0.620\).
- Dirac sea contribution parameter: \(a_{\mathrm{D}} = 0.405\), compared to \(\asigma\fsigma = 0.0296\) in the RHA.
- If \(a_{\mathrm{D}}\) were neglected in the RHA, \(F_0\) would be \(-1.56\), implying \(K < 0\).

## Context

This work extends the analytic treatment of giant monopole states in relativistic nuclear models, building on the numerical findings of Giai (1998) that monopole states cannot be well described without Pauli blocking terms in the RRPA built on the relativistic mean field approximation. The authors provide analytic confirmation that the Pauli blocking terms are required for consistency with relativistic Landau theory, and they show that the full Dirac sea (including N\(\bar{\mathrm{N}}\) excitations) is essential for stabilizing the nucleus in the renormalized Hartree approximation, as previously studied by the same authors (1998). The results enable a clear understanding of how the Dirac sea affects the incompressibility and excitation energies of giant monopole and quadrupole states, and they clarify the relationship between relativistic and nonrelativistic treatments of these collective modes.
