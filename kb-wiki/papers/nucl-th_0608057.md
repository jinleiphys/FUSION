# A Fast Hadron Freeze-out Generator

> We have developed a fast Monte Carlo procedure of hadron generation allowing one to study and analyze various observables for stable hadrons and hadron resonances produced in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. Particle multiplicities are determined based on the concept of chemical freeze-out. Particles can be generated on the chemical or thermal freeze-out hypersurface represented by a parameterization or a numerical solution of relativistic hydrodynamics with given initial conditions and equation of state. Besides standard space-like sectors associated with the volume decay, the hypersurface may also include non-space-like sectors related to the emission from the surface of expanding system. For comparison with other models and experimental data we demonstrate the results based on the standard parameterizations of the hadron freeze-out hypersurface and flow velocity profile under the assumption of a common chemical and thermal freeze-out. The C++ generator code is written under the ROOT framework and is available for public use at http://uhkm.jinr.ru/.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fast Monte Carlo hadron freeze-out generator that can produce particle multiplicities, four-momenta, and four-coordinates for any kind of freeze-out hypersurface, including non-space-like sectors, but the example calculations shown assume a common chemical and thermal freeze-out with simple parameterizations rather than the full generalized formalism.

## Method

The generator uses the Cooper-Frye prescription generalized for non-space-like hypersurface sectors, computing particle multiplicities from equilibrium distribution functions integrated over the freeze-out hypersurface. Particle four-coordinates are sampled on the hypersurface according to the element \(d^3\sigma_\mu u^\mu\), and three-momenta are generated in the fluid element rest frame from the equilibrium distribution, then boosted to the fireball rest frame. A von Neumann rejection step accounts for the residual weight arising when the hypersurface normal differs from the flow four-velocity, and resonance decays are simulated using ROOT utilities.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Snigirev (1996), Kruglov (1997), Lokhtin (2003), and Lokhtin (2005) by formulating a fast Monte Carlo procedure for any freeze-out hypersurface, including non-space-like sectors. The generator is compared to THERMINATOR (Kisiel et al., 2004) and is reported to be more than one order of magnitude faster. The code enables systematic studies of hadron observables at RHIC and LHC energies and can incorporate freeze-out hypersurfaces from relativistic hydrodynamics.
