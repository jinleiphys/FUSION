# Lattice Studies for hadron spectroscopy and interactions

> Recent progresses of lattice QCD studies for hadron spectroscopy and interactions are briefly reviewed. Some emphasis are given on a new proposal for a method, which enable us to calculate potentials between hadrons. As an example of the method, the extraction of nuclear potential in lattice QCD is discussed in detail.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD can now calculate hadron interactions and nuclear potentials from first principles using the HAL QCD method, though results at physical quark masses remain challenging and current calculations are performed at heavier-than-physical pion masses (e.g., 700 MeV) where the attraction is still weaker than experiment.

## Method

The paper reviews three approaches to hadron interactions in lattice QCD: direct calculation of nuclei, Lüscher's finite volume method for scattering phase shifts, and the HAL QCD method. The HAL QCD method extracts a non-local but energy-independent potential between two hadrons from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function, then expands this potential in derivatives (truncated at leading order for practical use) and solves the Schrödinger equation to obtain scattering phase shifts and bound state energies.

## Key numbers

- Neutral kaon mass splitting: \( m_{K^0} - m_{K^+} = 4.54(1.09) \) MeV from 1+1+1 flavor QCD+QED simulations at physical quark masses, compared to experimental value 3.937(28) MeV
- Binding energies of \( ^3 \)H and \( ^4 \)He compiled as a function of \( m_\pi^2 \) from PACS-CS and NPL QCD collaborations, with results scattered but order-of-magnitude comparable to experiment
- \( \rho \) meson resonance phase shift \( \sin^2\delta \) for \( \pi^+\pi^- \) scattering in \( I=1, L=1 \) channel at \( m_\pi \simeq 330 \) MeV and \( m_\pi \simeq 290 \) MeV, fitted to effective range formula
- Leading order NN potential in isospin-triplet (spin-singlet) channel obtained in 2+1 flavor QCD at \( m_\pi \simeq 700 \) MeV, with \( ^1S_0 \) scattering phase shift compared to experimental data
- Leading order spin-singlet potential compared at two energies: \( E_k \simeq 0 \) MeV and \( E_k \simeq 45 \) MeV in quenched QCD, showing almost no difference
- Phase shifts of \( I=2 \) \( \pi\pi \) scattering from HAL QCD method agree with Lüscher's finite volume method at spatial extensions \( L = 3.7 \) fm and \( L = 1.8 \) fm
- H-dibaryon binding energy and root-mean-square distance shown as function of pseudo-scalar meson mass in flavor SU(3) limit

## Context

This review extends the Lüscher finite volume method (Lüscher 1990) for scattering phase shifts and the direct nuclear binding energy calculations by PACS-CS (Yamazaki 2009, 2012) and NPL QCD (Beane 2010, 2012) collaborations. The HAL QCD method (Ishii 2006, Aoki 2009, 2011, 2012) provides an alternative approach that enables calculation of potentials between hadrons, which can then be used in many-body Schrödinger equations to study nuclear structure. The method has been validated against Lüscher's method for \( I=2 \) \( \pi\pi \) scattering and applied to systems such as the H-dibaryon in 3-flavor QCD (Inoue 2010, 2011).
