# High energy rho meson leptoproduction

> We investigate the longitudinal and transverse polarized cross-sections of the leptoproduction of the rho meson in the high energy limit. Our model is based on the computation of the impact factor gamma*(lambda_gamma) -> rho(lambda_rho) using the twist expansion in the forward limit and expressed in the impact parameter space. This treatment involves in the final stage the twist 2 and twist 3 distribution amplitudes (DAs) of the rho meson and the dipole scattering amplitude. Taking models that exist for the DAs and for the dipole cross-section, we get a phenomenological model for the helicity amplitudes, we compare our predictions with HERA data and get a fairly good description for large enough virtualities of the photon.

## Key claim

The model provides a fairly good description of HERA data for the longitudinal and transverse polarized cross-sections of rho meson leptoproduction, but only for large enough photon virtualities (Q^2 greater than about 5 GeV^2); the discrepancy at lower Q^2 indicates that higher twist corrections become important.

## Method

The authors compute the impact factor for the transition gamma* -> rho using a twist expansion in the forward limit, expressed in impact parameter space, involving twist-2 and twist-3 distribution amplitudes (DAs) of the rho meson and the dipole scattering amplitude. The helicity amplitudes are obtained by convoluting these impact factors with the dipole cross-section, using the k_T-factorization scheme. The DAs are modeled via the conformal expansion of Ball, Braun, Koike and Tanaka (1998), and the dipole scattering amplitude is taken from numerical solutions of the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation fitted to inclusive structure functions, leaving no free parameters.

## Key numbers

- The model agrees with H1 data for Q^2 greater than about 5 GeV^2; for Q^2 less than about 5 GeV^2, higher twist corrections become important.
- The factorization/renormalization scale for the DAs is chosen as mu^2 = (Q^2 + m_rho^2)/4.
- Predictions for the total cross-section sigma as a function of W (center-of-mass energy of the gamma*-p system) are compared with H1 and ZEUS data, showing good agreement for the W-dependence.

## Context

This work extends the earlier collinear factorization calculations of Ginzburg, Panfil, and Serbo (for longitudinal impact factors) and Anikin et al. (2009, 2010) (for transverse impact factors) by reformulating the impact factors in impact parameter space, which allows implementation of saturation effects. The model uses the dipole cross-section from Albacete et al. (2010), based on rcBK equation solutions, and the DA model of Ball, Braun, Koike, and Tanaka (1998). This approach enables probing non-perturbative aspects of rho meson leptoproduction and can be extended to non-forward kinematics and other helicity amplitudes, as well as to lower Q^2 values near the saturation scale.
