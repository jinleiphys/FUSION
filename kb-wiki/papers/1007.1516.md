# Valence particles and the correction to relativistic mean field binding energy

> The differences between the experimental and the theoretically calculated binding energies in Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach have been calculated for a large number of odd-$Z$ nuclei from $A=47$ to 229. Neutron-proton (n-p) interaction is expected to be the major contributor to this difference. This difference, excluding certain mass regions and taking other effects as well as the odd-even mass difference into account, may be linearly parametrized by the Casten factor, a recognized measure of the n-p interaction in the nucleus. The results follow the same pattern as in the case of even-$Z$ nuclei observed earlier.

## Key claim

The difference between experimental and RMF-calculated binding energies in odd-Z nuclei, after correcting for effects unrelated to the neutron-proton interaction and for odd-even mass differences, can be linearly parametrized by the Casten factor P = N_p N_n / (N_p + N_n). The linear relationship holds across a large mass range (A=47 to 229) but excludes certain mass regions where experimental binding energies for closed-neutron-shell reference nuclei are unavailable.

## Method

The author calculated the difference Δ_{νπ} between experimental and RMF binding energies (using the FSU Gold Lagrangian density with zero-range pairing of strength 300 MeV-fm) for 278 odd-even and 245 odd-odd nuclei. For each isotopic chain, the correction B_corr(Z) was defined from the isotope with a magic neutron number (where N_n=0 and n-p interaction is expected to be small), and Δ_{νπ} was set to zero at those magic-N nuclei. The resulting Δ_{νπ} values were fitted as a linear function of the Casten factor P, with an additional term d/√A included for odd-N nuclei to account for the odd-even mass difference.

## Key numbers

- For 252 odd-even nuclei (excluding P=0 points), the fit Δ_{νπ} = aP gives a = -1.973 ± 0.024 MeV with rms deviation 1.126 MeV.
- For 245 odd-odd nuclei, the fit Δ_{νπ} = aP + d/√A gives a = -2.035 ± 0.042 MeV and d = 10.48 ± 1.30 MeV with standard deviation 1.138 MeV.
- For the combined set of 932 nuclei (even-Z from prior work and odd-Z from this work), the global fit gives a = -2.070 ± 0.015 MeV and d = 12.05 ± 0.70 MeV (for odd-N nuclei only) with average deviation 1.132 MeV.
- The coefficient d for odd-odd nuclei (10.48 MeV) is close to the 11 MeV coefficient in the semi-empirical mass formula.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier study on even-Z nuclei (Gangopadhyay, 2009) to odd-proton nuclei, confirming that the n-p interaction correction to RMF binding energy follows the same linear pattern with the Casten factor across both even and odd Z. The approach provides a simple spherical RMF correction that yields two-neutron separation energies comparable to or better than deformed RMF calculations (e.g., Lalazissis et al. for Nd isotopes using NLSH and deformed NL3), enabling improved mass predictions for nuclei far from stability without explicit deformation treatment.
