# Properties of $ÎŁ_Q^{*}$, $Îž_Q^{*}$ and $Î©_Q^{*}$ heavy baryons in cold nuclear matter

> The in-medium properties of the heavy spin-3/2 $ÎŁ_Q^{*}$, $Îž_Q^{*}$ and $Î©_Q^{*}$ baryons with $Q$ being $b$ or $c$ quark are investigated. The shifts in some spectroscopic parameters of these particles due to the saturated cold nuclear matter are calculated. The variations of those parameters with respect to the changes in the density of the cold nuclear medium are studied, as well. It is observed that the parameters of $ÎŁ_Q^{*}$ baryons are considerably affected by the nuclear matter compared to the $Îž_Q^{*}$ and $Î©_Q^{*}$ particles that roughly do not see the medium. The results obtained may be used in analyses of the data to be provided by the in-medium experiments like PANDA.

## Key claim

The spectroscopic parameters of the heavy spin-3/2 baryons $\Sigma_Q^{*}$, $\Xi_Q^{*}$ and $\Omega_Q^{*}$ (with $Q = b$ or $c$) are shifted in cold nuclear matter, but only the $\Sigma_Q^{*}$ baryons are considerably affected; the $\Xi_Q^{*}$ and $\Omega_Q^{*}$ particles "roughly do not see the medium," a result that depends on the specific quark content and the in-medium QCD sum rule approach used.

## Method

The authors employed in-medium QCD sum rules, calculating the two-point correlation function of interpolating currents for the spin-3/2 baryons in cold nuclear matter. They performed an operator product expansion (OPE) in the medium, including perturbative and non-perturbative condensates up to dimension-5, and matched the hadronic and QCD sides of the sum rules to extract the in-medium mass, residue, and vector self-energy. The analysis was carried out in the rest frame of the nuclear medium at saturation density and as a function of density, with the Borel mass and continuum threshold fixed according to standard sum-rule criteria.

## Key numbers

- For $\Sigma_b^{*}$ at saturation density: $m^{*}_{B_Q}/m_{B_Q} = 0.946^{+0.015}_{-0.018}$, $\Sigma_{v,B_Q}/m_{B_Q} = 0.072^{+0.017}_{-0.015}$, $\lambda^{*}_{B_Q}/\lambda_{B_Q} = 0.755^{+0.037}_{-0.032}$.
- For $\Sigma_c^{*}$ at saturation density: $m^{*}_{B_Q}/m_{B_Q} = 0.985^{+0.011}_{-0.010}$, $\Sigma_{v,B_Q}/m_{B_Q} = 0.054^{+0.010}_{-0.009}$, $\lambda^{*}_{B_Q}/\lambda_{B_Q} = 0.728^{+0.025}_{-0.023}$.
- For $\Xi_b^{*}$ at saturation density: $m^{*}_{B_Q}/m_{B_Q} = 0.999^{+0.011}_{-0.009}$, $\Sigma_{v,B_Q}/m_{B_Q} = 0.005^{+0.001}_{-0.001}$, $\lambda^{*}_{B_Q}/\lambda_{B_Q} = 0.976^{+0.009}_{-0.008}$.
- For $\Xi_c^{*}$ at saturation density: $m^{*}_{B_Q}/m_{B_Q} = 1.003^{+0.001}_{-0.001}$, $\Sigma_{v,B_Q}/m_{B_Q} = 0.004^{+0.001}_{-0.001}$, $\lambda^{*}_{B_Q}/\lambda_{B_Q} = 0.971^{+0.005}_{-0.004}$.
- For $\Omega_b^{*}$ at saturation density: $m^{*}_{B_Q}/m_{B_Q} = 1.004^{+0.002}_{-0.001}$, $\Sigma_{v,B_Q}/m_{B_Q} = 0.002^{+0.001}_{-0.001}$, $\lambda^{*}_{B_Q}/\lambda_{B_Q} = 0.984^{+0.008}_{-0.007}$.
- For $\Omega_c^{*}$ at saturation density: $m^{*}_{B_Q}/m_{B_Q} = 1.007^{+0.003}_{-0.002}$, $\Sigma_{v,B_Q}/m_{B_Q} = 0.002^{+0.001}_{-0.001}$, $\lambda^{*}_{B_Q}/\lambda_{B_Q} = 0.978^{+0.004}_{-0.003}$.
- Maximum vector self-energy: $\Sigma_v = 420$ MeV for $\Sigma_b^{*}$; minimum: $\Sigma_v = 55$ MeV for $\Omega_c^{*}$.
- The shift in the residue of $\Sigma_c^{*}$ reaches roughly 27% of its vacuum value.
- The perturbative part constitutes roughly 70% (for $b$) and 80% (for $c$) of the total OPE contribution in the $\Sigma_Q^{*}$ channel.

## Context

This work extends the in-medium QCD sum rule studies previously applied to heavy spin-1/2 baryons (Azizi 2016) and light decuplet baryons (Azizi 2016) to the heavy spin-3/2 sector, specifically $\Sigma_Q^{*}$, $\Xi_Q^{*}$, and $\Omega_Q^{*}$. It also builds on earlier finite-density sum rule calculations for $\Sigma$ hyperons (Jin 1994) and heavy $\Lambda_Q$ and doubly heavy baryons (Wang 2011, Wang 2012). The results are intended for use in analyzing data from in-medium experiments such as PANDA, and they enable future studies of heavy baryon-nucleon interactions and the behavior of hadronic matter under extreme conditions.
