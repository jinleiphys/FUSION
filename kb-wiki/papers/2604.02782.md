# Spin effects in superfluidity, neutron matter and neutron stars

> We review selected aspects of the interior physics of compact stars, focusing on the microscopic and macroscopic manifestations of spin, magnetic fields, and nucleonic superfluidity and superconductivity. Spin statistics of fermions allows quantum degeneracy pressure to determine the stability and global properties of neutron stars, whose structure depends sensitively on the strong interactions among baryons in dense matter. Using a generic meta-modeling framework based on an expansion of the nuclear energy density around the isospin-symmetric and saturation-density limits, we highlight how various lesser-known terms in this expansion affect compact-star observables and review multimessenger constraints on mass, radius, and moment of inertia. The influence of magnetic fields on dense matter is examined, showing that substantial effects in their structure require extremely strong fields, whereas lower fields are sufficient to affect their superfluid phases. At the mesoscopic scale, the coexistence of superfluid and superconducting components features vortex and flux-tube lattices, with pinning and mutual friction processes playing central roles in neutron-star rotational dynamics. We discuss unresolved issues concerning vortex structure, flux-tube configurations, and the origin of pulsar glitches and post-glitch relaxation. We also briefly address the possible emergence of deconfined quark phases in compact-star cores, including their color-superconducting properties, as well as the associated vortex structures and magnetic-field responses in such phases.

## Key claim

This review argues that while the spin-statistics connection and Pauli exclusion principle are fundamental to neutron star stability via degeneracy pressure, the specific effects of spin on the nuclear equation of state and superfluid pairing patterns are often subtle and depend sensitively on density regime, magnetic field strength, and the particular interaction channels (spin-singlet vs. spin-triplet) that dominate under different conditions.

## Method

The authors review the equation of state of neutron star matter using a meta-modeling framework based on Taylor expansion of the nuclear energy density around isospin-symmetric and saturation-density limits, with coefficients such as binding energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy, and their density derivatives. They examine how variations in the skewness parameter Q_sat and symmetry energy slope L_sym affect mass-radius relations and moments of inertia by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, and compare these predictions against multimessenger constraints from pulsar timing, gravitational-wave observations (GW170817, GW190425), and NICER X-ray pulse-profile modeling.

* Maximum neutron star mass measured: PSR J0740+6620 at 2.08 ± 0.07 solar masses (Fonseca 2021)
* PSR J1614-2230 mass: 1.908(16) solar masses (Demorest 2010, Arzoumanian 2018, Cromartie 2019)
* PSR J0348+0432 mass: 2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses (Antoniadis 2013)
* NICER radius constraints for PSR J0740+6620: 12.4–13.7 km (NICER 2021a, 2021b)
* NICER radius for PSR J0030+0451: 12.7–13.0 km (NICER 2019a, 2019b)
* Recent combined NICER/XMM-Newton reanalysis: radius ≈ 12.3 ± 0.6 km (Vinciguerra 2024)
* GW170817 effective tidal deformability: approximately 300 (+500, -190) under low-spin priors (Abbott 2019)
* GW190425 upper limit on effective tidal deformability: ≤ 600 (Abbott 2020)
* Upper bound on moment of inertia of pulsar A in PSR J0737-3039: I_A < 3.0 × 10^45 g cm^2 at 90% confidence (Kramer 2021)
* Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: E_sat ≈ -16.0 ± 1 MeV
* Symmetry energy at saturation: J_sym ≈ 32.0 ± 2 MeV
* Saturation density: ρ_sat ≈ 0.15–0.16 fm^-3
* Incompressibility range: 200 ≲ K_sat ≲ 300 MeV
* Skewness parameter range: -600 ≲ Q_sat ≲ 1000 MeV
* Magnetar surface magnetic field range: 10^14–10^15 G
* Upper limit on internal magnetic fields: B ≲ 10^18–10^19 G (Bocquet 1995, Broderick 2000)

## Context

This review extends the line of work on compact star structure and dense matter physics, building on the foundational Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff solutions (Tolman 1939, Oppenheimer 1939) and the Bethe-Weizsäcker mass formula. It synthesizes recent multimessenger constraints from LIGO-Virgo gravitational-wave detections (GW170817, GW190425), NICER X-ray observations, and pulsar timing measurements, and discusses unresolved issues including the hyperon puzzle (Bombaci 2017, Oertel 2017), the nature of pulsar glitches, and the possible emergence of deconfined quark phases with color superconductivity. The work enables future refined measurements of neutron star moments of inertia from the double pulsar system PSR J0737-3039 (Kramer 2021) and continued multimessenger constraints on the nuclear equation of state.
