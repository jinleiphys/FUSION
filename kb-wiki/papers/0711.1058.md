# Equivalent hyperon-nucleon interactions in low-momentum space

> Equivalent interactions in a low-momentum space for the $ΛN$, $ΣN$ and $ΞN$ interactions are calculated, using the SU$_6$ quark model potential as well as the Nijmegen OBEP model as the input bare interaction. Because the two-body scattering data has not been accumulated sufficiently to determine the hyperon-nucleon interactions unambiguously, the construction of the potential even in low-energy regions has to rely on a theoretical model. The equivalent interaction after removing high-momentum components is still model dependent. Because this model dependence reflects the character of the underlying potential model, it is instructive for better understanding of baryon-baryon interactions in the strangeness sector to study the low-momentum space $YN$ interactions.

# Equivalent hyperon-nucleon interactions in low-momentum space

## Key claim

The equivalent hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions in a low-momentum space (cutoff Λ = 2 fm⁻¹) remain model-dependent, unlike the NN case where different potentials collapse to a universal interaction; this residual model dependence reflects the character of the underlying bare potential model and is instructive for understanding baryon-baryon interactions in the strangeness sector, but the paper does not claim that the low-momentum YN interactions are universal.

## Method

The authors compute energy-independent equivalent interactions in a low-momentum space (P-space, k ≤ Λ = 2 fm⁻¹) for ΛN, ΣN, and ΞN channels by solving the decoupling condition for the mapping operator ω via half-on-shell T-matrices, using the SU₆ quark model potential fss2 (with energy-dependence removed via a renormalized RGM formalism) and the Nijmegen OBEP model NSC97f as bare interactions. The method extends the Fujii et al. discretization procedure to coupled baryon channels (ΛN-ΣN, ΞN-ΛΛ-ΣΣ, ΞN-ΣΛ-ΣΣ) and treats Pauli forbidden states in the quark model by projecting them out.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff momentum: Λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹ for the low-momentum space (also Λ = 3 fm⁻¹ for one ΞN case).
- For NN: the quark model fss2 and Nijmegen NSC97f give nearly identical low-momentum equivalent interactions in both ¹S₀ and ³S₁ partial waves.
- For ΛN: fss2 and NSC97f produce almost identical diagonal matrix elements in the low-momentum space for both ¹S₀ and ³S₁ channels, despite different bare matrix elements.
- For ΣN ³S₁ T=3/2: fss2 predicts repulsive interaction (due to quark Pauli effect), while NSC97f predicts attraction; the repulsive character persists in the low-momentum space for fss2.
- For ΣN ¹S₀ T=1/2: both models predict repulsive interaction; NSC97f becomes more repulsive after renormalizing high-momentum attractive components.
- For ΞN ¹S₀ T=0 (fss2 only): the low-momentum equivalent interaction shows large attraction originating from baryon-channel coupling (ΞN-ΛΛ-ΣΣ).
- For ΞN ¹S₀ T=1 (fss2): diagonal matrix elements change sign between Λ=3 fm⁻¹ and Λ=2 fm⁻¹, indicating strong channel mixing.
- For ΞN ³S₁ T=0 (fss2): the low-momentum equivalent interaction becomes slightly attractive.
- For ΞN ³S₁ T=1 (fss2): diagonal matrix elements are slightly negative due to ΞN-ΣΛ-ΣΣ coupling.

## Context

This work extends the low-momentum renormalization approach of Bogner et al. (2003) from the NN sector to the hyperon-nucleon sector, where scattering data is insufficient to constrain potentials. It compares the SU₆ quark model fss2 (Fujiwara et al., 2004) with the Nijmegen OBEP model NSC97f (Stoks and Rijken, 1999), following similar calculations by Schaefer et al. (2006) who used NSC potentials with Λ ≈ 2.5 fm⁻¹. The results enable future studies of hyperon single-particle potentials in nuclear matter and provide guidance for interpreting hypernuclear experiments at J-PARC, particularly for the ΞN interaction where experimental data is rudimentary. The paper notes that for channels with strong baryon coupling (e.g., ΣN ³S₁ T=1/2 in NSC97f, ΞN ¹S₀ T=1 in fss2), the coupling problem must be solved within the low-momentum space to obtain physically meaningful quantities.
