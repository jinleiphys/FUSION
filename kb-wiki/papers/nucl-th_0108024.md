# A renormalisation-group approach to two-body scattering with long-range forces

> We apply the renormalisation-group to two-body scattering by a combination of known long-range and unknown short-range forces. A crucial feature is that the low-energy effective theory is regulated by applying a cut-off in the basis of distorted waves for the long range potential. We illustrate the method by applying it to scattering in the presence of a repulsive 1/r^2 potential. We find a trivial fixed point, describing systems with weak short-range interactions, and a unstable fixed point. The expansion around the latter corresponds to a distorted-wave effective-range expansion.

# A renormalisation-group approach to two-body scattering with long-range forces

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a Wilsonian renormalisation-group (RG) approach, regulated by a cut-off in the basis of distorted waves for the long-range potential, can determine the power counting for short-range interactions in the presence of a known long-range force. For the specific case of a repulsive 1/r^2 potential, the method yields a trivial fixed point (weak short-range interactions) and an unstable nontrivial fixed point, with the expansion around the latter corresponding to a distorted-wave effective-range expansion.

## Method
The authors apply a momentum cut-off to the low-energy effective theory and demand that observables be independent of the cut-off, integrating out physics above the cut-off scale. The cut-off is imposed on the Green's function in the basis of distorted waves of the long-range potential, and the short-range interaction is represented by a delta-shell potential. The RG equation is derived by requiring the fully off-shell distorted-wave scattering matrix to be cut-off independent, then rescaling to obtain a differential equation for the dimensionless potential.

## Key numbers
- For the repulsive 1/r^2 potential, the "angular momentum" parameter is given by λ = √(βM + 1/4) − 1/2, where β is the strength of the potential and M is the particle mass.
- Perturbations around the trivial fixed point scale as Λ^(2n+2λ+1) and are assigned order d = 2(n+λ).
- Perturbations around the nontrivial fixed point scale as Λ^(2n-2λ-1) and are assigned order d = 2(n-λ-1).
- The distorted-wave effective-range expansion takes the form p^(2λ+1)[cot(δ̃_S) − cot(π(λ+1/2))] = Σ C_(2n) p̂^(2n), where p̂ = p/Λ.

## Context
This work extends the RG approach to two-body scattering with short-range forces developed by Birse, McGovern, and Richardson (1999) to include known long-range potentials. The method is illustrated for the repulsive 1/r^2 potential, which is analogous to the interaction in three-body systems such as quartet nd scattering in the limit of infinite two-body scattering length, as studied by Efimov (1970). The approach enables systematic power counting for effective field theories with long-range forces and has been applied to the Coulomb potential (elucidating results of Kong and Ravndal, 1999) and the attractive 1/r^2 potential.
