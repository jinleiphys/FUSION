# Fission fragment orientation and gamma ray emission anisotropy

> Experimental data on angular distributions of gamma rays emitted from binary and ternary spontaneous fission of $^{252}{\rm Cf}$ are analyzed. Their difference indicates that the alignment of fragments is higher in ternary fission than in binary one. The consequences of possible relation between the mechanism of ternary fission and the excitation of collective modes during the saddle-to-scission stage are discussed.

# Fission fragment orientation and gamma ray emission anisotropy

## Key claim

The difference in angular distributions of gamma rays between binary and ternary spontaneous fission of 252Cf indicates that fragment alignment is *higher* in ternary fission than in binary fission, contrary to the expectation that alpha particle emission would destroy alignment. The shift of the angular distribution maxima from 0° and 180° to 20–40° and 140–160° can be explained by a larger contribution from the fourth-rank spin-tensor τ40, rather than by a simple tilting of the alignment axis.

## Method

The author analyzes experimental gamma-ray angular distributions from Pilz and Neubert (1993) for both binary and ternary spontaneous fission of 252Cf using the standard helicity representation formalism. The fragment spin state is described by spin-tensors of orientation τQ0(J), and the gamma-ray angular distribution is calculated from the standard expression involving Racah functions for stretched E2 transitions along the yrast line. A Gaussian helicity distribution with width parameter σ is assumed for the fragments, and the resulting angular distributions are compared to the experimental data for the transition 8+ → 6+.

## Key numbers

- The mean number of gamma rays emitted in spontaneous fission of 252Cf is ⟨Nγ⟩ = 9.35 (from Ref. [14]).
- The previously measured angular anisotropy for ternary fission of 252Cf was w(t)(0°)/w(t)(90°) = 1.015 ± 0.022 (from Ref. [8]).
- The ternary fission probability is approximately 1/300 of binary fission (from Ref. [7]).
- The angular distributions in ternary fission show maxima at 20–40° and 140–160° with respect to the light fragment momentum, while binary fission maxima are at 0° and 180°.
- The analysis uses a Gaussian helicity distribution with parameter σ values of 0.5, 2.5, and 5.0 for a nucleus with spin J = 8.
- Example gamma-ray energies for 104Mo yrast transitions: 606 keV (8+ → 6+), 520 keV (6+ → 4+), 369 keV (4+ → 2+), 193 keV (2+ → 0+).
- Example gamma-ray energies for 144Ba yrast transitions: 511 keV (8+ → 6+), 432 keV (6+ → 4+), 331 keV (4+ → 2+), 199 keV (2+ → 0+).

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Pilz and Neubert (1993), who first measured angular distributions for ternary fission of 252Cf and interpreted the shift of maxima as evidence for tilting of fragment alignment by the recoiling alpha particle. The author instead proposes that the mechanism of ternary fission is closely related to the excitation of collective modes (bending, wriggling) during the saddle-to-scission stage, building on earlier discussions of such modes by previous authors. The analysis enables future comparison of high-spin isomer yields between ternary and binary fission, as well as investigation of the enhancement of ternary fission probability in uranium fissioning isomers found by other researchers.
