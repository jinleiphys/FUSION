# Quantum stress and torsion distributions in the deuteron

> Stress distributions in the deuteron are related to form factors of the asymmetric energy-momentum tensor through three-dimensional Fourier transforms. There are eleven such form factors, which we calculate in an impulse approximation. We compare the obtained form factors to prior results for the six form factors that have been previously calculated. We then elaborate on the formalism for relating the form factors to internal distributions of mass, mass flux, momentum, stresses, and forces, and obtain results for all of these distributions. We obtain the principal stresses for the symmetric part of the stress tensor, and show that the antisymmetric part describes reorientation of fermion spin by torsion stress when the nucleon moves between the S- and D-waves. Force distributions in the nucleons depend on the so-called non-conserved form factors through the Cauchy momentum equation, and are non-radial owing to the presence of tensor forces and spin-orbit coupling.

## Key claim

The paper calculates all eleven form factors of the asymmetric energy-momentum tensor (EMT) for the deuteron using a non-relativistic impulse approximation, finding that the one-body contributions already saturate the momentum and spin sum rules, but that local energy-momentum conservation is violated because exchange contributions from force carriers are omitted.

## Method

The authors compute one-body contributions to the deuteron EMT form factors by convolving nucleon EMT form factors with the non-relativistic deuteron wave function (AV18 potential) in coordinate space, using Eq. (1.1) to relate deuteron matrix elements to nucleon matrix elements. The eleven form factors are isolated by projecting onto unpolarized, vector-polarized, and tensor-polarized deuteron ensembles, and analytic expressions are given in terms of the radial S- and D-wave functions u(r) and w(r). The calculation is performed in the impulse approximation, meaning only proton and neutron contributions are included while inter-nucleon force carrier contributions are omitted.

## Key numbers

- A_U(0) = 1, saturating the momentum sum rule from one-body contributions alone (Eq. 4.5)
- A_T(0) = (2m_N)^2 Q_d, where Q_d is the deuteron quadrupole moment (Eq. 4.5)
- J(0) = 1, saturating the spin sum rule from one-body contributions alone (Eq. 4.9)
- S(0) = sum over nucleons of (1 - 3/2 P_D) S_N(0), where P_D is the D-wave probability and S_N(0) is the quark spin contribution to the nucleon (Eq. 4.9)
- The deuteron depolarization factor (1 - 3/2 P_D) is of order 90-95% for realistic deuteron wave functions (text after Eq. 4.9)

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of deuteron EMT form factors by Freese and Cosyn (2022), He and Zahed (2023, 2024), and Panteleeva, Epelbaum, Gasparyan and Gegelia (2024), which each obtained only six of the eleven form factors. The present calculation is the first to obtain all eleven form factors, including the four non-conserved form factors (c̅_U, c̅_T1, c̅_T2, s̅) that quantify forces felt by nucleons through the Cauchy momentum equation. The results enable detailed spatial maps of mass, momentum, stress, and force distributions inside the deuteron, and provide a baseline for future calculations that include two-body exchange contributions.
