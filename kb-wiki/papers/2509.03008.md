# Origin of nucleon mass in the light of PSR J0614-3329 with quark-hadron crossover

> The recent NICER observation of PSR J0614-3329, revealing the smallest reliably measured neutron star radius of $R = 10.29^{+1.01}_{-0.86}$ km at mass $M = 1.44^{+0.06}_{-0.07} M_\odot$, provides an unprecedented constraint on the equation of state of dense matter. We investigate the implications of this measurement for the origin of nucleon mass within the parity doublet model framework, which naturally incorporates both chiral variant and chiral invariant mass components. We construct unified equations of state by employing the parity doublet model with isovector scalar meson $a_0(980)$ for hadronic matter up to twice nuclear saturation density, smoothly connected to a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type quark model at higher densities through a crossover transition. By systematically varying the chiral invariant mass $m_0$ and quark matter parameters, we determine which values simultaneously satisfy all current astrophysical constraints, including gravitational wave observations from GW170817, NICER measurements of several pulsars, and the existence of two-solar-mass neutron stars. The inclusion of PSR J0614-3329 dramatically refines the allowed range of the chiral invariant mass from the previous constraint of $580~\text{MeV} \lesssim m_0 \lesssim 860~\text{MeV}$ to $800~\text{MeV} \lesssim m_0 \lesssim 860~\text{MeV}$, raising the lower bound by approximately 220 MeV. This result indicates that the chiral invariant mass must constitute at least 85\% of the nucleon mass, challenging the traditional picture of nucleon mass generation through spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking alone and highlighting the importance of gluon condensation and other non-chiral mechanisms.

# Origin of nucleon mass in the light of PSR J0614-3329 with quark-hadron crossover

## Key claim

The chiral invariant mass component of the nucleon must constitute at least 85% of the nucleon mass (approximately 800-860 MeV out of 940 MeV), challenging the traditional picture where nucleon mass arises predominantly from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. This conclusion depends on the specific assumption that a smooth crossover transition between hadronic and quark matter occurs in the density range of 2-5 times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors constructed unified equations of state by combining a parity doublet model (PDM) with isovector scalar meson a0(980) contributions for hadronic matter up to twice nuclear saturation density (2n0), smoothly interpolated to a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type quark model at higher densities through a crossover transition. They systematically varied the chiral invariant mass m0 from 600 to 900 MeV and quark matter parameters (diquark coupling H and vector repulsion strength gV), then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations. The allowed parameter space was constrained by requiring consistency with gravitational wave observations from GW170817, NICER measurements of multiple pulsars including PSR J0614-3329, and the existence of two-solar-mass neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- PSR J0614-3329 radius: R = 10.29^{+1.01}_{-0.86} km at mass M = 1.44^{+0.06}_{-0.07} solar masses, the smallest reliably measured neutron star radius
- Previous constraint on chiral invariant mass: 580 MeV less than or equal to m0 less than or equal to 860 MeV
- New constraint after including PSR J0614-3329: 800 MeV less than or equal to m0 less than or equal to 860 MeV
- Lower bound raised by approximately 220 MeV
- Chiral invariant mass must constitute at least 85% of the nucleon mass (940 MeV)
- Nuclear saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Saturation properties used: binding energy 16 MeV, incompressibility 240 MeV, symmetry energy 31 MeV, slope parameter L = 57.7 MeV
- Hadronic model applied up to 2n0, quark model applied above 5n0, interpolation in the range 2-5 n0

## Context

This work extends previous parity doublet model analyses by Gao (2024) and Kong (2025) that had established the broader 580-860 MeV range for m0, and is directly motivated by the recent NICER observation of PSR J0614-3329 by Mauviard (2025). The results align with lattice QCD simulations by Aarts (2015-2019) and QCD sum rule calculations by Kim (2020-2021) and Lee (2023) that suggest a significant fraction of nucleon mass persists in the chirally restored phase, as well as skyrmion crystal calculations by Ma (2013). The finding that gluon condensation and non-chiral mechanisms dominate nucleon mass generation challenges the traditional spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking picture and enables future work to refine the upper bound of m0 through continued observations of compact neutron stars with small radii and massive pulsars.
