# A Baseline Study of the Event-shape and Multiplicity Dependence of Chemical Freeze-out Parameters in Proton-Proton Collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV Using PYTHIA8

> The event-shape and multiplicity dependence of the chemical freeze-out temperature ($T_{\text{ch}}$), freeze-out radius ($R$), and strangeness saturation factor ($γ_{s}$) are obtained by studying the particle yields from the PYTHIA8 Monte Carlo event generator in proton-proton (pp) collisions at the centre-of-mass $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV. Spherocity is one of the transverse event-shape techniques to distinguish jetty and isotropic events in high-energy collisions and helps in looking into various observables in a more differential manner. In this study, spherocity~classes are divided into three categories, namely (i) spherocity integrated, (ii) isotropic, and~(iii) jetty. The~chemical freeze-out parameters are extracted using a statistical thermal model as a function of the spherocity class and charged particle multiplicity in the canonical, strangeness canonical, and grand canonical ensembles. A clear observation of the multiplicity and spherocity class dependence of $T_{\text{ch}}$, $R$, and $γ_{s}$ is observed. A final state multiplicity, $N_{\rm ch}\geq$ 30 in the forward multiplicity acceptance of the ALICE detector appears to be a thermodynamic limit, where the freeze-out parameters become almost independent of the ensembles. This~study plays an important role in understanding the particle production mechanism in high-multiplicity pp collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies in view of a finite hadronic phase lifetime in small systems.

## Key claim

Using PYTHIA8 simulations of proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV, the chemical freeze-out temperature, freeze-out radius, and strangeness saturation factor show a clear dependence on both charged-particle multiplicity and event shape (spherocity), but only when the final-state multiplicity reaches Nch ≥ 30 do the freeze-out parameters become nearly independent of which statistical ensemble (canonical, strangeness canonical, or grand canonical) is used, indicating a thermodynamic limit.

## Method

The authors generated approximately 250 million inelastic non-diffractive proton-proton collisions at √s = 13 TeV using PYTHIA8 (version 8.235) with the Monash 2013 tune and MPI-based color reconnection. Events were classified by transverse spherocity into three categories (spherocity integrated, isotropic, and jetty) and divided into ten V0M multiplicity classes matching ALICE detector acceptance. Particle yields (π, K, p, φ, Λ) at mid-rapidity were fitted with the THERMUS statistical thermal model using canonical, strangeness canonical, and grand canonical ensembles to extract Tch, R, and γs as functions of multiplicity and spherocity class.

## Key numbers

- The highest multiplicity class (V0M1) corresponds to Nch = 50–140 charged particles in the V0 detector acceptance.
- A thermodynamic limit is observed at Nch ≥ 30, where freeze-out parameters become ensemble-independent.
- The spherocity selection defines jetty events as 0 ≤ S0 < 0.29 (lowest 20% of events) and isotropic events as 0.64 < S0 ≤ 1 (highest 20% of events).
- The scaled PYTHIA8 charged-particle pT spectra agree with ALICE data within 10–20% at low pT and are consistent with unity at intermediate and high pT.
- χ²/NDF values for the highest multiplicity class (V0M1) range from 22.138/2 (jetty, strangeness canonical) to 33.681/2 (isotropic, grand canonical).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chemical freeze-out in small systems (Sharma 2018, Rath 2019) that showed high-multiplicity pp collisions yield parameters similar to peripheral Pb-Pb collisions, and complements kinetic freeze-out studies using event-shape techniques in pp collisions at 13 TeV (Tripathy 2019, Khuntia 2018). The results provide a baseline for future experimental measurements of event-shape-dependent chemical freeze-out parameters at the LHC, particularly to test whether the thermalization-like features produced by color reconnection and multipartonic interactions in PYTHIA8 correspond to actual thermal behavior in high-multiplicity pp events.
