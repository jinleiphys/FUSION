# Three-boson relativistic bound states with zero-range interaction

> For the zero-range interaction providing a given mass M_2 of the two-body bound state, the mass M_3 of the relativistic three-boson state is calculated. We have found that the three-body system exists only when M_2 is greater than a critical value M_c approximately 1.43 m (m is the constituent mass). For M_2=M_c the mass M_3 turns into zero and for M_2<M_c there is no solution with real value of M_3.

## Key claim

For three equal-mass bosons interacting via zero-range forces in a relativistic Light-Front Dynamics framework, the three-body bound state exists only when the two-body bound state mass \(M_2\) is greater than a critical value \(M_c \approx 1.43 m\) (where \(m\) is the constituent mass); at \(M_2 = M_c\) the three-body mass \(M_3\) becomes zero, and for \(M_2 < M_c\) there is no solution with a real value of \(M_3\), which the authors interpret as a relativistic counterpart of the Thomas collapse.

## Method

The authors used the explicitly covariant formulation of Light-Front Dynamics, defining the wave function on a light-front plane determined by a null four-vector \(\omega\). They derived an integral equation for the Faddeev amplitude \(\Gamma_{12}\) for three equal-mass bosons interacting via a zero-range (contact) interaction, where the interaction kernel is replaced by a constant \(\lambda\). The two-body input is fixed by requiring a given two-body bound state mass \(M_2\), which determines the coupling constant \(\lambda\) via a logarithmically divergent integral that is regularized by taking the difference \(I(M_2) - I(M_{12})\) to obtain a finite off-shell scattering amplitude. The resulting equation for \(\Gamma_{12}\) was solved numerically for the ground state, with the integration limits for the internal variables \(R_\perp\) and \(x\) taken over their full physical ranges (\(0 \leq x \leq 1\), \(0 \leq R_\perp < \infty\)) rather than imposing a cutoff that restricts the domain to \(M_{12}^2 > 0\).

## Key numbers

- The critical two-body bound state mass below which no real three-body solution exists is \(M_c \approx 1.43 m\).
- In the limit of zero two-body binding (\(M_2 \to 2m\), \(B_2 \to 0\)), the three-body binding energy is \(B_3 \approx 0.012 m\).
- For \(M_2 \to 0\), the three-body mass squared \(M_3^2\) tends to approximately \(-11.6\) (in units where \(m=1\)).
- The three-body bound state exists for two-body mass values in the range \(M_c \approx 1.43 m \leq M_2 \leq 2 m\).

## Context

This work extends the study of zero-range interactions in few-body systems from the nonrelativistic regime, where the Thomas collapse (unbounded binding energy as the interaction radius goes to zero) occurs, to the relativistic regime. It contradicts earlier relativistic calculations by Noyes (no year given) and Tobias (no year given), which concluded that relativistic repulsion prevents any collapse and yields a finite three-body binding energy for all two-body masses. The present results show that while the Thomas collapse is avoided, a different "relativistic collapse" occurs at a finite two-body mass, where the three-body mass vanishes. This work enables further study of the limits of zero-range models in relativistic few-body physics and the role of off-shell effects in the three-body problem.
