# Extraction of dihadron-jet correlations with rigorous flow-background subtraction in a multiphase transport model

> Dihadron azimuthal correlations in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{S_{NN}}$=200 GeV have been explored by using a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model. In order to obtain the contributions from jet-medium interactions, the combined harmonic flow background is subtracted from the raw dihadron correlation functions. The signals are compared in three associated transverse momentum ($p_{T}^{assoc}$) bins: 0.2-0.8 GeV/c, 0.8-1.4 GeV/c and 1.4-2.0 GeV/c from central to semi-peripheral geometries. The medium modifications are observed from changes in the signal shape and the relative jet contribution has been obtained within the change in the centrality from peripheral to central one. A strong $p_{T}^{assoc}$ dependence of the RMS width of jet correlation function is observed within the central geometry bin, i.e. 0-10$%$.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT model with a rigorous flow-background subtraction that treats two different background formulas as upper and lower limits, the jet-medium correlation signal in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is extracted, and the away-side jet relative contribution in central 0-10% events is found to be less than 5%.

## Method

The study uses the string-melting version of the AMPT model to simulate Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, with hadronic rescattering turned off to focus on partonic-stage interactions. Dihadron azimuthal correlations are constructed between trigger particles (p_T > 2.5 GeV/c) and associated particles (p_T <= 2.5 GeV/c) within |eta| < 1.0. Two harmonic-flow background formulas are employed: one using event-averaged products of single-particle flow coefficients (Eq. 1, upper bound) and one using products of event-averaged coefficients (Eq. 4, lower bound), with flow coefficients calculated from the initial parton spatial configuration to minimize nonflow contributions up to fifth order.

## Key numbers

* In central 0-10% collisions, the away-side jet relative contribution is less than 5% across all three associated p_T bins.
* Near-side jet relative contribution in 0-10% centrality ranges from 0.7%-1.8% for p_T^assoc 0.2-0.8 GeV/c, 1.1%-3.5% for 0.8-1.4 GeV/c, and 1.6%-5.7% for 1.4-2.0 GeV/c.
* Away-side jet relative contribution in 0-10% centrality ranges from 1.3%-3.1% for p_T^assoc 0.2-0.8 GeV/c, 1.5%-4.4% for 0.8-1.4 GeV/c, and 0.5%-3.4% for 1.4-2.0 GeV/c.
* In peripheral 50-80% collisions, near-side jet relative contribution ranges from 2.0%-7.1% (0.2-0.8 GeV/c) to 9.1%-18.1% (1.4-2.0 GeV/c), and away-side from 3.0%-7.8% to 8.2%-16.2%.
* The difference between the theoretical normalization factor and the ZYAM adjustment is less than 3%.

## Context

This work extends prior dihadron correlation studies at RHIC that observed an away-side double-peak structure (soft-soft-ex, sideward-peak2, dihadron-2009) and addresses the challenge of separating harmonic flow background from jet-medium signals, as previously discussed by Ko and collaborators (Ko, GL). The use of two background formulas as upper and lower bounds follows the approach of the STAR collaboration (STAR-dihadron-1, STAR-dihadron-2). The results enable more precise extraction of jet-medium interaction signals and may help constrain models of jet energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma, such as shock wave models (Stoecker), gluon radiation (Koch), and medium-induced gluon bremsstrahlung (large-angle, opaque-media-radiation).
