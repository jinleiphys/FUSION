# Collinear parton splitting in early thermalization and chemical equilibration

> Early local equilibration of a hot medium created in high-energy heavy ion collisions has been one of the long standing issues in hadron physics. The glasma model predicts that the medium initially has a large amount of high-momentum gluons but few quarks. We develop a phenomenological model based on collinear splitting and recombination of quarks and gluons for a simplified description of thermalization and chemical equilibration. We find that both could be achieved in a short time but chemical equilibration is slower than thermalization.

## Key claim

The authors develop a phenomenological model based on collinear parton splitting and recombination to describe early thermalization and chemical equilibration in high-energy heavy ion collisions, finding that both processes could be achieved in a short time but chemical equilibration is slower than thermalization. The key hedge is that the model uses a simplified one-dimensional non-expanding system with roughly estimated coefficients, and the results are qualitative rather than quantitative.

## Method

The model evolves gluon and quark phase-space distributions using collinear splitting rates based on the Altarelli-Parisi splitting functions (for processes g→gg, q→qg, and g→qq̅) with a phenomenological emission rate Γ ∼ α_s^{1/2} (q̂/p)^{1/2}. Recombination terms are introduced to enforce the second law of thermodynamics, ensuring the splitting and recombination rates cancel at equilibrium. Off-shell effects from parton-medium interactions are incorporated via a relativistic Fokker-Planck equation with drag and diffusion coefficients estimated from weakly-coupled thermal media.

## Key numbers

- The saturation momentum is set to Q_s = 2 GeV and the strong coupling constant to α_s = 0.2 for the initial CGC-like gluon distribution.
- The initial time is chosen as t = 0.2 fm/c, of the order of the decoherence time O(1/Q_s).
- In the pure gauge system (N_f = 0), the equilibrium temperature is T = 0.711 GeV, set to match the momentum density of the initial distribution.
- In the quark-gluon system with N_f = 3, the equilibrium temperature is 0.458 GeV due to additional quark degrees of freedom.
- The gluon distribution shows near-complete transition to equilibrium by t = 0.8 fm/c in the pure gauge system.
- Chemical equilibration of the quark distribution occurs in approximately 2 fm/c in the current parameter settings.

## Context

This work extends the line of research on early equilibration in heavy ion collisions, including parton cascade models (Geiger 1991, Wong 1996, Molnar, Xu 2004), bottom-up thermalization (Baier 2000, Arnold 2003, Mueller 2005), and recent Bose condensate approaches (Huang 2013, Blaizot 2013). The model differs from these by providing a simple phenomenological framework that does not explicitly use the Boltzmann equation, introduces recombination from thermodynamic principles, and explicitly treats quarks and chemical equilibration for the transition from gluon condensate to quark-gluon plasma. The results suggest that collinear splitting processes are essential in early-stage dynamics and that the off-equilibrium distributions could provide initial dissipative quantities for subsequent viscous hydrodynamic modeling.
