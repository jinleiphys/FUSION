# Seeing phi meson through the dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions

> Dilepton spectra from the decay of phi mesons produced in heavy-ion collisions at SIS/GSI energies ($\sim 2$ GeV/nucleon) are studied in the relativistic transport model. We include phi mesons produced from baryon-baryon, pion-baryon, and kaon-antikaon collisions. The cross sections for the first two processes are obtained from an one-boson-exchange model, while that for the last process is taken to be the Breit-Wigner form through the phi meson resonance. For dileptons with invariant mass near the phi meson peak, we also include contributions from neutron-proton bremsstrahlung, pion-pion annihilation, and the decay of rho and omega mesons produced in baryon-baryon and meson-baryon collisions. Effects due to medium modifications of the kaon and vector (rho, omega and phi) meson properties are investigated. We find that the kaon medium effects lead to a broadening of the dilepton spectrum as a result of the increase of phi meson decay width. Furthermore, the dropping of phi meson mass in nuclear medium leads to a shoulder structure in the dilepton spectrum besides the main peak at the bare phi meson mass. The experimental measurement of the dilepton spectra from heavy-ion collisions is expected to provide useful information about the phi meson properties in dense matter.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the dilepton invariant mass spectrum from phi meson decay in heavy-ion collisions at SIS/GSI energies (around 2 GeV/nucleon) can reveal medium modifications of both kaon and phi meson properties, but the observability of these effects depends on the specific scenario: kaon medium effects broaden the phi peak, while a dropping phi meson mass creates a shoulder structure alongside the main peak at the bare phi mass.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic transport model to simulate central Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 AGeV, including phi meson production from baryon-baryon, pion-baryon, and kaon-antikaon collisions. Cross sections for the first two processes were obtained from an one-boson-exchange model, while the kaon-antikaon channel used a Breit-Wigner form through the phi meson resonance. Background contributions from neutron-proton bremsstrahlung, pion-pion annihilation, and decays of rho and omega mesons were also included, and the dilepton spectra were computed with a 10 MeV bin size corresponding to the HADES detector resolution.

## Key numbers

- Phi meson free-space decay width: about 4.4 MeV
- In QCD sum-rule calculations, rho and omega meson masses drop by about 20% at normal nuclear matter density (rho_0), while phi meson mass drops by about 2% at rho_0 assuming nucleon strangeness content y=0.15
- The phi meson peak in the dilepton spectrum (without medium effects) is about a factor of 4-5 above the background from pion-pion annihilation in central Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 AGeV
- The histogram bin size for the dilepton spectra is 10 MeV, corresponding roughly to the HADES mass resolution in the phi meson mass region

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of dilepton production at BEVALAC energies by Xiong et al. (1990) and Wolf et al. (1990), and builds on the authors' previous study of phi meson production through the K-anti-K decay channel (Chung et al., 1997). The paper addresses the feasibility of detecting phi meson mass reduction in nuclear medium using the dilepton channel, which will be measured by the HADES detector at GSI. The results are relevant for understanding medium modifications of vector mesons predicted by QCD sum-rule calculations (Hatsuda and Lee, 1992) and for testing the suggestion by Shuryak and collaborators (1991) that phi meson production can probe kaon medium effects.
