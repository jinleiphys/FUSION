# Parity Violation in Neutron-Nucleus Collisions at Very Low Energies

> We investigate parity violation in neutron-nucleus collisions at very low energy region $(E \leq 100 \ev)$, including resonance states. We use the coupled channel formalism for resonances with radiative emission. The parity mixing between resonances with opposite parities is taken into account. Finally, our theory is applied to $n-^{139}$La collision and relevant remarks are given.

## Key claim

Parity violation in neutron-nucleus collisions at very low energies (E ≤ 100 eV) can be enhanced by orders of magnitude when a parity-violating nuclear potential (PVNP) mixes two closely lying s-wave and p-wave bound states of the same spin but opposite parity, even though parity mixing in the potential scattering channels remains small (of order 10⁻⁶–10⁻⁷ in amplitude). The authors apply this mechanism specifically to the case of n-¹³⁹La, where the large observed left-right asymmetry A_L ≈ 0.098 is attributed to Z⁰ boson exchange between the valence neutron and the core nucleus.

## Method

The authors formulate a coupled-channel Schrödinger equation for neutron-nucleus scattering that includes s-wave and p-wave parent channels (bound states) and daughter channels (continuum scattering states with optical potentials). They introduce parity-conserving couplings V_Wj between parent and daughter channels, then add a parity-violating nuclear potential V_X (from Z⁰ or W± boson exchange in the standard model) that mixes the s-wave and p-wave bound states. The resulting scattering amplitudes are expressed in a Breit-Wigner form with a 2×2 matrix that includes both diagonal neutron widths and an off-diagonal mixing term proportional to η√(Γⁿ_s Γⁿ_p), where η is a phase factor. The formalism is applied to the p-wave resonance in ¹⁴⁰La* at 0.734 eV, using the average level spacing D ∼ 100 eV and the Z⁰ exchange matrix element calculated in a simple shell-model picture.

## Key numbers

- For n-¹³⁹La, the Z⁰ exchange matrix element between s₁/₂ and p₁/₂ states is ⟨s₁/₂|H_PV|p₁/₂⟩ ≈ −1.7 eV
- The parity mixing parameter P_s = |⟨s₁/₂|H_PV|p₁/₂⟩/(E_p − E_s)|² ≈ 4.2 × 10⁻³ for the ¹³⁹La case, using E_s = −48.6 eV
- The ratio of s-wave to p-wave neutron widths for ¹⁴⁰La* is estimated as Γⁿ_s/Γⁿ_p ∼ 10⁻², leading to A_L ∼ 10⁻¹
- The p-wave reduced width for ¹³⁹La is γ_p² = 4.1 × 10⁻⁵
- For the ¹³⁹La p-wave resonance at E_p = 0.734 ± 0.005 eV, the experimental asymmetry is A_L = +0.098 ± 0.003
- The average level spacing between resonances of the same spin-parity is D ∼ 100 eV
- The number of configurations N in the resonance wave functions is N ∼ several × 10⁴ (from several MeV / 100 eV)
- The W± exchange matrix element is estimated as ∼ 0.01 eV, much smaller than the Z⁰ exchange value

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Yamaguchi (1988, 1991) and Cho, Kasari and Yamaguchi (1993) for parity violation in neutron resonances, providing a more complete coupled-channel treatment that explicitly includes the parity mixing between bound states. The paper addresses the large parity violation effects experimentally observed in medium-to-heavy nuclei by Alfimenko (1983), Masuda (1988, 1989), Yuan (1991), Masaike (1993), and Shimizu (1993), particularly the systematic positive sign of A_L seen in many nuclei. The theory enables quantitative estimates of the Z⁰ exchange contribution to parity mixing and predicts that negative A_L values should be found in future experiments, while noting that the required identification of s-wave and p-wave resonances sharing the same spin has not yet been experimentally confirmed.
