# Interplay between QCD and nuclear responses

> We establish the interrelation between the QCD scalar response of the nuclear medium and its response to a scalar probe coupled to nucleons, such as the scalar meson responsible for the nuclear binding. The relation that we derive applies at the nucleonic as well as at the nuclear levels. Non trivial consequences follow. One concerns the scalar QCD susceptibility of the nucleon. The other opens the possibility of relating medium effects in the scalar meson exchange of nuclear physics to QCD lattice studies of the nucleon mass.

# Interplay between QCD and nuclear responses

## Key claim

The paper establishes a universal scaling relation between the QCD scalar response of nuclear matter and the nuclear response to a scalar probe coupled to nucleons, showing that this relation holds at both the nucleonic and nuclear levels. The authors demonstrate that the proportionality factor linking these responses is the same for nuclear excitations and for single-nucleon excitations, though they note that the linear sigma model prediction for the nucleon scalar susceptibility from the scalar meson is too large compared to lattice data, indicating that additional mechanisms (such as confinement) must contribute.

## Method

The authors derive the relation between the QCD scalar susceptibility of the nuclear medium and the nuclear scalar response using a nuclear chiral model with scalar and vector meson exchange at the mean-field level, working from the expression of the quark condensate modification in the nuclear medium. They then extend the analysis to incorporate two-pion propagator effects and generalize the relation to include all components of the individual nucleon contribution, regardless of origin, using a hybrid model where the nucleon mass arises partly from coupling to the condensate and partly from confinement. The lattice data on nucleon mass evolution with pion mass is used to extract phenomenological values for the non-pionic sigma commutator and susceptibility.

## Key numbers

- The non-pionic sigma commutator from lattice data: σ_N^(non-pion) ≈ 29 MeV (using a₂ ≈ 1.5 GeV⁻¹, a₄ ≈ -0.5 GeV⁻³) or 20 MeV (using a₂ ≈ 1 GeV⁻¹, a₄ ≈ -0.23 GeV⁻³)
- The non-pionic nucleon scalar susceptibility from lattice data: χ_S^(N, non-pion) ≈ -5.4 GeV⁻¹ (using a₄ ≈ -0.5 GeV⁻³)
- The scalar charge of the nucleon from the scalar field: Q_S^s ≈ 2.4 (using 2m_q = 12 MeV and a₂ ≈ 1.5 GeV⁻¹) or Q_S^s ≈ 1.66 (using a₂ ≈ 1 GeV⁻¹)
- The mean scalar field at normal nuclear density: |s̄(ρ₀)| ≈ 21 MeV
- The linear sigma model prediction for a₄ from the scalar meson contribution: -a₄ = 3.1 GeV⁻³, much larger than the lattice value of 0.5 GeV⁻³
- The bag susceptibility from the quark-meson coupling model: χ_S^(N, bag) ≈ +0.25 R ≈ 1 GeV⁻¹ (for R = 0.8 fm), corresponding to a₄^(bag) ≈ +0.1 GeV⁻³
- At normal nuclear density, the medium correction to the scalar meson mass squared is approximately 0.06 GeV² (for g_S = 10), representing about a 6% decrease of the sigma mass from m_σ = 0.75 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Ericson, Chanfray, and collaborators (2003, 2005) on the mixing of nuclear response into the QCD scalar susceptibility, and addresses the reciprocal problem of how QCD scalar response influences ordinary nuclear scalar response. The authors connect their results to lattice simulations of nucleon mass evolution with pion mass by Leinweber, Thomas, and Young (2004), and to the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1988, 2005). The relation derived enables the inference of medium effects in scalar meson exchange from QCD lattice studies, and the authors note that the linear sigma model's failure to account for saturation properties is linked to its prediction of too large a susceptibility from the scalar meson, suggesting that confinement effects (as in the quark-meson coupling model) provide a cancellation mechanism.
