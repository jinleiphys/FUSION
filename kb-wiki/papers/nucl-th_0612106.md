# Shell-model study of mirror nuclei with modern charge-dependent \textit{NN} potential

> The properties of $T=1/2$ mirror nuclei in the $fp$ shell have been studied with a microscopic residual interaction. The isospin-nonconserving interaction is derived from a high-precision charge-dependent Bonn \textit{NN} potential using the folded-diagram renormalization method. The level structures of the nuclei are calculated, obtaining excellent agreements with experimental observations till the $0f_{7/2}$ band termination. The role played by isospin symmetry breaking on ground-state displacement energies and mirror energy differences is discussed, which may help to explain the long-standing Nolen-Schiffer anomaly. Electromagnetic and weak transition properties are presented, with discussions on the asymmetry in analogous transitions.

## Key claim

The isospin-nonconserving effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential can reproduce experimental level structures of T=1/2 mirror nuclei in the lower fp shell "till the 0f_{7/2} band termination," but the study acknowledges that the Coulomb effect on single-particle energies is still absent from the Hamiltonian, leaving open the question of whether charge-dependent strong force alone can fully explain the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model diagonalizations using the OXBASH code in the fp major shell, employing an effective Hamiltonian derived from the high-precision CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential via the folded-diagram renormalization method. The isospin-nonconserving interaction was decomposed into monopole and multipole parts, with the Coulomb force and charge dependence in the strong force embedded at the two-body level. For comparison, calculations were also performed with the isospin-conserving KB3 interaction.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state ISB contribution to mirror displacement energies (MDE) for T=1/2 mirror pairs: 0.359 MeV (A=45), 0.604 MeV (A=47), 0.658 MeV (A=49), and 0.936 MeV (A=51)
- Ground-state ISB contribution to MDE for T=3/2 mirror pairs: 1.269 MeV (A=45), 1.586 MeV (A=47), 2.197 MeV (A=49), and 2.579 MeV (A=51)
- For the A=49 mirror pair, the calculated 25/2^- state in 49Cr lies at 8758 keV, compared to an observed state at 8879 keV
- B(E2) values for the 11/2^-_1 to 7/2^-_1 transition in 45Ti range from 105 to 132 e^2fm^4 across different bases, and from 134 to 158 e^2fm^4 in 45V
- B(M1) values for the 9/2^-_1 to 7/2^-_1 transition in 45Ti and 45V are 0.40 and 0.42 mu_N^2, respectively
- B(GT) values for the 9/2^-_1 to 7/2^-_1 transition are 0.12 (beta+) and 0.13 (CE) in the A=45 pair

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model studies of mirror nuclei in the lower fp shell by Bentley (2000, 2006), Brandolini (2005), and Poves (2001), and addresses the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly first identified by Nolen and Schiffer (1970s). The authors build on the approach of Brown (2000), who added a charge-symmetry-breaking term to the Skyrme interaction, and of Muther (1999), who studied CSB in partial waves with L>0. The results enable future investigations of whether the charge-dependent strong force can fully account for the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, and provide predictions for electromagnetic and weak transition asymmetries that can be tested by future experiments on fp-shell mirror nuclei.
