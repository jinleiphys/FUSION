# The influence of electromagnetic fields on the generation of the directed and elliptic flows of heavy quark in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the impact of self-generated electromagnetic fields (EMF) on the charm quarks momentum evolution in the partonic and hadronic medium created in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energy within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) off-shell transport approach. In the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase, the charm quark interacts with the off-shell partons whose mass and widths are given by the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model (DQPM), which can reproduce the lattice QCD thermodynamics. The background electromagnetic fields are computed dynamically within the PHSD considering both the spectators and participants protons as well as newly produced charged hadrons, quarks, and antiquarks, which reflects naturally the electric conductivity $σ_{el}$ of the medium. We study the directed and elliptic flow of the $D$ mesons in the presence of the electromagnetic fields. We find that electromagnetically induced splitting in the $D$ meson $v_1$ through $D^0$ and $\overline{D}^0$ mesons is consistent with the experimental data. Furthermore, we notice that the splitting in the heavy quark $v_1$ as a function of $p_T$ is more prominent as a probe of the produced electromagnetic fields. However, we find only a small impact of electromagnetic fields on the heavy quark elliptic flow $v_2$.

## Key claim

The self-generated electromagnetic fields (EMF) in heavy-ion collisions produce a measurable splitting in the directed flow \(v_1\) between \(D^0\) and \(\overline{D}^0\) mesons that is consistent with STAR data, but the effect is mild and almost zero in the rapidity range explored by the experiment; the splitting becomes more visible at large forward-backward rapidity and as a function of transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors used the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) off-shell transport approach, where charm quarks are produced via PYTHIA with Glauber spatial positions and interact with off-shell partons whose masses and widths are given by the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model (DQPM). The electromagnetic fields are computed dynamically from the Liénard-Wiechert potentials of all charged particles (spectators, participants, and newly produced quarks and hadrons), and the Lorentz force is applied to the propagating quasiparticles. The directed flow \(v_1 = \langle \cos(\phi) \rangle\) and elliptic flow \(v_2 = \langle \cos(2\phi) \rangle\) of \(D\) mesons are then evaluated with and without the electromagnetic fields for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- The initial magnetic field at RHIC energies can reach \(eB \sim 5-50\ m_\pi^2\), which is several orders of magnitude larger than fields at the surface of magnetars.
- For 10-80% central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the directed flow splitting \(\Delta v_1 = v_1(D^0) - v_1(\overline{D}^0)\) as a function of rapidity is consistent with STAR data and is almost zero in the measured rapidity range.
- The hadronization coalescence process operates when local energy density falls below 0.75 GeV/fm\(^3\), and forced fragmentation occurs below 0.4 GeV/fm\(^3\).
- The electromagnetic fine-structure constant used is \(\alpha_{em} = e^2/4\pi \simeq 1/137\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of heavy quark directed flow as a probe of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions, notably the predictions by Das (2016) that heavy quark \(v_1\) would be an order of magnitude larger than light hadron \(v_1\), and the measurements by STAR (2019) and ALICE (2019) of \(D\) meson directed flow. The authors improve upon earlier calculations (e.g., Oliva 2020, Chatterjee 2018) by using a self-consistent dynamical EMF treatment within PHSD that naturally accounts for the temperature-dependent electric conductivity \(\sigma_{el}\) of the medium, rather than relying on spectator-only approximations or constant \(\sigma_{el}\). The results enable future experimental measurements of \(v_1\) splitting as a function of \(p_T\) to serve as a novel probe of the electromagnetic fields, and the authors plan to extend the study to LHC energies.
