# Many-body approach to superfluid nuclei in axial geometry

> Starting from a general many-body fermionic Hamiltonian, we derive the equations of motion (EOM) for nucleonic propagators in a superfluid system. The resulting EOM is of the Dyson type formulated in the basis of Bogoliubov's quasiparticles. As the leading contributions to the dynamical kernel of this EOM in strongly-coupled regimes contain phonon degrees of freedom in various channels, an efficient method of calculating phonon's characteristics is required to successfully model these kernels. The traditional quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) solvers are typically used for this purpose in nuclear structure calculations, however, they become very prohibitive in non-spherical geometries. In this work, by linking the notion of the quasiparticle-phonon vertex to the variation of the Bogoliubov's Hamiltonian, we show that the recently developed finite-amplitude method (FAM) can be efficiently employed to compute the vertices within the FAM-QRPA. To illustrate the validity of the method, calculations based on the relativistic density-dependent point-coupling Lagrangian are performed for the single-nucleon states in heavy and medium-mass nuclei with axial deformations. The cases of $^{38}$Si and $^{250}$Cf are presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The authors show that the finite-amplitude method (FAM) can be efficiently employed to compute quasiparticle-phonon vertices within the FAM-QRPA framework for axially deformed superfluid nuclei, overcoming the prohibitive computational cost of traditional QRPA diagonalization solvers in non-spherical geometries.

## Method

Starting from a general many-body fermionic Hamiltonian, the authors derive Dyson-type equations of motion for the quasiparticle propagator in a superfluid system, with the dynamical self-energy expressed in terms of quasiparticle-vibration coupling (qPVC) amplitudes. They link the qPVC vertices to variations of the Bogoliubov Hamiltonian, which are computed using the finite-amplitude method (FAM) to solve the relativistic QRPA equations in the deformed Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov basis. The method is implemented using a relativistic density-dependent point-coupling Lagrangian, and the phonon vertices are extracted from the FAM-QRPA solutions at the poles of the strength function.

## Key numbers

- Calculations are performed for the single-nucleon states in $^{38}$Si and $^{250}$Cf, which are presented and discussed as illustrative cases of medium-mass and heavy nuclei with axial deformations.
- The quasiparticle propagator residues (spectroscopic factors) satisfy the non-energy-weighted sum rule: $\sum_{n} S^{(\eta)n}_{\nu\nu'} = \delta_{\nu\nu'}$, and the energy-weighted sum rule: $\sum_{n} {\cal E}_nS^{(\eta)n}_{\nu\nu'} = \delta_{\nu\nu'}E_{\nu}$, where $E_{\nu}$ is the mean-field quasiparticle energy.

## Context

This work extends the particle-vibration coupling (PVC) method beyond spherical nuclei, which was previously limited by the prohibitive cost of QRPA diagonalization in deformed geometries as noted by the authors. It builds on the equation-of-motion approach for the fermionic propagator developed by Litvinova and Schuck (2019) and Schuck (2021), and utilizes the finite-amplitude method (FAM) for QRPA solutions developed by Nakatsukasa (2007), Kortelainen (2015), Niksic (2013), and Bjelcic (2020). The approach enables future calculations of fragmented single-particle spectra and level densities in deformed superfluid nuclei, which are relevant for understanding shell structure in exotic nuclei studied at radioactive beam facilities.
