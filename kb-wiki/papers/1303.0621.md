# Potential energy surfaces of actinide and transfermium nuclei from multi-dimensional constraint covariant density functional theories

> Multi-dimensional constrained covariant density functional theories were developed recently. In these theories, all shape degrees of freedom β_{λμ} deformations with even μare allowed, e.g., β_{20}, β_{22}, β_{30}, β_{32}, β_{40}, β_{42}, β_{44}, and so on and the CDFT functional can be one of the following four forms: the meson exchange or point-coupling nucleon interactions combined with the non-linear or density-dependent couplings. In this contribution, some applications of these theories are presented. The potential energy surfaces of actinide nuclei in the (β_{20}, β_{22}, β_{30}) deformation space are investigated. It is found that besides the octupole deformation, the triaxiality also plays an important role upon the second fission barriers. The non-axial reflection-asymmetric β_{32} shape in some transfermium nuclei with N = 150, namely 246Cm, 248Cf, 250Fm, and 252No are studied.

## Key claim
The paper claims that in actinide nuclei, triaxial deformation lowers not only the inner fission barrier (by more than 2 MeV) but also the outer fission barrier by about 1 MeV, a feature that can only be seen when both axial and reflection symmetries are simultaneously broken. For transfermium nuclei with N=150, non-axial octupole (β₃₂) deformations produce energy gains larger than 300 keV in some cases.

## Method
The authors performed multi-dimensional constrained covariant density functional theory (CDFT) calculations using either meson-exchange or point-coupling nucleon interactions with non-linear or density-dependent couplings. They solved the Dirac equation in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis, imposing constraints on multipole moments β₂₀, β₂₂, β₃₀, β₃₂, β₄₀, β₄₂, and β₄₄ to map potential energy surfaces. Pairing was treated with either BCS or Bogoliubov transformation using a delta force or separable finite-range pairing force.

## Key numbers
- For ²⁴⁰Pu, triaxial deformation reduces the inner barrier height by more than 2 MeV compared to the axially symmetric calculation.
- For ²⁴⁰Pu, the outer fission barrier is lowered by about 1 MeV when triaxial deformation is allowed.
- For even-even actinide nuclei, triaxiality lowers inner barrier heights by 1 to 4 MeV.
- For most even-even actinide nuclei, triaxiality lowers the outer barrier by 0.5 to 1 MeV, accounting for about 10 to 20% of the barrier height.
- For ²⁴⁸Cf and ²⁵⁰Fm, the non-axial octupole deformation parameter β₃₂ exceeds 0.03 and the energy gain due to β₃₂ distortion is larger than 300 keV.

## Context
This work extends the multi-dimensional constrained CDFT approach developed by Lu et al. (2012) to systematically study fission barriers and exotic shapes. It confirms and quantifies earlier findings from macroscopic-microscopic models (Pashkevich 1969, Moeller 1970) and non-relativistic Hartree-Fock theories (Skalski 2007) that triaxiality and octupole deformation affect fission barriers, but demonstrates for the first time that triaxiality also significantly lowers the outer barrier. The results enable more accurate predictions of fission barriers for superheavy nuclei, which is important for extending systematic calculations beyond actinides.
