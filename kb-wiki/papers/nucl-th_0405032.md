# Pairing transition of nuclei at finite temperature

> Pairing transition at finite temperature was investigated by the shell model and BCS calculations. The definitive signature of pairing transition is identified by a "transition temperature" $T_t$ estimated from a "thermal" odd-even mass difference, while there is no sharp phase transition because of the finiteness of nucleus. It is found that $T_t$ is in good agreement with predictions of critical temperature $T_c$ in the BCS approximation, and the pairing correlations almost vanish at two points of the transition temperature $T\approx 2T_{t}$. The BCS calculations show that the critical temperature $T_c$ increases with increasing deformation.

## Key claim
The paper identifies a "transition temperature" $T_t$ derived from the inflection point of the thermal odd-even mass difference as a definitive signature of the pairing transition in finite nuclei, even though no sharp phase transition occurs due to the finiteness of the system. The authors claim that this $T_t$ is almost identical to the BCS critical temperature $T_c$, despite the pairing correlations not vanishing completely at $T_t$ but rather at approximately $2T_t$.

## Method
The authors performed shell model calculations in a large model space ($sd + fp + s_{1/2}d_{5/2}$) using a spherical Woods-Saxon potential with an independent-particle approximation combined with the USD interaction in the $sd$ shell. They calculated the canonical partition function, thermal energy, heat capacity, and a "thermal" odd-even mass difference $\Delta_n^{(3)}(Z,N,T)$ for Ne and Mg isotopes. Separately, they solved the BCS gap equation and number equation using single-particle energies from an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential, with the pairing strength $G$ fitted to reproduce the experimental odd-even mass difference at zero temperature, for a wide range of even-even nuclei from oxygen to ytterbium.

## Key numbers
- The transition temperature $T_t$ for $^{27,29}$Mg is approximately 1.3 MeV, identified from the peak of $-\partial\Delta_n^{(3)}/\partial T$.
- The BCS critical temperature $T_c$ for $^{28}$Mg is approximately 1.4 MeV, close to the peak of the heat capacity.
- The $N=Z$ nuclei $^{20}$Ne and $^{24}$Mg show a high transition temperature $T_t \approx 2.7$ MeV, while other $N>Z$ nuclei have $T_t \approx 1.3$ MeV.
- For $^{166}$Er, the critical temperature is $T_c = 0.58$ MeV with a pairing gap $\Delta = 1.02$ MeV and deformation $\beta = 0.334$.
- The critical temperatures of $^{162}$Dy, $^{166}$Er, and $^{172}$Yb are around $T_c \approx 0.5$ MeV, corresponding to the peak of experimental heat capacity.
- The critical temperature for $^{64}$Fe is $T_c \sim 0.8$ MeV, agreeing with SMMC calculations.
- The pairing correlations almost vanish at $T \approx 2T_t$.
- The heat capacity parameter $a$ from $C = 2aT$ has roughly the empirical value of $A/8$ MeV$^{-1}$ for light nuclei.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of pairing transitions in hot nuclei using BCS theory (Sano, Goodman) and shell model Monte Carlo calculations (Rombouts, Liu) by introducing the thermal odd-even mass difference as a new indicator. The results support the interpretation of the S-shaped heat capacities observed in $^{162}$Dy, $^{166}$Er, and $^{172}$Yb (Schiller, Melby) as signatures of Cooper pair breaking. The paper enables future extraction of pairing correlations from measured level densities of neighboring isotopes (e.g., $^{170}$Yb, $^{171}$Yb, $^{172}$Yb) and suggests further study of proton-neutron pairing effects in $N=Z$ nuclei.
