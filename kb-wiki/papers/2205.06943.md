# New Insights into Backbending in the Symmetry-adapted Framework

> We provide new insights into backbending phenomenon within the symmetry-adapted framework which naturally describes the intrinsic deformation of atomic nuclei. For $^{20}\text{Ne}$, the canonical example of backbending in light nuclei, the ab initio symmetry-adapted no-core shell model shows that while the energy spectrum replicates the backbending from experimental energies under the rigid rotor assumption, there is no change in the intrinsic deformation or intrinsic spin of the yrast band around the backbend. For the traditional example of $^{48}\text{Cr}$, computed in the valence shell with empirical interactions, we confirm a high-spin nucleus that is effectively spherical, in agreement with previous models. However, we find that this spherical distribution results, on average, from an almost equal mixing of deformed prolate shapes with deformed oblate and triaxial shapes. Microscopic calculations confirm the importance of spin alignment and configuration mixing, but surprisingly unveil no anomalous increase in moment of inertia. This finding opens the path toward further understanding the rotational behavior and moment of inertia of medium-mass nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for both $^{20}$Ne and $^{48}$Cr, the backbending phenomenon observed in experimental energy spectra under the rigid rotor assumption does not correspond to an anomalous increase in the microscopic moment of inertia or a change in intrinsic deformation, contrary to what the traditional energy-derived backbending curves suggest.

## Method

The authors used the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) for $^{20}$Ne with the NNLO$_{\text{opt}}$ chiral potential at $\hbar \Omega = 15$ MeV and $N_{\text{max}} = 8$, and the symmetry-adapted shell model (SA-SM) for $^{48}$Cr with a $^{40}$Ca core, the $pf$ valence shell, and the GXPF1 empirical interaction. They computed microscopic translationally invariant moments of inertia from SU(3) basis states using the relation $\frac{2 \mathcal{I}_z^{N(\lambda\, \mu)}}{\hbar^2} = \frac{4}{3{\hbar \Omega}} \left(N - \lambda - \frac{\mu}{2} - \frac{3}{2}\right)$ and decomposed wavefunctions into $(\lambda\,\mu)$ configurations to extract deformation parameters $\beta$ and $\gamma$ and intrinsic spin content.

## Key numbers

- For $^{20}$Ne, the microscopic moment of inertia from the SA-NCSM is $\mathcal{I}_z \approx 3.7 \ \text{MeV}^{-1}$ for the $8^+_1$ state, showing no large increase compared to lower-spin states.
- For $^{48}$Cr, the microscopic moment of inertia increases by only 1.3% for the upper band compared to the lower band.
- For $^{20}$Ne, the microscopic moment of inertia increases by only 1.2% for $J=8$ compared to lower-spin states.
- For $^{48}$Cr, the lower subband has an average $\langle \beta \rangle = 0.1$ and $\langle C_3 \rangle = 603.16$, while the upper subband has $\langle \beta \rangle = -0.003$ and $\langle C_3 \rangle = 145.39$.
- For $^{48}$Cr, the largest deformation in the lower subband is $\beta \sim 0.2$.
- For $^{20}$Ne, the dominant $(8\,0)$ deformation accounts for 50% to 60% of the wavefunction in the yrast band.

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) analysis of Herrera et al. (2017) for $^{48}$Cr by using the third-order Casimir invariant $C_3$ to distinguish prolate from oblate and triaxial deformations, resolving an apparent contradiction about the structure above the backbend. For $^{20}$Ne, this is the first ab initio study of backbending, extending prior work by Dytrych et al. (2020) and Zbikowski et al. (2021). The findings enable further understanding of rotational behavior and moment of inertia in medium-mass nuclei by showing that band crossing and spin alignment, rather than shape changes, drive the backbending phenomenon.
