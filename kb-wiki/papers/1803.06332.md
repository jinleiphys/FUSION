# On the MSW neutrino mixing effects in atomic weak interactions and double beta decays

> Matter effects on the mixing of the neutrinos mass eigenstates, also know as the Mikheyev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein effect, seem to be well established in describing the propagation of the neutrino from the source to detecting devices. These effects were mostly considered in bulk matter, but not inside the atoms. Here we consider the effect of the high electron densities existing in the atomic nuclei. We investigate if these effects can affect the known neutrino phenomenology. It was reported that the mixing of the neutrino in high density matter, such as inside a supernova, can affect the Majoron decay probabilities. We investigate if the neutrino mixing effects in the high electron density inside the atomic nuclei can change the neutrinoless double beta decay half-life formula. In both cases we found that the standard results stand. The results look simple, but the road to them is complex and it opens the possibility that the neutrino mixing in atomic nuclei may affect other observables, such as the neutrinoless double beta Majoron decays.

## Key claim

The high electron density inside atomic nuclei (about four orders of magnitude larger than in the Sun's core) does not change the standard neutrino emission, detection, or neutrinoless double beta decay phenomenology, because the evolution of neutrino mixing through the atomic electron cloud is extremely non-adiabatic, causing flavor amplitudes to remain unchanged while mass eigenstate amplitudes change dramatically.

## Method

The author solves the Schroedinger-like evolution equations for neutrino amplitudes in matter, first using a two-state approximation for neutrino propagation through atomic electron densities modeled by hydrogen-like s-state distributions. The analysis is then extended to three-flavor mixing using the formalism of unitary transformation matrices \(\alpha^{(-)}\) and \(\beta^{(+)}\) from Giunti et al. (1992), applied to the neutrino field expansion in terms of matter mass eigenstates. For the neutrinoless double beta decay case, the author calculates the electron neutrino propagator in high-density matter using two-component spinor fields in the Weyl chiral representation and compares it to the standard vacuum propagator.

## Key numbers

- Electron density at the nucleus for a medium-Z nucleus such as \(^{136}\)Xe is about four orders of magnitude larger than that in the Sun's core
- The electron density formula for hydrogen-like atoms: \(N_e(t) = 10^{30} \frac{2}{\pi}\left(\frac{Z}{53}\right)^3 e^{-2t Z/53}\) cm\(^{-3}\), where \(Z\) is atomic number and \(t\) is in pm
- The condition for validity of the MSW evolution in atoms is \(2\pi\frac{\hbar c}{Pc} \ll \frac{53000}{2Z}\) fm, satisfied for neutrino energies larger than 2-5 MeV and for relevant double beta decay cases where \(P \approx 150\) MeV
- For a neutrino with \(P=10\) MeV produced in state 2 inside a nucleus (Z=20), the outgoing probabilities evolve to vacuum values: 68% state 1 and 32% state 2
- For a neutrino arriving at the nucleus with relative phase factor \(e^{-i\Delta\phi} = -1\), the probability of state 2 at the nucleus is 0.13

## Context

This work extends the MSW matter effect formalism, previously applied to bulk matter in the Sun, Earth, and supernovae (Smirnov 2016; Giunti et al. 1992; Garcia et al. 2003), to the atomic scale for the first time. It directly addresses whether the high electron density inside atomic nuclei could alter neutrino phenomenology, particularly the neutrinoless double beta decay half-life formula of Bilenky et al. (1987) and the Majoron decay probabilities studied by Giunti et al. (1992) and Berezinsky (1994) in supernova contexts. The finding that standard results stand enables future investigation of whether other observables, such as neutrinoless double beta Majoron decays, might be affected by atomic-scale neutrino mixing.
