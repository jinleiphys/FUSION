# Isospin dependent properties of asymmetric nuclear matter

> The density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy is determined from a systematic study of the isospin dependent bulk properties of asymmetric nuclear matter using the isoscalar and the isovector components of density dependent M3Y interaction. The incompressibility $K_\infty$ for the symmetric nuclear matter, the isospin dependent part $K_{asy}$ of the isobaric incompressibility and the slope $L$ are all in excellent agreement with the constraints recently extracted from measured isotopic dependence of the giant monopole resonances in even-A Sn isotopes, from the neutron skin thickness of nuclei and from analyses of experimental data on isospin diffusion and isotopic scaling in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions. This work provides a fundamental basis for the understanding of nuclear matter under extreme conditions, and validates the important empirical constraints obtained from recent experimental data.

## Key claim

The density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) interaction yields values for the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter, the isospin-dependent part of the isobaric incompressibility, and the slope of the symmetry energy that are consistent with experimental constraints, but the authors hedge that the slope parameter L = 45.11 ± 0.02 MeV lies at the lower end of the recently revised experimental window (L ~ 45–75 MeV) rather than the central value of 75 ± 25 MeV from earlier neutron-skin analyses.

## Method

The authors calculate the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter using the isoscalar and isovector components of the M3Y-Reid-Elliott effective interaction with a density dependence of the form g(ρ) = C(1 − βρ^n). The constants C and β are determined by imposing the saturation condition (∂ε/∂ρ = 0) at the empirical saturation density ρ₀ = 0.1533 fm⁻³ and saturation energy ε₀ = −15.26 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter. From the energy per nucleon as a function of density and isospin asymmetry X, they extract the symmetry energy E_sym(ρ) and its derivatives at saturation to obtain the slope L, curvature K_sym, and the isospin-dependent part K_asy of the isobaric incompressibility.

## Key numbers

- Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: K_∞ = 274.7 ± 7.4 MeV
- Nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density: E_sym(ρ₀) = 30.71 ± 0.26 MeV
- Slope parameter of the symmetry energy: L = 45.11 ± 0.02 MeV
- Curvature parameter of the symmetry energy: K_sym = −183.7 ± 3.6 MeV
- Isospin-dependent part of the isobaric incompressibility: K_asy = −454.4 ± 3.5 MeV
- Density dependence constants: C = 2.2497 ± 0.0420, β = 1.5934 ± 0.0085 fm² (for n = 2/3)
- Saturation density used: ρ₀ = 0.1533 fm⁻³; saturation energy: ε₀ = −15.26 MeV

## Context

This work extends the DDM3Y mean-field approach to asymmetric nuclear matter and compares its predictions to constraints from giant monopole resonance measurements in Sn isotopes (Li et al., 2007), neutron-skin thickness analyses (Centelles et al., 2009), and isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion collisions (Chen et al., 2007). The results are contrasted with relativistic mean-field models (NL1, NL2, NL3, NL-SH, FSUGold, DD-ME1, DD-ME2, DD, DD-F) and with the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall variational calculation (1998). The authors claim their calculation provides the best theoretical estimates among the models compared for the isospin-dependent properties, and the soft symmetry energy (increasing up to about twice normal density then decreasing) is consistent with evidence for a soft symmetry energy at suprasaturation densities.
