# Baryon Current Matrix Elements in a Relativistic Quark Model

> Current matrix elements and observables for electro- and photo-excitation of baryons from the nucleon are studied in a light-front framework. Relativistic effects are examined by comparison to a nonrelativistic model and can typically be of order 20-25%, but can be larger for certain matrix elements, such as radial transitions conventionally used to describe the Roper resonance. A systematic study shows that the violation of rotational covariance of the baryon transition matrix elements stemming from the use of one-body currents is generally small.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects in baryon electroexcitation amplitudes are typically of order 20-25%, but can be much larger (factors of three or four) for certain matrix elements such as radial transitions used to describe the Roper resonance; the violation of rotational covariance from using one-body currents is generally small.

## Method

The authors used light-front Hamiltonian dynamics with a Bakamjian-Thomas construction, solving the three-body relativistic bound-state problem for constituent quarks with the Capstick-Isgur interaction (one-gluon exchange plus confining terms). They computed current matrix elements for electro- and photo-excitation of baryons from the nucleon by expanding in a complete orthonormal set of basis states satisfying rotational covariance, performing six-dimensional numerical integrations over relative three-momenta. Only one-body current contributions were computed, assuming the struck quark carries the current of a free Dirac particle.

## Key numbers

- Relativistic effects are typically of order 20-25% when comparing to a nonrelativistic model
- Light-quark mass taken as m_u = m_d = 220 MeV
- Harmonic oscillator size parameter alpha_HO = 0.41 GeV
- For the Roper resonance N(1440)1/2+, relativistic effects produce differences of factors of three or four between relativistic and nonrelativistic calculations
- For proton targets, the longitudinal amplitude C^p_{1/2} reaches about 40-50 x 10^{-3} GeV^{1/2} at Q^2 values between 0.25 and 0.50 GeV^2
- The axial coupling constant g_A is reduced from the static nonrelativistic quark model value of 5/3 to more like 1.25 using simple single-Gaussian wavefunctions, and further reduced using CI wavefunctions
- Numerical uncertainties are typically on the order of a few percent for the largest matrix elements

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic quark model approach of Isgur and collaborators (Isgur and Karl, 1978) to a fully relativistic light-front framework, using the Capstick-Isgur interaction (Capstick and Isgur, 1986). The results confirm earlier findings by Chung and Coester (1991) that relativistic effects reduce the axial coupling constant, and by Cardarelli et al. (1995) that soft quark form factors are needed to fit nucleon form factors with these wavefunctions. The large relativistic effects found for the Roper resonance suggest that the discrepancy between nonrelativistic model predictions and experimental data for this state may have multiple sources, including relativistic effects, rather than necessarily requiring exotic interpretations such as excited glue. The work enables systematic calculation of transition form factors for many baryon resonances simultaneously, and provides a framework for future inclusion of two-body currents and configuration mixing effects.
