# Thermal QRPA with Skyrme interactions and supernova neutral-current neutrino-nucleus reactions

> The Thermal Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation is combined with the Skyrme energy density functional method (Skyrme-TQRPA) to study the response of a hot nucleus to an external perturbation. For the sample nuclei, $^{56}$Fe and $^{82}$Ge, the Skyrme-TQRPA is applied to analyze thermal effects on the strength function of charge-neutral Gamow-Teller transitions which dominate neutrino-nucleus reactions at $E_ν\lesssim 20$~MeV. For the relevant supernova temperatures we calculate the cross sections for inelastic neutrino scattering. We also apply the method to examine the rate of neutrino-antineutrino pair emission by hot nuclei. The cross sections and rates are compared with those obtained earlier from the TQRPA calculations based on the phenomenological Quasiparticle-Phonon Model Hamiltonian. For inelastic neutrino scattering on $^{56}$Fe we also compare the Skyrme-TQRPA results to those obtained earlier from a hybrid approach that combines shell-model and RPA calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Skyrme-TQRPA method, which does not rely on the Brink hypothesis, predicts a significantly larger thermal enhancement of neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections than earlier calculations that assumed the Brink hypothesis, particularly at low neutrino energies and for the downward GT₀ strength.

## Method

The authors combine the Thermal Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (TQRPA) with a finite-rank separable approximation for the Skyrme energy density functional (Skyrme-TQRPA) to compute temperature-dependent GT₀ strength functions for ⁵⁶Fe and ⁸²Ge. They diagonalize the thermal Hamiltonian in the thermo-field dynamics formalism to obtain thermal phonon states, then calculate inelastic neutrino scattering cross sections and neutrino-antineutrino pair emission rates at supernova-relevant temperatures. The results are compared with earlier TQRPA calculations using the phenomenological Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) Hamiltonian and, for ⁵⁶Fe, with a hybrid shell-model/RPA approach.

## Key numbers

- For ⁵⁶Fe at T=0.86 MeV, the total downward GT₀ strength from Skyrme-TQRPA exceeds the QPM-TQRPA result by about a factor of 4; at T=1.72 MeV, the difference is about a factor of 3.
- The total downward shift of the energy-centroid for upward GT₀ strength when temperature rises to T=2.5 MeV varies from 1.5 to 1.8 MeV in ⁵⁶Fe and from 1.0 to 1.8 MeV in ⁸²Ge, depending on the Skyrme parametrization.
- The critical temperature for pairing collapse is T_cr ≈ 0.8 MeV for ⁵⁶Fe and T_cr ≈ 0.6 MeV for ⁸²Ge.
- At T=0, the proton and neutron pairing gaps are Δ_p=1.57 MeV and Δ_n=1.36 MeV for ⁵⁶Fe, and Δ_p=1.22 MeV and Δ_n=0 MeV for ⁸²Ge.
- The total GT₀ ground-state strength S(GT₀) for ⁵⁶Fe ranges from 4.3 (SGII) to 5.4 (SLy4) in Skyrme-QRPA, compared to 3.6 in QPM-QRPA; for ⁸²Ge it ranges from 5.1 (SGII) to 7.2 (SLy4), compared to 5.2 in QPM-QRPA.

## Context

This work extends the TQRPA method previously developed by Dzhioev et al. (2014, 2015) using the phenomenological QPM Hamiltonian to a self-consistent framework based on Skyrme energy density functionals. It contradicts the Brink hypothesis used in earlier shell-model studies by Sampaio et al. (2002) and the hybrid approach of Juodagalvis et al. (2004), showing that thermal effects on the GT₀ strength function are more pronounced than those approaches predict. The method enables systematic studies of neutrino-nucleus reactions for a wide range of nuclei, including neutron-rich species far from stability that are important for core-collapse supernova simulations.
