# Anomalous dimension of transverse momentum broadening in planar $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM

> The typical transverse momentum $Q_s(t)$ (or "saturation" momentum) acquired by a hard particle propagating through a $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM plasma increases over time like $t^γ$, with an anomalous exponent $γ>1/2$ characteristic of super-diffusion. This anomalous exponent is a function of the 't Hooft coupling $λ=g^2N_c$. Recently, a method has been proposed to systematically compute the perturbative series of $γ(λ)$ at weak coupling. This method relies on the traveling wave interpretation of the time evolution of $Q_s(t)$ and on the dominance of soft-collinear radiative corrections at large times. In this paper, we compute $γ(λ)$ up to $\mathcal{O}(λ^{2})$ using the double logarithmic behaviour of the BFKL equation in planar $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM at three loops. This calculation allows us to discuss the transition towards the strong coupling regime where AdS/CFT calculations predict $γ\to 1$.

## Key claim

The anomalous exponent \(\gamma(\lambda)\) of transverse momentum broadening in planar \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM is computed up to \(\mathcal{O}(\lambda^{2})\) using the double logarithmic behavior of the BFKL equation at three loops, and the weak-coupling series suggests a rapid convergence toward the strong-coupling limit \(\gamma \to 1\) for \(\lambda \approx 4\pi^2/10\), though the series is not reliable beyond \(\bar\alpha_s \approx 0.1\div 0.15\).

## Method

The author maps the non-linear evolution equation for the jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) (which resums double-logarithmic radiative corrections) onto a traveling wave problem in the BFKL formalism. By expanding the BFKL kernel \(\chi_{\rm BFKL}(\omega)\) around \(\omega = 0\) using known three-loop results in planar \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM, and solving the traveling wave velocity equations \(c-1-c\beta_c = \chi_{\rm BFKL}(\beta_c)\) and \(-c = \chi_{\rm BFKL}'(\beta_c)\), the anomalous exponent \(\gamma = c/2\) is obtained perturbatively in the 't Hooft coupling \(\lambda\).

## Key numbers

* The leading-order exponent is \(\gamma = 1/2\) (standard diffusion scaling).
* The one-loop correction gives \(\gamma = 1/2 + \sqrt{\bar\alpha_s}\) with \(\bar\alpha_s = \lambda/(4\pi^2)\).
* The two-loop coefficient is \(\gamma = 1/2 + \sqrt{\bar\alpha_s} + \bar\alpha_s\).
* The \(\mathcal{O}(\bar\alpha_s^{3/2})\) coefficient is \(\frac{1}{2}(1 - \zeta(2))\) where \(\zeta(2) = \pi^2/6\).
* The \(\mathcal{O}(\bar\alpha_s^{2})\) coefficient is \(\left(\frac{5+4k}{8}\zeta(3) - 2\zeta(2)\right)\) with \(\zeta(3) \approx 1.20206\) and \(k\) a rational number from the four-loop BFKL kernel.
* The strong coupling limit gives \(\gamma \to 1\) as \(\lambda \to \infty\).
* The weak-coupling series is not reliable beyond \(\bar\alpha_s \approx 0.1\div 0.15\) (corresponding to \(\lambda \approx 4\pi^2/10\)).

## Context

This work extends the resummation of double-logarithmic corrections to transverse momentum broadening initiated by Liou et al. (2013) and the traveling wave approach of Caucal et al. (2021, 2022) to higher orders in planar \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM. It uses the three-loop BFKL kernel computed by Gromov et al. (2015) and Caron-Huot et al. (2016). The calculation enables a quantitative discussion of the transition from weak to strong coupling, complementing AdS/CFT predictions by Hatta et al. (2007, 2008) and Dominguez et al. (2008) that give \(\langle k_\perp^2\rangle \propto L^2\) at strong coupling. The results suggest that the convergence to the strong-coupling limit is faster than for the Pomeron intercept studied by Brower et al. (2006) and Kotikov et al. (2013).
