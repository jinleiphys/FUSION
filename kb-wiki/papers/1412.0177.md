# Resonance-state properties from a phase shift analysis with the $S$-matrix pole method and the effective-range method

> Asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) are fundamental nuclear constants playing an important role in nuclear physics and astrophysics. We derive a new useful relationship between ANC of the Gamow radial wave function and the renormalized (due to the Coulomb interaction) Coulomb-nuclear partial scattering amplitude. We use an analytical approximation in the form of a series for the nonresonant part of the phase shift which can be analytically continued to the point of an isolated resonance pole in the complex plane of the momentum. Earlier, this method which we call the $S$-matrix pole method was used by us to find the resonance pole energy. We find the corresponding fitting parameters for the $^5\rm{He},\,^5\rm{Li}$, and $^{16}\rm{O}$ concrete resonance states. Additionally, based on the theory of the effective range, we calculate the parameters of the $p_{3/2}$ and $p_{1/2}$ resonance states of the nuclei $^5\rm{He}$ and $^5\rm{Li}$ and compare them with the results obtained by the $S$-matrix pole method. ANC values are found which can be used to calculate the reaction rate through the $^{16}\rm{O}$ resonances which lie slightly above the threshold for the $α^{12}\rm{C}$ channel.

# Resonance-state properties from a phase shift analysis with the $S$-matrix pole method and the effective-range method

## Key claim

The authors derive a new relationship between the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the Gamow radial wave function and the renormalized Coulomb-nuclear partial scattering amplitude that is valid for both narrow and broad resonances, whereas the previously known narrow-resonance formula (Irgaziev and Orlov, earlier work) gives incorrect ANC values for broad resonances with widths comparable to or larger than their energies.

## Method

The authors fit experimental elastic scattering phase shifts for $n\alpha$, $p\alpha$, and $\alpha^{12}\rm{C}$ systems using two independent approaches: (1) the $S$-matrix pole method, which expands the nonresonant phase shift $\nu_l(k)$ as a power series around a point near the resonance pole in the complex momentum plane, and (2) the effective-range expansion method, which expands the effective-range function $K_l(k^2)$ as a power series in $k^2$ around zero momentum. Both methods are used to extract the complex resonance pole momentum $k_r$, from which the residue $W$ of the renormalized scattering amplitude, the nuclear vertex constant $\tilde{G}_l^2$, and the ANC $C_l$ are computed.

## Key numbers

- $^5\rm{He}$ $J^\pi=3/2^-$ resonance: $E_r = 0.629$ MeV, $\Gamma = 0.448$ MeV ($S$-matrix method); $E_r = 0.675$ MeV, $\Gamma = 0.560$ MeV (effective-range method)
- $^5\rm{He}$ $J^\pi=1/2^-$ resonance: $E_r = 1.476$ MeV, $\Gamma = 3.520$ MeV ($S$-matrix method); $E_r = 1.563$ MeV, $\Gamma = 4.155$ MeV (effective-range method)
- $^5\rm{Li}$ $J^\pi=3/2^-$ resonance: $E_r = 1.328$ MeV, $\Gamma = 0.994$ MeV ($S$-matrix method); $E_r = 1.481$ MeV, $\Gamma = 1.041$ MeV (effective-range method)
- $^5\rm{Li}$ $J^\pi=1/2^-$ resonance: $E_r = 2.504$ MeV, $\Gamma = 4.667$ MeV ($S$-matrix method); $E_r = 2.213$ MeV, $\Gamma = 4.640$ MeV (effective-range method)
- $^{16}\rm{O}$ $J^\pi=1^-$ resonance: $E_r = 2.364$ MeV, $\Gamma = 356.2$ keV ($S$-matrix method); compared to $E_r = 2.416$ MeV, $\Gamma = 388.0$ keV from Tisch (R-matrix analysis)
- $^{16}\rm{O}$ $J^\pi=3^-$ resonance: $E_r = 4.214$ MeV, $\Gamma = 811.7$ keV ($S$-matrix method); compared to $E_r = 4.320$ MeV, $\Gamma = 864.0$ keV from Tisch (R-matrix analysis)
- ANC values for $^{16}\rm{O}$ $J^\pi=1^-$: $C_l = 0.1530 - i0.1032$ fm$^{-1/2}$ ($S$-matrix method); narrow-resonance formula gives $C_l^a = 0.1759 - i0.1135$ fm$^{-1/2}$
- ANC values for $^{16}\rm{O}$ $J^\pi=3^-$: $C_l = -0.2332 - i0.0201$ fm$^{-1/2}$ ($S$-matrix method); narrow-resonance formula gives $C_l^a = -0.2718 - i0.0311$ fm$^{-1/2}$

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier $S$-matrix pole method (Irgaziev and Orlov, 2011) for finding resonance pole energies to also extract asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) and nuclear vertex constants. It provides a comparison with the effective-range expansion method previously applied to bound and resonant states by Orlov and collaborators, and with R-matrix analyses by Tisch and by Forssen and collaborators. The derived ANC values for $^{16}\rm{O}$ resonances slightly above the $\alpha^{12}\rm{C}$ threshold can be used to calculate astrophysical reaction rates, and the method is applicable to both narrow and broad resonances where the standard narrow-resonance approximation (Akram and collaborators, 1977) fails.
