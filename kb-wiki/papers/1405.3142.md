# Regge signatures from forward CLAS $Λ(1520)$ photoproduction data

> The $γp \to K^+ Λ(1520)$ reaction mechanism is investigated within a Regge--effective Lagrangian hybrid approach based on our previous study of this reaction [Physical Review C89, 015203 (2014)]. Near threshold and for large $K^+$ angles, both the CLAS and LEPS data can be successfully described by considering the contributions from the contact, $t$-channel $\bar K$ exchange, $u$-channel $Λ(1115)$ hyperon pole, and the $s$-channel nucleon pole and $N^*(2120)$ resonance contributions. However, for higher energies and forward $K^+$ angles, systematic discrepancies with data appear, which hint the possible existence of sizable quark-gluon string mechanism effects. We show how the inclusion of a $\bar K$ Regge--trajectory exchange in the $t$-channel leads to an efficient description of the $Λ(1520)$ photoproduction channel over the whole energy and angular ranges accessible in the CLAS experiment.

## Key claim

The systematic discrepancies between CLAS data and the effective Lagrangian model of Xie (2014) at high invariant masses (W > 2.35 GeV) and forward K+ angles can be resolved by including a t-channel antikaon Regge trajectory exchange, yielding a significantly improved fit with chi-squared per degree of freedom of 1.3 compared to 2.5 without Regge effects.

## Method

The authors constructed a hybrid model that interpolates between an effective Lagrangian approach (including contact, t-channel antikaon exchange, u-channel Lambda(1115) hyperon pole, s-channel nucleon pole, and s-channel N*(2120) resonance contributions) and a Reggeized t-channel antikaon exchange mechanism. Two variants of the Regge model were tested: model A used a standard Regge propagator replacement, while model B employed a Gaussian form factor with a simplified Regge expression. A smooth transition between the two regimes was implemented using sigmoid functions in both invariant mass W and momentum transfer t, with gauge invariance restored through a multiplicative prescription.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit chi-squared per degree of freedom: 1.3 for both model A and model B, compared to 2.5 in the previous work without Regge effects (Xie 2014)
- N*(2120) resonance mass: 2146 ± 5 MeV (model A) and 2145 ± 5 MeV (model B)
- N*(2120) total width: 174 ± 14 MeV (model A) and 171 ± 13 MeV (model B)
- Transition momentum transfer parameter t0: 0.73 ± 0.04 GeV^2 (model A) and 0.94 ± 0.05 GeV^2 (model B)
- Transition width parameter Delta t: 0.28 ± 0.02 GeV^2 (model A) and 0.30 ± 0.04 GeV^2 (model B)
- Reggeon normalization factor hat f: 0.38 ± 0.01 (model A) and 0.37 ± 0.01 (model B)
- N*(2120) partial decay width to Lambda(1520)K: 22 ± 7 MeV (model A) and 25 ± 7 MeV (model B)
- Helicity amplitude A_{1/2}^{p*}: -9.7 ± 4.1 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} (model A) and -8.8 ± 3.8 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} (model B)
- Helicity amplitude A_{3/2}^{p*}: 2.3 ± 1.1 x 10^{-2} GeV^{-1/2} (model A) and 2.1 ± 1.0 x 10^{-2} GeV^{-1/2} (model B)
- Cutoff parameter Lambda_B fixed to 620 MeV from previous work
- Transition energy W0 fixed to 2.35 GeV with Delta W = 0.08 GeV

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian model of Xie (2014) for gamma p to K+ Lambda(1520) by incorporating Regge trajectory exchange, motivated by systematic discrepancies in the forward-angle, high-energy CLAS data. It contrasts with the approach of He (2014), who included both antikaon and antikaon* Regge trajectories along with multiple N* resonances fixed to constituent quark model predictions; the present work finds that only the antikaon trajectory is needed and obtains a narrower N*(2120) width (approximately 170-175 MeV versus 330 MeV) with helicity amplitudes consistent with the measurements of Awaji (1981) and the 2008 PDG, but inconsistent with the analysis of Anisovich (2011). The improved fit provides further support for the existence of the two-star N*(2120) resonance and enables more reliable extraction of its properties from combined CLAS and LEPS data.
