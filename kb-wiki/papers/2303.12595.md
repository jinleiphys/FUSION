# Jet momentum broadening during initial stages in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the jet quenching parameter $\hat q$ in the initial pre-equilibrium stages of heavy-ion collisions using the QCD kinetic theory description of the anisotropic quark-gluon plasma. This allows us to smoothly close the gap in the literature between the early glasma stage of the collision and the onset of hydrodynamics. We find that the pre-hydrodynamic evolution of $\hat q$ during the bottom-up kinetic scenario shows little sensitivity to the initial conditions, jet energies and models of the transverse momentum cutoff. We also observe that, similarly to the glasma case, the jet quenching parameter is enhanced along the beam axis as compared to the transverse direction during most of the kinetic evolution.

## Key claim

The jet quenching parameter $\hat q$ during the pre-equilibrium kinetic stage of heavy-ion collisions shows little sensitivity to initial conditions, jet energies, and models of the transverse momentum cutoff, and its values smoothly connect glasma-stage results to hydrodynamic-stage values, contrary to phenomenological studies that find $\hat q$ initially suppressed or neglect jet energy loss during initial stages.

## Method

The authors performed numerical simulations within QCD effective kinetic theory (EKT) for a gluonic plasma undergoing the bottom-up thermalization scenario, solving the Boltzmann equation with elastic scattering, effective splitting terms, and longitudinal expansion. They computed the jet quenching parameter matrix $\hat q^{ij}$ from the perturbative expression for elastic scatterings of a leading jet parton off plasma constituents, using isotropic hard thermal loop (HTL) resummed matrix elements with the Debye mass calculated dynamically from the simulation. The simulations were initialized at proper time $\tau = 1/Q$ with two different momentum anisotropy parameters ($\xi = 4$ and $\xi = 10$) and evolved for couplings $\lambda = 0.5, 1, 2, 5, 10$, with results scaled by $\lambda^2 T_\varepsilon^3$ and time rescaled by the parametric thermalization time $\tau_{\mathrm{BMSS}} = \alpha_s^{-13/5} Q^{-1}$.

## Key numbers

* For jet energies $E_{\mathrm{jet}} = 20$ and $100$ GeV with $\lambda = 10$ and $Q = 1.4$ GeV, at $\tau \sim 0.2$ fm/$c$ the total $\hat q \approx 4-5$ GeV$^2$/fm, matching glasma values from Ipp (2020).
* The variation of initial conditions ($\xi = 4$ vs $\xi = 10$) changes $\hat q$ by less than 15% for the considered parameters.
* For $\lambda = 10$ at $E_{\mathrm{jet}} = 100$ GeV, the LPM and kinematic cutoff models differ by less than 20% during pre-hydrodynamic evolution.
* The energy-density matched temperature at the close-to-equilibrium triangle marker is $T_\varepsilon = 0.21 Q = 295$ MeV for $Q = 1.4$ GeV.
* The ratio $\hat q^{yy}/\hat q^{zz}$ shows a suppression of up to 20% between the glasma and hydrodynamic regimes.

## Context

This work extends the bottom-up thermalization scenario of Baier (2000) and Kurkela (2015) by computing $\hat q$ throughout the kinetic stage, closing the gap between glasma calculations from Ipp (2020) and Carrington (2021) and hydrodynamic descriptions used in phenomenological extractions by the JETSCAPE collaboration (2021). The results enable inclusion of pre-equilibrium jet quenching in frameworks such as JETSCAPE (2023) and models by Andres (2019) that previously required $\hat q$ suppression before 0.6 fm/$c$ or neglected initial-stage energy loss entirely.
