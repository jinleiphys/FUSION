# Particle production from gluon-nucleon interactions in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We propose a particle production mechanism analogous to the particle photoproduction processes, arising from the gluon-nucleon interactions in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The comparison is made on the effect of the gluon-nucleon interactions on the photon production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$2.76 TeV. The numerical results indicate that as the collision energy increases, the contribution of gluon-nucleon interactions becomes more prominent.

## Key claim

The authors propose that in relativistic heavy ion collisions, high-energy gluons radiated by initial-state quarks scattering off small hot QGP droplets can subsequently interact with cold partons from other nucleons, producing particles via a mechanism analogous to photoproduction. The contribution of this gluon-nucleon interaction to photon production becomes more prominent as the collision energy increases, but the paper hedges that isolating this signal experimentally is not feasible with current detectors.

## Method

The authors derive the leading-order (LO) amplitude for gluon radiation from an initial-state quark scattering with a thermal parton in a small hot medium, using the light-cone gauge and the leading pole approximation to obtain the gluon radiation spectrum. They then fold this spectrum with nuclear parton distribution functions (CTEQ6L1 with EPS09 nuclear modifications) and the LO Compton scattering cross section \(gq \rightarrow \gamma q\) to compute the invariant differential cross section for photon production from gluon-nucleon interactions. The calculation is performed for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, including direct photons, fragmentation photons (with BDMPS-based jet energy loss), and thermal photons from QGP and hadron gas phases, and compared to PHENIX and ALICE data.

## Key numbers

- In Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, the contribution of gluon-nucleon interactions to the yield of hard photons (direct + fragmentation) drops from 25% at \(p_T=2\) GeV to 12% at \(p_T=7\) GeV.
- In Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, the contribution of gluon-nucleon interactions rises gradually from 17% at \(p_T=3\) GeV to 22% at \(p_T=13\) GeV.
- The average number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions used is \(\langle N_{coll}\rangle = 770.6\) for Au+Au at 200 GeV and 1210.8 for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV; the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections are 40 mb and 64 mb, respectively.
- Correction factors applied: \(K_{dir} \sim 1.5\) for both RHIC and LHC; \(K_{frag} \sim 1.8\) at RHIC and 1.4 at LHC.

## Context

This work extends the study of particle production mechanisms in relativistic heavy ion collisions by introducing a gluon-nucleon interaction channel analogous to photon-nucleon interactions in electron-proton deep inelastic scattering at HERA. It builds on the BDMPS and GW models of gluon radiation in a hot medium (BDMPS, 1997; Gyulassy and Wang, 1994) and the WHS phenomenological model for jet energy loss (Wang, Huang, and Sarcevic, 1996). The results suggest that at LHC energies, this mechanism can produce photon yields comparable to fragmentation photons, but experimental isolation remains challenging; future work could explore other final-state particles or higher collision energies to test the mechanism.
