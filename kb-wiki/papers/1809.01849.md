# Momentum distributions and short-range correlations in the deuteron and $^3$He with modern chiral potentials

> We study momentum distributions and short-range correlation probabilities in $A$=2 and $A$=3 systems. First, we show results with phenomenological and meson-theoretic two- and three-nucleon forces to verify consistency with previous similar studies. We then apply most recent high-quality chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials up to fifth order in the chiral expansion together with the leading chiral three-nucleon force. Predictions are examined in the context of a broader discussion of short-range correlation probabilities extracted from analyses of inclusive electron scattering data, addressing the question of whether modern interactions can be reconciled with the latter.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether modern chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials up to fifth order, combined with the leading chiral three-nucleon force, can produce short-range correlation probabilities in the deuteron and 3He that are consistent with those extracted from inclusive electron scattering data, finding that predictions display significant model dependence that propagates to analyses for heavier nuclei.

## Method

The authors calculated single-nucleon and two-nucleon momentum distributions in the deuteron and 3He using the Hyperspherical Harmonics method, which allows working in both coordinate and momentum space with no restriction on the nuclear potential model. They first used phenomenological potentials (Argonne v18 and CDBonn) with and without three-nucleon forces (Urbana IX and Tucson-Melbourne, respectively) to establish a baseline, then applied the latest chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials from leading order to fifth order (N4LO) with cutoff values of 450, 500, and 550 MeV, including the leading chiral three-nucleon force with low-energy constants constrained to reproduce A=3 binding energies and the Gamow-Teller matrix element of tritium beta-decay.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron SRC probability a2N(d) for the chiral N4LO potential at cutoff 500 MeV: 0.024 (compared to 0.042 for AV18 and 0.032 for CDBonn)
- Deuteron D-state percentage for chiral N4LO at cutoff 500 MeV: 0.0410
- Integrated neutron probability Pn(k^-=1.5 fm^-1) for AV18: 0.068; for CDBonn: 0.052
- Integrated proton probability Pp(k^-=1.5 fm^-1) for AV18: 0.041; for CDBonn: 0.031
- For chiral N4LO at cutoff 500 MeV with 3N force (model II): Pn(k^-=1.5 fm^-1)=0.042 and Pp(k^-=1.5 fm^-1)=0.027
- The lower integration limit for SRC probability definition is k^-=1.4 fm^-1 (276 MeV)
- The deuteron SRC probability from CLAS analysis was taken as 0.041 plus or minus 0.008

## Context

This work extends the study of Marcucci et al. (2015) on deuteron momentum distributions by using the most recent chiral potentials from Epelbaum, Meissner, and collaborators (2015) which show improved convergence compared to older chiral potentials from Entem and Machleidt (2003) and others. The calculations address the question of whether modern chiral interactions can be reconciled with short-range correlation probabilities extracted from inclusive electron scattering measurements by the CLAS collaboration, finding that the model dependence observed in the deuteron propagates to 3He and likely to heavier nuclei, which impacts the interpretation of experimental SRC analyses that used AV18 predictions as a reference.
