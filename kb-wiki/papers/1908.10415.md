# Cold quark matter with heavy quarks and the stability of charm stars

> We study the effects of heavy quarks on the equation of state for cold and dense quark matter obtained from perturbative QCD, yielding observables parametrized only by the renormalization scale. We investigate the thermodynamics of charm quark matter under the constraints of $β$ equilibrium and electric charge neutrality in a region of densities where perturbative QCD is, in principle, much more reliable. We also analyze the stability of charm stars, which might be realized as a new branch of ultradense hybrid compact stars, and find that such quark stars are unstable under radial oscillations.

### Key claim
The paper extends perturbative QCD to include heavy quarks in the equation of state for cold dense matter and finds that charm stars, while thermodynamically possible for some parameter choices, are dynamically unstable under radial oscillations, ruling them out as stable astrophysical objects.

### Method
The authors extended the perturbative QCD thermodynamic potential formalism from the Nf = Nl + 1 case (massless plus one massive flavor) to Nf = Nl + Nm (multiple massive flavors), computing the equation of state up to next-to-next-to-leading order in the strong coupling. They imposed beta equilibrium and electric charge neutrality for charm quark matter (up, down, strange, charm quarks plus electrons and muons), solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for stellar structure, and tested dynamical stability using the first-order coupled radial oscillation equations of Gondek et al. (1997).

### Key numbers
- The charm quark vacuum mass was fixed at m_c(3 GeV, Nf=4) = 0.9851(63) GeV.
- The strange quark mass was fixed at m_s(2 GeV, Nf=3,4) = 92.4(1.5) MeV.
- The renormalization group invariant charm mass was found to be approximately 3.0895 GeV.
- For X=3, the charm threshold occurs at strange chemical potential mu_s approximately 1.340 GeV, with central energy density 77.988 GeV/fm^3, gravitational mass 0.999 solar masses, and radius 7.310 km.
- For X=5, the charm threshold gives gravitational mass 1.745 solar masses and radius 12.87 km.
- The Lambda_MSbar values were 343(+18/-12) MeV for Nf=2+1 and 290(+18/-12) MeV for Nf=2+1+1.

### Context
This work extends the perturbative QCD formalism of Kurkela et al. (2009) for Nf = Nl + 1 flavors to include multiple heavy quarks, and revisits the stability of charm stars previously studied within the MIT bag model by Kettner et al. (1994) and Glendenning (2000). The results confirm earlier bag-model findings that charm stars are unstable under radial pulsations, now using a first-principles perturbative QCD equation of state. The softened equation of state from charm quarks reduces the renormalization-scale uncertainty band, but the instability persists even at higher perturbative orders as shown by Gorda et al. (2018).
