# Strangeness and Quark--Gluon Plasma

> I review the foundational motivation which led us to the ultra relativistic heavy ion collision research at SPS, RHIC and now LHC: the quantum vacuum structure; the deconfined nature of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase filling the Universe for the first 30$μ$s after the big-bang; the origin of stable matter mass; and of the origin of flavor. The special role of strangeness enhancement and strange antibaryon signature is highlighted. It is shown how hadron production can be used to determine the properties of QGP, and how the threshold energy for QGP formation is determined.

# Strangeness and Quark–Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
Strangeness enhancement and strange antibaryon yields serve as a functional remote-sensing probe of quark–gluon plasma (QGP) formation across the full range of collision energies at SPS, RHIC, and LHC, but the interpretation depends on the assumption that chemical non-equilibrium parameters (phase-space occupancies) are required to correctly describe hadronization from a deconfined source. The threshold energy for QGP formation is identified with the onset of baryon transparency and a peak in net baryon density near the energy where the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio horn occurs.

## Method
The author reviews the theoretical foundations of strangeness production in QGP via gluon fusion ($gg\to s\bar s$) and light-quark annihilation ($q\bar q\to s\bar s$), and describes the statistical hadronization model (SHM) implemented in the SHARE code, which fits final-state hadron yields using temperature $T$, volume $V$, fugacities $\lambda$, and phase-space occupancy factors $\tilde\gamma_q$, $\tilde\gamma_s$ for light and strange quarks. The analysis compares total $4\pi$ particle yields from SPS and RHIC data across collision energies, extracting bulk properties (energy density, entropy density, baryon density, pressure) and strangeness-related ratios ($s/S$, $s/B$, thermal energy per strange pair).

## Key numbers
- The Hagedorn temperature (QGP boiling point) is $T_H = 160$ MeV.
- The chemical relaxation time for strangeness near equilibrium at $T=400$ MeV is $\tau_s < 1$ fm/c.
- The running coupling at the $Z$ mass is fixed to $\alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.1182$; the 1982 fixed value used was $\alpha_s = 0.60 \pm 0.10 \pm 0.07$ at $\mu = 0.86$ GeV.
- The QGP phase filled the Universe for the first $30\ \mu$s after the big bang.
- The bulk energy density of the hadron source at hadronization is roughly $0.5$ GeV/fm$^3$.
- At LHC, the normalization volume from SHARE fits is 30–50% larger than predicted by HBT systematics, and the $s/S$ ratio is about 5% smaller than at RHIC.

## Context
This paper extends the author’s three-decade program (Rafelski 1982, Biro 1981) on strangeness as a QGP signature, building on the Hagedorn phase-transition picture (Hagedorn 1984) and the Fermi statistical model (Fermi 1950). It contradicts approaches that assume chemical equilibrium among hadrons without allowing for non-equilibrium occupancy parameters, and it enables the use of SHARE fits to extract QGP bulk properties from LHC data. The analysis of the $K^+/\pi^+$ horn (Gazdzicki 2011) is interpreted as the onset of deconfinement and baryon transparency, rather than a purely hadronic effect.
