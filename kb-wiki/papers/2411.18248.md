# On the possible existence of a $S=-3, \, I=1$ pentaquark

> We analyze the possible existence of a strangeness $S=-3$, isospin $I=1$ pentaquark state $P_{sss}$ generated dynamically from the $\bar{K}Ξ$ interaction. We employ a unitarized scheme in coupled channels based on the chiral Lagrangian expanded up to next-to-leading order (NLO), and show that the inclusion of the NLO terms is crucial to provide the necessary attraction that favors the existence of such triply strange pentaquark. The $\bar{K}Ξ$ femtoscopic correlation functions are calculated as example of a possible experimental measurement in which a direct signal of the $P_{sss}$ state could be observed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a triply strange pentaquark state \(P_{sss}\) with strangeness \(S=-3\) and isospin \(I=1\) can be dynamically generated from the \(\bar{K}\Xi\) interaction, but only if next-to-leading order (NLO) terms in the chiral Lagrangian are included; the leading-order (LO) contributions alone provide a repulsive interaction that cannot bind the state.

## Method

The authors employ a unitarized coupled-channel scheme based on the chiral Lagrangian expanded up to NLO, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell factorization. The interaction kernel includes Weinberg-Tomozawa contact terms, Born terms (direct and crossed), and NLO contributions parameterized by low-energy constants (LECs). The LECs are taken from two existing models (BCN and VBC) that were previously fitted to \(S=-1\) and \(S=-2\) scattering data, and the pole content of the scattering amplitudes is analyzed in the \(S=-3\) sector for the \(\bar{K}\Xi\) channels.

## Key numbers

* BCN model predicts a \(P_{sss}\) state with mass \(M = 2151.61\) MeV and width \(\Gamma = 399.18\) MeV, coupling predominantly to \(\bar{K}\Xi\) in \(I=1\) with \(|g_i| = 2.21\).
* VBC model predicts a \(P_{sss}\) state with mass \(M = 1800.79\) MeV and width \(\Gamma = 0.00\) MeV (bound state), coupling to \(\bar{K}\Xi\) in \(I=1\) with \(|g_i| = 2.52\).
* BCN model predicts an \(\Omega^*\) resonance with mass \(M = 2014.06\) MeV and width \(\Gamma = 296.10\) MeV, coupling to \(\bar{K}\Xi\) in \(I=0\) with \(|g_i| = 2.12\).
* VBC model predicts an \(\Omega^*\) bound state with mass \(M = 1707.24\) MeV and width \(\Gamma = 0.00\) MeV, coupling to \(\bar{K}\Xi\) in \(I=0\) with \(|g_i| = 4.27\).
* The VBC model \(P_{sss}\) appears approximately 10 MeV below the \(\bar{K}\Xi\) threshold (average threshold around 1810 MeV).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dynamically generated baryon resonances in the \(S=-3\) sector, which had only considered LO interactions (Kolomeitsev and Oset, 2003; Xu et al., 2015) or employed a consistent \(SU(6)\) extension (Gamermann et al., 2011). The novelty is the inclusion of NLO chiral corrections, which are shown to be essential for providing the attraction needed to bind the \(P_{sss}\) state. The paper also calculates femtoscopic correlation functions for \(\bar{K}\Xi\) pairs as a proposed experimental observable, noting that ALICE collaboration data on these correlations are currently being analyzed. The results motivate experimental searches in \(\Omega_b^-\) decays and femtoscopic measurements to constrain the NLO parameters and confirm or rule out the existence of this pentaquark.
