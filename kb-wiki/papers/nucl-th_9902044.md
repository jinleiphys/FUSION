# True parameters of He-4-Sigma hypernucleus

> It is shown that the true parameters of He-4-Sigma differ from the observed ones. The reason is that the amplitude of He-4-Sigma production in the reaction He-4(K-,pi-) sharply varies just in the corresponding mass region. It leads to the small, but noticeable shift of the binding energy and the width. Besides, the data at the threshold of Sigma-0 production is found to give an additional evidence that He-4-Sigma width cannot exceed 8.0 - 8.5 MeV.

# True parameters of ^4_ΣHe hypernucleus

## Key claim

The observed binding energy and width of the ^4_ΣHe hypernucleus, as extracted from the ^4He(K^-,π^-) reaction, are shifted from the true values because the production amplitude varies sharply across the resonance mass region. The true binding energy and width are each approximately 1 MeV larger than the observed central values, though this shift remains within the error bars of the original analysis.

## Method

The author reanalyzed the missing mass spectrum from the ^4He(K^-,π^-) reaction at 600 MeV/c, originally reported in Ref. [1], by explicitly including the production mechanism via triangle graphs with two-particle intermediate states (^3He–Σ^0) and graphs with three-particle intermediate states (p–d–Σ^0). The amplitude of the triangle graph was calculated using an oscillator wave function for ^4He with parameter p_0 = 90 MeV/c, and the resulting sharply varying amplitude was multiplied by a Breit–Wigner resonance factor to fit the experimental spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Observed parameters from Ref. [1]: excitation energy E_ex = -7 MeV (corresponding to binding energy 4.4 MeV) and width Γ = 7 MeV.
- True parameters from the corrected analysis: binding energy 5.4 MeV (E_ex = -8 MeV) and width 8 MeV.
- The width of ^4_ΣHe cannot exceed 8.0–8.5 MeV, as larger values would produce too strong a peak near E_ex = 0.
- The oscillator parameter p_0 = 90 MeV/c was used for the ^4He wave function, based on ^4He(e,ep) data from Ref. [4].
- The optimum oscillator parameter for describing the Λ production tail was p_0 = 150 MeV/c.

## Context

This work corrects the analysis of the ^4_ΣHe hypernucleus peak observed in the ^4He(K^-,π^-) reaction by Nagae et al. (Ref. [1]), who did not account for the rapid variation of the production amplitude near the resonance. The sharp amplitude behavior is analogous to the well-known cusp in stopped kaon capture K^- d → p Λ π^- (Refs. [5,6]) and in stopped K^- capture in ^4He (Ref. [7]). The corrected parameters are important for estimating Σ–nuclear interactions (Ref. [10]), and the analysis provides a unique example of a resonance on a sharply varying background. Future work with better statistics will require a realistic ^4He wave function, inclusion of form factors, and a more reliable model for the Λ production tail.
