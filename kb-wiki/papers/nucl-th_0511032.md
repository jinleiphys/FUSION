# Periodic-orbit bifurcations as the origin of nuclear deformations

> Semiclassical analysis of shell structures in realistic nuclear potentials are presented using periodic-orbit theory. We adopted r^alpha potential model and examined classical-quantum correspondence using Fourier transformation technique. Spin-orbit coupling is also taken into account in the model Hamiltonian. Gross shell structure for a certain combination of surface diffuseness and spin-orbit parameters are investigated and its relation to pseudospin symmetry is discussed. Analysis of superdeformed shell structure in realistic model is also presented.

## Key claim

Periodic-orbit bifurcations in the classical counterpart of a nuclear mean-field potential are the semiclassical origin of gross shell structures, including those associated with pseudospin symmetry and superdeformation; the claim is conditional on specific combinations of surface diffuseness and spin-orbit coupling parameters (e.g., \(\kappa \simeq 0.05\) for the pseudospin-related shell gaps).

## Method

The author used the \(r^\alpha\) potential model (a simplified Woods-Saxon potential with variable surface diffuseness parameter \(\alpha\)) including spin-orbit coupling, and applied periodic-orbit theory to compute the semiclassical level density via a trace formula. Classical periodic orbits were identified and their scaled periods \(\tau_\beta\) were compared with the Fourier transform of the quantum scaled-energy level density, with particular attention to bifurcation points where the stability factor diverges. The analysis was performed for spherical and deformed shapes, and the semiclassical level density was coarse-grained and compared to quantum results to identify which orbits dominate the shell structure.

## Key numbers

- The Fourier amplitude corresponding to circular and (3,1) orbits is strongly enhanced around the bifurcation point \(\kappa \simeq 0.05\) (spin-orbit parameter), implying a significant growth of gross shell structure at that value.
- At \(\kappa \simeq 0.05\), large energy gaps appear in the single-particle level diagram corresponding to nuclear magic numbers 50, 82, and 126.
- For superdeformed shell structure, significant enhancement of Fourier amplitude occurs at deformation parameter \(\delta = 0.5 \sim 0.6\) (where \(\delta = 3(\eta-1)/(2\eta+1)\) and \(\eta = R_z/R_\perp\) is the axis ratio).
- The coarse-graining smoothing width used was \(\Delta \mathcal{E} = 0.3\), which suppresses orbits with scaled periods \(\tau_\beta > 2\pi / 0.3 \simeq 20\).

## Context

This work extends the author’s previous semiclassical analyses (Arita and Matsuyanagi, 2004; Arita, Sugita, and Matsuyanagi) by incorporating spin-orbit coupling into the \(r^\alpha\) potential model and demonstrating that periodic-orbit bifurcations explain the emergence of pseudospin symmetry (first discussed in the context of the Nilsson model by the pseudospin symmetry authors). It also shows that the superdeformed shell structure can be understood via bifurcations similar to those in models without spin-orbit coupling (Arita, Sugita, and Matsuyanagi), implying that spin-orbit effects are not essential for that phenomenon. The results enable a semiclassical interpretation of shell effects in realistic nuclear mean fields and suggest a connection between classical bifurcations and quantum symmetries.
