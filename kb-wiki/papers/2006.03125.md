# Upgrade of IAEA recommended data of selected nuclear reactions for production of PET and SPECT isotopes

> An IAEA research project was dedicated to the compilation, evaluation and recommendation of cross-section data for the accelerator production of $^{11}$C, $^{13}$N, $^{15}$O, $^{18}$F, $^{64}$Cu, and $^{124}$I positron-emitting radionuclides clinically used for PET imaging, and for the accelerator production of two gamma emitters, $^{81}$Rb and $^{123}$I, used in SPECT imaging. Cross sections for 19 charged-particle induced reactions that can be employed for radionuclide accelerator production were evaluated including uncertainties. The resulting reference cross-section data were obtained from Padé fits to selected and corrected experimental data, and integral thick target yields were subsequently deduced. Uncertainties in the fitted results were estimated via a Padé least-squares method with the addition of a 4\% assessed systematic uncertainty to the estimated experimental uncertainty. Experimental data were also compared with theoretical predictions available from the TENDL library. All of the numerical reference cross-section data with their corresponding uncertainties and deduced integral thick target yields are available on-line at the IAEA-NDS medical portal https://www-nds.iaea.org/medportal/ and also at the IAEA-NDS web page https://www-nds.iaea.org/medical/.

## Key claim

The paper provides updated recommended cross-section data with quantified uncertainties for 19 charged-particle reactions producing six PET and two SPECT radionuclides, using Padé fitting with a 4% assessed systematic uncertainty added to experimental uncertainties, but the fits for the low-Z short-lived isotopes required piecewise approaches over limited energy intervals because the fitting code becomes unstable when the total number of parameters exceeds 35–40.

## Method

Experimental cross-section datasets from the literature were compiled, corrected for outdated nuclear decay data and monitor cross sections, and selected or deselected based on data quality and consistency. Selected data were fitted using a least-squares Padé approach at IPPE Obninsk, with uncertainties estimated from the fit plus an added 4% systematic uncertainty. Integral thick target yields were subsequently deduced from the fitted cross-section curves.

## Key numbers

- Padé fit for $^{14}$N(p,$\alpha$)$^{11}$C: L = 75 parameters, N = 306 data points, $\chi^2$ = 3.61, covering up to 35 MeV; uncertainties range from 60% around 5 MeV to below 8% between 13 and 28 MeV, rising to 10% at highest energy.
- Padé fit for $^{16}$O(p,$\alpha$)$^{13}$N: L = 40 parameters, N = 607 data points, $\chi^2$ = 1.96, covering up to 20 MeV; uncertainties range from 65% near threshold to below 7% between 11 and 18 MeV, reaching 12% at highest energy.
- Padé fit for $^{15}$N(p,n)$^{15}$O: L = 93 parameters (assembled from 3 partial fits), N = 389 data points, $\chi^2$ = 2.40, covering up to 20 MeV; partial fits: 2.518–3.563 MeV (L=27, N=106, $\chi^2$=1.25), 3.563–5.836 MeV (L=36, N=189, $\chi^2$=0.56), 5.836–30 MeV (L=30, N=189, $\chi^2$=0.59).
- Recommended decay data for $^{11}$C: half-life = 20.364(14) min, $\beta^+$ branching = 99.7669%.
- Recommended decay data for $^{13}$N: half-life = 9.965(4) min, $\beta^+$ branching = 99.8036%.
- Recommended decay data for $^{15}$O: half-life = 122.24(16) s, $\beta^+$ branching = 99.9003%.
- Recommended decay data for $^{18}$F: half-life = 109.77(5) min, $\beta^+$ branching = 96.73%.
- Recommended decay data for $^{64}$Cu: half-life = 12.701(2) h, $\beta^+$ branching = 17.6%.
- Recommended decay data for $^{124}$I: half-life = 4.1760(3) d, $\beta^+$ branching = 22.7%.
- Recommended decay data for $^{81}$Rb: half-life = 4.572(4) h, $\beta^+$ branching = 27.2%.
- Recommended decay data for $^{123}$I: half-life = 13.2235(19) h, electron capture = 100%.

## Context

This work updates the IAEA TECDOC-1211 (2001) database and its 2003–2005 updates, as well as the IAEA Technical Report Series 473 (2011) for therapeutic radionuclides, using the same Padé fitting methodology developed in the 2012–2017 IAEA Coordinated Research Project that produced separate publications for monitor reactions (Hermanne 2018), diagnostic SPECT reactions (Tarkanyi 2019), diagnostic PET reactions (Tarkanyi 2019b), and therapeutic reactions (Engle 2019). The new recommended data with uncertainties fill a gap where previous evaluations lacked uncertainty information, and all numerical values are available online at the IAEA-NDS medical portal. The work enables more accurate production planning and quality assurance for medical cyclotron facilities producing these clinically used PET and SPECT isotopes.
