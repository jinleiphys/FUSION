# Recent developments in the shell model Monte Carlo approach to nuclei

> The shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) approach provides a powerful method for the microscopic calculation of statistical and collective nuclear properties in model spaces that are many orders of magnitude larger than those that can be treated by conventional methods. We discuss recent applications of the method to describe the emergence of collectivity in the framework of the configuration-interaction shell model and the crossover from vibrational to rotational collectivity in families of rare-earth nuclei. We have calculated state densities of these rare-earth nuclei and find their collective enhancement factors to be correlated with the pairing and shape phase transitions. We also discuss an accurate method to calculate the ground-state energy of odd-even and odd-odd nuclei, circumventing the sign problem that originates in the projection on an odd number of particles. We have applied this method to calculate pairing gaps in families of isotopes in the iron region.

## Key claim

The shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) approach can describe the microscopic emergence of collectivity in heavy nuclei and the crossover from vibrational to rotational collectivity in rare-earth isotopes, but only when using a thermal observable (the thermal expectation of total angular momentum squared) as a proxy for spectroscopic signatures, because SMMC does not provide detailed spectroscopic information.

## Method

The SMMC method uses a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to express the Gibbs operator as a functional integral over auxiliary fields, then evaluates thermal expectation values via Monte Carlo integration with importance sampling. For heavy nuclei, a proton-neutron formalism was used where protons and neutrons occupy different shells (the 50–82 major shell plus the 1f7/2 orbital for protons, and the 80–126 major shell plus the 0h11/2 and 1g9/2 orbitals for neutrons). For odd-even and odd-odd nuclei, ground-state energies were extracted from the asymptotic imaginary-time behavior of single-particle Green's functions of the neighboring even-even nucleus, circumventing the sign problem from odd-particle-number projection.

## Key numbers

* For 162Dy, the SMMC fit to the rotational model gave a ground-state moment of inertia of 35.5 ± 3.3 MeV−1, compared with the experimental value of 37.2 MeV−1 (derived from 3/E2+).
* In 148Sm, which is spherical in its ground state, the collective enhancement factor K decays to approximately 1 at excitation energies above the pairing transitions.
* In 150Sm, a shape transition from deformed to spherical shape occurs at an excitation energy of approximately 12.5 MeV.
* The SMMC pairing gaps for iron-region nuclei in the fpg9/2-shell model space have statistical errors of approximately 0.01 MeV and agree reasonably with experimental values.

## Context

This work extends the SMMC method to heavy deformed nuclei, building on earlier SMMC calculations in medium-mass nuclei by Nakada (1997) and others. The approach enables microscopic calculations of state densities and collective enhancement factors in model spaces inaccessible to conventional configuration-interaction diagonalization methods, which are limited by combinatorial growth in heavier nuclei. The Green's function method for odd-particle ground-state energies, introduced by Mukherjee (2012), enables calculation of pairing gaps in iron-region nuclei and can be applied to other mass regions where the odd-particle sign problem previously prevented SMMC calculations.
