# Predictions in modified Glauber model of total charged-particle yields centrality dependence in O+O and Ne+Ne collisions at LHC

> In this article we present the results of application of the Monte Carlo modified Glauber model for the predictions of collision centrality dependence of the total charged-particle yields for 16O +16O and 20Ne+20Ne colliding systems at the LHC. Our model differs from the Standard Glauber model by the effective account of the energy losses in successive inelastic nucleon-nucleon collisions. For this purpose, a single model parameter k is defined as a mean fraction of the momentum loss that happens in any binary nucleon collision due to the production of multiple particles. Therefore, the decrease of the nucleon momentum after each inelastic collision leads to a corresponding reduction of the inelastic cross section and the mean multiplicity yield in the subsequent interaction. All these effects are taken into account in the MC model in each of the subsequent inelastic binary interactions. We discuss the purely geometrical effects for these light colliding systems that could be considered useful in future studies of QGP properties in energy density scanning. Predictions in this single parameter model are based on the previous successful analysis of non-linear centrality dependence of charged particle yields observed in Pb+Pb collisions by ALICE at the LHC.

# Predictions in modified Glauber model of total charged-particle yields centrality dependence in O+O and Ne+Ne collisions at LHC

## Key claim

The Modified Glauber Model (MGM), with a single universal parameter k = 0.19 ± 0.05 representing the mean fraction of momentum retained per inelastic nucleon-nucleon collision, predicts that the total charged-particle yields normalized to the number of participant nucleon pairs in central O+O and Ne+Ne collisions at √s_NN = 5.36 TeV will be very close to the values measured in semi-central Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions at the LHC. This prediction depends on the assumption that the parameter k, fitted from heavy-ion data, remains universal for light-ion systems at similar energies.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations using a Modified Glauber Model that incorporates energy-momentum conservation by reducing each nucleon's momentum by a factor k after each inelastic binary collision, which correspondingly reduces the inelastic cross-section and mean multiplicity for subsequent interactions. Nucleon distributions in 16O and 20Ne nuclei were generated using a three-parameter Fermi (3pF) distribution with parameters a = 0.513 fm, R = 2.608 fm, ω = -0.051 for oxygen and a = 0.698 fm, R = 2.791 fm, ω = -0.168 for neon. The parameter k = 0.19 was determined as the mean value from fits to published ALICE data on Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV, and Xe+Xe collisions at √s_NN = 5.44 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Parameter k = 0.19 ± 0.05, obtained as the mean value from fits to Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe data at LHC energies
- For Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV: k = 0.22 (+0.01, -0.02)
- For Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV: k = 0.21 (+0.002, -0.01)
- For Xe+Xe at √s_NN = 5.44 TeV: k = 0.16 (+0.01, -0.002)
- For central O+O collisions (b = 0.0-1.0 fm) at √s_NN = 5.36 TeV: ⟨N_part⟩ = 25.7, ⟨N_coll⟩ = 28.4, ⟨N_ch⟩ = 28.6
- For central Ne+Ne collisions (b = 0.0-1.0 fm) at √s_NN = 5.36 TeV: ⟨N_part⟩ = 33.6, ⟨N_coll⟩ = 40.3, ⟨N_ch⟩ = 40.6
- The MGM predicts a factor of approximately 2 decrease in ⟨N_coll⟩ compared to the Standard Glauber Model for very central O+O and Ne+Ne collisions at √s_NN = 5.36 TeV
- 3pF distribution parameters for 16O: a = 0.513 fm, R = 2.608 fm, ω = -0.051
- 3pF distribution parameters for 20Ne: a = 0.698 fm, R = 2.791 fm, ω = -0.168

## Context

This work extends the Modified Glauber Model previously applied by the same group (Simak and Feofilov, 2024) to successfully describe the non-linear centrality dependence of charged particle yields in Pb+Pb collisions measured by ALICE at the LHC. The predictions for O+O and Ne+Ne collisions are motivated by the need to understand initial-state geometry and energy-momentum conservation effects in small systems, which are relevant for future studies of quark-gluon plasma properties through system-size and energy-density scans at the LHC. The results contrast with the Standard Glauber Model approach (described in references by Kharzeev and Nardi, and by the ALICE Collaboration) which neglects energy-momentum conservation and overestimates the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions.
