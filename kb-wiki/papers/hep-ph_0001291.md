# Intermediate-Mass Dileptons at the CERN-SpS and RHIC

> The significance of thermal dilepton radiation at intermediate invariant masses (1GeV<M<3GeV) in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions is investigated. At CERN-SpS energies, a consistent explanation of the excess observed by NA50 can be given. At RHIC energies the thermal signal is dominated by early emission indicative for QGP formation. Chemical under-saturation effects and the competition with open-charm contributions are addressed.

## Key claim
The excess of intermediate-mass dileptons observed by NA50 in central Pb(158 AGeV)+Pb collisions can be explained by thermal radiation from an equilibrated expanding fireball, with the QGP contribution constituting only a moderate fraction of about 20% of the thermal signal at SpS energies.

## Method
The author employed an expanding thermal fireball model based on an ideal-QGP and resonance-hadron-gas equation of state, with entropy and baryon-number conservation fixing a trajectory in the temperature-chemical potential plane. Thermal dilepton production rates were computed using perturbative QCD for the q-qbar annihilation process, which is applicable for both QGP and hadron gas phases in the intermediate-mass region due to the duality threshold near 1.5 GeV. The experimental acceptance of the NA50 spectrometer was approximated using geometric cuts and a lower energy cutoff determined by reproducing Drell-Yan detector simulations.

## Key numbers
- The excess over open-charm and Drell-Yan sources in central A-A collisions at CERN-SpS is a factor of approximately 2
- The QGP contribution to the thermal signal at SpS energies constitutes a moderate fraction of about 20%
- At RHIC energies, the charged particle multiplicity at midrapidity was fixed at ⟨Nch⟩=800 with an entropy per baryon of s/nB=260
- Alpha-s corrections to the thermal rate may be as large as 20-30%
- If charm quarks undergo energy loss of dE/dx ≈ -1 to -2 GeV/fm, their contribution above M=1.5 GeV could be suppressed by factors of approximately 100

## Context
This work extends previous analyses of intermediate-mass dileptons at CERN-SpS by the NA38/50 and HELIOS-3 collaborations, which found a significant excess over Drell-Yan and open-charm sources scaled from p-A systems. The thermal radiation approach is contrasted with alternative explanations including open-charm enhancement by a factor of about 3 (considered by the NA50 collaboration), charm quark rescattering effects studied by Lin and Wang (1998), and secondary Drell-Yan processes evaluated by Spieles et al. (1998). The results are consistent with low-mass dilepton spectra and chemical freezeout analyses, indicating that CERN-SpS conditions probe QCD matter near the hadron gas-QGP phase boundary. At RHIC energies, the work enables assessment of QGP formation signatures and identifies open-charm contributions as a major uncertainty requiring experimental input.
