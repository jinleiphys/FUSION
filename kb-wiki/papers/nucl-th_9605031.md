# Virtual Compton Scattering and Generalized Polarizabilities of the Proton

> Threshold photon electroproduction off the proton allows one to measure new electromagnetic observables which generalise the usual polarisabilities. There are -- a priori -- ten "generalised polarisabilities", functions of the virtual photon mass. The purpose of this paper is to lay down the appropriate formalism to extract these quantities from the photon electroproduction cross sections. We also give a first estimate of the generalised polarisabilities in the non relativistic quark model.

## Key claim

The paper lays down the formalism to extract ten "generalized polarizabilities" of the proton from threshold photon electroproduction cross sections, and provides a first estimate of these quantities using a non-relativistic quark model, though the actual extraction from experimental data is not performed.

## Method

The authors develop the theoretical framework for Virtual Compton Scattering (VCS) in the one-photon exchange approximation, separating the Bethe-Heitler (BH) amplitude (calculable from QED) from the Full Virtual Compton Scattering (FVCS) amplitude. They then express the FVCS amplitude as a linear combination of VCS amplitudes, and derive the formalism needed to extract the ten generalized polarizabilities from photon electroproduction cross sections.

## Context

This work extends the concept of proton polarizabilities from real Compton scattering to the virtual photon regime accessible through electroproduction. The formalism enables future experimental extraction of generalized polarizabilities from photon electroproduction data, which would provide new electromagnetic observables of the proton beyond the usual polarizabilities. The non-relativistic quark model estimate serves as a first theoretical prediction, to be compared with future experimental results and more sophisticated calculations.
