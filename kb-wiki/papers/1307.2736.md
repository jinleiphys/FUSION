# Holographic thermalization in N=4 Super Yang-Mills theory at finite coupling

> We investigate the behavior of energy momentum tensor correlators in holographic $\mathcal{N}=4$ super Yang-Mills plasma, taking finite coupling corrections into account. In the thermal limit we determine the flow of quasinormal modes as a function of the 't Hooft coupling. Then we use a specific model of holographic thermalization to study the deviation of the spectral densities from their thermal limit in an out-of-equilibrium situation. The main focus lies on the thermalization pattern with which the plasma constituents approach their thermal distribution as the coupling constant decreases from the infinite coupling limit. All obtained results point towards the weakening of the usual top-down thermalization pattern.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the top-down thermalization pattern observed in holographic N=4 Super Yang-Mills plasma at infinite 't Hooft coupling weakens when finite coupling corrections are included, finding that all results point toward such a weakening, though the strong coupling expansion may not be fully trustworthy for all displayed poles.

## Method

The author studies energy momentum tensor correlators in the collapsing shell model of holographic thermalization, working in the quasi-static approximation where the shell's motion is slow compared to other scales. Linearized metric perturbations are solved numerically for the scalar, shear, and sound channels, incorporating leading-order string corrections to type IIB supergravity proportional to gamma = (1/8)zeta(3)lambda^{-3/2}. The quasinormal mode spectrum is obtained by solving for zeros of the ingoing mode at the boundary, while the spectral densities are computed from the retarded correlators using standard AdS/CFT techniques.

## Key numbers

- The finite coupling parameter is gamma = (1/8)zeta(3)lambda^{-3/2}, where lambda is the 't Hooft coupling
- The gamma-corrected temperature relation is r_h = pi T/(1 + 15 gamma)
- Quasinormal mode spectra are computed for q = 0 and q = 2pi T, showing the imaginary part of omega_n increases as lambda decreases from infinity
- Spectral density deviations are shown for lambda = 300 and lambda = 100, with the shell positioned at u_s = 0.5
- The relative deviation R approaches a constant for large frequencies when c = 0 (plasma constituents at rest), but grows above a critical frequency for larger c values

## Context

This work extends the analysis of quasinormal modes and spectral densities for energy momentum tensor correlators in N=4 SYM plasma, building on the infinite coupling results of Kovtun (2005) and the finite coupling hydrodynamic calculations of Benincasa (2005) and Buchel (2008). The collapsing shell model was introduced by Danielsson (1999) and applied to thermalization at infinite coupling by Lin (2008). The results are consistent with earlier findings for R-current correlators by Steineder (2012, 2013), suggesting the weakening of top-down thermalization is a general feature rather than specific to photons or the collapsing shell model. The paper contrasts with the geometric probe analysis of Baron (2013), who found that UV modes thermalize faster and IR modes slower at finite coupling, speculating that the difference may arise from Ramond-Ramond five-form corrections to current correlators.
