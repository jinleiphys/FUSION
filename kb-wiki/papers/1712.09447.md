# Microscopic description of fission in odd-mass uranium and plutonium nuclei with the Gogny energy density functional

> The parametrization D1M of the Gogny energy density functional is used to study fission in the odd-mass Uranium and Plutonium isotopes with A=233,\ldots,249 within the framework of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) Equal Filling Approximation (EFA). Ground state quantum numbers and deformations, pairing energies, one-neutron separation energies, barrier heights and fission isomer excitation energies are given. Fission paths, collective masses and zero point rotational and vibrational quantum corrections are used to compute the systematic of the spontaneous fission half-lives t$_{SF}$, the masses and charges of the fission fragments as well as their intrinsic shapes. Although there exits a strong variance of the predicted fission rates with respect to the details involved in their computation, it is shown that both the specialization energy and the pairing quenching effects, taken into account fully variationally within the HFB-EFA blocking scheme, lead to larger spontaneous fission half-lives in odd-mass U and Pu nuclei as compared with the corresponding even-even neighbors. It is shown that modifications of a few percent in the strengths of the neutron and proton pairing fields can have a significant impact on the collective masses leading to uncertainties of several orders of magnitude in the predicted t$_{SF}$ values. Alpha-decay lifetimes have also been computed using a parametrization of the Viola-Seaborg formula.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that both the specialization energy and pairing quenching effects, treated fully variationally within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Equal Filling Approximation (HFB-EFA) blocking scheme, lead to larger spontaneous fission half-lives in odd-mass uranium and plutonium nuclei compared to their even-even neighbors, though the predicted fission rates exhibit strong variance depending on computational details such as the choice of collective mass and the zero-point energy parameter.

## Method

The authors used the Gogny D1M energy density functional within the constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Equal Filling Approximation (HFB-EFA) framework, assuming axial symmetry and allowing reflection symmetry breaking. They computed one-fragment (1F) and two-fragment (2F) fission paths by applying constraints on the quadrupole Q20 and octupole Q30 operators, with additional constraints on a necking operator for 2F solutions. For each odd-mass nucleus, they identified the ground state K quantum number by blocking several one-quasiparticle configurations, then constructed the fission path assuming conservation of that K quantum number, and computed spontaneous fission half-lives using the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) formalism with both adiabatic time-dependent (ATD) and generator coordinate method (GCM) collective masses.

## Key numbers

- For 243U with K0 = 9/2, the inner barrier height is 10.05 MeV at Q20 = 28b, the first fission isomer lies 4.28 MeV above the ground state at Q20 = 44b, the second barrier height is 11.22 MeV at Q20 = 64b, the second fission isomer lies 5.91 MeV above the ground state at Q20 = 88b, and the outer barrier height is 8.40 MeV at Q20 = 112b.
- For 243U with E0 = 1.0 MeV, the ATD masses yield log10 tSF = 60.5530 s while the GCM masses yield log10 tSF = 39.2109 s; with E0 = 1.5 MeV, the ATD masses yield log10 tSF = 56.5407 s and the GCM masses yield log10 tSF = 36.5494 s.
- The average (AV) HFB inner, second, and third barriers for 243U are 9.68, 10.72, and 8.05 MeV respectively, compared to the HFB-EFA values of 10.05, 11.22, and 8.40 MeV.
- The average (AV) excitation energies of the first and second fission isomers for 243U are 4.02 and 5.82 MeV, compared to the HFB-EFA values of 4.28 and 5.91 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous Gogny-D1M calculations for even-even uranium and plutonium isotopes (Rodriguez-Guzman and Robledo, 2014; 2014) and for odd-mass nobelium isotopes (Rodriguez-Guzman and Robledo, 2016) to the odd-mass uranium and plutonium chains with A = 233 to 249. The study enables systematic comparison of spontaneous fission half-lives between odd-mass and even-even nuclei in this region, and demonstrates that modifications of a few percent in the strengths of the neutron and proton pairing fields can lead to uncertainties of several orders of magnitude in predicted tSF values, highlighting the need for improved treatment of pairing correlations in fission calculations.
