# Wigner-Eckart Theorem and the False EDM of $^{199}$Hg

> In neutron electric dipole moment experiments, 199Hg is used as a comagnetometer. The comagnetometer suffers from a false EDM arising in leading order from a gradient dBz/dz in the magnetic field. Our work concerns higher-order multipole corrections to the false EDM of 199Hg. We show that for spherical traps, all higher-order multipole are identically zero. We further show that for the usual cylindrical traps used in EDM experiments, selection of quasi-spherical dimensions for the trap can reduce the higher-order contributions. The results are another indication that trap geometry is an important consideration for experiments desiring to control this systematic effect.

## Key claim

For spherical traps, all higher-order multipole corrections to the false electric dipole moment (EDM) of 199Hg are identically zero; for cylindrical traps, choosing quasi-spherical dimensions (height H = √2 × radius R) can suppress the leading higher-order term (g₃₀) and reduce the next term (g₅₀) relative to the standard ILL/PSI geometry.

## Method

The authors decompose the magnetic field inside the trap using a spherical harmonic expansion of the magnetic scalar potential. They then apply the Wigner-Eckart theorem by analogy to quantum mechanical matrix elements, treating the differential operator (x∂/∂x + y∂/∂y) as an admixture of spherical tensors of rank 0 and 2. For a spherical cell, selection rules from the Clebsch-Gordan coefficients restrict contributions to only the ℓ=2, m=0 term. For cylindrical cells, they perform explicit volume averages over the cell dimensions to compute the contributions from the ℓ=2, 4, and 6 multipoles.

## Key numbers

- For the ILL/PSI nEDM experiment cell (R = 23.5 cm, H = 12 cm), the g₃₀ term coefficient is −240 cm² and the g₅₀ term coefficient is 6.35×10⁴ cm⁴, giving d_f,Hg = 1.15×10⁻²⁷ (g₁₀ − g₃₀·240 + g₅₀·6.35×10⁴) e·cm with g in pT/cm^ℓ.
- For a quasi-spherical cell with H = √2 R, the g₃₀ term is zeroed; for a cell with R = 17 cm and H ≈ 24 cm (keeping approximately the same volume), the false EDM becomes d_f,Hg = 6.1×10⁻²⁸ (g₁₀ − g₅₀·2.26×10⁴) e·cm.
- The quasi-spherical condition is H = √2 R, which zeroes the ℓ=4 (g₃₀) contribution.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of false EDM in 199Hg comagnetometers beyond the leading-order vertical gradient ∂B_z/∂z studied by Pignol and Roccia (2012) and the Monte Carlo studies of higher-order effects by Pendlebury et al. (2004). It provides a theoretical framework using the Wigner-Eckart theorem to understand why spherical traps suppress higher multipoles, and quantifies how cylindrical trap geometry choices can reduce systematic effects in neutron EDM experiments at ILL and PSI. The results enable experimental groups to consider trap geometry as a design parameter for controlling systematic uncertainties, though the authors note that the full correction scheme must also account for neutron false EDM, spin relaxation times, and frequency ratio measurements.
