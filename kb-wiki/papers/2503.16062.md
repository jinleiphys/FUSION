# Constraint Phase Space Formulations for Finite-State Quantum Systems: The Relation between Commutator Variables and Complex Stiefel Manifolds

> We have recently developed the \textit{constraint} coordinate-momentum \textit{phase space} (CPS) formulation for finite-state quantum systems. It has been implemented for the electronic subsystem in nonadiabatic transition dynamics to develop practical trajectory-based approaches. In the generalized CPS formulation for the mapping Hamiltonian of the classical mapping model with commutator variables (CMMcv) method [\textit{J. Phys. Chem. A} \textbf{2021}, 125, 6845-6863], each {connected} component of the generalized CPS is the \textit{complex Stiefel manifold} labeled by the eigenvalue set of the mapping kernel. Such a phase space structure allows for exact trajectory-based dynamics for pure discrete (electronic) degrees of freedom (DOFs), where the equations of motion of each trajectory are isomorphic to the time-dependent Schrödinger equation. We employ covariant kernels {within the generalized CPS framework} to develop two approaches that naturally yield exact evaluation of time correlation functions (TCFs) for pure discrete (electronic) DOFs. In addition, we briefly discuss the phase space mapping formalisms where the contribution of each trajectory to the integral expression of the {TCF} of population dynamics is strictly positive semi-definite. The generalized CPS formulation also indicates that the equations of motion in phase space mapping model I of our previous work [\textit{J. Chem. Phys.} \textbf{2016}, 145, 204105; \textbf{2017}, 146, 024110; \textbf{2019}, 151, 024105] lead to a complex Stiefel manifold $\mathrm{U}(F)/\mathrm{U}(F-2)$. It is expected that the generalized CPS formulation has implications for simulations of both nonadiabatic transition dynamics and many-body quantum dynamics for spins/bosons/fermions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the generalized constraint phase space (CPS) for finite-state quantum systems, when constructed through the covariance condition of the mapping kernel, has each connected component equal to a complex Stiefel manifold labeled by the eigenvalue set of the mapping kernel, which allows exact trajectory-based dynamics for pure discrete degrees of freedom without singularities.

## Method

The authors establish the relationship between the generalized CPS formulation and complex Stiefel manifolds by imposing the U(F) covariance condition on the mapping kernel, which requires that unitary time evolution of the kernel corresponds to a trajectory on the phase space. They diagonalize the mapping kernel to identify its eigenvalue structure and determine the isometry group, showing that each connected component of the phase space is the quotient space U(F)/U(F-r), i.e., the complex Stiefel manifold V_r(C^F). Using covariant kernels within this framework, they develop two approaches for exact evaluation of time correlation functions for pure discrete electronic degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- The constraint relation for the CPS is given by sum_{n=1}^{F} (x_n^2 + p_n^2)/2 = 1 + F gamma, where gamma ranges from -1/F to +infinity.
- The phase space mapping model I from previous work leads to a complex Stiefel manifold U(F)/U(F-2).
- For the CMM method, the inverse mapping kernel contains the factor (1+F)/(2(1+F gamma)^2) in its expression.
- The weight function w(gamma) for the wMM method must satisfy the condition integral_{-1/F}^{+infinity} dgamma w(gamma)(F gamma^2 + 2 gamma) = 1.

## Context

This work extends the constraint phase space (CPS) formulation previously developed by Liu and coworkers (Liu 2016, 2017; He 2019, 2021, 2022) for finite-state quantum systems, which was implemented for electronic subsystems in nonadiabatic transition dynamics. The paper unifies the CMMcv method (He 2021) and phase space mapping model I (Liu 2016, 2017, 2019) under the complex Stiefel manifold structure, and contrasts with SU(2) or SU(F) Stratonovich phase space approaches (Stratonovich 1956; Varilly 1989; Klimov 2002, 2008, 2017) which involve nonlinear equations of motion with singularities for F>2. The generalized CPS formulation is expected to enable simulations of nonadiabatic transition dynamics and many-body quantum dynamics for spins, bosons, and fermions.
