# Bound states of $_{c\bar{c}}^{9}$Be within $c\bar{c}+α+α$ cluster models based on state-of-the-art HAL QCD charmonium-nucleon interactions

> The possible bound state of the $ _{c\bar{c}}^{9}$Be, a charmonium-nucleus system, is investigated. The analysis is carried out within a three-cluster model, where its binary subsystems are represented as $ c\bar{c}\textrm{+}α$ and $α+α$. The hyperspherical harmonics method is employed to facilitate a convenient description of this three-cluster configuration. The calculations are done by employing the effective $ c\bar{c}\textrm{-}α$ potentials. These potentials were derived recently based on state-of-the-art lattice QCD calculations, which provided interactions for the spin 3/2 $J/ψN $, spin 1/2 $J/ψN $, spin 1/2 $η_{c}N$ and spin-averaged $J/ψN$ interactions, all obtained with nearly physical pion masses. The Coulomb interaction was also incorporated into the current calculations. It is determined that, despite neither the $ _{c\bar{c}}^{5}$He nor the $^{8}$Be binary subsystems being bound, a bound state of the $ c\bar{c}\textrm{-} αα$ nuclear system could potentially exist. The maximum central binding energy is found to be approximately 1.71 MeV, based on the spin 1/2 $J/ψN $ interaction, while a minimum value of about 0.56 MeV is obtained from calculations involving the spin 1/2 $η_{c}N$ interaction.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a bound state of the charmonium-nucleus system \( _{c\bar{c}}^{9}\)Be (a \(c\bar{c} + \alpha + \alpha\) three-cluster system) could potentially exist, with binding energies ranging from about 0.56 MeV to 1.71 MeV depending on the spin channel of the charmonium-nucleon interaction. This result holds despite the fact that neither the \(c\bar{c}+\alpha\) nor the \(\alpha+\alpha\) binary subsystems are individually bound, making the system a candidate Borromean nucleus.

## Method

The analysis uses a three-cluster model where the \( _{c\bar{c}}^{9}\)Be nucleus is treated as a \(c\bar{c}\) pair (either \(J/\psi\) or \(\eta_c\)) plus two \(\alpha\) particles. The hyperspherical harmonics (HH) expansion method is employed to solve the three-body Faddeev equations. The \(c\bar{c}\)-\(\alpha\) potentials are obtained by single-folding the nucleon density of the \(\alpha\) particle with state-of-the-art HAL QCD lattice QCD \(c\bar{c}\)-N interactions (spin 3/2 \(J/\psi N\), spin 1/2 \(J/\psi N\), spin 1/2 \(\eta_c N\), and spin-averaged \(J/\psi N\)), and are fitted to a Woods-Saxon form. The \(\alpha\alpha\) interaction uses an Ali-Bodmer double Gaussian potential with a hard-sphere Coulomb term. The Coulomb interaction is also included in the \(c\bar{c}\)-\(\alpha\) system.

## Key numbers

* Maximum central binding energy \(B_3\) of the \(c\bar{c}+\alpha+\alpha\) system: approximately 1.71(1.60) MeV, based on the spin 1/2 \(J/\psi N\) interaction.
* Minimum central binding energy \(B_3\): approximately 0.56(30) MeV, based on the spin 1/2 \(\eta_c N\) interaction.
* Binding energy \(B_3\) for spin 3/2 \(J/\psi N\) interaction: 1.13(1.00) MeV.
* Binding energy \(B_3\) for spin-averaged \(J/\psi N\) interaction: 1.31(1.10) MeV.
* Nuclear matter radius \(r_{\text{mat}}\) for the spin 1/2 \(J/\psi N\) case: 2.60(51) fm.
* Nuclear matter radius \(r_{\text{mat}}\) for the spin 1/2 \(\eta_c N\) case: 3.05(49) fm.
* The spin 1/2 \(J/\psi N\) interaction produces a loosely bound \(c\bar{c}\)-\(\alpha\) two-body state with central binding energy of approximately 0.1 MeV; all other \(c\bar{c}\)-\(\alpha\) channels are unbound.
* Convergence of the HH expansion was achieved with maximum hypermomentum \(K_{\text{max}} = 30\) and maximum hyperradial excitations \(i_{\text{max}} = 25\).

## Context

This work extends the search for charmonium-nucleus bound states, originally proposed by Brodsky (1990) via QCD van der Waals forces, to a three-cluster system using modern lattice QCD inputs. It builds directly on the HAL QCD Collaboration's lattice calculations of \(c\bar{c}\)-N interactions at nearly physical pion masses (Lyu et al., 2025), which provided the first-principles potentials used here. The study contrasts with earlier phenomenological approaches such as QCD sum rules (Kim and Lee, 2001), one-boson exchange models (Liu et al., 2001), and the quark model (Barnes and Swanson, 2007), which predicted a wide range of binding energies. The finding that \( _{c\bar{c}}^{9}\)Be may be a Borromean nucleus (bound three-body system with no bound binary subsystems) enables future experimental searches at facilities like Jefferson Lab, J-PARC, and FAIR, and motivates further lattice QCD calculations at the physical point by the HAL QCD Collaboration.
