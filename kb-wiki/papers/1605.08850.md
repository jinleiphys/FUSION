# Asymmetric vector mesons produced in nuclear collisions

> It is argued that the experimentally observed phenomenon of asymmetric shapes of vector mesons produced in nuclear media during high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions can be explained as Fano-Feshbach resonances. It has been observed that the mass distributions of lepton pairs created at meson decays decline from the traditional Breit-Wigner shape with some excess in the low-mass wing of the resonance. It is clear that the whole phenomenon is related to some interaction with the nuclear medium. Moreover, it can be further detalized in quantum mechanics as the interference of direct and continuum states in Fano-Feshbach effect. To reveal the nature of the interaction it is proposed to use a phenomenological model of the additional contribution due to Cherenkov gluons. They can be created because of the excess of the refractivity index over 1 just in the low-mass wing as required by the classical Cherenkov treatment. In quantum mechanics, this requirement is related to the positive real part of the interaction amplitude in this wing. The corresponding parameters are found from the comparison with $ρ$-meson data and admit reasonable explanation.

# Asymmetric vector mesons produced in nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The experimentally observed asymmetric shapes of vector mesons produced in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions can be explained as Fano-Feshbach resonances, with the specific mechanism of the asymmetry attributed to Cherenkov gluon emission in the nuclear medium; however, the nature of the primary interaction that initiates the Cherenkov gluon emission is left unknown.

## Method

The authors fit the dilepton mass spectrum from semi-central In-In collisions at 158 AGeV (NA60 Collaboration) using two equivalent phenomenological models: a Fano-Feshbach interference formula with a single parameter q describing the relative strength of discrete states and continuum, and a Cherenkov gluon model with a parameter w_r proportional to the real part of the forward scattering amplitude. Both fits were restricted to the low-mass (left) wing of the ρ-meson peak to avoid contamination from higher-mass resonances.

## Key numbers

- The Fano-Feshbach fit to the NA60 In-In data yields q ≈ 0.363, with Γ_r = 184 MeV and normalization C = 29.
- The Cherenkov gluon fit yields A = 25, Γ_r = 184 MeV, and w_r = 0.838.
- The admixture of direct states relative to continuum is estimated as πq²/2 ≈ 0.21.
- The PDG parameters for the ρ-meson are m_ρ = 775 MeV and Γ_ρ = 149 MeV; the in-medium fit requires a much larger width of Γ_r = 336 MeV to attempt a Breit-Wigner description, which still fails to reproduce the asymmetry.
- The ratio of ρ:ω:φ meson peaks in the dilepton spectrum is approximately 10:1:2.

## Context

This work extends the Fano-Feshbach resonance formalism, previously applied to narrow resonances with c- and b-quarks by Lenzi (2015) and Lenzi (2016) to explain vacuum-induced asymmetry, to wide vector mesons in nuclear media. The authors propose Cherenkov gluon emission as a specific physical mechanism for the interference, contrasting with earlier approaches using effective hadronic Lagrangians (Pisa group, Harada, Brown, Boreskov, Dusling, Leupold, Rupp, Chiu, Eletsky, Eletsky and Ioffe, Martell, Rapp, and van Hees) or hydrodynamics of the expanding fireball. The work enables experimental searches for tetraquark states in the low-mass wings of heavy resonances at RHIC and LHC, and calls for improved experimental precision (potentially by two orders of magnitude) to test the universality of the low-mass asymmetry.
