# Superheavy Nuclei in the Quark-Meson-Coupling Model

> We present a selection of the first results obtained in a comprehensive calculation of ground state properties of even-even superheavy nuclei in the region of 96 < Z < 136 and 118 < N < 320 from the Quark-Meson-Coupling model (QMC). Ground state binding energies, the neutron and proton number dependence of quadrupole deformations and Q$_α$ values are reported for even-even nuclei with 100 < Z < 136 and compared with available experimental data and predictions of macro-microscopic models. Predictions of properties of nuclei, including Q$_α$ values, relevant for planning future experiments are presented.

## Key claim
The Quark-Meson-Coupling model with pion exchange (QMCπ) predicts ground state properties of superheavy nuclei that are of similar quality to traditional models with many more parameters, but it yields significantly different Qα values and shell closure locations, particularly showing no evidence for the Z=114 or Z=126 shell closures predicted by other approaches.

## Method
The QMCπ energy density functional was constructed by extending the published QMC model to include explicit pion exchange, using a Hartree-Fock plus BCS code (SKYAX) adapted for axially symmetric and reflection-asymmetric shapes. The four model parameters (Gσ, Gω, Gρ, Mσ) were fitted to the experimental data set of Klüpfel et al. (2009) using the POUNDERS fitting package, with volume pairing in the BCS approximation fitted to the same data. The calculation was limited to bound single-particle states where the Fermi level does not reach the continuum, covering even-even nuclei with 96 < Z < 136 and 118 < N < 320.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matter properties from the QMCπ parameter set: E0 = -15.8 MeV, ρ0 = 0.153 fm⁻³, K0 = 319 MeV, S0 = 30 MeV, L = 27 MeV
- QMCπ predicts Qα = 13.2 MeV for ²⁹⁶120 (Z=120, N=176), compared to 13.69 MeV (FRDM) and 13.23 MeV (MM)
- QMCπ predicts Qα = 11.9 MeV for ²⁹⁶Og (Z=118, N=178), compared to 12.29 MeV (FRDM) and 12.06 MeV (MM)
- Calculated α-decay half-life of ²⁶⁸Sg: 0.21 s (QMCπ), 3.6 days (FRDM), 5.9 hours (MM)
- Calculated half-lives from QMCπ Qα values: ²⁸⁴Fl = 1.4×10⁻⁵ s, ²⁹⁶Og = 4.0×10⁻⁴ s, ²⁹⁶120 = 2.3×10⁻⁶ s, ²⁹⁸120 = 5.8×10⁻⁶ s, ³⁰⁶124 = 1.0×10⁻⁸ s
- QMCπ predicts low deformation (β₂ < 0.1) for isotopes with neutron numbers around N≈180 and for Z>114 near N=260, and for isotones near Z=100, 120, and 136

## Context
This work extends the Quark-Meson-Coupling model approach of Guichon (1988, 1996, 2004) from finite nuclei and nuclear matter to the superheavy region, using the QMCπ version that adds pion exchange to the earlier model of Stone (2016). The predictions differ from macroscopic-microscopic models such as the Finite-Range Droplet Model of Moller (2016) and the Muntian et al. model (2003), which predict a canonical island of stability at Z=114, N=184, and from self-consistent mean-field models that predict Z=126, N=172-184 (non-relativistic) or Z=120, N=172 (relativistic). The results enable experimental planning for synthesis of new elements including ²⁹⁶Og, ²⁹⁶,²⁹⁸120, and ³⁰⁶124, and suggest that laser resonance ionization spectroscopy (Laatiaoui 2016, Block 2017) could distinguish between model classes through measurements of quadrupole moments.
