# The Spinless Relativistic Hulthén Problem

> The spinless Salpeter equation can be regarded as the eigenvalue equation of a Hamiltonian that involves the relativistic kinetic energy and therefore is, in general, a nonlocal operator. Accordingly, it is hard to find solutions of this bound-state equation by exclusively analytic means. Nevertheless, a lot of tools enables us to constrain the resulting bound-state spectra rigorously. We illustrate some of these techniques for the example of the Hulthén potential.

# The Spinless Relativistic Hulthén Problem

## Key claim
The spinless Salpeter equation with the Hulthén potential can be rigorously constrained without relying on approximate analytic solutions or purely numerical methods, but the Hamiltonian is bounded from below only when the ratio of coupling strength to range parameter satisfies h/b ≤ 16/(3π) ≈ 1.69765; for the specific parameter values m=1, b=0.15, h=0.11 taken from prior work (Zarrinkamar), the system is not too relativistic and admits bound states.

## Method
The authors apply the Rayleigh–Ritz variational method using orthonormal basis functions defined by generalized-Laguerre polynomials in configuration space, with Fourier-transformed counterparts in momentum space to compute expectation values of the relativistic kinetic energy T(p) = 2√(p² + m²) in momentum space and the Hulthén potential V_H(r) = -h/(exp(br)-1) in configuration space. They also derive lower bounds on the spectrum by comparing the Hulthén potential to the Coulomb potential V_C(r) = -κ/r, for which known lower bounds from Herbst (1977) and Martin–Roy (1989) exist, and use operator inequalities to relate the semirelativistic problem to its nonrelativistic Schrödinger limit.

## Key numbers
- Critical ratio for boundedness from below: h/b ≤ 16/(3π) = 1.69765...
- For the parameter set m=1, b=0.15, h=0.11 (from Zarrinkamar): h/b = 0.73̅, satisfying the boundedness condition.
- Nonrelativistic s-wave bound states limited to n ≤ √(m h)/b = 2.211..., allowing at most two such states.
- Bargmann bound on total number of nonrelativistic bound states: N_NR < 36.357...
- Herbst lower bound for ground-state energy E₀ ≥ 1.635 (binding energy B₀ ≥ -0.365) for κ = h/b = 0.73̅.
- Martin–Roy lower bound for ground-state energy E₀ ≥ 1.833 (binding energy B₀ ≥ -0.167) for κ = h/b = 0.73̅.
- Variational upper bound for ground state (n_r=0, ℓ=0): Ē = -0.10577.
- Variational upper bound for first excited s-wave (n_r=1, ℓ=0): Ē = -0.0022398.
- Schrödinger upper bound for ground state: Ē_NR = -0.0850694̅.
- Expectation value ⟨p²/m²⟩ ≈ 0.26 for the variational ground state, indicating a mildly relativistic system.

## Context
This work extends rigorous bounding techniques previously applied to the spinless Salpeter equation with Coulomb and Woods–Saxon potentials (Lucha 1996, Lucha 2014) to the Hulthén potential, which has a finite number of bound states unlike the Coulomb case. The analysis provides rigorous checks on approximate solutions from earlier studies by Ikhdair and Sever (2008) and Zarrinkamar (who used a generalized Hulthén variant), showing that one entry in Zarrinkamar's Table 1 violates the Martin–Roy lower bound. The results enable future studies to benchmark numerical or approximate solutions of the semirelativistic Hulthén problem against these rigorous bounds.
