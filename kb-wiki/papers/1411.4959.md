# Complex Saddle Points and Disorder Lines in QCD at finite temperature and density

> The properties and consequences of complex saddle points are explored in phenomenological models of QCD at non-zero temperature and density. Such saddle points are a consequence of the sign problem, and should be considered in both theoretical calculations and lattice simulations. Although saddle points in finite-density QCD are typically in the complex plane, they are constrained by a symmetry that simplifies analysis. We model the effective potential for Polyakov loops using two different potential terms for confinement effects, and consider three different cases for quarks: very heavy quarks, massless quarks without modeling of chiral symmetry breaking effects, and light quarks with both deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration effects included in a pair of PNJL models. In all cases, we find that a single dominant complex saddle point is required for a consistent description of the model. This saddle point is generally not far from the real axis; the most easily noticed effect is a difference between the Polyakov loop expectation values $\left\langle {\rm Tr}_{F}P\right\rangle $ and $\left\langle {\rm Tr}_{F}P^{\dagger}\right\rangle $, and that is confined to small region in the $μ-T$ plane. In all but one case, a disorder line is found in the region of critical and/or crossover behavior. The disorder line marks the boundary between exponential decay and sinusoidally modulated exponential decay of correlation functions. Disorder line effects are potentially observable in both simulation and experiment. Precision simulations of QCD in the $μ-T$ plane have the potential to clearly discriminate between different models of confinement.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a single dominant complex saddle point, constrained by an antilinear $\mathcal{CK}$ symmetry, is required for a consistent description of QCD at finite temperature and density in phenomenological models, and that this saddle point generally lies close to the real axis, with the most noticeable effect being a difference between Polyakov loop expectation values $\langle {\rm Tr}_{F}P \rangle$ and $\langle {\rm Tr}_{F}P^{\dagger} \rangle$ confined to a small region in the $\mu$-$T$ plane.

## Method

The authors model the effective potential for Polyakov loops using two different confinement potentials (Model A and Model B from Meisinger (2001)), and consider three quark cases: heavy quarks with mass 2 GeV, massless quarks without chiral symmetry breaking, and light quarks with chiral dynamics included via a Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with two flavors. For each case, they find complex saddle points of the effective potential that respect $\mathcal{CK}$ symmetry, parametrized for $SU(3)$ by two variables $\theta$ and $\psi$, and analyze the stability of these saddle points using the mass matrix of Polyakov loop fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- For pure $SU(3)$ gauge theory, the deconfinement temperature is $T_d \approx 270$ MeV, used to set parameters for confinement models.
- Model A confinement parameter: $M_A = 596$ MeV.
- Model B confinement parameter: $R = 1.0028$ fm.
- Heavy quark mass: 2000 MeV.
- PNJL model parameters: current quark mass $m_0 = 5.5$ MeV, four-fermion coupling $g_S = 5.496$ GeV$^{-2}$, cutoff $\Lambda = 631.4$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formalism (Nishimura (2014)) for complex saddle points in finite-density QCD, applying it to phenomenological models with confinement effects from Meisinger (2001). The paper identifies disorder lines in the $\mu$-$T$ plane for most models, marking the boundary between exponential decay and sinusoidally modulated exponential decay of correlation functions, which could be observable in lattice simulations and experiments. The results provide guidance for lattice simulations by indicating the behavior of the dominant field configuration and suggest that precision simulations can discriminate between different confinement models.
