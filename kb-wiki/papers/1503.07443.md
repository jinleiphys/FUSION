# Nonconformal viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics

> We generalize the derivation of viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics from kinetic theory to allow for non-zero particle masses. The macroscopic theory is obtained by taking moments of the Boltzmann equation after expanding the distribution function around a spheroidally deformed local momentum distribution whose form has been generalized by the addition of a scalar field that accounts non-perturbatively (i.e. already at leading order) for bulk viscous effects. Hydrodynamic equations for the parameters of the leading-order distribution function and for the residual (next-to-leading order) dissipative flows are obtained from the three lowest moments of the Boltzmann equation. The approach is tested for a system undergoing (0+1)-dimensional boost-invariant expansion for which the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation in relaxation time approximation is known. Nonconformal viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics is shown to approximate this exact solution more accurately than any other known hydrodynamic approximation.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics (vaHydro) to massive particles by including a scalar field Φ in the leading-order distribution function that accounts non-perturbatively for bulk viscous effects; the resulting nonconformal vaHydro approximates the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation in relaxation time approximation more accurately than any other known hydrodynamic approximation for a (0+1)-dimensional boost-invariant system.

## Method

The authors start from kinetic theory and expand the distribution function around a spheroidally deformed local momentum distribution, adding a scalar field Φ to the anisotropy tensor to handle bulk viscosity at leading order. They take the three lowest moments of the Boltzmann equation (zeroth, first, and second) to obtain hydrodynamic equations for the leading-order parameters (Λ, ξ, Φ) and for the residual dissipative flows (bulk viscous pressure Π̃ and shear stress tensor π̃^μν), using a 14-moment approximation for the deviation from the leading-order distribution. The approach is tested by solving the resulting equations numerically for a (0+1)-dimensional boost-invariant expansion of a massive gas and comparing to the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation in relaxation time approximation.

## Context

This work extends the viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics (vaHydro) framework of Bazow, Heinz, and Martinez (2013) from massless to massive particles, building on the anisotropic hydrodynamics approach of Martinez and Florkowski (2010) and Florkowski (2010). It generalizes the nonconformal leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics studied by Nopoush (2014) and Denicol (2014) to next-to-leading order by including residual dissipative flows from the δf term. The paper enables more accurate hydrodynamic modeling of quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where finite particle masses and bulk viscous effects become important, especially near the quark-hadron phase transition.
