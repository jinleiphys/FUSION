# Universal low energy features of two-body systems

> We apply renormalization ideas to study low-energy interactions in two-body systems. As we will see this method highlights a model-independent description of a broad variety of systems ranging from ultra-could atoms to NN and Lambda-Lambda interactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that low-energy two-body scattering observables (scattering length and effective range) are linked by a universal bilinear relation whose coefficients depend only on the long-distance potential, making the short-distance physics reparameterizable as a boundary condition. This universality holds across ultra-cold atoms, nucleon-nucleon (NN), and Lambda-Lambda systems, provided the scattering length is treated as an independent input rather than predicted from the potential.

## Method

The authors apply renormalization with boundary conditions to the effective range expansion for S-wave phase shifts, deriving a bilinear relation \( r_0 = A + B/\alpha_0 + C/\alpha_0^2 \) where the coefficients \( A, B, C \) depend only on the long-range part of the potential. For atom-atom systems they use the van der Waals potential truncated at the \( 1/r^6 \) term; for NN systems they use a one-boson-exchange (OBE) potential with \( \pi, \sigma, \rho, \omega \) mesons in the large-\( N_c \) limit; for \( \Lambda\Lambda \) they use SU(3) relations for the meson couplings. The short-distance physics is absorbed into the scattering length \( \alpha_0 \), which is taken as an input parameter.

## Key numbers

- For atom-atom van der Waals systems (leading \( 1/r^6 \) term): coefficients in units of the vdW radius \( R_6 = (2\mu C_6)^{1/4} \) are \( A/R_6 = 1.39473 \), \( B/R_6 = -1.33333 \), \( C/R_6 = 0.637318 \).
- For np scattering in the \( ^1S_0 \) channel: input scattering length \( \alpha_{0,np} = -23.74 \, \text{fm} \), predicted effective range \( r_0 = 2.672 \, \text{fm} \) (experimental: \( r_0^{\text{exp}} = 2.77(5) \, \text{fm} \)).
- For pp scattering with Coulomb interaction (pp(C)): predicted scattering length \( \alpha_0 = -7.706 \, \text{fm} \) and effective range \( r_0 = 2.747 \, \text{fm} \) (experimental: \( \alpha_0^{\text{exp}} = -7.8149(29) \, \text{fm} \), \( r_0^{\text{exp}} = 2.769(14) \, \text{fm} \)).
- For nn scattering: predicted scattering length \( \alpha_0 = -19.626 \, \text{fm} \) and effective range \( r_0 = 2.771 \, \text{fm} \) (experimental: \( \alpha_0^{\text{exp}} = -18.9(4) \, \text{fm} \), \( r_0^{\text{exp}} = 2.75(11) \, \text{fm} \)).
- For \( \Lambda\Lambda \) with OBE potential using SU(3) couplings: coefficients \( A = 1.705 \, \text{fm} \), \( B = -1.475 \, \text{fm}^2 \), \( C = 1.245 \, \text{fm}^3 \).
- For the OBE potential fit to NN data: sigma meson mass \( m_\sigma = 501(25) \, \text{MeV} \) and coupling \( g_{\sigma NN} = 9(1) \).

## Context

This work extends the renormalization approach previously applied to NN interactions by Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola (2005, 2007) and to atom-atom systems by Arriola et al. (2009) and PhysRevA (2010). It shows that the universal low-energy theorem linking scattering length and effective range applies across vastly different energy scales, from ultra-cold atoms to nuclear and hyperon interactions. The results enable model-independent predictions for baryon-baryon systems (including charge-symmetry-breaking channels and strangeness) using only the long-distance potential as input, and suggest that short-distance effects like \( \rho-\omega \) mixing can be reparameterized into the scattering length.
