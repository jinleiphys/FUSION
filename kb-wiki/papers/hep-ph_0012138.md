# Excited Heavy Baryons and Their Symmetries II: Effective Theory

> We develop an effective theory for heavy baryons and their excited states. The approach is based on the contracted O(8) symmetry recently shown to emerge from QCD for these states in the combined large N_c and heavy quark limits. The effective theory is based on perturbations about this limit; a power counting scheme is developed in which the small parameter is lambda^{1/2} where lambda ~ 1/N_c, Lambda /m_Q (with Lambda being a typical strong interaction scale). We derive the effective Hamiltonian for strong interactions at next-to-leading order. The next-to-leading order effective Hamiltonian depends on only two parameters beyond the known masses of the nucleon and heavy meson. We also show that the effective operators for certain electroweak transitions can be obtained with no unknown parameters at next-to-leading order.

## Key claim

The paper develops an effective field theory for heavy baryons (states with baryon number one and a single heavy quark) based on an approximate contracted O(8) symmetry that emerges in the combined large N_c and heavy quark limits, but the authors explicitly note that the expansion parameter λ^(1/2) ~ 3^(-1/2) is not particularly small, so the approach is at best semi-quantitative and useful only for describing the ground state and first excited orbital excitation of Λ_Q baryons.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Hamiltonian by performing a canonical transformation from QCD degrees of freedom to collective variables (relative position and momentum of the brown muck against the heavy quark) and intrinsic variables, using power-counting rules where λ ~ 1/N_c, Λ/m_Q. They derive the form of the QCD Hamiltonian in the heavy baryon sector up to next-to-leading order (NLO, order λ) by evaluating multiple commutators of the collective variables with the Hamiltonian, using Poincare invariance, heavy quark effective theory, and large N_c Hartree picture arguments. The coefficients in the expansion are treated as intrinsic operators that commute with the collective variables.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter is λ^(1/2) where λ ~ 1/N_c, Λ/m_Q; in practice λ^(1/2) ~ 3^(-1/2), so relative accuracy at NLO is nominally of order λ
- The reduced mass μ = m_N m_H / (m_N + m_H) appears in the kinetic term p^2/(2μ)
- The heavy meson mass m_H = m_Q + O(m_Q^0), with m_N, m_H ~ λ^(-1) in the counting scheme
- Collective excitation energies scale as λ^(1/2), while intrinsic (brown muck) excitation energies scale as λ^0
- The NLO effective Hamiltonian depends on only two free parameters beyond the known masses of the nucleon and heavy meson

## Context

This work extends the formal development of the contracted O(8) symmetry for heavy baryons from Chow, Cohen, and Gelman (2000) (Ref. hb1) by constructing the explicit effective field theory that bridges to phenomenology. The approach is analogous to the contracted SU(2N_F) symmetries for light baryons in the large N_c limit (Witten, 1979; Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar, 1994-1995), where only the lowest-lying states are well described in the real world despite an infinite tower of low-lying baryons predicted by the limit. The effective theory enables future phenomenological studies of heavy baryon spectroscopy and electroweak decays (to be done in Ref. hb3).
