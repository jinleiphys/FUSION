# Azimuthal modulation in light-by-light scattering from ultraperipheral collisions at LHC

> Elastic light-by-light(LbL) scattering, one of the most fascinating processes in the Standard Model(SM), has recently been observed in the ultraperipheral collisions(UPCs) of relativistic heavy ions in the Atlas and CMS experiments at the Large Hadron Collider LHC. Recognizing that the incident quasi-real photons in LbL scattering are strongly linearly polarized, we re-investigate the LbL scattering in UPCs by incorporating the joint dependence of the impact parameter and transverse momenta of the incident photons. We show that the linear polarization of incident photons generates a sizable $\cos2ϕ$-type azimuthal modulation, which awaits the test in future LHC and EIC experiments.

## Key claim

The linear polarization of quasi-real photons emitted from relativistic heavy ions in ultraperipheral collisions generates a sizable $\cos2\phi$-type azimuthal modulation in elastic light-by-light scattering, which has not been previously predicted and awaits experimental verification at future LHC and EIC measurements.

## Method

The authors re-investigate light-by-light scattering in ultraperipheral collisions by incorporating the joint dependence of the impact parameter and transverse momenta of the incident photons within a QED transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization framework. They compute the azimuthal-angle-dependent differential cross section using one-loop helicity amplitudes for $\gamma\gamma\to\gamma\gamma$ from the literature, including contributions from three charged leptons and five quark flavors, with the photon distribution amplitudes of the heavy nuclei calculated using the Weizsacker-Williams method with a Woods-Saxon parameterization of the nuclear form factor.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for Pb+Pb UPC at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=5.02$ TeV: $78.5 \pm 2.9$ nb at one-loop level, adjusted to $83.6 \pm 3.1$ nb after applying a $K$ factor of 1.065 from two-loop QCD/QED corrections
- The $\cos 2\phi$ azimuthal asymmetry $2\langle\cos 2\phi\rangle$ remains relatively stable around 16% across the invariant mass range $5<Q<30$ GeV
- The $\cos 2\phi$ asymmetry reaches approximately 17% at $q_\perp \sim 0.02$ GeV and remains stable for $q_\perp > 0.02$ GeV
- The $\cos 4\phi$ asymmetry remains below 5% for $Q > 10$ GeV and peaks at around 5% at low $q_\perp$
- For e+Au collisions at EIC energy ($E_e=18$ GeV, $E_A=100$ GeV), the total cross section is approximately 11 fb in the specified kinematic region, with $2\langle\cos 2\phi\rangle$ approximately 10%

## Context

This work extends the study of azimuthal modulations from linearly polarized coherent photons in UPCs, previously observed in the Breit-Wheeler process $\gamma\gamma\to e^+e^-$ by Li, Zhou and Zhou (2019) and confirmed by the STAR experiment (2019), to the light-by-light scattering process. The predictions based on standard collinear factorization approaches from Klusek-Gawenda, Lebiedowicz and Szczurek (2016), SuperChic v3.0 by Harland-Lang (2018), gamma-UPC by Shao (2022), and the two-loop corrected results by Ajjath et al. (2023) all underestimate the ATLAS measurement of the total cross section. The new azimuthal modulation observable may help discriminate between different beyond-Standard-Model scenarios in future LHC and EIC experiments.
