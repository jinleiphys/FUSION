# Chiral kinetic theory with self-energy corrections and neutrino spin Hall effect

> We systematically derive the chiral kinetic theory for chiral fermions with collisions, including the self-energy corrections, from quantum field theories. We find that the Wigner functions and chiral kinetic equations receive both the classical and quantum corrections from the self-energies and their spacetime gradients. We also apply this formalism to study nonequilibrium neutrino transport due to the interaction with thermalized electrons and nucleons, as realized in core-collapse supernovae. We derive neutrino currents along magnetic fields and neutrino spin Hall effect induced by the density gradient at first order in the Fermi constant $G_{\rm F}$ for anisotropic neutrino distributions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general chiral kinetic theory (CKT) for chiral fermions that includes self-energy corrections and their spacetime gradients, finding that these corrections modify both the Wigner functions and kinetic equations at classical and quantum levels; the authors then apply this formalism to neutrino transport in core-collapse supernovae and claim the first demonstration of a neutrino spin Hall effect induced by density gradients, but only for anisotropic neutrino distributions and at first order in the Fermi constant \(G_{\rm F}\).

## Method

The authors start from the Kadanoff-Baym equations for the lesser and greater Wigner functions of chiral fermions, expand in \(\hbar\) (gradient expansion) and in the self-energies at weak coupling, and solve perturbatively for the Wigner functions up to \(\mathcal{O}(\hbar)\), \(\mathcal{O}(\bar{\Sigma}_{\chi})\), and \(\mathcal{O}(\Sigma^{\lessgtr}_{\chi})\). They then compute the retarded and tadpole self-energies for left-handed electron neutrinos in a background of thermalized electrons and nucleons under a homogeneous magnetic field, using the low-energy approximation \(q^2 \ll M_W^2\) to replace \(W\) and \(Z\) boson propagators by \(i\eta_{\alpha\beta}/M_{W,Z}^2\).

## Key numbers

* Neutrino self-energy in electron background: \(\bar{\Sigma}^{\mu}_{\rm e} = \frac{G_{\rm F}}{\sqrt{2}}\left[(1+4\sin^2\theta_{\rm W}) N_{\rm e} n^{\mu} + \frac{\hbar}{2\pi^2} \mu_{\rm e} B^{\mu}\right]\), where \(G_{\rm F}\) is the Fermi constant, \(\theta_{\rm W}\) the Weinberg angle, \(N_{\rm e}\) the electron number density, and \(\mu_{\rm e}\) the electron chemical potential.
* Neutrino self-energy in neutron background: \(\Sigma^{\delta \mu}_{\rm n} = -\frac{G_{\rm F}}{\sqrt{2}} N_{\rm n} n^{\mu}\).
* Neutrino self-energy in proton background: \(\Sigma^{\delta \mu}_{\rm p} = \frac{G_{\rm F}}{\sqrt{2}}\left(1-4\sin^2\theta_{\rm W}\right) N_{\rm p} n^{\mu}\).
* Electron number density expression: \(N_{\rm e} = \frac{\mu_{\rm e} T^2}{3} + \frac{\mu_{\rm e}^3}{3\pi^2}\).
* Magnetic-field-dependent coefficient in self-energy: \(\mathscr{C}_{\rm \ell} = \frac{\hbar G_{\rm F}}{\sqrt{2}\pi^2} \mu_{\rm e} |B|\).

## Context

This work extends the previous CKT derivation by Hidaka et al. (2017) that did not include self-energy corrections \(\bar{\Sigma}_{\chi}\), and generalizes the neutrino transport formalism of Yamamoto (2020) for core-collapse supernovae. The derived neutrino spin Hall effect, analogous to spin Hall effects known for photons (Liberman 1992, Bliokh 2004, Onoda 2004) and gravitons (Yamamoto 2017), enables the study of novel chiral transport phenomena in supernovae and other astrophysical settings where anisotropic neutrino distributions and density gradients coexist.
