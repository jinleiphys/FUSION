# Ballistic diffusion of heavy quarks in the early stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC

> We study the diffusion of charm quarks in the early stage of high energy nuclear collisions at the RHIC and the LHC. The main novelty of the present study is the introduction of the color current carried by the heavy quarks that propagate in the evolving Glasma (Ev-Glasma), that is responsible of the energy loss via polarization of the medium. We compute the transverse momentum broadening, $σ_p$, of charm in the pre-thermalization stage, and the impact of the diffusion on the nuclear modification factor in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The net effect of energy loss is marginal in the pre-thermalization stage. The study is completed by the calculation of coordinate spreading, $σ_x$, and by a comparison with Langevin dynamics. $σ_p$ in Ev-Glasma overshoots the result of standard Langevin dynamics at the end of the pre-hydro regime. We interpret this as a result of memory of the color force acting on the charm quarks that implies $σ_p\propto t^2$. Moreover, $σ_x\propto t^2 $ in the pre-hydro stage shows that the charm quark in the Ev-Glasma is in the regime of ballistic diffusion.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that charm quarks in the evolving Glasma exhibit ballistic diffusion (σ_x ∝ t^2) rather than standard Brownian motion, but the net effect of energy loss via the color current back-reaction on the nuclear modification factor is marginal in the pre-thermalization stage, with the drag force slowing diffusion by at most 20%.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills (CYM) equations for the evolving Glasma fields consistently with the Wong equations for charm quarks, including for the first time the color current carried by the heavy quarks as a back-reaction on the gluon fields. They initialize charm quarks at formation time τ_form = 1/(2m) with both δ-function and realistic FONLL transverse momentum distributions, using SU(2) color group and test particles for statistics. The transverse momentum broadening σ_p and coordinate spreading σ_x are computed and compared with standard Langevin dynamics without memory.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass m_c = 1.5 GeV used in the Wong equations
- Formation time of heavy quarks τ_form ≤ 0.07 fm/c
- For Pb-Pb collisions at LHC, g^2μ_Pb = 3.4 GeV corresponding to Q_s = 1.9 GeV
- Diffusion coefficient D = 3.37 GeV^2/fm and drag coefficient γ = 0.026 fm^{-1} for charm with p_0 = 0.5 GeV
- Thermalization time τ_therm = 1/γ ≈ 38 fm/c, much larger than the pre-hydro stage lifetime
- For p_0 = 0.5 GeV at t = 0.3 fm/c (typical pre-hydro lifetime), the drag force lowers σ_p by approximately 13%
- Memory time τ_mem ≈ 0.07 fm/c extracted from fitting σ_p data, consistent with electric field correlator decay time ≈ 1/g^2μ
- For p_0 = 0.5 GeV at t = 1 fm/c, the drag force lowers momentum broadening by approximately 40%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark diffusion in the Glasma by Liu et al. (2019), Sun et al. (2019), and Ruggieri et al. (2018) by adding the color current back-reaction that was neglected in those calculations, which only considered momentum broadening without energy loss. The ballistic diffusion regime (σ_x ∝ t^2) contrasts with standard Langevin dynamics predictions and is attributed to memory effects in the color force correlator, similar to non-Markovian Brownian motion. The results enable more complete modeling of heavy quark evolution from the pre-equilibrium Glasma stage through the quark-gluon plasma phase in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
