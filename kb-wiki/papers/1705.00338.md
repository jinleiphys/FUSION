# Charge asymmetry in elastic scattering of massive leptons on protons

> The charge asymmetry due to higher-order QED corrections in elastic lepton-proton scattering is estimated without employing the ultrarelativistic approximation. Our calculation is performed by generalizing the soft-photon approximation approach suggested by Tsai. Corresponding loop integrals that take a form of Passarino-Veltman scalar three-point functions are calculated analytically without neglecting the mass of the lepton. Our results provide model-independent charge asymmetry predictions for scattering of unpolarized and massive leptons on proton targets. These predictions can be used in corresponding experiments to determine the contribution coming from model-dependent hard two-photon exchange processes.

## Key claim

The paper provides model-independent predictions for the charge asymmetry in elastic lepton-proton scattering due to higher-order QED corrections, calculated without employing the ultrarelativistic approximation. The authors generalize Tsai's soft-photon approximation approach to include lepton mass effects, but note that the separation of photon phase space into soft (model-independent) and hard (model-dependent) regions is not unique, and they follow Tsai's definition as more self-consistent.

## Method

The authors calculate the charge asymmetry to order α³ by evaluating soft two-photon exchange (TPE) box and crossed box diagrams and soft bremsstrahlung contributions, expressing loop integrals as Passarino-Veltman scalar three-point functions calculated analytically without neglecting lepton mass. They generalize Tsai's soft-photon approach by not using the ultrarelativistic approximation and not applying the approximation Re(C₀[(-k₁,m),(p₁,M)]) = -C₀[(k₁,m),(p₁,M)] that Tsai made. The infrared divergences from TPE and bremsstrahlung contributions cancel when summed, and the final result is shifted to give zero asymmetry at Q²=0.

## Context

This work extends the soft-photon approximation approach of Tsai (1961) by removing the ultrarelativistic approximation, making it applicable to muon-proton scattering where lepton mass cannot be neglected. It provides model-independent predictions that can be used to extract the model-dependent hard two-photon exchange contribution in experiments such as MUSE, which will measure elastic μ±p scattering at low energies. The calculation is relevant to the proton radius puzzle, where a 7σ discrepancy exists between electron-based and muon-based proton charge radii measurements, and complements prior hard TPE calculations using hadronic frameworks (Blunden et al., 2003; Blunden et al., 2005) and partonic models (Chen et al., 2004; Afanasev et al., 2005).
