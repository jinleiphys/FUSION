# Coulomb Gauge Model for Exotic Hadrons

> Hadron structure advances provided by the Coulomb gauge model are summarized. Highlights include a realistic description of the vacuum and meson spectrum with chiral symmetry and dynamical flavor mixing, accurate hyperfine splittings for light and heavy systems, glueball and hybrid meson predictions in agreement with lattice QCD, and tetraquark results providing an understanding of the observed pi_1(1400) having unconventional quantum numbers.

## Key claim

The Coulomb gauge model suggests that the observed $\pi_1(1400)$ meson with exotic $J^{PC}=1^{-+}$ quantum numbers is not an exotic hadron but rather a conventional meson-meson (tetraquark) resonance with quarks in two color singlet states, contradicting the interpretation that it is a hybrid meson.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic field-theoretical approach using an effective QCD Hamiltonian in Coulomb gauge, truncated to a model space of dressed (constituent) quasiparticle Fock states. They solve the equations of motion using Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) theory for the vacuum, Tamm-Dancoff approximation (TDA) and random phase approximation (RPA) for two-body states, and variational Monte Carlo methods for three-body (oddballs and hybrid mesons) and four-body (tetraquark) systems, with the Cornell potential providing confinement.

## Key numbers

- String tension $\sigma = 0.135$ GeV$^2$ and $\alpha_s = 0.4$ set the scale for the calculation
- Bare quark masses: $m_u = m_d = 5$ MeV, $m_s = 80$ MeV, $m_c = 640$ MeV, $m_b = 3.33$ GeV
- Dressed constituent quark mass at zero momentum: $M_u(0) \cong 125$ MeV; dressed gluon mass: $M_g(0) = \omega(0) \cong 800$ MeV
- Quark condensate: $\langle q \bar{q} \rangle = -(177\ \text{MeV})^3$; gluon condensate: $\langle \alpha G^a_{\mu\nu} G_a^{\mu\nu} \rangle = (433\ \text{MeV})^4$
- Predicted $\eta_b$ mass: 9395 MeV (data: 9389 MeV); $\eta_b - \Upsilon$ splitting: 70 MeV (data: 71.4 MeV); $\eta_c - J/\Psi$ splitting: 125 MeV (data: 117.7 MeV)
- Lightest exotic $1^{-+}$ tetraquark mass: 1.32 GeV, near the observed $\pi(1400)$
- All hybrid meson $1^{-+}$ masses above 2 GeV
- Oddball $3^{--}$ mass: 4150 MeV; $1^{--}$ oddball mass: 3950 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Coulomb gauge model previously applied to mesons and glueballs (Cotanch and Llanes-Estrada, various years) to three-gluon oddballs, hybrid mesons, and tetraquark systems. The predictions for hybrid mesons agree with lattice QCD results (lattice references in text) and the Flux Tube model (flux reference in text), while the tetraquark interpretation of $\pi_1(1400)$ contradicts the hybrid meson assignment. The results enable future experimental searches for odderon trajectories in differential cross section measurements at large $t$, and suggest that tetraquark states with $I=1$ and $I=2$ at similar energies provide a useful experimental signature in different electric charge channels.
