# Quark-Hadron Duality and Effective Continuum Thresholds in Dispersive Sum Rules

> A novel procedure for extracting hadron characteristics from QCD sum rules, based on effective continuum thresholds (necessary for the implementation of quark-hadron duality) which may depend on the involved momenta and on the Borel parameter(s), enables us to quantify the uncertainties induced by the quark-hadron duality approximation and to achieve a significantly higher accuracy of our predictions for the properties of hadronic bound states.

## Key claim

The paper argues that allowing the continuum threshold in QCD sum rules to depend on both the Borel parameter and the involved momenta, rather than treating it as a constant, quantifies the uncertainty from the quark-hadron duality approximation and yields significantly more accurate predictions for hadron properties.

## Method

The authors test their procedure in quantum-mechanical models with a harmonic-oscillator interaction, where exact bound-state results are known, and compare those exact answers to the outcomes of the approximate extraction method. They then apply the same idea to QCD, using a momentum- and Borel-parameter-dependent effective continuum threshold fitted to experimentally known hadron masses, and extract predictions for heavy pseudoscalar meson decay constants.

## Key numbers

- The exact effective continuum threshold \(z_{\rm exact}\) depends on both the Borel parameter and the relevant momenta, and is not universal but varies with the studied correlator.
- The paper reports successful predictions for heavy pseudoscalar meson decay constants as the first QCD application of this method (no explicit numerical values for these decay constants are given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the authors' own recent series of papers (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2007–2010) on improving the implementation of quark-hadron duality in dispersive sum rules by introducing effective continuum thresholds. The procedure is first validated in quantum-mechanical models with a harmonic-oscillator interaction, where exact results are known, and then applied to QCD to predict heavy pseudoscalar meson decay constants. The approach enables a quantitative assessment of the uncertainty inherent in the quark-hadron duality approximation, which was previously treated as an uncontrolled assumption.
