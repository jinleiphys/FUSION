# Quasinormal modes of magnetic and electric black branes versus far from equilibrium anisotropic fluids

> Detailed knowledge about equilibration processes is of interest for various fields of physics, including heavy ion collision experiments and quantum quenched condensed matter systems. We study the approach to equilibrium at late times within two types of strongly coupled thermal systems in 3+1 dimensions: systems in the presence of (i) a non-zero charge density, or (ii) a magnetic field at vanishing charge density. Utilizing the gauge/gravity correspondence, we map the aforementioned problem to the computation of quasinormal frequencies around two particular classes of black branes within the Einstein-Maxwell theory. We compute (i) the tensor and vector quasinormal modes of Reissner-Nordström black branes and (ii) the scalar, as well as tensor quasinormal modes of magnetic black branes. Some of these quasinormal modes correspond to the late-time relaxation of the above systems after starting with initial pressure anisotropy. We provide benchmarks which need to be matched at late-times by all holographic thermalization codes with the appropriate symmetries.

## Key claim

The late-time approach to equilibrium of strongly coupled anisotropic plasmas with nonzero charge density or magnetic field is governed by specific sets of quasinormal modes of the corresponding black branes, but only for systems whose initial conditions break the appropriate symmetries (e.g., pressure anisotropy between the xy-plane and the z-direction). The authors provide benchmarks that must be matched by all holographic thermalization codes with the appropriate symmetries, but the specific quasinormal modes that dominate depend on which symmetries are broken by the initial perturbation.

## Method

The authors compute quasinormal frequencies of metric and gauge field perturbations around two classes of five-dimensional asymptotically Anti-de Sitter black brane solutions to Einstein-Maxwell theory: Reissner-Nordström black branes (dual to charged plasmas) and magnetic black branes (dual to neutral plasmas in a constant magnetic field). For the Reissner-Nordström case, they solve the linearized Einstein equations for tensor fluctuations (spin 2 under SO(3) rotations) and vector fluctuations at vanishing spatial momentum, imposing infalling boundary conditions at the horizon and Dirichlet conditions at the AdS boundary. For the magnetic black branes, they first numerically solve the background Einstein-Maxwell equations to obtain the metric functions, then compute scalar and tensor quasinormal modes around these backgrounds.

## Key numbers

* For Reissner-Nordström black branes, the extremal charge in rescaled coordinates is \(\tilde q_{ex} = \sqrt{2}\), derived from the condition that the temperature \(T = 0\).
* The temperature of the Reissner-Nordström black brane is given by \(T = (2 - \tilde q^2) r_H / (2\pi)\), where \(r_H\) is the horizon radius and \(\tilde q\) is the rescaled charge.
* For magnetic black branes, the physical magnetic field is \(\mathcal{B} = b/v\) and the temperature is \(T = u_1/(2\pi)\), where \(u_1\) is a near-horizon expansion parameter.
* The entropy density of the Reissner-Nordström black brane is \(s_{\text{RN}} = r_H^3/(4\pi)\).
* The energy density and pressure of the Reissner-Nordström black brane are \(\epsilon_{\text{RN}} = 3m/(2\kappa)\) and \(P_{\text{RN}} = m/(2\kappa)\), where \(m\) is the mass parameter and \(\kappa = 8\pi G_N\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quasinormal modes of AdS black holes and black branes, which had not systematically computed the non-hydrodynamic tensor and scalar quasinormal modes of the five-dimensional Reissner-Nordström black brane over the full range of charge densities. Prior work by Maeda (2006) studied vector quasinormal modes of Reissner-Nordström black branes, while Son and Starinets (2006) found analytic solutions for tensor fluctuations in the hydrodynamic limit. The authors also compare their magnetic black brane quasinormal modes to the late-time behavior of far-from-equilibrium systems studied by Fuini and Yaffe (2015), finding good agreement. This work enables consistency checks of holographic thermalization codes and provides benchmarks for future studies of equilibration in strongly coupled plasmas with charge density or magnetic fields.
