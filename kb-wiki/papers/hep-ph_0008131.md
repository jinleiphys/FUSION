# Meson loop effects in the NJL model at zero and non-zero temperature

> We compare two different possibilities to include meson-loop corrections in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model: a strict 1/N_c-expansion in next-to-leading order and a non-perturbative scheme corresponding to a one-meson-loop approximation to the effective action. Both schemes are consistent with chiral symmetry, in particular with the Goldstone theorem and the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation. The numerical part at zero temperature focuses on the pion and the rho-meson sector. For the latter the meson-loop-corrections are crucial in order to include the dominant rho -> pipi-decay channel, while the standard Hartree + RPA approximation only contains unphysical qqbar-decay channels. We find that m_π, f_π, <qbar q> and quantities related to the rho-meson self-energy can be described reasonably with one parameter set in the 1/N_c-expansion scheme, whereas we did not succeed to obtain such a fit in the non-perturbative scheme. We also investigate the temperature dependence of the quark condensate. Here we find consistency with chiral perturbation theory to lowest order. Similarities and differences of both schemes are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including meson-loop corrections in the NJL model via a strict 1/N_c-expansion in next-to-leading order can simultaneously describe m_π, f_π, the quark condensate, and rho-meson self-energy quantities with a single parameter set, whereas the non-perturbative one-meson-loop scheme fails to produce such a fit because the constituent quark mass does not become large enough to suppress unphysical decay channels.

## Method

The authors compare two symmetry-conserving schemes beyond the standard Hartree + RPA approximation: (1) a strict 1/N_c-expansion to next-to-leading order, where meson-loop corrections to the quark self-energy and mesonic polarization functions are treated perturbatively using Hartree quark propagators and RPA meson propagators; and (2) a non-perturbative scheme where the gap equation is extended to include self-consistent dressing by RPA mesons, with the axial Ward-Takahashi identity used to construct the consistent pion propagator. Both schemes are regularized with an independent meson-loop cutoff parameter Λ_M, and the numerical evaluation at zero temperature focuses on the pion and rho-meson sectors with full momentum dependence of all expressions.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass m ~ 300 MeV is the typical value at which the rho-meson (mass 770 MeV) would be unstable against decay into quarks in the standard approximation.
* The rho-meson mass is 770 MeV, and its dominant decay channel is rho → ππ.
* The quark condensate temperature dependence is shown to be consistent with lowest-order chiral perturbation theory in both schemes.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of mesonic fluctuations in the NJL model by Dmitrasinovic (1996) and Nikolov (1996), who developed non-perturbative symmetry-conserving schemes, and by Oertel (1999) and Oertel, Buballa, Wambach (1999) who examined the 1/N_c-expansion scheme. The paper directly addresses claims by Kleinert and Van den Bossche (1998) that chiral symmetry might be restored by strong mesonic fluctuations, showing that instabilities in the pion propagator occur only for parameter regions far from realistic values in the 1/N_c scheme. The results enable improved descriptions of vector meson properties and thermodynamics in effective quark models, particularly for studying the chiral phase transition and medium modifications of hadrons.
