# Reflection positivity in Euclidean formulations of relativistic quantum mechanics of particles

> This paper discusses the general structure of reflection positive Euclidean covariant distributions that can be used to construct Euclidean representations of relativistic quantum mechanical models of systems of a finite number of degrees of freedom. Because quantum systems of a finite number of degrees of freedom are not local, reflection positivity is not as restrictive as it is in a local field theory. The motivation for the Euclidean approach is that it is straightforward to construct exactly Poincaré invariant quantum models of finite number of degrees of freedom systems that satisfy cluster properties and a spectral condition. In addition the quantum mechanical inner product can be computed without requiring an analytic continuation. Whether these distributions can be generated by a dynamical principle remains to be determined, but understanding the general structure of the Euclidean covariant distributions is an important first step.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that reflection positivity, which is highly restrictive in local quantum field theory, is significantly weaker for relativistic quantum mechanical models of finite numbers of degrees of freedom, allowing construction of Euclidean covariant distributions that yield a Hilbert space with self-adjoint Poincaré generators satisfying cluster properties and a spectral condition, though whether these distributions can be generated by a dynamical principle remains to be determined.

## Method

The authors construct the general structure of reflection positive Euclidean covariant distributions (quasi-Schwinger functions) by first establishing Widder's theorem as the prototype for reflection positive kernels, then building unitary irreducible representations of the Poincaré group for positive mass and positive energy using the isomorphism between the covering groups of the complex Lorentz and four-dimensional complex orthogonal groups. They transform standard Poincaré-covariant single-particle states into Lorentz-covariant basis states using polar decompositions of SL(2,C) matrices, showing that the Wigner rotation functions extend analytically to complex angles, which allows the construction of two-point quasi-Schwinger functions that serve as building blocks for multi-point reflection positive distributions.

## Key numbers

* The normalization factor for unitary transformation from zero-momentum states to momentum states is N(p) = sqrt(m / omega_m(p)), where omega_m(p) = sqrt(m^2 + p^2)
* The spin representation D^s_{mu nu}[R] is a homogeneous polynomial of degree 2s in SU(2) matrix elements with real coefficients, and the matrix elements R_{ij} are entire functions of rotation angles
* The support condition for Widder's theorem requires that the integral converges for 0 <= t <= 2T where support(f(t)) is in [0,T]

## Context

This work extends the Euclidean formulation of relativistic quantum mechanics previously developed by Kopp (2011), Polyzou (2013, 2019), Aiello (2015), and Samad, building on the Osterwalder-Schrader axioms (1973, 1974) for Euclidean quantum field theory while relaxing the locality axiom. The approach contrasts with the complicated recursive construction of Poincaré generators satisfying cluster properties developed by Sokolov (1977), Coester (1982), and Keister (1991), which has never been used in practical applications beyond two or three-body systems. The paper establishes the mathematical foundation needed to eventually formulate a dynamical principle that could generate reflection positive quasi-Schwinger functions, potentially enabling practical relativistic quantum mechanical calculations without analytic continuation.
