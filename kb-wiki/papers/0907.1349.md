# The line shape of the radiative open-charm decay of Y(4140) and Y(3930)

> In this work, we study the radiative open-charm decays $Y(4140)\to {D}_s^{\ast+} D_s^- γ$ and $Y(3930)\to{D}^{\ast+} D^-γ$ under the assignments of $D_{s}^*\bar{D}_s^*$ and $D^*\bar{D}^*$ as molecular states for Y(4140) and Y(3930) respectively. Based on our numerical result, we propose the experimental measurement of the photon spectrum of $Y(4140)\to {D}_s^{\ast+} D_s^- γ, D_{s}^+D_{s}^{*-}γ$ and $Y(3930)\to D^{*0}\bar{D}^0γ, D^{0}\bar{D}^{*0}γ, D^{*+}D^-γ, D^+D^{*-}γ$ can further test the molecular assignment for Y(4140) and Y(3930).

## Key claim

The line shape of the photon spectrum in the radiative open-charm decays \(Y(4140)\to D_s^{*+}D_s^-\gamma\) and \(Y(3930)\to D^{*+}D^-\gamma\) shows a sharp peak near the large end-point of photon energy, with a smooth left side and a very rapid drop on the right side; this distinctive feature can test the molecular state assignment for these resonances, provided the molecular interpretation (specifically as \(D_s^*\bar{D}_s^*\) and \(D^*\bar{D}^*\) S-wave bound states) is correct.

## Method

The authors model \(Y(4140)\) and \(Y(3930)\) as S-wave molecular states of \(D_s^{*+}D_s^{*-}\) and a superposition of \(D^{*0}\bar{D}^{*0}\) and \(D^{*+}D^{*-}\), respectively. They compute the three-body radiative decay \(Y\to D_{(s)}^{*+}D_{(s)}^-\gamma\) using a hadron-level amplitude that factorizes into the collapse of the molecular state (described by a wave function \(\Psi(\vec{q})=\sqrt{8\pi\kappa}/(q^2+\kappa^2)\) with \(\kappa=\sqrt{2m_\tau E}\)) and the radiative transition \(D_{(s)}^{*-}\to D_{(s)}^-\gamma\) via an effective coupling \(g\). The differential decay rate \(d\Gamma/d\omega\) is derived and plotted as a function of photon energy \(\omega\).

## Key numbers

- Mass of \(Y(4140)\): \(m=4143.0\pm2.9(\mathrm{stat.})\pm1.2(\mathrm{syst.})\) MeV; width \(\Gamma=11.7^{+8.3}_{-5.0}(\mathrm{stat.})\pm3.7(\mathrm{syst.})\) MeV (from CDF Collaboration).
- Binding energy parameter \(\kappa=\sqrt{2m_\tau E}\) used in the molecular wave function, where \(m_\tau\) is the reduced mass of the \(D_{(s)}^{*+}D_{(s)}^{*-}\) system and \(E\) is the binding energy.
- Photon energy \(\omega_0=138.9\) MeV for \(D_s^{*-}\to D_s^-\gamma\) and \(\omega_0=135.8\) MeV for \(D^{*-}\to D^-\gamma\) (from PDG 2008).
- The differential decay rate formula: \(d\Gamma = \frac{g^2\omega^3\alpha^2\mathcal{C}^2}{3\pi}[\phi(\vec{k}+2\vec{p})]^2 \frac{d^3p}{(2\pi)^3}\), with \(\alpha=1\) for \(Y(4140)\) and \(\alpha=1/\sqrt{2}\) for \(Y(3930)\).

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of \(Y(4140)\) and \(Y(3930)\) proposed by Liu (2009) and Liu (2008), who suggested they are \(D_s^*\bar{D}_s^*\) and \(D^*\bar{D}^*\) molecular states, respectively. It contrasts with alternative assignments such as tetraquark (Stancu, 2009) or radial charmonium (Liu, 2009), which are disfavored by the narrow width or hidden-charm decay patterns. The predicted photon line shapes provide a direct experimental test for future measurements, complementing earlier studies of hidden-charm and double-gamma decays by Branz, Gutsche, and Lyubovitskij (2009).
