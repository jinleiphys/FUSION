# Hadron-Quark Phase Transitions in Hyperon Stars

> We compare the Gibbs and Maxwell constructions for the hadron-quark phase transition in neutron and protoneutron stars, including interacting hyperons in the confined phase. We find that the hyperon populations are suppressed, and that neutrino trapping shifts the onset of the phase transition. The effects on the (proto)neutron star maximum mass are explored.

## Key claim

The hadron-quark phase transition suppresses hyperon populations in neutron star matter, which stiffens the equation of state and increases the maximum mass of cold neutron stars; however, the simple MIT bag model used is not capable of reaching currently observed neutron star masses of about 1.7 solar masses.

## Method

The authors used the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) many-body theory with the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential plus Urbana three-body forces for the hadronic phase, including interacting Lambda and Sigma hyperons via the Nijmegen NSC89 potentials. For the quark phase they employed the MIT bag model with u, d, and s quarks and a density-dependent bag constant. They compared the Gibbs construction (allowing separate charge in each phase) and the Maxwell construction (requiring charge neutrality in each phase) for the hadron-quark mixed phase in both cold neutron stars and hot neutrino-trapped protoneutron stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of cold neutron stars with pure hadronic EOS including hyperons: below 1.44 solar masses
- Maximum mass of cold neutron stars with hadron-quark phase transition: slightly above 1.5 solar masses (difference between Gibbs and Maxwell cases very small)
- Gibbs mixed phase density range for neutron star matter: 0.20 to 0.74 fm^-3
- Gibbs mixed phase density range for protoneutron star matter: 0.29 to 0.71 fm^-3
- Temperature considered for protoneutron star matter: 40 MeV with electron fraction Y_e = 0.4
- Bag constant parameters: B_infinity = 50 MeV fm^-3, B_0 = 400 MeV fm^-3, beta = 0.17
- Strange quark mass: m_s = 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the BHF approach of Baldo et al. (2001) and Burgio et al. (2002) by including interacting hyperons at finite temperature in the hadron-quark phase transition study. It contradicts the expectation that hyperons alone would soften the EOS too much to reach observed neutron star masses, showing that quark matter can suppress hyperons and raise the maximum mass. The results enable future work with more sophisticated quark models (such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) and studies of finite-size effects (pasta structures) in the mixed phase with trapped neutrinos, as the current MIT bag model cannot reach the approximately 1.7 solar mass neutron stars observed.
