# Domain-wall Skyrmion phase in a rapidly rotating QCD matter

> Based on the chiral perturbation theory at the leading order, we show the presence of a new phase in rapidly rotating QCD matter with two flavors, that is a domain-wall Skyrmion phase. Based on the chiral Lagrangian with a Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) term responsible for the chiral anomaly and chiral vortical effect, it was shown that the ground state is a chiral soliton lattice(CSL) consisting of a stack of $η$-solitons in a high density region under rapid rotation. In a large parameter region, a single $η$-soliton decays into a pair of non-Abelian solitons, each of which carries ${\rm SU}(2)_{\rm V}/{\rm U}(1) \simeq {\mathbb C}P^1 \simeq S^2$ moduli as a consequence of the spontaneously broken vector symmetry ${\rm SU}(2)_{\rm V}$. In such a non-Abelian CSL, we construct the effective world-volume theory of a single non-Abelian soliton to obtain a $d=2+1$ dimensional ${\mathbb C}P^1$ model with a topological term originated from the WZW term. We show that when the chemical potential is larger than a critical value, a topological lump supported by the second homotopy group $π_2(S^2) \simeq {\mathbb Z}$ has negative energy and is spontaneously created, implying the domain-wall Skyrmion phase. This lump corresponds in the bulk to a Skyrmion supported by the third homotopy group $π_3[ {\rm SU}(2)] \simeq {\mathbb Z}$ carrying a baryon number. This composite state is called a domain-wall Skyrmion, and is stable even in the absence of the Skyrme term. An analytic formula for the effective nucleon mass in this medium is obtained as $4\sqrt{2}πf_πf_η/m_π \sim 1.21$ GeV with the decay constants $f_π$ and $f_η$ of the pions and $η$ meson, respectively, and the pion mass $m_π$, which is surprisingly close to the nucleon mass in the QCD vacuum.

## Key claim

The paper claims the existence of a domain-wall Skyrmion phase in rapidly rotating two-flavor QCD matter, but only within the deconfined phase of a non-Abelian chiral soliton lattice (CSL) and only when the chemical potential exceeds a critical value; the phase is established using leading-order chiral perturbation theory with a Wess-Zumino-Witten term matched to the chiral vortical effect, without requiring a Skyrme term for stability.

## Method

The authors construct the effective world-volume theory of a single non-Abelian sine-Gordon soliton within a non-Abelian CSL using the moduli approximation, obtaining a 2+1 dimensional \({\mathbb C}P^1\) model with a topological term originating from the Wess-Zumino-Witten term. They then analyze the Bogomol'nyi bound for topological lumps (baby Skyrmions) supported by \(\pi_2(S^2) \simeq \mathbb{Z}\) on the soliton world-volume, showing that when the chemical potential exceeds a critical value these lumps acquire negative energy and are spontaneously created, corresponding in the bulk to Skyrmions supported by \(\pi_3[{\rm SU}(2)] \simeq \mathbb{Z}\) carrying baryon number.

## Key numbers

- The effective nucleon mass in this medium is given analytically as \(4\sqrt{2}\pi f_{\pi}f_\eta/m_{\pi} \sim 1.21\) GeV, where \(f_{\pi}\) and \(f_{\eta}\) are the decay constants of pions and the \(\eta\) meson, respectively, and \(m_{\pi}\) is the pion mass.
- The tension (mass per unit area) of a single Abelian \(\eta\)-soliton is \(\sigma = f_\eta\sqrt{C}(8 - 2\pi S)\), which becomes zero at \(S = 4/\pi\), defining the onset of the Abelian CSL ground state.
- The critical rotation parameter \(S\) for the non-Abelian CSL decreases for \(\epsilon > 0\) compared to the Abelian case, as shown in the phase diagram for \(\beta = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of domain-wall Skyrmion phases in QCD under strong magnetic fields (Eto 2023lyo, Eto 2023wul) to the case of rapid rotation, where the anomalous coupling is via the chiral vortical effect rather than the chiral separation effect. It builds on prior work establishing \(\eta\)-meson CSLs under rotation (Huang 2017, Nishimura 2020, Chen 2021) and the decay of \(\eta\)-solitons into non-Abelian solitons (Eto 2021gyy), and it enables future studies of baryonic matter in rotating neutron star interiors and heavy-ion collision conditions where vorticity reaches \(10^{22}/{\rm s}\) as measured at RHIC (STAR 2017, STAR 2018).
