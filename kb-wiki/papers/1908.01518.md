# Jet charge in high energy nuclear collisions

> Averaged jet charge characterizes the electric charge for the initiating parton, and provides a powerfull tool to distinguish quark jets from gluon jets. In this paper, we give the first prediction for the medium modification of averaged jet charge in heavy-ion collision at the LHC, where jet productions in p+p collisions are simulated by PYTHIA6, and parton energy loss in QGP are calculated with two Monte Carlo models of jet quenching: PYQUEN and JEWEL. We found that the distribution of averaged jet charge is significantly suppressed by initial state isospin effects due to the participants of neutrons with zero electric charge during nuclear collisions. Considerable enhancement of averaged jet charge in central Pb+Pb collisions relative to peripheral collisions is observed, since jet quenching effect is more pronounced in central collisions. Distinct feature of averaged jet charge between quark and gluon jets, together with the sensitivity of medium modifications on jet charge to flavor dependence of parton energy loss, could be very useful to discriminate the energy loss pattern between quark and gluon jets in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first numerical predictions for the medium modification of averaged jet charge in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, but the observed enhancement of jet charge in central relative to peripheral collisions depends on the assumption that gluons lose more energy than quarks (with a ratio of 9/4), and the effect is primarily driven by changes in the quark-to-gluon jet fraction rather than by direct modification of the quark jet charge itself.

## Method

The authors simulate p+p collisions using PYTHIA6 with the Perugia 2012 tune and reconstruct jets with the anti-kT algorithm (R=0.4) using FastJet. For Pb+Pb collisions, they incorporate cold nuclear matter effects via EPPS16 parameterizations and simulate parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma using two Monte Carlo models: PYQUEN and JEWEL. The averaged jet charge is computed as a momentum-weighted sum of hadron electric charges within each jet, and nuclear modification factors (R_AA and R_CP) are constructed by comparing results in central and peripheral Pb+Pb collisions to p+p baselines.

## Key numbers

- Jet charge in p+p collisions at sqrt(s)=8 TeV is compared to ATLAS data (Aad 2015) and shows good agreement; the same method is then applied at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV for the heavy-ion baseline.
- In Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, the nuclear modification factor R_AA for averaged jet charge in central 0-10% collisions is considerably larger than the cold nuclear modification ratio (which is suppressed to about 0.8-0.9 due to isospin effects).
- The central-to-peripheral ratio R_CP (0-10% centrality versus 60-80% centrality) is significantly larger than 1 over the whole jet transverse momentum range studied (leading jet pT > 100 GeV, subleading > 50 GeV).
- In a scenario with r = Delta E_g / Delta E_q = 1, R_CP is approximately 1, while with r = 9/4, R_CP is clearly above 1, demonstrating sensitivity to the flavor dependence of energy loss.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of jet charge in p+p collisions (Field 1977, Krohn 2012, Waalewijn 2012) and the ATLAS measurement of jet charge at 8 TeV (Aad 2015) into the heavy-ion regime. It builds on the understanding that gluons lose more energy than quarks in the QGP (with the asymptotic ratio C_A/C_F = 9/4 in perturbative QCD models, versus a weaker dependence in hybrid strong/weak coupling models such as Casalderrey-Solana 2014). The results enable future experimental measurements of jet charge in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC to discriminate between different models of the flavor dependence of parton energy loss, complementing existing jet quenching observables like jet R_AA and dijet asymmetry.
