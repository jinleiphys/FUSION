# Mini-review on QCD spectral sum rules

> Taking the example of the most popular and well-established Borel / Laplace / Exponential sum rule (LSR), I shortly review some of its recent applications in hadron physics namely the estimates of non-perturbative condensates, the determination of the light and heavy quark masses, the extraction of the heavy-light decay constants, the estimates of charmonium and bottomium molecule masses and the properties of scalar gluonium. In addition to the standard τ(sum rule variable) and t_c (QCD continuum threshold) stablity criteria, I introduce a new stability criterion versus the arbitrary QCD subtraction point μfor extracting the optimal results when radiative QCD corrections are included. Future improvements on further uses of QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) are commented.

## Key claim

This mini-review argues that the Borel/Laplace/exponential sum rule (LSR) remains a successful semi-analytic tool for extracting hadron properties from QCD, provided one applies three stability criteria (against the sum rule variable τ, the continuum threshold t_c, and the newly emphasized subtraction point μ) to obtain optimal results when radiative QCD corrections are included.

## Method

The author reviews the standard SVZ (Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov) sum rule framework, focusing on the exponential (Laplace) sum rule and its ratios. The method uses an operator product expansion (OPE) to parametrize non-perturbative effects via vacuum condensates of increasing dimension, and a minimal duality ansatz for the spectral function (a single narrow resonance plus a perturbative QCD continuum above a threshold t_c). The new element is a stability criterion against variation of the QCD subtraction scale μ, applied alongside the traditional τ- and t_c-stability criteria, to extract optimal values for quark masses, decay constants, and condensates from various hadronic channels.

## Key numbers

*   Gluon condensate: ⟨α_s G²⟩ = 0.07(2) GeV⁴, from e⁺e⁻, Υ-η_b, and J/ψ data.
*   Tachyonic gluon mass parameter: -(α_s/π) λ² = (7 ± 3) × 10⁻² GeV², from e⁺e⁻, π-Laplace sum rule, and lattice data.
*   Light quark running masses at 2 GeV: \overline{m}_{ud} = 3.95 ± 0.28 MeV, \overline{m}_u = 2.64 ± 0.28 MeV, \overline{m}_d = 5.27 ± 0.49 MeV, from the π channel.
*   Strange quark running mass at 2 GeV: \overline{m}_s = 99.0 ± 5.5 MeV, from K, e⁺e⁻, and τ-decay data.
*   Charm quark running mass: \overline{m}_c(\overline{m}_c) = 1261 ± 12 MeV, from J/ψ and D channels.
*   Bottom quark running mass: \overline{m}_b(\overline{m}_b) = 4177 ± 11 MeV, from Υ and B channels.
*   B meson decay constant: f_B = 206 ± 7 MeV (upper bound ≤ 235 ± 4 MeV), from N2LO analysis with μ-stability.
*   D meson decay constant: f_D = 204 ± 6 MeV (upper bound ≤ 218 ± 2 MeV), from N2LO analysis with μ-stability.
*   Predicted mass for the \bar D*D (1⁺⁺) molecule (Z_c(3900) candidate): 3738 ± 152 MeV.
*   Predicted mass for the \bar B*B (1⁺⁺) molecule (Z_b(10610) candidate): 10687 ± 232 MeV.

## Context

This review extends the long line of SVZ sum rule applications (Shifman, Vainshtein, Zakharov, 1979) and the subsequent work by Bell and Bertlmann (1980s) on stability criteria from quantum mechanics. It updates earlier comprehensive reviews by the same author (Narison, 2002, 2004) by incorporating a new μ-stability criterion for handling perturbative QCD corrections, and by presenting recent N2LO results for heavy-light decay constants and exotic molecule masses. The results enable more reliable comparisons with lattice QCD calculations (e.g., FLAG, UKQCD) and with experimental candidates for exotic hadrons such as Z_c(3900) and Z_b(10610).
