# Superpenetration of a high energy Q barQ bound state through random color fields

> The transmission amplitude of a color dipole through a random external color field is computed in the eikonal approximation in order to study the absorption of high energy quarkonium by nuclear target. It is shown that the internal color state of the dipole becomes randomized and all possible color states are eventually equi-partitioned, while the probability of finding a color singlet bound state attenuates not exponentially, but inversely proportional to the distance L of the random field zone which the dipole has traveled.

# Superpenetration of a high energy Q\bar{Q} bound state through random color fields

## Key claim
The survival probability of a high-energy color-singlet Q\bar{Q} bound state passing through random color fields attenuates not exponentially but as 1/L for large target thickness L, provided the transverse dipole size is frozen by Lorentz time dilatation and the quantum coherence of multiple scatterings is fully taken into account. This result holds for both SU(2) and SU(3) color groups, contradicting earlier claims that non-abelian color degrees of freedom would restore exponential (stochastic) behavior.

## Method
The authors compute the eikonal transmission amplitude of a color dipole through a random external color field, dividing the target into uncorrelated zones of thickness l. They treat the color field as a random variable and average over field configurations, using a 4x4 (SU(2)) or 9x9 (SU(3)) stochastic matrix M(𝐝,𝐝') that describes transitions between color singlet and color octet states. The survival probability is obtained by integrating the kernel K_{0,0}(𝐝,𝐝';n) over the dipole size distribution ρ₀(𝐝) of the bound state wavefunction.

## Key numbers
- The absorption cross section of a dipole of size 𝐝 is σ_𝐝 = 3γ₀𝐝²/n₀, where γ₀ ~ g²Λ_QCD³ is a phenomenological parameter and n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is the mean nucleon density in nucleus.
- The coherence time of a J/ψ at SPS is t_c = τ_c γ ~ 3 fm (with γ ~ 10), comparable to the mean internucleon distance d = 2 fm; at RHIC (γ = 100) it becomes 30 fm, and at LHC (γ = 2000) it becomes 600 fm.
- For a thin target, the survival probability is S_φ₀(L) = 1 - 3⟨𝐝²⟩γ₀L = 1 - L/L_abs, where L_abs = 1/(3⟨𝐝²⟩γ₀) is the absorption length.
- For a thick target, the asymptotic survival probability is S_φ₀(L) ~ L₀/L, where L₀ = 2 × (1/4) × 2 × Δ⟨𝐝²⟩ L_abs for SU(2), and for SU(3) the prefactor becomes 2/9.
- For a Gaussian dipole size distribution ρ₀(𝐝) = (1/πd₀²)exp(-𝐝²/d₀²), the two length scales L_abs and L₀ coincide, and the exponential absorption formula overestimates suppression by 20% at L = L_abs for SU(2).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of positronium superpenetration through metallic foils by L.N. (1981) and L.P. (1981) to non-abelian color interactions relevant for quarkonium absorption in nuclear matter. It contradicts the conclusion of H.L.N. (1990) that stochastic (exponential) behavior reappears when non-abelian color degrees of freedom are included. The result is consistent with the semi-classical treatment of Baym et al. (1996) which also found 1/L behavior, but the present work provides a full quantum treatment showing that the 1/L law arises from destructive interference of transmission amplitudes for different dipole sizes, not just from small-dipole components. The findings suggest that the standard exponential nuclear absorption model used in J/ψ suppression phenomenology (e.g., G.H. (1992), K.L.N.S. (1997)) may need revision at RHIC and LHC energies where coherence effects dominate.
