# Nucleon Compton Scattering in Chiral Effective Field Theories

> Chiral effective field theories have a long history studying the process of Compton scattering on the nucleon. In this contribution I want to focus on the new developments that have occured since the last Chiral Dynamics conference in Mainz 1997. I will focus exclusively on the spin-dependent sector, where most of the recent work has been done.

# Nucleon Compton Scattering in Chiral Effective Field Theories

## Key claim
Chiral effective field theory calculations at next-to-leading order (NLO) can predict the four dipole spin-polarizabilities of the nucleon without free fit parameters, but the qualitative agreement with dispersion analyses depends on whether one subtracts only Born terms (procedure A) or all one-particle reducible graphs (procedure B) from the Compton matrix element. The two procedures yield identical Compton matrix elements but differ in the separation into polarizability-dependent and remainder parts, with procedure B requiring a large Delta(1232) pole contribution added by hand to match dispersion results for the isoscalar magnetic spin-polarizability.

## Method
The author compares NLO heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (chpt) predictions for the four dipole spin-polarizabilities (gamma_E1, gamma_M1, gamma_E2, gamma_M2) of the nucleon with dispersion theoretical calculations, using two different subtraction prescriptions (A and B) for extracting polarizabilities from the full Compton matrix element. For virtual Compton scattering (VCS), leading-order (O(p^3)) heavy baryon chpt predictions for the six generalized polarizabilities in the Guichon limit are compared with experimental response functions measured at MAMI at a three-momentum transfer of 600 MeV, and with leading-order small scale expansion (SSE) calculations that include explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers
- Isoscalar dipole spin-polarizabilities from chpt_NLO^A (procedure A) in units of 10^{-4} fm^4: gamma_E1^(s) = -2.8, gamma_M1^(s) = +2.8* (asterisk indicates +2.5 x 10^{-4} fm^4 from Delta(1232) pole still missing), gamma_E2^(s) = +2.0, gamma_M2^(s) = +0.3
- Isoscalar dipole spin-polarizabilities from chpt_NLO^B (procedure B) in units of 10^{-4} fm^4: gamma_E1^(s) = -3.0, gamma_M1^(s) = +0.4*, gamma_E2^(s) = +2.0, gamma_M2^(s) = +0.6
- Dispersion analysis (DKH) values for isoscalar spin-polarizabilities in 10^{-4} fm^4: gamma_E1^(s) = -5.0, gamma_M1^(s) = +3.4, gamma_E2^(s) = +2.4, gamma_M2^(s) = -0.6
- VCS response function at |q| = 600 MeV from MAMI experiment: [P_LL - (1/epsilon)P_TT] = 23.7 +/- 2.2 +/- 0.6 +/- 4.3 GeV^{-2}; chpt prediction = 26.0 GeV^{-2}
- VCS response function at |q| = 600 MeV from MAMI experiment: [P_LT] = -5.0 +/- 0.8 +/- 1.1 +/- 1.4 GeV^{-2}; chpt prediction = -5.3 GeV^{-2}
- VCS response functions at |q| = 0 (real Compton limit) from MAMI: [P_LL - (1/epsilon)P_TT] = 81.0 +/- 5.4 +/- 3.3 GeV^{-2}; chpt = 83.5 GeV^{-2}
- VCS response functions at |q| = 0: [P_LT] = -7.0 +/- 2.7 +/- 1.7 GeV^{-2}; chpt = -4.2 GeV^{-2}

## Context
This work extends the chiral effective field theory treatment of nucleon Compton scattering beyond the status at the Chiral Dynamics conference in Mainz 1997 (Holstein, 1997), focusing on spin-dependent polarizabilities. The NLO chpt results are compared with dispersion analyses by DKH, DGPV, and BGLMN, and with leading-order SSE calculations (spinpolas, 2000) that include explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom. The VCS predictions from 1997 (PRL) are compared with the pioneering MAMI experiment (Nicole, 2000) at |q| = 600 MeV, showing good agreement despite the large momentum transfer. The work enables future double-polarized Compton scattering experiments at MAMI, BNL-LEGS, and TUNL, as well as a planned polarized VCS experiment at MAMI to access spin-dependent generalized polarizabilities, and a second VCS experiment at Bates at lower momentum transfer to test the chiral prediction that pion-cloud dynamics dominates the q-evolution up to unexpectedly large momentum scales.
