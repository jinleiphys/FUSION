# Polishing the Lens: I Pionic Final State Interactions and HBT Correlations- Distorted Wave Emission Function (DWEF) Formalism and Examples

> The emission of pions produced within a dense, strongly-interacting system of matter in the presence of strong radial flow and absorption is described using a relativistic optical model formalism, replacing the attenuated or unattenuated plane waves of earlier emission function approaches with ``distorted wave'' solutions to a relativistic wave equation including a complex optical potential. The resulting distorted-wave emission function model (DWEF) is used in numerical calculations to fit HBT correlations and the resonance-corrected pion spectrum from central-collision STAR Au+Au pion data at $\sqrt{s}= 200$ GeV. The parameters of the emission function are constrained by adopting a pion formation temperature taken from lattice gauge calculations and a chemical potential equal to the pion mass suggested by chiral symmetry restoration. Excellent agreement with the STAR data are obtained. The applications are extended by applying linear participant scaling to the space-time parameters of the model while keeping all other parameters fixed. This allows us to predict HBT radii over a range of centralities for both Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions. Good agreement is found with STAR HBT data for all but the most peripheral collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including pion-medium final state interactions via a complex optical potential (distorted waves) is essential to simultaneously describe HBT radii and pion spectra from RHIC data, and that the commonly used eikonal approximation fails to capture the effects of the real part of the optical potential, which can cause oscillations in extracted radii at low momenta.

## Method

The authors replace the plane wave pions of earlier emission function approaches with distorted wave solutions to a relativistic Klein-Gordon equation that includes a complex optical potential representing pion-medium interactions. They use a hydrodynamic-motivated emission function S0(x,K) with a Bose-Einstein distribution, fix the pion formation temperature at Tc = 193 MeV from lattice QCD calculations and the chemical potential at the pion mass (139.57 MeV) from chiral symmetry restoration, then fit the resulting distorted-wave emission function (DWEF) to STAR Au+Au pion data at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV. They extend the model by applying linear participant scaling to space-time parameters to predict HBT radii across centralities for both Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions.

## Key numbers

* Temperature fixed at Tc = 193 MeV from lattice QCD calculations (Katz 2005), reducing free parameters by two to a total of nine
* Chemical potential fixed at mu_pi = 139.57 MeV (the pion mass)
* Model reproduces 32 data points from STAR central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV with high accuracy
* At momentum p = 197.3 MeV/c, using a pion density about ten times baryon density of ordinary nuclear matter, Im[U(0)] is approximately -0.15 fm^{-2}
* HBT radii decrease by about 50% over the measured momentum range
* Good agreement with STAR HBT data for all but the most peripheral collisions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Cramer 2004) by providing a detailed formalism for the DWEF approach and adding new applications including centrality dependence. It addresses the "RHIC HBT puzzle" (Heinz 2002) where measured R_O/R_S ratios are approximately 1, contrary to hydrodynamic predictions (Rischke 1996, Kolb 2003) that expected large R_O/R_S from long emission times. The approach differs from the blast-wave model (Retiere 2003) by including optical potential effects, using a non-zero chemical potential, and describing emission within the hyper-volume rather than on a freeze-out hypersurface. The work enables future applications to other collision systems and energies, and demonstrates that classical eikonal treatments of pion-medium interactions are insufficient.
