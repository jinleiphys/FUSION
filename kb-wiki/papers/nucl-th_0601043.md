# Occurrence of Hyperon Superfluidity in Neutron Star Cores

> Superfluidity of $Λ$ and $Σ^-$ admixed in neutron star (NS) cores is investigated realistically for hyperon ($Y$)-mixed NS models obtained using a $G$-matrix-based effective interaction approach. Numerical results for the equation of state (EOS) with the mixing ratios of the respective components and the hyperon energy gaps including the temperature dependence are presented. These are meant to serve as physical inputs for $Y$-cooling calculations of NSs. By paying attention to the uncertainties of the EOS and the $YY$ interactions, it is shown that both $Λ$ and $Σ^-$ are superfluid as soon as they appear although the magnitude of the critical temperature and the density region where superfluidity exists depend considerably on the $YY$ pairing potential. Considering momentum triangle condition and the occurrence of superfluidity, it is found that a so-called `` hyperon cooling\rq\rq~(neutrino-emission from direct Urca process including $Y$) combined with $Y$-superfluidity may be able to account for observations of the colder class of NSs. It is remarked that $Λ$-hyperons play a decisive role in the hyperon cooling scenario. Some comments are given regarding the consequences of the less attractive $ΛΛ$ interaction recently suggested by the `` NAGARA event\rq\rq~$^6_{ΛΛ}$He.

## Key claim

Both Lambda and Sigma- hyperons become superfluid as soon as they appear in neutron star cores, but the magnitude of the critical temperature and the density region where superfluidity exists depend considerably on the hyperon-hyperon pairing potential, and the results are subject to uncertainties in the equation of state and hyperon-hyperon interactions.

## Method

The authors constructed neutron star models with hyperon mixing using a G-matrix-based effective interaction approach, with the Nijmegen model D hard core potential (NHC-Dm) for hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions, and the Reid soft-core potential with a phenomenological three-nucleon interaction (TNI) for nucleon-nucleon interactions. They solved the energy gap equation for Lambda and Sigma- in the 1S0 pairing channel at finite temperature, using three different hyperon-hyperon pairing potentials (ND-Soft, Ehime, and FG-A), with density-dependent mixing ratios and effective masses derived from the neutron star models.

## Key numbers

- Threshold density for both Lambda and Sigma- appearance is approximately 4 times nuclear saturation density (rho_0 = 0.17 nucleons/fm^3) for all three equations of state (TNI2u, TNI6u, TNI3u)
- Maximum neutron star masses for the three equations of state with universal TNI inclusion: 1.52 solar masses (TNI2u), 1.71 solar masses (TNI6u), and 1.83 solar masses (TNI3u)
- For TNI6u at rho = 4.50 rho_0, Lambda energy gaps are 0.339 MeV (ND-Soft), 0.638 MeV (Ehime), and 0.635 MeV (FG-A)
- For TNI6u at rho = 4.50 rho_0, Sigma- energy gaps are 0.219 MeV (ND-Soft), 0.486 MeV (Ehime), and 0.641 MeV (FG-A)
- The hyperon direct Urca neutrino emission rate is larger by 5-6 orders of magnitude than the standard modified Urca process
- Effective mass parameters for hyperons at densities above threshold are m*_Y approximately 0.8-1.2, significantly larger than nucleon effective masses m*_N approximately 0.5-0.7 at rho greater than 4 rho_0

## Context

This work extends earlier simplified treatments by Takatsuka et al. (1999, 2001) that treated hyperon mixing ratios and effective masses as parameters, now using realistic density-dependent values from G-matrix calculations. The results provide physical inputs for hyperon cooling calculations of neutron stars, where hyperon superfluidity can suppress the otherwise too-rapid cooling from hyperon direct Urca processes. The paper also addresses the tension between the NAGARA event observation of 6-Lambda-Lambda-He, which suggests a less attractive Lambda-Lambda interaction than previously thought, and the implications this would have for the realization of hyperon superfluidity.
