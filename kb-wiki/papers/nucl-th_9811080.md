# Comments on the last development in constructing the amplitude for the radiative muon capture

> It has recently been claimed by Cheon and Cheoun that the discrepancy between the experimental value of the induced pseudoscalar g_P, obtained at TRIUMF from a measurement of the radiative muon capture by proton, and its value predicted by using PCAC and pion pole dominance can be explained by a contact term generated from a simple pion-nucleon Lagrangian. We show in our comment that this claim is ill founded.

# Comments on the last development in constructing the amplitude for the radiative muon capture

## Key claim
Cheon and Cheoun claimed that a contact term generated from a simple pion-nucleon Lagrangian could resolve the discrepancy between the experimental value of the induced pseudoscalar g_P from TRIUMF and the PCAC/pion pole dominance prediction. Smejkal and Truhlik show that this claim is ill founded because the contact term is multiplied by a factor (g_A - 1)/g_A ≈ 0.20, strongly suppressing its effect, and its appearance in leading order would contradict low energy theorems, current algebras, and PCAC.

## Method
The authors re-examine the derivation by Cheon and Cheoun starting from a linear sigma-model Lagrangian of the pi-N system with electromagnetic coupling introduced via minimal substitution. They identify that a term proportional to (D_mu eta) in the Lagrangian leads to the large contact contribution, but show that under the correct local transformations a new term proportional to (i e epsilon_mu eta) appears, which modifies the divergence of the axial current and ultimately multiplies the contact term by (g_A - 1)/g_A. They further demonstrate that in their own chiral invariant Lagrangian of the N pi rho a1 omega system (constructed within hidden local symmetry), such a contact term is cancelled in leading order by another contact term, leaving only higher order terms in k and q.

## Key numbers
- The contact term in question is multiplied by the factor (g_A - 1)/g_A ≈ 0.20, strongly suppressing its effect.
- The authors reference their own earlier work (Smejkal, Truhlik, Khanna, 1998) where the RMC amplitude was derived from a chiral invariant Lagrangian of the N pi rho a1 omega system, with the contact term appearing in their Eq. (3.11) but cancelled by another contact term in Eq. (3.32).

## Context
This comment directly addresses the claim by Cheon and Cheoun (1998) that a contact term could resolve the g_P discrepancy from the TRIUMF radiative muon capture experiment. The authors extend their own prior work (Smejkal, Truhlik, Khanna, 1998) on constructing the RMC amplitude from a chiral invariant Lagrangian within the hidden local symmetry approach (Bando et al., 1985; Meissner, 1988; Kaiser and Meissner, 1990; Smejkal, Truhlik, Gmitro, 1996). They also reference Fearing (1998) who argued the term violates gauge invariance, though the authors clarify the issue is actually violation of the CVC constraint. The work enables a correct treatment of contact terms in RMC amplitudes consistent with chiral symmetry and PCAC.
