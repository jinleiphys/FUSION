# Calculating Dilepton Rates from Monte Carlo Simulations of Parton Production

> To calculate dilepton rates in a Monte Carlo simulation of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, one usually scales the number of similar QCD processes by a ratio of the corresponding differential probabilities. We derive the formula for such a ratio especially for dilepton bremsstrahlung processes. We also discuss the non-triviality of including higher order corrections to direct Drell-Yan process. The resultant mass spectra from our Monte Carlo simulation are consistent with the semi-analytical calculation using dilepton fragmentation functions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a formula for scaling QCD Monte Carlo events to obtain dilepton rates, showing that Sudakov form factors must be included in the scaling ratio to avoid results that would differ from semi-analytical calculations by orders of magnitude, contrary to earlier work by Geiger and Kapusta (1993) which neglected them.

## Method

The authors use the PYTHIA event generator as adapted in the HIJING model to simulate QCD hard scatterings and final-state parton cascades in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. They count the number of virtual gluons radiated from quark lines with a given invariant mass M, then multiply by a derived differential ratio R(M², Q²_max) that accounts for the different branching probabilities and Sudakov form factors between QCD and QED processes. For direct Drell-Yan processes, they scale the number of similar QCD subprocesses (q\bar{q} → q\bar{q}, gg) by a ratio R_DY and include higher-order corrections via an overall K=2 factor.

## Key numbers

* Minimum virtuality cutoff μ₀ = 0.5 GeV terminates the parton shower, giving a minimum dilepton invariant mass of 2μ₀ = 1 GeV/c²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous semi-analytical calculation of minijet-associated dilepton production using dilepton fragmentation functions (Eskola and Wang, 1993) by validating it against a full Monte Carlo simulation. It corrects the approach of Geiger and Kapusta (1993) by demonstrating that Sudakov form factors are essential in the scaling ratio, without which the dilepton rate would be incorrect by orders of magnitude. The results enable reliable Monte Carlo simulations of dilepton production in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, and the authors note that extending this method to include final-state parton rescatterings in a quark-gluon plasma requires further analysis of higher-order corrections in finite-temperature QCD.
