# Chirality of nuclear rotation

> It is shown that the rotating mean field of triaxial nuclei can break the chiral symmetry. Two nearly degenerate $ΔI =1 $ rotational bands originate from the left-handed and right-handed solution.

# Chirality of nuclear rotation

## Key claim

The rotating mean field of triaxial nuclei can break chiral symmetry, producing two nearly degenerate ΔI=1 rotational bands corresponding to left-handed and right-handed solutions, but the authors note that strong degeneracy is not expected in practice because the calculated energy barrier between the two chiral solutions is only about 60 keV, leading to substantial tunneling.

## Method

The authors developed a three-dimensional cranking code that permits any orientation of the deformed average potential, using the Tilted Axis Cranking (TAC) approach as a mean-field approximation to the two-body Routhian. The quasiparticle Routhian was diagonalized using spherical Woods-Saxon energies for the spherical part and the Nilsson model for the deformed potential, with the total Routhian calculated via the shell correction method including Strutinsky averaging and liquid-drop energies. The deformation parameters ε and γ and the tilt angles θ and φ were found for a given rotational frequency ω by minimizing the total Routhian for a fixed configuration.

## Key numbers

- For the configuration [πh₁₁/₂, νh₁₁/₂] in ¹³⁴Pr at ω = 0.35 MeV, the equilibrium shape has ε = 0.175 and γ = 27°, with tilt angles θ = 65° and φ = 41°
- The energy barrier between the two chiral solutions in the φ direction is less than 100 keV, with the depth of the energy minimum with respect to γ being 180 keV
- At ω = 0.35 MeV, the calculated angular momentum J = 11.9 for the [πh₁₁/₂, νh₁₁/₂] configuration in ¹³⁴Pr, with intraband B(M1) = 0.80 (μ_N)² and interband B(M1) = 0.41 (μ_N)²
- The calculated ratio B(M1)/B(E2) ≈ 3 (μ_N)²/(eb)² around J = 14 is consistent with the experimental mean of 5 ± 3 (μ_N)²/(eb)²
- For the configuration [πi₁₃/₂, νi₁₃/₂] in ¹⁸⁸Ir, the equilibrium shape is ε = 0.21 and γ = 40°
- The experimental branching ratios B(M1,I₂→I₁-1)/B(M1,I₂→I₂-1) = 0.1, 0.1, 0.8, 0.5 for I = 13, 14, 14, 16 in ¹³⁴Pr

## Context

This work extends the earlier particle-rotor model study by Frauendorf and Meng (1997), which first suggested that rotation of triaxial nuclei may attain chiral character, by providing microscopic self-consistent mean-field calculations that confirm the mechanism. The paper demonstrates that the TAC approach, previously developed by Frauendorf (1993) and Kerman and Onishi (1981), can produce chiral solutions for ¹³⁴Pr and ¹⁸⁸Ir, and shows that the observed pair of ΔI=1 bands in ¹³⁴Pr (reported by Petrache et al., 1996) correlates with the calculated chiral scenario. The work enables future studies of tunneling between left- and right-handed solutions and the search for chiral bands in other mass regions, while noting that the weak breaking of chiral symmetry is analogous to the weak breaking of reflection symmetry in light actinide nuclei as described by Butler and Nazarewicz (1996).
