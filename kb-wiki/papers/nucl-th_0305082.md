# Finding the remnants of lost jets at RHIC

> A fast jet propagating through partonic matter loses energy by medium-induced gluon radiation. We propose a method to detect the energy loss of jets and the final-state interaction of the radiated gluons and the jets with the partonic medium. By embedding jets and radiated energy into a parton cascade, we find that the fate of lost jets can be studied by analyzing the momentum distribution of soft hadrons relative to the jet axis.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the energy lost by jets in partonic matter can be detected not just through suppression of high-\(p_T\) hadrons, but by analyzing the momentum distribution of soft hadrons relative to the jet axis; the authors hedge that this method provides "irrefutable evidence" for final-state jet energy loss only if experimental data can be analyzed with sufficient statistics and careful background subtraction.

## Method

Hard jets from LO pQCD (with a \(K=2\) factor for NLO corrections) were embedded into a parton cascade generated by HIJING and ZPC for central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt s = 200A\) GeV. The jets lost energy via medium-induced gluon radiation using the first-order GLV reaction operator approach (Eq. 4), with the radiated energy converted into thermal gluons via a statistical prescription based on local entropy increase. The resulting partons were fragmented using the BKK parametrization, and the final hadron momentum distributions relative to the trigger jet axis were analyzed.

## Key numbers

- Fractional energy loss \(\Delta E/E\) for gluon jets shows an almost linear energy dependence for \(E=4-7\) GeV, while at higher energy \(\Delta E/E\) reveals a \(\log(E)/E\) decrease (inset of Fig. 1).
- Elastic collisions add about \(25\%\) to the radiative energy loss (inset of Fig. 1, solid line vs dashed line).
- About 5.5 soft gluons are emitted per jet per event in central Au+Au collisions.
- For triggers with \(4 < p_T^{\rm trig} < 6\) GeV/c, the net number of extra particles at mid-rapidity was \(\Delta N = 19\) for Au+Au, versus 14 for \(pp\) collisions.
- The momentum balance \(\Delta P_x\) for the same trigger range was \(-4.2\) GeV/c, verifying momentum conservation.
- At large negative \(p_x\), the Au+Au to \(pp\) ratio shows a suppression factor of approximately 3; at low \(p_x\) the enhancement is approximately a factor of two (inset of Fig. 2).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of jet quenching developed by Wang (1994), Baier et al., Wiedemann, and Gyulassy et al., and directly addresses the interpretation of RHIC data from PHENIX, STAR, PHOBOS, and BRAHMS showing high-\(p_T\) suppression and away-side jet disappearance. The proposed method enables a direct experimental test to distinguish final-state energy loss from alternative explanations such as the gluon saturation model of Kharzeev, where mono-jets from \(2\to 1\) processes would produce few extra soft particles. The authors note that high-statistics, large-acceptance experiments like STAR or PHENIX could perform this analysis with approximately \(10^5\) trigger events.
