# Effects of three-nucleon spin-orbit interaction on isotope shifts of Pb nuclei

> We investigate effects of the $3N$ interaction, which effectively adds a density-dependent term to the LS channel, on the isotopes shifts of the Pb nuclei. With the strength so as to keep the $\ell s$ splitting of the single-nucleon orbits, the density-dependence in the LS channel tends to shrink the wave functions of the $j=\ell+1/2$ orbits while makes the $j=\ell-1/2$ functions distribute more broadly. Thereby the kink in the isotope shifts of the Pb nuclei at $N=126$ becomes stronger, owing to the attraction from neutrons occupying $0i_{11/2}$ in $N>126$. The density-dependence in the LS channel enables us to reproduce the data of the isotope shifts by the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations in a long chain of neutron numbers, even without degeneracy between the $n1g_{9/2}$ and $n0i_{11/2}$ levels. We exemplify it by the semi-realistic M3Y-P6 interaction.

## Key claim

The density-dependent spin-orbit (LS) interaction, derived from the three-nucleon (3N) interaction, strengthens the kink in the isotope shifts of Pb nuclei at N=126 by broadening the wave function of the neutron \(0i_{11/2}\) orbital, enabling reproduction of experimental data without requiring near-degeneracy between the \(n1g_{9/2}\) and \(n0i_{11/2}\) levels, unlike previous studies.

## Method

Spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations were performed for Pb nuclei using the M3Y-P6 semi-realistic interaction, with the LS channels modified by adding a density-dependent term \(v^{(\mathrm{LS}\rho)}\) of zero-range form with \(D[\rho] = -w_1 \rho/(1+d_1\rho)\). The parameter \(w_1 = 742\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^8\) was fixed to reproduce the \(n0i_{13/2}\)-\(n0i_{11/2}\) splitting at \(^{208}\)Pb from M3Y-P6, while the 2N LS interaction was returned to that of the original M3Y-Paris interaction; this variant is called M3Y-P6a.

## Key numbers

- The mean-square radius of the \(n0i_{11/2}\) single-particle function increases by \(0.49\,\mathrm{fm}^2\) when switching from M3Y-P6 to M3Y-P6a at \(^{208}\)Pb.
- The root-mean-square matter radius of \(^{208}\)Pb changes by \(-0.03\,\mathrm{fm}\) and the neutron-skin thickness by \(+0.001\,\mathrm{fm}\) between M3Y-P6 and M3Y-P6a.
- The energy difference between \(n1g_{9/2}\) and \(n0i_{11/2}\) is \(0.78\,\mathrm{MeV}\) from experimental data on \(^{209}\)Pb; with M3Y-P6a the difference is \(0.72\,\mathrm{MeV}\) at \(^{208}\)Pb.
- The binding energy changes by \(+0.4\,\mathrm{MeV}\) at \(^{16}\)O and \(-4.9\,\mathrm{MeV}\) at \(^{208}\)Pb when introducing the density-dependent LS channel.

## Context

This work extends the finding by Kohno (2012, 2013) that the 3N interaction from chiral effective-field theory gives significant density-dependence in an LS channel, which contributes to the \(\ell s\) splitting. Previous mean-field calculations by Reinhard and Flocard (1995) showed that the kink in Pb isotope shifts arises from occupation of the \(n0i_{11/2}\) orbital but required near-degeneracy with \(n1g_{9/2}\) to match data; the present study shows that the density-dependent LS interaction removes this requirement. The results enable further exploration of 3N interaction effects on wave functions in medium-to-heavy nuclei, including deformed mean-field and random-phase approximation calculations.
