# Analysis of Theta^+ production in K^+Xe collisions

> The reaction K^+Xe->K^0pX is investigated in a meson-exchange model including rescattering of the secondary protons with the aim to analyze the evidence for the Theta^+(1540) resonance reported by the DIANA collaboration. We confirm that the kinematical cuts introduced by the DIANA collaboration efficiently suppress the background to the K^+n->K^0p reaction which may contribute to the Theta^+(1540) production. We find that these kinematical cuts do not produce a narrow structure in the K^0p effective mass spectra near 1540 MeV. We study the effect of a narrow Theta^+ resonance of both positive and negative parity in comparison with the DIANA data. We show that the K^+Xe->K^0pX calculations without Theta^+ contribution as well as the results obtained with a Theta^+ width of 1 MeV are in comparably good agreement with the DIANA results. More dedicated experiments are called for to establish this exotic baryon resonance.

## Key claim

The authors confirm that the kinematical cuts used by the DIANA collaboration suppress background from nuclear rescattering, but they find that these cuts do not themselves produce a narrow structure in the K0p effective mass spectrum near 1540 MeV. Crucially, the calculations without any Theta+ contribution and those with a Theta+ width of 1 MeV are in comparably good agreement with the DIANA data, meaning the data do not clearly require the existence of the resonance.

## Method

The authors performed a microscopic calculation of the K0p effective mass spectrum for the charge-exchange reaction K+Xe → K0pX using the Jülich meson-exchange model for the KN scattering amplitude. They included the momentum spectrum of incident K+ mesons, a nuclear spectral function for bound neutrons in Xe within the Thomas-Fermi approximation, and single rescattering of secondary protons via elastic pN scattering. The Theta+(1540) resonance was added as a pole diagram in the KN potential, with its bare mass and coupling adjusted to produce physical masses and widths of 1, 5, and 10 MeV for both positive and negative parity.

## Key numbers

- The DIANA collaboration reported a Theta+ resonance at a K0p effective mass of 1539 ± 2 MeV.
- The incident K+ mesons in the DIANA experiment had momenta distributed between roughly 300 and 550 MeV/c.
- The Fermi momentum used for Xe in the uncorrelated part of the momentum distribution was qF = 220 MeV/c; the total nuclear momentum distribution for 131Xe gives qF ≈ 265 MeV/c with an average nucleon separation energy of roughly -42 MeV.
- The kaon mean free path in nuclear matter is λK ≈ 5 fm, while the proton mean free path is λp ≈ 1.5 fm.
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom for the calculation without Theta+ was 1.2 for data before cuts and 2.3 for data after cuts.
- For a positive-parity Theta+ with width 1 MeV, chi-squared/dof was 1.4 (no cuts) and 2.7 (with cuts); for width 5 MeV, 3.0 and 3.9; for width 10 MeV, 6.7 and 7.4.
- For a negative-parity Theta+ with width 1 MeV, chi-squared/dof was 1.4 (no cuts) and 2.9 (with cuts); for width 5 MeV, 3.6 and 5.2; for width 10 MeV, 9.2 and 11.2.
- The DIANA collaboration claimed an upper limit for the Theta+ width of 9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Jülich group's meson-exchange model for KN scattering (Juel1, Juel2) to analyze the specific experiment by the DIANA collaboration (Dolgolenko, 2004) that claimed observation of the Theta+(1540) pentaquark. The authors had previously applied the same model to the reaction K+d → K0pp (Sibirtsev, 2004) and found good agreement with data. The study contradicts the optimistic assessment of the Theta+ in the PDG tables and calls for more dedicated experiments using both strong probes (e.g., at COSY-TOF) and electromagnetic probes to establish this exotic baryon state.
