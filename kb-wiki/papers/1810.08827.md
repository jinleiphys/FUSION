# Polarization of nucleons in the deuteron stripping reaction on nuclei

> A general analytical expression has been obtained in the diffraction approximation for the polarization of nucleons arising in the deuteron stripping reaction on nuclei at intermediate energies of the incident particles. A tabulated density distribution of the target nucleus and a realistic wave function of the deuteron with correct asymptotic limits at large internucleon distances were used in the calculations. The nucleon-nucleus phase shifts were calculated in the Glauber formalism using the double folding potential. The calculated angular dependencies for the vector analyzing power $A_{y}$ of the reaction $^{3}\text{He}(\vec{d},p)^{4}\text{He}$ are found to be in satisfactory agreement with the corresponding experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general analytical expression for the polarization of nucleons from deuteron stripping reactions in the diffraction approximation, but the method is limited to a relatively narrow angular range where agreement with experiment is obtained, and the D-component of the deuteron wave function is neglected (expected correction to the vector analyzing power would be several percent).

## Method

The author calculates the density matrix for the stripping reaction in the diffraction approximation using Gaussian expansions of the nucleon density distribution of the target nucleus and the deuteron wave function, which allows analytical integration. The nucleon-nucleus phase shifts are computed in the Glauber formalism using a double folding potential, and the polarization is obtained from the trace of the spin operator with the density matrix. The formalism is applied to compute the vector analyzing power \(A_{y}\) of the reaction \(^{3}\text{He}(\vec{d},p)^{4}\text{He}\) at three deuteron energies.

## Key numbers

- Vector analyzing power \(A_{y}\) calculated for the reaction \(^{3}\text{He}(\vec{d},p)^{4}\text{He}\) at deuteron energies \(T = 140\), 200, and 270 MeV.
- Spin-orbit interaction parameters \((\gamma_{1},\delta_{1})\) used: \((0.143,0.143)\) for \(T=140\) MeV, \((0.141,0.117)\) for \(T=200\) MeV, and \((0.139,0.106)\) for \(T=270\) MeV.
- Normalization constant of the imaginary part of the double folding potential, \(N_{I}\), set to unity for all cases.
- Gaussian basis size \(N=10\) used for the deuteron wave function expansion with the K2 triplet nucleon-nucleon potential.
- Root-mean-square radius of the target nucleus \(R_{rms}\) used in the Gaussian expansion of the nucleon density distribution.
- Mean-square radius of \(NN\)-interaction \(r_{NN}^{2} \cong 0.65\) fm\(^{2}\) used in the double folding model.

## Context

This work extends the diffraction approximation formalism for deuteron stripping cross sections, previously developed by Serber and by Akhiezer and Sitenko, to the calculation of nucleon polarization. The method builds on earlier work (Kovalchuk, 2015) where the cross section integral was solved analytically using Gaussian expansions, and now incorporates spin-orbit interactions to compute polarization observables. The results enable comparison with experimental data for the vector analyzing power of stripping reactions and can be extended to describe inclusive processes of deuteron breakup and reactions with light and heavy ions.
