# Nuclear chiral density wave in neutron stars?

> Anisotropic phases potentially play a role in the internal composition of neutron stars, the main laboratory for the phase structure of QCD at high baryon densities. We review the study of such a phase, the chiral density wave, within a phenomenological nucleon-meson model, including nucleonic vacuum fluctuations within a renormalization scheme recently developed. Neutron stars in this model and within our approximations either do not contain a chiral density wave core or they are too light to agree with observations.

## Key claim

The paper concludes that neutron stars in the nucleon-meson model either do not contain a chiral density wave (CDW) core or are too light to agree with observations, but this result depends on the specific choice of model parameters and the inclusion of nucleonic vacuum fluctuations; the CDW is only preferred in a parameter space corner that produces equations of state too soft to support realistic neutron stars of at least two solar masses.

## Method

The authors study the chiral density wave within a phenomenological nucleon-meson model containing neutrons, protons, scalar and vector mesons, including nucleonic vacuum fluctuations via a recently developed renormalization scheme with proper time regularization. They impose neutron star conditions of beta-equilibrium and electric charge neutrality, compute the free energy density with the CDW ansatz (periodic chiral condensate), solve the stationarity conditions for the condensate modulus, wave number, and vector meson expectation values, and then use the resulting equation of state in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to construct mass-radius curves. Two different fits for the vector meson self-couplings are employed, Fit(ddd) and Fit(00d), with parameters adjusted to reproduce vacuum properties and isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at saturation.

## Key numbers

* The nucleon mass at saturation M0 is varied between approximately 0.7 mN and 0.9 mN, where mN is the vacuum nucleon mass, to explore the parameter space.
* The incompressibility K is fixed at 300 MeV, at the upper boundary of the empirical range.
* The numerical constant c in the renormalization scale is chosen as c = 1/exp(1 + 2pi^2/g_sigma^2) approximately 0.3, the smallest value that keeps the effective potential bounded in the wave number direction.
* For Fit(00d) with M0 = 0.75 mN, the binding energy of pure neutron matter at 0.5 n0 (half saturation density) is approximately 10 MeV, matching chiral effective field theory results from Tews (2018).
* The maximum mass of stars with a CDW core for Fit(00d) falls far below the observed masses of PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0952+0607, which are two of the heaviest observed compact stars.
* For Fit(ddd), the maximum mass across all M0 values considered is too low to reach the approximately two solar mass threshold required by observations.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the chiral density wave in quark-based models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Nakano, 2004) and the quark-meson model (Carignano, 2014), as well as earlier nuclear matter studies (Dautry, 1979; Heinz, 2013) that either ignored nucleonic vacuum fluctuations or were plagued by artifacts from them. The authors build on their own recent work (Pitsinigkos, 2023; Papadopoulos, 2024) that developed a renormalization scheme curing these artifacts. The results enable future investigations of whether magnetic fields can stabilize the CDW (as suggested by Ferrer, 2019 and Gyory, 2022), whether competing effects from Cooper pairing matter, and whether complementary approaches such as gauge-gravity duality (Kovensky, 2021; Ecker, 2025) can provide controlled strong-coupling calculations.
