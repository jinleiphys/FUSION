# Nonflow Subtraction Beyond Two-Particle Correlations

> Establishing collective flow in small collision systems is crucial for pinning down the minimum conditions for quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation. In two-particle correlations, nonflow has been subtracted with good control, pushing the reach of flow measurements down to very small particle multiplicities $N$. However, the multi-particle nature of collectivity has not been established in the same $N$ regime, because the residual nonflow surviving the subevent procedure in multi-particle cumulants has never been quantified. We develop a general nonflow subtraction framework for $m$-particle cumulants, built around the approximate $1/N^{m-1}$ scaling of nonflow in the independent-source picture. Correlators containing $v_1$ serve as clean nonflow estimators, since the $p_{\rm T}$-integrated dipolar flow nearly vanishes. Using \HIJING{} as a controlled nonflow-only environment, we test the subtraction for three target observables ($\langle v_2^2\rangle$, $\langle v_2^2δp_{\rm T}\rangle$, and $c_2\{4\}$) in O+O and $d$+Au at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.36$ TeV and 200 GeV. Most of the nonflow is removed, with residual fractions typically within 20--30% when converted to the two-particle level, though the best estimator differs across the three targets. We identify a multiplicity-reweighting correction, previously overlooked in two-particle correlations, that explains the long-standing undersubtraction of the naive $1/N$-scaling method; its impact grows as a power of the correlator order. The framework gives a systematic route to nonflow subtraction beyond two-particle correlations, broadening the class of multi-particle observables accessible to the small-system flow program.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general framework for subtracting nonflow contributions from multi-particle cumulant observables in small collision systems, extending the established two-particle correlation subtraction methods. The key hedge is that the framework is validated only in a nonflow-only Monte Carlo environment (HIJING) and is reliable only at low transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T} \lesssim 2$ GeV/$c$), where the genuine dipolar flow nearly vanishes and the nonflow structure is dominated by global momentum conservation.

## Method

The authors construct a scaling framework for $m$-particle cumulants based on the independent-source picture, where nonflow scales approximately as $1/N^{m-1}$. They use correlators containing $v_1$ (the dipolar harmonic) as nonflow estimators, since the $p_{\rm T}$-integrated dipolar flow nearly vanishes, making these correlators clean proxies for nonflow. The framework is tested using the HIJING event generator (which contains jet fragmentation and resonance decays but no genuine collective flow) for three target observables: the two-particle elliptic flow $\langle v_2^2\rangle$, the three-particle elliptic-flow--mean-$p_{\rm T}$ covariance $\langle v_2^2\,\delta p_{\rm T}\rangle$, and the four-particle elliptic-flow cumulant $c_2\{4\}$, in O+O and $d$+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.36$ TeV and 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Residual nonflow fractions after subtraction are typically within 20--30% when converted to the two-particle level, though the best estimator differs across the three target observables.
- The four-subevent four-particle cumulant loses a factor of $4^4/4! \approx 11$ in the number of unique quadruplets relative to the standard method.
- The effective multiplicity $N_m$ for an $m$-particle correlator is defined via $1/N_m^{m-1} \approx \sum_i N_i^m / \sum_i N_i$ for $N_i \gg m$, and satisfies $N_m \geq \langle N \rangle$.
- The framework is tested at two collision energies: $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.36$ TeV (LHC) and 200 GeV (RHIC).

## Context

This work extends the nonflow subtraction philosophy developed for two-particle correlations (the $c_0$, $c_1$, near-jet, and template methods used by ATLAS, CMS, STAR, and PHENIX) to multi-particle cumulants, which previously relied only on the subevent method (Jia, 2017) for nonflow suppression without explicit subtraction. The framework enables quantification of residual nonflow systematic uncertainties in multi-particle observables, separation of nonflow from flow decorrelation effects, and extension of flow measurements to lower particle multiplicities where nonflow dominates even after subevent suppression. The authors identify a previously overlooked multiplicity-reweighting correction that explains the long-standing undersubtraction of the naive $1/N$-scaling method in two-particle correlations.
